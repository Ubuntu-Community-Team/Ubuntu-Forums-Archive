### Post by manfo on 2005-11-04
Hello, that's very easy using GnomeBaker or K3b.

If you're on GnomeBaker, click the "Copy Data CD" button on the toolbar, then tick the "Create ISO only" box and click "Start". You will be prompted to enter the .iso filename, and that's it.

If you use K3b instead, click the "Copy CD..." button on the welcome screen, then tick the "Only create image" box in the "Options" tab, choose a path for the  .iso in the "Image" tab and click "Start".

Bye!
