### Post by molhki on 2006-12-19
The drifting mouse pointer is a well known problem with Inspiron 4100, and can be annoying as hell.
The problem is hardware related, namely the track stick. 
Since I'm new to Ubuntu and Linux in general, I don't know if there is a more elegant way to disable the track stick and leave the touchpad operational, but I sorted this out by disconnecting the trackstick cable.
To do this you need to remove the keyboard. Instructions can be found at dell support pages:
[http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/ins4100/en/sm_en/remove.htm#1112781](http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/ins4100/en/sm_en/remove.htm#1112781)

The track stick has its own cable, and can easily be removed from the keyboard connector. It's probably a good idea to isolate the exposed end of the cable with a piece of tape.
