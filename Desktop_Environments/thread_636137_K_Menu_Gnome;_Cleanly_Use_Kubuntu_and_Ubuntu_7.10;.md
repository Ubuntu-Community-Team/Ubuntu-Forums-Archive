### Post by iammeagain on 2007-12-09
I hope people will be able to find this because I have run into many threads where people are wanting to try out both Ubuntu and Kubuntu at once, then ending up with a big mess of programs. This should help keep it cleaned up.
[COLOR="Sienna"]
[SIZE="5"]In Ubuntu[/SIZE]

To organize your KDE Programs, or programs from Kubuntu, in Ubuntu use Gnome Menu Extended.

[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Gnome+Menu+Extended+%28Debian+Package%29?content=31035](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Gnome+Menu+Extended+%28Debian+Package%29?content=31035)

Download and install the Debian package. It will make a KDE folder under your Gnome/Applications Menu to store all your KDE programs. That way they are accessible but out of the way.

Now you might find you just want one in the end here is a way to get back to just Gnome Ubuntu:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome)
[/COLOR]

[COLOR="Blue"]
[SIZE="5"]In Kubuntu[/SIZE]

Now for organizing your Gnome programs, or programs from Ubuntu, in Kubuntu use K Menu Gnome:

[http://www.kde-look.org/content/show.php/K+Menu+Gnome+%28Debian+Package%29?content=31031](http://www.kde-look.org/content/show.php/K+Menu+Gnome+%28Debian+Package%29?content=31031)

Download and install the Debian package. What it will do is make Gnome folder under your K menu to store all your Gnome programs. That way they are accessible but out of the way.


Now you might find you just want one in the end here is a way to get back to just KDE Kubuntu:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purekde](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purekde)
[/COLOR]
