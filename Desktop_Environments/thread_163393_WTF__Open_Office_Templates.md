### Post by jazzmuzik on 2006-04-21
No problem. I didn't even use the minus operator in my search because I didn't know what the OP wanted to exclude, but it is a great way to find stuff. This isn't a good example, but a Google search on something like this:

openoffice OR "open office" templates -calc -oocalc

Will exclude openoffice templates for the spreadsheet, and will tend to concentrate on the oowriter instead. It's not a good example because some pages may have templates for BOTH calc and writer and those pages would be falsely excluded. But that's the best example I can come up with after one cup of coffee! Anyway, the minus operator is your friend. Point being a well constructed search can eliminate the drudge of looking through tons of search results.
