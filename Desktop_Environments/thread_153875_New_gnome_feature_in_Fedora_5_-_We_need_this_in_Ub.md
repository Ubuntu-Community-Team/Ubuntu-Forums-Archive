### Post by futz on 2006-04-01
I just recently formatted over my fedora4 install on my non-ubuntu box and installed fedora5 (FC5). They've added a feature that's SO NICE! When you move a window around the screen, it will attempt to "magnetically" align, or snap, its edges with screen edges or edges of other windows. If you've used Winamp in windows, you know what I mean. Makes it so nice and easy to move things around quickly and still have em useable.

Does anyone know if this is a built in function of X windows or if the fedora guys did it themselves? Ubuntu Dapper MUST have this!!!

Oh ya, BTW: FC5 is fast and nice. Faster than FC4 by a very noticeable amount. Programs start faster - menus respond quicker. Quite polished. There's a few little snags, but nothing show-stopping, and they're being addressed, I'm sure. I like it a lot. FC4 was my favorite distro before Ubuntu dragged me away. FC5 might drag me back, at least partly.
