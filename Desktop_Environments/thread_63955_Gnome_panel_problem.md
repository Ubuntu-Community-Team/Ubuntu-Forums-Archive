### Post by mchatel on 2005-09-09
Yeah, I ran into that same situation when I first installed Ubuntu.  I think that part of Gnome must be buggy or something.

Anyway, I had the same trouble you did, in that, because of the position of the bar, and the placement of icons on it, I couldn't grab any "free" spot of the bar, to re-position it.

I ended up having to delete or remove an icon entry on the bar, to free up a spot I could "grab".  Once that was done, I was then able to grab the bar and move it back to its previous spot, and then I simply added the icon back onto the bar.

All my icon spacing on the bar (and date placement) was off too, after this happened, so I had to readjust them to my liking.

Despite these little annoyances...  I have found that I really like Ubuntu, and now probably spend 80% of my time in it.  I still have to dual-boot to Windows XP for some things, but who knows, maybe not in the future.  I haven't even tried Wine yet.
