### Post by chavo on 2006-07-26
There is a filesystem driver that let's you use linux filesystems as native, but I got nasty BSOD from it. You can get it here [http://www.fs-driver.org/](http://www.fs-driver.org/)

There's also a standalone program that will let you explore your linux filesystems here [http://www.chrysocome.net/explore2fs](http://www.chrysocome.net/explore2fs). It is read only but like I said the other one caused BSOD on my machine. It might work perfectly on yours though so it's worth a try.
