### Post by Joreus on 2011-08-19
Pretty sure you can do it by looking up "Xubuntu" or xfce in the Ubuntu Software Center. Install that and it will allow you to log into a xfce desktop environment. 

You will however, still have all of your Gnome applications in your menus on your top panel. Nothing impacting, but it feels cluttered. :)

EDIT: Search for "Xubuntu" and at the bottom of the window select: "Show technical items." Look for an item that says "Xubuntu desktop system" and select install. It will take a while to install.

### Post by SoFl W on 2011-08-19
Yes, I run both. Look for xfce4 in the Synpatic Package Manager. When you log in, at the very bottom of the screen in the section marked "Session" you can choose between Gnome and xfce.

However I am not sure if 10.10 has 4.6 or 4.8, with 10.10 you can upgrade to 4.8

I wouldn't un-install gnome, just leave it there and make sure xfce is selected when you log in.
