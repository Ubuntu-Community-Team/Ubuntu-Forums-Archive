### Post by rosslaird on 2005-02-20
Thunderbird also has calendar integration if you use the calendar extension. It also supports html signatures (E. doesn't, at least not yet). Also, the new Mozilla Calendar app is well underway (It will fully integrate with Thunderbird by mid-2005).

Thunderbird is faster, crashes far less, has a very nice rss setup, and is generally more user-friendly. The mail-notification extension for the notification area is also better than the Evolution notification. The single advantage to Evoluton is that you can view your appointments and task list by clicking on the time/date in the gnome panel. Other than this small item, Thunderbird wins hands down.
