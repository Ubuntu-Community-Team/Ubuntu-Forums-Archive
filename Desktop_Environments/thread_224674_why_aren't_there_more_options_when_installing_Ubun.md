### Post by b_martinez on 2006-07-29
Perhaps it is not so much a way to minimize your options as it is to present a tightly integrated OS. Unless you limit yourself to 'stand-alone' programs, you will increase your dependencies with each new installation of software. This can [not necessarily WILL ] lead to a bloated, slow system, just from the amount of libraries needed to run.
  On the other hand, maybe it is a good way to make new users learn the inner workings of an OS.
  Or, it may just be the limitations of the cd that it comes on. Even compressed file systems have an upper limit as to what may be put on a 700 MB cd. 2+ GB is a lot, but it will not fit every optional program that is in the repos, and I personally think the software we do get is oriented to ease of use and low learning times.
 But then I could be wrong. LOL

Then make a well reasoned spec in Launchpad detailing why you don't like that principle, what you want it replaced with, and how, as well as preparing a wiki page that goes into more detail on your rationale, use cases, technical implementation details, etc. Then take it to the developers. You'll have very little chance of changing this principle because it's there for a reason, it's one of the essential principles making Ubuntu what it is, but if you want to cause a change in Ubuntu, any change, this is the way to do it. If you fail to cause a change, consider making your own Ubuntu derivative with the changes you want, or if you can't, try creating a community effort with people who can. It's not hard to do.
