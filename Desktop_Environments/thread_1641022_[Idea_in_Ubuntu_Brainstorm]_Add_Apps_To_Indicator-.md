The idea consists in making indicator-message(the enveloppe next to the shutdown button) configurable in order to add applications that _we_ want (with a plugin manager packed into .deb files, available in universe or multiverse repositories) but above all integrate them (informations of new messages, etc...).

Very few apps are compatible now: let me be clear, only the ones beloved by Cannonical evolution, empathy, gwibber, liferea... 
But Thunderbird ? Pidgin ? Hotot ? Skype ? (And the others less used: Claws mail, Sylpheed, Balsa, Ekiga, emesene, Chokoq, Pino, Turpial, StatusNet ?)

The solution proposed have big chances to succeed and to be integrated to Ubuntu 11.04 a.k.a. Natty Narwal. 
Why ? Because  the main objective of Canonical at this very moment is to make its OS  multi-hardware (PCs and future phones but also especially tablets, touch screens  environment). But the proposed solution  is precisely the same that is used in Rhythmbox, Banshee, Firefox or  Chrome(-ium): plugins for modularity and ease-of-use exacerbated (with only checkboxes).
