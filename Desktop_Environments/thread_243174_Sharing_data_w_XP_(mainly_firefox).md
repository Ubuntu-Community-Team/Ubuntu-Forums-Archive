### Post by teasum on 2006-08-24
Google Browser Sync is especially useful for syncing bookmarks and cookies between different machines.  However, for your dual-boot (XP-Ubuntu) system, I would check out this thread: [http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=203524](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=203524).  Specifically, I would look at post #5.  This worked flawlessly for me, and I have Thunderbird and Firefox on both XP and Ubuntu sharing the same data.  

Theoretically you could probably do the same with GAIM, although I haven't tried it.  It's not worth the effort for me, since I only have two chat accounts, and I don't need to save or log chat sessions.  I just set up my accounts in GAIM on both platforms, and left it at that.

Hope this helps.
