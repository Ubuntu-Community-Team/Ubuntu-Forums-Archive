### Post by Curlydave on 2005-10-18
Right now I have two main options for web browsing in Linux: Firefox and Epiphany. Each has advantages over the other, but I want one that has the advantages of both.

*Firefox Good: Great browser, does everything I need.
*Firefox Bad: Is slow in Linux. Startup time, web-page load time, scroll lag, tab click lag, back/foward lag, very sluggish overall feel.

*Epiphany Good: Instantaneous response, quick, snappy feel, none of the problems mentioned with firefox.
*Epiphany Bad: Interface is too big (read: less browsing area, especially when using tabs, and even with tweaking.), just too much overall wasted space in the menus which could be greatly condensed, no way to change scroll wheel rate, web-page icons rarely show up for bookmarks, harder to get mouse 4, 5 working.

Essentially I want a firefox that's not slow and laggy (like the Windows version), or an Epiphany that gives you more browsing area (with tabs open) and has working bookmark icons. Sigh. Is anyone else frustrated?

### Post by Turgon on 2005-10-18
You can tweek firefox to be much faster. 

Type in: "about:config" in the address bar. 

Type in tpv6 in the filter.

Toogle the Tpv6 so it shows TRUE

More tweeks can be found by searching in the howto forum.
