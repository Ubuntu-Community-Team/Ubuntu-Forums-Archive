### Post by Herman on 2006-02-11
Try using some kind of partitioning software to make a small linux swap area somewhere on your hard drive. The liveCD will find this and use it.

My test computer is a 'book pc' (pictured in my avatar) and it has 127mb RAM, 400 mhz CPU, and runs the 'Breezy' live CD well enough if there is a swap area somewhere on the disk. It's a little slow to get going, but it improves after a while. It never does get very fast, but it can do almost everything as long as you're not in too much of a hurry.  One thing it can not do is use GParted after applying the swapoff command. It just about freezes up.
Without the swap area it is just hopeless.

 It only needs as swap area of about 400 or 500 mb at the most (approximately half a GB), and any at all will be a big help.  This applies to some other similar live cds as well, such as Knoppix. :-D (Herman).
