### Post by afhp on 2005-12-17
The package you're looking for is *mp32ogg*.

Ogg files aren't really smaller than mp3 ; both are compressed formats. The main interest of ogg is that it's a free and open format (mp3 has strings attached).

### Post by el3ktro on 2005-12-17
I'd NEVER convert existing MP3s to OGG (or vice versa) but I'd strongly suggest to encode NEW tracks from a CD as OGG, with a quality setting of 5 or 6 (I use 6). I think not many people can hear the difference (I also know someone who does, he is a professional guitar player), but you can definitely see a difference when you analyze the waveform of an WAV/MP3/OGG file and OGG wins hands-down here (compared to MP3, not compared to WAV of course!).
