### Post by geo909 on 2008-06-21
They use different desktops.
That means other tools, other ways things are done..
Difficult to explain if you haven't try it yourself.

Xubuntu uses xfce desktop which is considerably lighter and recommended for older machines.

Kubuntu uses kde desktop. It's a more windows-like (that's what everyone says, I don't really agree) environment and it's definitely more customizable than ubuntu's gnome.

Gnome (ubuntu's default desktop) keeps things clear, user-friendly, simple and it is also considered stable.
