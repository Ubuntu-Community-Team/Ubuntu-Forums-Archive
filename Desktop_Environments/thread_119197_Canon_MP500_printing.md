### Post by vr6stress on 2006-01-18
for anyone running new canon printers, you kmnow driver support seems pretty lacking. just another reason people can't make the switch *completely*...

i got a mp500 for christmas and i just got back around to installing ubuntu again, to see how much i could do without going back to windows. i mean sure i've got specific reasons i need windows, but i wanted to explore the real possibility of making a completely switch...

but of course there's snags...wife's windows programs, my windows programs that are a must, games...and now the printer...

but through a bit of luck and testing (i'm too lazy and cheap to go through them all) i found a driver that seems to work pretty well for the canon mp500.

with the automactic driver for the mp5500 used at first everything was squished down to a small segment of the paper (and i probably missed a setting somewhere else - but i don't know where) i went searching for a new driver. the first one i came across was a "high quality (gimp print)" driver...i thought yeah this should be good - hardly...lol but it was full page. so i used the same printer with a different driver. it's the BJC-8200 with the bj8pa06n.upp driver. print quality isn't fine, but it's usuable for print (haven't tried graphics, and i doubt i will...lol), but it's full page, and so far isn't too edgy...

so for anyone else out there, if you've found something better, let me know, if not - here ya...

if i've missed something somewhere, let me know...
