### Post by smoshe on 2005-08-02
Hey guys,
I wasn't sure where to post it, so I thought I would post it here.

This weekend I went on my annual search for a Linux distro that would get me away from Windows once and for all. I don't know how I found it, but I stumbled on to Ubuntu and I downloaded it. It was the third nix OS I tried this time around. I had previously tried Suse and PC-BSD, with no luck. 

Suse fried expensive pieces of hardware on my system this time around (and sound would echo non stop. This was very annoying), and PC-BSD refused to see either my DVD+RW or my CD-R. I was frustrated. 

With the Linux distros I've tried over the years, I've always had trouble finding a comfortable balance between good hardware support and usability. That was... until Ubuntu.

Ubuntu is the best Linux distribution I've ever used. It's simple, but powerful enough to do what I want it to. My girl friend who has been using Windows all of her life has no trouble with it at all. I did get her used to using Firefox, Open Office, Gaim, and Gimp under Windows before we made the switch though. 

Every piece of hardware I own (except the Graphire tablet that Suse destroyed) is supported under it.

I love the fact that the distribution doesn't come with 20 email clients, 17 word processors, and 50 news readers on a default install. All I really wanted was Firefox, Evolution, something for MP3's, and Open Office. I was amazed to find that it was all just there. All I needed to do was make XMMS my default MP3 player. The rest of the setup was just perfect.

So I thought I would write a quick note to let you guys know that I appreciate Ubuntu, and what it does. It's outstanding.

Keep up the great work!

-Sam
