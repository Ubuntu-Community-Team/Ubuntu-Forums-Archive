### Post by ahaslam on 2006-07-08
I too had this problem with xfce. I have tried Xubuntu twice, once during beta testing an once last week. 
The first time I enabled drop shadows & window border transparencies. Once I rebooted I had the exact same problem. I reinstalled and it was the same. I changed graphics drivers in xorg.conf, so that the composite effects couldn't be displayed. This removed the drop shadows & transparencies, but still no menus.
Last week I tried a fresh install of the final release. It worked, but was very slow & jerky (even without composite).
I submitted a thread on the topic (during testing), but nobody answered. Generally I find both GNOME & KDE faster & more stable. I would recommend that you install Ubuntu and add  other DE's if required later, eg. xubuntu-desktop.
