### Post by JKyleOKC on 2014-10-04
Right-click any item on the panel and from the pop-up menu, select "panel" and then "panel preferences" to get a dialog that has three tabs on it, with the rightmost tab being "items." Select that one and scroll down to the bottom of the list. If you see a Separator there, click on it to select it and then click the "edit" button which is the second one up from the bottom of the column of six immediately to the right of the list. This brings up another dialog, titled "Separator," which will have an "Expand" checkbox that I suspect will be checked. Clear the checkbox, and everything should immediately move to the RH end of the panel. You can delete the separator entirely via the "-" button just above the edit button, if you like.

The "Separator" plugin is intended to provide space between other plugins, and can be configured four ways via a pulldown list in its properties dialog. I usually configure mine to be transparent. The Expand checkbox, if checked, causes the space to expand as far as possible to fill any unoccupied space either side of the plugin. For instance, I have a number of my plugins located at the LH edge, an expanding separator immediately to their right, and then more plugins. The expanding separator pushes that second group over to the RH edge, leaving an expanse of blank space between the two groups.

You can also position the plugins via that pulldown list in the panel preferences items tab, using the up and down buttons to move the selected item to any spot you want. If you have lots of rearrangement in mind, this is often easier than using the "move" command on each in turn.

Hope this helps!
