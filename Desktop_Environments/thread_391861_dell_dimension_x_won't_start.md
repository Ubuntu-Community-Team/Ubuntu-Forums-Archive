### Post by hikaricore on 2007-03-23
Are you wanting to try and use a different driver or go out and buy a new video card?

If the machine is worth it to you, I suggest anything mid to high range Nvida GeForce card.
Avoid ATI like the plague, driver support in linux is aweful.  Anything else is hit and miss, I've
had luck (using this term very loosely) with internal Intel chipsets and old Matrox cards with
updated drivers, but some of these require manual installation.

If you're just trying to get drivers working, search around the forum for info on discovering
the exact make and model of your video card.

Good Luck,
