### Post by antidrugue on 2005-12-22
Maybe installing the proper ATI drivers could help (the "fglrx" one from ati, not the "ati" one).

```
[FONT=monospace]
[/FONT]sudo apt-get install xorg-driver-fglrx[FONT=monospace]
[/FONT]echo fglrx |sudo tee -a /etc/modules[FONT=monospace]
[/FONT]sudo sed -i -e 's/"ati"/"fglrx"/' /etc/X11/xorg.conf

``` 

Myself I have an ATI card, so I did try those instructions and they worked. They come from [ here]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BinaryDriverHowto").

Other than that, [ GATOS]("http://gatos.sourceforge.net/") makes drivers exclusively for that purpose: video capture. I haven't tryed them.

Before you change anything, BACKUP your "/etc/X11/xorg.conf" file.
