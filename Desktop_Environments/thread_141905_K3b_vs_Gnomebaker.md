### Post by coldrick on 2006-03-09
It always intrigues me when I hear people say that k3b is so good relative to gnomebaker, and figure they must be doing something pretty off the wall.

 If I pop a blank CD/DVD into a drive, Gnome comes up with a dialog inviting me to drag 'n drop files into a window, and to push Burn when I'm done.

If I want to burn from an image, I cancel the window, right-click on the image, and say Burn.

If I want to copy a disc, I put it in the drive, right-click on the desktp icon, and say copy.

For the life of me, I can't think of anything else I'd want to do.

Oh, and btw, I've had Nero screw up - tried to burn a DVD image (Knoppix), and the result wasn't bootable (well, it booted, then hung). Used Gnomebaker, and all was well.

So maybe we have a religious issue here. Surely not - never heard of anything like that between KDE and Gnome fans :D
