### Post by DuckHook on 2020-02-29
Hi oygle,

The first steps with a new DE tend to be like a fish out of water. Just take it slow and easy and pretty soon, the new ways of doing things become second nature.

Ubuntu can't move from Unity to Gnome 3 and keep things the same. They may try to keep the look and feel as close to each other as possible, but it's really done for psychological comfort and not because they are really similar.

When I first moved onto Bionic, I missed some things that I liked about Unity, but it didn't take long to discover things I really liked about Gnome 3. As just one example, I really apreciate the night light setting that is native to the new DE (I'm a night owl and reducing the blue at night is essential). I didn't have to install and futz around with Redshift like I had to in Unity. There are dozens of other examples.

Xenial reaches EoL next year. I think you are acting wisely moving to Bionic now. It has had two years to work out its teething pains and you are leaving yourself plenty of time to get used to it before Xenial dies.
