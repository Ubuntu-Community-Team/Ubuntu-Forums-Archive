                             VertRefresh                C-D

fill up A,B,C,D from monitor's manual. save xorg.conf and reboot.

### Post by markovuc on 2005-11-24
You can use program xvidtune to center the screen. Start it from terminal, and after clicking on 'left' or 'right', click on 'apply' button to see the changes. They will be lost after you reboot, to save them you must do the following: Click on the 'show' button in xvidtune, and line will appear in terminal window (from which you started xvidtune). Copy that line to monitor section of your xorg.conf file.

I have dual boot machine at home, and had similar problem. Used xvidtune to set the screen in linux, don't know how to do that in win ;) , but after I installed nvidia proprietary drivers (I have gf2), screen was centered the same in win. as in linux.

I hope this will help.
