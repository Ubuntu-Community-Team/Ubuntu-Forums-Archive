### Post by Rockvole on 2013-01-30
I was wondering how to disable all mouse gestures in Lubuntu 12.04 ?
I use a contour rollermouse and to click the mouse button I push on a rollerwheel which is what I also use to move the mouse cursor.
Any tiny movement on the rollerwheel while clicking can cause a gesture (e.g. forward/back page on browser) - this happens almost every time I click.

I tried installing easystroke to see if I could disable all from there, but that didnt seem possible. I looked into chrome and firefox help to see if it could be specifically disabled in the browser but it seems like its not part of the options in there.

Does anyone know how to disable gestures completely ?
Thanks.

### Post by Rockvole on 2013-02-07
So I fixed the problem myself although not in Lubuntu.
I found that the newer versions of rollermouse pro have a dipswitch to disable the mouse button press when pushing the rollerbar. Mine is an old rollermouse with no such option.

So I disconnected the switch on the rollerbar.

Procedure :
1) Remove all the screws on the back.
2) Remove the silver "switch settings" sticker and remove the screw under there.
3) On the left hand side is a switch which is pressed when you push the rollerbar.
4) Cut the red wire to this switch and it will no longer work.
