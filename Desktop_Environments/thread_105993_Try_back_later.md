### Post by Stormspace on 2005-12-19
Well, I've been slugging away at Ubuntu for about two months and have made considerable progress with a few notable exceptions. 

1. Unexplained program crashes that only a reboot will correct.
2. Very poor game support. The few games I have are all older and should be supported by wine, but they are not. The same reason I don't play world of warcraft is why I won't pay for Cedega.
3. The spam filter in the only reasonable replacement for Outlook (Evolution) is crap. A better solution might exist but I weary of looking for apps just to get the PC working. 
4. Palm support isn't very good. Only one in ten attempts to sync the palm in the past month has worked and setting it up initially was a large pain.
5. Still no support for my scanner and joystick. Though I was completely surprised at the digital camera support. Very good indeed.
6. Slow rip speeds for Audio CD's. - Even when I tweaked this out the rip time for an audio CD was barely better than real time. A better option might have been available, but again how many apps do I have to preview before I get one that works?
7. No legal DVD support - While I'm not one to be overly concerned about this, it does have some drawbacks as most of the freely available editing software is windows based.
8. Time - For the past two months I've had to spend hours configuring the PC, installing software, asking questions on the forum, reposting when they went unanswered, very thankful when there was an answer. 
9. GIMP - I tried GIMP, it seemed ok. But I just didn't have the time to work out all the features. It's probably a great app, I just didn't have the time to use it with all the system issues I was working on.
10. Networking - Networking in a mixed enviroment is just not a friendly thing to do with Linux at present. Namely is if for some reason the networking apps fail there's no notification that they aren't working. The file browser/app just dies requiring a reboot.
11. Font's - It's a minor issue really but the default font doesn't render very well and is hard on the eyes. I could probably work through it given time. 

You know I think Ubuntu is a great OS but my frustration level has been slowly increasing over the past couple of weeks and I've even found myself dreading to do anything on my PC since there's always an issue waiting. Support here on the site has been absolutely great and I will very likely continue to use Ubuntu in a lesser role, or at work where the machine doesn't have to be as versatile. I'm just not going to use it on my main Desktop any longer. Perhaps I'll try again when Vista comes out since I'm determined not to give MS any more of my money. At any rate, thanks for all the help.

### Post by Stormspace on 2006-01-25
I pretty much still am not going to use Ubuntu or Linux as a desktop machine, but I did discover something that I thought I'd mention here. My wife gave me a copy of Guild Wars for christmas so after installing it on the newly installed Win 2000 machine I discovered strange lockups and reboots. After quite a bit of investigation I determined that the issue was being caused by a bad stick of memory. This memory issue was also probably what was causing many of my Linux issues as well, but with no messages it was impossible to diagnose.

At any rate I ended up hosing my mobo in the process of installing new memory when I slipped with a screwdriver while trying to rejumper the board for DDR memory. <insert comic yosemite sam cussing here> So it was all good since I ended up with a system upgrade. Sempron 2400. 

So, the main reason for this post is to remind those installing Ubuntu that not all issues can be directly attributable to linux, some are hardware related and aren't discovered until you use the PC at it's extreme limits.
