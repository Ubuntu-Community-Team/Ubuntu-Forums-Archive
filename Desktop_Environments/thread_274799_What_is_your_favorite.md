### Post by Mr Frosti on 2006-10-10
I have tried about 5 different desktop environments and I have a pretty good idea for the strengths and weaknesses of each.

Gnome - Gnome is powerful but simple. If you are looking for complex features, and applications with hundreds of options, you are looking for something else. Don't confuse simplicity with weakness however. Many common tasks are effortless. For the most part, the complex options are not missed. Minimalism seems to be the approach

KDE - KDE is more for the what I will call "artistic", or "power-user" groups. Its focus is to develop applications that are complex and powerful. If you could possibly imagine an application doing something, it can inside of KDE. Even the options for the KDE window manager blow anything else away. Its strength is also its weakness, however with applications and operations so complex, it is easy to become confused or overwhelmed. It always seemed that their complex options were for functionality that I never needed to begin with.

Enlightenment - This is a very fast running desktop environment, and highly customizable, but the familiar "desktop" feel is gone. Is is just window management with few applications for common tasks such as screen resizing, or theme management.

Fluxbox - This is also a very fast running desktop environment. Same as enlightenment, this isn't for the faint of heart. Much of the setup involves editing configuration files.

All this said, I would recommend Gnome for a first time user, and KDE for someone looking for something more advanced.
