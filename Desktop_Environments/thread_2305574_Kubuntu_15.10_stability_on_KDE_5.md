### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-12-07
If you like KDE, stay with a KDE4 release, like Kubuntu 14.04. Or the very similar Linux Mint KDE edition. Nothing new is going to happen with KDE4, and it has been effectively put out to pasture by the KDE team, but it has benefited from regular monthly fixes for some time and is quite stable, IMO. 

The issues I've seen in Kubuntu's KDE5 I've also seen in other distribution, so I'll attribute those to KDE, not to a distro's packaging efforts.

A fair amount of the KDE4 packages have not yet been ported to KDE5, but need to be included by distributions so users get the expected functionality out of KDE. This includes KMail and everything associated with it. This mixing and matching seems to me to be a recipe for potential problems.

One thing this mix and matching requires is the inclusion of two KWallet engines, one for KDE5 programs and one for KDE4 programs .  Sadly, both KWallet programs continue its tradition of confusing, broken, user-hostile behavior and design.

Frankly, I've concluded that KDE5 is a developer-centric release that delivers very few new capabilities to users.
