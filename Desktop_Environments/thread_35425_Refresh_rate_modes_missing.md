### Post by beefsprocket on 2005-06-15
If you want to do it manually you can look up the specs for your monitor (Horizontal and Vertical Refresh rates) and specify them when you run xorgconfig (not sure about xorgcfg). This has always worked for me in the past. 

Before you get too carried away however, check to see what the defaults are in your xorg.conf file. Specifically screen resolution and bit depth. Sometimes it is a simple matter of adding a resolution to your default line i.e. 
> 
        SubSection "Display"
        Depth           24
        Modes           "1280x800"
EndSubSection

So you'd want (for whatever bit depth is your default) to add Modes "1600x1200" to the beginning of the line. Make sure this res comes before any others at the depth or it won't be the first line X sees. 

You can configure these options near the end of running xorgconfig.
