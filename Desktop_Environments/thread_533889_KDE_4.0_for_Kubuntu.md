### Post by shakin on 2007-08-24
> **logan_huff said:**
> Is the new KDE 4.0 far enough along to run on kubuntu, maybe in beta form? How would I go about making the desktop upgrade? Thanks, Logan.

kde4 beta 1 is in one of the repositories. Kubuntu.org has the info.

It's not nearly stable enough to run and it's not nearly complete enough to bother testing. When you first start it you'll think you're in KDE 3.5 except for a weird plasmoid in the top left corner. You can load a clock and a few other plasmoids, but that's it. The UI look and feel is nowhere near complete.

To be honest, even as a KDE user I am a little disappointed at what I've seen so far in KDE 4. I understand it's not complete, but KDE 4.0 is supposed to be released in two months and the release candidate is supposed to be available in one month. Unless they have done a lot of work that has not already been committed to the beta I don't see how the development team can get this ready in time without it being a disappointment. Perhaps nearly all the work is complete and it's just the UI lagging behind.

Having said that, I am very excited about the KDE 4 APIs that should make later KDE 4 versions much better.

Sorry for the long-winded answer. Hopefully by the release candidate on Sept 25 we will see a lot more KDE 4 features instead of the 3.5 features that are in the beta.

On the UI side, it's pretty much What You See is What You Get: the oxygen style & windec aren't (IIRC) in the Beta, but they've recently begun a re-write from scratch, and so are also very rough.  Plasma has quite a few little applets in trunk/playground which weren't included in the Beta, but things like proper panels, system tray and a taskbar are still missing (although all of them have appeared, in very rudimentary form, since the Beta).

I've been playing the "KDE4.0 will be a huge disappointment and basically for early adopters only" tune for a few months now, and I'm actually becoming more pessimistic as time goes by.  Thankfully, all the pieces for long-term success are in place, so I have little worries about the state of KDE4 by the time 4.1 rolls around, but KDE4.0 is going to be a pretty big PR black eye for the KDE project, I think, unless things improve drastically over the next couple of months.

### Post by Erunno on 2007-08-25
I'm also starting to think if it would be not wiser to delay the final release of 4.0 for a few months (for instance first quarter of 2008 ) until all relevant pieces (panels, Oxygen, Decibel, Kopete, etc) are in place and have some additional Beta and RC releases instead. I am still under the impression that, considering the major overhaul, the beta phase is way too short. Since a lot of people are looking forward to the release of 4.0 and will try it out without being aware of current and feature developments I fear that the public backlash will be huge and KDE will struggle a long time to repair the damage done to the public perception of the 4 series. In a perfect world 4.0 would have at least have all functionality and critical apps of 3.5 production ready with a complete new style to make it visually distinct from KDE3 but right now it looks like they'll unleash an unstable and incomplete desktop upon the curious public.

Oh well, I guess that's the difference between commercial software development where you work on the project as long as time and money allows and open source where releasing often is commonplace to get the necessary feedback from real users.
