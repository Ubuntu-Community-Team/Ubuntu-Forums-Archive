### Post by kotty1216 on 2007-04-12
Ok, so how do I do that?

---

### Post by ahaslam on 2007-04-12
Add this to the end of your xorg.conf:
> 
Section "Extensions"
    Option	"Composite" "Enable"
EndSection

And reboot

### Post by teryan2006 on 2007-04-19
Currently, the "radeon" driver doesn't support the ATI X1xxx series card yet, so you are stuck with using fglrx. To get desktop effects working, you'll need to use XGL instead of the default AIGLX composite extensions.  You might want to take a look at these instructions [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=399643&highlight=beryl+200m+feisty") .
