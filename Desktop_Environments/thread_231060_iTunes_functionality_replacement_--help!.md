### Post by n00buntu NJ on 2006-08-06
I don't care about replacing iTunes per se, although it was an easy, one-stop shop for what I want out of a music player.  What I really miss is the ID3 tagging, automatic cataloging + album art.  I might not have used the correct phrases so let me explain:

**Album Art**
In iTunes I could select a group of songs and drag and drop an album cover to use for album art.  This was way to easy, and I really miss it.  I'm anal about collecting all the album art of my music, so I've gotta find a replacement.

**Cataloging + ID3 Tags (the "Keep my music organized" option)**
This is probably the thing I miss most.  A lot of times I would have several albums by the same artist, but with incorrect spellings of the artist's name.  If I would select all the songs by this artist, type the correct artist name and it would not only edit the ID3 tags, it would reorganize the directories of my computer (moving the actual files to the proper directory) and delete the redundant folders.


My Ubuntu machine is becoming a media server of sorts and now stores 30+ GB of music that is becoming wildly disorganized.  

I no longer care about finding an iTunes replacement, although Banshee is so close (yet so far away).  I just want a ID3 tag editor that will also sort the files on my HDD like the "Keep my music organized" option of iTunes.  With 7000 songs and climbing I can't do it manually.
