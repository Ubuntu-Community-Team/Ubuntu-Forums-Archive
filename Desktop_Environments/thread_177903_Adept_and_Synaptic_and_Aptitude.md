### Post by Jucato on 2006-05-17
You can have Adept, Synaptic and Aptitude all installed with no problems.
However, you can only have one of them running at a time. This goes also for apt itself. 

Synaptic is, in many ways, superior to Adept, at least in Breezy. I'm not sure what progress has been made in Dapper. I find Synaptic to be more user-friendly. Adept, IMHO, is for experts or people who know what they are actually doing (not for people working late at night :D). This is coming from a full-time KDE user.

### Post by Jucato on 2006-05-17
Some features of Synaptic that Adept does not have:
1. User Interface that is easy to use/understand.
2. Prompts you for any change that you will make. It informs you of what actions will be taken when you hit the Apply button. The only way you get these info from Adept is through the status bar or through the Preview Changes button, both of which are not good places to place such important information. I've seen so many posts complaining about accidentally removing important packages because Adept does not prompt them for confirmation. In this sense, Adept is for experts/experienced users.
3. Keeps a log of changes that you made or allows you to save marked changes in a file.
4. Categories. Adept has tags, but they are not as easy to use/understand as Synaptic's categories.

One thing I like about Adept, though, is the filter search. It's much faster than Synaptic's own Search function. (Synaptic's quick search function only searches package names).

Of course, Synaptic is GNOME's package manager while Adept is Kubuntu's (note: only a Kubuntu app, not present in other KDE distros).

Other than those things, they're practically the same. It's your pick. But I generally recommend Synaptic for beginners/intermediate users, reserving Adept for the more experienced ones who prefer to stick to Kubuntu's defaults.
