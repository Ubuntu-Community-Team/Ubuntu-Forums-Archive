### Post by fragos on 2006-09-18
The most recent AMD CPU socket is AM2.  Selecting a mobo with an Nvidia chip set would give you 3D acceleration and a reduced overall cost.  PCIe does seem to be the hot video bus and of course DDR2 is the latest memory technology.  The first decision I make when I upgrade a mobo is do I want to reuse existing hardware like DDR.  If not, it makes more sense to me to go for the newest bits because then come next mobo upgrade I'll be more likely to reuse some bits.  The best CPU/mobo prices I've seen in the US are at pcclub.com.  They have a big selection and if nothing else can give you a sense of whats available.

### Post by nullmind on 2006-09-19
For what it's worth newegg.com has always been very good to me, along with AMD, ASUS and nVidia.

I don't have many complaints, but I recommend stay FAR away from anything with a Broadcom related chip in it or something made from ECS (Elite Computer )

I notice when I go into Fry's (not sure if they have these outside of Oregon) they have the $150 machine that does boost some good stats for a cheap system. (Usually a 2Ghz+ processor with 512Mb ram), but the board is an ECS board that will easily screw up quickly. After some digging around I found out that ECS and Fry's have a common parent company.
