### Post by funicorn on 2017-11-01
That's part of the nature of Linux desktop, i.e., separately developed components led by separate parallel projects and teams with no special role responsible for the subtle and exquisite transition between the two components, and thus lacks of beauty and smoothness of integrity. The sin hides in the core of Linux Community which is, relative to a commercial company,  organized in a loose and flat way that refrains sustainable development of hierarchically structured projects.
