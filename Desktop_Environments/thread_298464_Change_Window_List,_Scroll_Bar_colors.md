### Post by cenoxo on 2006-11-12
How can I change the default colors of active buttons in a Window List panel, and the default colors of the active window's scroll bar?

I'm using Ubuntu 5.10 with the default 'Human' theme on an older laptop, and have rearranged the Desktop similar to Microsoft Windows. There's a single-row navigation panel at the bottom of the screen, with Applications/Places/System menus on the left end, a Window List in the middle, and Network/Power/Volume icons and Clock on the right end.  

Question 1 -

Unless I have my LCD screen tilted at just the right angle, the light gray color of the active window button in the Window List shows up poorly. 

How can I change the active button from a gray background and black text to a brown background and white text (to match the standard colors of highlighted text in Ubuntu)?

Question 2 -

The default grey background of the active Window List button causes another navigational problem. It's the same shade of grey as the background of the scroll bar in the active window, but the scroll bar itself is white (which is the same color as inactive buttons in the Window List.) Thinking that active colors should be alike, I'm constantly clicking on the scroll bar's grey background instead of the white scroll bar itself, causing my window contents to inadvertently shift up or down.

The scroll bar's grey background is OK, but how can I change the white scroll bar to brown?

Taking some cues from general [color theory]("http://www.graphics.com/modules.php?name=Sections&op=viewarticle&artid=431"), warm colors tend to advance, and cool colors tend to recede. In [aerial perspective]("http://psych.hanover.edu/krantz/art/aerial.html"), more distant objects tend to desaturate and turn grey. Grey is thus better for backgrounds, but warmer, more saturated colors -- especially reds, oranges, and yellows -- are better for foreground objects.
