Most of the files I encoded myself and they play fine in windows and on my iPod, I believe I used the lame encoder. I did some google and forum searching but couldn't find anything besides install gsteamer, which is already installed. I've tried playing the file in Totem and Rhythmbox.

The error I get in Totem is:
Totem could not play 'file://home/park/50cent Many Men.mp3'. 

You do not have a decoder installed to handle this file.

### Post by stalker145 on 2006-07-15
You need to install the various proprietary CODEC's to be able to play this music. You can either piece together support for mp3, wma, wmv, etc or you can download and install one of the ready-made solutions (EasyUbuntu, Automatix, etc. - I personally use Automatix). With those, it's as simple as click and run.

Good luck on this. It's a pain to have to do the extra work, but I feel it's worth it in the long run.
