### Post by louis_nichols on 2006-05-05
The KDE desktop environment has an app called klipper that does this. It can be started under Gnome also, but it will come with all libraries required for kde.

Gnome doesn't have one, and actually lacks the ability to even remember clipboard after closing the app where text was copied from. Although this can be avoided with an app called [gnome-clipboard-daemon]("http://members.chello.nl/~h.lai/gnome-clipboard-daemon/"). I saw that there is a project on sourceforge called [gnome-clipboard-manager]("http://gcm.sourceforge.net/") but hasn't released files yet.
