### Post by deadflowr on 2018-08-27
The date-time setting in the middle of the panel is how it's always been on the gnome shell.
Unity on the other hand has always used the more common right side location.
Where the older version of gnome tended to place it.

It's one of several things which you'll find you need to adjust yourself for.

Two quick things I think you can do to make life a little easier on yourself is one
Install the package Gnome Tweaks ( It should be gnome-tweaks in apt and Gnome Tweaks in Software store)
Peruse the Software stores Extensions section; (extensions offer you a fair amount of customization so you can tweak various settings and designs to fit your needs.
Or at least allow you to set it more tolerable for yourself if need be)
You can then control added extensions through Gnome Tweaks (should show up as Tweaks in the App menu system)

Here's a couple links on gnome3 that might be helpful:
[https://wiki.gnome.org/Projects/GnomeShell/CheatSheet]("https://wiki.gnome.org/Projects/GnomeShell/CheatSheet")
[https://wiki.gnome.org/Gnome3CheatSheet]("https://wiki.gnome.org/Gnome3CheatSheet")
