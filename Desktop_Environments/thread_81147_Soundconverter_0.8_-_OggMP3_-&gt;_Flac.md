### Post by abou on 2005-10-23
There wouldn't be a point to it.  Any information that was excised during the compression is gone forever, which is another reason why converting from MP3 -> OGG or vice versa would actually produce a worse sound quality.  This is because that the respective converters remove different portions of audio for the compression and both would be removed in the change.

Now, if you had actual audio CDs with the original, uncompressed audio (e.g. bought from a store), that is a different story.

### Post by abou on 2005-10-23
OGGs work differently than MP3s in terms of compression.  Generally, they use quality settings, but those are semi-equivelent to MP3s kbps settings - for example Q7 ~ variable 256 kbps.  Sometimes they have kbps in menus, but those just tend to screw people up.

I would just use the Q10 setting.

### Post by abou on 2005-10-23
I have heard a number of things about VBR.  What I have noticed is that unless you set a max and min, 320 will be the starting point and the compressing engine will expand or shrink that according to the needs of the frame it is looking at.  So softer parts will be encoded less that 320 and more bombastic stuff will be greater than 320.

The average then would be around 320 kbps.
