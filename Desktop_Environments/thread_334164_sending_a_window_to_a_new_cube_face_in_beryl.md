### Post by jpneron on 2007-01-15
Try this:

- click in the window you want to move
- Press Cntrl+Alt+Shift, then use the left/right arrow to move the desktop cube. The cube will rotate under the window, and just stop when you get to where you want it to be.

More than a few keystrokes, I know, but really cool to watch...:-)

### Post by dabl on 2007-01-15
Some interesting things happen with the Function keys, too.  Give it an F8, for example, after you have opened windows on multiple sides of the cube.

You can spin the cube with your mouse wheel.  I noticed that if you slide an open window to the right or left, so that the window edge goes past the edge of your display screen, the missing part of your window appears on the adjacent side of the Beryl cube, when you turn it.
