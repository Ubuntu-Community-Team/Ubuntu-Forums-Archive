### Post by Michael Steinberg on 2005-12-22
What you have is a Winmodem. It's not a full hardware modem; it relies on Windows to do part of the work. Winmodems are not a lot of fun to set up in Linux--I just bought a cheap used PCMCIA modem card to use in my laptop instead because I didn't figure my time was being well spent messing around with the software. But lots of people have made them work. You can check in the Wiki for a general introduction to Scanmodem and kernel modules. For your specific modem there's a how-to on the web (not Ubuntu-specific) at [http://www.linux.com/howtos/PCTel-MicroModem-Config/index.shtml](http://www.linux.com/howtos/PCTel-MicroModem-Config/index.shtml)

Probably if you get started & have specific porblems others can help guide you.

Good luck.

Michael Steinberg
