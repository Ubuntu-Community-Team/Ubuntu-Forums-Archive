### Post by evil-boweevil on 2005-11-11

### Post by xequence on 2005-11-11
OGG is better quality when encoding from a lossless source, but if you take any lossy codec and encode it into another lossy codec the resault isnt very good. Its called transcoding. Some cant hear it, but some can. Its like the whole thing about what bitrate to use, some people think 128 Kbps is CD quality and others wouldent stand for it, opting for something higher.

Id recomend ripping your CDs to ogg just dont convert MP3 to OGG.

And also there is the problem of compatability. MP3 works with almost anything while OGG support in mp3 players isnt as strong.
