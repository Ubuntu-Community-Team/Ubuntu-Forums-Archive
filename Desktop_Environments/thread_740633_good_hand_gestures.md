### Post by Mehashi on 2008-03-31
Do you mean in a virtual environment? Things like opening and closing hands to open and close files? 

The best way to find out what the 'intuitive' commands are, is to put someone else at your computer and get them to perform various actions. Take observations of your own gestures as you explain what you want the person to do. Even better if you can film the whole thing. 
Careful attention would need to be given to make sure frustrated gestures and things like moving hair out of your face wouldnt give errant commands. 

This is very similar to how I could produce a catalogue of body language for a particular person in varying situations for psychology. I would be very interested in seeing virtual technology take off as it has endless possibilty!
What are you using to translate the gestures to commands? Some sort of glove?
Good luck!

### Post by chewearn on 2008-03-31
I would imagine, when you start to investigate this, the conventional mice interface would need to be discarded, because it would not fit (possibly became less efficient).

Think of sign language.  The computer will make use of visual cues (via a webcam?) to read your commands:
1. A dialogbox pops up with OK/cancel; therefore, I make a half-joking suggestion of thumbs up or down.  There is no need to point the mouse cursor to the OK/cancel button, which will be tiring after a while

2. When you want to open application, it could be a search based launcher; you wave your hand, the launcher pops up; you make alphabetical gestures for apps name, the list is trimmed until you arrived at your selection.  Then, a thumbs up or down to run or cancel.  The system could learn your favorite apps, and put the most often used on top of the list.

3. To close an apps, do a karate chop :lol:

4. To flip thru apps, do like Tom Cruise, one palm in front of another, and "cycle"...

5. The commands would only be read when you gesture closer than three feet (or some suitable distance) from the camera; so random hand movement outside the "read" zone will be ignored.

See how much better this could be, compared to mice based interface? :mrgreen:
