### Post by Phlod on 2005-08-06
Just something for you to consider, re: the ugly text.

Covering it up is all fine and well while your computer is functioning correctly.  Should your computer suddenly fail to boot, that ugly text can help you, or whoever you call in to fix it, to diagnose and fix the problem in no time.  If you cover that up with a 'Loading' screen, it can sometimes be quite difficult, or impossible to see that text once the computer has crashed.  Usually you can hit the Esc key and get rid of the Loading screen, but some problems are funny, and I found once with Mandriva's loading screen that I couldn't get around it to see what the problem was.  I had to just reinstall.

So, while you may not understand a word of what flashes by as Linux boots, it's very useful information in the right hands.  And when the chips are down, it's good to be able to easily get at that information.

--Phlod

(Incidentally, nearly all the Windows users I've introduced to Linux exclaimed, 'Oh neat, it's actually telling me what it's *doing* when it boots up!')
