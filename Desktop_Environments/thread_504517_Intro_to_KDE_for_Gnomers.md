### Post by RickR on 2007-07-19
As for the panel (taskbar), you can fix that easily. Right-click the existing panel and click "Add Application." Add the applications you want to it. Then, you can right-click the KMenu and whatever else you'd like to remove and hit remove. To change the size and other options, right-click then hit configure panel.

Not sure about the rest, I've always liked the one-click-open.

1. You can change whatever you want with KDE -- just add another panel! KDE is just as customizable as GNOME, it's just different the way you do it. To add panels, right-click the existing panel and choose "Add new panel".

2. You can change the icon theme quite easily. First, hop over to [http://kde-look.org](http://kde-look.org). Get an icon theme you like, and download it. Then open up system settings, and get to the icon dialog. Just click install, and select your downloaded theme. It will install automagically. The same way as GNOME does!

3. That is in the mouse settings.
