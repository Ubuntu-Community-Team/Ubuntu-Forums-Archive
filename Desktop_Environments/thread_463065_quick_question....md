### Post by Interestedinthepenguin on 2007-06-13
Here's what I did, using the GIMP:  

Go to File > New, and set Image Size to about 20x22 pixels (22...or whatever the height of your task panel is).  

Fill the image with any color of your choice, and select the Rectangle tool ('R' key).  

With the Rectangle tool, select about a quarter (or half) portion of the image, starting at the top, then select the Gradient tool ('L' key).  

After you've selected the Gradient tool, change the foreground color to white, and make sure: 

the opacity's set to 100 
mode: Normal
Gradient:  FG to Transparent.
Offset:  0.0
Shape: Linear  
Repeat:  None  

Now, take the Gradient tool, click at the top of the selection that you made earlier, and drag the tool about halfway through the selection.  Let go of the mouse button.   

When you are satisfied with how the gradient looks, you may cancel the selection. (I get the rectangle tool, and just click the image.)

You should now have a square that looks shiny.

You'll most likely have to enlare the view of the image to 200%, so you don't strain your eyes too much.
