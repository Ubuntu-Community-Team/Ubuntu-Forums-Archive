### Post by ben2talk on 2011-02-03
After using Gnome-do (with docky as it started developing) and then a separate install of docky for the last year or so, I think it's best not to comment until I've tried AWN.

Having loaded it up, I find the 'edgy' setting is very attractive. Personally I am not so crazy about the animations (it doesn't 'feel' as nice as docky - or is it just different?)

It does the job of taking the application launchers (custom items mostly for me, as I use keyboard shortcuts for my net/terminal/updates and any regular jobs I can remember a simple shortcut for). I loved Trayer to handle indicators - but it seems AWN takes this job on a little nicer than Docky did, though not without some problems (just had to manually install vobject to get one applet working, and the clock is still borked - need to find out why a click on the applet tells me it can't read data from an external source....

Not without some serious papercuts or bugs. Docky loads up and just works... and I loved it for it's simplicity. I must say, however, that looking at the 'edgy' AWN now with launchers/running apps on the left side, applets in the centre, indicators and 'tray' items nicely handled on the right side - it's looking like a fairly complete dock.

I think I'll give it a try for a few days and see if it's any better (or less of a barrier between 'thinking to doing') for my system than docky.
