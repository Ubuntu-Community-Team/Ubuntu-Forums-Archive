### Post by auricle on 2006-06-29
I thought finding the Philosopher's Stone or the Holy Grail was difficult (I have them both on the mantlepiece by the way), but finding a mail/PIM client that suits my needs is even more difficult!

My needs:
1) Good, solid IMAP support
2) Good LDAP support with read & write capability
3) The ability to access a ical file on the web and read & write to it.

Just a list of three but I was totally unsucessful in satisfying them.

As with everything else, mail clients have pros and cons. I have summarised the three most common ones below:

Kontact - KDE's PIM
Pro: Excellent LDAP and remote ical support - all works without a hitch
including reading/writing to LDAP directories.
Con: IMAP is a bit 'twitchy', can crash the application when trying to
update, and reports have been made of people losing emails.

Evolution - Gnome's PIM
Pro: Excellent IMAP support, good options for synchronisation. Read/ write for CALDEV calender servers.
Con: I could not get write support for LDAP on my Tuffmail account, only
read only. I have read in several places that Evolution has LDAP read/write! Read only for remote ical calenders.

Thunderbird with Lightning Calender extension- Mozilla's mail client
Pro: Rock solid IMAP support, Lightning calender plugin shows great
potential. At the moment it can read/ write to ical and caldev.
Con: LDAP is read only. Thunderbird is not a 'full featured' PIM
application like Kontact (with KMail) or Evolution yet.

I have briefly tried Opera's mail client. It looks good and works very well but it doesn't have LDAP support yet.

I haven't tried Seamonkey, can anyone give me the rundown on this? Does it have the same capabilities and limitations as Thunderbird?

So my advice to people looking for an open-source solution is choose
which feature is the most important to you and go with the appropriate
client because at the moment there isn't one that can do everything very
well.

I really would like to use Evolution as it integrates so well into Gnome, including Beagle support but at the moment I am using Thunderbird. It is quick and simple to use and IMAP feels rock solid. I also like the way I can add to it with extensions to get exactly the features I need. I know that there is a Thunderbird backend being made for Beagle now. I hope it is finished soon!

Just my little rundown. If you have any thoughts, comments or suggestions, please feel free!!
