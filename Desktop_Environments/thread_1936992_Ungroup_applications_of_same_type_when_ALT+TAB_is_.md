### Post by NamiJason on 2012-03-07
The way I explained doesnt require the mouse.

Hit Alt+Tab. 
Let go of Tab (continue holding Alt)
While still holding Alt hit Tab until you get to the group you want.
While still holding Alt hit the down arrow.  

You will now get a great display of all of the windows in that group and can arrow over to whichever you want.

No mouse needed and super fast to switch windows.

### Post by markbl on 2012-03-08
> **NamiJason said:**
> 
Hit Alt+Tab. 
Let go of Tab (continue holding Alt)
While still holding Alt hit Tab until you get to the group you want.
While still holding Alt hit the down arrow.  

You will now get a great display of all of the windows in that group and can arrow over to whichever you want.

This is not the intended way to do this. You alt-tab between applications and then alt-<key above tab> between windows of the same application. <key above tab> is usually the backtick key on (most?) US keyboards. If you want to switch windows immediately in the current app then you can alt-backtick immediately.

The alt-tab/backtick scheme I quote here is how gnome-shell, Unity, & Mac OS X all work now (by default) so I suggest you get used to it. I.e. it is an improvement on the old alt-tab "all windows" switching.
