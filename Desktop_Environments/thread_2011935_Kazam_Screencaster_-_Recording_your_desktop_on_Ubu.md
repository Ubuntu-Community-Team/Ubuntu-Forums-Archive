### Post by snowz on 2012-06-28
There is this free screencaster named **Kazam** that is not that well known under **Ubuntu** than **RecordMyDesktop**. Basically it is a lightweight desktop screen recording application that is pretty much easy to use.

It's **GUI** looks something like this:

[IMG]http://ubuntuone.com/3snHKDdXZMZQxIz4T3Robz[/IMG]

Long time it didn't have the record region option but it was still a very good recording application. This is how the** RECORD REGION **option looks like:

[IMG]http://ubuntuone.com/3KaGJJ4NVr4eGEKNsEKIZi[/IMG]

Basically you can resize the area however you like till the screen limit.

The video quality is very good. For example you can easily record high definition recordings to upload to YouTube. 

At the end of every recording you get two options to choose from. 

[LIST]
[*]Record with one of your video editors
[*]Save to file
[/LIST]
It looks like this:


[IMG]http://ubuntuone.com/7RZtWViop2IKMv910WcORb[/IMG]


The format the video files are saved in is [SIZE=2]**.webm**[/SIZE]


I don't think it is needed for me to write more in this thread.


The **Launchpad** link is: [https://launchpad.net/kazam](https://launchpad.net/kazam)
