### Post by pksings on 2006-07-04
Actually there is a program called wallpapoz that will do just what you want. Quicky google search should turn it up... 

It's a daemon that monitors what desktop you are on and changes the wallpaper accrordingly.

It seem that early on in the Gnome days there was a "territorial" disagreement and the nautilus folks won out.  So nautilus paints your background. And they refused to include this functionality. I am still not clear if that is because of technical difficulty, laziness, useability issues or political reasons. Whatever, it seemingly ain't gonna happen so some other folks wrote programs that provide this functionality.

There are others, I just found this one most recently.
