I have no idea about the debconf point in your question, but to uninstall properly try reinstalling, noting any other dependencies it brings in, then use synaptic to "Completely remove" all that was installed.  You could also try to use the Status button on the left of the synaptic window, and then choose "Not installed (residual config)" and see if you can get rid of any bits left behind.
