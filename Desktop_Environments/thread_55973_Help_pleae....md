### Post by GavinX on 2005-08-10
I'm sorry to hear that your Ubuntu experience was not as good as you intended it to be. If you needed to get Ubuntu, what you should have done was to go into Kynaptic and searched for 'Ubuntu Desktop' and/or Gnome Desktop and GDM. Having done that you just select all the relevant entries, then remove KDM. Then, you would have just selected Gnome at startup. And voila, you would have been in the money.

But you said you wanted to remove Ubuntu, right? All you need to do is to enter Kynaptic and search for 'gnome' or 'gnome deaktop' and select all that are installed and then click remove. Then, you would be without any Gnome.
