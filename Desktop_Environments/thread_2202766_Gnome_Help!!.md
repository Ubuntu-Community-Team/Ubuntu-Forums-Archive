### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-02-01
Unless you are very short of disk space, leaving Unity alone does no harm. It does not execute when you use Gnome or another DE, so removing it will have no impact on memory usage.

Some packages that have nothing to do with Unity, Thunderbird, for example, are compiled against one or two Unity libraries. They expect those libraries to be available.
