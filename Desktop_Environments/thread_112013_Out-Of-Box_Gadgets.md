### Post by Aphorism on 2006-01-03
Being a very happy amd64 user, I have been reading an awful lot about issues with Java, Flash, Realplayer, MP3, etc.  If you don't see a problem with these formats, perhaps you should take a moment to educate yourself on the legal implications of using such technology.  Onwards...

In this light, I am going to try and make a little post about stuffs that I have found useful for getting things to work 'out-of-box' with Ubuntu.  In many respects, I have found these choices to not only work as good as the standard and commercially-well-flogged items, but better (Read iPod here)!

First, MP3 players and such.  We all want Ogg Vorbis, and now we want FLAC.  Here is a very handy link to compatible players complete with information on the firmware items etc.  Further, I am sure it requires some updating, so maybe we can get off our asses and update it.  (By the way, the FLAC player I found was iAudio's)

[http://wiki.xiph.org/index.php/PortablePlayers](http://wiki.xiph.org/index.php/PortablePlayers)

Printers are a whole realm alltogether.  The most influence we can have here is to be heard, so send mail to your printer manufacturer and ask for the disclosure of their documentation.  Please *do not* ask for a Linux specific xxx as that does not solve the problem!

[http://www.linuxprinting.org](http://www.linuxprinting.org)

Hardware in general has been useful to me as well... to get an ultimate list of very open source style hardware is very difficult.  To this end, I turned to the OpenBSD team's list to see exactly what hardware plays fair.  OpenBSD has a mandate much like Ubuntu, so there is a certain kinship there.  If it plays fair with OpenBSD, it has a chance to play fair with Ubuntu.  Here is their Hardware Compatibility Listing - just click on the relevant architecture to get a full listing of hardware with notes.

[http://www.openbsd.org/plat.html](http://www.openbsd.org/plat.html)


If you are looking for a great wireless router that you can convert to running Linux, go buy a Linksys WRT54G (applicable version) and install this freeware firmware so that it will run Linux.

[http://www.openwrt.org](http://www.openwrt.org)

[http://www.dd-wrt.com](http://www.dd-wrt.com)
[http://wrt-wiki.bsr-clan.de/index.php?title=Main_Page](http://wrt-wiki.bsr-clan.de/index.php?title=Main_Page)


I sincerely hope this helps someone out there.


Further, it would be nice to help out our brothers and sisters using OpenBSD, FreeBSD, NetBSD, etc.  **Documentation and specifications** are the only way to help everyone in opensource land.  Also, and this is important, stress that you do **not** want them to do *anything*.  A lot of companies think that you want them to invest time and money developing drivers!  This **isn't** the case, and stress that to the poor fellow who reads the emails.  We don't want the companies to spend a single cent more than what they have -- only to disclose their stuff so that their *hardware* will work.  Period.
