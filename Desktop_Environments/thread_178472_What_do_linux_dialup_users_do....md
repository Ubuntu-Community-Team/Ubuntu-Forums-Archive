### Post by changlinn on 2006-05-17
not 100% correct, the v.92 standard is supposed to support putting the internet connection on hold when a phone call comes in, I don't know if linux supports this as I wouldn't use dialup unless I was desperate...
Or the OP could mean something like voip on dial-up which can be done but gives poor performance, you can just get skype it's compression is not to bad and it will work on dial-up although badly.

Edit: v.92 is a pretty rare standard for isp's to support and if they don't have the modem racks that support it then your out of luck anyway, I have only seen one isp in australia support it, none of the big ones do yet, and I doubt they will most are on the push to broadband and I'd say the same can be said for the rest of the world (Australia is so far behind in the internet stakes, I am surprised we are even talking dialup).
