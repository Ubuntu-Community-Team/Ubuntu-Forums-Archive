### Post by coffeecat on 2006-07-09
> **CheeseAndToast said:**
> I  don't want windows on the same HD as dapper.

Why not? Having Windows and Linux as a dual-boot on the one HD is a perfectly good solution - so long as the HD is big enough. Anyway, since there are so many ways of setting yourself up with two OSs, you need to give more information on what sort of configuration you want otherwise people may post information that may not be relevant.

Questions: Do you want a two-disc setup with Windows on one disc and Ubuntu on the other? Do you want a shared data partition? Have you thought of using a removable hard-drive caddy fitted into a spare bay at the front of the case. You then simply pull out your Linux HD in its carrier (say), insert the Windows one and boot into Windows. (Or vice versa). Or had you something else in mind?

Another thing to consider. If you re-install Windows and you want a dual-boot you'll need to have some way of getting back into your Ubuntu installation to re-install Grub to the mbr. 

There are several other things you need to bear in mind but without a better idea of how you want to set things up it's difficult to advise.
