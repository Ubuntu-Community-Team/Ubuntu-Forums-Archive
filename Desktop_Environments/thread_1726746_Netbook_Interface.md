### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-11
Unity is still in Beta and getting faster every day. Wait until the final release (in a few weeks) before you make the call whether you like it or not.

Other than that, I can suggest XFCE. It is a lot like Gnome2, however, it is very fast and "snappy". You can easily adjust it to something similar to Unity and/or Gnome2 or whatever you like. XFCE also has more features than LXDE and is overall more stable.

Gnome 3: I don't think Gnome 3 is faster than Unity. I for one find it very dysfunctional, although the features that I am missing may not be something that affects you. If you don't use your mouse a lot, Gnome-shell will probably work for you.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-04-11
On my netbook I looked at 10.04 UNE but hated the netbook interface, so went straight back to the classic gnome but using only a single bottom panel.  10.10 pushed me even further down that road as I found the UNE interface on that version totally un-usable.  I have not looked at 11.04 yet, but from what I hear, I am not going to like it, though time will tell whether or not that is correct.

However, I have now tried and love Lubuntu 10.10 on the netbook.  It really flies along, boots from pushing the button to the desktop in 20-25 seconds, shuts down in about 3 seconds, and apart from a bit of a steep learning curve regarding configuration, is at the moment exactly what I want and need for my netbook.  At rest it uses about 80MB ram.
