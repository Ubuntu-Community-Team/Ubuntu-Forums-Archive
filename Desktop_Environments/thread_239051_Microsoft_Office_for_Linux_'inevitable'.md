### Post by Sethro on 2006-08-18
Microsoft will release a version of Office to run on Linux within the "next couple of years", according to the chief executive of the Open Source Development Lab (OSDL). "They did it once with Apple; they will do it again with Linux," Stuart Cohen said during an interview with vnunet.com at the LinuxWorld conference in San Francicso.

OSDL aims to advance Linux and open source. The consortium employs Linux founder Linus Torvalds, and is funded by IT vendors including IBM, HP and Intel. Cohen argued that Microsoft will be compelled to create a Linux version in a move to pre-empt the further rise of Sun Microsystems' open source OpenOffice productivity suite.

### Post by tturrisi on 2006-08-20
> **Ziox said:**
> then there is no point in making a Linux version of MO, cuz no one's going to use it...
On the contrary.  The majority of businesses, corporations, universities, schools, home users, etc., use MSOffice as the main office applications.  like it or not, the business world relies heavily upon msoffice apps.

However, many networks may switch to a linux distro as the operating system in order to save money IF their employees-users can accomplish everything they would normally accomplish using windows.  Presently, openoffice pales in comparison to what msoffice can do for a business net.  Verfy few businesses running windows are switching to the windows version of openoffice because it costs too much to train employees to use new software.

MS would be smart to release a linux version of office.  MS is primarily a software manufacturer & the real money they make is not in operating systems but in other software such as office.  And who cares if software is free in cost or not?  If the software does what you need & it does it well, then a price is worth it.

I have client businesses that run msoffice, openoffice, corel, intuit & others, all on the same networks.  Each suite & application has their specific uses, openoffice is used primarily to sometimes repair docs that get corrupted. (even ms has admitted that the Redmond offices sometimes uses openoffice to repair word docs that get corrupted)

Bottom line is that releasing msoffice will broaden their market, not by much, but if the numbers look promising to ms then they will do it & if the numbers don't look promising then they won't.
