### Post by mc4man on 2010-11-13
While I use click and hold for r. click context (then select/execute on mouse up/release), you can get the the context menu to 'stand' by using a r. click 'tap' instead of a r. click 'push'

It's more likely to open on r. click push on my desktop w/ a razor mouse (high dpi) then on a laptop.

At least here the cursor is always in the menu so something is always highlighted (as compared to windows where the cursor is always outside of the menu). which also adds to the possibility of an unintended action

Edit: or enable the sim. secondary click and just use the left button, shortest time is about 1 sec. (when cursor changes fully). Then a release will only open the context menu, not exec.
