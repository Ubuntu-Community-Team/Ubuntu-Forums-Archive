### Post by Marshall2day on 2005-12-27
The easiest thing to do in my opinion is to resize your windows partition first with a tool like partition magic or something. Before you do this, it's best to defrag your disk. After you resized your disk just boot from the install cd and tell the ubuntu installer to use the unallocated space. This should work without any hassle.
