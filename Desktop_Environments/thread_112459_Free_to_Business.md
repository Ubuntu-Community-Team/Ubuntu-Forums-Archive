### Post by az on 2006-01-04
[QUOTE=f1dave]
*resell it for money
[/QUOTE]

Sure you can.  RedHat and Linspire sell their distro.  You still have to provide the source code and make any modified version GPL licenced just like the original.


[QUOTE=f1dave]
*modify the source code and claim it for yourself
[/QUOTE]

You can modify it and claim the recognition for your work.  You just have to recognise the work that was done before you.  It is not your software, it is everybody's software.  That's the idea behind free software.  Pay the programmer for her work, not for the right to keep her code as your property.

[QUOTE=f1dave]
*modify the source and sell it
[/QUOTE]

Sure, you can do that.

[QUOTE=f1dave]
*or anything equally silly

You can go right ahead and slash your IT bills :)[/QUOTE]

The only thing silly is to make software into property.  Making money is important.

The GPL describes all the rights of free software.  There are no restrictions to using it in a business setting.
