### Post by ddrichardson on 2009-04-17
They're just different interfaces, its down to personal preference. I like XFCE because most of the UNIX systems I cut my teeth on used CDE, making XFCE a shallower learning curve.

Ubuntu does use Gnome but I wouldn't go so far as to say this is a deliberate choice over KDE or XFCE, one of Mark Shuttleworth's requirements was for the OS to fit on one CD so multiple interfaces are not viable size wise. X/K/Ubuntu follow the same development cycle too so really the underlying sub structure is the same.

As it happens, Xubuntu is quite Gnome heavy anyway, certainly in comparison to other distributions.
