### Post by MacDuff on 2007-07-23
Oh you people don't realize how you have made my day!   Perhaps I am becoming a Penguinista.  

Actually there is a way of moving the panes (but I could not find a way of dragging them with the mouse unless I was using Gnome Commander)

For those who don't know the secret of moving the panes here it is:
1. Right click on a BLANK part of the pane.  My problem was that when the pane moved to the side, all the icons were jambed together and there was no blank space on which to click.  I had to delete some icons to create a blank spot.
2. If you have clicked on a blank spot you should see a menu appear.  Click [Properties]
3. Under the "General" tab you will see an input box called "Orientation"
4. Click on [Orientation] and select top, bottom,  left or right.
Voila the pane moves to it appointed place.
