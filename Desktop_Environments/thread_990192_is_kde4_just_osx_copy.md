### Post by Skripka on 2008-11-22
On the surface it could be viewed that way.  Many ideas are cross platform--as they are good or great ideas.  

Vista stole the KBFX start menu interface, for instance.  

Vista also has widgets a la KDE4 or Mac.  KDE4 permits Mac widgets--as there are LOTS and LOTS of mac Dashboard widgets--and only 15 or so native Plasma Widgets (to my knowledge).  

Mac Finder is a primitive tool, Dolphin is much superior in most respects.  

Mac stole the roots of its OS from BSD.



There is lots of "borrowing" going on, and finding the root of a good idea is a troublesome endeavour at best.  I'd wager most of the polished ideas on Mac came from Linux first.  I cannot prove that, but that is my hunch.
