### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-09-12
It is the various **global-menu** packages that put the window menus in the panel, not very useful for small non-maximised windows.  You can uninstall the global-menu packages, I think, or just remove them from the autostart programs, and you will be back to what gnome used to be like.

I am note sure from your description which way you want to go, as I don't use unity at all, but play around with those suggestions above and I'm sure you'll get things as you want.

### Post by vasa1 on 2011-09-19
Try turning off the two "Ubuntu" extensions in Firefox (though I suspect you just need to turn of the **Global Menu Bar** one). And then restart Firefox a couple of times. If you now choose Alt, View, Toolbars, Menu bar, it will now be there at all times and not just when you hover.

I don't like doing that because the menu bar hogs a whole row. With the way it's set up when the two "Ubuntu" extensions are enabled, there's an additional row of real estate freed up. So I keep both the "Ubuntu" extensions enabled.
