### Post by woodrobin on 2006-01-04
Scribus is an Open Source desktop publishing program.  I find it capable of doing most, if not all, of the things I would use InDesign for.  Its internal ability to modify graphics is not equivalent to InDesign's inclusion of some of Photoshop's functionality,  but the GIMP is so far superior to either, IMO, that the "hassle" of using it to change the image before flowing it into the page is negligible.

Scribus is available in the main Ubuntu repository in Dapper, and I believe in Breezy as well.  If you want to have scribus-template (a selection of additional templates, naturally :) ), you'll need to have Universe enabled in your repositories, though.

Here's a link to their [website]("http://www.scribus.org.uk/index.php") and their [screenshots gallery]("http://scribus.sourceforge.net/gallery/") if you want to check them out before installing it.

It does seem to imply that, on rereading.  That's not quite what I meant to express, though.  I use InDesign at my job, and the level of Photoshop-ish elements included in InDesign is what I regard as inferior to the GIMP's capabilities.  I get far better-looking results, IMO, from prepping images with GIMP and including them in documents than I do working with the image resizing tools in InDesign, for example.
