### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-10-21
You should blame the people at Gnome.org. They have stopped developing the usual version of Gnome (now called Gnome 2) and have switched to something called Gnome 3. Ubuntu like other major distros has had to switch to basing the distribution on Gnome 3.

At the moment Gnome 3 has limitations. This is the reason for the lack options that were available in earlier versions of Ubuntu. A lot of effort has gone into putting Ubuntu on top of Gnome 3.

As Gnome 3 develops and as Unity is developed things will no doubt improve. As someone in another thread said, It took years for Gnome 2 to get where it it today. Allow some time for Gnome 3 to develop.

You might like to read this article on the Pros and Cons of Gnome 3

[http://itmanagement.earthweb.com/osrc/article.php/3931801/GNOME-3-Seven-Pros-and-Cons.htm]("http://itmanagement.earthweb.com/osrc/article.php/3931801/GNOME-3-Seven-Pros-and-Cons.htm")

And there in effect improvements. In system Setting under Network you will find that the wireless connection now has an option to put the wireless adapter in Access Point mode. Did not have that before.

Regards.
