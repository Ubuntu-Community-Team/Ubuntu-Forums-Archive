### Post by Miguel on 2005-03-15
In short: yes. I could send you a screenshot of mi laptop.

Longer answer:

First of all, the refresh rate you see is related to your current graphic card and monitor, nothing to do with XFree's or XOrg's capabilities. It is worked out from some numbers (Horizontal synchronization and Vertical refresh) that appear on a file (AFAIK, I'm also quite a newbie).

And about that resolution: my Dell Inspiron 8600 also has a 15,4 wide screen, with your resolution (1280x800). I'm running it without problems at that resolution. I would tell you my refresh rate, but the ATI drivers for my card touch a OpenGL lib, making the resolution change dialog unusable. 

BTW: Wide screens are beautiful. I wouldn't buy a laptop with 4:3 ratio after trying it. You might have a little trouble finding wallpapers, but a 1280x960 one centered will work allright. There are also some nice wide wallpapers at [www.deviantart.com](www.deviantart.com)
