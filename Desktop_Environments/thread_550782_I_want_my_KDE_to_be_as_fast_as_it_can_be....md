### Post by hikaricore on 2007-09-14
* Go through the menus (i hate kde for this) and disable all the animations.  This helps alot.
If you're a true KDE user you know what I mean, they're (animation options) in like 5 different places throughout the settings.

* Keep your menu light or add submenus.
Having 25-30 items in 1 menu is a definate slowdown even on newer systems while using KDE, sort them into submenus or remove un-needed applications from your menus to keep it snappy.

* Disable desktop icons.  1 less thing for KDE to refresh.

* Keep your applets few or nill.  Using 10 different applets on your Kicker bar on a slow system will only add to your frustration.  Keep them to what you like/need and don't run alot of bloat there.

* Dolphin File Manager.  It's a much quicker browser for local files than Konqueror.  Don't get me wrong I love Konqueror, but it's too much for standard file browsing.

I'm running Kubuntu on a file/(local)webserver at my work right now with a similar setup to what I described.
This system is running well considering it's a dual 333mhz /w 512mb of ram.  >.<   I think you'll be fine.

Hope this helps.

--Aaron
