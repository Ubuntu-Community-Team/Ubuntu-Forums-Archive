### Post by JELaVallee on 2005-09-29
idn,

I'm using a 20Gb Creative Nomad Zen 2.0 (USB 2.0) with Ubuntu.  The installation process is tricky, but I have successfully gotten it to work with a custom build (not hard at all) of libnjb2.2 for the plug-n-play driver support and Neutrino for the actual management of the files on the player while it is plugged in (good for moving files too/from the player and your computer).  GNomad2 is another good program for managing player files.  

To note: Neither Neutrino or GNomad2 have a syncronizing function... that is, you can manually transfer to the device or from the device, but there's no process for having maintaining identical sync between your device and filesystem.

I'm actually seriously considering upgrading to an iRiver H10 20Gb model because it is supposedly seen and mounted by the computer as an external hard drive.  No special drivers needed and you can literally drag-and-drop MP3 and playlist files from your local filesystem directly into the player.  I'd definitely recommend that for best ease-of-use (pluse the H10 has a picture browser for viewing thumbnails of photos on the device).   

According to Tom's Hardware, the iRiver H10 can be treated as a USB hard drive... but I've seen mixed commentary on the reality of such.  Does anyone have first hand experience with the H10 on Linux?

And for that matter, is there a recommendation for an hard drive-based MP3 player that just mounts as a USB hard drive?

cheers,
Etienne
