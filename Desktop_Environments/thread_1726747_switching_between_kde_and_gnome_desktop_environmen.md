### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-11
Gnome and KDE can live side by side without conflicting. 

Installing "kubuntu-desktop" will only change the boot screen and nothing in Gnome itself. Here is a tutorial about different environments.

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php)

You will lose your Gnome settings only if you decide to remove Gnome. If you keep both KDE and Gnome, then you will have both.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-04-11
Your gnome customised settings should not be changed in any way by logging into kde, though your menu-bar may become rather larger and difficult to navigate as all the kde applications will be added to it.  Also in previous versions of kde 3.5.x, which is the last time I used it, desktop icons could be added rather than the widgets, which I am not sure about, and those icons would then also appear on your gnome desktop and vice-versa.  Your theme will not be affected, and the same will apply to the kde desktop settings, which should not be affected by your gnome settings.

None of your data files will be affected in any way.
