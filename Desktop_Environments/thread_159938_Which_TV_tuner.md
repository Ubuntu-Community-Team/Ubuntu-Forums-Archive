### Post by Prefader on 2006-04-14
I'll second that.  Getting the drivers and firmware sorted can be a little bit annoying the first time through, but once it's done the cards listed work great, particularly on older hardware, since these cards all have hardware MPEG-2 encoders.  The 350 also has a hardware MPEG-2 decoder and a TV out, but I believe it lacks support for things like forward scan and closed captioning through it.  A search for "ivtv" should return lots of good information for getting the card installed and configured.

I've been able to get good picture quality with the 350, although it did take quite a bit of fine-tuning to get every station looking as good as possible.  This was on an old HP with a celeron 667 with 768MB of ram and a VIA chipset (can't remember which one, though).  I did not use the tv out on the 350, but instead used the onboard video.  I also used the onboard sound, and so far everything has been peachy.  There's a lot of really good information about getting these cards working well on most of the sites relating to MythTV, too.
