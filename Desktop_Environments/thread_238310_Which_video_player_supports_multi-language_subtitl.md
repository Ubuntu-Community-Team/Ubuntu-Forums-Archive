### Post by cptnapalm on 2006-08-17
The best format that I know of to rip video to, if you want to have subtitles which can be turned on or off, is mkv format.  It has less overhead than avi and has some good additional options.  I use a script called iso2mkv, which calls up mencoder, normalize and other things to rip the DVDs I own to my laptop.  (Somedays, I just need to see the Road Warrior or Conan the Barbarian).

Just FYI.
