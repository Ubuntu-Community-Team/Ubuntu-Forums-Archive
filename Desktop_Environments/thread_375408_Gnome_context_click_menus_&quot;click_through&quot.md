### Post by Robvdl on 2007-03-03
Does this affect anyone else?

If I right click on my desktop or in a Nautilus window in Gnome to bring up the context menu, then I can either left click or right click a menu item.

The problem with this however, is often if I right click to bring up the conext menu, the click "goes through" and automatically clicks the top menu item too, which in many situations is "create new folder".

This is quite annoying, I don't know how many times a day I have to delete accidentally created "New folders".

However this does not happen if I tap my right mouse button to bring up to context menu "very, very lightly". If I tapped the button for a millisecond too long, it already clicks through to the top menu item and creates a new folder.

Maybe the timeout between bringing up the popup menu and accepting the second click is just too short. Or maybe, a right click on a menu item should not even be allowed, just a left click, which is Windows-like behaviour I guess.

Anyway, is there anything that can be done about this? Can this behaviour be disabled? Can the timeout be increased? Is it even a "bug"?

This "bug" if it is one, has happened to be in Dapper, Edgy, Feisty.
