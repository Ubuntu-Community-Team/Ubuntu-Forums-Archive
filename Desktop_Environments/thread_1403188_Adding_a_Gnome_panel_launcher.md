### Post by jrssystemsnet on 2010-02-10
> **stumbleUpon said:**
> Right click on the panel. Select Add new items. And then select the Gnome panel (or whatever application you want) from the list that appears.

Actually it's a little more involved than that.  First you've got to right-click and unlock the separator to the right of the CoF icon, then right-click and move the separator to the right to get some empty space, then right-click and remove the existing CoF icon from the Panel... THEN you can right-click the empty space where it used to be, and Add to Panel either Gnome Main Menu (for all menus expanding from a plain CoF icon) or Menu Bar (for desktop-style separate menus labeled Applications, Places, System).

See [http://ubuntuwiki.net/index.php/Ubuntu_Netbook_Remix,_launcher_not_running](http://ubuntuwiki.net/index.php/Ubuntu_Netbook_Remix,_launcher_not_running) for screenshots.  (This problem vexed me GREATLY when I encountered some situations where the launcher itself was not running for one reason or another... especially since I didn't know about alt-F1.)
