### Post by chris00 on 2007-10-19
So, Gutsy looks ok, but with a few annoying problems... 

Most notably, Firefox fonts look awful compared to Feisty. If we look at this page we can see some of the differences:

[http://polishlinux.org/linux/ubuntu/ubuntu-710-gutsy-gibbon-critical-review/](http://polishlinux.org/linux/ubuntu/ubuntu-710-gutsy-gibbon-critical-review/)

Bizarrely, the author prefers the Gutsy fonts. Just look at the letters "KDE" on the Gutsy screenshot and you can easily see the spacing problems.

Looking at a google search page also reveals horrible large rounded blue fonts for the search result titles.

Finally, the terminal font layout when using monospace 8 also seems to have changed so that the characters are sometimes touching each other - also ugly looking, especially on the wider characters.

And why do I have to go via "Change Desktop Background" to change the fonts??

When I first tried Compiz using gnome GL Desktop, it appeared enabled, allowed me to change all the settings and then just didn't work and turned itself off next time I opened GL Desktop. It turned out I didn't have xgl installed, but it took me a while to figure this out. This type of thing really needs sorting out.

Eclipse was rendered useless by the installation of gij - again, fixable, but annoying that I had to spend half an hour trying to work out what was wrong.

And for some reason, Firefox seems to crash quite a lot now whereas in Feisty it was always rock solid.

So, not a bad release, some nice touches - but _please_ fix those fonts!!!
