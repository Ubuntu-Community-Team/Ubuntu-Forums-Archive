### Post by siminone on 2006-09-24
I put my regularly used applications as icons on the taskbar. These  can be dragged from the Gnome application bar.

You can also set a seperate task bar for this. You can also put these icons where you want on the taskbar. What I do is put all my ordinary applications on one side and system applications ie System monitor, synaptic on the other side for ease of use.

### Post by fragos on 2006-09-24
Icons on the desktop are a good idea, as is Gnome.  Make sure anything they download goes on the Desktop so they won't have to find it.  I've even created text files of directions for some tasks that I also place on the desktop.  They have one major advantage over new users -- they're not Windows brainwashed and expect to have to learn rather than complain about how things work.  Email them regularly with tid bits of information and to give them a comfortable forum on which to ask questions.
