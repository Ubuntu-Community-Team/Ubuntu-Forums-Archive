### Post by skymt on 2006-10-02
ScummVM supports a lot of games, but most of them are still under copyright. The two in the repositories are there because the developer made them freely available and redistributable.

[quote=scummvm.org]ScummVM lets you run these adventures: Adventure Soft's Simon the Sorcerer 1 and 2; Revolution's Beneath A Steel Sky, Broken Sword 1 and Broken Sword 2; Flight of the Amazon Queen; Wyrmkeep's Inherit the Earth; Coktel Vision's Gobliiins; Westwood Studios' The Legend of Kyrandia and games based on LucasArts' SCUMM (Script Creation Utility for Maniac Mansion) system. SCUMM is used for many games, including Monkey Island, Day of the Tentacle, Sam and Max and more. Compatibility with supported games is continually improving, so check back often.[/quote]

### Post by CatKiller on 2006-10-03
> **ardchoille said:**
> There are two games in the repos called: Flight of the amazon queen and Beneath a steel sky. I installed both, they look cool and I like them. I understand that they use scummvm as their engine.

Are there any more games I can get for the scummvm engine? If so, where can I download them?

As has been pointed out already, the only reason these games are in the repositories is because of the generosity of the developers. Those games had already been released commercially previously, but were made available for free out of respect for what the ScummVM project were doing. As such, it would be the height of bad manners to download any other games.

Instead, you should buy some games that work through ScummVM. There are [lots of them]("http://scummvm.org/compatibility.php"), and they are mostly cheap since Adventure games have fallen out of fashion. You can even get compilations of lots of them in one go.

> Are there any more "cartoon" type games like these?

I would start with either [Broken Sword]("http://www.revolution.co.uk/_display.php?id=14") or [Broken Sword II]("http://www.revolution.co.uk/_display.php?id=15"), since they are made by Revolution Software, the ones that made Beneath A Steel Sky available that you enjoyed. You might want to drop them a line thanking them for doing this. The first two Broken Sword games are cartoony in style and very good. I haven't played the later ones.

Otherwise, you could try some games by LucasArts - they made lots of adventure games using the SCUMM engine. It was this engine that inspired the ScummVM project. If you like cartoon-style graphics, you could try Sam & Max, Day of The Tentacle or Full Throttle. These are all good.

### Post by DarkN00b on 2006-10-04
[Adventure Game Studio]("http://www.adventuregamestudio.co.uk/") is a Windows based game creation system for the type of games you are looking for.  There is a Linux version of the engine available for download that lets you play (but not create) games made with AGS.  There are many independent games available for download, some of them are very good to outstanding.  You might give that a look.
