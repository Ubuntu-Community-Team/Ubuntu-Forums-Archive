### Post by ben2talk on 2008-05-01
> **c0rrupt said:**
> Ugly!  SLAB looks much nicer.

Slab is okay, good in the way that it opens a window with all the items listed - but GIMME wipes the floor with SLAB- it's nicely endowed with preferences settings and you can type into a text box to use it as a 'finder'. You can choose how many tabs are on your menu (good for mostly just 'programs'. It basically reproduces the gnome menu's in a more useable fashion, but it's a bit 'heavier'. The standard setup is hard to beat... ALT+... F1 and arrows to play in the menu when you don't know what you want. F2 when you do know, and F3 for finding files.

Experts prefer a lighter approach, and this to me seems perfect. Gnome-do is very nice, but not a replacement.

Incidentally, aren't experts are the people behind the matrix, and would not be bothered if they had any menu, or even desktop, open at all? Whilst my friends were playing with their computers twenty years ago, I was working for a few days writing a machine code program to do something very basic and simple in an efficient manner. They laughed, but this is where the experts are at. This is where the power is! The slab is just there for the posers (and posing is better with gnome-do --glass

Searching menu's isn't faster - it's archaic. If you have any expertise and know what you want, you'll use 'gnome-do' and simply hit (super+space) and type the first letters of an app, then hit enter. It's the fastest and requires no mouse whatsoever. Similarly the ALT+F2 is excellent. Isn't this what the expert would wish to do? As a non expert, I'll either do this, or I'll look at my notes and launch from a terminal. As a last resort I'll look in menu's which might, or might not contain the item I need.

The more I use Ubuntu, the more I shy away from complicated parts and opt for simplicity. I like an empty screen, and when I have a purpose, ALT+F2 is the quickest and most efficient method (gnome-do does a bit more and has better eye candy maybe... but ALT+F2 is ultra cool.

Slab isn't for advanced users, it's for people who never remember 

'<ALT+F2> centre <tab, tab, tab, tab> <Enter>'. It takes perhaps 5 seconds, no menu, no mouse, no hassle. 

Let's all stop playing games and just admit that we love windows, so we want those horrible menu's which Apple and Windows are trying to leave behind in favour of 'finder' style launchers.

It's as close as you'll get to talking to your com and having your com trying to help you find what you want.
