### Post by apollyonis on 2005-05-19
[QUOTE=valnar]I wondered what the "save desktop" button was.  What else does it do?

Robert[/QUOTE]

To my knowledge, it just saves what programs you were running prior to logging out. I.E. You had Nautilus file browser open to My Documents, the next time you log back in that same folder will be open. I know there are a few programs which can't do this like Firefox and when you log out and save current setup, it will even give you a message along the lines that whatever program can't be saved on exit. That's about the extent of what it does. You can have the sessions changed to automatically do it, but personally I prefer to have a choice since sometimes a program starts and if its pointing to something that doesn't exist (I've erase a document) then when it comes back up, I have to click an error message out.
