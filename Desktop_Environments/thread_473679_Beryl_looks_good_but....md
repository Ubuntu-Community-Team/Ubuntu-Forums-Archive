### Post by Bagboy23 on 2007-06-14
If you are still working with your computer and working with old habbits, then you'll never realise how productive Beryl can be. I work in the management information domain. Here is how Bery is very productive for me:

1. The cube lets me allocate an application or a document for each viewport. A simple mouse swipe or keyboard action takes me to each cube. In Windows XP, there is only one work space and you have to juggle through them to find the right application. Overall, it's cleaner, nicer and more manageable. 

2. The tile plugin easily allows me to cascade documents or windows without manually having to do them. 

3. Transparency plugin allows me to quickly fade out one application whilst seeing whats underneath. Good for reference lookup.

4. Scale plugin helps me to manage the many documents I have with ease especially after assigning screen edges to detect the action. This includes show desktop plugin.

5. The saturate and brightness adjust is an absolute must for those who are working on a document and want to fade things in the back for less of a distraction.

There are just far too many to list that most of us who take Bery/Compiz for granted overlook but when it's gone like the white-cube-of-death days; then we all really begin to appreciate what we had.
