### Post by cmost on 2006-10-05
Even though you have a 64 bit processor, a 64 bit operating system is not the best place for someone whose's last love affair with a UNIX style OS was SUSE 7.0.  It is well known that much more fiddling is required with 64 bit versions than their 32 bit counterparts, and this is true of all operating systems designed for 64 bit processors, including Windows XP 64.  I just recently purchased a speedy AMD Athlon 64 myself, but I know i'm not quite ready to play with a 64 bit version of Ubuntu...yet.  I would suggest that you download the 32 bit version of Ubuntu and master it before attempting to transition to the 64 bit version.  Your experience will be much gentler and you'll have a much better time of it.  These forums are here to help you.  Good luck!
