### Post by Anzan on 2008-05-02
I primarily use GNOME but one of the very many insanely great things about Linux are the various window managers available.

I've tried KDE, don't like it and so haven't installed it on this fresh Hardy install, but use it in the full desktop Xandros install on an Eee. I've tried KDE 4 on a Live CD and don't like that either.

I like Xfce and will set it up on the Eee and will recommend it for older boxes.

I've tried Openbox and Fluxbox and like them but they are time consuming to configure.

Ratpoison is fun in its own way, so I'm slowly learning how to get around. With xnest I can even start another window manager within it.

But another window manager that I enjoy but don't see much mention of is Window Maker. This is based on GNUstep (which was based on the NeXT GUI) and has a weird early 90s feel. It is highly configurable but simple to use.

Is anyone else using it and do they have any advice for me?

### Post by Anzan on 2008-05-02
Interestingly, it is still big in Brazil.

[http://br-linux.org/brazil/?p=11]("http://br-linux.org/brazil/?p=11")

> Alfredo Kojima, the brazilian hacker that started WindowMaker development when he was still in university and wanted to offer an alternative to AfterStep, recently had a short interview (in portuguese only) with BR-Linux about the current status and plans for the project.

According to media-shy Kojima, now a GUI developer for MySQL AB and formerly a developer for brazilian distro Conectiva (now part of Mandriva) - where he wrote the first version of the popular Synaptic Package Manager, it’s hard to get motivated to maintain WindowMaker’s code in his scarce idle time, but he is still very attached to the project. “I got my first job, at Conectiva, thanks to WindowMaker, and the people at my current job at MySQL also knew the project and my part on it”, he tells.

Asked if the project is in official hiatus or even closed, Kojima gives some hope to those who still prefer the beautiful GUI of his brainchild: “The intention is to keep working on it when possible”, he says, but both him and Dan Pascu (the other prominent historical wmaker developer) are deeply involved in other technology projects for now.

Far from it. Reality is that it has been in existence for a very very long time. Most bugs that were found were fixed over the years. It is in fact, a very mature product now. I use it both on my Laptop and Desktop at work (both of them now running Debian Lenny). I first used WindowMaker in 1998. I loved it instantly. Later, as the GNOME project picked up some popularity, I got distracted and forgot about WindowMaker. Now, after 10 years of suffering with GNOME, I am back to my old love, WindowMaker. I doubt I will abandon it this time. In my opinion, it is the most productive GUI environment. Just my 0.02$.
