### Post by WiseElben on 2006-12-26
That computer uses an onboard graphics card, which means you might not get the performance you want, if any at all. That computer is actually overpriced, at least in my opinion. Plus, it's from TigerDirect, a place I woldn't trust as I've heard many horror stories about wrong/broken things shipped. I would think about building your own computer. I built my first one without much knowledge, and it's still working fine today. I recently build a computer for a friend's of mine for $500, which came with an AMD 64 3500+, an nVidia GeForce 6600 GT, a gig of RAM, DVD burner, and an 80 gig HD. 

If you still prefer to not build your own computer, at least consider buying a good (yet still cheap) graphics card such as the GeForce 6600 GT or maybe a lower-end 7000 series. 

Good luck,

### Post by Tanker Bob on 2006-12-27
I agree about TigerDirect.  They specialize in selling yesterday's products at yesterday's prices--i.e., more than they are worth today.  I buy most of my hardware from newegg.com.

I've build most of my boxes over the years, and will be building a new one in the next couple of months so I've been keeping track of hardware lately.  There's lots of good hardware out there for not much money.  With Ubuntu and open source, you don't need all the software bundles that OEMs like Dell and Gateway use to drive up their prices.  In the very old days, building a computer was a bit of an art due to compatibility issues, but today things work much better together.

Personal opinion:  You're better off buying a good graphics card (as was recommended by the wise Elben) and a lot of RAM (at least 1GB for Ubuntu) than an ultra fast CPU.  You'll see much more performance from a good graphics card and RAM than from an incrementally faster CPU.  AMD64 CPUs are particularly well priced at the moment.  Entry-level Intel Dual Cores aren't bad either.  Check out tomshardware.com for comparison performance data.
