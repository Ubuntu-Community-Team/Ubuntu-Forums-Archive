### Post by kadath on 2007-05-13
Generally I like GNOME but there are two things have caused me problems multiple times: lack of a thumbnail view in the open/save dialog, and problems with copy/pasting.

I upload images to several websites on a constant basis, and my image collection is huge. Most of their filenames are numeric, therefore a thumbnail view is absolutely necessary to navigate through my images when I'm uploading them via web forms.

Are the GNOME devs ever going to offer a thumbnail view in the open/save dialog, or is this going to be the thing that drives me to a completely different DE?

It's also highly annoying to copy a few lines from a file and close the file, only to find that the text I copied has disappeared. Is there a way to fix this, or is it just a stupid mistake on GNOME's part?

### Post by hartz on 2007-05-14
I think the clipboard problem has got mostly to do with applications not properly integrating with your window manager.  I've seen this too, and it is usually with older software or programs written for another DE other than the one you are actually running.  It may also have to do with poorly written software, e.g. programs not playing by the rules of the X-windowing system.

I don't think you should worry about switching to another DE.  In fact, I would encourage you to try out a few and switch to the one that makes you most productive.  Currently I am switching between XFCE and Enlightenment.  I love both, can't make up my mind about which one is nicer.  Oh, I do dislike some DE/window managers, but I have not investigated enough time or energy into researching and customizing any one seriously enough to feel locked in.

My opinion:  Install a couple of window managers, and use whatever works for you.
