### Post by tig4 on 2005-11-09
I understand what he is saying. I actually felt the same way the first time I installed a Linux Distro.

The best thing I can recommend to do is go into your theme editor, size down the window borders, choose a squared minimalistic theme, with small borders.

Then go to each of your menu bars (top and bottom if you have them) and bring the size down to maybe 24 to 25 pixels high.

Then go to your fonts menu and lower your font size down to 8 and a small styled font. Finally I'd say take a look at your font anti-aliasing and play around with the settings that you like.

After doing this you should see a vast improvement in your viewable space. 

:) Hope this helps.

Tig

### Post by 23meg on 2005-11-09
Right, using a theme with small paddings between widgets will help. I recommend themes that use the latest clearlooks engine in this regard. Plus, some fonts will render better and make more economical use of space with particular DPI settings; for example I find that Tahoma looks best at a size of 8 in 96 DPI. Play around with font sizes and DPI settings and you'll get what I mean.
