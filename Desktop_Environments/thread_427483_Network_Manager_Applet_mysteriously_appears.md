### Post by novabox on 2007-04-29
Got a bit of an odd issue happening on my Ubuntu (feisty fawn) desktop.  This morning I just noticed that the "NetworkManager Applet" is now on the panel at the top of my screen, between the Gaim applet and the Volume Control applet. (Note: I am *not* referring to the "network activity applet", which lights up to show incoming/outgoing network traffic).

The odd part is I never added this applet to my panel, and nothing changed on my system to make it show up.  It was just suddenly there this morning, and it wasn't there last night.  What's odder is there doesn't seem to be a way to remove it from the panel.  I only have one network device on this system (wired ethernet), so it's not like my system wanted to give me a choice among network connections.  

This obviously isn't a critical problem, I'm just a little perplexed that this applet magically showed up without any obvious reason, and I can't get it to go away.

### Post by pebo on 2007-04-29
System->Preferences->Sessions and untick Network Manager should get rid of it next time you log in
