Sancho,

As much as I think your idea is great (and wholeheartedly agree with and support anyone willing to spread the word about Linux)...

...You mentioned that your main target market is elderly people who will mainly want to surf the Web, send and receive e-mail, etc...

The quick start/user manual that someone else on the thread mentioned is an excellent start... However, you might want to keep the following in mind:

- If your customers are new to computers, who will be supporting them once you've sold them the boxes? You? What happens if you're not available and a customer calls their (grand)son/(grand)daughter/friend/etc. for support? Since most people use and are familiar with Windows, their reaction will most likely be "Ku what?"...
- If your customers only plan to do specific things with their machines, you might consider locking down the machines such that whatever they want to do is all they can do. Little old ladies aren't going to a) know what something like the shell is and b) aren't going to care (nor should they). This should reduce your support calls.
- Taking the previous idea one step further, a simple configuration application that pops up when one of these machines is started for the first time (or can be launched at any time thereafter) and allows users to enable or disable features might be a nice touch... i.e...
   I want to use my computer to:
   - Surf the Web
   - Send and receive e-mail
   - ...
Selecting the first option would add Firefox to the menus, etc. 
 
Remember... You're competing with the likes of Dell who sell good cheap machines  with Windows pre-installed and offer a 24x7 support infrastructure. So from a marketing perspective, you need to answer the question "Why would I want to buy a Linux machine from you, foresaking a larger vendor's support infrastructure and saving what may end up being just a couple of hundred dollars. To sell Linux to the masses (A Good ThingTM), you need to make it both cheaper and easier than the laternatives.

Apologies if I've overthought the whole thing... :-) Best of luck...
