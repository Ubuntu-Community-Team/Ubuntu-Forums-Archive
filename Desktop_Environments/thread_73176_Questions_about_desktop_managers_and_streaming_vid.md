Well, you think wrong...

The legality depends on just What you are "ripping".  If you own the media (eg a CD) then you probably have the right to shift it to a different media for your own personal use.  So making a tape of a vinyl LP to play in the car is probably acceptable.

If you don't own the media but the material is in the Public domain (older books for example) or has been released under a non restrictive licence (creative commons for example) then you are within your rights to copy it... (assuming the licence grants you that right).  So Project Gutenberg can release the works of Shakespeare and Cory Doctorow can release his own books.

If it is a copyrighted work, not released under a non-restrictive licence, and you do not own that work then it is a breach of copyright to copy it.  So downloading the latest boy band/teen slut hit from limewire when you didn't purchase it is not allowed.

Because of this minefield the tools to allow you to do this are available and generally legal per se.  (The DMCA may make some of those tools illegal because they are designed to circumvent copy protection mechanisms but that is a different point)...

I am not a lawyer, none of this is legal advice, this is just my understanding of the nightmare that copyright has evolved into.
