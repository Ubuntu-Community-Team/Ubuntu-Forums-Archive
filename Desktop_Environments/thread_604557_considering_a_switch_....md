### Post by seshomaru samma on 2007-11-06
I'm no guru, but I think that the key to a fast desktop is fast applications.
I personally cannot stand fluxbox, if I had to go with a light environment I would go for icewm. You can find some very good tutorials for both Fluxbox and IceWM in the Debian Forum by a guy called Lou. ([here]("http://forums.debian.net/viewtopic.php?t=5450") is the IceWM one).
Some people like enlightenment but I could never figure it out, you can try that as well.
I think there are two things to consider:
1. Heavy applications will still be heavy. I think you are much better running abiword in Gnome than OO in fluxbox, 
2. The light desktop environment do not have much in the way of GUI configuration. Changing a theme or hooking up wireless can be very time-consuming.
I think that the best compromise will be XFCE. But if you have a laptop I would personaly keep some gnome aps like teh Gnome-network-manager.
You can apt-get as many desktop environments and check them out yourself. Just logout and choose a different session at the GDM screen.
just my 2 cents
