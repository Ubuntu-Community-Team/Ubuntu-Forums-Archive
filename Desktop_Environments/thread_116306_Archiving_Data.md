You will have issues with any media used for archiving - if it isn't the gradual degradation of the actual media it is finding new hardware (and software) capable of working with your old archives.

The only really effective way of maintaining access to your archived data is to regularly (in archive time - which means every few years) move your data from whatever "old" storage format it is on to the latest format.

Currently this may mean taking data off old DAT tapes/CDs and burning it onto DVDs, because one day you may not be able to get a DAT drive to actually work with your new hardware (a SCSI port?, where do I get one of those ancient things?......) or those old CDs may have well deteriorated to the point where the error correction just doesn't work no more.....

In 2 or 3 years, it may be moving data from those old-fashioned DVDs to whatever is the current storage technology at that time.

As far as "what's supported", that is a moving target and there is little doubt that some things supported now won't be in the future (or the Linux code will become bloated with unused features), so don't let anything get too far out of date (or keep old versions of Linux - somewhere - in the hope that you can access old stuff when you need it).
