### Post by everything on 2005-08-24
PNGs support alpha-blending, that means complete or partial transparency of parts of the image. If you like to modify your photos with Gimp, you can for instance make a sky totally transaparent and when used as a desktop wallpaper the photograph will have a sky of the color/colors you configured for the desktop in Gnome.
Apart from that there's no reason to convert a JPG to PNG.
