### Post by blackbird34 on 2013-04-08
Dell does a remixed "developer edition" of its XPS ultrabook line, looks like what you might want:
[https://www.dell.com/us/business/p/xps-13-linux/pd.aspx?~ck=mn](https://www.dell.com/us/business/p/xps-13-linux/pd.aspx?~ck=mn)
"Developer Edition" won't stop you from using day-to-day desktop Ubuntu. It's a bit expensive though.

Otherwise a couple of vendors do Ubuntu:
System76 do a 14" laptop that looks very good, although it's probably a bit heavier with a bit less battery life: [https://www.system76.com/laptops/model/lemu4](https://www.system76.com/laptops/model/lemu4)
They've been selling Ubuntu laptops with a very good reputation for years. 

Zareason do good looking stuff too, including one that is explicitly called an ultrabook: [https://zareason.com/shop/Laptops/](https://zareason.com/shop/Laptops/)

Out of the three I'd go for System76, but i'm not going to be getting a new computer for a while. In the case of Dell and System76 there are corresponding sections in this forum where you can get more information :D

Canonical also does certification for computers to be guaranteed to work with Ubuntu: [http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/desktop/](http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/desktop/)
