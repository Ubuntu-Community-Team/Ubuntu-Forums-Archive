### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-08-28
There is no such thing. As example, Android phone makers try to lock their phone to their own OS, but hackers succeed time after time to break through the protection and to load custom ROMs (btw, it's called ROM because it's written to what should be a Read-Only-Memory).

So if the manufacturers can't do it, I don't expect you can do it.

But thus far, there are no known hack that give root access to non-root users (be sure, if this was possible, than a lot of servers would get hacked at once, including banking servers). So if you don't work as root, the OS is safe.
