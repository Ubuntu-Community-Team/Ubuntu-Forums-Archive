### Post by ndrwgn on 2007-01-20
I don't know if this helps, but I had this same problem. Coming over from windows, its still hard for me to get used to the whole super user thing. I tried, and tried, but I could not connect.  Then I got the sneaking suspicion that I didn't have "permission".  You see most native linuxers take it for granted that they use the command line (at which point they can identify themselves as sudo), and don't understand that some people are accessing gaim, and everything else for that matter, from the drop down menus.  

Here's what I just did:

alt+f2
enter "gksudo nautilus" minus the ""
browse to gaim:  /usr/bin/gaim, and double click.
now enter your info and presto changeo it works! (at least for me). 

Hope that this helped somebody.
