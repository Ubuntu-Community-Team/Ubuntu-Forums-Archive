### Post by Peti29 on 2007-02-28
Someone more experienced / deeply into Ubuntu should answer this. Until then, my view:
First of all I think that'd be a good idea, however there are some (pretty serious) obstacles. Beryl is still in beta phase, so it's not claimed to be stable yet.
On some (Ati) videocards it runs smoothly only with proprietary drivers and those cannot be distributed with Ubuntu out of the box for whatever legal issues.
In some cases (I believe that's when proprietary drivers are used but I'm unsure) an XGL session is required for Beryl to run accelerated.

### Post by gh0st on 2007-02-28
I think there's a really good case for this to be included in Fiesty. I am sick of hearing people tell me how great the visuals are with Aero in Vista. Beryl is better in my opinion. I was using Compiz until a couple of weeks ago because I was told it was more stable and usable than Beryl. What a load of rubbish that turned out to be, when I installed Beryl I found it easier to use and just better all round.

There are some concerns that Beryl won't work with a lot of 3D cards and this will cause install problems if it's included in the OS. Why can't the installer have an option to enable 3D desktop when you setup your system and just grey out the option if your card isn't up to it?? I think that would be the best solution. Most people are just installing propriatary drivers and 3D desktop straight away anyway, why not give them an option to do this at install. Don't make it compulsory but we should have the option. I tested Mandriva 2007 a while ago and while I wouldn't choose it over Ubuntu in a million years, one of the nice things was the option to enable 3D desktop at install.

I'm sure others will disagree but that's just what I think. We should be showing people what a great OS Ubuntu is and 3D desktops adds a wow factor for most new users. Just look at the MS slogan for Vista "The WOW starts NOW" ......yeah it started over a year ago on Ubuntu :)
