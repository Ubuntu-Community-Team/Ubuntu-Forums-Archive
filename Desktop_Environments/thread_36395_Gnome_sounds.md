### Post by Xian on 2005-05-23
You should be able to download what you want from Gnome-Look's [System Sounds](http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=25&PHPSESSID=7c6ebe3cb215c6958bb5e6cb7856d373) section, place it in a directory of your choice (default is /usr/share/sounds), and associate them with events from the sound module which can be accessed from the main panel:

System > Preferences > Sound
Click on 'Sound Events' tab.

Hightlight the desired sound event with your mouse.
Click 'Browse' to associate it with the applicable .wav file.
