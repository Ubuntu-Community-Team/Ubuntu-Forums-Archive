### Post by ajgreeny on 2007-06-17
To be honest, if you have plenty of disk space, just leave things as they are and it won't make any difference at all to the speed of your system.  It can also occasionally be useful to have an alternative desktop available just in case an upgrade renders kde unusable (unlikely but not impossible). ### Post by dabl on 2007-06-18
I run both Ubuntu and Kubuntu Feisty, on the same hardware system (there are reasons, kinda, or at least there were ...).

You can use a lot of the same packages on either one -- K3B, Amarok, Synaptic, Open Office, lm-sensors, Beryl, Audacity, etc. etc.  Some of the packages work better on one than the other -- Amarok takes forever to launch in Ubuntu, compared to Kubuntu. Beryl is more rugged on Ubuntu -- if I don't manually "quit" Beryl in Kubuntu, then it sometimes won't auto-start at the next boot.  You should be able to do what you want in either 'buntu.
