### Post by Nevon on 2008-03-08
The gnome apps use the GTK toolpack, and the KDE apps use the Qt toolpack. By using gnome apps in KDE, or vice versa, the visual look won't be consistent. But you're right, it works just fine, if you don't mind that they look a little odd.

### Post by Nythain on 2008-03-08
there's really more to it than just looks
its not noticable on newer computers, but to run a kde app inside of gnome and vice versa you have to load the libs and everything for it, on top of your main de's libs

while not a big deal on a newer machine, try running superkaramba in gnome on a 1.6ghz with 512megs of DDR and a gforce fx5200... starts to make an impact then :P

but overall it boils down to snobbery and purity... the common thought is, "I use <insert de here>, why would i want to spoil it with <insert other de here> apps" :)
