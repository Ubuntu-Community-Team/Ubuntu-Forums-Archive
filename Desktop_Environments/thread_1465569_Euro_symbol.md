### Post by AFD8766 on 2010-04-29
Or "how I learned to love the third-level key."

On Jaunty Jackalope, I couldn't find the Euro symbol (€) in the character map.  Viewing by Unicode Block, all, Nimbus Roman No9 L.  Although one tends to become bleary-eyed looking at so many characters without any context, I'm pretty sure it's NOT there.  

So I dug into some threads here (see appended links).  But that's not where I found the solution.  

The solution for me was in 
System | Preferences | Keyboard | Layouts [tab] | Layout Option [button] 
and 
"Adding Eurosign to certain keys"
with
"Key to choose third level"

This explanation wasn't that clear to me, but it did point me in the right direction:
[http://library.gnome.org/users/user-guide/2.27/prefs-keyboard.html.en]("http://library.gnome.org/users/user-guide/2.27/prefs-keyboard.html.en") from GNOME Documentation Library, 8.4.1.1; Keyboard Preferences.

I chose the "e" for the Euro sign, and the windows key as the third-level key.

### Post by simosx on 2010-05-07
It depends to they keyboard layout, whether the &#8364; is there or not.
If you have the US English default layout, then you do not have the euro, nor any other interesting characters such as ñáé&#7785; and so on.

In the Keyboard/Layouts Preference dialog you can identify which layout has the &#8364; and simply use that one. If your keyboard follows the US English layout, select from the Keyboard preferences one of the variants of the US English.
