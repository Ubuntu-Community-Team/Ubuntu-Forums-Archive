I'm about to write a how-to on NVIDIA HDTV-out (there wasn't much helpful information when I started my search), but before I do that, I'd like to have a way to deal with what is bound to be a common problem for those using HDTV-out: many HDTVs (including mine) don't display every pixel of a HDTV transmission: in order to get the picture to fill their screens, they clip off significant chunks from the top and bottom or sides, and usually both...for computing, the big impact is that they trim off the menus at the edge of the screen, making it difficult or impossible to use the taskbar at the bottom and the menus at the top.

What I would most like to do, if it is possible, would be to reduce the size of the available desktop area, so that the Gnome menus come fully into view, and programs can be maximized without losing their outer edges off-screen. Changing the actual resolution put out is not an option, using YCbCr outputs means it's no longer necessary to find the right mode-line for your HDTV, but you're limited to the standard HDTV modes for component video...what I need is a way to tell Linux (or my desktop manager) to make ten or twenty pixels on each side of the screen out-of-bounds, and to keep menus, programs, icons, etc. within the visible screen area.

If that isn't possible, I'd be happy if I could at least find a way to get the menus adjusted so they would stay visible, and preferably fixed in place...I'm also open to suggestions of what desktop manager might handle the situation better than Gnome.
