### Post by iamhugeinjapan on 2006-08-01
I haven't played with SuperKaramba much because I'm mostly a Gnome guy, but it has the usual stuff like CPU/RAM monitors, media controls and weather plus other things.
[http://netdragon.sourceforge.net/ssuperkaramba.html]("http://netdragon.sourceforge.net/ssuperkaramba.html")

Opera web browser ([http://www.opera.com]("http://www.opera.com")) as of version 9 has widgets that act like mini browser windows that can be left on the desktop while Opera is open. You can see their widget site here: [http://widgets.opera.com/]("http://widgets.opera.com/")

In my experience Gnome's aDesklets and gDesklets have been a frustrating waste of time.
