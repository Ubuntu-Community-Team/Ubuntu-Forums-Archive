### Post by Mads_7 on 2008-11-14
Well all items on the GNOME bars can be freely moved, added and removed. You might have to Unlock them to move them. (That's done with a right-click)

You can also delete or add new bars and place them like you want.
Almost everything you want can be done by right-clicking and GNOME bars can be customized for most needs. Location (Top, Bottom Left, Right) Background image, Transparency and width. It should be pretty easy.

The Mac style bar, I believe you can get by using adesklets, though it's little tricky.

Apart from that. I haven't used KDE very much, but I haven't really experienced more crashes or stability issues than with GNOME. KDE4 is still pretty new, maybe switching to KDE3.5 would serve you better?
