That would be the typical recommendations.  What kind of work is being done in this business?
> 
It also needs to be very stable.. Is there a Ubuntu version for me or should I look at something else?

Stable means different things depending on context.  If you mean stable as in "bug free", most distros aim for this with varying levels of success.  If you mean "not subject to major upgrades for long periods of time", I'd recommend Debian stable or Ubuntu LTS.  Centos also seems to fit your bill, though I haven't used it personally.

Stable generally has the trade-off of "old".  If you are ok running versions that are 6 to 18 months behind the curve, "stable" distros should be fine.  If you must have the latest app versions, it can be a problem.

### Post by steveneddy on 2010-01-09
The very first thing I would do if you are going to be using Gimp is increasing the RAM to at least 1 Gig instead of 512 mb.

I would do my homework about the operating system that you use to operate your business.

As I stated before, Suse and Red Hat, not Fedora, are built for business. 

Fedora is the free version of Red Hat, OpenSuse is the free version of Suse. Why would you use an experimental version of an operating system to run your business.

If you must run Ubuntu I would use the 8.04 LTS version for it's stability. This is classified as the Enterprise version.

If it were MY business, which it is not, and I were choosing a Linux distro to use to make money with, I would use and pay for Red Hat. 

Business men who are serious about their businesses rarely use free versions of operating systems to run mission critical operations. Although Ubuntu may actually be up to the task and you seem as if you have very little Linux experience, I would pay for support from Cannonical for at least a year as you learn your way around the technical operations of a new OS.

Remember, you asked us for recommendations for an OS that you make your living with, that you pay your employees by using and you keep food on the table for your family by using this operating system. You should contact Suse and Red hat to see what they can do for you also. Call Cannonical to ask them what type of support they offer. Will they offer phone support for the Gimp?

I run my business on Ubuntu, but it's just me. Only me. I also use Windows when it is necessary. I use the software that works for the job I need to do. I only run the LTS versions (8.04) on my servers (2) and my laptop and desktop both run 8.10. I understand computers and I know why Windows gets slow and why you don't plug Windows into the internet. If I needed Windows to keep making money, I would use Windows.

My point is that you are making decisions about how your business runs from a computer perspective. Be careful, do your homework and make the right decision. Try to find a local LUG (Linux Users Group) and ask those guys what they use in business to run their companies. Find others that use Linux in business and ask their advise.

I wish you luck in whatever you decide.
