Maybe this has been thought of, already.  As we all know, when patches are released for Ubuntu, we get notified, and *apt-get* sorts the updates out.

I was thinking, when Dapper, or future releases come out, it might be a good idea for the update notifier to give us the option to update our */etc/apt/sources.list*, therefore upgrading the whole distro.

The potential problem could be bandwidth issues (flooding the servers/ mirrors).  Could we not rely on a bit-torrent style approach (ie peer-to-peer/ distributed)?

Maybe this will trigger some comments/ thoughts?
