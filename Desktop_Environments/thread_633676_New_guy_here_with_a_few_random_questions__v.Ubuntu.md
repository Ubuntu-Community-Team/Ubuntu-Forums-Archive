For your last questions:

A) Again, I'm not sure. Likely you can change this in Preferences -> Mouse, but I find that ngit is much easier to just click the middle button (this is also how to turn the cube) when in an application, which will allow very quick scrolling. Click again to deactivate it. 

B) I highly recommend **not** turning off the password for everything (high security risk), but if you really want to, here is the way to do so in the terminal: 
```
sudo visudo
(enter password)
(add this to the end of the file:)  username    computername = NOPASSWD: ALL
(press "control+x" to exit, "y" to save changes, "return" to continue) 
```

Again, I highly recommend NOT doing this. 

You can safely login automatically, however, via Preferences -> Login Window -> Security. (This is without doing the above steps)
Oh, and I think you will find GIMPshop fairly close to photoshop. It's based off the GIMP, but with a redesigned interface to resemble photoshop in menu structure and design, to ease just your type of transition. It does not have *all* of the capabilities, but those are mostly for professional work. If you are just a home user/ non printer, this should work fine.
