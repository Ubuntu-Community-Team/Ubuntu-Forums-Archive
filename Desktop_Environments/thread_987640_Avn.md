### Post by keithld on 2008-11-19
The white lines are "Separators" between Launchers & Tasks...

You can get rid of it by going to Preferences > Awn - manager (click the tab) Bar Appearance and it's at the bottom, just "Uncheck it"...

Firefox will show up in the dock after you launch it... I'm not sure how to put it in there from the git go but I think you would need an "applet" to do that then add the applet...

### Post by joey-elijah on 2008-11-19
Go to applications > internet > firefox and just drag it onto the dock and let go. it should then appear.

Or right click on an empty part of the dock and choose preferences. Go to the 'launchers' tab and drag n' drop it on to there or click add and enter it's command.

don't know it's command? just drag whatever app from the menu onto your panel > right click > preferences > look in the 'command' field and copy n' paste that into AWN.

edit: and the white line may be due to not having certain python bits n' bobs installed. There are quite a few python based dependencies for different applications, but head [over here]("http://wiki.awn-project.org/Awn_Extras:Dependency_Matrix") and just search and install them via synaptic until it works.
