### Post by DuckHook on 2020-11-19
I will let others answer your technical question. I am not aware of a way to default to a multi&#8209;line app description. Frankly I have never been bothered by the truncated names because I rarely use the launcher and have no problem working out the identity of the app when I do.

My observation has more to do with your repeated reinstalls. This is a painful way to learn Ubuntu basics. Moreover, excessive customizations usually render your base install very brittle, which increases the likelihood that it will break with your next upgrade. For new users, it is highly advisable to keep the base install as close to default as possible. I am what most would consider a power user old&#8209;timer and I still follow this same good practice. It keeps me out of trouble.

If you want to tinker with Ubuntu's settings, layout, tweaks, etc, a better way is to run a virtual machine and do all of your experimentation within that. Upon breakage, just roll back to a known good snapshot&#8212;never a need to reinstall. Keep your host squeaky clean; dirty your guest(s) as much as you like.
