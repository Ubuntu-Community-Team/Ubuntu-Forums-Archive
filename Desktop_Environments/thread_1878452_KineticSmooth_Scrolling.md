### Post by Copper Bezel on 2011-11-10
It's specific to the application, unfortunately, since scrolling is itself an interaction within the application and nothing to do with the DE. (Unity's overlay scrollbars, for instance, are just a widget that replaces the way that scrollbars are normally drawn, and applications can opt not to use them. When you scroll with a scroll wheel or trackpad, you're simply sending a button-press event that the application has to interpret and react to accordingly.) Depending on the specific application, particularly in browsers, there may be a configuration option for this, but you'll need to find out from that specific app's documentation (so Google search for, say, "Firefox smooth scrolling".)
