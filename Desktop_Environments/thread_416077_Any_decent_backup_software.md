### Post by GuiGuy on 2007-04-20
About six hours ago I started to backup my edgy box with a view to installing Feisty. I set out determined to do it with Linux software.

I'm in Kubuntu. I tried Konserve which fell over with a bunch of bewildering error reports the moment I started the configured backup. Then I tried sbackup. This seemed to work looking at what services were running. But there was no indication what it was doing. Eventually it finished, so I tested the backup files. Ownership of everything had been changed to root. What is the point of that, especially in regard to the /home/<user> directories??

I tried a couple of other backup packages I saw in the package list. One said it finished the backup in less than a second. I thought, gee, that was good. But then I realised it had done bugger all. The other fell over in another heap of error reports. Another wasn't able to to backup to the MOUNTED USB drive, telling me that the drive wasn't mounted although the software was seeing it as mounted.

I gave up and broke  out the Acronis Trueimage rescue disk, booted off the CD and 20 minutes later there's my image.

So, is there a decent, easy to configure, reliable desktop backup utility for Linux, specifically (K)ubuntu?
