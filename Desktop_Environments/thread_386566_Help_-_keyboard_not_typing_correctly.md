### Post by hikaricore on 2007-03-17
Honestly does sound like a hardware issue.  There is a sort of "pad" under the keyboard similar to the type you will find inside a calculator (we've all broken a cheap calculator and torn it apart right?) this usually happens when the "paths" in the pad are broken or cross connected.  This can be a result of damage in shipping, user damage (use your imagination here) or even faulty production/shotty materials.

Hope your replacement works out for you.
