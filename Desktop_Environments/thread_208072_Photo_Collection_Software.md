### Post by claypole on 2006-07-20
Here's my take:

What I love about Ubuntu is it's simplicity.  It looks nice, runs very fast, and ultimately it does what you want without trying to be flashy and keeps the most important options available to you while providing the capacity to fine tune underneath (I would recommend installing Automatix) and enables you to contribute directly to new revisions.

So, Picassa is very slick, Google are making an effort to redefine the apps market in the windows space by providing "free" apps that make working with windows better.  "Porting" a version to Linux is in recognition of this rising force.  I haven't used F-Spot or Picassa  enough to form an opinion yet, and its important not to rubbish an app just because of the way its delivered to the platform (yes it uses Wine but they have made this seamless to the end user and it works).  What I don't like is that the menus remind me of why windows is so damn cumbersome, there are just too many options.  Simple is good. Keep the complexity and intelligence in the code, not on the screen.

Try both, and see which one works best for you :-D
