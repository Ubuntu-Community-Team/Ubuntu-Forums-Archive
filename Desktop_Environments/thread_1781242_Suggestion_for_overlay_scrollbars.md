### Post by klorenzo on 2011-06-13
Hi, this is both a suggestion and a request for infos about the new scrollbars.

I like the idea a lot, for the space it saves, but the thing that makes it clumsy to use is that the area you have to point the mouse to to activate it is different from the area where the scrollbar appears. So you have to do two mouse targeting: first left of the small band, and then move again the pointer to target the scrollbar on the right.

When the window is maximized things work smoothly: I target the "invisible scrollbar", the scrollbar appears and I can just use it just pressing the mouse button.

I suppose the first behaviour was chosen not to hide the contents of the window during scrolling, but I think this could be fixed with a little transparency. Anyway, considering the very small size of the scrollbar, I would happily give away that little visibility in the few cases when I do need to see the latest few pixel in change of a smoother experience the whole day. You can always scroll up a little to expose the hidden content.

So, first, is there already a way to configure this? I would be happy wiith the "maximized window behaviour" for all windows. A quick search on google pointed to nothing.

Otherwise, what do you think about this? Do you think is worth filing a RFE?
