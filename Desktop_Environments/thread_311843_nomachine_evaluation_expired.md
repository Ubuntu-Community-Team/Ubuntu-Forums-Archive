### Post by Macchi on 2006-12-11
Hello mr. talz13,
Clearly you must have been using some evaluation license instead of the free (as beer) version of NX. I am sure there are many other users of NX around here, but for the moment they might be as disapointed with nomachine as I am.

I used to recommend NX as a technology but I do not agree anymore with their market strategy regarding open source. Furthermore I definitely dislike their pricing for entry-level licenses and would ask any fellow to look for alternative solutions. 

I have been using FreeNX for almost a year and still believe that NX is a very interesting technology. In the beginning I could live with most limitations that are explicitly imposed on the free version, such as lack of smooth support to suspend sessions and a limit on two (2!) users for the server. Unfortunately nomachine seem to have changed abruptly their marketing strategy regarding free software since newer versions broke compatibility with earlier versions. A couple of months ago I was then "forced" into the newer versions since the font aliasing for the client got apparently buggy. Upon upgrading the clients I immediately discovered that the server also had to be upgraded. For some time it did not work well neither with Dapper nor with Edgy. The commercial license is in my opinion too expensive for developers and small organizations.

My solution was to fallback to XDMCP logins, that are built-in in Ubuntu and most linux distros.
