### Post by Jorhajnes on 2008-07-12
Never thought my first post here would be about something this small but it's starting to bug me out.
I moved around some applets on my panel to get the feel right, and I accidentally moved the Shutdown/Sleep/etc-button so that it's not on the edge anymore (where I want it) instead the network manager is.
The problem is that I can't move the nm-applet... I can move all the other stuff on the panel but not that one.

I'm sitting on a Ubuntu 8.04 - Hardy Heron with Gnome 2.22.2

### Post by coffeecat on 2008-07-12
There's a sort of vertical dotted line or wall-thingy (powers of expression failing me here :) ) just to the left of the NM icon. If you position your mouse pointer carefully over it and right-click, you can unlock and then choose move. If you get your pointer slightly in the wrong place you get the Enable networking, etc menu instead. You may have to unlock some or all of the nearby applet icons to be able to move the NP applet to where you want it. Don't forget to lock them all again! :p

**Edit:** what I've just described is true for my desktop machine connected via ethernet. Out of interest I booted up my laptop (wireless connection) and the battery icon is between the NM applet icon and the vertical dotty thing (it's called the notification area). So I tried moving it, and the battery came along for the ride. Then the red downward arrow with the exclamation mark telling me there are updates appeared between the battery and notification bar. So now I've got three icons all tethered together chugging right to left and back again across my panel.

It's better than playing with model trains. :wink:

Have fun!

### Post by mcduck on 2008-07-12
The thing here is that the nm-applet icon is not individual panel applet or icon. It's icon that the nm-applet places inside the Notification Area-applet (about the same as systray in Windows).

So you can't move the icon, but you can move the Notification Area-applet.
