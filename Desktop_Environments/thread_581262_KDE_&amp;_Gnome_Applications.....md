### Post by jim_p on 2007-10-19
Yes they do work under a gnome enviroment since they just make use of there kde-dependencies which can be installed no matter what desktop enviroment you are using.

In order to install a kde app, use synaptic and do whatever you would do in an everyday installation.
Some decent kde apps are k3b (cd/dvd rom burning tool), kopete (multi protocol instant messenger) and ktorrent
