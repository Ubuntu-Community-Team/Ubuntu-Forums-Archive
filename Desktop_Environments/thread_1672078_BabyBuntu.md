### Post by nick_goodfate on 2011-01-20
[Sugar]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugar_(desktop_environment)") is not OS, it's [desktop environment]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desktop_environment"). Like Gnome and KDE.

[KIDO'Z]("http://kidoz.net/plus/product_features.html") isn't OS either, it's "the safe, fun and easy-to-use Kid's Web Environment".

Qimo is basically Ubuntu customized to be usable by kids.

So [go for Qimo]("http://www.qimo4kids.com/page/Download.aspx") i guess...

### Post by klossor on 2011-01-20
Im not all that familiar with Linux or children-aimed distro's, but I would suggest getting a basic but standard Ubuntu distro. Kids have an incredible aptitude for anything when theyre young and can adapt and learn very well, even with technical stuff. I speak from personal experience and the benefits are substantial.

You may even give him a leg up if he is able to know how to use a standardized linux based OS from a young age, even if it takes longer for him to learn how to use it, he'll certainly remember and learn from it, just my two cents ;)
