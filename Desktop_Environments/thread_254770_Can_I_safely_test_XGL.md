### Post by cptnapalm on 2006-09-10
Using Xgl should be fine.

There are a couple of small things that you should do first, though, as a few people have had some problems with certain things when switching off of Xgl.

1) make a backup up of your gdm.conf and gdm.conf-custom.  Both are in the /etc/gdm directory.

2) You might want to make a backup of your dot files in your home directory too.  Or at least the .gnome* and maybe a few others.

Nothing horrible will happen if you don't.  Perhaps nothing at all will.  But these two things are simple and don't take much time and maybe will help out should something go awry.
