### Post by fadumpt on 2006-07-21
If you have a good router already or don't use broadband internet, then an access point will be fine.  If you don't have a router and need one, or just want to replace your old one, then a wireless router is your best bet.

Some of the linksys routers are capable of being flashed to run linux router software but I don't know what's involved in doing that, might be a consideration when you decide.  

I personally prefer netgear products because the seem a little bit more stable.  You might consider a Netgear RangeMax router, 7 antennas that adapt to your enviroment :)

The linksys might work, all though whether that is "out of the box" working or a week of hacking away at your system, I'm not quite sure.

The orinoco card should just work.
