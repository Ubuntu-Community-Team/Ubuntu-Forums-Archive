### Post by louis_nichols on 2006-04-22

The answer to this is always a matter of personal opinion, so you are likely to get more than one answer. Myself, I'd say yes. I started my Linux experience with Fedora Core (which is also great), and tried Debian (very powerful - but perhaps it's true what they say that "ubuntu is an ancient african word meaning *I can't configure debian* - certainly true in my case :) "), Ubuntu, even FreeBSD (not that it would be a Linux distro, but it's pretty similar) and countless LiveCD's, and Ubuntu seems the most user-friendly.
