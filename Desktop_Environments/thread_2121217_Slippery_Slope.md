### Post by VanillaMozilla on 2013-03-01
Try Lubuntu first.  It's fairly fully configured, but easy on resources.  It has a nice, lightweight desktop manager.  I found the desktop to be very responsive even when the computer is disk swapping its heart out on some memory-intensive task.  That's an important and unusual virtue.  It also has the resources of Ubuntu behind it.  Plan on spending a few hours arranging the desktop and finding out how things work.

### Post by ExigentM on 2013-03-03
Here is a quick recap of my lubuntu install on an hp mini110 (1 gig ram, 1.6ghz processor and 160 gig drive):


[LIST=1]
[*]Downloaded unetbootin [http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/](http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/) and created a bootable USB with lubuntu (unetbootin does this in a single step)
[*]started netbook and changed primary boot drive to USB
[*]Netbook booted into lubuntu and I just followed the steps as they came up to replace windows with llubuntu
[*]once complete I plugged into my network with Ethernet (wireless was not. Functioning), chose additional drivers from the lubuntu menu and the wireless worked immediately
[*]did updates (313 - lol) and it works perfectly!
[/LIST]
[LEFT]
Thanks for the tip on lubuntu, it resurrected a paperweight and created a great email, surfing, light doc creation device.[/LEFT]
