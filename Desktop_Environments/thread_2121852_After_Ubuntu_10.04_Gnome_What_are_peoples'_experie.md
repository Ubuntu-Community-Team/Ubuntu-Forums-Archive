### Post by welshmike on 2013-03-03
With Ubuntu 10.04 Gnome end of life in April I've been trying out various desktops to install.
I've tried 12.04 Unity but found it slow and a bit buggy on all my PC's including this one, my msi CR620, 2GiB RAM, 4 i3 CPU @2.27GHz and especially bad on my ASUS Eee PC 1001P net book that runs happily with 10.04 Gnome.
So I won't go with Unity for the future.
The most recent distro I've tried is Lubuntu 12.04. This is on my 8 year old Toshiba Equium M40X, 1.5GiB RAM, Pentium M CPU @1.60GHz.
Thus far Lubuntu performs quickly on it and no bugs have shown up.
I like the way that the LXDE desktop is customisable and I attach screenshots of my evolving one.

I would be very much grateful if people using Lubuntu would post their experiences in this thread. Thanks.

### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2013-03-03
Interesting.  The last Ubuntu that I had installed was 10.04.  I went from there to Lubuntu and have not looked back.  I have it installed on an ASUS EeePC as well (1000h). ### Post by JLeon85 on 2013-03-03
I've had a great experience with it. It's very fast and stable, and unlike Ubuntu you can fully customize the panels. The software center is not as nice as Ubuntu's though. 

You may also want to check out Peppermint, which is based on Lubuntu. It's a bit more visually pleasing and it has a transparent taskbar.
