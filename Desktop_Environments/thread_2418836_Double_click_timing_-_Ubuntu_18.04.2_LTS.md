### Post by Autodave on 2019-05-12
Just a thought: try another mouse.  Also, how many USB items do you have connected?  You may be running out of power from the power supply.  To wit: I had a desktop that did a very similar thing like your's is doing.  It ended up being a bad HD that was going bad and drawing way too much amperage.  This caused the 5V line to drop too low.  I kinda found this out accidentally because I had 2 other mouses (mice?) with me and instantly tried replacing the mouse with one that I had.  Same problem.  I had one other mouse, but it was an old style with the roller ball inside (no laser).  This mouse worked!  It worked because it did not require the power to power the laser in the other 2 mice.  Replacing the HD eventually fixed the problem and I could use a laser mouse.

If you have other USB items connected, try disconnecting all of them and see if the mouse clicks are normal.  If then normal, you either have too many things connected, a weak PSU, or something else going bad internally.
