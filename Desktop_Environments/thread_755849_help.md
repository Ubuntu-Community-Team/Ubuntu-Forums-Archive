### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-04-15
Ok let me see if I am understanding your info request...

You want to rotate your screen 180 degrees so the top is on the bottom with a key press rather than rotating it with the mouse in the compiz cube.

Did I translate your statement correctly?

If you meant to start the cube effect with a key press, you can simply add it to the key bindings in compiz. (Default is Ctrl+Alt+C) Should in the 'rotate cube' selection, last tab on the right, 'Actions'. That will allow you to change or set any of the rotational actions to any key you want. It is still going to require a mouse to make a smooth rotation however.

Technically with a newer graphic's card and their software, you can rotate your screen in XP, just not in a cube like Linux does. Certain screens also have a rotate feature which is independent of the OS, but they are rather pricey just to be able to flip your monitor sideways.
