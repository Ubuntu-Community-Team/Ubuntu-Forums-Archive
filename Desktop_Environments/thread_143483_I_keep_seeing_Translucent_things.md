### Post by bluevoodoo1 on 2006-03-12
An easy way... With gnome-terminal you can adjust its transparency with Edit > Current Profile > Effects. Click on the Transparent background, then set it closer to "none." There are many more advanced things around the forum for "true transparency" but I have not messed around with that yet. 

Here's a link for "true terminal transparency"
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=141307](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=141307)

All of the cool new stuff has to do with xgl and compiz. I have not tried either, or anything with 'true terminal transparency' so I can't really ansewr any questions regarding those...
