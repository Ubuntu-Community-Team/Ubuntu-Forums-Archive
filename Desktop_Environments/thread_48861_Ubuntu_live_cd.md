### Post by badrinarayan on 2005-07-14
First you may want to check out [other live cd's](http://www.frozentech.com/content/livecd.php) which may serve the purpose before creating your own. For example, you may use the wonderful [klik](http://klik.atekon.de) to install it in [Knoppix](http://www.knoppix.net/). Also if a live DVD is ok, then [ExTiX](http://www.extix.se/) has it already (it is called valknut now). 

You could customize [slax](http://slax.linux-live.org/) or [klax](http://ktown.kde.org/~binner/klax/) with "[modules](http://slax.linux-live.org/modules.php)" thanks to [UnionFS](http://www.fsl.cs.sunysb.edu/project-unionfs.html). You may even use a [friendly installer](http://myslax.rabidhutch.co.uk/about.php) to do that.

However, if you do want to make your own live cd, the following may help:
[ibuild](http://ibuild.livecd.net/)
[Customize Ubuntu LiveCD (Ubuntu Wiki)](https://wiki.ubuntu.com//LiveCDCustomizationHowTo)
[Linux Journal Tutorial](http://www.linuxjournal.com/article/7233)

Regards
Badri
