well, your log's don't indicate a major problem to me 

said again i only use a nvidia card, **maybe sb. with an RADEON XPRESS 200 (RC410 5A61) chipset can give much better specific support.**

some stuff that looks not good:
(WW) fglrx(0): ***********************************************
(WW) fglrx(0): * DRI initialization failed!                  *
(WW) fglrx(0): * (maybe driver kernel module missing or bad) *

(WW) fglrx(0): * no 3D acceleration available                *
(WW) fglrx(0): ********************************************* *

does the ati driver ship no 3d accel support?

i advice you to (temporarily) change the driver back to vesa. your devices support the following video modes (according to your log)

(II) fglrx(0): Supported VESA Video Modes:
(II) fglrx(0): 720x400@70Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 640x480@60Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 640x480@67Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 640x480@72Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 640x480@75Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 800x600@56Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 800x600@60Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 800x600@72Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 800x600@75Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 832x624@75Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 1024x768@60Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 1024x768@70Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 1024x768@75Hz
(II) fglrx(0): 1280x1024@75Hz

So change the following in your xorg.conf (after checking that BusID PCI:1:5:0 is correct with "lspci -v")

```
Section "Device"
	Identifier	"ATI Radeon XPress"
	Driver		"vesa"
	BusID		"PCI:1:5:0"
EndSection
```

and in the Section Screen
```

SubSection     "Display"
        Depth       24
        Modes      "1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
EndSubSection
```
