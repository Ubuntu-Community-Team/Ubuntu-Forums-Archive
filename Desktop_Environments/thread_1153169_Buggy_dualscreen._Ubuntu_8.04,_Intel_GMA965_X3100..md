Hey there!

I usually connect an external display to my laptop which runs on U8.04 and uses the integrated graphics controller Intel Graphics GMA965 X3100.

Well, the dualscreen works, but it's very buggy.

The following events take place when I switch to dualscreen:
* The gnome-panel is **ALWAYS** moved over to the external screen and needs to be dragged back.
* Whenever I close/move windows (for instance moving gnome-panel back in its place), a ghost of the window remains on the external screen until it gets covered by an active window.
* The background is split in two on both screens, not very esthetic.
* cairo-dock is moved in the bottom on the middle of the virtual display. As my laptop screen runs 1280x**800** and the ext.disp 1280x**1024**, only the half of cairo-dock is shown on the bottom left on the external display. I would like to keep cairo-dock in the center bottom of my laptop screen.

Besides, it would be great if I could specify which programs that ran on which screen (EXPORT DISPLAY:=0 or however the syntax is), doesn't work (whenever using another display than 0, the window manager can't locate the display). I think this is caused because the windowmanager (gnome) interprets the virtual screen as one giant screen.

Is is possible to separate the external screen from the window manager? (Like having 4 different workspaces on the laptop screen, but always 1 workspace on the external, that isn't affected by switching workspaces etc on the laptop screen)
