### Post by markbl on 2012-01-27
You want a "sticky" window. Bring up your video in a movie player, vlc, etc. Then click on the window title bar and select "always in visible workspace".

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2012-01-28
> **Rebant said:**
> No, I mean workspace.
Setup: One laptop, two workspaces.
Put video on workspace 1. Full screen it.
Go to workspace 2.
Have a window which displays the contents of workspace 1 in the bottom left corner (or anywhere else) of workspace 2.
So, what you want is *not* full-screen in workspace 1, but a mini-screen in workspace 2. Yes?

Well, just show the video in a small screen on workspace 2!

[LIST]
[*]If you want the video to play on top of your other work, right-click the top bar and select *Always On Top*.
[/LIST]

[LIST]
[*]If you use VLC as your player, you can also find that option in the menu *Video > Always On Top*; and you can use *View > Minimal View* to show just the video and title bar. I'm not sure which other players have similar options.
[/LIST]
