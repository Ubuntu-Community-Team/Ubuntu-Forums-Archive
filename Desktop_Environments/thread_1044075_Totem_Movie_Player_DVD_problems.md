### Post by adamlau on 2009-01-19
If you are indeed a Linux and OSS advocate, you should know that there are many 'extra' applications which extend the flexibility and simply work better than the default choices of Ubuntu. Regarding 'less supported' applications, I presume you are referring to Canonical and community support for default Ubuntu applications. I have not found that to be the case as the developers of the applications which you are referring to (be it a Ubuntu default, or otherwise) as the best source of support. I understand how you feel. Outside of development libraries and headers and packaging tools, I rarely use a anything Ubuntu offers as a default application. And as for a solid DVD and general media player, I have found SMPlayer (though burdened by Qt) with MPlayer SVN to be the best :) .
