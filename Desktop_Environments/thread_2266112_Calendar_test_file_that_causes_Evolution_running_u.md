### Post by cigtoxdoc on 2015-02-19
I have reported here and elsewhere that e-mail containing a VCALENDAR attachment will cause Evolution running under 64-bit Ubuntu 14.04 to crash.  For those of us who use Evolution and Ubuntu in our businesses, such crashes are not only an embarrassment (I have to call my clients and ask them to send me a separate plain-text e-mail with the details of the teleconference), but put our businesses at a competitive disadvantage.  I recently learned that those using the version of Evolution that comes with Fedora 21 do not have this problem.  I added Fedora 21 to my "guest" PC (hp/compaq dx2200 microtower) that also has Win XP SP3 and Ubuntu 14.04 64-bit loaded on the hard drive.  I was able to confirm that Evolution under Fedora works on same e-mail that causes Evolution under Ubuntu to crash.  There is a test file located at [http://www.healthsystem.virginia.edu/pub/pnso/calendar/icsfilesforoutlookcalendar/testcalendar/view](http://www.healthsystem.virginia.edu/pub/pnso/calendar/icsfilesforoutlookcalendar/testcalendar/view) that can be downloaded and attached to an e-mail. The name of the test file is "Calendar test of ics files.ics"  I hope that others who have multiple e-mail accounts on different PCs will send an e-mail containing that ics file on one of their PCs that is running Evolution with html enabled (most of the e-mail I receive has critical information in the html part of each message).  I hope that those who try the test will report back their findings (including OS version and Evolution version) in replies to this e-mail.

Thank you in advance for your assistance.

John
