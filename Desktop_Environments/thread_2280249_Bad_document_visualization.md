### Post by mcduck on 2015-05-29
No, there isn't. Font rendering is an area with surprising amounts of patents and proprietary ways of doing things, and all operating systems do things slightly differently (OS X will display your documents differently from Windows or Linux, for example, as their hinting and font rendering system is different). And due to the patents and restrictions, same mechanisms can't be replicated exactly on the other systems.

Also make sure you actually are using same fonts, most document formats don't include the fonts and instead just rely on you having the correct font installed on your system.

If you need the document to look exactly the same on different platforms, you need to convert it into PDF. (And even then the rendering can change slightly, unless you convert all text into paths. Of course that would mean the text can be selected or searched any more)
