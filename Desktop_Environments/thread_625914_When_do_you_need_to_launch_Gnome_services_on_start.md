### Post by jis on 2007-11-28
Settings > Sessions and Startup Settings has option to "Launch Gnome services on startup" but when do you need to do it? Don't Gnome programs work, if the setting is unchecked? What are the programs that the setting affects to? I mean how do you recognize such programs?

---

### Post by sirdilznik on 2007-11-28
Starting GNOME or KDE services on startup in Xfce does not affect the programs you can run.  You can run GNOME or KDE apps just fine without that option.  However when you check those options, KDE and GNOME services come up when Xfce first starts so when you run your first GNOME or KDE app it will start faster.  However the boot into Xfce will take a bit longer.  So it is a trade off.
