### Post by teet on 2006-02-19
I set my mom up with gmail/thunderbird.  For windows, there's this handy little program called mozbackup that I use to backup my thunderbird/firefox information.  I'm sure there's a manual way to do it in linux too...

But I must ask, why would you want to burn your emails to a CD?  With gmail, I have it set to both download the messages to my mom's computer and automatically archive the messages on the gmail server (you get over 2 gigs of space...why not use it!).  Hence, my messages are automatically backed up without having to do anything (you still should backup your address book from time to time however).  

Also, thunderbird does have HTML support.  You may have to click a button that says "view images" to see images in your email though.
