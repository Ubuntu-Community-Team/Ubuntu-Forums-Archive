### Post by krunge on 2007-04-25
Maybe you can force things by settings in /etc/X11/xorg.conf?  I.e. the Modes and/or Screen sections.
Have a look at them; you can tweak this remotely (e.g. /etc/init.d/gdm restart).

If it is not going to ever have a monitor, you could also put in a Monitor section that puts in 
acceptable VertRefresh, etc to fake up a good monitor.

Although I obviously love x11vnc, why are you doing it this way with the physical video
hardware, instead of using Tight or RealVNC vncserver RAM only servers?

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-04-25
I don't see vertical or horizontal refresh rates.

Try adding to the "Monitor" section:
        
        HorizSync       32-75
        VertRefresh     60-75

You may have to make up slightly different numbers to force 1024x768.

vncserver will kick off anyone else on display 0 when you log in. (Unless set to share/view only)  I don't see the benefit of locking Display:0.
