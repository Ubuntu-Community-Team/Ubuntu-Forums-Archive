### Post by speartip on 2014-04-22
You are correct it is a totally different animal.
I started off with Gnome 6 years ago, but have slowly become more accustomed to KDE. Now it is my main OS.
All I can suggest is trial & error. System Settings is a good place to start, & tweak away to your hearts content.
It is far more configurable than Gnome. Also the ability to download different Themes, Workspaces, Borders etc.. from within the setting is good.
I personally Use the Glacier colour scheme with the oxyzone workspace, & add my own Desktop background. But as I say go into all the different categories, within system settings & tweak away. Even if you mess up you can always click the default button & put it back to how it was.
Or if you make a complete mess of it, delete the .kde4 folder in your home folder to go back to  a vanilla Kubuntu.
Have fun.
