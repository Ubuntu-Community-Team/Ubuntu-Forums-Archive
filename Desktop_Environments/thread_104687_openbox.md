### Post by chombee on 2005-12-16
dcast - you might consider installing imendio's gnome-launch-box (it's in one of the repositories, probably universe). I'm currently using OpenBox and looking for ways to access stuff like the GNOME menu too. Launch box is not quite contain everything in the full GNOME menu, but it is a useful way to launch many applications, open files from your homedir or recently accessed files, send emails to your evolution contacts or open firefox bookmarks. You can bind it go a key in your openbox configuration file, the thread that a previous poster linked to shows how to do this binding, just substitute in the command gnome-launch-box.
