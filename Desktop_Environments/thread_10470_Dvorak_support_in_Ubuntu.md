### Post by Maniak on 2005-01-09
Go to Computer/Desktop Preferences/Keyboard/Layouts and choose dvorak from the right hand window. Shift it to the top of the left and you are away laughing (or typing.....)

### Post by moose6589 on 2005-01-10
I use a qwerty keyboard as well for Dvorak typing, and I don't suggest buying a Dvorak keyboard because the few that are on sale don't seem to be of decent quality. 

To train, you could get one of the really old versions of Mavis Beacon(5.0 or before I think)  that support Dvorak and train with that. If you can touch-type, the process is quite fast and easy. This is only my 5th day using Dvorak, but I already know all the letters and can type at about 30 wpm, which seems to be pretty good progress. And if you need to know where the letters are, look on Google and find a picture! It really is easier than it sounds.
