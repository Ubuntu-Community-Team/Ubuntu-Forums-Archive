### Post by Dominicus on 2018-02-12
I have a notebook running Ubuntu 16.04 connected to my home network via WiFi.

It had been running reliably for couple years with ubuntu 16.04 as a remote PC. I use TeamViewer to remotely access it.

Over the last four months or so, it's been dropping off from the network, sometimes after <4 hrs from reboot, other times after a few days.

When I physically check it, open the lid to show the screen, I find it in the lock screen.

I type the correct profile password, ubuntu tells me it's the incorrect password.

At that point, I can do couple things to get back to the desktop view:

1. I can click on the top-right gear icon, trigger a restart, and enter the password upon booting, OR

2. I can click on power, then "switch account...", on the next screen I'm presented with the one and only user account defined, I enter the same password, it'll again throw the red "incorrect password" warning, but Ubuntu proceeds to open the desktop.

After either of these, the notebook loads desktop and auto-connects to WiFi.

I'm now able to close the lid and connect to it remotely without issue...for about 4 hours or so.

What's going on?

### Post by benjimenez805 on 2018-02-14
make sure that your notebook doesn't have any special keyboard feature turned on. I found one time that my laptop had a special key layout that I had accidently activated by pressing the function key and other key. So my keys were not entering what I thought they were.
