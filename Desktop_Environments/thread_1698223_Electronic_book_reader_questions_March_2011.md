Hi,

### Post by Old_Man_Unix on 2011-03-02
Some suggestions:

If you do a lot of serious reading, I strongly suggest a reader with an electronic ink display rather than an LCD display.  The difference in eye-fatigue is very noticable.

IMHO, the E Ink displays are the best ones.

I also strongly suggest that you get a reader with at least Wi-Fi capabilities.  With today's technology, you shouldn't be required to tie your reader to your computer if you don't want to.  If your Wi-Fi is unreliable, then you can opt for the 3G connectivity.  Otherwise, you don't need it.

Full disclosure: I have always had Kindles (now using  Kindle 3).  

I have no trouble connecting a Kindle to Ubuntu and transferring files when I want to do that.  

I generally use Amazon's free service to download file conversions of free books directly to the Kindle,  so I can't speak to that aspect of things.  But if you download a lot of free books you might want to look into a  program such as Calibre to organize your collection.  Calibre will also give you access to free periodicals.
