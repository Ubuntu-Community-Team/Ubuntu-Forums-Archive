### Post by hoodwink on 2006-06-16
Is there a way to rearrange the taskbar so that the workspace selectors appear on the left and the active tasks appear on the right?

I find that most of my mouse activity is on the left edge of the screen, and it would be nice not to need to swing the mouse pointer all the way to the right edge to select another workspace.

I'm sure there are hotkeys, but this is a question of usability with the mouse.

---

### Post by BitTorrentBuddha on 2006-06-16
Yeah, just right click the workspace switcher and choose not locked to panel, then likewise unlock the separator type thing between the show desktop and active windows, then move the workspace selector over. The hotkeys of which you speak are, by default, ctrl+alt+(direction).

---

### Post by Blitzace on 2006-06-16
yes there is, right click the window selector, deselect "lock to panel" and use the middle button of your mouse (or  right click again and select move) to move it where you want on the taskbar. 

nvm you got there first. Hope it works for you.
