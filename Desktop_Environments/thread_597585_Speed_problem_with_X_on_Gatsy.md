### Post by Mariano Oliveto on 2007-10-30
Well I'm actually a noob myself so I don't know is this will be of any help.

As far as I can see, you've installed the restricted drivers for your ATI Radeon x1400 video card and they seem to be working OK based on your fglrxinfo outcome.

I read in another post here at Ubuntuforums.org that one guy had some problems with an AOC monitor and it's xorg config... but that was solved by choosing the generic drivers for the monitor (which I see you have chosen as well).

Maybe you can check if the desktop effects are turned off (they should be since you have both Compisite and AIGLX options off in your xorg.conf). Go to Systems -> Preferences -> Appearance and go to the "Visual Effects" tab. There you should choose "None".

Also, maybe you could try the following in a terminal:
```
fgl_glxgears
```

Wait for 5 secs and it'll give you the FPS. This can be a good diagnostic of your 3D performance (correct me if I'm wrong).

What I can propose is:
a) Try the opensource drivers ("System -> Administration -> Restricted Drivers Manager" and disable the ATI accelerated graphics driver ar whatever they're called)
b) Try unsing ATI installer to install their 8.42 drivers if you haven't already  (I wrote the instructions that worked for me in this thread: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=580949&page=3](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=580949&page=3))

I actually got very bad results with the repo's restricted drivers on my ATI Radeon x1600 and the ones from ATI are working like a charm!

Ah! Don't forget to check your CPU and RAM usage... maybe an application is eating your resources!?!?

Best luck! :D
