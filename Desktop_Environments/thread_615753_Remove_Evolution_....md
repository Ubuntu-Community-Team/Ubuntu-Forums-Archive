### Post by ruibernardo on 2007-11-17
neocortex, I think you don't have to remove evolution to install/use Thunderbird - from what I read it's a pain to remove it. 

Why do you want to remove it? If you are not using it, it will do no harm. If you're using it, disable the mail accounts.

EDIT: just for the record, Evolution is a pain to remove because it is integrated with Gnome by Gnome itself (from the sources - Evolution is Gnome's mail client/agenda/etc and it is integrated in the Desktop Environment - should be in all distros that uses Gnome, no way around it). That's why it is a dependency of ubuntu-desktop and it is a pain to remove from Ubuntu (Gnome version).
