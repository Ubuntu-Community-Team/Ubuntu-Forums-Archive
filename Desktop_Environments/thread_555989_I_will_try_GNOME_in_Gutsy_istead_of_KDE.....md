### Post by daveshields on 2007-09-26
I used to use KDE, back in the days when I ran SuSE. I liked the appearance and SuSE loaded up my machine with gobs of packages.

However, now that I have become an ardent fan of Ubuntu, I use GNOME, for two reasons:

1) GNOME is more minimal. It gives you a good starter set that you can then grow as you see fit. Apt-get is so powerful (See my post [Why Ubuntu? It uses apt-get to manage packages]("http://daveshields.wordpress.com/2007/09/22/why-ubuntu-it-uses-apt-get-to-manage-packages/")) that you can freely install new things, play with them, remove them if you don't like them and re-install the packages you were using.
2) GNOME is the Ubuntu default desktop. Like it or love it, you are better off using the default, as it will be ahead of the derivatives such as kubuntu.

Moreover, the more Ubuntu users collectively converge towards this default, the easier it will become to grow Ubuntu's market share.

And that is why Ubuntu is so important, and why it will be the focus of most of my blog posts going forward, as it has been the last few months.

Ubuntu is the best effort I have yet seen in bringing Linux to the masses. To do that we need -- above all else -- to grow Ubuntu's market share.

### Post by Azriphale on 2007-09-27
I tried Gnome 1 and KDE 2, and there settled on KDE. A few years later (with Ubuntu), I switched to Gnome 2, and am currently back to KDE mostly because I find Nautilus takes forever to start.

Part of what I like about KDE is its use of Qt. Qt is natively a C++, object oriented toolkit, while GTK is not. As a result, my own application development makes use of Qt 4 as it makes for a much neater and cleaner design and implementation, as well as for the fact that Qt is more full featured than any other GUI tookit (for the reason that it is more than a GUI toolkit, with XML parsing, HTML libraries, FTP libraries, and a whole lot more). I also prefer the look of Qt widgets, and they are far more customisable. Most of this, however, only becomes really important to me with KDE 4 (because it uses Qt4).

As for stability, in KDE 3.5.7 I have absolutely no problems.

I did enjoy the cleanness of the Gnome desktop, but I have found that KDE has been cleaned up somewhat over the past few releases.
