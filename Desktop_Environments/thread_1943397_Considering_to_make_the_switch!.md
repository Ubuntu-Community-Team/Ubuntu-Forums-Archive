### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-03-19
Have you read this?

[http://www.ubuntu.com/devices/tv/experience]("http://www.ubuntu.com/devices/tv/experience")

It is my understanding from reading some of the publicity about Ubuntu TV that it is intended to be put in TVs and other devices pre-installed.

This I think is sensible because the OS can be modified to work with the hardware so that everything works out of the box.

I am not sure that the Ubuntu TV aspect will be included in the standard Ubuntu distribution. But then, what do I know?

I do know this, At the moment some people have difficulty getting Ubuntu to work on their hardware. There is such a wide range of hardware possibilities that it is next to impossible to produce a Linux Distribution that is guaranteed to work out of the box, as you put it. Now add TV tuner cards into the mix. Can you imagine the problems?

I have a DTV card does not work in Linux. If Ubuntu TV was released as a distribution and I was to download it, it would not work with my TV card. And then I would be coming on to this forum expecting someone to have the answers. That would be out of the range of this forum.

There is a web site dedicated to Video (TV cards) in Linux. The developers there try to find ways of getting these TV cards to work in Linux. If a card does not work in Linux it will not work in Ubuntu. 

So, anyway, I am very doubtful of Ubuntu TV ever being released as part of the Ubuntu distribution. Besides, there is already a Ubuntu variant designed for VDR capablities - Mythbuntu.

Here are two links should you wish to experiment

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuTV/Contributing]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuTV/Contributing")

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2012/01/how-to-install-ubuntu-tv-right-now-via-ppa/]("http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2012/01/how-to-install-ubuntu-tv-right-now-via-ppa/")

The purpose of making this download available is for developers to develop applications to work with Ubuntu TV.
