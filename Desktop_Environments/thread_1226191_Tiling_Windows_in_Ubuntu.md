I thought that too, but I read about it somewhere and thought I'd give it another try.  Basically, it maximizes a window to fill the free space between it and the other windows on your desktop.  Doesn't work so good if you have a lot of windows open on top of each other.  Try this- open three windows, arrange them on an otherwise empty workspace, not overlapping (though, I think it still works if they are slightly overlapped), then highlight a window and hit super+m.  Pseudo tiling.
