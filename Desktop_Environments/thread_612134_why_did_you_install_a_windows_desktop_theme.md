### Post by beckfield on 2007-11-13
Yeah, Jouke is right.  I am a fairly new Kubuntu user, and I felt it would be easier to make the transition if the environment was as familiar as I could make it.  So far, this has proven to be a helpful choice.  In addition, having looked through the available themes, I just liked the Redmond better.

I really don't like a cartoonish, overly graphic UI.  I like things simple, and the Redmond is simple.  I shrink the toolbars, buttons, and icons to be as small as I am comfortable with, and turn off most animations.  All those things just slow the system down, in my opinion.

I want my operating environment to **support** my productivity, not **be** my productivity.  :)
