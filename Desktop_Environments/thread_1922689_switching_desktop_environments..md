### Post by GraeW on 2012-02-09
I don't have my laptop with me at the moment (I run Ubuntu 11.10 on it) but there should be meta packages listed for each of the desktop environments associated with Ubuntu. Installing through Synaptic will bring each of those into your system, and then when you start up your system (as long as you do not do an auto-login) you will be prompted for your password, and there will be a menu or button option to select the different desktop environments (tough to say what to look for because with each distro it changes.. mine looks like a little gear next to the password box).
 
I prefer XFCE over the others (available as Xubuntu) but usually do install the standard Ubuntu first, then add KDE, XFCE and others as I feel like playing around. Have never had any major issue with multiple desktops - just have to remember to not run one on top of another, but choose them at log in separately.
