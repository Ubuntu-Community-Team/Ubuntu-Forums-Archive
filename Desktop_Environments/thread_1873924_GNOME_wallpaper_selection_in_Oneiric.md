### Post by jejones3141 on 2011-11-02
Once upon a time in GNOME you could add wallpaper from any directory you could access. I see that now in Oneiric you have two choices: the wallpaper directory that presumably all users have access to, and ~/Pictures. No other directory, and you aren't even given the ability to go into a subdirectory of ~/Pictures.

Silly me; I tried to organize my images, so I can't use the vast majority of them as wallpaper. Is there any way around this other than destroying what order I have managed to impose on my pictures?

---

### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-11-02
If you go to the pictures folder (in that wallpaper settings screen), you also have a +-sign at the bottom. There you can add another directory.

---

### Post by qamelian on 2011-11-02
Click on the plus sign below the list of default wallpapers and you can navigate to and select any image you want.
