### Post by kurros on 2005-04-27
They are likely left in the mailbox storage format. The way mail attachments work is that they are translated/encoded into standard text and is a part of the message. It would all be stored together on disk. You would need the mailer to decode it back into a usable binary file for you anyway.

I hope that makes sense. In short, No.
