### Post by viperdvman on 2011-09-02
I just wanted to create a new thread just for posting for everyone to see your Ubuntu/Kubuntu/Xubuntu/Lubuntu/etc. desktop, to show us how you have your Desktop Environment set up to work for you :)

Here's mine on my main desktop:

[http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v245/viperdvman/ScreenshotUbu4.jpg](http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v245/viperdvman/ScreenshotUbu4.jpg)

I've given my Ubuntu 11.04 "Ubuntu Classic" the Mac OS X look :) I added the Application menu on the top panel next to the GNOME menu, I changed the icon for my GNOME menu to the color one instead of the monochrome one. I also am using Avant Window Navigator as my dock program. That dock is possibly my best user interface, with all my program commonly-used app launchers and my task manager integrated into it. It easily allowed me to move a couple things that are in the lower panel by default into the upper panel, and thus delete the lower panel.

I love this setup, and it works for the Unity desktop too when I set up the Unity launcher to auto-hide instead of just window dodge. It even works with my KDE desktop as well, though I had to move the panel from the bottom to the top of the screen.

Here is my netbook's desktop:

[http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v245/viperdvman/ScreenshotUbuNet3.jpg](http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v245/viperdvman/ScreenshotUbuNet3.jpg)

It's a very similar setup to my desktop computer's setup. The right side of hte upper panel is slightly different, with fewer buttons. The GNOME menu too is more simplified, all due to the netbook's narrower screen resolution vs. my desktop's. The app menu is still there, of course, as is the Avant Window Manager. But, I'm using smaller icons in AWN, again due to the smaller screen resolution.

There you have it, my Ubuntu desktop environment setups. Show us yours :)
