### Post by aysiu on 2006-03-11
Short answer, no.

Medium-length answer, you can use GIMP instead of Photoshop, aMSN instead of MSN messenger, and Quanta instead of Frontpage. Of course, there are also emulators (like Crossover Office) that allow you to run Windows programs in Ubuntu, but if you need those *programs* so badly (and not what they *do*), you're probably better off using Windows.

### Post by adam.tropics on 2006-03-11
For MSN, try Gaim, it handles pretty much all IM varieties, photoshop would require the use of emulation/virtual windows machine (or crossover office, which may or may not handle CS@ ??) . Skip Limewire and go for Frostwire, it's pretty much the same with far less irritations, and no constant 'upgrade now' stuff!

In general, you should consider looking at open source alternatives as at least perhaps an option. Photoshop is a great program, but a quick google will find alternatives in linuxdom!

Finally, go geo-caching.....I just heard about it recently, who'd of thought we could perhaps convert people by hiding the disks!!
