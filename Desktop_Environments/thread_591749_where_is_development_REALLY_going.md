### Post by Happy_Man on 2007-10-25
1. Dude, I am with you there on this one. Although, realize that you do have to cut the devs a little slack. They are working the best they could, and some of those other apps you mentioned (I think Trillian Pro could do that?) are hiring devs who are paid. So, they have an obligation to the customer to provide functionality. Pidgin has no such thing. To look at it from another viewpoint..... Trillian has been around for at least a decade. Pidgin is only a couple years old, *plus* had its entire codebase rewritten recently. They have to get all the UI and crash bugs worked out before they can even think about more functionality. Would you rather have a buggy, crash-prone Pidgin that can nudge or a stable, fun-to-use Pidgin that is working on it?

2. You are saying you had Beryl installed on Gutsy? Hmmm... due to the wonky way 3rd party repos work with this kind of thing, I suggest removing *all* packages relating to Beryl and Emerald before installing Compiz Fusion. 

3. Emerald Theme Decorator never affected what was inside a window,... just the outside. Enlightenment is a great example of the buggy but feature-filled vs. stable but not-so-flashy. Enlightenment is an example of the former. It'll crash once every 2 minutes, but it'll look great doing it. Of course, Windowblinds made Windows unstable also.... :p
