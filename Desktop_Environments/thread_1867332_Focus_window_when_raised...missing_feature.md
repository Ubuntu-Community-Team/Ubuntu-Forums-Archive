### Post by bhh1988 on 2011-10-22
I'm using xubuntu and compiz. Compiz is doing great things for me so far, but I can't seem to find exactly the desired behavior I would like for my window raising/focus behavior and I wonder if such a thing simply does not exist and was overlooked.

The behavior I'm looking for is to put focus on the window that I raise. I have "click-to-focus" set on and I have shift-ctrl-click mapped to raising a window. If I do this click on any part of the window, the window gets raised. This is better than having to click on the small title-bar of the window to raise it. But when I raise the window, the focus does not get set on it. What this requires me to do is to shift-ctrl-click then click to put focus, or click to put focus then shift-ctrl-click to raise. I think it's fair to say that most of the time, if someone chooses to raise the window he wants to also put focus on it. 

Note that this is not the same as just raising every window that I put focus on. I like the ability to put focus on a window without raising it. But when I do choose to raise a window, I would like focus to automatically follow. Is this possible?

Or is it possible to map certain keys to putting focus on a window? That would also solve my problem, because then shift-ctrl-click could double as another map to focusing a window and so doing that key-sequence would both raise the window and put focus on it.
