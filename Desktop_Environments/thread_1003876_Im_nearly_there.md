### Post by dyous87 on 2008-12-06
There is really no need, or equivalent to defragging for a Linux installation. The file system checks that occurs every 30 or so times you boot up your computer will take care of any inconsistencies that may arise. 

Apart from that, the only cleaning up I can think of is getting rid of hidden directories in your home folder that are left overs from uninstalled applications. You can access hidden folders by pressing "control h".
