### Post by FXWGBill on 2007-03-24
Alot of the time, I prefer the simplicity of gnome, but I like them both.  All you have to do is go to system, then Admin, then down the list to Synaptic package manager.  Bring that up and do a search for KDE and most of what you need will be right there to check off and install.  Start with KDEbase and it will auto check off all the basic stuff you need....  Then go from there...
