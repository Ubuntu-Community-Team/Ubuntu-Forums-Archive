I'm personally convinced that KDE could come out with something that looks utterly and completely nothing like Windows, and people would still say "kde luks liek windooze!11elven" as soon as they found out what it was - it's almost a conditioned response! :)  Seriously, if you put one of the mockups side-by-side with a screenshot of vista, the differences outweigh the similarities by a huge margin - the window frames are different, as are the button styles they contain; the panel is thicker and central, with the menu button located in the centre of the panel and, when expanded, having a completely different approach to Vistas'.  The taskbar is two rows and is located to the left of the menu button, and similarly for the tray icons.  The clock is completely different.  Basically, about the only similarities that I can see are that the bottom panels are both black, and the wallpaper is (admittedly very) similar! If, in spite of all this, people are still going to say "just liek teh windoze!", then there's very little anyone can do: they'd likely say it even if KDE were skinned to look like GNOME ;)

Edit:

As a general point about the possibility for being a marketing disaster - I think everybody in the KDE team is now very aware that KDE4.0 is going to be a very, very rough release - if it were an airplane landing, it would be roughly akin to a plane with one wing and its remaining engine on fire striking the ground hard, and upside down ;) The marketing department has pledged to make it abundantly clear that KDE4.0 is just the beginning, and there is even (unofficial) talk of very explicitly marking 4.0 as a release intended mainly for die-hard fans and early adopters, right through the promo material to the name of the release itself, and having the first, proper "Everyman" edition coming a few months later.  It's going to be fairly tricky to handle, so let's hope they're up to it!
