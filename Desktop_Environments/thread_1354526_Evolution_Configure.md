### Post by donato roque on 2009-12-14
Hello lotusalive

    First of all, to configure/make changes in your "actual" email account you will have to go to your email service provider's website.  All that Evolution is doing is get that info which is in your "actual" email account.    

    You can use Gmail, Yahoo mail, Hotmail or any other email service with Evolution.  The desktop email name is any name you choose to uniquely identify the account.  The server type has nothing to do with the hardware you're using.  It is the protocol being implemented by your email service.  It could be POP or IMAP.  This depends on your particular email service provider.  

Whether it's SMTP OR SENDMAIL- well if you don't know then I would guess that you are using SMTP.  

What is important here is the email address:  [email]username@mail.com[/email]
Is the service using POP or IMAP?  Server is:  pop.mail.com or imap.mail.com
Does the service require authentication?  SSL/TLS or none

### Post by thatguruguy on 2009-12-14
I see that you use comcast as your ISP.  Have you considered going to the comcast website for the information you need?  Setting up Evolution is almost exactly like setting up Outlook Express, the instructions for which you can view here: [http://customer.comcast.com/Pages/FAQViewer.aspx?seoid=Configuring-or-setting-up-Outlook-Express-for-email](http://customer.comcast.com/Pages/FAQViewer.aspx?seoid=Configuring-or-setting-up-Outlook-Express-for-email)

The incoming email server is a POP3 server, and its server name is mail.comcast.net.
The outgoing email server is SMTP, and its server name is smtp.comcast.net.

Hope that helps.
