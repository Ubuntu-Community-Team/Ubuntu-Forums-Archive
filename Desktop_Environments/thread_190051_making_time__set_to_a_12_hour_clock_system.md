### Post by charles.keisler on 2008-07-18
Hi I am a new to Linux but have known about the Linux \ UNIX infrastructure For About Four Years Now It Was About Ten Years Ago I First Saw A UNIX workstation. 

Anyway, The Benefits OF The KDE are Great, in fact it was designed for a work environment, However there are downfalls to an Enterprise type environment, it measures time in 24-Hour Days ONLY and Measures Years in 52-Week Calendars ONLY, so on and so forth.

I'm sorry to tell you that i have tried changing the format in KDE 4 and there is absolutely NO WAY to change it back to Standard 12-Hour Time Because Of How The KDE Clock Functions. It Was That Way Even Back in [KDE 2]("http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5e/KDE-2.0-es-es.png") according to My Research.

However, KDE 4 Dose Have A Widget For An Analog Clock you can Place ON Your Desktop. Simply Right Click Your Desktop -> Ad Widgets... -> Analog Clock -> Add Widget. A Standard 12-Hour Analog Clock Will Appear On The Desktop.

For a More Customizable Digital Clock You Might Want To Try 
[http://www.kde-look.org/]("http://www.kde-look.org/")
