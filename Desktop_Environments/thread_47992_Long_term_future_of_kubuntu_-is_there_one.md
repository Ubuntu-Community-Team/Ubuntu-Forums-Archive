### Post by apollo2011 on 2005-07-10
No, Kubuntu is an official project of Ubuntu.  As long as Ubuntu is around, Kubuntu should be around.  The only difference is that Ubuntu installs Gnome by default, and Kubuntu installs KDE by default.  Once one is installed, the other can be added.  As it says on the Kubuntu web site, Kubuntu is very much an official part of Ubuntu.  Also, everyone working on Ubuntu, or at least  most of them, are volunteers, as open source does imply that the developers are all volunteers and unpaid.

### Post by SanderAnt on 2005-07-12
[QUOTE=apollo2011]No, Kubuntu is an official project of Ubuntu.  As long as Ubuntu is around, Kubuntu should be around.[/QUOTE]

'An official part' is a somewhat nebulous term, while the packages are part of the official repository, the last I heard the primary developer Johnathan Riddell was not a Canonical employee, and you can't order Kubuntu CDs, like you can normal Ubuntu CDs.

I brought up the same issue a while back on the Kubuntu mailing list and Jeff Waugh (of Canonical) responded that it had support, but my feeling is this is meaningless without dedicated resources, and some level of commitment.

To the best of my knowledge Canonical hasn't issued any official support policy in regards to kubuntu specifically, or offered any commitment on future Kubuntu releases.

My guess is if Jonathan moves on to greener pastures then Kubuntu is a memory, even though there other KDE developers like Andreas Mueller and Aaron Siego has expressed interest. That said there haven't been a lot of packages and Kubuntu's number of KDE specific packages greatly pales in comparison to Debian.

It's a shame because the cities of Munich and Vienna are going with Debian/KDE so it seems like there are legitimate business opportunities. I hope with the opening of resources *cough PowerPC* Canonical will consider funding KDE development.
