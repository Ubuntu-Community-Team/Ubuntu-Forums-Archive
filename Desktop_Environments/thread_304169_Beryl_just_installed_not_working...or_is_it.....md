### Post by Tasu on 2006-11-21
Hmmm! It seems the programmers have changed something in the way beryl is run, before you have had to add beryl-manager to your sessions, but now you have to add both beryl and beryl-manager to the session, the first actually runs the 3d desktop, the latter is just an easy way to manage beryl.

So the commands you have to issue are these two (in no particular order, if you have beryl-manager already running, just issue beryl):

beryl
beryl-manager

### Post by PriceChild on 2006-11-21
Click the beryl icon. Choose "Select Window Manager" and then choose "Beryl"

I STRONGLY advise you not to add beryl to startup. - beryl-manager can help save you and go back to metacity in crashes.
