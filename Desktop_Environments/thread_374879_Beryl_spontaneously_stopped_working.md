### Post by Fizzstickle on 2007-03-03
I had Beryl running quite beautifully for about three weeks, and was enjoying showing it off immensely.  Then, abruptly -- about two days ago? -- and after an ordinary, run-of-the-mill sort of reboot, Beryl failed to load.

Beryl *manager* loaded just fine - its little red gem icon was there in my system tray as it had been for the past three-ish weeks or so, but all of the pretty effects, drop shadows, animations, and the Emerald window decorations *didn't*.  Just plain old boring Metacity   (I'm not exactly bashing Metacity - I just got spoiled on Emerald).  No jelly-like, wobbly windows that warp and fade when you drag them around.  No rotating-desktop-cube effects.  And I believe I miss the dramatic drop-shadow under my gnome-panel most of all (if there's a way to get even *this* without having to use any composite managers, I'll totally settle for it.  I'm all ears.). :P

At first, I scrolled through the drop-down menu that the Beryl manager tray icon offers, checking to see if it was in fact using Beryl window manager and Emerald theme manager.  Both were checked.  I even unchecked "Launch Fall Back Window Manager in case Beryl crashes" then restarted X just to see what would happen.  I still ended up with what appeared to be non-Berylified, strictly GTK/Metacity desktop afterwards. 

Then I did a few searches on Google and on these forums.   Some posts suggested I try checking "Force XGL" on all of the rendering options.  That didn't work.  So I tried "Force AIXGL" (though I have to admit I don't actually understand what either of them are).   I restarted X several times in the process.  Still no dice.   

Then, I went about comparing my xorg.conf to all of the example xorg.conf settings in the various "how to install Beryl in Ubuntu under Nvidia" sort of guides out there *(there's one on the Wiki, there's one on these forums somewhere, and there may be one other)*.  From the looks of it, I seem to have included everything I need to include in my xorg.conf to have Beryl working.

My problems seem to have begun after I installed (and then uninstalled) the enlightenment dock-style application "Engage" via Synaptic. That day, I also did one of the Ubuntu automatic updates, so I suppose *that* could have also been a culprit.

Anyway, none of that is really useful to you guys, so here's some information on my system.

I am running Beryl version 0.1.9999.2~0 under Ubuntu 6.10 (Edgy Eft?).  I have an nVidia GeForce 6200 OC with the beta nVidia drivers installed (I used a script I found via these forums called Envy to install them).   As I said before, all of Beryl and Emerald's beautifying special effects mysteriously stopped working approximately two days ago, my not having done anything short of completely inadvertent to mess with them.

When I try running "beryl" in a terminal, I get the following:

[COLOR="Green"]Detected xserver                                : NVIDIA

Checking Display :0.0 ...

Checking for XComposite extension               : failed

No composite extension[/COLOR]

One post I read mentioned trying to run "beryl-manager && emerald --replace" in a terminal.  Absolutely nothing happens.  The terminal seems to freeze up.  Or maybe I'm not waiting long enough.  I'd think waiting a full ten minutes, though, *would* be enough.

The one thing I've read about that I *haven't* tried (because I'm scared to death of breaking something) is rolling back to the previous version of Beryl (I don't know, is there a really safe, idiot friendly/newbie friendly way of rolling back to the previous version that won't blow up my xorg.conf and render it useless?  I've had some scary experiences involving blowing up my xorg.conf in the past, and I'm a little traumatized.  Totally irrational fear, ya think?  Most likely.)
