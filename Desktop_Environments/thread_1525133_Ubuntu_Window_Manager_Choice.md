### Post by cversion7 on 2010-07-06
While I have not seen a big difference in going with XFCE vs Gnome, you might look at more options like LXDE or Fluxbox which are possibly a bit more lightweight than XFCE even. Don't limit yourself. You can just install a WM and log into it to find out. You don't need to load any specific variant of Ubuntu to try them out. sudo apt-get lxde or whatever you choose. Then on your login screen, choose your session by selecting something other than Gnome. This way, you're not losing any current configuration and if you don't think it is fast enough to make you stay full time, you can always just hop right back to what you know.
