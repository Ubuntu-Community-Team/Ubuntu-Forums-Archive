### Post by Riverside on 2005-10-21
[QUOTE=Gadget]In 5.04, I just ran with xine, which was on the system.  I played some .wmv files just fine.  Now, after the upgrade, those same .wmv files have sound, but no video.  I generally work under the rule "if it ain't broke, don't fix it."  But 5.10 took away xine, and the ability to play those same files..  I've only been playing with ubuntu for a couple of months, so temper your responses accordingly, please.  What do I need to do in order to get my video playback capability back to where it was before the upgrade?[/QUOTE]There are (as usual with Linux!) several ways to resolve this issue.

Mine was to first visit [http://www.mplayerhq.hu/homepage/dload.html](http://www.mplayerhq.hu/homepage/dload.html) and to download the essential codecs package.  Once unzipped and untarred, I then copied the files therein to a newly created /usr/lib/win32 directory (sudo is needed to both create that directory, and copy the files, of course).  I then ran sudo ldconfig (I am not sure if that is actually needed, but as it can't do any harm and these are library files, I did so).  That's it.

### Post by department27 on 2005-10-21
Assuming you have all the codec's installed, and you still have no video then go to settings within xine, then setup, and look in the video tab. Make sure that it says xshm for the video driver. If it says auto, then change it to xshm, and that should fix it.
