This is a "won't fix", as the KDE team seems to assume that you should be using Wayland rather than XOrg (Wayland works fine, X does not) and the Wayland team thinks it their call to decide what users should and should not do.  Both parties have taken an arrogant "we know best and we get to decide what you can and can't do because we think you should or shouldn't do" attitude.
