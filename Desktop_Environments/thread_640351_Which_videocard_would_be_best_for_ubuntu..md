### Post by danwood76 on 2007-12-14
I think the nvidia drivers are much raved about but the ati drivers are advancing a lot quicker and are catching up very quickly.

The ATI card in my laptop works with no problems and I didnt have any problems installing drivers and desktop effects. (Radeon Xpress200M)

On my desktop at home is a different matter, I have an x1950Pro, installing ubuntu was hard as the default drivers included on the install CD dont work with this card.
But once I had it installed (had to modify the xorg.conf and reboot the xserver to get it to run) and the ATI drivers installed its been running perfectly ever since with excellent performance.

As Dixon said 'what works well for one person, might not for another.'

Intel cards are supposed to have the most stable drivers and be the easiest to install, but if your going for graphical performance then they arent reccomended.

I would expect if you bought a quite recent ATI or Nvidia you wouldnt have too much trouble installing drivers and enabling the effects.
