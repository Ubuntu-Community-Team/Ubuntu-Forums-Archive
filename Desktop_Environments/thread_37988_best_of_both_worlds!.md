### Post by brickbat on 2005-05-29
This is just a post to let you know what my problem was and how I solved it.  Maybe someone else has similar problems.

I moved to Ubuntu.  Best decision I ever made (IT related) BUT 

1 the drivers for my printer only half worked
2. my wife loves a program that she only uses once a month but it only runs in windows
3  my scanner is not recognised by linux
4 there is nothing as good as dvdshrink in linux

solution?

I had an old laptop I had stopped using.  It was slow as anything after installing anti-virus , etc and had a 10gb hd.  it has only a cdrom drive.

I networked them, in both directions using samba and terminal service to run the laptop from the main pc.

it drives my printer and scanner
it has acrobat to let me scan direct to pdf., my wifes program and dvdshrink
i linked the dvd burner to it via samba and it can rip directly from it and writes direct to my main machine.  ripping works at full speed! 
the laptop has been blocked from internet access at the router so has no firewall or antivirus

If I could find a way to get windows to recognise the dvd burner as such then i could get dvdshrink to burn to it directly but its good enough  burning it to an image and using k3b

btw, except for my wifes program, these are all temporary problems. Although they were a pain in the ass and they required me to dual boot once a week. now no dual boot and everything runs great.  As a side benefit, I can continue working without any delays while ripping.
