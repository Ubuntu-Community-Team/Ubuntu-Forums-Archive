### Post by leech on 2006-01-08
That's easy.  Boot off of the Install CD.  Then just select Automatically partition my drive.  It'll wipe it all off and partition it for you.

Or you can just go into the partitioning part and [http://www.howtoforge.com/perfect_setup_ubuntu_5.10_p2](http://www.howtoforge.com/perfect_setup_ubuntu_5.10_p2) will show you an example of how to partition it.  It's pretty easy.

### Post by matthew on 2006-01-08
Put the Ubuntu installation disk in the drive and reboot. When you get to the disk partitioning part of the process erase all of the partitions and then create new ones as you like. 

It's easier than it sounds, just read as you go along and you will be able to do it.
