### Post by greyfaux01 on 2018-01-18
First thing i would do is google how to 'completely remove KDE from ubuntu'. Then after you do that, you can assume kde changed some configs that ubuntu's window manager was also using.

Try pulling up your window manager and look around in those settings, theres a program i have on mine called 'window manager', and also theres the 'appearance' program, sometimes ticking/unticking a box will bring it back to normal. Sometimes takes a reboot after that. Understand im no pro, these are just things ive done in the past to fix very similar problems. Try changing the theme in 'appearance', and then changing it back, or just stick with a different theme if you like it.

Not saying its the best advice, but thats what i would do if i was in your shoes.
