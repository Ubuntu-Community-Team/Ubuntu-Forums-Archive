### Post by dipaish on 2009-04-02
It's as simple as you have come to this forum.

First down this extension [http://downloads.mozdev.org/moztraybiff/mozTrayBiff-1.2.3-tb2.0.0.0.xpi](http://downloads.mozdev.org/moztraybiff/mozTrayBiff-1.2.3-tb2.0.0.0.xpi). (I always recommend to check the latest version)
Open your thunderbird .
Select tools >>>addons>>>>install>>>>point to the location you have saved the aboce file
Restart your mozilla thunderbird
Select tools >>>addons>>>>Under extension you will get new mail icon >>><click on preferences >>>check all 3 options.

That is what you need to minimize thunderbird to tray. 

Now you can simply minimize you will get a tray icon and click on it.......Thats it simple and easy.

### Post by sjprice on 2009-04-21
Kdocker, in the repos. then I created an app launcher:

kdocker /home/<name>/thunderbird/thunderbird and it launches and then sits in the dock.

Fantastic! That's a great little application.  I already had put a thunderbird launcher icon on my top panel so all that I needed to do was right click the icon, select "properties" and change the command from "thunderbird %u" to "kdocker thunderbird %u".  Works like a charm!
