### Post by TurbofanDude on 2011-10-16
Hello Ubuntu developers,

I recently attempted to upgrade from 10.04 to 11.10 Oneiric Ocelot. I did so, but I was sadly disappointed that the classic desktop environment had been removed, as did easy access to about half of the things that I wanted to play around with. I must ask what made you decide to turn Windows-like in this release? 

Please don't get me wrong, I am in love with Ubuntu, and if my apps ran naturally on it, I would use it as my primary OS. I respect the amount of work went into it, and I admire the developers' decision to provide it for free. 

That being said, the biggest improvement over Windows I saw immediately was the fact that everything wasn't over GUI-fied. There were some menus, and just about all of the options one could need on a regular basis were readily available, and there was room for many applications on the launch bar. With 11.10, I couldn't find admin/system tools and options, the icons got huge and started glowing, and my system slowed to a crawl (I am using a VERY old machine on Ubuntu). The question I pose is to the reasoning behind the decision to implement this change.

I personally do not need support, as I just re-installed to 10.04, and as it is an LTS release, I am not worried about it. 

Anyway, this is not an attack on Ubuntu devs, as the new version looks very good, but doesn't appeal to me as much.

I appreciate your answer,

Respectfully,
Collin Biedenkapp,
Flight Simulator software developer

### Post by paulisdead on 2011-10-16
There's a gnome-shell extension to add an application menu in gnome3.  It uses the same shortcuts that the application menu in gnome2 would use.  You can enable a ppa to more easily install it and some other common extensions.
