### Post by TravisNewman on 2004-12-19
XFCE is fantastic. Not as easily configurable as KDE and Gnome, but as useable. The downside to the boxes and NexStep is that they are VERY configurable, but that takes a lot of manually editing files, and then using them is not as intuitive. 

Enlightenment used to be the GNOME Window Manager (at least in the RedHat versions I used in the past), but they've switched to Metacity. Enlightenment rocked hard back in the day, but when I tried it in Ubuntu about a week ago it kinda flaked out on me. Basically it didn't work. I'd suggest you just install some and try them out, find one that works for you. I'm still using Gnome personally, just because it's in my comfort zone, but I really want to get the hang of the others just to expand my knowledge. When I first installed Warty I had all of them installed I think. All that were in the repos anyway. It won't break anything that you can't fix by just logging back into Gnome. Didn't for me at least.

### Post by LongTooth on 2004-12-19
After many years with  Linux I've tried them all. Fluxbox, Blackbox, Enlightenment, IceWm, Xfce and the more well know KDE and Gnome as well as others. It boils down to taste. In some cases it depends on the equipment. On older underpowered machines the 'Boxes' are really the only way to go. They require less resources. If I have to make a list on my druthers, it will go this way: Gnome, Xfce, Fluxbox, KDE. But when I think about it, a full DE is what I want. And Gnome meets those needs. I like the lay out of Gnome and the elegant look of it. Try them all. Explore. Have fun.
