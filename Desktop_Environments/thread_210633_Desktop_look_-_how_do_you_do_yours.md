### Post by phibxr on 2006-07-07
<rant>
Most window managers are built on paradigms that forces you to manage the windows yourself, clutter your desktop and in the end inevitable suffering from losing space.

I think what you're looking for is called Ion (sudo apt-get install ion3), a window manager best described by the author himself.

> So-called "modern desktop environments" are totally unusable, and present-day mainstream graphical user interfaces in general are far less usable than they are praised to be.

Those of us who prefer to use the computer primarily with the keyboard for reasons of efficiency or health, are forgotten when "modern" graphical programs are designed. Mouse-based search-and-click interfaces simply are not efficient except for some very specialised tasks and in other cases involve lots of tedious repetitive clicking and searching.

Similarly it is difficult to navigate through an unorganised stack of application windows, also using the mouse. In most common window managers and operating systems it is usually left for the user to attempt to keep the windows organised instead of the window manager. As a result the user often ends up in a jungle—or at the desk the overlapping window paradigm tries to emulate.

Ion was written as an experiment on a different kind of window management model. It tries to address the navigation problem by dividing the screen into mutually non-overlapping frames that take up the whole screen.

To finish this post off, I'll attach the screenshot I took for my human_look-style for Ion (download instructions are in the thread [http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=210325)](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=210325)), but screenshots do not do Ion justice in any way. Use it for a week, and see why even a long time Gnome supporter as myself find no reason to look back at the world of clutter and windows management.

</rant> ;)
