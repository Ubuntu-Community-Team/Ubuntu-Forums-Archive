### Post by mcduck on 2006-02-06
.mid is a MIDI file, so it's not actually audio at all. MIDI stands for Musical Instrument Digital Interface, and it's used to connect different synthesizers and other electronical instruments together and control them. So .mid file doesn't contain audio but instructions for a MIDI instrument/player what notes to play and when. What it sonds like depends on what instrument you use to play it.

So, the short answer is that you can't convert audio file to .mid. The long answer is that some programs can do that, but it only works for very simple audio files, with only one instrument playing, and even then the results are mostly useless.
