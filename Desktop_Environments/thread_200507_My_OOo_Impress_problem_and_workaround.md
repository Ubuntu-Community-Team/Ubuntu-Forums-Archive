### Post by meng on 2006-06-20
I've heard that Impress is famous for crashing. Here's my own experience:

I like to file copies of scientific journal articles as PDFs. Many of the journals publish recent articles (last 5-10 years) as PDFs, but the older articles are available in paper format only. So I photocopy these, scan them into my computer, then put each scanned page on a separate Impress slide and export as PDF. (gocr is not quite good enough to convert to text in my experience)

Here's the problem - after 2, 3 or 4 slides of these roughly 2 megapixel black and white images, Impress will crash and then try to recover the file, but will always crash again unless instructed not to recover the file.

My "solution": to turn off the preview/slide sorter panel on the left side of the screen. For whatever reason, if Impress is not being asked to generate thumbnail images of each slide, it won't crash. It ain't particularly elegant, but it suits my purposes (articles may be up to 15 pages sometimes, but are often shorter).

Comments and other suggestions welcome.
