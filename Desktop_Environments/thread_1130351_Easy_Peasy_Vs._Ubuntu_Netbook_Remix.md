### Post by savagenator on 2009-04-19
ONly you can decide. Would he want a netbook to behave like a normal laptop (easy peasy) or like a true netbook (netbook remix)

The difference is the slightly different interfaces, other than that its all good.

Why would you want to stick with the custumized easy peasy then? For just that reason: Its made for the eee series. It may give better performance and features (such as eee-control) that you would have to set up on netbook remix in the default ubuntu system.

Personally I go with crunchbang eee version. Fastest and gave me the best battery life on my eee 901 out of everything so far (i got a whopping 7.5 hours on the default battery, wifi off, bluetooth off, sd card off, screen 50%, powersave mode. 5.5 with wifi on.

### Post by blackened on 2009-04-25
I've been using Jaunty (and ext4) on my 900 since alpha 4 with very few issues. Most of the eee specific acpi functions have been incorporated into the kernel modules and work out of the box. The exception to this being the webcam. I had to tinker with eee-control (for 8.10) to make it play nice with python 2.6.

All that said, you should let him try them all and see which one he likes best.

Also, Jaunty's version of network-manager plays so much nicer with cdma modems than Intrepid's did, it's not even funny. What was pulling teeth in Intrepid is plug-n-play in Jaunty.
