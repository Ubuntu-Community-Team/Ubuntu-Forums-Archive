### Post by jdong on 2005-04-08
Due to personal preference, some people (like me) prefer KDE over GNOME.

In this case, I'd prefer to be able to install KDE instead of GNOME, not alongside GNOME. Canonical realized this, too, and to keep to their "ask-no-questions" installation philosophy, decided to make a separate CD for the KDE flavor of Ubuntu.

Why they gave it a separate name? Don't ask me. I think it should've just been Ubuntu KDE edition and Ubuntu GNOME edition.
