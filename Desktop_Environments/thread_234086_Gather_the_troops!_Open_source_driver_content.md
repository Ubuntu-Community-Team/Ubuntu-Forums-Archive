### Post by palmiter on 2006-08-10
I want a choice in bleeding edge operating systems.  I want to run the latest, greatest desktop environment without sacrificing features.  I want to get the most out of my computer.

But I can't.

The reason: GPU manufacturer's won't open source their drivers.  ATI (er, AMD-ATI) [is thinking about it]("http://www.infoworld.com/article/06/08/02/32OPcurve_1.html"), but that's not good enough.  Nvidia won't even give the idea the time of day.

Things have to change.

From a CNet article in April: "'It's so hard to write a graphics driver that open-sourcing it would not help,' said Andrew Fear, Nvidia's software product manager. **In addition, customers aren't asking for open-source drivers, he said.**"

Let's unleash the bull in the china shop and twist Nvidia's arm.  Let them know that we want to use a flavor of Linux that isn't castrated by inadequate driver support, and that a company's stance on developing for the open source community does, in fact, play a major role in buying decisions.

Ask a question, or leave a comment.  Come back and do the same tomorrow.  And the next day.

Bump.  I want this thread at the top until everyone sees it and acts on it -- let's harness the power of our community and change the way a company does business.
