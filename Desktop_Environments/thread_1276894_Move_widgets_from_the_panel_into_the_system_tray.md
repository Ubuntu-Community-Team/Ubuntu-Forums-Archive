System tray in distro's like SUSE and Kubuntu is separate from the panel, and it's actually better this way, in case you want to hide a couple of widgets to save more space, whatever... The only KDE distro that has incorporated the wireless and battery widgets (or better, icons) on the system tray was Linux Mint 7 KDE CE. It makes more sense anyway, since kde4daemon is more of an app than a widget, so I think it deserves at least, a place on system tray.
