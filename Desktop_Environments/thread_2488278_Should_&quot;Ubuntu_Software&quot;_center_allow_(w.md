### Post by ian-weisser on 2023-06-29
I recall lots of bellyaching about categories during the 15 years they were exposed on desktops before they were replaced by Desktop Search. Need more categories, need fewer categories, need a billion subcategories, need to rename categories, need badges or colors or tags to sort categories, etc. A million cranks, each with a billion complaints and only their solution would solve everything. And meanwhile a torrent of users whining that they couldn't find anything, that application XYZ was mis-categorized, etc. 

There's a reason developers and designers ran away from categories as fast as they could when alternatives finally appeared, and have resisted being dragged back into that fire-swamp.

Meanwhile, Synaptic will happily let advanced users search and browse by categories. 

Also, the Taskbar already does expose the name of the application. It occurs when hovering. No Gnome extension is needed.
