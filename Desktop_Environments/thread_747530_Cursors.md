### Post by widowmaker on 2008-04-07
Nightingale,

I am running Ubuntu 7.04 and mine is also in System>Preferences>Mouse. You can also go to Synaptic Package Manager and due a search for cursors and you should see an entry for Xcursor Selection. Check it to be installed and then install it. Sometimes it picks up cursors and gives you selection choices that the regular mouse selector won't give you. It will also be found in the same menu as the regular mouse cursor selector as before. System>Preferences>Cursor Selection. While in Synaptic Package Manager you just as well check the other cursor selections, which will give you extra cursors to choose from. If you don't like them go back and uninstall them through Synaptics. The XCursor Selection Panel can also be used to install cursor themes you may download off the internet. Just use the install feature or drag and drop the cursor theme on the XCursor window and it will install.

You could also go to your Home Folder and click on the View menu and click on "Show Hidden Files" and go to .icons folder and or .themes folder and see if your mouse is listed in one of these folders providing you can remember the name of you mouse theme. I have found a cursor theme I really like called "Grounation" It goes well with about anything. You need to load it from the Gnome-Look .org site.

Hope this helps some. I am still feeling my way around Ubuntu 7.04 and 6.06, haven't explored 7.10 yet and I don't figure I need to just yet. However, I like them better than Windbloze.
