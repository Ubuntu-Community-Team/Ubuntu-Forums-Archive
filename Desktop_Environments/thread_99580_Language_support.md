### Post by hca on 2005-12-05
During instalation of 5.10 (and earlier) considerable time is spent on installing language support for several languages which I even cannot read. How do I control an installation so that only two languages are supported? In my case Danish, my primary language and English, my first forreign language.

---

### Post by metoo on 2005-12-06
I would assume this is a feature, as ubunut aims to be accessible to as many people as possible after installation.

Use Synaptic and search for 'language-pack' (s) (Base/KDE/Gnome) and install/deinstall unwanted ones.

Search for 'fonts' or 'truetype' to uninstall fonts like bengaly and others, you will never use.

Install 'localepurge' to automatically get rid of man pages in unwanted languages.
