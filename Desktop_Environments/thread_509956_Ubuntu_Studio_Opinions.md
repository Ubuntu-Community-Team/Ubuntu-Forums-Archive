### Post by Kpax1 on 2007-08-15
I installed the Ubuntu Studio packages after a clean install of regular Feisty Fawn, and ran into problems... Basically I could not get K9copy to function, and then the system would fail to recognize other package updates, so I ended up reformatting and re-installing. 

There were alot of programs in U. Studio, and unfortunately it was overwhelwing. I have Ableton Live for Windows, and it is really nice. I think I will stick with that. It is a lot of work to learn new programs, and I think quality over quantity. I am however excited to start using Cinelerra video editing with Kino import software, as It looks really powerful.

Ubuntu Studio reminds me of of Windows 3.1 as far as the level of development it is at right now. I think that after another 10 years Linux may be up to speed with professional quality apps. But as it stands, its still very much in the experimental stages.
