### Post by aramazan on 2005-03-28
[QUOTE=KDE-fan]I think that Kubuntu should come with KOffice as default. OpenOffice is a great package, but is becoming too dependent on Java. [This article](http://software.newsforge.com/software/05/03/22/204244.shtml) was a real eye-opener for me.[/QUOTE]
The real concern for me is that OO.o seems to be under serious influence of Sun, at the top tier project management. The decision OO.o has taken cannot be explained out by convenience or pragmatism. It may be so at the developer level (i.e. Sun employees experienced and conditioned in Java) but this is a deliberate strategic move at the management level. I don't consider OO.o as truly open anymore. I suspect a fork will be on the horizon if OO.o keep their direction. And I suspect their direction will even worsen by time.

As to what this might mean for Kubuntu, it seems clear to me, as there's not very many options I can see (no, I don't see gcj as a viable option). Go with OO.o (Java disabled) for the immediate future, until either OO.o forks and stabilizes (several years at least, if ever) or KOffice matures, ports to Windows (already?) and gets an established place there. Why Windows port? It is important, because if people don't use and get familiar with it in Windows, then platform switching will be really hard for them. One of the most important features of OO.o is that it's a facilitator for both mixed environment and two-step switching: First you switch the apps keeping the platform (Windows), then you switch the platform keeping the apps. The most easy-to-switch and user-friendly way. Otherwise, the change-shock might be prohibitive for a good number of would-be switchers. Also mixed environment is a necessity, because companies can't migrate overnight, neither individuals.

With Qt being GPL, and (according to what I hear) KOffice getting better and better everyday, I guess KOffice possibility can be earlier achievable than OO.o fork route. Whichever happens first, possibly it won't be before several years, though. 

Well, time to re-install Koffice and inspect it throughout.
