### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-07-17
Is the update upgrading the kernel also? Are you doing a restart then nothing happening? 

If so, try dropping back to the previous kernel (the one that works) and making that your default. Then you will be able to boot and have a look at what's on the other install that's killing it, perhaps look at the dmesg.

Are you getting a list of kernels to boot to at boot? If not, hit Escape (think it's shift in newer releases, actually) and that should get you there. Choose the third kernel, not top or its recovery; that should be your working kernel. Good luck.
