### Post by matthewv on 2005-12-23
Depends what splash screen you are talking about. The first you mentioned is not actually a boot screen, rather, its the boot loader (grub) and the second is a splash screen, managed by a program called usplash. But any program can have a splash screen... it's just a screen that shows while the program is starting. Gnome has a splash screen too, it's the pic that shows after logging in before your desktop comes up. Other progs like openoffice also have splash screens... :)
