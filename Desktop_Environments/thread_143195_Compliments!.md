I'm guessing that the core of Bunts success is the decision to focus on desk top users and sticking to that focus.   

In that context it makes sense to focus on software choices and preferences that favor the end user, not the enthusiast.   The enthusiast has the desire and better capabilities for tweaking.   The burden of tweaking should be on that group, not end users.

It looks Bunt got that right.  It has problems, but I was impressed with how little I had to do out of the box to have things the way I wanted them.  Almost all of the default software installed is stuff I use or can see myself using with very little redundancies.
