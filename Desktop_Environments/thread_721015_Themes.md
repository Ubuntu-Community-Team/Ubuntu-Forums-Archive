### Post by x1a4 on 2008-03-11
> **Twitch6000 said:**
> I did find something called gnome art to download which helped some.Now though I am wondering two things,1.How to give my themes to that gnome art site?

Visit [art.gnome.org]("http://art.gnome.org/") and [create an account]("http://art.gnome.org/account.php")

> 2.What the other peoples themes look like(the people here).

Look in the [Gallery]("http://ubuntuforums.org/g/")

> The second question is so I can get more ideas for me to theme.I like programming and making funny art with gimp :p.

Copy a theme from /usr/share/themes/ to ~/.themes/ and start modifying it.  

If you want to change GDM themes, they're in /usr/share/gdm/themes/

Install the **grokking-the-gimp** package.  It is an HTML e-book on how to use the GIMP to its max. potential. It will be installed in /usr/share/doc/grokking-the-gimp/

You can also get wallpapers and GTK, Compiz, cursor, icon themes at [xfce-look.org]("http://xfce-look.org/")
[box-look.org]("http://www.box-look.org/") has wallpapers, icons, fonts and themes for various window managers.
On [beryl-themes.org]("http://www.beryl-themes.org/") you can find Beryl, Emerald, and Metacity themes, icons and other themes.
[compiz-themes.org]("http://www.compiz-themes.org/") has wallpapers, Compiz, Emerald and Metacity themes
