### Post by Tom_ZeCat on 2008-07-21
A couple times I've accidentally hit some kind of hot key that showed me three of my open apps on the desktop kind of like a tri-fold science project poster board.  Each time I was trying to hit Alt+Tab to move from one app to another.  I can't seem to figure out what command I actually hit that did this.  Does anyone know?  I'm in Ubuntu 8.04.

### Post by mtbsoft on 2008-07-21
I think you'll find you've pressed Super-Alt-Tab (Super=Windows key) - it's the Shift Switcher compiz plugin.

### Post by tamoneya on 2008-07-21
just so you know there is super-alt-tab and super-alt-shift-tab which rotate through all of your open applications.  There is also super-tab and super-shift-tab which rotate through just the applications on your current workspace.  Its a small difference but it can be very useful.
