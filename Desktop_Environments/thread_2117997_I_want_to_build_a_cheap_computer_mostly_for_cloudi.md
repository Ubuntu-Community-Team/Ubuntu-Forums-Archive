### Post by sanderj on 2013-02-20
I think that is feasible: search second-hand forums (like eBay) for a computer (including monitor) with at least 2GB RAM.

For example, on my national second-hand forum (marktplaats), I found "HP Compaq dx2000mt pentium4 2.8ghz 1,5gb ram 55gb hdd" for 15 Euro. (That's without a monitor.)
Such a system is capable of running Ubuntu, certainly if you leave out Unity.

I think quite a lot of people are happy to get rid of their old system.

So: at least 2 GB RAM and at leat 2 GHz CPU, and you should be OK.

### Post by sanderj on 2013-02-20
Ah ... I overlooked the "mini PC" part. Answer: if you buy a second hand computer, it will not be mini at all; it will be bulky.

A new mini-PC that can run Ubuntu and has x86, will cost you more than $100.

A mini-PC based on ARM that can run Ubuntu can be had for less than $100. However: it will need some software hacking. Not for the faint of heart.
Browse [http://www.cnx-software.com/tag/ubuntu/](http://www.cnx-software.com/tag/ubuntu/) to find a device.
