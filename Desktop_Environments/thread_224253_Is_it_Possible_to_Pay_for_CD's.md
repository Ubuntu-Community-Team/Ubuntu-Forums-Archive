### Post by jgcamp99 on 2006-07-27
Gee, what a terrible inconvenience, download a live cd and burn it yourself. Then be up to date on what you want by using the repositories and selecting the packages. Then again, what the repositories is putting out isn't "Edgey" or cutting edge enough for some.

I prefer the single cdrom download and then putting only what you need or use on the system. For Linux this works great. Pay your broadband bill and be able to get what you want, even update and upgrade while you're sleeping or elsewhere running errands.

I could understand if you had to download 5-6 iso's, but one, for even dsl lite, this is an overnight job at worst. Or even a job you kick off as you walk out of the house to go to work. I remember the day of RH, every 6 months you were downloading at least 3 iso's over 56K dialup. Of course you'll need to find a source that sends you a white box Linux. Those days are gone, because Ubuntu manages repositories and the next version will come out and you'll be going thru this process again and again. It would be cool if Ubuntu would simply update the iso to be the latest and greatest every month or so when it undergoes a kernel change or jumps to the next gnome desktop. That way you have a choice for new systems, install the original Junr 2006 version and download updates or download the latest and greatest Ubuntu 6.06 LTS and install that way, because you'd still need to  download the applications you wanted.

Either way, a system installation and setup is going to be time consuming. Think of it from a standpoint of cloning though for a larger company, they do it once on a corporate standard computer and then roll out an image for subsequent systems where the hardware is a common denominator.
