### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-11-09
On the login screen, if you go to the Options menu in the bottom-left corner, then go to "Sessions...", you will hopefully see an entry there for Window Maker. Click that, then click OK, then log in.

### Post by scooper on 2006-11-09
Just thought I'd mention that you're not just changing the look of the desktop, you're changing everything about how it works.  WindowMaker is great, but you won't be able to transfer your Gnome skills without a learning curve.  It also will not show your desktop icons or panel layout from Gnome.  They live in different worlds.

I would think of window managers in terms of behavior, rather than appearance, and choose one that fits best with your way of working.  You can make most of them pretty once you're comfortable.
