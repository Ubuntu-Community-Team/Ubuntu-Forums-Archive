### Post by snowpine on 2010-04-26
Kubuntu is just Ubuntu with a different "skin" (KDE instead of Gnome). Both are equally well supported and share the same release cycle and repository of applications. 

Gnome and KDE are not developed by Canonical, the company that creates Ubuntu. They are developed "upstream" by their respective projects:

[http://gnome.org](http://gnome.org)
[http://kde.org](http://kde.org)

So check out some screenshots, and if you think you might like to try KDE, you can install the kubuntu-desktop "metapackage" and then select KDE from your login screen.

ps this is a *very* frequently asked question and you will find a ton of lively discussion on the subject if you look around. :)
