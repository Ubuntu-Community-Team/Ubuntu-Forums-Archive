From 90% of what I have read on this forum, unity is unusable, clunky and crashes often, yet I am posting from the 11.04 Unity desktop and I find it a delight to use. Rather than go by  often-misinformed opinion, try it for yourself with an open mind. If you don't like it, that's fine. In which case all you need to do is to choose classic desktop from the login screen. You need to enter your username first and then the choice of desktops appears before you enter your password.

That will keep you going in 11.04, but things may be different in 11.10 which will use gnome3. It is upstream gnome itself that is abandoning the traditional gnome panel, not Ubuntu, so a move to an alternative distro will not help you. These are uncertain times for those who like the traditional gnome desktop, but something will emerge to satisfy them - one hopes.

Good luck with whatever you choose, but I ask you to try Unity and/or gnome-shell yourself before making up your mind. Don't believe what others say - including myself! :)

### Post by coffeecat on 2011-04-16
> **johann_p said:**
> If that is true

It is.

Don't forget the need to enter your name at the log in before the choice appears. That has confused some people.

Also -  I believe that the global menu is enabled by default in the classic desktop. That's the focussed application menu in the panel - somewhat like what you get in MacOS. That irritates some people, but you can disable it and use the conventional window bar menus. I can't remember how to disable global menu offhand, but I'll post back if I find it.

**EDIT**: found it - couldn't be easier. The global menu is a panel applet in the classic gnome desktop. If you want to disable it, right-click on it and "Remove from Panel". If you want to re-enable it, right-click on the panel > Add to panel > Indicator Applet Appmenu.
