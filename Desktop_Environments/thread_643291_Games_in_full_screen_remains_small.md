### Post by eriqjaffe on 2007-12-20
Is this using a CRT or LCD display?  Some/most/all LCDs behave this way when they're not running at "native" resolution.  If the game's  fullscreen setting attempts to set the monitor to a lower resolution than your standard environment uses, you can see this sort of thing.  Used to happen in Win98 on my old Vaio laptop - I had to use the Fn-F4 key to "stretch" the image to fill the screen.
