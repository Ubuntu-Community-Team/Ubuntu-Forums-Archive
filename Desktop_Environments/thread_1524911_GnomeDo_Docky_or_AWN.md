### Post by Roasted on 2010-09-11
Hate to bump this but, why not? :P

I've been a hardcore Docky user, but lately I've been playing with AWN. I feel both of them are extremely solid, but at the same time I feel like they are intended for slightly different audiences. For example, Docky craves ease of use, hence the sheer lack of customizable options in the menu, whereas Awn at least has some extra options in a very easy to follow menu that users who want more options can utilize. So for someone who wants to tweak their dock quite a bit, Awn may be up their alley, whereas if you just want to install a dock and never touch it again, perhaps Docky is right for you.

Both are solid projects and both respond great. But I'm beginning to like my tweak-able options a little bit in Awn, so I may be sticking with it for some time now.

Note - my experiences are with both up to date PPA versions of both Docky and Awn and not "Gnome-Do's docky" or an older version of Awn in the repos.
