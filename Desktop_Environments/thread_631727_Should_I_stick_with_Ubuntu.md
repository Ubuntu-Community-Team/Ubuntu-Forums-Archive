If you are tired of GNOME and KDE, you might try some alternate WMs like Fluxbox, IceWM, fvwm, or one of the many others out there.  They can be much more customizable and challenging than GNOME and KDE.  Search for window manager in Synaptic and you will get a list of several to try.

### Post by DJiNN on 2007-12-06
If you're tired of Gnome (& i can see why that could happen, although i think Gnome is pretty cool) then maybe give either Xubuntu 7.10 a try or perhaps the XFCE version of Linux Mint? 

In fact, if you install the Gnome (Daryana - Linux Mint 4.0) version of Mint, and then install OpenBox (via Synaptic etc) then you can have the option of using OpenBox as your WM, but still using Nautilus & some Gnome deps to get a really snappy but somewhat different DE experience....

Other than that, Maybe try one of the Enlightenment distros out there. I personally don't get on with Enlightenment as i feel it's just too big and unwieldy, but you never know.... it may be just right for you. 

KDE is OK.... it's always been very fast to me, but just comes over as somewhat "Plastic" when compared to Gnome or XFCE etc. 

Whatever you try.,.,.... good luck & have fun! :)
