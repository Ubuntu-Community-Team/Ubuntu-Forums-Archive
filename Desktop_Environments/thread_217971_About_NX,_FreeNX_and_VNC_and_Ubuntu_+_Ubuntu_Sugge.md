### Post by gevans on 2006-07-17
Just thought I would drop a little note in here and say that after spending the majority of my weekend attempting to get VNC working and failing (never had a problem before) and then trying to get FreeNX working to no avail, I finally went to nomachine.com and downloaded their server and client and had NX up and running in less than 10 minutes.

Works flawlessly and I can connect to my Linux box (my first one that actually works as advertised [ Ubuntu 6.06 Server with Gnome ] )flawlessly from my Mac and my Windows machines. :)

Thus far Ubuntu 6.06 has impressed me. Being a NetBSD guy typically and absolutely loving NetBSD, I can say now that I like Ubuntu as much as NetBSD.

Good job, just wish that FreeNX would have worked out of the box as does the nomachine.com version.

One of the things that I think would make Ubuntu better is a slight redesign of the Synaptic Package Manager. It is good as it is for people that know what they are doing, but what would make it even better would be a Basic mode, for the every day user.

As an example, there are at least 3 or 4 different vnc servers and clients in there. That's fine when someone has a preference of which one they use, but what about Joe User that wants that functionality but doesn't know much?  That is where a "Basic" mode oculd come in. It would just say "VNC" and install a preconfigured package that "just worked" without any extra configuration.  This could apply to many packages, maybe that is the way it is supposed to work now, but it didn't in my case, for whatver reason and I think a basic mode where the user didn't have multiple choices would be good.

Anyway, overall I like Ubuntu :)
