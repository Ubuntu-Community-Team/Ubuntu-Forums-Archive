### Post by Takis on 2005-11-06
When you say you burnt the iso image again, do you mean you downloaded the image again, or did you just copy it across to your other computer and burn that? Your image file itself may be corrupt. You may like to a) run an md5 check on the iso image, and b) burn at a slow speed, such as 1x or 2x.

If you need help with either of these, ask - or if you can't be bothered waiting, search the forums, there's a lot of info on how to do both.
