### Post by jlx on 2005-11-23
I'm not trying to compare Songbird with Rhythmbox. I'm just saying that is going to be an alternative to iTunes for Gnome users (I know Amarok, but, though you can use it in Gnome, is for KDE and you have to install all the libraries). I have a huge music collection (about 32 Gb) and for me is hard to find an application that let me manage such collection in a confortable way. Most of them just crash, and the others doesn't have enough options to categorize my music my way. iTunes has many options and is quite simple. The interface is great (like everything Apple does, we must admit it), you can add your podcasts, buy music, buy videos, add music to your iPod. It's a great music center. And I hope Songbird be close to that. I know all the alternatives for Gnome (Quod Libet, Banshee, BMPx, XMMS, also tried Amarok, Rhythmbox, etc) but just Amarok is close to perfection, and is for KDE (lots of libraries, some crashes, etc). Maybe Rhythmbox is perfect for you and that's great, but I still haven't found what I'm looking for...
