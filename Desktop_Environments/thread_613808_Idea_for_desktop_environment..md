Hello world!!!

This is my first post on Ubuntu forums. Before I begin my post, I would like to say that I have been thoroughly impressed with the capabilities of Ubuntu and with the support I got while I was trying to set up my operating system. I intend to help others at least as much as I was helped, and have already started aiding members of my family and friends with installing and configuring Ubuntu.

To the point:
While I was playing about with Beryl, I just got the idea for a desktop envirionment with great usability and impressive graphics. (Please don't laugh :) ).

How about a spherical environment? The sphere could be divided into grids, to emulate folders and you could switch from grid to grid in a fluid animation just like in Beryl. There could be a sector in the center of the grid that will be / and the subfolders could branch out. Also, if necessary, you could switch between multiple sphere environments.

There could also be the potential for adding cool graphic effects, such as asteroids and spaceships in a space theme, resizable icons (as in Gnome) or icons in the forms of primitive 3d objects such as cubes and spheres, ranging in size and color depending on level of importance and even physics, such as bouncing icons around or throwing them into another sector of the sphere (or galaxy, if you like the space-theme).

There are many other tweaks and modifications currently going through my mind, such as a sun in the "core" of the sphere, representing the root directory, etc.
Also, you could add sidebars and the like, in such a way as to make it look as if you are navigating in a vehicle/spaceship.

This all may sound a little vague and hard to accomplish, but by witnessing the achievements of Beryl, Emerald and even Gnome, I think something like my idea is possible to materialize.

Anyway, if someone skilled decides to undertake this project please count me in, as I have many more ideas with which we could improve the interface and I have some 3d modelling skills and knowledge with digital image editing. Also, I could even do something for sound effects, although I should probably leave that to someone more experienced than myself :).
My coding skills are still inadequate for such an endeavour, but I intend to learn more.
Even if you want me excluded from the project, please just give me some credit for the original concept.

That's all for now, sorry for tiring you :).
