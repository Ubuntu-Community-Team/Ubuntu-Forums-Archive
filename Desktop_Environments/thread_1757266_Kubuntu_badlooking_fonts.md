### Post by PaulW2U on 2011-05-13
Have you played around with the font settings at all?

During the last few months I've been able to get almost perfect looking fonts, not as good as Gnome's but almost. Some applications have shown a greater improvement than others.

On the Font Settings page of System Settings > Application Appearance > Fonts I enabled **anti-aliasing** and set **Use sub-pixel** rendering to RGB and **Hinting style** to Slight. On the Advanced tab of **Desktop Effects** I set the scale method to Crisp.

A couple of days ago I changed all my fonts from Ubuntu to DejaVu and to improve the appearance of Konsole I changed the font to DejaVu Sans Mono 9.5.

I hope there's something there that you haven't tried. It's worth the effort as I noticed such an improvement that I decided to stay with Kubuntu for the foreseeable future.

Hope that helps.
