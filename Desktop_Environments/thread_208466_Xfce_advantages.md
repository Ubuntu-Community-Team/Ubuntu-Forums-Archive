### Post by Zelut on 2006-07-03
I prefer Xfce on my notebook for its speed & minimalistic approach to the desktop.  I prefer minimal menus & clutter, and that's what I find in Xfce.  It is also better on speed, as you mentioned.

On that hardware I would run Xfce as it will definitely be more responsive.  You can still load the optional gnome/KDE apps that you're looking for, but launching then from Xfce will use limited resources and keep your machine responsive.
