### Post by euchrid on 2007-12-03
I know this is a seriously old thread, but I came across it in a Google search (for 'best' terminals, just to see) - I thought others might find what I turned up to be useful:

Firstly, I came across [this site]("http://lifehacker.com/software/command-line/windows-and-linux-terminal-performance-comparison-297363.php"), which links to [this site]("http://martin.ankerl.com/2007/09/01/comprehensive-linux-terminal-performance-comparison/"), giving a hotly debated guide to the *fastest* terminals; surely this would count towards what is 'best', particularly for programmers? It is certainly a good starting point for deciding which terminals to try out.

Here is a run down of my own, personal experience in case it helps others out or encourages debate:

I have tried pretty much all of the ones that I could find in the repositories through Synaptic, and I am now fairly confident that Konsole and XFCE Terminals are pretty much the easiest to read and use ( am running Xubuntu with GNOME and KDE installed), and to get going without any fuss. Konsole seems to load quickest out of the two, which is good if you keep having to re-open it or are just fussy.

rxvt loaded pretty quick, but has some difficulty displaying things correctly (which I'm sure can be put right, but with about 20 terminals to use, why bother?) I thought xterm had the nicest colours, but it definitely seemed slower than Konsole or XFCE Terminal. Gnome Terminal was fine, but I thought it loaded a little slower than the others (perhaps it's quickest on GNOME, as logic would hopefully dictate). Why Konsole should load faster when it has more features (and other KDE applications aren't as quick) is a mystery, though.

Please note, I am not talking about how quick these terminals are to run programs - only how quick they are to load either from another terminal or from the menu (I tried both methods over several different sessions for all terminals mentioned). All of this is personal rather than comprehensive experience, so take it as you find it, and add to it for the benefit of others if you are so inclined.

However, I found Yakuake the most useful terminal; it loads the quickest, and completely disappears. Yet the fact that it disappears when hidden means that I don't use it like other terminals; it doesn't show up with Alt+Tab, and something about the way it hides means that I don't like running things like music players or file managers or other things I want to look at from time to time. It is brilliant, though, for short commands, like top or htop, or reading man pages quickly, that sort of thing. There is no practical reason that I can see that would prevent you from using it as your only, or a normal terminal, but I get a kind of buggy 'feeling' about it from the way it drops down initially to the weird rendering of the tabs (called shells). It crashed a couple of times, but I'm pretty confident that was because I was doing a lot of stupid things at once in different terminals! It restarted easily, and I haven't had any problems since. Also, I'm running XFCE, so that could be a factor...

eterm is pretty, with its groovy transparency features, but I found it a little slower and less immediate feeling, and its display of curses applications was not appealing... Otherwise, it was the most configurable of the 'normal'/'old school' style terminals.

XFCE Terminal and xvt have one drawback in that backspace doesn't work when you use 'screen' - but a lot of people won't need or want screen in XFCE Terminal, because it handles tabs nicely (although there is some debate about whether tabs actually use more memory).

mrxvt is old school looking, like rxvt and xvt, but seemed somehow faster. mrxvt has tabs, too, but none of these three terminals do nice displays for curses programs.

I couldn't see a lot of difference in use between aterm, wterm or even xterm (which in the links above comes out slowest), and I would lump them in with mrxvt, rxvt, and xvt as part of the 'old school' set. They are all probably best for just running command and programs that you don't want to look at, but yakuake does a good job of that, too (and with tabs and split screens). 

I didn't get on with Tilda, but I don't know why - it was a while ago. It was probably just aesthetic, as it didn't fit in with my desktop, although it should have, being small and drop down. Perhaps it just made me want to play Quake. Anyway, I can't remember anything bad about it.

So, Konsole seemed the 'best' overall, but your particular uses may dictated something different. Another factor I have not gone into here is opening applications in a terminal from another application (such as the customisable right-click menu in Thunar or Nautilus). Konsole seemed as good as any for this, but xterm was too slow and froze mid-command on a couple of commands from Thunar. XFCE Terminal also did well with accepting external commands.

I won't go in to any more, as I've blathered on enough.

There are, of course, a lot more terminal emulators around. Hope this helps someone else pick the 'best'...
