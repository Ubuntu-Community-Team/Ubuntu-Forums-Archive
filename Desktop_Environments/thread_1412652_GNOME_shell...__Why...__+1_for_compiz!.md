### Post by Zorgoth on 2010-02-21
So, I just tried out Gnome-Shell and found that it seemed to have absolutely no redeeming features.  Now, I know that this is still early days in the development, but the animations were wooden, it zooms your desktop even if you just want to open a calculator, and it adds not one, but *two* extra clicks to just opening a standard application!  And I am supposed to give up compiz for that when GNOME 3 comes out?!  I don't think so...

I think that looking at the direction GNOME has been going in, there should be a concerted effort to make a 3D GNOME fork based on compiz with a shiny new panel with proper transparency and everything that's integrated with compiz (with a widget like xfapplet for xfce so we can use gnome panel applets) and maybe with glx-dock (just because it's awesome).  If canonical got behind something like that I think we would get a much more forward-looking and customizable system then the bizarre interface GNOME is trying to come up with.

Just bouncing the idea and seeing if anyone thinks anything of it.  Probably not.
