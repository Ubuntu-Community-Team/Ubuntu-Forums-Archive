### Post by jackdirt on 2006-02-04
If you are on a  fresh install
-open up synaptic package manager
-enable global and common repositories
-search for fglrx
   -install 
    fglrx-control
    fglrx-kernel-source
    xorg-driver-fglrx
 and I installed xorg-driver-fglrx-dev

-open up terminal
-sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
-now select fglrx as your driver
 go through all the semantics of keyboard mouse blah blah
do not autodetect your monitor manually select your resolution and don't display refresh rates (this is what i did and it worked)
after you are done
sudo reboot
and voila
no more 640x480 res for me
