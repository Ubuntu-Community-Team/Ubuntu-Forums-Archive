### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-12-16
Could be a power-supply problem.  The 5V standby power has crapped out.  This would drain the motherboard battery and with no standby power the front power button will not work.

In a pinch you can run a paperclip on the backside of the power connector (20 or 24 pin) from the GREEN wire to a BLACK wire.  This will turn on the power supply and boot your computer.  Be careful not to short any thing else with the paperclip.

Good luck.
