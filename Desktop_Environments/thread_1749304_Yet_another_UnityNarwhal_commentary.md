### Post by Shoopity on 2011-05-04
Forgive me if this has been beaten to death.  Forgive me if there is a better place to put this and feel free to move to the proper place.

A little background: played with various Linux distros since '99, usually for never more than a month.  Came to ubuntu about a month ago and I'm really trying to stick with it this time (I even put it on my Netbook).  I really liked Maverick; seeing things like wobbly windows had that cool factor (in Windows XP, I have ALL the special effects turned off, and I don't have Vista or 7), then with Emerald, I could make it do all the things Windows 7 does, but tweak it even more.  So I upgrade Ubuntu without knowing they made the change to Unity and it really surprised me.  Also know that I hate icon grouping; I always turn it off in XP and I hate how they handle it Vista/7.

But, I always try to have an open mind about new UI's.  Here is a list of things I feel were bad decisions with Unity:
1) Removal of customization (or at the very least, the ease of customizing).  The original Office Ribbon BLEW my mind.  Sure, being forced to relearn where stuff was and being forced to click 3~4 times to do what used to take me 2~3 clicks is annoying but now I am unable to create my own menus like I used to be able to (at an application level).  Removing functionality in newer versions of something is a bad idea 9 out of 10 times.
With Unity I can't choose where to put the Dash; I can't choose what to put on the Dash (and applets don't work anymore, just indicators).

2) Turning on special effects, Unity no likey.  Every time I've turned something on like Wobbly Windows, the Dash disappears.  It's still functional and it re-appears when I restart X, but most of the time when I make a change in CCSM, Unity freaks out.  I know not everyone experiences this, but this happens to me even though I'm on a fresh install of Ubuntu (new, blank HDD).  If they're trying to get Joe Average to come to Linux, these sorts of things are turn-offs.
I'm going to throw in the pain of turning on the Cube and Cube rotate in this section.  The messages make it appear that you can't use the Cube and Unity at the same time.  After some finagling, I got it to work, but from my perspective, it's something that used to work flawlessly and now it's giving me a headache.

### Post by Shoopity on 2011-05-04
3) At first I thought it was kind of cool how the Dash worked, it's sort of like a shared Title Bar or menu bar while combining the functionality of a system tray/notification area.  But then I was doing some work in a terminal while having a maximized Firefox under it.  I needed to get to a window under Firefox.  Normally I'd just minimize Firefox (that's faster for me than alt+tab through the list of open programs which can, at times, be rather large) except... I can't do that now.  Now I have to click on Firefox so the Dash will show it's menu button, then I can minimize.  Just another click I have to do, kind of hearkens to my problems with the Office Ribbon; why are you making me do more work than I used to.  I will gladly sacrifice 17 pixels of screen space that the Dash saves (by combining a panel with a title/menu bar) for giving each and every window its own title/menu bar at all times (maximized or reduced).

4) The gosh darn grouping of the Launcher.  And as far as I can tell, I can't not group icons.  So if I have three Nautilus windows open (but minimized), first I have to click the button once to get a list of those windows open, then I have to click again to actually pick the window I want.  Or, what if I want to open a fourth Nautilus window?  I can't just click on the icon in the Launcher, I have to use the programs New Window function.

5) This is more of a Narwhal complaint than a Unity one.  Emerald doesn't work... at least not easily.  I've gone through all the hoops of compiling it myself, and I've gotten it working, but I'm extremely surprised they upgraded Compiz without having some sort of fancy window decorating option available.  If I'm wrong in this, if Compiz has the ability to give me Aero looking windows, please tell me where and I'll retract this one.

Yes, I realize I can just pick classic when I log in.  But I'm trying to show how Unity is, in its current state, inherently not the best choice.  Sure, some people may love it as it fits their personal likes/dislikes, and there are aspects to Unity I like.  But due to the lack/difficulty of customizing Unity, I either choose to use Unity with all the things that irk me about it, or not at all.  I want to show that I'm not just complaining because it's something new and I'm not used to it.  Just like the Ribbon, I'm unable to do things I used to do (e.g. applets), and somethings I have to do more work than I used to.  As far as application launching, in my somewhat limited experience with the Dash, I have yet to see it have functionality that Gnomenu didn't already have.
