### Post by PierGroup on 2008-01-16
Newbie to Ubuntu. I've searched the forums but can't find this problem:

I started with Feisty Fawn and that was great. Just upgraded to Gutsy and now all deselected text and icons which are (correctly) greyed out are almost invisible, they are so faint. All other displayed text and graphics, and colours, are absolutely fine.

This faintness affects all applications, menus, Desktop - everything. 

I have tried various settings of Theme, resolution and font without any effect. It feels as though the attribute for grey has been assigned to a fainter shade than is desirable. Is there a central control I can tweak? (using Gnome 2.20.1)

### Post by PierGroup on 2008-01-18
Forgot to add that the display is an Acer AL1716 LCD. What I can't understand is that the greyed-out text was perfect under Feisty but near-invisible under Gutsy. There MUST be a control for this somewhere!

### Post by PierGroup on 2008-01-23
SOLVED: Video driver was using Ubuntu release. The Restricted Drivers Manager offered a proprietary ATI Video Graphics Driver which, when selected, solved the problem. Greyed-out text and icons now display correctly, and Totem Movie Player now works again!

Thanks for the Forum, people. The hint for the solution came from another topic, but the throughput of technical issues generally is a great help.

(The driver changed sometime during the upgrade from Feisty to Gutsy. I am guessing that there was no choice previously.)
