### Post by VanillaMozilla on 2012-05-16
Check for hard drive activity.  It may be indexing your hard drive or performing some other kind of maintenance like that.  There's a service called Nepomuk that causes some performance problems, and another one called akonadi, that sometimes cause problems instead of solving them.  If problems persist, you could look into those.  I don't think you can just uninstall them outright, though, as at least part of nepomuk is essential for some programs.  Don't ask me for more details.  That's all I know.
