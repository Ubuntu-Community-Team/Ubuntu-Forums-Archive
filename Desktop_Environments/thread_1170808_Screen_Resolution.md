### Post by sideaway on 2009-09-21
Use a terminal to run;

gksudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf

Then find the section "Screen", if it isn't there, just c&p the below into the bottom of xorg.conf, change the "Unknown" to your device name and ALL the 1440x900 to your respective resolution. Hope this helps :)

```
Section "Screen"
        Identifier      "Default Screen"
        Device          "Unknown"
        Monitor         "Generic Monitor"
        DefaultDepth    24
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           1
                Modes           "1440x900"
        EndSubSection
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           4
                Modes           "1440x900"
        EndSubSection
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           8
                Modes           "1440x900"
        EndSubSection
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           15
                Modes           "1440x900"
        EndSubSection
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           16
                Modes           "1440x900"
        EndSubSection
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           24
                Modes           "1440x900"
        EndSubSection
EndSection
```
