### Post by Therion on 2007-10-24
Just a few days ago I decided to reinstall Gutsy.  My first install seemed to be little more than one minor annoyance after another.  Nothing huge, no absolute "deal breaker" issues, but a lot of little things.  It didn't seem all that fast, applications would hang or crash, display issues, sound would come and go, Compiz-Fusion settings were not "sticking"... Things like that. I always managed to "work things out" but I had this nagging feeling that things just weren't up to snuff.  Well, I did what any ex-Windows user would do in such a case:  I reformatted and reinstalled Gutsy from the ground up (64bit) and boy am I glad I did.  Everything installed smoothly, video drivers are no longer a problem, everything is working just like it should.  I'm still sans-boot screen, but I can live with that.  I rarely watch my computer boot to begin with.  

Anyway, post-reinstall, all I can say is... **WOW**!!!  Now... Now I see what all the fuss is about!  Good lord it's fast!! Compiz-Fusion rocks, every app is running rock-solid stable, my sound works like a champ, I can stream my favorite radio station, play movies, my iPod integrates...  And, just to top it all off, I was stunned to find that my secondary hard drive is read/write accessible even though it's formatted with NTFS!  While it still holds my WinXP install, I've suddenly got tons of extra room for back ups and general storage.  I nearly fell out of my chair.  Color me convinced, Gutsy rocks, babe!!

I also wanted to send out a general :KS Thank You :KS to the forums and the people here that have helped me get going (nay, HOOKED!!) on Linux and Ubuntu in particular.  I'm one of "You" now...
