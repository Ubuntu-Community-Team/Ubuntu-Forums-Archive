### Post by xdevnull on 2006-11-13
I just moved over to gnome after using KDE for a while and I have a few general questions about the gnome interface that I was hoping someone could educate me on. First: ctrl-shift + click doesn't seem to do anything.  In the other interfaces I have used one can select a series of items with shift + click and then select another series with ctrl-shift + click, without losing the selection of the first series.  This doesn't seem to work in nautilus, nor does it work in evolution (selecting emails) and I was wondering if gnome does this sort of thing in a different way, or not at all. Obviously this was very handy when wanting to select a series of items without launching the first one.  I really hate the double click=activate (seems a waste of time and energy, not to mention the double click doesn't always register as a double click), so I was wondering if there was a way to turn this ability on in someway.

Anyway, hope someone knows something about this. I've found nautilus very usable, even if it is simpler than konqueror (which is maybe too complex), and I really don't want to run konqueror within gnome just for file management.

### Post by mgmiller on 2006-11-13
The closest I could come to what you want to do is to use shift+click to select the first batch of items and then ctrl+click to select everything else without losing the first items selected.  This made me also try the windows trick of "invert selection" where you use ctrl+click to select the items you don't want and then click edit>invert selection, but this does not seem to be an option in gnome either.
