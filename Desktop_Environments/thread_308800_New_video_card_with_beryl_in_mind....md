### Post by Shatrat on 2006-11-28
Go with something with lots of onboard memory.  As far as Beryl is concerned, a 6800 with 256mb of onboard memory is better than a 7800 with 128mb.  Right now you can only have as many textures as you can store in your video memory in beryl using ithe nvidia beta drivers.  This means that once you've opened up enough windows to fill it up any window you open with be black.  For me with 128mb this seems to be maybe a dozen windows of medium size.  The number goes down with filtering and antialiasing on.

### Post by jgcamp99 on 2006-12-09

Remember one caveat, you get what you pay for.

I'd be more inclined to indicate that a 256 MB video card is fine, but look for a faster gpu. For nvidia, spend a little more and get the 6600 or better. I'll assume you are limited to AGP than PCI-E, ATi is the better choice for faster gpu's in terms of selection for price, nvidia seems to have made a leap towards offering PCI-E and AGP is a rarity. I usually budget around $ 100 for an upgrade on a video card. That way you make enough of a technology leap where it makes a difference and you feel like you got your money's worth.
