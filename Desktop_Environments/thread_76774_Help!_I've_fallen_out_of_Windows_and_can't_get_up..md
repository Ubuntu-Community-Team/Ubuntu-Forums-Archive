### Post by aysiu on 2005-10-15
I turned to books when I first started--I don't recommend them. Linux develops at a rapid pace (improvements happening on a monthly or sometimes weekly basis), so by the time a book about Linux gets published, it's often outdated.

That said, if you absolutely must start with a printed book, I'd recommend *[Linux Desktop Garage](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/0131494198/qid=1129411877/sr=8-1/ref=sr_8_xs_ap_i1_xgl14/103-6031602-0937443?v=glance&s=books&n=507846)*.

You may be interested in a [piece I wrote](http://www.psychocats.net/essays/linuxguide.php), which is everything I wish someone had told me when I first started with Linux.
