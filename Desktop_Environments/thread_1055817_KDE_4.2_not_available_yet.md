### Post by gnuvistawouldbecool on 2009-02-04
Well, to get rid of errors like that when I did it I just uninstalled KDE then reinstalled it.  But, that was on top of standard Ubuntu so that wasn't a problem.  In your case if you have enough space I would recommend installing Ubuntu-desktop or Xubuntu-desktop through apt-get to get some sort of graphical interface, then trying uninstalling and reinstalling kubuntu-desktop.

Someone probably knows a better way than this though, so this might not be your best option.  In fact in terms of sheer brute force and lame hacking, it probably sucks as a method of fixing a problem.

Anyway, links that may help:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome)
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/xfce](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/xfce)
Second link is graphically, but simple enough to do with apt-get from the ctrl-alt-f1.
