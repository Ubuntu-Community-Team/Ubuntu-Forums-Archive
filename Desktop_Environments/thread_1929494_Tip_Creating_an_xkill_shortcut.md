### Post by Gaygerbil on 2012-02-22
Not sure how many users are aware of the xkill command which turns your mouse into a killing machine for those super unresponsive programs.

This is especially useful since opening up a task manager with unresponsive program can be slow and painful. Instead creating a proper keyboard shortcut for Xkill will allow you to simply click on the program with your mouse, this works even in fullscreen programs that have crashed!

Simply assign a keyboard shortcut like Windows - X or something to the command xkill and viola!

Just thought I'd drop this tip for people who weren't aware of it already, since it seems not too many people are aware of it yet and to me personally this is one of the biggest perks in Linux.
