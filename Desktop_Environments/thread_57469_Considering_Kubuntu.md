### Post by adrislayer on 2005-08-16
well, don't worry, everthing is free. With the package manager everthing goes smoothy. You don't have to compile everthing.

You may have to add some backports list to have everthing you want, very easy to do, just follow the guide of ubuntu and use kwrite instead of gedit.

Kubuntu is well made but it has some irritating bugs. But, you only have to look in this forum and quickly you will learn how to do or fix things.

Ubuntu or Kubuntu were for me the best ever made distribution in linux. I'm quite newbie with linux and I could install it without any problems. It is also light, only one cd. You don't have to download 4 or more cds with a lot of packages you will never use. Well, at the begining, you may spend some time installing different packages and learn how to use apt-get (very easy as well). But once you reach that point, you will never let ubuntu go.

The differences between ubuntu and kubuntu is only kde and gnome. But as I said before, kubuntu has some little bugs...

That's all I have to say.
