### Post by woodyg on 2011-08-02
I'm using Ubuntu 11.04, with its launcher and attempt to rethink navigation. No matter how pretty the launcher is... it is still one or two steps backwards when it comes to multitasking that require someone to jump between documents and applications, back and forth. With simple tabs (e.g. at the bottom as in Windows) any other document or application is only one click away + it provide complete overview of things. 

In my new and shiny Ubuntu on the other hand, things are less satisfying. If I for example have Evolution open, with the main window, another window for an open email and a third one for the reply I'm writing plus two spreadsheets, then navigating between them is a nightmare. Every time I go from one Evolution window to another I need to click the Evolution launcher icon to get all three windows displayed in front of me and then click again on the window I want to go to. **That's 2 clicks instead of 1 **(though it feels more because it is so incredibly awkward). If I instead want to move from Evolution to one of my spreadsheets, then I click the launcher icon to go to Calc, but it will switch straight to one of them, meaning that if it is the wrong one then I need to click the launcher icon again and then select the correct spreadsheet. **That is 3 clicks instead of 1!** (and again, it being so annoying that it feels even more than three clicks). 

**I truly hope that there is some sort of clever way of producing tabs or magically solve this annoyance**, and that this post is simply a result of me not having realised something obvious, because otherwise I may not last long with Ubuntu... Which would be a shame as I really like everything else about it (not the new "Application menu" of course, which is almost as bad)
