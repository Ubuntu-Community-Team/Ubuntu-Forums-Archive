 The reason they are stacked like that is because the KDE panel is about twice as tall as the Winblows taskbar, and if the applications in the taskbar were stretched to the height of the taskbar they would be huge.  If you haven't noticed the buttons are not manually resizable in Winblows either, and that is the generally accepted norm.  I don't know of any window manager on any platform that allows that.

However if you do want the application buttons in the taskbar to be side by side only like in Winblows you can right click in an empty space on the panel and click "Configure Panel"  the dialog that comes up will have an option to change the Size of the panel if you change it from Normal to Small then the panel will resize to one that is similar in size to the Winblows taskbar.
