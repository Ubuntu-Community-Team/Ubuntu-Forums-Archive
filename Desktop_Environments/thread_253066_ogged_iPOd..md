### Post by stmiller on 2006-09-08
I agree with Bruce. Though the recent lame 3.97b2 is much better, esp with VBR encoding than lame 3.96. Worth compiling the latest lame, IMO, if you are into mp3s. It encodes much faster, too.

The AAC format was developed by the same company who engineered the mp3. 

[http://www.iis.fraunhofer.de/amm/techinf/aac/index.html](http://www.iis.fraunhofer.de/amm/techinf/aac/index.html)

> MPEG-2 AAC is the consequent continuation of the truly successful coding method ISO/MPEG Audio Layer-3 developed in Erlangen.

They actually meant it to replace the mp3 format. It compresses at better quality at lower bit rates, among other improvements. It really is much better, though you are in a coke vs. pepsi debate when talking about this to mp3 fans.

I just wish there was a working aac encoder for Linux. The faac open source encoders don't do so well at all.

### Post by &#12465;&#12452;&#12488; on 2006-09-08
> **skymt said:**
> Actually, I've seen studies that showed LAME MP3s had quality similar to OGG at the same bitrates. Just something to think about.
MP3 is covered by software patents, which makes every company have to pay fees to Thomson Consumer Electronics for being able to decode MP3 files in hardware players. Or operating systems, for that matter (you want an example of what's affected? Then, how about Ubunu?). 

Using Free alternatives like Ogg Vorbis, will lead to better hardware player support, as more people are using it, and will eventually (at least hopefully) lead to the end of the software patented MP3s, which are making things more expensive and complicated.

Just something to think about.
