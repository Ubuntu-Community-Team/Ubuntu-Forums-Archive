Hello,

I have one little problem for my trial to get a job as a transcriber. I've got three days find a solution to this problem !

I have an audio file to transcribe. I am playing it in VLC and using LibreOffice for the transcription. Because speed is one of the goals in processing the document, I need to use hotkeys in VLC (pause/play, rewind, etc). I have activated the hotkeys I need in VLC, but they only work when the VLC window is focused on. What I need is to have these hotkeys usable while typing in LibreOffice.

I know there is Express Scribe for linux, I have it installed but it is very buggy (the audio controls just don't work properly). Also I don't fancy buying a pedal when I can use the num pad on my keyboard.


If anybody has a solution to my problem, I would be very grateful for helping me get the job.

### Post by sean24 on 2013-10-02
Thank you for your replies. I have figured out why they weren't working. It seems that I was trying with the numbers on the numpad and they were probably conflicting with Libreoffice shortcut keys. When I changed them to the PAGE UP and PAGE DOWN keys for example, and restarting VLC, it worked out for me.

I was using Linux Lite, XFCE
