### Post by odrop on 2005-03-28
As some others have mentioned, Enlightenment (the E17 version) is not really ready for public consumption.  Most of the work for the past year or two (i cant remember when they started developing E17), has been on the core libraries for E17.  So while you might not see too much on the WM side of Enlightenment, the core libraries are getting pretty much stable now (I think).  Though whenever that work is done, I assume they'll begin working more on the WM and you'll see more of the functionality you were hoping for.

And Enlightenment was one of the reasons I started using linux years ago also, though I haphazardly stumbled upon it on an ancient Slackware CD I was using, was floored at its beauty and I've been a linux junky ever since.

I was kind of saddened that Enlightenment development seemed lax for awhile, while other desktops began grabbing the spotlight (KDE and Gnome).  But this next version of Enlightenment holds many promises (and eye candy) that might steal me away from KDE.
