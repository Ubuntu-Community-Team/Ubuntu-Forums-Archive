### Post by Stray Wolf on 2012-01-19
I'm submitting this to Ubuntu Brainstorm and it requested a link to these forums for a view of the discussion regarding the topic. So, I'm threading it here to start the discussion.

Left-clicking an icon in the Unity Launcher just opens that app which makes those icons kind of dumb in my book. I mean dumb in the sense that they're one-trick-ponies. I'm proposing dashes for icons in the launcher to make them behave a little smarter. So, if you right-click an icon it opens a dash with app specific options. Right-click on Firefox and it has it's own dash with bookmarks, suggested add-ons, open new instance, open in safe-mode etc... Nautilus opens up to a home folder on left-click, right click should open a dash from the icon/launcher that lists files, folders, operations and extensions. Libre-office could have a dash open on right-click that lists documents, functions and add-ons  too. Pretty much every app I can think of could have it's own dash that could be initiated through it's launcher/icon.

I guess that an icon's dash would also have to contain the current right-click options.

Note: In trying to get my terminology right the thread title should read: "Icons in Unity Launcher should have Dashes".
