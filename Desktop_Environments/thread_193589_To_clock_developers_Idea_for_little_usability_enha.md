### Post by kiwibird on 2006-06-10
I guess this is directed at Gnome-developers / Ubuntu-desktop developers:

Whenever someone else is using my Gnome-ubuntu laptop, they will, at least once within the first half-hour, inadvertently click the clock in the upper-right corner, making the calendar appear and block whatever they were trying to click. They have no idea what they clicked to make it appear. Would it be possible to have, as default, a mode where it fades away after some seconds of non-usage, or maybe has a little X on it to remove it, or perhaps one of those little up-arrows on the top border that pulls it back ("into its shelf") when clicked on, or Something that makes it easier for Gnome-newbies to see how to get rid of it? It's just a minor annoyance, but seems easy to fix.
