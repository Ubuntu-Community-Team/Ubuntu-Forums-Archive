### Post by arctic on 2006-03-04
What is slow exactly? The boot process, the desktop behavior? Both?

The problem is most probably the RAM. Gnome and KDE love RAM, just like Windows XP. However, there are lightweight solutions for Linux that will dare better on your system than Gnome or KDE as desktop managers. E17 (enlightenment), XFCE4 and fluxbox are low on ressources and will speed up your system. But then... they will not come with all the rings and bells that Gnome and KDE offer. Google for XFCE and Fluxbox or E17 and check what they offer and decide for yourself if they are interesting for you. Btw.: Although those alternatives do not have icons on the desktop by default, icons can be placed on the desktop nonetheless. It just needs some tweaking.

If it is only booting, then disabling some system services that you don't need will speed up the process.
