### Post by aysiu on 2006-10-07
Read this:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome)

You can run Gnome-based applications in KDE and vice versa. The non-native applications may load more slowly and look ugly, but they'll work.

If you install both desktop environments, your applications menus will get really cluttered up.

Other than that, they won't interfere with each other, for the most part.
