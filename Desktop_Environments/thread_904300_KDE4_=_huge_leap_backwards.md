### Post by Zorael on 2008-08-30
One must remember that KDE 4 isn't "based" on KDE 3, being a complete rewrite/refacturing. 3 has had since 2002 to mature, fix bugs and produce features, whereas 4 is up-and-coming in every sense. I'd still call it ready for everyday desktop use, but there's still a lot (of features) left to port/rewrite, as well as tune.

I like to avoid using the term scalability, but 4 is designed to be. The classic example: the sound system in 3 is based on the aRts frontend, the development of which was abandoned somewhere in 3's release cycle. Phonon is KDE's own child. Correct me if I'm wrong.

In my opinion, 4.1 is a huge improvement over 4.0, and I have very high hopes for future 4.x releases. The only thing that keeps me from using KDE 4 on all my systems is [the video garbage bug](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=905380).
