### Post by djme on 2011-06-06
Hi All,
I have been using Ubuntu for the past year or so having been really impressed with 10.10. I recently upgraded to 11.04 and couldn't believe the Canonical guys chose the rubbish Unity interface as the default interface. Why such a radical switch? I am sure most people would agree that there wasn't too much wrong with the previous Gnome setup in 10.10. If netbook users need more screenspace they can choose to auto-hide menus, view apps in full screen etc. 
Unity, to me, has proven to be a real eye-sore and is buggy as f*ck. Sometimes apps don't launch and other times it can take numerous clicks to drill in and find the app you want. I have read that the intention is to use Unity going forward and not provide an option for the classic login. As soon as I heard this, I researched for a new Linux distro and have settled on Linux Mint. Anyone who knows about Ubuntu will be aware of the advantages of Mint over other O.S's (including Ubuntu) so I won't bang on about them here, needless to say I am delighted with the setup in Mint.
Out of curiosity, does Canonical take into account any feedback from it's users? Was there a poll completed before Unity was rolled into the final release of 11.04? Is there a poll amongst users to decide if it should be discontinued? The customer always gets what the customer wants and I definitely do not want Unity.
It is such a pity as I really like/liked Ubuntu and would certainly consider reinstalling it once Unity and the associated bugs are striped out. Canonical must remember, even though Ubuntu has a huge user base, it is just as easy to switch from Ubuntu as it is to switch to it. 
Like I say, I'm off to enjoy Linux Mint but I'll keep an eye on the evolution of Ubuntu and will be eager to see what Canonical roll out in the next Ubuntu release. P.s I am aware of Mint's dependency on Ubuntu and have no problem with installing an OS which has taken the newest version of Ubuntu and given it a well needed scrub and slapped a new name on it.
