### Post by mitya on 2008-09-22
Good day.

I am using Kubuntu from 7.04, now I have 8.04, so it is my third Kubuntu release.

Every time I install a new release, or upgrade a system, I see lots of 'BROKEN TRANSLATION' messages in some KDE apps where numeral adjectives are used (I use KDE only, so can't say a word about Gnome desktop, sorry). Ktorrent and Amarok are among them (I use them more often than anything else). 

A few months later some of these bugs are fixed &#8212; the updates are installed. But every time the new Kubuntu release is out &#8212; these bugs are back! All of my favourite 'BROKEN TRANSLATIONS', oh yeah!

So my question goes to those Canonical guys who supervise the overall translation of the system, and to those, who say "Yes, this is A-RELEASE-READY-STATE-OF-THE-SYSTEM! Let's release it!"

Hey, guys, what's up?

I DO NOT complain about the fact that not all programs are translated, but the fact that users meet the same bugs again and again with EVERY NEW RELEASE.

I was always thinking that the bug once solved is solved forever. So what's the matter with Kubuntu?

I do not believe that translations are changed too quickly for maintainers to be in time with them (I think Amarok is pretty same as two years ago).

I think it is time to make N.07 and N.12 releases and call them 'Kubuntu x.12 FIXED-RUSSIAN-TRANSLATION-EDITION'. (You may insert any non-english language instead of 'RUSSIAN'.)

Guys, can't you see that the language is the face of the system? The way the system speaks to the user.
What does the user think when he or she sees all that old language bugs over and over again?

What is Canonical's policy of supervising the translations? Who is responsible for this problem?

Is that the way Canonical is going to punch M#crosoft out of desktop computers?

I don't think so.
