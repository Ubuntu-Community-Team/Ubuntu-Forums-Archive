### Post by reuben on 2005-07-10
Hi bluemeanie, I advise you to install kde. It is a lot slicker (and more XP like) than gnome. I find gnome to be very primitive on the surface.

To do the isntallation, open the "package manager - synaptic" (I don't know where it is in the gnome menus; should be system, or utilities, or similar.) Search for kde. Install. Reboot. When you are prompted for your username and password, click the "menu" or "session" icon (I don't use the gdm boot manager either!!) and choose kde.
