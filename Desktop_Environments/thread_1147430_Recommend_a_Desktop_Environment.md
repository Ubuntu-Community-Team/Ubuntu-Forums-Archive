### Post by killermist on 2009-05-03
I'm looking for a desktop environment as close to KDE 3 as KNOPPIX used a couple years ago.
Since KDE3 has been officially abandoned, I've tried KDE4, and Gnome, and have been unsatisfied with both.
KDE4 implements too many of the features made popular (not good, just popular) by the MacOS series and XP/Vista.  While the masses seem to love these new features, I and my processor generally don't.  Further, in "improving" the interfaces of things like Konqueror, lots of behaviors that I was used to have been changed in ways that really don't make me happy.
I tried Gnome for a while and it's interface was more stomach-able, it kept reminding me of Finder from Macs of 1991.
I upgraded to Jaunty recently, and then went the Kubuntu direction again to give KDE4 a chance again, but I can't stand the new configuration of the k-menu.  I didn't want a vista-like menu.  Worse, my time with Gnome got me looking up for the clock, so I had to move the panel to the top, then got confused looking for the task bar at the bottom of the screen, where Gnome had it, so I had to spawn a new panel to fit the taskbar, and tray, and now the closk isn't in the corner where it should be because it auto-expands to ginormous proportions because nothing else is squeezing it back into the corner where it's supposed to be and there's no "blank expanding divider" applet to force it back into the corner.
I just can't understand how from KDE3 that was so strong, rugged, and functional, the developers could have taken KDE so far in so many wrong directions so fast.

Long story short (too late, I know), I'm looking for something else.

Primary concerns, in order are:
Responsive, low CPU utilization (fancy graphical task switcher not necessary)
Look and feel similar to KDE 3, KDE 3.5, or even Windows 95/98.
A couple always visible applets for CPU monitoring, and weather.
The virtual desktop switcher should actually look like it has something to do with desktop switching instead of just looking like a jewel decoration thing.
