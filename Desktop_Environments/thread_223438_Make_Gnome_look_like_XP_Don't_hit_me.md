### Post by becominglumberg on 2006-07-26
From my experience (which admittedly is very little)  you will get a much better head start starting from KDE. I prefer GNOME, but KDE is much more windows-esque in its appeal.

If you are set to remain in gnome, the first step would be to re-arrange the panels. One on the bottom, get rid of the one on the top. There is one panel that only uses one drawer menu-*something* you can add, and that will be more windowsish. Then do some icons, then the application list. Limit it to one desktop and take away the switcher. Then put systray and clock.

Go to home, make some file structure like 'My Docs' etc, and add icons to his desktop. Maybe event he gdesklet launcher if his head wont explode if he has some mac-ness on there.:p 

You also might want to google for the instructions on how to add the new gnome bar from SLED into Ubuntu. Its very xp-ish, and might make him feel right at home.
