### Post by etitor on 2006-08-27
Several threads in Ubuntuforums complain about Firefox being slow on Dapper. I was experiencing that too: in my case, it meant slow starting, very slow pop-upping of new windows when executed through javascript, sometimes slow drawing of a webpage, etc.

Several solutions to this, including SwiftFox and ipv6 disabling, gave me some small benefits in the past. But Firefox was still slow.

I just did something very simple that gave me a Firefox as fast as it works on a comparable Windows machine. I just uninstalled everything Firefox-related ("uninstall completely" in Synaptic), and reinstalled only Firefox (I mean *only* Firefox, not Gnome integration, not anything else Firefoxish). I had backed up my bookmarks.html, of course, which I then recovered.

It is now much swifter than SwiftFox used to be!
