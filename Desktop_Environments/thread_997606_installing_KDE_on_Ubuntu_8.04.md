### Post by wilbbe01 on 2008-11-30
You should be able to have them both installed at the same time.  Prior to logging in you have an option to select your session.  You can then choose between the different window environments prior to logging in.  I personally do not like having them both installed at the same time, because in my opinion KDE is big and a waste of space.  If you're not sure what you want I would stick with gnome.  In the end it is your decision though.  I'm assuming there is a kubuntu live cd.  I would try that and see if kde is something you would want to use before deciding to actually go through and install.

### Post by scottmac112 on 2008-11-30
Just 

"sudo aptitude kdebase".

That's the metapackage for KDE. It'll install KDE without changing any of your settings. To get into it after APT is done, just log out and in the login screen, click "Sessions", then click on KDE.
