### Post by mikodo on 2011-11-03
I used to know how to do this without CCSM (Compizconfig-settings-manager), but cannot remember, anymore. So, if you want to try it with CCSM installed (it's in Synaptic/Ubuntu Software Sources) and have the resources to use it; install it then. Here is how to do it, this way:

a) Go into Preferences (I use Ubuntu 10.04) and don't know what you are using, so, find your way in to it -> Appearance -> Visual Effects -> and choose either "Normal" or "Extra".

b) Go to Preferences again; this time click on CCSM -> General Options -> "Focus and Raise Behavior" and choose the setting as I have shown in the screenshot below. If it is not exactly to your liking, play with the settings and see if you can find something more suitable, for your tastes.

Good Luck!

Oh, I forgot to mention; this doesn't work properly with open web-pages, they get minimized, or your pages do; but, if you are just switching between open windows from your OS, they should then, do what you want. I am not sure how well this works for "Unity", as I have never used it.

### Post by mikodo on 2011-11-04
I did some searching with the default Window Manager for Gnome Desktop in Lucid for "Focus Follows Mouse" as others have called this.

Here is how I used it before the "CCSM" Window Manager:

System -> Preferences -> Windows -> "select windows when the mouse moves over them"

There, that's how I used to do it in 10.04 using the default WM (not CCSM).

Again, with Unity.... I have no clue. ;)
