### Post by OffAxis on 2008-01-15
What does 
```
fglrxinfo
```
return?

can your video card output to that resolution of 1680x1050?
The only chart I can find (which admittedly isn't exhaustive) doesn't list it.
[http://h18000.www1.hp.com/products/quickspecs/12425_na/12425_na.HTML]("http://h18000.www1.hp.com/products/quickspecs/12425_na/12425_na.HTML")
I know your max resolution is well above that, but some of the 'in betweens' usually get left off.

> sudo aticonfig --resolution=1600x1200,...
Are you sure your monitor can do 1600x1200?
[http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/monitors/2007WFP/en/about.htm#Specifications]("http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/monitors/2007WFP/en/about.htm#Specifications")
which, if it was auto detected, is probably why there isn't an entry for it in your xorg.conf


you can try forcing it by eliminating all other resolution entries from a 
```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg
```

CAUTION: you may lose your xserver and have to reboot to a command line off a LiveCD to get it working  again (by forcing the xserver-xorg back down into the 1280x1024 res). I've also heard that you can damage your hardware by forcing options, but I've never personally fried anything; I've just tanked my xserver.
good luck.

### Post by Brazilian Joe on 2008-01-15
Are you on ATI drivers 7.12? If that's the case...

For the life of me I couldn't get more than 1280x1024 from them, on my TravelMate 8210 (which is also 1680x1050). I have read some people managed to force it into submission and run at 1680x1050, while others failed. anyway, it's a known limitation of the driver acknowledged by ATI/AMD than some 1680x1050 screens are unsupported.
In my case the solution was to roll back to 7.11. 
I believe it MAY work if you roll back to 7.11 and use 'xvidtune -show' on a command prompt, use that line on your xorg.conf for the resolution, then reinstall 7.12. MAYBE. 
I prefer to stick with 7.11 until 8.01 comes around.
