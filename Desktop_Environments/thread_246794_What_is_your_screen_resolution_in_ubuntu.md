### Post by VirtuAlex on 2006-08-31
> **sefs said:**
> How many hz should it be at that resolution?  and is this automatic in ubuntu?
If ubuntu correctly detects your monitor it should set it accordingly. If not, it will set it to 60 for safety reasons (not your safety, but your monitor's safety). It is easily fixed by setting refresh rate manually with dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg. Refresh rate depends on your monitor abilities, but the bigger the better for CRT, since it noticeably flickers on 60. I would rather work on lower resoulution than on lower frequency. LCDs usually work fine on 60 cause pixels emit light a little longer, but it accounts for trailing with fast moving objects, so in general cheap LCD is not good for gaming.

### Post by DoktorSeven on 2006-09-01
> **CatKiller said:**
> For some reason, although 1280x960 *should* be the right resolution, on mine at least it leaves gaps at the top and bottom.:???:

That's what your monitor's adjustments are for ;)

And yeah, you have to actually put 1280x960 into /etc/X11/xorg.conf since everyone else seems to think 1280x1024 is natural too... 

```
        
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth           24
                Modes           "1280x960" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
        EndSubSection

```
