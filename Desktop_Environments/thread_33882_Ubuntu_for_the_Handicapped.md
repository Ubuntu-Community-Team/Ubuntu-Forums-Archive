### Post by ltmon on 2005-05-13
There are specialized screen-readers that are available.  They basically read out whatever text is displayed on the screen.

We have to deal with this when coding on high profile websites in AU, because our discrimination laws require us to provide access to those with handicaps.  Screen readers only work well if standards are followed, so there is a dual benefit.

Our testers often use Fangs, which is a Firefox plugin that aims to emulate a screen reader for testing purposes.

L.

EDIT: Oh yeah, computing blind doesn't sound easy at all.  But given the skills of many blind musicians on guitar, piano and other instruments I don't think it's insurmountable.  I guess the difference is that musical instruments give primarily aural feedback whilst computers are primarily visual.
