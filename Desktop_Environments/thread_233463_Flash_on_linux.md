They are already blogging about the progress on Flash 9 for Linux at [Penguin.SWF]("http://blogs.adobe.com/penguin.swf/"). And they are now quite clear about their intended release date: [early 2007]("http://weblogs.macromedia.com/emmy/archives/2006/06/a_pocket_guide.cfm").

> Q. Wasn't there supposed to be a simultaneous release of the Linux version of Flash Player 9?
A. Not exactly. We never said it would sim ship. We did say that we were skipping the v8 port to get a start on porting v9 (formerly known as v8.5). We are working on it, and expect to release in early 2007. Which means a beta should be here very soon. For progress and updates, check Mike's Penguin.SWF blog. 

It's unfortunate that it will be taking so long, since more and more sites are requiring Flash 8 and Flash 9 already. But Adobe's priorities are somewhat understandable.  

For now, you will have to resort to Wine and Firefox for windows + Flash for windows to enjoy demanding sites under Ubuntu:
