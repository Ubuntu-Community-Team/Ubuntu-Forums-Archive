### Post by 3Miro on 2012-02-12
Unity 3D relies on graphical power, while Unity 2D relies on CPU power. For graphical purposes, good GPUs will crush any CPU, and that is the only reason why people buy expensive video cards. Unity 3D is expected to be smoother and faster on machines with decent graphics, that's why it is the default. Unity 2D is for machines with low graphical capabilities, where Unity 3D will be too slow or even impossible to run. Unity 2D is designed to have good response on machines with weak graphics, it is not designed to be faster than Unity 3D.

Basically, what you observe is absolutely normal.
