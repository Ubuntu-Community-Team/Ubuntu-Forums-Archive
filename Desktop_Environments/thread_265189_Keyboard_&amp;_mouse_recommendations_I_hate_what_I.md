### Post by reformist on 2006-09-25
I need to get a new keyboard & mouse, and it's hard to discern good from crap (and there's a lot of crap keyboards and mice out there)! I figured many in the Ubuntu community have the same needs I have. I'm looking for recommendations on good keyboard/mice combos.

I realize just about everything works fine with linux & Ubuntu, except maybe the bluetooth keyboards (especially the Mac bluetooth one). I'm not looking for what works, but what works well. I'm a programmer, hate tons of superfluous shortcut keys I never use, and I hate keyboard lag (I type ~80wpm).

Currently I'm using the [Microsoft wireless optical desktop elite](http://www.microsoft.com/hardware/mouseandkeyboard/productdetails.aspx?pid=016). It has a billion shortcut keys, and the only ones I've found to be useful are the volume control/mute buttons. I bought it because it has a good feel to the keys. Unfortunately, it comes with the dreaded "F Lock toggle" button, where you have to press this stupid F-Lock button to make all of your F1-F12 keys work; otherwise, they're generic useless shortcuts like "Undo, Redo, new, open..." They're not F1-F12 by default, which means I have to hit this button every time I boot. [Synergy](http://synergy2.sourceforge.net/) also gets confused by F-lock.

The keyboard types pretty well and has a built-in pad wrist pad that feels pretty good. The backspace is nice and big, and the home/end/delete buttons are not in weird locations (although, I don't think their configuration is standard).

The mouse feels ok, but the wheel has this mushy feel to it and is very difficult to turn. Some of the buttons are hard to press too (middle-click), which is not a good thing for a device whose buttons you push hundreds of times a day.

This desktop set is also wireless, and while I have rechargable AA batteries so I don't have to keep buying new ones, I hate changing them. They always seem to run low immediately, so I end up having the wireless receiver literally right next to my keyboard so keystrokes don't get dropped.

Maybe I should drop this wireless stuff and just used corded equipment, but I do like having less cords running everywhere. That's the tradeoff, to each his own preference I guess. If I went wireless again, bluetooth would be considered, because that would eliminate this big corded receiver. I'm wary of bluetooth though; I don't have any BT devices but I have support for it, and I hear it can be quirky and annoying (in both windows & linux). Also I think BT devices are much more expensive then regular wireless keyboards.

I've heard split keyboards are better for your hands.

I guess the most important things in a keyboard&mouse are no quirky crap to get in your way, good response on the keys for fast typing, and padding for the wrist.

Any experience with good keyboard/mouse combos?
