### Post by iamhugeinjapan on 2007-02-23
In terms of the way windows are decorated and appear, I still don't think you can go past [Baghira]("http://baghira.sourceforge.net/OS_Clone-en.php") with KDE.

KDE also supports the Mac style of program menus in the top taskbar (provided they are qt apps)

Stir in the OS X wallpapers and icons, Beryl, Avant Window Manager or KXDocker and you have a pretty good OS X clone.


(EDIT: after reading the Aqualooks page I will say that GNOME can support menu taskbar too (for some programs)  but the window look is still superior with Baghira I feel)

(Second EDIT: Looking at screenshots and from experience I think you can take screenshots of your set ups that look very OS X like, but when you actually use them they very quickly feel like cheap imitations so I gave up and now do my own thing)
