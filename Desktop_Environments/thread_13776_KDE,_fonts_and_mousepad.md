### Post by valadil on 2005-02-02
You need to turn on antialiased fonts.  I know its in kde's control panel but I've got no clue where since I prefer gnome.

For the second question I'm gonna assume you meant touchpad.  A touchpad is a cursor device commonly used in laptops.  A mousepad is a piece of fabric that sits under a traditional mouse to ensure that it glides properly and stays clean.  Try playing with the synaptics X11 extension.  I have no idea if it comes with ubuntu since my laptop uses debian and I've never had to play with synaptics on my desktop.  Either way, synaptics should be in apt.  And no, I'm not talkking about the gtk2 frontend for apt.  This is entirely different despite the similar name.
