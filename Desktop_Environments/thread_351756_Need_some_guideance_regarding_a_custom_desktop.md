### Post by Kingstrum on 2007-02-02
<loserboy> took care of the MacOSX bar...
   As for #2:

      * In a blank spot on the top panel, right-click and select "Add to Panel".
      * Under "Desktop & Windows", click once on "Window List" and drag it the top bar.
      * There'll be a dimpled vertical bar, right-click on it and select "Move", dragging it where you want it on the panel.
      * Keep in mind, it'll jump over any other icons that are "locked" (you can see if they are by right-clicking them and seeing if there's a checkmark next to "Lock to panel") or will not pass a group of locked items that butt up against the end of the panel.

   Check out some of the other cool stuff in the "Add to Panel" selections. Personally I hate two bars, especially on small displays and I'm not fond of "shortcuts" to particular apps. My one panel, at the top of the screen, usually goes:

      "Main Menu"|"Window List"|"Notification Area"|"Network Manager"|"Trash"|"Workspace Switcher"|"Window Selector"

   If there's more screen real estate I add some of the geegaws & trinkets. Play around with it and have fun.

   -Kingstrum
