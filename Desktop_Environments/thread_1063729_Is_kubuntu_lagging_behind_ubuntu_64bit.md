### Post by cmnorton on 2009-02-08
Based on fewer than twenty installations of various Ubuntu releases and empirical observation, it seems like Kubuntu lags behind Ubuntu. It is hard for me to pick the areas of lag, other than gut feel.  

The biggest lag area I can describe is Networking/Network Manager area. Ubuntu just seemed to work well with VPN after 6.10, but Kubuntu still has its problems offering VPN in its own menus once you have installed the right components. About six months after the July 2007 reload release, KVpnc started working. To this day, there is no VPN pick available in KNetworkManager.

Which desktop I use is becoming a small concern these days. I like working in the Ubuntu family of releases. I can easily turn the desktop into a workstation by adding build tools and server components. It really just does not make much difference to me if its Gnome or KDE.
