### Post by celloandy on 2005-08-12
To some degree, there's some security through obscurity, here.  Desktop linux users have such a small market share that they're not really a worthwhile target for hackers, at this point.  The downside is that since there's not much in the way of viruses/spyware, people don't write much in the way of antivirus/spyware tools for linux, either.  If you're really worried about it, list all your processes with 'ps aux' at the command prompt, and figure out what all of the prosesses that are running do (Google is good for this).  If something shouldn't be there, kill it with 'killall.'
