### Post by CatKiller on 2019-02-28
> **cybrsaylr said:**
> Did Tap the Copy but have no idea where clipboard is, or if clipboard even exists?

Yes, the clipboard exists. It's a buffer to temporarily store things. In text editors or the file manager or wherever, Ctrl-C (copy) will copy things to the clipboard, Ctrl-X (cut) will move things to the clipboard, and Ctrl-V (paste) will copy things from the clipboard. In the Terminal it's almost the same, except that you also need to use Shift, since those other shortcuts are used for other things. KDE comes with a utility to view the contents of the clipboard without having to paste them anywhere.

There is a separate buffer that gets filled by simply highlighting text. Pressing middle-click on the mouse will copy the contents of that buffer to whatever you middle-clicked on.
