### Post by KdotJ on 2010-10-21
Unity would be fine without Gnome. Have you installed Unity? 
To be honest, you will be absolutely fine leaving it where it is, (probably better off, as gnome is a lot more then just the desktop. E.g. you power manager applet, volume controls etc (which mind you are probably used by Unity anyway)).

So basically, you can just keep Gnome but just boot into unity by default.

To do so, select "Ubuntu Netbook Edition" (Or to that effect) at the login screen. You have to select your username first, then the selection is at the bottom.
