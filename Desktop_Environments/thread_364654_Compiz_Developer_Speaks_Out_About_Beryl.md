### Post by talkingwires on 2007-02-18
I saw [this post]("http://lists.freedesktop.org/archives/compiz/2007-February/001413.html") by David Reveman, one of the main developers of Compiz, on the freedesktop.org mailing list archives. He discusses a number of things, including plans for the future development of Compiz.

One thing that I thought was particularly interesting was his meeting with Quinn Storm, of the Beryl project. Mr. Reveman addresses the assumptions and fears that led the fork of Compiz in first place, dismissing them as unfounded. He also questions whether the Beryl project's short-sighted hacks are doing more harm than good:> Beryl includes temporary solutions and workarounds that paper over
issues in the overall infrastructure. I've been very unwilling to
include such things in compiz as I believe that it hurts the open source
desktop as it hides the real issues and I don't want to do that for my
own projects benefit. Helping other projects by fixing issues where they
should be fixed is how we make the open source desktop unbeatable.
Temporary solutions can be maintained outside the official tree or in
branches for those who need them.I know that Beryl is quite popular right now, but from what I've read on these and other forums, Mr. Reveman is basically right. Nobody on the Beryl project is working on improving the core of the project, Compiz. They did a fork for short-term gains ("pretty" effects, ugly hacks that work but aren't easily maintainable), but in the long-term, they forked alpha software. Remember the parable of building a house on a foundation of sand?> So how does this affect us? I know that the temporary solutions and
workarounds that beryl includes might to some users give the impression
that beryl "works better". That they've also focused on implementing new
effects and taking the existing once even further have meant that
they've gotten a lot of attention recently, while we've been working
really hard here on compiz to get the difficult pieces together so we
can move to the next level. I know that some people are concerned by
this and the fact that they are forking everything we do without giving
anything back but there's nothing to worry about, people will sooner or
later understand where the real work is being done. I can admit that
it's a bit annoying to me too as we could be moving faster forward if
people were just a bit more interested in working together instead of
against each other.
