### Post by lyceum on 2006-10-27
Fluxbox is already there. Just go to 

Synaptic Package Manager (type in your password)

click on "search" & enter "fluxbox"

Choose all the fluxbox items you want, bbmail, bbtime, fbdesk, fluxbox, and fluxcnf. 

Mark these for instalation and when it is done, log out of your session. 

Before you log back in, go to "sessions" and choose "Fluxbox."

The point of Fluxbuntu is to create a light OS for installing on older computers. Changing your session from gnome with Ubuntu really doesn't lighten things up at all. However, I have fluxbuntu installed on an old PII and it works great. 

As for your question, I hope so. I really enjoy fluxbox. I may switch to it on my PC if it becomes offically sanctioned like kubuntu. I think it shows a lot of promise.

cheers! :cool:
