### Post by tedrogers on 2007-01-08
Hi Ubuntuans,

Does anyone else get annoyed with Gnome, but doesn't want to move over to KDE as its basically hideous and is too much like MS Windows for my liking?

I love Gnome, really I do, but it has a few annoyances that I would just like to voice right here.

My particular issue is with Locked items on the panels, and in particular these two things:

1. It is so laborious when moving items around the panels, unlocking them all and moving then re-locking again. The mouse-click / effort ratio is really high here. I have no ideas on how this could be improved, but wonder if this is something that could be dealt with...after all...everyone likes to customize their Gnome.

2. This one really really bugs me as I have done it so often. Has anyone else out there accidentally removed the Trash item from the panel when really all they wanted to do was empty it. I do this regularly and I should probably have learned not to do it by now...it's just that sometimes I get a bit click-happy and *poof* there goes my Trash item again! Why is it that when an item is locked it can still be removed from the panel? Surely it would be better for a locked item to be just that, locked?

Anyway, that's my rant over for now.

Looking forward to hearing anyone else's grievances.

Keep Ubuntuing.

KDE and Gnome aren't the only choices. I definitely had Gnome annoyances when I used it and KDE annoyances when I used that. Now that I'm using IceWM, I can't think of any annoyances for my current environment off the top of my head.

Gnome ones, though?

The default had the choppy minimize/maximize animations. Then I did the *gconf-editor* tweak and got the wire frame on moving windows. Then I did Accessibility and got [an "empty" entry in Firefox that hasn't been fixed yet as of Edgy](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=189168)--don't know about Feisty.

Sometimes Control-W closes programs. Sometimes Control-Q closes programs. A little consistency would be nice.

That's all I can think of right now.
