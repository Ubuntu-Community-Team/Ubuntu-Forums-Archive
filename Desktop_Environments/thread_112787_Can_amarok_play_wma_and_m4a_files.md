### Post by gingermark on 2006-01-06
I can play both m4a files and wma files in amaroK.

I think this is more of a general installed libraries problem.

From memory, I think you need w32codecs installed for wma support, and faad for m4a support. As with many media things I think there may well be legal issues for folks in the States.

faad should be in Universe or Multiverse I think, and check out the following page for instructions on installing w32codecs: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RestrictedFormats](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RestrictedFormats)
