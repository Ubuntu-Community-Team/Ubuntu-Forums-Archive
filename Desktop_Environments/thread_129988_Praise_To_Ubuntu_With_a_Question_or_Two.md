### Post by calden on 2006-02-15
Hello all, I just wanted to say how happy I am to have found Ubuntu Linux. I recently bought a Toshiba Libretto U100; 1.2GHz Centrino, 1Gb Mem, 60 Gb HD, 1280 X 768 on a 7inch screen. The computer is great but I was really disappointed with it's performance under Windows. Also coming from a Solaris box I wasn't used to all these virus utilities running in the back ground, always popping up saying you're being attacked or we've contained a fast moving virus. What the fu*k!:evil:  I was pretty much ready to toss the little guy and call it loss when my neighbor, a uber Linux geek recommended Ubuntu Linux. I was reluctant, even though I consider myself  a Solaris/Irix junkie.  I've always thought Linux was unfinished and a pain in the butt to get setup correctly, yes I've tried many distributions. Sorry I'm probably spoiled with Sun and SGI. Anyway I'm happy to report that Ubuntu turned out to be the Saviour of the little Libretto. I love how lean and clean it is, unlike Suse which thinks they have to give the user every friggen program on the planet. Setup was a breeze and for the first time with any Linux I've experienced the video drivers were installed right off the bat and they we're the correct ones, geesh even the obscure rez of 1280 X 768 came up after the first boot!\\:D/  So to make long praise short me love you long time Ubuntu, keep up the good work.

I do have a few questions if someone doesn't mind answering. I'm looking for a better WiFi detection GUI, the card is an  Atheros Communications, Inc. AR5212 802.11abg NIC (rev 01). In windows the driver set came with this really cool utility that had this proximity detection thing that looked like a sonar panel pinging for hosts. The one that comes with Ubuntu is slow and kind of cheesy but I might have to upgrade the driver.

I figured out how to bind most of the function keys to their  Windows equivalent but I cant figure out how to make my machine hibernate or sleep when the lid is closed.

Anyway, nice job Ubuntu, you made me a Linux believer and will convert many.:KS
