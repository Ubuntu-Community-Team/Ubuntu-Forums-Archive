### Post by Sprawn! on 2010-01-10
I recently edited a date that was a link title and ended up with a catastrophic failure like this. I think perhaps Tomboy should issue a warning when the number of title/links being changed exceeds a user-specifiable number. Something like, "Warning: Tomboy is about to change 650 link titles. Should I proceed?"

I edited a date down to "2010", the link/title database updated, creating a new note titled "2010". I then changed the title/link in the original note to "Today: Saturday, January 2, 2010" and Tomboy promptly changed every instance of "2010" in every note to "Today: Saturday, January 2 2010". So, that amounted to something like 400 changes.

Of course, the solution which I figured out after changing a dozen or so, was to change the title of the note to 2010, copy its contents to a new note, and then delete the note 2010. But that still left me with a few hundred "2010"s to highlight, make small, make italic, make bold or otherwise change the formatting.
