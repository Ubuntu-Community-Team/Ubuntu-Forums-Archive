Also, the devs have declared that they will not support moving the dock, and they have been rather adamant about it. They plan on moving it to the right for right-to-left languages, and *possibly* for multi-monitor setups for people who have the primary monitor on the right, but that's it - and they appear to have made those concessions only grudgingly.

Once the interface settles down a bit, you may see some unofficial hacks to allow it, but I doubt there will ever be any official support for that without a high-level change of leadership at Canonical.

### Post by darkhelmetchris on 2011-10-02
I, too, have discovered that I can not move the Unity launcher and  "panel" to other locations on the screen.  I have also read the comments  about how they have decided that they always want the launcher to remain "under" the Ubuntu logo.  "Under?"  I don't want to separate it - I like it together - it works WELL that way.  I want to move ALL of it, together.

This decision to limit how the interface is presented is one of the most arbitrary and, I feel quite justified in saying so, arrogant "rules" that I've seen the Ubuntu team make in years.  Imposing the layout of the interface and removal of customization options goes against the whole spirit of "Ubuntu" by its own definition.  Not to mention that it's just plain rude.  There is no good reason to prevent me from moving it to where I want it.  I don't care if you want to link it with the logo, fine -- move the logo with it, but it should BE movable.

Sadly, after giving Unity a good try, and enjoying it VERY much, the fact that I can't relocate it has forced me to go back to Gnome - and I do mean forced.  I wanted to keep Unity.  It's a very good interface, and I will certainly miss it as I have really become quite used to having it.  I just don't see how I have a choice.  It's either "have it their way and hate it" or "have it my way" .. and frankly, I am going to have it my way.

"If it were truly Unity, it would make me want to be a part of it, not make it be a part of me."-- Chris 2011.

Imposing the layout of the interface and removal of customization options goes against the whole spirit of "Ubuntu" by its own definition. Not to mention that it's just plain rude. As Krytarik pointed out, this was only part of the rationale, and the version of Unity that ships with the next Ubuntu version this month doesn't even use the Home Button in the panel. However, Unity can be disabled and replaced quite simply, and it's not meant to "force" anything or reduce your options in any way. It's just that the interface is very structured for a specific workflow in a way that other shells aren't, and most customization options wouldn't really make sense.

As Krytarik noted in the other thread, there are a variety of other panels and docks, such as Cairo Dock, AWN, DockBarX, that you might want to try out. Just do some Google searching. Like Krytarik, I'm using AWN (with DockBarX added as an applet) and quite happy with it.
