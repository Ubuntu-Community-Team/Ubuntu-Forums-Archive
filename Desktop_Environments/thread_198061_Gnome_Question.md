### Post by reclusivemonkey on 2006-06-17
[QUOTE=thunderduck3141]no i mean the menue that comes out of the little button
[ATTACH]11336[/ATTACH][/QUOTE]

If you have XGL/Compiz installed, then you can do this with the "state" plugin. The easiest way is with the gset-compiz utility. Add "opacity" to Window Type "unknown" and you will get a transparent menu. However this will make just about every menu on your desktop transparent. If you don't have gset-compiz installed, then you can do it through the gnome configuration editor.
