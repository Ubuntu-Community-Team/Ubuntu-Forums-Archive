### Post by charlieg on 2006-01-24
Sadly, in my experience, it is pretty buggy.  Everything from artifacts on screen to crashes when files get big.  During early 2.2 I was using it to write a spec for some open source software (~20 pages).  When I brought problems up on the mailing list one of the developers actually said, "I wouldn't use AbiWord for that!"  If that's not a sad reflection of the then state of AbiWord, I don't know what is.

Things have improved since then, but issues will remain in one form or another until the developers properly dogfood on AbiWord with some serious documents, which they seem reluctant to do.  It has a lot of potential.  It just suffers from the chronic FOSS problem of focusing on features when focusing on fixes would do more for it's users.

Like any FOSS software, the best thing you can do is file bugs when you encounter them.  The development team are good at responding to bugs.

### Post by John Jason Jordan on 2006-01-24
[QUOTE=Bou]Hi. Looking for an alternative to Open Office -which, being a great office suite, doesn't comply with HIG and annoys me a bit for that- I found Abiword. ...[/QUOTE]
I am a dedicated OO.o user because I need its features. However, OO.o has a problem with copy/paste, both on the Linux and the Windows versions. Sometimes it gets "stuck." You copy something to the clipboard in another application and then paste into OO.o, and instead of what you just copied you get the last thing you pasted into OO.o. I found a cure for this -- just select one character in an OO.o document, even just a space will do, and copy it to the clipboard. This seems to reset OO.o to accept what is on the clipboard.

On Linux there are worse copy/paste problems with OO.o. You can't copy/paste from many other applications. That is what I keep AbiWord around for. I use it as an intermediary location. When OO.o won't take whatever I have copied from another application, I paste it into AbiWord first, then select and copy from within AbiWord, and then paste into OO.o.

AbiWord is too limiting for me to use it for much of anything else. For example, it can't see OpenType fonts that are installed in the system.
