### Post by garyjmellor on 2011-02-17
@TravellingJourneyman

I would suspect that your battery is on its last legs. How old is it? I had an old HP 510 laptop running Lucid and it exhibited the same behaviour. Fortunately I was able to test it on a Windows machine (i.e. same model) to rule out anything Linux specific. It also died just like Linux - the minute it was unplugged it died. I bought a new battery and the problem is gone.

@Eric J - yours is a more puzzling issue. Initially I thought of just adding the battery applet to the top panel but it seems like you've already tried that and got spurious results. Perhaps your make and model combo has an issue with power management in Linux? That's the only thing  I can suggest. How long does your laptop last on battery only? If it is a very short time (< 1 hour) I would also suggest that perhaps your battery is giving up the ghost but a little more gracefully that TravellingJourneyman's. It's not much help and it's very much clutching at straws I'm afraid.
