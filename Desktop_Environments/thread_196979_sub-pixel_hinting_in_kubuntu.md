### Post by dolio on 2006-06-16
I'm not 100% sure about this, but from what I've gathered reading in the past...

The various options correspond to different possible subpixel layouts. The color components of each pixel have to be ordered some way, so you end up with (I guess) either a vertical or horizontal layout of either RGB or BGR.

One of the settings probably matches up best with your particular monitor, and probably produces less color fringing than the other settings (or looks better in some other way). However, I don't know how to find out how a particular monitor is laid out (short of it happening to be in the manual somewhere), and in fooling around with it in the past, I can't say I could ever tell much of a difference between the settings.

So, I guess, try them all out, and see if you like one better than the rest. It might actually be better, or it might be psychosomatic.
