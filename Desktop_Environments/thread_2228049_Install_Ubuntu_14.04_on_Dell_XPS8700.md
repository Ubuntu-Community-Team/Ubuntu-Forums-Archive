### Post by egeezer on 2014-06-05
That can wait until you get the installation up and working.  When you first boot up the CD, or DVD or USB that holds the installer, run the part that says Try Ubuntu Without Installing.  That will let you see how it works on your machine, but it will run a LOT slower from the installation medium than it will when you get it installed - don't be disappointed at the performance.  The reason for trying that is to make sure everything runs fine on your machine, and also to get used to what the new system looks like and how it behaves.

I remember very well the first time I installed Ubuntu (it was 8.10) - I was nervous and worried, but it went so nicely it turned out to be no challenge at all!

Once you have your system installed, you can look on your menu for a listing called Additional Drivers (or maybe it is Restricted drivers), and again, click on it, read what it has to offer, and make a choice.  I don't know much about Radeon graphics, but you might try:

  	 	 	 	   [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver)
