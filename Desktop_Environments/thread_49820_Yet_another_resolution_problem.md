### Post by blueturtl on 2005-07-17
What is the too small resolution? Your config file would suggest you're running 1024x768. Taking a closer look at your configuration file the only major difference (discluding the hardware vendor ofcourse) between yours and mine is that your monitor doesn't have the Horizontal or Vertical synchronization lines that define how many times per second the monitor can be updated by the display adapter. I don't know that much about Xorg, but to me it would seem you should atleast get 60 Hz on higher resolutions without predefined settings. Ofcourse you can try adding the lines yourself and see if this helps. If you're running a flatscreen monitor please note that they work different than CRTs and usually have a default refreshrate of 60 Hz. Also note that if you add values too high you could possibly risk damaging your CRT. Here's my configuration file as a reference:

Section "Device"
	Identifier	"NVIDIA Corporation NV28 [GeForce4 Ti 4200 AGP 8x]"
	Driver		"nvidia"
	BusID		"PCI:1:0:0"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"HP D8905"
	Option		"DPMS"
	HorizSync	30-70
	VertRefresh	60-85
EndSection

Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"NVIDIA Corporation NV28 [GeForce4 Ti 4200 AGP 8x]"
	Monitor		"HP D8905"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		1
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		4
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		8
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		15
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		16
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		24
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
EndSection
