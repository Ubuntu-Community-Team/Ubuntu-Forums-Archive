### Post by kelsey23 on 2005-10-15
Yesterday, I upgraded my x86 computer from Hoary Hedgehog to Breezy Badger. I was excited about the new release, but after using it for not even two minutes, I was already disappointed. Where is the Ubuntu Linux community headed? It seems apparent that you are more set on catering to the idiot user than to a person who is more comfortable with the command-line. Why is the terminal right-click menu-option gone? I used that thing about every five minutes with Hoary. Now, I have to go Applications > Accessories > Terminal and that was after I had to enable it, which it was not even enabled by default! I don't even want to get into the update bubble, which I could thankfully disable. Also (less of an issue) I am not a real big fan of the updated human theme, either. What's up with the services control under System > Administation > Services? I like the idea that it allows for easier control over daemons, but it doesn't need to tell me that Apache webserver is used to share webpages on the internet. If I didn't know that, then why would I have it installed? Same thing with MySQL server. If I didn't know what MySQL was, then why would I take the time to install it for no reason? But the worst part about Breezy Badger is preformance. Hoary was great. On Breezy, I can't have more than two or three applications open at once, or else it crashes.

What is the goal of the Ubuntu community? To create an aesthetic, stable, Linux operating system that advanced users and Linux idiots can appreciate alike? Or, as it seems in this release, to create a reosurce-pig, not-too-stable, replacement for Windoze fro people who are too cheap to buy a Windoze liscense and do not even appreciate open-source and free software, and a "Linux" OS that GNU/Linux Idiots will love, but more advanced users will feel limited and uncomfortable?

I love Ubuntu (5.04 at least), but this new release is totally disappointing. I know that Linux is about choices, but it isn't very cool to make something that allows cheap people to exploit open-source and free software, like Linspire has done, and now Ubuntu. I don't excpect Ubuntu will be on my HD much longer, time for me to utilise that broadband and download Debian :-) !
