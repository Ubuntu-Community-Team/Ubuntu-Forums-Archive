### Post by stwog on 2005-01-13
First off, welcome to the world of Linux. I'll try to answer your questions 1 by 1.

No.1: There is no such thing as a C: drive or A: drive, or any other letter drive. Linux doesn't work like that. To get to your drives, use the "Computer" menu. It will list your drives and there is even a general Computer icon that will show you your drives. Though, by now I'm sure you have found all that.

No.2: Again, your not using Windows any more, so things are going to be done differently. Most of the options you want are under the "Computer" menu again inside the Preferences directory, but again, I"m sure you have already found that.

No.3: Desktop icons are created by right clicking on the desktop and selecting "Create Launcher"

No.4: Icon sets are controlled int the Themes selector that is found under the Preferences menu.

No.5: You shouldn't have to use the command line for much, if at all. Installing software is completely different in the linux world. Check out synaptic and you'll see what I mean.

Good luck with your learning.
Simon

### Post by hashimoto on 2005-01-13
MatrixSurferX

Welcome to the GNU/Linux world. Linux is a totally different OS so don't expect things to work as they do in Windows. Many if not most things are in fact done differently so you are going to need a totally new set of mind to cope with that; Windows isn't the absolute truth about how things shoud be done. There is a lot to learn and an curious mind will help you through.

Some basics about Linux in general can be learned in the net, e.g. at [http://linuxsurvival.com/index.php](http://linuxsurvival.com/index.php) (needs java though). Googling will give you a lot more of those.

You may also want to visit [http://gnome.org/learn/](http://gnome.org/learn/) to learn more about how Gnome works. Ubuntu may have some differences to general Gnome, but you will get a good picture.

Just take your time, make "stupid" questions here (there are none) and try not to be frustrated. Learning new isn't always easy.
