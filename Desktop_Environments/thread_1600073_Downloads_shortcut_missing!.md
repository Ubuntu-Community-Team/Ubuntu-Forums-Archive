### Post by alexan on 2010-10-18
Places > Home >


right click [empty space] > make new folder > rename to "Downloads"



Right click [Downloads folder] > make link >/or/>

drag&drop "Downloads" folder while pressing [shift] key



What did happen:
case 1. you'd erase the folder
case 2. you dragged&drop the folder in another folder

### Post by neogarfield on 2010-11-01
Actually, Alexan's solution works... In Lucid, its simple, open up Nautilus (your files explorer) go to your Home folder, drag the Downloads folder and drop it on to your sidebar. That'll make the shortcut appear on your Nautilus sidebar, and after a restart, it will be available on Gnome Do as well.

Hope that helps...
