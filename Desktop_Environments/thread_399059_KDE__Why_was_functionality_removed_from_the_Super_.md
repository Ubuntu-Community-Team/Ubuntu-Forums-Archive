### Post by mukiex on 2007-04-01
In KDE 3.3, the Super_L key (known to most as that Windows key, or the Tux key for everyone who found that sticker ;) ) could be used both as a key and as a modifier key.

In KDE 3.4 and on, this ability was removed.

Now, back in Windows land, it was nice to run the start menu or kick up the run box/show the desktop/etc. In KDE +3.4, your choice now consists of one ability or the other.

Given that KDE has superior support for launching the Kmenu (it launches where your mouse is if you hit ctrl+esc, rather than in the corner, saving time), why did they remove this functionality?

I suppose I should ask over @ KDE, but I'm sure they've gotten that question a million times...
