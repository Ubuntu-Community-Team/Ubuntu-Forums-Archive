### Post by skip27 on 2007-03-11
Right-click on your top panel, select 'New Panel'. Afterwards, right-click the new panel that appears to alter its orientation, etc. to your liking. Right-click again, add a window list, etc. You should be back in business.

In the event that you have deleted all of your panels, you will need to execute 'gnome-panel'. Alt-F2 is the global shortcut to access the 'run' box. Gnome by default loads at least one panel, so I doubt you'll have to resort to that. Log out and log back in again and you should have at least one panel.

Hope this helps.
