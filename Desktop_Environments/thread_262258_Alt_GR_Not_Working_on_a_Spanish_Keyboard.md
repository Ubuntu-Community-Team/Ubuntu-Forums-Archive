### Post by virg on 2006-10-22
I was having the same problem with a German keyboard. After much searching, it ended up being a fairly easy to solve problem.

I am using gnome, but I am pretty sure that an equivalent solution is readily available through KDE.

In gnome, you go:

System > Preferences > Keyboard

(above takes you to the keyboard preferences panel... get to the equivalent thing in KDE).

There you will see at the top a tab called "layout options".

After clicking on this tab, among several categories you will see one called:

"**[SIZE="3"]Third level choosers[/SIZE]**".

The symbols available through the "alt gr" key are apparently called "third level" symbols.

Make sure that "Right alt key" (alt gr) is selected (click on the appropriate area) among the "thrid level choosers".

It is seemingly possible to choose several keys as "third level chooser", so that pressing any one of them does the same job as pressing "alt gr". 

NOTICE: Another choice under that tab is for something called "compose key position". In my case, the right alt key (alt gr) was by default chosen as the "compose key". To avoid conflicts, you may want to make sure that the right alt key is NOT selected as compose key or for any other purpose, other than "third level chooser".

Best wishes.

Virg.
