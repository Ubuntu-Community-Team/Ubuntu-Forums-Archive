### Post by stuporglue on 2005-12-20
If you want to use nautilus as your file manager with XFCE, and preserve the XFCE desktop (with no icons, and the right click XFCE menu), you need to launch nautilus with "nautilus --no-desktop". 

You may be able to set this as an alias somewhere, but I'm not sure where would be the best place.
