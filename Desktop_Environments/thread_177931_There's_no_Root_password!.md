### Post by Sendervictorius on 2006-05-17
Theres a constant stream of people new to Ubuntu, but dabbled in other Linux distros before, who are completely bamboozled by the fact that there's not supposed to be a root password in Ubuntu. 

Sure, there are ways around it, you CAN set a root password up if you like. I'm sure there are many many Ubuntu users who have subverted the sudo idea, and built themselves a root login and password. But that's missing the point.

We have threads and threads on this forum started by newbies who have just installed Ubuntu for the first time wondering, "where do I enter my root password?". There are Wiki pages explaining the wisdom of it. There are many support threads painstakingly explaining, with the inevitable airings of contrary views.

I personally think the concept is great. It's convenient and makes systems more secure. But there are many out there who simply don't get it.

So I think there is a selling job to do. How about giving your ideas onto this thread about what what we can do to get the philosophy understood, and head new users off at the pass, before or when they install for the first time.

Questions:
1. Why is a having root account such a psychological crutch?
2. What's so hard to understand about the idea that "there's no root password"?
3. Is there a better way of explaining the key points  than we are doing right now?
4. Are there better ways of advertising to unsuspecting new installers?
5. How can we get the volume of forum threads on the subject to reduce? (after all, this is a well known problem)
6. Has anyone started with a root account and been convinced to swap back to sudo? What did it for you?
7. For those new to Ubuntu - if you were slow to catch on, why do you think that was?

### Post by shrimphead on 2006-05-17
When I first started using Ubuntu (I came from SuSE via Slackware before settling on Hoary) I hated the Sudo logic and created myself a root password, not because I log in as root (because I always tried to avoid that) but because I liked to have a terminal open and just su and work as root and then exit.

now I am very definitely behind the Sudo idea, and I've finally realised why I didn't like it to start with: I was being lazy! I resented having to type sudo in front of every command I wanted to carry out as root.

sorry if this goes on a bit but I was one of the people who until fairly recently scoffed at sudo and just created a root account.
