### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-01-29
If you are looking for a working environment that is fast and beautifull, than Enlightenment E17 is the answer.  

To begin with, I'll give you an example of how light Enlightenment E17 really is.  I recently installed E17 from a command line installation.  After E17 was up, my entire system with E17 running was only using 68mb of ram.  After opening Firefox (GTK app, more on that later) my ram useage was just over 100mb.  Very comparable to Fluxbox but looks way better.  

E17 is the latest version of the Enlightenment Window Manager.  In some ways E17 is similar to other window managers such as Fluxbox, Icewm, and Afterstep but also includes configuration tools to help users easily change their themes, wallpapers, window management options and more.  

Just as you can use KDE or GNOME applications in either desktop environment, you can also use Gnome GTK and KDE QT apps in Enlightenment E17.  E17 is very stable and fast and includes many themes by defaulty.  

I would recommend installing E17 and giving it a try.  Installation is very easy.  I created a guide that you can find here.

[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=319336&highlight=how+to+e17]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=319336&highlight=how+to+e17")

You can find more information on Enlightenment E17 at get-e.org

[http://www1.get-e.org/]("http://www1.get-e.org/")
