### Post by Drone4four on 2013-01-01
Before installing your new O/S, back up your home folder onto a USB stick or onto a DVD or onto a storage partition.  Once you have installed your new O/S, restore your Gnome 3 settings by copying the contents of your backup into your new home directory.  This should restore font, icon and other settings.  To restore your gtk3 themes, do the same thing to the contents of /usr/share/themes as you did with your home directory.  The only thing I am unsure of is how to restore your plugins.  Perhaps someone else can comment on that.  Like you, I prefer Gnome 3 over Unity. Gnome 3 and e17 are my window managers of choice.  If you have less than half a dozen, it might just be easiest to take note of your favourites, and then just re-downloading them from [https://extensions.gnome.org/](https://extensions.gnome.org/)

If you go through with this O/S installation process, be sure to report back here regarding your success.
