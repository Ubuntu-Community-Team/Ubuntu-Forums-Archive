### Post by ajgreeny on 2014-12-07
If you don't want to bother installing any packages you can make a second panel at the top, bottom or either side and add launchers to that to make the equivalent of a dock, admittedly without the animations possible in cairo-dock or docky, but still very useful and needing almost no extra resources.

Here's screenshot of mine, on the left hand side a bit like the unity launchbar, using just the standard panel setup in XFCE-4.10; I have it set to autohide to keep as much screen estate available as possible.

If you wonder why I don't therefore just use Ubuntu and unity, it is because I don't like other parts of the unity DE, eg workspace switching is not nearly so convenient for my way of working, and I like to use panels and add many different things which I have not found possible in unity, or not to my liking.
