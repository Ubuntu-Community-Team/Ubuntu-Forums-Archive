### Post by rverrips on 2006-06-14
It's more than just KDE vs. Gnome - It's all the "tools" around 'em.  I've tried both direct (i.e. fresh) installs of Ubuntu 6.06 and Kubuntu 6.06 on my Laptop:  

The powermanager is not as mature in Kubuntu as what I experienced under the Gnome/Ubuntu installation - I hibernate in K and she dies, where-as a Hibernate with G/U works like a charm (Need to admit, this is only since Dapper, with Breezy same thing happened under Gnome)

Another thing (sorta unrelated) is that my imap server folders displayed fine under Evolution, but KMail (i.e. Kontact) choaked and I still can't get them all as direct subfolders (i.e. not subfolders of the INBOX) - Although I can't prove it, I also think during my efforts to get it working KMail deleted all the mails in my Inbox ... 

The Bluetooth OBEX in Ubuntu also just seemed to "work" while KBluetooth took a bit more persuading, nothing impossible though, just needed to bond the device three or four times more.

To summarise I think Kubuntu is still not as mature a compilation of free software around a linux kernel as Ubuntu, but fully agree that with a bit of work and swapping out of sub-elements (i.e. apt-get"ing whatever) it's all the same thing at the end of the day.
