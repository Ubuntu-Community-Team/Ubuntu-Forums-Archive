### Post by Lil_Eagle on 2006-06-30
CNN is a strange site.  If I hit it with firefox it gives me a different page than if I hit it with mozilla, or with konqueror.  Even though I told konqueror to identify itself as firefox.

In either event, konqueror does indeed krash :grin: not only if you try to access one of the links, but if you just refresh the main page.  Firefox will also crash on occasion.  I don't think it is konqueror's fault in this case, rather cnn's fault.

You have to understand that modern browsers are more like compilers than text viewers.  The myriad of code they must translate in order to display a page is quite astounding.  CNN is one of the sites that uses some of the latest technology in web browsing, and while konqueror is a good web browser, it is not IE compatible, and unfortunely it appears that many sites, including CNN cater to the IE crowd.  I wouldn't be suprised if they were paid to do so.

One of the major problems that we face is the fact that there are too many different standards.  At least there are some standards or we'd really be in trouble.  The problem with the Internet is that nobody really has to follow the standards.  If a site wants to only support foo, then people without foo are unable to view the site.  That's the way it is.  It's not a smart idea for a major international news organization to limit who can access their site, but it's their decision to take.

In any event, having a browser (especially a tabbed browser) crash when you hit a site is not acceptable and a bug should be filed if it isn't already.
