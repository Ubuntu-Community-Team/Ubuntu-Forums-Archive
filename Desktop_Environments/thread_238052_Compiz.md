### Post by peabody on 2006-08-17
You are in for a very long hard ride my friend.

I don't think I ever spent more time getting something to work in Linux.  I got it working though.

The first important question is, what kind of video card do you have?  If you have an nvidia or ati card you'll want to make sure that the proprietary drivers are working and that you have 3d hardware acceleration.

Here is a reference to an Nvidia howto that contains reference to an ati howto that is outdated but that contains a link to an up-to-date howto.

[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=131267&highlight=xgl+compiz](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=131267&highlight=xgl+compiz)

*sigh* what a mess.

If you have neither an nvidia or an ATI card, but rather one of those intel on-board things that's capable of 3d stuff, I think you'll need to go looking for the aiglx setup rather than the xgl setup.

Good luck man.  May you not screw up your system.
