### Post by GepettoBR on 2010-04-01
Drivers are kernel modules. The installation/removal of a kernel module and a kernel upgrade are the two things in Linux that require a reboot to work.

The message "the driver is installed but not enabled" means that you haven't rebooted yet since installing it. If you reboot, it should be enabled.
