### Post by x-shaney-x on 2010-05-16
On certain apps I don't use frequently, it can be handy to have labels for the toolbar icons but having them below icons does increase toolbar height quite a lot.

So I prefer text beside icons but in gnome this is completely pointless.
I realise having text beside EVERY icon could mean a lot of icons not fitting on the toolbar on some apps so would need a dropdown arrow or something but in gnome it only has text beside the most obvious icons.

Look at nautilus:  text beside back and forward.  
Even someone seeing linux for the first time is likely to know what the back and forward buttons do.

Evolution:  send/receieve and reply/forward but the mark/delete/junk/cancel buttons have no labels.  Even worse, at first glance how would anyone know which of the delete/cancel buttons is delete or cancel?

I notice this throughout gnome and to me it seems they may as well not have text at all by default.

Or if you are going to have text beside only select icons then why not group the standard nav buttons (back/forwards/stop/reload) without text and add text to the less obvious buttons?
