Hey all,

I just recently installed the Budgie desktop UI on my laptop with a 4K display, in hopes of achieving better display scaling than MATE was providing me.  So far, I am VERY impressed with the Budgie desktop's display scaling on my 4K monitor and I think this UI will be my daily driver on my Ubuntu 22.04 laptop for the foreseeable future. :) 

I have one weird issue I'm betting is a bug.  The Budgie screensoht tool seems to be unable to capture a full 4K screenshot.  When I take a screenshot, either by pressing the Print Screen button (which on Budgie seems to auto-save a screenshot to my Pictures folder), or by using the screenshot application:
[IMG]https://binaryimpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/Screenshot-from-2022-07-10-19-17-52.png[/IMG]

What I end up with is a 1920x1080 screenshot of the top-left corner of my screen, and a bunch of black space filling the rest of the 4K canvas.

[IMG]https://binaryimpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/Screenshot-from-2022-07-10-19-14-30.png[/IMG]

If I use the functionality to take a screenshot of a window, instead of a full screen screenshot (or press ALT+Printscreen) I am able to take screenshots of windows larger than 1920x1080.  Just not a FULL capture of the ENTIRE screen.

[IMG]https://binaryimpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/Screenshot-from-2022-07-10-19-15-15.png[/IMG]

My assumption is something must be hard coded somewhere in the screenshot application.  Is this a known issue?  I don't see any settings to adjust in the screenshot tool so can't be my mistake.
