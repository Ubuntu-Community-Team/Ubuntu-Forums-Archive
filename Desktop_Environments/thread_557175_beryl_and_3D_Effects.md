### Post by davosmith on 2007-09-22
Assuming you have the beryl-manager up and running (the red jewel icon in your system tray) you can just disable beryl by right-clicking, clicking on 'select window manager' and choosing 'metacity'.

If you want to remove beryl completely, either use the synaptic package manager and (click: system, administration, synaptic package manager) scroll down and find anything beginning 'beryl' with a green square to the left of it (which means it is installed). Click on the green squares, choose remove, then click apply at the top of the screen. It should then be gone.

If these don't work, then maybe I've misunderstood what you are trying to do.
