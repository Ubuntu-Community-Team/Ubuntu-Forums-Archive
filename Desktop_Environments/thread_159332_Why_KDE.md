### Post by aysiu on 2006-04-13
Because KDE does a lot of stuff Gnome doesn't do.

When someone asked "How do I make a desktop shortcut without a middle-click mouse button?" It wasn't immediately obvious. You have to mess with the Alt button to try to figure that out.

When people post saying "How do I lock down Gnome?" all I can tell them is that KDE has a kiosk function. I don't believe Gnome does.

When someone says, "How do I get numlock to always stay on?" I have to tell them to install *numlockx* for Gnome. In KDE, you just go to the Control Center and click a box.

**Edit**: And, don't forget [rotating wallpapers](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=159720) without a third-party program.

I could go on and on. People who bash KDE (or bash Gnome, for that matter) just need to realize not everyone has the same computing needs and preferences. KDE suits many people's computing styles.

### Post by GeneralZod on 2006-04-14
[QUOTE=apjone]I think KDE is to fat and flashy where as know works great for me. So why do others choose kde?[/QUOTE]

What on Earth does "fat and flashy" mean? :)

I use it for the following reasons:

- Massively and easily configurable, from the DE itself to the individual apps
- Superb integration and consistency between apps
- Awesome C++ libraries and API's make it a dream to develop for
- Some best of breed apps like K3B, amaroK, kdevelop, Kile, Kontact etc
- Pretty much every app uses kio_slaves, which are entirely awsome! :D
- Konqueror is by far my favourite file manager
- KParts are a really cool technology
- Clipboard manager as standard - no more losing stuff that you were going to paste just because you closed the source app!
- Katapult (which I initially didn't care much for) has now become indispensable! :)
- Mouse gestures as standard
- Pretty much every KDE app offers a useful DCOP interface.
- All this, and it's not substantially slower or more memory-hungry than GNOME.  In fact, many people say that it is *more* resource-friendly than GNOME, but a lot of these posts tend to be fairly subjective.

... and more reasons that I probably can't remember at the moment!

For every post saying "GNOME is faster than KDE!", I see another one saying "KDE is faster than GNOME!", and vice versa.  I've seen so many bits of conflicting anecdotal evidence from both sides that the only conclusion I'm willing to draw is this:

*If KDE and GNOME do indeed have a difference in speed, it is dwarfed by the placebo effect and human bias*

:)

One thing that is demonstrably true, however, is that KDE apps tend to start up more slowly, mainly because of an extremely inefficient bit of font configuration which is needlessly performed for every app started up, and because the Linux library linker does not deal that well with C++.  The former will be ameliorated by KDE4, and hopefully, by that time, GCC's -fvisibility=hidden flag will be ready for prime-time (I asked jriddell if Kubuntu uses it, and he said no, as GCC's implementation will be largely broken until GCC 4.2) which will mitigate the latter.
