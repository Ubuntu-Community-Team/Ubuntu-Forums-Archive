### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-12-19
It's hard to track the progress of the Lubuntu project from the Wiki, but it does indicate a few signs of slow progress.

I've read that Ubuntu-based Linux Mint is working on an LXDE edition and someone new has taken over their project.

LXDE runs awesomely on **Debian**. The **Fedora** LXDE spin was awesome. **PCLinuxOS** has an LXDE edition which was far less buggy than LXDE on minimal Ubuntu. But **[COLOR="Blue"][B]every Ubuntu-based variant I have tried is crazy buggy**[/COLOR][/B] (Crunchbang, minimal Ubuntu - even U-Lite and Masonux). Why does LXDE run fine on non-Ubuntu based distros but become a clusterbug on Ubuntu-based ones? I call it **[COLOR="Sienna"][B]"the 'Buntu Barrier."**[/COLOR][/B] It's the only reason I can find to explain why the Ubuntu family and it's offspring are having such trouble getting an LXDE edition to market.

Am I alone? Has anyone else run into the 'Buntu Barrier?
