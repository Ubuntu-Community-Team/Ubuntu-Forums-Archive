### Post by anthro398 on 2006-07-08
I imagine that computers stores will deny your request to boot from a cd on one of their machines.  Most webcams come in packages that can't be opened without destroying the packaging, too.  Besides, for many webcams you'll have to connect to the internet and download the driver.  Sorry, but your plan seems unlikely to work.  

On a more positive note, I have a couple of Logitech Messenger cameras and they all work ok with the driver installed by lauchcam2.  Of course, I have to reinstall the driver every time the kernel is updated and I had to add "quickcam" and "videodev" to /etc/modules to get the drivers to load after rebooting.  However, I've had several cameras that didn't work at all so I'm fairly satisfied.  I am annoyed that every time I accidental push the button on top of the camera my entire computer freezes.
