### Post by jej2003 on 2008-01-07
athough I have never done it with ubuntu, it should be easily acomplishable.  What I would do is if your BIOS detects the USB drive as a boot device (mine did) hook up the drive, change the boot order, and when you want to boot from that device just plug it in, when you don't just remove it.  This way you don't have to mess with grub at all and your other hard drive will remain completly untouched.  

The other option is to drop the livecd in and give it a whirl.  The setup wizard will allow you to chose the device to install to before you get to the end and you can see if it finds the drive or not.
