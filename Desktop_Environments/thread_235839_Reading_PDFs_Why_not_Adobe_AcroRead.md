### Post by DoktorSeven on 2006-08-13
"Free" here refers to open-source, not the price/cost.

Adobe Acrobat isn't open-source, therefore it's "non-free".

### Post by maniacmusician on 2006-08-13
Well, it sometimes sets things up more easily. I dunno, I don't exactly like it better; they're both good, but different. Automatix was created to help newbies install stuff, and though i'm not exactly a newbie anymore, it still makes it a lot easier to set up my computer. Within an hour of installing my OS, I have a nice arsenal of 30 or so odd programs that are pretty handy. For something like that, Automatix is great. but of course it only has a small selection of software, whereas synaptic has thousands. Automatix also easily installs codecs, multimedia software, and sometimes hard to install things such as java runtime environment. Automatix is usually a one time thing (unless they add new programs to their list, then you go try those out too), and synaptic is for everyday use.
