### Post by quietglow on 2005-11-27
Yup, that's the workspace switcher! And in checking to make sure that is what its actually called, I think I shed some light on my question. If you have the width of your gnome task bar set to less that 27 or so pixels, you won't see any icons. Try resizing your bar--firefox, nautilis, abiword etc will show up in the switcher with icons in their little box. It sure helps when you have 10 apps spread over 5 desktops! ### Post by VictorR on 2008-01-27
> hey guys, not that we're talking about "workspace switcher icons" how can i put this as a desktop icon? Do i have to setup an applet or some sort? Tnx!

Just right-click on the panel, where you are going to have it, select Add To Panel, select Workspace Switcher and click Add. Move it to the proper position on your panel, right-click on it and select Lock To Panel.
Make sure it has number of columns same, as workplaces at your desktop, and 1 row.
