### Post by barney_1 on 2007-03-18
> **Fuzzy Logic said:**
> 1st question:
I just installed Beryl, it really looks nice and works well. The only thing I could not figure out is how i can get [this]("http://wiki.beryl-project.org/images/1/1f/DesktopCube.jpg") effect. I searched around but as I said, I couldn't find it.

That is the desktop cube.  It might already be enabled.  Try holding down CTRL-ALT and click and dragging the mouse anywhere.  If nothing happens, or if you want to tweak the settings:

Press ALT-F2
```
beryl-settings
```

When the settings window comes up, click on "desktop" and you should see "desktop cube" as one of the list options.

Good luck!

### Post by barney_1 on 2007-03-19
> **Fuzzy Logic said:**
> Thanks, it does work indeed, but I don't get such spectaculair effect with transparency and everything like in that picture (wich I btw got from the official Beryl site).

Anyone ideas?

In beryl-settings choose "desktop" and then click on "desktop cube" in the left-side list.  There will be a section to the right with tabs.  Choose the "Caps" tab and uncheck the "Draw Caps" box.  Choose the "Transparency" tab and check mark the "Transparent Cube" option.

It should be just like in the picture.
