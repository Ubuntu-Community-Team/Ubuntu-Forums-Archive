### Post by jgoguen on 2006-06-13
Sure is:[LIST]
[*]Right-click an empty area on the deskbar (I put mine on the top one)
[*]Choose "Add to Panel..."
[*]Under "System and Hardware" choose "Network Monitor"
[*]Close the window; right-click the new icon and choose "Properties"
[*]Under Connection -> Name, choose the name of the interface you want to monitor (most likely eth0 for ethernet connections)[/LIST]All you do now is left-click the icon once and it'll show you packets/bytes received/sent, for wireless it'll show status (receiving, transmitting, idle) and connection strength.  Under the Support tab you get the IP address, broadcast address, and subnet mask, and also the MAC address (hardware address, physical address...).
