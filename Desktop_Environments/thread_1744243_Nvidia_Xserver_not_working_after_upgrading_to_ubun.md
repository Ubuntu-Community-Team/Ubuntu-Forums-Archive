### Post by Losgrinn on 2011-05-03
I have just installed 11.04 Natty AMD64 and was having trouble getting getting it to boot in Ubuntu mode, I was left with just the desktop background and no panels and no Unity Launcher, the only way I could get anything to appear on screen was to press Ctrl+Alt+Del.  This resulted in the shutdown window to flash off and on the desktop and little else.  Rebooting in Ubuntu classic worked fine but the NVidia Graphics  (173) driver although installed was marked as not in use.

I removed the driver (using Additional Drivers app) and rebooted
on reboot into Ubuntu Classic session I opened the Additional Drivers app and the new ***Experimental 3D support for NVIDIA cards ***driver was then listed. I activated the Experimental driver and rebooted once more.  Selected Ubuntu from the session options and when it started hey presto Unity is functional and the Launcher panel was working too.

Hope this helps someone :p

Oh yeah I'm running a self built system AMD Athlon 64 with an nVidia GeForce on an ASRock 939A8X-M motherboard.

Not too sure I actually like the Unity interface but as I'm running it dual boot alongside Lucid with Compiz I'll give it a chance just to see if familiarity does make it grow on me
