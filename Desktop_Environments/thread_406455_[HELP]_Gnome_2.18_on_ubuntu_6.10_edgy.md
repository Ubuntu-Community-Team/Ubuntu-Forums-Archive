### Post by mmcmonster on 2007-04-19
Truthfully, I don't think that's a safe idea.  Gnome makes up the desktop environment and putting a newer version of gnome on 6.10 is likely going to break a number of things.  To get it to work at all, you'll probably have to compile gnome yourself, as the copies in Fiesty are built on newer versions of various libraries.

The equivalent on Microsoft Windows is trying to update a Windows XP desktop environment to Vista Aero, without upgrading to Vista.  Theoretically possible, but probably not worth the effort unless you are a real power user (and even then, questionably more useful).

You are much safer just updating to Fiesty.  It's pretty uncommon for things to break during the installation, and that's what the forums are for: to ask questions when things break.
