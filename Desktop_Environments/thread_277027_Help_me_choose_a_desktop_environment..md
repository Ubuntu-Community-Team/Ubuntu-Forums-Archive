### Post by nalmeth on 2006-10-13
I suppose fluxbox is considered a window manager, but xfce and e16/17 would qualify as desktop environments.
I think it has to do with whether applications (file manager) and applets (like a desktop, menu, themes, configuration utilities) are supplied with the window manager.
Bundle fluxbox with fbdesk/idesk, debian menu, and thunar, you've got a desktop environment.

### Post by llanitedave on 2006-10-14
I can understand the desire to explore different desktop environments, to discover the strengths and weaknesses of each, but if your only priority is "blazing speed", you're probably better off taking emarkay's advice and not using a DE at all!.  The purpose of a DE is not to enhance your machine's speed, it's to improve *useability*, of which raw speed is only a small factor.  The fastest is not likely to be the best.
