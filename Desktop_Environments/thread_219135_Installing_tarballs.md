### Post by blanks on 2006-07-19
A tarball is simply an archive of files created by the tar utility, not neccessarily something you can "install." It's often used to pack source code, which is what you have, but this won't always be the case.

Anyway, I think your particular error is because you don't have permission to create the executable files.  Give yourself write permission in the directory or use sudo.
