### Post by RedSquirrel on 2008-01-30
I played around with them in Fluxbox a while back but I don't use them now. I used wmmatrix and an assortment of those "classic" LCD-type dockapps for monitoring the system. They were kind of fun, but I prefer a one-line conky for that.

There were a few more that I tinkered with but I can't recall them now. Probably nothing that made use of *new* programs or technologies though. I never found any dockapps that were "indispensable".

I suppose the real reason I don't use dockapps is that I don't like screen clutter. It didn't take long for me to drop the Fluxbox slit and dockapps altogether.

Which one or two dockapps are you using?

### Post by jviscosi on 2008-01-30
I use a whole bunch of DockApps (Red Squirrel knows, he's probably seen my [screenshot]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=3919161#post3919161")) but they don't utilize any "new" programs like Beagle or BitTorrent.  I don't have a real need for such things so I've never looked for them; the only DockApp I'd really like that I don't have would be one that shows Amarok album art, but that'll probably never happen unless I write it myself, and I'm too lazy ... uhhh, busy, to do that.

My impression is that there aren't a whole lot of people writing new DockApps these days; they're not as sexy as Compiz and the like.  As far as I know even Ben Sinclair's DockApp warehouse is still off-line.  That's just how it seems to me, though; there could be armies of DockApp programmers that I don't know about.

EDIT: Don't overlook the power of key bindings in Fluxbox ... I have a bunch of stuff bound to keystrokes that I don't need cluttering the screen, like a desktop search, calculator (okay, I also have a calculator DockApp, but still), dictionary lookup, etc.
