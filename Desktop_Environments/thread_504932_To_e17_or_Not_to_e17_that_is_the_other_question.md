### Post by kvonb on 2007-07-21
I have E17 running on my Feisty install, it is really nice.

The good points in my opinion are:

1. Extremely fast, on a par with the low end managers like Fluxbox et al.

2. Pretty layout, very fancy graphics.

3. Drop shadows built in, no glx/aiglx/beryl/compiz type nonsense.

4. The right-click menu anywhere feature

5. Light on resources, no 15 megs just for a clock applet!

Downsides:

1. Lack of "cohesion", things just don't seem to communicate, like you can't "open with" if you see what I mean.

2. Can't add/remove things from ANY menus, newly installed apps do not appear on the menu.

3. Limited customise-ability, the idea of having a global theme is a good one, but limiting.

It could be really good, and I would definitely consider moving to it, but it doesn't quite make the grade for me :(.

On a lower spec system though it blows all the ***box variants and xfce out of the window though.

### Post by picpak on 2007-07-22
Every 6 months I try e17 and every 6 months I remember why I don't like it. What it more than makes for in the eyecandy department it sorely lacks in the functionality department. As it stands, it's unusable for everyday use. Here's why:

1) No native system tray :( this was a big blow to me; the system tray is my god!
2) Terminal transparency doesn't seem to work...neither does Conky, even as its own window or on the root window. I didn't try adesklets as I intended to use Engage (see #7).
3) Maximized windows aren't maximized...they still have a border. (xfwm4 had this problem for years)
4) The menu is severely limited compared to XFCE and IceWM...I mean, Synaptic and urxvt aren't even in there.
5) Everything is so cryptic! I don't know how you eyecandy people can stand so many obscure images and symbols. I wanted to make the panel a fullscreen width, and when I found the option I had to choose between a series of tiny, uninformative buttons. Even then there was a little blank space at the left side where the start button usually is. That just appeared and disappeared on its own. The default taskbar doesn't even show most of the windows, and after trying to find Opera in it I realize it just shows the icon, no text. After some searching I found out to enable the taskbar module (well...just "r"...the icon covered up most of the text). Even then the taskbar was awfully squished, but I'm sure there was a way to fix that. Then I go to the Run command and I get this big black box, with no indication of typing in it whatsoever. I stared at it for a minute until I pressed a key and realized what it was. The theme I was using also made it hard to see the minimize, maximize and close buttons, but that's not e17's fault.
6) Focusing windows is really bad by default. I can't simply click the window, I have to click the window decoration.
7) Engage, the one program I was looking forward to, isn't even supported anymore. Oh well, I could never figure out those eap files anyway. I heard about Itask, but from what I saw I had to compile it and it didn't have a system tray.

Still, the heaping amount of eyecandy was impressive, as was the use of the "Advanced" button. However, I take functionality over eyecandy any day, and that's where me and e17 don't meet. XFCE or IceWM may not be walks around the park, but at the end of the day I can do what I want with them without resorting to hacks.
