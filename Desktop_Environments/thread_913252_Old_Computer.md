### Post by damis648 on 2008-09-07
If it is a BIOS password, just unplug it, open up the case and remove the CMOS battery (watch-type battery) for about 30-60 minutes. Then put in back in and try entering the BIOS again.

### Post by damis648 on 2008-09-07
When the computer is booting, it will probably say "Press [some key] to enter setup". Press that key. Navigate through the options to find the boot order. Set the CD drive to number one, and the hard drive to number two.
