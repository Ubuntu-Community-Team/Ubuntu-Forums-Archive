### Post by unix1980 on 2012-07-18
I like 12.04 Unity.  It takes a little while to get used to the changes but once you do you find some nifty things.  The workspace switcher works well on my dual monitor setup, which was important for me. Install My Unity and reduce the launcher size, then install and use Cairo Dock if you want the access apps in the more standard fashion.  One downside is that Unity 3D uses a lot of memory.

I run the accounting side of a business on Ubuntu and I was careful to check it out thoroughly before upgrading to 12.04. So far, so good.

### Post by Version Dependency on 2012-07-18
> **vyoufinder said:**
> Where is the Terminal?  Wait, where is the menu where the Terminal icon should be?! 

Open a terminal?  Let's see...you can CTRL+ALT+T to open a terminal....you can press SUPER to open the dash and begin typing terminal ("ter" is probably fine) and hit ENTER...or you could hit SUPER and click on the applications tab and under "installed" click "see more results" and look for terminal....or you could hit ALT+F2 if you just need to run a command without seeing the terminal output.  

Of course, if you use the terminal alot, just add it to the launcher.  Once you get a terminal open, right click on its icon in the launcher and click "lock to launcher."  You never have to look for it again.

> **vyoufinder said:**
> Why do I have to click  multiple times, even more than before to install anything or remove  anything?  Ugh!!!!

Not sure what you are talking about with this one.  The icon for the Software Center is on the launcher...click on it and search for an app...click install.  It's the same as always.
