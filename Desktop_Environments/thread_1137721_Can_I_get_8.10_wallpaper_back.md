### Post by yabbadabbadont on 2009-04-25
[http://packages.ubuntu.com/search?suite=intrepid&searchon=names&keywords=ubuntu](http://packages.ubuntu.com/search?suite=intrepid&searchon=names&keywords=ubuntu)

Download the appropriate deb files, then extract the contents.  DO NOT JUST INSTALL THEM.  First off, they probably have dependencies that are old and secondly, they would probably overwrite the current files and might break things.

Once you have the files extracted from the debs, you can manually place the ones you want where they belong.
