> I also know that wireless is at best, a huge pain in the *** to get working under all of the linux distributions I know of

This won't solve your problem at all, but, just to reassure others who read this thread, my experience was quite the opposite. I bought a Belkin Wireless G Notebook Network Card for my clapped-out old Dell Latitude laptop, plus a Netgear Wireless ADSL router. Logged into the router and set up the WEP encryption and ADSL connection, then activated my laptop card in Ubuntu using the System, Administration, Networking and it worked beautifully, first time.

So, it's not ALWAYS a pain, although I feel yours when it doesn't go smoothly.
