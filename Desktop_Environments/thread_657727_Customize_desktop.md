### Post by rainwalker on 2008-01-04
I recommend gnome-look as well, and just to explain;
GTK themes will usually change everything about your theme, but at the very least they affect the controls (the way buttons, scrollbars, etc. look)
Metacity themes are for your window borders only.
Beryl (Emerald) themes are only if you decide to use Emerald, they won't work with Metacity.
Icon themes are (you guessed it) for icons.
GDM themes are for the login screen.

All but the GDM themes can be installed by saving the file (usually a .tar or .tar.gz archive) to your desktop, and then dragging it into your appearance preferences window (System > Preference > Appearance)
To install GDM themes, go to System > Administration > Login Window and look under the "Local" tab. You drag and drop the themes just like the others.
