### Post by justin.rixx on 2010-07-10
I had a very strange sound issue a while back and couldn't figure out how to fix it. I could get no sound from any apps unless they were preinstalled. I also couldn't get sound from flash, HTML5, or anything web-based. I had pretty much given up hope, until my friend told me how to fix it. So now I'm posting it here. :D

Please note: I am using KDE SC 4.4.5. You may have a different version. They will probably be the same methods for fixing, but if they are not identical, they should be similar.

1 - Click the sound applet in the system tray (bottom right corner)

2 - Make sure that "Master" is not muted

3 - If that doesn't fix it, click "Mixer"

4 - Now there should be some sliders. You may have multiple tabs. (My computer has two, one for my speakers, one for my HDMI port) My fix was turning up the middle slider titled "PCM" in the "HDA ATI SB" tab. Your fix may be different. Just turn up any of the sliders if it is down.

This should work for any distro, though I have not tried it on any others besides Kubuntu and Linux Mint 9 KDE RC.
