### Post by sir_cheats_a_lot on 2008-09-28
well.  are you you dual booting windows and linux or did you rid yourself of windows entirely?   
   if you are dual booting then go here:
[http://www.fs-driver.org/](http://www.fs-driver.org/)
and download and install that and it'll give you access to your linux partition from windows and you can just copy over your files to the windows side or to the external drive that way. 

   if you wiped the whole drive, then you might be able to mount your linux install from a livecd session and just move the files to the external drive that way too.

i just use these methods because it's usually simpler and less frustrating; not to mention i can never remember the bash command for it, lol
