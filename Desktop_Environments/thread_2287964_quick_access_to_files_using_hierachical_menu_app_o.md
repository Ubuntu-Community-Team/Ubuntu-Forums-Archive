### Post by mfox on 2015-07-23
I am trying to replicate something I have on my Mac that helps my workflow. It is provided by a piece of shareware called FinderPop. I can right-click on the desktop to gain quick access to my frequently used files and folders; it provides hierarchical menus, and I can open a file from that menu. It is also customizable, so I can can put into it my frequently used folder shortcuts.

Does such a thing exist for Ubuntu, or something that at least allows me to quickly drill through folders and open a file of interest? The closest thing to this that I know of is the openbox window manager, but even it doesn't provide a hierarchical menu or a way to open a file by drilling through folders. The two desktop environments I typically use are Unity and Gnome, so a utility that does what I'm looking for would be most welcome. Suggestions?

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-07-23
The Dash does it for me. Ctrl+F will open the Dash and reveal recently used files and folders and give the ability to filter the search results.
