### Post by silverbullet007 on 2007-05-21
I doubt if any of you have noticed but I've (along with MANY others) have been fighting Wireless issues on Fiesty using both WPA and WEP since it's release.  While I love the idea of an every 6 month major release, not at the expense of the trouble Fiesty has caused.  I had a minor success with it on an IBM Thinkpad A31...everyhting worked perfectly.  But on a desktop (HP sff, Dell Optiplex GX 520) with wirless PCI cards (Using both the Atheros and Intel chipsets) had nothing but problems.

Over the weekend I installed PCLinuxOS on a whim, WPA connected right out of the box.  Perfectly.  No extra config, no modifying of config files, no apt-get of anything else...it's just worked.  Couple with the Beryl that's bundled in as well as the 2.6.18.8 kernel I'm loving it.

i was an avid Ubu follower until Fiesty and yes while like many I could have gone back to Edgy I didnt want to go "backwards".

I hope to return to a solid distro whenever the devs get these Fiesty bugs worked out.

Thanks to all that helped me and gave assistance.
