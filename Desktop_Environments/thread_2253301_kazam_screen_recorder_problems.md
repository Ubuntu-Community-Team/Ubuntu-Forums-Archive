I think it is a gnome shell problem. It works very well in Ubuntu (unity) The button is in Kazam's tray icon. In GS the tray icon either doesn't show up (bug)  or shows up in a strange place (by design)  In GS the notification area is at the bottom of the screen and it is normally hidden, you have to move the mouse down and hold to see it, maybe it is hiding there (e.g it is where you will find skype's icon)

### Post by courtney2 on 2014-11-30
I found the best solution. Yes, it's a GNOME shell issue, but with legacy icons! Since they edited the menu to the top right, an icon doesn't appear for you to end the recording. Best solution was to install the TopIcons extension :)

[https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/495/topicons/](https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/495/topicons/)

In my opinion, all other screen recorders kind of suck...hehe. Haven't found anything as good as kazam yet, even though framerates aren't the most impressive and 23fps is what appears to be the only good setting
