### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-11-07
Install arandr from the repositories. That will allow you to do anything you like. If you need the full explaination (which you probably won't after playing around a bit) Rex Bouwense link says it all (or just ask).

I have a laptop with a separate monitor on a shelf above and use them as two separate screens (they can even have separate wallpapers). Extremely handy, like having two computers in one. Once you have it how you like, save layout and when you reboot, launch arandr, click File>Open>Open the saved layout>click the green tick. Done.
