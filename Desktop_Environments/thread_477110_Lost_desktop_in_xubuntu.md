### Post by dolphin_oracle on 2007-06-18
go into your xfce settings and you should find settings similar to "Allow XFCE to manage my desktop" or close to it.  Check that box and you should get your desktop back.  I'm at work and i can't tell you exactly where int he menus the setting is, but its fairly obvious once you find it.
