### Post by CelticWarrior on 2021-02-03
> [COLOR=#000000]add desktop shortcuts manually (where/how)[/COLOR]
Don't.
Gnome has been designed not to make it easy. Some may disagree with the decisions/concept/worldview/philosophy of the dev team behind Gnome but I tend to agree with them in this instance: Desktop should be kept clean. Files/folders operations and even simple program shortcuts is an &#8203;unintended side-effect of a feature added to a certain proprietary OS a long long time ago with the goal of imitating the workflow of another more polished proprietary OS. Once the cat was out of the bag this became a nasty habit for users going back a generation, at least.

That said, you may want to take a look at [https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/2087/desktop-icons-ng-ding/](https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/2087/desktop-icons-ng-ding/)

> [COLOR=#000000]add unlisted files/programs to the favorites bar[/COLOR]
Programs yes, files no (really?)
Just open any program you want to add, right-click its icon and choose "add..." Couldn't be simpler.

> [COLOR=#000000]change the size of the icons in the favorites bar, hide the favorites bar, etc.[/COLOR]
Settings > Appearance for size and to enable auto-hide.
You need to be more specific about the "etc." though.
