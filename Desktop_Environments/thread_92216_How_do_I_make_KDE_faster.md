### Post by bin on 2005-11-22
The spec of your machine is well adequate for KDE, the 2 best ways to make linux go faster are to fit more memory and a faster hard-drive. If those options are not open to you then lets ignore them and focus on what you have got.

It's worth checking the size of your swap partition, min 500mb for your machine. Make sure you've got at least 500mb free on your / ptn as well to allow elbow room.

Check to make sure that your IDE cables are all seated properly, your power supply vents are not home to a family of dust puppies and that all fans etc are clean. Make sure your hard drive is not on the same IDE cable as any CD drive.

Enable DMA on your hard drive - you'll find many threads about this in the forums

I use a PIII 700 laptop with 256mb ram and 6gb 4200rpm drive. 8mb on board ATI graphics
Partitioned 3gb / 500mb swap /home the rest

1. Konqueror is a grea file manager, but IMHO a rotten browser - I also find it sluggish so it's not just you:)  Firefox, Epiphany-Browser, Opera - all offer good alternatives. I prefer F/Fox because I know how to get RealPlayer working with it, but your needs may vary.

2. Window decorations - KDE 2 - disable stiple
3. Style - lightstyle 2nd or 3rd revision
4. Icons - kde hi-colour
5. GUI effects - disabled - plain colour desktop
6. Show Windows contents moving/resizing - off
7. Klipper - disabled.

General
8. All the foreign language fonts deleted
9. Use the 686 linux-image - easily available via adept or synaptic.
10. Disable unused services - not a big saver, but if you don't need fetchmail or ppp or CPU freq scaling then switch 'em off - search forum for the topic -  and yes I know most services sit idle!

Don't be afraid to trash it all and start again, if you have /home on a different ptn you can keep all your user stuff. If you do this, you can also copy the contents of /var/cache/apt/archives over before re-installing. Copy them all back again afterwards and when you go to reinstall them via adept / synaptic they won't have to be downloaded again.

It may also help to install Ubuntu first, then drop KDE on top. I cannot explain why, but I find it seems crisper and more stable than Kubuntu - it also makes for some very messy menus:) :)  HTH

in light
