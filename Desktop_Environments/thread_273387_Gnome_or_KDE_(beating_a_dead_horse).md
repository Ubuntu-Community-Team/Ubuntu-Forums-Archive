### Post by aysiu on 2006-10-08
Most of the problems of having Gnome and KDE together are minor and fixable. These three are the most common:

1. **Cluttered menus**--get a bunch of KDE applications in your Gnome menus and vice versa. Can be fixed by manually deleting entries.

2. **KDE themes "taking over" certain Gnome themes.** Can be fixed by selecting the blank theme from the gtk-engines part of KControl.

3. **Weird invisible "desktop" file appearing**. If you keep switching back and forth between KDE and Gnome a lot, you'll notice that a weird invisible "desktop" file will appear as "no name" in Gnome dialogues. If you delete it, it'll be gone again until you log in and out of KDE again. Then it'll reappear.

Those are the only problems I know about.
