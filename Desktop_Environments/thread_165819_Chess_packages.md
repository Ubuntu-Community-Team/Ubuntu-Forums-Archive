### Post by Qrk on 2006-04-25
I recommend gnuchess and eboard, both of them are available from the repositories. You can change gnuchess' skill level through eboard by how long it is allowed to think before a move. 

```
sudo apt-get install gnuchess eboard
```

gnuchess is the engine, eboard is the interface.
I haven't found a good chess GUI for Gnome to compare with KDE's Knights. Most of the Gnome chess frontends are quite ugly, but they work well enough.

I also like fruit and crafty chess engines but they seem far more buggy.

### Post by ComplexNumber on 2006-04-25
there is also csboard. it uses mono because its written in C#, though. its rather minimal, but the level can be changed.
i don't find knights to be that good. i run that in suse at the moment.  the level can't be changed, and it has a number of other annoying quirks.
there are a range of engines to choose from - crafty and gnuchess being the most common. they each have their own 'personalities'. i find crafty to be more tactical(aggressive), whilst gnuchess to be more positional

you may want to have a look at [this]("http://www.enpassant.dk/chess/softeng.htm")
