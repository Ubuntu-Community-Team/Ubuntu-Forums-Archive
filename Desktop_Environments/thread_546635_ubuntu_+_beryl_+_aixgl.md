### Post by Sqwishy on 2007-09-09
If you don't have your cd then ...
At [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download) look under "What type of computer do you have?" and choose "64bit". Then choose a place to download it from and that should be the cd image to burn onto a blank disk. 

If you've got your cd already, you can try it on your own, it's pretty strait forward, especially if you don't have any other OS's on the HD (like you said you got a 'brand new harddisk'). 

Compiz fusion is unstable, but is still usable. it's unstable in the way that a bunch of features won't work and will crash compiz. But it's not randomly unstable, so _most_ of the time you won't be using something and all of a sudden for no apparent reason you're window manager will die. Like I said, It's defiantly usable!
