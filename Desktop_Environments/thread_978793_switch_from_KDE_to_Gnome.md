### Post by pickarooney on 2008-11-11
So, I upgraded my laptop from Hardy to Intrepid and, once I'd got over the inevitable problem of not having a graphical interface (way to go, team, another fantastic innovation to make life easier for neophytes) booted up to find, to my horror, that not only had my desktop wallpaper, shortcuts, toolbars, window decorations, shortcuts and file associates all been callously discarded by the Intrepid installer, but the whole lot had been replaced with some dark and foreboding half-arsed imitation of Vista!

Well, that's quite enough of the abomination that is KDE4 on that machine, I said, and promptly installed gnome and the Mac4Lin theme (ah, irony!). It's all looking swell, with a couple of exceptions. I can't seem to 
- change the bootup splash to Ubuntu instead of Kubuntu (no biggie)
- change the login screen to something other than the pink sky horror included in the theme I installed
