### Post by ddennedy on 2006-08-06
I am lead developer of Kino. I am not aware of any Linux support for DV over USB. Even though it has been made into a standard, I believe it still somewhat rare to find on devices, but I could be wrong now. It is something I would like to support, but I lack such a device, and I would rather have HDV when buying a new camera :-). However, doing a little research now, I have found [http://www.pixela-1.com/captycable/](http://www.pixela-1.com/captycable/) that is affordable and ought to help. That does not help you now or even in the near term. Therefore, I encourage you buy a cheap firewire card.

### Post by Quicky on 2006-11-21
I bought a Sony Handycam a couple of days ago, then realised I couldn't transfer the video to my laptop via USB. Even using Windows was difficult thanks to the terrible software supplied by the camera.

Today I bought a Firewire card for my laptop, plugged it in, plugged in the camera, loaded up Kino and hit capture. Everything worked flawlessly.

They don't crop up very often in Ubuntu, but it's moments like this that make me glad I made the switch from Windows. I had resigned myself to spending the night getting stressed and dicking aimlessly about with the terminal all evening, but instead I can go to the pub and watch United. Awesome.
