### Post by 3Miro on 2010-11-23
KDE comes with applications of its own. If you install kubuntu-desktop, you will have both KDE and Gnome apps, which may cause some clutter in the menu. There are also additional libraries and packages that one uses and the other not. Once installed, KDE and Gnome are hard to remove, since it is hard to figure out what you are still using and what not. Kubuntu will leave you will clean KDE and Gnome.

If you want to just try KDE, the best option will probably be to install kubuntu-desktop. If you decide that you like Gnome or KDE better, you can just no use the other one. This will occupy some extra space on your HDD, but unless you have a small HDD making a clean reinstall is an overkill.
