Any ideas?
its because  you now have no transparency. i guess the xorg.conf must have been reconfigured back to its original state (ie without graphics acceleration  and transparency etc). 
when a person first installs linux, the xorg.conf is in its initial state. so when you run things that require transparency such as cairo-clock etc, then they will just appear with a black background. when the changes are done to the xorg.conf and transparency is enabled, cairo-clock and others that require transparency will become transparent.
