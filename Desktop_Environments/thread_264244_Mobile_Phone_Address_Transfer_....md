I'm not sure about your current phone model, but most modern phones store contacts in a vCard format, which you should be able to import into Evolution or Thuderbird Addressbooks.

I recently discovered this site which I use as a backup for my phone's data;

[https://zyb.com/](https://zyb.com/)

However I have a Nokia N80, so with the built in WiFi its easy to transfer information back and forth and also I can email to and from the phone easily. I am not sure whether you will be able to upload your current phone's information. Looking at your phone's specs, the only method I can see is by sending your contacts as SMS Emails, but this could be costly if you have a lot of contacts. Alternatively, you could look into getting an infra-red receiver for your PC and sending the contacts that way. You may have some luck with Gnokii but I haven't tried it;

[http://www.gnokii.org/index.shtml](http://www.gnokii.org/index.shtml)

What sort of budget do you have and how soon do you need the phone? Trolltech have just launched a Linux phone, but it is currently aimed at developers and so there are no commercial models out yet.

[http://www.linuxdevices.com/news/NS8030785497.html](http://www.linuxdevices.com/news/NS8030785497.html)

HTH
