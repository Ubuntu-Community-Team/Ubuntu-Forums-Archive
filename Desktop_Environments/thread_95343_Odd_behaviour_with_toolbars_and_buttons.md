### Post by vaibhav on 2005-11-26
I am experiencing some odd behaviour in GNOME. I don't know if this is Ubuntu specific or applies to GNOME in general, but since I use Ubuntu, I thought I'd post here.



In the Menus and Toolbars preferences, I've enabled the "Detachable Toolbars" option. Hence I get a nice handle on the extreme left of a toolbar in all GNOME apps, which I can drag around. But when I detach a toolbar, I'm not able to attach it again. A detached toolbar just doesn't get dragged.



Another problem is sensitivity of buttons. Say there's an application which has a disabled button, and is doing some work in the background. I position my mouse pointer over this disabled button, and once the application does it's job and enables the button again, the button won't click. I have to move the mouse pointer away from the button and then position it back over this button before I can click it.

For example, in the Ubuntu Device Database application, I experience this behaviour with the Forward button. Also I've seen this in Synaptic; when I open Synaptic, the toolbar buttons are disabled while it scans for the package list. Position the mouse pointer over a toolbar button, and once it's enabled, it won't click until you move the mouse away and then back over it.

Am I doing something wrong or everyone else is also experiencing this behaviour? I haven't searched bugzilla to see if this has already been filed.
