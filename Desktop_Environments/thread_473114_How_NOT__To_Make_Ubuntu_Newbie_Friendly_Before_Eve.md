### Post by jimwg on 2007-06-13
To whoever handles public relations at Ubuntu:

This is more a public relations (and we all could do with good ones!) than programming issue, but it'd REALLY be nice for Ubuntu download pages to state that Feisty Fawn does not do flash drive persistence mode before a long download. That was a nasty surprise and put-off to one of my PC newbie colleagues here and maybe a bad word about Ubuntu right off the bat now. Heck, even I was vexed when my 6.10 persistence flash drive files don't take, along with no "Read Me" warning ahead of time. A newbie wanting to try out Feisty Fawn or any "People Friendly" Ubuntu software shouldn't have to comb a web site to find out that a key feature is faulty or lacking. It should be right smack on the download page. Good public relations makes good customer relations 

James Greenidge
