### Post by jonrkc on 2006-10-14
I use IceWM as window manager in Ubuntu because of its flexibility in arranging windows.  You can tile horizontally, vertically, randomly, or "arranged" (makes sure all windows are visible), or cascade windows, or just keep them where you move them manually.  All the configurations are available with key combinations which can be customized.  For example, I have my setup such that pressing ALT+z tiles all the windows so they're nice and visible.  I could just as easily tile them vertically or horizontally the same way if I wanted to.

I suspect there's a way to do this in KDE, too, since you can do almost anything in KDE, but KDE has enough drawbacks for me that I seldom use it.  IceWM is very fast, very reliable, and very customizable.

Just my preference for the past three years or so.
