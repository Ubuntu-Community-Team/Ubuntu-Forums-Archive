### Post by bored2k on 2006-03-01
Although using Automatix shouldn't affect your performances, most of the packages it installs are not officially supported by Ubuntu, so we can't *officially* say those packages do any wrong or good on your computer. That said, for the most part, **I** don't think the extra unofficial packages installed by automatix should do any bad on your system. On the other hand, using some of it setups like the DMA can modify your performance on certain things (for better or worse).

In short, Automatix not being an official package and thus not officially supported, I can't really force myself into saying Automatix is good or bad on your system or to say that it's what making your box sluggish.
