### Post by Beggar on 2007-09-19
So after looking around for a while I didnt see a good post where anyone explained how to fix the nvidia twinview, background gets stretched out bug.

Then I thought about it for a second, and realized theres a simple fix. However I have seen alot of posts that ask how to fix this, so I wanted to post this up here in case anyone is looking. So without further ado:



step 1) get the image you want to be displayed as your background and open it in the gimp.
step 2) go to system -> pref -> screen resolution and find your screen resolution.
step 3) in the gimp, go to image -> scale image and change image to be your exact screen resolution. (This may mean you need to click the chain looking button to make it not exactly to the original scale.) Then save the image.
step 4) go to system->pref-> background add your image, and change the style to tiled.

Done. Now when you have a single monitor (like my laptop normally does) it will display your background once. When you have a second output hooked up, it will just tile the image and display a second copy of it on the second desktop.
