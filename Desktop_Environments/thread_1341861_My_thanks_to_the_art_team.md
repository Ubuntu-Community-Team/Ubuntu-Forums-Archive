### Post by operative.terminus on 2009-11-30
This thread is for the xubuntu 9.10 graphic designers. I have been using GNU/Linux for some time, and one thing I always do is change the theme and wallpaper with every distro I install. xubuntu 9.10 is the first distro I have installed in which I have not felt at all compelled to change its default appearance. I wish to give my heartfelt thanks in your creation of such a eye-pleasing release. 

PS: I don't want to leave the xubuntu devs out of this. I have been using Archlinux with Xfce for some time, and I have never been able to assemble an Xfce desktop quite so nice by myself. Good job!

### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-11-30

Yes, and I wish them well. The "lubuntu-desktop" package is available in the karmic repos even now. But frankly, it sucks.

LXDE has been so darned buggy in every single Ubuntu-based variant I've tried, that I have come to feel that it isn't "ready for the desktop" yet.

I'm still a LXDE fan, because my old remix (minimal Ubuntu with LXDE), based on jaunty, was instrumental in converting a few families at the dance studio to Linux. None of its bugs was a show stopper by itself, but taken together and over a period of time, and across multiple distros, they just became a nuisance I don't need to bother with.

I did earn alot during my brief but intense love affair with LXDE, and I still use the awesome PCManFM file manager from LXDE. I wish Lubuntu well. But I'm not surprised that the Lubuntu project (and U-Lite, and MoonOS, and Masonux, and other Ubuntu-based LXDE projects) has been fraught with delays and bugs and kept out of the mainstream all this time. 

I've learned that LXDE takes most of its code from Xfce, and I suspect that when it finally matures, it will be very little different from Xfce.

-Robin
