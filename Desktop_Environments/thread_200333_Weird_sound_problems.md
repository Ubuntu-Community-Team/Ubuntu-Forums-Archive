### Post by pellgarlic on 2006-06-20
it is best to choose one, and forget the other - having onboard sound enabled and an "add-on" sound-card seems to cause problems for some people. either:

1. forget the sound blaster, and stick with the onboard sound. or
2. disable the onboard sound in the bios, and go with the sound blaster.

which you choose depends on which is the better card for you, although onboard sound tends to be pretty weak... but i don't know how your "old soundblaster" compares to it.
