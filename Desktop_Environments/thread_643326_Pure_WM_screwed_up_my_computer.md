### Post by J.C. on 2007-12-17
I notice that it seems to be the rage to do a netinstall for Debian or have a server edition on Ubuntu and pick your own apps.  This was something I looked forward to during this weekend.  So I did a command line install on my T23 Thinkpad and worked in a pure fluxbox environment.  The strange thing about this was that my system began to heat up, and there was a small scratching noise in the back of my system.  I don't know what was the cause of this, but at that time, I thought it was just a coincidence.

I've went back to a GNOME environment since, and I no longer have those issues-- my thinkpad no longer heats up, and the scratching noise is gone.

Is it not nesscarily a good idea to have a pure WM environment on an old computer?... And why wouldn't it be a good idea?  Because this seems ironic, doesn't it?
