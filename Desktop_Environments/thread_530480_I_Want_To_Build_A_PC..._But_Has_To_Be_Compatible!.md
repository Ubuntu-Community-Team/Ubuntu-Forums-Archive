### Post by logos34 on 2007-08-20
Can't recommend specific make and model components, but here's my general observations:

Shop at Newegg.com.  They're have overall the lowest prices and best selection.  Read the reviews--more often than not someone will have tried out a particular piece of hardware on linux (often ubuntu because it's the most popular distro).  

As anyone will tell you, nvidia is your best bet for graphics compatibility. (avoid ATI).  If you want to save even more money (and you don't game) consider integrated video (up to 256MB).

Personally I'd wait a few months--the [new Phenom quad-core]("http://multicore.amd.com/us-en/AMD-Multi-Core/Products/Desktop-PC.aspx") AMD desktop chips are due out soon.  They'll probably be aggressively priced to compete with Intel's offering.  But if they're a little over your budget, you can always pick up a screamin' top-line X2 Windsor core chip for ~$175USD or less.  

You may not even need a separate sound card if the board you choose has HD audio (6 or 8 channel).  If the onboard card has s/pdif out, and you have a digital stereo receiver, pipe it to that.  But if you do any kind of audio production then you will want a high-quality add-in card.

RAM: DDR2 667 or 800 (the lower latency/CL the better)

You can get a good (non-SLI) board for less than a $100.  Best: Asus, MSI, ABIT, Gigabyte, Biostar, DFI and others.  Avoid Jetway, PCChips like the plague.  Preferably with a good Bios (especially if you're considering OC'ing), RAID, gigabit ethernet.  MicroATX is ok if you want to sacrifice PCI slots.

Hard drive: perpendicular-recording technology SATA with 16MB cache, like Seagate 7200.10 series.  Or Western Digital Caviar SE series. 

Finally, don't even THINK about skimping on the power supply--chances are you'll regret it.  Seasonics in general are top-notch.  The higher the efficiency rating the better (+ ~80%).  500W + dual-rail, passive or active PFC (modular cabling a real plus)

Edit:  See hardware support links here:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/)
