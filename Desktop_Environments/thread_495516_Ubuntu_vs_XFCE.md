### Post by Taino on 2007-07-08
I used Gnome (Ubuntu) for the longest and have switched to Xfce (Xubuntu) now, At first i felt i was missing something, things seemed lighter after the switch, and i really was missing something... (the added Gnome bloat). Gnome is great but it depends on the system your running it on and on how you like to run your system, I like to compute quickly about as fast as i can think, and the added speed of Xfce in doing everything impressed me alot so im sticking with Xfce (Xubuntu).

Its lighter, faster, simpler, and customizing it is only really limited by your own imagination. My Xubuntu install went from default to me making a bunch of tweaks and changes here and there to have the system look and feel the way i want it and im happy with it right now.

From editing menus, to dragging and dropping application icons and status icons, these things can all be done in Xfce (Xubuntu) in settings and menu properties, similar to Gnome, just have to work with it and learn how to make it do and look they way you want.

Then there's (Fluxbuntu) Fluxbox based Ubuntu, still in development which is even lighter and faster still and can be customized quite a bit if you make the effort, Its faster and lighter than Xfce and way faster and lighter than Gnome.
