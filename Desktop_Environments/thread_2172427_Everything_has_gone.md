
And choose your desktop at boot (login) by clicking on the icon.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-09-06
The feature you complain about is not new. It has been Ubuntu since 2011. I am surprised that you are not aware of the change, which by the way was required anyway by the Gnome organisation stopping development of the old Gnome interface that you like so much in favour of Gnome 3 and Gnome 3 shell. All Ubuntu has done is put in its own Unity user interface instead of Gnome 3 shell. Did you not run the Ubuntu live session to see what you were getting? 

Just press Super+A and there you will find all the applications. Click on Filter Results and there you will have the menu structure you desire, kind of. It takes me just a few seconds. Myself, I just press the super key and type the name of the application that I want and the Dash finds it for me. Then all I have to do is press Enter. Of course, I can always fix the application to the Launcher. Then I is just one click away from being launched. Even a dummy like me can learn to do this. 

The thing that drew me to Ubuntu in the first place was that it was a distribution for humans.

There are alternatives, such as Xubuntu or Lubuntu or even another Linux distribution entirely.
