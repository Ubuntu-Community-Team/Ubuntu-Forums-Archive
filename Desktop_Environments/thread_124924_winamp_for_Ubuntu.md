### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-11-17
Much of the multimedia may be found under the Medibuntu repositories due to license concerns; if you don't already have this and choose to use them you must add them manually. See thier page "Repository How-To"
[http://www.medibuntu.org/](http://www.medibuntu.org/)
You can do this in Synaptic. After adding them and updating your list, you may then be able to find and install certain packages.

For WinAmp, my fav player in Windows, you can try out XMMS, BeepMediaPlayer (BMP) or Audacious. Audacious, particularly the latest 1.4rc1 you'll find at their site, is probably the best and is the most actively developed at this time; it may be tricky to install. If you're pretty new to all this try the versions in the repositories via Synaptic first. All of these will allow you to use WinAmp skins as well. What they don't offer is a "media library manager" for which you may wish to try Amarok, Exhaile, gmusicbrowser or Rhythmbox. Amarok also integrates easy access to the wonderful music available at MagnaTune. Personally I tend to use Amarok for music and VLC (which you may have used in windows?) for video. 

Anyway, enjoy :)
