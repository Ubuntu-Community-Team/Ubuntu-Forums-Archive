### Post by alquimista on 2005-09-27
Hi,
I’m a Software Engineer  that as most of us started 20 years ago with the only option available on the market for PC: MS-DOS, and then Win… a couple of years ago I had close friends involved in the Open Source community, and most of the day I heard about Linux and saw it operating as server; I read a little and some articles mentioned that on the server side, Linux  was powerful and a clear option vs Win server. In the desktop yet, there was a way to go and convince 95% of desktop users to move or try Linux. I felt tired to be tied to Microsoft… and I started to test Open Source  software (still on Win OS) as Open Office 2, PostgreSQL, PHP, Firefox. I liked it, and I liked more the fact that there was a community willing to help to those we were “awaking from the Matrix”.
So 3 months ago I decided to make the bold move: installing Kubuntu on my notebook, sharing a FAT32 partition with WinXP. I was happy, and saying “I’m finally free”… and I was stubborn enough to have Kubuntu as my main OS, leaving WinXP just for “emergencies” or for software I could not have in Linux, like some data base designers.
I posted questions in 4 Web forums and searched over Google trying to solve initial problems operating Kubuntu in my Toshiba A60 notebook; I think 90% of them were solved, yet there was 10% that bugged me … Recently I started a project that unfortunately needed MS Office, and I had to take it even I tried to choose OpenOffice 2. Now I’m in the verge of sadly going back to WinXP… the reasons are:
1.	Open Office 2 is even slower to start up in Kubuntu than in WinXP. It is less stable in Kubuntu; there I lost 3 or 4 files, something that never happened in WinXP using OOffice 2 with updated releases, along 6 months. I know it’s a beta not recommended for production, but in Win it just runs better. 
2.	Trying to use MS Office 2000 on CrossOver, is slow… and having maybe 3 o 4 office windows opened, suddenly get freeze or crash.
3.	I tried Koffice, yet I make extensive use of tables, auto-numbering, indentation, and exports to HTML; I could not find enough features in Koffice to edit/save my documents having those requirements. I had to accept that MS Office has an excellent export functionality to HTML, but what angers me is that generates special code that obligates you to use just Internet Explorer; Firefox can’t always read those HTML files.
4.	I always had trouble sending large attachments using Kontact or Evolution. Attachments of 1 MB or more, just could not be sent, getting wired mail errors or time outs, even when I configured the Server TimOut period in Kcontrol. In Outlook that happens sometimes on the first file send try; but after 2 o 3 tries, it goes through.
5.	I could never control the Synaptics mouse Touchpad in Kcontrol; it always was speedy and my brain had trouble operating the computer with such a mouse. The hardware seemed to ignore Kcontrol settings. I followed a post in this news group that suggested to change “xorg.conf”, changing the driver “synaptics” to driver “mouse” in the “Section "InputDevice" Identifier "Synaptics Touchpad"”. Settings in Kcontrol now were taken by the touchpad, the problem is that some behavior changed tapping the pad and moving the mouse (behavior not related to settings in Kcontrol), and the solution was not good.
6.	I was never able to play a video DVD using Kaffeine,  Mplayer or other multimedia software.
7.	I was never able to play a music CD on amaroK, just in Kaffeine; I preferred amaroK because had better sound quality and equalizer controls with the xine-engine. In Kaffeine, the sound was “basic”. In amaroK I got device errors wit the music CD… wired when Kaffein could read it.
8.	I have two accounts in MSN, and the funny thing is that I just could open one of them, and the second, the one I use the most, rarely could be opened using aMSN or Kopete.
9.	One thing I needed like in Win, using Neros’s InCD, is the ability to see a CD-RW like another storage unit, deleting and writing individual files on the CD. In K3B, I could not delete individual files on the CD; I had to append files like a normal CD or reformat it completely, if a wanted to change its contents. 
10.	Sometimes, when I created a file under Kubuntu in my FAT32 partition, I could not see it under WinXP; I had to run a checkdisk in order to “refresh” the directory content to see it in Windows.

I hope to come back to Linux soon… I liked it a lot; Kubuntu was my first Linux experience… some people have recommended me to test other distros like OpenSuse… Any way, I think one must go and take the best option for his/her needs. Yes, I want to follow the Open Source / Linux  path as a user and eventual contributor, but may be I have to be more patient to have open source software that covers all my needs, or just have common sense and accept the reality that all my needs will be fulfilled sometimes by Win or commercial software, and sometimes by Linux/Open Source, and maybe that’s ok.

Best regards,
Guillermo
