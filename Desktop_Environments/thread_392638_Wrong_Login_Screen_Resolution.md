### Post by mgmiller on 2007-03-25
I just did a little research on your monitor and I found it is analog only.  This being the case, while you are on the log in screen, is there an "auto adjust" button on the monitor or in its onscreen display that you can access?  Assuming your desktop is displaying correctly, it may only be a refresh rate on the log in screen issue.  This can be be addressed by using the "auto adjust" button on the monitor or the onscreen monitor controls to resize your image while on the login screen.  Being a multi synch monitor, it should remember this setting and use it automatically on future reboots.

Also, your xorg.conf does show you using 1440x900 with a 24 bit color depth.

### Post by ?@yk0&amp;^4 on 2007-03-27
I had the same problem, but I was able to fix it by adding a 'Virtual 1440 900' line after the 'Depth 24' line in my xorg.conf:

```
SubSection "Display"
                Depth           24
                Virtual         1440 900
                Modes           "1440x900" "1280x1024" "1024x768" "832x624" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
        EndSubSection
```

Hope that helps!
Greg.

### Post by ubutufan on 2007-11-25
Hi. I had the same problem. I downloaded the beta 7.10 and continued with updates until Gutsy was released. For sometime I thought the problem was the beta installation. After a lot of try and error, I managed to solve the problem. You need to edit the xorg.conf. So in console type
sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf

Look for Section "Screen"
Comment out the Modes. You now have no resolution option in xorg so you need to make one. Type
Modes   "1280x800@53"   (this is the case for my nvidia card GeForce Go 7400). You need to type the resolution and refresh rate applicable to your graphics card and monitor.

It solved the problem in my installation.
