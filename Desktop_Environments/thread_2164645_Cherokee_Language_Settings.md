### Post by gifford on 2013-08-14
I would suggest that with the misinformation out on the internet, you stick with the Cherokee Nation resources or with the Eastern Band resources. They are both federally recognized and are authentic, I am not sure if the UKB has online language resources but they are also legitimate. Both are devoting lots of resources to the language and we do have a comprehensive vocabulary. While there may be some variation because of dialect, they are the only authentic and legitimate sources for the language.
Tsalagi is a very complex tonal language and cannot be learned except by learning from a native speaker. As a citizen of the Cherokee Nation, I believe it is important for our language to be taught and preserved by us. It is inappropriate and an act of cultural appropriation for others to do so.
