### Post by Miniberger on 2006-08-27
I recently installed Ubuntu 6.0.6 on my Dell desktop in a dual-boot with XP. It is now working very well except for the fact that there is no sound.

I loaded my music into Rhythmbox, downloaded an mp3 encoder/decoder from automatix, and it now plays mp3s but no sound is produced. I then tried playing an audio CD with Soundjuicer, but still no sound. No application seems to make any sound.

I checked my device manager, which seems to recognise my sound card's brand and model(Creative Soundblaster Live).

Has anyone else had this problem, or does anyone know a possible solution?

---

### Post by RadixLecti on 2006-08-27
I've had trouble like yours.

In my case, linux had recognised and decided to use the built-in soundcard on the motherboard.

Try to disable the built-in soundcard in BIOS.

---

### Post by Miniberger on 2006-08-27
I went into my BIOS and disabled the onboard sound card.

Then, I booted Ubuntu, turned on my speaker and heard the login sound. I opened Rhythmbox and it's working perfectly, playing my .mp3s.

Thanks for the help RadixLecti.
