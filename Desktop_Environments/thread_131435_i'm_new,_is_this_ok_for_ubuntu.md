Everywhere I go, and everyone I talk to says NVIDIA has the best linux support of any graphics card, so odds are you'll be a-ok there. Did you order amd64 cd's or the regular i386? I have an intel 64 chip, and at first thought the 64 bit kernel would be better, and supposedly its supposed to be faster (though I don't notice a difference). The problem is that most of your favorite apps might not have 64-bit support yet (this is not ubuntu related, its the same on windows, in fact I've heard some terrible horror stories about getting drivers and everything going on windows 64-bit) , so you have to make a 32-bit chroot environment within your 64-bit environment. It is ALOT easier than it sounds (follow how-to = copy and paste commands), and it works pretty well, but the special i810 driver for my intel graphics card didn't work, so I just went with the standard i386 (for most all PC's), and everything works great!

I don't know much about your other hardware. Try searcing these forums with your model numbers, and you'll likely find some answers.
