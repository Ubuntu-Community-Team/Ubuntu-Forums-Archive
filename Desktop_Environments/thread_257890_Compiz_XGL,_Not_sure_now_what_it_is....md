### Post by Darkling on 2006-09-15
XGL and compiz are simply eye-candy, and not installed by default. Your video card may not be entirely useless tho, it will perhaps allow you to use higher resolutions or more colours.
And later, if you choose to, you can install xgl and compiz, and enable all that lovely eyecandy and reap the full benefit of having a 3D card. ### Post by cmost on 2006-09-15
To say that xgl/compiz are "just eye candy" is to mischaracterize the technology.  xgl is an accelerated xserver, like xorg or xfree86 (as well as some other commercial alternatives.)  With xgl, however, everything that's drawn on your screen is offloaded to the video card for rapid rendering and then mapped to the display.  The effect is a highly accelerated, smoothe desktop experience.  The so called eye-candy is provided by compiz, which is a window manager (like AMI or metacity.)  While it does offer lots of desktop bling, many of the the effects are quite useful and improve productivity.  Of these, cube (for managing virtual desktops,) expose (for quickly finding a particular window out of many open windows,) and transparency (for copying text from a window below a top-most but slightly transparent one) come to mind.  Since i've installed xgl/compiz, i've found my Linux to offer the most professional desktop operating system experience bar none!
