Typically, TV cards come with a cable that connects the TV card's sound output with the sound card's input. This way, you can control TV sound from your sound card controls. Have you connected this cable? If you don't have one, I'm sure it's easy enough to find, it's basically a heaphone jack on both ends (male/male).

If all connections are made correctly then start your sound console (alsamixer, probably) and put everything to max for testing. Often, the sound from a device you haven't used before comes from an obscure dial that is easy to forget.

HTH.

### Post by spizkapa on 2005-11-05
It's not the mic you should connect it to. It's the sound IN port. You probably have a sound OUT port for your speakers, a mic port (which should be left alone) and another... it's that one.

Apologies if this doesn't solve it, the possible hardware configurations are endless. For example, my sound card has many more holes at the back. Just try all of them!
