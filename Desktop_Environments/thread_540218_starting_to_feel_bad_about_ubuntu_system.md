### Post by raycosm on 2007-09-01
I don't know how well this will work for you, but there's a distro called Linux Mint, and it comes with Beryl preinstalled, and not a half sort kinda Beryl like desktop-effects that comes with Ubuntu. Linux Mint was the only distro I've tried out of four (actually, I haven't tried it on Fedora because I couldn't get my Wi-Fi working) that works with Beryl or CompizFusion. On Ubuntu and Kubuntu, Beryl **never** worked, I always got missing window borders, a whole bunch of extra workspaces, and a whole lot of other problems. On Linux Mint, Beryl worked perfectly. One thing though, Linux Mint took **forever** for me to download, unless I was using a Softpedia download. And Softpedia didn't have the KDE version that will fit on a CD.

I don't know how well it will work for you, but that's how I got my Beryl working. Although I just screwed it up a few minutes ago in [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=540165").
