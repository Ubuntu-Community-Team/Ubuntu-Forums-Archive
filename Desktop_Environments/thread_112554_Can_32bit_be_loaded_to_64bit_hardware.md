### Post by brentoboy on 2006-01-06
my advice - run 32 bit.

end of story.

Actually, there is a long story to go with it... but it boils down to this - ubuntu is based on debian. Debian looks at amd64 as a step-child - it doesnt have an official stable amd64 build.

Ubuntu recognizes that Amd64 is a significant player in the near future, so they target it.  And, it works great!

But, alas, the majority of linux apps are written and tested on 32 bit machines, so that is were they will work best.

I used Breezy Ubuntu64, and it works great, but some of the stuff in the universe just didnt do what I wanted - I had particular issues with CD burning.  Rather than troubleshoot them, I installed Ubu32 - which is working quite nicely.

Sure, I dont get the "performance benefits" but you know what - its doesnt seem any slower to me.
