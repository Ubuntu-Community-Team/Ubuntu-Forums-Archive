### Post by cptnapalm on 2006-08-05
The only time I've ever encountered something even remotely like that was with this mouse I had with a mind of its own...

If it is only in Penguin Racer, then my best suggestion (which may be worthless) is to wipe out the configuration files in your home directory for the game.  I'm not sure what they would be, but if you open Nautilus, click View and choose "Show Hidden Files", then you can peruse the files and folders which begin with a "."

Look for something like .ppc or .penguinracer or .planetpenguinracer or somesuch.  When you find something, open it up, just to make sure.  If it is the personal configuration file, then just delete it.  Fire up the game and see if it still does it.
