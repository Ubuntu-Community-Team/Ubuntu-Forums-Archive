### Post by hikaricore on 2007-03-24
Well I finally decided to make the big switch from Gnome to KDE on my main desktop.

Don't get me wrong, I loved my Gnome, and I customized the bloody hell out of it and made everything exactly how I wanted it.  But that's where the fun ended, I was
tired of laggy menus, slow loading apps, and hell even the inability to easily modify screensaver params.  So I took 2 hours out of my not so busy schedule tonight and made the switch.

I've been running Kubuntu on my server here at home and several servers at work, and as I became more comfortable with the interface and less dependant on Gnome, I realized
it had a lot of potential.  I installed the kubuntu-desktop meta package, and went on from there.

The part I was not so kean on was rebuilding my painstakingly constructed menus.  But after using the KDE menu editor along side Alacarte to make sure I wasn't missing anything, I feel
even happier I made the change.  My menus are huge, I mean abosultely huge.  I keep every application I've ever installed a few clicks away, and with this Gnome was struggling.
Alacarte never started out fast, but after adding hundreds of programs to the menus, it honestly takes about 1 minute just to launch the damn thing...and this is when I usually have
to add just one application, or fix one problem icon and what not.  There have been times where sorting my menus took me nearly 45 minutes, just due to the app's cumbersome methods. 

But anyway I managed to get everything recreated in my menus except my games menu which I think I'll tackle tomorrow when I have a few hours free.  It was just way too big for
today and I couldn't stand looking at alacarte much longer while double checking.  :)

I have my desktop setup pretty much the exact same way I did in gnome, KDE loads up in about 15 seconds where Gnome took nearly 2 minutes after all my tinkering.  I've managed
to closely match my hand crafted Clearlooks modification in the KDE environment, and I'm even running Beryl with Helidor so as to match the window borders of said theme, that I've
become used to while in Gnome land.

My two panels are near twins of their Gnome counterpart and react quickly and trustfully to my various clicks.  For a lack of a better word I'm overjoyed.  I believe I will be staying with KDE
for a long time to come.  Though my nautilus extensions and script menus will be missed, I doubt I'll have too hard of a time replacing all of those functions or even adding the scripts menu
somewhere around here.

### Post by dsm iv tr on 2007-03-24
> Kill Applet - (To kill a non responsive process in one click, like an eyedropper tool of death hehe)

KDE offers a keyboard shortcut to launch an instance of xkill. It's set to CTRL+ALT+ESC(ape) by default - hit that sequence and click on the window you want to kill. Alternately, you could create a shortcut on the panel or the desktop to launch `xkill`.
