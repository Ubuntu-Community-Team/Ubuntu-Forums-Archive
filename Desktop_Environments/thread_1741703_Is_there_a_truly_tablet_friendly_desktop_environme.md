### Post by Copper Bezel on 2011-04-30
Ubuntu is really pushing the netbook thing at the moment - Unity might be functional on a tablet, but it really wants a hard keyboard and preferably a trackpad. The objective is to move in the direction of more touch-friendly interface design and, far more importantly, adequate driver and gesture suite support to back that up, being developed in the ginn and utouch projects, respectively. For right now, Ubuntu isn't remotely there yet. (When it is, though, it'll be through Unity; no other project related to Ubuntu, or desktop Linux for that matter, is making as much an effort to push in that direction.)

It's still a situation where Android is a "phone OS" and Ubuntu is a "desktop OS," and Android is still moving faster toward the middle than Ubuntu is; but of course, it doesn't come with the perks of a more open operating system and a ready suite of real desktop-sized software.
