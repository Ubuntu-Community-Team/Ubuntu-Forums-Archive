### Post by mcduck on 2010-04-09
I suppose this is in Gnome? In that case, no, Nautilus doesn't have such option.

But there's still something you could do, I think Screelets has a widget that displays contents of a directory inside the widget, in the same way KDE4 does. You could place that on your second monitor, set it to show the Desktop directory and then just turn off the desktop icons from Nautilus (you can do that with gconf-editor). That way you'd have your icons where you want them, even though they are contained in a widget instead of directly on the desktop itself.
