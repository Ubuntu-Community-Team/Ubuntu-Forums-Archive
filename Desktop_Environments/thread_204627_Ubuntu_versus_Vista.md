### Post by x64Jimbo on 2006-06-27
It's not all about looks. Yes, some people only care about eye candy, but those people are certainly not the majority. The real test for possibility of ubiquity is usability and functionality. Vista will be DRM crippled to the point where many people will have significant problems with it and they won't be able to fix them without upgrading their hardware. This is where Linux will step in. It's capable of doing almost everything Windows does, and can actually do a lot more that Windows can't. The real area of focus for gaining market share is windows binary compatibility. People want to be able to run their windows programs. If the Wine engine is perfected and windows programs can be installed seamlessly and effortlessly, people will switch to Linux in droves.
