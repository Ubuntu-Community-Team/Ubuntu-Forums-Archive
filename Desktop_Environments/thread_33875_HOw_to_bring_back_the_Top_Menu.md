### Post by bkivey on 2005-05-12
If you still have the bottom panel, you can right click on it and select "New Panel".   This will make a new panel on your screen.  Then right click on the new panel and select "Properties" and make the Orientation "Top" (If it isn't already).  Then right click on it and say "Add to Panel" and select "Menu Bar - A custom menu bar".  That will put back the main Gnome menu, and then you can add the clock or whatever other panel applets you want.  Hope this helps...

### Post by Segovia on 2005-05-16
[QUOTE=pannerrammer]Err... I've just done the opposite (deleted the Bottom Menu) which shows the apps I'm running. How do I get it back?[/QUOTE]
Click whatever remaining panel you have: "New Panel".  Drag/Drop it where you want it (bottom), and add all your stuff back to it, as you like.

In the case that you don't know which thing to add to the panel and are looking for the one that "shows the apps I'm running", as you say - right click the new panel: "Add to Panel".  Scroll down and select "Window List".  You may need to resize it, as you like.
