### Post by shae on 2007-12-22
I would not be so gung-ho on installing linux if you interest is just Games.  Some games DO have problems in wine and even the games you listed may be tricky to install/configure to someone who is completely new.  Therefore you might consider dual-booting at first at least.  Also remember that basically any problem that you encounter can be solved with patience and willingness to learn how things are done in Ubuntu and Linux rather than being dead-set in your ways of thinking you picked up from only using windows.  

The greatest advantage of GNU/Linux to me is customization.  I can make my desktop look basically however I want from the buttons to the task tray.  No more are the days of windows where you had the choice of 3 colors and a background wallpaper.  

The next advantage of GNU/Linux is the practical lack of viruses, trojans, and spyware that windows suffers from.  Your main concern in Ubuntu is tracking cookies which an ABP filter takes care of nicely.  

No one can forget package management.  All your updates in one place basically.  An easy way to keep a system up to date and secure.  

You really cannot beat the price (free) including free updates and upgrades forever.

Your one real expectation which you must take away before you make the dive is:
[SIZE="5"]Linux is not Windows.[/SIZE]

### Post by Lorenz on 2007-12-23
What I recommend is using the Live CD.
You can download Ubuntu from the main website (ubuntu.com) and burn the image file to a CD. Then you restart your computer and it boots from the CD into the Ubuntu environment. This will allow you to test it without installing anything :) You will have access to your hard disks, and if you can get internet working (you need a codec for mp3, but it's just a click) you can try all the different programs to play movies, mp3, etc.
