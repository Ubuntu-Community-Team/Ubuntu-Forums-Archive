### Post by elvis on 2005-06-24
1) The "bunch" of RAM you have - is this all tested and working?  Is it proper ECC/Registered/Buffered stuff as required by the motherboard?

For many dual-processor systems you can't just slap ordinary RAM in them, as it will fail.  Make sure it's the right RAM for the board.

2) Test your system with the Ultimate Boot CD:
[http://www.ultimatebootcd.com/](http://www.ultimatebootcd.com/)

Run a variety of memory and CPU test programs for 12-24 hours per test to see if anything freezes.  If it does, it's your hardware at fault.

3) Ensure you are using the most up-to-date BIOS from Dell.  Dell have a bad habit of releasing flakey BIOSes, and updates later down the track when they figure out what went wrong.  In my last job I adminned an all-Dell network of a few hundred machines, and the quality of their BIOSes and last-minute fixes left much to be desired.

[http://support.dell.com/](http://support.dell.com/) is the main Dell support site.  Select your country at the top.  Enter the site, insert your machine's unique ID number and select the latest BIOS for download.
