### Post by Mimsy on 2006-10-26
Instructions for how to burn an .iso can be found [here.]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto") The faster you burn it, the higher the risk of errors. Burn slowly, in other words.

When you boot from the CD you will be given a number of options. "Start/install Ubuntu" should be at the top of the list, and below Memtest and a few others. Chose the one that lets you check the CD, and then follow the on-screen prompts.

/Mimsy

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-10-27
Put your burned CD into your CDROM drive. When the icon appears on the desktop, double-click it. If you see just one file sitting on the disk, you've burned it incorrectly. If there are lots of files and folders sitting on the disk, you've done it right.

Now restart the computer with the CD in the drive. Hold down the C key. When the black Yaboot screen appears, press the Return key. The rest should be self-explanitory.
