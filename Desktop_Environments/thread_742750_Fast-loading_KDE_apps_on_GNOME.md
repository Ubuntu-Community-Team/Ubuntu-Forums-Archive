### Post by bailout on 2008-04-02
It sounds to me as if you would be better running kubuntu and installing the few gtk/gnome apps. I ran both gnome and kde to start with and found I much preferred kde apps so went with kubuntu. In my experience running gtk apps in kde is better than kde apps in gnome. I also ran into problems running konq in gnome and much prefer it to nautilus. KDE is very customisable and it is quite easy to set it up with menus and toolbars close to gnome if that is what you prefer.

I always recomend that people try the apps from both and pick the de that their preferred apps come from rether than choosing on the basis of menus or icons or toolbars etc. These are easy to change and it is apps that you actually use not just sitting there looking at the desktop.
