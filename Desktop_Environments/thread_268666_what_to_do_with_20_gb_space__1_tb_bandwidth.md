### Post by djRobbieF on 2006-09-30

### Post by ropers on 2006-09-30
Host "forbidden" literature/publications, ie. stuff that's banned in many jurisdictions. 

Don't break your own local laws (unless you really wish to take a stand and possibly do time for it or worse), but host stuff that's controversial to the maximum degree your local laws will allow. Free speech is under severe threat right now, especially in the US, UK and some EU countries. Sure, Ben Franklin might have said:[quote=Benjamin Franklin]They that can give up liberty to obtain a little temporary safety deserve neither liberty nor safey.[/quote]...but occidental countries are currently surrendering their liberties like crazy. 

Some content that I could think of: 

"Der Totale Widerstand" by [Major Hans von Dach]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_von_Dach"). (engl. tranlation: [Total Resistance]("http://www.amazon.com/Total-Resistance-H-Von-Dach/dp/0873640217")). Manual re. how to run a resistance war against unwanted occupiers -- written by the said Swiss major for the Swiss army and also expressly for the Swiss public after WWII. The book discusses a guerilla warfare response to a hypothetical invasion and occupation of Switzerland by a foreign power, but its content is not just applicable to Switzerland only. At least the German version (which is outlawed in Germany) has been seen floating around on file sharing networks as a PDF, but I do believe the books is still under copyright, so you may want to try and contact the publisher. Don't ask me though. While I have lived outside of Germany in the past and seen the book, I am currently back in Germany and I need to comply with local legislation.

Record [Al-Manar TV]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Manar") video streams via [mencoder]("http://www.mplayerhq.hu") and host these files. AFAIK Al Manar is the TV channel of Hezballah, and people in the US have been persecuted for spreading that information. So you may want to be extra careful if you're in the US. The US are rapidly hitting rock bottom when it comes to due process rights and their freedom of speech legislation is being eroded as we speak. Count yourself lucky if you even end up in front of a judge (of a real public court trial, not a secret kangaroo one).

Record and host the programme of the Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting (IRIB) organisation's English language [World Service]("http://english.irib.ir/"). The [description]("http://english.irib.ir/frequency%20and%20time%20table/englishradio.htm") of the US-targeted program "The Voice of Justice" bears repeating for its idiosyncracy and humour value alone:
> It is worth noting that every day a 60-minute package is aired, addressing all truth-seeking Americans. This edition is entitled:“The Voice of Justice”. The objective behind this edition is campaign against the US administration’s interventionist policies.

Of course these suggestions assume that you're based in an occidental country. If you're not, then other content --whatever your local regime hates most-- might be what would be most ethical to host. Eg. if you're in China you may consider hosting content about the Falun Gong or about the slaughter at Tiananmen Square. If you're in an Islamic country you might consider hosting Salman Rushdie's [Satanic Verses]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Satanic_Verses_(novel)") (assuming you get copyright clearance). 

**Again, whatever your local government most wants to see censored, that's what you might want to host.**

EDIT: If you're in the US, you might also consider hosting strong language, atheist essays, papers on evolution, papers discussing the medical indication of abortion, violent video games and/or pornography. The US Christian Taliban is preoccupied with, and hates all of these WAY more than they hate even the most blood-thirsty and anti-Israeli/anti-Jewish Islamist Jihadist propaganda.

Of course this is a dangerous business. People will wrongly presume you shared the views expressed in the controversial content you are hosting. They will disbelieve you and refuse to understand when you say that information wants to be free and that you are just spreading information without agreeing with it. They will contest your assertion that spreading information doesn't imply endorsement. People might very conceivably label you a terrorist. Nothing is more dangerous these days than exercising exactly those civil liberties that are currently being taken away, and they will be successfully taken away if no one exercises them. Which is why those who exercise them find themselves to be so hated. Even to the point where they might end up dead.

Of course I'm not telling you to do this and take that kind of a risk. I'm just giving you some ideas that you might or might not choose to pick up on. It's your own choice and at your own peril, but it's the darnest most ethical use of that amount of bandwidth and disk space that I can think of right now.
