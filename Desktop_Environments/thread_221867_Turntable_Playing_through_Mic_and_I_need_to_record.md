### Post by mcduck on 2006-07-24
By the way, you aren't going to get too good sound quality that way.. Turntables require using preamplifier as the output is different from line level/microphone signals and also the sound needs some corrections. (when pressed to vinyl, low frequencies are reduced to avoid stylus skipping and high frequencies are boosted to make the vinyl last longer in use without loosing sound quality. Amplifiers with Phono connectors then correct the signal to sound like it sounded before recording).

Recorded through line or mic connectors the sound quality is far worse than old C-casettes.. Audacity isn't enough to fix that..

I'd suggest connecting the turntable to amplifier and the routing sound from amp to your computer, or is you don't have a suitable amplifier you could buy a vinyl preamp, they aren't expensive and you'll get good enough sound quality to make some CD's from your old vinyls..

(also don't forget to ground your turntable somewhere, othervice you'll record more static than sound..)
