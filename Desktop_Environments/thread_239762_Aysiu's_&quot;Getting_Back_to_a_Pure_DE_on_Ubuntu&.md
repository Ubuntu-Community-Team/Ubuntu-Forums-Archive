### Post by harisund on 2006-08-19
Somewhere on the forums I saw Aysiu's pages on 

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome.php)
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexfce.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexfce.php)
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purekde.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purekde.php)

I think the title is bit of a misnomer. What you get is not 'pure gnome', 'pure xfce' or 'pure kde'. What you get is actually 'pure ubuntu-desktop', 'pure xubuntu-desktop' or 'pure kubuntu-desktop'.

The reason is, I was looking to install just Gnome, as you would when you compile from sources, and not all the enormous junk and customizations that come from ubuntu-desktop. For example, on installing ubuntu-desktop, the update-manager gets installed on startup. After days of searching I finally figured out I had to delete /etc/xdg/autostart/update-manager.desktop. Similary, I wanted just xfce, and xubuntu-desktop in reality gave me a version of XFCE that the Ubuntu devs thought everybody would like.

So, those pages are in a way incorrect. It should be getting pure  ubuntu-desktop (and not gnome), pure kubuntu-desktop (and not KDE), pure xubuntu-desktop (and not XFCE). Pure gnome,kde,xfce are what you get when you download them from the web directly.
