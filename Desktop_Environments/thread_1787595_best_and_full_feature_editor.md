### Post by FormatSeize on 2011-06-21
> **rastar101 said:**
> 
1-open all files in a folder when drag and drop the folder on it
2-search and replace a statement in all open files
3-have an extended mode which include special characters like \n,\r and so on..
 

For most intents and purposes, Gedit is what you are looking for. It is highly customizable, which is why it is my choice for editors with a GUI (if Kate isn't around). As for the drag and drop and automatically open all files, I don't know. I didn't even know that Notepad++ could do that either though. 

Searching and replacing in multiple files... I don't know, but if I had to guess I would say that it could do that. But a quick Google search shows that you have to get [this plugin](http://www.webupd8.org/2010/10/gedit-plugin-to-search-and-replace-in.html) for that. Easy to get and easy to use. 
 
As for your special characters, gedit comes with a Python plugin preloaded, but not in effect by default. You have to go in the menu and mark the checkbox, but it's there, and it should let you take advantage of the special characters since Python uses them.
