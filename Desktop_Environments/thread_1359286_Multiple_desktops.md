### Post by 3Miro on 2009-12-19
1. Under KDE it is better to use Kwin. It is the default manager. It has most of the effects of Compiz (the useful ones anyway) and KDE apps interact better with it. It also has additional functionality that Compiz cannot get (like preview a minimized window).

2. Once you enable compiz, it takes charge and most of the regular video settings of KDE are no longer valid (compiz overrides them). You need to install compiz-config-manager, then you can change number of desktops and such from those menus.

In kde, right clicking on the desktop switcher applet gives you a menu with two options [I don't remember it exactly]. One to change the number of desktop and the other asks for the number of rows. Change the first to 4 and the second to 1. That will give you the cube.
