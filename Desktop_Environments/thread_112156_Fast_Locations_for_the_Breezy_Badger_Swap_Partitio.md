### Post by xenoxaos on 2006-01-03
My opinion.....it doesnt matter where you put it.  I would find a spot on your hard drive and stick it there.  I doubt that your computer will actually need it in any case.  I have the same setup and rarely have i seen my computer use my swap file. 98% of the time it uses ram.
-Mike

Edit: Don't put a swap on an external drive. It will most likely be slower, and I have better uses for external drives. Taking shit from one computer to another, and if you take out your swap space....well there it goes with it, then your computer freaks out.
