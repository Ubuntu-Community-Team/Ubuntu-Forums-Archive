### Post by nrundy on 2011-05-19
I recommend using 10.04 LTS for the time being. 

in 5 months 11.10 will be out and natty will be refined. but there is a reason for LTS releases. you should be aware that the non-LTS releases (e.g., 11.04) are not intended to be a long term stable release. The non-LTS releases mostly work but they basically are testing ground and refinement ground for the LTSs. from the sounds of your post, you should use the 10.04 LTS release. play with 11.04 on your own as a dual-boot or on another computer. if you experience bugs help the community and report them on Launchpad ([https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ReportingBugs](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ReportingBugs)). if you don't have time to submit bugs and just need a working puter, wait for 12.04 to enjoy unity (or try 11.10 and see if it is problem free for you). Again, the non-LTS releases mostly work but there are those for whom it don't work perfectly.
