You could try swapping your video card with something else to see if it's the problem. ATI drivers generally at least are considered a nightmare (in both Windows and Linux) so instability through the driver module could be the root of your problem (although I don't see how it causes the whole system to freeze on Linux anyway). ### Post by blueturtl on 2005-08-24
A search in Google for a socket 478 Foxconn motherboard returns plenty of pages but no reviews. Also the prices of Foxconn hardware seem to be rather on the low side ($30-60). You wouldn't happen to know the actual model?

Anyway with the information you give it's kind of hard to determine what might be wrong. I've done my fair share of tech support and sometimes common sence is the best way to go if there's not much to go on. For example, if you suspect overheating, how are your system fans on the inside? Are they making an unstable noise? Is there a lot of dust? If you can take a peak inside your case while running you should soon have an answer. If the fan on the video card is not spinning it or is spinning at an uneven rate it might be overheating.

If nothing is wrong with your hardware on the inside it could be a software issue (in which case we need the complete hardware spesifications to see which of the components is causing a conflict).

Conserning the motherboard, I once had an Abit motherboard that started freezing on me after added memory. Turned out it was a very poor quality mobo and it died soon after. When looking at the capacitors on the motherboard their tops were brown and bulged. This could be a bad sign to look for.
