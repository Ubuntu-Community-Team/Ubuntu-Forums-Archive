### Post by mannheim on 2006-06-10
nami's original post points out something that is often overlooked by people who compare vista and compiz/xgl. The translucency effects in vista are quite different from anything implemented in compiz, as far as I can see. In vista, some of the "glass" has a "ground glass" finish, so that it blurs what it is behind it (quite different from 50% transpacency) This is particularly visible in the Start menu. The titlebar buttons in vista give a convincing imitation of opaque paint on top of shiny glass. These effects go well beyond what compiz can do: compiz can apply a uniform translucency to the whole title bar.

Probably we will see these things built on top of XGL/AIGLX or whatever their successors are; but we don't have these things for linux now.
