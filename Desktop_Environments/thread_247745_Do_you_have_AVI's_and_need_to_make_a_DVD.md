### Post by danielph on 2006-08-31
I have just revisited the world of making DVD's and I thought I would post my findings. It may help someone choose what to use. There are so many options available and the GUI DVD applications are growing everyday. I think we are getting close to having some really good ones on Linux now.

Here is a list of what I find to be the best AVI to DVD processes in Linux. I have started with the easiest to use, working through to the most powerful.

[DeVeDe]("http://www.rastersoft.com/programas/devede.html")
This must be the easiest to use, but it is not lacking in power. It will take AVI's and convert them to DVD formatted file structure, mpeg files or make an ISO file. You can select video and audio rates, PAL or NTSC, audio delay, scaling modes, chapter splits, final size and custom parameters. It seems to be reliable and simple to use. You can put together a number of avi under different titles, but it won't do menus.

[Tovid]("http://tovid.berlios.de//en/index.html")
Has been popular for a while now and just does the job.  Just as simple and does a simple menu creation and is a complete. It seems to be reliable and is certainly simple to use. I have heard reports of burn not working, but this is not a major problem.

[ManDVD]("http://www.kde-apps.org/content/show.php?content=38347")
Under development and changing nearly on a daily basis at the moment. It is one of best complete GUI Linux DVD creators I have seen.  An English version is available.  I haven't gone through the whole process, but the GUI worked well and it was powerful in terms of features -  with menus, subtitles, slide shows etc. and an easy to use, polished interface. If it works as good as it looks then there is a great app here. I found the menu creation a little difficult here, not as intuitive as say DVDstyler. 

[Avidemux]("http://avidemux.sourceforge.net/") and [DVDstyler]("http://dvdstyler.sourceforge.net/")
Note: avidemux is available in the repos
Next and more powerful are two applications. For a very powerful video editing and coding from avi to mpg is avidemux. It also has good codec support. It is also very easy to use with lots of filters for effects and subtitles etc. (Kino can also do this but I think avidemux is better). This is also available for Windows and Mac. The second application is the excellent DVDstyler for taking it from the mpg format through to DVD and burning it. It has a really powerful menu creation interface, is easy to use and will burn the DVD for you at the end.

Do it [manually]("http://www.transcoding.org/cgi-bin/transcode?Tutorials/Authoring_PC_Media_To_DVD")
The tools are all there to use from the command line and you can do as much or as little as you want. This is the most powerful way and if you are regularly working with DVD's this is probably the best option.

[A breakdown]("http://mightylegends.zapto.org/dvd_images/dvdauthor_howto.png") of the whole process of DVD Creation

I hope this is of some help to someone.

### Post by danielph on 2006-08-31
DVD creation has really been something that has been left to the command line and this works very well. Now there are many developers that have looked at what they want and done something to try and get a good GUI interface. There are some great examples of that kind of work here and all the people working on the apps should be proud. I think most people expect to be able open up an applcation load up there AVI files, drag and drop, point and click and out pops a DVD in the right format with the right subtitles and a polished menu. This of course is possible, but video coding is complex and bulky - and with all the choices of codecs and formats changing as technology improves - a difficult thing to keep up with. People from a windows background had things like Nero and this does a good job, but there still isnt really that kind of user interface in Linux, although it looks like manDVD may give it a run for the money (or not as it is free!). What you tend to get in Linux is more power, but of course a little more knowledge is needed. 


I have only been using Linux for 2 years and I am seeing some great improvements in the applications in general and certainly some improvements in this area of DVD creation.

Dan
