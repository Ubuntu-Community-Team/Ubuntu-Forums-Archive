### Post by qpieus on 2006-09-16
I don't have any of the boards you listed, but I am running dapper on an older ECS socket A board. It runs just fine.
As far as the specific boards you listed, the ECS nForce3-A would be my choice due to its nvidia chipset. It has no onboard video though, if that is of interest to you.
The other boards you list are Via or sis chipsets. I have no experience with sis. The socketA board I have is a via chipset, and like I said, dapper runs fine on it.
If I can make a suggestion, take a look at this Foxconn board:[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16813186085]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16813186085")
The price of this guy is similar to the boards you listed.
I have a similar board and dapper runs great on it. It has onboard nvidia video that works well. Nvidia stuff seems to play well with linux, for me at least. Newegg has several other variations on this board as well. (The link I gave is for the cheapest one). The one I have is [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16813186086]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16813186086") 
It's about $67 at newegg. Browse around newegg a little, they have a giant selection and lots of reviews.
