### Post by Herman on 2006-03-10
Are you using Gnome or [COLOR=Black]kubuntu[/COLOR] desktop?

I use the PrintScreen key, (or usually Alt+PrintScreen actually), quite often in 'Breezy' (Gnome) and for me it seems easier than the equivalent operation in Windows. I don't have to paste it anywhere, I get a small 'save screenshot' window that comes up automatically, with two dialog boxes in it. One dialog box for 'Name:' (for the new file), and another for 'Save in folder:' with a spinbox showing me some choices for where to save it to. 
Do you not get the same?

The Ubuntu screenshots are saved in [.png image]("http://www.libpng.org/pub/png/") format too, which is a really great image format. You can open it with G.I.M.P. and save it as some other format if you need to.

If you are using a kubuntu desktop, after using the printscreen key, right-click on your desktop and select 'paste clipboard contents', or just use your Ctrl+V keys. You'll get a 'Kdesktop' dialog box where you can name your file and select an image format (there are eight to choose from).
Also, you may notice a small clipboard icon down by your clock. You can click on that to see the images you have stored on your clipboard, and clear the history or configure klipper if you want. The kubuntu klipper idea is less obvious to the first-time user, but after you've done it once it's easy and seems to be a little more advanced then Gnome's printscreen function.
I hope something here helps someone. (Herman) :D
