### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-03-15
There is a controller from Nvidia in the repos that handles resolution better than the Ubuntu screen resolution device.That's only helpful though if you're running an Nvidia GPU. (Use Synaptic to find it, just search for Nvidia and it should come up) The Nvidia controller is accessable through terminal. It does not automaticly put iteslf in the menu. (you can make a launcher to do that though)

You may want to try fiddling with the Ubuntu Resolution control by changing what type of screen you have. Some of them will prevent your screen from passing a certain resolution, just be careful of the refresh rate. You do not want it geting too high or too low.

My philosophy is fiddle with it till it breaks, then ask for help. But then again, I do not have a lot of irreplaceable data either. Everything I have can be picked back up relitively easily. It would just take time. Altering your resolution may turn your monitor off, but it will reset in 15 seconds anyways.
