### Post by howefield on 2017-04-09
What do you have at the moment ?

Ubuntu 16.04 will be supported till 2021 so a fair bit of water under the bridge before then.

In my opinion the two environments are not massively different so it isn't a huge shock going from one to the other. You could have both environments installed and choose between them at the login screen. My preference fwiw, will be to run Unity till at least 18.04 but have one machine on Gnome just to pick through the tips and hints for using it., eg which are the best extensions to use, if any.

### Post by howefield on 2017-04-09
16.10 is supported till July this year so you will have to upgrade at some stage to 17.04 and then on to 17.10 and 18.04 in turn, I'd suggest staying on Ubuntu Gnome until you get to 18.04 when "vanilla" Ubuntu will come with the Gnome desktop environment by default. 

There doesn't seem to be a legitimate reason for you to switch in the meantime to Ubuntu (Unity) for now.
