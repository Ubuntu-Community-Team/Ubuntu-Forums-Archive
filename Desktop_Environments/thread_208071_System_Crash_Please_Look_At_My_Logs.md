### Post by vigleik on 2006-07-03
Well, if Memtest reports errors with your RAM, then that's almost certainly your problem. My guess is you're not experiencing any problems right away because you're not using the "bad part" of your memory yet.

Try with just one stick, then the other. Also try moving your sticks around in the different slots on your motherboard, since it's also possible that there's something wrong with one of the slots. My guess is you'll have to send your RAM back for a replacement.

### Post by darenw on 2006-07-03
if it's crashing at random after some time, maybe it's like a problem i once had - my machine froze up solid at random times, several minutes after booting up and acting useful.  Turned out to be a loose IDE cable - making just good enough contact to get by, but dropping a bit on rare occasions, leading to sudden death.  Pushing the connector onto the disk drive solved the problem permanently.    So, check  all your internal cables.  (For the *computer*, i mean)
