### Post by mynimal on 2006-06-18
Back when I was a Windows (XP SP2) user, I used to mod the great googamooga out of my computer. I got really used to making skins and submitting screenshots to websites like Customize.org and deviantART, where I usually got PMs asking me how I did something or other, favorites on my screenshot submissions, and tons of comments. I really don't mean for that to sound arrogant. I just miss doing all that and having a nice, clean, usable, and more fit-for-me computer setup.

But now that I'm on Ubuntu, there are a few things I noticed. By default, the OS is much more customizable. But in a sense, it's also less customizable than Windows. Sure, you can change the icons, window borders and controls, etc around and install new themes, but those are all sticking to one thing. On Windows, I was a very very very heavy LiteStep user and skinner. I consider it the holy grail of alternate shells. On Linux, Blackbox and it's various other branches (I use OpenBox) are as close as it gets to LiteStep, but even those are limited. In LiteStep, it didn't skin window borders, but everything else was practically unlimited. I used to make LiteStep skins daily, but here on OpenBox I can't use pixmaps or even remove menu titles without hacking the source.

Basically, I'm wondering if there's something I'm missing out on when it comes to my tastes. I need something with tons of room and capabilities when it comes to customization. Something that allows usage of pixmaps with alphatransparency, changing fonts, etc. I hear FluxBux allows usage of pixmaps, but I'm hesitant to try skinning it because I don't really have any idea how or what I can do. I'm used to editing skins with a text editor, so that's just fine with me.

Should I try FluxBox? I've noticed that it uses menu icons in its menus, that's a big turnoff to me. I know you can just not set them, but that leaves a big blank space in the menu.

Here's my Customize.org account so you can see the kind of stuff I did and still want to do on Ubuntu: [http://www.customize.org/list/user/mynimal/0/30/pub_rating-desc](http://www.customize.org/list/user/mynimal/0/30/pub_rating-desc)

Or am I better off just using Windows? Some applications I really wish I had on Linux are Miranda IM, foobar2000, and LiteStep (Obviously), but I haven't found anything that can match up to them.
