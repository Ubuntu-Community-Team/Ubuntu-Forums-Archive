### Post by slizovskiy on 2012-11-07
Dear Developers,
  I am always using a laptop (with Intel built-on graphics) and always plug an external monitor, which is located above the laptop. Believe me, it's really convenient.  It was always a hard problem for Linux to deal with such a configuration and that was the reason that I was not using Linux in the past. I believe, KDE and other desktops may still have problems with this.  
   Gnome on Ubuntu 11.10 works ok with two monitors, although I usually have to manually install the upstream kernel to avoid a bug when unplugging the external monitor, and I move the panel to the bottom. (I submitted this bug, it was found to be upstream-fixed, but still the correct upstream kernel is NOT included in recommended updates).
   Now, as concerns the Unity, I have nothing against it in general, but the upper panel hangs in between two monitors and covers the application windows, that's why I don't like it.

Conclusion:  Dear Developers, please, test all your innovations on dual monitor configuration!
 Best wishes,
Sergey
