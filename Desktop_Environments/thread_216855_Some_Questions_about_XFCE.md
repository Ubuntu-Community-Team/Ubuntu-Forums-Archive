### Post by bonzodog on 2006-07-16
Xubuntu Cd's are in the pipeline, and will hopefully be available for when edgy ships in October. 

I personally now use Zenwalk linux, as it is an xfce based system but runs on the slackware base and is much lighter than Xubuntu. 
You will inevitably get some bloat with having to load certain apps, but you need to look at the dependencies and consider what each app needs that you want. 

In some cases, that might mean considering something else if you want to keep the system light (for example, installing Amarok on to a xubuntu system would mean getting alot of the qt libs needed for the interface.)

My advice: Stick to gtk based apps, and make sure they are more gtk based than gnome integration apps. sole Gtk based apps will not need much much in the way of dependencies in the way of adding additional libraries for the graphical front end. Remember; Xfce is already using the gconf registry for it's apps, and it has a lot of the gtk base already included.
Stay away from KDE/qt apps, and that will avoid a lot of bloat.
