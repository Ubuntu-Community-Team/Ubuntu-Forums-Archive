### Post by Zotova on 2005-12-20
Try installing gtk2-engines-gtk-qt. From my understanding this basically makes gtk apps use qt to draw the actual windows - thus as a result they look more like KDE apps.

I use it personally, gnome apps don't look perfect but they look better than they do as standard.

### Post by f1dave on 2005-12-20
Agreed.

I also use this for my gtk apps (firefox, thunderbird among others) and whilst it makes for a nice uniformity you need to be prepared for small issues like status bars overshooting their edges and blue highlights over an entire area rather than round the edge of an icon...
