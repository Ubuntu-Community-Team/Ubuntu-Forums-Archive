### Post by Cynical on 2006-09-09
Go to Applications > Add/Remove Programs and search for gdesklets, make sure to click on 'show unsupported software'. After thats installed go to Applications > Accessories > gDesklets. Look in the left-side pane and choose  System/Disk and double click on SideCandy Mount Display. After you place it somewhere on your desktop, you can right click on it and configure its settings. If you want, you can click on the tux drive icon and have it minimize to show the percentage of the drive that is full. You can also do this for your network traffic, cpu usage, and ram usage.
