### Post by po0f on 2006-11-03
clifton,

I don't know about configuring Gnome's workspaces to do this, but [Beryl](http://www.beryl-project.org/) is probably what you want for a usability upgrade.  It's main focus is eyecandy, but from a usability stand-point it's great.  The task-switcher shows a thumbnail of open windows in the alt-tab task switcher, windows can span multiple workspaces, it has Mac OSX's Expose feature, move your cursor to the edge of the screen and the next workspace is only a mouse-wheel away...  I love it.
