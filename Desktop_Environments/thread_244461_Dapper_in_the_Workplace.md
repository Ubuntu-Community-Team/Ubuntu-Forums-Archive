I work as a senior manager of a mid-sized City government and supervise an I.T department (among others).  I am not an I.T. person by training, but consider it a strong interest and hobby.  I have a keen interest in Ubuntu and use it at home on both a desktop and laptop (I also have a second windows computer that I keep for certain things that linux does not do well).  I recently talked with my I.T. manager about trying dual boot windows - linux to test liux and open source software in a real world environment.  While having this conversation with the I.T. Manager and our consultant, he recommended SLED 10 as being the easiest to intergrate into our work environment.  I had wanted to try Ubuntu, but I figured I would hed his advice.  I installed SLED and the other applications needed to integrate into our office environment including:
Groupwise
Novell Client (to access to server directories)
iprint (for using our print server)
tn5250 (for access to our AS/400)

After working on it yesterday, I appeared to have everything working fine.  My main complaint is that SLED 10 can't hold a candle to Ubuntu in terms of stability.  For example, everytime I tried to change the screen resolution, the conmputer crashed (and I mean _every _time).  Our consultant said that he had the same problem with the crashes.  Not a great first impression (by the way I'm using a basic p4 Dell Opti-plex with an intel graphics card).  

My concern is that while I want to use Dapper in this instance, without a trouble-free Groupwise and Novell Client (is their another version that runs flawlessly in Ubutu that lets you log into the network?)  How can I expect to "give it go" in the workplace if I can't intergrate into our existing environment?  

Does anyone run Dapper in a large office environment (approximately 250 desktops)?  with Groupwise? etc.
