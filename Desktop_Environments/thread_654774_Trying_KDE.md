### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-12-31
I'll rattle on about it if you'd like. It's frustrating to many folks, I do understand, but as much frustration as it might be, it's a beautiful thing about Linux that it's all about choices. There's often more than one "best way" as the best way is often simply "your way," so to speak. In other words, give it a try.

I've never met SUSE, but I use Kubuntu as my primary setup. I love it, despite finding that ubuntu proper is more polished on some details (icon theme management for one). I do feel that ubuntu is a better choice for anyone starting out or just wanting a relatively "no-fuss" desktop. For instance, most applications in Kubuntu/KDE give you more detailed options for this and that, while ubuntu tends to keeping it "uncluttered." That aspect alone might clue you to which one you might focus on.

In terms of "feel" it's my impression - yours may of course be different - that KDE tries to be more progressive and performance oriented, while ubuntu/Gnome tends to feel a bit slower but possibly more reliable (not that I've had any serious crashes or anything with Kubuntu). KDE seems better looking, although I find ubuntu better looking than many folks seem to find it.

If you add Kubuntu to your ubuntu, the menus could easily get very messy and you'll have plenty of redundant apps. Consistant theming will be challenging or impossible if you're fussy about the looks. But I don't discourage it. Both have lots of wonderful features and apps to offer, and although I'm partial to Kubuntu, I think a person could be absolutely delighted with mixing & matching their own hybrid 'buntu.

Things Kubuntu/KDE to look for? The apps are the main reason I favor Kubuntu. Many are quite task-specific. Hm... k3b, that's a great burning app, sort of like "Nero done right" in my book - richly featured without all that bloat. Quanta Plus if you're into web design at all, and there are a few handy utilities to go with it like KImageMapEditor, KLinkStatus, KDiff3 etc. It has it's own optional Office suite that's better than it seems to be credited for, and looks set to be even better in the upcoming KDE4 versions. Krita is a comfortable, to me, graphics app I tend to use more than GIMP. I don't do much in torrents beyond linux stuff, but Ktorrent seems like a darn nice torrent app. KGet is a nicely featured download manager. Konqueror is a great file manager / web browser, though it's taken time for me to warm to it and appreciate its features. Krusader, a two-panel file manager, is similarly amazing. I don't think there's much you can't configure 'em to do. BasKet Note Pads is real nifty. Lots of people love AmaroK, a full-featured type music manager / player. Kopete's a really nice multi-client chat app.

It goes on, actually, so my setup is mostly Kubuntu. One thing I don't like is Adept, the default Kubuntu package manager; fortunately, there's a KDE version of Synaptic, so no problem there. Have to go do dinner now, probably ChicKen if not Kfc, but hopefully you and anyone else curious will be helped one way or another by all that chatter. No matter the choice, do enjoy yourself with all the wonderful things so many people kindly offer for our use and pleasure. :)
