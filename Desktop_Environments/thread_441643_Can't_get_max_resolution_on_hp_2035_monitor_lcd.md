### Post by sonic6567 on 2007-05-19
Arrrr... wipe out.  Failed to load X. crash.  recovery.

But guess what.  Now it is working.  I am typing this in glorious 1600x1200!! finally!!

Ok, here it goes, this is how it began working.  (and yes, everything I list here in this order was required. I tried just the final setting by themselves without going through this process and it did not work)

[LIST]
[*]First, I got everything up and running correctly on vga with a max resolution of 1400x1050.
[*]Then I connected a dvi cable and rebooted with vga still selected as my monitor input.
[*]Then I switched input to dvi. The screen was, well, freaking out.  Like a television with poor reception- noise/staic over the picture with an occasional horizontal line scrolling past.
[*]Switch input back to vga.
[*]run the xorg configure process and choose defaults except for refresh rate (modified to match monitor specs) and resolution choices- chose 1600x1200, 1400x1050, 1280x1024, and the common lower ones.
[*]reboot and crash the X server.
[*]reboot and recover the previous xorg file.
[*]configure the correct refresh rates.
[*]reboot in vga.
[*]modify the xorg modes to "1600x1200" "1400x1050"   (only)
[*]reboot in digital dvi
[*]the welcome screen is "wonky" (like the poor tv). Logged in and the desktop was "wonky" (but, at 1600x1200)
[*]at this point, switch the monitor input to vga
[*]now, reboot in vga mode.
[*]system comes up and operates as normal in vga mode at 1600x1200 !!  (dvi does not work)
[*]also, still with only those two choice listed in xorg, the resolution control panel now lists all possible choices.
[*]Bingo!!
[/LIST]

I list all of those steps because apparently they all had some bearing on the outcome (including the crash of x and the "wonky" tv look)

Now, I whole heartedly believe that there is a logic behind this process (after all, this is linux. and it does what it is told.  as opposed to windows, which, where if a problem like this arose, you would be out of luck because NO ONE knows why windows does what it does when it feels like it for no reason whatsoever inconsistently)  Perhaps someone with a deeper understanding of the X environment and its integration with Ubuntu could extrapolate from my aforementioned endevour exactly what occurred.

But none the less, I am now operating in full native 1600x1200 and I cannot tell you the positive difference that it makes.  No more image up scaling.  The quality is outstanding!
