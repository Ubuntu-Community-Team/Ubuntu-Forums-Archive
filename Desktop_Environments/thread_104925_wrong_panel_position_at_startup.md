### Post by bonzodog on 2005-12-17
I'm not actually sure what causes this,but it normally happens if you have upgraded your gnome environment or used older config files on the newer environment. The only way to stop this is to go into your home dir, and reset your gnome settings by erasing any .gnome2 dirs, anything that mentions gnome in it. That worked for me last time, but it did mean I had a vanilla gnome desktop, and had to start again on personalising it.
