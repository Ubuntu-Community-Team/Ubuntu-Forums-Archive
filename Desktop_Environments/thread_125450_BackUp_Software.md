### Post by Herman on 2006-02-04
> I want to back up my windows partition from Ubuntu, as I can't boot to Windows. Is there a software that will allow me to do this (preferably GUI)? And if so, will the backup disks be compatible with Windows We already have the best back-up software, it's called Ubuntu.
Just pop a CD-ROM into your drive and an icon will appear on your desktop. Right-click on it, to open the CD-ROM browser window.
Click 'Go' on the top panel for a drop-down menu, and then CD/DVD creator.
Leave that now, and click on you hda1 icon on your desktop, (if that is your Windows partition), and navigate to the files you want to save.
Copy what you want and paste it in your CD/DVD creator.
When you have enough to fill a CD, click 'write to disk'.
Then click 'write to disk once again' when it asks the second time.
Mine said some files might not be Windows compatable, this is nonsense because that's where I just got them from, so I recorded the disk anyway. I think it only means the file-name is too long, I will re-name them if I need those file when I restore them. (Probabaly they are just trash anyway).
Does this help?
You can also compress your files if you want, in Ubuntu, and/or use networking to move them to another computer or disk on your LAN or the internet somewhere.
Why won't Windows boot? Will GAG on System Rescue CD help? Or you can [make you own GAG]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p12.htm") CD or floppy.  Do you need some help with Grub?
If you need more help, or that wasn't what you wanted, just ask again. I'll try if it's something I know about. :-D (Herman)
