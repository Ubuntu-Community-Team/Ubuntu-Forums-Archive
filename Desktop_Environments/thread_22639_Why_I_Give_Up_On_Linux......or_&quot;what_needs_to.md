### Post by Leif on 2005-03-29
I hear you, **Paperweight**. I took the plunge into using linux only a couple of months ago. I've been using computers heavily for about 20 years now, but I'd never used only linux. And it has been hell. Display configuration sucks - xorg doesn't work for me the way xfree did. DMA isn't turned on for my drives by default (wtf?). Nvidia drivers result in segfaults for a dozen apps. Skype is a no-no. And many more such issues. I agree wholeheartedly that linux is not ready for a lot of things on the desktop. I'm sorry, but it just isn't.

On the other hand, if you have some patience, it is a very rewarding experience. Once you do learn your way around a bit, linux is stable, powerful, and a vastly better choice for most programming and development, although I don't know if this last one matters to you. Linux is also on the rise, so if you're in any sort of technical work environment, it's quite a plus to have on your resume. 

You need to factor in the time you spent learning whichever OS you're used to. Everything takes a while to get working. And I get the feeling you haven't really given the forums enough of a  chance, because at least the CD burning, networking, and ftping seem like problems that would get sorted. Maybe I'm wrong, and I'm definitely not saying it's your fault things don't work. It isn't, a lot of things are just setup poorly. What I am saying is that if you give it a bit more time then most of your problems will probably get sorted, and at least the effort you've put in so far won't have been wasted  :)
