### Post by jbradley5000 on 2009-06-06
This happened to me when I didn't have a monitor plugged in to the machine when I first booted.  When I plugged it in, I only had the option of 800 x 600 or lower.  Rebooting (with a monitor plugged in) allowed ubuntu to detect the display properly and set the resolution to the proper level.  It's also possible that reloading the graphical side of Ubuntu (X window?) would redetect the display.  There's a keyboard shortcut to do that, but I don't recall it off the top of my head.
