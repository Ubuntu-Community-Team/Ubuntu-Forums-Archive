### Post by CatKiller on 2006-08-28
> **CatKiller said:**
> The problem as I see it is that for some reason either X or Gnome aren't picking up the modes subsection for "Screen1". And I'm stumped as to why.
After looking through the logs, I came to the conclusion that the higher resolutions were being discounted because the refresh rate was out of the default range. So I hunted for some specs for my very old monitor on the Wayback machine and added ```
	HorizSync	31.5-60
	VertRefresh	40-150
```to my xorg.conf. Restarted X, and it worked :) 
Now I have two pretty desktops. If only the nvidia and fglrx driver packages weren't mutually exlusive :sad:

As it turns out, the official Ati drivers don't support a card this old (the best PCI card I had kicking around). The ati X.Org driver works fine though.

All it means is that I don't get OpenGL screensavers displaying on one of the monitors. Anything that's particularly demanding for 3D gets opened on the monitor connected to the better NVidia card anyway.

I suppose that this type of configuration will become more common now that graphics card slots are becoming general purpose again. I guess we'll have to find an abstraction that makes configuring multiple monitors on arbitrary graphics cards easy, regardless of desktop environment. I don't know what it is, though.
