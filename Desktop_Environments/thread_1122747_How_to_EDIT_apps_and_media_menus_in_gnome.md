### Post by coffeecat on 2009-04-12
As far as I understand, the netbook remix interface was developed by Canonical (the company behind Ubuntu) in collaboration with Dell who wanted an interface suitable for netbooks for when they released their netbooks with Ubuntu. Of course, being open source it meant that any developer was permitted to pick up the code for netbook remix and adapt it for their own use. Hence all these eesy-peesies, eeeeeeeeebuntu, and the like. :wink: They're mostly custom respins developed by just a couple or handful of enthusiasts.

Ubuntu itself is working on a freely available netbook remix version of the upcoming Jaunty release. I've installed the Beta version on my EeePC and it runs very well. But I haven't run it enough to be really familiar with it, which is why I didn't realise what you meant by the media menus.

Best of luck with editing those menus. :)
