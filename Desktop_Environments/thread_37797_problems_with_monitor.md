### Post by royg1234 on 2005-05-28
I had this problem as well.  It happened with Suse 9.3 also, where my refresh was at like 80 Hz (and my monitor's manual maxes it at 79).

I think what causes it is your monitor specs are off, i.e. (or just e.g.???) your resolution or refresh is too high.  Just lower it <-- this is where the real challenge is, decreasing in sane increments instead of Ubuntu's default (on my comp at least) 15 Hz deltas.  855resolution will help you manage this.

This is a very annoying shortcoming of Linux IMHO.  In Windows, you don't have to do anything to get your monitor to work (and certainly no editing of cryptic text files).  My laptop especially took a lot of effort to get widescreen working, much more than the average computer user would put up with.

Previous post on this: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=31015](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=31015) <-- we couldn't figure it out exactly, but take a read anyway.  I put some screenshots in there, for you to compare.
