### Post by CeilingCrash on 2008-01-31
I had this problem too.   The hitch is - if you accidentally move the gnome-control-panel (top 'taskbar') to the left or right side, then it is much smaller.   The icons are packed together and there is no free space so you can grab the taskbar.

Easiest thing I found was to remove some icons, move the bar back to the top, and add icons back.   There is probably one or two you don't use anyway.

Go to an icon on the taskbar, RIght-Click, and select Remove From Panel.   Keep doing that until you get some blank space on the task bar.   Then you can click on the space, and drag the bar back into position.

Feature Request : It would be nice if the gnome-control-panel (taskbar) always kept a small area available from which to grab the taskbar.
