### Post by mcduck on 2011-09-09
+1 to above, Openbox is a window manager, not a desktop environment, so you aren't even supposed to get anything else than the ability to move & manage your windows plus the right-click menu.

If you want wallpaper, panels, configuration tools etc, it's up to you to choose the programs you like and install them.

Alternatively you could try LXDE, which is a lightweight desktop environment based on Openbox but including more of the commonly used stuff by default. Or do what I do and just pick the tools you want from LXDE and use them on your pure Openbox setup...
