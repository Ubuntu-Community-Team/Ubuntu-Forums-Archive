### Post by Glucklich on 2009-05-03
As for the headjack problem. Acer probably tried to do something funny, so the detection is not made the traditional way. I did not found a definitive solution for this problem but I found a workaround. Go to the sound options of your Operating System and if you are using Ubuntu or Xubuntu, add the tab that says "Surround", unmute and turn it up. So, the principle is to mute the "Front" option when you want to hear from the headphones and unmute the "Surround" one. Mute the "Surround" and unmute the "Front" when you want to head from the speakers. If you are using Kubuntu, it is labeled as one of the "3D"'s in the sound manager. Aply the same principle for the right options (and don't forget to turn up the right 3D option).
