### Post by dkbg on 2007-02-12
As someone said above, from the [Wikipedia article on GNOME]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME"):
> GNOME is part of the GNU operating system, and is its official desktop environment. The official pronunciation of the name is /g&#601;&#712;no&#650;m/ with a hard “G”, although /no&#650;m/ (as in the English word “gnome”) is also in common usage.

Also from the same article:
> The name “GNOME” was proposed as an acronym of **GNU Network Object Model Environment**...

The GNU project is officially pronounced "guh-new" (from the front page of [gnu.org]("http://gnu.org")):

> GNU is a recursive acronym for “GNU's Not Unix”; it is pronounced guh-noo, like canoe.

So, since GNOME is the official desktop environment of the GNU project, it follows that the official pronunciation is "guh-nome". Of course, you can pronounce anything however you wish, so it really doesn't matter, those are just the official versions.
