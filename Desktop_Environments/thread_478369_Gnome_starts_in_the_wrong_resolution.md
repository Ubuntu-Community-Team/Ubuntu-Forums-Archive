### Post by jongkind on 2007-06-19
What you can try to do is edit xorg.conf like by adding the refresh rate that your screen requires (in my case this is 60 Hz, see below the red-colored text):

[I]Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Default Screen"
    Device         "Generieke videokaart"
    Monitor        "Generieke beeldscherm"
    DefaultDepth    24
    Option         "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"
    SubSection     "Display"
        Depth       24
        Modes     "1024x768[COLOR="Red"]_60[/COLOR]"   
    EndSubSection
EndSection[/I]



Another thing that you can try is to verify whether the HorizonSync and VertRefr in xorg.conf are ok. These numbers are monitor depentent (you can likely find the right numbers in the manual of your monitor, e.g.)
[I]Section "Monitor"
    Identifier     "Generieke beeldscherm"
[COLOR="Red"]    HorizSync       30.0 - 81.0
    VertRefresh     56.0 - 76.0[/COLOR]
    Option         "DPMS"
EndSection[/I]

### Post by jongkind on 2007-06-21
Here are the specs of your monitor (assuming that you have a Optiquest V95)
[http://www.cybergeo.com/newsystem/v95specs.html]("http://www.cybergeo.com/newsystem/v95specs.html")

According to this you can modify the section monitor to:
[I]Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"Optiquest V95"
	Option		"DPMS"
	HorizSync	30-95
	VertRefresh	50-160
EndSection[/I]

And change the relevant lines in section screen to: 

*Modes		"1600x1280" "1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600"*

If this this does not work you can try the following:

*Modes		"1600x1280_76" "1280x1024_88" "1024x768_116" "800x600_146"*

Don't forget to make a backup of your xorg.conf file each time that you change something.
