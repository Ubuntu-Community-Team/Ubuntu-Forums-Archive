### Post by paulisdead on 2011-10-16
I have to admit, Gnome3 has actually grown on me over the last couple days.  I spent the first day in gnome fallback.  Then forced myself to use gnome3 for a day in my IT job, and actually found it to be really efficient.  Ubuntu 11.10 does seem to really love the sandy bridge GPU in my work laptop.  It is a very different mentality, but after spending a bit of time away from it, I realized I was missing gnome3.  It did take a little tweaking to get it a few more things right, but overall I'm surprised at just how fast I am with it already.

At first I bitched and complained, and gnashed my teeth, but am really starting to like it.  3 days ago, I was a huge Gnome3 hater.  I do use Unity on my netbook, which it is fantastic on, but find it completely unusable on a large dual monitor setup.  Dash is just terrible, and even after using it for a bit, I still find it a pain to navigate.  At least in gnome3, when you go to applications, you see your applications.  It always annoyed me that dash only showed the most opened apps.  Since if I'm in the menu, I'm obviously looking for an app I don't use that often, since I didn't put it on the dock.

I had some issues with gnome3 on my desktop at home when I installed 11.10 on it today.  I was getting segfaults from the nvidia drivers left and right, so gnome would only run in failsafe.  So, I spent some time with kubuntu for a chunk of the day.  It wasn't terrible, but there just some nagging issues, like the fonts just never quite looked as good, no matter how much I tweaked them.  KDE has always seemed to have longer startup times, and apps seem to launch slower in it, even though I'm using an SSD now.  It's been like 10 years since I tried KDE, and that still seems to be true.

I definitely have found myself really hooked on nautilus as my file manager over the years, so that's another reason I like to stick with gnome.  I've just always loved that it's not overly clunky, you can search by just typing, something windows explorer only got fairly recently.  Plus it's thumbnailing has always been superior.  Even back in the day when I ran a heavily customized fvwm setup on gentoo, I still used nautilus as my file manager.

I eventually wiped kubuntu and did ubuntu with gnome-shell again, and I'm not getting the segfaults from the nvidia drivers, this time.  All seems to be working well.  I do find it really efficient to just flip the mouse to the upper left and be able to do everything.  Once you get used to the placement, it's ridiculous how much you can do with a quick flick of the mouse.  It's just been annoying that clicking on terminal just brings the same one to the forefront.  I just wound up mapping super+c to open the terminal, and have been using that.  I'll bet Linus wishes he'd thought of that ;)

It's still a nagging issue that a blank panel extends across my second monitor, but overall, I'm actually enjoying gnome3.  Plus, the shell extensions can give it a lot more customizability.  If you want an application menu, there's an extension for that.  If you want a dock, there's also an extension for that.  I've actually started to appreciate the cleanliness of not having those things showing all the time, and just flicking the mouse to access them.
