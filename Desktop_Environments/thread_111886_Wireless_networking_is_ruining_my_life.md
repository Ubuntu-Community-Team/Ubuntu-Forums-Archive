### Post by Night Owl on 2006-01-19
Hi,
getting your wireless to work with WPA can be a bit tricky. I had trouble getting my wireless working at first, but reading various posts in this forum really helped. I am using an ipw2100 built-in wireless card and a DLink DI-524 router. My connection used to be a little bit unstable, but that was due to the bad firmware of my router. I recently upgraded to the 1.21v firmware (I have an A1 hardware version) and now my connection is rock solid. It's amazing how much better my router is now! Very few packets are being dropped (only ~0.1%). In general, I would recommend getting the latest stable drivers, like the 0.47v of wpa_supplicant. If you read through the README file for wpa_supplicant, you would find a great deal of useful information. Unfortunately, since I don't have your wireless card, I cannot give you specific pointers.
