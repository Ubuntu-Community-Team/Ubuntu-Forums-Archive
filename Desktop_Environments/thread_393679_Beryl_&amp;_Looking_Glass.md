### Post by ZERO_SHIFT on 2007-03-26
I recently installed looking glass on my laptop and it was quite astonishing just looking at it. For those who dont know looking glass is SUN's view of the desktop future.

Looking glass is quite different from beryl/compiz.Project Looking Glass creates a 3D environment in which you can work in. You get a sense of of feeling that you are looking at a virtual reality.

Project looking glass is not fully functional yet, but it sure is an interesting environment to experience. Might I add that Looking Glass is available on multiple OS's.

To download Project Looking Glass search for (LG3D) in synaptic. Then I recommended using the following method of installation:

[IMG]http://www.zefhemel.com/upload/project-looking-glass.jpg[/IMG]


```
cd /usr/share/lg3d/bin
```

```
sudo ./postinstall
```

This will install the Looking Glass session. You can then choose the Looking Glass session from the gdm login screen.

I am interested in hearing peoples feedback about looking glass, and whether they prefer its concept on that of compiz/beryl.

### Post by endlessurf on 2007-03-27
Starting up glass was sort of hit and miss, it took a few times before it would start.  Once it started it was fine.  It was hard at times to see the active transparent window over an inactive transparent window.  I did like the idea of having the windows be scaled down to give depth and to have them look in a perspective view when they were on the sides of the screen.  I never really got the hang of manipulating the windows and would rather liked to click on the bar and snap my wrist to "throw" them into place (more work but just my preference).  The desktop picture selector and the cd selector were cool but i hate clicking the mouse to change each item.  I prefer sliding the mouse to once side or the other to rotate the items.  The part that i thought was the slickest and made glass look clean was the fact that the desktop was not visible.   My desktop is always a cluster  bleep and having a small icon that would bring the desktop up in a quick and cool way was something i liked the most.  The design of the task bar set up at the bottom of the page was decent.  Just my opinion and 2 cents
