### Post by Sxeptomaniac on 2009-07-16
It's not too bad when you get the hang of it.  If your normal panel is completely gone, then right-click on the screen and choose the "Add Panel" option.  It should put a blank bar on one edge of the screen.  If it's not where you want it, then click the little Plasma swirl symbol on the bar, and use the "Screen Edge" option to drag it where you want it.

Next, fill it up with what you want by clicking the Plasma symbol (again, on the panel) and choosing "Add Widgets".  Here are the ones you'll want:

Application Launcher or Lancelot Launcher (your preference)
Task Manager
Pager
Device Notifier
Battery Monitor (for Laptops)
System Tray
Digital Clock (if you want)

Clicking the Plasma symbol will allow you to drag the order around, too.  When you're done, right-click empty space on the desktop and choose "Lock Widgets" to avoid accidentally removing things in the future.

Hope that helps

Edit:  I've gotten to like how configurable things are, though.  I attached what I've done with my desktop as an example (shrunk a bit and reduced in resolution).  Since my screen is so wide, my main bar is to the left, while my Task bar is to the bottom set to auto-hide.  I then put one more bar for shortcuts on the right set so windows can cover it.
