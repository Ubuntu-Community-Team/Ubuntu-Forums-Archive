### Post by elvis on 2005-06-24
KDE and GNOME are made by different teams with different ideas and design philosophies.  At a basic level, they are both Windows Managers and Desktop Environments.  This means their design is all about making the life of the end user easier through integrated desktop design and metaphors.  

How they look and feel are totally different.  You can see their websites including screenshots here:

[http://www.kde.org/screenshots/](http://www.kde.org/screenshots/)

[http://www.gnome.org/start/2.10/notes/](http://www.gnome.org/start/2.10/notes/)

The link I gave you eariler details stp by step how to install KDE on Ubuntu.  There's no point in me re-writing it here, as it does a fantastic job.

And there is no "Enterprise Edition" of Ubuntu.  Read the "about" page on the Ubuntu website in detail rather than just skimming it.  Their philosophy is that their software is, and always will be free.  There is no "Enterprise Edition", or pay-for service that will give you more than the free alternative.  

That is real freedom.
