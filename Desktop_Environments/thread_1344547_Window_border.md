### Post by yossell on 2009-12-03
One common way in which this can happen (it happened to me!) is if you are running compiz (i.e. have visual effects enabled) and have somehow unselected 'windows decoration'.

Bring up the compizconfig settings manager (alt+f2 and type 'ccsm' (without quote marks), scroll down to the effects section and make sure the 'windows decoration' box is ticked. 

It may not be this so - just to help people narrow down the possible source of the problem - does the problem exist  when you go to system->preferences->appearance, choose visual effects and set it to none? Or do the borders reappear?

### Post by shrimpboi13 on 2009-12-03
Thank you for the help i am running compiz but it turned out to be caused by the "extra visual effects" setting at system>preferences>appearance>visual effects.
