### Post by Ramaddan on 2007-04-30
As long as I can remember, from the first days of using Ubuntu,
there was this dictionary applet that I discovered on the Gnome panel.

Me and my dictionary went through beautiful and rough times together,
but then my applet started to fade away, option after option disappearing.

As I look upon its weak structure today, I realize that it's days of youth
and usefulness is approaching a violent and abrupt death.

Edgy hacked at some of its limbs, but left it still able to fend for itself.
However Feisty was not as kind, as it gleefully grinned at the horrified applet,
and sawed away its final dim of light.

If my dictionary could think, and if it could talk,
it might ponder: "Where did Edgy bury my extra features?
And where did Feisty stack away my advanced options?"

But alas, it is too late for this brave little applet,
who has survived many times the vicious attacks of its foes,
and continued living, albeit crippled.

As its friend and companion, I ask thee:

"Why? My dictionary never did anything bad to you or anyone else?
What happened to my dictionary's remains?
Where have you hidden it's options?"

Maybe hope still remains, and it did not yet breathe its last breath.

### Post by Ramaddan on 2007-04-30
Hi,

This is me answering myself.
I finally figured how to use the Gnome-Dictionary in its present form.

Just so as for others not to go through the pain I went through, here is what I did:

To add a dictionary to either Gnome-dictionary, or its applet ->

1) Choose Preferences
              --> For the dictionary-applet, just right click and choose it
              --> For the Gnome-dictionary application, click on the **Edit** menu, and then choose it

2) Now click on **ADD**, and then type in the description, and then hostname.

3) Don't do anything else except click on **ADD** after you've entered the information in step (2).

4) After the dictionary is added, find it in the list, and double click on it.

5) The properties of that dictionary will come up again, then click on the **Dictionaries** tab,
     and a list of dictionaries available on the server you added should show up.

6) Choose the dictionary you want to use and then click on **CLOSE**

6a) **_This step is optional._** While you were in step (6), before clicking on close,
      you can click on the **Strategies** Tab and choose the method you want
      the dictionary to search and give you back results, such as exact match or
      a different kind of search.

Hope this helps someone out there.
