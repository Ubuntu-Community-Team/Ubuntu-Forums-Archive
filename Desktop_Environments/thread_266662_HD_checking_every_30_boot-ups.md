### Post by alistair77 on 2006-09-27
Why is a HD check forced after every 30 times the PC's been rebooted?  I've read in quite a few posts in these forums that one of the mistakes ex Win users make is thinking that there must be a defrag utility in Linux, when actually there's no need.

It seems to me that this boot-up disk checking is very, very similar to Win's defrag - except that it's more inconveniant as you have to wait for it to finish.

Am I missing some important difference between the two? 

cheers

---

### Post by NetworkGuy on 2006-09-27
This disk check is more similar to a Windows chkdsk than a defrag.  It's looks for errors on the hard drive partitions and does nothing to defrag the hard drive.

### Post by cmost on 2006-09-27
Don't be tempted to turn this off or increase the frequency!  This feature actually alerted me to a hard disk problem, before I lost all my data!  When this pops up every 30 boots, I simply go fetch a cup of joe or do something else for the few minutes it takes to complete the check.
