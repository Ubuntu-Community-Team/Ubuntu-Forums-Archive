### Post by Shay Stephens on 2007-01-15
Easy answer, buy a new monitor.  Just make sure it has more horizontal resolution than you currently are using.

A second thought.  Verify you have the right resolution being used for the monitor.  If your monitor can display 1280 pixels horizontally and you are only using 800 pixels (for example) then changing the resolution to use 1280 would be a big boost, like getting a new monitor.

If the websites you are going to are displaying wider than 1280, then you will likely have problems with many of the monitors currently out.  So get used to scrolling.

I am curious now to see what kind of website would display that wide anyway.

### Post by Hubris2 on 2007-01-15
I think this would pop up most often on forums when people insert an inline image that is enormous...it will cause the remainder of that page (all threads below it) to be formatted such that they too extend past the length of the normal parameters and thus extend off the screen.

As Shay said...there really isn't any way to make more real estate on your screen than your screen has the ability to display.  Even if you buy a widescreen monitor, you'll likely find that the extra space is better-used to show additional windows rather than expanding the width of a browser.  It should be an exception rather than the rule that windows extend past your normal screen.
