### Post by jviscosi on 2007-11-19
I've never had any luck at all running Superkaramba in Fluxbox.  Either it doesn't refresh as you mention, or it displays as a black box, or it won't load its client apps when it starts, or the apps don't remember where they're supposed to be, or the apps won't go "sticky" ... nothing good ever came of Superkaramba and Fluxbox for me.  (In KDE however it's fabulous.)

You might want to look at gdesklets instead.  It works a lot better with Fluxbox and there used to be lots of interesting desklets for showing system information.  I've also heard of people using something called "torsmo" and something else called "conky" to make sysinfo sit on the desktop, but I am not familiar with them.
