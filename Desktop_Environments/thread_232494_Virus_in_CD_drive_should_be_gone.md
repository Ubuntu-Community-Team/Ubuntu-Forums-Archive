### Post by computergroove on 2006-08-09
It is - by definition - possible for a virus to be written for a cdrom drive but the variables required for it to actually happen are next to impossible. You can put your mind at ease regarding wether the virus was wiped out or not - it was. Viruses are operating system specific and there is no virus that has ever been written that spread successfully to linux systems. If you had xp and you got a virus then installing ubuntu would have eliminated it. There are firmware updates for optical drives (usually only for DVD Drives) but if you find your cdrw drive model (you can find the exact model on the top of the drive itself) you might be able to find the original or an updated firmware that will make the internal software like new. You have nothing to lose. I am certain that the drive itself has gone bad (it doesnt have to be from misuse - it just happens). You can get a new one for $20 ish shipped from newegg.com or better yet get a dvd-rw for $45 shipped.
