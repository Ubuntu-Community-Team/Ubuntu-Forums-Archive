### Post by claydoh on 2005-10-27
LiVES is good as it has the ability to grab video from DV cancorders as well as open/export many other file types. But Kino has a better interface. I recently used Kino to grab some video shots from my Sony Handycam, and exported it to DVD file stucture using the DV pipe/ffmpeg options (no menus or anything like that) and burned the resulting files using k3b. QDVDauthor looks good for assembling DVDs with menus etc. I have not yet tried out any of the KDE-specific apps, but they do look promising.
