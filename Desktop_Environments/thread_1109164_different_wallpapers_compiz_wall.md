### Post by malfindin on 2009-03-28
Hi all. Here's a screen shots of my config:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/album.php?albumid=997](http://ubuntuforums.org/album.php?albumid=997)

To do this requires: 

1. Compiz Fusion Settings manager, which can be installed through Synaptic, The Compiz Icon is nice to get as well. Just put in compiz and search.

2. No virtual machines to my knowledge work with desktops effects, accept for the ones in Kubuntu 8.04 KDE 3.5. In fact I have a special installation of Kubuntu on USB thumb-drive that I carry on my key chain. Boots in 1 min on nearly anybody's sys with desktop effects and 8 different wallpapers on 8 different desktops. If anyone knows different I'd love to see your posts.

3. Upload the desktops in the order you want into Compiz under the Wallpaper tab.

4. MOve everythig off your desktop it will no loger be displayed by nautilus.

5. Right click on your appicaltion menu and click on edit menu, enable "Configuration Editor" Download this through synaptic if you don't see it.

6. OK here we go---browse to /apps/nautilus/preferances and uncheck show_desktop. I advise bookmarking it before you do this. It's hard to find.

Optionally you can download and install a program called Wallpoz if you just want your desktops to switch when you go to a new one. This also allows you to use directories as slide shows, but it's not easy to install. Don't attempt any of this if you are a newbie.

Is it worth it???--why yes. You'll be the envy of any silly windows user.

Oh and try running Windows in Linux VirtualBox instead of Linux in Windows VMware. It actually runs faster than it would installed...go figure. Or better yet, just stop using Windows for anything. I have found that with a little effort, there is nothing windows can do that Ubuntu can't do better, faster and cheaper. I started out with Ubuntu in Vbox too, so don't think I'm harping. We all started somewhere.
