Okay, I think I know what you're talking about because I found your quistion while I was having the same (or similar) trouble while trying to install 9.10 remix on the netbook I gave my wife for Christmas.  I right-clicked on the top panel and instead of getting "properties" in the dialog box, I just got "preferences."  I found this is because where i right-clicked I was in a space Window Picker was using.

There is an Ubuntu logo at the left of the panel, then some space, and then two vertical dashed lines.  The dashed lines mark the beginning of the window picker applet.  Right-click in the space between the logo and the dashed lines.  There you will find a box for adding a new panel,  adding applets to the panel, etc.

I added a panel to the bottom of the screen and made both panels auto hide.  On the bottom panel, I added a show desktop applet, a separator, windows list, workspace switcher (adjusted for three workspaces,) and a trash can.  When I had them all placed where I wanted them, I locked them to the panel.  Now, I have a list at the bottom of the screen of all active apps in the workspace and can minimize them or switch between them just like in the deskop version of 9.10.

You can probably now be able to remove that window picker on the top panel.

I hope this helps.  If you need more clarification, please leave me some mail.

If this works for you, please mark it as solved.
