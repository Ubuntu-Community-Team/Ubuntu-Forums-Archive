### Post by LordHunter317 on 2005-06-30
It should be able to just fine, I can't see any reason why it won't.

Your bigger issue will be finding a board with support for dual dual-core processors.

You can't use Athlon64 X2's, those don't support dual physical packages.  They're missing the extra HT ports.

You'll have to purchase 2xx series Opterons.  This means among other things, registered RAM.

Prepare to spare a small fortune.

### Post by LordHunter317 on 2005-06-30
I'm sure it was a typo then.  Someone probably got confused, which is easy to do when the difference is 1 pin.

And while Opterons are cooler, they're also considerable more expensive, when you look at the whole package.  The processors may cost about the same, but the RAM is much more expensive, as is a motherboard with comparable featureset to a 939 board (usually 2x as much).

Also, on a high-end board, you have to buy 4 sticks of RAM to get dual-channel on both CPUs, instead of 2 sticks.  This depends on the board though and how the RAM sockets are connected to the CPUs.  It's certainly true of the Tyan Thunder K8WE.
