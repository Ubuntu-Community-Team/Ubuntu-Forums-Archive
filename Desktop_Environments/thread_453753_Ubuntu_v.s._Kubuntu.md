### Post by NeoLithium on 2007-05-24
They're basically a matter of personal preference.  I tend to switch back and forth between the two. But the good thing about Ubuntu, is that no matter which you install, you can try them out respectively with each other.

A simple way to try, is:

sudo aptitude install ubuntu-desktop (If you have Kubuntu Installed)
or
sudo aptitude install kubuntu-desktop (If you have Ubuntu installed)

That way you can select which one you like at login, and move between them easily.  I still find myself bouncing between the two just for my own enjoyment and something different from time to time.

### Post by bjornhakon on 2007-05-25
I prefer Gnome over KDE, mainly because KDE feels like a straitjacket compared the more laid back Gnome. I came from Windows 2000 when I first tried Ubuntu with Gnome. I looked through the menues and after 15 minutes I was familiar with the interface. I didn't have to look for anything, or rather, when I had to look for something, I knew where to look. Then I thought, perhaps KDE is even easier for me. I had been told that it looked more like Windows. So I installed KDE. And yes, it did look like Windows, with one Start-button in the bottom left corner and menues that came out from there, like in Windows. Then I started to look for everything. Yes, everything was there, but it took me like 2 days to learn where to look for all the different things. And when I wanted something, it was back to the Start-button, open a menu, open a submenu, look for the wanted item, look for another submenu, study it and finally find what I wanted. KDE looks good, though. But, it didn't take me long to get back to Gnome. It was far more relaxing to work with. 
I knew my way around in Gnome right away. I had to *learn* KDE. I prefer Gnome.
And this comes from someone who has been using Windows only for some 10 years.

Bjorn Hakon
