### Post by ae6rt on 2006-06-16
Good day.

I first started using Linux in 1991 with Slackware and its many floppies distribution.  Over the years, I have remained with Linux, and have engaged in the give and take of using new distributions.

I like Ubuntu.  But there is one thing that I cannot live without, amongst all those gives and takes:  changing workspaces or "desktops" by simply slewing the mouse to the edge of the screen.  FVWM offers this feature with its "EdgeResistance" notion.

My workstation typically has many windows open at once, with mail in "workspace 1", my browser in workspace 3, and application development in workspaces 2 and 4.  These workspace are arranged in two rows, so moving from 1 to 3 involves pulling the mouse cursor downward to the screen edge until 3 appears.  And so forth.  It's a very efficient way to work, and if I had it available to me in Ubuntu's default desktop, I would strongly consider using that default.  If I cannot find a way to slew from one workspace to another in the default, I'll probably revert to FVWM.

Rapid slewing from one workspace to another is just one of those things I cannot give up.  So I'm beseeching the developers of Ubuntu to give us a way to do this, so we can stick with the beautiful default window manager.

Is there a reasonable chance such a feature will be added soon?

Thank you.

Mark
