### Post by mocoloco on 2007-09-01
Did you enable these effects with say the Beryl Manager, or by editing the xorg.conf file, or what?
If it was Beryl or Compiz try this.  At the login screen click on "Options" in the bottom left corner, and select to change session.  Choose "Failsafe GNOME".  Login with that and see if the problem still happens.  Then you should be able to change back whatever you changed.
If you were editing the xorg.conf file, I hope you saved a backup first, or if you used gedit it automatically saves a backup called xorg.conf~
