### Post by mgmiller on 2006-09-16
I have a Canon SD 550 and I use g-thumb to import the pics and then delete them from the camera if I want.  As far as I can tell, Nautilus does not even try to mount my camera and if I search my system when the camera is plugged in, I can't find it.  The g-thumb application works perfectly for me though, so I don't care about nautilus.  I do all my photo manipulation in gimp and picassa and keep the originals on my hard drive, so I don't care about them on the camera.  If you insist on seeing the pics on the memory card, then use a card reader.  If I take the SD card out of my SD550 and put it into my card reader, nautilus will open a window and allow me to browse the card.  I can copy files off the card to my harddrive this way.  It is not a good idea to try writing to the card this way or to try erasing pics on the card this way.  This is an OS independant tip.  It soesn't matter if you are using Windows, Mac or Linux. You can mess up the formatting on the card as far as the camera goes if you try writing or erasing directly onto the card when it's in a card reader.  At that point, the only fix would be to have the camera reformat the card, losing all pics on it.  Whether you experience this problem is seemingly at random.  Sometimes you "get away with it", sometimes you don't.  This is probably why nautilus won't read the camera, but g-thumb will.  G-thumb is designed to speak to the camera and get it to do the erasing, rather than your OS trying to do it.
Hope that's not too confusing.
