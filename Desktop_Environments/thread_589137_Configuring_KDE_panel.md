### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-10-23
You sure can :) You can make Kubuntu's panels just about the same as Gnome. Right click on the panel. Unlock if it's locked. Note the options to add panels, applets, etc. You want to add a panel. Note each feature on the panel has a little button beside it with options to move or delete it etc. Don't be shy to delete something from one panel, 'cause you can right click on the other panel and add it there. Use the appropriate options until you've configured the panels just the way you want. :)

edit, p.s., the minimized programs buttons part is the "taskbar" applet.

### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-10-24
No, those particular frills are Gnome things. There are always trade-offs. On the other hand, your perception is quite correct; KDE tends to be more about options and configureability in many respects, which extends to many of its applications as well. Whereas Gnome tries to aim for simplicity. Also there are a number of KDE apps which are superior, such as Konqueror, Krusader, AmaroK, Kate, Kopete, k3b, BasKet, even such things as KSnapShot. I'm fond of Gnome/Ubuntu and I actually do like the warm look these days, but KDE's apps are the primary reason I chose kubuntu for the base of my install. Evidently KDE is also in a progressive phase with a purportedly impressive KDE 4 coming down the road. Anyway if you do prefer the "more options" approach, KDE just may be your kup 'o ubuntu. :)
