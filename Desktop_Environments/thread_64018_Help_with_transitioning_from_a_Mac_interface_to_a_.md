### Post by graigsmith on 2005-09-12
you can make nautilus more mac os like.

you know how if you're in your home folder, and you double click a folder, it will close the home folder, and open the new folder?  you can make it so it won't close the last folder just like in mac os9.  that way with a regular double click it will just open the folder, and leave the last folder open as well.

if you wanna have that happen.  open system tools/configuration editor/ and then under apps, nautilus, preferences.  scroll down on the right, till you get to 'no ubuntu spacial'  check that box, and then nautilus behaves much more like mac 9.  then just close the configuration editor and your done.

 you know in ubuntu when you open a program or a folder or whatever, on the bottom of the screen there is a task list that shows the tasks that are running.   just to the left of the list you can right click the little space there and click preferences.  then you can choose to always group windows.
