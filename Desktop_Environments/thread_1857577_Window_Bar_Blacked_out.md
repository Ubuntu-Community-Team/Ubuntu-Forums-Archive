### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-10-10
A couple of thoughts.

******* The Window List applet may already be in the bottom panel -  you just can't see it, nor can you see the open apps in the panel. I'd  try changing the color of the panel first (second thought below), before adding another window  list applet. *******

First: I don't see the the two vertical dotted lines in your bottom panel that show you have the "Window List" applet installed.

Right click on a blank spot in the bottom panel and select "Add to Panel" When the add to panel menu opens select "Window List" from the menu. This should add two vertical dotted lines to the panel. Right click on the dotted lines and select "move". Then move the lines to the left keeping any other apps to the left of the dotted lines.

Second: If you can't see the dotted vertical lines you might try changing your bottom panels color - not black. Find a blank spot on the bottom panel and right click. Then select "Properties", select the background tab when the properties window opens. Then select "solid color" from the background tab. I'd start with white (solid color, color button). You could select transparent from the style slider, but with a black background screen this would have no effect. I'd set the slider somewhere in the middle.

Your panel should then turn off-white and you should then be able to see the two dotted vertical line of the Window List applet. All apps opened should then appear to the right of the two dotted vertical lines.

One last thought: Don't forget that you customize your theme and appearance settings in the "system/preferences/appearance" app.

Good Luck & hope this helps.
