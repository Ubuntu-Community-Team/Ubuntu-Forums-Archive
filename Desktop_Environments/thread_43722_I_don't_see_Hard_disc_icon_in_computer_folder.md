### Post by frodon on 2005-06-23
You don't need sudo at all, you can use "df -m"  in a terminal, it will give you all information on hdd. But you can also modify your folder, using Smeg for exemple and I think it should be possible to add HDD icons. Or you have also Gdesklets which you can add icons on your desktop showing you free space of the wanted HDD and also provide you a link to it.

### Post by frodon on 2005-06-23

Smeg is a program which allow you to customize your menu bar, and Gdesklet allow you to add desklets to your desktop, have a look at that : [http://www.lynucs.org/index.php?screen_id=1220565764401fe6e4d3674&p=screen](http://www.lynucs.org/index.php?screen_id=1220565764401fe6e4d3674&p=screen) that show you what you can do for your desktop with Gdesklets, it's beautiful but you must take the time to configure it.

You can find gdesklet in synaptic.
