### Post by egeezer on 2016-11-26
Personal opinions: I really hate icons on a desktop, so I stopped using Gnome when it began looking like Ubuntu.

The virtue of Xfce4 for me is that it can be configured to look like just about anything, which in my case is a top panel and a bottom hidden dock (hidden until you mouse-over the area).  I added Compiz to get windows to do what I want them to, plus for the glitz and the Sphere and different backgrounds for workspaces to remind me where I am (of course, stock Xfce has that anyway).

Sort of a sales pitch for Xfce, I know, but you asked!
