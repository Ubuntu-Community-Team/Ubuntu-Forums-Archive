How about some opinions?

### Post by cmost on 2006-10-04
Personally, I recommend PCLinuxOS for Linux beginners.  I've had great success converting my 65 yo Dad, and two computer illiterate sisters to Linux with this distro.  PCLinuxOS comes on a single CD-ROM that runs as a Live-Distro from which you can install it to a hard disk in a few simple steps.  there are three flavors of the live cd to choose from:  1.  PClinuxOS Mini is a minimalistic KDE installation that you can customize to your exact specifications.  2.  PCLinuxOS Junior is more filled out than mini and typically offers only a single, proven application for any particular task.  3.  PCLinuxOS Big Daddy is the whole nine yards.  It's a full featured KDE desktop environment with many programs installed by default.   PCLinuxOS utilizes only KDE, but it's styled very well (reminiscent of the upcoming Vista but even nicer looking) and most things are where a Windows user would expect them.  Also, most common web and multimedia plugins are installed and enabled by default enabling instant gratification when visiting web pages that use Flash, or Java; or when playing multimedia files.  PCLinuxOS uses a tweaked Mandriva Control Center for manageing the system with familiar GUI tools and it uses Synaptic (and Apt for RPM) for easy package management.  PCLinuxOS's hardware detection is excellent, rivalling even Knoppix.  DKMS is heavily used for painless installation of proprietary nvida or ati drivers and NDISwrapper modules (aka Wi-Fi.)  One huge benefit is that PCLinuxOS is known to have one of the friendliest, most helpful forums of any distro.  If your friend runs into occasional problems, he'll find a warm reception and immediate help.  Finally, with several thousand programs in the repos, it's a newbie's delight.  PCLinuxOS holds a covetted top ten spot on distrowatch.org and I highly reccomend you at least try it out for your friend.  Good luck!
