### Post by Krytarik on 2011-09-18
Make sure you have the correct profile selected for either session in "CompizConfig Settings Manager -> Preferences (lower left)":
- classic Gnome: "Default"
- Unity: "Unity"

Then in classic Gnome, make sure the "Ubuntu Unity Plugin" is disabled in CCSM.

Greetings.

### Post by Frogs Hair on 2011-09-18
You could remove dash from the synaptic package manager , but then Unity would be incomplete and that may cause other problems . You would have the best idea of how you enabled dash in Ubuntu Classic . Just be sure you are logged into Ubuntu Classic before changing anything . I think Krytarik is correct though , enabling the Unity plug-in in Classic may account for dash being present .
