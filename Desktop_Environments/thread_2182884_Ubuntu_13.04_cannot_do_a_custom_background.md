### Post by UltimateCat on 2013-10-23
I have a few ideas:

Download a wallpaper that you like and save it to your pictures.
Rename it. Then right click on your desktop background and go to "change desktop backgrounds" when Ubuntu loads all of the pictures look for a minus and plus sign.
Click the plus sign and choose your 'pictures' and from your pictures directory choose that wallpaper and see if it works.

It worked for me but my DE is Unity not Cinnamon--

If that doesn't work you should be able to create any size composition in GIMP or BLENDER and change the file extention and use that art work that you created as your background.

Hope that helps-;)
