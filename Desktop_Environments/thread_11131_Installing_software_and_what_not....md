### Post by hashimoto on 2005-01-14
Hi MatrixsurferX,

Calm down, take it easy. I told you it is going to take time and effort and you will be frustrated sometimes (OK, quite often in the beginning. Believe me, I've been there several times)

Installing software is more difficult than in Windows. But that way you are also more safe from the viruses (they just can't install themselves so easily). That said, installing software in Ubuntu with Synaptic is actually really easy when you _learn_ it.

Ubuntu quide is your friend, really.  [http://ubuntuguide.org/](http://ubuntuguide.org/)
In this special case (pdf-reader) go first to: [http://ubuntuguide.org/#extrarepositories](http://ubuntuguide.org/#extrarepositories) 
then: [http://ubuntuguide.org/#acroread](http://ubuntuguide.org/#acroread)

You can do it! But it's just like learning a new language: you got to learn and it takes effort.

BR
