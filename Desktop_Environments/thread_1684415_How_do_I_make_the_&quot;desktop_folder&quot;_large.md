### Post by ankspo71 on 2011-02-09
Hi,
> I am trying to increase the size of the desktop folder but cannot see any way to do this.

I think you are asking about resizing the folder view widget... Right click on the desktop and click on Unlock Widgets. Now hover over your desktop's folder view widget and you will see a small menu pop up on the side of it. 

Inside that menu is a little box (on top) with the two tiny arrows inside it that allows you to resize the folder view widget by dragging the little box with your mouse. When you are done, lock the widgets by right clicking on the desktop again. 

The KDE (Kubuntu) desktop is capable of drawing icons across the entire desktop too, just like Gnome (Ubuntu) desktop does. But do this you will need to switch from KDE's "Desktop" layout to the "Folder View" layout. If you want to try this, right click on the desktop and click on unlock widgets again, then right click on the desktop again and click on Desktop Settings. When the desktop settings window opens, go to the view category on the left, then change "Layout:" from 'Desktop' to 'Folder View' using the dropdown menu. Now click apply, then Ok.  If you like what you see you can close the folder view widget that covers the desktop. The right click on your desktop to lock the widgets again.

Hope this helps.
