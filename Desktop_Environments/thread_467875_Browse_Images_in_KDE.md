### Post by otherdave on 2007-06-08
Hello all.

I'm switching from Gnome to KDE to try it out. So far I like it, but there's one little thing that annoys me: browsing images from my digital camera.

What I did in gnome was copy all of my images to a folder.
Then, I'd go to that directory, open the first one.
This would launch the gnome image viewer (eog, I think) and then I could browse (I think it was page up/down or left/right) and delete (with the delete key).
The ones that I didn't delete outright got imported into F-Spot.

Is there a similar program, or view for konqueror, in KDE that makes it quick and easy to scan over images before importing them? 

I've found the photobook view, but that is not only slow, it has some annoyances - when I delete an image, it always reloads the first image in the list. I can't delete with delete or shift-delete so I have to drag the image from the sidebar to the trashcan on my desktop.

I've also used the regular image viewer mode with some limited success, but that also seems kind of slow.

Any ideas for this kind of workflow in the KDE world? Or do I just need to import all of my images and then start deleting them from F-Spot?

### Post by bunker on 2007-06-08
Gwenview.  I think it is installed by default.  Right click and select open with, either on the file (this way gives the behaviour it seems you're looking for) or on the directory in konqueror.  You can also make konqueror use an external viewer rather than open the image in it's own window on double-click as well.  You might have to add gwenview to the file associations in kcontrol or by the 'open with -> other...' option.
