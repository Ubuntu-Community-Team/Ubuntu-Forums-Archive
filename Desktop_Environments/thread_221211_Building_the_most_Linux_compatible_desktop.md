### Post by Taiden on 2006-07-22
So guess what guys? I'm done with games, they bore me, I have other things I would rather be doing. So, I have Ubuntu installed on my tired gaming machine. Luckily for me, a friend of mine is coming soon to pick it up for a fair price. That puts me in the market for a new desktop, which I want to design solely around Linux compatibility.

I am asking you guys to help me come up with a list of hardware that I could use, that would be cost effective, good buy for the money, and 100% linux compatible. I am looking mostly in the direction of audio support, because Linux seems to have trouble with that sometimes.

So, here are the things this box will NEED to do, and do it well.

1) Dual-head. 2x1280x1024 monitors. Sweet. :mrgreen:

2) Full screen video without a hiccup.

3) Burn CD-Rs. This should be really easy to cater to. :D 

4) Run applications, and run them quickly.

5) EXCELLENT quality audio. This means no hiccups, no washy sound, no 1 second lag, full support in everything. amaroK, wine etc.

6) Must be compatible with wine, (x86)

7) Must be cost effective (below $500 not including monitors).

8) CPU stats and fan speed must be easily read for datalogging and geekiness. :)



So, here were the things that I was thinking. I am a long time AMD and nvidia fan, and the nvidia drivers seem to be very good.

AMD XP t-bred of some sort. Perhaps a 2400+ semperon?
512 mb of DDR400 ram
2x80 GB 7200 RPM ATA-133 drives in RAID 0
Some stable 350w power supply
Some decent dual head video card. My AGP 6600GT seems to be doing the trick, but it is currently only set up with one monitor.

I need ideas mostly for the sound card. I need something with good drivers so I can make use of a good set of speakers. :mrgreen: I also need an idea of what mobo I should be aiming for.

Thanks a lot guys. :)
