### Post by emastro on 2011-05-18
For the last 2 days, my desktop environment (Maverick, Gnome) has been behaving in a baffling way. After I log in, I can access the menus and open *one* window. After I've opened it, the mouse stops working on most of the UI; I cannot iconize the window, and clicks outside it, on the menus or on other desktop icons, are ignored. I can access the opened window's menus, usually. If I close it via the menus or with Alt-F4, I can suddenly access other parts of the UI and open other windows. I can also open multiple windows if I do *not* click on them after I've opened them - although this is not always true. Anyway, the moment I click on one of the opened windows, I'm stuck; and after I alt-F4 it, I can only interact with the one which auto-gains focus until I close it too.

Last night I upgraded to Natty+Unity, with no change. When I open a window I'm stuck there until I close it. I can (only once) hover the mouse in the top-left corner and, after visualizing the menus there, gain some control on the window controls. This only happens once, after I've moved/resized/iconized-and-restored the window I lose any control and the top-left buttons do not reappear. The only way out is Alt-F4

In the case of Firefox, though, it appears that I can have either control of the window or of its content, alternatively. If I'm interacting with the content (as I am doing now by writing here) I am basically stuck; but if I right-click on the browser tab, and just re-click anywhere to make the right-click-menu disappear, I can access the top-left menus and the window controls. I cannot, however, interact with the contents of the browser window anymore until I select one of the menus and then click again somewhere inside the window.

I must stress that I have NOT made any changes to the UI configuration in over a month.
