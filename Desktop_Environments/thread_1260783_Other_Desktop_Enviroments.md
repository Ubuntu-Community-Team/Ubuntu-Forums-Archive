### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-09-08
I have some older hardware, so I needed something lightweight, but it also had to be graphical, with point-and-click simplicity because a lot of other kids use this computer during the day (people who are used to Windows and Mac mostly) and I wanted a desktop that wouldn't require a lot of "coaching" or instruction. 

My search led me to [LXDE]("http://lxde.org"), which is so lightweight it has been "accused" of not being a true desktop environment at all. Much, much lighter and faster than Xfce and brings in *very* few dependencies. 

It brought new life to this old Dell Dinosaur, and it runs minimal Ubuntu at mind-bending speed. I get frequent compliments on the speed and simplicity of this old computer, and have won quite a few new users over to Linux with it.

LXDE is sweet, simple, and blazing fast. No frills, no hassle. I'm a fanboy.

-Robin

### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2009-09-08
I'm a total e17 fan - all my machines, desktop, laptop and two netbooks run it over various Ubuntu/Xubuntu installs.  I do like fluxbox too, but e17 is much prettier.

e17 is not well supported and terribly misunderstood (yes, there is a different kind of logic needed to get the most out of it and that does take a bit of getting used to).  It is, however, blindingly fast compared to gnome and kde and very configurable for all sorts of users.

My most recent install (on a Xubuntu Karmic Alpha 5) was from the "official" enlightenment repos ([http://packages.enlightenment.org)and](http://packages.enlightenment.org)and) it has been trouble free (on a Dell Latitude netbook).
