### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2013-03-17
I always stick to the LTS editions.  The "regular" editions seem like beta software, with new users unknowingly and unwillingly used as guinea pigs.  The LTS versions are built from Debian Testing, and the regular releases are built from Debian Unstable.  That *suggests* (but of course, doesn't guarantee) that the LTS versions are safer and less likely to be broken by some random update.  Try installing Xubuntu 12.04.2 and see if you have better luck with it as I have.
