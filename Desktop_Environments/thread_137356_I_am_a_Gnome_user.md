### Post by Jucato on 2006-03-03
KDE pros:
- kioslaves: integration - how a desktop really should be
- konqueror: the most customizable app in KDE, despite being a bit of a bad browser (at times)
- superkaramba: eye-candy + functionality.
- DBUS: operate your apps anywhere, anytime
- KWin: next to Compiz, best composite window manager (ask Poofy)

KDE cons:
- crashes up sometimes, especially Konqueror. Though I haven't fully tested out GNOME enough to make a comparison
- can be a bit slow (?), again, not enough experience with GNOME to verify
- "K" naming convention can get a bit annoying (but I don't mind :D)

2 Kubuntu things I don't like, though:
- System Settings: doesn't show all KControl options, or at least doesn't have the option to show it.
- Adept (Package Manager): too young, too immature. It can mess up your system IF you don't pay attention to what you're doing. Better to use Synaptic or KPackage (at least).

### Post by GeneralZod on 2006-03-03
[QUOTE=Fenyx]
OT: General Zod, have you found the DCOP call to change the desktop background after kdesktoprc has been manually edited?[/QUOTE]

I thought I actually posted this in the original [thread](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=781860&postcount=12), albeit in the form of a link, but it turns out that I completely forgot to include the link I used :) Basically, changing the wallpaper via kdesktoprc isn't needed - you can do it straight from dcop instead.

```

dcop kdesktop KBackgroundIface setWallpaper <image> <mode>

```

(taken from [http://www.bender-suhl.de/stefan/english/comp/gkrellmbgchg.html](http://www.bender-suhl.de/stefan/english/comp/gkrellmbgchg.html))

Remember that your script need to know what DISPLAY you are working on.
