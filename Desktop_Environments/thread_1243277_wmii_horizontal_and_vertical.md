### Post by TheKid965 on 2009-08-28
I don't know if it's possible in wmii, as I haven't used it in quite some time, but this effect is relatively simple in Awesome and Xmonad, both of which I've tried and found superior to wmii for my own uses.  Just cycle through the different tiling layouts until you find the one you like, with the master window on the bottom and the secondary ones on top.  

Unfortunately, neither these nor wmii are as versatile as Ion is when it comes to layouts; for example, only Ion lets you control both the horizontal *and* vertical size of each frame, while other tiling WMs pretty much force you to choose one or the other and let the WM handle the dimension you can't control.  Of course, Ion has its own problems (some people don't like the forced titlebars on each frame, and its control scheme could be a tad more newbie-friendly).
