### Post by napzilla on 2007-12-05
The devilspie package could probably do it, although this may be overkill. I previously used devilspie to display system information and logs inside a terminal that appeared to be part of my desktop. I also used it to direct the windows of some applications to certain workspaces (I use 8 ), while causing other windows to automatically persist on all workspaces. So, if it could do all that, it might also be able to do what you're looking for.

I almost forgot to mention that I stopped using devilspie because it wasn't playing well with Gutsy on my machine. So, try it at your own peril (everything went back to normal once I removed devilspie, though).
