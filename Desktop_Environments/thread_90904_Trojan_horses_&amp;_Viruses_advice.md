### Post by shof2k on 2005-11-16
Hi,
This question may be better suited to the absolute beginners section as it would give some useful background to those new to linux. I'll leave that to the mods to decide though.

Trojan horses etc spy on your computer in various ways, and report back to the originator.  A good firewall will Stop / Warn such suspicious network activity.

If you want some additional protection, I'd recommend installing a firewall (firestarter and guarddog are good) and a virus checker (such as clamav).  T

If you're really paranoid you can install a rootkit and try out some of the kernel customisations for secure linux.

Basically linux doesn't suffer from spyware, malware, viruses as badly as windows (if at all).  The chances of getting something really nasty is remote.  There are various reasons for this such as the number of linux machines, the nature of linux, and the nature of open software.  However it is good to be sensible on the net and only download / install stuff that you are confident with.

Hope that helps,
