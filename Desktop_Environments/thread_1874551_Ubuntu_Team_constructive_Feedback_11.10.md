### Post by linuxcool on 2011-11-03
Hello Mark S and your Team,
Testing done on 900A Celeron EeePC
Running Ubuntu and Xubuntu both suffer from their fonts not rendering correctly. Different letters say for example the letter "P" will be missing say the bottom half of the letter and changing the font only causes a different letter to not render properly.
Unlike most others Unity and Gnome3 excite me with the promise of what will come but sadly these are incomplete desktop environments. System setting you take for granted in Gnome2 are missing requiring the install of 3rd party applications. Example: Changing Fonts. In the default install of Ubuntu 11.10 show me which application allows the fonts to be changed?
Now "Unity" has this great feature of showing what images, documents etc I've viewed recently to allow me to find them again. My problem is there isn't the option of deleting those files you don't want others seeing.
I have personal files I don't want others looking over my shoulder seeing at work.
Another problem with my install of "Unity" is there are now 2 "Download" and "Music" folders. on the default desktop with no way of removing them. They just appeared after restarting my PC.
After installing the gnomeshell packages, Banshee freezes making it unusable. Synaptic now crashes upon starting. 

Now I know there will be those who say "Try creating your own Distro and see how you go!" well in advance I do. My Distro is currently in the top 100 on Distrowatch and isn't based on Ubuntu. My Distro focus and vision differs from Ubuntu's requiring the use of Ubuntu on a number of PC's I use.
Usability of any Operating System should be the goal of any Distro creator which would prevent me from releasing Ubuntu 11.10 in its current form if it were my Distro.

I think a big problem with Linux is the releasing of buggy or incomplete software that's not ready for general use. KDE4 was released before it was ready and only now we are seeing it finally coming together after 4 years.

I guess because developers want as many users using these unfinished environments to find bugs and provide possible fixes, this is the way it has to be. Only trouble is you lose new Linux users along the way because they expect their desktop to be complete ready to use.

Ubuntu has the resources $$$ to bring Unity to a more complete state of readiness for general use and needs to get the basics in place as quickly as it can, way before 12.04 is released.

Those looking for a more usable Ubuntu should start with Xubuntu 11.10

I was excited to ready Ubuntu is heading to mobile phones, tablets and this isn't expected to happen until 2014. The mobile phone user won't be as forgiving as the current Ubuntu PC users are. Ubuntu may only get one chance to impress Apple and Android users over to Ubuntu.
