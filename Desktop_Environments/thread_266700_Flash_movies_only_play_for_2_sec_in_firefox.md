### Post by Darxus on 2006-12-23
I was having the same problem as all of you, using flash player 9.  In the process of downgrading I noticed that what Adobe is currently providing for download under Linux is flash player 7.  (there was no flash player 8 for Linux)

I had installed flash player 9 via automatix.  I'm guessing the rest of you did the same?

Anyway, go to:
[http://www.adobe.com/products/flashplayer/](http://www.adobe.com/products/flashplayer/)
Click "Get Flash Player 9" (not a typo)
You'll get a page to download flash player 7, click "Download Now".

Direct link:  [http://fpdownload.macromedia.com/get/flashplayer/current/install_flash_player_7_linux.tar.gz](http://fpdownload.macromedia.com/get/flashplayer/current/install_flash_player_7_linux.tar.gz)

The answer to the installer question "Please enter the installation path of the Mozilla, Netscape, or Opera browser (i.e., /usr/lib/mozilla):" is /usr/lib/firefox

It's nice to have youtube back.

I'm using Ubuntu Edgy (up to date).
