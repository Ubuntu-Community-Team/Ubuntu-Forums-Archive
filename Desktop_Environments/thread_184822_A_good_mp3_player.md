### Post by groggyboy on 2006-05-30
I use amaroK myself.

But there are lots of music players out there.  In addition to the ones already mentioned, you should take a look at banshee, quod libet, muine, exaile, audacious, XMMS2, rhythmbox, juk (KDE based), kaffeine (also KDE based), mpd (command-line based), music123 (also command-line based), quark, and many many more that I don't know about.  Apparently there is also a linux port of winamp in progress!

There are no shortage of music players in linux.  Some have more/different features than others.  Try out several or all, and find the one(s) you like.  Most of the players I've mentioned are in the repositories.  But if not, a quick google search will point you in the right direction.

While amaroK is my main music player, I also have banshee, listen, quod libet and xmms installed.

happy hunting!
groggyboy
