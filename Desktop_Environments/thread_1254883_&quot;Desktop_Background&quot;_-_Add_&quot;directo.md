### Post by Shazaam on 2009-09-01
I use wallpaper-tray (get it either with Synaptic or apt-get install). With wallpaper-tray (or similar) you can do what you want (use your own folder, have a different background with every boot, etc). If you just want to use the default "Appearance" window, drag/drop or copy/paste pics into the window or use the "Add" button.
Here is a hint for you...
If you do decide to go with wallpaper-tray, after installation right-click a panel- choose "Add to panel". :)
