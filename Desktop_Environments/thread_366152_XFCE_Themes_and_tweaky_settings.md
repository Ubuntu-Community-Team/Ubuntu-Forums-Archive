### Post by kerry_s on 2007-02-20
menu> settings>setting manager
that should give you the xfce control panel.

The simple thing to do is create your own menu with just the entries you want.
So menu> accessories> appfinder
now right click the menu icon> edit menu
Now just drag the applications you want in appfinder to the top of the edit menu list, check the box under hidden for the ones you don't want to show, system is the main 1. click on the + sign to add entries, use the drop down menu to chose type of entry, i usually use submenu, then just drop the applcation on the submenu name to place it there. If you want to chck what it looks like as you go along, just click file>save on the top and click on the menu icon. I've got my system pretty much how i want it so i only use a few entries, see pic->
oops, forgot 2 entries i would keep, also just so you now in xfce you can drag and drop most things, which really makes making icons and launchers easy to make.

### Post by crimesaucer on 2007-03-06
Once you get comfortable with xubuntu xfce, check these 2 threads for instructions on how to modify your own xfce-engine gtkrc files. It's really easy and you can basically write and use your own themes/colors/size buttons/scrollbars: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=371110](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=371110)

and this is my "How To" for modifying the gtkrc: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=377397](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=377397)

I even put a gtkrc in there that you can use or change to your own color preferences using the List that put in my "How To" to show you where to change the colors for certain things like text color, browser color, Thunar, mousepad, and your menus and task bars.
