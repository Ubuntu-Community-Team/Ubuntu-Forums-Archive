### Post by mcduck on 2010-02-12
That's definitely related to your graphics card. Could be a driver issue (if you don't know if you succesfully installed the drivers then you probably didn't), but in worst case it could just be that your graphics card is broken or overheating.

Check that the card's cooling fan is working, and is not full of dust or anything. After that try to get the driver installed. If that doesn't solve the problem then your card is probably busted, you might be able to continue using it if you disable desktop effects and avoid all graphics-intensive stuff but buying a new one before the card fails completely would be a good idea.
