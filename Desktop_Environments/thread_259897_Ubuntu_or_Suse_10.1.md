### Post by fudoki on 2006-09-19
I have SUSE 10.1 on my main "server" (P-4 w/1Gb & 2x80Gb HDD, dual boot w/WinXP Home), on my "web server" (P-3 w/384Mb & 80Gb HDD, exposed to the Net with web site, ftp, and numerous tools - no mail), and on my "Windows only" HP ze5468CL Laptop (P-4 w/512Mb & 60Gb HDD, dual boot w/Win2k)and have Ubuntu LTS 6.06 on my "Games" machine (P-4 HT w/1.5Gb & 250Gb HDD, dual boot w/WinXP Home), so I use both systems every day.
I am a Network Engineer and have been providing professional support for Linux, Windows, BSD, Unix, and other systems since the late 1970's, and have taught Computer Science and Networking while Networking Coordinator at the Univ. of Georgia College of Education for 8 years.  I have personally trained hundreds of technicians, programmers, and network administrators.
There has been some real good advice on here, except for one thing - don't put Gentoo on a laptop unless you hate life!  I will explain: Gentoo is like Slackware, with improved updating and installation scripts.  Everything is done "from scratch" and from the command line.  There are no safety nets and things only work if you know *perfectly* what you are doing.  Gentoo has a server orientation, and only a bona fide expert, or a masochist, would put it on a laptop - so help will be scarce if you can find it at all!
All of the major Linux distros provide a quality platform and are easier to install and use with every passing day.  Of all the distros, SUSE and Debian are my personal favorites.  But this is personal taste - not a technically supportable position - they are all so darn good.  But here's the real meat: SUSE, like RedHat, simply refuses to support any kind of decent multimedia fearing "copyright and patent infringement".  This is because they are based in the United States and subject to being taken to court under the ridiculous, pro-corporation, American laws like "The Millenium Copyright Act" and others, that make natural laws, prior art, and functions of mathematics and nature able to be made the private personal property of individuals and corporations.....
So if you want to watch movies, rip DVD's and CD's, and generally enjoy your computer, a Debian or Mandriva (based on RedHat) based distro is the way to go.  But you can, with a couple hours of manual installation and a few compiles, visit the Mandriva "PLF" archives and install the Mandriva (10.2 and 2006) multimedia components, like Xine, Lame, and mPlayer and make your SUSE machine a stable entertainment and music authoring machine.... To me, the ability to use a computer for entertainment is critical, and for this purpose Ubuntu , and Debian (or Mandriva) are just way ahead of SUSE.
For a business only application, I would always recommend SUSE or RedHat, in that order, because I simply love Yast (the SUSE managment tool) and these distros are both just rock solid, and as flawless as software can be.  If the business is a Technology business, that recommendation would change to Debian.  Most businesses want to eliminate the ability to be entertained at work, so the absence of multimedia capability is a plus in these cases - but if they need a "full featured system" then Debian or Mandriva are the best "packaged" choices.
If you have the technical "stones" to do it, Slackware or Gentoo will both provide a superior server or workstation, but will just not be time effecient for most production settings - too much reading and looking for help!  BUT - IF YOU GOAL IS TO LEARN LINUX, THE GENTOO COMMUNITY AND DISTRO ARE THE GOLDEN PATH TO BECOMING GOOD ENOUGH TO USE SLACKWARE.  If you know any "Slackware Guru's", you know some real experts.  Slackware is Gentoo without the great automatic scripts and excellent help and documentation.  These are both rock-solid distros.
When I went from Debian to SUSE on my laptop, it detected, AND CORRECTLY CONFIGURED, all devices on the machine.  Even the IR interface that Debian could not see and Windows does not correctly address.  SUSE detected, and CORRECTLY configured ALL of these.  I was stunned!  Since then I have installed over a dozen SUSE servers with the same result.  It's simply hard to beat, and I only know of ONE distro with an equally good install and maintenance capability - Ubuntu.
I have installed around 100 copies of Ubuntu, on every kind of machine from old Pentium 100 to dual core screamers - always with the same result - a perfect install and config with minimal problems.  Sure, there is the occasional oddball part that needs special attention, but if your machine is in any way "standard", Ubuntu will install and run without a hitch.
Ubuntu, IMHO, is the best thing to happen to Linux, ever.  When Windows came out, X-windows was already almost 10 years old.  I had a NeXT at the time, running X with what is now the "WindowMaker" interface - my all time fave.  Windows has never been as good as X, and never will be.  When SUSE 10 came out, I thought I had seen the ultimate "Winblows killer" - that a better distro (in terms of installation, appearance, performance, and applications) was not possible.  The I installed Ubuntu "Hoary Hedgehog".  Since then, I have installed SUSE on request and on servers, and have recommended and installed Ubuntu of EVERYTHING else. Many of my clients are old and retired, like me, and want a hassle-free platform to email their grandkids and swap pictures with friends - Ubuntu ends their Winslows problems, usually after a virus or spyware incident.  Not a single one has wanted to go back to Windows.  To the contrary, many of their kids and grandkids have installed Ubuntu on their own computers and laptops after using it at "Grandma's house".  It has been amazing to me how eager people are to free themselves of the constantly broken M$ product line.
So, in short, I would install SUSE or Ubuntu interchangably, but if your goal is to learn to be a Network Administrator - the non-standard handling of the superuser account (root) by Ubuntu will mess you up.  You need to learn how to BE "root", that's a Network Admin's job.  For this, you need a "standard" distro, so in the context of your question, I would have to suggest SUSE.
If you want a system with FULL CAPABILITIES (which was not your question), I would recommend Debian "Stable" with "Etch" multimedia and browser components (but stick with Etch/Stable), or Ubuntu with "Root" restored for compatibility with standard distros.
Ubuntu made a political decision with their handling of "root", one that I understand and, in fact, support; it will make Ubuntu able to be the "Windows killer", but it does not provide a standard platform and therefore is disqualified as a training platform.
After you have learned SUSE or Debian inside out, get a really powerful computer and install Gentoo from scratch - it will probably ruin your life for three months, but you will become a really good Network Admin and have the fastest machine any of your friends have ever set eyes on...
Sorry this is so long, and I hope you find it helpful.  Reply with any questions or comments.

fudoki
