### Post by deadflowr on 2015-06-06
Add it to startup applications.
I do not know the command, but it should be rainlendar, or rainlendar2.
reference from here
[http://ubuntuguide.net/install-rainlendar-customizable-desktop-calendar-in-ubuntu-12-04](http://ubuntuguide.net/install-rainlendar-customizable-desktop-calendar-in-ubuntu-12-04)
The guide is for 12.04, but...
Startup applications in 14.04 (my guess that this is the version you are using) can be accessed using Ubuntu's dash-search.
