### Post by Brachabre on 2006-06-01
I think you can just add resolutions to each color Depth "8bit, 16bit, 24bit" under the section "Screen" in your /etc/X11/xorg.conf  file.    Me thinks you can go in front of all the resolutions listed like this ex. "yourresxhere" "1024x768" "832x624" "800x600"


If you aren't sure how to edit stuff do this command:

sudo nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf

### Post by redxninja on 2006-06-01
This is how I do my widescreen setting in xorg.

Open up the terminal and type this comand.

[COLOR="SeaGreen"]
gtf 1900 1200 60[/COLOR]

what that mean is 1900x1200 at 60Hz.
gtf will out put some thing like this...

[COLOR="SeaGreen"] # 1904x1200 @ 60.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 74.52 kHz; pclk: 191.96 MHz
  Modeline "1904x1200_60.00"  191.96  1904 2032 2240 2576  1200 1201 1204 1242 -HSync +Vsync[/COLOR]

Copy that to the Monitor section in your xorg.conf...It looks like this.

[COLOR="SeaGreen"]Section "Monitor"
        Identifier  "FPD2185W"
        VendorName  "GateWay"
        ModelName   "FPD2185W Widescreen"
        Option "DPMS"
        HorizSync  31.0 - 81.0      # use your own lcd settings for this
        VertRefresh 56.0 - 76.0    # use your own lcd settings for this
        
        # 1904x1200 @ 60.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 74.52 kHz; pclk: 191.96 MHz
        Modeline "1904x1200_60.00"  191.96  1904 2032 2240 2576  1200 1201 1204 1242  -HSync +Vsync

EndSection
[/COLOR]

### Post by dmorel on 2006-06-01
GOT IT!
Found this idea in another thread:

In xorg.conf for depth of 24, remove all other options.

It worked...


so it now looks like:
SubSection "Display"
		Depth		24
		Modes		"1900x1200"
	EndSubSection


nice.
