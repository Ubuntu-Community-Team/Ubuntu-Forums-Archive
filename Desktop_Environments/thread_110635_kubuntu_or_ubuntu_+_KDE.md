### Post by hoodwink on 2005-12-31
[QUOTE=valnar]What's better/more stable overall?  Going straight to Kubuntu or running Ubuntu and adding KDE? 
[/QUOTE]
There probably is no such animal as better/more stable. It all depends on your preferences. If you have any preference for Gnome and its applications but want to use KDE or its applications, then by all means install Ubuntu and then add KDE.

In my case I have little love for Gnome [flame war begins again, no doubt], so I installed Kubuntu. After a moderately rough upgrade from 5.04 to 5.10, it's been rock solid. I've upgraded to KDE 3.5 and it's still rock solid. I keep hearing mutterings that Kubuntu is less stable, but I've seen no evidence of that on my own systems. I believe that this is an urban myth.

My $.02. The only disadvantage to running Kubuntu is the prevailing attitude(s) in Ubuntu land. Most of the helpful hints you find in posts on the forums (except for the KDE forums) are very much Gnome-centric, and Kubuntu remains a poor stepchild for much of Ubuntu land. This in spite of Shuttleworth's comments that both are to be treated equally.

YMMV,
