### Post by aysiu on 2005-12-30
From my limited experience, I think it's one of two things:

1. Most icons are named the program.png. For example, JuK's icon is juk.png. KWord's icon is kword.png. So when you change icon themes, Gnome is looking in your new icon theme's folder to see what the icon is for the new program. I *think* I remember GIMP's icon not being called gimp.png but something crazy like gimp2.2-48x48.png (I'm going on memory).

2. Most icons refer to the icon theme (in /usr/share/pixmaps) or your manually selected theme. I think (again, speculation based on limited experience) GIMP, for some reason, draws on the program's icon rather than your theme's icon.

Your best bet is really to change it manually.

I believe these same rules do not apply for OpenOffice, but the names are a little strange... I think it's ooo2-writer.png or something...
