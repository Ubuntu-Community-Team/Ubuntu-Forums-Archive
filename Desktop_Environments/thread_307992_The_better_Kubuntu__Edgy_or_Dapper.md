### Post by Rodneyck on 2006-11-27
Why not install Edgy/Ubuntu? It seems to be very stable for me. 

I started with Edgy/Kubuntu and have gone back and forth a few times between it and Edgy/Ubuntu just to test drive before settling. I decided to stick with Ubuntu (Gnome) I can say that I had more applications crash in Kubuntu than Ubuntu. Actually, I do not think I have had one, cross my fingers, crash in Ubuntu, yet. 

I understand that Kubuntu is more like Windows in intuitive feel and some of the applications parallel this, such as "add and remove", so understandable why it would be a good transition. In the long run, Ubuntu/Gnome just works, not to mention for my setup, it seems faster than KDE. 

Whichever you choose, I would definitely download Automatrix2 and install all the codecs, browser plugins and everything that makes it hard for a newbie to install and configure off the bat. Linux is not just point-n-click like Windows, although it is getting better. I had issues with a five button Intellimouse Optical and had to modify my boot file, I think that was the file, so make sure everything is working properly.
