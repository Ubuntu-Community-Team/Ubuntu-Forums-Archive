### Post by Npl on 2007-11-23
Might be higly dependend on the programm you use for ripping. Audio-CDs dont have solid error-correction and some Programms use various techniques like reading everything multiple times to ensure the ripped audio is accurate.

CD-Rom Drives also might have very slow Audio-Extracion - they are focused on Data-CDs after all.

The only thing you can do is trying to read a Data-CD (into .iso file) - if its just as slow then the driver is at fault. Otherwise either your Drive is slow for audio-ripping or the App you using focusses on acurate data over speed
