### Post by SmartBits on 2010-12-07
To start off with, I have to say I have finally decided to abandon redhat/fedora/centos  family for Ubuntu. 

I spent 3 grueling days trying to get nx to work on fedora 14. But, it took me a couple of hours to get freeNX going on ubuntu maverick( the setup is by no means a breeze, but at least it works if you follow instructions from [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FreeNX") ). And Ubuntu seems to have excellent community support for ubuntu on the amazon cloud ( thanks to Eric Hammond's alestic.com, CloudInit by canonical etc ). 

Also, I could not for the life of me figure out how to install or find the equivalent of resize2fs command in fc14 which was available in ubuntu.
   
I am no means a linux expert. And this is by no means a exhaustive determination of which flavor of linux is best suited for our environment. But, good ec2 support and getting usability features like NX working is very important to us. 


note: on both cases i used the official AMI releases for the cloud. 
[fedora images]("https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/Cloud_SIG/EC2_Images")
[ubuntu images]("http://uec-images.ubuntu.com/releases/10.10/release/")
