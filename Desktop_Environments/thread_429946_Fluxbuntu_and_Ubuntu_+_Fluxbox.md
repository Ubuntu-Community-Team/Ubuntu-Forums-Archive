### Post by dolphin_oracle on 2007-05-02
I also played around with fluxbox sitting on top of a edgy xubuntu installation.  It worked fine.  I liked it so much I decided to try fluxbuntu, and it worked great, very speedy.  Until I tried to print via cups.  I never got it to work under fluxbuntu, despite setting it up numerous times under xubuntu/ubuntu.

One other fluxbuntu caveat - it doesn't install everything by default that a xubuntu/ubuntu system has.  For instance, OO is not there, nor is gnumeric.  The iso for fluxbuntu is in the 300+ Mb range as opposed to xubuntu's 600+ range.   Its easy to add apps ater via synaptic, apt-get, or whatever, which are there.  Automounting of USB thumb drivers didn't work either, but it was a relatively easy fix.  All that still worked for me when it was setup under xubuntu +fluxbox.

but both experiences were plenty fast for me on a 128 mb 900mhz laptop.
