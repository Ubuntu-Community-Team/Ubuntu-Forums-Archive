### Post by ColinG on 2005-03-14
Having read some great write-ups I thought I'd give Hoary a try to see how it compares to my main distro, Fedora and one that I have been playing with, namely Mepis.

Firewire capturing of video from camera is not implemented properly in that the raw 1394 module is not loaded at boot. I have found a manual workaround. This is a problem I also found in Mepis and Fedora.

The desktop implimentation is pretty slick although I have experienced the odd icon being left on the desktop after burning a dvd. I think there is some confusion betwen my internal dvd read only drive and my exteral usb dvd +/- rw drive. The actual burning is fine though.

Sound, well there seems to be a problem here. Reading one of the threads on these forums I was able to get sound working but it is flaky at best. I use Kino quite a bit but it would not play the sound back smoothly on the clips it had captured. The playback starts off in silence, splutters and then comes to life. I can get round this by turning off desktop sounds but clearly there is some sort of conflict. Also Kino crashes when I try to save my work . The error is "Could not save SMIL". Does anyone know what that means? I have not seen these issues in either Fedora or Mepis.

The boot and running speed of Hoary is impressive and it compares favourably with Mepis and boot wise is much better tha Fedora. I love the GNOME dektop, I do find that KDE can look a bit messy (perhaps too much like Windoze). The desktop is a nice point on Fedora but Mepis lacks in this dept - it is just a bog standard KDE jobbie.

Operationally, Mepis and Fedora come out on top as they do not have the above sound bug (or the Kino issue which may well be related) or the icon issue. Should these get ironed out, then I can safely say that Ubuntu will become my distro of choice. It is slick and feels as though it belongs on the home users desktop.

So, will I now return to Fedora. Well probably. Unless, that is, someone in these forums can get me over the sound issues I mentioned (including Kino). I need to be able to use Kino.

All that said, Haory is only a preview and some bugs are to be expected so well done to the develpment team.

Now, do I reinstall Fedora or can a kind sole or two help me with this sound/kino thing?

Many thanks
Colin
