### Post by irv on 2011-05-31
Right now I am running 11.04, 10.04, 10.10. and I am using Unity, Gnome 2.32, and Xfce DE. Since 11.04 came out I have tried it with Unity, Gnome 3, Gnome 2.32 and Now Xfce.

I have two internal hard drives and one USB hard drive that I use for testing. The two internal hd are running 10.10 and gnome 2.32 and the other running 11.04 and Unity. The USB hd now has 11.04 and Xfce.

After trying 11.04 with Unity and 11.04 with Gnome 3, I find them both designed wrong. They are very clumsy to use. (To many mouse movements and clicks) To many things need to be open and to many things to search for. I spend more time trying to do simple things that use to take me seconds to do in gnome 2.32.

Now that Gnome 3 is out, I believe gnome 2.32 is going away so I am looking for other avenues to go in. I have tried fedora and suse, but don't like the RPM's and there package manager. A few years ago I tried Xfce but liked gnome better.

This morning I decided to install it on a USB drive and give it a test drive. I find I really like it because I do things in seconds again. Things are easy to find and there is some things that are a lot like gnome 2.32. And one of the biggest things I like is it is so customisable. Also it boots very fast even from a USB drive. I believe I will be going to it as my main DE. I always liked Ubutnu and have use it since 2005 so I am glad I can stay with it even though I do not like or plan to use Unity or Gnome 3 in the future. This doesn't mean I will not change my mind, but as of now I do not like either one. Let see what will happen with the 11.10 release.

### Post by luvr on 2011-06-10
In my opinion, neither Unity nor the GNOME 3 Shell were developed with the traditional desktop (or laptop) primarily in mind. Rather, they seem to target new generations of devices—like tablets, perhaps.

My limited exposure to Unity and the GNOME 3 Shell seems to suggest that, at least to me, Unity is probably better usable on traditional desktop systems than GNOME 3 Shell—but that's, of course, just my opinion.

For now, I'm not particularly thrilled about either of these “innovative” desktop environments. Furthermore, I've never really felt at home with KDE, and KDE4 alienates me even more than KDE3. I can imagine that others will love KDE4; it certainly has its qualities, but it just isn't my cup of tea.

As far as Ubuntu is concerned, I will stay on 10.04 for the time being—it ain't “broke,” so why should I insist on “fixing” it? Plus, it's a long-term support release, so I needn't hurry to migrate off it at this time.

I'm also playing with Fedora 15, to get a feel for different current desktops—KDE4, GNOME 3 Shell, XFCE, and LXDE. I'm quite charmed by XFCE, which may well become my desktop of choice once classic GNOME dies off. I haven't, however, seen enough of LXDE just yet to form an opinion about it, but I will certainly take a closer look before making up my mind.

### Post by luvr on 2011-06-12
> **irv said:**
> [SIZE="2"]I can remember back with I started using Ubuntu and I could install and run any DE I wanted.[/SIZE]
You still can, can't you? You can run Unity, GNOME classic, KDE, XFCE, LXDE. The issue is not that you no longer have the choice; rather, it is that GNOME is undergoing massive changes—which you may or may not like *(and I happen to be among those that **don't** like the way GNOME is evolving).* On top of that, Ubuntu is developing its own desktop experience—namely, Unity—which, again, some will like, and others won't.

While it is true that the traditional GNOME desktop will die off, and thus, it won't generally be a practical choice to keep using that, you still do have a choice; you don't **have** to accept the default desktop environment.

Some time ago, KDE underwent its own revolution, with the move from KDE3 to KDE4; these changes, again, didn't please everyone. As I mentioned earlier, I never really felt at home with KDE3, so I tried KDE4 just to see if I could like that any better—and found that I couldn't.

Hence, given that nowadays the two major desktop environments don't appear all too attractive to me, I'll be checking out other options, specifically, XFCE and LXDE.

That adds up to five desktop environments to choose from: Ubuntu Unity, GNOME 3 Shell, KDE4, XFCE, and LXDE. That's almost beginning to sound like *[“l'embarras du choix”]("http://ofthekosmos.blogspot.com/2006/09/lembarras-du-choix.html")*... :)
