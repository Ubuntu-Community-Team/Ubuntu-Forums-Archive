### Post by roger64 on 2006-09-05
Sorry for the mistakes. Ze suis French. 

Quite recently, I switched for good from XP to Ubuntu. To begin with, I am just an "average Joe" type of user, a true Ubuntu fan and keen about user-friendliness. In this framework, I modestly present you this idea: Mr Clean (or whatever name, that's not the point).

Intended users: Ubuntu (and Linux) beginners

I only know some cleaning tools. You probably use much better ones. Let speak about them.

I just learnt that Mr fsck checks the files every thirty openings -this number may vary-. I am very thankful for him because he also works at home when I am away. I do not know if he does his work for all mounted partitions? For example, I just created a new partition, /virtual, will he take care of it?  And also, Mr fsck is very bad tempered. He hardly speaks. Some even say that he can be violent at times...

Sometimes, memory fails me. I cannot find a file, even with Miss locate's help. She is very bright though (I stopped using Mr find's services, we do not speak the same language). I realized that Miss locate works much better after a little cup of #sudo updatedb. 

Cleaning is not only vacuum-cleaning. It's also about ironing and putting clothes in the cupboards. The small gnome family is very helpful for that, #sudo clean, #sudo autoclean, #"deborphan, #localepurge are working hard. I do not know who can clean from time to time my /tmp waste basket. Then we use the wonderful Vaporetto with #sudo apt-get update, and for the whole house and the garden #sudo apt-get upgrade.

Now everything is spot clean and Ubuntu is humming nicely. So, What about M Clean?

Well, it could be a kind of script or a small graphical front-end targetting squarely the beginners.  Its use could be recommended every ......(to be filled up by specialists) or after ............(again).  Mr Clean would keep in one place many of these so useful and so ignored cleaning tools.

One little more thing : knowing that they can rely on Mr Clean, new and very afraid Windows users just switching and shaking, totally upset with their previous experience, would get an instant and easy help with a visible and reliable tool. 

I  just drop this idea on a public place because, unhappily, I do not know how to write a script, even less a front-end. But you can rely on me for the fool's proof.;) 

To begin with, we could just set up a list of all these small and useful "cleaning something"  tools, just to get an idea of what could be done. I am sure I know only a very small part of them.
