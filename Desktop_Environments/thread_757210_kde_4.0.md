### Post by benerivo on 2008-04-16
To install alongside your existing kde3, I would add the [latest kde4 repositories]("http://www.kubuntu.org/announcements/kde-4.0.php") for gutsy and then install kde4-core, which is a basic kde4 installation. The kubuntu-kde4 release is really just kde4-core, plus a selection of kde3 apps.

When using KDE4 it is obvious that it's still in it's infancy and doesn't have many programs developed for it yet. As a result you may find it slightly buggy, and unconfigurable, and you will definitely need to keep using many of your kde3 apps such as digikam, k3b, amarok, etc. This will be fixed in time, and I would expect that by the end of the year, kde4.1 will have made a more usable environment and that many kde3 apps will have been ported to kde4. [This]("http://itmanagement.earthweb.com/osrc/article.php/3740561") is a nice article about the current state of kde4.
