### Post by Bartender on 2005-11-24
Hi, sherwin -
Being an uber-newb myself, I feel your pain.  There are install guides all over the place.  Here's one:

[http://ubuntuguide.org/](http://ubuntuguide.org/)

That oughta keep you busy for a little while...
Google "ubuntu linux", "ubuntu install", "ubuntu help", etc. and you'll find enough stuff to empty your printer's paper tray.  Many of those hits will lead you right back to these forums.  The problem then of course is to make sense from all that data.
I'm gonna make a couple of suggestions, based on my impressions from cruising the forums for the last couple of weeks.  Take them with a healthy grain of salt because I'm still waiting on my CD's and haven't any actual experience with Ubuntu.
- Dual-booting seems to create a lot of posts.  I think that Ubuntu's perfectly capable of a painless dual-boot experience, but it seems a lot of people messed up their installs.  Sometimes their Windows partitions become inaccessible.  I'm going to install to a second machine and bypass all of that worrying.
-Apparently hardware that's roughly three or four years old is less likely to give you grief than bleeding-edge stuff.  Check out the Wiki's Hardware Compatibility lists.  There's a limit to going back in time...a 486 with 64MB of RAM and a 2GB HDD ain't gonna work real good either. 
-I'm stuck with dial-up, which isn't an insurmountable problem but broadand sure would be nice.  If you've got broadband, super.  If not, you'll find that the vast majority of dial-up modems pawn some of their workload off on the CPU and need Windows-specific software to run.  They are difficult to impossible under Linux.

Good luck, and I hope that we both have flawless installs!
