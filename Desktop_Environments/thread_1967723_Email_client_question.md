### Post by Lars Noodén on 2012-04-28
A couple of thing here:

It might be time to say goodbye to MS Hotmail and pick a service that can provide you with IMAP.  [gmx](https://help.gmx.com/mail/overview/pop3) and [gmail](https://support.google.com/mail/bin/answer.py?hl=en&answer=75726) are just two.  There are many others.

As far as transferring images goes, e-mail is not a good surrogate for a file system.  The base64 encoding needed to send the picture as an attachment ensures that it takes up a *lot* more space than a plain binary.  So you might look to other services there for your file transfer needs.  [Ubuntu One](https://one.ubuntu.com/) is just one of many free, paid or freemium services.  There is also SpiderOak, Dropbox, RapidShare, MegaUpload, or Box.  There are dozens of others all marketing as "cloud" storage services, the new name for hosting.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-04-28
I don't think there's any file size limit in Thunderbird beyond those imposed by the operating system.  That 2 GB limit might apply to Windows and FAT32 file systems.  On a Linux box, your folders can be much larger than that.

On the other hand, there are good reasons not to have folders anywhere near that big, primarily performance ones.  My biggest folders are about 900 MB.  If you have really large folders, I'd recommend splitting them up, perhaps by year.
