### Post by Dodge on 2005-07-01
Edit xorg-conf

In your monitor settings, change it to read the following:

Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"hp 5500"
        HorizSync     30-70
        VertRefresh  50-140
	Option		"DPMS"
EndSection

And then reboot.

Dodge

### Post by frodon on 2005-07-01
you can also add 1280*1024 resolution if your screen can do it : 
```
Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"Intel Corporation 82845G/GL[Brookdale-G]/GE Chipset Integrated Graphics Device"
	Monitor		"hp 5500"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		1
		Modes		"1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		4
		Modes		"1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		8
		Modes		"1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		15
		Modes		"1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		16
		Modes		"1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		24
		Modes		"1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
EndSection

```

you don't need to reboot to restart xserver just do Ctrl+Alt+Backspace.

if you want specify exactly the resolution you want in xorg.conf, paste the result (in bold) of this command in the monitor section, I give you an example of the command if you want 1280*960 80Hz resolution: 
```
gtf 1280 960 80
# 1280x960 @ 80.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 80.40 kHz; pclk: 140.22 MHz
**Modeline "1280x960_80.00"  140.22  1280 1376 1512 1744  960 961 964 1005  -HSync +Vsync**
```

I suggest you to have the VertRefresh parameter higher than "pclk: 140.22 MHz" and HorizSync higher than "hsync: 80.40 kHz" like that in this case : 
```
Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"hp 5500"
	Option		"DPMS"
	HorizSync	30-149
	VertRefresh	50-172
EndSection
```
