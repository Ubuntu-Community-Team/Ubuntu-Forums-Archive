### Post by zgoda on 2007-01-10
FVWM2 with decent theme/style may be very demanding for computing power, so don't rely on common notion of FVWM2 being *lightweight*. ;)
I'd consider trying XFCE first, then giving a try to [IceWM]("http://www.icewm.org/") if XFCE would not fit. I ran both of these desktop environments on even worse hardware (P200 w/64MB RAM) and, while not comfortable, both was usable.

### Post by jem7v on 2007-01-10
My computer is only 750 mhz with 384 megs ram and a 32mb Geforce2 MX, but I suppose that Is a bit faster than you're running.  Nevertheless, Plain Ol' Ubuntu with GNOME works fine for me.  For you, though, I'd probably stick with XFCE.  I've used Fluxbox (which may or may not be similar to FVWM?) in the past and it's pretty fast, but the amount of noodling around you have to do to make it work really nicely can be frustrating if you don't know what you're doing already.

I'd just stick with XFCE.  It's pretty good... lightweight-ish, but still easy to configure and use.  Start there, at least, and if you decide it's too sluggish you'll at least have a little more experience using Ubuntu Linux and it'll be easier to install something like Fluxbox or FVWM to replace (or work alongside) XFCE.
