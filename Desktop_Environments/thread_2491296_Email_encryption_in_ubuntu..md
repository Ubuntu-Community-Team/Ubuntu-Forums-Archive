### Post by TheFu on 2023-10-03
I though thunderbird added PGP and S/MIME encryption like 3 yrs ago, as part of the base. No addon needed.

[https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/openpgp-thunderbird-howto-and-faq](https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/openpgp-thunderbird-howto-and-faq) says:
Thunderbird 78 has built-in support for two encryption standards, OpenPGP and S/MIME. 

Enigmail isn't compatible with my current Thunderbird, v102.x.x.

For GPG, I'd use this: [https://wiki.gnupg.org/EMailClients/Thunderbird](https://wiki.gnupg.org/EMailClients/Thunderbird)
