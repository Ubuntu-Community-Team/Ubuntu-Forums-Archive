### Post by nelfer on 2009-06-08
Hi,
I'm having an issue with some PDFs that are tickets to events from the website Livenation.com
The way they show in Evince is with a watermark, like red spray paint on top of it. Then when printed, the 'watermark' becomes solid and the barcode is not visible at all.
This is happening in Ubuntu 9.04 64 Bits and also in Ubuntu 8.04 32 Bits.
I know the PDF is fine, because I've opened it with other PDF viewers, like xpdf, foxit reader and adobe and they all show the barcode properly.

Only evince shows this watermark on top of it.

Since evince is the default document viewer, it is a pain to switch just for these tickets to another viewer (as I like evince in general).

And this is not particular to just one PDF file. All PDF I've got from this website have the same barcode being covered by the red watermark. All other PDF viewers display it properly.

I'm attaching a screenshot of how the barcode looks on the screen.

Thanks for your help.

P.S. This only happens with tickets from Live Nation. Tickets from Ticket Master are Ok. I assume that it must be some 'security' feature?

P.S. 2: I found a way to convert the PDF so that the watermark is removed, which involves several steps: opening the PDF in xpdf, then 'printing' it to a PS file, then using ps2pdf to convert PS to PDF. Then the pdf is fine and I can see and print the barcodes from evince. This is however, a lot of work just to show/print the barcodes.
