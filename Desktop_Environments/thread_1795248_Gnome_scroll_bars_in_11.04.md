### Post by msknight on 2011-07-02
Hi Folks,

I really need help with 11.04 here. It is driving me spare and the tricks for 10.10 obviously don't work any more.

Due to a cut and paste bug with Kmail, I had to bite the bullet and upgrade from 10.10.

My personal opinion on the Unity interface is well documented, so I won't repeat my thoughts here. Needless to say I'm using the Gnome desktop mode.

However, the scroll bars have changed to be this sort of pop-out handle thing which is causing me a lot of heartache.

As I do design and coding stuff, I have a lot of fonts on board and in The Gimp, I'm having trouble scrolling my fonts ... well, I just can't do it because every time I try and grab the pop-out scroll tab, it disappears. That leaves me with the scroll wheel which is a real pain when you've got a lot of fonts to work through.

With my coding window, I do a lot of clicking on the end of a line, or the beginning of a line which is right next to a smaller side-window to the left of the coding window. With the coding window at full screen, that means that the scroll tab pops out from the right and stops me clicking on the code, or else it pops out from the left window. Net result is that I've got to click further in line and press HOME or END, or else tap the cursor a few times.

It is also making tabs, particularly the ones on the bottom of a screen, harder to hit. Whether it is fate or what, I don't know but particularly with BlueFish, where the files are opened as tabs on the bottom left of the editor window, whenever I go down to hit one, the horizontal scroll tab pops out over the top of it. I've got to over shoot the tab and come at it from below.

This might seem like small fry, but when I'm concentrating on a task, little things like this are driving me to absolute distraction. It has been such a pain in the neck that at one point I just broke down and cried because I couldn't do what I needed to do. Linux was ... shock ... horror ... getting in the way of my work flow.

I desperately need to get these scroll bars back to how they used to be. They are really screwing me up.

Yours in desperation ... with Windows 7 installation disk standing by, because I've really had enough of Unity and the changes that have come as a result.
