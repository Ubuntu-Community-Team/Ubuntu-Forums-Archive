### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-11-09
Hello, I second the suggestion of IceWM. 

While you will want to customize some, but it's easy to do with just a few simple text files, or post back for help. And find the IceBuntu theme for it. The thing is that unlike Fluxbox - which I use and enjoy btw - IceWM is more conventional in its feel and outlay, with a task bar, "start menu" etc. It is very light and fast, moreso than xfce, and quite stable. Add idesk for icons and you can make it a dead ringer for Windows hehe

All that said, my 933mhz PIII with 384mb ram (and 64mb video card) is quite happily running Kubuntu.
