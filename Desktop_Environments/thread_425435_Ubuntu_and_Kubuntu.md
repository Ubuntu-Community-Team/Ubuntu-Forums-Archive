### Post by ART_Adventures on 2007-04-27
Hello,

Having read around on the forum I came to believe, based on what I'd read, that there was essentially no difference between Ubuntu and Kubuntu, except the former used Gnome and the latter KDE. However, for the benefit of those considering which to choose, I would like to mention why I found that notion of difference misleading.

NOTE: Perhaps I'm wrong about a few things, but being a Linux novice, it's likely others will also find this. Perhaps somebody could tell me where I went wrong.

If I have a clean system and I install Kubuntu, I notice some important differences:

1. Trivial point: But Kubuntu has no Games.
2. Ubuntu detected my windows drives and any FAT and NTFS partitions, and automatically mounted them. Kubuntu didn't.
3. Ubuntu dectected my graphics hardware (3D accelerated NVidia) using the Restricted Driver Manager. Whereas, Kubuntu didn't, and I could not find a corresponding feature in Kubuntu.
4. Kubuntu doesn't seem to feature an email program, like Evolution.
5. Kubuntu didn't offer to install codecs for me when I attempted to play specific kinds of media, whereas Ubuntu does.
