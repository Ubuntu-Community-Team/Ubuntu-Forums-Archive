### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-03-20
Why is *top* installed, but not *htop*?  There are literally hundreds of monitor programs in Linux.  Why just pick on two?

```
apt-cache search monitor
```

If you are not happy with either one of them, you can delete them.  It's your system.  Why do you have both of them installed?  How come you don't have *htop* installed?

They are written with different toolkits, but both measure the same thing.
