### Post by twigsby on 2005-08-02
You'll be able to send pictures and music to the phone, but might have trouble sending them from phone to computer, or at least It never worked for me.

Setting up Bluetooth in Ubuntu isn't exactly a painless process, but there should be some useful guides in the forums.

I'd personally look into getting the D500 Data cable (much faster transfers).

### Post by gingermark on 2005-08-02
I don't know much about using Bluetooth with Ubuntu, but assuming there is a compatible port, it might be a better option than a cable if you expect one day to get another phone.

Your bluetooth port should be compatible with any future bluetooth compatible phones you buy, whereas a cable would not be. It is true that the speeds are slower, but if money is more of a concern than speed Bluetooth is the way to go.
