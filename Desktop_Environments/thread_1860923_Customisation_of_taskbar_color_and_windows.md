### Post by mcduck on 2011-10-15
The old themes and methods won't work, since Ubuntu 11.10 uses Gnome 3 and GTK3, while the old themes are for GTK2.

Installing gnome-tweak-tool will give you options for fonts, icons, mouse cursor etc.

...and transparencies work just like before, either adjusted from the program in question or through compizconfig settings manager, depending on what type of transparency you are talking about and if it's a feature of the program itself or something done by the window manager.

I'm not sure what you mean with taskbar, since there is none, but the top panel looks are simply determined by the theme you are using, an the launcher panel has no options apart from the level of transparency (controlled in compizconfig settings manager, under the Unity plugin)
