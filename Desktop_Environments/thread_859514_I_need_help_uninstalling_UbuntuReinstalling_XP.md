### Post by Nivuahc on 2008-07-15
Personally, I think you're making a mistake by going back to Windows but I suppose I'm biased. I understand your frustrations with your video card but, if I may suggest it, try asking around your friends if anyone has a spare video card lying around. You would be surprised at how many "yes" answers you get (especially if you have friends who work in IT). I have, I believe, 5 or 6 Nvidia 128MB+ cards in my basement, for example.

Regarding your inability to boot from the XP CD I have had it happen to me on an occasion or two that the CD drive, itself, is what was giving me the problem. In almost all cases (except the most extreme) I have been able to force a boot from CD using the floppy bootdisk images from Slackware (provided you have a floppy drive).

[http://ftp.lanet.lv/ftp/mirror/Slackware/bootdisks/](http://ftp.lanet.lv/ftp/mirror/Slackware/bootdisks/)

I usually use **bare.i** (instructions for using the images are on the linked page) for just about everything and it's bypassed whatever issue the particular drive in question was having on more than one occasion. It doesn't work 100% of the time but it's saved me more than once, for sure.
