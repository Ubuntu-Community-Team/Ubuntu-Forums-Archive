### Post by Bartender on 2006-01-05
Just checking back in.  The two bookstores I ordered from via the B&N website were "College Book Service" and "BargainBookStores".  Both books ("Running Linux" & "Linux in a Nutshell") looked to be unused.  Both were 4th edition, not some older version.  I'd give a thumbs up for either store.  
"Nutshell" says right up front - "..this book is geared toward users who know what they want to do and have some idea how to do it, but can't always remember the correct command or option."  Over half of this 920+ page book is dedicated to a bewildering collection of Linux commands.  Wow.  It'll take some learnin' to make use of it.
At first blush, I'd guess "Running Linux" (670+ pages) might be more useful to someone such as myself who needs basic instructions at every step.  The authors modestly claim the book to be "...the classic text on installing, maintaining, and learning to use a Linux system."  I hope they're right because I sure need some help!
