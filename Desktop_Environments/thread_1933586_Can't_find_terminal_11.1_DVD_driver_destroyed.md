### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-02-29
I think that machine is not powerful enough to do justice to Ubuntu 11.10 with the unity DE, nor probably any of the other DEs available in the standard Ubuntu, but Xubuntu or even Lubuntu should be good with that hardware.

Alternatively you could just add another DE to your current Ubuntu 11.10 with ```
sudo apt-get install xubuntu-desktop
``` or lubuntu-desktop which is even leaner, and then choose either xfce (xubuntu) or lxde (Lubuntu) at the login screen.

If you want to reinstall and are able to boot to the xubuntu 10.04 CD/DVD that you burned, it should be very easy to get those files that you want to save, and copy them to a flash drive.  You will not be able to run an OS and burn files to a CD-R unless you have two CD/DVD drives on your machine, which I think is what you are saying you have.

Can you read CDs or DVDs from one or both drives, or are they both totally dead?
