### Post by 505 on 2007-10-23
You could install the package 'compizconfig-settings-manager' In the Appearance/Visual effects this give you an extra option Custom. Here you can select or deselect many effects, and customise the behaviour of all the effects. It also includes descriptions of the effects.

### Post by Flyingjester on 2007-10-23
Do a search for compiz in add/remove programs, click on compizconfig-settings-manager, that is under you applications menu, then it will pop up in system > preferences > advanced desktop effects settings
