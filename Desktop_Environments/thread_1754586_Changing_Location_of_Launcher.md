### Post by coffeecat on 2011-05-10
> **beercoder said:**
> I guess its simply missed out

I don't believe it was simply missed out. Unity had to be developed from almost nowhere to where it is now in less than 6 months, and what they have achieved in the time is remarkable. (Unity in Maverick doesn't really count except for some basic design concepts because it was based on mutter and Unity in Natty is based on compiz.) I think sikander3786 is right to hope that the ability to move the launcher will come in 11.10. If we see as much added to Unity in 11.10 as was developed in 11.04, we'll see an extraordinarily good user interface.

However, while the launcher remains unmovable, may I offer a thought - something that only occurred to me recently. If you speak a language whose written version goes from left to right, it makes a lot of sense to have the launcher, window buttons and global menus on the left. Which they are. The application menus read from left to right so they need to be towards the left of the panel. With the menus at the left, it's logical to have the launcher and buttons there to minimise mouse movements.

Now that I've got used to it, I prefer it this way and wouldn't move the launcher even if I could. Indeed, I prefer it so much that I tried moving the dock on my Mac Mini desktop to the left. Looked odd, but made sense in the same way because MacOS buttons are on the left as are the panel menus.

Of course, none of this applies if you speak a language written from right to left. I would be interested to hear what a speaker of, say Arabic, makes of the Unity desktop as it is now.
