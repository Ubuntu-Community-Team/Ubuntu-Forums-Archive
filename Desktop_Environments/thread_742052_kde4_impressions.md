### Post by eldragon on 2008-04-01
since kde4 is in the hardy repos, i decided to give it a go, and see what the fuss was all about. 

first i'll start with my backgrounds so that you can draw more accurate conclusions from my post,
ive always used windows, always one version behind the latest release (win98 when 2k was out, win2k when xp was out, winxp since sp2, never used vista myself more than to testrun it).
my move to ubuntu was fast and simple, i tried xfce, gnome and kde3 to see what fitted me best.
i never liked kde's unpolished result, always looked to me like win95 looks. blocky, and old fashioned. thats why i ended up using gnome and xfce on old machines.

now my take on kde4.
i feel nothing changed, even desktop effects enabled, everything seems blocky, unpolished and ammateurish. first thing they should have done, is replace the default fonts, which look like a word document. the oxygen decorator (or plasma or whichever is the decorator) looks far better than kde3's, and the widgets look great (quite useless, but thats not the point).
fading windows is primitive, transparencies dont have crossfade transitions, much of the effects seem to have been coded overnight and slapped into the the release.
i know its highly customizable, and thats great, but i feel the default theme should be something apealing, not something that needs tweaking. its great to have the option, but to be forced to tweak it to your liking?

they are definately far behind compiz. and will take monthes (if not years) for kde to have something appealing....


what do you think?

If you're rejecting KDE on the basis of trying Kubuntu, then perhaps you're not giving it a fair go.  AFAIK, Canonical don't have many people working on Kubuntu, and you'd get a better idea of KDE's capabilities by trying a distribution that puts more resources into its KDE desktop, such as OpenSuSe.

Having said that, I don't care for KDE myself.  I've used Kubuntu in the past and KDE on top of FreeBSD before, but I find GNOME more amenable.  (And although I mentioned OpenSuSe, I got rid of that within minutes, because I couldn't stand their menuing system -- click, click, click to get to anything.  But, AFAIK, that's down to Novell not KDE, and anyway I guess one could give up and launch from the Terminal instead.)

To me the KDE GUI looks cluttered, and there always seem to me to be too many possible locations where anything could be.  I use Mac OS X a lot, so I'm used to clean, well-organized desktop design, but, unfortunately, KDE is the reverse of that.

I think the default look for KDE4 is OK, but nothing special.  It also has rather low contrast.

But KDE is a powerful desktop with some very good applications.  I do prefer GNOME myself, but I wouldn't write it off.  They've made KDE4 highly portable, too.  They obviously want to be able to be running everywhere they possibly could be.  Whether that will happen in practice ...

### Post by randomstuff on 2008-04-01
Alright, since we're all about to get flamed in the holy desktop war anyway, I'd like to say that my impressions of KDE (have not tried 4.0) are the same as the thread starter had.

In german there is a term called "clickibunti" which AFAIK is mainly used about websites created by Internet/HTML beginners. You know the sort of websites (myspace comes to mind) that are filled with blinking text, ugly javascript menus, frames, unnecessary flash apps, ugly backgrounds and so on. Somebody just threw a lot of "cool" stuff together and now they think they have the most awesome homepage ever (hey, I did the same back in the days).

My impression about KDE is just like that. Nothing fits together, the configuration and administration applications are even worse and senseless than in gnome, everything looks ugly in some way, even if you're using some pretty slick widget theme.

The gnome default at least isn't ugly per se. True, It's nothing revolutionary, but it does not get in the way either and is rather easily changed to my liking. I spent a few hours trying to get KDE to look managable, but I failed completely. I have heard so much about how configurable KDE is, but for me there are way less obstacles getting GNOME to work as I want.

Supposedly GNOME users are more simplistic, but so far nobody has come up with an explanation what (useful) changes you can make to KDE that I cannot do in GNOME. If anyone has any suggestions on that I'd be happy to hear them. If they're good you might even get me to switch (if you care about that).
