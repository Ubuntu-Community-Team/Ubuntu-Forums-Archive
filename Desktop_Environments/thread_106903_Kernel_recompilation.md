### Post by matthew on 2005-12-21
The answer is "it depends." Really. Are you a power user doing math-intensive stuff? Lots of graphics processing? Serious gamer? It might be worth it. For most people the difference is negligible, plus you can download architecture-specific kernels via synaptic, so unless you want to learn how or have a compulsion to get rid of unneeded drivers in your kernel, I would say it is not necessary...it's fun, though. :)
