### Post by PacSci on 2009-04-12
I've got nothing. However, you can compress everything down to one, Start-menu-like menu. Right click the panel, hit "Add To Panel", and select "Main Menu." It'll have all the Applications categories, a Places menu, and the System menu items under a single Ubuntu logo. This might not be what you're looking for, though.

If someone is interested:

Despite being a lighter, and in principle less-featured desktop than gnome, the xfce desktop allows to customize your menu very nicely. 

The particular thing I was trying hard to do in gnome (to eliminate the words in the top panel menus), is simple and straightforward in xfce. You only have to right-click on the menu and navigate to the proper option.
