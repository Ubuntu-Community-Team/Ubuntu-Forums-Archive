All I was looking for was a comments section.  True to Unix form this was not intuitive.

I have used Ubuntu for a few years, not bad - not perfect.

Now I switched to Unity, the bosses seemed to think that was best.  Not all bad, but I will list my observations:

The notification area is the biggest problem.  Skype appears  there intermittently.  The alarm/timer app is the worst (more later).

First, I see no need to move the window controls from the right side to the left top.  Perhaps it makes a few Mac users happy - not me.  Motivation?

I have gotten accustomed to the disappearing app bar on the left.  Yes, I could size my app windows to show that bar all the time, but that wastes more screen space than I would like.  Why I can't just see what I have open in a small bar on the bottom I can't imagine.

It doesn't help that the upgrade to 11.04 coincides with the upgrade to Firefox 4 - but that is not all your fault, only partly.

What prompts me to find an address/form to send this email is, the alarm/timer function FAILS.

I start a timer for 20 minutes, then kill it when the time gets close.  The timer app shows no active timers.  Yet, the timer goes into alarm mode (an annoying sound).  After several efforts I reboot the machine.  Perhaps I should have gone to the system monitor to kill the process.  Too late, I am way to pissed at that point.  I am so aggravated I will track down some place (WAY too many screens) to provide feedback.

Obviously, I don't want a response.  I just want to help Ubuntu - as challenging as that may be.  Unity doesn't suck, but it is far from perfect.  App integration is everything.
