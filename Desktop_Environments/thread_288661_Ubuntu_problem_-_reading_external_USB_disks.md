### Post by Robert Leithe on 2006-10-30
I switched from XP to Ubuntu 6.06 some time ago, and ran it for about two months before I found I had to switch back.

There were minor snags along the way, but no one snag alone is enough to "drive me back to XP". All in all however, and especially including the "last drop" made me realize then that I had to go back :(

Here's the "last drop":
As time passed, I frequently received read-errors on my external USB-drives (Maxtor OneTouch II/300 GB/USB 2.0, and one Seagate 250 GB disk I mounted in a case myself). The error was frequent when I copied large amount of files (folder structures) at once. I found myself having to continue the operation manually from where it failed.
I also ended up with some files being unreadable.

After I switched to Ubuntu, I reformatted my external drives to ext3.

When it came to the problem with read errors, I found I didn't want to risk any more data than what was already unreadable. I bough another external drive (Western Digital/500 GB/USB 2.0), formatted it to fat32, copied all the data I could and (saddened) reinstalled WinXP.
Reformatted the two ext3 drives to NTFS and copied all my data back. No problems with the disks have occured since.

And now the question(s):
Does Ubuntu have problems reading external drives? USB 2.0? Driver incompatibilities, either with the USB or the drives itself? Anyone heard about this? Or external drives and ext3? I know I'm reaching here, but I would like to switch back to Linux - but not if I'm at a risk of not being able to access my data.

Help anyone?
