### Post by Roasted on 2012-04-01
Lately as I've been tinkering with 12.04, I've really grown to like Unity. It's begun to drag me away from my beloved Gnome Shell as Unity has begun to address several shortcomings Gnome Shell has as well. (as a result, each time I refer to Unity in this thread, it's 12.04's Unity variant I'm speaking of)

The curve ball is, I have a decently powered desktop (i3, 8GB RAM, SSD) whereas my netbooks are Atom 1.6GHz Dual Core/average SSD/2GB RAM and E240 1.5GHz AMD APU/regular SATA HDD/2GB RAM.

The E240 can slug a little bit, but it's also a single core with no HyperThreading. Unity 2D is somewhat decent but sometimes leaves me wanting a little more speed, however I also have to acknowledge at times I am running a few apps at once. XFCE and LXDE, however, are a bit quicker.

The Atom netbook is kind of in the same boat, but it's still quite a bit faster than the E240. Menus load a bit quicker when multitasking and while I get no lag using Unity 2D, I can see my processor spiking as high as 90% for very short spurts. It often hovers around 20-40% in general usage if I'm just flipping around in some menus. LXDE on the other hand spiked at an all time high of 19%, but hovers at 10% or less.

I like using the same interface across all of my systems when possible, just because I get "set in my ways" and I like things to be similar. The speed differences between the units on Unity 2D really isn't that much, so at times I have to question if it's me being pushy and impatient (which is almost always the case when I get on an electronic device) or if I really could benefit from a slimmer environment. These questions kind of sparked "wonder what everyone else is doing?"

So, that being said, what are you guys doing for your netbooks? Do you folks match the desktop environment as best you can do the unit itself, or do you find Unity 2D in your cases to be more than slim enough?
