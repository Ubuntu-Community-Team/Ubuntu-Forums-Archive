### Post by phersotty on 2006-10-25
Xubuntu is lean with no frills as in "eye candy", it will work great for your purpose.  > I only need a computer for web browsing, e-mail, instant messaging and office documents, but I want everything to go pretty fast, if possible. 

Also you can use both on the same installation.  If you install Ubuntu first and it feels sluggish then open up a terminal window and type: 

apt-get install xubuntu-desktop

When its done log out and choose an xubuntu session from the log in screen.

### Post by Lin-X on 2006-10-25
I have a 12 year old 900mhz machine with only 30 g of disk space and 256mg of ram. I have installed all three desktops, Gnome, KDE, and Xcfe, and have no trouble with slowness. Ubuntu has always been much faster than my creaky old Win98. When you partition your hard drive for Ubuntu, or whatever you decide to try, make a parttion twice the size of your ram to use as a swap drive; this will help Ubuntu run even faster --- and don't worry about the speed of your cpu or getting more ram first, you will have no trouble, believe me! My installation, with all three desktops, still only takes about 5 g of space.

If you are interested in trying a set-up with all three desktops, the September issue of LinuxFormat magazine had a free dvd with a Ubuntu distro that includes the three and some nice extra software too. This would cost you about $15 but it's worth it, I think. No need to order it online, just go to a Barnes & Noble or similar bookstore and tell them what you are after. I'm pretty sure they can get it for you.

And, by the way, please don't forget to come back here and let us know how things went and how you like your new operating system. Good luck!:-D
