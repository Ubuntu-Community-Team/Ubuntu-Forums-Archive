### Post by jpeddicord on 2008-07-12
To turn off multiple desktops, right-click on the two boxes on the bottom-right of your screen (see attachment, mine has 3), then go to Preferences. Set Number of Workspaces to 1, close the window, and you now only have one workspace.

### Post by SmokinJuan on 2008-07-13
Jacob is right about the desktops (though its not "workspaces" on my machine, but rather columns).

As for the scrolling, most laptops I've used (all Microsoft) have a synaptics touchpad whose driver allows you to set a region on the right side and bottom of the pad to do "virtual scrolling" in applications.  Look for something in your System/Preferences or System/Administration menu for your touchpad to see if theres a scrolling setting.

### Post by coffeecat on 2008-07-13
SmokinJuan is right. You've strayed into the vertical scrolling area of the touchpad. This is not a Linux issue. Exactly the same happens in Windows. Go to System > Preferences > Mouse > Touchpad and untick Enable vertical scrolling.

Personally, I prefer scrolling on. I've just learnt to be careful where I put my fingers.
