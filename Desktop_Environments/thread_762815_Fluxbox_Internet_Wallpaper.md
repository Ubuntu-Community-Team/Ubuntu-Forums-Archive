### Post by markjensen on 2008-04-22
Normally, a wallpaper setter (scaling/cropping/etc) app such as "feh" is called on to set the wallpaper to the "root" window (background).  You can use a simple switch on mplayer to direct it to use the root window (background) with "-rootwin" as an option.

But if I understand you coorectly, you want an html background, like a maximized epiphany or firefox display.   Can't say I have ever heard of this being done, not to say that it _cannot_ be done...
