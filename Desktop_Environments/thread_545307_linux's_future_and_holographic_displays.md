### Post by TruthSayer3 on 2007-09-07
As I was watching a video @ [http://www.ubuntuvideo.com/ubuntu_7_04_on_dual_19_lcds](http://www.ubuntuvideo.com/ubuntu_7_04_on_dual_19_lcds) I thought of the computer platforms future. More importantly with Compiz fusion and holographic displays coming more in to focus I thought what it would be like for linux in the future. Imagine a computer lab or a table with a holographic display in the center. Then, as with compiz, you have the cube floating in mid air; 1 side for you (you can switch to different viewpoints) or multiple sides expanding into a circle for each person in the room. Now with the technologies of voice control or with simple hand gestures through the interface (like in the minority report) this would also eliminate the need for mice or keyboards. Also don't forget about those devices you use to control it with your mind. Though I must say...they need to get small enough to fit just around your ear like a bluetooth headset for cell phones.

Now of course our holographic displays can't accommodate to those specifications right now but in the near future this should be very possible. However would it not be a marvelous idea to be able to work on that type of platform especially in schools or conference rooms or even libraries?:smile:




The price of the future lessons everyday with accomplishments we make now...
