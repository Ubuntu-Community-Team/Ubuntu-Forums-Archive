### Post by matthew on 2005-10-16
You don't have to use the k7 kernel, but if you do your system will be better optimized for your Duron chip and will run a little bit faster.

I recommend you install it via synaptic. 

[COLOR=Red]***DO NOT ***[/COLOR]remove your current, working kernel until you have rebooted and confirmed the new kernel has installed properly and works. I would even wait for a day or two first or just leave the original kernel intact as a backup. While that may not be necessary, until you get past feeling noobish it's probably a good idea.

There is no downside to switching.
