I recently installed Ubuntu 9.10, and I have encountered a strange issue. When typing certain combinations of characters anywhere (e.g. eee, fff, 111, 222, etc...), a small box with useless special characters appears, and I have to chose from one of the special characters to be able to exit the box. This makes it impossible to write words containing e, f and numbers, since the special character box then always appears.

### Post by mandreas on 2009-11-03
I solved it. Just in case anyone encounters the same problem:

Special characters/symbols are activated with pressing Ctrl+Shift, and deactivated the same way. Quite unnecessary feature for most I think...
