### Post by el toro on 2005-01-21
Regarding the difference between GTK and Metacity themes:
GTK is a toolkit for drawing widgets, like buttons, scrollbars, etc. etc.
Metacity is a window manager, so it controls the way that windows behave, look, etc. 
So, installing a Metacity theme will change the way your titlebars look (I believe it's called "window border" or something like that in the gnome theme prefs chooser) while installing a GTK theme will change the way widgets and other controls are drawn.
There are "metathemes" around that actually contain both GTK, Metacity and even sometimes icon themes.
Hope this is helpful to you.
