I would agree, but for a different reason... If you're studying for LPIC - try several distros, especially Gentoo and any other roll your own varieties that force you to get your hands dirty. Ubuntu and all its offspring are great distros, but not neccesarily best suited for learning Linux from a LPI certification point of view. I would take a look at VMware Server too, it's free and allows you to run several OSes on one machine - great for study purposes and light on the pocketbook. With virtualization you can easily move an OS between physical machines too... In case you find your lower end machine doesn't cut it for some of the things you need to do.
