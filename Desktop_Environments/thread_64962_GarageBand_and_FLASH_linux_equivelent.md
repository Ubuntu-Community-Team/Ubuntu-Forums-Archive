### Post by myfunny on 2006-11-13
for music editing, this is the software i like the most:

- lmms: very user friendly, it's a sequencer/recorder/allyouwantittobe. it has got lots of synths, and you can write the notes you wish to be played or use your pc keyboard as it was a music one.

- audacity: a hi-fi sequencer/recorder. i usually use it to edit the instrumental parts i record with lmms, and to apply effects (a looooot of them are included in the software package). it gives you the total control on the music you're making.

i didn't ever hear about wired, but i'm going to download it. on its website ([HTML]wired.epitech.com[/HTML] there's the ubuntu package.


about flash: if you *really* find it *that* useful, you may try to use flash4linux ([HTML]f4.sourceforge.net[/HTML]) or take a look on [HTML]osflash.org[/HTML]. uh: if you would like to make just things as animations and such, with flash, you'll also be interested in [HTML]www.flameproject.org[/HTML]: it has a very simple GUI, surely more user friendly than the f4l one.
