### Post by neul on 2005-06-25
[font=Arial]This should go in the "kudos" directory.  

I am not easily pleased.  After experimenting with a bullshit version of Redhat, Psyche I think, I was sick of the download difficulties, the update mystery, the shitty fonts and generally primitive feel of Linux.  

Ubuntu/Kubuntu 5.04 is different.  This is smart programming that works reliably and is completely upgradable.
The install disc found and allocated the partition I had made, loading there and leaving my Win XP Pro intact.  Amazingly there was only one glitch during the install(which took about 90 min), and it was easily tweaked.

Complaint:  in order to get the complete repositories of new files, I had to re-write the "sources.list" file at root, using text from the Unofficial Ubuntu User Guide, and update using line commands.  But it worked!  I have countless apps, docs, etc available for instant download.  

I had wanted KDE rather than Gnome, so the first thing I did was look for KDE archives in the repositories.  All the KDE folders were there.  I just chose everything that said KDE and DL'd it.  100% success on reboot-- I am now running Kubuntu.  357 archives from all over the place, and the Synaptic manager app did it perfectly.

I also installed a firewall and antivirus-- no problems so far.  

I am very pleased with the Hoary Hedgehog distro; it works, and the KDE GUI is a gem.
