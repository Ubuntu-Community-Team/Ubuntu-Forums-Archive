### Post by michaelramm on 2007-10-30
Hello everyone,

Today is a new day for the Alabama LoCo. We are starting an all out blitz to do a couple of things.

I have signed on to help Jason get the AlabamaTeam organized and spreading the word about Ubuntu from the mountains of Huntsville to the Mobile Bay and all points in between.

I am trying to get everyone associated with the Alabama LoCo to get on this list. We have 40 members on out LaunchPad page and only 14 on the mailing list. I have mailed a good number of them (all but 9 of them) asking that they join this mailing list.

Some of this will be redundant to some of you as I have taken it from my other email to folks:

I have begun redesigning our Wiki page at [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AlabamaTeam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AlabamaTeam) and we have started discussion on some Goals for the remainder of 2007 and for 2008, including a possible three state LoCo Conference with Alabama, Georgia and Florida LoCos. Another goal that we have set for next year is to become an Approved LoCo. Currently there are only a handful of Approved LoCos in the United States.
There are a lot of benefits to being Approved, one of which is better support from Canonical with materials and CDs and such.

We would like to see the Alabama LoCo start to grow and spread the word about Ubuntu. And that is going to start with you guys!

We are also going to start meeting in the IRC channel [#ubuntu-alabama on irc.freenode.net] every Thursday night at 8pm CST to discuss events that we want to be a part of and how we can work together to spread the news. Please visit [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AlabamaTeam/Meetings](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AlabamaTeam/Meetings) to see what we will be talking about, and add any items that you would like to see discussed.

Be watching here and on the mailing list, ways that you can get involved to spread the love about Ubuntu.

If you have any questions, please email me [michael@1manit.net] or Jason [crane.aswp@gmail.com] or post it to the list.

Let work together to get Alabama as the next Approved LoCo team in the US.

Michael
