### Post by itnet7 on 2010-06-05
> **RealG187 said:**
> Okay so what's the point of that? Can't they just goto Ubuntuforums.org?

Ubuntu forums offer teams the ability to have their own subdomain for ubuntuforums.org. It provides the LoCo with a friendly URL to give out to people they meet at conferences and installfests. It's much easier for you to remember and give out this:

**[http://florida.ubuntuforums.org](http://florida.ubuntuforums.org)**

instead of giving out: 

**[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=225](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=225)**

This allows for the team to have a central forum, that is cost-free, and always (99.999) available. Believe it or not, many people from the state, as they begin clicking on both getting involved links, and getting help links somehow wind up to their states LoCo forums. If the Idaho team doesn't have their own slice within Ubuntu Forums, then they could potentially be losing out on some potential members/contributors.

Just my $0.02
