### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-11-22
Are you positive you are using 14.04??? I wouldn't go near it for any business use. It is totally a testing release at the moment and not released for nearly six months. Nowhere near ready, especially not for use in a business.

You can't 'force' a Windows program onto Ubuntu UNLESS you use Wine. That is what Wine is all about; getting Win programs to work on Linux ...

If you are new to Ubuntu, I think you should perhaps do a bit of research as to what it can and can't do, what it is and isn't. Ubuntu/Win are fundamentally different. ;)

If you are running production machines (i.e. for business) or servers you should be running the current, stable 12.04 LTS, supported until April 2017. Upgrading to 14.04 LTS next April will be a one click process and that will be supported until April 2017. I repeat, you should not be going anywhere near 14.04 LTS unless you are experienced user and intending to use it for testing and bug catching to help devs and definitely NOT for any real world business applications where there is going to be a problem if it breaks, crashes and burns.

All posts regarding it should be posted in the ***Ubuntu +1*** section of the forums only which is where this thread will be moved to if you are intending to stay with 14.04.
