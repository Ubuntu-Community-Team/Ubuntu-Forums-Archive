### Post by jbicha on 2017-09-25
I encourage you to contact Canonical about trademark concerns if you are creating a product. The contact info or a link is present on the [trademark page]("https://www.ubuntu.com/legal/terms-and-policies/intellectual-property-policy") linked earlier in this thread.

I don't speak for Canonical and my statement here is completely unofficial. I think there was probably little need to rename Unity to say Yunit. Keeping Unity working is much more important than the name. And Canonical may even be happy to let a continuation project keep using the Unity name.

It's possible you'll have more trouble with having "Ubuntu" in the name of your project than "Unity". Resolving the trademark and naming issues is one of many reasons Ubuntu GNOME and Ubuntu Budgie applied to become official Ubuntu flavors.
