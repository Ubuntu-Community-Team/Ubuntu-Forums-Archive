### Post by sudodus on 2013-07-31
Lubuntu Saucy alpha 2 32-bit works for me. I have tested it from the desktop iso as well as the alternate iso.

I have also made a special version to flash directly to a 4 GB USB pendrive. The fake-PAE makes no difference for most computers, but it makes it possible to update/upgrade with Pentium M and Celeron M CPUs.

This installed system is portable (works with most computers, provided a built in graphics driver can manage the graphics chip).
