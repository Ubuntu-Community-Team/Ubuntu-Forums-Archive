### Post by AlpineCarver on 2016-03-11
I've been experimenting with a daily build of Ubuntu 16.04 desktop on a tablet. The system appears to be handling the touch screen hardware properly - gedit works very well using 1-finger gestures for scroll, select, and right-click.

I'm having lots of problems with individual programs' handling of the touch screen, but the one that is affecting me the most right now is thunderbird. There is no reasonable way to scroll through a mailbox with many messages or to scroll through a single large message. In these cases, moving the scroll bar controls the tiniest bit jumps too far. A solution like the firefox "grab and drag" extension would be great, but it would have to be aware of which of thunderbird's panes it was invoked from and scroll only that particular pane.

If anyone has a solution for this problem, or, if there is an alternative email client that works better on a touch screen, please let me know.

Thanks.

### Post by AlpineCarver on 2016-03-12
A partial answer to my problem - I tried evolution today, and it works better than thunderbird. Scrolling works very well. The operation I perform most (deleting a message) is less than perfect - clicking an icon in the tool bar, which is very small on my tablet, even with "settings / displays / scale for menus and title bars" set very large. Firefox's large "delete" button works much better, but evolution is more usable overall.

Evolution also gives me a lot of "TLS connection closed" error messages, but they don't appear to be doing any permanent damage.
