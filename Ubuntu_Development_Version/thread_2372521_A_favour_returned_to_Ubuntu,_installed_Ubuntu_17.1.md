Gnome and Unity are 2 different DEs and won't work well together. 
Apps too responds differently. Nautilus, one of Gnome main apps moves jerkily across the screen in its own Gnome, while it swims smoothly in Unity. 
Compiz is a compositing window manager. 

What do we get by testing Gnome+Unity? Its always better not to have 2 DEs together in one install. You still have to log out to get to the other DE, so its better to have it in a separate partition. Even having Gnome on Wayland and Gnome on Xorg in one install is a problem.
