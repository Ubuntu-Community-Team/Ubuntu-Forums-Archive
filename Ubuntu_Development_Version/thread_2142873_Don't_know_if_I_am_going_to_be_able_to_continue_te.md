### Post by arpanaut on 2013-05-07
To help lessen the amount you need to download during testing of the iso's you could use zsync that will only download the changed bits of the iso.  If you keep syncing the image on a regular basis you only need to get 20-30% (or less) of the total iso at a time.
That may save you some bandwidth.  Or don't do as much testing of iso's and installations, just keep up with the updates on an installed system.  Just some suggestions...  You know this isn't required participation, do your testing at a level that you are comfortable with and your budgeted bandwidth allows.
 [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ZsyncCdImage](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ZsyncCdImage)
