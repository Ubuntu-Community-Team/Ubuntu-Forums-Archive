### Post by TheFu on 2024-01-13
I can see internal apps, never seen anywhere on the internet having 10-12 yrs of support with ZERO real changes.  Some apps are tightly coupled to the hardware, so that wouldn't change either. This happens more and more with virtual machines.

Internet-facing systems are completely different.  Even 5 yrs is pushing a reasonable support period as the world moves forward, but there are lots of non-technology companies who want to avoid any major changes for as long as possible.  There's a "hassle level" graph for where the time running any server vs the "hassle level" of performing an upgrade becomes too great.  I suspect each application has a different graph, but think somewhere after 4 yrs, but less than 5 yrs is the optimal period.  Systems that get nothing but patches, no upgrades for too long lose expertise in performing the migrations.  This happens whether support is you or we are paying someone else.  

I'll keep to my 4 yr cycle. It has been working well, though I wish I could avoid the hassle completely.  Then the real-world beats me about the head for a few hours and I come to my senses. ;)

Longer support periods really aren't the gift they appear to be. They are an albatross around the neck almost always.
