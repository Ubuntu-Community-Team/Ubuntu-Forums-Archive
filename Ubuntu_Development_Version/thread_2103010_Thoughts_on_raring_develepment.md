### Post by kyleabaker on 2013-01-09
One thing I think you have to accept is the fact that Ubuntu really is becoming a popular alternative. That comes with good and bad. The good is that it becomes stable and there's a lot of support for most applications, tools and even companies like Valve and Spotify. The bad is that, the more stable ANY software becomes... the less likely they are to make major changes that *might* upset a large portion of their user base.

Mark Shuttlework has never been shy about introducing major changes, but if you look at the history of many proposed changes you'll find that most are abandoned or delayed indefinitely. Windicators were one feature that Mark has apparently abandoned... and possibly for the best, thought I was somewhat enthusiastic about the concept.

I think you'll find that the heavy features come in the release immediately after as LTS (the last one being 12.04). This is because the platform has more time to mature. With only one more release coming before the next LTS Canonical is likely focusing on making the current state of Ubuntu as reliable and stable as possible. I'd suggest that you wait for 14.04 if you're looking for MAJOR changes. I gladly chanllenge anyone to argue with this logic (hopefully I'm wrong), but as a software developer myself I think the most appropriate approach is to mature Ubuntu until we reach the next LTS and only then begin to morph into a *slightly* different feel. (Once again only my opinion)

You'll find several major changes from release to release but the closer they get to LTS the fewer you'll see. Hope that helps.
