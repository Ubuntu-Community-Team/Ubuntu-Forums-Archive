### Post by jbicha on 2017-09-29
Click the system status menu at the right hand side of the top bar.

At the bottom of the menu, you should see 4 buttons. Click the 2nd button from the left to lock the screen auto-rotation.

Open the Settings app. Open the Displays panel (it's in Devices). There should be an Orientation section to allow you to change the screen orientation. On my computer, I don't see the Orientation section so hopefully you have better luck than me!

### Post by tfbielawski on 2017-10-05
If your screen is going sideways, it may b e that you have rotation sensors in your laptop. In order for screen rotation to work by itself, IIO-sensor-proxy needs to be installed. Im sure its installed by default in 17.10. So, IF you can get to a terminal sudo apt-get remove iio-sensor-proxy should kill your rotation sensor functionality. Hope that helps.
