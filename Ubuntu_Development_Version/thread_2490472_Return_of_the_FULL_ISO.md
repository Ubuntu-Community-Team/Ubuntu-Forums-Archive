### Post by guiverc on 2023-09-05
Myself, I don't really care what get installed by default... I'll just add what I need anyway.

The *full desktop* ISO makes sense to me, especially for beginners or those that are going to keep their systems.

I've always felt the *minimal* options are wanted by *distro hoppers* (*less to download*), or the experienced users who have a specific use of their system thus will add what they need anyway (*and I've always seen removing it as equally easy if its a full system*).

For those with limited internet, both (full/minimal) have *pros* and *cons*, but my views are shaped by my own world view, and I won't be aware of what other users have to deal with, thus I don't know.
