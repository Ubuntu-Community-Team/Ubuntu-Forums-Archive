### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-02-11
I saw that, too.  To be honest, I think it's a bit of a misnomer calling Rawhide a distribution. It's essentially daily Git builds. Zero promises about stability.

At a point in the development process, a "branch" of Rawhide is "branched" off Rawhide and that becomes the next development release, going through the standard pre-alpha/alpha/beta/rc cycle. If I was looking for a "development" release to use with even any expectation of stability, I'd use "branched" when it became available.  The number assigned to the current Rawhide is often *2* releases ahead of the current release.

So, Rawhide is pretty volatile, in the "gets hundreds of updates and may or may not boot on any given day" sense.
