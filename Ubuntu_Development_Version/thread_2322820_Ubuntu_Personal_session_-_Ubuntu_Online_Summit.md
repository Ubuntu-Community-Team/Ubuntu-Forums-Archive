### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-05-02
From what you and I have seen of Ubuntu Personal I think that Canonical has the makings of a very secure OS that can be updated without an update breaking the OS. But, and it is a big but, Ubuntu Personal will be of no use to anyone if none or few of the traditional applications are converted into snap packages.

Ubuntu Personal is the kind of disruptive technology that Mark Shuttleworth will love and everyone outside of the Ubuntu community will hate. And I suspect that not a few inside the Ubuntu community will also hate. It is fool proof and there are a lot of fools who will hate being unable to fool around with the OS. It is the kind of Linux OS that is needed on consumer devices.

What we are getting with 16.04 is a hybrid Ubuntu. No Mir. No Unity 8. But debs and snaps together. It is less disruptive and if it shows application developers that providing both deb and snap packaged versions of their apps is no big deal, then I am all in favour of it. 

Regards
