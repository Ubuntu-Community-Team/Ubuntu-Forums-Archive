### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-10-09
Why are you telling us? This is a user forum not a developer mailing list. 

The Ubuntu developers are in the habit of syncing Ubuntu with Debian. This will happen in good time but first any upstream changes have to be tested to make sure they do not cause regression bugs.

So, there really is no point is saying: "Look! Look! A new version of such and such a package. Get it in Ubuntu double quick!" You assume that Ubuntu developers do not know what they are doing. Their purpose is to produce a stable release every 26 weeks and not a buggy release because of rushing to include upgraded packages that come out too close to release date.

Ubuntu 14.10 is one week away from final freeze. If this upgraded package does not get into 14.10 then it will get into the 15.04 development release. It may even be back ported to 14.10 if the upgrade is of sufficient value. Keep in mind that LTS releases are now recommended over interim releases.

As for modemmanger GUI it is installable through a PPA. We may even get the 0.0.17 version that way. But is it something I am lacking on my desktop computer because it is not part of a default install of Ubuntu? I do not think so. I am not convinced.
