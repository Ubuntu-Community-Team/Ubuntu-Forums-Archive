### Post by VinDSL on 2013-08-22
I have a sneaking suspicion that 3.11.0-031100rc6 was built on a broken RC release.

SOURCE:  [http://www.eweek.com/developer/linux-3.10-kernel-receives-two-updates-in-one-day/](http://www.eweek.com/developer/linux-3.10-kernel-receives-two-updates-in-one-day/)

> **The stable Linux 3.10 kernel received not one, but two updates in the span of less than seven hours on Aug. 20, after a small development mishap.**... 

At 9 a.m. PT on Aug. 20, Linux kernel developer Greg Kroah-Hartman announced the 3.10.8 kernel update. At 3:44 p.m. PT, Kroah-Hartman announced the Linux 3.10.9 kernel.

"**[COLOR="#FF0000"]What happened was that a patch that was reported to be broken during the RC [release candidate] review process, went into the release, because I mistakenly didn't pull it out in time**," Kroah-Hartman told eWEEK. "S**o, I just did a new release with that patch removed[/COLOR]**."...

**[COLOR="#FF0000"]A similar quick update was also made to the Linux 3.0.93 kernel, a few short hours after the 3.0.92 kernel was released[/COLOR]**. As is the case with the Linux 3.10 kernel branch, the Linux 3.0 kernel is also a long-term kernel release.

It affected multiple releases.

I'd be willing to bet our mainline kernel RC contains the bogus patch.
