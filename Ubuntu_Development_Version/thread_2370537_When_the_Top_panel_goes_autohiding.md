### Post by Chanath on 2017-09-04
When the Top panel goes autohiding, this is what happens. Either the the top panel has to be blocked as in Unity, which none of the users like, or try to make the Ubuntu Dock from going underneath the reappearing top panel. The "dock" expands to cover the free space. When the autohiding top panel reappears, the "dock" has to move down, but that appear to be not an easy thing to achieve. It maybe why the Unity top panel was never allowed to autohide, after so many years of asking for that feature.
