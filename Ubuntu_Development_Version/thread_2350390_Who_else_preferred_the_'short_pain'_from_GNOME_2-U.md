### Post by Antoon Stessels on 2017-01-24
I am just curious to know what you think of this ...

Looking back on the past 3 years, during which Unity 8 has been under active development (and we still have a long way to go, I have the feeling), I feel that I actually preferred the quick change from GNOME 2 to Unity instead of the long wait for Unity 8 to be ready.

I mean, I DO understand that it is far from ready, but the problem is that in the meantime, Unity 7 has basically been in maintenance mode. 

**Some of the nice features I liked in Unity 7 actually started disappearing; **

[LIST]
[*]No more Webapps? ...
[*]It isn't possible anymore to play songs from the dash, install apps from the dash? ...
[/LIST]

I don't want to blame anyone, just express my thoughts and hear what you have to say about this.

I think it would have been better:
[LIST]
[*]not to push Ubuntu on phones and tablets that quickly;
[*]instead focusing on the desktop, developing Unity 8, and in the meantime further improving Unity 7 to make the transition smoother;
[*]polishing the themes and icons in Unity 7, again to make the transition to Unity 8 smoother.
[/LIST]

Just my thoughts ...

### Post by ian-weisser on 2017-01-24
Hindsight is always wonderful.

Canonical, when they started down the convergence path, originally hoped to have both Gnome and Wayland aboard as partners, each carrying a heavy share of the burden and adding tremendous expertise and resources from those partners' backers.
When both hoped-for partners chose a different path, Canonical leadership knew that proceeding was bold, risky, and expensive...but Ubuntu had done bold and risky things before.

One risk was underestimating the complexity and resources required to create Unity...and then Converged Unity.
Another risk was overestimating community contributions. The community had contributed much more to desktop projects.
I think neither of these reflect poorly upon Ubuntu nor Canonical - it's hard to estimate when the estimator lacks data. Its hard to have appropriate data when nobody has tried it before.

That's the nature of risk.

I'm very pleased with Canonical for continuing to pursue Convergence in the face of great resistance - continuing work on Unity8, Mir, and Snaps while waiting for handset market conditions to improve.

Hi Ian,

  There have been  problems with some of the older Debian apps in the repositories and the person/s who maintain them in the past.  A case in point is - try downloading  the app <voice recorder> and <audacity>.
If you have audacity previously installed and install voice recorder it will  break audacity and voice- recorder will not record. There are several open apps in the current 14.04 repos which will break other apps. This is because there are not enough human resources to vigorously test all the version changes and these apps get updated into the Software center none-the-less.  Unity8, containers and snap packages are supposed to address this quality assurance problem. SABDFL adressed this issue a while back at a keynote where  he stated that containers and the way they were to be set up would bring added security while using minimal resources in lxc containers.  lxc (lxd) would work through  libertine via unity8  using xapps or snap packages. It was recently announced that  unity8 itself will be a snap package and not a desktop option.  Personally it is a bitter pill to swallow because I have to re-educate myself on snaps/framework and try to teach that in a simple and comprehensive way to persons who have just converted to Ubuntu from Windows.

Ubuntu Development Version Testing has more or less become more confined to testing ISOs, a duplication of QA service, and any other testing of apps is purely a venture  that individuals take up on their own. Steven Webb had commented last cycle how the automated QA testing was inadequate and did not deal with real time bugs in the wild and controlled testing scenarios was wanting - or to this effect. (I think it was Steve)

Unity7

It will not save the WEP if you try to use  the selected wirless source from the network pulldown menu. This was a big surprise and really threw me off.
