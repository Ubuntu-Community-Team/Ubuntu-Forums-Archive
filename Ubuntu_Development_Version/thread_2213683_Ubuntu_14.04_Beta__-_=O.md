### Post by craig10x on 2014-03-30
I have had excellent results doing upgrades...but not using the software updater or terminal way of doing it...if you only run one ubuntu, and you pop in the live iso installer, it give you the option of installing the new version but bringing over all your data and most of your apps (where possible...brings over about 95% i find)...and that is the way i do it...i turn off any ppas i have installed (you lose those anyway) and about the only things that don't make it over for me is Chrome and the Ms Core Fonts...but all my personal settings make it over with no problem as well...saves me a lot of work of doing a totally clean install...

Actually, it feels like a clean install to me...you even get the slide show as you would doing a regular (wipe out the old system completely) install... :D

I'm running 14.04 development, and when 14.04 final iso comes out i will use the same method to "clean up" all the excess from development and get a nice fresh install of the final release, yet not have to re-install all my stuff again...just a few items which takes no time at all...
