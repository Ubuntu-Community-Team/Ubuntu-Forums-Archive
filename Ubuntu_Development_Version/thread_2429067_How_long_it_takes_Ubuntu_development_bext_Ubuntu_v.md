### Post by PaulW2U on 2019-10-13
Development often starts straight away with the first ISOs available within a week or two. In order for the new repositories to be set-up it needs Mark Shuttleworth to give a name to the new release. But don't expect any great changes during the first couple of weeks as all you will have is 19.10 with a few updated packages but something that will identify as being 20.04.

### Post by PaulW2U on 2019-10-13
The release names for the 18.10 and 19.10 releases were both released one part at a time.

Although the full name was not known until 23rd May we knew that the first part would be eoan as early as 18th April as discussed [here]("https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2416965"). That was enough to get development of Ubuntu 19.10 underway by setting up the new repositories.
