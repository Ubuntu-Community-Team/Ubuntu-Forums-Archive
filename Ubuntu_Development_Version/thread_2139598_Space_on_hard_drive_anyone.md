### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-04-27
> [COLOR=#000000]I mean Ubuntu is still trying to work well for noobs to the Linux world, yes?[/COLOR]

The file manager (Nautilus) is owned by Gnome.org. Why blame Ubuntu when it is only a distribution? Are you really still using 9.04? I am having to guess what version of Ubuntu you are talking about. Come on, how can anyone give you advice if you do not tell us what version of Ubuntu is being used?

Now, if this was 13.04 under discussion I would say click on the hard disk icon in the launcher relative to the partition in question and when the file manager opens right click on the main panel and select Properties and you will see a nice little graphic showing how much is used and how much is free space.

Or you can use Disk Usage Analyser. That gives nice graphical representations of each partition. Just click on a partition, when the dots start spinning click on the back arrow and you will see a bar showing how much space is used and free. But this is all on 13.04. So, in answer to your question I do find it easy to find out how big the partitions are and how much space is used or free. The trick is knowing how to do it. And I had to experiment to find out how.
