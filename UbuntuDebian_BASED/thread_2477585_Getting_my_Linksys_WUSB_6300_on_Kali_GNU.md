### Post by guiverc on 2022-07-31
Kali Linux is not a Ubuntu [*flavor*]("https://ubuntu.com/download/flavours").

I'll provide [https://www.kali.org/docs//introduction/should-i-use-kali-linux/](https://www.kali.org/docs//introduction/should-i-use-kali-linux/)

Kali Linux does provide  [https://forums.kali.org/](https://forums.kali.org/) but do note Kali-Linux is intended for experienced GNU/Linux users and users are expected to fix their own problems, and only when that fails to have them ask a question with a well-documented problem. 

I'll also provide [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessTroubleShootingGuide](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessTroubleShootingGuide) and note for any wireless issues I tend to find 3.1 ([*Device Recognition and Operation*]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessTroubleShootingGuide/Devices")) the most useful in solving issues as it provides useful detail (ie.* chipsets in the product are more useful than make/model you provided in your post*).
