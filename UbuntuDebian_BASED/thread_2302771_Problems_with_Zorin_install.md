### Post by Rob Sayer on 2015-11-16
The first thing I generally do if I'm thinking of installing a distro (or even with app software) is look at their support site.

Well, I've looked at the support sites for both Zorin and LXLE.  The most charitable thing I'll say about Zorin is that it will never grace my HD.  And LXLE isn't much better.  But Zorin really has pitiful support, especially for a distro pitched at newbies.

Linux is different from windows.  It differs in some very fundamental ways.  Trying to make it look like windows is just a cheap trap for noobs.

I'm actually an ex ubuntu user at this point ... 2 Mint installs, though that could change.  But I always recommend ubuntu for newbies.  You will probably need tech support at some point, and there is just no other distro suitable for beginners that comes close to ubuntu support.
