### Post by CatKiller on 2020-05-31
Most distros are available as a live installer: you boot into a perfectly functional environment of what you're about to install. If you want to know what a particular distro is like, just burn its image to a USB stick and boot from it and try it out.

### Post by kurt18947 on 2020-05-31
I might suggest looking at a couple other distros as well. Linux Mint and Zorin. Both are oriented toward people used to 'classic' Windows with the start menu. Both are based on Ubuntu so share the software repositories and good hardware support. I know nothing about elementary so can't compare them. Create as many Live USBs as you like and boot each one then compare them. You may need to adjust your habits a little, there are no exe or msi installers for example.
