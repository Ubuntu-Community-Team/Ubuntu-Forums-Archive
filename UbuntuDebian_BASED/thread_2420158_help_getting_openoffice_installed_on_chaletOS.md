### Post by coffeecat on 2019-05-31
*Thread moved to **Ubuntu/Debian Based**.*

You are making a common Linux newcomer mistake by downloading stuff for installation from the internet. I have no experience of ChaletOS, but since it appears to be built from Xubuntu, it probably uses the Ubuntu repositories. In which case there should be some sort of software centre or manager. In the software centre, look for LibreOffice which is in the Ubuntu repositories. Libreoffice forked from Open Office some years ago, and is what most Ubuntu users use and is, arguably, better than Open Office. Simply mark whichever modules of LibreOffice you want installed and the software centre will do the rest for you, including downloading and installing dependencies - so long as you are connected to the internet.

The details of how to use whatever software centre ChaletOS uses I cannot help with. It may or may not be different from what the official Ubuntu flavours use.

Do this for anything you want to install. Downloading stuff yourself from the internet for installation should be a last, and hopefully never used, resort.

**Edit:** Out of curiosity, I checked out ChaletOS on Distrowatch and noticed that Distrowatch gives it a "dormant" status. The ChaletOS site itself was last updated in April 2016. It is possible your operating system is abandonware. You may wish to research this, and if ChaletOS is no longer being actively developed, you may wish to consider transitioning to a properly supported Operating System. If you would like the advice of our membership on which distro to choose, you are very welcome to start another thread in one of our chat areas.
