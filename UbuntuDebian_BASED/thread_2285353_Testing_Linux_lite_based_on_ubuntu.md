I just looked at the support forum and it's better than I expected.  But nowhere near as good as here or askubuntu.

Actually, it's just another ubuntu 14.04 based distro with the Xfce desktop environment.  Like Xubuntu 14.04 ...

I use 64 bit Mint 17 Xfce on my laptop that I generally use at home.  The reason I'm using Mint instead of ubuntu on that machine is that Intel support for the i915 open source video driver has been a bit flaky lately, and the new mint 17 upgrade path makes it easier to hold the install at a point where my video works the way it should.

But for beginners I always recommend Ubuntu.  The only other distros I've seen with comparable support are Debian and Arch.  And those are not suitable for beginners.  The support is good but they don't hold your hand.

And according to this (see the chart of RAM usage etc for a bunch of Xfce distros) ...

[http://mylinuxexplore.blogspot.ca/2015/01/linux-lite-22-beryl-review-good.html](http://mylinuxexplore.blogspot.ca/2015/01/linux-lite-22-beryl-review-good.html)

... I'm not convinced it's any lighter than a number of others.

Not saying you shouldn't install, but there are alternatives.
