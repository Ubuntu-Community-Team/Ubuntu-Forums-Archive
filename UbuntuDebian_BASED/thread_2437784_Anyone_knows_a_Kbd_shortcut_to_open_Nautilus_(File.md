### Post by CelticWarrior on 2020-02-29
Shortcuts often vary dependending on the desktop environment and other factors like distro specifics. You don't say what you're using...

In standard Ubuntu, now with Gnome, some shortcuts from the old Unity seem to have been "ported". Namely the SUPER+n where n is the number of the program's position in the launcher, still works. The difference is, in Unity, once we pressed and hold the SUPER key numbers would overlay the icons in the launcher and in Gnome it does nothing. But the shortcut works nevertheless. In my case I have nautilus (files) as the seventh from the top so SUPER+7 opens it for me.

All the above is applicable to the standard Ubuntu only.

### Post by Dennis N on 2020-02-29
For Gnome Desktop, go to Settings > Devices > Keyboard, then:
Change shortcut for opening Files to whatever you like by first clicking on 'Home Folder' in the list and then entering the combination to you want. See screenshot. The default was 'Explorer' - I changed it here to 'Ctrl-F'.
