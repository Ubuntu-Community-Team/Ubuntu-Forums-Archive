### Post by TheFu on 2023-01-15
> **kuen2 said:**
>  
I am interested in learning Linux. 

Don't get too caught up in the GUI/DE when learning.  [http://linuxcommand.org/tlcl.php](http://linuxcommand.org/tlcl.php)

If you want to learn Linux, the same base knowledge is required, regardless of the GUI used. That link is to a free PDF book to learn that base knowledge in the most efficient order.  Learn 1 chapter at a time, perhaps 1 each week.  Do that for 2-4 months and you'll gain the base knowledge.  

From there, you can start learning stuff specific to your needs.  Admins need to know different things than programmers or end-users.

Of course, if you have specific stuff you'd like to learn, follow that. Passion for any specific area overrides following some book, though getting the basic knowledge will greatly enhance the speed to learn other aspects of the OS.
There are books, websites, videos available, all free, to learn Linux. Just depends on which style of learning fits you best.
