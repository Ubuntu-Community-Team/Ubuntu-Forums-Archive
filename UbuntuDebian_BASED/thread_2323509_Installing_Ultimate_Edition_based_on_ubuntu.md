### Post by Rob Sayer on 2016-05-07
I just had a look at the ultimate edition site.

IMO the most important thing to look at when considering a linux distro ... or app software for that matter ... is their tech support.  Here's the ultimate edition support forum ...

[http://forumubuntusoftware.info/](http://forumubuntusoftware.info/)

... and frankly it's the most pathetic one I've ever seen.  It's just useless.  For newbies it's hopeless and *no* experienced users would touch it.

There are a gazillion distros but most of them are a joke and many of the decent ones aren't suitable for linux newbies.  I'm actually using Mint nowadays but the only Linux distro I recommend foe beginners is Ubuntu.

So my recommendation is to dl the various DE versions of Ubuntu, make bootable USB drives, and test them to see which DE you prefer.l  Then do a clean reinstall of Ubuntu.  Either 14.04 or 16.04 since they're both long term support versions.  I won't use any non LTS releases except for hardware support issues.
