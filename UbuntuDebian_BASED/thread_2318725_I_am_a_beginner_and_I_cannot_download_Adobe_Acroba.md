### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-03-28
You can do this yourself in a spreadsheet. It is called an Amortization schedule.

This is how to do it in Excel but the method will be the same for Libreoffice Calc although the formulas might be slightly different.

[http://smallbusiness.chron.com/make-spreadsheet-track-loan-31410.html](http://smallbusiness.chron.com/make-spreadsheet-track-loan-31410.html)

[http://www.ahuka.com/?page_id=821](http://www.ahuka.com/?page_id=821)

These are for Excel but they do open in Calc. Download the file and open it in Calc and then the spreadsheet can be edited. 

[http://www.money-zine.com/financial-planning/buying-a-home/amortization-schedule/](http://www.money-zine.com/financial-planning/buying-a-home/amortization-schedule/)

It is not clear whether you want Adobe Acrobat or Adobe Acrobat Reader.  

The former will not run on Linux.  A Linux program that will perform the same PDF operations as Adobe Acrobat is [PDF Studio Pro]("https://www.qoppa.com/pdfstudio/").

Linux equivalents of Adobe Acrobat Reader are discussed [here]("http://www.linux.com/news/software/applications/792447-3-alternatives-to-the-adobe-pdf-reader-on-linux").
