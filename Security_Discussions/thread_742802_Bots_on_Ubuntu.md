### Post by HermanAB on 2008-04-03
Hmm, there are Linux bots.  I clean up one or two per year, for example "redone".  These things are typically installed on public web servers, since those have good bandwidth and can blast out phenominal quantities of spam.  You will know when you have a bot infestation since the machine will be unusably slow and your network will be very busy, but this is extremely unlikely on a home account.

The best way to avoid trouble, is to always use very long passwords.  Common password brute forcers go up to 8 characters, so make your passwords longer than that and you will be safe.  I use 16 character semi-pronounceable passwords.  Every time I have to re-install a compromized machine, it is due to some bozo who thought that a 4 letter password would be easier to remember.

Cheers,

H.
