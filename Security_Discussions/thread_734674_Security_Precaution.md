### Post by picopir8 on 2008-03-25
Keep in mind, the results displayed are for your public address.  So it tests the security of your outermost device, which for most people is not their computer.  Many cable/dsl modems now have built in routers in which cases it tests its security.  Otherwise it is the first device connected into your modem which for most people is going to be their router or perhaps a VOIP box.

A full stealth warning is not necessary (and may not be possible) because some internet providers or VOIP services require some ports to be pingable.  Yes you will be visible but if you are behind a router, voip box, etc. then there are multiple layers of devices that need to be hacked before someone can get to your computer.  Every layer exponentially decreases your odds of being hacked.

### Post by picopir8 on 2008-03-27
> **muchas said:**
> but how can I really know if that is my modem? and how can I know if my modem has built in router?

If your modem only has one ethernet, no wireless capabilities, and no VOIP capability then it probably does not have a built in router.  However, you enter the model number into a search engine to be sure.

The best way to test is to not rely on a website for testing.  Instead get a laptop and install a port sniffing program.  Connect to at various points on your network to test the devices at that point.
