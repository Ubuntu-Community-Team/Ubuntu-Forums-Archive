### Post by openheart on 2008-03-15
Hey there people. got a second to hear an interesting story?

A few months ago after we had signed up with an AT&T data plan for our internet service I decided to have a look at traceroute results.. I noticed that whenever I issued the command to say.. find the route to google, all my results would time out after my own PC's IP. Hmm... strange. Well I investigated some more and found that issuing a traceroute  from a site on the web TO ME worked untill it hit an ip then it to kept returning time outs. 

So it seemed to me that I was unable to account for this block of IP's that my connection was travelling across. This concerned me so I spent HOURS of time calling the AT&T tech people asking them about this issue. I specifically asked them if they had ICMP filtering on or set up correctly. Here were their answers:

1. Either I dont know
2. I Cannot tell you

ok.. so.. down I go to my local store and I begin talking to someone who seems more interested in impressing me with his knowledge than actually helping me... his response...

1. traceroute is an old "program" that no one uses anyome.
2. dont worry.. it is all safe

So at the risk of this post burning up in FLAMES all I have to say right now is

1. I spend 75$ a month for a service that is only a LITTLE faster than dialup and

2. Is not responsive to addressing (what I see as) a citizens fourth amendment rights with regard to his use of private telecommunications system.

grrrrr! anybody got an idea what I should do?
