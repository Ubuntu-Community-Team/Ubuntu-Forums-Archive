### Post by RumorsOfWar on 2008-03-15
Linux is safer in several ways.
1/ It's open source. You can see the code and literally thousands of people read it. It's reached the point where security is often updated *before* an attack reaches a user !
2/ It's no danger to your hard drive ( in response to the M$ propaganda.). In fact you can choose a filesystem that doesn't need defraging, thus saving your HD some wear and tear.
And unlike Windoze, Linux doen't just, *go corrupt*.... Well, I've wreaked it, but that just the loose wire at the keyboard. My bad. ;) (That's just my wreched way of learning.)
3/ Your machine will probably run cooler and use less power with Linux, because it wastes less resources running programs that monitor programs, that monitor systems....
4/ And of course its basicly immune to viruses/ malware. 

Just don't do stuff like mix repositories, or use a web browser with root permission and you'll be fine. 
   Windows server systems have been known to crash weekly. Linux servers have been known to run for *years* without shutdown.
