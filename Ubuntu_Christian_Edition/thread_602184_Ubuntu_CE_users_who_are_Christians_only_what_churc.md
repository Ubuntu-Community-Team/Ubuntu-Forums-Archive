Every Calvary Chapel has the same structure and teaching and has to be consistent with the overall Calvary beliefs in order to belong to the Calvary network and use the name.  That is essentially what a denomination is and there is nothing wrong with that.  I can definitely see why Calvary is fighting the label and reality of being its own denomination because with an official denomination status comes many problems. 

I attended Calvary Chapel Tucson while I lived in Arizona and now attend an unofficial Calvary Chapel here in the Portland, Oregon area.  The teaching at Calvary Chapels is hard to beat since they go straight through the Bible chapter by chapter, verse by verse.  There are no topics other than those they come across in that particular teaching.  There is no agenda other than to read God's word and hear what it says to us.

With that being said, I would say that Calvary is a denomination but not in the normal sense as you pointed out.  There is headquarters, and no "leader" (though some would say Chuck Smith is) but rather a network of churches with the same statements of belief and structure for teaching God's word.

### Post by tak1150 on 2007-12-08
For those who are voting for "non-denominational," please read my first post. There is a specific definition for it. It doesn't describe just any denomination that is not listed there. For example, Assemblies of God --> Pentecostal; Nazarene --> either Pentecostal or Methodist.

Difficult ones to classify are, for example, Church of Christ / Christian Church; these are under the "restorationism" movement and can sort of trace back to Methodist (or Pentecostal) church. Also Congregational Church --> Baptist (I know there are great differences in theology, but historically this is true).

I'd hate to open a can of worms, but I didn't intend to include "denominations" that are usually regarded as non-Christian. I won't name them here.

As I said earlier, this poll's imperfection is recognized. It's just a poll, so please squeeze your church somehow into one of the categories :)
