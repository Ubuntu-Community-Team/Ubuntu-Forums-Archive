Going back to what I wrote earlier, do you think those churches would sign support contracts for Ubuntu Christian Edition, or "generic" Linux support for Churches, Schools, and similar institutions?

To be viable, a business needs to generate at least US$1,000,000 in revenue the first year, and average a minimum of US$5,000,000 per year, within five years.

A _rough_ business plan:
* White box maker/distributor;
* Migration to Ubuntu Christian Edition;
* Analog to digital migration;
* Hard copy to digital migration;
* Support for all software installed;
* Support for all hardware installed;

Products/services/support to offer are:
* Asterisk server, with the required hardware. (This handles phone/fax/sms/voicemail/picture-phone/etc requirements.);
* Webserver, if they insist on doing it internally.(This probably should be located at a company that specializes in web hosting.);
* Security orientated server;(Anybody else noticed the increase in the number of churches that have 7/24 video cameras for both the inside and outside of their building? (I've been in churches that had more surveillance equipment that the bank that was next to it.))(The main expense here is the hardware.);
* Linux based Digital recording studio;
* The Church office server. (LAMP with Koho, Wikimedia, LedgerSMB, for internal use _only_);
* Standard church office client/terminals;
* Media centre for each sanctuary & chapel;
* Church Library checkout system (Koho clients. Can be part of the standard terminal used at the facility);
* Sunday School/Bible Study/Small Group Meeting room/Classroom terminals;
* Standard school teacher clients. (This is for the parish school teachers, etc.);

# Migrating existing electronic data to Linux;
# Migrating existing hard copy data to electronic formats;
# Migrating existing analog content to digital format. (This can, but need not include digital remastering of that content.);
