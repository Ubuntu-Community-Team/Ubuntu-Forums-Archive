### Post by Anastasis on 2010-02-04
At the request of saint, I  thought I'd go ahead and start a thread to discuss a little known applet for Linux called Hdate, or Hebrew Date.

On the surface, Hdate is just another Hebrew Calendar. However, it is what the Bible student and researcher can do with Hdate that makes it a truly useful Bible study tool.

Do you want to know exactly what the 20th year of Araxerxes was based upon the Jewish calendar? Hdate can tell you that. Perhaps you want to study the exilic period of Bible history. What is the 10th day of Nisan in the reign of Xerxes? Hdate can help you get pinpoint accuracy in building your timelines.

[IMG]http://i947.photobucket.com/albums/ad313/Archangelos2010/YomKippur-YearofJubileesSatSept20AD.png[/IMG]

Or perhaps you want to know some A.D. New Testament dates. When was the Day of Pentecost? When was the Nativity? When was the Passion of the Christ?

For example, I believe that I can make a good case by using Hdate that Jesus of Nazareth was in Nazareth upon Sabbath 10 Tishrei 3786 (A.D. 25) Yom Kippur. I believe that based upon the information from the synoptic gospels, we can know that it was upon this Sabbath that Christ made the Jubilees annunciation prophesied by Isaiah for the following Year of Jubilees, A.D. 26

"THE SPIRIT OF THE LORD IS UPON ME, BECAUSE HE ANOINTED ME TO PREACH THE GOSPEL TO THE POOR. HE HAS SENT ME TO PROCLAIM RELEASE TO THE CAPTIVES, AND RECOVERY OF SIGHT TO THE BLIND, TO SET FREE THOSE WHO ARE OPPRESSED, TO PROCLAIM THE FAVOURABLE YEAR OF THE LORD." - Luke 4:18-19.

Therefore, if Christ was born during the winter census of 6 B.C. (probably Dec. 18 or 19 6 B.C. based upon Dr Molnar's study), then Luke's chronology is proven to be accurate. For Luke states that Jesus was about 30 year old when he began his Ministry. And this would be correct, because on Yom Kippur A.D. 25, Jesus of Nazareth would have been just about 30 years old

Also then, if Jesus began his ministry upon Yom Kippur A.D. 25 his basptism would have been earlier in the spring or summer of that year before he began the Wilderness temptation period and re-emerged in Nazareth in the harvest time of A.D. 25 to begin the 3 and 1/2 year ministry throughout Judea and Galilee.

So also then, the Passion of the Christ would have taken place Friday, April 13, A.D. 29 (Nisan 3789), which lends credence to a long standing tradition of ancient Christendom that the Passion was upon Good Friday the 13th, on the day of Preparation before the Sabbath of Passover week.

 Now the possibilities for using Hdate in combination with Bible study are endless. And you can pretty much research anything you want now and get very good results in accuracy.

So, I simply  submit Hdate for your consideration. Try it out. And if you think it's worthy of CE, I request that it be added.
