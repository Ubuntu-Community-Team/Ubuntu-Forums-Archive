### Post by McMurdo on 2009-09-15
So, since roggan seemed to like [my idea]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=7694864#post7694864"), I thought I would go ahead and post it.

Here's the meat of it: how about writing an open letter to Covenant Eyes kindly asking for technical data so people with CE accounts can submit data to them from 3rd party programs, such as [Net Responsibility]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=550287")?

I believe the idea to be beneficial to both Covenant Eyes users of non-CE software. They have built some excellent log filtering and report making tools, and this way we could use them (and pay for it as we always have) without them having to give up any secrets.

Now some may say at this point 'there's a reason Covenant Eyes hasn't made a Linux version.' That may very well be true, since if you have the knowledge and high enough privileges on the system, you can circumvent or disable or do whatever to accountability software. But I ask you, is this the point? Covenant Eyes and closed systems (*cough* windoze *cough*) are not an end all. One can easily enough get around CE by using Linux (LiveCD or installed). And the internet is not the only place to get pr0n.

Once you get down to it, it's a heart issue, whether you're a Christian or not (I am). You use accountability software because you want to be accountable (and if you don't want to be accountable and are more interested in getting around such things, you probably won't be interested in the rest of what I have to say). There's always going to be a gap in the system. What drives you, though, is a heart conviction from God (whether you would acknowledge that or not). Accountability software might help you, but it doesn't magically save you from your problems. It's God changing your heart that does (again, whether you believe that or not).

Sorry if I haven't been real clear on any point.

So, all this to ask, what do you think should be in the letter?

**Edit 02/02/2010:** [DustStuff]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=913700") has asked me to link to the new Net Responsibility website, [so here it is]("http://www.netresponsibility.com/"). It's the community's best second to Covenant Eyes at the moment and there are plans to improve the website and create some central documentation there.
