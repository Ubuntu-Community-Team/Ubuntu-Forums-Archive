### Post by jonathonblake on 2010-08-29
> **houndi said:**
> What are the current gaps in Christian software that runs on *Nix as a native application?

The biggie is that there is no FLOSS equivalent of Libronix. Xiphos might eventually be able to equal, if not surpass them in functionality and features. (The last time I looked, they didn't appear to be heading in that direction.)

Accounting software for churches.  

Sermon/Bible Study Lesson planner/Sunday School lesson planner software. (I'm not aware of any native Linux apps, or FLOSS apps that offer this functionality.).

"Membership" software:  Membership is in quotes, because it needs to include not only those who are active members, but also those who won't ever be members, but have some type of association with the church, even if they never come within ten kilometers of the church. This software also needs to track who is related to who. It also needs to track things like christening, baptism, confirmation, marriage, etc.

Whilst worship software is available, it has a ways to go, before being functionally equivalent to that available for Windows.

These are just some of the things I am aware of.

jonathon
