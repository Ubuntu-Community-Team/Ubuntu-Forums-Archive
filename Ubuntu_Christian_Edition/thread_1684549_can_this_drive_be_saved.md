### Post by lix on 2011-02-21
As an update that I hope might help someone else along the way......since I'm dealing with a Seagate drive, I went to their website & researched this issue & found & bought their software which enabled me to recover some data with it. I was not able to recover the files I wanted, but I'm not giving up. I have the drive in a case & can access it via USB & will continue recovery efforts via Micro$oft Window$ & Linux tools. I did get some important text files back but I did not get the huge email from Thunderbird that I needed nor the pictures & videos I needed the most. 

Be careful if you're installing Ubuntu 10. The installer will write over the entire hard drive unless you manually select the partition you want to use. Do not assume it "looks good". Verify & re-verify where it's going to install........then pray.
