### Post by Raphink on 2006-09-29
Yes, GS and BT have module managers. Those are adapted for users to add modules manually, eventually, but not for system-wide installation of modules. You can do anything without using packages: adding backgrounds, themes, tuning the configuration. But as we told you before, this will lead you nowhere. More and more derivative projects see it (Fluxubuntu, mUbuntu), and turn to us for help in switching from the script way to the metapackages way. 

Now if I say all this, it is because I have done what you have done, a year and a half ago. I began Ichthux as a Knoppix live CD, tuning the conf manually, adding modules with the BibleTime bookshelf manager, adding backgrounds and splashes. Such hacking leads to problems eventually, and doesn't allow to give back to the community.

When I began to work on Ichthux, I didn't know anything about packaging, bash scripting, or distribution making. It took me one year to become an Ubuntu developer, gather other Ubuntu/Debian developers around me and get to do some real work. Packages take longer time to get to the same point as what you would do with manual hacking. But on the long run, it's really worth it, trust me. 

Now if I and others have learned to package and be distribution makers, I'm sure you can do so, too. We have proposed to helped with this several times. There are more than 5 Ubuntu/Debian Christian developers on #ichthux on Freenode all the time, and you can come and ask for help.

I am happy to see that users rejoice in your project, but sooner or later you will have to work with developers too, and preferably to work in Ubuntu.
