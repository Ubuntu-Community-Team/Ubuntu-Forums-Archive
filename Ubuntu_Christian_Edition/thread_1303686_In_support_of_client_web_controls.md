### Post by smandoli on 2009-10-28
Well done Roggan and team!  I've decided to start a thread instead of tagging onto Net Responsibility.  

We have to work on explaining the justification for client web filtering and monitoring, from an individual moral point of view.  A web site like Roggan's will need a good 500-word summary statement to counter the erroneous "no censorship" protest.  

Compare Internet access to the electric wiring in your home:  You want to control the lighting and appliances, but you don't want to physically absorb a lot of amps.  The professional electrician needs access, obviously -- all that has been thought out, and he knows just where he'll find the breaker box.  

If your electrician were to rant his opinion that all conducting copper should be exposed at all points, we'd laugh and revoke his license.  

We don't all need little plastic safety stoppers for the wall outlets, but the smallest of us might. 

Applying this to the Internet:  The truth is that we must develop and deploy tools that allow people to use the Internet while avoiding traumatizing abuse. 

Appeals to the First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution are based on an absurd misconstruction of its meaning.  I've had it with grim, holy lectures about "freedom of speech" and the "dangers of censorship."  The underlying attitude is actually contempt for freedom, contempt for people, and foolishness concerning danger.  People should learn that freedom becomes real when it is joined with wisdom.  And that freedom becomes free when you are allowed to personally exercise it, instead of bowing to the basest common impulses of a large unstable community.

> **smandoli said:**
> 
We don't all need little plastic safety stoppers for the wall outlets, but the smallest of us might. 

In most jurisdictions, an on/off wall switch for each electrical outlet is not part of the electrical code, but it is permissible for the builder to include, because it makes the outlet slightly safer.  

Likewise, client web controls are preferred by some people, because it makes the web slightly safer to use.

In both instances, the additional protection is voluntarily added by the owner/user to meet their specific preference.
