Hi.

(Note: I write this assuming the reader believes in God and the scriptures as God's divinely inspired word as the rule for faith).

When I had Ubuntu, I would go about my day and every now and then the thought of using Linux came to my mind. Its like my mind has a mind of its own - it seems to seek out Linux. I do see the aspect of Linux that it can sort of waste our time because it's a lot more fun to tweak than Windows (well, you can't do much tweaking w/ Win). And the verse you bring out is a really good and relevant one. We do need to be busy doing the Lord's will and not our will.

Having said that, it is permissible, as Paul said, for us to spend time tweaking your Linux Box for a "hobby." However, as you have, I have been convicted about myself doing this (and in fact, I thought about this today as I prayed). I have been using Linux primarily for 2~3 years now and there have been times when I was busy serving the Lord that I only used my Linux machine for just pure work and no "geeking out." In a way, the simplicity of Debian (my primary OS) kind of helped me focus on work and not the computer (when I was doing spiritually well).

I suppose that it is when I get "greedy" and try out new distros or new unstable versions (like say, Ubuntu 9.04 beta) that I "waste" time that I could've used reading the Bible, praying, or investing in people's lives. So I think there is a way we could have Linux and not be a bad servant of the Lord. But if we feel convicted about having Linux, then we shouldn't. For it says:
> But he who doubts is condemned if he eats, because he does not eat from faith; **for whatever is not from faith is sin**. (Romans 14:23)

But if the conviction from the Holy Spirit is about not "getting greedy with or coveting new systems" and not about just having a Linux system, then we can continue to use Linux prayerfully.

Anyways, no matter how much people ridicule you, I commend you in the Lord for having a heart that fears God and wants to obey Him in all things. I will pray along with you both for you and myself.

### Post by geo316 on 2009-05-09
I know this is a dead topic already... but I just wanted to weigh in by saying that I'm a pastor and every week I compose sermons, study the bible, minister to people, follow christian blogs and web sites and more all without worrying about blue screens of death, corrupted files, security breaches, viruses, licensing fees and other distractions. Moving to Linux was in a smaller, physical way like being born again... burdens lifted, joy restored, life more productive.

While God doesn't seem to be directly concerned for what OS we use, He is certainly concerned for how we spend our time and live our lives. Coveting, striving and the other stuff mentioned in this thread can certainly be an issue, but using Ubuntu seems to do more to keep me from all that - not feed into it. Windows even tempted me to swear on occasion! :-)

From past user experience, I prefer Mac above all OS's but that is pure vanity for sure! I think Linux tends to be as cool and as stable but not as pretty as the Mac OS and hardware. If we were to assume God had a preference it would be NOT to use a mac I would think! See 1st John 2:16 :-)
