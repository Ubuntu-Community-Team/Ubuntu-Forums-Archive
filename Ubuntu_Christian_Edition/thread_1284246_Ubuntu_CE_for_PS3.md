### Post by jonathonblake on 2009-10-07
> **rsaavedra74 said:**
> who already own a PS3 that isn't the slim?

To port Ubuntu Christian Edition to the PS3, the porter needs to have a PS3 system that has the "OtherOS" feature.  Browsing thru both New Egg, and Amazon, I  don't see any  listed. 

> prefer the pre-ps3 slim 

In theory, the pre-PS3 slim models are ideal systems for churches to use during the worship service. In practice, the distinct lack of worship service software for the PS3, using either its native OS, or Linux, is the deal killer.

#####

I'm not sure I understand why Sony removed features from the PS3.  I've been looking at game consoles for about two years, trying to decide which one is the  "best" one to buy.   
* The Wii hearkens back to the VR games of the late 70s, but this time done "properly". (I had code for a driver for the VR Glove, to use it on  my Apple //e.) I'm somewhat surprised that Linux drivers for the Wii playing accessories (steering wheel, golf club, tennis racket, nunchakas, baseball bat, etc)  aren't available.

This the least expensive option, but the graphics, and most of the games suck. (Shovelware is a not a winning strategy in the marketplace.) The sole saving grace of the Wii is the VR dimension;

* The XBox 360 has the "best" games. Priced between the Wii and the PS3, the only reason to choose it was the games. Oh, and if you were unlucky enough to buy HD DVDs;

* The PS3 was the most versatile. "OtherOS", which enabled both PS2 games, and Linux, or other operating systems was the major contributor to this versatility. Its functionality as a media centre was a secondary contributor to that versatility. This was the most expensive option;

In a perfect world, a gaming console would have the versatility and graphics of the PS3, the interactivity of the Wii, and the games of the XBox 360.

In the meantime, I'll wait until either Wii accessories, or analogues of them can be used on a Linux box, and use that as a gaming console. (The best flight simulator  ---  X-Plane  --- is available on a Linux box. If one is willing to pay a minimum of US$100K --- Precision Flight Controls  --- for the additional _hardware_, one has an FAA certified Flight Simulator whose "play" hours can be included in one's flight log.)

jonathon
