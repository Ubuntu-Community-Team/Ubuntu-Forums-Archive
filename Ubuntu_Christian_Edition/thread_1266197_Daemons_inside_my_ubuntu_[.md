### Post by mmlj4 on 2009-10-15
This is silly, to be honest. It's been stated that a daemon is a server, and no computer worth using operates without them.

First, some background: I am a Christian and a preacher of the gospel. I'm also a Linux consultant and have been running Linux for 10 years. In other words, my opinion is not without merit.

Yes, the term "daemon" originally referred to the pagan idea of little helpers working behind the scenes, keeping everything running. Since server processes operate in the background, the idea pretty much describes what a server does. However, the term is generic these days, and again, it's another word for "server" or "service". Microsoft operating systems have "services" and can't work without them (try stopping the print spool service and see if you can get anything to come out of the printer). To say it yet again, the words "service" and "daemon" mean exactly the same thing, and computers can't operate without them. What about Apple Macintosh? OSX is built on FreeBSD and Darwin... don't look these up, you'll only feel worse.

"But I don't want any daemons on my computer", i can hear you replying. Fine, run some other operating system that doesn't use the offensive term. However, if you connect to the internet at all, your email, IM, web browsing, etc. all pass through servers and routers running daemons. You simply can't get away from them, short of buying a buggy and moving out to Pennsylvania and becoming Amish.

Let's take it even further. If you surf the internet or make a phone call or watch TV, you're quite probably interacting with satellites. Aerospace historically used pagan terms such as Gemini, Mercury, Atlas, Apollo, etc. OK, so let's forget the internet and drive to meet our friends in person, instead of placing a phone call. Many cars today have pagan names, such as Mercury.

My point is, don't sweat it. As I understand it, this edition of Ubuntu is helpful to Christians as it ships with bible programs, Christian wallpaper, Dansguardian, etc. If that's what you're looking for, then fine, use the edition that your fellow Christians have taken the time to put together for you. Obsessing over techical terms you apparently don't understand enough to comment intelligently on doesn't portray you as a wise believer in Christ, but a fearful and ignorant one.
