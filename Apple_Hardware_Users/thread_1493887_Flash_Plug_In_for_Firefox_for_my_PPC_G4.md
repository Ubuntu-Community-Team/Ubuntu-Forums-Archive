### Post by sha.goyjo on 2010-05-26
Your only really serviceable option is gnash (I recommend compiling it from source, as it yields better performance). Swfdec kinda went bad a while ago. For youtube videos you can also use the vlc mozilla plugin from the repositories and a greasemonkey script from userscripts.org (pick your favorite one) to watch the html5 versions of videos in a vlc box in your browser.

That's my approach to solving the problem, anyways. Gnash is slow and buggy, but it's one of the best things we've got.
