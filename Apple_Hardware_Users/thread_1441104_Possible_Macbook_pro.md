### Post by Nandux on 2010-03-29
I don't know why you need 3 O/S but by experience a tripleboot is really annoying. I'm currently a third year student in computer engineering and after almost a year of experimentation with a MacBook I decided to keep it to a single boot computer and to use virtualbox if I have any work to do with windows. I liked OSX at first and it's really good at a few things (emails contact calendar video/image/sound editing) but unless your school is supporting apple product you will always have problem when trying to move your programs/work from one operating system to another. Try to keep it simple when your need to get work done with your computer.

To answer your triplebooting questions there's a good faq here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MactelSupportTeam/AppleIntelInstallation](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MactelSupportTeam/AppleIntelInstallation)

And for the backup it will be a bit complicated to have an efficient unified solution, you could try to have an external harddrive  and use the following programs:
-OSX : TimeMachine or CarbonCopyCloner
-Ubuntu : Back In Time, Deja Dup,  Remastersys (to make a custom install disk) or simply backup your home folder
-Windows 7: Build-in backup tool with pro/ultimate

Hope it help!
