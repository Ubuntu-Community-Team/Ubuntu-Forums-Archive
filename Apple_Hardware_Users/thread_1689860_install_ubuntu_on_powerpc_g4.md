### Post by Stephann on 2011-02-17
Sitting to my direct left are three G4 PowerPCs, all with roughly 500mhz processors.  They're all humming along nicely, but like any old technology, they can be completely frustrating.

I like Ubuntu on my desktop systems, but on my G4s, I've had far more luck with vanilla Debian installations, running Xfce4 (Gnome just takes too long to load, and is too sluggish.)  On top of Xfce4, gparted & gdm hum along nicely, I have no problems with iceweasel, and they make great little mail servers.  My house gateway/dhcp server is a G4.

A big tip though; zap your pram before you install.  (Hold Command+Option+P+R till it chimes.... then chimes again....then chimes again, takes about a minute.)  Otherwise you may have troubles getting your graphics card to play nicely.

Just my two bits.

### Post by Stephann on 2011-02-17
In my experience, the net install isos are generally the better way to go.  Most of the components you install from a CD will need to be upgraded, meaning you're just downloading it twice.  Also, older hardware (i.e. G4s) dont' tend to have the fastest and most reliable optical drives; the less it needs to read, the less it can screw up.
