### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-06-01
Installation of Ubuntu, or any other Linux, on a Macboob is sensitive to hardware changes between Macbook versions.  E.g., the 8.1 and the 8.2 differ enough that installation techniques that work on one may not work on the other. (Said as a former 8,2 owner.)

I'd suggest using Google and doing some research keyed to installing Ubuntu on your specific model.  Video can be tricky, especially if the machine has 2 video cards, e.g., Intel and Radeon, or Intel and Nvidia. Apple hardware and Apple code handle switching between two cards seamlessly.  In Linux, since that code is not available, one or the other card must be disabled, which can be a bit of a trick.

Fan control can also be a problem, as in there may be no control and the fans run at maximum all the time.

Try booting up an Ubuntu ISO image and opt for the "Try It" mode and see what happens.  Hardware that works well in that environment *should* work well on a real physical install.

Your machine probably has a very small hidden restoral parition that may become visible during to Ubuntu's tools. Don't delete it, as it's used to reinstall OS X.
