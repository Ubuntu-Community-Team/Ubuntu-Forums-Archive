### Post by WA_Garrett on 2009-05-16
I think the fn key is only on laptops. On laptops the row of keys at the top double as both function keys F1 F2 ect and keys that control brightness volume ect. holding down fn while pressing the function keys means your pressing F# but when you don't hold it down it means your demanding the keys other functionality. Since you're on desktops, you have more keyboard space, and you can have keys that are just F# keys so there is no need for the fn key.
