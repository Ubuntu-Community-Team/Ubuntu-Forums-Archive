### Post by sunbird on 2008-11-04
I switched to Ubuntu on my MacBook Pro nearly 1 year ago and haven't looked back. I keep a 10GB mac partition for dealing with any firmware upgrades or other meltdowns, but have used that partition only a handful of times. 

I do load some "bad" non-free software so I can have the full use of my DVD player, but I use it to watch movies, etc. Even GIMP is getting pretty good as a consumer-grade photoshop-replacement. Obviously, for professional video editing or graphics work, I'm not sure Ubuntu will work (but there is ubuntustudio), but I use it every day and love it. I do some radio production work on ubuntu (just regular ubuntu, not studio) and audacity works great.

In short, I'm very satisfied and encourage my friends to switch!

Same here. While I love the look of OSX and run some OSX games on it (the good old Warcraft III and Diablo II amongst others....) the main reason for me to keep OSX is the DVD player (acompanied by an external drive to cover DVD regions 1 and 2 as I don't want to flash the drive codefree while I still have warranty).
DVD-playback on Intrepid is so buggy for me right now that it's virtually unusable. VLC works but it has crappy deinterlacing filters, Xine (my preferred program) displays only completely distorted video in all driver-modes (excluding opengl which works but has its own issues) with atis fglrx-filters. In all other aspects I prefer Ubuntu to OSX: Free, open-source software for every task (moving my evolution-database to OSX would be a PITA), much more freedom to change the look and style, repositories where you find the stuff you need and install it with a single click, the possibility to fix errors you'd otherwise have to wait until Apple fixes it...
