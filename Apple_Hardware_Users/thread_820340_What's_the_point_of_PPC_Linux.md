### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-06
I'm just glad that heavy-hitters in the dev community like CJW and others support *both* Debian and Ubuntu, so we should be in good shape from a community-supported standpoint.  There is also a lot of PPC support coming from other distros as well as kernel developers themselves.

For many of us, the ability to keep a good machine useful with an up-to-date Linux, rather than relying on an older out-of-date OSX (dependent upon hardware limitations / model) is also a good reason.  Some of us just prefer Linux to OSX.

The PPC boxes make good general-purpose Linux machines, and although we don't have native flash or sometimes even 3d support, not every machine has to be turned into a multimedia, proprietary-codec, social networking box. :)

PPC boxes were generally well-built, and it is hard to throw away a machine you may have paid a pretty penny for back in the day, or just dump in the recycler or on ebay.

What throws some off is that in some cases, putting Linux on a PPC box isn't a drop-in installation due to the lack of hardware manufacturers providing sufficient info to the devs.  We do the best we can with their support and the user community - something which is also desirable for it's own merits.

Join us - no matter what distro you use!

### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-07
Problem is, each distro can have it's quirks, so we rely on the community, whatever that happens to be.

> **jackoverfull said:**
> 
-and no improvements on at least 3 points:
     still no automatic loading of the sound module;

Gutsy had this issue for *some* machines with the snd-powermac module.  Faqs show how to correct it with a one-line (or two lines to make it permanent) correction.

> still no automatic loading of the fan management module;

Fixed post-Edgy for most G5's.

> still no way to install the system on an external disk without setting up manually the boot loader;

You will have to do this manually on ALL distros for the foreseeable future..

> but these are no longer problems for me, since i know how to deal with them...

Exactly.  That's why we rely on the community.

In fact, the fantastic Debian community responded well to some of it's quirks:

* Ugly fonts until you do a dpkg-reconfigure fontconfig-config and choose NO for bitmap fonts.

* No default cpu frequency scaler installed.  Had to use powernowd or cpudyn manually.

and so forth.  But the issue here is not to start a debian vs ubuntu war, but to point out that all distros have their quirks and the community, wherever that may be, is essential for a PPC'er.  And for Ubuntu, since we're essentially running a custom "sid" flavor, we hit the issues earlier than say anyone running Debian Etch or even Lenny-testing.

Ubuntu Hardy prepared me for what is to come with Lenny with the new x server and it's "bulletproof" xorg.conf. :)

* Common to ALL 20" G5 iMacs with the new X server in all new distros:  the nv driver has a slight shift to the right by about a half-inch.  Awaiting updates from freedesktop.org to fix and trickle back down to everyone.  Much of the common stuff is not distro-specific, but are depending upon kernel and other upstream sources.

I really don't want to start a distro-cheerleading team, as I run both Debian and Ubuntu.  At times I'll even run OpenBSD, so just wanted to point out that running a PPC has quirks no matter what distro you use, and the quality of the community is just as important as the distro itself.
