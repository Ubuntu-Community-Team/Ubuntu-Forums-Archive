### Post by este.el.paz on 2015-11-08
Yes Bucky B . . . you are correct on your assumption and your advice . . . indeed OSX does not play well with others and it will "dismantle" "stuff" during its install process on a harddrive . . . .  Possibly if they were on two different HDs it might not matter, but, even that I wouldn't guarantee.  I have a "triple boot" set up, actually quadruple boot if you count the OSX Recovery disk, two partitions with various OSX flavors and one for LM 17.2 . . . .  The general wisdom is to install OSX first, and then add the linux install ****last**** . . .  so idea being to set up your partitions for projected system(s) . . . using Disk Utility . . . install OSX, which could always be "upgraded" later . . . and then install Lu or whatever.

Even now, if I do a "security" update on OSX, the "forks" for rEFInd are removed and have to be re-installed . . . so, in that sense there is always something to do, and, OSX "runs the show" . . . .  Lately I have been suggesting to posters that running linux in virtual app of some kind is probably the ***easiest*** and perhaps ***best*** way to run linux in an Apple machine, then, no worries about heat and/or fan control issues with Wintel units . . . OSX is always running the machine, and easier to move from OSX files, rather than having to reboot.  That advice is not always "welcomed" . . . indeed if someone is a hacker by trade then taking care of all the tiny details of running a computer via linux might not be a problem . . .for the typical OSX user, used to having everything covered by OSX, then might be an issue.
