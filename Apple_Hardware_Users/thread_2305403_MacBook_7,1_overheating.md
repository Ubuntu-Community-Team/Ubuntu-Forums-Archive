### Post by BobUgh on 2015-12-08
I have seen users complain of hot MacBooks on the apple discussion forums, while running OSX. Sometimes it is normal. If you are doing something intensive and the macbook fan[s] are on high speed, it is probably normal. The terminal command "sensors" may tell you the internal temperature[s]; I'm not sure how to query fan speed, but on medium or high speed you should be able to hear them; at low speed you may need to put your ear near the hinge.
If your mac is getting hot but you can't hear the fans, you have a problem, likely a hardware problem. If the fan[s] is on and it feels hot, you need to know the internal temperatures.

If you have any external fan blowing on the macbook, that can help a lot. also see [https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT201640](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT201640)

I don't know if resetting the SMC does anything if you are booting into Ubuntu.  [https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT201295](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT201295)
