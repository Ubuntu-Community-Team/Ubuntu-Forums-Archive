### Post by cyberdork33 on 2008-07-30
Well, I will give my same ol' spiel...

If you want to run Ubuntu and Ubuntu only, don't get a Mac. There are much better options out there as far as compatibility. Also, for the things you want to do, I would look at a lower end laptop. There is no need to get a MacBook Pro with all it's features just to take notes, do email, and listen to music.

So, I think you would be better off with something other than a Mac, and if must get a Mac, then I would shoot for the MacBook over the MacBook Pro. Save some money and buy some accessories or support your favorite software developer.

I have to say that Ubuntu is one of the better distros for the Mac, but there are several that can work for you depending on what you want. The great thing about LiveCDs is that you can try them out before you install.

P.S. Knoppix is really just a LiveCD and not a distro that you would "install" on your machine. You can get an overview of many distros here:
[http://distrowatch.com/](http://distrowatch.com/)

I'm a huge Ubuntu fan. Don't get me wrong. I triplebooted my Macbook Pro. Windows for games only Mac for daily use, and Ubuntu for fun.
