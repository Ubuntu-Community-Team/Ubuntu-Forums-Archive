### Post by linuxopjemac on 2010-03-31
I would try the alternate installer without gui ([http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ports/releases/9.10/release/ubuntu-9.10-alternate-powerpc.iso](http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ports/releases/9.10/release/ubuntu-9.10-alternate-powerpc.iso)). You can always try to get your mouse and keyboard working when you have a working Linux box.

As a general advice though, I would start with Debian Lenny, as it is so much easier to install than Ubuntu on PPC. If you have a wired connection to the internet, I can recommend the netinstaller: [http://cdimage.debian.org/debian-cd/5.0.4/powerpc/iso-cd/debian-504-powerpc-netinst.iso](http://cdimage.debian.org/debian-cd/5.0.4/powerpc/iso-cd/debian-504-powerpc-netinst.iso)

I wish you luck with whatever type of Linux you are going to install. For mamy tips and information I advize you to have a look on my website under the line.

After you have a working box, I can advize LXDE, it's great for old hardware, I run it on a PowerBook G3 Pismo, it rocks!
