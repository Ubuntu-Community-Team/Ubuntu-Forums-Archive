### Post by linuxopjemac on 2010-03-22
Last weekend I installed LXDE as desktop manager on my PowerBook G3/400 with 512 Mb RAM. I have Debian and not an Ubuntu derivative on this machine, but the idea is the same. I was really impressed by the speed of this desktop! I tried many desktop managers, most are either too clunky (GNOME, XFCE, KDE) or either too lightweight (Fluxbox and stuff). LXDE is a very good alternative to all of them, if you are low on CPU and memory. Since recently I know of the WattOS project which aims at Intel processors. It's a liveCD with LXDE as desktop manager. A PPC version of it will be released too according to their website.

I also know of the existence of Lubuntu. I have never tried it out myself. But again, this derivative is only for Intel based machines. As that there is no liveCD with X11 for PPC based computers yet to my knowledge, wouldn't it be great to have such a thing for PPC ? I have never tried such a thing in my life, it might be a little bit too challenging, but I was thinking about trying to create a PPC version of Lubuntu. Are there people here with knowledge about such a project? Would there be people interested in this ?
