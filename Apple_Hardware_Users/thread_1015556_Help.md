### Post by stream303 on 2008-12-23
Without knowing your machine's specs, are you prepared to put in a bit of effort and possibly money to possibly get the equivalent of a 700mhz pc with 64mb of ram that won't do flash or 3d?

You'll want to take a look at the following wikis to get an idea of what you might be facing:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCKnownIssues](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCKnownIssues)
and
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCFAQ](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCFAQ)
and
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCDownloads](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCDownloads)

I'll assume you have attached a keyboard and a mouse - they don't have to be Apple.  You'll need to burn your download as an iso-image (not just copied over), and hold down the "C" key while booting.  Tips for burning (it can be on another machine as you have already done) :

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto)

AND, you may want to see if you can narrow down what your machine's specs are - although you probably don't know if the ram and/or hard drive has been upgraded:

[http://www.everymac.com/systems/apple/imac/index-imac.html](http://www.everymac.com/systems/apple/imac/index-imac.html)

Depending on which model you have, if your hard drive has been upgraded beyond oem, you may have to do custom partitioning to keep the root partition below 8gb, typically make it 7.5gb to be safe.

If this is your first time installing, I'd suggest a practice run with Ubuntu 6.06x for ppc.  Even better these days for that type of machine is Debian 4.0r5 (soon to be 4.0r6):

[http://www.debian.org/distrib/](http://www.debian.org/distrib/)

I guess this covers everything so far...
