@jason:

You've come to the wrong place if you want "idiot-proof" . . . linux is all about DIY . . . making mistakes and then figuring out how to fix it.  It's probably better to "try something" and fail, and then post here about your failure, rather than saying, "Help me, I haven't done anything . . . ."  

It seems like you read a wiki, which is good . . . I would recommend that you select a LTS version, now that is 14.04 . . . and, it seems like there are now "mac friendly" iso's . . . some people seem to like them some don't.  Try to burn a "Live Desktop" version .iso of your choice to a DVD . . . this will give you a "test" version of the system that you can boot into and try out the system . . . if you like it you can run the installer.  Try out a few "flavors" of 'Buntu, which for the most part is the most user friendly to install and run.

Seems like XFCE or Xubuntu provides the most basic and stable DE for the beginner . . . .  In my triple boot system I have "rEFInd" installed in front of one of the disks to help boot linux systems; you can read about that by searching "rodbooks" and reading the wiki's that Roderick Smith has provided for installing a "dual boot" system.  You should keep an OSX disk on your computer . . . again, some people here seem to use rEFInd, and others seem to just use GRUB alone.  There is a fair amount of information to learn about to get linux running on Apple, but not insurmountable . . . follow along on other threads . . . read the FAQ's . . . try out some Live DVDs . . . and then try an install "along side OSX" . . . .  But, back up your OSX partition first, etc.  Good luck with your investigation(s) into the wonderful world of linux.

e.e.p.
