I think Macs are great for new computer users. ANY application a new user would ever want to try is available for OS X (MS Windows, Open Office.org, Photoshop, GIMP, etc).

In addition to a ton of available software, the design of software in OS X is very intuitive and clean: perfect for new users, or those just experimenting and getting to know certain software.

In my honest opinion, OS X is the best desktop OS out there; it comes with a price, though for the OS alone, I think it is justified and worth it.

The hardware on the other hand is very expensive, and often subpar and difficult to upgrade (finding a new gfx card for my Power Mac was a mess, and involved re-flashing, etc...not something that should be the norm or recommended for non-technical users).

If your friend likes the Mac, I'd see no reason to convince them out of it. Apple warrantees are great, and the computer will be very solid, stable, and capable.

In addition, OS X is also virus free, just like Linux. Being based on BSD, you can install and compile just about any UNIX code (gcc comes with the OS X cd's, too).

Don't get my wrong, I love Linux and Ubuntu, and use it at home.

Though to make a long story short: The pc I'm using is a home built, high-end, machine. Spec-wise, this hardware absolutely runs circles around any Mac on the market, though I only paid less than 1/4 of the price of the top-end Macs. But darn..I sure do miss OS X, and all the great software that I used daily with it. I sometimes wish I would have bought a slower, less powerful Mac.

Basically, in my opinion, the advantages of OS X more than makes up for the disadvantages of the hardware Apple has chosen, and the idea that less software exists and Apples are for niche markets and specific uses is a myth.

I own a first generation Macbook, and have both OS X and Ubuntu installed.  That being said, I rarely boot into Mac OS X.

From a hardware standpoint, I recommend Macs.  I have had very few problems with this computer, and the ones that I did have were solved by Apple's excellent support without question. ### Post by Joshuwa on 2008-07-09
FWIW, as both an Ubuntu and OS X user, I would install OS X on my home-built PC the day it was released from being dependent on Apple hardware.

I'd suggest having your friend make an exhaustive list of what they would like to do on their computer, and break it into two categories:

Thinks they KNOW they will do regularly, and things they don't do, but have a feeling that might or would like to some day.

Then, have them do those tasks on each OS, and see what they like better.

For example, a short list might be like this:

**Daily Tasks**
1) Web Browsing..........Ubuntu: Firefox.......OS X: Firefox
2) Chat..................Ubuntu: Pidgin........OS X: Adium
3) Music.................Ubuntu: Rythmbox......OS X: iTunes
4) Photos................Ubuntu: F-Stop........OS X: iPhoto
5) Home video............Ubuntu: Cinelerra.....OS X: iMovie/iDVD

..and so on.

Now, we all have our own preferences, but I am willing to bet your friend will be more impressed with the options that come with OS X, or are equivalently available for free (Adium).
