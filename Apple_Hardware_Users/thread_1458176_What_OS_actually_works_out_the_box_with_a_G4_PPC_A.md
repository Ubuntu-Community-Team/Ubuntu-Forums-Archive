### Post by oswaldkelso on 2010-04-23
Ubuntu may try and mimic OSX but Debian does not. You get a base system with the Default DE of your choice (if any). You decide what you want your system to be like. All other PPC Gnu/Linux incarnations have their own view as to what is included by default. Running a free system is not like running a proprietary one. You and I are part of the out of the box experience. If you don't like that, you should pay your money to Mr Jobs.

Any PPC issues could be Ubuntu issues, I don't know. I left it after my first year. Yes you do need to read the manual when coming from another OS, and there are bugs. But if you have genuine issues they tend get fixed fast. File a bug report or subscribe to the mailing list.
 I run Debian testing so expect it to break every now and then. In those 4 years I've had two issues that I remember that have affected **some** of my 4 PPC machines. One was X broke after the new Xorg came out. I filed a bug report (via w3m) and the developer not only got back to me personally and fixed the bug, but used chance to gain extra info from my machine to implement  dual screen support at the same time. The other clive and other youtube applications broke after a youtube update. All other problems have been down to user error or my lack of knowledge. 

Anyone that expects a free version of OSX or Windows on Gnu/Linux has missed the point. That will not happen soon, and hopefully not at all. If you depend on Adobe flash tough. Flash support will be a while but does look like it will happen now, albeit it via the back door. However to say Gnu/Linux doesn't work out of the box is just not correct. I wouldn't sit in front of a non working OS or expect anyone else to. Support for PPC is not as good as i386, we all know that but the issues are few and well known and of course if you don't like it you could always re-view the code.
