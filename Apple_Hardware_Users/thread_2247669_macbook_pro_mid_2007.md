@akshayn:

The general wisdom is to do a dual boot set up . . . use DU to set up about 50GB and label it "Free Space" . . . then try to read through the "rodbooks" wiki on installing "rEFInd" . . . then install your "fav linux system" . . . after doing that, shut down the computer.  On reboot you should see the rEFInd window, select what system you wanna run; if it doesn't open, reboot holding the option key . . . then select your flava fav . . . .

### Post by jeehyun on 2014-10-22
1. Burn CD not USB stick
2. Insert CD and click C key when mac boots.
3. Install ububtu.
4. After reboot, go to "drivers" and install wifi driver.
I recommend to install only linux because old mac cann do nothing with old osx.
If ubuntu is too heavy to use, try linux mint xfce version. It's very light and easy.
