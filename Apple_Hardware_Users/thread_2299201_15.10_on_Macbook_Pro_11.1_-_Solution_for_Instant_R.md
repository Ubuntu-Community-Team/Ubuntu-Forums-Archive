### Post by MikeBraxner on 2015-10-16
Just relaying the information.

After installing Ubuntu 15.10(beta) on a MacBook Pro Retina 11.1 I ran into the 'instant resume from suspend' problem encountered by others in earlier releases. The already documented XHC1 suppression didn't quite do the trick to allow for a proper suspend. Turns out, the LID0 needs to be kiboshed as well, ideally in such a way that suspend via lid-close still works properly. Thanks to **respiranto**'s little guide, this is as easy as copy and paste.

In short...

1) suppress XHC1 wakeup call ([see here]("https://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/MacBook#Suspend_and_Hibernate")).
2) set up a service to properly handle lid-closing ([see here]("https://bbs.archlinux.org/viewtopic.php?pid=1556046#p1556046")).

(For a cheat-sheet on Systemd, check [here]("http://askubuntu.com/questions/19320/how-to-enable-or-disable-services").)

A big thanks to **respiranto**.
