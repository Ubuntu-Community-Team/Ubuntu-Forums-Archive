### Post by rakan21 on 2008-12-01
Yes there is. I only know how to do so with Mac OS X. Simply download Linux Ubuntu (I recommend the latest version). Then When your done downloading it open up Disk utility (search for it through spotlight). and then you should see the Linux ubuntu.iso on the side bar (if you don't then drag it from where ever you saved it and put it into the side bar). Click on it. Then insert your CD or already have it inserted. click burn on the top. It looks like a yellow and black circular radiation thing. Then when your done burning it (at any speed you want) take you the CD put it into your laptop and install ubuntu. 

Oh and if you use Vista when you first start up your computer. Click F10 or F1 before you see the windows screen. Go to the start up disk and rearrange the CD Disk Drive thing to be first and then the hard disk after it.
