### Post by stream303 on 2009-03-31
I often have to use Ubuntu's installer disk in single-user mode, or rescue mode, which is ok and works on a few things.

However, I wanted to point out something that is in my toolbox - Finnix.

Finnix is a rescue / sysadmin disk for both PPC and Intel/AMD, and some might find it extremely handy.  I know I do when I blow out my yaboot bootloader or partitions. :)

[http://www.finnix.org/](http://www.finnix.org/)

No sound, desktop, or productivity tools - just a cli, but man what a great tool when you need the heavy equipment to fix your PPC or Intel Apple box.

It also makes a great minimal live-cd to see if any used Macs still work before purchase - especially if the machine has low memory where a live-cd of Ubuntu is out of the question.

Highly recommended for tough cases where you need a fix or diagnostic before installing Ubuntu.
