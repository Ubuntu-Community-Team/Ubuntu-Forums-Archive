### Post by este.el.paz on 2015-07-14
Short answer, should be yes ubuntu will work . . . there might be some "additional drivers" that you would need to install for your graphics card.  As I've recommended to numerous people I wouldn't wipe OSX from an apple, not until you have lots of "experience" using linux and you could "know" whether or not it meets your needs.

Can you "restore OSX"???  If you have a "bootable clone" of the system stored in an ext HD . . . otherwise apparently "Time machine" doesn't "make a clone" it just copies files . . . so you would have to run a fresh install of OSX and then move your files over . . . which takes time.  Actually "best" option is to run "ubuntu" or whatever, in OSX in a virtual app like Parallels, then OSX is running the base computer needs, so no worries about "fan control" and "heat" issues that seem to occur in Intel ubuntu or other linux OSs . . . .

First try to download a "live desktop" and make a USB bootable or burn a DVD and boot up the system and give it a "test run."  Try a few different flavors, see what seems to work best on your computer.
