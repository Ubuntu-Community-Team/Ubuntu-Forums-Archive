### Post by scottuss on 2010-09-09
Time Machine needs an external hard disk to archive all of your data onto. If you haven't plugged it in and initialised it as a Time Machine drive, you won't be able to use it.

Plug your external drive in (if you have one) and the OS should ask you if you want to use it as a Time Machine drive. If not, fire up Time Machine and take it from there. Once you've done that, you can revert your files by "flying through space"

Note: it doesn't back up your OS, just your files.

Or better still, get rid of OS X and put Ubuntu on it :)
