### Post by ClosedDoors1559 on 2011-02-28
Okay, since you are as new as I am with Ubuntu on PowerPC, I'd say you can better try the older version of Ubuntu (10.04 or 10.10 for example). On PowerPC-based Macs (G3, G4, and G5, older Macs could work aswell), you have to download the PowerPC version of Ubuntu, or it won't work. The PowerPC-version isn't on the regular download page of Ubuntu, but you can find 10.10 for PowerPC here:

[http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ports/releases/10.10/release/](http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ports/releases/10.10/release/)

and 10.04 here:

[http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ports/releases/10.04/release/](http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ports/releases/10.04/release/)

The one you have to download is Mac (PowerPC) and IBM-PPC (POWER5) desktop CD. 

(This version will not work on your Intel iMac, just saying)

The PowerPC-version has some problems, so you just have to be prepared for it.
