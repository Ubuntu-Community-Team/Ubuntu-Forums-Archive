### Post by ian_hawdon on 2008-10-03
Hello Ubuntuers :-)

I have kind of solved the age old "how do I 'REISUB' on a Mac?" question!

Ubuntu 8.04 cras..... ok, it didn't crash (Linux doesn't crash ;-) ), the front end became none responsive! Luckily I had a USB keyboard nearby, so I just plugged that in. Ubuntu was still alive enough to detect it, and use it! So I REISUBed and got back up and running..

Then the idea hit me. Would using the Mac's (Macbook in my case) keyboard AND the USB keyboard work together. So I tried holding ALT on the Mac keyboard, and SysRq on the other keyboard, and type REISUB on the Mac keyboard. And YAY it worked!

So, I was wondering, maybe someone with the know how, could make a USB device that, when plugged in, act as a keyboard with either: A button you press to send the SysRq keycode OR give a SysRq "stuck key" type thing, so when you plug it in, you simply press ALT and REISUB to safely reboot.

Anyone have the knowledge on how to create something like this?
