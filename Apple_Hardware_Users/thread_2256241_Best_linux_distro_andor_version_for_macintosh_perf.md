### Post by T0nyI on 2015-02-14
I have a 1998 Powerbook G3, and in my case, I had to install Mac OS 9, then BootX and after that manually copy the kernel and ramdisk image to the Mac OS 9 partition in order to boot the Ubuntu install CD. AFAIK, this applies to all [Old World ROM]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_World_ROM") machines, including your Performa. Most of the info [here]("https://gonz.wordpress.com/2006/03/22/installing-ubuntu-510-breezy-badger-on-an-old-world-powerbook-g3-wallstreet/") should be applicable, I think.
