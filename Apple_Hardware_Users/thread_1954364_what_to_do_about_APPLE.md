### Post by K0ri on 2012-04-08
Files that you run with other programs, like your music and text files, don't need to be converted. However, your programs, such as your media player, messenger, and games, will not work between systems. (Not without some unstable compatibility layer jiggety-pokery, anyway)

I'm not familiar with Mac computers, but I would imagine you'd be able to install Ubuntu on one with no major problems.

Also, an obligatory word of warning: If you aren't good with technology, you'll probably have a lot of problems using Ubuntu, or any form of Linux. You will likely run into several problems over time that will require *you* to fix them via command line techy stuff, be prepared for that. Mac and Windows are much better at being 'user friendly'.
