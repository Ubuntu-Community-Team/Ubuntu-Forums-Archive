### Post by mikespug on 2011-01-21
Perhaps you could provide a link to the video you are referring to so that others might be able to better answer your question.  The only project that comes to mind that allows the native running of a 2nd alternative OS, specifically Android, is the [openiBoot]("http://www.idroidproject.org/wiki/OpeniBoot") project, though it currently only supports older hardware such as the iPod Touch 1st Gen and iPhone 3g. You can find more information at the provided link. Currently, unless there's another project that I am unaware of, your best bet to use Ubuntu on the device is to connect through a VNC server and make use of a virtual install.
