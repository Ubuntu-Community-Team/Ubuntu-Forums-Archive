Anyway, to cut a long story short this means I needed to get my music off of my iPod onto the computer as I had lost all the files. ### Post by ajgreeny on 2016-08-31
There may be other ways to do this but I have some files copied from an iPod years ago all with similar 4 character names. You can install the package **mediainfo** which will tell you the real names of the files using command ```
mediainfo path/to/file.m4a
```

Also if I open them with **audacious** the real track names are shown so there may be some way to rename them to their proper name that I am unaware of.  Other music players may also do the same, but audacious is my audio player of choice.

I have absolutely no idea if those tracks were DRM limited when I copied them, so it is possible that if yours are, these methods may not work.  If they are DRM I do not think you will be able to play them so this may be an academic exercise.

.m4p is protected and .m4a is not protected.
You can convert .m4a  -to no DRM protection - files from within iTunes by changing your import  settings. The only way to convert an .m4p (DRM protected)file is to  burn it as a audio CD into the AIFF format within iTunes.
The format  for both .m4a and .m4p is AAC. An iTunes+ will be at 256 bit rate and  the original ones will be a .m4p at 128 bits. The higher the bit rate  the better the quality.
Hope this is useful.
