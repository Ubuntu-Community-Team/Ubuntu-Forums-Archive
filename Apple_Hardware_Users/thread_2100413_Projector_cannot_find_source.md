### Post by Rutilus on 2013-01-04
If anyone else reads this with a similar issue here is more of what I tried and how I resolved - if not solved - the issue.

I installed Kubuntu 12.10 but as before the projector could not find the source. I tried to reinstall Mac OS X but couldn't, which was worrying, since last summer I created a full back up memory stick (the method where you don't need to be online to reinstall the full system); I tried two pieces of advice in forum posts re resetting NVRAM/PRAM and repartitioning but neither worked. So I rang some of the guys in the club and one of them's taking his non-Apple non-Linux laptop and projector to the meeting and I'm taking my re-formatted presentation on a memory stick.

Some background. Last year, soon after its purchase, my laptop with Mac OS X connected with the same equipment that Ubuntu won't connect with this year. At that time I had Mac OS X single boot. Afterwards, I first installed Ubuntu as dual boot, but the boot up process irritated me, so I made the full Mac OS X recovery disk (which now doesn't work!) and installed Ubuntu single boot.

Phew. I hope you have better luck or skill than me if you try to do something similar.
