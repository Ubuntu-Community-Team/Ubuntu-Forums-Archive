### Post by dr.david.maloney on 2014-11-30
Well, Rhythmbox makes a decent iTunes replacement for music, although you can't buy or rent music there. Amazon is probably your best bet if you really want to buy music. I personally haven't bought music in years, if I want to listen to something I just fire up the ol' youtubes. I have never not found what I am looking for, even with the obscure 80's and 90's alternative I like, on the youtubes. Never. I've heard that some clever folks download the .mp4 and convert it to an .mp3 in your friend VLC, but to be clear: I am not advising you to steal anything.

XBMC and the Navi-X extension will also provide you with an abundance of free movies tv shows etc, its up to you if you are comfortable with where that, uh, media is coming from. Its also now possible to use Netflix on Ubuntu natively, omgubuntu dot co dot uk for details. I don't know if you have a Powermac or Intel mac though, isn't going to work on PPC. At all.

Also, the open source Rockbox on a Sandisk (and many others) mp3 player works very well for me as an iPod replacement. 

I can't ever imagine buying another Apple product, ever. And coming from a ex Apple Fanboi extraordinare like me, that is saying a lot.
