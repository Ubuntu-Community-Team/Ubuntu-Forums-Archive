### Post by DoubleClicker on 2011-07-07
This is not a simple question that can be answered yes or no.  You need to be clear on what your priorities are.

MacBooks, have the best battery life by far, superior displays, are lighter weight, and have a power chord that prevents it from being pulled off the table (this has saved my macbook on many occasions).   Over all I consider them to be the best laptop for my use.   

On the other hand, ubuntu will not "just work" on a macbook, you need to do a fair amount of tweaking. 

The fglrx driver, frequently lags behind other system updates, so you could end up with an impaired machine, for a week or 2, after an early kernel or xorg upgrade.  

The fan will run too slow and overheat if you don't install additions from the mactel-support PPA. See [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MactelSupportTeam/PPA](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MactelSupportTeam/PPA)

For a clear overview of some of the issues see [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MactelSupportTeam](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MactelSupportTeam)

### Post by The_Underscore on 2011-07-09
I'm using a Macbook Pro 7,1 and Ubuntu 11.04 (with Nvidia's proprietary graphics drivers) runs fairly well on it. However, one has to follow some tutorial (like the Ubuntu documentation) in order to install Linux on a Mac. After booting OS X and restarting the PC to use Linux, it sometimes refuses to boot. Also, the brightness of the keyboard light isn't remembered after rebooting and starting an OpenGL program resets screen brightness to something that's way too bright for me (see [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=10995048#post10995048")). Battery life on Linux definitely is shorter than on Mac OS X (maybe 4½ hours instead of 7). The price is very high compared to other laptops with similar hardware.
The multi-touch trackpad works really well though (although the behavior is not as good as on OS X, where you can move the mouse pointer until you reach the end of the touchpad with your finger and then use another finger to continue moving the pointer; it's hard to explain). The hardware is good enough to run games and cooling works well. The DVD drive allows circumventing region coding. I also did not have any hardware problems since I bought it about 8 months ago.

The main reasons why I would not buy another Macbook after this one are what Apple is doing to Android (which is probably not relevant to you) and the high price.
