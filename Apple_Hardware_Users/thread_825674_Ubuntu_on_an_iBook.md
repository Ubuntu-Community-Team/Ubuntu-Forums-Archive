### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-11
He's right.

While we're glad to help, it is best to see what you are getting into and if you will be comfortable with something that may not be a "drop-in" installation, and require some file-editing.  Peruse the faqs and threads here.

To save time, it is best to do a bit of research on your hardware, for example, which model do you have?  How much ram is on board?  Do you want to dual-boot OSX and ubuntu, or dedicate the whole thing?  Do you have the original OSX disks so you can get back to something if all goes wrong?  Are you comfortable with an osx reinstall in the first place?  And most importantly, have you backed up all your data?

There is always the chance that you'll end up with a brick despite the forum's help.  While not likely, you need to consider your risk-factor vs experience.
