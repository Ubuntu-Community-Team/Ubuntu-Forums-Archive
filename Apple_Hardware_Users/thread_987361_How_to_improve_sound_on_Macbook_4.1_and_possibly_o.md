### Post by transmition on 2008-11-19
Normally, Ubuntu will only let you raise your sound volume to a certain level, and the same is true to OS X. However I discovered that through entering the full CLI (control-alt-F2) and then use the sound keys on your keyboard, you can raise the overall sound level past what Ubuntu normally lets you. I also noticed that this carries over to the Macbook's startup chime, and OS X's overall sound.
