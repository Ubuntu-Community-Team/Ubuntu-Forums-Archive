Long term backward compatibility is not a baseless concern - Apple has a track record of moving forward faster than other companies, and sometimes old products are simply left behind by their developers.  That being said, "long term" in this case is quite long term indeed - PowerPC emulation support is only now being dropped for the *next* OS X release, approximately five years after Apple switched to Intel CPUs.

Building your own computers is definitely cheaper, but you're ignoring the cost of warranty and support.  If you feel comfortable tinkering ad nauseam with systems you may or not be entirely familiar with with only google to guide you then by all means, go right ahead.  If you've been buying iMacs all these years you may want to consider a Mac Mini instead - stop paying for a new IPS monitor with every computer.

Free OS and software is again a matter of support - and sometimes (though certainly not always) quality.  There are plenty of free (as in speech or beer) projects for OS X, but certainly not as many as there are for Linux.

Website compatibility has more to do with the web rendering engine than the OS.  Safari and Chrome use WebKit, Firefox uses Gecko, IE and Opera use their own home baked systems.  If a website doesn't work in one, it's normally worthwhile to try it in another.
