### Post by monway on 2010-04-26
My Friends, 

Ubuntu and Linux Mint users, I have found a solution for getting around the Adobe's Flash camera settings for sites like facebook and such. I have attached pictures that show how to do this. visit the site below 

[http://www.macromedia.com/support/documentation/en/flashplayer/help/settings_manager06.html](http://www.macromedia.com/support/documentation/en/flashplayer/help/settings_manager06.html)
This is your global settings manager for your local flash preferences. 

You can fix many websites that ask you for camera permissions or other settings by adding the websites you want the camera's permission to and such. Your settings will not pop up again.
Initially I figured this out by pressing tab on the elements of the broken flash and was able to press enter on the element of "advanced" which lead me to that page. 
Please feel free to share this to all Linux users for this solution until Flash 64bit on any distro is resolved even betas. Since I have an iMac 24" (iMac8,1) with Linux Mint installed only I figured that this may help the Apple Community with isight/flash problems. This may prove to be true for others also. Hope this helps all.
