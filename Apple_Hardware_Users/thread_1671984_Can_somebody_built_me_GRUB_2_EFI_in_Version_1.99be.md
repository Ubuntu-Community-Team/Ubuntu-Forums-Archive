It's imperative that you be more precise. Just how far do you get? If you can boot the installer but then get errors during the installation, then the problem is not with GRUB -- at least, not with GRUB getting the installation started. (You could conceivably have a problem with the installer's setting up GRUB, but that's another issue.) If you get the GRUB menu and select your Linux installer but it doesn't start, then that's likely a GRUB configuration error and the workaround will depend on the *precise* options you've got.

So, please post back with details. Use a digital camera to take a shot of the monitor to show us what's happening, if necessary.
