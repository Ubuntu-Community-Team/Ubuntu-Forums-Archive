### Post by kixome on 2009-09-07
let it dry for at least 48hrs in a place with low humidity, such as in front of an a/c, do not apply any heat. an a/c will help pull the humid air out of the lappy and thus the water evaporates and your lappy should be ok. Do not plug it up for a minimum of 48 hours.

### Post by rjcalifornia on 2009-09-07
This is something I read on Engadget... so....

First of all, remove the battery. Then open up your laptop, and put the parts on rice for about 4 days. White rice, not preboiled. Why? Rice will absord the humidity of the laptop. Then, clean it up and try to use it.
