### Post by frog_pilot on 2008-10-23
For me separating the apple forums section seems to be a good idea. After both apple sections were merged, it was very hard to follow the ppc-related problems, because - as supposed by the ppc crowd after merging the sections - nobody uses the ppc- and intel-prefixes. For the apple section IMHO the whole prefix-thing has no effect. Almost everyone is using "ubuntu", this doesn't increase clarity a bit.

The only thing which merges apple-intel and ppc users is the proprietary os. E.g. if there are questions like "how to access my hfs+ volume from ubuntu". But on the hardware-side the two platforms have very less in common. In fact the mactel-platform is a special case of x86-hardware. To solve problems with ubuntu on ppc-hardware, or at least to know what packages are necessary or available for ppc, or how to install ubuntu on ppc, no ppc-user has to follow the communication about mactel-related problems.

I would be glad to have an alternative platform section. Hopefully there it would be evident, that a thread-starter has to use the right prefix for his particular architecture...

### Post by stream303 on 2008-10-27
Because of their varied hardware differences, a PPC specific forum would be ideal for users to continue contributing to the non-official community-support; so essential these days where the global economy may put further emphasis on re-use of good resources.
