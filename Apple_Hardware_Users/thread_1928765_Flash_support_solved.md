### Post by Javelin Dan on 2012-02-20
helios16v - 

To the very best of my limited knowledge, you cannot get any kind of Adobe Flash to work with modern Powerpc Linux distos as they dropped support after version 9. I have seen some chatter about downloading version 9 and making it work, but it won't help you much as most web sites today require version 10 or above. About the best you can do is go into synaptic and download "gnash SWF viewer", and "browser-plug-in-gnash".  Then, If I remember right, you have to go into your Firefox plug-ins and enable (Adobe) Flashblock. This will allow you to open any page that has Flash content, but it won't play very well. I understand you can also download "Lightspark" which is an experimental flashplayer. From what I've read, gnash handles older content, and Lightspark handles the newer stuff - they sort of compliment and hand-off to one another. You can also play flash content on some sites using "Download helper" or "VLC"., and other media players. Here, you have to first download the videos, but the advantage is that they play much better. I can personally attest to the fact that VLC rocks! I understand you can also play some videos on HTML5 which is also supposed to rock, but has limited availability so far - supposed to be the wave of the future don't ya know. That's all I know about Flash on Ppc..that and $4.00 will get you a cup of coffee at Starbucks.

### Post by svtguy88 on 2012-02-27
HTML5 video is about as good as streaming support as you will get on a PowerPC machine.  You can try browser-plugin-gnash or browser-plugin-lightspark if you want to see what flash support is like.  Both of those plugins work to some degree.  Note that you can only have one installed at a time (if I recall correctly, Firefox sees these plugins as actual Adobe Flash plugins, so you can only have one at a time).
