### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-11-27
> **jessirelli said:**
> Is running ubuntu (and the like) a good way to extend the life of a MacBook?

Welcome. Yes, an excellent idea and has extended the life of many an old machine, but with a caveat or two. How much RAM do you have? I'm guessing not much so you would probably be better with Xubuntu or Lubuntu, which are a bit more lightweight on resources, than a full Ubuntu 16.04 install.

If the hardware is failing, then Ubuntu is not going to save that, of course. Hard drives are cheap and SSD's are getting cheaper. Chuck in an SSD and some more RAM (although I don't know how possible that is on a Mac, particularly an older one) and you'll have yourself a new, fast machine.

A machine from 2008 is not generally regarded as an antique around here. That might be the case in WinWorld, but here it's called the perfect candidate for a Linux rebirth. :) Even the latest releases of lighter flavours work absolutely fine on machines that old and older, again, depending on the RAM. 

If you can be more specific about the hardware on that machine (RAM specifically and anything else you can grab) then that can help us give better advice.
