### Post by tiggerntatie on 2013-05-07
In a fit of absentmindedness I installed nvidia drivers (nvidia-current I think) on my MB Pro. I uninstalled immediately, but then upon rebooting I found that unity was gone (not working, that is). I followed tips from a different post on re-enabling unity, but it refuses to work. I see errors popping up about opengl, GLX, etc. I have tried reinstalling the Intel drivers for the machine, but no joy. 

Long story short.. is there some way to have Ubuntu re-do a default display setup for the machine? It was working fine (with the exception of not working with a projector) before I started messing with it.  I know.. it's my own fault.

EDIT: Installed Gnome, just so I have something I can use. Seems to work fine with Gnome, but I still can't log in with Unity.

EDIT: SOLVED! Returned to the situation a couple of months later and installed unity-tweak-tool while in the Gnome environment. Run unity-tweak-tool and hit each tab/category and click "Restore Defaults" button. Log out of Gnome then back into default Ubuntu environment and all is fine (though defaulted!).
