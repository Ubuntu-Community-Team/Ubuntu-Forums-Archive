### Post by FAMUgolfer on 2008-12-02
Not sure what the question is but my experience with ipods and ubuntu is fairly good. I have an 80gb ipod classic that syncs perfectly with Amarok.  I started off using Rhythmbox but I found Amarok to be much better when it comes to cover art.  

As for the "activation," there is none.  With every generation ipod, Apple updates the firmware in order for people to use itunes exclusively.  But with every distribution of Ubuntu, these wonderful geniuses have been able to break through the firmware allowing us to use other third-party applications.

Now the only problem with this is if you connect your ipod to itunes after syncing with other third-party applications like Amarok, itunes will try and update your firmware and thus require you to wipe out all of your data on the ipod before syncing with itunes. Bastards.

Another great application for putting pictures on your ipod is GPixPod. 

Hope this info helps!!

### Post by Vincent Masamune on 2008-12-17
Rockbox is great for any open source geek or tinkerer. But there is no Rockbox for the iPods after 5.5G, and it drains battery life far too much. Power users, however, who want FLAC and OGG playback don't mind and literally abide by Rockbox religiously.  

Apple formats their iPods in FAT32 for cross platform compatibility, so they should work for GNU/Linux and the various media programs that tout iPod sync compatibility. And from what I have read, Apple doesn't mandate or even have an iPod activation mechanism. The iPhone, however, is a different story. 

It seems that you are leaning towards an iPod, but before you take the leap, consider the Cowon S9(S = Sexy?) sold at [Jetmall]("Jetmall.net"). I'm no oracle of information, so if you want more, visit [Head-fi]("Head-fi.org"). The audio enthusiasts there have well-developed opinions of most digital audio players on the market. They aren't ruthless, but they are zealously demanding of their media players.
