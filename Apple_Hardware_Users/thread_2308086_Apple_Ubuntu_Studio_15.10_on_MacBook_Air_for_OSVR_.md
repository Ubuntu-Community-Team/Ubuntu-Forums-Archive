### Post by play-coolgames on 2015-12-30
[B]Christmas 2015 came along with next topic for 2016.

[/B]In November [OSVR.org]("http://www.osvr.org/") was released for Open Source development of virtual reality technologies.

The developers are using Linux and Ubuntu but others in the community monopolize the resources 
to gaming and therefore Windows based applications.
I have spent a week trying to get though building the OSVR-Core on Ubuntu Studio 15.10 on an Apple MacBook Air which has
the right NVIDIA hardware to fit the current requirements for development of new solutions to virtual reality products.

It makes HTML5 website migration look simple by comparison.
Next week the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas will show many more 3D based consumer products which will be next ready by
Christmas 2016.
OSVR is experimenting on Linux with Steam and Blender so there is a lot of talented developers already on the task.

I would ask here for help learning all it will take to get a first test of this promising hardware.
I would hope to document the process somewhere as a way to help it succeed.
I am not a programmer and have been around Linux since 1995 and DECUS in Washington DC.

I started with Apple devices in 1999 when DEC and HP were fiddling with Red Hat.
I was using PowerPC until 2008 and Apple went to Intel CPU.
Lately NVIDIA seems to be more Ubuntu friendly so I have merged all my experiences to [Ubuntu Studio 15.10]("https://ubuntustudio.org/").

As cell phones begin to stitch 360 degree views together, I see dual 360 displays as being more available next.

This is another application diverging from gaming so it should attract Ubuntu visions of 360 degree Stereo view of existing applications. 

Reply here if you are or will be developing virtual reality on Ubuntu especially getting OSVR-Core to build with Ubuntu applications.
