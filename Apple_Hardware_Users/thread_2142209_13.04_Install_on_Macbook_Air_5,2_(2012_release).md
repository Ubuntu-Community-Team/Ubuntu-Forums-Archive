### Post by orthodoc on 2013-05-04
I got ubuntu raring installed on the apple mba, and everything works out of the box. Caps lock led lights up! (it wasn't for me on 12.10) Wifi, screen brightness, keyboard lighting all work with the appropriate keys. No roaring fans at last. For the function keys, you will have to press the fn button first. To get the two finger scrolling you will have to enable it under Mouse & Touchpad setting.

Do visit [Rod Smith's website]("http://www.rodsbooks.com/ubuntu-efi/") before you start your installation to get a detailed overview of the entire process, especially the /boot/efi partitioning. This way I was able to have a protected mbr ([what's wrong with hybrid mbr?]("http://www.rodsbooks.com/gdisk/hybrid.html")) using [gpt fdisk]("http://www.rodsbooks.com/gdisk/"), and have [rEFInd]("http://www.rodsbooks.com/refind/") handle the bootloading process. Rod Smith has been doing wonderful work there, and as you can see all the links are to his articles on EFI. Cheers to him!

You can skip grub as well. There is information at his site on that too. But for detailed step wise information head over to [Random Tutor]("http://randomtutor.blogspot.co.uk/2013/02/installing-ubuntu-1304-on-retina.html"). I must say I haven't attempted this. Hopefully someone can attest to this working on the mba as well.
