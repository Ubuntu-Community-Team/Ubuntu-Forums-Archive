### Post by conundrumx on 2010-09-29
Running Ubuntu on the latest and greatest anything is a bad idea, it's hard for F/OSS projects to keep up with proprietary systems.  Add to this that Macs are a minority in the market at large, and especially in the world of Linux, and you can understand why hardware support isn't top notch.

Macs are made by Apple, for the explicit purpose of running OSX.  If you want to run OSX as your primary OS, or even dual boot with approximately equal times spent in either OS, then a Mac is a great purchase.  If you just want a good laptop to run Linux on, buy a Thinkpad.

### Post by skullmunky on 2010-09-30
Although on the other hand, PC vendors do all kinds of weird things to their hardware as well that creates headaches in Linux.  One nice thing about using Apple hardware is that there's less profusion of different options, so it's easier for the community to work on and document fixes because there's a consistent platform even if it's wacky.

Case in point, I need a laptop to do 3D work on (see my other teeth-gnashing threads in other forums here ... :)  So something with a good graphics card.  Lo and behold, many of the PC laptops including a lot of the Lenovos now have this handy thing called nvidia optimus switching to automatically balance load between a discrete Quadro or GeForce card and an integrated Intel card.  This nifty feature is apparently implemented solely in the Windows 7 driver space so for any other OS the 'good' card just doesn't exist.  

Graphics and CS people I ask keep telling me if I want to run Linux and do graphics work I should just buy a Mac :D  

I did have ubuntu for a couple years on a MBP, I think (5,1), and it worked quite well as my OS for everything except occasional Flash work.  And I'm probably about to go buy a new 15" i7 MBP, so wish me luck!
