### Post by mrsteveman1 on 2008-08-23
I know very little about the architecture of the iPod touch and iPhone, but from what i do know, it is ARM based (Which Linux runs on just fine), the chipsets involved aren't entirely Apple proprietary and drivers could likely be written.

However the bootloader seems to do some checking to prevent modified OS from booting (though the pwnage tool people seem to have gotten around that).

So is it possible? yea sure, but i don't think there are any active efforts to run the Linux kernel on the platform yet, much less an entire environment like MID.
