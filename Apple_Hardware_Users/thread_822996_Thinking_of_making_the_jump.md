I don't see why not unless you have major objections to OSX itself.  It makes a nice reference platform for comparison's sake, and you could help out others that aren't into Linux with your Leopard experience.

Even though I don't personally use OSX much, I still have it around on a bootable external drive.  Because I run Linux, I tend to use third-party cross-platform apps on OSX anyway, such as Firebird, Thunderbird, and Sunbird for my browser, mail, and calendar needs, rather than rely on Apple's own apps.  OpenOffice is slated to go aqua-native later this year, and I run Gimp in an X11 environment.  VLC is available too.  When you feel the need for cli, just drop into a bash/csh/tcsh shell, and have much of the basic *nix structure underneath.

I'm no apple fan-boy, but I don't pretend that OSX doesn't exist either.  :)  I've got a couple of friends who run third party apps, who aren't quite ready to make the jump to Linux - but if/when they do, it will be a very familiar environment.
