### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-05-25
> **noxiemolina said:**
> [COLOR=#000000][FONT=Times New Roman][COLOR=#333333][FONT=arial]what happens to a laptops battery life if its not charged before the first use? i got a laptop yesturday and used it before i completely charged the battery but had it plugged in while i was using it. willl the battery life be less now forever?[/FONT][/COLOR][/FONT][/COLOR]

The so-called "Memory Effect" is a myth, IMHO, spread by people who overcharged AA-size cells and didn't hear the noise of the electrolyte venting, and then wondered why the cells didn't seem to have much capacity anymore.

Your laptop battery will not lose capacity by shallow discharging, nor by keeping it plugged in.

Discharging the battery (known as "exercising" the battery) every few months certainly won't hurt it, but you don't need to get paranoid about it ("Oh no, I need to discharge my battery for another hour so I can recharge it!"). You don't even need to fully charge the battery either; Li-Ion batteries charge to 80% very quickly and then to 100% very slowly, so sometimes it's not practical to wait around for the extra 20% charge.

### Post by sha.goyjo on 2010-05-26
Tahakki, I guess what I meant is that it is possible (by adjusting a lot of fan, hdd, cpu, and brightness options, to get approximately the same power consumption. By default, ubuntu tends to run things faster and hotter. If you want to try to get some more life out of it, use the cpu frequency scaling monitor applet in your panel (the application bar on the top of your screen) and set it to powersave, lower your monitor brightness, and see how much extra life you get out of that.
