Hi Bill.

Have you tried installing gnome-alsamixer?

Once you do, you can adjust many settings and perhaps you can adjust the one that's giving you difficulties.

My sound is pretty good in Ubuntu on my 5,5 Macbook Pro, although the Macbook speakers are pretty subpar to begin with.  

Does it still sound poor with a decent set of headphones plugged in?

For most other Macbook quirks I've been hitting this guide pretty hard:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBookPro5-5/Lucid#Sound]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBookPro5-5/Lucid#Sound")

For demystifying what each of the settings does, I've got this site bookmarked:

[http://www.methodshop.com/gadgets/tutorials/ituneseq/]("http://www.methodshop.com/gadgets/tutorials/ituneseq/")

It's aimed at an iTunes audience but the information pretty much applies to any equalizer.
