### Post by photochuck on 2011-07-17
Today makes one week with Ubuntu (Linux). The system is working well.

The installation went well. Very straight forward, if you don't consider yourself an advance user. I did click on the advance button just to take a look. I am too new for that. So I immediately changed back to the normal installation. The installation was fairly quick. A lot faster than Apple's installation in fact.

After installation and restart, the system found my wireless network in no time flat. I was able to go on line within seconds of the restart.

I was prompted to update out dated software. I did and the software was upgrade without an event or problem.

I logged into my facebook and gmail accounts without a problem.

I decided to test the printer. The system said I needed a driver and I thought to myself here we go, this was going too well. But the system went on-line and found the driver within seconds. Less than a minute and only two clicks of the mouse the printer was up and running 

So after a week, I  have had only very small problems, nothing big. To start with the ear phone jack does not work so there goes the external speakers.

Something else that may get on apple users nerves is there is no way to type that “at” symbol. I have been copying and pasting from a old email.

I have also noticed today after a week the “Movie Player” won't play my bought and paid for DVD's. Strange since the software is called “Movie Player”.

I however am well beyond satisfied with this OS. In fact thanks to Ubuntu I can get a couch laptop well under the 1000usd needed for the cheapest Apple mac book. And don't have to deal with Windows.

You open source folks are on to something and have a new follower. Thanks!:)
