### Post by yugnip on 2012-01-01
In a nutshell, Macs are expensive because they make their own hardware. You cannot buy anything but Apple if you want to run OSX (sure hackintosh, but it's not the same).  

As well, macs come with a pretty feature-rich set of applications -- everything one needs to begin computing right away with no additional installs. Like it or not, that software would end up being pretty expensive to buy on it's own. 

Lastly, mac's are pricey because of the 'black turtleneck design-boy' approach. They pay a lot for design R & D, and push that cost back to the consumer. They figure you are either willing to pay for design, or not.

In the end, macs are just expensive. However, I do find their recent popularity has pushed PC manufacturers to up their price ante. Comparable machines in the PC world are slowly edging up to mac prices, and beginning to add some design elements as well.

Hope this helps a little bit.
