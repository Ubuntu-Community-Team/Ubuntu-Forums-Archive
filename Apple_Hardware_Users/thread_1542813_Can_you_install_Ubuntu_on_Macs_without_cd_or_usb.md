### Post by DarkEnergy.0 on 2010-07-31
There is no support for Usb's on Mac's.
You have to get a cd and burn it.

Go to Disk utilities.

Finder>Aplication>Utilies>Disk utilities.

Grab the downloaded file 32 bit or 64 bit and place it on the blank left pane. Some times it autom atically appears.

Insert your CD or DVD.

Click on the file and then click burn on the toolbar

Make sure that the verify burned data button is ticked.

Click burn and wait for it to copy. 

Then just follow the installation procedures.:D

Oh press c when you start up your computer so you can start the computer with the cd or dvd.

Then Ubuntu will load up.

I recomend the 32 bit because it has more functions or spports on java and flash and in the 64 bit there are less.

### Post by Macskeeball on 2010-07-31
Looking at your other posts about this issue, I'm not sure what kind of Mac you have. In one, you say you have a PowerBook5,2 and in another you say you have a MacBook5,2.

Let me ask you some simple questions to help identify your Mac.

1. What color is it? All white? All black? All aluminum? Part aluminum and part black? Also, what color is the keyboard?

2. Look right below your screen, in the middle. What does it say? MacBook **Pro**? MacBook? PowerBook G4? iBook G4?
Edit: I took a picture of mine to make it really clear where to look. As you can see, mine says "MacBook Pro."
[img]http://dl.dropbox.com/u/3629205/IMG_0118.JPG[/img]

3. At the top left of the screen, click on the Apple logo and then click on "About this Mac." What do you see next to "Processor"? Does it say PowerPC G4, Intel Core Duo, Intel Core **2** Duo, Intel Core i5, or Intel Core i7? How many GHz? For example, mine says 2.53GHz Intel Core 2 Duo.
