### Post by sviola on 2011-02-21
I just found this [thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1445826&page=2") on what happens when a release (like Ubuntu 6.10) has reached its end of life. It looks like most of the people think its best to keep up to date with the releases, so it might be best for you to go with the newer community supported versions.

Basically, I don't think you have anything to lose with going with the community supported versions (unless you can't get it to run). If you were to go with Ubuntu 6.10, you wouldn't get very much support from the community, and you don't get any patches or updates. The thread I mentioned earlier discusses it more in detail, so I would suggest you read that.

Let us know what you decide on and whether or not it works.
