### Post by Regunirun on 2010-08-28
I was in a similar boat. I had a powerbook G4 with karmic on it. Unfortunately, there are no official builds of Flash from adobe for PowerPC linux.. nor would it be sane to expect any in the near future :D

There are a few options though. There's gnash ([http://www.gnu.org/software/gnash/](http://www.gnu.org/software/gnash/)), and lightspark ([http://sourceforge.net/apps/trac/lightspark](http://sourceforge.net/apps/trac/lightspark)). The latter should offer much better support for embedded flash, and a PowerPC version should be availble in weeks or it might have been released already. No matter how you slice it really, flash on PowerPC Linux stinks, sadly. 

Oh, and there's SmokeScreen ([http://smokescreen.us/](http://smokescreen.us/)), which converts Flash into HTML5 and JavaScript dynamically.

Good luck!
