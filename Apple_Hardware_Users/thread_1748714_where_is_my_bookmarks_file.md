### Post by handy on 2011-05-04
Check out xmarks.

I've been using them for years, back before they changed their name & became owned by someone else.

Their privacy policy holds good & you can set your data to be encrypted as well.

What this means is even if you are not wanting to sync Bookmarks (& passwords if you choose) between machines, you can still use xmarks to keep an encrypted backup of this data for a single machine.

Beyond that, you can access your account from any machine in the world & get an html page holding all of your Bookmarks.

Show me a better solution than that?
