### Post by stream303 on 2008-11-18
One thing that is lacking on my ppc G5 iMac is a brightness control.  It is just on the edge of being too bright, and I've faked it over the years with xgamma, but even then that was not enough - not to mention changing xgamma is not advisable if you do any photographic editing. :)

Intrepid 8.10 includes a nice dark theme, Darkroom (System > Preferences > Appearance > Themes), that handles most of the situation.  Obviously it is a personal preference, but all I had to do was enable it in Appearances, and then change some of the nuclear-white input boxes to a darker gray in the Customize section.

Non-related tip: in the customize section you can change that dinky mouse pointer to something you can see.. :)

So changing the them is faking it also, but it was nice for it to be included in Intrepid - I'm sure you can find it for Hardy in gnomes themes downloads etc.

The ONLY thing it doesn't do automatically is handle Firefox' blindingly-white background, although you can change it but I don't like how they do it - so on to searching the generic help forums for that one...

Anyway, this might help out some who have screens that are just too bright, and there doesn't seem to be any brightness controls on the horizon...
