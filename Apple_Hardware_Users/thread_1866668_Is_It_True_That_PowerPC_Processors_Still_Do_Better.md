### Post by dpny on 2011-10-21
Uh, no, and no.

Compared to the x86 processors of the time, G5s were as fast or faster. But that was seven years ago, which is forever in the lifetime of processors.  Nowadays, the Mac Mini is almost three times as fast in benchmarks than the fastest PPC Mac ever, the dual dual-core 2.5 GHz G5.

AltiVec, the PPC SIMD implementation was much better than the X86 SIMD of the time but, as above, that was forever ago.

### Post by viperdvman on 2011-10-22
True, at the time Apple was using PPC chips, they were faster at almost everything than comparable x86 and x86-64 chips. However, the newer G5 PPC's were poor for lower-power laptops. That and the fact that IBM/Freescale really slowed down their development of the PPC architecture vs. Intel and AMD's development of the x86 and x86-64. Let's just say it took Intel and AMD until '05/'06 to finally catch up to the superior power-per-MHz of the PPC architecture.

Nowadays, outside of more specialized processors (video game platforms and supercomputers), the G5's are WAY outclassed by what Intel and AMD have.
