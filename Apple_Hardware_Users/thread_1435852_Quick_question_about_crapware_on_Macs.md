### Post by penguirl on 2010-03-26
Apple does include some trial software and/or services, but they are all tucked in a folder named "Installers" in the Applications folder, things like Earthlink and Office trials. If you want to try them you have to install them, they won't be in your startup items bogging down your system. They do not bundle any junk with the included applications, whether they are Apple or 3rd party like OmniGraffle, Graphic Converter, Otto Matic, Quicken, etc...

There used to be more free or donation software in the days of 10.3 or 10.4 but as the platform has grown commercial development has become more common. Any software that you find at [http://macupdate.com/](http://macupdate.com/) is pretty safe, I've never gotten anything malicious (not to say that there aren't poorly written apps that don't work) and if there was something posted that was malware it would be taken down real fast.
