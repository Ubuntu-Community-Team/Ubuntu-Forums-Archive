### Post by PartisanEntity on 2014-05-26
In general it is not a problem to dual boot OSX and Ubuntu. Depending on your hardware the installation of Ubuntu can be a little tricky, though I would never classify it as rocket science. Just requires being able to follow tutorials and have some basic knowledge of computer hardware.

I was dual booting OSX and Ubuntu for a long time without any problems. Lately, ever since the OSX updater started creating new recovery partitions and giving itself the freedom to edit the MBR, I started having problems such as not being able to boot Ubuntu since somehow the Ubuntu boot partition appears to have been changed and was no longer in sync with GRUB.

I recently decided to remove OSX from my iMac completely, it now resides on an external HDD and when I need it I can simply boot the iMac from this external hard disk.

On the internal hard disk of the iMac I have installed Ubuntu by itself as I don't want the headache.
