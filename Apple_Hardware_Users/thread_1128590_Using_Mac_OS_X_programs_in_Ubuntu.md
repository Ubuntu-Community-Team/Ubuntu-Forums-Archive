### Post by stream303 on 2009-04-19
> **matthhar said:**
> Any help? To be more specific, I'd like to run Photoshop CS3 on Ubuntu.

Unless you run it in Wine on X86, you may want to look into the Gimp.  With PPC, we have no choice since Wine isn't available.

The only problem is if you can get comfortable with the user-interface differences, and avoid the endless threads about Gimp vs Photoshop since many seem to forget that Gimp is NOT nor will ever be a Photoshop clone.  And with Gimp, you don't have any built-in image organization - that's left to you.

I use Gimp successfully to do the basics like cropping and resizing, hue, brightness, sharpness, spot healing, noise reduction etc.  When you first fire up the Gimp, it can appear to be daunting until you find you can safely ignore 90% of the options for just basic photographic work, since the Gimp can do much more than just photo-related stuff.

And of course you can put Gimp on nearly anything, so you can leverage your knowledge and experience no matter what machine you put it on.

But, this all depends on if trying the Gimp is even acceptable to you.  Be prepared for a lot of FUD that will waste your time, when you could actually be manipulating images, rather than reading about the chest-thumping of why the Gimp is crap.

As long as one remembers that Gimp is NOT nor will ever be a photoshop clone, and can deal with it, you'll be very productive. :)
