### Post by hajk on 2008-09-16
Well, I can't believe that Ubuntu (or any brand of GNU/Linux) could be blamed for that, unless the OS was specifically set up to exercise the HD to the fullest 24/7. I've run various brands of GNU/Linux on my 1st generation MacBook (now 2.5 years old) and the HD is still going strong.

Nevertheless, hard drives do fail and I think that you've just been unlucky. Asking Apple reps for advice on whether Ubuntu could have caused the problem is not likely going to get you a well-informed answer. 

Now, why did you send the MacBook in for repair? You could have replaced the HD yourself with any stock 2.5-inch SATA drive for much less, probably getting a few years warranty on the drive as well.
