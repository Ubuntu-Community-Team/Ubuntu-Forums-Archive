### Post by not-published on 2021-08-26
oops.  that's not a laptop it's an iMac.  but my "slip" was not by mistake.  they *sometimes* use laptop chips sometimes use video cards.

the thing is for laptops and apple:  they have different bios but same chip.  so you can't necessarily use "the stock video driver".  and on apple you may have a mobile chip on a motherboard or a full card:  it appears to me the radeon late 2009 is qualifies as "mobile integrated graphics controller" type.  it is not on an  epci slot i think.

I have a nVidia 1070 chip but on a ASUS epci bus card not AGP bus.  Maybe ASUS didn't connect all the bus wires to save money - maybe their bios is a little different in bad ways from nVidia's.  For mobile chips the situation is the same except worse:  they most certainly have a different bios and different way to access memory (need a different driver).  because both memory access and bios are different they end up needing a "laptop specific driver".  if it were AGP i'd have kernel issues for support of that.  but for mobile:  i would have model specific issues of how to access that "special" bus that is CUSTOM and may or may not be shared with other mobile models of the same graphics controller.

*right now your not using ANY video driver so you haven't yet confirmed the "stock driver" will not work - but if it doesn't perhaps an (older or newer one) will.  takes allot of time in the doing however.*
