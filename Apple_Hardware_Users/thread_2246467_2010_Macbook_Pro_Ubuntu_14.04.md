### Post by este.el.paz on 2014-10-01
@jon:

The question has been asked quite a number of times here, but the general wisdom is to do a dual boot system on apple hardware . . . or these days I'm even leaning toward the Virtualbox approach, so that the hardware is being run on the native system, etc.  

I have a '10 MBPro set up for triple boot, 2 partitions for OSX flavors . . . the 3rd now running Xubuntu 14.04 . . . .  I personally haven't tried the USB install, so I can't help there, but the Ubuntu wiki should have it covered.  There is some concern for the "fan control" issue on the MBPro, so find the threads that deal with adding the "mactel" ppa . . . I have a thread on this sub-forum asking about how to get that set up and Mike Braxner?? offered some excellent details on that, so search for those threads here . . . but without the "macfanctrl" ppa added . . . the computer runs hotter . . . even with it, ubuntu runs the computer much warmer than OSX does . . . .

Used to be "Additional drivers" was the app that showed you the video drivers for Nvidia cards, and the Broadcom wifi driver, now that seems to be in "Software?? Updates?" or something like that . . . perhaps those could be added thru CLI or using synaptic . . . .
