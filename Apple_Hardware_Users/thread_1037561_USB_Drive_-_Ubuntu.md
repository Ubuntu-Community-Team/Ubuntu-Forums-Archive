### Post by Hagablog on 2009-01-12
Its actually really easy.  When in your livecd plug your pendrive in.  In the menu bar go System/administration/create a USB startup disk.  The persistance feature allows you to save your data and not have a temporary session like a livecd. Follow that that through and voilà: you have Ubuntu on a pen drive. 

At this point I would like to point out the disadvantages of this as I use it and find it slightly less than what I need.  
1) from what I can tell it is more akin to a livecd than a full installation.  You automatically boot into "live cd user" and there is the big install button from the livecd. 
2) in my experience it takes longer to boot this way. 
3) to do this on a mac you need a USB boot cd.  Download one at [http://www.pendrivelinux.com/usb-boot-cd-for-ubuntu-810/](http://www.pendrivelinux.com/usb-boot-cd-for-ubuntu-810/).
