### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-12
> **gphaze said:**
> while I was trying to learn which distro was right for me to try, I encountered a link where there was a discussion among Linux developers as to whether they should drop PPC development, including their reasons for doing that.

There is a thread for *users* in the PPC forum archives which addresses that issue, but you have to very carefully define what "drop PPC development means".

> I have what I feel are compelling arguments in favor of continuing their support of  PPC, and would like to post them.

Not to worry - but first understand what "support" in this case means.  Ubuntu, like other distros, may have dropped *commercial* support, that is, you can no longer buy support contracts for it, and PPC is now (actually has been for a few past releases now) a "port" that is supported by the community, which means devs and users alike scratching their own itch.

PPC-specific releases are still being compiled and released, updates are still coming down, and they seem to incorporate many of the ppc-specific additions to the kernel.  Take a look at the kernel changelogs from kernel.org and look for "powerpc".  PPC is still being supported.

Perhaps a better way to show support is to just be active in the forums like you are doing.  AFAIK, most devs don't really have the time to read web-forum posts anyway, and Ubuntu has made the decision to drop *commercial* support long ago - and nothing will bring that back unless for some reason the PPC platform becomes very significant again.  So posting your opinion may make you feel better, but I hate to say it, is not an effective way to show support since the fact is, **we still have support - it is just community-based.**

At any rate, all of the experience you gain here can easily be transferred to any other distro, most notably Debian, in case the sky falls, and we are left without an Ubuntu distro. :)

I don't see that happening, but if it does, it doesn't worry me.

### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-13
Well, since commercial support has been long gone for some time now, the only reason to make Ubuntu PPC go away is if they are under the mistaken belief that developers should spend their every waking moment working solely on X86 in the hopes of fixing "their" problems usually.  The key issue is that most don't seem to really be aware of the difference between commercial and community support, and tend to spout the company-line about commercialism, confusing the issue for newcomers who mistake that for a lack of community.

Ubuntu has already pared down the repositories to a "port" system, and mirrors no longer carry it to save space and bandwidth.  Ok, understandable, that still works.

The real question is how much involvement does it take to produce a ppc distro that is most likely auto-built in most cases without much human intervention?  This I don't know, but I suspect that it isn't really taking away any devs complete time, and possibly a small handful of PPC machines sitting in a corner.  I'd love more info on this.

The good news is that despite the doomsday predictions, I see that the Intrepid is prepared for ppc in the ports.

I guess most of us would like to see a better installer, but unless Nvidia, ATI, and even Apple let go of some docs, we will always be faced with manually editing some files for some models.

In the end, I see that actions speak louder than words.  Releases are still forthcoming, and unless they discard the ppc boxes they are cross/auto-building on, we've got a future as a community.

### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-16
My concern about the popularity contest results is that I'm not sure it is accurate or up to date.

The readme and faq links don't seem to bring up anything (at least on my system) and if you click on the architectures at the bottom, they all refer to Dapper pages for Launchpad.

The data can also be misinterpreted by the fact that the popularity contest isn't turned on by default, and you can view it as "see, we're in third place!" or "statistically, the numbers point to ppc being insignificant".

Anyone running pre-hardy installs with the "noatime" parameter in /etc/fstab won't count, so if you are using noatime, don't bother with the contest.

There is no clear indication that this data helps or hinders us, and if being a "port" is even taken into account. :)
