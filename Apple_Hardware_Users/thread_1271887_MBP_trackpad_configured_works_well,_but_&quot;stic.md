### Post by swarup on 2009-09-21
I have my MBP trackpad configured for left-click using single & double-tap, and it works very well-- with one exception. Any time I highlight some text and copy it for pasting elsewhere, once that procedure is done the cursor doesn't move to a new place when I move with my trackpad to a new place and tap. 

Normally, moving the trackpad to any spot i.e. in a text document and tapping there once lightly, will immediately place the cursor in that spot. -- an effortless maneuver. But after doing a copy-paste, tapping once in a new place does not bring the cursor there. One has to tap multiple times-- three or four times. Or alternatively, one has to actually click the trackpad-- which I do not find at all convenient or comfortable. Indeed, after doing so multiple times, I develop discomfort in the finger. And furthermore, when I have to go to this extent of actually clicking the trackpad, it often sends too extreme of a signal, so that the new location is actually highlighted rather than simply bringing the cursor there.

I will add that, after doing copy-paste, one similarly cannot do other procedures with the usual single tap which one could normally do. For example, closing a window. After doing copy-paste, I go and try to close some window by clicking on the "x" in the upper right of the window, and nothing happens. I have to then tap again, upon which it will close.

Have others also experienced this? What is the solution, to enable one to tap lightly and get the cursor to a new location (or to carry out some other single-tap procedure), after doing copy/paste.
