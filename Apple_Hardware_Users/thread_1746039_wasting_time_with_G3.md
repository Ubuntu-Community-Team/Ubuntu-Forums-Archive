### Post by sword_king on 2011-05-02
I have just had success installing Natty on a g3 imac. It's a little slow, though. MintPPC is a "mint-themed" Debian w/ LXDE as Desktop, and much snappier. My monitor is slowly failing, so MintPPC was off-color and dark (and I don't really enjoy LXDE), so I switched to Debian+Xfce with a ligher blue/gray theme, which shows up just fine. I really like it and prefer to use it over my much faster x86 Mint Linux box.

I have a gig of RAM, so made a 256mb swap partition, 2 5gb Linux partitions, a 5gb Mac OS9 partition and the rest is devoted to OSX Panther. I check email, websurf, etc using Linux, and really only turn OSX on for iTunes music throughout the day. I actually lost my OS9 partition a while ago and haven't bothered to reinstall until last week, when I wanted to run an OS9 program in Mac Classic.

Sleep mode doesn't work for me w/ Linux, so I put it to sleep at the end of the day in OSX, and restart in the AM to check email, etc.

So, yeah, keep it. You're not wasting time, it'll be useful, and not everyone has one.

HTH, SK
