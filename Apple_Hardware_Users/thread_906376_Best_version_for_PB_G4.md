### Post by mfox on 2008-09-19
My vote is for 8.04 as well.  I'm running it on a 15" PowerBook G4/1.33 and it runs pretty well for the most part.  My only major complaint is that Hardy won't run Mac-on-Linux, a program that allows you to run the MacOS9 or X within Linux.  The problem is that no one in the Ubuntu community has yet patched it to run on the new kernel.  (It has been patched in openSUSE and most recently in Debian, so hopefully one of the Ubuntu programmers will pick it up and make it work in Hardy or the next version.)  My only other complaint is that the video colours are screwed up during the bootup sequence, but they're fine when the bootup is completed.  However, the colours were screwed up in 7.10 as well.  (I haven't tried anything earlier.)

### Post by mfox on 2008-09-19
> **SyL said:**
> Nobody is using PPC anymore ???
Unfortunately, not enough people who know what they're doing in Linux (and I'm not one of them).  The number of relatively inexperienced people who ask PPC questions far outstrips the few who can answer them, even on a popular platform like Ubuntu.  My own experience has generally been that I don't get answers to half or more of the questions I raise, so you have to be prepared to spend a lot more time and fly somewhat blindly to get some things to work with Linux on a PPC than on the more well used x86 and AMD-64 platforms.  In the case of Canonical, they have voted with their feet, no longer officially supporting versions of Ubuntu beyond the last LTS on the PPC.  Fortunately, Ubuntu just works for most things; for example the wireless setup for AirPort Extreme is by far the easiest of any Linux distro that runs on the PPC.  Ironically, Yellow Dog Linux, which is made for the PPC, is one of the worst for AirPort Extreme and I never could get it working.
