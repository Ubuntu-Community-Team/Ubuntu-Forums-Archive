### Post by mkvnmtr on 2009-01-11
The first question is have you burned the disk correctly?  When I burn a disk on my Mac I start with the download on the desktop. I open Disk Utility and click on burn. It is in the top left corner on mine.Then in the window that comes up I choose the ISO from my desktop. It tells me to insert a disk. Then I burn it.I let mac verify the burn and I have an image. If when you double click on the disk icon that is now on your desktop you see only 1 ISO file it is not burned as an image and you will need to burn it correctly.

### Post by avtolle on 2009-01-11
Which version of Ubuntu are you trying to install; are you using the Live or Alternate iso burned to CD? When burning to CD, did you use a CD-R burned as an image at a slow speed? If you burned the Cd from your Mac software, [http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/maciso](http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/maciso) may be of some help to you. Also, on the iBook G3, how much RAM does your system have? You need a minimum of 384 mb to boot from the Live CD to install, and 256 mb to boot and install from the alternate CD. 

Other thoughts: have you cleaned the lens on your CD drive lately?
