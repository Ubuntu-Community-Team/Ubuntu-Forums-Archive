### Post by rkmugen on 2013-06-28
Did you burn that ISO DVD from within OSX?  Perhaps you did it wrong.  Do you see _**any**_ kind of errors while you're trying to boot the DVD?  Are you certain you're holding down the 'C' or 'D' key while you have the DVD inserted and as you're rebooting your MacBook?  If your DVD was burned incorrectly, I'm afraid your only choices are to:

1)  Put back your older hard drive (the one that presumably still has OSX) and re-burn the ISO onto a new, blank DVD... or better yet, a USB flash drive (>1GB)
2)  Order an Ubuntu DVD disc and have it mailed to you.

### Post by JoeBlack2k on 2013-07-03
3) Download the ReFIT boot disk
[http://downloads.sourceforge.net/refit/rEFIt-0.14.cdr.gz?use_mirror=](http://downloads.sourceforge.net/refit/rEFIt-0.14.cdr.gz?use_mirror=)

4) Hold "ALT" when booting to see the boot disc's if "CD EFI" doesn't work try "CD WINDOWS" 

Fallback option:) Reinstall OSX make a Bootcamp partition this makes a Hybrid MBR on the hard drive which forces the macbook to boot into legacy bios mode and skips EFI
