### Post by Javelin Dan on 2011-12-21
[FONT=Arial]Just thought I’d pass along some info that might be of interest to someone else. I had heard a rumor that in version 12.04 (Precise Pangolin), Lubuntu would be releasing a version ported for powerpc. After Googling around, I found it here: [/FONT][[FONT=Arial][COLOR=#800080]http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/lubuntu/daily-live/current/[/COLOR][/FONT]]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/lubuntu/daily-live/current/")[FONT=Arial]. Not sure, but I believe this is not yet an official release and therefore not considered “stable”, but I burned an image yesterday and booted it on my e-mac G-4 (3.3) {1 GHZ, 256 KB cache, 1GB memory,133 MZ bus speed, 1024 x 768 resolution}. This rascal has always been very temperamental towards booting any live CD and getting the screen resolution right. However, this distro booted right up without drama. It looks very cool and lightweight, and while my particular needs are small, everything I wanted to work did so without issue. While only slightly sluggish as a live CD, my previous experience with Lubuntu was that once it’s installed it flies like the wind – every bit as fast as Puppy Linux! This may actually be the final solution to us Powerpc dinosaurs that have been looking for a truly fast and lightweight distro for that architecture. I can’t wait to actually install it (probably after the first of the year) and see how it runs in real time. Once I do, I’ll report back about my success or failure for the benefit of others. Merry Christmas to all! [/FONT]
