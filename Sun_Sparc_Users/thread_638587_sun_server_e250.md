### Post by netztier on 2007-12-12
> **otake-tux said:**
> I have the opportunity to acquire a sun e 250 server.  have any of you had experiences with it?  would it be a nice thing to have for personal home development and such?


Slow (2x 400MHz UltraSparc II at best), large, heavy, noisy, power-hungry and still not cheap if you want to add more storage capacity (where 80pin SCA SCSI Disks are basically the only valid option).

AFAIK, It lacks an UPA graphics slot, so you can't add Elite3D or Creator3D cards (which are supported by Linux, but don't offer any hardware acceleration), so using it as a workstation might not be the most clever idea.

But: it keeps your feet warm, so get one! :-)

To be honest: it can make a nice small-scale server to host your mail, file, web site and things like that - but performance-wise, you won't see it rush past your 1,6Ghz P4 standard PC from four years ago. Ok - having SCSI disks and it's good I/O architecture could make some difference over the PCs shabby IDE disks - but not in orders of magnitude.

Marc
