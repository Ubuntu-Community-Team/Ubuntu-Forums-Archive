### Post by reidms on 2007-06-07
Sun is on a higher level than AMD/Intel(opinion- but they are "elite" to me)

They make Sparc processors which are not anything like a x86 or 64 proccessor
Also, on Sun computers, you can access the BIOS while the computer is on- leaving no downtime

Sun Sparc processors are limited to few operating systems- which are basically Solaris (Suns own Unix-like OS) and these Linux distros-(Ubuntu, Gentoo, Aurora, Debian-of course there are a few more- but these are the main)

Unless you are a Sun fan, a big Solaris user, or a Java developer(these are not the only reasons- but they are the main ones for workstations)- there is no point in buying a Sun computer

Although- Sun does sell x86/64 based computers now if you really wanted to go with Sun without their Sparc proccessor

> But try as I might I could not find one review or comparison on the internet for this sun desktop hardware (well my search only involved googling it).
This is because they are not really geared towards the average users

You can buy older models for reasonable prices(I got one with 400mhz processor and 256mb ram with keyboard for 60USD)if you wanted to see what they are all about before spending anywhere from 2400USD to 32000USD on their new workstations(their most expensive server is 5 million I  believe!)
