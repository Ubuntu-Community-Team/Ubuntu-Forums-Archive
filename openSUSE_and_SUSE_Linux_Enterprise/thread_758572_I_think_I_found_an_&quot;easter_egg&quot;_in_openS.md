### Post by Growbag on 2008-04-18
No, it's still GRUB, just a more modern version.

You can do that to Ubuntu by installing a package called grub-gfxboot and wrestling with making it work :)

See this thread:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=208855&highlight=gfxboot](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=208855&highlight=gfxboot)

It is default in openSUSE, and if you ever lose GRUB (ie when you have to re-install another temperamental O/S, not mentioning any names!), it can be simply and easily restored from your boot CD/DVD.
