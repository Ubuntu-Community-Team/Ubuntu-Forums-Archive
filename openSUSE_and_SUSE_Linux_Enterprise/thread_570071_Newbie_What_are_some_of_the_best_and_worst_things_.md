### Post by isaacj87 on 2007-10-07
I'm brand new to OpenSUSE. I just put 10.3 on my desktop and I find it to be a little better than average.

Visually, it's very pleasing to the eye. It's pretty user friendly, but it definitely feels like a completely different beast. I'm quite used to Ubuntu and Gnome (I installed the KDE version of OpenSUSE).

The package manager is weird to me and doesn't feel quite up to par with synaptic. I had a good bit of trouble with Java (something to do with permissions?) and I'm pretty sure not all of the restricted multimedia is working as it should/installed. 

The installation process was long and drawn-out (which isn't bad, but not good) So many options to choose and it took me over an hour to just get out of the setup. 

Boot up time is impressive though. It literally takes less than 20 secs to come up. And I was impressed with all the splash screens. It all feels very professional. 

I like it. It's nice and it feels complete and polished. I just like Ubuntu better even though it doesn't feel as "professional."

### Post by RedDwarf on 2007-10-08
It's a good distro. I don't like the package manager interface (nothing to do with speed or dependency resolution... what I hate about YaST PM is the interface), but you can always use Smart, APT/Synaptic or yum (RedHat). Install any of them just takes five minutes, so...

Multimedia support just requires you to use the Packman repository, and through the Build Service you have a lot of packages in their last version (no need to wait for the next release to update xmoto...).
I never used Gnome in openSUSE, but the KDE desktop is great.

### Post by Incense on 2007-10-08
OpenSUSE 10.3, offers one of the best out of the box experiences of any distro I have used. Both KDE and Gnome offer a very unique feel with the SLED and Kickstart menus. The branded splash screens make the entire OS feel very integrated and add a nice level of polish. The newest release feels very snappy, and installs a great group of apps by default. The YaST installer is crazy customizable, and allows you to set up your desktop any way you want during the install. YaST is a fantastic tool for configuring your system. Yes, it does take a moment to load the repos when installing packages, but it works very well, and you'll be able to load most newer programs without having to wait for a new distro release. Give it a go, and see what you think your yourself. If you don't like it, it's all good. There are about a million other distros that you can try as well.

Cheeers!
