### Post by laptoplinux on 2007-12-18
So, let me explain myself.  When Suse first went open, I tried it out and stayed with it for 10.0, 10.1, and 10.2 because at the time it was , IMO, the best Linux distro I had seen.  But I left 10.2 for Ubuntu 7.04 due to the ugly package manager screw up in 10.2.  And after I left, there was Novell's deal with the devil.  I've been skittish about returning to Suse ever since, but no one does KDE like they do, and I have issues with some of the other KDE supporting distros:

Fedora:  hasn't supported my wireless card out-of-the-box in 4 releases since the hardware was made, despite numerous empty promises.

Mandriva:  latest release includes an old OpenOffice version.  Getting the newest version is one reason I need to upgrade.

Kubuntu:  Kubuntu is not a well done KDE.  Go use OpenSuse to see what I mean.  Also, more importantly, it seems to me that Kubuntu is always more buggy than Ubuntu and trailing in features by a least 1 release.  Not to mention that switching logins from Ubuntu to Kubuntu has been know to screw up icons back in Ubuntu and other weird issues.  There isn't the desktop-agnostic attitude that a distro like Mandriva usually has.  The day Kubuntu truly receives equal standing with Ubuntu is the day I'll never even think about switching from the *buntu family.  


I've been missing KDE ever since going to Ubuntu, but otherwise Ubuntu is by far the best distro out there.  I've been thinking about Opensuse, but here are two things holding me back:

1.)  10.2 was slow in all respects (bootup, package management, you name it), and near the end the package manager completely died on me.  If 10.3 is not an improvement, I don't care to blow my good Ubuntu away for that.

2.)  The ever looming shadow and influence of Microsoft.  I'm not really sure I can trust Novell or Suse again.  How well founded is this fear in 10.3?


Please don't interpret this as any kind of attack on Ubuntu.  I have a little downtime for the holidays and need to upgrade my fiesty install.  I can blow it away with anything and I'd like to explore all options since I'll be using Linux very heavily this spring.

### Post by K.Mandla on 2007-12-18
I don't think you're being silly. I think your reasons are important, actually. I don't use KDE enough to appreciate the differences between distros, but if one appeals to you more than another, that's completely understandable.

On the other hand, I do sympathize with your reaction to the MS-Novell "pact" -- I don't find that particularly appealing either. I hope I'm wrong, but I think that may turn sour for Novell one day.

What level of confidence would have in customizing an existing KDE setup to resemble the OpenSuse version? In other words, are your preferences functional or aesthetic, and if they are the latter, can you rearrange Kubuntu, for example, to your liking?

My only other suggestion would be to look at [KDEmod]("http://kdemod.ath.cx/") sometime, which is a version of KDE tailored to Arch Linux. It's my only recent run-in with KDE (aside from the Slax distros), and I thought it very well done. Arch isn't like Ubuntu or OpenSuse, which means you'd have to know your stuff before trying it, but I thought KDEmod was quite attractive.

P.S.: I'm going to shift your post to Other OS Talk, because there are some OpenSuse fans over there who might be able to give you better advice. Cheers! :)
