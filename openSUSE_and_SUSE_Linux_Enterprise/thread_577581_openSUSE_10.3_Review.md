### Post by grinby on 2007-10-28
I, too, have been getting more and more impressed with 10.3.  I will say that the key moment when I went from questioning whether I'd keep that installation to liking openSUSE was a switch to KDE.  While I generally like Gnome better, I found the GTK Yast interface too simple to do anything elaborate with (like installing KDE, for instance).  You can change to the yast-qt interface in Gnome by going to yast, System, /etc/sysconfig Editor, System, Yast2, GUI,WANTED_SHELL.

I did this, but then decided I wanted to get the whole SUSE KDE experience.  I thought the Gnome desktop was nice, but I really like the KDE implementation.  The SLAB menu, in particular, is much better put together in KDE.  And the yast-qt interface is a vast improvement.
