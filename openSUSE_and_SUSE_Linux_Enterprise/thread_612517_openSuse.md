### Post by x64Jimbo on 2007-11-15
No, it means that Microsoft has an unfair monopoly on the commercial operating system market, which has absolutely nothing to do with the open source operating system market. I believe that Ubuntu's numbers surpass openSuse's because of the community surrounding the distro, not the quality of the product. Even if Ubuntu were slightly less polished than openSuse, the awesome atmosphere of these forums and the willingness of its members to help each other out would overcome what little deficit there might be in quality. 
These aren't just my sentiments. Pretty much every article or spot I've seen on Ubuntu trumpets the community's merits as its leading cause of success. Without enthusiasm and support, software is just software. 
I am not trying to say that openSuse is bad. I am also not trying to say that its community is not helpful or enthusiastic. I am sure that it is, and I am glad that there are so many well-developed and well-supported Linux distros out there. They all help contribute to the general success of OSS, and that makes me very happy!
