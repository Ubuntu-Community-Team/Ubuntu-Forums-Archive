### Post by Sunspark on 2007-10-24
I dunno.. I think if I was doing it over again, and I knew I was going to be using Linux, I would get an officially Linux supported laptop like the System76 Darter, or the Dell Inspiron 1420 to save headaches.

When you buy a MB Pro, you buy a nice notebook that has LED backlighting. You also purchase hardware that is proprietary and locked down, and you'll spend awhile waiting for Linux to fully support components of it.

Who has time for that?

That said, I'm still running OS X. I had hoped to try switching to GG, but I'm seeing problems with it on both the MBs and Vaios so we'll see. I don't blame Ubuntu or anyone else for this. The people who write the drivers can only do what they can with available information. If the manufacturers won't release specifications, then they are limited with what they can do. Apple uses components that have restricted specs, so your milage will vary.
