### Post by manidhanu on 2008-03-26
Hi everybody,

as I've seen, many mac users are trying to install ubuntu on their new Penryn-based MacBook Pro.

I propose to make an effort to post three good explained, high quality, step-by-step, fully working tutorials for common users:

1) Installation of Leopard and Ubuntu (dual-boot) on new Penryn-based MBP

2) Installation of Leopard/windows/Ubuntu (triple boot) on new Penryn-based MBP

3) Other types of multiple OS installations on new Penryn-based MBP: Parallels,...

The result should be a straight-foreward document that "just works" (with wireless, keyboard backlight, new track-pad functionality and everything), for a full ubuntu experience on the nice last-generation MBP. A particular attention should be deserved in making the tutorial as simple, complete and comprehensive as possible, that means to avoid "freak" language as much as possible.

I really encourage the best of you to make that effort of synthesis. I think it would be an important and widely appreciated work and will motivate many new common users to finally switch to open-source.

Thank you!
