### Post by schauerlich on 2007-12-20
I used xrandr to set up my MacBook with an external display. I had a problem where I would get two overlaying areas of video on the external monitor; An invisible 1280x800 box on top of the normal 1680x1050. When a window was completely inside the invisible 1280x800 area, it would maximize to 1280x800; if it was outside of the box, it maximized to 1680x1050 like you would expect it to. Also, when I used the cube, there was a visible seam along the lines of the 1280x800 box. The only way I found to fix this was to set the resolution lower and then bring it back to 1680x1050. So, I added the following to my GNOME session: ```
xrandr --output LVDS --off
``` to turn of my laptop screen and ```
xrandr --output VGA --mode 1024x768 && xrandr --output VGA --mode 1680x1050
``` to take care of the problem I just described. However, occasionally when I log in, the video is unintelligibly choppy and static-y when it's set at 1680x1050. 1024x768 works fine, but it won't even switch to any other modes it is supposed to work at. Any idea what's going on?
