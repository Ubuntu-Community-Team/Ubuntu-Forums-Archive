Glad someone said it. I think this is really, really important, in a typically Apple intuitive way. The physical interface should mirror the virtual when there's a physical paradigm as foundation. It doesn't make sense to have different rates of cursor movement vertically and horizontally, and I would think that'd be necessary to use a regular ratio touchpad in conjunction with a widescreen monitor. Anything else would drive me insane.

Also, in Linux I use three-finger tap as a middle click, and this is much easier on a wider pad. Same with two-finger scrolling (which you don't see a lot of Acer fans doing!).
