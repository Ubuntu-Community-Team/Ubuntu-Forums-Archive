### Post by jonny on 2007-03-19
Running 915resolution -l only tells you the resolutions that are pre-programmed into you video BIOS.  The purpose of the 915resolution software is to overcome that limitation, so you wouldn't expect the command you used to find the resolution you need unless the display is actually attached.

As for whether your Mac can drive the resolution you're looking for, I have no personal experience.  I haven't tried my Mini with anything higher than 1366x768, and 915resolution automatically finds and uses that resolution for me.

Can you try taking your Mac to the shop where you plan to buy the display?
