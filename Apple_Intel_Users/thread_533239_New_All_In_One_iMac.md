### Post by Greywhind on 2007-08-23
I have an older Intel iMac, and I can say that Ubuntu Feisty works extremely well on it (with Bootcamp used to make the partition).

The only issue was installing the wireless drivers, but that's because the older iMacs had Broadcom cards, whereas I don't think the new ones do.

Of course, since it's an Intel processor, everything will work on Ubuntu (once installed and configured correctly) just as it would on any other PC.

The Ubuntu installation even detects the Mac hard-drive partition by default.

The ATI graphics card is the only real problem that remains (ATI's graphics drivers work, but are terrible compared to NVidia) - that's why my next computer will be a MacBook Pro (which has an NVidia card).
