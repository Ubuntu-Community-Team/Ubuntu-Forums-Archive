### Post by Grey Box on 2007-12-04
This is a problem we've all seen now - basically, the battery life under Ubuntu is less than OS X. This of course is because of higher energy consumption, but it's not really clear where the problem exactly lies. The big energy spenders on your laptop will be the screen and hard drive. The processor isn't really that energy hungry really (at least, it doesn't seem to use much less power when it scales down than when it's running full speed). 

My macbook runs a bit hotter with ubuntu than OS X, and the power brick (transformer) is hotter too, like yours. 

Part of it I attribute to a less aggressive power saving policy with the screen and the hard-drive not spinning down as much. But OS X's power policy is quite aggressive. You just have to sit there and read a paragraph of a website on battery mode and the screen is off already.  It's not much different on power.

Maybe you could set your monitor to switch off after a couple of minutes like the OS X. Seriously, without the monitor back-light, the computer doesn't use much energy for anything if it's not busy.
