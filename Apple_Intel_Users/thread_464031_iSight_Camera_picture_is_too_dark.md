I believe this is because OS X/Photobooth tweaks the image in the camera for best results. Have you ever used iChat for voicechat? After switching from that back to Skype, my chat partner told me ambient noise was much greater in Skype. I think this is because Apple applies filters to the audio in iChat so people can hear each other better. Just another example of a small and undocumented tweak which results in a better user experience. I've tried to use my iSight in WinXP, too, and didn't like the results there, either. I've heard of [programs]("http://www.ecamm.com/mac/iglasses/") which allow additional tweaking of iSight picture quality, too. 

I think we'll just have to wait until there's better software support on Linux for this. Keep an eye on threads [like this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=479711"); maybe something good'll pop up.
