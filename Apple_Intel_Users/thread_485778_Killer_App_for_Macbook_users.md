### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-06-27
Is it just me, or is [this article in the wiki]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBook") Ubuntu's killer app for Macbook users? I considered many others, such as Sabayon and PCLinuxOS to run on my MacBook (sick of paying for software for it, and my other Linux system, while having more RAM, isn't nearly as fast, and doesn't have a DVD burner, not to mention lower resolution), but none had this kind of tool for setting up my system 100% perfectly. For the past six months, I've done most of my Linux computing on a ThinkPad T41, which is now running Pardus (great distro, by the way. The installer even detected my 1280x800 resolution), and on that computer, I can pretty much use any distro I please because the resolution and hardware are very, very standard.

Anyway, thanks to everyone who created this great article in the wiki!
