### Post by Jussi Kukkonen on 2007-10-18
Short answer: No, not out of the box.

Longer answer: There are a few games with native linux clients. Many more work with wine (see [http://appdb.winehq.org/](http://appdb.winehq.org/) for list of working apps). Many do not work at all.

btw: next time, pay attention to which subforum you post to. This has very little to do with Apple Intel hardware...

### Post by Greywhind on 2007-10-18
Many of the newer games have Linux clients - Quake Wars and Unreal Tournament 3 for example.

Many others work with Wine, often with some configuration. These include Half Life 2 and many other Steam games.

Some won't work at all, but you should be able to get a good selection - I'm a gamer myself, and I don't own a copy of Windows. So look up some of your favorite games, and see if they will work.
