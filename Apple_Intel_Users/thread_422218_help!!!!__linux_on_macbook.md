### Post by hajk on 2007-04-25
There is an alternative to the dual boot/BootCamp mess: use an external Firewire drive. You could either install Linux on the internal HD (as you've already done) and OS X on the external drive; or you could do the reverse. It depends on which of the two OS-es you're going to use most.

As to getting wireless (and other functionality) on the MacBook going in Linux, have a look at my notes on [http://www.xs4all.nl/~hajk]("http://www.xs4all.nl/~hajk"). I used Debian Sid instead of Ubuntu Feisty, but the main ideas would be the same.
