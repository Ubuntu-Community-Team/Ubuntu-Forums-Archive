### Post by Sunspark on 2007-09-24
Hi I am really curious to know this..

I have a MacBook that has always had flickering fluctuating backlight brightness in OS X. I've had the logic board replaced, didn't work. I've had the LCD's inverter replaced, didn't work. The flicker is most noticable when the computer is waking up from sleep, or after it's been off a long time. Sliding the brightness slider in the display system preferences often stops the flickering and pops it back to full brightness.. though over time it dims slightly again subtly.. Sometimes it will go brighter on it's own. Once the system is fully warmed up over a course of several hours, there is no more brightness fluctuations in the backlight until next time the backlight has been off for an extended time. Often I can make it flicker by moving the screen up and down.

If one goes to [http://discussions.apple.com/forum.jspa?forumID=1168](http://discussions.apple.com/forum.jspa?forumID=1168) there are hundreds of messages over the last year with people complaining of unfixable fluctuations, especially in this thread: [http://discussions.apple.com/thread.jspa?threadID=628019&tstart=75](http://discussions.apple.com/thread.jspa?threadID=628019&tstart=75)

Now, why am I writing this here you ask?

One service advisor suggested that this wouldn't happen in Windows or whatever. There isn't a single message in this forum complaining about flickers. I have been reading about partitioning schemes for Ubuntu and I've read that some people nuke the EFI partition entirely.

I am wondering if the backlight flickers have something weird to do with software with EFI or whatever. I am uncertain because if it was software it shouldn't really be able to tell that the screen is moving.

So, what do you think? Hardware issue, or people here have been luckier than most?

Thanks!
