### Post by technodigifreak on 2007-08-21
I finally got a Mac.  After having been on the fence for (literally) years.  I've had it for about 2 weeks now and I have to admit, I've been steadily more and more impressed.  I got the latest white macbook with the superdrive.

I went ahead and did the upgrade to 2GB of RAM.  Made all the difference in the world.  I've also loaded VMWare Fusion and have to say that, like all of VMWare's products, Ubuntu runs great in it. I have to say, I did notice OS X behaving rather sluggishly when I had 256MB dedicated to a VM, before I did the RAM upgrade.

So, basically, upgrade the RAM if you are running VM's, it's worth the extra hundred bucks, especially if you are routinely working with VM's.
