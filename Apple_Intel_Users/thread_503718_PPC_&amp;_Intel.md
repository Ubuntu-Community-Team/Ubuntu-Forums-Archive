### Post by cyberdork33 on 2007-07-18
well, the biggest issue is the fact that most linux apps are developed for x86 and have to be ported to ppc. 

But, really the difference is exactly what you think, they are totally different CPU architectures. and since the PPC machines are older, the other components may be different. Apple changes the hardware parts in their machines quite often, and it is difficult to keep up with what they all are.

### Post by cyberdork33 on 2007-07-18
you can use the i386 version or the AMD64 version depending on if you want to run 64-bit or not. 

Once you get Ubuntu installed and the hardware working (there are some issues, though they are being taken care of over time) you pretty much have a standard PC running linux. So, most if not all the apps that run on a normal PC install of ubuntu should run on your mac just the same.

If you have an intel mac, you don't want the PPC version (it wouldn't work).
