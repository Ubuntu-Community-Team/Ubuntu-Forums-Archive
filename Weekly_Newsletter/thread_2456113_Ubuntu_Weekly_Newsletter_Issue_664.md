### Post by TheFu on 2021-01-04
LoCo Events do a really good job of staying with Canonical and Ubuntu brands, but sometimes they are a little too good.  In my region we have a fairly active LUG - Linux User Group.  We do not exclude people due to the distro they run and most of our members run Ubuntu-family desktops.

This week the LoCo rep for our LUG got a message suggesting we should  "restart the LoCo."  That is confusing since we have multiple meetings every week and Ubuntu is commonly discussed.

It would be worth pointing our that most Linux systems are closely related, so there is little need to avoid a LUG, just because it doesn't restrict itself to Ubuntu-only, especially when the vendor isn't organizing or funding it.

A number of LUGs have moved online the last year, so physical location doesn&#8217;t matter, just whether the meeting is at a convenient time. We have people joining our meetings from 4 timezones away. All are welcome.

There's nothing like getting direct, live, help over video chat with screen sharing.

Search for a LUG. Join a meeting once. See if you like it.
Search terms:
[LIST]
[*]lug {city}
[*]lug {county/parish}
[*]lug {state/province}
[*]linux {city}
[*]linux {state}
[*]linux {region}
[*]Also try a local college or university name with LUG or Linux.
[/LIST]


Don't be afraid to try facebot, twitter, and meetup too. My LUG has a meetup presence. Wish we didn't need to waste money on that, but it is helpful for people who never learned to web search in finding or group.  We are old school, so most information sharing happens via email listsrvs.
 
Some places have half-state LUGs.  "southern alabama LUG" or "bay area LUG".   Get creative, but most will include a city somewhere on their page which makes google searches fruitful.
