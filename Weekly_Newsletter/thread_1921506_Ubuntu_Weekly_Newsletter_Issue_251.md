***[size="3"]How to do Juju - Charming oops-tools[/size]***

Diogo Matsubara, of the Launchpad Red Squad and Product Team, writes about a new  a new cloud project this team recently began work on. Matsubara notes that as part of this  new project the team will be using juju -- a tool that helps users easily deploy services on the cloud.

In his quest to learn more about juju, Matsubara has written a new charm to deploy oops-tools.  The charm is described as an open source Django application that helps visualize and aggregate error reports from Launchpad, on Amazon's EC2 and Canonical's private OpenStack cloud.

More information on this charm and juju can be found at the link below.

[http://blog.launchpad.net/general/how-to-do-juju-%e2%80%93-charming-oops-tools](http://blog.launchpad.net/general/how-to-do-juju-%e2%80%93-charming-oops-tools)

### Post by EmmaSystem76chick on 2012-02-07
Great work putting this together News Team! Thank you for all the information! I'd like to comment on the summaries about Unity and HUD...
 
There is  so much negativity in the blogosphere surrounding Unity and HUD and it's unnecessary. It's a step in the right direction for taking Ubuntu mainstream. New users will love that they don't have to learn the applications and where to find everything. I'm excited because Unity and HUD make it easier for me to convince my friends who are used to windows and mac, to switch to Ubuntu.

With HUD, it doesn't automatically initiate the application or function, you have to press “enter” to complete the action. It also offers alternatives in the search results. Why are people so concerned that the UI will automatically launch the actions? Some of the examples in the blog posts from the skeptics were extreme, especially the one about the voice commands and executing ram. 

I want to give some props to Swapnil Bhartiya for his post about not quitting Ubuntu. His alternatives to try the different flavors of Ubuntu instead of quitting at the sake of the new HUD are the most optimistic I've seen so far in the blogosphere. Swapnil's right. Nobody has to quit Ubuntu just because of HUD, and there are plenty of flavors for all of us. I hope to see some more positive remarks about Unity and HUD, and can't wait to see more developments!
