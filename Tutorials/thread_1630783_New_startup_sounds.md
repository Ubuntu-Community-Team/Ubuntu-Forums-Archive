Included here are many new sounds for system ready (if you have a  username and password protected Ubuntu OS log-in) and desktop log-in, most often in an alphabetic order (targeting the   artists' names, not the title of the songs - the exception is the list under "New Material", where the latest additions are listed on top regardless of their names).  The  material here is very varied in flavor   and possibly targets many   different tastes. If you like what I have   made in terms of source of  inspiration, then much of this can provide   you with a lot of raw  material that you can develop and tailor to your   meet own preferences  (for example shorten the spans). I have used   "Audacity" for most  parts, working on this. 

*All compressed files here (that is every single download) contain at   least one "system-ready.ogg" and one "desktop-login.ogg". If there are   any deviations this is noted, and always this means that there are   several other versions of either "system-ready.ogg" and/or   "desktop-login.ogg".*

If you don't know how to change your startup sounds please read below:[/COLOR][/B][I][COLOR=Red]

[/COLOR][/I]1. Open the downloaded files with (for example) **Archive Manager**, or download/save the file and then use an archive manager to open it (unless you prefer the Terminal).

2. Extract the files to a location you prefer (for example in the Downloads directory). 

3. Get substitute-user-do, for example through this command in the Terminal: **gksudo nautilus** and then type in your password. You will become root user, and should maneuver yourself to: **/usr/share/sounds/ubuntu/stereo/** 

4. In there you should (for backup-safety) rename the following files: **desktop-login.ogg**  (should/could be the default intro melody when you enter your operative system, unless you have already altered it) and **system-ready.ogg**      (should/could be the default short drum-session if you have a login      screen, unless you have already altered it) to something like:** _bak_desktop-login.ogg** and **_bak_system-ready.ogg**  

5. Open up the directory where you extracted **_bak_desktop-login.ogg** and **_bak_system-ready.ogg** (they may have a slightly deviating prefix, depending on what file you downloaded, like for example: _bak4_), remove the **_bak_** (or for example: _bak4_) in front of each file, restart your system, log in (this is where you should hear the new **system-ready.ogg**), observe/listen to your desktop appearing (this is where you should hear the new **desktop-login.ogg**) - and enjoy (or cover your ears...).
