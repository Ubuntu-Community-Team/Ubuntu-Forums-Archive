### Post by boomshop on 2009-02-02
I've got a MacBook 2.1 white unibody (2007) and am very disappointed with it.

After exactly one year the following hardware is crippled:

- body: the top plate including keyboard and trackpad is falling off in thin stripes at the edges. Started some months ago there only existed a complete edge at the display side. was fixed two days before guaranty ended.
- display: was flickering from the beginning for about 15 mins. after boot or reboot. was fixed also.
- harddrive: made some noise for a couple of weeks, apple's local service-team "couldn't recognize any unusual sound", two weeks later the drive crashed hardly with complete data loss. fix after weeks of discussion with the service, finally directly with apple. (got two bills about, and apple the third one)
- memory: first change three weeks before guaranty time ended, second change some weeks later.
- dvd: makes horrible noises. started not ejecting media a few weeks ago, apple service can't find any error (I have to try every media at least 3 times, apple-install-dvd needs about 10 tries). was never fixed.
- keyboard: some keys are making annoying "clicks" if they aren't hit exactly in the middle and the backspace was loose. Keyboard was changed (due to the ripping off stripes on edges), the new one had an unpressable mouse button and was "thrown in the body" like the 2-year old so of my buddy would do a better job. Next (and actual) keyboard has those clicks again - on different keys than before. I'll live with them from now on, my patience ended up.
- cooler: is getting louder and louder, especially at low speed. Started about 4 months ago, he sometimes doesn't let me listen to a dvd without headphones.
- after missing my book several weeks for all this ****** up hardware the service team spoiled the complete body - display hangs over the right side, body at the left.
- and after all my power supply is just gone, the cable is broken exactly after disappearing in the box.

Okay that were my main probs with it. Additionally it sux because of the following:

- display is way to dark. working outside with overcast (? cloudy) isn't possible at all.
- heavy temperature problems: it *has* to stay on a table or notebook cooler, lying on the lap for 5 mins will cause heavy virility problems.
- the graphics adapter sux. 128MB from RAM is a joke. but compiz is running.
- sound is way to quiet, no bass.
- display can't be opened enough, I wish it could have about 10 more degrees in some situations.
- Mac OSX 10.5 needs about 20G on my 120G harddrive. Can't remove it because of firmware updates.
- It is soooooo ugly!

There are many more things I hate about this piece of ... computer but there is a lot I like on the other hand:

- keyboard: aside those problems I really like the feeling while typing. many keys are missing (delete, screen up/down, home, end for example) but linux gives you every option to make a setup you like. so the lack of keys is a feature for me :) never had a keyboard with "faster" layout.
- touchpad: with the right module it is a bit choppy (standard module is much smoother but in comparison with native macOSX both will loose the game) but has really cool features like scrolling with two fingers in every direction, 2/3-finger click, circular scrolling and more.
- plugs are only on the left side. I am right handed so a mouse will never be blocked by cables and stuff. but that feature results in only few plugs, only 2 usb is not the jackpot.

after all my experiences over a year I wanted to take a youtube-vid about rolling over it with a back tyre or so and go and buy a *real* computer. ThinkPad was the next choice. But while I waited in the shop to get served I asked a young guy with a cardbox of a R500 in his arms "you are buying? or what?" he replied that - after three services - he's sick of it and will return this one RIGHT NOW. applause.

But in an intensive talking with the salesman it turned out that there were a lot of huge problems due to unready/missing/buggy drivers under vista. Not my problem it seems...

Apple? never ever ever more. From my personal POV. A computer with 5 different hardware versions over two years is a test site in my opinion, not a 1500 euro notebook.
