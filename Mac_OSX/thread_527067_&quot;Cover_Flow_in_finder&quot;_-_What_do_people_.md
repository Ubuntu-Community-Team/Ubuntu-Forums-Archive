### Post by DataTracer on 2007-12-13
For almost all purposes, it's pretty useless. I only like it for one thing that has always been lacking in OS X, picture browsing! Everyone seems to focus on album browsing because Cover Flow started in iTunes. I absolutely hate iPhoto, so I find Cover Flow more useful for looking at my photos. I also really like Quick View with the space bar.

I only wish in full screen view it didn't "bonk" at you when trying to go to the next/previous photo with the arrow keys. There is a workaround however. While in full screen view, Apple-Tab to another app, then switch back to finder. Your arrow keys will now work in full screen! Weird huh? I hope they fix that with the 10.5.2 update. They should have also included the ability to zoom with the +/- keys IMHO.

Honestly, I think Linux can live without Cover Flow!
