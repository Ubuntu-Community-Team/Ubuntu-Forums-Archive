### Post by BarfBag on 2008-12-28
The time has come to upgrade my machine.  It's at that age where you've maxed it out completely, and you know it's going to croak any day.  I'm seeking advice from you guys.  I'm not looking for Mac bashing; I've read my share of that.  Here are my options:

1.  I could build a new desktop PC.
2.  I could buy an IBM/Lenovo Thinkpad.
3.  I could buy a System76 laptop.
4.  I could buy a Mac.

I'm 18, and I work at a fast food restaurant.  I make $7.25/h, which is higher than minimum wage and pretty good for fast food.  I mention this because finances are a factor in this purchase.  Even though I'm doing pretty good in the situation that I'm in, money comes in at a slow pace.  The cost is what scares me about Macs.  Is it really as bad as it seems?

I've been dual booting various GNU/Linux distros with Windows XP Home Edition for years.  I have currently settled with Ubuntu.  Unfortunately, work and school have taken all of my time away.  I no longer have the time to tinker.  I'm aware that you can make Ubuntu "just work," but I find myself working in XP more and more due to convenience.  That's where all of my school is.  My school software is compatible with Mac (at least, more compatible then it is with Linux; if that makes any sense).  So, I'm just wondering, is Mac right for me?  Or, would I get frustrated and end up chucking it?

### Post by bashveank on 2008-12-28
A Mac is right for you if you want the compatibility of Windows with the stability of Linux.
But to give you meaningful advice we'll have to know what you'd like to do with your computer. What programs do you use? What websites do you go to? What devices do you have that need to sync with your computer? Will you be buying a laptop or a desktop.
I can tell you that you will not get frustrated with a Mac. The interface is very polished and well done.
