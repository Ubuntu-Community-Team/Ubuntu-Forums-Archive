To the OP, (I have not read the 3rd page of the thread, so I could be speaking too late for you?)

You were talking about building a desktop equipped with an SLI capable motherboard & running 2 hefty graphics cards.  Obviously you want to build a gaming powerhouse, under those circumstances you would be unwise to buy a Mac notebook, as it can not possibly compete with your GPU horsepower.  The other thing that is worth thinking about is the type of CPU you use, if you are building a gaming rig.  Multi-core is not as fast as a single core CPU that dedicates all of it's processing power to the game as opposed to having multiple processors that  are not involved in the game at all.  (This may or may not be changing with the most recent games?) It is worth investigating whether you are wasting money on CPU's that you don't need for the desired purpose, when a single core AMD CPU will give you more gaming grunt for less dollars.  Also, on the saving money subject, you could purchase 1 most recently superseded very fast model nVidia graphics card, which would save you money due to it being superseded & then in 6 - 12 months time if you find that you still have the need buy a 2nd identical card new or 2nd hand to do the SLI thing.
