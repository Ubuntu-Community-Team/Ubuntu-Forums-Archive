### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2006-12-19
I really don't get the whole "Vista is a rip-off of OSX" thing. Sure, it all works in a pretty similar way but the fact is these are all modern conventions that have cropped up.
People want these things now! If Microsoft didn't include them, people would be on about how "Mac OS has a dashboard, Vista doesn't!"; they have to do it. It would have been smarter of them to maybe come up with something new, but I don't think there's really a huge amount more that can be done there besides virtual folders.

Besides that, Vista's sidebar is far more useful than Mac OS's. Vista applies the idea of Desklets, where the little applets are imediately there upon starting up. On the other hand, OSX's thing involves the Dashboard which is a whole mouse click away and does not feel at all integrated with the desktop.

Anyhow, what do I care? Ubuntu does all this and better :)
We have panel applets, desktop applets, virtual desktops, a fantastic window manager (with lots of power-user features that anyone can appreciate), *and* live search! (Superior live search, at that).

### Post by geoken on 2007-03-08
People should do some research and check out some screencaps and reviews of the early Longhorn/Vista builds. I think you guys will be surprised to learn that many of the so called 'stolen' or 'copied' features were present and functional in early Longhorn/Vista builds before they were ever announced in OS X. Index based searching, a sidebar with widgets, smart folders, etc were all present in Longhorn/Vista builds as early as '02. I understand their long development cycle allowed Apple to beat them to market, and Apple deserves credit for bringing these features to market first, but saying MS copied the features is patently false considering they were present in alpha/beta builds of the OS well before they were ever even announced for OS X.
