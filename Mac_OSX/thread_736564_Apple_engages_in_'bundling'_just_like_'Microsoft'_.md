### Post by aysiu on 2008-03-27
I don't think this makes Apple evil, but it certainly is an underhanded move--basically trying to trick Windows iTunes users into installing Safari by disguising it as a security update. Even if these users do not actually launch and use Safari (that is, they stick to IE or Firefox), Apple can then claim Safari for Windows has had X million downloads over the past two weeks... even if few of those downloads turned into actual use.

I think Prince did something like this--including copies of his new album with sales of tickets to his concerts in order to up the sales of his album and send it to the top of the charts. People were there to see Prince perform, not to get his over-the-hill new release. Likewise, Windows iTunes users are there at Apple Software Update to update existing software or get security patches, not to get previews of other beta Apple software ported to Windows.
