### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2008-11-17
In my own opinion, Mac OS is just like any other OS.  It has it's own fair share of quirks, charms and pitfalls.  But at the base of it, as several people have already pointed out, it's underpinnings are based on BSD UNIX, and therefore is superior in many ways to Microsoft Windows.  I personally am not a huge fan of OSX, but I like it a hell of a lot better than Windows.  I'm in the process of trying to draw my wife away from the Microsoft-Power-Teat, but to put it bluntly, Linux is far too complex for her.  And not to be condescending, I completely agree.  Linux is far to complex for the vast majority of computer users right now.
I'm not a huge fan of OSX, but it looks nice and is very "finished" looking.  It's very clean and tight, and doesn't have a lot of the loose ends and bugs that are prevalent in most Linux systems and even in Windows.  For what my wife and the rest of the average computer users in the world want from an OS it's damn near perfect.  It's easy to use, it's secure (no viruses and such) and when you get the iLife software package, it makes doing all the basic things that you may want to do like sending photos to family members, making home videos on DVD, or making your own website simple and straight forward.  Far too simple for my taste though.  But given the choice between being a slave to Microsoft or paying a premium price for a computer that is only marginally better or equal to an equivalent PC, it's a tough call.  I'm not a big fan of either option, but I don't like viruses much either.
I won't encourage you to do anything illegal, but if you did happen to want to try out the Mac OS before deciding to drop a considerable chunk of change on a real Mac, there are a lot of hacked OSX install iso's floating around the net on torrent sites that allow you to install OSX on a PC.  Results are hit or miss in terms of compatibility from what I understand, and obviously the closer your computer is to real Mac specs the smoother your install would go.

I think Ubuntu is the best operating system I've used hands down.  It's certainly the best value for the money (LOL).  By far the easiest to use and most developed and supported "free" Linux OS available.
A distant second choice in OS for me is OSX.  It's decent, pretty to look at and feels very professional and well delivered compared to most other OSes
And last and most certainly least IMHO is any distro of Windows.  I use XP pro SP3 because it's got a lot less problems than Vista, and if it weren't for the fact that there are just some things you can't do in Linux (yet) with Windows, I'm still reluctantly using Windows for such things as web camming with sound, or playing some games.  Cedega and Wine just don't cut it and they probably never will.  game developers will eventually take notice of Linux and start developing more software for the platform, but until then Microsoft still remains the king in that area.
