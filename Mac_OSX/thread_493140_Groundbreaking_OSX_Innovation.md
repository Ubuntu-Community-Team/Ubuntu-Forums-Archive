### Post by Quillz on 2007-07-05
While Spaces isn't anything new, I think the way that you work with them is. Whereas on Linux, you usually just have an icon in place on the taskbar, Spaces goes a bit deeper. You can have a Dock icon or a menu bar icon, and you can also map entire keyboard shortcuts. On my Leopard beta, for example, I have Cmd+1-4 and Cmd+arrow keys mapped, so I can quickly get to whatever Space I want by toggling the arrow keys or hitting 1-4. In addition, you can press F8 to open all the Spaces at once, and then F9 to toggle Expose, so you can quickly see every single application open in every single Space, and then drag-and-drop them at will. This makes working with the Spaces very efficient, this is what I think sets Spaces apart from the typical virtual desktops of Linux.
