### Post by blippy on 2008-02-17
I own an iMac 20" with Tiger, purchased April 2007. For a little more than what I paid for my iMac, you can now get a whopping 24-incher.

iMacs are elegant pieces of hardware. My mum marvelled at how trim it all looked. Look at YouTube - there are many people who post vids of their proud new iMac. I saw a workmate with a Mac laptop, and saw how good it looked. Laptops are not my thing, but checking out the Mac website, I realised what a good idea putting everything into one box was - without all the wires'n'**** all over the place - if you'll forgive my French. It's up to you, of course - if the hardware doesn't turn you on emotionally, then I'd say "don't bother". 

As regards choice of OS ... that's a toughie. I've never used Vista - but a workmate who has describes it as, *ahem*, "less than impressive". Looks nice, though. Vista seems to be so uniformly panned that I for one am not rushing to try it out.

So it's a toss-up between OSX and Ubuntu. I think the choice you make is a personal one. If you buy an iMac, then you should at least give OS X a fair go! Maybe a good idea is for you to go into an Apple shop, and see for yourself if you like it. If you're not sufficiently "wowed" - then you kind of have your answer right there.

I'd say the pros of OSX are that it's highly polished. iMovie and GarageBand are way cool. I bought a piece of software called RapidWeaver - an OSX-only piece of software that was excellent at authoring a website. It came with nice themes that you could change. Also, with an iMac you can play DVDs with your remote control. That can't fail to draw a series of oos-and-aahs. You'll definitely be the coolest kid on the block if you own an iMac ;)

Buying an iMac was the closest thing I've got to re-experiencing the nostalgia of my old Amiga days (apart from running AROS). You feel that you're running something just that bit more special and cooler than the standard choices.

It's not all pros, though. I'm currently typing this message from Ubuntu, for example. I can boot into OSX, but I rarely do. Being a developer, I tend to have something of a "UNIX mindset", and distrust the way that OSX secretes files away in places that I don't know about. I like to be in control. OSX has other niggles, too ... like how I can get my Quickcam Pro 5000 to work under Ubuntu but not OSX. I also like lots of freeware - and here I think Ubuntu wins outright. Linux freeware will most likely be much more current than the OSX offerings, and I never have figured out what the deal is between MacForge/Darwin/MacPorts. 

Ubuntu gets better with each release - although often it's a case of 2 steps forward, 2 steps back. I'd like to see a lot of the elitism go in Linux, and see Ubuntu become a really really cool desktop machine. Imagine if an Amiga OS and OS X mated - that's how cool it should be.
