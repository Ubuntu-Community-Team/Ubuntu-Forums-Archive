Please go [here]("http://wiki.mythbuntu.org/mythbuntu/contributing") for more detailed information on joining a specific team and the different team jobs.

[/LIST]

[LIST]
[*]Try to confirm bugs (try to reproduce the error)
[*]Assign bugs to the right team (ie. who can fix it)
[/LIST]
[*]Coders
[LIST]
[*]Code Applications
[*]Fix Bugs
[*]Implement Blueprints (features)
[/LIST]

[LIST]
[*]Write documentation
[*]Test documentation/file bugs
[*]Fix documentation bugs
[/LIST]
[*]Reviewers
[LIST]
[*]Write reviews about Mythbuntu and get it placed somewhere (blogs, etc)
[/LIST]
[*]Testers
[LIST]
[*]Test applications/releases
[*]Usability testing
[*]Write blueprints (feature requests)
[*]File bugs
[/LIST]
[*]etc
[LIST]
[*]Have another skill not listed?  PM me, I'm sure I missed something that could be a great help.
[/LIST]
[/LIST]

If you are interested in helping in any of these areas please either PM me or come to #ubuntu-mythtv-dev on irc.freenode.net or reply here




CALL FOR HELP:  One of our developers is overworked IRL, we are looking for someone to take over the weekly builds.  The builds are automatic, but if for some reason they don't build, this person would need to figure out why.   PM me or superm1 for info.

Great :) 

MythTV is written in C++. If you have an itch you'd like to scratch concerning MythTV itself, this wiki page might be useful: [http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Development_guide](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Development_guide)


The MythBuntu additions are mostly written in Python, which is not hard to learn at all. Of course, shell scripts are used as well. We're looking for people to help with refactoring of Mythbuntu-Control-Centre. 

Sometimes it's not about code, though. We often need to figure out how things work so we can automate them (and do so in python or shell scripts).

Of course, testing and bug reports are also appreciated.
