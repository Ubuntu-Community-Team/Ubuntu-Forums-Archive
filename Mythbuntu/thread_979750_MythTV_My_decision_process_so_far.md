### Post by SiHa on 2008-11-12
No-one ever built a MythTV system because it was cheap.:)

Admittedly, my initial desire was simply to build a 'Linux-Based DVR' because I didn't want to have to shell out for another Windows license.

Then I entered the world of MythTV and separate Backends and Frontends.

Approx £300 - £400 later, I have a combined BE/FE in the loft (FE serves the master bedroom) with three tuners (SD), and remote FE in the lounge.

The thing is, MythTV is sooo much more than a DVR. It's aim is to be the Mythical convergence of all the home media systems into one. And I personally think it's doing quite well at it.

To be fair though, if you just want a combined BE/FE for SD DVB-T, you could put together a reasonable system for < £150 (that's GBP btw) if you're prepared to go second-hand. 
Both my tuner cards, I got for <£30 and the FE Mobo & CPU for £14 off eBay.

### Post by newlinux on 2008-11-12
Comparing myth to a dvr is apples to oranges. You control most everything in myth and can easily add functionality and many 3rd party apps. In  DVR the you don't usually have good control of the everything (especially the content due to DRM) and you are at the mercy of the DVR's provider most of the time for increased functionality and 3rd party apps.

That said You didn't say what hardware you were looking at and their costs. We can probably help you with that.

My first couple of mythboxes started with computers I already had and were getting old by Windows standards, and they made perfect mythboxes, so in their case cost wasn't a factor (already purchased) which is one advantage of Linux. Not counting my laptops I have 5 mythboxes (all HD capable dual frontend/backend) and none of them cost more than $250 US. I built almost all of them with used parts.

How much are you looking to spend?

### Post by Caps18 on 2008-11-12
The options for two tuner HD DVRs with large hard drives wasn't as good as it is now.  TiVo and the cable companies have caught up.  

My thing was I wanted a system to just get over-the-air HD, and not have to pay a monthly fee for it ($20/year I can live with).  TiVo requires an internet connection or a land phone line, neither of which I regularly have.  And I didn't need cable or satellite TV, I have enough problems keeping up with the OTA stuff I record now.

So, while it did cost money (I wasn't expecting to get a two tuner HD capable DVR for nothing), I don't think about it now because it works so well.  It has freed me from the TV schedule so I can do whatever I need to do in life without worrying about catching a TV show.  On top of the normal computer price, I added two pcHD5500 tuners, 2 TB of hard drive space (only have 1TB working right now...), SATA card, 2GB RAM, and a nvidia 6800 graphics board.  It did take a few months to get all of the bugs worked out and get a good looking skin for the player (pear-TV but with the mythbuntu logo instead of the pear)

(There are a few issues I haven't had time to work on or don't know enough to make it simple...adding hard drive, networking, mythvideo organization... so it isn't as easy as plug & play all the time.  But it works great when it's on a dedicated computer with enough hard drive space.
