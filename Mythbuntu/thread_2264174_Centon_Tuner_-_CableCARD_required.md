### Post by TheFu on 2015-02-06
My CATV provider started encrypting all channels - even local broadcast channels - about 3 yrs ago. In 2010, they began by encrypting all but the local broadcast stations and shopping channels. If the channels on your cable system are encrypted-QAM too, then yes, you need CableCARD from the CATV provider. Any TV with a QAM tuner can test this - TVs made for the North American market in the last 8 yrs probably do have this tuner. Just connect the CATV coax to the TV, set the TV tuner for "digital cable" and have it scan for channels. If you get any, then those are the channels you'd get without CableCARD until your CATV provider decides to encrypt everything too.

I don't have a Ceton - don't know if it works with "clear QAM" or not.  What does the spec sheet say?
Also - I thought CableCARD DRM only worked with Windows media center. Am I wrong?
