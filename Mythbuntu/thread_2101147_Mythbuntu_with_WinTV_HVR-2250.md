### Post by jlp_engineer on 2013-02-06
When you do get it working, I would be interested to know whether both the analog and digital tuners are working, and if there are any known issues with either.  More than half of the content that my local provider sends me over cable is analog, so it is still a large part of any MythTV setup for me.

HDTV is nice, but with almost all of digital cable being encrypted, it becomes less important due to sheer number of channels that I am able to freely record via analog.  I would go the route of getting an HDHR or a Ceton with a cable card, but I am not even certain that any content could be utilized by my MythTV backends because of the Copy Freely flags.  

I think we are all stuck for as long as the cable companies cannot wrap their heads around the primary way all the content providers get paid for their channels - advertising.  Advertising income impacts their analog content as well as their HDTV content, but I would think that those advertisers would want their message viewed by the widest audience possible, as easily as possible.  Imagine the difference, if your ads were viewed in HD by anyone that had a flat panel TV, not just the ones with a digital tuner box leased from the cable company.  I am sure that the folks at the History Channel or the Discovery Channel would lover a wider audience.
