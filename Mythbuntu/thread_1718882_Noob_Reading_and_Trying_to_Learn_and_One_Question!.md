Basically I am a noob to computers, and am hoping to eventually set up a Mythbuntu PVR system. I am almost 60, so I will never have any appreciable computer expertise, but this linux thing is getting easier for everyone, so it seems!

QUESTION: From the FAQ for MythTV main page here: [http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Frequently_Asked_Questions](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Frequently_Asked_Questions) it states:

[B][I]I upgraded X, Y, and Z and now everything is broken. Help!

The one piece of advice I can most often give Myth users is to keep it simple. Distribution packages, automatic installers, and many other Linux packaging features have made it very easy to get a MythTV system installed by even the most novice MythTV user. This does not mean that you should arbitrarily upgrade on a whim.

MythTV is designed as a PVR appliance, first and foremost. Once it is working, it should be hands off, even for what seems the most innocent of upgrades. As tinkerers, most of us can't resist installing the latest this, or the newest that. But more often than not, a mass upgrade of every component on your system leads to trouble. 
[/I][/B]

So, what upgrades is the author speaking of?

Updates to New Mythbuntu releases will mean needing reconfiguration, right?

What else, would the author(s) be speaking of, that an upgrade would break it?

### Post by LowSky on 2011-04-01
As with many linux users I have a tendency to tinker. Tinkering with thing I know little about. this can sometime lead to things breaking. And that is why disclaimers like the one you mention are written.

One of the saying in the mythtv community of users is, "if it aint broke don't fix it".
 and updating to new versions of mythbunu or mythtv doesnt always mean new reconfiguring. my system was updated from .23 to .24 with no issue.

the best rule to fllow is to rad every line in the documentation. do not skip steps and measure twice and cut once.
