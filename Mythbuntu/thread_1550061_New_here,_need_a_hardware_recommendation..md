### Post by LowSky on 2010-08-10
TV1 TV2? Sorry not sure what you mean by that. 

My HD PVR is hooked up directly to my cable box using RGB component cables. The HD PVR is then connected to my PC using USB. It can record up to 1080i. MythTV can use it for LiveTV if you like, it will work. Because the HD PVR has a pass through you can watch directly from your TV's normal mode if you like as well. Just be careful when recording, if you change channels in the middle of recording using the receivers remote it will record whatever the satellite  receiver is tuned to. 

I think it can change channels, but I use a different means to change channels so I can't comment directly. According to this it can with a few extra steps: [http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HD-PVR](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HD-PVR). That link has all the info on getting the HD PVR working.

Just to point out, If you just looking to record TV and not upload other media, you might be better off just getting a Dish Network DVR or Tivo.

### Post by LowSky on 2010-08-11
blubill if you are willing to learn and spend money on hardware then MythTV can do all those things. I've been using MythTV for 2 years or so and I am still learning new things all the time. Not knowing if you have used Ubuntu or Linux, I will say it can be a learning curve coming from Windows where things have a tendency to "just work".

If you just want things to work without much fuss, then buying or renting a DVR from your Television providor might be the best option. Most providors offer models that can allow you to watch one thing and record another, or record two things at once.

As for playing downloaded media. depending on you have downloaded those files, the [Western Digital WD TV Live HD Media Player]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136472&cm_re=wd_tv_live-_-22-136-472-_-Product") might be a great option.
