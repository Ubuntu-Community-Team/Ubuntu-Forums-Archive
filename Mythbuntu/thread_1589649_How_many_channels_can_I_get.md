just because your cable subscription give you over 200 channels doesn't mean a tuner card can watch them. most cable companies encrypt their channels so you need a cable box for them.

more than likely if you hook a digital tuner up to a straight cable connection you will only get about 5-20 channels that are availible using QAM, mostly the local broadcasters like nbc, abc, cbs, and fox, and so on.

if you want all your channels you will need to look at a device like the hauppauge hd-pvr which can connect to your cable box and record off that.

### Post by klc5555 on 2010-10-07
> **jrd said:**
> So basically I can't build a dvr that will function like a comcast dvr (record one channel while watching another etc) and support all my channels. That's a bummer.

Not strictly true.  

Rent multiple STBs or DTAs  and use with multiple capture devices, and record the encrypteds simultaneously from each of them. In addition the clear QAMs on your cable (all 5-to-40 of them, depending on area) can be recorded directly and independently of the others.

I use 7 tuners (on multiple backends)with Comcast to record up to 7 streams simultaneously. Four are for digital on the clear QAM channels, 3 are capture devices hooked to 3 settop devices to cover the encrypteds that are included in my Comcast subscription. Everything runs through one remote control (which also controls Hulu desktop and stored DVDs on the main machines). Everything recorded from TV plays on every TV screen, computer, or laptop in the house, in the garage, or in the middle of the backyard. As does live TV.

Can that sort of thing be done nowadays with a Comcast DVR?
