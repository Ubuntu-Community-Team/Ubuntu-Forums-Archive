### Post by klc5555 on 2010-08-17
Since, except for the locals, whatever channels Comcast switched over from analog to digital in your area are (or certainly soon will be) low-level encrypted, you'll likely need to get directly from Comcast whichever STB they're distributing in your area for standard digital. If that's the level of reception you wish to continue with. Comcast is unlikely to be willing to activate any STB or DTA you may acquire from any 3rd-party source and attempt to connect. Other content-providers in the US are equally restrictive.

On the plus side, if they're transitioning your old analog cable account over to digital, Comcast may well be offering you a deal wherein your first device (STB, and possibly one or two DTAs in addition) are provided rental free. Check with your local Comcast office.

The Pace DC50X DTA is pretty much the standard digital transport adapter for Comcast's former analog customers, and works quite smoothly nowadays in Mythtv with LIRC. I use three of them. The output quality from this (admittedly cheap) DTA is better than from the old analog cable signal, but less than the same standard digital channel would have via clear QAM if Comcast did not encrypt it. 

For Comcast's standard digital (not HD) STBs, a brief but useful forum discussion may be found here: [http://www.avsforum.com/avs-vb/showthread.php?t=1116481](http://www.avsforum.com/avs-vb/showthread.php?t=1116481)
