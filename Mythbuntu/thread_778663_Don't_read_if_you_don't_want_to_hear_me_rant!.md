### Post by mozetti on 2008-05-02
Well, just because it's got a *buntu tacked on the end doesn't mean it's necessarily Ubuntu. That being said, 8.04 seems to have its share of problems anyhow.

The advantage of Mythbuntu is it makes it a little easier to have a Myth setup versus installing vanilla Ubuntu and then adding Myth. But, Myth is a complex system and isn't just a matter of installing it like you would Firefox or Exaile.

The 7.10 upgrade that broke the ATI HDA SB notwithstanding, I wouldn't have made many changes to my Myth setup once I got it running. I wouldn't avoid running updates, but there are alot of details included in the Update Manager descriptions. From there, you can get an idea of whether an update might break your system. 

For example, language packs are benign so I'd update them when they become available.  But if an update is changing a package dealing with video or audio then it might cause problems and I'd wait a week or two to see if there's any fallout on the boards. Or, you could just decide to only install security-related updates unless you know an update is going to add or improve a feature you use or have been waiting for -- then I'd still wait for any fallout if it's critical Myth.

Finally, similar to how like many people dislike the fact that MS forces its users to upgrade, there's no rule that says you have to update to the latest version of Ubuntu. Especially when it's first released. I've got 8.04 installed on my laptop just to see how it goes, but since the problems with Hardy have been reported I decided to stay at Gutsy on my desktop for awhile.

Oh, and a disk image wouldn't be a bad idea. ;)
