### Post by Chris Musampa on 2010-04-28
I recently spent a fair amount of time pouring over specs for a reasonably priced laptop that had to linux compatible as well as being windows games capable (not high end as I don't game that much), the specialist linux ones seem overpriced for the specs so I settled on the machine in my sig.  It has nvidia graphics (I didn't fancy fighting with ATI) and Atheros wireless chipset so is linux friendly (everything works OOB with kubuntu 9.10) and plays crysis at medium settings.  The build quality seems excellent (Asus also come top in a laptop reliability survey published by a company who underwrite warranties), the 1600x900 display gives a little extra desktop real estate which is always helpful and it manages to somehow stay cool in front of the keyboard so your hands don't get sweaty :)

It doesn't come with linux preinstalled but it installs with no hitches.  In short I can't recommend it enough.

### Post by Objekt on 2010-04-28
I am not in the market for a notebook/laptop, but I can give broad recommendations by brand, based on recent experience (2008-present):

Acer: AVOID at all costs.  "Acer" is a Chinese word for "junk."  I learned this through owning three Acer netbooks (long story!) and one Acer monitor, all of which died or malfunctioned in some way within a few months of buying them.  100% of my Acer purchases have had to be repaired or replaced at least once.  I can't imagine their full-size notebook offerings are any better.

Dell Latitude line: Very well-built and worth the money.  I've used these for years at my job & seen few failures.

Alienware: Overpriced garbage.  Much to our later regret, we bought 11 of these where I work in early 2008.  Just under 2 years later, only two are still in service.  The rest broke, both within and outside the warranty period.  Not at all worth the ~$4000 each we paid for them.
