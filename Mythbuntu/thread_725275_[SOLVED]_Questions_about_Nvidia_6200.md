### Post by volkswagner on 2008-03-15
I have a similar card (non-low profiles vesion).  The sivid works well.  I purchased this card in hopes of HD.  Unfortuneatley my TV will only accept HD via component input. I suggest to allow mythbuntu install the drivers, and enable the restricted drivers during install.  Don't try to install drivers manually.

I think the statement concerning svid unable to take full advantage may be true.  This may be that the 6200 may be able to display HD if the screen will accept the resolution via DVI, or VGA.  This won't be possible via svid.

Spend your money wisely.  You may be just as happy with an Nvida mx440, second hand on ebay.  Your rig probably won't be able to handle HD anyway.

Just my two cents.

### Post by nasha on 2008-03-16
I have a 6200 in one of my frontends... Has no issues displaying HD content, but im unable to test the Svideo connection, because i dont have an Svideo compatible tv anywhere. I use the DVI, and a DVI-HDMI cable.

As the previous poster suggested, im not sure whether your spec machine will be upto the job... It certainly wont be able to handle multiple HD recordings as well as a few streams.

Spending the money now on a working, reasonably spec'd machine will save you a lot of hassles in the long run. Not to mention a longer lifespan 'in theory'.

When installing, select the nVidia proprietary drivers, and thats the best hope you have at getting the Svideo to do what you want.

Good luck with your setup :) Any further questions, PM me,
Nasha
