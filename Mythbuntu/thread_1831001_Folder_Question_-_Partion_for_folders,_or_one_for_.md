Hi All,

Should I format separate partitions on my RAID5 for each media folder - recordings, videos, and music?  Or should I simply have one large partition for the whole shot, and create the folders (and shares, etc.) from there?  I was worried about efficiency and ease of use.

------

Originally I have it set for 3 x 1.5TB (videos - 2TB; music - 1TB) and 3 x 1.0TB (recordings - 2TB); two partitions and one partition respectively.  I finally got MegaRAID Storage Manager working on my MythBuntu 10.04LTS 64-Bit machine (with 0.23.1+fixes) to expand my RAID5 on the Perc 5i installed.  As a result, I can now move forward to expand my storage options.  The plan is 4 x 1.5TB for everything, maybe adding a fifth disk.  This allows me to dump the 3 1.0TB drives, as my case maxes out at 7 drives (I have a separate system drive I plan on replacing with a SSD and a DVD drive), and offer some future storage expansion options.

Please let me know your thoughts on 1 partition for all, or to continue partitioning for each media folder?  My guess, especially with current and future expansion, is that it is easiest to have one partition, that way only one partition is needed to expand, and all folders can share the extra storage depending on needs.  However, was thinking that access across the board might benefit from the usage spread across the separate channels.

I have 2 frontends running, with one master backend that houses all of the media.  Recordings are done via HDHomeRun (2 tuners via FiOS).  Two additional laptops and one desktop (Windoze machines) draw media (mainly music, but desktop also passes burned ISOs) from the backend.  That probably doesn't tax the server enough (it's just the wife, daughter, and I).
