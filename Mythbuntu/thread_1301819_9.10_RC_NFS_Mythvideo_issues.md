### Post by johnnybirdman on 2009-10-26
I currently have my music and videos stored on a freeNAS server and allow nfs shares from there to my network.
I have several other Ubuntu 9.04/9.10/8.04 desktops running and NFS shares are working.

Mythbuntu setup: combo front/back
I loaded 9.10 RC this weekend. I don't do any TV recording and simply use mythbuntu for a media player for the networked media.

I setup in /etc/fstab NFS share mounts for both my music and videos.  The odd part is that the music is seen by mythbuntu and the videos are not.  I mount with.
```
192.168.xxx.xxx:/mnt/to/media/video /var/lib/mythtv/videos nfs rsize=8192,wsize=8192,timeo=14,intr
```
I used the same line and changed it for music.

Both mount sucessfully and if I got to /var/lib/mythtv/video or music in Thunar I see the files.

In mythbuntu I noted that in
> Utilities/setup/media setting/music settings/general settings
There is a "Directly to hold music" which is populated with /var/lib/mythtv/music/

However, in the 
> Utilities/setup/media setting/video setting/general setting
The "Directory that hold videos" was empty after initial install.  Then after I _manually _entered /var/lib/mythtv/videos/ the video manager shoes nothing and there are no videos to play.

Anyone have anything similar or any solutions?
Thanks,
J.
