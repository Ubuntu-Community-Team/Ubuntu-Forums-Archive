### Post by larsolav on 2008-09-24
I don't know what is better, but using Mythbuntu is much easier. Mythbuntu also comes with the Mythbuntu control center which makes changing settings much easier than when installing Myth on a Ubuntu. I installed Myth on Ubuntu 6.10 before Mythbuntu was available, and could not get a lot of things to work, but then I installed Mythbuntu 7.10 and most of the stuff I care about worked out of the box.

Also, If you are using it as a dedicated HTPC and not also as a desktop computer you may be better off with Mythbuntu because Mythbuntu is a stripped down version of Ubuntu (I think) and does not clog the machine with the typical desktop applications you don't need for a HTPC.

Hope this answers some of your questions.

Cheers!

Edit:
You can decide yourself what art work appears when you scroll the video menu. You can download the information on the dvd from IMDB by telling myth to look up the database and it will also download the poster on IMDB. If you don't like that poster, you can just upload the image you want to the directory on your computer with the poster and change the metadata for the DVD to display your other image or poster.

### Post by newlinux on 2008-09-24
You can do hierarchys by putting storing them that way in your directory structure. For instance I have whole seasons of BattleStar galactica and various other shows and movies (like all the Star Wars episodes) in their own directories by show and by season, and that is the way myth displays them (In myth I would Select TV-Shows, Battlestar Galactica, Season 1 and get then see a list of season 1 episodes because this is how I have them stored on disk). Yes, it has parental PINs.

for more info on mythvideo:

[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php?title=MythVideo](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php?title=MythVideo)


Also, if you decide to go with a standard ubuntu install, you can just install mythbuntu-control-centre and then do most of the rest of the myth install and configuring from there. This is how I did it because I use my machines as a bit more than dedicated mythboxes. You can still user a lighter weight desktop environment with an ubuntu install if you want.
