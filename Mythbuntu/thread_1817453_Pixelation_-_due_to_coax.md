[FONT=Palatino Linotype]Hi Folks!

Had had recent problems with one of my tuners acting up ==> recorded programmes getting noisy (pixelation) and most recently short-recording (quits recording because of loss of signal for too long a time).  Ends up the center conductor of the coax had moved: one end was sticking out about a half inch beyond the outside flange and the other end was barely sticking out inside the flange.  (This is a commercially-manufactured piece of cable about 18" long.)  Used a pair of needle-nose pliers to pull the short end out -- was surprised how easily it slid!   Reconnected, making sure the center conductor hadn't moved.  Hopefully my recording problem is corrected!

I'm posting this and marking it as 'solved' to remind other users to check for the not-so-obvious things.  I've been using this configuration for approximately a year; didn't move cables and yet the problem started about a week ago.
[/FONT]
