### Post by Kawayanan on 2008-01-12
Well, it would be nice, but I REALLY doubt they will allow it.  I believe that to make a "open" device that can communicate with them on their new protocol, they would have to certify it.  I suspect they would not certify a device that lets you do things Mythtv does.

I'm not sure how this is different from CableCard.  CableCard was supposed to allow you to make devices that could use a CableCard, and not be stuck with the provided cable boxes.  In practice, for a device to use a CableCard, it has to be certified by the CableCard people (who probably happen to be the cable operators).  I think they only certify devices that won't let you do things they don't want you to do.  Like the HD Tivo for example (it uses a CableCard, but is set up so removing the video is hard - or at least supposed to).  Their idea of "open" is to allow you to make new hardware, provided they still get to say what you can and can't do with it.
