### Post by mookie60 on 2010-04-16
I've had a 1600 for about a year.   Works ok with 9.04, not 9.10.

One thing I've learned is that it's very sensitive to signal strength.  For instance, I've just relocated to a new residence and it would only pick up a few digital (cable unencrypted qam) channels.  I messed with all kinds of arrangements trying to split things as little as possible . . . fewer splits always improved things, but was not really practical.

I had the local cable company come and checkout the line.  They found a poor connection coming into the house, and verified that the wall connection (at the mythbox) had good signal strenght.   With the bad connection repaired things definitely improved; very good in fact if I went straight from the wall to the 1600 digital connection.  The problem is, I have two tuner cards (the Haup.1600 & Haup. 150).  So when I come out of the wall I have to split 1 into 4 (2 analog connections, 1 digital, 1 back to the tv).

As soon as I made the spliter connections, the 1600 (digital) picked up fewer channels.  Ultimately, I needed a 12db amp (Phillips from Lowes) right before a 2 way splitter (1 output to the digital connection, and the other to a 4 way for everything else).   This seems to be sufficient to lock all my digital channels again, and the extra power has improved image on both the 1600 analog and the 150.

Bottom line:  my 1600 can function ok, but it needs a reallly good signal.
