Thats fairly typical of fanless power supplies, they tend to run hot. 

If you ever want a quiet power supply that doesnt get really hot and can still deliever a serious amount of wattage, try a seasonic X650 gold. They run fanless at low loads, and the fan ramps up as loads increase. 

BTW, heatsinks are the same, they only dissapate heat. You probably already knew, but its suprising how many people dont know that. ;)   

@ anarkhos- LowSkyis just undervolting fans (well, possibly he is undervolting other stuff as well, but thats all thats mentioned in this thread). 

Its a pretty easy trick, you dont have to runfans at 12volts, and lowering the voltage lowers the spinning speed, which lowers noise......while still giving at least some airflow, stopping the parts from cooking. Running fans at 7v or 5v is easy, if you want to run different voltages you'll need a fan speed controller. 

You cant 'undervoltage' power supplies. 

I've run an undervoltaged CPU (+ a minor overclocked at the same time!) for ages. If the motherboard hadnt died, I would still be running that computer. I put down the motherboard death to virtually nill airflow in the case. It was water cooled with an external radiator, fanless video card, fanless northbridge, no intake or exit fans......the only fan in the main case was in the power supply.
