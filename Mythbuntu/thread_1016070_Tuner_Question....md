### Post by tgm4883 on 2008-12-19
I think I know what you are trying to ask, and to clarify, Digital != HD.

You are better off getting a digital tuner and not an analog one.  The digital changeover that happens in the US in Feb 09 affects only broadcast (OTA).  However, IIRC that does not mean that your cable company will continue to broadcast an analog signal (they may be mandated to do it until 2012, but i'm not sure about that).

It may also be difficult to find a analog tuner now.  Just go with a digital one or a hybrid.

### Post by 4dognight on 2008-12-19
Both. HD is HD, doesnt matter where you get it from.

Cable or Satellite doesnt mean you are getting HD, on all your channels. Digital doesnt mean its HD. So, to answer your question. You do not need an HD tuner, just because your getting cable or satellite. But if you are **expecting** to watch/record channels in HD, then yes you will need an HD tuner, whether its satellite, cable or over the Air.
