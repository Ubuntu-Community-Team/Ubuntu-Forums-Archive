### Post by MonsterMaxx on 2009-04-13
Can't help you on that capture card, but as someone else who doesn't have a HD TV I'd say go with the cheaper stuff until you upgrade the TV. Also your machine will need to be a LOT faster to handle HD than if you use the 'old stuff'. Since your TV isn't HD, you won't be able to tell the difference.

Connection to your TV is done via the video card. Most video cards today have s-video out, some of the better ones add hdmi too. Historically the Nvidia stuff is much better supported by Linux than ATI is. Buy as much video card as you can afford. $150 would get it done.

I've built all my DVRs out of leftover computer parts, but if you are buying new stuff, for sure go for a dual core 64 bit capable stuff. I've had good luck with Asus on the consumer stuff and Supermicro on the business/server stuff. The Supermicro tends to not have as much cutting edge goodies, but often has better stability.

hope that helps
