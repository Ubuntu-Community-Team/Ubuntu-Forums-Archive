### Post by newlinux on 2008-02-23
Yeah, a few cards can capture both:

Kworld ATSC 110/115
pchdtv 5500
Dvico fusion 5
and many more...
Here is an incomplete list, but the most comprehensive one I know of:

[http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/ATSC_Devices](http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/ATSC_Devices)

Before you get digital cable, you could try tuning the OTA ATSC channels with one of these cards to get digital recordings...
Please note, the chances of you being able to tune all the digital stations you receive with a tuner card is small. You can only tune unencrypted digital stations with a tuner card, which for most people is their locals (in HD and sometimes in SD as well) and a few other stations. To get the other stations you'll need to feed the cable box analog output to an analog input (like the ones on the Hauppage PVR-150s) - but your HD stations will be downconverted to SD this way. You can also look into firewire... but that's a whole different topic. Just an FYI for digital cable. You can get all of your basic analog cable channels with any NTSC tuner supported in linux.
