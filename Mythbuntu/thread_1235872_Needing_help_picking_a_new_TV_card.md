Single or double tuner? PCI or PCI-e?

Among the single tuner PCI cards, the PCHDTV-5500 is a reasonable and traditional choice and comes as close to working "out-of-the-box" as anything ever does in Linux. A tad pricey, but less so now than formerly, having been price-dropped a bit from $114 to $99.

Cheap Phillips-tuner cards (like the K-World 110 or 115, or the AverMedia 180 also work very well and can be had frequently for $40 - $80, but are frequently fairly tricky to set up the first time.

Hauppauges (PCI and PCI-e) like the 1600, etc. are recently becoming popular. But their driver support seems a bit spotty, and some of these cards, like the 1600, seem to require exceptional signal strength to function well in digital.

The gold standard among HD tuners is probably the network tuner the HD HomeRun by Silicon Dust. 

Everybody has his own preference. Consult the capture card pages on the Mythtv wiki. They'll help you make an informed choice.
