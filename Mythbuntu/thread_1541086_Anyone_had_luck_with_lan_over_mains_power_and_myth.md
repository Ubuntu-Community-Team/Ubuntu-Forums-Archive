### Post by newlinux on 2010-07-29
I have tried (And still have) them all (DS2, Homeplug AV, wireless N, wireless G). Wireless N works best for me. The second generation powerline adapters work well (I've had netgear ones and currently have linksys ones) but, like wireless, can be affected by the environment (how your powerlines are setup, how much noise is on them, did you turn on the microwave, etc.). So both  fall in the YMMV category, but both can work. I get very different speeds with my powerline adapters depending on where in my house they are plugged in.

### Post by newlinux on 2010-07-30
All I can say is when it comes to HD streaming, keep your receipts when buying powerline or wireless equipment to rely on. There are so many factors with whether or not it will work well enough in your home, regardless of what anyone or any review says. You could take the exact same units that are working well in one home (wireless or powerline) and they may not get the job done in another home. This is also true if you kept things in the same home and just changed rooms. Both technologies are in the YMMV category.
