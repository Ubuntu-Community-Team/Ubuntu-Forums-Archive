### Post by LowSky on 2010-09-30
Best way to figure what track you need to rip is to start the movie in VLC or another player. See what track number it uses for the film and then directly point the ripper to that track. It seems to work on a good many films.

Some movies will not work at all in ripping you have another option and it is to cache the video form the dvd. once the film is chached you can then transcode it to a smaller container in many cases.

### Post by David Grigor on 2010-10-04
I personally prefer to use handbrake and create mkv files since they take up less space and still look great in mythvideo. 

k9copy though works well for creating ISO. For Disney you need to play the movie using VLC first and display the title to find out the #. Then you can usually use K9copy to rip that particular title # to ISO format.
