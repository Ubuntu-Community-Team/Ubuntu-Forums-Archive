### Post by volkswagner on 2008-06-14
Are you looking for a MOBO all in one solution?  Most video cards with s-video out include an adapter.  The adapter will allow you to use your composite input on the TV.  Just make sure the video card is supported by linux and includes the s-video to composite adapter.


Do you have any existing hardware?  Seems like a good idea to make sure your investment will allow for future upgrades, like HDTV.

### Post by allene222 on 2008-06-14
You can get a cheap adapter if you already have svideo out of  your video card or if your favorite card does not come with one.  They rage from $3 online to $25 at Radio Shack.  Google "svideo to composite adapter" to find some.  I have used some and they work great.  Fry's has them for not much money.  It is hard to tell the difference between the composite and the svideo signals.  The difference is "dot crawl", which is noticeable if you are looking for it but not really otherwise.  In other words, if you have not been bothered by dot crawl all these years on your TV, you won't mind sticking with composite, your wife will be happy, and your marriage saved.
