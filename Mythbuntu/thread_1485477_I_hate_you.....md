### Post by iamlindoro on 2010-05-18
> **LowSky said:**
> Schedules Direct is $20 a year...

You can also say its his time in repairing, and money in parts.

Its a shame the OP really doesn't get into his problem, but it could be anything.

Schedules Direct is not a service provided by MythTV, nor does paying for listings data entitle anyone to make unrealistic demands on the MythTV developers, who are themselves hobbyists developing the software for forum, in their (our) spare time.

Open source means you are free to use it for nothing, modify it for nothing, and release your changes for nothing.  It also means that you are not "locked in" and you are free to go use something else.  It does *not* entitle you to demand any more than we are able to give while balancing real life against our hobby.  It's an unfortunate tendency to see the end result (the release) and extrapolate what our priorities and decision making process was based on that.  Generally speaking, people get what our priorities and decision making process were completely wrong, but are all too happy to hold it against us.  They also have a tendency to single out some feature that they find essential and deduce that the developers don't care about the feature, or that we are willfully ignoring the need for it.  Sometimes we just need to postpone some fix or feature because doing it right is more important than doing it right now.

So the end result is, you can reasonably a) use something else, b) get involved in the development so that you can see why things happen the way they do, c) submit bug reports for anything that is broken (and missing features are not bugs) and we'll do our best to fix them on our own schedule and when our lives allow.  You can not (reasonably) demand the same prompt attention that you do from a paid product.  We are not slaves, we are not employees, and every one of us has a real life that comes first.  We do this because it's fun, and remember that we are actually far, far more motivated to polish our work than the users are, in general-- it's just that when it comes to fixing a bug that doesn't affect us balanced against our spouses, our mortgages, or our children, the latter will win every single time.

If you'd like to post to the MythTV users list with specific issues and not generalized ranting, we'll do our best to address your specific configuration issues and/or bugs.
