### Post by zansatsu0 on 2010-01-30
I'm typing this message from my Adesso Media Center KB with integrated Trackball and I'm enjoying it.

Brief Layout:
The trackball is positioned on the right for the right thumb (its probably a little awkward for left-handers) with a right click mouse button trigger for your right index. The left side has left and right mouse buttons for the left thumb and a mouse wheel for the left index.

Brief Setup:
The media keys are instantly recognized in 9.10 and I had no setup time as the keyboard uses generic HID signals and was fully Plug and Play. The wireless is a little weak, but I have good functionality with a USB extension cable (not included) and the transmitter placed and hidden, but in open air.

Conclusion:
I'm also a gamer and use Linux for gaming (yes it can be done). I have a second KB and mouse that is geared for that purpose. But for surfing and fiddling with my computer from anywhere in the room, I'd definitely say that the Adesso keyboard is well suited. Think of the keyboard layout like a laptop keyboard: it has a function (Fn) key that allows you to use extended keyboard keys, but it's a pain when you forget numlock is on and you have to disable it because your typing starts to look like th5s when y64 f6rget t6 d5sab3e. I'm not fond of the Fn key, but the utility of the keyboard as a whole outweighs the annoyance of its existence. Just my $0.02.
