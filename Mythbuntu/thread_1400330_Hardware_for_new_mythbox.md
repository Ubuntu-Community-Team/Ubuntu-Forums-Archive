### Post by itlarson on 2010-02-06
I have been unhappy with the performance of my current mythbox's  nvidia 8300 motherboard graphics, and am considering building a new system.  Here is what I am considering: 

-AMD Athlon&#8482; II X2 240e cpu 
[http://www.provantage.com/amd-ad240ehdgqbox~AAAMD2C8.htm]("http://www.provantage.com/amd-ad240ehdgqbox~AAAMD2C8.htm")
AMD's 45w cpu's seem to offer a good balance of performance and power consumption.  The X2 (dual core) models offer a better  selection of AMD recommended motherboards than the X3 and X4 models, but will still offer better performance than my current (more than adequate) athlon X2 4850e.

-GIGABYTE GA-MA770T-UD3P motherboard
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128392&cm_re=Gigabyte_GA-MA770T-UD3P-_-13-128-392-_-Produc]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128392&cm_re=Gigabyte_GA-MA770T-UD3P-_-13-128-392-_-Produc")
Since all the motherboards AMD recomends that have onboard graphics use ATI, I chose one without graphics. This motherboard has the needed space between the pci express x16 slot, which the video-card needs, and the pci slots, which I am using for tuners. In the past I have had a graphics card heat a tuner card to the point where the tuner stopped working, so I will leave two pci express x1 slots unused.  One which will be blocked by the video-card heat-sink, and another just for air circulation.  This will still leave two x1 slots available.  Other nice features of this motherboard are optical, as well as coaxial spdif outputs and six sata conectors.

-MSI N210-MD512H GeForce 210 video-card.
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127454&cm_re=nvidia_videocard_x16-_-14-127-454-_-Product]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127454&cm_re=nvidia_videocard_x16-_-14-127-454-_-Product")
This card is passively cooled, has a hdmi out, and should support every VDPAU deinterlacer except advanced 2x.  

-2 x 2GB Kingston HyperX DDR3 1333 

Aditionaly I will be using my existing case, power supply, hard-drives, and dual pchdtv-5500 tuners. Any thoughts on this setup?
