### Post by LowSky on 2010-08-18
The Rii Mini Wireless Keyboard; I just got this from eBay and All I can say is wow! I wasn't expecting such a great device.
It costs about $50US runs on Wireless 2.4Ghz (a bluetooth model is also now available).

Lets face it, most of want a small wireless keyboard or remote to use with MythTV. This thing is just a bit longer than a  Nokia candy bar style phone, has built in rechargeable batteries and a spot for the wireless dongle so it doesn't get lost in travel. It even has a built in laser pointer if  you want to use it for presentations. It wont look out of place on your coffee table too.

the good:
[LIST]
[*]size a TV remote
[*]has a built in track pad and laser pointer
[*]about half the cost of logitech's di novo mini and has F keys but only to F8
[*]backlight keyboard
[*]can be used while booting PC
[*]great build quality
[*]trackpad can be used in two different orientations
[*]works right out of the box on Ubuntu and Windows XP
[*]has media keys for media playback and volume adjustment
[*]has built in sleep mode to save battery life
[/LIST]

the bad:
[LIST] 
[*]keyboard isn't lined up like a natural keyboard, you need to look while typing. I keep hitting M when trying for N.
[*]not bluetooth and needs dongle (not really a con for me and they also make a bluetooth version)
[*]Track pad is a bit slow, lots of swiping required to move across a screen
[*]no gyroscope for track pad, to switch between vertical and horizontal you need to hit Fn+ALT
[*] No F9 to F12 (my motherboard need F11 to boot from another device)
[/LIST]

### Post by nickrout on 2011-03-30
> **tomtdl said:**
> Is there a way to control volume on the Rii keyboard?  I am using HDMI output with VDPAU on an Ion ITX.  Seems I need an analog output but can't get that to work.  Any ideas to avoid a second remote just for tv volume?

Controlling volume on a digital stream before it gets to the amp is generally seen as a Bad Thing (TM) as it involves decoding the stream, adjusting the volume, and encoding it again, only for the amplifier or tv to decode it to analogue again.

But if you do want to do this, try setting the mixer device in general settings to software. then the normal myth volume controls should work.
