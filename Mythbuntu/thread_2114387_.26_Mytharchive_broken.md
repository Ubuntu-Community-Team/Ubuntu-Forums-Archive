### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2013-02-13
Mythtv looks for local copies of the encoder profiles in ~/.mythtv/MythArchive/*.xml. These copies override the others.

so in that location I have my hacked ffmpeg_dvd_pal.xml

These cmd are setup for real ffmpeg (recent build). The latest mythffmpeg could be okay.
```

<profile>
        <name>HQ</name>
        <description>A high quality profile giving approx. 1 hour of video on a single layer DVD</description>
        <bitrate>4121.19</bitrate>
        <passes>2</passes>
        <parameter name="-v"           value="1"/>
        <parameter name="-i"           value="%inputfile"/>
        <parameter name="-f"           value="dvd"/>
        <parameter name="-target"      value="pal-dvd"/>
        <parameter name="-b:v"           value="8000K"/>
        <parameter name="-flags"       value="+ilme+ildct+mv0"/>
        <parameter name="-s:v"           value="720x576"/>
        <parameter name="-aspect"      value="%aspect"/>
        <parameter name="-mbd"         value="rd"/>
        <parameter name="-trellis"     value="2"/>
        <?parameter name="-mv0"         value=""/?>
        <parameter name="-ildctcmp"         value="2"/>
        <parameter name="-cmp"         value="2"/>
        <parameter name="-subcmp"      value="2"/>
        <parameter name="-acodec"      value="ac3"/>
        <parameter name="-b:a"          value="192K"/>
        <parameter name="-ac"          value="2"/>
        <parameter name="-copyts"      value=""/>
        <parameter name="-pass"        value="%passno"/>
        <parameter name="-passlogfile" value="%passlogfile"/>
        <parameter name=""             value="%outputfile"/>
    </profile>

```


I think your dvd video size is wrong: "-s 352x576"

You may have to increase the ffmpeg probesize/analyzeduration
Try:
ffprobe -analyzeduration 30000000 -i "yourvideofile.mpg"
