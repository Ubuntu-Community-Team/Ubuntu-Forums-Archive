### Post by iwasinnamuknow on 2011-03-22
I was tasked with making a new TV setup for my parents and supplied them with a new MiniITX HTPC. Orignally it was running gentoo, but I had problems with audio over HDMI so I just went with mythbuntu in the end.

Anyway, my parents couldn't (be bothered) to learn a new system so I go it back and am now using it myself.

Useless information overwith, I've still got a couple of problems:

I'm running DVB-T tuners based on the af9015 chipset, which worked perfectly on my laptop for testing, but on this machine seem to have a small problem. If I stop livetv, such as when I go to sleep, I get No Lock for everything. I usually have to delete all the channels, reboot and leave the machine for an hour or so, and then retune. It doesn't always work. I work around this by leaving livetv playing 24/7 at the moment but finding a cause would be nice. Please let me know what information I can supply.

Secondly, I got a PS3 BD remote and support USB bluetooth adapter. Initally, after configuring with the blueman applet, it was flawless. However over the next week or so, the remote became less and less reliable until now I have to remove and replace the batteries before I use it. The remote then goes to sleep again after 2-3 mins and I have to repeat if I want to use it again.

I'm currently using mythmote on my galaxy s which is great, so again I have a workaround, but I'd still like to get this fixed.

Another small problem (they're all small but I'm something of a perfectionist) is that after watching livetv or a recording, a grainy copy of the last frame is left as the background in all the menus. So if I switch to mythmusic I've still got this remenant of TV plastered all over the display. It just looks tacky and unprofessional which is a shame as the overall mythtv look is great.

Oh, one last problem: I enabled all visualisations in mythmusic but about 10% of them segfault or crash the frontend. Another 10% refuse to display in fullscreen and just stay blank. The other 80% do work, but are very laggy, sometimes up to 2 seconds behind the music.

The machine is a dual core Intel Atom 330 (1.6GHz HT) with 2GB RAM and the nVidia ION graphics chipset. Processors never run over 50% total and the machine doesn't seem to touch the swap. I wouldn't performance issues on visualisations this much, at least not on the simple ones like the spectrum.


It's a long list and they're all petty problems, but if anyone has any tips I'd like to hear them :)
