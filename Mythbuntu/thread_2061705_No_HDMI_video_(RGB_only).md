### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2012-09-24
It's not the DVI cable that prevents audio over HDMI..
Electrically the 19 pin HDMI is a single link DVI-D. But the video card has to be able to output audio data on the jack.

The FX5200 is a AGP card with no HDA codecs & (I believe) no S/PDIF header (input).

And finding a replacement AGP video card with VDPAU is going to be difficult as AGP is obsolete.

AFAIK The VGA cable/jack is preferred by the driver.
Could use in /etc/X11/xorg.conf:
<CODE>Option "UseDisplayDevice" "DFP-0"
</CODE>
But unplugging the VGA cable should lower the load on the weak GPU because it does not have to render both screens.

More than 1 attached screen on nVidia cards causes them to run at max performance/clock/heat. This condition may not apply to the latest models.

### Post by Rotak on 2012-09-24
Unplugging the VGA cable leads to autoconfig... So DVI/HDMI is activated.

Whether your video card is able to send audio via DVI/HDMI can be found out by using "aplay -l".

If it shows HDMI audio devices, it should be possible, but may need additional configuration

### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2012-09-25
There are no audio hot-plug events pre-azalia HDA..
AFAIK..
The first GPUs to get HDA HDMI audio (& VDPAU) were nForce MCP mobo chipsets.
The first discrete video cards to get any HDMI audio (S/PDIF) were **some** 8800/9500GT/9800.
The first discrete video card to get real HDA HDMI audio was GT210 or 220
(nVidia only considered)

VPDAU is an open std.
About 1.5 years ago there was an announcement that the radeon driver had the beginnings of vdpau (state tracker & shader only).
I'm not holding my breath.

There is always va-api and/or go intel.
