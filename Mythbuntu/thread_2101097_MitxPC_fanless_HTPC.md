### Post by topcat5 on 2013-01-06
You could do the same thing with a zotac ID-41 (except for wireless) configured with a SSD & 4G for about a $100 less.  It's smaller, comes with stuff to either mount on back of TV or on wall, and while it has a fan, it's pretty quiet.  

I have a wall mounted TV in my bedroom and the zotac is behind it out of sight.  I don't hear it at all.

Edit: Opps, I see you made another post about your ID-41 and the fan.  I'm not sure what the difference might be, as I can't hear mine at all unless the TV is turned off and I'm standing next to it.  You might want to see if the heat from the TV is affecting it.  I don't remember what the inside of one of these things looks like but another trick to reducing fan noise is to wire a resistor in series with the fan power to reduce speed.   I used this trick on another much more powerful front end that I built.

I have a $150 AMD E-350 box+case that handles 1080p content thanks to GPU drivers. Had to swap out the PSU included with the case for a $50 "picoPSU" - it is completely silent.
* Case + E-350APU + ATI GPU (onboard) $99
* picoPSU - $47
I had HDD, RAM, and HDMI cable laying around.

Your choice seems reasonable - depending on the price.

I'm running XBMC on it and it took a few months to get all the drivers "just write."  I found a dev-like guy that posts and maintains exactly step-by-step which settings to use with which drivers.  If you want a happy family, THAT is what I'd recommend.  Find someone how built what you want and get their exact setup.
