### Post by miegiel on 2008-09-29
I agree with the posters above, you probalbly won't need a system this powerful for HTPC use. The 65W cpu is a good idea though, if you insist on getting an amd phenom, but there are dual core amd's that use even less power.

1 thing I personally would change is the psu. Unless you are planing to upgrade it with 2 sli or crossfire cards you really don't need 600W. it's just an estimate but i'd expect your HTPC will use about 150-200W during normal use. Assuming the Nesteq EECS 600W Power Plus has a passive PFC it will suck a lot more then those 150-200 watts out of your wall socket.

With a 300-400W you have enough head room and will be using your psu more efficiently. Also you might want to look into getting a psu with an active PFC wich are more efficient then the (normal) passive PFC psu's. They're a bit more expensive too, but you'll be saving on your energy bill and the enviroment.

I have a hardware/power wise very similar machine here with a 353W psu. When i was using the onboard graphics my psu never got warm even while gaming. Now i'm using a mid range graphics card for beter gaming performance, it's a bit warmer in linux (running a 3d desktop) and a lot warmer while gaming.So as long as you are using the onboard graphics DON'T get a 600W psu.
