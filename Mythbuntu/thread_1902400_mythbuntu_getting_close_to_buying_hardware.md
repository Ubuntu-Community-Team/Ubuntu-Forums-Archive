### Post by nickrout on 2012-01-01
I haven't bouht any new hardware (other than for strictly front ends) for some time, and hardware is a fast moving world. I would go to newegg.com and search. There are facilities to search by number of pci ports, chipsets, price and many other factors.

Of course you haven't followed any of the tuner recommendations here, but if you had, you wouldn't need any PCI/PCIe slots for tuners as the HDHR is network based and the HDPVR is USB.

If you want to know more about VDPAU you could look here [http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/VDPAU](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/VDPAU). NVidia's numbering system went up in the thousands, 6xxx, 7xxx, 8xxx, 9xxx and then they started at 200 again, and there are now 2xx, 4xx and 5xx cards. VDPAU is supported in the 8xxx series upwards, so anything other than 6xxx and 7xxx should support VDPAU. However some have better support than others which is why I recommended, eg, 430 or 520. I have a GTX220 silent card in a frontend and it is fantastic.

I saw recently on the mythtv mailing list someone opine that "$180 on Newegg gets you a 3GHz dual core AMD, 4GB of memory, MicroATX board, case, 300W power supply, and GT210 video card. It's not going to be pretty, but it's cheap, powerful, relatively efficient, and has several expansion slots, SATA ports, and USB ports to play around with, while experimenting with MythTV." I can't be bothered to test his statement, and I wouldn't use a GT210 (it's one of the cards without quite enough power) - but that statement presents you with a challenge anyway :)

### Post by thatguruguy on 2012-01-03
You may need more than one tuner.

You also need to check with Comcast about what they mean.  I'm on Insight Cable (I'm in Kentucky), and they now offer a small tuner box to people with old analog TVs who only have "basic" cable and don't want a full-function tuner.  However, people with digital tvs (at least here) can still get all the basic channels (including ESPN and ESPN 2) without any external tuners. Any channels you can get without an external tuner, you should be able to record using mythtv. 

If you do need an external tuner, you can sometimes set up an "IR blaster" which will send a remote signal from your computer to your tuner to change channels. However, you are then limited by how many external tuners you have.
