### Post by jb5 on 2009-07-19
There are sites that detail what to listen out for when compressing music.

I haven't visited them myself, as personally, I know that if I did learn what to listen out for, I would spend all my time listening for the compression artifacts instead of the music!

If you have the disc space, rip a perfect copy - then if you need to, rip a second compressed file from that. That way if a "better" codec comes along you can quickly re-rip your collection from your perfect copies.

If disc space is an issue, rip your collection based on your storage capacity, so long as the rip exceeds your tolerance for artifacts.

I'm not sure that answers your question exactly, but with this type of question your own ears are your best guide (in combination with your hardware).
