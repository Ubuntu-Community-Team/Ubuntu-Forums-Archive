### Post by chazh on 2008-04-15
I'm using a Nova-T-500 which has replaced my old Nova-T and I'm very happy with it, especially with Myth 0.21 and the ability to record multiple channels on the same multiplex with a single tuner.

The only proviso is that you need to make sure you don't buy the "Diversity" model, which was shipped for a short wile in the UK. Check the label on the box and make sure you're getting a "WinTV-NOVA-T-500 model 289 SL-289-V2.0-UK", NOT one that states "V2.1-UK"

That aside, I've had no problems at all with the Nova-T-500 and there's good documentation on it at both the MythTV wiki and the IVTV wiki:

[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hauppauge_WinTV_Nova-T_500_PCI](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hauppauge_WinTV_Nova-T_500_PCI)

[http://linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hauppauge_WinTV-NOVA-T-500](http://linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hauppauge_WinTV-NOVA-T-500)

### Post by RobJohnston on 2008-04-15
A quick heads up on the Nova-T 500, some people are having issues with  it and MythTV (frustratingly, me included). "Partial Lock" and "Disconnects" seem to be the problems.

That told, many folks have said it's stable. Personally, I think it's a good card when it's working.

Good luck, Rob.
