If I rip the dvd to the hard drive it plays great.  I've tinkered with the drive speed setting and it doesn't help.

So I pull the drive and put in an IDE dvd.

Hmmmm, the /dev/dvd symlink vanished when I booted. What /dev device should it point to?  I tried sr0.  It plays but it has the exact same stutter.

When you change from sata to ide on the dvd is there "hardware rediscovery" task I need to be doing?  It seems odd to me both sata and ide would be called sr0.

What dvd controls can I tinker with that might affect this stutter problem?

1) Ubuntu went to all-scsi drive labelling with ver. 8.04. So in Ubuntu all the drives are sXX whether IDE or whatever. So sr0 is correct.

2) The other thing the two installations have in common is the mythtv internal player. Having exited the frontend, do your DVDs play OK (or at least better) from the desktop directly through say xine or vlc? Then you have your culprit. I tend to avoid playing DVDs through myth, because of glitches like you mention and others. I'll generally either rip the DVD and play the rip, or use a dedicated DVD player that I've kept for this purpose.
