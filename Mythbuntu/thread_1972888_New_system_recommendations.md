### Post by Kantalias on 2012-05-04
I'm very happy with my system.  It works really well and it's silent.

[IMG]http://i1075.photobucket.com/albums/w427/Kantalias/System.png[/IMG]

I went to a Sandy Bridge i5 specifically to support streaming Flash content (to your requirement about H.264 playback).  I haven't had very good luck using hardware acceleration for Flash.  Rendering a 1080p stream occupies about 50% of the processor (4 threads a 50% per core).  But Flash playback is smooth.

MythTV playback has always worked well for me using VDPAU.  I started out two years ago on an Atom 330 with an ION chipset and have never had any issues playing live TV or recorded MPEG-2 TS HD video.  The Geforce N210 is more than sufficient for VDPAU at 1080i (I'm on cable).

I've just recently updated to Mythbuntu 12.04 and everything continues to work well.
