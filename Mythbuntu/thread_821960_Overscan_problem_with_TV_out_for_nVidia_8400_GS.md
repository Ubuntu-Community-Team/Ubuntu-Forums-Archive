Oh yeah, and I did finally figure out how to get the Myth window resized correctly.  Took me forever to discover it, so for those still puzzled:
 - Drill down through the setup windows on the frontend until you get to an option for correcting or adjusting screen size.
 - It should give you two "arrows" (triangles), one in the upper left and one in the lower right to play with.
 - Hit the MENU button to bring up another menu.
 - Select the Reset option.  This will make Myth fill out the entire screen (including the over scanned part you can't see).
 - _NOW_ go back and fiddle with the arrow/triangles until Myth fits on your screen.

### Post by Lepy on 2010-02-09
Find your TV's optimal resolution from the manual or online (eg 1366X768@60)
Issue the gtf command (using ssh from another machine makes this super easy):
```
gtf 1366 768 60

  # 1368x768 @ 60.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 47.70 kHz; pclk: 85.86 MHz
  Modeline "1368x768_60.00"  85.86  1368 1440 1584 1800  768 769 772 795  -HSync +Vsync

```

In your xorg.conf, under your monitor section add
```
        Modeline "1368x768_60.00"  85.86  1368 1440 1584 1800  768 769 772 795  -HSync +Vsync
```

and under the screen section add (using only one "Modes" until you find a few that are appropriate, works best)
```
        SubSection "Display"
                Depth       24
                Modes   "1368x768_60.00"
        EndSubSection

```

Next issue "/etc/init.d/gdm restart" and hope the overscan is fixed.

If not, you may need to try a different "gtf" depending on your tv resolution and don't be afraid to tweak the gtf until you get something that fixes the overscan (e.g. 1360 760 60 and 1368 766 60)
