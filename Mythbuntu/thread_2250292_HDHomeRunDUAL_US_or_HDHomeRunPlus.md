### Post by TheFu on 2014-10-28
I have 2 HDHR3 models. These require software on the network to tune the different stations and channels.
As I understand the HDHR4, it does that, but also supports DLNA.

While I've never gotten mythtv to work, DLNA is handy and would be a way for non-Myth devices to have access to live TV.  That is something the HDHR3 cannot easily do without some sort of tuner software.  Sad to say this, but I use Win7 Media Center (running inside a KVM virtual machine) to record OTA ATSC TV.

If you are looking at some different model than the HDHR4, what I've said probably doesn't apply.

### Post by TheFu on 2014-11-02
The HDHR3 has been in the market for about 3 yrs.
The HDHR4 is recently released, but also from SiliconDust.  People are still using the original HDHRs.

So - you have to decide if having the added features of the HDHR4 is worth $20 more AND current support (drivers, etc) from the company. I do not expect any newer drivers than exist today to ever be released for the HDHR3 whereas the HDHR4 support should last for at least the next 3+ yrs.
