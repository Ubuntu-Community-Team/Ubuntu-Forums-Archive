I have the PVR-150, too, and am very happy with it. It's one of the easiest ones to configure for MythTV. But I'm not sure where you're getting the $90 from. I bought mine over a year ago (with a remote) from Circuit City for around $70-80. Newegg has it for $70:

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16815116625](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16815116625)

And yes, all you need is a male-to-male S-Video cable, as far as hardware, BUT keep in mind some things:

[LIST=1]
[*]Make sure your video card will work alright in Linux, with S-Video out support. What's the card model?
[*]You'll need drivers before S-Video out will work alright. So, make sure you have a backup VGA monitor so that you can use it when you're installing, doing maintenance, etc. (Does that make sense?) (This isn't a Linux thing - the same happens in Windows :D)
[/LIST]

The card isn't dirt cheap, but $70 is a reasonable compromise, since you're getting a hassle-free (or less hassle, anyway) setup, a remote, and hardware encoding (VERY friendly to your processor).
