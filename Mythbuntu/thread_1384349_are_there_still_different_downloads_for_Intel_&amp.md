### Post by gradinaruvasile on 2010-01-18
The AMD64 naming stuff is confusing for some people. So:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X86-64#History_of_AMD64](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X86-64#History_of_AMD64)

- It doesnt has to do with a specific architecture be it AMD or Intel
- It means it is a 64-bit based distribution/program meaning that the CPU uses 64 bit operations - both AMD and Intel that support 64 bit are supported.

And its not going to make your system 2x faster compared to 32-bit. they are some improvements in speed in some areas as well as native addressing of more than 4 GB RAM. But, as you may find out, there are many apps out there that simply dont have 64-bit versions available. Also it uses more RAM than the 32-bit version.
If you really want compatibility and stability just use the 32-bit version.
