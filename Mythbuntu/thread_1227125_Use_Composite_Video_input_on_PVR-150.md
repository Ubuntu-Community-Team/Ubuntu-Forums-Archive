### Post by dsbw on 2009-07-31
I'm not sure what you're asking.

If you've got a composite signal coming from somewhere and plugged into your video card, you just have to open up a command line and type:

cat /dev/video0 >capture.mpg

Assuming your card is video0 (versus video1, video2, etc). 

If you mean "how do I hook it up in MythTV?" there's nothing extraordinary about it. Set up the card (step 2), set up your video sources (step 3), and then set up your info connection (step 4), hooking up your card to the video source.

### Post by dsbw on 2009-08-02
Well, I'm not an expert on this but, I've done:

cat /dev/video0 >capture.mpg

And it worked. When you think about it, this make sense. The card has just one stream it can output. (Not all cards are like this, but the PVR-150 is.) So, it doesn't matter where the input is coming from.

In fact, it could be that Myth only cares because you could have different "video sources" for each input. That'd be my guess.
