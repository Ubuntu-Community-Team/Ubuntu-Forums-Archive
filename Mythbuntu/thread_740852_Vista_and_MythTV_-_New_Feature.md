### Post by Calash on 2008-03-31
I am not sure this is a new feature, but it did impress me so I thought I would share.

I have a fairly extensive music collection on my Mythbuntu system, managed by MythMusic so I can stream it to MythWeb.  For the rest of the computers in the house I have the folder setup as a SAMBA share, and for my Linux systems I have it also setup as an export (Faster speeds that way :) )

For work I had to setup a virtual Vista machine.  So I went through the motions and got a nice Vista VM setup on a bridged network.  Stripped out all the junk stuff and was left with a not-half-bad OS (Don't shoot me, I kind of like Vista now ;) )

Here is the interesting part that I did not expect.  I told Vista to scan my network and it found my Mythbuntu box and saw it as a media server.  After letting it do it's thing it connected and loaded all my music in the list.

O.O

I am not sure if that is a new function of MythMusic, or just Vista playing nice for a change but it was a very pleasant surprise.  Almost makes me want to upgrade the other Windows computers in the house....almost :)
