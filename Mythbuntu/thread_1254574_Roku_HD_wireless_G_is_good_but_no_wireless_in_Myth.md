### Post by mitchell2345 on 2009-08-31
mythtv has a lot more communications between frontend and backend then just video streaming.

Also, MythTV does not buffer the video.  So as wireless video comes and goes so does the bandwidth/video.  I have read someone a dev was working on fixing this.  As you are right, you *should* be able to with a good connection.

### Post by newlinux on 2009-09-02
I'm able to stream SD content over wireless G on my mythboxes with no problem. HD mpeg-2 content is iffy though. Depends on what other interference is going on in my house. Sometimes it is stutter free,sometimes it is full of stutters. Buffering would be nice. VLC and mplayer can buffer, so you could try using those for non backend recorded media.
