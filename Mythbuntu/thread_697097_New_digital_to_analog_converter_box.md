### Post by newlinux on 2008-02-15
> **dflipb said:**
> where would I find how to get/setup the irblaster.  And how would I handle the schedule?

There are lots of IR blaster out there. I don't use one, but if you look at the mythwiki you'll find pointers to ones others use: [http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php/Using_an_IR_Blaster_with_MythTV](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php/Using_an_IR_Blaster_with_MythTV). There is more info out there, but this is a good starting point, as is:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MythTV_External_Channel_Changer](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MythTV_External_Channel_Changer)

 Myth can control the IR blaster, so it will change the channels for you when you are watching live tv and change the station or when you schedule recordings.

### Post by newlinux on 2008-02-15
> **stevejesus said:**
> Are there any cards that will decode digital over-the-air signals?

Many. For North America here is a short list:

Air2PC .02 or some revision of it
Kworld ATSC 110 or 115
Avermedia A180
Dvico Fusion 5
Pchdtv 3000 or 5500
HDHomerun

and more... [http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/ATSC_Devices](http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/ATSC_Devices)

You'll want ones that do ATSC 8VSB for digital OTA.
