### Post by GaryP2 on 2014-05-27
I fully update my system manually every 1-3 months or so when I have time to deal with potential issues. I don’t apply updates when I’m in a hurry to do something else. I do an image backup of the system partition first just in case something bad goes wrong. The backup takes 2-3 time longer than the updates do, but it’s good insurance.

I don’t want the system to update automatically and then have a surprise to deal with when I don’t have time to deal with it.

I’ve had several minor bugs get introduced that eventually get resolved by taking this approach. I typically try to read this and a couple other Myth forums to try and ensure that there aren’t any major issues going on with an update that I should try to avoid. I deferred an update once a while back by about a week because I read something suggesting there was an issue that was subsequently resolved with a patch a couple days later.

There's definitely the “don’t fix what’s not broken” approach, but in general I think it makes sense to not get too far behind. The further you get behind the higher the potential may be that something breaks when you finally do perform an update for whatever reason.
