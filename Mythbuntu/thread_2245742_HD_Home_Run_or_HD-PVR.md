I am considering a shift in my usual MythTV systems in my home.

Currently I have two old P4 towers loaded with PCI based analog and digital tuners, that handle all the recordings.  The content provider is CableONE here in the state of South Dakota.  My digital tuners only get clear QAM, so they are not much good unless you want to record NFL games on Fox or ABC or something like that.  Most of the stuff I record comes from the analog channels like AMC, Fox, or The History Channel.  CableONE is doing away with the analog channels soon, so I have to make a decision:

1 - Stay with CableONE and use a CableCARD tuner, like the HD Home Run

or

2 - Ditch CableONE altogether and use DirecTV and HD-PVR's

My question is, which approach is better?   Both have their problems.  The ones that come to mind are:

1 - If I use a CableCARD tuner, the cpp flags may be an issue.  IF the copy once flag is set, can I still view the recording on my separate Frontends, or am I screwed?!?  Copy freely is no problem.  Until I try this setup, I will not even know if my configuration of using a separate backend and multiple frontends (my preferred configuration, since I have a rather loud 1U server do the backend chores, and quiet frontends with light CPU's but decent graphics to do the frontend display of content to each TV).  Can you only play back the recording on the system that made it?  How is that tracked when you are using a CableCARD equipped tuner like the HDHR3 that streams the content over the network to possibly multiple locations?  Is all of CableONE's content copy freely or copy once, a mixture, does anyone have any first hand experience with them in this area?

2 - If I ditch cable and go with Satellite, am I going to be able to encode enhanced audio, like 5.1 or 7.1 dolby, or can the HD-PVRs not even do that?  I see a digital audio input, but I have not seen anybody anywhere that is really using it, or says, hey, this works or this sucks!!!  If I go with DirecTV, I will eventually want multiple tuners, so I can watch one show, and my wife another, at the same time, and also be able to record one or two other shows at the same time, so that means at least 3 or 4 STB's.  What is the best method of controlling multiple STB's, and we would be talking about the most recent hardware available from them I assume, so that would be the HR44 with the C41 Genie's.  The best method seems to be doing it via network, but I am not sure if the HR44 or the C41 Genie's support that.  If not, the next best solution seems to be to use the IR transmitters on the HD-PVR's to control them - how well does that work, particularly with multiple receivers?  Can the Genie's be connected to the HD-PVR?  Would you want to use Genie's, or try to get DirecTV to send me non-DVR tuners?  I am pretty sure using the HD-PVR2 is out of the question, since copy protection would prevent any digital content from being recorded by the PVR, right?

Your opinions and experience are appreciated

Thank you
