### Post by superm1 on 2008-05-04
Regarding Intel chipsets, odds are XvMC is not in Hardy for them.

Quoting [http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php/XvMC#OBTAINING_XvMC_CAPABLE_DRIVERS_FOR_YOUR_CHIPSET:](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/index.php/XvMC#OBTAINING_XvMC_CAPABLE_DRIVERS_FOR_YOUR_CHIPSET:)

> Intel chipsets are often considered 'low-powered' but in fact the drivers have been deficient in enabling the capabilities of the hardware. For Intel chipsets, as of May 1, 2008, the intel driver which is included in most distributions does NOT enable XvMC, except on i810 and i815 chipsets. However, XvMC capabilities are now available through version 2.3.0 of the xf86-video-intel driver for ALL Intel video chipsets. This driver is presently available as a source tar from: 
  [http://xorg.freedesktop.org/archive/individual/driver/xf86-video-intel-2.3.0.tar.bz2](http://xorg.freedesktop.org/archive/individual/driver/xf86-video-intel-2.3.0.tar.bz2)  or through git from  
  $ git-clone git://anongit.freedesktop.org/git/xorg/driver/xf86-video-intel
