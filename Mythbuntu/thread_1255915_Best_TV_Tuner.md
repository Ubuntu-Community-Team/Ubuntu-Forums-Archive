### Post by hackmeister on 2009-09-02
If you want to be able to record all the channels off an HD cable/satellite box then the PVR-1212 will be the #1 choice with the coming release (tentatively end of this month) of Myth 0.22 (Mythbuntu 9.10):
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HD-PVR](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HD-PVR)

If you want to record  either QAM (unencrypted open digital cable stations) or ATSC (over the air HD digital, requires a digital antennae) any of the following capture devices work well in MythTV:
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/PcHDTV_HD-5500](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/PcHDTV_HD-5500)
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/PcHDTV_HD-3000](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/PcHDTV_HD-3000)
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Silicondust_HDHomeRun](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Silicondust_HDHomeRun)
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/AVerTV_HD_A180](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/AVerTV_HD_A180)
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HVR-1250](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HVR-1250) - I think the 1300 is the newer version of this card
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HVR-1800](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HVR-1800)  - I think the 1900 is a newer version of this card
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HVR-2250](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Hauppauge_HVR-2250)

For QAM your mileage will vary depending on your cable provider. I have RCN and literally went from 30 HD open channels to only 6 over night. The trend is the cable companies only leaving the regular network channels (CBS, NBC, ABC, Fox, CW, PBS) open.  Sad.

FYI, you can also capture via a firewire card off a cable box but that will tie up your cable box to whatever channel is being recorded at the time.
