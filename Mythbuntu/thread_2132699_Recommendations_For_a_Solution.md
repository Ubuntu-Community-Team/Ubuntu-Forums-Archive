### Post by jlp_engineer on 2013-04-05
I have been with Mythbuntu since version 8.xx.  I have liked the progress and am very grateful for all the people that have contributed to what brings a lot of enjoyment to the rest of us.  I am looking for some recommendations and some success stories with regard to the following:

1 - I still record several hundred Gigs of analog programming using three PVR-150 tuner cards.

2 - I have three digital tuners that grab a limited amount of programming, from the clear QAM channels that my local cable company (CableONE) graciously sends my way.

3 - I have two backends, with the slave containing a RAID array for the local analog tuners, and master backend using a larger RAID array that holds the digital recordings and DVD ISO files.  I have to have the backends split up, as I only have five PCI slots in each system, and cannot fit all six tuners in one system.

4 - I have access to a 1U server that I could use as a backend, but the problem is there are no PCI slots in this newer server.  I have considered using HD HomeRun boxes that are networked, but there is no analog equivalent that will network an analog tuner into the 1U server.  Local storage on the server could be a problem, but I may be able to use some SAS to eSATA cables and connect the external RAID arrays I already have.

5 - I have also considered a ceton tuner, with a cable card, but don't know how many channels I would really be able to record using MythTV, so even though my cable company's box will allow me to record whatever I want, it seems you cannot do the same thing with the Ceton and a cable card, primarily due to the copy freely flag or lack thereof.  

6 - I like to keep the DVD's I have as ISO's on the server, since I want to keep the ability to have subtitles, special features, and alternate version of movies.  I will eventually begin putting bluerays on the server, but I am not certain how well the MKV files play in MythTV.  I know that some DVD menu's still don't play correctly, or at all, and sometimes playing the movie will crash MythTV player.

7 - I want to have live TV, recorded TV, and movies all within the same menus, look and feel.  The only thing I would wish for, would be for Netflix integration, but that will likely never happen - shame.

My concerns with analog programming would not be an issue, if my local cable company would just re-transmit those same channels in SD resolution in digital via clear QAM, like some companies are doing right now (or so I have heard).  If it wasn't for that, the 1U server, with the HD HomeRUN multiple tuner box, or a few of them, would work just fine for me.  But for now, I am stuck with backend servers that are close to 10 years old (P4 3.2GHz), just because I need something with a lot of PCI slots.  I would even consider going with a more modern tower, with PCIe slots and dual or quad core CPU's, but most of those only have a few PCIe slots, so I would have to go with something like the HVR-2250 to get the tuner densities I need, and I have heard all kinds of issues with the analog portion of the tuners on them, and I am not that confident, that I could plop down several hundred dollars for a new server and tuner cards, given the current track record on them - maybe I am wrong.  Wading through the hundreds of messages in the HVR-2250 threads is confusing at best.  Not to mention, that the HVR-2250 digital tuners would only be able to grab clear QAM, so that brings me back to the ceton or HD HomeRun, at least for digital.  But there again, only the digital channels that the cable card would see as copy freely.  Would that really mean SyFy, Discovery, History channel, or anything???  I think you start to see the problem.

Given all the above items, what would you all recommend as a way to move forward.  

Thanks for your opinions.

MythTV Fan
