### Post by coldraven on 2015-11-20
Hello and welcome to the forum.
Your old XP equipment will probably run better with a lightweight distribution like Xubuntu or Lubuntu.
Ubuntu with it's Unity desktop needs more resources and might not work at all. 
As for a media centre if you don't want a large power bill then consider buying a Raspberry Pi 2, it only uses a few Watts. I have one running  Kodi and it's very impressive. You can download the OpenElec based Kodi from here (scroll down) : [http://openelec.tv/get-openelec](http://openelec.tv/get-openelec)

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-11-20
Welcome. 

+1 for a [Raspberry Pi]("https://www.raspberrypi.org/"). Or an [Odroid]("http://www.hardkernel.com/main/main.php"). They'll both run Kodi just fine. There's a few flavours for both. RaspBMC is an XBMC/Kodi spin specifically for the RPi. (Kodi used to be XBMC but changed its name. :))

My Odroid runs Lubuntu with Kodi faultlessly.

Bottom line: for a Kodi setup, you don't need much. A gig or two of RAM, a P4 (or less) processor. That would get you going. Sure you can cobble something together from what you have.
