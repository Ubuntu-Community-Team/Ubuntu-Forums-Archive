### Post by Boppy3125 on 2010-12-21
I am sorry that I can't fully answer your questions.  I tried this a couple of times and never found my way through the tangled web of threads and old howto's myself.  I gave up using my STB for now.

I use an Hauppauge 150 for basic cable channels and a HDHomeRun for getting the locals in HD off of my cable QAM.  To answer one of your questions, the HDHR gets the cable feed directly and uses QAM to find the channels.  You can only capture what your cable provider passes "in the clear".  Check out the Silicon Dust website to see what your cable provider gives you.

[http://www.silicondust.com/support/channels/](http://www.silicondust.com/support/channels/)
Enter your zip.
On the very top of the page, change from Digital Antenna to your cable operator.
In Myth you have to scan QAM channels, then you edit the channels to match your channel lineup you have from SchedulesDirect.

The advantage you could have from getting the SA4250 firewire working is that you might be able to also record the other HD channels.  In my area the non-local HD channels are all sent using SDV, which means there is no set QAM channel for anything, plus they encrypt those channels.  There is no guarantee that using FireWire and your STB you could get more than what they give over clear QAM anyway.

It comes down to what channels you want to capture and what your cable company is doing.  I finally decided that it wasn't worth the effort to occasionally get USA or HDTV in HD recorded.  Most of what I really want to record is on the locals.  If I am desperate I get the SD version using the Hauppauge 150.

If you find out how to get this working, update here.  I would love to know how you get along and decide if it is worth it to try again.

### Post by LowSky on 2010-12-22
I have the same cablebox. Maybe you have Cablevision too so I can say what to expect.

1. Firewire port does work.
2. Most of the channels will be blocked either permanently or randomly. Due to amazingly annoying DRM.
3. To get around the DRM I purchased a Haupauge HD PVR, it uses the analog loophole of the component cables to record video.
4. I use the firewire port to change channels instead of relying on IR transmitter that can potentially fail. The isa thread on here about an Program made by a forum member for firewire control.
5. I also have a Haupauge HVR-2250 to record QAM channels (local channels like NBC, CBS, ABC and Fox) that way I can record multiple channels at once and not continually tie up a cable box. It is annoying when you want to watch something and the PC changes the channel because it needs to record.
