### Post by westie457 on 2013-06-15
Hi, welcome to the Forum.

Your second question answered first. The old releases can be found here. [http://old-releases.ubuntu.com/releases/](http://old-releases.ubuntu.com/releases/)

In response to your first question, your 10TB of data is in a raid array I presume.

Is the installed OS on a separate HDD or is it on a partition as part of the array?

If the OS is separate then you could clean install 9.10 and upgrade to 10.04 LTS and then 12.04 LTS (Long Term Support) the relatively quick way.

Also you could start with 9.10 and upgrade through every release. This is the slow way with lots more chances of breakage along the way.

Which is the best way to go? I have no idea because really I have never dealt with any form of Raid installation and do no want to suggest you do something that could hose your system. 

For the best route to take you need someone who understands what is involved with Raid.
