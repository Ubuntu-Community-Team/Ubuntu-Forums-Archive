### Post by sabre2th on 2010-01-08
I see Windows Media Center remotes recommended most often.  Any of those should work out of the box with no issues.  Myself, I bought an Anyware remote (with USB sensor) from Newegg for about $25.  If you spend a bit more, you can get a learning remote that will also operate your TV.

Don't overthink it.  Just look around for one that looks decent and you should be fine.

Edit: For the record, remotes that come with cards can be kind of hit-and-miss as far as setup.  Some aren't a problem, others may not work at all.

### Post by underquark on 2010-01-08
A good learning remote will be useful for your other devices too.  The JP1 site ([hifi-remote.com]("http://www.hifi-remote.com/forums/index.php")) is a good place to look for inspiration and also will help if you subsequently want to program that remote (which you will).  Nobody can tell you the best remote but I'd go for one with plenty of real buttons and preferably try the feel of one in a store before buying it.  I have  URC8820 (well, three actually) bought from US for about $20 each, plus various others.  In the UK I just bought a SilverCrest remote from Lidl for £6 or so- it isn't a fully programmable remote but it can learn 150 signals, has many built-in codes and has lots of nice buttons including the coloured ones.
