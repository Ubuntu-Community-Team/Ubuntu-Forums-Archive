If you have decided that the problem is that someone has gained access to your system, then back up your data, reset your router & reinstall your operating system.  Only way to be sure you've cleared your system.

However, I hope that you are open to considering some other possibilities.

255.255.255.0 isn't an IP address. It's a subnet mask. An IP address looks like this: 195.233.169.147 (I made up that one).  The subnet mask basically tells the computer that the first three octets in the IP address (195.233.169) indicate which network the computer is on.  The last octet (.147) indicates which computer it is inside of that network.  

So 255.255.255.0 is not in the least bit suspicious.

The time fluctuations, the inconsistencies with software center, files & icons missing- all of these things can happen when a hard drive is failing.  You said you replaced the hard drive- did you replace it with a new one?  It might be worth testing the hard drive before you lose all  your data permanently.

As Cortman says, one step at a time. If there is a chance that your activities are being monitored/affected by someone else , then the first thing to change is the device that you use to connect to the internet.

After reading all the re-installs etc you have performed it would appear that if anything has been compromised then it is the router/modem which is still compromised after you have re-installed your system.

Obtain a replacement router/modem, reset it to factory defaults then re-install the password and configuration details on someone elses computer.

Then plug it into your home connection and try to connect whilst using a live cd in your computer. If there is still erratic behaviour occuring after doing this then I would say that it is extremely unlikely that anyone has any control over your computer or internet connection.

As a secondary measure, inform your service provider of your concerns and have them monitor what or who is connected to your home connection for a week or so.
