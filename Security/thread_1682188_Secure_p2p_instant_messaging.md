### Post by MtnDew on 2011-02-10
"Secure P2P": The biggest oxymoron of the century? 

Theres little you can actually do to secure peer to peer sessions. P2P is flawed in a security aspect by design inherently. You could apply encryption to your outgoing traffic, however if "replayed" or caught by a third party, it's then vulnerable to be cracked as most of the popular p2p encryption schemes have already been cracked. 

Then you must worry if your recipient is encrypting her traffic. If she isn't, forget encrypting your traffic, it's useless. There is too much room for mistake.

To be honest, I couldn't really imagine exchanging any confidential information over a p2p network. Unless you're downloading copyright material, there really is no logical reason for concern on a p2p network (ON LINUX).
