### Post by bashiergui on 2014-05-23
Researchers at Dr.Web identified several variants of a trojan that infects desktop and server, 32- and 64-bit versions of Ubuntu and CentOS. They use the infected machine to carry out DDoS attacks.

This does not mean that the sky is falling. Trojans are delivered to users by tricking them into executing a malicious program. Attackers trick users by providing software masquerading as something else. 

Protect yourself by not downloading software from unreliable or unknown sources. 

[http://www.net-security.org/malware_news.php?id=2768](http://www.net-security.org/malware_news.php?id=2768)
