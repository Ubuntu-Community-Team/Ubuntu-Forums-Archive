After following the Ubuntu Server 10.04 instructions on how to secure a SAMBA install:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/serverguide/C/samba-fileprint-security.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/serverguide/C/samba-fileprint-security.html)

I ran **apparmor_status** and saw the following status:

> 
3 processes are unconfined but have a profile defined.
   /usr/sbin/nmbd (971)
   /usr/sbin/smbd (954)
   /usr/sbin/smbd (974)Restarting smbd and nmbd resulted in both being properly confined, but this does not persist after a system reboot (i.e., I need to restart smbd and nmbd to regain confinement).

How do I get it to stick?

Marc

### Post by rookcifer on 2011-02-01
The problem is that those services are not starting until after AppArmor has loaded.  This is a common problem, as I have seen it on my Ubuntu installs quite a lot (with Samba specifically).  The only way around it is to delay the starting of those processes at boot or to restart them manually after boot.
