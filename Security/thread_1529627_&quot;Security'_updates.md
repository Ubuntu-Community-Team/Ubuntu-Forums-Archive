### Post by yeleek on 2010-07-13
Ok try this - see if it makes a tad more sense.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Configuration_management](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Configuration_management)
'Configuration management (CM) is a field of management  that focuses on establishing and maintaining consistency of a system's or product's performance and its functional and physical attributes with its requirements, design, and operational information throughout its life.[1]  For information assurance, CM can be defined as the management of security features and assurances through control of changes made to hardware, software, firmware, documentation, test, test fixtures, and test documentation throughout the life cycle of an information system.'

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Change_control](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Change_control)
Change control within Quality management systems (QMS) and Information Technology (IT) systems is a formal process used to ensure that changes to a product or system are introduced in a controlled and coordinated manner. It reduces the possibility that unnecessary changes will be introduced to a system without forethought, introducing faults into the system or undoing changes made by other users of software. 

So in english
1) make sure the changes we make are consistent and don't introduce flaws

2) Once we are happy with the changes we want to make in our test/dev environment, introduce them in a controlled professional manner to our next suitable environment - i.e. a production like environment, or production itself - depending on the org.
