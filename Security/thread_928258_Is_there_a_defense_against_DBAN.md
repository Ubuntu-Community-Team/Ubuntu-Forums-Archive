### Post by ealrgrey on 2008-09-23
I recently ran DBAN for the first time, and apart from being impressed with how well it does it's job, I was a little disconcerted with how easy it was to permanently destroy my data without any passwords/permissions being required. I get why that is good and necessary, but it does make me think... anyone could have popped DBAN into my disc tray, booted up, and deleted my disk, and been out of the building within a minute or two.

Now I'm not saying that is ever going to happen to me - I'm not paranoid - but just hypothetically, couldn't I just as easily walk up to any computer I feel like and run DBAN?

Besides backing everything up at another site, is there any defense to the DBAN boot disk?

### Post by chenrazee on 2008-09-23
The only thing that comes to mind is to remove 'boot from cd/dvd' as an option in your bios setup, and password protect the bios.

(I don't know if a BIOS reset would over-ride this, but it'd take a lot of messing around to do so.)

One extra level of physical protection would be to buy a key-lockable case.

None of these methods are infallible, and they won't stop a determined person from destroying your data, but they'd certainly prevent someone from doing what you mentioned, which is to 'sneak in, reboot with the dban cd in the drive, wipe your disk and disappear.

### Post by jerome1232 on 2008-09-24
Yeah pretty much boils down to not much you can do, I'll second chenrazee's suggestions.

Adjust the bios settings to prevent booting from cd.

Password protect the bios settings, the password can be removed by simply flashing your cmos, there's a little jumper that you can move on the motherboard to do this.

A sturdy case with a good lock would help prevent getting access to the interior of the case to flash the cmos.

Removing the optical drives altogether, or I've seen some cases with a lockable front bezel which covers the 5 1/4" bays to prevent access to them without a key.
