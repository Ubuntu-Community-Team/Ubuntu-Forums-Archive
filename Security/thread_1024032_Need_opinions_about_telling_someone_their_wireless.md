### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2008-12-28
I was setting up a dedicated wireless network using a Linksys wireless router for my grandchildren to use with their hand-held Nintendo games. They can play multi-player games if they can connect to the router. The wireless network does not have Internet connectivity because the hand-held games only support WEP, and I will not allow anything less that WPA on my network. 

When I logged into the Linksys with the default password, I found it was set up for an ISP with a user ID equal to an email address. I knew right away I was not connecting to my Linksys. 

I live in a big city where people are not very trusting, and we don't get to know neighbors a few houses down the street. Anyway, with the ISPs primary account holders email address, which is based on the persons real name, I was able to look the person up on the Internet (Googled). I know his address, phone number, and his age. The account holder is 67 years old. 

I am 60 myself; however, I'm not an average computer user.

So, should I go to the trouble of helping this guy secure his network? Given the fact that we live in a very distrustful environment, should I just drop it and let him learn about the consequences the hard way?

Thanks for any opinions.

### Post by lswest on 2008-12-28
I would at least give the guy a heads-up that he's wide open, but if you don't know if he'll believe the story, give him an anonymous note in the mailbox with a number of a computer shop or something that will secure it for him.

Otherwise just go up there and start off casually like "I live down the street from here" lead in the bit about the kids, then mention that while you were setting up a network for their hand-helds you noticed that a linksys was open, since you were curious whose it was you found an address and name, and decided to let the person know before someone less kind comes along and starts abusing it.

Just my opinion.

And kudos on being 60 and yet not computer shy.
