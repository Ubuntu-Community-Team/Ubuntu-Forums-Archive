### Post by running_rabbit07 on 2010-01-05
Not viruses, but malware. There have been a few cases where people have downloaded screensavers from Gnome-look in the form of .debs and they had other commands added in. As always, as long as you are not installing software from untrusted sources, then you have nothing to worry about with malware nor viruses. 

Firewalls have always been important. IPtables are running in Ubuntu from install, but if you want more security, you can start the Ubuntu Firewall with this command,```
sudo ufw enable
``` and from there your firewall will be started upon power-on.

### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2010-01-05
> **premamotion said:**
> it`s just my impression or I`m right about it? 

And if I`m right, why this is happening?

We have always had tons of these threads, check recurring discussions.

Recently the discussions have been much more let us say informative and less rambling discussions from the same people (search recurring and you will see the difference).

Since the threads, for the most part, stay on topic they do not need to be moved.

Such questions show:

1. We have an almost constant influx of new users. These users are conditioned to use such things as a firewall and antivirus so they feel they need such things on Ubuntu.

2. No one reads [the stickies](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=510812).

3. People do not bother to search the forums for answers to these common questions, they simply start a new thread.

In all it is an opportunity for us to educate these people ;)
