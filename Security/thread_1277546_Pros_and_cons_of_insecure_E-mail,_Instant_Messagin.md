### Post by alvevind on 2009-09-28
Virtually all e-mail and instant messaging (IM) between individuals is being sent completely or partially insecure/unencrypted. 

Often the information being sent is of a confidential nature and the parties assume they are the only ones able to read it.

This thread aims to discuss the various aspects and implications of security/privacy the different one-to-one communication models, like e-mail, IM, Facebook, etc. 

The focus for the discussion should be on the individuals using the technology and the effect on society in general, and not only on the technology itself.

What levels of security do the different technologies offer? 
Do people use it? Why? 
Do they not use it? Why? 
What are the dangers of not having more security?
What are the possibilities lost when increasing security?
What is the future of one-to-one communication 5 years on? 25 years on?


To start off the discussion:

OTR ( [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Off-the-Record_Messaging](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Off-the-Record_Messaging) ) is a open source secure communication technology available in some IM clients. Is this something everyone should be using? Will people actually use it if they knew about it? Is OTR really only for people who are paranoid?
