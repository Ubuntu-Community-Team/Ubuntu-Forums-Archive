### Post by slickymaster on 2014-09-30
There are several important issues around letting your browser save your passwords and the question itself should not concern a specific browser but the fact that if you’re not careful, anyone who walks up to your computer can access websites as you, or perhaps even walk away with a copy of all your usernames and passwords.

Browsers maintain their own databases of usernames and logins information that you allow it to collect on your behalf, and fetches the appropriate information as needed. Unfortunately these databases are often not quite as secure as we might want it to be. Depending on the browser, it’s often possible for a hacker to extract its contents should they ever gain access to your machine. In fact there are even utilities that will display the database contents, including the passwords, for some browsers.
Another issue is that most people fail to place a “master password” on the browser’s database. This can further encrypt the database and prevent the hacker’s access, but it does something more important: it can prevent casual access.

### Post by slickymaster on 2014-09-30
There are a few good applications you can use to save your passwords, website logins and more. They store your information in a database on your machine or a pen drive. Unlike your browser, however, a master password is required and you won't be able get nothing out of their databases until you've specified your master password.

Just search for LastPass or KeePass.
