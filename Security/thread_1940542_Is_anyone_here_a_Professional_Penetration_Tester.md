### Post by Dangertux on 2012-03-13
> **listerdl said:**
> Just wondering - 

Is anyone here a professional PenTester? Id be interested to know what the biggest challenge was to become one and is it as much fun as I imagine it to be? HAcking into sites (with permission ;) )

When I was with my previous employer I performed numerous manual penetration tests. In my opinion, it was not a fun or glamorous job as many people make it out to be. The landscape is not what it used to be in the late 90's early 2000's, and truthfully we didn't often win. However, we still provided thousands of pages of reports that were largely ignored by the client. 

They liked to throw around terms like "this is a risk we can sustain". Which made me wonder why they spent the money in the first place.

In my opinion, it wasn't as hard to "become one" (if by become one you mean get hired) as it was to be one on a daily basis. It's a constantly evolving "battlefield" if you will , with constantly changing metrics and standards. You aren't "hacking" a system with permission. You are providing actionable documentable results while simulating an attack from a malicious attacker, all while bound within the constraints of very unrealistic rules of engagement.

Bottom line, it was frustrating, unrewarding, and generally made it very hard to get employment in other parts of the IT industry. Let's be real, who wants to hire the guy that spent the last however many years breaking into trusted systems (even if it was ethical).

Hope this helps.
