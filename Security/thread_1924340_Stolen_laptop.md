### Post by cometdog on 2012-02-12
If the data on the HDD are not encrypted, then it is trivial for the thief to attach the HDD to another computer and access anything on it.

If the data are encrypted but the laptop was left hibernating or sleeping, then it could in principle be possible for a knowledgeable person to break in.  Depending on many factors (which I am not personally knowledgeable about) it could be reasonably straightforward or not.

If the data are encrypted but the laptop was turned off entirely, and if your password is strong, then there is a good chance that it will be impossible or nearly so to access your data.

### Post by Dangertux on 2012-02-12
Pretty much what cometdog said. If the laptop is powered off and the data was encrypted and the key was protected with a strong passphrase the odds of the data ever being obtained are very low. That being said if a variety of other things were stolen there is a good chance the thief is not technologically skilled and just wanting to pawn the stuff for some quick cash. ### Post by winh8r on 2012-02-12
Firstly, sorry to hear about you suffering a break-in, not nice at all.


Most stolen laptops have the drive removed as soon as possible after being stolen, this lessens the chances of being caught with stolen property due to identifiable data being on the drive.

The laptops are then sold on without a hard drive which has allegedly been removed "for security reasons"

Most of the scum who go around breaking into peoples houses are only interested in getting cash for what they have taken and are not generally interested in accessing data on the stolen item as the time they waste on a possibly failed attempt at accessing the data could have, in their eyes been spent planning or carrying out another burglary.

Just make sure you have changed all your login details for any websites you use, just in case though.

I am not sure where you live but keep an eye on local sites like gumtree, etc for adverts offering laptops which have had the hard drive removed for "security reasons" chances are that at fair few of them have started out as someone elses property.

The hard drives that are removed from the laptops are either thrown away/destroyed or they are formatted and re-installed with another OS , usually a pirated copy of windows I believe and sold on as just a hard drive.

I have been told that it is extremely easy for people to sell driveless laptops in areas where there are a high number of students.

Consequently there is a high demand for second hand laptop drives in these areas also.

They might be scum but they appear to be cunning scum that break into houses and steal laptops.

### Post by Dragonbite on 2012-02-13
The more hurdles they have to get by (strong password, encryption, etc.) the less likely anybody other than the most determined of criminals (or pranksters ;) ) will get through.

The more difficult it is for them to get anything interesting (CC information, bank accounts, etc.) the less likely they will pursue it.  Maybe they'll just wipe off the hard drive and install a (likely pirated) version of some OS and sell it.

So another think to do is NOT let the web browser hold onto your passwords because if they DO get in, you don't want helpful firefox or chromium or whatever filling in your username and password to your bank account for them!  There are products to protect against that (roboform I think), but those are primarily Windows-only and I don't know if there is a Linux version.
