### Post by defaultusername on 2009-04-13
d_071892,

It depends on your skill as a sysadmin. For a security research, serious tinfoil hat stuff is possible. SELinux, stack/heap protection, hardened kernels, NIDS, full disk encryption with cascading ciphers, aggressive honeypots, ignitable thermite attached to HDDs in case of compromise.

For most users, a simple, logical design is more than enough to ensure security. Installing updates/patches and managing your network appropriately isn't a bad idea either.
