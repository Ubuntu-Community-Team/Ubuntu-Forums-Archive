### Post by TheFu on 2018-01-08
You can compare your current files to those from backups made PRIOR to the issues.
OTOH, a little ghost is the icon for "ghostery" - a privacy addon for browsers.  Could that be what you are seeing?

If you cannot validate the files on the system and you don't trust it, then it is time to wipe and start over, being more careful this time.

IMHO.

Versioned backups are really handy, BTW.  I keep 120 days of daily backups. The storage cost really isn't that much for all the piece of mind I have.  Not everything is included in my backups.  Large files, like videos, are not included, for example.

TOR isn't perfect. There are attacks against it.  It is also easy for humans to make operational mistakes that would broach the TOR privacy.  Happens all the time.  OpSec is hard.

### Post by TheFu on 2018-01-11
Unshielding a TOR user isn't THAT hard.  Most people make operational mistakes.  They allow javascript, they use the same accounts in and outside TOR.  They click links that launch downloads from outside TOR.  Or they simply write paragraphs online - our writing styles have unique characteristics.  I've been uncovered by that last one, BTW.

OpSec is hard, even for the professionals.  There are about 10 very interesting write-ups about how different people and organizations failed and were uncovered. For the paranoid, it makes for interesting reading.
