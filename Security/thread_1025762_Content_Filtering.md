### Post by lensman3 on 2009-01-02
You might block your users from going to certain IP addresses.  I could make a case that any web surfing to China or Korea from a work machine is not required to perform that employees duties.  For instance, if you work for a bagel company, surfing on clothing sites in Eastern Europe wouldn't be a work requirement.

I block my home network from most of Asia, Eastern Europe, South America, etc.  So far nobody has complained.  I get from 500-2000 IP attacks a day on my home computer network when I bother to record the hits.  I usually just silently drop the attempted connects.  When I fire up a torrent everybody comes looking.

Just a thought.
