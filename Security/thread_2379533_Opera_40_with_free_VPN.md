### Post by TheFu on 2017-12-29
> **anontrust said:**
> i can't really suggest to use a free vpn, it's just an honeypot, be carefull guys

Using a VPN just shifts where the security issue lies.  If you are in a cafe or airport, shifting to a data center somewhere else could be a good idea.  Does that mean the exit node in that remote data center is secure?  Nope.  Pick your risks.

If you want security between 2 systems over the internet, be careful using some unpaid, third party. At least with a paid VPN, you know they have some minor interest in being secure. With a free VPN, that tiny interest becomes ZERO interest with only their reputation at risk.

Of course, we each need to determine our risk acceptance for using any particular VPN or other service.  Just be aware that most VPN providers use PPTP (especially the free ones) which has been hacked since 2002.  Easy to follow "How-to" videos have been online since 2012.

Nobody cares about your security and/or privacy like you do. Don't believe the marketing/sales posts. Do your own testing and validation.
