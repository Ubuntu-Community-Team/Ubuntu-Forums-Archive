### Post by Lars Noodén on 2014-11-29
The way the Tor project recommends is to download the [Tor Browser Bundle](https://www.torproject.org/download/download-easy.html.en) and run with that rather than trying to tune your web browser.  It can be run along side your other browsers and starts up Tor automatically.  It has a lot of adjustments and gives the greatest chance of blending in using Tor.  Setting up Firefox yourself increases the chance for mistakes and because of the unique constellation of settings will stand out with a unique fingerprint.  It's only available for x86 but then so is Ubuntu (mostly).

### Post by Lars Noodén on 2014-12-01
Java is already disabled by default in TBB and it is a good idea to disable it even in your regular browsers.  Javascript, though is a different animal.  And although in a regular browser you might leave [javascript for some sites](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-us/firefox/addon/noscript/) but I would also disable it completely in TBB as suggested in #12 above.  

Apparently device tracking is in wide enough use that it is recognized as a problem:
[http://www.theguardian.com/technology/2014/nov/28/europe-privacy-war-websites-silently-tracking-users](http://www.theguardian.com/technology/2014/nov/28/europe-privacy-war-websites-silently-tracking-users)

The TBB, with javascripts either wholly on or wholly off, will help avoid most of that.  As the EFF project shows, most other browser configurations are as good as unique.  I'd say wholly off wit javascripts in TBB.

Flash is already out and should not be added.
