### Post by nova47 on 2010-12-22
A lot of times you'll find that anti-virus really isn't worth your time or money. Main stream anti-virus programs are really only going to catch either old viruses or really basic viruses. The issue is that anti-virus programs essentially operate on what's called a signature based system. This means that the way they detect a virus on your computer is by comparing the actions of something to a known database of viruses. This means that if a new virus is developed it won't even be caught until the signature database is updated. While I certainly wouldn't go so far as to call mainstream anti-virus a sham it certainly wouldn't be out of bounds to say that they give the average user a very false sense of security. If I go back to my computer and write a new keylogger that hasn't been seen before chances are anti-virus programs will never catch it. My advice to you is to be careful about what you download. If you stick to legitimate sources for your downloads there's a very low chance you'll pick anything up. Having spent a lot of time working for a college university IT department viruses seem to come from one of a couple places: #1 limewire, #2 piracy in general, #3 fake e-mails, #4 porn. If you stay away from those things chances are you'll be fine.
