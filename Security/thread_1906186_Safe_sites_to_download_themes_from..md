I've seen some pretty shady things come from gnome look. I would just be careful, most of them come in the variety of malicious shell scripts as there really shouldn't be any binaries included in a theme. I would be rather cautious of "binary installers" with themes from an untrusted source as well. 

That's the best advice I can give since there is no way to account for the malicious (or not) intent of a particular theme posted on any given site. Paritcularly since most of those sites do not moderate what goes on them prior to them being posted.

Hope this helps.

### Post by OpSecShellshock on 2012-01-09
A couple years ago the people on this forum did actually identify a bot that had its origins in a theme on gnome-look. Within probably an hour folks were able to find the problem, determine what it did, and get it reported to the admins of that site. Really that's the best we can hope for.

Remember that themes don't actually need installers, and they won't need privilege escalations in order to work. It's really just a matter of decompressing a folder, copying it to a directory, and then selecting the theme from a list in the Appearance utility. I would be wary of any theme that wanted me to run a script or elevate privileges in order to use it.
