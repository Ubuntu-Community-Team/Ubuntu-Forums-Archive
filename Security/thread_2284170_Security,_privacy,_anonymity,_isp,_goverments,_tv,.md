### Post by TheFu on 2015-06-27
I think everything you report is just a coincidence.  

So ... it is highly unlikely that anyone is doing a MITM attack on both video and data traffic.  I haven't been in CR since 2008, but back then the CATV was standard analog and I don't remember any specific channels being possible to push a stream to just 1 household. Without digital cable, it really isn't possible to push a specific channel to a specific home without a truck at the cable head near your neighborhood.

If you want the most security possible from TOR, never use it with your normal OS booted. Only use a liveCD and only choose exit nodes that are outside your country. The USA doesn't care much about you any more than they care about me.  That doesn't mean that the NSA isn't capturing all the data traffic coming from every home in the USA, CR, and 50% of the rest of the world.  

There is a big difference between data capture and being able to decrypt it in a timely way.  128-bit SSL that doesn't trust any CAs is pretty secure. I don't think anyone can decode that in real-time.  For email, I use 4K GPG and don't worry at all about that being decrypted by anyone on Earth. After all, those family recipes **must** remain a family secret!  Basically, the only way the USA will see your traffic when using TOR is if you request unencrypted data from a server inside the USA or use a TOR exit node run by a US Govt agency.

Someone just did a study about Free VPN services - Over 75% didn't take the steps the guy doing the survey/testing thought was necessary to securing traffic. [https://privatoria.net/blog/the-hidden-dangers-of-free-vpns-or-why-a-good-service-costs-money/](https://privatoria.net/blog/the-hidden-dangers-of-free-vpns-or-why-a-good-service-costs-money/)  Any VPN using PPTP is useless against anyone who cares to get your traffic.  PPTP has been hacked multiple times over the years. MSFT doesn't even suggest using it anymore.

Are you certain that your ISP goes through the USA for internet?  [http://maps.level3.com/default/](http://maps.level3.com/default/) shows that traffic from CR probably goes through either FLA or CAL, unless there is an outage. Of course, that map is just level3 - there are other networks and I don't see a few links I'm positive exist.
[http://www.submarinecablemap.com/](http://www.submarinecablemap.com/) - that's better. It shows that your traffic can get to Africa and Europe without going through the USA. It all depends on peering.  It is still missing a link I'm positive exists (not related to CR at all).

Anyway - enjoy the maps.

Pura Vida!
