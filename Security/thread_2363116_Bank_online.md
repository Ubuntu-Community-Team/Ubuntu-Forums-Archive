### Post by HermanAB on 2017-06-09
So, just relax and be happy.  I haven't had security issues with Linux or BSD in many a year.

It doesn't mean to be complacent though - do your security updates once in a while!  I only do updates once a year around Christmas, or when there is a lot of tears and fears about a newly discovered issue - Heartbleed for example.

If an elderly family member complains about viruses and cruft, I give him/her a Macbook Air - problem solved permanently.

If it is a younger member I recommend the Linux flavour of the month, with Windows in a virtual machine or dual boot if they like playing games.  One or two followed my advice, some went and bought a Macbook after playing with granny's.  In general, as soon someone makes the switch all their computer problems disappear and word sloowly gets round.
