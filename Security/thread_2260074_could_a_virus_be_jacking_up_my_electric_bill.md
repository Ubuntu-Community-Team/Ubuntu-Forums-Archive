### Post by kpatz on 2015-01-09
When you say "obscene", how much higher than usual?  I presume it's winter where you are and it's cold and you're probably running the heat more than you were a month ago.

A computer with the CPU fully pegged isn't going to draw "obscenely" more power than when idle.  Maybe a few watts.  It would probably add less than $10 to your bill.

But kerry_s said it, run top to see what's running.  By default it sorts by cpu% so if something is hogging things you'll see it at the top of the list.  Also, if you can hear the fans, you would probably hear them running faster if the computer was generating more heat than usual.

But I would look elsewhere around the house for the source of your electrical usage.

### Post by coldraven on 2015-01-10
If the meter reader person could not gain entry then they usually issue an estimate. You need to check the actual reading on the meter against the units used on the bill.
Look for the letter "E" (or some other mark) next to the reading on the bill.

The other side of the proverbial coin is that if you're recently had a number of estimates which are low followed by a real reading, you could be in for a nasty surprise. Another possibility is if there's been new meter reader who has misread the meter, e.g. reading 1234.5 as 12345.

I haven't had the meter reader over for a while, many of the properties in my area had their meters replaced a couple of years ago with smart meters which send the readings in automatically using one of the local mobile phone networks. Some people had a nasty surprise in the form of higher bills, which was attributed at the time to the replacement of older mechanical meters which had been giving readings that were too low.
