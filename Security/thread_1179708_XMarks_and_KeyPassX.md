### Post by GavCity on 2009-06-06
Xmarks stores your information on one of their servers so if you delete your local information it will just synchronize and bring the information on their server to your local machine again, unless you uninstall the extension.

I use Xmarks myself for bookmarks but don't use the password part of it. I don't mind storing my bookmarks on a server somewhere as someone knowing that I have the BBC site and the Ubuntu Forum etc. bookmarked wouldn't really affect me a great deal. However, although I'm quite sure that the people behind Xmarks aren't dubious, losing all my passwords to a hacker hacking their servers would be a bit of a nightmare. It wasn't so long ago that the job site Monster emailed me about information about me being taken from them.

I see having passwords stored on my computer as a bit of a risk and having them stored elsewhere as well is at least doubling that risk. I don't let Firefox remember my most important passwords, for banks or anything related to money, at all anyway.

My personal advice is to only store things that can't cost you but I'm not an expert.
