### Post by The Cog on 2012-06-13
I don't think there are any specific laws on using Linux or developing games. 

Both Linux and AV software are protected by copyright law of course, so you risk being sued by the authors if you violate their terms when you make copies of that software.

Many other laws may apply, for instance if your game includes extreme violence or pornography, but these laws are not specific to Linux, AV or games.

### Post by The Cog on 2012-06-14
There is no legal requirement for you to run antivirus software that I am aware of. 

Depending on circumstances, you may have a general legal requirement to take "reasonable care" or to excercise "due diligence", e.g. of other people's data that you hold on your computers. What is reasonable would presumably depend on the value of the data, size of the percieved threat etc. and would probably eventually be decided by the courts in a negligence suit. If these circumstances might apply to you, you should seek proper proffesional advice rather than ask here.
