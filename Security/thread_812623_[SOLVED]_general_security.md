### Post by sonofusion82 on 2008-05-30
you are right that firestarter is not a firewall by itself. it is just a configuration frontend for iptable which is the firewall built into the kernel.
for anti-virus, well, it is generally a subjective matter. for now and the near future, very little (if any) virus actually target linux. for me, there is no real need for anti-virus.

### Post by lisati on 2008-05-30
For me, a combo of the defaults for Ubuntu and what came built into my router & ADSL modem are usually sufficient in the firewall department. 
As for AV under Linux it's more a matter of courtesy to avoid passing on something nasty to the Windows machines I'm likely to be in contact with. (I run a dual boot Ubuntu & XP machine, and an XP-only machine; my neighbour who shares my internet connection uses NT (I think); and the family members I'm most likely to contact by email use assorted flavours of Windwos)

For the most part, a modicum of good sense combined with keeping a cool head are a good place to start.
