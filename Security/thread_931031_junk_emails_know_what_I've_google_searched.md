### Post by Dr Small on 2008-09-27
That, I highly doubt. It sounds more like his Google Account is monitoring everything he is searching for, and linking his Google Account to his email address. Thus, Google is sending his information on to third party companies, and it then gets passed around to the proper company that emails him about his recent search. That doesn't sound far fetched at all.

A simple expiriment that I would try.
Log out of your Google Account, delete cookies, and then try the same test of searching for something that you haven't been receiving in your inbox. Check 24 hours later and see if you get any emails regarding the subject you searched for.

Also, another test, try a different search engine and attempt the same thing. If you continue to receive emails about the things you are searching for, while signed out of Google and or at a different search engine, then DrMega may very well be correct. Sounds like spyware.

But, if the emails stop, then it is undoubtedly Google.

Dr Small

Of course the laws in Britain are only applicable to British firms and subsiduaries, but the law also prohibits companies from sending the data (or making it accessible) outside of the UK without the subject's consent, so that would pretty much rule out a common google wide practice of misusing the data.

Incidentally, I use google all the time as my preferred search engine, and I use some of their other features, and I've never had any trouble of this nature.
