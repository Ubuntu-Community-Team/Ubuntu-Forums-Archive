### Post by Paddy Landau on 2017-04-12
> **macdonald3 said:**
> &#8230; Am I relatively safe with Ubuntu?
Relatively, yes.

Linux desktop & laptop has far fewer malware attempts than Windows for the simple reason that it is far less popular. (Android &#8212; which runs on Linux &#8212; obviously has plenty.)

The Linux community has a strong record of quickly fixing security bugs once they have been found, sometimes within hours.

As long as you adhere to the security policy of Linux (i.e. use a strong password for your account and keep your system up-to-date), and beware both scams and dodgy websites, you should be safe.

There is never a guarantee, of course, so ensure that you back up your data preferably daily, but you are pretty safe.

It sounds like you came across another fake tech support scam. Another poster came with the same issue. Here is the advice I gave her: [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2344822&page=2](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2344822&page=2)

I'm sorry. It seems as if you reinstalled your system for no reason since these are just scary looking, fake websites that pop up trying to get you to call their so-called "tech support." You can read more about that here: [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_support_scam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_support_scam)

If you ever come across one again just close out the page. However, sometimes they do have pop up boxes that you must close before you're allowed to close the browser window, in which case, they keep popping up boxes, sometimes with alarm bells and a voice telling you you're infected. You may need to open a terminal or your processes list to kill the browser window to get around that.

As far as these messages popping up on Ubuntu it can happen, but again, just close out the window just as on Windows.
