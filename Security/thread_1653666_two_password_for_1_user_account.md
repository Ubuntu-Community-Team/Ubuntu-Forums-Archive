### Post by jroa on 2010-12-27
I have never heard of having two passwords for one account.  I don't think it is possible. 

Why would you need two different passwords?  Do you want to let someone have temporary access to your system?  It would be better to create a Guest account that you can delete when the person is no longer welcome on your computer.  You probably do not want the person to access your account directly because they would also have your sudo privileges.  

Or, you could temporarily change your password and then change it again when your guest is done.  But, once again they would be able to sudo with your account.
