### Post by jp.hendrix on 2011-11-25
Late response, I know. Probably you found your solution, but other people may find this thread on Google and ... find no answer.

People all over the world are always trying if they can log in to your system through SSH, it is a fact of life, live with it. What these people do is trying to brute force into your system, by trying hundreds (if not thousands) of login name / password combinations. SSH facilitates in secure logging on the a system:

1. Always make sure you have your system fully patched;
2. Consider if you really need SSH access from the Internet;
3. Disable password login through SSH, enable key authentication. If you log on to your box over the network, you do *NOT* want a Password:-prompt.

Keywords to Google for 3. are: PasswordAuthentication no, UsePAM no, PubkeyAuthentication yes, authorized_keys, id_rsa, ssh-keygen, ...

Check this: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SSH/OpenSSH/Keys](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SSH/OpenSSH/Keys)
