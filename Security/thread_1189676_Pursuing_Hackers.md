### Post by scorp123 on 2009-06-18
> **scott_ttocs46 said:**
>  My question is: If you're not a fortune 500 company, what realistic avenues do you have of pursuing a hacker [legally]? You could try and talk to the guy's provider... You said you can obtain the hacker's IP? So a "whois" should give you his provider. Maybe they will react and terminate his Internet connection. Most Internet service providers in the US and the EU do have an "abuse" clause and if you provide them with enough proofs they might indeed do something like that. No guarantee though. But if you succeed you'd at least have the satisfaction that the hacker is now forced to find himself a new ISP. Maybe. Maybe not.

But: Depending on what country that hack attack originated from and depending on what country that provider is from they may not give care one little bit about your wee wee problems. In fact the wannabe hackers and the people working for the provider might be the same guys. And they will not hesitate one second to sell the e-mail address you just provided them with in your complaint to their spammer colleagues. Of course they will happily ignore the rest, regardless if they speak English or not.

So if the IP address you got hold of is from a private company and/or some Internet provider in India, Korea, Russia, Ukraine, China .... Forget it. They will sell your e-Mail to their spammer friends and that's it. 

Just reprogram your firewall to block all the connections from that IP range and that's about all you can do.

Your best chances are if the hacker is located in the US or EU.
