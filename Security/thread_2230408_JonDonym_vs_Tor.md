### Post by Nobody Nessie on 2014-06-19
I guess there are a lot of things to say.

I already tried both of them (including JonDo premium) and it is hard to choose between them. I am assuming that all of us are using a VPN or SSH tunnel first before entering Tor or Jondo. 

Tor is free, slow, with a large users bases, a part of its network is really dirty, it is almost useful only for the darknet (or for journalists, militaries, activists in difficult countries, using hidden services). Most of the Tor exit nodes IP are blocked for a lot of websites and we can expect that most (a few of them, or most of them sounds similar to me when comes up the question of the privacy) of the exit nodes are logging your traffic, or sniffing your ID data, etc. There are a lot of studies to "desanonymize" Tor users, by sniffing exit nodes, by controlling several nodes in a path, by slowing down a Tor path to correlate with a target traffic speed, etc. I beleive (I hope), most of us are using Tor for anonymity, but for legal purposes (anonymity should be a right, till you are not doing illegal things). It is my case. The digital world is something out of control and I would prefer to prevent now what could happen later about our own right to privacy.

JonDonym is very smaller. You can not access Tor Hidden services and it is focus on the clearnet. You have 4 free 2-mix cascades, as slow as Tor is, with an overall number of 2000 users at the same time. Like Tor, each one has the same browser fingerprint (until you enable javascript) and something like 11 premium (non free) 3-mix cascades with a smaller number of users. Unlike Tor, the intelligence agencies have surely less to focus on this anonymous network as it can only access clearnet. No child porns (JonDo has a tracker, as far a I know for this kind of thing, if I am wrong, please, feel free to correct it), drugs or weapons hidden services to put down, it also means less "high criminals" on that network. With the premimum account, you get a good speed, not as fast as a fast VPN (your data have to be encrypted 3 times, + packets retiming and resizing to make harder any traffic analysis). The bad point is that your entry mix server can help determining your exit mix server because, unlike Tor, the circuits are fixed, even if mix servers and operators are crossed and have in charge a partucular mix server used by different circuits. One mix server is used for different circuits, as entry, middle or exit server. The biggest point for me is that your anonymity depends also of your behavior. When I tried JonDo premium, I paid with paypal, which means : with my name, address and credit card number (but you can also pay anonymously by different ways). I does not mean I have nothing to hide, it means that I prefer to wear clothes when I walk in the street, and I am used to avoid dangerous or worring quarters or streets (like I avoid Tor hidden services and Tor exit nodes. The clearnet is enough for me) : But all depends what privacy and security means for you. Unless you are doing something highly illegal, you will never get all the necessary international warrants in all mix and operators countries with JonDonym. It is definitively, for me, a cleaner anonymous network but with a possibly lower anonymity strengh, but still better than with VPN providers.

But this is just to open the topic, I suppose there are a much more to say !
