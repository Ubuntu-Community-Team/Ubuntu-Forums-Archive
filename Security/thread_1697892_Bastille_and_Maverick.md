### Post by cariboo on 2011-03-01
From the looks of the [web page]("http://bastille-linux.sourceforge.net/") Bastille hasn't been updated since 2008.

I you are just running a normal desktop system with no services enabled, you really don't need to go to the extra effort of installing and configuring Bastille. 3 years is a long time in internet years, so there could be quite a few things missing.

I'd suggest you read bodhi,zazen's security stickies at the top of the page, and implement what you feel you need.

### Post by standingfire on 2011-03-01
I have used Bastille on every load of ubuntu since 2007, just load perl-tk package so that you have a readable gui. It asks clear concise questions and you have choices. You can use it best as a guide and correct some of the out of the box issues all distros have relative to security. 

When you are complete, you may loosen or lock down more, depending on your requirements.
