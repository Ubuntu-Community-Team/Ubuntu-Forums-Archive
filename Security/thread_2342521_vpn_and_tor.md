### Post by isolated-thinker on 2016-11-10
The nice thing about connecting with Tor is that your entrance and exit nodes change. Using a vpn forces those nodes to be pseudo-perminate thus increasing your chance of being tracked.
If you use a vpn before connecting to Tor, you are cutting your internet speed down without adding any extra security. If you connect through Tor then go through a vpn, that is when you force Tor to use psudo-perminate nodes. 

Since Tor is already encrypted, and if you spoof your MAC ID (like Tails can do), there really is no reason to add a vpn into the works.
