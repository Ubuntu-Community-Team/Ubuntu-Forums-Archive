### Post by FunkyRes on 2011-05-31
I'm not sure about this, but -

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto/Nvidia](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto/Nvidia)

That is documentation on a .ubuntu.com domain.
Within that documentation is a link to what appears to be a third party web site:

[http://www.webupd8.org/2010/06/how-to-install-nvidia-25635-display.html](http://www.webupd8.org/2010/06/how-to-install-nvidia-25635-display.html)

At least for me, there was big graphic that says "Download Now Update Nvidia Driver" that links to a doubleclick.net link.

webupd8.org does not appear to be a web site under the control of ubuntu, has mis-leading social engineering advertisements on it, and thus I most certainly do not trust it as a place to give accurate information on how to add/upgrade a PPA.

Please have someone update that documentation to link to a site on a Ubuntu domain that instructs users on how to add the PPA. Or better yet, just put the darn instructions on the BinaryDriverHowto page itself.

Furthermore, please implement a policy disallowing links to third party web sites in documentation hosted on a .ubuntu.com domain. That's just asking for a social engineering exploit that will make the distribution look sad and pathetic, and already makes it look unprofessional.

webupd8.org may be good people (I really don't know), but they (and others) that are not under the control of Ubuntu should not be linked to from the Ubuntu online documentation as sources for information.

Thank you for your time,

Michael A. Peters
