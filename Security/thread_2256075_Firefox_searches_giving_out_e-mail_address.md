### Post by PondPuppy on 2014-12-10
It would be interesting to know where the specific emails originate. For instance, I have done business with Amazon, and so they have one of my email addresses. I get spam email from them sometimes.

I also know that if I use Google, Bing, Yahoo, or Yandex without no-tracking measures, some details of my searches are recorded. The info is then used to service clients of the advertising businesses associated with the companies -- Google Ads, Yahoo Bing Advertising, Russian Search Marketing, whatever.

So theoretically, if a company has your email address and it has bought info about your web searches, it can send you targeted emails. I don't find that very common in my experience.

Again theoretically, a third-party firm could buy lists of email addresses (legally, black-market, or gray-market) and use those even if you have not intentionally given your email to them. (Some time ago I sent a small donation to a conservation organization -- and they apparently "shared" my mailing address with everybody from "Cats for the Poor" to "Save the Seaweed", bless their hearts. I get ten or twenty solicitations a week as a result of one donation.)

But I'd guess the operative word in the OP is "search": it's the search engine which is blabbing to the advertisers.

You might try using Startpage.com, IxQuick.com, or DuckDuckGo.com for searches. These do not track your searches, and all three are reputable.

You might also try installing Ghostery, Disconnect, or other privacy add-ons to Firefox, if you haven't already.

------

Oh, addendum: If you do try something new like different search site or new add-on, you might post back and tell us if it made a difference, and if so, what that difference was.
