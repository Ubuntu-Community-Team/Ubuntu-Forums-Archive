### Post by OpSecShellshock on 2010-02-27
Keyloggers in Windows are also installed by the users themselves; they just don't know it. Knowing what you're installing is the best defense. Any software that purports (regardless of OS) to undermine keyloggers is either failing to take into account that they also include form-data grabbers or is intentionally avoiding that point to sell you their product. There is always (and will probably always be) a point at which the credentials submitted will match what the authentication mechanism is looking for.
