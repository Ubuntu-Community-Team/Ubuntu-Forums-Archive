### Post by rookcifer on 2010-05-05
A few thoughts:

Search engines: Use startpage -- it seems to be the best option for privacy, even though I find it slow.  Another option is [Yauba]("http://www.yauba.com/").  if you prefer Google, you can use one of the scrubbing front-ends like Scroogle.

As for encryption:  Whole disk encryption with LUKS is a good idea.  If you really want to go 007, you can install your Linux partitions hidden within a "decoy" Windows OS (Truecrypt has this ability -- you can read more on their page).

As for IM's: Yes, everyone should be using encryption if you value your privacy, especially since (in the U.S.) there is no 4th amendment rights when it comes to computer data (like e-mail or chat logs) older than 180 days! (I just recently found this out -- it was passed in 1986).  On top of that, we have the PATRIOT Act which further erodes privacy, and, as the guy above said, most ISP's in most countries are now required by law to store your data for months.  Here in the U.S., the FBI has been lobbying Congress to make this 6 month voluntary rule a mandatory 2 year rule.  I believe the EU has similar laws.

So, as for Pidgin, I recommend people use the OTR plugin.  It is better than the plain vanilla SSL plugin because it provides for plausible deniability.  Moreover, it is resistant to MITM attacks, which are probably SSL's biggest weakness (because CA's can't always be trusted).  OTR is essentially PGP for IM -- the key fingerprints are verified in person, over the phone, etc.
