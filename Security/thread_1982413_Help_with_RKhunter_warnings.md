### Post by Ms. Daisy on 2012-05-18
I haven't used rkhunter so I can't speak to your specific files. However, rkhunter is notorious for false positives in Ubuntu.  See this for more help:
 
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1805555](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1805555)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1679780](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1679780)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1875239](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1875239)
 
The crux is that once you get a base scan, you will get far less false positives.  But whenever there is an update to certain parts of the system, you'll have to create another baseline or you'll get more false positives.  You'll always have to google suspicious results because of the high percentage of false positives.
