### Post by coffeecat on 2016-07-04
First of all - you have nothing to worry about.

To pick up a few points from your post:

[quote=mohammadbagheri2010]Length of numbers has been changed completely.[/quote]

That's not unusual. Any of the four numbers in an (IPv4) IP address can be anything from 0 to 255. See this wikipedia article:

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address)

[quote=mohammadbagheri2010]I found it somewhere in Iran that is definetely NOT my location!!!! [/quote]

That's not unusual either. If I lookup my current IP address in this site - [https://www.iplocation.net/](https://www.iplocation.net/) - it tells me variously that I am in London or in a Northern English city over 200 miles from London. I am in neither!

But to your specifics. As a forum admin I have access to records of the IP addresses that you (and all members) have used when registering with or posting to this forum. Don't worry - that is quite normal with all forums, and we keep that information strictly confidential. Only forum staff have access to it, and we would never divulge it to a third party. What I can tell you is that the forum software has recorded 3 distinct IP addresses from you, two of which are of the form you mention. I have looked them up in a whois site, and they are all from the same Iranian ISP. The explanation is that you have a dynamically assigned IP address which will change regularly. This is quite common with domestic connections. It's probable that most ISPs around the world use dynamically assigned IP addresses for their customers. In my case when I first joined the forum, I was with an ISP that changed my IP address very frequently and the forum software has recorded several hundred different IP addresses that I have used since that time.
