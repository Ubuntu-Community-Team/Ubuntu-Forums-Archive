### Post by TheFu on 2014-12-11
> **fugu2 said:**
> Geez, what did yout "friend" do?

In some countries, often those with strong fundamental religious ties, but other places too, accessing forbidden content is illegal, especially if that content is anti-government or pornographic by local standards.  Often in those countries, the government runs the ISP and telcos, so getting around that filtering is only possible to smart, technologist who use stronger-than-normal VPNs that terminate in other, more tolerant, counties.  Generally, wealthy people will have an IPSec VPN service to either Europe or North America, while less wealthy people either won't have any VPN or will use PPTP believing that is secure, which it is not.  In some places, using a VPN or VoIP of any sort is illegal. Many places like that will have block lists for commercial VPN providers around the world, so making a friend who can terminal an ssh tunnel in a different country is highly desirable.

Many countries have filtering rules that would surprise us. [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_censorship_and_surveillance_by_country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_censorship_and_surveillance_by_country) 

OTOH, I don't know anything about the OP or where he lives.  I do know that I've traveled to places with filter rules and have been blocked from accessing my blog (nothing controversial there, IMHO) for some reason sometimes, but not always.  Generally the filtering hasn't prevented any access that I noticed. Many countries feel the need to protect their citizens from anything deemed "controversial" by local standards on the internet.  I'm not taking any stance on this, but in my country, I find any filters offensive even if I dislike the content and wouldn't want my family or myself seeing it.  That doesn't mean it is my place to restrict that content for others, outside my family.  That judgment is between that person and whatever deity they believe, IMHO.
