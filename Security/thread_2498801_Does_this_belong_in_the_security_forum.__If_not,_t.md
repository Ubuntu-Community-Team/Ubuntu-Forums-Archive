### Post by 0f4d0335 on 2024-06-29
Despite being off-topic to Ubuntu, the security concern is a compromise of confidentiality (your personal identifiable information). Here's where you should feel a bit safer: though it's an attack on your privacy, it's only a potential risk until someone actively uses it or archives the information. So as long as no one is currently doing this (and you may not know), then the threat impact likelihood is very minimal, especially since most strangers online are not looking to hurt others. Regardless, the risk is still out there. What can you do?

You will need to contact YouTube. This is certainly against their TOS (terms of service), which will result in a ban on the account who abused their policy. And this may be done automatically or may have been reported by someone else by the time you see this.

So if the video is still up, be sure to report it to the abuse authorities of those applications. They will be legally / regulatory required to take it down for you once reported.

Best of luck. ):P
