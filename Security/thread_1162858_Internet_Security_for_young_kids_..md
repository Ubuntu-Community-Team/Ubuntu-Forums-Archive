### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-05-18
You might want to use some parental control software to reduce the amount of bad things he could be exposed to, but in my opinion, education is the best thing to prevent serious issues. Nevertheless, I wouldn't allow him to access the net on it's own or without parental control software, until he is mature enough to understand the risks and follow your recommendations.

I guess the most important things are related to [social engineering](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_(security)) and user identity. He should learn how to protect his privacy, which is kind of hard with the social network fever out there, and learn that nobody can be trusted. I might be a little bit paranoid, but who knows who is in the other side of the keyboard? 

If I had a child I would teach him to stay away from social networks or at least be very careful about which information is disclosed and only accept the contact of friends he knows in the real life. It might sound a little bit early to teach this, but he will be wanting to use these tools pretty soon.

I also think that teaching him how to properly search the web using tools like Google is a must. Whenever I find some suspicious activity, e-mail or whatever, I go to Google and search for the subject before taking any action. Most of the time I find the information I need. Identifying trusted web sites and sources of information is also an important part of this process. 

I would also teach him to protect the identity and the privacy of others. I would teach him how simple things like sending an e-mail to bunch of contacts without using the BCC feature can cause so many trouble. I still don't believe how people that I know, who are well educated, can fall for so many stupid things the receive by e-mail, then send a message to hundreds of people with my e-mail attached. Do we distribute our friends home addresses to anyone freely? No. So why we should do it on the net?

There are several tools like Firefox extensions that can help you to protect your child activity on the net. Try [PublicFox](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/3911) and check others related to parental control. Take a look [here](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/search/?q=Parental+Control), [here](http://www.google.com.br/search?q=Parental+Control+site%3Ahttps%3A%2F%2Faddons.mozilla.org&ie=utf-8&oe=utf-8&aq=t&rls=com.ubuntu:en-US:unofficial&client=firefox-a) and [here](http://www.google.com.br/search?q=Parental+Control+Linux&ie=utf-8&oe=utf-8&aq=t&rls=com.ubuntu:en-US:unofficial&client=firefox-a).

Good luck.
