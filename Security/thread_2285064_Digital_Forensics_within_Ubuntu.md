### Post by TheFu on 2015-07-03
In as much as digital forensics is about understanding where stuff gets stored and how it can be modified and left behind, you'll need to be at least a power-user for every OS you intend to address.  To learn the internals of Linux, a distro like Arch/Slackware, then Gentoo would be my uninformed (I know next to nothing about formal forensics) recommendation. Also, becoming an expert as regex will payback in spades and knowing how to script a little will make a 2 hr manual job into a 5 min automatic job.

You've probably seen this list: [http://resources.infosecinstitute.com/linux-and-disk-forensics/](http://resources.infosecinstitute.com/linux-and-disk-forensics/)

To become a power-user for Linux, learn the shell. [http://linuxcommand.org/tlcl.php](http://linuxcommand.org/tlcl.php)
