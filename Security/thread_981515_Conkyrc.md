### Post by transmition on 2008-11-13
I have noticed that if you want conky to check your gmail account, you must include your gmail username and password in your .conkyrc file. While this is fairly convenient, it would be a simple matter for someone to open the file and gain access to your account.

Would it be possible to make it so that in order to view the files contents, one would have to supply an administrative password? (As opposed to regular encryption, which would require that conky decrypt the file in order to read it)

### Post by cariboo on 2008-11-13
If someone has access to your computer with malicious intent, you have far more to worry about than your gmail account. Use a strong password, and always log out or lock the screen, when you are away from your desktop, to keep casual lookers away. If you are using Intrepid open the guest account when you are away. that way they can play without harming anything.
