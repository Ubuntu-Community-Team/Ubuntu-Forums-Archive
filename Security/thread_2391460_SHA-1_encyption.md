### Post by QIII on 2018-05-09
Hello!

Everyone is fee to use whatever encryption they want.  So the answer to your stated question is "probably not every site."

SHA-1 is old and probably not useful for bullet-proof security.  SHA-1 most often used in general to produce a hash that can be used to ensure that a file has not been tampered with.  Still not perfect.  Google has proven that SHA-1 collisions do occur.

Although some sites may use it for password hashes, SHA-2 or 3 are recommended.
