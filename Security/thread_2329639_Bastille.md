### Post by DuckHook on 2016-07-04
I am not familiar with "packages" that try to harden our systems. Security-conscious users tend to be a suspicious lot and therefore like to home-spin their security from individual services, apps and procedures. See the link in my sig: Security Basics.

There are whole distros that are security-based, more-so than the various 'buntus in which user ease-of-use and convenience is emphasized over security. I'll just mention two: *Tails* and *Qubes OS*, but it is easy enough to Google for more. They are all more secure out of the box, but more complex and inconvenient too. This is always the trade-off when dealing with security: more safety = less convenience and vice versa.

I will make one observation even at the risk of offence.

Many new users, especially from Windows, make the mistake of thinking about security as an "app" or a "service" or a technical "fix" of some kind. This is a bad way to think about security. Real security is a process and has far more to do with an ever increasing user awareness and fundamental changes in behaviour than it does with any app or service. The earlier mentioned link in my sig will start you off on a far better security footing than any app you are likely to install. It does take you through the installation and configuration of many apps and services, but it is the general attitude and philosophy taught by bodhi.zazen (the thread's author) that is of real value and the true essence of security. Apps and services are just tools. It is what we do with those tools, and *why* we use them than constitute real security.
