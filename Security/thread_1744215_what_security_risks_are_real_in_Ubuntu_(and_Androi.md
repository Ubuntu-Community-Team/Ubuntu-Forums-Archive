### Post by tpprynn on 2011-04-30
I've just gone back to Ubuntu 10.04 for the moment, and don't have mobile Broadband credit to add the updates. Because of this and because I've just bought an Android tablet (£75 on eBay) I'm wondering what are the _real_ risks with Linux, as opposed to what are theoretical risks which we generally install updates to protect us against.

I've seen someone say once, possibly in this category of this forum, that with Linux even with ufw not enabled we are pretty safe. I've enabled ufw, I always do, and I don't see anything weird happening, and have also been in this lack of many updates situation several times while distro-hopping.

Is it that various breaches _would_ eventually or soon happen, or that they may, or that the real risks are practically negligible? If I installed 9.04 (still my favorite edition) or 8.04 now, how would the real security issues change?

I don't use any risky sites, mostly just Gmail, Wikipedia, Vimeo and a couple of forums like this these days. My brother's Vista laptop had 130 viruses removed from it by me following his son's regular use of Flash games, and he has also been safe and not robbed.

I see when it comes to Android - maybe because of its current prevalence on phones - there are risks, and I think my brother-in-law had something dodgy on his phone through over-casual use of Android Market, leading to the quick loss over one weekend of about £20, some programs that wouldn't uninstall and stop charging fees despite issuing the famous 'STOP' text. I generally don't go online with my tablet, it's just a small word processor and media player to me. But if the security issues have become greater since the popularity of the tablets and phones then it makes me wonder what the state of Linux security is right now, in general, familiar ideology aside. I did wonder also, with the price of some tablets being so low, whether some are actually sold with keylogging programs pre-installed, that in other words online crime might make their cheap price worthwhile. But maybe that's paranoia...

Thanks for all thoughts and facts.
