### Post by SeijiSensei on 2016-06-14
I've used KDE-based distros for at least fifteen years, and Kubuntu for the past eight (starting with 8.04).  Security has never been an issue for me.

The KDE applications in Kubuntu are compiled and tested by the Debian and Ubuntu developers, just like the other 40,000+ applications in the repositories.  

I don't have any KDE repos in my sources lists.  I don't see any reason to do so.  I haven't upgraded to KDE5 yet, in part because I like to wait a few months for the LTE versions to get all the bugs worked out.  But my tests of KDE5/16.04 have yet to give me any compelling reason to switch.  I wouldn't be surprised to be using Kubuntu 14.04 until its end-of-life three years from now.

Avoiding KDE/Kubuntu for "security" reasons is closer to the paranoid end of your spectrum in my mind.

### Post by xen3 on 2016-06-17
There is just not going to be any difference unless you really know what you're thinking of.

Don't forget that malware typically won't make it to official repositories, and with that out of the way, the only way a Linux system can be breached, is through exposed services.

These are typically going to be identical from system to system, ie. from Kubuntu to Ubuntu and the like.
