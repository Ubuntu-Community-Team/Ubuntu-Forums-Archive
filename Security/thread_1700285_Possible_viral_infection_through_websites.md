### Post by dionysius on 2011-03-05
Well I *did* open the site, has a clean record (Opera Fraud & Malware) and the WOT website has little or no information about it. It redirects to bakati.com, which again has a clean record, but WOT shows people have some issues with the website. 

Personally, I wouldn't worry too much about it. It's good to be paranoid, but viruses are the least of *nix users concern. Even if you were to download something without being aware of it, it would:
* need to be able to run on your system
* need the right permissions

Though I don't trust websites that redirect and then offer to show me fabulous deals on everything under the sun, I suspect Windows users will need to be much more cautious about visiting these sites than we need be. 

You *can* install a virus scanner, however they'll scan for Windows executables and a few Linux baddies that haven't been seen in the wild for ages and even if they were to show up on your system, are highly unlikely to be able to do anything. 

If it makes you feel more safe and secure having a virus scanner on your computer by all means - it just doesn't do much to protect your system. A safe browser, frequent (security) updates and careful browsing on the other hand will help your system stay healthy.
