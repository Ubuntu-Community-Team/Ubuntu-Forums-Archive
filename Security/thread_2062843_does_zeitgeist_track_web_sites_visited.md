### Post by ottosykora on 2012-09-26
it does track this and that and stores its knowledge local. It not supposed to report it anywhere by itself. It record the use of browser as whole etc, so you may not find single websites logged as text entries somewhere.


There are some thoughts however about zeitgeist, as when something stores diffuse knowledge about the users behavior, it makes it more simple for potential malware to  grab this data and make some use of it.

This is why many people try to restrict the zeitgeist operation as much as possible. Unfortunately we are no more able to simply uninstall it, as this will break for example unity desktop too much.

I am one of those who hate zeitgeist.
