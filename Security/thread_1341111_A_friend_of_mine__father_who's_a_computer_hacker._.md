### Post by OpSecShellshock on 2009-11-29
If he has physical access to the computers, there's not much they can do. If he doesn't, but he still communicates with them and they can prove it, then he is in violation of the restraining order and can be arrested.

Was he the one who initially set up the network? Is his name on the ISP bill, giving him access to that account info? Does he have logins on any of the machines? Also, what makes them think he's the one doing it?

What you seem to be referring to is the Pwn to Own contest at CanSecWest. And yes, the Mac went first, then the Windows, and not the Ubuntu box. But, you have to understand that it's mostly because no one bothered to try. That said, the default setup of Ubuntu is less accessible to remote intruders (both human and bot) than the default setups of the other two.

Anyway, advise them all to start new email accounts that he doesn't know about and put really strong passwords on them. They shouldn't get rid of the accounts he knows about, but should only do inconsequential communication through them while doing the important stuff on the new accounts (if they wipe out the old accounts, he'll know something's up, but if they continue using them, he's seeing what he expects to see and will be misdirected). They should stop using social networking sites entirely. Also, they should make up nonsense answers to "security questions" when setting up online accounts from now on.

Now, as much as I'd advise all these things, please understand that network and data safety is probably the least of their concerns.
