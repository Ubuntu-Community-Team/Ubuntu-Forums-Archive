### Post by MarkC502 on 2010-03-13
Why would the old owner care, I have owned at least 50 PC's in my life and I can only say where 6 are right now ( The 6 I use ). Unless you suspect that your purchase is stolen property then I would forget about any such notion.

In general you can be tracked as follows 

1. By sofware installed on the machine to aid in theft/loss recovery. Or just a VNC server that the thief is too stupid to realize is running, then login and do a "whats my IP" and then call the cops LOL

2. By technology built into the lalptop itself, there are several of these types of systems out there in mostly high end laptops.

3. By the MAC address of the laptop. Every network device has a MAC address and while this can be temporarily changed it could prove a valid way to track down a stolen laptop. For example someone steals my laptop at a college campus and I might be able to ask an IT guy at the college to see if that MAC shows up etc and email me so I can then call the police to retrieve it.


You took out #1 when you installed Ubuntu. You can rule out #2 since you said its old and I doubt its equiped. And #3 only matters if your laptop is stolen goods because it would only be possible to do this with the help of others in local IT & law enforcement.
