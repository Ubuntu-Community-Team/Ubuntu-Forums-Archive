### Post by cariboo on 2010-04-16
I haven't had a virus in an email for about 10 years, but if there is a virus attached to an email in Thunderbird, it shows up as an attachment, which can be easily removed. 

If you are getting viruses in your emails, it may be time to drop the free email service providers. My ISP does active virus scanning and spam filtering before the emails even hit my inbox. I may get one or two spams every other month at the most, and I've never seen a virus since I started with the them

If you really need active virus scanning it looks like Bitdefender is one of the few that do.

### Post by OpSecShellshock on 2010-04-17
I have yet to receive so much as one unsolicited message on my local client. I rarely check the web client, but when I do I'll occasionally see spam in there. I just don't have the items in there forward to my desktop.

In any case, e-mail borne malware usually gets delivered through links or html rendering within the mail client instead of attachments, which are too easy to strip or remove. And they'll almost certainly be written for Windows.
