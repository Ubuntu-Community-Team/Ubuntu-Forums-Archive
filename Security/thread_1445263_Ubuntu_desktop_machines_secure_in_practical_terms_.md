### Post by julianb on 2010-04-02
A story about computer security:

> Two guys are starting out on a hike in the woods. One says to the other, "You know, I'm kind of worried about getting attacked by bears. I hear there are a lot of bears in this area."

The other guy says, "I'm not scared."

"Why not?"

"I'm a pretty fast runner, you know."

"Faster than a bear? They can run over 30 miles per hour!"

"No, I don't have to be faster than a bear, just faster than you!"

Linux isn't perfectly secure. But it doesn't have to be. When it comes to home computer systems that aren't storing lots of high-value data (lists of credit card numbers, that sort of thing) the attacks are going to be going after the easiest way to exploit large numbers of machines. (To mix our metaphors, many attackers are after "low hanging fruit".)

So for this particular type of attack, Ubuntu systems are just not the target. Not that our security is the very best, but it IS better than the next guy's. (you know, that really popular OS.) 

And yes, this is similar to the "security by obscurity" argument but not the same. Being "more secure than the next guy" and being obscure both matter *only* when the stuff you're protecting doesn't have particularly high value.
