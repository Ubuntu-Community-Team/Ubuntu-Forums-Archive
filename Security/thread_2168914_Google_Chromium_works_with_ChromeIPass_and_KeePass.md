### Post by Welly Wu on 2013-08-19
I finally got Google Chromium to work with ChromeIPass and KeePass 2 successfully tonight. I had to download a special KeePassHttp.dll and Newton.Json.dll files and I copied those files to my /usr/lib/keepass2 directory using the sudo cp command. I cleared my plug-in cache and re-compiled the KeePassHttp and KeePassRPC plug-ins successfully and I authenticated into ChromeIPass using my private encryption key for my System76 Lemur Ultra Thin (lemu4) and Google Chromium web browser. It works. I can use KeePass 2 and ChromeIPass to log into my accounts and to access my confidential secure notes in both Mozilla Firefox and Google Chromium.

On a side note, I finally got the new Pipelight project to work with the Chrome User Agent Switcher plug-ins so I can watch my Netflix streaming account in Google Chromium. I also got Spotify Premium and Hulu Plus to work in Google Chromium and Mozilla Firefox.

It was a lot of hard work doing the research and the hacking, but it was well worth it. Everything is backed up to multiple locations and to my CrashPlan+ account along with Deja Dup.

I am happy and tired tonight.
