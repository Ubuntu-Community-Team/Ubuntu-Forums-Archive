### Post by peculiar penguin on 2010-11-01
She needs to
- call her relatives/friends/whatever contacts immediately and tell them not to send money to the scammer,
- get to a clean computer and change/reset ALL her passwords,
- nuke and reinstall her computer (unless she plans on getting the law involved, but the chances of that going anywhere are pretty much zero),
- learn about safe computer usage (complex passwords, don't enter them on untrusted computers, don't click on all those funny links people send you, etc.).

### Post by robsoles on 2010-11-01
You may have to just report the email account as a spamming scammer and make your friend a new email account.

It is probable that someone else will report the rogue email account before long but it is always better to report your account as compromised to the provider of the account as soon as you know - if it is their policy to just kill accounts that get compromised then at least your or your-friend's name isn't being used by a criminal.

If it's within their policy to help you restore the account it will be quicker and easier with their assistance.
