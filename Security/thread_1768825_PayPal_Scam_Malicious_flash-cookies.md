### Post by Dry Lips on 2011-05-27
A few days ago I signed up with PayPal, and yesterday I got a couple of strange-looking emails. I hadn't confirmed my password yet, and I got two emails sent a couple of hours apart that encouraged me to confirm my email adress. The mail seemed authentic, but Thunderbird warned that this might be a possible scam, so I didn't click any of the links in the email.
 The mail was sent from this address: [EMAIL="paypal2@info.paypal.com"]paypal2@info.paypal.com[/EMAIL], whereas all the other mails I've got from PayPal came from: [EMAIL="service@intl.paypal.com"]service@intl.paypal.com[/EMAIL].
 

 I'm pretty confident that this is a scam, all of links in the email pointed to the same address:
 [https://email0.paypal.com]("https://email0.paypal.com/") + *many, many letters and numbers in the url*. The authentic email included my username in the url, and didn't look anything like the url in mail the scammers sent me.I did report those two mails straight away.
 

 What I need to figure out, is how the scammers could have gotten my email-address and how they could have found out that I had registered with PayPal. The only thing that I can think about is flash-cookies. I use firefox with NoScripts, BetterPrivacy, etc, but sometimes I have to use other browsers, and perhaps I've been a little too lazy when it comes to removing flash-cookies afterwards.
 Does this explanation s seem plausible?
 

 And if the problem was malicious flash cookies, could they have recorded sensitive information such as passwords on forums, etc? (Fortunately I have no credit card) Would changing passwords be a good precaution?
