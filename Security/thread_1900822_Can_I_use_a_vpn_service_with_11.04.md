### Post by /dev/random/username on 2011-12-27
Yes you can use VPN on linux.. and the software needed to establish the connection is also free.. such as openvpn or pptp. If you are looking for a VPN Service for free, you may get disappointed by the limits/caps on bandwidth or the occasional connection drops.

The only 2 free services I know of are > Hotspot Shield and CyberGhost VPN... however I think they're for windows only.

### Post by kalfusisagod on 2011-12-28
I use a VPN but not for Linux and not for free. However the service I use does support Linux on their site but not 11.04 Here's the link to set it up [http://www.strongvpn.com/setup_ubuntu_10.10_pptp.shtml](http://www.strongvpn.com/setup_ubuntu_10.10_pptp.shtml) and it's 7 dollars a month. I use this to VPN back to the states from Germany so I can watch Netflix. Very good speeds (as if I wasn't even using a VPN) and no bandwidth caps. Too bad you can't watch netflix on Linux (last time I checked). Hope this helps.



EDIT: In Ubuntu 11.10, if you click the arrows on the top right and click VPN Connections, you can set it up there. Also, I thought VPN was a open standard if you're using PPTP anyway. Well hope this helps!
