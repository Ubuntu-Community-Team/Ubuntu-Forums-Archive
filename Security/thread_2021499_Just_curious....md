### Post by JKyleOKC on 2012-07-13
Let us know the make and model numbers of both your modem and your router, and we will probably be able to give you step by step instructions for verifying their security.

The AT&T password situation **might** be related to a change that they're reported to have made to some accounts recently. They do have a "network" password that's used to gain immediate access to their login program, and it's not the same as the account password that you normally use such as to access webmail. That recent change was supposedly to the network password, which you normally have to enter into your modem or router only once, the first time you connect to the system. The hardware then remembers it and uses it each time you connect afterward.

The reason I said "reportedly" above is that I am with AT&T DSL, and no change has happened to my connection capability since April. Thus it may have happened to only some accounts, or may not have happened at all -- not everything I see reported on the web turns out to be true!

As for why they wanted to know your router make, that was probably one of their offshore Tier 1 support folk reading from a canned script; they have to follow their scripts even when it doesn't make sense.
