Not really. Unless you have done something to change the way Ubuntu works. We may be the administrator but we work most of the time as a standard user. It is only when we try to do a task that requires administrator privileges that our password works as an administrator's password. And then when the task is completed we revert back to being a standard user.

Have you tried changing the setting? Have you created a root user account that is now on automatic login? If so, then is was your choice and it is your responsibility.
