### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-09-08
*Thread moved to **MINT**.*

With my printers there is a button on the printer that gives the printer report. Nothing I've seen on Linux of any flavour that give an onscreen report (but that may be possible depending on how well the printer is supported in Linux by the manufacturer).

Perhaps plug the printer in, give it some paper, check the manual for which button you press on the printer for it to print a report. On mine, you hit the big red button five times for a complete report, three times for wireless settings (from memory).
