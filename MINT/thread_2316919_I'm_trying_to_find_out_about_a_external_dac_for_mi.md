### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-03-12
*Thread moved to **MINT**.*

Anything Mint related goes here. It is not supported in the main forum areas here.

Best to post on their active forums as well.

Draw yourself an I/O map. That will help. Sound would also be output through USB to the DAC and out through headphones or speakers attached to that if it is like most other outboard USB audio devices. Improving the sound from the computer is not a concern as you are not using it. 

The DAC is doing the processing and the signal I/O is being handled by USB, not RCA (if you have the amp inputting to the DAC> computer then computer out RCA> amp you are probably going to experience some rather horrendous audio loops). 

A setup with RCAs to the amp is not really the intention of one of these devices, presuming its specs (and I haven't looked them up but I would advise you to have a good read about what this thing does and the I/Os). You would attach any output to the DAC, not the minijacks on the computer. They don't come into it.

Good luck.

PS: This actually has nothing to do with Mint, or any OS that I can see. You might be better to do some research and try posting on an audio users' site.

### Post by Rob Sayer on 2016-03-12
I use the UCA202 as an external DAC.  I don't see any difference between it and the UCA222 except that it's red and comes with different software.

It works excellently.  Frankly I was really surprised at how good it sounds.  Being ISO standards compliant it's plug and play in Linux.  The UCA202 is popular with electronics geeks for measurement so there are good reviews and measurements out there on the web.

There aren't a lot of downfalls.  One may be that they don't support 24 bit or sampling > 48KHz.  I don't care one bit about that.  24 bit audio is quite justifiable for recording because of the quantization artifacts caused by all the processing people do.  It puts them below the LSB for CD Redbook.  But for playback it's just another bit of 'golden ear' snake oil.

The other thing is that the output impedance of the headphone output is too high for many headphones.  I don't use them much anymore so this isn't an issue for me.

Highly recommended, and at the price it's just ridiculously good value.
