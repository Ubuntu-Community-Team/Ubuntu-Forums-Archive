### Post by TheFu on 2015-08-06
Mint is a desktop distro.  It would be completely inappropriate to use a desktop for complex networking needs outside of trivial testing.

Anything you can imaging can be accomplished with Linux networking. There are thousands of different outcomes, depending on what you need.  Nobody knows them all. Nobody is going to walk you through even 10 of them.  
* [http://www.tldp.org/LDP/nag2/](http://www.tldp.org/LDP/nag2/) should get you started.
* [http://www.tldp.org/LDP/intro-linux/html/sect_10_01.html](http://www.tldp.org/LDP/intro-linux/html/sect_10_01.html) - remedial networking
* [http://www.tldp.org/LDP/GNU-Linux-Tools-Summary/html/c8319.htm](http://www.tldp.org/LDP/GNU-Linux-Tools-Summary/html/c8319.htm) - some old-school commands, some are deprecated.
* manpages - for every command, there is manpage full of useful information. **man ip** will be enlightening. Be certain to check out the "see-also" section.

In a couple of days, you should know more than I about this stuff.
