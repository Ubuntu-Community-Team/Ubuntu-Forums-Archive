### Post by chiefonion on 2017-12-01
The issue is essentially resolved as far as I am concerned. It is actually an Acer Aspire One 257, not 255, [https://www.cnet.com/products/acer-aspire-one-d257-n57dqkk-10-1-atom-n570-windows-7-starter-1-gb-ram-320-gb-hdd-series/specs/](https://www.cnet.com/products/acer-aspire-one-d257-n57dqkk-10-1-atom-n570-windows-7-starter-1-gb-ram-320-gb-hdd-series/specs/)

I found the main issue with the mouse. I had a VGA cord connected to a larger monitor. It correctly detected the monitor as HP, but by default it only showed icons if the actual netbook screen was closed. I had to change that setting and it worked fine.

I am not sure why my initial try to install Linux Mint didn't work, but perhaps my netbook was overheated even though it didn't feel hot. Also, when I tried a second time I didn't connect the VGA cord. This might be something the developers will want to look into.

My experience with this netbook tells me that it would be a waste of time. I am actually considering now a laptop with a larger screen, and in that case there would be no VGA issues. If anyone wants me to try something, let me know now. Otherwise, I will simply use the netbook to store stuff until it goes. I won't be experimenting (reformatting or partitioning) with this netbook again I don't think.
