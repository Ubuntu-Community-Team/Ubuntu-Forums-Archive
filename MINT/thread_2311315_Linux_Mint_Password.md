### Post by Dennis N on 2016-01-26
You need a password for identifying yourself to the OS so you can assume the privileges you have as administrator for administrative tasks. You can bypass the password for log in, but not with multiple users on the system.

### Post by Dennis N on 2016-01-26
You look for a Users and Groups dialog in your settings. It should allow you to change your password, or the option to not require it for log in. I can't be more specific, as the dialog will be a different name and design depending on your desktop environment. I have a Linux Mint MATE, for example.
