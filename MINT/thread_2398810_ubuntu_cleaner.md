### Post by kc1di on 2018-08-18
I use ubuntu cleaner and it works well for me. But like any cleaner you need to know what your deleting.  I recommend the ppa if your going to use it. 
[found here.]("https://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2016/12/free-space-ubuntu-cleaner-janitor-app")
I also use an appimage of stacer which will do a good job of cleaning but will not remove any kernels. [found here.]("https://github.com/oguzhaninan/Stacer/releases")
the appimage does not need to be installed just download and make exicutable and run. 
bleachbit also works well.  Linux does not need as much cleaning as windows but it's helpful to keep the system as clean as possible. 
Good Luck.
