### Post by buzzingrobot on 2013-11-22
The way  Cinnamon handles workspaces isn't to my liking.  Does anyone know fixes or workarounds for these issues? (Cinnamon 2.0.12 on the Mint 16 RC):

1. When I create the number of workspaces I want to use, those workspaces are persistent between reboots.  However, if I select the option to relaunch open applications after a logout/reboot, all the applications are re-opened in the first workspace, not in the workspaces they previously occupied.

2. If a browser is open in one workspace and I click on a link in an application on another workspace, the browser is moved to the current workspace and removed from the workspace I want it to occupy. I want the link to open in the browser but I do not want the browser to be moved.  Nor do I want the display to move to the browser in its assigned workspace. I want the links to open in the browser and be available when I switch to the browser's workspace.

3. The Workspace Switcher panel applet shows only numbers.  If you edit the names of your workspaces, the switcher does not use those names.

4. I can't find a way to have all open windows reflected in the panel on *all* workspaces.  The panel only shows the apps that are open in the current workspace.  I want the panel to always display all windows in all workspaces.  Then, when I click on a minimzed window in the panel, I want the display to move to that window's workspace rather than being brought to the current workspace.

MATE can do most of these things, but Cinnamon is a bit more aesthetically pleasing, to my eyes.
