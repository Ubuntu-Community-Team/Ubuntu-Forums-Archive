### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-01-08
There are several posts in this section from people asking the same question but the answer is the same. Is your device listed here?

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

Keep in mind that Ubuntu Touch, although at version 1.0, is still under heavy development and by installing it you become an experimental tester. The install instructions are here:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Install](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Install)

> [COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu Beta]The Ubuntu Touch Developer Preview is intended to be used for development and evaluation purposes only. [/FONT][/COLOR]**It is an experimental development snapshot that can potentially brick your device**[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu Beta]. It does not provide all of the features and services of a retail phone and cannot replace your current handset. This preview is the first release of a very new and unfinished version of Ubuntu and it will evolve quickly.[/FONT][/COLOR]

Ubuntu Touch has involved quickly. It is a little over a year ago that it was announced and here we are expecting some announcement of a Ubuntu phone being brought to market. I do not think that the progress that has been made should be taken as meaning we can install Ubuntu Touch on all the various mobile devices on the market.

Regards.
