### Post by Pitidd on 2013-05-24
Hey Ubuntu Community!
I was just struck by a thought regarding Ubuntu venturing out into the mobile world. While it is of course an EPICLY AWESOME idea to have a phone that turns into a tablet that turns into a PC that turns into a media centre, I see some possible problems that might rise because of other recent developments. I'm talking about more and more new games with higher and higher software requirement being ported over to Ubuntu via Steam and/or WINE. I don't think that current phone processors and graphics chips are capable to run those games on a screen that has the size and resolution of a regular PC screen. So my idea is that perhaps one could still let the PC have an own processor and pool the processing power of that with the phone's whenever connected to facilitate use of "high-tier" software with the new all-in-one Ubuntu.
Is this idea absolutely crazy or am I not the first one to come up with this? And more importantly: is this a good idea?

Sincerely,
Pitidd

### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-05-24
> [COLOR=#000000]I don't think that current phone processors and graphics chips are capable to run those games on a screen that has the size and resolution of a regular PC screen. [/COLOR]

Oh, no? Who is going to develop this idea of yours? The Ubuntu developers are most certainly not going to spend any time or effort on it. They have already been set their goal. So, in that context it is not a good idea. I guess that it would take even more development work than what is needed to produce Ubuntu Touch convergence. And in the mean time the hardware specifications are not standing still.

It would defeat the purpose of putting Ubuntu on a smart phone that can be plugged into a hub that is connected to a monitor and that can then be used as a PC. It does not remove the need to buy a desktop base unity.

The target purchaser is not a gamer but business owners who at present purchase a PC, tablet and phone for their employees. The aim is to reduce purchasing costs to tempt business owners to buy. The phone + hub + monitor combination will run a full Ubuntu 14.04 desktop.
