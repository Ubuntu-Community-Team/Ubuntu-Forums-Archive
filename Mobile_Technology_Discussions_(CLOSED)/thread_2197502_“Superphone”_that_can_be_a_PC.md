### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-01-04
There are phones already on the market with the right hardware specifications but there are no phones as yet on the market running the Ubuntu Touch phone/tablet platform. We recently heard news that Canonical had recently signed an agreement with an OEM for a Ubuntu phone to be marketed but when and what hardware are yet to be announced. We have not been informed which OEM it is.

The whole point of the Ubuntu super phone concept is that right now phone hardware is as powerful as standard desktop computers are. Or, in comparison with my desktop machine, more powerful. To bring this concept into the real world two things are needed. 1) a form Ubuntu that provides the required capabilities for both a phone, tablet and desktop. 2) Hardware makers bringing a product to market.

Ubuntu Touch for the phone is already where it needs to be. It is at version 1.0. Over the next six months it will be optimised for the tablet and that will bring Ubuntu Touch to version 1.5. Community developers are designing and building applications that will scale from phone to tablet and to the desktop and we can test some of these on the desktop. Some such as Web Browser are standard in Trusty Tahr (use the mouse to do swipe gestures).

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

[http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/12/13/partners-community-and-success/](http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/12/13/partners-community-and-success/)

Regards.
