### Post by dotte on 2015-01-14
Not sure if this is the right place for making suggestions, but here goes. 

I've been using Ubuntu Touch on my Nexus 4 for a few months and have so far been very impressed. I didn't like the music app to begin with, but the recent(-ish) update has improved things greatly, which was perfect timing because my aging philips media player has decided to kark it on me. 

One thing I miss from using a dedicated media player is the absence of real buttons for pause, skip etc. Rather than pulling out my fancy touch screen phone and look for the virtual button on the screen, I'd much rather just fumble in my pocket and push the right button. 

It got me thinking that I rarely (if ever) change the volume once I start, and I'd much rather use the volume up button to skip to the next song and volume down button to pause. If I need to change the volume, I can always pull it out and swipe the slider, but this is less frequent than skipping a song when the random shuffle produces an undesired result. I don't think this is unique to the Nexus 4 - most phone and tablet devices have dedicated volume control buttons. 


Would it be possible to implement this as an option in the standard app? If not, how hard would it be to produce a modified version that does this?

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-01-15
> [COLOR=#000000]is the absence of real buttons for pause, skip etc. [/COLOR]

But what if the hardware does not provide a physical button or two or three? I am sure that if OEMs want to bring out devices with physical buttons, even physical keyboards. then Ubuntu for Devices pre-installed on those products will be fully compatible with the hardware.

Run Music app on the desktop and it has the usual click-able buttons that we would expect from a desktop music player. At the moment Music app plays audio files in the Music folder but it cannot import audio files from other partitions. This shows its origins as a mobile device music player. I expect modifications to be made in time.

You want to use physical volume control buttons to play and pause? Now, you really are adding complications.

See, the developments being done to Ubuntu for Devices in preparation for bringing Unity 8 to desktop Ubuntu.
