### Post by Jon_Thomas on 2016-11-23
I will say this much.  I own the other device made by the company I was referring to.  

It is a 7" Android tablet with a built-in HDTV tuner.

This Android tablet works pretty well, even with having Android 4.2 on it.  It travels with me on my Harley and I have never had an issue with it this last year.

From what I know about the tablet and the company's goals, the near term plan is to support all the major Linux distros in the future with this tablet series, as users ask for them.

One thing to keep in mind with that crowd-funded tablet.  

It presently will come pre-loaded with the current openSUSE Leap release, but the near-term future plans are to support ALL the major Linux distros, including Ubuntu and it's derivatives, Red Hat, Kali, Elementary, Linux Mint, Debian, and others as the user base grows and people show an interest in installing a specific distro.

It will not just be a single-distro focused tablet/2-in-1 device.  With time and community support, it will be open to install whatever distro/OS you want. You would only be limited by the available storage space.
