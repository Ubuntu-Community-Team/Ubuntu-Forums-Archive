### Post by ofnuts on 2013-09-04
My son (an Android addict who passes for fairly knowledgeable on Android forums) has a Nexus 4. The good point of the Nexus (if you get the one from Google) is that its an unadulterated Android. Everything the manufacturers add is pretty much worthless and what the networks add is a steaming pile of Software Hazardous to your Intelligence and Taste.

 I use a Sony Xperia Kyno with a 16GB SD card it, and it's far from full... 8GB of music is already an awful lot of listening time, and the photos in such phones aren't that big so I don' t see myself fill the rest. The SD card is mostly a way to quickly transfer the music to another phone if that one breaks (and if it breaks I can still recover the SD contents)(*). Otherwise backups becomes even more important (but how many people backup their phones?).  But the useless Facebook app that burns CPU (and battery) is getting on y nerves and I'll soon either root the thing or get me a Nexus.

(*) An Apple minion once told me how elated he was when the AppleStore exchanged his failing iPhone (yes, these things break, too) and offered to transfer everything to the new one (via "the cloud") if he could just be patient for about one hour. Not something that us lowlifes with Android phones could experience... He just overlooked that with an SD card it would even been a matter of seconds even if the old phone had become braindead.
