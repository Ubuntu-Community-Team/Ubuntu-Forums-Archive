### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-12-04
> [COLOR=#000000]Ubuntu is offered as the best solution for a tablet or phone,[/COLOR]

That is not yet true. I do not think that it will ever be true. I do not know why you think that. Ubuntu is being developed so that Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) can pre-install Ubuntu for Devices on phones and tablets for retail. We await every day for news of products going on sale.

There are at least 2 OEMs lined up to produce Ubuntu phones. I have not heard any news about the Ubuntu tablets except that the code is under development along with the code for phones. I also understand that when these devices are released on to the market we will be able to buy them through a Ubuntu web site as well as from the 2 OEMs

It is not as easy to install Ubuntu on a tablet as it is to install it on a desktop. Why? Different computer hardware architecture. Desktops and laptops use CPUs that are compatible with an Intel designed architecture called x86. Tablets have CPUs that are designed by ARM and it is a completely different CPU architecture. To complicate things ARM does not manufacture CPUs. It only designs them and then licenses the designs to whoever wants to make the CPUs. So, the vast numbers of mobile devices mean a great variety of mobile device hardware that makes it next to impossible to port Ubuntu to, even if the hardware specification was good enough to run Ubuntu.

So, you are starting off from at at least two wrong assumptions. The second wrong understanding is that there are those on this forum who are experts at porting Ubuntu to ARM devices. I have yet to see such people posting on this forum.

To put it simply to try to install Ubuntu onto that device would make you an experimenter. And then there is the need for drivers for the graphics, the WiFi, the camera, etcetera, etcetera.

Regards.

### Post by carlwsnyder on 2014-12-04
Chiming in.  There are guides, on YouTube and elsewhere to installing Linux on Android.  Watch some of the videos, and you will find that if you are comfortable using Arch, Gentoo, or Slackware, you may find these informative and challenging.  If you don't even know the names of the distros I mentioned, you will probably find more of a challenge than you can handle, _and_ you will need another desktop computer to aid you in setting up the required installation.  Nowhere have I found any guide which will take you through a process with the resources of a tablet alone.

Yes, you can find guides to installing Linux on ARM processors, but the majority of those are for Chromebooks using ChrUbuntu.
