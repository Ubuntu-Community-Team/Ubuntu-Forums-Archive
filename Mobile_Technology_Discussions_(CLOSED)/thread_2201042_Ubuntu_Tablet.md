### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-01-22
That web page that you linked to has, it seems to me, two aims: 1) To invite developers to write apps for Ubuntu Touch. 2) Invite OEMs to release tablets for retail with Ubuntu Touch pre-installed.

Any discussions between Canonical (supporter of Ubuntu) and OEMs regarding the manufacture of Ubuntu phones/tablets will be covered by confidentiality agreements. Those Canonical employees who know about these arrangements will not be allowed to speak publicly about them. Those of us who provide assistance on this forum are as much in the dark about this as you. We are also waiting for official announcements.

The development of Ubuntu Touch has reached version 1.0 and is suitable for pre-installation on phone hardware. For the last few months the developers have been working on Ubuntu Touch for the tablet form factor. That will be version 1.5 and the target is the end of April 2014. The next 12 - 18 months is when we may or may not see announcements regarding the availability of ubuntu phones and tablets.

Ubuntu users expect to be able to install Ubuntu on most, if not all, desktop hardware because the Linux developers work hard to keep the Linux kernel compatible with desktop hardware. The same cannot be said about the variety of mobile hardware. Canonical is not planning to make Ubuntu compatible with the various types of mobile hardware that are at present on the market.

Community developers may take it upon themselves to port Ubuntu to some of these existing devices. And some have started doing this.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

[http://forum.xda-developers.com/ubuntu-touch](http://forum.xda-developers.com/ubuntu-touch)

Regards.
