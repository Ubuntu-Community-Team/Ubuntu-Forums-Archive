### Post by Cheesemill on 2013-05-07
It's not on the list of devices that currently run Ubuntu Touch...
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices#Working.2C_but_not_available_from_cdimage.u.c](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices#Working.2C_but_not_available_from_cdimage.u.c)

Also the specs are lower than the official system requirements...
[http://www.ubuntu.com/tablet/operators-and-oems](http://www.ubuntu.com/tablet/operators-and-oems)

Also Cyanogenmod hasn't been ported to the Auraslate, which makes it unlikely that Ubuntu Touch will be either.


Currently the only officially supported devices are the Galaxy Nexus, Nexus 4, Nexus 7 and Nexus 10 so I would recommend getting one of those if you want to run Ubuntu Touch in its current state of development.
