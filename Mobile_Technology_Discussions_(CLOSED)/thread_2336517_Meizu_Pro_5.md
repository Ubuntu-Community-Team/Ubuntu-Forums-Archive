### Post by kj.curry on 2016-09-09
Greetings,

I just don't know what to do. With over 40+ years experience of System Admin & Tech experience (now retired) under my belt. I sorry to say but the (Mezu MX Pro 5 and Ubuntu Touch combo) is a whopper of a flop. I've waited for several years before taking the jumps from a well built and very functional Palm 680 Treo smartphone to this. I also thought the growing pains of a new product idea had matured during its evolution over the past few years assuming the MX Pro 5 was the first "True Ubuntu Phone". I thought this transition was going to be leap forward. Instead I'm running in to difficulties and frustration at almost every turn. My leap has turn out to be a stubble back.

No I'm not just a complainer. High School Computer club 1970 - 73. Programing in FORTRAN Watt-IV, OS IBM-360 & JCL, COBOL-74, Many flavours of BASIC, PASCAL. Many DEC hardware from PDP-11-20's to 11-75. OS RT-11, RSX-11M, + others + machine code on various platforms. Built my first Altair 8800 for just under $500US in 1976. Started using UNIX like (OS-9) in 1983 on a CoCo2 Colour computer. Dec-Sys10 & OS TOPS 10. Apollo & SGI hardware during the 80's. Finally downgraded to IBM and Windows in later 80's til today, but always overlapping with UNIX systems with IRIX, IRIS, HP-UX, Red Hat, Fedora and finally Ubuntu since 2008. Never really got into the Apple / Mac stuff. Begrudgingly used several Windows products & OS's (a requirement of employment) Desktop, workstation and server environments.

The phone - the Meizu MX Pro 5 on one level has great potential. While it does have features that exceed the functionality of my Palm Treo 680 AND While Ubuntu itself has grown into a reasonably mature OS. I've been using it since Hardy Heron. BUT. the current offering of apps are immature and ill functioning and need a lot of work just to bring the up to par where the Palm OS and apps left off before HP killed it.

Text editing via the GUI. Usable apps don't exist. A light weight Gedit, might be nice. Better yet GVIM. (Which would easily get around the lack of cursor movement key, defect of the GUI keyboard - just to name one.) Yes I do use VIM in terminal but task switching is not nice ops on the phone. Not knowing the layout protocols is also problematic.

The X-Desktop app suite are unusable on the MX Pro 5 screen.

On-screen selection is wildly unstable and very difficult to manipulate - probably a OS issue.

The Calendar app - marginally / minimally functional. "Repeat" function is a nightmare and (who thought that up anyway).
If there is a Calendar type definition capably (which only appears to be the case) is not there is reality. How to sync them on touch. Yes it's based on an Evolution engine, but I've yet to have a successful xfer of Vcard data from Palm, or Pegasus to the Calendar app under touch. A non tech person simple could not do it.

Contacts - broken. In OTA-11 I had an "address field". In OTA-12 its' gone. However No capability within the app to add additional field types. Why is it so limited? (It's reminds of the mindset of IBM eng people). There is also no capability to distinguish Contact types. i.e. personal, vs business, friends, Club A, Club B etc....

Email app -Dekko is extremely minimal. Again the OTA-12 upgraded but crapped out the program. It will launch - but disappears. Thanks, I now don't have email. 

Terminal - seems to be the only reasonable app. At least one can slow poke through Ubuntu - but I still waiting to receiving a Bluetooth KB that works with Ubuntu (apparent several aftermarkets KB make a distinction to Windows, iOS, and Android and won't link to Ubuntu / Linux) - (who knew).

The Calculator apps lacks several functions item like a programmer mode, higher trig functions, Scientific and Eng modes, units conversion. This is also a feature lacking in Ubuntu Desktop too, so its not surprised. (yes there are some limited 3rd party apps, but less so in Ubuntu Touch.

The Link to Google - is very disturbing because of Google violation of Privacy Rights in their quest to be either the BIG, in Big Data and/or Big Brother by capturing all our data. OOur smartphones should should function independently of any link to American Corporate Interests  / American Government chance of access. If you want to exercise the folly of releasing all your personal junk that should be a last difficult to access option not a first and default option.

The Ubuntu Store does weird things. Example 1: I had a Units Conversion app "Converter" install under OTA-11. When I upgraded to OTA-12 the app disappeared. Dekko stopped working under OTA-12. This demonstrates upgrade instability.

The lack of a convergence Desktop app that allows a local syncing of smartphone data to say - Evolution. (See Google comment above).

Also there are so many Ubuntu web sites, but virtually no road map as to what site does what.
Where do I report bugs, errors and issues and discuss stuff with regard the Ubuntu Touch OS itself.
Where do I report bugs, errors and issues and discuss stuff with regard to Ubuntu core apps.
Where do I report bugs, errors and issues and discuss stuff with regard to apps from the Ubuntu store.
The operation of Launchpad is "by best guess" and very confusing. In short I'm all over the place and still confused.
Yea I'm sort of figuring stuff out BUT... it's taking weeks and months. If Canonical and Ubuntu are trying to take Ubuntu, it's convergence with multi-hardware it/they need to streamline the minefield.

...and this is the tip of the iceberg.

My effort here is not solely to voice my disappointment but to spark and  improve the Ubuntu Convergence vision. If any of this is going to go  mainstream then the Ubuntu Team needs to move more efficiently. To date, 'user friendly' is not an adjective I'd choose. Product usability is not yet a feature an "ordinary user" is going to discover. 

My intent is not to bitch, but get involved. I've had my new phone since June but I consider it minimally functional. If I were a business person and not retired I throw this phone in the garbage. 

Yes I've already registered under various Ubuntu flavored web sites, but I still need a road map as to what can be done where so I can best figure out my best way of contributing.

I thought that after 40 years of working with, debugging, fixing and spending long nights troubleshoot OS's & Software & Hardware, documentation etc.. etc. would be over. Looks like I can't retire after all.

Thanks for listening.
KJC

PS. It may be a flop, but a flop with promise and potential.
