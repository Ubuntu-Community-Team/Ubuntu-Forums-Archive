Hello Everyone,

I haven't been on the forums for almost 6 months! It's been a while. I wanted to inquire with fellow users and see how you guys feel about Androids fragmentation?

How about the moving away from Java and to OpenJDK with Android N?

What device do you use? If it's not an Android (iOS or WP), why?

Here's my take on it all:

The android fragmentation is horrible. I feel I have to buy a new phone every 3-6 months just to make sure I get ample updates for new features. I know that I could get a phone that supports Cyanogen or even one to install Ubuntu, but the funds just aren't there. The problem I have with the fragmentation is it seems like some manufacturers, BLU namely, seems to hinge on instead of upgrading their android version on newer devices, they just release a new phone. While I have developer skills, they aren't far along enough to where I could learn to implement what I wanted into the ROM. iOS drops support for their devices after what... 2 years maybe, so IOS doesn't seem that good. Ubuntu phone is still an infant and WP is having trouble maintaining market. I dunno it frustrates me.

Regarding their shift to OpenJDK, I really enjoy the idea. I hope it doesn't cause issues like Kitkat to Lollipop caused. I had a lot of apps that failed to open on Lollipop when my Nexus 7 updated. OpenJDK is a change, but hopefully for the best! =)

I currently use a BLU Studio C HD. I'm thinking about buying the BLU WIN HD LTE. (I know I complained about BLU up above) I'm going to work on an app tonight to try out the schema of things.

Finally, micro-transactions - this is causing me a loathing, a hate, an absolute disgust for Android overall. I dislike IOS in general, but Android app developers have become greedy. It's like game DLC, you just bought a game for oh lets go high and say $59.99 USD. But guess what, get these maps for $4.99 that we decided you can put into the game. Oh want this gun or outfit? $1.99 each. DLC aggravates me as well. I can understand expansion packs, but DLC, come on. Subscription services? Fine, MMOs that do maintenance, patches, upgrades, etc., sure I don't mind subscription fees, but DLC? Oh its just so frustrating.

How do you guys feel about Android and its many areas that I feel are negative?
