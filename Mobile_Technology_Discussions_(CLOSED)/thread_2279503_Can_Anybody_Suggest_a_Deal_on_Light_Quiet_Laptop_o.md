### Post by TheFu on 2015-05-25
Tablets are interesting for consumption only. Writing an email on 1 makes me want to shoot myself in the head. Plus none of the mobile OSes has anything to do with real security, so putting important gpg keys on those devices just isn't a realistic option. I love reading and listening to books on tape using a tablet, however. Plus it makes a nice DLNA client and controller. If you have a calibre server on the network, accessing ebooks and daily news is easy.

Been using a chromebook the last 18 months. Keyboard sucks, but everything else is fine. Wiped ChromeOS the first 5 min I had the machine and loaded Ubuntu Server+openbox. It is silent. The Haswell and later CPUs are FAST and sip battery. This c720 has a celeron with VT-x support. I see 7+ hrs of real battery use. It also has an m.2-ssd - tiny, fast, silent.  

Wish I would have gotten the Dell 13" - with the same CPU, more RAM -- just to get a real keyboard and more external ports. Had to buy about $150 in upgrades for the chromebook to make it useful, which makes it more expensive than the dell version was back then.  The first c720 I got had bad pixels, so definitely look for that problem.  

I upgraded from an Asus Eee to this - going from 600px to 768px made a big difference. Nothing like my 15" laptop with 1080p, but that beast never got more than 2 hrs of battery and it is noisy if the CPU gets busy.

Anyway, lots of options out there.  Might be worth watching slickdeals and bensbargains for a month to get the June deals.

### Post by SedaliaSteve on 2015-07-03
It did install quickly from a USB. After awhile it started dropping the Wifi and wouldn't reconnect. If I rebooted it got it back. I searched in the forum and tried this command:
echo "options rtl8723be fwlps=N ips=N" | sudo tee /etc/modprobe.d/rtl8723be.conf

Rebooted and it has been solid since. The one piece mouse is driving me mildly crazy. I'm used to mouse buttons. Still, I am very pleased. This would be a real woofer with windows but right now I have Chrome with 10 tabs and gimp with a bunch of pictures and it is full of pep. Using it for two hours has left me with 90% battery.
