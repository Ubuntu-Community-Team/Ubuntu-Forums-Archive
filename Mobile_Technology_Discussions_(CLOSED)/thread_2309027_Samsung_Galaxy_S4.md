### Post by duncedrive on 2016-02-24
I know I'm late to this party, but I definitely think it's worth putting some words in.

As an Android user who got converted to BlackBerry, I miss some things about Android. For one, I miss that freedom of keyboards, launchers, and deeper customization through Busybox and the like. Android's freedom to make those changes was something that I really enjoyed, as it helped to make the phone feel that much more like my own. My first Android phone was an [LG Vortex]("http://joesteiger.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/133586-lgvortex.jpg"), and I certainly did not hesitate to dump a de-ODEXed pre-rooted ROM of Android 2.2 onto it when the time came. Once I got myself a [Galaxy SIII]("http://www.laptopmag.com/images/wp/purch-api/incontent/2012/06/Samsung-Galaxy-S-III.jpg"), I was in real business. Android 4.2.2 along with some deep support from XDA Developers meant I had a lot to work with. I spent no time throwing CyanogenMod onto it. And yes, I did upgrade to CM11 when Kit Kat got released.

I'm an experimental person, though... So I've actually jumped around a lot when it comes to phones. In the past 3 years, I've gone around in US Cellular, Verizon, AT&T and Sprint territory with varied results. I had a [Kyocera Echo]("https://cdn3.vox-cdn.com/thumbor/HCM97tiSmDKlObxcUBQPFXMSUsQ=/0x49:1000x612/1600x900/cdn0.vox-cdn.com/assets/764832/kyocera-echo-review-DSC_0600-rm-verge-1000.jpg"), which shows me that there ARE good design potentials, but sometimes, you need more time to flesh it out. Then I got myself a [Sharp Aquos Crystal]("http://i-cdn.phonearena.com/images/articles/134152-image/Sharp-AQUOS-Crystal-hands-on.jpg"), and bar the terrible nature of the bootloader forbidding the option to root it, I stuck with it as my daily driver for about a year, which was impressive to me.

Dabbling on the Blackberry side, I started out with a BlackBerry Z10 in summer of '15, and I thought it was great, except for... it was a GSM phone. Sprint and Verizon are the only ones that get service at my house, so I was restricted to using my Z10 as a proper phone about 40% of the time. I had to go back to the Sharp.

As of January, I picked up the Q10, and I absolutely love it. It's a little offputting sometimes. Apps don't always play nice. I actually had to get an older APK of Skype because the existing one was bogging it down to the point that the Android framework would crash, cutting me off until I did a power cycle. Once I did that, however, it has been smooth sailing. I don't find it too good for gaming, due to the square screen, and the keyboard is good for typing, but not emulating games, per se, but I came to accept that, and for good reason.

It's a phone. I bought the Q10 for a few reasons... The Z10 showed me that BB10 was a fantastic OS, and it's the only one I would want. The Q10 was my only choice on the Sprint side. Finally, I just... wanted a physical keyboard. And I like it. I'm glad that I have it.

Companies are selling these high-end smartphones pushing 4K displays, octa-core processors, 4GB of RAM with 64GB of storage... and then you get things like "3 hours of screen-on time" in the reviews... I kind of hate it. I like phones to be powerful enough to sell that idea of a mobile workstation. It's exactly why Ubuntu's convergence is such a hot thing that Microsoft copied it for Windows 10 Mobile.

However, there's a thing called "time and place." When I'm out and about, at college, or at work, I need a phone that'll last me through the day, that I can pull out, pound several messages out, send them off, and put it back. I need a phone that I can do that several times through a 8-10 hour day, without having to resort to using a charger in the middle. I need a phone that can handle a break to listen to some music over a Bluetooth connected headset.

When I'm at home, I can worry about playing games, and watching movies, and the like. My phone doesn't need to run Half-Life 2, it needs to run Skype and an IRC client, and be able to take e-mail from 3 accounts, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, BBM, and any other services I use for communication. It doesn't need a 4K display, it just needs a big enough and bright enough display for me to read text and maybe look at the odd photo.

***ahem***[B][SIZE=2]
[/SIZE][/B][SIZE=2]
As for your situation regarding the Galaxy S4, I would definitely suggest you take a look at XDA and see what you find. General consensus is that Samsung's TouchWiz UI is, has, and always will be, terrible. You have a fairly high-spec phone there, considering what it is, so I would look into dropping a custom ROM on it. It's too bad there isn't a port of Ubuntu Touch for it yet :P

If you dislike it enough, though, you should definitely try your hand at a modern BB device. As a Q10 user, I'm very happy with my purchase, and I don't have enough reason to jump ship... Not until John Chen says it's dead, anyway.[/SIZE]
