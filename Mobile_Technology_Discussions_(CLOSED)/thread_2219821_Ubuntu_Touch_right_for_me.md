### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-04-25
It is best that you base you decision on official information

[http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/](http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/)

> [COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu]After installing Ubuntu, upgrades to newly released images and apps are automatically provided through [/FONT][/COLOR]**Settings**[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu] >[/FONT][/COLOR]**Updates**[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu].[/FONT][/COLOR]

[http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/devices/](http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/devices/)

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/CoreApps](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/CoreApps)

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/TrustyTahr/ReleaseNotes#Ubuntu_Touch](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/TrustyTahr/ReleaseNotes#Ubuntu_Touch)

From what I understand it would be wrong to think that Ubuntu on a phone or tablet will be updated in the same way as Ubuntu on the desktop is updated. In future Ubuntu on the desktop may be updated differently. More like the way it is done on the phone/tablet. 

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ImageBasedUpgrades](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ImageBasedUpgrades)

> [COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu Beta] we're looking at doing image based updates, basically generating new images on the server side with the usual tools (debootstrap, apt, dpkg), then generate deltas between those images and have the client pull the delta and apply it without ever actually dealing with packages.[/FONT][/COLOR]

Think of all the data that an update on the desktop pulls in. Not nice if you are paying for downloads by the byte. With image updates the term "delta" means the difference between what on is the device and what is on the server and only downloading what is different. Less data to download. As I understand it with my little understanding.

> [COLOR=#333333][FONT=Ubuntu Beta]The current target for image based updates are Ubuntu Touch devices (phone and tablets) however the same design can easily be applied to any other device with only minimal changes to the upgrader component.[/FONT][/COLOR]

So, fasten your seat belts.

Regards.
