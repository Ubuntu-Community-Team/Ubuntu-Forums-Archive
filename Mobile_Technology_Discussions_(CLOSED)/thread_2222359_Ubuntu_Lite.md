### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-05-06
It is not going to happen. Canonical does not have the resources (people's paid for time) to do what you want. Ubuntu is not an operating system. It is made up of many component parts which are in fact Free and Open Source Software of other developers. Yes, Ubuntu for phones and tablets makes use of other developer's open source code. A Ubuntu lite would indeed be a crippled OS. What would you leave out?

The issue is not the software but the incompatibility of the Linux kernel with the hardware. Is Canonical going to develop it own version of the Linux kernel? I do not think so.

We also have to consider why Canonical is developing Ubuntu for phones and tablets. It is to make money. The aim is to get equipment manufacturers to pre-install Ubuntu on their devices and for Canonical to receive some small payment for the use of Ubuntu by a profit making corporation. From the beginning the target audience has been the manufacturers.

There is no profit for Canonical in what you want them to do. You want a free download. Even if a device meets the hardware specifications do not expect to be able to download Ubuntu and install on the device in the same way in which we download Ubuntu for the desktop. That is also unlikely to officially happen. Some community developers are working on porting Ubuntu to other devices. How successful they are is open to discussion.

[https://insights.ubuntu.com/2013/02/15/get-the-faqs-about-ubuntu-on-smartphones/](https://insights.ubuntu.com/2013/02/15/get-the-faqs-about-ubuntu-on-smartphones/)
