Are you converting from source files (CD, WAV, raw audio etc) to MP3/OGG? Or are you converting from MP3 to OGG?

There is no advantage converting from MP3 to OGG because MP3 is a lossy compression format, so you will be translating one lossy format into another. The final OGG file will have less information than the original MP3. On the other hand if you are converting from a "raw" audio file then OGG is very good but poorly supported by third party hardware.

Some people fell into the trap of ripping all their CDs then reselling them, not realising the lossiness of compression algorithms.
