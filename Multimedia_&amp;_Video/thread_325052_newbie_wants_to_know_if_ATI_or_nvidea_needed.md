### Post by cilynx on 2006-12-24
Your graphics are by SiS.

Some names in the graphics field: ATI, nVidia, SiS, Intel, 3dfx

There are many many older companies.  (anyone else remember the Diamond Stealth?)  By no means do you need a new / high end brand name graphics card for daily tasks.  If you want desktop candy spinny swoopy wobbly wonderfulness on the other hand, I'd advise droping a paycheck on a nice nVidia.

Currently ATI and nVidia are the only big players.  Intel sometimes does graphics on their own motherboards, but they're generally not as "high end" (some would disagree with that statement).  

ATI provides Linux drivers as of recent, but they are not well supported or amazingly functional.
nVidia has been providing drivers for Linux for some time.  They are much better supported.

### Post by goatflyer on 2006-12-24
That means your graphics chipset is integrated on the motherboard (you didn't buy a separate graphics card like some people do.)  NVidea and ATI are probably the two largest companies that make graphics cards.  Your system doesn't need their drivers.
