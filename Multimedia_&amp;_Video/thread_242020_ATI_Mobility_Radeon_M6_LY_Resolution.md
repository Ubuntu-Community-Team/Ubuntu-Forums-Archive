### Post by Eddie Hung on 2006-08-23
Unfortunately, Xorg does not always detect the right resolutions of your setup. And annoyingly, in order to manually set them, you need to edit the config file.

Try:
```
sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```
from a terminal application.

This will open up the gnome text editor. Scroll to over half way where it mentions "Monitor" and there will be a list of resolutions you can fiddle with.

Once you finish, restart X with Ctrl+Alt+Backspace or reboot.

(Be warned that you can crash the Xserver by editing this file... do it at your own risk and that bla bla bla!)

I'm not by my computer at the moment, so sorry I can't be more specific - maybe an expert can come along and correct me!

Hope it helps,

Eddie
