### Post by madscientist on 2008-06-05
oconnor663 nailed it for me!  Ubuntu 8.04.

I'd been tearing my hair out trying to get dual screens working properly (by "properly" I mean allowing the nvidia card to give correct xinerama info to the window manager so that the WM understood there were two screens and not one big one).  And, I couldn't get nvidia-settings working: it just said I wasn't using an NVIDIA card, when I clearly was.  I've been trying everything I can think of and I know a LOT more about nvidia xorg.conf settings than I really care to.

Then I discovered glxinfo gave "direct rendering: no", then almost immediately I found oconnor663's post: I removed xserver-xgl, logged out and back in, and that's it!  Now *everything* works: direct render is yes; nvidia-settings works properly; and I have proper xinerama support for my dual screens.

Happy happy!

### Post by madscientist on 2008-06-23
OK.  It looks like your /etc/group file is messed up.  You should have a line in there for a group "video" with a group ID of "44".  But, you apparently don't because the group on your /dev/nvidia* files is listed as just "44" instead of "video".

Have you configured your system with NIS or LDAP or some other networked login facility?  Have you messed around with /etc/nsswitch.conf?  Please run these commands and show me the output: ```
grep video /etc/group
grep group /etc/nsswitch.conf
```

If the first item doesn't show anything, you need to edit your /etc/group file, using something like "sudo gedit /etc/group" and add a line like this:```
video:x:44:username
```Replace "username" with your username, and the usernames of any other user accounts on your system (separated by commas, no spaces!) that you want to be able to log in graphically.
