### Post by wieman01 on 2006-11-26
This is not meant to be any kind of product placement of marketing, but I have to share this with someone. After a few hours of research on the web, I came across a wireless streaming device called **"Squeezebox"** from a manufacturer which has recently been bought by Logitech. 

Most of the music streaming devices support Windows, but (naturally) fall short of support for Linux. Squeezebox, however, comes with an **"open-source"(!)** streaming server that supports all major Linux distributions including Ubuntu/Debian. You simply add a lines to the source-list, that's it. You install the software which takes you about 5 minutes... The software is all open-source and constantly enhanced by a dedicated community.

What impressed me was the fact that it took me 30 minutes to set it up. Even wireless security **(WPA2, AES, PSK)** is supported, **hidden ESSID** is no problem either. The only stumbling block was the volume which was set to 0 by default. Took me 5 minutes to figure that out!

So on the whole, this was the easiest installation you could possibly think of. I can recommend the device to anybody who is looking for wireless streaming... The **sound quality** is excellent as well. The setup is a piece of cake. In my opinion Squeezebox is the best example of how beautiful open-source can be in conjunction with a commercial product. Nice, simple, open-source, and cutting-edge. Sadly the hardware itself is anything but free... That's the only drawback.

Hope this helps anybody out there looking for similar solutions. Sqeezebox may not be the cheapest thing on earth but all means - **it JUST works**.
