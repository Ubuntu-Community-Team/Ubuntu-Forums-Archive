### Post by daverave999 on 2007-02-05
Just thought I'd relate my story...

Last week I wanted to watch a particular program on TV and couldn't because I was out. I had heard of MythTV and thought I'd take a look into it. It seemed to do an awful lot of stuff that I probably wouldn't need but might be useful in the future. I'd owned an analogue card in the past (used on windows) and I was not impressed by the software at all. MythTV did look a little better so I decided to splash out on the Hauppauge (how DO you pronounce that...?) Nova-T PCI card.

Following [this]("http://parker1.co.uk/mythtv_ubuntu.php") guide, EVERYTHING went just as it said it would. All very quickly too. I have to say I am so impressed I have just been scouring ebay for bits to build a dedicated MythTV box.

For those who don't know what MythTV can do, I'll relate what I have mine set up to do already:

Watch freeview (of course...)
Record freeview
Set programs to be recorded by logging into my pc from work (MythWeb)
Burn those TV programs to DVD so I can watch them on the big telly downstairs (MythArchive)
Read the news from my bed :)

There's loads of other stuff that it can do too, that I just haven't got around to trying yet. Oh yeah, and the remote works absolutely as you would expect it to. I'm far from a 'linux guru' but it was incredibly easy to set up. Only snag was the tuning, and that was 30 seconds on google to find the local transmitter frequency.

An absolutely amazing piece of software. I just wanted to tell those who didn't know about it how good it is.

Thanks for listening. :D
