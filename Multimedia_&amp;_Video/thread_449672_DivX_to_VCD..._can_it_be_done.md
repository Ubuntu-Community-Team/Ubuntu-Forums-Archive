### Post by caveman56 on 2007-05-21
Yeah it can be done, here's a great guide for doing it with windows, not sure what you'd use under linux, but this is a start for you.  [http://www.videohelp.com/divxtovcd.htm](http://www.videohelp.com/divxtovcd.htm)  I used to use this myself before I had a DVD burner, but you have to be careful with the VCD, some players have problems playing certain color media, on DVD's the writing surface is pretty much a uniform shade of purple, but cd's you can find in lots of different colors, I found one that worked on my player, but just a shade off wouldn't work.  But consider purchasing a DVD burner.  You can get a nice one for 50 bucks at Wally World :-) and you can fit a full 9 gig dvd on a 4.3 blank with compression, looks great, and I know I always had to split my movies in half on VCD's.

hope this helps.,

Dave
