### Post by pseudonym on 2007-01-01
> **wilko10 said:**
> a) Would a soundblaster style £30 PCI card sound much better than the built in one.
Most definitely. Plus it's one of the  best supported cards for Linux, apart from the most recent models like the X-Fi.

> **wilko10 said:**
> b) What soundcard would represent the best sound, bearing in mind we can only use the line out jack.
When you say you're limited to the line-out jack, is that because it's the only free input on your rceiver? If not, then you would use the outputs that come with the new card.

If you have the right speakers/receiver, digital audio is always better than line-out. In this case even a live! 5.1 is still a good card. but for probably not too much extra you could pick up an audigy with digital out. If it's just analogue you want, then one of the more basic audigy cards is your best bet.

[Here]("http://www.alsa-project.org/alsa-doc/index.php?vendor=vendor-Creative_Labs#matrix") is a list of all soundblaster cards currently supported by ALSA.

HTH and happy new year! :)

I don't know what your motherboard is, but it's more a case of the majority of onboard AC97 sound solutions being pretty shoddy. So shoddy that even an ipod sounds better (though TBH I dunno much about ipods. haven't caught the bug....yet :) )

I installed a pretty cheap and nasty dell soundblaster 5.1 card (I think the cheapest model soundblaster still being produced) on a motherboard with C-Media 8738 sound chip, and that cheap card blew it away.

> **wilko10 said:**
> This started off being a second PC as an experiment into linux but has become the main one. So i'm not too adverse to upgrading as I go along

A decent but still very affordable Soundblaster Audigy will go a long way then. If you care about your audio, onboard sound is only worth it if it's digital.
