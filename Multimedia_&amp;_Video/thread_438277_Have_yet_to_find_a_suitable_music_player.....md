### Post by Znupi on 2007-05-09
Well, music is a very important aspect of my time on the PC. So I need a nice music player... But have yet to find one. Here's what I tried:

**Rythmbox:** was good, but the music stops very often (when there's more CPU usage, like when I'm spinning the mighty Cube :p) and it's annoying.

**Songbird:** it doesn't stop playing when CPU usage spikes, but it can't minimize to tray. I need it to be able to do that.

**XMMS:** Just like Songbird. Works excellent but doesn't minimize to tray. (And the menus are ugly, too, but that's not a definitive criteria)

**Amarok**: Well, this may have been the closest one: minimizes to tray, doesn't stop playing, but it's made for KDE and like most KDE applications, I don't like the UI (bleah, I had Fedora with KDE and hated how everything looked, GNOME ROCKS!!) both because it's looks, but it is also complicated, I need something simple.

**Exaile:** This is what I am using now, but I don't like it because: it can't import any kind of playlist (or at least I haven't been able to) -- although I was able to import my playlist by dragging and dropping from Rythmbox. But has a bug, also (I think) -- it sometimes scrambles the playlist (when restarting it for eg) and I have to reimport it because the order of the files is important :D.

So, what player should I try next? Are these the only ones? Basically all I need is:
1. Minimize to tray
2. Open / Save playlists (.pls or .m3u)
3. Equalizer (not mandatory)
4. Simple, but nice-looking UI (like Songbird has, I really liked it... can't wait until it gets out of BETA...)
5. Steady playback, unlike Rythmbox :|.

Any suggestions?...
