### Post by leonidas666 on 2008-01-23
Are you using a analog Microphone, connected through the mic-input of your sound-card? 
If this is the case, then most probably you have some problem with your sound-card driver. Sometimes the mic-input of the sound card just does not work properly.
Or are you using a usb-microphone /headset? In this case it should be just a matter of using the right driver for the usb-device. Myself i have not used a usb microfone yet, but i have heard that there is a standard (like usb-mass-storage) for the devices, so the installation should be painless...

### Post by farscape on 2008-01-23
Could it be muted?

Doubleclick the Volume Applet on the right side of the upper panel. It looks like a speaker. You should see a window with 2 tabs. One says PLAYBACK and the other says RECORDING. On the RECORDING tab there should be things like VOLUME, LINE-IN, MICROPHONE...etc. Make sure the mic is not muted. If you don't see the MICROPHONE option then go to EDIT>PREFERENCES. Then check the box next to MICHROPHONE. Then it should show up. 
Just a shot. Hope it helps.
