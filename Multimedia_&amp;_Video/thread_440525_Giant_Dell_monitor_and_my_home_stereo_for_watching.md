### Post by spamking2000 on 2007-05-11
Hey Bob,

Not a problem at all!

>>-is it reasonable to use a computer for movie watching instead of a TV and dvd player? What are the pluses and minuses of each way of viewing movies?

Absolutely, the only downside is that unless you have a PC with a remove and the software to run it, then you're stuck using your keyboard and mouse to control watching the movies. The plus side is that its like having the ultimate picture in picture TV with kick *** resolution.

>>-Will Ubuntu work with a 30" dell or 27" monitor?

You betcha.

-Will it work instantly or will I need to do some complex configurations? In other words, do I just plug in the monitor and Ubuntu will automatically work with it properly?

No complex configurations required. Put your DVD in, open it in mplayer or vlc and you're good to go. One note, I would not recommend watching DVDs in Totem. Regular SDTV (and therefore standard non HD or non Blue-Ray DVD's) and DVD's use interlaced video, your monitor does not. What interlacing does on a TV is make it so lines are refreshed in alternate, think 1,3,5,2,4,6 whereas a PC monitor is non-interlaced... meaning it refreshes 1,2,3,4,5,6. Totem does not do a good job with interlaced video and it comes across as choppy and you can see lines. Soooo... it is worth it to at least get mplayer or vlc. Totem is the standard "Movie Player" under Applications -> Sound and Video.

As far as the monitor, yes. However you may want to adjust the resolution. You'll have to check with what both your video card supports and what your Dell monitor supports.

>>-Can I output the audio to my stereo from the computer? I am not interested in surround sound.

Yep. You need a 1/4 mic to RCA Y adapter... which you can pick up pretty much anywhere. Its a pretty standard cable. Target, KMart, Radio Shack certainly should all have them. You might even have one lying around. It would be the same thing you would have used 10 years ago to plug your discman into your home stereo. The 1/4 mic plug is basically just the same thing as a headphone jack.

You may want to adjust the volume on your PC though... having it at 100% going through the mic jack and into your stereo (depends on the stereo) might be loud and distorted... just play with it so that the input is about the same volume as the radio.

>>Will the above work easily and quickly or do you think I will have troubles?

Very easily and quickly. Once you have the cable, the hardest part should be moving that big monitor. I hope its a panel... of course if it is, then I jealous and want it. :) Its as simple as plug the monitor into the PC, plug the PC into the stereo, pop the DVD in, open it in mplayer or VLC and start vegging!

Another thing you can add... basic analog TV cards are pretty easy to get ahold of and cheap these days. Video4Linux supports the Hauppauge cards natively, so if you pick one of those up... think $20 on ebay, you can also watch TV on your computer. I do it on mine and just use the TV time viewer. If you have a remove for your PC, I hear its easy to configure remote software to work with TVTime so that you can control the hotkeys in TVTime from the remote and don't have to be there at the keyboard. I don't have this myself... but just a thought.

Good luck!
