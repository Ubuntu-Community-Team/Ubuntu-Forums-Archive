### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-03-20
Bluefish and Geany have got to be CSS "compliant," but what's the point? There's very little to do in the way of validation with CSS. If you need to know selectors and attributes quickly, you're better off (much) if you dumb the $10 and buy [http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/csspr2/](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/csspr2/) . I'm a web designer and it's a lifesaver for those tough-to-remember things like list-style and other values.
