### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-06-01
Can you get 75Hz in Windows at 1280 by 1024?  If so, then your video card can drive that resolution.

Try bumping up the vertical and horizontal rates in xorg.conf.  I had to do that on one of my machines to get a higher refresh.

Sometimes the monitor auto refresh scanning gets tripped up.  It's possible that your video card puts out 75 Hz but the monitor doesn't pick it up right away and sends a fault signal which causes the card to revert to a default scan rate.

You are running "fglrx" driver, what happens if you switch to "ati" driver?

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-06-01
You realize that Direct Rendering and 3D support use up video memory and video card resources.  Sometimes your card will restrict resolution to free up memory to support texture drawing and other direct render capabilities.  In Windows you don't notice this limitation.  When you start up a game, the screen goes blank and the resolution changes (usually lower) to allow the 3D effects within the game.  When the game is finished, the resolution changes back to full 2D.  DirectX makes this happen in Windows.  

Unfortunately, the xserver architecture is 30 years old (really).  It's amazing that it works at all.  So you are trying to enable effects that are usually only enabled during game play.  You want 3D effects all the time and your card may have limitations.  Or the available ATI Linux drivers have limitations (most likely).  Because the ATI drivers are closed-source, they are reverse-engineered and limited compared to Windows capability.

Buy a used nVidia card where the drivers are better supported (not completely open source) but at least vendor-supported.

Sometimes trying a different distribution helps.  Try Freespire or Suse Linux.  The Live CD's may have better resolution because the drivers are sometimes tweaked by the distro developers.  I found that both Freespire and Suse had better graphics support on machines that I tested (not sure why) but I got 1280 by 1024 at 85 Hz on a crappy Dell GX1 S3 embedded video, 8MB, that I could never get under Dapper.  Don't know what changed.  So I'm happy with SUSE 10 on this Dell, but I miss Synaptic and its package goodness.  I haven't investigated what video driver is being used, because I don't want to go through the pain of a Dapper reinstall on a working system.
