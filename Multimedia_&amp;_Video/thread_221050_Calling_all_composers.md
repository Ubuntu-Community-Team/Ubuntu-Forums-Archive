### Post by KoRnholio on 2006-07-22
You might also consider Rosegarden.  Its a graphical-based front-end for composing, somewhat like Finale or similar programs.

It even lets you dictate notes via a midi input (such as a keyboard).  I'm not aware of its ability to notate a rhythym from midi input, but you can play all the notes and then edit the rhythm rather quickly after the notes are all in.

### Post by stmiller on 2006-08-03
I'm a composer for a living. I use ubuntu on my iBook, and dual boot Gentoo and OS X on a powermac G5. I find the latest release of Rosegarden to be very good. You have to compile it at the moment to get it; none of the distros have it in package form. Rosegarden can do quite a bit; esp with midi editing.

I use Sibelius for scores, personally. Lilypond scores look great but are too time consuming to do, esp for contemporary music. I'd rather spend the time writing music then trying to figure out how to get it notated on the page.

The biggie powerful programs on Linux are Csound (use Cecilia GUI), and pd. Pd is basically Max/MSP. Written by the Max/MSP programmer. And audacity runs very good on Linux. Install the LADSPA plugins and you have tons of good stuff in Audacity. Audacity can handle more audio tracks in Linux than in OS X on my same hardware, curiously enough.
