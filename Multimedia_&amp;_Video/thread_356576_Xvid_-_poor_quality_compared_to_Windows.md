### Post by majoridiot on 2007-02-08
what codecs do you have installed?  the audio/video codec package i installed with automatix
plays xvids flawlessly.  if you haven't installed them, you might try it.  to install automatix (if
you haven't already):

```
# sudo apt-get install automatix2
```

the audio/video codec pack is listed under the "multimedia" tab.

if you have already done that, or there is still a problem, it might have something to do with 
your video driver.  what video card do you have,  and what driver are you using?  

it might also help to post your /etc/X11/xorg.conf and /var/log/Xorg.0.log
to see if there are errors.

### Post by bom28 on 2007-02-08
I wonder if your "poor quality" could be due to wrong luminosity and/or contrast. I had this problem once, can't remember the cause, everything played through the accelerated driver had a very wrong luminosity/contrast. You can easily check by playing your video with "mplayer -vo x11 nameofthevideo.avi" (or choosing the x11 video driver in the graphical mplayer preferences), this will not use the accelerated driver.
