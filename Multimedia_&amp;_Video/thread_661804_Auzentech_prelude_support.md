### Post by Shakey_Jake33 on 2008-01-08
As I'm sure you know, Prelude uses the X-Fi chipset.  There is one existing X-Fi driver by Creative (64bit-only, and likely doesn't support the Prelude, though you could check), but if you're lucky enough to get it working (I was unable, and I actually had to reinstall Ubuntu), it's very unstable and doesn't support imo basic features like SPDIF passthrough.

Only choice is to wait for Auzentech to release theirs.  While their driver is probably based on the buggy Creative one, they do fix all the major problems in the drivers.

I've got an X-Fi XtremeMusic and have had to revert to my onboard Realtek HD Audio.  I've not missed my X-Fi at all tbh, though I do use SPDIF.
