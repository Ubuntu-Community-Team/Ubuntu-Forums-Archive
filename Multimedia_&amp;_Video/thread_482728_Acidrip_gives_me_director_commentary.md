### Post by booljayj on 2007-06-24
I'm trying to find a good way to put all of my movies onto my computer, and I thought that Acidrip was the answer until this moment. I spent 6 hours encoding an avi of Napoleon Dynamite at a resolution of 720x448, a bitrate of 2048, and LameMP3 audio at vbr quality 3. And the result is: the director commentary is on. As soon as I started watching my new avi of Napoleon Dynamite, I got to hear Jared Hess and Jerusha Hess telling me all about how they made the opening title sequence. What the heck? I could understand this if perhaps Acidrip has a feature that I accidentally had checked that gives you the director commentary overlay, but there is no option. Is there any reason at all that Acidrip would be doing this? Or, more accurately, that MEncoder would be doing this? I can't help but feel that this problem will come up again

---Solution---
Director commentary is included as a separate audio stream in the movie, and Acidrip accidentally selected that one by default. Just change the selected audio stream under audio options. Usually, the first track of a language (like the first english track) is the standard track played by a dvd player.

### Post by handy on 2008-03-07
This may or may not go against your politics, but what you could do is source a copy of the freely available (in some parts of the world) DVDShrink.

DVDShrink 3.2, the last version that he made, will allow you to choose which audio tracks you want to include on the ripped to HDDrive DVD movie.

If you want to be able to edit & re-author, like removing chapters & the like as well, then the earlier DVDShrink 1.03 is the one to use.  

SmartRipper is another good free ripping program that also requires Wine to run.

After ripping the DVD to your drive with one of the above (which takes about 20 minutes usually), use whatever you use to compress or change formats.  DVDShrink, shrinks the the movie down so it will fit on a single layer DVD.
