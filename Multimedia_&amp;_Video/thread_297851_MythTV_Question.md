### Post by Orbit45244 on 2006-11-11
I'm planning to setup a MythTV setup on Ubuntu 6.10.  The setup will consist of a backend computer, and two frontend computers.  I want to be able to record 2 shows and watch 2 shows simultaneously.  I've determined that I need 2 WinTV-PVR-350's for the backend, as they can record and playback at the same time.  I am going to use MPEG-2 hardware-based encoding.  But I have a question: Do I need to have a TV Tuner card in both frontends too?  Or do I just need to have regular graphics cards in the frontends? Any help will be appreciated.

---

### Post by PilotJLR on 2006-11-11
Only the backend needs tuners, since it's essentially the recording server.

The frontends are typical computers with no special hardware needs... just make sure the connection between them is fast enough. 100MB ethernet or a very strong 802.11g wireless is ok for SD video. Gigabit is needed for HDTV.

### Post by superm1 on 2006-11-12
Gigabit isn't needed for HDTV.  I easily can stream HD on 100mb ethernet.  If your streaming HD to multiple HTPC's maybe.

For the tuners, don't waste your money on 350's.  They are much more expensive then the 150's or a 500.  You'll get more bang for your buck with the 150's I say.  And you will only need two if you want both frontends to stream live tv.  If you are intending on timeshifting all of your tv (as you should with a PVR :), the last time I flipped on live tv was a few months ago when we had a weather warning.  Time shifted all the time elsewise), then you will only **need** one tuner until you run into recordings on two different channels at the same time.
