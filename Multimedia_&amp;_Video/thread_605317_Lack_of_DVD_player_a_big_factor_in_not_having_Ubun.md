### Post by Omnimaxus on 2007-11-06
I've tried out Ubuntu before, and I love it.  But the one thing that's stopped me from adopting it as a permanent OS on my system is the lack of a simple, easy-to-use DVD player within the OS itself.  I know about Automatix and everything, but even with the codecs, playing DVDs can be a pain.  And since I like my DVD playback to be simple and without issues, it's been a decision-breaker for me on whether or not I should keep using Ubuntu.

I'd be willing to fork over some money just to avoid the hassle of needing to set up codecs and whatnot to get a cleanly designed DVD player that'll support closed-captioning and subtitles right out of the box (since I'm deaf myself); if anyone in the Ubuntu, Linux, or even the movie-making industry made a single package possible that'd play DVDs at ease and provide closed-captioning and subtitles, then I'd stick with Ubuntu, no questions asked.  Anyone out in the Ubuntu community willing to comment on this?

### Post by ohzopants on 2007-11-07
Kaffeine, gxine, mplayer, even totem (which I hate) all play DVD's fine for me, subtitles too (I'm not deaf, but I don't speak korean).

The codec issue is not really anything that can be fixed, it's a legal thing.  In order to play DVDs you need software to break the encryption on the DVD, in the US it is illegal to provide these things without some sort of licensing   You can install them yourself later, but Canonical would be opening themselves up for a world of hurt (legally) if they included it on the disk.

Check out the medibuntu website [http://www.medibuntu.org/]("http://www.medibuntu.org/")

The whole idea behind Ubuntu is that it is comprised of free software...
[http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory/philosophy](http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory/philosophy)
They're not including support for DVD playback or including other non-free and restricted codecs because they would have to start charging us a licensing fee for each liveCD we download...

What you do with it once it's installed, Now that's a different story... :twisted:

Between Medibuntu, Universe and Multiverse repos, it's pretty painless to get DVD and other codecs installed... While not as simple to set up as if it were out of the box, these guys have put together some pretty good media howtos.. and in the case of medibuntu, they were swell enough to host the repos...

Universe and Multiverse repos for Totem playback:
[http://linuxhelp.blogspot.com/2007/10/install-multimedia-codecs-in-ubuntu-710.html](http://linuxhelp.blogspot.com/2007/10/install-multimedia-codecs-in-ubuntu-710.html)
