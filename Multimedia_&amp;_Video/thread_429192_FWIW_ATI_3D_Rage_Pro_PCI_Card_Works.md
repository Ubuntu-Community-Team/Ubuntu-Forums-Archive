### Post by Chappy7777 on 2007-05-01
I installed Dapper 6.06 in my new to me Linux Box today. It is a good step up from what I was using to run 6.06

I was using a P2-333, w/768mgs SDRAM. It had an old Nvidia TNT2 Ultra 32mg card in it. (My 1st 32 bit card that I bought years ago to replace 2 Voodoo 2 8mg cards that were 16 bit. I was bigtime into Flight Sims.

The machine I have now has an AMD Duron 1 Gig CPU and 1 Gig DDR Ram. But the main thing that had me worried and posting on here, was that my TNT2 card will not work on this PC. I changed the BIOS to accept AGP and that didn't help. (The card is AGP). All I got when I booted the PC with this card was a blank screen and lotsa beeping. 

I took the TNT card out and put in an ATI 3d Rage Pro PCI card w/a whopping 8mgs ram. I was worried it wouldn't work at all.  Today I got up the nerve and time to install 6.06 in the thing. The install went great. No hassles at all. And it found and is fine w/the old ATI card. I was able to get connected to the net and send out an email to my geek friends bragging about getting Linux up and running on the new machine.

Bottom line: Unless one needs a major honking video card for gaming or heavy duty graphic work, an old card will do the trick. I priced my ATI Rage card at $14.99 at Tiger Direct. Expensive does not always equal better.

If you're looking for a cheap video card that is happy w/6.06 at least, then the ATI card will do the trick.

Thnx for the tips,
Chappy
