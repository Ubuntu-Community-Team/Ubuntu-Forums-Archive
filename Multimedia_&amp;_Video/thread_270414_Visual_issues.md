### Post by Aikon- on 2006-10-03
I've seen similar screen corruption (as well as the "interference"-like vertical line form of screen corruption); I recommend the first things to check (after powering down your computer):

[LIST]
[*]That your video card is firmly secured in the AGP / PCI-Express slot (also try removing the card and making sure there is no dust or other foreign objects on the connector pins or in the connector port on the motherboard).
[*]Make sure that your video-card's heatsink fan is running (I've experienced this issue before when the bearings went on my VGA cooler; had to replace it then everything was fine). Also check your Northbridge chipsets fan (if it is actively cooled) and your processor fan (just because its easy to check and helps eliminate them as sources of the problem).
[/LIST]

In my experience those are the two most likely (and easiest to check) forms of hardware semi-failure. It takes a couple minutes at most and may fix your problem. Failing that, you may have a more serious hardware malfunction of either your video card or motherboard, or a software issue somewhere in the drivers.

Good luck!

-Aikon
