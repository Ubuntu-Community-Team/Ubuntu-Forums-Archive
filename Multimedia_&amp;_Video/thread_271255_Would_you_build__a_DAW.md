### Post by dolson on 2006-10-05
You don't say what your budget is... But to me, the most important things, in order, are:

Good sound card for audio work
1GB RAM or more
Good, reliable power supply (I use Enermax, myself)
Big, fast hard drive(s)
Fast CPU

That's the order of consideration I would use. Unfortunately my sound card is the worst part of my setup I think, but it's better than some. If you have a lot of money, the RME Hammerfall or whatever it is that Ardour is being developed with these days is probably a good choice. Or maybe one of the M-Audio cards that people use, maybe a Delta 1010 or something.

Personally, I stick with 32-bit. No particular reason, other than some 64-bit users complained to me a lot about how far behind 64-bit is at the moment.

I wouldn't use a notebook myself, but it's just that my experience with laptops and lifespans hasn't been good. Personal choice - depends if you are planning on using your PC to perform concerts (boring in my opinion, and I've seen a few) or if you require portability.

### Post by SnakeFingers on 2006-10-07
Regarding power supplies. This is one of the noisiest parts of any computer. 
Enermax makes good power supplies, the quiet units from PC Power and Colling are considered good too. Anyone thinking of building a DAW would do well to check out [www.endpcnoise.com](www.endpcnoise.com). They sell very quiet power supplies, ones that use a huge heat sink instead of a fan. They also have options to reduce HD noise.
