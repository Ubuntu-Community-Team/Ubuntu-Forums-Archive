### Post by quigleydoor on 2006-07-26
Hello, I just installed Xubuntu 6.06 on my old PC.  I am amazed at how easy it was, and how well the old nag (433MHz Celeron, 256MB RAM) performs now! :) 

I bought a new 1440x900 monitor (Envision H193Wk) and a NVIDIA GeForce 6200 PCI card on eBay.  I was able to install the NVIDIA driver and get it to display at the correct resolution.

The problem is that the image does not cover the whole screen.  About two inches of black, unused space exists on the left side of the screen.  The image is squeezed horizontally, so that it fits into the remaining 14 inches of space on the right.  In other words, it looks like the 1440 pixel columns of the image are squeezed into the rightmost 1280 columns of the physical screen.  (I am guessing at the 1280 number.)

In the vertical dimension, everything is fine.  900 pixel rows of the image are correctly displayed in 900 physical rows of the screen.

This hardware works fine under M$ Windows at 1440x900, and it works fine under Xubuntu at the default resolution, which I guess is 1024x768.

I am new to Linux and X windows.  What can I do to try to fix this weird issue?
