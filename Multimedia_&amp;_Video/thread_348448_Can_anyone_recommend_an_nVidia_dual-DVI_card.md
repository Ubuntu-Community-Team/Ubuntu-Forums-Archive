### Post by glabouni on 2007-01-29
not sure why you want two DVI output. you could use a DVI/VGA adaptor.

anyways here are the 2 nvidia based card I own at the moment. both are heatsink cooled for noise reduction, a Gigabyte 7600GT based [ GV-NX76T256D-RH ]("http://www.gigabyte.com.tw/Products/VGA/Products_Overview.aspx?ProductID=2235") (2 DVI output) and a Gigabyte 6600GT based [ GV-NX66T128VP-SP ]("http://www.gigabyte.com.tw/Products/VGA/Products_Overview.aspx?ProductID=1293") (DVI + VGA)

I have yet to test the dual monitor features with linux as I have no need for it atm.
These cards might be a little overkill for your 3D needs, unless you plan to use more 3D in the future, you should consider buying a cheaper one and using a VGA/DVI adaptor.
