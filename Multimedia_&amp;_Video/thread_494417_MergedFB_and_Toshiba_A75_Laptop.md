### Post by itFinallyWorks on 2007-08-09
Well, I can't believe it!  I finally got it to work!  Beryl even works fine with it (as long as I have one monitor above the other, due to the 2048x2048 limitation of mergedfb).

So here's what I did:
First, I commented out all the mergedfb stuff in my xorg config.
Next, I rebooted my computer with the external monitor cable unplugged.
Next, I setup the xorg config file with mergedfb locked on and the monitors setup the way I wanted them - no autodetects (and of course restarted X).
Finally, I plugged my external monitor in hot - without rebooting my computer.

And what do you know, dual monitors with mergedfb.  The only limitation is I have to unplug my monitor before I boot my computer and plug it in after POST.

Now I think the reason the hot plugged monitor is necessary is I think my bios (this a laptop) is playing games with which monitor is plugged in where.  This is especially noticeable on boot screens: the boot screen will appear on the laptop screen if no external monitor is plugged in and on the external monitor if there is one.

One thing I don't know is why X was not able to overcome whatever games are being played by my bios, especially since my Windows setup has no problems (but, oddly enough, the laptop display is LOCKED as the primary monitor, probably due to the custom driver Toshiba is using).  Maybe they'll fix it in X.org 7.3.  Ideally (and I think in at least some cases it does), x.org should be able to autodetect the two monitors, enable mergedfb, grab the resolution info off ddc, and just work.
