### Post by nicedude on 2008-04-19
The PCI slots you have are not for video cards but for other addon cards. If you have an AGP slot then that is the only slot intended to be used for graphics card update so, yes you have to get a AGP card. from your last post I normally would say get the best Nvidia chipset and most ram but the 6200 & 5200 cards mentioned have some people mentioning issues I don't like to see ( like died in one month & heat issues ) so I can't recommend that you get either of those but after a little looking around there I found this [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814130207](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814130207)  

EVGA 128-A8-N319-LX GeForce FX 5500 128MB 64-bit DDR AGP 4X/8X
Its more than your choices at $38.99 but it has a $20 mail in rebate making it a silly low price of $18.99 after rebate, Also if you read the previous purchasers reviews for it one states that the TV out works in Debian and another that this worked with Ubuntu 7.10 with restricted drivers with ease, which is music to the ears.

And the 128MB of RAM is not allot but if you consider that your current onboard video probably has no ram of its own and has to borrow its ram from your 512MB system memory instead, then it becomes more clear that you will still come out ahead with it. So with this card and 512MB of system memory you should be fine to playback DVD and other videos such as Xvid & Divx . I just wouldn't expect to play any games with high end graphics or anything.

And you mentioned sound in your first post, does your motherboard not have onboard sound? Or is it just not working?
