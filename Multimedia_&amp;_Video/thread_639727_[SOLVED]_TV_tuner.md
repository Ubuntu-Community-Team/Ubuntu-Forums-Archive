### Post by sirdilznik on 2007-12-13
Get the pcHDTV 5500.  It's a great card that does NTSC, ATSC, QAM.  Pretty much any format and the quality is awesome.  Also MythTV was (in some ways) built with this specific family of cards in mind.  The only drawback in it doesn't have a hardware encoder, but today's CPUs are plenty blazin fast.  I barely touch my Opty 165.

The best part is that you will be supporting a company that makes products specifically for Linux.  Support hardware vendors that support Linux!

### Post by fenian on 2007-12-13
You don't need a hardware encoder for digital broadcast signals  because they are already encoded: in mpeg-2 for the video and AC-3 for the audio.However when using the NTSC feature of a card like the pcHDTV it will put some load on the cpu.
