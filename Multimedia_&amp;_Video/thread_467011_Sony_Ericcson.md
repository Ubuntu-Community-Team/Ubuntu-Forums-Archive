I cannot understand why this happened to you. I guess it must depend on your advanced phone more complex software?
This is not what happened to me (or to be more precise, what happened to my kids).
To be frank, I was quite amazed by Ubuntu (Feisty) capabilities regarding this "Sony Ericsson" recognition.
My kids received two (different) Sony Ericsson gsms this year: a W300 and a W810.
They use a desktop with dual boot windows XP/Ubuntu Feisty.
First things they did (I disapproved, but it was interesting to watch) was to search for pirated mp3 and pirated 128*160 jar games on the web.
Of course they found almost at once everything they wanted. (I still disapprove it, but I was duly impressed by the quality, quantity and speed of such retrievals).

Now the problem was to transfer files between the download folder and the "M2" memory cards (those uselessly expensive specific  things that these idiots at sony insist you have to use with their products).
Two possibilities: follow the  instructions and use Windows (installing the appropriate, enclosed software) or just plug and play with Linux.

While with windows you had to install all kind of crap to have such transfer working, with Ubuntu it was just simple real "plug and transfer files", working on the fly (ok, admittedly you have to learn that you have to create a directory for your new games files on your memory card, and that such directory will appear inside  the "other" directory, but that isn't exactly rocket science). 

In windows you have to create "different profiles" for each user, get used to slowness, obey every whim of the proprietary software you have installed and ** be aware that everything gets syphoned to redmond every time you transfer whatever** (with all obvious further added delays this will provoke). 
In GNU/Linux/Ubuntu/Feisty it's just **moving files between folders**. That's all.

I'm really impressed: Gnu/Linux is quicker, effectiver and hence BETTER than windows in order to transfer any kind of files to our GSMs.
Note that the kids don't need to change profile, change parameters, settings, nothing. 
They just plug whenever they care their own gsms **on the same box, with the same cable** and start transferring files from the box to the gsm.
Or browse and delete files on their gsms.
Quick and effective. After all who really ever needed to keep track of what mp3 or jar files he had transferred or not on the phone? 
I mean, I can maybe understand a copy of your own addresses and private own-produced stuff, but why the hell should you need to keep a special mirror  of the software you install?
In doubt or in crash it's always quicker to just retransfer everything again from a folder repository (or -maybe better- to fetch newer versions from the web :-))  than to have some cumbersome and slow proprietary software to reinstall everything -with no guarantee of success- from some equally slow "automated" back-copies...

So Linux is better and quicker than windows for transferring files to Sony Ericssons gsms. Definitely.
That's good news!  :D
Eat that Gates: we now have **more** privacy, **more** choice AND **more** speed!
