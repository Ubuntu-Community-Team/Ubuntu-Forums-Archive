### Post by Kourosism on 2007-05-31
Over the past year or so, I've been downloading songs through the Napster to Go service. For the benefit of anyone who doesn't know what this is, it's a service where for a flat fee each month you can download as much music as you want, and listen to it as often as you'd like, as long as you keep up the subscription. It's heavily DRM restricted, and I think that in this instance, that's fair.

Now, such a service has benefits and drawbacks, but I have a lot of music through it, and this is the position I'm in.

I don't mind downloading all the tracks again, but can anyone recommend another service which will run under Linux with similar capabilities? Or another alternative altogether?

### Post by Alex Fernandez on 2007-06-01
Its not a matter of there not being any DRM support under Linux, its a matter of the companies that develop/market these DRM enabled tracks not creating a DRM enabled player/system for Linux - and of course you cant open-source such a system (ie cant be made by the community) because the specs are sorta guarded as a trade secret.

But, DRM does suck - if I buy something I should be play to use it (in this case play it) whereever and on whatever I want, be it Windows, Linux, IPod, Zen, Zune, CD or even teach my dog/cat/parrot how to sing it.
