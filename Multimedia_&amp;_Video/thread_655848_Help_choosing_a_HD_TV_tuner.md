### Post by twin_57103 on 2008-01-01
I’m looking for a HD TV tuner for my dual-boot computer to replace the TV (my 22” widescreen monitor beats it hands down and I'm trying to move to a much smaller apartment).  I’ve been working on this for a couple days, and I’ve got an idea what I want, but I’m having trouble finding a product to match the idea.

I’ve found plenty of information on the forums and other web sites (linuxtv.org, mythtv.org) about older cards, but little about current cards.  If necessary, I’ll get a Windows-based card, but that would mean that I’d spend even less time in Linux, which would be a shame.  I’d appreciate any help you could give!

I am running WinXP Home and Ubuntu 7.10 on a 3.0 GHz P4, 1.5 Gb RAM.  I don't have cable right now, but hope to add it in the future (we have Comcast digital cable in my area).

**What I need: **
A current HD TV tuner that will work on both OS’es.

**Things I’d like:**
Remote included
I’d prefer internal (PCI), but USB is OK, too.
Video capture capability (this is why I’m looking for a computer card rather than a direct-to-monitor unit)
Composite/RCA input (can always use an adapter to S-video if need be)
Ability to accept and display digital cable
NTSC (standard broadcast TV) in addition to ATSC (digital TV) - it would be nice, but not a priority

### Post by twin_57103 on 2008-01-06
Update: I ended up choosing the KWorld 115.  It seemed to suit my needs, including flexible input options, PCI, Windows and Linux compatible.  I ordered it online, and I am waiting for the card to arrive.

It's useful to note that the packaging for KWorld cards isn't obvious about the model numbers - this was part of my frustration in choosing a card.  The image of the box is marked PlusTV, with the model number hidden away in the product description.
