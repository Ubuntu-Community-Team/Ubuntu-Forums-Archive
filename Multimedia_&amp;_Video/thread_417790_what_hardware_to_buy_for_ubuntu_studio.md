### Post by Mike'sHardLinux on 2007-04-23
Any of the current processors should be excellent. X2 or Core 2 Duo. Heck, I started PC recording on a Cyrix M2 running at around 200MHz, I believe.

What you need will be dependent on things like how many simultaneous tracks you will record, and how much real-time effects you like to use. More tracks and more effects will want more ram and more processing power.

I would say that 1.5GB of ram is plenty for now. Are you planning to use some parts you already have?

Here are some things to consider:

1) Do you have a preference of AMD or Intel? Honestly, they are both good. You'd be better off to decide on a budget for the cpu, and see who has the best bang-for-buck cpu in that range....and this can change from week to week.

2) The motherboard is a tougher choice, mainly because there are so many to choose from. I have had good luck with Gigabyte, Abit, Asus, DFI and MSI. There are a lot of personal opinions about which brand sucks and which is good. Unfortunately, a LOT of people don't really build that many machines and base their opinion on 1 or 2 good or bad builds. Your best bet is to research several models. Read the reviews on newegg. Read the articles on the hardware sites likes Tom's Hardware and anandtech. Maybe narrow down your search to a few choices, and then do a search on the forums and see if people have had good or bad experiences with these specific models.

This pc I am on right now uses a Core 2 Duo E6400 and an MSI P965 Neo, and it has been rock solid. I have another system that is using an Athlon X2 3800+ and a Gigabyte board and it has also been a good system. I can't remember what model the Gigabyte is, but can find out if you are interested. Oh, and both systems are running Edgy.

3) You may want to try to make your PC as quiet as possible; but usually, this will cost more. A LOT of products claim to be quiet, but very few of them actually are. You can compromise and get a reasonably quiet PC.

I think your budget is very reasonable, and you should be able to build a kickass pc for that amount!

### Post by DJiNN on 2007-04-23
I'd have to agree with the previous posters. I use a Delta 1010 and also have 3x 2496 cards in several machines used in different locations, and ALL of them work absolutely perfectly. The M-Audio equipment is superb, and the prices are great. Can't recommend them enough. 

As for hardware, the only advice that i feel qualified to give is this.... "Don't buy too cheap, and don't spend too much!" :) ....... Aim for somewhere down the middle (Where there's a LOT of choice) and you'll pretty much be OK.  Of all the machines that i have run Music software on (Mainly Windows though.... I'm only just delving into Ardour & Rosegarden etc) i have ended up opting for "Shuttles" with nVidia chipsets. They're reasonably priced, small & compact so fit well into a small studio environment, have exactly what i need (Couple of PCi's, AGP, Full compliment of USB & Firewire etc) and just do the job supremely well. I have 3 shuttles that i use regularly (I sing & record for a living) and they ALL do exactly what i want, day in & day out. 

One other thing that i would say is this, regardless of what machine you end up with, you may want to steer well clear of VIA chipsets...... 

Why? Don't know, they just don't seem to cut it IMHO. Everytime i have tried a machine with a VIA chipset (For music) they end up cutting out, or crashing/hanging frequently...... just a lot of hassle that i never get with a decent chipset. 

Microphones - personally i use a Behringer B2 for most of my work, but i've also just purchased a couple of SE Electronics Mics (Can't remember the model numbers but they're large Diaphragms) and i've set one up for my partner and she loves it, and the results (For such a well priced mic) are superb. It's running through an ART V3 preamp into a 2496. The Behringer (Which i have been using ever since they came out) runs through a Joe Meek compressor (Which i cannot praise highly enough) and again into a 2496. Results are wonderful.

Hmmm, i didn't mean to rant on that much. :)  Good luck with your studio, i'm sure that you'll learn a lot & enjoy the process. I'm sorry i can't really help you with regards to using the Linux software but i'm only just starting down that road myself, and even then i'm somewhat hesitant because my Win setup (Cubase VST/32, Sound Forge, Reason V3 etc) just works so well.... But i'm going to give it a try and see how i get on. Just got to put an old machine together to try it all on first, hopefully running the latest (Feisty) Xubuntu. (I LOVE Xubuntu!) 

All the best........ & keep me posted with your progress.... always good to hear how others are doing it. :)
