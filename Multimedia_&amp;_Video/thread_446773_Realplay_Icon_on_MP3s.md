### Post by m.musashi on 2007-05-18
Not too sure, but here is one idea. Icons are usually tied to themes so try opening the theme preference tool and selecting a new one or selecting a new icon theme. You can also find the icon and delete it (or rename it). If the icon it's looking for doesn't exist it will fall back to a default icon. Open your file browser and press ctrl+h to unhide folders. Look for .icons and open it and see if you can find the icon in question. It could also be in one of the root folders.
