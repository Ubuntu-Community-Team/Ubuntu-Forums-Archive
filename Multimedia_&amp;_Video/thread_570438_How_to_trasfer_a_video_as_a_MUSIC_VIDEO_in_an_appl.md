### Post by Axx83 on 2007-10-08
Hello everyone, my problem is simple, I encode my videos to be played on my 60bg iPod video with Vive [http://vive.sourceforge.net/]("http://vive.sourceforge.net/") which is a front end for ffmpeg that has a built in profile and setting to encode for ipod, specifically created for newbies like me...the problem is:

When i transfer the file with gtkpod it is recognized as a "movie" in my iPod and not a "music video" (it means that it will not be played as a song in shuffle mode, for example, or will be able to put in a playlist) even tough when i encode it I write the title and the artist in the author slot in Vive before pressing start. The output is recognized as a movie by my iPod, the title is show on the iPod (at least that is ok) but not the artist and not as a music video as i want to...

Any help?

ps. i used the medibuntu repository to install ffmpeg and all the things requested by Vive.

### Post by kostkon on 2007-10-08
It does not matter how you encode your video but how the video is saved in the iPod library file. *Gtkpod* is not able, at the moment, to define files as music videos in the library.

I recommend you *Floola*. It is a very good program for *iPod* management, and every time you upload a video file, you will be able to define if it is a movie, music video or a TV show.

You can get a [deb package from *getdeb.net*]("http://www.getdeb.net/app.php?name=Floola") or [get the binary from it's site]("http://www.floola.com/modules/wiwimod/index.php?page=download").
