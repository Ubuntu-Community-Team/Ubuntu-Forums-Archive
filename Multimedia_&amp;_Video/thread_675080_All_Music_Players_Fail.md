### Post by rosegarden78 on 2008-01-22
The restricted extras contain CODECS and drivers to decrypt MP3, DVD, and protected content.  For some reason Windows has the legal right to include codecs in USA but Ubuntu doesn't.  You should be OK as long as you don't sell copyrighted stuff or use file sharing.  Amarok and Kaffeine are music player and disc burner.  They are KDE apps you can just install them alone but I just install kde & xfce.  That way I get two more windows environments at the login screen with all the programs and dependencies.  You still need the restricted extras if you want to play DVD and MP3 files.
