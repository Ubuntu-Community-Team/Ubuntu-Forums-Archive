I have a Samsung 26 inch tv that I am trying to use as a monitor. In the tv manual it has info about display modes for using the tv as a monitor. It says the Optimum: 1360x768 pixels with vertical frequence refresh rate 60 Hz.

I previously was running a 17 inch monitor when ubuntu was set up and then put on the new tv.

I am running Ubuntu 6.06.1 LTS.

With the new monitor connected I ran sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg. It detected my monitor. It detected my video card as ATI Technologies, Inc. RV350 AR [Radeon 9600 XT]. The list of resolututions I was presented with in 'reconfigure' didn't include the highest resolutions but I figured I could just edit the xorg.conf file myself.

I added "1360x768" to my xorg.conf (after backing it up) to several depth subsections, but this resolution doesn't appear in my list when I go to change screen resolution.

1024x768
800x600
340x480
832x624

### Post by Cereus on 2007-06-14
I got the thing to work so I thought I should post a followup in case someone else wants to try.

I was running with the original Ubuntu provided video drivers.  Ati drivers aren't open so they aren't installed. I had to follow instructions on this tutorial:
[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Dapper_Installation_Guide](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Dapper_Installation_Guide)

And once I did that the higher resolution that I had added to my xorg.conf file appeared as an option. I also was put to 60Hz without making the change as suggested above (but thanks for your reply!).
