### Post by Zimmer on 2006-02-10
[http://www.dpreview.com/](http://www.dpreview.com/)
will give you all the specs you need ,
If you want ease of use a Fujipix A345 is a good bet, particularly if you are not a  seasoned photographer. I have a Minolta Dimage S414 which is feature packed for the aspiring photographer, but I find I always use it on auto ! (It also eats batteries!!, something the Fuji does not. I also recommend you get a camera that uses standard AA batteries.Then get some Nimh batteries and a charger, so budget for that,too.
Advice for transferring data from Camera to PC: buy a usb card reader. I have a Trust 16-1 card reader which is spotted without fuss by Ubuntu. That way you do not need fancy cables and software to connect the camera and you do not risk the camera's batteries failing while you have it connected and trying to read/write data. And always format the card in the camera, not on the PC
