### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2009-05-20
I'm not a lawyer but, yes, there are laws that could have consequences for copying content from YouTube, plus a bunch of other possible consequences not related to the law. What you copy and what you do with the copied content pretty much determines how likely you are to get in trouble.

Chances are you won't get caught and nothing will happen, but on the other hand the owner of the content you copied could pursue copyright infringement damages if you reuse or sell their content without permission and YouTube could ban you from their services for breaking the EULA/terms. Or worst of all you could end up facing criminal copyright infringement charges that have substantial fines and possible jail time (at least in the United States). Depending on who your ISP is there could be issues there as well with their terms, you could get expelled from school or college if using their networks as a student, or get fired from work if using an employers internet for copyright infringement.

Its probably best to stay on the safe side and just not copy any content from YouTube. Besides, is there really anything on that site that you absolutely can't live without or that would be worth the consequences? That's my two cents. Cheers!
