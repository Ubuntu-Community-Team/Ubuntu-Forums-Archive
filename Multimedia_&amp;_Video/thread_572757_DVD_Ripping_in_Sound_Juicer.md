### Post by nzadLithium on 2007-10-10
when you burnt the dvd did you explicitly tell it to burn it as an audio dvd?

Is there even such a thing?

Anyway chances are you didnt so sound juicer wont be able to open it.

You just need to go into places, then click the dvd (the one that looks like a cd but isnt called cd/dvd creator.)

All your music should still be on there in the m4a format.

Burning you music to an audio cd/dvd is not the best way to convert it anyway. It would be best to use an actual conversion program. you can probably use soundconverter which is in synaptic.

Linux should be able to play m4a files anyway so i would just leave them as they are. I don't know if gxine or anything that packaged with ubuntu will be easy to get them to play. I would just install VLC (also in synaptic) and use VLC to play them.

And if you havent already found a program that will get songs onto your ipod you could try gtkpod (in synaptic :D)
