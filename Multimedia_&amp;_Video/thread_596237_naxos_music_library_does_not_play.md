### Post by MikeEllis on 2007-11-03
Hi,
I'm having the same problem accessing Naxos under Gutsy and previously under Feisty.  I get my access as a student at UNCA.  No problem logging in, searching for tracks, or selecting them to play, but I get no sound..  I have no problem accessing other audio services, including the Alexander Street Press classical music streaming library provided by the university.

Googling for "linux" with "naxos music library" turned up a blog by an admin at another university that's worth reading. It's at [http://www.jacob-head.com/naxos.php](http://www.jacob-head.com/naxos.php).  

He says "Listening to files from Naxos Music Library on Linux is somewhat tricky because of the large number of nasty propriety formats used by Naxos Music Library to obfuscate and hide the streams."  He details one workaround that involves pasting static URL's for Naxos tracks into Totem Movie player, but that seems completely unsatisfactory as is means that you have to look up each track you want to play on Naxos, display its status URL, and do a copy/paste to hear it.

There's got to be a better way :-)

Hope this helps (or at least helps you know you're not alone.),
Mike

### Post by MikeEllis on 2007-11-07
Following up on my last post, it seems as though installing mplayer and mplayer-plugin is not sufficient in all cases.  I just upgraded my wife's desktop and found that, additionally, I needed to install the "ubuntu-restricted-extras".  After that, Naxos works well. See [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats") for more info about installing the restricted extras.
Cheers,
Mike
