### Post by Hal58 on 2006-08-05
Is there a CD-player/ripper program that works under Ubuntu 6.06 (Dapper) that displays CD-Text info, and for ripper, extracts the text info?  Back in the 2.4 kernel days with Turbolinux, Grip did the job, and filled in Artist and Title information in the .inf files extracted.

I have tried the normal programs available in the Ubuntu distro as well as some other programs including cli players, and direct routines such as cdda2wav (which worked in the old 2.4 days) and cdrdao.  cdrdao is the only one that seems to recognize CD-Text CDs any more, but will not rip individual tracks, simply named indices indo the single raw image.

Since I play old music captured from LPs and cleaned up, the CDDB accesses commonly used with CDs are not an option for artist/title data, nor is it a solution for the Korean CDs I enjoy.

As an aside, I can still create CD-Text discs in Dapper by manually creating a .toc file and burning with 'cdrdao', and my Toyota Prius with the stock CD player displays the information (although Album and Title, not the preferred Artist and Title).
