### Post by eye208 on 2007-11-13
> **01steven said:**
> Output container: MP4
Output video codec: H.264/AVC
Output audio codec: AAC
Video scale size: 320x240
Video Aspect: Keep the original aspect the video is in
Video Frames per second: 23.98
Video Bitrate: 128kbps
Audio sampling frequency: 48000
Audio Channels: 2
Audio Bitrate: 64kbps
```
mencoder inputfile.avi -vf expand=:::::4/3,scale=320:240 -af channels=2,resample=48000 -ovc x264 -oac faac -of lavf -x264encopts bitrate=128 -faacopts br=64 -lavfopts format=mp4:i_certify_that_my_video_stream_does_not_use_b_frames -o tempfile.mp4
ffmpeg -i tempfile.mp4 -vcodec copy -acodec copy outputfile.mp4
```
I am not sure how stable MEncoder's AAC encoder is at this point. If these settings do not work you might have to resort to MP3 audio instead. The additional ffmpeg call fixes a problem with the MP4 container generated by MEncoder.

I know you asked for a graphical tool, however you should give the command line a try. Once you have found the right settings you can put them into a script file which will be very handy when used on a large number of video files.

### Post by eye208 on 2007-11-14
> **Marco Polo said:**
> i would recommend you to forget about MP4 format and install SmartMovie Player to your phone. This video player is able to play DivX or Xvid encoded videofiles (audio is in MP3), which as a matter of fact are current standarts for compressed video.
Your suggestion does not make sense.

DivX/Xvid and MP3 are media codecs. MP4 is a media container format. A codec cannot replace a container.

DivX/Xvid and MP3 can replace H.264 and AAC codecs. But that does not make sense either because H.264 and AAC are superior to DivX/Xvid and MP3.

Here is a relation chart between codecs, formats and MPEG specifications (oldest first):

MP3 = MPEG-1 Part 3 Layer 3
DivX = implementation of MPEG-4 Part 2 (ASP)
Xvid = free implementation of MPEG-4 Part 2 (ASP)
AAC = MPEG-4 Part 3
FAAC/FAAD2 = free implementation of MPEG-4 Part 3
H.264 = MPEG-4 Part 10 (AVC)
x264 = free implementation of MPEG-4 Part 10 (AVC)
MP4 = MPEG-4 Part 14
GPAC = free implementation of MPEG-4 Part 14

If you have a device that can handle H.264 video and AAC audio in MP4 containers, consider yourself lucky, because this is the current state of the art. Forget about DivX/Xvid and MP3. They are a waste of space.
