### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-03-22
Sometime drive speed matters but if you actually look at the speed of the rip (at least in windows), it will usually hover around 6x to 8x. Sure a faster drive couldn't hurt either and with current prices, look into a DVD burner. It may only cost a small amount more than a read only drive.

A faster processor would be your best bet. But I see you have a Pentium M, is that a laptop you are using to rip. If so your options may be limited and cost vs performance may not justify the money you will have to drop. More RAM may help but I doubt it as this is really precessor intensive.

But keep in mind if you rip a movie in real time already that is not bad. For what you may end up spending on laptop parts (still assuming you have a laptop), you may as well buy a refurbished desktop to do the work.
