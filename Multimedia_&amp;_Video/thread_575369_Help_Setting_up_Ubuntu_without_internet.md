### Post by AMDBuntu on 2007-10-14
If you get high speed internet you should be able to hook it up, via the service provides router, dirrectly to the ethernet connection on your computer. I have Verizon FIOs and there was absolutely no configuration. Just plugged the cable in an took off.

That said, I'm running two Ubuntu machines on wifi. Both of my wifi cards have Atheros chipsets in them. Ubuntu picked these right up and installed a restricted driver for it. You can check out recommended wifi cards in the recommended wifi card section - or I'll go look at what brand I have. Think I got mine for about $16 on eBay.

I gave up on the gstreamer and plugins, etc for DVDs. Just use VLC player. It works better and doesn't need any plugins. It's availbe in the repository - once you get on line. They also have a website. I use it for windows too. Works awesome!

My wireless is:

TRENDnet 108Mbps 802.11G Wireless PCI Adapter TEW-443PI
