### Post by airtonix on 2007-10-27
Itunes treats your ipod files as "despensible", in that itunes expects you to keep two copies of your music.

Itunes  obfuscates your ability to navigate the ipod via nautlius or windows explorer and identify songs by file name, or folder name, unless you have system plugin in your OS thatdynamically replaces file names (on display only, wont permeantly rename them) with infor found in mp3/acc metadata

itunes only supports mp3 or acc. not ogg or flac or wav or any other opensource media format.

I highly recomend you install rockbox on your ipod..

for i have it on mine, now i can : 
1 take an mp3 off my friends computer(which doesnt have itunes),
2 drag it onto a folder in my ipod(named by me and controlled by me)
3 unplug my ipod (notice i skip the whole itunes dance)
4 play the mp3/ogg/wav/flac straight away.

If i did not have rockbox i would have been able to copy the file onto my ipod, but i would have no way of listening to it unitl i reached an itunes enabled computer, at which point i would have to then move the song off again and then get itunes to move it back again.


Rockbox also allows me to read 5mb text files....Itunes fails here only show small files.

Rockbox is working on video support, and when it does i no longer need to dual boot my ipod. not that i bother with video via itunes way since it requires a convoluted dance to sync the data.

Rockbox allows me to create simple and effectively beatifull themes based on bmps and a text file for layout....I often make themes whislt travelling on the bus through town..as rockbox also has a text editor)

the amount of plugins avail to rockbox demostrate the ease of which it is expanadable.

what else? rockbox make the appleos on the ipod look like a joke really. 

its not even faster than rockbox anymore. all it has over it is video. nothing else.


Gnome Music Browser is a player that i recommned. it has a good filesystem viewer and its helps with categorising music into genres if your into that sort of thing...

I personally like to keep my muci in compilations of my own creation....i also like to just drag one of those folders onto xmms or winamp....or gmusicbrowser

last thing i want to do is spend five minutes build ing a playlist from a list of artists. but sometimes i might want to ... gmusic browser does all this in one app...
