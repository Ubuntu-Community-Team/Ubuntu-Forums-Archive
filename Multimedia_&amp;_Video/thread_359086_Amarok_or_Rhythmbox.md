### Post by sayhello on 2007-02-11
I was a big iTunes user. However, after switching to Ubuntu, I was intrigued by Amarok's approach to music player UI. Since then, I'm totally hooked! I like all the functionality, the neat features, organization, configurability etc...
However, I do think that Rhythmbox is easier to use for people with less familiarity with computers.

I'm sure i'm going to draw lots of flame but... less is more sometimes. That's the overall philosophy with Gnome apps; Less clutter, complexity etc. perhaps at the price of configurability.
Rhythmbox's UI designer was obviously inspired by iTune's UI, which isn't a bad choice at all since Apple is know for simplicity and elegance.
I voted for Amarok, since its my personal preference. However, I do think those music players are for users of different affinities.

### Post by coolen on 2007-08-19
I think it depends what you do with your music.
For my needs, Rhythmbox is perfect. Light and attractive, with just enough of those extra features to keep me happy.
It's got smart playlists, a good library interface, limited iPod support (I prefer to use a separate application for syncing, anyway), and everything else I've ever asked for from a music player.
I tried Amarok, and it was nice, but I didn't like the interface. Many options, but more than I'll ever need. For those who prefer a greater degree of control over their music, it'd be a fine option, but I just put it on random. Sometimes I'll choose a particular artist. So you see, for me, Rhythmbox is the perfect match.
