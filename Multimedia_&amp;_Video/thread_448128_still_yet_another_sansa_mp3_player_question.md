### Post by alfredeneuman on 2007-05-19
Experience has taught me help is not forthcoming here so I dug a little deeper and offer the following for other people also new to Linux having trouble with/understanding their Sansa mp3 player.

I called the Sansa help line with question about how to access the removable micro disk in the player. I was told it is not specifically identified by the player; nor can it be directly accessed by the player when not connected to a computer. Rather it functions as a kind of covert storage/overflow container for the mail Sansa disk, thereby increasing space on the main Sansa disk. A caveat is you can only put mp3 songs into the removable micro disk--not videos, etc.

Lastly, I was wondering where my all my space had disappeared. After reading a lot of postings, I found  this: inside either drive, simply hit ctrl and the letter "h." That will bring up a hidden "trash" folder, which then can be emptied and subsequent space recovered.
