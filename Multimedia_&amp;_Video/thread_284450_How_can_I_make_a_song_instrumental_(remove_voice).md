### Post by spin2cool on 2006-10-25
Short answer:  There's no good way without access to the original, multi-track recordings.  

The frequency of the human voice overlaps the frequencies used by many of the instruments.  You can certainly cut out those frequencies using any decent audio editor, but the resulting music quality will be pretty shitty.

### Post by skymt on 2006-10-25
You want a program called Audacity. Install it via your favorite package manager. I use aptitude (sudo aptitude install audacity). Open it (Applications > Sound & Video > Audacity), and open up your song. Find the bit you want to chop out, select it, delete it, and save.
