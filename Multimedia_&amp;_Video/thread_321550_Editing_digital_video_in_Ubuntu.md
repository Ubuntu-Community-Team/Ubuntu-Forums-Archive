### Post by tcnguyen on 2006-12-30
Well, I am pretty new with Kino. Just finished my first video. It is not as bad. Although I agree that Studio DV is so easy to use, I had been using it for a quiet a while. But what to expect for the free software :-) here is my thought of Kino:

pro: 
    - plugin choices are good. I used ffmpeg, the encode time is quiet fast, about 1 minute for 1 minute video.
    - vi binding key is great. 
    - free :-)

con: (or wish list)
   - audio mixing feature (I am on 0.8); wish it could be like Studio where you can drag mp3 file to the frame.
   - Combine the "edit" mode with "timeline" mode? I found it's rather slow every time I split the frame, then Kino jump back to edit mode, then I have to go back to timeline to cut/split the frame ... maybe I am so new that I do not know better way of delete some frames?

Overall, it's the same quality as when I first use Studio DV 0.1 :-) with less crash (and cash).
