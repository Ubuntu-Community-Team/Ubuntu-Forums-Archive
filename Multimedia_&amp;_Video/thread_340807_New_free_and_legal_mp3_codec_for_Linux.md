### Post by ubuntukid on 2007-01-17
Just thought it might interest people to know that there is now a legal free way of listening to mp3's on linux. Fluendo has just recently released a free (not open-source mind) mp3 codec for gstreamer. You can download it from their website [https://shop.fluendo.com/](https://shop.fluendo.com/). They also sell quite a collection of other codecs like windows media codecs. I think I might buy the codec pack (28 euros) to support them.

They're also in the process of making a DVD player which will be sold to individuals and distro makers. I think this is great and should be advertised more. This means that people can use a whole range of propriatery codecs on Ubuntu for a cheap price (without braking the law).

They currently have the following codecs;
Windows Media Audio Decoder
Windows Media Video Decoder
Windows Media ASF Demuxer
Windows Media MMS Networking

MPEG2 Video Decoder
MPEG4 Video Decoder
MPEG2 Program Stream and Transport Stream demuxer
MPEG4 ISO Demuxer
MP3 Audio Decoder
AC3 Audio Decoder

Doesn't look like their capable of encoding mp3's though, which is a shame.
