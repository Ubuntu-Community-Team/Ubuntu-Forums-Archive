You know, Linux isn't for everyone.  Neither is Microsoft or MacOS.  Each has it's own strengths and weaknesses.  With your comment ("...if Linux is good") I wonder if you're making the wrong choice.  Linux is GREAT.  For me.  I love it.  Windows is constraining and annoying.  Your mileage may (and undoubtedly WILL) vary.

Keep in mind that support for Microsoft programs like MSN in Linux is not a given, remember what the "MS" in "MSN" stands for?  Furthermore, because many hardware companies focus on Microsoft drivers and compatibility, it's often up to volunteers (essentially) to provide support and they often get little or no help from the hardware manufacturer.

I recently installed Vista for my father and his old scanner and webcam became paperweights as a result since there are no Vista drivers for them.  

Please approach this decision with an open mind but look before you leap.  There's no sense in having another person feel "cheated" after switching to Linux.  Video conferencing support in Linux has just evolved.  As a newbie, I would use caution.
