### Post by glabouni on 2007-01-30
I've been trying out to find replacement for the software I used in windows with little success till now.  AFAIK there's simply no equivalent of [foobar2000]("http://www.foobar2000.org/"), [supercopier]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/supercopier/"), [flashfxp]("http://www.flashfxp.com") for linux among others.

I'm currently trying out various linux media players, looking for a replacement to [media player classic ]("https://sourceforge.net/projects/guliverkli/")

what's bothering me is the fact that I fail to find the "always on top" option in most of the players. so far I've found it in totem ( which I'm not too fond of ) and in vlc (which I already knew from windows and dislike for personal reasons) and failed to find it in xine, mplayer, kaffeine, aviplay, xmovie.
kmplayer and noatun just refused to play any file and crshed on load.

I couldn't believe such a feature would be left out of linux media players. That could only mean one thing: this feature is offered by the system/windows manager itself. 

So I finally tried to right click the window title bar and found a "on top" option, and there was it.

I suppose you noticed I fixed the problem on my own, and in you case you wonder why I post anyways, it's because I've made a forum search with no success, so I decided to post hping this could be be helpful for someone in the same situation.
