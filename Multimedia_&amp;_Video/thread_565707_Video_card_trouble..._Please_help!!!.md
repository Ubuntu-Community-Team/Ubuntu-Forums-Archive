### Post by greenstar on 2007-10-02
Short version:

I've not tried Gutsy yet, but that being said, I've never had a problem with Ubuntu and the Radeon 9250. I have 2 machines with a Radeon 9250/Athlon XP and 1 with a Radeon 9250/P4. I've even installed Beryl, now switched to Compiz Fusion on 2 of them with some success, although they're more limited to the basic effects than a faster PC/Gfx card combo.

At a terminal do:
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg

If you need help with the configuration steps, I'll see what I can do for you.


Long version:

I'll have to try it myself, but I have several machines with ATI Radeon 9250 and I've had no troubles with Ubuntu & Radeon 9250. Some other ATI chipsets are nothing but a big PITA. I quit buying ATI video cards a while ago because Nvidia products work so much better on Linux. My advice: Buy the best GeForce your budget allows.

I'm not trying to sound like an Nvidia fanboy. I used to use ATI exclusively after 3dfx settled with Nvidia and went under. I loved 3dfx back in the day, and was PO'ed that Nvidia shut them down. I used ATI exclusively for 5 years or more, but with the shoddy quality & support for open source over the last 3 years, I walked away. Now AMD has bought ATI and has begun working with the FLOSS community toward open-source drivers (I believe). I hope AMD turns ATI around, because I like AMD procs, and I imagine that the merger means we will be seeing lots of integrated AMD/ATI chipsets in laptops (likely desktops also) in the future. 

Greenstar
