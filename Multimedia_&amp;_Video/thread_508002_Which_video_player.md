### Post by zanglang on 2007-07-24
That's a hard question... there really isn't such a thing as "One size fits all" in Linux, you know. If there were, Ubuntu wouldn't exist, and we'd all be using an ancient Unix distribution by IBM... or Minix... or Windows. :D

Imho,
VLC offers a ton of keyboard shortcuts and has more features than any other players, but I think isn't as well-polished on Linux yet.
Mplayer is the fastest player for me, and renders larger videos that totem would choke on with a little extra configuration, but I really dislike its GUI.
Totem's minimalistic design is great, and does everything under the hood *just* nicely, but offers a tad too little controls for me. The gstreamer backend is a little iffy, so I use Xine.

As a result, I just left all 3 of them installed and use one whenever I feel like it. I'd highly recommend that you *explore* the choices thoroughly yourself too, after all, choice is a good thing. ;)

### Post by RomeReactor on 2007-07-24
Hi. I agree with **zanglang** in that each player has its own strengths and weaknesses; however, my do-it-all player of choice is Totem: clean interface, compatibility with virtually every format out there (including DVD playback) and perfect integration in my Gnome desktop. I also have Mplayer installed, since I prefer it's Firefox plugin, and when I want to watch a particularly high resolution video (with which Totem admittedly has sometimes less than stellar performance). VLC is very good, but between Mplayer and Totem, I don't need it.

Having said that, Totem is my player of choice.
