### Post by stmiller on 2007-09-11
iTunes music which is wrapped in DRM has the extension .m4p. 
.mp4 and .m4p are both AAC files. There is currently no way to decrypt them, or strip the DRM, though several projects did this in the past. The music is locked to the computer where the purchase was made, and that user's iPod. :(

DRM = yucky

You can just burn the iTunes music store files to an audio CD, then rip again which will free them from DRM, but also will degrade the quality.
