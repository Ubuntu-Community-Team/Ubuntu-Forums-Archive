### Post by xjgnsdof on 2007-06-11
I'm a proud Ubuntu convert and am realizing its superiority over Windows more and more each day. One such realization involves encoding AVIs.

When archiving old TV episodes, I used to use Nero to create menus, encode, and burn the whole project onto DVD. For a certain TV show with 45- to 60-minute episodes, I normally had to use dual layer DVDs to burn more than 2 episodes onto each medium. Strangely, with AVIDemux 2, I can fit four or more episodes of the same TV show onto one *single layer* DVD.

How does AVIDemux 2, unlike Nero, encode similarly-sized episodes in such a way that the resultant file is so small?

### Post by vanadium on 2007-06-14
Indeed it is a matter of compression, or bitrate of your video and audio streams. You can fit 2 hours of very good quality video on a single layer DVD. Fitting 4 hours is also possible at the expense of video quality (a difference that might not be very significant on a traditional television, but that will be very clear on a flatscreen TV).
