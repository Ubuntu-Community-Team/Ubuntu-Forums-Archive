### Post by FG123 on 2007-11-10
I'd try disabling (mute) any devices in the Gnome Volume Control that you don't need activated. If you're recording from a mic for example, you could mute the Line-in, or vice versa. Also, it's sometimes a good idea not to have the mixer volumes for everything as high as possible. I have the Master at max but the PCM's about 3/4 up; any more PCM and I get a hiss all the time too.

### Post by jpkotta on 2007-11-11
I've had what could be called success with the noise removal.  The output sounded better than the input, anyway.  Make sure you have lots of silence (i.e. just the noise) because it needs to get statistics on the noise.  Maybe 10 seconds every so often.
