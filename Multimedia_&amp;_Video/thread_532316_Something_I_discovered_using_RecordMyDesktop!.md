### Post by onemanwaking on 2007-08-22
Just thought I'd share this discovery with anyone who has experienced this same issue.

I was trying to make a screencast last night on the laptop using RecordMyDesktop, the great little screencasting tool you can find through the Add/Remove utility.

I noticed that when I played back the recordings, the sound and video were sped up and quite choppy, to the point where the video wasn't usable in any way. It puzzled me because earlier that day I had recorded a few screencasts that came out perfectly.

I messed around with the settings but nothing seemed to work. Then I thought about the difference between the two sessions - my laptop was plugged in on the earlier one and running on battery power later on when it was choppy. So I tried plugging back in and voila! It worked like a charm. Super smooth video and audio.

So, obviously it would seem there are power saving features at work or at least the battery is not as capable at sustaining even power to everything the way a 120V current is.

Maybe there's a way to fix the battery setting?

Anyway, hope this detail helps anyone who is as frustrated as I was getting this this to work properly  :)
