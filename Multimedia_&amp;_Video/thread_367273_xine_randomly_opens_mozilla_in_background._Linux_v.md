### Post by hotani on 2007-02-21
Occasionally, when I'm *trying* to have an issue-free session of MythTV (it is becoming more and more difficult lately), Xine will, on random, open Mozilla in the background and go to some oddball page in the xine docs. Maybe there is a reason for this. Maybe someone thought it would be a neat trick, a handy device for one nerd in a million that, for whatever reason, decided that going to the xine docs while watching a video was a Very Good Thing. I am not this nerd. Actually I am a really pissed off user who is sitting on the couch wondering why none of the  buttons on my remote work.

This is a tag-team play, however. Not only is opening a browser I *never* use, then going to a website I did not want to go to (especially while watching a video), the stupidest thing a video player could ever do, it sets off the stolen focus bug in Gnome. That is, when a new window is opened, it steals focus from whatever you were doing so it can have your full, undivided attention. In MythTV, this still happens, but with one minor difference: the window never comes to the front. That's right. All control is sent to the now hidden window while the user stares at the still-playing video wondering why none of the controls on the remote work. Why don't they work? Because they are controlling Mozilla!

I know there are some fans of MythTV out there, and honestly, the interface to Myth is great; I have no problems with that. What does cause me to involuntarily begin to stab my eyes out with a dull, rusted fork is the sorry set of video players that we are forced to use with MythTV. First and foremost, none of the players can agree on what keyboard commands to use for what actions. For some, ESC is quit. For others, it is "q". For some, "f" means fullscreen, for others it is "ctrl+f". Sometimes the spacebar pauses and plays, sometimes it just pauses. Sometimes it doesn't do anything. No two players agree on the basic commands which should be as follows:
**ESC** = quit the frakking program and go back to the MythTV menu
**left arrow** = go back some number of seconds (I'm ok with setting up how many)
**right arrow** = see above, and reverse it
**spacebar** = play/pause. Not just play, not just pause, but play AND pause.

That's it. 4 keys. The essentials of sane remote control configuration. Why am I even typing this up? Why is it even an issue?

The next problem with linux video players, that wouldn't really be a problem if the issue above was resolved, is that no single video player can play every file type. I use xine for most, but then it can't figure out mkv files, so I have to use VLC. Then the whole remote control thing comes back to bite me in the arse. 

Oh, and for the love of all that is holy, don't even get me started on DVD playback. 

Really, I just want to sit down and watch a video, but it is damn near impossible because I have to keep getting up to reconfigure, or force quit something. ](*,)
