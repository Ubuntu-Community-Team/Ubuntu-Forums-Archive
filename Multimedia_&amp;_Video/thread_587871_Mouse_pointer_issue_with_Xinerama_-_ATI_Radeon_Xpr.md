Summary - Mouse pointer lagging and distorting display in Xinerama setup with laptop, LCD screen and ATI Radeon Xpress 200M.

Hi,

I've been trying to get a dual monitor setup working over the last few days and can't work out my problem on my own (researching these forums and googling all over the place).

It started when I upgraded to Gutsy under the illusion (delusion) that Gutsy would make setting up dual monitors easy. Well, what a joke that was...

When I worked out that just wasn't going to happen with the gui, I started learning how to edit xorg.conf manually. Oh, what geeky fun...

Anyway, I learned new words like Xinerama, big desktop,  twinview, dual heads, merged-whatevers.

Xinerama looked good, and I have it set up great except for one niggly thing - my mouse pointer leaves a trail of destruction sometimes across the display. It will warp the pixels under its path and leave copies of itself here and there on the screen. It's hard to explain.

I believe it's got something to do with this...

```
Option		"SWCursor"	"on"
Option		"HWCursor"	"off"
```

...in xorg.conf, which I put there to stop the pointer on my second screen from being a big 100px by 100px messy and unusable square graphic rather than an arrow.

Any ideas on what to try to get a normal cursor/pointer that doesn't lag around and mess things up?
