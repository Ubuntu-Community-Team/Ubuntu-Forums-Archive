### Post by Steve S. on 2007-02-05
Yeah, absolutely soundkonverter (note the "k" rather than a "c."  Soundconverter is not nearly as good, in my opinion).

Also, you can use Audacity to change the quality, pitch, volume, etc.  It is a little grumpy sometimes on Linux, due to the sound-priorities I believe.  I usually have to run it from terminal and have all other sound options turned off (no Amarok or anything else running).
