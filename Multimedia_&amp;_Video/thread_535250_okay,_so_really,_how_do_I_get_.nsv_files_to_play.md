### Post by dmizer on 2007-08-26
well, it uses mp3 for audio so that's why you can hear audio.  but interestingly enough, it uses vp3 (vorbis) for video which is open source.

i couldn't get anything play streaming with my setup, but when i downloaded the video it played fine with vlc, mplayer, totem, as well as xine and anything else i wanted to try it in.

so the problem is getting the thing to recognize that nsv is more than just an audio file when streaming from the internet.  

nullsoft video (nsv) is supported by the mediaplayer connectivity plugin for firefox.  i suggest giving that a shot: [https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/446](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/446)
