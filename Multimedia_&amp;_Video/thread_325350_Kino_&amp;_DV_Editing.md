### Post by Unterseeboot_234 on 2006-12-30
I have to say, I junked my pinnacle card (it was an old one). I worked great in WIN98 but it was constantly halting, backing up the tape to try a second pass and invariably would drop one frame. No biggie. That Pinnacle cost me $450 in 1997. Using Kino, or the terminal program dvgrab, with a 64-bit ubuntu and I am capturing as fast as the tape will play. My Sony is NTSC dv format 1 and so dv1394 is the Quasar-made codec running the capture in Kino. Speed, I have come to learn, is doing it right the first time. I wouldn't ever want to go back to Windows98 or XP except for some sound effects, Adobe After-Effects. Give Kino some time. They mentioned their next phase is to make a multi-channel editor with the sound track info. I find I can really storyboard -- the rough cut -- a video and get it ready for the Windows software to finish off. Can't wait until I never boot Win ever again.
