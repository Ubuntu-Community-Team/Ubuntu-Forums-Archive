### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-04-09
I would suggest acid rip or thoggen. I like acid rip more as it has more options. The both do basically the same thing, DVD to avi. You would set up bitrates for both audio and video, start it and wait. DVD to avi can take some time depending on procesor speed. If you have a modern rig (new within the last two to three years) expect the process to take the time of watching the movie (realtime) or slightly faster. If you are using an older PC, start it up and go to bed/work and check it when you get back. Check the movie by scanning through for quality. The parts you may want to look it action scenes or scenes with lots of movement.

Then if you just want a certain scene you can use avidemux to cut out a certain scene. Assuming you are happy with the quality, you don't need to reencode and can do a direct stream copy.

Is this what you need?

Just keep in mind that almost anywhere you may live, this may be illegal if you don't own the movie and maybe even if you do. Sorry, just wanted to get that legal warning out of the way.
