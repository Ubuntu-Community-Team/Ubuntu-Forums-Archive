### Post by shirilover on 2008-04-02
Unfortunately, you have not provided enough information to effectively solve your problem.
I have played several .mkv files with MPlayer wothout problem.
Some items that may cuase playback problems:
Desktop Effects or compisited desktop -- XVideo rendering and a composited desktop do not play together well. Turn of desktop effects before playing video.
Using an undesirable video output -- I tend to use -vo xv, since it is the least resource intesive. Others you can try are gl, gl2, x11(must use -zoom if you watch in fullscreen).
Not enough CPU power -- If the video codec of the file is H.264, more processing power is required to decode and render the video. Happens quite frequently with high resoultion H.264 video. Also, soft-subs add an additional CPU requirement in order to render them onto the video stream.

Any of the above can result in less than acceptable playing of video.
