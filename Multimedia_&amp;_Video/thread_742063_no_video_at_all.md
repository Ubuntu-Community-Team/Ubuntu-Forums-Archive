### Post by warp99 on 2008-04-01
That command should be:

```
sudo aticonfig --overlay-type=Xv
```

try that, If it does not work then:

```
sudo aticonfig --force --initial
```

that should get you back to square one. Here is some more information on setting up ATI and Ubuntu:

[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Gutsy_Installation_Guide](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Gutsy_Installation_Guide)

Ignore 'Method 2' since that's for those that want to install the driver direct from ATI.

### Post by warp99 on 2008-04-02
It could be the video processing driver that you are using to play the videos. With mplayer or gmplayer (mplayer with a GUI) you can change the video processing driver used to render the video through the settings dialog, unlike with Totem. It could also be a flaw with xine, the video engine for totem, and your particular video card.

The video players have diferent "quirks" with different video formats and video cards. I would suggest that you turn on the "universe" and "multiverse" repositories in Synaptic, that would be under Settings > Repositories, and download the following applications:

mplayer
mplayer-gui
vlc

You may have better results with these players. Also you can change the video processing driver in these players by going either to the settings or preferences dialog and making the changes.
