### Post by brsseb on 2006-05-27
I have a similar problem, with a Dell X1 and a Dell 24" (1920x1200 native). I have flashed the bios using 915resolution, and it starts. In my case, the screeen is slightly offset, with 100pixels to the missing. Also, the image quailty is blurry. 

The 915resolution is called using:

```
915resolution "A" "X" "Y" "BIT"
# like 3c 1680 1200
```

Try using 915resolution -l and pick a different "A"-number, like 3c or 58, etc, whatever values the BIOS lists. I have seen different responses/errors given for picking a different "A" number. Dunno why.

### Post by htmlguy716 on 2006-10-10
On the VX2025wm, 1680x1050 only works at 60 Hz. Try replacing your screen section with this:
```
Section "Screen"
Identifier "Default Screen"
Device "Intel Corporation Mobile 915GM/GMS/910GML Express Graphics Controller"
Monitor "ViewSonic"
DefaultDepth 24
SubSection "Display"
Depth 1
Modes "1680x1050_60" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 4
Modes "1680x1050_60" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 8
Modes "1680x1050_60" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 15
Modes "1680x1050_60" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 16
Modes "1680x1050_60" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 24
# Modes "1680x1050" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
Modes "1680x1050_60"
EndSubSection
EndSection
```
