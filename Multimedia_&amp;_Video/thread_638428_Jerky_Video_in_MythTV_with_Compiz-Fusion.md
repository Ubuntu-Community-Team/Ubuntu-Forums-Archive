### Post by awthomp on 2007-12-12
Hi!

I'm currently running Ubuntu 7.10 with Compiz-Fusion installed (Nvidia 8500GT graphics card).  Anyways, when I go to play videos, recordings, whatever, in MythTV, the video quality seems really jerky.  Horizontal lines appear and the image gets semi-distorted.  When I turn off Compiz through the GLDesktop, however, the jerkiness goes away.

Thanks in advance for any help! :)

---

### Post by acoustibop on 2007-12-12
This is usual.  Always a good idea to disable Compiz for activities like this.

---

### Post by awthomp on 2007-12-12
I was poking around the MythTV settings this morning, and noticed the "Enable Open GL Vertical Sync" option was not checked under the Playback options.  I enabled this option, and the jerkiness was fixed.

For anyone experiencing a similar problem, try this.
