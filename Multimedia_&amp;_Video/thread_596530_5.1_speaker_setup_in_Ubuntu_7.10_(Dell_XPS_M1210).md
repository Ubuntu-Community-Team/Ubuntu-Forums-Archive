### Post by silverguy on 2008-02-07
I used SYSTEM/PREFERENCES/SOUND

just click those from the menus in the top of ubuntu's main screen, and then select (within the mixer at the bottom of defaultmixer trakers) all analogue (Front, side etc)_

Now they are selected , right click the little sound speaker icon in the top right of ubuntu main screen (next to the networking icon) and prefeneces. (Make sure these anologue speakers are selected , by holding ctrl and left clicking) right click the little speaker icon once more, select open volume control.

play some music in the background.

Increase the rear/side/front speakers to maxium to hear the surround sounds!

PS

Most mp3 are stereo sounds, not surround sounds. It worked for me though, by multiplying the stereo into all outputs.

This is what worked for me. hope it helps.
