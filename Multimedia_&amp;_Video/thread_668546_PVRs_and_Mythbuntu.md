### Post by dannyboy79 on 2008-01-15
mythbuntu is awesome! I run it with a PVR-500, it's got 2 built in tv-tuners. when I hook up my coax cable to it, I can view and record channels 3 thru 99 more or less. i know there are some restrictions about recording HD. I only am recording SD and Digital downgraded to SD thru an s-video cable from my SA3250HD set top box from Time Warner Milwaukee. I am using a serial ir blaster to have mythtv change the channels on the STB. SO I actually have a Master Mythtv Backend (Feisty) running mythtv package 20.02-fixes which has a PVR-350 (which has a s-video in port) to capture video from the STB. THen I have a slave mythtv backend running mythbuntu with the PVR-500 in it. So I can record 3 shows at once. Between the 2 mythtv servers there's 1TB of storage.

there's a plugin for mythtv called mytharchive which allows to save any recordings on to a dvd for watching in DVD players. I use a modded xbox along with xbmcmythtv to watch my recordings in my family room. I don't ever watch live tv through mythtv, only use it as a DVR.
