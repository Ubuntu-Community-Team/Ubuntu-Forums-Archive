Well, as far as listening to music/podcasts goes I've used all the following at one time or another and would recommend them all

Amarok
Banshee
Rhythmox
xmms/beep-media-player/xmms2

I currently use Rhythmbox because it looks best in GNOME and I find Amarok's interface confusing, though there is no faulting its (amarok) features. Banshee comes closest to the balance I'd like between features/aesthetics/usability but I don't use it because currently you can't easily browse by Artist/Album. xmms (and it's various forks like beep-media-player and xmms2) is more or less an exact clone of Winamp, you probably know what winamp is so I won't go into that.

For CD ripping there is no beating Sound Juicer. That's not to say that other tools (e.g. grip) aren't good, but Sound Juicer has more active development. At the end of the day a CD ripper can't really compete on features because it only has one.

For tagging I'd recommend easytag. It's not very good looking but it gets the job done and is by far the best when organising large collections.

For music creation take a look at Audacity and Jokosher.
