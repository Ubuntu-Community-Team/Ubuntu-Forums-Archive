### Post by cozmicharlie on 2008-02-12
I noticed this has been up for a week with no response.  I'll answer some:
1.  I am not sure what you mean - do you mean naming the files?  You may be confusing converting with tagging. In SoundKonverter you add the files you want to convert, right click on the file and there is an edit options>tag.  You can add the artist etc. as tags.  You can also use gtkpod to rename files or tag from filenames.      

2. No - you should not need to plug it into Windows first.  

3.  Lots of options other than ipods.  Linux works with most of the major brands.  This site has good info on alternatives to ipod [http://www.anythingbutipod.com/](http://www.anythingbutipod.com/).  Personally I like the ipod.  

Managing the ipod under Ubuntu is easy.  Personally, I installed Rockbox on my ipod so I can play flac.  If all your music is in flac then you will have to convert to another format (even if you are using Windows)- Ipods do not support flac.  However, Rockbox will - then you can add your files as flac and you don't have to convert.  

Good luck
