### Post by forrestcupp on 2008-01-28
I have never gotten Kino to work with anything other than the DV format.  Kino does have great capture ability, but that is about it.  You might try capturing with Kino to DV format and working with your files in [Kdenlive](http://www.kdenlive.org/index.php).  Kdenlive is excellent for video editing.  You could just import your DV files and export them to mov's if that is all you want to do.  Kdenlive is in the repos, so you can install it with apt-get or Synaptic.
