Hello fellow Ubuntu enthusiasts!

I come to you with a problem i have tried to find the solution to for some time.

I own the ATI Radeon X1950 AGP graphics card. Because it's an ATI card, i had my share of problems (Who haven't :) ?

I have been doing some research, and it seems to me that people are getting very mixed results trying to get drivers working for this card. Some are instantly successful, and some fail miserably (like me).

What hit me one night, is the fact that the card comes in 2 flavors. The native PCI-E version, and the AGP version.

Now, the AGP version has a special chip made by ATI that "translates" the signal from PCI-E to AGP (The card only supports PCI-E by default, hence the translation chip).

The chip is codenamed Rialto, and i think it's the source of all the troubles.
