### Post by secretspicy15 on 2007-09-03
Ok, first of all, wait for your collection to build. In the screenshot you showed it's still building the database. Everything will work much smoother once thats done. 

Also, do you have it set up to scan the folders that hold your music? Go to Settings->configue Amarok->Collection and make sure your music folders are checked, and check the boxes for scan folders recursively and watch folders for changes.

once the collection is built (which may take a while, depending on how big your collection is... go make a sandwich) it should be organized alphabetically by Artist, followed by Album. You can expand these out on the collection tab on the main screen, double clicking at any level to play (i.e. double click on The Beatles to play all Beatles music, or expand it out and choose an album, say Abbey road to double click and it will play abbey road, or expand abbey road and just double click on Come Together to just hear that)

If you want all of your music in one big stinkin playlist, which I certainly like to do sometimes, go to the playlists tab on the main screen, expand out SmartPlaylists->Collection and double click on "All Collection" This will put all of the tracks in your collection in the active playlist. Hit play and it will play. if you want everything out of your way, close the amarok window, it will continue to play from the tray. Click the tray icon to open the window again.

I honestly don't know why you would want to get rid of that big vertical bar on the left. That is where everything takes place. It is where you control your collection and select songs to play, and it is where you can get information about what is currently playing (context) If you don't want to look at it, really just close the whole window... You can pop it open again if you want to change something... 

I hope that was helpful...
