### Post by handy on 2006-08-27
I am running an nVidia 6800GT, 256Mb RAM, agp card.

I use [Automatix]("http://getautomatix.com/") to install the proprietary nVidia drivers because it is so easy that way, apart from all of the other wonderful things that Automatix, makes easy... :D 

I run Guild Wars via Cedega, A LOT, & it works beautifully.

Unreal Tournament 2004, installs natively on linux, & runs faster & the graphics quality is at least as good as it was, on the same hardware, when I ran it under windoze.

It's allways best to buy computer hardware that's **not** cutting edge.  It's much cheaper & better supported.  If you are only going to be playing on linux, you won't be able to play directX 10, games for a long time.  Epic & ID, usually make native installs of the Unreal, Quake & Doom engine games that run OpenGL to the max'!  In fact they may even develope on Linux... :KS

**[Edit:]** *I should mention that I run Ubuntu at 1600x1200 24bit, & play GW & UT2k4 at that res' with everything turned up to the max' in UT2k4, & not far off max in GW.*
