### Post by cmspaz on 2007-08-13
So, I finally got around to upgrading the hardware in my server/entertainment box, but not wanting to deal with the drivers for the integrated UniChrome Pro on the new mobo, I decided to throw my old TNT2 Riva M64 in, since the NVidia drivers are pretty straightforward.

I installed the legacy drivers, and they've worked beautifully, as expected (after enabling EDID detection). However, as of moving back to school, I'm again being forced to use my HDTV as a monitor.

My TV apparently doesn't return an EDID, as I've had this problem before with other boxes and OS's (Windows included) where it cannot detect the panel's supported resolution. I know this is where the issue lies because in the past week at home, I had this box plugged into an LCD monitor and a different HDTV, both of which were autodetected and subsequently driven at their respective native resolutions.

I'm currently stuck at 800x600, and have tried adding 1280x720 (the TV's native res) to the mode lines, adding a modline, and manually specifying the panel's refresh rates. So far, nothing has worked.

Any ideas? You help is greatly appreciated.
