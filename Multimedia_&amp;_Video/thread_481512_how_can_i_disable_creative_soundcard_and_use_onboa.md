### Post by Chappy7777 on 2007-06-25
I guess it depends on what you consider "too hard". If you can only live with using the Onboard for Ubnutu and the SoundCard for Windows, all you have to do is remove the card when you are using Ubuntu and set your BIOS to enable onboard sound. Ubuntu will most likely find the onboard sound. 

When you want to use Windows, reinstall the card, physically, and go into the BIOS and disable the Onboard sound. If you didn't remove the soundcard from your PC using XP's add/remove hardware, then it should work fine. 

Of course you'll have to go thru this process everytime you change your OS. To me, this sounds too hard. 

I am using the onboard sound for both Ubuntu and XP, and am having no hassles at all when I change systems.
What is it about the hardware soundcard you have to have for Windows?
