### Post by Redeyes_Gambit on 2007-01-11
Well I don't actually own any of the above players so you will have to take this advice with a grain of salt :D . It seems to me that of the three you mentioned the ipod seems to be the best choice.

From some initial web searches it appears the Zen 5 doesn't work on Ubuntu ( [http://forums.creative.com/creativelabs/board/message?board.id=dap&message.id=169613](http://forums.creative.com/creativelabs/board/message?board.id=dap&message.id=169613) ) or it would at least take some seriouse tweaking to get it to work ( [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=199250](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=199250) ).

The Zune would also take some pretty deep tweaking to get it to work and I can't seem to find anything on the net about getting a Zune working in Linux.

The ipod, being somewhat standard, will probably be the best candidate. However, you must remember that itunes will not work without cross-over office 3.1+ ( [http://news.com.com/Startup+to+make+iTunes+sing+on+Linux/2100-1041_3-5293915.html](http://news.com.com/Startup+to+make+iTunes+sing+on+Linux/2100-1041_3-5293915.html) ) which will add to the cost. I'm sure someone else around here has an ipod and can give more detailed feedback on how well it works in Ubuntu.

As for the VMWare solution, I think that you could use it to sync/buy songs just as if you had XP running normally. I have played around with VMWare server in the past and getting USB devices to work doesn't seem too hard. I have noticed, however, that sound in a VMWare environment is extremely slooowww and stuttttttttery so playback inside a VWMwared XP may be a problem. I have a 1.5 ghz celeron m proc and was running win 98se vmwared when I tried playing some music. With a more powerful proc you might be able to get smooth audio playback.

Some other options that /should/ work would be some no-name mp3 players that act as external HDDs:

### Post by tpg on 2007-01-11
I've got an iPod Photo, although I use the [Rockbox]("http://www.rockbox.org/") firmware replacement, which is excellent, for example because you can play many more file formats (e.g. OGG, FLAC) than you can with the Apple firmware. I don't think the most recent iPods work as well, but rockbox works on a lot of players (see the website). 

Integration with Linux for a player running Rockbox is simple and reliable, as all you have to do is drag-and-drop your music onto the device!
