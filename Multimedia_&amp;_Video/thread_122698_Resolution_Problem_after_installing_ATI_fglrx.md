### Post by JAwuku on 2006-01-28
Try the command ```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```
to reset the video resolutions. I don't think 1280x800 is a standard resolution; try 1280x1024.

If you dig out your monitor manual (or failing that, go to the support pages on the manufacturer's website), you can enter the horizontal and vertical refresh frequencies manually to better utilize your monitor's refresh capabilities.

### Post by NunoCorreia on 2006-01-29
No, that's not the problem; 1280x800 should work fine (it's the standard resolution on many 15.4" laptops) anyway.

Actually, there's a bug in the 8.16.20 version of the ATI drivers which causes modes greater than 1024x768 to be inaccessible. This is fixed on versions 8.18 and up.
