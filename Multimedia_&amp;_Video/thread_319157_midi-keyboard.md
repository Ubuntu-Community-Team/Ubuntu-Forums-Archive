### Post by falkenberg_cph on 2006-12-15
just wanna second. I have the m-audio as well. Very basic, but it works well. 
But if i were to choose another kind of midi keyboard today, i would definitly go for something with some tweakable knobs, which makes synth control so much easier,
I dont know what kind of music you're playing, but if you make synth lines that needs tweaking, and will not be using the board so much for "piano" playing, i would choose something like M-Audio 02, which has fewer keys, but has some knobs instead. Its almost the same price.

I really miss live tweaking

Hi...if you will be dualbooting windows, consider Emu Xboard49/61,  for the
included relabeled Proteus 2000 soundset is great,  and easy to modify,  plus lots of
realtime knobs for Friday night! It is also a standalone controller for external rackmount
or hardware synthkeyboards. If no windows, test its usb connection on your setup to make
sure it works...(wise, to physically test, regardless of choice, linux being the beast it is)
but the keys and knobs get nice reviews in mags/forums...

The earlier reference from an educator should be considered, classrooms being
torture test labs by nature, maybe they can verify what distro and kernel are working
in the classroom setup? and if its a usb,  or midi 5pin din cables...

Now if a complete spectrum  of sound is a more priority than realtime controls,  I'd say get
a used yamaha 88 key model that has velocity sensing keys...should be some in your
price range...check ebay...(bonus of nice yamaha XG soundset!) Try arpeggio 34 (Sequence 2) in zynaddsubfx with
some massive speakers, and a city grid sized power amp,  and you'll know you -need-
all 88 keys!
