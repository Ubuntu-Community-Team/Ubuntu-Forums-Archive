### Post by CameO73 on 2008-04-03
I'm currently using the Bose Companion 5 speakers in Ubuntu (7.10 and 8.04) and they're working perfectly! I had to do some tweaking (for Alsa sound in 7.10 see [this thread](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=339071&highlight=bose+companion), for Hardy I choose to use PulseAudio in Preferences -> Sound), but it's been working fine from the start.

Some programs (e.g. Totem Movie Player) offer the 5.1 sound setup (which the Companion 5 simulates with the 2.1 speaker setup), but there is no global setting like in Windows. The speakers sound great (IMO) for movies and music playback. And the remote control thingy is very easy to control (and stylish). 

In short: if you have the money and want a nice surround setup without all the cables and extra speakers, I can really recommend this! (although a real surround system would of course give you more of a surround sound effect).
