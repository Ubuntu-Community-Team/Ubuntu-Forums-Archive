### Post by raycosm on 2008-03-30
Well I turn on my computer this morning, and the problem seems to have solved itself. But for reference, or if you just don't want to restart your computer to get a script running, your Amarok scripts are located in ~/.kde/share/apps/amarok/scripts. If you right click the script that needs to be executed, you can enable executing the file under Permissions.
