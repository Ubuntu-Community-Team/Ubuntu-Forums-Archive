### Post by philidox on 2007-11-26
Well I finally did it, I made my own ringtones and music for my mobile phone but before you try this PLEASE READ the Prerequisiets and Disclaimer.
Disclaimer:  This is the method I used on MY PHONE(Samsung SGH-T519) if you have anything else besides this make and model, I simply just do not know.  Hopefully it will guide you in the right direction.

Prerequisiets:  You'll need THREE pieces of HARDWARE to do it my way.
1. A computer with Ubuntu
2. A mobile phone WITH A MICRO SD CARD inside your mobile phone
3. A connecting usb cable OR bluetooth connection.(One end plugs into you phone and the other plugs into to you computer via usb).  If your good at the whole bluetooth transfer, then by all means use it but i used the cables. In other words, a means of transfering the data from your computer to your mobile phone.

If you do not have it already, Install a program called "Sound Converter".  You can do so by going to "Applications">>>"Add/Remove...".  Type "Sound Converter" in the search bar and it should pop right up. Check the little box off to the left and then click on "Apply Changes" in the bottom right hand corner. 
newb notes: Make sure you install "Sound Converter" and not something else like "soundKonverter".

Go to "Applications">>>"Sound & Video">>>"Sound Converter".  When "Sound Converter starts up, go to "Edit">>>"Preferences".  Under where it says "Type of result?" select mp3.  Then set the "Bitrate mode:" to **Constant (CBR)** AND the "Quality:" **Very Low**.  At the bottom it should say "Target bitrate: 64 kbps". Then click close

Now that the settings have been made for Sound Converter all you have to do is click "Add File".  Find the file you want to convert into a ringtone/music. Don't be shy here,  Sound Converter is awesome at this, I add mp3's, ogg's, wav's, mp4's, wmv's, wma's(yes it will convert video files into ringtone ready format!!!).  Once you done adding files,  simply click on the "Convert" button and presto!

Transfer those newly made mp3's on to the mobile phone.  Like I said earlier, I used usb connecting cables so when I plugged up my mobile phone to my computer a hard drive popped up on my desktop and I copy the mp3's to my mobile phone that way.  I put all my mp3's into the "Sounds" folder.  You must have a micro sd card at this point because the hard drive that appears on your desktop will be you micro sd NOT your phones hard drive.  If you know how to use bluetooth to transfer your mp3's be my guest.

Once your done transferring, unmount your mobile phone from ubunutu and disconnect the cables from your computer.  At this point your done with your computer.  Go into your phone's memory card and it will give you the option set it as your ringtone, or add the mp3's as a music playist.  For me, I went to "Menu">>>"Fun & Apps"(or press 6)>>>"My Files(or press 1)">>>"Memory Card".  Like said earlier I put my mp3's into the "Sounds" folder, so it only makes sense that  I went into the Sounds folder.  All you have to do now is hit "Options" and do whatever you'd like.
