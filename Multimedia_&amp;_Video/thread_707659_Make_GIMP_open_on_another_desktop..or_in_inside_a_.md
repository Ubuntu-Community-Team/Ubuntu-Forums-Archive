### Post by julian67 on 2008-02-25
Here's what I do, though it's more suitable for doing a session of editing in Gimp. I have 2 workspaces. In one workspace I have F-Spot and in the other Gimp already opened. When I select an image in F-Spot to edit in Gimp I click to open it with Gimp and immediately switch to the Gimp desktop. The image always opens in the active desktop. 

I believe that other window managers are more configurable in this way than Metacity (the Gnome default) and xfwm (Xfce default which is what I use). There are some tools to help you configure your applications to always open in a particular workspace at a particular size etc, here's a link to an article that might point you in the right direction: [Take charge of your window manager with WMCTRL and Devil's Pie]("http://www.linux.com/feature/122471")

### Post by Support the 2nd on 2008-02-25
I figured it out.  :)  I am in KDE so this might not apply to gnome users.  Right clicking the title bar of a window offers a selection of options from affecting that specific window, or the whole application.  The configure option allows you to select a desktop to "force" it to.  It works great.  If I open a picture with GIMP on desktop 1, now it goes to desktop 3.  No questions asked.

Now I have a dedicated GIMP desktop without anything else.  A nice simple grey desktop is my makeshift "window" for now.

I am still interested if I can make GIMP run inside a window if anyone knows.  I am liking linux more and more.  :)
