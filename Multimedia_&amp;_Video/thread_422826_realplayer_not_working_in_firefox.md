### Post by muncrief on 2007-04-25
Please see my post on this forum at this link - [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=422255](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=422255)

It will guide you through the creation of an alternate Ubuntu RealPlayer package that would probably help almost everyone with RealPlayer plugin problems. Unfortunately, it got buried pretty fast.

And I must say, I'm still shocked at how Linux developers seem blind to the fact that Linux will never be adopted by the masses until users can browse multimedia content on the Internet reliably, with full fast fast forward/rewind/pause/play stream controls. In fact, Linux has made incredible strides in everything except for the number one thing people notice when they try to use it, they can't browse the Internet!.

I can't tell you how many times people have called our emailed me, truly confused, about why they couldn't watch news clips or other videos online with Linux. And I can't tell you how many times they were shocked when I told them that it was one of the major problems with Linux. Normal users are just astounded and simply go right back to Windows.

Fellow community members, it doesn't have to, and shouldn't be, this way. It would only take a concerted and coordinated effort of about six months to bring full multimedia capability to Linux browsers, but I've been saying that for about six years now and not one Linux developer has listened.

Oh well, as a user I'm trying to make things work as best I can without trying to rip apart the Xine, MPlayer, or Totem code myself. Sheesh. Over a decade and still no fast forward or rewind. And yet the graphics developers managed to produce Beryl, which is far more complex and astounding. What have the plugin developers been doing all that time? All the codecs they need have been around for years now.
