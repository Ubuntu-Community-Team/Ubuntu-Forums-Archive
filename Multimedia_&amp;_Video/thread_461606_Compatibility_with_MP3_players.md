### Post by daschmidty on 2007-06-02
Many of the lower end mp3 players will simply show up as flash drives so you can just mount them as filesystems. I personally own a creative zen vision, which is a tad more tricky as it uses mtp, and cannot simply be mounted (unless you compile mtpfs, but that is a little more involved). At any rate the feisty version of amarok has mtp support compiled in so you can transfer from there, and there are also programs like gnomad2, which are solely for transfer and syncing to mtp devices. From what i have heard, ipods can be tricky, but there are plenty of howto's out there. I have not heard of any mp3 player that does NOT work, so really you should make your decision based on all factors including sound quality etc, linux compatability will likely not be an issue.

### Post by uputer on 2007-06-02
I suggest going to anythingbutipod.com to read up on mp3 players.   The people there discuss comparisons of players and quite a few people have more than one which is very helpful, imho.   I am steering clear of the Creative as I don't want the hassle of trying to get it to work.   I am considering Cowon, Archos and Samsung players.
