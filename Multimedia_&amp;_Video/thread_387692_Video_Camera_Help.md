### Post by chewearn on 2007-03-19
Apologies first: I don't own this particular videocam, but I have a JVC harddisk videocam.  So my suggestion might not apply.

I check your videocam spec, it comes with a usb port; does it appeared as a usb drive when your plug-in the usb port?  Can you copy the mpeg2 files to your local harddrive?

In my case, I don't use the stock application that comes with the videocam: it is very buggy (crash all the time), and works only in Windows.  But I am able to copy the video files via usb.  For some reason, the JVC files are named as *.MOD; but I have found out that they are simply MPEG2; just renamed to extension to .MPG and I can play the files in ubuntu, and import into Avidemux for processing.
