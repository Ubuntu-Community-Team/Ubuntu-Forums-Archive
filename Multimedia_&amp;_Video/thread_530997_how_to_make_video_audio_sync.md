### Post by WakkiTabakki on 2007-08-21
The easiest way (I think) is to use the VLC-player and adjust the synch between video and audio. It doesn't fix the problem in the file (if there is one, could just be the codec used), but it'll play it back in synch...
Install VLC from the "Add/remove..." under the Applications Menu.

Once VLC is installed and you've opened the video, open "Preferences" under the "Settings" menu, choose "Audio" in the left panel and ensure the checkbox "Advanced options" is ticked.
Then, adjust the value in "Audio desynchronisation compensation" to get the audio in synch (negative value makes audio play earlier, I think)...

I believe MPlayer has the same feature, too bad it's not available in Totem...

Good luck
