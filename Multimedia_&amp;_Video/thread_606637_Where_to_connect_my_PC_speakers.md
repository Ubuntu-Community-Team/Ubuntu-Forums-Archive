### Post by Schalken on 2007-11-08
I would doubt any sound will come out of your sound card at all, and you will hence have to plug your PC speakers into the jack on the motherboard. If sound does come out of the sound card somehow, or you find drivers for the card, good, but I wouldn't count on it.

Also, "Standard personal computer (...)" is the 32bit version, which works on any x86-based processor such as from Intel or AMD, while the "64bit AMD and Intel computers" is only the 64bit version.
