### Post by dando80 on 2007-01-28
Hi Theimon,

I don't see what the problem is but I can say that I have had no end of trouble in the past using the nvidia driver through apt. It may seem to be the way to go, due to easy updates and upgrades etc, but I have had much less trouble using the Nvidia driver installer direct from the Nvidia website.

I would be inclined to say to remove every Nvidia related package from your system (apart from the open source nv driver of course), download the Nvidia driver installer from Nvidia driver page and install it that way. So long as you have your kernel headers installed and your compiler set up ok, it should be a breeze to install. If you get any complaints about header files such as version.h being missing, just copy or link the files to the location that the installer expects them.

I have far fewer problems these days. The driver just works. On the rare occasion that I have any problems with it I simply do sh NVxxxx.sh --uninstall and then reinstall it. So it might be a good idea to keep the driver installer on your system after you install it.
