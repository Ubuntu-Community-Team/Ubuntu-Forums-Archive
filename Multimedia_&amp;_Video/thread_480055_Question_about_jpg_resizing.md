### Post by silverglade00 on 2007-06-21
The short version:

Is there a fast and easy way to resize an entire folder of .jpgs to a specific size?

The long version:

I have a digital camera that takes pictures at 2816x2112 and 3MB apiece. I would like to reduce them to about a quarter of the size for use on my website, which has very limited HD space. Is there a fast and/or easy way to resize them all if they are all in a folder? 

Thanks!

Edit: The camera does not have built in capabilities for this.

### Post by reclusivemonkey on 2007-06-21
Yes! You can use ImageMagick, which is specifically designed for this sort of thing. Unfortunately there is no GUI for it as far as I know.

[http://www.imagemagick.org/script/index.php](http://www.imagemagick.org/script/index.php)

The examples here should be enough to get you going;

[http://www.imagemagick.org/script/command-line-processing.php](http://www.imagemagick.org/script/command-line-processing.php)

If you get stuck, just post back.

### Post by silverglade00 on 2007-06-21
That was exactly what I was looking for. After a short trip to Synaptic, I used the examples on the second link and I only had to change the resolution to what I wanted and it was done in about 10 seconds. Thanks!!! :)
