### Post by wyth on 2007-06-23
I actually like the way Amarok manages podcasts better than iTunes.  There's more fine-grain control.  You can set up folders for categories (I have some podcasts that are for news, some for tech, etc., and they're all in their respective folder trees), and you can update one category alone.  You can also right-click on any single podcast and determine where all downloads will be saved.  If you want to get creative, you can save all your geeky lectures to a separate partition, and all your cool stuff to a shared external drive, or wherever you want.  iTunes never let me save each podcast to its own location.

The one thing Amarok doesn't have yet is a way to determine the overall podcast directory off the bat (although I'm sure there's a tweak in some config file).  But this is no big deal; add your 20 feeds, don't download anything yet, right-click on the top folder and set all the children to a certain directory.  Exaile is trying to become a GTK alternative to Amarok, but none of the GTK music managers have that kind of fine-grain control.  

I'm looking forward to some better MTP support.  I just got an mp3 player that was *supposed* to be firmware upgradable to a UMS device, but the week I got it that support flew out the window.  Amarok can handle MTP, as can the svn version of Rhythmbox and Gnomad2, but neither all that well.  In Amarok, certain characters in the tag info (umlauts, etc), won't let the file transfer, and can make the whole process crashtastic.  When I tried with Gnomad2, it just left the tag info out.  And I couldn't get the svn Rhythmbox to keep from crashing long enough to try it. 

And has anyone noticed that Rhythmbox only downloads one podcast at a time?  What's up with that, and can you fix it?  It's just another thing keeping me on Amarok.
