> 
1. The speakers on my TV are crappy. I have an extra set of nice computer speakers that I would like to use. I know I will have to get a TV tuner card for mythbuntu. Will I be able to use that card to also hook up my computer speakers to my TV through the mythbuntu computer.


 If your TV speakers are crap then use speakers connected to your computer ;-)

 The "sound" from the tv card comes out your normal audio out jacks on your computer.

> 
2. Is mythbuntu for watching TV on your computer, or for acting as a DVR for your TV set? Do I need a computer monitor for my mythbuntu machine or will the TV set essentially be the machines monitor?


 Yes, you can watch TV on your computer using MythBuntu, a tv-tuner card, and a working input source (either unscrambled antenna cable and/or a/v out from your set top box to the a/v in on the tv-tuner card).

 In addition, while watching LiveTV MythTV allows you to pause/ff/rwd the show (pretty kewl).

 If your video card has additional outputs to connect to a TV you can use only the TV screen to see what your computer is doing. If you want dual screens, a TV and  a monitor, you can do that, but it's not necessary.

 Video cards using the nVidia chipset tend to work great for this in MythBuntu. I am running one machine that outputs to a 26" HD LCD TV via component outputs. Another machine outputs video to a 32" CRT TV via SVideo out, and a 3rd machine outputs video to a 20" CRT using composite video out. I don't use a computer monitor on any of my 3 setups, I only use the TV screens.

> 
3. How exactly does mythbuntu work anyway? Once it is up and running, does it function similar to TiVO or what?

 I think to know more about that you should take a look at the [MythTV ]("http://mythtv.org/")web site and do some reading. What is does and how it works is better understood there.

 But basically, once it is running, you decide if you want to watch live tv, watch videos, listen to music, view photos. The scheduler will allow you to easily record your favourites. The scheduler is probably one of the nicest features! You can tell it to record this showing only, record one showing daily (for soaps), record one showing weekly (for weeky series), heck, you can even type in a program name and tell it to record whenever that show appears (for example, a christmas special you don't want to miss but don't know when it comes on).

 And from there it can auto flag and auto skip commercial breaks when you play it back ;-)

 The MythWeb page, a local web page delivered off the MythTV backend, is great and gives you another method of interacting with the guide, scheduling, updating, etc. from any computer on your network. 

 If you don't already have a tv-tuner card, make sure you research what cards will do the job. Standard Definition is easy with a hauupage pvr-150. It gets more complicated with High Definition. Everything I'm doing is SD and I am having good results on the HD TV sending out the picture at 720p.

 There are tons more features, but like I said, have a look at the MythTV web site for complete details.
