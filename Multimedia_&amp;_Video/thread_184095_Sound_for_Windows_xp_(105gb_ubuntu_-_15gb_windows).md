### Post by jgoguen on 2006-05-29
First, I suggest that you're in the wrong place for detailed Windows help :)

Have you installed the drivers for your sound and graphic card?  Windows isn't like Ubuntu (or virtually every other Linux distro), you actually have to install drivers for sound and video or you'll get, at best, just basic operation.  Is your sound built into your motherboard?  If so, installing all the drivers for your motherboard (on the CD that came with it, hopefully you kept that otherwise you'll have to start looking on the ASUS website and good luck with that :) ) should give you your sound back.  Then you can go into Control Panel -> Sound and Audio and do whatever you need to do there.  For your video card, it too came with a CD that if you have you can install those drivers and if you don't have it you're going to have to look online.


As for it being slow, it won't matter if you have it installed on 40GB or not, what matters there (most) is your processor and installed RAM.  For me, Athelon64 3200+ and 2GB RAM, Windows XP ran fairly quickly after I installed all the drivers I needed...and did all the updates...and updated again...
