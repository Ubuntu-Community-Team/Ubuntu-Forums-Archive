### Post by mjfleck2000 on 2008-02-03
Several months back I used a spare box to set up Mythtv.  My main goal was to be able to record some TV (actually Cable).

I used one of the recommended Hauppage tuners.  Mythtv installed no problem, recognized the card.  
It worded very well.  I did sign up for the 1 week TV schedule test.  I remember that I did have a problem on my first try to use the scheduling but got it to work within minutes.  I don't remember what the problem was... I think it was the login format. 

Anyway, It works quite well for recording SG-1 for me so that I can view it later.  Or to record a football game so I can watch it again and again (if my team wins).  

I have also used Mythtv to rip my DVDs to Mythtv.  So I have my Harry Potter DVDs, my Horatio Hornblower DVDs , etc available to be  viewed in Mythtv.

Then I added Mythtv frontend to my wife's computer (also running Ubuntu Gutsy).  So, from her computer downstairs, she can view live TV, or any of the TV recordings  or any of the DVD movies that I have ripped.   Works very well.

I added Mythtv-frontend to MY personal computer (Ubuntu Gutsy).  I can watch TV, TV recordings, DVDs from my Computer.  


I have now started to rip some of my music CDs into Mythtv so that they can be shared just like the TV or DVDs.  

This all worked rather well.  The CD music section is the least advanced.  I have not explored that section that much, but making a play list of songs did not seem very straight forward.  

So, from my limited experience,  Mythtv seems that it certainly can fulfill your requirements for a PVR.  

Hope this was helpful.
