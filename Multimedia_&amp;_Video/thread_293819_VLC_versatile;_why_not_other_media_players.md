### Post by jonrkc on 2006-11-05
I'm not up to speed on multimedia by any means; I play DVD's and MP3's and am now experimenting with a cheap but fun little DXG video camera; that's about it. Someday I think I might enjoy video editing and use of a good camera.  I take it the kind to get would be a Mini-DV camera, though I prefer flash memory (a 1 GB SD card yields almost two hours' of 380x640 video).

I've found with video media that VLC will often play what Kaffeine, KMPlayer, and GXine will not be able to.  As far as I know, all the codex's are installed that they need.  Only VLC will play the ASF files that my DXG video camera produces.  (I just found out today that ASF seems to be the most detested file format in the world, and for good reason.)  It will even convert the ASF files into a number of other formats, some open-source and GPL licensed, unlike MPEG's and ASF itself.  

Why aren't Kaffeine, GXine, KMPlayer, and a couple others I tried and discarded, able to play ASF files?  I can understand them not being technical enough to convert and stream and do all the 1001 things VLC can do, but it seems they should be able to play a common, if hated, file format as long as the codex's are there.  I checked and as far as I can tell GXine has all the proprietary codex's it needs.  I'd imagine the same is true for Kaffeine.  When Kaffeine fails, it remarks that it's unable to open the rng image file.  Research on this left me totally in the dark.

If anybody reading this wonders why I didn't mention XMMS, it's because though it was good when I first started with Mandrake 8.0, it's become almost totally unusable for me now under Ubuntu 6.06, and under later releases of Mandrake (Mandriva).  So I gave up on it.

As for MPlayer itself--I could never even get it to function in any way.
