### Post by ltmon on 2006-09-18
1. I guess it's a GUI issue.  Totem uses the "Xine" backend, which is good sometimes also.  I tend to use one or the other depending on which video I'm viewing... xine is best for some and mplayer works better for others.  It's a sorry state of affairs, but it's mainly caused by proprietary codecs.

2. Yes.  In some countries it's illegal, in others it's a grey area in others there is no impediment.  The second reason is that the Ubuntu philosophy is to be Free (free as in 'freedom').  By adding non-Free components by default they would be going against this philosphy.  Read more at: [http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/philosophy](http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/philosophy)

3. See 1... they are just different and sometimes one works better than the other.
