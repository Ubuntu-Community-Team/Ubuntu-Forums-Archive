### Post by joft on 2006-02-14
.bin is NOT a video format. It's an CD image format. mplayer supports opening such files, if it's an image of a (S)VCD. mplayer extracts the corresponding MPEG1/2 data and displays it.

The codecs mplayer uses are codecs for video/audio/media-container formats. So, I think, it won't help coping them over to xine in any way.

AFAIK mplayer is the only video player application, which can open .bin files as if they were (direct) video files.
