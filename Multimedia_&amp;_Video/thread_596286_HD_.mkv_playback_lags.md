### Post by XVampireX on 2007-11-05
There's a few things you gotta do to get HD working nicely on your Linux system:

1. Make sure you got a good and fast video card.
2. Make sure you got a good and fast video card driver...
3. Make sure the player you use doesn't have any problems with HD, mplayer (And its frontends) and kaffeine should be fine, if you're in doubt, try VLC ([www.videolan.org](www.videolan.org))
4. Make sure you have a good CPU too, 2.4ghz should be enough although more is better...
5. Make sure you use a good video output driver such as xv or shm. Also I've heard about something called xvMC....
6. Compiz shouldn't cause any problems with video (Unless your PC is really weak), so you're suggested to review the above suggested points...

Take all of this with a grain of salt, HD works excellent for me for a reason (PC)
