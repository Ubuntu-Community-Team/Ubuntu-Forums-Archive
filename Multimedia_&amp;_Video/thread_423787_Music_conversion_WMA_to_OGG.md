### Post by bllambert on 2007-04-26
I am having a little trouble with my music library in Ubuntu.  I have a lot of music that was encoded in unprotected wma format.  I have no problem getting ubuntu to recognize the files, or even play them.  My problem is getting a music player to recognize the meta-data correctly.  I have tried just about everything with limited success.

Rhythmbox & Exaile! - recognized the artist, albums, and song names, but cant seem to find the track numbers
Amarok - recognizes everything, but cuts off the first and last 5 seconds of every song.
Songbird - only recognizes the metadata on non-wma tracks.

I know that converting from a lossy format to another lossy format is not recommended, but it may be worth it.  I want to convert my library to OGG.  I downloaded the soundconverter from the repos, and it seemed to work fine.  When I attemped to play my new OGG files, the tempo was all screwed up.  The songs would speed up and then slow down at random.  

So I feel a little stuck, and hope that a wiser linux user has tackled these problems
