### Post by gitfiddler on 2007-05-23
I don't know why there are 2 sizes, but when the computer is starting, the actual size of your DDR RAM should be displayed (briefly, so read fast!).

I'm guessing that since your video RAM is low, your system RAM is also "low" by todays standards. How much system RAM  do you have?

I can think of 4 things that affect video playback: CPU clock speed, system RAM, video RAM and proper video drivers.

I run a 1.3 GHz AMD processor with a  NVidia video card (64 Mb RAM) and the NVidia driver, and get good video playback results. I also have a 1.6 GHz AMD system with a cheap SiS video chipset, 128 Mb of RAM and DVD's simply won't play. So from this I gather that a faster CPU and more video RAM do not necessarily result in better video performance. Instead, a quality video card with proper drivers are more likely to yield higher performance.

At least, that's been my experience.
