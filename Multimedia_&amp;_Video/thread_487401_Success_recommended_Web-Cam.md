Ok folks, I've been shopping for a web-cam, and one problem I found is that people usually ask "which webcam works out of the box"? Usually, there is no answer to the question. Since I found an excellent, inexpensive camera that works out of the box, including sound, I thought I should let others know (A post like this would have saved me a week of research).

This is the **LOGITECH QUICKCAM COMMUNICATE STX WEB CAM**. The part number is: **961410-0403**

The image quality is superb. Sound quality pretty good. I am using amsn for video through msn with windoze users, and skype for audio. 

Hope this helps ... ** you **.

Yes, these are valid concerns. But let me point something out. There are two stages to get a camera working: the "driver" and the "application". The former, meaning that the linux kernel needs to detect and talk to the camera. What was meant in this thread was just that: a camera that is recognized without any tweaking/driver hunting/guru level configuration/etc. In the case of the camera I recommended, the sound was also properly recognized right away. That's awsome.

Video applications talk to the camera through an interface (v4l), without caring which camera in particular is plugged to the system. So, the problems (driver and application) are de-coupled. That's good design. As for good video applications, I agree. We need a killer app. Too bad Skype didn't release video for linux. Skype's sound is so, so good. I know, it is a binary application, not free as in freedom. But it beats everything else, hands down.  Anyway, I think the killer video app will come from a big gun: skype (ebay), google, etc. We''ll see :-) (intended pun)
