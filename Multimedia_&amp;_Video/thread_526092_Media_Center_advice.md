Hi! This is such a fun thing to do -- I'm running MythTV on Edgy, and it's already turned a few of my friends on to Linux.

As the previous poster said, MythTV does all these things pretty well. I can't stand the music player, so I use MPD instead, usually controlled by a Sonata client on my laptop. That way I don't have to turn on the TV to hear music. Also, MPD is lightning quick at searching a database of about 19,000 tracks.

For NES emulation, check out Mednafen. It's easy enough to set up -- just press F3 to configure controller buttons. I use Mednafen and Zsnes from the MythGame launcher, but you may find an even more elegant way.

I chose my PS2-USB adapter by checking the hardware compatibility list at Stepmania.com, then Googling around for Linux-specific reports. Unfortunately it looks like yours is listed as "compatible," so that might not be much help. [http://www.stepmania.com/wiki/Xbox_to_USB_Adapters](http://www.stepmania.com/wiki/Xbox_to_USB_Adapters)

Good luck!
