### Post by Ek0nomik on 2007-06-10
MP3 files are actually already compressed.  You can bundle them into a tar.gz easy enough if you want one simple file to transfer that will hold all the files, but it won't save you any space.

### Post by Chappy7777 on 2007-06-10
I don't know off the top of my head the program you'd need to do this, but they're out there. Do a Google. All you need is a program that will allow you to change the bitrate of the mp3 files. Most mp3s I see online are 128bits, you could cut this in half, but you'd lose sound quality. If you cut them to 96bits that is not too bad. 

It's not the best sound, but it will make them smaller. As Ek0nomik said, mp3s are compressed already, so you can't really compress them again. If you do, they'll really sound like do-do.
