### Post by qamelian on 2007-10-03
You can't convert midi to MP3 because midi contains no sound data, only instructions for how midi instruments should play the sounds. However, most midi sequencers, like Muse or Rosegarden will allow you to render the audio created by assigning sounds as samples or virtual instruments (or real instruments), as well as any effects, as wave, flac, mp3, etc. This is really over-simplified but hopefully helps point you in the right direction.

### Post by qamelian on 2007-10-03
You need to use a sequencer, like Muse or Rosegarden, as I already mentioned in my previous post. You need to import the midi file into the sequencing software, assign appropriate sounds/instruments to each track in the song as well as apply whatever effects are necessary. The process is going to vary somewhat from one application to the next. 

The quick and dirty method, I suppose would be to just connect the audio output on your soundcard to the line-in jack, set you recording source to line-in, and record the sound as you play it in timidity or whatever midi player you are using. The down-side of this is that some cards generate a lot of audio artifacts or feedback when you do this. 

To do it up right, you really need to learn how to use a midi/audio sequencing program.
