### Post by sleepingdragon on 2008-03-15
I'd be concerned if what you plan would be admissible as evidence, it would easily be argued that the audio was tampered with. You would be better off handing it over to an A/V forensic team who are accredited by the courts to handle such material.

However, for your own interests, you could run the .avi through something like Avidemux, separate the audio, then run the audio through Audacity with it's myriad of filters to clean the sound. You could even import this new audio back into Avidemux along with the original video to get a new .avi with the enhanced sound.
