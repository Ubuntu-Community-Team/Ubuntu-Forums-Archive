### Post by mc_h8r on 2008-03-20
I hate to say it, but I returned the ATI Radeon 7000 card I bought because of the lack of support in Ubuntu. I have an old work machine (Compaq DeskPro) with insufficient onboard video support to watch YouTube videos, let alone gaming. I didn't want to spend much (if any) money on the machine, but I upgraded RAM (to 512 MB) and bought the least expensive video card I could find with reasonable specs (ATI Radeon 7000 - 64 MB DDR). 

After installing the card, I could never get better than 800x600, which Ubuntu refers to as "low resolution mode". There's the equivalent of a "generic" Linux driver that knows what the Radeon 7000 is, but tops out at 800x600. Apparently, an ATI card must be a 9000 series or above for higher resolutions. This fact is backed up on the ATI support site. 

I didn't find a workaround in the forums or elsewhere, so I returned the ATI card and tried an nVidia card instead. That's not the happiest of stories, either, but I'll spare the details until I can spend some time troubleshooting. ;)
