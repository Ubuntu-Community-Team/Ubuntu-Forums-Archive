### Post by foolsh on 2007-01-06
this seems a little beyond i think what vmware player was made for. To gain "twinhead/twinview" functionality you would need a second video device, this would have to happen in vmware's emulated hardware.

but having the desktop stretched across both monitors is what Xinerama doest, I can see that having ubuntu snap its open windows to the left or right monitor would be useful in your situation.

without Xinerama on a two video card system ubuntu shows  separate desktops on each screen, you can not drag windows between desktops or maximize windows across both screens.
with Xinerama turned on the two desktops become one huge desktop, allowing you to drag and maximize and otherwise treat your two monitors as one desktop.

So i don't think you have to do anything to get the Xinerama feel on the guest OS with an emulated desktop area of 2560x1025
