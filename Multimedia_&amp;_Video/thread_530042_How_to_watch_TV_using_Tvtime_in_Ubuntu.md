### Post by Harlequin on 2007-08-22
PM is now worries.

> By the way i have AverTV Go 007 FM Plus Card.

The *latest revision*(PCI ID 1461:f31f) of the Avermedia AVerTV GO 007 FM works "out of the box" in recent kernels. Earlier revisions WILL work, but you must  use them once on Windows with the official drivers to load the internal EEPROM, and manually specify the card type as 57 and tuner as 54

So type

sudo modprobe saa7134 card=57 tuner=54

That should get the card up and running for you.

Depending on how that goes you may end up with video but no sound being processed.  If so add the line

Sudo modprobe saa7134-oss

technically you should be able to run straight with saa7134-oss card=57 tuner=54 as it should load the saa7134 firmware by default when the oss and alsa drivers go in... But try it this way first.

This should now mean your card is processing the sound signal.  But chances are you can't hear it.  so,

sox -c 2 -sw -r 32000 -t ossdsp /dev/dsp1 -t ossdsp /dev/dsp

this will take the output from the tv tuner and route it to you sound card.

Note: the oss tag could be swapped for the alsa tag do use different sound decoding drivers - however from what I understand the alsa results are poor and sound quality is better on the oss driver.

If all of this works then

sudo nano /etc/modprobe.d/options

and add the line

options saa7134 gbuffers=2 card=57 tuner=54

if you are running a low end system or a heavily loaded system it may be worth increasing gbuffers to 4,8,16 or even 32.  But you get quicker channel change at 2 frame buffer.
