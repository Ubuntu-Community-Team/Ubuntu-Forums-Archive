### Post by izhitzza on 2008-01-31
As far as I know, there isn't any software that would allow you to preserve the original DVD structure, special features, main features, and the DVD menu. What you could do is rip (perhaps using mplayer) all the video segments (including special featurs), extract the subtitles, convert them to images (so you can see them) and convert them to text file, fix the spelling, and reauthor the DVD putting back all the video segments. That way you'll have everything from the original DVD, but you'll need to make a menu yourself. I wrote a small program that kind of helps you do this sort of thing, you can check it out at [http://uglysub.sourceforge.net]("http://uglysub.sourceforge.net"), but editing the subtitles in this program would mean retyping all of them, not just fixing the mistakes.
