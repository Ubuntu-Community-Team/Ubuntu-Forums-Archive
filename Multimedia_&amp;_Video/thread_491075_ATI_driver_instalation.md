### Post by weblordpepe on 2007-07-03
Hey I fixed it. I didn't actually follow that tutorial at all when I did it. Didn't do anything with the .debs etc.
What I did do though is read through that article & looked that you were meant to blacklist the ubuntu restricted driver 'fglrx'. 
I rebooted and I guess that instead of loading the driver which came with the restricted drivers module, it loaded up the raw ATi driver.

> gksu gedit /etc/default/linux-restricted-modules-common
> Add "fglrx" to the line "DISABLED_MODULES"
And I also did this.> aticonfig --initial --force
Now when I do the fglrxinfo I get stuff about ATi and not Mesa. Videos are also back to normal with no amazing cpu usage. YESS!
