### Post by Yoooder on 2007-07-31
A standard definition TV is very very low-res in comparison with a PC monitor, which explains your resolution and also your blurriness to a degree.  The bluriness could also be caused by the type of card or it's connecting cable.

Even my 32" LCD HDTV only does 1280 according to specs, but I run it at 1024 as it suits my needs as a MythTV playback screen.

It may cause burn-in on your TV, where if you switch it to a blank input you will see "ghosts" of your PC screen.  Burn-in happens when a TV shows the same image for a long period of time.  Screen Savers were actually developed (and named) to prevent burn-in on old monitors, so use one for this PC for sure :)
