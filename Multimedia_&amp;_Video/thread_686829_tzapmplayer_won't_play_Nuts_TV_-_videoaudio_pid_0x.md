### Post by Pigeon. on 2008-02-03
Ah, I have solved it :D :D :D

I compared the channels.conf line for a working channel (Five) with those for Nuts TV:
```

Five:649833334:INVERSION_AUTO:BANDWIDTH_8_MHZ:FEC_2_3:FEC_AUTO:QAM_64:TRANSMISSION_MODE_2K:GUARD_INTERVAL_1_32:HIERARCHY_NONE:6017:6018:12866
Nuts TV 1:649833334:INVERSION_AUTO:BANDWIDTH_8_MHZ:FEC_2_3:FEC_AUTO:QAM_64:TRANSMISSION_MODE_2K:GUARD_INTERVAL_1_32:HIERARCHY_NONE:0:0:15904
Nuts TV 2:650166670:INVERSION_AUTO:BANDWIDTH_8_MHZ:FEC_2_3:FEC_1_2:QAM_64:TRANSMISSION_MODE_2K:GUARD_INTERVAL_1_32:HIERARCHY_NONE:0:0:15904

```

and the same in channels.dvb from kaffeine:

```

TV|Five|6017(2)|6018(eng),6019(eng),|0|12866|12290|Terrestrial|650000|0|v|-1|-1|-1|-1|8|-1|-1|-1|29|6022(16)(2)(2)(eng),||
TV|Nuts TV|6785(2)|6786(eng),|0|15904|12290|Terrestrial|650000|0|v|-1|-1|-1|-1|8|-1|-1|-1|45|6790(16)(2)(2)(eng),||

```

and noted that in the case of Five, the first two records after the channel name in the channels.dvb entry, ie. the numerical records 6017 and 6018, were also in the channels.conf entry for Five, as the two numerical records immediately following the HIERARCHY_NONE record, whereas in the case of the Nuts TV entry in channels.conf, these two numerical values were zero.

So I copied those numerical values for Nuts TV from channels.dvb and entered them in channels.conf, thereby synthesising a further two entries for Nuts TV:

```

Nuts TV 3:649833334:INVERSION_AUTO:BANDWIDTH_8_MHZ:FEC_2_3:FEC_AUTO:QAM_64:TRANSMISSION_MODE_2K:GUARD_INTERVAL_1_32:HIERARCHY_NONE:6785:6786:15904
Nuts TV 4:650166670:INVERSION_AUTO:BANDWIDTH_8_MHZ:FEC_2_3:FEC_1_2:QAM_64:TRANSMISSION_MODE_2K:GUARD_INTERVAL_1_32:HIERARCHY_NONE:6785:6786:15904

```

Now tzap shows those two values (in hex) in the audio and video pids:

```

pigeon@pindix:~/.tzap$ tzap -r 'Nuts TV 3'
using '/dev/dvb/adapter0/frontend0' and '/dev/dvb/adapter0/demux0'
tuning to 649833334 Hz
video pid 0x1a81, audio pid 0x1a82
status 1f | signal 6753 | snr 0000 | ber 001fffff | unc 00000012 | FE_HAS_LOCK
status 1f | signal 6745 | snr 0000 | ber 00000000 | unc 00000000 | FE_HAS_LOCK
status 1f | signal 6745 | snr 0000 | ber 00000000 | unc 00000000 | FE_HAS_LOCK

```

and cat, mplayer and mencoder are now capable of playing/recording Nuts TV :D  My Lucy Pinder recordings are assured :D

Presumably it is a bug in scan that it did not pick these values up in the first place whereas kaffeine does.

Perhaps this problem and its unexpectedly-quickly-found solution will be of use to any other people having similar difficulties with their channels.conf - googling for a solution found a few complaints "it works in windows but not in Linux" with similar symptoms, but no solutions. So there you go - if kaffeine works but tzap gives zero video and audio pids, pull the video/audio pids out of kaffeine's channels.dvb and stick them in channels.conf by hand.
