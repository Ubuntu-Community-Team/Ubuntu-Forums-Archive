### Post by Lutherian on 2007-03-20
Hello Voltsy.

This may not be a complete solution, but you may find this interesting.

Long ago I when I was was using Windows, I had a program called RipCast, that would download streams, because I only had a phone line and could listen online. 
Anyway, when aac stream came out I tried connecting, and ofcourse nothing happened, RipCast would just downloaded the files *with MP3 extensions*. And when I tried to play these files, they didn't work. I got the idea of renaming all the files to .aac - and ...... it worked.

I'm 99% sure you can do the same thing with StreamRipper (and that perfect GUI that works with it.) Infact I think I did this a few months ago ...

Because you mentioned "reduced bandwidth" I'm assuming you don't need to listen to the files immediately. What I do is, I download a few tracks overnight and then the next day or over the weekend I queue them. 

This (for me is the easy part) the trick was renaming *.mp3's to *.aac's in Linux (Yes I'm a noob) anyway I worked that too - let me know if you need the command . it's at home on a post-it on my screen LOL.

So, in conclusion.

You download *.aac with a program designed to download *.mp3 files (StreamRipper)
The *.mp3 files on your hard drive are actually *.acc files in disguise and wont play, so you have to rename them back to their original *.acc format (Phew)
