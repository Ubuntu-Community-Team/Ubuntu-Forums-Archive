I recently installed ubuntu Fiesty on my machine , and tried to configure it to work with dual monitor.
I got nvidia 6150 graphics card , with vga and dvi connections.
So I found the linux 64bit driver for my ubuntu from nvidia's website , and installed it.
It is called NVIDIA-LINUX-x86_64-100.14.11-pkg2.run.

After I finished the installation I typed startx and everything was cool.
However , When I rebooted the computer , it blinked twice when I was still at the console mode.
Then there was an error message box about that xserver can't find good configured monitors , so it can't start.

I went back to the installation file , sh-ed it. It wrote that the driver is already installed , and let me the option to exit the installation. I chose that option.
Than I typed startx and xserver worked. [before sh-ing the installation it didn't work].

It is kind of annoying to sh the installation every time I reboot. I wonder why it happens , and maybe there is a way to automate it , as I hate to see the strange grey error message box every time I reboot my computer.
