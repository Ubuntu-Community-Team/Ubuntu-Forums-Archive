### Post by mivo on 2007-10-26
8500 is a little low-end in the 8er series. I would consider the 8600GTS if you want one of this series, and a card that will support OpenGL 3.0 (and DX10). If neither of this is important, the 7900 and 7950 cards are good value, too. If all of these are too expensive, a top-end card of the 6er series might be okay (and sufficient if you only want it for the 3D desktop), though I personally wouldn't buy a three years old card now, if you are going to get something new -- but it also depends on the rest of your equipment. Also see the [Comparison of Nvidia cards]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_NVIDIA_Graphics_Processing_Units") at Wikipedia for useful information. You're looking for memory bandwidth and tact primarily.

### Post by mivo on 2007-10-26
8400 is not a card I would recommend. It is significantly worse than even some cards of the 5er series -- and those are four, almost five, years old.  If you do want a 8er card, the only choices I would recommend are 8600GT and up (but "up" also means "fairly expensive). If you are looking for something inexpensive, I think a 6800 will do nicely (just won't support OpenGL 3.0 and DX10). Not sure if they are still sold. :)

With Nvidia cards you can't only look at the numbers. 6800 is better than 8400 for example. The number only says something about the generation, though within a series (i.e. 8xxx) bigger numbers almost always mean "better").
