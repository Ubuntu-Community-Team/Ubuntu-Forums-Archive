### Post by .exe on 2007-11-29
Wow, I've had my Zune for just over 24 hours, and its been one of the most frustrating products I've ever handled! I'm serious. The Zune software is so far beyond incompetent, I can't even imagine how a multi billion dollar international corporation could have screwed up this badly! I really wish I were exaggerating.

I gave the little doodoo brick the benefit of the doubt. I took it to school and back, and I actually enjoyed using it. Its not nearly as big or as ugly as I thought it would be. On the contrary, it fit into my tightest pair of jeans with ease, and it is actually kind of attractive. However, the trouble came when I tried to clean up my library. I used Media Monkey (great piece of software...if only it worked with the Zune) to download additional album art that I was missing. Turns out the Zune doesn't pick up the images unless its called folder.jpg or something like that. So I added all the many missing images in by hand. Tedious to say the least. After syncing, for some reason adding album art made messed up my albums, splitting some of them in two...So I tried as best I could to fix the library from the computer end (slow and tedious - so many navigational issues I wonder if it was designed for a human...). 

After syncing again, the problem was even worse: instead of updating the missing album art, it made a copy of every album I updated with art! WTF! After going through and trying to delete all the duplicates by hand, as there is no other way I could find to do this, I decided to just format the thing and copy everything again, for the 3rd time. 

The Zune it self was a decent piece of hardware, and the firmware is decent. But the software is so crippling its surreal. Its as if M$ WANTED the thing to be detestable! There are so many, better, media management applications out there (including iTunes), its insane to be locked into this POS. I'd try doing the 2.2 update, but I hear its even worse (0_o)!

I plan to give it a go for at least 14 days or so (until I "figure it out" or my patience runs out). For those of you who love to be told what, when and how to do things, where to do things, this might just be what you are looking for.

30 gigs for $99...it was just too good to resist. It still might be...
