### Post by gali98 on 2007-09-17
I would definately  recommend Wacom. Frankly my Graphire4 4x5 is enough for me. I got mine on sale at Circuit City for something like $60. But you can get it online for around $80-85. Just search google products or buy.com. IT all works great except unfortunately the mouse. As far as my condition goes the scroll wheel goes backwards and doesn't work a lot of the time including in Gimp, which kinda stinks... But it will probably get fixed someday. And I think there are some fixes for this, but you kind of have to search around. I love it that you can use it out of the box in Ubuntu, even on the live cd... I don't think all the buttons work, but I know there is a program that can map them to keys. I will try and find it and post it later. Overall the wacom experiance on Linux was pretty good. 
You do not actually have to reboot to use it, just restart X (ctl+alt+backspace) it isn't too bad. You have to log back in but it doesn't take too long. Hopefully there will be active support for that later. I hope this helps,
Kory
