Hello, everybody!

According to an _extremely_ informative Adobe site, [http://help.adobe.com/en_US/Reader/8.0/WS8CCCD02C-92AA-4cba-A83F-5C71AEF7CC82.html]("http://help.adobe.com/en_US/Reader/8.0/WS8CCCD02C-92AA-4cba-A83F-5C71AEF7CC82.html"),
there is a remote possibility that acroread 8 is now able to play embedded multimedia.

My test setup was the following:

File: 
LaTeX pdf presentation with embedded DivX/avi.

System: 
openSuSE 10.2/KDE 3.5.7 on Samsung R55 laptop (sorry, no Ubuntu here)
acroread 8.1.1
installed players: kaffeine, mplayer, vlc, realplayer
codecs: w32codec-all, divx4linux, gstreamer good bad and ugly

preliminary results:
acroread states: "Cannot find player software for the movie." Acroread multimedia dialog (Preferences...) gives "Real player" as only software choice. Real player refuses to play avi files, playback works on any of the other players.

In my opinion there are two possibilities: either to get acroread to recognise a different player, or to get real player to play divx/avi and see what happens next.

Any ideas out there?

Many thanks in advance,

Rosencrantz
