### Post by disturbed1 on 2007-04-22
For a one click solution google for either devede or ToVid

Authoring a DVD takes more than one step. Obtain the source, edit the source audio and video, encode the audio and video, create menus, author the DVD, burn the DVD.

Obtaining the video source can be from your frame grabber (TV Card) or DV Camera (with Kino).

To edit/encoe the video look at Kino, Cinerella, or Avidemux. For the Audio Kino and Avidemux offer simple audio editing, Cinerella is a little more advanced.

To edit the audio look at Sweep or Audacity.


To create your menus Gimp is an obvious choice.

For authoring I like DVDStyler.

DVDStyler can burn for you, or create an ISO and burn directly with Nautilus, Brasero...

There are also many CLI programs such as dvgrab, ffmpeg, mencoder, transcode, dvdauthor.

If you open Synaptic and do a search for things like, DVD, or Encode, or mpeg, and google also, you'll come across quite a few other options.
