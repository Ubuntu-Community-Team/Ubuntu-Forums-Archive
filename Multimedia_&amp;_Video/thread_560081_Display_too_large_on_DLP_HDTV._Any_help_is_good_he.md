### Post by neuralzen on 2007-09-26
I found an answer that worked, and it may work on other HDTV sets too!  It had nothing to do with messing with my xorg.conf. Here's the message exerpt:
> 
I have no access to all video settings in PC mode. I had read that they rely on your video card settings, but they have to have noted that people use the PC mode for more than just PC!!! I also cannot access "Fit to screen", in any modes...Any input here is appreciated. The book says it is accessible in all modes.

If you're not using a PC with your PC input on your TV, the "Auto Adjust" should stretch the image to fit the screen usually. (Also make sure the P.Size is on "Wide PC")

If it doesn't stretch it right, you might need to fine tune the parameters...

If you ARE using a PC, usually just setting a resolution of 1280x720 or 1152x684 and setting the TV in "Wide PC" mode acts like "Fit to Screen".

If you have a nVidia video card on your PC, you can use the nVidia scaler to scale it with either fixed or non-fixed aspect ratio to fill the screen in any resolution.

If you turn off nVidia scaling, your TV should do it on it's own.

**You can also use the HDMI1/DVI input for a PC mode too, just rename the input to "PC" and it should stop overscanning the input, stretching it on it's own if it's under 1280x720.**

Hope it helps someone else too!
