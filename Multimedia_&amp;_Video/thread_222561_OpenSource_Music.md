### Post by dumtratt on 2006-07-25
I've been thinking a lot lately about OpenSource media, and how to apply the thoughts and ideas of OpenSource software onto things that are not traditionally OSS. Such as music.

For example, what if you sat down to create some music with Wired (haven't used it, just took it as an example) and to stay true to the OSS thoughts and ideas, you put your loops, patches, sounds and effects up for anyone to use and manipulate and maybe even give you ideas or inspiration. 

I haven't heard of anyone doing this or trying it out, and I would like to try. However there are certain things that needs to be discussed:

- Who is the original author and how can you tell? 

- Is there a need for an original author in music?

- How would this change the way people listen to music?

- The composer of a piece of music would probably want some sort of payment, but can you earn money with music made with OSS software? This of course is an ethical question.

----

This idea can also be applied on film, for example. If no money (or very little) is put into recording the film (borrowing cameras, re-using tapes, free actors etc) then the production could be free using Diva (haven't used that either).That is another discussion, but I think there are some really important and interesting issues that needs to be discussed in the Open Source community. 

Fire away!

### Post by Buffalo Soldier on 2006-07-25
> **dumtratt said:**
> Yeah I did, and I learned that the Creative Commons license system is always offering some sort of protection for the author of the music, and there are different kinds of licenses (sampling, sharing and so forth) all of which are non profit and only free in some way.

This is incorrect. There are many *variations* of the Creative Common License. But the six main ones are:[list=1]
[*] Attribution Non-commercial No Derivatives (by-nc-nd)
[*] Attribution Non-commercial Share Alike (by-nc-sa)
[*] Attribution Non-commercial (by-nc)
[*] Attribution No Derivatives (by-nd)
[*] Attribution Share Alike (by-sa)
[*] Attribution (by)[/list]

I would like to point out one of the this license, the Attribution No Derivatives (by-nd) License. "This license **allows for redistribution, commercial and non-commercial**, as long as it is passed along unchanged and in whole, with credit to you."
