### Post by Scotty1982 on 2007-07-14
i'm too new to linux to know which work best, but i can point you toward a good deal for whatever you figure out will work:

check out [www.geeks.com](www.geeks.com), they have some good deals, i'm going to be ordering parts from them next week to build a new PC. what i'm going to build is under $300 for all the parts i need (which is everything other than the monitor, keyboard, mouse, and speakers). and it'll probably be able to do what you're wanting to do.

for basic specs, i'd recommend at least 1.5 GB RAM (2 or higher would be ideal), either single core over 2.8 Ghz or dual core over 2.0 Ghz, and a PCIe video card with 512 MB+ Ram, just limiting those 2 aspects should limit the minimum speed of the GPU to something acceptable (they don't make PCIe 512MB cards that are too slow) but even at that i'd aim for one of the ones over $100 at geeks.com in the 512 MB PCIe catagory.

i'm a noob to linux so take this lightly, but it seems like people have a easier time setting up nVidia cards. all it usually takes is nvidia-config to get it running xserver then in the GUI open another terminal and run nvidia-settings and remember to click the save button after setting up your display resolution. (nvidia-settings should be installed with the driver)
