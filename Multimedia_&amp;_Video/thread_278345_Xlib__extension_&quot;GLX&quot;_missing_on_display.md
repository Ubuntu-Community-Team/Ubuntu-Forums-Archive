### Post by Pri0n on 2006-10-18
I also had this problem.  I couldn't get the nvidia video card to work properly, and whenever I tried to run glxinfo or glxgears, I'd get segmentation faults.

What I had to do was add the following at the end of my /etc/X11/xorg.conf file:

[B]Section "Extensions"
        Option "Composite" "Disable"
EndSection[/B]


I also commented out the line "Load dri" in the Module section.  i.e.

[B]Section "Module"
        Load    "bitmap"
        Load    "ddc"
#       Load    "dri"
        Load    "extmod"
        Load    "freetype"
        Load    "glx"
        Load    "int10"
        Load    "type1"
        Load    "vbe"
EndSection
[/B]

And finally I confirmed that under "Device" the driver was set to "nvidia" and not "nv"

[B]Section "Device"
        Identifier      "NVIDIA Corporation NV15 [GeForce2 GTS/Pro]"
        Driver          "nvidia"[/B]


Of course, all of this was after installing the nVidia drivers (for those who don't know, see 

[https://help.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/desktopguide/C/hardware.html]("https://help.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/desktopguide/C/hardware.html")

After I modified the xorg.conf file, I simply hit CTRL+ALT+Backspace to restart X-Windows, then tried glxinfo.  It worked.  Tried glxgears, it worked.
