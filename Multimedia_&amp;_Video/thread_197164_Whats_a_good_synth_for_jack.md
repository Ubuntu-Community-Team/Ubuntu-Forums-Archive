[http://jackit.sourceforge.net/apps/](http://jackit.sourceforge.net/apps/)

Plus: forget reason, nuendo, logic whatever!

Linuxaudio is different, there are no suites like those but a modular concept that is not as easy to be used but provides more functionality and power than any single commercial suite offers.

Combine 2, 3 or more synths with FX-Chains and a sequencer of your choice and get more than ever to be expected from a single suite.

### Post by Sheik Yerbouti on 2006-06-25
Just use your VST plugins. I have pretty much all my VST plugins working under linux. Check it out [http://dssi.sourceforge.net/download.html](http://dssi.sourceforge.net/download.html) for dssi-vst or [http://www.joebutton.co.uk/fst/](http://www.joebutton.co.uk/fst/) for fst. I can't believe it actually works but it does. I can play MrRay73 or even Sytrus and what not just fine and even using my M-Audio KB 88 Pro keyboard as a controller even. I still can't believe it all works. Some compilation required for this stuff and wine is needed but it's worth it. 

Only problem I have is that rosegarden seems to be crashtastic for me. Especially when it comes to dssi plugins even native ones never mind dssi-vst ones. I think I amy end up going the modular route using seq 24 and outputting to Ardour or something. 

The only VST plugins I could not get to work at all were some from Steinberg and the only reason they would not go is because they would not install due to some copy protection on the CD which wine can't handle. I bet if I got a cracked version they would work.
