### Post by kubug on 2007-12-11
Amarok is a KDE application (you are running Gnome most likely). 

KDE apps will work fine under Gnome (and vise versa) but sometimes don't work 100%.

I had the same message come up until I checked the same message box. From what I know, KDE uses aRts as a "System Sound", meaning, when you get an error message you also hear an associated sound with it. 
This is what seems to crash. 

But I wouldn't worry too much about. :rolleyes:

Have you looked at other players though?? Maybe a player that is native to Gnome. I use Rhythmbox, since it supports the media keys on my keyboard and is a pretty decent player. Doing that with Amarok was too much of a hassle. :neutral:
