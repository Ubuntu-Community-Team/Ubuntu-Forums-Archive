### Post by rich.bradshaw on 2007-05-03
Don't bother converting those WMA into MP3 at a higher bitrate, you won't gain anything except bigger files.

WMA isn't renowned for being a great format - doesn't work with all players etc.

MP3 is the standard, but isn't a free format, meaning that you have to be licensded to make software that can use MP3.

OGG is a compleletly free format, the specification is open to anyone, and it's free to use in any situation. However, not many DAPs let you play it.

I keep my whole collection as 256kps MP3 - I know it's not a free format, but it's compatiable with everything.

The problem with WMA is that if Microsoft stop using it, it will be hard to access those files in the future.

As OGG is free, even in 100 years, someone can look the format up and write software to read and write it.

It's a bit of a null argument really.

Main thing is that 64kps will sound like *** on proper speakers!

The RIAA pays no attention to individuals that purchase CDs and then back them up for archival purposes.  As a matter of face it is perfectly legal to make archival backups of your CDs.
