### Post by rje_nc on 2008-01-08
I have the same video card in a Lenovo T60p I use at work.  Ubuntu 7.10 runs fine once it is installed but it is a real pain to install.  The problem is the Video card, you will not get any usable display until you update the video driver to the restricted driver after the initial install.  You will also need to use the alternate installer, and then follow the instructions to install the current frglx driver in a terminal window that you probably won't be able to see!

It was frustrating to get it installed, but Ubuntu runs great on the T60p with the same card once you get over the initial problems.

Either the Intel or the Atheros wireless cards should work fine (the wireless card you mention in your post is most likely an Atheros chipset).  If I have any option to get an nVidia graphics card, I would jump on it.
