I am looking to put together a Mythbox running mythbuntu based upon 7.10.  I have looked through the forums here and there and am thinking this will be a fairly cost-effective and simple system that will get the job done.  It would run as a frontend and secondary backend.  The primary backend would house movies and be a video jukebox while this machine would be more for viewing/recording tv while querying the primary backend for movies.  Some components will be pulled from existing parts lying around.

I am interested in any problems people may have had with the components listed below (mainly motherboard/case).  The black case will fit in with the room ascetics a lot better, but it seems the older silver cases are easier to work with.  Alternatives would also be appreciated.  I hope this machine will last me several years with possibly an upgrade to HD-DVD or BLU-RAY when those become affordable.

Motherboard:  [ASUS M2A-VM HDMI]("http://www.asus.com/products.aspx?l1=3&l2=101&l3=496&l4=0&model=1585&modelmenu=1")
CPU:  AMDX2 64 bit (low power type 65/45 W)
Memory: 2G RAM (prolly more than needed, but DDR2 is cheap now)
Case & P/S: [Antec Fusion]("http://www.antec.com/us/productDetails.php?ProdID=15738") (Black) 
Old Raptor 67GB -> 
     / (ext3)  
     /home (xfs)
     /swap
Old 250 GB IDE HD -> /var/lib/mythtv (xfs)
SATA DVD-R/RW
Silicon Dust HD HomeRun for capture (NSTC)

I put the two SATA power connections on the Raptor/DVD-R and use the IDE for recording storage with the option of putting a little bit on the Raptor if needed.

I have seen some problems posted on the new (black) case pertaining to the display, but it looks like there are workarounds/solutions.  Same for the motherboard with BIOS updates.
