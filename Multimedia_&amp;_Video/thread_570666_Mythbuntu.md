### Post by leg on 2007-10-08
I think Mythtv comes into its own when you use it as a distributed system for centralising the storage of all your media and streaming it to multiple boxes. That box could be your set top box or computer. You can for instance have all your dvds ripped onto a machine and watch them back from any machine connected to your home network. Same with music etc etc. In this arrangement you can set to record a tv program and the master will decide which of your machines will record if any overlaps occur. If you just set it up on your computer and don't intend to connect to it from any other machine you will still gain the database type organisation of the files in question. As the previous poster said it is a true media centre and you gain that functionality also. If you are happy as you are you don't need it but if you would prefer the media centre experience MythTV is a good choice.
