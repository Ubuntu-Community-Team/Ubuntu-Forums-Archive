### Post by perryrt on 2007-08-23
I've been using a cheap Pengo unit, like this one here:

[[COLOR="Blue"]Pengo MP3 Player on Newegg[/COLOR]]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16855151004")

I bought it for exercising with, and I wanted a unit that was

(1) cheap

(2) had an FM tuner for news etc.

(3) cheap

(4) basic USB plug type design (no extra cords)

(5) cheap

(6) would mount on my arm

(7) cheap

(8 ) battery powered

(9) did I mention cheap?

It worked ok until recently (see below), so I can't say I was dissatisfied with it. However, there were a few things that I found annoyed me about it after some time:

1) Inability to transfer/use playlists. The only way I could get music to play in an order was to number the items (01-Song, 02-Song, etc.) so that it would drop them in alphabetical order. I found the easiest way to deal with this was to set up folders in my music library, copy the music I wanted to it, renumber/rename them, and then transfer en masse.

2) FM radio reception was really spotty, both outdoors and in the gym "listen to the TV" settings.

3) The arm band thing was hopeless. Couldn't reach the controls, kept cutting off circulation in my arm, etc. etc. Just a hassle. After about a month, I tossed it.

However, I've been walking/running/shuffling more and more outside recently, and I've been caught in a few rainstorms. Lasted OK through three of them, but the last one seems to have killed it.

Oh well - did I mention that it was cheap?  :^) It lasted a year plus, so I can't complain.

So all-in-all, a limited recommendation, I guess. I think my next one might be a bit higher on the price scale, though. Those zen units look pretty good - I may try one of those, if the Ubuntu support is really as easy as it sounds here.
