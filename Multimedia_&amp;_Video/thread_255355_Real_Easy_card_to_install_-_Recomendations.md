### Post by handy on 2006-09-11
You are best advised to steer clear of ATI cards for linux.  ATI don't have a reputation for easy installs, & only a few happy customers on linux.

This will change, but we don't know how long it will take ATI to get it's act together.

nVidia is not perfect apparently. But is what I would recommend if you want to play around with 3D stuff.  nVidia is supported in the repositories, or if you want the best 3D performance, then you can install the closed source nVidia owned drivers.

I use those drivers, they work really well.  I also recommend that you have a look at [Automatix]("http://www.getautomatix.com/")
for installing the nVidia closed drivers, & what ever takes your fancy out of the other 45+ softwares that it will install for you if you so choose...

I hope that helps?

All the best... :KS
