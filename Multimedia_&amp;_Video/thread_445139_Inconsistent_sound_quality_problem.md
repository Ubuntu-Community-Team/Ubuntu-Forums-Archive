### Post by IntrinsicAnomaly on 2007-05-15
I'm a very seasoned Windows user but have decided to become more experienced with Linux. I'm willing to tinker, but I lack intuitions for this OS, so bear with me. 

I'm using Edgy Eft, by the way.

I added my music to Rhythmbox Media Player to play it as I usually would in Windows, but the quality was terrible. I figured it was related to my sound card drivers. I downloaded the Linux sound drivers for my integrated audio on my motherboard (Asus P4P800-E), but the instructions for installation were a little too abstract for me to understand. I decided to hold off on the sound for a while. The integrated sound chipset is Realtek AC'97, which I believe is pretty popular.

However, when experimenting with file types (I was making sure it wasn't a codec-related issue, so I converted some MP3s to Ogg Vorbis files), I noticed that the sound quality is perfect in Totem Movie Player for both file types. I played both file types in both Rhythmbox and XMMS, and both were of poor quality, so it had nothing to do with codecs.

This leads me to believe that Totem Movie Player, for whatever reason, has the location of drivers while Rhythmbox and XMMS don't.

I have no idea what to do about this, because I'd rather use Rhythmbox as its library feature is closer to Winamp. I just can't stand the terrible quality.

I don't know what terminal commands to use to gather information about my configuration, so someone will have to basically walk me through it. I'll do as much probing and tinkering as needed, as I am an extremely experienced computer user, just not in Linux.

Thanks in advance.

edit: I'd like to add that, after further observation, the quality isn't perfect. It's not as good as on Windows, so I assume I do need to install the drivers, but that can be saved for another time. I just want to know why there's a difference between Totem and the others.
