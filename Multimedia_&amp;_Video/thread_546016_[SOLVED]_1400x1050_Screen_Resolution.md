### Post by w4ett on 2007-09-08
From the command line type:

```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg
```

This will give you the interface to modify the driver and resolutions. Controls are UP and DOWN cursor keys move the cursor and scrolls through available options, the spacebar selects options, and <enter> confirms selections.

Select "via" as the driver and press <enter>, then at the next screen you can enable any addttional resolutions (mark them by use of the spacebar)that you want to use.....then use the cursor keys to highlight {OK} and <spacebar> to select the resolutions and press <enter> to save the selections as prompted, then type:

```
startx
```

This will get you back to a GUI desktop.

This will enable the correct driver for your video chipset and will modify your xorg.conf file to enable the required resolutions.  Addidionally you might have to manually input your refresh rates for your monitor.

Let us know how you get on.

Good Luck
