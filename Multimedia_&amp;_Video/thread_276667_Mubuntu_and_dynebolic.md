### Post by capsid on 2006-10-13
How many here have tried out dynebolic?

It's a live-cd multimedia studio for "nomads" with much more of a focus on hacktivism in art instead of producing saleable commercial art, though I'm sure you could use it for both purposes.    

I think there are some things about it that Mubuntu could learn from, though it does depend on the target audience.  For one, despite a lightweight window manager, IMHO it's more user friendly than Gnome, KDE, or OSX.  All your storage media and optical drives appear as big icons on the right side of the screen.  The control panel is a launcher-style app that features big icons and only shows the most crucial of options (network, soundcard, screen res, etc...).  Pressing the Soundcard button runs an Alsa autoconfig script that works great as long as you don't have other apps grabbing the soundcard (like the webbrowser).  

I popped it in at a computer lab and within minutes I was jamming out with hydrogen and jack rack.  

Another nice thing it features is the ability to Alt-LeftClick anywhere in windows to move them (like Gnome) but also Alt-RightClick from anywhere to resize.  I think the latter is absolutely critical for working with these multimedia apps because it is so easy to populate the whole screen and you often need to reorient stuff as you work.  I haven't figured out a way to do the Alt-RightClick resize in Gnome, does anybody know?

Dyne can run on a computer with 64MB of RAM.  Optimized!!!!!!

So I just thought it would be fun to discuss Mubuntu in relation to the feature set that dynebolic has.  What are gonna be the individual strengths of Mubuntu that set it apart from other multimedia distros?

Also, is there a thread that lists the apps that will come preinstalled with Mubuntu?
