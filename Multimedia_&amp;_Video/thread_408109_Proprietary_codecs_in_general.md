### Post by CompShrink on 2007-04-13
a good place to read on how to play such things is here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats)

Of course, there are many other options. The default MPlayer and VLC in the Universe repository (see other threads about enabling repositories in synaptic) plays many things without having to figure out what you need.

It's always a personal opinion on what you should. Many people install any and all codecs, and just play whatever they want. You have almost no chance of reprocussions. Others convert everything to open formats or otherwise boycott whatever you can't use with free codecs. It's up to you if you install the restricted formats codecs listed above. Also look for "w32codecs" if you want to play a lot of formats, It has no dependancies and can be installed easy, works with Xine or MPlayer. It is generally regarded as clearly illegal, but effective.
