### Post by timnoc1456 on 2006-09-13
I don't know wether this will work for, but I was able fix mine. I was also facing similar type of problem was unable to change my screen resolution to 800x600 from default 1024x756.My Graphic card is "Intel Corporation 82865G Integrated Graphics Controller" and monitor is HP 5500. I followed the following steps:

1. Backup your "xorg.conf" file.
   
   foo$: **sudo cp/etc/X11/xorg.conf /hda3/ubuntu_backup ***(the backup folder    may differ in your case)*

2. foo$: [B]sudo gedit/etc/X11/xorg.conf
[/B]

[/I]
Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"Intel Corporation 82865G Integrated Graphics Controller"
	Monitor		"hp 5500"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		1
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		4
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		8
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		15
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		16
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		24
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection

**To:**

### Post by sgtBiafra on 2006-09-13
My experiences with Intel 82_945_ lead me to the following:

1. in the BIOS, you may have an entry that allocates memory to the integrated video. Mine had options for 1MB and 8MB (where 1MB limited me to ridiculously low resolutions and 8MB opened that up considerably).

2. Also, the i810 driver supports shared video memory. This option can be  configured on the video memory screen within dpkg-reconfigure ("sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg") or, IMHO more easily, by adding VideoRam "SIZE" to your Device section. I'd recommend at least 32768 (32MB) if not 65535 (64MB) if you can spare it.

I think your video chipset is memory starved so it is defaulting to 640x480 (VESA standard VGA).
