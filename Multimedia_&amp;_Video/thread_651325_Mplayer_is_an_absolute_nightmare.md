### Post by TWO on 2007-12-27
In the few months I have been using Ubuntu, one of the problems that just doesn't seem to be going away is trying to get flawless playback from media on the Internet. I think I have tried pretty much every plug-in available and I just don't seem to have any luck.

Though I am able to play embedded media which require things like Shockwave Flash, streams which would require Windows Media Player or Real Player on Windows are a nightmare to deal with.

I haven't found a single program or plug-in that has been able to flawlessly deal with them. For example, in Windows if I am to watch a stream in WMP format then what I get is a section of the web-page with an embedded version of WMP and an indication of buffering status and a play button. 

However having jumped between VLC and Mplayer, I have to say it has been nothing short of a nightmare. With the VLC plugin, I get a  "(no video)" message in the middle of the embedded media area then the video will play without me having the option to pause. 

With Mplayer and say a Real Media stream I get an embedded version of mplayer on the web-page then what looks like a buffer indicator and a GUI however the stream just fails. On other occasions with Mplayer, on attempting to play streams I have gotten the following error message: 
"Fatal error! Error opening/initializing the selected video_out (-vo) device"

I appreciate that many of the programs under Ubuntu are still work in progress and that these programs are having to work as well as they can in a world where everything is geared towards Windows based programs and the like but there has to be a more successful solution with regards to on-line media playing.

I have all the plug-ins possible as well as restricted codecs and the like.

But this is a problem that I have been battling with for weeks now and I am unwilling to give up the fight!

Does anyone else have problems with playing media on the web? If not, please tell me your secret!

Any advice would be greatly appreciated!

TWO
