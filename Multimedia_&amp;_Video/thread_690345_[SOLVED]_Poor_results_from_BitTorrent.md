### Post by BitTorrentBuddha on 2008-02-07
Could be one of many things.

1) The torrent has very few seeds, this is especially common in public trackers.

2) Improper port forwarding. Go to [http://portforward.com](http://portforward.com) and follow the instructions for your router and bittorrent client.

3) ISP throttling. Many ISPs have started throttling common bittorrent ports; use a higher port range (up in the 63000s works well for me), and utilize encryption if possible.

If that doesn't cover the problem, post back and I'll try to remember to respond later tonight after I'm out of class.
