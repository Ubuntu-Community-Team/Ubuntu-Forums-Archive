### Post by anothertallorder on 2006-08-30
You want to install and use 'XMMS music player' this will play your MP3 files.
you may need to 'Enabling Extra Repositories'to do this, check:

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/sources.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/sources.php)

After you have done this, look in Synaptic Package Manager and find it under multimedia (it may already be in before enabling extra respositories, I can't remember)

Its not the best looking player (skins are available) but it's the only thing I have come across to play MP3's.

### Post by gunksta on 2006-08-30
Try "Googling" or "sudo aptitude install" for:

Amarok (KDE)
Juk (KDE)
Banshee (GTK)
Rhythmbox(Gnome)
Muine (GTK)

I think XMMS is your best option ONLY if you are using a computer that is really old/slow/low on RAM. Otherwise these other options are way more fun.

Others, not in the repos. 
Exaile
Listen, Just Listen
