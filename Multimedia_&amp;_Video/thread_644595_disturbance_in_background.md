### Post by eye208 on 2007-12-19
> **eskay_karthik said:**
> is there any changes that can be done in the sound settings so that i dont get much of noise ??
Noise is introduced by poor quality analog-->digital converters on the sound card. Make sure to disable all unused inputs in the mixer. Set the input gain as high as possible without distorting the signal. This will improve signal/noise ratio.

If that doesn't help, get a sound card designed for audio recording and music production (e.g. M-Audio Delta, Terratec Phase series, etc.). Converter quality is the key here. Also make sure to use a proper microphone. Some mics capture more room noise than others.

Audacity has a noise removal function. It may help to a certain extent, but removing lots of noise also degrades audio quality.

For more info on recording and music production, consider visiting [http://www.gearslutz.com/](http://www.gearslutz.com/) or similar forums. They also have sections for Linux users.
