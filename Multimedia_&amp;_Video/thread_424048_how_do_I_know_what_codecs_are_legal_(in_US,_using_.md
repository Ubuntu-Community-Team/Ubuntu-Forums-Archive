### Post by Tanker Bob on 2007-04-26
There's two separate questions there.  First, what's legal.  Well, if you bought the DVD or CD or made it yourself from your own material, then as far as I'm concerned any driver that lets you play on your PC what you legitimately own is legal.  That's part of what's called Fair Use.  Don't get hung up on the RIAA/MPIA propaganda.

Second, where to get codecs.  Supposedly Ubuntu has a Restricted Driver Manager somewhere.  I haven't found it in Kubunut yet.  However, the [medibuntu repository ](http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/repository.php) has everything you need.  Follow the directions at the link to access the necessary drivers.  That site will also offer to update some of you core players with additional codecs embedded.
