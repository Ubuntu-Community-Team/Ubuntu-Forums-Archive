### Post by Mike'sHardLinux on 2008-02-04
This is just my personal opinion. I have 2 different (Viewsonic and V7) models of monitors that offer VGA and DVI connections, and when I compare, I don't really see a difference. I haven't done extensive testing. I just loaded up the OS, played a game, and browsed the web.

There shouldn't be any real signal loss due to the connector itself. As far as I know, there are no electronics in it. It just routes certain pins from the DVI connection to certain pins on the VGA connection.

the 8600GTS was a huge disappointment on launch failing to be more powerful than it's predecessor the 7900GS. Drivers have improved and the situation has gotten better but a far as I'm concerned the 8600GT/S is still underwhelming.
the 8500 however just plain out sucks.

here's a rough spec sheet of the 8800s the 8600s and the miserable 8500s

8800 ------8600 -----8500
128sps---32SPs---16SPs
376bit------128bit----128bit (memory interface)



now the 8600s and 8500s clock better, but they well... they're litterally 1/4th and 1/8th the card that the 8800GTS is as far as functional units. The GTX is pushing around 100GB/s in memory bandwidth while the 8600 is doing around 50GB/S and the pure crunching power of the GPUs is just sad.

my advice if you plan on gaming or doing anything 3d heavy... the 8600GT isn't too bad at roughly $90 on sale. cheaper the better and don't worry about vRAM capacity, the cards aren't powerful enough to use past 200mb or so since they won't be doing high settings.

if you don't game, then WTF just buy the cheapest card on the market... period.

if you game a lot, get a 8800GT 512(or 3850/3870)

market price on the 8800GT 512 is around $270ish, on the 3850 it's around $160(3850 is IMO best bang/$ card for the gamer right now) 8600GT is like 90ish

### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-04-13
The card is perfectly capable of accelerating hi-def content. It's just not built into nvidia's linux driver. (Though it is build in to the windows driver.)

In theory someone could write opengl code themselves to make an opengl powered hi-def accelerated h264 renderer for an nvidia card, however, currently, the nvidia driver itself doesn't provide video playback acceleration for anything but 1 or 2 formats, I believe.
[http://forums.nvidia.com/index.php?showtopic=35695](http://forums.nvidia.com/index.php?showtopic=35695) might have better info.
