### Post by Handssolow on 2008-01-04
I wanted to play the intro music of Jeremy Clarkson's BBC program Extreme Machines from 1997. Despite the composer David Lowe having kindly made this available on his website [here](http://www.davidlowemusic.com/content/cmc000007.htm), I was unable to play it except with XP and  Windoze Media Player.

Attempting to play the [music](http://www.davidlowemusic.com/content/audio/jeremy_clarkson.wax) I see this error message-

<ASX VERSION="3.0">
   <ENTRY>
   <REF  HREF = "../audio/1997_jeremy_clarkson.wma">
   </ENTRY>
</ASX>

After I found information about .wax files [ here](http://filext.com/file-extension/WAX).
I changing the url line to .... /audio/1997_jeremy_clarkson.wma then I could download the .wma file and save onto my desktop. I can now play the file with one of my media players.

OK sound quality could be better but there's no commercial music CD available as far as I know. I'm also saving the file first and not playing the streamed audio.

Any other ways?
