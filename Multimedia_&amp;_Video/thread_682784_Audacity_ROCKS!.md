### Post by Gannin on 2008-01-30
Often times vocals are mixed in the middle.  So if you take a stereo image and divide it into two tracks, invert one of the tracks, then mix them back together, it'll mostly remove the vocals from the middle.  I've used it to record music before.

### Post by Bruce H. McCosar on 2008-01-30
> **tech9 said:**
> Has anyone recorded their own music with it?

Hey, a wide open invitation to plug my own music!  :D

In the old days, I mixed down manually using . . . well, a mixer.  Provided you have panther like reflexes, six arms, and the instincts of Rudy van Gelder, not a bad idea.

For the rest of us . . . I discoved audacity about a year ago, and quickly switched over from manual mixes to that wonderful world where you can 'undo' your mistakes and preview your ideas before committing them to a fully rendered audio file.

I used a Korg D888 for the actual recording.  Only now, because of Ubuntu, linux-rt, and JACK, is it possible for me to record directly to the computer.  Still, I'm a little paranoid about plugging Apollo 13 directly into my computer (an improvised rack system that accommodates organ, bass, guitar, and drum mics through various inputs).  There is a very careful power on / power off procedure I follow to keep transients from zapping equipment further down the line (hence the nickname for the system, if you saw the movie Apollo 13).  I'm just not brave enough to hook the high voltage stuff into my computer.

I transferred the tracks over to the computer via USB, and did all the mixing -- including all the effects -- using audacity.  I kept all the tracks, and all the audacity files, in case I want to remix or reuse parts later (or even post them for other people to remix, eg on ccMixter).

My music is on Jamendo.  Here's my main artist page:

[http://blue.jamendo.com/us/artist/bruce.h.mccosar/](http://blue.jamendo.com/us/artist/bruce.h.mccosar/)

And here's the album I recorded, *La vie sous la mer*:

[http://blue.jamendo.com/en/album/9479/](http://blue.jamendo.com/en/album/9479/)

Recently, I've been playing around with all the Ubuntu MIDI toys -- synths like Aeolus, Zynaddsubfx, etc -- and of course Rosegarden, which right now has the unofficial title of Best. Program. Ever. (I used to take hours to write out complicated passages ... now I can zap em straight into Lilypond, another favorite of mine).
