### Post by damu on 2006-09-07
I have a sony GRT laptop. It comes with with firewire and pcmcia. 
My sound card - RME Multiface - is connected through its pcmcia connector (you have the choice when you buy RME to go through either a PCI or PCMCIA connector). PCMCIA is much more efficient than firewire (same performances than PCI), so you won't be limited if you want to go bick in multitrack...

I use my firewire port for an external Hard drive. Firewire is prefered to USB2 for audio prod , as it allows a more constant data trasfert rate.

An external Hard drive is the way to go if you want good performances with a laptop. There is no way the hard drive of the laptop will be able to handle system + softwares + soundfonts + many audio tracks at once...I changed the internal HD of my laptop for a 7200tr/min to improve performances, and it did, but I still use an external HD where all my soundfonts and audio projects are saved.

Enjoy

### Post by damu on 2006-09-07
I don't think there are many other brands than RME offering pcmcia support. However, I don't think it's a big deal, as RME sound cards are considered as some of the best you can get :)
Ardour has been build with this sound card in mind.

As for many firewires ports, and/or linking devices , I didn't see any laptop with 2 fire wire ports either, but if you ever find a solution in this direction, make sure that the rate transfer is not divided by the amount of devices connected...it would then defeat the object, and it's definitely the case with some usb2 ports and some firewire configurations...
