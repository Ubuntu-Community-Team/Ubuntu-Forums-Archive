### Post by oldmanstan on 2006-07-15
i'm tempted not to even try to help you since i saw your post in the other forum, but i will cuz i'm really really nice. people on here are helping for free in their spare time, i have other things to do besides sit in front of my computer being your tech support guy... lecture over

your problem (i think) is that in the "SCREEN" section of your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file 1280x975 is not one of the resolutions listed

here's how to fix it... go to the "SCREEN" section of this file (you have to edit it as root, so in a terminal ```
sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

now change each of the "Modes" lines to match this

```
Modes    "1280x975" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
```

save the file and reboot, that *should* solve your problem assuming your other drivers and such are setup properly
