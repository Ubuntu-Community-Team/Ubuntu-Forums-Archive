### Post by PlainShane on 2007-07-07
So, I am running both feisty 64 and xpSP2 pro. To be honest, the feisty 64bit is a tad buggy for my liking. Although that may be true, I am absolutely in love with the rhythmbox music player that comes with the distro. I have never found anything like it for windows. 

Here is what I like about it:
-Lightweight (resource wise; very responsive)
-Automatically somehow finds all of the album art per song!
-Has a small popup bubble which tells me the name of the artist, album and song as well as displaying a small album art clip
-Best of all: search feature. Without having to reload a playlist or anything, opening up an annoying media library to search, it has an advanced playlist with search feature. Simple. So simple. All integrated.
-Just right click on the icon and you can play, pause, turn up volume, or whatever!

Point at hand - the program is perfect for me and I have been searching for something like this forever. The closest I have found is iTunes which I truly loathe and do not want to use due to the services it runs in the background and intensive gui. I usually use Winamp, have been for years, but it lacks a good search feature. So, I just load all my songs and search with the mouse and scroll bar. What keeps me with winamp is the translucent "mini blind" window it can become at the top of the screen which is very accessible. Today I have been trying out VLC, which is alright. Nice and low on the resources but the worst playlist feature ever.

**So without any further a due, what is a comparable media player for windows? ** By the way, I have tried foobar and did not want to mess around forever customizing the thing. I understand it is great, and I am not particularly lazy as I will spend an entire day trying to get one program working fine but I do not want to mess with that program again... Thanks! Peace

PS: I know winamp has the media library and it is a pain in the ***. Over complicated for an easy job. By that I do not mean I have a hard time comprehending; it is full of superfluousness.
