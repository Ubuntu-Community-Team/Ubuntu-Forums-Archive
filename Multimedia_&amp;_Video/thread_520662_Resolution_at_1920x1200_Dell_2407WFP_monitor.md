### Post by brm on 2007-08-13
I have come in rather late in this conversation, and I hope that the following is helpful.

I have owned (and am using to write this message) a Dell 2405FPW monitor which is the immediate predecessor to musinc's model. I assume that her's offers at least the same functionality as mine and, I hope, some improvements as well.

I have owned the monitor since January 2006 and have been using it (with great satisfaction) with 1920 x 1200 resolution @60Hz horizontal refresh since the days of Ubuntu Breezy.

The key difference is that I have an nVidia display adapter --- although not a very recent one; it is a 5700 series, at least two years old.

Another key factor is that the Dell 2405FPW has a bug in its firmware and does not correctly identify itself or its specifications to Linux configurations utilities. I therefore had to check its specs very carefully and enter them all by hand into xorg.conf using "dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg".

I have a suspicion, based on the contents of a kerneltrap.org article ([http://kerneltrap.org/node/7020](http://kerneltrap.org/node/7020)) that there is a specific driver for the Intel 965 graphics chipset. If that is so, a check of the kernel config for the vanilla Ubuntu Feisty Fawn kernel (I am running the most recent update) shows that it is not enabled in the current Feisty kernel (grep 965 /boot/config-2.6.20-16-generic). I got a number of intetesting hits from "Intel 965 Graphics" on google.com/linux. (BTW, if you are not already familiar with it, Google's Linux filter is a godsend.)

If my hunch is right and if you are reluctant to upgrade to Gutsy (and the upgrade is at this still early stage recommended only for the experienced and the thick-skinned), you might look into recompiling your kernel instead. It sounds intimidating, but there are dozens of good "recipes" out there.

Hope this helps :-)

### Post by phenest on 2007-08-13
nVidia drivers are noted for their superior support for screen resolutions and not just the video card. Intel, although very forthcoming with drivers, seem to have problems with widescreen resolutions on the 800 & 900 series video cards, which is why users with Intel video cards, tend to have to install 915resolution to solve the problem. I think that perhaps Feisty's 915resolution is a little out-of-date compared with Gutsy's. Perhaps someone with a Gutsy install, could downoad 915resolution for muzincs so she can install it on Feisty. The current version of 915resolution in Feisty is 0.5.2-10ubuntu2. Can anyone confirm the version in Gutsy? This could be why Gutsy is ok, and Feisty is not.

### Post by mgoycool on 2007-08-13
I have been having the same problem as muzincs, and just solved it with the following command:

sudo apt-get install xserver-xorg-video-intel

([http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Feisty](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Feisty))

Regarding the previous email, which also seemed a promising direction: Gusty is using 915resolution 0.5.3. However, Feisty is using 0.5.2. I found that 0.5.3 just recently supports the i810 with 946GZ chipset, which, by looking at my /var/log/Xorg.0.log file, seems to be the chipset I have in my lenovo desktop. If the previous command does not work, this could be a reason. Hope it helps!
