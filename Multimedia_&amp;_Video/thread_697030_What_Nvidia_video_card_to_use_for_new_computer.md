### Post by TheThinker on 2008-02-15
I'm using an old Nvidia Geforce 4000, which has 128 mb of memory yet no pixel-shader support (sigh). You can probably get it really cheap now, and it does work perfectly fine on my E-Machine Gutsy computer using the Nvidia-GlX-New package from the repositories. If you ask me, stick with a video-card that you KNOW has support and will last you a couple of years at least. Age is irrelevent; reliabilty trumps all.

But since you're not into gaming, it's often the case that a RAM upgrade improves performance the most. It's also the easiest to do! I bumped my RAM to 750 MB without having to get a driver for it, and now my video-playback is much faster. Not only are RAM chips cheap now, but this also makes other applications more responsive, in case you need to work.
