### Post by bjd on 2006-11-19
I had a similar problem being stuck at 640x480, that was solved by setting:
Option "ModeValidation" "NoDFPNativeResolutionCheck"

There are more options available that may be applicable, check here:
[http://download.nvidia.com/XFree86/Linux-x86_64/1.0-9629/README/appendix-d.html](http://download.nvidia.com/XFree86/Linux-x86_64/1.0-9629/README/appendix-d.html)

### Post by owyn on 2006-11-19
I have been fighting through the same problem.

What was really annoying was that I had a PCLinuxOS 0.93a install on the same system that was working just fine.

After a lot of experiments, and cross comparing xorg.conf files, finally identified key fix.

Copied

```

HorizSync 30-69
VertRefresh 50-120

```

from PCLos to Section "Monitor" in Ubuntu.

Bob's your uncle.

Ctrl+Alt+Bspc and now at 1280x1024.

All Screen Resolutions now available in System Preferences.

You can get the appropriate settings for your monitor by booting a PCLos Live CD, or, possibly some other Ubuntu tool.
