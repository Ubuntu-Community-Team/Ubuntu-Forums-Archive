### Post by decoherence on 2008-04-11
Hello,

The school I work for is currently looking at setting up video screens to show various announcements, photos and video clips from events. It might look something like those 24-hour news channels, with the screen split up in to different segments... weather, headlines, talking head, stock ticker, etc. The screens will get their content from a central server.

I'm not concerned about the hardware aspect of it... that much looks like it'll be fun to put together.

What I'm wondering about is management software. Basically I'm looking for something that will let us to have one of these 24-hr news channel screens and allow teachers to submit video, photos and text that will be shown in the appropriate places on the screen. Ideally this could be done through a web interface.

We are also looking at commercial solutions but they are very expensive. If anyone knows about a free solution, or even something close that could be adapted in to doing this, I'd love to hear about it.

I'd also love to hear from anybody who has experience with a commercial solution, and the software that comes with it... what does it let you do? What do you wish it did? How easy is it to use? Web interface?

Much thanks for any replies!

### Post by decoherence on 2008-04-28
We carried on our research and I think we've reached a conclusion. 

On the open source front the most interesting projects looked like OpenLaszlo ([http://www.openlaszlo.org/](http://www.openlaszlo.org/)) and Blender's Game Engine with Video Texture Player patch ([http://www.ash.webstranka.info/?page_id=4](http://www.ash.webstranka.info/?page_id=4)). While I think both of these could do the job, they would both also require I create a friendly user interface for whoever is managing the sign. That seemed like too much work for our timeframe considering the interface would cater to non-technical users.

We came across a company called Exhibio which sells exactly the product we're looking for. ([http://www.exhibio.com/products.php](http://www.exhibio.com/products.php)) It's not dirt cheap (though it's also not nearly as expensive as some other solutions we looked at,) we can use it to drive multiple screens and most importantly, it has its own web-based management interface. Good thing because at $3200 I know I ain't paying for hardware.

So we'll probably go with that. I'm quite interested to try out the web management software and see if I can put together a clone for us GPL folks. That could make doing your own high-end signage cheap and easy!
