Actually most dvds new or otherwise can be ripped without any problems. Some of the major mainstream titles do have structure protection that may or may not present issues when trying to process (re encode, transcode, shrink, ect.) As far as ripping though i've only seen a couple of titles that couldn't be ripped with linux tools. Depending on what you want to do with your rip you may need to do some ifo and vob editing .
As far as disney  they use variations of ripguard, some titles like pirates3 are very simple and easy to fix, others like Ratatouille are a bit more involved. Ratatouille has a small number (4-11) of block errors at the beginning of the 1st. 11 vts's that would need fixing after the rip. The only titles that aren't possible or not worth the time and frustration are recent New Line titles (hairspray) and a revision of the last ARccOS. I don't think we'll see many more ARccOS releases in R1 (sony's got it in for R2 though) and as far as New Line screw em (or use defabhd.... in wine)

edit:not surprisingly K9 can do Ratatouille completely, full disk, menu's, ect. I would expect it to have no trouble with any disney title, the protection is pretty basic.For example in Ratatouille the main movie (vtst 6,1) the 1st. 6 cells are pretty much nothing with encoding errors, the movie starts on cell 7. Theres nothing that remarkable about deleting or blanking those cells and the few unreferenced ones and making proper adjustments

### Post by mc4man on 2007-12-11
[http://k9copy.sourceforge.net/](http://k9copy.sourceforge.net/)
 I was getting errors at the very end of the make - k9avicodec h:17 blah blah errors so in addition to the build depends I told you of in the other thread I added ffmpeg, and some related -devs. of which 1 or more did the trick. History shows - libavcodec-dev, libavformat-dev, libavutil-dev, and libtheora-dev. the 1st. 2 were probably what fixed it. It (1.2.1) seems to be running very well, no errors when using thru terminal.
being somewhat inexperienced at this i used checkinstall right off of the make 
```
sudo checkinstall -D make install
```  followed by
```
sudo ln -s /usr/local/kde/bin/k9copy /usr/bin/k9copy
```  which allowed me to open k9 in the terminal and add to the apps/menu
you'll also get a .deb in your k9 1.2.1 folder for future use ect.
