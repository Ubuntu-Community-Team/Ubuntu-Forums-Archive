### Post by wkulecz on 2007-08-02
They didn't send you a video, but an "activeX" program(control) that probably then goes out and plays the video, or it could be a trojan/keylogger or other malware.  Be careful.

Ask for a direct link to the video on YouTube, it can be cut and pasted from the browser window while the sender is watching the video.

When using windows I have all activeX blocked except at "microsoft.com trusted sites"

ActiveX is why I moved to mozilla/firefox since MSIE becomes nagware once activeX is not given free reign over your system.  Javascript is a necessary evil only made tolerable by the existence of the firefox "NoScript" extension, don't surf without it!

Once you downloaded the flash plugin for firefox youtube videos play perfectly for me on 7.04.  The plugin installation was prety much automatic once I encountered the first flash site I didn't have flashblocker block.

--wally.
