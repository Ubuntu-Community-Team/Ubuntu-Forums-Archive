### Post by aashay on 2008-03-15
My laptop runs a resolution of 1280x800 (16:10). Whenever I play videos in fullscreen, most of which are 4:3, two large black spaces are left on the left and right of the video. (The video fills the height but not the width). Is there a way to make only fullscreened videos appear at a 16:10 ratio so that they fill the whole screen? Either totem or vlc would do. I found a way to do it in mplayer using a compiz plugin, but I'm not a big fan of mplayer (mostly due to compositing issues)

---

### Post by xc3RnbFO8P on 2008-03-15
In Preferences> video check advance box
General video settings> source aspect ratio> type 16:10

---

### Post by mc4man on 2008-03-15
If you change the aspect ratio to fill the screen you'll get some distortion - in this case squishing. Another option is to leave the aspect at default and choose crop>16:10 . You'll lose some picture info from top and bottom but maintain the proper aspect ratio. The choice would be what bothers you less
