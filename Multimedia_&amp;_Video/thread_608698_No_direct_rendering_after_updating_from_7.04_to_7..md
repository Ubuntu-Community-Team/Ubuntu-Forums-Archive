### Post by carlinuxlearner on 2007-11-10
You could try installing your driver again using "Envy".

1.1 
Go to your (System>>Administration>>Software Sources) make sure you have all your repositories enabled. (Check mark them all)

Download "Envy" to your Desktop (to make thing easy) [http://albertomilone.com/ubuntu/nvidia/scripts/legacy/envy_0.9.8-0ubuntu10_all.deb](http://albertomilone.com/ubuntu/nvidia/scripts/legacy/envy_0.9.8-0ubuntu10_all.deb)
(if this link does not work, just visit this web site and download the latest version [http://www.albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html](http://www.albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html) )

1.2
Double click on the file you just downloaded and tell it to install

1.3
If all those steps went well then Envy should be installed and you should be able to find it on your "Applications" menu under "System tools", just run it and you should be able to install your driver all by your self very easily.

### Post by daborg on 2007-11-27
I got direct rendering working by uninstalling and purging the configuration of xserver-xgl and then reinstalling it and restarting X:

```
sudo aptitude purge xserver-xgl
sudo aptitude install xserver-xgl
sudo /etc/init.d/gdm stop
sudo /etc/init.d/gdm start
```

I have no idea why it worked, but it did and I'm happy.

### Post by RobCampbell on 2007-12-04
This situation of having working Compiz effects and, for instance, reasonable FPS from glxgears, is due to Xgl. I don't know why Xgl does this but it turns out that you can't have direct rendering on an Xgl sever. Since Xgl is enabled by default in 7.10 you have the problem you describe. 

I have also found that some .avi files have a green band along the top and messed up colours. Also the display switches off after 10 minutes of inactivity and there's no setting I can find to stop this. 

My solution to all these problems is to watch movies or play games on a different X server. This lets me keep my Compiz and not have to re-boot each time I want to do one of the above. Typicall I:

sudo X :2.0 #Gets new blank sever
#Now go back to kde/gnome 
xterm -display :2.0 #To get an xterm on your new sever
You can now vlc ./movie.avi or quake4 from your xterm. All should be well.
