### Post by Sophont on 2007-06-17
Late last year I bought a new laptop and eventually did what I planned for over a year - say goodbye to windows and hello to Ubuntu.

And mostly I'm very happy about the switch. Things ain't perfect - b ut the same is true for windows and what feels like a downward trend on windows, feels like rapid improvement on Linux.

For many apps (FF, TB, OO, Gimp, Inkscape, python) the change didn't make much of a difference - I'm using a lot of the same apps as before.

The games I'm most interested in NWN and Eve Online can be made to run on Ubuntu - and the rest ain't that important to me.

Yet there is one app I'm really missing (not so much to switch back :-) ) and that's the CoreCodec Media Player: [http://www.corecoded.com/]("http://www.corecoded.com/").

That's the best media player I ever used. Well - most of it's functionality is available either in other players - or I can do without them. 
But there is one that feature that I'd really appreciate to find in Linux media players: Pre-amplification/volume-gain/dynamic range control - I'm not sure what the proper technical term is - but the effect is to make sound LOUDER. :-)
When I watch DVDs on Ubuntu they tend to be too quiet - even on max volume. It's not a problem with the loudspeakers - they are capable of doing much more - when I boot the small windows partition that I kept (and haven't booted in months :-) ) the volume is no problem.
The control in asla mixer is maxed too of course.

I tried the Ubuntu Movie Player, MPlayer, Kaffeine, Beep and VLC. VLC is the only one that seems to have  that feature at all in it's equalizer settings - but it doesn't get me much farther and the sound quality goes down quicklly (unlike in TCMP).

Sadly TCMP doesn't work on wine. And no linux player that I tried so far is really capable in that regard. I hope I just overlooked some nice setting somewhere and somebody mentions that here. :-)

BTW, I'm sure I'm not the only one who noticed that we have a lot of players - but all with their particular strengths and limitations? I wish they would merge into 2-3 projects. Choice and competition is great - when you get real architectural/philophical differences and the competition makes them all great products. But to me it looks like we currently have a choice between too many almost-there players that are either too limited and or too clunky to be really great.

YMMV of course. :-)
