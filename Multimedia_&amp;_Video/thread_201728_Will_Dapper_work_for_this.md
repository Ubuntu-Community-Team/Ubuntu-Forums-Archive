[QUOTE=Getwild2]What I do is record every spoken word into a laptop via Goldwave through my line-in port, then Goldwave will break up the file that has been recorded into user-defined track lengths and then will ultimately save them as MP3.  [/QUOTE]

There are dozens of Programs that can do such a job in linux. Because I guess, that we are talking about long continuos recordings, my first choices would be:

1.) audacity (can do such job, is comfortable, resembles goldwave somewhat)
2.) qarecord recording via jackd (is most reliable, allows best to automatize the whole thing via scripts)
3.) ardour (can do much more than audacity, needs some more experience though...)

[QUOTE=Getwild2]

Linuxaudioapps can do much more than the thing you describe BUT none of them is *exactly* the same as goldwave - so you´ll have to learn a few houres and maybe return here to get to the point, you want to reach - you´ll be welcome :-)
