### Post by toorima on 2006-01-26
Link to your other post [http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=121862](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=121862)

If you haven't installed the ati drivers here is a good guide [http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=24557](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=24557)
In short 
1. sudo apt-get install linux-restricted-modules-<your-kernel-version> xorg-driver-fglrx
2. sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf-old
3. sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
4. change
Section "Device"
	Identifier	"ATI Technologies, Inc. Radeon 9600 (R300 AP)"
	Driver		"ati"
to
Section "Device"
	Identifier	"ATI Technologies, Inc. Radeon 9600 (R300 AP)"
	Driver		"fglrx"

5. Once you've edited and saved xorg.conf restart your x server (<Ctrl>+<Alt>+<Backspace>), log in.

If you have installed the ati drivers you only have to follow the C part from his link [http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=96765](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=96765)

I have done it and will attach my xorg.conf so you can see what you need to do, I have almost the same laptop as you have, Inspiron 8600 with ati radeon 9600
You will need to change where it says S-VIDEO and PAL-B to your needs

### Post by Jason_25 on 2006-01-26
I think you should start out by trying to load the fglrx driver.  It's available through the repositories.  Once loaded, you will probably need to change the driver section under "device" from ati to fglrx.  Then log out and reboot or ctrl-alt-backspace.  It should then work on s-video.  With a monitor and the tv hooked up, it should default to clone mode.
