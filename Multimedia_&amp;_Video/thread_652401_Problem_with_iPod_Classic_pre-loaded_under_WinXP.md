### Post by puneit on 2007-12-28
I did the same mistake that you did. Only difference was, my Ipod Classic didn't contain any media. I tried loading media into it using Linux. On the comp, I could see the music in the Ipod but not in the Ipod. 
So I read surfed and read. To my surprise, Ipod Classic is not supported in Linux as of now. Reason, Apple has changed the way they encrypt and the Open source community is working on it.
My solution: ](*,) I had to make my box a dual boot with MACROSHIT
Now I only use it to transfer media. 
As far as recovering the media. Since your Ipod still shows music in Ubuntu, you can try to copy it and save it on your hard-drive. Then using microcrap, you can transfer it to your Ipod.
You will have to reinstall software on your Ipod, because same has been corrupted.
Its still safe to connect the Ipod to Ubuntu to recharge its battery. 

I know I am not of any help.... but this is all I can share
