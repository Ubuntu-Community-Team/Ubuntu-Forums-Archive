### Post by archivator on 2008-01-06
aticonfig --initial simply adds the proper Device, Monitor, and Screen sections to your xorg.conf. aticonfig --overlay-type=Xv adds VideoOverlay and OpenGLOverlay options to your Device section. If you would check [this](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Gutsy_Installation_Guide#Method_2:_Install_the_Catalyst_7.12_Driver_Manually) up-to-date guide, you would see that this command is part of the configuration after a successful driver install.

You don't want the AIGLX implemetation in fglrx, trust me. I'm yet to meet a person who's happy with it and finds it working flawlessly. From what I've tried and heard, the AIGLX implementation is far from being stable.

Instead, you're better off with a stable fglrx driver for direct rendering and XGL running on top of it to provide Composite.

I would suggest you play with xorg.conf a little more and if it still doesn't want to cooperate, just erase it and use the Restricted Drivers Manager.
