### Post by mungy on 2007-05-03
The "Do Not Disconnect" is just to warn you not to unplug it. If you right clcik your Ipod icon of the desktop and click eject, it should disconnect and you will see that your ipod has charged.

You can also tell if it is charging by the little icon over the battery, if it has a little zigzag shape it I'm sure it means the ipod is charging.

### Post by SishGupta on 2007-05-03
The behaviour you describe is exactly how my iPod works in windows when in disk mode (which is how I like it). If you don't put it in disk mode in windows, the ipod will eject automatically after updating.

Just know that barring any extraneous circumstances as long as your iPod is plugged in it is being charged. But for reassurance, the teenie tiny battery icon in the upper right hand corner should show a lightning bolt if it is being charged and a plug if it is charged and nothing if it is not being charged.
