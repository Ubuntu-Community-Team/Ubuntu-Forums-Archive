### Post by paulg on 2007-06-07
I think Slimserver is in one of the Feisty repositories. I tried installing it from the repo's but it was a downgrade from the version I had been running previously in Dapper (which I got from the Slim Devices website). I have since included the Slim Devices repository (for debian/testing) and it's working fine again. I would recommend you use a version from the Slim Devices repository at this time.

For anyone curious, Slimserver will run on Linux, Mac and Windows. It serves/streams music to hardware devices (SqueezeBox or Transport) sold by Slim Devices (Logitech) or to a Java based program called SoftSqueeze which again is platform independent. I'd love to get a Squeezebox some day but currently use it to send music from my Linux Desktop to a MacBook I can take around the house and access my entire music collection without overwhelming it's puny hard drive. My only gripe with Slimserver is that currently a collection cannot span hard drives and is limited to one folder.
