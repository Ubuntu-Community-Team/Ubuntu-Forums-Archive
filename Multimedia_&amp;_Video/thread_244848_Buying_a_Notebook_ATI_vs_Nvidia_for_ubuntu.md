### Post by ltmon on 2006-08-27
I bought a laptop with the ATI, simply because I couldn't get a Nvidia chipset that was any where near as good as my x1600 for the same price.  I also couldn't find a nvidia with 1600x1050 resolution in my price range at all.

My experience in Ubuntu has overall been actually pretty good, which I was surprised at.

Pros:
- Got 3d acceleration and full resolution on my first shot.
- BigDesktop working after a bit of messing around
- Dynamic display management works easily.  This is a big plus that nvidia drivers don't currently have. It is actually a hidden feature in the ati drivers, but it should be fully supported soon.
- Got the power management profiles working easily, so great battery life compared to not having this feature.
- New driver releases monthly, so there is new stuff all the time.  I haven't even tried the latest release to see what has improved.

Cons:
- Frame rate is half what the hardware can actually achieve given better drivers.
- Couldn't get XGL working at any stage (although others have).
- Got suspend to ram working, but it was a lot of messing around to do so.

So there you have it.  I probably would still get a nvidia if it was available in a similar price range, but I no longer think there's a clear winner between the two brands.

Also I hold high (but possibly naive) hopes that ATI's new owners, AMD, will accelerate their linux driver development and hopefully even release the drivers as free/open source software.

L.
