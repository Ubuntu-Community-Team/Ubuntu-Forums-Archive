### Post by ClarkePeters on 2006-09-28
Sometimes the best solutions are too simple.

I was having trouble with sound in firefox on flash player (no sound) on some websites. Other websites, such as youtube.com, gave me no sound or video at all with a message that I didn't have the current flash player.

After several tweaks and no results, I went to the source @:
[http://www.adobe.com/shockwave/download/index.cgi?P1_Prod_Version=ShockwaveFlash&P2_Platform=Linux&P3_Browser_Version=Netscape4](http://www.adobe.com/shockwave/download/index.cgi?P1_Prod_Version=ShockwaveFlash&P2_Platform=Linux&P3_Browser_Version=Netscape4)

Download and follow the instructions. (I let archive manager automatically download and extract for me and then did the command line as instructed). If you don't want to extract to a default file make your own directory before downloading and extracting  i.e.  MyInstalls  or whatever you like.

Even though this is only flash player 7, it allows me to use youtube. I thought my problem was that youtube was using a higher version but apparently this is not the case. I can even get CNN things now (not streaming video, just the flash player)

This may not work for everyone but it won't hurt a thing to try.

good luck

### Post by DavidFourer on 2006-11-30
The Flash video player works great for some web sites and not for others.  
New York Times, Washington Post, and YouTube have always worked well.  Upgrading from Flash version 7 to 9-beta finally nailed the sound-sync which was always off.  There appears to be no version 8 for linux.

Lots of other web sites give error messages like "please install flash player" or "requires flash version 8" 
ABC news site opens the flash player but it doesn't play.  As reported here, there was a bug with YouTube but they fixed it.

Video in Microsoft media player format opens the gxine/firefox plugin but it doesn't play, at least on MSNBC and other sites.
