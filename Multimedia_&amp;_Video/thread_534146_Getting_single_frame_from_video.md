### Post by Irihapeti on 2007-08-26
I had the green frame problem with Kino and fixed it by upgrading to a different version from another repository. I know that Trevino has a version of Avidemux. I'm pretty new at this myself and just finding out things as I go along. (and I haven't always kept the best notes, either!) Plus, I'm working with a camera that produces those little DVDs that you can put straight into a DVD player, and I get the impression your system is different.

You could try: copying it from the original video source with a different ripping or copy program. I've found that can make a difference. Running it through a conversion program such as Mencoder or FFmpeg, though I've found that FFmpeg can also throw out an error message sometimes.

My last suggestion is a bit low tech. Pause the video in VLC or whatever and do a screen capture. Doesn't really solve the problem but might get you the still pictures that you want.

Irihapeti
