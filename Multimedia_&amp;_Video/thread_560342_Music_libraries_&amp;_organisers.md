### Post by Skerit on 2007-09-26
Music collections aren’t what they used to be, lyric-books, vinyl, 8-track, cassettes, … they’re all a thing of the past. When I was young (and it’s funny to say this because I haven’t even turned twenty years old just yet, but bear with me :) ) having 100 mp3s in a single folder was considered to be a big collection. (At least in my “posse”)

Why would I care about id3-tags back then? (I even was amazed at how WMP could know what album the song was from, lol)

Times change, of course! Now most u us have enough music to fill 20 hard drives of the year 2000, and storing them in 1 single map has become a joke (even though I still know people like this, hear my disappointment!)

Statistics have also become immensely important, another side-effect of the web 2.0 revolution perhaps? I personally can’t live without knowing when I first played a song or how many times I have played it in total.

**So I ask of you, what is the *perfect* structure to store YOUR music files? And what organiser/player do you prefer?**

_My music is stored like this:_

A Regular Hard drive / “Audio” / Artist / Year – Album Title / Track Number. Artist – Track Title

In my “audio” map I also have a folder called “Singles”. In here I place another folder for each year, eg “2007”, and in that folder I put another folder with the number of the week, so 2007/2007.33. In here my music’s filename is “Artist – Track Title”

Even though I always loved the simplicity of rhythmbox, the lack of new useable features over the past few years have become intolerable. I just made the switch to Amarok and, even though it’s a bit bloated & clashes with my GTK+ applications, it handles lyrics, labels, cover art, statistics, ratings (& scores) and especially external audio players & web-integration a lot better than rhythmbox. (Oh, and I found this nifty script which let’s me import all my last.fm statistics into my amarok database, lovely!)
