### Post by kerry_s on 2007-07-09
Hey my friend, i just found something interesting. adobe has a stand alone flashplayer. of course it's a little hidden, but hey i like to look around. :)
anyways here's what you do, grab this> [http://download.macromedia.com/pub/flashplayer/updaters/9/flash_player_9_linux_dev.tar.gz](http://download.macromedia.com/pub/flashplayer/updaters/9/flash_player_9_linux_dev.tar.gz)

unpack it, ( tar xvf flash_player_9_linux_dev.tar.gz ) click on standalone> release, you should now see flashplayer.tar.gz, that's the one were after. unpack that ( tar xvf flashplayer.tar.gz ) you should now see a flashplayer executable, copy that to /usr/bin/. that's it you now have a stand alone flashplayer you can call with just " flashplayer ".
but there's more, to make it useful we need flash to play. so grab a little firefox add on> [https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/446](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/446)
now you have choice's you can either download it or yank that sucker out of firefox or click on the x in the upper right corner of media to play it in firefox normally.


There are some sites where it won't work like pandora.com, but anyways i've only been playing with it for about an hour. thought i would share, have fun. :)
