### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2006-12-31
I have recently acquired a whole bunch of comics (Calvin and Hobbes) which are being stored digitally, and I decided to set them to a screensaver.
With xscreensaver's glSlideshow, I can get this working and it runs fairly well.

However, there are a few things that would be cool but this screensaver does not provide:

-Images displayed in the screensaver are randomly selected. This doesn't work very well when it's a comic strip, since a lot of the time they will have some kind of order in which they want to be read.
-The computer I am using does not display the images very well. Sometimes colour comes out black and white, and the colour images (which I think are gifs) appear blurry! (I believe they are being scaled up instead of left in their original size).
-The colour ones are much bigger than the black and white ones. As a result, people (yes, this screensaver is quite popular!) end up with only barely enough time to read the colour ones but way too much time to read the smaller ones!

So, I am looking for a slideshow screensaver which:
-Displays images without any fancy stuff; preferably exactly like the regular image viewer, except as a screensaver.
-Displays images in order instead of randomly, and remembers what the last displayed image was. (So it doesn't start from the beginning every time).


I guess I'm looking for a bit of a miracle here, but it seems like the sort of thing that *somebody* must have wanted before me!
Do you know of any fancy, overly featureful slideshow screensavers, or directories of free screensavers for either xscreensaver or Gnome?

If necessary, I could try writing it myself. For that, though, I'll need to know how to create screensavers! (Don't worry, I know how to program in a variety of languages) :)
Do you know of any tutorials / code samples for how to get started with such things?
