### Post by dmizer on 2006-05-28
there is no tool.  the player (i had one) will only recognize sony's proprietary format ... which can only be loaded with their incredibly terrible connect player.

if you have the ability, return it in exchange for an iriver ... which will work nativly with your linux through drag and drop of mp3's (and several other formats).  the iriver is cheaper, it plays radio, the battery lasts longer, and it has text and voice recording ability right out of the box.

otherwise, the sony is a hdd device.  if you're brave, you could wipe the disk and install linux on it.  but that's the only way i could suggest getting the thing to work with your linux box.  the connect player software is terrible even in windows.  if you intend to keep it and want to update it with  your windows box, go download the sonic stage from their website.  it will break some of the features on the player, but it will be alot more stable ... sony themselves suggest it.

neither connect player nor sonic stage can be made to work in wine.  there's really not much hope for this player in linux even in the future primarily because of  it's need for proprietary format.
