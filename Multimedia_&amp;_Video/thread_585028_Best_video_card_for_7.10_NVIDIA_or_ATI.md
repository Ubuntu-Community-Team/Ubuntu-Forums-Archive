### Post by yota on 2007-10-26
I have two pc with an ati and two with nvidia, these are my two cents:

RadeonHd will be great once it's finished, but now is just not yet a viable solution for real life needs.
E.g. this is what I get if I try to use it:
> 
(EE) RADEONHD(0): This hardware is marked as EXPERIMENTAL.
        It could be harmful to try to use this driver on this card.
        To help rectify this, please send your X log to
                radeonhd_at_opensuse.org.
        or file a bug for the radeonhd component
        of the X.Org product at bugs.freedesktop.org.(II) UnloadModule: "radeonhd"
(II) UnloadModule: "int10"
(EE) Screen(s) found, but none have a usable configuration.

Fglrx works sometimes and sometimes not, (video playback on dual head, xvideo on avivo, aiglx and tv out are sometime missing).

The nvidia closed source driver is years ahead, and is the only one of this list that I have seen to work fine consistently across different hardware combinations and distributions/xorg releases.
There will be also an opensource driver for modern nvidia cards: nouveau, but is still in development.

So my bottom line is that there's no real contest: nvidia has far more polished linux support.
Hope this helps!
