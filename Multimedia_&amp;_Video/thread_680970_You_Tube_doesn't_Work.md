### Post by Chappy7777 on 2008-02-22
This is what I did to solve the "flash bug" of a couple weeks ago (I got it when I downloaded and installed the system updates that included the latest version of "flashplugin-nonfree"). I read this forum and lots of others were having the same problem; FFox was crashing when any Flash video was clicked on. Instead of seeing the video, FFox died.

I solved the problem w/Synaptic since I am still a NooB and terminal stuff is usually beyond me, or too hard to follow. Anyhow, I went to Synaptic, searched Flash, and did an uninstall of Flashplugin. Then, using Synaptic instead of the System Update (which I'd just erased) I reinstalled Flashplugin-nonfree. That solved the problem.

Maybe this will help someone.  The really wierd and "unLinux" part of the whole episode was that I had only a couple minutes before this, posted a question about downloading System Updates. Should I do them all, pick and chose, or what. The consensus was that I download and install all System Updates. No sooner did I read this, I went and downloaded the batch that had the flashplugin bug. 

At least we know that Linux and Ubuntu are works in progress all the time, not just reacting to bugs and security flaws like MS. Linux is always an attempt at building the ultimate mouse trap. so to speak.
