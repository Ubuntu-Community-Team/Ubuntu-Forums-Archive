### Post by stinkerweed999 on 2007-01-31
I recently solved this problem.  My laptop has a nVidia chipset, 6150 I think.  Pretty handy too as I'm doing a presentation at work with an external viewer today.

My x configuration (/etc/X11/xorg.conf) relies on the "twinview" feature of the nvidia driver/chipset.  I can have the laptop LCD cloned to be the same as the external VGA, or as a dual monitor where the desktop is as wide as both screens.

The external screen (whether projector, CRT or LCD) has to be connected when X starts.  If X is already up just restart with (control-alt-backspace).

The magic happens in the "Device" section.  It took a little while to get it just the way I want it.  Modify as you see fit.

### Post by stinkerweed999 on 2007-02-03
ant1060,

Try modifying your "device" section to something like mine:

--------------------------
Section "Device"
Identifier "Generic Video Card"
Driver "nvidia"
BusID "PCI:0:5:0"
Option "TwinView" "yes"

# clone view (both displays are the same)
#Option "TwinViewOrientation" "Clone"
#Option "MetaModes" "1280x800,1280x800" #16:10

# dual head view
Option "TwinViewOrientation" "RightOf" # or "LeftOf"
Option "MetaModes" "1680x1050,1280x800" # 16:10
#Option "MetaModes" "1440x900,1280x800" # 16:10
EndSection
--------------------------

Since you want clone you can ignore the "dual head view" part.

Try using one resolution for the MetaModes, something nearly any display will take like 800x600 or 1024x768.  Keep it at this resolution until you find the solution, then scale it up.

You might want to specify the BusID, it may not matter though.  Get it with "lspci |grep nvidia".

Try commenting out the monitor specifications "Option "MonitorLayout" "CRT,LFP"".

I took out the meaningless depth settings in my screen section.  There's no way I'm running anything less than 24 bit.  Doing this also simplifies things.

The best debug method is to change one and only one thing, restart X, see what happens.  If still broken, change one and only one thing, etc, rinse and repeat.  Changing multiple things at once is less efficient unless you have a very clear understanding of X, and the graphics driver.  I'm not an expert on either, but I know how to debug methodically.

As to the metamodes, the resolutions separated by commas are for different displays (see [http://nvidia.custhelp.com/cgi-bin/nvidia.cfg/php/enduser/std_adp.php?p_faqid=175&p_created=1101836633&p_sid=lbNtJcsi&p_accessibility=0&p_redirect=&p_lva=&p_sp=cF9zcmNoPTEmcF9zb3J0X2J5PSZwX2dyaWRzb3J0PSZwX3Jvd19jbnQ9NSZwX3Byb2RzPTAmcF9jYXRzPTAmcF9wdj0mcF9jdj0mcF9zZWFyY2hfdHlwZT1hbnN3ZXJzLnNlYXJjaF9mbmwmcF9wYWdlPTEmcF9zZWFyY2hfdGV4dD10d2ludmlldw**&p_li=&p_topview=1](http://nvidia.custhelp.com/cgi-bin/nvidia.cfg/php/enduser/std_adp.php?p_faqid=175&p_created=1101836633&p_sid=lbNtJcsi&p_accessibility=0&p_redirect=&p_lva=&p_sp=cF9zcmNoPTEmcF9zb3J0X2J5PSZwX2dyaWRzb3J0PSZwX3Jvd19jbnQ9NSZwX3Byb2RzPTAmcF9jYXRzPTAmcF9wdj0mcF9jdj0mcF9zZWFyY2hfdHlwZT1hbnN3ZXJzLnNlYXJjaF9mbmwmcF9wYWdlPTEmcF9zZWFyY2hfdGV4dD10d2ludmlldw**&p_li=&p_topview=1) and search for metamodes).

If you tested both displays with 800x600 then its probably not a resolution issue since every display made in the last 10 years can handle that resolution.

I don't think there's a difference between twinview yes and true, I just know the one that I use works.

Another approach is to just plug my xorg.conf in and see if it works.  If so, then tailor it to your needs.

As far as control-alt-backspace and restarting X, this should work every time.  Try to use something else in place of the projector, just to get it out of the mix initially.  Maybe the projector is trying to be smart by keeping things low power and the graphics card doesn't detect it.

Good luck.
