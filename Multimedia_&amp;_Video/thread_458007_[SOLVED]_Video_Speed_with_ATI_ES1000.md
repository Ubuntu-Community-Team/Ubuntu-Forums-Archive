### Post by The Foz on 2007-05-29
I have just installed a new Dell SC1430. It has an ATI ES1000 graphics card. I can't get Totem or VLC to display anything when I try to play video files (AVI, MPEG, etc.), but MPlayer does at least display something.

My problem is, like I have seen in many other posts, that in anything larger than "normal size", the playback is about half normal speed. All the similar posts that I have read are for different graphics cards, and none of the fixes or workarounds seem to work with my ES1000.

I am using the gl2 driver. Everything else either gives an error, displays nothing, or (with gl) displays with lots of horizontal lines and ghosting.

I have installed the fglrx driver, but my X-server won't work when I set the display driver to this, in the xorg.conf file.

I am running Feisty Fawn (7.04), on a quad-Xeon, with 1GB RAM. There should be enough resource. I have noticed that, even in "normal size", one of my CPUs is always utilised at about 100% (the graphics load doesn't seem to be shared between CPUs - no surprise there) - it seems that my system is not utilising the hardware graphics capabilities of the ATI card.

I have checked the AMD web-site, but had no luck finding a better driver.

Any ideas would be welcome.

### Post by The Foz on 2007-07-02
This is for all you people out there who also have an ATI ES1000.

After reading hundreds of forum posts, many of them several times, playing around with the xorg.conf file and installing/removing packages, what I have found out is as follows.

**_ATI Driver_**

The OpenSource driver ("ati") ***technically*** does support the ES1000. It uses the Radeon sub-driver, so it works the same whether your xorg.conf contains "ati" or "radeon". The Xorg log file shows that the ES1000 is one of the cards supported by the Radeon driver. This driver, however, at the time of writing, does not support any hardware graphics acceleration nor direct rendering. Hopefully this will change in time.

This means that it is pretty much useless for full-screen video, and also for most gaming.

**_FGLRX Driver_**

The proprietary driver from ATI does not support the ES1000. If you try it you will get a fatal error due to no "Screen" found, due to no supported "Device" found.

The proprietary driver is regularly updated by ATI, so there is a chance that proper support for the ES1000 will be available in the not too distant future.

**_Vesa Driver_**

The Vesa driver also supports the ES1000, but also with no hardware graphics acceleration and no direct rendering.

**_Conclusion_**

Our choices are:
[LIST]
[*]Either buy a graphics card that is supported with hardware graphics acceleration,
[*]Or wait until one of the drivers is updated with support for hardware graphics acceleration for the ES1000.
[/LIST]

I will update this post if/when the situation changes.

Now a x1 card is compatible with any larger slot because it simply negotiates 1 channel in a x4, x8 or x16 slot.  Similarly, a x8 card should work in a x16 slot.
