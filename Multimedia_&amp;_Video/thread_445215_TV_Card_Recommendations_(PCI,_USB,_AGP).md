### Post by newlinux on 2007-05-15
The HD capable cards I've used are the Avermedia A180 (QAM/ATSC), Kworld ATSC 110, pcHDTV 5500, and Dvico Fusion 5 RT Lite all (QAM/ATSC/NTSC). For me there isn't much of a difference in quality. The Kworld ATSC 110s were the least expensive (and thus, my favorites), the pchdtv gave me the most problems (but not much), and the Dvico was the easiest to setup. In Feisty, all of these should be fairly easy to setup. I'd go with the one you can get the least expensive.

For ATSC only, which is what you are interested in, many have gotten the Air2PC cards (ATSC only) to work well, and have got them cheap off ebay for between 20 and 40. This is probably your least expensive option if all you want is ATSC.

[http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/ATSC_PCI_Cards](http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/ATSC_PCI_Cards) for more ideas (also lists QAM and NTSC cards)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MythTV_Feisty_hardware_list](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MythTV_Feisty_hardware_list) gives setup instructions for some cards in Feisty.
