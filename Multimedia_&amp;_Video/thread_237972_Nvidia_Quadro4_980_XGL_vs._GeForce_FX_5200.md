### Post by em3raldxiii on 2006-08-17
Well, I can't answer your question directly, unfortunately.  But I can help with some food for thought perhaps.

The Quadro4 graphics card is a workstation card, which means it was targetted to computers that will be used in model development and stuff.  So I don't see why it wouldn't perform okay with XGL.

Now on a totally different point:  The FX5200 is not a particularly high performance card.  It will handle your XGL & compriz probably just peachy (I'm running on a 5600XT and it's happy).  However, you won't be able to boost the brightness settings (unless someone else knows how to do so).  Also, and perhaps more critically, the FX5200 runs pretty hot and has NO active cooling (as in, it just has a heatsink and no fan), except a few specific manufacturers' versions.  This could be a problem if you are running XGL & Compriz ... overheating.  I cooked an old FX5200, so I ended up changing its capacitors and slapping on a crappy little fan.  It was for my dad's computer so it's all good ;)

So I know I didn't answer your question, but I did move your post to the top ;)
