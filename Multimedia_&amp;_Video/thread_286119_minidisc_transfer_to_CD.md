### Post by pressman57 on 2006-10-27
Audacity is a great (imho) recording program that may already be installed. You'll need a microphone and a way to plug it into your soundcard (Radio Shack sells adapters). Or you can spend thousands and thousands of dollars for professional mixers, etc. It's really up to you.

Once you're happy with the way the track sounds you can output a .wav file that can then be burnt to a C.D. or it can output an .mp3. Some players play .mp3 data files (around 11 hours of music).

### Post by Zimmer on 2006-10-27
I have used both methods, using Audacity. Attached minidisk to output from the Church sound mixer and recorded . Then attached minidisk line out to computer sound card line in/mic socket and used Audacity to record the input in real time.

When I could not use the minidisk (the friend I had borrowed it from moved away :(   ) I took my SFF  desktop to Church and connected the mixer output to the computer line in/mic socket and recorded directly into Audacity.
