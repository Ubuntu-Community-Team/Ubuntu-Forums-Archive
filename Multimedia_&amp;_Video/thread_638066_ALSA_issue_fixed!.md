### Post by reagentz on 2007-12-11
Okay, so last week I made a post begging for help from the community after almost 2 months of trying to get Mic to work in games that have VOIP chat. I didn't get one response other than saying it was time for me to get something besides the Audigy LS I was using (go figure).

So after no help at all with my issue from the community, I made a post saying piss on it I was going back to Windows at least everything works out of the box.

Well after formatting my drive and preparing to install XP Pro, i started feeling defeated, and degraded for going back to Windows because I couldn't figure something out like sound under Linux. I booted my laptop also running Ubuntu and started looking at other distro's. I went with openSUSE and installed it, right after install and reboot everything worked, sound, mic, etc. I just didn't like how openSUSE operated, command lines, the whole interface etc. I started thinking, if openSUSE has everything working right then there is NOTHING wrong with my sound card (Audigy LS) like I was told, and like I figured there wasn't!

Being one that doesn't let something get the best of me without figuring out why it isn't working, I formated my drive again, and installed Ubuntu 7.10 amd64 build all over. As soon as I reinstalled and ran the updates I went to the ALSA Project site got the latest packages, built them, and added the modules into my kernel just like their HowTo describes. Rebooted, left everything just as Ubuntu had it preconfigured, loaded the sound recorder under Sound/Video applications and it recorded my voice.

I quickly installed Wine 0.9.50 and set it for my games, loaded a game I had on my game drive under Wine. Once I got logged in I went straight for audio/VOIP settings, BAM it was working!

So after 2 months of reading, trial and error, bashing my head against a wall, coming here asking for help (and not getting anywhere), I got it to work! So yea it took me awhile, but you know what, I've learned more doing it myself and being forced to read every single piece of documentation I could find. I wasn't forced back into Windows because of the lack of community support which makes it bitter/sweet.

So from now on I'll just keep at it until I figure it out myself, and I won't expect to get any help from a "helpful" community, Yes, I do sound bitter about it, but I am happy I got it to work myself, I just won't expect any help with anything dealing with my Linux distro like I never have gotten since the mid-90's with any Linux distro community. It would be even more helpful if people would actually try to wrap there head around an issue people come here with instead of blaming their hardware.

For all these Linux communities wanting Linux to be more widely accepted and have game/application software companies to have builds for Linux it would help to not have elitist attitudes and help people who could really use some input on their issue vs. shrugging them off with the thoughts if they cant get something to work go back to Windows. If that's not whats intended that's how it comes across in every community I've been apart of.
