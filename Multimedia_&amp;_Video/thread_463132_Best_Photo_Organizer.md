### Post by Julolidine on 2007-06-03
I have a decent amount of photos (no where close to 160,000), but I have found that both f-spot and digiKam to be excellent photomanagers.   They both allow you to download and manage photo collections.  

I haven't ran f-spot in a while, however, the Ubuntu repos were really really far behind, so I compiled the latest from source.  To me digiKam is a bit more friendly, but that will require getting the KDE base libs.  

Picassa also works.  

The one thing I can't remember is that you might have to have all the pictures in one directory strucutre for them to index correctly.  However, it has recognized my D70 and a couple other cameras, and they download automatically.

### Post by amd-64 on 2007-06-03
There are many alternatives to choose from. My personal favourites are 

f-spot
picasa (same as in windows)
digikam

For online galleries, I like gallery2 and the gallery tool embedded in konqueror.

The Gimp is the most extensive image editor in linux, but again there are others. 

The best advise is for your friend to try some of these tools (all available and free to download and use) and select what meets his needs. He won't be disappointed.
