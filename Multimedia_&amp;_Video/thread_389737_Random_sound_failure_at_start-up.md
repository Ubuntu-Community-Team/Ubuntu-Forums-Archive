### Post by gewitty on 2007-03-21
Some time ago I posted a query regarding the random failure of sound on my system when booting. I got quite a few replies from people who were experiencing the same problem, but we never identified the cause. The problem persisted and, despite having tried various fixes found on Ubuntu sites, the sound still fails regularly during start-up and can take several reboot attempts to get it going again.

In addition to my desk-top machine, I have recently installed 6.1 on my laptop and found exactly the same fault occurs. I have also corresponded with other users who report the same failure. What we have concluded is the following:

    * The fault occurs during the boot sequence
    * It appears to be a random event
    * It appears to be hardware independent (i.e. it occurs on  a variety of desktop and laptop machines of varying specification)

Which leaves the question, what is the random event? Logically, start-up should follow an identical sequence of identical start-up processes each time. But is there a deliberate (and necessary) randomizing function in the process and, if so, how does it affect the sound system and why on only some (apparently diverse) machines?
