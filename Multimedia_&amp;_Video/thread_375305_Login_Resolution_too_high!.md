### Post by webramp on 2007-03-22
I'm a beginner but the following steps fixed my problem with the login screen.
I would recommend printing the xorg.conf file before you start so you can put your 
settings back in case you have problems.

1. Find the Horizontal and Vertical refresh rates of your monitor.  I did a search on google.

2. Then edit the xorg.conf file using nano from the command line.
     sudo nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf

3. Find the section for your monitor
    Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"DELL P991"
	HorizSync	30.0 - 107.0  --- I added this line.
	VertRefresh	48.0 - 120.0 --- I added this line.
	Option		"DPMS"
EndSection

4. Next look for the "Screen" section and make sure that only the modes that your
Video Card supports is listed in the "Modes" section. 

Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"Intel Corporation 82815 CGC [Chipset Graphics Controller]"
	Monitor		"DELL P991"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		1
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480" 
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		4
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		8
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		15
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		16
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth		24
		Modes		"1024x768" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
	EndSubSection

5. With nano save the file by using the "Control" key and press the letter "O"
6.  Exit nano with "Control" key and press the letter "X"
7.  Restart your computer

If your login screen doesn't display correctly you can login at the console and then follow the instructions again and put your settings back.

### Post by AndersonCouncil on 2008-05-03
I use nVidia graphics card and I found a very simple solution. I edited my xorg.conf and scrolled down the the 'screen section':

Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Default Screen"
    Device         "nVidia Corporation NV18 [GeForce4 MX - nForce GPU]"
    Monitor        "Generic Monitor"
    DefaultDepth    24
    Option         "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"
    SubSection     "Display"
        Virtual     1792 1344
        Depth       24
        Modes      "1024x768@75" "1024x768@70" "832x624@75" "1024x768@60" "800x600@60" "1152x864@75" "800x600@75" "1280x1024@75" "800x600@72" "1280x960@60" "640x480@75" "1280x1024@60" "640x480@72" "1280x960@75" "640x480@60" "1400x1050@60" "1400x1050@75" "1600x1200@65" "1600x1200@60" "1792x1344@60"
    EndSubSection
EndSection

Where it says 'Virtual     1792 1344', I changed it to 'Virtual     1024 768' (my desired resolution). This seemed to work for me. I hope it can help someone else!
