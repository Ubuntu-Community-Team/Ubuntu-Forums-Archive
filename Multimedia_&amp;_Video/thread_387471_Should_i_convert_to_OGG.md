### Post by Lord Illidan on 2007-03-18
If you can rerip the cds which you converted to mp3s, you'll find it better quality than converting mp3 to ogg. Particularly if the tracks are at a low standard (128 kbs). I haven't experienced any sound defects from converting 320 kbs mp3s to ogg, but if you are an audiophile, you might notice some defects.

I would just leave the mp3s...

### Post by Circus-Killer on 2007-03-20
as anohter poster explained, if you want your mp3 tracks as ogg files, rather rerip them than straight up converting from mp3 to ogg. converting mp3 to ogg will convert your lesser quality mp3 to an even lower quality ogg file. its better just to rerip them all to ogg.

but this only applies if you really and truly want to use ogg. although i am very supportive of open formats, audio equipment manufacturers arent. so what file format you use depends on what you are going to play those songs on.

for example, i like to listen to my mp3s on my car radio, and my car radio only supports mp3, no ogg support. 90% of audio equipment that support compressed audio formats support mp3. so it boils down to, are you only going to listen to those ogg files on your pc, and if not, does whatever you plan on using support ogg files.

as i said, i support the idea of open formats, but if the only car radio i can get my hands on plays closed formats, then i will have to stick to those closed formats.
