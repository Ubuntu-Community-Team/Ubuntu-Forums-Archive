### Post by Turgon on 2007-04-21
To your first question: Yes, it should be an exact copy of the original cd, but some quality might still be lost under ripping (reading) and burning (however not noticible for viritualy anyone and the difference would be very very much smaller than between 256 kbps mp3 and 320 kbits mp3, where no one can tell the difference in blind-tests).

I can't really answer your second question, but I suppose Sepentine uses a common burning-backend and the quality should therefor be smilar to other burningprograms for linux. There might however be burningprograms with different backends that burns ether better or worse.

All in all I really dubt that anyone can hear the difference in what burning app you use anway, so using sepentine should be just fine.
