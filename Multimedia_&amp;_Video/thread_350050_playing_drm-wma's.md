### Post by mizar001 on 2007-01-31
Welcome to the drm world. DRM not only limits your audio/video experience as places where you can play the media, but also binds the video with your machine and OS.

Depending on the DRM technology in use, you have some quite different limitations. A wma file is bind to a media player for windows, and only the MS media player. 

Crack the DRM is ILLEGAL, even if you bought the media, you are accepting the terms of licences. This is the cause behind the DRM failure over time. 

Don't buy DRM material. If you are a MS Windows user and you are not going to change your machine, no problem. In any other case, drm is a threat to your experiences.
