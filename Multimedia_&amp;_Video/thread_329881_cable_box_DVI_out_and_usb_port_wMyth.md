### Post by majoridiot on 2007-01-02
what make/model stb do you have?  support/functionality depends on that.  i use the firewire
out on my motorola 6212 and it works very well with mythtv.  i wouldn't get your hopes up
that the USB ports will be active or useful for anything... from the reports i've seen, most
seem to be useless.  i know that my stb has 2 firewire ports, usb ports and an ethernet port
and only one single firewire port is usefull for anything.

btw, the 6212 firewire connection not only pulls in the digital audio/video stream, but it also
changes channels too...

sadly, mario is absolutely correct.  i took a look at that stb and it does not have firewire. 

however... :D

if this is an HD box from the a cable company, you are in luck.  FCC regs state that cable
companies have to provide a firewire-capable HD box if you pay for HD service and stb. of
course, most cable companies don't tell you this... you have to demand it.  fortunately, my
local cable company complied and provided the 6200 series with one of the two firewire ports
active.

i suggest you start making *firm* inquiries with customer service.  if you are paying
for HD and a "digital cable" or digital HDTV service, then per 47 CFR 76.640 (united 
states code concerning the FCC regulation of cable companies), they must provide a firewire-
enabled box (at no additional cost beyond what you pay for your current stb)

if necessary, show up in person with the regs printed out or email them to customer 
service.  plainly, do not take no for an answer- they are mandated to provide it. 

from 47 CFR 76.640:

> (4) Cable operators shall:
    (i) Effective April 1, 2004, upon request of a customer, replace any 
leased high definition set-top box, which does not include a functional 
IEEE 1394 interface, with one that includes a functional IEEE 1394 
interface or upgrade the customer's set-top box by download or other 
means to ensure that the IEEE 1394 interface is functional. 

[http://a257.g.akamaitech.net/7/257/2422/13nov20061500/edocket.access.gpo.gov/cfr_2006/octqtr/pdf/47cfr76.640.pdf](http://a257.g.akamaitech.net/7/257/2422/13nov20061500/edocket.access.gpo.gov/cfr_2006/octqtr/pdf/47cfr76.640.pdf)
