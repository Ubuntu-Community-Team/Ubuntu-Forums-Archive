### Post by kebes on 2007-02-03
Well your xorg looks fine to me... but you could try generating a new one and see if it works. Backup your current one, and then run "xorgconfig" (or use "Xorg -config" or "xorgcfg" ...) to have it create a new xorg.conf for you. In the setup program you can pick your hardware and the resolutions you want to support.

Like I said your current xorg looks fine, but maybe by comparing the newly autogenerated one you can spot the problem.

### Post by blackened on 2007-02-03
You need to generate a proper modeline for the monitor section. Do this in the terminal with
```
gtf 1680 1050 60
```
Insert the modeline generated by that command at the bottom of the "Monitor" section.
Then change the mode in your "Screen" section to "1680x1050@60".
