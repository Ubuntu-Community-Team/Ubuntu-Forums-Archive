### Post by disturbed1 on 2006-06-04
I second the FX5500. I have one of those too ;). It will play some games just fine, like UT2004, TuxRacer..... It actually performs on par, if not better, than an ATI x1600 in Linux. The FX5500 works out of the box with the nv driver, and even better with the Nvidia closed source driver.

For sound, just about all of the entry level consumer cards will work well. Most of these are based on a CMI chipset and are 99.9% supported. Audio quality and actual volume is a little less in Linux, so make sure you have a good set of speakers. The cheaper SBLive! 5.1 is emulated. Support was iffy for that card, not sure if it's improved lately or not.

I use the built in nvidia sound on my mother board, on other systems I have a Yamaha oplsax (ISA), and a Creative ES1373 card. The Yamaha card sounds the best, but it's old, and they don't make cards anymore.
