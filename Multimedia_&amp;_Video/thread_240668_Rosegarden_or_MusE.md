### Post by djst on 2006-08-21
Has anyone had any experience in actually using Rosegarden or MusE to create music, similar to Cubase/Reason on Windows? And I don't just mean using some lame built-in MIDI sounds, but actually using it with professional quality samples and synth/effect plugins.

I am interested to hear which program(s) is/are best suited as a replacement for the great Reason studio. And I especially would like to hear from people with actual experience in using these programs.

Basically, I'm trying to figure out if it's worth the effort to try to learn how to make things work under Linux, or if it's best to just buy proprietary software that's known to just work.

I have been playing around with Rosegarden and its synth plugins and actually managed to produce some good quality sound, but it's a bit complex and I still don't know how to e.g. control the plugins through the sequencer (for example dynamically change the cutoff frequency of a low-pass filter plugin), so everything is pretty static. I.e. nothing changes over time during playback, every plugin setting is static.

I should add that I found out how to get things like JACK (the framework around which most audio apps in Linux are built) to work by reading the simple tutorial on UbuntuStudio. It explains how to connect the output of one app to the input of another, and thus creating a virtual studio rack based on separate software. For example, you can direct the audio output from app1 to the input of app2, which could be an equalizer or whatever. Then the output of app2 could be sent to your sound card so you could actually hear something. It's pretty cool actually, but as I said, I have yet to find out a way to control the sound effects through MIDI/sequencer instructions.
