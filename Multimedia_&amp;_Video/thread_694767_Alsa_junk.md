Hi everyone,

I'm just done reading an article  which, if the writer is correct, is very disturbing.
[Here]("http://insanecoding.blogspot.com/2007/05/sorry-state-of-sound-in-linux.html") is the link.

What is basically comes down to is that  ALSA doesn't work as well as the "new" OSS which happens to be closed source (which was the main reason for developing ALSA in the first place anyway).
ALSA seems to be complicated to program for compared to OSS (I have no personal experience developing for ALSA), it's only available for Linux (Advanced **Linux** Sound Architecture), and of course we all know the ESD / aRts "catastrophe" (for those who don't know: try using a KDE sound app like Amarok at the same time as, e.g. Totem Movie Player, it most probably won't work :( )
I already hear some of you thinking "yeah but PulseAudio is coming in Hardy:confused:".
Very true, but all the extra layers don't do the sound latencies any good unfortunately, and the sound  infrastructure as a whole becomes unnecessarily complicated.

These are my thoughts on the matter, and I'm curious to hear yours, my fellow Ubuntu users!
