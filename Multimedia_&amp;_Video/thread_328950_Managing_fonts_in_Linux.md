### Post by Unterseeboot_234 on 2006-12-31
I missed not having a font management program like Adobe Type Manager in Linux. The Adobe product installs a symlink into the fonts folder of the OS and lets you keep your fonts organized anywhere you want to put them on your hard drive. Important if you want to keep from dragging down your computer resources.

I found an open source product called Fonty Python. Download the .tar from the following link. 
[**fonty python**]("http://wiki.wxpython.org/index.cgi/FontyPython")

There is an Info link at the bottom to get to the archives. Download, untar and ReadMe. The author includes what *buntu needs. 

The concepts to use this software are a little less obvious at first, but after you try it by making a folder with your new font(s) and linking it in a "suitcase" -- the author calls it a "bog".  Log off/log on ubuntu and it works. Be sure to do a font cache update whenever you make up a new fonts folder. You can click fonts on or off in fonty python, restart whatever software you were using and the fonts menu would have been updated. Beautiful. Works in 64-bit even. The only limitation is we must use TrueType fonts.
