### Post by acoustibop on 2007-12-01
If you don't want to risk it, install the repository driver (currently 8.37.6) with the Restricted Drivers Manager.  The ATI drivers usually take several months to find their way into the repositories ATM; it's possible this may change in future as ATI seem to be adopting a more proactive stance towards the open source community.

The current drivers appear to install very easily, however.  The method that always works for me is the [Unofficial Wiki for the ATI Linux Driver]("http://http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Main_Page") one - it looks complicated, but it's really just a matter of copy-and-pasting lines into your terminal; there are only a few simple decisions to take, eg have you installed the ATI drivers previously etc.
