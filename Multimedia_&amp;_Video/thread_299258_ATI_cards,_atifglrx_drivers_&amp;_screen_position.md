### Post by whatever on 2006-11-14
> **igknighted said:**
> Instead of the 'ati' driver you may want to try 'radeon', as it is a little more capable... 
as the 'ati' driver will load the 'radeon' driver anyway if it detects a radeon card, it does not matter at all if you put 'ati' or 'radeon' in your xorg.conf. loading radeon directly only helps if the ati driver fails to detect your card.
