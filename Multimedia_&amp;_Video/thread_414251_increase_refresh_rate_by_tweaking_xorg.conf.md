### Post by 67GTA on 2007-04-19
You can boot into recovery mode and run "sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg" 
This will take you through the steps to set a lot of options. You can select "advanced" at the monitor section and set them to what you want. It would be a good idea to have your monitor specs handy before you start. Just be sure when you select the screen resolutions that none are marked higher than it will support. If you are not sure about a setting, just leave it as it is marked.
