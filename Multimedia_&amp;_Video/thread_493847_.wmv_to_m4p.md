### Post by morgan_greywolf on 2007-07-06
You need to look into a program called [transcode]("http://www.transcoding.org/cgi-bin/transcode").  It'll take and convert between various video container formats and codecs.  Converting video is not so straightforward as you might think and there is a LOT to learn so read the wiki.

For instance, video has a *[container format]("http://www.transcoding.org/cgi-bin/transcode?Container_Formats")*, which holds both video coded in a particular *[codec]("http://www.transcoding.org/cgi-bin/transcode?Codec")* along with an audio portion that is also coded in a particular codec.  So just saying 'convert from wmv to mp4' doesn't really tell the whole story.  For instance, an MP4 file can contain video in MPEG1, MPEG2 or DivX or XviD formats, along with audio in MP3 or various other codecs.

There are various frontends to transcode floating around, check the repositories.  There's also a tool called Avidemux which is a video editor.  It's in the Ubuntu APT repositories as well.
