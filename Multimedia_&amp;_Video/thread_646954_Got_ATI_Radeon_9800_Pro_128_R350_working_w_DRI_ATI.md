### Post by l33ch on 2007-12-21
[FONT=Arial][SIZE=4][COLOR=DarkGreen][SIZE=2]Hey All,

I'm a novice at Linux and even more so with the Ubuntu distro. I have an ATI 9800 Pro 128 (R350 GPU) and for the last few days I've been having problems using the closed source ATI Radeon 7.11 or as of yesterday 7.12 driver on refresh rates over 60hz.  I could get the driver installed and the ATI control center would come up, but I couldnt change the refresh rates above 60. Whenever I try to change the monitor and reboot is causes it not to load into X with the driver and loads the VESA driver instead.  Before this issue, I couldn't even get glxinfo  to show ATI.  For those of you having these issues, like glxinfo showing mesa instead of ATI, and refresh/resolution changing issues, this is what I did  to get to where I am now. I did this on a fresh install of Ubuntu 7.10:

Follow the directions on the following link to install the ATI 7.12 drivers. Follow method 2 for creating .deb packages and installing. 
[/SIZE][/COLOR][/SIZE][/FONT][COLOR=DarkGreen][http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Gutsy_Installation_Guide]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/")
When you get to Configuring the driver, reboot (not reload X). Go back to that section and it will have you issue sudo --initial aticonfigs commands. It will also offer an alternative of editing your xorg.conf. I just followed along after the reboot and issued the sudo aticonfig --initial and sudo aticonfig --overlay-type=Xv commands like the guide says and reboot.

Now the problems I had:

After the reboot glxinfo and fglrxinfo showed ATI..Yay! So I goto screens and graphics. Video card shows fglrx and monitor Plug N Play 1600x1200.  Whenever I tried to change the monitor here to anything else , like Dell p1110 (my monitor),or generic monitor and reboot, my monitor will click through the modes and I would get the low graphics mode error.  

The ATI driver will make entrys in your xorg.conf. Because the 9800 (and alot of other radeon cards) support duel displays, it detects 2 Monitors. On my xorg.conf file I had:

[COLOR=DarkOrange]Section "Monitor"
    Identifier   "Generic Monitor"
    HorizSync    30.0 - 70.0
    VertRefresh  50.0 - 160.0
    Option        "DPMS"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
    Identifier   "aticonfig-Monitor[0]"
    Option        "VendorName" "ATI Proprietary Driver"
    Option        "ModelName" "Generic Autodetecting Monitor"
    Option        "DPMS" "true"
EndSection[/COLOR]

The monitor "aticonfig-Monitor[0]" is ati's weird way of implimenting the sync rates and resolutions. What it DID NOT DO was put in sync rates for the main display "aticonfig-Monitor[0]. So this is what I did:

[COLOR=DarkOrange]Section "Monitor"
    Identifier   "aticonfig-Monitor[0]"
    HorizSync    30.0 - 121.0
    VertRefresh  50.0 - 160.0    
    Option        "VendorName" "ATI Proprietary Driver"
    Option        "ModelName" "Generic Autodetecting Monitor"
    Option        "DPMS" "true"
[/COLOR]

I simply pasted the Horiz and Vert under Identifier, saved and rebooted. After reboot, no more weird monitor mode finding. Came up to the login screen, logged in and went to the ATI control center this time. On my install applications->accessories->ATI Catalyst Control Center. From there you should be able to change your resolution and refresh rates accordingly.

Again these are the steps to get where I am now. Below is my entire xorg.conf if it helps anyone. I was so excited to get this working, and these forums have helped me out so much I wanted to share what I learned and what helped.
