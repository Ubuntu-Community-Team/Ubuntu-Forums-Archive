### Post by oni5115 on 2008-01-14
I've got an XPS m170 laptop, and using the composite video and s/pdif audio adapter, I was able to get audio out just fine.

Select preferences, and scroll down the list of tracks to be visible.  Make sure you have IEC958 and IEC958 Playback AC97-SPCA checked.
On the switches tab check "IEC958".
I mute or turn down the PCM (or it plays on laptop speakers AND receiver) and AC97-SPCA (only plays on laptop) volumes and now it is playing through my receiver perfectly.  =)

I'm not sure what model you have, or what specific cable you need though.  (I know the XPS m1710 had both a composite video and an RGB based cord, and the Dell site DID NOT show pictures.  I ended up getting both of them, and only the composite video one worked.  (Yes, they were marked for the m1710, and worked ono the m170... no model of the part was labled for the m170.)
