### Post by Unicow on 2007-07-16
I'd like to make an apology right off the bat if this is in the wrong forum; I hopped around looking at different forum descriptions, and this one seemed most apt. If there's somewhere better to be, just tell me and I'll get out of your hair.

In a nutshell, I am running World of Warcraft under the latest version of Wine. I had been having a whole lot of problems with World of Warcraft crashing every time I hit a loading screen, though, after rereading through the linux/wine page on WoWwiki, I found that adding this one line to my config.wtf file ```
SET gxApi "opengl"
``` made everything a whole lot better. Suddenly World of Warcraft was running perfectly and sharper to boot. 

A whole new issue was raised the first time I alt-tabbed out of World of Warcraft, though -- everything looked a good deal sharper, with lines much less blurred and everything showing up much clearer. It's not so blurry without wine that I was having trouble seeing before this new change was enacted, but now that it has come to pass, I'm definitely able to tell the difference between using my computer while WoW's running in the background and when it's not.

Since it's not viable at all for me to run World of Warcraft in the background 24/7 (:P) I was wondering if maybe there was some way I could tell my computer to do what it was doing while WoW was open to do it all the time? I've no clue at all really what's making things show up better, and I'm crossing my fingers hoping that someone else has heard of this happening.

Can anyone please help? thank you in advance for anything offered.
