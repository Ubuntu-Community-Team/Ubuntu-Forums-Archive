I just bought one of the Sony NWZ-S618F 8 gig Flash walkmans, because you can load music and videos on it through drag 'n' drop on any operating system.

I have one problem though. I want to convert one of my DVDs to play on this thing, but it always says that the file is damaged or invalid. Here is what the manual says the video file has to be:

File Format: MP4 file format, "Memory Stick" video format
Codec: Video: AVC (H.264/AVC); File extension: .mp4, .m4v
             Audio: AAC-LC

And elsewhere it says the max bitrate (which I'm well below) and maximum resolution of 320x240, which I am matching.

I tried K9Copy's MPEG-4 option, which didn't work. I then tried AcidRip with lavc MPEG-4 and MP3 audio, saving to .avi format. Then I tried AcidRip with lavc MPEG-4 and AAC audio, saving to .avi or .mpg format. Neither of these work - the player keeps complaining that it is a damaged or invalid file.

Has anyone else got one of these walkmans and been able to encode video to work on it? If so, how in the heck? I should also mention that I tried using Acidrip to encode using x264 (as the player also supports h.264), but it just chewed my CPU and didn't seem to be actually saving anything to any file I could see. Any other frontends I should be trying?
