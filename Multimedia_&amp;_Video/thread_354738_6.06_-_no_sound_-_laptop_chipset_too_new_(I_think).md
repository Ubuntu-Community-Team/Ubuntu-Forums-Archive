### Post by LMelior on 2007-02-06
As it says in the title, my new laptop has the ATI South Bridge 460 chipset, and on the soundcard matrix, ALSA only lists support up to ATI SB450.  I think (not completely sure) that's the one that Ubuntu is trying to use.  Ubuntu is also trying to use SigmaTel STAC9200, and my laptop has the audio listed as SigmaTel 9200.  

As close as the match seems to be, I can't seem to coerce any sound out of my system at all.  I checked every volume setting I could find and made sure nothing was muted anywhere, and I tried running totem as root and playing some sample media, but no luck with the sound.  Everything else worked right out of the box - including the built-in wireless card.

When I right-click on the volume control and go to preferences, it lists two devices:
HDA ATI SB (Alsa Mixer)    <-- I think this is a.k.a. the SB450 (on the soundcard matrix)
SigmaTel STAC9200 (OSS mixer)

So what are my options?  Do I just wait until the ALSA folks can catch up?
