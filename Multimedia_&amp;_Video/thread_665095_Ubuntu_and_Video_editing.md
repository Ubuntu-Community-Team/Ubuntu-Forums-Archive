### Post by jesushero on 2008-01-11
Most of the stuff in the repositories (Synaptic Package Manager stuff) is not really good. I'm into video editing myself, but not very demanding. I have found Cinelerra to be VERY good. It is of a very high professional standard, with loads of functions and effects. If you are used to Premiere (I am, because of work) it is quite straightforward to use. The Gimp (photoshop-like image editor) also has some video stuff that I have never tried, I guess it can be used to manipulate frames or so. It works great with images, I can safely say I like it more than photoshop although most of my friends disagree with me on that. 

Maybe you should go for Ubuntu Studio instead of Desktop. I've got 7.10 Gutsy Studio. It has some extras for video and audio programs, libraries and from what I understand, Kernel differences. It comes with loads of Video, Audio and Image stuff pre-installed, which can be very useful if you're not familiar with the software.

Also, Cinelerra was not on any of the default repositories from what I can remember, I got it off the developer's website if I remember correctly.

### Post by nhandler on 2008-01-13
I recently stumbled upon kdenlive. It works pretty well for what I do. Here is a list of features from their site:

> Here is an incomplete feature list for Kdenlive

    * Multi track editing
    * Accepts many audio / video / image formats: mpeg, avi, dv, vob, wav, ogg, mp3, gif (non animated), jpg, png, svg, ...
    * Export to many formats: mpeg, dv, vob, realvideo, flash, theora, wav, mp3, xvid, quicktime, ...
    * Audio and video thumbnails
    * Basic audio / video effects: blur, sepia, volume, brightness, ...
    * Basic transitions: crossfade, push, picture in picture
    * Real time preview of all effects
    * Add markers and guides to easily manage your project
    * Copy & paste of clips, effects and transitions
    * Easy firewire dv capture
    * Customizable layouts
    * Desktop integration (add files to your project with drag & drop)
    * Multi track view (preview monitor is split in 4 with one track in each part)


Even though it is a kde app, it will run fine on gnome.
