### Post by Dubstar_04 on 2007-08-26
ok a bit of a description as my last post was a little vague.

what mythnettv does is allow you to set up subscriptions to your favorite podcasts / webtv / movie trailers you name it, if its on the web and you know the address of it then it can be used. It will look at the specified address(s) and find all the video content assoiated with the tile supplied in the subscription, it will then download all videos for you and add them in to the mythtv recorded tv page on your frontend along with a description of the show and the date that the show was added to the web.

it also runs user jobs on the downloads, however this is limited to detecting commercials at the moment.  

check out the screenshot of my recorded videos with the poscasts from revision3, and the indigital show that can be found here.

[http://revision3.com/indigital]("http://revision3.com/indigital") 

feel free to add more video locations to the thread.

### Post by am_pcguy on 2007-11-29
It is different from Mythstream because it pulls the file to your local machine, insted of streaming on request.  This allows you to download higher quality videos than you would get with streaming video.   Also there are a lot of webcasts using flash for streaming video content and I don't think that is supported with Mythstream.  

Many webcasts have a Xvid, Dvix, or other video format feed, you could just subscribe and your MythTV box would have the shows as they are released.
