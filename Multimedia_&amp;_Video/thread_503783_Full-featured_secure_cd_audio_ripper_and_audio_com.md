### Post by VCSkier on 2007-07-18
Back in the day, I was hardcore EAC (Exact Audio Copy) and foobar2000 user.  It helped me sleep better at night, knowing that I had images of my entire cd collection backed up on an external hdd, and that at a moment's notice, I could burn a bit-perfect copy of any cd I owned, including all pre-track data and gaps.  Also, those lossless images served as the source of my lossy copies (ogg or mp3) of my audio collection for playback from my local hdd on my laptop, or for transferring to my portable DAP.  Using foobar2000, I could quickly and easily do this transcoding process of my entire collection to whatever audio compression format I wanted (FLAC, Vorbis, MP3, Wavpack...) while retaining all tags in a standards compliant way.

I've looked in the past for some FLOSS alternatives to EAC and foobar2000, and I haven't had much luck.  I'm hoping there have been some recent developments (with the recent release of Ubuntu Studio, and some other similar projects gaining popularity).  Is there anything new?  What do you guys use?  I'd even be okay with using something not FLOSS, but I don't want to have to depend on Wine.  I'd rather compromise on my methodology alittle, before having to use a Windows program through Wine, so I'm basically just looking for the best-available solution.

Thanks guys.
