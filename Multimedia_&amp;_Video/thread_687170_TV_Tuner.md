### Post by manimal347 on 2008-02-05
BT878-based cards are old and have a mediocre picture, but they work out of the box on most Linux distributions. Seriously, power down the computer, install the PCI card, turn the machine back on, install TV Time, and you should be watching either cable or broadcast TV. Usually, it's that simple. I did say the picture is mediocre, and it is, but in truth, it won't look any worse than your typical Chinese bargain-brand flatpanel. In general, look for cards/chipsets that are supported by Video For Linux.

I bought this for about $25...
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814122180](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814122180)
And can verify it works under Linux using Debian Etch and Lenny, as well as several Ubuntu flavors. I never tried it under Gutsy, but it should work swell. It might still not be a horrible buy, as apparently, that glut of $20 TV tuners I seem to remember seems to have disappeared.
