### Post by wylfing on 2006-03-02
Others might have different things to say, but I find nVidia's Linux drivers to be quite good. I wouldn't worry at all about installing an nVidia card. Ubuntu usually has up-to-date drivers available in the repositories, so you can install them very easily.

As far as sound goes...eh, this is kind of dicey. I honestly think anything based on the CMI 8738 chip is the right thing to do. Yes, this is a budget chip, but you can get 100% working 6-channel sound from it without banging your head against a wall -- it just works.
