### Post by cronholio on 2006-11-29
To fix the resolution issue, you will have to add horizontal and vertical sync rates in the monitor section of your xorg.conf, like this:

```
HorizSync     31-101
    VertRefresh    60-160
```

Consult your monitor's manual or Google for the right specs, too high settings might fry it.

### Post by mannih2001 on 2006-12-08
> **kweiner said:**
> I have a new Dell Dimension 9200c with an Intel G965 chipset and Intel GMA X3000 onboard graphics chip.  The monitor resolution is supposed to be 1680 x 1050, but I am only able to set it to a max of 1280 x 1024 using **System > Preferences > Screen Resolution**.

The driver for intel chips (i810) will only be able to switch to resolutions that are listed in your chips' bios. Chances are that your bios simply doesn't list 1680x1050.
However, you can install 915resolution and then edit /etc/default/915resolution. 915resolution can modify the list of resolutions and you should then be able to use 1680x1050.

Good luck,
Manni
