### Post by ron999 on 2007-12-22
Hi
There's a website called youtubehack.
This allows you to enter the URL of the youtube page. (Use copy and paste).
Then you can download the video in various formats.
There is also an option to download just the soundtrack as an mp3 file instead.
Then K3b will allow you to burn mp3 files to a CD.

This is the link:- [http://www.youtubehack.net/]("http://www.youtubehack.net/")

:guitar:

If you want to split the sound from a video that's already on your computer then Avidemux will do it, if it's in a suitable format. Use Audio > Save...

### Post by onero on 2007-12-25
Personally, I just download the file to flv (using any number of Firefox extensions --- my favorite is UnPlug), and then use VLC's transcoding wizard to save the audio. I use either Vorbis or MP3 for the codec, and Ogg for encapsulation,which works fine.

EDIT: You can use VLC to save the audio for any of your other videos, as well. And since it's cross-platform, you can even use it when on Windows instead of commercial RealPlayer :D
