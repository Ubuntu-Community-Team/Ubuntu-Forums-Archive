### Post by wouterla on 2007-05-29
AVI files are (usually) compressed with an mpeg-4 algorithm (such as divx or xvid), which is much more efficient then the mpeg-2 used on DVDs.

A typical DVD-R can store 4.7GB, which is enough for about 2 hours of video, if you don't need too high a quality.

Remember: store-bought DVDs are usually DVD-9 (dual layer), which store 9GB. And that's filled pretty much up with one movie and a few extras...
