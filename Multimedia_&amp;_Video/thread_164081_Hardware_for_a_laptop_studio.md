### Post by dolson on 2006-04-22
A good MIDI keyboard is the E-MU Xboard49. It works, plug & play with Ubuntu. This is the one I use. There are other ones that work in Linux too, but when I was evaluating for my own use, the E-MU Xboard's keybed got much better reviews than any of the M-Audio or Edirol boards, and so I bought it (despite being told that it did NOT work in Linux, both by older ALSA mailing list posts and from Creative/E-MU tech support). I was more than happy when I plugged it in (for USB power) and it showed up in JACK Control's MIDI list. :)

A decent little mixer that I have is the Yamaha MG-10/2 mixing console. It is very basic, but it works great.

There are pics of my gear up at my site: [www.rivironline.com](www.rivironline.com) or on my profile page of my wiki, if you wanna see pics of my stuff.

### Post by dolson on 2006-04-27
Let me know what you think of the Xboard if you get that one. I like the keybed, it has synth action and aftertouch and velocity sensitivity.

If I had to do it over again, I wouldn't likely change anything, but I would read reviews just as I did before, from non-Linux-using musicians so I know I'm getting something quality.

The only real issue raised with the Xboard was the buttons are sorta loose/rattly, but uh, at least keys don't go dead after a year for no apparent reason. The loose buttons don't cause any issues for me. I didn't even need to read the manual to figure out how to program the 16 knobs to various CCs, it was really easy.

According to reviews I was reading when making my decision, the quality from one Edirol device to the next varied greatly, so it would really depend on the model you choose to buy. Just read some reviews at Harmony Central, zSounds or whatever to see what others think.

The M-Audio boards I would say to stay away from. Many people have reported some keys just going dead after 6 months or a year. That's not something you want to buy into, I would think.
