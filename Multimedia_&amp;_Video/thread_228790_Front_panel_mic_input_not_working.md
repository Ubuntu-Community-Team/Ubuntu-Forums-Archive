### Post by Scunizi on 2006-08-03
I've often wrestled with this issue depending on the motherboard and the connector(s) that fit onto the audio header for the front panel.  What I've found is some motherboards automatically turn off the rear connectors when there's something plugged into the front.  If this is the case you can't use the software switch in Ubuntu.  If this isn't the case, check the wiring for the front panel.  If it's one molded plug that fit on the audio header you can't mess it up.  However if the front panel header connectors are all seperate wires it's often difficult to match the label on the connector with the appropriate pin on the motherboard.  Some trial and error might be in order.
