### Post by tigerpants on 2006-07-09
In my experience, Linux is generally not very hot at ripping CD's. The best programme I found on Windows was Audiograbber. I downloaded it recently to my dapper install, and installed through wine. relived to say it works a treat, rips and encodes at 32x on my cd rom drive, which is far faster than Grip or any other linux based rippers I've used on my system. So, if you're having problems with linux based rippers, try an audiograbber install. What have you got to lose?

Audiograbber 1.3 is available here: [V1.3]("http://www.audiograbber.com-us.net/download.html")

Once downloaded and installed fire up a terminal:

winecfg

Drives tab: make sure you have your CD ROM drives path correctly configured. Click advanced button and select your CD rom drive from the list and then CD-ROM from the dropdown in Type.

Then hit the applications tab and add application. Map to audiograbber.exe then select windows XP or Windows 2000 for Windows version dropdown at the bottom.

Et viola! Hit the desktop icon for audiograbber, and away you go. You can map to lame codec on your linux install, or if you are having problems with that then you should be able to download the lame files for window, stick them in the audiograbber folder in wines C: drive and audiograbber should be able to map the codec from there (not tried that yet).

Happy ripping.
