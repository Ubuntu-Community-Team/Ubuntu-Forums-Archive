### Post by tommohawk on 2006-07-01
Here is a section of my xorg.conf file.  I think if you modify yours in a similar way to this then it might solve your problem.

i had a similar issue before I added the KernelModuleParm line and the useinternalAGPGART line.

```
Section "Device"
	Identifier  "ATI Technologies, Inc. Radeon Xpress 200M (RS480)"
	Driver      "fglrx"
	Option	    "(null)"
	Option	    "VideoOverlay" "on"
	Option	    "OpenGLOverlay" "off"
	Option	    "useInternalAGPGART" "no"
	Option      "KernelModuleParm" "agplock=0"
	BusID       "PCI:1:5:0"
EndSection

Section "Device"
	Identifier  "ATI Graphics Adapter 0"
	Driver      "fglrx"
	Option	    "(null)"
	Option	    "VideoOverlay" "on"
	Option	    "OpenGLOverlay" "off"
	Option	    "useInternalAGPGART" "no"
	Option      "KernelModuleParm" "agplock=0"
	BusID       "PCI:1:5:0"
EndSection
```

### Post by pRedat0r on 2006-07-03
I've got a 9800 Pro and have been having blank screen issues for months now, and have gone through several driver revisions, none have fixed it.  I did find a workaround for the blank screen at login though.  I added a vga=791 (or whatever other resolution value) to my grub config, so that it will boot the kernel with that video option.  I am at work and dont have my system to look at, im sure if you search on that parameter you will find tons of posts on it.  I think that somehow that kicks the driver into the correct mode at boot time, where the default doesnt do that.

My problem was that usplash would show fine when booting, but once X tried to start, and display the login, the screen would go blank.  I have a 2 monitor config, my CRT would be fine, but the login screen was on my LCD and that would stay blank.  Once i used that parameter everything worked fine in this situation.

I still have an issue that once my LCD monitor goes to sleep, it wont wake up unless I do CTRL-ALT-F1 to switch to a virtual terminal, then ALT-F7 to switch back to the graphical desktop (on my system I have to use left ALT for some reason to switch back -- might be the keyboard I am using).  Most times, if I time it right, that will trick the monitor into coming back on.  However, if I switch to the virtual console, and wait until the LCD comes on, then switch back, it will turn back off -- I have to switch, then switch back a second later, and it remains on..  Goofy, but at least it works..

Hope that helps.

Edit:  FYI I havent tried the new 26.18 driver yet, waiting for it to be in the repos -- it was annoying having a driver update come through the repos and blow up my manually installed driver last time..
