### Post by Dark Neutron on 2007-12-20
While, it seems this was a non-problem caused by myself.

In the boot screen, there were two kernels listed, 2.6.22-14-generic and 2.6.20-16-generic. When I briefly glanced at it during boot, I only looked at the last two digits and thought that the kernel had been updated and booted that one. Being the older kernel, it obviously did not work with my newer configuration files and drivers.

Note to self: look before leap.

Sorry if I distracted anyone with this absurdity. :oops:
