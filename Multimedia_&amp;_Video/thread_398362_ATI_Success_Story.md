I installed Ubuntu about a month ago and fell in love with it. But it was a love/hate kind of thing because I couldn't get my ATI card to work properly.  I finaly gave up on getting the graphics card to do what I wanted it to do.  It seems that it wouldn't turn the direct rendering ON.  Eventually I gave up on that.

I did a major overhaul on my computer yesterday. I installed a new motherboard, CPU and a new harddrive and reinstalled Ubuntu. After the live CD booted up but BEFORE installing it to the hard drive I decided to check the grapics. Much to my supprise, direct rendering was already ON!

I loaded up the Planet Penguin Racer just for a test the FPS. I got 50+ FPS @ 800 x 600 screen resolution! It more than DOUBLED after installing Ubuntu to the hard drive.

Here is my OLD gear:
Motherboard: Soyo SY-K7VME chipset VIA KM400/VT8235 
Processor: 1.7 AMD

New Gear:
PCChips P32G motherboard chipset VIA P4M800Pro / 8237
Processor: 3.46 Celeron D
