### Post by ratbagradio on 2007-05-01
There are a few players you can install but the easiest I find for Mp3 access --and a darn good player -- is Amarok. Check it out among your Add.Remove options. When you load Amarok for the first time it will offer to download and install Mp3 stuff so you can play that copyrighted format.

It's a very good player with a lot of great widgets...I love it. 

As for video...check out Penguin TV and the Democracy Player but Democracy was having a few issues with Feisty however the site you were referred to offers work around stuff and I'm having troubles with Penguin loading.. so don't rush where newbies go too quickly. None of my other pre-installed  vid players work either. 

But if these things are over whelming  UbuntuStudio will be out, touch wood, on Friday and that "may" resolve some of this confusion you have to face when you want to engage with multimedia on Ubuntu.

I'm new to Ubuntu too so I know it's a rocky road to find your way with media on Ubuntu as it is a  weak area because of the propriety formats used  and the language employed in FAQ talk is very confusing. But Amarok has useful  & helpful forums and here you may also find helpful advice too for when you get stuck. But I think you'd also get more assistance in Absolute Beginner Talk.
