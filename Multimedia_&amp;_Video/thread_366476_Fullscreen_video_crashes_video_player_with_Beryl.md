### Post by lucas42 on 2007-03-04
I also had a problem with mplayer fullscreen & beryl.  Try changing the video output driver in your mplayer settings.  in ~/.mplayer/config change "vo=x11" to "vo=xv".  Hope that helps.

### Post by alexnihilo on 2007-05-13
In the same idea, start your prog. in a console to see if mplayer REALLY use x11:

Indeed, I also had some problems with mplayer and beryl when trying to play some video streamings with firefox, for instance. Mplayer appeared briefly and closed immediatly... I had to close Beryl to watch these kind of videos, even with x11 driver (Ximage/shm) configured in mplayer.

Solution: launching firefox in a console, and playing such a video, I saw that mplayer used by firefox was still using xv driver. So I edited the file /etc/mplayer/mplayer.conf and replace this line 

    > #-vo=xv

with this one

    > -vo=gl

if I put x11 instead of gl, then I can't resize the window (bug)

now, it's OK ! I hope the tip can be useful !
