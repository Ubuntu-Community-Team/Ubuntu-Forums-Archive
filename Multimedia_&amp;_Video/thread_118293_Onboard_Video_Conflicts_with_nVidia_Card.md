### Post by AvatarofVirgo on 2006-01-16
I have one of those funky mother boards from a old eMachine I got back in 2000.  This board doesn't give you the option to disable the onboard video via jumpers or BIOS.  It's suppose to auto disable the onboard video when ever I have a video card in there.  Right now I'm on SUSE 10 and I have it setup fine.  No matter what distribution I've tried they all give me the same problem.  When installing SUSE, Kubuntu or any other distribution the initial setup works fine.  But when it gets time for it to auto detect my video and monitor the screen goes black and I get a blinking curser at the top left of my screen.

Here's how I got by that and fixed it in SUSE.  After installing SUSE and the screen goes black, I turn the system off.  I remove the card, reboot into safemode and log on root.  Then I run sax2 and add my monitor and video manually.  I save it with out testing and shutdown SUSE.  Then I put the card back in and reboot and I got working video with my card.

The whole problem has to do with my funky motherboard not working right when any Linux distribution tries to auto detect the video.  So I basically end up setting up the x11 config file manually as autodetection is out of the question.

I have a older hard drive in my system that I want to test out Kubuntu on.  It installed fine except for the same video problem.  I have a nVidia GeForce2 MX400 card. I tried and I got all kinds of new problems, good thing I made a backup of the old file.
