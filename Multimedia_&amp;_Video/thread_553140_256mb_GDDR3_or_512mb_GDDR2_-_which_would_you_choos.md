### Post by hypersire on 2007-09-17
Hi all, I am building a new Linux HTPC and I have narrowed my video card choices down to 2:

ASUS EN8600GT SILENT/HTDP/256M GeForce 8600GT 256MB 128-bit GDDR3 PCI Express x16 HDCP Ready SLI Supported HDCP Video Card for $129

OR

ASUS EN8500GT SILENT/HTP/512M GeForce 8500GT 512MB 128-bit GDDR2 PCI Express x16 HDCP Ready Video Card for $95

I realize that the 512mb card is an older model with older memory (GDDR2) and it does not support SLI. However, I am not a gamer at all - al this PC will be for is viewing 1080p content.

Which would you choose given that the price is very close? Is it better to have less but faster memory or more slower memory for viewing HD content?

Thanks a lot!!

### Post by kebes on 2007-09-17
I would also go for the 512 MB card. It does everything you need. If you are not going to use SLI, then you are paying a premium for nothing.

I think you'll notice more of a speed boost from doubling the memory (whereas some people say that there is no noticeable speed difference between DDR3/DDR2 in current setups).
