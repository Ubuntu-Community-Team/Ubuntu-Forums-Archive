### Post by happy-and-lost on 2006-11-16
I've got one of those, and the MIDI output doesn't resemble any kind of USB port, but I've never used MIDI equipment *shrugs*. It's a great beginner's keyboard, but, personally, I'd recommend splashing out a little extra on a keyboard with touch sensitive keys. It's impossible to really add dynamics to your music with this one because however hard or for however long you hit a key, the note will always sound the same.

It is, however, a good keyboard if you're just looking to learn a couple of pieces for fun (I like setting 75 - Sustained E. Piano 1!)
