### Post by kebes on 2007-02-10
Kaffeine and MythTV are optimized to do different things.


Kaffeine is a media player, and it does a good job at that.


MythTV is a software system for recording live TV (from a TV capture card) and storing it on the hard drive for later viewing. It is optimized for output to a television (although it can output on any display). It includes the components necessary for scheduling recording, viewing recordings (with commercial skipping), using a remote control, and so on. It includes plugins for viewing DVDs and other media files, but that is not its main purpose.

In short, MythTV turns your computer into a Personal Video Recorder (PVR). That's what all the fuss is about!

### Post by majoridiot on 2007-02-13
well, i enjoy the ability to record from multiple tuners while watching another, or a movie or
streaming music.  then there's the self-updating program guide, commercial flagging, the 
ability to custom transcode any recording automatically and to the format i specify, plugins
for weather, netflix rentals, serving dvds and other media files, etc. etc. etc...

the number of things you can do with mythtv is staggering and i wouldn't consider switching to 
anything else for any reason, unless it was a damn good one.

myth can be tricky to set up, but with .20 in the dapper and edgy repos and the GREAT install
guides in the ubuntu help wiki, it's *considerably* easier than it used to be.  my first myth
install six months ago took two weeks to get working and now i can get seperate frontend and
backend boxes installed with myth from a clean format in less than two hours.  myth install is
getting easier every day and the *correct* information is steadily being compiled in the wiki.

IMO the primary reasons people have so many problems with myth install are- following too
many (non-ubuntu) guides or mixing and matching info from multiple guides; the fallacy that
"it should work the first time", even though every mobo and system setup is different; and
the tendency to either panic or give up if a step fails, instead of trying to figure out why it
fails- and i speak from experience on these issues... i was guilty of each (except for giving
up).

again IMO, a lot of the anti-mythtv sentiments and complaints you see are from the folks
that fought it and just gave up.  mythv, as with many things linux, requires willingness to
*learn*, willingness to *share* what you have learned and dogged determination not to give 
up until the problem is solved.

when you can just turn on the radio?
