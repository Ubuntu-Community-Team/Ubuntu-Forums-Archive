### Post by Jim March on 2008-03-11
I have a Rumor and am getting music on and off it REAL easy via Ubuntu.

Step one: get a MicroSD card.  Mine is 4gig.

Step two: get an adapter that lets you put the MicroSD card into either a USB port, or a full-size SD card adapter.  If your computer has an SD slot that Ubuntu is seeing, fine, otherwise adapt SD to USB.

Step three: treat the MicroSD card like any other insertable media, read data off it, write data to it :).

So far the real nuisance has been the videos that the Rumor creates.  I've not found anything in Ubuntu that can play them yet although I haven't explored various options i Hardy (possible upgrades to Totem, etc.).  BUT, I run an XP virtual machine in Virtualbox and I can get full playback in Apple Quicktime on the virtual machine so that's good enough.

On virtualbox: use the one from the virtualbox website and set up "network shares".  You want at least two: one share for your home directory, another for /media - that way, from within XP (accessing the "/media" share) you can directly read/write your insertable USB/SD/whatever disks.

The cool part: no cable is needed whatsoever!

WARNING: ALWAYS TURN THE PHONE OFF WHEN REMOVING/INSERTING THE MICROSD CARD!!!
