### Post by Shampyon on 2007-04-19
I've been wondering this myself. I know that it was a mistake to buy an ATI card when I upgraded a few months back. NVIDIA apparently provide far better support for their Linux users than ATI.

Further than that, I haven't a clue.

I do know you'll get a more accurate response if you post up the specs of your desktop and what kind of things you use it for.

Example: I have a P4 3.0 GHz CPU, 1GB RAM, a Creative Audigy Soundblaster Value sound card and an ATI Radeon x700 512MB video card. Can't recall what motherboard I have. I use it mainly for watching videos, listening to mp3s, surfing the web and image editing. I'd like to get a new card so I can finally get Beryl running on my box - I love a fancy GUI.

You might have different specs and be more interested in gaming, or image editing, or audio editing and the kind of card people recommend will depend on that.
