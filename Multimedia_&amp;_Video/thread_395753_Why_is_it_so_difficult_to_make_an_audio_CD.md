### Post by ajgreeny on 2007-03-28
Have you tried using k3b and just copying the disk as if it was any data disk.  If that doesn't work then the original may just be copy protected by the manufacturer.  If that is the case then you may need to do it the hard way by playing the disk and recording the sound (audacity is good for that), and then record an audio disk from the tracks recorded.  A bit long winded, but it works.

If a disc is copy protected it should say on the cover somewhere that it is crippled :) or it WON'T have the Compact Disc Digital Audio Logo on it since it doesn't meet Phillips specifications.

This is normally done by having recorded more than one session to the disc, your basic music cd player will only play the first session (inner part of disc) which is the music, Your cdrom drive is smarter and reads all the sessions which include the copy protection garbage. If you look at your cd you can sometimes see the two seperate sections, the outer one is the one we don't want so obscure it somehow :) I've used black plastic electrical tape in the past, but rather risk it damaging my drive if the tape were to peal off I now use a permanent marker but be carefull not to over do it or you'll loose the music aswell. You don't have to obscure all of the outer session just a small bit so your cdrom reads it as corrupted and ignores it.
