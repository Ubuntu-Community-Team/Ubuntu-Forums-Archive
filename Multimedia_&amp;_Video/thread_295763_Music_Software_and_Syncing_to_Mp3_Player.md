### Post by Judicata on 2006-11-08
First, I must say that subscription is just not the way to go.  You should own your music.  After you investigate whether the site is legal in your country, you could check out allofmp3.com. 

Second, I think Rhapsody provides some linux support.

Third, just about any mp3 player will work.  Most people here will recommend one that supports codecs like FLAC and ogg vorbis, because they are "free" audio codecs (no real legal restrictions on their use, and no royalty problems etc..).

Most "MP3" players don't support these formats out of the box, but even the iPod can be configured to play them with a firmware replacement (see rockbox). 

I admit - I have an iPod (5th Gen. "video").  And I'm happy with it under linux.  I got it because its sleeker, thinner, and more attractive overall than most players.  There are plenty of alternatives though that are cheaper and support more formats.

In sum, check on Rhapsody. I know it runs on linux, but I'm not sure you can sync with it (some Googling for "rhapsody" and "Linux" will probably do the trick).  But, don't rent - buy.  It's like buying a home - you spend money for years, but then you have something to show for it. If you rent your music, you pour money into it, and then when the subscription is over, you have nothing to show for it.
