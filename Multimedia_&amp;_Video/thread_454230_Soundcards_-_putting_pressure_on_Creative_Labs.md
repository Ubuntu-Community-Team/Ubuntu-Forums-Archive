### Post by mhenriday on 2007-05-25
I'm unable to receive audio on my (64-bit) **Feisty** boot on a new **AMD 64 X 2** machine ; if I want to listen to anything, I have to resort to my (32-bit) **XP** boot. This is due to the fact that no **Linux** drivers - 32-bit _or_ 64-bit - are available for **Creative Labs Sound Blaster X-Fi **cards, as seen from the quote, below, taken from **Creative**'s website :> X-Fi series
The X-Fi series of products are not supported under Linux. (Yet)
Closed-source drivers will be available for the X-Fi series of sound cards.
It looks like the first public Beta will be available end calendar Q3 or early calendar Q4. It has taken more resources than expect[ed] to redesign our software and drivers for Vista.
It is planned that the drivers will have full support for ALSA (playback, recording, mixer, MIDI, synthesis) and OpenAL 1.1 (with EAX effects).
I fully understand the need to release drivers for **Vista**, but does anybody have any suggestions about how **Ubuntu** users could convince **Creative** to get the finger out and create and release those drivers (hopefully ones that work on 64-bit as well) ? It seems to me that, given the fact, that **X-Fi** was released two years ago, the fact that no **Linux** drivers have yet been released reveals a rather cavalier attitude to an admittedly small, but not unimportant user segment....

Henri

### Post by mhenriday on 2007-05-27
Sort of a **Catch 22** situation, is it not ? We **Ubuntu** users can't get drivers until there are more of us so that companies producing closed-source drivers find it economically worthwhile to appeal to us - but at the same time, it's difficult to convince people to go over to **Ubuntu** if they can't expect to find drivers for the hardware installed on their computers ! To suggest to them that they purchase additional hardware for the sheer pleasure of converting to **Ubuntu** is to place more faith in human altruistism than perhaps is warranted. But perhaps if the **Dell** decision to sell computers with **Feisty** pre-installed turns out to be a success, even companies like **Creative Labs** will listen....

Henri

### Post by mhenriday on 2007-06-02
Writing email to hardware firms that don't provide **Linux** drivers for their products is, I think, an excellent idea, not least to let them know that there are **Linux** users who are interested in purchasing and using their products ! Another method, which I have tried to use, is to refer to these difficulties in postings to various web fora on which relevant matters are being discussed. To my mind, the important thing is to be as concrete and specific as possible - **not** «we have difficulty finding **Linux** drivers», but rather «Company XX does not provide a **Linux** driver for product YY». If we want specific measures to be taken, we must come with specific, rather than general complaints....
