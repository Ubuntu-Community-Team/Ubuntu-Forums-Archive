### Post by david_2001 on 2007-07-03
Two possibilities for the second question (not that I've tried them myself but they may work):
[LIST=1]
[*]Add
```
Option "UseEDIDFreqs" "FALSE"
```
to the "Device" section of your xorg.conf.  This will stop the driver overriding the HorizSync and VertRefresh lines in your xorg.conf with frequency ranges obtained directly from the monitor.
[*]Set up modelines for all of the display resolutions that you want to use and list only those modes as possibilities in the "Display" subsections of the "Screen" section of your xorg.conf.
[/LIST]

### Post by mig5 on 2007-07-18

It sounds like either the horizontal sync range or the vertical sync range is either wrong in your xorg.conf, or maybe its not there at all.  Vertical sync is the refresh rate range e.g I only to use resolutions with refresh rates between 75-85 Hz, and Horizontal sync is a range that is specific to each monitor that you can find out using the ddcprobe command.  For info on how to implement these sync ranges, look under the monitor section on the last xorg.conf that I posted.

### Post by mig5 on 2007-07-20
> Section "Monitor"
Identifier "LCD-MONITOR"
Option "DPMS"
EndSection

You need to put in HorizSync and VertRefresh in, like this:

```

Section "Monitor"
Identifier "LCD-MONITOR"
HorizSync *{ILL EXPLAIN WHAT COMES HERE BELOW}*
VertRefresh *{ILL EXPLAIN WHAT COMES HERE BELOW}*
Option "DPMS"
EndSection

```

OK, now you know where to put them, but not the numbers to put after them.  To get the HorizSync for your monitor you need to go to a command line and run ddcprobe as described here: [http://www.cyberciti.biz/faq/howto-use-linux-ddcprobe-command/](http://www.cyberciti.biz/faq/howto-use-linux-ddcprobe-command/) .  You can use the same command to get the vertrefresh rates, or you can specify your own if you like (for example: if you know that your monitor can do from 55hz  to 75hz refresh rates then you could just put 60-75, that way your monitor won't try to use such low refresh rates).

Once you have them your monitor section should look something like this (DONT USE THE NUMBERS I'VE PUT IN, THEY ARE EXAMPLES.  USE THE ONES FOR YOUR MONITOR.):

```

Section "Monitor"
Identifier "LCD-MONITOR"
HorizSync 30-107
VertRefresh 55-75
Option "DPMS"
EndSection

```
