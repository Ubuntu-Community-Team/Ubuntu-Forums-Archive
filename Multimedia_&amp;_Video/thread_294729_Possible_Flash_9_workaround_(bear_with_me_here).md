### Post by Seantiago on 2006-11-07
OK, so I just downloaded the [Video Downloader]("https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/2390/") Firefox Extension, which allows you to easily download embedded videos without grepping through the page's source code.  It works on movs, avis, mpgs, wmvs, whatever.  What I was surprised to find out, though, is that it also works on embedded flash videos (flvs).  So you can go to youtube, download whatever, and watch from your hard drive.  

*Another* thing that surprised me was what you use to open the flvs: mplayer.  You can download a dedicated flv player if you really want, but why?

This is probably old news to most of you.

But here's something I noticed:  When you open flash videos using mplayer, the sound is actually pretty much in sync.  Like, about as in sync as you could expect if you had Flash 9 installed.

So, I have no idea how one would go about doing this (not much of a programmer, I) but wouldn't it make sense that you could use mplayer automatically open embedded flvs when you surf to a youtube or google video page?  It's probably not that simple, but it *seems* to make sense.  To me, at least.

Is there something wrong with this idea?  If it's viable, does anyone know how to go about doing it?

Peace.
