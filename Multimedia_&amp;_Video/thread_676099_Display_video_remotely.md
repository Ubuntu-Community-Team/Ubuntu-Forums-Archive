Ok here is the scenario:

Large manufacturing environment. In inspection we have white boards where the inspectors write down the rate of rejects, so that everyone walking by can see how it is going. The information they are writing is already in our large ERP Database, I can run reports etc to show the exact same stuff. I am trying to come up with a way where we could place large TV/Monitors 42" or bigger over these inspection lines.  This monitor would display a picture from our network of the rate they normally would enter.  Like I said before the data is not a problem, I can export the data every hour, every minute it makes no difference.  Format does not matter either, it can be a picture, pdf blah blah.  I just don't know of a good way to get the data to show up on a monitor placed remotely out in a manufacturing setting.

I think having one of those large dot-matrix LED panels are much more reliable than large screen plasma TV or LCD.  Especially if they are going up for years, with limited maintenance.
Drawbacks: not as sexy/pretty; ascii text/graphics only. 
 Advantages: no need to stream videos, just texts.  Simpler/cheaper running micro servers in those panels.  One of those Ip-enabled microcontroller should do the job.
