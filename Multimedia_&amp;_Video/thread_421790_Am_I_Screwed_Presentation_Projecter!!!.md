### Post by craigyjack on 2007-04-24
Same way any laptop or computer would connect to the projector. Stick the adaptor into your video card out. The problem would be if you need an adaptor because of different connectors. If the projector is hooked into a computer currently, then just unplug it from that computer and stick it into your nvidia out on your laptop. They'll either be VGA or DVI output/inputs, so see if it'll plug into your laptop. 

Plus, OpenOffice Presentation can save your presentation in .ppt powerpoint format also, although I do not know how different it would be playing in powerpoint (versus the openoffice version). Best bet would be to play it from OpenOffice on your laptop.

### Post by mjpatey on 2007-04-24
In my experience, PPT files created by OpenOffice's "Impress" presentation software translate very nicely to a Windows computer running PowerPoint.  It never hurts to first save in the default "ODP" format, so in case you do lose anything in translation, you have all your original stuff.

Also, OpenOffice can be installed on Windows computers.  Or in a pinch, you could run it from a thumb drive.   Do a Google search for "OpenOffice portable"... you can install the entire suite or individual elements on a thumb drive, and run it on any Windows computer.  It never leaves any files on the host machine.  A beautiful thing, and very handy!

-Mark
