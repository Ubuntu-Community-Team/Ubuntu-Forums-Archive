### Post by Jose Catre-Vandis on 2006-10-08
So what is your video card, and what problems are you having with it?

Having read your other thread, suggest trying to load bttv or saa7134 modules to see what you get back. Are you also sure it is a tuner, and not just a capture card, given that you used the TV IN? Did it tune channels under XP or just capture an input source, like your vcr?

Most tv tuner cards will work with Linux (especially if you update the dvb core via mercurial (see [www.linuxtv.org)](www.linuxtv.org)), but best to check it runs with bttv or saa7134.

### Post by geekchic9 on 2006-10-08
I don't have a TV Tuner, but my boyfriend does, and I'm trying to get him to convert to Linux. So, I've done a little research on TV-Tuner cards.

[This one]("http://www.hauppauge.com/html/wintvpvr350_datasheet.htm") is well-supported by Linux and has a TV tuner, FM tuner, and remote control. I know it's much more expensive than that cheapo card, but Hauppage is known to work with Linux, plus it has hardware built-in that processes the video, so it will run on slower computers with little or no lag. 

There is some software that works with this card called [MythTV]("http://www.mythtv.org/"). You can find more information about it [here]("http://www.debian-multimedia.org/"). 

That's about as much research I can do without actually owning a TV Tuner card. I hope this helps give you a good start. Good luck!
