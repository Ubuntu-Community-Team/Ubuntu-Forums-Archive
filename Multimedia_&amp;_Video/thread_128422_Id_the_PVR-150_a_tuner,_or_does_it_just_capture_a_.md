### Post by Panhead Bill on 2006-03-03
I looked at the three cards' descriptions on Hauppage's website and I can see why you would ask the question - they could have described it better than "Watch TV on your PC screen using a 125 channel cable ready (or aerial) TV tuner." 

The 150, 250 and 350 are tuners. 

MythTV seems to be the preferred software.

I'm a newbie doing the same type of research, I will probably end up with a 350 for hardware video in & hardware video out, and then add a 150 or 250 to get multichannel record capability similar to the 500MCE, but without the CPU overhead.

### Post by klueless on 2006-03-03
The PVR-500 is equivalent to 2 PVR-150's and there is no additional CPU overhead.  All of the Hauppauge x50 cards have built-in MPEG2 encoders. The 350 has additional hardware decoding as well as TV-out.

MythTV is great.  I've got 2 PVR-150's in my Myth box.  And I was trying to get an ATI TV Wonder VE working to get 3 tuners, but disaster struck and my motherboard and/or CPU fried (AMD 2600).  I'll be re-doing my Myth box this weekend with Ubuntu (previously Fedora 4).

You don't realize how good MythTV is until it breaks down . . .

Good luck with your Myth projects!
