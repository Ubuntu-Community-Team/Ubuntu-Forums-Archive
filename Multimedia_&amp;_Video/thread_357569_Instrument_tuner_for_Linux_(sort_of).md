### Post by Patrick K on 2007-02-09
A while back someone ask if there was an instrument tuner available for Linux. I found several but none were very far advanced beyond the concept stage. However, APTuner 3 ([www.aptuner.com](www.aptuner.com)) will run under wine. It loses its original instrument presets but if you "edit note presets" it will retain them between sessions. It is a chromatic tuner so can be used for any instrument. Anyway, just passing this on. Oh yeah, it's shareware but fully functional with no time limits, you just have to click through the registration screen.

### Post by barney_1 on 2007-02-10
To tell you the truth, why waste your time with a PC.  You can buy a QwikTime tuner at just about any music store for about $12.99.  I have all of my private students get this tuner, it runs on a couple of double A's and is just about indestructible.

I'm all for Linux and open source, but when it comes to intonation you need portability.
