### Post by drf_av on 2007-04-30
I am using linux for Audio Production.
First of all, i don't need MIDI, but, you're right, even if MIDI support in Ardour is on his way, Ardour doesn't support MIDI. But you have RoseGarden. And, to connect everything, you have Jack. So, you just have to route audio in/out Ardour and RoseGarden.

VSTs... ok, you can say that. But I'm using LADSPAs plugins, and for what I need to do they work really well, in addiction, they're not wasting CPUs like VSTs are. You can say "it's a matter of quality", honestly, I've never found any VST plugin that could give me a REAL improvement over TAP suite.

And there is also a huge factor that makes me use Linux: low latency kernels. I've never been able to get such latencies in windows, and I can have total control of my audio through the simple interface of qjackctl. It's like having an immense rack with thousand of cables, and you can just use your mouse to connect/disconnect ins and outs.
Also, thanks to Linux I can do the same things that I can do with a dual core processor and a gig of RAM in windows with a PC that is half powerful than that.

And, about ease of use, I've reached the conclusion that nothing is easy and nothing is hard, there are just things you're accustomed to.
If you use something everyday, you'll find easy everything that works like that. It's just a matter of changing habits, in my case, I thank the day I decided to switch to Ardour over Cubase, it wasn't easy in the beginning, but now I can't be happier.

Obviously, this was my case. Maybe in yours nothing will work like Ableton. Freedom of choice, you use what you want. Actually, I don't use Linux because it's free, or other crappy reasons, but simply because it suit my needs perfectly. I think everyone has to use what fits their needs. So give a chance to Ardour and RoseGarden, you like them = keep it up, you don't like them = back to Ableton.
