### Post by ricrac on 2007-05-19
Is it a new commercial DVD?  Some of the copy protection schemes used on DVD's will cause numerous problems, both hardware and OS.

DVD writers are cheap, when you decide to buy a new one eventually check online compatibility databases for a unit that performs well across all existing standards.

Generally speaking, a dvdrw that can burn playstation, dvd-r, dvd+r, redbook audio, etc.  will perform better on playback with funky copy protection schemes.  Not many can do this.  I have never burned a playtstation disc (I don't play games) but would never buy a drive that couldn't do this, it implies an "allowed" manufacture defect to achieve copy protection.

Upgrade the flash on your dvd drive.  After about a year I started having numerous problems with a NEC3540.  Flashed to the new version and the issues went away.

Many standalone dvd players can be flash upgraded also.
