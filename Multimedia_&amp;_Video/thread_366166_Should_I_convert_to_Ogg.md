The application I'm using to convert the files (mp32ogg) lets you choose between 10 different quality levels ¿Which one should I choose?
Transcoding is always bad.  No matter what Q level you choose, you will lose sound quality, unless you're ripping from a lossless format such as FLAC or a source CD.  OGG files would certainly not save enough space to justify the poor quality of the new rips, though that is certainly only my opinion/experience.

If you do decide to go with OGG in the future, I suggest ripping a single piece with which you're very familiar and rip it to a few different Q levels.  Then, try to listen to each file with the same equipment (earphones, speakers, or whatever) and choose the one that suits your ears best without looking at which file happens to be playing.

To my ears, OGG sounds a bit richer than the equivalent mp3, but that's personal taste.  Some people can't hear a difference.
