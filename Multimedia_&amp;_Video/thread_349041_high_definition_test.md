### Post by emarkay on 2007-01-29
[http://www.nasa.gov/multimedia/videogallery/hd_index.html](http://www.nasa.gov/multimedia/videogallery/hd_index.html)

It's based only on lines of resolution on TV, Enverex, "Normal D" is 480i (interlaced)  and HD is 720p(progressive)  or 1080i.  

For pixel counts, an approximate of the old NTSC, one gets this: 480 scan lines x 720 pixels/line = 345,600 pixels, whereas the potential for an HDTV signal is :1,080 scan lines x 1,920 pixels/line = 2,073,600 pixels.  A good plasma scren can deliver about 1/2 that: 1,024 lines x 1,024 pixels =  1,048,576 pixels.

Just a FYI...

BTW, there is apparently NO native video player that will play these videos...
