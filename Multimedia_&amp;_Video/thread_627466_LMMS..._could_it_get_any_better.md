### Post by BCtom on 2007-12-01
Hey Warbread,

I don't' know how far you are into the process of setting up plugins and using them, so I'll do a cursory overview of what I do.

[step 1] A good piano sound module, and I just installed and used it a few minutes ago, is [4Front Paino]("http://www.yohng.com/piano.html"). Just look at the "Download for Free" section, and choose the one of the three links. I used the "Larger DLL" file. Extract it, and you should have a DLL file: Remember the Window$ days..... same binary library file!

[step 2] In your LMMS directory, the one which you had to make during its first Boot, create a new directory and call it something like, "plugins." Place just the xxxx.dll file there.

[step 3] Now fire up LMMS and open the Instruments Plugins window along the left. Choose VeSTige plugin wrapper and drag & drop it into the Song Editor window. Click the "Default" button to activate VeSTige, and the green plugin window pops up, then click the folder button to load the VST plugin. Then search for your VST plugin in your ..../LMMS/Plugins/ directory and choose open. Depending on how big and complex the VST plugin is, it may take a while for it to load. You'll know when the plugin instrument window pops up.

[step 4] Again, to use it like this in LMMS, the piano-roll option is about the only way you can get it to function. 

There are hundreds of VST plugins out there. Some will work, some will not. Some are very sophisticated, while others, like the 4Front Paino plugin I talked about, are not--it is just a sound module with no settings--probably why its free....  I currently have about thirty plugins right now. And of course I always choose the "free" ones :)

A good Cello is [Cello-Fan]("http://soundkeysvst.free.fr/"). 

I guess the grand-daddy of VST-plugins listings are found here at [Audio Mastermind]("http://www.audiomastermind.com/browse-free_vst_plugins-5886988-1.html"). 

I think you should be OK making music with a good processor. I have a weak sound card on my test machine, and it seems to do a good job with sound reproduction? So, you should be good. 

Anyway, I hope I got you a little further into making some good tunes. Good luck!
