### Post by SonicSteve on 2006-10-21
OK so I'm thinking that this is definately a "bug". I had this exact same issue. 
If fixed it by doing this. (pretty basic)
1. Open the master mixer control by clicking on the taskbar speaker.
2. Click the edit menue at the top and click on preferences and put check boxes in all item if you wish, I added only a few that seemed related to playback. I.e. I ignored things like "mic in", Aux etc. Just make sure you enable the ones near the end that say DXS after your chipset make.
3. I played with the one called VIA DXS. This somehow kicked the sound back in!
don't ask me why I'll leave that for the programmers to answer.
Hope this helps at least a few others.
