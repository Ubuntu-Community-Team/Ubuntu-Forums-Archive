### Post by sgx on 2007-01-04
Wine is a moving target...I had things work in Wine Ubuntu 9.9 that don't in 9.27, and the situation is not
likely to change. Historically, one must get, and guard dearly wine versions that support a
particular important setup. If I were you, I would buy a commercial Wine product, as they are
way ahead of Wine, and then, if x or y does not work, give detailed accounts to the team you
are supporting with $$$

If you can google info that indicates this product is Mac compatible, it might also
mean it is standards compliant, increaing odds you will have success, and check
Digitech forums, cause if lots of problems exist there on windows, it will  be tougher
still in the alien atmosphere on planet TUXsound...

As for linux guitarists, Creox and Ecamegapedal offer an array of fx and multifx that
are quite nice, (Eca uses ladspa fx) and sit happily at the end of jack patch cords, awaiting your line-input
cable sending wav signals their way...great for midi keyboards and vsts also...
Good luck!
