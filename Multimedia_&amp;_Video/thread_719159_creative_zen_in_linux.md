### Post by granny6989 on 2008-04-03
which version of ubuntu are you running? How did you install gnomad?
The best bet is to drop in gnomad through add/remove or through synaptic. Do all that and log out/in. 

Make sure you have all dependencies, especially libnjb, libusb. You can get those through synaptic. Probably best to get those installed first. Then go find Gnomad2 in add/remove, and let it get all installed. At this point, log out and back in just for kicks. Now, plug in your Zen and give it a second, until the screen shows that it's docked. Now go to your menu's and start Gnomad (or Rhythymbox). The Zen should be there. I have done this with Feisty and Gutsy with no problems, and I'm pretty sure that it will work with earlier distro's as well. See how that works for you.
