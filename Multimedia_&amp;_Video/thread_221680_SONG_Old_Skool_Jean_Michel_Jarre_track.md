### Post by Sheik Yerbouti on 2006-07-23
Here's a track I have been working on to learn to use Linux audio tools. The track is modeled after late 70s electronica specifically Jean Michel Jarre Oxygene part 4. I adapted this from a tutorial in Computer Music number 89.


It was created in Rosegarden a Linux midi sequencer. I utilized several DSSI softsynths including Whysynth, Xsynth, Fluidsynth, and even CM101 a VST softsynth. This is also using my Korg minipops 35 soundfont I created with Swami a Linux sound font editor. I bounced it to disk using Timemachine a simple but cool jack recording tool. And I did final editing including compressing, normalizing, exporting in Rezound. All in all I am impressed with what I can accomplish with Linux audio tools. I also learned a ton along the way and am satisfied with how it turned out. I have made an ogg version and mp3 version available for download the MP3 is much bigger at ~7MB. I wanted the two formats to sound alike so I used 320k on the MP3.


[Ogg format]("http://www.allenjeter.com/jmj.ogg")
[MP3 format]("http://www.allenjeter.com/jmj.mp3")
