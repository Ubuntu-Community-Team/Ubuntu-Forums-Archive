### Post by 64mgb on 2006-12-26
I found the solution to this.  First, follow the instructions here to load Nvidia drivers:

[http://doc.gwos.org/index.php/Latest_Nvidia_Edgy](http://doc.gwos.org/index.php/Latest_Nvidia_Edgy)

There are a couple of important notes.  First, this does not work with kernel 2.6.18 or beyond...apparently the restricted modules are not available for them yet.  Also, make sure you do all the steps (I used method 1).

if you completed all the steps you will have a new menu item under Applications -> System Tools called Nvidia Settings.  Click on this and then click on the TV-0 under Display Device.  Here you will see several settings for the TV out, including a slider to adjust overscan.  Mine was set to 8 by default.  By setting it to 13 I now have a full screen!

Rich
