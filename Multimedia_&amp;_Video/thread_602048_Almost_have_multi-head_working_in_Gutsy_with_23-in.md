### Post by dmgursky on 2007-11-03
I've recently upgraded from Feisty to Gutsy, and lo and behold, my multi-head configuration stopped working.  While it had issues even in Feisty, it plain did not work in Gutsy.

For what it's worth, my configuration is as follows -- IBM Thinkpad A-21m, with built-in ATI Radeon Mobility.  I now keep the Thinkpad (having born the brunt of several years of travel) docked, and the docking station has a Diamond Multimedia Stealth 9250 (ATI Radeon S9250, 256 MB).

The culprit turned out to be the Open Source ATI driver shipped with Gutsy (6.7.195-1) -- it simply does not work with multi-head systems.  I downloaded the prior version (6.6.3-2) and lo and behold, things were back to their pre-Gutsy state.  That's the good news.

[I leave it as an exercise to individuals to the specifics of downgrading your video driver, but the file you need is [FONT="Courier New"]**xserver-xorg-video-ati_6.6.3-2ubuntu6_i386.deb**[/FONT].  I leave it as an exercise to you to search for the file on the Internet...

The bad news is that things are back to their pre-Gutsy state.

There is an issue on the Apple monitor.  There's a flickering phenomenon at work.  If there are no windows open on the monitor, all is fine.  If there IS a window open however, the window will flash or flicker as I type inside of it.  [Specifically, it flashes AND flickers, but it does not do both at the same time from what I can tell.]  Occasionally, the screen will black out momentarily.

Has anyone else experienced this issue?  I've attached my xorg.conf file below.

Regards

David
