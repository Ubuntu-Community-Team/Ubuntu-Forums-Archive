### Post by Jovec on 2007-02-11
Perhaps explain why you don't want to go with an Ipod?  The hardware is quite nice overall.

I "replaced"  the Apple firmware on my Ipod with [Rockbox]("http://www.rockbox.org/"), specifically for ogg file playback (Technically only the boat loader gets replaced, the original Apple Ipod firmware is still in place and you can dual boot between the two).  As the Ipod is seen as a USB disk, I simply copy my music from my hdd to my Ipod.  You can replace the firmware under Ubuntu and you'll never need to run Itunes.  Of course, Itunes purchased music files won't play under Rockbox due to DRM.

My daughter has the Creative Labs device and it works great.  I personally use a generic mp3 player (it serves as both player and pen drive) due to cost.  I recommend staying away from anything too thin especially if it will be put in a pocket with keys, change, etc.  You can iPod it if you wish, but why go through all the trouble (unless your music dB is all ogg)!
