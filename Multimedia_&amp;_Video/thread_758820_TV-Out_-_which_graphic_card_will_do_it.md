### Post by nicedude on 2008-04-18
Most graphics cards that do tv out do it with either S-video or with some kind of cable with RCA jacks, I have used Nvidia cards for years and they all come with the S-video which I used to use allot. Then I found a better way to handle my multimedia needs, there are now several makers of a new type of device thats a portable USB hard drive and also plays multimedia files stored on it to your TV (mine plays Divx, Xvid, Mpeg even MP3 all right out of the box) . I bought one that's a 500GB portable hard drive and media player for like $190 US (but there are smaller capacity slightly cheaper ones) - and I couldn't be happier with my purchase as it currently has like 400 movies and a bunch of TV episodes on it and I can take it with me to anyones house and plug it up and be watching movies. they are several brands that make them but mine is a Inoi  (look at [http://www.inoi.com](http://www.inoi.com)). As far as the composite thing I haven't seen a cable connector on a video card for awhile but the easy solution to that problem is to get a simple converter from walmart that has RCA or S-video IN and then goes to composite OUT for older TV sets to use newer devices.
