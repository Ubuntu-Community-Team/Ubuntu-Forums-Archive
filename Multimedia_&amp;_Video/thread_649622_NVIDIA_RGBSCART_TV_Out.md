### Post by billbofagends on 2007-12-25
Hi folks,

I'm running a nvidia GeForcce4 ti in my mythbox and I've set up X to run two screens with it - 1 for a VGA crt and the other to drive a tv (standard def CRT). I can quite easily output to the tv via s-video or compsite (set with Option "TvOutFormat" "SVIDEO"  or Option "TVOutFormat" "COMPOSITE" in xorg.conf

What I'd like to do is get output from this card in RGB scart format. According to the nvidia docs, COMPONENT and SCART are also valid options for TVOutFormat has anyone had any sucess in using these options?

This is what I've been trying...

I knew component was not going to give me what I want as the output would be YPbPr so I've been experimenting with SCART. The physical connection I'm using from the card is the break out lead supplied with the card. It has s-vid out (normal din connector) and R G B phono connectors (labelled as Pr, Y and Pb respectively). To get this in to the TV, I have bodged together a SCART with 3 phono connectors, wired:

Pin
5 Blue ground
7 Blue input
9 Green ground
11 Green input
13 Red ground
15 Red input

This didn't work at all and gave no picture.

I realised that my TV wasn't selecting RGB automatically and was trying to display a composite input. To cure this, I wired up Pin 8 (Switch signal input), 16 (Blanking signal input) and 18 (Blanking signal & switch input ground) to the output from my Sky box. With the sky box switched on, this forced the tv into RGB mode (the input icon changed to reflect this) - but still no picture.

Before I gave up, I read some stuff on RGB with Sync. The sync signal supposedly comes from  part of the composite signal. So I wired up Pin 19 on the scart (composite in on TV end) to the s-vid out (with an s-vid to composite adapter)  on the break out lead from the card. This gave me a picture but was very blue but seemed to be sharper than just composite

I put the blue-ness being down to one of the RGB phono's not making a connection. I tested my bodged lead with a multimeter and it all checked out ok. When I switched the sky box off (sending tv into composite mode), I got a correctly displayed picture (using just composite) - so it proved that the RGB connections I had made some affect and tv was being forced into RGB mode.

Looking through the Xorg.0.log - its shows (**) NVIDIA(1): Forcing SCART output so I think I've got xorg.conf set up correct. I've also notcied in the log 

```
(--) NVIDIA(1): Philips 7108A TV Encoder (TV-0): 165.0 MHz maximum pixel
(--) NVIDIA(1):     clock
```

This appears to be what is doing the TV encoding - does anyone know anything about this device and its capabilities?

Sorry for the long post - but hopefully this might help somebody else in the same situation.

Thanks

sam
