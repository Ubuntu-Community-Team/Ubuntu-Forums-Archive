### Post by spii on 2006-11-04
I have 5.1 speakers (logitech) through an onboard VIA 8237. For those that have trouble with this, two simple things caused me a lot of grief: (1) Alsa was set to 2 ch audio for ages (until I discovered you have to set it to 6 ch) and (2) my center/sub plug was in the wrong jack on the back of the PC. Can't blame linux for that one :-? 

What I haven't been able to fix is front stereo. Front left AND front right come out both speakers (i.e. front left audio is heard on both speakers). They are plugged into the right jack on the back of the motherboard.

When I plug my headphones in, they go into a separate jack on the front of the PC. Left and right stereo works properly in them. What's more, when headphones are plugging, the speakers also function correctly (front left audio is only heard on the front left speaker).

The good news is I can get surround sound with proper front stereo channels by just leaving my headphones plugged in. But I'm sure I shouldn't have to do that, and maybe somebody knows why and how I can fix it? ;)

### Post by spii on 2006-11-10
Nothing to do with ubuntu! (Always been happening in windows and I'd never noticed!). Turns out my front panel audio pins were crossed over. Fixed now (well, headphone plug on front of case has left and right reversed - motherboard and case pins don't agree on the standard it seems!)
