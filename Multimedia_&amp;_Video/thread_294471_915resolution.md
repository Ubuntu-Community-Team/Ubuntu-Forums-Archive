### Post by matt_b on 2006-11-07
Aha, I have fixed it now after some more reading and experimentation...

It seems that although modes 7d/7e already contain my desired resolution (i.e. no overwrites needed), if I attempt to use them (by running *sudo 915resolution 7e 1280 768 32*) nothing happens. If I overwrite an existing unneeded mode (4d 1920 1440 16) by running *sudo 915resolution 4d 1280 768 16* and relaunch X I get the correct resolution. I then edited */etc/default/915resolution* and entered 4d, 1280, 768, 16 in the respective fields, saved, rebooted and hey presto, 1280x768 glorious widescreen :)

Thanks wieman01 for your help,
