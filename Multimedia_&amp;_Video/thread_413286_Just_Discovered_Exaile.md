### Post by wyth on 2007-04-19
I *want* to like Exaile, but it's not quite there yet.  I use Amarok for podcasts, and one of it's killer features is you can select whatever directory you want to save whatever feed to.  I love that -- keeps my podcasts separate from my music, and generally more organized.  Amarok also allows me to update a single feed at a time.

Exaile runs better on Gnome, but it lacks these podcasting capabilites.  I'm also not sold on the way it opens a new tab for every stream.  It's nice, one of the nicer one's in Gnome, but it's not even up to version 1.0, and has some work to do.

### Post by darweth on 2007-04-19
I really want to like Exaile, but I also agree that it is not there yet.  Something about it just feels clunky, especially when you have a library of 25,000 tunes.  Amarok is the ONLY big-box media player I've seen that can handle an enormous library to perfection, runs speedily, and plays nicely with tags.

The main problem I had with Exaile is importing a database.  On almost every player, I get around 24,800 songs.  Exaile gives me 23,921 or so... what happens to the rest???  heh.  

I find GTK players lacking in general (although I have not tried the mpd-client options, which might be cool)

I have tried Listen, BMPx, Exaile, Quod Libet, Audacious, Rhythmbox, Banshee and others multiple times and always always always go back to Amarok.  I really want a GTK player that works as well as Amarok.
