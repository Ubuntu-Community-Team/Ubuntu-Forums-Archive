I have a hopefully simple question about using my widescreen Samsung lcd tv/monitor with Ubuntu.  I have a Nvidia geForce fx5200 and I have the proprietary nvidia drivers.  I installed Gutsy while using a normal square-ish monitor, then plugged it into the Samsung after it was set up.

The desktop looks great in general and fills the whole screen, but a couple of things seem weird:

1. my photos in f-spot look a little bit squashed, as though f-spot (and the photos) were being stretched horizontally to fill the wide screen.

2. commercial DVDs (at least, the one I tested) has the top and maybe bottom of the image cut off.  I.e., the actors' heads are cut off and there is black space above and below the image.

I'm not sure if the two things above are related at all. 

I guess my question is, do I need to change some settings to get my photos to display in the same shape that they used to?  I don't need the extra pounds that widescreen is applying to me [http://ubuntuforums.org/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif](http://ubuntuforums.org/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif)
:)

### Post by NilsE on 2007-12-31
It sounds like the wrong mode is selected for the wide screen or still defaulting to 4:3.  

You can go into System > Administration > Screens and graphics and try different display setting until it looks right.
