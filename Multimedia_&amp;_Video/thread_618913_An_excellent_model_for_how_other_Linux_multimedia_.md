### Post by OCTOG3N on 2007-11-20
I do video editing working on Mac and Windows. sometimes I like to use programs like Soundtrack, Garageband, and Acid to create loop based music. Sometimes I like to dabble and mess around in these programs (anyone has a mac has probably dabbled in garageband) I also dabble in Linux. Ubuntu is not my main OS, I kind of use it for fun. One reason is that linux doesn't have any stable, easy to use, attractive, intuitive consumer/prosumer level multimedia editing programs that are just fun to use (don't even mention kino) that's why many people like ilife so much, just to play around in. Ladies and gentlemen, I give you Jokosher

[IMG]http://i71.photobucket.com/albums/i128/octolink_64/Jokosher-1.png[/IMG]

This program changed my mind about Linux editing programs. It's logical, it's intuitive, it's easy and most of all FUN to use. It's the only linux multimedia app I could actually do something with. And, it's only been in development for a year. Of course, that comes with some incomplete features: buggy, lack of loop based editing, but otherwise, this app is golden. This is my word to the linux community: make a video editing app like this, and we'd have imovie of linux.
