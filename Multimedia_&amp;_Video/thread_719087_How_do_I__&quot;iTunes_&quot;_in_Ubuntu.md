### Post by Tarmael on 2008-03-08
There is a CD extractor in the Programs -> Sound and multimedia section of the top panel.

I'm not too sure on the actual wording as I am not at my home computer, but I know it does exist and gives you options to convert to FLAC, OGG, and I think MP3. If it doesn't do MP3, then I would look at getting the application LAME running. It's a good Open Sourced converter. I think you have to install it from source, but if you can work out how, it would be worth your time.

I know for a fact that LAME is the best out there.

A good conversion structure is going from FLAC to MP3, or just sticking with FLAC. Do not convert the CD to OGG then to MP3 as both OGG and MP3 are lossy compressions where FLAC is lossless.

As for the other requests, I'm not sure of a media player that will equalise sound

Totem has visual effects, and so does VLC (Please confirm) 

VLC has connection to internet radio.

rhythmbox displays the album covers

That's about all I can help with at the minute, as I stated before, I'm not at home so I can't help as much as I would like.

### Post by Tarmael on 2008-03-09
> Two applications to perform the work of iTunes..?

Well the difference is the market type.

iTunes is marketed and designed to be the be all and end all Media Player for people who use iPods, Mac users, and Windows users.

Two big things you need to look at there...

First of all, iTunes is competing in the Windows world against Windows Media Player, I know for sure that Media Player has nothing on iTunes, but that's only because iTunes has funding. This funding means that they can have as many features as they need. And they need the features to keep up with the market.

The biggest reason why they have to compete against WMP is because it comes stock standard.

Secondly, Linux is free. It's away from all this competition with it's own free and open software. If a company had a like that of iTunes and a fan base that required it to make a program, then it would flourish, trying to make as many features as it could to make sure people used it.

But sound juicer and sound converter do exactly what they say. So you know you get the right program that you need.
