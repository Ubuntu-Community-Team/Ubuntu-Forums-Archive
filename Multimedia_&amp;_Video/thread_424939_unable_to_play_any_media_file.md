### Post by kvonb on 2007-04-27
The CODECs that are required to play proprietary formats like mp3 and avi are not free and in the US are illegal to even download!

That is why they cannot be included by default.

They have to be downloaded and installed, but seeing as you are using the live CD, they will vanish when you reboot.

If you want to try it, run the add/remove tool from the start menu and search for "codec", select the ones you want and install.

Once you install Ubuntu, the CODECS will be offered to you when you try to open one of the files, although if you live in the US or other restrictive country, you will be breaking their laws if you do install them.
