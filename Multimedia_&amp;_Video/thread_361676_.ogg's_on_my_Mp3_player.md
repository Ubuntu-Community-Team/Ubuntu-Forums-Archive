### Post by Richard Kut on 2007-02-16
Hello theTailor, and welcome! One way to convert from ogg to mp3 is with the ogg2mp3 Perl script provided here:

[http://www.igso.net/nkb/Converting_ogg_to_mp3_in_Linux](http://www.igso.net/nkb/Converting_ogg_to_mp3_in_Linux)

The other option is to paste the following into a terminal:

for i in *.ogg; do ogg123 -d wav -f - "$i" | lame -h - > mp3/"`echo "$i" | sed -e 's/.ogg$/.mp3/'`"; done

However the above does require that you have the ogg tools and the lame encoder installed. The lame encoder allows you to record straight to an mp3 format. Therefore, when you rip music in the future, you can go straight to mp3 instead of incurring the loss of quality by changing from ogg to mp3.

MetalMusicAddict  wrote a good HowTo for that here:

[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183125hp?t=25151](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183125hp?t=25151)

As his guide says, it is based on grip, which you may or may not be using for ripping.

More likely, you are using SoundJuicer. If so, there is another short guide here:

[http://www.emcken.dk/weblog/archives/99-MP3-encoding-with-Sound-Juicer.html](http://www.emcken.dk/weblog/archives/99-MP3-encoding-with-Sound-Juicer.html)

I hope that this helps. Please update this post with your results. Good luck!
