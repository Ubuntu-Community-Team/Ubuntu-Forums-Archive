### Post by ninepillars on 2007-10-07
I just ran into the same problem.

Surprisingly, Rhythmbox is the only application I've found that can burn my m4a's straight to audio CD. Just append your tracks to the Play Queue, right click and select (I believe) "burn audio CD". Nowhere near as neat as k3b, but it works.

That's only if you don't mind using a Gnome application. I've recently switched over to Kubuntu, but I still like my old Rhythmbox!
