### Post by WinterWeaver on 2007-02-03
Hiya ^_^

Right, I've had this problem for quite a while now, and I just thought that I'd post my temporary solution here, just in case anyone else gets the same problem.

A while back I've noticed that some of my mp3 and other formatted music clips played insane amounts of base, through my left speaker. Needless to say this is deeply disturbing, cause I dont want to destroy my speakers. (The speaker distorted even though the volume is set very very low).

At first it might be a problem with my 5.1 surround, etc. I've played around with the mixer settings etc. And could not fix it.

I then thought, well, it only happens on certain songs, so it may be a corrupt or bad compression. Online radio plays fine, movies plays fine, most mp3's and other formats play well. It's just certain song.

So yesterday, I had this thought that I'd try play those clips through another player, installed amarok, loaded up all my songs, and started comparing the sound between the two programs.
BAM, problem solved. So I assume this is some problem with rhythmbox then.

ok, so now I'm using Amarok. It looks nicer, has lots of nice features. Unfortunately it eats a LOT of resources (I'm running ubuntu, not Kubuntu, dunno if it makes a difference to resources). But other than that, I'm happy for the swap.

So If anyone else has the problem... maybe it's worth trying another music player.

Thanx
