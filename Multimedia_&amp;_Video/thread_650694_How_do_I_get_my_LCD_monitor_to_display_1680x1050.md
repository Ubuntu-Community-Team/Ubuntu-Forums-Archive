### Post by moron on 2007-12-27
Hey 449.  To display in 1680x1050 your video card, monitor and your Xorg configuration must support it.  If the video card and monitor can handle it then it is a case of having the right "xorg.conf" file configruation setup.  Since this depends pretty highly on the specifics of your system there is not a single, 1 step generic answer (life sucks then you die, whatcha gonna do).

I don't run Gnome so I am not sure how it handles adjusting desktop resolution but if whatever system configuration dialog you have for it does not list 1650x1080 in its list of available resolutions then you likely will have to adjust your xorg.conf file.  Usually this means adding a "modeline" entry for your monitor as well as listing the available modes in the screen section.   The modeline is something that you can figure out from your xorg log file but since it is a but esoteric, I'll leave that alone for now in case someone else has a FAQ specific to your situation.

You should definitely post your current xorg.conf file so folks have an idea of how you are currently setup.  

Cheers

### Post by moron on 2007-12-27
If 1680x1050 is not available in your desktop configuration tool for Gnome then you may need to customize the "Monitor" section to match yours.

Here is the Gentoo distro's FAQ on widescreen configuration:

[http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Widescreen_Resolutions_(WSXGA](http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Widescreen_Resolutions_(WSXGA))

The specific thing you should look at is the section that talks about stripping out the mode list from your Xorg log:

[http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Widescreen_Resolutions_(WSXGA)#If_that_fails_-_Use_Modeline](http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Widescreen_Resolutions_(WSXGA)#If_that_fails_-_Use_Modeline)

Basically you need to scan your logfile for "Supported additional Video Mode:" and then take the next couple of lines to create your modeline as discussed on the page above.   Once you have added the modeline to your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file you will need to restart X.  If there is a typo, X will not start but getting back to where you were before just requires editing the file to comment out the modeline.

Cheers
