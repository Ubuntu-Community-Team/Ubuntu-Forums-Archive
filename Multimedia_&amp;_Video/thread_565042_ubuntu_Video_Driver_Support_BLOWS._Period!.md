### Post by mliving on 2007-10-01
Ok, I've heard a LOT of people flap their gums about how close ubuntu is to the mainstream and to be brutally honest. IT'S NOT!

Installing videos drivers on ubuntu continues to be a HUGE problem and trying to get my Radeon 700X working using the restricted driver manager and the ATI Linux driver has been nothing short of absolute madness.

No operating system that requires users, even the more advanced users, to spend hours scouring the user forums for solutions can be considered ready for the masses. And even after spending hours in the forums you'll find hundreds of posts and replies that offer every sort of 'terminal' solution know to the uber Linux geek. Often solutions conflict with each other or simple do not work. They are NOT written for the average user and PLEASE don't hand me that garbage about Linux is a cutting-edge, blah, blah, blah... OK.

Come on folks. We're talking video drivers for crying out loud!

I would really like to move to ubuntu and I can honestly say that I am extremely disappointed with the results I have had with 7.04. ubuntu 'may' be the leading distro but giving the amount of technical barbed wire users have to crawl through to get even the basics working properly I can not see how ubuntu is going to succeed at home even with a partner like DELL.
