### Post by dolson on 2006-04-05
I think you misunderstand what exactly "Ubuntu Studio" is.

It is a wiki of tutorials for using Ubuntu as a music Studio.

This doesn't mean I can't add a section for video editing, but as I don't have any kind of method to get any original video files onto my PC, I do not have any experience with it. As it is, I've done about 95% of the work that you see currently in the wiki for music stuff, but if someone decides to add some video stuff, then that is fine with me. BUT the focus for the wiki should definitely be music apps.

"Ubuntu Studio" is not a distro, not a source of packages, not anything other than a wiki of how-tos.

Perhaps if you want to package software, you should just contribute it directly into Ubuntu, or even better, directly into Debian.

Dapper does not have Diva from what I see; if you hurry and package it, you can probably get it included. Upload your source package to REVU and ping someone in #ubuntu-motu to review it. I'd be interested in checking it out myself, as I haven't heard of it before.

The only video editing stuff I saw was very immature and supported no common formats that I could use without having a digital video camera. If the situation has changed, I'd love to get involved, but I can't afford a camera right now.
