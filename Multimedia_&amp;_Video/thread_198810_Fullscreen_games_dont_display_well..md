### Post by andnobodyslept on 2006-07-17
Ok so I figured it out. I would say if you try to run glxgears and you get a message saying that the XFree86-DRI (which is what was happening to me) then you need to install the DRI and DRM modules. Follow this How To
[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=7200&highlight=3d+acceleration+mach64]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=7200&highlight=3d+acceleration+mach64") 
the only difference is that instead of mach64 choose the r128 (Rage 128). It didn't work right away for me I had to reconfigure my xorg.conf by 
sudo dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg 
after that everything worked great my DRI was enabled and my glxgears jumped from 70fps to 830fps. Also I could now go to a lower resolution for games. It worked for me, hopefuly it will work for you.
