### Post by barney_1 on 2007-12-04
I'm a huge fan of huappauge tv encoders.  I have a PVR-250, but have heard great things about the PVR-150 as well.  Just be careful when ordering the PVR-150 because some boxes come with a different card in them that is unsupported by linux.  If you order a PVR-150 you should call first and make sure you are getting the right card in the box (newegg supposedly will make sure you get the right card before shipping it out).

Here's a list of cards that work with the ivtv module: [http://ivtvdriver.org/index.php/Supported_hardware](http://ivtvdriver.org/index.php/Supported_hardware)

One thing I think is really important when considering a TV card is to get an encoder card and not just a tuner card.  Tuner cards capture the video and rely on your processor to encode it to a compressed format.  Encoder cards feed the compressed format to your HD, taking the load off of your CPU.
