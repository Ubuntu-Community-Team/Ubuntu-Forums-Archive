### Post by Yougo on 2011-01-04
maybe you can get an older version of msn messenger to run if you really want to, but that would actually put you behind almost every linux native IM client.

while i agree aMsn looks the most like WLM, after a month of using it i had security issues like random people trying to sell me 'those blue pills', watches, iphones and whatnot, and even got spam from bots posing as people from my buddy list. :mad: needless to say i was less than pleased. i don't hear many other stories like this, so i don't know what went wrong here, but i didn't trust amsn anymore. it's leaky for me.

i switched to Pidgin then, and while i still got some spam at first, it subsided. guess the lack of exposure on pidgin got me off the spam radar.
then after some denial-of-service (as in "oops we changed servers or protocols and didn't tell you" by MS) i switched to ubuntu-native Empathy. it copes better with this kind of tricks. it's less feature rich than pidgin, but it's getting better, and looks clean.

i'm not really up to speed on webcam support for these three, as i don't really use that.

that's my three cents anyways
