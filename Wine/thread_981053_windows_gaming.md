### Post by cogadh on 2008-11-14
Its a "letter of the law vs. spirit of the law" argument. Transgaming basically took the hard work of many other people, repackaged it, then sold it as their own. That may not be stealing according to the old MIT license agreement, but in any other situation, it would be.

In all honesty, that is only one of the problems with Cedega. They also have that awful subscription pricing model, which supposedly gets you better game support as well as tech support. Unfortunately, they have been known to go up to a full year without adding any new game support, so if you are unlucky enough to sign up for the 12 month plan in a year when they don't add any new game support, you basically wasted $45. Their tech support is absolutely atrocious. Don't even bother trying to contact them if you don't have an Nvidia graphics card, they won't help you at all.

About the only good thing they have done is start offering a demo of Cedega again. For a long time, you had to pay for it without ever being able to try it to see if it would actually work for the games you wanted to play.

While I obviously prefer Wine, if you feel Cedega is the right solution for you, by all means, try it out. But you should also take the time to try out Wine and CrossOver Games before committing to Cedega. You may be surprised to find out that one may actually be far superior to the others for what you need it for.
