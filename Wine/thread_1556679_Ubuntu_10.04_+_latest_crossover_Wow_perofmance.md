### Post by texaswriter on 2010-08-19
It's really the ATI card. Can't much explain it except to say I've had nothing but problems getting support for mine [on my desktop PC]. I used to play WoW on my home PC and had a really nice ATI HD card, plenty of memory. Really bad FPS though compared to what I should have been getting. I think people who use NVidia cards get much better FPS; can't confirm myself, but check it out. 

Otherwise, I disable things like Compiz, etc. I also usually install the driver from ATI website [problematic to install, but usually works fine if you follow instructions on ATI website]. 

That said, I don't really game much on my PC. People want to be serious about restricting games to  Linux, I can go from buying 5+ new PC games a year to buying 0. Whatever works for them, people who use Linux obviously don't buy software, isn't that what they say. 

Anyways, yeah, almost any Linux gamer will tell you ATI sucks for Linux, I'm not sure why actually. They do actually release drivers for Linux. I couldn't give you the reasons why. 

I know I've done a lot of work getting my respective games to work and even where I get them working flawlessly, something about the ATI card makes them crash randomly or hang up the card/computer. Oblivion I got working flawlessly, max settings. It was beautiful, especially considering I never got it working max settings on Windows with that hardware... Randomly from 3 minutes after playing to 20 minutes [rarely more than that] green static would appear on the screen, image would freeze, and the computer was usually frozen [requiring a reboot]... 

So yeah, in conclusion, I'd recommend you get an NVidia card, checking out the hardware compatibility thread [somewhere on Ubuntuforums.org] to see how well it is supported. 

HTH and GL
