### Post by beeldings on 2008-05-23
To better help you, I have a few questions that I would like you to answer:

1) What video card are you using?

2) How did you install your video card drivers?

3) How did you install the game?

4) Are you running the game using Direct3D or OpenGL?

5) Do you experience any similar problems when launching other games or 3D-intensive applications?

From my experiences, I have been running World of Warcraft on Ubuntu for almost two years now. Presently, the game runs very well on Hardy considering the age of my hardware (AMD Athlon 64 3200+ {Socket 754}, 2 GB DDR RAM, GeForce 7800GS AGP). If you're looking for a solution that provides technical support, I would highly recommend that you try out CrossOver Games. I have been using the CrossOver product for about two years and I cannot recommend it enough. I used it exclusively for World of Warcraft before I purchased The Orange Box in addition to other Steam titles. The product is superb and the Codeweavers team is an honest, knowledgeable, hard-working group, and they also give back to the Wine project which is always a plus.
