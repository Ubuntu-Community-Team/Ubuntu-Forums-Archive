### Post by stevem531 on 2012-09-09
I am new to Ubuntu, and as I have a few Windows programs that I would like to be able to use in the new environment, running them under Wine looked like a good thing to try to begin with.

I am not a member of the Wine Forum so I though I would post here as a suitable place to share some of my initial experiences

Amongst my Windows applications are two programs by Algorithmix in their 'Sound Rescue' product series, both closed source, and used for restoring noisy sound recordings :-

1. Sound Rescue 3.0

2. Sound Rescue TerraTec edition 2.1 -  bundled for use with some TerraTec sound cards.

Based on the (albeit limited) testing that I have done so far on Ubuntu 12.04.1 with Wine 1.4.1.4  they seem to be at least basically functional with just some graphics issues affecting the Sound Rescue TerraTec 2.1 version.

I am especially pleased that Sound Rescue 3.0 appears to be working with Wine as I remember paying actual money for it not that long ago. (But that was in the days before I discovered open source software :P.)

I have looked in the Wine reviews but could not find any references to these programs so perhaps they could be added to the list once some more testing has been done, possibly by me if no one else beats me to it !!

Best Wishes
