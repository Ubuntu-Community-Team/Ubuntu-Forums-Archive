### Post by Nico0020 on 2010-07-26
I game on linux for what works easily.  I'm a student so I only have a laptop.  So gaming on anything more intensive than say Half Life 2 is not an option.  So far in wine or crossover anything I like to play works easily as it would in windows.  I still have a windows partition but it is very tiny. 

Main games I play are like plants vs zombies and Sven Co-op (HL mod).  I am not much of a PC gamer at all, but have quite a few steam games I got on good deals.  I have just about all the valve games (even though my laptop can't run some of em).  I used to play a lot of UT2K4 with my friends and loved that it was native.  Now most people on there just play boring racing mods so I mainly play UT3 on my 360 (where most my gaming occurs.) 

If steam actually did come out native for linux I would probably buy more games and play them on my laptop.  I never really ran into problems playing UT2K4 native which I just completely loved.   I was VERY disappointed when UT3 never got a linux client like it was supposed to.  That was a big slap to the face of the linux and osx gamers out there.    

I have steam installed on my windows partition, but don't really use it (or windows for that matter) ever.  If steam were native on linux I think there would be tons of users buying games for it.  With that indie games pack where you got to name your price, linux users were the most generous across all three platforms since we are starved for games.  A platform like steam is very much what linux needs to get its foot in the door of the gaming world.  

As you can tell I didn't really have a direction on where I was going with this, but those are my thoughts.  In the end I like to game on linux because my computer is always on, so I use gaming to kill time.  It needs to be quick and easy to install (native or painless through wine) and let me unwind while I play with no random crashes or errors.
