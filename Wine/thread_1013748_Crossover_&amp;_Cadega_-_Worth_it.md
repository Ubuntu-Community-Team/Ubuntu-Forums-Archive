### Post by ajackson on 2008-12-17
CrossOver can be worth it and they also but back into the wine project.

I think they include some stuff that isn't really right for inclusion in mainstream wine (ie hacks to get around certain problems) to get their supported applications working. It also makes having multiple WINEPREFIXes (called bottles under CX) easier to deal with and of course you do get official support.

Cedega don't put back into wine and from what I've seen there support is very poor, which considering that you pay a monthly subscription is poor. With CX you pay a one-off fee (OK I think major updates you have to pay for but don't quote me on that as I'm not 100%).

At the end of the day if vanilla wine does what you want and you don't mind dealing with multiple WINEPREFIXes (if you need them) yourself then I'd stick with wine. If you like the reassurance of paid support and like having a GUI front end to make configuration easier then go for CrossOver as that does help the wine project.

### Post by FrozenFox on 2008-12-18
Almigi:

PlayOnLinux uses the current system version of wine or any other version of wine you might install with it (it can download and handle multiple versions for you), so you will get no performance difference from PlayOnLinux than regular wine. The only two possible exceptions to this is if they have an installer script with some kind of custom configs for your desired game (they dont for yours, you said) or if a different version of wine than the default one on your system is used in it and runs better.
