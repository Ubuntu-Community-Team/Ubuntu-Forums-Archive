### Post by handy on 2008-01-22
My understanding is that Cider is being implemented as a collaboration between Transgaming & interested game developers/distribution companies.  Transgaming are selling it as a cheaper solution than porting the game to OS X, & any copy protection can remain intact.

There is no way that a Cider game will work with Linux.

Transgaming give nothing back to the community, or Wine, they are purely interested in their shareholders & they make their product sound better than it really is.  Their support is also virtually non existent.

Codeweavers, on the other hand, give back their improvements to Wine.  They have been developing Wine in the OS X direction, & they have stated that they are going to start handling copy protection in their soon to be released version 7 of CrossOver.

They have made dramatic improvements to Guild Wars over the last couple of months in their pre release versions, for both OS X & Linux.

I have found the attitude at Codeweavers to be the opposite of that found at Transgaming.  The dev's reply & have a consistent presence on the Codeweavers' forum, & the attitude is very honest & positive all round.

CrossOver costs a comparatively small amount compared to Cedega's monthly hit as well.
