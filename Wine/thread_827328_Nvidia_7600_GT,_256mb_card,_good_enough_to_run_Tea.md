### Post by Sleaka J on 2008-06-13
I have an nVidia 7600GS and I can't get decent framerates in Team Fortress 2 in DX9 mode using Wine. TF2 works perfectly fine in Windows in DX9 mode, but I have to revert to DX8 mode to get acceptable framerates using Wine. Even so, I still use TF2 under Windows because it still runs better. It's smooth in Windows DX9, but still jerky (but playable) in Wine DX8. You WILL encounter a performance drop.

As for Source and Steam being ported to Linux? A job to port Source to Linux on the Valve website has been there for a couple of years. Valve have repeatedly said "No" when people ask if they will port Steam to Linux, even going as far as banning people who constantly ask the question on their forums. It is highly likely that if they fill the position they will only port the Source engine for other developers to use, but will not port Valve games (Half-Life 2, Counterstrike, Team Fortress2 ).

Considering there are numerous other developers who use Steam as a distribution method, they would all have to port their games to Linux and the chances of that happening are 0.0000000000000000000000000000000001%. Native Linux Steam will NEVER happen as long as the userbase is so small.
