### Post by Welly Wu on 2015-06-26
[http://www.pcworld.com/article/2940470/hey-gamers-directx-11-is-coming-to-linux-thanks-to-codeweavers-and-wine.html](http://www.pcworld.com/article/2940470/hey-gamers-directx-11-is-coming-to-linux-thanks-to-codeweavers-and-wine.html)

I own Codeweavers' CrossOver for Ubuntu 64 bit GNU/Linux and I have been waiting for this news headline to break for several years. This software product will support Microsoft DirectX 11 technology by the end of 2015 and it will mean that more Microsoft Windows 7, 8.1, and 10 64 bit PC games will just work right out of the box using CrossOver for GNU/Linux. It also means that other Microsoft software products that rely on DirectX 11 will be supported as well such as Office 2013 or 2016 perhaps. It could be possible that Microsoft Office 2016 will rely on DirectX 12 and future versions as well. In any event, this will make CrossOver a better software product that is more modern and useful for both GNU/Linux and Apple Macintosh OS X customers worldwide. This means that a larger and more modern list of Microsoft software products will become compatible and officially supported by Codeweavers in the future if you purchase, register, install, and use CrossOver. Finally!
