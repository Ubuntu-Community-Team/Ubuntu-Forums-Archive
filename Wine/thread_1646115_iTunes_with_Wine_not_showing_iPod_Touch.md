### Post by cogadh on 2010-12-17
Considering you are talking about something as sensitive as a firmware update, you are much better off doing it the way it is supposed to be done and not shoe-horning it through Wine and Linux. Find a Windows machine (or Mac) and use that to run it; firmware updates can far too easily "brick" a piece of electronics to risk trying to do it any other way.
