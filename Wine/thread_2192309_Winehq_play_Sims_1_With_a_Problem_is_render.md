### Post by lah7 on 2013-12-09
The only version of The Sims 1 that's had a bit of success is The Sims Complete Collection. If you don't have this version, I'm afraid you are out of luck. The original and all of its expansion packs, are rated garbage since the game simply refuses to start for various reasons. :(

The complete collection has a good chance of not starting, as it's rating of Bronze states: [http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=8696](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=8696)

It requires GLSL to be disabled and there's a lot of tweaking around in winecfg. Even if you get the game working, it's still unplayable as:
> **3D Graphics:**[COLOR=#000000][FONT=bitstream vera sans] The sims themselves do not render, they're simply white and **cannot interact with another sim**.[/FONT][/COLOR]

Installation may succeed, but the game is in an unstable state at the moment. If you insist in trying anyway, install the latest wine package, disable GLSL, and have a play around, but if you intend to play this game properly, it's no use. A prefix manager like PlayOnLinux might be easier to manage wine versions to the latest and tweaking the advanced settings. Do this at your own risk.

I know, it would be great if The Sims 1 & 2 worked under Wine, maybe some day!
