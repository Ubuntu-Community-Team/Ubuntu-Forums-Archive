### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-11-27
I think we should put together a list of games that don't use any rootkit/device driver hacking prevention, since Wine can't currently emulate it (which may be a good thing since many Windows viruses act the same way). This way, we could get more of the MMO crowd in the door and help some of those gamers to take Linux more seriously in their communities (MMO communities are HUGE). If there's a Linux option, gamers are more likely to try it out just to boost their speed and decrease lag, which works well in a lot of Wine games.

So, these don't have to be reported as working, just available for testing, since Gameguard stuff is pretty much out of the question at this point. I'll state the obvious.

World of Warcraft (working)
Guild Wars (working)
Final Fantasy XI (working)

This is as much for my own benefit as anyone else who chooses to contribute. Hoping I can get one of my friends to put the list on one of their Linux informing sites for people to bookmark (or even this thread if we get enough hits). This is a giant category of Windows software that should be addressed, and while I also believe productivity applications may hold a higher priority, making this compatibility visible gets a lot more people in the door to fix bugs than just a few business users.
