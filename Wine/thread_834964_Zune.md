### Post by cogadh on 2008-06-20
Wine runs software, not hardware. If you are trying to use a particular piece of Windows software to access your Zune, it might work (depending on the software), but if you are having issues with your Zune being detected by the OS, then Wine won't help at all. If your Zune is detected OK and it is an MTP device, then [Amarok](http://amarok.kde.org/) should allow you to manage your media on the device. Amarok is the default media player in KDE, but it works in Gnome too and is available through the Ubuntu repositories.
