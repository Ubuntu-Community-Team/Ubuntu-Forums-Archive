### Post by cogadh on 2010-05-10
Frankly, it is a miracle, even after 15 years of development, that anything at all runs in Wine. They built it from the ground up simply working from Microsoft's ****-poor documentation of the Windows API and with no access to Window's underlying code at all.

As for why after all this time the problems with Garena haven't been fixed, its probably because the 7 or 8 documented bugs still associated with Garena are not a high priority. The [major bug related to WC3]("http://bugs.winehq.org/show_bug.cgi?id=15518") only has 16 votes on it with a "normal" priority. There are a hundred or so bugs with a higher priority than Garena's and it is among the *many* hundreds of bugs with that same priority. If you want the problems fixed sooner, get involved in Wine, even if that only means voting for bugs to increase their priority.
