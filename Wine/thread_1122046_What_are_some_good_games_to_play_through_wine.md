### Post by jnewl on 2009-04-13
As others have said, the WineApp Database is your best bet for finding out what runs in Wine, and how well. Nevertheless, here for your viewing pleasure are the the better-running Windows games that currently occupy slots on my Games menu list:

Armageddon Empires (sometimes has problems generating the gamemap; otherwise, flawless)

Avencast: Rise of the Mage (flawless, except for sometimes being blocked by an invisible wall that can be defeated using game cheats)

Elven Legacy (flawless with shadowmaps turned off)

Euro Truck Simulator (flawless)

Fantasy Wars (flawless with shadowmaps turned off)

F.E.A.R. 2 -- Project ORIGIN (flawless)

Ghost Master (flawless)

Grand Ages Rome (flawless, but for some minor graphical corruption involving the surrounding terrain)

Mount & Blade (flawless, but for a mildly annoying mouse capture problem)

Northland (flawless)

Railroad Pioneer (flawless as far as I can remember; haven't played in awhile)

Ryzom (flawless)

Sins of a Solar Empire w/ Entrenchment expansion (flawless, but for a few inconsequential text overruns)

Space Colony (flawless)

Spaceforce: Rogue Universe (flawless; had to do some major fiddling to get it going initially, though)

Space Rangers 2 (flawless)

The Nations (flawless)

Birth of America 2: Wars in America (flawless)

--------------------------------
Running with Ubuntu 8.10 Intrepid Ibex, AMD Athlon 64X2 2400+, 2 Gb RAM, Nvidia Geforce 8800GT, winetricks fully installed
