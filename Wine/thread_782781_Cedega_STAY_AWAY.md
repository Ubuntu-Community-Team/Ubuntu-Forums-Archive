### Post by abhiroopb on 2008-05-05
I was really keen on playing COD4 which I had purchased a while back. It was on 2 DVDs. I tried with wine with all the necessary patches (such as directx, etc). And it gave me an error when starting. So, I thought that it wouldn't be too bad to pay GBP 10 (in the UK) to play COD4. You have to get minimum 3 months subscription. So I got it and before it even started installing I got an error (NB: worse than wine). I had actually gone to this page: [http://games.cedega.com/gamesdb/](http://games.cedega.com/gamesdb/) and seen that COD4 was a most popular game, and had naturally assumed it worked. Honestly I had spent a lot of time researching the error I had gotten with wine, and I thought that since I was paying cedega it would be a simple install. Not so.

I tried UT3, again I got an error when installing.

I tried Condition Zero, and this time it installed but I couldn't figure out how to play (as the play button had been greyed out).

I tried UT2k4, and it didn't install.

NB: ALL these games should have worked.

To be fair I was frustrated and didn't really read the forums thoroughly. Also, I had found a linux installer script for UT2K4 and happily started playing that and forgot about Cedega.

All this was only yesterday.

Today I looked at the forums and found a lot of people disliked cedega. Basically people expected it to work out of the box. The response was overwhelmingly that it would not, and that people were "stupid" to think that it would work so easily and that the Cedega team were working hard at fixing all the problems and some tweaking is always required.

Frankly I was appalled. If a game didn't run in wine, I'd say fine I'll try and figure it out, I'm not paying anything so it really doesn't make a difference.

GBP 10 isn't much, but according to the cedega page:

"An easy, out-of-the-box gaming experience is at your fingertips. There's no better way to play on Linux!"

What I expected was a simple install as in windows.

I am a long time Ubuntu user and I know that tweaking is often required. After all people are making these software out of the goodness of their hearts I can't expect out of box experience for everything. However, when I pay for something (no matter how much), I except out of box experience when it is EXPLICITLY stated.

I agree the team has done a great job at making a clean useful GUI interface (something not always common in linux), and they are working hard. However, I am paying for a software, and to be told by people on the forum that: 

"f WoW isn't working its likely not the fault of Cedega.

I have played WoW on Cedega from the day the game went retail. So have many others.

However, as mentioned above it looks like you didn't even look at the Wow forums before posting..."

Ummm okay... So an out of box experience involves me looking through hundreds of threads?

and...

"I think the guy thought that WoW works on cedega 'out of the box' for some strange reason...."

I hope this guy was being sarcastic.

Anyway if I hadn't paid for it I would most certainly have gone through forums. Do, I complain that hibernate still doesn't work in ubuntu? No, I have read up on ACPI and know the difficulties involved here, and I can manage without it. 

However, if hibernate didn't work in windows, I wouldn't go through forums to figure out why, I would call up tech support!

According to a lot of forum members tech support will take a long time to reply. In any case it was arguably my fault for not having seen that COD4 would not work (at least for now), even though it was listed as working, but there was contradictory evidence in the forums.

Anyway if I ever get around to buying a new game I will try it out.

In my opinion use wine, its free, thats it really!!

PS: Apologies if this seems a little harsh, again I understand that Cedega developers work hard and the payment is not that much. I guess I was just shocked at the responses in the forums more than anything else, and I have been so happy with ubuntuforums that I was shocked that the linux community becomes so defensive about the developers rather than helping the consumers.
