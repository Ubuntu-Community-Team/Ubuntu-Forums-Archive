### Post by cogadh on 2007-12-09
CrossOver is the retail version of Wine and it is usually a little behind Wine in terms of its completeness (it is based on older versions of Wine that are established as mostly stable). However, any development that is done to CrossOver goes directly back into the Wine tree and vice versa. So if CrossOver improves something like DirectX support, that improvement ends up included in Wine. On top of that, CrossOver is now the main financial backer of Wine, so by purchasing CrossOver, you are also supporting the financial needs of further Wine development.

To summarize:
CrossOver and Wine - fantastic products, well worth using and supporting
Cedega - giant POS

### Post by cameran on 2007-12-09
i've been using cedega for a few months now and i think it's a fantastic product.  after seeing the 70+ pages and pages thread on how getting guild wars to run with wine, i was pleasantly surprised to be able to pop in my guild wars discs and be playing in no time with graphics on highest quality and high quality sound.

my guild wars performance is about 95% of what i got in windows.  i have yet to be able to get guild wars to work with wine.  also, half-life 2 would not work for me in wine but works flawlessly (thanks to the recent snudda update from cedega) in cedega, with awesome graphics and excellent performance.

i was also able to pop in oblivion and morrowind with cedega and have them running in no time as well.  my girlfriend did the same with morrowind and cedega, having excellent performance on her older machine.

the only game i run in wine is lord of the rings online (lotro) because i couldn't get it to work with cedega.  it runs excellent in wine, i am very impressed.

i'm a linux web server admin at work, and while i have nothing but respect for the wine team, spending lots of time messing with the registry and utilizing various commandline switches when trying to play a game after spending all day at work messing with commandline and various switches doesn't seem like much fun to me.  to be able to just run my games with a gui tweaker fast and easy makes cedega something i really enjoy and has made my home linux experience even better than it was before.

i'm not trying to disregard your apparently negative experiences with cedega but offer an alternative experience.  there are many users of cedega that i know who use it with no problems.

i guess the moral of the story is: your mileage may vary.  wine is great and so is cedega (for me and my friends at least).  we play more games under cedega than wine, and have spent very little time "tweaking" cedega to get games to run.  i spent more time tweaking wine for lotro than i did tweaking cedega for guild wars, half-life 2, oblivion, and morrowind combined.  for my friends and i, cedega "just works".

i think the best approach is to take a look at the game you're trying to use and ask around about what works best and people's experiences.  if there's anything about online forums it's that everybody has an opinion about something :popcorn:

my .02 cents, hope it helps somebody!

cameran
