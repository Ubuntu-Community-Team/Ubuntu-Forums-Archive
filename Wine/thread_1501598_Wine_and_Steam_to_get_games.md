### Post by cogadh on 2010-06-04
Steam is just a storefront and game management client, a separate piece of software from the games themselves. You will have to check AppDB, not all games sold on Steam will work with Wine.

### Post by cogadh on 2010-06-04
No, you can't put the games on disk, they do require Steam to install them and run them (Steam is basically the copy protection for the games) but you can re-download and install them to as many computers as you own (in most cases, there are exceptions), as long as you install Steam on those computers first. In theory, you could install Steam on your virtual machine, re-download your game and run it, assuming your virtual machine support 3D hardware acceleration.
