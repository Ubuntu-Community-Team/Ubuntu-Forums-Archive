### Post by woodsmoke on 2008-02-10
HI
I'm a poster over at Klikit.
I have successfully run a train simulator called Train Player in Wine.

And, it even puts an icon on the desktop!

It is not like MS Train Sim, not a "sit in the cab" kind of thing, but it instead is two parts.

a) first part is the "player".  One "plays" model trains on "virtual tracks" that have been overlaid on "maps" of model train libraries, all of the 101 model RR layouts of Linn Wescott and others are provided.  These range from the very simple to the quite complex.

Everything works, really..... except the "graphics" of the customized "tops" of the cars.  It is an overhead view of the layout.  The customized cars are rendered with some kind of specialized "rotatable" graphic, which Linux hasn't been able to get to work yet.

What one sees are "generic" tops that are color coded by car type.

However, all the rest works, the physics, the coupling and uncoupling and sounds. etc.

b) the second part is "track layer".  it is a very simple, but not simplistic, track layout proggy that completely works, and I mean COMPLETELY...and one can put trains on the track and they run immediately, all the way to making a helix.

It is not the "lay track sections" of Atlas, you "draw" the track.

You can then run the train, and if it is ok you can print it and build the layout.

If you send them the layout, and they like it, they said they might publish it.

Unfortunately this is a paid proggy, but you can download a timed demo to confirm that it does work.

It works so well because it is not bound up with DirectX like the other proggys of the type.

I worked with them somewhat(mainly me telling them what didn't work and what it looked like) to determine just what it is that doesn't allow Linux to see the tops of the customized cars, and they are going to give consideration to whether it would be financially worthwhile to do the graphics in some other manner.

They have had a few Linux folks inquire about a port but not that many to seem to justify it. 

If you're into this type of train proggy, since it DOES otherwise work, you might d/l it and comment to them, if for no other reason than to show them that there is, at least, "some" demand from the Linux community.

If you ARE going to download it, it "just installs" with a click under WINE, simple as that, you do have to go through the "Windows" installer as usual.

The ONLY thing you should remember when you run it, is to go to "options" and there is a box to tick that is "show cars', tick it to "NOT" show cars(that is the rotatable fancy graphics) and you will see the "default" shapes and colors.

[LINKY TO THE SITE ]("http://www.trainplayer.com/")

woodsmoke
