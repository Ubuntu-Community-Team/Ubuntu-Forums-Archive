### Post by stan.distortion on 2011-11-25
> **Dlambert said:**
> Regnum wont work on Integrated graphics. [RUNES OF MAGIC IS A GREAT GAME]("http://us.runesofmagic.com/us/downloads,client,us.html")
Runes of magic is possibly the most inefficient, bloated and bug ridden pieces of crap that ever infested my system and was deleted when it refused to run under wine. Hardly surprising as it didnt run under windows for very long before crashing either.

Ryzom is highly recommended, its open source now and it free to play up to about half the maximum skill levels and thats a lot of game time. It has many great features, the spell system is the best I have ever seen in any rpg and crafting is nicely done. Kind of dated now but not at all in a bad way and has very friendly and helpful players.

Regnum has come a long way in the last few years, it's lacking in features and content atm as the dev's have been working hard on the game engine to improve performance, stability, graphics and balance. What it lacks in content it more than makes up for in the core of the gameplay which battles. These range from 1 on 1 pvp right up to massive battles with hundreds of players involving focused combat and careful strategic planning. It's more or less unique in this area (influenced by Dark age of Camelot), not as twitchy and fast paced as a fps and much more depth in battles than any other guild v guild or realm v realm game i've tried. Highly addictive.

Savage 2 is more like a fps than an rpg, fast and furious and great fun. Graphics are nice, performance is good and never had stability issues or other bugs. The game has a command option, a single player commands ground units much like a rts. With a good commander and units (players) that follow commands it works very well. A little to fast and furious for my tastes but well worth checking out.

Planeshift is.... Planeshift. It has some nice features and loads of content but its not highly polished and feels a little clunky. Macro's are a very nice feature and crafting and harvesting are well thought out and based on real world rules rather than making electricity pole sized swords out of spider guts and flowers. Not tried the role playing servers, I should have tried them as it would be very good as a role playing game. The highest quality pure open source mmorpg I know of so would be the first to recommend trying for that alone.

An as yet unreleased title worth keeping an eye on is Dilogus: The Winds of War. Its using the Unigine engine which is the main benchmark for high quality linux graphics and appears to have an open minded and imaginative development team. Very little information available at the mo but announcements are expected before the end of the year.

Sorry for dredging up an old thread, its one of the first to come up searching for linux mmorpg's so wanted to bring it a little more up to date.
