### Post by khelben1979 on 2011-04-14
If you got some applications in the background, I suggest you close them down to save some RAM, that can increase performance. 

At the present, not knowing anything about your pc hardware it's hard to determine where the bottleneck is. Slow harddrives can cause a performance drop as well as the free open source video drivers which are slow in comparison to non-free.

I would agree to the above poster that you probably cannot do much to increase your performance, you got an old graphic chipset over there which is restricted to open source video drivers.

You might see some performance increase in the next version of Ubuntu, because they have done some work with the open source video drivers affecting the newer kernels, but it would not include any major increase in fps for that video chipset, from what I believe.
