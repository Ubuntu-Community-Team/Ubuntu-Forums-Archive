### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2018-03-24
Because MS-Windows and Linux are completely different Operating Systems, a Windows program (MS-Office) cannot be installed or run in Linux unless you run a middleware program called Wine . . Or . . you install a program called a Virtual Machine (like Virtual Box).

But, before spending time to understand what Wine is or what a Virtual Machine is . . . just open the program "LibreOffice" and do a document or do a spreadsheet and then save it using the format for MS-Windows 97.    (the document will then have a .doc extension, and the spreadsheet will have the.xls extension.    Windows will then recognize it and open it, and likewise, LibreOffice will open those documents saved by MS-Office.
