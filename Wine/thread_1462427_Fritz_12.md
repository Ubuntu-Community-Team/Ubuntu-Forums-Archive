### Post by cogadh on 2010-04-26
Terminal output = run the application from the terminal. To do that, just open a terminal window, change directories to wherever the app is installed, then type "wine <executable name>.exe"; obviously change that <executable name> to the correct file name for your app.

System info = your hardware specs. To be completely honest, if you don't know what hardware you have in your PC, you probably shouldn't be using Wine or Linux for that matter.

AppDB = Wine website. Its an abbreviation of Application Database, basically a searchable list of all the applications that Wine users have tested, including information on what they may have had to do to get a particular app working. [http://appdb.winehq.org/](http://appdb.winehq.org/)
