### Post by pafufta503 on 2011-02-17
wine is definitely not always going to work with software outofthebox.  some apps, generally the most popularly used ones for wine, have support.  this includes many games and office suites.  other software is hit or miss, as wine is not trying to emulate windows, but replace the necessary driver support to run the software.  this means that a win32 program might expect the OS to do something, that wine or linux may not support (like USB).

wine, i would say, is for the adventurous and persistent computer user.  if you are willing to study support forums and try new things on your computer, then go ahead.  otherwise, for people like my mother or older brother, i wouldn't even mention wine.  for them things need to work asis, so wine is not for them.

so no, wine is not a joke, the programs i have seen running in wine amaze me they even run at all.  oblivion for example, in 2006 i would have never in a million years thought it would run smoothly on anything but windows, but here we are!  the future looks bright :)
