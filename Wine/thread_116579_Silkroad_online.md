### Post by ddcc on 2007-07-11
Finding out how to get most apps to work with wine is pretty simple, you go to appdb.winehq.org, then search for the name of the app and look at the comments and instructions. The link to Silkroad Online's "profile" is [http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iVersionId=5391](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iVersionId=5391) . I've also reproduced the instructions below. I'd attach screenshots, but I'm not at my computer, I'm using a shared computer elsewhere.

1)Make sure that your computer meets the recommended requirements for Silkroad (Pentium 4 or higher, 512MB RAM, 3D and DirectX 9.0b compatible video card, and 3GB of free HDD space) and that you have the necessary drivers for your graphics card. 
2)Follow the instructions at [http://www.winehq.org/site/download-deb](http://www.winehq.org/site/download-deb) to add the wine repository, then follow the directions to use Synaptic or Terminal to download and install the package. 
3)Download the Silkroad client and unzip it if you need to. Then run "wine executable.exe" (without quotes and replace executable.exe with the silkroad installer exe) 
4)Run "winecfg" in terminal (without the quotes) and set compatibility for sro_client.exe and silkroad.exe (in folder ~/.wine/drive_c/Program Files/Silkroad) to Windows 98. 
5)Run the client either from Applications>Wine>Programs>Joymax>Silkroad or from your Desktop. 
6)Wait for patching to complete. 
7)Hit play. 
Depending on your distro and other variables, you may need to build wine from source in order to get Silkroad to work at a usable speed.

### Post by BuggyDE on 2010-03-28
An old post i know, but it come top of google search for silkroad online.

I have silkroad running under wine on Ubuntu 9.10 64bit with only minor issues.

PC Spec:
Asus F3sv 
Nvidia 8600m GS (256MB)
4GB memory
Ubuntu 9.10 64Bit 

Software:
SilkroadOnline_GlobalOfficial_v1_240
Wine --version - 1.0.1

So far everything has been working well except some minor sound issues, which are sorted out if i load SR before I load any other programs.

Silkroad has changed from gameguard to HackShield from what i can see, gameguard does not work right on linux, but hackshield seems to be more linux "friendly" than gameguard.

Since I quit playing this game when I moved full time to Ubuntu and did not want a dual boot setup, i thought i would give it another go today and have been surprised at how well it is working now. 

Good luck
