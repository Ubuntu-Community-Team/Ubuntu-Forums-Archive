### Post by buntunub on 2007-11-15
I decided to try this game out because CCP offers a free Linux wrapper. The .deb that you can install just works great, and installing the windows version via wine works even better after you place the ariel.ttf into the wine drive fonts folder. Anyway, this was a HUGE move by CCP to reach out to the Linux community, and so I supported them on it. Anyway, never having given a space based game a go, I figured I would be in for a mixed bag with a very steep learning curve. The learning curve is a bit steep at first, but there is tons of things to do and many paths you can choose, so there never is a dull moment as there always is with most of the MMORPG's ive tried (WoW, DAOC, Guild Wars, etc.). Also, the game is totally open PVP based, which is a definite plus because that adds an even better element. 

Do you Ubuntu fans play or have you tried out EVE? Are you willing to give it a shot on the 14 day credit cardless free trial now that they offer a .deb client?..

I hope you all do. I think its important that the Linux community stand behind gaming companies when they make gestures like these, as so very few do. One could argue that its not a full Linux port, and this is true, but how much development time/effort would have to go in to doing something as big as that? Im sure it would be a very LARGE investment on CCP's part to write a whole client patch all in Linux and then make it totally compatible with their servers, work out bugs, etc. etc... Based completely on a 2% market share. Sounds like a very bad ROI (Return on Investiment) strategy to me, but at least they worked with Transgaming to create a completely stable, free Linux wrapper with a .deb package, unlike Blizzard, Mythic, EA, and many others whom alot of you support despite the fact.

Given the above, I will support CCP with this, and in truth it IS a much better and more enjoyable game than WoW or DAOC, at least for me. I plan to make a Corporation (Guild) on EVE strictly for Linux/Ubuntu users which I will call "Penguin Raiders", or something similar as I am open to suggestion. I do hope that you all will join me in this endeavor, and that we will rule our portion of the universe fueled on Linux!

From the Transgaming newsletter:

> EVE Now on Linux and Mac - Free 14 Day Trial


If you haven't heard EVE Online is now available on both Linux and Mac. For those who just want to jump in and start playing you can get a free 14 Day Trial here.  

We are very proud of this release. For the first time ever EVE Online is available on multiple platforms to a worldwide audience.  Thanks to Cedega and Cider, both Linux and Mac players will be able to join the countless Windows users on EVE's single shard server. This means that everyone gets to play with everyone else, regardless of operating system.  With TransGaming providing portability, CPP gets to remain focused on what they do best: building the most sophisticated MMO available. Behind the scenes CCP and TransGaming have closely tied their processes together so that no one gets left out in the lurch. EVE patches will be tested on all systems before releases to ensure that all three platforms are updated simultaneous and so that every update, patch, revision happens in parallel for Windows, Mac, and Linux users.  ! 
 
The official announcement was made on the last day of the EVE Online Fanfest, a three day party where pilots get to rub elbows with the folks behind the game. Let me tell you the folks at CCP really know how to throw a shindig while at the same time hosting great forums, round tables, technical sessions, and more. TransGaming also facilitated Mac and Linux specific round-tables that were met with wonderful enthusiasm.

During the key note, Hilmar Petursson, CCP’s CEO invited TransGaming's CEO, Vikas Gupta, to the stage to unveil the Mac and Linux EVE clients. The reception was excellent from Mac, Linux and Windows  alike.  

“Players will get a congruent experience on any platform,” announced Vikas to the applause of the crowd.  

Mac players were treated to the site of 4 concurrent EVE sessions playing on a single Mac Pro. Did I mention the 30” cinema display. It was gorgeous.   

The  TransGaming staff that were able to make it to Reykjavik, Iceland for Fanfest almost hit the level of celebrities. In fact when one happy Mac user fell to his knees and bowed before TransGaming founder Gav State we knew that everyone was happy for the arrival of EVE on Linux and Mac.

TransGaming would like to thank CCP for their recent hospitality and for having the courage to break into new territory. We hope that this level of unprecedented integration of Cedega and Cider into one of the fastest growing online games in the world, and definitely the most sophisticated online game in the world, serves as a model to bring other top titles to Linux and Mac.

Get more information on this release at the EVE Online information page.
