### Post by JeQhdMD on 2014-10-22
OK, one thing to remember about "installing" in general . . . . use the Ubuntu Software Center (USC) to install the package PlayonLinux first . . . (no need to download from any other source/location/website).

_Follow the workflow provided by the software install "wizard"_, re handling of your .exe file.    NOT all windows games & programs are supported.   Running Wine without the PlayonLinux front-end may require more config and work.

You might go to the Wine webpage to see if your particular game is actually supported, and at what level.  (If I recall - platinum and gold are highly supported, silver, bronze less so, others not at all).

I highly recommend using native Linux games for the best experience, there are hundreds available via Valve/Steam, ([http://store.steampowered.com/browse/linux/](http://store.steampowered.com/browse/linux/)) and many others available via the repos (USC) and other 3rd party PPA's . . just google "gaming on linux"

Supported games matrix:   [https://appdb.winehq.org/](https://appdb.winehq.org/) . . . . looks like your game not supported very well (use search field at this site to see info)
