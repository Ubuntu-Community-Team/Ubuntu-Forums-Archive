### Post by Marshal0505 on 2009-01-28
There is no single best version.
Some applications perform better under either an older or newer version.As new versions of wine introduce regressions and fixes for an ever changing list of applications.Trying to determine what is the 'best' version is a hopeless attempt to oversimplify this situation.Instead you should find out what works best for you considering the apps you use.

### Post by cogadh on 2009-01-28
CrossOver Games uses the latest bleeding edge Wine code as it applies to gaming. Regular CrossOver, which is better suited for office-type applications, relies on the stable Wine versions. Both are updated regularly, though not as often as Wine updates. Since CodeWeavers, the makers of CrossOver, are the primary financial backers of the Wine project and nearly all of their code ends up back in Wine, neither can ever really be considered "out of date".
