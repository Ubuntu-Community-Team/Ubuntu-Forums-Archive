### Post by cogadh on 2007-11-09
You should try reading it yourself. Fair use, as defined in the EUCD and even the DMCA declare that you can circumvent copy protection for the purposes of "noninfringing uses of copyrighted material", such as running a legally owned copy protected game in Linux or making a legal backup of a purchased DVD. What is illegal is the process used to create the cracked executable and the distribution of the crack publicly, as it violates license provisions against reverse engineering of the executable and allows for circumvention for infringing purposes, i.e. piracy. Before making use of a crack, you really should check on what the law is in your country, just to be sure you aren't doing anything illegal.

### Post by cogadh on 2007-11-09
The DMCA's attempted limitations on fair use are in violation of basic Constitutional law. No single case based on the use of cracks with legally purchased software or making backups of legally owned media has ever been prosecuted. Even the lawmakers know it can't stand up in court.

Basically, if you:
[LIST=1]
[*]Legally own a copy of the copyrighted material
[*]Don't copy it for the purposes of reselling
[*]Don't copy it for the purposes of claiming it as your original work
[*]Modify it for productive use only (i.e. running a Windows game on Linux in Wine)
[/LIST]
then it is considered fair use, is in no way illegal and is protected by Constitutional law in the US. No matter what an EULA says, it can't change that.
