### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-08-26
K, you guys. I've run a few newly purchased softwares, and some old stuff this week. Like, stuff that should be well documented on the appDB.

WinMUGEN (which I don't need since there's a Linux version, but valuable nonetheless with extra hacks), Well of Souls, And a new game, Virtual Families.

But there are many other programs I've been using with increasing compatibility and perfect use in Wine lately. However, the appDB doesn't reflect most of these improvements. Some of these programs aren't even on there yet!

So, I challenge all of the people who are lucky enough to have their favorite Windows-only programs running flawlessly (or good) in Linux to sign up, get an appDB account, and either 1. Learn to report bugs with the bugs system, or 2. Update the pages for your favorite applications if they're outdated.

We can't see the progress (or regressions) with Wine unless people put up current results on an annual basis, I think. If we aren't gonna' donate, we can at least donate our time to improving the database's accuracy.

That's all.

P.S. Please refrain from making comments like, "find alternatives," or "Wine is bad for Linux." Some of us need our paid-for, professional, Windows-only software to work in Linux, and I have personally been able to continue doing my job with Linux thanks to Wine. We'd all love our programs ported over, but we can't persuade everyone. Not yet.
