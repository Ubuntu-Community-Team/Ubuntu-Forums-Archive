### Post by Praadur on 2008-01-03
(It looks like Nevon beat me to the punch with the short answer, so I'll offer the long one instead!)

There are two systems for getting Windows-native games to run under Linux: Wine and Cedega.

Unfortunately, neither of them can do MapleStory at this time (according to their database pages) and this seems to be because of GameGuard, which is apparently a particularly unfriendly thing to say the least.  You might have some success if you can somehow remove GameGuard from MapleStory, but I can't help you with that.

You do have some other alternatives you could look at, though...

First of all, you could stop by the [MMORPG list](http://appdb.winehq.org/appbrowse.php?catId=103) at WineHQ and see if there's anything you like the look of there, but keep in mind that you're better off only paying attention to games that have been reported as running at a Gold or Platinum level, as they have the broadest chance of success across various distros and wine versions.

If you do come across such a game, you could [install wine](http://www.winehq.org/site/download-deb) with the instructions at the page I've just linked.  Once you've done that, download the game you're interested in and from the terminal, run the exe with 'wine filename.exe', where filename.exe is the name of the installer you've downloaded.

Failing that, there is one asian-style MMO out there which has released a Linux version called [Regnum Online](http://www.regnumonline.com.ar/), so you could try that out if messing with wine sounds too complicated.

I've had some pretty good results with wine though, and I've used it to play an MMO or two, so you might want to look into that.  It's a shame that all games don't already work with it, and that some such as MapleStory are a lost cause at the moment, but work on wine is progressing all the time, and you never know what the future of that project might hold.  If it keeps steaming ahead like it has been, you might be able to play your favourite MMO on Linux sooner than you think.
