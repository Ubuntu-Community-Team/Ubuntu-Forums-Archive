3.what are necessary Winetricks to be installed and the recommended to gurantee working of Babylon and other windows common program?

1.  Wine does have a Add/Remove Programs utility, but like the windows equivalent it doesn't work that well.  It is often better to just delete or create a new WINEPREFIX.

2.  From reports in the winehq appdb, it looks like Babylon partially works.  Since wine is constantly improving, reports more than a few months old maybe inaccurate.

3. Many windows apps have different requirements to work in wine.  Stay focused on getting one working at a time. See the winehq.org appdb for tips to try and get Babylon working.
