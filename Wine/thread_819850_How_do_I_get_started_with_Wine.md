### Post by cogadh on 2008-06-06
I'm sorry, but that is not accurate nor was it all that useful. Yes, in **some** cases, you will need to get DLL files from Windows, but if you are using the most recent version of Wine, those instances are actually rather rare. Not to mention, you don't really need to get into the whole possiblity of replacing DLL files when you haven't even tried to configure Wine yet.

Secondly, don't waste your time Googling for an install/configuration guide for any app until after you have checked Wine's [Application Database](http://appdb.winehq.org/). 9 times out of 10, the information you will find through Google will be woefully out of date and have information or steps that no longer apply to the current version of Wine. AppDB is kept a bit more current, as long as you are trying to run a common application. If you are trying to run something that is rather rare, I don't think it will matter where you look, helpful information will not be easy to find.

EDIT - Actually, if you are trying to run something uncommon, one of the best places to look for help is right here. You won't necessarily find another person who has tried to run the same application, but you will definitely find people who have tried to run many things with Wine, which has given most of us really good experiences to draw on.
