### Post by handy on 2010-01-16
Guild Wars works with Wine, though I prefer to use Crossover Games, (which is made by the main dev's of Wine, so they can have a mortgage).

Guild Wars is a great game, but it is suffering from the disadvantage of having been around for well over 4 years now, so there are nowhere near as many regular players as there used to be.  This has an effect when it comes to buying/selling from other players, finding runners for once very popular runs is now very difficult.

Still, if you are player that doesn't need to always be in a team of other people, the Hero's are fantastic, so I would recommend starting with the Nightfall campaign, as that is where you will get most of your Hero's from, apart from the fact that it is a great campaign, then if you were still into the game I'd go for the original GW Prophecies campaign next (which I prefer to Nightfall, but there are next to no Hero's available there).  Factions is generally considered to be the worst campaign & likely only worth buying for some of the skills you can purchase from skill traders there.

Anyway, all the GW fans are hanging out for GW2, though we may be lucky & see it next year...

By the way, you can get a free trial of GW at their site currently. ;)

***[Edit:]** I should have mentioned that GW doesn't require a powerful computer or internet connection; I have played it when my ISP has cut me down to 64bps speed with no problems. A graphic card capable of DirectX 8 is enough, if it will handle DX-9 you will be better off visually.*
