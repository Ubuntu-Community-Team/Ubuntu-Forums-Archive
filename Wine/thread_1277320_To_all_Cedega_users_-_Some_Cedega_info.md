### Post by beastrace91 on 2009-09-28
Cedega on a netbook works poorly at best. All of their menus are sized for at least a 1024x768 resolution, meaning at many points (especially during the installation) you have to feel your way through the menus using tab/enter and hope you are hitting the correct buttons. Also with Cedega expect little to-no support (other than their at times less than friendly forums) with your netbook as it does not contain an nVidia gfx card.

All three of the options you listed will support running your games in "virtual desktops" (which on are netbook screen are basically limited to 640x480) My advice would be to give Wine ago (its free so at worst it just costs you some time) and then if that does not work/gives you a hard time give Codeweavers a try. I currently have both Cedega & Codeweavers, and well lets just say I am letting my Cedega membership drop at the end of the year they where so great
