### Post by cogadh on 2010-08-09
I'm not sure I understand what you are saying, but it still sounds to me like you are blaming Ubuntu for the poor choices made by app developers who have no relation to Ubuntu. The fact that iTunes does not work on Linux is the fault of Apple, not Ubuntu. They were the ones who coded an app that only works on Windows and OSX. The Ubuntu dev team had nothing to do with that and cannot do anything to change that, only Apple can.

Ubuntu is more than powerful enough and is capable of doing everything and more than both Windows and OSX, but when app developers still see Linux as "that other OS that no one uses", they don't make software for it. There are Linux native alternatives for almost everything available on Windows and OSX, some of which are superior to their counterparts, but if you are not willing to check them out, then the best thing for you is probably to stick to those closed environments that you are comfortable with. Don't go blaming Ubuntu for that though, that is your choice, not theirs.
