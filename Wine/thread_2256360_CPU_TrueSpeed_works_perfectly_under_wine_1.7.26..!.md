### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2014-12-11
Evening, all.

Just for everybody's information, I have discovered a small Windows utility that works under wine 1.7.26...almost by accident.

I was having a browse through a large, 250 GB partition I have on my external hard drive; full of Windows stuff, a left-over from my XP days. I was trying, out of curiosity, some of the utility programs, to see if any might work.

Completely by chance, I found one that would. It's called 'CPU TrueSpeed', and is a small app to tell you your precise CPU CLOCK speed (down to 5 decimal places!), second by second, as well as other, CPU-related information.

It works PERFECTLY under Wine 1.7.26 (a beta version, but one which enables me to use one single graphics app that I haven't been able to find a direct equivalent for; PhotoScape ver. 3.65.)

It is version 1.8.1.860, and was written by a gentleman by the name of Igor P. Zenkov, in 2009. His site was [http://www.zenkov.com.]("http://www.zenkov.com"), but it appears to have been closed in late 2009. 

Anybody is interested in this app, simply Google the above title & version, and you should be able to find it....probably on Cnet. com, which is where I originally found it.

Regards,

Mike.

BTW: I shall be adding this to the Wine database in the next few days.

EDIT: [13/01/15] Have just tried this under Wine 1.7.33, the current beta release, in a Xubuntu 14.04 flash-drive install. Works after a fashion.....but the mouse develops a mind of its own while the program is running; small, short, jerky movements. Can't really recommend it under the newer beta of Wine; a clear example of a newer version containing a regression. Unfortunate, really.

However, the newer version of PhotoScape (my fave graphics editor), 3.7 against 3.6.5, works even better now. Printing used to be somewhat 'delayed' (like several minutes to respond); it works a lot quicker now.....maybe 30 secs or so to respond. MUCH better!
