### Post by regor210 on 2012-06-22
You could check your opengl to see if its working

Open a terminal, press ctrl+alt+t all at the same time. Then cut and paste the following commands into the terminal window one at a time minus the $

$ lspci | grep VGA && glxinfo | grep -w 'direct\|OpenGL'

$ LIBGL_DEBUG=verbose glxinfo

ATI video drivers can be tricky. I'm hearing that the latest drivers are not fully supporting the older video cards and the open source Radeon drivers have to be used. So if you used Jockey IE the property drivers you may not have 3D.


[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsVideoCardsAti](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsVideoCardsAti)
 

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto)


[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver)
