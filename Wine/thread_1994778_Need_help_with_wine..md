### Post by nj_peeps on 2012-06-04
Most older games will work in wine. As na5h pointed out the wine application database is your best bet for finding what games will work, and if any workarounds are needed.  I have found that using winetricks very useful. 

Also, I have found that some games work best if you put them in their own wine prefix to avoid configuration conflicts.

For example, [StarCraft]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=149") I:
In the test results section of the page, look for the OS and wine version that most closely matches your config.  and click on the "Show" link for that line.  The selected test results will tell you what the tester did to install the game/program, what, if any, workarounds were needed to get it to work.  Also, it's a good idea to read the comments as other users may have suggested other workarounds, or tweaks.
