### Post by Muffinabus on 2008-07-10
They offer the game as a download because you can't do anything without an account.  When you bought the game retail, you have an account creation key that you use to create an account.  This is what is key here.  You can't login to their game without an active account, so there is no harm in allowing people to download the client.

You have a free 30 days to play, and $15 a month after that.

Downloading the client is the same exact thing as installing it from the DVD, it's the same software.

Also, if you bought the battle chest and or bought the burning crusade, you're going to have to download that as well from the same link I gave above.

EDIT:  Maybe the FAQ better explains it:

I have a World of Warcraft account, which version do I need to download?

If you already have a retail World of Warcraft account and simply need to reinstall the game, you will want to download the Full World of Warcraft Client. If your account includes access to the Burning Crusade, you will also have to download an additional Burning Crusade client from this page. Trial users who have a slower Internet connection may wish to use this version instead of the above Streaming version.
