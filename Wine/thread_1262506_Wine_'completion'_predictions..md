### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-09-10
So, there's a page somewhere on WineHQ that details their progress with the DLL implementation. If the face of computing as far as operating systems goes doesn't change in the next ten years, I'm quite certain Wine could become nearly complete in the next decade.

I mean, it's been about 15 years so far, right? See how far Wine has come, then think of what improvement we could have in 10 years!

They might even port Wine to ARM and PPC when it's gotten to a certain point in development, meaning you can potentially play the original Sims on a cell phone. XD Or other similar implementations.

There are already many programs that run with enhanced speed and stability due to the Wine API. It's very possible that, when the code gets to a certain level of compatibility (nearly 100 percent, with Safedisc and Gameguard support), they could work merely on improving the speed of the code. This could make Windows programs through Wine run with similar speeds to native Linux programs of the same type.

Either way, I think that, in the not-too-distant future, Win32 applications will be thought of as 'legacy' applications, and used primarily for businesses built around older programs, or just casual gaming you can't get without Win32 compatibility.

Of course, I'm being extremist and overly idealistic. How long do you guys think it will take to get to 100% compatibility? I'm gonna' say 5 years.
