### Post by handy on 2010-02-06
Members of the Codeweavers team are the prime developers of Wine. It is what they do to pay for their lives.  Their site is really honest & they watch the forums closely. They are interested in supporting the Linux community.

Their Crossover products, can make it quicker & easier to install & have applications/games running than getting there via Wine. Sometimes things that won't run on Wine will on Crossover, & vice versa.

Crossover is cheap as their website will explain.

Transgaming (Cedega) from my experience, are very poor at communicating with their customers. Currently some people have doubts if they will release anymore updates?  Time will tell, as they have been working for game distribution companies, using the Transgaming, Cider, application, to modify windows games for the Mac, & making money that way.

Some games will run on Cedega that you can't get to run on Wine/Crossover, as Transgaming pay license fees to go beyond the copy protection of some games.

The Cedega forums are a horror, they are populated by cranky, frustrated people; very seldom does any input from the dev's arrive in the forums.  You can email & try any way you can to get a reply from Transgaming without result.  The only time I got a reply was to my harsh email notifying them why I was cancelling my account & why I would never promote them in the wilds of the internet.
