### Post by cogadh on 2010-05-10
CrossOver gives you an easy to use interface and technical support from Codeweavers, but underneath all that, it is still just Wine. It is worth it if you want to support the further development of the Wine project (Codeweavers is the primary financial backer of Wine), but if that is not what you are concerned about, then just stick with Wine.

### Post by Elvish Legion on 2010-05-11
I had a question along the same lines, is cross over a one time charge or is it a monthly/yearly license

---

### Post by cogadh on 2010-05-11
One time charge that gives you tech support and application updates (i.e. new versions of CrossOver) for 6 months (a year if you buy the Pro package). After that, Crossover will still work, but if you want to get a new version of CrossOver when it is released, you'll have to buy it again.

### Post by cogadh on 2010-05-12
The thing is, the "bottle" feature of CX is just creating separate Wine prefixes, which you can also do with plain Wine. They did make it easier to do (i.e. you have a gui to do it instead of the terminal), so for people not comfotable with the terminal, it can be a good thing, but it not one of CX's unique features.
