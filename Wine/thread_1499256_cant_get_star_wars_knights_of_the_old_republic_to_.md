### Post by cogadh on 2010-06-04
"Page not found" error on that link.

If you are looking to do any kind of serious gaming on a laptop, then you need to consider only those machines that have an ATI or Nvidia graphics solution, especially if you plan to do your gaming on Linux (personally, I would stick with Nvidia only in that situation). Intel does not have very good driver support in Linux and while it may work fine for some Linux native games, it is always going to be lacking when it comes to gaming via Wine. Unfortunately, in order to get a machine with better grpahics, you are probably going to have to buy one of those near $1000 laptops. One of the ways manufacturers keep the prices of laptops like the Presario down in the sub-$500 range is by using the much cheaper and less powerful Intel graphics solutions. This is one of those scenarios where you really do get what you pay for, at least in terms of the hardware.
