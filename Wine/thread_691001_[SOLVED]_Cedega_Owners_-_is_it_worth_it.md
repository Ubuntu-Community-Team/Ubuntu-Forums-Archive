### Post by GSF1200S on 2008-02-08

### Post by Tadock on 2008-02-08
I run both Wine and Cedega. Cedega is actually based on an older version of wine that branched out with a more user friendly GUI. Anyways, I have had problems with both and I can get Cedega to play certain games and Wine to play certain games, but games that I can get to play on both Wine does run them close to the same as windows and Cedega is slower. Example I run Half-Life 2 at 60 FPS under wind and 45 under Cedega. But, Cedega is far better at running games that require CDs then Wine. So, it is a trade off. I also, have tried CrossOver which in my opinion is better then Cedega  also, CrossOver has CD support and runs about as well as wine as far as I can tell.

### Post by rune0077 on 2008-02-08
Well, Cedega and Wine are uses a lot of the same code, so it could be argued that there's no reason to pay for Cedega when you can get Wine for free. However, some games seems to run better with Cedega than with Wine, and vice versa, so your best bet may be to have both. In either case, check both websites databases for games that will run, and don't expect anything else to run very well.

Considering that Cedega is a product you pay for, it should be said that their support is considerably worse than even Microsoft support. Go to their support page and there's a very long list of unanswered support questions from users, many of them are many months old. You pay for Cedega, but they really don't give a flying hoot about you as a customer. You'll get lots better help on their forums (or here) from other users, than you do from the developers.

---

### Post by ahaslam on 2008-02-08
Cedega runs Oblivion in higher detail than Wine, while returning a higher fps. despite this it is quite unstable & game support is very limited.

Wine offers you flexibility & freedom, if it doesn't work it can always be patched & tweaked. A good example of this is COD4. It wont run under Cedega or a vanilla Wine, though a simple Wine patch soon fixes that, allowing it to run at near native speeds.

My recommendation is either to use Wine or to have a Windows partition handy. So in short, no.
