CADKEY v19 (Windows) is a mid-range 2D/3D/Cubic Parametric/Solid Modeling CAD system.  It has very good IGES/STEP translators, and is easy to learn.  I taught a room of students to use it at commercial 2D level in less than 1 week.

CADKEY is now owned by Kubotek, and the product is now KeyCreator.  It was the first truly 3D desktop CAD system available, predating 3D support by AutoCAD.  In the late 80's it could do complex cubic parametric surface modeling, something that is still pretty much state-of-the-art.   Objects like turbocharger impellers could be designed on a PC instead of a mainframe or workstation.

Sorry, I'm just so JAZZED that it worked so flawlessly.  It even rendered 3D solids and rotated them in space with no issues, and even printed rendered objects.

Since it is an "orphan" product now, it does make me curious if Kubotek would consider releasing it to the public domain.  It is still a very powerful tool that will create and communicate engineering data with the latest products.  Way better for mechanical design than AutoCAD.
