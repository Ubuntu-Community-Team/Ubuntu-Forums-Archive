The Land of Nod (Hebrew: ’eretz-Nod) is a place in the Book of Genesis of the Hebrew Bible, located "to the east of Eden", to which Cain was banished after murdering his brother Abel. The Hebrew word nod means "wandering".

    "And Cain went out from the presence of the LORD, and dwelt in the land of Nod, on the east of Eden." (Genesis 4:16, King James Version)

"Nod" (&#1504;&#1493;&#1491;) is the Hebrew root of the verb "to wander" (&#1500;&#1504;&#1491;&#1493;&#1491;) and is possibly an etymological etiology intended to explain the peripatetic lifestyle of Cain and his descendants, the Cainites. One interpretation of Genesis 4:16 is that Cain was cursed to wander the land forever, not that he was exiled to a "Land of Wanderers", otherwise absent from the Old Testament.

### Post by andlinux on 2007-09-05
If there are people who don't know how to play it, well I will explain it here. ;)

First you must have wine installed, you can find it in synaptic.

If wine is installed open up a terminal and write "winecfg" (without the quotes).

Then you will see a sort of M$ Windows menu and select there windows 95, so wine will act as Win 95.

The next thing to do is, put your disc in your dvd/cd drive and go to your cdrom station in a terminal. Here it is "/media/cdrom" (cd /media/cdrom)

Then do "wine setup.exe" and the installation will start. It will ask you something about directx, I didn't installed that. First I selected it to install the directx stuff but it didn't worked.

You can also install "westwood online" and "adobe acrobat reader" but that's not necessary. 

After that's done. Go to "/.wine/drive_c/WESTWOOD/C&C95/" (it's normally in your home folder) and do that in your console.

The latest thing you have to do is write this: "wine C\&C95.EXE" (this is how I start it in the console and remember without qoutes and the cdrom needs to be placed in the drive). 
If everything is working you can make a shortcut so that you can start it with a few clicks.

this is my short guide to play C&C.

### Post by splintercellguy on 2007-09-14
You might also have to add the ISO mount point to Wine drives, if you haven't already. The best bet is to burn, if you wish to spare the trouble.

EDIT: Outlining my steps:

1. sudo chmod 777 /dev/loop0
2. Check winecfg drives, add ISO mount point (I also added /dev/loop0 just in case).
3. mount the ISO on the mount point
4. launch seutp
