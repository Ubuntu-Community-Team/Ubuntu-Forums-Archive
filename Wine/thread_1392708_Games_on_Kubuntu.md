### Post by Duskao on 2010-01-28
Your gonna have to look into WINE. Check out the WINE forums here [http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=313](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=313)
And the official Wine download and app database at [http://www.winehq.org/](http://www.winehq.org/)

There are a couple other options out there for a newbie as well. Crossover Games [http://www.codeweavers.com/products/cxgames/](http://www.codeweavers.com/products/cxgames/) 
Cedega [http://www.cedega.com/](http://www.cedega.com/)

Both Crossover and Cedega are pay programs. Relatively cheap though.

A free alternative is PlayOnLinux [http://playonlinux.com/en/](http://playonlinux.com/en/)

Other then that, you can try dual booting Linux/Windows or run it in a Virtual Box (emulator)

Or ReactOS has made a lot of big jumps of late. It might work for you, but this is your least likely chance for the time being. With this you would also have to dual boot.
