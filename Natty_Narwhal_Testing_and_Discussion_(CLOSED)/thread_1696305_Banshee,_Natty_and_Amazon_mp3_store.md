### Post by coffeecat on 2011-02-27
Seeing as Amazon are still not supporting anything newer than Karmic or 64-bit installations for their mp3 downloader (yes, I know you can get it to work in Lucid with some fiddling) I was intrigued to see the Amazon store in Natty's Banshee. So I tried it. I was able to log into my Amazon account and order an album which started downloading happily into a folder in /tmp. Impressed, I left it to its own devices but found that Banshee had closed (presumably crashed) when I came back. There were only two (out of five) tracks in the temporary folder, named 8a36c52177f7718aef77d9c340cf9e8b.mp3 and f2cb3139f016a73e224ead172aee8490.mp3 - which is informative. :| Presumably, if the process had finished gracefully, they would have been transferred to somewhere in my home folder and renamed.

Whatever. As far as the Amazon server was concerned I had downloaded the tracks, so I had to email them to unlock the order for me to re-download. Which they did, and I did, but using MacOS this time.

Does anyone have any experience of Banshee in Natty with Amazon mp3's? What's the final plan? I presume the intention is to have a fairly seamless browse, order, pay, download, and then listen experience. I'll put off trying again until I can be sure it's reliable, but I'd be interested in others' experiences.

And, yes, I know about the Ubuntu One store.

### Post by coffeecat on 2011-03-02
Thanks. Yes, I've since installed Banshee in Maverick and successfully used the Amazon mp3 store with it. It's good - much more elegant than Amazon's own mp3 downloader.

I guess the Banshee crash was just one of those Alpha testing glitches. No harm done. I'll look forward to having access to both the Amazon store and Ubuntu One Store in one app once things are more stable in Natty, although I'll try to use the Ubuntu store for preference even though it seems marginally more expensive - at least for the albums I've compared.

Also - the way the Ubuntu One store is laid out, and its search, is **dreadful**. A tip: you're better off going to 7digital.com (at least in the UK) for your initial search to see if the store has what you want.
