### Post by kaldor on 2011-01-19

That seems a bit silly to me; if the 2D Unity Shell ends up being just as good and stable as 3d, it should be the default fallback.

I'm a huge sucker for consistency; I don't want to have to explain "This is GNOME, this is Unity.. GNOME is there because your drivers aren't installed" and all that to people when they discover/need help with Ubuntu.

"How do I do X on Ubuntu?"
Are you on Unity or GNOME?
"Huh?"
Are there 2 panels on the top and bottom?
"There's one on the left"
So it's Unity. Okay, press the File Browser button
"Where?"
On the left, find the icon that looks like an orange folder.
"Nevermind, I found it in the Places Menu; I put my bottom taskbar on the left"

^
I can imagine conversations and mix-ups like that. It just seems useless to have 2 totally different GUIs by default with no guarantee which one will be used on a fresh install; nightmare for helping brand new or non-technical Ubuntu users.

Edit: That type of conversation has happened before with me; my dad was experimenting with the Live CD and accidentally moved the top panel to the bottom... I had no idea how to explain "THAT bottom panel!" to him because he isn't very advanced.
