### Post by kansasnoob on 2010-12-07
I'm sure I'll be bombarded with comments telling me I'm crazy, but I seriously think this looks very good for an Alpha1 with so many major changes.

I'm impressed with the option to use "Classic Gnome"! I hope they keep that option.

While USC stopped working a few days ago, (I've not been able to test every day) today it started working again and I'm quite impressed. I'd disliked USC to begin with, it reminded me too much of Linspire's CNR, but my opinion has changed dramatically! Once again Ubuntu proved me wrong.

While I'm using the classic desktop as default almost every day I see improvements to Unity. I like it! I think once they get the menu and Compiz issues straightened out it'll be a truly viable option.

So, why say this? Quite simply I read a lot of negative reviews yesterday at Distrowatch and I wonder who the heck doesn't understand what an Alpha1 is?

I love Ubuntu's decision to always be on the "front-lines" of development. Someone has to push forward and Ubuntu has shown that they won't be intimidated by a bit of negative press.

I'm also very impressed with some of the minor changes to grub2 (listing old kernels as a separate option), and plymouth is now working on my intel gpu perfectly! I'm booting in under 20 seconds!

Lots of great work going into this :D
