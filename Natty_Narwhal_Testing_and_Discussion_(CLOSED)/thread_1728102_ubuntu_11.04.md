### Post by tgyorgyi on 2011-04-14
Here you can find general information on which is the current stable version, and very basic info on upgrading Ubuntu:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes")

Here is some more info:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes")

Canonical recommends the network upgrade, as that is the most automatic.

The regular ISO files are for clean install. If you have a separate partition for your /home directory, all your files and most of your settings will be safe, as they are stored normally in the /home directory.

If for some reason a network upgrade is not feasible (e.g. due to a good Internet connection), the _alternate ISO_ is for CD-based upgrade rather than a new install. You can download alternate ISOs usually from the same sites where all the other ISO files are available.

Here is more of the same, Natty-specific:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NattyUpgrades]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NattyUpgrades")

But do wait until the end of April, I'm right now using Natty, and some things do need to be ironed out yet. :p
