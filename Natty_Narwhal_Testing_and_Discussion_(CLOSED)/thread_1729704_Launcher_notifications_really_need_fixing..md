### Post by kaldor on 2011-04-15
I've been using 11.04 on and off since October. Ever since Beta 1, I've been using it a lot more. Something about Unity that is genuinely annoying to me is the lack of a *noticeable* notification event in the launcher.

The tiny blue triangle is just useless to me. I'm missing Xchat messages a lot and not noticing when programs need my attention until a long time after. I've gotten into the habit of always checking Xchat or other programs just to make sure I am not missing something. 

Despite looking bad, the Blue Ubuntu logo was obvious and drew my attention to the launcher. Still, with autohide it sucks because you don't know which icon needs attention.

Also, the glow on icons that have changed/need your attention (example, an empathy or Xchat message comes in) is barely noticeable to me. 

I can't be the only one who thinks this. If the software isn't compatible with the indicator applets, I don't notice any notifications at all. Maybe a pulsing/glowing Ubuntu button would be better than the atom-sized triangle currently used.

I love Unity, but certain non-needed (and easily fixed) design decisions are detracting from the experience.
