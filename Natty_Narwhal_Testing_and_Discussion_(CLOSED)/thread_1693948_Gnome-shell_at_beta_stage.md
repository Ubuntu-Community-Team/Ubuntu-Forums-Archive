### Post by seeker5528 on 2011-02-24
I use minimize all the time, not as much at home, but in the work situation definitely.

For me the most common reason is there are many times where I need to read something in one window and type something in another. 

Sometimes it's more convenient to have two less than maximized windows and to click the first, click the second, click the first, etc...

Other times it's more convenient to minimize, read, unminimize, type, unminimize, read, etc....

Still other times it more convenient to have a larger or maximized window in the back, make the window in the foreground smaller and just drag it around as necessary to see what I need in the window behind.

Most commonly it is the minimize/unminimize that I use often in combination with copy and paste.

I use the maximize button a fair amount too. 

I could almost do without the maximize button if (like Unity) when you drag a window to the top of the screen it maximizes, drag it away it unmaximizes, I have not tried it, but one of the articles I read implied that's how Gnome Shell works. My issue being that I have plenty of situations where I position a window, sometimes more than one, in the unmaximized state, maximize it, then when I unmaximize it want it to snap back to the postion it was in before I maximized it. If double clicking the title bar works to maximize/unmaximize I could live with that..... if I had too. 

Claiming minimize doesn't make sense because there is no window list doesn't make sense to me. Click on activities, click on the window you want to switch to. Not as efficient, but it works. But if Gnome Shell is performing sluggish on your machine it's a huge annoyance. 'Alt'+'Tab'+'Left Click a window' also works and performance is less of an issue, but if you have multiple windows of the same app, which are then grouped, you have to mouse over the application, mouse down to the individual window you want, which may have it's own issues, it was a little quirky in the version Debian previously had, haven't tried it in the newer one yet. So there are actually two "window lists", Activities and holding down 'Alt'+'Tab', there just isn't an ever present task manager in the foreground always showing you a list of windows. 

So what was the point of doing away with those buttons again. :P

Later, Seeker
