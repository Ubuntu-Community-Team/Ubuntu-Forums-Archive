### Post by dwielunski on 2011-04-19
I am with you 100%. My "workaround" for this right now is: use firefox, hide menus, go to about:config and make fullscreen autohide false, always show tabs, use small icons and put a fullscreen button on the toolbar. Then when I open up firefox, I hit the fullscreen button and I have an interface comparable to Chrome with no visible panel.

All the major browser developers have made a specific point in their latest releases of removing as much as possible from the top of the screen. I find it really odd that Ubuntu would want to override this design choice and force you to see an extra bar up there.

Not quite. This forum is "for the discussion of Ubuntu Natty Narwhal".

I actually think I prefer the fullscreen toggle button to a panel autohide, which might end up triggering accidentally when the user moves the mouse up to the top in order to switch tabs.

I think for this release Unity has focused on integrating the program menus into the system title bar. For most applications, this reduces vertical space used. However, Chrome & FF4 have already removed their menu bars and put that functionality into buttons, so this particular innovation isn't all that helpful. I guess the "solution" if you're determined to keep the panel visible (and I think the designers of this release clearly are) would be to cram the tabs into the top panel bar as well. Maybe this only looks feasible to me because I've removed the mail & messaging icons from the panel--does it look too crowded up there on your screens?
