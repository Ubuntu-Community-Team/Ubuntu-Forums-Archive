### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2011-01-07
In general you should use a development release of any OS for testing only, this applies to Ubuntu (Natty) as well as other development versions.

If you use Natty, expect bugs and be prepared to both fix them yourself and file bug reports.

What I would advise is that you use natty, but make a separate /home partition. Keep all all your data in your home directory. This way if (when) Natty breaks you can re-install without losing your data.

Even better, keep backups.

So long as you are willing to test and accept bugs, file bug reports, back up your data, and are willing to re-install if it breaks, keep using Natty.

Otherwise stay with a non-development OS (10.10).

### Post by cariboo on 2011-01-08
If you want to test Natty, I would suggest you keep your present installation, and create anywhere from 10 to 50Gb of empty space on your hard drive, and install Natty in the empty space. You should use manual partitioning to set up your partititons that way you are assured of not over writing your present installation.

You should also create an account on [Launchpad.net]("https://launchpad.net/"), so you can report bugs.

The reason we suggest you keep your present installation, is just in case Natty breaks to the point where it is unusable, at least you have a working system to fall back on, and you can use it to repair your broken Natty installation.
