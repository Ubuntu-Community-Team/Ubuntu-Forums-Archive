### Post by cimh on 2011-03-07
In my experience ubuntu alpha 3s are usually quite good. BUT this one has miles to go. So unless you like testing and watching paint dry Id give it a wide berth.

On my eee 901. 
It has a very slow boot.
The wifi will not connect.
Its very slow to react eg ff takes nearly 8 secs to launch, chrome takes about 5s whereas on 10.10 it takes half that time or less.
Its also really annoying having to unlearn the gnome interface.
Finally it makes poor use of the screen as the icons on the left are really designed for a touch screen - I see no benefit at all for a screen with a keyboard especially on a netbook where pixels are at a premium.
As I understand it unity depends on compiz. If so I think thats a real problem for netbooks. Many netbook users want a fast,lean, quick booting  os - I do not think this version is going to do that. As it stands its one of the most sluggish versions Ive tried on my eee and I must have tried over 40 in the last 2 years.


Its brave of the team to attempt to do so much within the 6/12 update schedule but if all this is sorted by launch I will be very impressed.

cimh
