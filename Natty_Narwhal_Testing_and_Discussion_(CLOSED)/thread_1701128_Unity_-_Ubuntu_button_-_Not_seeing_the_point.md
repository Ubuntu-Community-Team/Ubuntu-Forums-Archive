### Post by Handle123 on 2011-03-06
The Ubuntu button at the top left button is the most visible and one of the most important places on the new UI. But in terms of functionality, it seems very poorly thought out. 

Being a place to search through applications - Excellent. Except, its not like Launchy where you can type a few letters and hit enter to launch the most commonly used application. With this, hitting enter does nothing, you need to click. It doesn't really deal with typos well either. These are fairly small quirks that can be improved though.

My main problem with is that most of the default shortcuts are very pointless. Check email, view photos, browse the web are just shortcuts to the appropriate applications which are most likely in your launcher already. And "Find [x] apps" are just worse versions of the hierarchical menu in the classic Gnome menu.

I'm trying to give Unity a solid chance, but I just can't see where they're going with this?
