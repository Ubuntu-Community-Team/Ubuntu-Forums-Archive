### Post by ninjaaron on 2011-04-23
> **MadMan2k said:**
> got any reference to prove that? or is it just your opinion?

Well, the muscles for clenching tend to be a lot more developed because they often used to carry loads, and there is generally no resistance when opening. Just try opening your hand when you are holding it closed with the other.

This means it is theoretically "more difficult" to flick our fingers outward. I'm not sure it makes much of a difference in this case however, since you probably want to move your mouse back up to the content at some point anyway.

Course, if your really a power user who's worried your work flow, initiating the dash with the Win key and typing is a much, much faster way to access your applications that anything you can do with a stock gnome-panel setup. Likewise, keyboard initiated window switchers are much faster than fiddling about with a panel-based menu.

If you're really worried about "getting work done," chuck your mouse, turn off your track-pad, install unity, and learn how to interact with your computer via keyboard. That's where the speed is at.

course, you can also run the command [FONT="Courier New"]gnome-panel[/FONT] inside of unity and use them together. There are a couple panel widgets I wanted for my netvertible notebook, so I have a hidden panel with them. It's no problem whatsoever.

Unity is app running under Gnome. The panel is another gnome app, and they are perfectly capable of running at the same time.

Interestingly enough, this doesn't seem to work with Gnome-Shell and the panel.
