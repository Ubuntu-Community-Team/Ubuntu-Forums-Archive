### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-03-12
> **rosswmcgee said:**
> I lost my calendar on the system panel. How to replace it?

It's part of the clock applet.  If you still have clock, right click on it, then click preferences, and specify display date in panel display.

If you've lost the clock, right click on the panel, add to panel, then select the clock applet.

I've had applets go missing due some odd error on boot, but they appear again on the next boot.  If you add the clock when this has happened, you will have two clocks when you next boot.

Chris.

### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-03-12
> **rosswmcgee said:**
> Hey thanks! I have it now. I upgraded to Natty 11.04, and have had a few problems today.


Natty is not really for public consumption at this point - it's still an alpha release.  I'd really be surprised if you had no problems at all.

You certainly should not replace your regular release with an alpha, or even a beta or candidate release.  

Set up a separate partition and install development releases on it to play with by all means, but you're not supposed to replace your stable release with something that's still under development - it can and will get broken at any time, even if it is stable enough to run on your machine at the moment.

Chris.
