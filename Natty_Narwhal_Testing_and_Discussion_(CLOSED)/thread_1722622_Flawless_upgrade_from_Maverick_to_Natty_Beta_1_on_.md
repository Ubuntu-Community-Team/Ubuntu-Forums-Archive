### Post by manzdagratiano on 2011-04-06
All right Ladies and Gentlemen!

I am aware this thread should in time be moved to the Testimonials/Experiences section, but I felt it would be at the moment more appropriate here since we are in the Natty Testing phase still. I just witnessed an absolutely flawless upgrade on Dell Vostro V13, and it was ***lightning fast***! It started at 10pm and was all done by 12:30am, which I am truly surprised with, since a) this machine is not too fast, and b) by 11:00pm, it had not even finished downloading half of the files! And yet, at 12:00am, when I looked, things were **blazing before my eyes** like nothing before - and, to compare, I would say, WAY faster than even pacman in Arch Linux has ever performed in front of me!

There was absolutely no trouble **whatsoever** (well, aside from the usual compiz crash upon fiddling with ccsm, which, I am sure will be ironed out in due time)... The framebuffer is as glorious as ever... I can see that the miracle kernel is performing its miracles, and even the new Grub (1.99) is absolutely more awesome than the previous one (1.98) - this one detects my Gentoo install right off the bat, while in the previous one I had to add an extra entry by hand in /etc/grub.d/40_custom. And Unity is working just as awesomely as anything else.

All in all, suffice to say that Natty is just about mature to blow everybody out of the water!
