### Post by reiki on 2011-03-06
I tried Natty several months ago and it was missing a lot and extremely hard to get to a desktop at all for me. I recently downloaded A3 and used the Startup Disk Creator to make a bootable USB stick with Natty AMD64 desktop. 
Lo and behold, it booted. First thing I saw was a box telling me compiz failed but there was a button there to restart it, I did, and I have a unity desktop.

Hardware s a Del ZinoHD ultra small form factor desktop using an AMD Neo X2 6250 dual core, 750gig sata hard drive and 8gigs of memory. Graphics card is ATI HD4330 in an MXM slot (like a laptop).

I ran the update manager and let it update. Rebooted pretty normally if a little slowly, no doubt due to it being from a usb stick. And I'm typing this from Firefox on Natty running from the usb stick.

Personally, I'm not sure I like Unity, but I know that classic is still there somewhere. :)  All in all, things seem to be working even if I'm having trouble finding them. Long time Ubuntu ser.... since August 2005 I think, and I'm an old man and don't like having to relearn stuff. :  hehehe... not bad though. Looks like a nice simple interface.  Maybe too simple.
