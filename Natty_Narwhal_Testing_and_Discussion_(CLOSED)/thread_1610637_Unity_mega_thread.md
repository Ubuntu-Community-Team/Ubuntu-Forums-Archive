I have no doubt that is true, Mutter by itself doesn't have *that* significant of a requirement above what you might expect for Compiz and if your hardware *does* support it performance probably won't be all that different than with Metacity for basic operation.

Gnome-shell on the other hand brings whole new levels of incompatibilities, some resulting from the requirement that a broader range of OpenGL features be supported by hardware.

And the question of how many people actually have a problem with Compiz itself as opposed to having problems with specific extensions.

There are a lot of comparisons that can't be done between Mutter and Compiz at the moment because it doesn't have any FX extensions. Unity and Gnome-shell were done as extensions, but they are breaking new ground and I would guess that it would be more technically correct to blame performance and compatibility issues on clutter than it would be to blame them on Mutter.
