### Post by 1clue on 2011-04-12
Awesome.

BTW When I said non-boot I meant not /boot.  I use one 512M partition for that and everything else lvm autodetect.  I also find having fstab use UUID makes it easier if you keep switching disks out, which happens fairly often on some of our test systems.

LVM is awesome if you either have multiple boots or if you have a continually changing system.  For me it usually winds up with one bootable system at a time, but then that gets rewritten over and over.  So I have my backup/archive/vm partitions which can be remounted over and over, and then the standard directories for each specific OS.

LVM2 lets you resize as you run, without unmounting the filesystem.  I think it's just ext2 which can't resize, but it might be ext4.  I use only formats which support hot resizing, then you can make a super minimal partition and then resize it as needed.


Good luck and have fun.
