### Post by coffeecat on 2010-12-20
Desktop edition = Unity. Classic = standard gnome. If the screenshot you posted is what you got after selecting desktop edition at the gdm login then, yes, Unity has failed on you. A bit odd because you seem to have the proprietary nvidia driver installed and the Unity plugin enabled in ccsm.

Having said that, I have Unity experience only with ATI and Intel graphics. It may be that there is something with nvidia that I've missed. If that screen is what you got when you selected desktop edititon, then I'd suggest trying either or both of these commands from a terminal:

```
unity --reset
compiz --replace
```Both will cause the desktop to seemingly crash but, hopefully, start up again with Unity. But wait until someone with nvidia experience posts before you try them.

By the way, it might be a good idea if you go to System > Preferences > Keyboard > Layouts Tab > Options button, and enable ctrl-alt-backspace for killing the xserver. I've been thankful for that recently on more than one occasion when confronted with a blank, broken Unity desktop. It enabled me to log back into the classic desktop.
