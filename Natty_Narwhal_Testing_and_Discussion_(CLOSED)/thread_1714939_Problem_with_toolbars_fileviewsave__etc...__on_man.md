### Post by Iehova on 2011-03-26
Shantiq,

You're quite welcome, but it's very interesting that you raised the point. There has been some discussion on the Ayatana mailing list about whether new users would be able to discover that application menus had shifted to top top panel, or whether they would struggle, as you evidently did. The general consensus, I believe, was that users would stumble upon it fairly quickly just by mousing around, but the question is whether they would notice or make the connection. As you evidently have the savvy to use a pre-release version of Ubuntu and yet couldn't find the application menus, this suggests that further work is definitely needed.

In any case, I personally believe that the current set up is counter-intuitive, if you have a non-maximised window in the middle of the screen, you would not expect the application's menus to be miles *away* from that window up in the top corner of the screen. Removing the menus from their own toolbar increases vertical screen space, which is, in my opinion, a Very Good Thing, but it would be far far better for them to move to the title bar of their own window, as here: [http://www.webupd8.org/2011/02/unity-mockup-menu-integrated-in-window.html](http://www.webupd8.org/2011/02/unity-mockup-menu-integrated-in-window.html) . This, of course, wouldn't make them any more discoverable (so I apologise for going off-topic), but it does seem infinitely more logical to me.
