### Post by Lucius Noctis Dacre on 2010-12-06
How about being able to add/update appointments/tasks to the integrated calendar without having an email client open up every time to do so?
Instead, calendar would be able to add and edit its appointments/tasks, etc, all whilst staying apart from emails until one specifically asks for it (e.g. with a command like: right click -> open appointment in email client). 

I don't oppose the functionality of adding/editing appointments through the email client, i just advocate the need to quickly browse&add/edit appointments in the calendar without an email client popping up every time and asking for passwords/checking and starting to update email folders (you can imagine the hassle when one has quite a number of email accounts!).

Plus, when doing away with an email client altogether, the calendar will be already there, safe, integrated and fully functional!  :smile:

If there is already a way to do this, I am sorry, I just haven't found it yet. 

Nevertheless, I think this would be a great addition to the next version of Ubuntu!  :smile:
