### Post by gnomeuser on 2010-10-14
My top priorities for Natty are:

1) Finishing the Btrfs support (which is I believe still hung up on the unintentionally hilarious and tragic gplv2 v. gplv3 superior freedom definition, not working code).

2) Getting Banshee as the default media player on the desktop and UNE releases. I am tired of being told next release or "you're buggy" without a list of bugs we need to fix (or worse after we fix said bugs, being brushed off during the following cycle).

3) Mono 2.8 (SGen, performance enhancements and fixes, I crave your goodness).

4) Initial Systemd support.

5) Nouveau power management and 3D acceleration.

6) PackageKit used in USC.

7) Having Apport finally catch all PulseAudio crashes to identify the source properly and assigning blame rather than spewing technical crash dialogs (see e.g. [https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/apport/+bug/644651](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/apport/+bug/644651)).

8) Finally getting good integration with webmail solutions (e.g. being able to pick GMail as my preferred mail application).

9) Finally addressing the multiple block layer and mm issues that cause io to make the desktop suck. (upstream are supposedly working on this for kernel 2.6.37).

0) Launchpad being able to push a bug to upstream with a single click (thus cleverly allowing me to use apport to do all the dirty work without losing the ability to file bugs upstream).
