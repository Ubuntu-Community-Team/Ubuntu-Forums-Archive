### Post by Yougo on 2011-02-14
Well, the name says it: Alpha.
the whole system is still under heavy construction and performance changes from day to day. depending on how up to date your machine is, and depending on what hardware you have, all kinds of instabilities can and will occur.

it is NOT recommended yet for use on your work machine.
if you just want to test or see how far along it is, i suggest to run 11.04 in a virtual machine or on a seperate partition.
if you want to use it for real, then either wait for the end of April (or give it a month for the bumps to smooth out, and wait for May to pass.), or put up with some crashes and other weirdness.

i myself run it on an extra partition, just to check up with how it's coming together :) it's getting more and more stable.

[s] if you have an NVIDIA graphics card, it may cause issues as the new  drivers haven't landed yet. in the mean time, switch to he nouveau drivers [/s]


you could add a gnome-panel with the basic menus to the lower end of your screen. it gives you a way to navigate your apps and restart unity through ALT+F2, and end your session properly, should unity crash.
