### Post by coffeecat on 2011-02-13
Up until  now I've been partly put off Xfce by the lack of a  quick way to mount internal partitions. I've been spoilt by the Gnome Places menu (or Nautilus left-pane) which has just got better and better - so much quicker than using the terminal. Reading that this has been rectified in Xfce 4.8, I booted up a live USB prepared from the 10th February daily live Xubuntu ISO to find that all 9 of my internal partitions (yes - 9 :)) had been automounted - the desktop was splattered with volume icons.

That wasn't quite what I had expected, or wanted. I don't want to mount a partition until I say so, and some I don't want to mount at all. The Windows 7 C: partition was one that was mounted and, predictably, Windows had a moan at me when I next booted it up.

So... Question to those testing Xubuntu Natty: is this by design, or is this a temporary glitch?

True, but I think you'll find that they're not mounted, simply listed and available for mounting. It's the same as has been there in the Gnome Places menu for some time - it lists your available partitions but doesn't mount one until you click on it. The problem with Xfce in previous versions was  that it simply didn't have the functionality that you find in gnome for internal partitions. Or in KDE for that matter. Boot into Kubuntu and your partitions are listed in the left pane of Dolphin just as in Nautilus/Gnome - listed but not yet mounted.

Now it seems that Xfce has hurtled ahead of both gnome and KDE and mounts the damn things before you can blink. :) Hence my "Whoa!" comment in the title.
