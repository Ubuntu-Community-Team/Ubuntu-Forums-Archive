### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-03-07
People make the mistake of thinking that Unity is "finished" and unchanging. Unity in Ubuntu 11.04 is a long way advanced from Unity in 10.10. You can now sort your icons, you can change settings regarding when the launcher hides and what the launcher looks like, you can drag files onto programs in the launcher.

Plus, of course, there's different programs and other features that don't have anything to do with Unity itself. There's also more recent hardware support.

If you aren't sticking to LTS releases (which I never do), then you should definitely upgrade to 11.04 when it is released, or sooner if you don't mind doing a bit of testing.

### Post by bennachie on 2011-04-04
11.04 beta1 actually runs quite well on my Acer em350 (from the main hard disk, not a separate USB disk), and Unity suits the netbook environment rather better than the normal desktop environment. 

There are still quite a few serious bugs around (I seem to be experiencing about one non-fatal crash about every half hour - most of the relevant bugs have already been reported by other users, and many, but not all, are listed in the release notes). Whether all these bugs will be squashed by the release date remains to be seen - I wish the developers well. We may have to wait for 11.10 before the accustomed level of Ubuntu stability is achieved again.

In the meantime, openSUSE 11.4 seems to be the best iteration of that distro for many moons and, given the stated intention of the developers to avoid using Unity, Mint 11 will probably avoid almost all the problems, and still secure most of the underlying benefits, of Ubuntu 11.04.
