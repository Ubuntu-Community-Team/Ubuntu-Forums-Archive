### Post by Ozor Mox on 2007-07-25

So I helped him backup, brought my dodgy copy of Windows XP because he doesn't know where his reinstallation disc is or if he even has one. Formatted, installed Windows, set up the internet (he has a direct connection to broadband, no router, not the best idea I know)... I went on the net for a few drivers, internet died, and promptly suspicious spyware things started happening like popups, those annoying MSN Messenger things, etc. After not being able to get the internet working, I formatted and reinstalled again, this time going straight for anti-virus once the internet was active. 25% of AVG downloaded and the internet died, shortcuts appeared, more popups, requests for internet connections from suspicious addresses and so on...

I know this is unpatched Windows XP here, but was this really because of his direct internet connection and having no anti-virus straight away, and his computer got infected within minutes? The only other possibility is that my dodgy copy of XP has some "problems"...though I've used it before without too much trouble.
