### Post by Sunflower1970 on 2007-03-16
So. My story begins right around the time Windows Media Player came out...roughly end of October, at least that's when lots of people at my work-place updated their player. We're not supposed to be downloading any other player except WMP since it came with Windows XP Pro. 

Around November a few of us, including me, began to have trouble with our copy and paste function. Copying text from emails to our front end system, or word documents etc (I had the problem, every once in a while same with some other people, and one lady has had problems every single day since November.) It becomes a very important part of our job, since we deal with very large chunks of text that have to be copied and pasted. 
 
Anyway. At the time, no one had any idea what was going on with this one person's computer. Our departmental systems people couldn't figure it out, nor could our IT guys. One day all the supervisors, and managers came down and had a pow wow around this one person's computer to discuss what needed to be done, and no one could figure out why she had the problem every day, and some others just every once in a while. Our IT guys finally decided to wipe her hard drive clean, reinstall everything and see if that would clear out whatever was causing the problem. In the meantime, she had a temporary computer given to her, and it worked perfectly (Turns out it she was using WMP 10 on it). When they gave back her regular computer, her problems started all over again (Of course! WMP 11 was on it again). Finally, it was decided it must somehow be the network connections to the computer that are causing the problem so she will be moving her stuff to the other side of the office (which was fine by me, since she's a drama queen anyway) How they came to that conclusion is beyond me...but many of us think she was told this just to shut her up

But. Now to the funny part of the story. Yesterday, one of our Systems people came by and mentioned someone else had a very strange problem. They could not copy and paste their stuff when playing CD's. Once they closed the player everything was fine. She was using WMP 11. So, I tried it, out of curiosity, and yup. Sure enough, the same problem happened to me over and over and over as I tested it. And, the co-worker who was having the problem since November listens to music daily. One of the first things she does is open up WMP to get her CD's and other music ready to listen to for the day. 

What I find so amusing is the fact that it took so long to figure this out, and it came from the least expected person in our company, and also from a program that no one would have expected to be the culprit. I guess it does make some sense, though. WMP 11 has some sort of DRM inside it and that is causing the problems. I did a google search last night and found one person who, on an adobe forum who was having problems using the print-screen button when WMP 11 was active, but when it was closed he had no problems.

But, now drama-queen will not be moving, and she'll be complaining daily she can't use WMP 11 but has to use something else like WinAmp. (iTunes cannot be downloaded or used at work)
