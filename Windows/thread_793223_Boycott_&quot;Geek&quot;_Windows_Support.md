### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-05-14
Very simple problems, I will solve in very simple ways.

For instance, I noticed that a friend's computer was running awfully slowly. I finally found where the hardware list was, and the CPU was decent, so I looked in the system monitor. Most of the CPU time was being taken up by the anti-virus program. So I told them to dump the anti-virus and instead be careful about what they download from the Internet.

Anything that involves me doing research, I refuse to do on Windows. I can grep, less, ps and dmesg with the best of 'em, but those MS-DOS commands on the Microsoft Knowledge Base are crazy. Have you seen them? For instance, if Outlook Express stops retrieving all your e-mail, you have to put almost three lines of text into the terminal - those three lines are ONE COMMAND. And don't think that Microsoft tells you anything about exactly what the command does. More often than not, you put in the essay-length command, hack around with the registry, reinstall the program, and finally give up and install Thunderbird.

If a Windows problem is in the "too-hard basket", I just tell people the truth: I don't know very much about Windows, because I only really use Ubuntu. Sometimes they get interested enough to give it a try.

It's also an uphill battle to deprogram the Windows-user mindset of "Gotta run antivirus 24/7, need anti-spyware, anti-adware, two personal firewalls (plus one in their router that's been silently protecting them from attackers for two years, that they don't know about), and get a registry cleaner. Oh, and when that copy of Windows gets past the point of no return, just buy a new computer."
