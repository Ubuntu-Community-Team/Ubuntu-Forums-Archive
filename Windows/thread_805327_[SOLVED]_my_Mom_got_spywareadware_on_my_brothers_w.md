There has to be more to this.

By default UAC stops anything running until you specifically allow it, and IE is sandboxed, so anything that seeks to take advantage of an IE vulnerability actually installs itself in an isolated virtual folder.

So in order to get spyware you have to go to an infected website, and specifically allow the suspicious action admin level access, or have turned off UAC and IE protected mode, in which case it's your own fault.

I deliberately went to a few infected websites with Firefox 3 on my vista box to test this out, and no problems here.

I think this is a case of the OS only being as secure as the person using it.

### Post by bobbob1016 on 2008-06-11
I agree with aysiu, once you have spyware, there is NO way to be sure it is removed by a program, without a reinstall.  I deal with the elderly all the time, and they get nasty spyware.  I've done scans, and removed what they had.  Then after rebooting, after all the viruses and spyware were removed, they got reinfected while I was there.  The only way that happens is if they have something left.  I think you should suggest that he install Ubuntu, on a partition or through wubi, for people to use to check email.

However, let me reiterate, there is NO WAY WHATSOEVER you can be sure there isn't any spyware, or viruses left, without reinstalling vista.  When he does, he should use a program called driveimagexml, which images the drive, it can even brake the image into 700meg files, to burn them to CD.  Here is a link to DriveImage XML.
[http://www.download.com/DriveImage-XML/3000-2242_4-10443230.html?tag=lst-1&cdlPid=10635362](http://www.download.com/DriveImage-XML/3000-2242_4-10443230.html?tag=lst-1&cdlPid=10635362)

I do think EsetSmartSecurity (nod32) is the best, but once viruses/spyware (I like the term spyruses...) get into your system, they can do whatever they want, and there are many ways to hide from anti-virus products once they are on the computer.  Also, don't do what a lot of people do, and double up on anti-virus programs, if you have them just scan, that is more or less fine, but you can only have one running all the time.

Edit:  Reinstalling AND formatting the drive (not a quick format), not just installing vista over itself.
