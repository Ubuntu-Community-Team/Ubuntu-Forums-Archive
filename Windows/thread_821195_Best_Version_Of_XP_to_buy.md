### Post by Midwest-Linux on 2008-06-07
> **tdrusk said:**
> Okay. I see that the product I linked to is for system builders. Would this still be the right one for me? My problem is my computer came with Vista installed and only 512 mb of RAM. Not a good mix...

 What ever you decide on, make sure you can get XP drivers for your computer. Some Vista machines unfortunately only have Vista drivers available.

 OEM XP is for system builders and in most cases they make you buy a hardware peripheral along with the OS. I noticed on E-Bay that XP OEM sellers throw in some kind of hardware (even just screws or brackets) to satisfy the OEM requirement. OEM editions are meant for system builders and offer no support from Microsoft and you cannot later transfer that license to another computer like you could do with a full retail version. However you can save a LOT of money by going to a OEM edition.

Belarc advisor will help you find what drivers you have on your computer. Once you find out the name of the drivers, look for XP equivalents. You might want to ask the same question on the Vista forums too. 

[http://thevistaforums.com/](http://thevistaforums.com/)

 PS. XP will run good on 512 MB, but I would recommend 1 GB.

Good luck, keep us posted
