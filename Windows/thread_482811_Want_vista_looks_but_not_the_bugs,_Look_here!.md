### Post by FoolsGold_MKII on 2007-07-04
Two things I noticed about that "glass":

(a) That ain't glass, that's **transparency** you're using there. Vista uses true glass, which basically means it fogs the graphics behind the see-through area rather than just making it transparent.

(b) The taskbar has been rendered entirely transparent, which includes the text. Makes things harder to see as the white text is much darker than normal. In Vista, text and icons on the taskbar retains the same luminosity regardless of the level of glass.

Other than that, looks impressive.
