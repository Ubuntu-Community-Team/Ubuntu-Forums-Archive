### Post by sombertattoo on 2008-12-12
> You could always switch totally to Linux, and use virtualization to run your Windows games... Or maybe Wine may work...

wine is fantastic...I've played Call of Duty 4 in wine....it's slow at times (40-50fps on super low resolution cfg file with E2180 overclocked to 2.6ghz and a 9800GT Ultimate). The key with wine and performance is usually your cpu...the graphics card barely noticed the game but the cpu was under full load.

Wine is usually one of the first things I install on my machine.

Btw, normal Vista fps with COD4, same settings is 300fps..

I've had my own fair share of annoyances with Ubuntu, but they tend to simply be driver/minor bugs. Most of the annoyances are sorted out by the opensource community.

With Windows on the other hand, sure your support out of the box may be a little greater in general and less odd conflicts between packages, if you find a bug....no bets it'll be fixed in the next couple years. And if you need 64bit...just compile it yourself...no need to wait for someone with closed source to do it for you.

So to be honest, I've seen some ghastly problems with Ubuntu as well but I'm very much more accepting of them. In Vista, things like the inability to actually SPAN with Nvidia drivers is silly...you can dual view but for one big desktop, you have to downgrade. The problem with Span in Vista is deep inside it's dark innards.
