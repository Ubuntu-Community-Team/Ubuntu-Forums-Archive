### Post by goumples on 2008-08-13
Hey folks, I have a really weird issue with a new custom made PC.  

Whenever I shutdown the PC all the parts turn off, but the Power LED light stays on.  The only way I can turn it off is by holding down the power button for 5 seconds, or by flipping the switch on the Power Supply.  I've searched google and tried a few things but nothing has helped so far, any suggestions would be appreciated.

For reference the parts I used are a Gigabyte P35 Chipset Mobo, Corsair 650W PowerSupply, e8400 Core2Duo, 4GB OCZ Ram, EVGA 9600GT Video Card, Seagate 250GB Hard Disk, all running on Vista HP 64-Bit.

### Post by sayakb on 2008-08-14
Open your BIOS settings (F2/F10/Del at boot) and search for *Power down state*. Change if from **Suspend** to **Power down**.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-08-15
I just used Vista the other day. Remember that the "Off Button" in the Start menu in Vista actually just suspends the computer, which will cause similar behaviour to what you're describing. If you press the little arrow to the right, you can choose to properly shut down.
