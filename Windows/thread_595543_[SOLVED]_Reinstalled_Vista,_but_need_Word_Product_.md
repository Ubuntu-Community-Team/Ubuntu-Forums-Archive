### Post by wheredidrealitygo on 2007-10-28
Question for you:

Do you have a program in your start menu in the Microsoft Office folder called the "Activation Assistant"?

If you do, this is only a trial version of office, and you can only use it for the trial period.

Are you able to check which version of Word it is?

If it's Office 2007, then go to the pearl button in the top left, click it once, and then go to "word options".

In the lefthand column, go down to resources, and then in the right pane, click "about word". somewhere in that jumble there'll be a product id, consisting of xxxxx-xxx-xxxxxxxx-ish numbers. if the middle three numbers are "OEM" (and not numbers) then it was a preinstalled office version and the key should be with your office documentation. if the middle three are numbers I can tell you where to go depending on which numbers they are.

### Post by wheredidrealitygo on 2007-10-28
The replacement parts department can replace the disc & product key if it was a ***retail*** purchase (meaning on the receipt) but they *will* require faxed proof of purchase if they're sending you out a replacement disc. If you have a receipt that says that you bought Microsoft Office (as long as it's not an OEM version), that'll do just fine. If it was purchased within a certain amount of days (not certain the specific amount, I didn't work for that department) they can send it out for free, but if it's over that, there'll be a charge for $23 + shipping + tax (comes out to around 30-something).

The number is 1-800-360-7561, and it's a monday - friday business hour number. The agents are mostly english (maybe two french) first language, so you don't have to call India.

If it's not on the receipt, it may have been a promotion from best buy, but either way, they should still have given you a damn disc. I hate it when stores do that.
