### Post by TheKid965 on 2007-01-20
*IMO, Vista will be Windoze's downfall. Why pay $400 for a system that a.) will be very unstable and buggy for the first several monthes, b.) is highly prone to contracting all sorts of viruses and malware, c.) falls very short of being graphically pleasing (beryl/xgl on my lappy looks leaps and bounds better than any Aero demonstration I've seen), and d.) will require a massive hardware upgrade, which is out of the question for a poor college student with a 18 month old laptop.*

While I can't help but agree in principle with everything you say, there's just one little problem in there I can't help but point out:  I heard this *exact* argument *five years ago* when XP came out.  WPA, like Vista's DRM, was thought to be the end of the world and the catalyst that would drive everyone with a lick of sense to Linux.  Didn't happen, as once the typical user saw the activation process was quick and painless they decided it was worth putting up with - thus getting the camel's nose in the tent and beginning the erosion of users' rights that Vista represents the next step in.

And you know what?  MS will probably do everything it can to make the DRM as unobtrusive and easy to live with as possible, just as they did with product activation.  And like WPA, if it's done thus, the typical users will say "What was the big deal all about?" and accept it rather than seeing it for what it is... yet another reminder by MS that the only right they feel you should have is the right to buy more stuff with their name on it.

Will it drive more people to alternative OS's?  Undobutedly there will be those that do ditch MS for, say, Ubuntu in the wake of all this.  Will *enough* people make the jump to make a difference in the marketplace, and will those who jump to Ubuntu *stay* with it through the inevitable "Hey, this doesn't work like my Windows box did" frustration stages?  I'd like to think so, but recent history paints rather a different picture, sadly...
