### Post by dca on 2007-10-24
Maybe the '=' key is kaput.  Be careful removing it from the keyboard.  On laptops it's easy to get the keys off but a pain to put back on.  On most laptops, you remove the key, there's a kind of plastic lever effect running from  towards the top of the key itself into the keyboard and again on the back.  There's a rubber dealie that touches both the keyboard to underneath the key itself.  Inside that rubber is a little metal ball towards the top of it.  Sometimes that rubber ball gets mashed or fall closer to the metal (kind of raised up a bit) part of the keypad and creates a permanent indentation on that contact causing the laptop to exhibit what you're seeing.  If that's not it, thank God, if so the only recourse is keyboard replacement.  You may be able to get away with possibly re-seating the ribbon cable that connects the keyboard to the laptop.  Most laptops have screw holes w/ little pictures next to them under the laptop itself explaining what is being released when the screw is removed.  Should be between three and five for the keyboard.  Once removed, most laptops require a small flat tip screw driver that you pry the keypad itself up & out with.  Others require prying off a plastic plate, etc, etc...
