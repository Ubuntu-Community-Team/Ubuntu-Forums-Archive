### Post by BoyOfDestiny on 2006-12-23
Well, what can I say, I ditched Windows even faster due to fear of DRM etc... 
So here years have past, from longhorn to being dubbed Vista, although plenty of features were dropped, DRM went through... Vista is around the corner, and this aspect of it bothers me.

[http://www.cs.auckland.ac.nz/~pgut001/pubs/vista_cost.txt](http://www.cs.auckland.ac.nz/~pgut001/pubs/vista_cost.txt)

"Windows Vista includes an extensive reworking of core OS elements in order to
provide content protection for so-called "premium content", typically HD data
from Blu-Ray and HD-DVD sources.  Providing this protection incurs
considerable costs in terms of system performance, system stability, technical
support overhead, and hardware and software cost.  These issues affect not
only users of Vista but the entire PC industry, since the effects of the
protection measures extend to cover all hardware and software that will ever
come into contact with Vista, even if it's not used directly with Vista (for
example hardware in a Macintosh computer or on a Linux server).  This document
analyses the cost involved in Vista's content protection, and the collateral
damage that this incurs throughout the computer industry."

Seriously, without GNU/Linux, computing wouldn't be fun for me... Thanks to the Ubuntu Team for giving me a nice refuge too. :)

So count me in with the FSF and GPLv2 and v3... I like freedom zero of gnu way too much (and the the others too).

[http://badvista.fsf.org/](http://badvista.fsf.org/) 

I'd point this site out to someone you know who is planning on going from XP to Vista... I just hope for more mixed environments, but ideally (IMHO), for Vista to be a flop.

### Post by kevinf311 on 2006-12-23
I had been Beta testing Vista up to the Release Candidate 1. There was a mountain of crap that would keep me from installing it as a native, primary OS. The majority of that mountain was forgivable at the time because it was well documented in "TODO" lists that had what they were planning and why they went with the interim method. The networking was one of these. 

Not on the list though was the plan for keeping the OS from maxing out my system memory. Apparently Vista doesn't know what a page file is. Whenever the system needed to ask permission to perform an action, about every minute (not exaggerating), the screen would fade and a box would appear. This was very similar to the system used in Ubuntu. The difference was that effect pushed the resource draw over the top and my screen would blank, click, and then come back with the faded result. That's right, Vista maxed my 256MB video card *and* the 768MB of RAM I had. I came to this conclusion when I opened the system monitor with my computer idle (no programs, only necessary processes) and found that Vista required **70%** of my system memory to do nothing more than exist. I could not install the majority of my software because it was either deemed "incompatible" (including my Microsoft Wireless Desktop Elite Keyboard/mouse) or the install shield would get hijacked by the security devices mid-install.

Working in Vista was like driving on square wheels.
