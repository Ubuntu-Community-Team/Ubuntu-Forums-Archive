### Post by ComputerGeek31618 on 2008-06-25
You said it.  Bloated startup and background services aren't MS issues, they're the issues of the creators (although, as far as I can see, dell doesn't do too badly.  Not perfect, but understandable).  It's a way to hook customers on their services because thier basic computer experience is tied to somebody's battery meter, WiFi sniffer, media manager, whatever.

It's ALWAYS a good idea (I've done it to every computer in my house) to disable background programs and services you don't need (no matter what OS you have).  If you have Windows, msconfig.exe and disable all services that aren't function important like MS or nVidia, maybe some of your manufacturer's stuff.  Then disable almost all the startup items, unless you know exactly what they do and you know you want them.  And if anything has a gibberish name (not geekspeak shorthand) and the location or command is unfamiliar, it would be wise to kill it for security's sake.  

You will be surprised (at the least), maybe even shocked at who's eating your RAM during your computer session.
