### Post by Keyper7 on 2008-09-05
I like it. I think I now understand why they specifically hired Seinfeld.

A lot of people thought that using Seinfeld was a desperate attempt to reach out to Apple users and look cool. Seeing this ad now, I think the intentions here are exactly the opposite and it makes sense.

The characters of the sitcom were anything *but* cool. They were ordinary people, borderline losers, making and talking about commonplace stuff. In other words, they were human (all of them were based on people that Jerry and Larry David actually knew during their lives). One of the strong points of the series was that it had a very good sense of "real world" and showed situations that were extreme but were actually exagerated versions of situations most people face sometime in their life.

In this sense, this ad is very Seinfeld-ish. I think it succeeds very well in making the "we're not corporate overlords, we're human" point. I particularly liked when Bill Gates showed the membership card. "Yep, I'm a nerd, I won't deny that", he says by doing that. The point Microsoft is trying to make is simple: "Who are we? We are people like you, with your needs". They need ads like that because one of the most frequent criticism against them is that they now only see the money and forget what users actually want.

Stretching it a little bit, I see a subtle jab at Apple here. Gates, a guy with near-infinte money, prefers a functional, adequate shoe instead of an overpriced, less adequate one because looking cool is not his priority.
