### Post by aysiu on 2008-06-28
> **bijeeshvs said:**
> ok thats it, resetting the password but does it mean all our personal files can be made public and what will happen to the encrypted files.............
If someone has physical access and a little know-how, she has access to all your files, unless they are encrypted.

If the access is limited by time (several minutes alone with your computer), you can slow her down by setting a BIOS password and Grub password... or removing the optical drive.

If the access is not limited (i.e., your computer is stolen and can be worked on for hours or even days), then all your files are compromised, with the possible exception of your files being encrypted and the encryption being hard to break.
