### Post by LinuxIsInnovation on 2007-12-29
Well, a trojan/virus/worm has one or more active processes running.
Let me take the example of the Heap virus. It has 3 codes and one of them is "reproduce". All it does that it creates a hidden copy of the small codes into the Recycler or Recycled folder in USB disks and sets them to Autorun. A virus Passma spreads through the Autorun option only. Elucidating: when your USB disk gets infected by Passma, the Open, Explore and Autorun options are replaced by some ASCII Codes. Most of the users simply double click on the drive to open it. So when you double-click on the passma infected drive, the contents aren't displayed and passma is copied to the System folder :(
