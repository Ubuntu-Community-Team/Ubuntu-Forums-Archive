### Post by sub2007 on 2008-09-01
For what it's worth I don't see the point of a registry cleaner. I know of many security experts including Bill P (creator of WinPatrol) who also don't believe in them. I also know of plenty of people who've destroyed their systems by using one. A few thousand orphaned registry keys aren't going to majorly affect your system performance and I think the risk of inadvertantly removing something that you do need is too great to justify the benefit.

You should definitely make sure you know what you're removing if you do use one and always keep backups of your registry.

### Post by sub2007 on 2008-09-01
The problem I have with them is that they do seem to be touted as an essential system tool (with people like PC Tools touting their registry cleaner to people who want Spyware Doctor) and software vendors go to great lengths to make them newbie friendly when I would say they are more of a geek toy than anything else. The registry is a very dangerous place to go meddling with and on most systems you wouldn't see any real improvement in speed.

They certainly have no place in security. A misconception seems to be that you should run one after you have a malware infection to remove any fragments the infection left in registry. Any registry fragments left behind from a malware infection should be removed manually using expert guidance if necessary.

Russian Roulette is a perfect analogy.
