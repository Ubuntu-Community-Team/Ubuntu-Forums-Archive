### Post by solitaire on 2008-08-18
[http://www.computerworld.com/action/article.do?command=viewArticleBasic&articleId=9112885](http://www.computerworld.com/action/article.do?command=viewArticleBasic&articleId=9112885)

[quote=ComputerWorld]
More than one in every three new PCs is downgraded from [Windows Vista]("http://www.computerworld.com/action/inform.do?command=search&searchTerms=Microsoft+Windows+Vista") to the older [Windows XP]("http://www.computerworld.com/action/inform.do?command=search&searchTerms=Microsoft+Windows+XP"), either at the factory or by the buyer, a performance and metrics researcher said today.   According to Devil Mountain Software Inc., which operates a [community-based testing network]("http://www.xpnet.com/"), nearly 35% of the 3,000-plus PCs it examined had been downgraded from Vista to XP. 
 "Either these machines were downgraded by [sellers like] [Dell]("http://www.computerworld.com/action/inform.do?command=search&searchTerms=Dell+Inc.") or HP, or they were downgraded by the user after they got the machine," said Craig Barth, chief technology officer at Devil Mountain. "In any case, these machines are no longer running Vista." 
[/quote]

The issue is due to the speed of Vista compared to XP.

> 
 Last year, Devil Mountain benchmarked Vista and XP performance using other performance-testing tools and concluded that [XP was much faster]("http://www.computerworld.com/action/article.do?command=viewArticleBasic&articleId=9048658"). Barth said things haven't changed since then. "Everything I've seen clearly shows me that Vista is an OS that should never have left the barn," he noted. 
 Even when stripping Vista down to core components to make it as close in functionality to XP as possible, Vista was 40% slower, Barth claimed, citing recent tests Devil Mountain has performed. "Vista's performance had been an ongoing problem, and the only thing that's saving Microsoft's bacon is the faster processors and more RAM on today's PCs," he said. "Moore's Law is always on their side."

### Post by fiddledd on 2008-08-20
As I've said previously, I have no problems with Vista. Performance, stability, and external hardware recognition etc, are all just fine. But, as mentioned previously also, Vista came pre installed on my Laptop. So, as an experiment, and to try to understand what problems others are having, I installed it on the PC in my sig. I knew the CPU would handle Vista but wasn't sure about the GFX card, and especially the 1gb ram. Here's what happended:

Install went without a hitch, took 22 minutes, all hardware was recognised including the GFX Card.
Boot time was 36 seconds.
I installed the same software that I have on the Laptop, Avast Free and Zonealarm Free, Firefox, Thunderbird, so that I could see the difference with the laptop.
I also disabled the same services I always do.
I left Aero fully enabled as is with the laptop.
So the Desktop was running all the same programs and services.
I went online, sent and email, and then encoded a 40 minute video to DVD.
I didn't notice any difference between the desktop and the laptop. Programs loaded just as quickly, the conversion to DVD took about the same time. 

So I didn't have any problems, and, considering the 1gb ram, was quite surprised with the results. I didn't test any games, because I don't play games, and I would have been testing the GFX card rather than Vista.

I don't know if this proves anything really, except that I must be incredibly lucky.:)
