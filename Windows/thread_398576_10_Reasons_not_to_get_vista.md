The complaints about the pop-ups are from WINDOWS users who aren't used to this stuff... it's just MS's implementation of root permissions on windows.  Granted its tacked on top rather than smoothly integrated like linux, but having used it, it is a very comforting switch from all admin all the time.  As for that hospital stuff... lets be real.  Any decent hospital that is putting a BRAND NEW os in their ORs is out of their mind.  If this is really in issue, its the hospitals fault for deploying software incorrectly.

Look, I hate M$, and I don't use their OS.  But this is an improved windows.  XP was a seive.  I dread every moment I have to spend on an XP computer... they crawl at snails pace if the install is more than a few months old, have no real security built in, and no useful apps included.  Vista isn't great either, buts its better... security is vastly improved and windows defender is there to defend people who know nothing of security.  It doesn't look like it was designed for a four year old.  And the default apps (ie7, WMP, moviemaker, etc.) are at least fully functional.  Nothing special, but unlike their predecessors in XP they do what they are supposed to at an acceptable level.

The restrictive licensing is what scares me.  If I pay for an OS, I better be able to upgrade my PC and bring the license with it.  I better be able to run whatever version I buy on whatever hardware (or virtual hardware) I choose.  And it certainly better not EVER tell me that my legally purchased copy is illegal.  What is the one phrase you hear repeated about businesses?  The customer is always right.  F/OSS software takes this to the ultimate level, while M$ wantonly ignores it.  When enough people see (and I think KDE4 will go a long way towards achieving this) what F/OSS software can do, then I think M$ will be forced to listen and change.  But not until.
