you just need to uncheck the box.....no google or yahoo search bar will be installed. It isn't complex, just stay calm long enough to read the installer prompts ;-)

btw audiograbber has been freeware (i.e. no cost but no freedom) for over 3 years and coincidentally there has been no development on it for over 3 years :lolflag: I would go for cdex as it is Free, free of charge *and* under development, which is important due to the new and various ways media companies use to corrupt the CD standard to prevent copying. It's a 1st class ripper and encoder. 

If you don't mind non-free software then EAC is possibly the best ripper for Windows (if your drive caches audio....if your drive does not cache audio then cdex is better as it will rip and encode equally well and is also Free). It is free of charge but not Free/open source. 

You could also have a look at [http://cdrtfe.sourceforge.net/cdrtfe/index_en.html]("http://cdrtfe.sourceforge.net/cdrtfe/index_en.html")

>  
A small burning application for Windows. cdrtfe is a frontend for the cdrtools (cdrecord, mkisofs, readcd, cdda2wav), Mode2CDMaker and VCDImager. It can burn data disks (CD and DVD), Audio CDs, XCDs, (S)VCDs and DVD-Video disks.


cdrtfe supports the following features:

    * Data CDs
    * Audio CDs (including CD-Plus)
    * XCDs (with optional error protection)
    * Video CDs
    * Audio track extraction (wav, mp3, ogg, flac, userdefined)
    * Project files
    * Can be used with commandline options
    * Windows Explorer integration
    * Multi language support
    * DVD support: Data and Video DVDs
    * Remote drive support via RSCSI


It's a gnu gpl front end for cdrtools and resembles the older style windows CD/DVD tools like Nero 5. It can extract digital audio and convert into all the usual formats. It is updated regularly, the newest release was just 2 days ago.
