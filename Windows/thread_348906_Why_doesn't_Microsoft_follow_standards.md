### Post by kidders on 2007-01-29
Hi there,

The easy answer to that question is interoperability, imo. If Microsoft systems were designed to interoperate smoothly with others, it would be far too easy for customers to bail. For instance, buying a PS3 over an Xbox360 locks you into purchasing Sony games, all of which would become worthless if you were to switch to the Xbox. If they were intercompatible, fair competition (ie that in no way relied upon the "disincentive to leave" philosophy) would be the result ... which would be a corporate catastrophe!

Similarly, all MS's applications & services are designed to work well together, rather than to play nice with the neighbours. That way, once you buy one product, you're far more likely to buy two... then three... all from the same company.

Sucks, doesn't it!

What's particularly irritating about Microsoft though, is that, by and large, they *nearly* adhere to the standards. The level of compliance is almost always close enough to make grandiose claims ... but the reality is often quite different.

Hmm... there seem to be two fundamental issues being missed here.

The first has to do with common standards. These have nothing to do with making everything the same, or imposing unreasonable restrictions on people. Quite the opposite, in fact.

The second has to do with who is to blame when a company flouts international conventions. The vast majority of Microsoft customers use Microsoft products either because they have to, or don't know any better. How can *they* be to blame for Microsoft's anticompetitive activity?

Q. Okay. And now, again, for the Jury, what does embrace mean in this context as used by Microsoft employees?

A. It's used to indicate a strategy where Microsoft will embrace the standards or the specifications and interfaces of another company's software.

Q. Okay. And what does extend refer to?

A. Once the specifications have been embraced, then Microsoft will extend them and add additional interfaces proprietary to Microsoft.

Q. Okay. When you say add additional proprietary interfaces that are Microsoft's, what impact does that have technologically to other ISVs and OEMs?
