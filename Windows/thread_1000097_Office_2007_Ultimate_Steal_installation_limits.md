### Post by Therion on 2008-12-03
If you want the strict, letter of the law answer, read on...

You are permitted to install that copy of MS Office on ONE computer.  Period.  It doesn't matter how you install it, you get ONE install for your own personal and non-professional (assuming you got it cheaply because you got an Academic license) use.  

You my re-install it as many times as you need, but only on one computer at a time.  If you were, for instance, to sell computer A with Office installed on it, you are legally required to remove it from Computer A before reinstalling that license on Computer B.  That's the black letter law.  Make sense?

Now, just how tightly those laws are *enforced*, is another matter.
