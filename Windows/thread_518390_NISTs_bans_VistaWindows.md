### Post by satx on 2007-08-06
All,

While VISTA is what we refer to as "lipstick on the pig", the issue is not the VISTA OS per se (although I see code bloat for cosmetic sake), but the attendant hardware upgrades required to support it. For the Federal Government this is a rather large sum of the taxpayers' dollars. If there is to be a migration, it will be a phased approach as the older PCs in inventory approach their end-of-lifecycle.

The general end user is not ready/willing/able to migrate to Linux or Ubuntu. The cost of re-training the Federal workforce is also a major consideration.  FInally, there is no apparent return on investment (benefit vs cost) in migrating for the sake of migrating.

These are the real factors in determining when or if the Federal Government will migrate.

A humble Federal IT CIO.
