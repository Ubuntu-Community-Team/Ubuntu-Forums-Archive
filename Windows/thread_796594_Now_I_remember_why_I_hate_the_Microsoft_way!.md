### Post by Growbag on 2008-05-16
Imagine this:

I come into your home as a professional TV repairperson, and completely stuff up your TV for you.

You didn't actually ASK me to come in and mess with it, and you didn't give permission for me to mess with it either.

Then I say to you: "oh well, you can easily buy another TV from a TV store, that will fix it.  But if you want me to attempt to fix it, then it will cost you &#8364;70 (circa $150 US), oh and don't try complaining to my boss or anyone in my company because we have no phone numbers available, and even emailing will cost you money!".

You would want to kill me right there and then, that's a natural reaction in my book, and definitely justified.

So why do we allow Microsoft to get away with exactly the same tactic when it comes to "updates" (a term I use extremely loosely).

I have Windows Vista on my laptop because it came with it, and I use it once or twice a week to play a game that unfortunately won't run under emulation under Linux.

So I'm safe as long as I don't use it for anything serious, or do anything online with it methinks.

WRONG SUCKER!

Along comes Microsoft and deliberately and without any invitation whatsoever, and put what can only be described as a "virus" on my computer.

I never even got the usual "click yes to accept, or no to go straight to hell" box!

I simply wanted to shutdown my laptop, because I had finished my game, and it was late.

Oh no sonny, you're not allowed to do that!  Microsoft wants to install some updates right now, and you have no choice in the matter.

Do NOT power off the computer while we are f***ing it up for you, otherwise it might **still work** properly when you try to use the bloody thing tomorrow!!!!!!

I did NOT power off the computer, I left it to chug away for god knows how many hours, doing it's unholy work, and fell asleep.

I turn it on today hoping to play my game, and guess what?  Surprise surprise, it won't boot.

Oh, but I get a lovely message about how I should backup any important stuff before re-installing it again!



What annoys me the most, is that these morons are getting paid to do this kind of thing, and people don't mind!

What other product can you buy where you have no contact with the vendor, and have no comeback if something goes wrong?

Something is DRASTICALLY wrong with this whole business, there is NO hope for mankind!!
