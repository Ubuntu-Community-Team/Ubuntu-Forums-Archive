### Post by Dr. C on 2007-12-09
Windows Starter edition is a specially crippled version of Windows for certain "developing" countries.. 

The XP version is limited to 800x600 resolution and 3 applications open at one time (my Windows 3.1 box did far better than that back in 1993). There are also all sorts of limitations (memory must be less than 512MB, processor slower than an early P4 etc.). The is also a Vista version with similar limitations. Imagine a version of Vista that is limited to a maximum of 512 MB! of RAM.

Still since it is not available in the United States I believe a case could made be made against Microsoft for dumping software in contravention of international trade agreements, but that is another story. 

Personally I find the entire concept behind Windows Starter edition repulsive.
