### Post by ewtesterman@cox.net on 2006-12-20
Look at the back of you computer and see if you have another connection like the one your monitor is currently connected to. If you do I would recommend that you turn off your computer, remove the cover, take the card that your monitor is connected to out of the machine (carefully). Now connect your monitor to the other location and turn on your machine, if that fixes it then your video card is probably bad. If you do not have an extra connector you can get a video card a least a cheap one off the internet for under $30 us. You may want to try a different monitor if you can before you go buying parts to through at it though. Hope this helps. You may have stumbled across these forums by mistake, but I think you are in the right place. I have found it very difficult to find any forums for any operating system that have been this nice and informative. If you can get to a different computer with a cd burner I would recommend that you go to [www.ubntu.com](www.ubntu.com) and download and burn a cd boot from it. If the problem goes away then you have a problem with your windows installation, if it does not go away then you have a hardware problem of some kind and it would be difficult for anyone to help you with that kind of problem without being there.
