### Post by Ptero-4 on 2007-12-17
The "user experience" score is IIRC (only seen Vista in a internet cafe's computer once) the way Windoze Vista indicates you how "pleasant" is going to be using Vista in your computer and as you guessed it is calculated on each boot-up based on a list of brands and models of certain specifical hardware components (like your soundcard and GPU). This score is for the most part meaningless and Windoze Vista automatically decreases it if the computer have had the same components through a specifical period of time or number of boot-ups (I haven't seen it myself but the guy at the internet cafe keeps telling me that this score went from 4.5 to 2.9 in the Vista computer without he changing hardware components). 
To make it short. Don't worry about that "score", in fact it could even begin higher than that and decrease to 3 without you even noticing it.

### Post by digital_exhaust on 2007-12-22
The "score" always equals the lowest rated component in your system. If everything scores a 5.9 other than say hypothetically your hard drive, which scores a 3, then the system will be given an overall score of 3. 

The "scoring system" was devised as an easy way for Vista users to determine if a given piece of software (games, really) will run acceptably on their system. I think Microsoft feels that the average computer user (gamer) is too stupid to read the old Minimum System Requirements list that has been on software boxes for years..... 

It's a stupid system and doesn't mean anything....
