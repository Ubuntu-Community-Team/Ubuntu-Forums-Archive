### Post by newb85 on 2014-11-01
I was recently gifted a nice Bluetooth speaker.  It worked well with my phone, but my preference was to be able to use it for our (my wife's and my) laptops.  So, I jumped online and spent a couple dollars on two USB Bluetooth adapters that came (to the U.S.) slow-boat from China.

The adapters arrived this week, so it was time for testing.  

First, my laptop (running Ubuntu 14.04).  I plug in the adapter.  Ubuntu identifies it, adds the Bluetooth icon to the app indicator area, and turns it on (but not discoverable) automatically.  Beautiful.  I can easily add the speaker (which I've turned on) via a dialog accessible from that Bluetooth icon.  In almost no time, the speaker is added.  My only complaint is that I have to open the Sound settings dialog and choose to use the Bluetooth speaker instead of my laptop speakers, which in retrospect is hard to complain about.  After all, I can't expect Ubuntu to be a mind-reader about what devices to automatically switch to.  Everything works pretty smoothly.

Next, my wife's laptop (running Windows 7).  I plug in the adapter.  A notification pops up in the taskbar acknowledging the unknown device.  Windows quickly identifies it and begins downloading the drivers (really?) and then installing them.  Once Bluetooth is up and running, it's time to connect to the speaker.  Once I connect, Windows begins downloading the drivers for the speaker (again, really?) and installing them.  As in Ubuntu, I still have to elect to use the Bluetooth speaker.

Now for the conondra.  I want to use the speaker with either laptop at will, and purchased two adapters so they could be left in the laptops (they're very low-profile).  First, the adapters have the same MAC address.  (I guess I can't expect Chinese producers of $1 adapters to abide by FCC regulations).  So, having both turned on at the same time will confuse other devices.  Turning off the one in my laptop is easy, as there's a toggle switch on the menu from the app indicator icon.  My wife's--not so much.  Windows didn't see fit make things that easy.  Turning off the adapter requires going through multiple dialogs.  And there's no other way to disconnect from the speaker without un-pairing it (removing it from Windows' memory, so it has to be set up again next time).  Moreover, whenever I remove the adapter from the laptop, the next time I plug it in Windows wants to install the Bluetooth drivers again (give me a break!).  All in all, I find Windows' support of Bluetooth atrocious.  

Does anyone know of a free third-party utility for Windows for controlling Bluetooth.  It would be greatly appreciated.
