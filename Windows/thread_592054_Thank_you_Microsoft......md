..for making your OS so bloated.
I've been learning a bit about engineered obsolesence. [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planned_obsolescence]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planned_obsolescence")
Its inevitable in big business, in fact it tends to turn big business into big industry. the trouble with the computer industry is that electronic hardware is difficult engineer a 'lifespan' into( lack of moving parts/unpredictable usage).
Enter a software vendor who has the vast majority of the users convinced they need to buy from, - to stay young and up-to date,  to keep up with the Jonses down the street, or just so they don't feel themselves becoming obsolete.  
The computer makers count themselves as winner, because they sit on the same bandwagon. If people want bigger and faster computers to run vista, they'll usually buy a new one to make sure everything is big and fast enough.
I don't even mind Vista, but its obvious that it shouldn't need those hardware specs, but then, thats what was said about XP, and Win95....
If it weren't for Microsoft bloating the 'next big thing', the average computer would be much slower, and smaller, and because production numbers are smaller, they would cost much, much more. 
So I found a computer that was last years surplus, put linux on it, and for a bargain price, it ROCKS :)
Its just a pity that M$ gets to make so much money for something they deliberately made inferior to what they know how to build. 

Just out of fairness, let be generous and make sure the linux developers have the $ they need to keep us saving $
