### Post by karellen on 2007-08-25
yesterday I've finally decided to give windows vista a try. I've heard so many (especially negative things, on these forums and not only) about it that I was definitely very curios to have it in front of my eyes to see how it behaves, what does wrong and what does good. so I installed it yesterday. in my little review I tried to be as impartial and objective as I could, not to be influenced by other opinions that I've read, but only my own personal beliefs coming from my direct experice. after using it for aproximately a day, here are my thoughts and conclusions:

**installation**: very easy, follow a few steps and it will install. I like the new option of deleting, formating partitions.
**drivers**: as I have a pretty old rig - Pentium 4 with 512 mb of ram, msi mainboard, realtek network card, geforce 4 video card and so - all my hardware was properly set and detected. my 17 inch monitor had the correct resolution, I just set the refresh rate a bit higher. my network card was working properly, all I had to do is input my username and password for my pppoe connection and I was online.
**look and feel**: first of all, I can't use aero (and in fact I'm glad for that) as I have an ol geforce 4 with no enough video memory. I like the default non-animated theme. the fonts were ok. I really like the new start menu and the instant search feature. it was about time...
**speed**: it takes a liitle longer to boot than xp, but after that I couldn't feel any difference in terms of speed. it's pretty responsive. a quick glance at task manager shows that I use 394 mb of ram right now, with internet explorer, total commander, yahoo messenger and mcafee security center running in background. so, until now, it's not a resource hog for me (I already mentioned that Aero is disabled - for good).
**applications**: I happen to like new windows mail, it's a decent mail program - not on par with outlook or evolution, though, windows photo gallery. I installed vlc for playing videos (windows media player  couldn't open and avi or at least download the codecs for i, come on), winamp for music, total commander, mcafee security center, dc++, utorrent and a few others. in aproximately 2 hours, I had my system set the way I wanted and with the applications I wanted.
**overall**: I didn't have time to explore all the features until now, but I'll continue exploring the os and I'll probably find more pros and cons. it's not really that different from xp, it has some nice additional features but nothing revolutionary. my personal advice: if it comes with your new computer, take it, otherwise it's not worth the money (especially the ultimate edition, the one I iused, btw). maybe home premium, but anyway it's a matter of taste and subjective judgements.

### Post by Eezyville on 2007-08-25
I had Vista on my laptop for a good few months. I loved the Media Center and I wish Ubuntu had something just as good. The Aero effect looks nice but Beryl owns it if it stays stable, but thats just my configuration. I have 2 gigs of ram on my laptop so it was fast. My overall score in the experience index was 4.5 which is high. The problem came when I wanted to play games and do other normal things. You see when I looked into the task manager there was always 1 gig being used by something. So I only had 1 gig to do anything. My gaming suffered, day to day tasked took forever, and I felt cheated. It also crashed alot when I needed something done. One day it crashed 3 times back to back. I got pissed and decided to sacrifice pc gaming for a few months and installed Ubuntu. 

Well Ubuntu isn't perfect either and sometimes I missed Vista and I'll always miss XP. There are some features that I miss such as the media center. I also miss using some programs like Nero, iTunes, and my video converter. I download alot of anime you see and most of it comes in realplayer format so i have to convert it into avi. Media was not great on either OS. Some times the video quality was bad using some Ubuntu programs and some times the videos didn't play using Windows Media Player. Also Ubuntu takes a long time to start up for me, I don't understand why. It boots up fine but when I log in it gets slow. First my second hard drive has to be manually mounted, then three minutes later Beryl loads up and then my keyring manager and startup programs run. I have ubuntu loaded on another laptop and it boots quickly. I need to solve this problem.

In the end if I can get programs to replace the ones i miss; Media Center, Nero, video converter, and I'm currently experimenting with Amarok, then I'd be a little happier with Ubuntu. If I can get it to load up faster then I'll be impressed with Ubuntu. Finally if I can get this damn thing to hibernate and suspend so i can load up my previous works then I'd love Ubuntu. I'm not giving up, just running into bumps and I have only 4hrs a day to work on it thanks to work. That time is slowly leaving me. 

I feel empowered learning a new OS and leaving behind M$. I feel free but I now understand that freedom isn't free, it needs alot of time.:wink:
