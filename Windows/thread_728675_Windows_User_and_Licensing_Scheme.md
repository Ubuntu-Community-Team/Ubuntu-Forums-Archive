### Post by beatbros on 2008-03-19
Hi,
as far as i know if your Windows is OEM (packaged for your hardware from vendor) you can't transfer your license to another PC/Laptop.
If your windows is a retail version you can transfer to another pc, but you have to uninstall from previous pc, call M$ and reactivate.

Here you can find all license terms for M$ Products:
[http://www.microsoft.com/about/legal/useterms/default.aspx](http://www.microsoft.com/about/legal/useterms/default.aspx)

Here you can read about the important change for Vista retail version:
[http://windowsitpro.com/article/articleid/94117/microsoft-adjusts-windows-vista-license.html](http://windowsitpro.com/article/articleid/94117/microsoft-adjusts-windows-vista-license.html)

### Post by rickyjones on 2008-03-20
You definitely do not need to do anything special to get rid of Windows on one hard drive. In that regard your co-worker was definitely misspoken in my opinion.

If the copy of Windows is OEM then you are not supposed to use it on any other computer per the EULA that you agreed to. However it will work on another computer. Yes, you will need to call Microsoft to reactivate it. No, they will not scrutinize you. This is FUD. They will ask you for a long stream of #s from the Activation window. They'll read you a confirmation code. You'll be done after about 10 or so minutes, mostly waiting on hold.

Good luck!
