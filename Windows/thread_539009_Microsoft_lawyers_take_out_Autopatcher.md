### Post by BigSilly on 2007-08-30
Well, [it was inevitable sadly.]("http://www.theregister.co.uk/2007/08/30/autopatcher/") If you weren't familiar with it, Autopatcher was a user-friendly program designed to make the patching of Windows systems easy. You could basically put years of updates, fixes and patches on one disc and sort out your PC in one (fairly) quick fix should you need to perform a clean install. The thing that was great about it though, was that you didn't have to download WGA (Windows Genuine Advantage, that no doubt many of you are fully aware of). I suppose that Microsoft saw this as being potentially damaging to their business, and shut them down.

I was great for me some time ago, as our home PC got infected with WGA, which then claimed we had an illegal copy of XP. I managed to remove it, but the damage was done and my foray into Linux began in earnest. Thanks Bill! 

A moment's silence for the Autopatcher team then....
