IANAL

There are basically two way to play commercial DVD's

a) Crack the DRM encryption with DeCSS or a program that incorporates an implementation of DeCSS
b) Buy a program licensed by the DVDCCA [http://www.dvdcca.org/]("http://www.dvdcca.org/") that allows the playback of CSS encrypted DVD's according to the DVDCCA rules. 

Whether (a) is legal or not depends on whether the country in question has "anti-circumvention" legislation and if it does whether the particular DRM cracking application is allowed under the "anti-circumvention" legislation. The key point here to understand here, since this is an international forum, is that something that may be perfectly legal in one country could be a serious offense in another. 

In Canada Bill C-61  aka "The Canadian DMCA" has not yet passed (it may get amended or simply  die on the order paper if we get a fall election), so (a) is still, maybe for only a few months, an option. In the United States go with (b), and pay your dues to your corporate masters, or hire a lawyer.
