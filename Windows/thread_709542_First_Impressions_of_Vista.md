### Post by Sam Lars on 2008-02-27
So I just got my first real taste of what Vista is like.
I've seen screenshots and the demos in the stores that look all nice, but I hadn't really gotten down to actually installing and using it.

The installation was nice, really.  It gave me a reasonably good idea of what it was doing while still letting me know how far each step was and that it was still moving (not frozen).  I like how they've really cleaned up the startup screen.
One thing - when you try to log in as administrator, it automatically assumes you want to log in locally, even if you have a domain set up.  A little annoying to have to type domain\administrator...
What version do you have to have to get the fancy Aero transparency?  Business evidently doesn't have that... it look alright, just a different XP.  I actually think that XP is more attractive than Vista Business was.  It seems to fade and all better than Vista did.
And what's with the new system rating thing?  It may be fine for people that don't know much about computers, but I wanted to know how it was getting those numbers, and I couldn't get a straight answer.
There was also trouble getting it to retrieve Group Policy.  It kept erroring out until it finally worked.  I don't know if that was Vista or the DC replication that finally fixed itself...
I do like how IE loads really quickly, since that's a common app, though if it's not for you it might not be optimal.
The gadgets are a nice step, too, except for the fact that I wanted to to a right click and configure like with Gdesklets, but evidently you take it or leave it...
I do like the help that seems to be linked from everywhere.  I think they've really improved the system interface for those tasks.  Being used to navigating the old Control Panel, I felt a little bit not in control, but I still like what they did with it.

So overall I think it was a but uglier than XP, at least without transparency and such, and it seems dumbed down more, but in a way I like it.  It's not what I'm used to, but I like it.
Not for my computer, of course.  Ubuntu is awesome, does everything I want it to and more, and all for free.
