### Post by FoolsGold on 2007-03-15
Hi, new member here (well... sorta; went by the name "GFree" for a short while). Anyway, I'd like to ask a question for those of you who've totally converted to Linux, as in, Linux is the only OS you're running, no dual-boot. Running Windows in something like VMWare is fine, since it doesn't have the power to do the more advanced stuff like 3D gaming, so this still applies.

Do you ever suffer the temptation to pack it all in and go back to Windows? I suppose this would mainly affect those of us who've recently convered, and as such have the experiences of Windows still reasonbly fresh in our minds. Do you ever think, "bugga, I'm missing out because I can't play such-and-such games", or "bugga, that's some cool new software I can't use", etc?

One of the things that I (somewhat) enjoyed in Windows was gaming, and although there's enough in Linux to keep me occupied, I find myself still reading the news that occurs in Windows-land. I didn't mind coming to Linux because gaming had become a bit of a bore, but I still have a habit of reading old sites like Steampowered.com to see what Valve have added to their catalogue for example, even though none of it really applies to my system of choice. Old habits die hard, no?

There are also times, mainly when I'm aggitated, when I wonder why things are more complicated than they should be. Take for example, FTP servers. PROFTPD with the Gnome GUI vs FileZilla Server is a useful comparison; FileZilla server has less features than PROFTPD, but it's a heck of a lot easier to configure. GPROFTPD has some stupid bugs that ignore what I do; I click on a checkbox to remove the asking of a password, click apply, the bloody thing re-enables the checkbox. I then have to edit the config file manually to remove the password requirement, and when I go back, THEN the checkbox will stay off, but if I happen to click apply, it will turn back on again! When struggling with something that I really need to work, illogical behavior such as this becomes aggitating.

Note, I'm NOT saying that I have any intention to move back to Windows at all. I just want to know if any of you are ever tempted. There's always the option to dual-boot, but I hate dual-booting because I end up sticking with Windows and leaving Linux to rot due to the old "path of least resistance" element of human nature. So for me, it's one of the other. I suppose part of my problem is that I continue to make things difficult for myself by reading gaming sites that have NOTHING to do with me anymore, mainly out of habit. On the other hand, I like to know what's going on.

Remember, I'm asking mainly newbies like myself, who haven't been away from Windows long enough for the memories to fade. Then again, if you're been using Linux for ages and still find it tempting now and then, let me know too. Not that I expect anyone to actually fold, but surely I'm not the only one to have the occasional... lapse in will. :)

EDIT: I gotta learn not to write so much for such a simple question in the future. :D
