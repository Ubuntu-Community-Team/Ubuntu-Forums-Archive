### Post by Simon G Best on 2008-03-03
> **Midwest-Linux said:**
> One positive approach is to have manufacturers have two operating systems on all new computers sold. Its not that far fetched as it may seem. Any computer manufacturer could simply just add Linux to any windows machine sold. I am not sure is Microsoft would stand for it though.

Actually, I think Microsoft might like the idea, if it's done in just the "right" kind of way.

> I am willing to bet that if MS really wants to get the European Union off its back is to simply tell manufacturers to include a Linux OS installed with a Windows operating system for all new computers sold. Not just in the EU, but world wide and that act alone would satisfy a lot of Microsoft's detractors.

And the manufacturers need not bother properly setting up those Linux-based OSes - *and that's where Microsoft really turns it to Microsoft's advantage.*

Just think of it.  If all new PCs came with a (small) Linux partition, Microsoft, as you say, would find it a lot easier to dismiss allegations of anticompetitiveness, and the like.  And, what's more, as the manufacturers wouldn't have any particular need to set those Linux systems up properly, they'd often be somewhat inadequate for the hardware.  Those customers who even bothered to try those Linux systems would generally have bad experiences, and would conclude that Linux isn't very good.  They'd choose to stick with Windows, possibly removing the Linux partitions so as to make other use of that disk space.

And there'd also be plenty of users who wouldn't even bother to find out what it means for their computers to have Linux installed.  They'd, perhaps, quite happily think they must be using Linux in some way, even if they never use anything but Windows.

This would be a tremendous opportunity for Microsoft to market against Linux based OSes in a devious way that just seems, to me, to fit Microsoft's ways all too well.  But would they actually do this?  I don't know, and, right now, I've got to eat sausages.
