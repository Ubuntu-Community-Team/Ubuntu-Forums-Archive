### Post by Yuki_Nagato on 2008-08-14
Overall, I noticed very little good change about Vista.

Windows Defender still has not found some of my startup applications, and therefore, cannot stop them if they were malicious.

I turned Aero off, which left me with the more attractive 95 theme.

Vista comes with a RAM hogging Sidebar.  But if you want that, I suggest Google Desktop.  It even pops up on Tablet PCs, which have no screen space to begin with.

And the Administrative stuff.  Windows has their "sudo" like "Run-As" environment, where unprivileged users are able to do administrative things.  But it keeps warning you and asking for confirmation, and this bogs things down.

Otherwise, I have had a desktop running the original pre-installed XP for seven(?) years now.  It has survived two virus outbreaks (both Norton and I got lax) and has given me very few problems (beyond the slow-down.)
