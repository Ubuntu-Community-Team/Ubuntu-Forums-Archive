### Post by Midwest-Linux on 2008-02-26
I have been reading various posts on why some can't stop using Windows. A lot of reasons would be be for gaming or certain programs that only can run on Windows. 

 The solution to all this is rather simple and its a step by step process for some, much like quitting cigarette smoking or drinking. Some can and have gone "cold turkey" and just went to Linux exclusively, others have gone to Linux with occasional "lapses" back to Windows.

 My suggestion is simple, pick a Linux distro that you are comfortable with. Use that as your main machine. If you still need Windows for some programs, then make that your second or third machine...not your main one. 

 Make your main (Linux) computer set up with everything, printer, wireless, camera, MP3 player, burn CDs and DVDs, email...if it takes some time to download the drivers or work it out, and asking some questions on this forum, then do it. While it is great to have a computer that surfs the internet and watch video and audio, getting it to run everyday apps should be the end goal.


Make that your main goal, once you got that accomplished then the transition to all Linux all the time is that much easier. But you should  have a reason why you want this done, whether its freedom from proprietary operating systems, freedom from WGA, freedom from viruses and malware, and the knowledge that you no longer have to
pay tribute to those fine people in Redmond. Your computer is yours and you can tweak it, improve on it anyway you wish.



 Best of all, maybe you can start a small business of offering to install Linux on other people's computers. Once people see you can do everyday applications with linux, others can can shut the windows and open the source.  

 Start taking older computers, fixing them up and installing Linux on them. Go to computer shows and sell Linux computers there. Sell Linux computers on E-Bay.
