### Post by BogdanV on 2009-01-22
As a curiosity, how old is the HDD on which you have the C: drive ?

 It might be possible that the area of your hard-drive where the video drivers are stored has been damaged in time. 

For instance, it happened for me once that when booting up Win98, the system ran with absolutely no problem, only with a error box regarding network drivers popping-up at start-up.
All went fine for months until I decided installing ReactOS to replace Win98. Then, after some research, I realised that my HDD was the reason of failure.

### Post by BogdanV on 2009-01-22
> **DM was on fire! said:**
> Tomatz, thanks for understanding. :)



THe hard drive was put in in December 2007, but I'm unsure as to how old it was before it was put in. :(

 You mean that the hard drive was used before being mounted in your PC ? 
 Well, if this is the case, a possible hard drive failure would be plausible.
 If you know the manufacturer of your hard drive, you may try to search on their site for a disk check utility that can be booted like the Windows CD (without the need of a functional OS) and see what the results of the scan tell you.
 Normally, surface scans should detect errors on the disk's sectors.

 My advice, if you are interested in checking the integrity of your hard disk, is to get a Windows98 Boot Disk and in its DOS prompt, type : scandisk /surface to run a surface scan on your hard disk.
 I recommend this version of scandisk at least because from my personal experience, it has been much more useful than newer versions of scandisk/chkdsk.
