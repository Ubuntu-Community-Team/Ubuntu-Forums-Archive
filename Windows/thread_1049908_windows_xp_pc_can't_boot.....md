### Post by shadoweva00 on 2009-02-01
Jumper settings are set according to configuration, not changing them may be the problem. (there should be a diagram on the hard drive to tell you how if you don't already know.)

If that doesn't work, it's over and the drive is dead (or it's the rarer case of the connections to the motherboard not working, but you were able to detect it and write to it so very unlikely). Only certain RAID configurations are capable of hot swapping, and they don't usually have PATA connections (SCSI and SATA). Get a new drive, find your windows product key, and as the official Microsoft stance on installation media, you're on your own to get another install disk.

(Also you have to format it if you just started using it, xbox filesystem = fatx not fat, and not supported by windows. changing jumpers while a computer is running will destroy something, you should really just give up and use standard hard drives. A new drive might not work though,that hotswapping may have damaged the motherboard.)

Just don't hot swap drives on a machine that's not capable, and even if it is capable you shouldn't do it until the system is past bios and post stuff (and then usually RAID initializes). Or when the manual says you should do it. I hope this wasn't your main machine and don't hope losing it.
