### Post by mg620 on 2007-03-08
I am one of the few who will be moving back to WinXP and removing ubuntu.  I currently run a dual-boot, but need the HD space and don't have the money to just buy another drive.  I love ubuntu and what it stands for, but I spend *way* too much time messing around trying to get things to work and trying to find software that will do what my XP software does.

Specifics:
- nVidia drivers are a real pain.  Every time I upgrade ubuntu I have to rerun Envy to get them to work.
- my printer has no drivers, unless I want to pay turboprint for them
- my sound card delivers poor quality in ubuntu, even with newer drivers
- I can't get Quicken to run in Wine and I don't want to pay $30 for MoneyDance, which is a nice program and light years ahead of other linux money programs
- video editing (for dopes like me) in linux is just not there (kino is very promising, but is years behind what I already own)

In short, I have an OS with all the software I need, runs very quickly, and hasn't crashed in 5 years...it's WinXP.

All that being said, when I do eventually have the cash (and the need) for a new computer, I hope to just build one and start fresh with ubuntu (and with hardware known to work well with linux).  I really do love ubuntu.  It's a slick OS.  And the community thing is just excellent.  Hopefully, by the time I can do that, there will be some more application choices in the few realms of linux apps that I can't find what I need.

Thanks to everyone for all of their contributions to this great community.
