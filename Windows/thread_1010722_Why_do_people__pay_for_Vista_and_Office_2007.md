### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-12-14
> **upapilot said:**
> And they can still keep Windows optional for the foolish people who still want it!):P

Any OEM who offers Windows as an "optional extra" will get smaller/no rebates from Microsoft. This increases the cost of including Windows, and the store or manufacturer becomes less competitive.

This is against the spirit of the law in many countries, but it's not against the letter of the law because it involves rebates, not the actual buy price of a Windows license.

To answer the other question, people pay for Microsoft Office 2007 because either they don't know of the compatible alternatives, or they don't believe that Openoffice.org is compatible enough. Windows people spread a lot of FUD about OOo's compatibility with MS-Office documents.

Please call it "Microsoft Office" or "MS Office" - if you just refer to it as "Office 2007", you're not helping anyone to realise that there's a non-Microsoft alternative.

### Post by stefangr1 on 2008-12-14
They do it because windows / ms-office is a de facto *standard*. Everybody uses it, so it is a huge advantage to have a compatible product. And, also, time and effort has to be spend on learning how to use openoffice. whereas most people already have some knowledge on ms-office. The chance that the time and effort spend on openoffice will pay off in other places (for example at work), is relatively small, since as said ms-office is the standard.

The reason that companies use mainly microsoft / ms-office is the same: when they would decide to start using linux and openoffice, a lot of previously acquired experience of their employees would be useless, so a lot of time would have to be devoted to learn how to use the new systems. Therefore, linux and openoffice have huge nonmonetary costs.
