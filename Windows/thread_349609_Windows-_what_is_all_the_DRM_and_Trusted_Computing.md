### Post by az on 2007-01-30
> **presbp said:**
> What are the main implications of these things that make all of these things so controversial?  What do you think the impact of these things will be on the advancement if computing?

For the sake of simplicity, these are all DRM.  Digital Restrictions Management is a way for media companies to sell you content (cultural works) with more and more restrictions (you get fewer and fewer rights).  The dangerous part is the fact that you often do not have a choice to use a free-libre operating system to play this content. 

For example, if your main reason for using your computer is to play that content, you will forgo your rights to use a free-libre operating system.

The music/video/cultural indistry has to find a way that protects their rights while not removing rights from its users.  

If you read Lawrence Lessig's Free Culture (available free online), you get a good sense of how copyright law has evolved.  Never before have so few people controlled the culture of so many people.

In some countries (who were too poor to be part of the latter few years development of mainstream (pop) culture - Example Brazil) the freedom that the internet provides (it costs almost nothing to distribute your cultural works to millions of people) is being used and many people are starting to use the internet as the backbone of cultural expression.

Will people get sick of the big record companies and eventually get their music/media/culture elsewhere?  I think it will eventually happen, but not tomorrow.
