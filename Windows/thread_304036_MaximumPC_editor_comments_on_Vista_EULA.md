### Post by Bartender on 2006-11-21
Just got the December 2006 MaximumPC.  On page 5, Will Smith comments on Vista's EULA.
  
Quote: "Most software EULAs are documents filled with nasty lists of things you can't do with the software you purchased, but the clause Microsoft snuck in unannounced is especially evil.  It's titled; "Reassign to another device."
Here's the exact text: *'The first user of the software may reassign the license to another device one time.'* That means if you purchase a copy of Vista at retail - be it the $200 Home Basic or the $400 Vista Ultimate package - you have the right to install that OS on one machine.  When you retire that machine, you can install the OS on a second PC, but that's it.  Unlike the Windows XP EULA, which permits you to continue transferring the OS indefinitely (as long as you remove it from the old machine), Vista's EULA restricts each copy of the OS to two computers."

He goes on to concede that this will affect relatively few people because the vast majority of Vista builds will end up in OEM machines purchased by people who don't upgrade. 

But MaximumPC is geared towards the people who do go under the hood, and who will run into trouble with the Vista EULA.    

His summary:  "I've never been more conflicted about a product before.  If the EULA remains as-is, I'll be unable to recommend Vista to the MaximumPC elite.  The hitch is DirectX 10, which only works in Vista.  I just saw the first batch of games written for DX 10, and they make "next-gen" console titles look like bantha fodder.  Every hardcore gamer will be forced to make a Hobson's choice.
This is a dark day."

I realize that Ubuntu isn't a gamer's OS, but thought that this sort of reaction from a PC magazine editor might be of interest.
