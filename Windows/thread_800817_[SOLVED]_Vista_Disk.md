### Post by r76 on 2008-05-23
If it's any consolation I think the key words are "modern system", like you said yourself.  I bought a Dell Latitude D630 in October with Vista, played with it for a week then wiped the hard drive and installed XP (after a bit of monkeying with Gutsy).  So for 8 months was fine but having problems with my backups.  I then wiped the drive and installed Vista a month ago.  It absolutely flies on this machine, it feels snappier than my XP install and is a lot nicer to use.

I would say that is probably because the machine was bought as a Vista machine, a long time after Vista had been released.  Therefore all the hardware issues had been ironed out. Plus when specifying the machine I got Intel chipset (not AMD), nVidia graphics (not ATI) and Intel wireless (not broadcom) - and think that's kept things hassle free - installing SP1 was no issue here.

Regarding the disk, I got Vista Business from Dell. I wiped the recovery partition as a matter of routine, and used the supplied recovery disk to install.  It turns out there was no crapware/ bloatware on there, just vanilla Vista Business (probably restricted to installing on Dell PCs though) - I bet that definitely helped performance-wise over some of the OEM pre-installs.

With a new PC (and that might be the point) Vista hasn't been a bad experience for me at all - I really like it.
