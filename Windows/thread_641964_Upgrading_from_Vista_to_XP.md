### Post by igknighted on 2007-12-18
This is precisely why people will never  adopt linux en mass.  Vista is in so many ways a drastic improvement on XP... it's just different.  You solve problems differently, you change settings differently... and people just don't seem to get it.  They do what worked in XP and it either does nothing or makes things worse, and instead of thinking "Hmm, perhaps I am going about this wrong", the first thought is "God this thing sucks".  We see that attitude here in the forums all the time with users switching to linux.  You really cannot deny that Vista is technically far superior.

As far as performance, well, you simply have to choose between pretty effects and speed and pick a level that runs well on your system.  I have three systems running Vista: a P4 3ghz, 2gb DDR ram (Vista Business) w/ RadeonHD 2600pro - this runs aero and is fairly quick; an Athlon64 X2 3600+ w/ 3gb ram (see sig #1) - this runs everything; and my laptop (see sig #2) which has a lot of stuff disabled at startup.  None of these computers are truely top of the line, but none have any issues with Vista.  It runs just as smooth and fast as XP, but I get all the security and UI improvements as well.  

If anything, I am starting to think that Vista might be terrible for linux.  This is because the people that really get it are on Vista with no issues, while the people who couldn't adapt and change are looking for alternatives, and these people will struggle with linux just as much and probably turn around and bash linux as hard as Vista.

The simple fact is Vista is a better OS.  It is a different OS, and many are simply not trying to adjust.  The problem with blog posts like this is they tend to discourage users that might have seen the bigger picture and adapted to what is, in reality, a major improvement.  Instead the status quo is idealized by those who are unwilling/unable to change and all of us suffer.  I'm certainly not saying Vista is perfect.  Like any new OS, it has it's bugs (and issues... the creative XFi issue is a major one for example).  But they are (slowly) getting fixed.  And I for one will deal with some minor bugs in exchange for a superior OS (thats why I am using Ubuntu, which is based on Debian "unstable", yes?).
