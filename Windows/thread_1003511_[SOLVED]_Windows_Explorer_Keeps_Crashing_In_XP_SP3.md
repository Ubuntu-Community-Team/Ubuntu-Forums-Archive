### Post by s.fox on 2008-12-12
Well after 3 hours at it,  I finally fixed it.  In case anyone is interested,  here is what I did:

1)  Discovered that Norton Anti-Virus had run out
2)  Installed AVG Anti-Virus
3)  Ran a scan on the whole computer
4)  Found 2 Trojans, a Worm and countless cookies.
5)  Removed all the items listed above
6)  Restarted the PC

This fixed Windows Explorer :p  

I was then told Internet Explorer was also playing up.  I had a look and discovered it wasn't loading any pages (I checked firefox and that works fine).  Initially I thought it was because of security settings,  but they looked okay.  I then stumbled across the fact that it was Internet Explorer 6.  I then downloaded Internet Explorer 7 and installed it.  One restart later and that too now works. :p
