### Post by Dragonbite on 2007-04-04
I've been a member of DACS ([_Danbury Area Computer Society_]("http://www.dacs.org")) and last night they had a demonstration on the new Vista and the new Office. 
:popcorn: 
The presenter was pretty good and really ... um... enthusiastic about Vista (meaning he drank the Corporate Cool-Aid).  I have no problems with him but can only imagine what he could do if he was talking about Linux or an Open Source Project!

Vista looked slick, but I had to laugh some.  Some of the technology he talked about I'm thinking "I've already got that on my P3 500MHz machine!". Some of the things he talked about included:

**[SIZE="4"]Sudo[/SIZE]**
The "do you want to run as administrator?" pop-ups.  Another person noted that it came up 8 times during his demonstration and he wasn't even installing software!  When somebody in the audience brought up #sudo he seemed to misinterprate it with #su and thinking that once you do that then everything is running as root.  

Ubuntu's use of #sudo, I find, to be a good balance and not overly intrusive.

**[SIZE="4"]Switch User[/SIZE]**
The ability to sign in as somebody else without having to sign-off who you are curently logged in as is already present in Dapper.  I've tried it on my system (which becomes highly destabilized, but 256MB Ram (the system's max) may have something to do with that ;)

I just don't recommend doing it on a P3 500MHz system (but note, it DOES work just not well). 

**[SIZE="4"]Aero's 3d scrolling through windows[/SIZE]**
I'm don't know much about Beryl and Compiz (*you know what I'm talking about*) but the scrolling through windows reminds me of Project LookingGlass and other than looking cool I don't see much use for it.

I find multiple desktops as being MUCH more handy than fancy window-list scrolling.

**[SIZE="4"]Raining Desktop[/SIZE]**
One feature that was cool (for the 45 minutes I got to see) was the raining and raindrops in his wallpaper.

**[SIZE="4"]Centralized Updates[/SIZE]**
Ha!  I think we can mostly agree that it doesn't come close to APT-GET repositories.

-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~-~
Those are all I can remember right now, but if I remember any more I'll be sure to add them to the list.

One thing to note, though, is his laptop had 2GB of Ram, though I don't know the clock speed or chip type but I don't imagine they're anything but something good!

At home, I don't even have 1GB of Ram between all of my systems!  I'll stick with Ubuntu.. something that works for me NOW and in the future.
