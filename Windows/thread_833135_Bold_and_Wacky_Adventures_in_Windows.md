### Post by ComputerGeek31618 on 2008-06-18
I realize that most of us are UbuntuHeads (thats a good thing), but since some of us (like me), are former Windowsheads, we need a place for our best/worst adventures.

Mine?  Here you go.

The Adventure:
Have you ever needed System Restore?

The Story:

I have an XFX GeForce 6200.  I use the nVidia custom settings, dual monitor, the whole fancy setup.  Everything was perfect, and the drivers were fine.  Until I decided to put my driver cd back in and poke around (on the menu, not in the files).  I had already installed all the game demos, had everything set up fine, but what here?  Some media cinema drivers for "your (whatever) cinema card".  Would these drivers be on *my*cd if they weren't for *my* card?

Skip back in time to the original driver install.  I had to do it three times because it wouldn't work correctly.  In the end, I think I let windows find the ones I needed and install them itself.

OK, back to the present.  I install these cinema-thingy drivers and restart.  Corrupt graphics, lots of trouble, and a big green blob with teeth waiting to eat my HDD.  Well, not the blob monster, but I certainly wasn't expecting this!  I fought through a few more install attempts, but to no avail.  So it occurs to me that not all the drivers on the *regular* card's driver cd are for a *regular* card.  And I fight two installs to get the proper drivers installed again.  And lo and behold, my computer works just fine, the end.

No.  I didn't have all my settings for the dual monitor, the transparency effects, the application-specific settings and all that stuff that I loved.  And rather than spend a mere half-hour reapplying tons of settings just the way I liked, I turned to system restore.  Well, it occured to me to actually set a restore point during this whole disaster, but that was after my original settings were destroyed.  I tracked back to the previous friday, restarted, and only had to reinstall three small applications that had been installed after the restore point had been set.

So, have I ever needed System Restore?  Not really, but it's darn handy when windows slaps you in the face!
