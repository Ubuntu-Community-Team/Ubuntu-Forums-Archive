### Post by Monsuco on 2007-12-07
I don't want to see MS destroyed. 
First off lets say MS were to vanish tomorrow:
If MS were to be destroyed the billions of windows users would not receive updates, the billions of hardware devices such as Pocket PCs, MTP devices, etc. would cease to work. People would not switch to Linux, which still isn't ready for everyone or OSX which isn't affordable to everyone.

If MS never existed, Linux, 386BSD, and other OS's never would have existed. If you think about it the three most opposite mindsets in the world created Linux: Richard Stallman, the idealist who wants everyone to use FOSS, Linus Torvalds, who feels that Linux should be about choice but constantly reminds users that all Linux is is software and not a revolution (Linus has frequently used closed source software and supports "Tivoization"), and Bill Gates, who wants to snuff out Linux but accedentally created the environment on which Linux thrives.

MS created DOS and Windows, which popularized the i386. It is true Linux runs on PPC, ARM, etc. but Linus Torvalds started writing Linux to learn about his 386 and Minix. He started out wanting to replace portions of Minix, but he gradually built onto it. When he accidentally "dialed" his HDD instead of his modem, he ate his Minix partition thus destroying the partition. He was left with the choice of either reinstalling Minix, or he could use what he had written of Linux and try to write whatever else he needed as he went along. He decided to do the second. It was originally described by him on Usenet as being unportable and locked to the 386. It was made portable later. Linus never would have bothered had he not wanted to learn about his 386.

Windows is also genuinely best in some cases. There are hundreds of Linux distros for all uses, but Windows is much more standardized. If you learned to use 95 you could easily still use Vista today. Your average office worker never has to learn something new. Gnome doesn't look close to how it did 5 years ago. KDE doesn't either. IT training cost cannot deal with that.

Then there is all the money MS gives to charity, the thousands of jobs MS created, etc.
