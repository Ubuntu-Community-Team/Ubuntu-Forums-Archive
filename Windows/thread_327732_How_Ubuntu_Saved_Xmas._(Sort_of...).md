### Post by natrixgli on 2006-12-29
Hi,

I was visiting my parents for the recent holiday, at which time my younger sisters received little inexpensive digital cameras. They came  with driver CD's for XP, which I installed. After several retries, and reboots I couldn't get the program to run, or windows to recognize the camera even as a removable drive... :( The bearer of these gifts was very annoyed at this point. 

So, remembering that my mom's computer has Dapper installed, I rebooted into Ubuntu and plugged the camera into the USB port... just for the heck of it. The "import photos" tool popped up, and allowed me to retrieve the (poor quality) pictures from the cameras without installing any drivers!

Oddly enough, My mom hasn't booted back into XP since. She saw my laptop with XGL/Beryl installed and now apparently I'm going over this weekend to upgrade to Edgy for her! 

Yay Ubuntu!
