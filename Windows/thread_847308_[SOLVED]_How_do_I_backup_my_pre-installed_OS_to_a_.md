### Post by abgemacht on 2008-07-03
I'm not very familiar with Vista, but from what I was reading there doesn't seem to be any easy way to do this.

There are some features built into some versions of Vista that have this capability, but Home Basic doesn't seem to be one of them.

Here are a couple things I would suggest:

Check to see if you have a backup partition.  Some laptops have a separate partition with the OS and recovery utilities.

Ask your manufacturer to ship you the disks.  Unless things have changed, you should be able to request (or demand, depending on their customer service) a restore CD.  You may have to pay for shipping, though :/

You could also get some backup software such as Norton Ghost (although there are probably better open-source versions), but I don't think you'd have the option to back up just the OS.

### Post by fiddledd on 2008-07-03
If your Laptop is like others with Vista (including mine) you should have the option to burn Recovery DVDs. It's usually provided by the OEM so it will be under ACER in the Programs Menu or on the Desktop. This will restore your Laptop to Factory Settings (as you received it).

### Post by fiddledd on 2008-07-03
Just thought, maybe explaining what I did before I dual booted Vista with Linux Mint might help you:

First I burnt the Recovery DVDs (just in case)

Then I imaged my Hard Drive to a usb Hard Drive using    Acronis True Image (You could use Clonezilla it's free and OSS [http://www.clonezilla.org/](http://www.clonezilla.org/))

Then I changed my mind and restored from the image on the external drive and got back to exactly how I was.

Then I changed my mind again, but that's another story :)

BTW, I have a Recovery Partition also but didn't trust it to restore the Laptop to it's original state in the event of needing to return it for repair etc.
