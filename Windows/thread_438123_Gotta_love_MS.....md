### Post by Sunflower1970 on 2007-05-09
I don't know whether to laugh or cry. 

For the past few months, we've been testing a new site for our customers to place orders with us.  We worked out the bugs in the testing environment. It was working (almost) perfectly. The new site even worked in Linux, when the old site worked **only** with Windows (It wouldn't even work with a Mac!). No major server problems, database problems, only two minor problems: one server that they had to remove and buy a new one, and one that keeps resetting itself. It's still being used, but I'm betting they'll have to buy a new one to replace this one as well. These servers are here locally and I believe they run Linux.

But, there's a whole host of servers at our parent company in a different state which hosts the site. They Windows based servers. Sunday site was made live....and the problems have not stopped rolling in. Corrupted databases. Servers crashing. Customers unable to place ads (I get the brunt of the angry calls. Sooo much fun!) None of these problems raised their ugly heads before. All of a sudden the problems won't stop. 

We all knew there would be some kinks, but this has become ridiculous.

*sigh* Back to the trenches to field the angry emails and calls....
