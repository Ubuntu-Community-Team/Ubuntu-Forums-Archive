### Post by samadams on 2007-04-24
Sorry, the printer has won this round.  The drum is also not cheap.  Your printer was telling you that you need to change it or it will shortly stop working, usually when the light goes from blinking to solid it will cease all together.  This has nothing to do with anything but the printer itself. (a drum has a finite life span - it is what electrostatically charges the ink onto the particular spots on the page  which is then heat sealed onto the paper - without the drum, the printer can't tell the ink where to go or to stay there)  Unfortunately a drum regardless of the printer usually cost almost (like 20-50 bucks less) as much as a new printer.  If you like Brother - I've just purchased two 5270DN's (for networking) that work great for about $250 each.  They have cheaper ones and the 5250 is sans networking at about $100 less.  (you can still use it with other computers on the network, but it will be slower, require a little set up and it requires the local PC to be on for it to work) In comparison, the drum will likely run you about $200 and you'll probably have to order it directly from brother if they still have it in stock since the 2070 is an older model.  Best of luck.

P.S. you didn't break anything - but you failed to read the printer manual which would have alerted you to tell your boss he'd need a new printer soon.  But in the whole scheme of things, you could not have saved the printer or the drum, they are just built that way - this was inevitable nothing you could have done (that I am aware of) would prevent a drum from going bad in normal use. Hint, the drum is the thing that comes out when you try to change the toner.  (It 'holds' the toner cartridge - sometimes it stays in the printer but with most Brother models they come out together)  You should NEVER touch the drum roller with your finger or anything else. (you really have to try to on purpose)
