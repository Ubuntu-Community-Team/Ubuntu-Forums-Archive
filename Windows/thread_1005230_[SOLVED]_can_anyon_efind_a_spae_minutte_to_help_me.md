### Post by smoker on 2008-12-08
hmm, in windows, a quick fix is sometimes rare! i don't know a great deal about wireless in windows, but if the wireless card is showing up in device manager, and the drivers are installed ok, then check whether the windows 'wireless zero config service' is enabled in 'services' you can check the 'services' through 'admin tools' in the 'control panel' (going from memory!). some laptops also require the wireless to be turned on either by a switch, or a key combination.

there is also maybe a possibility that the wireless card is faulty, you could maybe try testing with a live-cd and see if it is picked up ok then.
