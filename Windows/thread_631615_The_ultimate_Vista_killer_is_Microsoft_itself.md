### Post by cipher_nemo on 2007-12-04
This was originally written to an audience of Windows users, so please excuse my layman-speak. I don't want to insult anyone's intelligence by spelling out what "distro" means. :)

Enjoy...

If you're a Linux fan, you're probably thinking that Ubuntu with its built-in Open Office applications and optional Compiz Fusion software is the real Vista killer. You'd correct in that assessment just at face value, but the real threat is Microsoft itself. Because of this, Microsoft will one day be downgraded to coding nothing more than a Linux/BSD distribution (distro). Before you flame me over my prophecy, consider the following.

First, Microsoft recently demonstrated its ability to run a Linux operating system inside Windows to companies and other industries within Europe. Second, Microsoft has been shooting its blanks at Linux regarding patent infringement since May of 2007. Only recently has Red Hat trumped customer concerns by stating that customers are safe, and even if legal issues arise, Red Hat will replace whatever code is found to be infringement in the courts. A world-wide community with the free exchange of open-source software cannot possibly be "taken" into court. Microsoft is merely performing this scare tactic to limit corporate, educational, and government adoption of Linux. Microsoft is scared, so it lashes out in futile attempts to defend its monopoly when cornered.

Third, Microsoft is playing hard-ball with Linux supporters by pulling out all of their guns. From their "free" Visual Studio Express" ([source]("http://www.microsoft-watch.com/content/operating_systems/virtual_pc_and_the_value_of_free.html")) to their more recent $3 weapon to sell Student Innovation Suite to students throughout "emerging" countries ([source]("http://www.desktoplinux.com/news/NS7642674933.html")), Microsoft is spending mucho deniro on campaigns aimed at toppling free, open-source software. But why spend so much on a competitor that does generate a profit?

It's all about future generations and what they will grow-up using on their PCs. Microsoft is nearing panic mode as more and more ground is lost in Europe to government agencies and corporations that are dumping Microsoft and switching to Linux. Microsoft is releasing their own studies, their own campaigns, and literally bribing others to keep using Windows. What's worse, they're bribing the media to keep their public image on the up-and-up ([source]("http://tech.blorge.com/Structure:%20/2006/12/27/microsoft-crosses-the-line-as-it-bribes-bloggers-with-vista-notebooks/"), [source]("http://www.whatsnextblog.com/archives/2006/12/edelman_doesnt_give_a_crap_what_you_think_about_their_ethics.asp"), [similar topic]("http://ubuntuforums.org/href=%27http://www.gamersquad.com/category/PlayStation-3/Sony-Microsoft-bribing-publishers-for-exclusive-content/")).

And of course, the bloated beast named Vista is sure to die among power users. Hardware incompatibilities, monopolistic pressures such as DirectX 10, the "Games for Windows" campaign, and the unnecessary hardware requirements just to run Vista without lag. Why recommend 2GB of RAM for a PC to do a little fancy desktop eye-candy, when a PC using Ubuntu and Compiz can do even better eye-candy with less, at just 512MB of RAM recommended? As for DirectX 10, allow me to let you in on a little secret: **IT WORKS IN WINDOWS XP!** Yes, you heard me correctly. DirectX 10 will work on Windows XP without any tweaking, yet Microsoft has purposefully neutered it from running on anything but Vista. Originally, DirectX 10 wouldn't work on XP due to memory virtualization issues. When Microsoft removed this requirement to bail NVIDIA out of reworking its drivers, they conveniently kept the monopolistic lock in place.

Microsoft wants you to use Vista. They don't care if you didn't pay them directly, so long as it was pre-installed on your new PC, and so long as you get used to it and treat it as the "norm" for computing. This is only a shallow victory by default. If Microsoft did nothing else then just release their new O/S and update it, the next operating system beyond Vista might have a chance. However, they have to muddy their own public image with bribes, legal threats, and brute force tactics. Remember when the tobacco companies' advertising campaigns were derailed when advocates screamed that it was wrong to market to children? Well, Microsoft is repeating history by marketing "Windows" to students of all ages. But using Microsoft isn't bad for your health and those around you (or is it?). Simply put, Microsoft is killing themselves by launching campaigns that use 20th century marketing strategies.

The more I play with Linux, specifically with the ubuntu distro, the more I realize how absolutely pointless it is to continue using Microsoft Windows. Why fork over an ever increasing sum of money to use sub-par software, with overly-crippling licensing, incompatibilities, and downgraded performance when something like Ubuntu is just a download away? Open Office will open your Microsoft Office documents, Firefox is a far better browser than IE, and Ubuntu won't make you to purchase another license when you decide to upgrade your PC.

The choice is clear. Getting there is the real challenge. The more people get to know a Linux distro such as Ubuntu, the more people will switch once they realize that Ubuntu is easier and more powerful to use compared to Vista.
