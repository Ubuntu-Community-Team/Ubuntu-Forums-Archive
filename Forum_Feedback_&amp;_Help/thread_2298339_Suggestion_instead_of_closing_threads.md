### Post by kumoshk on 2015-10-10
Suggestion:

Instead of closing threads routinely, why don't we just make it so commenting doesn't bump them, and make it so comments (only on old, unbumpable threads) have to be approved? That way, we can still comment and provide useful insight to those who are following the thread and interested in it, as well as those who find it via search engine. Because the posts wouldn't be bumped, administrators wouldn't be overwhelmed with new comments from new people all the time who act like the thread is new. Plus, it wouldn't annoy regular users looking for new and active posts. Because comments on old posts would require moderator approval (FYI, moderators would be notified), the lack of bumping wouldn't allow spam to make its way through easily.

I'm not suggesting to remove the ability to close threads, though (just to replace the routine aspect of it with something that hopefully would be more useful).

### Post by kumoshk on 2015-10-10
Anyway, in my experience with forums (I've used forums a lot, and run some), this scenario shouldn't provide lots of work for moderators, because most of the new posts in situations without closed threads come as a result of the action spurred by bumping threads (rather than the bumping itself and replies from people already following the thread). However, moderators who weren't following the thread may chime in and create more work for themselves.
