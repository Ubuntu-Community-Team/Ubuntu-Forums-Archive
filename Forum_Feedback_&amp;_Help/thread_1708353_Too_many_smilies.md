### Post by srs5694 on 2011-03-16
Can something be done about the forum software's overexuberance in turning certain strings into smilies? Far too often, parenthetical expressions get turned into smilies, which obfuscates their intended meaning. For instance:


[list=1]
[*]"Enter the sector number (such as [noparse]1128)[/noparse] in the dialog..." becomes "Enter the sector number (such as 1128) in the dialog..."
[*]"...access your Windows partition [noparse](C:)[/noparse] from Linux..." becomes "...access your Windows partition (C:) from Linux..."
[/list]


The first example could create a dangerous error, should the number be intended for literal interpretation by a reader. Some other inappropriate smilies, like the second example, are less likely to cause confusion or error, but they're annoying nonetheless.

These problems can be fixed by the poster by using [noparse][noparse][/noparse] and [noparse][/noparse][/noparse] tags, but that's inconvenient and it's easy to overlook the text that's at risk of smilie-fication.

Restricting smilie interpretation to just strings that are surrounded by spaces would help, although it wouldn't cure the problem entirely -- change "28" to "8" in my first example and you'd still get an improper smilie. Reducing the number of smilies might also help -- I count 75 of them, and I'm skeptical that even half of them are really helpful. Another possibility would be to default to no smilie translation but to enable it if the poster clicks a smilie in the "Smilies" box or selects an option to explicitly enable them. I don't know if any of these is a real possibility in the forum software, though.
