### Post by kko1 on 2008-07-19
Hello.

I'd say don't give up on the idea just yet. The problem of threads receiving *answers* yet remaining un*solved* is a real one, and I do believe it could be mitigated. An option to mark a thread **UNSOLVED** would, in my opinion, have more positive than negative impact towards the fluent functionings of the forum.

I wrote about the same thing three months ago [_here_]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=763974"), but as of current, it hasn't yet been implemented. (As a curious paradox, I would mark the thread UNSOLVED, but since the feature I proposed to do that doesn't exist, I can't!)

What *John.Michael.Kane* wrote seems, to me, to confirm my thoughts.
> **John.Michael.Kane said:**
> In the case of threads being answered, yet the answer does not resolve the issue...

One would have to look at the information provided by the OP, as well as the answer provided, and any follow up by the OP.

Then there is the possibility that the error, or issue cannot be reproduce by the forum members helping, as well as the fact the issue might be obscure.

There is also cases in which the OP might have figured out another fix after a member posted their answer, and did not follow up with a update

For the first case, in a thread marked as UNSOLVED, you'd have at least some indication (visible at a glance) that the OP has already evaluated the information provided. Also for the second. It would give the OP more responsibility (*and better means!*) for following up with their thread, which, IMO, would be a good thing.

For the last case, if the OP finds a solution and **fails** to remove the marking of a thread as UNSOLVED:
- I do think it's better to put, also here, the *responsibility on the OP* to follow-up with their thread with correct markings (if they've bothered to mark their thread UNSOLVED in the first place), *rather than encouraging the current situation* where (with no markings) the poster can freely leave their thread to abandon (*thus misleading others* reading the thread), and where this sort of behaviour is accepted at face value as "the norm".*

Sincerely,

kko1

*) I do know that you can't derive *ought to* from *is*, but people tend to see a common practice as "the thing to do", because everyone else is doing it as well.
