### Post by aysiu on 2006-08-01
"Shutting down" is not the same as "calming down." I was a moderator in real life, too, once, and we *did* receive training on how to moderate. Moderating never meant shutting people up or not allowing the people you disagree with to speak. I was moderating for a pretty controversial topic, too (sexual assault, particularly date/acquaintance rape). 

If you're a *good* moderator, you try your best to redirect the conversation to the topic at hand, encourage people to focus on the contents of their arguments and not express things angrily, and give equal time to various points of view. Now, I realize translating that skill to an online forum is difficult, but closing a thread is not "calming things down." Closing is or at least should be a last resort... always. If one person's causing trouble, you PM that person to take a break from the thread and come back when she's calmed down. If two people are having it at each other, excise their posts and make them their own thread in the backyard. Let the civil people discuss in peace. Stopping *all* conversation about a topic isn't making it calm.

I made a suggestion back in [this thread](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=218357): > I would propose that there be a standard procedure for threads that get out of hand, and that if special circumstances call for a deviation from the standard procedure, then the moderators discuss what would be the best course of action.

If a thread gets out of hand...

1. Send private messages to the offending parties asking them to cool it.
2. If the messages aren't heeded, cut the offending posts out of the thread and move those posts to the Jail or the Backyard
3. If the thread isn't as volatile but still seems too heated for the Cafe, move the entire thread to the Backyard
4. If the thread is redundant, merge it with the other similar thread
5. If the bulk of the thread (not just two forum members arguing back and forth and flaming one another--but several forum members generally kicking up ill will) is counterproductive, put in a public warning to have people cool it. Afterwards, close the thread if the warning isn't heeded.

Note, in my proposal, it takes a very extreme situation to close a thread. No one has to adopt this, but it is a proposal, and I'm putting it out there.
