### Post by coffeecat on 2014-07-28
> **bulrush2 said:**
> I realize I can't change my settings because I have less than 10 posts.

You're almost there! :)

> **bulrush2 said:**
> How can I see how many posts I have towards the 10 required posts? 

Your "bean" count is in your profile in all your posts, or under "about me" in your profile page - click on your name top-right of any page to get to your profile page. "Beans" are posts that count towards your total post count. There are four main sections of the forum - to see what I mean have a look at the home page. Posts in most/all of the sub-forums of the first, second and fourth main sections count towards your post count. Posts in most of the sub-forums in the third section don't. 

I have a suggestion. When you get to a post count of 10 and you can edit your profile settings, try "Through my control panel only" for default thread subscription mode instead of one of the email notification settings. You won't get emails - obviously - but if you log in from time to time and then click on the settings link at top-right, all subscribed threads with new posts will be listed as hyperlinks for you. 

> **bulrush2 said:**
> How do I see only threads I have created, not replies? Since I'm not sure I'm getting email notices at this point. This will help me avoid dupe posts here since I'm on 2 Linux forums right now. 

Again from your profile page - on the left: find latest posts, or find latest started threads. Or from Quick Links in the navbar: Find all my threads; Find all my posts.

By the way, the cron that adjusts permissions on accounts that reach a post-count of 10 runs every hour, and only on logged in accounts. You may have to wait up to an hour - and stay logged in!
