### Post by Brandel Valico on 2011-08-15
I have noticed a trend in the Forums here that while not a bad thing exactly does kind of make me scratch my head and wonder if it's a good thing.


When I first came here I asked a few questions. I had to ask only a few because 90% of what I would have asked had all ready been asked and **actually** answered. Now notice the bold word. It's bold for a reason. It seems now that when someone asks a question, instead of getting an answer (With a warning at times if the idea was a bad one. Explaining why it was a bad idea. Ex... How do I give myself permanent root access at log in.)

It seems more and more instead of just answering the question asked the forum has become a **Instead of answering or actually trying to help find an answer its just a warning or a comment about how dumb the idea is.**

I for myself preferred the older style of the forum responses. So just curious if anyone else has noticed this trend in the responses from posters as well? 

Now this is just me being curious if anyone else has noticed the change in atmosphere around the forums. This isn't me down talking the forums or the people here. It's simply noting a change in how responses have changed from direct and helpful with an explanation of why you may not want to do it. To Explanation why you may not want to do it and if your lucky an actual method to do it.
