### Post by slimdog360 on 2007-05-18
When a new account is made, make it such that the first 5 (or whatever) posts that the account makes, the user must go through a type of verification first. This is a bit hard to explain exactly what I mean so bare with me for a second. 

You all know when you sign up to various websites they sometimes make you first write down a random set of letters/numbers which are displayed in a warped type picture. Such that if a bot tries to set up a new account it can't read the letters/numbers in the picture and thus fails the verification process.

If this type of thing was implemented in the first 5-10 posts that a user makes this could stop a real person from signing up to the forums, then setting up some bot to spam the place. Or someother combination of things. Just have the picture and text box to enter the letters/numbers come up on a new page after the post is made. This separates the complexity of the whole system.

Obviously if an account was to fail the verification process a certain number of times their account could be automatically frozen and an email is sent to the person for them to reactivate the account, which they will do if they are a real person. Also a message could be sent to an admin for them to look into things if need be.

Of course this couldn't be implemented overnight but perhaps just something to keep in mind for the future. Let me know if you can't understand exactly what I'm talking about and I'll try to clear things up.

I thought that would be OK because my blog is pertinent to Ubuntu a lot of the time, and I want there to be an archive of my posts I have control over. It was never intended as a  promotional gimmick, but some of the very first responses were clearly hostile. After I got to know a few people in that forum, they explained, and we've since become friends, but it sure got off to a rocky start. Since then I've just been cutting and pasting from my blog instead so people don't get the wrong idea. If there were such a program in place such as yours, it certainly would have been helpful to me as a newbie on the Ubuntu Forums. Since I did not require much in the way of help when I started using Ubuntu, my first posts were in areas intended to be somewhat provocative to start lively discussion. It got lively, but in an unintended manner mostly due to my ignorance. Some respondents leaped to conclusions, one even believing I was a teenager just looking for attention, when I am in reality a 55 year old grandfather. Obviously you idea would have helped in yet another way besides what you outlined in your post.
