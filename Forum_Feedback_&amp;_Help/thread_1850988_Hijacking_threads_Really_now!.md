### Post by drs305 on 2011-09-27
Threads started in a support forum 'belong' to the original poster. Posts should be directed to solve the issues presented by this person. If the situation is exactly the same as your problem, then merely reading the responses to the original post should help you as well, without you needing to post in the same thread. 

If you feel the need to post, then your information or issue is not 'exactly' the same as the OP's and you should open your own thread.This will allow you to receive personalized attention. You can always provide a link saying that your post is similar to thread X and then provide your specific details.

Posting in a SOLVED thread is less likely to elicit a response from the helpers; they often have moved on to users still having problems and won't read SOLVED posts.

While there are certain 'megathreads' meant to allow multiple users to post their problems, in general the one poster/one problem idea provides clarity and avoids confusion. 

While the maintainers of these forums are concerned with the amount of server space used, it should not be a concern of the forum user when it comes to following established practice. The difference in space used by an additional post vs a new thread is probably minimal.

If you have a specific complaint about a 'hijacking' event, the proper place to take this up is the Resolution Center.
