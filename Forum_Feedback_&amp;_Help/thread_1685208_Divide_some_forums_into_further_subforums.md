### Post by *uu on 2011-02-10
Hey there,

recently, when I opened a thread in the "Desktop Environments" or "General Help" forums, I got no replies at all.

Seeing that, with recent increases in Ubuntu users count, especially in these forums there are several new threads every minute (many of which might be considered newbie questions, and most of them are probably repeats), my threads disappeared from the first page in these forums, as well as from the "New Posts" list, within five minutes. Within the hour, they weren't even on the first pages when searching for unread threads.

I consider my issues not to be or have been simple everyday issues, but rather something that barely ever anyone comes across, and therefore guess that the first ones who might have seen the threads indeed couldn't help. But I assume that most of those who could have helped did never even see the threads due to the vast amounts of new threads every day. And I'm also convinced that I'm not the only one not getting replies because of the sheer user and thread count that we have by now.

Sure, I could have bumped the threads, but then they would have disappeared from the top of all lists as well within minutes, and they would not be found as unanswered threads anymore.

So, given the circumstances, wouldn't it be useful or even necessary to further divide those forums into subforums? I have no idea as to what categories would be appropriate, maybe something like "Everyday issues" and "Exotic issues" -- though, that would be the point again that new users might not be able to distinguish (which is one reason why the "Desktop Environments" forum currently is **not divided**).

Or what could one do to be noticed by the more experienced users?

Until now, I resorted to translating my posts to German and posting them on the ubuntuusers.de forums, and in the future I'll probably start there directly, hence omitting this forum. But should a solution be found there, it wouldn't help international users here, which I consider somewhat less than perfect, either.

Any ideas or suggestions on this? :popcorn:

### Post by forrestcupp on 2011-02-10
It's a catch 22. Where there are less people posting problems, there are also less people posting answers. 

If you have specialized subforums to the extreme, then odds are there won't be much traffic. Even if someone might know the answer to your question, there's a good chance they won't be hanging out in that particular specialized subforum.

Just be glad it's separated out now more than it was in the early days.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-02-10
I can't and won't speak for others. However, when searching the helps  sections I use an advanced query with predefined variables. For  examples, searching for all threads with zero responses in certain  sections, etc.

I have serious doubts that modifying the forums to include more detailed  sub-sections will resolve your issue. The actual problem may have more  to do with the individual expertise of those monitoring the help  sections (at the time of your thread).

Not everything can be resolved by altering forum design. I understand  your frustration, but in all likelihood it will only  result in increased confusion. Should the forums add additional sub-sections,  members may become confused as to which section their request best  fits.

We must keep in mind that responses to help requests are voluntary and  as such are directly dependent on availability and knowledge base. If  your having better success with the  "ubuntuusers.de forums" then logic  might suggest.... It's my opinion, that we should utilize all resources when searching for solutions. Not just rely on any one source. 

As for getting noticed by experienced users - your 2 "general help" and 1  "desktop environments" requests were viewed by a total of over 400  members.

Just my $0.02
