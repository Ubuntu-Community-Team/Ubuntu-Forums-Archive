### Post by col48 on 2013-05-11
I observe that if I RE-edit a post of mine but do not give a reason for this second edit, the post subsequently says it was edited at the time of the REedit but gives the reason as that of the first edit.
There is no indication that it has been edited more than once.

Is this what was intended?

### Post by coffeecat on 2013-05-11
> **col48 said:**
> Is this what was intended?

Probably, although to be honest I'd not thought about it much until you asked just now. It's a design feature of the Vbulletin software that the forum runs on, and was the same with the Vbulletin version 3 that we were running before the upgrade in February. The reason for the "reason for edit" field staying the same is that it is automatically filled in for you with the previous entry if you do a subsequent edit. This is a useful default for those who have to repeatedly edit tutorials, for example, or staff updating stickies. You can always edit the reason field when you do a re-edit of the post.

As far as an indication for how many times a post has been edited, there is one, but not publicly visible. If you hover your mouse over "last edited by" in one of your own edited posts, you should see a link which leads to a full edit history. But only your own posts. The exceptions are forum admins and moderators who can view the edit history of anyone's posts.
