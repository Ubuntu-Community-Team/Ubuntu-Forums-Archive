### Post by jwbrase on 2012-08-13
I have observed a case on the forums where two threads were started and a post was made in the second thread that was nothing more than a link to the first thread. The threads were subsequently merged, with the result that the merged thread contains a [post]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=12170172&postcount=40") that does nothing but link to the first page of the thread.

So what's the best way to bring it to the attention of a moderator? I could use the "report abuse" button, but it's not really abuse, simply a post that became useless when the threads were merged. I could also mention it in the thread itself, but a moderator might or might not visit the thread again.

### Post by oldfred on 2012-08-14
You can use the report abuse for anything that requires moderation. It does not have to be abuse, but just explain the issue.

But we usually do not delete threads, just for posterity. Unless spam or something that is against Forum Policy and then we usually move them to the jail. There have been forum hiccups where duplicates get posted, then we may delete if we run across them, but usually are too busy with spam to worry about those threads.
