### Post by vidwarren on 2011-02-24
**An idea, well open to suggestion:**

Could we allow *any* poster in a thread to mark their problem as [SOLVED] with a 'post-reference'?

I.e. If you find a solution to your problem in the 27th post of an existing thread, you tick a box *in that post* saying 'This solved it for me', which takes you to a 'quick reply'.

This  would then allow us to include a bar-chart in the thread, showing the  posts that have provided a solution (against the number of people that  have ticked 'This solved it for me' on each post).

**Possible further  developments:**

The  bar charts could be filterable by Distro/hardware/etc. the Distro could  be automatically included from the users' account information. The  users could be prompted for any other information, such as hardware,  when they reply.

So, when I find a large thread about a problem  I'm having, I check the bar-chart, look through a drop-down menu to see  if I can find 'Ubuntu 10.10', look through a second drop-down menu to  see if I can find 'Samsung NC10 Plus' and then hit filter and choose the  posts that have worked for others on the same setup.

---

This  could make searching through a thread to find a solution quicker and  easier. Would it be possible to implement? Perhaps by modifying  vBulletin's 'Poll' feature?

Cheers,

Vid
