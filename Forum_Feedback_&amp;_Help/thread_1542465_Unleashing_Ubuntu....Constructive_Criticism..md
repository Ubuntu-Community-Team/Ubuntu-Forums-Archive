### Post by Nick_Jinn on 2010-08-08
When it comes to teaching people to use the command line, how about instead of talking 'about' the command line, maybe give some exercises. Walk people through a few examples. Make sure they have experienced typing in the command line before they go on....sometimes when you read something its in one ear and out the other, but when you experience it then it sticks a little better.


Another thing.....you gave some examples of what it should 'look like' when you type in certain things, and you posted these walls of code. While its not a huge deal, I think a lot of people might be intimidated by the site of it and it didnt really teach anything tangible by posting it....maybe include a photo of all that data somewhere but I would go easy on posting big walls output data unless its something you actually expect someone to memorize. It makes it look cluttered and intimidating and we dont need to memorize those walls of code.
