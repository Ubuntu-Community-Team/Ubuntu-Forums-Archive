Marking a thread as 'solved' also has the advantage that a prospective solver does not have to needlessly follow threads of any length just to perceive that help is not needed any more.

> **kevdog said:**
> Can I mark this thread as solved since I'm done reading it?

No, you can't, as you are not the OP (which is often perceived to be the owner of a thread). You can ignore the thread, but in that case you won't see new posts, either.

I suggest to do nothing at all. The thread is marked for you as 'read' as soon as you've read it. As soon as someone posts to the thread, it will become marked for you as 'unread' again.

### Post by NilsE on 2008-07-16
I like threads that are marked "Solved" for a couple of reasons.

1) If it is marked solved and I am not interested in the solution and I am not having the problem I can ignore it.

2) If it is marked solved and I have the same problem I know there is a good chance the fix is in there.

3) I even like it more when it is marked solved and the person that provided the fix is also thanked by the person(s) with the problem.  Give more credence to to solution.
