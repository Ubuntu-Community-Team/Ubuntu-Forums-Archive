### Post by QIII on 2019-10-20
I'd trust TheFu with no reservations.  That is the sort of respect one builds over years of excellent support.  But note that TheFu directs people to things that are specific answers to questions asked.  

Answers to questions not asked are of little value unless they are good tutorials for common tasks ... and for those I recommend reading our [guidelines]("https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2143602") very carefully.

Tutorials like [this]("https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2426633") are exemplary.

A list of scripts various and sundry would not make for an acceptable tutorial.  Don't do it.

If you read the tutorial guidelines, you will find that an attempted tutorial that is little more than a series of links to other sites will not be accepted.  Don't do it.

If you post for 9 or 10 years and have a personal blog that is not specifically commercial in nature, then you can refer to specific articles that directly answer a question.

If Phoronix has an article about a specific question, you can refer to that if it directly answers a question.

If Arch's forums have an answer to a particularly knotty issue, you can refer to that if it answers a question.

If a site that uses advertising to support itself has an answer to a question and if you do not stand to profit from the reference, you may link to that if it answers a question.  The staff may remove the post if it is questionable, however.
