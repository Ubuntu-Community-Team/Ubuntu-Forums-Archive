### Post by &#32 Greg on 2009-03-02
Since this is Forum Feedback & Help, I decided to give a little bit of the former.

I understand the logic behind closing it. UF is a very large forum with a significant member base. There are a large number of forums which can get confusing. Other OS Talk was pretty autonomous from the rest of the forums, and also didn't really fit in, espescially if you want the forums to become more Ubuntu oriented. Still, Other OS Talk belongs for a few reasons.

First off, its isolation from the rest of the forums is part of the reason why removing it is bad- you're destroying a subculture within the forums. That piece of the userbase is still hanging in there, though, with posts in Community Cafe, which gets messier from all the Linux questions that land in it.

The biggest reason, however, that removing Other OS Talk was a mistake is that Ubuntu has come to represent the general (Desktop) linux contingency. As representative of the entire linux community, it needs an area to talk about other OS's besides Ubuntu. Look at all the projects people announce here, that are available for download by attatchment. The Ubuntu Forums became more than a forum for just Ubuntu. And Ubuntu became pretty much a starting point in people's travels as a linux user. By closing Other OS Talk, there's not nearly as much here for those who moved on, and not as much for those looking to go to something more advanced.

Except Arch. There are still about 10 active threads on Arch.
