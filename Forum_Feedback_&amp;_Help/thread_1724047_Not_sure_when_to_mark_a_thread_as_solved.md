### Post by ClientAlive on 2011-04-07
I know the title sounds pretty ignorant. Of course I know when to mark a thread as solved when it was began as a technical question and the problem has been solved. What I am wondering about is this:

I'm kinda new to using a forum and I want to show respect to everyone here. However, I started a thread not too long ago that was more along the lines of a topic of discussion than a problem; and, after a while, it seemed to me just about all the possible positions had been stated by someone or another.

It got to a point where it seems things would begin to get redundant if they continued. As of this moment, I have not marked the thread as solved (because I'm not sure it is appropriate given the topic) and I have not visited it or responded in a couple days. I feel kinda stuck. Don't want to disrespect but it seems either way I go (whether I quit visiting it or whether I mark it at solved and quit visiting it) that is how things may appear.

Any suggestions?

Jake

### Post by cariboo on 2011-04-07
I would suggest if you get a satisfactory answer in the thread, that you should mark it solved, especially if it is a technical question. As far as a general discussion thread is concerned, whether you want to mark it solved, it's up to you. Usually there will be a few people posting after you've done it, but eventually it will die off. Again whether you want ot post in the thread, it's up to you.

### Post by Simian Man on 2011-04-08
I don't really like the concept of marking threads as solved in general to be honest with you.  Often times there are many different solutions to a problem and the first one given may not be the best one (it may even be a horrible solution).  Marking a thread as solved tends to discourage further discussion of topics that may not be as cut and dried as the OP realizes.

This is especially true in programming forums.
