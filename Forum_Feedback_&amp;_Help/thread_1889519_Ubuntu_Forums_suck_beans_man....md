Just wanted to respond to this. We appreciate the downside of the changes you mention, but, they are the lesser of two evils.

In terms of spam, yes these forums are *that* busy. We have had enough problems with enough spam we had to implement these changes. I would point out that before these changes we were receiving the opposite complaint, too much spam.

In terms of having to register an account to see posts / threads in the archives, the other option was to delete the archives. There was much discussion on what to do here, and it involved the community and mailing lists. Since you only have one bean my guess is you are a "lurker" and have not been active in the community and thus are at at huge disadvantage in understanding why these changes were made. Why the pressure to "delete" the archives ? Because it is a mixed bag. No question there is a lot of outstanding threads / posts in the archives, but there is a lot of bad information as well, and this bad information has caused support problem with the kernel team in particular.

The real solution, IMO, is to ask the community to contribute more to the wiki. Forums are a better interactive question  / answer format, wiki is better for information. With a community this size and this knowledgeable we should be able to rival even the Arch wiki.

We just need a paradigm shift from using the forums for documentation to using the wiki. We also need a method of keeping information current. Personally I like the way Fedora tracks documentation, by fedora release.

[http://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/index.html](http://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/index.html)

I see a lot of complaints about the wiki this and the wiki that - I remind you all it is a community project and rather then complain, please become a contributor.

My suggestion to you would be to become more active in the community and continue to put forward positive suggestions.
