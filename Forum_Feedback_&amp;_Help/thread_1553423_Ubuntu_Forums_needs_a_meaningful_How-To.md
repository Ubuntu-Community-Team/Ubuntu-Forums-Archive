### Post by off-ui on 2010-08-15
Ubuntu Forums has a major structural problem -- its design and content are extremely hostile to anyone who isn't already familiar not only with community forums in general, but in this forum system's software in particular.

I'm a computer veteran of 30+ years, and I've spent half that time interacting with just about every kind of web design. This system's capabilities are impressive, but they are NOT intuitive. Something as basic as "how to use the forum software" is almost completely missing, save three links under the "Forum Help" heading:
[LIST]
[*] "Ubuntu Forums Information" (linked from "About Ubuntu Forums" -- immediately we're into confusing inconsistencies): a nice hello message, but containing nothing an newbie needs to know.
[*] "Ubuntu Forums Code of Conduct": provides no real information on actually using the system, just on how to behave while doing so (an unfortunately necessary document in our raucous Internet society).
[*] A link called "Forum FAQ" which leads to nothing of the sort, but rather "Announcements in Forum : Forum Feedback & Help", which doesn't include any feedback, only two extremely long (and long-term) posts. An FAQ is supposed to be a list of "frequently asked questions" with specific answers. There is an incomplete one of these buried way down after much screen scrolling, after "ATTENTION ALL USERS: Malicious Commands", which is NOT an FAQ and merely confuses anyone looking for one. If someone does scroll down to the second, "Ubuntu Forums FAQ" (what was advertised by the link), one gets mostly housekeeping and community interaction info, not, **for example, how to use tags and prefixes in searches**, which should be *easily found in something calling itself "Forum Help"*.
[/LIST]
Most of what I found within the forums themselves dealt with Ubuntu information, not forum how-tos: Absolute Beginner Talk ("The perfect starting place to find out more about computers, Linux and Ubuntu", not forums); General Help ("All your general support questions for Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Edubuntu and Xubuntu", not forums); Tutorials & Tips ("The place to find Ubuntu related Tips & Tricks", not... well, you get the picture). I do seem to recall seeing something about BBCode usage somewhere, but even with 8 separate browser tabs currently devoted to researching where I might find a simple answer to obvious forum-use questions, I just can't find or recall it now (nor do I see it in any of the pull-down headings or in the main page's list of topics and sub-topics).

All this verbiage is to point out that the community seems to be assuming a lot of pre-existing knowledge about how to use a forum website like this. If this is a barrier to an old computer hand like myself, there's no way I'd ever suggest my computer-inexperienced family members to come here for answers. I can't even figure out efficient ways to pose the questions for searching! Yes, I could spend half an hour experimenting with quoted strings and weird punctuation to try to figure out, say, **how to filter for only "no sound" and any of the terms "10.04", "lucid", or "lynx"**. But people coming here are likely to be troubled enough by their Ubuntu problems; they don't need to fight their way through undocumented forum-usage experiments on top of that.

Could someone take a few hours out of a day sometime and provide some of this basic information? And could the administrators please provide links to this How-To in both the "Forum Help" pull-down heading and on the main page (so one can find it without poking arrow buttons!), and give both links a meaningful title like "How to use forum features")? Thank you for your consideration.

> **cpmman said:**

Thanks for the quick reply! However, the link and the information behind it mostly miss my point. First, the link to such information **must be in an obvious place on the main page**. It's rather absurd to operate a 20-story building with a thousand vending machines, place a single change machine on the 7th floor (down the second hall, third room on the right), and fail to post its location, forcing new customers to waste hours looking for it or repeatedly pestering staff and other customers with such an obvious question.

Furthermore, while I did find some useful-to-newbie info there, it's in a very poor format. A How-To is something that, _[as plucky suggested]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=5260260&postcount=41")_, can be printed out and placed next to the computer (or at least popped up in a separate browser tab/window). It's not a bunch of randomly added posts intermixed with commentary and other questions. In fact, this forum structure is perhaps one of the worst possible ways to provide factual, relatively-unchanging information that needs neither commentary nor opinions, and using it to do so is nothing less than a violation to the idea of user-friendly information presentation.

I get the impression that this forum operates like a hammer and everything in it is beaten like a nail. From a usability point-of-view (i.e., for anyone who's more interested in getting something done than gaining *forum-fu*), this **website** should include an ordinary **webpage** that groups basic "how-to-use-me" information into topical sections (how to edit, how to reply, how to search, how to use tags, etc.), tersely but clearly written. One should not have to sift through dozens of discrete posts with lots of irrelevant material just to find out how (in my immediate case, for example) one can include two tags, 'no sound' -- note the space -- and 'lucid', in a search attempt.
