### Post by SoftwareExplorer on 2009-09-13
I was asking also because of how it works with middle click. You get everything highlighted in another tab, and then you bring up that box and if you try to get rid of the http:// and don't do it right, it replaces the middle click text you were going to paste. And Firefox won't let you go to another tab to re-highlight without closing the box and therefore getting the http:// back. 

It seems like the best way to get around this is to insert the middle click text at the very start of the box, so that http:// is at the end where it is easy to backspace.
