### Post by Kopkins on 2012-10-04
If you look at the view of other threads in comparison to your own you may see that the number of posts compared to the views is much smaller. Generally it takes many more views to start generating posts. In the threads I have posted I have received around 60+ views before I get any responses at all. To me, several means 7. To me, 7 views on a thread means no one. There are thousands of members of these forums. Much fewer, under 100 or in the hundreds regularly contribute. 

Also, posts such as "I will post here later." without any constructive material are discouraged to my knowledge. The idea of the forum is support, so If a post isn't supportive, for example helping solve the problem, that post is not helpful or relevant. 

These forums are not a lifeline. It is not something you come to when everything else has failed and you have minutes left to find the answer. They are something to come to if everything has failed and you want an answer. It is often not as timely as you would like. I posted a thread here about a week ago. I still don't have a reply to it. If people don't know the answer they might not post. 

Beans are not they way of telling who is and who is not welcome. Being professional is. If a user has 1 post and their post is easy to understand and they are considerate and helpful to finding the solution to their problem, they will be helped. If someone posts and does not compose an intelligible message, or is overly impatient, they will often not be helped as well as the first user. 

Don't take this as me trying to bash you or anything like that. I am only trying to help you understand. It seems from what I see in the above post that you have been impatient. It is not uncommon for it to take at least 24 hours for someone to post in your thread. And if you don't get a post you can bump it to the top (after 24 hours). 

I hope you find a solution to your issues,

Kopkins
