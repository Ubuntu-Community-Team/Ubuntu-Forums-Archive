I would propose that there be a standard procedure for threads that get out of hand, and that if special circumstances call for a deviation from the standard procedure, then the moderators discuss what would be the best course of action.

If a thread gets out of hand...

1. Send private messages to the offending parties asking them to cool it.
2. If the messages aren't heeded, cut the offending posts out of the thread and move those posts to the Jail or the Backyard
3. If the thread isn't as volatile but still seems too heated for the Cafe, move the entire thread to the Backyard
4. If the thread is redundant, merge it with the other similar thread
5. If the bulk of the thread (not just two forum members arguing back and forth and flaming one another--but *several* forum members generally kicking up ill will) is counterproductive, put in a public warning to have people cool it. Afterwards, close the thread if the warning isn't heeded.

Note, in my proposal, it takes a very extreme situation to close a thread. No one has to adopt this, but it is a proposal, and I'm putting it out there.

### Post by TravisNewman on 2006-07-21
Let me start off by saying that I haven't been a mod in a while, so this whole thing is a little new to me, even though I had experience before.

I've resisted posting here because I don't want to get caught in the mess, but here's my thought on the post closings:

Censorship is bad. Any kind of censorship is bad. In some cases it's unacceptable. HOWEVER, we're a forum that abides by the Ubuntu CoC, and while the Backyard provides SOME leniency, until there's a way to verify age, we still have to keep that safe for the younger Ubuntu users, and regardless of age, we have to keep things from being overtly offensive.

Azz made a comment long ago when we were both mods, "The best surgeon is the one that has to cut the least," and I agree with that. My history teacher in high school made the analogy of law enforcement to the price of tomatoes: "When there's a surplus of tomatoes, they're worth much less." Basically, the more present law enforcement is, the less it will be appreciated. I agree with that as well. 

We tow a line between trying to give people a necessary amount of freedom and trying to make the forums accessible and comfortable for everyone. If a dispute leads to harsh feelings between people that causes unnecessary tension, that's unfortunate.

We're trying to make this the best linux forum that we can, and we need rules to do that. We need to close threads, we need to move threads to the jail, and in the most unfortunate of cases we need to ban users.

The problem is knowing how much authority is too much, how much authority is not enough, and how much is just right. Do you know how much is just right? I sure don't. Ubuntu-Geek has been admin here since the beginning, and KiwiNZ and jdong showed that they were prime candidates for admin over their time as moderators, but I doubt THEY know how much is too much. 

All we can do is try to moderate as best as we can. Sometimes we will do too much, sometimes we won't do enough, and in cases where we aren't sure, we consult each other. But we still make mistakes. All we can ask is that you trust us, and call us out in the resolution center if you think we've messed up. 

Long story short: We don't LIKE closing threads and such, but we feel it's necessary from time to time to keep things comfortable for everyone. If personal disputes ever get in the way of the comfort level to the point where some people don't want to come here for support then we've lost our purpose entirely.

Sorry if I've rambled on and bored you ;)

EDIT: all that said, after reading what Azz said, i wanted to add some things.
I DO think that steering threads is an important part of what we do. I personally try to do that before anything, but I can't comment on anyone else here, I've been out of the loop for too long.
