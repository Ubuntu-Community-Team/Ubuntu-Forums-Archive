### Post by Iowan on 2011-09-26
From the Code of Conduct:
> If a post is older than a year or so and hasn't had a new reply in that time, instead of replying to it, create a new thread. In the software world, a lot can change in a very short time, and doing things this way makes it more likely that you will find the best information. You may link to the original discussion in the new thread if you think it may be helpful.*Occasionally* I'll move a response to a new thread, and link back to the original (as the poster *should* have). As closed threads can still be used as links, there isn't often a need to unlock a moldy, old thread ;)

### Post by lisati on 2011-09-26
Sometimes it's a judgement call. With a forum such as this, the quality of advice available can vary considerably. When you figure in the number of things that can change in the space of even a month or two, it can get difficult to follow a thread.

As others have pointed out, sometimes the best option is to start a new thread with a link back to an older thread.
