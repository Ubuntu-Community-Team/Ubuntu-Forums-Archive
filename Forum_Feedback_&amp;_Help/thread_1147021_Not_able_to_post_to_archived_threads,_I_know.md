### Post by Saibot Sivad on 2009-05-03
Preface:
I know you can't post to archived threads. I know this because a Google search led me to the solution to my problem and I wanted to post a reply which clarified a few points. I had to fiddle around a bit because I just got a screen that said "You can't post here" and I didn't know why. I found out later, but it might have been nice to be told that right away. That last sentence is not what I am posting about, however.

A Thought:
Often I will look on Google for a solution to my Linux related problem. What usually happens is I find a bunch of half answered posts. It seems to go like this (all technical terms are made up, they just represent a typical scenario):

Helpless Noob: "I have a problem with my Toast-O-Matic USB Dongle. I plug it in and a screen pops up that says 'lib-fifty-cent-50C is broken, try installing lib-of-broken-dreams' but then when I tried installing it I got the same error. HELP ME PLEASE LOL!?!?"

My Thoughts As I Read: "OMG LOL? That is my exact problem!"

Helpful Hobbit: "Try using sudo i-am-awesome, it worked for me when I had that problem. Also, could you post the text that follows when you type in 'lsusb-doesnt-help'?"

HN: "Okay, I did sudo and it said 'lib-mc-hammer has no $PANTS.etc at broken code of 42" and when I type in your lsusb command I get:" (followed by lots of code)

MTAIR: "Hey, that is exactly what mine does, I may be on to something!"

HH: "Well, I don't know what to do next, sorry."

HN: No response

MTAIR: "AAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHH!!!!!!!"

Alternately:

MTAIR: "Oh, I see, and here is my solution."

Fin.

Okay, so here is my thoughts: If that forum page shows up on a top Google page, it is obviously going to happen again for someone else. What I like to do is keep track of the forums where I found half answers like above, and if I find a real answer I post it there. In this way, the next person who has the same problem, who will obviously look on Google, and who will pick the first thing that looks remotely close to their problem, will stumble onto that forum and find a solution.

Still with me? Wow! Thanks!

Okay, so I don't actually have a solution to this issue, I just thought that maybe if I brought out in the open my frustration, maybe others would have thoughts on the matter.

There are two issues with archiving old posts:

1) If you don't, people (the new folks) tend to post their slightly-to-very different question/problem (because to them it looks similar, they are new remember) and it gets hard to moderate things in an organized fashion.

2) If you do, people who look online for a solution (like me) will keep finding not-really-solved problems which highly relate, yet they will never be answered.

This issue, that I hardly ever seem to find truly solved problems, is something that aggravates me almost more than pop-ups used to before I discovered Firefox. Seriously.

Any thoughts? :confused:

Can someone who was in charge of the decision can tell me, for my own curiosity mostly, why the "older" posts were made non-reply-able?

I know this was a long post, thanks for putting up with me!
