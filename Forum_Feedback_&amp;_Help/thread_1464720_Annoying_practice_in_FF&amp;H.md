### Post by saulgoode on 2010-04-28
It is quite an annoyance when threads in Forum Feedback get locked because another person may be discussing the same topic in the Resolution Center (where only the thread's author and staff are permitted to post). This annoyance is perhaps unintended -- sometimes it is not readily apparent to those with staff privileges that regular members do not have access to certain board features. Nonetheless, it could easily be avoided.

Rather than providing one member exclusive rights to discussion upon an issue in which others have expressed an interest, it would be less discriminatory to close the RC thread and leave the FF&H thread open. In this way, all those interested in participating in the discussion should be afforded equal opportunity to do so.

An example would be [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1464430") being closed owing to the existence [this thread in the RC](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1464447). Neither myself nor Excedio are permitted further participation despite our expressed interest in addressing the issue.

That is rather my point. Why require that everyone who wishes to contribute a point to the discussion create a separate thread in the RC?
