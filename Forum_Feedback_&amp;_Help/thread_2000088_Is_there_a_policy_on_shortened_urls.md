### Post by coffeecat on 2012-06-09
If by shortened URLs, you mean the use of a URL shortening service such as bit.ly or tinyurl, then there is a mention in the Code of Conduct:

> Do not shorten your words to acronyms or abbreviations or use URL-shortening services, as is often done when texting or in a Twitter-style update. It is very difficult to read and understand and you are not limited to a small number of characters.

However, I must admit that this is something that doesn't seem to be generally known, nor is it much enforced. So thanks for raising this topic. And, of course, there is the problem of spammers who use URL shortening services to try to camouflage their spammy links.

The forum software shortens the visible part of a link in a post anyway, and you can see the whole URL by hovering your mouse over the link (at least in Firefox), so there is little or no point in using a URL shortening service. For example:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11613896&postcount=7](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11613896&postcount=7)

And this works for those links (individual products on the Amazon site come to mind) where the URL is very long.

One problem is enforcing this. Do we add part of the URL-shortening domain to the censor list so that [noparse] http://tinyurl.com/abc123 appears as http://*******.com/abc123[/noparse]? But that would make a nonsense of many helpful posts that have already been made using URL shorteners and new posters may not notice that their URLs have been mangled, thus causing irritation and confusion. Or do we rely on people reporting shortened URLs, which would entail much staff time in dealing with this?

If there is a general feeling that this policy needs reviewing or emphasising, I'm happy to take this further and have it discussed amongst the staff, so what do other forum members think?
