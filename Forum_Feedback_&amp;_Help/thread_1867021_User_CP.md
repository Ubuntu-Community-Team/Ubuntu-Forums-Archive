I'm somewhat confused by that statement.

A new user with limited knowledge and/or experience is supposed to  provide assistance in the support sections? If I recall correctly, when  first starting Ubuntu (7.04) the last thing I was worried about was  assisting others. :smile:

This isn't just a support forum. One of the most utilized sections of  the forums (I believe) is the Cafe. Which last I checked was a  non-support section. It's disingenuous to suggest that the "only"  function served by the forums is support. I guess one could rationalize that even the Cafe provides support for members who wish to communicate with other members around the water cooler.

For example, posting threads for  problems one may not have simply to acquire more beans. It's been my  experience that people can be quite creative, especially spammers.  Wouldn't surprise me if a spammer made use of the T&E or the support  sections to selectively choose a number of issues. Then start multiple  threads requesting assistance for said issues. Doubt it would take long to reach the required minimum.

I take exception to "you will get your beans and rewards for doing so"  when part of the forum FAQ documentation referring to beans posted by [[COLOR=#D40000]**ubuntu-geek**[/COLOR]]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=1") [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/announcement.php?f=48") specifically states this about beans:

> 
_**Ubuntu Forums FAQ**_ version 1.15 - Sept 5, 2011

There isn't a list of what you get, and when, because that's not the point.

We did this on purpose - because it was fun and whimsical.

Bottom line: it just a trivial, whimsically amusing little thing. Don't  read too much into it...it really isn't worth the slightest emotional  involvement.

Guess that's out the window. I have no problem with the forums altering or creating  rules as necessary. But one shouldn't imply that beans have no value  beyond being whimsically amusing little things. At least not anymore,  since they represent a direct link to the reward.
