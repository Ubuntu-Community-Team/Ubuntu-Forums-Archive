I beg your pardon, but the old forums are **very accessible through the Advanced Search**.  I've found things from two thousand and four(2004).  *From any page in the forum*, the button that takes you to the Advanced Search is between New Posts and Quick Links on the **top right side of the page**, near your **username**.

Once you are in the Advanced Search, the place where you decide which forums to search is **on the bottom right**, you can **hold down Control** on your keyboard **while clicking** the mouse in order to **select multiple forums** to search.  And you can scroll down in the same box to find more forums to select.  (Don't forget to answer the "**Random Question**" before hitting the Search button).  

The search also has options to search by *tag, desktop system, a particular user's posts, or posts with a particular number of replies; you can also organize them by threads or posts*, and it looks like you can use the **dropdown menu on the bottom left** to find posts from **up to a year ago**.  (*To put this in different words*, you wouldn't always need to select forums from the archives in the **forum selection box on the bottom right**, although I've always used the forum selection box out of *personal preference*).

Sorry that it was off-topic.  But continuing with **eugenevdm's question **about not being able to **reply to old posts**, I made an observation.  While the **Ubuntu Forums Code of Conduct** states that users should "not cross post, or post the same thing in multiple locations," I don't believe it says anything about *paraphrasing*.  

Although I do not fully understand the **Creative Commons** thing, I looked it up after seeing "*Public forum data is released under the *[*Creative Commons Attribution 2.5 License*]("http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.5/")," at the bottom of the Code of Conduct.  Even though Ubuntu Forums retains the right to make their rules stricter by disallowing the "**Share**" portion of the license, **the "Remix" portion remains**, as long as there is **Attribution**, and the Forums "**sort of**" allows this with the "**Quote**" feature.  

**In short**, eugenevdm should be able to *paraphrase old forums*, despite that Ubuntu Forums has posted that the Forums are using an **older version** of the Creative Commons License.
