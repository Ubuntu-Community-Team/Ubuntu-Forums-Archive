### Post by Jucato on 2006-07-13
Do we "need" separate forums? No.
Is it bad to have separate forums? I don't think so.

AFAIK, KubuntuForums.Net was started as a sort of alternative or backup support for Kubuntu users, back in the days (according to the admin/mods there) when Ubuntuforums.org was having server problems or was down quite a lot. It also became a place where Kubuntu users would, as aysiu would put it, not be told to run "gedit".

I don't see any reason why it couldn't harmoniously co-exist with Ubuntuforums.org. Kubuntu and Xubuntu have their own websites, and that has not become an issue. KubuntuForums.Net has existed for months now, and the "fears" of splintering the Ubuntu community has never happened. KFN has never said that it is, in any way, an "official" Kubuntu support forum, the way Ubuntuforums is the official community forum. 

In the end, KFN is just a place where Kubuntu users could hang around, ask questions, and discuss KDE only stuff, without hesitation from being misunderstood or something. You could probably think of Ubuntuforums as an official site, while KFN as a sort of "fansite".

If you've noticed that KFN is a bit less-updated or less "professional", the reasons behind it come from different factors. The most important one is the forum software being used. Ubuntuforums use vBulletin, KFN uses a SMF and MKPortal. Also, the people running the forums are different. AFAIK, Ubuntuforums has a team in charge of maintaining/modifying the forums and that it is owned by a group of people (not really sure). Also, if I'm not mistaken, Ubuntuforums runs on Canonical or Ubuntu's servers, right? On the other hand, KFN is owned/administered by a one-man team, with a group of moderators keeping close watch. I think that he's running the site from his own personal resources. He's recently been out of town to attend to some important family matters, which explains the lack of structure updates.

Please note that I do not speak on behalf of the KFN team, and that my statements are purely my own opinion based on previous discussions on the same topic.
