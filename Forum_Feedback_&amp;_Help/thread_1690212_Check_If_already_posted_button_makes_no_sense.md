### Post by MisterGaribaldi on 2011-02-20
As someone who has admin'd/mod'd on other boards, I can see where the admins here are coming from. It really is little more than a judgement call, and it would probably be the same if it were the regular users who went around locking threads and keeping a board cleaned up.

What I think a LOT of people forget is that much of what we do in life is context-centric. It is hard to make iron-clad rules because you often have situations which themselves are unclear and require a "spirit of the law" approach rather than a "letter of the law" one.

The best thing here is that the open-door policy can be used to resolve things on a case-by-case basis. The users here shouldn't discount the value in that. Also, when disputing a ruling, it isn't necessary -- and indeed isn't appropriate -- to take an automatically hostile approach. That's stupid and immature and generally uncalled-for, so just don't do it.
