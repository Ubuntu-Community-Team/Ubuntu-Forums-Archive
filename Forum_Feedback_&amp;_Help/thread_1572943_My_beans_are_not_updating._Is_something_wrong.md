### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-09-12
I've observed the ongoing questions regard beans in this section of the  forums for some time. It's become obvious that some members (especially  new) struggle with understanding the bean program and how it applies to  them. I equate that with the confusing associated documentation.  I've read the documentation numerous times and find it somewhat  convoluted and hard to comprehend. Of course, that could be reflective  of just my inabilities and not those of others. :grin:
 
Quite a few new users seem to be confused, by not only the bean count  but it's purpose. If bean count is primarily a simple (as the staffs infers) attempt to add humor to the forums, I think this could better be  defined. For example, I believe many new users associate bean count with  knowledge and experience. Or even worse - with forum status. Most  members who have been in the forums for a period of time know one of the  fastest way to accumulate beans is in the T&E. Where beans are  acquired for little more than a brief opinion or response. I seriously  doubt this equates to either knowledge or experience.

I'd like to suggest the staff consider providing users with an in depth  and yet simple description regarding the bean program. I'm certain many members would gladly  participate in drafting such a document in support of the forums. This  may significantly reduce the number of confused members and help  eliminate recurring questions with regards to bean count and acquisition  of said beans.

Perhaps, a team of members could work together (under the guidance of a  single staff member) and produce a number of drafts for submission. The staff  could then select the most appropriate and informational draft,  eliminating the need for staff members to invest time in this endeavor.  Understanding that the staff have limited time resources.

Just my $0.02 (for what it's worth). :wink:
