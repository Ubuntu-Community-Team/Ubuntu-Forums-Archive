### Post by kassetra on 2006-01-28
[quote=matthew]Kass: what are you using to create the avatars? Mine is a lot less smooth than the ones you made...it could be the program, but I freely admit it's probably because you're just better at this than I am.[/quote] 
Well... a mix of things... I have powerpuff body parts in illustrator, gimp, freehand, inkscape and photoshop. (Eep! I have a directory full of cartoon body parts!)

I usually finish assembling and coloring the pieces in photoshop - then I send them off to imageready for web post-production.

And my bitmapped-images are around 600px x 600px .... so that I can scale them down and correct them from more data. (Vectors, of course, scale to any size my processor/ram combos allow... heh.)

Most likely - I'm guessing you're getting such roughage (HA!) from flat paint bucket recolorings, most likely in gimp or photoshop with really choppy settings. For smoother results, you're going to want to use a more generous allowance on your paint bucket tool or possibly marquee the area you want filled, roughly first, fill it and then go back with a paintbrush tool and at a high zoom, paint with a 1 or 3 px brush, with soft edges, around the area to 'smooth' the color fill.

PM me some time and I can probably walk you through how to do this in your choice of photo editor.  :)


EDIT: *YE GODS I'VE BEEN DOING THIS FOR A LONG TIME... post-production?  what person outside of the industry uses that term?!*
