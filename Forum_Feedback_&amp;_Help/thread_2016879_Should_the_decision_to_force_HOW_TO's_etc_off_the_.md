### Post by cariboo on 2012-07-04
@Cobuntu, you can only vote on polls in the Cafe. Most polls serve no purpose, as the only forum community members that vote in them, are usually only those that are interested in the subject, and don't show a true result.

To the OP, you can still create howto's, just not in Tips and Tutorials. Wiki's are a better place for the information, as it is easier for users to find, and if they are well maintained, they remain relevant, another benefit, is that if the original creator decides to stop updating the page, someone else can take ove and keep the information current. The biggest problem with the majority of the howto's was that they didn't follow the criteria set out by the forum council for creating and maintaining tutorials.

This is also not the correct way to get yourself heard. If you have a problem with any decision we make, create an item on the [Forum Council Agenda]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ForumCouncilAgenda"), and attend the next meeting to discuss your concerns.

### Post by s.fox on 2012-07-05
This was agreed upon in [March]("http://ubottu.com/meetingology/logs/ubuntu-meeting/2012/ubuntu-meeting.2012-03-27-19.07.html"), this is not a new recent change. Plenty of time was given for people to raise concerns and objections to moving the information to the wiki.

The [Forum Council]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ForumCouncil") had been in discussions with the outside community about the information contained within the tutorials section long before then. Again anyone was welcome to participate and contribute opinions.

One of the major hurdles at the time was finding a means to migrate the post data, a solution was found with a simple [script]("http://bodhizazen.net/tweaks/ubforums2ubwiki.sh.txt") created.  It was also agreed that for every tutorial moved to the wiki a discussion thread would exist on the forums.  [Here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1949027") is the discussion thread in the Tutorials section.

I fear we are going around in circles, if you wish to discuss this further then please add it to the Forum Council meeting [agenda]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ForumCouncilAgenda"). If you decide to add it then please do attend.
