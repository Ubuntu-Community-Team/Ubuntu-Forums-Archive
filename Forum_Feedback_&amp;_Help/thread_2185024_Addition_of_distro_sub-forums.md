### Post by coffeecat on 2013-11-01
One reason why I wouldn't be supportive of the idea of having more specific sub-forums for the official flavours of Ubuntu is that under the hood of the desktop environment, the variants are more or less the same, and the problems that people need help are often the same with the same solutions.

If you have a look around the forum you'll find many threads about issues that are not specific to the Ubuntu flavour that the OP is running. Examples would be partitioning problems, filesystem corruption, folder/partition permissions, UEFI booting, wireless driver problems, and so on. If someone wanted advice on re-organising their partition layout, for example, and posted in a Kubuntu sub-forum, many members who would be able to help might not see the thread because they don't use Kubuntu and therefore might not visit that sub-forum. With our present setup, so long as the person starting the thread uses a descriptive thread title, others not using that variant might still see the threads and be prepared to help.

You will notice that we already have specific sub-forums for two official variants: Ubuntu Studio and Mythbuntu. They were created long ago and are really special cases, originally intended for things unique to those two. However, if you look in those two sub-fora, you'll find threads about issues common to all Ubuntu variants – networking problems, video card drivers, for example – that might have done better in one of the more frequented sections. I think this would happen on a larger scale if we had Xubuntu, Kubuntu, and so on dedicated sub-forums, to the detriment of those seeking help.

I join Elfy in thanking you for your interest. If you have a suggestion for making the forum structure simpler, but which still reflects the varying needs of the forum membership, we'll be glad to hear it! :)
