### Post by Frak on 2010-08-05
Too much orange. Follow the Pareto principle and leave roughly 80 percent neutral-blending colors, and 20-percent standout color. The orange should be used as a visual cue for important objects. Purple should be used somewhere as an accent (to mirror Canonical's use, restrict it to the top of the page). Finally, stick to #333 text on #FFF background, optionally using #F7F7F7 to hint at informational panels within a post container.

That's a good synopsis of how to emulate Canonical's style with the Ubuntu home page.

### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2010-08-05
That design draws my focus straight to the chrome and the background. The background is especially problematic to me. The dots pattern is a cool motif, but it's just that: a motif. It should not be used as a background that spans the full width and height of a page.

For example, here's how I am incorporating the dots motif in my design for Harvest:
[ATTACH]165588[/ATTACH]
[ATTACH]165589[/ATTACH]
The first picture is the footer of the page, so it's a *very* light touch. It adds a little bit of depth so I can give the rest of the page a plain, simple white background without looking monotonous. The second one is a text field being edited, because dotted lines are cool.

In addition, the denser dots are intended for technical documents. For consumer stuff, they should be spaced a bit further apart ;)

The downloadable Ubuntu Brand Guidelines may give you some nice inspiration to build from:
[http://design.canonical.com/the-toolkit/ubuntu-brand-guidelines/](http://design.canonical.com/the-toolkit/ubuntu-brand-guidelines/)
