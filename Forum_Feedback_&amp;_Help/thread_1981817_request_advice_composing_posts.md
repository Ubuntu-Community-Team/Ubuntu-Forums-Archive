### Post by coffeecat on 2012-05-17
I think what you want is the [Forum Guide to BBCode]("http://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=bbcode").

How did I do that? This is what I typed in the message field to make "Forum Guide to BBCode" as a link to the guide page:

> [noparse]I think what you want is the [Forum Guide to BBCode]("http://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=bbcode")[/noparse]

Or a simpler way is to type the word or phrase you want as a clickable link, highlight it, and then click on the [img]http://ubuntuforums.org/images/editor/createlink.gif[/img] icon in the message toolbar and add the URL where prompted.

Or a really basic way is simply to type something like this:

> Here is a link to the forum BBCode guide:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=bbcode](http://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=bbcode)

Simply copy and paste the URL and the forum software will make it a link, and will shorten it for display if necessary. Please do not use URL-shortening services such as tinyurl. They're unnecessary.

For posting code, simply something like this:

> [noparse]```

Your code here

```[/noparse]

Will appear as:

```

Your code here

```

There is also the [img]http://ubuntuforums.org/images/editor/code.gif[/img] button in the message toolbar for adding code tags, and it is worthwhile exploring the other buttons in the toolbar too.

Is this what you were after?

**EDIT**: And to get the URL of a single post rather than a whole thread, simply click on the post number at top right of the post and the post by itself will open in a separate tab. Open that tab and copy the URL from the address bar. You can then paste it into your post [like this!]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11944438&postcount=2") :wink:
