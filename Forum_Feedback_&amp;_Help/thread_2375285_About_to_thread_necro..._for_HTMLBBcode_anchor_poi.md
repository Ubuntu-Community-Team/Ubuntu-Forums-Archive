### Post by coffeecat on 2017-10-24
The reason the two examples you link don't work is because they are custom BBCode which have been set up on those two vBulletin forums but not here. 

I understand what you are asking for. This feature was requested once about 5 years ago, but consideration by the Forum Council was deferred then because of an impending major upgrade to the forum software which took up all our time. Once the dust had settled on all that the idea was not pursued. 

You don't make it clear whether the post you wish to include anchor points in is a tutorial or howto, but I suspect it may be. On the surface this sounds a good idea, but there are a few matters that need consideration.

[list][*]Since that time 5 years ago, the forum has suffered two major hacks. In response, Canonical IS has considerably beefed up our defences over and above what comes with a standard vbulletin installation. One result is that some very long posts trigger an error message. This is a small price to pay for increased security. It is possible that a tutorial post long enough to need anchor points, would cause this problem, but this is not certain.
[*]As a general policy since those hacks, any changes to the forum software code needs approval by Canonical IS to ensure such changes do not compromise security. Custom BBCode which invokes html would certainly need such approval. It is a time-consuming and necessarily bureaucratic process. 
[*]Although we do have a Tutorials section - please see the [guidelines]("https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2143602") - a forum is not really the best place for howtos and tutorials, particualrly long ones. A forum is at its best when specific problems and questions are addressed. Tutorials and howtos are better placed on blogs or, better, on a community wiki such as [the Ubuntu Community Wiki](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/CommunityHelpWiki). [/list]

The Forum Council wishes to be receptive to ideas to make the forum better, so I will take this suggestion to the FC but, in the meantime, please clarify what sort of post you are trying to modify. 

Is this an existing post? If so, please provide a link. You talk about thread "necro". What do you mean? Thread necromancy is generally frowned on. It may be better to abandon an old tutorial and start afresh. That is something you would have better control over on a blog or on a wiki.
