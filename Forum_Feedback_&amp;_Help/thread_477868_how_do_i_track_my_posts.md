### Post by Znupi on 2007-06-18
You have a couple of options:
1. Go to **Quick Links -> My Profile -> Find all posts by mjp29** or **Find all threads started by mjp29**.
2. **User CP -> Edit Options -> Default Thread Subscription Mode -> No email notifications**. Now you can go to **User CP -> User Control Panel** to view threads you have posted in and that have new replies, and to **Quick Links -> Subscribed Threads** to view all threads you have posted in.

Note that option 2. will only display threads / posts posted from the moment you set the default subscription mode. (a.k.a. if you made a post and you lost it and can't find it, go for 1.)

### Post by Sweet Mercury on 2007-06-19
> **mjp29 said:**
> Is there a place in the control panel or somewhere I can see all the posts I've made recently 

or does one have to track the posts another way?

There's also a drop-down menu next to the **Search** option. At the bottom of the menu, there will be two choices, **Find All Your Threads** and **Find All Your Posts**. How those function is pretty self evident.

Those drop-down menus only work in some browsers, though. If you don't have that option you can try a few other quick ways (if you don't want to deal with thread subscriptions).

First, just click on the **Search** link and it will take you to the dedicates search screen. On the right, there will be a box called "Search by User Name." Enter your name, choose the option underneath it, and there you go.

A third option is from the home page: scroll down to the list of currently active users. Your name should be first on the list, so it's easy to find. Click on it to view your profile/stats. There will be two links, "Find All Posts By Your_Name" and "Find All Threads By Your_Name."

edit: Just FYI, these methods work in pretty much all BBS forums unless the creators have specifically disabled them. Most proprietary forums have similar functions as well.
