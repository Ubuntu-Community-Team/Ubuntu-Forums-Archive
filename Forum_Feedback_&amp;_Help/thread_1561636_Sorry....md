### Post by eriktheblu on 2010-08-26
As a moderator of another forum, I close such threads because the additional posts add little or no value to the original discussion.

The necro posts typically fall into one of a couple categories:

"This happened to me too". Instead of tacking your issue at the end a 3 page discussion that ended years ago, better to start your own and eliminate confusion.

"I agree with X". Taking sides on an inactive argument is quite pointless. The arguing have obviously lost interest; best to let it be.

"Try this to fix your problem" Which would might have been helpful 2 years ago, but since then I've fixed it, or given up on it.

And my very favorite "I like your post, I also like <completely unrelated product>"

~1% of thread necromancy is relevant, and I allow it to happen. The vast majority however are n00bs and spammers who add nothing to the prior conversation, and disrupt current discussions.

### Post by lisati on 2010-08-27
Sometimes the best answer to a particular question changes over different releases. 

**Example:** A couple of months ago, when cobbling together a PHP page for my web site, I did some preliminary tests of the logic underlying the new section by using the CLI version of PHP on my laptop, and managed to get things working perfectly. Then, when I incorporated the new section of code into a live version of the web page, it failed miserably. ### Post by dmizer on 2010-09-01
The reality is that we cannot make everyone happy all the time.

While you may be irritated that threads occasionally get closed, others would get irritated if all threads were closed after X period of activity. Still others would (rightly so) get irritated if old threads were allowed to resurface and continue again.

The best course of action which makes the most people happy is to allow all threads to continue unless there is a good reason to close them.

Furthermore, we do not simply close any necro-posted thread. Each case is reviewed individually and sometimes there is a great deal of discussion among the mod staff.
