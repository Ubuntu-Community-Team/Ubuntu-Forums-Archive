### Post by etrek64 on 2011-04-30
It would be helpful if people would put their "Ubuntu distro" AND OS architecture as in "bit" 32bit or 64bit information in their signatures or in the left side panel under their name.  In forums for hardware discussions like "Toms Hardware" it's customary to list your hardware specs in the signature so responders know what their dealing with.

Why would this be helpful?  Because, I have been looking at a lot of forum posts (on many Linux distro forums) and find that people ask for help with a problem and get replies that are not relevant because they are using different OS architectures.  Especially with Multimedia questions.  An OP might ask, "why can't I view a video" or "I can't hear sound with my video, I've followed the guides, installed the codecs, and did the whole song and dance but the media still won't play".  Then responders say things like, "Huh that's funny, it works fine for me.  Why don't you do ...". Then the OP responds, "I tried that, It still doesn't work...".  - sound familiar?

The problem is that the OP is *probably* using a 64bit distro, while the responder is using a 32bit distro.  It seems a lot of things that work in 32bit just don't work in 64bit.   

Anyway this is just something that might be useful - at least the responders would know in advance what architecture the OP is using and that could be helpful.  :p

And yes, I know I don't have my specs listed - that's because I have yet to decide on going with 32bit or 64bit Linux yet (still using M$).  I really want to install 64bit OS, but seems it would cause too much problem with multimedia - so I'm leaning toward 32bit for now.:???:  will update my sig when I've made a decision.  Cheers.

### Post by yetiman64 on 2011-04-30
You're "Ubuntu distro" details can be displayed in the left hand panel (I'm showing as using Lucid 10.04 right now :smile:) by setting it up from your UserCP (user control panel).

Other system specs being displayed in a signature line is a good idea for new starters here who primarily need help as you point out. Though the suggestion doesn't work for everyone, for example, I am primarily a helper (when I need help with a problem I scour the forums and "help myself"). Currently my post count has just passed 800 but I am yet to start a thread here because I use the site as a research tool in conjunction with Google pretty effectively. 

One tip for new starters is to use the site tag (site:ubuntuforums.org) in a Google search eg. if you want to check for the Plymouth splash screen with nvidia cards in Google try,
> plymouth + nvidia site:ubuntuforums.org this allows you to specify only results from this site (try it without the site tag to compare). The forums themselves have an inbuilt search function and can be useful.

My sig line is basically full (500 character max including hidden BB code) with links for others to refer to. 

It really depends on your help needs as to what you display and any relevant data should be included in the post itself, as some helpers like myself will skip over sigs unless the OP refers to the details being there. For instance link #5 in my sig is regularly ignored by those I try to help ;).

By the way, welcome to the forums and enjoy. :)
