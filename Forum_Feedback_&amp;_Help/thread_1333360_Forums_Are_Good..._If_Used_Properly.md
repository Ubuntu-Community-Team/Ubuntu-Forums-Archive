### Post by oldefoxx on 2009-11-21
Ah me.  Had what I tought was a useful thread running, and suddenly the tap got closed.  It wasn't that there wasn't useful purpose behind the thread and the topics involved, but the name callers decided to jump in and gang up just to have their say, with little or nothing to contribute to the topic.  Seemed that if we learned anything from watching recent town meetings on TV, its those that yell the loudest and in greatest anger.

What can be done about it?  I believe it takes a certain degree of moderation to what is going back and forth.  If there is real substance seen in the topics, you should not immediately close the thread.  And you don't.  But if you don't screen the posts for those that contribute to the discussion and those that merely seek to express a differing viewpoint, one with no real substance offered behind for the disagreement, then you are really letting the thread deteriorate, because we should recognize that the nature of many people is to express their feelings rather than give any reason for those feelings.

I've participated in certain forums where the moderator takes more responsibility, and not only rejects certain posts as inappropriate, but warns the poster that the behavour is neither approved or nor accepted.  On all matters technical, there has to be substance given for any claims pro or con.  If you want to or are willing to abide an ***-kicking set of forums, just let the matter go on too long, and those that began the attacks will be encouraged to continue, and even stur others to the same behavior.  And it just spreads.  It also discourages those that do not like this conduct from participating further.

A lengthy exchange over Climate Change took place on one forum some years ago, and many contributed much of what was found in the press and from scientific sources.  Those that held other views sought to gain ground by tearing at the character and motivations of those that held a different opinion than theirs, and refuted the facts presented by going back to studies dating back decades, which were now deemed to be in error.  That went on for months, and the result is that many people that had been involved in both programming (the primary purpose for the forums) and the discussions, just decided to move on and never came back.

I'm just warning that I see some of the same poor conduct coming into play on your forums, and that this is not something you should be inclined to just accept.  You have control, and you can limit such outbursts short of just clamping down on the thread completely.
