### Post by handy on 2009-12-15
I noticed that a thread called Demonoid, was closed for review yesterday, due to (I expect) the fact that a Demonoid user gave out a number of invite codes that people could use to become Demonoid members.

The reason I suspect this, is that those codes have been edited out of his post.

So, as a Demonoid user, I know first hand that a large number of distros are available via Demonoid; Demonoid does give access to a lot of legal data. There is also a good number of freely available software & other data that is accessed via the Demonoid .torrent search engine.

New versions of Ubuntu, land on Demonoid incredibly quickly after release. They are there in such an efficient manner that I have always wondered whether the Ubuntu administration puts the torrents up at Demonoid to take the load of their own servers?
Though in reality I expect it is due to the efficiency of Demonoid's connection to the .torrent distribution network.

Though, the contentious question that arises is that just because a .torrent search engine lists illegal downloads as well as legal downloads, should the Ubuntu forums consider the use of, or the promotion of the use of Demonoid (in this example) as an inherently illegal activity?

Personally I think not, it is up to the individual user of Demonoid or any other .torrent search engine as to what they do or don't up/download.

Why should the rare inclusion of Demonoid (in this example) invitation codes be considered illegal, when legally free software is available/accessible on the Demonoid database?

So I made a poll to see what you guys/gals think about how the Ubuntu forums should deal with .torrent search engine talk in said forum.
