### Post by squakie on 2014-05-04
By posting on the forum.  However, beans don't really mean much (except for the real experts out there!).  By my old id I was something close to 6000 if I remember correctly, and already up at about 1800 now on this new id, and mine are mostly from asking questions!

So, don't be concerned about them.  As you learn, you will answer questions here for people.  As you do that, you'll watch your bean count climb, and you can do so with the personal knowledge that it was by helping, not just posting anything under the sun to try to run up a bean count.

### Post by QIII on 2014-05-04
Beyond the 10 required for modifying your profile (which are earned in the support forums -- and we watch for people who post only to increase their bean count and jail meaningless ones), beans are only a bit of fun.  They are not indicative in any way of how smart or expert a user is.

One bean earned by a person who only posts once but gives an answer that helps someone out is more valuable than 1,000 beans held by someone who has said nothing helpful to anyone.

The long and the short of it is this:  Don't worry about beans.
