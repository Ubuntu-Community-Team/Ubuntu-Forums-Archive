### Post by jonest1 on 2009-12-27
There are two methods to track a thread.

When creating a thread, there is a section near the bottom called 'Additional Options'.  A subsection called 'Thread Subscription' allows you to subscribe to the thread and track it.

If viewing a thread which is already created, there are red links at the top-right of the post.  Click the 'Thread Tools' and then click 'Subscribe to this Thread' from the drop-down menu.

To view threads to which you are subscribed, click the 'Quick Links', link in the top menu bar and select 'Subscribed Threads' from the Miscellaneous section.

You can also view threads by clicking on 'User CP' from the top menu bar and clicking the 'view all subscribed threads' near the right side of the page, or by clicking 'List Subscriptions' under the 'Subscribed Threads' in the left-menu bar.

### Post by memilanuk on 2009-12-27
Once you've done that, you may want to go to the User CP link, select 'Edit Options' and scroll down a bit... you should find an option to subscribe you to any thread you start or post in.  I believe the default is 'no', but you can change it to 'instant', 'daily' etc. depending on your email tolerance ;) You can also select that for each post either before you click 'Submit' (if using the full / 'Advanced' editor i.e. not Quick Reply), or at the top of the thread under 'Thread Tools'.  The default subscription bit is pretty handy for making it so all you need to do when you log on is go to the Quick Links menu and select 'Subscribed Threads'.  Even if you don't get email notifications, any subscribed threads with new posts will show up in bold font... ya may want to go through and thin the subscription list after a while, though... especially if you post a lot ;)
