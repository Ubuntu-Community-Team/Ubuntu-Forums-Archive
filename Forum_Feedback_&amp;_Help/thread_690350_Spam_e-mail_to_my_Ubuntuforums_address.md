### Post by hyper_ch on 2008-02-07
Everywhere I have to submit an email I use:

[www.domain.com@mydomain.com](www.domain.com@mydomain.com)

It's not a wildcard anymore, that means the "www.domain.com@mydomain.com" must exist as valid email address on my server. I used to have just a wildcard one but at some time people started forging wrong sender addresses and they then also bumped back. In one day over 3000 email addresses where forged. That's when I changed to non-wildcard...

It all get put into the same mailbox and I can delete any of it anytime.

So, just replace your email address in here with another and dump the one used sofar. Remember: When you change your email address you have to validate the new one before you can post again (this kind of sux especially when using grey-listing)

### Post by matthew on 2008-02-07
You might also take a look at one of the settings that you can control on your account. Go to the user control panel at [http://ubuntuforums.org/usercp.php](http://ubuntuforums.org/usercp.php) and click "edit options." Scroll down a little and uncheck the box marked "Receive Email from Other Members". That will prohibit anyone other than staff from seeing your email address or sending you email. I guarantee none of our staff have anything to do with spam. Again, sorry this happened.
