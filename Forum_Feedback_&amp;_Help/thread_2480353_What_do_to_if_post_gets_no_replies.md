### Post by TheFu on 2022-10-26
After 1 day, you can "bump" it.  That will raise it in the "activity feed" for some people who only look there to see. I wouldn't "bump it" more than once a day and after a week, I'd unsubscribe and forget it.

Not all questions have answers.  Was a question actually asked?  In some languages a question is implied, but that doesn't translate into English.  If I don't see a '?' character, I'm more likely to think someone is venting, not asking any question.  That's fine.  Sometimes the wording is almost abusive, so I won't respond.  Posters need to realize they are coming to an all volunteer forum and calling "out baby" ugly, questioning ancestors, isn't the best way to seek help.  I know I'm guilty of many of these things and I know that long-time posters give me the benefit of any doubt. New posters don't get that same treatment.  Most of this paragraph applies to me, not you.

Not all titles are worded in a way to get someone with the needed skill to notice.  Maybe rethink the title?  I only look at thread titles before deciding whether to reply.  I used to skim specific subforums, but found that a huge percentage of posts are placed in a less-than-great forum, so I stopped.  OTOH, I will almost never respond to an Apple HW post. 

Sometimes, the question is so specific that the population of users who might know anything about it in the world is about 100 and 2 of those people use Ubuntu, but either visits these forums.  Hard to get any ideas on something that specific.

### Post by deadflowr on 2022-10-27
> How do I "bump" a post? T
You can just write BUMP in a post.
BUMP is an acronym, Bring Up My Post.
It'll bring your post to the top of the recent posts lists.

We allow bumping posts after 12 hours or so if no one has replied.
We do this so you might get people who weren't around when you first posted the chance to see it.

(In olden times we only allowed bumps after 24 hours, but that made little sense as you ended up bumping the same crowd when you first posted,
who probably weren't going to be helpful. Allowing bumps after around 12 hours expands the chance of someone helpful taking notice)

### Post by coffeecat on 2022-10-27
> **Advait said:**
> Oh. So the forum code bump subroutine will get triggered if it sees a post with only one word "bump"?

Eh?!

There is no forum code bump subroutine. Simply post a new post and your thread moves up to the top of the list in its sub-forum. That's how forums work. BUMP is an acronym for Bring-Up-My-Post. If you have had no reply and you want to bring up your thread, but have nothing further to add to the thread, simply post the single word "bump". Some people post "up", but "bump" is the most common way of doing this on the majority of forums.
