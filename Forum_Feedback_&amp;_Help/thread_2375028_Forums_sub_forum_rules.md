### Post by coffeecat on 2017-10-21
Hello there. 

If by "slap on the wrist" you are referring to our recent pm exchange, please be assured no slap was intended. :) There was a problem with the way the forum software rendered the BBCode in your sig, which I wanted to draw your attention to, and you promptly fixed that. For which, thanks. My only reason for quoting the appropriate line in the CoC was to emphasise the need to keep signatures low key and modest in size - nothing more. In fact our exchange highlighted a couple of problems which was useful to know about.

Anyway, here are the two most important links:

**Forum Code of Conduct**: [https://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=showrules](https://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=showrules)

And linked from that are the **Posting Guidelines**: [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2158945](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2158945)

Those are really the rules as such. In addition, a number of policies have been agreed by the Forum Council, and they are usually published as stickies in the relevant sub-forums. Here are three examples:

**Tutorials Sub-forum Guidelines**: [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2143602](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2143602)

**Forum Data Attribution, Retention and Privacy Policy**: [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2231107](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2231107)
[B]
Forum policy on log-in-as-root tutorials[/B]: [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1486138](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1486138)

There is no particular relevance to the author of the sticky posts. They tend to be team efforts in the writing, the final draft being approved by the Forum Council, and one admin will then post the approved version. Any admin may then edit a policy post to update it if necessary.

There will be other policies, scattered around the sticky areas, but we don't keep a comprehensive list.
