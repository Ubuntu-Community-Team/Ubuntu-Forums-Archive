### Post by Greyed on 2008-09-28
> **wladicus said:**
> In another thread a day or two ago I suggested that perhaps the best place for a thanks button would be in the editor where we compose our messages.

No, it wouldn't.  It would then require actually composing a message to thank someone.  How would you determine whom you're thanking?  Limit it to when you're quoting someone?  If so, how would you handle multi-quoted replies?

I like the notion of being able to thank someone, and have it registered and conveyed to them, without the added clutter of an actual message which; absent of anything other than the thanks, would just waste space.

Not only that but how people use the thanks button is kind of personal.  Before really using the button I searched the forum to see what it was intended for.  What I found was that it was for giving thanks.  What the thanks was **for** was not explained so I surmised it was whatever the end user feels is appropriate.

For me that isn't limited to "thanks for helping me" since I am very self-sufficient when it comes to Linux and Ubuntu in general.  I rarely ask questions and those questions I do ask rarely get answered because they are doozies.  For me I also thank people when they answer someone else's question in a manner almost identical to what I would have said.  IE, they got there first, "thanks for helping this guy out and saying what I would've said!"  Also sometimes our tempers get the better of us and when people find it in themselves to get past their disagreement and are gracious in their peacekeeping I am apt to thank them, esp. if I'm one of the participants who got a bit snippy, "thanks for being so level-headed".  

Most of the thanks I met out I don't want to be tied to the posting of a message.  I want them there in lieu of posting a message.

The only thing about the thanks system I wish was coded and is not is the ability to search and find all the posts for which I received thanks.  I can do a search to see which posts to which I've given thanks but that doesn't help when my thanks post goes up 1-2 and I'm left to wonder why.

### Post by sydbat on 2008-09-29
I have found that, whether right or wrong, people looking for assistance on forums tend to appreciate knowing who "appears" to have some higher standing in that community. It gives the person posting a question, for example, an idea that the person answering knows what they are talking about. 

It is a multi-edged sword though, because people can forget to give that thanks or +1 or whatever because they may be very busy getting something to work. And it can skew perception because a person who has no "Thanks" but a ton of posts is 'perceived' as not being very helpful.

Does this mean that the OP has not been helpful? Absolutely not.

Does this mean we should not have a system like this here? Again, absolutely not.

Giving someone a thumbs up or +1 or Thanks is a nice boost to that individual. It shows that people appreciate what they have said or how they have helped solve a problem. Everyone likes to be praised for helping another person, regardless of whether they say it is important or not. It gives them an emotional incentive to continue to help others.

My $0.00000001/2
