### Post by aysiu on 2006-12-19
Well, I'll tell you--we do have *some* very knowledgeable users here (I am not one of them), but I'd say in general Gentoo users know more, since Gentoo is a harder-to-use distro than Ubuntu. Gentoo tends to attract a more sophisticated user base. Ubuntu has some sophisticated users but generally attracts beginners/intermediates (like me).

Easy questions (How do I mount my Windows partition? How do I execute a command as root?) get answered almost immediately. So do slightly more difficult questions (I lost admin access for my only account. How do I get it back?). But really advanced questions take longer to get answered, and sometimes don't get answered at all.

That said, to be fair--you gave it one day. The more difficult the question, the more time you have to give it.

I don't think it'd be terrible to ask your question on the Gentoo forums. We answer questions here about other distros or even Windows.

### Post by Bezmotivnik on 2006-12-19
If you go to the beginner's forum, you have somewhat better luck.  Other than that, it's tough.

Overall, I get around 15%-20% of my Ubuntu tech questions solved here, and that seems to be about the same for other users' question threads I've followed.  That's about the same as my results for *any* tech question of *any* sort about *any*thing on the net.  The difference with that failure rate here is that many people tend to be more hostilely defensive about it (see above) as it doesn't sit well with the cloyingly self-congratulatory "community" thing that often gets pretty nauseating on the forum in the absence of any concrete justification for it. :rolleyes: If in your innocence you expect you're really going to be taken care of here substantially more than somewhere else, you'd better keep it to yourself.
