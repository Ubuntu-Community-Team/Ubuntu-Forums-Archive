### Post by nicklogan on 2007-05-09
First let me say I am not trying to start a flame war or inviting rants on the topic I'm going to raise. I'm also not trying to criticize the Ubuntu forums or administrators. I've gotten good support from this forum on many questions and have even been able to answer one or two.

A bean counter is the phrase I've come to mentally assign to Ubuntu forum contributors who feel the need to chime in on many problems that are raised even though they have nothing to contribute. Their posts amounts to "Duh, I don't know." or offers no helpful information on the topic. They seem to be answering just so they can raise the number of posted responses they can display in their sig or raise their ranking (beans). Many other forums have a feedback system (ex. Slashdot) where users can rank the responses to a topic and even filter out the ones below a preferred value. 

Is it worth it to start such a system in these forums? If not, I'm afraid the generally great How-to's found here will be eventually overwhelmed by the amount of crap you need to wade through to find them.
