### Post by jenkinbr on 2008-12-16
Personally, I start at the man pages if I know (or have some idea) of what programs my issue involves.  If, for example, I would like to figure out how to display, quick and simple, what my system architecture is, and I [highlight]Don't[/highlight] know that uname is the proper command for this, I will be directed to google and will search from there, possibly leading to a post on these forums if I am too stupid to use the correct search terms.  However, should I find that uname is the program for this, but the command as it is spits out more than I want, "man uname" is sure to help.  A quick glance at said man page will reveal that the -m option is exactly what I am looking for.  Problem solved.

However, it could also be useful to post, even in a simple situation such as the one above, because google will come through within a few days and index that page, thereby possibly providing the answer to someone's burning question.

As far as time is concerend, I typically don't waste more than 1 hour on finding a solution to an issue.  If the issue is pressing, I'll post.  If it isn't, I just let it go, and maybe, just maybe, I'll end up stumbling upon the answer (as is the case in the above issue with uname).
