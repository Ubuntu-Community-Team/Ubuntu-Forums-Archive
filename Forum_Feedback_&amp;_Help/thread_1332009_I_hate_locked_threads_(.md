### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-11-22
I'm going to try and explain the situation, and hope this doesn't cause more misconceptions...

> **koenn said:**
> ...and some subjects are almost guaranteed to end up locked.

This is the problem.  Not because the moderators have a list of usual suspects, but because some topics universally cause problems.  These tend to fall into the category of, "my [feature / application / DE / WM / OS] is better than yours" or "this [feature / application / DE / WM / OS] is [rubbish / excellent]"

Often, if in the Cafe, they can be re-routed to Recurring Discussions, where the mods try and allow some debate to continue, but when the posts turn into a flurry of insults, they are essentially completely derailed, and salvaging them is impossible without at least a transient break.

Punitive measures against those causing the problems to stop them posting or banning them after a single such episode is often unnecessarily harsh. It is easy to get carried away in cyberspace, since interpretation of written language is often a lot more susceptible to personal demeanor; statements of personal opinion are often viewed as personal attacks.  This results in the "s/he started it" syndrome.

While the mods' actions can seem like the thread is just being closed, there will be a number of warnings / infraction points issued that fighting members will accrue (in private).  This means that only repeat offenders end up being banned, rather than after a single episode of losing your temper.

If reports come in soon enough, such that the mods can see and intervene early, complete derailment can be avoided.  However, once a further 10 off-topic posts have appeared from a number of different posters, clearing away the offending posts is essentially impossible.

The mods hate locked threads too, but there are some situations where there is no other reasonable solution, than to lock the thread and start again (if there is still genuine interest in continuing).

### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-11-23
> **The Funkbomb said:**
> The only people who suffer are the ones who try to play by the rules.  The ones who don't really don't care about the threads they crap on.

As I explained above, if someone does that, report them immediately.

While mods contribute their own time, because of the number of different international locations we are in, there is often at least 1 or 2 online at any time.

If we get the reports as they happen, we can help keep the thread on topic.  The longer it heads off-road, the harder it is to return.

There are some exceptions, where the mods (as a group) will temporarily close if they think the OP is deliberately trolling etc, and, if agreed, the thread will remain locked.

As for the contentious issues re: opensource (e.g. RMS, GPL etc), where opinions are highly charged, the mods try our best to allow them, particularly when the discussion centres around recent news.  Tweaking the system sounds like a nice idea, but there is no satisfactory solution.

The infractions for "obvious jokes" are a good example...  "Obvious" is difficult to define in the often brief written word, where facial expressions don't help guide context, and the "smilies" can often make things worse.  The international readership and community here that we are proud of also means that an "obvious" joke is not so obvious to others.  While the mods are not deliberately trying to stifle "fun" (as evidenced by the flurry of activity in the Cafe Games), any posts deemed to have the potential to inflame and derail a thread are dealt with swiftly, *before* the results are seen, in order to achieve what I explained before: maintaining threads and discussions on topic.

Catch 22... Intervene early, potentially *too* early with an infraction to ensure the smooth running of a thread vs penalising what may turn out to be a benign joke.  The mods try our best to use common sense, the Code of Conduct, and (private) discussion with each other to keep things in order.

I believe it is unreasonable to ask for more; the fact that a forum as busy as this has more sensible threads than trolled, derailed or general spam is pretty amazing.  If you feel differently, then that is a shame if you feel compelled to leave, but this forum has been successfully providing technical support alongside community discussion for a few years now, and will be maintained in a fashion to ensure this continues indefinitely.
