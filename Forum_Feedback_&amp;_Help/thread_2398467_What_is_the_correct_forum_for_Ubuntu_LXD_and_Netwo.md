Excellent question, since containers are NOT virtualization. It is a common mistake.
But Containers are what Juju uses (possibly even LXD containers), but Juju is less for home users and more for Cloudy deployments with lots of point-n-click.

LXD is different from most container deployments, in that most contains don't include anything inside other than the 1 program that is desired to provide a service, nothing more. No shell. definitely no ssh. LXD deployments are often performed in very similar ways to how a virtual machine deployment might be done.

Really, a separate "Linux Containers" subforum would be my suggestion - LXD, LXC, Docker and other cname-based solutions would fit there.

Good question.  Perhaps the moderators will get together and chat a little about this.
