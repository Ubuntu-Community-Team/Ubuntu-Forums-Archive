### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-08-26
One shouldn't mistake a low bean count for limited knowledge or a lack of comprehension.

For example: one can easily increase bean count by simply posting a thread or  responding to one in the T&E. Often, no extended knowledge is  necessary - as many of the posts in that section are opinion based. If  one's only interest is increasing their bean count, all one need do is  post in that section. When put in that perspective, bean count becomes  almost irrelevant. As stated in this [sticky]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=239560").
> Bottom line: it just a trivial, whimsically amusing little thing. Don't  read too much into it...it really isn't worth the slightest emotional  involvement.

There's no reason to believe that any member (new or long term) has  more value than any other member, based on beans. It could be deceptive and misinterpreted if used as a method of ranking. 

Just my $0.02
