### Post by mssever on 2008-04-21
Congrats on the upgrade. I realize that there are still changes coming, but I wanted to point out a bug in the new theme. Since I didn't see it addressed in the FAQ, I thought I'd post it here.

Many form controls, such as buttons, drop-down menus, and <input type="text" /> boxes are less than optimal. First, they're hard to distinguish, since the contrast between the button/whatever and the page background is too low. Second, because they don't follow the standard widget design pattern, they're not immediately recognizable as what they are. For example, buttons nearly universally have a 3D raised effect, but the buttons here are flat. Text boxes nearly universally have an inset effect; here, they're outset, which makes them liik like something other than what they are.

Usability expert Jakob Neilson (useit.com) frequently advises against using nonstandard controls, because they often confuse people. Since I'm a regular here, I imagine that I'll get used to the nonstandard controls fairly quickly. But what about people who are new around here or only occasional visitors?
