### Post by coffeecat on 2013-12-15
> **Mark de J said:**
> 
P.S. Is it also true that you need 10+ posts before you can post links, otherwise the http:// will be changed to h**p://?[/I]

New members can post links in the body of a post with their first post. Some forums restrict this because of spammers, but we prefer to catch them in the act, so to speak, and ban them. Visible hyperlinks make it easier to catch the spammers, and our membership is very good at picking up the spam posts early and reporting them - for which many thanks.

The 10-post restriction (originally 50) was introduced a couple of years ago because of signature spam, which is much more of an insidious problem, and a very big problem then. Spammers would sign up, spam their links in their profile signatures, but not post anything. The only way of detecting these was for forum staff to laboriously trawl through the profiles of new members. You might ask, was this really a problem? Yes it was. Search engine crawler bots can find profiles as easily as most things on this forum and they index the links, thus achieving what the spammers want - search-engine optimisation of their target sites. This is more valuable to them than selling direct to the people who read spam posts - and an abuse of forum facilities.
