### Post by _duncan_ on 2010-05-28
IMHO, T&E section does not exist on its own. It is part and parcel of this tech support site we call ubuntuforums.org. As such, rules, guidelines and expected behavior / conduct from participants should be uniform across all sections.

Take a look around this forum, especially in the resolution center. You will see plenty of posts from admins / moderators advising people to take a day off or two to avoid posting inflammatory messages while under an emotional state.

Such conduct should also be expected from posters at the T&E section. Well thought-out testimonials, whether positive or negative, are always welcome. But to open a section for frustrated users to vent seems contradictory to expected conduct elsewhere in the forum.

My suggestion is to make the T&E section resemble the Tutorials & Tips section. Posters will have to submit their testimonials for approval so that moderators will have a chance to weed out rants, misinformation, flamebaits, foul language, etc, or to re-classify the post as a tech support post depending on the content.

An approved testimonial will then be published as a read-only thread in the T&E section. The approving moderator can have the option to add a supplementary post to the thread prior to publication to thank the poster, or qualify inaccurate statements in the original testimonial.
