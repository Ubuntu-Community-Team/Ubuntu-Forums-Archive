### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-06-03
I believe the basis of this thread is fundamentally linked to  entitlement. The forums are not a dedicated branch of Canonical support  (it's all voluntary). Yet, I believe some users who join may be under  that misconception (I'm well aware of the signup info, just wonder how many actually read it). They may even feel deceived, should assistance not  meet with their expectations or be provided in a timely manner.

One issue I've noticed with requesters, is the fact  that they often leave out critical information. Which mandates that  assisting members must request said information. Which can be  extremely time consuming. Additionally, the ongoing use of statements  similar to "if I don't receive immediate assistance, I'll have to  return to Windows". These types of threats may alienate some members who  frequently provide support in those sections. I've read comments  (historical) on the forums that indicate some members don't appreciate  statements that impart a sense of entitlement or threats. Choosing to  simply Ignoring said threads.

I like to offer a potential solution. Instead of requester being given an  open box to fill in, they fill out an Informational Assistance Request  Form. The form could be designed to extract critical system information  and specific problem description(s). Which could solve a plethora of  problems.

Instead of a requester writing a diatribe, inputting their opinion or posting threats of  returning to Windows, the form would target only the problem, system  info and symptoms. This could also help in determining which  section/subsection of the support sections the request best fit. Another  possible benefit is the tailoring of requests to a support members  assistance level. By searching certain form criteria, support members  could reduce/eliminate the viewing of threads that are beyond their  abilities.

All to often one must read a dissertation just to identify the problem.  It's unnecessary and IMHO a waste of time. A small group of members  could easily generate a number of forms and present them to the staff  for approval. Implementation and integration of said form would  obviously be a significant change, but if  done correctly could really  simplify and enhance the support process.

Just a thought.
