### Post by dcstar on 2010-04-17
I believe that the title of the "General Help" forum needs renaming.

Currently there are many posts from people who seem to believe that "General Help" means "General Help" in just about anything. Posts appear relating to topics that have nothing whatsoever to do with the actual purpose of this particular forum - which is clearly stated on the front page:

**All your general support questions for Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Edubuntu and Xubuntu.**

The end result of this is that people either waste their own time by posting inappropriate things that have little chance of ever getting a useful answer, or waste the time of people who try to help genuine Ubuntu users with problems by clogging up the forum with posts that should just not be there.

Either situation will end up if frustration for all concerned and steps should be taken to minimise this poor situation.

Renaming the forum would be a good first step, as it would clarify the actual purpose of the forum for those who obviously do not currently understand what it is for.

I suggest something like "General Ubuntu Help" or even "General Ubuntu User Help" and also put a prominent message on the home page of the forum requesting that people please look for a more specific forum for their issue before posting.

Such steps should result in more issues being placed in a forum where they have a better chance of being addressed by people with the relevant knowledge and experience than happens now - which must be a better outcome for the people reporting any issue as well as those that try to help with these issues.
