### Post by PacSci on 2009-05-17
In my opinion, the Desktop Environments forum is incredibly cluttered, with the GNOME, KDE, and Xfce topics all combined into one. Though some of the problems are common to all of the DEs, those problems would probably be better in General Help or another section. My suggestion is that to split the DEs forum into GNOME Questions, KDE Questions, Xfce Questions, and Other Desktop Environments.

The first advantage this has is usability - people who are really having a problem with a DE would probably recognize a forum with its name more easily than a generic problem.

The second is targeting support. I regularly use Xfce and also GNOME, but many of the DEs forum questions are about KDE, making it a bit hard to focus. If there were separate GNOME, KDE, and Xfce forums, all of the KDE gurus (for example) would know where to hang out to troubleshoot KDE problems.

This is NOT a suggestion to subdivide the forums into Ubuntu, Kubuntu, and Xubuntu sections at the root level - just the separation of problems with specific desktop environments.
