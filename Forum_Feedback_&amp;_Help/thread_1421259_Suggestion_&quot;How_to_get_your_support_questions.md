### Post by undecim on 2010-03-04
I think that there should be a page/sticky on the forums that gives new users guidelines on how to post their questions so that they get answered quickly.

If possible, when the "New Thread" button is clicked in a support forum, there should be either a small page before the "Post New Thread" page or a banner on the page with text to the effect of "To learn how to get your support questions get answered quickly, Click Here" which links to the help page.

Guidelines that should be included:

[LIST=1]
[*]Search the forums for your problem fist. (With a link to the search page)
[*]Make the title of your thread meaningful and concise.
[*]In your post, indicate your level of experience with Ubuntu and with the topic of your problem.
[*]Provide information relevant to your problem. (error messages, hardware, etc)
[*]Check back on your thread often and answer any questions about your problem. (Or better yet, subscribe to the thread to get email notification when someone replies to your thread)
[*]Wait 24 hours before bumping
[*]When your problem is solved, mark your thread as [solved] and thank those who helped you fix your problem.
[/LIST]

Obviously, these need to be elaborated upon.

Other sections might include a list of files/information to include for various types of problems (or maybe links to scripts to gather and package that information into one .tar.gz file?), links to the various support forums and the search page.
