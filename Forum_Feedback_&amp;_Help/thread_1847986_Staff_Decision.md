### Post by Dragonbite on 2011-09-22
> **forestpiskie said:**

And that's out of 12 people! ;)

Don't get me wrong, I understand it is a lot easier for me to say this than it is to implement it, I think considering the nature of Ubuntu and the open source community, that this discussion should be done transparently.  Like a demonstration of how open source works (not in the technical sense).

This doesn't mean you have to give up your powers to the "mob". The community is at least able to see what is happening, have a means to voice their concern and ideas and the ability to offer up their own services (volunteer time). The Forum staff ultimately are the ones to decide one way or the other as they are the ones that not only have to handle it, but are handling it now and have (mertiocracy) seniority.

I think this would benefit the community, thus Ubuntu, in a couple of ways.  

[LIST]
[*]First, people interested in this will get a chance to say what they think and feel. There may be ideas the Forum staff hadn't considered, and it gives the staff a feel to what the community thinks.

Alternatively, the staff would then be able to inform these people who obviously have an interest, their reasoning behind things.

Ultimately the staff has the meritocracy position (since handling now and whatever is decided upon) but can benefit from new ideas, get a feel for what the community wants, and the ability to stop negative posts by people because they are being listened to.

Like I listen to my kids when they have an idea or concern, but that doesn't mean I am forced to act upon it or give in to their requests/demands/tantrums.  I can tell them "why", and ultimately our (wife and I) decision is final.
 

[*]Second it would build a reputation of Ubuntu being open.  Recent changes in Ubuntu have left some people feel like Ubuntu is loosing it's "openness" and people are just "sheeple" to be passed from one release to the next.


[*]Last, and possible most important, it would offer a visible demonstration of how an open (source) discussion can be run, what one can expect, and what goes into a discussion.  This may pay off in dividend as they take these ideals to their own scenarios whether it is at home, a family organization (PTO?), and even work.
[/LIST]

Just my thoughts.

This takes "open source" out from being just technologically orientated and places it into an organizational or business model position.  If you want to read more on "open source" in non-technical applications then I would recommend the Red Hat sponsored site [[COLOR="Blue"]_opensource.com_[/COLOR]]("http://opensource.com/").

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-09-22
> Joeb454
If you have any input, please feel free to share it.

I seriously doubt that the staff doesn't already know my opinion. But just in case. :D

1. I would support the RC concept or just make the section "Read Only".
2. Alter the sectional guidelines to identify what type of communications are acceptable, if any.
3. Require a minimum number of beans before one can start a thread or post in that section. Much like the implementation of the 50 bean rule to minimize spam. Many argumentative threads in that section are started by a member having few beans. This may reduce the number of troll threads/posts.

This issue has been discussed before in the FFH. The solution (at that time) was to implement a T&E team. In hindsight, that seemed to be somewhat effective - for a relatively brief period of time.

The T&E states it's purpose, as to provide a place "for users to post their reviews of Ubuntu". IMHO the reality is the section lends itself nicely to debating, trolling, aggressive posturing and reactionary (often demeaning) responses. Even though the sectional guidelines state that it's not to be used for debating. I've read a least one staff member's post that indicated they knowingly permit some debate in that section. Doesn't that seem disingenuous based on the sectional guidelines? No, I will not identify the member.

I actually believe that the T&E has the potential to be a very beneficial section. The question is one of how does the community wish the T&E to be perceived by members or even passive viewers? I doubt many would argue that the section is often inundated with debate and posturing. What message does that send and what purpose does it serve? The sharing of experiences can be beneficial (assuming there's an interest in feedback) and IMO it's important to provide a section for members to vent - without responses. It's also my opinion that the responses are often that which initiates the debates. 

If it's just about numbers in the T&E. Might I suggest the forums simply change the guidelines and advise members to be prepared for confrontation. Should anyone need examples of my opinions, just scrutinize a number of historical threads in that section. One might also note the number of recently closed threads.

Thank you Joeb454 for the opportunity to provide feedback.
