### Post by Perfect Storm on 2009-03-22
To get better help this is what you can do;

1) An Accurate Thread Title
[size=1]Describe the problem in one short line. A precise Title will attract the right people to solve the problem. Avoid words like "help", "newbie", "I'm new" etc.[/size]

2) Use Tags 
[size=1]Tags are usually used for search purpose, but it can also be a help for the people who's here to help you, to spot which problems this thread is about. Keep the tags to max. 3-4 words. Too many is confusing.[/size]

3) Use Proper Language
[size=1]No need for l33t, emotionel or swearing and cursing. Do not insult or trash the people who are trying to help you, even if it's frustrating and the first help you get didn't help.[/size]

4) Wall Of Text
[size=1]Avoid this at all cost. When people see a post without <full stops> and the text is written without empty lines in between, people tend to skip the thread. It hurts the eyes to read.[/size]

5) Describe The Problem
[size=1]Describe the problem with all the information you have. What you did before it happen, what you did when the problem show up, and what you have done yourself to solve the problem. If possible give terminal output - this will give the helpers a very accurate information or hints what's going on.[/size]

6) To Bump Or Not
[size=1]Do not bump a thread within the first 24 hours. People find it annoying and tend to ignore a person who bump a thread too quick and often. Also we have people who use our "search threads that don't have replies" option, and bumped threads won't be seen.[/size]


Hope this help :KS
