I'm surprised that a thread titled to at least somewhat suggest it had no content per se, but was purely for testing, and beginning with an explanation that clearly stated that, should attract so much interest. I expected merely to be talking to myself here, to get a better understanding of how the forum works. As stated above, I won't be testing anything here again, but I'll share what I determined in case anyone is interested:

If javascript is inactive in your browser, the forum will eat your line-feeds and run all your paragraphs together. That means if you use NoScript in Firefox for instance, it needs to be set to allow javascript on this site. Furthermore, the change does not take place immediately on enabling javascript, which can be confusing. It didn't change for me until I closed the browser and reopened it. 

The forum will eat your tabs in any case, javascript or no. So if you habitually set off your paragraphs with indentation, you must use the space bar, not the tab key.  I don't believe this is a javascript issue. Maybe it can be fixed in the UCP if you care about it.

The forum can also do strange things to quote marks, turning them into something else.  I THINK this is also a javascript issue, just like the deleted new-lines but I didn't finish testing on the quotes and haven't turned javascript back off to confirm that by replicating the error.

If for some reason you want to block javascript on this site, but still not have your line-feeds and tabs eaten or your quote marks mangled, I'd suggest you look at Cariboo and Bodh.Zazen's suggestions, for they are pretty bright fellows. For my purposes, now that I understand the problem, it is sufficient simply to adjust the settings of NoScript.  Perhaps this information can be of use to someone.
