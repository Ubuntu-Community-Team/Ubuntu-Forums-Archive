### Post by Anduu on 2008-05-21
In case you don't know what "cruft" is [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cruft](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cruft)

Maybe it is just me but the forums are getting to be a real mess.If it is getting difficult for me to find the answer to a problem I can't imagine what it is like for a new user to find the information he/she needs.There is so much outdated/duplicate/useless information floating around it is like finding the proverbial needle in a haystack sometimes.

Forum users need to try harder to to keep the mess down.Here are some examples of what I am talking about.

[LIST=1]
[*]I still see the same questions being asked over and over and over again.There is still a search function and a lot of people still refuse to use it.Why? [http://websearch.about.com/b/2008/04/30/how-to-effectively-use-a-search-engine.htm](http://websearch.about.com/b/2008/04/30/how-to-effectively-use-a-search-engine.htm)

[*]Something that really annoys me...A user will state that application a isn't working for them.Half a dozen responders will say "well why don't you use application b,c,d or e"...Completely useless.If the OP found application a to do task x then they most likely found references to apps b,c,d and e but have chosen app a.How about we try to solve the problem at hand and if that doesn't work we then suggest alternatives?

[*]"Me too" threads.Someone states they have a problem.A dozen people chime in saying "Me too".All this results in is a mile long thread and if there happens to be a solution in there somewhere it can easily get overlooked.If more than one person is experiencing the same problem a bug report needs to be filed rather than cluttering up the thread.

[*]"My way is better" threads.Someone will post a solution to someones problem and others will chime in on their way of doing it.If the first solution works leave it at that.As an example; someone will suggest a fix using the gui but someone always has to counter with their command-line version.

[*]People who have no idea what they are talking about chiming in on a thread.Don't.
[/LIST]

I can think of many other examples but you get the idea.I am mainly posting this out of concern for the ease of use of the forums for new users.**We** are the definitive source of information for all things Ubuntu.If information isn't easy enough to find new users aren't going to be users for very long.

If everyone would try a little harder it would make a world of difference.

### Post by Dr Small on 2008-05-21
The real problem is, Ubuntu is the "newbie gateway to linux". So everyday (and much more, every 6 months) we get new users, and instead of using their heads, their use their fingers to type the question and expect a response. This leads to, as you said, alot of duplicate threads.

With Arch, users are expected to read the Wiki, and if the have any questions, to read the Wiki. All information and questions are generally answered on the wiki, and users generally don't ask noobish questions on the forums.

That's just the Arch way. Ubuntu is different though.
