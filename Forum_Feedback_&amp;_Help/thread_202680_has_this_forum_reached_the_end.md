### Post by aysiu on 2006-06-23
There may not be new topics, but there may be new twists to old problems or new ways that old solutions need to be explained for *different users*.

I've become (over the past year) quite the pro at Google-searching the answers to Ubuntu problems, but for new users, this can be a daunting process, and they have no way of telling what's a legitimate solution and what's a terrible solution.

A forum allows the user to ask questions, get clarification, and get validation about different solutions.

And, yes, every now and then, a new problem arises (for example, the plain-text password in Breezy).

I agree. 

As a user who's been here since Warty I've observed that as Ubuntu got "bigger" the signal to noise ratio unfortunately went down, in all the forums, not just Ubuntu Cafe. Actually I'd be surprised if it didn't.

What to do to aid the situation in Ubuntu Cafe? Here's a snobbish recipe off the top of my head; flames welcome to /dev/null :

[SIZE="3"]**-**[/SIZE] Search before you post. The golden rule. Please.

[SIZE="3"]**-** [/SIZE]Don't post unless you're contributing something of some discernible value. To put it bluntly, I don't want to read someone's superficial two hour impressions on Klackware 13 or Cedora Fore 9 or AIXYZGL five times a week, or someone's ill informed opinions on "the future of Linux", or a newcomer's demand for "easier to use blah blah" based on an ill definition of ease of use and complete ignorance of FOSS / *nix fundamentals, all followed by five pages of topic jumping discussion. Do some research, get informed, put some energy and genuine thought into what you write, and if you can't be bothered, don't post. 

[SIZE="3"]**-**[/SIZE] Keep on topic. Please. If you must deviate within the thread, state you're doing so, and state that you don't want replies. If you keep getting replies, tell the repliers to start a new thread if interested. If you have to address a particular post, reply to that post, not just whichever post is the last one, so that context won't be lost in the threaded view mode. 

[SIZE="3"]**-**[/SIZE] Keep completely personal matters to yourself. I don't care how much you love your new Core Duo laptop and I don't want to read about it on a thread about Core Duo technology unless you're saying something useful or valuable or witty along with it. 

[SIZE="3"]**-** [/SIZE]When posting a link to a blog post, Slashdot article etc. that you want to trigger discussion about, sum it up in a few sentences in your first post to give people some context before reading it.

[SIZE="3"]**-** [/SIZE]Don't attack an OS or another piece of software or its users or authors; try criticizing them instead. Don't be childish. If you think the software "sucks", try to analyze why it sucks and break down the reasons that make it suck into pieces and list them one by one. If you can't be bothered, again, don't post. 

It's been on my mind for a while and the subject just happened to pop up, so I felt like letting go; I know the tone is harsh but so is the matter at hand, so no excuses.
