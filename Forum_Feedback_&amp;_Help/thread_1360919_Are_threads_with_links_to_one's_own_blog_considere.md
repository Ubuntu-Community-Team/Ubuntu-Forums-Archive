Of course this forum, like all such sites, is full of blog links.  I'm just mildly perturbed about when it becomes spam.

If someone in a help forum needs the aid of my super-duper tutorial, I'll link to it without a second thought.  I'll also link to other blogs that may help.  But that's a support thread.

It's even considered proper to put links to your own site in your sig (despite the fact that this distorts Google's page rankings beyond all recognition).  That's accepted.  Fine.

In the Cafe, it's okay to link to blogs written by other people, for reasons of discussion.  Fine.

But is it okay for me to link to my own blog for discussion purposes?  Do I have to actually start off the discussion?  How about if I just post the link, an leave discussion to others?  How about if I post several links a day, all in different threads?  All in one thread?  How about if I have Google Ads or similar on my blog?  Is it okay for me to drive traffic towards it then?  Can I advertise here, so long as I don't blatantly call it an ad?

Or how about if I *don't* post the link - instead I just quote my article in its entirety?  That way, I am genuinely posting here just for discussion purposes: the lack of a link demonstrating that I'm definitely *not* looking to drive traffic towards my blog?  Is that a fair compromise?  Is there a need for compromise?  Will Ubuntuforums mind if I make a few quid out of my fellow members?  After all, we're all here to help each other.  Right?
