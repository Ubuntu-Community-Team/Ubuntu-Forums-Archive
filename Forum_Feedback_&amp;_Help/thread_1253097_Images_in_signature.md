### Post by schauerlich on 2009-08-30
On a personal level:
I hate images in sigs. It's annoying. It breaks up the content of the page, and it makes it harder to distinguish posts. Anything taller than 20 pixels would irritate me to no end.

On a technical level:
Considering how limited resources already are (although the forums have been much more stable lately - we'll see if that lasts through October) enabling images in signatures would not only be a huge storage problem (only a few kilobytes per image... but multiplied by 20,000 (that assumes 1/3 of the currently active members put an image in their sig) it can be an issue. The second issue is bandwidth. Moving that many images around, every time a page loads, can be quite a pull on the server.

The two arguments against such images in sig are :

1. They are distracting. Many people feel they are clutter. Sure we allow them for staff, it is one of the few perks they get for their time and dedication to the community.

We would like to keep the forums accessible to all =)
