### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-09-01
> Martin Rabson
Sarcasm is a killer for new users, experienced Linux users MUST resist the temptation to belittle questions from new users. 

In defense of the members assisting in the help sections, I've seen very little sarcasm expressed in those sections. Especially, towards new users. Most members that provide assistance in those sections are extremely aware of the issues faced by new users and make every effort to assure they understand any recommendations or instructions. Of course, occasionally some get frustrated. 

I've also witnessed the "I'm a Windows guru" new user philosophy. Which I believe can be an obstacle when attempting to provide help. These individuals often endeavor to compare Windows to Ubuntu, rather than focusing on the problem. I think it's normal to try and relate based on ones own experiences, except when the need to learn interferes. 

One issue I've found while in the help sections, is the lack of information provided by new users and/or their level of competency when requesting support. One simple solution is to provide support requesters with a simple form that helps them define their problem(s), system information and to describe their computer competency. A significant amount of time is "wasted" with multiple posts in extracting and sharing information.

A fill in the blank/check box form would reduce or potentially even eliminate the need for multiple informational request posts. Not to mention, that the information collected from said forms could actually be used to create a DB of solutions.

In the future, when a new user has a problem, the first place to look would be the DB of resolved issues - acquired from the forms. I suspect this may even be an effective solution to the repetitive Q&A's that takes place in the help sections regularly.

Just my $0.02
