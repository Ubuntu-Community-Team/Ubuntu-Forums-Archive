So when someone comes in and all they know is "my wireless internet isn't working", they may get presented with "x" posts.  They check the first post, and as they read, they see things like "lspci -C network", "lsusb", "lshw -C network", and such returned questions as "what type of wireless adapter do you have?".  And what goes through their mind?  "I don't know what they are talking about - I better just list my problem [in actuality usually the symptoms of a problem they don't know] and maybe someone can help me".  These search extension that show like that can sometimes be overwhelming to someone new to Linux.  With more and more emphasis being placed on providing a more "generic" open source desktop environment, more and more "typical" Windows users are trying Ubuntu.  By "typical" Windows user I don't mean technical experts - I mean the normal Joe off the streets that wants to just surf the net and read email, the businessman who doesn't need or care to know the technical details but uses his computer for office like and other commmon tasks.  These people don't know what the problem is.  They don't know the jargon.  They simply know "this doesn't work and I need help", and those people are not going to follow the links showing possible solutions at the time they post their thread - because they simply want a solution and don't want to read through what sometimes becomes quite technical discussions.

This also brings me back to my original post on keeping multiple threads of similar title, etc..  One such thread may be a simple how-to the particular user can follow.  Another, with the same simple sounding title, may actually get quite technical from the start - "if you are using a Broadcom chipset", "if you are using a Railtec chipset" - these are the things the lose the user and makes them more confused, and the EXACT reason why they should open their own thread.

I believe such things as showing possible threads at the time you create a thread are there for someone other than the first time novice (first time to each problem that is, not first time to Linux or Ubuntu) - someone who has a better understanding of what is probably wrong and is willing to take the time to read.

I hope I'm not interpreting your posts wrong, and please understand I'm just commenting, not flaming you or your ideas.  I get the feeling that you genuinely care and want the simplest way possible for someone using the forum to find their solution.  This is what makes the Absolute Beginner Forum different from most others - most forums people go to having a pretty good idea that they (1) understand their problem, not just it's symptoms and (2) know the question to ask.  The ABF is a big exception to that - these "duplicate" posts are usually just a little different, and just enough to require a different response.  Sometimes you find a poster who via their responses and add-on questions make it clear they understand things about computers in general better, and the response you give that person is completely different from the response you give someone such as in the 2 samples I gave above.  Software cannot detect that difference, so it simply cannot make a decision that 2 items with the same or similar titles are the same - only a human can make that distinction.  I used to be a systems administrator and systems programmer on everything from large scale computers down to micros (what we called PC's back then when they were new), and I can tell you I found all kinds of ways to program editors, etc., to do very specific tasks such as program conversion from one platform to another completely different platform, that required a minimal amount of actual human interaction. ?No forum software can do what you want - a human would need to.  Then we get into the subjective areas of what should a title be, in what group does a question belong.  These decisions would be made by a human, and we all have different ideas on sorting and cataloging data - which means my groupings and titles would most certainly be different from someone else, and hence the inheritance of difficulty of search for someone with just the very basics of knowledge.

So, these are good ideas - I won't fault you for them, and in fact respect that you perceive a problem and want to work on finding a solution - a true Ubuntu fan if there ever was one!  It's just my opinion that the ABF is the one forum you will find that just doesn't give itself to what one might think are standard ideas and in particular to standardization of title, grouping, etc. - it's just a very different animal, and we just deal with it differently.

Again - I don't mean to imply any disagreement with you, your intentions or your ideas!!  This type of open, non-judgemental, non-flaming, friendly discussion is what helps to make Ubuntu an even stronger product. ### Post by anewguy on 2009-03-03
I guess I'll just jump in here with a couple more comments.

As a person searching for information, yes you'd like to see things standardized and catagorized.  Indeed, as gackt mentioned, back when I was working we also had online help for everyone because of the costs involved in answering each question individually.  It was sorted and catagorized by us tech people, so of course we could pick the titles we wanted people to search by.

This forum is so much different.  Most people come here initially because they are new and have no clue.  Almost every problem is slightly different, and the answer needs to be correspondingly different.  Standardization of titles, grouping of similar questions, etc., all sounds good, but it requires 2 things:

(1) EVERYONE who would come to this forum would need to know a title to search by fairly closely. This is not realistic in a world of people new to an OS who have symptoms but no clear idea of what the problem is.  Their description of their symptoms would be different from mine, and probably different from anyone else's, because we are human and all have our own unique way of "seeing" things and wording things.

(2) It would require a lot of manual intervention.  Keep in mind that when I was working I was being paid to make things the best they could be.  This forum is all volunteers, all of varying skill sets and skill levels.  Some spend their time watching this forum for questions they think they might be able to answer.  Others, like myself, stop by every so often and check the first page or so of the forum.  If there is something I think I might know the answer to, or a suggestion to offer, then I will do so.
