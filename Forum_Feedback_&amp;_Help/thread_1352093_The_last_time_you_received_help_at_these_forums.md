### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-12-11
Having provided help to folks LOTS of times, I don't share in the perception that folks don't get help here ... but ... if I were to run stats on recent posts, my guess would be that the solution rate has fallen way off.

My best guess is that this is due to the following:
1) not OUR OS problems: Lots and lots of Win7-related issues.  What part of "Ubuntu Forums" do folks NOT understand?  While some of us (including me) DO use Win7 and do offer some support, we're not the definitive source of MS Windows support.  For that, folks should use TechNet.
2) Unwilling to search for solutions.  In many cases, a 30-second search in the CORRECT forum would have yielded results, but folks seem unwilling to do that.  Which means, those of use reading the video forum or hardware forum don't see the posts in the other forums.
3) Recent introduction of Ubuntu/Linux changes that seem to have significantly increased the problem rate.  The combination of Win7, Wubi, Ubuntu 9.10, and GRUB2 seems to be a really bad one that is repeatedly mentioned.

And last, but not least, the GNU/Linux community has, and hopefully always will be, one geared toward self-help.  We can 't fix your machine for you; all we can do is point out some things you can try that might help YOU fix it.  As the community becomes more saturation with the MS Windows point-and-click crowd, the percentage of folks unwilling to learn how to diagnose and repair their machines grows enormously.

Seen a number of examples where folks ask for step-by-step instructions, we point them to a tutorial, and they come back complaining that it's too hard for them to understand.

And, finally, for a LONG time, the [SOLVED] feature was disabled in these forums, so anyone using that to get an idea of the solution rate was being mislead.  Even now, I see thread after thread that was solved and no-one bothered to mark it as such.

OK... rant over.
