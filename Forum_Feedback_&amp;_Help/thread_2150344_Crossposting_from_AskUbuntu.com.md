### Post by vasa1 on 2013-06-01
It's not uncommon for questions (asked anywhere) to go unanswered :(

From what I understand, the Stack Exchange sites don't favor "bumping" and so a question can become "invisible" quite soon. People there suggest editing the question to provide more information instead to bump it to the front again but what if the poster has done a thorough job in the first place?

The next option is to offer a bounty after a certain time has elapsed. Doing so puts the question on a featured short list for some days. The only drawback is that if you don't get a satisfactory answer, the bounty is deducted from your "rep" in any case.

Another option which I haven't seen advised anywhere is to delete the unanswered question from Ask Ubuntu and then to post it in unix.stackexchange.com, my first preference given the nature of the question, or in superusers.com . You will lose upvotes that your question may have attracted but let's assume we're more interested in the answer :)

Once you've deleted the question from Ask Ubuntu,  I feel you're free to post where you like.
