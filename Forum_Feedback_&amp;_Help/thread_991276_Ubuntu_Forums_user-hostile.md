### Post by Frogbarf on 2008-11-23
I've been trying to resolve problems relating to a usb external HD formatted FAT32. I asked about it over in the Craigslist linux forum, and the folks who responded helped me overcome about half the hurdles I face.

**It was very striking how much easier it was to use the CL forum than these Ubuntu forums!** The experience has made enough of an impression on me that I want to summarize it, in the hopes that Canonical will re-think how the forums are set up.

First of all, a thread on a CL forum is much more compact than here. Each message takes only a single line that shows the subject and author, so it is very easy to scan a thread visually. While the Ubuntu forums *look* beautiful, they have far too much white space, plus the quoting of text in all messages in a thread makes a thread take up much more room tham necessary, making it very difficult to quickly scan and understand.

This is very closely related to the usability guideline that deprecates webpages that require scrolling.

The Ubuntu forums in their present incarnation exemplify a GUI where looks has trumped usability.

Second, the Ubuntu forums use far too many obscure icons, pop-up lists, tiresome animated smilies and similar toys. As I'm typing, I'm looking at the editing icons and asking "which ones of these are instantly recognizable"? Of them, only these meet that description: "fonts" and "sizes"; embolding, italicization, and underlining; alignment; ordered and unordered lists. (Though the use of the last two isn't obvious.)

The rest of the icons, I'm sorry to say, simply do not communicate their meaning instantly. Sure, you can mouse over them and see what the pop up title says, but if that's necessary, then the icon design is a failure. The whole point of icons is instant comprehension. There are other icons sprinkled hither, thither, and yon on the Ubuntu forum pages that are equally obscure.

At the same time, some of it's done "right". The buttons for "check if already posted", "submit new thread", and "preview post" are simply labelled in plain English instead of being iconized. Kudos to whoever did that.

Third, every time I need to delete a message, I end up having to ask how to do it; likewise for marking a thread "solved". I'm sure I'm not alone in only using these forums when I have trouble, which is fortunately fairly rare. In the interval between one use and another, it's easy to forget the little details about how it works. A lot of the design may have been predicated on users turning to the forums frequently

To summarize, the usability of the Ubuntu forums would be greatly improved by:[LIST=1]
[*]having a very compact thread display, message subjects only
[*]replacing most editing icons with words; an icon you have to mouse over for a verbal explanation is not a useful icon. Better to just use the words.
[*]getting rid of the smilies; they're old hat now and no longer amusing on any system.
[*]putting "delete message" and "mark thread solved" buttons on messages belonging to the user, the latter if he started the thread.
[/LIST]

Wasn't it Walter Gropius who uttered the famous dictum "form follows function"? At present, the Ubuntu forums do NOT follow that dictum at all well!
