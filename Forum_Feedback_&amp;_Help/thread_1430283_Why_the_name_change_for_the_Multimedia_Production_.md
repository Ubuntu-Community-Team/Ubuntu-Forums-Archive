### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-04-02
I agree with the very well made points above, and thought I could add some evidence for why the previous name was preferable.
Firstly a lot of new (and some old I imagine) users do not know what Ubuntu Studio is (either the distro itself or what is supposed to come under the forum heading) and so post audio/video production questions into multimedia and video, where they are buried under a much higher quantity of posts about a broad range of topics. By inadvertently shifting prospective posts from a relatively quiet forum and into a busy one you are losing the opportunity for solving users problems. Examples:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1445319](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1445319)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1441312](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1441312)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1444681](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1444681)
Secondly, as mentioned above, the US forum is getting an increase in general help type questions about wireless connections and other non production issues. These either stay there or someone has to move them to the appropriate place. Examples:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1214983](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1214983)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1445034](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1445034)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1444840](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1444840)

There are better ways than this to promote Ubuntu Studio.
