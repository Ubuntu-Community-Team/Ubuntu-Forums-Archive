I don't know if the "remember me" option works so well nowadays with SSO login in use, mainly because of the activity time out limit (which seems to be about 24 hours). 

To simplify logging in here now I use the cookie managers in firefox "cookie culler" and "biscuit". Cookie culler allows me to cull unwanted cookies while biscuit allows me to preserve cookies. By using these 2 and "protecting" all of the cookies (including "session" cookies) for both ubuntuforums.org and login.ubuntu.com (the SSO login page) in both managers I have greatly simplified logging in, meaning if I do get logged out it is a matter of a few clicks and I'm back in without having to re-enter details or passwords etc.

Most of the time I stay logged into the forums but if I go more than 24 hours or my IP address changes then I have to click on the login with SSO button at the top of the forum page where I get redirected to the SSO page. As I have preserved the SSO page cookies when I get redirected there I am already logged in and can just click the ubuntuforums confirmation button and I get redirected back here. If I am impatient and don't want to wait I think it takes only 3 clicks in total and I am logged in without having to enter any user details or passwords etc. A bit complex of a method to set up if the OP is not familiar with handling/protecting cookies etc, but once set up it can streamline logging in here very much so.

If I am logged in here and without logging out I shut the browser down, next time I open the browser and open the ubuntuforums.org site I am still logged in. This as mentioned earlier depends on the 24 hour limit set in the forums cookies or if my IP address changes I am logged out, it works most of the time to keep me permanently logged in though.

Hope the OP can firstly make sense of the above description of protecting the ubuntuforums and SSO log in page cookies and if so it may help to make logging in much easier/quicker. See the attached screenshots (2 for cookie culler and 1 for biscuit) for how I have protected/preserved the cookies for such a set up. With such a set up, if in the browser I completely clear my history including cookies and active logins, I will still stay logged into ubuntuforums. Hope this helps a bit for the OP.

**Edit:** I just noted here there are two cookies/tokens also protected in the ubuntu.com settings just above the opened ubuntuforums.org cookies (but not shown "opened" in the first screenshot). If I recall correctly, (it has been quite a while since I set up these protected cookies) they are also needed for logins, but am not 100% sure, I suggest marking them as protected as well if they exist in your browser cookies.
