### Post by bilijoe on 2008-07-15
Maybe it's just me--something I'm not doing right, or something I've got set up wrong, but it seems that the search to "Check If Already Posted" needs to be looked at.  Since the forum changes, I have not had a single one of my posts show a similar or related "Already Posted" item--not one.  Before the changes, the search [almost] always turned up at least two or three existing posts, and, at least 75% or 80% of the time, one or more of them related directly enough to my pending post that it answered my question, thus making my post unnecessary, and reducing the load on the forum management system.  It also often caused me, by reading the posts that seemed close to what I had been going to post, to discover, or learn something new--something I wouldn't have been aware of, had I simply posted my own question.  Users with more experience and expertise often asked questions more intelligently, or in a broader way, that elicited a more complete answer, or that covered part of the issue I hadn't considered, which could have caused me to need to post a follow-up, which was, again, unnecessary because of having had the opportunity to read one or more posts, and their replies, already submitted.  

I get the feeling that there must be some kind of unusual difficulty (or maybe a political issue?) involved here, because when I posted similar comments, right after the forum change, it elicited some "you just hit a nerve" type responses (though, I admit, having the search crippled hit a nerve with me, as I had become so dependent on the education I was receiving from the posts I was being led to by the search results, so the tone of my post, at that time, was less than mellow.  Sorry 'bout that, and my apologies:redface:).  But being led to all those posts, related to questions I had, that others had already addressed, gave me such a significant push up the learning curve, I really felt I had been robbed of a highly valuable resource, when the search quit working.  

If it is at all possible, could someone please take a look at the current search mechanism, and at the old one, to determine if it isn't possible to get back the functionality (of actually finding things) of the old search.  I'm a retired programmer, so i know it's *possible*, but I realize there is a legitimate issue of "is it practical" to consider too.  However , I'm certain I'm not the only one who benefited from having the opportunity to read several posts/responses on topics of interest to them, and I'm sure, reading "related" posts made many new posts unnecessary, which had to have relieved the load on the system, and improved its efficiency.  Anyway, I'd really appreciate it if someone reasonably well versed in the subject of information searching and search engines could find the time to have a look at this.  I think it would benefit just about everyone.

### Post by bilijoe on 2008-07-20
I believe I have solved my own dilemma here.:)  In the "Post New Thread" window, above the Message editing box, the "Check If Already Posted" button has a drop-down menu with the items "Search Current Forum Only", and "Search Thread Titles Only" selected by default.  Apparently, this results in a rather constrained search.  

I have discovered that by unchecking both these items, I get a set of search results that is pretty much as useful as the results provided by the similar function in the pre-upgraded forum manager=D>.  This discovery has made me very happy:biggrin:, and I promise I will no longer be submitting any posts in which I grouse about the uselessness of the search for similar posts function.  My only request now would be that the defaults for these two items be changed to unchecked, rather than the current default of checked.  I hope anyone else who has been disappointed by the change in functionality of this feature sees this post, and finds the results provided with these items unchecked, as useful as I have\\:D/.  

I apologize for all my previous grumblings on this subject:redface:.
