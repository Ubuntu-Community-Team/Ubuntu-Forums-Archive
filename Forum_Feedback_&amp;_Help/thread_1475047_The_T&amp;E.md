### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-05-06
It appears that with every new release the T&E is inundated with the same posts arguing, debating and bickering over opinion(s). This section of the forum (as defined by the sectional guidelines) is specifically for:
> This space is provided for users to post their reviews of Ubuntu.When we argue or debate someone's review thread in the T&E, are we not then attempting to perform our own review of their post? Logic suggests if we wish to make a review or contribute feedback, we should start our own thread and not debate those of others.

What type of pressure does that place on members who wish to post a thread in the T&E, that may not want to debate. But only wish to share with the community their review and feedback? Where do they go on the Forums, to perform such a task?

I'd like to suggest that the staff consider permitting members to select an option of "read only" (forum software permitting) when posting a thread in the T&E. Providing members with the opportunity to post a thread in that section without the fear of coming under fire or feeling intimated. This alone might entice more members to share their experiences and give feedback. Which theoretically is the intended purpose of the T&E.

I suspect your response will involve the T&E team. But I think this suggestion presents a simple solution for opening the T&E to everyone, including those who don't wish to get involved in a debate. For those that do, simply leave the "read only" box unchecked. I have no idea if the Forums software is capable, but if so this might resolve the issue of debating in a section of the Forums that specifically states it's not for debating. I have been accused of over emphasizing the debate issue in the T&E. However, when put in perspective should one take a moment and read the T&E header guidelines - they blatantly state that the T&E is not a debating section of the Forums. Not to mention [this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1456778&page=4") post from [[COLOR=#d40000]**Ms_Angel_D**[/COLOR]]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=416115"), in that thread. Yet some members use the T&E as their own debating forum and refuse to accept the guidelines. I've watched the staff use sectional guidelines as (one of) the reason for disciplinary actions and yet many members continuously violate the T&E sectional guidelines as written. ### Post by _duncan_ on 2010-05-07
I'm indifferent to the idea of making the T&E section read-only as a whole, or allowing the OP to choose to accept comments or not. However, if we do so, I strongly recommend that the entire T&E section be excluded from forum search results. My reasoning follows:

1. Ubuntu forums as a whole is a tech support forum. Thus it is reasonable to assume that people conducting forum searches are a) looking for answers to their specific technical problems; or b) deciding whether to try out ubuntu but would like to know if certain hardware and/or applications they need could be a problem if they use ubuntu.

2. It is common to see comments like "Ubuntu does not support xxxx hardware" or "It is impossible to get xxxxxx functionality to work in ubuntu." in the T&E section. Most of the time, it is actually caused by the poster's unfamiliarity with ubuntu. Such statements are valid as testimonials, but are inherently fallacious and misleading from a technical standpoint.

3. If such testimonials are read-only, more knowledgeable users can not debunk the myths / fallacies being posted. Again, this is not a problem if the posts are taken as testimonials, but as entries to a forum search in a technical support forum?

### Post by Ms_Angel_D on 2010-05-07
For the past 6 months I have been on an personal mission to make T&E a comfortable place for persons to post their experiences, whether good or bad. 

It was then that I contacted staff(at the time I wasn't a moderator) about working on getting a handle on the situation in that section of the forums. A Team was established and we created a post which I eventually submitted for posting which the staff stickied.

T&E has cleaned up considerably in that time, I remember when I first began frequenting these forums 2 years ago, it wasn't uncommon for there to be a 30 or 40+ page thread in where you would frequently see the word "Troll", or read the phrase "Go Back To Windows".

While Making T&E Read only at first sounds like a great way to clean up the area I have to wonder if it wouldn't devalue the area. Ubuntu is about community and completely shutting down the community's ability to participate in any kind of feedback in that area in my opinion takes away from the community feeling. Some person's who post there and have bad experiences, sometimes get feedback that helps them or points out things they missed while looking for support.

I would Suggest that instead of Making T&E Read only We moderate replies to the area, hence cutting down on trollish behavior while still allowing members to give some feedback on peoples experiences. I would hate for someone to miss out on using ubuntu or simply leave because they were frustrated and walked away which no clue that their issue was just a few commands away from being fixed.

Angel
