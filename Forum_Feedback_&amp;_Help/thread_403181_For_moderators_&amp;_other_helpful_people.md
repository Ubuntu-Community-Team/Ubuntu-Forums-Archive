### Post by aysiu on 2007-04-06
I've moved this to a more appropriate subforum.

I think your suggestion can be a good one, depending on the situation: people should give a little explanation, but new users are also not helpless. They can ask! A simple "I'm glad that command works, but what did that do, exactly?" is an easy way to get an explanation. I've had a lot of new users ask me that, and when I was a new user, I asked that too sometimes.

*We shouldn't be robots* is the bottom line. 

Sometimes it's more important (at first) for a new user to have her problem solved right away (I'm thinking particularly of those threads that have the words *help* or *urgent* in them). When you think you've lost all your pictures from the last five years or your internet connection isn't working, sometimes explanations can wait!

Other times, people get *more* confused when you're just trying to give them solutions without explanations.

It really depends on the situation, and I think new users should (and do?) feel comfortable enough to ask for explanations if they need explanations.
