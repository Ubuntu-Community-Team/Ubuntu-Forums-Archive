### Post by the dsc on 2012-04-11
I think this common forum rule is simply irrational.

If we're allowed to bump an old thread from a subject, either to add more info or to ask for more info, the odds are that the information will be more concentrated in a few useful threads. Rather than several threads with snippets of information about the same subject. If threads are "richer" overall, it increases the odds that more people will find the information they want, whereas the other pattern makes more likely that anyone will have to look through many more threads until eventually finding what (s)he's looking for. And the odds are that some of those people would open yet more threads about the same subject.


The only reasonable argument I've seen or figured for such policy is that by bumping old threads you move down newer threads that can be more relevant in the present moment. That has some logic, but at the same time, it's often not the case that a thread is at the top at the moment because it's something of collective interest, it's just that someone with his/hers particular doubt just happened to open such thread recently. Such person (or even "the collective", if it's such a case of a thread of collective interest) will be equally affected if it's a old thread being bumped, or if it's a redundant thread being created from scratch. 

There's even a reasonable chance of the new-thread-from-scratch affect more negatively the "place" of the other thread, as compared with the old-bumped thread, as it will have less information, making it somewhat more likely that more posts will be required, in comparison with a thread where the subject has been discussed for a while.



The only instance where I think the "stealing the place of a newer thread" factor really plays a little is if someone just wants to add something to an old thread -- like answering a previously unanswered question, not to ask for anything new. 

This is "bad" as probably the question from 2006 was already dealt with in one way or another, but there's always the chance that such answer would be useful for yet another person who would eventually find the thread in the future, regardless of any use to the original poster. 

Furthermore, if the person who just wants to add/not ask just does that, it "steals the place" of newer threads just momentarily, and then it slowly goes to the bottom as newer threads are created and discussed. If some [s]anti-bump nazi[/s] moderator has to go there and post saying "don't bump old threads, create a redundant one, please, we have too much free space on the server. Thread locked so it becomes less useful." then the old thread is bumped **twice**, with the last post being the one which is really useless to anyone. 



If the latter case is common (or perhaps regardless), I think that it would be interesting to have some sort of forum mechanism or add-on that won't really bump threads created out of a given range of time, without locking them for new posts. Somewhat as if old threads were "*sticky in the past*", if the last answer were two years ago, or whatever would be the "ideal" criterion (set by an administrator). If it gets more discussed (multiple posts by various users) within a few weeks/months, then it's allowed to be really bumped along with recent threads again.
