### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-11-03
> dewdrop_world

If I'm unfamiliar with something and need to rely on information found  on the net or elsewhere, I generally seek multiple sites from which to  extract information. I would never suggest that an individual limit  themselves to just one site (if others are available).

Logic might suggest that one investigate alternative sources of  information/solutions. For example, Open Office and Firefox have their  own forums. These site address issues and provide information directly  related to the application. Other sites such as HP share information  regarding HP printer compatibility and drivers.

It's been my experience,  that when one limits their informational resources - they limit their  number of opportunities. If your seeking a free one stop shop for all  your (Ubuntu/Linux) needs, I seriously doubt you will find satisfaction  with any one forum or site. That said, these forums are IMHO the best Ubuntu resource and the first place I go when searching for information. It's amazing how much information is freely available here.

On a side note, I always recommend to individuals (I suggest join the  forums) not to exhibit any entitlement characteristics. Since the  forum's design is that of users helping users, entitlement is an  extremely undesirable trait. I'm not suggesting you exhibited this  behavior, only pointing out my own observations and recommendations  regarding forum behavior.

Good Luck
