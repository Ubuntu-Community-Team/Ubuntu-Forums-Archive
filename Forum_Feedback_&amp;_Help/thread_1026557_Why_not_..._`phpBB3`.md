Of course, the items you quoted are both consistent with open source licenses. The part that isn't (the part that makes it incorrect to call it open source by any commonly understood definition of that term) is:
> The Software is licensed only to you. You may not rent, lease, sublicense, sell, assign, pledge, transfer or otherwise dispose of the Software in any form, on a temporary or permanent basis, without the prior written consent of Jelsoft.
vBulletin is great software, has performed remarkably well under sometimes-unfavorable circumstances (too many users and too little hardware firepower) here, and I would recommend it to anyone looking for a flexible and scalable message board environment.

I would not call it open source, however, as that would be simply misleading. Open source licenses vary considerably, but they all grant the right of the licensee to redistribute the code as he or she sees fit. It has *never* simply referred to the ability of the licensee to work with the source code.
