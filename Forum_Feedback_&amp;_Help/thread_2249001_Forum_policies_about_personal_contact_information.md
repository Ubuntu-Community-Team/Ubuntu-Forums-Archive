### Post by coffeecat on 2014-10-19
An interesting question. As a formal policy, the nearest we have would be our privacy policy contained within the Data Attribution, Retention and Privacy Policy, But it doesn&#8217;t address the specific question you are asking:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2231107](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2231107)

By formal policy, I mean one discussed and then voted upon by the Forum Council. As a matter of working practice the staff will edit out the email address from a post if only to protect the user. However, it could be argued that we are in danger of over-nannying people who deliberately disclose their email address or other information. The forum is set up in such a way that a user&#8217;s email address is not publicly visible in their profile. If people choose to subvert that protection, should we interfere? I ask that question merely to stimulate discussion.

Another important motivation for staff to edit out email addresses is that some publish their email address inviting answers to their problem via email, or by private message. That, of course, undermines the purpose of the forum. The use of private messaging in this context is covered in our forum [Code of Conduct](http://ubuntuforums.org/misc.php?do=showrules), but not emails:

> **Private Messaging:** Asking support questions via private messages is strongly discouraged. It is unlikely that users will respond to these requests and it defeats the secondary intent of the forums to be a resource for people seeking assistance using internet searches and forum searches. Private messages sent to other users are subject to the Code of Conduct.

It would be helpful if you would link the policies of other forums, so we can get an idea of whether we need to address this.

I&#8217;ve concentrated on emails in my reply, but you are right in that other personally identifying information is just as important. The only phone numbers I remember seeing are those in spam posts, but that&#8217;s another matter! :)

By the way, I've edited your post to remove the link to the post showing the email address (Oh, the irony!). We'll deal with that separately. As a point of information to anyone reading this thread, if you see an email address in a post, please report the post so that staff can deal with it.

### Post by bapoumba on 2014-10-19
We usually remove any kind of personal info that may appear in a post, including email/phone/social sites profiles etc. but also server IPs or other kind of identification. We do not patrol the forums looking for that, posts are usually reported by other users or we do stumble upon them while reading threads.

In addition to cofeecat's post :
Users who are used to forums or online resources never (or extremely rarely) publicly disclose their personal info, new users tend to be less aware of all the sad consequences this could trigger (spamming an email is a moderately annoying one, identity theft is much more troublesome).

### Post by coffeecat on 2014-10-19
That's interesting but almost all of it is covered in our forum Code of Conduct - no porn, abusive messages, spam, obscenities, cross-posting, etc, etc. As far as IP addresses and emails are concerned, they are covered in the Privacy Policy I linked earlier:

> We retain records of email and IP addresses, but this is information which can only be viewed by forum staff (admins and moderators) and is considered to be confidential.

Also - I don't see how that ties in with what you said in your original post:

> **Vladlenin5000 said:**
> It's usual for an online forum to have a specific rule about not publicly disclose personal information such as email address, phone numbers, Facebook pages and so on and so fourth.

Users can elect to receive emails from other members, but if they change their settings to allow this, all that others see in their profile is a "Send email" link which leads to a send message page. Of course, anyone who uses this utility will reveal their forum registered email address to the recipient. Apart from this, personal information can only be revealed if the member does this in a post, and bapoumba has covered our usual routine for such instances.

I'm not sure that we can do anything else. If anyone has any suggestions as to how user privacy can be protected without unnecessary interference in what users may choose to post, then we will be happy to hear it.

@Vladlenin5000, by the way the link in the post you linked to (now edited out) in your original post was a sig link to the user's personal website, not an email link as I mistakenly described it earlier. This is allowed in the rules - forum members may link to their personal sites in a sig, so long as it is not a business site. That the particular member chose to include their email and phone number in their personal webpage is something outside of our control and, to be honest, not something I would wish to interfere with.
