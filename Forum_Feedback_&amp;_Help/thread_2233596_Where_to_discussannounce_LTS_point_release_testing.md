I've actually brought this up in the past but with 14.04.1 tentatively due on July 24th and 12.04.5 tentatively due on August 7th I felt it wise to ask in advance :)

My personal opinion is that opening a full-fledged discussion [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=427") would be disruptive and confusing to those testing Utopic, and it could possibly become an excuse to discuss any number of Precise/Trusty issues rather than sticking to Utopic.

So my thought was I could open a thread here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=201](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=201)

It's seldom used anyway, then I could post a simple announcement at the current dev version sub-forum including a link to that new thread. Then I would immediately click on Report Post and request the thread be closed but not moved.

Then those who wish to test that specific point release can discuss it further here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=201](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=201)

Does that make sense?

To recap the process would go like this:

Step #1: Open a thread in ***Development CD/DVD Image Testing*** with information specific to that point release.

Step #2: Open a thread in ***Ubuntu Development Version*** simply announcing the availability of those images and pointing to that thread for further information and/or discussion.

Step #3: Immediately after posting the announcement in ***Ubuntu Development Version*** click on Report Post and request that the thread be closed but not moved.

Any thoughts?
