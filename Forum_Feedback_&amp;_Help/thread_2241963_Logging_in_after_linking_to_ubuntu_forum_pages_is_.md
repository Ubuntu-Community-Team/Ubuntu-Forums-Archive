### Post by hughh on 2014-08-29
When I search for information about a problem on my system, quite often I get links to these forums.  This is great, because I find them a very good source of information.  Unfortunately, after opening those links, if I decide I need or want to log in, I find myself at the forums home page rather than at the page I logged in from.  Going back in browser history will get me back to the page I want, but I won't be signed in.  So after going through all the hassle of several pages of logging in, I finally must go back in my browser history to the page I'm interested in, copy the URL, go forward in my browser history to a point after I signed in, and paste the URL of the page I want into the browser's URL bar.

Needless to say, this is very frustrating, a significant waste of time, and easily fixed by a better log-on process.  Please do something to make it easier.  Or maybe I'm missing something, like a way to set a cookie that allows me to stay logged in for long periods of times.

### Post by QIII on 2014-08-29
My trick:

Log in to UF.

Open another browser tab.

Do your search in that tab.  When you click on the link you'll go to that item already signed in.

PITA, but workable.

Conversely, find what you find.  Open another tab and log in.  Go back to your original tab and click the link.
