### Post by varunendra on 2014-02-11
From a normal user's perspective, and of one who likes to offer help where possible, I personally think it is much better to post the problem in detail in a new thread. Of course you can provide link to an old thread as reference.

Old threads tend to contain suggestions that may be outdated and no more applicable to current versions of the OS/Software. And if someone posts in such threads, it comes up higher in normal search results.

Now if an inexperienced user searches or browses through the forums, they may not notice the dates (or ignore them because they have no idea about the difference) and may try those 'Outdated' suggestions. This may worsen their problem to a point where reinstalling may be the only way out.

Sure there may be threads that may be relevant for a long time, but the staff (or any kind of 'Experts') may not have the time or expertise to analyse its relevance, and so a general rule of thumb is to simply close it to avoid such Off-the-track or Irrelevant threads.
