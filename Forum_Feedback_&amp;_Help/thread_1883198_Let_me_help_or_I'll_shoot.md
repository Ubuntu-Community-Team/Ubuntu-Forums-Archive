### Post by Bachstelze on 2011-11-18
I guess I can answer on this since the "no guesswork" rule was passed from my proposal. Basically, the issue it is meant to prevent is that we used to have a lot of people who would throw random answers based on something they saw and thought was similar, without a word of warning. Some of those answers were not only wrong, but could make the OP's problem even worse. So we asked for a reasonable amount of certainty when you give a definitive answer, since in the person's mind, you are as good as anyone else, and they will just do what you say.

Of course if you see a thread with no answer, you can give a hint, but if it is just that, a hint, you are asked to say so, so that 1) the person you are responding to will proceed with caution, and 2) someone else can say if your idea is wrong even if they didn't answer to the thread in the first place because they do not know the right one (and please don't take it personally, we all make mistakes).

### Post by Bodsda on 2011-11-20
If I find a thread that no one has responded to and I can't find a quick answer to, I like to suggest posting additional info. Even if I know I won't be able to follow the whole thread through to the end, if I can get some extra relevant information from the OP then maybe someone else will spot what the problem is or will be able to help further. 

1 person doesn't have to take control of the thread and find the answer themselves, if 10 people all help nudge it forward eventually your combined knowledge is likely to come up with a solution that none of the helpers individually may have been able to find.

Bodsda
