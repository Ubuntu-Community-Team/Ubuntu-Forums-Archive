### Post by Bat on 2008-01-07
The biggest thing that annoys me about support forums, Ubuntu Forums included, is all the me-too posting.  One person will post a request for help ("I typed rm -rf / and now I can't find my thesis! Help!") and a million other users will post "me too" replies, saying they had the same problem.  Even when someone posts a solution to the problem, it gets drowned out in the me-too posts.

I just saw something that could solve this problem.  Have a look at the forums for Jottit - [this page, for example]("http://getsatisfaction.com/jottit/topics/preventing_http_jottit_com_donate_and_alike").  There's a big button marked "I have this problem too!".  Logged-in users can click the button and their "vote" is recorded.  There's no longer any need for people to fill the forum with me-toos.

Surely we could get something like that here... right?
