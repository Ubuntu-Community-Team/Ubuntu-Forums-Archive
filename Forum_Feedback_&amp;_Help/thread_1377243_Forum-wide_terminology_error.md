### Post by Gizenshya on 2010-01-10
When viewing profile pages, on the right of the screen, there is box called "**Mini Statistics**."  There is also clickable text in the box that says "**Show All Statistics**."

The term "**statistics**" is used incorrectly.  In fact, all of the pieces of information given are actually **parameters** (like X posts/day, X join date, etc.).  Parameter is the only correct term because the information given is actually based on the whole of the population (all data) for each user.  Statistics are similar to parameters, but they are information based on a sample, and not a population.  This is why statistics have margins of error, and parameters do not.

It'll probably be a quick fix.  How has nobody seen this before? :o lol

### Post by Gizenshya on 2010-01-10
> **KiwiNZ said:**
> [B]Statistics 
Definition: Classified facts respecting the condition of the people in a state, their health, their longevity, domestic economy, arts, property, and political strength, their resources, the state of the country, etc., or respecting any particular class or interest; especially, those facts which can be stated in numbers, or in tables of numbers, or in any tabular and classified arrangement.[/B]

That is a definition of the field of study of statistics.  That does not apply to the places I posted because that is a different context (a collection of measured characteristics, not refering to the field of study in general).  I see where your confusion come from, though.  It is an easy mistake.  Despite the fact that they are spelled the same, Statistics (field of study) != Statistics (plural of statistic).

That box and the page the link points to both give measured characteristics.  This clearly means that it is in a different context than applies to your definition.  This doesn't necessarily make it incorrect, however.

Definition of a statistic: a measured characteristic of a sample.

Definition of a population: a measured characteristic of a population.

also...

Definition of a sample: a subset of a population.

Definition of a population: complete and entire collection of elements to be studied, or in question.

What is the population in question?  Data on the user in question.

To find that out whether parameter or statistic applies we must answer a question: Are the measured characteristics of a sample or population?  If you look, the numbered are clearly based off of the complete data for the items in question (population), and not estimated from a sample of said data (sample).  Therefore, the is a very clear answer to that question: population.  Since the measured characteristics are clearly based only on the population, the correct term is clearly parameters (plural, of course, since more than one parameter is given).

So, the term "Statistics," as used where I pointed out, is clearly the wrong term.
