### Post by coffeecat on 2015-06-25
Thanks for posting and it's good news that your LoCo team appears to be re-invigorating itself. Best wishes!

In the aftermath of the forum hack a couple of years ago, a good hard look, as you put it, was focussed on all accounts with increased privileges – not just global moderators and admins, but LoCo mods as well. You may remember that it was an abandoned LoCo mod account which was the key to the door, so to speak, for that unfortunate episode. So principally for security reasons, but as good management too, we now only give sub-forum moderator privileges in those LoCo forums that have consistent and current significant activity. The Arizona LoCo sub-forum is showing insufficient activity at the moment so I suggest the following course of action until such time as we can allow the appointment of a sub-forum moderator.

Rather than edit the old stickies, perhaps you would prefer to write new ones. Please post links to the stickies you would like to be unstuck and I'll do that for you. I assume all three, but I'll wait until you confirm this.

Post new threads that you want to be displayed as stickies. If you want more than one sticky, post one thread per sticky. Once they're posted, simply use the “report post” in each one that you wish to be stuck, sending a request to the staff area. Please refer to this thread in your report/request. 

That should get you up and running again in the LoCo sub-forum. Thereafter, if you need any threads stuck or unstuck, or stickies that you need to edit re-opening if they've been autoclosed, or any other assistance, please use the report post facility in the same way. We're always happy to help.

If and when your group becomes active enough to warrant your own sub-forum moderator, we would need an email to the Forum Council mailing list (email address in this page: - [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ForumCouncil](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ForumCouncil) ) from your group, identifying the forum member you wish to be your sub-forum moderator, and identifying the Launchpad page for this person.

By the way, all sub-forum moderators, including banditti, had their moderator permissions removed after the hack and the handful of sub-forum moderators we have now have had to go through the scrutiny I describe above.
