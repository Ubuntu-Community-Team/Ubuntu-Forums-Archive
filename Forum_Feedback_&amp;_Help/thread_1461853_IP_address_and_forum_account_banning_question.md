### Post by jwbrase on 2010-04-24
It depends quite a bit on the architecture of the network the computer's on. In theory every computer on the Internet has it's own unique IP address. In practice alot of machines tend to be on local networks where the traffic for the whole network is routed through one router, and while they all have different IP addresses within the network, the router is the go-between between them and the Internet, so they all appear to the outside world to have the IP address of the router (and thus the outside world can't tell between them).
