### Post by matthew on 2010-07-03
Colloquially, among Kurdish speakers, the areas of Iraq and Turkey (and others?) where most Kurds live is called "Kurdistan." It does not refer to a specific political entity, but rather a non-specific geographic region populated by a majority of people of Kurdish ethnicity.

In the word, the suffix "stan" simply means "land of," as used in places like Afghanistan, Tajikistan, and so on. "Kurdistan" simply refers to the geographic area where Kurds live and the Kurdish language is used (and that includes places outside of Turkey, so your suggestion would not be helpful).

It is similar to calling the Middle East something like "The Arab World" because of the large number of people of Arab descent living there.

The title is not a political statement. It is not a "mistake" in any sense. It is simply a way to connect people of a certain language group and ethnicity to the wider Ubuntu community.

### Post by matthew on 2010-07-03
I understand what you are saying and have a deep understanding of the history and politics involved in the region. We have other teams that are language, and not country based. In this case, those involved asked (politely, by the way) if they could list their location as Kurdistan, again only to draw attention to the undisputed fact that Kurds and Kurdish speakers live in more than one country and that separating them by the use of imposed political boundaries when they have neither interest nor intent to involve themselves in politics as a LoCo team would be counter productive to the goal of making the software more readily available to people who speak the language.

While I sympathize with the emotional attachment many feel to the politics of the question of political loyalties and Kurds, in this particular case I think it is a trivial matter that is outweighed by the benefit of encouraging people to work together on a non-political software project.

So, your issue and request have been well understood. Unfortunately for you, we do not agree with your assessment of the situation and have no plans to change the name nor the group's listed location.
