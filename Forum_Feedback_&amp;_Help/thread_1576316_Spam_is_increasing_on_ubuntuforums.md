### Post by Paddy Landau on 2010-09-19
> **forestpiskie said:**
> If you could answer this definitively then I would think that you could make a lot of money.
There is no such thing as a foolproof method. But when I used to help a friend run a forum, I discovered a particularly effective method.

We had put in place the cleverest Captchas and none of them worked for more than a couple of days. The spammers were too clever. (Remember that most spammers are automated bots, not humans.)

As a solution, I got rid of all the Captchas. Instead, in the registration, I put a question that needed a human to answer. For example, "To help avoid spam, please type 'cat' in the box." Or, "To help avoid spam, what colour is green grass?" The question would vary randomly. It cut spam registrations and posts from dozens daily to about one a week.

On my own website, I've gone a bit further by automatically rejecting any post that contains something from a blacklist -- but that may not work on a forum like this, because nearly anything can be posted.
