I'm completely satisfied with the staff's moderation of this site. I've been on a number of other (so-called) Ubuntu/Linux sites and IMHO, most are way to permissive.

This site is maintained as a family/office friendly site. That alone reduces the number of potential conflicts that perpetuate a hostile environment. The forums are a neutral site that assures no individual will be chastised or berated by another. The mods permit healthy interaction, while assuming no member violates the COC. This environment IMHO is not only healthy, but attracts members who prefer a friendly environment versus one that is in a constant state of flux due to ongoing member conflicts.

The forums are very "new member friendly". Instead of a hierarchy based on communications skills or product knowledge, it prides itself on providing all members the opportunity to participate without fear of belittlement or aggressive communications. The staff is constantly striving to improve the environment and simply finding fault with their actions or demanding change does little to enhance this site.

I support and appreciate the staff for the outstanding job they do on a daily basis. I may not always agree with them, but they control this forum and I must respect their decisions. It's been my experience that not being privy to all the details regarding their decisions, can often result in a misinterpretation of said decisions.

I'm also interested in why you responded with a negative.

I wonder if fear of retaliation is stopping you from responding. If so, I can say without hesitation that it's unwarranted. I've voiced my opinion numerous times here and have never been targeted or witnessed any evidence of retaliation.

You have an opportunity to express your opinion regarding your assessment of the staff's moderating methods in the Cafe. If done with respect and attention to detail (using thread and post links to substantiate your opinions) your feedback may help the staff in viewing their performance from a different perspective.

It's been my experience, that the staff will review your assessments and either use them to improve their methods in the future or ignore them. Hopefully, your feedback will inspire some of the staff to investigate you concerns of how the Cafe is moderated and if they have merit, take steps to correct the issue(s). But when put in perspective, the staff can only modify that which they're aware of. Assuming they believe it's necessary or viable. Thus IMHO, providing constructive feedback should be a priority of all forum members.

My only recommendations are:
Don't have any expectations and provide realistic alternatives (if possible) to problems you present. Don't invest in any emotions or deliberately direct complaints at (attack) any specific staff member or individual. Just the substantiated facts that your opinion is based upon and any solutions that you believe may improve the situation.

Good Luck and I hope you post your constructive feedback.
