### Post by coffeecat on 2014-10-30
> **vadim7 said:**
> I feel as though leaving threads open makes for less redundancy.

Experience shows that leaving old threads open leads to repeated and often aimless necromancy, which in turn encourages inexperienced forum members to post their questions to them. Experience also shows that such members are better served by starting new threads. Young, fresh threads are more likely to be visited by those who have the knowledge and enthusiasm to provide support. 

Another reason for closing old threads is that they more often than not have advice relevant to unsupported versions of Ubuntu which may not be applicable to the latest versions. This can be misleading to inexperienced users and, again, experience shows that they are better served by opening a new thread. Old threads can always be linked to in the new if this helps.

> Just seems silly. But k.
