### Post by wieman01 on 2006-10-04
Hi Team, 

I have given it some tought and I have come to the conclusion that it may be worth to enhance the forum by a feature or two. This should be an open discussion so I am inviting you to make comments & tell me if these ideas are really worthwhile.

Basically I am impressed by the sheer number of Linux experts that are members of this forum. Many of us show commitment to the spirit & concept of Ubuntu & possess dedication that is beyond what I have seen so far in other internet communities. I also understand that each of has certain strengths & key focus areas when it comes to supporting others & seeking help. Having said that I think that is something we should build on and possibly enhance the way users customize their profiles. 

The new "tagging" approach is one step in the right direction if you ask me. Bearing that in mind I would go one step further and have users "tag" their own profiles i.e. furnish their profiles with key words, thus highlighting their expertise. To give you an example:
[U]
User ABC:[/U]
1. Wireless networking & security
2. Samba
3. Xinerama
4. Xgl, compiz

This would work both ways: Forum members find posts that relate to the "field of expertise" faster - and at the same time users seeking support would know at one glance to whom they need to address their concerns. Users would primarily see threads that corresponds to their personal tags, such threads would then be given a higher priority (e.g. different color). Again users seeking support could contact other users with the matching expertise directly & subsequently open up a new thread.

Personally I am much in favor of such a function as it would speed up the process of supporting others in need of help & finding solutions for technical problems. I am convinced we would have considerable time savings on both sides of the fence.

Your ideas on this?
