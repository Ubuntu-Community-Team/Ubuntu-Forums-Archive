### Post by ubuntu-geek on 2006-07-06
It's been a pretty busy day here for us, this morning our servers were upgraded and then followed by our forum software this evening (well at least here in the USA/East Coast)

Anyways, you will notice the new theme. Our attempt to blend more with the main Ubuntu.com website. We spent about a week testing and implementing this theme and we hope you like it. Yeah, we know it's fixed width but :KS

You will also notice a few other changes..
[INDENT] The tag system has been removed. This system had  many faults and ran a good test course for us to determine our next steps and re-implement this system.

Some of the more fine tuned searches are temporarily removed. We hope to have these back online within the next 24 hours. IN the meantime there are some new search options to explore :)

 You will notice we returned to a more "grass roots" forum home page. Like the tag system, we implemented that as
a test "product" or sorts to see how it adapted. We opted to remove it.

We will have a matching theme for Kubuntu and Xubuntu sometime in the next week.
[/INDENT]I am sure some forum template bugs will pop up here and there. Please bare with us as we work to fix any outstanding issues.

While we respect not everyone will like the new layout we please ask that this thread does not turn into a "flame/bash" fest of our work. We do however, ask if you notice issues with functionality or the theme in general to post here.

### Post by mssever on 2006-07-07
I have a couple of usability suggestions.

1. First, the default links are now practically invisible, since they're the same color as the surrounding text and not underlined. Unless it's clear from context that certain text is supposed to be a link, many people will fail to notice. I've found in my web design work that it's best to underline links unless there's a *really* good reason not to, because doing so makes sites much easier to use as your eye is easily drawn to the links.

2. How about either activating the quick reply box onclick (think Gmail), or removing it altogether? It's annoying to click on the box only to discover that it's disabled for no apparent reason. This is especially true since the reply icons are unfriendly (tiny, similar in appearance, and without text labels). *Edit: I just noticed the big reply button the left. Great!
