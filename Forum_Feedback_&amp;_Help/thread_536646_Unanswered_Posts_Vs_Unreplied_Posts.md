### Post by LaRoza on 2008-01-26
>I'm not sure if I'm missing something here.  If the thread is NOT marked [Solved] even after 25 responses, is the "Unanswered Posts Team" still looking at it?

It doesn't come up in the Unanswered Posts searches we do, but we do look for unsolved problems. However, with responses present, we have to read them. If I see a thread with 9 responses, I typically don't read it if I see another thread that has no responses. If I see a thread with 25 responses, I typically do tread the last page and the first post. In my experience, very few threads are OT for that long, and OT threads often forget the original problem.

>A post could be solved in 1 or 2 responses, but if it isn't marked [Solved], you're still looking at it.  I had no idea the the "Mark Thread as Solved" even existed when I first came here, finally when I did learn about it, I had to go to my CP, search for my threads, and mark them after the fact. I wonder if all users do that, bet not.

>My guess is that there are thousands of threads [Solved] but not marked so.

Yes, there are.

(Like the thanks icon) If a solution is found, the OP just clicks the button, which marks the thread as solved, and marks the post that solved it. Just my idea though.
