### Post by PmDematagoda on 2007-12-10
I decided to move the idea here completely:-

> What about the integration of Ubuntu Forum searches with those of Launchpad?

Many people have problems with Ubuntu(Which includes bugs) which are reported(And in many cases solved) on Launchpad, but most of them do not know anything about Launchpad which can lead to them not seeing those solutions when trying to start a thread for their problems.

If the searches of both Launchpad and Ubuntu Forums were integrated, the solutions concerning bugs or issues already reported or addressed in Launchpad can reach those people with the same problems which in turn can save them a lot of time and forum space and also may have the potential of providing them with better answers than what they might get on the forums(No offence meant to the forums).

I was just wondering since no one was replying whether this idea is hopeless or cannot be understood. If that is the problem then I would be more than happy to clear it up or prove the advantage of this idea if it was implemented.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-12-13
I agree with the OP and it may be difficult to implement, but there may be an intermediate step:

Better Howto's for sound, video, hibernate/sleep, samba, cups, etc.  With these Howto's could be a button to search for related bugs at the bottom of the Howto.

In other words, if you have a sound problem and you try all the techniques in the Howto's and sound still doesn't work, there would be a button at the bottom of the Howto that would search Launchpad for bugs specific to that person's problem.

The Howto's cover perhaps 80 to 90% of hardware issues.  Frustrated users in the 10% at least would know that a bug has been filed for the issue at hand.
