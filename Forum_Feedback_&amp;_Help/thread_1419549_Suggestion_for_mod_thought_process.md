### Post by hobo14 on 2010-03-02
I'd just like to suggest a possible philosophy/mission statement for mods.

Could mods lock threads, etc., only when they **must**, rather than whenever they **can**?

It appears that some mods (not all) view each thread/post and ask themselves *"Could I possibly close this thread?"* and locking any threads that don't return a definitive *"No"* to that question.

Wouldn't it be better to ask *"Can I possibly leave this thread open?"* and only lock threads that return a definitive *"No"* to this question?

It may surprise you that the staff work to keep threads open. There is discussion in the staff only areas about many of the threads that are closed before they are closed.

You may often also see threads "closed for review". These get a lot of discussion before either remaining closed or reopened.
