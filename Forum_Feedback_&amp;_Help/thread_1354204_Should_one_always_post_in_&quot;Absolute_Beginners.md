### Post by falconindy on 2009-12-13
If you're not getting a response, part of that may be in the way your OP was constructed. This is paraphrased from another forum, but its just as valid:
> How to Post
[LIST]
[*]Choose clear, informative subjects. This is more likely to elicit response from experienced users who have knowledge about that particular topic. It also makes the topic easy to reference and find in forum searches by future users with similar problems.
[*]When asking questions, provide as much information as possible, including error messages, terminal output, logs, what you have previously tried, what documentation and searches you have attempted, and related configuration files.
[*]Choose one topic per thread. Try not to post multiple questions in a single topic -- this makes it difficult to search for specific problems.
[*]Post your question in only one forum; pick the most relevant forum and post there.
[*]When responding to an existing thread, always read the original post and attempt to focus on the original topic.
[*]Finally, mark your thread as [SOLVED] when a solution is found.[/LIST]

### Post by ricardisimo on 2009-12-20
I would have thought that most people look at the forum's first page - as I do when browsing - which of course shows only newer threads. And I have some reason for thinking that. I do indeed have completely unanswered threads, where even I never followed up, and I've even forgotten about them myself... does that mean I should expect one of you guys to be answering them some time soon?

No, of course not... or at least I really doubt it. However, if I were to bump one of them today, the odds of getting some sort of response will increase from absolute nil to "could happen". That's why it's called "bumping" and not "blotting" or something similar. Bumping serves a dual purpose of moving the post back up to the top, and also letting people know the issue has not yet been resolved. It's irresponsible **not** to bump.

I think it comes back to my now-reformulated question about General Help. This board suggests a higher level of acumen, distinct from AB, and therefore invites some of the more veteran members. But if we're not actually getting responses, then we're wasting our time. So, again, why do we have General Help?

Similarly, perhaps we should ignore the stickies that implore us to be specific when titling our posts. Honestly... *why not* title every single post "HEEEEEEEEELLLLLPPPPP!!!!!!" if in fact (and I submit that it is fact) you'll get better results that way? Don't worry, I'm not planning on doing that. I'm just speculating on a hypothesis (for all you *Miller's Crossing* fans out there).

Just some random thoughts and observations. I'm just very pleased that this post received responses _after_ getting moved out of AB. The alternative would have been too painfully ironic to bear.
