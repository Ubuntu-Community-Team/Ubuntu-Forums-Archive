### Post by pmasiar on 2008-02-01
It recurring problem that people do not read FAQ.

Of course nothing can stop them 100%, but lets assume we want to prevent people from embarrasment of asking such FAQed questions.

Maybe having reminder "Did you read stickies? Read them now, before starting thread" question right next to "New Post" would stop some % of the people asking FAQ?

And then, in the page after checking relevant titles, could we have 
* text: "if you want to help improving FAQ, please read  below. If you don't care or have no time now, just ask."
* link to [How To Ask Questions The Smart Way](http://catb.org/~esr/faqs/smart-questions.html) by Eric Raymond, or other appropriate guide
* text " We assume you read FAQ. in your post, mention which one you read, and how your question is different, so we can improve FAQs"

... or something like that? not too boring, but really helping people from embarrasment of asking FAQ, and improving info/noise ratio?

Certainly it is not appropriate in all forums. "backyard", "cafeteria"  or whatever it is called, "Total beginners" probably not. "Programming talk" definitive yes.
