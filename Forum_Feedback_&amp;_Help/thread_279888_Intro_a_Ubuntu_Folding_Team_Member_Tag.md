### Post by fourchannel on 2007-03-03
I also think it is a great idea, and I think sometimes people let beauracracy get in the way of a truely beneficial idea. Does it matter that TeamUbuntu is not "official"? - The folding project is an awesome thing, and even cooler is that this machine increases performance with size, and that it cannot (practically) become too large to outweigh the benefits it also gains.

And I don't think that having the tag is to show how much better of a person you are than everyone else is. These people who fold, realize that lending a helping hand is a great thing. They take the time to do what they can for others, to improve the welfare of whoever their help reaches. I do not suspect that a person like that would bask in superiority, but rather encourage you to also rise above, and maybe take a few people with you.

I believe that the tag is requested so that everyone on the forums is made aware of the folding project (many people who fold have numerous posts throughout the forum, it's bound to draw attention), and so that those who are willing will take the time to make their computer a part of this machine. It will be especially effective seeing as how people will notice that a few people have cool tags and wonder, "why don't I have a tag? How did they get theirs?"

I think that the goal intended was to benevolently make everyone aware of a choice they can make to help.

In regards to being an official folding team -- We are all Humans, let's not let invisible borders come between us, and I know the forums are for support/help -- Many of us come here to recieve help, and many of us come to provide help. Many of us realize that if tomorrow, we stopped comming to the forums questions would still go on being answered. That someone else will take our place clicking that reply button.

We all do our part, let's not pass up a chance to attract people to the folding project.
