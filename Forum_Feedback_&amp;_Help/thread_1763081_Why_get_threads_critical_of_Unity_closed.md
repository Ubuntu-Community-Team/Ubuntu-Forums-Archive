Well, you see, the problems are:

1. We are primarily a forums for support not debate. If you do not like Unity don't use it, several alternates, and you do not need to distro hop. Use kde, xfce, *box, lubuntu, etc, ...

2. Most people get tired of the endless debates, so we try to move them to recurring discussions, but, it is a burden on the staff, so sometimes they just get closed.

If you want to debate these things, have at, we have a whole section of the forums just for you, see [Recurring Discussions]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=302").

All we ask is:
[list][*]Keep it out of the support sections[*]The code of conduct still applies, so keep it respectful.[/list]

3. There is a difference between constructive criticism and personal attacks or stating "Ubuntu sucks". The former is appreciated and leads to improvements. The latter leads to trolling, and closed threads.

If you have a specific question about a specific thread, feel free to ask in the Resolution Center. Be sure to include a justification for why you think a thread should be opened and do not expect the Administrative staff to do an extensive review of all the posts on all the threads on Unity.
