### Post by MisterGaribaldi on 2011-02-22
First off, welcome to UbuntuForums!

I have read in detail each of your three examples and, having done so, I will not sit here and pretend to have the competence or basic knowledge to solve your problems. Your posts are well written, friendly and non-combative, illuminative in both that you've *tried* to solve these issues and also in specifically *how* you've tried to do so. If anything, I would simply suggest that perhaps the problems you've described simply have yet to be read by someone with the requisite knowledge and expertise. For what it's worth, my own hope is that *that* help is soon forthcoming.

Now, that all being said, I don't know what your total background with Linux is, but I think three points bear repeating here.


[LIST=1]
[*]Wireless support in Linux does not have the same kind of history that wired networking does in that it's generally been fairly well documented and straight-forward to support physical networking, but classically it was very hard to support wireless because of the wireless component manufacturer's deliberate efforts to keep that hardware under their own control;
[*]The first principle mainstream "solution" to getting wireless hardware to work in Linux was to create the ndiswrapper system. This allowed regular so-called "win32" drivers to work in Linux. This really started out as a form of a "hack" to get hardware working amd was eventually superseded by reverse engineering of the hardware itself (and likely existing drivers etc.) to then make Linux-native wireless drivers.
[*]Though certainly not the only one, Apple is known for not disclosing details on the components used in their systems, thus making it very hard to support their hardware. Arguably the situation has improved significantly since Apple transitioned from PPC to x86 but it's still not all sunshine and roses.
[/LIST]
 
There are others on here far more knowledgeable and experienced in these matters than I. Therefore, I'll leave it to them to be able to solve the situation or at least point you in the right direction.

Above all, good luck!
