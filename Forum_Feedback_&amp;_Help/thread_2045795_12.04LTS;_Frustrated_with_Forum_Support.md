Having looked at your links only one of them is actually a thread  started by you! A couple you are pages deep and asking for help on  someone else's thread (hijacking). If you have a specific issue, please  post a *new* thread with a descriptive title and as much info as you can about your problem rather than join an existing thread. Thanks.

Threads appear on search lists along with all others, so not being ignored as much as other users perhaps feel they can't help with the issue. There is nothing any of us can do to get the community to deal with any particular thread or provide answers, unfortunately. If you look at some of my previous threads they are two page dialogues with myself!

Best way to draw attention to threads, as they do drop down the list over time, is to give them a bump every twenty four hours. You can just add a new post with 'bump' as the body text or report your progress which might add some clues and drag in helpers. Any activity on the thread will put it at the top of the search list again. ;)

Please wait twenty four hours before bumping, though.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-08-22
Fast, slick, highly configurable. I only need to use a few things most the time so I don't need the bloat that comes with a full Ubuntu install, even less so Kubuntu. I need no bells and whistles; my desktop is blank (see attachment) with one auto-hiding task bar which has few icons; just about everything is launched by a key combination. 

I have a recent Toshiba Satellite Pro with an i3 and 4Gb of RAM so not about specs for me. It goes like a rocket. About productivity. I'm also a fan of the minimal install. There you really do install only what you want, including DE, right from the get go. ;)

You can try out the DE by installing xfce4 from SCentre or Synaptics, log out, click 'Sessions' and choose xfce4, login. This won't install all Xubuntu apps; installing xubuntu-desktop will so avoid unless duplication and bloat is what you want  (all Ubuntu and Xubuntu apps will be in your menus in both DEs).
