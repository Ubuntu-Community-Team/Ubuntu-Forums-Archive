### Post by Matt Yun on 2008-01-21
I also have found that these forums are incredibly useful; so much so, that I have never needed to ask a question (I've only posted answers, as best as I can).  I've found that if you search diligently enough, and invest a little time in learning Linux, you can find the answers to most Ubuntu problems here.

The posts where "questions are answered by questions" usually arise because the initial question is not specific enough, and the good samaritans that respond are trying to probe for more information.

Here's a recent thread that I participated in, that shows how the original poster posed his question, received feedback, did a little of his own research, then found his own solution:  [Can't mount IDE disks]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=667004").

You'll notice that I initially gave him incorrect advice, but tried to correct my mistake later.  This is because many of us are not computer scientists or software engineers or Canonical employees, so you always have to assess the advice here carefully.

*Here are some sites that you could use as an alternative to ubuntuforums.org:*

[howtoforge.org]("http://www.howtoforge.org") : many, many excellent tutorials on common problems.

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu) : search the bug reports to see if your problem has already been reported, and if the developers have fixed it or not

[gentoo.org]("http://gentoo.org") and [linuxquestions.org]("http://linuxquestions.org") : more community-based forums, so you might find these frustrating too, but these are pretty comprehensive

When you google, you can add "site:ubuntuforums.org" to your search query to search only the specified site e.g. in this case, ubuntuforums.org.  This is a way of focusing your research.
