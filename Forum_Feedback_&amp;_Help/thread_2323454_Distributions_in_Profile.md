### Post by T.J. on 2016-05-05
I am a Debian user.  I prefer it because I am more "hands on."  To be honest, at times I am somewhat critical of the way Ubuntu does certain things.  Even so, that has not diminished my respect for the great job Canonical and the community have done in creating these forums as a place to solve problems and have discussions.  I enjoy coming here to help others with their questions. I understand the fact that the forums are intended to be Ubuntu-centric, and that will not change. 

The current profile setup does not seem to display other distributions, even when they are entered.  What I would like to suggest is that the tag is displayed openly.  A text box could replace the current "other distributions" dropdown, so that users may enter the name themselves.  In this way, you would not have to update the list frequently, and no one feels left out.  

I would like very much to have my choice of Linux distribution display in my profile, so that people may see and accept that you welcome everyone openly, even non-Ubuntu users as part of the community. I believe that simple change would make a huge difference in showing that users from different distributions work together.
