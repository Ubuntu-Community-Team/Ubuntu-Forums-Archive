### Post by lisati on 2009-05-05
Part of it is privacy, particularly the bit about using an email address. I have an email address associated with my profile here at these forums. I've only used this email address (to the best of my recollection) with websites related in some way with Ubuntu, and I still receive the occasional spam - I'd hate to think how much I'd receive if I'd used my email address as my username! As it is, the [spiders]("http://http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_spider") spammers use to harvest email addresses have to do a bit of digging around.

### Post by Dr Small on 2009-05-05
It is advised not to use your real name as your username, as lisati stated, for privacy.

Suppose you made an enemy [like this](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1149089), and had your city/state in your location (like you do now) and told someone you last name in a post (hey, you've done gave away your first name, what's the harm?). Then your stalker could easily look you up with all the personal information you have supplied, and pay you a visit...

I wouldn't want something like that to happen to me, that's why I don't give out my real name, or location anywhere. ;) You always have to look at all the options and risk factors or possible threats.

Dr Small
