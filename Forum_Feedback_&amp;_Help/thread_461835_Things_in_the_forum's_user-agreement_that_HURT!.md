### Post by Jenda on 2007-06-02
ken, I believe there is no way a forum, physical servers, or other objects of ownership could be owned by a vaguely specified community. We aren't talking about intellectual property here (which isn't actually property at all), we are talking about real, physical stuff.

By definition, property has to be owned by someone. Whether it is Ubuntu-geek or Canonical - what difference does it make to you? The forums are administered by a procedure carefully assembled by joint efforts from both the Forum Staff (headed by Ubuntu Geek) and the Ubuntu Community (headed in turn by the Community Council).

Just like Canonical can decide any day that it'll take down all its sites and sell all the servers and domains - and restrict the use of the trademark (!), it can happen that Ubuntu-geek will decide to take down these forums. Or that PDPC will stop running freenode. Or that the OSDL will stop working on the Linux kernel. Or that Google will go out of business.
They are all possible, and all just as unlikely.
What's more likely is that you and all the rest of us users will have to sacrifice a bit of our freedom for the fundamental value of respect to others - and the Forum Staff, Forum Council and Community Council will be the legislators and judges of that.

If you believe there is a better way of administering a forum or a community resource - feel free to describe it here, I'll be honestly interested.
