### Post by Dry Lips on 2012-05-09
On topic: I'm not especially fond of Google Analytics either. Why use Google Analytics when you have the option of using open source tools such as AWstats? Yes, Google Analytics gives webmasters some kind of information about the users of their site, but the problematic part is that Google collects and tracks this information as well. So no, this isn't about Ubuntuforums gathering information about us. I don't have any problems with that, you leave this information in the server logs anyway; the problematic part is that every site that uses Google Analytics becomes an extension of Google itself. 
> 
Due to its ubiquity, Google Analytics raises many privacy concerns.  Whenever someone visits a website that uses Google Analytics, **Google**  **tracks that visit via the user's** [IP address]("https://secure.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/wiki/IP_address").  If the website also embeds any content from a Google property with  which the website visitor has an account, then Google receives  sufficient information to identify the user and thus associate the  details of the website visit with that user.[[23]]("https://secure.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/wiki/Google_Analytics#cite_note-22")  Google has announced a new privacy policy** which will allow Google to  specifically identify and track users of any website that uses Google  Analytics**, if that user is also a user of any other Google product  (Gmail, YouTube, BlogSpot,etc) to which the same privacy policy applies.
[https://secure.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/wiki/Google_Analytics#Privacy_issues](https://secure.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/wiki/Google_Analytics#Privacy_issues)

Sure, there is a small percentage of us who use NoScript, but the majority of people on this forum probably don't. If Google paid sites for using G. A., it would be more understandable for them to do so, because at the moment you are really giving away information about your users to Google for free.
