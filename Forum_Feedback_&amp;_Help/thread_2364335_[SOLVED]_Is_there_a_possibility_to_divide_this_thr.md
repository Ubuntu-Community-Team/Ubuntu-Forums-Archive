### Post by coffeecat on 2017-06-23
> **Chanath said:**
> 
At least could we have a button/icon or something like that so, we can filter the entries on different DEs. 
If I am interested in looking for Xubuntu DE matters, for example, all other DE matters won't be of interest to me. I hope you'd understand what I mean.

More or less, and I've already told you how. Let me be more explicit.

Click on "Advanced Search" top-right of any page. Leave the keyword, username and tag fields blank. Now under "Additional options" choose 'Desktop Environments' in the 'Search in Forum' field, and 'xubuntu' in the 'search by prefix' field. Now click on the "search now" button and, voilà , you have all the threads prefixed with xubuntu in the Desktop Environments sub-forum listed. If you want to narrow your search, you can specify details in the other fields. You should also search for the xfce prefix, because those who have installed xfce in another flavour of Ubuntu will probably use that prefix, and the threads may be of interest to Xubuntu users. 

Which leads to why your suggestion of a number of flavour-specific sub-fora in the Desktop Environments section is impractical. Let's use Xubuntu as an example. People using Xubuntu itself would use the Xubuntu sub-forum - that's easy. But what about those who've installed their operating system using the standard Ubuntu installer, who have then installed the xubuntu-desktop package with all its dependencies, and who experience a problem in the xfce desktop? The Ubuntu sub-forum or the Xubuntu sub-forum? Strictly speaking, that's not Xubuntu, even though they're more likely to find those who can help in the Xubuntu forum. Some will post in one sub-forum, others in another. Or Kubuntu with xubuntu-desktop installed? Or Ubuntu with the xfce4 package and dependencies - that's vanilla xfce without the xubuntu-specific modifications. Xubuntu or Ubuntu? Or, heaven forbid, do we have Unity, Gnome, Xfce, Lxde and whatever else sub-fora as well?

It would be an absolute mess, and would involve significant unnecessary staff time, moving misplaced threads from one sub-forum to another, and trying to decide where on earth a thread about login page problems in Kubuntu with lubuntu-desktop and xubuntu-desktop packages installed belongs.
