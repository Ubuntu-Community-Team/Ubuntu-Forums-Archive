### Post by SeanBlader on 2009-12-04
I've been having a really great experience with the Koala, here are some things that I'm very happy with:

Network Manager - finally connects to the office network, hidden SSID, WPA2 enterprise, PEAP, MSCHAPv2, TKIP, and even integration and VPNC which works with my office where I can't even do that with Windows Vista or Windows 7.

Bluetooth - My Logitech mouse connects every time, and just recently I got an Altec Lansing/Plantronics Backbeat that works with A2DP on the fly.

Display - external DisplayPort through HDMI/DVI/VGA dongle works with office projectors, external display, and is pretty easy to setup, impressive.

I however might be exact target audience for Ubuntu, I've been a PC power user since the days of MS-DOS 5, I bought my latest system specifically for Ubuntu even though it wasn't supported or designed for it. It doesn't have a fingerprint reader or a touch screen, like my previous laptop. But literally all the hardware that I need on this system works well. It's lacking the use of the internal GSM card, but considering I don't want to pay an additional $60/month to get unlimited data that's fine. And ALSA is still a little low on volume, maybe 50% but I can make out sounds now.

The only thing that's keeping me stuck with windows at the office is Outlook/Exchange and IBM/Rational ClearCase at the moment, and coming up next year, I'm transitioning into Flash development. I could use one of the pair of VM's I have on my Ubuntu machine, one for XP one for Vista, but I use those to get access to IE6 and IE7 when necessary, but that's just not the best way to do it.

In any case I wanted to send thanks to all the developers that work on Ubuntu and Linux in general. I bought a polo shirt and a 5 pack of CD's to send around in trying to get it out there more. But it seems to me like the progress to Bug Number 1 is well under way. Nice job all.
