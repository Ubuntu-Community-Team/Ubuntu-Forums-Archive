### Post by glondor on 2010-09-05
Question from a newbie: Is it really necessary to host some of the forum's javascript on Yahoo APIs?

Web sites' reliance on JS and other services hosted by Yahoo, Google, etc. has exploded over the past couple of years. While it is convenient, I can think of at least two reasons why it is bad: (1) If their site goes down, so does yours. (2) From a privacy perspective, it concentrates too much information (IP addresses, referers, etc.) in the hands of a few behemoths. This provides one-stop shopping for marketers, nosy governments, and other unsavory snoops.

Couldn't the scripts be hosted on ubuntuforums.org instead?

glondor

### Post by Joeb454 on 2010-09-05
We've had a brief discussion in the staff area after lisati closed this thread, and here's a response that seems fitting :)

> vBulletin uses Yahoo APIs:

[HTML]<script type="text/javascript" src="http://yui.yahooapis.com/2.7.0/build/yahoo-dom-event/yahoo-dom-event.js?v=384"></script>
<script type="text/javascript" src="http://yui.yahooapis.com/2.7.0/build/connection/connection-min.js?v=384"></script>
[/HTML]

I believe the reason is because using Yahoo-hosted scripts puts less load on the server (instead of hosting YUI on ubuntuforums.org)

Hopefully, that answers your question :)
