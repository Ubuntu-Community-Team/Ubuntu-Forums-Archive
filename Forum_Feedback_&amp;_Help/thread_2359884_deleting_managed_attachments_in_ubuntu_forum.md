### Post by coffeecat on 2017-04-29
> **Nailhac said:**
> How do I delete files that are shown in this forum under additional options "Managed Attachments"??

You can delete attachments in your own posts in the way described below. Please do not delete your own attachments arbitrarily. Although post editing is allowed, the intention is that editing, including removing or adding attachments, is there for improving clarity where this is needed. If any editing is done that changes or obscure the original content of the post, this might be considered to be forum vandalism. Please see our relevant policy:

[https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2231107](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2231107)

Please be assured that comment is not directed at you. With many people reading these threads, I need to be sure that our stance on significant post content removal is well understood by all.

Having said that, this is how you remove an attachment.

Edit post -> Go Advanced -> Click on Manage Attachments button.

Near the bottom left of the Manage Attachments popup window, hover the mouse pointer over the thumbnail of the attachment you wish to delete. ? and X buttons will appear in the thumbnail box. If you hover over the ? or X you will see descriptions of their functions - "Delete" for X. Click on the X. Now click on OK for "Are you sure that you wish to delete this asset?" Close the popup window. You will now see something like this in your text representing your now-deleted attachment:

```
[noparse][ATTACH]123456[/ATTACH][/noparse]
```

If you leave that there, you will leave a link to a non-existent attachment in your post. Delete it all, being careful you delete the correct one if you have more than one attachment in your post. Now save changes and your post will appear without the attachment.

**Edit** Caveat: I'm fairly sure that what I've described is not an admin-only privilege. I've checked the somewhat labyrinthine permissions for representative non-staff usergroups, and it looks to be that those groups can remove their own attachments. However, vbulletin has sprung us some surprises before, and usergroup and sub-forum permissions can be quite tricky in practice. If you find my instructions don't work for you, let us know, and we'll all be the wiser.
