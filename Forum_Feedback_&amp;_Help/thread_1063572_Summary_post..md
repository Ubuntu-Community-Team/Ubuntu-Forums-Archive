### Post by UniqueCrash5 on 2009-02-08
Problem: Threads provide solutions, but are long.
Solution: Allow users to summarize them.

 Searching ubuntuforums is an excellent resource for solutions to problems.

 These solutions are in the form of a conversation with a minimum of three exchanges: a question asked, a solution suggested and a confirmation that the solution worked.  Often of course threads are far more complex than that.

 Most people searching the forums are looking for an immediate and complete answer that will help them with the similar problem they are having.  What they need is a simple summary.

 _Feature description: Summary post_

 An extra post placed above #1 in the thread, with the same title as post #1; in place of the post number is "Summary (x)". Three buttons replace the quote buttons: "This is a good summary" increases x for that post by one (casts a vote, logged-in users only), "Write a new summary" takes the user to a standard reply to thread screen (auto-populated with a quote of post #1) and "See other summaries (y)" (where y is the number of other submitted summaries) reloads the page with all submitted summary posts above #1 sorted by date each showing (x) and also with three buttons described above.  The summary post with the highest value for x is the one displayed by default.  In the event of ties no summary is displayed in the thread, but a button "Read summaries (y)" is displayed.

 Next to the "New Reply" button add a "Summarize" button and a "Hide all summaries" button (global setting for the user, replaced by "Unhide summary" button if used). The Post New Thread screen gets an Additional Option "Thread can be summarized?" defaulting to Yes.
 

Advantages:
 A Summary post provides users looking for solutions to a problem with a possible solution clearly stated, rather than having to be interpreted from the thread conversation.

 Most summaries will consist of a quote of post #1 and a quote from one of the reply posts - this makes them very quick and easy to make. Users who lacked a summary and had to hunt down the proper reply post can create a useful summary for others in moments - and get credit for it! You can easily tabulate how many votes total a given author has.  Many summaries will be references to another, better thread - right up front.

  Users who have been looking for a fix for their particular problem can vote for a posted solution with a single click, and create summaries almost as easily.  This makes contributing very easy and offers a reward (prestige, bragging rights) for it as well, encouraging good and frequent use of the function. It matches the Ubuntu philosophy well.

 Feature requires no new pages be built and can be constructed easily from existing forum elements with a handful of buttons and label changes.
