### Post by tonyr1988 on 2007-03-27
I *love* the [RESOLVED] feature. It makes scrolling through the forums to help people much easier. Unfortunately, many people don't use it (most probably don't know it exists). This would take a tad bit of coding, but wouldn't be too bad, and I think it would help the helpers:

A couple days after starting a thread (in the Help forums), the user is greeted with a notification (like the ones when updates break something) about their thread(s). It asks if their issue had been resolved or not. If it has, it changes appropriately. If not it stops bugging them (or bumps it for them, or offers to notify of changes, or something...or nothing :D)

I just think it would really help browsing through the Help forums without having to consistently open threads to find that they have already been solved.

### Post by kko1 on 2007-03-28
I, too, would like a way to more usefully separate resolved and unresolved issues, and wish people would use the feature more. Combined with a way to limit the forum search to only resolved *or* un-resolved threads (see [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=392210](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=392210) ), _this would work wonders for the forum usability._

If this is at all doable, I fully support the idea, for what my support is worth. :-)

Here's a couple considerations:
- The time for the question could perhaps be after two weeks of inactivity in the thread. That should be enough to see if the topic has "settled", i.e. no-one is actively planning to post in it. Then the feature would mainly do what's (IMO) most useful in this situation - serve as a friendly reminder for the thread starter to check if their thread is marked resolved. After this, like you write, it can be forgotten.
- I suspect some internal "tag" (whatchamacallit) needs to be set (per thread) when the question has been posed, so the user doesn't have to answer the same question again later.
- I don't think auto-bumping would be useful, I think it would only add clutter. On the topic of bumping - I think it usually makes most sense to at least *try* to search for information from other sources, and then post your findings. (A bump without additional information is a bit like pushing your problem on the others to solve for you, but I'm willing to admit that sometimes you may be at your wit's end, and can't really help yourself further without outside input.) Anyway I'm glad you included the "...or nothing" alternative. ;-)

kko1
