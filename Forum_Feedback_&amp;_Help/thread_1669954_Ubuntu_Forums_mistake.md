### Post by 3Miro on 2011-01-18
You had a similar problem, you resolved it. Your experience could have helped the other guy, or maybe not. None of us can be 100% sure about what the other person is asking. We have no access to their machine and we don't know in what state it is. Many of the people that ask questions are not computer/Linux savvy enough to explain what the problem is. At the end, we do the best that we can.

On the other hand, if you are not sure about the subject, then by all means, don't post. I sometimes see questions that cover areas beyond my limited expertise, then I am watch the thread out of curiosity, but I don't post, especially if I the only thing I can think of can potentially damage someone's system (like mess with xorg configuration).
