### Post by niteshifter on 2007-12-13
What follows is just my perspective on this. As always YMMV :) First a bit about my background: Long time Unix and GNU/Linux user (+30 years), also Windows (since Win 2.0) and Dear Old DOS. Technician, instructor and programmer having done work from in-the-trenches grunt coder to project lead.

It's always difficult for someone more experienced to pitch help at a suitable level: How do I know what you know? A formal classroom or workplace setting gives me the ability to quiz, test - and most important - observe the student. This is primarily a text-only medium of communication. I can quiz, but I can't test and that important one - observe how you set about solving the problem to judge what you need is completely blocked. I frequently worry about some of the answers I give to questions I see here - did they get it? Did I aim to high? To low?

"There is no such thing as a stupid question. Overly complex and therefore stupid answers abound". I got that gem of wisdom from one of my early Comp Sci professors - who immediately followed with the statement "If you didn't understand my answer - ask the question again ... and again ... and again, until you get what you need." You can, indeed must, do that here. Some responders may lose patience (a minority, from what I've seen here) - even so, so what? Somebody else will step in. It's a process and you supply the essential ingredient - feedback. Ubuntu - people helping / sharing with others - is a two-way street.

What seems to be suggested is not more categories for help, but breaking Absolute Beginner (and General Help) in tiers. I'll wager that won't work: Even were the forum to be set up for three tiers - Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced - whichever one gets the most traffic will become the place for all questions to be asked. After all, that's where the action is, right?

It would also open the door to distracting, petty bickering about which tier what question belongs in. The moderators already have enough on their plate without that. Believe me when I tell you as a long time observer of the GNU/Linux phenomenon, this forum is the nicest I've ever seen along with one (if not the most) useful I've encountered. When I settled on using Ubuntu last February I had questions, and I found answers here, in these forums. Even as a former Red Hat Rat (and a spell of build-from-source only). You might argue that of course *I* did - those over-the-head responses where exactly what I needed. Not so much. Some yes, but it was those sessions of back-and-forth between not-so-experienced user and the responder that were really helpful (in figuring out the "Ubuntu way". Various distros have their quirks).

So, to recap: If an answer I or anyone else gives comes across as alien lingo, tell us that. Say you didn't get it at all, or part X is unclear. Keep asking until you get what you need. Tell us you are a newbie to A) Linux, B) Ubuntu, C) Computers, etc. Help us help you!
