### Post by pizza-is-good on 2009-08-13
I have been asked a few times how to have links in Signatures and Posts that do not show the URL, but rather, is just a whatever you want it to be text.

For example: instead of having [http://ubuntuforums.org/group.php?groupid=574](http://ubuntuforums.org/group.php?groupid=574) you could have [Dell E-Laptop Users Group]("http://ubuntuforums.org/group.php?groupid=574").

This both reduces the number of characters, which will give you more space in your signature, and will make whatever you post or link to look cleaner and more professional.

My computer can only see attachments, so since I have more than 5 pictures, I'm going to explain 1 step per post.

Let me know if there is anything I missed or that could be useful to add.

OK, sorry that I took so long, I didn't know if the tutorial had been approved yet, and I had to sleep....:lolflag:
 
Anyway, step 1.
 
Go to where you want to post, either your signature, a post, a PM, or whatever else.
 
In the case of the picture, it is my signature that I was editing, but this will work for everything.

Step 2
 
OK, now on a new tab or other browser, go to the URL of the site you want to link to, in the case of my picture, [www.ubuntu.com]("http://www.ubuntu.com").
 
Then copy the URL.

Step 3
 
Go back to what you want to insert your link in, in my case, my signature, write what you want the link to look like, and then highlight it.
In this case Ubuntu.

Step 4
 
OK, now with your text highlited, click on the 'Insert Link' button. It is right underneath the Smilies one, and in the picture it is circled in red.

Step 5
 
When you clicked the 'Insert Link' button, an small window looking like the one in the picture poped-up.
 
Now paste the URL that you copied in step 2 into the pop-up, as shown in the picture.
 
Then click OK.

Step 6
 
OK, you are done!
 
The picture shows my signature with the link to the Ubuntu website without showing the URL.
(On my picture, the link is not on the bottom line as I already had four lines and couldn't fit a fifth)
 
I hope this is clear enough and helps.
 
Please let me know if I forgot anything, or if there is anything I might add to make this tutorial more clear.

### Post by schauerlich on 2009-08-14
Since you're going to be linking to something, it makes sense to use the URL tag. The URL tag is BBCode, which allows you to do fancy things with text on the forums. Here's an example of using the URL tag.

```
[noparse][All foobar, all the time!]("http://foobar.com")[/noparse]
```

Now, what does all of that junk mean? The section in quotes is the link you'd like to send people to. The text between the [noparse][ and ]( and )[/noparse] tags, on the other hand, is the text that will actually show up in your post underlined and that people will actually click on. The above example would look like this:

[All foobar, all the time!]("http://foobar.com")

### Post by aysiu on 2009-08-14
How about this, then?

If you want to post a link but have the link text be different from the link, this is how you do it.

Let's say, for example, that you want to post a link to [http://www.ubuntuforums.org](http://www.ubuntuforums.org)

But instead of the link saying [http://www.ubuntuforums.org](http://www.ubuntuforums.org) you want it to say *The Ubuntu Forums*.

You would just type this ```
[noparse][The Ubuntu Forums](http://www.ubuntuforums.org)[/noparse]
``` and it'll appear like this: [The Ubuntu Forums](http://www.ubuntuforums.org)

You can substitute in, of course, another URL for *[http://www.ubuntuforums.org](http://www.ubuntuforums.org)* and your own text for *The Ubuntu Forums*.
