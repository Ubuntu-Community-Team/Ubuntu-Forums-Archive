### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-01-05
> Miter_J
And then I send the admin a private message asking for the reason of closing the thread, but he didn't reply to me.

Perhaps, it might have been wiser to send a PM or private e-mail to the requester (OP of the thread requesting info on unblocking). This would effectively eliminate any need for censure. However, I would strongly recommend one confirm that said requester completely understands their role and the potential ramifications of their actions - prior to providing any advice.

The forums (in spite of the disclaimer in the COC)* restrict certain communications regarding topics that are perceived as questionable. For example, root access or unblocking. I understand this rationale, but also understand the need for individual responsibility. It's been my experience that all to often people don't realize the potential for reprisal. Finding themselves in (shall we say) an undesirable position with few options. :-k

The forums not only limit the liability by restricting communications of this nature. They also reduce the opportunity for members to be compromised when receiving instructions or advice on the forums. I've questioned this in the past (the concept of personal liability/responsibility), but after considering the potential for catastrophe - I must agree that the forums actions are not only logical, but justifiable. I believe that the goal of the forums (in this case) is to protect not only itself, but also it's members.

Especially, when considering the number of younger members who may have zero knowledge of potential ramifications to their actions. The forums are a very large community consisting of people from many walks of life and age groups. A mistake could cost substantially more than just being little embarrassed.

* Quoted from the COC:
> [COLOR=RoyalBlue]All posts made to these forums express the views and opinions of the  author of each post. Those liable for the content of posts and private  messages are the ones submitting the material, and not the  administrators, moderators, webmaster, the Ubuntu community, Canonical,  or anyone else.[/COLOR]

Just my $0.02, for what it's worth.
