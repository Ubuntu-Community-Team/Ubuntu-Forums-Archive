### Post by yc2 on 2011-09-28
I couldn't find a more appropriate category, so I put my post here, sorry if I didn't find the right board.

I am a member of the Swedish Loco. We had a support forum that worked well. The number of members was given as being around 15.000. That figure was way too high, though. It was based on all accounts in our phpBB forum, not on active members. But we had an active support forum, some members ran the test-version and we reported bugs when we thought we had found any.

Our status as official Loco was not renewed by the Loco council at their meeting in November last year ([https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoCouncil/Agenda/20101116](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoCouncil/Agenda/20101116)). We could still keep our domain name and run the forum on a server belonging to a Canonical employee.

Most members wanted to get our official status as a Loco back. I am not sure about all efforts made in this direction. But we now have IRC-meetings and use Launchpad for electing Team Leader/Contact. (I definitely think participation in meetings/elections has dropped since we now do not have these activities in our "own" forum.) The number of members participating in IRC-meetings often vary between 2-5. The members participating often vary from meeting to meeting. In my opinion we had more members participating when discussions were held in our "own" forum and not in IRC. Our meetings do not seem to decide on any vital matters, that is my personal opinion. There is not much to decide about, we mostly continue running our support forum. (We have always translated programs, had a web portal, bloggers, Wiki, ... IRL activities are few, but we have release parties.)

However, I do not see any real difference in our activities. On the other hand I think we have lost focus on our support forum - an activity we did well, IMHO. Activity is lower than before. The support forum is not considered an important factor for approving us as an official Loco and therefore members are not stimulated to participate in it.

_OK, I only give the Swedish Loco as an example. We can't discuss it here._ There are also many other factors involved in our specific situation, of course. To discuss this matter, I think we should please keep the Swedish situation out. I just think it is needed as a background. _I may have missed many aspects on the Swedish situation._

_**[COLOR="Red"]But my question is: Why can't an official Loco just run a support forum only?[/COLOR]**_ A support forum has a very clear goal and many members enjoy participating and also help other users a great deal. An active support forum in the local language helps spread Ubuntu considerably, that is my conviction. Discussions/meetings/elections can be held in the forum and not in IRC.

I am still not quite sure what is required to become an official Loco, but we don't seem to have been able to fulfill the demands. My thought is that **if Locos running active support forums (only) were approved, this would show appreciation for support forums and stimulate such activity**. Giving support is a very important factor for spreading Ubuntu.

I understand if the Loco should send regular reports to the Loco council about its activities, but **I can't understand why a Loco should be forced into activites it is maybe not capable of**.

[B]As I said above, I only give the Swedish Loco as an example. I hope we can leave its situation out of the discussion. I may have failed to represent it properly and going into the details from our history would make this discussion fail its purpose. (I still write about it here to explain why I ask this question.) My question is quite simply: _Why not let approved Locos run only a support forum and hold their meetings and elections there?_
[/B]
Sorry, I am on a trip and cannot correspond regularly, hope members still want to discuss this :)

Thanks for reading.

/ycc
