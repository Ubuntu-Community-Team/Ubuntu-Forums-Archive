### Post by coffeecat on 2013-08-07
I'll answer this more fully as this point is not fully understood. It is all covered in [this sticky]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2164051"), but there's a lot in that to get over and I need to see if the sticky can be expressed more clearly.

The initial association is done on first login. If your Ubuntu One preferred email matches an email in the forum database, then the two accounts are associated (linked) and you log into your old account. If the system cannot find an email match in the forum database, it creates a new forum account with that email and a forum username based on the Ubuntu One account name and again an association between your Ubuntu One account and new forum account is made.

Thus the emails need to match for the initial association, but this is not necessary thereafter.

Once made, the association between the Ubuntu One and forum accounts can only be removed by a forum admin. This means that once the Ubuntu One and forum accounts are associated (linked), you can change the email in either or both without breaking the link. You can make them different from originally and the same as each other, or different from each other - you won't break the association.

**EDIT**: just to add. If you change the email in your forum account, please remember to re-activate the account from the verification email sent to the new email address. Until you do so, you will not be able to post anywhere except the Resolution Centre.
