### Post by sudomania4 on 2006-06-02
Ubuntu-Geek has said we are adopting a tagging method for the dapper forums: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=1068475&postcount=4](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=1068475&postcount=4) and [http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=1076845&postcount=5](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=1076845&postcount=5)

Most users are not going to want to dig through three pages of irrelevant information to find a thread that applies to them. I know that we are adopting a tagging system, so why not make tabs that control the filter, for quick and easy filtering, without having to set things in a search. For example, in the "General Support" Forum, you would have a tab at the top with-"[general] Xubuntu 6.06" clicking this would ONLY show threads tagged as "[general] Xubuntu 6.06", to save users of ubuntu, kubuntu, and edubuntu from having to see these posts.

Another way to reduce the number of irrelevant pages seen when merely browsing the forum is to have an option in the profile after the part where you tell "additional information" and then "distribution" that says "only show threads tagged with my distribution". The problem with this method is it would only work with registered, logged in members, not newbie guests, who would easily become confused at the current layout, and wouldn't be familair enough with the site to "search" with proper paramaters.

Tell me what you think!
