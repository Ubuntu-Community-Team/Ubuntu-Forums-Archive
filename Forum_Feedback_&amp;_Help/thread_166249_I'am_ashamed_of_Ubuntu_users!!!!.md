### Post by kassetra on 2006-04-26
lmierzej: 

While your post(s) come off as extremely graceless, I will give you some answers nonetheless.

You say that people shouldn't answer posts unless they have, in your opinion, a solution, or have, also in your opinion, read the question.

If we were to start requiring either of your two definitions, we would close up shop today.

Now, I'll explain why.

The first - do not answer unless you have a definite solution.
If the human mind and all of it's paraphernalia were a completely identical/linear entity, then this approach would be sound. It's not. The human mind is anything but identical/linear. Computers, cars, and any other complex item that the human mind creates will most certainly also not be identical NOR linear. 

This leaves a situation where your fan and my fan, even though both items should technically be identically the same, are not. What works for my fan may or may not work for your fan.

Given THAT situation, if I give you as much information/knowledge as I have or can think of with how to fix your problem, it is very likely that none of the solutions I have will work for you.

Here is where the magic of the human mind as well as the willingness to help *EVERYONE* comes into play. While my suggestions may not help you solve your problem DIRECTLY, it is very LIKELY, that you will come across a solution while reading my suggestions and then trying them out. Even if I post something that may not seem like I even read the question, it may ALSO propel you to see the solution for your problem. The value in these forums is that everyone is willing to give you the best info they have, and that may be what you need to help you connect all of the dots of your particular problem and solve it.

By having a place where everyone is willing to help, even if they don't have the answer - more answers are found than in a place where this does not happen.

This forum has been criticized countless times for having "unknowledgeable" users answering posts. Yet, we have seen more and more solid answers come from our approach. I have found myself looking at a list of our threads when searching google for answers to problems.

The second - that in your opinion, people should read/understand the question before posting
This one follows closely behind my explanation above. People are offering advice based on what they know. They are understand the questions from their point of view, and all in all, people are reading and understanding the questions just fine - but not from your context, and so, you have deemed that they are not reading or understanding the questions. This is especially true when they give you information based on what they know and understand and it is radically different from your perspective.

Requiring people to have your understanding of your own question is an exclusive attitude and would prevent people from helping others, not encourage it.



We help countless number of people with real problems. We provide countless pages of documentation, howtos, help, solutions, and the like, answering real questions. Almost all of this information is spawned through a non-linear, non-identical pattern of question-response-more information-response, etc., and not question-answer. 

I'm sorry that our approach is not one you understand, but it works for the majority of people that use these forums when they have a problem. If we were to change it to the approach you are describing, we would be of no help to anyone.

Just to put this into more context:  Have you ever called technical support when you had a problem?  This is support that is paid for (even if through original product warranty).  The conversation is usually a lot of back and forth, question and answer, then several attempts at different things that might fix the problem.  This happens on even simple things (I have voip phone service and I had to go through several phone calls with tech support, huge amounts of time on hold, when it turned out to be the modem that was the problem, which someone from the cable company told me, not the voip guy).  It is difficult for someone remote from your situation to at first clearly understand what exactly is happening, what you are working with and exactly what you would like to have happen.  Then there are usually a few different things that may work in that given situation.  I don't know how you short-cut the necessary back and forth communication that fills in all the blanks.

There are several posts on this subject, I asked in a couple of different threads for help, got answers, that didn't work, but lead to different searches and different questions.  eventually I came across a thread that had an xorg.conf that worked for me.  It took me coming across someone that had a similar enough situation to mine to find my solution.  Of the 1000's that are on these forums, it can take time to come to the attention of the person who has succesfully accomplished what you are trying to do.  

Better the free flowing of ideas, that you are free to evaluate and try or discard at your discretion, than no responses as no one at the time has the perfect answer.
