### Post by Rubi1200 on 2011-06-03
As lisati said, it is a form of social contact.

Let me explain how I see it. Since joining the forums I have either asked other users to be my friend or been asked by others to be their friend.

It is a mutual relationship, meaning that if , for example, I ask you to be my friend and you accept then I will appear on your list and you will be on mine.

The decision as to whether or not you cultivate a friendship rests entirely upon you.

You are also not obligated to accept a request and can either ignore it or, I think, remove it from your list.

As an example, there are users I helped with problems who requested I become their friend. I willingly did so, but half of them have never even returned to the forums since that time.

On the other hand, I have friends on my list with whom I am in regular contact via private messaging either just to chat or, as is more often the case, to help each other help other users by asking advice on how to do/solve certain things.

In my opinion, it adds to the sense of community that makes the forums a great place to be involved with.
