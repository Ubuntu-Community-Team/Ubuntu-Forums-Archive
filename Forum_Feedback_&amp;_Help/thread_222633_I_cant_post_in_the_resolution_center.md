But it's a resolution center and not a court.  Since all the issues here are forum ones, everything is on black and white - what person A said and what person B replied is there for the whole world to see (unless the issue is deleted posts, in which case there is no evidence.)

What I am getting at is that the point of the resolution center is not to assign blame, but to change opinions.  I fear that "rules of evidence" usualy deal with objective things, and that the discussion may be set in stone before it even happens.  

What if the OP doesn't know if anyone else shares the same opinion, but would welcome any input?

To not set limitations does not mean that you will lose control - nobody wants that.  It just provides for a change of heart, opinion, point of view, etc...

One problem with the OP deciding whether to include others or not is that it has to be done before anyone else can chime in.  Things could get ugly if the OP forgets to mention that at the start and then decides that it should be a closed discussion simply because the input from the community is not favorable.

Perhaps the first step for an admin in responding to a post would be to make sure the OP states their preference?

Just thoughts...  I am happy this is being discussed.
