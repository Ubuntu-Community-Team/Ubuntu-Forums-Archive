### Post by mattheweast on 2006-07-31
You must attribute the original author if you copy material written by them on the forum, according to the terms of the license. If you get permission from the author to waive the terms of this license, i.e. put the document in the public domain, you do not need to attribute them.

However, note that the license deed also says:

> For any reuse or distribution, you must make clear to others the licence terms of this work.

This means that the license must be maintained on derivative works.

At the moment the plan is for the help wiki to be in the public domain, so maintaining the license is not possible. You should therefore always get permission from the original author to release the material into the public domain before adding it to the help wiki.

Matt
