### Post by arvevans on 2008-08-01
Posting the same question too often will get you ignored.  As stated earlier, we are mostly all volunteers here and do have lives of our own to attend to in addition to helping others with Ubuntu problems.  You are not paying for help from this forum and we are not getting paid to help you.  It may take anywhere from hours to days for you to receive a response if the problem is complex or poorly stated.

I did check on your earlier posts and found that you did get responses, which you apparently ignored or did not agree with.
[LIST=1]
[*]ICON ISSUE:  you got responses, even though your problem description did not make a lot of sense.
[*]JackD issue:  you got responses and a suggestion that you ask in a different group of the forum (because they would be more familiar with your problem), which you apparently ignored.
[*]Gpartd issue:  You got several responses.  You stated that you wanted to make a backup to a non-existing partition, and then have the data available after you had configured and formatted that partition.  At that point most of the support did fade away.  I suspect they all thought that you were not being realistic with your expectations.
[/LIST]

Profanity (even mild profanity) is usually not welcome on a public forum.  Drop the curse words and keep it clean.  When we see profanity, or even abbreviated profanity, many of us will just click on "next thread" and go on.

Please think through what you are asking.  An un-organized and incomplete request for help is almost impossible to work with.  You have to help us if we are going to help you.  Forum based help is usually a give-and-take interaction between yourself and several other persons.  Read what they say and think through your responses before you post them.  It is just like a face-to-face conversation, but with much longer time between statements.

You have the responsibility to try to resolve your own problems.  If you get stuck somewhere, then ask here for information to get you back on track for the fix.  Use the forum's "search" facility to see if your problem has been discussed and possibly solved in an earlier thread.  Do some Internet searches to see if additional information regarding your problem might be available there.  if appropriate, read the 'man pages' for commands that might relate to what you are trying to do.  Go to the Linux documentation pages and do the research to see if your problem is addressed there.  Then, if you are still stuck with the problem, post an easy to understand description on this forum.

Linux has a set of language terms that have become part of system and troubleshooting activities.  Some of these are the same as other operating systems and some are new and different for new Linux users.  Learn the accepted terminology for what you are trying to talk about.  This helps us understand what you are trying to do, and what obstacles you are running into along the way.

You mentioned a possible language barrier.  If English is not your native language, we will still try to work with you.  That possibly adds a layer of complexity, but it is not an impossible barrier.  Also, there are many different ethnicities, nationalities, and locations represented on this forum.  Someone will probably be able to help with translation, as long as you are willing to work with us in the process.

This forum has helped a great many people resolve a great many problems.  You are not unique or special.  Take your turn along with everybody else and you will find that things work much better.
