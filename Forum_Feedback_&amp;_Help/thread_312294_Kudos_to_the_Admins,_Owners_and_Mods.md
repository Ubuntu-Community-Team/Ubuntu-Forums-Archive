### Post by charlie. on 2006-12-04
An experience that I had, yesterday, on another forum has made me realize exactly how well run the Ubuntu forums are.

The forum in question was for a commercial product that I paid good cash for. I won't mention any names, but I will state that it had nothing to do with Microsoft, to prevent people from automatically jumping to that conclusion. The forum was run by mods that locked any thread that was critical of the product and insisted that any criticisms be made in a thread entitled "sour grapes", which completely glossed over the fact that 99% of the posts were real, "legitimate complaints".

They topped it off by locking the "bug report" thread and, oh yeah, limiting any thread to 10 pages of replies. Woot.

Nobody pays for Ubuntu so nobody really has the right to complain. That said, this community takes complaints seriously for a very good reason - if complaints were ignored, the product would suffer.

To compare apples with apples, one must compare Ubuntu's forum community with the support communities of other distros. I cannot understand why other distros (Debian included) generally don't even have official forums, choosing to use traditional mailing lists, news groups and IRC. IRC is real time, so, while being faster for people in the most popular timezone, it is useless for people on the other side of the planet. News groups and mailing lists are debatable. The community may be as good, but forums are a whole load more usable.

Well done all round.
