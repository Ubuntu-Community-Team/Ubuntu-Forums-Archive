### Post by Thelasko on 2009-02-17
Although I am disappointed, I understand the Ubuntu Forums staff doesn't want to continue maintaining the "Other OS Talk" section of the forums.  However, I must complain about the note at the top of the section that suggests users of this section turn to Microsoft for support of it's Windows operating system.  Many Linux/Ubuntu users occasionally have to use Windows out of necessity and may require help.  Posting a link to the Microsoft website is not an adequate substitute for this section of the forums for the following reasons:  

[LIST]
[*]Ubuntu Forums provides better support than many major support websites, particularly Microsoft's.
[*]Microsoft does not support many of the tasks these users are trying to perform (ext3 compatibility, some types of virtualization, etc.)
[*]Microsoft does not support much of it's older software.
[/LIST]

In conclusion, I recommend the staff suggest a more suitable substitute or leave this section of the forums in tact.

### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2009-02-17
Thelasko, thank you for your feedback.  We will be allowing other OS talk to take place the the ABT or GH sections of the forums using the [other] prefix on threads.
One of the benefits of removing Other OS Talk, other than helping to clean up the forums a bit, was to send some of the Other OS Talk traffic back to their respective communities.  This benefits these other OSes as well and is a responsible approach following our "housecleaning" here on UF.
