### Post by ajgreeny on 2008-10-14
You can get rid of the extraneous pages in OOo by hitting the backspace to put the cursor at the end of the last line, not on the next empty one, just like in windows and word, as far as I remember.  I don't think this is specific to OOo.

To turn a picture around, either horizontally or vertically, just right click in the picture and go to Picture, then to the Picture tab where what you are looking for lives.  Did you look in the help files?  I would be surprised if the info isn't there.

### Post by 73ckn797 on 2008-10-14
You can place the cursor at the end of the last sentence/line of text on the page and hold the delete key until all extra spacing past that point is eliminated.

You can also, while at the end of the line, press control-shift-end to highlight to the absolute end then press the delete key.

### Post by Kellemora on 2008-10-14
Hi K

We have over 600 msWord documents that didn't convert right, due to the fact we squeezed every last millimeter out of Word when making these pamphlets.

That may sound like a lot, but we did have over 35,000 that converted to .odt perfectly without a hitch in viewing or printing.

Although I have no way of proving this, my personal opinion is that the Open Office Writer page is slightly smaller in size than the msWord page.
Nonetheless, it is faster and quicker to fix a Word document in Word, then convert it over, than to convert it then try to fix it after the fact.
In most of our documents, we just use a quick search and replace using the hidden paragraph marker and change it from 12 point to 8 point.  This picks up the extra space on the page which makes the conversion work properly.

We use a LOT of graphics and found that OOw works nearly identical to Word.  It's just that you have to look in different places for the controls and some of them are named differently too.  For example:  Page Setup found under File in Word is under Format/Page/Page tab in OOw.
It just takes a little getting used to like when you get a new car and try to figure out where the darn buttons are now.

I think after you use OOw for awhile, you'll come to appreciate how much FASTER it is than msWord and that it don't suddenly move everything around to places you don't want them.  Our biggest problem with msWord was images can suddenly move nearly anywhere, sometimes completely off the visible pages.  We have NOT had that happen yet in 1-1/2 months of using OOw for most of our work.
