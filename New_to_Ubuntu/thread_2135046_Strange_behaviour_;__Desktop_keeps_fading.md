### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-04-13
Do you mean the whole screen goes dim, or is it just a window (or all windows)?

If the latter, it's showing that the program or programs are not responding. Usually this means that they've crashed or are doing heavy work, but it can also mean that your whole system is under heavy load. Normally this wouldn't happen without a good reason.

It's possible there's a program on your system that is running at full steam due to a bug. If you open up System Monitor, or use "top" in a terminal when the problem is happening, you should be able to see what program it is, and then kill it.
