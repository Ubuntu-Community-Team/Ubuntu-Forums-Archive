### Post by mapes12 on 2009-12-23
Lexmark do not provide open source drivers for Linux (or at least I couldn't find any after hours of searching for my Dad's printer). HP provide excellent support and most printers will be detected and work straight out of the box. See here: [http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/index.html](http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/index.html)

If your system doesn't do it automatically on detection of a HP printer you may want to install the following packages from Synaptic:

hplip

hplip-gui

HP scanners are also well supported. I have both a HP PSC 750 and a HP Deskjet 450 which work fine.

### Post by anaconda on 2009-12-23
samsung ML-2010
Bw laser.

Had it for 2-3 years, and it has worked perfectly with ubuntu. Autodetected and all.

I don't know, if it is already a bit old, but the reason I chose it was: 
it prints more pages with one laser-toner-cartridge than eg.(similarly prised) HP, and the new toner-cartridge is a lot cheaper than hp:s. 

One toner-cartridge for samsung 2010 lasts about 3000 pages and a new (non brand) cardridge costs ~39&#8364;. 
When you buy remember to compare how many pages a cartridge prints, and how much a new one costs... it is pretty important..

Eg. hp:s cardrige costs about as much as a new printer..
