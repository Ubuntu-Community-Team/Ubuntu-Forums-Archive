### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-12-25
Old computers work very well as home servers. I have an Atom-based home server (1.6Ghz single-core Atom, performance like a 1Ghz Pentium 3) and it does just fine. For home use, you don't need much power.

I do suggest getting a decent power supply. Not powerful, just decent brand and quality. It's going to be working every single minute of the year, after all, and the PSU you got with your cheapo case is likely to become a blue smoke generator well before your uptime reaches four digits.

My system uses the mini-ITX form factor, but that tends to be a bit more expensive, and often quite limiting. For instance, my server only has two SATA ports; they're both in use. On the upside, it's very power efficient. Make sure if you go down the Mini-ITX route, that the case you choose can handle 3.5 inch desktop hard disks.

Just run regular Ubuntu on it. I'd suggest using Ubuntu 8.04 or Debian Stable, because 9.l0 has been no end of troubles for my server.
