### Post by anasofiapaixao on 2009-11-28
(Note: actually, in this whole post 'we' = 'my friend and I', and she's the one with the computer in front of her dabbling with the interface for the first time while with me next to her, writing.)

We were just trying out empathy, and if the goal is to make it easier to your average user then they are failing at a couple of important points. **Most people will use it with the msn protocol**, there's no way around it; and certain tasks that are important to a msn user aren't properly represented in the interface.


**Zeroth**, we were puzzled right on the login screen, because after importing from pidgin it didn't prompt for a password. The contact window would not say anything either. We had to edit the account on the account manager, store the password (something she really doesn't want to do), and wait for the login process to finish - with only a blank screen to look at and to not have a clue whether it was actually logging in or not. An animated image saying 'logging in...' may seem pointless, but it's essential to give us a clue about what's happening.


**First**, it took way too long to find out how to make such a simple thing as changing the display picture/changing the personal message. Tried the edit accounts, tried the help which didn't say anything, finally went to the 'edit personal info' dialog. Why is there no showing both of the display pic and the personal message on the main contact window?



**Second**, why are smileys buried in top-window menu, and no button to put a smiley from the smiley list? Actually, why are there no buttons at all near the input window (changing font face/color/size for one), and why can't I change its size? When the window is maxed out it feels just weird to have such a small input window. A toolbar on it with common actions is also really important.


**Third**, still on the naked-chat-window thing, the help says audio and video is supported for msn, but the buttons do not appear when it says they should, and it's not nice not existing an accessible way to do it from the chat window. Even in the top menu it's there but deactivated.


**Fourth**, and that's something that had always been an annoyance in Pidgin, there is no easy way of adding a contact to an existing conversation. Actually, we haven't figured it out yet.


**Fifth**, we still haven't figured out how to deactivate logs. For my part I like the fact that logs are active by default, but not being able to easily deactivate loggin isn't good.


**Sixth**, first the chat window separators worked fine, but out of the blue opening a new chat resulted in a new window. We don't know what in the world may we have changed for that to happen. [update: now they show up again, and in the second separator either the display pic appears or the up menus work, but not both. And at random. It's seriously voodoo, the tabbing system appears still realy unstable.]


**Seventh**, though this has only little to do with the interface, one of the things I most loved about Pidgin was the fact that a plugin API existed and the plugins it already was shipped with, as well as the plugin management options being easily enough to access. I figure there may not be a lot of plugins for Empathy yet, but at least a plugin manager to manage plugins to come would be good.


Also, though this is minimal, the blue theme is way too dark. The themes for the chat window to separate each user's messages are a really nifty feature though.

A non-interface-related bug: I can't send/receive offline messages from wlm 8 to empathy. The other way around works, though (with me offline on empathy and online in wlm).



Maybe this is not the best place to give feedback on possible changes to the interface, but... I hope it helps anyway. I'm the first one to go for minimalistic interfaces, but there's still many essential (in the eyes of me, her, and probably lots of people who use msn on a regular basis) things missing. Found also [COLOR="Navy"]_**[this]("http://blog.thesilentnumber.me/2009/06/replace-pidgin-with-empathy-in-karmic.html?showComment=1245129696913#c2804172017331277774")**_[/COLOR] and [COLOR="Navy"]**_[this]("http://blog.thesilentnumber.me/2009/06/replace-pidgin-with-empathy-in-karmic.html?showComment=1245137826399#c780556914991733376")_**[/COLOR] and [COLOR="Navy"]**_[this]("http://blog.thesilentnumber.me/2009/06/replace-pidgin-with-empathy-in-karmic.html?showComment=1245175239666#c1537859467398652562")_**[/COLOR] comments on a random blog post found through a google search which sum some points up quite well.

### Post by shelterpaw on 2011-02-04
After using AdiumX on OS X, neither empathy or pidgin come close to the usability and features. 

One thing I like about AdiumX is the auto sizing of the client when more people come online. Another is the way you can customize the client appearance to an amazing degree, comparatively. My AdiumX client is 90% transparent with just the names in white. It also resizes extremely well and the window has tons of options.

As for showing the protocols people are using, the icon is clear and you can actually use the smiley for that particular protocol. In addition you have the option to use someone's picture they put up, set your own or use your address books picture for them. 

Obviously I'm just talking about the one's I personally use and like. Adium is opensource, but it uses the cocoa API, which is an OS X only thing, I believe. If I had the know how, I'd port it. But if a developer wants a guideline for a great IM client, Adium is it.
