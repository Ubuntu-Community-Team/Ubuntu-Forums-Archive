### Post by coffeecat on 2011-05-14
> **Bob Adams said:**
> I suspect that it may be because my printer is an HP Officejet 6500 which is not in Photoprint's list.

I suspect you are right. I installed photoprint (in Natty) because I was intrigued by your post. Now that I've tried it I can say nice idea, shame about the implementation.

Your HP Officejet 6500 is supported in Ubuntu as are my HP Officejet 6310 and HP Deskjet 9800, yet neither of my HP printers is listed in photoprint's list either. The only way I could get photoprint to print a photo to my Deskjet 9800 is to select one of the other deskjets, but the result was poor. Printing the same photo from Eye of Gnome gave an excellent result.

My guess is that you are trying to use one of the other HP choices in photoprint's inadequate list and photoprint is getting even more confused than it did with me. The only use I can see for photoprint is to make a montage of several photos, and even for that it's limited to setting your photos in boring square columns and rows. My only suggestion, short of finding better software for your purposes, is to use the export function in the File menu. You can export your montage to tiff or jpeg and then print this with eog. 

Bear in mind that photoprint is not Ubuntu supported software; it's in the universe repository.
