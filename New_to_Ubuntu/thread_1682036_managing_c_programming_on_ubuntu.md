### Post by GregBrannon on 2011-02-05
There's a sub forum in UF called Programming Talk, found here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=39]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=39")

Though I haven't seen your specific question there, I'm sure you'll find many useful things already discussed there, and it will be a good place to ask your future programming questions.  Review the stickies at the head of the forum, too, as there is a lot of good info in them.

As for your current question, what are you trying to manage?  Are you concerned about the number of files created, or keeping track of them?  Are you committed to gedit as your programming editor?  I'm not trying to talk you out of using gedit, but if you're overwhelmed by 4 files (which shouldn't bother you) for a small project, I'm concerned that when faced with the number of files resulting from a large project, you might go postal or harm yourself.

Please relax.  Using an Integrated Development Environment (IDE) may help you logically organize your larger projects, hiding some of the file management from you, thereby reducing your stress level.  There are many threads discussing IDEs in Programming Talk.  I don't use gedit for any size project, but many do use it for big and small projects.  Those with experience using gedit (vim, emacs, etc.) as a programming editor may have advice for you on how to organize your projects.

Good luck!  Keep programming and seeking truth!
