### Post by drmrgd on 2012-08-15
I would definitely mirror krustenBrot's advice here.  

In regard to build vs. buy, my personal preference is to build as you know exactly what's going into your box so that you can be confident that everything works, and that the components are sufficient quality for you.  It's my experience that the major vendors will cut corners where consumers are less likely to look in order to pad their margins a little.  For example, I was shopping for a new Dell, and called to find out the size of the power supply they were putting into the computer I was interested in.  I learned that it was only a 430 watt PSU, which concerned me greatly as I think that wouldn't have been enough if I wanted to grow the system as I intend.  It's also a LOT less expensive (for one particular computer I was looking at, it was about half the cost to build vs buy with similar specs - depending on how you look at the specs (i.e. see point #1)) to build your own, and if you have the know how, it's not much of a time investment to do so.  

The problem for you may be the need for Photoshop and Final Cut.  According to Wine DB, Photoshop CS5 is rated "Gold" and CS6 is rated "Silver" meaning that they'll run under Wine, but may require some Tweaking:

[http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?appId=17](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?appId=17)

I couldn't find an entry for Final Cut, which may mean that it won't run under Wine.  It could also mean that no one's yet tried.  

Native to Linux, there are equivalents.  For Photoshop, the open source equivalent is Gimp, which is quite nice.  However - and I'm not even close to being a Photoshop pro, but was comfortable enough in the program to do intermediate level tasks - I found there to be a pretty steep learning curve as I was trying to convert my Photoshop skills into Gimp.  Of course, you may find it easier to convert.  It looks like you can run Gimp on a Mac, and I would recommend trying it out to see if it works well enough for you to convert in the event that you can't get Photoshop to run reliably in Wine:

[http://www.gimp.org/macintosh/](http://www.gimp.org/macintosh/)

I know nothing of Final Cut and can't advise here.  But, I'm sure you can find a Linux equivalent here too.  I would recommend searching that out to find out if an open source alternative would work well enough for you.

Finally as krustenBrot said, it definitely worth test driving Ubuntu to find out its limitations as it pertains to what you want to do before taking the plunge.  I don't know the ins and outs of running a Live Session on a Mac.  I would recommend you download the .iso, make a bootable USB drive and try it out.  Worst case scenario would be losing a few hours playing around.  You can find the .iso and instructions for making a bootable live USB here:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick/](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick/)

When you boot from the USB, just choose the Try option rather than install, and you'll be able to play around with the OS without making changes to your current system.

Hope that helps!
