### Post by hetx on 2009-08-17
[http://wubi-installer.org/](http://wubi-installer.org/)

Wubi is a good way of trying it out. Basically you get a virtual hard drive installed Ubuntu 9.04. It'll run a little bit slower, but it's persistent (things are saved) and you can do anything you can on a normal install.

If you decide you don't like it, all you do is uninstall Ubuntu from inside Windows, and your computer is unaffected. No partitioning needed!

### Post by northern lights on 2009-08-17
Unless you do actually own a netbook, you want to download [this iso]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download"), burn it and boot from it.

Make sure that during installation, when choosing modi, to not pick "Guided installation - use entire disk".
Personally I'd advocate manual installs, but if your completely new to Linux and even partitioning is new territory for you a guided installation is the way to go. This choice is yours and depends on your general PC aptitude. Just avoid the common mistake of choosing "Guided installation - use entire disk" (first and default option), as this will wipe your windows.

Don't expect everything to work perfectly on the first try, but all further problems/mistakes can be tackled then - the forums are uber-active and you'll most likely receive near-immediate advice.

Good luck with your installation and enjoy your new OS!
