### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2013-08-03
Yes, you can install and use other DEs in Ubuntu. You don't need to move to another distro - and the couple you cited are Ubuntu based anyway -. However, you must keep in mind that you'll end up with some "redundancies", i.e., different software which is part of the new DE for the same task, and the probabilities of having conflicts noticeably increases. That said it's also true that most of the times is safe to install and later revert the process.
Here's an how-to for Cinnamon: [http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-install-cinnamon-1-8-on-ubuntu-13-04.html](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-install-cinnamon-1-8-on-ubuntu-13-04.html)
And for Enlightenment: [http://linuxg.net/how-to-install-enlightenment-17-e17-on-ubuntu-13-04/](http://linuxg.net/how-to-install-enlightenment-17-e17-on-ubuntu-13-04/)
