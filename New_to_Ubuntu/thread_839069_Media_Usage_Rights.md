### Post by shad0w_walker on 2008-06-24
I'm afraid to say you may just have to accept the fact. This is why DRM is bad. If the company stops running the server, or goes out of business, your license server is gone. There is no law that requires them to unlock the files if they go out of business or decide to turn off the server.


[http://yro.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=08/04/22/2130225](http://yro.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=08/04/22/2130225)  

I would suggest you atleast have a skim that, which gives details on how basically, consumers are only renting the content and that the company can choose to take away your access at anytime they choose with out any legal problems. It's written into the contract you agree to when you buy that sort of thing.

If you are lucky you maybe able to get them to respond and fix your problem or find a way to remove the DRM from them, however I can't help with the latter as it is (I'm pretty sure it counts as piracy) against forum policy, even though you have payed for the content.
