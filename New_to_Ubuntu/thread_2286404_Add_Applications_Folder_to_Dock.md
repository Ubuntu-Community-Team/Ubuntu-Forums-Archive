### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-07-14
Boom? No, not really...

Standard Ubuntu with the Unity DE has a way to do things and a workflow that's innovative: Everything irradiates from the left and, at the top of the launcher, there's a Ubuntu icon that lets you access all the available lens, where you search your contents (personal files, etc.) and your apps, in a very sophisticated and productive manner. There's neither a classic "start" menu nor a classic menu tree and they aren't needed. Your most used apps can and should be pinned to the launcher; everything else is accessible by searching with the first letters of its name, in the home lens or in a specific one. 

If you don't want to benefit from this, you have (and always had) the option to use a different desktop environment. Almost all the others except the new Gnome have the classic button and menu tree so, any Xubuntu, Lubuntu and even Kubuntu and thousands of other flavors and spinoffs would, apparently, be better for you. With those you'd probably feel "at home" and wouldn't be now finding unproductive ways in an otherwise top of the line productive environment.
