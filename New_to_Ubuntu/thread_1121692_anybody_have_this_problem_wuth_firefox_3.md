### Post by alphacrucis2 on 2009-04-10

> My bank has a site key the recognizes my pc
but when i delete my cookies i lose that and have to enter a bunch of question answers to get to the site key, anybody know how to selectively keep the appropriate history part, in firefox 3

Firefox does allow you to accept cookies on a site by site basis. You can set this up under Edit/Preferences/Privacy/Exeptions. The same Privacy menu has a show cookies button which allows you to manually select and remove cookies. There are also various Firefox Addons that do cookie management tasks. I can't recommend any myself as I haven't tried any.

### Post by _Purple_ on 2009-04-10
> **DarinB said:**
> My bank has a site key the recognizes my pc
but when i delete my cookies i lose that and have to enter a bunch of question answers to get to the site key, anybody know how to selectively keep the appropriate history part, in firefox 3

Go to Edit > Preferences > Privacy then remove the tick beside "Accept cookies from site". Then click the Exceptions button and add the trusted sites e.g. your bank site there. This will allow firefox to store cookies only from the sites in your white list.
