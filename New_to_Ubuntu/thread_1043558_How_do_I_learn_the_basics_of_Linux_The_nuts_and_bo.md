### Post by noelvh on 2009-01-18
I want to jump in here and share how I started to learn linux.
I installed Linux in a virtual machine under windows. I used VirtualBox, but you can find others, and some like VB are free.
Once I installed linux (Freespire linux) was my first distro. I made a back up of the VDI (linux virtual machine file). Then I dove in and when I screwed it up I deleted the VDI and copied over the back up copy, and I was back to the beginning with out having to reinstall it. Now freespire installs in 10 to 15 minutes, so I did not really have to back it up. Once I got a handle, I did a dual boot XP freespire. This is still good if you screw up cause you still have XP installed. The soon I ran just Freespire. Once I figured that one out, I moved to Kubuntu, now I run Ubuntu as I don't have the time to screw around with KDE 4, and its bugs. 

What I am suggesting is a way to get in there but not kill you system. And if you do screw up you will have a way out.

Any way gool luck and no matter what happens don't give up there is tons of help to be had here and other places.


Noel
