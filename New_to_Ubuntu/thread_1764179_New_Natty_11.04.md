### Post by bcbc on 2011-05-21
Yes it's possible - you can boot Ubuntu and use the Update manager to do this. As a precaution you should first boot Windows and backup the \ubuntu\disks folder (this contains the virtual disks and will be large) and the C:\wubildr file. Then if something goes wrong you can just copy them back over to fall back to 10.10

Yes your applications will be there - they'll be updated e.g. openoffice to libreoffice. I suppose some obsolete ones might be removed but it should prompt you first and you can stop this.

Yes you will need extra space - the upgrade should tell you how much it needs, but I have heard about people running out of space during the upgrade, so don't do it if you are tight on space.

PS the upgrade could take 3 hours (maybe more depending on your internet connection). I should also caution that many people seem to have problems upgrading (even on non-wubi installs). So consider that - maybe a reinstall might work better for you. It might also be a good time to switch from Wubi to a normal install.
