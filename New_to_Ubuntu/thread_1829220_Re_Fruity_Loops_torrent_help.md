[/INDENT][My Reply @kaasproav]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=1031645"):

Instead of asking for help with breaking [copyright law]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright") another option might be to seek out an [alternative to]("http://alternativeto.net/") the software in question.  A musician friend of mine turned me on to an [open source alternative]("http://www.osalt.com/") to Fruity Loops called [LMMS (Linux Multi-Media Studio)]("http://lmms.sourceforge.net/")  which he claims is nearly identical in how it operates.  The benefits  of using proper open source alternatives are many, but in case you were  unaware, let me list a few of the most common:


[LIST]
[*][Open source software]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_software")  is FAR less likely to contain viruses or malware than pirated "warez"  are due to it being harder to hide something in a completely open  program.
[*]Popular open source software tends to be updated frequently, so you can expect new features and bug fixes often.
[*]Open  source puts YOU in control of your computer, instead of some faceless  corporation somewhere who may or may not have your best interests at  heart.
[*]The open source "community" tends to have far more  support options than typical commercial software and usually tend toward  being much more knowledgeable and helpful.
[*]Running software  native to the operating system you are on will always give better  results than emulation layers like WINE, and one benefit of most open  source software is it tends to come in native flavors for most of the  popular OS's found on hardware today.
[/LIST]
Of course I've listed  only a few of the benefits of open source here (some of the ones that  matter to me specifically) but there are a great many more benefits  which can be easily discovered with a little research on the Internet.   Explore a few of the links I've embedded in this post and do a search or  three on [Google]("http://www.google.com/"), [Bing]("http://www.bing.com/"), [Yahoo]("http://www.yahoo.com/"), or whatever other favorite search engine which you prefer.  You may find that you prefer Freedom.  :grin:
