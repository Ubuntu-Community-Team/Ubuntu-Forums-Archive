### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-05-23
I do not agree with those who say 11.04 is buggy. I tested 11.04 beta 2 and there were only a couple of bugs and they solved before the 11.04 distribution was released for upgrade.

I see lots of reports of people breaking their system by changing the setup. A lot of these people are doing things that they do not fully understand and that is the cause of the problem. Others assume that they can modify the system in the same way that the have modified 10.04 and 10.10. Try doing that with 11.04 and the Unity/Ubuntu User Interface and you will break it. This is the cause of a lot of problems.

If you like messing around with the setup then use Ubuntu Classic as the UI.

If you want a good, fast, stable Os to get on with some work, then use 11.04. I am. I recommend it.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-05-23
One of the main reasons for trying out a new release in LiveCD mode is that there is no basic roll-back capability in Ubuntu; meaning, that if you do the upgrade and run into serious problems that can't be fixed -- you're stuck!

Your only recourse then is to completely remove what you have and reinstall from scratch -- a LOT of work just to try out a new version.

Would suggest you look into using Clonezilla to image off your present setup to an external drive.  That way, if you DO encounter serious problems with the upgrade, you can easily restore your old, working, version.
