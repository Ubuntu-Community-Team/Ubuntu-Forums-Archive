### Post by santosh83 on 2012-04-02
> **chitragurung said:**
> where can I find it ?

It would be available in your system's repositories, maintained by Canonical. Just open Software Center (Applications->Software Center or Applications->Add/Remove Programs) and search there for LibreOffice.

[http://doc.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/switching/applications-installing.html](http://doc.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/switching/applications-installing.html)
[http://doc.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/switching/applications-equivalents.html](http://doc.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/switching/applications-equivalents.html)
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LibreOffice](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LibreOffice)

On a related note, since Microsoft uses proprietary black-box formats for its documents (including docx, though it's more 'open' than doc was), which also tend to keep changing in undocumented ways with every update of MS Office, free software will always struggle to read/write MS document formats. As such, LibreOffice and OpenOffice do a surprisingly good job.

Also it's possible, with varying levels of success, to get MS Office working natively under Linux, through WINE [(http://www.winehq.org/]("http://www.winehq.org/")) or [Crossover]("http://www.codeweavers.com/").
An even better way is to run Windows concurrently on Linux through [VirtualBox]("https://www.virtualbox.org/") or [VMWare]("http://www.vmware.com/products/player/") (provided your hardware has support, and you've enough RAM installed), and use Office from within Windows.

Hope this helps.
