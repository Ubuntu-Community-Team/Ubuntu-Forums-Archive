### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-08-31
Yes, you CAN upgrade, but NO you should not.  Why? Because Oneiric is still in Alpha stage about to start Beta testing.

Which means, if you WANT bugs, upgrading to a pre-release version is exactly the right thing to do!

You would do better waiting until 11.10 is well into the beta testing before you attempt an upgrade.

And, remember, there is no way to "roll back" to what you have now if you find 11.10 to be too buggy.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-08-31
You can always do what I have done: Create another partition and install a newer version in the new partition and then test it before upgrading your working Ubuntu. I have done that with 10.10 and then 11.04 starting with Beta 2 Edubuntu and now 11.10 starting with alpha 2.

I will not upgrade my working 11.04 to 11.10 until I see that it is stable and 11.04 is stable, by the way.

It all depends on what you call a bug.

Some people think that unity was implemented too early. It is not buggy. It is not a finished product in the sense that there are plans to develop Unity still further so that by 12.10 it will look very different from what we see now and do a lot more.

Some people like to mess around with their system and when they mess it up they claim the OS is buggy.

Some people find that they cannot do what they want to do and they say - buggy.

Try out and test for yourself. Protect your working system.

Regards.
