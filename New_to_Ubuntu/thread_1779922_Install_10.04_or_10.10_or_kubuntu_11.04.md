Hi, Ngubk.  I use Ubuntu 11.04.  I upgraded just as you did.  I experienced some problems with the difference.  I was tempted to run the Ubuntu Classic login option, but continued with the default.  I'm sure I had just as much frustration as you, probably more, but so far I have hung in there since the release.

I decided to give the designers a benefit of doubt that some wisdom exist with their work and to experience their efforts.  After during this I find that most of what I was having problems trying to do, I probably didn't have to do it anyway, but was just trying to do it because it was a habit.  There's a new way of doing lots of the things I had been doing before, and it's just a matter of exploring and getting familiar with the environment.

At first, the familiar Visual Effects configuration option of the right click on the desktop (or through the System->Preference menu) was much missed.  However, I eventually learned that you can get the wanted Effects through CCSM.

At first CCSM was a pain also.  It still is to some degree, but I'm learning how to use it.  I probably would have never, or hardly used it if it weren't the only way (that I know of) to get the desired visual effects.  It's very likely that some of Canonical's reason for removing controls such as that is to have the users become familiar with using these features of the environment (such as CCSM), for feedback.  The feedback wouldn't be as complete with other options to skip them.

So, I'm biting.  I'm trying to use the environment as intended and asking questions on the parts I can't understand, or can't get to.

I believe doing it this way will help me keep up-to-date with a much tested environment which will grow with Canonical.  It's almost like having my own team of developers under the hood, and I'll contribute with my feedback.

In direct response to your question, there are lots of 10.04 (users of the long-termed option preference) who will suggest 10.04.  Maybe they will tell you why and there reasons will suit your purpose.  I tested kde and it was too much different from the environments I had been familiar with (over the users starting out with Slackware -> Red Hat -> Fedora -> Ubuntu)... having always used the Gnome desktip... it was too different for me... but again, some of the users who prefer the KDE Linux flavor will give you their reasons which might coincide with yours.

In my case, I can get to the options of my computer task.  Getting there is a bit different, but I can get to all of them, even if I have to create a link for my frequent task on the desktop (or place the menu in the side bar).

You might have already posted your problems with 11.04 and couldn't find resolution.  If you haven't, my suggestion is for you to use the 11.04 and flow with the waves.  There might be a good chance that you, like me, can do without some of the things that you were doing before as long as your application will run in the environment (and it probably will), or post a question of how to perform the task that you can't get to.

Oh yea, every once in a while I run “gnome-panel” in a terminal window to perform something (like when tweaking with CCSM breaks something).  I kill the gnome-panel with ctrl-c after I finish.
