### Post by jre on 2011-03-12
PeerGuardian and PeerBlock (both Windows) and PeerGuardian Linux, moblock/blockcontrol/mobloquer and iplist all allow to add your own blocklist sources. All blocklists that I know are available at iblocklist.com. Choose those that you need and add them to your IP blocker application of choice.

These lists are all made by humans (except those for defined ranges like the IANA lists), and serve their respective description. Some will be better, some not, but you will never get 100%!

Further you have to configure your application correctly: If you have a quite restrictive blocklist setup many websites will be blocked completely. Therefore many disable the blocking on port 80 - which will give you back all websites, but also the ads on them.
