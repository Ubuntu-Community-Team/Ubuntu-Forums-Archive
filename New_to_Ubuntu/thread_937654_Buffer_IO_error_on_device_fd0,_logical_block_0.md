### Post by Orange_and_Green on 2008-10-04
Hello! Welcome to the Ubuntu world!:)

fd0 is the floppy disk. It probably means your floppy disk drive is defective. Try going to BIOS (right after switching on your PC, it should say what keys you need to press to enter setup. It's usually F2, del, or esc, but it depends on your PC's manufacturer) and disable the floppy from the BIOS menu if your BIOS gives you that option. If your BIOS doesn't allow you to disable the floppy drive "the soft way", you might want to open the computer's case and physically pull the plug from the floppy drive.

To install Ubuntu you need an Ubuntu install CD. You can [download it from here]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download") (it'free! :)) but if connecting to the internet is a problem, you can [order a full DVD from here]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/purchase") (for a really small fee, basically just the cost of the DVD itself + shipping), or even [request the CDs to be sent to you for free]("https://shipit.ubuntu.com/").

The installation process itself does not need an internet connection.

Have fun with Ubuntu! Good luck! 

Good luck!
