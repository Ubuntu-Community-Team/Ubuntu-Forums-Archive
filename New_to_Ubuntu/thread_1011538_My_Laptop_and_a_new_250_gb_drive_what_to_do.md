### Post by laffinet on 2008-12-14
Have you used (X)ubuntu before ?

If not, I would suggest you dual boot. Gives you the added safety of being able to use XP if something goes wrong. Plus, once you find yourself using Ubuntu more and more and hardly booting into XP anymore (which normally happens) you can still wipe XP from the drive and have an all Ubuntu computer.

### Post by k3lt01 on 2008-12-14
I put a 250gb in my Acer a few months ago. Just went straight Ubuntu with a 10gb /, 2 gb swap, and 238gb of /home. 

My advice would be to forget Windows and install Ubuntu, then if you also want Xubuntu go to Synaptic and install the Xubuntu Desktop package. After a while, say a few months when you have the swing of things with either X/Ubuntu, you can remove the one you don't want and have all that extra free space in your / partition.
