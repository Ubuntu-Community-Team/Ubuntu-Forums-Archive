### Post by Cypher on 2008-05-30
Pidgin has an IRC client..to use IRC you would connect on a particular IRC server and then choose a channel on that server. So to get onto the Ubuntu channel, you'd connect to irc.freenode.net and open up the #Ubuntu channel. 

Once connected to a channel, you'll join the ongoing conversation midstream..on busy channels you'll see a lot of traffic. Communication here isn't necessary one to one, so you'll see a lot of "@<username>: text:" and so on..

The #Ubuntu channel is a good place to get immediate responses on questions..

### Post by Joeb454 on 2008-05-30
Most IRC clients will have the IRC details filled out - they will often say "Freenode" which will automatically connect you to irc.freenode.net on the correct port (6667).

Channels are preceded by either a # or ## it depends on the channel (I won't go into more specifics).

Other than that there's not much more you need to know :) just join an Ubuntu channel, and you shouldn't go too far wrong ;)
