### Post by jonest1 on 2009-12-26
In truth, the reputation of Linux + sound is not very good, but is getting better.  My understanding is that different hardware and software gives different results.

For my part, I usually just do playback and am not into creating music, podcasts, and the like.  I choose sound cards which are well supported by Linux and have been pretty happy.

If you want to research the topic, the default sound service in Ubuntu is called Pulse Audio.  There are also a good deal of jukeboxes and sound editing tools (ie:audacity, amarok, songbird).  It's a recipe without a good answer, but it is getting a lot of attention from developers.  

If you're into sound creation and editing, check out jack.
    [http://jackaudio.org/](http://jackaudio.org/)
The applications tab gives some neat apps which utilize jack.  These apps should be available from the Ubuntu software repositories.
