### Post by bobbob1016 on 2008-07-04
If I remember correctly, you need to have /usr on a different partition.  I usually only have /home on a different partition, /home is where all the settings are, so you should keep that on a different partition too.  Then when you reinstall, or upgrade fresh, you need to tell the installer that your /home is whichever partition, and your /usr is whichever partition.
