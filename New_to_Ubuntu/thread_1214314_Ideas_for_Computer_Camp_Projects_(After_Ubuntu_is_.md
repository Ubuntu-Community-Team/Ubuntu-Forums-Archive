### Post by Darko Beta on 2009-07-15
Hi! I'm teaching a computer camp this week (10-11 year olds), and we have successfully installed Ubuntu, and gotten our wireless up and running. Since then, we've tinkered with our wallpaper and the desktop appearance a little bit.

Does anyone have recommendations for fun projects we could try in the next few days? ### Post by CatKiller on 2009-07-15
Showing them how to install stuff probably wouldn't take long :)

You could run through a design project, setting some specifications for an image, say, or a logo, and getting some students to actually interpret your specifications, some others to sketch it, and another group producing the final image in the GIMP or Inkscape. The more broad you are with your specifications, the more decisions will have to be made by the group, and the wider the feeling of ownership would spread. Although I don't really know how much "fun" that would be :)

If you have access to some kind of sound system, they could try some collaborative music. I'm a strictly analogue man myself, so I don't know exactly what's available, but there's probably something that would be quite straightforward to use.

Actually, the Edubuntu project might already have some suggestions for projects. That's probably worth checking out.
