### Post by jpoRS on 2009-01-19
Hey,

There are a few things you should try before you break out the big-problem solutions.

0. Is this YOUR wireless, or is it some sort of institutional wireless?  If you are getting your wireless from a school/business/coffee shop/whatever, you may need to do something specific (register, pay, enter a reciept code, I don't know what your situation is.) to get wireless to work on any computer. If this is the case and you are registered/logged in for Windows, you need to re-whatever for Linux.

1. Reset the router.  I have noticed that Ubuntu is more sensitive to a "tired" router than Windows.

2. Change the wireless security settings for the router.  I forget the particulars, but I know Ubuntu has difficulty with some wireless security protocols.

3. I know you said you don't currently, but try and do a wired connection, see if that works.  That will tell us if your problem is the Internet in general, or just wireless.

4. If possible, try a different wireless source.  That will tell us if it is a problem with your network, or wireless in general.

I don't know the particulars for your system, but nine times out of ten Linux doesn't need drivers.  Maybe your hardware is on the shortlist that does need drivers, but for the most part you should be fine.

If you are on the short list, there are ways to get the drivers in XP and transfer them (via disk or other removable media) to Ubuntu.  But I am not 100% on the particulars so you will have to seek that information out.

Good luck and welcome!
