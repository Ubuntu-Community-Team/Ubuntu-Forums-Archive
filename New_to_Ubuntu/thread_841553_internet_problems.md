### Post by dca on 2008-06-26
You can try installing the NTFS Config utility from the 'Applications' Add/Remove selection.  It will put a small app in your 'Applications -> Accessories' or System Tools sub-menu.  Plug your drive in, select the app and put a tick in the 'enable write support for ext drive'..  Other than that it's easier to re-format w/ FAT32 file system because Linux & OSX have native support for it and gives you ability to go back/forth Linux/Windows...
