### Post by hellion0 on 2009-08-06
Tell me, do your hands shake? If they do, this may not be something to try.

Get a Dremel tool or something similar, put a very thin grinding wheel on it, then VERY CAREFULLY cut a slot into the top of the screw. After that, you should be able to get it out with a regular slotted (flat-head) screwdriver.

I've encountered the same problem with an iBook, and that's what I did to remove a stubborn screw like you have.
