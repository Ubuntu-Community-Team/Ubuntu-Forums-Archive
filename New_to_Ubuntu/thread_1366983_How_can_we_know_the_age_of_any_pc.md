### Post by lavinog on 2009-12-29
Many motherboards have a datecode on them.

The hard drive will also have a date on it.
You can also look on the CPU and IC packages, but many manufacturers stockpile them for several months.

Sometimes even the case might have a QA stamp

Bios will usually only have the firmware date, which can be newer than the computer.

No, in my opinion, not possible, even if you find when files were first created on the system you have no way to know if the hard drive is original or not, or if it has had the OS re-installed, or for that matter, any of the rest of the computer's hardware.
