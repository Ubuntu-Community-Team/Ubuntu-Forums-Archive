### Post by Impavidus on 2018-09-20
No upgrade from 17.04 to 18.04 was ever designed as 17.04 was already end-of-life before 18.04 was released. What you were supposed to do was upgrade to 17.10 sometime around last December and then to 18.04 sometime around last June, but it's too late for that now. Backup your important documents and try a fresh install of 18.04.

### Post by Autodave on 2018-09-20
Every 2 years, a long term service release is made.  !8.04 was the last one: released during the 4th month of 2018, hence the name 18.04.  The next LTS release will be in 2020 during the 4th month.  In the meantime, a new, short term release will be issued every 6 months.  These short term releases are only supported until the next short term release. However, the LTS releases are supported for 5 years.  It is always best to stick with the LTS releases. When it comes time to upgrade one of those, I have always found it easier and quicker to just backup what I need to keep, wipe out what is there, and do a clean install.

### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2018-09-21
Just to add a bit to Autodave's excellent advice.  Back before Lubuntu released LTS versions, those of us that used Lubuntu had to upgrade at every release because of the EOL feature.  After Lubuntu released their first LTS version with 14.04 this no longer became necessary.  We could maintain the release for two years much as the users of the "Mother" distro, Ubuntu, do.  The need to upgrade to the interim releases was almost eliminated for the normal every day user unless he had to have the latest and greatest.  We do not have the luxury of keeping the LTS release for 5 years as Lubuntu is only supported for 3 but by then a new LTS version has been released.  This saves you the hassle of upgrading every six months.  You can upgrade every 2 years or do a fresh install.  Your choice.
