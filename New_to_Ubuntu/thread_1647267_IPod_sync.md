### Post by earthpigg on 2010-12-18
> Could you imagine if Apple or Microsoft were to say something like that?

Microsoft and Apple have employees that can be told what to say, and those employees will either toe the "party line" or possibly get fired.

This isn't the case with Free and Open Source Software, so you will frequently have a diverse range of opinions and statements about the exact same issue that may come off as curt or even rude to folks used to the normal customer-salesperson paradigm.

Have you read [this]("http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm") yet? It doesn't have much use for people that only ask "how", but for people such as you and I that are curious enough to want to know both the "how" and the "why" it can be very enlightening.

There is no "party line" with Linux, Ubuntu, or Open Source in general. Everyone kind of just says whatever they feel like and so long as the coding, bug reports, and development continues... no one cares.

>

If you want to see something radically different, look for videos demonstrating one or another of the *[Tiling Window Managers]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiling_window_manager")*.

Suffice to say, Gnome is just the beginning.
