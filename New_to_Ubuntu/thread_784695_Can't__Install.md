### Post by mlentink on 2008-05-06
There's another thing you might try.

If you've downloaded the .iso, burn it at a slow speed (please remember to use the iso option in your burning software, do NOT burn it as a regular cd).
Pop in the cd in your cr-drive. Turn off your machine. Turn on your machine and hit the button to select the bios-boot-options-menu (differs on different systems, usually F9 or F10). Select to boot from cd. Boot from cd.
Does Ubuntu run? If so, it is capable of running on your system. If not, the specs may not be what is needed or there may be another hardware problem.

If the live-cd runs, getting ubuntu installed is usually only a matter of time.
