### Post by loomsen on 2009-03-19
No, I'm not able to see any Pros of not going with the latest upgrades available. Other than proprietary software, where updates actually are patches, open source software does not have a certain goal. It's more like ongoing development.

But a stable system can be of some advantage in times as well. I.e. writing your master thesis, you bet you wouldnt want to suffer from a system freeze including data loss.

I'm usually running two OS therefore, almost all the time. The current stable release as my primary reliable OS, and as soon as theres alpha 1 of any new release out, I'm installing it too.
But I like the bleeding edge a lot, sometimes I even cut myself to see how much it bleeds. :D

Still, get your upgrades n -dates folks, you're omitting functionality otherwise imho.

### Post by mkvnmtr on 2009-03-19
So far at least you can upgrade from one long term release to the next long term release. What you can't do is upgrade from for example 6.10 to 8.04.
Probably the best thing to do is have another home partition. Then if you upgrade and don't like it you can just reinstall the one you like and still have all your settings. 
For example if you have your 8.04 just like you like it you can make an disk to have to reinstall it just like that. Then when you upgrade and don't like it you could put it back the way you had it in about an hour.
