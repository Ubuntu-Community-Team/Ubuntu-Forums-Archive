### Post by Isaacgallegos on 2011-03-09
I use a netbook and have no middle mouse button, other than trying to press both left and right at the same time, which is very hard to do on my touchpad. Doing pressing both while dragging is impossible to me. 

Document Viewer (I don't think it has another name) has a "flick" function that should be on the left (or primary) mouse button, instead of the middle mouse button. 

Why just using the scroll wheel isn't as good:
The scroll wheel on my touch pad works for horizontally and vertically. However, this can be slower, than the flick function, and snaps to specific increments, rather than allowing for infinite increments, as the middle mouse button currently allows. 



To see what I'm talking about, right click and save this casio owner manual. It's a pdf:
[http://pathfinder.casio.com/resource/manuals/module_2471.pdf](http://pathfinder.casio.com/resource/manuals/module_2471.pdf)
Open it in document viewer.
Once there, hold down the middle mouse button and drag on the page to move it.
