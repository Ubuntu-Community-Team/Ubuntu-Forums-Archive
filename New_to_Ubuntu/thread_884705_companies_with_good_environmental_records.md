### Post by Thingymebob on 2008-08-10
Under WEEE regulation, any company in the UK selling electrical/electronic goods has to, at no expense to the consumer, provide a facility by which a similar used item (that is being replaced) can be safely disposed/reclaimed/recycled. What this means is that if you go and buy a new laptop from a retailer you can leave your old one on their counter and expect them to get rid of it responsibly at no charge. 
[QUOTE=UK Environment Agency]The WEEE regulations also affect retailers and other distributors who sell electrical and electronic equipment (EEE). If they’re selling EEE to the public, retailers will have to ensure that their customers can return their WEEE free of charge. This will be on a one-for-one basis, as long as the new equipment is of a similar type and has the same function as the old equipment.[/QUOTE]
By this, they probably all have a similar record

### Post by cariboo on 2008-08-10
You have to remember that most laptops are manufactured by a couple of companies in Taiwan. The recycling of computer components has nothing to do with the manufacturers but with the country, state or province they are sold in. This past January here in British Columbia they finally enacted a law that resellers have to pay a recycling fee for all computers, monitors and ups they sell. It is up to the reseller how much they charge the customer for recycling.
