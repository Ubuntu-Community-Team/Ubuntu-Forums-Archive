### Post by khelben1979 on 2012-09-07
I believe that part of the reason is because your Ubuntu installation are using open source drivers for your graphics. You need AMD Catalyst or nVidia official drivers for that. The open source drivers are less efficient + forces your cpu to work harder making you get less battery life left.

From what I know, Windows 7 is more efficient with your battery life than any Ubuntu installation no matter how it's configured. I'm certain though that you can increase the battery time by doing some additional configurations regarding power management.
