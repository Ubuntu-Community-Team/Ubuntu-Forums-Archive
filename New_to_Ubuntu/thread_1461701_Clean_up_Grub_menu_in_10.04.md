It depends on how far you want to go in changing things. We recommend you keep two kernels as standard in case issues develop with the new one you can go back and use the older one.

Custom menu:
[http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/bootinfoscript/index.php?title=Boot_Problems:Custom_Menu](http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/bootinfoscript/index.php?title=Boot_Problems:Custom_Menu)

### Post by Paqman on 2010-04-24
The easiest way to clean up your Grub menu is to open up Synaptic and remove all your old kernels. Search for "linux-image" and remove all of them except your current, and maybe a spare if you want. Uninstalling them automatically removes them from Grub, and frees up a lot of disk space.
