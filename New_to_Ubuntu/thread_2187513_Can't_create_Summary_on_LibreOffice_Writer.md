### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-11-12
Welcome. If you haven't used paragraph styles ...

Mark out the text you want to include in the Table of Contents (or Index);
Insert>Indexes and Tables>Entry;
Set it up how you want it;
Hit OK.

Then:

Insert>Indexes and Tables>Indexes and Tables

How you like, hit okay and there it is.

From here:

[http://blog.worldlabel.com/2012/creating-tables-of-contents-in-libreoffice.html](http://blog.worldlabel.com/2012/creating-tables-of-contents-in-libreoffice.html)

### Post by hugodaniel2 on 2013-11-12
Thanks Bucky Ball, it worked... the other way that I've learned to create a Summary simply doesn't work, but this one works fine, and yes, you can change and update the Summary, or TOC, any time you need.

Answering your question, you can change line spacing, etc., for the TOC, just pressing F11, and on the tab for the "Paragraph style", or just clicking on the title inside the summary, you'll see the options Paragraph 1, 2, 3, etc. (levels), just click with the right mouse button and choose "Modify", to get there.

You can do a lot of changes just by clicking F11 and changing these options for the "Titles" (1,2,3, etc.) inside the text, or inside the Summary.

After you made the changes on the Titles, just click with the right mouse button in the Summary/Index and choose "Update", the changes you've made in the Titles will automatically be shown on the TOC.
