### Post by stalkingwolf on 2008-12-19
There are several good books, depending on what you are looking for.

The Official Ubuntu book is good, the first third of it is history,
HBD an so on. interesting stuff.

The Linux Bible is also a very good book.  If you are interested in getting into the DNA of Linux.  Not necessarily for a beginner.

Ubuntu for non-geeks is also a good book.  Very basic and a lot of fun.
nice intro to CLI.

In short there are many Good Books, It just depends on what YOU want and need
at the time.  I have a shelf full. I use different ones at different times.

As for the ones you were given they may not be the best at this time, but they are far from useless.  Sometimes good is better than great.  You can at times find pearls in older publications not found in newer ones simply because newer editions assume previous knowledge.
