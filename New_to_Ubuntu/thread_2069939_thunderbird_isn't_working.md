### Post by DuckHook on 2012-10-11
> **Dungeon_Beast said:**
> I am using thunderbird on linux 9

You must mean something else as there is no such thing as Linux 9. Do you mean Ubuntu 9.x? If so, then you are using an awfully old distribution and should upgrade to one that is still maintained. If you mean Thunderbird version 9, again, this is generations old and obsolete. Please provide specific version of Ubuntu and T-Bird.

> it is not downloading my messages.

This is almost always caused by not setting up T-bird with the proper settings to connect to your service provider. Please post the settings you are using for POP and SMTP (but *not* your username or password--*never* post that information publicly, no matter who asks). If your service provider is using IMAP, please post those settings.

> Is there a more dependable email reader that I should be using?

T-bird is very dependable. But it must be set up properly with the information that your service provider gives you. If you don't use the right settings, then it is not possible for any e-mail reader in any operating system to work properly.
