### Post by echo314 on 2009-03-04
How acceptable is using keyboard patterns for creating strong passwords? My thinking is patterns are much easier to remember than long random strings. This would be for the person who wants good password security, has a modest amount of login accounts but not enough to warrant a separate password manager program. 

For example, if I wanted to make a password for my U.S. Bank online account, i could start at b for bank, make a triangle pattern starting diag up-left, and SHIFT every 2nd and 3rd key. This would get me bGT5^&8UH. Thats just a few elements to remember, and a long password I get out.

Assuming I use a different pattern for each password, so you can't derive one from another, does this provide me a reasonable level of security? Does knowing that I use a pattern, for example if someone knew I used that triangle, really hurt my other passwords NOT related to a triangle at all, even if I included stuff like skipping keys and symmetry?

I know there is no perfect solution to this whole security thing, I would just like some input on this method of password generation in general, the security you get out of it for the effort you put in. Thanks for any tips or suggestions! I think its really important we try and educate others about proper security, and by making the process easier, all the better.

### Post by DBrocks on 2009-03-04
Yeah, for my important stuff like encrypted volume, i choose 2 random numbers:
 
19
 
then I choose a random key on the keyboard, and press every key around that key, alternating shift. so, say the key was "g", it would look like gFtHbV
I would also include spaces if i chose a key on the bottom row. Then I would pick another random key, and do the same thing. For this, i will use the key "o" so: oI9)pLk
Then i would press another 2 random letters: 94.
 
So, my final password would look like: 19gfThBvoI9)pLk95 Thats about 15 characters of total garbage, and its so easy to remember, bacause you just have to remember 6 things: 4 numbers, and the 2 keys. Also, if you wanted to add more randomness, you could use 3+ keys, achieving passwords of 20+ characters. You could change how many times you go around the key, and make super strong passwords. This is how I make passwords for my server. Tried and true method. Feel free to take it and change it however you like.
