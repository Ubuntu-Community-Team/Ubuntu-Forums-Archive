### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-21
x86 = a family of CPU architecture/instruction sets, starting with 8068 in 1978. 

i386 = the 386 version of x86. 

AMD64 = 64bit version of x86 (sometimes called x86-64). 

PPC= power PC, a totally different architecture to x86. 

In general, i386 is for 32bit x86 systems (from intel 386 CPUs up to current AMD and Intel CPUs). AMD64 is for 64bit version of x86 CPUs (all th current desktop Intel and AMD CPUs will run on this). PowerPC is for older, non intel apples and macs.
