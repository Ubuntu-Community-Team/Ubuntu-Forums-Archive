### Post by OzzmanNT on 2009-07-11
I believe it is possible. Pop in the extra HD into a computer already running WinXP or Linux and run unetbootin, uncheck the option at the very bottom of the dialog that says "Show all drives" (doing this from memory but I know it has a warning beside the checkbox).

You should now see that HD as well as everything else attached to your system. Select the HD letter that is associated with the newly installed drive. Tell your BIOS to boot from that drive and boom should be good.

The only concern I have is the fact that the boot manager may freak out and write something to the wrong drive, so be sure you make sure it writes to the correct drive.

The other thing is, why not use a flash drive, they are cheaper and a bit easier to carry around than a full HD.... I assume the machine you are doing this to is either very old and doesn't support booting from USB or you just want to have something new to do :P

The good news is that if it works correctly (boot managers cooperating and all) you will have a Live HD that you can pop into any machine.
