### Post by earthpigg on 2010-08-21
hi,

Google is telling me that the Dell wireless card you mention is a rebranded Broadcom card.

Post 52 in [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1390979") has a list of possible solutions. Once you try one solution, you may need to undo it prior to trying other solutions - if you don't, then things wont work even though no error message will tell you that. I discovered this the hard way: check out post 72.

If you've been trying different things, i would suggest taking a look at post 72, and then moving to post 21. I'm biased, of course, as those are both my posts documenting what has worked for me. :D

### Post by babo1453 on 2011-02-13
Late to the party, but if anyone is still having problems:
 
Dell 1545 - Ubuntu 10.10 install with Broadcom 4312.  After hours of issues and reinstalls, I've found that the Broadcom STA driver works perfectly.  Somehow, however, in install my BIOS reverted my Fn/multimedia keys, so everytime I hit the Wifi button it was just hitting F2 and vice versa.
 
Properly connected now.
