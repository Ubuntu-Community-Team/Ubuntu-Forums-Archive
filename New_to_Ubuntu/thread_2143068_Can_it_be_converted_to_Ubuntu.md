### Post by DuckHook on 2013-05-07
You should not need any of your old software to "convert" because installing Ubuntu is not a conversion process. Unlike Windows, you won't need to download drivers because Ubuntu comes with all of the drivers, either already built in to the kernel, or downloadable through its repositories. So, if they exist for your HW, they will likely be installed automatically, or manually but readily. If they do not exist, then you are plain out of luck. Because yours is a relatively decent box and quite mainstream, you should not have any problems with drivers.

Whichever flavour of Ubuntu you are thinking about, I encourage you to try running it from a LiveDVD/USB first to see if you like it and if it works. You can pick and choose your favourite flavour after test driving them all.
