### Post by billgoldberg on 2009-04-01
Don't install Ubuntu, install Eeebuntu instead.

Everything will work OOTB with Eeebuntu, not with Ubuntu.

I presume you know it's best to format your usd stick as fat32 and use unetbootin to "write" the iso to your usb stick?

Simply press "esc" at the bios screen and select your usb stick from the list.

The left usb port in my Asus Eeepc will not boot from usb, only the two right ones.

edit: it must be noted that not all usb stick are bootable. 
edit2: you can use an external HDD if your usb stick won't boot. Just make sure the partition you will boot from is the first partition.
