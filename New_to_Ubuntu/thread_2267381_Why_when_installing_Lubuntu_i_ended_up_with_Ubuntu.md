### Post by ajgreeny on 2015-03-01
The Linux Format DVD versions of Ubuntu are often respins by the magazine itself and usually have four or even more DEs included; as far as I'm aware there is not any way to choose which version of DE to install from that DVD and you have to take all or nothing, though you can, of course choose which DE to use at login, just as if you added other DEs after installation.

If your version came from LXF 192 it had 5 DEs included, unity, xfce, LXDE, KDE, and Mate and I personally think that makes for a very confusing system and is one reason I have never used their remixes for my installations, though I have used their "pure" versions before now. I do add a few applications from KDE to my Xubuntu system, but not all of them by any means as prefer to keep an almost vanilla version on my machine; it makes a much neater system overall
