### Post by Mariane on 2011-02-22
Many support services will tell you Ubuntu is not supported by them, it just means the person answering your question has no clue about it. I once had the dialog: 

Support person: "Which version of windows are you using?"
Me: "None, I'm using Ubuntu"
Support person: "No. Which version of windows are you using? Windows XP, windows 2000 or windows vista?"
Me: "None of these."
Support person: "Please reboot your computer"

I had strong doubts but I did it just in case. 

Support person: "Now you should see something written on your screen saying Windows. Can you read all of this aloud to me?"
Me: "You are not qualified to work for computer-related support!"

Finally I found the driver I was looking for, and my device worked perfectly fine with Ubuntu. Keep on looking, associating the complete device name with "Ubuntu" in your search. Chances are it's out there somewhere because you are not the first person trying to do this. 

Mariane
