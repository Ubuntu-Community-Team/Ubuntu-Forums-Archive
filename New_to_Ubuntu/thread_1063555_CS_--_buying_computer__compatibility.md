### Post by Kellemora on 2009-02-08
Hi Sanubie

Check around your area for REAL Computer stores and shops!

I just bought a brand new dual core AMD64 5200 assembled in a nice 688 watt power supply cabinet on an Asus M2N68-AM MOBO with 4 gigs of memory and a Western Digital 500 gig Sata 3g HD for 400 bucks.
He charged me 25 bucks labor to add a multi-card reader, DVD burner and extra case fan from a computer I was retiring.
I could have went with a quad core for only like 100 bucks more.

Barebones from Tiger Direct usually comes completely unassembled, not even the CPU is plugged in and the last time I got one from them, not a single fan was included with the MOBO or CPU kits.  Price was the same.
In fact, our local dude is cheaper than Tiger Direct on most things as it is, the MOBO is 100 bucks even, 129 at TD, the 688 watt power supply 40 bucks, 55 at TD, etc.

So check around your area, I think you might be quite surprised.

Beware of off the shelf computers like Sam's, or Electronic stores.  They usually have ZERO expansion room.  EG:  Only 2 Sata ports instead of 4 or 6 and BOTH are USED for the HD and DVD.  Many TD barebones do NOT have video on board either.  That can be good or bad, depending upon how you look at it!  For the normal computer user, having everything on board is usually better, less set-up and fiddling to get things to work together.

TTUL
