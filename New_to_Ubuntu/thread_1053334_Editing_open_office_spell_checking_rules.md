### Post by Mariane on 2009-01-28
Hi everyone, 

I sometimes have to type in French, and I hate 
it because I'm usually using a keyboard which 
does not have an easy option for all the 
accented characters. 

So under Open Office I have to do "Insert / 
Special character", then find one of them 
in the list, then change the font because 
it usually appears in a font different from 
the one I'm typing in. Or then copy-paste 
it into a text editor and then copy it back 
into Open Office, in which case it will 
automatically adopt the right font. 

I would like to edit Open Office spell checking rules, 
so that for example it automatically replaces "tres" 
by "très". There is no such word as "tres" in French. 
As it stands, "tres" is recognised as a mistake but 
"très" is not event part of the list of proposed 
replacements. 

I would like to change the rules so that replacing 
unaccented characters by accented ones becomes the 
first thing Open Office offers when it encounters a 
mistake. (When such replacement(s) can solve the 
problem, of course). For example, if I type "ete" 
in French the top of the replacement list would be 
"été", even though it makes 2 modifications. 

Then I would still have to copy/paste accented 
characters, because sometimes both with and without 
words are possible ("couche" and "couché" are two 
valid French words, the first means diaper while 
the second means lying down). But at least most of 
the work will have been done by the dictionary. 

All suggestions welcome. 

Mariane

### Post by HotShotDJ on 2009-01-28
> **Mariane said:**
> I sometimes have to type in French, and I hate 
it because I'm usually using a keyboard with does not have an easy option for all the accented characters. This part is easy.  First, add the keyboard indicator to one of your panels (top or bottom, whichever pleases you) by right-click on an empty space in the panel and selecting "Add to panel."  Scroll to "Keyboard Indicator," select it, then click "Add."

Now, right-click on your new Indicator and choose "Keyboard Preferences" then click on the "Layouts" tab.  Just under the "Selected layouts" section, click on the "+" button.  Assuming your default layout is "USA" select "United States" in the Country drop-down (or choose the proper layout for your location.  In any case, it should match your actual keyboard, NOT the language you want to type).  Next, select the proper variant... for a United States keyboard, you'll want "USA Alternative international (former us_intl).  Now, click "Add."  You can now switch layouts "on-the-fly" by clicking the Keyboard Indicator.

You might want to print out the new keyboard map to help you out until you are used to where everything is.  Left-click on your Keyboard Indicator to select the new layout.  Right-Click on the Keyboard Indicator and then choose "Show Current Layout"  -- you'll have the option to print it out.  Remember, in this mode, to actually type certain characters (such as 'or ", you'll have to type the space bar after pressing the character).

I hope this helps a bit.
