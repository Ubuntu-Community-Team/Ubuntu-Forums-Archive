### Post by resander on 2012-01-22
Speed was not a problem for me, but the wireless sometimes dropped on the second floor and streaming video stopped.

Try experimenting changing the distance between the hub and the pc/laptop.
I had four 'bars' signal for wireless on ground floor and only two bars for a netbook on the second floor. When I moved the hub to a position more directly under the room on the second floor I received three bars strength and that was sufficient for undisturbed wireless on 2nd floor.

Does wired (using ethernet cable) connection work?

If you have another router you can run the hub in modem mode. Then plug in the router into the first ethernet port of the hub. Your router then takes over and you can observe any differences in performance.

Wrong hub power-in/out levels and electrical interference can also affect the wireless. Read posts in the cable forum and post. The veterans there are very knowledgeable.
