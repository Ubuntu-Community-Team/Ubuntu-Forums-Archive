### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-07-27
Pentium 4s are very old CPUs now. You'd be much better served by a more modern CPU such as a Core i5, or even a Core 2 Duo.

Also, someone else suggested buying Kingston RAM. Don't. Avoid Kingston; every single stick of Kingston RAM I've ever bought has failed within a year.

The Core i5 CPUs require a motherboard with an LGA1156 socket, and they use DDR3 memory. Dual-channel, so you need 2 sticks, or 4 sticks.

You're pretty safe with Nvidia graphics cards. I have a GTX260, but the newer GTX3xx cards work too. No point going with anything older than the GTX2xx series, and I don't think there's any point going to the newer GTX4xx series as they are expensive and terribly inefficient.
