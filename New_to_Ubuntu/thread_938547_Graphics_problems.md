Well, that is most likely because you installed it using Wubi.  That is a virtualization of Ubuntu, running on your Windows partition.  If you installed it on it's own partition you'd see a huge graphical and performance jump.  That's true of all virtualized OSs.

Wubi is really meant more as a stepping stone.  That way, when you're comfortable and sure of yourself, you can graduate up to a full install.  You can keep Windows, just dual-boot it.
