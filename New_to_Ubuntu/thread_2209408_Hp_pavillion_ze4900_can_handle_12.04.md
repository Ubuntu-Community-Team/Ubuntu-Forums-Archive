### Post by Jordan_Cameron on 2014-03-05
Light use can be considered internet/email and some document editing, but probably no heavy audio/visual work.

With those specs, to get the most performance, try xubuntu first.  It uses XFCE, which is a lightweight GTK 2 (?) desktop.  If that still feels a bit sluggish, then Lubuntu would be your best shot.  Lubuntu is LXDE, which is built around Openbox, one of the BEST lightweight Window Managers IMHO (Ex-arch user here, so I may be biased).  Try Kubuntu if you want, but you'd have to configure KDE to be more lightweight probably, and I'd start doing that by disabling akonadi and trying to stay away from nepomuk too.  That will bring the RAM requirements of KDE down by a tonne.

Just give it a try and see what you like.  That's the beauty of Linux; it's usable on some of the most ancient hardware around :3
