### Post by Paqman on 2012-08-21
Amahi does look nice, I've not used it.

Ubuntu with something like Webmin would be a bit more bare-bones, but most of the stuff you're trying to do is pretty well-documented and a more generic system might give you more flexibility. The purists would probably even turn up their noses at Webmin, but IMO being at the bottom of a learning curve is suckful and anything that can help is a Good Thing&#8482;.

You can get pre-configured "appliances" from [TurnKey Linux]("http://www.turnkeylinux.org/") that do one thing really well. Nothing to stop you installing one of those (file server?) to get you started then simply adding the extra features, just like you would with a regular install of Ubuntu. The ISO images from TurnKey can be installed on "bare metal", not just virtual machines. They're essentially just stripped down Ubuntu images.

It really depends what you want. Do you want to have a "one click = bam!" solution that you don't have to ever touch, or are you up for doing a bit of tinkering and learning?
