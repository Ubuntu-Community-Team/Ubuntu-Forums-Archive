### Post by TBABill on 2010-03-23
All comments above are applicable and accurate. ISP's provide a connection dependent upon the hardware at the end of that connection, which is an ADSL modem, cable modem or variant. However, their outputs are the same and will work with any router or direct connection via Ethernet. Don't fear any incompatibility with your ISP as a reason to avoid an alternative operating system, regardless if it's Linux, Unix, BSD or other. 
 
On the hardware side you very well may need to get access to your router or cable/DSL modem directly (via Ethernet cord, not wireless) in order for your system to go online and pull in the appropriate driver(s) and ask if you'd like to install/activate them.
 
Like those above have stated, download the .iso, verify your download is error free (md5sum), burn to CD or DVD at the SLOWEST setting possible to eliminate potential for write errors, and boot without installing so you can test all features like sound, wireless, video, etc. before committing. Worst case is you decide not to install so you shut down, remove the disk and return to Windows. Best case you install side by side, use Windows for whatever you want to use it for and enjoy learning Ubuntu with the help of so many great people here in the forums that can guide you through your questions or problems.
 
Good luck!
