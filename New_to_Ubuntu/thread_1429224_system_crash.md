### Post by ipatfrmww on 2010-05-10
I take it that with 1,444 posts you already know about Ctrl Alt F1 (if you don't it halts GDM and gives you a terminal which you then log in and can do stuff), but then again I didn't know about it for like 2 months, and only cause somebody told me who I talk to about these sorts of things. I probably wouldn't have found out about it for ages otherwise.

It isn't one of those things that you sort of find out about when you move to Ubuntu.
 
Something useful you may want to know is how to get back to GDM. This is very easy once you know how, its just Ctrl Alt F7 (the first time I tried this I didn't know how to get back to GDM and had to do a sudo shutdown -h now. I'm still a noob and just try things without thinking them through)

Ctrl Alt Del does something completely different in Ubuntu to Windows. Ctrl Alt Del is the panic sequence in Windows, but it just brings up a shutdown menu in Ubuntu(completely usless if GDM isn't working).
