### Post by anhhung on 2008-07-13
Dear friends,

My name is Hung and I'm an undergrad pursuing my degree in International Business at the Foreign Trade University, HCMC, Vietnam.

I have been working at the 15 May School (htpp://www.15mayschool.org), a school for disadvantaged children in District 1, the central of the city. After the last two weeks of vigorous work with a brilliant team of 20 members from around the world to build a sustainable Computing program for the school, I find it crucial to bring technology education to the children in need to help them escape their hard life. You can read more about this 2-week project from here:

[http://sealnetpv08.wordpress.com](http://sealnetpv08.wordpress.com)
[http://www.sealnetonline.org/projects/project_vietnam_2008](http://www.sealnetonline.org/projects/project_vietnam_2008)

As the project neared its end, we found out about the Information Society Innovation Fund ([http://www.isif.asia](http://www.isif.asia)), which basically provides a maximum US$30,000 grant to help organizations bring innovative technology solutions to make life in the region better. The deadline for the project proposal is on September 1st.

We are all so excited about it and for the past days I have been brainstorming ideas for the proposal as follows:

A community hub which provides Computing skill education and Internet access to the children. There will be about 20 computers running Edubuntu:

     + As you may know, most Vietnamese use pirated software and I wonder if teaching the kids how to use Ubuntu/ Edubuntu is good for their future.
     + Both OpenOffice and Google Apps for Schools will be taught with the emphasis on the latter since the world is increasing getting online. I wonder if I can get any help from Google people in terms of technical and financial support.
     + I've just learnt about thin client and wonder if it is possible to use this kind of networking technology because it's very hard to find good technicians who can use Linux and speak English.
     + Training for teaching and technical staff will be a must. I certainly need someone from Ubuntu to train the teachers how to use Edubuntu, set up the thin client network (if it is chosen), the technician how to set up and maintain the network. And other issues which I haven't been able to foreseen.
     + Vietnamese online educational sites will be used since most of the software CDs are Windows-based.
     + Are there any other ideas that you may wish to recommend and some directions as to who I should contact? Are there any other areas besides education that you think may be more in need of help?

All suggestions are highly appricated! Thanks and regards.

### Post by kahlil88 on 2008-07-13
> **anhhung said:**
> As you may know, most Vietnamese use pirated software and I wonder if teaching the kids how to use Ubuntu/ Edubuntu is good for their future.

Absolutely! [Free software]("http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html") (free as in "speech") is the way of the future. The [GNU/Linux]("http://www.gnu.org/gnu/linux-and-gnu.html") operating system was created out of respect for the users' freedom, by a bunch of hackers who didn't like putting their faith in the hands of a software company when a program didn't work or lacked certain features. Non-free operating systems, such as Microsoft Windows, often contain software that spies on the user or restricts the user from certain functions that people are supposed to believe must be paid for. With free software, there are no secrets about how a program is supposed to work or what it does, and with the proper knowledge, the users are free to make any modifications they wish, as long as they keep the software free.
