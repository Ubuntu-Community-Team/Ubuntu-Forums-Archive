Is this a correct statment?Many people have had no trouble upgrading in situ. A reformat is not typically necessary.

Having said that, I strongly recommend that you set up your Ubuntu system with a minimum of 3 partitions:  System (/), Swap, and Home (/home).  On my laptop and home desktop systems, 15 Gig of / has always been more than enough (and really, 10 gig would probably be fine).  Swap = RAM X 2, and the rest for /home.

This way, if you DO have a problem and need to do a reinstall, you can do so without loosing your documents, pictures, music, whatever you keep in your user's folder(s).
