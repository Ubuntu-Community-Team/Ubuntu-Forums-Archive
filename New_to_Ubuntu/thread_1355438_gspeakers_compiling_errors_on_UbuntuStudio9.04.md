### Post by lloyd_b on 2009-12-15
Go into Synaptic, and look at those packages you installed.  Now, check and see if there's a package with an identical name, but with "-dev" on the end of it.

The "-dev" packages need to be installed for each of those libraries.  These contain the "development headers" for the library.

This is most likely what's causing those errors - you should never have to mess with PKG_CONFIG_PATH unless you've manually compiled and installed the packages to a non-standard place.

Lloyd B.

### Post by bakechad on 2009-12-29
BudBear,

After quite a bit of pain, I got gspeakers to compile and run successfully. :)

A few things I ran into that may be causing your problem.

- Do you have automake-1.7 installed?  It is a dependency, but due to symbolic links, it is unclear until you start digging around

- Did you run make as sudo?  It complied, but it did not write the binary due to permissions.  I re-ran make as sudo and everything went okay.

- Then I did 'sudo make install'

I now have gspeakers in /usr/local/bin and it runs without a problem.
