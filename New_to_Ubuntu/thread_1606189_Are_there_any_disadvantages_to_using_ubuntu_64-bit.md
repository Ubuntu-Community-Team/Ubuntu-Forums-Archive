1. You can't use 32-bit debs without some messing around, but the majority of things are 64-bit compiled now. Linux itself works just as well in 64-bit.

2. Some extra performance in your processor doesn't get used, and you can't access more than 4 GiB in total RAM without installing a different kernel.
