### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-04-01
> **Panarchy said:**
> Speed.
Security.
Accessbility.

As a long-time user of LOTS of different OS's I can tell you from experience that you're NOT going to get these to the degree you expect if you try to run Windows apps through Wine.

I have a machine that runs both XP and Ubuntu 8.10 (among other OS's) and neither is appreciably faster than the other.  Most of the "speed" claims compare Ubuntu to Vista and gee, even XP is faster than Vista!

As to security, I've been running XP behind a router ever since it came out, and have NEVER had a virus or infection of any kind. Of course, I don't go to suspicous websites or click suspicious emails.  If they do that, then running Linux would help prevent infections, but a decent AV product (Kaspersky, ESET, Norton AV 2009) will do the same.

Accessibility -- you mean handicap stuff, such as louder volume, on-screen keyboards, and on-screen magnifiers? XP has had these for years, as has Linux.  I'm not aware of any superiority of one over the other.

Basically, as others have said ( and I know because I've tried), you're NOT going to be able to make Ubuntu behave, look, and act exactly like XP -- so don't try. And if you think that WINE is a miracle cure that will allow you to run MS office 2k3 and other windows apps "just like windows", you're sadly mistaken. 

If they are comfortable using XP, then why make their lives miserable forcing something different on them? Let them use what they know and like.  Get them a halfway decent AV product and turn on the accessibility features (they're in Control Panel, you know).
