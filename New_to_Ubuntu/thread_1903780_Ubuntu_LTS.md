### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-01-03
All Ubuntu releases are stable! The main difference is the length of support for security fixes and other stuff. The purpose of defining a release as Long Term Service is to reassure business managers that installing Ubuntu is a good business decision. Knowing that support is long term helps with planning migration to updated operating systems.

You will notice that other releases are not unsupported. Each release gets a minimum of 18 months support. And as Ubuntu release every six months then there is an overlap of support so that we have plenty of time to upgrade.

I shall be using 12.04 when it comes out. In fact I am testing it now. And although it is a development branch I am finding it very stable. Then I shall go to 12.10 and 13.04 and onwards (unless I die). During that period I will not be using an unsupported Ubuntu. So, the fact that 12.04 is LTS and the desktop is now supported for five years is immaterial to me.
