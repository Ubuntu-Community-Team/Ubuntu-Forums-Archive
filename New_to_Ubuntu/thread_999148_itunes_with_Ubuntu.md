### Post by DarKnyht on 2008-12-01
The closest that I have come to iTunes in Linux is Banshee and using the Amazon Store for DRM-free mp3 purchases.  However, I made the switch before they axed the software (at least that I know of) that allowed you to strip out the DRM from iTunes purchases.  This was important so that I could play my iTunes purchased music in Linux without the hassle of burning and re-ripping.

The only hassle I have had with the amazon store is when I get the download .amz file.  I have to save it before opening it so that I don't get a permission error.

One of these days I will get around to digging out my old ipod and test it with Songbird.  I prefer the look to it, although they really need to add CD playback/ripping to it's features.
