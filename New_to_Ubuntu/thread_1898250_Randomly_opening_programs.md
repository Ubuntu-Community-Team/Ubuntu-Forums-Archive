### Post by Fernhill Linux Project on 2012-01-19
Firstly go to the launcher bar on the left, click 'system settings' (the cog with a spanner), then click 'screen' and you will be able to adjust the screen lock settings.

---

### Post by Fernhill Linux Project on 2012-01-19
Then go to 'system settings' again and click 'power' (the battery icon) and you will be able to choose the behaviour of closing the lid to 'nothing' or 'hibernate'.

You can use the keyboard shortcut 'Ctrl-Alt-L' to lock the screen.
You could suspend the computer while you are away using the 'suspend' option on the cog at the top right of the screen and see if the problem still happens.

I have not encountered random programs starting on their own.
