absolutely.  I'd even say 512 isn't enough if you're going to be doing anything heavy.

The other reason why FF is so much slower is because the linux verson of it uses gtk+, which are the graphical libraries gnome and xfce are built on.  kde uses Qt.  Currently there is no official port of FF to Qt, and I don't really expect one any time soon (bummer, i know). I just remember what a hassle it was to get compiz/beryl (at the time) running on Kubuntu Edgy -- needless to say it was pretty unstable, too.
