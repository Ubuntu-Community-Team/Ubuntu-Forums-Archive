Since some time I'm following a bit of a different approach.

Years ago I tried to convince people to use Linux, but now I first ask them why they're interested in Linux, and what they want or expect from it, and whether they have friends or relatives who can support them when they run into trouble with it.

Do you make regular backups ?
If you do, why worry that things can go wrong ?

And I agree that it's good to ask your friend why he thinks it can cause problems. Where did he get that information from ? Is it a rumor, or is it based on one mistake from one person he knows ?

I've never tried Wubi, but that sounds like a nice and safe compromise for your friend to try.
But again, you should try to find out whether he is *really* in Linux or not really or not enough.
