### Post by ezsit on 2010-10-25
If you are running a regular desktop system, sticking with 9.10 for as long as you want is fine. The security updates that trickle in after 12 months are pretty few and far between. I would update your 9.10 installation just before support ends and grab whatever software packages you need prior to EOL - end of life. Try new releases as they appear and switch when you find one that works for you. No need to sweat a few months or even a year of an unsupported distro. I know many people successfully using old software with ZERO detriment. Sure, a few people will complain that my advice is not safe, but unless you run a server, desktop Linux is far safer than going back to Windows, even an EOL'ed ubuntu release is safer than Windows.
