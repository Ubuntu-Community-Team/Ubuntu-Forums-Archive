### Post by efflandt on 2010-05-22
Apparently there are different versions of the Presario V2000.  I inherited an older one when I got someone a new laptop (probably about 5 years old with AMD Sempron 3300+ which is an Athlon crippled to 32 bit with smaller cache).  The same model with different hardware could make troubleshooting help more difficult.

The original hard drive in the one I have died a year or two ago and has been replaced with a 160 GB WD Scorpio dual booting XP home and Ubuntu.  RAM has been upgraded to 1280 MB.   Video is ATI 200M.  I used the **nomodeset** kernel boot parameter for 10.04 LTS because I have had trouble with the new kernel mode setting (KMS) video driver in other computers with legacy ATI video (using default video drivers). There seems to be little difference with this laptop either way, glxgears is quicker with nomodeset, but that is not really a good benchmark.

In 9.10 its primary problem was keyboard lag and mousepad lag/jumpy, and broken suspend and hibernate.  The keyboard dropping characters was especially annoying when entering a password with no feedback (like sudo).  And the laggy jumpy mousepad made it easy to click on the wrong thing. One thing I have not figured out is that if shut down for a short period of time, it boots right up.  But if shut down for awhile, I have to temporarily disconnect all power and remove the battery before I can get it to boot (maybe failed CMOS battery?).

In 10.04 keyboard lag is almost gone and mousepad lag is less noticeable (rarely, typically immediately after booting).  So far everything else in 10.04 LTS seems to work fine, including Broadcom B43 wireless.  It is even able to suspend now.

Since my mousepad sometimes seems to lag during disk access on that computer, I could see where a failing hard drive having write issues could result in application lag (browser cache).

PS: My V2000 BIOS seems incapable of booting from USB, flash or hard drive.  So I had to use an Ubuntu CD to salvage data from the failing hard drive to a USB drive, and later when I got the laptop with XP hopelessly infected by several viruses.
