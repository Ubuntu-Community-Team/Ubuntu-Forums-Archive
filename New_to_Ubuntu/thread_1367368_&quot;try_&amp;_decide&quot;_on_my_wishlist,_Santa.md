Hello.

I am interested in the functionality offered by Acronis' "Try & Decide", see for instance:

[http://www.acronis.com/company/inpress/2007/12-pc-pro-recommended.html](http://www.acronis.com/company/inpress/2007/12-pc-pro-recommended.html)

I quote, not to plug, but for lack of a better way of putting it myself:

"It uses [Acronis'] True Image's familiar Secure Zone® — the partition you set aside to house a recovery image of your system in case of total failure — but operates differently. When Try & Decide mode is enabled, any changes you make to your system — potentially damaging actions such as software installations and opening attachments — will only be performed on a virtual replica of your system drive within the Secure Zone. Depending on the size of your Secure Zone, it can keep tracking your activities for considerable time, although it's best used in small doses when you know there's a risk involved. Once you're satisfied your PC won't keel over, simply choose to either apply or discard the changes. It's an interesting addition, and reduces the likelihood of needing the whole backup-restore routine."


Now that would be really cool in and for Linux / Ubuntu! - Especially for the aspiring noob tinkering about...


If a similar feature or program already exists (or you are keen to develop it), please be in touch!
