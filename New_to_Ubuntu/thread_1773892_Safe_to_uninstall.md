### Post by soylentcola on 2011-06-02
So after running into an error, I had to reinstall 11.04 and just recently installed a bunch of applications, some of which I think are better than those bundled with my install...

I have the "Image Viewer" installed in Ubuntu, but since I downloaded another image viewer, is it safe to uninstall? I got a warning that said: "If you uninstall Image Viewer, future updates will not include new items in the Ubuntu desktop set. Are you sure you want to continue?"

I'm just confused as to what exactly this warning is telling me...

---

### Post by ubudog on 2011-06-02
I believe the warning is saying that new updates for the apps that come with Ubuntu will not be installed.  So I don't recommend uninstalling it, as you may need those new updates for the other apps.  If you want, you can hide it from the menu.  System>Preferences>Main Menu.  And also change the default image viewer to the new one.  When opening a picture, right click on it, and select Open With.  Click on Other Application, then check "Remember this application", select your new app, and then it will always open it with that new app.  :-)
