### Post by howefield on 2009-02-08
If things are that bad, I think I'd simply reinstall.

Then once you are backup and running, look at a back up solution to make it easier when you mess up again.

Clonezilla can clone your disk or you can look at sbackup. I prefer Acronis True Image, however that is a commercial product but it does  what I want.

The point is once you have a way of restoring your machine, you can experiment and mess things up with no worry about ruining your install.

### Post by utnubuuser on 2009-02-08
Hi -- before you re-install, have a look at this thread:> [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1060406](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1060406)

One of the Forum staff-members posted a thread a couple days ago about to many people giving the advice to "just re-install", and (s)he was perfectly right.  One of the great things about this operating system is that you **you can almost always fix it**.
A messed up desktop might be a blessing in disguise - you could come out the other side of it knowing a whole lot more than you did before.
Most likely only one set of "user" files/configurations are messed and you can simply delete the user and create a new one.
Or keep the old one, work on it, and set up a new user account for any pressing concerns.
