### Post by james_xxx on 2008-11-01
An easy way to go about setting up printing to a network CUPS printer, is to simply open up a browser, both on the PC the printer is connected to, as well as the remote PC, and enter:

> [http://127.0.0.1:631](http://127.0.0.1:631)


That should bring up the CUPS configuration options. The PC being used as the print server needs to be set to publish/share the printer with other PCs on the network, the remote PCs need to be set to use shared printers.


Hope this helps!

### Post by aggiemarine07 on 2008-11-21
I figured it out (after a lucky guess) but here is what I did to successfully connect to our CUPS server here at Texas A&M.

Since I was going no where with the gnome-print-manager. I decided to see how I could connect to it through the web interface ([http://localhost:631](http://localhost:631)). 

I then googled "add printer to cups" and came across this article ([here]("http://smeserver.norhex.com/contribs/rvandenaker/beta/e-smith-cups/documentation/howtos/cups-add-printer.html")) and scrolled down to "adding to a RAW print queue" because here at A&M since we have so many printers in the labs everything gets thrown into a huge queue rather than an individual printer.



Basically in the article he says to follow the following steps:

open up [http://localhost:631](http://localhost:631)


sign into CUPS and go to Administration (at the top)


then enter in whatever you want to call this printer (or print queue in my case)


then its asks for you how to want to connect to the device (we use IPP over here but our print queue is accessed via http)


then I enter in my device URI for the print queue (we have to enter in a username and password 
here so mine was similar to this [http://username:password@webaddress/computerlab-IPP/.printer](http://username:password@webaddress/computerlab-IPP/.printer)


then it asks me for a driver or PPD, and since xerox does not have a driver to linux I downloaded the WinXP PPD for the Xerox DocuPrint/DocuTech 65/70/80/90 and used that PPD.


Viola! I printed a test page and it worked!
