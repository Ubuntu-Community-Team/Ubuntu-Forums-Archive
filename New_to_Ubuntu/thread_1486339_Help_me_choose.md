### Post by sidzen on 2010-05-17
Since it is a netbook, I would recommend:  First, cloning Win7 to an external hard drive to have backup.  Second, wiping your internal hard drive with zeros and partitioning (/, /home, swap), marking the root (/) with the boot flag.  Then, going with lubuntu *in lieu* of the more resource-hungry ubuntu.  

Go to the great help pages at [https://help.ubuntu.com](https://help.ubuntu.com) and look up and learn how to install only those packages specific to your wants and needs.  It will be time well spent.

Welcome to Linux in general, Debian less generally, and Lucid, specifically!  It's good to have you!
