### Post by perce on 2010-09-02
A proprietary driver is a driver which is distributed under a non free licence. Some cards (i.e. nvidia graphic cards) need them to use their advanced functionalities, but most don't require them. If everything works you shouldn't worry.

Proprietary drivers have the disadvantage of being a "black box" for the Ubuntu developers: they can neither study nor modify them, so they tend to play not so nicely with the rest of the system. You should be happy you don't need any.
