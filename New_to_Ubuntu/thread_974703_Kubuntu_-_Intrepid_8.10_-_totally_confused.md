### Post by candtalan on 2008-11-07
I have been using ubuntu, mostly kubuntu in fact, for several years, and am one of a very few people in UK who regularly display and distribute Foss and K/Ubuntu at computer fairs. I do this monthly, for several years now, so I am in fairly close touch with a throng of windows users, many of whom are interested to find an easy free, alternative to Windows.

I often recommend Kubuntu to such people because it is a more comfortable experience, more convenience feature rich, than ubuntu, particularly for existing windows users. In this way, kubuntu is at the sharp end of my advocacy, which is effective and regular.

I have just tried Kubuntu 8.10 live CD for the first time and I am horrified. In two PCs and a laptop it has significant display issues, even with safe graphics chosen the display on one is not really usable. In the laptop it does not run even with safe graphics. In the one pc which does work with safe graphics, I now see - presumably kde4 - brilliantly clever and potentially beautiful - but totally confusing to me - an experienced user. And also very obviously slow in an ageing machine, ones just like most windows escapees use. 

I cannot continue to offer windows escapees kubuntu! I will personally continue to look at what kubuntu offers and may come to appreciate its benefits, but there is no way at all that I can continue to say to my 'customers' that kubuntu can be for them. In marketing terms, on the street that is, it is an adverse factor I am sorry to say.

### Post by phidia on 2008-11-07
Xorg in hardy and intrepid has changed considerably. When you run into problems with the video not being recognized one option is to boot in recovery mode and type > xfix.

I think there are issues to be dealt with regarding the way Ubuntu now deals with xorg.
For instance /etc/X11/xorg.conf doesn't appear to respond to editing, the dpkg-reconfigure commands are all automatic now-meaning no input from the user.

However rather than just noticing these things and being befuddled by it if enough people let Ubuntu developers know they don't want to be eliminated from the configuration process maybe they will listen?
