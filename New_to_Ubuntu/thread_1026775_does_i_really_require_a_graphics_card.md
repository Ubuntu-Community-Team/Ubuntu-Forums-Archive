### Post by donkyhotay on 2008-12-31
Well, you need *a* graphics card just to display anything on the screen but a basic built-in VGA card will do for that. If your just going to be doing some web browsing and stuff then you'll get with a VGA card but you'll be stuck with 800x600 screen resolution. Getting and configuring a better graphics card will allow you to get better screen resolutions and 3d graphics. Personally I'd recommend getting a card that will at least allow you to go at least 1024x768 as many programs won't fit within a smaller screen size.

### Post by ciborium on 2008-12-31
Then if you plug in a monitor and you get a picture, you're golden.  

BTW, if you have a recent computer, (and based on the Core 2 processor you say you have, then you do) you ought to be able to choose a 1024x768 resolution, as long as Ubuntu recognizes your card.  

Since most web pages are created for 1024 or 800 width, if you choose a higher resolution, you end up with wasted space on pages that aren't programmed to fill the space.
