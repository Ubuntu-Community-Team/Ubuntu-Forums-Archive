### Post by Lars Noodén on 2012-01-14
The variants have different default settings and default applications.  The most striking difference is probably the default desktop environments.  Ubuntu uses Unity, Kubuntu KDE, Xubuntu XFCE and Lubuntu LXDE.  You can still run KDE on Ubuntu and Unity on Kubuntu, and so on, if you want.  Again, those are only the defaults, you can configure the appearance and behavior as you want them to be and choose the applications you want your system to have.

If you are really curious, you can burn the live CDs to CD-RW and then give them a try on your machine.
