### Post by Paddy Landau on 2010-08-14
> **uonuon said:**
> The Pentium III desktop has 2 gig ram. I have external hard drives, and plan to buy a flash drive.
More than enough RAM and hard disk space!

> **uonuon said:**
>  The recording interface ... would process eight microphones better if the OS is not be connected to the internet or antivirus programs.
I think you'll be pleasantly surprised. Connection to the Internet should have no effect whatsoever on your computer's processing under Ubuntu. I can't comment on Windows.

> **uonuon said:**
>  I'm not sure if dual booting, VM or what would isolate the laptop hard drive or it's external drive from background services.
When you dual boot, each time you start (or restart) the computer, you have a choice of Windows or Ubuntu. The two are completely separate from each other. The one will have zero effect on the other. So dual-booting is absolutely fine.

Just one thing: Don't use Wubi, because it won't be suitable for your purposes.

> **uonuon said:**
> How could I get a demo of what Ubuntu would be like to use for sites like MySpace, Facebook, Youtube, and of course forums like this. I hope Ubuntu will prevent the system problems I've had with XP.
Easy.

[LIST]
[*]Download the Ubuntu Live CD ISO (64-bit if you have it, but I think Pentium III is only 32-bit).
[*]Save the ISO onto a flash drive rather than CD (follow the instructions on the download page), because the flash drive is significantly faster than CD (though still a lot slower than a proper installation).
[*]Boot off the flash drive and choose "Try Ubuntu". Nothing you do will be saved on your computer, so play around to your heart's content.
[*]You will be able to view your computer's Windows hard drive. If you do so, take care not to accidentally change anything there, because you will be able to add, modify and delete files there.
[*]Test your Internet, sound cards, video (e.g. with YouTube), and anything else that comes to mind.
[*]You may run out of RAM eventually, since the Live CD has nowhere to store anything. If that happens, just reboot and try again.
[/LIST]
One more thing. Your Pentium III is -- I believe -- quite old. If you're planning on doing CPU-intensive work with audio and video, you may find [Lubuntu]("http://lubuntu.net/") faster than Ubuntu. It's not as pretty, but it's faster on low-end machines. It's still in the beta phase, but I've had no problems whatsoever with it.

Have fun!
