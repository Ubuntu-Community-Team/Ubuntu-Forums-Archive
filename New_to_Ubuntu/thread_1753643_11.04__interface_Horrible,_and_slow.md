### Post by bennachie on 2011-05-10
For the most part, Unity actually works quite well for me, particularly in the netbook environment. When the global menu paradigm was introduced in MacOS way back in the 1980s, a 15-inch CRT screen was cutting edge stuff. I agree that global menus are literally a pain in the neck when you are using a 24-inch or 26-inch screen, either with OSX or Ubuntu NN, but they do work rather well on a netbook or a small-screen laptop.

Never mind, Kubuntu 11.04 is excellent, probably the best current KDE-based distribution, and, if the release candidate is any guide, Mint 11 should provide a very attractive Gnome option, taking full advantage of all the Ubuntu infrastructure improvements.

I suspect, given all the negative feedback, that Unity in Ubuntu 11.10 will be much improved and, in particular, will make many more configuration options available to the normal end-user.
