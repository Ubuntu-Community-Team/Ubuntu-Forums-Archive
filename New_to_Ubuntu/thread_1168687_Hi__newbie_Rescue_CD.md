### Post by The Cog on 2009-05-24
If the ISO images of the CDs you want to burn are there on your Vista C: drive, then using a live CD of Ubuntu to find them and burn them to CD is a reasonable approach. Assuming you manage to burn a bootable ubuntu CD first, of course. There may be a chicken-and-egg problem to solve.

Once you have Ubuntu booted, you should find your C: drive in the Places menu pull-down. Right-click the .ISO files and choose to burn to CD.

### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-05-24
Instructions on how to create a "LiveCD" from an Ubuntu iso are here:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/burn](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/burn)

You can then boot the "broken" Vista computer from the LiveCD (ensure that CD is selected as 1st boot device) by choosing the "Try Ubuntu without making any changes" option.

This will allow you to access (and back-up) your files on the internal HD, provided it's not toast.

Of course, reinstalling Vista will require a Vista CD.  You cannot "fix" Vista from Ubuntu.
