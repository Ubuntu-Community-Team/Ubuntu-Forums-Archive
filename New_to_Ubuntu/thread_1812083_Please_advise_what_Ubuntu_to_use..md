Yes, you can.

What I would do is Fresh New Installation but I understand that everyone has his/her own way to do things :)

Problem is your CPU as [**Celeron**]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celeron") is not much capable as [**Pentium 4**]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pentium_4") and that's why 11.04 is slow.

People think "it's all about how much [RAM]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Random-access_memory") I have", which is in my opinion not true. It's all about the [CPU]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_processing_unit") and how fast it's.

Yes, of course there are slow RAMs and High Speed RAM but I'm talking about the size of the RAM. Even if you'll upgrade your 512MB RAM, you won't get much CPU Speed, you are just increasing the SPACE (memory) that your CPU will deal with directly.

Sorry if it's a bit of hardware approach :)

### Post by I2k4 on 2011-07-26
I've been experimenting with Ubuntu for several months using Live USB with "Persistence" - the official "show me how" install steps are very good:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download)

Unfortunately, these instructions don't tell about "Persistence" which is necessary for settings to be carried over after rebooting.  The PenDrive software makes "Persistence" pretty clear during set up, and I'd suggest setting something over half your USB drive to "Persistence" to get a good tryout of the operating system.  After that, learn to uninstall what you don't want and install and update what you do.

On a weak machine, I suggest avoiding 11.04 since it seems to be following the Windows/Apple theory of offering fancy GUI for more powerful systems.  My current preference for speed and simplicity is Lubuntu 10.10 but you should certainly experiment and find your own way.
