### Post by howefield on 2011-05-22
You might want to check what is on the DVD version, from the download page...
> 
DVD downloads

Don't be confused, even though DVDs can hold far more data than the typical Ubuntu CD, the main benefit of the DVD downloads is to get access to all of the available language packs. Most people will be fine with the standard CD installer. There are fewer download locations for the DVD images and this list is updated less frequently than for the CD images.

You might end up downloading 3.9 gigabytes and then still need to download all the stuff you want.

Could be better simply to download the CD and then the packages you need, which can be saved to backup media after downloading. You will probably end up downloading less data that way.

I used to know exactly what was on the on the DVD but am probably out of date now.

To give you a comparison, I have just over 500 megs in my /var/cache/apt/archives folder, which accounts for installed packages over and above the base CD install plus updates added to the original 700 megs CD download means about 1.2 gig of data download.
