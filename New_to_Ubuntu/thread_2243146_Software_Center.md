### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-09-07
Not really... Ubuntu has a new version every 6 months and all the flavors including Lubuntu and most derivatives get those as well. Each 2 years there's a version with long time support (LTS) - 10.04, 12.04, 14.04 and the next one will be 16.04 - so the current version is a LTS and it's supported for 5 years, until April 2019. Normal non-LTS releases now have only 9 months support.
As a matter of fact only recently Lubuntu adhered to the LTS but not before.
So, in a nutshell, now and only recently, Lubuntu is as much a LTS as the standard Ubuntu. You can upgrade to newer versions as soon as they are available, _but you don't have to._
