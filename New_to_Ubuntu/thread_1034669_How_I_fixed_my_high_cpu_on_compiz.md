### Post by maphilli14 on 2009-01-08
I have a Lenovo T61 laptop with nVidia Corporation Quadro NVS 140M (rev a1) and Hardy Heron. I used these guides to update the NVidia drivers:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=5936769](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=5936769)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-940862.html](http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-940862.html)

And I did notice a bit of a difference. Not being satisfied I pursued a way to get the latest compiz update via this easy guide:

[http://tombuntu.com/index.php/2008/07/25/upgrade-to-the-latest-compiz-fusion-release/](http://tombuntu.com/index.php/2008/07/25/upgrade-to-the-latest-compiz-fusion-release/)

I wasn't convinced that it was behaving they way it did when I first installed Hardy when the computer was very new. I realized that the the CPU freq scaling was not behaving the way I *told* it do. I set it to high and it was not obeying. Determined to get this fixed, I found this article:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=868378](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=868378)

Then I realized that I've had issues with the fan and overheating on my previous laptop a T60p. I used a compressed air can to blow the dust out of the cpu heat sink on the back left and rear left. I also installed the thinkpad fan control:

[http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_control_fan_speed](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_control_fan_speed)

Now, I think I've got a good handle on all the performance all around! The cpu freq scaling behaves the way I tell it to and most all my effects are happy! :)

Happy compositing!

Mike
