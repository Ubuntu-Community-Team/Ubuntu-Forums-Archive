From the sound of things, you've simply encountered the Grub boot options screen. Using your up/down cursor buttons, highlight the linux kernel you want to have running and press enter.

The latest kernel is 2.6.31-20, and that will probably be listed at the top of the list and will be selected automatically by default if you don't select an alternate within an allotted time (usually 10 seconds).

The others in your list are legacy kernels that remain on your system until you're confident that the latest one is good, when you can simply uninstall them in the usual way.
