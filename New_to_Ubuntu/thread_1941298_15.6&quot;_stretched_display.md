### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-03-15
It has been a long time since I use 10.04 and things are done differently in more recent versions of Ubuntu. So, I may get this wrong.

Visual Effects on Ubuntu require 2 things. A video card/adapter that can run the visual effects and a video driver that can make use of the capabilities of the video card.

Let us assume that you have a capable video card. Then when you select visual effects in the Appearance menu the utility should search for a proprietary driver. To do so you must be connected to the Internet.

Is that the problem? Not being connected to the Internet was the reason I could not activate a proprietary driver and get visual effects when I first installed Ubuntu. The utility just tells you that no driver could be found. Doesn't it? If I remember correctly.

For your information, on later versions of Ubuntu we use a utility called Additional Drivers. We do not do this through the Appearance menu.

If you can install a proprietary video driver then you will get a settings utility with that driver that will have options for solving the stretching of the desktop.

On my system I have options for scaling in the proprietary driver settings utility.

As for the touchpad, someone else will need to advise you.

Regards.

Don't worry, it happens to all of us once in a while.

I took a look at it, and this is what I see:
[LIST]
[*]The edid from the monitor seems to be getting OK.
[*]Instead of the intel driver, you seem to be using the vesa driver.
[/LIST]
I believe you have an Intel HD Integrated Graphic (contained in the i5 core itself). Since support for them came stable after 10.04, I would recommend using a ppa to update the driver:
```
sudo add-apt-repository ppa:xorg-edgers/ppa

sudo apt-get update

sudo apt-get upgrade
```
Then, make sure that your xorg.conf uses the intel driver:
```
...
Section "Device"
    ...
    Driver      "intel"
    ...
EndSection
...
```
Then restart your machine.

Tell us how it goes.
