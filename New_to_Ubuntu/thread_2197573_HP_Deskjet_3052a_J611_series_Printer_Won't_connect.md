### Post by jordan-graham34 on 2014-01-04
I've spent a little bit trying to connect my printer over my wireless network. I'm working with Ubuntu 12.04 LTS and have been trying to use the HPLIP toolbox to connect my printer. I have got my printer working via the USB cable with 0 problems, but for some reason the HPLIP toolbox won't recognize the printer as a potential wireless device. It says only select wireless printers are supported by the utility, but from what I understand from this link: [http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/models/deskjet_aio/deskjet_3050a_j611_series.html#note4](http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/models/deskjet_aio/deskjet_3050a_j611_series.html#note4) , my printer (HP Deskjet 3052a J611) should be supported. 

I've tried combing throught the forums and didnt come across anything that helped. As I am new to Ubuntu I have this overwhelming feeling its a simpler fix than I realized or this printer simply isnt supported. Any help would be appreciated!

### Post by jordan-graham34 on 2014-01-04
I now have my printer connected over my network. I ended up having to first get my computer connected the the network with the right IP through the HP software on my windows device. Afterwords, I used the Network/Ethernet/wireless I/o connection type and only then was my printer noticed. After that the rest of set up was easy! once my printer was connected to the network I didnt even need to use the usb connection.
