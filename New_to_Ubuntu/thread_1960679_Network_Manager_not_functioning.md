### Post by yankees3142 on 2012-04-17
I installed 10.04 a few weeks ago, alongside Windows 7.  Initially, it took me a total of a minute to get my wireless working in Ubuntu.  I rightclicked on nm, gave it my network id and key, everything was fine.  

A couple days ago I opened firefox and noticed I had no connection.  The icon is still in the panel.  I right click and the only options it brings up is move, properties, and launch (or open, whatever the term it is they use :?), but when I try launching it, nothing happens.  Also, there's no option for enable or disable, and no indication that it's detecting any networks.  I've tried killing it and nothing seems to change.  I've killed the panel and nothing changes.  Basically I can see the icon but it's not doing anything.  I've searched quite a bit but can't seem to find my exact problem.  I hope I'm explaining it okay.


edit:  Nevermind, I figured it out.  I was confusing the 'network manager' icon with the 'notification area' icon.  I added notification area back to the panel and it brought up an identical icon, which allowed me to access networks. ugh :-)
