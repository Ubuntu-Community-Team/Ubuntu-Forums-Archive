### Post by Impavidus on 2022-07-23
There are many generations of Intel Core i5 processors, but the i5 series was introduced in 2009 and Windows 7 was succeeded in 2012, so that gives us some idea of the type of computer you use. I'm now using a laptop with i5 processosr originally coming with Windows 7 too. I have increased the RAM to the maximum supported by my computer, which is 8GiB. If you haven't getting some second hand RAM stick may be worth it. To play with virtual machines you may like a bit more capable hardware, but you could already play with low-end virtual machines.

To answer your direct question, you can download the live disk image of Ubuntu (or one of the flavours; on older hardware they may be better), create a bootable usb and wipe the entire drive clean. Of course, all files will be gone then. The installer has an option to wipe the drive clean and install the OS.
