### Post by Vignesh S on 2009-12-09
Based on my 1 year with Ubuntu, I can safely say that Ubuntu doesn't need an antivirus to keep it secure :-D. This can probably be attributed to the simple fact that there are so less linux users compared to windows users, if a virus maker wants to wreak havoc and want to affect the most users possible, then they'd make a virus for Windows, simply because there are a LOT more users of it out there.

That's just from my personal opinion however, and if you are very security conscious, and you are particularly worried that you might send one of your friends a virus via files you give them, then yes, an antivirus would be of comfort to you.

 But a word of warning: BitDefender for unices is VERY system intensive. It used up so much system resources on my old laptop, I practically couldn't do anything with it. And the result: no viruses :shock: Even my powerful desktop suffered greatly performance wise while BitDefender was running. Yet again, no viruses. It drove me mad, and I haven't touched it since.

At the end of the day, its your choice, but with certainty I can say that Ubuntu doesn't need an antivirus.

### Post by bumanie on 2009-12-09
Not a bad idea to have antivirus if you are sharing to a windows machine/partition - otherwise it is basically useless on linux. [This article]("http://linuxmafia.com/~rick/faq/index.php?page=virus") spells things out very well and has been a work in progress over a number of years. It is not just lower number of users, it is the fact that linux is a hostile environment to viruses. Most major servers in the world run on linux, so there are many reasons why virus writers would want to infiltrate those machines, but it doesn't happen or is quickly caught and patched before any wide-ranging damage can be done.
