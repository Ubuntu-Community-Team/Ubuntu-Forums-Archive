### Post by youngalfred on 2008-09-22
With default settings both gnome and kde take up the same screen realestate.  Kde does this with one panel, and gnome does this with two.  I am currently using an asus eeepc 7 inch, and i find gnome is great with the bottom panel removed.  but as kerry_s said, its linux.  do what you want, you can change everything. If you are running ubuntu, go system> preferences> appearance, select a theme, then click customize.  play around with all the settings until you find something you like.  controls are a good place to start.

### Post by Greyed on 2008-09-22
> **opendevlite said:**
> Which is best for my monitor kde or gnome?

Yes.

What you're asking is something like this.

"I have two closets full of clothes.  One on the left, the other on the right.  They both contain the same basic clothing.  Which door should I choose to make a decent casual outfit?"

Either closet will work, however pulling clothes out of the closet at random probably will not suit your desires.  However if you pick and choose from the clothes available to you then you probably will come up with an outfit that will suit your desires.

Closets = KDE/Gnome (or XFCE, or LXDE, or....).
Clothing = GUI options
