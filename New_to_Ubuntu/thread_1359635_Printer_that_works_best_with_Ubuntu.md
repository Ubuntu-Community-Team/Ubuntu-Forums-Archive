### Post by sgosnell on 2009-12-19
Most older HP and Brother printers work fine.  Brand new ones, at least HP, don't seem to have drivers available, but there are reports that they work anyway.  I have an HP 6310 that works much better under Ubuntu than it does under Windows.  It won't scan under XP, even after hours on the phone to India and the receipt of a couple of new CDs.  With Ubuntu, it was a matter of clicking on Add a new printer.  The wizard found the network printer, got the drivers for it, and that was that.  I have hplip installed, but it's not strictly necessary, and I seldom use it.  At work we have Brother printers, and with the Windows computers there it's a long, involved chore to get one to work.  With Ubuntu, I just did the same as for my HP, but for full functionality needed to download and install a couple of drivers from the Brother website.  For Windows, it involves 70+MB of downloads.  I haven't had the chance to check the newest printers from Brother, though.
