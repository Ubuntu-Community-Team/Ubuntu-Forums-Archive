### Post by Dougie187 on 2008-08-21
Your laptop should be great in ubuntu. one thing to note however, even though your wireless card supports draft n, the current wireless stack doesn't support draft-n. so if you do get it, and you have an n speed router, don't be surprised if you can't acheieve the same speeds as you can in windows. From what i hear this will be fixed in ibex though, which will be out in around 2 months.

Also, I personally don't recommend using wubi if you are serious about using ubuntu. I have just heard way too many issues. Granted it is a great way to "try it out" but personally I feel like its better to just dive in, or dual boot. Though the choice is up to you.
