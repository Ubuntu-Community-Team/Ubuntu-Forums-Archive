PS I was just now able to boot from a live 10.04 with no problem!?

Flakey hardware is usually just that - flakey hardware.  I just bought a faulty USB thumb drive which went bad over the course of a week.  I wasted hours messing with it because I thought something else was corrupting it.

Put a lens cleaner in your drive (can't hurt) as I've found that sometimes gives new life to an old drive, but either way it's telling you it's almost time for a replacement.  If you use the drive for backups or anything important you will never know if you've had a good burn, or if you've just made a frisbee that can't be read on any other drive, and not even the original drive when it finally breaks for good.  Use the slowest burn speed for important stuff.

A drive that consistently writes bad disks at medium to high speeds is another sign of a failing drive: it's one of my criteria for finding out if I can trust it or the drive needs replacing
