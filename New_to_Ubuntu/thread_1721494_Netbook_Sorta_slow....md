### Post by davidmohammed on 2011-04-04
I think that netbook comes with intel integrated graphics - so no, very little you can do with it to speed up via newer graphics drivers.

You could try the latest x-swat ppa (but since you posted this in absolute Beginner Talk I'm a little nervous recommending you take this approach since this may cause more problems than its worth).

Can I suggest you wait for three weeks.  Natty (11.04) will be released.  Upgrade to this - if the new Unity interface doesnt work for your netbook you can always install "Unity-2D" from software centre.  Both Unity and Unity-2D are the new "netboook remix" that you are currently using in 10.10 - both are significantly faster to use than netbook remix.

As to your battery issues.  This has been a known issue for netbooks and linux in general.  Various websites such as [http://www.webupd8.org/2010/06/increase-your-linux-netbook-laptop.html]("http://www.webupd8.org/2010/06/increase-your-linux-netbook-laptop.html")this have some hints and tips to increase battery life.

### Post by earthpigg on 2011-04-05
> **jgaffney86 said:**
> 
I thought Ubuntu would be good because it said it was able to video edit and photo edit and everything so I wanted to give it a try. But it running so slow is steering me away....

as others have said, you could try lubuntu or crunchbang.

another alternative, while using normal ubuntu and looking to save battery life, is to perhaps take a look and see if your CPU is being throttled at all. 

as in the attached image, add the "CPU Frequency Scaling Monitor" by right clicking on a panel and selecting "add to panel". then click on it, take a look at & play with the options. scaling down your CPU when you are not doing anything CPU-intensive should save some battery life.

EDIT: also, firefox isn't netbook friendly in general. i use chromium on mine.

also, avoid flash in general. it sucks up battery life, and no one in the open source community can fix it because it is closed source.
