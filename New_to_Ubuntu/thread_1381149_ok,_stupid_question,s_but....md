### Post by akand074 on 2010-01-14
A bad sector is a section on your hard drive or flash memory that can't be used due to permanent damage (whether physical or not). Actually there generally could bad sectors on all hard drives even those straight from the manufacturer its just space on the hard drive that can not be used.

Mounting an HDD is simply "activating" the drive so you can access it. Its like when you put in a USB stick into your computer the drive is mounted and now you can access it.

Grub is the boot system that ubuntu uses. So when you first turn on your computer, Grub will load showing you all the operating systems available to use basically.

Alrighty.

Let see here- A bad sector is just a bad group of data on your drive. imagine a honeycomb is your hard drive, bad sectors would be where someone squishes some of the cells. theyre still there, but they arent very usable.

Mounting a drive simply means 'hooking it up' to your computer for the OS to see. it might be physically attached, but until it's mounted, its not used.

Grub is a boot loader. it stands for GRand Universal Bootloader. It is a program that organizes all of the operating systems on your computer and makes them available to start when you turn your computer on.
