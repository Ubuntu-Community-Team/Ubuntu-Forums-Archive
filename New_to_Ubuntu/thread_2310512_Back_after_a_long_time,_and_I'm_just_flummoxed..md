### Post by Rob Sayer on 2016-01-21
I agree about Unity ... I frakking hate it.  But I do not recommend just installing another desktop on your existing Unity DE version.  It generally makes things less reliable.

My suggestions would be KDE or Mate or Xfce.  KDE is a large, heavyweight desktop like Unity but you can seriously speed it up.  The other two are lighter and faster.

I have the Mate version of Mint 17 (Ubuntu 14.04 based) on my netbook.  It's pretty well sorted but there are a couple of things that drive me batty about it.

You cannot use the mouse wheel scroll to switch virtual desktops, which may be minor but it's very annoying to me.  Every other DE I've tried expect Cinnamon does this, even LXDE, which is what I installed there and use.  LXDE is very ligh and non integrated so I make an exception to my no multiple DEs for it.  But I'm still probably going to install Lubuntu on it ...

The other thing about Mate and Cinnamon is that I like to add an auto hide panel on the left as a dock, and neither allow this.  ANother perhaps petty thing that drives me nuts.

However, I prefer any of the above to Unity.  I mean, people hate WIndows 8 because it's pretty much a tablet interface on something that isn't a tablet, but Unity did it first.

My suggestion would be to download all the other Ubuntu DE live CD versions and burn them to a USB stick.  Then just boot them and play around to get a feel for them.  Then back up your data and do a clean reinstall of the one you prefer.

### Post by mikodo on 2016-01-22
Yes, Unity is a surprise to people when they first see it. Though many who didn't like it initially stayed with it and have come to love it. I don't use it, I switched to Xubuntu, (Xfce) when Unity became the default DE for Ubuntu. Its' .iso is plain but, like mentioned above it is extremely modular and configurable. What I would do is go ahead and install the other flavours on your "Unity" install, and choose each when booting to get a feel for them. There is going to be a lot more apps installed and you'll see kind of a hodge-podge, (?spelling) of the DE's with apps from other distro's which I didn't like when I did it but, you get a picture what the DE looks like. You can take it one step further and do some configuring if you wish. Then, reinstall with just the one DE you think you like the best.

The point of Ubuntu-Mate DE is, that it is a fork of the old-Gnome2 DE and, it will look really familiar to what Ubuntu had stock in days of yore. I am guessing if you only tried one of the original flavours you would find that looking and performing like you remembered Linux DE "should" be like.

Or, play some more with Unity and, see if you come to like it like so many have. It has it's advantages as, Canonical/Ubuntu are working to make it the "convergence" DE to eventually work with Desktops, Laptops, Tablets, Phones and possibly TV's (I'm not sure), to name a big advantage of it.

Good luck.
