### Post by desperado665 on 2009-08-21
I think you should be looking for system>preferences>startup applications. You will need to add cairo-dock there. I have done that and it works great, but then again I am running 9.04, but I assume it should be the same in your version.

### Post by nhasian on 2009-08-21
before it was called startup-applications, it used to just be called sessions.  you can add it to the startup programs by going to system->preferences->sessions if your in ubuntu 8.10
