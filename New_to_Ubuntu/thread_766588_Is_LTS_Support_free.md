### Post by Oldsoldier2003 on 2008-04-25
> **mister_pink said:**
> Nope, thats per year. Its meant for businesses so its not cheap. For home users these forums and the internet in general should be more than enough.

Agreed, a home user needn't worry about paying for support, and tbh most business users shouldn't have to worry either if they have a competent staff. The guys that paid support will be worthwhile for are businesses that have critical application servers that run oddball hardware, for example; if a kernel module update goes sour canonical support will jump right on it as a priority, etc...
The reason I say competent staff usually overrides the need for tech support is that servers rarely get touched once they are up and stable, and competent staffers wouldn't upgrade production machines in a mission critical environment without testing first.
