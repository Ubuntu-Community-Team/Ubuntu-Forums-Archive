### Post by -kg- on 2010-08-06
I think it's just a "reporting" error.

There is nothing in software or the OS that's going to make the receiver or transmitter in your WIFI hardware any more or less sensitive.  The signal strength you see is the level of reception reported to the OS by the device.

I've done research on this issue, and the consensus is that the sensitivity of WIFI cards cannot be adjusted.  The only way to increase their sensitivity is to build an external antenna (if one can be built and somehow connected) or to buy one that has better sensitivity (and an external antenna or two).

@ marioward1: I've looked up your unit, and found the following tech specs as far as receive sensitivity:

>     *  54Mbps 10% PER @ -68 dBm (typical)
    * 11Mbps 8% PER @ -83 dBm (typical)


@ inameiname: I couldn't find any definitive specs on your router (manufacturer discontinued them), but several sites had the router rated very lowly ("Reviews/CNet" rated at 1/2 star) with bad connectivity problems, mainly.

I think your best bet would be to buy a USB WIFI adapter that has a better range.  The following link seems to have some good suggestions:

[http://www.data-alliance.net/Page.bok?template=long-range-clients&gclid=CI2EtZeXpqMCFRJBDQodbkvX5A]("http://www.data-alliance.net/Page.bok?template=long-range-clients&gclid=CI2EtZeXpqMCFRJBDQodbkvX5A")

Also, you might be able to get better height or position by hooking the WIFI device to a USB cable extension and moving the WIFI device to various places, rather than having to move the whole computer there.
