### Post by Ratscallion on 2009-12-12
As well as all that's already been posted (and this may be one of them in other words) I think that Ubuntu doesn't use as many Internet apps simultaneously. For example, when I load Windows, I load: iTunes (It'll download any podcasts automatically), Windows Live Messenger, a web browser, an antivirus, windows updater, Twitter client, Skype, DropBox, Wakoopa.  Ubuntu loads: Pidgin, Skype, web browser, twitter client, updates, Ubuntu One, DropBox, Wakoopa. Although there's only a difference in one program, this may make all the difference. 
From experience, the Windows Windows Live Messenger client uses a TON of bandwidth, whereas Pidgin uses hardly any. The more of the bandwidth being used, the less there is left for browsing, and that's where Ubuntu comes in.

### Post by The Cog on 2009-12-12
I see no fundamental reason why a download should be slower with Windows than with Linux. Their TCP stacks are both well capable of handling internet type traffic levels - the figures you quote amount to around 4 Mbit/Sec - a little better than I get from my broadband. 

So two explanations I can think of: 

The download sites you use with Windows are busier and slower, i.e. the slowness is not yours but the servers you download from.

In windows, your antivirus scanner is scanning your download for viruses and this is slowing the transfer down.

Both, either or neither could be the reason, I dunno.
