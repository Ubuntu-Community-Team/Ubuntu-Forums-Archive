### Post by swoody on 2009-05-03
It may not be just that easy. While one D-link adapter may work specifically on your computer, it's not good to make generalizations about the company's entire line of products. 

Masterwares - What's the model name/number of your specific adapter? Let us know, and we'll be able to find out for you what kind of support you can expect from it. Your best friend will probably be Google. Try searching for your wireless adapter's specific model name, and "Ubuntu", "Linux Supported", etc. to see what you can find. This is also a great way to see what problems others have run into with your specific adapter, so you can be a little more prepared if anything does give you problems. You can also try looking for it on one of these websites (among may others out there) to see if it's supported in Linux/Ubuntu:

[http://www.ubuntuhcl.org/](http://www.ubuntuhcl.org/)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported)
[http://www.linux.org/hardware/](http://www.linux.org/hardware/)
