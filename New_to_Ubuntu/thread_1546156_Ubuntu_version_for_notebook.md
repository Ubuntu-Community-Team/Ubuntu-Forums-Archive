### Post by Kixtosh on 2010-08-06
Without installing something completely new, you could try just adding the LXDE (Lightweight X11 Desktop Environment). You will have to use the software center from the regular application menus or the synaptic package manager to do this.

For my old machines, this yielded a significant improvement in performance, especially in terms of time needed to boot up. It's an easy modification to make and in my experience, doing this will give you 90% or more of the benefits of the lighter distro options (including even Puppy), although I started from Xubuntu, not regular Ubuntu, when I tested this option.
