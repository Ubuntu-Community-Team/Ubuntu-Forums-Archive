### Post by luisuebel on 2011-06-21
There are many reasons for a commercial speech recognizer be available and an open source doesn't match the performance: 
1. Any speech recognizer needs to have LOTS of speech database and this is very expensive.
Voxforge is a good alternative, but needs that people record their voice.
It is good to have at least 400 hours to have a good speech database.

2. It is time and CPU demanding to produce good acoustic models.
Only in electric power you will expend a lot of money.

3. Language models require a LOT of text to produce good and reliable statistics.
This means a lot of effort to collect, clean and produce good language models.


If your idea is only to use open source because money issues, open source will not be a good alternative. US$ 500 is not so much by effort to produce a good speech recognizer. If your idea is to make a good impact for disable people, record some file in Voxforge and help to build a good acoustic models.
