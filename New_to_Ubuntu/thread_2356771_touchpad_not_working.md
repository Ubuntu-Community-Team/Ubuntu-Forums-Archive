### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2017-03-27
Some Acers have "unusual" touchpad behavior.

You can try to turn the touchpad on via the keyboard (fn + f7) on my Acer TravelMate B.    Then . . . reboot the the laptop and try using the touchpad again (it should work).    IF you turn it off (again via FN + F7), be sure to re-activate it BEFORE turning the laptop off, otherwise you'll see the same behavior.

Your system may be different, but I thought I'd share this as it may be common to more than one Acer laptop model.
