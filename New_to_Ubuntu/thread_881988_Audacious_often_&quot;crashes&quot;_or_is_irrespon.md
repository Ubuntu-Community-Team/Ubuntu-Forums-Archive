### Post by cozmicharlie on 2008-08-07
It's hard to know what is going on.  I have read numerous posts of people having problems with Audacious.  I am sure others would argue that it works great and for many I am sure it does.  I found xmms to be the most stable player in Ubuntu - it's similar to Audacious but it just seems to work better for me.  Unfortunately, it is not in the repos anymore because the developers are focusing on xmms2 (not the same as xmms).  However, there is an excellent tutorial that works great [http://blog.sartek.net/2008/04/install-xmms-on-ubuntu-804-hardy-heron.html](http://blog.sartek.net/2008/04/install-xmms-on-ubuntu-804-hardy-heron.html).

It is command line and I know you are not a power user but it really is easy.  All you have to do is open a terminal and cut and paste all the commands just as written in the tutorial.  If you do decide to give it a go and run into trouble just post back here and I can help.

If you are not comfortable with installing from the command line then you may want to look into another player.  Quod Libet is very good and very stable.  Good old Rythmbox usually works well for me.  Also, make sure you have all the codecs installed to play your music (the gstreamer plug ins work great).
