### Post by lolpenguin on 2011-12-03
Simply, yes. 10.04 reaches its End of Life (EoL) in 2013, you should upgrade, to either the current stable or current LTS when you need. Here are a few reasons:
1) After the EoL bugfixes and maintenance discontinue, while more vulnerabilities are discovered.
2) New features in new releases.
3) You don't get left behind with old versions of programs.
4) It won't stay usable forever; you'll eventually need a new computer.
5) Much more...
If you do not feel comfortable with normal stable releases, you can upgrade to LTS releases only. But as always, we won't MAKE you do something.

With 10.04, you have until April 2013 before they stop sending you updates, so you're good until then. After that, you can either upgrade to 12.04, which will be the next LTS release, or switch to another distro. 12.04 will have 5 years of support rather than 3. Another option for you is to switch to Debian 6, which is essentially the same as 10.04, and will probably be supported for several years after 2013.
