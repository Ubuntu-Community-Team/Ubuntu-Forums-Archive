It's not really a case of removing it, it's a case of not putting it in. Amarok 2 was essentially a complete re-write, so all the features that were there before need to be re-done before they'll be in Amarok 2.

@Gen2ly: He's right, software equalisers are crap at making bad things sound good. But they might be good enough for some people. Whether it gets included really depends on whether the person that writes it thinks it's good enough to be worth the effort. The beauty of having access to the source code, though, means that even if a million people don't think it's worth the effort, the one person who does has the means to do it themselves.

### Post by CatKiller on 2009-08-24
> **cascade9 said:**
> Stupid question, but how does it go for you?

Pretty nice :)

I was using a set of Labtecs that had a sub before and, although they weren't great (really good for the money, though) they had a nice bass peak that just begged to be turned up just to put a stupid grin on your face. Fluffy highs, but brilliant mids.

These ones are just smooth, and the sounds just keep going lower and lower. It took a little bit of tweaking to get a nice balance between the sub and the monitors, but not much; maybe half an hour of listening. The sub is ludicrously large compared to a standard "computer" setup - it's a 15" cube - but it doesn't just thump like you might imagine a sub to do. I can only really tell what's coming out of the sub rather than the monitors by putting my foot on it. Or by using the bypass, which is the sensible way to do it :)

I've worked in a few studios before, so I was used to what things should sound like, but my other half had only really heard computer music through the usual cheap speakers. Once I'd set these up I sat her down in front of my computer with some tunes playing. She was still there three hours later. I think that's an affirmative vote.
