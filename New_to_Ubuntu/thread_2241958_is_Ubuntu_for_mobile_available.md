### Post by mcduck on 2014-08-29
It's still under development. As far as I know the development version can be installed on Nexus 4 and 2nd gen Nexus 7 and Nexus 10, and there's some phones coming available in some countries later this year.

Based on the state of things, I'd only recommend it at this stage if you are really interested in helping with the development & testing the OS or building apps for it, not as a fully-featured replacement for Android or iOS.

(I'm not using it myself since my Nexus 5 isn't officially supported yet and I need a fully-featured smart phone most of the time, so if someone here is actually running Ubuntu on their phone feel free to correct me if I'm wrong. I'm definitely waiting for the day when I might be able to replace my Android with Ubuntu. :D)

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-08-29
Ubuntu for phones/tablets is officially being developed for the purpose of giving a Ubuntu image to OEMs to pre-install on their mobile devices for retail. There is no intention of making that kind of image available for download on all and every mobile device in the same way that Ubuntu desktop is available to us right now.
