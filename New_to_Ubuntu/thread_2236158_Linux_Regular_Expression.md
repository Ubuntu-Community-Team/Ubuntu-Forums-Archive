### Post by steeldriver on 2014-07-25
... and most importantly, don't forget [Some people, when confronted with a problem, think “I know, I'll use regular expressions.”   Now they have two problems.]("http://regex.info/blog/2006-09-15/247")

On a more serious note, a lot of what you have probably seen (especially if related to things like 'find') are [shell globs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glob_%28programming%29) rather than regular expressions - be careful not to confuse the two things

### Post by bulrush2 on 2014-07-26
During your learning process these are super helpful: 

This one lets you test your regex online, and explains and highlights each part. It has regex flavors for PCRE, Javascript and Python.  [http://regex101.com/](http://regex101.com/)

This one is similar to above. Put your mouse over a match or part of the regex for an explanation. Really handy. [http://www.regexr.com/](http://www.regexr.com/)
