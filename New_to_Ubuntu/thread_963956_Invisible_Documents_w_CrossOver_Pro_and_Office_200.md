### Post by Kellemora on 2008-10-30
Hi KT

Sorry, I can't help with your problem!

I own a small publishing company, mostly pamphlets, yearbooks, heavy graphics, and newsletters, things like that.

All of our work has historically been done on msWORD, from office97 to office2003, we never jumped up to office2007, to costly with 8 machines.

We have converted well over 35,000 .doc files over to .odt files, with less than 600 of them needing a tweak due to the fact we used every hidden pixel of space in Word.

Between Gimp and OOwriter, after only a short learning curve, we have actually increased production and rarely if ever have any of the problems we did using msWord, such as images jumping around 2 minutes before publishing.

Sure, there are a few things in Word not found in Writer, but by the same token, there are many things in Writer not found in Word that can handle the job better in many cases.  Mostly in weekly's and periodicals that have the same format week in and week out.  Every single edit in msWord we had to tell it NOT to change the normal.dot template.  That has NEVER happened once in OOwriter yet.

Many of our .odt documents have to be converted back to .doc so we usually save those as .doc throughout the process.  But going from .odt to .doc has yet to be a problem for us on anything.

Just thinking, perhaps on small things, you might try getting used to OOwriter, it really is great!  AND you can put it on ALL of your machines legally.
