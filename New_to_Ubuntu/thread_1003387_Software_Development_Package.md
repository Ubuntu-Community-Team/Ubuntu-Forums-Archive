### Post by snova on 2008-12-06
Of course not. I find Linux a very enjoyable development platform.

For C and C++, the build-essential package is important (hence the 'essential' :)). The C compiler is 'gcc'; C++, 'g++'.

Fortran and Ada have compilers in the repos, but I don't know what they are. But they are both part of GCC (which supports many languages and countless architectures).

I don't know about Visual Basic, it may not even be doable... Nor do I know about Pascal.

As for Visual Studio, you want an IDE- there are many. A (non-comprehensive) list of some I know of:

[LIST]
  [*]Eclipse
  [*]NetBeans
  [*]KDevelop
  [*]Anjuta
  [*]Geany
  [*]Code::Blocks
  [*]More...
[/LIST]
