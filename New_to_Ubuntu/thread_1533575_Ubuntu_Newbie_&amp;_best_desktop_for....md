Hi there, and Welcome to Ubuntu!

I have helped three friends buy large display laptops from Linux Emporium, and I also have bought a couple of Novatech desktops myself. You may know that there are linux forums on novatech:
[http://forum.novatech.co.uk/forumdisplay.php?f=53](http://forum.novatech.co.uk/forumdisplay.php?f=53)

Although items are priced with, and without, Windows, Novatech do not select any of their products to be favourable, or otherwise, towards Linux.

At the time when I priced the laptops I concluded that the LE price was approximately 50 uk pounds more than a same windows machine on ebay etc. My friends wanted a reasonable retail experience from their laptops purchase, including normal support within warranty period. The support they have received from LE is quite outstanding, and I have no doubt at all that the price is good value.

However, if you are eager and willing to do it yourself then the value from LE is less applicable. However, they do (obviously) carefully select machine types with Linux in mind.

I buy Novatech machines because on principle I strongly dislike the Windows tax. I bought an isys elite pro e8400 320gb desktop (no OS) in 2008, and also a couple of months ago I bought another desktop an isys pro q8300 500gb 4gb. At time of purchase the elite pro on board sound (nvidia chips) (!) were not picked up in ubuntu so I simply added a pci soundblaster card, but in the subsequent several months, the onboard chips were included in ubuntu updates (I had anyway registered a bug with information). 

The main reason for my second purchase was because the first machine had failed (!) after little more than 2 years. I did a lot of diagnostics myself and concluded it was not a simple fault, probably mainboard, so the machine is now on one side awaiting probably a novatech motherboard kit package when I have time. I have a lot of elderly recycled PCs and a 2 year failure is not good in my opinion.

The second machine also failed, after 2 months, only it was one of the two ram sticks, which was easy to replace  under warranty, no problems since I am near to Reading store. However the diagnosis of the ram stick fail was not at first easy to know because the bios did not respond in any way at all, appeared dead. Out of business hours too of course. Novatech support were helpful and accurate though, the next day.

I will still continue to use Novatech until I can find another non windows supplier however, in their drive to make a profit I wonder if Novatech are looking closely enough at quality. And be aware that if returning items to novatech, you will need to use suitable packaging if posting, N. advise you to retain the original packaging.

good luck

### Post by YesWeCan on 2010-07-18
You know, one of the great advantages of linux is that it is a very efficient OS and many people including myself have breathed new life into old hardware by replacing lumbering Windows.

I think at least 50% of my friends have a spare or disused PC lying around. Take my brother, for example, who has 3 high-spec Windows machines in his home office and had two old shells of PCs in his basement until I paid him a visit. One of those old PCs has an AMD64 3500+ single core with 2GB of RAM and thanks to Ubuntu it is now an openVPN server for remote access to the Windows network, MySQL server, webpage server, complete email system, and Java/Netbeans development platform. Wow. And it is now the most reliable PC on his network.

For university *work* I imagine you don't need lightning fast gaming speed and if you did you'd probably want Windows anyhow.

Then there are online 2nd hand sites too, and eBay of course.
Just a thought.
