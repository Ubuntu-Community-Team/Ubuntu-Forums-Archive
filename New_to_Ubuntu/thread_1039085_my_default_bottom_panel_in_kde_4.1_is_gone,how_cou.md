### Post by fooman on 2009-01-14
i'm sure there are better ways and i don't use kde much anymore...but when that happened to me,  i just deleted my entire .kde folder and it reverted *all* the kde settings to their default state.

open your home folder and in the toolbar, click "view" and put a check next to "show hidden files"....then find the .kde directory,  right-click on it and choose "move to trash".  log out & back in again and you should have it back.

hope that helps.

### Post by drs305 on 2009-01-14
If you use a normal delete and find that too many customized settings have been removed you will be able to restore the deleted .kde folder from the trash bin. It will be located in /home/yourusername/.local/share/Trash/files
