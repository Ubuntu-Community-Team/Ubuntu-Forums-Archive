### Post by HubertB on 2009-11-05
Your apps will be replaced by more recent versions. The package management software will take care of this.

Since almost all settings you've made (Firefox bookmarks, Evolution email accounts and so on) are stored in your home directory (e. g. /home/dave), they won't got lost.

But keep Murphy's Law in mind: There is always the chance that something goes wrong.

### Post by madmax75 on 2009-11-05
The upgrade won't touch your Home directory, where you have (hopefully) stored all your own stuff.

I still advice to backup everything in your Home directory. I have an external USB hard drive and I regularly backup my entire Home directory into it (some 60 GB of stuff). This is the most hassle-free option IMHO. You can also use USB memory sticks and cd-r's or dvd-rs for this.

WARNING: do not update to 9.10 yet, especially if you have a mobile broadband 3G modem (you connect to the internet via the cell phone network). Most 3G modems seem to have major problems with 9.10, especially the Huawei brand of modems just don't work. There is apparently a bug in 9.10 kernel and reparations are coming at some point, but consider yourself be warned.

in my opinion, 9.10 got released way too soon. it still has major bugs that just need to get sorted out. I recommend sticking to 9.04 for some time - it just works like a charm!
