### Post by Therion on 2009-06-11
You're not going to be able to do much with that notebook.  For starters, the maximum RAM it will accept is 4GB.  The video is integrated so dropping in a new video card where none existed before is not going to be an option and I doubt very seriously that upgrading the CPU is going to be an option either.  It's possible but highly unlikely.

I would hazard to guess that your integrated graphics chipset is "borrowing" a large portion of your system RAM, possibly as much as 1GB.  Maxing out the RAM would be the cheapest, simplest and overall best upgrade you could do for that machine.  Even then I'm not sure how much you'll be able to squeeze out of it, but you can never have too much RAM and your integrated graphics are definitely chowing down on what RAM you do have.
