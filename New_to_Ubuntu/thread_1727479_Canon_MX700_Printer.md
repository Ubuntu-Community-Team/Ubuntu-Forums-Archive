### Post by markymark64 on 2011-04-13
rosencrantz yes more than happy to.

Here's what I did. 

First, I clicked on System>Administration>Printing.Choose add a printer. My device showed up at the very top of the list as MX700. So, I chose MX 700 and clicked forward. Now ubuntu is searching for drivers but I want the drivers for a printer that my printer can work with. A list comes up and it shows generic at the top. Scroll down to Canon and click forward. Then scroll down and choose the Pixma MP 520, which is a multi function printer that is very similar to the mx700. Choose this printer and  click forward. Click apply and you will be prompted to print a test page. Print the test page to make sure it works. 

I did discover that if I want to print in grayscale I have to tell my printer that it's a grayscale printer before it prints. However, I'll let you or anyone else play with this one. I know there are better drivers out there but at least I have my printer back. Now I'm off to do a short snoopy dance. Yay!
