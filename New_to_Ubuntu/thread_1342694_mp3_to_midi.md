### Post by dvlchd3 on 2009-12-01
I'm not sure if that software exists for free.  Because midi files "simply transmits digital data "event messages" such as the pitch and intensity of musical notes to play, control signals for parameters such as volume, vibrato and panning, cues and clock signals to set the tempo." ([http://www.thecomputermechanics.com/forums/showpost.php?p=240197&postcount=4](http://www.thecomputermechanics.com/forums/showpost.php?p=240197&postcount=4))

Converting any audio file to midi is difficult, and requires a lot of guessing work, therefore, the results may not be what you expected.  Typically midi files are generated directly from instruments.  

From the thread at thecomputermechanics.com, someone recommended two sites, but it looks like this is proprietary, closed-source, pay to use, software.  Here they are:

[http://recognition.qarchive.org/](http://recognition.qarchive.org/)
[http://convert-mp3-to-midi-linux.qarchive.org/](http://convert-mp3-to-midi-linux.qarchive.org/)

And of course, the original thread that most of this information came from:
[http://www.thecomputermechanics.com/forums/showthread.php?t=33534](http://www.thecomputermechanics.com/forums/showthread.php?t=33534)
