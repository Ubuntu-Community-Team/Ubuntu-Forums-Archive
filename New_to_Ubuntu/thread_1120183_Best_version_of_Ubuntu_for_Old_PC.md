> **yeehi said:**
> On Broadband.

Trying another distro would be fine. I enjoyed the read about Vector. I will check out puppy and Arch.

One reason I am fond of Ubuntu is because it is Debian based.

Here is one I just saw and hadn´t heard of before: [fluxubuntu]("http://distrowatch.com/table.php?distribution=fluxbuntu").

Fluxbuntu is a great distro, but I would advise against installing anything based on 7.10 Gutsy Gibbon at this point. Canonical is ending support for 7.10 in a couple of weeks.

Crunchbang is in my opinion the best (unofficial) Ubuntu variant for older hardware. It should run okay on your pentium 2 if you have enough ram (256mb or more) and avoid the heavier applications.

If you are open minded about trying non-Ubuntu distros, you have a lot more choices. My personal favorite in this category is SliTaz. It also comes in a 'loram' flavor that you can use with less than 256mb of ram. 

Here is some good reading material: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456)
