### Post by Ranemills on 2010-03-06
Hey,

How should I store a motherboard? I've just gutted a computer and want to keep the motherboard. Should I store in an anti-static bag, or something else?

Also, would it be advisable to take the CPU, heat sink and fan off of the motherboard and store them separately? If so, how should I store those?

Thanks in advance

---

### Post by RedRat on 2010-03-06
Store it in an anti-static bag. And be careful with it, suggest you put it in a safe place where it will not be bumped. I think it would be prudent to remove the CPU and heatsink, however, remember that there may be a film of heat transfer goo between the two. Sometimes that comes off easily, but sometimes not so much. Usually the CPU and heatsink can weigh quite a bit so it would put stresses on your motherboard.

### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2010-03-06
Yes, you should store it in anti-static wrap or in such a bag.  You don't need to remove the CPU and heatsink unless they interfere with storing it safely.  You will want to remove any cards or memory modules on it though.  If you do remove the heatsink and CPU, take note of RedRat's comment about the heat transfer material that sits between the heatsink and the CPU.  You will most likely need to get a new pad or paste to replace the existing material there since it will likely flake apart and will also lose its effectiveness after being disconnected from the two pieces.

You should place the wrapped/bagged board in a box if possible, and store it out of the way (like on a top shelf with nothing on top of it).
