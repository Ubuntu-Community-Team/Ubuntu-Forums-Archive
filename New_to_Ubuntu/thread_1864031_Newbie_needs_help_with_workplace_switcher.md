First of all welcome to ubuntu and to the forum,

Ok first that task switcher you can use this key combination to scroll through your available workspaces.

```
Ctrl+Alt+ <Arrow Keys left or right>
```

Or

```
Ctrl+Alt+ <Arrow Keys up or down>
```

This will open up a small wall type area where you can move around using the arrows and don't let go of the Ctrl+Alt keys until you come to the desired workspace. When you skip the workspaces the workspace selected will light up or brighten up and the other once will be darker once you get to the needed work space let go of your keys and that workspace will load.

And bro remember this Linux is very different from Windows and Mac for help we're here and be little patient with s 'cos we also are volunteers.

Ask any thing we will help you.

### Post by Gone fishing on 2011-10-18
Not dead sure what you are doing but try this: 

Open a program then 
click super (windows key) + S you should see 4 windows
double click on another workspace
the open another program 
click super + S and you will be able to go from one workspace to the other and if you want use both Apps full screen

After clicking super +S you can even drag an App from one work space to the next.

Note if you minimize in Unity you can't see the App is open other than the arrow on the doc.
