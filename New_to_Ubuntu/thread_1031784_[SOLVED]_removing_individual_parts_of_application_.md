### Post by Kinetic Being on 2009-01-05
They are all contained in a single *meta-package* called (something like, not exactly this) gnome-games, and if you try to remove one of them, they will all be removed.

You may be able to install individual games (though maybe not those specific games, but there are other applications that provide basically the same game, such as chess, sodoku, tetris, and the others.) but you won't be able to selectivly remove the individual games through the package manager. 

If you really wanted to, you could manually remove the files to the games you don't want, and remove them from the menu, but that's messy...
