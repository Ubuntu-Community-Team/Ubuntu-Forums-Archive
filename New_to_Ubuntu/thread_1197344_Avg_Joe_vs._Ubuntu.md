### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-06-26
Why not ask your friend to explain a bit more?  We are all honest people, but we are all fond of Ubuntu, and use it in preference to Windows alternatives.

Nevertheless, here are some pointers:

1. Ubuntu is not Windows
Sounds obvious, but not everything works the same.  Not better or worse, just different.  However, different can require a bit of learning and patience.

2. You do not *have* to use Terminal commands
Lots of advice here recommends using the Terminal to copy and paste (or type manually).  This is simply because it is much clearer and faster to do this than try and describe which bits of the screen to click on (i.e. less chance of misunderstanding, especially if you cut & paste - removes typos!)

3. Most Ubuntu users are not programmers
However, there are a few here on the forum; unsurprising that programmers are often best equipped to help use software correctly.  However, they remain the minority even here.  I am not a programmer (and never have been), but hope I have solved a few problems over the last year or two.  What can be helpful is the ability to use Google effectively, and the ability to describe problems here on the forum in detail to obtain solutions.  Nevertheless, most users do not actually need any help from the forum at all.  Try looking through a Windows forum, and consider what your impression of it would be if you had never used it.

4. Ubuntu supports lots of hardware, but not all flawlessly
Drivers are essentially built into Ubuntu, so no need to hunt around the internet or look in all your old boxes for driver CDs.  However, due to somewhat poorer support from some hardware manufacturers, there are issues with some hardware and peripherals (printers and scanners are notorious).  Try the "Live CD" (aka Destop CD) to see if everything works as it should.

5. Windows programs (and games) do not work on Ubuntu
This is not strictly true, but I would recommend you assume it is, and anything that does not conform to this statement is a bonus.  Ubuntu has its own alternatives to most software you might need, and it is all generally free.

Hope that helps you to decide.  While being annoyed with Windows is not the best reason to use Ubuntu, it is a good reason.  In fact, it was the reason I first used Linux...

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2009-06-27
Further to all those other replies, there is one area that you may find frustrating in Linux (or Mac, for that matter).

That area is the few programs that have been carefully and explicitly designed to not to run in anything other than Windows. I don't understand why programmers would do that, but if you need one of those programs (e.g. for a specific hardware item that comes with a Windows-only program that won't run under Wine), then you'll need to boot into Windows for that. (Having said that, you can usually find a Linux replacement, albeit with reduced functionality.)

By the way, when you use Wine, I recommend that you install [PlayOnLinux]("http://www.playonlinux.com/") and use that instead. It's a kind of manager for Wine, making it dead simple to install, run, and uninstall Windows programs.
