### Post by opodaniel on 2011-02-14
Well is a little late, but here is my experience , in case someone else happens to be in the same situation as you and me. 
I am studying Chinese, so I taught that it would be an good idea to use my Ubuntu in Chinese in order to increase the contact with the language.
While switching to Chinese, some important and usual used folders will have the name changed : 
Desktop, Documents, Music, Download etc... So your files should be in the folders with English name, and if you switch from one to another, you should take care that the files do not sync between the folders.

As for me.. I didn't manage to get back to English until I completely uninstantiated the Chinese language pack. Now have to reinstall and setup pinyin.
