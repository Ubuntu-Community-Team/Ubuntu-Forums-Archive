### Post by markbuntu on 2008-06-30
The first time I used linux was about 1996 or so. The kernel was version 0.94 or something. It was a complete horror show unless you had a lot of free time and money, which I did at the time. But it was also a fascinating learning experience. 

But then, I didn't use linux again for about 10-12 years and this year I figured, I have a lot of free time now, I'll give it another go. I have to tell you the progress has been amazing, it actually mostly works right out of the box. It has a functioning x that won't catch my monitor on fire trying to configure it. 

And, the most fascinating thing for me is the things from my previous experience that my feeble and failing memory actually retained, are still useable. Which was extremely helpful when I first installed Ubuntu and could not log in. (I sort of expected that, something had to go wrong.)

Nevertheless, I am not a big fan of terminal. I used terminals when they were actual terminals connected to a  mainframe with no gui. I use it when I must and that's it. The greatest thing ever invented for computers is the graphical user interface. Besides, thousands of people have spent millions of man-hours building graphical user interfaces to make our life easier. Is is a disservice to them to not use them and Bill Gates did not invent the gui.

Sure, you can spend a year learining all the terminal commands etc, but why bother when there are easier ways to do most everything. So, if you want to learn linux, figure out all these gui apps and keep a few notes around for the increasingly smaller amount of stuff that requires you to use terminal.
