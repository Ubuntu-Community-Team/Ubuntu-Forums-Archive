### Post by Darkade on 2008-06-03
Hello! metacity is the default gnome window manager, i.e. it's the application that allows you to have and manipulate windows in your desktop.
Now to install your theme just download it bellow where it says

download(ATER-metacity-theme)

i've copied the link for you so you can download it from here
[http://illusions.hu/Themes/Metacities/ATER/ATER-metacity-0.7.tar.gz](http://illusions.hu/Themes/Metacities/ATER/ATER-metacity-0.7.tar.gz)

you will download a tarball, wich is a package format like ZIP or RAR download it to your desktop so it will be easier to find

Now go to your menu and in System>preferences>appearance click where it says "install" now just find ATER-metacity-0.7.tar.gz in your desktop, close session and log in again
the go once more to System>preferences>appearance and find your new theme in the list, select it and close

### Post by johnl on 2008-06-03
A Desktop "theme" is a combination of a GTK theme (what the controls look like) a metacity theme (what the window borders look like) and an icon theme (the icons used on buttons and in the menu).

What you should do is click on your current theme in the appearance settings, and choose "customize...".  Then select "window border" and choose the metacity theme you installed.

Hope this helps!
