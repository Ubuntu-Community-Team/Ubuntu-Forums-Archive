### Post by drs305 on 2010-09-18
nnjond,

I don't see a problem with your results. I'd like you to try to boot by editing the menuentry from the Grub2 prompt. 

Highlight your first Ubuntu selection and press "e" to open the entry for editing.


1. Remove the entire "search" line. Use the Up/Dn cursor to place at the start of the line and hold the DEL key.
> [COLOR="Red"]search --no-floppy --fs-uuid --set 45fba1ab-2864-4e2f-97a7-be780f8e355d[/COLOR]

2. On the "linux" line, change the part "root=UUID=<long number>" to "root=/dev/sda1" with X being your Ubuntu drive and Y being your Ubuntu partition. Leave the rest of the line as is.
**From:**
> linux	/boot/vmlinuz-2.6.32-21-generic root=[COLOR="Red"]UUID=45fba1ab-2864-4e2f-97a7-be780f8e355d[/COLOR] ro   quiet splash

**To:**
> linux	/boot/vmlinuz-2.6.32-21-generic root=**/dev/sda1** ro   quiet splash

3. CTRL-x to boot.

If it boots, run "sudo update-grub" and see if it boots without changes the next time

Note: I changed the "QUOTE" tags to "CODE" tags to make your results a bit easier to read. You  generate "CODE" tags with the # icon in the post menubar.


**ADDED:** If this does not fix it, the next recommendation would be to boot to the LiveCD, mount the partition (sudo mount /dev/sda1 /mnt) and then inspect /mnt/etc/fstab to see if the UUID is correct on the / line. You might try changing it from "UUID=...." to "/dev/sda1" to just to make things simpler for analysis.
