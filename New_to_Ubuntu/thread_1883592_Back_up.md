### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-11-19
The best thing is setting up a separate home partition. That way, when you install no-matter-what Linux distro, you will keep your files and settings (though you will still need to reinstall your apps).

A separate /home partition isn't crash-proof though, but it doesn't waste space.

### Post by lolpenguin on 2011-11-20
oneConf and UbuntuOne (Ubuntu SSO account required) backs up your home folder, settings, software sources and list of installed programs to the Ubuntu One cloud, so all you do on a new computer is sign in with your SSO account and your stuff is back[!]

### Post by paul_h on 2011-11-20
I run a dual-boot system and I really don't want to lose it and do the necessary rebuild. (How would I ever remember what programs and utilities I have installed over the past three years.)

So, every time I am about to do a major upgrade I use Clonezilla to make a clone-image of the whole system HDD onto an external HDD. It gives me peace of mind and has saved me on several occasions when things have gone wrong.
