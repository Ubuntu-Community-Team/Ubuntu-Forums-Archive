### Post by Brightbelt on 2008-08-03
Yes Adudutz, Xubuntu does not come with the Open Office Suite, although you can download it in Add/Remove or the Synaptic Package Manager. 

I tend to like the Xfce graphics better than gnome defaults in Ubuntu, plus there's more Xfce-oriented graphics available for download through the Synaptic Package Manager if you're interested.

I may not have all the facts perfectly, but from what I see, the Xubuntu ISO is about 550 MB (approx-a guess from memory there), about 150 MB less than the Ubuntu ISO (699 MB), at most. 

So if you end up installing a few other things you want -  like Open Office etc - I'm not so sure you end up with a "lighter version" of Ubuntu in the end. 

I'm just sharing a perspective, plus I run Xubuntu on a newer HP laptop (1.67 Ghz Core 2 Duo/2 GB of ram) and I don't see a huge difference in performance or speed; of course maybe mine just runs everything fast for all I know.

Also, just so you know and don't think you have a sound problem, one way in which Xubuntu lightens its load is by not having system sounds as part of its OS. 

I found this out the hard way, thinking I had a problem but then I learned that I could record sound and play back just fine.  

Good Luck!
