### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-04-04
You can't change where programs are installed. They spread out over the filesystem (logically) so everything is in the correct place.

### Post by wojox on 2011-04-04
Have you tried bleachbit. I've freed up as much as 1Gb of space with it.

Like 3rdalbum stated you can't change it now but you could plug-in your sticks and set up a partitioning scheme a little different.

### Post by Paqman on 2011-04-04
One of the biggest things gobbling up space is usually old kernels. Get rid of all your old ones, you'll free up over 100MB per kernel. You should be able to keep your system running on the 4GB stick for a while without too much hassle.

If you want to shift anything to a second stick, the easiest thing to shift would be your data, not apps.
