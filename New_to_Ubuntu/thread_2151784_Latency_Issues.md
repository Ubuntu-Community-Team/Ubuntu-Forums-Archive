### Post by ohnonot on 2013-06-07
900MHz?
that is very old.
is that the most your cpu can? one cpu only?
i'm surprised you were ever able to view live broadcasts (flash video, basically?) with that.

i've experienced latency problems while browsing the web when my browser doesn't delete old cookies. make sure your browser deletes them when it closes, or after one hour or whatever the setting for chromium is.

chromium is probably not the best choice for such an old laptop. try sth more lightweight.

also lubuntu itself might be too much. take a look at **[this]("http://distrowatch.com/search.php?ostype=All&category=Old+Computers&origin=All&basedon=All&notbasedon=None&desktop=All&architecture=All&status=Active")** and consider changing distros. puppy linux might be recommendable.

ps: all this microsoft content your viewing on the interwebz, it's all very bandwidth and resource intensive. consider getting your information from somewhere else.
also consider installing adblock plugin for your browser.
also trackers like google analytics take resources on *your* machine. a lot can be disabled with e.g. ghostery addon but some content might not work anymore.
