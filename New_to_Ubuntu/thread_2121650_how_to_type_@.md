### Post by cwsnyder on 2013-03-02
Yes, your keyboard layout matters.  In fact, it is the source of your problem.  According to [http://matadornetwork.com/abroad/the-ultimate-guide-to-computer-keyboards-around-the-world/](http://matadornetwork.com/abroad/the-ultimate-guide-to-computer-keyboards-around-the-world/), you use the right ALT key, marked AltGr, held as a shift key while pressing the 2 key in the row above the A and Z keys.  If you have a keyboard without a number row above the alphabetic keys, which your post seems to indicate, you may also have to press the Shift key first, then the AltGr key, then the 2 to access the @ symbol.  Not something easy for a touch typist.

As far as changing the response of your Caps Lock key, in Xubuntu, the proper place to change the setting is in Settings >> Hardware >> Keyboard >> Layout.  Untick the *Use system default* box, and choose your exact keyboard and variant (click on the right pointing arrow).  If that does not give you the setting you want, you may want to look at the Accessibility options, especially Sticky Keys.
