### Post by bumanie on 2009-01-17
As said above, just ensure your bios is set to boot from cd-rom, allow ubuntu to open up and then find your windows files and copy them. The live cd does not alter your computer in any way. Once open, go to Places --> Computer --> File System and you should see your windows drive. Double click on it and you should be able to open it and see all your files.

### Post by caro on 2009-01-17
I personally recovered all the files from a friend's computer after he tried to change the memory and for some reason the computer wouldn't boot in Windows afterwards.  I popped in the Ubuntu Live CD and found all his files and moved them to an external hard drive.  One of my employees was able to do the same thing with one of his old computers.  

It does depend on what the problem you experienced is.  BTW, if the computer works until it gets hot, you may be able to save data still. I know it sounds crazy, but I have had success with putting a bad hard drive in the freezer for a while to get it really cold and the installing it and booting very quickly.  It stayed cool just long enough to move the data to the network before it crashed again.  Replaced the hard drive and moved the data back.  

Good luck.

### Post by aesis05401 on 2009-01-17
On the freezer note, this can work, but you will not get more than a few minutes out of a seriously deteriorating drive without doing the following:

1. Masking tape over the data and electrical connectors of the drive.

2. Put the drive in a ziplock bag of minute-rice - data/power connectors must be sticking up. This MUST be minute rice  it acts as a poor man's silica gel.  Close the zip-lock.

3. Freeze the bag of rice with the hard drive in it.  You want it completely frozen so try 24 hours.

4. Remove the bag from the freezer.  Plug the drive into a machine while leaving the drive immersed in minute rice.  Have your new drive ready to accept the recovered data.  Keep the drive as immersed in rice as possible.

5. You now have about 20 reliable minutes to copy data, but the drive will operate more slowly than normal until it warms up.  Once it warms up you are almost out of time, so hurry!

Edit: uncooked minute rice I should mention ;)  When freezing/thawing components you have to absorb all extra moisture that is around.
