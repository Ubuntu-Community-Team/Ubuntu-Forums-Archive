### Post by Lateralis on 2011-05-19
I dual boot at home with Ubuntu and Windows 7 on my desktop (gaming anyone?) but my laptop which I use for work is only ever booted into Linux these days.  A couple of weeks ago a program did break in Linux that I couldn't fix particularly easily.  I have the same program on the laptop's Vista partition so booted into that, but by God was that weird!  

Personally, if I were you, I would keep a functional copy of Windows lying around on the hard drive.  I know you have other computers that you can use, but in case something goes wrong with Ubuntu you might be glad to have another bootable OS on the hard disc.  But that's my paranoia for you!  If you can't spare the space on your netbook to have Win 7 and Ubuntu, then nuke Win 7.  By the sounds of it you're pretty comfortable with Ubuntu so even if you did miss something from Windows, you'll be able to find a way around it...
