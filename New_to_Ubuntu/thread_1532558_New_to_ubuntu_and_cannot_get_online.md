### Post by NFblaze on 2010-07-16
It looks like your internal IP address isnt the right type. I dont have a Belkin N router. I have a Linksys with dd-wrt micro installed on it, so I dont know how yours is configured but it looks like your router is configured to broadcast as an adhoc router instead of AP/Access Point.


Also upon further inspection of the wireless config network manager (the one in the top panel that you right click > Edit Connections > Wireless) I see they have a dropdown box for Infrastructure or Adhoc. Try changing that before accessing the router 


See if you can connect to your router's configuration page and make sure it's broadcast settings is for AP. You can usually do this by typing [http://192.168.1.1]("http://192.168.1.1") into your browser. If that doesnt work then try plugging your ethernet cable directly from your computer into port 1 of the router and try connecting to [http://192.168.1.1](http://192.168.1.1).
