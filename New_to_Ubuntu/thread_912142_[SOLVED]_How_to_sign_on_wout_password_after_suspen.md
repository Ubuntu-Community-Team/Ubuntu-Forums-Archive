### Post by lukjad on 2008-09-06
People at first find it a drag to have to enter their root password when they start, but they get used to it soon enough. The trouble with running an Operating System without a password being required is that there is then a lack of security. Anyone can just waltz up to your PC and install, uninstall or reconfigure whatever they want. Not good.

### Post by lukjad on 2008-09-07
*Chuckles*
But you get the idea. Also, the *sudo* command is there to warn you that something you are about to do could mess up your system. So it will stop you from really messing up your system unless you really try. The bonus is that a theoretical virus that would try to do damage could only hurt those files that are not protected but *sudo* permissions.
