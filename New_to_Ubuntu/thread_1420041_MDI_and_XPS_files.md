### Post by *Boots* on 2010-03-02
MDI and XPS files are convienient at the time, but in my endeavor to port over to ubuntu these files were a pain in the neck to get converted. In an effort to make things easier for you I will tell you how I solved this problem. 

MDI: 

In most windows systems there is a program called MSPVIEW.EXE you can search the c: drive to get to it:

Get your pen drive and save all your MDI files to it an go find some computers with windows on them... The computer I tried it on was one with office 2003 on it... Well I opened this program and dragged my mdi files onto it. Once in this program and viewable you can save these files as tiff files(which can be opened by pretty much anything).  If having trouble after trying this go to this url and see if doing this will help: 

[http://forums.techarena.in/ms-office-support/667816-2.htm](http://forums.techarena.in/ms-office-support/667816-2.htm)

XPS:

This may not seem like a problem now, but in two or three years when you decide that the virtually printed document you saved is important and you want to retrieve it, and the xps thing might have been surpassed my something bigger and better from good ol microsoft. Now your out of luck.

 Well this solution requires the computer your working with has a 'to PDF file printer' on it(I used a program called BullzipPDFPrinter 'works great').  Well when you find this computer with IE 7 or higher and open the xps document and print it to a PDF file.  I'm sorry no url for this one..... this is just how I worked out how to solve the problem.
