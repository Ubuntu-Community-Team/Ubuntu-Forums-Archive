### Post by ShadowVegan on 2013-05-30
I sometimes have similar issues, I'm pretty sure it's just because of a loose connection (physically). Is your computer old? If so, maybe the USB ports (or whatever ports you use for your headset) are a bit off-center or loose. Or perhaps it's an issue with your headphones. If this is the issue, with a laptop it would probably be hard to fix, but with a desktop you may be able to open it and realign or replace the USB ports. Be careful though, trying to open a computer and change stuff can harm your computer (and even be dangerous because some parts hold electricity even when unplugged) so don't try it yourself unless you know what you're doing.

No guarantee this is the issue, just a thought since I am having a similar issue and this is the cause in my case.
