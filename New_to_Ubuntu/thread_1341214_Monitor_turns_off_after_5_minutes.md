### Post by lkraemer on 2009-11-30
Grandma, you're going to have to explain to us in detail what
"If I leave my computer idle for 5 or more minutes the monitor shuts off"
really means.

Does the Power go off to the Monitor, or the Power LED go "OFF"?
Does the Display change to BLACK or some other Color with the Power
LED "ON"?
Do you have a Screensaver enabled?

When this happens, if you shine a bright flashlight at the blank display
can you see anything?  If so then the backlight is probably turning off.
If this is the case, try turning Power "OFF" on the Monitor ONLY, then
turn it back "ON".  If that doesn't work, Leave the Power "ON", and try
unplugging the cable from the Computer to the monitor, then plug it in again.
The backlight should now come back "ON" and you will be able to see the display.

If either of the above problems cause the display to be back to "NORMAL"
then there is a problem with your Monitor's backlight circuit.  Most
likely it is a power supply problem with bad capacitors.  (You may have
noticed some degrading of the text prior to this problem, or may have
slightly darker streaks of text noticeable down the monitor, or a wavy
text display.)  There is a forum about problems with certain brands of 
monitors.  (I had this exact problem with an older 17" MAG LT716S 700P.)

[http://www.badcaps.net/forum/forumdisplay.php?f=30](http://www.badcaps.net/forum/forumdisplay.php?f=30)
[http://www.badcaps.net/forum/showthread.php?t=7155](http://www.badcaps.net/forum/showthread.php?t=7155)
[http://www.badcaps.net/forum/showthread.php?t=7130](http://www.badcaps.net/forum/showthread.php?t=7130)

You might want to search for your Model number and read up on what folks
say on the forum.

The easiest thing to do is to substitute a know good monitor where yours
is currently plugged in, and see if the problem is in the removed monitor.

Good luck.
