### Post by Flymo on 2011-11-06
Xubuntu has had its own modest compositor for a while now (much less demanding than Compiz).  

It adds transparency to menus, window decorations and foreground/background windows according to your taste; shadows, too.  

It's not as accomplished (imho) as  the Enlightenment compositor, but it does a good job and it comes built-into XFCE.  

Machines that cannot run Compiz successfully are often happy with this option or the Enlightenment E17 option.  E17 is particularly good at doing the bling without slowing down the system.  We like Bodhi Linux's interpretation - Ubuntu Lucid + E17.

Lubuntu with **xcompmgr** is yet another option that works well on less powerful/older machines.
