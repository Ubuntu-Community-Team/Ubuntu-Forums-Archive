### Post by qyot27 on 2010-08-21
Beaten to it, but oh well.

> **earthpigg said:**
> what?

i installed ubuntu on a computer with 256 mb of ram just two weeks ago. it ran slow, but it ran.
It would appear that the [Ubuntu System Requirements](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements) page on the Wiki (and the [Wikipedia article](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubuntu_%28operating_system%29#System_requirements)) both refer to 512 MB being the minimum, but this is not totally accurate - even the Release Notes for Lucid refute that part*.  The real answer is that it varies - the lighter environments reduce it back to 256 or below, and the server version only needs 128.  GNOME or KDE will also run on 256, but they'll be rather constrained.

*[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LucidLynx/ReleaseNotes](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LucidLynx/ReleaseNotes)
> The minimum memory requirement for Ubuntu 10.04 LTS is 256 MB of memory.



[quote=Behemoth0089]So, what should I do?[/quote]
Burn yourself a LiveCD of 10.04 and test it out; CD-Rs - or even DVD-Rs, for that matter - are cheap (or you could download the .iso, mount it in Daemon Tools or Virtual CloneDrive, and try a Wubi install).  That's the easiest way to find out if your hardware is supported.  The LiveCD won't hurt your computer as long as you don't tell it to Install (the 10.04 LiveCD launcher is very clear on which one is the Install option and which one will keep you in LiveCD mode).
