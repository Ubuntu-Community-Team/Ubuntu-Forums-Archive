### Post by lkraemer on 2010-01-20
oingk,
Synapsys has a good site for you to use and study.

The very first thing you should always do is create a good set of the
Recovery CDR's, so you can re-create the original state of your computer
as it shipped to you.  So, I recommend that you do that first thing.....
keeping in mind that this won't harm your current data, but will just
make sure you can have a copy of CDR's to re-build your computer.
You need these CDR's so you have all the drivers available when and
if your Hard Drive becomes defective.  So do it for safety.
THIS WILL NOT SAVE YOUR DATA FILES OR INSTALLED SOFTWARE.

Next I'd find some way to backup your data or Hard Drive so you don't
lose all your important information. Maybe Clonezilla or xxclone

Now, from this point you have several options.  You can:
1.  Purchase a new hard drive, install it where your existing one is now,
    and use software like Clonezilla to copy the existing drive to the
    new drive.  Then work on the new larger drive which will give you a cheap
    and safe way to keep from losing any data until it's rebuilt.
2.  Copy the Data files and downloaded software to a CDRW or DVDRW until
    you get Windows booting properly.  Then re-install all your software
    and copy your data files back to where they are needed.
3.  Get someone to assist who has done this before.

Then, you can try a repair install, that is supposed to NOT DESTROY your
current data files and software that is installed.  OR you can do a regular
install, which will wipe out your data files and software and
set your system up as it was originally.  (This is the same as using the
RECOVERY CDR's to go back to a factory shipped state.)

Use Google to search for repair install or rebuild install.  I think I have
some sites bookmarked, but will have to search a bit.  Will post them a bit later.

Basically, make a good plan, so you don't lose your data or software, and
end up with a good backup until it's running fine, then use Clonezilla to
copy the running system for a good hot backup that can be plugged in and
powered up.  Just don't be in a hurry and blow your data away while
trying things.

LATER:........

[http://michaelstevenstech.com/XPrepairinstall.htm](http://michaelstevenstech.com/XPrepairinstall.htm)
This site has lots of good information, so read careful and look at the
other information on the left menu.

HOW TO CREATE A WINDOWS SETUP CD FROM A PRELOADED SYSTEM:
[http://www.howtohaven.com/system/createwindowssetupdisk.shtml](http://www.howtohaven.com/system/createwindowssetupdisk.shtml)

This should keep you from blowing your data files.

Don't forget your Favorites, Any emails in Outlook, or Outlook Express,
and contacts that are saved on your computer along with any special drivers,
or downloaded files that were installed.  If you purchased software you most
likely have those Original CDR's for installing.
