### Post by pizza-is-good on 2009-11-28
I'm not an advanced user, but I can solve most of my own problems.

When I started using Ubuntu (my first linux distro), I didn't discover UbuntuForums for a long while, when I did, everybody was very helpful and I learned a lot from having problems, asking for help, and fixing them.

Today I occasionally start threads for help, when I can't fix something myself, and I keep learning from that.

Just a suggestion for you(and all 'newbies'):

When you start a thread for help, say everything you know about the problem, and say everything you know about ubuntu(or linux) that you think should help you, but that for some reason, the problem is out of your reach. That will get you more help, and keep away those that know so much that they are bothered by simple questions.
