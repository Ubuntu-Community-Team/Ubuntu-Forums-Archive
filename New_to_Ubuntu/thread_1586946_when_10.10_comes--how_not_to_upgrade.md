### Post by aeronutt on 2010-10-02
To insure you don't upgrade by 'accident', you can:
Administration->upgrade manager
Select 'settings'.
Then under 'release upgrade', select either long term only, or never. You'll still get all the updates for your current version.

### Post by decoherence on 2010-10-02
> **hollowtd said:**
> When is the best time to upgrade from 04 to 10.10? A wait time of like a few months for instance?

A wait time of a few months after release is reasonable if you want to play it safe. Remember, it's a six month release cycle. Personally, I have one 'stable' computer that I upgrade three months after a release and an 'unstable' computer that I upgrade three months before release (which is usually when developmental builds get interesting.)

Unless something goes terribly wrong in the release process, most problems will be specific to certain types of (hopefully uncommon) hardware. Just check to see if people with similar hardware are having problems. When they start getting them solved, it's a good time to upgrade. Of course, you can (and should) always test things yourself with a 10.10 live CD before upgrading your 10.04 system.

### Post by k3lt01 on 2010-10-05
This thread has gone full circle away from the original question.

There is no real incorrect way to upgrade-update. You do what your hardware will cope with. Up until 10.04 my laptop would clean install off an AlternateCD. 10.04 come along and it wouldn't do it so I had to upgrade via update manager. I still have 10.04 just not a perfectly clean install of it.

A couple of points have been made that are not quite accurate. 10.10 is NOT the next version of 10.04. The next version of 10.04 is 10.04.1. Soon w will have 10.04.2.

If you stay with 10.04 you will NOT get all the features of 10.10 but 10.04 will be supported for much longer so "should" in theory be more stable.

10.04 will NOT automatically obtain 10.10s features, 10.04 will stay with Gnome 2.30 while 10.10 already has Gnome 2.32 and its associated upgrades. You could backport some features if you want but you may as well go to 10.10if your going to do that and at least keep the security features that 10.10 gets.

10.04 has the 2.6.32 Kernel and will stay with it. 10.10 has the 2.6.35 (or 36) Kernel.

Basically it is all personal choice, just don't be swayed by misinformation.

EDIT: I have also moved to each new version within hours of release except for Lucid and Maverick which I tested and am testing. The only release I had difficulty with was Gutsy 7.10.
