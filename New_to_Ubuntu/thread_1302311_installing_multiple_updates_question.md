### Post by swoody on 2009-10-27
Hello clhuene, and welcome to the forum ):P

I would agree with shadow120 here. Update Manager is perfectly capable of handling all your updates. It will download them, and then install all the new packages itself. You don't need to do anything besides telling it to start the process, and then to restart your computer if you get a notice telling you to do so. Typically you're going to get a load of updates when you first bootup a fresh install of Ubuntu. Then updates will come much more spread out than this. So don't worry too much, this is typically a one-time deal to have a large number of updates at once :)

### Post by egalvan on 2009-10-27
Taking 24hours to download the updates sounds slow.

When you are done with that set of updates, you can check for a faster server.

got to 

System -> Administration -> Software Sources

click on the tab named...

Download from:

and choose...

Other...

another window will open up, called "Choose a Download Server"...

Choose your country, then click on the right-side tab called...

Select Best Server


This will perform a series of tests, and you will most likely end up with a faster download.
