### Post by Hendrixski on 2008-07-12
Oh man.  I gotta share this.  I just started a new job, and the sound doesn't work on the laptop that they gave me from work.  I spent some time with the IT support staff and they couldn't get it working... nor the wireless either. So we agreed, I'm upgrading the laptop to Linux.

Guess what, the sound works now and the laptop runs faster with Ubuntu than it did with Windows XP. :-)


As for you Xubuntu sound problems... well, there's probably a sound mixer in XFCE in the menus somewhere.  On Gnome it's on the top right hand corner.  That will show you all of your levels.  There *might* be a problem sometimes with the sound-drive so just google the brand of computer/laptop that you have and the word Ubuntu.  So for example "HP 1234X Ubuntu" and there will be a few sites talking about how well that model is supported on linux.

Hope that helps
