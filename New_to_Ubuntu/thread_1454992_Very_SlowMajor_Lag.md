### Post by snowpine on 2010-04-15
There are some good tips for Ubuntu on the Dell Mini 10v on this site: [http://www.ubuntumini.com/](http://www.ubuntumini.com/)

(make sure you actually have a 10v and not a 10, because they are *very* different creatures!)

I personally have had great luck with various Linux distros (including Ubuntu) on my Dell Mini 9 (same "guts" as your 10v). Are you sure your expectations for a netbook are realistic? Netbooks are slow, low-power computers for checking your email, IM'ing, etc. They are not good choices for multimedia, number-crunching, manipulating large documents, etc.--for that, you'd be better off with a small laptop than a netbook.

By the way, the 1.6ghz Atom processor is roughly equal in power to a fast Pentium 3 or slow Pentium 4. Clock speed is not everything, so don't compare the Atom with a 1.6ghz Core Duo or anything like that. :)

Also if your Dell has an SSD drive instead of a HDD, that could explain why it is so slow. A lot of users have good results upgrading to faster aftermarket drives.
