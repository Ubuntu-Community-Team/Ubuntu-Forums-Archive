### Post by snowpine on 2009-05-19
Back up your important files and do a fresh install. There is no other way. Each release of Ubuntu is supported for 18 months (except for long term support releases like 8.04) and it is important to upgrade before official support ends.

### Post by jpoRS on 2009-05-19
The short and easy answer is that you can't.  Fiesty stopped being supported about six months ago, and even Gutsy is no longer supported.  That means that you can't just leapfrog your way up to date.  There are some sources where you can get older versions of Ubuntu, but I will leave that to someone else to explain because I am not very experienced in that.

That said, the easiest and most effective thing to do is probably a fresh install of 9.04. If done properly it won't effect your Windows side, and it will be much easier than tracking down old releases.

Also, in the upper right corner of this page there should be a search function.  People ask this question pretty regularly and generally get the same answers every time.  Save yourself the time of waiting for new versions of the same answer by searching next time.

### Post by Didius Falco on 2009-05-19
You could always split your Home partition off to a separate one - that's save your data and configuration files as long as you set the Home partition, but did not set it to format it, using the Manual option in the partitioner during the install.

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome)

Still, jumping that many versions, I'd recommend just saving your data and doing a fresh install. Jumping four versions is going to be a big jump, and your old configuration files may cause more problems than they solve.

Good luck!

Regards,

Didius
