### Post by nosehat on 2011-05-27
There's other files as well, that some people might consider "temp" files.

For example, you will notice that you have a .thumbnails directory in your home directory.  Inside this is a thumbnail for every image that Gnome has ever shown in any directory.  I'd consider these to be a type of "temp" file, and you can sometimes clear up a lot of space by deleting these!

I'd love to see a list of other such files, like log files that just pile up and aren't really needed, etc.  Such a list would be useful for people who want to clear up disk space, or for people concerned about respecting user privacy.

### Post by nosehat on 2011-05-28
The .thumbnail directory is what Gnome uses to display thumbnails of your picture files when you are browsing a directory in "icon" view.  It's useful to keep these on your computer for files/directories that you typically browse in this mode--they will just be recreated the next time you visit that directory anyway if you erase them.  But there's no need to keep them around for pictures you've deleted or archived, or for media that you plugged into your computer once (like browsing someone else's SD card while they show you their vacation pictures, etc).

Since the thumbnail files are made by Gnome for Gnome to use, I would be surprised if a different program used them too, but in theory they could.
