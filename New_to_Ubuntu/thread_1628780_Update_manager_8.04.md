### Post by col48 on 2010-11-23
Synaptic tells me that I did updates on 12 Nov and 20 Nov.  I normally let updates happen as soon as the system tells me about them, but for once the last one was a day later.

What it is telling you is that the 'package information' has been updated within the last hour, not that your system has been updated in that time.  My understanding is that there are three things which would ideally be always synchronised.

1. Your system.  This is updated when you request that it is, so you would be aware of it happening.  Mine is requested manually and usually takes a few minutes - this will depend on your internet speed.

2. Your system's knowledge of what updates are available.  This is the 'package information' referred to and is updated when you request a check.

3. What updates are actually available in the repositories on the net.

I am not sure how the periodic security updates fit into this pattern, but updates of this type will incidentally cause your system's 'package information' to be updated as well.
