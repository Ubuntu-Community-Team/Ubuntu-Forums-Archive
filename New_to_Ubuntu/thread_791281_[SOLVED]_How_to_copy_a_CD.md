### Post by HotShotDJ on 2008-05-12
> **tamoneya said:**
>  I feel that it is outside the rules of the forum to copy this disc.  It seems to me like that would be copyright infringment.It is perfectly legal to make a backup copy for yourself of ANY CD, data, music, or otherwise.  In the case of music, media-shifting is permissible under fair use clause of the copyright act. (At least this is true in the United States, even if the RIAA would like you to believe otherwise)

Install k3b along with ubuntu-restricted-extras.  This is by far the best tool for the job.  It will be added to Applications --> Sound & Video --> K3B automatically when you install it.

Open K3B. Insert the media you wish to copy. Click "Copy CD" in the bottom panel of the K3B application.  Look to see that everything looks sane.  The defaults should be just fine.  When you are ready, click on Start.  You will be prompted for a blank CD at the appropriate time.

Good Luck.
