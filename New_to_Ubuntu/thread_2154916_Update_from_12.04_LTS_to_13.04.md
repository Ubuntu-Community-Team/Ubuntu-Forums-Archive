### Post by Frogs Hair on 2013-06-16
You would have to perform two upgrades , first to 12.10, which increases the risk of problems. If you have a choice back up your data and do a clean installation. Your current release has support for 5 years and 13.04 does not , so make sure you want to proceed . In any case, back up your data prior to any upgrade and be aware that upgrades take a very long time.

### Post by Isamu715 on 2013-06-16
You could either upgrade to 12.10 and then to 13.04 or if you have a separate /home partition you can make a fresh install of 13.04 while preserving your data. But it's still recommended to do a backup.

### Post by arpanaut on 2013-06-16
Not Directly, you would need to upgrade to 12.10 then to 13.04, but you would be losing 5 year support.
13.04 is only supported for 9 mo.

You could back up your data, then reinstall 13.04, but then you would need to reinstall all the programs you have added.
Your call... Unless you enjoy upgrading and reinstalling a lot, stick with what you got (5 yrs. support) and upgrade at the next LTS (14.04)
