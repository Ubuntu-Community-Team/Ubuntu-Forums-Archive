### Post by dvlchd3 on 2010-02-24
First, ensure the computer is turned off, and unplugged from any power source.  Then, just make sure you're not standing on carpet, and you touch some part of the case so you don't discharge on the circuitry.  Other than that, just unscrew, and pull straight upwards.

If you want to get real crazy, it is ok to take the fan off the graphics card.  It is usually attached by 4 screw on all four corners.

But I cannot stress enough how important it is to ensure you do NOT release any static discharge onto the card or any other circuitry in your computer.  The metal parts of the case are ok, but most everywhere else, DON'T touch unless, 1) you absolutely have to, and 2) you have touched the metal on the case first.

### Post by Bartender on 2010-02-24
> **dvlchd3 said:**
> First, ensure the computer is turned off, and unplugged from any power source.  Then, just make sure you're not standing on carpet, and you touch some part of the case so you don't discharge on the circuitry.  Other than that, just unscrew, and pull straight upwards.

I would add that you need to take a close look for the plastic lock tab that projects from the motherboard slot and grabs the little notch on the card.  AFAIK the tab is present on all new PC's.  I think it started showing up with PCIe-X slots.

If you "pull straight upwards" on the card without pressing the tab sideways you'll probably break it off and that would be embarrassing.
