### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-03-04
Can I add something that you might not be aware of?

The built in power supplies of many desktop PCs have there own little on/off switch. It sits just by the vents at the back for the Power Supply Unit (PSU).

When we switch off at the front of the case power is still going to the motherboard. So, this little switch is put there so that when we open the case we do not need to pull out the power lead to make sure that there is no power going to the motherboard.

You might want to try toggling that PSU on/off switch.

I suggest this because I recently tried out a live CD version of an operating system called ReactOS. As the CD began to load it suddenly switched off the machine and pressing the on/off switch did not switch the machine back on. But by switching the PSU on/off switch I got power back to the case on/off switch.

By the way, next time look for the cog icon at the right of the top panel. click that and you get a menu that includes shutdown.

Regards.
