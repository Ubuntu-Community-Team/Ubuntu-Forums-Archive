### Post by gdea73 on 2011-02-05
Maybe not exactly what you're looking for, but my iPod Nano (first generation) works great with Rhythmbox. If you only want it for music, it works great. And if you install Rockbox (hacked firmware/OS) on it you can actually do a lot more, if you want.

The only problem for music-lovers is the first-generation Nano only has 1 GB of space to store music on it.

[Supposedly]("http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-hardware-18/getting-rhythmbox-working-with-a-3rd-generation-ipod-nano-608459/") 3rd-gen iPod Nanos can work great with Rhythmbox and those have greater storage space. In fact, most of the iPod Nanos or iPod Classics should work.

Basically any iPod (not the Touch) that you can have "Disk Mode" enabled on will work fine with Rhythmbox. Think Nano, Shuffle, Classic, Mini... those would be your best options.

### Post by colin.p on 2011-02-05
I have an Archos Vision 3 that didn't show up in Rhythmbox, but I could add music through Nautilus. However, I found this interesting article about getting a Sansa Fuse to work and it also works with my Archos.
Everything shows up in Rhythmbox and I can add/delete music from within Rhythmbox as well. The only issue is that when I add a song, it puts the song into a separate "album name" folder, so I just move it into the root "music"folder. I found out later, when I re-read the instructions, that I could change the "folder depth" to put it into the "music" folder and not create a separate folder after all. Doh!!
The link is: [http://wiki.birth-online.de/know-how/hardware/sandisk-sansa-fuze/rhythmbox](http://wiki.birth-online.de/know-how/hardware/sandisk-sansa-fuze/rhythmbox)
