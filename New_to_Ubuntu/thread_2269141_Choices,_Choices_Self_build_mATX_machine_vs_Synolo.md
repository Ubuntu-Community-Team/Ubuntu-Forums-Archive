Side note about RAID5. If your looking to just do a RAID rebuild and continue using your system when a drive fails (data redundancy), don't do it. With 1TB drives, RAID5 is not that reliable and are slow to rebuild. Use either RAID10 or RAID6.

### Post by oldfred on 2015-03-15
I think a Z97 motherboard should just work. Some require a boot parameter or UEFI setting change.

I just built a new Desktop with an Asus AR Z97 board. While I had lots of UEFI settings to change in UEFI/BIOS to get it to boot in UEFI mode, it otherwise just worked. Two drives, SSD boot & main hard drive for data. 

Some vendors has ASmedia ports for drives. And those do not work with Linux yet as far as I know. I have seen many posts with users and Asrock z87 and z97 boards and they only worked without using Asmedia port even for a DVD. Not sure what vendors use that chip for extra SATA ports.

### Post by 2hot6ft2 on 2015-03-22
The advise already given is all good.

I decided to build a NAS Server last year and there are soooo many options to consider. I looked at them all like you're doing and this is what I finally decided on, what I've changed since then and what I'm playing with now.

First I decided against a pre-built system. FreeNAS and Synology sounded great at first but the more I read the more I decided against them and the same went for the other pre-installed solutions so I decided to go with Ubuntu Server 14.04LTS. A decision i am still happy with.

I started with mdadm RAID which I regretted and found undoing it a pain especially with limited disks at the time to copy everything to in case I screwed up along the way. Switched to mhddfs and SnapRAID which I am extremely happy with at this point.

For hardware buying the components and putting your box together is the way to go (at least it is for me). This is what I went with, some is definitely overkill:

from newegg
Fractal Design Node 304 FD-CA-NODE-304-BL Black Aluminum / Steel Mini-ITX Tower Computer Case
    Model #:Node 304 Black
	$79.99 (some of the screws are installed TOO TIGHT, other than that it's nice)
	
ASRock FM2A85X-ITX FM2 AMD A85X (Hudson D4) SATA 6Gb/s USB 3.0 HDMI Mini ITX AMD Motherboard with UEFI BIOS
    Model #:FM2A85X-ITX    **(has 7 SATA Ports)**
        DISCONTINUED MODEL

Antec EarthWatts EA-650 GREEN 650W ATX12V v2.3 SLI Ready CrossFire Certified 80 PLUS BRONZE Certified Active PFC Power Supply - Intel Haswell Fully Compatible 	
    Model #:EA-650 Green
	$74.99
	
AMD A8-5600K Trinity Quad-Core 3.6GHz (3.9GHz Turbo) Socket FM2 100W AD560KWOHJBOX Desktop APU (CPU + GPU) with DirectX 11 Graphic AMD Radeon HD 7560D 	
    Model #:AD560KWOHJBOX
    Out Of Stock

Crucial Ballistix Tactical 8GB 240-Pin DDR3 SDRAM DDR3 1600 (PC3 12800) Low Profile Desktop Memory Model BLT8G3D1608ET3LX0 	
    Model #:BLT8G3D1608ET3LX0
	$64.99
	
Western Digital Red NAS Hard Drive WD30EFRX 3TB IntelliPower 64MB Cache SATA 6.0Gb/s 3.5" NAS Hard Drive 	
    Model #:WD30EFRX
       Most I have paid is $105 incl. shipping each

For the operating system I use a Kingston 60GB SSD that I already had but would just go with a 20GB drive if I was doing it over. I may swap it out for a 20GB SSD at some time. Anything over that is just wasted space.

I have had no problems with the WD Red NAS Drives, but I put a WD Green in and it died within days with a total of less than 280 hours of service on it. It came out of a "WD My Book". I have thrown everything I could at it and other than learning it had "279 hours" of total uptime it's completely dead, a real brick.

A few years back I got a Linksys E2000 Router and flashed it with "Toastmans tomatousb firmware". Started looking at bottlenecks on the LAN only to discover that the tomato firmware took my 10/100/1000 router and limited it to 10/100. It got bricked trying to switch back to stock firmware. Another one is on the way. Lesson learned.

Could easily get by with a smaller PSU, less RAM, and a CPU without a built in GPU. But, I don't have to add a graphics card if I need to sit at the server and do something as opposed to SSHing into it. I don't have a swap partition since I have plenty of RAM for a server, and the PSU was so cheap and has all the connectors I could want (too bad it's not modular) that would have made it perfect.

So this build will let me run the OS from a SSD + 6 data drives (thanks to the 7 SATA ports on the MB).

I started over when I broke the mdadm mirrored RAID I lost the OS so I just started all over from scratch. Lesson learned no mdadm for me thanks.

Check the support for the network adapter on the motherboard you want. Mine has the Realtek RTL8111E which some people have problems with. I haven't had any problems, but I did download the linux driver for it before hand so I could install it right off the bat.

Keep a list of what you do along the way in setting things up. In terminal "history" and "history | grep ???" you fill in the ???'s are your friends.

Hope that helps some. Have fun.:guitar:
