### Post by germanix on 2008-10-19
You have to be more specific with your questioning for anyone to give you a specific answer. What is it exactly that you want to know as far as the installation procedure is concerned?
The "Swap" is a small partition on your HDD, normaly at least the size of your Ram but preferably bigger. When you put your computer into hibernation you would normally lose all of the data on your ram, as the ram cannot hold the data when it has no power, as would be the case when the computer hibernates. To solve this problem the computer transfers the data from the ram to the swap partition and keeps it there for as long as the ram has no power. When you then reboot the computer or wake it up out of hibernation the data is again tranfered to ram. When you install Ubuntu it normally automatically creates a swap partition for you by default.
