### Post by cariboo on 2008-09-12
I'm not sure what you mean by not being able to get rid of the f-spot symbol, if you mean getting rid of the icon in the graphics menu, just right click Applications, then click Edit Menus. Next click Graphics and remove the check mark from beside F-spot. Be aware that fspot is a core part of gnome, so you will probably still get updates for it.

You have to post the error message you are getting once you get back to your laptop. In the future if something like this happens, I would suggest always have a notebook with you to write down what you have done, this has saved my butt many times to resolve a problem.
