### Post by stevoo on 2009-01-14
Ubuntu (IPA: [u&#720;&#712;bu&#720;ntu&#720;] in English,[2] [ù&#595;úntú] in Zulu) is a computer operating system based on Debian GNU/Linux, a popular Linux distribution. Its name comes from the Zulu word "ubuntu", translated as humanity, describing the ubuntu philosophy: "I am who I am because of those around me," a positive aspect of community. Ubuntu's goals include providing an up-to-date, stable operating system for the average user, with a strong focus on usability and ease of installation. Ubuntu has been rated as the most popular Linux distribution for the desktop, claiming approximately 30% of desktop Linux installations in 2007.[3] Ubuntu is also used on some high profile servers, including those belonging to the Wikimedia Foundation.[4]
