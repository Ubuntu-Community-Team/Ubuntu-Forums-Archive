### Post by drs305 on 2011-03-18
You can remove older kernels in several ways. 

You can hide them from the menu but keep them on your system (where they continue to take up space).

You can physically remove them from your system.

Most experienced users keep at least one older kernel they know works so they can revert should a newer kernel causes them problems.

This guide I wrote a while back details how to remove older kernels:
[_http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1587462_]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1587462")

I highly recommend Ubuntu Tweak, which is included in the guide's instructions. 

If you want to just hide them from the menu, use Grub Customizer (see link in my signature line).
