### Post by Chronon on 2009-06-02
In fact it is not exactly true.  Nobody has, to my knowledge, gotten 3rd party code running on a Zune.  All of the first through 5th generation (Video) iPods (not the Classic!), as well as the 1st generation Nano, have been hacked and are able to run, for example, Rockbox or iPodLinux. The more recent models of iPods have been locked down and nobody has gotten custom code running on them either (except for the various models of the iPhone and its cousin the iPod Touch).

If you are after both space and hackability I would select for the latter and just buy an aftermarket HD of whatever size suits your needs.  Just about any player will accept a larger HD, provided it's the right dimensions, connector, etc.  You should check into whether the native firmware supports LBA48 if you want to use an HD larger than about 137GB.  

I would get a Rockbox capable HD-based player.  It will certainly operate as a simple USB Mass Storage device.  Plus, you get the benefits of Rockbox, including the ability to support CF cards or large drives requiring LBA48 support (not to mention all of the audio codecs, plugins, etc.).  If you care about such things, Rockbox is GPL software.

I don't know anything more about the (various) Zune(s) and whether or not it allows you to use it as a UMS device.  I think it may use MTP only, which would kill your chances of doing what you want.
