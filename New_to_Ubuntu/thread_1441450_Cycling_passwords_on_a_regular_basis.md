### Post by johnbraun on 2010-03-28
I'm looking for a way to rotate passwords on a Ubuntu box on a regular basis (say, every 48 hours).  I haven't found anything even remotely close while searching the web, so let me describe my needs/desires and what I hope the end result would be.

The PC I'm working on is a shared computer in a common room of the hostel I work at.  Right now, users are asked to pay $2 to use the computer for unlimited use in the next 24 hours.  Once they've paid, they're given the password and can use the computer.  

Unfortunately, right now, people who paid for the password a year ago can still log in, as the password never changes.

Here's what I envision as a solution: I generate a list of, say, 365 random passwords, each one "paired" with a date.  I print out one copy of the list for the front desk staff.  Thus, when someone asks for the password, the front desk staff looks up the current date on their list, and gives that password to the user.  At an arbitrary time, say, midnight, the password will change to the next day's password.

I know this isn't the most secure, robust solution.  Someone could steal/copy the list, but honestly, if a guest goes that far, they can use the computer.

If anyone has any ideas/solutions/suggestions, I'd love to hear it.  Maybe I'm thinking along the wrong lines, so another suggestion is also welcome.  My only request would be that any proposed solution does not require a second computer, as this is the only computer in the building.

As for specifics, I'm currently running Xubuntu 9.10.  If your solution requires a different desktop enviroment, I'd be amenable to switching to KDE or Gnome.

Thanks in advance, I know this is a strange question.

John

### Post by Didius Falco on 2010-03-28
This is a completely non-software approach to solving your problem.

I spent 25 years in the hotel field, most of it in the rooms side.

My solution would be to raise daily rates by "X" (with the actual amount determined by your hostel, taking into account how many of your guests actually use this service and any other relevant factors) and just announce that it is available to any registered guest.

Now you don't have to deal with all the password hassles, your guests get access to a pc without having to try and remember a password, etc.

If you still want to press on with a password-based solution, I'll take a look around for you, but I think you'd save yourself, your staff and your guests a lot of hassle with the above approach. 

Regards,

Didius
