### Post by xedi on 2012-04-29
It should be automatically updated centrally. Occasionally the update manager will pop up and ask you whether you want to update parts of Ubuntu and software you installed (including LibreOffice).

If you want to start the update manager yourself, just start typing update into the dash. To be sure that really every update is found you can also click on check in the update manager.

In general default software and all software you get through the software-center should be automatically updated through the update manager. Also, depending on how they are configured, software installed through .deb packages you download from websites (such as google chrome) will also update this way.

The central update system and more generally package system in Ubuntu is one of my favorite features in Ubuntu and many other Linux operating systems.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-04-29
It is intended that Update Manager gives us stable updates not only to Ubuntu but to some of the default programs like Libreoffice.

You may hear of newer versions of Libreoffice being available and you may wonder why these are not being made available through the Update Manager. Things do not work that way. We do not get every little revision or improvement that the developers of these programs make.

The change to Libreoffice 3.5 was made during the development period of 12.04. At first it was a beta version that we got and it looked like a Windows program. Then after a few weeks it became a stable version and then it had the Ubuntu look.

You may find that even major revisions of something like Libreoffice come through in the next version of Ubuntu.
