### Post by coffeecat on 2010-10-23
> **getyourkarthick said:**
> By mistake i have erased my recovery partition in my acer laptop.How to make it again..?

Did you make a set of recovery DVDs when you first acquired the machine? I believe the only way of restoring the recovery partition is with the recovery DVDs. If you don't have those, you will have to contact Acer and see if they will sell you a set.

**Edit:** I've just looked in the manual for my Acer. It seems if you have made the recovery DVDs, you have only three options:

1 Restore machine to factory default state.
2 Restore Operating System (they mean Windows) and retain user data.
3 Reinstall drivers or applications.

Which mean that if you have the DVDs and if you select option 1, apart from re-creating the recovery partition, it will restore the Windows C: partition to the factory default and also (I guess) take out your Linux partitions.

Contact acer and just buy the OEM replacement discs. This will provide the same recovery as the original recovery, and auto activate. I was able to get the XP set for 20$ from acer about 5 months ago. They also have a free XP ISO on the web.

If it was me I would try to get a straight install dvd from them that you your stock key will activate, with out the extra acer-ware.

The DVD will allow you to do custom installs, say if you have other OS on the HD and want to just slip W7 back on. The OEM set will overwrite the whole HD.
