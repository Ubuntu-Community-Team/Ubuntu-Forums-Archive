### Post by mb-editor on 2008-07-13
Hi,

I've been using OpenOffice 2.4 word processor for sometime now, after years and years of MS Word, but haven't been able to resolve a couple of issues.

One of them is this: MS Word offers a header/footer option where you can select 'different first page'.  That's important to us in our organisation where we don't put a page number on the first page.  How do I replicate it in a .odt document?  I tried creating different sections, or selecting a different 'Next Style' in the 'Organizer' tab under 'Format --> Page' but am not really getting the desired results.

Any ideas?  Thanks :)

### Post by nvteighen on 2008-07-13
This is maybe one of the most annoying features in OpenOffice: everything is a format template.

1. Select the first page's footer.
2. Open "Styles and Formatting..." (toolbar or just F11-key)
3. The 4th button (whose name I don't know), be sure you're showing "All" styles in the drop-down menu.
4. There should be a style called "First page". Double-click to apply.
5. Your page number should have disappeared (in that page).
