### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-03-19
> **COMPUTIAC said:**
>   Would I be better off replacing the MOBO or trying to replacing the caps.


Replacing electrolytic capacitors is one of the easiest and cheapest fixes it's possible to still do on a motherboard.  Ideally you need a desoldering tool (like a soldering iron with a hollow tip on one end and vacuum pump on the other) and some experience working on multi layer boards.

The most common error is to apply too much heat for too long, and damage the pads.

The most common cause of failure of electrolytic capacitors is heat, so the ones crowded together with other components or jammed up against heatsinks are the usual culprits.

> 
  It has a 240w PS now ,  should I increase this while I am in the house ?


Only if you're going to add a lot of power hungry components, like hard drives.  Do test the voltages with the system running however - and if it's not in spec replace it.  If you wait for it to fail it can take out hard drives and MB when it dies.

Chris.
