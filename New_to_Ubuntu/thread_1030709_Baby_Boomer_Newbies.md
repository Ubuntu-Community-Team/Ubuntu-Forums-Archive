Hello to anyone who is new to Ubuntu/Linux, raised on Windows and starting to get a bit long in the tooth :).  Just to let you know, you're not alone.

I plan on translating tech-to-English to the best of my ability on this thread as I go along.  It'd be great if others will too.  In the meantime, take a deep breath, relax, and maybe go putter around in the garden or something if you get too frustrated.  Remember...when we were little, we got by on just three commercial TV networks and newspapers ;).

### Post by HotShotDJ on 2009-01-04
I suppose I somewhat fit into this category, with the exception that I was introduced to computers in High School using a Mini-Mainframe running some sort of Unix (Probably Bell Labs) with actual TTY terminals (which is why the /dev/tty* devices in Linux never confused me, I know the history behind it). Later, in college and after (the 1980's) I used all different kinds of machines, using different operating systems -- Unix (via DEC VT-100 terminals), Apple, CP/M, MS-DOS, Commodore 64, etc.  The idea that different computers used different operating systems and that skills learned on one were not necessarily fungible to another, has never been a difficult concept to me.  Those who were only introduced to computers after DOS/Windows took over the industry did not develop that cognitive flexibility when it comes to all things computer.

Since I understood the idea of choices in software, I never drank the MS kool-aid.  During most of the 90's, I used OS/2 on IBM-Compatible machines.  I didn't see the point of Windows 95 -- OS/2 was far superior and had all the most commonly used software at the time (Lotus Suite, Borland Data Base, Netscape, etc).  Only later, about 1999 did I first really use Windows (Windows 98) and it was quite disappointing.  Then Windows ME REALLY soured me on Microsoft products.  A couple years later, Windows XP was just the last straw for me.  I switched to Linux  in 2002 (Mandrake, Red Hat, SuSE, Gentoo, and now Ubuntu) and have never looked back.

I guess I have been fortunate... I only really used Windows for about 2 or 3 years out of the 30 or so years that I've been using computers.  Not all of us Baby-Boomers were brain-washed by Microsoft!
