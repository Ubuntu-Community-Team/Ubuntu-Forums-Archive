### Post by rsiddharth on 2009-08-21
Hi dzon65 and welcome to ubuntu forums ! ,

Looks like you have installed ubuntu **inside  **Windows ( wubi install ) . I will give you a simple and logical solution to your problem , try and see whether it works . 

First check this link out : [http://www.microsoft.com/enable/images/products/kbqwerty.gif](http://www.microsoft.com/enable/images/products/kbqwerty.gif) 

Take a print out or note down the key ordering of a **qwerty  **keyboard .  

Boot your system and click into ubuntu , once prompted for your user-name and password , do this ,

for instance say your user-name is **dzon **and your password is **Pj^bqD   **. 
     
  since , you say your keyboard is being recognized as **qwerty **and what you actually have is an **azerty  **, you must type ' a' for ' q'  , 'z' for 'w' , 'e' for 'e' , ' q ' for 'a' and so on ,  understand the logic ! . 

Now the user name that you got to type will be  **dwon **and the corresponding password will be  **Pj**shift+**6baD  . **


If the above proves successful to you , then as mathay says its just a matter of keyboard layout .
