### Post by Rob Sayer on 2013-10-27
I believe the OP installed lubuntu because unity based ubuntu was crashing/freezing.

What hardware specs are we talking about here?  I had ubuntu/unity on a 4Gb i3 laptop and yanked it.  Too slow.  And buggy.

LXDE is the one ubuntu desktop I haven't tried, but from what I've seen I wouldn't use it unless I had less than a half gig of RAM.  Lighter weight desktop means less ram/cpu usage but also less built in functionality.  Past a certain point that slows *me* down to where as a whole it doesn't matter that the computer is running a bit faster.

However, you're not going to get similar function to unity in lubuntu.  Maybe you could add stuff to make it approach it but you'd also be adding extra dependencies and weight in general.  Then you're losing the low resource usage.

I have kubuntu installed on my 1Gb netbook and I've had xubuntu on it before.  KDE is weightier than XFCE for sure but you can set it up pretty well for speed.  It's still a bit slower and takes up  more RAM than xfce on the same netbook but not that much.  And it is *so* much more powerful than xfce ... I find it more powerful than unity actually ... that even with a little latency it's faster.
