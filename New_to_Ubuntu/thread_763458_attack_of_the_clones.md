### Post by nicedude on 2008-04-23
Ghost for Linux (G4L) will do exactly what you want.

You just download the iso image, burn it as an iso to a CD and boot the system from it.

One of the options is to make a local clone of your hard drive.

It makes an exact 1:1 copy, including GRUB.

Here is the link to download the iso

[http://sourceforge.net/projects/g4l](http://sourceforge.net/projects/g4l)

burn it SLOWLY. No faster than 4X.

### Post by Xiong Chiamiov on 2008-04-23
I just used [partimage]("http://www.partimage.org/Main_Page") successfully to backup my Kubuntu installation onto my home server, then restore it after doing some partitioning.  It took about 5 hours to backup some 130 gigs of data, but it worked very well.  You have to use it on an unmounted partition, so I ran off the LiveCD and installed partimage through apt.
