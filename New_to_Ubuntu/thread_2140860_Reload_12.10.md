Ubuntu does not have that feature and the Windows System Recovery is NOT as complete as you think.
It definitely does NOT "reload the operating system back to a brand-new state".

In other words, it is not a snapshot of the entire drive.

### Post by fantab on 2013-04-30
Windows (Microsoft) does not make that "system recovery". The 'one button' recovery is the work of the OEMs. Windows, at best, offers an option in its tools to make a 'recovery/repair disk'. We (Ubuntu) do NOT have anything like "System Restore" because most of the times we don't need it. Windows gives that option because 'they' know that you will need it, sooner or later.

However, there is 'recovery mode' in Ubuntu. But you will have to access it from Grub menu at boot by choosing "advanced options". This only a repair tool and NOT really a  'restore to factory image' kind of stuff. The best way to "reload' ubuntu is to reinstall. If you don't want to lose your settings and preferences then you will have to back up certain files 'n' folders before you 'reload' and restore them after you have reinstalled Ubutnu. **[READ HERE]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1748541&p=10765330#post10765330")**. 
[URL="http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1748541&p=10765330#post10765330"]
Clonezilla[/URL] is one of the utilities to "Clone" your partition or HDD.
