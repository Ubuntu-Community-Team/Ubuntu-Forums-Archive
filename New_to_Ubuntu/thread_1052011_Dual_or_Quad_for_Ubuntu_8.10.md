### Post by Ben Page on 2009-01-27
Four are always better than two, but I don't see a need for four cores yet, especially in Ubuntu. Only REAL advantage of dual cores over singles are noticed in games (windows games). Depends what are your plans, what do you need to do with your comp. For desktop workstation and office work, its enough to have a singlecore pentium with HT (virtual 2 cores). As for VirtualBox goes, you always get one core emulation, and it will always be slugish because of other components emulation like RAM, GPU, HDD...So I dont see the need for upgrade yet to quad, especially not from a dual core. I would wait for better support and utilization of quads, and then youll have quads that are much advanced then they are now, and todays quads will be cheaper.
