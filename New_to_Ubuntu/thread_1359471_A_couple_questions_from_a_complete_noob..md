### Post by ScottinSoCal on 2009-12-19
It depends on how adaptable you are.

The first time I spent any time on a Mac, I was forced to, and I hated every minute of it. It didn't do what I wanted it to, and the commands were strange, and if I'd had the money, I would have bought a new one, just for the thrill of throwing it off a tall building. I knew DOS, I liked DOS - why would anyone want anything but DOS? Years later, I got a job as a PC repair tech at a Mac shop. They wanted to branch out. Because there weren't many people bringing their PCs to Mac shops, I had to learn how to work on a Mac, and I learned to appreciate them for what they are. They aren't DOS, and they aren't Windows. They're great at what they do, but they're not what I was used to. Once I accepted that and moved on, I was fine with Macs. I own an Apple notebook now.

Linux is like that. It isn't Windoze. Don't try to make it be Windows, because you'll just mess up your computer and be frustrated. It's a new operating system, with its own rules and its own features. I love it. I recently installed WINE, and I've mothballed my Windows notebook. I'm strictly Ubuntu now. You can do the same thing, if you're ready to accept it for what it is - and what it isn't.

> But I got to ask in your opinion what would you say " What Ubuntu is and what it isnt?"

windows is great for gaming and running specific pieces of software that you are absolutely tied to for whatever reason. windows has more drivers available, but that is only significant if ***you*** happen to have that specific hardware with poor linux support. and that gap gets smaller by the day.

everything else, Ubuntu.

in my humble opinion.

this document isn't as "kind" and "gentle" as most of us around ubuntuforums.org tend to be when we write, but it bluntly hits several important nails on the head: [http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm](http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm) 

dont read it all now. bookmark it (along with this thread), and skim/read it when you are bored. (make sure to save your firefox bookmarks in windows so you can import them later in ubuntu -- cross platform windows/ubuntu software can almost always read its files across operating systems.)

in fact, while you are still toying with the idea and whatnot... also bookmark the psychocats website someone linked above. great website.

also worth a read: [http://www.whylinuxisbetter.net/](http://www.whylinuxisbetter.net/)

especially note the bottom secion: "The few cases where you should stick to Windows (for now)".... no one wants to drag you kicking & screaming to Ubuntu if it isn't for you :D
