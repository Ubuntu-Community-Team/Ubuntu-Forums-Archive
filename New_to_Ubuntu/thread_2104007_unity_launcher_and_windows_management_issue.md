### Post by MadmanRB on 2013-01-11
Yeah cycling between multiple windows is kind of sucky in unity, but it can be done.
Either hit the icon on the launcher or do alt+tab to switch between windows.
Or install a dock, I personally use cairo-dock as an alternative to the unity launcher when working in unity (whenever I feel like it that is as I do play with multiple user interfaces in ubuntu)
If you feel the above methods are not right for me I may recommend Kubuntu or Netrunner.
Both are based on Ubuntu and offer the same packages as it, or use linux mint as multi window management is better over there.
