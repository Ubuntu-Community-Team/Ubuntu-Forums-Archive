Perhaps that is also their way of seeing what you know, or maybe their way of saying "if you don't know this you have some learning to do before we can feel you are qualified for such a position".  I have no idea obviously of your experience level at system administration and server administration, so I can't know for sure what they are thinking.  Sometimes it's best to flat out ask "since I currently have no knowledge of that, can you explain what you are looking for?".  With the vast depth of people looking for jobs now, it is an employers market - they can wait for exactly what they are wanting and be picky about it.  While some might just dismiss you at asking such a question, others may be more willing to explain what they are looking for and what you may need to learn to meet their qualifications.
 
It can be really tough, and it can make you doubt yourself.  The trick is to learn when you probably aren't being accepted real well and just ask what types of things they would prefer you knew - this can give you a lot of guidance for what type of job you might currently qualify for and what things you need to learn to move yourself up to the type of position you eventually want.

It's "LAMP stack", as in **L**inux, **A**pache, **P**HP and **M**ySQL, combined together.

(a *solution stack* is a set of software components required to deliver a functional solution, in this case a web server with databases and a programming language)
