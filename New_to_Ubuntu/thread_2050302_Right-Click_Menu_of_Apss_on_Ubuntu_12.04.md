### Post by MadmanRB on 2012-08-30
If you are using gnome shell sadly there is no way to do it.
It mainly comes from the clean desktop idea and not overloading your screen with an array of clickable icons.
You can get one of the docks for gnome shell though that are based on your favorite applications.
I like these two when i occasionally like to mess around with gnome shell:

[https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/307/dash-to-dock/](https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/307/dash-to-dock/)

[https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/105/panel-docklet/](https://extensions.gnome.org/extension/105/panel-docklet/)

Also most stuff in gnome shell is single click so keep that in mind, personally I have gotten used to single clicking
