### Post by starcannon on 2009-01-24
Xfce is generally lighter; that comes with a price though.
The lighter interface means that some things that are more intuitive in Gnome or KDE are a little harder to get to in XFCE.
While my next point is subjective, I think that XFCE is not as nice looking as one of the heavier desktops, your tastes may be different.
If your doing an Xubuntu install, you will find that XFCE is not the only difference; Thunar is the default File manager in Xubuntu, where as in Ubuntu(regular) its Nautilus.

Anyway, XFCE is a great desktop manager, and it has some really cool bells and whistles, it's just not going to have a lot of the features that its heavier counterparts do.

Best bet is try it out for a week or 2, its Linux, theres nothing saying you have to keep it :)

GL and have fun

P.S. A)Nope, you can keep Gnome. B)I don't think it will change the splash screen(not certain, but pretty sure it won't)

### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2009-01-24
XFCE is very nice and I've run it a few times, but I'll say this: on Ubuntu, there's really no upshot to it. Installing the XFCE packages (or xubuntu-desktop, I did both) gave me an XFCE session that was not significantly faster or lighter than the GNOME equivalent. ### Post by Paqman on 2009-01-24
> **jrusso2 said:**
> The XFCE on Xubuntu seems to use almost as much resources a gnome.  I would call it a middle weight desktop not lightweight.

One reason for that is that by default Xubuntu loads the GTK packages when it boots. If you uncheck that option, you'll have a much lighter system. However, any GTK-based apps will take longer to launch. You can set it to pre-load QT instead, btw.
