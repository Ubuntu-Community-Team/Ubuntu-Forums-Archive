### Post by mikechant on 2011-06-19
These are only a couple of vague thoughts, but as no-one else has replied:

Have you considered the possibility that your hard drive is about to fail, so there could be repeated errors writing the image files which don't actually give an error message because the operation suceeds eventually?
Two things to try:
1/ Is it very slow still if you make a copy of one of the image files you've downloaded, or is it only the original download that's slow?
2/ Have a look at the Smart data for the hard drive (i.e. System/Admin/Disc utility, then select the relevant drive, look at the smart status (6th item down on the right) and click the 'smart data' button below this for more info.

The other thing I'd be looking at is some sort of issue with your ISP.

You said you didn't have this sort of problem with windows, but have you tried your connection with Windows recently or do you not have Windows setup on any PC you can try (I know you said you didn't have windows on the PC you are having the problem with). Or if you don't have a windows setup to compare with, you could see if you get this issue with
a) A live CD/USB of Ubuntu - possibly trying different releases
b) A live CD/USB of a different Linux distro, e.g. Fedora

Basically try testing as many different combinations of hardware/software as possible to try to get some sort of pattern.
