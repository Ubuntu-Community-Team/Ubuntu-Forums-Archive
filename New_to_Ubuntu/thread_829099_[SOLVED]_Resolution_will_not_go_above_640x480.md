### Post by YaroMan86 on 2008-06-18
1. Make sure you have the best driver for your system. This is typically what the Restricted Driver Manager offers. (I know people seem to swear by Envy, but for me, it just messes up X and makes it impossible to use. Do yourself a favor and don't use envy.)
2. In Gutsy and earlier, go to System -> Administration -> Screens and Graphics and explicitly pick your specific monitor from the list. DO NOT USE AUTODETECT or you'll just get some crummy generic monitor 9 times out of ten. In Hardy, press alt + F4 and type in displayconfig-gtk and select your monitor from the list there. As before, do not rely on auto detection, it doesn't work most of the time. On all versions, make sure you select the maximum resolution.
3. Restart your computer, or at least restart X.
4. Check System -> Preferences -> Screen Resoltuions for higher resolutions.

NEVER EVER use Envy. It breaks things, including X! It always has with me, and I bet that's what happened with you. NEVER EVER grab the latest driver from the nVidia website, either. Sounds crazy? IT breaks things liek Envy does, once you manage to install the darned thing, that is.
