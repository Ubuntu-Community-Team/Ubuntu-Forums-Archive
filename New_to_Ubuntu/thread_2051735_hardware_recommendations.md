### Post by Wim Sturkenboom on 2012-09-02
Can you give a price range? Not too expensive for me might be US$200 and for you it might be USD$500. Does it need to include a new monitor? Windows?

What do you want to do with the computer?

1)
Just browse the web, read emails and do some office work (spreadsheet, database, type a letter)? In that case I guess any processor will actually do (Celeron G530, Pentium G620); 4GB memory. Use the built-in GPU (it will eat some of the memory and hence 4GB advised).
2)
Or watch movies and play games in which case I can not really advise.
3)
Or do heavy image processing and other number crunching? In which case you can opt for a better processor (I3).
4)
Virtualisation? The more memory, the better. More cores as well. As your not going to run a huge number of virtual machines on a desktop, 2 cores with HT or 4 will do and 8GB.
5)
Do you need plenty of expansion slots or not TV card, extra USB, ...., sata connections? sata3, usb3? That more or less determines the motherboard. VGA out and / or DVI and / or HDMI? That determines the motherboard or videocard.

I went through the exercise this week. My needs are mostly category 1, a little more than the average user in category 3 (Gimp and co) and I want to play with other distros (and Windows) in a virtual machine (category 4).

I ended up (yesterday) with an I3-2120, 8GB memory, el-cheapo motherboard (asrock, H61 based), 1 TB harddisk. Probably a bit overkill but performance wise future proof (in my opinion). Limitations are that I don't have USB3, SATA3 and only one expansion slot. And that, after moving my existing HDs, my SATA connections are already full (250GB, 1TB and 1TB, DVD rewriter). 

Local price (converted) around US$500; no monitor and no Windows. I guess that in the States this will be a bit cheaper.
