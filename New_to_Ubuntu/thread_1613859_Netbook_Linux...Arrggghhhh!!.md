### Post by wilee-nilee on 2010-11-04
You can probably get a faster boot by holding down the shift key at powering on and getting to a similar try or install screen and choose try. If you wait for the new installer gui version without the shift key prompt it does seem to take much longer.

You will get a screen of some sort like this until you install. 

You can do full installs to a thumb a 8 gig would be the best as to the smallest to use, you just have to be sure the bootloader grub2 is installed to the mbr of the thumb.

Edit: Just for informational sake I am using a acer-aspireone d250 I bumped the ram to 2 gigs and have maverick dual booted with W7 pro it runs very nicely.
