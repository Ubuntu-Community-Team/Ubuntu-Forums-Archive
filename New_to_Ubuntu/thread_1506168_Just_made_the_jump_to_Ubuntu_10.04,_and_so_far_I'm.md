### Post by Zed7 on 2010-06-10
After years of being increasingly annoyed by Windows getting in the way of my simple computing needs, I began researching alternatives. I became intrigued by Linux, specifically the apparently newbie-friendly Ubuntu, but was hesitant to make the jump so I just kept tabs on it as it developed.

(I had long since modified the existing XP installation on my laptop to the point it was unbelievably rather lean and mean... yet it no longer felt right clinging to an outdated product that was clearly being shown the door by its makers.)

Well, having tested the Live CD of Ubuntu 10.04 a few times and being pleasantly surprised that there were no issues with sound, headphones, wireless, USB mouse, etc., I finally decided to take the leap yesterday... and could not be happier that I did. After some incredibly straightforward customization (toggled some of the default startup apps, primarily), I have an operating system that runs better with eye candy than XP did without, right out of the box!

To be honest, with the exception of installing Flash on Firefox (ridiculously quick and easy), I have not had to do anything close to "under the hood" like going to the command line, but considering that was often the best way to achieve something in Windows anyway, I'll have no problem resorting to that if and when need be.

Depending on how the next few experimental weeks go, I'll seriously consider suggesting it to select friends and family. My initial impression is that for ordinary users, Ubuntu would actually be simpler and more intuitive to grasp than Windows. Maybe power users struggle, but I just don't see everyday people having a hard time with this once you factor out the familiarity with Microsoft (although aesthetically I do like having only one taskbar, and prefer my min/max/close buttons in the top right, so I changed both).

My laptop's specs are:
Toshiba Satellite A135-S4467
Pentium Dual Core 1.73 GHz
1 GB RAM
72 GB HDD
...and believe it or not this shipped with Vista!

Oh, and hi to all, obviously I'm new to these forums. Apologies if this thread is posted in the wrong place, I just had to share my positive introduction to Ubuntu. I'm sure I'll have questions the more I interact with it, but for now it's been a welcoming and responsive operating system that treats me like a grown individual, and I couldn't find that more refreshing.
