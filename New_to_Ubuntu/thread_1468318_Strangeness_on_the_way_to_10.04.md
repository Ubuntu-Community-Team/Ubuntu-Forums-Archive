### Post by ScottinSoCal on 2010-05-01
I don't know if I should report this as a bug or not, but maybe people should be aware of it.

I'm running a Dell D810 notebook computer. I've had Ubuntu on it for about a year with no issues. It has a second hard drive in the module bay. Primary HD (sda1) is a 320 GB Western Digital, secondary (sdb1) is an old 80 GB Hitachi. I use the secondary to backup critical files. Both are formatted as EXT4. Before upgrading to 10.04, I backed up my /home folder to the secondary and went ahead with the upgrade. The latest updates applied to 9.10 - just seconds before the upgrade. No errors to speak of during the process, and everything runs great, but my secondary HD is wiped. Partition was still there, everything looked fine, and I can copy things back to it, but all the files I had on it before the upgrade are gone.

I didn't lose anything, it was just a backup, but if I'd had anything important there it would have created a problem. Just FYI.
