### Post by baruch60610 on 2008-11-04
After upgrading from Hardy to Intrepid (with KDE4), I found that my system was unbelievably sluggish.  Simply clicking on a new tab could take several seconds to get a response.  It felt as though I had gone back to that *other* operating system.

top revealed that the main hog was knotify4.  I don't know what knotify4 is supposed to be doing, but when I killed it, my system began to run more quickly.  There had been two instances, one started by root, one by my username.  I only killed the one for my username, as that was the one taking all the cycles.

What is this process for, and why is it such a pig?  I had let the computer run all night, thinking perhaps knotify4 might be doing some post-upgrade bookkeeping or something that was a once-off effort.  No such luck.  I regret that this upgrade didn't have a rollback feature.  I'd go back to Hardy.  The glitz of Intrepid and KDE4 aren't worth the loss of responsiveness.
