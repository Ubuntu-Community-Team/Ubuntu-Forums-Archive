### Post by Paqman on 2011-10-08
> **morningstar.fallen said:**
> Can anybody please give me a brief introduction into differences I could experience between 32bit and 64bit versions of Ubuntu?

Nothing really. Just install and use. Nobody seems to be reporting any issues on that mobo in these forums. 

Drivers are not an issue, I've never even heard of a device that didn't work on 64-bit but did on 32-bit. I've been using 64-bit for probably about 5 years or so.

Software wise it's very rare to find something that's not available in 64-bit and even if you do it's generally possible to just install the 32-bit version. Support for this will come out-of-the-box in the next version of Ubuntu, but even now installing 32-bit Flash (for example) is automatic. Short answer: don't worry about it.

> 
Also could you please share your thoughts on using Virtual Box on x64 OS? And if it's even possible?

Definitely possible, I do it myself. You'll find the extra computational power and more efficient use of large RAM sizes will be a distinct advantage over 32-bit.
