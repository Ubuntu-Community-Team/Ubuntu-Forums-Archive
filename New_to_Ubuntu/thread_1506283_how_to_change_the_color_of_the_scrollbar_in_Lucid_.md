### Post by Old Jimma on 2010-06-10
Hi Ubuntu Community:

For those of you who want to change the color of the scrollbar to make it easier to see, here is a howto that worked for me. This howto makes the scrollbar **[COLOR="Orange"]Ubuntu Orange[/COLOR]** when:
[LIST]
[*]your mouse is in the active window
[*]when your mouse hovers over the scroll bar, and
[*]when you click on the scollbar to slide it up or down (or back and forth).
[/LIST]

Here you go:

First read: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1172617]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1172617")

Then,
install gnome-color-chooser
applications > accessories > terminal window
gnome-color-chooser
specific tab
check scrollbars
check normal
click on the color window and put [COLOR="DarkOrange"]**#FB8B00**[/COLOR] in the color name box (see: [http://www.perbang.dk/rgb/FB8B00/](http://www.perbang.dk/rgb/FB8B00/))
PRESS APPLY
do the same for the hover and button pressed check boxes, if you like
PRESS APPLY
engines tab
check scrollbar
choose Murrine
PRESS APPLY
PRESS CLOSE

Have a look at the attached screenshot to see how it looks!

I'm an old man and can't see very well anymore and had to fix that scrollbar so that I could see it!

I hope this helps somebody else!
