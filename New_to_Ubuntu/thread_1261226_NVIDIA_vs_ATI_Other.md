### Post by CatKiller on 2009-09-08
> **Excedio said:**
> nVIDIA is historically better when using Linux. However, ATI is making some huge strides and I think they make overtake nVIDIA in Linux support someday.

This.

In terms of current performance, it's


[LIST=1]
[*]nVidia
[*]Ati
[*]Intel
[/LIST]
In terms of Linux-friendliness, it's exactly the other way round. Intel develop open-source drivers, Ati have now released the specifications for their cards to open-source developers (and their proprietary drivers have historically been a bit rubbish) and nVidia make a strong proprietary driver but are hugely secretive about their special sauce. That means that the nVidia driver will only work on the platforms and kernel versions that nVidia choose and if they decide to drop support for your card so that you have to buy a new one you have no choice but to comply.

Once the Free ati/radeon/radeonhd drivers catch up with the new information, Ati will be in a very strong position; much more performance than Intel and much more flexible than nVidia.
