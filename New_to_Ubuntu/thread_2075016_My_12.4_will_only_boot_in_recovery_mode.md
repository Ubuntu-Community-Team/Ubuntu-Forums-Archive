### Post by searchfgold6789 on 2012-10-24
Thank you for posting the info, and also, thanks for your private message. I am not an expert in the area of Next-Gen Intel graphics in Linux, but it's something I occasionally read about in my Linux.com RSS feed. You could return your PC to the shop and replace it with hardware that uses older, beefier technology, but I wouldn't recommend doing that quiiiiite yet, because I have a hunch, and there's always the possibility of someone coming to this thread who has experience or knowledge about your setup that I do not.

From what I've seen on the internet, the hardware you have is old stuff for Windows, which has drivers readily available for everyone. Conversely, in Linux, developers are scrambling to get these drivers developed still.

First question is, what do you mean by "not proper graphics"? Are you getting the wrong resolution, not enough FPS, or other odd behavior? or no graphics at all?

What difference is there from running a Live CD, booting normally (without entering recovery mode), and booting into recovery mode and choosing "resume normal boot"?

You should also try installing [version 12.10]("http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop") instead of 12.04. Your hardware is new - really, realllly new. 12.10 probably has drivers for this newer hardware that 12.04 does not. Before you try that, though, try comparing the different modes as suggested.

Also, there's the option of trying the upstream X.org software (X.org is the software that handles graphics in Ubuntu.) You can read more about this [here]("https://launchpad.net/~xorg-edgers/+archive/ppa"). Basically, you addd a software source to your system and update software from there (to get newer software) instead of the default source. To do that, open a terminal and type these commands: ```

sudo add-apt-repository ppa:xorg-edgers/ppa 
sudo apt-get update
sudo apt-get upgrade
```Sincerely,
 - searchfgold
