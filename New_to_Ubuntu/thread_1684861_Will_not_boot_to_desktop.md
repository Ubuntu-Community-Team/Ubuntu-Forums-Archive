### Post by robsoles on 2011-02-11
sorry, a little distracted and hard to see a recovery path because just entering repair mode on one installation of Ubuntu shouldn't even harm that installation let alone the other - **unless** something similar (because both are broken in the same way) was done to both of them; ie.; if you went into recovery mode of one of them and did something to it's xorg drivers and/or configuration and then you mounted the other one's partition and messed with it's xorg stuff too.

If you got into the repair mode by accident then surely you just closed it, when it became available to close, and on reboot everything should have been as good (and working) as it was before your brief visit to recovery/repair mode.

From the info I have the quick way I can see for you to fix it is just to reinstall each entire OS.


As much as you can remember selecting or executing in repair mode will help.

How did you get into the repair mode? (There are a couple of different 'repair/recovery' modes/methods and knowing which one you accessed will help no end).

Please do your best to list steps you took, in this repair mode, before restarting the PC to find this problem.
