### Post by penguinv on 2009-05-25
This post is to say there was no problem.

I booted up. I attached the "dongle", the Zyxel USB gizmo.
I clicked on the network icon on the top panel of the desktop, and there it was already, a list of available connections.

It doesnt get better than that.

I have a general hint for USB connections. I get better results and the USB gizmo is safer (from bumps) because I have it on a 4 foot extension cord. I can place it to get a best signal, like moving an antenna.

I searched and found no information about attaching a wireless USB card to an Ubuntu system and installation and since it worked easily I am posting this with workds about USB installation in Ubuntu so that others who seach may find this and be heartened.

Good karma to us all.
