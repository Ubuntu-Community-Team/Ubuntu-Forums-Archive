### Post by rocketshiptech on 2019-05-02
Its some sort of input or screen refresh, or app switching problem that I originally thought was the machine hanging. 

  It only happens after the machine’s been on for a while, and by then I usually have A Vbox Win7 session going, Firefox minimized and either VLC or Spotify playing.  So I’m not sure if its one of those apps or a combination of them or what.

  What happens is that clicking or typing just stops doing anything. It looked like it was just hanging.  Given when it was happening, (after I had a bunch of stuff open) I was thinking it was some memory problem. But then I realized it isn’t really frozen, there are ways out of it.

  For one, if I’m working in the Linux session (i.e. any Linux app has the focus) then control-alt-delete will wake it up. (But that forces you out of vbox). Or alt-tab works, but not always at first, sometimes I have to hit alt-tab _two or three times_, but once I see the app switcher, then everything's fine.

  If I _am_ in the Win7 session, I can get out of it but with some very strange behavior indeed; 

  I have to click the Ubuntu  “start button” the one with the nine dots, “Show Applications”  button, and what happens is I see the animation of all the icons flying out, but then they disappear! I don’t get the Linux Apps screen, its still in Win7. Again, if click that button two or three_ or four_ times THEN I see then I see all the linux apps and then the machine responds normally again.

  So it’s not memory and freezing. Its some kind of input/display or app switching problem (maybe its taking the input, just not showing me the results on the screen.) its not a screen saver issue, it happens while I’m working.

(And I've never seen a computer problem where just trying the same thing over and over eventually works.) :/
