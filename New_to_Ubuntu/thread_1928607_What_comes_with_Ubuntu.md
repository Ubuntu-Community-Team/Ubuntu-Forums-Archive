Welcome to the Ubuntu Forums :-)

The flash drive is probably a live USB drive. You should boot your computer from it and

1. Run a live session testing ubuntu without installing it. During the testing, you should be able to 'see' your computer and its HDD, CD, DVD, USB drives. Do a lot of testing like this!

2. When you feel that you want to install Ubuntu, come back here to get some more details, and at that time also describe your computer, so that we can help you decide how to install ubuntu.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-02-20
Any installation, especially a dual-boot, comes with some risk.  If you were in a position to (1) easily back off what you have now, (2) easily reconfigure your PC to handle dual-boot, and (3) easily restore your PC into a fully-working state should something go wrong -- then I would encourage you to take the leap.

However, given that you admit to having no technical folks around to help you, then the best advice I can give you right now -- is to leave well enough alone. If your PC is working OK for you, then don't mess around with it until you are back in a situation where you can get immediate help.

And, yeah, everyone else is probably going to jump in here strongly encouraging you to throw caution to the wind and make the leap -- but if something goes wrong, it's not going to be THEIR PC that gets hosed, it's going to be YOURS.

So, while I would encourage you to experiment with using Ubuntu from the flash drive (and that's to develop some familiarity with it), unless you want to be faced with having to repair MS Windows big time, you should avoid the temptation of installing Ubuntu to your PC -- for now.
