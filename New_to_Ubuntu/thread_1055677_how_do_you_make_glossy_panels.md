### Post by jerome1232 on 2009-01-31
Open gimp, click File->New, set the pixel Height to 24, the width to whatever the width of your screen is. Click Ok

On the left hand side there is a Blend tool, looks like a square that fades grey to white. Select that. Down below the selection of tools you can set your foreground color and your background color, pick the colors you wish (usually a dark version of the color for foreground and a light nearly white version for the background, you'll have to play with it to get the look your like)

Now move your mouse over to where your picture is, click near the bottom of the picture, hold ctrl (to keep a 90 degree angle) and drag your mouse towards the top, release the button and it should paint a gradient on their. Play with it untill you get the right look.

Save your file as a .png image and set it as the background for your panel. I've included a few screenshots to help.

This is just the basic way I figured out how to do it, there's probably ways to create even better looks using gimp.

See how my bottom panel seems to be a glossy light blue color in the last screenshot?
