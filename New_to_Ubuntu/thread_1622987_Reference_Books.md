### Post by eotakos on 2010-11-16
My complete transition to ubuntu some -a little less than- three years ago, was assisted by the "ubuntu linux bible". This one is a huge book, covering most aspects of what you would, propably, want to do, and how to do it. It covers a wide range from who to edit some picture with gimp, to who to host a service (one of the popular ones). some aspects are covered thoroughly, others not so much (for example the servers chapters). Anyhow, the book referes to a much older version of ubuntu, but if you are in a kind of "how could I possibly do that" situation, it may provide you with some good answers.

this pocket guide might also prove usefull to you, and it is free:
[http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html](http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html)

When sometime ago I tried to start a similar thread, the first answer I got was "you have to get your hands dirty" kind of thing. But as far as I'm concerned, you must have some basis in order to get your hands dirty... so here's where I get my basis from these days:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/)

And ofcourse, don't forget our favourite forums :-)
