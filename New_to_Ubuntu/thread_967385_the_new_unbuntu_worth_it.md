### Post by scragar on 2008-11-02
> I didn't know about this the first time I installed ubuntu, but there are long term support versions, then there are others that aren't? I figured this out when hardy came out and thats the new long term support.Yes, Hardy is the LTS(long term support) release, which means it will be supported for 2 years(since Ibex just came out that makes it about 1 and a half years left)

> What does this mean for Ibex? Is it the next long term support? If not, whats the point of installing it?Ibex will be supported for 6 months(a bit more over the switch over from Ibex to whatever the 9.04 release will be called(tempted to guess Jaunty)). The advantage to using Ibex is that new software is not added to the Hardy releases, security updates are about the only updates Hardy will get, Ibex will get new software.

> Also, if I do install this, and I have hardy, can I just upgrade it from that without downloading the ISO?You can upgrade hardy to Ibex (just be warned that it's a slow procedure), I do recommend if you do decide to try Ibex you wait a short while before you upgrade, there is always a large push of updates around the 3-4 weeks mark which will designate the 8.10.1 release(containing security updates etc).

And welcome to the community.

### Post by Ahunt on 2008-11-02
Before upgrading from Hardy to Intrepid you may want to carefully check out your hardware on a live CD session. Intrepid does not support the same hardware that Hardy does.
