### Post by Paqman on 2008-10-13
We can't really tell you how much it costs, because we don't know exactly what stuff you need, or even what country you're in!

More RAM is generally a good idea. How much do you have currently? Go to System > Admin > System Monitor and have a look at the Resources page. It'll tell you how much RAM you have, and how much is being used at any one time. If your RAM is working really hard (say over 75% in use) and/or you're using any swap, then more RAM will help.

You need to get *exactly* the right kind of RAM for your motherboard. So you'll need to find out what exact model of motherboard you have.

For your graphics card, again, you'll need to know what kind of mobo. Or more precisely, you'll need to know whether it uses an AGP slot or a PCI-Express slot for its graphics card.

At the end of the day, a PC is only as fast as it's slowest component. If your CPU is maxing out a lot, then a RAM and GPU will only have a limited effect.
