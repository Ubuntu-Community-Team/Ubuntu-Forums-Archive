### Post by abn91c on 2009-01-03
> **baxter1980 said:**
> yes I did all that. Is there a driver I need?
no driver needed for ubuntu, however you need to make sure the new cdrom is also recognized in the bios. the drive door will still open and close but to get to work it needs to be activated in the bios as well.

### Post by baxter1980 on 2009-01-03

### Post by abn91c on 2009-01-03
> **baxter1980 said:**
> how do I go about doing that?
reboot the POST will tell you to press **a key** for setup, usually **DEL, f12, f2, f1,** depending on the bios brand. when you press that key it takes you to the bios, look there where the drives are listed, some bios have an auto detect screen, it tell you what key to press to scan the computer. Also make sure you save the settings when you exit the bios,** f10** on most computers.
