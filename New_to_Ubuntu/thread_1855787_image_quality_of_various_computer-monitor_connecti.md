### Post by WasMeHere on 2011-10-08
DVI is digital and VGA is analog. The general conclusion is that analog is more sensitive (it picks up disturbing electromagnetic fields), so keep as much as possible of your signal digital!

But normally you get good quality video with VGA, and most laptops still have VGA. I guess it's because they must fit the projectors that are around in conference rooms. So I think you will be satisfied with your external video quality :-)

Test the option to have different pictures on the two screens and to have full resolution on both!

### Post by CatKiller on 2011-10-08
> **hanzj said:**
> How do you mean by VGI-DVI? which parts of the connection are you talking about?

You probably got an adaptor with your monitor. You potentially also got one with the laptop. If you didn't, they're a couple of quid. Just pop it in the VGA port and plug your DVI cable into it. I've yet to see an adapted signal that looks any different to plugging directly into a DVI port.

The problem you're trying to avoid is signal noise in the cable. Your computer deals with the display as a digital device and your monitor is a digital device. Having an analogue transmission between them is unnecessary. Having said that, a good quality VGA cable will be perfectly fine too. You only really get significant noise with a long run of unshielded cable too close to the mains.

@oldsoundguy: HDMI is not better quality than DVI, it's exactly the same. HDMI is a DVI signal with sound and DRM.
