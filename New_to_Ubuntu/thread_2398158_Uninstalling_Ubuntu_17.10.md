### Post by Impavidus on 2018-08-09
If you want to remove Ubuntu without a trace, which is indeed a good idea if you transfer the computer to someone else, you have to wipe the hard drive. Use a live disk to boot the computer. It doesn't matter what flavour or distro or version, as long as it boots it's fine. Then wipe the drive. See for example [http://how-to.wikia.com/wiki/How_to_wipe_a_hard_drive_clean_in_Linux](http://how-to.wikia.com/wiki/How_to_wipe_a_hard_drive_clean_in_Linux)
