### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-02-19
If you're sure it's not a clatter coming from the fan(s), most likely the hard drive is thrashing trying to find the MBR.  Normally final and complete failure is not far away when the drive starts to do this.  Back up your data _immediately_, and plan on replacing the drive.

Chris.

### Post by frotzed on 2011-02-19
First suspect for noise within the case is always a fan (imho).  Open one side of the case and boot the PC, you should be able to fairly easily get the basic location of the noise source just by listening closely.

From what you say, however, I do agree with the above poster that it's likely the hard disk failing.  Back up your non-replaceable data immediately.

### Post by deconstrained on 2011-02-20
Since it happens only the first few times you boot up after the computer being off for a while, I'm inclined to think that it's a component like a fan whose components expand and...run differently after exposed to some heat. This does not preclude the hard drive, however.

Back up your data. You should do this regardless of your computer's health.
