### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-09-05
I have a reasonable amount of experience with this.

A few years ago I bought a $200 box that digitises and compresses a video input into MPEG-2 format. You could plug a video camera or a VCR into one end, and your computer into the other end.

The quality was alright, but not fantastic. Disk space was an issue; storing a lot of lightly-compressed footage took up a lot of room. And recompressing the footage to put it onto DVD took a while, and was a bit hands-on.

Eventually we bought a VCR/DVD Recorder Combo unit. It's a VCR and DVD recorder that you plug into your TV, and it allows you to dub videotapes to DVD (or record straight from TV to DVD). The quality is basically indistinguishable from the source, and the whole process is very quick; put in blank DVD, play videotape, after the end of the tape you wait about a minute and you've got yourself a playable DVD with a menu.

Honestly, it's the easiest way to do it. If you want to put the footage onto your computer for any reason (i.e. posting onto Youtube) you can just rip the DVD.
