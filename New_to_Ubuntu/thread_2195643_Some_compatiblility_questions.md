### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-12-25
I am not laughing but you do have to limit your expectations. Ubuntu developers have been working on putting Ubuntu on mobile devices but not so that Ubuntu can be installed on any and all phones and tablets. The aim was to get Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) to bring Ubuntu mobile devices to market. The developers selected 4 mobile devices to install a developer preview on so that they could test the code as they developed it and software developers could join in or develop and test applications on it. The four devices selected were Galaxy Nexus; Nexus 4; Nexus 7 and Nexus 10. These are all 2012 devices and not 2013 products.

Ubuntu is free and open source software and so community developers are free to try and port the code to any devices they choose.  The information is here.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

Even if I had one of the four chosen devices I would think twice before attempting to install Ubuntu Touch on it. It is a scary process from what I have read. After all, Ubuntu on mobile devices is still in development.

Ubuntu Touch recently reached version 1.0 and has been optimised for the phone. Over next six months work will be to optimise Ubuntu Touch for tablets. So, you see we are not dealing with a working product.

We have just had news that Canonical (the company that supports Ubuntu) has just signed an agreement with an OEM to bring out a Ubuntu device. We do not know any details and we will not be told until the OEM announces the product is on sale. There is not yet an official press release on this subject. These devices will be commercial products and may also be limited in the same way as other mobile devices are restricted. That is just business.

This will help you see the situation at present

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/ReleaseNotes](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/ReleaseNotes)

These are the known issues

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SaucySalamander/ReleaseNotes#Ubuntu_for_phones](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SaucySalamander/ReleaseNotes#Ubuntu_for_phones)

[http://insights.ubuntu.com/news/latest-ubuntu-13-10-includes-first-step-to-mobile-pc-convergence/](http://insights.ubuntu.com/news/latest-ubuntu-13-10-includes-first-step-to-mobile-pc-convergence/)
