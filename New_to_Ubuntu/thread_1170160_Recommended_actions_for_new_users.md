### Post by 73ckn797 on 2009-05-26
Greetings to all,

I want to encourage any and all new Ubuntu users to do a few simple things that can avoid many questions. This is not any official comment but my desire to help the new user experience.

After you have installed Ubuntu for the first time and barring any problems with that installation, take the time to browse through any and all menus. You will find so much there. Simple things like volume settings, menu listing options, programs available and sources from which to get programs. Leave no menu selections unopened. Many times a solution will be found with a simple exploration of what is readily available.

Read "sticky notes" at the top of any relevant forums. Especially [http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=326](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=326) 

There are also many sources apart from what is available in the installation of Ubuntu.

Help is available via the forums and other sources. Google can be your friend.

Check here also:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/) 
[http://manpages.ubuntu.com/](http://manpages.ubuntu.com/)

Other people may and will provide many other sources to answer your questions.

These are only a few sources. Browse the forums and you will find many available links recommended. 

We/I hope your adventure & discovery with Ubuntu is pleasant and as trouble free as possible.

Feel free to ask any question and someone will offer a solution. Sometimes those solutions require command line interface (CLI) entries. Many solutions also have Graphical User Interface solutions (GUI) that can accomplish the same things. Make your questions specific as possible and you will get a prompt response.

Happy Ubuntu!!
