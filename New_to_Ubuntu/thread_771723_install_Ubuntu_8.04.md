### Post by jimrz on 2008-04-27
Gotta be some kind of hardware problem. Those older Dell's are very well compatible with Ubuntu (and linux in general). A while back I got several free Optiplex's ( GX110 + GX200 + 2x GX300's) through a friend who's company had taken them out of service and rotated in newer equipment and did not care to bother with the paperwork and such involved in sending them to a recycler. All of them loaded Ubuntu quite easily from the regular install cd. None, that I know of (gave 2 away to friends and donated another to a local charity), have 8.04 on them but all did well with both 7.04 and 7.10. I still have one of the GX300's as my home server which still has 7.04 on it, but I tested the 8.04 "live" cd on it and all went well. Will be moving this machine to 8.04 shortly (as soon as I can find a bit of spare time, ha) and plan on leaving it that way through the LTS cycle.
