### Post by mtalexan on 2010-07-06
I don't know if you're still having problems with your NB 100, but the reason has been the lack of driver support.  In general you have to be careful about new distros and the absolute latest versions of those distros, especially when you're using cheap hardware or laptop hardware to run it.  Both cheap and laptop hardware tend to do some crazy stuff that requires pretty specialized drivers, or strange setups to support them.

In my experience, full desktop Ubuntu is extremely stable and supports pretty much anything you can think of, and the newer versions keep getting better.  Since I actually use my computer(s) and don't want to always have to spend hours trying to get something simple to work, I don't upgrade to the newest release as soon as it comes out, but wait a month or two before doing so.  By that point, most of the issues everyone's complains about are fixed (usually just the last few kinks in the process) and it runs smoothly.

For the Toshiba Netbooks (NB 100, 200, 300) it seems they are using Wifi and Audio hardware that doesn't have great driver support.  The drivers are slow to release, and even slower to be ported into the distro.  Luckily, the audio and Skype issues you've been running into are fixed in UNR 10.04 and only need a minor tweak to a config file for everything to work perfectly.

For those asking questions about Ubuntu Netbook Remix (UNR) versus Ubuntu Desktop, the upgrade keeps you on the same distribution, but upgrades your version.  It's really not that critical to begin with anyway, it's just a skin change more than anything else and the power savings have more to do with your settings than the distribution.
