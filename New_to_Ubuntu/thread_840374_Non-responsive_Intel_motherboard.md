### Post by H.Callahan on 2008-06-25
My guess would be the motherboard is shot.  It is also possible that it could be the CPU, but if you don't have another CPU to test with, it would be hard to tell.

It most likely is not the CMOS battery.  The battery is only used to hold settings during times when the machine is off.  When power is applied when the battery is dead, the machine will default to the default CMOS settings and should be able to at least get through the POST (assuming CPU, RAM and the video card are ok).

As sydbat said, it might be cheaper, if your time is worth anything, to junk the board/cpu and get new ones.  A low edge MB/CPU shouldn't set you back much at all.
