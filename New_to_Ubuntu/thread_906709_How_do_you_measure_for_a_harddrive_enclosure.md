### Post by halitech on 2008-08-31
I *think* the 3.5 refers to the size of the actual platter inside the drive as most drives (IDE anyway, not sure on SATA) are 4" wide so go with one that says for 3.5" as the 2.5 are usually for laptop drives.

as far as firewire, that depends on if you have a firewire port or not

### Post by egalvan on 2008-08-31
> **halitech said:**
> I *think* the 3.5 refers to the size of the actual platter inside the drive

These platters are actually 3.75". :) 

> ...most drives (IDE anyway, not sure on SATA) are 4" wide so go with one that says for 3.5" as the 2.5 are usually for laptop drives.

The WIDTH  is set at 4". They must fit standard openings.
 SCSI, IDE or SATA. All the same width.

The HEIGHT is either FULL, HALF, THIRD or SLIM (1")
(for IDE, the norm recently (last 10 years) is 1", HiPerformance SCSI are usually 1/3 height)

More than you ever wanted to know. :)

> as far as firewire, that depends on if you have a firewire port or not

Yes, you need a Firewire port to run Firewire.

Even though FW runs slower than USB (400m vs 480m) it is usually quicker.
FW was written to be fully bus-sharing, devices can fully drop control of the bus, so contention is handled more robustly.

A superior bus for data transfers, such as hard drives.

FireWire-II runs at 800mHz. Far superior to USB.

I love my Firewire external drives!

Far more than you were looking for, I'm sure.
