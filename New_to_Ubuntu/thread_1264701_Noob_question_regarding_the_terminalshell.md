### Post by NoaHall on 2009-09-12
Reasons for learning the terminal - 
It's a great recovery tool for when something goes wrong and you can't use the GUI.
It's more powerful in the things you can do with it.
You can do things quicker and lots of things at once(e.g, I could just enter "cp /home/noah /media/disk && cp media/disk-1/music /home/noah/music" - and that's much quicker than clicking and waiting for each job to just be over.)
Here, on the forums is a good place to learn. See what commands others are telling people to do.

### Post by SuperSonic4 on 2009-09-12
Above it is more efficient to use the terminal than to have a GUI solution

The terminal is less ambigious (pasting commands in terminal is much easier than telling someone how to navigate screens)

There are some things which are easier in terminal, like the **-u** flag with **cp** which means update ie: to only copy files when they are newer than the destination files (this is excellent when backing up music regularly!)

Troubleshooting is often done from a shell/terminal, especially if X breaks

### Post by snowpine on 2009-09-12
There are a lot of benefits; I will just name one, maybe other readers can add more...

It makes helping other users on Ubuntu Forums easier. You can copy a terminal command from the forums and paste it into the terminal on your computer. There is no chance of clicking the wrong button or making a typo. Also, terminal commands generally work whether you're using Ubuntu, Kubuntu, or Xubuntu... as you know, the menus are are different for each version, so the menu-driven solution that works for me in Xubuntu won't work for you in Kubuntu. But the terminal solution is usually the same.

That's my favorite reason. :)
