### Post by swoody on 2009-10-20
Typically in Linux, if a product is supported on one distro, it *should* be supported on other distros. Now I'm sure there are special cases, but this is the norm. There is a bit of difference between releases, but with backports, and being able to manually install drivers yourself, this shouldn't be a *big* issue, it's more of a question of what will work 'out-of-the-box' not 'what will work ever'.

I can't speak as to Xandros' device support, but I know Lexmark has pretty horrible Linux support. I ditched my all-in-one Lexmark for an HP after looking into it for a while. 

It may be a bit old, but I think you should read over this How-To, it may be able to lend you a starting point for trying to get your printer working:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=49714](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=49714)

As others have mentioned though, for simplicity sake, I would recommend going with a printer that is well supported in Linux. I know that's probably not what you want to hear, but a new printer may be worth it's weight in gold when it 'just works' :)
