### Post by Bearly Able on 2009-05-31
OK, well, I'm no expert, but I've just been doing something similar.

When you use gksudo nautilus, it should open the file browser window.  There should be a menu at the left-hand side of this with various options, including the Ubuntu partition that you just mounted.

You have to navigate to wherever you want to be from within the nautilus window you opened with gksudo, otherwise you're no longer working as root.  When you close the original window, it automatically ends your nautilus root session.  It took me a while to get used to this.
