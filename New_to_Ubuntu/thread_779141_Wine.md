**W**ine **I**s **N**ot an **E**mulator :)

But what it is, is a way to 'fool' Windows programs into thinking they are on windows, and running fine. When a windows program asks for "windows_screen" or something, wine says "sure!" and gives it "ubuntu_screen" dressed up as "windows_screen". Therefore, the best words for it are "compatability layer"; it is like the little things you use to plug your electronics in, in another country to make the plugs fit in the sockets; the same electricity is going through, it just needs to be given to it in a different way.
