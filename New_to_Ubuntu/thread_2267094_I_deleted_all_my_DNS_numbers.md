### Post by steeldriver on 2015-02-27
The 'auto' DNS settings just come via DHCP: most home routers include a DNS server if you set the IPv4 settings for your connection to 'Auto (DHCP)'

Alternatively, you could use one of the public DNS services such as google (8.8.8.8, 8.8.4.4) or OpenDNS (208.67.222.222 and 208.67.220.220)
