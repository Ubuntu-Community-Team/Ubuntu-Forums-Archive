### Post by Mike_IronFist on 2010-06-07
> **IAmAnarch said:**
> According to [CNET]("http://news.cnet.com/8301-31001_3-20006900-261.html?tag=rtcol;inTheNewsNow"), the RIAA seems to think that Limewire is used exclusively for music piracy. Is the Limewire network decentralized enough to continue functioning if the judge ordered Limewire to shut down?

Nope, LimeWire uses the Gnutella network, which means that regardless of what happens to LimeWire, you could still access stuff from that network, including with FrostWire or gtk-gnutella, both of which exist on Ubuntu.
So I say, ditch LimeWire anyway! You don't really need it, FrostWire works fantastically anyway - in my experience, it's got a nicer look than LimeWire.
