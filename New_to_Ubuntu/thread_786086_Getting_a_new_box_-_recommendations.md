### Post by bobpur on 2008-07-02
I think your main consideration here is your graphics card. For motherboard supported graphics use Intel or Nvidia. For an add-on card use Nvidia. You can still find new Nvidia cards out there back into the 5200 series (PCI?). ATI graphics card support has come a long way lately, but I think Nvidia still has the edge for ease of install and driver support.
It doesn't have to be the latest technology, either. You can still buy new components that are a bit outdated. PATA drives instead of SATA drives and AGP or PCI video cards instead of PCIe. 
A good place to look would be: [www.newegg.com](www.newegg.com) or [www.tigerdirect.com](www.tigerdirect.com). 

Good Luck.

### Post by bluepowder7 on 2008-07-02
CPU - the AMD stuff is cheaper and some may say faster / better
mobo - pick one for the AMD processor, and with nVidia on-board graphics chipset
ram - 2gigs is enough and not too expensive if you snag stuff that's got rebates
sound - again, stick with on-board stuff, i like nVidia again
hard drive - any SATA will do, make sure the mobo has at least 2 sata ports
dvd drive - either IDE or SATA


my desktop machine which is used for simple games on windows (rpg's mostly, sometimes unreal or half-life) and video / audio encoding on linux (and basic desktop duty on liux, like word processing, chat, surf, watch movies) has:

asus uATX mobo w/ nVidia onboard graphics and sound (around $60)
amd athlon 64 x2 4000 processor (the lowest / cheapest socket AM2, around $50)
2 gigs of ram (OCZ that was supposed to cost me $18 after rebate, but i had to pay $70 for it and haven't seen the rebate yet...)
Western Digital 160G sata hard drive (around $55)
LG dvd burner, plain ol' IDE interface (no more than $40)

add a power supply, decent mouse & keyboard (in my case, both microsoft), a few fans (i love the Arctic Cooling stuff), and it's done.  runs xubuntu 8.04 (64-bit version) wonderfully, never gets too hot, and was cheap to put together.

i've stuck with ASUS / AMD / nVidia and haven't had issues at all.  the LG i'm 80% happy with.

### Post by markbuntu on 2008-07-02
SATA for everything, hard drives, DVD, CD. It is far faster than PATA and reduces clutter inside the machine with its tiny little cables. Get a mobo with at least 4 or 6 SATA connectors. All the new fast DVD drives are SATA, even the blue-ray.

Your processes will run faster the less time they have to wait for the drive.
