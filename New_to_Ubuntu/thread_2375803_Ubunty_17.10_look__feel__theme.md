### Post by drs305 on 2017-10-27
You can install gnome-tweak-tool to give you different themes.
You can create/check some keyboard shortcuts via the Settings menu: Settings > Devices > Keyboard
If you are booting into 17.10 default (Wayland) you will find that many window tweaking tools (compiz) and shortcuts (xmacro) will no longer work. You can boot 17.10  into Unity which may give you more of what you are looking for:
[https://itsfoss.com/use-unity-ubuntu-17-10/]("https://itsfoss.com/use-unity-ubuntu-17-10/")
