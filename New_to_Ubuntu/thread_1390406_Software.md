### Post by audiomick on 2010-01-25
Using F Spot, you are looking for the "import" function. It depends on your computer finding the camera when you plug it in, of course. If you have a card reader, take the card out of the camera and read it from there. It is probably faster and might be more reliable than reading out of the camera, according to a clever book I have. I do it that way, and find it very comfortable.

I don't know off hand where the import function is, as I don't have it in front of me right now, but it is obvious. You click on that, then choose folder and choose your camera. The default is to copy the files into the "photos" folder in your /home. If you have the photos on the computer already, you can still include them in the F Stop catalogue by importing and unchecking the "copy to photos" box.
