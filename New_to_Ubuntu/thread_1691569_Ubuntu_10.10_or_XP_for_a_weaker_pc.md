### Post by wep940 on 2011-02-21
I agree with mastablasta - just the specs alone for your computer showed it should run any flavor of ubuntu without trouble, and as they said, I would go with regular ubuntu myself.  I also agree on Skype, especially since you are trying to do video.  I would check the xorg log file to see if it shows a driver, such as radeon, being loaded by default (the open driver).  Then I would check for updates to 2 drivers - your video card but also your webcam.  Either of those could be causing problems.  Another thing to also consider - your CPU is fine for Ubuntu, but it may not quite be enough for adding audio/video Skype on top of that.  Skype will eat the CPU, and remember you have networking running, etc., at the same time.  What type of internet connection are you using - broadband or DSL??
