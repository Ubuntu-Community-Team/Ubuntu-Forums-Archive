### Post by terabyte1 on 2009-02-17
I have Ubuntu 8.10 on my machine - it will install with no problems at all. You can even dual-boot it with Vista until you are sure, but obviously, it is easier to install Intrepid Ibex (8.10) as a sole machine ie single boot machine. Just follow the prompts and you won't go far wrong.


When you reboot the machine it will download maybe quite a few files via the Synaptic File Manager when you click on the red arrow on the bar at the foot of your page. This too is pleasantly easy. What you will find noticeable is the lack of viruses attacking your machine, and although you don't need to download a free Antivirus program (ClamAV), one is there should you feel you need more security. The ideal Firewall on Ubuntu is Firestarter, and if you follow the notes in the help section, it is pretty easy to set up as well.


Best Wishes and Cheers!


terabyte1;)
