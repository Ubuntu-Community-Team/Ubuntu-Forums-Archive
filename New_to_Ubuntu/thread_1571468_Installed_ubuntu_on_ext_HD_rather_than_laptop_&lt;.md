### Post by beew on 2010-09-09
Actually it is tremendously cool. I have a Ubuntu installation on a 40G external drive and I get a fully portable system. I can plug it in different computers and I get an instant Ubuntu system working. The beauty is that it is not limited by things such as ram. In my own laptop there is only 1g of ram, but if I plug in the desktop at work suddenly I have 4Gs. How do you beat that? :)

Since you haven't done much why not just do a new installation on your internal drive and you can either keep the portable install or erase it with gparted.
