### Post by LowSky on 2009-03-05
Any linux distro is going to handle games the same way.. Your biddest issue is going to be getting directX support, as there isn't any, since it is a microsoft application. Some games may work is they support OpenGL, best bet is to check out CEgada or WineHQ to see the current ratings on playable games. Many PC gamers will dual boot just to be able to play their games at better rates or at all.


Voice and Video chat you can do through Skype and Pidgin (Pidgin to a lesser degree), Pidgin can be use for all major instant messaging brands.

As for the BlackBerry, I have a 8330 Curve and can transfer files between Pc and device just fine. I dont sync it with my Email box or Contacts so I cant answer for that support. I hear their is a way to get the thing to be used as a modem for internet use, but never tried it (as I dont really need it)
