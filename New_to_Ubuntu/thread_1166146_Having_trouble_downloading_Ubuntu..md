### Post by Cypher on 2009-05-21
Those businesses and institutions are providing storage to hold the Ubuntu ISO so that we don't overload Ubuntu's server.

So you should choose the one closest to you to get the fastest download speed.

You will want to download the Desktop .ISO file and then burn that to a CD. Boot your laptop with that CD to see if Ubuntu is happy with your configuration.

I would strongly suggest you backup all of your vital data before playing with Linux. If you are a newbie, it's VERY easy to hit the wrong button and blow everything away from your machine.

So before doing anything, ask questions and understand what you are doing..

### Post by QIII on 2009-05-21
Danger Will Robinson!

If you just got the computer there is one thing I would do BEFORE I started working at getting Ubuntu installed (and dual booting one disk can be a tricky proposition!)

If your computer came with only the typical restore disks you get from an OEM, DON'T do anything yet!

You are entitled to a full copy of the OS from the OEM.  Not just the restore disks, but a full copy of Vista with the Microsoft seal.  Contact your OEM and get that in your hands first.

It is really easy to make a mistake if you are unfamiliar with computers in general, and Linux distributions in particular.

You could end up borking your whole system and not even being able to use, or restore, Vista.

Although I'd love to tell you to trash that Microsoft garbage right off, I won't.  You spent a lot of money for it.

Get your copy of the complete Vista installation disk first.

Then come back here.  There are a lot of us who can help you through the process.
