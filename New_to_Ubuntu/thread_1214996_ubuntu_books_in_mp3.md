### Post by starcraft.man on 2009-07-16
> **shadowseeker said:**
> does anyone out there know if any of these "how to use ubuntu" books are availible as audio books so i can listen to em while i work ???

I just had a quick look through audible.com, amazon.ca (and com) and I think the answer is no. I know of many good books that offer a comprehensive guide, I don't know of any that are spoken word. You might want to try iTunes, that's the only other large retailer I can think of such books but I find it unlikely they'd stock a linux audiobook when Amazon does not.

If your interested in a good comprehensive guide to using Ubuntu, I'd recommend the Practical Guide to Ubuntu Linux by Sobell, available at Amazon and other outlets. It's well written and easy to understand, it is comprehensive though at 1200 pages.

If you want something much lighter, you might look at something like Beginning Ubuntu Linux by Keir or the Official Ubuntu Manual both also good. All of these are real books though, not audio.

If the price you want is free, then I suggest you search for good linux podcasts (quick google will find ya some good results) and or simply learn from the free guides offered here and on other sites when your not working.
