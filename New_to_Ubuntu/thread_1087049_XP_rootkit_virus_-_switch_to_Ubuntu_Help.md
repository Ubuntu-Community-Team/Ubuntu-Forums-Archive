### Post by kanikilu on 2009-03-04
Any existing malware shouldn't be an issue when you switch. Personally, if I knew I had an infected computer, I wouldn't connect to the internet on it - if possible, just download and burn the CD from another computer.

If that's not possible, I would just spend the minimum amount of time connected (certainly don't do any personal email, shopping, banking, anything like that) to get the ISO and burn it.

Obviously back up any important data to your external hard drive before installing Ubuntu. Don't try to copy the whole drive, most of it will be useless to you after installing anyways (e.g. Program Files). Just copy things like "My Documents", music, pictures, videos, Office documents, things like that.

Once you have everything backed up (and verify that it's complete), unplug the external drive and put the Live CD in and reboot the computer. 

If your computer isn't already set to try to boot off the CD/DVD drive, then you may need to press a button immediately at start up to select a boot device. This can vary by manufacturer, for instance it's F12 on Dell's, but it should say somewhere on the first screen you see after turning the computer on.

After that, you can follow one of the walkthroughs to install, for instance: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/GraphicalInstall](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/GraphicalInstall)

I believe those instructions have you resize your existing drive so that you can dual-boot Ubuntu and Windows. That's certainly an option, but if you just want to blow away Windows, choose the "erase entire disk" option - just to emphasize, that last option will make anything previously on your hard drive unrecoverable, so make sure your backup is thorough and up-to-date.

If you have problems or more specific questions and can't find answers by doing a search of the forums, just ask and someone should be able to help you out.
