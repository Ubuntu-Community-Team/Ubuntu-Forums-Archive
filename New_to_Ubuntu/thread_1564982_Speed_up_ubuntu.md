### Post by snowpine on 2010-08-31
Welcome to the forums!

I googled the links to [Puppy]("http://puppylinux.org") and [CrunchBang]("http://crunchbanglinux.org") for you; I would at least give them a test-drive before sinking too much more time into Ubuntu Netbook (which in my limited experience is one of the very slowest Linux distributions).

That being said, there are many tips and tricks to speed up your existing Ubuntu install. Perhaps the biggest change you can make is to install a lightweight desktop environment or windows manager (Xfce, LXDE, openbox, icewm, etc). You can do this without a reinstall by using the Ubuntu Software Center, then logging out, choosing Sessions from the login screen, and selecting the newly-installed DE/WM.

I have 2 netbooks with similar specs (Atom 270 with 1gb ram and integrated Intel graphics) and please understand it will never be a "fast" computer; due to the power-saving features of the Atom 270 chip, it is roughly comparable in performance to a high-end Pentium 3.
