### Post by nu2this on 2008-09-24
Try the latest version of VLC it has the equalizer you are talking about.
To get the latest version just do a search for VLC here in the forums the how to's are in here.
However to get to the Vlc equalizer is a trip so:
1.open your favorite calibration music with VLC
2.go to tools on the vlc toolbar click it select Extended Settings
3.Set it to your likings then type the  the numbers on your equalizer on gedit like so 3.6 5.5 11.0  with a space between each number until you have all 10 of the settings copy those settings
4.Now got to tools again this time select preferences then at the bottom you'll see simple with a dot in it & all with no dot click all
5.click Audio> filters>Equalizer
6.you'll then see a blank space called Band Equalizer paste the settings in that space
7.click save, done!

### Post by treris on 2008-09-26
My personal favorite is Banshee and then especially the 1.3.1 unstable version which is currently available through the banshee team's unstable ppa. ([https://launchpad.net/~banshee-unstable-team/+archive]("https://launchpad.net/~banshee-unstable-team/+archive"))

Now finally it seems to remember from session to session how I sorted my music, which I always do by location and not by artist or album or something.

Other than that it's fast, easy on the eyes and stable, just what I like in a media player. Only downside is that it doesn't do visualizations, but that seems to be coming with the 1.4 release later this year (??).

PS Almost forgot it has an equalizer as well, I think already in the 1.2.1 version, but certainly in the 1.3.1 version I'm running
