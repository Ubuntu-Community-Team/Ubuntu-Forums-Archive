### Post by PinkFloyd102489 on 2008-07-13
Ubuntu installs a generic kernel that works on just about every system. Users are left to their own devices to install their own kernels if they want. A few "special" kernels are in the repos, but if you're looking for something extremely specific, best to Google.

### Post by YaroMan86 on 2008-07-13
Long story short, its likely because compiling can be extremely taxing on your hardware and very time consuming.

And it's not always easy as well. Those three things are not really winning for a user friendly distro like Ubuntu. Gentoo, on the other hand, is a distro that takes kindly to the "installing by compiling" methodology, but its a "power" Linux.
