### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-05-13
I went down the route of using one of those capture devices. The quality left a lot to be desired. It wasn't especially cheap and the darn thing broke under warranty... I took it back to the shop and 2 months later they admitted that they'd lost it... but that's neither here nor there.

I bought a DVD recorder (the sort you plug into your TV), plugged my VCR into the front of it, and ripped the footage from DVD to my hard disk. The quality is almost exactly as good as what went in, and unencrypted DVDs are perfectly Linux-compatible :-)  I definitely recommend this approach as the quality is so much better than any video capture card I've used before.
