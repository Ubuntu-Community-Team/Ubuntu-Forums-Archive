### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-03-17
Copyright owners can catch Bittorrent users by downloading from a copyrighted torrent and noting the IP addresses and times of computers that supplied the file to them. Then after they have completed downloading, they stay on the torrent as a seeder, and note the IP addresses of computers that are downloading the file from them.

There are only two ways currently to evade this:

1. Piggyback off someone else's wireless connection, so it looks like your neighbour has been downloading the torrent. Unethical because your neighbour could get into trouble and illegal in some parts of the world as you are stealing your neighbour's bandwidth.

2. Use a proxy. Unethical because proxies are there to prevent you from being blocked from certain content, not to help you break the law. Ineffective as proxies probably keep a log of activity, and if you've been downloading illegally using a proxy they'll probably track you down themselves!

There is no such thing as an illegal torrent tracker... at least not until the verdict of The Pirate Bay is handed down :-)  Nobody has a problem with you downloading legal content from Bittorrent, and you won't get "caught" doing this either - people and corporations that own copyright on works will only sit on torrents that illegally share their own works, not someone else's.
