### Post by tangibleorange on 2008-08-21
An LTS release is an Long Term Support release. it receives support for 3 years after its release, as opposed to the standard 1.5 years. the following releases have been designated LTS:

6.06
8.04

so your 8.04.1 is an LTS release. no need to worry - you are running the latest and greatest Ubuntu!

### Post by mc4100 on 2008-08-21
There's no difference, LTS simply stands for **L**ong **T**erm **S**upport.
[http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu](http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu)
> Ubuntu is designed with security in mind. You get free security updates for at least 18 months on the desktop and server. With the Long Term Support (LTS) version you get three years support on the desktop, and five years on the server. There is no extra fee for the LTS version, we make our very best work available to everyone on the same free terms. Upgrades to new versions of Ubuntu are and always will be free of charge.
