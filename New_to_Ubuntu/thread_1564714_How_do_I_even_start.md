They will probably have a much more pleasant experience if you increase the ram to 1gb if you want to run the standard ubuntu install with the gnome desktop manager. Otherwise you should look into lubuntu or some of the more minimalistic distros. You can find more info at distrowatch.com.

### Post by NUboon2Age on 2010-08-30
Ubuntu and some of its derivatives like Puppy (for low resource machines) or Mint might work well since they are very user friendly.  How much memory does the machine have?  With only 384M Ubuntu Netbook Edition is a definite possibility.  

Someone else just recommended Qimo to me (i think its an Ubuntu derivative) since its kid-oriented.  I don't know how it is on low resources.  Peppermint is definitely good also on low resources.

Certainly there are all kinds of browsers (but not M$ Internet Exploder) available, most notably Firefox, and Open Office works reasonably well, (or for just word processing part AbiWord is nice).  Its all fairly user-friendly so if i were you i'd try it out for yourself and see that its really quite doable.

### Post by migs73 on 2010-08-31
I think your best idea is to make a LiveCD (many,many instructions on how to do that out there) and try it out for yourself, before you decide which distro to go for. This allows you to try them out without installing them, to give you an idea of what you will get. The performance will be reduced, but it is better than other peoples opinions. You can try them on any PC as well, you never know you might just end up with one on your machine if you like it that much!
