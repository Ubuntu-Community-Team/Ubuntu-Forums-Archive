### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-12-22
The panels are governed by the GTK theme, so you need to find out what GTK theme is being used in the screenshots. The number and configuration of panels are not governed by themes, so don't worry, you won't lose any panels.

Okay, it sounds like you're hazy on the different sorts of themes available. Emerald doesn't theme the entire desktop, it just provides a way of theming the window borders and title bars. If you don't see a .emerald file, then you need to drag it onto the theme selection panel of the Appearance program, and possibly click the "Customise" button.

So, Emerald allows glassy window themes. Metacity themes are the old style of window theme. GTK themes control the look of buttons, scroll bars, progress bars, menus, panels etc. Icon and cursor themes are self-explanatory.

I am unsure how to change back to the regular Heliodor (Metacity themes) Compiz engine, except to say that it's generally best done through a program that puts a little applet into your notification area.
