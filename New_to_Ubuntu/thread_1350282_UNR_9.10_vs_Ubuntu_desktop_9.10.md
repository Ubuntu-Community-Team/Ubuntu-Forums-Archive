### Post by b1lancer on 2009-12-09
I too, had to think about which to choose for my netbook. It does have an atom processor, so I decided to give it a try. I do think that overall, the atom processor is not as fast as I think many expect, and I've just found that 9.10 is a bit unstable (my netbook has frozen a couple times in just a few days). 

The biggest drawback of the UNR is the loss of use of the desktop. I can't save things to the desktop but that's ok- i just a different folder. The nicest thing is the layout and look__ it looks awesome!!

The biggest difference I've discovered is the fact that UNR does not have the bottom panel and had a program (Maximus i think it's called) that just maximizes everything and docks it into the upper panel directly. That's not a big deal when it comes to things that shouldn't be maximized, such as an error dialogue box. That's easily fixed-- double click it and it goes back to the little size it should've had. They do this to maximize screen space. 

All in all, UNR is great to look at, works just like the regular desktop. the only thing is 9.10 should be a little bit more stable and it'd be perfect. 

BTW. Be careful when using the updates when it comes to GRUB.

### Post by Slim_Jim on 2010-01-01
I installed UNR on my Aspire One two days ago and so far I like it.  No stability issue and performs well.  I installed the restricted-extras on it and I've been watching video clips on Youtube.  I like the look but I'm not use to that type of work flow yet. Should be OK after a few days.  I've been using the Gnome desktop environment for years and I love it. So really if you are due to install or update a linux distro on your netbook, you could give a try to UNR and you would most likely love it.  However,  If you are happy with your actual setup, I would not recommend to switch to UNR (unless you are like me and like to try new things just for the fun of it).  It's the same Gnome environment but with a different look and feel.  For those wondering if they should install it on their larger notebook,  I would not recommend it.  I would stick to the "normal" desktop.  UNR will be great for new linux netbook users with its cool and appealing look.
