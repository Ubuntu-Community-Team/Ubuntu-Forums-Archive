### Post by ohzopants on 2008-11-13
If you're just transitioning from windows to linux, you should definitely use gnome since it's approximately a gazillion times better. (OK, that's not really true, I just happen to prefer it)

Seriously though, one way to get your "default" taskbar back would be to create a new user, log in as that user and look carefully at what is in the taskbar by default.

Your networking issue appears to be a known bug. See here:
[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/network-manager/+bug/106129](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/network-manager/+bug/106129)

In particular, the 6th comment looks promising.

Hope this helps.
