### Post by avtolle on 2008-07-10
Dapper-->Hardy is a LTS to LTS upgrade, thus a specific exception to the general rule was made to allow the upgrade through update manager. To those with other earlier distributions, such as Edgy or Feisty, the choice is the sequential upgrade or a clean install. From Gutsy to Hardy is the normal "to the next release" upgrade, which may be done directly.

To anyone considering using the update manager approach to go from Dapper to Hardy, please read the specific upgrade instructions on the Ubuntu upgrade page. There is a certain repository which needs to be enabled under Dapper specifically to allow a successful total update of the system before proceeding to the upgrade to Hardy. I don't know, but I have developed an opinion, that many who have had major troubles with this did not enable the additional repository, but just clicked on the update manager to upgrade with bad results, and have ended up either returning to Dapper, doing a clean install of Hardy, or installed an earlier post-Dapper release.

### Post by mdsmedia on 2008-09-09
I eventually got around to biting the bullet and upgrading directly from Dapper to Hardy.

I don't know about the additional repository, but I have a feeling that it was enabled automatically during the upgrade process.

I simply backed everything up and clicked on the upgrade button in the system update module.

The upgrade did take some time, obviously packages had to be downloaded. I have a magazine DVD with Hardy on it and I could have burned an ISO to CD and enabled it as my "download" source but I simply clicked on the upgrade button and went for it. At times the download speed reduced to verrrrry slow, but other than that it went off without a hitch.

Overall it's much better than my experience with the gradual upgrade of my desktop machine from Edgy through Feisty and Gutsy to Hardy. My system feels very clean again and very snappy. The desktop just feels nicer and, having expected a possible slowdown due to updated software requiring updated hardware, because of how my desktop feels in Hardy, now, I'm very pleased to say that, on the contrary, it actually feels quicker with Hardy. Maybe it just cleaned up my system?

Overall...very happy :).
