### Post by audiomick on 2012-06-21
> **Linuxnewbie2000 said:**
>  Does anyone see any problem with a 30Gb hard drive? Does Ubuntu have a minimum size of Hard drive that it likes to have?

I reckon 30 GB is a bit small. It is a size that means you will, sooner or later, have to think about cleaning out some stuff.

On top of that, my experience is that finding a drive that small can be more trouble than it is worth. When I bought my desktop a few years ago, I decided to install one drive exclusively for the OS, and one for /home and other data. The smallest drive I could easily find then was about 60 GB. A couple of months back, I decided that the time was right for the long contemplated step to a SSD for the / partition. The smallest one in the shop at the time was 80 GB.

What I am trying to get at is this: The minumum you need to get an Ubuntu install running is around 10 GB. If you are sure that your drive space usage is so small that the question is warranted, then just buy the best space for the money compromise you can find at the small end of the scale. SSDs, for instance, tend to get a bit dearer again below a certain size. What ever you end up with, it is likely to be big enough by a long way, at least for the bare system.

If the reason for the question is that you have a working 30 GB drive and are unsure if it is still big enough these days, yes, it is. It doesn't leave you a real lot of space for careless accumulation of data, but it is definitely enough for a functional Ubuntu install.
