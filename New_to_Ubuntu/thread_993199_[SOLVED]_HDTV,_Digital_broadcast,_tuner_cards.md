### Post by Thelasko on 2008-11-25
> **Hospadar said:**
> I can't seem to cut through the baby-talk BS on comcast's website, It seems to me they will continue piping analog TV through their lines after analog broadcast is shut off.  Will they also be using a digital signal (for basic cable) that I can get with a digital tuner.
This is REALLY complicated.  The FCC says they have to keep the analogue signal until 2012.  There is really no spec for digital cable.  They can do whatever they want.  The channels on "digital cable" you won't be able to get, because they encrypt it so you need to rent a cable box or [cableCARD.]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cable_card")  (good luck getting them to provide a cableCARD)

Long story short, a buddy of mine has basic cable with Comcast and an HDTV.  He can get a handful of digital channels with it.  Most of the digital channels are local broadcast channels which are free anyway.  Keep in mind, that this is purely up to your cable company.  They don't have to give you any digital channels if they don't want to.  Most of them are using the digital switch over as a way to make a few extra bucks by charging extra for digital.

I really wish the FCC would step in and force them to provide the digital signal at no additional cost.
