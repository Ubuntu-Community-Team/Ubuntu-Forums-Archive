Have I understod you correctly.No, it's worse than that. You can buy little gadgets called "hardware keyloggers" that plug in between the keyboard and the main box, that will capture and record every keystroke -- regardless of the operating system you use! It's relatively easy to install such gadgets on public computers, without anyone suspecting that they're there. If you happen to run into such a situation, your banking passwords and other data are toast no matter how you try to secure them.

There's more. Almost all public installations send data to their local servers as clear text that can be "sniffed" again regardless of the system in use. And once more your secrets are out in the wild...

In short, there's no way to safely use public systems for private business without incurring significant risks! This isn't being paranoid, just realistic. Incidentally, using cell phones for sensitive tasks is equally risky. It's a dangerous world we live in...
