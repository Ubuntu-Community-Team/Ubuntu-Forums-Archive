### Post by BenAshton24 on 2010-03-27
Sure you can... It depends on what you're looking to do... do you just want to make your own GNU/Linux based OS or are you looking to make something from scratch. If it's the latter then you've got a lot of work to do.

If you just want to do something basic then you could customize ubuntu and make your own Live CD: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/LiveCDCustomization](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/LiveCDCustomization)

You might also be interested in LFS: [http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/](http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/)

### Post by Paqman on 2010-03-27
You can also try using the [minimal ISO]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD"). This installs a basic command-line version of Ubuntu, which you can then add whatever packages you want from the repos.

You could then use Remastersys to create a custom ISO to distribute.
