### Post by ibjsb4 on 2013-10-15
Ubuntu Classic is like Mint.  You could move panels and launchers to where you want.

To install Classic to your current install, open a terminal and enter:

```
sudo apt-get install gnome-session-fallback
```

At boot (the login window) click on the icon and choose which desktop you wish to run.

### Post by newb85 on 2013-10-15
Unfortunately, the Unity launcher is on the left side and cannot be moved.  (Canonical preferred uniformity to flexibility in their design of Unity.)  As far as I know, your options are limited to installing a Desktop Environment that doesn't rely so heavily on the left side of the screen.

I don't know what you didn't like about Mint, or even which version of Mint you tried, but the Cinnamon and Mate DE's can both be installed in Ubuntu.  (Cinnamon is in the default repositories so it's a little easier to install.)

From the default repos, Cairo-Dock, LXDE, KDE, and XFCE are all options, too.
