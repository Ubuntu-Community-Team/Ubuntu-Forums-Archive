### Post by blazingnikons on 2009-03-17
This is a post for all the newbs like me who are sipping their first cup of Ubuntu/Linux.

After installing Google Earth (see: [http://ubuntu-tutorials.com/2008/11/14/install-google-earth-on-ubuntu-810-intrepid-ibex/](http://ubuntu-tutorials.com/2008/11/14/install-google-earth-on-ubuntu-810-intrepid-ibex/) ), I found that upon zooming-in the map would fade to white to the point where I couldn't see anything.  Google Earth would white-out as I zoomed in.

The fix is ridiculously simple.  While in Google Earth, go to View > Atmosphere  and uncheck Atmosphere.  For some reason, my Asus Extensa 5630Z running 64-bit Intrepid didn't like that option.

I thought I'd share, as it took me a long time of surfing Google to find the fix for Google Earth's blanking screen problem on zoom.

Thanks!:D

### Post by Shampyon on 2009-03-19
I just had a very similar problem. I unticked "Atmosphere" and it didn't work. Then I zoomed out and noticed something...

I'd enabled *Labels//GalleryNASA//Earth City Lights*. The white I was seeing was the city lights! Once I unticked that everything was fine.

So for those of you who tried the atmosphere fix and still had problems, check if you've got the *City Lights* layer activated.
