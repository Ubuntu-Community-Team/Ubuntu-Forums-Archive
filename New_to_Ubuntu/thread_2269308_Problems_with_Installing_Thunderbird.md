### Post by JeQhdMD on 2015-03-14
The user name is usually just your first and last name . . . and doesn't include your email data.   There is another window with fields for the other required settings:

>  smtp config
>  imap or pop3 config
>  email name
>  short name (like vgilbert)

Most of the above 4 fields will be pre-populated by the Thunderbird wizard.

IF not clear, just goto Youtube and watch one of the several excellent demos of Thunderbird setup.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-03-14
> **JeQhdMD said:**
> The user name is usually just your first and last name . . . and doesn't include your email data...

Well... maybe.

The username is whatever is assigned/used by the actual mail provider, and it often does include a domain name, e.g., [email]PedanticMan@MailCorp.com[/email]. 

Be sure the correct ports are set in Thunderbird. That can generate error messages that don't say anything about ports. I've also seen the Thunderbird account wizard assign the wrong ports while getting the rest of the account info correct.

Ditto encryption method.

### Post by bonzo2 on 2015-03-15
I concur that manual configurations are always best with Thunderbird as it just never seems to get every setting right.  

If this thread is still accurate ([http://www.certforums.com/threads/btinternet-imap-settings.47048/](http://www.certforums.com/threads/btinternet-imap-settings.47048/)) your user name should include your log in name and domain, like this:
Username = [EMAIL="xxxx@yahoo.com"]xxxx@yahoo.com[/EMAIL] [xxxx@btinternet.com also worked fine for the commenter]

Thunderbird manages several email addresses for me, some of which require only the name before the @ symbol, and some of which require the domain, too (just as BuzzingRobot said). As stated upthread, this is determined by your email provider.

Good Luck
