### Post by jem7v on 2008-06-15
I noticed a big speed difference between 7.10 and 8.04 as well, and it's nice to have the latest versions of all the applications.  Other than that, most of the differences are pretty subtle; I think they're mostly in the backend rather than up front where we notice them.  The online upgrade from 7.10 to 8.04 went so smoothly for me that after my computer rebooted, I wasn't even sure if it had upgraded at all until I checked the version number!  Everything worked and everything was still there. (That was my experience, at least; I'm not sure how many problems other users had.)

That said, in my first few weeks running 8.04 it was a lot more prone to mild crashing than 7.10 was (nothing requiring a reboot, but enough that I had to Ctrl+Alt+Backspace to restart the XServer), but now that they've had a couple months to release updates and bug-fixes it's quite stable again.  I haven't had anything strange happen for a while now.
