### Post by CharlesA on 2010-09-21
It's sorta like that.

Most programs are either in:

/bin - binaries
/sbin - system binaries
/usr/bin - user binaries
/usr/sbin - cannot recall off the top of my head.

Most of your documents and settings are stored in yer home directory.

Here's a [page]("http://www.debianadmin.com/linux-directory-structure-overview.html") with a bit of a better explanation.

### Post by SmokeyThePanda on 2010-09-21
Because, if the system looked in your home directory too often, it would likely mess up your /home partition, assuming you have it on a different partition, like most people. Besides, if you know what you're looking for, it's easy to find.
