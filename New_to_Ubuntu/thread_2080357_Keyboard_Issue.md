I think you had kept your USB keyboard on your HP laptop keyboard so that some keys were pressed on your laptop keyboard which was causing automatic filling of letters.
Or may be some keys of your USB keyboard/laptop keyboard don't 'push' themselves up after being pressed. So, ubuntu assumes that keys have been pressed and so it starts filling them. :)

### Post by John Peschken on 2012-11-05
I am using a USB keyboard with my ASUS netbook trouble free.  No need to disable the built-in keyboard.  I just plugged it in and it works.  The only annoyance I have seen is that when I unplug it, the built-in keyboard comes up in numlock mode.  That makes sense, since the external had numlock on when I shut it down.  It did confuse me for a while, since you can't see what you're typing for a password at login. 

Sorry for the detour! What I'm getting at (FINALLY) is that there does not seem to be anything inherent in Ubuntu that makes USB keyboards misbehave.  My opinion is that you have a bad USB port or a bad keyboard. Trying other ports and keyboards would be the first things to try.
