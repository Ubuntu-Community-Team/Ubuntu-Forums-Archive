### Post by Tony Flury on 2009-02-25
Go to you applications menu - click Add/Remove.
Select Games on the left hand side - and scroll through the list on the right to choose which games you want.

### Post by handy on 2009-02-25
Also, you can run some windows games quite well, & others just about perfectly via Wine.

& this is probably the easiest & cheapest way to do it at the moment:

*_[http://www.playonlinux.com/en/](http://www.playonlinux.com/en/)_*

Beyond that you can pay these people who are absolutely conscientious & dedicated to the FOSS movement:

*_[http://www.codeweavers.com/](http://www.codeweavers.com/)_*

If you ferret around on their site you will find a demo which will allow you to test whether their software works for you, before you pay them the little that they require.

Last, & probably least as far as I'm concerned Transgaming make a product called Cedega.  It works for some games, their support sucks, they are only in it for the money, & they haven't given any code back to the Wine project for some years (to the best of my knowledge).  You can go to their site & pay them, sometimes it is worth it, it all depends on the game you want to play.  Check out the games forums first before you spend your money on any of them.
