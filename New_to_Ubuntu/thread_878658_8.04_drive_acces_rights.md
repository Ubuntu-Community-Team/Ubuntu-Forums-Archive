### Post by silkstone on 2008-08-03
I can't offer a simple solution, but the problem is that FAT32 and NTFS drives don't recognise Linux ownerships and permissions and you cannot change them with chown and chmod. The owner of Windows drives is normally shown as 'root'.

If you copy the files to an Ext3 or Ext2 drive/partition, you will then be able to change the ownership and/or permissions with sudo chmod a+rx or whatever.
