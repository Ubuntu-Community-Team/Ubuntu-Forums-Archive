### Post by adelphos on 2010-01-03
I own that book...

And to be honest, I'd advise you not to buy it. It's not particularly bad, but there is very little in it which is specific to Ubuntu, and it relies heavily upon teaching the command line, and a few of its chapters seem to be written more for system administrators than for the average user. Also, the organization is not the best.

It contains some very short chapters on very advanced topics, making a few of them nearly useless. For instance, only a short chapter on Perl scripting or Apache is not going to help you much...

However, it is rather big, and has lots of solid information in it. Just be aware that none of it is really tailored for Ubuntu 9.10. Some of it may be slightly inaccurate or inapplicable. As a general Linux reference however, it's about average.

Why not just buy a few smaller books on the topics you want to know about in detail, and use internet tutorials for Ubuntu-specific features? For instance, you might get a book on the command line or one on bash scripting in Linux. No book is going to apply to the current Ubuntu release for very long.
