### Post by DuckHook on 2013-01-04
Visited site. It tries to embed a number of scripts which I will not permit due to security concerns (I don't know these sites from Adam), so cannot test for you. Do the sites use a Windows-only or IE-only codec unavailable in Linux-Firefox-Chrome? If so, you are out of luck. If they do work with Linux codecs, then try:

1. closing, then opening your browser,
2. using a different browser,

In general, there are lots of sites that only work with MS IE. These sites will not work with Firefox or Chrome, even in their Windows incarnations, never mind their Linux ones. Rather than pull my hair out trying to make them work, I usually leave a message saying that their shortsightedness and lack of support for other platforms has just lost them a customer (in your case, a listener). Many don't care, but I have received a couple of replies which are at least encouraging. I don't know of any other solution to sites that willingly wall themselves off from open standards.
