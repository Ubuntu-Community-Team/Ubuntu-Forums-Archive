### Post by Double.J on 2012-01-18
Hi there! 

No, unless you are having issues with a device and proprietary drivers are offered int this screen, then it's nothing to worry about. As you know Ubuntu is opensource - that means that it's default set up tries to comply with the ownership licences of various aspects of the system - it's why you have to add the restricted extras package for media codecs and the like - because these are heavily licenced codecs.

In terms of hardware the 2 most common are nvidea video drivers and broadcom wireless drivers. As I say if you aren't having hardware issues its nothing to worry about?

All the best!
