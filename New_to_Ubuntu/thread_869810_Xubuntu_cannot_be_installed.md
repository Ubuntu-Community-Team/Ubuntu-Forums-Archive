### Post by snowpine on 2008-07-25
I also have a 500mhz PIII, and I have successfully run Fluxbuntu, Xubuntu, and even Ubuntu on it. The difference is that I upgraded to 384 mb of ram (ram is cheap).

If you are stuck with 128mb, Xubuntu (using the alternate CD) and Fluxbuntu are both good suggestions, as others have mentioned. I have been experimenting with Crunchbang lately; my first impression is that it is a perfect compromise between Fluxbuntu and Xubuntu. The application selection is very thoughtful, and it even plays Flash videos out of the box. 

ps There is no reason to "downgrade" to 7.10; 8.04 should work just fine.
