I did start to measure boot time last year.

I may have a strange definition for boot time.  I define it as the time from power up to useful working ability.  Windows: power on. POST. wait. wait. 1min 30 sec. login screen. Login wait wait. 3 min desktop shows up, but you can't use your pc for anyting meaningful.  Finally after 5 to 25 additional minutes, the hard drive stops and the computer is actually ready to do meaningful work.  Nice break for employees.  Time lost, therefore, money lost to the company.  As time goes by, with largely ineffective patches, performance degrades to the point that you may as well use your pc for a boat anchor. (except that heavy metals will pollute.)  Or, you can migrate to a Linux version.

Koala: power on POST wait wait login screen wait wait. 1 min. 10 sec since power on hard disk access stops.  Computer up and ready for anything you may throw at it.

Lucid: power on POST wait 1/2 wait Login. Wait 55 sec since power on hard disk access stops.  Computer up and ready for anything you may throw at it.

My machine (your performance may vary)

  Windows: indeterminate, but a long time, which increases over time.
  Karmic:   ~1:10 sec.
  Lucid:     ~55 sec.
