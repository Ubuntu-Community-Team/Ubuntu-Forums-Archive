### Post by Temposs on 2009-01-23
hi twill2

Do you know that Ubuntu already has a weather display program installed by default?

Right click on the clock in the top-right, and go to Preferences. Make sure "Show weather" and "show temperature" are checked. Then click on Locations tab, and make sure you have your location as an entry there. If not, Add your location.  You can also click on the Weather tab, and change the weather settings there.

Alternatively, right-click on a blank section of the panel, and click "Add to Panel". Locate and click "weather report", and click "Add". Then you need to right-click on the newly visible applet to change the preferences for your location. With this applet you can also get a textual forecast, which isn't available with the weather integrated with the clock.

I use the weather feature integrated with the clock, personally.
