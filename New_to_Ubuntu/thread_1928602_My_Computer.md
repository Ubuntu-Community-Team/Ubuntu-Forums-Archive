### Post by Penguin360 on 2012-02-20
Click Home Folder from the left bar, then click View and choose Status Bar. Now you should be able to see how much space available at the bottom of your Home Folder window.

The terminal commands introduced above are very important, and you will get used to them because you will need them a lot in Ubuntu.

BTW, when you open a terminal window, its icon will appear in the left bar, you can right-click it and choose Keep it in Launcher, so you can just click the icon to load it next time when you need it.

### Post by pbpersson on 2012-02-20
Please note that many people will give you command line answers to your questions because it is faster and they will work in all versions of Ubuntu.

However, (almost) everything you would ever want to accomplish can be done using your mouse.  You don't have to ever use the command line if memorizing all the commands is somewhat overwhelming.  It is up to you how much or how little you use the command line.  Some people really like it.

Just let us know what questions you have as you explore Ubuntu, we feel there is no such thing as a "stupid" question as we were all Ubuntu newbies once upon a time.
