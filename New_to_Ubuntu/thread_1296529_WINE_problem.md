### Post by Unanimated on 2009-10-20
The same way you installed it. Go to Add/Remove and search 'Wine,' then deselect it. After you click apply, it will remove Wine.

Unless you're talking about removing iTunes and all related software. In that case, under Wine click 'Uninstall Wine Software.' And you CAN install iTunes under Wine. Google 'appdb' and search iTunes in the seemingly most related result.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-10-21
Had you gone to the CodeWeavers Application Compatibility site before you installed Wine, you would have seen the page below clearly indicating that Wine will NOT work with the current versions of iTunes -- and saved yourself a lot of trouble:

[http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=1347]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=1347")

In future, suggest you check there first before you attempt to use anything in Wine.
