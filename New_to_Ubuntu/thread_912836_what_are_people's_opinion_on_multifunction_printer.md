### Post by ingeva on 2008-09-07
> **TheUbGuy said:**
> I'd have to second the choice of HP.

There's more than one printer for every need. HP and Epson has very good support for printers/scanners, while Canon does not.

I have and old HP PSC 1215 which worked right out of the box. The printer broke down and since I have an OKI color LED I don't need a new printer, but instead of buying a new multifunction thing I just ordered an Epson scanner which is "guaranteed" to work with Linux. This one can also scan transparencies and film, and you don't get that with a PSC, at leat not with one of the cheaper ones.

For printers you'll be quite safe if you choose PostScript. The PS printers use a generic driver and a text-based Printer Definition file. Some manufacturers supply one, but there's an abundant selection of PDDs with the Linux distributions.

Another advantage of PS printers: They are more accurate with vectorised output.

It was interesting to observe that when I used the PSC for scanning, the results were much better with Linux than with Windows and the driver that came with the printer.
