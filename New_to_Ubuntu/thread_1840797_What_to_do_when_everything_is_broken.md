### Post by MangoLaw on 2011-09-08
Hi guys, 

I'm relatively new to Ubuntu. I've used linux on and off over the last couple of years and I'm a .NET programmer by trade.

I like doing a lot of experimenting with things in Ubuntu, but every time I do I find myself in a familiar situation where I have broken whatever it is that displays the graphics terminal, (Control-Alt-F7).

Most of the time this manifests itself as a black, back-lit screen where I can see and move the mouse but nothing else. I find myself doing the following:

1. Control-Alt-F1. 
2. $ lynx -accept_all_cookies google.com
3. Hours of fruitless searching
4. Hours of trying to get back where I started.

This has ended up happening to me when I've been: configuring extra monitors, trying out kde, playing around with desktop effects, experimenting with xmonad, etc. At some point later I usually give these things a try again, and sometimes I get them figured out. 

The whole process is very exhausting, and I know this is a really vague problem but I guess I am hoping to compile myself a great big list of tips, tricks and things to do when whatever I have just done has killed my windowing system. 

So tell me, do you find yourself in this situation a lot too? If so, what do you do?
