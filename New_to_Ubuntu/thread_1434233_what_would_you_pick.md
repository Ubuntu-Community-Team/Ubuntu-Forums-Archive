AMD all the way! (Sorry Ozy! LOL!)  While I don't hate Intel, I've always had very good luck with AMD.  My desktop (which I built around 3 years ago) has an AMD 64 3200+ and it's been running nearly 24/7 for all that time with no problems.  Intel runs well enough, though.

I agree with Ozy about the NVidia, though.  I have an NVidia card in the desktop, and it runs really well.  You might want to check on compatibility with some of the newest cards, though, if you're going to go the "bleeding edge" route.

As far as 32/64 bit goes, I'd go with 64 bit.  *All* my computers are 64 bit based, both Intel and AMD. If you're going to build a machine (and can afford it), why not go for the gusto?  My motto is, "Do it right the first time," and you won't need to do upgrades in the future (for a while :p ).

### Post by synicalx on 2010-03-20
Considering whats on the market at the moment and what hardware you need to run Ubuntu, AMD is your best bet for price/performance. Case in point, the new Athlon II CPU's are way cheaper than similar Intel chips and can overclok much better as well (if thats of any interest to you).

### Post by The Real Dave on 2010-03-20
Intel, but only because I have no experience with AMD ;)

Nvidia, because the drivers are much better ATM

And 64bit, regardless of the amount of RAM your installing. 64bit will be faster on a 64bit capable computer, which most are now. There's no point sticking with 32bit, because on the user end, very little is different. The one thing you will need to do is grab 64bit Flash from Adobe's site, no big deal.

My sig rig, a 4 y/o Pentium IV with just 1.4Gb of RAM runs 64bit Ubuntu blazingly fast, I've had no problems at all. 32bit Ubuntu 9.10 was taking >1min to boot, 64bit 9.10 is 22seconds. The RAM usage is up by ~50Mb, but I'm sure you can spare that ;)

And yes, 32bit Ubuntu will see all your RAM thanks to PAE, but RAM through PAE takes a performance hit (I've no idea how much) as opposed to with 64bit
