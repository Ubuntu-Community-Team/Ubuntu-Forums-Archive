### Post by seawolf167 on 2011-09-21
Your internet bandwidth is limited to the plan you purchase through your ISP. The only thing a new router might improve is your LAN speeds, however, unless you have *a lot* of computers constantly networking between eachother, you'll probably not notice a difference.

Now upgrading from a class 'b' router to a class 'g' or 'n' router would be a huge bump for a wireless connection to your router... you still need the bandwidth from your ISP though for WAN connections though.

### Post by Lisiano on 2011-09-21
Only way a new router might improve your connection is if the router experiences hundreds or thousands of connections. This scenario might be prominent when you are torrenting or seeding Linux ISO's or any popular torrent, some routers allow only a few hundred while some upto a few thousand but the more connections, the slower it will get, not only for you but for everyone else who lives in your household.
So in short no, a new router will not improve your connection unless it is being used in high-load scenarios.

### Post by quadproc on 2011-09-21
I have recently searched for router information because my old one has become unreliable.  Unfortunately, all of the ones that I read about have some negatives so it becomes a matter of choosing which one has negatives that don't matter to you.

Incidentally, the router's actual speed capability may not be mentioned in the literature!  Most routers will be fine at speeds up to about 6 Mbits/sec but  a faster connection (such as fiber optic, most cable TV services, etc.) may be throttled by such a router.  If you have a wideband connection then getting a router which can handle your ISP's speed offering may be appropriate.
