### Post by BlinkinCat on 2011-10-26
Hi again,

I asked this question at "ask ubuntu" where there was an answer posted. It appears that the problem lies with the installed proprietary driver. Solution was to uninstall it. Sure enough the problem with the top panel was corrected.

However, I then found that the Unity Launcher had disappeared, as well as the information in the right hand side of the top panel. Rather than search for a further fix for Unity, I decided to cut my losses and reinstall Unity and do without Gnome Shell altogether. As it happens, I am very pleased with how Unity is working for me.

I will mark this Question as solved as the original problem can certainly be rectified by the removal of the proprietary driver.

Thanks all - :)
