### Post by Bucky Ball on 2009-11-06
Just wondering what exactly it is you want to do from day to day with this machine ...

It is always a good idea to get VERY specific on about what you are intending to achieve with your build. 

You say a budget computer, well, stick to that. If you are watching vids, listening to music, Skype, internet, what you have here is more than adequate. Why exactly do you need a 'better' processor? It is only better if it is better suited to the task at hand and if you intend doing the things I mentioned earlier you have plenty already.

If you want to upgrade a component, ask why. My desktop box is an AV dedicated machine with 2Gb of RAM and an old Intel P4 with hyperthreading. Not a problem for pro-level audio and video editing. Your 2.8 dual core leaves that in the weeds so hopefully you get my point.

Don't spend more money to get bigger, more powerful, supposedly 'better' unless you can come up with a good reason. If you are looking to launch a fleet of satellites, well, you might want to beef it up a little ... ;-)

* SATA uses a LOT less power than IDE. Not sure where people are getting their facts from here. SSD considerably less that that. Go to the calculator link I provided earlier and just calculate one IDE drive, reset then calculate one SATA drive. Subtract the initial 38W for the single socket dual core CPU. SATA uses about 5-6W and IDE about 15-16W. The GreenPower SATA drives use less again.

### Post by Paqman on 2009-11-06
For running VMs you want two things: a ton of RAM, and a fast multi-core processor. You look like you're aiming for 4GB of RAM, which should be enough to get acceptable performance out of Win7 in a VM. I ran it alright on a 3GB system (3.3GHz dual core), but it was pretty sluggish.

If there are Windows apps you need to use, you're probably better off running XP instead of Win7. It'll run much faster, eat up a lot less space on your hard drive, and you'll be able to save a lot of money by buying more modest hardware.

### Post by synicalx on 2009-11-06
I can see a few things that I would change with your build;

1. A 9600gt with 512mb of V RAM will suffice for your purposes, it can run Crysis on Medium-High settings if your other hardware is up to par.

2. Drop that dual core and get am AMD X3 720 Phenom II, it's a tri-core processor that is the cost of a mid range dual core and it has a decent clock speed to boot. Your mobo will handle it just fine as well.

3. May as well pay the extra $5 for 1066mhz DDR2, considering the X3 can handle it and it'll go nicely with a 64bit Windows 7 install as well as an VM stuff your doing

4. That case is fantastic and I use one myself, but for a budget PC it's overkill. Save yourself $20-$30 and get a Coolermaster Elite, you can even get one that looks like the CM690 if that look is your style :D

But overall a decent build, 7 and Ubuntu would run fine on that with no changes and even better with a few more changes!

Your components will fit in either case just fine - some after market CPU coolers and water cooling systems won't go so well in the Elite or indeed the CM690, but by the sounds of things you won't have any need for overclocking.
