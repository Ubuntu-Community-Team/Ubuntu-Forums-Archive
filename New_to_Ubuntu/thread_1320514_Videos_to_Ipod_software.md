### Post by jimmy the saint on 2009-11-09
For now, try this software

[http://www.floola.com/modules/wiwimod/](http://www.floola.com/modules/wiwimod/)

It installs to the ipod.

  If you are serious about sticking with Linux, when you replace your ipod, look at a player made by Sansa.  They offer devices every bit as good, but a lot cheaper and without being tied to itunes.  They operate like disks so you can drag and drop or even set them up to be synced by programs like Rhythmbox, just like an ipod would with itunes, but without the encryption/DRM/Drama.  I paid 40 bucks for mine.  It supports microSD, recording, FM radio, audiobooks (meaning resume), video, pictures, etc.

Ipods are popular, but are pretty limited in their ability to interoperate with non-itunes environments.
