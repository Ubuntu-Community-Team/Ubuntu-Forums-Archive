### Post by uberdonkey5 on 2008-08-27
This sounds like a really bad installation. Not sure if I can help you completely. I know when I tried to reinstall ubuntu I was trying to get a good image (copy of ubuntu) on a disk by downloading over the internet, and I don't know if it was because of a slow connection, but none of the copies worked (I tried 6).

Your computer definately has capacity to run ubuntu easily (infact, why are you not using 8.04 Hardy? I think there are security problems with 7, although this will be solved when you check for updates).

My advice (though I am no expert) is:
1. try to borrow a CD of ubuntu that you KNOW is working, from a friend and reinstall. (thats what worked for me)
2. try another download with a fast connection and reinstall

Failing this you can get a disk posted to you (minimal cost, and worth it, cos then you have what you know is a good copy (you should ALWAYS keep a working copy on CD near by)

Alternatively, some books on ubuntu actually supply a DVD or CD in the back for installation. If you are a beginner it is worth having a reference book anyway, so it doesn't cost you anything.

Hope this helps. (P.S. I think just trying to bypass systems to get the thing running may cause major problems in the future.. you should really only have minor problems with running devices on a modern laptop).
