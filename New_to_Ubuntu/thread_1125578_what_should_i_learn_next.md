As suggested there is no real set order.  However as a system administrator I recommend that you take a look around the filesystem and especially your logs and note where things are and what is normal for your system, as well as what is not.

Just having an idea of what is normal and where everything is will help a lot in the case of errors or security issues.  It will also make later configuration changes much easier.

Also a wise man (or woman) would make sure to learn the art of backups as soon as possible. ;)

### Post by brunogirin on 2009-04-14
As advised above, what to do next depends on what you are interested in. Here are a few suggestions:

Learn how your system works by having a look at some of the interesting bits such as the logs files in /var/log; the configuration files in /etc; the system info in /proc and /sys.

Learn how to make your system work your way by programming it using bash, perl, tcl, python or whatever language takes your fancy. Or learn some of the Linux/Unix power tools like awk, sed, grep.

Learn how to do cool stuff with the software you have on your machine, such as graphic design with GIMP or Inkscape, image manipulation with ImageMagick, sound recording with Audacity, etc.

Learn how to do the boring stuff efficiently with OpenOffice or Planner.

The list is endless. Happy learning!
