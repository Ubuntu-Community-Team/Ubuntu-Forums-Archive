### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-05-30
I've not personally found a service that really works.  I tried a couple when I was first working with upgrading an old PC to run Vista, and in both cases, even though I knew for a FACT that the machine had the very latest drivers, in both cases, the products claimed that they had newer drivers and FOR A FEE, they would be glad to download them for me.

I contacted both services, asking for a free trial of only ONE driver -- to check out that it really was more current than the drivers I had. In both cases, I got no response.

I can't vouch personally for the recommended service, but it's a relatively simple AND FREE process to search the net with google for newer drivers for your hardware.

If you find free services, at least you only run the risk of wasting time (not money) downloading "old" drivers hidden inside "new" zip files or self-exracting archives.

### Post by freeman2000 on 2009-05-30
> **Testrum said:**
> Is there a program that I can use to update all the drivers for Windows XP?

I'm looking for one that isn't freeware, but not to expensive.


Here's one that is not only free, but you already have it!  It's called the Windows Update Manager.  Just connect to [www.updates.Microsoft.com](www.updates.Microsoft.com) and run it.  It will not only get you the updates to Windows, but also many of the driver updates for your computer.  You can also try the website of your computer's manufacturer, whereby you would just type in the model number and it would know what drivers you needed.  You can also go to [www.download.com](www.download.com) or [www.filehippo.com](www.filehippo.com) for many software updates and driver updates - all free.  For strictly drivers though, the all-inclusive site is [www.drivermax.com](www.drivermax.com)   Good luck.
