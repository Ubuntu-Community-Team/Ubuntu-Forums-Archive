### Post by linuxford on 2008-12-09
Let me try to help... here is what I would do.

First right click on the little speaker icon (bottom right of your monitor"), and select "Open volume control." This brings up a menu called "Volume control." On the bottom right of this "Volume control" menu, click "preferences." 

This brings up another menu called "Volume control preferences", and enable everything (all have checks). 

And then click Close on the same "preferences" menu.  

Now play with the initial "Volume control" menu, and figure out which of the channels are the headphones and speakers, and play around with both so they are both the same loudness whether you are listening to the speakers or the headphones. (Also try to mute some of the channels to see if it gets rid of the hiss, you may be getting some feedback, I got this when using a mic) Now, once you have that set, then you should only need to mess with the "master" channel to increase/decrease the volume of all at any time.

Now close all the menus.

Now right click on the little speaker on the bottom right of your screen again, but now pick "preferences". Now make sure that "Master" is highlited. Now when you adjust your volume, it will now adjust the main volume.

Don't know if this helps, I am pretty green myself.
