### Post by DigitalAlcatraz on 2011-07-31
You could always drag and drop. Windows 7 doesn't only gives options to move to other attached devices, not just anywhere. You will have to copy and paste or drag and drop if you want to move it to a specific folder on your flash drive, or even if you want to move it to any other drive in your computer. You could use Ubuntu Tweak to add more options:guitar:

### Post by Paqman on 2011-07-31
You can move it to any attached device through the "Send to" rather than "Copy to" option. A bit counter-intuitive IMO, but works just the same.

### Post by plucky on 2011-07-31
Open **View > Extra Pane** and you can Copy To or Move To **Other Pane** where the other pane can be any Folder.

Good luck
