### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-12-23
Ubuntu has a desktop guide. It is accessed through the power/ocg menu. It is called Ubuntu Help.

You may be confused about the use of the global menu in Ubuntu's Unity user interface. To create more screen space the panel that holds an application's File, Edit, View, etc., menus has been moved out of the application window and into the top panel and at the left. It is hidden from sight until we mouse over the left end of the top panel.

If you want you can go to System Settings>Appearance>Behaviour tab and under the heading Show the menus for a window, tick "In the window's title bar." And then there is this for the future:

[http://www.webupd8.org/2014/12/unity-to-probably-get-option-to-always.html](http://www.webupd8.org/2014/12/unity-to-probably-get-option-to-always.html)
