### Post by gordintoronto on 2017-06-17
Is the computer already running Lubuntu, and you are thinking of switching to the M.2 SSD?

It seems to be a working configuration, as long as you use 17.04. If the SSD is your only storage, you might consider stepping up to the 240 GB version.

Also note that spinning drives often give a bit of warning about failing, so you have time to make a backup. An SSD generally gives no warning, so you need to be more conscientious about backup.

### Post by oldfred on 2017-06-18
I have an M.2 drive & a HDD.
You do need to check that motherboard supports 2280 which, I think is the longer one. The motherboard may have different screw holes to fit different sizes of M.2.

My system just saw it as another drive in system. And works just like any other drive.
The newer faster NVMe drives are more expensive, and some have had to update UEFI to get them to work.
