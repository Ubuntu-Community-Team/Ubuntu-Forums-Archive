### Post by PaulW2U on 2012-08-24
> **stompinround said:**
> It looks absolutely horrible, the fonts are the most annoying thing, they are so chunky and un-smooth that they are nearly unreadable.  It does not have the fit and finish of anything near the quality of Unity nor KDE on either of the other two distros i tried.

In System Settings | Application Appearance | Fonts there is a setting called 'Use anti-aliasing'.  I have this set to Enabled with 'Hinting style' set to slight and 'Use sub-pixel renering' set to RGB under the 'Configure' button.  Try changing these settings. You might need to log out and back in again or may be reboot to see the changes.

I too used to see poor fonts on my old PC and changing these settings made a big difference. It might be a good idea to make a note of the current settings in case your changes make things worse.
