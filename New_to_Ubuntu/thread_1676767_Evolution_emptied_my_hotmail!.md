### Post by HereInOz on 2011-01-27
The only way to redress this situation is to email it all to yourself again, but, before you do, go into Evolution and set it to "Leave messages on server" in the account editor.

This will stop Evolution from deleting messages from Hotmail.  Yes, it would have been good to know this beforehand, but it is done now:-(

So you have the choice to make the settings change and then simply let your new email accumulate in Hotmail, or take the extra step to forward all the downloaded messages to [email]yourself@hotmail.com[/email] and that will put them all back, albeit with the sender appearing as you in every case.

### Post by HereInOz on 2011-01-31
It is not weird at all.  The Evolution POP client, by default, deletes the messages from the POP server unless you tell it not to.  

This is actually the default behaviour of most POP clients, except the latest offerings from Redmond, which have "Leave messages on server" turned on by default.

This is why Nalced lost all the email in his inbox, but the email in the other folders remained untouched.

It is interesting to note the origin of the name POP. The letters mean Post Office Protocol.  Think about it:  This means that when you collect your mail from the Post Office (your mail server), the mail no longer resides at the Post Office - it is now in your hand (your POP client).

This is why it is not weird
