### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-11-29
If you don't like Unity, the best bet is to look at a different distro. Staying with Ubuntu but not wanting Unity will be fighting a losing battle.


[LIST]
[*]If you wish to remain in the Ubuntu family, try [Xubuntu]("http://www.xubuntu.org/") or [Kubuntu]("http://www.kubuntu.org/").
[*][Mint]("http://www.linuxmint.com/"), while not in the Ubuntu family, is Ubuntu-based.
[*]Other popular distros are [OpenSUSE]("http://www.opensuse.org/") and [Fedora]("http://fedoraproject.org/"), which have nothing to do with Ubuntu.
[/LIST]

Having said all that, the desktop is changing. Microsoft is about to come out with Windows 8, which will have a radically different approach (making Unity seem far less radical than it seems now); and Gnome 3 is a big change from Gnome 2. Apple has already made changes with the iPad, and probably won't take long to make changes to its OSX.
