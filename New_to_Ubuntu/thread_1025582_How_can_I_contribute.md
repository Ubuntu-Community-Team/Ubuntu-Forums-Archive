### Post by Bucky Ball on 2008-12-30
That just about says it. Welcome FireBlazer and fantastic to have you here. Check out launchpad and become a tester of all the 'bleeding edge' releases. This would require having a computer you don't rely on being stable (not a 'production computer'). That is the best. 9.04 is just around the corner and the only way it can be bigger and better is with testing and feedback. And the more 'thirteen year old computer geniuses' the better for attaining that end!

And yea, open source rocks hard!!! :) \\:D/

ps: I am at the other end of the age scale and windoze was so nowhere: I'd built machines, screwed with the quirks for years, then had an epiphany after playing around with Ubuntu for six months. Now it is hard to use anything other than my totally customised Xubuntu/Ubuntu Studio box! Ubuntu really does cross all age and cultural barriers! Or is that my imagination? Try out some desktop enviroments/managers like Openbox, Xfce (for super-fast!), Icewm. They don't screw your kernel, just give you a different view. Just check out the 'Sessions' after installing, logging in and out. Good luck.
