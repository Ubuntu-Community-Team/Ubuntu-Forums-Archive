### Post by I2k4 on 2012-04-06
I've been playing with lightweights on Live USB for some time now.  I installed Lubuntu 10.10 on my Acer One Atom with no regrets. I suggest comparison testing on Live USB, since the OS will only work better on a hard drive installation. 

My experience is that the jump from version 10.x to 11.x on ANY distribution was a big one in resource demands, it reminds me of the jump from Windows XP to Vista.  If something built on the Ubuntu 10.x satisfies for functionality, I'd go with it for weak systems.

My one beef against Lubuntu was the amount of software changing I had to do - I didn't want installed games or most of their default software, and ended up spending (wasting) time getting rid of many things and installing my preferred software.  

On my stronger main machine, I've recently been trying out Peppermint 2 as a "cloud" focused Lubuntu 11 based distro that is pretty much a blank slate for software - the working theory seems be a lot like Google's Chrome OS, Linux motor but Google cloud functions, and you're free to add whatever you like from repositories.
