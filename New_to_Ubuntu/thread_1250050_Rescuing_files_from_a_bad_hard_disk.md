### Post by RavUn on 2009-08-26
Somehow my Vista partition got trashed and was giving an io error when trying to access the C: drive.  I ran chkdsk /r and it went for over 2 days deleting and fixing stuff, which made Vista bootable, but it was still worthless.

I am still able to boot up my linux partition, but when I mounted the windows partition from there and attempted to copy files I would receive errors for some of the files and they could not be copied over.  Eventually, everything began shutting down and my laptop rebooted.  For some reason I cannot access those files.

Then I decided to just get rid of the Vista partition completely so I removed it and tried to grow the linux partition but that was giving errors, too!  

First of all, is this just a bad HDD that's toast or is it possible to repair it?  Also, could this be the result of some virus and I shouldn't even attempt to recover any files (even from the linux partition)?  I wish I had some of the error messages, but I wasn't really keeping track since I just thought this was an "ordinary" issue with the Vista bootloader not working after upgrading Ubuntu.  Any tips or insight would be very helpful!

### Post by brunogirin on 2009-08-26
That sounds like a toast HDD indeed! So I suggest you backup as much of your files as you can on an external drive, a USB key or any other external device. It should then just be a case of replacing the HDD, re-installing and recovering your backups. The fact that it rebooted when you tried to correct the problem in Vista is a bit worrying though, it could be a more serious hardware problem.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-08-26
Sounds like a failing (or failed!) hard drive, but to check, if you find out the manufacturer of the drive, they general provide a downloadable utility for scanning their drives for errors.   If the drive is still under warranty and their utility reports errors, you can often get a replacement from them.
