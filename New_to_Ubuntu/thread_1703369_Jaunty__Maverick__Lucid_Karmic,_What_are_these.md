Yes, these are Ubuntu versions, Android is similar, or even OS X.

You don't need to be wrapping your head around the names, just look at the numbers. 10.4, 10.10, soon to come 11.04. The first double digit is the year of version release and the second is the month. Ubuntu releases it's versions in April and October (the 4th and 10th month of the year).

Differences are sometimes notable and some times not, it's basically a version update, some version contains big improvements, some don't, but in any case it's best to use the latest one (except if your hardware is not supported by it).

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-03-09
Someone, I do not know who or why decided to name these releases are animals. And also to add an amusing adjective to the animal name. But, if we refer to the release by name and not by number we do not use the animal name but the adjective. So, I am now using Maverick. I do not say I am using Meerkat. Why we speak like this I do not know. 

I think that the six monthly release pattern was decided upon to push the development of Ubuntu forward. Holding back the release of a distribution until everything was done that could be done, would mean that we would have to wait years for improvements and we might lose interest in that distribution.

### Post by col48 on 2011-03-09
These are (part of) the nicknames for successive Ubuntu versions

 9.04 Jaunty Jackalope
 9.10 Karmic Koala
10.04 Lucid Lynx
10.10 Maverick Meercat
11.04 Natty N....   (next release)

The current LTS (Long Term Support) version is 10.04.
The numbers reveal the release dates.
You will note an alphabetical style, which applies after the earliest couple of releases.
