### Post by LowSky on 2009-02-24
There isn't really a simple solution, as PAL and NTSC are different by just enough to be annoying.

First you will need to rip the movies to Mpeg4 (best option), then a Program called DeVeDe can re-encode them to the correct format. Note that the images might look streched or even poorly cropped. Nothing you can do as they were edited into an oposing format. Widescreen film wont really be an issue, but old fullscreen movie will be a big issue.

### Post by taurus on 2009-02-24
Install either dvdrip or k9copy and make a copy of your DVD.  Then, use devede and convert it to .iso in NTSC format.  Now, burn the new .iso image to a DVD with whichever burner app that you use.
