### Post by wickstopher on 2008-08-05
For the cube, first make sure you have 4 desktops enabled.  The easiest way to do this is by right-clicking on the Workspace Switcher applet on your desktop panel.  If you don't have the Workspace Switcher applet on your panel, right-click on the panel, select "Add to Panel" and add the Workspace Switcher applet.  Right-click on the applet, select "Preferences," and set it to 4 Columns, 1 Row.  You can then remove the Workspace Switcher from the panel if you have no desire for it.

Once you're certain you have 4 desktops, try clicking the scroll wheel on your mouse and holding it down.  It should enable the desktop cube effect and you will be able to move the cube around with your mouse.

Also, make sure that you have Desktop Effects enabled.  Right click on the desktop, select "Change Desktop Background," and there will be a tab for Visual Effects.  Select Extra, and you should be good to go (assuming you have the proper graphics drivers installed and adequate system resources).
