### Post by bennachie on 2009-04-27
In general terms, the answer seems to be "yes". 

In my (limited) experience, the easiest way to get a portable version of Ubuntu is to create a "Live USB" memory stick using the USB Startup Disk Creator, with "persistence" enabled. That will allow you to store your settings, additional applications and frequently used documents on the stick. I find a cheap 4GB stick pretty satisfactory for that purpose, but YMMV. Most modern computers will boot from the resultant USB stick with no problems (although you may well have to adjust the BIOS settings during the startup phase to get them to do so).

I'm not going to buy into the argument between Gnome and KDE - both environments have their fervent advocates, and both are a credit to their respective development teams. On the whole, I suspect that, within the Ubuntu/Kubuntu/Xubuntu environment, much the largest proportion of the user community uses Ubuntu, with the Gnome desktop. In consequence, that branch of the distribution probably benefits from the largest proportion of the available development and testing expertise. The reverse situation applies in, for example, openSUSE.

### Post by bolucpap on 2009-04-28
As a testimonial, I can also vouch for the simplicity and ease of using an external hdd (with an actual spinning head, not a USB stick) for linux OS. The USB sticks are problematic, because write times are not so hot and people are suspicious of their suitability for hosting an OS because they support write operations only so many times. LiveUSB support with persistence has gone from mediocre to excellent from Intrepid to Jaunty in my experience (Intrepid used to forget installed packages from boot to boot, no such problem with Jaunty, although I ran into an issue with not being able to activate nvidia restricted drivers). However, if you suspend the computer with a LiveUSB stick, then take it out and boot it on a second computer, then shutdown the computer, stick it back into the first computer and hit the resume button, there is a high chance you will corrupt the casper-rw persistence filesystem (speaking from experience). Did not have the same problem with an external real harddisk tho. (or maybe I was just lucky).
