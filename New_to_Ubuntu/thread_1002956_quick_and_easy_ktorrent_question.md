### Post by hyper_ch on 2008-12-05
seeders: those who have completed the torrent and are "seeding" it to other people
leeches: those who have not completed the torrent yet

Share: the % compared to how much you have downloaded of that torrent and how much you have uploaded to others: 100% would be a 1:1

### Post by nhasian on 2008-12-05
when browsing which torrents to download, you should pay attention to the Seeders VS Leechers ratio.  if you have only 1 seeder and 100 leechers than your download will be very slow and if the seeder goes offline you will not be able to complete the download.  

if on the other hand there are 500 seeders and 100 leechers you're download will come in a lot faster.  I usually get 300-700KB/s downloads if there are a lot of seeders.

if you dont get more than 20-30KB/s then you may not have your ports forwarded properly in your router.  this link will help [http://portforward.com/routers.htm](http://portforward.com/routers.htm)

also give deluge a try.  that's what i use ):P
