it's termed "AMD64" because AMD processors were the first to offer 64 bit processors (meaning the CPUs offered 64-bit wide [integer registers]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Processor_register")).  Intel duals and quads offer the 64-bit register now, because it can offer (in certain situations) increased performance, and those are often referred to as "i686" or "x86_64", but AMD64 is essentially the correct term and just refers to the width of the integer registers.

Telling us your processor is a 32x system won't tell us if Ubuntu will run on it.  Give us information about how much RAM, speed of CPU, hard disk space, graphic card, wireless or not?, etc.  That will help us figure it out for you.  Option #2 is just downloading and burning a liveCD and seeing if it will boot with your system.
