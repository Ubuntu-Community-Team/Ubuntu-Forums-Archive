### Post by caffienefree on 2009-05-14
Xubuntu only makes sense if you plan on using this machine as your primary desktop.

Aside from the limitation on storage, the specs are reasonable for a server. You could purchase an external drive (if you don't want to upgrade the internal one), and install Ubuntu 9.04 Server for a variety of tasks.

Also, you could install ubuntu in LTSP mode and run thin clients: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuLTSP](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuLTSP)

### Post by -kg- on 2009-05-15
With 1 GB of RAM and four P-III Xeons I would think that you could run any version of Ubuntu you want to.  It all depends on what you want to do with your computer.

The only issue is with your hard drive space.  Are you intending to run Ubuntu exclusively, or are you dual booting?  If you're dual booting, then you might want to follow caffienefree's advice and upgrade your hard drive space.

Either replace the original hard drive or add another one, then you can install to the new hard drive and even create other partitions on it that can be shared with your other (I would assume Windows) installation.  You might want to upgrade your hard drive capacity anyway.  One can never have too much...this from a guy who, among two computers, has a total of near 3 TB of hard drive space. :)
