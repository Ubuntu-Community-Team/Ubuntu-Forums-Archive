### Post by Nessa on 2008-06-04
Turn on your laptop and wait for a black and white text thingy about your setup. If you know what DOS is, it'll look something like that. It usually has the word BIOS right on top. While you're still in that black and white screen, press F11 on your keyboard. This will give you the options you need. Press the arrow key until the CD/DVD drive option is highlighted (if you don't have the cd inside the drive, go ahead and insert it) then press enter.

Note: F11 may not work for you. If that's the case, please check your laptop manufacturer's website.
