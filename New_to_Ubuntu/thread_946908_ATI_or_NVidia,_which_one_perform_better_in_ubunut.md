### Post by porchrat on 2008-10-13
As you can see different people have different preferences.  This is a discussion that has raged on for years.

Realistically it is a choice you need to make for yourself.  Although recently ATI is producing the faster (and cheaper) graphics cards, and the functionality of their drivers is improving in leaps and bounds.  ATI have traditionally been viewed as less linux compatible, while nvidia have always worked well.  However since ATI was bought by AMD that has started to change and now linux support is available on release day for all ATI graphics cards.  Also linux drivers are now shipped in the driver CD that comes with the card!  Something not offered by nvidia.

Also with ATI firestream technology moving into the open-source world, and nvidia increasingly moving into the proprietary world of CUDA, ATI is also looking better for linux users wanting to run GPU processing technologies.

With the possibility of crossfire capabilities for ATI cards on linux the future looks bright for ATI in my opinion, and less so for nvidia.

However I once again say that you should do your own research and make the choice for yourself.
