### Post by yetimon_64 on 2017-10-30
In firefox when you use the History menu > clear recent history option, you will be given the option to check off exactly what you want cleaned. When you click on the option in the history menu a window will open up with a selection of check boxes for you to set what you want cleaned.

Edit: at the top of the window that pops up you can also limit how far back to clean or clean all entries (time wise). To clean all entries selected since the browser was installed choose "everything", only what you check off in the bottom section will be removed though.

Edit 2: just noted the lubuntu tag on this thread, I am assuming firefox is the same in lubuntu as the standard ubuntu release, I use and am basing my answer on the standard ubuntu version.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2017-10-30
Yes, firefox is firefox and not dependent on the *ubuntu flavour you are using.

To clear the cache you have to use **Edit ->Preferences ->Privacy and Security** tab where you can set a limit on the size of cache and the manner in which history is saved as well.

I have a very low level of cache set at 1MB as I have a fast web connection and don't miss the cache at all.
