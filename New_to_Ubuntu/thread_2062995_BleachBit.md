### Post by critin on 2012-09-26
When ubuntu updates, I believe it removes most of the old packages for you.  It leaves stuff that is needed by other apps., and it knows what it's doing.  Trust it.     I would suggest not using this if you don't know what it does.   It shouldn't be necessary.  

Windows leaves junk behind when you uninstall so ccleaner actually does good and is needed.   Trying to use windows habits with ubuntu can cause a broken system.  Regular cleaning like this isn't necessary in ubuntu.  I also, like you, use ccleaner in windows, but I skip the register cleaner option for the same reason, it can lead to a broken system.  The general advice is 'leave the register alone unless you know what you're doing!'   The tiny bits and pieces you remove doesn't add up to free usable disk space.

A good rule for me is 'if it works, leave it alone'.   

I read another post of yours asking the same question about BleachBit and someone advised against using a cleaner at all--I agree with that.

Since you still want to use it, leave the default checkmarks and run it.  That's the only way you'll know and learn for yourself.  Everyone can only advise how they do it, so your advice is varied and you still have to decide for yourself.  There isn't any 'one way is the right and only way' and here is the right answer.   It just doesn't exist.  

Default checkmarks are in ccleaner too.  How do you decide which to leave and which to remove in windows? 

I find ubuntu just doesn't *need* all the extra maintenance that windows does.

Good luck and keep exploring.
