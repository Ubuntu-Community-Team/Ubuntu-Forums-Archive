### Post by admiralspark on 2010-06-15
Why don't you just ctrl + Q to quit it, just like 99% of applications in Linux do?

I read an article somewhere...can't find it now...where they discussed how Rythmbox was *supposed* to open and close to the tray, so that it's out of the way when playing in the background. Maybe you can edit config files to make the tray icon just disappear altogether? It looks like the notifications and tray icon are all tied together, so simply removing the plugin would make it look crappy. 

Description of the logic behind the tray icon:
> 
_Minimize to System Tray_
To hide the player don&#8217;t close it &#8211; that will quit the application. If you minimize it, it goes to the taskbar &#8211; I want to minimize it to the system tray. To do that, just click on the RhythmBox icon in the system tray. It take a little getting used to &#8211; but a workable method.
