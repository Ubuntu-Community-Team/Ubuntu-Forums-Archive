Oli.
If a PC is connected to a public open network like the Internet then there is always a risk of infection or intrusion of some kind. As you say Ubuntu is **less prone** to infection.

There are a few virus/worms out there at the moment targeting Linux. But they aren't targeting users systems. Instead they are targeting Linux powered routers with poor security **passwords and user names** that they can **easily guess**. These virus/worms don't actually circumvent any of the security measures in place within Linux or use any vulnerability exploits. They simply exploit user laziness.

So make sure all your passwords and user names are strong. Passwords should be at least 8 characters long and be a mix of letters, numbers and other keyboard symbols.

Opening malware infected websites and e-mails is always a stupid idea. Good common-sense practice should always prevail. Stay away from bad websites. The exploits they use to gain access to systems isn't always OS based. Sometimes they are browser based. If the malware can interact with your web browser it can get at your details.

So common-sense practice should prevail. Stay away from suspect websites.

You should also have your firewall enabled.
