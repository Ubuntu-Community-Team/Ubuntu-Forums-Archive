### Post by mkstallings1 on 2012-08-16
If you are dual booting with windows, you can use gparted to get rid of your ubuntu partition.  That will effectively uninstall ubuntu.  Next you'll have to fix your windows Master Boot Record.  I use my windows install disk to do that through the recovery console and the fixmbr command.
