### Post by audiomick on 2010-02-03
Hi Chris.
I wouldn't worry about what is in that thread too much. If you look at the start, you can see he is talking about Ubuntu 7.10, so it is two years old.

I don't know which monitor resolution you have, but the "wide screen" format that is being spoken about in that thread should be supported without a problem. I had a problem in this direction myself a few years ago; I had a laptop with a 1280x800 screen, and that resolution was, at that time, not supported by default. After the next upgrade, though, the problem had disappeared.

As far as the problem you described goes, I also think it might be a hardware related thing. What you described sounded like the monitor not syncing properly. This is not necessarily related to the "no sync" message you reported earlier today. That always appears when the monitor is getting no signal at all. There is a small chance that the two things may be related. One possibility is that the video card is getting a bit tired.

just keep an eye on it for a while. It might have been a once off. If the problem turns up repeatedly, try a different monitor. If a different monitor is stable, then the monitor you had the problem with might have something wrong with it. If another monitor starts having trouble, maybe the video card has a problem.
