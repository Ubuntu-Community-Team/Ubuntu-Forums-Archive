### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-08-12
If XP is that slow on a 2+ GHz machine with 1GB of memory -- something is seriously wrong with the XP system.  I'm running XP on an "old" 1GHz Celeron with 760KB of memory and it flies! So, first thing you should do is take a look at their XP system to see what is causing the slowdown.

I would ordinarily recommend Wubi for a tryout, but give the underlying problems with their XP setup, that could be a disaster.  Therefore, you should set up a dual-boot configuration for them. That will give them an opportunity to try out Ubuntu, with the comfort of having XP to fall back on if they simply can't work with Ubuntu.

Also, some of what you said is contradictory.  At first, you paint this picture of folks who just do casual stuff like web browsing and using Open Office.  Then, you mention doing "eldergeek" stuff.  The first would pose little problems switching to any Linux distro because web browsing (with FF) and Open Office would be nearly identical to what they're doing now. But the second could spell disaster. Why? Because if she's happy tinkering in XP, how is she going to feel when you dump Linux on her and she learns that she has to learn all new ways of doing that tinkering?

You should give folks the opportunity to try something different, not work to force it upon them.
