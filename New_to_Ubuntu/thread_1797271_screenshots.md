### Post by sidzen on 2011-07-04
Use GIMP to first Scale the image (Image => scale) to a size smaller than the max for the forum, save it as a JPG, then either go to forum's advanced menu and manage attachments or post on some sort of site like image shack and enter the URL to the image that has been scaled.

### Post by nzjethro on 2011-07-04
^ Nice example! ;)

Yes, it's probably easier to use Manage Attachments. Just save the image to somewhere easy to find (like your Desktop, or your Pictures folder). Then, click New Reply, and down the page (below the "Post Icons" box, and "Miscellaneous Options", you'll see "Attach Files". Click "Manage Attachments", which will open a new window. In this new Window, click "Choose File", then navigate to your screenshot. You can choose up to five images. When you have selected your image(s), click "Upload", and it will upload your files. Then, in the "Attach Files" box you should see a list of your attached image(s).

Like sidzen said, you can scale your images, however if you are not comfortable doing this, just post it full size (only a thumbnail will appear in the message box), and mention that it is a large image in your post, so that people with slow connections don't wont accidentally try to open it.
