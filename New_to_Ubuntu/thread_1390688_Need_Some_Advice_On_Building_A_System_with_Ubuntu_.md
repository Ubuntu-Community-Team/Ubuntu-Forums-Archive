### Post by carbonbased on 2010-01-26
I installed Ubuntu 9.10 on a new build recently (2-3 wks.). Being that I'm new to Linux, I'm not the best judge of stability, however, this user is the most unstable component to system function. But I've not seen a glitch in anything--maybe being a totally fresh install makes a difference.

I built on a Gigabyte MA785G-UD3H mb.  Nice mb features, great warranty too, but the LAN chips went out within a month, so I plugged an ethernet card in and was back online.

I put 4gb of RAM onboard and have tried to strain that resource a few times with things I use regularly, like running 5 or 6 apps all at once. I couldn't get it to peg more than ~2gb of memory at any time. Swap was never touched. I'm sure someone could get better results, but I tested it how I use it. Linux seems to be very keen with resources. For my type of activities, 4gb is much more than necessary.

I put in a very inexpensive Athlon II x2 245 (AM3) cpu. I am very please with how Linux runs on this machine.

My HP Laserjet 1022n prints at about 1/2 the speed as it did under WinXP, which must be the drivers.

The drivers for the Nvidia GTS 250 video card seem to be working very well. At times I can see a line appear horizontally when watching some video. This is true whether it's a movie from the HDs or a DVD. I'm guessing it's the drivers, but I'm no video buff so I'm not highly knowledgeable about the technicals on video.

I ditched WinXP less than a month ago, and other than my own lack of knowledge on Linux, I'm very happy with this OS and I'm amazed at its flexibility. For the most part, the only fail/deficiency in the Linux platform is on the part of manufacturers being so slow in supporting their hardware for the OS.
