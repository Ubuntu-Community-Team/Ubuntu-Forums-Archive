### Post by o.besner on 2008-10-14
Audacious is close to Winamp. It can even use the same skins, if I remember well.

Songbird is kinda shaky right now (it's in development), but it's gonna become badass. Huge developing crew. In terms of functionality, you can do what you want with it. You can even browse the internet (based on Gecko - the Firefox engine)

Exaile is stable, fast, clean, and adapts to your desktop theme. Easy to use. My favorite music player. It was a clone of Amarok, but it has it's own way of doing things now. You can make it use Sound Juicer to rip your cds, you can edit your tracks or a whole album at a time and the interface is clean and intuitive. Automatically fetches your album cover. Install 2 optionnal packages and it will get the wikipedia article and the lyrics.

Amarok is the best player I've ever used yet, may it be Linux or Win. Very fast, clean, complete, incredible list of plugin and support. But it's integration in Gnome is so-so (plugins, KDE, load sloowly, appearance doesn't match the rest of your pc)

Banshee is more similar to iTunes. It doesn't have the extended fonctionality of Winamp, so it's probably not what you're looking for.
