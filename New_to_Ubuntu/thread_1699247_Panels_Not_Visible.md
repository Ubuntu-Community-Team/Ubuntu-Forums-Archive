### Post by outskut on 2011-03-04
I think I've actually had this problem intermittently.  I have my dell mini 10 hooked up to an external monitor, and it's had a hard time maintaining the "extra" visual effects I have set.  What happens is that if the computer's coming out of sleep, or sometimes after I've booted it normally changing the settings to extra visual effects requires "searching for drivers" and a strange process.  Anyway, it's worked well enough, but intermittently the drivers crash.
When that happens I'm not permitted to choose extra settings, and my panels go nearly blank.  They don't really go blank though, it's almost as if the tabs have just failed to render, because I still see the outline of the (transparent) panel.
For me the problem has always been solved by rebooting, but it might help to try changing the panel properties to non-transparent (that is by right clicking somewhere on it > properties > backgrounds > default) and changing your visual effects to minimal (by right clicking on the desktop background > Change Desktop Background > visual effects > none).
Hope my anecdote helps.  It seems like some resource of the graphics card driver is just crashing.  I could be wrong though.
