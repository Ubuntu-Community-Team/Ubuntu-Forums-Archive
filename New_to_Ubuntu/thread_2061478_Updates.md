### Post by deadflowr on 2012-09-22
How many updates are you getting?

But anyway, generally speaking, kernel upgrades are normal. It's something linux does, instead of forever patching and overwriting a single kernel, turning it into a frankenstein's monster, you get a fresh version, fully patched out of the box.
The beauty of it is that it doesn't overwrite the existing kernel, so upon boot, through grub or other bootloading programs, you can still use an older kernel, just in case the newer version causes you problems.
Older kernels will stay on your system until you either manually remove them or reinstall your system.
