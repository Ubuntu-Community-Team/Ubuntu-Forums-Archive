### Post by jcpahman77 on 2012-11-04
I got it, forgot to post. It was a BIOS setting. The BIOS has a setting for the onboard audio with auto, enable, and disable as options. I switched it from auto to enable and it worked.

I bought 2 more gigs of RAM but it turns out it's not dual channel or whatever the proper term is so it'll likely be going back, however, I just played a full HD movie and my load values for 1, 5, 15 were all around 1.2 -- 1.5 so not too bad for an old machine.
