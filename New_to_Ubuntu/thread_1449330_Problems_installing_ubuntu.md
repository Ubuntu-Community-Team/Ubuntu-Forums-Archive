You've got serious overheating problems. Solve that and the rest will start to fall into place.

Check your fans and vents to make sure they are all working and  aren't clogged with dust and/or pet hair. Take a look inside the case and see if it's full of dust bunnies. It may need a good cleaning.

Excess heat and moisture are two of the worst enemies your PC has.

Try using it when it's coolest - take the side of the case off and place a fan a couple of feet away blowing at a slight angle into the case, right on the motherboard and hard drives. If everything works okay, that *may* mean you haven't fried anything else yet.

Boot with a live cd and see if you can read the files on your hard drive from it. If you can, back up all your files.

Job One is to fix the overheating, though. Once you eliminate the overheating problem you can start trying to figure out what, if anything, has been fried from overheating.

Good luck!!
