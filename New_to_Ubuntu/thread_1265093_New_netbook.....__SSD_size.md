### Post by sgosnell on 2009-09-13
If you do it right, 4GB is enough, 8 is great, and 16 is overkill.  Get an SDHC card, 16GB or so, and save all your data on that.  Use the SSD only for programs, and you'll have no problems.  Consider inserting the SDHC card before installing Ubuntu, and then selecting a manual install, selecting the partitions yourself, and put / on the SSD, and /home on the SDHC.  That way, you don't have to worry about where to save stuff, it's automatically saved on the SDHC card, and if you have to reinstall the OS, your data remains on the card, and won't be lost.
