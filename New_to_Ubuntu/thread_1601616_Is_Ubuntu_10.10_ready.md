### Post by Chesamo on 2010-10-20
Every version of Ubuntu is "ready". The numbers designate their launch date, not their level of development (though obviously later releases will tend to be more stable than older releases).

There is no "Ubuntu 10". There are, however, Ubuntu 10.04 and Ubuntu 10.10. Ubuntu 10.10 is not the tenth version of Ubuntu; it is the thirteenth.

### Post by trikster_x on 2010-10-22
I would say that if you are going to do a fresh install, it should be fine.  If you are planning to upgrade, however...

I upgraded my system from 10.04 to 10.10 early in the week, and I wish I would have waited.  

The biggest bug I came across had to do with having any type of compiz repo enabled.  Completely fragged compiz and cost me a few hours figuring out how to fix it.  Other than that I have issues with slower boot and shutdown times, and a **serious** hang problem when I open root directories.  I know they sound like small things, and to most they would be.  I just don't like that I spent **hours** on an upgrade to end up taking a step back (Honestly, it would have taken half the time to install from liveUSB, reinstall all my programs, configure everything, run post-install updates, and customize what I wanted.  I'll never upgrade through update manager again...).
