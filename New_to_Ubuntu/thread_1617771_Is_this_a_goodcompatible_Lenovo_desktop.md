### Post by cariboo on 2010-11-09
Ubuntu should run well on either of those systems. the only thing I would have any doubts about, is that they don't specify what wireless chipset they use, but there isn't much you can do about that, as they may use a different chipset for each production run. 

Most of the popular wireless chipsets are well supported, and if worse comes to worse, you can always use ndiswrapper.
