### Post by putonghua73 on 2008-11-10
Depending on what type of learner you are **, it may well be beneficial to learn the principles behind unix/linux to make best use of the commands. [_Slashbook_]("http://www.slackbook.org/") is a good site that provides a structured understanding of linux. Otherwise you run the risk of simply entering commands without any real understanding of how they affect your system and why.

** researchers have devised a classification system: Reflector, Theorist, Activist and Pragmatist that seeks to map the learning behaviour of individuals, to facilitate the most appropriate form of learning based upon their classification e.g. I'm a mixture between theorist and reflector - hence, I need to understand the principles of what I am working on, and to have sufficient time to think about these principles and their application. Use google for more info if you're interested.

P.s. Thanks for the link to LinuxCommand - I really like the guided approach and the site is well-structured.

**Update:** LinuxCommand is even better than I thought! From perusing the site, the guided approach provides a really good gentle introduction to linux principles underpinning the commands, in bite-sized, easily digestible chunks. _Definitely_ recommended - thanks simtaalo!
