### Post by oldsoundguy on 2010-04-03
I use a Logitech pro 9000 web cam and it works OK (not great) .. because of lag.
Now that lag could be corrected IF there were utilities that would allow dialing down the resolution as that is what is causing the lag.  Also the "follow face" does not work nor can you zoom in and zoom out.
Right now the utility package from Logitech is sorely lacking.

Graphic card .. for a long time the only real ball game was NVidia, but ATI finally got off the pot and now is catching up very quickly, but only on NEWER CARDS.  Very little in the way of legacy support.

Audio card .. If you want all the bells and whistles that come with some cards such as Creative, good luck!  Analog audio out on the back will work with almost every card .. including surround analog.  But DIGITAL out is a crap shoot and most of the time will not work (but it doesn't work on older cards in Windows either . thanks to Creative and built in obsolescence.)
Front panel .. maybe the headphones will work and maybe not.  NONE of the other features on a front panel card will work in most cases.

The code writers at Linux (not just Ubuntu) have been fighting this up hill battle for years.  And the lack of co-operation between hardware manufacturers and OS developers is the open wound that really is the reason for the lack of mass acceptance of Linux in any form.

IF you design your desktop around cards that WORK in Linux .. you will have little or no issues with hardware compatibility.  
BUT, if you insist that that 5 buck wireless network card MUST work or your 15 buck, bought at a sale at the drug store, printer just has to function .. you will have a battle on your hands.
