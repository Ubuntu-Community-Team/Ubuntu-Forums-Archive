### Post by halitech on 2010-03-21
most of your devices don't require drivers like they do in Windows as they are built into the kernel or loaded as modules when it detects the hardware. The main exception to this is video cards and wireless. Depending on your motherboard, it could be an intel video card, an ATI or possibly an Nvidia. 

As far as the data card, is it a USB device or is it connected to the motherboard? ie. a PCI card

Others have already mentioned iTunes and how to run that but if you just need to transfer music to an iPod or other device, most will work with drag and drop or you can install apps like gtkpod to manage it.
