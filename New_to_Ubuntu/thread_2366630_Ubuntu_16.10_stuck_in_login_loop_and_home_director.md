### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-07-20
Welcome. Have you tried booting from install media, 'Try Ubuntu' then backing up your personal data to an external drive or USB? Can you access your data that way.

16.10, if it isn't dead already, will be in a week or less, so strongly advise you backup your valuable data, if you can find some way, and install either the long-term support 16.04 LTS (supported until 2021) or 17.04 (an interim release supported for nine months from April 2017). I suggest 16.04 LTS. (LTS release can upgrade via net to next LTS, for instance, 16.04> 18.04 LTS, leapfrogging the interim releases in between. Interims can only upgrade via net to next release.)

Goes without saying, but I'll say it. Always pays to keep reliable backups on hand. ;)
