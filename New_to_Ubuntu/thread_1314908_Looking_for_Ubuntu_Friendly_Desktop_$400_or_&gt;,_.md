### Post by iansane on 2009-11-04
I built mine for $300 dollars buying all the parts at Micro Center. I have a cheap MSI mother board. Unfortunately they all have SATA and no IDE where I shop. But a PCI IDE card can fix that I think.

My only problem is one that I had on my last Gateway machine too. I can't get ATI or Nvidia integrated or PCI to work with Ubuntu.

I also purchased a 64 bit AMD Athalon dual core 2.9Ghz processor real cheap and 2 gigs of DDR2 RAM. It's cheap but runs better than the $1500 gateway that it replaced.

As far as I know the problem with 64 bit Ubuntu will be wireless drivers that require NDIS wrapper and only come in 32 bit like my netgear usb wireless and there may be video problems so be sure to look into those two things carefully before buying hardware.
