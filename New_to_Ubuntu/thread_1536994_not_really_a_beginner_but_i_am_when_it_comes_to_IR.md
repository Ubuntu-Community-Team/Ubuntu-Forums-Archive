### Post by Nytram on 2010-07-25
Why do you want a shell account for IRC - to hide your IP details?

Many networks have a way of registering a username which then cloaks your info, I'm not sure if Freenode also does this. I know Undernet and Quakenet do, two of the biggest networks.

Free shells accounts are hard to find, and are usually of poor quality. It's possible to get a decent paid-for shell for a dollar or two (US) / month.

EDIT

It is possible to cloak your nick on Freenode, this link has more details:

[http://www.wikihow.com/Register-a-User-Name-on-Freenode]("http://www.wikihow.com/Register-a-User-Name-on-Freenode")

IRC can be a great platform for support on Ubuntu or a host of other applications, programming languages, etc.  If you are in the #ubuntu channel, you'll notice that the vast majority of people do not have shells or cloaks.

If you want a shell, there are a couple free ones out there that are of decent quality.  I have one through shellium, which serves my purposes quite well.  You can set up any IRC client to connect to your shell once it's set up.  If you want something terminal based, you can take a look at irssi as mentioned above.  If you like a GUI-based client, xchat comes with a default Ubuntu install, and is a great option...there are lots of others out there as well.
