### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-08-09
Depends on why the BIOS doesn't allow it.  If it won't permit changes to multipliers, frequencies, or voltages, any software you try most probably won't work, either.

Also, given the complete lack of flexibility of adding better cooling devices to laptops, you'll be lucky to get even 5% improvement before it overheats.

And finally, I've been overclocking PCs for a LONG time, and as time goes by, the relative improvements have been dropping off rapidly.

You would do better getting an older desktop motherboard and CPU -- to learn about overclocking.
