### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-12-03
It's a "legacy" tray capability added to handle applications that don't honor Gnome standards.

I don't know of a way to move it to the top panel.  But, the "Topicons" Gnome extension accomplishes much the same thing.  Extensions to eliminate the legacy tray also are at extensions.gnome.org.

(Whether a Dropbox icon shows up anywhere these days seems to be a bit of a question since they rewrote the tool.)
