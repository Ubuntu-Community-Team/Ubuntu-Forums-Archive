### Post by dchurch24 on 2009-01-25
Hi all,

When browsing round the web, I'd often come across posts/blogs that say things like "Is Linux (or ubuntu) ready for the desktop, or "Ubuntu isn't ready for the desktop" etc...

Well, I was a little dubious to start with (about 2 years ago now 6.04) and went for a dual boot. I found I wasn't booting into Windows at all after about 6 months and 'reclaimed' the HDD that it was sitting on.

Since then, I have had visitors to my house that have had new PC's or laptops for Christmas or Birthdays and have seen my set up (with Compiz of course - always the selling point to newbies ;-) )

They are all very impressed by it's ease of use.  My brother-in-law had a laptop with Vista on it - he was constantly complaining of spyware, viruses and generally being bugged by the OS every time he wanted to open a bit of software, download something or even just simply changing a simple setting. I told him that I had no such problems and maybe he should look at installing Ubuntu.

He declined (or maybe, resisted is a better word).

After another 5 months of this, he turned up one night at my house, slammed the laptop on the table and said "FFS, PLEASE but Ubuntu on here before I throw this thing in the sea".

I did. Now I get no support phone calls from this guy; at all.

Another friend (who I had given a very old Dell PC to with XP) got a new PC for Christmas. Yes, it had Vista.

On Christmas day (Yes! Bloody Christmas day!) I got a call from his screaming at me to 'fix' his computer. I explained that it was just a new OS and he would have to take time to learn it.

Two weeks later, it had Ubuntu. I've not had a support call from this guy since either.

They are both very happy.

Now to put this in perspective, the second guy cannot read or write, and until a few months ago when I gave him the old Dell with XP on it was also completely computer illiterate too. I even had to show him how to use a mouse.

Yet...there are some that are not willing to give it a go, claiming that Windows does everything they need it to do, email, browse the web a bit and maybe write the odd letter or two.

Yes, I agree. Windows (and assocs) can do those things, and in some cases it can do them well. 

However, I used to use Windows and I used to write my own software using c# as and when I needed it, so not quite your average user, but not too far removed.

Now I have moved to Ubuntu (and subsiquently 64_Studio, xubuntu, kubuntu and mythbuntu on my other machines) I now find that my computer setup can do so much more for me than Windows ever could. Many others may find the same; yes, Ubuntu could just let you print a few letters, browse the web and pick up emails, but it also allows so much more, and with fewer mouse clicks and annoyancies.

For example, I have a touchscreen monitor on a mini-itx pc in my kitchen with a barcode scanner attached. All my food etc... gets scanned into the cupboards and is removed from the list at the touch of the screen - this machine also keeps the temperature of the room in a MYSQL database sitting on my main Ubuntu box. It also lets me know what is currently being watched on my mythtv clients (Myth is a fully featured entertainement centre, a bit like Sky plus, but with more in it).

My main Ubuntu box, also has a frequency scanner plugged in and is using gPredict (oddly, when I had Windows I could not get Predict working!) to learn where certain weather satelites are and record their images as they pass over. 

These are just some of the things that a short, quite shallow learning curve can enable you to do with Ubuntu. Yes, I suppose Windows could do the same, but after trying both, I found that the speed of Ubuntu, not only in itself but in the way that it enabled me to do things, outweighs the Windows way ten fold.

When I plugged my USB key into a friends Windows machine, I had to install DRIVERS???? When I took it back to my Ubuntu machine, bang! Up comes Nautilus showing what's on the key within a second.

If people are still worried about taking the plunge, then try a dual boot - you won't regret it.
