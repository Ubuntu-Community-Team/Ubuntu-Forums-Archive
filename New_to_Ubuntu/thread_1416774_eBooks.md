### Post by kwacka on 2010-02-26
As capnthommo says the Gutenburg project has the widest range of free (non-copyright) books. 

[Gutenmark ]("http://www.sandroid.org/GutenMark/features.html") can convert 'Gutenprint' to HTML so can be read on browser (it automatically removes the first few 'disclaimer' pages and automatically italicizes 'etc' etc. ;)).

FBReader (universe repository) reads  fb2, html, chm, plucker, palmdoc, ztxt, tcr, rtf, oeb, openreader, non-DRM'ed mobipocket, plain text, epub formats.

Calibre is designed as an ebook manager, can read most formats and can also convert to ePub format.

### Post by 416inversed on 2010-02-26
Another program you might want to look into is Jupiter. It's not an eReader but in my opinion, it significantly improves the eReader experience on netbooks. 

Basically, Jupiter is a power management/config applet that also adds an easy menu to rotate your screen's orientation.

So instead of constantly scrolling to read an eBook on the netbook's short screen, you can rotate the display 90 degrees and read the page lengthwise. 

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2010/02/jupiter-awesome-new-netbook.html](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2010/02/jupiter-awesome-new-netbook.html)

has more info.

### Post by I2k4 on 2011-05-03
Big discovery for me:  I have the Kindle PC app set up on Windows to read the free out of copyright classics from [www.gutenberg.org](www.gutenberg.org).  

I installed FBReader on Lubuntu and presto, all the Kindle mobipocket books in the Kindle folder show up when that folder is selected through the preference menu.  For anyone with a Kindle, just use the Kindle version of the download on Gutenberg, and the FBReader will read it on Ubuntu.
