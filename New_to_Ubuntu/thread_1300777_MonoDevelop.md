### Post by dv3500ea on 2009-10-25
Their are other popular IDEs such as Anjuta, KDevelop and Eclipse
(see: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_integrated_development_environments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_integrated_development_environments))
I think you'll find the simple gedit (included) very good as well (good for interpreted languages such as perl and python) or as an alternative, notepad++ under wine. These are simpler, have syntax highlighting but don't automate any building, packaging or project management.
