### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-12-04
You do realise, do you not, that you are asking this on a Ubuntu user forum? None of us work for Canonical. None of us can give you an answer. Canonical is a commercial organisation. It no doubt has several levels of paid for service agreements which may or may not be restricted by the support period of Ubuntu LTS releases.

[http://www.canonical.com/products](http://www.canonical.com/products)

[http://www.canonical.com/services](http://www.canonical.com/services)

[http://www.ubuntu.com/management/ubuntu-advantage](http://www.ubuntu.com/management/ubuntu-advantage)

Canonical does not provide a free to download and use edition of Ubuntu & a paid for enterprise edition. All Ubuntu products are free to download and use. Providing services is one way that Canonical makes its money. 

The future for both Ubuntu desktop and server is Snappy Ubuntu Core with its transactional updates feature that holds promise of support that will be undefined. Although not without end.

> Transactional, image-based delta updates for the system and applications that can always be rolled back.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/cloud/snappy](http://www.ubuntu.com/cloud/snappy)

[https://insights.ubuntu.com/2014/12/09/a-new-transactionally-updated-snappy-ubuntu-core/](https://insights.ubuntu.com/2014/12/09/a-new-transactionally-updated-snappy-ubuntu-core/)

I do not think that those who direct & manage Canonical are thinking in terms of "doing more of the same but to more people." I cannot see them promising to do it longer, either. They have a vision and they are working towards that vision. There will be a traditional Ubuntu desktop & server LTS release at the end of April 2016 and both will be given 5 years support. Personally, I cannot see the next Ubuntu LTS getting more than 5 years support unless someone enters a commercial agreement with Canonical. And that is out of the range of a user forum.
