### Post by lhb1142 on 2011-03-12
On occasion, I wish to copy video + audio that I can play on my Ubuntu Linux (10.10) computer (Acer Extensa 5620-6419, Intel Core 2 Duo Processor T5550, 1.83 GHz, 667 MHz FSB, 2 MB L2 Cache, 3 GB DDR2).

I have no trouble recording any audio (I use OutRec < [http://sourceforge.net/projects/outrec/](http://sourceforge.net/projects/outrec/) > for most audio) but I have been unable to record certain video programs which I can see AND hear on my computer.

I have tried XVidCap but I have been unable to get the sound to work; I can capture the video, but I cannot capture the audio. XVidCap is supposed to record the audio as well but I cannot figure out how to make it work. (At least for me, XVidCap is awfully clunky.)

I'd like to have a program which, if I am playing a video on my computer, will, by clicking one button, record everything playing (picture and sound) in the same quality I am viewing/hearing it.

OutRec will easily record any and all audio playing on the computer. Just press the 'record' button and you are recording. Is there a similar Linux program which would record video + audio?

In other words, is there a Linux program similar to OutRec but which is also for video?
