### Post by Scryer on 2013-06-21
I recently installed 13.04 on an ASUS G75VW laptop, dual-booting through GRUB with Win8.  13.04 worked fine at native resolution (1920x1080) with the installed nouveau driver, and with the normal 3rd party nvidia driver that I got by clicking the "install 3rd party drivers" common one.

Today it booted up in low resolution: big login entry on the front screen, and when I log in as me or as guest I see the center of the display, with no accessible buttons on the left.  Booting from the 13.04 LiveCD in "Try it first" mode works fine - I'm sending this message from the LiveCD version.  Win8 still works as well as it did before.

I assume I somehow installed a new nvidia driver that didn't get integrated with the system.

How do I get back to a working screen or an earlier driver?

Thanks!

Solution: I ended up downloading and installing the latest driver for my graphics card from NVIDIA.  Lost my icons on the left, did more googling and got them back with "unity --reset-icons".
