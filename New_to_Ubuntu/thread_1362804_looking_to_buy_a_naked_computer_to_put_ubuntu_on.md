### Post by relay_man on 2009-12-23
My new HP laptop came without any discs.  What little literature that came with it encouraged an immediate "rescue disc" be made on a DVD/R.
I did that.  It restores the computer to the state it was in as it came from the carton.  I put that disc in a heavy duty case and stored in a safe place.  I then popped in the Jaunty disc, re-booted, and chose "use the entire disc" option, and I've got a brand new laptop with Ubuntu.  It works wonderfully!  Later, if I want to sell this laptop, give it someone who wants (?) Winblows, I'll just re-install and hand it over.
Having that option is nice.  I don't think you are going to find a laptop that is significantly cheaper just because it's without an OS, or with Ubuntu already installed.  
BTW, if you think you can stiff arm M$ into giving you a refund, I wish you the best of luck.  You'll excuse me if I don't hold my breath...:rolleyes::rolleyes:
