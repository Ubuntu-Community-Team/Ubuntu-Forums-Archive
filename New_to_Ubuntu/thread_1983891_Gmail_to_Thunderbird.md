### Post by Lisiano on 2012-05-20
Gmail has a awesome option that makes it so that if you download an email, it will not be deleted from their servers.
[https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#settings/fwdandpop](https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#settings/fwdandpop)
I'm assuming that is what you want, if not, then set up Gmail in Thunderbird as always but set it to sync only a certain folder instead of the inbox.

### Post by lisati on 2012-05-20
From within your gmail account, you'll need to edit your settings - click on the cog-wheel icon on the top right of the page then click on settings. 

On the forwarding & pop/imap you'll need to enable your choice of pop or imap (or both). 

Once you've done that, you're set for configuring Thunderbird. IMAP is good if you want to keep your messages on the gmail servers, and POP is OK if you don't mind downloading your messages: links to configuration instructions for Thunderbird are available on gmail's forwarding/pop page.
