### Post by Temposs on 2009-02-04
hi zorrofox,
   I also have had to introduce Ubuntu to my better half, and also my Mom. After they get over the initial shock of the new look and interface, they'll find that a lot of things are actually very much the same as Windows, and some things they'll like even better(or worse). Once they get in a rhythm with what to click on when they want to do one thing or another, they'll feel as at home as on Windows, I think.

I guess the most important thing for someone who doesn't understand computers very well is to make shortcuts for all their favorite programs on one of the panels(the things at the top and bottom of the screen). You can do this by going to the item you want in the Applications menu and right-clicking, then you can add a shortcut to a panel or even to the desktop(though I don't prefer littering the desktop with links). Then everything will be extremely easy to access. Also, try to address how to achieve each functionality in ubuntu that your better half needs. For most things applications there are very good solutions in Ubuntu.

Good luck for now :-)
