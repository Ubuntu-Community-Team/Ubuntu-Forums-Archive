### Post by Bartender on 2009-12-30
I did it the old-fashioned way.  This was moving away from a Juno email address, but the principle is the same.  Started a gmail account, and over the course of the next few months I slowly migrated all the personal contacts and "business" contacts (not really business, but newegg, credit card, bank, stuff like that) over to gmail.  Like I said, it took a few months.  Finally pulled the plug on the old dial-up ISP and found one that works with Linux.

As far as passwords, I don't know if anyone has a perfect solution.  If you have your own little computing space that isn't regularly ransacked by kids, dogs, or other unauthorized co-inhabitants you could probably just keep a small notebook in the bottom of a drawer or something similar.

Speaking of simple passwords, someone hacked my PayPal account just a week ago and tried to eBay themselves a $200 gift certificate.  PayPal caught it.  They sent me an email, which directed me to a site where I had to enter a new password, new secret questions, etc.  My old password was pretty weak and it actually spelled a word, so I concocted a jumble of upper case, lower case, numbers, etc. that was much more appropriate and I wrote it down carefully so there wouldn't be confusion a year from now.

Got to looking at some of my other online stuff, and found several other miserably weak passwords.  Am in the process of changing them.
