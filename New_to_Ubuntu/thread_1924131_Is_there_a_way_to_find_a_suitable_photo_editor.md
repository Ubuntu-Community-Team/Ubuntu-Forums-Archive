### Post by fractalman on 2012-02-12
I would recommend gimp (GNU image manipulation programme) it's in the repos so you can add it through software centre or synaptic package manager,

it's a linux equivalent of photoshop and has really powerful features, one bonus is you can take all your photoshop brushes and patterns and just drop them straight into gimp without having to recode.

there's a registry of plugins here

[http://registry.gimp.org/](http://registry.gimp.org/)

install gimp, then open it to create  the gimp folder in your home directory, it will be hidden so in your file browser select veiw>show hidden files and you will see the gimp folder

.scm plugins go in the script folder,  
.py plugins go in the plugins folder

### Post by JKyleOKC on 2012-02-12
The Gimp is, as everyone has said, an excellent graphics editor, on a par with Photoshop. However, like Photoshop, it has a pretty steep learning curve.

If you don't need all its bells and whistles, "fotoxx" is a pretty fair editor that can do most of the usual things. It can even do some that I never found a way to do in the gimp, such as correcting the "leaning-back" distortion due to pointing the camera up to get an entire building into the picture.

If you don't feel too uncomfortable working at a command prompt, the suite of tools known as ImageMagick can do all sorts of things, also. It's especially useful for doing resizing, format conversion, or contrast correction on an entire batch of pictures at one time...
