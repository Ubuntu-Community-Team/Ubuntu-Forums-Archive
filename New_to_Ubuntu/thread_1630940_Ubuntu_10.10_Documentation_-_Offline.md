### Post by nothingspecial on 2010-11-26
To search man pages ......

press /

the enter the search term.

For example, you want to know how to increase the volume in mplayer

press /

then type "volume" without the quotes

the man page will then jump to the first occurance of the word (string) volume.

if that`s not what you are looking for press N

and it will go to the next one

(this is actually a feature of less, which, by default, opens the man pages......... but I digress)

### Post by jmszr on 2010-11-28
Dark7man,

To access the "man" pages open the terminal (Applications > Accessories > Terminal) and type man<space>name of application and press enter (example):
```
man gparted
```

I find that some of the man pages aren't particularly enlightening.

Here is a link to the printable version of the 10.10 Ubuntu Guide: [http://ubuntuguide.org/index.php?title=Ubuntu:Maverick&printable=yes](http://ubuntuguide.org/index.php?title=Ubuntu:Maverick&printable=yes)

Hope that helps.
