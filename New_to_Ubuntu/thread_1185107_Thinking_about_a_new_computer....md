### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-06-12
My suggestion:

Do a diagnostic on the HD on your IBM; if that is the problem, just buy a new HD for it.  Keep that as your "main" computer, and invest in any of the many linux compatible netbooks.

I have a (new-ish) Dell Mini 9, which I use 1-2x daily, and a 14.1" Acer laptop, which I have had for about 2 years now, and only turn on once or twice a month now (if that).  I wouldn't necessarily recommend Acer's for durability though; mine has proved good enough though.

I also have an enternal 2.5" USB HD for storage (and backup), given my Mini only has an 8GB internal SD.

Doing this would cost approx (in UK money):
Mini 9/10: £200
New HD for IBM: £40
External USB HD: £50

Cheaper than many other options, other than a bottom-of-the-range new laptop, and you get the benefit of an ultra-portable option while recycling your current computer.

If you haven't already got an LCD screen for your existing computer, they are pretty cheap now too, and would give you the majority of the eeeBox space-saving bonus.
