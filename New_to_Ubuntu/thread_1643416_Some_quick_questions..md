### Post by Bluenoser81 on 2010-12-12
If you have a 64-bit laptop and your only install the 32-bit version, you really aren't taking advantage of the speed boost that 64-bit will give you. One downside of 64-bit is that there are certain programs that run in 32bit only (there may be a way to force run them anyway, I believe), although these programs seem to be very few and far between. I have been running 64-bit on my laptop (10.10) for about 4 months now, and haven't had any problems. The reason I chose 10.10 is that there is certain wireless drivers that come packaged out-of-the-box starting in 10.10 that weren't available in 10.04lts, and I was having lots of wireless adapter problems until I upgraded to 10.10, and everything has been smooth since.
