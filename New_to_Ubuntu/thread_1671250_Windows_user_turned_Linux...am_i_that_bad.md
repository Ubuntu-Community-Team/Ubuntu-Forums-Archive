### Post by The Cog on 2011-01-21
It all depends what your interest is. If the operating system itself is your interest then making your own distro can be a good, interesting and rewarding hobby. On the other hand, if your interest is in using the operating system as a tool to get other things done, then finding and using someone else's ready made distro is the most productive approach. 

A friend of mine built his own kit car. He did if for the pleasure of building hid own car. He's rightly proud of his work. Me, I use a mass produced car to get from A to B. Neither is better or worse, and the world needs both types of folk.

If you use Ubuntu as a tool to do other work, that's fine. I like it because in my opinion it's a lot better for the kind of things I do than other OSs I've tried. It's a really fully equipped toolkit. Like a really good lathe, it can be a pleasure to use.
