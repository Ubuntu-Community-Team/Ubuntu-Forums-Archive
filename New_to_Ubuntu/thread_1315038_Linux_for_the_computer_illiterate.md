### Post by jimmy the saint on 2009-11-05
This is what I did for my grandmother who transitioned from a typewriter two years ago.

I made the panels about twice as big (the icons resize so they are bigger as well)

I got rid of EVERYTHING that she didn't need to use from the panels.

I then placed icons for Abiword, Firefox, and a couple others on the panel.

I put desktop icons for a few folders that she needed.

She had an icredibly simple desktop that was quite literally a point and click experience that didn't require her to deal with strange menus or anything that she didn't absolutely need.  As she became more comfortable I showed her how to do other things.  In my experience, Gnome is great in that it can be made as sparse as you want it to be.  She like it and I didn't have to walk her through everything all the time.  (I can't tell you how many times she called me about the popups and warnings on windows between the firewall, AV, system etc.).

I also took the time to write and pring simple instructions that explained simple things like shutting it down, saving documentes, dialing in (yep, dial up out in the boonies) etc.

Just my 2cents.  

Good luck
