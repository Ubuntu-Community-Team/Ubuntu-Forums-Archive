### Post by Roger Allott on 2009-11-06
Gnome has been designed by people who favour function over form.

KDE has been designed by people who favour form over function.

Xfce has been designed by people who favour efficiency.

If you're going to use your PC for lots of audio/video stuff, KDE is probably better because the best audio/video apps seem to be optimised for KDE and so don't inherit your Gnome theme.

If you're going to rely on this forum for technical advice, Gnome is probably best because most responses you'll get assume that you use Gnome apps.
