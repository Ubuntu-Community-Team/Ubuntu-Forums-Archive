### Post by Impavidus on 2013-02-09
Most laptops will work without problems, but you may want to pay attention to the video card and the wifi. People often have bad experiences with ATI video cards, as ATI doesn't always provide proprietary drivers. Nvidia does, for Intel good open source drivers are available. Broadcom wifi can give trouble because of lack of drivers too.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-02-09
One problem you're going to run into today is that most new laptops come with Windows 8 and use UEFI -- which presents more difficulties in installing Ubuntu than does the more traditional MBR approach.

Also, many laptops also come with Hybrid graphics (two different video chipsets) which also pose difficulty in Linux.

So, if you can get a laptop running Win7 and NOT having hybrid graphics, you will encounter less compatibility problems.
