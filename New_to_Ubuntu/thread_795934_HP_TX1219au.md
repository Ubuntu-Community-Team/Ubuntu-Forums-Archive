### Post by stairwayoflight on 2008-05-16
Hi!

How good are you with backups? Before you do install anything, I would make sure you have backup up all your data--win, programs, everything. If you want to see if Ubuntu will recognize your hardware, pop in a live cd and select 'Start Ubuntu'. Then if something doesn't work, you can work on fixing it. (eg. my webcam didn't work on Gutsy till I downloaded and compiled the driver, but it worked out of the box on Hardy.)

Personally I found Vista annoying, so I would recommend trashing it and installing Ubuntu. However, if your machine has a 'recovery partition', you may want to keep that just in case you want to switch back.

Good luck!
