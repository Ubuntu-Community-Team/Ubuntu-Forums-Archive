### Post by hailtothethief on 2009-09-24
System > Preferences > Appearance

You can choose from that list, or go to [http://www.gnome-look.org/]("http://www.gnome-look.org/?xcontentmode=100") to download more themes. Just drag the file into that window to add the theme to the list.

### Post by blackened on 2009-09-24
If you mean the progressbar in Firefox, then there are a few ways. 

The first would be to directly edit ~/.mozilla/firefox/*profile.default*/userChrome.css. 

The second, and easier in my opinion, would be to install the [Stylish extension]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/2108"), then find an appropriate style over at [http://userstyles.org/]("http://userstyles.org/") that suits your needs. 

You could also use Greasemonkey, but that is not its main purpose. Some of the downloads for Stylish will also work in Greasemonkey, and vise versa.
