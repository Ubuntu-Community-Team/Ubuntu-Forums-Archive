### Post by Hospadar on 2008-12-22
I know it's annoying when people tell you it's just a matter of personal preference, but its just a matter of personal preference.  A lot of people really like kde and a lot of other people really like gnome.  They just have different sets of software, both of which are pretty mature and functional.  You might just want to try both and see how you like them.  KDE has a really nicely integrated set of main applications, kmail, koffice, konquerer, dolphin all work together really nicely, and have tighter integration with eachother than their gnome equivalents.  That said, they do the exact same thing, and I suspect a lot of people don't really need the extra features.

You may just like the look of gnome a little better (or the other way around)

Also, anything that runs in kde will run in gnome just fine, so if you want to pick and choose it's easy to do.

### Post by drs305 on 2008-12-22
> **projecthikari said:**
> 
I wonder if I could Dual Boot both? Of course, what would be the point, right? ha~

Sure, just install 'kubuntu-desktop' and you can boot to either by changing your sessions options. The sessions options on the login page include setting a default, using the last one, or selecting any of  those available. You don't need a separate install and your grub doesn't even change if you just install the desktop.

It's a great way to see what the other flavors offer.

The psychocat's link in the previous post gives information on installing the various desktops.
