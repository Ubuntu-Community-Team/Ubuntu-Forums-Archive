### Post by lusbyclark on 2013-03-29
I know I may well be engaging in the *post hoc ergo propter hoc* logical fallacy, but I had a PC go completely bad after downloading several upgrades around noon today.  Actually the problem may had first surfaced while the download was underway.  Unlike every other time I have accepted the invitation to download upgrades, a hiccup occurred during the download process, making me think something was not quite right with this download.  When I first noticed the Upgrades Available icon that was posted over the Settings icon, it displayed the number 1, which I took to mean there was but a single upgrade being offered.  Shortly after I started the download process, the hiccup occurred, followed shortly by the number in the Upgrades Available icon changing to 108; I may not be precisely recalling this number, but it was definitely greater than a hundred.  I allowed the download process to continue, but was quite surprised by the short time it took the “download” to complete given the number of upgrades offered.  

The next hiccup occurred when I attempted to shut down the PC from the drop-down menu accessed by clicking on the cog icon (or is it a twisty-knob icon?) in the upper right hand corner on my display.  This did not work as it always had before.  The drop-down menu did come down and I was able to move the cursor down the "Shut Down" selection, but the PC would not shut down.  As I was going out for the afternoon, I was forced to turn the PC off with the power ON/OFF button.  

When I came back later this afternoon, I found I could no longer boot this PC up to full operate.  I made repeated attempts to get it to boot up but with no success*.*  Most times it balked during the boot process, displaying a message I am unable to now recall precisely but it was something about missing boot media.   One time it did manage to get all the way to Unity desktop display with the column of application icons along the left hand edge.  But despite this initial success, nothing else functioned properly; no access to the internet with FireFox, LibreOfficeWriter would not activate, the Home Folder would not open, and neither would the Dash.    

 

 My inquiry on this occasion is discover if anyone else has recently reported a similar problem or experience?   Fortunately for me this is a new PC still under full warranty.  I took it back to the store from whence it came in hopes the guys there will have it up and running once again by Monday afternoon.   I ask about the possibility of a similar experience just to make sure I am not overlooking something the PC store guys will be very unlikely to think of.   For the most part they are stuck on Windows and really don't have a great deal of experience or confidence in Linux.   I suppose I should also ask if Canonical did indeed offer and upgrade push today and if so, how many upgrades were included?

### Post by AndyOpie150 on 2013-03-30
I think your downloaded .iso file became corrupted while downloading. It happens when you download a large .iso file like a linux distro, the download can become corrupted if connection is slow. To compensate and allow a faster download speed of the file use 'Free Download Manager". Then check the md5 sum against the md5 sum on the download site. if they match your file is good. If not, your file is corrupted, do not use.

For a very good beginners linux install tutorial go here: [https://www.linuxdistrocommunity.com/forums/showthread.php?tid=524](https://www.linuxdistrocommunity.com/forums/showthread.php?tid=524)
