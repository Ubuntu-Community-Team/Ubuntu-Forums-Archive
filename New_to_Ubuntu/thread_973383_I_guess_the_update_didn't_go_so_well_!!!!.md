### Post by terry_gardener on 2008-11-06
yes i have had this problem opening vlc player. 

click places then computer then click filesystem, double click home and then right click your home directory. 

click properties, click open with, change to open folder. 

you also might need to do this for the folders within your home directory. 

hope this helps

### Post by stephanvaningen on 2008-11-06
*Go to Applications -> Accessories -> Terminal
 ```
nautilus
```
Go (almost) to the folder which don't work via the Places-menu, right-click it and choose 'Properties', there choose the tab 'Open with' and select 'File browser' or 'nautilus' (which-ever is available or most at top)
