I am new to Ubuntu and Linux. I am running 8.10. I spent the last week loading, messing up, installing again. I spent about 2 days reading many posts about backup and recovery options. I did get the 'dd' copy to work well. It ran over 2 hrs  copying 40g to 40g. It worked and the cloned drive booted fine.

NOT a pleasant process and slow. I searched all over these forums and Google for a decent program that I used in XP, like Ghost  or Casper. I did not want to 'backup files' I wanted to use the HD clone method to make bootable clones of my working master. I use this method for XP since almost ALL the image programs failed.

I found 2 very good programs that really worked. Both boot from a live CD and run a GUI interface. They WORK great. Both took about 45 to 55 minutes to perform a clone. I run the master as IDE and cloned to a USB HD. I could then boot from the USB clone -no problem.

Programs are
HDClone 3.7 [www.hdclone.com](www.hdclone.com)  FREE but they have a paid version.

Disk Copy 2.3 [www.easeus.com](www.easeus.com) FREE

DL and open the files and burn the ISO image. I  did the process in XP, but both can be done in LX.

I tried many others - all failed to work or were way to complex. I really do NOT want to learn all the terminal line syntax.
I just want to play around with Ubunta and the programs.
I like to hack around and find programs that can do what I want. I want to make sure I do not have to keep doing this re-install process all the time. 

There are many threads from other users that asked this same question

What program will give me a perfect clone of my HD ?

 Easy to use and mostly free.
Well these 2 are great - I am sure this will get posted other thread topics.
