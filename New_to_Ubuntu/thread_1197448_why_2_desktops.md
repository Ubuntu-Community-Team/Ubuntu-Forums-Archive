### Post by Mornedhel on 2009-06-26
> **ave2 said:**
> is it one of those must have features that makes life easier, or is it a nice to have that no one actually uses

It definitely is a must have feature. I cannot understand anymore the people who can't switch workspaces. I feel crippled under Windows (yes, I am aware you can install something to emulate multiple desktops under Windows).

I use it on a regular basis. Right now I have four workspaces.

Upper left : emacs in a terminal, fullscreen ; other terminals. This is my workspace for work.
Upper right : this Firefox instance, Pidgin when it's started, Liferea. This is my workspace for Web browsing and general loafing around.
Lower right : GMPC.
Lower left : free workspace for miscellaneous tasks.

The canonical example is the GIMP. It has multiple windows that all belong to a single application. Send them all to the same workspace, without any other applications cluttering your taskbar, and it's easy to switch between tasks.
