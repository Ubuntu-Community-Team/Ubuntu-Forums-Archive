### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2008-10-10
well for the first question, open office can save files as .doc's if you need it to.

### Post by Kellemora on 2008-10-10
Hi BSG

I write numerous newsletters with some mighty heavy formatting in them.

If you write them in Open Office Writer.odt, they convert to .doc files nearly flawlessy.  But the other way around Mickey$oft WORD.doc to OOw.odt can sometimetimes cause problems, if you are squeezing every last millimeter of space you can out of Word.

As a rule of Thumb, Word.doc, we keep margins in a full inch and at least 0.8 top and bottom, but prefer an inch there too.  
Else you may end up with messed up columns moving from msWord to OOwriter.

But going the other way around, from OOwriter to msWord has yet to cause a problem, provided we DO USE page breaks, else you may have to ADD a couple of carriage returns at the ends of documents sent to Word for OOw.

I do a lot of Tri-fold pamphlets.  Going from Word to OOw, OUCH is putting it mildly.  But going from OOw to Word, NEVER a problem!  So, needless to say, almost ALL of our work is now done on OOw.
