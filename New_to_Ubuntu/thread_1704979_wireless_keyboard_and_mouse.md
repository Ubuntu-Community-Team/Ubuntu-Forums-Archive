### Post by I2k4 on 2011-03-12
Let me just add there's a lot to be said for going to a store for a "hands on" idea of what they feel like before buying - a lot of these devices look great and have nice features, but are spongy or odd in some way.  I have a Microsoft wireless mouse with four little bumps on the bottom that slow it down, and have had keyboards that are very spongy and unresponsive, or irritate you with their click or lack of click.  The MK300 is a little soft for me, but was a great buy and works well.  Good luck with your purchase.
