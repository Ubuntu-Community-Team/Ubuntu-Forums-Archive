### Post by coolbrook on 2009-10-21
Determine the make and model of the wireless router.  How about the adapters?  Is it a "Wireless N" (802.11n) network?  Your IT guys and girls should be running a router that can handle office traffic and best deal with RF interference.  I trust that they have the router in a central location and perhaps they're using repeaters if the space is large.
