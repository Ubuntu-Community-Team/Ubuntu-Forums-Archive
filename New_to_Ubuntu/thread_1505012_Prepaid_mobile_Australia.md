### Post by bumanie on 2010-06-08
I use a virginbroadband 3g modem - Huawei E620 - it worked out of the box in 9.04, but not 9.10 or 10.04. With the latest kernel on 10.04, however it now works. Most of the 3g modems on the market will work with linux - some are harder to get going than others. I can only reveal my experience and can say at present the Huawei E-620 works fine on 10.04.
To set up, you have to go to network manager and fill out a couple of windows (via edit connections under "Mobile Broadband" - add connection number and VPN etc.) and then disable a couple of authentication methods to get this 3g modem to work - it's not hard and if you get a 3g modem that you cannot get working, post here and someone will help. As said, most will work with the minimum of fuss and there are usually workarounds for those that don't work out of the box. By they way, when I last looked, virgin still had among the best 'internet deals' - but you can check out providers and deals yourself, as they are always changing.
