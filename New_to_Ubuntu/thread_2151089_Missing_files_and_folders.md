### Post by MidnightGrey on 2013-06-03
Hi,

So assuming you correctly installed Ubuntu, your windows partition is intact! not to fear. To access your Windows C-drive, so to speak simply open up nautilus.
You can do this by: Pressing Alt-F2 then typing nautilus in the pop-up; or locating your "Home Folder" button in the left-dash menu.

Once in nautilus, The left-hand panel should lists your "Devices". 

Click on each one to open them until you locate the partition which resembles your C-Drive. It should contain these folders: Documents and Settings, Program Files, Users, Windows.

Now, assuming you were used to your Windows file-structure/shortcuts and saved your files in the folders such as My Documents and My Pictures, etc you might not know where they are kept. You can locate your folders Here: <C-Drive>/Users/<YourName>/(My Documents/Pictures/Downloads etc...)

Hope this helps.
