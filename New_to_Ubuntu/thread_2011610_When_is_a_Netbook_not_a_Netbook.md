### Post by Cheesemill on 2012-06-27
Personally I would class anything with an ARM or Atom processor and 2GB or less of RAM as a Netbook, the fact it has an SSD or an HD doesn't make any difference.

The high powered models you are thinking of are probably Ultrabooks, which do have a fixed specification:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultrabook#Requirements](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultrabook#Requirements)

Also there isn't a version of Ubuntu designed for Netbooks any more, what used to be UNR (Ubuntu Netbook Remix) was just the first trial of the Unity interface which now comes as standard with Ubuntu.

PS - These are all personal opinions so are likely to be wrong :)
Also when Windows 8 is released we are likely to see laptops with ARM processors and higher specs, for example [Microsoft Surface]("http://www.microsoft.com/surface/en/us/default.aspx").
