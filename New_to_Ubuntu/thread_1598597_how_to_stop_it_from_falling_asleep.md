### Post by yetiman64 on 2010-10-16
Actually to remove the password input requirement, System > Preferences > Screensaver, and detick the box for "Lock screen when screensaver is active".

Edit: You may actually have a sreensaver called "blank screen" (blacks out the sreen) and your machine may not be "sleeping" but the screensaver may be running instead.

### Post by DayLite on 2010-10-16
> **Proto_Blaze said:**
> i did that long ago and it still sleeps

[https://launchpad.net/~caffeine-developers/+archive/ppa](https://launchpad.net/~caffeine-developers/+archive/ppa)

The latest Ubuntu packages for the Caffeine application to easily disable and re-enable the screensaver and powersaving features of your computer.

System > Administration > Software Sources > Other Software > +Add >
 deb [http://ppa.launchpad.net/caffeine-developers/ppa/ubuntu](http://ppa.launchpad.net/caffeine-developers/ppa/ubuntu) lucid main

Click Close, then click Reload

Go to Administration > Synaptic Package Manager and enter caffeine in the search field & right click Mark For Installation

 Click Apply & then click Apply again

Caffiene will now be installed & will be in the Accessories menu

Click on Caffeine, the program will then launch, you will now see a small Coffee cup icon in the top panel, click on it to disable screensaver.
