"No source connected" just means that the computer is not sending any information to the monitor.  If this is a desktop, unplug and replug teh monitor cable to the tower.  Sometimes reseating a connection can get stuff going ... 

If it's not that simple (most likely) then a couple more questions:

1.  When you turn on the power to the computer, do you hear the fans inside turn on?
   -- if not, then it is likely a power supply issue
 2.  If the fans inside the computer turn on, do you hear the Hard Drive making noise? 
  -- If not, then it is likely a problem with your motherboard or something complex and you might be better off taking it to a repair shop to be sure
 -- if the Hard drive Does make noise, then it is likely an issue with your video card not giving info to your monitor.  Depending on how good you are with computer hardware, you can try reseating the card and see if the connections were the problem

If the connections are not the problem, then I do recommend taking it to a professional ... not that the advice you get here will not help, but it is a lot easier and faster for a pro to look at it in person, then diagnosing at a distance.  

I hope that helps :-)

### Post by irv on 2013-01-17
If you see a message on the monitor you have power to the monitor by not a video signal. Check your video cable connection by unplugging it and then re-plugging it back in. Then check the power light on the PC, if you have power to the PC but still don't get a video signal, then you might have to try reseating the video card in the PC. If this doesn't work then most likely your video card when bad. You can test this if you have another video card to try.
Now if you have no power light on the PC when you start it, then it could be a power supply.
I have had power supplies go out on me, and also have had video cards go bad. These are the two most likely things to go wrong.
One last thing you can try is plug the power into a different source. Maybe you blew a fuse or braker.

### Post by irv on 2013-01-19
One thing no one mentioned. When you keep a computer in a cold area and you then run it, you will have heat build up in the power supply when it starts up. This will cause condensation in the power supply casing it to short out and burn up. If you smell the power supply and if it smells burnt this could be the problem.
Before I discard a computer I would have it tested. If you can't do it yourself take it somewhere where someone can do it for you.
