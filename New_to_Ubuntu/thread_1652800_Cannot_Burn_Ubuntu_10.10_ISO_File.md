Take it easy :)
TeoBigusGeekus has given you the best advice. From our experience, upgrading an old version of Ubuntu to a new version is problematic enough to avoid. The upgrade might be successful but after that, perhaps you'll face some problems.

The best way is to backup all your important data, format and start installing.

I'd suggest to download and install 10.04 not 10.10. However, it's your call. Why? because simply 10.04 is LTS (Long Term Support) which means you don't have to worry about upgrading it for 3 years.

If you don't want to go for 10.04 and you already have 10.10, just to be in the safe side, try to check MD5SUM for your downloaded iso. In my signature, there's HOWTO (check step1 link).

In order to create the LiveCD or LiveUSB, here's how: [http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download)
Check #2.

What OS are you using at the moment to create the LiveCD? what program?

If your machine is able to boot from USB then I'd recommend to go for that option ([http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/](http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/) to create that) but if your machine doesn't boot from USB, forget that. Even though there's a solution for that but it's a bit advanced now.

Try to burn at the low speed, say 16x.

Let us know what will happen with you!

Good luck ;)
