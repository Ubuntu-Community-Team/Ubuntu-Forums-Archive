### Post by Cypher on 2009-02-23
You can go down two paths there. One is the headset that comes with the standard audio in/out inputs that would get connected into your soundcards mic and audio out jack. The other is the USB route.

If you go down the audio-jack route then you just need to choose a set you like and as long as your soundcard is currently working in Ubuntu, the headset will work fine.

If you go down the USB route, in all likelyhood the mic will also work, unless it's some really crazy brand that needs their own drivers installed in Windows/Mac before it even functions as a regular microphone.

I bought a "no-name" USB microphone recently, plugged into my Ubuntu machine and it was found and recognized. I could configure it as a sound input source and record audio with it. I found the mic to be of lacking quality and the volume to be terribly low even though I'd jacked up the volume everywhere I could find. I then tried the microphone on my wife's XP machine and found that it worked no better than an on my Ubuntu machine, so in this case the microphone is to be blamed..
