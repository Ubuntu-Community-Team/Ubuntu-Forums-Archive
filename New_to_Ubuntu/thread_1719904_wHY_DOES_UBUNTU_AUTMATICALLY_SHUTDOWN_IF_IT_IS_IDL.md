### Post by r-senior on 2011-04-02
Have a look at your Power Management settings. If you have a battery you can get to it from the battery icon in the panel, otherwise it's in System -> Preferences -> Power Management.

Look at the "Put computer to sleep when inactive for ..." options.

---

### Post by Paqman on 2011-04-02
You can also inhibit the power management feature if you want. Right click on a panel > Add to panel > Inhibit applet. When you click on the inhibit applet it will stop the machine from going to sleep until you click the applet again.

### Post by Shibblet on 2011-04-02
It may actually be set to "Hibernate" when idle for too long.

Hibernate is similar to suspend/sleep only instead of suspending to RAM, it suspends to your hard drive.

I personally set my Laptop to suspend/sleep when idle for too long, for two reasons.  First, it saves on your battery life, and secondly, it turns back on REALLY quick.  Like within 2-3 seconds.  Right back to your desktop.
