### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-11-19
The desktop environment is just the GUI and a set of programs. Installing KDE (which is more Windows-like than Gnome) will not make Windows programs run, will not increase hardware support, and won't necessarily make your new system more familiar to you. KDE is slightly similar to Windows, but it's not their aim to copy Microsoft.

I just thought I'd warn you of that so you don't go into it with the wrong impression.

You can install KDE from Synaptic Package Manager, or [you can just click this link.]("apt:kde")
