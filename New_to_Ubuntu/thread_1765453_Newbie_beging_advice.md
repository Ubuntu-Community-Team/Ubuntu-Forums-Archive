### Post by Anuovis on 2011-05-23
I wouldn't advice to buy books right now because a lot of essential information is around the net. Once you get around your system more and know what sort of things you really want to learn, then you might try looking for something more specific.

As it is now, don't be afraid to play around. As a rule of thumb, anything that requires a password can alter and damage your system but otherwise it's pretty safe to click and see what happens.

Another good way is to solve particular problems once they arise. You can search for all sorts of recipes around the net and pick up practical knowledge along the way. Once you do solve something, make a note and save it. It really helps to keep a small personal database of how-to because sooner or later you might have to perform the same task again.

It will be confusing at first but Ubuntu is no harder than Windows once you get used to it.
