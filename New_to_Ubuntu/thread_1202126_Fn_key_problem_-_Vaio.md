### Post by quixote on 2009-07-03
I had a Vaio a few years ago, and even though I loved it for the general gorgeousness, I had the same problem with keys not working as it got older.  Very annoying.

Two things, both of which you may have already tried.  

Get one of those cans of compressed air at a computer store, hold the keyboard upside down, and carefully blow all the crap out of it as much as you can.  If you do it over a clean dark surface, you'll be amazed at how much falls out. :D

It's possible to map functions onto keys of your choosing.  It doesn't look simple, eg [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=962098"), but maybe that can get you started in finding a workaround.  Also search for "key remap ubuntu" (no quotes).  There has got to be some simple little program out there that will do this.  ??
