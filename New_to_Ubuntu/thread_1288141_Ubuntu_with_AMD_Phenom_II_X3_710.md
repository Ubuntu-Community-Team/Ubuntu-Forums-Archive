### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-10-12
If it's an AM3 processor, you might as well have an AM3 motherboard. Firstly, so you can take advantage of the faster RAM. Secondly, because I'm sure future AMD processors will work in AM3 boards and not in AM2 boards. And thirdly, because not all AM2 motherboards have the necessary BIOS upgrade to work with AM3 CPUs.

The answer is much easier for the question about the recommended GPU. Whatever your price range is for graphics cards, spend that much on an Nvidia card. Don't even bother looking at anything else. I personally have the GTX260 but they are very long and might not fit into your case! If you don't do much in the way of gaming, then a 9800GS will be fine, and you can even get silent passive-cooled versions of it.
