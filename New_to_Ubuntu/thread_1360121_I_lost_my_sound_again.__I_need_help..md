### Post by Sir Jasper on 2009-12-20
Hi,

Sooner or later something is likely to ¨break¨ beyond fixing. Hard drives can fry in seconds - that happened to me once.

If you have a reliable clone you can fix almost any problem yourself and have your system re-running the way you had it (and presumably liked it) and with all or almost all of your documents, music etc intact.

Backups can be used between clones.

Two days ago I had a strange problem with my Gnome-system-monitor that I could not figure how to fix - it took me a total of less than 10 minutes to clone everything back to full working order - it has taken me months to configure Xubuntu and Wine to my entire satisfaction so I believe in applying the KISS principle. 

It is a question of using sensible precaution rather than hopeful anticipation.

My regards
