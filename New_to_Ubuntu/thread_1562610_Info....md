### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-08-28
> **Zombie Robot said:**
> Is it really that easy or are you making it sound easier then it is lol

The codec installation is as easy as, the first time you play a DivX movie, Ubuntu will ask you if you want to install the codec for that (and give you a reason why you might not want to). Click OK twice and it will install.

Dual-booting is not much harder than what I describe. Choose the option "Install side-by-side", move the slider on the bottom bar graph to the left to allocate some space for Ubuntu, and that's it. It's also a good idea to defragment Windows (from within Windows) before doing the installation, but it's not necessary as Ubuntu's installer can make space in the same manner if necessary; it's just that it might be quicker to do it from Windows.

### Post by presence1960 on 2010-08-28
> **3rdalbum said:**
> The codec installation is as easy as, the first time you play a DivX movie, Ubuntu will ask you if you want to install the codec for that (and give you a reason why you might not want to). Click OK twice and it will install.

**_Dual-booting is not much harder than what I describe. Choose the option "Install side-by-side", move the slider on the bottom bar graph to the left to allocate some space for Ubuntu, and that's it._** It's also a good idea to defragment Windows (from within Windows) before doing the installation, but it's not necessary as Ubuntu's installer can make space in the same manner if necessary; it's just that it might be quicker to do it from Windows.

First back up your data. The install process is smooth but always have a back up just in case. You don't want to be the one crying on here that you lost your data.

Then defrag windows once, twice is even better. Turn off system restore in Control Panel. This will allow the partitioner to easily create the partitions for ubuntu when you choose the install side by side option. After ubuntu is installed you can boot to windows and turn system restore back on.
