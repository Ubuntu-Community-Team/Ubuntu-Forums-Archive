### Post by mcduck on 2013-02-18
> **sammiev said:**
> It will show you when important updates are available. I usually just check for updates once a week otherwise.

Actually Ubuntu will also automatically notify you about less important updates as well. It's just limited to one per week (counting from previous update), so if you update manually every week or more often ypou'll never see it happening... :)

So, the rules are that important security updates are offered to the user immediatelly, everything else after at least 7 days have passed from previous update.

### Post by HermanAB on 2013-02-18
I don't fix it if it ain't broke...

Linux is as reliable as a refrigerator and most problems are due to finger trouble.

I completely re-install my desktop/laptop machines about every two or three years.  Apart from that, I almost never apply any updates.  I do watch the security bulitins, but only apply a patch if it is extremely serious, for example I applied the SSL patches some years ago.

Even servers, I install, lock down and then I leave them alone until something fails.  My last web and mail server ran for 4 years till the PSU blew up.  My present web and mail server has now been running for 1210 days (3.3 years) with zero downtime and zero maintenance.
