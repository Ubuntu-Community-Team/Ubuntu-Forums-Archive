### Post by inameiname on 2010-05-09
I noticed this is the default if say you have a non-digital tv hooked up through composite or svideo to your video card. Lucid's set automatically on mirroring the two, and of course the max you can get on a tv is much less than a computer monitor or lcd screen, so it sticks with the lower one until you change. Just an fyi, as if you don't have another monitor hooked up, then it's not your problem. hehe.
