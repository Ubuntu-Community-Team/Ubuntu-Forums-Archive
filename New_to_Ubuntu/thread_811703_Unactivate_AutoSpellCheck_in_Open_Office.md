### Post by Ludwig9 on 2008-05-29
I am in Hardy and Open Office. I want to write a single lower case i but Open Office won't let me. It keeps changing i to I. How can I stop it from doing this? HELP says that you can activate the AutoSpellcheck icon on the standard bar, but how can you unactivate it? I have both a Spellcheck icon and an AutoSpellcheck icon there. I want to turn both of these off permanently and for all files. I have gone into Tools/Options/Language Settings/Writing Aids and unchecked everything I can but Open Office still refuses to allow me to type a separate i. 
> Automatically Check Spelling 
You can have OpenOffice.org automatically check spelling while you type and underline possible misspelt words with a red wavy line.
To check spelling automatically while you type:
1.Activate the AutoSpellcheck icon on the Standard bar.
2.Right-click a word with a red wavy underline, and then choose a suggested replacement word from the list, or from the AutoCorrect submenu.

To exclude words from the spellcheck:

### Post by Ludwig9 on 2008-05-29
To answer my own question: while in OpenOffice go to Format/AutoFormat/WhileTyping. Uncheck "While Typing" and the AutoSpellcheck stops.

### Post by theophiles on 2008-05-29
There are two things you can do.
If your only major autocorrect problem is wanting a lower case "i," simply click on tools and then click on autocorrect. Choose the "replace" tab. There you will find a list of items in alphabetical order that Open Office automatically replaces. Scroll down to "i" then select it with your mouse and click the delete button on the right side of the box. This should remove that single problem. 

If you really want to turn off autoformat entirely to go click on format>autoformat and then deselect "while typing"
