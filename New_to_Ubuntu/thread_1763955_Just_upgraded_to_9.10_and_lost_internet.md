### Post by Martyn Thompson on 2011-05-21
If it was wireless I had a similar problem with both THAT update and a couple of following updates too. 

I found I had to connect to the router directly (via a network cable) and then use proprietary drivers (SYSTEM > ADMINISTRATION > ADDITIONAL DRIVERS) followed by activating those. 

Please note that this stopped the boot splash screen displaying properly and in some versions I was able to remove the video driver.  It did seem to require this to install the wireless driver first though. 

Once you got those working you can disconnect from the network cable and wireless *should* be OK. 

Best wishes. 

You could post your netbook too - might help other correspondents.
