I recently started to use ubuntu 9.10 and I really really like how easy it is on my hard drives. With my raid0 of 1st generation sata drives I had constant stability issues with xp, with ubuntu, it runs solid as a rock. (on some hd benchmarks I can max out the sata bandwidth)

Okay, back on topic.

I recently acquired a large number of old IBM desktops from a small company closing down and have been tinkering around to breathe some life back into them. They range from 3/4 year old computers to computers over a decade old. The newer ones run ubuntu or xubuntu fairly well, however some of the older ones simply can't.
One of which is a Pentium MMX 233MHz system with 128MB of ram and 2 hard drives 4.2GB and 2.5 GB. I have currently been trying Puppy Linux with this, it boots off of CD if I use Wakepup2 on floppy, but runs a little sluggish. Also, after installing it to hard disk it won't boot at all. I am currently working this out, but would like suggestions for any minimalist, active or recently active within the past 5 years, ubuntu-like(preferably not a must) distros for computers with similar specs to this.

I have a good amount of random hardware at my disposal that I can shift around to try different distros at the same time so please fire .
