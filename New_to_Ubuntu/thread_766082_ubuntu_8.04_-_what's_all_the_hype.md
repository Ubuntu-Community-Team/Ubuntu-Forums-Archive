### Post by BaffledMollusc on 2008-04-25

Well, some of it is hype and publicity, which tends to feed on itself.

Still, it's a very good distro. It's specifically focussed on the desktop and ease of use, rather than being more server or geek oriented. Not that it doesn't have all the power and flexibility of linux, of course.

What else? Very good hardware detection, and because of its popularity it has a large community and forums to offer help. Finally, because it's so popular, most open source software is available as a .deb specifically for Ubuntu, saving you doing the compiling thing.
