### Post by karasuman on 2008-06-30
Okay, let's see if I can remember everything.

The transparency on the cube is a setting on the rotate cube plug-in for Compiz.  Just double-click, and you should find a lot of different options.  You also want to enable a skydome.  The image on the background is a skydome image; I found it on gnome-look.  I also have cube reflection enables (another compiz setting).

I installed emerald for my window themes and played around with the gradient one.  I made the borders wider to show off woo transparency, etc.  To adjust window transparency, you can either use alt+scrollbuttononmouse to change the transparency of an individual window, or go to general settings in Compiz and add transparency settings.  If you pick to add a new setting, you can use "grab" to get the name of the window you want.  If you want everything to be just a little transparent, use type=normal and set it to about 95.  If you screw up and make things too transparent, don't worry, because you can still change them with the scroll button on your mouse.  :)

To make the panels transparent, just right click on them, choose properties, and edit the stuff in the background tab.  Also, it's fun to right click on the panel, click "about panels", and then type fff, if you haven't done that yet.  :)

The background on the cube itself is just a wallpaper.  I couldn't tell you where I got it now, but if you specifically like it, I'll send it to you.  I recommend gnome-look as a great source of wallpapers and, well, all this stuff.  :)
