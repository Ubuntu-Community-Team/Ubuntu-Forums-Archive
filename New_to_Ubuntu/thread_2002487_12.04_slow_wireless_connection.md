### Post by chili555 on 2012-06-12
> There are various guides and suggestions, like this one, but i'm not sure what would work in my specific case.No. It's for the driver *iwlwifi* and your wireless card uses the notoriously twitchy *rtl8192se*. There are a few things you can try. Please open a terminal and do:```
sudo modprobe -r rtl8192se
sudo modprobe rtl8192se swenc=1
```If it helps, we can write one file and make it permanent.

Is your router set to WPA and WPA2 mixed mode? If so, you probably will have better luck with WPA2 only. Is your router capable of N speeds? Many Linux drivers are troubled by N. Try setting the router to B and G only. Finally, you might try different channels in your router; I have the best speed at channels 1 and 11.
