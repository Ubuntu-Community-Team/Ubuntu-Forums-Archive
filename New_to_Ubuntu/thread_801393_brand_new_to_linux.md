### Post by ebeckhusen on 2008-05-20
Well, for me, Ubuntu runs _much_ faster than Windows on my machine.  I think it makes better use of resources (ram, disk usage) than Windows, but I'll let someone better informed speak to that.  You can definitely use a CD-R to burn the image onto disk.

I don't know about the games, I'm not a gamer, but you can get many games to work under Wine.

I would suggest that before you wipe out Windows completely you do a "dual boot" and keep it around for a little while to test out Ubuntu and make sure it does everything you want.  Besides, there's a little bit of learning you'll need to do, so it might help to have Windows around for a little bit, in case you get frustrated with something and need to go back to it.

Good luck, and remember to use these boards whenever you have a question, there's a wealth of information to be had here.

### Post by lazyart on 2008-05-20
Well, I would advise against taking the wrecking ball to your Windows partition.

Dabble in Ubuntu or whichever Linux distro you choose, then go back to the familiar confines of Windows.  At some point you'll get frustrated with the Linux way of doing things and you'll want to take a break and just get stuff accomplished, like playing a game. :)
Come back the next day, spend an hour or so looking around and continue to get acclimated.  Eventually you'll either hate it and stay with Microsoft, or you'll decide it's worth the time to learn and stay with linux.

This is what worked for me.  I don't really venture much back into Windows, except when I am administering my home network.  A year an a half after installing Dapper Drake I just don't miss anything from the Windows side.  I surf, use OpenOffice, do my banking, etc.  Burn music, watch movies-- it's all there.  What you'll find really amazing is that the software that would set you back a few hundred dollars you can find equivalents for that don't cost a penny.

Good luck.  We're all here if you have questions.  And you will. :)
