### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-11-06
Laptops CAN be very different from desktop -- having a hard time understanding why anyone would say different.  

The primary difference is that laptops tend to come with specialized hardware for which Linux drivers may not be available.  Look at all the threads on hybrid graphics -- and the enormous amount of work you have to go to on Linux to get that work -- at all!  Other hardware than can pose problems include SD memory card slots and USB 3.0 jacks.  While these work "out of the box" on MS Windows (because the specialized drivers come preloaded), they often work poorly or not at all in Linux.

Furthermore, with desktop PCs, unless you get a low-priced entry-level model, there are nearly always additional "slots" allowing you to add your own graphics and/or networking cards.  Laptops generally do NOT provide this flexibility.

I think a better way of looking at this compatibility with laptops is to ask if there is anything inherent in Ubuntu that prevents it from being used in a laptop -- and in that case, the answer is NO.
