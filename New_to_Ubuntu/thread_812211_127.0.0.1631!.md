### Post by neurostu on 2008-05-29
I by no means am an expert and I could be wrong, but 127.0.0.1 is the loop back address, meaning any data directed to that IP will get automatically routed back into your machine.  Essentially the ip address 127.0.0.1 points back to localhost or self.  

If you're trying to setup a network printer that uses that address you will need to forward all inbound traffic on port 631 to your printer, that way any request to 127.0.0.1:631 gets routed back to localhost and then forwarded to where ever your printer actually is.
