### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-02-16
> Ubuntu website says that when I am logged out it doesn't mean that I am logged out of other websites WHAT DOES THIS MEAN

What we have with Ubuntu is simply a simple system called Single Sign On Service. Also known as a Ubuntu One account. We sign up with a email account for a user name and give a password and that one account can then log us into other Ubuntu related web sites.

So, the account that lets us log in to Ubuntu forums, will also let us log us into AskUbuntu, Ubuntu Discourse & Launchpad. And most likely others that I do not know about.

When I log into Ubuntu Forums I am not automatically logged into these other web sites. No. I have to manually login to each of these web sites.

Now, of course I can be logged into more than one web site at a time. So, when I log out from Ubuntu forums I do not wish to also be logged out of any other Ubuntu site that I am logged into. That would be most inconvenient. And it does not happen. 

When I log out of Ubuntu Discourse I am not automatically logged out of Ubuntu Forums. That is what that statement means.

[https://login.ubuntu.com/+faq](https://login.ubuntu.com/+faq)

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2016-02-17
First, Linux isn't Windows and can't be made to be Windows, any more than Windows can be made to be Linux.  Or OS X can be made to be either.

If you need Windows, i.e., specific Windows software, specific Windows services, or specific Windows file formats, use Windows.  Linux was not created a couple of decades ago as a corrective for the annoyances of Windows.

Go slowly. Don't panic. Don't expect to make the transition in one day.

What software?  What sites? How was that software installed?  Did you refresh -- close and reopen -- the browser afterwards?
