### Post by QIII on 2012-01-18
It is quite possible that your "vintage" GPU is simply not up to the task with Unity.  While Linux generally is happy to run on vintage hardware, sometimes a lighter desktop environment (DE) is a better choice.  There are a bewildering number of DEs, so don't panic when people start talking about KDE, GNOME shell, GNOME 2, Xfce, LXDE, etc, etc.  They are all just various graphical environments to display stuff on the screen for you.  As with just about everything else Linux, you get to choose from a wide (and sometimes very confusing) array of items from the a la carte menu.

When you get to the login screen (it's been a while since I fiddled with a standard Ubuntu installation) there should be a couple of buttons around the actual login text box.  One of those will give you the option to select from several "desktop environment sessions".

Choose something other than Unity.  Classic, perhaps.

Welcome to the forums.  Don't give up on Ubuntu (or Linux in general yet).  It'll just take a while to learn a new language when you are used to another.
