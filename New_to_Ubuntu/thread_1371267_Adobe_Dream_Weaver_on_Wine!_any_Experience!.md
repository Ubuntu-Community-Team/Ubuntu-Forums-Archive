### Post by starcraft.man on 2010-01-03
[http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=183](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=183)

^ Lists all versions of dreamweaver and their rating. It would seem mx2004 works best though I can't vouch for that. I'd recommend you take a good look at native alternatives listed [here]("http://linuxappfinder.com/development/web/websiteeditors"). I know it takes some effort to learn other apps but pays in longrun, especially considering how expensive adobe suites are. Not to mention some of my web friends really don't like dreamweaver, that's subjective opinion you can take or leave.

If you really must use dreamweaver then I'd suggest as a last resort a VM (see VirtualBox) of XP and then inside that dreamweaver will work perfectly, but then you'd have to wonder if your really moving to linux.
