### Post by Impavidus on 2014-11-08
It seems to me that OP uses the word "download" where most people use "install". Confusing, but when enough people around you get the terminology wrong it can be very difficult to learn it the right way. In addition to possible translation confusion. On some other systems people "download" an "app" and then have it ready for use, leading to the impression that "download" and "install" are synonyms. Under this assumption the installation procedure was the standard, using automatic partitioning to install alongside Windows.

I rarely read books on open source software. They tend to be outdated even before you finished reading. There are some exception, specific programs or programming languages that people rely on and haven't changed their basics for over 20 years. Think of vim or bash. But not something as an entire operating system, including its user interface.
