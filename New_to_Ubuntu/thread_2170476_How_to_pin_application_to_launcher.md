### Post by baker-quin on 2013-08-26
How do I pin an application to the launcher? I wish to pin FileZilla3. I can see this in the home directory when I type ls -a as .filezilla. I can also see it in the dash menu but I cannot right click on it for a task menu.

I'm running 12.04

---

### Post by coffeecat on 2013-08-26
> **baker-quin said:**
> I can also see it in the dash menu

If it's a GUI app, simply start it with a left click on the dash icon which will cause a launcher icon to appear. Right click on this -> lock to launcher.

The hidden .filezilla folder you can see in your home directory is not the app but where your personal configuration files are for the app.

---

### Post by deadflowr on 2013-08-26
When you launch the app from the dash-ubuntu_unity-menu-system, you should get an icon in the launcher, follow Coffeecat's advice and right click lock to launcher.
Or simply drag and drop from the dash to the launcher panel.
