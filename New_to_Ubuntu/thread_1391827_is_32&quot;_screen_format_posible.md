### Post by Hospadar on 2010-01-27
Depends a lot on your video hardware and what sort of inputs the screen has.

Typically nowadays if the screen has dvi in it should allow full native resolution.  Also hdmi can be converted with a simple (read: cheap) converter (it's the same format in an electrical sense, just a different plug).

It really depends more on the actual resolution of the screen and if your video drivers support that mode.

I'd just make sure I got a screen I could return if it didn't work, or if you have a laptop or a non-gigantic computer, try taking it to best buy (or wherever it is they sell big tv's near you) and ask if they'll let you plug it in and try it out.  I bet they'd let you if you made it seem like you'd be interested in buying an expensive tv from them.

On a side note, if I had to make a guess as to what video hardware/drivers would have the highest likelihood of working, I'd say probably an nvidia card with the "restricted" (published by nvidia) drivers.
