### Post by jbrown96 on 2009-12-10
> **Cheesemill said:**
> They sound well above spec.

You may want to check how much space is available for the PCI slots if you want to put in TV cards to use it as a PVR. If it's a rackmount server they often only have half-height slots.

You don't need much processor power for a backend. If you get good TV cards, then all the encoding is done on the card. Those processors (probably P4s) will just eat electricity for no reason. 

For a backend, you need good hard drives (fast if you need to do multiple jobs at once: multiple records, record and playback, multiple playbacks) and lots of space. As Cheesemill said, the pci slots are your largest concern.

### Post by jbrown96 on 2009-12-10
Make sure that there are SATA connectors for the hard drives. It may have something weird like SAS or Fibrechannel as the primary connector, which would be very bad. You'd have to spend 2-3x for hard drives that have significantly smaller capacity. Servers don't tend to be configured to take large drives (>1TB)

### Post by jbrown96 on 2009-12-10
> **daveb272 said:**
> Thanks to all.  I believe i am limited to only one pci slot with this particular rack mount.  My original idea was the zotac 9300, but now that the dual core atom ion is out, i thiink i might go with that one.  My goal is to record, catalog, burn espisodes of my favorite programs on to dvd.  I wasnt sure what is better for this purpose, dual core, or dual processor.

Dual processor motherboards are extremely expensive; you are generally forced to get motherboards designed for servers, which have lots of extra stuff (many ram slots, SAS/fibrechannel, ECC support, etc.) that you very rarely need. 
I think that you would need something better than an atom; I've heard that the performance isn't really up to par. A Core 2 Duo should be plently to do this. This will allow you to burn, record, and playback simultaneously.

Many servers, especially rankmounts, don't even have DVD drive slots. Plus if you are going to be doing a lot of recording, then a large desktop box that can hold 2 hard drives, probably 2 tuner cards, and 2 optical drives would be perfect. It won't be expensive, and you can get stuff done really fast, by which I mean you can record two shows at once (using both tuner cards, and not have any hard drive write problems because you can balance the recordings between the disks). The two optical drives makes sense because you can get all your stuff on disk very fast if you don't burn anything for a while. 

I'm not sure on the cost of the tuner cards, but a brand new system like this should cost more than $400. Not sure what kind of deal you are getting on the server, but I wouldn't think that it would be too much cheaper.
