### Post by RusH487 on 2009-01-19
I recently installed Ubuntu 8.04 on my laptop to see what all the 'linux hype' was about and I have been very impressed.  So impressed, in fact, that I decided to partition my desktop and set up a dual-boot with Windows.

So far, while I've experienced a lot of headaches (as was expected with starting a brand new operating system), I haven't found anything I could not fix through an hour or two of searching the web/forums.  Unfortunately, I seem to have come across a problem that I cannot fix for the life of me.

I cannot get audio to play through my computer at all.  I am trying to use my Sound Blaster Live! Value card (CT4832) and I have even tried using the on-board sound card but to no avail.  I have searched and searched and found a lot of forums all suggesting many different approaches through ALSA and even one that suggested I shut off the mainboard sound in bios.  I have followed all of these tutorials to the best of my ability but still nothing works.  So far, this is what I can tell you:

- I know my sound card is not muted...  I have checked this both in the terminal and by double-clicking on the volume controls.

- I have followed every step in [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=205449&highlight=Sound+Blaster+Live") successfully as far as I can tell...  I used the emu10k1 module.

For whatever reason, the sound still will not work and I am at a loss.  I should also let it be known that I am not very savvy when it comes to the command prompt (unless of course commands are written out exactly as they are to be executed) 

Please, any help will be much appreciated as I'd really hate to have to boot up Windows every time I wanted to watch an episode of Lost or listen to music...  Thanks in advance.

### Post by RusH487 on 2009-01-20
Update:

Un-freakin-believable...  After trying everything I possibly could imagine to get my sound to work correctly, as a last-ditch effort, I plugged in an RCA cable to the back of my surround sound receiver and connected it to a Coaxial output I had hooked up to my motherboard when I first built my computer 5 years ago.  In the 5 years that I've had this machine, I've never once been able to get that coaxial output to work correctly in Windows but as soon as I plugged it in this time I heard crisp, clear, 5.1 audio.

Now, I still can't get my creative sound blaster live value card to work correctly but it seems I've found a better solution that I'd once thought to be impossible.  Hopefully no one else runs across this thread with the same problem I had and no alternative way to fix it.

Ubuntu just keeps getting better and better it seems...
