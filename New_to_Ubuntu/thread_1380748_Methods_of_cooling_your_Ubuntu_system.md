### Post by ankspo71 on 2010-01-14
Hi,
If I had a laptop with Ubuntu on it, and it ran a little too warm, I would disable anything that I didn't need that consumed resources. Some of these are a little drastic, but they should help a bit.

disable 3d effects
disable any unneeded services
disable any unneeded startup applications.
change wallpaper to a low res wallpaper (or none at all) 
install gtk2-engines-xfce to make your gnome theme a little lighter
Consider a second desktop installation (something lighter than gnome)

Consider using lighterweight programs.... (no need to uninstall the originals)
Brasero -> xfburn
Open Office -> Abiword
Totem & rhythmbox ->vlc
I can't think of any more off hand...

Always run your laplop on a flat smooth surface.
Run a fan in the room where your laptop runs.
elevate the laptop to increase airflow under it. 
(not sure what you could use though, but you might be able to buy stick on rubber feet somewhere)

Then of course, there are laptop/netbook cooling pads...

just a number of ideas off the top of my head...
Hope some of these help.

### Post by Miljet on 2010-01-14
I don't think that your temps are very far from normal. My Toshiba P-205 normally runs 46 to 50 c at idle or light load.

Streaming video runs my temps up faster than anything else. I once had it up to 98/99 c while checking U-Tube videos before I noticed. As soon as I kicked out of U-Tube, the temps immediately began falling back to normal.

As the above poster suggested, elevating the base of the computer, even a little, helps a lot.

By the way, this same computer seems to run much cooler than it did under Vista. I didn't have sensors in Vista to be sure, but it just "feels" cooler to the touch.
