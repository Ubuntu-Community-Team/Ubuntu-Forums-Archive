### Post by decoherence on 2009-04-24
Recent versions of Photoshop do have tools that make this quite easy and give decent results. However, with a little patience, you can get results just as good as anything Photoshop can give you (and better than Photoshop's easy 'forground selection' tool.)

What you need is to learn how to use the pen tool. You trace a bezier path around the subject, convert it to a selection, copy and paste (really!) A bezier path can be tweaked after the fact, and additional points added. This is what makes it such a great masking tool, but the quality of the output depends directly on how much time you spend tweaking the path before you copy/paste.

You can experiment with feathering the edges if the background is a suitable colour for that. Then you can go in and touch up any edges that look too hard or soft. It's generally less than an hour's work once you know what you're doing, and the results are as good as your eyesight.

The GIMP has other tools like "select by colour" which can work in some limited circumstances. But you won't generally get as good results as you would using the pen tool and exercising some patience.
