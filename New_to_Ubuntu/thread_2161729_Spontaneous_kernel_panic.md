### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2013-07-11
I can't see any reason not to turn it back on. It's not like you heard grinding noises or got an error message threatening to rm everything. There's a good chance it will work ok when you turn it back on. Seemingly random malfunctions that don't happen often and do no damage and go away when you reboot may not be worth your effort to track down unless you just want to for the fun of it. Which if you do, I do understand. If it happens again, I'd suggest you copy on paper some of the "gobbledy gook". If you study it some it's likely you can figure out which parts are more likely to be important.
