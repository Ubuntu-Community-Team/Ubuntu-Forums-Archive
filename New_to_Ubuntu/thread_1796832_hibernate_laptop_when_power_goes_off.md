### Post by Sleepy-zz-John on 2011-07-04
I'm afraid you may be asking for the impossible because the hibernation process involves saving the current state of all your open windows on your hard disc in its swapfile partition.   This process, depending on how much you've got open at the start of hibernation, takes about a minute,  and has to be powered by something.  For this to happen when your mains power goes off,  you would normally rely on your battery for that approx 1 minute saving.    Without any battery capacity,  I'm afraid nothing's going to happen!    
If you don't want to replace your dud laptop battery, have you considered running on a UPS instead?
