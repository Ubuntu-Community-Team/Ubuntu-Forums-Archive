### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-08-18
Windows software only runs on Windows. You can't use Windows software on Ubuntu.

Ubuntu comes with Libreoffice and a PDF reading program. There may be astrology software available in Ubuntu - I don't know for sure, but you could try looking in Ubuntu Software Center or on the internet. Instead of Photoshop, you can use The Gimp - it's available in Ubuntu Software Center.

I imagine if you've got Pagemaker you are a professional typesetter or something like that - Ubuntu does not have the software that a typesetter needs for their work. Adobe has not rewritten their software for Linux yet.

In most of the world, you should be able to connect to the internet immediately after plugging in the Ethernet cable (or use wifi). The broadband connections in India are a bit different to the rest of the world and I've heard that there are extra steps required to get these to work in Ubuntu. You should post a new thread about the internet connection, and give us as much detail as you can about what equipment you use to connect to the internet, and somebody here might be able to help.
