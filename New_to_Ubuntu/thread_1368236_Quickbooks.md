### Post by audiomick on 2009-12-30
You could have a look at Gnu Cash
[http://www.gnucash.org/](http://www.gnucash.org/)
which is available from the Ubuntu repositories.
I don't think it can import from Quick Books, but is an alternative. I stopped using Quick Books in favour of Gnu Cash due to expensive and lousy Quick Books support in Germany.

I am about to change again to this
[http://mcrichter.macbay.de/Seiten/Englisch/Linux/LinHaBu.htm](http://mcrichter.macbay.de/Seiten/Englisch/Linux/LinHaBu.htm)
which is not open source and costs money, but has a couple of Germany specific features that will make life a bit easier (I hope), although I actually quite like Gnu Cash.

Another option for you might be to keep using Quick Books in a Virtual machine.
