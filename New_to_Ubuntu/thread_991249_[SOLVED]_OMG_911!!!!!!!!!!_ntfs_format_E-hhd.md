### Post by GregA on 2008-11-23
tenfoot,

Try this out.  Go to your Gnome menu:  Applications>Accessories>Terminal (this should open a dos-type of interface in linux called a terminal session).

Then, type in "lsusb" and see if your usb external drive is recognized and then mounted (it might take a few seconds).   

You can close the Terminal session by typing "exit", and then pressing enter.  If this works, you can also add the terminal icon to you launch menu for future use.
