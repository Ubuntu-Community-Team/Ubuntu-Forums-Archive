### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-11-11
Just a couple of concerns to think about BEFORE you do this ...

First, it's not unusual for a company to consider any equipment they furnish as THEIR property -- and in those cases, courts (at least, US courts) have been consistent in ruling that there is "no reasonable expectation of privacy" when using company-provided resources.  

Which means, any time they want THEIR PC back, they can demand it, they can do a complete search on it, and not only can they take action against you for anything they find, (at least, in US courts) they can turn that info over to law enforcement for prosecution.  AND, they can do all of this without requiring any kind of search warrant.

So, if you have ANYTHING you don't want them to see or know about, the only safe thing to do is to NOT have it on "their" PC.

Second, it's also not unusual for companies to have policies that allow them to immediately terminate (as in "fire") anyone they determine to be using company property "inappropriately" -- and again (in US courts), THEY get to decide what is "inappropriate".

Just think you should be aware of some possible risks you're taking when you decide to use company-provided resources for personal purposes.
