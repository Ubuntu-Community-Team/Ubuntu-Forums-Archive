> [h=2]Hello How Do I play a DVD in Ubuntu 11.04?[/h]

You don't. Actually there's nothing you should do with a dead release without security updates since 2012, especially if connected to the internet.
Please install a supported release and then follow instructions: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats/PlayingDVDs](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats/PlayingDVDs)

### Post by Impavidus on 2020-04-13
Indeed, you don't. 11.04 is so old that the repositories have been removed. LTS releases of Ubuntu (16.04, 18.04, 20.04) get 5 years of support, interim releases (19.10 is the only interim release currently supported) only 9 months. 20.04 LTS will be released next week and may be your best option. If you're in a hurry, you can also try 19.10, the beta release of 20.04 or the previous LTS, 18.04.

[https://ubuntu.com/about/release-cycle](https://ubuntu.com/about/release-cycle)
