### Post by Potters Son on 2009-07-08
Yeah; apparently some mass-storage companies jip you out of space by using 1000-byte kilobytes instead of 1024-byte ones. This difference gets bigger when you scale up the situation to megabytes and gigabytes.

According to Wikipedia ([http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibibit](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibibit) for instance), people are trying to clear up the confusion by defining new prefixes to represent 1024-ness. While kilo-, mega-, and giga- represent 1000, 1,000,000, and 1,000,000,000 units, respectively, their new derivations, kibi-, mebi-, and gibi- mean 1024, 1048576, and 1073741824 units. So, whenever you see KiB, MiB, and GiB, it means it's talking in powers of 1024 instead of 1000.

So, for instance, my 260.1 GB hard drive in the Places menu shows up as 242.24 GiB in GParted. However, when I divide the 260.1 GB value by 1.073741824 (the ratio of one GiB to one GB, or 1024^3/1000^3), I come out with 242.24 GiB.

I guess that until the partitioner works things out with the GNOME panel, there will be no real way to have round-numbered partitions. Instead, I generally try to name them (like "winvista" or "swap" or "ubuntu-home").
