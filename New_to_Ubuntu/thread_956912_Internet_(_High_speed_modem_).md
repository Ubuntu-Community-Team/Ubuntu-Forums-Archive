Are you using a laptop or a Desktop PC and do you have wireless or LAN (ethernet). If so, any idea what their model is? If you use a laptop, whats the make and model? If you don't know, you might find something relevant in Control Panel > System > Hardware > Device Manager.

### Post by PhoenixP3K on 2008-10-23
Bell High-Speed user here myself =)

Now, is your DSL Modem also a Router (because I know Bell has those).
You've got two ways to connect your PC to the modem, USB and ethernet. The Ethernet connection is the easiest because it shouldn't require much configuration or any drivers. 

So you can either have your PC handle the connection or your router. One of the two has to provide the modem with your user name and password to access the Internet. I would guess that under Windows XP, it's your computer that takes care of the ~PoPPEE connection and sends the authentication information.

Now the same thing needs to happen in Ubuntu so check the settings inside System > Administration > Network.

However, if you look back in your manual there should be instructions on how to use the router to store and use the account information to connect automatically to the Internet. That way neither Windows or Ubuntu have to handle the connection information, as soon as the Ethernet cable is connected they just find the Internet.
