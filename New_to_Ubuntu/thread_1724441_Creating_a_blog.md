### Post by Blasphemist on 2011-04-08
I have to agree with the post that you should use Wordpress, if pictures are high in your priorities. The free use of Wordpress includes 3 GB of file storage. My other favorites limit you to considerably less.

It really depends on all of your needs. You can do as was also mentioned in another post and store you pictures off of your blog but link to them from your blog if the blog system that best meets all needs doesn't allow enough storage without payment.

I like to use the cloud as backup for my pictures and for sharing. Picasa meets my needs best and I signed up for more storage than is provided for free. I use Drupal Gardens for blogging as it has been as much one of the ways that learn Drupal as it is my blog platform. Drupal Gardens beta is now over and their limit to storage as a free user is now 50MB. WordPress.com allows 3GB. Ubuntu One is 2 GB and I think that is the same as Picasa/Google. 

WordPress ([http://en.wordpress.com/features/)is](http://en.wordpress.com/features/)is) likely the best all around offering. I use Drupal Gardens and a local install of Drupal for the main purpose of learning Drupal as it is my overall CMS of choice. It is more flexible than WordPress. Having skills in both including theming is valuable though.

### Post by supergirlkara on 2011-04-08
If you use the free version of Wordpress on Wordpress.com they are not as customizable as you might think and don't allow you to add your own side bar ad monetization because of their lack of support for JavaScript. You can't even add a banner to your side bar because they don't allow JavaScript. I recommend blogger because of the unlimited band width and the ability to fully customize and monetize your blog. Once your blog starts taking off you can even use a domain to point to your blogger through the blogger interface. I am a long time user of Blogger and I have never been happier. Before you start bashing me about the open source version of Wordpress script, consider this if your blog gets popular you will have to pay for all the bandwidth as well as worrying constantly about new Wordpress exploits. Using blogger will keep your blog secure, no one hacks google, and if they do they fix it sitewide meaning that you have to do nothing and you don't have to worry about checking for upgrades that is all done for you. That's my 2 cents
