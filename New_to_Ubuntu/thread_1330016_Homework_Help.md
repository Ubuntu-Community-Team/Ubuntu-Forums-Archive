### Post by dillandriskell on 2009-11-18
I like the way you think OP, I've been known to seek similar "homework environments" as well.

If you really want to go all out a good idea would be to create a new user maybe named "school", just to give yourself a mental "I only do schoolwork here" train of thought, rather than mixing your schoolwork environment with your leisure environment.  Also make sure that user is just a desktop user so you can't access your "leisure" files.

Here's another good psychological booster for you, right click the desktop and click change desktop background, then pick a blank background.  Now change "colors" to "horizontal gradient".  Then change the first color to black and the second one to yellow.  Yellow stimulates the thought process and boosts concentration, but I've found that all yellow is too distracting but when with a black gradient is subtle enough to help studying.

As far as blacklisting stuff, I don't know.  Anything you can block yourself I've found you can unblock yourself, so I've yet to find a solution for this.  Anyways, hope this much helped, good luck!

### Post by dillandriskell on 2009-11-18
Oh, another thing.  It's been proven that classical music also boosts brain productivity, and although I'm usually an alternative/metal kind of guy I always listen to classical while I'm working.  You can set up a channel on [pandora.com]("http://pandora.com/") to play only classical music, I'd recommend starting with an artist like David Nevue or Beethoven.  Just another thought as well, certainly not a necessity.  Good luck.

Another thing that *might* work for you is a program called Gnaural, which alters your state of mind through binaural beats, apparently it doesn't work for everyone but it works for me.  You need headphones for this though.   To start using this type this code in a terminal; 

```
sudo apt-get install gnaural
```When it's finished installing you can find it under **Applications > Sound and Video > Gnaural**.  You also need to download the files that you want your state of mind to be at, which can be found here [http://pantheon.yale.edu/~bbl2/GnauralExampleFiles.html]("http://pantheon.yale.edu/%7Ebbl2/GnauralExampleFiles.html").  Just right click the one you want and download it to a location you can find, then open gnaural and go to file > open and find your file.  It's still experimental but I've had good results with it, just another idea for you to chew on ;)

### Post by BinaryFeast on 2009-11-18
I've found the following tip quite useful: Whenever you get an impulse to do anything other than what you are supposed to be doing, make a little mark on a piece of paper. Then make it your goal to reduce the amount of marks that appear each time you sit down to do homework!

Also: Keep the web browser shut down (not even minimized) at all times, except when you actually need to look something up.

And yes; classical music does seem to help, but also electric/ambient (especially some of the hypnotic trance music that's out there). I think it's the lyrics that are distracting in other music styles (normally I listen to metal, but if I were to do that while doing homework I would just impulsively reach for my guitar instead).
