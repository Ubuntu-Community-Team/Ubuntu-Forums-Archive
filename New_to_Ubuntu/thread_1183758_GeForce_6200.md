### Post by Miljet on 2009-06-10
After a year and a half, I finally convinced my wife to let me install Ubuntu on her desktop computer. She immediately started having random freezes. It is a Pentium 4 machine with 1G RAM and an ATI Radeon 7000 Series AGP video card that I built about six years ago.

Searching old posts, I discovered that similar freezing problems were traced to using old ATI Radeon cards.

Since this computer has the old AGP interface, the choice of replacement cards is rather limited. Most of the cards that I have found seem to favor the GeForce 6200 chipset.

But a search for this chipset turned up several posts of people having major problems getting the drivers installed for them.

So the question is - Will I just be trading one problem for another, possibly worse, one?

### Post by Hospadar on 2009-06-10
I suspect you will have much better luck with the nvidia card, the nvidia drivers are, on the whole, much better and easier to set up.

I use an nvidia AGP card at home (probably a 6xxx or 7xxx series not sure at the moment) with no problems, it was very easy to set up.

As with the above problems, probably you'd notice that the posts are pretty old, and also keep in mind that usually people post when they have problems, not when things go so smoothly that they didn't even know there might be a problem.

Furthermore, those AGP cards are so inexpensive today, that even if it doesn't work, and you can't return it, you can always save it for later or just take the hit.
