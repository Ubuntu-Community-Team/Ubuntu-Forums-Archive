### Post by Pro-reason on 2008-07-27
The best FM is always the program itself.

I've never inserted audio into a presentation before, but looking at the OpenOffice.org Impress interface in front of me, the blindingly obvious thing to do seems to be to click on the "Insert" menu and then choose "Movie and Sound".  Have you not tried that?

As far as I can tell, it inserts the audio as an object on the current slide, so that it will play automatically when that slide is reached, and stop when the next slide is reached (if it hasn't already finished).  That seems like a great idea if you have relevant audio for each slide.

I suspect, however, that you just want a sort of soundtrack to the presentation running all the way through, with no particular relevance to each slide.  It seems to me that the most sensible way to achieve that is to play your audio in a separate application (i.e. Amarok), pressing play before you start your presentation.

Press F1 within OpenOffice.org Impress for more information.

I just did a search for sound in Powerpoint presentations, and found guides indicating that the way to do it is to add sound to the slide transitions.  Looking in the OpenOffice.org Impress documentation, it explains how you can do the same in OOo.
