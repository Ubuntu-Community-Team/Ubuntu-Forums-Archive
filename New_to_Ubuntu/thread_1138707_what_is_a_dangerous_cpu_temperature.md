### Post by mcduck on 2009-04-26
> **libihero said:**
> on my laptop, my cpu temp stays in the 60s C, is that too hot?

Depends on your CPU model. Recommended max temperatures range from around 60C to above 100C.

You can check the max temp for many CPU models on this great page: [http://users.erols.com/chare/elec.htm]("http://users.erols.com/chare/elec.htm")
(It's the "Max Core Temp"/"Max Case Temp", meaning max temp for the CPU packaging and not the computer case ;))

Without specifying the CPU model you really shouldn't pay much attention to what people say about safe temperature. THey simply can't know what's the limit for *your* CPU.

For example, my laptop has a Core Duo T2300, with max temp of 100C. The fans don't even really start spinning until somewhere around 65C. On the other hand my old desktop machine would be almost dead if it ran that hot..

Anyway, if that's idle temperature it's a bit high. You might want to clean the laptop a bit, there's probably quite a lot of dust in the air vents and cooling sinks if the system runs that warm when idle.
