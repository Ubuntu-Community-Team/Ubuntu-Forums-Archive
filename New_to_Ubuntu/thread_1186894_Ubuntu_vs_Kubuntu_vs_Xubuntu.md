### Post by Chemical Imbalance on 2009-06-14
The main difference is they use 3 different Desktop Environments (DE's).

Kubuntu- KDE4
Ubuntu- Gnome
Xubuntu- Xfce

KDE4 is the new kid on the block.  Gnome is what you are probably using now.
Xfce has a slightly simpler and lower-requirement setup.

You can of course install xfce and kde on Ubuntu, etc...these distros just make it convenient.

Gnome probably should be fine on your hardware.  They all will.

### Post by Chemical Imbalance on 2009-06-14
You can try the desktops out now if you want.

To do this, just install the packages "kubuntu-desktop" and "xubuntu-desktop" in Synaptic Package Manager (System, Administration).

Then log out to the login screen and choose "Sessions" in the lower corner.

Choose either KDE or Xfce and log in to try them out.
