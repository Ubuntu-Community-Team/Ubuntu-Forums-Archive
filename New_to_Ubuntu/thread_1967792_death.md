### Post by souravc83 on 2012-04-30
If the screen with the manufacturer's logo does not come up, is there any sound which you hear after you press the power button.
When the RAM fails, or the motherboard fails, there are these long beeping sounds which signal that they have not passed the self test.
Look up your computer's hardware manual to find which sound means what.
But if you can't get to the manufacturer's screen, it is likely a hardware problem, rather than anything to do with software.

Your best bet is to take out and reinsert the RAM once, if you can locate where it is, and are confident enough to open it. Cleaning the motherboard might help, but its also taking too much of a change. I must warn you, motherboards are very delicate, handle them carefully. If you are not experienced with opening and repairing computers,motherboard is the last thing you want to mess with. I would rather take it to a professional.

### Post by souravc83 on 2012-04-30
Please look up you computer's hardware manual for booting errors. I have never owned an HP. I faced similar problems with a ThinkPad. They had a detailed list of which error is specified by what sound. The flashing of the Num keys might indicate some error, I am not sure. 

One way to check a RAM could be to take out the RAM and put it in a different computer and see if it is working, or to get a new RAM (they are very cheap these days), and put it in, and see if it works. 

One other risky way of checking the RAM is to take it out, and try to boot.See if it gives a different error, and then putting the RAM back in, and booting again. I don't recommend this practice. Booting without RAM might not be good for the motherboard. I am not sure. Do it at your own risk. The ones mentioned in the above paragraph are safer.
