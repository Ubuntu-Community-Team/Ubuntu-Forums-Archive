### Post by Fenris_rising on 2010-03-01
Some motherboard manufacturers do provide linux based drivers on the motherboard disk. My ASUS board did for one. But the better way to look at this is from the lists of compatible hardware on the forum. I have installed Hardy Heron 8.04 on 5 different machines which had, for the most part, different specs and hardware. All of them run fine WITHOUT any outside provided drivers.

I think it would be true to say that unless the MB is very new and different just about any will run a linux based OS. Again you would need to check the compatibility of attached graphic cards, or other PCI cards. But on the whole you should be fine. Run a live CD of your chosen linux. This will help discover any potential issues.

Fenris
