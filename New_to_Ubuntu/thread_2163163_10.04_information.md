### Post by snowpine on 2013-07-17
Welcome! Linux is all about freedom, and you may manually install any version of any package you wish. :)

10.04 uses stable, well-tested software from April 2010. However, it constantly receives bug fixes and security patches from newer versions. For a good explanation of how software updates work in a "stable" distribution (such as Ubuntu LTS or Red Hat), read here: [https://access.redhat.com/security/updates/backporting/?sc_cid=3093](https://access.redhat.com/security/updates/backporting/?sc_cid=3093)

Generally speaking, the majority of users will upgrade their entire system to a newer release (such as 12.04) rather than upgrade individual applications piecemeal. (An analogy: Most people buy a whole new car to get the new features, rather than replace individual parts with parts from a different model/year.) However this is not a hard and fast rule, and knowing nothing about your situation or reasons for wanting the newer versions, I cannot give an informed opinion.
