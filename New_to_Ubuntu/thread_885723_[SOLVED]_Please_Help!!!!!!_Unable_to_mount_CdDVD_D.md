### Post by bobbob1016 on 2008-08-10
First off, it is unlikely playing with things in Windows messed with your ability to mount your CD/DVD-Rom, unless you were playing with your Linux partitions, but if you can boot Ubuntu, I doubt you did damage.

Second, did you try multiple CD/DVDs?

Third, did you try CD/DVDs in Windows?

Fourth, when you open a terminal and type "cd /media/cdrom0" then "ls" all without quotes, with the CD/DVD in the drive, is anything listed?  Try "cd /media/dvd" then  "ls" if it is a DVD.
