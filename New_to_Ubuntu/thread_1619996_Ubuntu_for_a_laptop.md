### Post by lobralleo on 2010-11-12
It will mostly depend on your specific laptop: as you can read on the forums, everyone has a different experience with Ubuntu on a laptop, mainly due to hardware compatibility.
As far as I can tell, slightly older laptops tend to perform better and have all their hardware detected out-of-the-box; assuming your laptop is not a cutting edge machine (as the small HD would suggest), you should go quite fine. I remember being quite happy with my old HP Compaq laptop, while I always had problems with my next laptop, a HP dv6500 (audio, wireless or other issues, depending on the distro and release).
Running the LiveCD is always a good choice before installing, just to check for zero-order problems.
