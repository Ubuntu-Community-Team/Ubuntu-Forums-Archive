### Post by GMachine_24 on 2009-05-10
Hey there: yes, this is the beginner section.

On your desktop, go to the upper left corner with your mouse-pointer and select Applications>Internet and then find, at the bottom of the list that opens up, Transmission Bittorrent Client. Click on that - the program will open.

Or, if you are at a torrent site, and click on "download" to copy a torrent file to your hard drive, Ubuntu will offer you the chance to open the torrent file with the Transmission Bittorrent Client - which you can then select to download the file you want.

Or, if you want to use another torrent client, you can install that one and essentially do the same thing, choosing your favorite torrent client instead of Transmission.

1 - Is a good idea, since it will block bad peers from connecting to your torrent client. You can add your own lists to the folder *~/.config/transmission/blocklists*. You can download more lists from [http://iblocklist.com/lists.php](http://iblocklist.com/lists.php)

2 - You don't need to select a limit for download, but it is a good idea to set the upload limit to 80% of your actual upload limit imposed by the ISP, otherwise you might slow down your downloads. For example, my upload limit is 70 KiB/s so I set the upload limit to 56 KiB/s

3 - You need to have Transmission running and the torrent must be active

4 - Peerguardian is only for Windows, but you can use [moblock](http://moblock-deb.sourceforge.net/) or [iplist](http://iplist.sourceforge.net/). Both work perfectly  (better than Peerguardian IMO). I prefer moblock but iplist is almost identical to Peerguardian.

### Post by GMachine_24 on 2009-05-14
Hey Luke:

If you are downloading a file via torrent, you are probably also a leecher. Most torrent programs have uploading set as a default - meaning you will be uploading and downloading simultaneously, until your download completes at which point you will become a "seeder" if you keep your torrent software open and don't kill the process.

As far as finding out what is being downloaded, if you click on the torrent icon at the top of your screen on the right hand side of your top task bar - you should be able to select an option to view what file(s) you are uploading and downloading. You can also stop the sharing process and quit the torrent program by clicking on this icon and selecting what you want to do. Keep in mind that if you stop sharing, that doesn't automatically close the torrent program.

 I hope I understood your question correctly.
