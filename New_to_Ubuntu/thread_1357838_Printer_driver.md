### Post by Sef on 2009-12-17
If you want a printer to work with Ubuntu (or Linux in general) your best bet is to get an HP.  You can check if the HP Printer is supported [here]("http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/index.html").

Dell printers are rebranded Lexmarks, Lexmark is not very friendly in the home consumer market.  (Their business line, Optima, does have drivers for Linux.)   Canon is also a company that is not very Linux friendly.

Update: Check out '[The Canon PIXMA Linux blog, for all Canon PIXMA all-in-ones]("http://mp610.blogspot.com/2009/01/new-mp620-and-mp630-printers-ppds.html")'

Update 2: For the Dell AIO 966, you would need to set up an Windows machine as a print server.  An old  P3 would work just fine.

### Post by Thelasko on 2009-12-17
[The Canon is probably your best bet out of the two.]("http://www.openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Canon-PIXMA_MP620")

[The Dell is a "paperweight" on Linux.]("http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Dell-AIO_Printer_966")

P.S. Canon is a very strange company.  They provide different drivers based on what part of the world you live in.  If you can't find drivers on the US site, try the [Asian]("http://support-asia.canon-asia.com/") or [Australian]("http://www.canon.com.au/en-AU/Support-Services/Drivers-and-Downloads") site.
