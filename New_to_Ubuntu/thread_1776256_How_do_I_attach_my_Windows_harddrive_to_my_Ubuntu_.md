### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-06-06
> **brawnypandora0 said:**
> Why does it need a power supply? I thought USB already has the power supply.

USB (and even then, it must be v2.0 or better) only has the power to run small hard drives (2.5").  

If that is the case with yours, and you put your drive into an enclosure, you should be able to use it without an extra power connection.

If yours is a larger drive (3.5"), you MUST provide extra power for it.

### Post by seawolf167 on 2011-06-06
The physical hardware question has been answered - as for the software side, you do not need to use samba or anything unless you want to share the drive over your network.

If you are simply using it as a "plug and play" external hard drive you are good to go, simply plug it in and Ubuntu will mount the drive automatically.
