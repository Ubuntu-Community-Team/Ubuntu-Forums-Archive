### Post by mousestalker on 2009-03-19
Forgive my ignorance, but I'm very new to this community and to Linux/Ubuntu in general.

I thought posting  my experience with installing Ubuntu might be helpful to some.

I have a Asus p5w dh deluxe mobo with an Intel 975 Conroe processor, 3 gigs of RAM and a 1 terabyte HD on Intel Sata and an optical drive on  micron IDE. My grafix card is a evga nvidia 260. I have been running Windows XP Pro.

Some time ago I burned a distro of Ubuntu to a cd and tried an install, which failed. Many many screens of garbage script and much waiting. So I went back to Redmond and my CD waited for me.

This weekend past my compy caught a virus. I spent most of Saturday fumigating it. As that process is unfun (and I do not recommend AVG at all, btw) I thought revisiting Ubuntu might be a good plan.

While I found much good advice here and other places, and the posters seemed friendly, there really is a steep learning curve. That being the case, I over thought. The problem I had been having with the garbage install was the common problem of people with my mobo of really needing an AHCI hard drive controller instead of IDE, which mine was set to.

There is also much good advice on how to accomplish the trick of updating drivers from IDE to AHCI, but since most of them either involved registry hacks or, even worse, doing a repair/re-install of Windows (see the paragraph about my fun weekend with a virus, above) I wasn't really thrilled.

Then I found [this forum discussion]("http://forums.vr-zone.com/archive-general-hardware/195867-switching-sata-ide-compatibility-mode-ahci-windows.html"). It's a cheap trick. But it's also bloody brilliant. The second technique worked a treat, and I now have my sata drive on AHCI.

The second install (from inside windows, with the latest version of ubuntu) went very smoothly. What I found deeply ironic is that there is now an option menu with an IDE workaround. So the cheap trick is probably useless.

Over the next few months, my plan is to migrate everything over to linux and relegating windows to support status.

As for why make the change? Well first, the software I'm already using for productivity is open office and firefox. So, I'm not losing anything by switching. My favourite game is Victoria, which from all accounts runs just fine with Wine. 

Second, the security is better structured than with Windows. Mere user access does not seem as confining as it is under windows. Both xp and vista present configuration challenges for both my work and gaming with anything other than administrative privileges. MY test projects with linux as well as my one game played went off without muss or fuss.

Which is what using a computer should be. As much fun as playing tech geek can be, and it can be fun, most people need something stable and secure. So far, that's what I have found.

The only remaining issue is the optical drive. My current plan is also a cheap trick. A local retailer is offering name brand bluray readers/dvd burners at a very nice price. The drives are sata, so I can simply install the darn thing and resolve compatability issues and get an upgrade at the same time.

My thanks to those who have prepared guides and to those who have written the code.
