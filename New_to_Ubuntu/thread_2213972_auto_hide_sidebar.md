### Post by squakie on 2014-03-29
In the menus, select "System Settings".  This will open another Window.  On that window select "Appearance".

This will open the Appearance settings for your desktop.  There are 2 tabs at the top part of this window, "Look" and "Behavior" - click on the "Behavior" tab.

The settings for hiding the menu sidebar is on this screen.  If things are grayed out you will need to click the option on the right where it probably says "Off" and this will enable the option.  You can configure to your wants from there.

### Post by David D. on 2014-03-29
To turn this feature ON, start by clicking on the gear shaped icon on the top-right.  Select "System Settings" from the drop-down menu that appears.  Click on "Appearance", then select the Behavior tab.  Turn the "Auto-hide the Launcher" to ON.
