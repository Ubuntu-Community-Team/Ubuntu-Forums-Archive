### Post by anewguy on 2010-11-03
Just to note, everyone who replies on this forum was a beginner at one point in time.  Many, many, many of us have so much to learn you wouldn't believe it, but since people helped us we try to help where we can.  Just remember it's an all-volunteer forum and you may have to wait a day or possibly a lot longer, to get replies, let alone a fix.

The best way for us to help you is for you to tell us in your own words what you are trying, or want, to do, what you have tried, and any error messages, etc., you received.  Don't worry if it seems wordy or stated to simply - we will ask for more information as we need it.

Welcome, and I hope things go better for you!  If you have any questions or problems, just post them and we'll try to help.  You'll find there are some VERY expert people here who usually wait to answer on the more difficult problems, but sometimes chime in on the more routine ones as well.

### Post by Cheesehead on 2010-11-03
You have received a great deal of help from this forum, and -looking through your previous threads- many of your issues have been resolved.

Your posts often seem hasty or panicked, while lacking essential details. "It's not working" is not helpful unless you explain in great detail what it is, what you were trying to do, and how you know that it failed. That takes time and effort on your part.

Again, you need to explain your problems in much more detail, which is hard for many people. Equipment models, application names, failure messages, links to instructions you were following, the name of the control you were fiddling with, and what you expected to happen are all examples of information that should be in your first post.

Volunteer helpers get frustrated when they keep asking for information and don't get useful replies. For example, when you ask for help with your router, the thread runs to Page 3 (so far) before you tell what kind of router it is, and still no description of exactly what you did to set it up.

I'm not criticizing you at all. You asked why it's hard to get help. The answer is that you're not giving the helpers the information they need to help you. That's not a reflection on you - good writing is a skill that must be developed and exercised. Nobody's born with it.
