### Post by heyandy889 on 2011-05-25
Well, the work-around I found was to print the essay as a .pdf. Open Office supports printing to pdf natively. That way, the formatting is preserved, and anyone who opens the document will see the same thing, no matter what.

As far as .doc or .rtf, there shouldn't be a problem unless you're moving back and forth between Open Office and that other Office software. Ha ha, I mean Microsoft Word.

Anyways, yeah. Just go ahead and open up your nice, clean .odt essay. Click File, Save As. In there, you'll find an option to save as .doc. You might see the error message you mentioned, the "formatting" might not be preserved, yada yada yada. Usually it's not an issue, more with power point slide shows, or if you're transferring essays btwn OO.org and Word. 

So. Did that work? You should have a shiny, new .doc file. Try exiting out, opening your .doc file back up. Try attaching to an email, opening, maybe even opening inside a real Microsoft Word application.

Usually, moving between programs produces minimal distortion. Did any of this work? Good luck, pal, yes, it can feel frustrating sometimes.
