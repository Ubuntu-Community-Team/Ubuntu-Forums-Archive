### Post by thetree on 2009-06-27
I checked out the website, Tonido's features look awesome. I mean seriously, a cross-platform P2P collaboration and social networking app that is accessible from the web AND keeps my data stored on my local machine: That's what I've been waiting for!

But on the website it says Tonido is "Licensed under GPL and commercial licenses". Is this the downside? [This]("http://www.codelathe.com/blog/index.php/2009/04/16/tonido-q-and-a/") is from the developer's blog:
>  When you say Tonido Platform is open source, does it mean the apps that are being shipped with it?

No, currently only the Platform is open, which means that developers can build new apps on top of it easily. The individual apps shipped currently are not open source.

If third party developers want to build great Tonido apps and want to get paid for their efforts they should be.  If they want to release it open source they can too. We didn’t want to limit the type of apps only to be free ones. As an end user you will care more about the apps than the platform. If you don't mind Tonido's apps are not open source, give it a try. Then come back and tell us how it is.
