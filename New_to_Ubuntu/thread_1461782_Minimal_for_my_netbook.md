### Post by snowpine on 2010-04-24
[Here is a great tutorial]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal") for a minimal Ubuntu install. Just use 'lxde' instead of 'icewm' and add your choice of applications to the list.

Even a full-blown install of Ubuntu uses less than 4gb, so you will have plenty of space left on your 16gb card. If speed is your #1 concern, however, you should install to your internal hard disk instead of the SD card, as flash memory is very slow compared with a full-speed hard drive. This is just my advice anyway. :)

The "micro distros" like Puppy and SliTaz can run entirely in RAM, so it does not really matter whether you are loading them from an internal or external drive. Either of these distros will be smaller than even the most minimal Ubuntu install.
