### Post by alienexplorers on 2008-05-04
Hardy Heron I believe requires a minimum of 256MB of memory.  Maybe that is your problem.  You may have to load xubuntu which is for systems with less resources and less memory.

### Post by NightwishFan on 2008-05-04
Xubuntu live cd likely will not even load with 128mb of ram. Use the alternate installer, or try fluxbuntu (i recommend this) or a lighter distro such as debian or puppy.

### Post by iSplicer on 2008-05-04
Night is spot on, the LiveCD requires a fair bit of RAM to run on, since it does not use the hard drive at all. You need to use the alternate to install.

You are going to have a tough time using GNOME with 128MB, use something lighter, I too, recommend Fluxbox (you can use "fluxbuntu", its installed by default)
