### Post by mikechant on 2011-10-18
> Is there a cheque printing software available for Ubuntu?

> No. Cheques are obsolete. Only Americans still use cheques.

This reply is wrong on all three counts.

There *is* cheque printing software for Linux, and although cheque guarantee cards have gone in the UK and cheques are not accepted in most shops, cheques are still the only easy way to pay for some things - e.g. £1600 to a handyman for a new fence. Many such people do not accept cards and I would have to make 7 visits to ATMs on different days to get that much cash out!

The UK banks have abandoned plans to completely abolish cheques in 2018 because they were forced to admit that there were **no suitable alternatives** for certain types of payment. I.e. cheques are *not* obsolete until the banks produce a viable alternative.

Anyhow, getting to the point, the very common package, GnuCash has a cheque printing function. See the following page:
[http://www.ehow.com/how_6612208_print-check-linux.html](http://www.ehow.com/how_6612208_print-check-linux.html)

As to whether it will work for your printer and your cheques, that's another matter...
