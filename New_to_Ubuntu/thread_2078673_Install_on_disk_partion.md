### Post by Bashing-om on 2012-10-31
andyjack38; Hi ! Welcome to the forum.

In response to your "directed" question, in my experience, with ubuntu installed to what used to be the "d" partition, will require a dual boot scenerio. (choice of which OS to boot) . --installing within windows (wubbi) is also done --

With all the differing hardware/formatting/booting that have recently become available; It is no longer a straight forward install with out being aware of what hardware conditions exist in respect to the hard drive .
SSD drive ?
GPT partitioning ?
msdos partitioning ( how many primary partitions are on the disk at this time ?
UEFI booting ?

Ubuntu can cope with any medium, but, the methods to cope differ.

There are many guides and tutorials to install ubuntu ..when you are ready to proceed -> ask your questions;  answers and appropriate tutorials/guides will be provided.
