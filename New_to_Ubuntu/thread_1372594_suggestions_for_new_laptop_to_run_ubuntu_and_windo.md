### Post by cenzorrll on 2010-01-04
i see you're busy on th forums!

i have had no problems running ubuntu on laptops (well, "no" might be a strong word).  to help you out here's some things to look for that i've noticed:

-i have an HP and they leave a bit to desire as far as build quality (cooling not very good, which leads to failed components)

-i also have an IBM (now made by lenovo) which is very well built, but they are expensive the only reason i have one is because i bought it 3 years old off ebay, and it is a tank. (it's now 7 years old and still truckin')

-i had a toshiba, which was somewhat of a middle ground, not the best built machine, but definitely held it's own with the big boys. (spilled beer on it, i swear i was sober)

-Dell. i really hate...hating.  but i think dell has a piss poor business model (the company, not their business laptops) if you buy a cheap dell it is CHEAP.  failing hard drives, poor components, broken ac adapters, are some problems you SHOULD expect.  but if you're willing to shell out gobs of cash you will get a well built machine. so Dell is pretty much the epitome of what everybody hates about business (can you hear my disdain? feel free to go with dell though if it's what you want)

what i've also found is that business laptops tend to be more robust than their home/office counterparts.

hope that helps!

p.s. if you see a laptop that you like, and others that have it also love it, it doesn't matter what OS you have, it's gonna be a good laptop.
