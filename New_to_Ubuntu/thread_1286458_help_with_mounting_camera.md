### Post by scorp123 on 2009-10-09
I don't know that camera ... But usually such cameras have some kind of card, e.g. SD cards, or MemoryStick, or some such thing.

And most desktop systems and laptops have card readers these days ... So if the camera gives such troubles, wouldn't it be possible to remove the SD card (or whatever the camera uses) and insert it into your PC? Such cards should behave like a normal disk drive, e.g. you can copy files off that card via drag & drop.

Or you let programs such as F-Spot (installed by default if I am not mistaken) handle picture management for you. If I am not mistaken it will even offer that option when the card is detected upon insertion.

So if the camera won't work ... the card most likely will.
