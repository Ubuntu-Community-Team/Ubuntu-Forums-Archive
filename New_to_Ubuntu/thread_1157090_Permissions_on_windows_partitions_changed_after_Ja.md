### Post by KhaaL on 2009-05-12
ubunchu, i think I'm onto something. Take a backup of your fstab file, then try replacing your line:
# Entry for /dev/sda1 :
UUID=7E60CD7160CD3127 /media/windows ntfs-3g defaults,locale=en_US.UTF-8 0 1

With
# Entry for /dev/sda1 :
UUID=7E60CD7160CD3127 /media/windows ntfs-3g defaults,locale=en_US.UTF-8,umask=007,gid=XXX 0 1

change the gid=XXX line to the group number *plugdev*. open a terminal and type "id", which will type out all the ids on your computer.


Example output follows:
khaal@Xeraphim:~$ **id**
uid=1000(khaal) gid=1000(khaal) groups=4(adm),20(dialout),24(cdrom),29(audio),_46(plugdev)_,106(lpadmin)


I'm not entirely sure this will fix it, but its worth a try and will in worst case just make your partition unmountable (which is fixed by restoring the fstab).

Good luck!
