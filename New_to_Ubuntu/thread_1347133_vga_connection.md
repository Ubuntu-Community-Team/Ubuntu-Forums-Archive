### Post by cartisdm on 2009-12-05
You will not need to plug in both cables.  Pick one of the connections (yellow RCA or the S-Video) and use it.  Are you on a laptop? If so, most keyboards have a Fn button and another button (like f8) that says something similar to "CRT/LCD" it will switch the video output

### Post by Bradj47 on 2009-12-05
The easiest way to do this I've found is to just use an S-video cable. If your computer has a VGA output (which I've never seen before), then you could just go with that instead. You shouldn't need any extra programs to do it, though you'll probably need to change the settings in System -> Preferences -> Display.

*** EDIT ***
Sorry, I missed the part where you described the cable. I'm guessing by VGA port, you mean [serial port]("http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ea/Serial_port.jpg"). (Click on link to see what a serial port is if you don't know.) Either S-Video or VGA should work, as long as you have the correct settings in System -> Preferences -> Display.

### Post by LowSky on 2009-12-05
VGA to S-video will not work
[http://www.amazon.com/TV-out-VGA-S-Video-Cable-Adapter/dp/B000P3UB24](http://www.amazon.com/TV-out-VGA-S-Video-Cable-Adapter/dp/B000P3UB24)
most of the reviews are from  people who cant get it to work, because they dont know the capabilities of their computers

Serial to S-video is not going to work at all, as serial is not made for video signals.

Check you computer's model to see if it can even connect to a TV.

Unless your TV has a VGA connection (some newer LCD models do, or HDMI (some newer laptops have htis feature and can be connected to HDTVs) chances are unlikey you can use your TV


Lastly older TVs (not HDTVs) their image quality is not made for computers, the resolution is no where near a normal computer screens. Newer HDTV can handle the images as they have higher resolutions.
