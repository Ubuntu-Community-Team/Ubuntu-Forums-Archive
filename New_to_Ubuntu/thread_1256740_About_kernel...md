### Post by sideaway on 2009-09-03
kernel. basically rockerphil is correct. that's really basic interpretation however. I'll have a go at explaining it :P

Linux has a three layer system. The kernel is your most basic level. This is all gobble-de-gook to 99% of the population. It's the fundamental, the kernel dictates what your computer will 'act' like, what 'morals' it has. think of it like a set of rules your programs have to follow. 

Then there's your 'shell'. This is what you're accessing when you open a terminal. It's text based commands and instructions that connects you to the kernel. It can control your computer, and you ultimately control it.

Then there's you personal layer, what you see on the screen a GUI, this is all your programs, files, personal settings etc... The last layer makes a computer usable for 95% of the worlds population, although the computer *can* be used and manipulated at either of these levels.
