### Post by SecretCode on 2010-11-22
No and yes ... no, not at all like upgrading to another windows OS.

Linux is not Windows and none of the programmes you have installed will work under linux, though most have alternatives included in Ubuntu or available for download.

Yes in a sense ... if you choose the option "install alongside Windows", your files will still be there and can be accessed from your linux installation, and (if you have the right programs) opened and edited. You'll also still have Win 7 installed and can boot it when you need to.

You should try out Ubuntu on the live CD (run from the installation CD without installing) to get a feel for it, before you decide to install.
