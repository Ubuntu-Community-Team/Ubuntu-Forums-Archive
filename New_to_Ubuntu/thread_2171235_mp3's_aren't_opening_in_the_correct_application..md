### Post by skitter on 2013-08-29
When I double click on an .mp3 file, it's opening in Handbreak instead of VLC. 

I've gone to System settings / Details / Default applications, and have both music and video set to VLC. I've tried changing the setting to Handbreak, then going back and changing it to VLC, but that's not helping.  FWIW, videos are opening in VLC as I expect them to. 
 
I'm running Ubuntu 12.04.2 LTS.

Any suggestions for how I can get my mp3's to open in VLC?

---

### Post by dhunt84971 on 2013-08-29
Have you tried using Nautilus and browsing to an .mp3 file then right-clicking on it and selecting VLC as the application to open it with?  I believe once you have done this the last selection should become the default.

---

### Post by skitter on 2013-08-29
Thanks dhunt. That didn't exactly solve it for me, but it did lead me down the right path.  After I right clicked on the mp3 and chose to open in VLC, the problem persisted. I tried another right click and selected 'open with other application'. In that dialog, there was an option to select Handbreak, and then click 'Forget Association', and that resolved the issue for me. Thanks again!
