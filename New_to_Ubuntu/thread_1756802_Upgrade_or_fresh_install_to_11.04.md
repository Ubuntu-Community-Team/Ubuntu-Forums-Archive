### Post by fballem on 2011-05-12
This is a question that has been asked a lot, and the general consensus when it is asked is that it is best to do a fresh install. I agree, and here are my reasons:

[LIST=1]
[*]The advantage to doing an upgrade is that the settings for your programs are preserved. There are new versions of all of the programs and not all of the settings are valid. There may also be new ones that are better. By doing a fresh install, you will redo your settings and take advantage of the new ones.
[*]Upgrades often go wrong, and often for reasons that are not clear.
[*]Cleans the old, accumulated crud out of your computer.
[/LIST]

Please make sure that you back up all of your files. If you use Evolution, make sure that you back it up as well (there is an option in the File menu to backup your settings. This will also back up your e-mail folders. You should also backup your Firefox bookmarks.

It takes a while, both to do the backup and to do the installation, but it is worth it.

The Unity interface will take a little getting used to, and I don't know how much you custom configure your computer. If you do custom configure it, then there are a lot of changes and you should read the forum postings on Unity (both good and bad). I don't hate it, could get to like it, but it's not quite ready for primetime.

Hope this helps,
