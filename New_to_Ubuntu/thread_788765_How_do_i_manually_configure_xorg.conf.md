This is my screen section of my xorg.conf file.  
Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"

	Monitor		"Generic Monitor"
	Defaultdepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Modes		"1280x800"

Most of the information is identical to yours except for my video card and the 'Modes' line.  I notice that it is written in a different format, perhaps this format is newer, yours is older, and no longer recognized.  You did say that you pasted it from an earlier distro.  

Furthermore, I only have one resolution lisetd in the 'Modes' line.  I recently changed my screen resolution to 800x600 and the stupid thing wouldn't turn back (using Ubuntu's screen resolution).  Rebooting it reseted it fortunately.  I wonder if it resetted cause of the xorg.conf file.  If that were true, perhaps you could get your desired resolution by deleting the other resolutions from your 'Modes' line.  

So try changing your 'Modes' line of :
Modes "1024x768@60" "800x600@60" "800x600@56" "640x480@60"

to
Modes "1024x768@60"

Like I said I'm a newbie, but it sort of makes sense, especially since my messed up resolution reseted itself.
