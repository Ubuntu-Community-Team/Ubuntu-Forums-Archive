### Post by Sef on 2009-08-11
[OpenPrinting]("http://www.openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Lexmark-X3650_3-in-1") says it is a paperweight, i.e., there are no Linux drivers for it.   However, all is not lost.   What you need to do is get an old XP machine, PIII or PIV, and set that up as a print server.  

The problem here is Lexmark.  They are very unfriendly towards Linux, and rarely provide any drivers, except for their business line.

### Post by Barafu Albino Cheetah on 2009-08-11
Lexmark provides small grey boxes called standalone printservers. They allow to connect LPT printer by network. Most of them require drivers, but not all. You may look for this solution.

### Post by routed on 2011-12-24
I have an X3650 Lexmark printer. I had the same problem that you did. Lexmark has released rpm and deb installation files for this printer, so it will now work in Linux! I am not sure when they were released, but they are there. Their site detected Ubuntu on my machine and directed me to the correct drivers. I am glad that Lexmark has come around!
