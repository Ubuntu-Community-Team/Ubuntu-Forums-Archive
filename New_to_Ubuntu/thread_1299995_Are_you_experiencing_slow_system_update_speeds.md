For those of you who are experiencing slow download speeds when installing new software or updating your computer, here is a solution that I found helped me quite a bit.

Go: System -> Administration -> Software Sources (enter your password) 

Click the "Download from:" drop down menu under the Ubuntu Software tab.

  Select "Other"

A window should pop up that says "Choose a download server"

Click the button on the top right that says "Select Best Server"

The computer will run a series of tests that determine which server will provide the fastest download speeds.

When this is done, you should be able to update and/or download software at a higher speed than before.

### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2009-10-24
The main servers tend to be slower since they are the default repository source, and most people don't change them.  Also, if you are downloading the .iso image for a LiveCD or alternate installer, it is best to use torrents to take the load off the server, and you will most likely get better speeds, too.  Many universities also keep their own local mirrors, so if you live near one or are on their network, ask about it (it may not be listed officially in Ubuntu).
