FYI. I had the opportunity to spend some email chat time with a Tech at Avast on that very subject. He said they have one for Linux/Ubuntu and it only costs about $3500!

When I asked if he was joking he said that they actually design one for the servers and that most of the major servers use Linux but, for the "home user" the anti-virus that comes with it is more than sufficient.

No one is developing virus/trojan sets for Linux because they have to know exactly what all you have installed in your system before 1 can be written for it and it's not worth the time it would take.

When I told him I had purchased Avast for years, and never had a problem, he replied that their service was no longer required if I was going Linux. He also said that if I had Win$, in VBox, not to worry because the virus could not get into the computer thru the VBox. (Virtually it does not exist so there is no "connection" to transfer the problems.)

So, basically, relax. You need just about everything available for Win$, but almost nothing for Linux.
