### Post by jackson21 on 2013-05-11
Hi, all Ubuntu users,
Sandisk +  and Sandisk Fuze  is for me the absolute linux mp3player
Just set in the player settings usb  to 'msc'
Insert the mp3 player into the usb slot of your PC
In the folder that opens, choose "Music" .
From your collection of music you can copy (or just drag into folder)
any music you want (mp3-only)
In the SANDISK" you can choose  from "Recently added"  or "Artists"
your music.
I personally don't care too much about setting up music lists,
I can choose with the SANDISK  from  'Artists' and  'Recently added'

Not to forget : A SDHC microcard  slot is added  which can extend your
mp3 pleasure   from  8GB  to  16GB

### Post by Bartender on 2013-05-11
Doesn't have to be mp3.  The Fuzes recognize .ogg also.  However, forum regulars at Sandisk Forums claim that playing .ogg takes more battery power than .mp3, so I've re-encoded all my .ogg files to .mp3.
