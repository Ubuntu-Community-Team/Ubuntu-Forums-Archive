### Post by JKBurton on 2008-12-12
When I had that problem two weeks back, I thought it was BIOS settings. Then I thought it was the motherboard. Turns out it was the 5+ year-old disk drive I was trying to boot from. The drive apparently failed while I was reinstalling Ubuntu, but there were no errors shown. It just started freezing before displaying the BIOS logo screen. It would start okay maybe one in ten attempts, but fail back to the BIOS screen without warning before Ubuntu could get as far as the login screen.

Try unplugging the disk, then power on and see if you get your logo. If you now get the BIOS logo, I'd say hardware failure. Try to borrow a drive from a friend to test before you throw money into a new motherboard like I did* :)

*It is a nice motherboard, but I just had to have the quad CPU, and that was not in my budget for this month!
