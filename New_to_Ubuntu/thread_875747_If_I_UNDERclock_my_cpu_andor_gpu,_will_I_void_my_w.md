### Post by Eviljim on 2008-07-31
Underclocking the CPU should not affect your warranty, as you are not stressing the components out (beware that _overclocking_ would invalidate it).

Most laptops have an ability to lower the clock speeds automatically when the CPU is idle, and then speed up when you go to do something. 

The clocking options for your laptop will be stored in the BIOS of the laptop, which you can access by pressing the relevant key (like F2 or F12 etc) during boot-up.

However, like most things PC related, I would advise that you take a look at the PC manual, before attempting to play around with the BIOS settings. With a laptop, its harder to reset them, unlike a desktop.

Another option to consider is a laptop cooler to place your laptop onto whilst you are using it. Something like the Akasa range for example contain 2 slow spinning fans which push cool air into the intakes underneath your laptop. 

Because the laptop has a nice supply on cool air blowing onto it, the internal fans usually slow down and in some cases stop spinning, as they aren't required anymore. The slow spinning fans on the cooler, would not generate nearly the same amount of noise.
