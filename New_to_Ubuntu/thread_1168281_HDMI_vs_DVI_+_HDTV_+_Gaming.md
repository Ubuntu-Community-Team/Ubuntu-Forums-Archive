### Post by powel212 on 2009-05-23
I run an Nvidia Gforce 8600 Graphix card with VGA and DVI out. It is not the latest or most expensive but it works great with Ubuntu. 

HDMI is better, I have heard, but I have not tested it myself. I actually run the VGA out to my monitor ,(24"), and the DVI out has a DVI to VGA adapter on it and I run that out to my 37" LCD T.V. The picture is full 720p and looks great. 

If you are running a 1080p T.V. you probably want the HDMI video card though in order to get the best performance out of your T.V.

I run my sound out separately from my sound card to my Amp, and then to my stand alone speakers for best sound. But I have in the past run a minijack to RCA cable from my computer to the T.V. to use the in T.V. speakers. This works but the built in speakers are usually not very good unless you bought a Dennon T.V. or something. 

In my experience Nvidia cards are very well supported in Ubuntu but I have had trouble with intel cards and ATI cards as well. Seems drivers are a little slower coming out for these varieties.

If you just want cheap and effective I would say get a decent $100.00 USD Nvidia card with VGA and DVI outs and run a VGA cable out to the T.V. Then run a seperate cable, (minijack to RCA), to the T.V. speakers. This will get the job done.

### Post by freak42 on 2009-05-26
> **Xero Xenith said:**
> I'm pretty sure VGA is lossless; I haven't noticed any quality loss using VGA for my 19" or 22" TV/monitors. :)

I'm pretty sure VGA is never lossless, it is an analog signal, a video card converts it's digital signal to vga and a (digital) monitor will convert it back to digital, while the use of dvi or hdmi will omit these two conversions.

The low quality of vga signals tend to make the image blurry with higher resolutions (1920x1600 and up).
