### Post by e4uforums on 2010-04-16
PlayOnLinux is just a front-end to WINE, it just automates WINE configuration for certain programs.  It also has a nice interface for manually configuring WINE.

I stumbled on PlayOnLinux when first testing out Ubuntu a few months ago.  It mostly caught my attention because of the gaming aspect of it.

What I like most about PlayOnLinux is that it's all graphical.  Coming from a Windows world I'm most comfortable with a nice GUI - PlayOnLinux gives me a nice GUI where WINE by itself does not.  I didn't do any testing with Cedega as I'm trying to move to free software to get things done.  

I have been extremely pleased with PlayOnLinux (I recommend it on here all the time).  I don't use many of their install scripts though.  I typically just choose to manually install a program, then set the WINE version afterward according to [http://appdb.winehq.org/](http://appdb.winehq.org/).

### Post by lyceum on 2010-04-16
I can now say that I am fluent in both GIMP and Photoshop. Here is my assessment: 

**Web designers**

If you are a web designer, take the time and learn GIMP. It is easier and better to use for the web. I really feel like it was made to cater to web designers more than to photographers. 

**Artists**

If you are an artist, either one will do. The real test for me is the operating system and how well the pen on your WACOM tablet works with it. Both can use or make custom brushes and have great tricks/effects. 

**Photographers**

If you are a serious professional photographer, like it or not Photoshop is the industry leader for a reason. Yes, GIMP is great and if you are you own boss, learn it and use it - GIMP will do you proud. I have done great things with GIMP on a professional level. There is no reason to say it cannot be done in my mind. That said, Photoshop is more advanced at this point. 

**Conclusion**

I feel that a professional company needs to step in and fund GIMP or it will be playing catch up forever. However it is a force to be reckoned with and may well be one of the greatest FOSS programs out there, hands down. To me, OpenOffice.org and GIMP are ties in the number one spot, but if I had to pick on, OpenOffice.org would be the winner as best FOSS program.
