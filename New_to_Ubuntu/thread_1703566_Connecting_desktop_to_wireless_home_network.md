### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-03-09
You say

> modem and router are located in the basement.

Is that two devices or one? I have a wireless modem/router. There are four ethernet ports on the back. so, I can connect four devices to this modem/router by ethernet cable if I so wish.

I wonder if you can do the same. You will do doing long runs of cable. So, make sure you get good quality cable. It will be more expensive.

If you have a wireless modem and a separate router, then move the router closer to the computer and printer. Use the router to route the data around your network. You could also think about a ethernet hub or a switch. You might find that these devices allow you to connect more devices than you need to.

Here is a link that I just got from google:

[http://www.duxcw.com/faq/network/hubsw.htm](http://www.duxcw.com/faq/network/hubsw.htm)

Regards.

### Post by quickk on 2011-03-14
In the end, I went with a wireless bridge.   It seems that a bridge is essentially the same as a wireless adapter, with the difference that there are multiple ports to which I can connect wired devices.  

It works well, and I don't need any drivers.  The only thing is that the speed still isn't the greatest, but at least it is fast enough so that my internet connection does not saturate.
