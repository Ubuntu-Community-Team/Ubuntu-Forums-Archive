### Post by Colin Keenan on 2011-06-30
In case other people who like the Unity Panel want to be able to kill misbehaving windows, I figured out a way to get that good old "x" mouse pointer that kills whatever window you click on. To set it up, follow these steps:

1) Click on the upper left corner of your screen and start typing "ke..", you should see "Keyboard Shortcuts" as the second choice.

2) In the Keyboard Shortcuts app, click the "Add" button. Name the new shortcut "Xkill" or whatever. Set the command to "gnome-terminal --geometry=70x1 -x xkill".

3) Click the "Apply" button. Your Xkill command will be listed under Custom Shortcuts. Look in the far right column and click on the word "Disabled" and it will change to "New Shortcut...".

4) Do the key sequence you want this to be assigned to. I typed Ctrl-Alt-Esc which was already assigned to something useless so when it asked if I wanted to disable that useless thing, I did.

And that's it. Now, whenever a window is misbehaving, I just type Ctrl-Alt_Esc; a small terminal window pops up telling me to click on the window I want to kill and my mouse pointer turns into the familiar "x". When I click on the bad window, it closes, and so does the terminal window. My mouse pointer and computer all go back to normal with no need to open System Monitor and hunt down the problem process, or no need to log off or reboot either.
