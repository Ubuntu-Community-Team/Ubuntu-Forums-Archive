### Post by donkyhotay on 2008-12-31
Operating systems don't get 'uninstalled' like applications. You can remove them by removing the partition or installing over the top of them. Depending on how you install ubuntu you can either have it completely replace suse or have them both on your computer at the same time (dual-booting). Linux is very different from windows and any distro will take some time to learn. If you're not too attached to the data on your computer I would recommend installing a couple different linux distributions and find the one that works for you. I use ubuntu almost exclusively now but when I began migrating to linux I started with fedora then moved to debian before finally settling on ubuntu. Installing a couple different distro's will also give you familiarity with installing an operating system and partitioning your drives. Now if you have important information on your hard drive that you can't afford to lose then your better off getting a few liveCD's of different distributions and testing them way (the ubuntu installer disc is also a liveCD BTW) and of course back up that data before you try installing a new operating system.
