### Post by Autodave on 2018-09-07
Could be many things.  Some info on your system might help us.

My first guess is a memory stick going bad. If you have more than one in the machine, try removing one at a time and see if problem persists. If problem goes away, the stick laying on your desk is the bad one. :-)  Always make sure that you have the sticks in order: there always has to be one in the #1 slot.

Next guess would be heat build up. Clean dust and dirt out, make sure that allo fans are working. If desktop, try leaving side cover off and maybe even blowing some cool air in there with a small fan.

Third guess would be a power supply failing. Again, dust build up could contribute to this.

### Post by DuckHook on 2018-09-07
From your description, I would suspect HW. It's not a strong suspicion, just a hunch. The main reason is that it's unusual to have two very different OSes go south in a short time. You also state that your HW is old.

Have you checked HDD integrity? Could it be cracked MB? Is your PSU putting out reliable power? Are all fans functioning and machine not overheating? Run RAM check. Check GPU as well as CPU. What do your logs say?

If all HW components check out, then try running from a LiveUSB to see if problem persists. If LiveUSB is fine and stable for extended period, then you have a possibly unstable OS installed and should try reinstalling it.

The above are the usual procedures for tracking down issues like these, but they are sometimes hard nuts to crack.
