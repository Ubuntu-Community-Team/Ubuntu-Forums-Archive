### Post by yetiman64 on 2011-09-28
I would agree with paqman re the updating. Also with dangertux re 10.04 (1 more year of support compared to 10.10) - unless of course your hardware requires the use of 10.10 in particular for some reason.

Although such beasts are largely a worry in the Windows world, [[COLOR=Blue]botnets[/COLOR]]("http://www.google.com/search?q=linux+machines+in+botnets&num=30&hl=en&safe=off&client=firefox-a&tbo=1&rls=org.mozilla:en-US:unofficial&output=search&source=lnt&tbs=qdr:y&sa=X&ei=QHKDTsbFCeeaiAevkKD-Dg&ved=0CAgQpwUoBQ&biw=1023&bih=472") it appears are also possible with linux machines. These generally aren't just to gain access to your files/data but more so to commandeer your hardware for nefarious purposes on the internet in general. A fully patched system in an open source environment (lots of code oversight/accountability) should be far less prone to such problems.

Advising someone not to do updates (which include patches for security exploits - yes they do exist in Linux) is really not advisable in itself. So OP although you "don't have to" as far as updating goes, it is very advisable to do so IMO.
