### Post by QIII on 2009-09-18
Some tasks are substantially greater than 20% faster, some not.

But you definitely get the RAM.

On a 32 bit system with PAE enabled (which takes a bit of a performance hit, though) you theoretically have a max of 64GB.  Certainly more than you can fit on a commercial motherboard.

With a 64 bit system, you can address about 16 exabytes of memory.  How many copies of the Library of Congress did you want in RAM at one time?
