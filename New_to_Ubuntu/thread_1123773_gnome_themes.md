### Post by Yvan300 on 2009-04-12
When you click customize you will see a lot of tabs. All the tabs put together make up a theme. A theme is a combination of the different options in the tabs. The theme you have installed may not really be a theme but may be an aspect of the theme, thus showing up in one of those little tabs. So search through the tabs to see if you find your theme :)

### Post by thunk77 on 2009-04-12
Go to System->Preferences->Appearance, then click on the "customize" button. A new window will pop up that has several tabs (Controls, Color, Window Borders, Icons and Cursor). The parts that are of interest to you are Controls and Window Borders. Say for example you downloaded the Dust theme. Scrolling in the "Controls" tab you should find an entry called Dust. Click on it and you should have the Dust theme controls. Do the same for Window Borders and so on.

P.S. Don't be afraid to look around and find out for yourself.

### Post by RuleMaker on 2009-04-12
You are talking about GTK theme, right?
If so, a directory named like your theme should appear in $HOME/.themes
Make sure its there, if it's not extract the contents of your downloaded theme in that directory.
Then go System -> Preferences -> Appearance -> Customize and select the controls tab (if you are trying to apply a new GTK theme) and select your theme.
