For long battery life, the atom processor is good.  Next to compatibility the most important things are battery life and weight/size.  Don't discount the netbooks too quickly, as nothing gets old quicker than lugging a large laptop around.  Ideally you want something that you hardly notice until you need it.  You adapt quite quickly to smaller size - you just have to judge the best compromise between lugging hardware and small size.

I've had good luck with my ASUS Eee PC working out of the box with all of 10.04, 10.10, and 11.04.  It would be best to investigate any WIFI and video problems before buying however, regardless of what you go with.

Netbooks are cheaper because they use smaller screens and are generally lower power units.  Mostly, Linux runs quite well on them.

Chris

### Post by Learning Linux 2011 on 2011-05-16
You might try a site like NewEgg.com
They have a ton of laptops, most of which are around $400 +/-

You would be very hard pressed to find a laptop that has Linux installed by default. But you can get alot for $400 these days.

You may want to reconsider the Netbook.  They are extremely small (usually less than 10" screens (hard on the eyes), tiny keyboards (hard on the fingers/wrists) and don't have DVD or CD drives.  Although they do get good battery life and are nice if you really don't use them much.

You will have a hard time getting a full laptop for under $300.

You may also try something local like your newspaper or Craigslist.  There are usually tons of computers on Craig's list (unless you live in the middle of nowhere).

### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-05-17
> **hanzj said:**
> I don't really need a small size, I think. I think I'm okay with a regular size laptop. Plus, I prefer a big screen over a small one. I prefer a full-size keyboard (actually, I prefer an ergonomic keyboard, like the Microsoft split keyboard I use for my desktop computer).

I think you have to work out what you want to use the machine for.  Initially I wanted something just to use as an E-reader and web browser.  I got my 900SD off Ebay for $100.  It came with windows installed but I wiped that and immediately put 10.04 LTS on it.

I love the machine for it's portability, and wouldn't dream of taking my 15" laptop on airlines and so on.  The 900SD with its 9" screen is tiny and the keyboard is very small.  I adapted enough to use it on a recent trip for learning Python programming - I took some tutorials with me as I knew I would be out of range of 3G and wifi internet most of the time.  I'm a touch typist...  Good job my hands are small!

I realise this machine is not for everyone, but I can touch type on it while standing up, holding the machine against my body with my wrists.  Super portable. ;-)

There's some nice 12" machines now that would be a good compromise.

You won't really find any laptop or netbook keyboard very ergonomic, though they do try.  I have a small keyboard that I take with me sometimes when I've got a lot of writing to do, but I prefer not to carry too much most of the time.  The portable keyboard is full size but does not have a numeric pad or or other extra keys except as an extra function control on the keyboard.  It fits in the netbook bag quite nicely.

Another factor to consider is how cheap you can get extra batteries for.  I have three batteries for the Eee PC, one original, one same size from a third party, and another 3rd party double capacity battery.  Recently I went on a three day trip and forgot the charger.  Just made it through with enough power, having read three books, done a lot of browsing, and wrote about 12 foolscap pages.  I had to get rather creative with power consumption however.

Chris.
