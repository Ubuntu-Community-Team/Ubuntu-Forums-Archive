### Post by Autodave on 2020-02-07
I have used Stacer before.  What I found is that it pretty much didn't do anything.  That could be because I don't muck up my system with junk.

Keep in mind that Linux is NOT Windows.  Things that you needed to do constantly in Windows (defrag, check for orphaned registry files, remove things from the boot-up menu, etc) do not need to be done in Linux except on very rare occasions. I have 12 machines right now running Xubuntu and I do zero maintenance on any of them.  And they are all just as fast as the day that I installed Xubuntu 16.04 or 18.04 on them.

### Post by MartyBuntu on 2020-02-07
I just use Ubuntu Cleaner to free up storage space by getting rid of old kernels and headers. Other than reclaiming room on the drives, they don't really "improve" the performance of your installation, as Autodave has stated.
