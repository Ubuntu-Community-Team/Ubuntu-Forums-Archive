### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-12-01
Did you have Enable Wireless ticked? When you shut down your computer did you use a keyboard function key combination to turn off the wireless adapter? That would immediately disconnect you from the wireless router. Did you shut down Ubuntu or hibernate? The hibernate process would naturally switch off the wireless adapter or it would drain the battery.

If this happens again try clicking on the network manager icon and making sure that you have both Enable Networking and Enable Wireless ticked. These two menu options act like on/off switches for wired and wireless connections.

Regards

### Post by Lisiano on 2011-12-02
About the bad sectors. Keep an eye on that and do a extended self-test. If the amount of bad sectors keeps increasing. Consider throwing out your HDD and getting a replacement. If not, try to remember if you dropped your laptop or accidentally put it down on a table too hard. If you remember doing that, then it's physical damage and it might not increase, but if you don't, it's showing signs of old age and will probably die soon. Note that if there is serious physical damage, it will also increase.
