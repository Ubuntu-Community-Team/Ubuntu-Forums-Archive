### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-02-16
May I suggest something? There is a big difference between 10.10 and 11.10 as you have noticed.

You can do two things:

1) click on the folder icon in the Launcher (side panel). That will open the file manager and you can use that to look for the folder where your files are. It works much the same as in 10.10.

2) click on the Ubuntu icon at the top of the Launcher. That will open the Dash. At the bottom of the pane you will see four icons. Going from left to right they are Home, Applications, Files, Music. Click on the third icon from the left and you can search for you files.

You should also search for the Help application. That will give you the Ubuntu Desktop Guide. You should read it. Here is a link to it online:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/11.10/ubuntu-help/shell-overview.html]("https://help.ubuntu.com/11.10/ubuntu-help/shell-overview.html")

That will help you find your way around 11.10.

Your documents will be in the same place as before and if you are using upgraded versions of the same programs those programs will be accessing the same setting folders as in 10.10. There might be one difference.

In the past Openoffice was the office suite. Now it is Libreoffice. So, if you created any documents with Openoffice you now need to open them in Libreoffice to help Libreoffice work out where you store your documents.

Regards.
