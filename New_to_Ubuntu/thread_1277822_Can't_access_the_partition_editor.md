### Post by oldfred on 2009-09-28
On my portable it is F2 and my desktop it is F12, you should see a quick screen as it boots telling you what key to push. 

Older computers may not have that choice and you have to go into the BIOS often F8 or delete key but again the initial screen from the computer should tell you what to push. My old computer I pushed keys until it worked. Once in BIOS you have to tell the system to boot from the CD first, then the HD drives or floppy.

Really old computers will only have a choice of the floppy or hard drive in BIOS for booting. You then have to create a floppy that lets you boot other devices with GRUB.
