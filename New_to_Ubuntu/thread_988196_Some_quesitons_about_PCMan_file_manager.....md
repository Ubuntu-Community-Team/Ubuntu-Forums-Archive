### Post by cozmicharlie on 2008-11-20
I assume you mean you installed pcman, replaced and removed Nautilus.  However, I am not sure what you mean by "it does not have control over the desktop"?  Do you mean it does not open automatically and you have to go into the menu and manually start it?  If so then add it to your sessions menu.  Go to menu>System>Preferences>Sessions.  Click add and add it.  If you don't know the command to start it then look in your applications menu - right click on applications and go to edit menus, right click on pcman and copy and paste the start command into the sessions dialog box.  Then when you log in it will automatically start.

Hope this helps.
