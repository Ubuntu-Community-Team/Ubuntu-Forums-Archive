### Post by mikechant on 2009-03-24
You don't specify which model the camera is, but looking around it appears the only digital connection on the camera is likely to be the firewire port. So you probably need a PCMCIA firewire card for your laptop (apparently there's no such thing as a firewire to USB adaptor for technical reasons). As to whether that card will work with Ubuntu, I guess you need to identify which card or cards you are thinking of buying and post that info here. The ones I found here:
[http://www.mrusb.co.uk/catalog/index.php?cPath=18&osCsid=ee22dfa4cd35bb53cb1e0fae72c6d2e4](http://www.mrusb.co.uk/catalog/index.php?cPath=18&osCsid=ee22dfa4cd35bb53cb1e0fae72c6d2e4)
were only about £10-£20 ($15-$30).

If you're not prepared to spend any money, I don't think this can be done as a digital transfer.

I guess your camera has analogue video outputs so if you don't mind losing quality you could maybe connect it to a DVD recorder, transfer the videos to DVD and then read the DVD in Ubuntu.

### Post by mikechant on 2009-03-27
> what program do I need to upload my video files from my JVC miniDV video camera through a USB transfer?

Like I said above, I don't think you can do this at all via USB; you need a firewire card.
If you get that, then a program like Kino should be suitable for video capture and editing. Link: [http://www.kinodv.org/article/static/2](http://www.kinodv.org/article/static/2)
Note that this article refers to Firewire by its 'proper' name - IEEE 1394
