### Post by squenson on 2009-11-06
Memory is the most important component for better performances. If you could increase it without breaking your wallet, do it.

You may consider another Linux distribution.

I am currently using puppylinux ([http://www.puppylinux.org/](http://www.puppylinux.org/)) on a 6-year old AMD 550 MHz laptop and it boots in 40 seconds and applications launch like I have never seen before. I put 512 MB more memory a couple of years ago to 768 MB and I paid $40 or so.
