### Post by diablo75 on 2011-01-16
The only mouse I've ever encountered where the software that came with the mouse actually made the mouse do something more was a Logitech iFeel mouse, which was a force-feedback mouse.  Basically, third party software would cause the mouse to vibrate in different ways to make you "feel" the window edges on screen.  Unfortunately there was no way to get this software to do anything useful in a Linux environment but that's okay because, to be honest, the vibrations were really annoying.

As for your mouse, if you don't know whether or not your missing potential functionality it's probably safe to assume that you are not missing anything at all.  Mice are pretty generic.  They have optical motion sensors and a few buttons and need not be more complex than that.  But if a mouse has more than three buttons on it, chances are the additional buttons won't be assigned to anything by default.  This doesn't mean they don't work, but that they won't work until you tell them to do something specific for you, whether it be a cool Compiz related shortcut or some other shortcut.
