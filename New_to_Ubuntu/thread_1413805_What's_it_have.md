### Post by mikewhatever on 2010-02-22
> **iRock said:**
> I am looking into putting UNR on my Netbook being Jaunty has no support for the NICs and Karmic's is not that good. I am hoping that the Netbook version does have this support so that I am not stuck with Windows on a highly capable PC.

UNR is basically the same as Ubuntu desktop, just with modified graphics. If something isn't supported in Ubuntu desktop, it most likely won't be supported under UNR.

> Does it(UNR) have a partitioner to install into the /, /home, and swap partitions?

Yep, like in Ubuntu desktop, it's called manual partitioning.:)

> Does it have most of the normal stuff in its menu that the ubuntu desktop has?

Yes it does, and if you don't find something, it can be installed.

> Is it capable of zooming by using two fingers on the touchpad like the iPhone does?[COLOR="Red"](This works in the Windows XP that is on it now.)[/COLOR]

Probably not, but that depends on the touchpad driver. Mine doesn't support anything but tapping and two finger scrolling.

> If I burn the image to a disk, will it run?


Don't think so, simply because netbooks don't have cdroms.:)
