### Post by FreewheelinFrank on 2009-03-04
A computer without Windows is like a fish without a bicycle.

To connect to your modem, you'll need the IP address of the modem.

For that, you'll need the exact model number, but it could be:

192.168.1.254

[http://www.dslreports.com/shownews/ATT-DSL-Customers-Getting-New-Modems-88088](http://www.dslreports.com/shownews/ATT-DSL-Customers-Getting-New-Modems-88088)

Once you have connected to the modem, you'll need to configure it to talk to your ISP.

Your ISP will probably give you a Windows CD full of cack or a lot of guff about how Linux isn't supported, but ignore that.

Usually what you need is username, password, is it PPPoE or PPPoA and a couple of numbers to bung in to the settings.

The exact model would help here.
