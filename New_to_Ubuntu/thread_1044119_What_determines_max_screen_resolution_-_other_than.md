I'm looking at the Dell Mini 12 on which to have my first foray as an end user into Linux. I've used Linux for servers (some huge) and in sys admin & DBA capacity. But not for my personal use. 

The only reason that I haven't ordered it yet is that I would like the capability to support an external monitor at 1400x1000 or more. The limit as quoted by Dell (in the spec and by phone) is 1200x768. The Mini 12 has a graphics chipset that I think is called the GMA 500. 

In general, I'm a bit up in the air as to what exactly determines max screen resolution other than the screen. Is it something in the chipset, the wiring (VGA vs. WVGA), the drivers, the OS???

Would appreciate a brief explanation or a pointer to where I can read up on this.

### Post by mcduck on 2009-01-19
The display, and your graphics adapter. (With display being most important since LCD displays work best at their native resolution).

Cables _can_ limit the resolution, but only with very long or poor quality cables. On desktop use you're not likely to ever be limited by the cables.
