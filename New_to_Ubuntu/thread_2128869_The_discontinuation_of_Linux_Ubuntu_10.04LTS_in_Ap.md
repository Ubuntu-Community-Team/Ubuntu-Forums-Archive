### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-03-24
You can easily use gnome 3 but have it look just like gnome 2, as in ubuntu 10.04, by following the instructions at [12.04 LTS Gnome Classic]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1966370")

There are some differences in, for example, how you change the panel contents (Alt+Rt click), but it is basically very similar to gnome 2 so yu should find most of what you want much more easily than in Unity.

However, as others have said, you may prefer to try Xubuntu which is more traditional in its layout.
