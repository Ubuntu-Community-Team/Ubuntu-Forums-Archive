Hi Jan,
You didn't mention what your sister's level of Linux experience and "techiness" is.

Xubuntu is a good place to start, but you'll have to use the Alternate CD instead of the live CD. I personally would not be happy with Xubuntu's performance on that machine, but if your sister's needs are very modest, she may be okay. The main advantage to Xubuntu is that she can come here to Ubuntu Forums and get outstanding support and advice.

Fluxbuntu and I have a rocky relationship. If she is an experienced Ubuntu user and wants to stay within the Ubuntu family, Fluxbuntu is her best bet for performance. But, if she's never used Ubuntu before...

...if she's never used Ubuntu before, she won't know what she's missing, so my advice is leave the Ubuntu family behind and go with something like Puppy, DSL, or SliTaz. Personally, I would recommend Puppy (or one of its "Puplets" like Teenpup or Breeezy) for a younger person without Linux experience.

Good luck!

(edit)How on earth could I forget to mention Crunchbang? That's another Ubuntu-based option that I'd say falls between Xubuntu and Fluxbuntu in performance.
