### Post by diablo75 on 2009-03-15
You might try double-clicking on the sound icon at the top to open your mixer, then click on the Preferences button, and check everything off (or at least everything that looks like it could possibly produce sound).  After you do that, your mixer will have expanded with new options.  Try playing with the levels like "PCM Surround" "Surround" "PCM Front" etc, while you have some music playing.  Chances are your sound will come out after you turn up a seemingly mislabeled volume level.  It's how I got my sound to work.
