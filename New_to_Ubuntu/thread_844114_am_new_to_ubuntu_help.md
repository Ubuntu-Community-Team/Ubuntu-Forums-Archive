### Post by scotcella on 2008-06-29
Read lots, and ask questions.

If you're familiar with using open office and firefox in Windows, then using Ubuntu should be a breeze for you.

"PLEASE HELP" titles aren't helpful. Be more specific with what you want to be helped with, ie if you have problems with um.......  firefox for example, you could put your title as "Firefox issues, help needed". 

Sometimes I open a post that screams "HELP ME PLEASE" only to find that I'm unable to help the person due to the issue being beyond my knowledge. I've been only using Ubuntu on and off for 2 years and exclusively for the last 3 months or so.

Previous posts are good starting points. Perhaps start reading other posts and responses to get a feel of things. Start using your rig for everyday things ie word docs, emails, internet, what ever you use it for, to get a feel of OS- say for a month. When you get a grasp of the OS, then I guess you can start making changes to customise your rigg.

I'm not at the point of using Compiz yet, but I will when I feel ready!

Anyway welcome!! Ubuntu is great and the community here is wonderful. 

If you get frustrated, don't give up- keep trying!! If you feel that Ubuntu is not for you, it's not a bad thing. You can always try other flavours of Linux and find the one that suits you the best.

Is Ubuntu for YOU? [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=63315]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=63315") is another good starting point.
