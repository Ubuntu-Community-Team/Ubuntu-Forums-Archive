### Post by Temposs on 2009-01-22
Is there a particular reason you want to upgrade your version? Unless there is a particular functionality you're looking to gain, it's usually best to stay with what you've got. 8.04 will be supported for a long time, and the Dellbuntu mods are designed for your Dell machine.

You never know what will break on an upgrade. From a user perspective, it's more important to not make changes that might break things, when it's possible.

Now, if you just like to muck around with your OS like I do, then go ahead and upgrade to 8,10. Waiting for Jaunty won't do much good, because you'll have to upgrade to 8.10 first before upgrading to 9.04, unless you decide to install 9.04 from scratch.

### Post by Temposs on 2009-01-22
If there is a version 8.10 released by Dell, or Dell-approved patches for 8.10, then that will likely run well on your machine. However, if your intent is to upgrade using the vanilla/default ubuntu 8.10, then you may end up with some of the Dell tweaks being overwritten possibly, and new features that Dell didn't anticipate when they released their 8.04 version. This is just a possibility, not an inevitability. I just want you not to be disappointed if your upgrade isn't flawless.

Newer versions have more and better features, but it just depends on if you want to take the chance of something not working that used to work. But, I always upgrade my machine when a new version comes out, personally, because I like seeing the new features and I don't mind dealing with the bugs that pop up, especially since my hardware was not chosen with Ubuntu in mind.

So, have fun upgrading if you want :-) The community is here to back you up if you run into trouble!
