### Post by scorp123 on 2009-05-22
> **jlhaslip said:**
> Ctl-C Ctrl+C is used for aborting programs. I know I know ... On Windows it's used for copying stuff. And I know: there are Linux programs that use Ctrl+C too for this purpose. But it's kinda dangerous because most programs do interpret Ctrl+C the way it was meant: as an abort command. So I kindly suggest to change your habits a little. Instead you can highlight stuff with your mouse (yes, really: just highlight it) and then click the middle mouse button in the target window: voila, instant copy & paste.

As for your actual question: Do a right-click on the "parcellite" icon and check the "Preferences" ... in my case these two options are checked and active:
[LIST]
[*]Clipboards: Use Copy (Ctrl+C)
[*]Use Primary (Selection)
[/LIST]

Can you check please what your settings are?

---

### Post by jlhaslip on 2009-05-22
