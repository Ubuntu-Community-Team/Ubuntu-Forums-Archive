### Post by seadog101 on 2011-11-13
I've managed to install Ubuntu 11.10 sucessfully on my Samsung N145.  Although I'm not exactly a noob to this, I'm certainly not a boffin.

My only sticky moment was having to change the boot sequence so that I could install using a USB stick.  The samsung was set to always boot from the HDD first.  By doing this I have been able to have a dual boot Ubuntu/Win7.

It works well, in respect that there are no problems.  My only disappointment was speed.  I have previously ressurected an old and crancky Compaq Presario laptop with earlier versions of Ubuntu, and it is blistering quick to boot up and com eto life (38secs as opposed to XP's 2mins plus).  

My samsung has 2Gb of DDR2 RAM, but despite this it's not anywhere as fast as my older presario, which is running similar RAM and processor speed.

However, I am very pleased with 11.10 and will keep on doing my stuff on it.  My favourite moment of every day is the bemused look of less enlightened colleagues who see me doing my stuff on something so small and dainty.

### Post by I2k4 on 2011-11-14
Why not just play with some alternatives using Live USB?  The directions here are bullet proof

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download)

Your netbook will have more guts than mine does, and will run just about anything fairly well.  Mostly it will depend on how much you like the looks and feel, and how much you like the preinstalled software.  Although I'm dual booting Lubuntu, I found the nuisance factor in getting rid of unwanted preinstalled software and adding what I do want, would lead me to recommend starting out with Mint.
