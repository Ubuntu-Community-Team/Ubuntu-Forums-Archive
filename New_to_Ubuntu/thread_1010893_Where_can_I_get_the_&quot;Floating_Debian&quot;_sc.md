### Post by Kellemora on 2008-12-15
Hi JL

You can also change the other splash images as well.

The Log-in screen for example, that you get to through System/Administration/Login Window
I believe is stored in /usr/pixmaps/splash

and the screen just after log-in, your background image, is brought up using /apps/gnome-sessions/options/splash_image.
Just replace splash_image with the path to your image.

Also, you can find lots of them at gnome-look.org if my memory is correct.

I was used to seeing my company logo on all of my screens, so that was one of the first things I changed.  That was like over 3 months ago now, before I started making notes as clear as I do now for referring back to.  But I went back to the Hardy image after an upgrade or reinstall, since you only see it when nothing is open and I like Compiz-Fusion to harass friends with, hihi.........
