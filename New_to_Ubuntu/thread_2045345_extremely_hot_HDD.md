### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-08-21
If the processor is not running hot, it could simply be that the HDD is running hot by itself.  I used to use Hard Drive Inspector on my tablet PC and the drive ran so hot that the temp alarm kept going off.  Then, I installed tha "laptop" version and the alarms stopped -- basically because that version set the alarm ten degrees higher!  I think mine ran at 60 or higher most of the time.

Folks rush out to buy the faster hard drives they can for laptops -- but a fast drive generates more heat.  IF the heat bother you, you should consider replacing the drive with a slower one.  Believe me, you won't notice any difference in real-time performance with a slower drive.

### Post by sandyd on 2012-08-21
60 degrees and higher is a normal HDD temperature on a laptop. Laptops have a smaller form factor, so they run hotter.

Most of the heat is generated by the moving parts in the HDD (i.e. the spinning). If you want a cooler HDD, go for a SSD.
