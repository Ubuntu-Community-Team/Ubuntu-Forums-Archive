### Post by aysiu on 2010-02-19
Two things:

1. It depends. A lot of hardware will just work without you having to do anything. The Linux kernel includes a lot of drivers. Some hardware you will have to install drivers manually for. Some hardware just won't work at all.

2. Luckily, you don't have to *install* Ubuntu to find out. By default, it runs as a "live" CD (or live USB if you use UNetBootIn to "burn" the .iso to a USB stick), allowing you to fully testdrive Ubuntu on your computer without actually affecting your hard drive. Only if you actually click the *Install* icon will it install on your computer.
