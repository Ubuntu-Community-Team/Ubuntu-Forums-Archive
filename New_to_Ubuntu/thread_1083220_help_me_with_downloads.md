### Post by Xiong Chiamiov on 2009-03-01
Windows and Linux are not binary-compatible, meaning that software must be compiled separately for each of them.  Most applications aren't designed to be cross-platform, so you'll be using *different* applications to do the same things.  Two good sites to figure out what you want are [here](http://www.linuxrsp.ru/win-lin-soft/table-eng.html) and [here](http://www.linuxalt.com/).

I wouldn't recommend installing binaries that you download somewhere.  There's a concept called "software repositories" that gets explained (mostly) [here](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installingsoftware).
