### Post by sydbat on 2008-10-27
Viruses/other malware tend to do things like that. If you can, find another computer to download the Ubuntu .iso and run it as a "live session". Then, if you can access the internet, go to Trend Micro, or some other online virus checker and let it run.

If, however, you cannot get the live CD to run at all (even after changing the boot sequence in BIOS), there may be a hardware problem.
