### Post by d0006 on 2013-04-06
Some printers are just noisy.  All machines make more noise when they are old and/or dirty.

You can try to clean the printer.  Do a search for the printer to see if someone has posted information.  Do a search to find a manual.

Some suggestions for cleaning:

Tools: vacuum cleaner, paper towel or clean cloth, swabs, alcohol, something to blow air, a clean place to work.

1. Be careful. Unplug the printer! Do not force any part.
2. Take the printer apart in a safe way.  This means if it is easy, do it.  If it is hard, do not do it.  Opening a cover or removing an ink cartridge is easy.  Removing the motor is not easy. In general, do not remove any screws.
3. If you have never done this before, take pictures so you can put the printer together again.
4. Be careful with the vacuum cleaner.  Do not suck up parts from the printer!
5. Clean the print heads.  Clean where the ink cartridges mount.  Vacuum up the paper dust. Clean paper rollers. Etc.

Good luck

(These suggestions are for an ink jet printer.  Cleaning a laser printer is different.  Laser toner is very bad to breath. Most vacuum cleaners can not filter the toner particles.  If you try to vacuum toner with a vacuum cleaner that does not have a HEPA filter, the vacuum will blow the toner into the room and you will breath it.)
