### Post by DogDragonsm31 on 2008-08-23
I'm still a newbie, But a friend gave me a book to help.
It's called Beginning Ubuntu Linux: From Novice to Professional
and it's great it tells you about the history of linux and Ubuntu.
The part I like is I was alway a window user and this book puts
windows side by side with Ubuntu and shows you the programs windows
use and what programs Ubuntu use to do the same thing.:cool:
You can get it here [http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ubuntu-Linux-Novice-Professional/dp/1590596277](http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ubuntu-Linux-Novice-Professional/dp/1590596277)
It starts with Introducing the world of Linux than Installing Ubuntu,
The no-nonsense getting started guide,The shell and beyond,Multimedia,
Office tasks, Keeping your system running.
It's helping me at my own speed and this book covers everything in an 
easy to understand language and shows you what you would see on your 
screen. That's the part that help me the most,I wish I could just
pass it out but it has a copy right.:(
So if you're looking for help this book is a must get.
Just sharing something a friend shared with me. :)
