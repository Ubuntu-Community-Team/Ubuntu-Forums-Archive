### Post by Hospadar on 2009-10-19
you might be able to use avidemux, it actually has it's own encoder but it's pretty straightforward to use and I've had success in the past.

That said, I'd just as soon use the command line.  The learning curve may be a little steep to start with but if you spend 10 or 15 minutes staring at the man page you'll figure it out, and then you'll have a lot more options available to you than any GUI could give you, and you'll be able to process stuff much more quickly than you could with any GUI.  Furthermore, the time spent learning the CLI arguments and switches probably won't be a whole lot longer than the time you'd spend wrestling with the GUI to do what you want.

Anywho, that's my plug for CLI
