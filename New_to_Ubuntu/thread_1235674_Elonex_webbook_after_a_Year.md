### Post by mollyj on 2009-08-09
I haven't actually logged into the Ubuntu site for a while.... too many other things to do. I did try to keep track of webbook matters until Christmas when the webbook blog fizzled out. Any how I can give you a rundown on the years experience.
My webbook is still working
I haven't dared upgrade the OS from the original having heard of all sorts of horror stories about losing it altogether.
The machine does serve a purpose.
It can browse the web.
It can be used to write documents.
It will run inkscape so I can draw diagrams.
It runs Scilab in basic terminal mode ;I haven't tried anything complex
It occasionally shows a corrupted screen on boot which is usually cured with a reboot.
The battery lasts just long enough to get me to London but needs recharging before the return journey.
No, the sound has never worked correctly and even when just playing mp3's  it was almost inaudible.
It doesn't like booting from anything other than the hard drive
My one contact with Elonex support was distinctly unhelpful: verging on the downright rude.
Community support is obscure and mostly incomprehensible to me...........It seems to be mostly arguments about competing operating systems, peculiar idiosyncracies and pointless diatribes on copyright.

In short it works..............but I am not a linux expert (or even a competent beginner) and I haven't the time or inclination to fix problems by "sudo grep -X-V $ *#£** foo etc.-pling" which should have been sorted out before the product was sold.
It was bought as an experiment.....It serves a purpose and I fully expect that it will fail three weeks after the guarantee expires.
