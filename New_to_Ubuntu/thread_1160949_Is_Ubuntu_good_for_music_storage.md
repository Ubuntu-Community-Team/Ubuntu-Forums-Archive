### Post by ChildOfMana on 2009-05-16
[Banshee]("http://banshee-project.org/") always does the trick for me. It'll download cover art by default and will sync your iPod no problem (I don't think it supports iPod Touch or iPhone yet though). It'll also transcode on the fly when syncing with an iPod (e.g. if you have an album in your Banshee library in FLAC format, it'll automatically transcode it to MP3 as it adds it to your iPod).

It's all down to personal preference though. Some people don't like Banshee.

The beauty of Open Source is that you get to try them all and choose the one that most suits you.

Happy hunting :)

### Post by wbee on 2009-05-16
Hello,

Lawrence,let me take a shot at your question.

--Among the other fine players listed here,consider VLC. It is lightweight by design,if you prefer just the audio output with an equaliser.

--If you have or decide to add a separate hard drive to store your music,you can store it in FLAC,which is just as good as 44.1,red book compact disk quality.
