### Post by Therion on 2008-10-29
Yes you can move them from one drive to another.  

Whether or not you can use them depends on what kind of file they are.  

.mp3 files?  Sure.  .pdf or .jpg? Sure, no problem.  

Proprietary file types may, or may not, work with your Ubuntu install; it would depend on the specific file type.

### Post by Axos on 2008-10-29
I've got an NTFS-formatted USB hard disk plugged into my Ubuntu 8.04 system and it works great. When the drive is plugged in an icon appears on the desktop. Double-click it to look at the contents of the drive. To eject the drive, right-click the icon and click Unmount volume.
