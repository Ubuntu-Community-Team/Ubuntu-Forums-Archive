### Post by eriqjaffe on 2008-05-09
> **saj0577 said:**
> Any tips how to make it look better? Add background transparency statistics etc..Xcompmgr is the best (only?) thing for transparency, and I recommend [conky](http://conky.sourceforge.net/) for statistics, both of which are in the repos.  Looking at the screenshot you posted....the statistics tools are a a bunch of dockapps running in Fluxbox's "slit", which I never really liked.  But if it's your cup of tea, [this](http://dockapps.org/) is a good place to start with dockapps.

> **saj0577 said:**
> How i change the settings for fluxbox without editing xml files?For best results, you can't.  There's a tool out there called [fluxconf](http://devaux.fabien.free.fr/flux/), but I found it to be a little clunky.  Technically, Fluxbox doesn't use XML files, they're far simpler than that.  Just plain text files that are pretty easy to edit with vi, nano, or whatever text editor you prefer.

One thing I find that goes a long way towards making a *box look cleaner is having a solid GTK2 theme.  I use [lxappearance](http://www.gnomefiles.org/app.php/LXAppearance) with Openbox for that, but it should work just fine with Fluxbox as well.
