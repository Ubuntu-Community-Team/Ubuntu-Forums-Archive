### Post by QLee on 2011-01-30
The higher refresh rates were important in the days of CRT monitors because of characteristics of the hardware. The phosphors on the screen had to "glow" and needed to be refreshed faster than the "glow" decayed or else human eyes perceived "flicker" when looking at them. With LCD monitors the display essentially doesn't change state until the video interface sends the signal to do so. No need to worry about fast refresh rates to avoid eye strain.

### Post by weedeater64 on 2011-01-30
**Liquid crystal displays**

 Refresh rate or the temporal resolution of an LCD is the number of times per second in which the display draws the data it is being given. Since activated LCD pixels do not flash on/off between frames, LCD monitors exhibit no refresh-induced flicker, no matter how low the refresh rate.[28] High-end LCD televisions now feature up to 240 Hz refresh rate, which requires advanced digital processing to insert additional interpolated frames between the real images to smooth the image motion. However, such high refresh rates may not be actually supported by pixel response times and the result can be visual artifacts that distort the image in unpleasant ways.
