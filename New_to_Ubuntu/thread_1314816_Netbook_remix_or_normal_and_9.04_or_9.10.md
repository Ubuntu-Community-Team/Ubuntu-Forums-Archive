### Post by Whatever Whoever on 2009-11-07
Hi! I am using an Acer Aspire One 110 with UNR 9.04 since 07.2009 (?).[B]


How/Why

[/B]I did add a couple of thingies to it to improve the useability:
* stuffed the box full of RAM (1.5 GO) (note 1)
* added a 4 GO SD card in the "storage expansion" slot (note 2)
* added an 8 GO USB memory stick (note 3)
* added a Bluetooth module (note 4)
(For more info on the hardware modding of Acer Aspire One, check the **[site]("http://tnkgrl.wordpress.com/")** of the person who documented it first time in an understandable way.)
* Internet connection exclusively through a cell/mobile/handy/whatever modem (Huawei E220) 

I know at least two issues with [COLOR=Red]UNR 9.04[/COLOR] which can drive you nicely crazy:
[INDENT]**A**. whatever is responsible with storage management keeps showing me in the bookmarks area a link to the (unmounted!) block device of the SD card (see note 2). If I'll click on it, it will nicely bork the contents of the SD card. Sick, but avoidable. UNR 9.10 is figuring out that the SD card has a partition table on it.
[/INDENT][INDENT]**B**. Maybe connected with the point above, the system is completely borking itself if I put it to sleep. The only solution is to disable any kind of suspend/hibernation. No idea if this pops up also in UNR 9.10.
[/INDENT]
The funky part of the clean install of [COLOR=Red]UNR 9.10 [/COLOR]was that, whatever is responsible with the bloody network management, it completely borked the routes and also let me with a squeaky-clean resolv.conf . The netto result was that while the sytem installed smoothly and the modem came up nicely, I could not do anything Internet-related (updates, browse, email, etc.). After about 30 minutes of fixing routes with what I still remember from RedHat 6.x, I gave up and mirrored back the UNR 9.04 installation from the backup.


**Notes**

**1**. Does this void the warranty? Yes!
Is it worth? Yessiree!

**2**. This card is split into two partition via a dos partition table:
*p1* has 3 GO and is mounted to */home*
*p2* eats the rest (~800 MO) and is mounted to */var*
After borking completely an install by accidentally ejecting the card during netbook operation, I decided to remove the spring mechanism which spits the card out.

**3**. For cosmetic reasons, the USB memory stick is hidden inside the netbook. As I like to have the outer ports of the machine accesible, I have soldered the stick to the USB port of the outer PCIe port. I'm using it to boot an alternative live Linux OS and for storing backups.

**4**. Also for cosmetic reasons, the USB Bluetooth adapter is hidden inside the netbook. This is soldered to the USB port of the inner PCIe port. It's live all the time the netbook is powered on, which means that it is draining a little bit of current.
