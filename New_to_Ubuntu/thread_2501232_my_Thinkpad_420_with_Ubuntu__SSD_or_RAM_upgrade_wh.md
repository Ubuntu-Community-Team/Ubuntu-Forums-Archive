### Post by him610 on 2024-09-30
Don't know what your budgetary constraints are, but all of the suggestions will improve your system. You can make it a multi-step improvement process:
1.  Replace HDD with SSD.   Probably more expensive than adding RAM
2.  Replace Ubuntu with either Lubuntu or Xubuntu.  Cost nothing for for improved performance.
3.  Upgrade your RAM.  Probably most economical improvement; although, it won't be as noticeable as the SSD.
 
I have done this with all of my (5) systems: SSD first followed by additional RAM - sometimes a year or so later; I have been using Xubuntu for several years now.

### Post by DirtyPC on 2024-10-08
I'm running a T440 that started off as base spec i3 with 4GB RAM. I did a bunch of upgrades. In order of significance:

1) SSD - Night and day difference.

2) CPU - Jumped to an i7 4710MQ.

3) RAM - Upgraded to 16GB. Might be a bit overkill for my use but it was cheap for a kit. I haven't seen more than 7GB being used in 24.04 - even with a bunch of tabs open in Firefox.

Your T420 is the last of the great ThinkPad keyboards. It's also very upgradable. You can fit a 1080P IPS screen, whitelist remove for a modern WiFi card, ad additional storage via the Ultrabay and a bunch of other stuff.
