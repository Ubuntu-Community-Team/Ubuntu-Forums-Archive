### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-12-26
This ["old" article/post]("http://www.geeksinphoenix.com/blog/post/2011/03/06/A-computer-that-randomly-and-frequently-freezes-up.aspx") has two pictures you can use as reference. 
Slightly bent but otherwise cylindrical and with flat top usually isn't a problem. Swollen capacitors - if confirmed - is NOT your fault. It isn't Dell's fault either, it's entirely on the capacitors' manufacturer and many brands suffered the same at the time, including Apple. I'm not sure about other parts of the world but in EMEA (Europe, Middle East, Africa) Dell extended the warranty for free and replaced hundreds of thousands (or more) boards up to 5 years after the purchase date, no questions asked.

In an unrelated yet on-topic note, Dell's Optiplex line are low-end budget machines for office tasks. So, you should set your expectations accordingly.
