### Post by zerhacke on 2009-07-09
Ktorrent will use any port you tell it to.  If you have a router, you will need to configure your router to forward the port you tell Ktorrent to use.  If you are directly connected to the modem, you don't need port forwarding at all most of the time.

Take your pick on the port.  It's your call.  I suggest using one of the higher port numbers instead of lower, as most services are mapped to low port numbers and using them will cause strange things to happen.
