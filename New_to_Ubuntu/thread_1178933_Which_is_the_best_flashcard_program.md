### Post by nomnex on 2009-11-29
you probably tried them all looking at the date of your question?

Aki is best with Japanese (it's an SRS - spaced repetition system). You have to schedule your questions. Many options and configurations. I use it. A bit or reading before getting the best of its features. the version in repo did not work for me on Jaunty. The .deb from the download page on the Anki site did. The latest version 0.9.9.8.5 has many bug fixes. It will not be backported on Ubuntu Karmic. There is a thread on Launchpad about it.

Granule can handle Japanese too but you have to modify a configuration file. The rep version on Jaunty has a bug with the photo position. Compile from source 1.4.0-7 (Or install Karmic). It's a good flash card application. Very easy to use. I like the decks (flash card stacks) and the box cards using the Leitner method. The developer is responsive and friendly via sourceforge, you can ask him your questions. I use it too. It's light. It's less polish than Anki, but I learn best with it ( that's personal)

KWordQuizz is not relevant for me since it s KDE, and I am using Linux Ubuntu (Gnome)

As a word of advice, try them all, read their manuals, make a sample cards collection in each of them, use them for a while, and keep the one that best suits you.

### Post by nomnex on 2009-11-29
mnemosyne is cross platform - probably the reason why your academic team uses it, but it's not Gnome. Granule is the flash card software for Gnome. I find it superior to mnemosyne for long term memory training (see: Leitner method)

a good point for the downlodable decks available on the mnemosyne website. There are only a very few available with Granule, although I believe you best learn definitions you input yourself. Granule has a simple markup language to customize your cards.
