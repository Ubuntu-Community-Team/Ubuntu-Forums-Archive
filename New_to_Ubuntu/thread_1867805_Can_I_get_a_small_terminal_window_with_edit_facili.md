### Post by WasMeHere on 2011-10-23
How do you invoke the full screen terminal?
*ctrl + alt + T*
or
*ctrl + alt + F3*
returning to graphics mode with
*ctrl + alt + F7*

I suggest that you use *ctrl + alt + T* to get a windowed terminal (either fullscreen or as a small window) for 'normal' linux copy and paste between applications.

Have fun finding out about Ubuntu :-)
Olle

### Post by donkyhotay on 2011-10-23
Personally I use yakuake (it's in the repos) for terminal. Once you set it up you just press one button (F12 is default) and a terminal screen scrolls down similar to many FPS's like quake. Hit F12 again or just click a window behind it and it goes away and continues to work. It's great when doing tasks in the terminal that take a long time (like compiling) since I don't have to see it clutter things up but it continues to do what I told it to do last. It also allows for copy/paste, at least with the mouse, for some reason ctrl-c/ctrl-v doesn't work for me.
