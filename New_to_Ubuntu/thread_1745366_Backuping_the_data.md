### Post by Linux&amp;Gsus on 2011-05-01
You need to be a bit more specific if you want a satisfactory answer.
First you wanted to backup, now you want to compress your data efficiently...

If you have a failing HDD I'd say the most important things is that you get your data to a safe place. With or without efficient compression. Or is your problem that you don't have a big enough backup medium?
It also depends what you want/need to back up. If you have 90% videos, images, and music then there isn't much to compress, as those wont get much, if any, smaller.

Since you have to replace the HDD anyway, why not buying a replacement, set everything up and then access your failing drive from there to move over your data.
If possible get 2 new drives, even better if those are a different brand, as they are less likely to fail at around the same time then.

For backup I personally use "Back In Time". It's in the repository.
Make sure to install either the KDE or Gnome version, depending on what you are running on your machine.
