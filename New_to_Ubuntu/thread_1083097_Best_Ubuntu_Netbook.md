### Post by blackened on 2009-02-28
To my knowledge, only the Dell Mini 9 and a couple models of HP netbooks come from the factory with Ubuntu installed. Acer uses Linpus, Asus uses Xandros, and MSI uses SUSE (though not for much longer if sales of that model keep plummeting).

Really this will be a matter of personal choice: you can install Ubuntu on all of those and have most things working out of the box. All you need is a USB stick (none of the netbooks I mentioned have an optical drive), another PC (to create a bootable USB stick), and Google (for the bad times, if there are any).

Since the EEE were first on the scene, there are two flavors of Ubuntu tailored specifically for them (and work great): Easy Peasy, which has widened support to include netbooks by other manufacturers, and eeebuntu.
