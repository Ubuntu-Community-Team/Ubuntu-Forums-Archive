### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-18
Unfortunately no I do not think this is possible or at least not likely. I saw your post was getting old and had no answers yet so I thought I would say something so you would at least know. The one chance you have would be to use the  keys on the monitor itself to change the margins etc. like it will have horizontal & vertical so change the vertical and try to make it move past the bad spot, this isn't the greatest situation but I fear it might be your only chance to still use this monitor untill you can get a replacement. I don't mean to be telling you what to do, but in my opinion in the future you should shy away from Emachines PC's. I know they are cheap but unfortunately when I go to do computer repair work I see quite allot of Emachines with hardware problems which tells me they cut corners to be so cheap. I swear by ASUS products as they are the best for the money in my book but can be hard to find an ASUS desktop unless you order it online.
