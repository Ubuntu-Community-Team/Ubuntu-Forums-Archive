### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2010-07-29
A simple mixer like this one would do the trick. There are lots out there but this one is especially inexpensive and still reasonable quality.

[http://www.behringer.com/EN/Products/802.aspx](http://www.behringer.com/EN/Products/802.aspx)

It has balance and pan adjustments so you can send a mono microphone to the left or right channel (or any mix of the two). So set one microphone all the way to the left, and the other all the way to the right, then you have a stereo microphone. Note it has two microphone inputs, some have more and some have only one. You'll need two for the stereo effects. A lower end microphone like this dynamic microphone should do the trick.

[http://www.behringer.com/EN/Products/XM8500.aspx](http://www.behringer.com/EN/Products/XM8500.aspx)

You'll also need two 3-pin XLR cables for the microphones and a dual 1/4 inch mono to stereo 1/8th cable to go into your sound card. The brand and supplier don't really matter, using B&H for convenience.

[http://www.bhphotovideo.com/c/product/492510-REG/Samson_SATM10_XLR_Male_to_XLR.html](http://www.bhphotovideo.com/c/product/492510-REG/Samson_SATM10_XLR_Male_to_XLR.html)
[http://www.bhphotovideo.com/c/product/160640-REG/Hosa_Technology_CMP_159_Stereo_Mini_Male_to.html](http://www.bhphotovideo.com/c/product/160640-REG/Hosa_Technology_CMP_159_Stereo_Mini_Male_to.html)

Alternatively you could get two USB microphones and set each to record on their own channel in Audacity, then export one as the left channel and one as the right channel. Though this might not have the same audio quality depending on the quality of the microphones and would also be less flexible in the future. A basic mixer and microphones can be used for many different projects in the future. Mixer, microphones, and cables should run about $125 in the United States (not sure what it would be where you live). Cheers!
