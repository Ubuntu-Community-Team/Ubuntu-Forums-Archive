### Post by asmoore82 on 2010-11-24
I wouldn't go so far as to call them "Lazy and Unintelligent," but some good hacker programmers got their start by just being driven to try to make something work for themselves. Open-source is very conducive to copying, pasting, and rearranging the well-written code of others. For those who aren't especially gifted programmers, I think they are much more driven to "plug and chug" with code and hammer out bugs.

The only catch is that as a project matures, someone has to keep an eye on the big picture. That someone needs to be a good programmer to make sure the project isn't heading toward a dead-end.
