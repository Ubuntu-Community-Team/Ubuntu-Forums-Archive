>

Good news! You don't have to! Beryl has been replaced by Compiz Fusion, and is installed by default on recent versions of Ubuntu. Just go to System > Appearance > Desktop Effects and enable them. If you want to play with your settings there a couple of different managers for it, such as simple-ccsm and compizconfig-settings-manager.

### Post by lostlinuxkiwi on 2008-06-08
you can download compiz config manager with synaptic. Synaptic will be under the administration menu in the system menu. open synaptic and search for "compizconfig-settings-manager" then selected for install. this will then add "advanced desktop effects" to your preferences menu and add a new option in the appearances window called custom effects.

You can download emerald as well. this is another window decorator that kinda goes with compiz. On ubuntu 7.10 i had to use emerald when i enable compiz. I see that that has changed in kubuntu 8.04 now so you might not need it but if your window borders disappear when you turn compiz on then you might need emerald.
