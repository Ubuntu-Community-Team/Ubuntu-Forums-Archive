### Post by fd0man on 2008-10-13
If you're just getting started with programming, I'd recommend getting started with something like Java or C#.  Both languages are quite high-level.  You can work with both Java and C# on a large number of platforms, including a great many number of distributions of Linux-based operating systems and Windows.

Also, spend some time looking at more languages than just the one(s) that you want to learn.  Learn a little bit of C, learn a little bit of Python, learn a little bit of PHP, and so forth.  Even take a look at Assembly language for various platforms.  Work in as many languages as is possible, with little projects here and there that maybe have no significant relevance whatsoever.  Write little throw-away programs that help you to understand the libraries that you're using, too.  Working in any programming language will help to give you a broader experience and give you ideas that will carry over from it into other languages.

Also, spend some time reading higher-level books that cover the subject of programming but not in any particular language.  A couple that are very good reads, once you're a decent ways into learning one or two programming languages, are "Code Complete" and "Writing Solid Code".  Both are easily found on Amazon and generally on the Web.
