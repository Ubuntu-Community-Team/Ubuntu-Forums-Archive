### Post by hanzj on 2009-08-08
I would like to hear from those of you who have a digital audio player ("mp3 player") and who connect/sync it with your ubuntu computer.


I currently have an iPod. It's a 5th generation iPod (30 GB) with video. I sometimes have a hard time syncing with my Ubuntu computer. I have tried various programs: Rythymbox, gtkpod, Banshee, Amarok, and others. At times, I am successful in syncing music to my iPod. Other times, with one of these programs, I put audio files into my iPod, but when I click Sync or when it looks like things are synced, I check my iPod but don't find the audio files on it.
Just to let you know, when I'm on a friend's WinXP computer, I don't have problems syncing stuff onto the iPod.

Recently, I have acquired a small Sansa Clip (2 GB). On my Ubuntu computer, I love how easy I'm able to sync my MP3s onto the Sansa Clip. I just open up a Nautilus File Manager window and start dragging and dropping from my hard drive onto the MUSIC folder of the Sansa Clip.


I was thinking of buying a high capacity drag-and-drop capable mp3 player and perhaps getting rid of my iPod. But I am hesitant to sell my iPod because I bought several accessories for it to make it usable in my car.  If I could find an easy, no-hassle way to sync my digital stuff onto the iPod while on Ubuntu, please tell me. I'm close to giving up with iPod's special syncing system. 


If you are a Ubuntu user however, who has tried and tried and tried to make you syncing with your iPod on Ubuntu work but have given up because of all the hassles, please let me know, too. Please let me know whether you have switched from using an iPod to using a drag-and-drop mp3 player like Sansa or Creative or something else.

Thank you to all.
