### Post by cloyd on 2009-11-30
I used Unetbootin in Vista to create a bootable flashdrive for 9.10 netbook remix. The only problem was any change I made in the system was not persistent  . . . on reboot, changes were gone. However, files I saved on the harddrive (my windows drive) were there after reboot. I'm sure there is a way around this, but I shrunk my windows drive (using utilities that are in Vista)  and installled UNR in the free space.  It is _great_ !

Unetbootin was pretty intuitive . . . it was easy to see what I was supposed to do.

By the way, as I was considering going Linux, I purchased _Linux for Dummies_.  It didn't have much specifically about Ubuntu, but it did steer me right in doing an install so that I could dual boot Vista and UNR.
