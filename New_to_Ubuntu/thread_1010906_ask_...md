### Post by snova on 2008-12-14
Gnome is no longer an acronym, I think.

Ok. Ubuntu is the operating system. It's composed of countless small pieces. Some are invisible; you only come across them when plumbing the depths of the system. Others are more prominent. There is a program to draw the menus, the panels, the desktop... everything.

Gnome is a desktop environment. Basically it's most of what you're looking at now. Some of the programs may be separate projects, but for the most part, Gnome is what you're using. The login screen is GDM, part of Gnome. Gedit is part of Gnome. Probably most of the utilities on the menus from a fresh install are part of Gnome.

Don't take this to mean that Gnome is most of the system. It's a large portion of the *graphical* system, but there's a lot more working behind the scenes.
