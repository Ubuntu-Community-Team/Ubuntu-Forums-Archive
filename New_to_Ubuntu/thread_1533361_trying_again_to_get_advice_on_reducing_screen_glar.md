### Post by Palanthas on 2010-07-17
I will rule out screen resolution as that won't effect how bright the screen is. Colour maybe, but only if you set it with very light colours, like white, grey, or something similar...

If you are talkin about true "screen glare" like having a lamp or the sun glaring off the screen, I don't think I can help you. If you are saying that your screen's light is to bright you could try the function key (located on the bottom left of your keyboard if we have the same presario 700 laptop) plus F7 or F8 (so [Fn]+F7/F8).

If you are running Compiz, you can adjust the brightness (also opacity and saturation) by opening the "CompizConfig Settings Manager" (System>Preferences>CompizConfig Settings Manager) and going to the Accessibility section (second group down according to my computer) and selecting the "Opacity, Brightness and Saturation" and adjusting the setting in there. (if you don't already have Compiz, you can install it via the Ubuntu Software Center)

I want to think I remember seeing another setting somewhere else in either the Preferences or Administration menus about screen brightness on either Ubuntu 9.04 or 9.10 but I can't remember now. I am running 10.04 at the moment...

I hope one of those helps ya! :popcorn:

### Post by Kellemora on 2010-07-17
Hi dl

Why don't you use the mechanical settings on your monitor, the software settings can only vary between a small range within where you have the actual monitor itself set.

EG:  If your brightness setting runs from 0 to 30 and you have it set at 20 then the software can only change it between 15 and 25 more or less.

You usually don't even have to go into Menu on your monitor, most have a picture of SUN on them or something equivalent that brings up the brightness control at the touch of a button.
