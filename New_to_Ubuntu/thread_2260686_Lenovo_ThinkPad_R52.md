### Post by JeQhdMD on 2015-01-13
OK,  the important thing we don't know from what you've provided so far . . . . is . . . . . what graphics chipset does "your" laptop have (ATI graphics or Intel integrated?).   And did you bump up the ram to at least 1 gb (although) 2 would be preferable.   IF Intel graphics, the install should go well if you have a good iso, at least 1 gb ram, but, if ATI graphics, you may have to change the boot-up parameter to "nomodeset" so the machine can get by that issue.

PS:   the odds of installing Linux on this lappie improve considerably if you use something like Linux Mint 17.1 "Mate" edition or "Xubuntu"  (because of the ram and/or graphics available).

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-01-13
Welcome. Or you can try Lubuntu, which is lighter again. A full Ubuntu install, even with 1Gb of RAM, will probably not be a great result, but you can try and see what happens. 

I suggest the 'nomodeset' option given above. When you boot to the options screen from live media, hit F6 and you should find a list of options. Choose nomodeset from there and proceed.
