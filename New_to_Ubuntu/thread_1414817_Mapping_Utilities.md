### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-02-24
Yes indeed.

There's quite a few you could use.

I'm a volunteer mapper myself contributing to [openstreetmap]("http://www.openstreetmap.org").
OpenStreetMap has quite a few tools that help you map places.

You can use aerial imagery, GPS logs, or even a rough printed map and a pencil to start mapping.

Map editors that you may be interest to you:
[JOSM]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JOSM")
[Merkaartor]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Merkaartor")
[Potlatch]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Potlatch")

Once you've edited up your map (and if you like, upload to openstreetmap so it benefits us all), you can then render your map using a few customizable tools:
[Mapnik]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Mapnik")
[Osmarender]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Osmarender")
[Kosmos]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Kosmos")

... or you could just upload your contribution to openstreetmap, and wait about 10 minutes, and you'll be able to see your contribution on the main map!  - where you can download it all.

It's also possible to make up an atlas style map, with grid and street names using openstreetmap data with [maposmatic]("http://www.maposmatic.org/").

I've probably given you an information overload, but there are plenty of map making tools available, all the ones I've listed are compatible with openstreetmap.  If you wish, it's possible to do all this without contributing to openstreetmap's database, but it'd be nice if you did :)

Let me know if there's any further help that you need.

### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-02-24
If you're familiar with GIS software, that sort of tech stuff, I'd recommend you focus your attention to JOSM for the map editor.
It's easy enough to learn (look at the screencasts for help), and is really excellent if you've got a reasonably big monitor.

I use JOSM for most of my map editing, and occasionally potlatch (which is openstreetmap.org 's flash based app that opens up when you press the edit tab on openstreetmap.org 's site (which you get once you've signed up and logged in).

It's all quite fun :)

### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-02-24
> **baddnady23 said:**
> ok real quick just to get me started, in JOSM when ever i go to download data from OSM, it says that there is no data imported and it just gives me a black workspace... any ideas?

There's a chance that there's nothing currently existing there in the OSM database for the area that you have selected.

To start out, I suggest you just download an area that's been already mapped, open it up, and have a play around - obviously you should only upload stuff that's actually there.

You need to select the area you wish to download from OSM with your left mouse button.  I've provided a brief animated gif that roughly demonstrates this.

Alternatively you can have a look at much better JOSM video tutorials listed on [this]("http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JOSM") page.
