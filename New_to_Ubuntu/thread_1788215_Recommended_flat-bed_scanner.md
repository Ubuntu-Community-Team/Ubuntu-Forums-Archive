### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-06-22
Is this for professional-level scanning or just for personal use?

If it's for personal use, you can't go past HP multifunction printers. They have flatbed scanners in them, they are plug 'n' play on Linux with official open-source drivers included in all major distros, and you can buy those machines for next-to-nothing. Example: I have a catalogue next to me for Australia Post shops, they're selling the HP 1050 multifunction printer for $39. That's a printer and scanner with a set of ink cartridges included.

For high-quality or commercial use you'd probably want a dedicated scanner or a more expensive MFP, but I can't really recommend something at this price range. Apparently Lexmark supports Linux, and HP does too.

### Post by sleepingdragon on 2011-06-22
HP scanners are the way to go. HP build some very nice Linux software and their hardware plays nicely. 

As mentioned, you can get a MTP (Multi Function Printer) with a sensible scanner for next-to-nothing, and they often go up to 1200dpi - more than enough for photo/hi-res scanning.

If you need the latest drivers for any HP hardware, just check out [http://hplipopensource.com]("http://hplipopensource.com"), and they provide sensible instructions for their latest driver installation.

Maverick might have problems with some newer HP hardware (v3.6.x), but Natty runs the newer HP drivers (v3.11.x).
