### Post by Shhnap on 2009-01-11
As far as i know port forwarding speeds things up only because your router does not have to search for open ports on all of its computers before sending the data out. I would reccomend keeping it on because it cannot hurt. If you are having a problem then i highly doubt it is due to port forwarding.

### Post by Joeb454 on 2009-01-11
What port forwarding would do is most likely increase your visibility to the peers/seeders, as any requests on the torrent port would get sent straight to your computer.

In the same respect I port forward port 80 & 8000 (amongst others) at home, 80 goes to my home server's webpage, and 8000 comes through to my desktop, which runs a small web server

### Post by mkvnmtr on 2009-01-11
I use Deluge and if you check it out it has an option to use random ports or just use one. If I just use one I must use port forwarding. If I choose random ports I find no difference in my speeds and my router does not block my torrent traffic. Either way I reach the max speed that my ISP allows if I have good seeders. I much prefer to use random ports. I had always used the port forwarding method before using Deluge.

I do a lot of torrents. From looking at the traffic in Firestarter what it means by random is every transmission is through a different port. Not that it chooses one port for today.
