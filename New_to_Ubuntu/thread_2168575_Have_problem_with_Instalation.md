### Post by verymadpip on 2013-08-19
Hi there.
First of all - don't do anything else!
Then: boot into windows & launch the disk management tool so we can see how many primary partitions are already on your disk drive.
A screenshot may even be helpful here.

I would also be tempted to install Neosmart's Easy BCD, just in case you decide to return fully to Windows further down the line.

I also think it would be helpful to know which version of Xubuntu (12.10, 13.04 or whatever; 32 or 64 bit etc) you are trying to install & the specifications of your machine - how much RAM, what CPU & graphics card, that sort of thing.
