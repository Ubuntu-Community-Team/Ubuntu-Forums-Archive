My thoughts too :D

To qualify, I do amateur photography and desktop and web publishing. GIMP and Scribus meet my needs just fine. Professionally, a decade ago, I helped friends in the graphic design field with publishing - today, I am able to do the same projects with open source software.

With advancements in Adobe Photoshop and page layout programs, may be cost effective to use them - with the publishing product you create. However, I would be leary of Adobe's *service-fee *model of renting their software - I personally do not see how it is worth it.

I would offer, that you should use open source software if possible, and contribute to those projects. [GIMP]("http://www.gimp.org/"), [Scribus]("http://scribus.net/canvas/Scribus"), and [Inkscape]("http://inkscape.org/") are a few programs that might be of interest to you :)
