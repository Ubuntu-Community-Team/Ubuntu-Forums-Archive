### Post by gobbles414 on 2008-08-09
The Ubuntu installation CD is also a LiveCD, which means that you can try Ubuntu on your laptop without installing anything to your hard drive! Ubuntu will pretend like the CD is your hard drive. This means that you can easily get a rather accurate idea of how well Ubuntu will work on your laptop.

Download the LiveCD from the Ubuntu homepage. If you're using DSL or cable internet, it won't take too long to download. Then, follow the directions found [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto") to transform the file into an actual CD. You MUST follow the directions! Otherwise, you're LiveCD might not be created correctly.

If your laptop works in the LiveCD, chances are excellent that it'll work the same or better once you install Ubuntu on your computer's hard drive. If something on your laptop doesn't work correctly in the LiveCD, post some details about the problem to this thread. Then, I and/or another Ubuntu Forums member will investigate.
