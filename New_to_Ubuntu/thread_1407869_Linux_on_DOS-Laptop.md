### Post by adam814 on 2010-02-15
Yeah, that one's even out of the specs of the usual "lightweight" linux distros like Puppy Linux or DSL.

BasicLinux might just be the way to go with it.  The only other alternative I could add is a-Linux.  It's a distro based on asm-utils (re-writing simple system binaries in assembly for performance and small size).  I haven't used it, so I don't know what limitations it has (I'm assuming there are quite a few), but it should be small enough to run well on hardware that old.

[http://asm.sourceforge.net/asmutils.html](http://asm.sourceforge.net/asmutils.html)
