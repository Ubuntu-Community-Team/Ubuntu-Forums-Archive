### Post by baruch60610 on 2009-01-18
Use a flatbed scanner.  They're relatively inexpensive, and they have good support with Sane or XSane.

As for copyright violations - if you're using this text exclusively for your own use, then there should be no problems.  If you share, you could face serious fines or worse.  But making a copy only for your personal use is considered "fair use".  You're allowed to make photocopies, for example, if you only wanted to carry a few pages instead of the whole book.  This isn't significantly different - AS LONG AS YOU DON'T SHARE.  Oh, yes.  IANAL - I Am Not A Lawyer.

You might check with the publisher to see whether they offer an online version of the textbook.  This is becoming more popular, though it's not catching on with textbooks as quickly as in other areas.
