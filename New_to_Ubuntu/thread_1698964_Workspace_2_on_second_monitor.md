### Post by Ben Page on 2011-03-03
No, at least not with available settings, maybe it is possible, but I'm not aware of it. "Workspace" is basically a "Desktop", so it's just a Gnome way to say multiple virtual desktops. Even if you plug in another monitor, your graphics card (and Ubuntu) will "clone" or "extend" your desktop onto other monitor. You are able to choose whether you want the same desktop to be shown on both monitors or do you want to extend your desktop onto both monitors in order to get more space. By changing the workspace, you'll change the desktop on both monitors to workspace 2/desktop 2 and thus getting the new, second desktop on both monitors.
