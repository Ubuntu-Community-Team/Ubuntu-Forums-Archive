### Post by northandersen on 2009-04-28
Figured it out this morning. 
 
Simple!
 
[LIST=1]
[*]Took a working computer.
[*]Unplugged the internal hard drives off the working computer.
[*]Took my laptop hard drive and hooked a usb adapter to it. (Ebay for $15, it will fit almost any drive)
[*]Plugged it into my usb port on the newer working computer. Now the computer only has the usb drive that is possible to load.
[*]Booted off the CD, wiped out the hard drive to completely new and installed Ubuntu to it.
[*]Before the working computer completely restarted I unplugged the usb drive (as it was rebooting).
[*]Put the laptop drive back in the computer.
[*]It started up and within a couple of minutes -BY ITSELF....found the Internet and started downloading updates!
[*]I started this process at about 7:45am and I walked to work and was there before 8:30. Laptop was working (updates were still happening, but I could actually surf the net and the basic applications were already loaded). Took like 25 minutes or so.
[/LIST] 
Impressive!
 
 
If Windows was that easy, probably would be out of a job! Will I be as impressed with the free software? I have spent several days trying to get Windows to work.:P :P :P
