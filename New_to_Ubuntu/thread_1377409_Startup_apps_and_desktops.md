### Post by barnex on 2010-01-10
It's under System>Preferences>Startup Applications.
You could also choose to "save" your session at logout, so that all open applications are restored when you log in, leaving, e.g., Evolution where you set it up last time.

### Post by mk1w86 on 2010-01-10
If you goto:

System>>Preferences>>Startup Applications

you can set applications to start at login.  You can also have Ubuntu save all your running applications when you shutdown.

### Post by mk1w86 on 2010-01-10
> Is logging off different to shutting down? That is, if Ubuntu remembers my settings after logging off, then will also remember my settings after shutting down and starting up?

Logging out is you logging out of your user account so another user can login to the system.  Shutting down is the steps your computer takes before it can power off.

According to the option in Startup Applications Ubuntu remembers your running programs when you logout.  If you are logged in and you shutdown your system it logs you out first then shuts down so your running applications will be remembered whether you logout or shutdown.

When you login again after you have logged out or you are logging in after turning on your computer, Ubuntu will start your applications.  

At least that is what I understand it to mean. :D
