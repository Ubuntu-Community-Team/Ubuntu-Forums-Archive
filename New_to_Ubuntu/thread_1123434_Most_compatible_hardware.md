### Post by Paqman on 2009-04-12
Anything with Intel gear in it should run everything out-of-the-box. Watch out for wireless, it's the most common problem with laptops. Avoid anything with a Broadcom chipset like the plague. The trouble is that retailers rarely advertise what wifi card their machine has in it.

### Post by jwaclawsky on 2009-04-12
I am running a Dell Insperion mini 9" netbook. It came with Ubuntu 8.04 already installed by Dell. I have a camera, Bluetooth, WiFi, USB hub with a USB keyboard and a USB mouse attached and an external hard drive and another hub attached to that hub with a RW DVD/CD drive attached. The net book also drives my 24" Dell monitor at 1920 x 1200. Everything works well. For what I am doing which is web browsing (including youtube), e-mail, papers and charts, I am happy. My only issue (which is very minor) is that it is a little underpowered when I open too many things. But overall I am very pleased.
