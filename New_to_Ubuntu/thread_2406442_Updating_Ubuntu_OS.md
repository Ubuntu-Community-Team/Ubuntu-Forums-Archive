### Post by QIII on 2018-11-21
It'll be supported, but all the software available and installed will remain at the same version except for security patches.  That is, it'll be supported, but the packages will get long in the tooth and you'd miss out on new stuff.

A long support period is most advantageous to those who run servers that must be stable over long periods to avoid the disruption caused by updates.

For a desktop user it's not the best way to go.

### Post by Autodave on 2018-11-21
+1 with both who replied to you already.  Keep in mind that 18.04 is a LTS release.  The next LTS will be 20.04.  I normally use just the LTS realeases.  If you choose to update to each new release every 6 months, then you will need to keep with that since the interim releases are only supported until the next one is made available.  On top of that, quite often upgrading from one release to the next often causes many problems which sometimes are not easily fixed.  My opinion: stay with the LTS releases and always do a clean install: don't upgrade.
