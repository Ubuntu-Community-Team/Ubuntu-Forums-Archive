> **jwalters said:**
> Question #2: How easy is it to get a virus when downloading a movie to watch while using Ubuntu? . . . and what do you do about it if you think you are having a problem. I was under the impression that Ubuntu was practically virus immune.......Yes? No?

Don't play movies by double-clicking on them. Always open the player you want to use and then play them.
On Windows, this method isn't 100% safe, since I think .wmv files run in media player are still able to execute code.
Not sure how safe it is on GNU/Linux.

Just avoid .wmv files and also use Firefox+noscript.

**GNU/Linux may be safer than Windows, but its safety still relies on the user ultimately.**
If you go on illegal sites and download illegal movies, the risk of getting infected/compromised is obviously higher.

[Hulu](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hulu) it's not illegal, but is only available in the US. 

> Hulu is a joint venture of NBC Universal (GE) and Fox Entertainment Group (News Corp), with funding by Providence Equity Partners, which made a US$100 million equity investment and holds a 10% stake.[2]

There are other legal services. The best way to avoid the illegality without knowing it is to stick with sites provided by the TV studios and channels.
