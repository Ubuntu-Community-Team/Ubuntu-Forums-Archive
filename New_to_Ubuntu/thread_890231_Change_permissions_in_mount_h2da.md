### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-15
Sound like they have a XP system that will not boot and they want to live boot a linux distro to copy their important files to another drive so as to save them. Well the permission message would be because you didn't mount the XP NTFS partition with ROOT priviledges, if you do that and then were to say plug in and mount a portable drive you should be able to copy any files that exist on the NTFS partition to the portable. The only thing that would change that is if the NTFS XP partition is damaged in anyway. Is it just your MBR on your windows hard drive that is messed up ? A little info on what happened to this and what you have done and everyone can give better advice.
