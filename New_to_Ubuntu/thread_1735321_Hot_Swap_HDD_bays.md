For example, I have several SATA bays, into which I can hot-swap any SATA drives; the machine does not need to be turned off.

### Post by Grenage on 2011-04-21
> **Arwen17evenstar said:**
> It looks like there's a bus in the back and the tray slides all the way out. Once you find the right kind of drive for your system, do you just screw it onto the tray and slide it back in?

That depends very much on the type of tray it is.  There are some caddy systems where you screw the drive into the caddy, and that slots into the bay.  There are others (like mine) where you simply open the bay door, slide in the disc, and close the door.  The former tend to be more secure if the PC is being moved around, the latter is not - it is however more convenient if you're swapping drives frequently.
