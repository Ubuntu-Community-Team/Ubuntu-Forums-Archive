### Post by Bölvaður on 2012-03-01
nonFree means it is proprietary, free would mean it is open source.
The word is taken from 'freedom'.

As an example: Adobe Flash is nonFree, as Adobe owns the code and doesn't want you to get it, but you can freely download it.

### Post by jerome1232 on 2012-03-01
from [http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/categories.html](http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/categories.html)

Nonfree software is any software that is not free. Its use, redistribution or modification is prohibited, or requires you to ask for permission, or is restricted so much that you effectively can't do it freely.


Essentially it means it's not free as in speech, you can't freely modify the code, redistribute etc... like you can with free open source code.
