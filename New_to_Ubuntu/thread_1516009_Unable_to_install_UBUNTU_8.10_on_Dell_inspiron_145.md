### Post by Paqman on 2010-06-23
Check the disk for defects. When you burn a LiveCD it's a good idea to burn it at the slowest possible speed. Unlike music and videos that have error correction built in, and tiny defect in software burnt to a CD can cause it to behave weirdly.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-06-23
Two things:

1. Don't bother with Ubuntu 8.10, it's very much out-of-date. The current version is 10.04, try that instead.
2. I/O Error means that it can't read the disc properly. Either your drive is bad or you've burnt the CD too fast or onto bad-quality media. Use good quality CDs and burn them at around 8x.
