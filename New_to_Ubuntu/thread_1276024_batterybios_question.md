### Post by mcduck on 2009-09-26
The BIOS itself is stored on a EEPROM or Flash chip, so bad battery won't affect it. And it definitely won't affect anything stored on your hard drive.

However all your BIOS settings will reset on boot, including date & time, boot order and all device configurations. Depending on your setup you might have to configure all these, and possibly also CPU & RAM settings on every boot..

Considering that a new battery really doesn't cost that much it would be a good idea to just replace it. Configuring BIOS settings on every boot will be a lot more work than visiting the nearest computer/electronics store and getting a new battery. :)
