### Post by Dynaflow on 2009-05-12
I have not been able to get the newest-firmware iTouch to even mount under straight Linux (see [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1150268") for my lamentations).  According to [this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1146558"), it is possible to run at least an older version of iTunes in Linux, but it apparently doesn't work all that great. 

I'm not sure what Apple is doing with their newest iPod'ish devices' firmware that effectively prevents them from being used on anything other than a Windows PC or a Mac, but until someone successfully reverse-engineers it, your best bet is to either jailbreak the thing, or just keep a Windows partition around or run Windows through a virtual machine, because I don't expect a Linux version of iTunes from Apple any time soon (Why should we expect them to help fix a serious barrier to the adoption of their primary competitor for Windows refugees?  It would make much more sense for them to maintain and augment that barrier.).
