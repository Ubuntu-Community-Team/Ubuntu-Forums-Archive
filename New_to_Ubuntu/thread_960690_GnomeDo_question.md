### Post by redseventyseven on 2008-10-27
System > Preferences > Sessions.

You can then add Gnome-Do to the list of programs that run at start-up.

(Note: That's the way it works in Linux Mint, anyway, which is Ubuntu-based. If those aren't the exact names then it'll be something similar. Also, Linux Mint has Gnome-Do included in the distro by default! I heartily recommend it - once you're used to it, it's practically indispensable. I have a Windows machine in my office and I keep hitting Windows+Space on it every time I want to check my email or open Firefox!)
