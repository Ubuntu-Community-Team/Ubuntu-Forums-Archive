### Post by northern lights on 2008-09-17

Burn on slow speed.

If it still fails try either the Alternate CD (tick the bottom left-hand box when downloading the .iso from ubuntu.com) or try it via [unetbootin]("lubi.sourceforge.net/unetbootin.html"). The latter would only work with another OS installed or a via a flashdrive, though.

### Post by ieduarte73 on 2008-09-17
Hey Misterhan,

That computer you've got is a fine piece of equipment, DELL XPS M1330, the problem is, like someone on this post said before, that new hardware sometimes has compatibility issues with LINUX, [www.distrowatch.com](www.distrowatch.com) is a good place to look for other distributions, however, I can tell you that Ubuntu is the one that worked with all my hardware (I tried OpenSUSE 11 before Ubuntu, and though It worked, Ubunto worked better).

I own a Dell Dimension XPS 710 (bought a year and a half ago) with 4GB of RAM, a NVidia 7900GS (512MB), 2X320 GB SATA HD, but, I had problems with the Audio Card (Sound Blaster X-Fi Xtreme Music), not supported by Linux, and the only way that I made it work was through OSS, and not through ALSA (Linux kinda Default Audio Manager).

Bottom Line, even though my computer was somewhat new, some things didn't work since the very beginning with Linux (OpenSUSE or Ubuntu indistinctly), however, I am very pleased with Ubuntu, which I have installed on the computers at home, and I use it every day at the office.

If you are willing to switch to linux, go ahead, you will be surprised of its capabilities.

By the way there's a new release of Ubuntu coming soon (October 31st),  maybe that will do the trick.
