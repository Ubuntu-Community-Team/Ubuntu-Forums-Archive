### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-10-05
***NEVER*** switch a machine off while you are upgrading. Machine's probably irretrievable (or it's going to take some work).  I wasn't aware you could upgrade from 11.04 to 12.04. Thought it had to be 10.04 or 11.10.

Anyway, I would say you're fairly stuffed. A fresh install of 12.04 LTS is quickest and easiest and your best bet. Unless someone pipes up with a miracle cure. ;)

### Post by NikTh on 2012-10-05
Fresh install is the best way , as [[COLOR=#DD4814]**Bucky Ball**[/COLOR]]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=504316") suggested. Create a Cd/Dvd of Ubuntu 12.04.1 and boot from this Cd/Dvd , if you want and if you can, copy any important data on a external HDD , do it . Then proceed with a fresh installation. 

The other option is to upgrade (again) from this LiveCd/Dvd you create. You will see the option to Upgrade. But  again , I bet on fresh install for better results.

### Post by Wim Sturkenboom on 2012-10-05
Slightly depending on your machine, but half an hour or so. Do not connect it to the internet as it might try to retrieve the latest versions of the packages during the install which will make it take far longer; personally I rather update to the latest packages afterwards.
