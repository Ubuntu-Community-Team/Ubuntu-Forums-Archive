Well Dumpy, you can choose to do whatever you want to but, in my opinion, your time could be more usefully spent. PCMCIA is older technology, you are going to be a bit limited by what you can find to purchase, so I'd think you should start with what you can find available. In addition, many of those older wireless chips now have modules included in the kernel and use of ndiswrapper is not required, so you don't need those windows drivers.  You might also want to look at this site to help identify compatable hardware.
[http://www.linux.org/hardware/](http://www.linux.org/hardware/)
