### Post by audiomick on 2013-01-07
I suspect that the machine is a couple of years old, and probably doesn't have the newest Ubuntu on it. You also indicate that you have backed up anything of interest on the machine. Given that, the easiest way might be to simply re-install with the current version. Perhaps 12.04, the current LTS (long term support) version.

However, since you say it is freezing, it would be worth booting into the live environment from an install CD or USB to see if that runs properly to exclude the possibility of a hardware fault.
