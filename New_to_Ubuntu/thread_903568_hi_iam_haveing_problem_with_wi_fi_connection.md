### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-29
Ok blake if it is a WEP or WPA networks in question that you know the key to use but cannot get your PC to connect to, is that right and if so which one WEP or WPA. Secondly I am then thinking that your wifi is working correctly when you try to connect to open non encrypted networks, is that correct?

If I am reading this correctly and it is just encrypted networks that you are having trouble with then I would say its 90% chance that you are just not getting the encryption key entered correctly, it can seem like you did it right and still not work as there are several settings for WEP or WPA. For example if it is a WEP protected network you could just right click on the network manager icon in the top menu bar and select "edit wireless networks" and once there on top of selecting WEP and entering the key you also need to specify one of 3 choices on how the key is going to be entered ( passphrase, hex & ascii ) so choosing the wrong type would cause this not to work even though you are using the right key. Also I would say double check with whoever at the cafe admins the wifi and verify that the key you know is the correct one as some people and businesses change these on a frequent basis for security, so verify that as well.

Please if you can't figure out what setting you need to change, then feel free to PM me with the details of what type of encryption and what type of key you are trying to use and I will see if I can help you figure it out.
