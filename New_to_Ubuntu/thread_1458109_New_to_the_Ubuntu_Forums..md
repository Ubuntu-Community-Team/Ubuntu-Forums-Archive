### Post by QIII on 2010-04-19
Welcome to the "free side" and Happy Ubuntuing!

There are no silly questions except those not asked.

A few notes for getting better answers:

Let us know the specs on your machine (although virtualization may make that moot.  Let everyone know you are running it in a virtual environment, at least)  Many people put their specs in their signature.

Let us know exactly what you were doing when you encounter a problem.  Take note of any error or warning messages.

Include any long messages returned from commands in code tags.  "[ code ]" to start and "[ /code ]" to end. (Remove the spaces.  I had to do that so the text would appear properly!)

Remember that we have become accustomed to saying "blah blah blah", which will probably seem foreign to you.  If we do, just say you don't understand.  We'll slap ourselves for forgetting that what seems straight-forward to us may not to you -- and we'll do a better job of explaining ourselves.

Also bear in mind that we are all here on our own time and voluntarily, so an answer may not come within 5 minutes of a post.  It may take a while for just the right person to see your post, and he or she may live in a different country and be asleep when you make your post.  Give it some time.  Generally, it is considered sporting to "bump" your question in about 24 hours, but that is not hard and fast.

Oh...

Take a look here.  There are a lot of good "getting started" books, and a sticky here on the forum, but this free .pdf is highly recommended:

[http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html](http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html)

### Post by QIII on 2010-04-19
Just to add an observation to slumbergod's post.  I don't think he is saying that a reference to some other thread or direction to a particular URL is necessarily a put-off.

Sometimes it is best to refer to something someone has already posted and done some good work on, rather than trying to reinvent the wheel.

But what you should expect people to do is say something like

"I found a good answer at this URL and it might work for you.  Let us know if it helps..."

That's helpful.  

"Google it for yourself!" is not.  Google's great when you know what you are asking for.  Not so good if you don't.

So don't take a reference to a URL or another thread on the forum to be, in and of itself, a brush off.

But do ignore the jerks.
