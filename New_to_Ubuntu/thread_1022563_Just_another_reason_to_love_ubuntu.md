### Post by lazyart on 2008-12-26
Found out my video card was going bad on me.  It being an old AGP card and I didnt really want to sink more money into old technology (and on top of this my motherboard got flaky on me as well), I put into service an Intel socket 775 board that someone gave to me recently.  I ran to CompUSA and picked up a dual core processor, some memory and a PCI Express video card.

I ripped out the AMD motherboard that was already there and obviously went with a whole new architecture.  Then I plugged in my optical drives and finally my hard drive and turned it on.

And it just worked.

No OS reinstallation hassle.  No registration/activation nonsense.  My virtual machines (running my webserver and mailserver) are just humming along.  Heh, and now I have 6 GB of memory for them to play in.
