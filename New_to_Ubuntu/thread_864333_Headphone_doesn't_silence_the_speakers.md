### Post by ConMan318 on 2008-07-19
Right-click your volume icon on the panel, and 'open volume control'.  Go to the 'switches' tab and see if 'headphone as line out' is checked.  If not, check it.

Not sure if that'll fix it, but I know I had to do that back in the day.

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2008-07-19
Ah, I did find that there's a PCM and a PCM-2.

If I change PCM-2, then it lowers or raises the volume of the headphones without affecting the main speakers.

However, if I control PCM, then it lowers or raises the volume of both the headphones and main speakers.

Thus, I can turn off the headphones and leave the main speakers on... But I can't turn off the main speakers and leave the headphones on. :confused:
