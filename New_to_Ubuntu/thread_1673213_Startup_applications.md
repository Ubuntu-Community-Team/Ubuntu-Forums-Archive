### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-01-22
I'd keep Power Management turned on. For a couple of Ubuntu releases, Gnome assumed that Power Management was running, and would freeze up for a minute when you clicked the Logout button if Power Management wasn't running.

I turn off the Accessibility one and Ubuntu One, but none of them use up very much memory or CPU time, so it's not really worth the meagre effort.

### Post by JKyleOKC on 2011-01-22
Another reason to keep Power Management is that the screensaver is tied into it rather closely, in order to power down the monitor after a half hour or so of inactivity...

I also keep the update notifier, so that I am warned when security updates arrive.
