### Post by buzzingrobot on 2016-02-17
Linux isn't infested with the malware and simple junk that seems to accompany a lot of add-ons for Windows. But, avoiding unnecessary extensions is a good idea.

That said, I recommend using a good ad blocker.  Sites typically do not sell or host the ads we see on their sites.  Instead, they contract with other businesses that sell the ads. (Ads on sites can come from multiple servers that the site itself does not control.) This scenario means a compromised ad server or a compromised human can use those ad networks to deliver malware to mlllions of people via their browsers,  Linux is not immune to all of that, especially phishing  and other attacks that trick a user. So, just block the ads.

Read the blurb Firefox and Chrome display about an extension, then take a look at the developer's page that is usually linked from there. I doubt I'd use anything that doesn't show signs of recent development work.
