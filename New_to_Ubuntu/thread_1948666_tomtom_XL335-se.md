### Post by Cheesehead on 2012-03-28
You need to go to [http://www.tomtom.com](http://www.tomtom.com) and install TomTom Home.

Unfortunately, TomTom Home does not have a Linux client, so the simple answer to your question is "You cannot do that because your device was made incompatible by the manufacturer." (that's why I don't buy TomTom)

According to [http://appdb.winehq.org/](http://appdb.winehq.org/) , TomTom Home does not seem to work with Wine. I just tried it, and that's certainly correct.

If you want to learn to instal a Virtual Machine and install Windows in it, you can (I do and it's easy). Or you can return the GPS to the store and exchange it for a linux-compatible GPS.
