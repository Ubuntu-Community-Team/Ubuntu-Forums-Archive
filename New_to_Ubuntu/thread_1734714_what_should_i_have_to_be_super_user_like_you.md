### Post by idoitprone on 2011-04-20
try arch, gentoo, or linux from scratch. I might recommend arch, since you dont have to compile. You are still force to learn how to tinker a system. The installer is simple, but not "easy" since everything is not automatic configured like ubuntu. Their wiki is sometimes just as good as Ubuntu. So you might like it or just get lazy and like ubuntu even more. Get used to the command line since pacman is command line only.

Edit: what marin123 and me are advocating is try other distro. Every distro does things differently, therefore has problems of their own. Learning how to deal with those problem will make you into a better superuser. Take off those training wheel and try to do new things. Sometimes the solution to your headaches will make you feel like an idoit and accidents are prone to happen. Go for it.

### Post by BertN45 on 2011-04-20
Nothing special, just solve the problems you detect. Promote Linux to the people you know and help them in solving their problems. Don't just copy shell commands from this type of forums and execute them like a monkey (unless you are in a hurry). Try to understand what caused the problem and why the solution (e.g. the set of shell commands) works.

Create a new challenge for yourself from time to time, when your study permits it. Use VirtualBox or KVM and use them to try other distros.

Superusers are superusers, because they were in the position, where they had to solve a lot of problems, remember some of them are professionals working full time in IT with Linux systems.

### Post by th1rt3en on 2011-04-29
I would recommend learning about BASH (BASH being your terminal) to get you started, that's where I started. I used this website: 
[http://linuxcommand.org/](http://linuxcommand.org/)

Honestly though, I learned most of what I know about BASH when I did my "only a terminal for a month" challenge. For an entire month I only used a terminal (the one you can access by hitting Ctrl+Alt+F1). When I started I only knew how to access the Internet with this code:

```
w3m google.com
```

(if it says you don't have w3m then):

```
sudo apt-get install w3m
```

Anyways, by the time I was done I had an email client set up, I knew how to use IRC, I checked my Facebook regularly, I could chat on Gmail IM, I could look at pictures, I could watch videos, I could listen to music, I could stream music, I could download videos off of youtube, I could convert media to different formats, and probably a couple of other things I'm forgetting. I would highly recommend taking that challenge.
