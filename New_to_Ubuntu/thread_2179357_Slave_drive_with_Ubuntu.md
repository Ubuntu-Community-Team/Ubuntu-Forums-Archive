### Post by DuckHook on 2013-10-07
Hi Stan, and welcome to the forums.

Linux enthusiast here. I am basically 99.9% Linux at this point after getting fed up with the MS mill years ago, but even I won't give up Win entirely. Example: Wife bought a gadget the other day with a configuration app that only runs on Windows. It's either fire up XP to configure the damn thing or tell Spousal Unit to take it back. Since Spousal Unit's alias is "She Who Must Not be Disobeyed", this is a surefire way to ensure that I "won't be gettin' no sugar tonight". So, I keep Win around in the interest of my sucrose fix. You may wish to as well. The key is to sandbox it in a virtual machine, pull the internet cord (in the virtual sense), and isolate it for all time from Evil.

With respect to question (1), it is impossible to predict how HW components will act when installed. We can tell you that things "should" work fine, or haven't been known to cause problems, but no one can predict future behaviour in the Linux-sphere any more so than in Windows. I can only say that most problems tend to get solved on these forums, that Ubuntu has turned into a very solid platform with reasonably wide component support, and that if your parts are mainstream and widely used with standard interfaces, that millions of users have installed such with little to no problems.

Re (2): If you choose the "Something Else" option during install, you will be offered the opportunity to partition on the go and fine control over where each and every element goes. However, it is probably better to run a LiveDVD/USB session first, partition your HDDs beforehand using gparted, and meticulously plan out your campaign like a penguin Napolean. Not only is this forward-thinking but it's a more fetching metaphor.
