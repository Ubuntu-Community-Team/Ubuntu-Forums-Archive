### Post by TheGuyWithTheFace on 2013-05-18
Well, I think everyone else has hit the nail on the head, but to surmise it:

1. Ubuntu and other GNU+Linux distributions are different from Windows. That's what makes it so refreshing, in my opinion. Ubuntu is a great one to start with though, because it's pretty darn user-friendly.

2. You can do just about anything on Ubuntu that you can on Windows. Gaming used to be an issue, but now we have Steam, and more and more games are being added. Other things, like photo editing, you can do, but with alternatives to Photoshop that can do just as much, but are different interfaces, and they may have a slight learning curve. With that in mind, if it's just a few simple photo tweaks, Ubuntu's built in program called Shotwell can do basic cropping, rotating, etc.

3. In my experience, if you want to do something with/to your system, if you are willing to experiment and get your hands dirty, there really aren't many limits to what you can do. (That being said, if you do that, make sure you have backups if you do that, I blew up my installation within my first week because I was trying to get a 3D cube for my desktops.) But as people said, if you don't like one interface, use another. If orange isn't your favorite highlight color, just change it, etc.

4. Yes, Ubuntu and GNU+Linux systems are in general really secure. Just be careful when you run something as root (like an administrator in windows) and you'll be fine.

5. Lastly, Ubuntu and other distros are free as in free beer, and free as in freedom, so have fun and play around with it! We're here to help if you have questions, and remember you don't have to commit to anything if you don't want to. Good luck and have fun!
