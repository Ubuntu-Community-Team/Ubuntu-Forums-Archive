### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-10-19
What we get appearing in the left side of the top panel changes according to the application window that we are working in (has focus).

The aim is to reduce the amount of screen space that applications windows take up by putting the application menus into an area of the screen that was under used, or used for system menus that could be put elsewhere.

Regards.

P.S. For completeness - the Panel on the left side is called the Launcher. And the panel that appears when you click the Ubuntu icon on top of the Launcher is called the Dash.

### Post by deadflowr on 2012-10-19
Like howefield, I'm not sure you can lock just the menu to the panel, without the Title bar. 
However you can disable global menu:

[http://lifehacker.com/5887462/how-to-disable-ubuntus-annoying-global-menu-bar]("http://lifehacker.com/5887462/how-to-disable-ubuntus-annoying-global-menu-bar")

The advantage of disabling it is you'll get your old menu system back(menus will appear as they'd always have, on the open window).

The disadvantage is you lose the HUD function.
