### Post by TheFu on 2018-10-12
Nobody here works for Canonical, so we cannot provide the answer you seek, but many of us are or have been professional software developers.

Bugs first have to be accepted as bugs.  This usually takes a clear step-by-step way to reproduce the issue by someone other than the person reporting it.  Until that happens, I wouldn't assume the bug has anyone working on it.

When bugs come in, they are prioritized.  How that happens depends on the specific bug and whether a developer knows exactly how to fix it already or if there are 1,000 complaints about it or 1.  It sorta works like how the power company fixes power outages based on the number of people that would get power returned by any fix.  Of course, if there is someone on a respirator at home, their power line and phone lines should be prioritized higher than most other residential locations, but not as high as hospitals or police or fire stations.

Clear as mud?

I've stayed on 16.04 and plan to remain there for a while longer. At least until the issues with 18.04 in these forums are reduced greatly.  I don't load non-LTS releases, so 18.10 isn't an option.  6 months of support just isn't sufficient for my needs.
