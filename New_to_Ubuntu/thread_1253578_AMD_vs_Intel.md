### Post by cascade9 on 2009-08-30
> **ddg said:**

Any recommendation which is better? Of course the intel mainboard can not be used with AMD processor.

Generally- Asus and Gigabyte make decent motherboards. Intel does as well, but you normally pay extra for that Intel sticker, and they have less BIOS options than the others. ASrock was originally made to be a more 'budget' version of Asus, I've never been that impressed with ASrock myself. 

There should be 0 problems running on the newest AMD ATI chipsets (dont worry, the ATI chipsets dont have the problems that the video cards do). They have been around for a while, the newest releases are older versions with support for newer DDR3 ram and the AM3 socket. 

Since your planning on running a seperate video card, have a look at the Asus M4A 78 AM2+ or Gigabyte GA-MA 770T-UD3P. I would probably go for the Gigabyte, its DDR3, a tiny bit more expensive on ram but probably worth it, and AM3. Make sure its a 770-T-UD3P, the 770-UD3 is DDR2 (confusing of gigabyte, but they do that). While AM2+ motehrboards do support AM3 (you can drop an AM3 chip into an AM2+ motherboard) its always nice to have the newer socket for future upgrades, etc.

### Post by Paqman on 2009-08-30
> **NoaHall said:**
> Nope, mostly motherboards will run fine under Linux.

I'd disagree strongly with that. Pick your motherboard with caution.

The best thing to do is search for the exact board model on these forums. If you strike a ton of threads full of people trying to get that model to work, run a mile.

Generally speaking, Intel boards work extremely well, but most are pretty pedestrian. Don't expect lots of options in BIOS for things like overclocking. Gigabyte also do well, as a lot of their boards are Intel anyway. They tend to be better for messing about with though.

CPU-wise, some people favour one or the other, but I don't think it makes any difference. Get either AMD or Intel, the main deciding factor wil be price and the suitability of matching mobos.
