### Post by epswinde on 2008-11-20
Are you typing on a laptop?

Because in ubuntu, middle click is another way to copy/paste text, and it will past whatever you last highlighted with the cursor.  This happens to me (really annoying) when i'm typing because the heel of my left hand finds its way to the touchpad, and the system recognizes it as a two-fingered touch, which is interpreted as a middle click.

in terms of disabling this behavior, you can install gsynaptics (the touchpad config. tool in gnome -- look in synaptic package manager) and alter that behavior -- that is, a two fingered tap is no longer recognized as middle click.  kind of an ungraceful workaround, but at least you won't have random copy/paste events anymore.

If you're not typing on a laptop, I have no idea what could be causing this behavior.

There's also a how to in this forum [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=271052").  Much more elegant than my previous suggestion.  Also, there's a program called touchfreeze that does much of the same thing.
