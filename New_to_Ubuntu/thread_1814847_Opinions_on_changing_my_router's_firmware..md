### Post by sadaruwan12 on 2011-07-30
My dear friend I've been working with router for a long time as a Network guy so in my experience if the new firmware doesn't bring any thing new to the table don't do it 'cos it might not brick your router but have the tendency to break some thing else in the router.

For a example I was the help center manager for a PROLiNK router agent in my country we got a new firmware for our Wi-Fi router which was a top notch product in our range it had good coverage to but for testing we only upgraded one thank god for that as soon as it finished the upload we noticed a very bad drop in Wi-Fi signal around 40% then we managed to downgrade.

After informing the dev's at our mother company they found a bug in the firmware which caused the signal drop. So be cautious when you do firmware upgrades if there's nothing new don't do it.
