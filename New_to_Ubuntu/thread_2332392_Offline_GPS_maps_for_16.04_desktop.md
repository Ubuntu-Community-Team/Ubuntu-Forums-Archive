### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-07-31
The snap store has something called jtiledownloader. It is described as: "download OSM maps." I have not installed this snap application. So, I have no idea of what we get or how it works or what we do with the maps once they are downloaded.

If you have a Ubuntu phone or tablet you can install UNav.

[http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/UNav](http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/UNav)

I doubt if it is installable in 16.04. Not until the Unity 8 desktop session has matured into a proper desktop user interface and Unav is converted to a snap. Perhaps you could make a suggestion to one of the UNav developers

[http://ubuntunext.com/2016/04/28/unav-0-59-now-available-for-ubuntu-touch-owners](http://ubuntunext.com/2016/04/28/unav-0-59-now-available-for-ubuntu-touch-owners)

The 16.04 Ubuntu Software store has Maps (Gnome Maps). It is the 3.18 version

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2016/02/can-now-edit-openstreetmap-info-gnome-maps](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2016/02/can-now-edit-openstreetmap-info-gnome-maps)

[https://wiki.gnome.org/Apps/Maps](https://wiki.gnome.org/Apps/Maps)

Whether it does what you want. Who knows?
