### Post by AoSteve on 2009-11-20
This is coming from an aspiring NA.  I've completed the CCNA courses and I'm ready for the certification.   I know what you're saying every bit.  Here's the facts.

First, DSL and it's different layouts can always be better then cable for one simple fact.   The amount of users on a DSL network is hardly ever going to be as high as cable can reach.   There's spots in big cities where there are thousands of people sharing the same cable hub.   However, DSL doesn't usually have a quarter of that number who can even get service on that particular hub.   

DSL can be the wise choice when living in a heavily populated area, such as downtown New York City.   

Other times, cable is the best option.   The average speed as of last year for a cable hub was estimated at 1GB/s total throughput.  This isn't your possible speed, but the full speed of the hub.   Each person on the network hub takes a small percentage for the stuff they want.   When the overhead reaches a mark, QoS (Quality of Service) is initiated.   This simply means, the hub will then prioritize X amount of bandwidth to each client system.   So, the more people on your local hub, the slower it will be.  I have a friend who lives pretty close to D.C. and lives in a heavily populated area.  His old Comcast system usually ran at about 400kb/s actual speeds at 3AM in the morning.   However, 6PM at night, dialup was usually a better option.

FiOS.   I've only had the chance to use it once.   If you could imagine a T1 connection without split connections, it's similar.  However, Lance, who has the 20mb Fios connection hasn't had anything but troubles.   Constant downtime since he first had it installed.  However, over the last two months he's said it's been getting pretty decent.   He was an ADSL user with Verizon for two years previous from the FiOS install.

From my contacts and real life experiences, FiOS is the fastest alternative.   However, Cable seems to be the best if you're in a rural area like myself.  My cable connection (at the house I'm moving into) runs around 1.5mb/s true speed.   It's a comcast "22mb speed boost" package.  But it's only $60/m.    DSL is great when you're living in a heavily populated area though.   I'm not exactly sure on the FiOS and how it's networking works.  I'm pretty sure it'd be more typical of traits with DSL as cable can travel for LONG distance and DSL variants cannot.   FiOS (I believe) has to be on OC lines wherever it's running.  So if you don't have Fiber to the house, you don't get it.

I'd try the FiOS if I had the option.  It may do you a lot of justice!
