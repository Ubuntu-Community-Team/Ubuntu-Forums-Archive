### Post by gweaver on 2009-03-13
I have a K510i, and can confirm that it does work with Ubuntu without having to install any drivers.

Unfortunately my bluetooth adapter doesn't currently work, due to a regression in the kernel (patch/fix should be in Jaunty). I can, however, still connect with the USB cable (grey one that comes with the phone).

I have just been able to connect to the internet with my K510i and Vodafone PAYG using the NetworkManager applet (in the notification area). It is slow though - I guess the phone isn't 3G capable.

I'm also able to access the calendar, call history, contacts, messages and todo list using Wammu (install through add/remove programs). It's ugly but it works. I've attached a settings screenshot, though you might find Wammu can autoconfigure your phone.

To sync with Evolution (but not Thunderbird, unfortunately) you can use Multisync. I can't remember how, but I'm sure there are instructions elsewhere on the forums. Be careful, I suggest a one way sync from the phone to computer here, until/unless you have an address book backup.

For greater things you will probably have to wait for opensync, [conduit]("http://www.conduit-project.org/") and the like to mature.

I hope this is of help?
