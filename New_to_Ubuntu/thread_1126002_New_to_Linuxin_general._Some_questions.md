### Post by TekMuzik on 2009-04-15
It really is personal preference on this topic since there is so many different choices, but just thought I'd give you my opinion since you asked ;)

For Movies I just use the Totem Movie player that (I believe) comes preinstalled on Ubuntu. You just have to do a little work to download the codecs to to get it fully working. There's guides all over the Internet and this forum to do that, and it's pretty simple. I also keep a copy of VLC around in case I get something I can't seem to get to play in Totem.

For burning I use Brasero which comes preinstalled on Ubuntu. Ubuntu also has a cool feature where if you insert a black disc, it will open up a black folder for that disc that you can drag and drop files in to, and then burn at the end. I've used that to make a few data DVDs.

For music and Ipod, I love Amarok. I wouldn't even think about using anything else. I don't have an Ipod, so I'm not sure about syncing it, but it should work fine. It has a devices tab, and when I connected my phone it showed up automatically on there and I had no problem syncing my music, so I assume it would be just as easy for Ipod.

For torrents, I just use Transmission which comes preinstalled. It's lightweight, stable, and has every thing you need for a simple Torrent program. It doesn't have a lot of big fancy features, but it you just need simple downloading, it works great.

For general knowledge, just read some of this stickied guides on this forum, and browse through other peoples problems and you can learn a lot. If you ever have any problems, do a search on the forum and you can probably find your answer. If you can't find your answer post a new topic here and we'll be glad to help. 

Welcome to Ubuntu and have fun. :)
