### Post by bennachie on 2011-05-16
Lots of printers and/or scanners can be persuaded to work with recent Linux distributions, and Ubuntu/Mint does a better job than most of reducing the need for manual intervention. Fedora, openSUSE and Mageia are getting close, though.

In some cases, ironically, the best route is to use HP software to access the HP-compatible modes under which most laser printers, and many inkjets, can operate. The relevant packages are HPLIP or, if you only want basic printing services, HPIJS.
