### Post by fluxlizard on 2009-03-20
red eye is caused by your camera's flash- stop using it.

Try taking your photo in better lighting, disable your flash.

Most of the time even in poor lighting if I take the picture in the gimp and go to color-levels- and click on the auto button, it will magically fix poor lighting in 90% of my pictures. Even extreme dark amber pics it usually fixes so they look normal and it always amazes me after years of using this feature. So I've found no need for a flash in most situations.

The other thing- red eye removal can be re-applied in the gimp over and over again on the same spot, and you can adjust the intensity.

 You can also increase the magnification of your photo and go in and use the color select eye dropper and the pencil to repair anything pixel by pixel not removed by red eye removal by hand without much difficulty. Eyes aren't usually many pixels unless you are very close up.

But bottom line- red eye is *always* caused by a bad flash on your camera. And nearly all cameras come stock with a bad flash- some however are worse than others. Sounds like yours is a bad one- don't feel bad- I've got a $600 camera and it is bad for red eye in many situations too. So I just don't use the flash.
