### Post by Tatty on 2008-07-09
Well, being a ubuntu forum I suspect most people here will probably recommend Ubuntu :popcorn:

The great thing about linux is that you are not restricted to one distro, Its pretty easy to switch between them, so why not just try a few and see what you like?

Ubuntu is definately good for a beginner due to its vast community support, particularly on these forums.


I would also recommend not starting to use linux with the aim of getting rid of windows as soon as you can.  A lot of people (including myself) are keen to do this when they first start using linux, but in reality it will probably take you a little while of getting used to linux, before you can do everything you need without hassle and can really drop windows.

In the meantime its best to dual-boot, so you can always boot up windows if you are struggling with something in linux :)

### Post by protogenesis on 2008-07-09
One thing that holds back many Winder$ users from Linux is the overly-popular familiarty with the Windows GUI coupled with the prejudice that Windows is superior due to its simplicity.  Linux *is* different, yes, and it takes some getting used to, just like switching from a Mac to PC or vice-versa takes some adjustment.  However, unlike those two, Linux offers you unbridled power.  It's the difference between a Subaru, Volvo and the NCC-1701-D Enterprise.  

I strongly encourage you to explore Linux, all of it's GUI goodness, familiarize yourself with the programs and how things run and once you feel that this is a good fit, go for the install.  I am a fan of KDE versus Gnome, but with all of the windows managers out there, you'll find one that gives you a warm fuzzy.  Spend time reading the beginner's links and guides and soon you'll be doing what you normally do in Windows, only better!

My wish list for Linux, and this is a *huge* wish as it'll take a while, would be a way for all PC-based software to run under Linux natively.  A while ago in Babbages, they sold off-the-shelf Linux games.  I remember being *given* a commercial copy of Railroad Tycoon II for Linux (the manager wanted to promote the games - he was so excited!) I took it home and played it for hours.  Sure, I had it for the PC and played the crap out of it, but to now play it in Linux was just...well, I was ecstatic I didn't have to reboot to enjoy one OS for its beauty and the other for its games.  Workarounds do exist, but still it would be nice to get past the "This won't run on Linux" issue.  

Good luck with it all and welcome to Linux!
