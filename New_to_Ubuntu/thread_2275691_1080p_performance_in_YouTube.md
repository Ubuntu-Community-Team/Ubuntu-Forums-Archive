### Post by mattharris4 on 2015-04-28
Pool Man, I cannot claim that the new version of Ubuntu Gnome definitively is or is not the problem but 1080p video takes up a lot of bandwidth -- let's just put it this way, I can only go up to 480p comfortably on a 6Mbps down connection, a 720p video will play at times but if there is any blip in the connection at all the video skips.  I would guess 1080p would require at least 12Mbps, maybe more to reliably run.  I would check the speed of your internet connection first before attempting to hunt down an issue with the OS.

As for purchasing a pre-loaded computer, System 76 is one provider, Linux City ([www.linucity.com](www.linucity.com)) is another, they have more selection and are a bit cheaper.  I cannot vouch for either one as I haven't used their computers.  The cheapest advertised desktop from System 76 is $659, from Linux City $389 (only considering full-size computers). I am sure there are other sellers out there, these are just two that I know of.  

Whatever computer you buy from a Linux computer retailer, make sure it comes with the 14.04 LTS version of Ubuntu (or whatever Canonical distro you choose), just make sure it is 14.04, the latest version with all updates is 14.04.2 but updates will load once you set it up after telling it to via a pop-up if you get 14.04 or 14.04.1).  Do not go for 14.10 or 15.04, you don't need the headache of bugs that comes with a non-LTS version -- especially 15.04 which people seem to be complaining about left and right here on Ubuntu Forums (granted, it just came out earlier this week).
