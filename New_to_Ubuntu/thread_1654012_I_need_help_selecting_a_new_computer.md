### Post by techningeer on 2010-12-27
R.A., I would highly recomend that you buy a used but in very good condition IBM T23 Laptop. I have used one with Ubuntu for many, many years. I have had Ubuntu on it since 5.04-10.04 and have had less problems with it than with  many newer computers. There are no hardware driver issues, and you can get up to 1 GB of RAM. Combined with a 2-speed Intel Pentium 3 Mobile processor, it has a reasonable, if not very good operating speed, and can play all types of multimedia: DvDs, CDs (audio), MP3, Flash, etc. It can also edit audio and video very well. If you are planning to compile software on it, I would get a Desktop PC, not a laptop, as they can overheat. God Bless!

### Post by msandoy on 2010-12-27
Since you are going for a rig to last for many years, I would suggest a bigger PSU (650W should do nicely), so it will not wear out so quickly, a dedicated GFX card, like a nVidia gt210 as mentioned earlier, and if you have sound issues, spend a little extra on a non standard heatsink and a quiet fan. It really does wonders. And your system runs at a lower temp, which in turn helps it last longer. 
For maintenance, you should replace thermal paste once every 12-18 months or so, to maximize heat transfer and keeping the system cool.
