### Post by thulefoth on 2010-02-07
Hi t1nm@n,

Linux of course has massive programming resources, Open Source & free.

Some of it will already be on your Ubuntu.

To see what you already have installed, go System > Administrator > Synaptic Program Manager.  In SPM, look at the categories-list on the left, and pick Programming.  Look down through the results-list (it's a lot), concentrating on titles that begin with GC (GNU C, C++).  The ones already on your box are check-marked.

You say that you are interested in C++, which is the primary language these days.  If you know other particulars about how & why you have decided to learn programming, those will help to refine how you go about achieving your goals.

If you are interested in games, that's one thing.  If systems-work has caught your eye, that's another.  Science, music, web-apps ... different areas have different needs, and if there is a known specific interest, it will pay to let pre-existing resources for that field guide & channel your efforts.

You will want an IDE, Integrated Development Environment (search SPM using IDE).  A 'pretty face' for the programming program.  There are a number of them.  Search for them, read up some about them, and let your interests & experience be your guide in selecting one (or more..).

If you are very clueless about the nature & practice of programming, in the broader sense, then it may be well to give a bit of time & effort to this phase of your undertaking.  There are choices other than C++ that would serve better, for 'cluing up'.

Tell us more about what has prompted you to pursue programming, and we can make specific comments that will help get you to where you want to be, easier & faster.

Programming is great!

Ted
