Unless you are extremely hard-pressed for space on your computer, I would definitely leave most of those applications on there.

The gnome-terminal, archive manager, and TSC are very important programs that are used to help service the machine, and if they arent available the only way to fix the computer is to reinstall. 

You could 'hide' the applications from the menus, simply by going to System>Preferences>Main Menu and unchecking the entries.

Also, despite the fact that the computer may be used for inline transactions only, you must remember to keep the computer up to date, particularly if you are surfing the web with firefox. 




My answer would be, keep em but hide the icons, and make sure your computer is up to date.
