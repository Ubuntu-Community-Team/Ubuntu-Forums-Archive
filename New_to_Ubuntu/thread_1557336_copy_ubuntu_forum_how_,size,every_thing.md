Not wishing to dampen your enthusiasm, but the forums are mirrored accross the world on many servers, with many different sub-addresses. 
You will find it far less painful to just put in a link to your area, as a lot of posted replies also refer over to [Help Area](https://help.ubuntu.com/) which are also continually being updated. There is also the small matter of 'fair-usage' and if you put un-needed loading onto the servers to pull everything in, you may find your self not very popular.

One of many really good links to put on would be the link the most recent release of the [10.04 Manual edition 2](http://ubuntu-manual.org/download/10.04e2/en_US/screen) If you actually download that file and host it yourself it would take strain off the servers, much in the same way that the ubuntu project asks people to use torrent downloading as opposed to causing un-needed load on the servers.
If you'd like more information, please take a look at [Getting Involved](http://www.ubuntu.com/community/get-involved) where you will find teams who will welcome you with open arms for spreading the word of Ubuntu.
