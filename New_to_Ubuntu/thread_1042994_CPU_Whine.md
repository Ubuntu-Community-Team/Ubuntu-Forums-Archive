### Post by mcduck on 2009-01-18
Sounds like the normal case of motherboard components creating a high-pitched whine. Most commonly comes from CPU power feed components when the CPU is idle or running at low frequency (and thus using less power).

So the sound definitely doesn't come from CPU itself, and is not a sign of any hardware compatibility issue. Just a normal feature of electronic components. The coils are usually covered with a layer of lacquer to reduce the sound, but motherboard manufacturers don't always do that great of a job with the lacquer and you end with a whining motherboard..

You can get rid of it if you take the motherboard to some electronics workshop, they'll spray the coils with a new layer of lacquer.

edit: Disabling CPU power saving and configuring your system to always run the CPU at full speed would most likely also stop (or reduce) the sound.
