### Post by SeijiSensei on 2014-04-14
You can delete the icon by right-clicking on it and either moving it to trash or deleting it with Shift+Delete.  

If you want to remove kmail entirely from the system, use "sudo apt-get remove kmail" or remove it using a GUI software manager.  I checked to make sure it wouldn't delete any needed libraries, and it appears you can remove just kmail itself.
