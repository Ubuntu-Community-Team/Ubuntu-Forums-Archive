### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-08-20
Not being facetious, but Linux is not Windows, meaning, that unlike with MS Windows, you really don't need to install any drivers.  Ubuntu does that automatically for you when you install the OS.

If you have something that is not working, then mention that specifically, and perhaps we can help regarding the drivers.

But, if everything is working, there is nothing for you to install.

Also, before you go updating your video drivers, post here what video card/chipset you have -- because ATI recently dropped support for lots of cards, and if you go forcing an install of their current driver, you will lose video on your system.
