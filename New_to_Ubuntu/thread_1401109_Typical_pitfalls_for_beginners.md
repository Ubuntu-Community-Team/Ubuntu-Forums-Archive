### Post by audiomick on 2010-02-07
My advice to beginners: "Think about it first" i.e: really read what the screen is telling you, think about what you are doing, observe what happens, don't assume too much.

### Post by houseworkshy on 2010-02-07
A simple little thing.  When one first installs ubuntu the software drivers are not working.  So, following some guide, one uses the hardware drivers in Admin to add them.  You are then asked to reboot the system but as the hardware drivers are not yet enabled the only gui link to restart is out of the display.  So mentioning the terminal and "sudo shutdown -r now" is very handy.  Also on drivers. It is a good idea to search the forums first for posts on hardware as, naturally when a newcomer gets Ubuntu they will tend to get the latest stable version. Often the drivers are not up to date yet and the fiddling around to make it all work is often beyond newbie level. 
I think that the current state of hardware compatabilty should be very prominant on the download page.  Judgeing by the forums, many newcomers are put off by graphic drivers which are not yet quite ready and cannon printers.  For someone who has never used Ubuntu before a new stable release is often not such a good choice as one which has been around for long enough to be "out of the box".

I've just been informed that the thing about display is my own personal bug. It shouldn't happen.  I'll not cut the post though just in case someone else encounters it.

### Post by Chris Edgell on 2010-02-07
Of all the tools I've learned to use, I learned this best one from Sir Jasper.

Get to the Google search page, type in: "How to google tutorial" and hit "I feel lucky".

You can learn so much, if you open your mind to this tip.  I came to learn it one day when I came to the forum to ask, "What # cartridge ink do I get for my XXXXHP printer?  Oh, I got a few remarks, a couple being: Google it!  I said, "I did google it but can't plow through all the pages and pages of stuff."

With the "I feel lucky" option, if you type your "query" clearly, when you hit "I feel lucky" it will often provide you with the ONE BEST answer: like with my cartridge query, I got a chart that said, You use #60.  

This option cuts through a world of stuff.  If the machine intuitively offers you the completion of your query, continue to type out: it don't click it.  If you click it it will take you there and you'll never get the chance to click "I feel lucky".

I wish someone would say, "I think everyone knew that BUT you" or PM me and say you love it too.

When you get that 2-page tutorial, down toward the bottom you'll find a "cheat sheet" and it cuts through LOTS of stuff.  I put that on my desktop.  (This is like codes for queries.  Like the code for any phone number in any town, or government offices in any town..things like that that you may have tried and tried to get and then just gave up.)

Someone else said one day, "It's a shame that Google provides more and better service than Ubuntu searches.  But I say it's great.  I go there to Google first, often at the end of my question I type Ubuntu and I get a Ubuntu page.  

I just LOVED hearing about this; all the sites and tutorials are great for study but if you are searching for one specific answer right now.....DO THIS!

### Post by pdlethbridge on 2010-02-08
I'm also a nobie .I've been at this Ubuntu for about 4-5 years, and I'm still learning stuff each day. It's not as easy as windows to use but it is way far better.  When upgrading, don't be afraid to make mistakes. That's where you learn stuff. Also, if a upgrade goes bad and you can't fix it no matter how had you try, go back to what was working before. An example, for the life of me I have been unable to get 9.10 to work properly. So I stick with the 9.04. It's a solid system and everything works. Period.
  When you do an install, Try to create 4 partitions for your Ubuntu install. On my computer I have 15 gigs for / where the operating system goes. 40 gigs for my /home partition, a 20 gig fat 32 partition that is a copy of home and about 2 gigs for a swap partition.
And finally, it's okay to clear out the dust bunnies in your dirty computer with a leave blower.
