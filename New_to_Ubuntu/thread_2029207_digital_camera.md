### Post by lhb1142 on 2012-07-19
I don't use *any* program to copy photos from my cameras to my computer.

I merely insert the SD card into the computer's SD slot (or I can use an SD reader which plugs into a USB port). The card opens and I see the DCIM folder.

Within that DCIM folder are the individual folders containing my photo files. I **cut** and **paste** all of these **folders** into my Pictures folder (thus removing them from the SD card but leaving the main DCIM folder, now empty, on the card). I rename these folders (if desired), and then rename the individual photos (if desired) contained within each of the folders.

Cutting and pasting the folders is better for the card's longevity than erasing pictures or formatting the card too often.

To view them, I generally use Shotwell; Ristretto is also a good option. Gwenview is good too but a bit slow in loading.

Shotwell allows simple adjustments of the photos; for more complex adjustments, I use the GIMP.

I strongly recommend backing up your pictures to an external hard drive, maybe even more than one (or a DVD).

I hope this helps you.
