### Post by papibe on 2011-09-05
I've done that. This is how I accomplished it:

You can use the headphone output to transfer the audio to your computer. You'll need a cable like [this]("http://www.radioshack.com/product/index.jsp?productId=11300827"), or [this]("http://www.ramelectronics.net/ipod-mp3/ipod-cables/ipod-extreme-series/stereo-3-5mm-mini-plug-cable-ifepmm/prodIFEPMM.html") (sometimes called mini plug). Connect the other end to your computer's 'line in'. That's all you need in terms of hardware and connections.

As for recording software, there are several options. I would recommend Audacity (it's on the Software Center). In order to obtain the best quality possible, do several test with the volume levels: cassette player, input, and recording level. A good start would be setting the cassette player at around 75% (loud enough but not full volume)

I hope this helps,
Regards.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-09-05
With a sound card or motherboard audio, "line in" is the sound going into the computer or other device like an amplifier. And "line out" is the sound coming out which would be used for play back through headphones or into an amplifier and then on to the speakers. Not to be confused with "in line," "on line" and "line up", which are different things.

Regards.
