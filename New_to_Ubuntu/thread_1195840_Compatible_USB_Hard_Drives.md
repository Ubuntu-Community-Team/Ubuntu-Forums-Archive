### Post by mikechant on 2009-06-24
Something you might want to think about is were you're going to put it. If you have a restricted space next to your PC (like I do) you may need a drive that can stand vertically instead of lying flat. Mine just came with a plastic clip-on base to do this.
Also, do you want a lower capacity more portable drive - I think some of the smaller 2.5" based models can power off of the USB port - or a higher capacity less portable drive, usually 3.5" drive based model, which will require an seperate power supply?

Obviously it's not worth getting one for the fancy backup software etc. that some include because that typically will be for Windows only or Windows/Mac.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-06-24
All USB storage devices will work as storage devices with Linux, Windows, Mac OS 8.5, Mac OS X, Syllable, BSD etc. They use a communication standard known as USB Mass Storage or UMS.

You can buy any external hard disk / flash drive / drive enclosure with confidence because of UMS.
