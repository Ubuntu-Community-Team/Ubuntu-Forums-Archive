Ubuntu 12.04 is a LTS (Long Term Support) version it means Ubuntu will provide technical support and updates up to 5 years since its release. 12.04 was released in April 2012, so the support will be available until April 2017. However, 13.04 is a normal release which has about 9 months of support and its supports already expired in January 2014.

This being said, you should upgrade your system to 14.04 LTS which will have Ubuntu's support and updates until April 2019.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-12-05
In order to keep the development of Ubuntu moving forward a new version of Ubuntu is released every six months. Either towards the end of April or towards the end of October. So, Ubuntu 13.04 was released April 2013. Whereas, Ubuntu 14.04 was released April 2014. 

Every 2 years the April release of Ubuntu is designated Long Term Support (LTS) with a support period of 5 years. The versions released in between only have 9 months support because it requires a lot of time and effort often done by volunteers to support each version of Ubuntu. So, these Standard or Interim releases only have a limited support period to reduce the amount of work for Ubuntu maintainers.

When a version of Ubuntu reaches End of Life the software repositories are closed. That is why you are unable to install software. But you have other issues. You say that you have a 32 bit system. Is this 32 bit Ubuntu? Or do you mean the computer CPU is 32 bit? It helps us to help you if you tell us the details about the computer hardware and please include information about the video/graphic adapter, especially the amount of memory the graphic adapter has.
