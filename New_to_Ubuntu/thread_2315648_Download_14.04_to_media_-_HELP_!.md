### Post by X-RED_Tech on 2016-03-01
There aren't many alternatives to physical mediums. Either DVD or USB should be fine provided the computer can boot from one or the other. If it cannot boot from a USB flash drive there's still a workaround called Plop. It's also an ISO you burn to a CD, boot from it with the USB connected and then select USB or something from the Plop menu. It'll then use the USB drive as any other computer that supports it natively.

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-03-01
Bewildered Bob,

1).   Are you sure your PC is capable of running Ubuntu?    Ubuntu, and many other distros (distributions of linux) use modern graphics features that may not work on older PC's.    A really great alternative is Ubuntu Mate, which just released the "Mutiny" desktop -_ which closely resembles Ubuntu Unity_ but runs fast on older computers. 

2).   Here is a link from Youtube about the "Plop" Linux distro which X-RED referred to . . . [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4q3zsYCE5gA](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4q3zsYCE5gA)

Plop allows you to use a usb on older PC's that do not support booting from USB Flash-Sticks or any USB device.  (so, you download a Plop iso, burn it to CD, and bootup from that CD Rom, while you have the bootable USB Flash Stick inserted into your USB port (with Ubuntu or Ubuntu-Mate on it).

Remember, Youtube is your friend for learning about many things including Linux, Ubuntu, etc.   Search for "Linux Tutorials, Learning Linux, Ubuntu How-To's" . . . . . . that way, you won't be as Bewildered anymore, but maybe just a little dazed and confused (which is an improvement).  :lolflag:
