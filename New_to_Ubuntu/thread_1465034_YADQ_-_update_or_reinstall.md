### Post by zipperback on 2010-04-29
You can install the release candidate and then when the official release happens which should be sometime in the next 24 hours, you can update your system as you normally would and then your system will have the official release version installed. At this point, I would think there will be very little difference between the two versions.

I personally feel more comfortable doing a clean full install whenever a new version is released.

The official version of 10.04 gets released in less than 24 hours so it's probably worth the wait, then you can just download the .iso and you'll have the disc for future installations should you need it.

Just my opinion of course...
