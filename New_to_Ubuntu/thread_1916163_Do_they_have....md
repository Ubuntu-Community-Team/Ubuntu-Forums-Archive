### Post by ClientAlive on 2012-01-27
Something occured to me a moment ago that would be really cool. I wondered if they already have something like this. If not, I bet it would be easy enough to tweak into Linux.
 
Imagine you have a multi boot system. You're booted into some Linux distro and you have certain apps running and all kinds of things you are in the middle of (whatever work you're doing on the computer at that moment); and, for whatever reason, you decide you would like to boot into a different Linux distro (which you have on your machine). However, you would like to carry over everything you are currently doing into that other distro - automagically when it boots up. You reboot into your other distro and when it finishes booting - Poof! - here everything you had running on the other one a moment ago begins to open up - precisely as it was on the other distro when you left it.
 
Anyone know of anything like this for sure?
