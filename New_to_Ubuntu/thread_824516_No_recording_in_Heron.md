### Post by stoodleysnow on 2008-06-10
Go to System-Preferences-Sound and make sure everything is set up right there. You can get (sound card) basic info from the drop down menu at the bottom of this window too. Right click on your volume icon in the top right and Open the main Volume Control. Make sure PCM and all recording related volumes are set correctly.
If you go to dohickey.parsed.net and download, then install dohickey you can post info about your PC's motherboard and sound card. We need to know in particular who made the sound card, the model, what functions it is supposed to have in the first place and how EXACTLY this differs to what you are getting. It could be an incomplete or buggy driver, in which case your best action to take is to post a bug report on Launchpad.
