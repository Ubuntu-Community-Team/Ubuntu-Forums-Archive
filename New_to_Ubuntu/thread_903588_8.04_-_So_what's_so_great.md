### Post by egalvan on 2008-08-29
+1 for a "clean" install

But in my case, that means opening the computer and making sure it is not full of dust bunnies.
Your lock-ups could be caused by excessive heat, which can be caused by excessive bunnies.

Those cans of air are fine, but I like to take dusty machines to a friend's house and (CAREFULLY) use his air compressor. He has a rubber-coated OSHA-compliant air-gun that really does the job. Make sure to clean the power supply, it's often over-looked.

On an older machine, the other thing to check is the CPU heat-sink. The fan may have died, make sure it is running.
 Although most AMD-type boards check for a dead fan on boot.
On really old machines, the heat-sink paste may be dried out.
And the power-supply fan may be bad. Check it also.
(yeah, that's a lot to check :) )

Two other things can help, speed-wise (in order of importance)

Depending on the motherboard, you should be able upgrade the RAM, preferably to a pair of 512's, although a pair of 256's will still be great.
Try the local 'mom&pop" computer stores. They'll either say "it's obsolete stuff, you can have it cheap/free" or they'll say "it's extremely rare antique, give me an arm-and-leg". I've heard both.

The other is to see if that board can run an Athlon-XP, maybe a 1700-2100 model. The -XP's ran faster (better/faster bus) and cooler. Same as the RAM comments above.

I have a 933MHz P-III machine running Kubuntu. With 512M, it runs well. When it only had 256M, it was a dog.

ErnestG
