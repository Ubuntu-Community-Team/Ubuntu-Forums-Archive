### Post by HarrisonNapper on 2010-04-23
Exactly. Especially with Linux, the greatest security risk in any system is the user him/herself.

Some things to consider about security in Ubuntu:

1. Do you open attachments, torrent, or otherwise engage in behaviour which could result in a compromise?

2. Would you know if your machine was infected? Screen-scrapers, keyloggers, and trojans do not have popup windows to notify you of their presence.

3. Do you share files with other people who use Windows who may receive an infection from the files you send them?

For these reasons, it may be a good idea to have a firewall (like Firestarter, which is a nice frontend for configuring iptables) and a virus scanner (note: not necessarily an antivirus solution that will run in the background and eat up system resources) like clamav to run every now and again.
