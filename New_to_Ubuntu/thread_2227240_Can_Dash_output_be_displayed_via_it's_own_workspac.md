### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-05-31
We can open the Dash in any workspace we are in. You seem to be wanting to open the Dash in one workspace and then have the Dash kept open as you switch to another workspace. I am not convinced of the benfits. This I very much doubt:

> Potentially, this could save some typing/time (especially useful on mobi or tablets).

Ubuntu phone/tablet has its own UI suitable for those devices. Based upon the few demonstrations that I have seen there will not be workspaces on the Ubuntu phone/tablet and swipe gestures will be used to move back and forth through the open applications. The term being used is "surfaces." Each application will be a surface. As I see it, each application will sit in its own "workspace."

My advice would be to attend some of the sessions of the Ubuntu Online Summit (10-12 July 2014) and see the direction the developers are going. And then there is Ubuntu On Air where developers share what they are working on and answer questions. Sitting in on these sessions gives us a good idea about the direction of development. Professional designers are being used to style the phone/tablet.

[http://summit.ubuntu.com/uos-1406/](http://summit.ubuntu.com/uos-1406/)
