### Post by 2F4U on 2012-04-01
You could install a benchmark tool such as Phoronix Test Suite, get the current numbers, and when you get the laptop back do the same. A comparison of both benchmark runs should show you any differences.

[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=news_item&px=NzA3Mg](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=news_item&px=NzA3Mg)

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-04-01
No laptop manufacturer is going to replace a laptop screen because of ONE dead pixel.  IF in doubt, you should contact them BEFORE you send it back to confirm they are going to do anything.

Also, it's not uncommon for them to wipe the drive and reinstall the original image -- so be sure to back anything up you don't want to lose before sending it.
