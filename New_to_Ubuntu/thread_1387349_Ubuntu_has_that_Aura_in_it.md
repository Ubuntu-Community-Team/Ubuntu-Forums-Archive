### Post by toaste on 2010-01-25
This is really all a matter of preference. Some things you can start tweaking in Ubuntu 9.10:

[LIST]
[*]Find a good x-splash theme and install it. [_Some nice picks plus tools/info for installing them at webupd8_]("http://www.webupd8.org/search/label/xsplash?max-results=10")

[*]Set up a gkt theme. Shiki-colors is the one from Mint 8, and I think shiki-dust looks good when combined with Humanity icons, but that's just me. [_Find one you like at gnome-look.org_]("http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=100")

[*]Tweak compiz effects to your liking by installing compizconfig-settings-manager.

[*]Turn on transparency in the terminal. Open termial->edit->profile preferences. Select white on black in the colors tab if you like, and turn on transparency in the background tab.

[*]And finally, install a dock like docky, dockbarX, awn, or cairo-dock. Again, the [_webupd8 blog_]("http://www.webupd8.org/search/label/dock?max-results=10") is a good resource for customizing ubuntu
[/LIST]
