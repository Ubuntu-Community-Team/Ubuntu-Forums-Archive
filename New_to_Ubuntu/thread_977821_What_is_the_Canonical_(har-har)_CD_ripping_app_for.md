### Post by chrisod on 2008-11-10
I thought Sound Juicer was the default and supported app in Gnome. What wouldn't be supported is ripping to MP3, since that is not a free and open codec. Ripping to Ogg, which is how it is configured on install, should be supported.

BTW, realize that the supported stuff is mostly leagalise due to codec and licensing issues. There are lots of good reasons to stick with truly free software, but since you don't have a paid support contract it really does not matter. Any of the open source apps in the official repositories should be fine to use.
