### Post by Rob Sayer on 2013-06-08
You may get some support for mint here but not much.  Mint may be ubuntu based ... and actually not all mint versions are, you didn't specify which desktop you use ... but it's an older version of the kernel and it's not actually supported here.

I will make a couple of suggestions though:

First, drivers do not work the same in linux as they do in windows where you just download a driver.  All that hardware related stuff in built into the protected kernel.  Nvidia support in linux is generally quite good but I have no experience with them.

Second, I've used mint in the past on my netbook.  First mint 13 xfce (which was awful) and then mint 13 Mate with xfce desktop installed separately, which worked better.

However, I have kubuntu installed on it now, mostly because mint may be mostly based on ubuntu but their tech support just doesn't remotely compare.  As you may have noticed.
