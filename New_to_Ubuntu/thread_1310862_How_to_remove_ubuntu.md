### Post by NickJones on 2009-11-02
Ubuntu will not be in the Add/Remove programs of Windows 7, as it is an operating system, not a program. Turn off your computer and re-start with the CD in the CD drive, your computer will flash something across the screen when it starts, it's normally the logo of the manufacturer, and in the bottom left will give you an option to "Select boot device F12" or something along those lines, it will tell you to press a button on your keyboard, press it, then select the CD and hit enter. Ubuntu will fire up.

Any more specific info on your PC would be helpful so I can explain how to select the boot device.

Sorry if this is confusing, it was as clear as I could make it,

Nick

### Post by JBAlaska on 2009-11-02
Stolen from a windoze forum;
   1. Put the Windows Vista or Windows 7 installation disc in the disc drive, and then start the computer.
   2. Press a key when you are prompted.
   3. Select a language, a time, a currency, a keyboard or an input method, and then click Next.
   4. Click Repair your computer.
   5. Click the operating system that you want to repair, and then click Next.
   6. In the System Recovery Options dialog box, click Command Prompt.
   7. Type Bootrec.exe /FixMbr  and then press ENTER.
   8. Reboot without the CD and Grub should be gone.

I have NOT tried this on Vista SP2..erm I mean Windows 7 but it should work as advertised.
