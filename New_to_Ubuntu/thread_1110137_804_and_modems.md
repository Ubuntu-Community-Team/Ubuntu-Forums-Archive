### Post by ModelM on 2009-03-29
What type of modem?

I have a friend who scrounges up old dial-up modems & brings them to me. I have 7 USR 56k modems of different vintages & types: serial (RS232), USB, & internal, another USB modem of a different brand, and one other RS232 modem. All of these work perfectly in Ubuntu 8.10. All except one of the USB modems work perfectly in Ubuntu 8.04.

I would also point out that all of these are hardware modems. None are of the "win-modem" type which can be very troublesome to make work on any operating system other than windows.

### Post by ranch hand on 2009-03-29
I run both 8.04 and 8.10 on a dial up modem.

Make sure that you get a hardware modem as opposed to a winmodem (software modem).  The winmodems aren't that great under windows.

Esternal (USB generally) are well supported.  I highly reccommend USR modems.

I actually use a USR5610c internal and it was somewhat of a challenge to set up but works great.
