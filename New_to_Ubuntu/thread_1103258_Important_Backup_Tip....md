### Post by DonaldJ on 2009-03-22
Given that you are now running a brand new computer operating system.. coming from the Windows scene, which really needs no description..  You know why you switched to Linux... it's a good plan to make a running backup of your treasures..  posts, notes, pix, music, links, favorites, bookmarks...  

I keep a flash-drive in the slot, and every time I leave the PC, I first transfer all the new items of that session to the flash...  Then once a week I copy the flash to the desktop, to edit it a little, then copy it to a CD-RW...
This way should I do something that destroys the OS, I don't loose anything, only the time it takes to format the hd with the W98 CD, and reload the hd with Ubuntu...

You cannot trust cheapy flash-drives to be your only back-up...  Some cheapo flash drives can be destroyed just from a little drop to the floor...  Don't trust a flash drive...

And CD's, I've read, sometimes only hold data for a decade...   When you've got the time, take all your precious ancient CD's, and reload them to DVD's so you will be sure you don't ever lose your precious treasures...  

*Seems the only thing that's foolproof for a backup that will last thousands of years, are cave drawings..  but you'll need a quiet dry cave for that...*

### Post by Temposs on 2009-03-22
In my opinion, optical media are way too unreliable and slow and have limited space. They scratch easily and degrade over time, even DVDs. What if I only have CDs and want to backup more than 700mb? What if I have DVDs and need to backup more than 20gb at a time? Optical media are too limited in their space.

I prefer to backup on a massive external hard drive.  That makes backups faster and the space is always re-usable, when it fills up. Also, you can set it up to automatically backup without you having to do anything. With optical media you have to remember to manually insert a CD or DVD.
