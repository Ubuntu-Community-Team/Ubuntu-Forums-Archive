### Post by aef54 on 2012-07-15
I'm playing around with Libre Office and (especially for freeware,)I think it's pretty good.

LibreOffice has the auto text option. If you type a long word, the program recognizes it and if it already has appeared in the text, it will give the word as a suggestion. If you then press enter, it autocompletes the word and you can continue typing. (laziness is awesome).

However, I don't like that you have to press enter. Because if you want to auto complete a command  in the terminal, you need to press Tab. I'm used to that. Now every time it gives me a suggestion in LibreOffice, I automatically press Tab. But then it just gives me a Tab. Not cool.

So my question is: can you edit the program in such a way that instead of pressing "enter", you have to press "Tab" to autocomplete the word?

### Post by Vaphell on 2012-07-15
Tools > Autocorrect options > Word Completion > Accept with (End/Return/Space/Right/Tab)
