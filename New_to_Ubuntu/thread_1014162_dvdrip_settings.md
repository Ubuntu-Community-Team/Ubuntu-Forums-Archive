### Post by shifty_powers on 2008-12-17
a .vob file is the video file on the dvd. it is what gets played by a dvd player. however it is encoded so that people cannot copy it and do naughty things with it. (heh that didn't last long, did it?). when you break that encryption and take the video file off the dvd and place it onto your hd, you have ripped it :D

transcoding means changing the format of the video to another. so for example, the default encoding for dvd is mpeg layer 2, iirc. you can use a program to "transcode" it into xvid, which will take up a lot less space on your hard drive, but which cannot be played by a dvd player on your tv, fro example...
