### Post by Dougie187 on 2009-12-01
Or you could use handbrake, or brasero (which is already installed) or k9copy (for kubuntu). There are quite a few options. It kind of depends what format you are looking at.

Handbrake is my recommendation if you are looking a single multimedia file similar to avi. ** after reading your post again, this seems to be what you are looking for** [http://handbrake.fr/](http://handbrake.fr/)

k9copy does this as well, and backs up to iso files.

Brasero can do iso files as well.
