### Post by wolfen69 on 2008-09-06
i installed [DebianEeePC]("http://wiki.debian.org/DebianEeePC") and i love it. however, it is a net install. you need to choose "standard" and "laptop" (do NOT choose a desktop environment, as it will put you way over the 2GB limit) during setup. after install, you are left without a gui, but i installed xfce for that. it takes some setting up to get it going, but it's worth it. be prepared to read the wiki carefully. other than that, there isn't a whole lot you can put on a hard drive with only 2 gigs. if you decide to go for it, feel free to PM me for any help.

### Post by River_Rat on 2011-10-08
Hanzj, although I'm a retired IT pro with very dated Unix experience, I'm a Linux newbie.  I just got my hands on a like-new Eee PC 2G Surf, and I'm also shopping for a better OS experience than the Xandros OS that came with this cute, little box.

That 2GB SSD is the severely limiting factor.  I can run Ubuntu Natty from a USB flash drive, but I'm not happy with the performance.  The problem could be the resource demands of the apps as much as Ubuntu.

The next OS I'll try is Puppy Linux.  The variant called Breezy claims to be particularly designed for little machines.  In fact, the site specifies the 2G Surf as one of the target netbooks for Breezy.  You can try it out from a live USB flash drive.  It's at [http://puppylinux.org/](http://puppylinux.org/) .
