### Post by Alwimo on 2011-11-09
Sony removed OtherOS functionality from the PS3 over fears it could be used for piracy, or some such reason.
 
If you have a PS3 that hasn't been updated and still has the feature, you won't be able to play games online because you would need to update to do so (which removes the OtherOS feature). That's the only answer to any of your questions I know.
 
I wish I tried it out before the feature was removed.

### Post by x C0MMAND0 x on 2011-11-09
I had Xubuntu installed on my PS3 for a while and loved it, and had it customized and it worked very well.  I play PS3 games online, and so when I had to get an update I blindly installed it without giving it a second thought.

It wiped out my "other-OS" and I lost everything I had done.  I was pretty upset at Sony for a while, because that was one of the reasons I had gotten a PS3 in the first place, but oh well, I got over it.

If you have no desire to play PS3 games online and connect your PS3 to the Internet with content that requires new updates, then you can install *buntu on it.

I would not recommend it, as a PS3 not updated is in my opinion useless.
