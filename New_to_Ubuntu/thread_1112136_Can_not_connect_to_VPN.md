### Post by Narvinye on 2009-04-16
Hi there!  When you ran the ipconfig in Windows were you connected to the VPN at the time?  When connected to a VPN the ip address that is pulled is usually different than that of your normal connection to a router. (Since your authenticated onto another domain) Also, it may be helpful to know what VPN application you are using in Ubuntu.  I use KVpnc on a Gnome environment and it works for me well.  

If you able to save your current VPN connection as a profile in windows I would try and import the VPN profile into a Linux VPN application that supports the connection method that you are using.  (In my case Cisco)

Hope this helps.  Cheers!
