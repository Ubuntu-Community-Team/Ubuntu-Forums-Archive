### Post by Spectre5 on 2009-06-18
UT2K4 - a game you can buy that runs natively on linux

Sauerbraten - FPS, kinda like doom

I've played through KOTOR1 and KOTOR2 on wine (both AWESOME games)...as well as several others.  Even many of the newest games work right away or with only minor tweaks on wine.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-06-18
X-Moto is a simple-looking game, but it takes a lot of skill to master and complete a lot of the levels. It's a kind of 2D motocross bike game, a tricked-out clone of Elastomania. Around 25 megabytes.

Sauerbraten is a pretty good FPS (it has a single-player mode) but it will eat up 300 megabytes.

If you go to [www.bungie.org](www.bungie.org) you can download Aleph One and the Marathon 2 scenario. Aleph One is an FPS game engine that was behind the successful Marathon trilogy of games for the Macintosh in the 1990s, now open-source. The Marathon 2 scenario is the content; it's a challenging and enjoyable single-player FPS, and it's also very enjoyable in multiplayer deathmatch or co-op! The engine is very dated now, but it's a lot more sophisticated than the Doom engine.

I can't remember how big it is. The Marathon 1 scenario is 30 megs, the Marathon 2 scenario is bigger. The engine is only a couple of megs. You also need to put a copy of the engine into the scenario's folder so it can find the scenario.

SuperTuxKart is 100 megabytes, quite a bit of fun, but doesn't come close to Mario Kart Wii :-(

Tile Racer is available from [www.getdeb.net](www.getdeb.net), and it's pretty good. It's a solo time-trial racing game with big jumps and loop-the-loops. 50 megabytes, apparently.
