### Post by bogan on 2012-05-07
Hi!, **captainSEO,**

The only 'dumb' thing about it is you did not say which version of what you are running.

If you log in to Gnome Classic you will get those menu options; if you log into Gnome3 you get a button called Applications which, clicked-on, gives you a display otherwise known as the 'Dash'.
Logged into 'Ubuntu' you get a button top left with the Ubuntu logo on it, that is called the 'Dash' Launcher and it does the same as above.

You get a Search string box and you type in there the begining of the application you want, or of what you want to do, and it will do its best to find what you are looking for.

If you really want the traditional menus to which you refer, you can have them too. Just install an app called ClassicMenuIndicator and Hey Presto, it is at your command, you can even edit it to show things as you wish!

Chao!, **bogan**.

And go down to the bottom of the Google page, select "Advanced Search" and narrow the timespan of your search to the last month, too.

The "Dash" is the top icon in the dock, aka Launcher, on the left of the screen.  

If you have a Windows key, tap it and a search bar will open. Start typing what you are looking for and it will appear in the icons below the search bar.

But, a Software Center icon is typically in the Launcher after an install.  Move your mouse cursor over the icons, slowly, to identify them.
