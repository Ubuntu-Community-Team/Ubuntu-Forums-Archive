### Post by tbid18 on 2010-12-28
What's up guys.

So I installed Kubuntu 10.10 a couple days ago, because I have played around with linux before on live CD's and I was interested in switching to it. I went through the process of partitioning the hard drive and I now dual boot into Kubuntu and Vista.

I love the plasma interface; it seems very responsive and slick, and I just really like the feel to it. The problem is, when it comes to the command line or troubleshooting various tasks (e.g., getting Amarok to read ipod, attempting to mount devices), I am at a loss. So far I have been able to google most of my issues and resolve them simply by copying and pasting commands into terminal that I have found by searching for similar problems. I realize that trial and error isn't exactly the most conducive method to avoid breaking something, so I'm somewhat concerned with how long I can do this successfully. 

Well I may have already reached my limit; I think I broke KPackageKit and no matter what I try I cannot seem to fix it. This, in addition to other problems that I have touched on, leaves me with the feeling that I am in way over my head with Kubuntu. I have heard from various people that Ubuntu is easier to use than Kubuntu for a first time linux user, for whatever reason. I have also heard that Mint is a good entry level linux distro, although I would rather not go to something that is limited if I can help it.

Basically, does anyone here think I should switch from Kubuntu to Ubuntu, or perhaps another distro like Mint? I do really like Kubuntu as I am a huge fan of customization, I just don't know if I am ready for it. Any help would be greatly appreciated.
