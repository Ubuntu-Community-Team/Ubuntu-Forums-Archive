### Post by ad_267 on 2008-07-07
If that nvidia driver is enabled then you shouldn't need anything else. I have an nvidia 6600 gt and everything works fine. Sounds like it could be the crappy screen. If it helps, you can change video settings by pressing Alt+F2 and running "gksudo nvidia-settings".

Make sure you check the monitor cable. Unplug it at both ends and blow any crap out and plug it in tight. I was having weird issues with my new monitor a while back and found out I just had a dodgy cable.
