### Post by anewguy on 2010-08-12
Not getting on the net - I must assume you mean via a wireless connection?  If so:

- there is a little icon on the top bar that normally looks like 2 little terminals:
[LIST]
[*]is there a red "x" through it?
[*]if you right-click on it, there should be a line that says "Enable Wireless" (if your adapter driver is working).  Be sure this checked.
[*]do you know anything about how your router was set up?  This makes a HUGE difference because there are things from encryption types and passwords to filters that may be on the router saying which computers can connect wirelessly.
[*]has this computer ever had Windows on it, and did it connect wirelessly then?
[/LIST]

- please do the following:
[LIST]
[*]open a terminal window (Applications/Accessories/Terminal)
[*]type:
[/LIST]
[INDENT][LIST]
[*]lspci <press enter>  You will probably get a longer list of things - look for one that might say "wireless" in it and post it back here
[*]lsusb <press enter> copy the output and post it back here
[*]lshw -C network <press enter> and post the output back here
[/LIST]
[/INDENT]

That will hopefully give us more information to work with so we can start trying to solve your initial problem.  We can go from there for other things.

Dave ;)
