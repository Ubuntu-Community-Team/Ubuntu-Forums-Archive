### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2014-04-01
Yes, but I would not recommend the mix of kde and gnome since kde installs a lot of things, in the end you may as well run kubuntu. In general there will be duplicate applications (e.g if you have gnome and xfce you will get both thunar and nautilus, leafpad and gedit etc)  and with kde and gnome you will get a lot. I have setup one machine with Unity and lxde (need a non 3d de for only remote desktop sessions and the natural choice,--gnome-flashback,--is broken in 13.10)There is a fair amount of duplication, which is very apparent from lxde's cluttered menu, but this is already minimal, it would be much worse with kde.
