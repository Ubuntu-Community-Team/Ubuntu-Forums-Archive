### Post by RedRat on 2010-03-07
OK this is a cynic talking. Cloud computing is not all that new, way back when, it was called client-server computing. We had dumb terminals back then and all programs and data was stored on the mainframe. Nowadays, you have software companies providing data services and you can store data on their computers. Some, like Google, allow you to actually word process. If you like it, use it.

However, a word of warning. Recently a couple of online data storage companies went under, users were given about a month to find new places to store their data. If you are going to use these new "clouds" you surely, or must, back up and store important stuff locally, i.e., on your computer or somewhere just in case you your cloud burps.
