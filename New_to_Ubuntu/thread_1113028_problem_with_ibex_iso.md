### Post by mikechant on 2009-04-02
It sounds most likely to be a hardware problem. The burning function often seems to become unreliable first when a drive is failing.
At least replacement drives are cheap and easy to fit (assuming this is a desktop, not laptop), even if you have zero hardware related experience. All you typically need to be able to do is undo a few screws and unplug/replug a couple of connectors.

The md5sum business is probably irrelevant if you're getting the same problems with different iso files.
