### Post by BruisedQuasar07 on 2010-10-05
Note: Wikipedia is a highly unreliable "encyclopedia". It is useful only as a quick online information source. Never should a person rely solely on anything from Wikipedia.

Encyclopedia Britannica is the supreme reference source of its kind in the world. It can and is quoted in a court of law. Each article is composed and signed by a recognize authority in the related subject field.

A person who appreciates and uses Britannica will not be satisfied with something as loose and subject to rank inaccuracy, bias, & simple mischief and intentional misinformation as is Wikipedia will not accept it as even a comic replacement for Britannica.

I have waited for some time for a practical Linux workaround that allows owners-users of professional and quality resource books on CD & DVD. So far, I've seen none. Wine has progressed immensely but as things are dual booting Windows and Linux remains the most practical way to use reference and data CDs and DVDs designed to work in Windows and Mac O/S. 

Since MAC O/S is a unix variant, albeit a highly proprietary variant, I thought the Linux community would have a program that exploits the MAC install option that comes with all data & multimedia disks. 

I thought the hot popularity of netbooks and world wide rise of Linux powered "pocket" PCs like Nokia N810-900 and Droid along side the highly popular (in Japan & S.E. Asia) Sharp Linux powered Sharp Zaurus would have inspired development of a way to run data media and education multimedia CDs and DVDs in Linus Distros.

The only way to run Britannica software on Linux is the online Britannica subscription service.
