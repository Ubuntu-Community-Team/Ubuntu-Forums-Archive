### Post by snowpine on 2009-03-06
> **Yed Ied said:**
> How do these Operating Systems compare?

They are more similar than different, as Ubuntu is based on Debian. Most skills you learn in Ubuntu transfer to Debian, and vice versa.

Debian is faster and much more stable. Lenny is a good choice if you want to stick with the same package/application versions for a couple of years. It is comparable in this way to Ubuntu's LTS releases like 8.04.

Ubuntu 8.10 uses slightly newer packages and has arguably a more "human" vibe. The community forums are friendlier and more active. It is a good choice if you like upgrading your system frequently to a newer release, as it will be replaced by 9.04 next month, and then 9.10 six months after that.

Basically, the stable branch of Debian (Lenny) is conservative, releasing at its own pace, while Ubuntu has an aggressive 6 month release schedule. You should also consider the testing (squeeze) branch of Debian, or even unstable (sid). In my opinion, Squeeze or Sid are better suited for the average desktop user than Lenny, which is really the best choice for servers or situations where stability is the only concern.
