### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-10-11
If you can take it back to the shop and exchange it for something else, do that. The Zune will not work with Ubuntu; Microsoft designed it to be locked to their own software. I'm not trying to lecture you, but you really should have researched Linux compatibility before making a hardware purchase.

I like the Sony X-series Walkmans; they are excellent players. Pricy, but worth it. Noise-cancelling headphones, OLED screen, web browser and podcatcher, excellent sound quality and battery life, and Compiz-like visual effects to navigate through videos and albums.

And they work fine with Ubuntu 9.04 (if you want to encode videos on Ubuntu 9.10 you'll need to compile ffmpeg 0.5 yourself... it's a long story involving a licensing issue). You can use my Blacklight program to encode videos in the correct format :-)  You can drag and drop music, videos and photos onto the player in your file manager which makes everything a breeze.

Otherwise I'm sure there are lots of suggestions on the internet about good Linux-compatible players to get.
