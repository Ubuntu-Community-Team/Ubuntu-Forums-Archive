### Post by DenyingProduct on 2013-10-29
What most likely happened is the drivers failed to properly install and now the computer can not load the gui so it looks like its getting stuck on battery but its not.

On boot up press and hold the shift key that will take you to grub loader and you can load "advanced option for ubuntu">"ubuntu , with linux <newest kernal> (recovery mode)"
this will load up the recovery menu from here try running failsafex and you can even try the dpkg to attempt to fix the nvidia drivers. However if non of this works you might have to do this the long way and remove the drivers

You can drop down to a root shell form here and run any commands to uninstall the nvidia drivers.

Sorry as I do not know how to remove the drivers (AMD fan) but this link should help with that.
