### Post by mcduck on 2010-02-09
Android doesn't have any sync support with computers apart from Exchange sync for Windows users with Exchange mail/calendar accounts. Other than that, Android just syncs everything automatically with Google's services, directly through network connection instead of going through your computer.

Just set whatever mail & calnedar apps you are using on your computer to sync with Gmail and Google contacts & calendar, and you'll have everything synced without ever having to actually plug the phone to your computer. :)

But apart lack of direct sync, Hero works just great with Ubuntu. The memory card appears as removable drive, and internet connection sharing through the phone works out-of-the-box with Network Manager, both with the normal USB connection and through WiFi tethering (note that thethering requires you to root the Hero and install some custom ROM). So no, you really don't need Windows to do anything with your Android phone.

(in case you ever need to, the Android Market has plenty of apps that allow exporting/importing contacts to files on your memory card. Althoug I must say that syncing throgh Google is much nicer)

### Post by tom.swartz07 on 2010-02-09
Alllrighty.

After some digging, Ive found that it is POSSIBLE to sync your contacts, messages, etc. using a program called Multisync- 

Heres a link to describe it- its a bit out of date, but others have said that it works with relative success. [http://smealko.blogspot.com/2008/10/htc-touch-pro-ubuntu-should-work-for.html](http://smealko.blogspot.com/2008/10/htc-touch-pro-ubuntu-should-work-for.html)


THAT BEING SAID- for every 1 person that said you could sync with a program, 4 others said that it was easier to just load it into the cloud.

I cant tell you specifics on how to do this, but I can give you a rough outline of whats needed.

Using a Google account, enable google sync. This will upload all of your data to an exchange server so that you could access it on your computer's web browser.

[http://www.google.com/intl/en_us/mobile/android/](http://www.google.com/intl/en_us/mobile/android/)

For example, you could sync your calendar from your phone to the Google Calendar online site, and the changes will be reflected almost instantly. Same goes for GMail and Contacts. They all get loaded into your personal account for use on the full web interface.


From what ive read, it seems that enabling the sync is 10x easier than on the iPhone- makes sense, because the Hero is an Android phone.. haha


I hope that this helps in some way! Good luck!

### Post by mcduck on 2010-02-09
One mor thing I forgot to mention, related to adding stuff into your Hero's memory card:

Put any files you want to use as your ringtones or notifications into /media/ringtones and /media/notifications. That will make them appear in the same places where you'd choose any of the built-in sounds, and also hides them from your phone's music player.

And if you want to hide any directory from all the media apps in the phone, just add epmty file called ".nomedia" to that directory. This is usefull for example in case you install some navigator app that has it's speech as audio files on your memory card. You wouldn't want them to appear in your music player...
