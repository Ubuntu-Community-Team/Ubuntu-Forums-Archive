### Post by bumanie on 2008-07-15
Open source/GNU/linux runs the majority of hardware without issue. Problems sometimes occur with cutting-edge/new hardware. Main reason for this is that many proprietary manufacturers don't write drivers/firmware to be compatible with linux, thus the open source community have to write drivers to hardware they have not designed - it thus sometimes takes a bit of time for development of the OSes to catch up with everything. Wireless is not always good, but ubuntu is working really hard on improving that for the Intrepid Ibex release. Don't expect smooth sailing all the time, but most of the time things work well. Also remember; the price is good and you have the freedom to choose what programs you want, rather than having them chosen for you!!
