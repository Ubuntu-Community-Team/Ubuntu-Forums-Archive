### Post by wausaubill on 2008-11-19
It has been a while cominb, but I am just one week away from making the switch!

First a bit of background...I tried to switch to Linux about 4 or 5 years ago, but frankly the learning curve was steeper than I had time for at the time.  Apparently I had a weird monitor at the time time and none of the distros that I tried would recognize it.  There were other drivers that were non-functional as well.  I suppose I could have hunted around to find the right pieces, but well, you know.

But things have changed quite a bit.  Now it is true that at the moment, I can't get WUBI installed quite right, but I went ahead and burned the .iso to a CD and used it live.

Ubuntu does rock.  Booted right up, probably faster than XP does on my machine and considering that Ubuntu has to figure out everything from scratch, that is pretty good.

It recogized my two (different) usb drives and all my Windows drives (thank you Samba folks, I assume).  I clicked on a PowerPoint presentation -- Open Office fired right up and there it was.

It also recognized my wireless card and the network name.  All I had to do was type in my wep code and I was off and running on the net.

Yes, things were a bit slow because I was running off the CD, but not bad at all.  A tinch slower than XP, but that means it would be faster if it was on the hard drive.  Which it will be soon.

Why soon?

I have a second internal hard drive on my machine, which will be perfect to use as my Ubuntu drive.  But at the moment it is full of media type files.  I need to buy a USB 2 card so I can transfer those files to my large USB drive (there is about 75 gigs or so).  Once the disk is clean, I will have a dual boot machine.

Why dual boot?

Just a bit conservative, I guess.  There are a few programs that I am not sure I can replace in the Linux world (genealogy and ham radio mostly) but I don't use them often.

But for the most part, you can count me as another convert.  

I know I am standing on the shoulders of giants and I thank all of you that made this possible.

Have lots of fun!

Bill
