For web Bluefish is quite good otherwise you can use any text editor of your choice (plenty of those in Linux).

suggest you look into moving the website to Wordpress or similar platform. it is easy to setup or you can use the preset blog site (the .com domain). plenty of hackers targeting the platform but with good security tools installed the skript kiddies stay away and are blocked. with regular backups and propper lockdown, there is not much to fear. you don't have many sites so the move should be easy to do with a copy paste. wordpress has built in wysiwig editor. if you have more volunteers maintaining the site (e.g. news) you can give them access to specific sections etc.

 current website has issues when clicking pages. pics are not loading or i am getting:

Apache Server at [www.stfrancisfarm.org](www.stfrancisfarm.org) Port 80"

fonts seem to be different in various paragraphs.

a pitty. you have some nice pics and perhaps this could be a really nice interactive website. i think it would be worth paying for hosting. yearly it should not be that much for a good cpanel host with fast connections and all. then again it depends how much traffic you get and at what you are aiming.

another descent and cheap option is google's blogger or google apps website (14 or is it 24 USD a year or something like that). we use Google apps and Google pages, though in retrospect blogger might have been a better choice (more themes). anyway with google you get it all really cheap - website, emails, domain name, calendar etc. etc.
