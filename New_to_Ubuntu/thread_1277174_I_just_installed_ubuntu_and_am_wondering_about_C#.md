First off, most languages that are widely used today are based off of C, so learning C completely (not half way) would help you quickly pick up on many others.  It is hard to recommend a language if I don't know exactly what you want to accomplish.  Sounds like you are just inamored with programming or just computers in general so that leaves you wide open.

In that case, I highly recommend C++ and Java.  They somewhat related to one another but both have their special places in programming.  There will be varying opinions on this but mostly it is what works for you.  If you want data to back up your choice visit Sun Systems website to learn more about Java.

As far as web pages go. I highly suggest the tuturials at W3C.com.  They are the world wide web consortium and they basically make the HTML rules, so advice from them is a sure thing. ### Post by matthew.ball on 2009-09-28
If you want to become an uber programmer/hacker.

My recommendation is to learn C - *really* learn C.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_C_Programming_Language_(book](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_C_Programming_Language_(book))

It's a fairly small book and the standard on C, which is good (and quite opposite to most programming books these days).

As dvn3ch has said, most modern languages borrow heavily from C - if you understand the syntax and logic employed by C, you will understand the syntax and logic employed by 98% of languages.

If you have become comfortable with C, you may want to look into functional programming, maybe not to use, but it will definitely make you re-think how you approach programming, which is probably the most important.

### Post by dvn3ch on 2009-10-02
egalvan,

There is nothing wrong with learning C# and VB.net.  However, most of the posts have been to steer toward plain C or C++.  Python and the like are good options too.  But a programmer has to be well equipped with all types of programming styles to innovate elsewhere.

Some great military philosopher once said, "To be skillfully victorious, one must fully know the mind and goals of one's enemy or one is doomed to the advances or defeats of luck and luck is never generous."

I don't hate MS or programmers that sell their wares.  I just find FOSS more appealing and different than the mainstream.  There are many people out there that do not want to know the reasons why something works, they just want it to work.  These people could buy a car with the hood welded shut and would be perfectly fine with that.  We do not see things that way and choose another path. 

If calk decides that he wants to make programs in VB.net (which work very well in MS, btw) that is his choice.  However, the tendency or future trend is smaller, more agile, cross-platform programs that can be deployed in customized ways.  That is not VB.net or anything approaching MS languages.  So that is why I suggest plain C to start out.  This language is the gateway to all the relevant languages and will give calk a firm foundation to root his youthful programming mind.
