### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2008-06-05
To be honest, IMO the "best" distro for a new user is the one that has the best hardware recognition out of the box. Hardware support is quite good these days on all distros , but hardware problems out of the box are frustrating.

If you are new to Linux, IMO, best start with one of the major variants. Ubuntu/Debian, Fedora, SUSE, or even a Knoppix live CD. At the end of the day, under the hood, most of what you learn on any distro applies to all the rest.

The Ubuntu Forums is outstanding as we have a large community and the forums are quite welcoming to new users.

One last tip, I came across an interesting distro the last week. Kiwi. Kiwi is the new Mint (IMO).

>  Kiwi is a modified  Ubuntu live CD for the i386 architecture. It includes <clip> multimedia codecs, encrypted DVD support, Flash plugin for Firefox, PPPoE GUI for accessing local internet services (Clicknet and RDS).

The latest release, Kiwi 8.04 is based on Ubuntu 8.04. Differences from Ubuntu include replacing Evolution with Thunderbird, Rhythmbox with Audacious and dropping Ekiga.

[http://kiwilinux.org/kiwi/en/](http://kiwilinux.org/kiwi/en/)

I installed it for my family and it is a very clean implementation basically just a few (minor) modifications, otherwise all Ubuntu.

Mint is very nice, but as with Ubuntu / Debian they are drifting away from the mother ship (which is not bad mind you) and do not yet have a stable 8.04 release (RC is out). Again, not that this is a bad thing, it just shows the drift is all.
