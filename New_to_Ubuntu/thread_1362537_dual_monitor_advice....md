### Post by audiomick on 2009-12-23
Hallo.
There is a lot to be said for using two monitors of the same size.
Having said that, mine are different (1280x1024 and 1024x768  )

One thing to bear in mind is that, if they are different sizes, you will have a "dead space" around the smaller one equivalent to the difference between the smaller and the larger. That is, the total dimension of the x-screen overhangs the smaller monitor. This is visible as the gray area above and below the left hand monitor in the attached screen shot. Occasionally, when the computer gets a bit "confused", I end up with the top bar of an open window in the dead space, and have to enlarge it across to the right hand screen to get at it.

### Post by audiomick on 2009-12-23
OK
My 1280x1024 is a 19", and that is ok. If your card can only do 1280x800, there is probably not much point in going much bigger than that. I would probably get a 17" widescreen, to match the format of 1200x800, or maybe even only a 15.4", given that the laptop is so small. If find it disturbing looking backwards and forwards bewteen 2 screens with very different points-per-inch values.
