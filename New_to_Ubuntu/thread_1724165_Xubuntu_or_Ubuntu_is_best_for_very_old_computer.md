### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2011-04-08
As above: We really can't answer the question without knowing more about your machine. How much RAM? Processor speed?

Some things to consider:

**[COLOR="Purple"]1.[/COLOR]** - The new Ubuntu, with the Unity desktop, may be equal to Xubuntu in demand on computer resources *for the desktop environment*. 

**[COLOR="Purple"]2.[/COLOR]** - But most of the time it is *applications* - not the desktop environment - that demand resources. On the whole Gnome applications need much more RAM and processor resources than the lighter Xfce applications.

**[COLOR="Purple"]3.[/COLOR]** - There's this thing called "integration" that comes into play and also makes a difference in performance. Gnome/Unity is *designed* for Gnome applications, which are more demanding than Xfce ones. 

**[COLOR="Purple"]4.[/COLOR]** - Default applications in Xubuntu are *much* lighter and faster! In the new Natty version you'll find Abiword and Gnumeric in place of the very resource-hungry LibreOffice suite. Xfburn in place of Brasero, etc. Xubuntu has been "on a diet" recently and is lean and mean!

Lubuntu is prob'ly awesome as well! I haven't revisited LXDE since last year when I gave Xfce a try and absolutely loved it! LXDE (Lubuntu) is supposedly *ultra*-light and fast, but I found that it lacked some features that really matter to me, and it had this "Win98" look and feel that looks [COLOR="Purple"]retro-nerdy[/COLOR] instead of [COLOR="Purple"]wicked-kewl[/COLOR], lol. It's time I revisited it, though, because when I left it was still "under heavy development" (read: Beta, beware!) and I'm betting it has come a long way since then. 

Xubuntu runs absolutely awesomely on my modest Dell B110 with it's paltry little 512 mb of RAM. *I did not find any noticeable difference in performance on that machine between Xubuntu and U-Lite* (an early version of Lubuntu).

-Robin

Agree with above, need some specs.  On a minimum, we could tell you much more if we know how much RAM (memory) and how much hard drive space you have to work with.  I can tell you, I have been working to find an extremely lightweight distro and have found a few very good choices out there.  The obvious as you have mentioned the "something-buntu" distros.  I have successfully loaded up Puppy, WattOS (nice easy distro, once installed will run on as little as 96 MB RAM, did it, works well), Crunchbang, and my current toy that's loaded on my other laptop, Dreamlinux.  Dreamlinux is a nice full featured distro.  Only drawback is that you do need 256 MB RAM to run and you will need to set up a reasonable swap drive on your HDD.  The desktop itself appears similar to a MAC desktop and works in a similar fashion.  I'm still exploring that one, but it is a good option.  Of course, if you have 256 MB RAM, you can probably run Xubuntu or Lubuntu.  If you're interested, you can check out a thread I posted a couple weeks ago here: 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1709252](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1709252) 

Read through it, its a few pages long, but it might give you some ideas on where to start.  And don't be afraid to kick the tires on different distros and find out what's right for you.  You are the one who knows what you need to function and do what you want.  Good luck!
