### Post by Lord Stig on 2009-03-26
Seemingly every time I cold boot my machine and select Ubuntu from the boot menu (have XP as well) I get the message "Booting GRLDR". Except it doesn't, it just sits there. I have left it for a few minutes with no progress and no other messages appear. All I have to do to solve the problem is Ctrl Alt Del and then when I select Ubuntu from the boot menu it will flash something up briefly (I suspect the Booting GRLDR message) and then carry on to the 'press Esc to access the menu' countdown and boot up as normal.

Been using 8.10 for two months and didn't do this at first but started to occasionally a few weeks ago; now it's pretty much every time I cold boot.
Not a major problem but like to solve if possible.

Ubuntu was Wubi installed (Wubi for the newbee!) and I didn't choose a separate partition - it seems to Windows to be installed in a folder.

Appreciate your help.

### Post by Lord Stig on 2009-10-12
Just doing a bit of maintenance on my old posts and realised I never marked this as resolved.
A classic case of PICNIC - the drive was IDE and ages ago I changed the jumper setting on it and forgot about it. Setting it back resolved the issue.

Thanks to those who tried to help!
