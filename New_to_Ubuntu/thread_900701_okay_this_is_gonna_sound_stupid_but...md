### Post by deicidist on 2008-08-31
Making the cube seem smaller is pretty straight-forward. Go to the gnome menu> System> Preferences> Advanced Desktop Effects Settings

In the window that appears scroll down to the "Rotate Cube" option under the "Desktop" section. Click on it and in the first tab, adjust the slider that says "Zoom" to your liking. A lower value will make the cube appear larger and a higher value will make the cube appear smaller.

To change the background picture (or skydome) go to the gnome menu> System> Preferences> Advanced Desktop Effects Settings

In this window, go to the "Desktop Cube" option in the "Desktop" section. Go to the tab that says "Appearance" and near the bottom you'll find the options for setting your 'skydome'.

Let me know if you have any issues with this and I can add a little more detail if  necessary.
