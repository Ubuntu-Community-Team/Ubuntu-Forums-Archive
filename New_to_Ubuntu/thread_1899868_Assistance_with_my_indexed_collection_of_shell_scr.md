Hi, all.  I am an absolute beginner to Linux and Ubuntu. But I am sort of a Rip van Winkle who went to sleep in 1988 under 4.2 BSD and woke up today under Ubuntu.
[B]
BACKGROUND[/B]: I worked at the US Naval Ocean Systems Center (NOSC) from 1976-1992  starting from the Programmers Work Bench (PWB) through BSD 4.1, 4.2 and the onset of 4.3. (I coded, did teaching, writing, and published a Center-wide computer user newsletter from 1980-1988 as well as numerous user manuals and user script tools for Center developers and users)
[B]
THE ISSUE TODAY:[/B] In the course of starting to learn UNIX I began to squirrel away  a bunch of little files I could use as my own little cheat-sheet. When the files got too numerous I developed a script to serve them up by shell Command-name or by a primitive keyword search script.

From these, I developed a manual of 210 examples of shell scripts and command usage using BSD commands. Later I ported this whole mess to the Web in 1996. It now resides at
[CENTER][http://www.panix.com/~spkemp/examplex/index.htm]("http://www.panix.com/%7Espkemp/examplex/index.htm")
[LEFT](but minus the keyword search function, drat!)
[B]
MY QUESTION:[/B]
  **Can I expect most of them to work under Linux? That is, how compatible is the Ubuntu environment for running 4.2 BSD shell scripts **(Bourne Shell and [gasp!] C-shell)? 
(Also note that 90%+ of these examples *were* tested and *did work*, but may have occasionally suffer a little munging in translating them from ASCII into HTML in '96.)
