### Post by damis648 on 2008-11-23
Mainly, all you will need to do is to boot from the CD. To do this, enter your BIOS settings and set the CD Drive as the first boot device (It might already be). After doing this, turn on the computer with the CD in the drive. You will be prompted for your language, and then select "Install Ubuntu". Once it loads, you will be greeted by a nice welcome screen which will guide you through the installation. When it come to the partition stage, it will offer the option of shrinking your Windows partition and installing Ubuntu on the leftover space. Use that, and move the slider to your liking. Continue through answering all the questions. When all said and done, when you power up your computer it will ask what you want to use: Ubuntu or Windows. Good luck!
