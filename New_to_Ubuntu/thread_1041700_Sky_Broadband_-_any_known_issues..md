Sky won't let you have your username/password. You can access the router as normal, but it has slightly custom firmware and can only be used to connect to Sky. A bit of a pain really. There's no way that I know of to flash the firmware or hack the password/username.

### Post by solitaire on 2009-01-16
The only good thing about SKY is that you get a Linux based ADSL Modem. :D

The username and password is held / created in a custom app in the router's bin folder (i think).

Technically you can get your Username and Password direct from sky and flash it with the "DGTeam" firmware (google it! ;) ) but it's a fluddy hassle to get the info out of them!

### Post by redseventyseven on 2009-01-17
Step 1 - Plug it in and see if it works?

Back when I used to use Windows, I tended to find that the only useful thing that "Software Installation disks" did was to set up the network settings automatically - a step that could be done manually with very little trouble after a bit of rooting around in the literature. The other things they did were to mess about with my settings, plaster their own branding all over Internet Explorer and install a bunch of "manager" and "planner" and "organiser" type software which I had no use for and was a hell of a job to uninstall.

After that, you may need some more guidance! It may comfort you to know that there's already a thread about this issue here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=389743](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=389743)
