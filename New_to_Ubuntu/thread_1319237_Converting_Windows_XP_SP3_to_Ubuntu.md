I haven't watched the video given on the link but... on the menu given to you when you restart your computer and click on "try without changes", be sure to try everything on your system. Like sound (only plays .ogg, .flac and a couple other formats), video (not sure if .avi is supported by default but I think it is) and your networking devices. ### Post by lkraemer on 2009-11-09
A good place to start is:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/](https://help.ubuntu.com/)

Then after you have selected the version you want to try, you can
read up on the switch from Windows at:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/9.10/index.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/9.10/index.html)

Now, you just need to decide what Windows Software you CAN'T LIVE WITHOUT!
Then find a way to run it in Linux, or find a replacement package that is
just as good, or probably better.  In my case it was Quicken.

Then you are on your way.  You'll never look back, and in fact after you
have run Ubuntu for a year or two and haven't had any problems you will
hate the thought of booting the other Virus Prone, BLOATED, OS.

After you get over the shock of trying to learn a new OS, you will settle down
and actually enjoy learning Ubuntu.  After all, you didn't learn Windows
in a week or two.  You were strung along for years with upgrades,
and the vapor ware that finally was released with new security bugs.

Enjoy!  I sent you a PM.
