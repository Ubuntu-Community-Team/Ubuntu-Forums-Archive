### Post by linuxlizard on 2008-05-02
That link gives specifics. 

As far as day to day experience- it's much faster than gutsy on my machine (amd athlon 2800+, 756 mb ram). Firefox for example opens in about 2 seconds, previously it took more like 10.

But setting things up was a little hairy- gutsy everything just worked, hardy was not as simple with my video card, which I thought was sort of retarded (if it worked before why would "new and improved" software not work as well as or better?). Compiz seems to have taken 1 step forward and two steps back as far as compatibility with games, (I only had to turn compiz off in gutsy for full screen virtualbox, all games and other software worked fine with compiz turned on- I have to turn compiz off in hardy for almost all full screen games 2d or 3d). On the plus side my frame rate is better for 3d games- even alien arena which I play with compiz turned on. But the fact that hardy would have more problems with this stuff all working in harmony than gutsy has sort of left a sour taste in my mouth. I was going to give out hardy cds to some friends who work in IT in our local schools, but I won't be doing that now as I'm afraid I'll be haunted by requests for help with problems (edit that- more likely I'll be haunted by complaints and problems, and then have to hear about how linux sucks and they are staying with reliable windows). I had them excited for the release of hardy after showing them all the cool open source software that can do for free what they have been paying lots and lots of money to do on windows, so I had some pretty hot potential converts. Hardy sort of took the wind out of my sails.  

Maybe in 6 months when the next release comes out...

Don't get me wrong, it's great if you have a year or two linux troubleshooting under your belt- it's faster and pretty slick, but for beginners first trip into linux- there's potential for a lot of headache.
