### Post by mcduck on 2010-07-09
Do you mean you ave 3 GB of RAM, or 3 GB of hard drive space?

If you are talking about RAM, it's not something that you'd assign to operating systems (virtualization being the expection, of course). Whatever OS you are running at the time will use all the RAM.

..and if you are talking about hard drive space, 3 GB wouldn't really be enough, while you could fit Ubuntu and some programs into that space you really wouldn't have any room for your personal files. I recommend at least 10 GB partion for Ubuntu, but the more the better. :)

### Post by cong06 on 2010-07-09
If you're referring to RAM then 3GB should be good. In many cases 512MB could be enough.

If you're referring to Hard drive memory, then.. you'll want to allocate around 8GB at least to start.

When it comes to RAM you don't have to "allocate" since only one operating system will be running at a time. Only one operating system will be using your RAM at any given time.
