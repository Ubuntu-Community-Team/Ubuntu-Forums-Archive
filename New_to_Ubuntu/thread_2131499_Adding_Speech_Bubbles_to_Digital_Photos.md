### Post by alphacrucis2 on 2013-04-02
You can draw the bubbles freehand in Gimp but something like inkscape is probably better for that sort of thing. A quick google turned up an inkscape tutorial for doing comic style speech bubbles. It would be fairly easy to import a picture into inkscape and overlay the speech bubbles.

[http://thepinksylphide.com/2008/11/21/inkscape-web-comic-tutorial-the-speech-balloon](http://thepinksylphide.com/2008/11/21/inkscape-web-comic-tutorial-the-speech-balloon)

inkscape is a standard vector graphics drawing program in the repos


PS. I notice there are a bunch of youtube vids showing different ways of doing speech bubbles in inkscape
