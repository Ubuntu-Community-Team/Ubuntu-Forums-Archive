### Post by Aspiring_Failure on 2011-01-16
In a lot of ways, Linux does do a lot of things better than Windows. But Windows is way more accessible to novice users, and some 95% of all computer users are more familiar with it. I suggest you dualboot the most promising linux OS you've seen (I recommend PClinuxOS) with your favorite versio of Windows and try using one exclusively for one week, and then the other for another week.

It's important to remember that each OS has It's pros and cons. Linux is so, so, so much easier to customize than Windows. That's why I love it. But Windows, because It's the most common OS, has far more Software available for it. Including many games that may not work well in Linux, even under WINE.

There's no cut-and-paste superior OS. It just depends on what you're looking for in one.

If Ubuntu;s giving you any problems, we'll do what we can to help. :)
