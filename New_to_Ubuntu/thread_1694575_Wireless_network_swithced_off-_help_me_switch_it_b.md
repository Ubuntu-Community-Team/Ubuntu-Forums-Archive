### Post by Eddison on 2011-02-24
SOLVED!

Ok panic over!
found out how I disabled wireless. The original problem was Ubuntu pestering me about connecting with the wireless network, when I already had a wired connection. I have a Samsung laptop, and simply disabled wireless in the boot sequence.

For beginners, press F2 or similar to go to boot meny, when the laptop or PC is booting up. then find wireless and change the value to 'always on' 'always off' or better 'last status' which means the laptop will remember if wireless was on or not.
