### Post by kcohen1017 on 2010-03-21
I'd be surprised if there is such as thing, as it would have to derive track information out of nothing.  2.1 stereo is comprised of two front (left and right, obviously) channels and a low frequency channel (sub-woofer).  5.1 is comprised of discreet front left, front right, center, left rear, right rear and the low frequency channel.

Whenever you come across a "re-mastered" recording where it's now a more advanced audio scheme, the producers either used some very sophisticated equipment to create the new channel information or they had access to the original recordings that had discreet sound and remixed from them. 

That said, any 5.1 sound system will play a 2.1 mix just fine.  If it sounds weird to you, change the audio system decode settings until you find one that's pleasing to your ears.  There's usually a setting that matches 2.1 as well as pre-defined equalizer settings.
