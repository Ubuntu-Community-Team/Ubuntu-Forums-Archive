### Post by bogan on 2012-09-02
Hi!, **sunfis**t,

In your log-in screen, top-right in the box, click on the Icon and select 'Gnome Classic (no effects)' in the drop-down menu.

If the option is not there, install 'Gnome-fallback-session' in 'ubuntu software center' and log out and back in.

There are lots of other options, and I am sure some of those who favour them will hasten to recommend their favorite, but the above is the easiest and most simple.

### Post by uzumakifahim on 2012-09-02
Ubuntu gives you a good number of Desktop Environment (DE), to choose between them as you like. If you want to get the same look as Ubuntu 10.04, you can install Gnome Desktop Environment on your Ubuntu 12.04. To install Gnome go to Ubuntu Software Center and just write gnome is the search box. Install the package "The GNOME Desktop Environment, with extra component", as a normal package installation. Then restart your PC. During rebooting, when the login window will comes, click on the gear icon, select Gnome classic from the list and login to your desktop. That's it. Feel free to let us know the update.

Thanks.
