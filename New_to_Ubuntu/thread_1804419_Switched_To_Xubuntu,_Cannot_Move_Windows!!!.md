### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2011-07-14
Omygoodness, I'm sorry you're having trouble! It sounds like a graphics driver thing, but it could also be a pretty simple screen resolution setting. Try **Menu > Settings > Xfce Control Center** and check your resolution against your monitor's normal setting. 

While the newest version of Xubuntu is awesome on most hardware, I've found that on my 'puter it's better to stick with the LTS versions. Xubuntu 10.04 (Lucid) is flawless, while the newest version acts up a little on graphics. It usually takes a few weeks or maybe as much as 2 months for updates to fix some of the bugs in a new release.

If playing with the Xfce display settings doesn't solve your problem (and new settings usually take effect instantly) and you feel so inclined, try burning a Xubuntu Lucid (LTS) CD instead of the latest version. I always recommend that newcomers stick with the long-term-support versions of Ubuntu (and its siblings).

Hope this helps,
Robin
