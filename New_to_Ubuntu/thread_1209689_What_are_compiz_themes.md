### Post by QIII on 2009-07-10
The themes you see when you right click the desktop are some that come along with Ubuntu.  They are pretty vanilla, but they are worth trying out as an initial exploration if you'd like to see what changing the theme on your machine might look like.

Try selecting one, apply it, and see if you like it.

Fiddle around.

Then you can start looking for themes you like on sites like gnome-look.

All of those other themes are created by third-parties -- and some of them are pretty spectacular.

You can add background images to your panels, use the GNOME color chooser to change the color of the text on your panels, and any number of other changes.

### Post by Viva on 2009-07-10
Metacity themes change your window frame(title bar).
GTK themes change your window controls. To put it simply, they change everything except your title bar, icons and pointer.
Icon themes change your icons.
Emerald themes do the same as metacity themes, but they require compiz(desktop effects) and emerald running. You install them using emerald theme manager. They are often better looking and more flexible than metacity themes. They can add effects like title bar transparency.
x11 mouse themes change your pointer.
