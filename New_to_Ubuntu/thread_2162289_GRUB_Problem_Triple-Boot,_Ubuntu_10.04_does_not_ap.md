### Post by newb85 on 2013-07-14
First, of all, there's a big difference between Ubuntu 10.04 and Ubuntu 4.10.  10.04 (Lucid Lynx) was released April 2010.  4.10 (Warty Warthog) was released October 2004.  Neither one is supported anymore (although Lucid server edition is supported through April 2015).  Also, neither one will work with the EFI partitioning scheme in place on your hard drive.

### Post by Glkanter on 2013-07-14
I had the exact same problem, at this thread: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2133339](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2133339)

And here was the solution:

[ 						 							]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=1275004")[**[COLOR=#000000]fantab[/COLOR]**]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=1275004")
Do you mean to say that you have both Ubuntu-32 and Ubuntu-64? And that you are booting into Ubuntu32?

If YES, then boot into 32bit Ubuntu and run [copy/paste + enter] from Terminal:

 	Code:
 	
**sudo update-grub** 
Restart and see if Ubuntu64 is shown in the Grub Menu.

I suspect that the Installer did not intstall GRUB... so it could be  that you still have GRUB from your 32bit install. Just try the above and  see if it helps. If it doesn't then BOOTINFO SCRIPT, as adviced  earlier, is what we need.

Good Luck.
