### Post by vasa1 on 2011-10-14
As the title says, Update manager offers to upgrade to 11.10 since I'm on 11.04.

Just for the heck of it, I've decided to try it especially since Ubuntu feels it's the preferred route.

I first did a regular sudo apt-get update and upgrade to make sure 11.04 itself was fully updated.

Depending on what speed I get, it's going to take at least another 5 hours :D ~700 MB have to be downloaded!

But one positive is this: when the net connection drops, we have to go through some of the initial steps all over again (which doesn't take much time) but the downloading of files resumes from where the process was disrupted. It's not like the entire process starts from zero and that whatever was downloaded is lost and has to be downloaded again. So that's nice. I feel I can even stop the download and start from where I left off tomorrow. (It's pretty late here.)

What would be nicer is to come back here later and report success.

I'm prepared for failure as well (provided it's not at the hardware level). I've backed up my files and have a LiveCD (which works just fine). If the upgrade fails, I'm ready to wipe off 11.04 with gparted and do a clean 11.10 install using the LiveCD.
