### Post by jezebel on 2009-11-06
Yes, I want Windows 7, too.

Anyway, I've solved the problem to my satisfaction.

In windows, using Acronis Disc Director, I deleted the two partitions that had both Ubuntus on them. I knew this would destroy my reboot possibilities, but I also did want to reinstall Ubuntu.

So, I rebooted with my fresh bootable USB, and reinstalled Ubuntu on a smaller (20gb) partition, leaving the rest of the space free for whatever.

I rebooted, the Grub came up with only one (and a functional one!) Ubuntu, the usual other stuff (memtest etc) and my Win 7.

I occasionally get a hanging black screen with a flashing cursor when trying to boot into Ubuntu, but I think that will fix itself eventually through updates and bug fixes.
