### Post by Paqman on 2011-06-05
No, the fact that it's been reallocated means it is fixed (or at least that the defect is contained, which is the same thing from your point of view).

There's no point in formatting the drive, it won't change anything. That sector has now been marked as bad by the drive, and it won't ever get used again. But don't worry, one sector is an absolutely tiny part of the drive, you won't even notice it's gone.

Having a bad sector or two is pretty normal for drives after a little while. As long as it's only a couple and the number isn't going up, and they've been successfully reallocated then it's no cause for concern.

75 sectors is a problem though, I'd look at replacing that drive immediately Joe of Loath. In the meantime, make sure you've got a good backup system.
