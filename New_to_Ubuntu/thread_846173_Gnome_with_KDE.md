### Post by Canis familiaris on 2008-07-01
Go to System->Administration->Synaptic, search for kubuntu-desktop and install the kubuntu-desktop package.
When prompted to choose the login manager choose GDM.
It will not really affect the installation. Only may bring up few confusing menus.

### Post by alienexplorers on 2008-07-01
I'll tell you if you want a system with a lot of bells and whistles then Kubuntu is for you.  If you are like me gnome is best since it has a simple layout.  I have tried both and prefer gnome, but others I have talked to love KDE.  Have fun trying then out and if you like one over the other you can always go to [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/") and choose to remove the one you don't want to use.  Just run the Pure gnome commands or the pure KDE commands.
