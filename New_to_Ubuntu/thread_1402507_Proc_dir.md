### Post by mcduck on 2010-02-09
In short, /proc contains a file for every process that is running on your system. It's a virtual file system, and doesnt take any space on your hard drive, in case you were wondering about that. It's simply related to the Linux's way of seeing *everything* as a file, be it a process, device, or some data stored in your computers RAM.
