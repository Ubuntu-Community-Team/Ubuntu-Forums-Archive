### Post by bobnutfield on 2008-09-07
What you are talking about is a dual boot setup, which covered extremely extensively on this forum and on the net ingeneral.  You must first prepare a separate partition for Ubuntu, or do it during the installation process, and the installation process will take care of the dual boot with Windows.  READ EXTENSIVELY befoe you attempt this as a mistake could damage your Windows installation.  However, it is very easy and the instructions are straightforward.  Your most important task (and the most risky for newcomers to dual booting) is the partitioning process.  The Ubuntu installation process will take care of this for you, but be sure you know what you are doing first.

You will need to use virtualization software.

I suggest you use Virtual Box.

Note, google "Install Ubuntu in Virtualbox". You'll need something special I believe called guest additions. Guides should cover that.
--

Note that this will slow down your computer. I wouldn't do it if I had less than 1gb of ram memory.

--

You can have two "real" operating systems on your HDD.

Google "dual boot Windows Ubuntu". But this will only allow you to run one at the time. 

If you are thinking about using Windows for Gaming, dual boot is your only option. Gaming on Virtual Box or other programs like that isn't possible (well, real gaming isn't).
