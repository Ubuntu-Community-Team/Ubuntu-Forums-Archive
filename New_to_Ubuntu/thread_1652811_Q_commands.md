### Post by Trandre on 2010-12-25
In a manual I'm reading it is referred to what versions of different software I have. I see that sometimes it says: 
"program" --version and somtimes it says: "program" -V

Is this the same command?

---

### Post by TeoBigusGeekus on 2010-12-25
Double dash+option (--version) is BSD notation.
Single dash+option (-V) is Linux notation.
They are of course the same command.

### Post by TeoBigusGeekus on 2010-12-25
There's no core notation.
Both BSD and Linux have developed from Unix and they follow their own routes.
You could say that there are two core notations:BSD's and Linux's. :D
Programmers usually take care to implement both notations for compatibility in both systems.
Personally, I prefer linux notation when I'm lazy (most of the times) and bsd notation on long commands with a lot of switches and options for clarity.
