Best I can tell, and I'm no expert but, it seems obvious that this is nothing more than a security mechanism, basically preventing anyone who is unauthorized to access the computer from making changes that impact the entire system.  If you notice anytime there is an update of any sort, it will ask for your password because these updates affect the entire system (just like the periodic automatic Windows updates do....well, I guess they still do, don't use it much anymore, .... oh but wait, they don't require a password, damn!  So anyone can authorize them).  I really like it because it gives me the chance to actually say if I want what is being downloaded.  When the screen goes black, my experience has been that its due to inactivity.  I usually just touch the mouse pad on my netbook before it goes completely black.  There's probably a way to adjust that, it just hasn't been problematic for me.  Oh yeah, by the way, if I were you, I sure as hell wouldn't pay Microsoft for a new OS and license.
