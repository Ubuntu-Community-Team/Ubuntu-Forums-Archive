### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-05-20
Any machine that runs Windows 10 capably will run any Linux distribution more than capably because, as you have noticed, the hardware requirements of Linux/Ubuntu are much much less than the Windows hardware requirements. This has always been the case.

The hardware & the operating system are just two parts of the calculation. Another consideration is the use we want to put the machine to. Typing? The hardware/OS combination has been capable of typing for decades.

The story is different when it comes to games but the OS is not so much the problem as is the availability of quality games when compared to the Windows platform. There is more money to be made selling Windows games than Linux games. That is the realistic truth.

If you are serious about installing Ubuntu then you must read this.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UEFI](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UEFI)

A lot of the problems we see on this forum come from people who have not followed the guidelines in the Wiki page.

Regards.
