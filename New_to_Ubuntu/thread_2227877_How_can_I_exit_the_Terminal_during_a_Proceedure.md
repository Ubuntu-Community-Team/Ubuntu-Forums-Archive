### Post by Jay_E on 2014-06-04
Hi there,
  If the application does not respond to Ctrl+c ,you can use the system monitor.
If you don't have it, it is in package: gnome-system-monitor
The processes tab will give a list of running processes.
Clicking on "Process Name" will sort the list alphabetically, toggling between ascending and descending order.
Find your 'stuck' process and select it.
Down on the bottom right is an "End Process" button. I always double check before ending the process.
There may be a secondary are-you-sure? type of message.

As in above posts, cntrl+d or typing 'exit' in the window should then close it.

Hope this helps.
Jay
