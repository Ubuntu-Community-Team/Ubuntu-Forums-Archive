### Post by cariboo on 2009-03-28
You could use something like [Partimage]("http:///www.partimage.org/Main_Page") to transfer the contents of you old hard drive to your new one. Partimage is in the repositories. The good thing about partimage, is that it only transfers the data and not any empty space you may have. I would suggest putting the new drive in an external enclosure and doing the transfer, then exchange the drives.

### Post by scratman on 2009-03-28
Would it not be easier you just keep the old drive inside your machine, and put the new drive in the caddy.  That way you'll have a 500Gb mobile hard drive, which is handy.  You could transfer all of the media to the new HDD, and that way you'd free up a load of space.  Put all of your music and video onto an external machine, and then you can take it on holiday, or to a party at your friends house....

It's that I did.  Keep the Windows install on a machine that it's happy with, because if you upset it, it can be very difficult to recover, and free up space by making your media portable.  It also means that if your older HDD fails, your precious data will be on the newer drive.

### Post by Son of William on 2009-03-28
I agree with Scratman - if you keep your data on a separate, portable, hard drive you gain a lot of advantages.  

Admittedly, applications don't work well on a separate drive but 160 Gb should be enought to run Windows and Ubuntu with a pretty extensive set of installed applications.

If your application needs really dictate that you need a bigger "in the laptop" drive, I would recommend simply backing up your data and configuration preferences and reinstalling both Ubuntu and Windows.  Windows often benefits from a periodic clean reinstall.
