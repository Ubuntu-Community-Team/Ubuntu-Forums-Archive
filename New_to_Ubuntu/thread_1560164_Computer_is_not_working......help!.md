### Post by Zorgoth on 2010-08-24
I haven't used Windows for a long time now...

You can recover your files with an Ubuntu live CD.  Just pop it in, restart (you may need to press something like F2, F10, or F12 as your computer boots up to boot from CD rather than Hard Drive), and select the option to "Try without any changes to your computer" or maybe to "boot from cd" or something like that (not the install option - if it tries to install Ubuntu cancel).  Then you will be able to mount and use all the files on your hard drive.  Plug in a USB stick or external drive and you can copy your files right off.

I know you said it's been working fine, but PLEASE, PLEASE do not hook a Windows ME machine to the internet.  You are a virus magnet and a keylogger could easily get onto your system and get your passwords.  One bad click and your security is compromised with that old of an OS.

### Post by Silent Warrior on 2010-08-24
Don't panic yet: Have a look at distrowatch.com, and you'll see you can order CDs of various distributions. It'll cost a small penny, of course, but you'll get a CD without having to download and burn anything. Or you could ask a friend/neighbour to download and burn for you. As for what distribution, this is of course the best place to get instruction for Ubuntu and its variants. I've heard Knoppix is engineered more to your present needs than Ubuntu is, though, so I certainly won't advice against picking that one. Good luck.

Oh, you may also find LiveCDs at some libraries or other - or you could buy a Linux magazine, though that would risk getting a DVD rather than a CD. (I take it your system is too old to have a DVD-drive, is that the case?) If there is a local gatheringplace for open-source things, they're sure to have a few lying about. I happen to know a place in Gothenburg myself where they have openSUSE and Ubuntu discs, but I suppose that's not where you're at.
