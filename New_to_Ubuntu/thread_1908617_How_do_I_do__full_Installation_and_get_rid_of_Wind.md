First, backup all your files.

### Post by mörgæs on 2012-01-13
In a live boot you can open Gparted and delete the Windows partition(s). 
After that you can expand the Ubuntu /home partition (or / if you don't have a separate /home) into the new space.

Remember to have a backup of your data.

### Post by Rainbow_Dash on 2012-01-13
To backup your files you'll need an external hard drive, or a very large USB stick then you just copy those files over.

To install over your Windows installation, you'll just have to leave the LiveCD in the disc tray and reboot your computer. You may have to change the boot order in your BIOS, but I think that's only for USB sticks.

### Post by mörgæs on 2012-01-13
Please take care to use the correct terms.

Dual boot: Ubuntu and Windows installed side by side.
Wubi: Ubuntu installed in Windows.
