### Post by Rubi1200 on 2010-07-07
> **davidwroe said:**
> Can anyone please tll me what (if any) is the advantage of installing ubuntu 10.04 side by side with XP.  I dont understand the significance of side by side as opposed to a straight download to a new partition.    magoo

It all depends on your setup.

Let's say, for example, you have an 80GB hard-drive with Windows XP. If you choose to install side-by-side, Ubuntu will shrink the partition and take 40GB for itself.

Alternatively, you manually edit your drive, giving Ubuntu, for example, 20GB and leaving XP with 60, installing Ubuntu into the "largest free space/continuous space." (I forgot the exact wording)

Just know that if you created a separate partition for Ubuntu and still choose the side-by-side option during installation that Ubuntu will install as I described above and you will still have your free partition that you created!

The following links have more information:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowtoPartition](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowtoPartition)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DualBoot/Windows](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DualBoot/Windows)
[http://members.iinet.net/~herman546/index.html]("http://members.iinet.net/%7Eherman546/index.html")
