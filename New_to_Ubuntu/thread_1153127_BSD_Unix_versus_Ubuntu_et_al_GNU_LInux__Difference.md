### Post by Niniel on 2009-05-08
Old-school BSDers scoff at Linux because it's not a real operating system in their minds - just a kernel with a bunch of tools thrown in, which can vary by distribution. BSDs, on the other hand, include all those tools as part of the OS. 
Plus they tend to swear by their ports system, whereby all software can be built easily from source via a ports tree (that is actually very cool).
Some may also say that it's more secure (OpenBSD) and runs on more hardware (NetBSD).
In general, Linux seems to be a bit more "cutting edge", although FreeBSD at least is also pretty up to date (my impression).
