### Post by undecim on 2010-03-12
There is a common saying: Physical access is root access.

In other words, if someone has physical access to your computer, your password isn't going to stop them from getting in if they want. Resetting a password is trivial and only useful for stopping children, internet attacks, and honest people.

Disabling your password isn't going to make your computer any less secure. You will still need your password for administrative tasks (without using recovery mode)

### Post by undecim on 2010-03-16
> **ray33 said:**
> Thanks for the answers. Just to understand this correctly, regarding the internet attacks, does disabling the log in password in any sense make my computer less secure from these? I close my internet connection when I am not logged in myself.

Disabling your login password has zero effect on security. Anyone who might be able to (for example) use a firefox bug to gain access to your system will still need your password to do any administrative tasks.

The ONLY insecure thing that disabling your login password will do is make it easier for computer/linux illiterate users to get on your physical desktop without guessing your password. It simply bypasses the requirement that you use your password when you first start your computer.
