### Post by gmoney on 2008-07-29
I agree with the above posts.  Cairo-clock is pretty slick.  Here's a couple of links with more information:

[http://tombuntu.com/index.php/2007/10/30/cairo-clock-on-ubuntu-710/](http://tombuntu.com/index.php/2007/10/30/cairo-clock-on-ubuntu-710/)

[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AqpkD_PWWfk](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AqpkD_PWWfk)

It should be noted that, in order to run cairo-clock properly, you need to have desktop effects enabled.  It will still run without desktop effects, but it doesn't look nearly as good.

If you don't have desktop effects, you could also try out buici-clock.  It's not as full featured as cairo-clock, but for those people who may have an older graphics card (or are having trouble setting up desktop effects), it is a simple digital clock.
