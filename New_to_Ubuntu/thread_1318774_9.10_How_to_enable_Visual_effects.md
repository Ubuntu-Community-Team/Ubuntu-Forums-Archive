### Post by tacantara on 2009-11-07
Did you enable Ubuntu's advanced graphic settings?  If not, right-click the desktop, and select Change Desktop Background from the pop-up menu.  That will open the Appearance Preferences box.  Click on the Visual Effects tab, and select either Normal or Extra, then close the box.  That should allow you to go crazy with the Compiz settings.

### Post by tacantara on 2009-11-07
I've never attempted to do this from the terminal, so I'm not familiar with any command.  By right-clicking the desktop, you can do several things:  change wallpaper, change window dressing and icon themes, and set the graphics level (by default it is set to None, you can choose Normal or Extra, depending on how well your graphics card works with the default drivers in the kernel).  It may be an issue of your graphics card.  Have you checked the hardware drivers (System > Administration > Hardware Drivers) to see if you have any proprietary drivers available for graphics?
