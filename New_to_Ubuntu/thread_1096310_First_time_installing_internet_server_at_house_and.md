### Post by Temposs on 2009-03-14
I'm not sure, but I know it probably depends on how many people in surrounding houses and such are using using the connection heavily. It'll be enough for watching videos on Hulu, though :-) Basically, most things on the web will go smoothly at that speed.

If you connect with Ubuntu, you won't use any software they give you with your connection kit or whatever they give you. You'll just use the network manager app in the top right of the Ubuntu desktop to enter any info your need for your connection, if needed.

Oh, not sure if it applies to your ISP, but if the ISP says you need to 'activate' your connection with a piece of Windows-only software they give you on a CD(some ISPs do this, but not all), and you're not able to connect until the connection is activated, just call their customer service and say your CD drive isn't functioning properly, and they'll manually activate your connection right then. Then you should just be able to use Network Manager to connect.

A helpful hint: it's not a good idea to tell customer service that you're using Ubuntu/Linux, because they'll either tell you (at worst) that their internet connection isn't compatible with Linux(that's not true, though), or (at best) they'll say they don't support connecting with Linux. But really, they just don't want to pay to train their customer service in how to deal with Linux users, and their connection will work just fine with Ubuntu/Linux.
