### Post by tuxsheadache on 2008-11-12
If you live in the UK I personally recommend [http://microdirect.co.uk]("http://microdirect.co.uk"), they do some amazing deals and generally have good prices. Always shop around though, I got my graphics card on special offer off [http://play.com]("http://play.com").
I once upgraded my Dell PC, but came across quite a few problems e.g. the heatsink fixes on a Dell motherboard are special, so I could only use their provided heatsink. 
A new motherboard is one of most important parts, as is power supply.

The power supply is also important since most computer failures are linked to it. I found a 500W one for £15, which said it supported SATA. It only had one, so I had to choose between HDD or Optical Drive, and it was very loud.
Moral to story: if it seems too cheap, there will be a good reason for it.

### Post by igknighted on 2008-11-13
New mobo = new processor = new ram (almost certainly).  That is what I would do, because that Pentium D (while decently fast) is an energy hog and likely runs super hot (which will reduce the life of your whole system).  By getting a modern, more power efficient processor, your system will be faster and cooler, and should last much longer.

I would recommend an Intel Core2Duo.  They are the best architecture out there.  Sure you can brute force some of the quad core AMD chips to compare to the dual core intels, but why?  And this is coming from a LONG time AMD fanboy.  Even I bit the bullet and got a C2D.  The C2D's should be going on sale as the new i7 chips are coming out too.

### Post by tuxsheadache on 2008-11-13
I think the general idea is that you could upgrade your dell, but you may be liable to problems along the way caused by it.
A motherboard will not speed the computer up much at all, but it's the start of building a new computer, so is very important and should be bought first, since all the other computer parts are going into it.

For example, I bought an Asus M3A32MVP Deluxe, because it has AM2/AM2+ support, and 4 PCI-E slots. I have an AMD 6400 processor, which only needs AM2 processor socket, and I only have one graphics card, but it means I am ready in case I want to buy an AM2+ quad core processor, or if i want to buy more than one graphics card.

It all depends how much of an upgrade you want :)
If you are in to gaming, you may only need a faster graphics card!
Wait for a few more people to reply before acting, and decide the best thing for you/your computer/ your wallet.
