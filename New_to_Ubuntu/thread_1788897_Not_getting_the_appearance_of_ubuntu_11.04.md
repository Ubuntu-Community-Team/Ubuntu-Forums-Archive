Hi,

It's not that GNOME is better or worse than KDE, it's a matter of "Personal Opinion", that's all :)

I suggest to have  a look at these links:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Window_manager](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Window_manager)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kde](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kde)
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome)

And always remember that Google is the best source of information and the fastest too :)

After you finish reading some basic stuff, you can do one thing. Download both Ubuntu 11.04 (or 10.04) and Kubuntu 11.04 (or 10.04), burn the iso file to a CD (create a LiveCD or ***LiveUSB*** - it's faster than LiveCD), reboot your machine and boot from the LiveCD or LiveUSB.
Do NOT install, just run the Live Session and see whatever works for you and you may like :)

LiveCD/USB feature in Linux does not exist in Windows as far as I know. You don't have to install anything, you can "Try without installation" which is a very good thing.

My other advice is: Keep reading as long as you're new to this world and don't panic or feel bad, we were and still learning :)
Anything you don't understand, search it on google and if you did not find an answer, come back to this forum and everyone here will be more than glad to help :)

Please don't hesitate to ask about anything in your mind :)

Good Luck!
