### Post by lindsay7 on 2010-12-09
Well, with an older laptop it is hard to say if your wireless card will be able to be configured to work or not. Sometimes it takes a lot of effort to work on this and it may be to no avail.  I have found that, if you have a few dollars to spend and want to short circuit this process, it may be best to just buy a wireless card or usd device that just works with linux.  If you are so inclined, take a look at the Edimax line of products, they seem to work out of the box with linux and are inexpensive.  Look at Newegg or Tiger direct on the internet.

### Post by khelben1979 on 2010-12-10
Are you on a 32 or 64 bit Ubuntu system? 

In most cases, the hardware support which comes with Ubuntu is within the Linux kernel. With the modprobe command you can activate modules from the kernel which isn't run after Ubuntu have been loaded.
