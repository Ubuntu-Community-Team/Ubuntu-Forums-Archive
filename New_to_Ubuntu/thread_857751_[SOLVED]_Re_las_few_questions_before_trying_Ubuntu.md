### Post by gate on 2008-07-12
About buying Ubuntu: consider looking into your local Unix and Linux user groups. They might have an installfest soon or one of them will almost definitely have a copy of the disk.

For security: few/no viruses. Excrypted HDDs. (I use this on about half my systems)

Software compatibility: You can use anything from a virtual machine to Wine (emulation software) to run your app. I can't say what works since I don't know the software.

radio: what is the question here? will it work? I know there is internet radio on linux but I don't ever use it.

Yes. Microsoft is dominant. Its an ugly reality. That dominance has its costs and benefits. I personally think the costs outweigh the benefits. This has become very apparent with Vista, IE7 and Office2007, hence "alternative" operating systems are beginning to gain market share on the desktop.

Office: what is the question here? I really don't like MS office (or any other office application for that matter). Open Office is respectable, but I only use it when I absolutely must.


There are many reasons to switch to linux. I love how it works and the fact that I can literally change it however I want. Security is a bonus. I have a computer I keep virtual machines of Windows when I need to use it.
My suggestion: try it out somehow. Linux users groups will often be glad to show it to you and let you play around with whatever flavor they use.

And: I suggest researching alternative ISPs unless you are on Dial-up. A 1GB cap is preposterous on broadband.
