### Post by jkmerr on 2011-09-14
Basically, you don't. Low pass filtering fonts haven't been effective soft TEMPEST methods for a while, if there ever really was an effective soft TEMPEST measure. There used to be a weird low-pass filtering font package floating around out there, but I don't know if it was ever really tested for TEMPEST compliance or available for Linux anyway. 

Also, unless you're just playing tin-foil-hat for fun, TEMPEST isn't really a DIY appropriate project; if whatever you're doing is high risk enough that somebody coming up to you and electronically eavesdropping on your monitor from the next apartment is really a problem you might want to think about hiring an IT security professional with relevant experience.
