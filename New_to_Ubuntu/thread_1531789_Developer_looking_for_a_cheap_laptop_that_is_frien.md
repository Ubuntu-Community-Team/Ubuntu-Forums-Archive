### Post by QIII on 2010-07-15
> **tom.swartz07 said:**
> AVOID ATI AT ALL COSTS THOUGH!! Their  graphics and video card drivers are virtually useless.

Is that why they work perfectly fine for me?

The myth persists, and I have to keep dispelling it.

When ATI was ATI -- before they were bought by AMD and became AMD/ATI -- this was true.

In 2007 AMD made a commitment to Linux and turned that around.

AMD seems to have a particular sweet spot for Ubuntu, as a matter of fact.  With each of the last several versions of Ubuntu, AMD/ATI has made their latest drivers available in the repos by release time.  The drivers are available to Ubuntu before they are even available to general release.

It's really time to stop the AMD/ATI bashing.  It is no longer valid.

If you look through the forums, you will find people having problems with particular cards.  But you will also find people having trouble with particular nVidia cards.

And both companies have "legacied" a number of older cards, leaving people with the open source drivers as their only choice.

If you don't believe me, try running a GeForce2 MX400 nVidia card in anything after Intrepid.

I have machines running Intel, nVidia and AMD/ATI cards and drivers.  They all work perfectly fine.

### Post by QIII on 2010-07-15
> **mandazi said:**
> AMD laptops are usually fairly cheaper than Intel laptops.  Do you recommend any specific model and make with AMD?

The question about ATI was with regard to their graphics cards.

With regard to processors...

AMD is certainly less expensive than Intel.  However, Intel processors use smaller (32nm, I think) processes as opposed to AMD's (45nm, I think).  Intel processors yield faster performance, but they cost more.  AMD's processors are slower, so they cost less.  So it's a cost/benefit equation you'll have to consider.  Also, it has been my experience that my AMD machines tend (but this can't be taken as absolute.  Your mileage may vary) to run slightly hotter than my Intel machines.  No biggie for me, since I put after-market cooling on my desktops anyway.  I generally go with AMD machines.

Dell offers machines pre-loaded with Ubuntu.  I don't know what range/types of processors/graphics you might find.

There is also System76, which produces machines with only Ubuntu installed.  Again, I'm not sure what hardware each model may use.  System76 machines tend toward higher prices, but you'd be sure Ubuntu was well integrated.  I think the Pangolin laptop is pretty popular.


Edit:  Just checked on the Pangolin.  They are popular enough that they are currently out of stock!

Edit 2:  Just happened to think.  Geeks.com offers a lot of refurb machines, some without OSs so they can be fairly inexpensive.  See what they have and do some research to see if anyone here is having problems with that particular model and configuration.
