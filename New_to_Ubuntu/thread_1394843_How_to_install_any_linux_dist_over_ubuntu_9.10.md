### Post by RTrev on 2010-01-31
> **dinw3 said:**
> How to partition the files ? i am afraid that i do something wrong and my files go forever .

Oh, you don't have your data backed up?  You want to replace the OS with another one, but leave your data intact?

I'd start by buying an external USB hard drive.  Copy the stuff you want to keep up to the USB drive, unplug it, and put it somewhere safe.  Copy critical things to DVDs also.  Never trust a single device with things that matter.

Now just do the install.  Let it over-write Ubuntu.  When it's up and running the way you want, copy the stuff back from the USB drive.

When you're done with this, use the USB drive for routine backups.  I got one 500G Seagate back when CompUSA was having sales, and it's been indispensable ever since.

For a more long range plan, consider multiple internal disks.  A small and fast (perhaps solid state) drive as the system disk, and a couple of big drives 500G or 1T or whatever you need, running RAID-1 and used for just your data.

HTH
