### Post by clhsharky on 2010-05-03
HI DREvans

I think it all depends.

Is your system running well, I would upgrade.

Is your system troublesome. 
I would clean install. Why take a chance of bring forward trouble.

Is your system highly modified, like 'a lots of PPAs and apps not from respiratory's'. 
I would clean install. Incorrect PPAs and config files might be left behind or troublesome.

Did you upgrade to 9.10?
I would clean install. You might be missing some features like ext4.

I would also wait about two weeks to upgrade.
To give devs time to straiten out any wrinkles. These guys are on a tight schedule and bugs may not get fixed on time.

If you upgrade, you can always do a fresh install latter.

Sharky

### Post by warfacegod on 2010-05-03
> **mastablasta said:**
> Out of curiousity - why is this not done default by Ubuntu? Or at least you could get asked if you want to do it when installing. Partition for data only is always a good idea.

Repartitioning and/or resizing an HDD is a major operation. Doing this as a default is a very bad idea.

The idea is to make the install process as painless as possible for new guys. Asking about it could needlessly complicate things for those that are computer and/or Linux illiterate.

Although, in theory, I agree. I wish I'd known about it and all of the advantages when I first started with Linux, about three years ago.
