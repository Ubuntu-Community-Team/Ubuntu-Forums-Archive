### Post by m_duck on 2009-02-14
To move the panels, right click some free space on them and click "Allow to be moved", then just click and drag the panel to where you want it. If you want to (say) move the workspace switcher to the top panel, right click it, uncheck "Lock to panel", right click again and click "Move" then move your mouse to where you want it to be then left click to place it. After, right click it again and re check "Lock to panel". To delete items on your unwanted panel, right click and select "Remove this item". Finally, to remove a panel completely, right click and "delete this panel". At least one panel must remain.

For Firefox on the eee, look for the "tiny menu" addon which reduces the File, Edit, View etc buttons to just "Menu". Then you can move the address bar etc up to the same line as the menu is and effectively lose a row which is taking up screen space. Sorry that part doesn't make a lot of sense...

### Post by JustANewb on 2009-02-14
Right click on them and you can change the properties, the location of them (by choosing Allow panel to be moved and you can even add more functions to it.  I use just one bar on the top that has everything I need.  The Main Menu button is much more functional than the Menu bar (imo) and will also help save screen real estate.

You might also look into a dock or something like AWN or Cairo Dock that can have more options and hide itself under maximized windows.
