### Post by Orion8 on 2009-02-28
In general it is really tough to correct photos like you describe.  If you just want to view them on-line it may be possible to make them sharper by scaling them much smaller, then sharpening (Gimp does this just fine).  Sometimes this can hide small amounts of blur.  But really there is a good chance the picture are going to stay the way they are.  I have a lot of photography (and PhotoShop) experience and have found that the best thing to do is take pictures that are not blurry! (Easier said than done, I know.)  Set the ISO higher, use a larger aperture, or make sure you have better natural light next time.  In the meantime, good luck with the pictures you have.  Anything you try is liable to be labor intensive and not all that satisfactory.  Oh -- one more thing you could try -- sometimes you can disguise blurriness by doing something "artsy" with the photos.  Change to BW, add noise / grain etc...  But again it is iffy.
