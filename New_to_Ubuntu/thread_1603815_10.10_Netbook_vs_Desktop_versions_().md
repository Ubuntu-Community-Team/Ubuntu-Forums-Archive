### Post by mikewhatever on 2010-10-23
The netbook and the desktop editions are identical, except for a few packages that make up the netbook interface. What you use in both cases is Ubuntu.

---

### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-10-23
The netbook edition just omits packages that netbooks would not really need, eg brasero for CD/DVD writing or burning;  why have it with no CD drive?

Also compiz is missing, as the power to run compiz is probably better used on other work, and I doubt you will miss it on a small screen anyway.

Just choose the gnome interface instead of Netbook at the session menu of the login screen, and you should have everything you need.  No need to install ubuntu-desktop package at all.
