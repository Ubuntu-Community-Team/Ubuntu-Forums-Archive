### Post by Layvian on 2011-01-20
I have had to install Ubuntu a number of times on many times and makes of Personal Computers.  So maybe I might be able to help you.

Try following these steps:


[LIST]
[*]Update all Software Packages [System > Administration > Update Manager]
[*]Check for Restricted Drivers [System > Administration > Additional Drivers]
[*]Unmute Sounds(Sound is Muted my Default on Boot Up.)
[*]Check Dell for Linux Drivers(If all else Fails.)
[/LIST]
Those are the usual way to get any driver working that doesn't at start-up.  Also if you are using a Motherboard Sound Chip-set check that you have not turned this off in BIOS my accident, my motherboard is an Asus, and I am able to turn On/Off the Motherboard Chipsets from BIOS.

Also as far as your touchpad goes, check in the Mouse Options[System > Preferences > Mouse] that your settings are turn how you like them.  It is possible the Restricted Drivers may pop-up for them as well.

I hope it is simply one of those issues, and I wish you the best of luck, please let me know if this isn't the case.  Once you get it working we could put together a How-To for your system, if other have issues.

Good Luck,

Layvian
