### Post by beew on 2010-11-23
> **igotsanevo4g said:**
> I cant find that setting on ubuntu 10.10, i have 512mb's of ram, and what do you mean by video isnt installed? I installed everything that i was prompted too + i searched "restricted" in the software center and installed the proprietary too.

Maybe you just don't have enough ram. What resolution do you try to play full screen? Your graphic card is probably old and doesn't support GPU acceleration in Linux so it also uses a lot more cpu than your machine can handle.

For the slow loading I found one thing that works on my old machine with 512mb of ram Go to Youtube, open a video, right click for settings and disable hardware accelaration and change the amount of information the site can store in your computer to minimum (10k)

Flash apparently doesn't work well with Linux, there are many tweaks on the forum but the only thing that works on this old machine of mine is to not use flash all together, install the excellent flashvideoreplacer addon to firefox (created by forum member LovingLinux) and the gekco player from the repo, it works magic.  You can also install minitube, it is also good for watching youtube without flash.
