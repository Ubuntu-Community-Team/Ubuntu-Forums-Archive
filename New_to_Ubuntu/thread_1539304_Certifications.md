### Post by Locke_99GS on 2010-07-26
A Bachelors degree in Computer Science from a credible university will likely be the most profitable to your situation. After which, there are a variety of certifications you can acquire to show expertise in a specialized field. Some certification will bear more weight with a prospective employer than others. The LPIC is inexpensive and a good certification to have. RHCE is widely renowned as one of the best to have. Other certifications may not be *nix specific, but do show specific knowledge such as Cisco certification. 

Don't forget that most employers use Microsoft products on the workstation, and having one (or many) of the various Microsoft certifications will prove to show that you are useful outside of the computer room, where much of the IT work actually exists. (troubleshooting basic workstation issues on the user's end)
