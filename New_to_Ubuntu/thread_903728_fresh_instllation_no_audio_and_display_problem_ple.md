### Post by leetrefz on 2008-08-28
the graphic drivers can be seen in synaptic. They are the packages named xserver-xorg-video-x, where x is the name of the driver, for example, xserver-xorg-video-vesa. I suggest you make sure you know what graphics chip you have, and search synaptic for packages starting xserver-xorg-video-. You can see in their descriptions what chips they're used for. The one for your chip maybe already installed. Once it is, go to the terminal & type: sudo mousepad /etc/X11/xorg.conf 
Remember, it's case-sensitive. In the second half of the file you can find the line: 
Section "Device"
under which is a line that looks like:
Driver "name-of-the-graphic-driver-you're using"
You can replace that with the name of the driver for your chip. So if it says
Driver "vga" 
change it to 
Driver "radeonhd" 
if you found the xserver-xorg-video-radeonhd package matches your chip.
