### Post by zephyrcat on 2009-01-25
The method proposed by Stalker72 is not a true downgrade. It will just install 8.04 over 8.10, destroying any files you don't back up. (If you have your home folder in its own partition, this is a different story.) You might want to look at this page:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DowngradeHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DowngradeHowto)

You may still be better off backing up your files and reinstalling, though.

OK. In that case, just do what Stalker72 said. Put in the CD, reboot the PC, and install 8.04 the way you installed 8.10. If the live CD does not come up, you will have to press a key to get in to your system's BIOS and change the boot order.
