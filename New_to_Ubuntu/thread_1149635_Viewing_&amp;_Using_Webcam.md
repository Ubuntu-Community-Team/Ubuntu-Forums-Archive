### Post by Hospadar on 2009-05-05
Well first thing to check would be to make sure that your own webcam is actually working.  The quickest way to do this is open up ekiga (it's in Applications->Networking) and there's an option somewhere around in there to see the output of your own camera.

If your camera doesn't appear to be working, one of two things is happening:
a) it's just not supported yet.  the v4l project (video for linux) writes most of the linux camera and video capture drivers, you might want to check their website and see if you can locate any information about your camera or it's working

b) it is supported, but maybe something is wrongly configured.  Google around for info about your camera, you may turn up something, maybe not.  If you can't get it working at all, your best bet may be to just locate a different camera that does work (they arn't too expensive) and pawn yours.

If you find that your camera does work with some programs, but not the ones you want, you may just need to switch to a different communication program.  You can use ekiga or (i believe) skype, both are free and multi-platform, and I suspect there are others I don't know about.
