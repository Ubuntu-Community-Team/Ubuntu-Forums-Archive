Hi, folks.  I've contacted Ubuntu via the "Ship It" form on their site. 

Allow me to explain why I'm posting here.  

I'm a cartoonist and I'm using Linux in my studio to create my cartoon features. I also have a Linux advocacy blog at [www.oneclicklinux.com](www.oneclicklinux.com).

I recently became involved with a very worthwhile group called Toon-Ed ([http://www.toon-ed.com/](http://www.toon-ed.com/)).

Toon-Ed is having an exhibit and awards weekend in April.  They will also be conducting cartooning classes.  I immediately suggested Linux! I suggested that we obtain Linux disks, introduce it to the attendees (students and teaches alike) and get them involved using Ubuntu (and Linux).

I'm hoping Ubuntu can provide 200 disks to give to those attending the classes.  I'm posting this here to get input from the community.  Is there anything else we can do to promote the use of Ubuntu and Linux in cartooning and the classroom?

### Post by Temposs on 2009-02-18
Another nice cartooning program for Ubuntu is called Synfig. You can do still cartoonish drawings with them, but also very nice animations. I'm not a visual artist, but my wife is, so I look into this kind of thing.

[http://www.synfig.org/Main_Page](http://www.synfig.org/Main_Page)

A note: the version of Synfig in the repositories for Ubuntu 8.10 doesn't actually work, and that's a known issue. They have instructions on their site for installing onto Ubuntu 8.10:

[http://synfig.org/forums/viewtopic.php?f=13&t=277](http://synfig.org/forums/viewtopic.php?f=13&t=277)

There is a link on there to a couple debs that work:
[http://download.tuxfamily.org/morevna/morevnapackage/binaries/](http://download.tuxfamily.org/morevna/morevnapackage/binaries/)
