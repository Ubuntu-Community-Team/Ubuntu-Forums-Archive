### Post by mcduck on 2009-01-09
Just grab the panel applets with middle mouse button and drag them to where ever you want them to be. (Or right-click & select "Move" if you don't like middle-clicking.

If some applet refuses to move right-click it and make sure the "Lock to panel"-option isn't selected.

### Post by mcduck on 2009-01-09
Ole hyvä! :)

That has happened to me a couple of times, usually when some program (like a full-screen game, for example) changes the display resolution to smaller than your default. Although I really can't remember any Linux audio application that would mess with your resolution.

You said that some program caused your desktop to crash, most likely something just got a bit messed up because of that and caused the panel applets to load in different order than usually.

If you want, it is possible to lock the panels so the applets can't move and cannot be changed or removed, but since they are so easy to move back where you want them to be I suggest just leaving the panels unlocked.
