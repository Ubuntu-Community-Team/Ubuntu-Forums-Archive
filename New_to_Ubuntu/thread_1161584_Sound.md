### Post by Dngrsone on 2009-05-17
On most systems, the headphone jack has a built-in switch that disables the external speakers on the computer when headphones are plugged in.  I don't know if your computer is an exception to this general rule, but I'm thinking it isn't.  In order to get both speakers and headphones to work (which would load down your audio amplifiers), you will most likely have to bypass the built-in switch or use an external amplifier setup with a monitor jack (which should allow you to plug in headphones while still pushing sound through the attached speakers).
