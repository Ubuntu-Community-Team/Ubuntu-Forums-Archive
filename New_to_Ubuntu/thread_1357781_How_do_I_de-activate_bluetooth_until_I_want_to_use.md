Hi all,

I installed bluetooth a couple of months ago but since then my mobile phone has been through the wash twice. It didn't recover the second time.
Until I get another phone I don't really need the Bluetooth to kick in at startup like it does so I thought I would disable it.

I haven't got a clue how to do that.

I've left clicked and right clicked on the icon but can't see anything to disable it. There's nothing in apps column either. I don't want to uninstall it as my friends occasionally download pics from their phones to keep me posted whilst I'm getting well again.

Anyone know how I can disable it at startup and then run it when I want to please?

OS is Ubuntu 8.04 when I first installed it. I don't know if the updates have changed it to something else, I certainly haven't.

### Post by sydbat on 2009-12-17
System > Administration > Services. You'll have to enter your password, then uncheck Bluetooth.

Also, you might be able to do the same thing under System > Preferences > Sessions.
