### Post by newb85 on 2015-03-30
The biggest problem I have with books is that they go in the direction the author wants to go.  If books are your method of choice, start with one that is basic, and not very exhaustive.  (Not the 400+ page book that looks nice on the bookstore shelf.)  From there, look for books on the areas you feel you still need to learn.

Some other things to watch out for (books or otherwise) is how dated the information you're getting is, as well as what system it's written for.  A 5-year-old tutorial is sometimes problematic (though not always).  A tutorial that asks you to pacman something won't be helpful, either.

If you see something you're not sure about, ask about it on the forums.
