### Post by grahammechanical on 2020-07-12
This is what the Ubuntu Desktop Guide says:

> [COLOR=#6C6C6C][FONT=sans-serif]**Connect to mobile broadband**
[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#3C3C3C][FONT=sans-serif]You can set up a connection to a cellular (3G) network with your computer’s built-in 3G modem, your mobile phone, or an Internet stick.

[LIST]
[*]If you do not have a built-in 3G modem, connect your phone or Internet stick to a USB port on your computer. 
[*]Open the [COLOR=#6C6C6C][system menu]("xref:shell-introduction#yourname")[/COLOR] from the right side of the top bar. 
[*]Select [COLOR=#6C6C6C][IMG]https://ubuntuforums.org/gnome-help/figures/network-cellular-signal-excellent-symbolic.svg[/IMG] Mobile Broadband Off[/COLOR]. The [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Mobile Broadband[/COLOR] section of the menu will expand.

If [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Mobile Broadband[/COLOR] does not appear in the system status menu, ensure that your device is not set to connect as Mass Storage. 
[*]Select [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Connect[/COLOR]. If you are connecting for the first time, the [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Set up a Mobile Broadband Connection[/COLOR] wizard is launched. The opening screen displays a list of required information. Click [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Next[/COLOR]. 
[*]Choose your provider’s country or region from the list. Click [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Next[/COLOR]. 
[*]Choose your provider from the list. Click [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Next[/COLOR]. 
[*]Select a plan according to the type of device you are connecting. This will determine the Access Point Name. Click [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Next[/COLOR]. 
[*]Confirm the settings you have selected by clicking [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Apply[/COLOR]. The wizard will close and the [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Network[/COLOR] panel will display the properties of your connection.[COLOR=#3C3C3C][FONT=sans-serif][/FONT][/COLOR]
[/LIST]

Some phones have a setting called *USB tethering* that requires no setup on the computer. When the setting is activated on the phone, the connection will show up as [COLOR=#6C6C6C]Ethernet Connected[/COLOR] in the system menu and [COLOR=#6C6C6C]USB ethernet[/COLOR] in the network panel.
[/FONT][/COLOR]
