### Post by Hungry Man on 2013-07-14
Understanding the theory is, in my opinion, much more important than memorizing specifics about specific tools.

ex:

Your class might teach you about the importance of 'least privilege', and reducing rights of some component (application, hardware, thread, etc).

You can then look for a specific tool, such as Apparmor (default on Ubuntu) that does this, and you'll understand *why* it's important, and how it works to secure your system.



Now, naturally, you can jump straight to the tools but it's a less natural approach in my opinion. But... see my signature for an 'ubuntu hardening guide' that'll cover a lot of tools for desktop systems if you'd like to go straight to the tools. If you want to do it backwards, just find tools, learn what they do, learn why they do it.
