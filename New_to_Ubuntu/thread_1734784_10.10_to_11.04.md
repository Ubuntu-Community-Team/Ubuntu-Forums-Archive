Thanks.

I will again remind you, 11.04 is not yet released. As with all Ubuntu releases, when it is ready , there will be detailed upgrade instructions and notes. I HIGHLY suggest you read them.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/upgrade](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/upgrade)

And on that page is a link to the release notes:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MaverickMeerkat/ReleaseNotes](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MaverickMeerkat/ReleaseNotes)

In order to upgrade, yes you will need to upgrade every package you have installed, which means a lot of bandwidth.

And, although upgrades usually go well, it could fail. Which then means you would have to download the iso and re-install.

What is your rush to upgrade ? I suggest you relax, drink some tea, and watch the forums to see what problems others have. After a few weeks or a month , then upgrade.

Thanks.

Download and burn a new 11.04 disc (or buy one from Canonical's shop, when it's released). Before installing it, remove as much superfluous software from your 10.10 as you can - anything that you don't need, that doesn't come preinstalled on Ubuntu.

Then when you run the installer, choose the Upgrade option. This will install a fresh Ubuntu with your current /home directory, and it will download and install new versions of whatever software you have on 10.10.
