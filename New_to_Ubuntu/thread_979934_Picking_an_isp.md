### Post by sdennie on 2008-11-12
Probably all ISPs will work but, the important thing is to get a modem that connects to your machine/router via ethernet and not via USB.  The USB modems don't always have good support on linux but, an ethernet one should always work.  

I've had problems in the past with the installation techs taking one look at my Ubuntu machine, and after being unable to find a "Start" button in the bottom left corner of the screen, gravely announce that my machine is incompatible with the service.  If they say that, your best course of action is to then say (even if it's not true), "I'm a system administrator.  I know how to configure it.  Leave me the modem and get out." (maybe more politely than that).  I've had to do that the last 3 times I've gotten an ADSL line installed here.  If you then can't configure it, google or these forums should be able to help.
