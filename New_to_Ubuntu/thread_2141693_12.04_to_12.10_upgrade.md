### Post by galgalesh on 2013-05-03
Hi Okietechnician

Ubuntu 12.04 is the Long Term Ssupport release which means 7 (i think) years of support. Every .04 release with an even number (10.04, 12.04) is an LTS release. Every other release has a much shorter support period.

If you want the newest stable ubuntu experience you should upgrade to 13.04 (the latest). If you like the LTS, you should stay at 12.04 until 14.04 is available.

I have no experience with upgrading so I can't help you on that topic.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-05-03
Just to clarify things a little bit. Ubuntu LTS support is 5 years not 7. That means that Ubuntu 12.04 still has 4 years to go. It already has had 2 point release upgrades with another 2 to come by the end of January 2014. So, if you was to download 12.04 today you would not get 12.04 but 12.04.2. A slight but important difference.

Ubuntu 12.10 has support for 18 months and so it will get you as far as the release date of Ubuntu 14.04 (LTS). But Ubuntu 13.04 only has support for 9 months. That means that you will have to upgrade to 13.10 (also 9 months support) and upgrade to 14.04.

As you have found out upgrades do not always go perfectly. This is why many of us recommend a fresh install. It is difficult to give you advice because you only give vague references to problems. You could re-install Wine. I have had Wine running on 12.04, 12.10 and now 13.04 without the issues that you talk about. Frankly "stalling" and "acting funny" are not symptoms that I recognise. And the problem could be with the Windows programs that you are trying to run under Wine and not with the OS at all.
