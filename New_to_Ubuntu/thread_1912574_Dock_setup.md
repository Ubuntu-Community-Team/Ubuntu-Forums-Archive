### Post by corncob on 2012-01-21
I may be wrong but I don't think you even need to install Gnome although it wouldn't hurt and might be better.  Go to the shutdown menu and select LOGOUT.  In the window where you log back in click on the cog wheel and select cairo-dock (instead of Ubuntu), enter your password and log in.  Cairo-dock should be the only thing on your screen (unless you have desktop icons).  From the application menu launcher you can start your universal dock.  Does this work for you?  If so, you could put the universal dock in with the startup applications.
