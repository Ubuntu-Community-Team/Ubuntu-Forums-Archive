### Post by lockalidiot on 2010-08-23
As said before, have you looked in to inkscape? It is a very similar program, but installing it on Ubuntu is much less of an hassle.
If you absolutely cannot live without corel and move on to inkscape you need to install a program called wine which will enable you to use some native windows applications on your ubuntu machine. With some applications this will be a massive hassle but can be done, some will go in more or less smoothly and some will not work, no matter what.
I strongly suggest that you take a good hard look on ubuntu native applications first and use wine with windows native applications only as the desperate last resort.
I am not suggesting this due fanaticism or anything like that, just to save your time and nerves. The new apps do take some learning, but it is still a lot better option.

### Post by Vaphell on 2010-08-24
Years of experience should be about the tricks you know and not about where that god damn option is buried in the menus. That's like people who refuse to use open office to redact documents or spreadsheets because they know MSOffice menus by heart. Tricks are easily transferable because they are based on basic abstract concepts present in majority/all apps of a given category.
You could as well spend 2 days to see how much of your playbook you can translate to inkscape and then decide. Both inkscape and corel are vector graphics apps and have probably at least 80% of common functionality right off the bat. If inkscape fails horribly then you are free to get your hands dirty with wine.
