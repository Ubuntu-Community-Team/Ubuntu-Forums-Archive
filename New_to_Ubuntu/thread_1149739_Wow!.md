### Post by seanmobrien on 2009-05-05
I am a software developer with over 10 years of experience - however, all of that experience has been on the Windows platform.  While I did install a copy of RedHat Linux once years ago, I quickly abandoned it in favor of Microsoft's server operating systems.  Due to an unexpected breach of contract by a client of mine, I found myself with a bit of time on my hands and decided to see if I could get Ubuntu running on an old laptop I had laying around.  Within 30 minutes, not only was my laptop running Ubuntu, I found myself connected to the internet using my Windows Mobile device - all wihtout having to spend any time messing around with drivers, figuring out the difference between wlon0, eth0, and /ect, or any other complications that I remembered keeping me away from Linux in the past.

I guess I just wanted to let the developers of this project know that I am VERY impressed with how far this operating system has come since I have last looked at it, and let everyone know that I am very excited to continue to explore this fantastic operating system.  Great job!

### Post by decoherence on 2009-05-05
Thanks for taking the time to let us know about your positive experience. We always like to hear that! BTW, RedHat has come a long way as well... you might check out Fedora or CentOS if you're feeling like experimenting.

Not everyone has as good luck with linux, as you'll see if you stick around for a while. Still, the community has done a lot to make Linux 'just work' most of the time. Not bad considering it's generally used as an after market OS on a huge range of wacky hardware.

What annoys me is when certain companies *coughDELLcough* ship Linux systems without properly testing them. As far as I'm concerned, these companies are currently the biggest threat to linux 'mind-share' -- after all, if linux doesn't work properly even when it's pre-installed by the OEM, what does that say about linux? (actually, it says nothing about linux and everything about the oem, but your average consumer won't know or care.)
