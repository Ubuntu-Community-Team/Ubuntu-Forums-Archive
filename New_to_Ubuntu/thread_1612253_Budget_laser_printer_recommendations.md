### Post by Old_Man_Unix on 2010-11-16
Because of the less-than-complete support in Windows 7 for some older model printers, many  organizations and individuals that upgrade to Windows 7 often end up with "surplus" laser printers that are still very serviceable.  Therefore, you can often acquire them at very low cost or even free-for-hauling.  In any case, definitely at the 'low end of the price scale'

Since Ubuntu support for these printers is usually more than adequate, they can perform perfectly well for general-purpose printing on your Ubuntu system.  In fact, my current printer on Ubuntu is a Konica Minolta laser that I got gratis because it isn't supported on Windows 7 and the previous owner just wanted it out of the way. 

As mentioned, a low cost source for consumables is eBay.
