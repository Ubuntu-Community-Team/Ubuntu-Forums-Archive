### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2015-11-21
Just install "Virtual Box" . . . . it's free and available in the Ubuntu repo's.    It provides the Ubuntu user a method to run Windows applications (with a few exceptions - namely high end games).

But . . . . it requires need a decent modern PC with 4 GiB's or more of ram, although 8 or 16 GiB is preferable.

Also . . . . IF you have no clue what a Virtual Machine is . . . . then check out the tutorials available online especially at youtube and similar sites.   If this sounds like too much work, well, run a dual-boot Win and Linux system.

### Post by leunam12 on 2015-11-21
It depends (like my underwear, LOL), what Windows software do you want to run? I have successfully installed Microsoft Office 2003 and 2007, e-Sword, TheWord, Dragon Naturally Speaking 12, and Photoshop CS2 using WINE; I use them on a regular basis and I have no problems with them. For software that does not run with WINE the best options are dual-boot and Virtual Box, but the last one demands a lot of resources.

Actually, 4Gb RAM or less and a reasonable processor is all your need. Not really 'lots' by today's standards. The RAM is split between the two running OSs, two for Win and two for Ubuntu, say. I've heard of people running virtual machines with less than this RAM, and depending on the OS you are using as guest and host, that is definitely an option.
