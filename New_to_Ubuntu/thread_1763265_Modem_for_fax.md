### Post by Faan on 2011-05-20
Is there any means for UBUNTU to find out whether I have a fax modem installed?  Since I installed UBUNTU about 5 months ago I lost the ability to send faxes from my PC. When still under Windows I could do it with no hassle, but since UBUNTU it is just not possible any more.

I have asked on the forum before how I should go about sending faxes and made sure that the fax programs were installed, but still no success.  To me this is such a basic operation that I cannot understand why it is so difficult to be able to do so.

Originally I thought the problem was a fax program, but now I think the problem lies with the fax modem (internal). I am not sure whether the problem is that it could be a Windows issue, however I cannot see that a modem could be Windows equipment.

I would prefer not buying an external fax machine as I believe that is a step(s) backwards.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-05-20
In regard to your point about not being able to see that a modem could be Windows equipment, please look at this:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Softmodem](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Softmodem)

A similar situation exists with printers. To make the things cheaper some of the electronics is left out of the device and the operating system does the job instead. As the link shows the makers did this in conjunction with the Microsoft operating system and it is the responsibility of the maker to make sure the thing works in Windows. But they often do not care about making it work with Linux.

This might and might not apply to your internal modem. I suggest that you visit the motherboard maker's website and try to find out the details of the hardware.

Regards.
