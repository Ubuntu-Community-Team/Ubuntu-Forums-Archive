### Post by Sir Jasper on 2009-06-08
Hi,

97% of business spreadsheets contain errors (source KPMG).

I would suggest:

If you want to learn for personal use choose Calc,
then if ever you need to use Excel it will be very similar.

As you don't have a natural aptitude choose a hard copy book
with screen pictures, simple examples and a good index.
Also, ideally with a spiral binding so it stays open at the page
you need.

So whatever you do keep it simple (especially at the beginning).
Mentally test the results look reasonable and test e.g. that where
column and row totals should agree that the grand total of both does
agree. 
Also, suppose in a column analysis each and every sub-set
has a sub-total; then you can add the whole column and divide
by two.
Google for tips and tricks. 

Learn well, test your skills as you go and enjoy the experience.

If you warm to the tasks buy one or more complex books - the
design, methods and logic are more important than which 
spreadsheet is used.

My regards

PS Do you have any particular needs or aspects you want to master?
