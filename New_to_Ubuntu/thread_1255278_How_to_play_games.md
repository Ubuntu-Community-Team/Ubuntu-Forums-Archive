### Post by Paqman on 2009-09-01
To be honest, you'll find playing Windows games in Windows to be the best way to go. It's less hassle and you'll get better performance.

Virtualisation isn't really an option for most games, as 3D graphics acceleration is rudimentary at best. Wine is an option for some games, but the list of games that work flawlessly is fairly short, and you may need to fiddle about with them. Having said that, there are some big titles like Starcraft and WoW that love Wine. 

By far the easiest thing to do is dual-boot.
