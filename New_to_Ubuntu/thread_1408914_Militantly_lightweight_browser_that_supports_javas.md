### Post by abben on 2010-02-17
I'm on a very old pc (ive got 96 mb of ram and a 500mghz processor to work with). I'll run through my criteria, then review a few browsers to help explain what I'm looking for.

What I want:

1. extremely low system resource consumption
2. quick rendering of even heavy pages.
3. supports javascript
4. doesn't render css terribly
5. stable

**Opera**
Great on counts 3,4 and 5, but it consumes too much in the way of resources for my computer.

**Dillo**

You don't truly appreciate what a godsend Dillo is until you have to browse the web on 96mb of ram. Dillo is *perfect* on 1,2,5, but doesn't have 3 and fails completely at 4.

**Hv3**

Html Viewer 3 is almost exactly what I want. It is very low resource consumption, and has advantages over Dillo in that it actually renders CSS tolerably, and supports javascript. However, it is not entirely stable. It frequently encounters things it doesn't understand which cause either errors or bizarre behavior.

**Links2**

Great on every front except that it doesn't support javascript. It can display images with the **-g** option.

**Epiphany, Midori, Galeon, Kazehakase**

For most people these are considered lightweight. Not for me they aren't.  Kazehakase often runs quite well, but I *think* it gets increasingly sluggish as the session continues. Perhaps with some clever about:config tweaks, one of these could work for me?

**Arora**

I am using arora as I type this. It's os-independent commitment is very nice, and the qt4 libraries don't represent a particularly huge investment. However it too can be sluggish. It seems to not get phased by long browsing sessions or a couple of tabs, a sticking point that for reason causes Kazehakase to self destruct on me. Still pretty slow, though.

**Uzbl**

This isn't ready yet, but this might be exactly what I'm looking for.

So, do you know of some others? Something like hv3 that didn't crash, or links2 with javascript would be perfect.

Kazehakase is my favorite of all the lightweight gtk2 browsers. Though I have used both epiphany and galeon, I can not say one stands out as clearly faster.

Some googling has yielding these two great resources:

[browsers.evolt.org]("http://browsers.evolt.org/") - lists 109 different browsers and archives old versions of the browsers.
[user-agent-string.info]("http://user-agent-string.info/list-of-ua") - an exhaustive list of nearly 250 browsers and other programs that connect to the internet in some fashion. It even has uzbl, which means people are browsing with uzbl.
