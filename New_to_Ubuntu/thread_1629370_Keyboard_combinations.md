### Post by nickleboyblue on 2010-11-23
System->Preferences->Keyboard, click on the "Layouts" tab.  Find your keyboard layout, and add it.  If it only shows "USA" in the layouts you are using, there's your problem.  Personally, I'm not familiar with your keyboard layout, but that's how you get other layouts activated when you've already installed Ubuntu.

Once you've got your layout activated, try combining keys like super, alt, and ctrl with the character keys to see if you come up with anything.  It shouldn't take more than five minutes to figure it out once you've got your layout activated.
