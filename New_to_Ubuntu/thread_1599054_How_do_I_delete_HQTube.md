### Post by robsoles on 2010-10-18
Doing the opposite of what you did to install it should remove it.

Having a quick look at where you got it, it looks like you could disable greasemonkey in firefox and it won't be active anymore.

It is likely that you can kill/uninstall the script via greasemonkey and it is likely that you can kill/uninstall greasemonkey in the firefox addons manager - killing greasemonkey will kill the script's ability to do anything on your system.

It would help a great deal in getting a better answer as to how best to remove it by letting us know how you installed it.
