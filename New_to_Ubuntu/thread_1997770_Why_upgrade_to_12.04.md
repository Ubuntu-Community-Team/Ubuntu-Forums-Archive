### Post by 3Miro on 2012-06-05
The updates for the kernel that you are getting are bug fixes and security updates only, this is true for all the software on your machine.

Security and bug-fixes support for 11.04 will end in 5 months, while support for 11.10 will end in 11 months. Support for 12.04 will end in 4 years and 11 months. You don't have to upgrade right now, you can wait for bugs to be fixed in 12.04, but you do have to upgrade in a few months anyway.

Furthermore, Unity is only one small part of Ubuntu, 12.04 updates much more than Unity. You get newer kernel, not just a patched one, I got much better battery life with the 12.04 kernel on my laptop. You get newer Gnome (all Gnome applications) and newer Gnome-shell. You get newer Firefox and Chromium and Thunderbird and Libre Office ... 12.04 is way more than new Unity.
