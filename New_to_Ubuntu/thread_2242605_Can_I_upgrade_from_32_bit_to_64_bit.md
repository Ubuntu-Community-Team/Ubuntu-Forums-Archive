### Post by ThatBum on 2014-09-02
There shouldn't be any drawbacks to running 64 bit as compared to 32 bit.  Most software is compiled for 64 bit in addition to 32 bit. Historically, there were problems with 64 bit involving the availability of drivers and stuff, but nowadays there's no real reason to not run 64 bit if your hardware can support it.

Not to mention the [Year 2038 Problem]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Year_2038_problem")...
