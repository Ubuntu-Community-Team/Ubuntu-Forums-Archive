### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-08-20
Just plug it in, wait a couple seconds, and then scan for wireless using the Network Manager applet.   You should see a blue or green (depending on model) flashing light.    Panda (distributor) has been good to work with also.   Returns emails, even offered to call if any critical issue arose (which didn't).   

A few years ago, I had a couple laptops and 1 desktop that would not see the network without compiling the driver.    That worked OK, but it's just extra maintenance that I no longer want to spend time on.   There are another 3 or 4 vendors that also make plug & play usb wifi adapters for Linux distros - (Asus, AirLink, etc.) but I I always opt for smaller companies/distributors/system integrators (like System76 for example).   The product is equal, and the service is often FAR better (than the big boys).
   
And lastly . . . Ubuntu and Linux users need (imho) to start promoting Linux compatible hardware and "quit" with all the retrofitting of systems designed from ground up for that "other" operating system.
