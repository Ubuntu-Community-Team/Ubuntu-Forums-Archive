### Post by Koselara on 2008-12-20
I ran into similar issues when I first started -- Ubuntu isn't identifying the monitor's resolution correctly, and it's probably defaulting to 1024x768. The first thing to try is to go into the System menu, then choose Control Center. Then under "Hardware" click on Multiple Screens. Left-hand column should list your monitors, right-hand area is all the info about them.

I know there's a more detailed control setup for saying exactly what our monitors are, but I can't recall where it is -- so I'm hoping yours is just the system making the wrong choice, like mine usually is. :p
