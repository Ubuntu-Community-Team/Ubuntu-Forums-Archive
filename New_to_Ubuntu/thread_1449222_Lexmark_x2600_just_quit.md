### Post by cloyd on 2010-04-09
I got Hp Photosmart Plus . . . there was no number associated with it on the box. Somewhere in the documentation was something like 209b . . . can't get to it right now.  In the store, I ask the clerk for the model number, and he said it was simply  "Photosmart Plus".  This enabled me to go to hp website on a store display computer and check compatibility.  It it interesting that Hp supports Linux, but information on the box or in the setup information included tells you nothing about Linux . . . it is never mentioned. There are all kinds system requirement info such as Microsoft xp, vista, Mac and such, but not a word about linux or Ubuntu. 

There were all kinds of signs on the printer saying "run cd before hooking up usb." Linux wasn't going to run that cd. (it is a netbook at that). However, I unpacked it, took out the packing tape and such, plugged it up to power, installed cartridges,  followed the prompts on the built in touch screen,  and then hooked it to the computer via usb. It worked. Now, if I am ambitious, I'll see if I can make it work wireless, though that is not a priority for me.

I had realized that the lexmark was cheap to buy and expensive operate, but I hadn't spent that much for the color cartridge before. I do think the x2600 is one of the few all-in-ones that lexmark supports on linux.  It uses lots of ink.
