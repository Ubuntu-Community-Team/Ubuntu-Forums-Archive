### Post by Topsiho on 2014-10-21
I live in Holland where all keyboards sold are US-keyboards (and not Dutch, we live in a very funny world).
I use the "US international keyboard" on these keyboards (sic), and get the accents that you mention, first typing the accent (', `, ^) and after that the letter that needs to be accented (a, e, i, o, u). I then get é, î, and so on, as examples. Hope this also applies to your mac :).

### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-10-21
You should always choose the layout to match the keyboard's layout and, whenever special characters are required, one of its variants like "US international with dead keys". "Dead keys" is what allows accentuated characters.
