### Post by DFlame on 2009-08-25
There's probably a much cleaner way of doing it, but the only thing I can think of is to create a mirror of the Ubuntu repositories (the ones you need, anyway). This guide is a little dated but it should at least get you started: [Click Here](http://popey.com/blog/2006/10/24/Creating_an_Ubuntu_repository_mirror_with_apt-mirror/).

The idea would be to download the repository to a portable hard drive at your office, then bring this drive back to the home computer so it can update too. You will need quite a bit of storage space for this - a little 1/2/4/8GB pendrive will not cover it.

I hope that helps a little, I can't think of anything else right now
