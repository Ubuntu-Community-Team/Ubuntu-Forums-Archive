### Post by anewguy on 2010-11-21
My old computer recently died, so I bought parts to build a new one via our local MicroCenter and some deals they had going on.  To make a long story short, the motherboard is a MSI 785G-E53 and I'm using the on-board ATI Radeon HD 4200 graphics adapter.  Ubuntu 10.10 went in without a hitch and the open/default graphics driver was working perfectly.  After an update run it came up letting me know I could get the restricted ATI driver.  I was leary, but decided to go for it.  Well, the ATI Catalyst Suite and driver installed perfectly and all works perfectly.  Desktop effects are flawless.

I had heard all the horror stories about ATI graphics - I had an old ATI Radeon 9250 Pro AGP in my old computer, so I guess I was lucky the open driver worked on it fine - an exception to a rule I guess.  This made me leary of the restricted driver for the new adapter, but it is perfect.

Just a small success story for those who may be interested.

Dave ;)
