### Post by aktiwers on 2009-11-02
OpenDNS is a free to use [DNS server]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_Name_System").

That means you can turn on adult filters and stuff like that easy.

OpenDNS runs fast and does not censure your Internet (only let you self-censur like you want to do). 

You don't have to install software, just sign up at OpenDNS and change your DNS to OpenDNS servers (you probably use the DNS servers from your ISP now)

This is how to do that:
[https://store.opendns.com/setup/device/ubuntu/](https://store.opendns.com/setup/device/ubuntu/)

### Post by x C0MMAND0 x on 2009-11-02
+1 Open DNS

I would recommend setting up Open DNS  [http://www.opendns.com/](http://www.opendns.com/) on the router level.  This is a great way to ensure that ANY device connected to your router will be using the DNS servers specified.  This way you are able to have different "levels" of filtration.  You can sign up for an account for free just so that you can modify exactly what you want to block.

This is an excellent free service in my opinion and I strongly recommend it.

And no, your ISP will not care if you use different DNS servers.
