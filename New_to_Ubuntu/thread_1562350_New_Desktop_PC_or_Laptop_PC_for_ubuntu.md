Rob.Both could work for you. 

You can custom build a desktop, making sure that all the important hardware is Linux compatible. AND, you can upgrade in an almost unlimited way.

A laptop has many more hardware limitations. You cannot build one yourself, you are not 100% sure of the compatibility (many things might be fine, but just 1 thing could make life miserable), and they are a pain to upgrade, if they can be at all.

For development purposes, I would suggest a desktop.

Not that I know of, I think it's necessary to check each individual component such as graphics card, printer, wireless etc. Unless you bought a system with Linux already installed, in which case everything will be compatible.

### Post by Vaphell on 2010-08-28
imo PC is safer. It's build of standard parts and if some device doesn't want to cooperate you can replace it. In case of laptops there are plenty of vendor specific hacks that may or may not work, especially in the newest models - suspend/hibernate, sound/brightness regulation, non-standard implementations of sound input/output. You need to be more careful when buying a laptop because you are stuck with its configuration forever. When you choose one, google for 'linux <laptop model> problem' or something like that to see if it's linux safe or a minefield.
