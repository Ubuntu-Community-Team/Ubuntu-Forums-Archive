### Post by scottiw2000 on 2010-10-16
Yes, there are a couple of ways to do this. My favourite would be DockbarX. It is a panel applet that duplicates much of the function of the new Windows 7 panel (with previews, etc.) and presents your launchers and open programs as icons. It also comes with several nice-looking themes. You can download it from the DockbarX ppa, which can easily be activated through the "Sources Centre" of Ubuntu Tweak ([http://ubuntu-tweak.com]("http://ubuntu-tweak.com")).

You can also now replace your gnome panel entirely with Avant-Window-Navigator. It has applets now for the notification area and indicator menus, as well as for several different main menus (I use gnomenu). As an added bonus, DockbarX can even be run inside AWN. If you decide to replace your gnome panel you need to disable it in the gnome settings--otherwise whenever you close it the panel will just open up again. The easiest way to do this is to install Ubuntu Tweak and look on the "Gnome Settings" tab. Just delete the gnome-panel entry and leave that field blank. You'll also need to make sure that you set AWN to start automatically at login.

There are also a couple of less ambitious but still usable gnome panel applets that use icons for open windows. One is Talika. Another is the "Switcher" applet. I'm not really happy with either of those, but they do the basic job.
