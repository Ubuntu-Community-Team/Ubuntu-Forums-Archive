### Post by Raynman37 on 2008-12-23
A bump is simply to get your thread back up to the top of the list if it gets really far down there.  If your post makes it to page 3, people probably won't go searching for it, so you "bump" it back to the top.

As for asking questions, make a good title that explains the problem like "WINE Crashes" not "Boooo Linux."  The more descriptive in your questions the better.  It's easier for people to answer questions when they have a lot of info instead of having to ask you back for more info.

Keep asking questions!  I've already seen a bunch of people that really know how to use linux and they are all very helpful!

### Post by hyper_ch on 2008-12-24
It is advised to use a descriptive topic title, that means a topic title that gives some clue about the content in the thread itself...

A generic topic title like "noob here" or "need help" does not help at all. As you may have noticed, just about everyone posting in here has some kind of a problem or issue or question ;)
