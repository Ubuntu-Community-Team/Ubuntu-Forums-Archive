She probably just erased traces of what she was doing on your computer, that does not "delete gmail", she'll still have the account (or several accounts, in case you find out about one) on the GMail server. She may (probably) even still have the specific email(s) that she doesn't want to show you in the GMail account. She could access the GMail from any other computer she could use. 

Because of her age, she should allow you to sign on to her GMail account, so she should give you the password so that you can monitor her behaviour. That's my opinion. However, some will say that she deserves a certain amount of privacy. Only you, with your knowledge of your daughter, can assess whether privacy or possible danger is most important and how mature she is likely to act. Make sure she knows not to provide any personal information, etc. If what she's hiding is similar to the notes people pass back and forth in school, it might be harmless (often of the form, "I'm bored, there's nothing to do"). On the other hand, an email from a 17 year old boy that she doesn't know might not be as harmless.
