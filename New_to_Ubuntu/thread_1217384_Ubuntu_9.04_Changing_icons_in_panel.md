### Post by Vined Adobo on 2009-07-19
Ubuntu 9;04 panel:  To change Ubuntu 9.04  panel icons:  They [some] are in /usr/share/pixmaps.  Place your desired icon there. right-click on Launcher > Properties of icon you want to change.  Click on the uh.. almost upper left square, which contains an icon.  Choose the icon you want for your application.  Click close.  Your application should now contain your desired icon.

**Update:**  I'm still learning.  When you click on the square listed above, you will have to click browse.  You can navigate to any folder which contains an icon and grab that icon.  I had to do this with gufw.  My laptop found the icon fine.  My wife's laptop installed differently for some reason and I had to go search for it in /usr/share/gufw/glade.  You don't have to relocate the icon.
