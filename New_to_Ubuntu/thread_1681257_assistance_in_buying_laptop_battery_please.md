### Post by jonnny_j22 on 2011-02-03
First things first check your bios isn't getting confused - you may have a battery calibration tool in your bios, the names change, so read the descriptions to work out if you have one. if it's in there, fully charge your battery, boot into bios and run your battery tool over night. basically it'll just sit there until the battery runs out.
 
If this doesn't work, or your battery is nackered, I have done a quick serach and you're in luck, the mini 10 is very popular so you can get batteries everywhere! it seems that 3 cell and 6 cell are you're choices - netbooks sometimes have 9 cell batteries but as dell doesn't supply them for the mini 10, best stick to 3 or 6. 
 
Which one you choose is up to you, I would say that I think the extra $25 is worth it for the 6 cell as the reason you got a netbook is probably portability and you get twice the life from a 6 cell, obviously pay the extra to get it from a site you know or have used before, don't risk getting parts that seem too good to be true - you know the rule!
 
Finally weigh up the benifits - now netbooks are going multicore with battery lives upto 15hours and high def support are you going to be looking to upgrade your netbook in the next year or so? if so, maybe put up with the shorter battery life and save the cash?
 
Whatever you choose remember to look after your battery. Li-ion batteries do not like being fully charged and fully discharged every day - where possible try and keep it in the middle - 30-80% doing a full charge/discharge once a week or so to keep your battery meters calibrated. Also avoid letting it get too hot - if it's full and you're on ac just pop it out and try not to use your netbook too much where it can overheat - on pillows, sofas - your lap!
 
Best of luck

### Post by Lt. Surge on 2011-02-03
In the case of Dell, you might want to go with an official battery pack.

However, if you find a good deal on eBay (including at least 1-Year Warranty), go for it. The likelihood that it will work may depend on who you buy it from.

At least if you buy it directly from the manufacturer, there is less risk your component is fraudulent and you know EXACTLY what you're getting.

As per the cost and battery life, that's really up to you and what your needs are. If you're not able to find a power source where you're going, go long life. If you have access to power sources, though, don't waste your money.

Until you can get a 24-hr battery for under $120, there is really no reason to have super long life, if you're surrounded by electrical outlets, besides just having it. But if you had that kind of money I would go Toshiba and forget Dell anyway. But... that's beside the point.
