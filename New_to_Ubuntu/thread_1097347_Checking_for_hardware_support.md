### Post by sdennie on 2009-03-15
Most laptops work just fine but, if you intend to you run Ubuntu on the machine, your best bet is to buy a machine with Ubuntu pre-installed.  Dell, System76 and probably others sell machines like that.  If you live in a country where you can't get Dell Ubuntu machines or System76 machines, look at the Dell Ubuntu machines available in the US and buy the exact same configuration in your country.  It will work perfectly with Ubuntu.  In this case, for added bonus points, the first time you turn on the machine, jam the Ubuntu install CD immediately and mash F12 repeatedly until it gives you the option to boot from the Ubuntu Live CD.  No need to unnecessarily jinx your machine by having it ever boot Vista.  ;)
