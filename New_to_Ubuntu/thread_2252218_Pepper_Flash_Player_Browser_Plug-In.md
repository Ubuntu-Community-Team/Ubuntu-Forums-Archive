### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2014-11-10
Hi, Daveski.

As I understand it, the way it works is that the pepperflash plugin can ONLY be found in the Google Chrome browser build; it cannot be downloaded as a separate item on its own.

So, if you want to install it in Chromium (which is where all Chrome's development work is carried out anyway, due to Google sponsoring the Chromium Project), and which cannot include the flashplayer for licensing reasons, then the only way to obtain it is to download the whole of the current version of Chrome. The pepperflash plugin is then extracted, as nerdtron says, and the rest of the files are deleted; Chrome is NOT, however, installed.

It seems a rather 'round the houses' way of doing things, but it's been that way for quite a while now, I'm afraid. I guess the people who would be most concerned about that would be those with a 'cap' on their monthly data allowance from their broadband provider; it must seem strange to have to download TWO browser installations, in order to get ONE working version...

Regards,

Mike.;)
