### Post by jpeddicord on 2008-08-13
It's a part of the GNOME Human Interface Guidelines that most apps are supposed to follow. It basically states that the "default" button, or one that a user is most likely to click, should be on the bottom-right of a dialog so it is easy to snap the mouse pointer to after trained muscle movements.

In short, no you can't change it, but you'll get used to it. :)
