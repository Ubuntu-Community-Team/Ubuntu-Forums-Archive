### Post by KIAaze on 2008-09-02
I think this is the most reliable list concerning webcams working with Skype:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SkypeWebCams](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SkypeWebCams)

I don't have a webcam myself, but I've already tested two which worked under GNU/Linux:
-Logitech Quickcam Express Plus (worked with Skype)
-Philips Toucam Pro II USB Camera (not tested with Skype)

Useful pages:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Webcam](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Webcam)
[http://www.linuxquestions.org/hcl/](http://www.linuxquestions.org/hcl/)
[http://mxhaard.free.fr/spca5xx.html](http://mxhaard.free.fr/spca5xx.html)

But the easiest would probably be to use the [easycam]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EasyCam") tool.

Of course, it may be possible that the webcam works in other applications and not in Skype, that's why the first compatibility list I gave you is the most reliable.

Unfortunately, I was unable to find any GNU/Linux driver related to the "spc110nc" webcam. :/
Are you sure you wrote the name correctly?

Some specific driver pages with their supported webcams:
[http://linux-uvc.berlios.de/#devices](http://linux-uvc.berlios.de/#devices)
[http://www.lavrsen.dk/twiki/bin/view/PWC/WorkingWebcamsWithPWC](http://www.lavrsen.dk/twiki/bin/view/PWC/WorkingWebcamsWithPWC)

And make sure you have the latest Skype version of course (get it from their homepage).
