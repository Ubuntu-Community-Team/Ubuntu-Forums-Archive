### Post by KingTermite on 2008-06-04
I have two books. "Running Linux" (O'Reilly) and "Ubuntu Linux Bible" (von hagen).

I read "Ubuntu Linux Bible" and thought it was mediocre at best. I did get some info out of it, but he explains simple things until he's blue in the face and then breezes over more technical topics with a paragraph or two. I don't recommend this book.


Running Linux is not Ubuntu specific (which is probably better). It's an introduction to Linux type book that is "distribution neutral". I've just been reading it a big here and there now. I haven't read enough to give this an endorsement yet, but have not heard a bad word about it (Amazon reviews are high too).

Other than that....some online help as mentioned already is good too.

I like The Linux documentation project ( [http://tldp.org](http://tldp.org) ).

Also, check out this thread (TONS of links):
