### Post by diablo75 on 2011-04-03
Your video card probably needs to have it's proprietary drivers installed (assuming it's an nVidia chipset).

Check System>Administration>Additional Drivers (I think is what it's called) and enable the proprietary drivers.  Alternatively, you could try to open up your Appearance preferences and set the effects level to something higher than "disabled" and I think it will automatically inform you of it's need for a driver to be enabled and walk you through the install.

If nothing else, use the auto-adjust button on your monitor if you have one.  The horizontal and vertical edges just need to be moved to compensate.
