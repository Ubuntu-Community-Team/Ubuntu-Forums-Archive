### Post by Fenris_rising on 2008-05-14
hi all

as a noob of 3 weeks standing id just like to offer this piece of advice to fellow noobs. make notes! any problems you have be it with installation or getting hardware working properly or installing software will be 10 times easier next time round if you have to, say, reinstall. ive got 3 lots so far dealing with ndiswrapper, realplayer, and uif to iso software. plus i have bookmarked loads of pages in here that i will transfer into open office for my future reference. all this is also saved to a CD-RW incase i get wiped out, for installation of the OS i have a printed copy of the odd things i had to do to get my install running. also add notes to your notes if you had to do something slightly different from established tuts. trust me it will save you time and heartache if you have a serious breakdown. in theory by the time you have assembled all this info you will have a useful set of notes that will be very very helpful in the future even if you can remember it all. im 41 and i can remember bits but id rather have it all down so i know i can do it right the next time. ive had to reinstall twice because i stuffed it up big time and i can now do a reinstall and system tweak in pretty short order. **make notes, you know it makes sense!**

### Post by ByteJuggler on 2008-05-14
I suggest you make a new thread dedicated to your problem.  Title it something like "touchpad not working, considering reinstall", and include the following information:

1.) Open a terminal and enter: "sudo fdisk -lu", and post the command and the output of the command in your posting.
2.) Open a terminal and enter: "sudo dmesg", and post the command and the output of the command in your posting.
3.) Open a terminal and enter: "sudo lspci", and post the command and the output of the command in your posting.
4.) Did the touchpad ever work?  When did it stop working?  What did you do right before it stopped working?  Do you get any noticable errors anywhere?
5.) If you do end up reinstalling, do you want to keep your home folder or not?  Do you have an up to date backup of all critical files?  Depending on how your current install is organised and/or what your expectation of the reinstall is, the process might take several paths.

Anyway, all that information should help people either solve the touchpad issue without reinstalling, or allow them to give you some advice for reinstalling, if that's really what you want.

Hope that helps.
