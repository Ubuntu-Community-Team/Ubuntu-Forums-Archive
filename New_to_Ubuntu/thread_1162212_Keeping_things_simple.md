### Post by charliehorse55 on 2009-05-17
1. Install 9.04

2. Run Updater

3. Set up any programs, add shortcuts to desktop , etc. Make sure everything is working.

4. Turn off automatic updates. 

Turning off automatic updates will save you the trouble if a new kernel comes out and breaks stuff etc. Also this will minimize the amount of changes that occur to the system, making it more stable.

### Post by jimmy the saint on 2009-05-17
Here is what I did.
I got rid of every panel icon that was not needed for a specific task.  Then I added panel icons for the applications that would be used most.  I also made them bigger (it was for my grandmother) to make it easier to tell them apart.

Then I installed abiword (a simple but complete word processor) and a few other simple but effective apps for things she needed to do.  All of them have panel icons to launch them.  It is also a good idea to put a good description in the comment area of the icon properties dialog if the existing one is vague.

I disabled the update notifications and I take care of them when I swing by.  The fewer alerts the better.  They just scare people!

The most important thing is to set up remote desktop capabilities so that you can log into her machine remotely and fix whatever is broken without having to rely on her description and walk her through it.

Edit: Oh yeah, disable multiple desktops.  I got tired of explaining how to get all of her windows back.  She could not wrap her head around the concept of a virtual workspace and many windows users without experience will not understand.  One desktop is what they are used to.
