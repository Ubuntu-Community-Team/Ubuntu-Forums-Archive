### Post by Failboat88 on 2010-06-25
I see you want to use a DAW. I would check to see if your sound card is supported in linux and your input devices(usb or midi). I'm not familiar with support for these devices in Linux. I have fooled around with Ableton Live and it's really fun. A lot of the software will only run on windows or maybe mac. 

If you get a nvidia card dual-booting will be a lot smoother. I would budget low on the tower and get a nice sound card. You would have to tell me more about what you want to record though. You may not need a nice sound card if you don't want to record hardware that has 1/4 jacks. 

CPU: [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103724](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103724)
     This is such a great product at a great price. You might even be able to unlock the 4th core with your mobo. 

GPU: Anything Nvidia, I would stay low on the price if you don't intend to game. 

RAM: Go for 4gb ddr2 or ddr3. 64bit OS is not needed. The 32bit will only read that much.
Storage: Big HDd or a sdd boot drive for performace. Don't get the sdd if your budget is low.

Software: If you have a bigger budget just spend it on better software.
 
Sound: Depends on what your plugging in. I know, I used to look at presonus stuff; when I thought about recording. If you need a lot of mics; you will need an expensive card, with lots of 1/4 jacks to record it all at once. 

Tell me more about what you want to do with the DAW and what the budget is.
