### Post by natravis on 2009-08-28
You may have a corrupt disc image or the burner you are using may be malfunctioning. Also, just curious as to why you are using 8.04 instead of 9.04? Anyways, you can see how to calculate hashes of the disc image you are burning [here](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM) and the link for the correct hashes is in that page. Make sure your disc image is valid then you can rule that out. Next I would check the HDD, then perhaps the RAM. Computers can be temperamental little creatures, so be ready to play around with it.
