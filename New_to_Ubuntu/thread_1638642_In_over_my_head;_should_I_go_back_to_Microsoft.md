### Post by pricetech on 2010-12-07
Don't get discouraged.  I'm not spring chicken either.  Us old folks take a little longer to make those wrinkles in our brains.

Having said that;  Take a deep breath and relax.  Then boot to the live CD and back up any files you have, wherever they are.  Backups are a must anyway, so it's not like you're doing anything extra yet.

Take a few minutes to decide exactly what you want it the way of OS options.  Since you're comfortable with Windows XP, don't wipe it out yet.  You already have a dual boot, so it's more a matter of sorting out the "Linux half" of your hard drive.

I recommend you use Lucid, which is the latest LTS (Long Term Support) release.  I also recommend a fresh install.  It just works better for me that way.

Your data is safely backed up, so you don't have to worry about it.

Keep in mind that Ubuntu can see stuff on your Windows drive, but Windows can't see stuff on your Ubuntu drive, so keep anything that both OSes need to access somewhere on your Windows drive or on an external device.

Boot to the Live CD and CAREFULLY remove the existing Ubuntu partitions, leaving the Windows partition intact.

Install from the desktop shortcut and let Ubuntu do its own partitioning using the largest continuous free space.  (it's worded something like that)

Once you have Ubuntu installed, do the updates, add anything else you want in the way of software, and enjoy.

The community is here when you need help.

If it starts to feel like it's too much, take a break and go watch the grass grow for a little while.  You're not running a race, you're having fun.

Ultimately it is your decision what you with your computer.  Use what works for you.  I just want you to know you're not alone.

Joe
