### Post by ElEdwards on 2011-05-02
I let myself get buffaloed by this, too....I was used to seeing **File, Edit,** etc, in the Application itself ....but that's different now.

For example, click on your **Home Folder** icon in the Unity bar and after the window opens, notice that in the topmost portion of your desktop, in the dark gray bar, you'll see the words **Home Folder**.... now float your mouse pointer over those words and you'll see the normal **File, Edit, View,** etc. menu items.
To set your Mouse for Single-click, while you're looking at your **Home Folder**, select **Edit / Preferences / Behavior** and here is where you select either Single or Double click...it defaults to Double.

For your Firefox bookmarks, while Firefox is open, again move your mouse pointer over the title of the page your are viewing in that topmost gray bar (as I'm typing this, mine says "Ubuntu Forums - Reply to Topic - Mozilla Firefox") and you'll see the familiar menu bar.... select **Bookmarks / Show All Bookmarks**.  This will open a window titled "Library"....once again, move your mouse pointer to the topmost title bar and again you'll see it change to a menu; one of those selections is **Import and Backup** and you can do just what you want to do there.

You have all the functionality you had before.... the menus are just hidden in plain site ;)

Like I said, took me a while to find 'em, too.
