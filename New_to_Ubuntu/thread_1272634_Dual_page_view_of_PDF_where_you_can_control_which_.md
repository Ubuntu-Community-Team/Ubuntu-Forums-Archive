By book publishing convention, the first page of a book is a recto page (right side),
 and hence all recto pages will have odd numbers and all verso (left side) pages will have even numbers.

A properly scanned ( or created ) digital book will have a blank first page,
 so that viewing dual-page mode, page number one will be on the right-hand side (recto) and thus be properly viewed.

If not, then the book is not properly scanned or created.

WordPerfect had a "view printed mode" that always started with the first page (recto) by itself on the right side.
 This could be over-ridden to have information pages (such as copyright or printing history) on the left side before the first recto.

I don't know of any PDF viewers that have this option...
but I haven't used them all that much.
