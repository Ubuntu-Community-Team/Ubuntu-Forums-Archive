### Post by PcMojo on 2012-06-15
I'm still relatively new to Ubuntu/Linux, and have asked a similar question in the past. What I have found is that the make/model of laptop is actually of secondary concern. Let's say Ubuntu had issues surrounding video with the AMD APU chips or the newer Intel HD4000. It wouldn't matter much whether it was a Dell, Hp or IBM. After you get a few ideas on models you may want to research the components (especially any new WhizBang! features) on the forums and see if others are having problems with that particular piece of technology. That being said, laptops tend to be more proprietary than desktops so you may be safer with a larger selling brand just because any problems would effect more people and possibly get fixed quicker. Another thing you may want to keep in mind is that even if you get a laptop that works perfectly with the latest version of *buntu, that doesn't guarantee that the next upgrade (or even update) that things will still be perfect. My Dell620 worked great with 10.10 but after doing a clean 12.04 install my dvd/cd stopped burning. In fact, I need to make a post to find out what the issue is! Hope this helps!
