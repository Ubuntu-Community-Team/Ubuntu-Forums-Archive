### Post by spiderbatdad on 2008-05-15
These folks might be able to help: [http://www.linmodems.org/](http://www.linmodems.org/)
Winmodems are highly unreliable for any operating system other than windows. My understanding is that the modem relies on the OS for it's firmware, rather than containing it's own chipset. Internal modems on windows pc's are unlikely to work in Linux. It is advised to obtain a good external modem that provides Linux support.

### Post by spiderbatdad on 2008-05-15
I cannot stress enough that I don't believe you will ever get this modem to work properly with Linux. I believe you could spend days or weeks of being "almost there," seemingly just a step away from success, but if you must try, start by downloading scanModem and reading the instructions for its use. Also see this helpful guide.[http://www.faqs.org/docs/Linux-HOWTO/Modem-Dialup-NT-HOWTO.html](http://www.faqs.org/docs/Linux-HOWTO/Modem-Dialup-NT-HOWTO.html)

There are numerous external modems that work well with Linux.
