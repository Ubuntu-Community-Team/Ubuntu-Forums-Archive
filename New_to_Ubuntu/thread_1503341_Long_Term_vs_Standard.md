It's just a matter of personal preference. Some people like stability more than having the bleeding-edge. I personally look at what new functionality is being introduced in the new version and whether I want it badly enough to upgrade. Even if I do upgrade I usually wait a few weeks for bugs to be ironed out. My one suggestion is to back up your important data before upgrading in case the upgrade does not end well.

### Post by nhasian on 2010-06-06
yeah upgrading can be time consuming when you do it through the update-manager on the release day.  the servers get bogged down and the transfer rates are slow.  it can take several hours to complete.

I recommend using bittorrent download the ubuntu alternate ISO next time.  I was able to download the entire cd image in about 3 mins.  You don't even need to burn it to disc.  just mount the image in ubuntu, and then you can do the upgrade locally without connecting to the internet at all.  its waaay faster and you are only limited by the speed of your hard disk.

### Post by arvevans on 2010-06-06
I have always waited a few days after a new release before downloading and installing the new code.  That avoids the server congestion and also gives developers a chance to fix any glaring errors that show up for early users.

Personal preference here is to upgrade to each new release within a couple of weeks of availability.  Yes, that does sometimes involve problems, and having to develop my own work-around or fix for the problems.  That is part of being an early user, and is expected.  Part of the perceived benefit of using Linux is that being an open system, I can fix it myself if needed.  I like that.
