### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-11-03
Ubuntu is free and you can install it alongside Windows (or inside a virtual machine), so there probably isn't any need to ask those sorts of questions - just install it and find out! :-)

I'll answer your questions though (once you've read the two articles that sledge73 linked you to):

1. Many people believe Ubuntu is better than Windows. I certainly think it is. But computers are very personal things and you might not agree with me.

2. A program must be written for a particular operating system, and Adobe has not written Photoshop or Flash for Linux. We do have a couple of good image editors called The Gimp (preinstalled on Ubuntu) and Krita that are very powerful; I even know of a developer at Adobe who has sometimes used The Gimp at work.

For most things you could ever need, there is equivilant Linux software to accomplish the task. For audio editing, most people use Audacity (you might already be familiar with this program, it's available for Windows too). For video editing I use Kdenlive.

Adobe has ported Flex, Flash Player and Acrobat Reader to Linux, so you don't have to give up those programs.

3. There are lots of screensavers preinstalled on Ubuntu. Screensavers are just programs that run in full-screen, so Windows screensavers will not work on Ubuntu, but that also means that those lovely Ubuntu screensavers will not work on Windows :-)

It's normal to have lots of questions in the begining. Naturally, on an Ubuntu forum you are going to find lots of people who believe Ubuntu is "better" than Windows, but "better" is a value judgement and someone who is struggling to learn new ways to do things may have trouble being objective.

There are GNU/Linux equivalents for almost any Windows program, however, they usually aren't exactly the same and you might have to learn a new method. Sometimes when people can't find an exact replacement they go back to using Windows. In my opinion, that is their loss.

Here are a couple of links to Ubuntu help documentation, starting there you should be able to find what you need to read and please come back and post any specific questions you have or if you don't understand some of the documentation.
