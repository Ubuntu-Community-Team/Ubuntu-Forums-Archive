I run 10.04 at the moment but it is on ext3 file system due to auto upgrading for the past few versions. At the moment I am in a situation where I feel that my hard drive performance is really letting my machine down and therefore it seemed obvious that I should consider upgrading to ext4. My issue is that I am not in the greatest position to do a full OS installation; I could do it but it would be somewhat of a hassle for me. I have seen that there is a way to do the upgrade with only a live CD, however this suggested that the performance gains would not be so great. Is it worth considering doing this method?

Maybe this: you free up space enough to make an installation into and back-up/preserve the rest.

When you have made the new installation (as you boot into it), you move what you want into the new installation (with ext4) and repartition the other part and set it up with a /home or something... Good luck (and less of hassle)! Cheers! :)

### Post by snowpine on 2010-10-07
A clean start with a fresh reinstall is the best way to speed up your computer, in my opinion. Obviously you will want a full backup first, and you might even consider installing side-by-side as a dual boot, that way your old system will be preserved in its current working state, and you can switch back and forth to benchmark for yourself whether or not the project was a success.
