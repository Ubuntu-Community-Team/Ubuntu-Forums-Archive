### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-04-20
Right now, I can type 80 words a minute on a good day and 40 while carrying on a conversation that has nothing to do with what I'm typing, but looking at my family history, I'll probably be right you are in about 15 years, 20 if I'm lucky.  So, I feel for you.

I'm not aware of anything that is in any way up to the task you're talking about in the repos as of right now.

I do recall hearing about a project called soothsayer some time back, but I haven't kept up with it.

Be aware that openoffice does have some *limited* predictive text capabilities built in.  You'll also find that the spell checking functions in firefox do a pretty good job with skipped letters, so you can work on retraining yourself to to things like type "letter" as "leter" and use spell check to clean things up for you.  Certainly not efficient, and probably frustrating as all get out for somebody who knows how to touch-type properly.

Hopefully, somebody else can suggest more, and please post back if you find anything interesting.

EDIT:  Looks like DrMelon posted a link to soothsayer and a project I was unaware of while I was typing.  lol
