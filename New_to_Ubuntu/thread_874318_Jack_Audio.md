### Post by djxcon on 2008-07-29
What did you want to know?  In my opinion, Jack is one of the most useful programs in the Linux multimedia world.  I use it to send audio from one program to another.  All of my recording in general comes in through Jack and Jack routes it to other programs.  

Real life example: 
I record from an amplifier into the sound card.  Jack reads in the input from the sound card and sends the audio to a track in Ardour.  Ardour then records the audio onto the track.  Now, let's say that I want to add a drum patten to a new track in Ardour...I will open up my favorite drum pattern editor (Hydrogen) and route its audio through Jack into Ardour. 

Basic Info:
[http://jackaudio.org/]("http://jackaudio.org/")

qjackctl is a front end GUI for Jack
[http://qjackctl.sourceforge.net/]("http://qjackctl.sourceforge.net/")
