### Post by JerryI on 2009-03-12
I am a 68-yr-old mechanical engineer totally new to linux, but I have been messing with Intrepid for a few days.  I have a fairly new Vista Home HP desktop with 2GB RAM and a 5-yr-old XP laptop with a fading 100GB hard disk and 1GB RAM.

I have aged 5 years in the past 6 months trying to contend with Vista, mostly frustrated with having to fight over permission to access, change, or delete files that I myself create.  Also with that fact that MS frequent updates tend to unravel other apps already in-place.

I do have a few MS apps and some hardware I would like to continue to use, e.g., AutoCAD 12, Fritz 10 compatibility with the playchess.com website, Skype, and a USB Soundblaster.

So I'm not able to commit to a total-linux environment.  I have started a dual-boot experiment on the Vista and I don't like several things: e.g., having to boot back and forth, as-yet nonsuccess in getting any sound out of the Ubuntu install, the learning curve for an old guy, the fact that Skype's download barfs at my AMD64 architecture,

Here's what I'm toying with next.  I retired my XP laptop because the HD was failing, thinking Vista wouldn't be such a big deal.  But after Vista's attack on my sanity, I have decided to put a new HD in the laptop and resurrect it.  The only thing I will have to give up by abandoning the Vista is its dual monitor setup, which I will really miss and which I have already incorporated into Ubuntu.  At this point I really don't perceive much need for linux.  Nevertheless, I would really like to toy with it a bit, give it a fair shake, and see if I can become proficient enough to appreciate some of its advantages.  Should I be able to create a dual-boot system (say 150GB of Ubuntu and 100GB of XP) and also put a virtual XP machine inside the Ubuntu portion (dual-boot in case I decide to bail out of Ubuntu completely, but the Ubuntu with Virtual XP in case I end up liking linux and can surmount enough of the problems I have already encountered, like no Skype and no sound yet)?

Any thoughts would be much appreciated.

### Post by Therion on 2009-03-12
> **JerryI said:**
> Should I be able to create a dual-boot system ... and also put a virtual XP machine inside the Ubuntu portion ... ?
A perfectly viable solution if that's what you want to do.  You sound very much like you're dabbling and very "iffy" on the whole idea of switching so I'm going to strongly suggest you at least install a virtual machine, with dual-booting (or both) being very good ideas in my opinion.

I would further suggest you download a LiveCD of Ubuntu 8.04 (Hardy Heron) and try that versus 8.10 (Intrepid Ibex).  I've found it to be more stable and easier to work with than Intrepid over time.  

Of course that's me, and my hardware; your mileage may vary.  8.04 is a Long Term Support (LTS) release though, which many people find attractive.
