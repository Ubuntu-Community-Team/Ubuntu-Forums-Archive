### Post by andy_foley on 2011-02-03
Yeh I had this problem, ended up with the Director's commentary track after an 8 hour rip, even though I'd selected default English in the audio section!
 
I tried again selecting track 1 (unknown) instead of default, lo and behold English film track!
 
Rather than waiting hours for the rip to finish to see if you have the right language, you can play the first bit of the output avi file in your movie player even though it's still ripping, to check it's ripping the right sound track.
 
Apart from that, after much research and trials of other software, acidrip is ace for saving your DVDs as AVI files on your hard drive. After much experimenting, I've found great widescreen picture settings for a 1GB AVI rip if anyone wants them.
