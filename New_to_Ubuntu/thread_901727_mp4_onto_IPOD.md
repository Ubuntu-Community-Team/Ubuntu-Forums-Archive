### Post by Bigtime_Scrub on 2008-08-26
Well if you converted whatever video you want to transfer to mp4 you've done the hard part. I use amaroK. I just open the program, connect my iPod and click "connect" in amaroK.

Then I take the file I want to transfer and drag and drop it to the left side of the amaroK screen. It should come up in the que and then I click transfer. It will take a few seconds to finish and it will flush the iPod cache automatically and you are done.

### Post by Bigtime_Scrub on 2008-08-27
> **dmsymr said:**
> Thanks for this, alot of other people have had success with Amarok, I have loaded all my media onto it, but alas, I cannot get it to recognise my ipod....I am looking into it as we speak, but it just wont accept the mountpoint Im telling it! Did you have any probs when first installed?

Yes I did have that problem at first. I use an 80G Classic. 

What I found out was that i was using the wrong mount point. 
I suggest 2 things, first you need to back up your data and erase the iPod.

After back up plug the iPod into the computer via USB. You should see the iPod icon on your desktop. Right click that icon and choose to explore the contents. Right click on one of the folders and select all. Then press delete. 

That will reset the iPod to factory conditions, erase all data on it, and along with that erasure all apple firmware!

Then right click the iPod icon again and select unmount.

Then Connect it again.



Step 2 is to open a terminal and enter mount.
You will see a bunch of stuff flash on the screen. What you are looking for should be on the bottom of all that mess. It will look like YourIpod mounted at /mnt/sba1 or something to that effect.

That is the real mount point Linux recognizes and the one you will need to correctly configure amaroK.
