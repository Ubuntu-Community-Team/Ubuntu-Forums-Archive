### Post by tommcd on 2008-04-23
In general, the newer versions of any distro come with a newer kernel, xorg, desktop enviornments (gnome, kde, or xfce), and programs. Newer kernels have support for newer hardware. Newer versions of xorg, gnome, kde, xfce and programs come with newer (and possibly ever better) features, etc. 
Ubuntu 8.04 will be a LTS (long term support) version, which means it will be supported for at least 3 years, instead of the normal 18 months.
