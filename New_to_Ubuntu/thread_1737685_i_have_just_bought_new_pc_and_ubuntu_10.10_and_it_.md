If you're shutting down via the red button top right of your screen (next to your name) you might like to try adding another shutdown button on the top panel. Right click on the panel in a grey area> add to panel>choose Shut down as the item to add.

This worked for me when I had the problem that you have described.

There used to be an alternative shutdown via the Places menu (I think that's where it was) but for some reason its not available anymore.

### Post by laidback on 2011-04-29
A voice from the past has just hit me.
I came across this before and found that if I had the OOo Quickstarter running I couldn't shutdown. Remove the Quickstarter and all returned to normal.

I've just installed a copy of 11.04 and found the same problem with the default (new) view. However, switch to the classic view and all is well. Turn on Quickstarter in classic view and back we go to the same old problem. Perhaps some form of Quickstarter is running by default in the new view (big buttons etc LHS) which might be causing the problem. I haven't investigated how one could alter that situation yet.

It first cropped up in 10.04 (9.xx seemed to be fine) I and others shared posts about it. Pity that the problem is still there as in the new view of 11.04 I can't shutdown either, except by logging out and doing it from the login screen.

Hope this helps someone.
