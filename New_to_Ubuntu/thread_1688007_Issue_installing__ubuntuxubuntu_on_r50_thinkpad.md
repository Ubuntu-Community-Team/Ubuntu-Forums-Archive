### Post by Chrskyes on 2011-02-14
First and foremost, start over. 

Make sure you have the newest Ubuntu installer.
Burn to a CD at the lowest setting supported by the disc. Make sure there are no unneeded peripherals in your computer such as an SD card, another CD, etc anything you could have left in there. Additionally (and mind you, this is a TOTAL guess, and I probably sound stupid for saying something like this) but just to be 100% safe, check that your connections to the computer a secure. Make sure your RAM is properly seated, etc.

We need more details to help you, mainly, are you running another OS on that computer and trying to replace it with Ubuntu, or is your hard drive empty and you are installing Ubuntu for the first time?
