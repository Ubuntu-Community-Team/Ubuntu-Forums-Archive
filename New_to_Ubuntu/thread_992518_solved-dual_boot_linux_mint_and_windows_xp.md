### Post by irish66 on 2008-11-24
Well finally after a number of attemps, i now have a dual boot of windows and Mint.
So I'll just talk of the attempts that were sucessful at installing Mint, and one where I wasn't.
In attempt (1) I accidently chose use entre disk, and of course deleted Windows.
IN attempt (2) , I chose use largest free space, and of course ended up not giving windows enough space Well at least I think I did. Basically when I rebooted after installing, it went straight
inti Mint. I never bothered to check to see if Windows files were still there.
Any then I ended up with a hard drive divided into 3 partitions.
c (3 gigs approx) f (8 gigs approx) and G (223 gigs approx)
so attempt 3, i used partition editor, and freed up space on the g drive.
So then i chose "use largest free space", but was told, i had too many primary partitions.
so attempt 4, i decided to hell with it, I'll just give the entire g drive. So in windows management, i deleted the partition space I had made.
I booted back into Mint, live cd again, choose use largest free space again. It installed.
I restarted,
The grub menu came up. Yes it had an option for booting into Windows. So I tried that first.
Yep, everything fine there. So restart, grub menu, choose Linux Mint, I got in.
Yipee :)
