No boot CD is needed. Properly buring the .iso image to the disk will allow you to boot from it. Once the CD is booted (change your BIOS to set the CD to the first boot device), you can either 'boot and try ubuntu' or install it. I recommend trying it first...when you're ready to make the changes and install ubuntu to your hard drive, you can find the 'Install' icon on the desktop.

### Post by smurphy_it on 2011-07-19
If the machine you wish to try it on is capable of booting from a USB drive, you can always put it on a USB flash drive.

Steps involved:

1) Download the .iso and save it somewhere on your machine
2) Grab unetbootin from [here]("http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/")
3) Launch unetbootin, and point it at your .iso file, then choose your flash drive.. it will extract and send pertinent files to your usb flash drive
4) Reboot your computer, and hit the key to boot from "other boot devices", probably F9 or F12 on your keyboard.
5) Run ubuntu from there (don't attempt installing until you know the ramifications, and actually backup your O/S first).
