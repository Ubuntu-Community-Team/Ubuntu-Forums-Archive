### Post by Paqman on 2013-01-03
Your fans respond automatically to changes in temperature within your computer. If you fan is running fast that's because it needs to to get rid of the heat your machine generates. Trying to slow them down is likely to result only in your machine overheating and switching itself off.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-01-03
I think the actual speeds are programmed in the BIOS, without the ability to change them without reflashing the BIOS with different speeds.  I use thinkfan on my T43p which also has an annoying fan sound, but using lower speeds simply means running your chips hotter.  The small fans used means high RPM is required to move the amount of air required to keep the temperatures under control.  830 is a low speed by several measures.  I have a few servers that sound like airplanes taking off--6700 RPM! Times 7 fans!

Lenovo engineers don't pay attention to fan noise, just fan size, and air flow required to keep your chips from melting.  Fan noise is now your issue, not theirs.

If you want quiet, then a MacBook Air is in your future.
