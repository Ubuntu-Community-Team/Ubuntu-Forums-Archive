Hello and Welcome :)

First thing first, is this a PC or Laptop?
Is your BIOS able to detect your HDD (Hard Disk Drive)?

First step that you need to do is: make sure your HDD is detected.

If it's detected, you just need to go to:

[www.ubuntu.com](www.ubuntu.com)

and install the right version for your machine (32bit or 64bit).

Are you willing to Dual-Boot with XP or you just want Ubuntu as the only OS on your machine?
For Dual-Booting, please check the guide in my signature.

After you download Ubuntu, please check MD5SUM.
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuHashes](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuHashes)

Then, on the download page of Ubuntu, there is HOWTO create either Live Bootable CD or USB.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/LiveCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/LiveCD)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto)


After that, make sure your BIOS boots first from your CD (or USB) then your Hard Drive.

Check these posts:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11101432&postcount=126](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11101432&postcount=126)

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11101707&postcount=129](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11101707&postcount=129)

GParted is a great tool and will help you a lot.

Once you're done with GParted, try to have a look at this:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11101707&postcount=129](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11101707&postcount=129)
