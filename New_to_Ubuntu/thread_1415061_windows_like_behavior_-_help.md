### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-02-25
While this certainly COULD be a virus -- it's highly unlikely.

From what you wrote, a faulty keyboard could be the case of the problems -- including sticking keys, short circuiting in the keyboard circuit layer (from stuff spilled on it), micro-cracks in the keyboard circuit layer, stuff jammed beneath some keys, or general failure of the keyboard chips.

Laptop keyboard are more fragile and easily damaged.  I have a couple of older laptops (including a Presario) and have had to replace the keyboard in BOTH of them.

Don't know what you can afford to spend, but you might consider Googling on your laptop model and see what a replacement keyboard will cost.  On my laptops, replacing them only took a few minutes -- but you have to be REALLY CAREFUL about the fragile ribbon connector cable and the socket.  Break either and your laptop becomes useless.

### Post by gallifrey on 2010-02-25
I had a similar problem not too long ago, and by process of deduction and elimination, established that it was due to the touch pad! pressure on the areas either side of the touchpad spread to the touchpad itself, and it reacts as though you are directly touching it. It is so sensitive, that it will sometimes perceive a wrist movement as a double click. 

I turned down my touchpad sensitivity settings, and turned off the 'tap to click' function, which lessened the problem, but ultimately I just turned off the touch pad and started using a mouse. Problem solved. For me, anyhow.
