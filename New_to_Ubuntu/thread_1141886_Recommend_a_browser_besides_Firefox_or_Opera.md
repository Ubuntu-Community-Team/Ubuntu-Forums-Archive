### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-04-28
The history doesn't bother me because I rarely open it. I have the habit of bookmarking everything I need and Firefox is great for that. Some extensions that could make your bookmarking experience better:

[Bookmark Duplicate Detector](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/1553)

Finds duplicated bookmarks on-the-fly and has a tool for searching previous duplicates

[OpenBook](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/42)

> This extension allows for customization of the Add Bookmark dialog. The bookmarks tree can be automatically opened and elements can be revealed or hidden. Also, the dialog can be resized!

[Read It Later](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/7661)

> Read It Later allows you to save pages of interest to read later. It eliminates cluttering of bookmarks with sites that are merely of a one-time interest.

[Tagmarks](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/8485)

> This add-ons adds a set of icons to the quick bookmarks, allowing you to quickly add tags to your bookmarks by clicking on each icon.

[Tagmarks Modifier](https://addons.mozilla.org/pt-BR/firefox/addon/10458)

> Change the iconset of Tagmarks as you like 

[TagSifter](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/998)

> Firefox 3 lets you tag your bookmarks, but it doesn't give you a great way to browse your bookmarks by their tags. TagSifter tries to. Select a group of tags in the sidebar or menu, and TagSifter shows you all the related tags and bookmarks. Or search your bookmarks with set operators, like "tag1 - (tag2 + !tag3)". Comes with a tool to help tag your existing bookmarks as you move to Firefox 3.

[ScrapBook](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/427)

> Helps you to save Web pages and organize the collection. 

My productivity related to bookmarks increased a lot with these extensions, specially OpenBook, TagSifter and Tagmarks.

Opera or other alternatives won't give you these excellent extensions. 

In my opinion, you would be much more happy if you customize Firefox to make you more productive and less annoyed.

BTW, finding the link of these extensions to post here was pretty easy. Just opened TagSifter and and clicked the tag addons.mozilla. I don't even have to keep them organized in folders, but TagSifter offers an excellent integration with the Bookmarks Manager to add tags in batches, edit them and delete.
