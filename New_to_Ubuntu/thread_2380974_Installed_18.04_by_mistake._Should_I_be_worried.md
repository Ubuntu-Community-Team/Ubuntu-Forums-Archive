### Post by The Cog on 2017-12-24
You are taking a risk using 18.04, in that there are 4 months before it is due to be released. During that time there are quite possibly going to be changes that break things. As a newcomer to the OS, you are likely to find those breakages (even the "simple" ones) difficult. Installing 17.10 now would avoid that risk. Or perhaps you could wait and see - you might be lucky and not get any problems - just install 17.10 if you do see breakage appearing in 18.04. If you are intending to generate serious data/documents then reinstalling 17.10 now will avoid possible stress trying to save your work while reinstalling later.

P.S. Welcome to Ubuntu.

### Post by Autodave on 2017-12-25
Every 2 years, a long term service release is made. These LTSs have a service life of 5 years: security and software updates are available for 5 years after the release.  The last LTS was 16.04. 16 stands for the year of the release, 04 stands for the month (April). The next LTS will be 18.04. Releases in the interim will be 6 month releases and only supported until the next release.

I stick with only the LTSs and even then I usually wait a month after their release before installing them. Further, I do not upgrade to them but completely install the new release after copying all files that I need to save to an external HD.
