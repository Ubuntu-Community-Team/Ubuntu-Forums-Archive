### Post by Buuntu on 2009-11-27
Read and ask lots of questions.
Learn to use the terminal and some essential commands.
Learn things about how Ubuntu works, packages, and the repositories.
Use resources like these forums and IRC to make the switch go much smoother.
Don't get frustrated - there will be times where you will be confused and angry with your system, especially at the beginning.
Read this guide: [http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html](http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html)

### Post by lexfati on 2009-11-27
Buuntu's suggestions are right on the money, and so is jacobs444 - don't give up!

That being said, Linux is different.  Don't expect things to be the same as you are used to with Windows.  Avoid looking for ways to get the same commercial applications you are used to to work on Linux; instead search for equivalents available through the repositories.

These forums provide a wealth of information.  If you look you will often find your questions have already been asked and answered.  And don't forget to congratulate yourself every time you figure out something new.  You'll be amazed just how much you can learn in a short amount of time with a little exploration and some persistence.
