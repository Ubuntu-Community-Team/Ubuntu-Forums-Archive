### Post by DirtDawg on 2008-11-01
[1]Highlight what you want to copy, then move the cursor where you want to paste, and click the middle mouse button. Other than that, copy/paste buttons depend on the application. Firefox, for example, uses ctrl+c and ctrl+v while the terminial uses ctrl+shift+c and ctrl+shift+c. The first method I mention here (middle-mouse-click) is the universal Linux method.

[2]Yes. I'm not sure what it is. Try Google. You will negate the default copy/paste function, however.

[3]Check the 'Thread Tools' menu on the upper-right.
