### Post by zsugiart on 2009-05-23
Pidgin, Amsn, Kopete, Kmess plus all the other IM app for Linux Ubuntu
can not successfully establish a webcam session with sound. Tried this in Ubuntu 8.10. Either they do either one, none at all, or never both.

What puzzles me is that why does Linux have so MANY alternative apps, but all of them seems to suffer one or more of this basic shortcomings.

Wouldn't it be better if development effort be focused on a few apps instead?

Yes webcam with sound is no easy feat - I am a developer and I can perceive the complexity. But consider what apps in other OS have achieved in the past 10 years - surely there must be an equivalent counterpart in Linux? 

But no, that is not the case, we have about 5 or more IM clients for MSN alone, that can be run in KDE/Gnome/whatnot and none of them have evolved further that the other 'clones'. Of course, none of them live up to the quality of their counterparts from other OS too. 

It is rather sad when you come to that realization.
