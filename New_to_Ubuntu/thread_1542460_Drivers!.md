### Post by stoogiebuncho on 2010-07-30
What brand of graphics card is it?

The best way to see if your hardware will work is to download the CD and boot it up.  There's an option to try out Ubuntu without making any changes to your system.  It loads the whole OS from the CD (it will take a while to load, however, because CDs are slow compared to hard drives) and you can see if it works.

With linux the drivers are already there, so you don't have to find them or install them.  For some hardware there are proprietary drivers available that sometimes will work better than what Ubuntu has by default.  You can easily switch to those after you have installed Ubuntu by going to System > Administration > Hardware.  It will look through your hardware to see if there are proprietary drivers that you could use.

Edit:  I misread your post.  I see now that you already installed Ubuntu.  Is your hardware working, or are you having problems with it?  You won't be able to use your Windows drivers in Ubuntu.  Try going to System > Administration > Hardware to see if there's anything there for you.
