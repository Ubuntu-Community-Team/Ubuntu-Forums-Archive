Xubuntu's forums are right here - the Ubuntu forums! It's the Xfce forum - *not* the "Xubuntu forum" - that isn't accepting new members. Xfce is not a distro, but a desktop environment that is used in *many* different Linux distros! Xubuntu is just one of dozens of distros that use it.

You won't find the Linux Mint Xfce edition very different from Xubuntu, because it is *built* on Xubuntu! It *is* Xubuntu, with green paint and those very cool Mint Tools (and multimedia codecs and a few other things) tied on.

Ximo for Kids is another Xubuntu-based distro (it's intuitive enough for little kids, why not grownups?). 

Sidux used Xfce (not Xubuntu), Crunchbang Statler has an Xfce version, SalixOS uses Xfce (Slackware-based, wonderful simplicity). There are lots and lots of Linux versions that use Xfce, most of which are not based on Xubuntu at all. Each has their own different configurations of the awesome, wonderful, fast, full-featured Xfce desktop environment.

It's a shame that the terms get so mixed up. Ubuntu is just one of hundreds of Linux distributions, yet so many have come to think of Linux and Ubuntu as one and the same thing. Perhaps you have associated Xfce and Xubuntu as "one and the same" as well. Xubuntu is just one of dozens of Xfce-based Linux distributions.

It just happens to be my favorite because it works wonderfully on my hardware, it's got these huge, vast, wonderful Ubuntu repositories, and of course this huge,vast, wonderful support community!

For Xfce support apart from these forums, I suggest the Xfce mailing lists instead of their nearly-dead forum. More at [http://xfce.org](http://xfce.org)

Hoping you find what you're looking for,
Robin
