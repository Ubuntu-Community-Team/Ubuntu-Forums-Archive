### Post by schwascore on 2008-07-18
You will still need to download the programs, but if you copy your home directory over to the laptop, your settings and preferences for the programs should be preserved, saving you some setup time.

There are tons of options for Ubuntu laptops - it all depends on how much work you want to put into setting them up and how much money you want to spend.

I personally use HP Pavilion dv6000 series laptops, but not all hardware functions are easy to get working.  I would suggest finding a model you think you like and then searching the forums for the specific model number.  That should bring up any install guides and give you a heads up to any potential problems you might encounter.

[System 76]("http://www.system76.com/") also sells laptops with Ubuntu pre-installed, eliminating any serious work on your part.
