### Post by Jose Catre-Vandis on 2009-03-19
How to change settings:

From the main menu

Settings > Settings Manager > Window Manager Tweaks > Compositor Tab

Tick box for "Enable display compositing"

This will bring to life the opacities options and other tick boxes for shadows and overlays.

The xfce compositor is a very simple eye candy compositor, and offers no real functionality in the way that Compiz can (e.g. window matching)

I have a quick play with it about every six months, then turn it off :D

### Post by Kellemora on 2009-03-19
Hi Mesmith

Don't know if it's the same thing or not, but we use the FADE feature in Compiz and it speeds up editing work considerably.

We can open the page of a document or image, open a second copy over the top of it and FADE it out enough to see both copies, one under the other clearly, this makes differences stick out like a sore thumb.

Most of our work is with images, and some of those images are group images.
By opening the primary image first, then opening a second blank image over it, we can pinpoint the individuals and even outline them if necessary to make an overlay where the individuals are either numbered or their initials are shown right where their face would be.

Those are just a couple of the uses we find the FADE feature most useful for.
