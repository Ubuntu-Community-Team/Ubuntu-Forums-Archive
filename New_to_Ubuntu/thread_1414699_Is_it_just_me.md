### Post by degarb on 2010-02-23
Is it just me, or is the cd auto mount support leave much to be desired?

A user wrote, " I just realized, in all the years I used Linux, I never swapped out cds."  This is because not much linux commercial software, probably. So, I bet this explains the roughness of the cd swapping.  It is as buggy as windows 9x had blue screens.

With no cd in drive, I just lost my drive in nautilus, eject button wasn't working, and had to go terminal to type eject /dev/sr0.  New users cannot do this, wife could not.  Then while installing some stuff, I had to constantly unmount cd, eject, then remount in nautists.  Some times it was automatic.   a swap that would be 15 seconds in xp, took 90 seconds in ubuntu, as I would have to wait for menu items to appear to do next step.  Very perplexing at first, until I get the hang of it.  But will always irritate.   

I hope they fix this by next release.  If the kids were trying to play a game, they could never figure out how to unmount in order to eject.
