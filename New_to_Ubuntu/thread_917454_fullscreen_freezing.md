### Post by Bush_Roo on 2008-09-13
Generally it is enough to turn off the compiz screen effects thingi. They are great for a person that doesn't do 3D games and messes about with office applications, but for now they are a bit of a pain for most 3D gamers. If you are running 7.10 + it is pretty simple to turn it off:

System > Preferences > Appearance > Visual Effects (5th tab up the top) > None

This generally fixes the problem right away. Unfortunately though, there is a small screensaver bug that temporarily disables the keys from responding for a moment. Say you are walking down a long hall-way or something on a 3D game, and suddenly, you aren't walking anymore, but have to re-press the keys that make you walk again. This is caused by the screensaver. Some people live with it, but some people prefer to disable the screensaver:

System > Preferences > Screensaver > Untick "Activate screensaver when computer is idol"

Straight forward, and it is simple to reverse if you want the screensaver/compiz enabled again to show off Ubuntu to your friends :)
