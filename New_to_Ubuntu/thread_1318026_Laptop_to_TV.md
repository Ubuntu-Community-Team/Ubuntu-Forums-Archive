### Post by Calmor on 2009-11-07
I guess one of my first questions, may be obvious, was whether or not you enabled the output to the TV.  You can do this through the display settings, but often, laptops also have a hardware button that can ensure this works.

The other issue I've had in the past with VGA adapters is that some of them are just plain incompatible with some TVs.  I had a pair of VGA to HDMI converter boxes... real solid things, metal case, everything... but the TV wouldn't recognize its output as a valid signal.  It recognized that there was some signal attached and would let me switch to that input, but I never received a picture.

So, in my very limited experience, going from VGA to HDMI is a bit of a challenge.  On the other hand, I have a Mythbuntu server with native HDMI output which worked well, and I bought an NVidia card with DVI output that I use a DVI to HDMI converter on with no problems.  Your mileage may vary, but I'd bet it has to do with all the conversion...
