### Post by robin7 on 2014-02-22
To avoid trouble, don't "mix and match" too much until you know your way around.  You can certainly add Lubu to Xubu (and Kubu to Ubu and all in between), but use the meta-packages (lubuntu-desktop, xubuntu-desktop, etc) and choose log-in sessions accordingly (xubuntu rather than Xfce, Lubuntu rather than LXDE, etc) too.  I'm suggesting it because while it's possible to use the Xfce4 window manager in LXDE and the Gnome panel in Xfce, etc., you really have to know what you're doing to avoid issues.  The people who "mix" Lubu, Xubu, Kubu, and Ubu really know what they're doing.  It used to bother me that there was so much Gnome stuff in my Xubuntu, until I "purged all the Gnomey stuff out of my Xubuntu" and ran into all sorts of new and strange issues.  I ended up re-installing Xubuntu and leaving it be.  I still mix-and-match on occasion, but for the most part I have learned to trust the developers' judgment when they create these amazing mixtures that run so wonderfully.
