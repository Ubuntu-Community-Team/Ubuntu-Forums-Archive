### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-05-26
Preview the file (File > Page Preview). If the red lines do not appear in the page, then they are not in the document; they are (as already mentioned) your spell-checker showing you misspellings.

If your recipient has red lines, they are your recipient's spell-checker showing misspellings (and they may differ from yours, depending on his spell-checker).

If they *do* appear in your page preview, post again here.

### Post by audiomick on 2011-05-26
If you just want to turn off the spell checker so that it stops annoying you, in Open Office got to 
extras> options

you need to uncheck the box that is shown in my screenshot. Sorry it is in German.

"Spracheinstellungen" means "Language options" or something similar
"Linguistik" "linguistics"
"Rechscheibung während der Eingabe prüfen" means "Check spelling while typing".

If you can find that and remove it, it wont constantly check your spelling and wont show the red lines. If you then want to check you spelling, you need to invoke it manually. It is in "Extras".

Are the mis-spellings showing in the email client, or in the word processor being used by the recipient?
