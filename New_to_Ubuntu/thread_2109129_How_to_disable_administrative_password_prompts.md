### Post by monkeybrain2012 on 2013-01-26
The password requirement, among other things, prevents things such as viruses and malware to install stuffs behind your back or otherwise gain access to your system. It is not a measure against some guy physically getting hold of your computer. Password requirement also guards against you doing stupid things accidentally.If I understand you correctly that is not a very wise thing that you want to do (Windows mindset?)

### Post by frank604 on 2013-01-26
Yeah, it is good practice to have password requirement as the other poster says.  Especially if you are new to linux as you will probably be playing around with a lot of settings to configure ubuntu the way you want.

This is why I have my admin password as a weak password.  So that I can tap it in quick but have it as a safeguard against doing something stupid.  I am not expecting someone to 'hack' into my computer and guess my password. More as a safeguard. So my password can be 'hello' or simple words like that.  Hope that helps.
