### Post by AndyM3 on 2011-03-30
I say you should use either Ubuntu Maverick or Linux Mint 10. With GNOME, yes.
Before you go all "GNOME is fat, slow and will eat up my RAM", let me point out that, at least for me, GNOME eats up less than 250MB of RAM and zero swap. With Firefox open (you know, the #1 fattest browser ever), that figure rises to 350-400MB, and still no swap use. On Linux Mint Debian Edition, I hear GNOME takes about half of that space, by the way.

The reason I suggest GNOME is that I personally think usability is more important than anything else. And, as it stands today, GNOME is the most usable desktop environment for a Windows user migrating to Linux.

Just my $0.02. You're free to install whatever you want.
I do recommend you ask for permission, though. ;)

### Post by I2k4 on 2011-03-30
I'm still running XP on an Inspiron 8100 with the same specs, installed from the original CD-ROM with routine windows tweaks it idles on <200mb RAM, so be sure you're doing him a favor. There's also a question of what software he wants or needs, to do what he wants to do apart from booting an OS (OS's really do not fascinate most end users), and how much you'll be around for hand-holding in the next few weeks.
 
The full-featured Ubuntu Desktop edition is quite fast and resource light, and a lot easier for Windows converts than Lubuntu or I think most other versions.  I'm glad I tried it to learn a little  before moving on to the lighter and faster Lubuntu.  

That said, also be sure the machine actually plays DVDs before spending time burning one, mine only has a CD/RW drive and it will not read its USB 1.0 for boot.  You probably also want to take him through the install process.
