### Post by Paqman on 2012-02-08
> **SpaceyB said:**
> I'd also like to use Amarok as a standard musicplayer. Does Amarok work on both?

Yes.

To be honest, any version of Ubuntu will run acceptably well on your hardware. Ubuntu doesn't have particularly high demands for hardware. The limiting factor isn't going to be your RAM or processor, as Ubuntu runs fine on netbooks with lower specs. Graphics performance can be an issue, but even on standard Ubuntu there's a 2D version of the desktop that will run on pretty much anything.

Additionally, you're not stuck with one desktop after you install. If you installed Lubuntu but decided you preferred regular Ubuntu you could just install/uninstall the relevant packages from the repos and it would be exactly the same as reinstalling.

In terms of lightest to heftiest it goes Lubuntu > Xubuntu > Ubuntu > Kubuntu. But you do sacrifice both bling and features to gain that lightness.

In short: don't sweat it. Install whichever you like the look of.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-02-08
You do not mention what graphic adapter the laptop has. That is the bit of hardware that has to work your TV.

You may get a Linux distribution working fine on this laptop. It may even look good when using the TV as the monitor but you may be disappointed by performance of video playback because the video adapter does not have sufficient memory of its own to cache the video without waiting for more data to be downloaded.

Can the video adapter drive the TV at the optimum resolution of the TV?

Regards.
