### Post by QIII on 2010-07-30
The ATI driver you have in Windows is not Microsoft's work.  It is  ATI's.  Don't fall into the common misconception that Windows provides  driver support.  They allow "certified" drivers to be included in  their driver base.  That is all the "support" Microsoft provides.  The OEM is responsible for writing drivers that are  compatible with Windows.
 
 The company that is now AMD/ATI has discontinued driver support for your  line of cards and provides only a "legacy" Linux driver -- that is, it won't  be updated.  Unfortunately, that driver (and the CCC for it) will not work with the version  of X server that is now used by Ubuntu.  That is not Ubuntu's fault.   Nor do I blame AMD/ATI.  How much effort and how many resources do you  expect the company to expend keeping up with a driver in an environment  in which X may change for a card that may already have reached or passed its expected mechanical service life?
 
 Your only choice is is the Open Source driver.

In case you think that AMD/ATI is any different from NVIDIA in this regard, try using a geForce2 MX 400 card in anything after Intrepid.
