### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-06-30
> **Arazu said:**
> I have a failing HD as evidenced here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1791840](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1791840)

Second, I'd like to run Ubuntu on the laptop. I'm a little wary of Ubuntu on Dell laptops though. Am I being paranoid?

Probably.  Get the ISO's for 10.04 LTS and 11.04 and make a boot CD or USB whichever is most appropriate for you and try them out.  Select the one that gives you the most trouble free experience.  At most you might have to use a wired ethernet connection to get some wireless drivers and/or graphics drivers. 

> What I would like to do is move the files from my failing HD to the laptop, find my backup HD and swap it out as primary in my tower, then copy the files back over.

The cheapest way would be to get a crossover ethernet cable and make your own little two PC network.  No router is required, and for a couple of bucks you will have the two machines linked together.  If you've never set up a linux network before you may struggle with some of the details but there's plenty of help out there.

Another and simpler alternative would be to get a backup USB drive and use that.  They are getting a lot cheaper now, and it is something everyone should have for backups in any case because it makes life so simple.

Chris.
