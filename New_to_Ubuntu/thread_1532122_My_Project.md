### Post by cascade9 on 2010-07-16
I'd go for a 'real' AM3 motherboard over the AM2/AM2+/AM3 'fake' AM3 motherboards. You might have to spend a little bit more on the RAM/motherbaord, but its worth it. 

I am assuming that you like patriot memory, you can get 2x2GB DDR3 1600 cheaper than the 2x2GB DDR2 1066- 

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820220481](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820220481)

A motherboard might cost a little more, but you can get one with firewire onbaord and then its cheaper than the MSI + firewire card- 

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128443](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128443)

That board has USB 3.0 and SATA III, both of which will be usefull later, even if they arent now. BTW, from my quick testing, it looks like even USB 2.0 devices run faster under USB 3.0 than they did with USB 2.0...I know I can delete and copy files to my flash driver faster with USB 3.0.

Edit- you might not know this is possible, but with the right motherboards, Phenom II X2 CPUs can be 'unlocked' to become X4s. Nice, when a Phenom II X2 is cheaper than an Athlon II X4.
