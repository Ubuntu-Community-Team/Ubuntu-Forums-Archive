### Post by mdsmedia on 2009-02-14
Your Ubuntu CD should have a partitioning tool (GParted) on it. Don't ask me for instructions, I've never had to use it, but you'll need to repartition your hard drive. Booting from CD should bring up GParted. From there you should be able to partition your drive.

When you reinstall Windows I believe that it will remove (or at least upset) your boot record, so you won't be able to boot into Linux until that's restored. Let me find a thread on that for you.

[edit]I found this "How To" on reinstalling Grub:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=224351&highlight=restore+boot](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=224351&highlight=restore+boot)

At least, back up everything first.
