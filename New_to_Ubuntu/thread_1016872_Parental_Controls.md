I don't know of any specific software for filtering web content under linux.

However, my experience with parent controls on the web has been that they serve more as an irritant to the parent, as the child can generally get around them.

A better idea might be to personally supervise your child while they're on the internet. This practice can lead to some high quality interaction with your child, and has the added bonus of being difficult for your child to defeat.

If you're talking about a child that likes to sneak onto the internet while you're not about, give the child user-level access and turn off the ability to connect to the web on their user account.

Remember, this only works if they *never* get the password to an administrator account, however. If they can get in as root, they can turn web access right back on for themselves.
