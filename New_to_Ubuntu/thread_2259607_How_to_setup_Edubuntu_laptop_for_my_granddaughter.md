### Post by ajgreeny on 2015-01-05
To pin the gcompris icon to the launchbar on the left side of the screen just start gcompris.  An icon for it will then appear in the launchbar; right click on it and choose "Lock to launchbar"

To open gcompris at bootup just add it to the list of session autostart applications; I'm not sure how you do that in unity but it must be somewhere in the settings manager system.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-01-05
Do not underestimate a 6 year old. At that age they are quick to learn. Everything is new and natural. Learning new things is only hard for us oldsters. Or so some of us think and then resist learning anything new.

Startup Applications is not in Ubuntu System Settings. Search for it the Dash. You click Add and then Browse to where the application is. That is the best that I can offer as I have never felt the need to add an application to Startup Applications.

Let her learn how to use the operating system. Do not restrict her by setting everything up so that she does not have to think. That is my advice.
