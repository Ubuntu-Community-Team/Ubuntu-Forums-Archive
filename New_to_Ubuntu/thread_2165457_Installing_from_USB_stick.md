### Post by cortman on 2013-08-05
You can create a bootable USB drive with either LiLi or UnetBootin (I've had success with both). After that insert the flash drive into the netbook USB port, select "boot menu" at startup (I think on my Acer netbook the hotkey is F12) and then you'll be able to try out the Ubuntu desktop without installing. There'll be an "Install" link on the desktop for when you're ready to install.
If Ubuntu runs too slow for you (it did for me) you could look at lighter alternatives such as Lubuntu, Xubuntu, or (my preference) Linux Mint XFCE edition.
Good luck! :)
