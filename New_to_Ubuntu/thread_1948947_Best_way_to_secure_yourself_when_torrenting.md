### Post by tjoff on 2012-03-30
I agree that the Tor network should not be abused for petty criminal activity like copyright infringement.

However, I must question the statement > Leave it for those who need it for the reason it exists, to protect the anonymity of people living under repressive regimes.
I do not think the people running the Tor project would agree with this.

Firstly, according to the tor project homepage itself, Tor [is relevant to a much wider audience than just politically oppressed people]("https://www.torproject.org/about/torusers.html.en").

Secondly, the security model of Tor depends exactly on the fact that it is used by many different people for many different purposes. The larger and more diverse the network is, the better the anonymity is.
Quoted from the Tor project homepage:
> 
As Tor's usability increases, it will attract more users, which will increase the possible sources and destinations of each communication, thus increasing security for everyone.
> 
Each new user and relay provides additional diversity, enhancing Tor's ability to put control over your security and privacy back into your hands.
