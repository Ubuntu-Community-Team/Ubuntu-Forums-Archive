### Post by sdennie on 2008-08-27
Have you tried leaving everything at default and then just putting "nvidia-settings -l" as a command in System->Preferences->Session?  I don't know that it will fix the problem but, it's something to try.  You may also want to try:

```

sudo nvidia-xconfig

```

That can sometimes give you a valid xorg.conf and the "nvidia-settings -l" may then fix your problem.  Verify that nvidia-settings is installed though by typing it at the commandline at least once.
