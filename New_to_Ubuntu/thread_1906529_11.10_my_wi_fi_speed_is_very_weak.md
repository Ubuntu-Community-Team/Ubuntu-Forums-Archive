### Post by Bartender on 2012-01-10
I was asking about this same [problem]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1901351") a week ago.  wildmanne got me set up with the "fix".  Actually, turning off the "n" band isn't a fix so much, but our wi-fi is working now and I don't have an "n" router anyway.

I ran Speedtest.net over Ethernet, then wi-fi, and got results that were essentially identical after the "fix".  Beforehand, wi-fi was non-functional.

You should sign up at launchpad and add your name to this [bug report]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/network-manager/+bug/875780").  You can search for other Intel 4965 bug reports and maybe sign on to those also.  You'll notice that there's a button near the top of a bug report where you can say "This affects me too".  That's only if you create an account!

launchpad is a way to get the developers' attention.  They don't wade thru all the threads here on the forum looking for things to fix.

Hi, 
To disable 802.11n on this card create /edit your /etc/modprobe.d/options.conf file like this.
Code:
Code:
gksu gedit /etc/modprobe.d/options.conf
And add the following to it:
Code:
options iwl4965 11n_disable=1
