### Post by Kevdunleavy on 2010-10-04
Gnome provides the "panels" at the top and bottom of the screen. If you didnt have Gnome installed, you'd still have your Firefox and all that, you just wouldn't have the Applications menu etc. to use to get to it.

My understanding of it is that Gnome basically adds more functionality to the desktop by providing panels and the system tray etc.

### Post by Marlonsm on 2010-10-04
Gnome is a Desktop environement, just like KDE(for Kubuntu).
It manages what you see in your screen.
It is, the menus, the task bars, the border of the windows...
Programs for Gnome are made to look "at home" in Gnome, so their look should match the rest of the system. But you can also run programs made for other Desktop environements in Gnome, they will just look diferent.
