### Post by qyot27 on 2010-12-14
If you want something reminiscent of Winamp, take a look at Audacious ([Wikipedia article](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audacious_%28software%29), [main site](http://www.audacious-media-player.org/)).  While its current (GTKUI) interface resembles foobar2000, the older Winamp-style one can be switched to easily.  It also supports Winamp 2.x skins, if you prefer that.

You can probably find it by going through the Software Center or Synaptic.  Or you could do *sudo apt-get install audacious* in the Terminal.  I would partially suggest going through Synaptic, as then you can also find some of the other relevant decoding plugins, like the one for AAC/MP4.

That said though, [Winamp itself can be run with the assistance of Wine](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=5).  But you'd likely be better off using Audacious instead if that's the sort of interface/user experience you're looking for.
