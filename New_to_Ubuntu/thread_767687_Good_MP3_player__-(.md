### Post by xnef1025 on 2008-04-25
Try Amarok.  It's available through the Add/Remove... icon in the Application menu.  It should have most of what you want.  Multiple visualizations, library and playlist functions, close to tray when you want to do something else while you listen and it has an on screen display notifier that pops up when tracks change.

Now that you have mp3 playback enabled, it should work right off the bat.  It'll ask you where your music files are the first time you open it and populate your library automatically.  It's also got Last.fm support and a really nice album art tagging function.  It searches amazon to find the artwork for your albums and lets you choose which picture you want to use.

The only time it's given me trouble is when I first set up my "All Music" playlist.  The first time you try to drag and drop a few thousand files into the playlist window it could crash, but once you get the playlist loaded and saved you're golden.:guitar:

### Post by osinisa on 2008-04-28
There a couple you can try. 

[Exaile](http://www.exaile.org/) aims to be Amarok on GNOME. Good little player, has custom layout themes, couple of plugins. Overall good. Isn't Amarok of course since it's pretty new, but the basic features are there. You'll have to follow the instructions on the site to download the latest version cause it's not in the repository yet.


[Listen]("http://www.listen-project.org/") has more features than Exaile and I would use it, however the layout design is just awful. The least used column is right in the middle and I haven't found an easy way to change that.

There's also [Quod Libet]("http://www.sacredchao.net/quodlibet/"). I didn't like how you had to go in the bar on the top to change views instead of putting tabs like Amarok and Exaile do. Or at least have a column with a list like Listen.

There's XMMS that looks like Winamp if that tickles your fancy.

I came back to Amarok because it has the most features (it also has a lot of people on its core team). The only downside is that it's KDE and it clashes with the GNOME look and feel. That can be fixed somewhat if you get kcontrol and change the theme to "plastik" and play around with the colours (search the forums for "kcontrol" "Amarok"). And then if you feel like it, you can change the icons to [these]("http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Human+Amarok?content=76258")


I haven't tried [Songbird]("http://www.songbirdnest.com/") but I might. Looks like iTunes.
