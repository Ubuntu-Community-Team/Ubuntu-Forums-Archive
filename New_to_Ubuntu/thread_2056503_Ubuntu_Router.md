### Post by papibe on 2012-09-11
Hi Tomb49. Welcome to the forums ):P

Most routers will work just fine. I would choose one that supports local DNS for you machines, so they can easily access each other by name.

There are routers that come with Linux installed, BTW.

One of the best know Linux based router firmware is [DD-WRT]("http://www.dd-wrt.com/site/index"). A good alternative, then, would be to buy a supported device, regardless what it comes with, and install dd-wrt when you get your hands on it.

Just some thoughts.
