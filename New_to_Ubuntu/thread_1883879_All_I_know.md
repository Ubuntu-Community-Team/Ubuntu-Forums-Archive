### Post by I2k4 on 2011-11-24
Regarding choice of netbooks, there's really no substitute for going to a big box or three to compare the row of competitors, and get a feel for the keyboards, layouts, screen quality, looks and fit and finish.  Nearly all these machines have the same innards, are selling for about the same price, and like all computers are pretty reliable, but they really do look and feel different in daily use, and preference is subjective. Of course, once you actually pick a machine, check the online retailers for the best deal.

The couple of things worth comparing online, depending on your preferences, would be battery life and availability of different sized batteries aftermarket, and the ease of internal access for replacing things like RAM and hard drives.  The different models vary significantly in these, and often buyers don't think of them before committing.

My first Acer One bought in 2008 was twice the price of the second, yet except for the change from XP to W7 Starter, that led me to Linux experiments, everything about the new one is better.  I personally like the sharp and bright Acer screens and the recent keyboard has a quality feel.
