### Post by etali on 2009-04-18
Since you're asking in the Ubuntu forums you'll likely hear a lot of good things about Ubuntu :)  

Really, I'd suggest you download a few different Live CDs (assuming you're not on a capped net connection) so that you can try out a few different releases.  Live CDs run a little slow since the OS is running from a CD rather than installing to your hard drive, but it means you're not risking your Windows setup until you're sure you like whatever Linux flavour you decide to run. 

Kubuntu is my preference for the desktop.

Once you've found one that you like and you have all your hardware / apps working, you can make the leap to a full install.
