### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-12-06
> **keithconly said:**
> I'm sorry but i still do not know what to buy, is this just something i could look under the features of the product?

Look up the websites of your local PC stores to see what they've got available. Then go to Google and do a search for the product number and then the word "chipset", for instance:

Netgear WG111 chipset

This should tell you what chipset is used.

Avoid Realtek and Broadcom. Atheros seems to be alright these days and it will only get better.
