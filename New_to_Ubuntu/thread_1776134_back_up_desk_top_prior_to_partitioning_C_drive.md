### Post by Lateralis on 2011-06-05
In addition to dFlyer's sage advice, you may want to check out [Clonezilla]("http://clonezilla.org/"), which is a free disk cloning tool.  I personally have never used it (there are other tools I use from within Ubuntu/Linux) but it seems to be well regarded on these forums and is very often recommended.  As dFlyer says, this will create an actual image - a direct clone - of your hard drive, so you can fully restore your computer to its current state in the event of a "disaster" without having to install Windows, then install all of your drivers, programs, files, videos, music etc...  

As for where things are kept, files, folders and other things on your desktop are stored in C:/Users/<Username>/Desktop, where <Username> is your login name.
