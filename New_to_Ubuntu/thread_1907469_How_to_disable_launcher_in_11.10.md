### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-01-11
You can use the Ubuntu Software Centre to install something called CompizConfig Settings Manager. You will then find it in the Dash by typing Compiz. Look for something called Ubuntu Unity Plugin. Make sure that there is a tick mark against it to enable it and then click on the name.

You will see an option under the Behaviour tab to Hide Launcher. You have four options 1) Never - the Launcher will always be visible. 2) Autohide - the present setting. 3) Dodge windows - the launcher moves out of the way as a window comes near. 4) Dodge active window.

As much as some people do not like this look of Ubuntu it is not going to go away.

Regards.

P.S. have you read the Ubuntu Desktop guide? You will find it in the Dash by typing help.

### Post by m.a.baytas on 2012-01-11
In CompizConfig Settings Manager, under the "Behavior" tab, I set the "Reveal Mode" to "Right" and the "Hide Launcher" setting to "Autohide".

Now, for some reason, the launcher hides automatically and will not appear when I move the mouse to either the left or right edges of the screen.

When I press <Super> the launcher appears in its usual location, on the left. When I minimze a window, it minimizes to the left and its icon peeks from the left. Other than these, there doesn't seem to be any way to make the launcher appear.

I had Avant installed while I tried this, although I don't think Avant has anything to do with it.

Hope it works for you too.
