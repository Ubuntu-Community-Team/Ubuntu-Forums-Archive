### Post by lechien73 on 2012-01-09
Hi & welcome to the forums,

A lot of what you're talking about comes down to a correct diagnostic technique, which can take years to develop. A good initial guide to learning a diagnostic technique is here: [http://www.pcguide.com/ts/gen/diag.htm](http://www.pcguide.com/ts/gen/diag.htm)

Much, though, comes down to knowing what the computer is doing and why - you then develop a kind of "feel" for what might be wrong. When I first started out as a computer technician about 20 years ago, I learned x86 assembly language. This helped me to know exactly how the processor works.
