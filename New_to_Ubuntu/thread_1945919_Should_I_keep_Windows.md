### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-03-24
Personally, if your PC already has Win7 on it, I would leave it alone and just use the Win7 Disk Management utility to shrink it down.  Once you no longer need it, then you can look into removing it.

Why bother with a VM when you already have the OS?  You would have to reinstall Win7 and ALL the apps (and drivers) again -- from scratch -- if you decided to switch over to using a VM.  And all of this because of the MINOR inconvenience of having to reboot to use Win7?

You also need to realize that when a PC comes with Win7 preinstalled, you are NOT provided installation media; so basically, you would not have the media needed to reinstall it in a VM anyway.  Any media that DOES come with it will only provide the function of erasing the drive and reinstalling the Win7 partition from scratch -- the way it came originally.
