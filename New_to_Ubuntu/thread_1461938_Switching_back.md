### Post by QIII on 2010-04-24
While WINE may perform slowly or not at all for some applications, and while some virtualization may not perform well if you do not commit enough resources to the virtual machine, multi-booting has no effect whatsoever on the performance of any of the OSs used.

If you multi-boot, you load Windows or Linux or Unix or any flavor thereof natively and independently of any other OS that can be selected for use.

If there are things you simply must do in Windows (and don't let anyone tell you you shouldn't!  This is all about choice!) but want to also use a Linux distribution, then I recommend that you multi-boot.  The only disadvantage is that you have to completely restart the machine to select the other OS to begin running it.  

I don't think two minutes is too much to ask.

### Post by cloyd on 2010-04-24
Ubuntu is a great operating system. But one of the most important things to remember is that it is _not_ windows. It doesn't run windows programs. However, it uses other programs which will do pretty much the same things  . . . without hogging resources, without crashing, without having someone else take over your machine and force their web browser on you, without the worry about viruses and antivirus programs like norton (however, common sense about security is still necessary).  Ubuntu does what it is supposed to do faster, making the most of your machine's capabilities. My netbook runs better than my much more expensive laptop on vista or my wife's more expensive laptop on XP.

There are other programs that do the same things. There is a learning curve. The commands are not organized the same.  The upside is that the programs are free, and there are a world of them available to you. It is very much worth the effort to learn.  And yes, I would recommend that you dual boot.  Keep your windows for what you must do that you haven't learned to do in Ubuntu yet. But if you use it enough, I promise you, you'll find yourself using windows less and less. 

I'm not a gamer, but from what I hear from others here on this forum, if you have some windows games you must play, it may be a good idea to dual boot.  However, there is wine, and there are linux games and there is the possibility of setting up a virtual machine.  I have been told by someone that has reason to know that XP runs better in a virtual machine on linux than as the base install. 

For me, it is word processing, spreadsheets, music email and net surfing.  It works.
