### Post by pingram3541 on 2017-05-31
Running Ubuntu 17 on a dell XPS w/ a 4k display.  The laptop screen is 4k but scaled 1.5x and my external 42" is also 4k but scaled at 1x.  When I connect the second monitor (HDMI2) any time I open an app, it opens on the first monitor in the 0,0 coords (top, left) just underneath my top task bar making it impossible to click/drag the window because it is underneath the task bar.  I have to press Alt+F7 to move the window to reveal the upper portion of the window's chrome to be able to move it.

When I disconnect the second monitor all apps open just fine in the middle of the screen.  Any ideas?

### Post by pingram3541 on 2017-06-28
Through the help of another online service I was able to resolve this by installing the Compiz Config Settings Manager specifically Window Management > Place Windows > Placement Mode > Centered and also checked the Workarounds option.
