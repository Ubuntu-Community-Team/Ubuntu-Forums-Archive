### Post by cwsnyder on 2008-09-06
The Linux Format magazine ( [http://www.linuxformat.co.uk/](http://www.linuxformat.co.uk/) ) is a resource for ASUS EEE modifications and tricks.  They even had a version of Linux customized for the EEE on their DVD (Breeezy)which came with their August issue, and had 6 pages in the June issue on customizing the user interface.

I understand there is also a version of Puppy Linux (Puppeee) which has been tweaked for the ASUS nettop.

There is mention in their articles of installing Kubuntu and Fedora (alternately, not at the same time), although on the smaller ASUS EEE models with only 2G of off-line storage, I believe they installed them on either USB Flash or SD Flash memory.  They definitely mentioned methods of backing up the native operating software.
