### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-09-30
Couple of questions come to mind.

1. Do you have any message filters set up? edit/message filters
If so, check to see if it's setup correctly and if the filter results are being sent to the junk folder. To check each filter: left click once on the the filter to highlight and select edit on the row of vertical buttons on the right side of the popup window.

2. Where the incoming e-mails ever marked as junk? If so, when checking your junk folder after downloading e-mail, right click on the non-junk e-mails and select the option "Not junk".

3. What is selected in the edit/preferences/mail preferences/junk tab?

Hope this helps.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-10-01
One thought is to check your trash folder and see if the junk was moved there. However, if you've emptied your trash or have your trash empty automatically:
(edit/preferences/mail preferences/general tab/empty trash folders on exit - "every time - ????")
Then I don't know how to recover those e-mails. Unless they were left on the server.

On one account my wife & I share, I leave the e-mails on our ISP server for two days. This assures we both get the e-mail. If you wish to leave your e-mail on the server for a few days until your sure everything works:

(edit/preferences/select account and left click one time to highlight, then select the "edit" button on the vertical row of buttons on the right/ select the "receiving options tab")
Under message storage place a check in the box in front of "Leave messages on server".  Then on the next row down place a check in the box in front of "delete after" and in the box just to the right, insert the number of days you wish to leave your messages on the server.

Good Luck & I hope this helps.
