### Post by audiomick on 2011-01-19
I've had that occasionally on my Lenovo Ideapad. I don't know exactly why it happens, or what you can do to prevent it. However, there might be some help here. Go into
system> preferences> key combinations 
or maybe something similar to that. My system is in German, so I have to translate.
In there, there is a section on window management. There is a key combination to move the window, and a key combination to de-maximise it, which you may have to do before you can move it.
To toggle between maximised and not maximised on my machine is alt + F10
To move the window is alt + F7

With these two, you might be able to pull the window up to see the bottom of it. I haven't tried this, but I think it will work.

edit: the post below works.
If you use alt + F7, left click or esc to let go.

### Post by osb_tensor on 2011-01-19
i just had this problem answered on this forum this morning.... alt+leftclickmouse and drag the window up or down or wherever you need to go. you don't have to click on the titlebar either.. you can click anywhere on the window and move it. i can't guarantee that it will let you resize it once it's moved, but you can at least see all of the window even if in stages.
