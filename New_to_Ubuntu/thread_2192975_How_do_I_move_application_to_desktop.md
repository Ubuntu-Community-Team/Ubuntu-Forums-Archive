### Post by deadflowr on 2013-12-10
Do you want to add them to the launcher or splatter them all over the desktop?

If you want to add them to the launcher, drag and drop.
Or simply launch the app and then it's icon will show in the launcher.
Here you right click on it and select lock to launcher.

If, however, you like having launcher splattered all over the place across the desktop window, you'll need to do a little more work.
For some versions, you use to be able to simply drag the launch to the desktop, but in newer version(at least 13.10) you can't(or at least I haven't been able to).

So, in that case, try this.
Open the files, or home folder and click on "computer", or "file system" (depending on which version of Ubuntu you are running the names are one of the two)
In the file system go to usr then go to share then go to applications.
In here find the app you want and right click on it and select copy.
Then go back to your folder (hint look at the panel where the computer/file system lising is and click on your username that is listed at the top)
In your home folder open the folder "Desktop"
Now paste what you have copied.
The app should be visible on the desktop.

Note though, that this simply creates a shortcut to the original and will be missing some of the functions that one in the launcher will have, such as quicklists(that's the right click option; some quicklists have more functions built-in, like media players will have play,pause,stop,next, etc,etc)

Have fun.
