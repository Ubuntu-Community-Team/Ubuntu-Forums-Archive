### Post by jonnyboysmithy on 2013-08-27
Seeing after you dropped your laptop and your bios couldn't find any your operating system my guess is your disk is dead. 
It would also explain why ubuntu thinks theres not enough free space.

Boot your live system (cd, usb drive, or whatever it what you put it on.) select "try ubuntu without installing". 
After starting up t should give you a desktop that looks like this: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ubuntu_13.04_Desktop.png](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ubuntu_13.04_Desktop.png) 

Click on the button that looks like a folder in the top left corner second from top. Under "Devices" on the left your disk should show up. See attached screenshot. See if you can see your files, copy paste some. If it works I'd say its functional. But I'd get disk utility and run a self test to test for errors.
