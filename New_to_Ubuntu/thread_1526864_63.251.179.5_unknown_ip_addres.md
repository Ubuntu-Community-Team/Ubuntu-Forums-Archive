### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2010-07-16
IMO you should not be running samba or nfs over the internet, use ssh (scp, winscp, sshfs).

63.251.179.5 is a public IP address, your lan would be 192.168. 

[http://www.debianadmin.com/private-and-public-ip-addresses-explained.html](http://www.debianadmin.com/private-and-public-ip-addresses-explained.html)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address)

Your public ip address is assigned by your IP provider and (as with your LAN) can be static or dhcp.

Many public IP, going to private residences for example, will be dhcp, and thus will not be registered.

You can determine your private IP with ifconfig
Your Public ip on any number fo sites

[http://www.showipaddress.com/](http://www.showipaddress.com/)

run a whois on your public ip address =)
