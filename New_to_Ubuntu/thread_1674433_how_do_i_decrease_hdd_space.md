### Post by Bucky Ball on 2011-01-23
Slip in the LiveCD, boot into 'Try Ubuntu', open System>Administration>Gparted (or partition editor it may be called), unmount your Linux partition, right click and shrink it. ;)

---

### Post by cgroza on 2011-01-23
Boot a Ubuntu Live CD and there should be an utility called gParted under System->Administration. 
It is a partition editor. You need to resize your Ubuntu partition with that to the desired size.

### Post by beew on 2011-01-23
He should use the Mint live usb/cd  instead of the Ubuntu one, that way he could use gparted (in the mint live usb) to shrink the Ubuntu partition in the hdd and then install Mint into the unallocated space without having to reboot.
