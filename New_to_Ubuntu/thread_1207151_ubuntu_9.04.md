### Post by lswb on 2009-07-07
Direct upgrade from 8.04 to 9.04 is not supported. See
[http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/upgrading](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/upgrading)

Whatever you do, it is a very good idea to backup any important personal files and data first. If you read through the forums after a new release there are always a small but significant number of people who have problems and lose data.

I am currently running both 8.04 and 9.04 and have yet to make up my mind about switching over to 9.04 completely.

Network Manager is much improved (for my uses anyway, others say differently) in 9.04 vs 8.04.

If you have Intel graphics be prepared to follow one of the howtos or sticky threads to get acceptable performance, the stock setup has some real problems with video playback.

Boot time is much quicker with 9.04 than 8.04, which is a good thing, since 9.04 is not as stable in my experience. (Better than 8.10, though) It's very dissapointing to be using linux and have to reboot like it's windows 98 or something. I have crashes with 8.04 once in a while too, but not as often. Hopefully 9.10 ?

I have a few hardware items that stopped working with 8.04 and 8.10 that are working again with 9.04 

IIRC some tweaking of PulseAudio was necessary in 8.04 to get sound working 100%, I didn't need to do that with 9.04.

Those are the things that stick out in my mind, anyway.
