### Post by freduardo on 2008-06-05
You're right bumanie,

K9copy allows you to copy and shrink your DVD to your hard drive. It's stored as both an AUDIO_TS AND VIDEO_TS combination (vob files) and as an .iso image.

But, k9copy also allows you to encode it to an .avi. Though I think that, like billgoldberg suggested, dvdrip is better suited for that (more configurable).
