Well it's not ditched for another year, which is when Jaunty stops being supported.

The problem is this difference between what the end user (us) expects and the current state of Ubuntu. When we see a new version out, it's natural for us to want to install it and use it, and we have an expectation that it will work as well as the last version we used but with all of the new features. Unfortunately that's often not the case within the first couple of weeks of it coming out. Every other OS has these problems as well of course; sometimes the changeover is done well and sometimes it's more problematic.

I guess the best way to bridge this gap from our position is to spread the information to other users as broadly as we can - be on the safe side and don't install the latest version until most of the the bugs have been worked out (usually a couple of weeks), or you have the experience to sort them out yourself and want to take the risk.

And on a personal level we will all just have to resist the temptation to have the shiny new toy for 2 weeks or so. I was tempted to upgrade and try it out but I'm glad that I've held back, especially because I use MythTV and VLC a great deal and these would very likely have problems (because they are not maintained by Canonical).

Every other OS has these problems as well of course; sometimes the changeover is done well and sometimes it's more problematic.

I guess the best way to bridge this gap from our position is to spread the information to other users as broadly as we can - be on the safe side and don't install the latest version until most of the the bugs have been worked out (usually a couple of weeks), or you have the experience to sort them out yourself and want to take the risk.

And on a personal level we will all just have to resist the temptation to have the shiny new toy for 2 weeks or so. I was tempted to upgrade and try it out but I'm glad that I've held back, especially because I use MythTV and VLC a great deal and these would very likely have problems (because they are not maintained by Canonical).
