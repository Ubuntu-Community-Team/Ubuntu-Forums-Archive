### Post by saj0577 on 2009-06-26
If the computer is running edubuntu and its fitted with a dvd drive I dont see why it should not, from a manufacturing side having a dvd player without the other hardware to support it would seem crazy.

Dvd movie should run smoothly fine (you may notice problems when fast forwarding if low on system spec) but the standard requirements of edubuntu should be fine:

"CPU of at least 1 GHz, and 256 MB of RAM"

I've rarely seen a machine that had trouble with just plain dvd playback. It's more advanced things, like when your playing back 264 video with all the mainline extra features enabled that a CPU can really bog down and stutter.

Just install the gfx driver for whatever is there and I'm sure it'll be fine. No ram upgrade needed as long as they have least 512 IMO. If your a nice guy I'd bump em up to 1 GB for a good experience with a swap of around 1-2 GB.
