### Post by DrMilo on 2010-05-09
I don't know enough to tell you what's going on. However, I can tell you that Ipod keeps doing firmware updates that cause this problem with many Linux programs. All that I can say is that you'll have to find a Linux software that you are sure will work with your Ipod flavor, if you want to make use of it without borking you Ipod. 
And one other thing, when you use Itunes to put the songs in your Ipod, you're married to it, Linux programs pretty well never work with an Ipod after that.

### Post by zeekoman on 2010-05-09
What kind of computer did you plug it into? And.. did you open your iPod with iTunes or just browse the files on the hard-drive of the iPod? If you just looked at the files, you wont see anything.. Apple hides the music files in hundreds of folders throughout the iPod (They aren't arranged by Artist or Album) In Rhythmbox, did you try to play a song off of the iPod? Do you have any backup of your iPod? 
What I would recommend would to use WINE to run iTunes.. just so there isn't 3rd party junk messing around with the iPod. If you have a copy of all your music, use iTunes to restore the iPod, then put the music back on..
