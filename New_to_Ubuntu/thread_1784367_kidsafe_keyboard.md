### Post by deconstrained on 2011-06-16
Give them each their own, non-privileged user accounts on the laptop, and set up disk quotas;
[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-setup-disk-quotas-in-ubuntu.html](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-setup-disk-quotas-in-ubuntu.html)
Then, there's pretty much nothing harmful they could possibly do. Even if they did do something "so dumb it's smart" that messes something up I'm sure it would not be anything that couldn't be easily fixed/corrected.

Edit: I just found something that has no F-keys, super keys, or other keys that could cause "accidents":
[http://www.amazon.com/Crayola%C2%AE-USB-Type-Keyboard-11071/dp/B00167ZYMK](http://www.amazon.com/Crayola%C2%AE-USB-Type-Keyboard-11071/dp/B00167ZYMK)
Still though, they have the Ctrl & Alt keys to mess with. There's still a lot you can do with those two modifiers, but at least it's a start.
