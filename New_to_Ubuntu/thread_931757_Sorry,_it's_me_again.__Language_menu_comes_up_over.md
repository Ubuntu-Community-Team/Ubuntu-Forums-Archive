### Post by Elfy on 2008-09-28
For the moment - playing devils advocate, let's assume that the disc you got from shipit is no good, not likely but anything is possible.

Did you check that the download you burnt your livecd was good to start with - did you check the md5sum for it, if you didn't then do so, info [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM#MD5SUM on Windows")

If the download is good, when you burnt it did you use a cdr and not a cdrw and did you burn it ata slow speed - [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto)

You could well find that a usb kbd won't work then, mine won't but I have an oooold motherboard, if you can't find a ps2 kbd a ps2 to usb adaptor will be sufficient.

If you are able to do so, if you find that you have graphic card problems then for the time being you could just remove the card and try to boot without, but there's not any reason to be buying hardware - especially as we don't know yet what card you actually have.

I feel that until we can be sure that you have a 'good' disc we could be going in circles. 

For the moment having a ps2 kbd connection will make some difference.

A thought has struck me - do you know for certain that you have a graphics card, it could be that you have an onboard which will use part of your RAM, if the RAM available drops below 384MB then you will have trouble booting with the livecd and would need to use the alternate. You should be able to check that out in the BIOS - look for video shared memory or similar.
