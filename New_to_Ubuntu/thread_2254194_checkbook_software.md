### Post by ajgreeny on 2014-11-25
I find kmymoney2 is much like Quicken which I used a long time ago (probably the late 1990s, but I am not sure) and I still use kmymoney2 today.  It is perfect for my needs, whereas gnucash left me a bit baffled about how to use it; it appears to need a good knowledge of double-entry book-keeping to understand the workings of it, and after trying for some time when I first started using Ubuntu in 2005, I quickly gave up and looked for alternatives.

Homebank may also be of interest to you; I looked at it but have never used it, and it looked even simpler than kmymoney2.

### Post by Rob Sayer on 2014-11-26
As ajgreeny said, it's no problem running software that is from another DE unless you don't like installing a shedload of dependencies.  This will definitely happen if it's a KDE program because KDE is so highly integrated.  Unless you have very limited memory I wouldn't worry about it.
