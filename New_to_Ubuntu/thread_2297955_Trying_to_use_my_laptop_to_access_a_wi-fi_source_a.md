### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-10-07
Always confusing trying to fix a third-party's issue. Best if you can get your cousin to join and post here themselves, but having said that ...

With this much information there is not a lot of hope. When having trouble connecting at school or institutions with their own network, best to ask the IT department at the institution as your first stop. They will know a lot more about their system than we do. Have they talked to IT?

If all your cousin knows is the password for the network, which is of no help in solving the problem, and that's all the information you can provide here, your chances of help are slim. 

Good luck.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-10-08
Install *wifi-radar* on your machine and your cousin's machine.  Run it and have your cousin click on the school's AP.  Then talk him through the menus to set up the wifi connection.  I presume that your cousin's wifi works at home, so that the wireless connection works OK otherwise.
