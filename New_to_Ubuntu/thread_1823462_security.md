While opinions invariably vary, most people don't need a firewall because they are behind a home router, which will be protecting you; you can use Ubuntu Firewall ([ufw]("https://help.ubuntu.com/8.04/serverguide/C/firewall.html")).

As for anti-virus, you don't need it; there aren't really any Linux viruses in the wild.  Most of the AV apps predominantly scan for Windows viruses.

### Post by SavageWolf on 2011-08-12
Note that while there are very few viruses in the wild, people are still vulnerable to social engineering tricks. Don't install random debs from strangers, don't run anything as root unless you know what it does, don't give your passwords to people etc.
