### Post by Bucky Ball on 2010-11-24
My policy? Stick to the LTS releases if you need stability and long term support. Changing every six months ... asking for trouble and a nightmare. Unless you have LOTS of time to tinker or it is maybe even what you do for a living!

There is one other case: If there is a very specific reason for change to the latest greatest. I just bought a Toshiba Satellite Pro L510, brand new model, and what do you know? After no success whatsoever and much research I find that the only release that will run on it is 10.10! That's the only kernel that works with this hardware, and that with some tweaking.
