### Post by nickpaton on 2009-11-09
I appreciate that you are wanting recommendations on the best laptop to use for Ubuntu, but I carry out specialist laptop repairs, in particular rectifying faulty graphics chips which become unsoldered over time.

Certain makes are very problematical in this respect even if some manufacturers chose not to acknowledge the problem. I'm not sure if I'm allowed to mention makes but I suggest you Google "unsoldered graphics chip" or "graphics chip problem" adding the names of the major manufacturers on at a time, and you'll be very surprised at how widespread this is even with some current models.

There are ways of reducing the problem, for instance never switching the laptop off but putting it into hibernation, and making sure you never carry or move it by the front edge, screen or by one corner for instance.

Hope this does help you in your decision and appreciate that it's different to what you're asking.
