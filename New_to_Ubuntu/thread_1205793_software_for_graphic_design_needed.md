### Post by sloggerkhan on 2009-07-10
Gimp, Inkscape, Scribus, Fontmatrix or FontyPython, and fontforge is a good basic set of programs.

If your friend is interested in creating vector graphics, designing fonts, or doing page layouts, Linux can work. On the other hand, if they're used to indesign/illustrator/photoshop, the learning curve will be harsh and many things will be simpler to do in CS than in Linux, even if Linux can do them. Linux is also missing comprehensive CMYK support. 

That said, being able to get a a set of programs competitive and in some ways superior to Adobe CS for free is a big plus. The thing is, buying upgrades to adobe every year or two might be financially cheaper in the short term than the economic cost of your friend needing to retrain herself, which could easily take several months.
