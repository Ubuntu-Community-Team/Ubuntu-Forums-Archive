### Post by halitech on 2008-11-25
with PS/2 mice and keyboards its not advisable to unplug/plug them when the power is on as it could kill the ports. I have seen a few systems end up with dead ports from unplugging the keyboards and mice with the power on. If its a serial mouse (does anyone other then me even still have a system that uses serial mice and AT keyboards?), no problem at all. If they are USB, then no problem at all.

as always, YMMV

### Post by halitech on 2008-11-25
what you could do is go into the BIOS and check for an option on stopping on errors during boot and set it to disabled, then power down, disconnect everything and power it back on. Of course with this you will have no way of knowing if it booted properly or not until you try to connect to it or yo could leave the monitor attached to make sure then disconnect it.
