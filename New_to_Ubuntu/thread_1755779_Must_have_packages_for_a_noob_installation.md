### Post by dniMretsaM on 2011-05-11
For a total n00b, I would recommend installing nothing. Why? If they are a n00b, they won't need anything else since they are (almost certainly) using only basic functions and all of the programs necessary for basic/normal computing are already installed. If she wants something. Let her tell you she wants a program that does x and then find the most user friendly GUI for her. If you can't find anything suitable, ask around on here. That's my advice for a computer n00b.

EDIT: Obviously you will want to install the basic things like the restricted extras, Flash, and things like that so that she can do normal stuff like watch vids and listen to music. For Flash, use the Firefox add-on Flash-Aid.

### Post by sea_dawg on 2011-05-11
How old is your Mom? What does she like to do?  These are the things I'd think about.  My mom is in her mid 80s.

I think what Ubuntu comes loaded with will be more than enough to get her started.  As long as others have said, you have all the bits and pieces installed to make it all work.  

For my Mom, the biggest hurdle was feeling overwhelmed by too much choice and too much complexity.  Keep it simple as you can.  

Rather than worry about specific packages think about teaching her the basics.  Such things as how to organize her documents.  How to back things up.  Teach her in the simplest terms what the major components are.  My Mom was forever confusing HDD storage with RAM.  

If she's getting on in years make sure the machine is comfortable for her to use. A HUGE monitor so that when the typeface is big she can still see a lot of info on the screen. Watch how she uses the machine, get the keyboard and mouse/pointer device that best suit how she handles the hardware.

Change will be hard for her. Install only the LTS version so that she can learn the GUI and stick with it for years. My Mom is a Windows XP user.  It is going to be hard on her when XP goes away.

Then patiently step back and observe.  Let her tell you what she wants the machine to do.  Mom started off with some easy games, email and such.  Nothing too heavy.  Then she began to expand her use to other areas of interest.  Pictures and vids of the family. Connecting her camera to the computer, transferring pics to an electronic picture frame.  Geenealogy. Sewing, she got a sewing machine that allows her to make patterns for stitching and embroidering on screen and transfer to the sewing machine.  Playing bridge.

Don't force her to do it your way.  For example I like a minimal desktop, no clutter.  Mom wants an icon for everything she uses.  I can hardly see the desktop and it is scattered all over the place.  If me or one of the grand kids moves icons around she is confused.  I've learned to take a screen shot of her desktop at regular intervals so that I can get it all back 'just so' if it gets wrinkled up. 

Above all patience.  At first she may only use her computer for email and one or two easy games.  That's perfectly fine, she has to have time to get comfortable with it.
