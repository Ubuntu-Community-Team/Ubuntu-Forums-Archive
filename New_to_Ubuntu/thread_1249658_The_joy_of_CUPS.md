### Post by lazyart on 2009-08-25
If you don't quite understand the wonderful thing that is CUPS, let me tell you a story.

Two weeks ago, a client purchased a laptop from me.  Like any person trying to cultivate a customer base, I set up the machine for her (Windows) and went through the process of downloading and installing the driver for her printer.

Due to a bad check, I had to visit again today and pick up a replacement.  I fired up my Ubuntu laptop, connected back home to my accounting software (check out NolaPro.  Free, web-based, installs over the LAMP stack... poke a hole in your firewall and run your books anywhere), and drummed up an invoice.  

I plugged in the printer that I spent 15 minutes setting up two weeks ago.  Ubuntu/CUPS recognized it and had it ready in about 15 seconds and I fired off the paid invoice.  Didn't have to visit epson.com, locate the model, find the correct OS version, download, install... blah blah blah.  Truly plug and play.  Way cool.

Guess this belongs in the testimonials thread.  Forgive me?
