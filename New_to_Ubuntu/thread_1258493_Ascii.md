### Post by swoody on 2009-09-05
Hi michelle, and welcome to the Forums! Gnome has a nice little utility built in for all your character needs, it's called Character Map, and can be found at Applications>Accessories>Character Map. On the left hand side, you can select 'common' and then in the statusbar of that window it will show you the hexadecimal value for whatever character you select. Then to enter that into a text editor, you can just hold down CTRL+Shift and enter the value. Say if you wanted to enter an "¿" you would just hold down ctrl+shift and enter it's value: u00bf and voila!

---

### Post by swoody on 2009-09-05
Please also note, in Character Map you can double click the symbol you want, it'll appear next to 'Text to copy:' and you can just copy/paste it from there :)
