### Post by nandemonai on 2009-06-16
Highly doubt it has anything to do with the router activity. Is the surface you're using the mouse on clean? Base of the mouse clean? etc

Optical mice can be finicky sometimes, especially with certain imperfections on the surface you're using it on. Could also be that the laser is acting up.

First call would be a good cleaning of the mouse and the surface you're using it on, then try with a mouse pad or some such.

The only way someone could be messing with your mouse from the Internet would be if you had remote desktop enabled, port(s) forwarded and no authentication or notification enabled.
