### Post by BeachBuddah on 2011-04-26
IRC or Internet Relay Chat is a form of chat like the Yahoo or MSN chat rooms- just older.  There are many different servers on IRC.  The official Ubuntu channels are on the Freenode server, but there are other channels on that network discussing lots of other topics and there are plenty of other networks with their own focus as well.  XChat is just one program that accesses IRC - again there are plenty of others, this one is just very popular.

### Post by Anuovis on 2011-04-26
IRC is a wide collection of networks that contain chat rooms inside of them.

XChat is just one of the clients that allows you to connect to these networks. Another popular app you might have heard of is mIRC. You could also go to [http://mibbit.com](http://mibbit.com) and access IRC via your browser. I am sure there are many other applications that you can use as well.

You could think of IRC as something similar to the World Wide Web (or just the Internet if you wish, although that is not entirely accurate) and XChat as your web browser. One is the content out there, the other is a tool that lets you access it. 

As far as I am aware of, there are no restrictions within different IRC clients and you can usually connect to any network you want.
