### Post by da burger on 2010-10-04
If you don't have a separate /home partition then I would recommend that you simply tarball all your personal files and restore them when you install ubuntu. This does require you to have enough space on an external drive to store them in a compressed form. If you need help taring your files just post back here.

### Post by Kevdunleavy on 2010-10-04
Unless you have a separate /home partition there's no easy way that I know of doing it.

However, if you have a big enough external drive, backing up your data really isn't that much of a bother. You'd be surprised how much of the stuff on your system you really dont need.

If you dont have a big external drive but you do have a decent internet connection, another option is too use Dropbox to back up your stuff. You just install Dropbox and put all the files you want to keep into the Dropbox folder. Then when you have Ubuntu installed just install Dropbox and it will download all the files you put in your folder on Mint.
