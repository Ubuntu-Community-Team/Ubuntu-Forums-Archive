### Post by The Cog on 2022-12-07
I have an HP that happens to be using instantink though I don't think that's relevant for printing from Ubuntu. It's 2-3 years old, so their methods may have changed recently. 
I used the LCD on the front panel to enter the wifi password and it then used DHCP to get an address. I configured my home router DHCP to give it a fixed address every time.
I just clicked printers, add printer, search network, and put the IP address in. Set the discovered printer as my default printer.

### Post by tea for one on 2022-12-07
Approx 2 months ago, I purchased a HP Laser Jet 140we (mono print & colour scan).
It's a very compact unit, requiring minimal space.

I've used HP Inkjets for a few years but became fed up with dried ink cartridges and the expense of ink replacement.
Linux and HP never presented a problem with installation and hplip always provided the necessary functions.

I thought that this new printer/scanner would be up and running in 10/15 minutes (although I had read about the smartphone app, I was confident that it was not an essential requirement).
Two hours later, air blue and steam emanating from my ears, I ate humble pie and downloaded the Android app to my smartphone.

15 minutes later, both printing and scanning (using Ubuntu 22.04) were working perfectly.
The mobile phone app can also print and scan but I simply keep the app for contingency.

It's disappointing that HP have created this barrier, I hope that this is not a future trend.
