### Post by mcduck on 2011-12-08
Would you happen to know what format the video is in? That would help a lot in figuring out what's needed to play it (AVI is just a container file, and can include audio/video encoded in pretty much any format).

On the other hand if VLC doesn't play it, thinks are looking quite bad for you... Anyway, installing the [ubuntu-restricted-extras]("apt:ubuntu-restricted-extras") package and also the stuff from [Medibuntu]("http://medibuntu.org/") repository should work pretty much for every video that doesn't use some nasty DRM or something.
