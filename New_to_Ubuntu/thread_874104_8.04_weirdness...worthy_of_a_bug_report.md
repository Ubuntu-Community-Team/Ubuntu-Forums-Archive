### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2008-07-30
> **pcrussell50 said:**
> i am a patient sort, so i am willing to wait if you think the developers know about this and are intending to fix it.  do you think they will?

Well, again at this point I'm just guessing that you haven't got your system configured correctly, which if I'm right means that no, you won't get a series of updates that magically fix the issues.

It's possible that over the course of development of Ibex that their auto-configuration gets better and so in that sense  you could say it will likely be fixed. But we're assuming a lot based on very little knowledge right now.

There's a gui tool to try and set your resolution. I'd find that first, and see if it fixes your screen resolution (hint: not in the menu in hardy, you have to alt+f2 then type in the name of the program, which I forget). If it doesn't, next I'd search the forums for the xorg.conf of someone with the same video card and monitor as me. Or, just find an old xorg.conf from the Gutsy days (again, plenty of those lying around) and modify it for your system. This avoids the auto-config altogether and tells xorg to just use these settings. Obviously, you'd specificy the correct resolution in your xorg.conf.

So, with respect to video, try finding the gui tool and if that doesn't work, try copying in and older xorg.conf and modifying it.

As for sound, there's a thread called the complete sound solutions guide. That's a good place to start to make sure the fundamentals of the system are working, though the fact that they worked on Gutsy and not now just leads me to think you've got a pulse audio configuration issue. The easiest thing to do is just go into sound preferences (in gnome) and change everything to alsa.My own experiments with hardy left me with the distinct impression that pulse was pushed in, but not really that well integrated (no graphical tool installed by default to control the different channels? Why install pulse then if you can't control that?) so unless there's some feature you need of pulse I'd probably just search for anything pulse in synaptic and remove it. Though, you shouldn't have to do that to switch your sound over to Alsa.

Lastly, when Ibix comes out upgrade to it. I suspect it's going to be the release that everyone was hoping Hardy would be. Pulse has matured. Xorg has matured. Xubuntu team has a release under their belt since their recent self-evaluation and rededication. But that's a bit off-topic. ;)

### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2008-08-01
Best to open up a new thread on that issue, with a subject heading like "Volumes on Gnome Desktop missing after upgrade to 8.04"

Apparently, your windows partition (NTFS?) used to show up on your (gnome?) desktop, but you don't say if this is what you really want. I mean, it should be easy enough to restore that behaviour, but what would you ideally want to happen?

For example, I would personally like for all my partitions to be mounted automatically on boot, but only for my documents folder to show up on the desktop. I could browse for the rest in nautilus if I needed. 

Perhaps you would like none of them to be mounted, unless you need them, but they should all be on the desktop ready to right-click and mount?

In any event, whether you create a new thread or continue here you should explain what you'd like to happen and post the output of these commands:
```
sudo fdisk -l
cat /etc/fstab
```Also, open up gconf-editor and search for "volumes", then under >apps>nautilus>desktop look at which boxes there have checkmarks. Play with them a bit to see what they do if you're curious.

Oh, and if you do decide to open a new thread you could probably mark this one as solved, since you fixed it yourself by installing older version then upgrading, even though we never nailed down the problem, at least you found a workaround that may help others.
