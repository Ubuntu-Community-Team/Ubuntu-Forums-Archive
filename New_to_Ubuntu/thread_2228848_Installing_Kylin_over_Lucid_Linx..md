### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-06-09
Hi, welcome.

Lucid (desktop) is way out of support now but Lucid (server) still gets updates. It follows that only the common packages are being updated and that's a recipe for breaking things (on the desktop version). The bottom line is you shouldn't use anymore, period. It'll only get worse.
Now, answering your question... You're right, most people here, myself included, will (strongly) recommend a backup - you should do that regularly anyway and regardless of any intended upgrade or not - and do a fresh install of the currently supported version, 14.04, which happens to be a LTS like 10.04. I will also strongly advice AGAINST any direct upgrade of any EoL version.

PS - Why Kylin? I hope you're aware that Ubuntu Kylin is just the Chinese version, ie, it's the same current version Ubuntu 'customized' for China, namely for (and by) the Chinese government, as the 'official' OS.
