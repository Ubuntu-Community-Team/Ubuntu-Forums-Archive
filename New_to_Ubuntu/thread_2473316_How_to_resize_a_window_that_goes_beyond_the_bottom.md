### Post by DuckHook on 2022-04-01
Here's another method that I find almost universally applicable:

<Alt> <Space> will bring up the window manipulation menu. Using only the keyboard, scroll down to and select "Resize". Then, a single <Down Arrow> will lock the resize point at the bottom border of the window even if you cannot see it. A succession of <Up Arrow> presses will then move the bottom window border up the desktop. You may have to do this for quite a few keystrokes depending on how deeply your window is buried below your desktop viewing area. Release the lock with <Enter>.

I usually place my mouse cursor on the upper left or right corner until I get the two dimensional move cursor then pull the top edge down a bit, release it, then in the upper margin using the mouse again with the hand cursor pull the entire window up until the bottom edge clears.
