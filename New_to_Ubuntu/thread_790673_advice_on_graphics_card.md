### Post by autocrosser on 2008-05-11
As far as ram on the card--the more you have, the better you games run--on the opposite side--there are lower requirements for linux-native games.

I've only run Intel processors, so can't comment on AMD, but you have the option of running 32bit or 64bit--right now 32bit is easier--in a couple of years 64bit will be the way to go.

I'm running Core-Duo processors without any issues.

### Post by sdennie on 2008-05-12
There are actually a lot of problems with the nvidia drivers (specifically [that model]("http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=101161&highlight=8400") (and, in fact, [the whole series]("http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=87865&highlight=8400") is [terrible for 2D performance]("http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=111750&highlight=performance"))).  There are workarounds for the problems but and it wouldn't surprise me if nvidia released new drivers to fix some of those problems in the very near future but, I wouldn't expect to pop in a Hardy CD and have an nvidia 8 or 9 series card to work in a really amazing way without putting some work into it.
