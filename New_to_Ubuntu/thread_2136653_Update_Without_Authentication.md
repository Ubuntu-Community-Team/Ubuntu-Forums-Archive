### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-04-18
Would you not agree that not all updates need authentication? You will find that some updates still require authentication as do some software installs through the software centre. But as a general rule an attempt had been made to reduce the number of times we are asked to authenticate. It is felt that users may get into the habit of putting in the password without examining what or why it is required. That 'without thinking response' is weak security. By reducing the number of times we are asked to put in our password it is hoped that the user will be better protected against malware because they may give more thought about why they are being asked to give their password.

Regards.
