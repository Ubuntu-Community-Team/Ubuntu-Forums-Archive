### Post by drs305 on 2008-08-01
This is a bit convoluted but will work if you want to follow along. It will put a folder on your top panel, to which you can add all your favorite apps.

1. Right click the main menu symbol (ubuntu) and select 'Edit menus' (or "alacarte" in terminal).
2. Highlight "Applications" then select "New Menu" on right.
3. Enter name and change icon if desired, OK.
You should now have a new menu in the left column.
4. Right click the main menu symbol (or "alacarte") and again select "Edit Menus".
Select any of the the menu folders in the left pane and drag any apps you want from the right side onto your own menu folder and release. A copy of that app's link will be placed in your folder. The original will remain in the original folder.

Now put your menu/folder on the top panel:
6. Right click on top panel, Add to Panel, Add Launcher. Click and drag your new menu to the top panel and release.

7. Whenever you want to add an item to this dropdown menu/folder, either 
a) rt click on it, "Edit Menus", and drag and drop newly installed app shortcuts into your menu folder from other existing folders, or
b) if the app doesn't appear in any of the pre-existing folders, rt clk on your folder, "Edit Menus", highlight your folder in the left panel, select "New Item" from the right side and create a launcher.
