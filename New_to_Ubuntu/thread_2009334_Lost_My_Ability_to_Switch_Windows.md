### Post by SpongeBob SquarePants on 2012-06-24
Say I have three Nautilus windows open, and then start Firefox, what I used to do to switch back to a Nautilus window was to click on the Nautilus icon in the launcher (which would have three notches next to it, signifying three open windows), and the three windows would appear side by side, allowing me to click on one, which would then become the main window. 

For some reason, I can't do this any more. The three notches are there, but when I click on the launcher, only one window appears, and the only way I can access either of the other two is to close (or minimise) Firefox. It's the same with any program. If I open more than one window, and want to swap between windows by clicking the launcher, I can't.

I know I can alt+tab, but I like the way it used to work. Any ideas why it's suddenly stopped working?

---

### Post by SpongeBob SquarePants on 2012-06-24
Problem solved. Somehow the H desktop setting in 'My Unity' had slipped down to one. Apparently it needs to be on at least two to function.
