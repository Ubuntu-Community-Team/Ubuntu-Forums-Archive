### Post by mcgarry83 on 2009-04-07
PROBLEM SOLVED! While getting extremely frustrated with this issue, I decided to format my HDD and install OpenSuse to see if the problem was related to Ubuntu. It was not. Still had the same jerky mouse problem. I got to really thinking about what is causing this, when a pop-up informed me that my battery charge info could not be read. I knew I had a bad battery that no longer worked, so I pulled it out and re-booted with out it. PROBLEM GONE! Im guessing that the power management or something, is trying to check my battery, but not getting any info and just keeps looping every few seconds. I dont need the battery anyway, since it holds zero charge now after 8 years of heavy use. Time to get a new one off Ebay. Hope this helps someone.

### Post by poodoopealeoaph on 2011-01-25
There is a similar issue that has come up with my install of Maverick. I have an Emachines E725 and it seems that when I move across the exact center of my touch pad it increases sensitivity but only for a small part of the center. also, it only does this while moving horizontally. 

I found that it is because of having a dirty touch pad. So, just incase anyone has this issue, be sure to clean your touch pad thoroughly and make sure there are no specks on it. After doing this, it worked out perfectly. In retrospect though, I wish I would have left it alone. I had dealt with it for so long that I got used to it and now that my touch pad is working correctly, I keep getting frustrated.
