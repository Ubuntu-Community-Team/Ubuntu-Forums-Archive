### Post by oldos2er on 2011-09-09
> **nickos said:**
> What is the difference between ubuntu and an ubuntu desktop environment?I have passed through some DEs like fedora and ElementaryOS.These are desktop environments right?Does that mean that they have ubuntu as heart and the difference is only the look?

Fedora and Elementary OS are distros (distributions). What they each possess in common is a Linux kernel as a base, with other software that supports the kernel. Elementary OS is not Ubuntu any more than Ubuntu is Debian; they may be based on one another, but they are still considered separate distros.

Add on top of that any of the many available desktop environments (DEs), such as Gnome 2 or 3, KDE, xfce, and you have a full modern OS. A DE is the graphical user interface (GUI) you see, and also the programs you use; for example Gnome comes with the mail client Evolution, whereas KDE comes with KMail. Most distros allow you to install any and all of the DEs you wish to use.

Then there are window managers. A window manager (wm) is less than a DE in that it doesn't provide a suite of programs, it is software that creates window controls e.g. scrollbars, menubar, and minimize maximize and close buttons. See [http://xwinman.org/](http://xwinman.org/) for lists of both DEs and wm's.
