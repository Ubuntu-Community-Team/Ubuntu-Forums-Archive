### Post by srs5694 on 2010-05-11
If the 4KB sectors are giving you problems, you should be able to bypass the issue by creating a small (~50-200MB) /boot partition on another disk (presumably your regular internal disk). You can then tell GRUB to install itself there or on your internal disk's MBR. The Linux kernel and initial RAM disk both reside in /boot, so GRUB won't need to be able to parse the disk with 4KB sectors.
