### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-05-12
Ubuntu runs just fine on most older 'puters. But I got to playing around with it to see if I could resurrect a *reeeeeeally* old 'puter (Win98 on it) by making Ubuntu as "lightweight" and resource-thrifty as possible.

One of the things I like best about Linux is that it can be anything you want, as much or as little as you like!

The fastest and most lightweight desktop *environment* there is for Linux (for now at least), is [LXDE]("http://lxde.org"). You don't even really need a d.e. Linux runs at mind-bending speed without one, but it isn't pretty with cute li'l icons and stuff to click on. [Crunchbang Linux]("http://crunchbanglinux.org") is an Ubuntu-based bare-bones distribution that doesn't even have a desktop environment - just the Window Manager. It flies! But a newbie like me goes, "*Omygosh! What do I click on?!*" Reading the forums helps a LOT. 

Last week, just playing around to learn, I even made my own uber-speedy, very Windowsy-looking (nice and simple for this li'l noob) "**Ubuntu Lite**." I chose a lightweight window manager (Openbox) and added the LXDE desktop to it. It ran all my favorite programs at transwarp speed!! Very fun. But I think I goofed a couple of things up because it's getting buggy. It doesn't automatically open Firefox anymore when I click on a link in an e-mail message, and the Logout routine is messing up so sometimes I have to force a shutdown. But I learned alot just playin' around with it on an old dinosaur that me family gave up for dead a long time ago. That old machine purrs along now with Linux. Sweet!

There have been other attempts at creating an LXDE-based "Ubuntu Lite" (because Xubuntu is not so lightweight as it promised, and because LXDE is so much lighter and faster even than the XFCE desktop environment) like [U-Lite]("http://u-lite.org") (which is *not* "newbie friendly" at all - never could even get it installed, even with the complete iso). But there's a whole 'nother official Ubuntu team working on a project called "[Lubuntu]("https://launchpad.net/~lubuntu-desktop")" (*including* the lead developer of U-Lite who is a really nice lady). I wish I knew enough to help but I'm still a newbie just playing with a wonderful new toy.

-Robin
