### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-10-20
You have entered the "Walled Garden".  You may enjoy tea and the flowers and music inside the walled garden.  You just can't leave.

Ubuntu and open source software in general is outside the Walled Garden.  No tea for you.  No music either.

There is no simple way to do this and any explanation on this forum on how to do it would be in violation of the Walled Garden Rules.

Most files on iTunes have DRM--Digital Rights Management--restrictions on how and where you can use and put the files.  With Fair Play restrictions, you can move files around inside the Walled Garden up to 5 times.  So you can copy the files from the iPhone to iTunes on your MacBook.  But you would have a difficult time getting them out of iTunes to put them on the server.  iTunes can act as a server for these files to other MacBooks--again inside the Walled Garden--by turning on Sharing.  In Ubuntu, you can sometimes see these shares in Rhythmbox and play them in Ubuntu--a rare exception to the Walled Garden concept.

A web search should turn up several ways to do what you want, but I can't post them here.
