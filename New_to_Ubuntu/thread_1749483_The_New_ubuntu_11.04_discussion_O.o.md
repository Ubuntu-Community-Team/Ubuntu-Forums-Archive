### Post by wburden on 2011-05-04
I like to download the iso to burn a cd/dvd of the new version, create separate partitions and install the new version on the new partitions. That way I can keep the 'old' until I'm happy with the 'new'. 11.04 has a different feel from previous versions with new ways of doing things and renamed functionality - for example, I spent a half hour looking for the regular terminal only to find it had been renamed. Moving windows controls to the former system menu area at the top of the screen and the application menu changes take a little getting used to but after a few days, I am finding I am getting used to it. It's a process for sure and I'm quite willing to invest the time for now. After a few days I did perform the version upgrade on my wireless laptop and nothing broke but my wireless download speed which went from 30 Mbps to 0.91 Mbps. A quick trip to the forums provided a solution and I'm a happy camper. All in all a good experience.
