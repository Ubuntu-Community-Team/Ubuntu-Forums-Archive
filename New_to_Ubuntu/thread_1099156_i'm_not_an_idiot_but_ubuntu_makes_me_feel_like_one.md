### Post by Eastisle on 2009-03-17
The reason your DVD doesn't play on a fresh install has to do with legal restrictions on the codecs (patent and copyright), disallowing Ubuntu to come with them by default. More information about that: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats)
 
Solution: 
Open a terminal and type:
```
sudo apt-get install ubuntu-restricted-extras
``` 
Or look for it in Synaptic Package manager.

If you continue to have problems playing a specific DVD, then it probably needs other proprietary codecs. Luckily they can all be found in the medibuntu repository. If you don't have any problems, install them anyway.

Go here for clear instructions: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu)

I suggest you look into both links, they have tons of answers. Good luck.

### Post by Pro75357 on 2009-03-17
What kind of graphics card do you have? The "proprietary hardware tool" is found in the gnome menu under:

System>Administration>Hardware Drivers

I would check it and see if you have installed the "proprietary" driver for your card.  If you have not, click the device and click activate and follow instructions. This will truly enable your graphics card and will increase performance tremendously.

If you already have this driver activated, it will have a green button beside it.  While this driver should work nicely, it isn't the most up-to date, but it is probably the most stable.  

"...if you are using the proprietary hardware tool in Ubuntu you should disable **it** first and restart"   The IT here, i believe, is talking about the driver itself, and not the tool.  So, if you want to continue with the aforementioned guide, I would simply deactivate the driver if it were already activated. \

My best recommendation for you would be to simply use the driver that is suggested by the 'proprietary hardware tool.'  I have had to perform way too many re-installs due to installing the bran-newest catalyst drivers.  An Nvidia card may be a different story though.

Good Luck.
