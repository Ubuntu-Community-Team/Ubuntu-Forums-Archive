### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-12-08
Drag the top Gnome panel down to the bottom. I don't know why you "need" it at the bottom (I suspect you just "want" it at the bottom) but that's the way you do it. All the features of the panels are made up of applets, so you can delete applets (Right-click them and choose "Remove from panel") and add them to a single panel to end off with a truly Windows-like panel at the bottom of the screen.

As for your first problem, you might need to right-click on the volume control applet and choose the option that opens the mixer, then turn up the headphones volume in the mixer.
