### Post by whitesmith on 2013-10-19
A (sort of) short answer is that HDDs are sold on the basis of unformatted capacity. I have a 1 TB HDD that I formatted as ext4. Its size as reported by *nix utilities is 917 GiB, of which 858 GiB are available. All OSes drain headroom which they need for creating a filesystem and other things. The loss is usually around what it is here, 6.4%. Pay careful attention to what **oldfred** said. HDDs are sold on the tacit assumption that 1 K = 10 ^ 3 (1000 or 1 KB). After that 1 MB is 1 KB ^ 2, 1 GB is 1 KB ^ 3, a TB is 1 KB ^ 4, etc -- regardless of the meaning of K. Utilities usually report space on the basis that 1 K = 2 ^ 10 (1024 or 1 KiB). It may seem like a small thing, but it adds up significantly in the TB range.
