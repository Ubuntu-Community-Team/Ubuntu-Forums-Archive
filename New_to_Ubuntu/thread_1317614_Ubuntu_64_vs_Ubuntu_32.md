### Post by JJNova on 2009-11-06
I have been running 64-bit versions of Ubuntu for a couple of releases now, but I am by no means an expert on the differences between the two. All I can do is speak from experience, and experience has showed me that nearly every application nowadays has a 64-bit build. 32bit apps can be ran on 64bit machines, and support between the two is generally spot on identical. 

Performance on the other hand, is much better. Of course, that might be because I bought a new CPU at the same time I switched to 64. There have been no issues in my experience. Aside from the typical ones everyone experienced when updating to Jaunty. :D
