### Post by Xiong Chiamiov on 2008-05-26
Apps designed for GNOME will work just as well in KDE (aside from using some extra libraries and all that).

AVI is not actually a real format, but an encapsulating format that can hold pretty much anything.  If you go to Player -> Track Info it will tell you what audio and video codecs are being used in the video you're having trouble with, and then we can help you from there.
