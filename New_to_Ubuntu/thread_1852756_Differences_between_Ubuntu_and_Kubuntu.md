### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2011-09-30
They both have the same "guts".  It is just a matter of which desktop (KDE for Kubuntu and Gnome and now Unity for Ubuntu) and the various software that comes with each one.  Neither is better than the other.  They are just different and I advise someone coming to Linux and Ubuntu to try both and even Xubuntu and Lubuntu, which support different desktops and programs.  Xubuntu is supposed to support older machines and is a bit lighter.  At least that is what is maintained.  Lubuntu is super light and will run on almost any machine.  Xubuntu runs on the XFCE desktop and Lubuntu runs on the LXDE desktop.  As with the other two each comes with a set of programs that are supported.  If you have favorites you can install then on any of the desktops so long as you install the dependencies.
I run a dual boot Ubuntu 10.04 (Gnome) and Lubuntu 11.04 (LXDE) on the same machine.  Try them all if you like and pick the one or ones that best suit your needs.  Does any of that rambling help?

### Post by dwasifar on 2011-09-30
The best way to see the difference is to boot from a [Kubuntu live CD]("http://www.kubuntu.org/getkubuntu") and try it out.

Personally, I'm not a big fan of KDE in general or Kubuntu in particular.  KDE is very complex and resource-heavy, and Kubuntu has a reputation for being sort of a lackluster implementation of it.  I keep trying out Kubuntu and leaving it because of bugs and sluggishness compared to other distros.  But those are just my opinions, and there are other people who love KDE and Kubuntu and wouldn't change a thing.  So you should try it.
