### Post by Double.J on 2013-07-07
it is pretty device specific to be honest, but when it comes to photos from phones it usually not to problematic. Syncing things onto devices (iPhones) can be tricky, but taking photos off is often not too bad.

The reason for this is that most devices (including iPhones) will offer up their image storage to computers as a mountable volume. what this means is that it is unlikely that a phone will appear, rather a 'filesystem' of a certain size. double check that the device is not locked with any kind of passcode as well.

in general ubuntu is pretty good at realising you've stuck something with pictures on in it and will pop up and ask if you want to use shotwell to store them (I do). failing this have a look in your file manager some times they pop up as usb sticks, the name of the phone, the model number of the phone, the maker of the SD ard in the phone, etc. but your file manager on the left hand side is the place to be looking.

if there are no extenral devices mounted then double check things like USB sticks are mounting okay. if they are it's likely to come down to the phone. also worth checking if the phone is storing them on removable storage such as micro sd card?
