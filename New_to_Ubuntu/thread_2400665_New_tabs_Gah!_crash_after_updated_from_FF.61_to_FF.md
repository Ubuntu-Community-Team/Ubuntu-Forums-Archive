### Post by Holger_Gehrke on 2018-09-08
You might not like youtube, facebook, amazon, ebay, wikipedia and reddit but they are sites that have for years had consistently high traffic, so prepopulating an area with the heading "important pages" with these sites and slowly replacing them with the pages you actually visit often seems like a good solution to me as opposed to leaving that area blank, which would make it hard for new users to understand what this is about.

Firefox crashing is often the result of misbehaving extensions, so the first step should be to restart Firefox without them. You can do this by selecting "Restart with deactivated add-ons" (retranslated from German, actual name of the menu item might differ) from the "Help"-menu. If this stops the crashes, than it's just a matter of trial and error to find out which add-on is the responsible one.

Holger
