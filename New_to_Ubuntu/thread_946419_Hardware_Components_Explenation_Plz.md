### Post by bumanie on 2008-10-14
What you have asked for is very extensive and really beyond the scope of this forum without sending extra information. However, here are some general comments: nvidia graphic cards tend to work well with linux; hard drives at present are getting bigger and cheaper and to my knowledge all seaggate/maxtor, western digital, samsung etc internal drives, all work with linux ; at present ram is cheap; most pci-e graphics cards you would find at retail outlets would have the power to run desktop effects - even lower end ones, the gpu you get really depends on what sort of games you want to be able to play. If you have the technical skill to put the computer yourself from parts you source, this tends to save $$$ - the last system I built for myself was retailing at AUD$400 more than what I paid for the parts separately.
