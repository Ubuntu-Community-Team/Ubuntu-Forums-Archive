### Post by NightwishFan on 2009-08-07
When you get to grub, edit the Ubuntu grub line. I think just push **e** with it highlighted. You will go to another screen. Highlight the 'kernel' line and I believe push **e** again. Delete the words 'splash' and 'silent'. Then push **b** to boot. If I am wrong about any keyboard shortcuts, it shows them on the bottom for you.

This will not harm anything permanently. It is just disabling usplash for this boot only.
