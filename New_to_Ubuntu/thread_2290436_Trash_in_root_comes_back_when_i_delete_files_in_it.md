### Post by cogier2 on 2015-08-12
Try selecting the files then while holding down the [Shift] key, press the [Delete] key, then press [Enter] to permanently delete the files.

### Post by col48 on 2015-08-12
what cogier2 suggests is also how to permanently delete files without them going to Trash (assuming you have relevant permissions of course).

Don't do it unless you are sure you won't regret it.

Running everything as root is not recommended - you are much more vulnerable to mistakes and malicious intruders.
