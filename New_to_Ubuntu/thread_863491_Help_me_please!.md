### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2008-07-20
Try this:

1. Right-click on the [COLOR="Blue"]speaker icon[/COLOR] in your top toolbar

2. Left-click on [COLOR="Blue"]Open Volume Control[/COLOR].

3. When the window opens, click on [COLOR="Blue"]Edit Preferences[/COLOR].

4. Put a tick in Master, Headphone, PCM, Line-In, Line-In Capture, CD, CD-Capture, Microphone, Microphone-Capture, PC-Speker, Capture, Mix.

5. Click [COLOR="Blue"]Close[/COLOR]. 

6. Now, click on the [COLOR="Blue"]Recording Tab[/COLOR] and move the sliders up and also make sure that there are no x's on the speaker and microphone buttons at the bottom. Click on them to change state, if necessary.

7. Click on the [COLOR="Blue"]Playback Tab[/COLOR] and move the sliders to appropriate levels and again, make sure that appropriate icons at the bottom of the sliders are not x'd out.

8. You will probably need to close and re open the volume control before you make the next setting change - do it.

9. Re-open the volume control panel and click on the [COLOR="Blue"]Switches Tab[/COLOR].

10. Choose the appropriate item. [COLOR="Blue"]Microphone Capture[/COLOR] is usually the one needed.

Hopefully all will now be OK.
