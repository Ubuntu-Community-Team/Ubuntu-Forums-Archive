### Post by iaculallad on 2008-06-04
Do check your Ubuntu ISO file first for error, check its MD5 hash and compare it with the original hash file w/c could be read/copied from the website of where you downloaded your ISO. You can use Brasero or any Burning software installed on your PC. If possible, burn it at a lower speed (4x) to avoid corruption. After you're done, simply let your PC boot from your CDROM/DVDROM. (And insert your newly burned Ubuntu LiveCD).

### Post by wdaniels on 2008-06-04
Does your laptop have a CD writer? First you have to [burn the iso file to a CD]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto") and boot from that. You might have to [change an option in your computer's BIOS]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromCD#head-af1e4927aacfc62eafa73d64bcb83528c17a7753") to make it boot from the CD. If you don't have a CD drive you can always use a USB stick if you have one of those instead, but it's slightly more complicated that way.

This installation itself is easy. There's a guide [here]("http://www.howtoforge.com/the-perfect-desktop-ubuntu-8.04-lts-hardy-heron") with screenshots so you know what to expect.
