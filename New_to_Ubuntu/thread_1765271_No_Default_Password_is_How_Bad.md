### Post by remmian on 2011-05-22
I am switching my 89yr old Mom from XP to Ubuntu so she can pay bills online. She is flustered easily and all the popups in Windows irritate her (update this, firewall that, A/V stuff, etc), even though I tried to not have anything set to auto-update. So I figured Ubuntu would be good. I don't want her to have to login, so I set that to auto... but was surprised that I had to enter a pwd for wifi to connect. Per the link [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1244402](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1244402), I chose not to have a default pwd so Mom can boot and just click on FFox like she does in XP. IOW, so wifi auto-connects without user interaction.

But I would like to know just what "passwords are not encrypted" by not using a default password...? like the wifi WPA/WEP is stored in plain text I assume <?> ... and what else? And quest #2, who could gain access aside from the obvious scenario of someone walking in physically and sitting down at the computer? I mean... assuming there aren't hacks within Bluetooth distance, is the machine at risk even when installing Linux pkgs or ...?

Thanks for any clarification.

### Post by syntr on 2011-05-22
No, only the WiFi key is stored as plaintext. Your password (and, by default, your root password) is still encrypted.
