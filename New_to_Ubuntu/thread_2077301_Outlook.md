### Post by Zill on 2012-10-28
> **Lars Noodén said:**
> ...There are free mail services available from non-Microsoft sources like Gmail, Fastmail.fm, GMX and many others...
But it is worth remembering that "free" can have different meanings. ;-)

Free to us in the FOSS community means that we are free to modify the source code of the software.

Free of charge to the user may seem attractive until you realise that if you are not paying for a product then *you* may well be the product!

Many of the "free" email services fall into the second category.

If you need reliable and trustworthy email then my advice is to use your ISP's email service.  As you are likely to be paying for this you then become a *customer*, rather than a *product*, and have additional rights if things go wrong.

[This link]("http://dendory.net/blog.php?id=501c4261") explains things quite well.
