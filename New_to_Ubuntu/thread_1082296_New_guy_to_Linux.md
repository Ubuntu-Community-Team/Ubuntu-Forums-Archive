### Post by Kellemora on 2009-02-27
Hi ND

I tried SEVERAL Distro's before discovering Ubuntu!

One of those I tried was CentOS, which is a clone of Red Hat Enterprise Linux, the full blown package so to speak.

For me, there were many issues with Red Hat clones and not much help to solve them.  

Then I found Debian and worked with it for awhile.  That is how I found out about Ubuntu, a Debian based GNU/Linux.

At first I figured, if it's based on Debian, why not just use Debian.

And then I discovered these Forums!!!!!

It's the Forums and ALL THE WONDERFUL HELP I found here, plus all the resources and backup that comes with Ubuntu that made me finally chose this Distro.

In less than a month of learning Ubuntu, I switched my entire office over to Ubuntu and it's the best move I ever made in my life!!!!!

Also, Ubuntu has many different versions designed for different uses and for smaller or older computers too.  In other words, if you need it, you'll probably find it here, somewhere.
If not, someone will point the way for you!

Just remember, GNU/Linux is NOT WinDoze or Mac, it's BETTER in more ways than one.  But often you will find, it does not have the hardware support the expensive commercial OS's enjoy, but it's getting better in leaps and bounds.

Although many Doze and Mac peripherals will work in Linux, just like in the earlier days of the DOZE you HAD TO seek out which hardware items worked on which OS's.  Linux is like that!  That Chevy hood don't fit the Ford bonnet all the time!  But on the other hand, if you buy a peripheral known to work well on GNU/Linux and has supported drivers, it will probably work on the Doze or Mac also.  Just not the other way around.

Someone should come along and tell you which version of Ubuntu would probably be your best choice for your particular laptop.

Welcome Aboard!

TTUL
Gary
