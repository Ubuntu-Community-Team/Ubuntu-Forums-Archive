### Post by 3ldi5 on 2011-04-21
Hey guys, I'm here.

Well, Rasa already explained quite well this subject, so I will add just a few things.

First, most of my covers are intended for DVD cases, as it is the whole point of me creating "unofficial" Ubuntu covers. I don't like that much the feel of having an amazing software packed into small CD case, paper or plastic, doesn't matter.

Following that, DVD cover size in a real world is approx 27,3cm x 18,3cm (compared to CD case with 12,1cm sizes). And covers I designed are ALL in high resolution (300dpi or 500dpi), with dimensions 3224x2160pix or 5374x3600pix to perfectly fit DVD cover dimensions.

So if you are familiar with dots-per-inch and other resolution related stuff, than you will know what I'm talking about.

So, when we talk about preparing picture for printing, all my covers are already "prepared" for right dimensions. If you are using MS Office, or Open Office, they will be automatically scalled to proper printing dimensions (27,3 x 18,3) right after you import them into Office. The same thing is with PDF, or any other printing related software.

I'm not sure how did you manage to get small sizes, and why do you have to scale anything. About missing CD labels, there are two sets of "universal" labels I created for Ubuntu, so you can add a version number by yourself if you want, in Gimp or Ps...

Cheers...

My covers are intended for standard DVD boxes, that most PC games are shipped with, and most DVD movies too. And also the most easy to find, when you're buying empty boxes. Here are the pictures to show you that covers are fitting boxes perfectly fine.
