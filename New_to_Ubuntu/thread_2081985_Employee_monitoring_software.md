### Post by squakie on 2012-11-09
We can't know until they reply.  The concern here is that the OP wants to "spy" on their employees - to be sure they are actively working and doing company business.  That's why there is the very real legality concerns here.  In the USA, we have laws both protecting the employer and also the employees.  The employer deserves the old fashioned "honest days work" from it's employees, but there are other tools for monitoring business activity, firewall tools that can block access to non-business sites, etc..  The idea of using a "camera" to directly view everyone's activity seems hard-pressed to me, and perhaps not so much of a valid request.  Yes, there are companies, especially those doing restricted or high-clearance level jobs, that might require video observation.  But for the "normal" jobs - this would turn people away from the company wondering why the management does not trust anyone.  In these cases, I've also always been curious as to who spies on the "spier"?
