### Post by oldsoundguy on 2009-09-28
I purposely purchased a hand full of D-Link DWL 520X cards off of eBay.  Put them in all of my wireless desktops and have a couple of extras for when I repair computers and need to get on line on broadband for the tons of updates.
They are "super G" cards in speed.  Not as fast as the N cards, but for most at their current internet speed, more than enough.
They load right out of the box .. no tweaking involved. Just boot, select the network icon from the panel, and plug in your security password!

### Post by t0p on 2009-09-28
Another solution to the compatibility problem is to get slightly older gear.  Hardware incompatibility can crop up with the latest kit, when Linux hackers haven't had a chance to write the necessary code.

Of course, if you go for older hardware you may miss out on the most blindingly powerful machines available.  But I think even older equipment nowadays can be very very good.
