Hi all,

One feature that Windows and OS/X handle very well is the seamless switching from 1 monitor to 2 and vice-versa.  Using Windows, I'm able to plug my external monitor into my laptop and instantly have the system recognize and reconfigure for the extra display.  Similarly, when I unplug the VGA connector, all the windows I had open on the monitor are transferred back to the laptop display, etc.

While there's lots of info online for configuring Ubuntu for dual-monitors, I haven't been able to find much at all for how to implement the behavior described above.  I'm currently running Ocelot and my system has an NVIDIA graphics card.  Often after modifying Xorg.conf I have to reboot the machine, and sometimes the OS takes multiple reboots to properly respond to the Xorg changes.

Ideally, users shouldn't have to play with Xorg.conf or even NVIDIA X server, and certainly shouldn't have to reboot.  Is there any way anyone can see to make this happen?  Perhaps this is possible but the required knowledge is not widely distributed?
