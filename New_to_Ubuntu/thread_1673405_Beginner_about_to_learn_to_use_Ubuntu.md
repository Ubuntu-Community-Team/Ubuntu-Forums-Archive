### Post by akand074 on 2011-01-22
I can't imagine that Windows would degrade the battery life that much.. unless it was running within Mac at the same time (like a virtual machine). Anyways, Windows does use a bit more power than most OS. Ubuntu should be rather close to Mac OSX in terms of battery usage, the only configurations you may have to do are the same thing you'd do on your Mac (display brightness, etc.. ) though some people like to do CPU scaling. Though, if you buy a PC that says you will get 2:30-3h battery life, then you'll probably get around 3h battery life max.. even if you had Mac installed on it. It's still mostly the hardware, not the software, although it would increase/decrease depending on usage.
