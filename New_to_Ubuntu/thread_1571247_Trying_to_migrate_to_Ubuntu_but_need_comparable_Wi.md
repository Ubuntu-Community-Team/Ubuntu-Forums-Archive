### Post by sgosnell on 2010-09-09
Evolution syncs fine with Google calendar, no third-party software needed.  There are tutorials on the Ubuntu wiki.  The short version is just start a new calendar, type Google, and give it your google calendar address and password.  You can get the address from the Google calendar page, and the help explains it pretty well.  With the correct address in Evolution, the sync is automatic every time you access the internet.  Your phone should also sync with Google calendar, which will sync with Evolution.  It might be possible to sync the phone directly to Evolution, but I've never tried a WinMobile phone at all.  If it will sync to Outlook, it should sync to Evolution, but I won't guarantee anything.

### Post by sgosnell on 2010-09-10
I have my calendar, my daughter's, and my mother's, all syncing.  Just subscribe to all the calendars you want to sync on Google, and they will show up in Evolution.  I have each calendar's entries in a different color, so I can tell at a glance which calendar they're from.  If you want your appointments to show in the other calendars, you need to have them subscribe to your Google calendar.  That's easy enough to do.

I think gsyncit is charging you for stuff you can do for free, with just a little research.  That's often the case with Windows software.
