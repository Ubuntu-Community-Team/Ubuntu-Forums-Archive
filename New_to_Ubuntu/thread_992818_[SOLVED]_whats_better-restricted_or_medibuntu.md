The two are completely different things. Restricted drivers are drivers you might need to use a hardware device (wireless, graphics). and they are licensed by a third party, thus imposing restrictions for use and distribution.
Medibuntu provides multimedia codecs for those users who can legally use them according to their country's laws.

### Post by AndyCooll on 2008-11-25
Restricted-extras is a collection of apps which have certain restrictions and contains apps such as Adobe Flash and Sun Java, as well as the gstreamer bad and ugly set of codecs. 

Medibuntu is a repository where the legality of the codecs is even more questionable in some countries than the "bad" and "ugly" set. Primarily these are the libdvdcss2 and W32codecs packages.
