### Post by DapperMe17 on 2011-12-05
> Can I keep Ubuntu as it is and also interface with Zorin or other interfaces? I am not quite sure how it works.

Yes, you can install other flavors of *buntu, such as XFCE, KDE or LXDE. However, Zorin is a completely seperate OS, is not affiliated with *buntu, and is not a downloadable interface from the Ubuntu repo.

> I am dual booting with W7. I would like to eliminate windows but I need to work in Visual FoxPro and there are compatibility issues.

You should be able to run Windows programs in Ubuntu, by installing PlayOnLinux from the repos.

> Right now I am just fumbling around getting a feel of this and so far I am really impressed and comfortable with its use.

Great way to do it!

> How do I get items to the desktop? I figured out how to get FireFox, Opera, Chrome and ThunderBird to the desktop. I would like to get Banshee among others to the desktop.

Should be able to easily "drag 'n drop" from the menu. However, there may be a new way, with the new Unity desktop in Ubuntu. 

If you like the Windows interface, then you may wish to try Zorin. Zorin tries very hard to have the look and feel of Win7.

### Post by fantab on 2011-12-05
For Windows User making a shift to Linux/ Ubuntu, Dual Booting is an excellent idea until such time you don't miss windows any more. Like mentioned above in post **#3** you can use PlayonLinux to run most of your Windows software on Linux. 
Also read [Linux is not Windows]("http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm").

Desktop environment [DE] is basically a Graphical Interface you use to navigate through your computer. There are several DE available to use its your choice, some of the most common DE are [HERE]("http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/difference-linux-desktop-environments-technology-explained/").

Apart from DE there are numerous Linux OS (DISTROS) available to choose from. See [HERE]("http://distrowatch.com/dwres.php?resource=major") for the major ones.
