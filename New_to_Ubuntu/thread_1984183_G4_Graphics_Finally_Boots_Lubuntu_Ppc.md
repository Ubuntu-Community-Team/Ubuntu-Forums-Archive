### Post by Javelin Dan on 2012-05-21
rsavage -
 
If you remember, I'm the guy from a couple of months ago who couldn't get Ubuntu 11.04 or Lubuntu for powerpc to boot past a cursor on a dark screen (G4 "Graphics", 450Mhz, 20.5 GB hard drive, previously 512 MB RAM). I *_finally_* got a 512 MB memory chip that would allow my machine to boot (now have a total of 896 MB RAM) and I succesfully installed Lubuntu 12.04 on it. Once while running the live CD, and on the re-boot immediately after installation, it hung up the same way (cursor, no desktop), but after rebooting again all sems OK. 
 
A couple of first impressions:
 
This is a painfully slow boot; I haven't timed it yet, but it seems to take 3-4 minutes to boot up to desktop. Once booted, it seems to run reasonably well, but is not the speed burner that I had hoped for. Time needed to open programs - Firefox, Abiword, etc. is much slower than anything I've had before. But I've noticed this also in Ubuntu 12.04 (Ppc) on my wife's G4 eMac (1 GB RAM) and have seen a few similar complaints on the forums. May just be the nature of the beast, or may just be this particular type of machine.
 
Surfing the net seems entirely acceptable, probably a little faster than before due mostly to the increase in memory. I was surprised to find Gnash and Gnash Plug-in already installed. I also installed Lightspark and I've got typical Ppc Flash - works, but kind of crappy. I experimented around with various video downloaders and on this machine, I far and away like Video Download Helper the best. 
 
I've only had this up and running a couple of days, so more experimetation is in order. Overall, I'm not delerious, but very satisfied. I have to keep in perspective that I've just resurrected a nearly 20-year old machine and made it viable with a modern, up-to-date operating system that will be upgradable for at least the next 18 months, and all this was FREE! I want to thank you again for your previous help. I promised that I would report if I got it up and running and I did.
