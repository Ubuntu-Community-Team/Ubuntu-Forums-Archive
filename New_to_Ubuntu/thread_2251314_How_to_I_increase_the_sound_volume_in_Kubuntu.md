### Post by deadflowr on 2014-11-03
Aside from the simple answer of toggling the sound via the sound icon that you should have, or can add to the panel, do you want to either A) be able to toggle the volume via keyboard shortcuts, or B) increase the volume beyond 100%?

If A, then open System Settings >> Shortcuts and Gestures >> Global Keyboard Shortcuts then look at the line that says KDE component and click on it and then look for the entry marked kmix.
You should have entries there for Volume Up, Volume Down, and Mute. Set the shortcuts as you would like, and click Apply when done.

If B, click on the sound icon in the panel, and at the bottom where it says Mixer, click on the wrench next to it and then in the new pop up window select the section called General. In here is a setting to increase the sound beyond 100%(goes to 150%) I believe it requires pulseaudio; whatever that means. Again click apply when set.

Hope either of these helps.
