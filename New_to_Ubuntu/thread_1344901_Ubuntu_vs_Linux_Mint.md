### Post by earthpigg on 2009-12-03
> **Space-Wolf said:**
> So in most cases is it the same as Ubuntu?  I mean obviously there are going to be differences, but for the most part if I know my way around Ubuntu alright, will Mint be much different?

nah, it'll be the same. trivial graphical user interface differences. mintInstall instead of Ubuntu Software Center. things like that. either way, you are presented with some obvious and well designed pointy-clicky way to do something.

because of those trivial user interface differences.... if you truly are an ***absolute*** beginner, then Mint will be ***very*** different to you.

however, the more you know and learn, the smaller the perceived difference will become.

experienced ubuntu users regard mint with the comment: "why not just use ubuntu??"

likewise, experienced debian users regard ubuntu with the comment: "why not just use debian??"

if you know your way around any of those three, you will know your way around all three.

eventually, you will reach a point where it doesn't make a bit of difference what distro you use - you will be equally at home on any.


if it comes time to reinstall your OS and you don't feel like typing 'sudo apt-get install ubuntu-restricted-extras'.... sure, go ahead and download the mint .iso instead of the ubuntu one. 700mb either way, and the result is the same.

the short version of all that: flip a coin. maybe even find a magical three sided coin and consider debian testing (what ubuntu is based on).
