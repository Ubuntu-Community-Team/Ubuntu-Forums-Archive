### Post by Lisiano on 2012-02-27
Rootkits... I don't think they exist in the wild, feel free to correct me. In theory they are possible though. I know there are some Linux viruses but if you compare to the amount Windows has, it's just a single drop compared to an ocean.
Personally, I don't even use anti-viruses, due to the fact there is barely any need for it. Also most just scan for Windows viruses and as such are mainly useful only on file/mail servers or when you wish to trade files with Windows users and wish to protect them. Btw most viruses in Linux that I know of were just POCs (Proof Of Concept) and as such, not dangerous.

### Post by audiomick on 2012-02-27
You should not be complacent, but you can be fairly confident with Ubuntu. There just aren't that many viruses or whatever aimed at Linux.

I don't know about rootkits specifically, but I haven't seen anything much at all about them here, so I assume that they are not that much of an issue.

There is a virus scanner in the repository called ClamAV which I think is a command line tool, but has a graphic interface called Clam tk. Search for them in the software centre. From what I read here, most people consider that the main reason for them is if you are downloading stuff that you want to scan before you pass it on to a windows install.

There are some links to Info about anti virus stuff here
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Security#Anti-Virus](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Security#Anti-Virus)
