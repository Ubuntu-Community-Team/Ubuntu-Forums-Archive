### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-10-11
It sounds like you're happy with everything the way it is, and don't personally know of any reasons to upgrade to 11.04.

If you're happy with your system and don't see a reason to upgrade, don't upgrade. Ubuntu 10.04 will still be supported until 2013. Maybe upgrade in late 2012 (update to 12.04 LTS).

If you want newer features, newer hardware support and the new Unity user interface, then by all means upgrade to 11.10 when it comes out. And when I say "upgrade", I mean do a fresh install. Upgrading from 10.04 to such a newer version would take a lot of time and a lot of bandwidth unless you do a fresh install.

Ubuntu 10.4 is a **LTS** (Long Term Support) release while 11.4 is not. Next Ubuntu LTS release will be 12.4, which means LTS is released every two years and supported upto three years.

There is no such thing as 'BUG-Free'. However if you are looking for stability then LTS is usually the pick.

I would suggest that you dual boot 11.10 with 10.4, try and see for yourself and make a decision.

### Post by hodad on 2011-10-26
One thing to watch out for is that the desktop is changed to Unity.  Personally, I don't like Unity.  It seems that the icons simply aren't as flexible as in the "classic" desktop, so I went back to the classic desktop, which is still available under 11.04.  However, their are significant bugs in the classic desktop under 11.04.  Also, upgrades while using classic must be done on terminal, etc. 

If 10.04 LTS isa working for you, I'd suggest you wait awhile to do the upgrade until a few more bugs are worked out.  Also, you might want to give Unity a "testdrive" before committing to it. Just my 2 cents...
