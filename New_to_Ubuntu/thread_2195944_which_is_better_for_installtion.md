### Post by howefield on 2013-12-27
LTS releases such as 12.04 (and the upcoming 14.04) are supported for 5 years on both the desktop and server editions. Non LTS such as 13.10 are supported for 9 months, so it largely depends on how you feel about upgrading.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-12-27
I suggest the LTS version might suit you best. As mentioned, five years support. You can upgrade 12.04 LTS directly to 14.04 LTS when it arrives next April without having to go through the interim releases to get there. LTS releases are upgradeable to the next LTS every two years, while the interim releases are upgradeable to the next release only unless you do a clean install.

### Post by Impavidus on 2013-12-27
Usually I recommend LTS releases because they don't give the trouble of having to upgrade every six months, but in this case it doesn't matter that much if you intend to upgrade to the next LTS release when it is released. The current latest release (13.10) will be supported until three months after he next LTS (14.04) is released and there will be a one-click upgrade available, just as from the current LTS (12.04) to the next LTS. You didn't give any hardware details, but if it's recent, the latest release may give better support.

Don't use 11.10. You don't have to buy a new version at a shop, just download it for free (legally) from [http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop). You can make a donation though.
