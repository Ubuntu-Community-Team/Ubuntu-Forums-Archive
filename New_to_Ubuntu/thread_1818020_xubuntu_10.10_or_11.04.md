### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2011-08-04
For a total newbie, *definitely* use the LTS versions of Xubu and stick to the LTS releases!

Beginning with 10.04 (Lucid), LTS releases are being built from Debian *Testing* rather than Debian *Unstable* like all the other releases. The "Debian stuff" that Lucid was built from has since been released as Debian Stable ("Squeeze")! 

And it's good for whole 'nother 2 years! It's fast, simple enough even for a silly little dixiedancer, but full-featured enough to let me do everything I did on Windows effortlessly (and fast!).

My only issue with Xubu 10.04 was *sound* on my old Dell. It seems that the addition of PulseAudio created some conflicts on my hardware, but for most users it apparently isn't an issue. If it happens on your 'puter, it's an easy fix (clicky [here]("http://robinsrantsandraves.wordpress.com/2011/07/27/replacing-pulseaudio-with-alsa-in-ubuntu/")).

But definitely steer a new user to the LTS versions!!
