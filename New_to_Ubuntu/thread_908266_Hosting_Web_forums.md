### Post by keith.eckstein on 2008-09-02
**Messel** - For a site that I recently knocked up for a friend, I found it easier to use motigo.com and not have to worry about updates or strain on my server - the site that I did (very quick and dirty) is at [http://98438.forums.motigo.com](http://98438.forums.motigo.com)

Obviously, I need to spend some time tidying it up but it was a very quick and easy (and, more importantly, free!) solution - my server is hosted by 1&1.fr and has a plug in forum but, I found the motigo solution easier to do and obviously, saves me bandwidth and processor cycles.

I guess that it depends on how much you want to integrate the forum into your site - for my friend, the solution that I implemented works fine - there is a link to return to the main site and the link from the main site opens the forum in a new tab.

When a "Life in Brittany, France" expat type forum that I am a regular contributor to, went down last week - I was able to knock up a simple Motigo.com forum in minutes - as a staging post until the original forum came back on line.

My personal feeling about forums (and I know that I'm straying off the point here, a little bit), is that, no matter what technology you use - the most important (and difficult) thing is the Moderation - get that right (like on this forum) and all is sweet - too much (or too little) and the forum can easily become a real pain.

Anyway, best of luck with whatever you do..

Keith
