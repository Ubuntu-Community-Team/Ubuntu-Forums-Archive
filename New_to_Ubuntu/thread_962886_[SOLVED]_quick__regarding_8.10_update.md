### Post by le singe on 2008-10-29

### Post by OldTimeTech on 2008-10-29
There is a possibility that something you have setup in your install of 8.04 will break when you update through synaptic or add/remove.  Also each new Ubuntu also has new repos.

Many just feel that you get a better install without problems if you back up all your own data to another hard drive or flash drive.  That way you can see the all new 8.10 without problems.

I've been known to wait for 2 weeks to a month...when bugs seem to be worked out and then I just upgrade.  Sometimes it works, sometimes it doesn't.  I'm sure the back up and new install is the safest ;)

### Post by nkri on 2008-10-29
It is usually safer (no risk of old packages not getting along with new ones, etc.), and in my experience, the computer boots and runs faster on a clean install, but I can't say the same for a network upgrade.  This is all personal preference, of course, so do with what you feel the most comfortable:)

Also, if you decide to do a clean install, I recommend that you make a separate /home partition to keep all of your settings intact after the upgrade and just have to reinstall packages not included in the install to have a fully functional system again.  [_Here_]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome")'s how.

Good luck!
-nkri
