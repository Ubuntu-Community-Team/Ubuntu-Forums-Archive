It's OK :). Xorg.conf can be a bit confusing, so some explanation:

**monitor1** is your Samsung monitor
**device1** is your video card (GeForce 7300)
**screen1** is like a 'window' that X.org makes

X.org uses **device1** to generate **screen1** which is shown on **monitor1**. 

There are actually two resolutions that you can set: your screen resolution and your desktop resolution. These should always be the same; if the screen resolution is smaller than your desktop resolution, the screen will be like a microscope and the desktop would be the slide; you'd have to scroll left and right in order to view your entire desktop.

In the screenshot that shows "Screen and Graphics Preferences" it shows two screens in the menu, but one of them is greyed out. That's because your video card likely has two connectors, a VGA and a DVI. I'm guessing that your monitor is plugged into your VGA connector, and the DVI is unused.

If you had two monitors plugged into the same card, your xorg.conf would also have a section labeled "monitor2"; it wouldn't have "device2" or "screen2" because the same device (video card) is being used and "screen1" would just have a much larger resolution to fit across both screens.

The problem is likely with Widelands (can you play it? or does it crash with the given error?)
