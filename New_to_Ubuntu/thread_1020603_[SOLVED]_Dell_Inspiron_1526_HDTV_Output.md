### Post by ELF_O8 on 2008-12-24
Searched the forums for this and found nothing similar.
Figured it out, thought I'd post it for posterity.

So, I've been with Ubuntu for a while (since Gutsy and even a little experiment way back when with Dapper) and I have loved it; so many options, so much to do, all FREE.

Anyways, I just got a new laptop before going off to college and my studies really kept from putting Ubuntu on it. Now I'm home over the Christmas break with really nothing to do so I decided "Why not?"

My laptop is a Dell Inspiron 1526 and everything worked perfectly almost out-of-the-box (after running the update manager the first time even wireless was integrated seamlessly). The only thing that didn't was the HDTV output; I tried using the "Screen Resolution" utility because I had sen it work with previous computers with VGA outputs - no dice. That's when I remembered Envy. So, I hopped over to Synaptic and installed Envy, asked it to automatically fix whatever and it obliged perfectly. I looked in the App Menu and found it had installed the ATI Control Center Linux Edition. I had no idea that that even existed!

Needless to say, after some configuration and a quick restart, everything works amazing. Love Ubuntu.
