### Post by sub2007 on 2008-08-29
> **zamadatix said:**
> actually i not noticeing much of a difference in swiftfox, i imagine the orignal firefox code was already pretty optimized.

It's in how it's compiled. The idea with Swiftfox is that it's compiled for your processor rather than a plain binary and also it's compiled using flags that are designed to optimise speed. That should give a theoretical speed increase. Whether you do see one or not depends, compiling everything from scratch is a reason that many people choose Gentoo as their GNU/Linux distro.

But it very much depends on exactly what processor you have. I find Swiftweasel is both faster and more stable than plain Firefox.
