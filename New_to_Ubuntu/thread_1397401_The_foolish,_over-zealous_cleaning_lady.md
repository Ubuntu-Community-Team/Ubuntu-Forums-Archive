### Post by Bartender on 2010-02-03
Sheesh, all those big words, and *so many* of them.

As warfacegod mentions, removing the CMOS battery will reset BIOS back to default.  There should be a jumper on the motherboard (similar or identical to the little jumper on the back of a PATA drive for setting master, slave, or cable select) that can be moved over one pin to reset the BIOS.  

You do one or the other; you don't have to do both.

Go to Google Images instead of the default Google Web (the "Images" option is up in the left-hand corner of the main Google webpage) and type in ```
motherboard BIOS jumper
```
for some visual aids.  You can click on any one of the pictures to go to the original webpage.

EDIT: I've been told that using a vacuum cleaner is dangerous because the hose builds up static charge.  A couple of cans of compressed air is probably the safest, although I've cleaned out dozens of PC's with cautious use of a small air compressor.
