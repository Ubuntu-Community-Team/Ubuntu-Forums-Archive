### Post by Paqman on 2012-05-08
Stick to reputable sources for your torrents. There are a LOT of dodgy torrent sites out there. Pirate Bay used to be good and porn-free, but I notice they've got it now too, but you can filter it out.

Be careful about getting software or operating systems from torrent sites. Downloading Ubuntu is fine if you get the .torrent from Ubuntu.com, but don't ever install anything unless you're sure it's 100% kosher.

Check the comments on torrent sites. If something isn't what it purports to be, or is poor quality, people will say so.

### Post by iansane on 2012-05-08
In my opinion, if it's not from a trusted source then it's trial and error. torrents are supposed to be used for sharing legitimate non-pirated files and even though I've seen and uploaded legitimate torrents like Ubuntu distros, I'd say about 90% of torrents on tracker sites are pirated music, movies, and software. The fact that it's illegal to download all of these means it's going to be a "at your own risk" thing. 

Usually you can look at comments on tracker sites and see what the general consensus is on the quality and security of the torrent and you can see if the file is suspiciously large or small.
