### Post by snowpine on 2013-02-18
The way that I learned is by answering people's questions right here on these forums. If you have a question, then chances are 10 other people have the same question, too. If you find the solution, then share it with the others. Or find someone who seems really confused and, even if you don't know the answer off the top of your head, work together to research or troubleshoot the problem.

I think this is better than "book learning" (or, at least, an important supplement to it) because it exposes you to the actual everyday real-world issues that people face trying to use Ubuntu. Additionally, you will benefit the community, which is the meaning of the word "Ubuntu." :)
