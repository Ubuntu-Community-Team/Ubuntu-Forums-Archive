### Post by Rolcol on 2009-02-18
A music cd, a cd made to play in disc players, is not the same as a data CD.  The music files are converted before they're recorded onto the disks and you can therefore only store ~70min of audio.  Data CDs are not created the same way as audio CDs.

### Post by Rolcol on 2009-02-18
This isn't due to Brasero.  The problem is due to standards.  The songs/audio tracks are converted so any audio player up to standard will be able to play any song without problems.  You can burn those music tracks onto the cd if you create an MP3 CD which is essentially a data cd.  Writing raw data onto a CD will fit as long as it's under the 700MB limit.  Audio files turned into a pure audio cd are converted.
