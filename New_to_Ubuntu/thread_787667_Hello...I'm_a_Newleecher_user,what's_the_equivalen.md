### Post by FreewareFan on 2008-05-09
Hello.  I also used Newsleecher, since their very first release a couple of years ago.  When I switched over to Ubuntu, I looked around for a couple of months, and for me, the best replacement program is Hellanzb .  Once you get used to the fact that it's a terminal program, it's really very good!  There are a couple of GUI frontend programs that you can use with it, but overall, if you just get used to using in in a terminal, you will soon see that it does a very good job!  

That being said, there are a couple of things that I will clarify.  First, there is no SuperSearch, like in Newsleecher.  You will need to use a web based site for things like that, for example [www.binsearch.info](www.binsearch.info) .  You can find anything at that site, that you can with SuperSearch.   Second, once you find what you are looking for, you need to download the .nzb file for that item, so that you can use it in Hellanzb.  

Anyways, your mileage may vary, but for me over the last several months, Hellanzb is the way to go!!

[http://www.hellanzb.com/trac/](http://www.hellanzb.com/trac/)
