### Post by Bartender on 2012-11-30
Have you tried Chromium, the default browser?  Does it display normally?  It kinda sounds to me like your screen resolution might be off.

If you click on the little blue 'bird', or whatever that is, in the lower left, then click on Preferences, then on Monitor Settings, what numbers do you see?

Compare that to your screen's default resolution.  I'm assuming you're running an LCD panel?  Unlike CRT's, LCD's have a preferred resolution that they want to run at.

Also in Preferences, click on "Additional Drivers" up on top.  Does Lubuntu say anything about needing drivers for your video adapter?  

What is your video adapter?  Onboard or discrete?  Intel? AMD?  nVidia?

BTW, allow me to share what I've found to be the easiest way of dealing with wallpapers in Linux.  I create a folder in Pictures and call it "Wallpaper".  Then I grab pictures that I might want to use as wallpaper from my camera, or the internet, etc. and store all of them in that folder.  Leave them right there, and you won't have problems losing wallpaper.
