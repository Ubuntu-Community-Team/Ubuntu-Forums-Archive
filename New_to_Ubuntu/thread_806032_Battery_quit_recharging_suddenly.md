### Post by Sarah L on 2008-05-24
I've had the same problem on my laptop; in fact I even replaced my battery after it would no longer charge in any circumstances.

Does your battery charge not charge at all, or only under certain conditions? Sometimes my battery will refuse to charge if it does not have critically low power, or if it is past a certain temperature.

Try draining it by turning off any battery conservation methods (like suspending) and keeping your computer on. After it is completely out of charge, try plugging your laptop in and see if anything happens.

### Post by sayakb on 2008-05-24
Some LI-Ion cells refuse to charge when drained below even 40% of power but would eventually start charging if kept connected. Are you certain that the battery has no faults? Plus, try keeping it connected for a considerable amount of time and observe what happens.
