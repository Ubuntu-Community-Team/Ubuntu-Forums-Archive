### Post by jerome1232 on 2009-01-16
1) Odd I find it very easy to theme, customization is a strong point in linux. If you goto [http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/) have a look at gtk2.x theme's to change the overall appearance. GDM theme's change your login screen.

To install a theme simply download the .tar.gz file, right click your desktop, click appearance, click theme's, drag and drop the theme in. Same with gdm theme's but you  goto System-Administration-login window-local and drop the theme in there.

### Post by avtolle on 2009-01-21
Be sure, though, to remove any other instance of Flash (likely Gnash) or there will be conflicts. This is checked by going to Synaptic (under System>Administration>Synaptic Package Manager) and typing gnash in the search window. If it is installed (box by its name solid green on my computer) click on it to mark it for complete removal. After Synaptic does its thing, then close it out and install the Adobe Flash as suggested above. BTW, when checking on Gnash, also look for the plugin (it should show up in the list generated by the search) and be sure it is also marked for total removal.
