### Post by blazemore on 2009-01-14

Not all older drives are, so I'd check that first.

### Post by niteshifter on 2009-01-14
Hi,

The specs for your drive are [here]("http://www.sonynec-optiarc.com/products/slim_combo/index.html").

If it's a blank DVD disc: That drive can not write DVDs.

If it's a disc with something burned already on it: It could be -

A) Doesn't like that type of DVD (DVD-R vs DVD+R, even though specs say it should take either some drives are finicky about this).
B) Doesn't like the filesystem it sees (UDF version).
C) Doesn't like the brand of DVD.
D) Failed hardware

Will it spin up with a mass produced DVD, i.e. a movie? If not, then it's most likely a hardware failure.
