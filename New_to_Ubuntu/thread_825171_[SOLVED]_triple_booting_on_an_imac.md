### Post by gr4nf on 2008-06-10
While booted into Leopard, open up Disk Utility. Select your HD, and choose "Partitions". Set up the map so that there is plenty of free space, maybe 30% of the HD. Resize the Windows and Mac partitions, hit "apply", and then try installing ubuntu, with the "Use Largest Continuous Free Space" as the option for the partitioning.
