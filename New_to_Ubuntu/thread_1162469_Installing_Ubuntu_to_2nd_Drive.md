It's pretty straight forward. Burn a live-cd and try. There is plenty of helpful graphics and instructions of what to do, and nothing will change on your computer until you confirm that you are ok with the partitioning. Grub will default install over windows in the MBR. But before doing so it will show that it found windows, and ask if it has found all other operating systems. I will even import settings from windows if you wish.

Burn the disc at slowest speed possible on your burner. It minimizes the risk for corrupted files. Enjoy your new Ubuntu system! ):P
