### Post by bsalt on 2008-11-18
Well, I bought a SanDisk Sansa Fuze 4GB and I love it. It has an expansion slot for a MicroSD card, so I bought an 8GB card for $20, and upgraded my player to 12GB. The total cost was $100 - $110 (even after buying a protective cover), and now I own a 12GB device that has no hard drive, is tiny but with a big enough screen to make me happy... the battery life is around 20 hours of music play... and it supports Linux out of the box. It has two modes - MTP (PlaysForSure) and MSC (hard drive style).


I recommend that nobody buys a Zune only because it is so proprietary. Get a device that will work in Linux, Windows, and Mac - like my Sansa. Get an iPod even - they at least have good support and have been improved upon for a while already.


But those are just my two cents. Take it for what it is worth.
