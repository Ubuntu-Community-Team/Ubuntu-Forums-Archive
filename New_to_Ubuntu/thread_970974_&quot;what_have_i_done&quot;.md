### Post by Travarious on 2008-11-04
My first questions is how did you get Linux on your system was it on there when you bought it? If so what version is it? to find out the version click on system then on about Ubuntu. some where in that window it should have the phrase "Thank you for your interest in Ubuntu <someNumber>" what is the someNumber on your system?

secondly to answer your question "what is compatible(windows)or is linux out there on it's own." Linux is it's own operating system windows is it's own operating system both of them will have things that are created specifically for there environment. that being said an operating system is versatile and powerful it's limitation for compatibility are most often only limited by the experience of the user at the system. there is not to much that can't be done some way or another using Ubuntu.

A more direct answer to your second question "WHAT IS LINUX"what can i do with it etc." Linux is a powerful operating system supported by a community of users who want to have open source software that is little to no cost to the end user and work reliably in the manner it was designed with a high level of security. depending on A. the ability of the user. or B. your determination to accomplish something and willingness to ask for help. You can accomplish pretty much anything your imagination can conceive to do using Linux.

some questions you may want to ask yourself instead of what can Linux do might be.
1. is Linux the right operating system for me?
2. what do I want from an operating system?
3. how much learning am I willing to do to have a more secure and stable operating system?

after asking these questions of yourself you may want to go to Google and try and search for some reviews about your version of Linux.
