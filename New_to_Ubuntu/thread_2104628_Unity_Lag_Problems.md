### Post by drawkcab on 2013-01-13
If you are using 12.04 (Long-Term Support), you might try going to the software center and installing the unity-2d shell and logging into it rather than the full unity desktop.  It'll give you a unity desktop but drastically lower the overhead in terms of your resource uses.  Unity-2d is not available in 12.10 last I heard.

Keep in mind that your troubles might be other than the DE hogging resources.  Some folks have had real trouble with 12.10 and their video drivers which might be the problem.  12.04 has been solid on all four of my machines (intel, nvidia, AMD gpus).

Lastly, Xubuntu is a great option if you need to run ubuntu on resource-scarce hardware.  Add a dock like Cairo, Docky or even an xfce panel, customize it to your liking and you're good to go.
