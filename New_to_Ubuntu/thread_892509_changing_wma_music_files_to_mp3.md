### Post by jminker on 2008-08-17
Keep in mind that although wma files may have decent sound they are lower-quality copies of the originals. When you convert one such reduced format into another you will lose even more of the original quality of sound. I also have numerous wma files. By installing the Ubuntu Restricted Extras (found in Main Menu > Add/Remove) I can play wma's on my system. You may want to try that.

Jim
