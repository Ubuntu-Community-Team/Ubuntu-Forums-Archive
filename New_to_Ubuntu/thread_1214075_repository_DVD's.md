### Post by Hospadar on 2009-07-15
To my knowledge ubuntu doesn't do that, but I know debian (which ubuntu is based off of and very similar to) has several options like that
I once dowloaded a 14-cd mega set.  There may well be places to get the debian disks for free or cheap.  Try your local linux user's group, they often will do that kind of thing.  I know the one near me has regular events where people can bring their machines and get set up.  I bet someone in a group like that would be happy to burn some disks for you.  Just google "<your nearest major city> linux user's group" and you'll probably turn something up.

That said, the regular ubuntu cd has most of the essentials on it, and there is more on the cd than just what is installed.  If the problem is that your connection is too slow to download things in the ordinary fashion (i.e. slow dial up), you might consider getting one of the regular ubuntu cd's (which can be installed for free), and then building all the stuff you want from source.  It can be a little tricky at first, but you can save a lot of bandwidth that way.

You could always try a friend or public or university library, often you can burn stuff there (on your own cd's)

It is also possible to put stuff from the software repositories onto a cd or dvd with "aptoncd"  But I think that requires already having an internet-connected install.
