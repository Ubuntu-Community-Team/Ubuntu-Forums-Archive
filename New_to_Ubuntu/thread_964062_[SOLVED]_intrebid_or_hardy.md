### Post by nynoah on 2008-10-30
If you are a new user, I would always wait at least a month before you upgrade to the newest version of Ubuntu.  That gives it a month to let others discover the bugs.  For a new user, the first month of any roll of a new Ubuntu distro is always a head ache.  After being on Ubuntu for over 3 years now, history shows that the first month of any new release is when a lot of bugs are discovered.  Followed by tons of updates.  Its nothing out of the ordinary.  Its just how things go.

### Post by bhadotia on 2008-10-30
I upgraded to hardy from gutsy and that went without problems. Except having to reconfigure (which is pretty easy- sudo dpkg-reconfigure -a) packages because of the interrupted installation process, there was no problem. Of course, I don't mean that this will be the same with you- just sharing my experience.

If you installed some drivers (like wireless) manually , you will have to reinstall them again coz your kernel will be upgraded but that will be the last time you will be doing that because of the feature of the new kernel that automatically builds manually  installed drivers upon upgrade. There might be some problem regarding your old configurations not being compatible with the new software (thats what the experienced people say). But if everything is fine you will have actually more to gain that loose anything. And, of course, you should always backup the data just in case anything unexpectedly wrong happens.
