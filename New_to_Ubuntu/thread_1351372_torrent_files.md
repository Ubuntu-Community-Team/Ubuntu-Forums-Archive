### Post by jward3010 on 2009-12-10
> **karmicgaz said:**
> In my previous life (windows) using bittorrent when I finished downloading a film for instance it became a film (1 file) but I've just downloaded a film (free to download in torrent form) and it shows as 14 separate files?
This is quite common nowadays, to split a torrent video (or whatever) into multiple rars. I think it's done from this point of view. 

Often you'll get more than just an AVI in a film torrent. You'll also get a small sampler AVI, subtitles, an NFO file and so on and when you add the torrent to your torrent application you're given the choice as to what to download. I'd say a lot of people we're ticking JUST the AVI and leaving the other stuff be (can't blame 'em if they didn't want the sampler of the film they we're about to watch). That meant that although the AVI was widely available for download, the other parts of that particular torrent weren't and the torrent wasn't officialy finishing based on waiting for these tiny files to complete.

Now when you put the lot into 15 rar files you can't leave ANY out or else the entire archive is corrupted and won't decompress properly so you have to get 100% of the torrent with these split ones.

Thats a hunch.
