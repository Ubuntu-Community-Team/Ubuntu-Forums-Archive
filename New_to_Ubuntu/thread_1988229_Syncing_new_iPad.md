### Post by searchfgold6789 on 2012-05-27
(First of all, as a side note, if you try to install iTunes using Wine, you won't be able to sync music to devices. Sucks, right?)

You should give the gtkpod software a try. If that doesn't work, then Ubuntu does not support your particular iPad - I know that it supports older ones. I think you may find it necessary to install the Ubuntu One app and sync your music using that.

libimobiledevice ([http://www.libimobiledevice.org/](http://www.libimobiledevice.org/)) supports the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd gen iPads.

### Post by shaneo1 on 2012-07-22
I use an app called iFiles which can be downloaded from the Apple app store, the cost is £4.99 i think.  When you plug in the ipad Ubuntu will show your device in nautilus and in there you will se the ifiles icon, click on that and add your music to the music folder, create an artist folder if you wish, then you can play your music through ifiles on your ipad.  

The latest iOS 5 has stopped syncing my ipad, ifiles seems to be the only way.
