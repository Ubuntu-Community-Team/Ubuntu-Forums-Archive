### Post by UltimateCat on 2012-12-02
Well; that depends-

How old is the desktop?  Is it a Dell? A Toshiba? A Hewett Packard or Compaq?
How may GB's of a HDD? And how much RAM?

Linux Puppy is the smallest distribution that I know of but other members can make recommendations just like I'm able to-
[http://puppylinux.org/main/Overview%20and%20Getting%20Started.htm]("http://puppylinux.org/main/Overview%20and%20Getting%20Started.htm")

Some versions of Ubuntu are as small a 4GB's-
Here's a site where you can view all of the Linux Distributions:
[http://distrowatch.com/](http://distrowatch.com/)

As far as Security; Linux is pretty secure and there are applications that you can install for protection and intrusion protection.  One of the applications I use is rkhunter- If any root-kits are detected this application lets you know.

There are other practices that you can perform to make your OS even more secure like machine hardening for example. 

And updating a video card could be as easy as looking for the drivers to aid your card.  If you know which card you have it could be as simple as going to the manufacturers website and download the driver.
Otherwise you would have to look for a Linux driver (non-free) to help.

And depending on the age of the computer or the video card it might need replacing if a driver isn't available.  Or you could build your own driver but that's interesting to do to say the least-

Sorry I don't know much about a Karaoke player & music organizer-
I use Rhythmbox-
Rhythmbox is an integrated music management application.
[http://projects.gnome.org/rhythmbox/](http://projects.gnome.org/rhythmbox/)
Banshee is another application for music.
[http://banshee.fm/](http://banshee.fm/)
And Amarok is another Linux application for music. I think folks that have Linux Mint use it-
[http://amarok.kde.org/marok](http://amarok.kde.org/marok)

Hope this helps-
