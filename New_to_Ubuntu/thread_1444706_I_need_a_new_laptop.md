### Post by spillin_dylan on 2010-04-01
As someone who has run two Toshiba laptops with Ubuntu, I can tell you that everything works -- to a point.  Toshiba is relatively anti-Linux, at least as far as helping you to get any hardware working goes.  I haven't had any serious problems such as WiFi or video drivers, but that's not saying you're guaranteed to be good to go.  

Problems I did face: 
- Getting the Media keys to work in Intrepid & Jaunty (SOLVED)
- Cooling fan issues in Intrepid, Jaunty and Karmic (was OK until Karmic stopped using a custom DSDT file)

I think the cooling problem is a deep-seated problem with Toshiba's BIOS settings being custom-built for Windows only, and not adhering to BIOS standards (which Linux uses).

However, they do make pretty good hardware, and I would get another one again.

All that being said, if money is not an issue, I personally would buy a Macbook and throw Windows 7 and Ubuntu on it.  I think that Apple makes some of the nicest (but definitely most expensive) hardware around.  At least nicest looking.  ;)
