### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-10-17
14.10 is not laden with highly visible new features.  If you are not experiencing any problems with 14.04 that you know have been fixed in 14.10, then I'd argue you don't have a pressing need to upgrade.

In some cases, newer versions of some software will be available for 14.10. The same reasoning applies:  Does that software fix a bug I want fixed?  Does it deliver a capability I want to use?

Factor in the fact that 14.10 will be supported for 9 months, while 14.04 is an LTS release. Nine moths after its release, 14.10 users will need to upgrade, while 14.04 users will not.

After it's releases, burn and boot a live image of 14.10 and check it out.  See what you think.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-10-17
> [COLOR=#000000]I have it working just right for me,[/COLOR]

Do not upgrade. If you want a discussion on the differences between 14.04 and 14.10 then this post should not be in this section. But you have asked for advice. And I have given it. Even though I am not precisely answering your question. 

Anyone using Ubuntu has two basic choices.

1) use an LTS version with 5 years support and upgrade to the next LTS version or the one after before support runs out. 2) Upgrade every 6 or 7 months because Interim (now called Standard) releases only have 9 months support.

Upgrading after our release has reached End of LIfe is more complicated then doing it during release. This is why the recommendation is to use an LTS version. Another reason is that you might not like what is coming in the Standard releases. They are now places where ideas are tried out and tested before the next LTS is released. And there is no going back once we upgrade.

I have been running Uptopic Unicorn since the first week of its 26 week development cycle. One week after Uptopic becomes 14.10 I will upgrade to the 15.04 development branch. That is what i do. That is what I like to do. But I still say if you cannot find your own good reason for upgrading, then stay where you are.

Regards.
