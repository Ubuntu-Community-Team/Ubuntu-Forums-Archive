Hello and welcome,

Your goal of becoming a total Ubuntu user is a worthy one.  But "becoming" indicates a process rather than an instant change.  It seems unnecessary to just plunge in without some preparations.  I'd never recommend such a drastic change.  

There's no reason to avoid a dual-boot.  Think of it as a safety-net. If you find, over time, that you are spending all of your time using Ubuntu, then you can do away with the Win 7 partition.  But don't burn your bridges too soon. 

Give yourself some time to learn and get comfortable with Ubuntu, it will be a lot more fun if you do.

### Post by Jerry N on 2012-03-05
You haven't said what kind of user files you want to move to Ubuntu.  Word processor and speadsheet files generally can be opened with Linux tools such as Libreoffice but macros won't work and some of your formatting might change.  Database files could be more of a problem and files from specialized programs such as Family Tree Maker will not be opened by any Linux programs.  You need to investigate this before dumping Windows.  Some programs might work in Wine but you should determine that ahead of time.

And as has been mentioned: Before you do anything** MAKE BACKUPS OF YOUR IMPORTANT FILES!**  This cannot be emphasized enough!  Of course you already have backups of those files, don't you?

Jerry
