### Post by WA_Garrett on 2009-04-03
Concerns like that is what this fourm is for, we are here to help. Firstly, Defraging your hard drive was a good call.

For connecting to wireless internet, it may work instantly, then again it might not, if it doesn't work right away there will probobly be a way to get it working, but that is a subject for a later thread. 

Ubuntu needs to work on a separate partition than windows, OS files can't be on the same partition as each other or else there will be problems. The Ubuntu installation process will help you create a new partition. 

As for backing up files, BACK THEM UP, BACK UP ALL OF THEM!!! It's always a good idea to do that when ever you're doing things like resizing partitions and creating new ones on a Hard Drive, get into the habit. Back everything up by any means necessary, CD's, DVD's External Hard Drives, Flash Drives what ever you have, have all of it someplace other than on your computer. I always do it when ever I do stuff like this, it may take some time, but it's better to be safe than sorry.

### Post by halitech on 2009-04-03
> **Rigorous said:**
> Can you please explain to me what sneaker net is?

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sneakernet](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sneakernet)

Sneakernet is a tongue-in-cheek term used to describe the transfer of electronic information, especially computer files, by physically carrying removable media such as magnetic tape, floppy disks, compact discs, USB flash drives, or external hard drives from one computer to another.

Sneaker refers to the shoes of the person carrying the media. This is usually in lieu of transferring the information over a computer network. This mode of data transport is often used as an academic example to illustrate the trade-off between latency and bandwidth.
