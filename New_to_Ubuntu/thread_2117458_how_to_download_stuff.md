### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-02-18
You didn't mention it specifically, but if your goal is to run Windows apps, you can save yourself a lot of trouble up front by going to the CodeWeavers site (WineHQ) and looking through the application compatibility page for the apps and versions you want to run.

Anything not listed, or listed with a rating less than Gold is going to be a waste of time installing, and in that case, you would do much better looking for a Linux alternative app.
