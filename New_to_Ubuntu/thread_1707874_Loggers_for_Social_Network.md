### Post by Eiji Takanaka on 2011-03-15
You could use wireshark to log all facebook communicae. If other people happen to be on your local subnet you can log all their messages too. Facebook although offering 'https' securness when logging in, isnt too secure as far as communications encryption goes. The same goes for e-mails. I was horrified to see during a recent experiment that not only is your e-mail text available to all on your local subnet, but as are email attachments. (Msn).

This i feel is a massive security flaw and can lead users into a false sense of security with any 'https' webportal page.

Sorry to hi-jack this thread but does anyone else think this is a bit of a security issue?
