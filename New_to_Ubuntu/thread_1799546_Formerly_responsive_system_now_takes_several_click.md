### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-07-08
Just a quick thought.

Open your mouse preferences:
"system/preferences/mouse"

Left click on the general tab and at the bottom of the tab you'll find  the Double - Click - Timeout. The image of a light bulb should be just  below the timeout adjustment. If you single click on the light bulb, it  will light for a moment and then go out. If you double click on the bulb  it should stay on. If the timeout is to slow, it will not stay on. If  you can't get the bulb to stay on (regularly) then simply increase the  timeout. Move slider towards the right side (Long).

The problem may simply be that your mouse isn't double clicking fast  enough to open the app. I'm assuming your doing this on your desktop and  not from your menu or panel. As both those only require a single click.  In which case you should note immediately if the mouse is temporarily  lighting the bulb with a single click. If it's happening in the  menu/panel make sure you don't have "Dwell Click" turned on under the  "Accessibility Tab" of the mouse preferences.

If no results, do you have any issues with the mouse after opening an app?

Good Luck & Hope this helps.
