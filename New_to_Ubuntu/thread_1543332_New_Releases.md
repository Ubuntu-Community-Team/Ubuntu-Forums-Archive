### Post by j7%&lt;RmUg on 2010-08-01
Yea, a separate /home partition is by far the best way of doing it. Upgrading has never been smooth for me so i just reinstall.

A 320GB drive is plenty (although i do have over 2TB's) of space to have multiple partitions, i have an extra 180GB here that i have 4 partitions on. I see people all the time with really small drives with 2-3 partitions on them.

urh, DVD's are a bad way of backing up, you should get an external drive or something.

Also, i use 3 separate bash scripts when i do a reinstall, one to backup, one to restore, and one to install and configure everything i need. Since all my hardware is recognized out of the box, there is nothing i need to setup manually.

So i would say, separate /home directory + backup of your data + reinstall is the way to go.

EDIT: dont worry about 10.10 being "unstable", its nothing to worry about usually, just sometimes, after final release, some annoying bugs are found and quickly fixed. One way around this is to not upgrade until 2 weeks or so after final release. Usually, by then any bad bugs are fixed.
