### Post by lloyd_b on 2009-09-10
> **getyourkarthick said:**
> What is meant by inbound and outbound  connection?

It depends on the context, but generally an "inbound" connection is one originating from another computer to your computer, while "outbound" means a connection originating from your computer to another computer.

For example, when you connect to a website, it's an "outbound" connection (because you originated it).  But if your machine is a mail server, when it receives mail it's an "inbound" connection (the sending machine originated the connection).

### Post by mapes12 on 2009-09-10
In the context of Networks:

Inbound - all the machines on your local LAN i.e. behind the server (router) that connects to WAN's and the internet. IP addressing will be static or assigned using DHCP private addresses

Outbound - Connections out of your LAN via the server (router) to the rest of the world..........and beyond. IP addressing assigned by your ISP.
