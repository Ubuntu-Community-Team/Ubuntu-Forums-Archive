### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-04-15
I have no idea what SD1, SD5 and SD7 are; they sound a bit like partitions, but they should be sda1, sda5 and sda7, and are not usually mentioned in that way. If that is what you are seeing, it is unusual.

Normally the grub menu, which is what I think you are seeing, contains the list of kernels on the system, with their numbers, eg Linux 2.6.38-14-generic, and that same number repeated for the recovery mode.  When updates give you a kernel update with a new number you will see two new lines in the grub menu.  This all quite normal, so don't worry about it.  By default the system will boot to the newest kernel.
