### Post by Psumi on 2010-03-16
Is the non-working drive working in the SATA-USB Bay?

If so, then your computer's SATA Connector is bad.

if it doesn't work with the SATA-USB Bay, then the drive is bad.

The error, "gave up waiting for root device" means that the MASTER harddrive is not able to function as it should; and as you said, the drive crashed and is not bootable.

A harddrive can be bootable and not mountable; as well as vice versa: mountable but not bootable. Depends on what happened to the drive.

For example, I punched my gateway laptop where the harddrive was located, causing the drive to fail due to "shock." The symptom was that the access indicator on the laptop for the read/write to disk status was solid/lit up all the time. if your light is doing the same thing, then there's no way to access the files on the disk without taking it to a backup service.
