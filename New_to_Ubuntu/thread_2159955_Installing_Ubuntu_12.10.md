### Post by cub on 2013-07-05
How much space you need depends on if you are going to have your /home directory (where you will save all your documents, movies, music and son) on the same partition? 
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements) recommends at least 5 GB of disk.

But why would you use 12.10? 12.04 is the LTS release and 13.04 is the latest release, I would go for one of them instead of 12.10 (if you don't have any particular reason).

### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-07-05
What do you mean by "raw partition"?

Is it a new physical partition on the disk that you have not yet formatted with any filesystem?  If so you can go ahead and install ubuntu, but I suggest for safety you use the "Something Else" option at partitioning stage, and then install your OS to that unallocated space, making formatted partitions as required.

How much space is a difficult question to answer; you can see and use files on your windows partition from Ubuntu easily.
