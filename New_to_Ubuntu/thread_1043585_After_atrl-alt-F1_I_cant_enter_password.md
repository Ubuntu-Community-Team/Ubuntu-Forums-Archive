### Post by baruch60610 on 2009-01-18
I concur with everyone else - change that password as soon as possible.  It is never a good idea to share your password with anyone, especially total strangers.

The usual problem with failed logins is that either your username or your password were incorrect (or both, perhaps).  That could happen for any number of reasons.  They're case-sensitive, so 'Password' is NOT the same as 'password'.

Things to look at include, can you log in otherwise?  When you first boot up and have to log into your system, does your username automatically appear in the login screen?  If so, is it *EXACTLY* the same as what you enter when you're trying CTL-ALT-F1?  Same exact spelling, same capitalization, everything?

When using the terminal, you don't get any sort of feedback when entering your password - no text, no '*' symbols, nothing.  You have no way of telling whether you've entered the password correctly.  If you're a clumsy typist, you may hit a wrong key, or hit two accidentally.  Try typing very slowly, ignoring the screen, which won't show you anything anyway.

But remember - whatever you type as your password, capitals, lowercase, whatever - has to be exactly the same as when you created it.  If it's 'xYz123', then it HAS to be 'xYz123'; nothing else will work.
