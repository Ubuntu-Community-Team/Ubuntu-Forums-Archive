### Post by snowpine on 2011-07-03
The simplest solution is to physically disconnect the cable to your "regular" hard drive so that the "target" drive (in the USB enclosure) is the only hard drive. This will prevent accidentally installing to the wrong drive due to user error. :)

### Post by snowpine on 2011-07-03
I'm not familiar with your hardware or the problems you allude to, so I can't really help with that.

The easiest solution is to burn a Live CD and test-drive Ubuntu in "live" mode with no change to your computer. If it runs well and supports all your hardware, then go ahead and install. If not, you have your choice of 1) installing despite the problems then troubleshooting the problems with help from these forums; or 2) trying a different distro.

For older hardware you may have better luck with Xubuntu, Puppy, SliTaz, etc.
