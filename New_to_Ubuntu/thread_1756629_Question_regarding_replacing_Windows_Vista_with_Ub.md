### Post by snowpine on 2011-05-12
Welcome to the forums wflorie!

My recommendation is that you use Ubuntu in "live" mode (running from a CD or USB flash drive) for a while until you've got the hang of it and are sure you want to commit. Your Windows hard drive should show up in the File Manager, so you can browse your documents and copy the ones you want to keep onto an external drive (or burn them to CD, back them up over the network, etc).

Once you're ready to install you can follow the easy instructions at: [http://www.ubuntu.com/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/download)

Now the only problem is that your hard drive is full; this means you will not be able to download the Ubuntu installer (it's around 700mb) until you free up some space. If that's not possible, you can get help from your local Ubuntu users group, buy a Linux magazine with an Ubuntu CD inside, or purchase a Live CD/USB at a low price from one of the reputable online vendors.

### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-05-12
If you had the space to install side-by-side, you could just leave your data on the windows side and access it from Linux.

But since you don't have the space, you will need to replace the OS. The replacing procedure is one of wiping and reinstalling. So no data is left if you choose to replace the OS. The only possiblity you have is to copy it to an external HDD or another computer. 

BTW: you say your C: drive is full, but if you have plenty of space on the D: drive, Ubuntu can install to that too. In fact, Ubuntu will shrink the drive which has the most space left and create a new drive.

But as Snowpine said: first try a live CD or a live USB to see if your computer works with Ubuntu.

Yes, definitely! Although--at the risk of repeating myself--I really recommend trying Ubuntu in "live" mode first and opening your important files to verify whether they work to your satisfaction in Ubuntu. For example if you have Microsoft Office files with complex formatting, they might not appear 100% exactly the same in LibreOffice, that kind of thing. It's worth investigating before you commit to the change.
