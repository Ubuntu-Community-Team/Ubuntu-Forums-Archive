### Post by mro_k on 2009-10-08
Hi! 

I face a somewhat weird problem. I run ubuntu 9.04 with Gnome GUI on my Siemens laptop. In the upper right corner of the upper desktop panel there is a standard icon for volume control. Previously when I clicked on it I got the volume control slider and could adjust the speaker's volume level. But since some time ago when I click on it I get the slider for controlling internal Microphone. In order to control the speakers I have to open the extended volume control panel. (the separate window with many control sliders) This really bugs me, since I want to be able to adjust my speakers without unnecessary clicks.
I have already tried to reset it the way it was through System/Preferences/Sound where it says "select the device to control with the keyboard..." This does not help though. Doesn't matter what I select there I still have a default slider for internal mic. adjustment.

May be anybody knows the way to solve it? :(

---

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-10-08
I believe if you right click on the volume icon and select "preferences" it will open a window that will permit you to select which device you wish to control with the left click on the icon (display slider).

Simply select your choice from the available list (left click and highlight) then close the window. This action will give the main slider (left click on icon) - control of that selection.

Hope this helps
