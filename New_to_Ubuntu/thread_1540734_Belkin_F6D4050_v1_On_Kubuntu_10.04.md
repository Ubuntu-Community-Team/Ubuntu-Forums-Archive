### Post by hhh on 2010-07-28
Boot the Ubuntu Live CD and copy the driver from /lib/firmware to a USB drive (or another external drive). Then boot Kubuntu and transfer the drive to /lib/firmware.

If you're not sure which is the right driver, just transfer all of them.

Also, you can always install KDE onto Ubuntu, see these instructions...
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kde](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kde)
