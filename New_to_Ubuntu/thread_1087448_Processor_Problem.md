### Post by yther on 2009-03-05
How long has it been since you cleaned your fans and such?

If things have been fine until recently, there's a chance you are suffering from heat buildup due to lack of cooling.  You (or a pro, if you're not comfortable taking your laptop apart) might want to give it a cleaning, to get rid of dust, lint, dog hair, and other lovely things that tend to build up inside computers.  Laptops are very susceptible to this because they get used in lots of odd places and there is very little space inside them for air flow.

It could be something else, but that's an idea.  Did you make any changes to operating system, do any updates, right before it started?

### Post by brdmb on 2009-03-05
When you took it all apart, did you get some new thermal grease between your processor and heatsink?  It looks like white grease.  If not, I'd get some, clean off the old stuff, and put it on.  Search for "thermal grease" at your favorite online computer outlet.

A way to test if your processor is actually overheating, if you still have access to Windows, open the calculator and type in "100000000!".  If the computer starts acting really weird and slow (or shuts down) it's definitely a thermal issue.

Good luck!
