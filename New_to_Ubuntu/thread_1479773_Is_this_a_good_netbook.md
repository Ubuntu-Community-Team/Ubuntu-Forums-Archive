### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-05-11
> **mikewhatever said:**
> 500$ vs 400$, how is it much better?

64bit dual core CPU, which is almost unheard of in a netbook, but still an atom CPU.

stock with 2GB of ram, upgradable to 4GB.

250GB HDD stock instead of 160

12.1" screen, putting it in the no-man's land between notebook and netbook.

nVidia ION graphics that will allow you to watch 720p high-def video on the netbook, (because the screen is 720p) or use the standard HDMI connector on the netbook to output up to 1080p to a television as long as you're using playback software with hardware acceleration support.

Also, because of the ION graphics, it can do 'some' 3-d gaming.  You won't be playing the latest and greatest with all the setting turned on max, but you can play.  Nexuiz plays great with moderate setting, although it's unplayable with everything pushed to the max.

I just bought a 1201n last week, and I couldn't be happier.  I had just a few small hoops to jump through to get everything working, and it's just rockin' now.
