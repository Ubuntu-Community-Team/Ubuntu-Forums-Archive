### Post by oyroy on 2009-01-29
Vista Home Premium (or even Basic, if you can get the 64-bit version) is more then enough unless you need domene-access or need to be able to use remote-desktop into your computer (and even that you can fix with a hack on the home-version). You can save some cash on an easier version of Vista, and dual-boot with Ubuntu.
Unless you wanna game right now, I would sugges just buying a cheap-*** GPU like ATI 3450 instead of 3870. If you wanna game in the future, it will be better to buy a new GPU then then spending loads of cash on a medi-core GPU now that will be old in a year or so. ATI 3470 is what I use on my server, and works well enough for desktop and the simplest gaming.

If you wanna overclock, I would suggest buying PC8500 RAM for easier clocking, else just stick with the PC6400. 150Gb Raptor is enough for me. 120GB for Vista, for games and Photoshop, and 30GB for Ubuntu.

My own desktop-setup:
```
Asus P5E
Intel Core 2 Quad Q9450@3,33GHz
Thermalright Ultra 120 Extreme CPU-Cooler with Papst 120mm fan (CPU is at 33-35 degrees, even on load with this baby)
Mushkin PC8500 4GB (2x2GB)
ATI 4870 512MB
Raptor 150GB
MIST 500W
2xSamsung 250GB SATA (Going to fix theese in Raid1 when Ive dumped the stuff only my new server)
Random old IDE JBODs

```
This blasts through everything I got, of games, photoshopping and whatever. If you arent gonna game anything, swap the GPU with a cheap one, and save a couple of hundred bucks. Just make sure you have several Case-fans for cooling. The more fans, the more silent the computer gets (a lot of people have a problem understanding this :o). Personally I prefer Lian Li casings, but they might be a bit expensive if youre on a budget. Also get a good quality and silent powersupply. I use MIST, but thats a norwegian brand. I can heartly reccomend Corsair. The 450W or 650W depending on GPU. They are silent and got 5 year warranty!

I would prob throw the IDE into the old comp and use it for file-backup. Handy for vacation photos and stuff you dont wanna lose, and good for running screen with irrsi for all your 1337-IRC needs! :D

### Post by oyroy on 2009-01-29
The ATI3450 supports full HD (1080p), and might go for $25-40 or something like that, but the 3870 is a good card, and $89 isnt a bad price I guess (at least it isnt in Norway :D). The Zalman 9700NT is good, but not nearly as quiet as the Noctua U12P or C12P. That, or Thermalright Ultra 120 Extreme is what I can reccomend. Just find a few random-brand 120mm case-fans at 15-18dB and it should work well enough.

And, PC8500 RAM is DDR2, but easier to overclock with. A tad more expencive then the PC6400, but worth it if you wanna push your system. All the newer Asus MBs can use it at least. I mostly deal in Asus P5Q-series at work, with Intel 775. Havent clocked much on AMD, but should be able to do some.

I work daily with setting up computers at work, and build plenty of both silent and powerful comps every week, but we might have a narrow view on stuff as were a small private-owned computershop in Norway. I do however waste too much time on speccing computers for myself and my friends :P
Even my server was running a Intel C2D@3,2GHz with watercooling till I got bored of it, and swapped the watercooling into a USB-soundcard. Now its back to 1,8GHz, but its dead silet :)
