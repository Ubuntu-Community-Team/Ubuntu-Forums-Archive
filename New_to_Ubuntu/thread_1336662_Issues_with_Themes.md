That would be handled by the GTK theme. For instance, pick on of the dark themes and it'll change from grey to the dark colour. In Gnome you've got a lot of control over this, go to System > Prefs > Appearance and you can customise individual details and mix and match bits from different themes.

### Post by CaptainMark on 2009-11-25
no you got it exactly right the first time, the default behaviour is gtk controls the inner window, so change the colours by going to system>preferences>appearance then click the customise button and the colours tab. because emerald is taking control of the window border then going to the border tab and changing the settings will do nothing. 
2 ways of customising your settings effectivly
1. select a gtk theme that matches the emerald theme you want to use, some people make the same theme for both gtk and emerald to make them look seamless, 
2. pick your emerald theme with a general idea of what you want your windows to look like and select a basic gtk theme like clearlooks and change the colours to match, 

attached is gtk + emerald themes called slickness black working quite well together
