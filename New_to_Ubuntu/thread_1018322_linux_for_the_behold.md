### Post by Zeedok on 2008-12-22
Not that simple I am afraid.  I assume your are asking how to replace your phone operating system with a version of linux, right?

This is not currently common practice.  There are a couple of devices that run linux (Check out the openMoko project, Nokia's internet tablets with Maemo or Google's Android) and I think Ubuntu mobile edition is being used on some netbooks etc, but unlike PCs/Laptops, there are not a bunch of "phone" linux distributions that you can easy install.  It would be great if there were . . . I guess what I am saying is that you're a superhacker you could probably find a way to boot your device with a linux kernel - but step by step instructions . . .:confused:
