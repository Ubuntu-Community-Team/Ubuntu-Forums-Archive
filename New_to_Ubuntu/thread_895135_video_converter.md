### Post by Mizutsuki on 2008-08-20
Actually ND, it's just a container.  Technically, I suppose it's an MPEG4 Part12 file.  Which is to say it could contain really basically any modern popular video codec (XviD, H.264, etc.)
HL - I highly recommend mplayer and it's frontend gmplayer.  Basically, if MPlayer can't play it, you can be sure it's the fault of DRMs.
Also, if you still can't play the file, I can walk you through using mencoder to convert it (although I suppose if mplayer can't read, mencoder won't be able to either.)
