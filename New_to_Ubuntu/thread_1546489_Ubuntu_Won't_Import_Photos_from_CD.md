### Post by Loren Bliss on 2010-08-06
My greatest thanks to everyone (except Matt), because with your help not only is the problem  solved but I've learned  a much easier, quicker way to download photography that bypasses F-spot (which to me always seemed like nothing more than a rather difficult gatekeeper) and goes directly to Gimp.  

The core problem was of course my own ignorance, but that said, let's see if I can explain what was happening.  

I had already made certain the "always perform" box was unchecked, but it turns out there was an F-spot icon on the bottom toolbar or panel as Ubuntu calls it -- an icon I had noticed but had no idea as to its function -- the presence of which apparently acted as an automatic switch, causing the computer to default to F-spot any time I inserted a CD of photos.  

Probably not surprisingly given my level of unknowing, I discovered this only by accident. After yet another failed download attempt I was shuffling some icons around the panel with the same vacuous  purposelessness a dunce doodles on the margins of his failed mathematics exam and  was quite surprised when the touch of my cursor brought up an F-spot message. 

Once I removed the icon from the panel, jcolyn's instructions worked perfectly. Hence as noted I suppose the icon must have functioned as a kind of open circuit to Fspot that came alive anytime a photo disk was inserted. 

With special gratitude to jcolyn and big thanks also to jtarin, afroman and Jazzy_Jeff as well as erik the blu and mike555.  

Loren Bliss
