### Post by breNTx22 on 2010-09-29
It does seem like it would be a BIOS problem. I don't think it's hardware because the sleep function is controlled by software so that narrows the problem down to OS and BIOS. If you've tried reinstalling/switching the OS (which we have) that points the finger at BIOS. Simply setting the BIOS back to default may not fix it because that feature may be your default setting. If it is in fact BIOS related then we'll have to find out what the name of the setting is that controls this. Or it could be a matter of doing something special to bring it out of sleep. Most computers you just hit the power button and it wakes it but maybe there's an alternative button or key combo.
