### Post by abhiroopb on 2008-05-12
Decided to get rid of amarok because it was slow and would frequently crash. Also I had no need for 90% of its features. I started using banshee as I have heard good things about it. I installed the banshee 0.13 from the repos, however, when I synced it with my iPod it said No Music on my ipod (although the space was taken up). So I thought I would try the newer banshee. And this one is very slow, and seems to hang a lot, also I couldn't figure out how to sync my iPod. Anyway so I reverted back to the old banshee (as it actually worked!) And it said iPod not detected, and so I decided to rebuild the database. After doing that I decided to try syncing the iPod again. And again the same thing happened, it said no music. I went to settings and about (on my iPod) and I saw that about 32gb had been used but the number of Audio was 0 but there was a lot of "other".

With amarok I finally managed to get ipod sync working, but as a music player it was slow and cumbersome! Also playing a song was very very irritating and unintuitive. If you double-clicked on a song it would add to the playlist and then you had to click on it in the playlist, sometimes it would play without the second click. A bit annoying. So far other than the ipod sync (which I only need to do once in a while, as I rarely update my music) banshee is fast, easy to use, and does what I need the most..PLAY MUSIC!
