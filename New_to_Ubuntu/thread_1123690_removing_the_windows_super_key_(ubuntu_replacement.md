### Post by anewguy on 2009-04-12
Well, as an old guy, I can tell you that just prying off a keycap is looking for something to break.  They used to make a tool, somewhat like a tweezer but with some bends on the end, to go around a key and then press in on the release so you could pull the keycap straight up.

That being said, if you get lucky enough to get your old keycap off without breaking it, you could try using some of the decal "paper" they have for hobbiest.  I used to have model trains and printed off my own decals off the computer using this paper.  It's probably available at some hobby stores, but you could also try [http://www.walthers.com/exec/search?quick=blank+decal+paper](http://www.walthers.com/exec/search?quick=blank+decal+paper) and see if they carry it.  With that, you could print off your own Ubuntu key decals (and sell them I would think, as long as you don't violate a copyright).  You can also just use them in a copy machine.  They work as normal model decals - soak in water to release and slide onto the item.  It's best to spray a coat or two of a dull clear coat on afterwards to protect the decal.

Just a thought......

dave :)
