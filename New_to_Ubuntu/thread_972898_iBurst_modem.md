### Post by Mark_in_Hollywood on 2008-11-07
To make your modem work you must learn a little of Linux.  And I must learn the exact make and model and model number or other identifying info on/about your modem. The iBurst site alone isn't much help.

[http://www.iburst.com.au/?deviceofferings=overview1&whatisit=deviceofferings&main=whatisit&appCode=](http://www.iburst.com.au/?deviceofferings=overview1&whatisit=deviceofferings&main=whatisit&appCode=)

On to the top of your screen are:

Applications   Places   System

Click 

Applications

then pull down to Accessories

then Terminal. RIGHT Click and pull down to Add to Panel.

Do the same again for the word: Text Editor.

Once the Terminal icon is in the panel, click it.

You should see a box on the screen with your user name on the first (and only) line.

You will need to cut and paste information into the space following your user name. Do you know how to cut & paste?

If yes, then go here:

[http://kl-mall.com/modules/smartsection/item.php?itemid=14](http://kl-mall.com/modules/smartsection/item.php?itemid=14)

and be sure to read the cross-post:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1794253](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1794253) [COLOR="Red"](this post may work, or it may be out of date, I can't say -- I dont'have your iBurst)[/COLOR]

In the course of learning you will need to use both Terminal & Text Editor. This will seem scary now. It will not be so once we are "into it". Hang in there. It is also possible that the iBurst is already in-built in the kernel. So don't change anything, just yet.
