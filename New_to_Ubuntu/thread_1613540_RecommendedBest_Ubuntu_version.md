### Post by mcduck on 2010-11-04
The first one to try is always the latest version, or latest LTS release, depending on your preference.

In general every single Ubuntu release has improved on speed and performance over the previous ones, but there are always people who have troubles and find some older release to work better on their hardware. No surprise, people who have problems with something are more likely to make lots of noise about it, which is why after every single release the forums (and the Web in general) is filled with people recommending to use the previous one instead. (I'm not kidding, this has been happening since 5.04 :D)

So, my recommendation to ignore such things and try the latest version yourself first. It should give you best features, best performance and most up-to-date software. If you have problems, you can always try older release afterwards. :)

edit: really, when 5.10 was released the forums filled with people complaining about it and recommending every new user to get 5.04 instead. 6 months later came 6.06 and now the previously hated 5.04 suddenly became the best release, and everybody was supposed to use that instead of 6.06. Then came 6.10 and lots of people started making noise how it was unusable and everybody should use 6.06. Continue this to current date. You can be sure that next year when 11.04 is released you'll find plenty of people recommending 10.10 instead. :D

### Post by waynefoutz on 2010-11-04
Hardy goes End of Life in April, so installing that would be a waste of time and effort. They warn you that you'll get no more security updates, but the biggest let down is that they close the repositories so Synaptic no longer works, which means you won't be able to install software in the normal way, you'll have to compile it all yourself. The modern versions, 10.04 and 10.10, should work just fine on that rig. If you want faster performance, then perhaps you should consider one of Ubuntu's side projects, either Xubuntu or Lubuntu, which is meant for older computers.
