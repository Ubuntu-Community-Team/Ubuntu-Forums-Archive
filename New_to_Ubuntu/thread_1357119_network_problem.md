### Post by uniden9 on 2009-12-16
Have you been messing with ubuntu's firewall? My guess is your dhcp lease is expiring and your firewall is no letting in the dhcp responses back through.  The reason it work on a reboot, is the dhcp is able to respond before the firewall is completely inplace. Typically your network is configure/set then the firewall is initialized.

$sudo iptables-save
This will list your firewall rules.
