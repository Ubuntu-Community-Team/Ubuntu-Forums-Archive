### Post by snowpine on 2010-04-23
You do not need the Netbook version for a netbook; Xubuntu will run just as well (maybe even faster, since the desktop is lighter).

Unetbootin is a handy way to make a bootable USB device: [http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net](http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net)

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-04-23
1) Netbook Remix is just a launcher GUI over the top of regular Ubuntu. You can use regular Ubuntu or Xubuntu on your netbook, it's just a regular PC.

2) I always used an external DVD drive, but you can use another Ubuntu machine or a Windows machine and the Unetbootin software to convert the live CD ISO to a live USB. This will allow you to use a USB flash drive to act as the installation media for your netbook. Come to think of it, that's how I got Ubuntu onto my old netbook before I had the external DVD drive.

3) The trackpad might be more sensitive and typing won't be quite as comfortable (it's not bad though). But you'll love the low weight, better battery life and sheer go-anywhere nature of the netbook.

You can usually buy aftermarket big batteries on eBay. I bought a nine-cell battery for my Aspire One which is awesome - I get to use my computer all day on a single charge, and it tilts the keyboard due to its size, allowing more airflow. I'd definitely budget for one of those.
