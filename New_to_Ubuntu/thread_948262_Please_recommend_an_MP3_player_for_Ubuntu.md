### Post by bryncoles on 2008-10-15
i have a creative zen player, and there are two specialist apps for managing creative's hardware on ubuntu. they are: 

gnomad2
kzenexplorer

both work very will (though you might have to also install mpt protocols). all are available in the repos though, so there shouldnt be trouble. it took me longer to figure out kzenexplorer - and its a real 'slap upside the head' moment when you do figure out because its actually quite simple (just drag and drop EVERYTHING and click the 'update' button). 

and you can manage creative media players with amarok too, though it takes a little configuring and you end up with reduced functionality compared with using gnomad, (which has reduced functioanlity compared with kzenexplorer), so i wouldnt use amarok.

so; you could get a creative-brand....?
