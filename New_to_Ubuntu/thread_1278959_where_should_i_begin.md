### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-10-01
Are you running Ubuntu yet, or not?

If you're not running it yet, then your answer is clear: Install it and start using it! Gain some knowledge by exploring the OS and looking at the forums, especially my posts :-)  (because I try to explain how things work, or why things have been designed that particular way, when answering a question).

Once you feel that you're ready, you can start helping other people with their questions and contributing to the forum, or better yet with IRC.

Some people feel that there's one particular area that they can do the most good in. Some people are fluent, interesting speakers, so they might take up Linux advocacy. Some people are good at writing, so they help the documentation effort. Some people know multiple languages and can help translate strings and documentation. Some people are programmers, and some aren't afraid of compiling software and packaging it for the repositories (it's actually easy to compile and package software if you know how to compile and you can use some basic terminal commands).

If you feel yourself being drawn to a particular niche, a way of contributing to Linux, then go for it and don't let anything hold you back! We always need good people with a passion for what they do.
