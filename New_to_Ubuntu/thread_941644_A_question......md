### Post by Thelasko on 2008-10-08
Even if you were running 32-bit Ubuntu, there is [no guarantee]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4716655&postcount=3") those .deb files will work properly.  Ubuntu releases make sure all of the necessary programs bitpim (and other software) depend on are installed.  It doesn't look like the version of bitpim you require will be available until Jaunty Jackalope in April 2009.

If you try to install this newer version of Bitpim you may have a very difficult time.  You may even break your system.  I've been there.  Your two options are to force the architecture on the .deb file, or compile it from source.

Please understand that the delays in software releases are to ensure that you have a stable operating system.  If you need to use "bleeding edge" software, Ubuntu might not be the operating system for you.
