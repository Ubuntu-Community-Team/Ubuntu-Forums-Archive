### Post by XCan on 2009-09-14
Windows auto update and Ubuntu distro upgrade are generally not the same thing. Ubuntu will notify you of security updates, or critical bug patches, this is as close to Windows update you will get. Distro upgrade are bigger, and involves more or less overhaul of the whole system, you may think of it as a service pack if you find that easier. However, it is not a service pack either, as it brings far more changes than a service pack does (usually service packs are just consolidating patches with few real changes to the system).

There are valid reasons to immediately upgrade. That is the case when certain hardware that does not work on your old release is supposed to be supported in the new release.
