To me there is a difference between "sticking" and "not working" so I'm not sure what condition the unit is in. Was the condition caused by spilling a sugary drink on the keyboard? From the nick you have chosen, I'm thinking you are a technical person,  and that's reinforced by the fact that you can dismantle and replace and  still have things work as they did before the dismantle. I would not  suggest this to anyone who wasn't capable of that. If it was caused by spillage, it might be possible to soak and rinse it (out and disconnected from netbook) with clear water and then dry it completely before reassembly. Standard disclaimer applies, I'd only try that as last resort. If the keys have just stopped working from use then maybe the keyswitchs have failed and the best would be to replace the keyboard unit. Those netbooks have been around long enough for parts to be available on eBay so maybe you wouldn't have to order new from ASUS. I think you would find the key remapping solution pretty much of a pain for a vowel, how about a USB keyboard.
