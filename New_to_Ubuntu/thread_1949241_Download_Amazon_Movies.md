### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-03-29
Downloadable movies are almost always encumbered with "digital rights management" (DRM) controls for copyright reasons.  The studios won't allow distributors simply to send you a movie file that you can do with as you like.  

See [this page](http://www.amazon.com/gp/video/ontv/download/ref=atv_sr_download2) for more details.  Only downloads to the Kindle Fire, Tivo, and Windows PCs are supported.  Even Macs are off-limits, never mind Linux.

Maybe if you explain to customer service that you thought you could watch the movie on another device, they might refund your purchase.
