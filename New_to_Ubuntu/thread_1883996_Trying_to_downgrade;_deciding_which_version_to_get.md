### Post by egalvan on 2011-11-21
> **poopnate said:**
> Ok... 11.10 is *not* working out for me. I have an older computer, and everything is slow to the point of not even worth using. I'd like to keep Ubuntu if at all possible; **it was hard enough getting my family to understand *one* Linux OS, **another one may reopen that rift of hell.

Something that many folks ignore, 
is the fact that understanding *any* Operating System is hard **the first time**... Microsoft is no exception.
(understanding *_anything_* the first time can be difficult.
most folks don't remember the work it took)

> As far as I know, my computer has never been upgraded with any hardware; we never really had the money to make it a super-fast computer.

I purchased my first **NEW** computer back in 1980.
A Radio Shack TRS-80.
that was the first *and last* time I ever purchased a **new** computer.
I have **always** scrounged old computer equipment, and make no apologies for the fact that I had no money for new stuff.

> [B]Ever since I installed 11.04 on it, my family was saying it runs a lot faster than Windows XP SP2, which is good. The problem is that 11.10 is running even slower than it did on XP. :(
[/B]

If 11.04 was working fine, then why not return to it?
The only "down-side" is that support for 11.04 will end when 12.10 comes out, in October of 2012, about a year from now.
10.04LTS, which is *very* similiar to 11.04, will be supported until April 2013 (for the gui/desktop stuff), which is six months longer.
But its important to remember that 10.04LTS will still have the core systems supported until April 2015.
your machine will probably be non-functional by then.

> The specs are at [http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/document?docname=c00551508&cc=us&dlc=&lc=en&product=1162597#N59](http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/document?docname=c00551508&cc=us&dlc=&lc=en&product=1162597#N59) as far as I can tell... I honestly have no idea what all's been changed or what it came with, but these look like the original specs...

The specs show

[B]Base processor
Celeron 345 (P) 3.06 GHz
    533 MHz front side bus
    Socket 478[/B]

Actually a nice cpu, except for it being a Celeron, which are not ideal.
Many *modern* monster machines are not running much faster cpu's.
More efficient, quicker cpu, yes, by far, but not *faster*

In my opinion, the bottle-neck is the following:

**Memory Installed	[COLOR="Red"]512 MB (1 x 512)[/COLOR]**

512MB is just not enough RAM to run *efficiently*.
Enough the latest fire-breathing Octo-Core CPU from AMD will choke if it only has 512mb on the mb.


> I guess this is what I get for having a 6 year old computer...


Again, nothing wrong with old equipment.
My collection of equipment runs from the newest: a 3 year-old Dell, running an AMD at 2.4Mhz.. with maxed-out ram at 8GB
to the oldest: HP Mini-PC running a Pentium-3 at 1.2Mhz,
with maxed-out ram at 256MB.

My main lap-top is an old IBM ThinkPad TP60P, running at 1.8Mhz,
with ram maxed out at 4GB. It's running 11.04 just fine.

> Anyway, which version would you recommend I get for such an old computer?

Again, if 11.04 ran fine, why not return to it?
You have a year before support runs out. Take that time to find a 3-year-old  computer, which should run 12.04 just fine.
(except for the fact that many folks are disliking the direction Ubuntu is taking with its desk-top/interfaces, and switching to Mint, but that's another matter for another discussion)
10.04LTS will give you one more year to sort things out.

But in the mean-time, i will give you the same advice I give everyone in this situation...

Find a Mom&Pop-type computer store, and show them the specs on your machine.
Tell them its a school project to "re-purpose" an old machine.
 Ask them to be on the look-out for 2 sticks of 1GB RAM.
(even 2 sticks of 512mb will make your machine MUCH more efficient)
Ask them to be on the look-out for a P-4 cpu to replace the Celeron.
While there, check their stock of "used, old" machines.
you may be surprised at what you find in your price range.

Or find a computer club, and do the same.
many times members are enamored of the "latest-greatest", and will sell their "used, old" machines cheap.

**Even better, find a LUG (Linux User Group)**
That will give you Linux support also.

And one more thing to add...
the only thing I do not like to buy used is HARD DRIVES.
these are mechanical, and too prone to failure.
my data is worth too much to me.
rather have an old cpu and spend the money on new drives.

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-11-21
egalvan makes a number of great points.

However, if you don't want to spend any further money, really, go for [Lubuntu]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Lubuntu"). From the System Requirements:> A Pentium II or Celeron system with 128 MB of RAM is probably a  bottom-line configuration that may yield slow yet usable system with  Lubuntu.Your computer has double the RAM.

Ubuntu will struggle with 512Mb (you were lucky that 11.04 worked OK), whereas Lubuntu will fit comfortably in it. I run Lubuntu on a rather old netbook with a hard drive (not even SSD), and only 512Mb RAM. It runs absolutely fine.
