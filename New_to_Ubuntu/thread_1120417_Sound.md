### Post by iAtari on 2009-04-09
Your MacBook is the polycarbonate one, right? Did you try changing around your sound drivers in the sounds pane? (I think you can do that, not on ubuntu right now) I know on my MacBook 3,1 the sound goes louder than what OS X allows-and under linux it doesn't find the third speaker for midtones.

Try here, the original fix for sounds on the 2,1 MacBook:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBook2-1/Hardy](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBook2-1/Hardy)

There's also  a spot about 4,1 MacBooks having weak sound, which would describe your problem:

1. install the package gnome-alsamixer 2. Run Applications -> Sound & Video -> GNOME Alsa Mixer 3. Uncheck the mute box for "surround"
