### Post by martrn on 2009-03-13
Backports start of at the beginning with nothing in them.  As the distribution gets further away from the release date any newer releases of software since the release data, that don't include any security updates or that are not to fix any know bugs (eg. feature releases), get added to back-ports.  For example there is a newer version of the gimp in back-ports. (possiable with a couple of extra features). At the end of a release, just before ubuntu don't support the release any longer, backports will be full of newer stuff.

You can view the whole of intrepid backports here:
[http://packages.ubuntu.com/intrepid-backports/allpackages]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/intrepid-backports/allpackages").

The community documents on back-ports.
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuBackports]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuBackports").

I (personally) always enable back-ports.
