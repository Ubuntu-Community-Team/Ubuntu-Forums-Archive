### Post by simonmoon42 on 2010-08-13
You could always mic up the old ax and run it through the line in. Or buy a cheap hand held digital recording device... Record to that and transfer the files over. I have [_this one_]("http://www.google.com/products/catalog?hl=en&q=philips+digital+voice+recorder&um=1&ie=UTF-8&cid=6066184789723531652&ei=6sxlTLakMoSWsgPLzqDxDQ&sa=X&oi=product_catalog_result&ct=image&resnum=2&ved=0CDsQ8gIwAQ#") and the sound quality is surprisingly good. It is also instantly recognized by 10.04 and easy to manage the files. If money is no object you could always buy a full blown interface. ALSA has a project for compatible sound cards and audio interfaces [_here_]("http://bugtrack.alsa-project.org/main/index.php/Matrix:Main").

It really depends on the quality you're looking for and whether or not you want to buy equipment or use household objects McGuyver style.

Sorry for the random suggestions, but you said anything *could* be helpful.
