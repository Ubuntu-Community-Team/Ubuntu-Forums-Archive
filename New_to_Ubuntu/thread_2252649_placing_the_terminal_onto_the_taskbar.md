### Post by vasa1 on 2014-11-13
I think I may know what OP wants. When a default installation of Lubuntu runs, the lxpanel is at the bottom of the screen, full-width. From memory, and from left to right, there's the Lubuntu bird icon which provides the menu tree and then, IIRC, are icons for the file manager and internet browser (and a desktop switcher?). I think OP also wants a terminal icon there.

Try this:
[LIST]
[*]Right-click on some free space in the relevant lxpanel.
[*]Click on Panel Settings
[*]Click on Panel Applets
[*]Look for "Application Launch and Task Bar". It should be present in a default lxpanel.
[*]Single-click to highlight it.
[*]Click on Preferences (on the right).
[*]In the new window, Application Launch and Task Bar, click on the Launchbar tab.
[*]Then click in the right-hand column under whichever category the application you want may be present. In my system, I find LXTerminal in "System Tools".
[*]Single-click to select LXTerminal.
[*]Go to the middle-column and click "Add". LXTerminal should appear in the column on the left and immediately in the task bar at the bottom of your screen.
[*]Close all lxpanel windows.
[/LIST]
