### Post by DennyF on 2011-02-22
My old weary Pentium 4 w/512 mg RAM has risen from the dead, thanks to Ubuntu/Linux! I'm really impressed with the boot, load and execution speed since I installed Ubuntu Meerkat. The real test, though, came when I sat my significant other down to it and asked her to give it a whirl. At least 2 weeks went by before she asked, "How do I do ... ?" Under Win XP, she was always complaining about the load time, both program and web pages, and could never figure out how to get things done. Now, both she and i spend more time doing stuff on our two machines than we do trying to figure out "how to do things," as was the case under Windows. I can't believe I've personally waded through Windows since Ver. 1.0 up to Win 7! Each incarnation, although more glamorous than the previous, just added more and more overhead and "how do I do this?" headaches. At least Ubuntu's functionality/control is but a click or two away and not 7 or 8 layers deep in some obscure menu or control panel item.
Hats off to the entire Linux/Ubuntu community for making computers do what they should do -- work the way I want them to work and not the way some MegaDeth software/hardware giant thinks they should work -- as a means to sell more complicated software and high end machines to run it.
Really, I feel indebted to this community for freeing me from that paradigm. It gives a whole new meaning to the term "free" software, as the developers intended. Thanks again.
