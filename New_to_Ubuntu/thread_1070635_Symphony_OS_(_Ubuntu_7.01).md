GNOME, KDE, and Xfce are the three main Desktop Environments. GNOME is the Ubuntu default, KDE is the Kubuntu default, and Xfce is the Xubuntu default. Xfce is classically more "lightweight" than GNOME or KDE, KDE is classically the "heavyweight", and GNOME falls somewhere in the middle. Mezzo is based on FVWM, which is a window manager as opposed to a complete desktop environment. As such, it is even more lightweight than Xfce, though its use is often more difficult than the major Desktop Environments.

HP, as you mentioned, will be using a modified Ubuntu Netbook Remix, which is largely based on GNOME as far as I know or can estimate.

Ubuntu and its derivatives are based on Debian GNU/Linux, as you said. The differences between Debian and Ubuntu are, from a user's perspective, basically in regard to inclusion of "non-free" software/firmware in the default install - this helps with Ubuntu's default hardware support in comparison do Debian's.
