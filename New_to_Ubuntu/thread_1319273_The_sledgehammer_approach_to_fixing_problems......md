### Post by AlanInVancouverBC on 2009-11-08
I have 2 hard drives. I use Ubuntu X.XX on one and test other OSs on the other (Kubuntu, Mint, etc.).
So I was having problems with upgrading 9.04 to 9.10 on the main drive--couldn't get a CD to burn without errors. That's happening a lot here. So after about 10 disks (I don't learn quickly.), I had the bright idea (Well, I think it was bright!) to ditch Kubuntu (the other drive) and install (not upgrade) Ubuntu 9.10 over it. But I don't have a good CD!. What I DO have is a good 8.10 CD....
So I installed 8.10 over Kubuntu. Then I immediately installed 9.04 (a good CD) over that. Then I can get the "upgrade" screen for 9.10....
So now I have a fresh Install of 9.10 on my second HD. 
Great!
Now I can try to burn a new CD...
No. Still no good.
So I have an okay upgrade on my first HD, of 9.10, and a clean, strong, no-problems upgrade 9.10 on my second HD.
I just sent notice to Ubuntu to mail me a CD...
But now, I may not need it.....
I'm not the brightest bulb with the tekkier aspects of Ubuntu (20+ years with MS). I can't even get Terminal to change to the desktop directory!
Thanks for reading.....
Alan In Vancouver BC, Canada. Frustrated but satisfied.

Go beg, steal or borrow a USB stick if your system can boot from USB. They're pretty cool for when u have cd issues.
for burning an iso to cd

1) check the md5   ----> [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM)
2) burn CD at about 4X speed
3) check the cd ---> [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM)

You may want to pop in a cd drive cleaner into your drive - data cd's have a much lower tollerence of errors than a music one.

If you want some instructions for using a USB device to hold a copy of ubuntu on, these guys are pretty good ---->  [http://www.pendrivelinux.com/](http://www.pendrivelinux.com/)
