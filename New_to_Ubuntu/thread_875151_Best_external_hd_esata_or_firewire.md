### Post by maxino on 2008-07-30
Hi everybody,

What would you choose: esata or firewire for an external hard disk?

My current USB2.0 external hd has always been giving me a lot of grief (random slowdown, inexplicable lockups, a few times refused automounting for days...etc) and I am looking forward to trash it and get a better performing one.

In both cases I would need a controller card (esata or firewire) since my ancient motherboard has none.

Thanks,

### Post by ElephantGun on 2008-07-30
I would pick esata for a number of reasons. It doesn't need any adapters (to my knowledge) if you're using a SATA drive, it's faster than firewire, and if you're buying a controller card there's always the option of RAID, if the card you're buying supports it.

---

### Post by neurostu on 2008-07-30
2nd on the esata.

Both firewire and esata are supported in the latest linux kernel.

I currently use firewire for my external HDD, but only b/c my Mobo doesn't have esata.  I got the firewire drive mounted and recognized without any problems.
