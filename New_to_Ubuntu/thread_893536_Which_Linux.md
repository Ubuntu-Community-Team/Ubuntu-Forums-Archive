### Post by Too Late on 2008-08-18
I can't recommend DSL for CRT guys because [it can't handle](http://damnsmalllinux.org/cgi-bin/forums/ikonboard.cgi?act=ST;f=10;t=10374;st=0) refresh rates above 60 Hz. Other than that it could be good.

I've had Puppy and VectorLinux on my 8-year old secondary machine, which has P3 750 MHz and 128 MB. Another distro you could think about is Feather Linux.

However, amount of RAM is the most important thing in your computer. For example, the newest version of Arch Linux won't even install on a computer having only 128 MB (and your computer probably have less).
