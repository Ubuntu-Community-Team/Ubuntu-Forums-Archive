### Post by H3l0 on 2009-10-27
I think the actual connection from the power receptacle on the back of the laptop to the board is broken.  Is it loose when you plug it into the back of the laptop?   This isn't a definite sign it is broken but a pretty good indicator (it can also still be broken even though it is not loose.)  Not all is lost however.  If you know someone who is very very very skilled with a soldering iron it can be soldered back onto the board.  Do not be surprised though if the solder doesn't last for more than a few months though.

Hope that helps!

### Post by H3l0 on 2009-10-28
You would be in luck if you found a computer repair shop that would offer good quality soldering.   If you know anyone who does HAM radio as a hobby then those guys are pretty handy with a soldering iron,  might try a tv repair shop as well.  You are correct about going to HP to have them replace it.  MUCH too expensive.  If it came down to it I would look on Ebay for a replacement Mobo.  There are plenty of third party retailers out there with a good supply of older parts for lappy's.  

Primarily this is more likely to happen when you are moving the laptop around while it is plugged in.  Ive even seen people wrap thier power cord around thier closed laptop for easy transportation but as you can see it has a high price in the end.  Sometimes it just happens, whether the particular solder on your board was not as strong as it should have been, or just general wear and tear.

Ive never actually seen something like this occur in a desktop system before.  Power supplys in DT usually just go bad due to a cheap capacitor or being jostled around alot in shipping.  That is the good thing about a DT is that when one part goes out you are more likely able to replace the one part without having to change out the entire system.  

Again try to see if you or someone you know, knows someone who does HAM radio as a hobby or visit a tv repair shop.  You could also go to your local radio shack and see if they would know someone who is a regular that buys capacitors and resistors that might be willing to help for a small price.  Thats what I would do.
