hey there! welcome to the fold! first of all, congratulations on changing over to ubuntu. it's an amazing OS and you'll get a lot of use out of it and it'll be fun for you to use, for sure. 

i read your post, which is quoted above my reply here, and you say you haven't been able to figure out how to do a screenshot. first things first. take a look at your keyboard and try to find the key that has "prntscr" on it. that's a shortened version of "print screen." that button lets you take a snapshot of your screen with whatever you want on the screen and turns it into a file, such as a .jpg or .png, or whatever your laptop is setup to save it as. ok? if you find that, press it once and, maybe, just maybe, the screen will look like it flickers off and then back on, much like a camera shutter. ok? then it should have a window open with the picture and give you the ability to save the picture. now, most of the time it saves the screenshots in the pictures folder of your OS. so, once you save it, you should be able to go to your pictures folder and find it there. one key piece of advice to give you is to remember the file name that the screenshot is given and you can even go to the search bar of ubuntu and type in the filename and find it that way and then you can upload it here. sound good? reply back with your progress and we'll help you along some more.
