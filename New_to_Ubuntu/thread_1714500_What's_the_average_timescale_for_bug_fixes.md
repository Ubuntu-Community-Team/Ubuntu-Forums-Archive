### Post by stoogiebuncho on 2011-03-25
This can vary wildly depending on the priority given to the bug, the number of people that report that it affects them, and simply finding someone willing to do the work of fixing it.

It sounds like you've found the bug report already.  Is anyone assigned to the problem yet?  Is it marked as a critical bug? (I'm guessing probably not.)

I'm no expert, but I would guess adding support for additional hardware would mean it would be a change to the kernel, and I would expect the changes to be rolled out in a new version of Ubuntu, rather than being fixed by updates in your current version.  But that's a guess.

Sometimes people offer a $ reward to the developer who fixes a bug that is affecting them.  If this is a big deal to you, you might think about that - it might speed up the process a little.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-03-25
Just a hint that Sony laptops have some of the most proprietary, and thus poorly supported, hardware when it comes to running Linux.  I'm not anti-Sony (I have a PS3 and Bravia TV, for instance. and don't rant about "rootkits"), but it does design products that are just sufficiently incompatible with standards to create competitive lock-in.  The "[memory stick]("http://www.fastcompany.com/blog/kit-eaton/technomix/memorystick-micro-die-sonys-older-proprietary-standards")" for devices like the PSP is a good example.
