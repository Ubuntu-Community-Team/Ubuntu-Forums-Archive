### Post by mvalviar on 2009-03-24
They are the same. Except that Ctrl-Alt-BkSpc is handy when something messed up your GUI and you can't log out normally.

---

### Post by kpatz on 2009-03-24
Logout/login is a cleaner way to do it, as it gives running programs a chance to shut down gracefully (saving data, etc.)  Ctrl-Alt-Bksp is the emergency "restart X" command, useful if X freezes up or something, but any running apps in your X session will be killed.
