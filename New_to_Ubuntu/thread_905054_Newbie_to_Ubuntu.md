### Post by gmoney on 2008-08-30
I would have to agree with Sef.  There's no such thing as a question that's too simple.  While searching forums (and google) is always a good rule of thumb, when you just starting out with Linux (Ubuntu), it's kinda hard to know where to look for the right information.  I'm sure the aforementioned guide will help answer a lot of your questions.

Here's a few questions that you may want to think about before you dive into the installation process:

Is this your only computer?  If it's an old computer that's not used a whole lot (and you have a new computer running Windows), you may want to just use the older computer for Linux.  While setting it up to dual boot with Windows and Linux isn't terribly tricky, it's nice to just put the installation disk in, click "Next, Next, Next", and be able to have a Linux installation.  On the other hand, if this is your primary computer, I recommend doing a dual boot installation.  The few extra steps in setting up a dual boot may come in handy over the next few days, if (for some reason) you decide Linux just isn't for you.

As far as installation of Ubuntu goes, it really is easy, and I encourage you to do it.  If I could add one more bit of advice, a lot of new people have trouble burning the .iso file.  Here's a good program for doing that on Windows:

[http://isoburn.sourceforge.net/](http://isoburn.sourceforge.net/)

Good luck on installing Ubuntu.  Welcome to the community, and feel free to ask for help.
