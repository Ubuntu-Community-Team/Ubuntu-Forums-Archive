### Post by sideaway on 2009-09-20
Well basically, the motherboard is simple. buy either intel or amd chipsets, spend what you need on what functions the boards come with, say you need 6 sata ports, buy a board with 6 sata ports. a p4* chipset or 7*0 series should do you fine. ATX or mATX is fine, dependsing on your expansion needs. Note AMD chipsets work for AMD CPUs and Intels for Intels, pretty simple.


Before you pick a motherboard however, you need to pick a CPU, for gaming, dual cores still give you the biggest bang for buck, unless you're doing multitasking (gaming isn't) dualcores are the way to go for your wallet. e8*00 series are decent chips or the latest Athlon X2 series punch a lot higher for the cost.

Anyone on a tight budget, go the AMDs, they're just so damn cheap for their performance.

Noone can give you a fool-future-proofed computer, there's just no knowing what's gonna happen in the next 24 months. Buy for what you need now with maybe a few extra sata slots and maybe another pci-e slot. Although that said, there are reports of AMD pumping money/research/TLC into the latest am3 chipset, so it could be here to stay for a while yet, so room to upgrade that CPU later if need be :)
