Welcome to the Ubuntu forum!

If you are trying to boot a CD, you need to change your BIOS option to make the CD the first boot option. Usually, your BIOS will have a key that you can press while the BIOS is on the screen that lets you select the CDRom to boot first.

On quite a few, it's either the F8 or F12 key, but your may differ. Just watch the screen closely while it's booting and you should see what key to press. If you miss the opportunity to press it, just reboot and press it the next time.

In some older PCs you will need to change the BIOS  settings from in the BIOS itself. If that's the case, the BIOS boot screen will tell you what key to press.

Of course, if you have your PC's manual handy, you can just look it up.
