### Post by Frogs Hair on 2011-04-17
Here are some.

1. Docks have multiple themes and icon options available . 
2. The weather and clock applets are much nicer on my dock
3. The user can create & save themes
4. It is possible add multiple docks and move them to different positions on all sides of the screen

If you want a simple place to launch applications from , use a panel. If you like to tweak and decorate use a dock. The bottom is , they both launch applications.

### Post by mcduck on 2011-04-18
> **Longstreet said:**
> So then a Dock is like a Panel, or Task Bar, taken to the next level? More capable, more customizable, more versatile? That makes sense then. Thanks!

Not really. Panels are usually very customizable as well.

Mainly the difference comes from the intended purpose, docks are mainly taskbars/launchers (sometimes with support for other types of applets as well), while panels simply serve as hosts for any type of panel applets.

The line between the two is rather blurry, if one really exists at all. (it wouldn't be far from the truth to make difference between the two based on the size of the icons in them, really. Applications calling themselves as panels tend to have smaller icons and often text-based applets as well, while docks go for larger icon-based applets. Most programs support both in the end.)

Or you could make the difference by saying that a panel extends for the whole width (or height) of the screen, while dock is sized to fit it's contents. Although pretty much any panel can do that as well, and most docs can be expanded.  :D

(So in the end I'd say my answer is that there is no meaningful difference between the two, unless you compare most barebones implementations of each type of widget. In that case a panel would an all-purpose tool, while a dock would be a combined application launcher and tasklist.)
