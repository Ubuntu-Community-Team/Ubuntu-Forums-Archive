> **steigerjb said:**
> they say you need 2.5ghz I just don't know how well it runs at that much.

Pure Ghz is a bad way to compare performance. Eg- a Core2Quad 6600 (2.4Ghz) will chew up, and spit out ANY P4- even the last 3.8Ghz models. Even a Core2Duo 6600 (2.4Ghz again) will do the same. 

When programs say '2.5Ghz required' they are talking about the lowest, slowest 2.5Ghz they can think of- normally a celeron. All of the i5/i7 laptops will be faster than a 2.5Ghz celeron, even if the clock speed in under 2Ghz.
