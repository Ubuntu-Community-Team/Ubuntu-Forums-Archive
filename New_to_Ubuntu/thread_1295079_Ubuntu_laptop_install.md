### Post by Paqman on 2009-10-19
Your spec is good enough to run Ubuntu, but watch out for wifi cards. Some require a bit of massage to get working well under Linux. Luckily replacing them with a compatible model is genrally very easy.

Can you find out exactly what kind of wifi card it has in it? There's often a sticker on the bottom with details.

### Post by mivo on 2009-10-19
I would recommend Xubuntu instead. I had tried Ubuntu on a similar laptop a year ago (little faster CPU), and it was running slowly and felt sluggish. Ubuntu isn't really all that slim out of the box. Xubuntu will serve you better and almost all the Ubuntu advice offered on these boards applies just fine. :) Xubuntu isn't super light-weight either, but for your machine it strikes a really nice balance of performance and comfort out of the box while allowing you to learn at the pace you are comfortable with.
