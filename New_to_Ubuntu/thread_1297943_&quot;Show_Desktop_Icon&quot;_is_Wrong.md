### Post by mrboojive on 2009-10-22
You can try changing the icon theme and then changing it back. Go to System > Preferences > Appearance. Then click "Customize," then click the tab for "Icons" and try selecting a different theme. Then select your original theme again. That will refresh all of the icons on your system.

But are you sure that what you have on your panel is the Show Desktop widget? If you click on it, does it show the desktop? Maybe you accidentally added a different widget. Try right clicking on it and selecting "Remove from Panel." Then right click on the panel, select "Add to Panel" and add the Show Desktop widget.

### Post by Qboy61 on 2009-10-22
The fix you suggested worked.  I was using icons from the "Crystal SVG" theme.  I selected "GNOME" and the icon is correct.  I confirmed your other question by right-clicking and selecting "about" which shows details about which widget your clicking on.  

This icon may have been damaged before I deleted the the panel and I did not realize it until now.
