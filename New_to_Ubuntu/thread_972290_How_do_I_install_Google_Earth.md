### Post by Pro-reason on 2008-11-05
Forget all that.  Don't download random files in your browser.

Open Synaptic from the menu, and use it to install stuff.  (Search for software, double-click to choose it, then “Apply”).

When you have become more acquainted with the system, try the more complicated stuff.

Agree with this.  Confine your installs to the items in add/remove and in the synaptic package manager (read up on "how to enable repositories".  you will need to do that first)

Installing and loading from sites other than repositories is not a good idea for a noob .. not yet!  Some of the non repository stuff can and sometimes will create more problems than you can handle.  And some of them will require the use of terminal and the use of command line.  Something it takes a bit getting used to in order to accomplish what you need.

Google Earth is in your Synaptic installer and no need to download anything. (as a matter of fact, everything you need to get started is there!)
