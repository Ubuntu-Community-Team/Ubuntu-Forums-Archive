### Post by berksted on 2009-10-29
Go to System > Administration > Software Sources > Updates

Change the 'Relesase Upgrade' from 'Long Term Support' to 'Normal Releases'

Now you should be able to upgrade to 9.10 from Update Manager.

### Post by random turnip on 2009-10-29
> **aszxcv said:**
> Maybe a dumb question 

how  exactly do you do a clean install?

Back everything up first , on an external drive preferably.
Then go to the [official Ubuntu site]("http://www.ubuntu.com/") and click "get Ubuntu", this should download the iso image.  When that's finished downloading burn in the image to a disk (if you have windows use nero or whatever, there are other you can use if you're using Ubuntu), put the disk in you computer and reboot into it.  You should have the option to "try ubuntu with no changes" or just install.  I advise that you try before installing over your old Ubuntu, you don't want to find out something doesn't work with your computer and not have your old one to go back to.
