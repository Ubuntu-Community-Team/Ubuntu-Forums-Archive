### Post by dondiego2 on 2010-10-07
For VLC:

Click System on top.
Go to Administration and click Synaptic Package Manager
Type in your computer password if it asks.
Click the Reload icon near the top left to get the latest packages.
Type vlc in the search box.
Click the box to the left of vlc  (it should be the first one in the list).
Click the apply icon.

When done, go to Applications, Sound and Video and you should see VLC Media Player among your selections.

### Post by madmax75 on 2010-10-08
> **Legeril said:**
> 
I am really looking for beginner advice, anything I should take note of and/or any apps that deserve 'must have' status

Ok, here's my favourite apps list... ;)

**Screenshots:** Shutter
**Font managers:** Fontmatrix, Fonty Python
**Image/photo editing:** Gimp (what else do you need, really? :))
**Image viewers:** eog (Eye Of Gnome, the default viewer, very good in what it does)
**Web browser:** Firefox, although Chrome/Chromium is not bad.
**Firewall utility: **Firestarter (a graphical front-end to your firewall)
**Torrents:** Transmission
**Cleaning up old crud from the system:** BleachBit (use with caution!)
**Tweaking your Ubuntu installation:** Ubuntu Tweak (you can change the location of the Minimize-Maximize-Close buttons from left to right with this, and other things)
**Planetarium software:** Celestia, Stellarium, KStars (if you're into astronomy - Stellarium and Celestia are just cool programs anyways)
**Games:** FreeCiv (a Civilization clone), Billard-GL (3D billiards), KBreakout, Galaga, Quadrapassel (Tetris clone), Slingshot, Wormux, Sauerbraten (FPS)
**Office:** OpenOffice is great, but I don't intend to use it any more (Oracle etc.), so I installed Gnome Office instead (gives you Abiword and Gnumeric, which are great programs in themselves).
**Audio playback:** Audacious (a WinAmp-like little player, accepts XMMS skins), SoundConverter (transcode sound formats), SoundJuicer (rip your audio cd's)
**Video playback:** Totem (the default player), VLC (this I use for DVD's now, I used to have Kaffeine for that, but VLC handles the DVD menus better IMHO), Miro Internet TV
**Burning CD's/DVD's:** Xfburn

**Others:**

Google Earth, Unetbootin (create bootable USB distros), AptOnCD, WinFF + ffmpeg, GParted, Palimpsest (check the health of your hard disk), hardinfo (information of your computer system, like SiSoft Sandra in Windows)...

EDIT:

**Podcasts:** GPodder (great if you like listening to podcasts!)
**Antivirus:** clamav + clamtk (graphical front-end), BitDefender Antivirus Scanner for Unices (this you have to dig up from the net, you will have to provide your email address to get the free license key which lasts for a year; you can get a new free key after the old key expires)
**PDF viewing:** Evince (the default PDF viewer - it's way more faster than Adobe Acrobat, although you can get that one too)

### Post by oldos2er on 2010-10-08
Gparted is run during installation, at which point you'll be able to use the entire disk, install side-by-side with Windows (this option depends on how much free space exists on your disk; you may not see it), and manual, which allows you to create any partitions you want or need. The first and third options are obviously destructive, so backup any sensitive data first.

Much more detailed info here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/installation-guide/i386/index.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/installation-guide/i386/index.html)
