### Post by ubudog on 2010-08-14
Start the live cd, and when you get to the Partioning part of the install, select "Choose partions manually" or something like that (probably the last option).  Then select the partition that Ubuntu is currently on.  Select the "Format" option next to it.  This should reinstall.  Make sure you get the right partition, or you could really mess stuff up.  If you have any questions just ask.  Ubuntu should work fine with the usb dongle, at least in my experience.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-08-14
If you have not got rid of the netbook version yet from your mini, you have no need to do so at all.

Just logout from the desktop, and then at the login screen, hit your username but do not enter your password, go to the bottom right Session menu and choose Gnome, instead of the netbook, then login with password, and you will get a standard gnome desktop from then on.   I have also got rid of the top panel and put everything on the bottom one, using the main-menu instead of menu-bar to save space on the small screen.

You may even find that the Linux Mint 9, based on ubuntu 10.04, will fit you better; it even has all the codecs and flash etc etc when you install so may save some downloading.  Some people insist it is faster than ubuntu, but I have never seen that advantage on my machines.
