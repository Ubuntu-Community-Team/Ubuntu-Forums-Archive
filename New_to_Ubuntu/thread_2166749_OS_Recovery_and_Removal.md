### Post by DuckHook on 2013-08-10
You are a little bit caught between a rock and a hard place. Here are my observations:

1. You will have to locate a DVD of XP to reinstall it. It is not permissible for the people on this forum to advise you on outright illegal methods, but if you have a friend who has a copy, you can reinstall it using your product key, which would be legal.

2. Be aware that XP will no longer be supported in mere months--April 2014, I believe. Thereafter, you will be at the mercy of whatever holes will be discovered in XP from that point onwards. Speaking only for myself, I am not prepared to take that chance with MS OSes.

3. Ubuntu 13.04 is not faulty. Rather, you are trying to take flight with a horse and buggy and this just won't work. Remember, XP is 12 years old and was designed for the equipment of that time. Ubuntu 13.04 is designed for the equipment of today. 12 years in the computer world is equivalent to, say, 120 years in human terms?

4. Your equipment is too limited to run Ubuntu, but Lubuntu or another lightweight distro like CrunchBang or Puppy Linux might be okay.
