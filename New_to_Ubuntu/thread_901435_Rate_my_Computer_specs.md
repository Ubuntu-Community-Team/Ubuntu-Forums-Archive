### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-26
Your specs are fine to run Ubuntu efficiently and quickly. What is important is what you want to use your PC for, as the rig you describe for example would not be considered a good gaming rig for example ( too low ram and onboard graphics is never a game rig graphics solution ) Good news is games kinda are not why anyone would want to use linux since you either have to use Wine or one of the very few native linux games to play one ( I say very few meaning top shelf first person shooters, as in there is no crysis in Linux and we can all thank MS for their wonderful DirectX technology for that ). But say as a home computer to download stuff and watch videos I see no problem with your specs. If you want compiz fusion 3D effects and such you will probably have to invest in an Nvidia or ATI graphics card to get them to work however they can be had for under $50 US if getting the latest greatest one isn't the concern.
