### Post by Temposs on 2009-04-10
For Ubuntu 8.10, you are up to date. The version you have is the most stable version available for Ubuntu 8.10, and it is the version Ubuntu recommends for you to use. It is not just the stock Open Office that you have, but a version has been modified by Ubuntu developers to make it work very well on Ubuntu 8.10. 

I recommend that you wait for Ubuntu 9.04 to come out, which comes with Open Office 3. If you want Open Office 3 just because it's the most up to date version, I would just leave it be. I've seen that some people have problems with it working on Ubuntu 8.10.

If you really really want Open Office 3 on Ubuntu 8.10 for a particular feature or whatever reason, go to the Open Office website here: [http://download.openoffice.org/other.html](http://download.openoffice.org/other.html)

Download your favorite Linux deb file. The install process should be very easy.

### Post by Jimmyfj on 2009-04-11
You could go to [www.getdeb.net](www.getdeb.net) and download Ubuntutweak from there. You find it under the Categories>System tools section. Install it and go to the applications tab. Click Unlock and type in your root password. Then scroll down the list of t 3'rd parties software and there you'll find OpenOffice.org 3.0. Highlight it and click Update. You now have the right path for upgrading your OOo to the latest version through your Update.Manager.
