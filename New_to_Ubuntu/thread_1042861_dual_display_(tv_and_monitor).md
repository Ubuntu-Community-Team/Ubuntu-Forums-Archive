### Post by Ben Page on 2009-01-18
Paste this code into your screen section:

Section "Screen"
    ...
    [B]Option         "TwinView"
    Option         "TVOutFormat" "SVIDEO"
    Option         "TVStandard" "NTSC-M"
    Option         "MetaModes" "1280x1024,1024x768; 1280x1024,NULL; 1024x768,NULL; 800x600,NULL; 640x480,NULL"[/B]

note, don't paste section screen and those three dots, paste only bolded text.
You can edit your tv out standard just by retyping ntsc to pal-g, and correct your monitor resolutions.

### Post by diablo75 on 2009-01-18
Here's what you need to do to fix your video:

Since you're in low graphics mode, that means you're able to get to your applications menu.  Click Applications>Accessories>Terminal.

Type in:

sudo dpkg-reconfigure xorg-xserver

Go through all of the questions.  If you see a question about using the kernel as a video buffer, answer NO.  Then restart.  That should bring you back up to speed.

As far as the s-video to TV thing, you shouldn't have bothered with editing your xorg file.

Instead, type in terminal:

sudo apt-get install nvidia-settings

Just to make sure nvidia settings is installed.

Then in terminal type:

sudo nvidia-settings

A gui control panel will appear allowing you to adjust all your video card settings.  You want to put your TV feed into TwinView mode, and then set the resolution.  You'll likely also want to tell it to be a clone of your monitor... but putting it to the right/left/above/below is up to you.  Just fiddle around and hit apply to preview.  If it doesn't work, a 20 second countdown will revert to the previous settings (like when you changed resolution in windows).

Tip:  Add a shortcut launcher to your top panel and tell it to run nvidia-settings when clicked on.

Note:  When you run sudo nvidia-settings, you'll be able to save your changes to your xorg.  If you don't run as sudo, your changes will revert back to what they were upon next reboot.
