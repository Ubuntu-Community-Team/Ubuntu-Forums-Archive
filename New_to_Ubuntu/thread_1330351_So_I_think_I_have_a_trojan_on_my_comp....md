### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-11-18
Since you're running Linux on your machine, it's highly unlikely that your machine is infected in any way.

You mention having your emails hacked -- well, those are stored on mail servers along with your mail accounts, and it's possible that anything could have happened on those servers -- but again, if these are well-maintained servers, it's unlikely that happened, either.

The  most likely candidate (IMHO) is that when you were using a campus computer running MS Windows to check your mail, something you opened allowed the campus computer to become infected and that propagated to your email account there.

I know you're going to say you didn't click on anything to do this, but infections can be hidden inside picture files attached to emails.  Simply opening the email, or allowing MS Outlook to operate in Preview mode, is sufficient to grant that infection permission to install itself on the MS Windows box.
