Greetings
I'm a long time Microsoft user, who has recently fallen in love with Ubuntu a couple of days ago.
I tried Ubuntu v5 years ago, but quickly abandoned it due to having to type command lines instead of the lazy clicking policy. But now Ubuntu 10.10 is a different story. What happened? and when did it start to be as easy to use like that?

### Post by wyliecoyoteuk on 2010-09-24
9.10 showed the first hints of it, but for me, 10.4 was when I adopted it widely.
I moved my Suse PCs at home and work to 10.4.
I now run at home, Mythbuntu, Netbook and desktop editions (all 10.4).
At work, desktop and server editions. (10.4 again)
Contrary to some grumbles from other users, I have had absolutely zero issues with it, and I have so far installed it on 14 PCs, most of which have been running it since May.

I will wait before adopting 10.10, but at work we may remain on 10.4 , as it is an LTS edition, unless there are compelling reasons to move.
