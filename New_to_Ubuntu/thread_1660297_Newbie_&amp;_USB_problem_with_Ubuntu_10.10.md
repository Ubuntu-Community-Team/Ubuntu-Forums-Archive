### Post by mike1094 on 2011-01-05
Hope someone can help this Ubuntu Linux newbie to solve a frustrating problem please?

I have a Shuttle Barebone XPC SS51G with WinXP SP3 installed on the first hard disk and Ubuntu 10.10 on the second hard disk (from a downloaded .iso burned to CD).

Initially USB flash drives (& external USB HDDs) used to automount & place an icon on the desktop, but this has stopped since I ran the Update Manager.  I can't access USB removable media anymore!

There is an entry in Device Manager for the USB flash drive which seems to identify it OK, but the "USB Version" & "Connected at" fields are full of commas.

I thought reinstalling Ubuntu from scratch might help, but exactly the same problem happened.

Thanks in advance!

### Post by mike1094 on 2011-01-08
My PC had a Netgear WG311v3 wireless adaptor installed with ndiswrapper & WinXP drivers.

I re-installed Ubuntu 10.10 without these & it cured the USB problem.

First I tried uninstalling WinXP drivers, ndiswrapper & rebooting, but that didn't help.
