### Post by t0p on 2009-09-22
*"If it ain't broke, don't fix it"* - how I hate that expression!

Jaunty has several feature changes from Intrepid.  You might not want any of them... but you won't know until you've tried it.

Is your computer a mission-critical machine?  If so, you'd probably feel better if you stick with what you've got.  If not, then why not move up to Jaunty?  If it screws up your computer, you can always reinstall Intrepid.  Same if you find you just don't like Jaunty.  There's nothing irrevocable about an upgrade.

Remember, Ubuntu is Free software.  Which means: if you break it, you get to keep both pieces!  ;)
