If you feel that you have nothing to give becuase all you have are questions, you are wrong. It is the way we can all keep this wonderful thing going forward and no one needs to feel any sort of emotional distress over any of this. It is supposed to be fun so let it. Do not freak out, do not distress yourself or others. Remember, humor in all things. 

Help is on the way.

### Post by anewguy on 2009-10-30
I also have a tech background, but a dated one now.  I've been leery of being a tester in that I thought that perhaps for the alpha release they wanted people more intimately familiar with the kernel, etc..  If that's not the case, and they just want Joe Schmuck, I may give that a try.  I answer questions when I can, sometimes it turns out my answer doesn't work, and God knows I ask a ton of questions.  If everyone out there can tolerate me they should be able to tolerate anyone :)

I agree with you 100% - everyone sharing is what this is all about.  Part of the push with Ubuntu has been to bring Linux to the common person, so you don't have to be a techie to install or use Linux, whereas in the past with other distributions that wasn't true.  Linux USED to be really just a techies OS because it was younger then.  It has matured, and with that has come a concentration on making it more friendly to the average Joe.  I really commend the Ubuntu family for that, and these forums make up a larger portion of that family than some realize - people helping people is what Ubuntu is all about.

As far as Easter eggs goes, I knew someone many years ago while I was a systems programmer that was an applications guy.  He was having problems with an online program he was working on at the time, so he made up a screen of regular text characters that made the outline of a hand flipping the bird when it trapped at a certain point.  What he didn't count on was his boss's boss wanting to see the progress and of course it was when the programmer was there at the time.  All of us on the outside got a big kick out of the look on their faces, but he got canned for it but found a job quickly way back then.  I still remember that quite fondly!

Dave :)
