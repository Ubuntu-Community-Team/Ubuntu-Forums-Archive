### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-07-16
Welcome.

There's a heap of awesome players in Ubuntu. I advise doing a search for 'Ubuntu Windows alternatives' or something along those lines.

If you have a spare partition, maybe dual-boot with Windows until you are feeling confident that you are ready for the change. Many Win programs can be run in Ubuntu using Wine, some excellent, some c**p, some not at all. If you are determined to use mostly Win programs, probably best to stick with Win or dual-boot (or install Win as a 'virtual machine' inside Ubuntu).

Will work:

Skype
Chrome
WoW (with Wine I believe)
Diablo (as above)

The rest no idea, but there are plenty of IM apps. Android is open-source so doubt they'd be any problems there, but again, do some searching online to answer a lot of these compatibility questions. For instance, 'skyrim ubuntu' or 'skyrim linux'. WineHQ is another  good place to start.

  Make a 'LiveCD' (the install CD) and boot from it. That should take you to a desktop. Plug in an ethernet cable (wireless probably won't work running from the disk - if you are offered drivers for your wireless don't attempt to install), get online and have a look around. This won't change anything on your machine or drives; you will be running from the CD only.

I was in your position at the end of 2007. Had been thinking about Linux, tried it out and loved it, so took the plunge. I've just completed a degree at uni and am now doing honours and have used pretty much only Ubuntu for that entire time. Haven't looked back, but that is me. Might not suit you but that is no prob. We should all use what suits our needs best, whether that be Ubuntu, Win, Mac or anything else. Good luck with it all. ;)

PS: Lots of us dual-boot with Win or run it one way or the other (especially gamers!), even if it's used twice a year for many of us.
