### Post by drs305 on 2009-01-28
A good start would be to download and read the Ubuntu Pocket Guide. It is linked in a sticky at the top of this forum's (Absolute Beginner Talk). As you read through the guide, you will find topics which interest you and which you can control further.

I found that what I learned was dictated by what I needed to accomplish. When I needed to share files, I learned about samba and SSH. When I wanted to make a script to run a series of commands, I learned about basic BASH scripting. My needs dictated what I investigated. It is a self-paced way to learn linux and ubuntu, and it has the benefit of letting you concentrate on things you will actually use.

Of course, if you want to 'teach' linux, you would need to learn much more than what you as an individual require. For a structured learning experience, you can visit linux online, which has a series of lessons.
[http://www.linux.org/lessons/beginner/index.html]("http://www.linux.org/lessons/beginner/index.html")
This will take you to the beginners lessons, but there are more advanced sections on the site as well.

Good luck!
