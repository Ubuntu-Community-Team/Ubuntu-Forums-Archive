I have not seen these before and they are quite handy.   After clicking through them

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal#barebones](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal#barebones)

Doing a barebones and building up from it is a little more work on your part, so if you're a super noob you might want to just go for one of the two links pqwoerituytrueiwoq provided.   

Going barebones and only adding in what you want keeps a lot of the glut off your system but you still have the Ubuntu core which means easy package management/software installs and reasonably easy access to whatever media codecs and plugins you might need.   Its no Arch or Slackware but it is a quick and easy way to breath new life into old hardware.

You can make a list of the gnome apps you really like and add those in as you go.   Much less trouble then adding a new desktop environment and having things like 3-4 notes apps, a couple file managers, a bunch of different terminal emulators, etc.
