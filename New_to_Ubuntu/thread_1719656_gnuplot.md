### Post by Lateralis on 2011-04-02
I use an O2 mobile USB/wireless device when I travel around the UK with my laptop.  Getting the device to work in Ubuntu was straightforward for me.  

If you plug the USB stick into your laptop it should be recognised as a mobile broadband device.  Mine was at least.  In Windows the USB stick also shows up as a USB stroage device as it does contain a flash memory card.  

Anyway, when you plug the device into your laptop go to System -> Preferences -> Network Connections.  You will see a tab called Mobile Broadband.  Click "Add" to add the details for the mobile broadband connection.  Some information which might be relevant for you can be found in this [thread from a few years ago]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1021567").  It may be old but might well still be of use.
