From what I'm hearing, the files generated under iWork don't always cooperate with Microsoft Office and such. I don't recommend making important papers under iWork, especially for college. ### Post by tom.swartz07 on 2010-03-20
You could always get a mid-range laptop with similar parts to a macbook and just make a hackintosh/ubuntu dual boot.

I managed to get OSX running on my AMD x64 system for a while really nicely. 

The only real thing that youre getting with a Mac is the guarantee that the Unix/Darwin kernel will work on the hardware. (That and some freaking shiny apps to use!)

Matter of preference, basically. 

Ive noticed that the University IT depts dont really like Ubuntu or any 'non-mainstream' OS. If you go with a Mac, you wouldnt have to worry about connectivity problems, etc. (ESPECIALLY if they use Cisco Clean Access, ugh.)

Thats all.
