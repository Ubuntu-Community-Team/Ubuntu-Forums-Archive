### Post by Chronon on 2009-08-12
Most cards that I see have 3.5mm stereo jacks.  However, you should try to find out some specifics about your card.  Imagine if the input has a preamp leading to an ADC.  This won't work very well if you try to reverse the signal path.  It's possible for cards to work this way, but not all of them do.  It depends on hardware and drivers whether this is possible.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2009-08-12
In a nutshell NO!

In is IN.  Out is OUT.  
But, if you want to go ahead and try, be my guest.  But if those speakers are powered, you just MAY have to get another sound card.

IF you want to run two sets of speakers .. get a "Y" cord at Radio Shack. And hook it into the OUT!
