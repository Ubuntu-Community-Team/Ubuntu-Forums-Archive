### Post by Paqman on 2009-09-20
> **RHH said:**
> 
1) I understand that the name Linux comes from the first name of the originator (Linus Thorvalds?) and the UNIX operating system which it is modeled after somewhat.  True or false?


True.

> 2) I understand the open source concept and realize that there are different distro's, some for pay and some free for downloading, and some almost free except you pay a few bucks for the media or some such.  Ubuntu is the name given to one such distro.  Is Debian also a distro or does it refer to something else?

Debian is one of the most important distros. Ubuntu is actually based on Debian. That's why our software comes in .deb packages. Incidentally, Debian = Deb & Ian. Named after it's creator and his wife.

> 3) One of the distinctive things about Linux compared to sindows is that the GUI is not a part of the OS in Linux - a separate application (program) is required to use a GUI in Linux.  I think that KDE is one of them.  Is this information correct?  What other GUI's are there for Linux?  Are they interchangeable with different distro's?

The two main desktop environments are Gnome & KDE. KDE is also used on other OSes like BSD. Distros either tend to pick one and stick to it, or release versions of their system in both DEs.

Some other DEs include Openbox, XFCE and LXDE.

> 4) The little ASUS I bought is the 900SD Linux version.  My understanding from poking around is that the Debian Linux distro is installed with the KDE GUI.  Can anyone confirm this or educate me about it?  I've found scant activity on the ASUS forums I've found googling.

Yep, the actual distro is called Xandros. It's Debian-based, running KDE with a customised interface for small screens. 

> 
I'd really appreciate answers to my questions and pointers to any basic tutorials.


Lots of good tutorial stuff on [Psychocats]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/") to get you started, and there's a ton of information on this forum.
