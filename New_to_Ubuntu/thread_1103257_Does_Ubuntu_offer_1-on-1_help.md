### Post by Calmor on 2009-03-22
> **suomalainen said:**
> I don't know what you guy's/gals think but there is an elite class of Ubuntu specialists/professionals that exists and these are the people that can solve problems 1, 2, 3,.... How to reach them is another story....????

Well, there are two things to this...

One - the community experts you seek are in with the rest of us, and I'd venture to say that everyone has a specialty - for instance, a member who deals with Ubuntu Server on a daily basis (for work or pleasure) may not be the best person to ask how to get Java working in 64-bit Firefox.  You could search for issues similar to yours and then gauge the answers.

Two - if you really want a professional, paid support is available.  See [http://www.ubuntu.com/support/paid]("http://www.ubuntu.com/support/paid")

Otherwise, you have to trust those who are willing to volunteer to help you out.  If you're concerned that what you're doing may mess up your machine, you have a couple options.  You can either back up the entire system and then run the commands, or you can research what the commands actually do and make an informed decision on whether or not you want to do it.

As a side note, we're all here learning, even if we think we know everything there is to know.  Even the experts learn something, I'm sure, and some become experts by helping others.
