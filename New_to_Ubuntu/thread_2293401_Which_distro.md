### Post by DuckHook on 2015-09-04
Alas, CrunchBang (proper) ceased development in Feb of this year. A shame, as it was one of my favourites, but the developer/maintainer felt that there was insufficient interest. ### Post by DuckHook on 2015-09-06
> **stalkingwolf said:**
> is it a single core or dual core (core 2)  processor?   I have a box that has a p4 core 2 2.8 gz  2gb ram (pc5300) that works fine on my tv. i have mint 13 mate installed.  It also worked well for a friend of ours running japaneese videos and several gamesI don't believe the P4 ever had a true dual core. Some had Hyper-threading which made the single core look like two logical cores to the OS, but the underlying architecture was still a single core. Just as in your case, I found that whether uni- or multi-threaded, the P4 was fine with Lubuntu provided you didn't tax the CPU excessively.

@dave6

You might also want to look into ToriOS. I mention this one with some reservations because although ToriOS is a derivative of Ubuntu, it is (a) still in development, and (b) not an "official" flavour. However, it is designed to be even lighter than Lubuntu, and I believe that the team behind it is headed by some of the crew that originally started Lubuntu (I could be mistaken on this last point).

I don't believe that you really need an ultra-light distro for your specs, which are actually pretty reasonable. I mention ToriOS only as a further option.

### Post by Aidan_Owen on 2015-09-06
If you're looking for strictly orthodox Ubuntu "spin-offs" [Lubuntu]("http://lubuntu.net/") or [Xubuntu]("http://xubuntu.org/") are your best bet.
If you'd like to look beyond the basic software included with the DE and Ubuntu (i.e. included codecs, customized environment) give [LXLE]("http://www.lxle.net/") a shot
However the one I'd recommend the most is [Elementary OS]("http://elementary.io/") as it is both lightweight and very beautiful
