### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-04-16
Tips for always getting a good ISO burnt:

1. Download it using Bittorrent; every couple of megabytes it checks the MD5 sums to make sure that what it's just downloaded is correct. You're guaranteed to receive the ISO properly.

2. Don't use the CD-Rs you got from a market stall that cost you $5 for a pack of 100. Only use well-known, good quality media. Preferably, the dye on the underside of the disc should be a strong colour, such as a deep blue.

3. Burn at 8x. If your burner doesn't do 8x, then do as close to that speed as possible.

4. After burning, let the CD cool down naturally to room temperature.

5. If it seems to take forever to start up the Ubuntu CD, then your drive is probably dirty or faulty.
