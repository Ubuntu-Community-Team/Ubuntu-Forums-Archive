### Post by mkoehler on 2008-05-14
Differences between POP3 and IMAP:

-POP3: Downloads all of the e-mail messages to your personal computer (takes up a good deal of space depending on how frequently you receive e-mails).  Additionally, you can tell POP3 to leave the messages on the server, so you can have a backup copy.

-IMAP: Only downloads the headers for the e-mail messages to your computer, saving a good deal of space.  However, in order to read the e-mail messages, you need to have an internet connection (although you read the messages from within the client, they are downloaded over the internet).

*I personally opt for the latter because I don't have a whole lot of space to work with on my laptop's HDD, but you can use whatever fits you best.  According to your description, that'd be POP3. :)
