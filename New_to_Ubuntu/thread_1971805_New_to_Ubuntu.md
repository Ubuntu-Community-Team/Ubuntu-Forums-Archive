### Post by I2k4 on 2012-05-03
First, you should see about experimenting on your system with Live USB, or better, Persistent Live USB.  However, on some old systems you can't set BIOS to boot from USB (I have one like that.)

Second, (more controversial) with those specs you'll take a very serious performance hit trying to run version 11 or 12 of anything as opposed to running version 10.  I tried and realized my little netbook (twice your machine, by the way) runs Lubuntu 10 like stink, and stinks trying to run later versions of any Ubuntu-based distro.

### Post by morhin on 2012-05-06
I started with Ubuntu 10.04. The reason I did was I found the book Ubuntu for Non-Geeks, by Rickford Grant with Phil Bull, at my local library. I downloaded the cd that came with it and used the book as a guide. After I returned the book I went out and bought my own copy. 
It has been great as it walked this newbie, non-geek through the whole process. It's my go to resource today. I'll wait until the book for 12.04 comes out before I upgrade to that version. I recommend this book highly. Without it I'm not sure I have the patience or the learning curve to deal with a new system.

Good luck.
