### Post by mdebusk on 2008-12-28
> **Big Russ said:**
> Any suggestions/recommendations are appreciated

My suggestion is to avoid loading up on too many Ubuntu-specific books. Get one, maybe two. But consider looking into books about Linux in general.

(FYI: The links that follow are direct to amazon.com.)

I actually appreciated the newer edition of [Linux for Dummies]("http://www.amazon.com/Linux-Dummies-8th-Dee-Ann-LeBlanc/dp/0470116498/") when I was a Linux dummy. I wouldn't buy a copy, but borrowing it from the public library would be great.

To get up and running in the shell, I liked [Easy Linux Commands: Working Examples of Linux Command Syntax]("http://www.amazon.com/Easy-Linux-Commands-Working-Examples/dp/0975913506/"). I started with MS-DOS and moved to OS/2, so I'm comfy with the command line; I just needed a little primer on the basic commands.

I found [A Practical Guide to Linux Commands, Editors, and Shell Programming]("http://www.amazon.com/Practical-Guide-Commands-Editors-Programming/dp/0131478230/") to be an excellent resource.

For Ubuntu-specific books, I chose [Beginning Ubuntu Linux]("http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ubuntu-Linux-Novice-Professional/dp/1590599918/") and [Ubuntu Hacks: Tips & Tools for Exploring, Using, and Tuning Linux]("http://www.amazon.com/Ubuntu-Hacks-Tools-Exploring-Tuning/dp/0596527209/") and liked them both. I'm considering [Hacking Ubuntu: Serious Hacks Mods and Customizations]("http://www.amazon.com/Hacking-Ubuntu-Serious-Customizations-ExtremeTech/dp/047010872X/") and [Ubuntu Linux Toolbox: 1000+ Commands for Ubuntu and Debian Power Users]("http://www.amazon.com/Ubuntu-Linux-Toolbox-Commands-Debian/dp/0470082933/") currently.
