### Post by Ji Ruo on 2010-01-28
I can do you one better than a link!

You can use the documentation that comes with ubuntu, the man pages. Go to a terminal (Applications>Accessories>Terminal) and type in:```
man ifconfig
man iwconfig
```
...etc. It can look a bit like gibberish to a beginner, but you get used to it quickly. This is how most people who are proficient in Linux learn stuff. If the learning curve seems to steep, you can always just do say a google search 'what is ifconfig' and you will find something a bit friendlier.

Oh, and to get out of the man page you just press q.
