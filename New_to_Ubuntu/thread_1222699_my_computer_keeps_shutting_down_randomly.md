### Post by koleoptero on 2009-07-25
First of all, try typing in normal letters, we're not blind. :P

Second, when the computer starts, instead of selecting ubuntu to boot, try the memtest. It will check your memory for defects. 

If you don't have a problem with that, then it's probably some incompatibility with your hardware. It has happened to me in the past.

Also hit the ESC key when you see the loading screen to see what goes behind the scene. If it always crashes at the same point then it could probably be worked out. Check it out and let us know.

I second that :D

From my experience _true_ random crashes are usually caused by faulty power supplies. True random crashes even occur when your PC is doing nothing.

Random crashes _under load_ are often heat related. Check you machine for dust on the CPU cooler, for dust in the power supply and for any cables hindering airflow inside the PC.
