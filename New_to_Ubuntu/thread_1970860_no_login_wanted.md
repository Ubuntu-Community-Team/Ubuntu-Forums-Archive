### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-05-01
Go to System Settings>User Accounts under the power/cog icon.

Click Unlock. Enter your password. Select the user and move the Automatic Login slider to the OFF position. Click Lock.

Regards.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-05-01
> **ub9876 said:**
> 12.04   how do you skip the log in screen.. the one with your password..??

First, you reconsider what you are doing, and think about how if your computer got stolen, all its data can be easily accessed by the thief. Or how someone can visit your house and instantly get access to your data and to any websites that you automatically get logged into as well (for example, Facebook).

Then, you think "Is it really going to save any time, I'll get pretty quick at typing my password because I often have to type it anyway, and besides I'll still need to put in my keychain password after autologin so I can connect to wifi..."

And then, if you STILL want autologin, turn it on using the instructions given to you in the previous post.

Just thought you should know the risks, and probably reconsider.
