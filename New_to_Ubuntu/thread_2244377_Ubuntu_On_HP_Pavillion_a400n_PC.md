### Post by oldfred on 2014-09-16
Since you have mulitiple drives, it would be better to keep Windows on its drive and make space for Ubuntu on one of your other drives.
But it then is a bit more complicated as you need to use Something Else to install. That is the only install option that lets you choose another drive and which drive to install grub2's boot loader into. then set BIOS to boot that drive.

Since you only have 1GB of RAM Lubuntu or Xubuntu may be better choices. You probably also do not have good enough video to run Unity. Full Ubuntu & Kubuntu are more for newer systems or systems with a bit more RAM and newer video card or chip.

       Lubuntu, which uses the LXDE desktop environment, targeted at "normal computers" with 128 MB
With 512Mb of RAM and almost any processor, Lubuntu will perform more than adequately out-of-the box.
