### Post by Liviu-Theodor on 2008-11-27
Sometimes, when really the graphics card does not support hardware acceleration, Windows use software drivers (I mean that emulates a VESA card). And maybe Ubuntu is right, your card does not support 3D, even you can play 3D games under Windows, using a software driver.

If you're lucky, it might just be a question of enabling your graphic card. To check, go to: **Main Menu > System > Administration > Hardware Drivers**.
If there are no proprietary drivers available, then you've got an onboard graphic card, and it get's more technical.
