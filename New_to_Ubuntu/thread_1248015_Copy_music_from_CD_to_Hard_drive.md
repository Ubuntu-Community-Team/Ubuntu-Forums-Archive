### Post by SteveNorman on 2009-08-24
If you want to learn some linux and have some fun, install, configure and rip your cds to HD with an old tool called "abcde".

here is a great thread on the subject 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=109429](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=109429)

youll learn a lot about the command line and configuring directories etc in the process, and when your done youll have a great ripping tool.

### Post by PGScooter on 2009-08-24
I agree regarding abcde.

Once you get it set up (which isn't that hard. Just copy one of the examples that does what you want), ripping the cd the way you want (quality, name of song, right tags, etc.) is just a matter of typing "abcde" into your terminal. As SteveNorman hinted though, you have to be up for a little bit of a challenge if you're completely new.
