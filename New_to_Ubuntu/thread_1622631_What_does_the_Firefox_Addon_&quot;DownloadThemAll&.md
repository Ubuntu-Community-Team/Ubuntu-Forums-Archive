### Post by beew on 2010-11-15
> **WhatAreHackers said:**
> What is the difference between the three options given, when download files through the "DownloadThemAll" add on; "DownThemAll!", "dTa OneClick!", and save file.  I read their faq, and I am still confused.  What do those three options differ from each other?

Save file is just saving the file, if the download get interrupted (say you close firefox accidentally) then it is gone, you have to download it again.

"DownThemAll!" allows you to resume a partial download in a scenario like above. You just have to restart firefox and go to DownThemAll! > manager to resume the downlaod. I have tried resuming  a day later after several reboots and it still works. It is particularly helpful if you try to download large files over not very good internet connections (like wireless with weak signal) It also supports metalinks. 

"dTaIOneClick!" lets you download from all the download links  in the same webpage with just one click. You can also resume partial downloads.
