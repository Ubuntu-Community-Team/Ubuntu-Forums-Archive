### Post by c4v3m4n on 2008-05-09
I would recommend Ubuntu, I would go with 7.10 but you could try 8.04 if you wanted to.  Tons of options for bittorrent clients, bittornado, ktorrent, azereus.  In linux you can install vlc player, which will play pretty much any kind of video or audio format.  Should just need to use```
sudo apt-get install vlc
``` to install vlc player, so you wouldn't need to convert any video files.  For browsing you can use firefox which comes preinstalled, tons of extra freeware you can download too, like swiftweasel, opera, swiftfox, epiphany, and lots more.  As for dual booting all you need to do is install on the separate partition, might want to create a small swap too.  Most of that is easy to do with the ubuntu live cd.

### Post by bumanie on 2008-05-09
Firstly here is a good guide to follow [http://apcmag.com/how_to_dual_boot_windows_xp_and_linux_xp_installed_first.htm](http://apcmag.com/how_to_dual_boot_windows_xp_and_linux_xp_installed_first.htm)
As far as your other questions ,
1. Firefox beta 5 is included in the hardy heron installation.
2. Thuderbird mail client is available from the repositories and evolution is included in the installation.
3. Transmission bittorrent is included and there are many others you can choose to install.
4. Most software you need is included already in the installation, but due to proprietary codecs etc. you have to install some of them later to enable video and music function.
5. I find the program devede does a good job of converting video to various formats including avi.
However if you are planning to convert video, I doubt that 11g is enough once you have the file system and a swap partition installed - they will take about half your available space. You should consider a bigger partition or a second hard drive. 

Also of interest to you may the fact that ubuntu now has read/write capabilities to ntfs. All these things will sound difficult, but that is why the forum is here to help you. If you have further queries, or get stuck, make another post.
