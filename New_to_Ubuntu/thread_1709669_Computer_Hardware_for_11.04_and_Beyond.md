### Post by seawolf167 on 2011-03-18
This *greatly* depends on your use for the computer.

If all you are going to do is surf the internet & use pidgin, you can use the cheapest computer you can find. If, on the other hand, you want to play the latest and greatest 3d games that you can get working on Ubuntu, you'll need a high end computer with a good graphics card.

The best advice would be to Google for "mid level computer", "gaming rigs", etc. to see what the current hardware is for that particular machine. Or look at guides to building your own machine which usually give multiple options for different price points.

Hardware changes very fast, so what I'd say today is going to be completely different one month from now. Note that almost all new[er] graphics cards are now support by their manufacturer with drivers for *nix.
