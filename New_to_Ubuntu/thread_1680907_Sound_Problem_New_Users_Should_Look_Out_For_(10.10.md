### Post by Lt. Surge on 2011-02-03
As I learn more and more about Ubuntu/Linux I get the distinct feeling as a Windows User that I'm proverbially "not in Kansas anymore". Some aspects that are glaringly different reveal that Linux is a very manual interface compared to Windows. And I thought Macs were user-friendly!

At first I thought I was going to have to go into the Terminal in order to fix my problem in which I had NO SOUND despite my speakers being at 100%.

But then I just remembered that I had used Headphones earlier and that might be a problem. Turns out I was right. All I had to do was switch the device I was using in the Connector settings.

[IMG]http://www.upload3r.com/serve/030211/1296742947.png[/IMG]

If you are just starting out I recommend you take a look at the Sound Preferences (Linux equivalent of Sound Settings in Windows, but a little more advanced). Test the differences between selecting "Analog Headphones" and "Analog Speakers" without plugging in Headphones. You'll see what I mean. This is the first thing you should inspect before needing to go into the Terminal (Linux equivalent of Windows DOS).

If you're still not able to hear anything and/or you see no devices at all, I recommend consulting [threads like this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1593095").

I hope this may help someone in the future :lolflag:
