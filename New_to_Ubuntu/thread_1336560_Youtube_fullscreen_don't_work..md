### Post by themusicalduck on 2009-11-24
I think this is down to a weird youtube bug you get when you use dual monitors. I have the same problem. Basically it shows the video as if it was stretched over both monitors, but only shows it in one screen.

The bug has been around for a fair while, but youtube haven't bothered to fix it yet. It's strange how it works for every other video site that I know of.

A semi-workaround is to use the Super key with right click and dragging a box around the video, which zooms onto the video and fills the screen (with compiz enabled).
