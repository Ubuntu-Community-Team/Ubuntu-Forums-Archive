### Post by earthpigg on 2009-09-14
hi,

> how do I remove Gnome?

ive tried to completely remove gnome several times.

the problem arises because many lxde things you may be used to using are things **_also_** used by gnome, and you can end up breaking lxde by removing them.

my advice:

unless you are really short on hard drive space, go ahead and keep gnome installed and just don't use it. having gnome installed will not slow down your computer any when you are logged into lxde.

then, *next fresh install*, go ahead and do an ubuntu command line install and install lxde from there.

it is always easier to buld up to what you want than strip things away to achieve what you want, in my opinion.

reading around on the [link in my sig]("http://sites.google.com/site/masonux/") may be able to help you out since i have already faced & overcome the minor difficulties of doing this :D
