### Post by NightwishFan on 2010-03-11
The easiest way is to create free space inside Windows by shrinking your available space. Do so by fight clicking on My Computer, hitting Manage, and finding the Windows partitioner. Shrink your main drive until you have at least 15gb of free space at the end. Do not create a new partition, just reboot into the ubuntu installer, and select: "Use Largest Continuous Free Space". Ubuntu will automatically be set up in the free space and dualbooted with Windows7.
