### Post by davidmohammed on 2011-01-15
A common misconception among computer buyers is that processor is key.  It is not.

The most important components are RAM and Graphics for "performance" buyers.  Look for systems with 2 or more GB -the amount depends on the type of applications you intend to run.  Again, the type of applications you intend to run would drive the graphics card type.

  Personally I would also look into SSD hard-drive or the O/S with a sizable Hard-drive to data.

### Post by akand074 on 2011-01-15
I'd say the highest end i5 may seem a little faster sometimes in terms of nothing but speed because it can reach higher clock speeds. But in terms of overall performance, the i7 would definitely still be better obviously, especially when it comes to multi-tasking and highly threaded applications.

Honestly though, for your uses, any processor in that list would be more than sufficient, they are all great processors. Having a decent size SSD where you can have all your files on would be nice though. The Envy series all have enough RAM and most cases more than enough, and good graphics cards so unless your an avid gamer you'll unlikely need to look too much into that because you are going to get something good regardless.

I disagree with [davidmohammed]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=600258"), the CPU is by far the most important part of the computer. Everything else only is important/beneficial based on your own personal uses, but the CPU is always important as everything is controlled by it. If you're a gamer, you would want to look into the best graphics card, if you're a big content creater you may want to look into high quality/amount of RAM, if you are anybody who uses a computer, you will definitely need to look into a good CPU (regardless of it's priority, though for most people it comes first). Just to name a few, sure there may be exceptions, but generally.
