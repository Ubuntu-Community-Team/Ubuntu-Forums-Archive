### Post by QIII on 2018-06-14
Kubuntu is an official flavor of Ubuntu, so there is no need to look for a special Kubuntu subforum.  You can always add the Kubuntu tag to your posts if you would like.

If you have a question specifically about the KDE desktop, you can start a thread in Desktop Environments.  Beyond that, just chose an appropriate sub-forum.  Security, Networking & Wireless, General Help, etc.  To avoid the encumbrance of needing to know the minute details of the navigation of a particular DE of the dozens people might use, most instruction given here is done in the "common language":  the command line in a terminal emulator.  You'll see references to "the terminal", "CLI", "command line", "console", etc.  Don't be afraid.  You don't need to be a programmer.  You won't be writing code.  You won't be using "codes", which is a misnomer.  You will be issuing commands -- which you can usually simply copy and paste from the posts of those who will be helping you.

If you look to the right in the header of my posts, you will see that I use Kubuntu primarily.
