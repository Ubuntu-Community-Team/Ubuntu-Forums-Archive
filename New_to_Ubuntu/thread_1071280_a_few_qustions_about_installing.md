### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2009-02-16
For 1: No, Ubuntu has a very basic and easy to use installer.
Its very graphical and is very easy to figure out
2: To see if your hardware will work Ubuntu runs off something called a live CD. a Live CD is almost like a virtual OS, it gives you a basic OS to play around with initially to show off what Ubuntu can do.
It has a installer right on the disk too, so for those who like it can give it a full install.
3: Dual boot is very possible, during the installer process you will be offered a choice to install ubuntu with windows.
The setup should be quite simular.
4: I am not sure, but there is WUBI.
