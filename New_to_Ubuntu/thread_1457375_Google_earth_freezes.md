### Post by waynefoutz on 2010-04-18
If your video driver is configured correctly, it's not a hog, in fact it's quite responsive. If you have an ATI graphics processor, it will work fine if you use the ATI Catalyst driver. I can't get it to work correctly with the open source ATI drivers, which I'm now forced to use because I have an older ATI chipset. The open source driver does 90% of the tasks I throw at it, but google earth falls into that 10% that I can't get to run. When I load up Intrepid or earlier, and use the ATI Catalyst driver, I can spin the globe like a top. With Karmic and the open source, it moves slowly, then crashes shortly there after. 

The alternative is to run Google Maps [http://maps.google.com/]("http://maps.google.com/") inside your browser with Flash. You can do just about everything on the Google Maps website that you can with the Google Earth app. That's the work around that works best for me.

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2010-04-20
Google Earth is very buggy on Linux, and terribly sensitive to the graphics card being used.

Turn of the atmosphere effect, and you may get lucky.

As waynefoutz says, your best bet may be to use Google Maps in the browser. Not as good, obviously, but better than nothing.
