1. How do I see a list of all my applications? I notice if I click on Dash Home I only see the recent applications I use. 
 
Open the dash home, click the application lens (bottom of dash, looks like a ruler, pen, and paintbrush), Where it says "installed" in the middle, click see more results. It's probably more useful to employ the  filter to see them by category (top right, click filter, click the categories you want shown). Employ the search feature in the dash home to find things easily.

Instead of all the clicking to get to the application lens, you can also press super+a to open it right to the application lens, press and hold super to see a list of keyboard shortcuts. Super is the windows key on most keyboards.

### Post by hakermania on 2012-09-03
Hello drakewrites! Welcome to the forums!

My answers:
(1)
a) Right-click, select applications on the Ubuntu sign on the top of the list on your left
b) Under Filter results on the right make sure that 'All' is selected
c) Click on the 'Installed' on the left and you will have a list of all of your installed applications
[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/yjlxK.png[/IMG]

Please take note that there are more applications in your system, actually approximately 2000 in the regular clean install, but most of them are not directly usable by the average user, so the system chooses to show you the graphical user interface applications that are directly usable by you, without any special knowledge of using the terminal.

(2)
Haven't heard of something similar, but make sure you are not pressing one of these keyboard shortcuts that work with firefox for going one back/forth page:
Alt + left / right arrow
Ctrl + [ / ]
Shift + Scroll down / up

These shortcuts can be found here: [http://www.mouserunner.com/FF_Shortcuts1Printable.html](http://www.mouserunner.com/FF_Shortcuts1Printable.html)
