### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-08-22
Welcome to the forums.  If it is a bad battery that is causing lockups on the Vaio, just get a new battery.  Add more RAM and maybe an SSD, that will give it a 2nd life.  Yes, updates can cause regressions and some downtime to fix things--so you have to have another working computer, on the network to research fixes.

If money is not an issue, then buy a new computer.  Of course your father will complain about how everything has changed and "where is the email icon?".  An important issue is migrating personal data over from the old machine to the new machine.

Don't be surprised if he complains about it being slower than his old computer:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2276887&page=2](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2276887&page=2)

Create a launcher for ssh-server on the desktop so your father can launch the server so you can log in remotely to perform updates and check on things.  Don't leave it running all the time, because it is a security risk.  Make sure you have a strong sudo password.  You can remotely fix most software problems.  Hardware problems are nearly impossible to fix over the phone--you have to see the symptoms and perform several troubleshooting steps and see the results.

### Post by howefield on 2015-08-22
I've just done a Sony Vaio of a similar age, but still a very beautiful machine. Nice screen, 4 gig of ram and enough cpu and graphics to run any of the Ubuntu family. You don't mention the specs of your fathers machine ?

Seems to me that Xubuntu at the least would be a good bet, (depending on specifications) and installing something like Teamviewer on it would allow you to take care of the support. I certainly wouldn't expect it to be difficult to refurb the machine to suit that kind of computer usage.

If you go for the HP 255 - watch the wireless card, at the moment it only easily works with 12.04.
