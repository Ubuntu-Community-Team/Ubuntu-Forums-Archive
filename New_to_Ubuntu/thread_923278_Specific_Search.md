### Post by drs305 on 2008-09-18
I generally first try to use Google to search only the UF by starting a google search with this string, then adding words/phrases:
```
site:ubuntuforums.org 
```

Did you do the search as: fan not shutting off ? That will return hits on any post with those words in it. Unless it's a phrase enclosed in quotes, use only the key words. Also if you need a linux solution, include 'linux' or 'ubuntu' at the end of the search (unless you are specifically searching the ubuntu forums, where many posts would not include those terms).

Try using quotes around key common phrases to reduce the hits.  I tried your search with "shutting off" and got no hits. So I changed it to "shut off" and got a couple.  Pick key words and even better key two-or-more-word phrases that you would expect to see ('shut off' vs 'shutting off'). 

If you get a huge number of hits, take a look at some of the entries and pick out key words you may not have used initially. In this case, I saw some hits that included 'cooling' and I thought that might be a good word to include since some posts used 'fan' not as a machine but as someone who liked something.

Including the word 'solved' in a search on a popular topic also may help in giving you better results.

Good luck.
