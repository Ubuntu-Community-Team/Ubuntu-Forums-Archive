### Post by snowpine on 2012-04-28
> **roodypoo said:**
> That is a good analogy. But in the computer world the more things that are installed the greater the security risk.

Please read the sticky thread on Ubuntu security: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=510812](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=510812)

I do not think uninstalling gnome-bluetooth will meaningfully improve the security of your system. (Especially if you do not have bluetooth hardware!) A side effect will be to remove the ubuntu-desktop package, which may or may not be something you desire.

There are a few distros that cater to the *bespoke* philosophy: arch, gentoo, linux from scratch.

A similar goal can be achieved in Ubuntu by performing a Minimal install: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal)
