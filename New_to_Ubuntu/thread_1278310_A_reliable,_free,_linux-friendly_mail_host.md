### Post by Hospadar on 2009-09-29
Honestly, I think the best option is to host your own mail server.  

You can get a domain name and dynamic dns from someone like dyndns.org for somthing on the order of $10-20 USD a year, and setting up a mail server isn't really all that bad with the right guides (i.e. look it up before you set it up).  

I'm not sure there is any [email]yourname@yourdomain.com[/email] type stuff for free, because that would require you to have a domain registered to yourself, i.e., you gotta pay, because registrars don't give away domain names for free.  *If* you own the domain, maybe there are people who will serve the mail for free or almost free.  I know a lot of web hosting providers also do mail hosting and are pretty cheap ($10-20 a year for something low volume like a couple email accounts).
