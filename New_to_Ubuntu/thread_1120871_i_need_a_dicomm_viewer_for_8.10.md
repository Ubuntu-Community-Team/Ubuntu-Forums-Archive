### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-04-09
Gimp will open and display dicomm files. I generally, use Gimp to convert these files to jpegs which can be viewed by almost any viewer. Once opened just click on "Save As" and select jpeg as the saved format.

Hope this helps.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-04-09
> DarinB
 agree but it is labor intensive if i have to convert each image. 	

As I indicated, it's unnecessary to convert. Just use Gimp to view and keep the files in their original format. It's been my experience that it's often easier to convert the files for sharing.

Right click on any one dicomm file in the file browser and select "properties". Go to the "Open With" tab and add Gimp to the open with tab. That way all dicomm files will automatically open with Gimp.
