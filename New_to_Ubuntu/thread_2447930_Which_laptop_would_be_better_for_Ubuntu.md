### Post by CatKiller on 2020-07-29
Ryzen is the better choice. The integrated graphics are stronger and, although some tasks are difficult to parallelise, the Ryzens have better single-threaded performance too, now. More cores also mean that you can do more single-threaded tasks at the same time.

I'd second checking out the Phoronix benchmarks.. 

The one thing to be aware of is that AMD graphics benefit from being on the newest software: Intel have the resources to get support lined up in advance of the release of their hardware and AMD don't.

There are some other European companies that you might want to look at as well, but Tuxedo is a good choice.
