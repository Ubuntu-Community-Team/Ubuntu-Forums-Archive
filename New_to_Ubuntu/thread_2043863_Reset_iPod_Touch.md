Hi there.

You can do a hard reset by holding the home and sleep button at the same time for up to 15 seconds (until the apple logo comes up).

If you're getting the connect to itunes symbol as a result of either too many pass-code fails or a botched update/recovery, then you really want to use windows or os x through itunes to restore it... really don't attempt to run itunes in wine; it's sketchy at the best of times!

if you literally know no-one with either (whoo-hoo linux has won!) then you can try the genius bar at an apple store - software flashing is free, but if it's due to pass-code fails you may need to plead your case/prove ownership as they aren't usually in the habit of helping out pass-code blocks for fairly obvious reasons. Also bare in mind they may just re-flash the ipod, so if you don't use icloud/back up to the pc you'll loose data.

If you're talking about just a reset to factory, this is fine from inside the touch settings->general->reset->erase all content and settings (obviously use at your own risk)

To undo a jailbreak... itunes.
