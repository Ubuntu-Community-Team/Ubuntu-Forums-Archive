### Post by snowpine on 2008-10-01
> **connie8890 said:**
> Where do I find this "puppy ubuntu"? Will I be able to get online, do email, play online games, with it? My other 2 computers both can run any of the other ones, but I'm worried about messing with them because my husband and my son have been playing an online game that they downloaded, then they connect online and play on the server. Will I still be able to connect to a server and play the game, like world of warcraft or everquest?

Any flavor or "distro" of Linux will include a web browser so you can get online and check your email. The only catch is that your hardware, especially your network adapter and video card, may--or may not--work with certain distros "out of the box". It's good to do a little research first.

As Terl mentioned, Puppy is not related to Ubuntu, so unfortunately you'd be missing out on the awesome resource of Ubuntuforums.org! :) I personally prefer Ubuntu for a number of reasons, however, Puppy is popular and easy to use, and runs well on older hardware. 

Applications/games designed for Windows (like Warcraft) require a few extra steps to install into Linux, and some of them do not work at all. Switching to Linux is really a big step that you should make sure you fully understand before making the switch.

Your best bet is to try out Linux on one of your computers for a while, see if you like it. One nice thing about Linux is that, if you follow the steps carefully, you can set up a "dual boot" which means both Linux and Windows are installed, and you can choose which one to use each time you boot. This is the setup I have on my main computer (I use Linux most of the time, but a couple of my applications only work in Windows, like iTunes and Netflix watch-it-now) and it is the best of both worlds.
