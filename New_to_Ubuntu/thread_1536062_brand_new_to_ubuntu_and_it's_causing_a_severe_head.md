### Post by staf0048 on 2010-07-21
Welcome to Ubuntu!

There a lots of things you can do to make your desktop look the way you want.  It's extremely configurable.  For starters though, right click on the desktop and go to "change desktop background" and it should bring up a window with a few different backgrounds and options you can tinker with.

While you're there, notice the tabs on the top.  Go to themes and click different themes that look interesting to you.  They should change automatically.  If you like most of one, but would like to change a few things, click on customize toward the bottom of the themes tab.  

That should keep you busy for a while.

If you're not into trying to come up with your own theme and want a nice desktop check out [http://www.bisigi-project.org/?page_id=6&lang=en](http://www.bisigi-project.org/?page_id=6&lang=en) they have a few nicely configured themes.  

Beyond that, you can also check out [www.gnome-look.org](www.gnome-look.org) for LOTS more stuff, but you'll have to install it yourself.

Hope this helps!

### Post by Paqman on 2010-07-21
> **zyrtek69 said:**
> yes its 10.4 netbook remix.. i would take a screen shot but i dunno howv ;/..

OK. The netbook remix has a different interface from the regular desktop. If you want to use the normal Ubuntu interface, log out, then when you go to log back in select your username and change the session at the bottom of the screen to "GNOME", then log in.

That will get rid of the menu that spans the entire screen and give you normal drop-down ones.

### Post by kaldor on 2010-07-21
Just so you know, you do not really have to use the Netbook Edition on a netbook. It's just more "netbook-oriented" than the traditional desktop OS. You can use regular Ubuntu or any other Linux distro if you can't stand the netbook style.
