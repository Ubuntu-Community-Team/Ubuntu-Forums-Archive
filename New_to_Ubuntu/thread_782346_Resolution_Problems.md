```

	Identifier	"nVidia Corporation NV34 [GeForce FX 5500]"
	Driver		"nv"
	BusID		"PCI:1:0:0"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"SyncMaster"
	Option		"DPMS"
EndSection

Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"nVidia Corporation NV34 [GeForce FX 5500]"
	Monitor		"SyncMaster"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Modes		"1440x1440" "1440x900" "1280x1024" "1280x960" "1152x864" "1024x768" "832x624" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
```

### Post by ameanjoe on 2008-05-08
Quasimoto...Try this:
When you boot and the first mention of "GRUB", hit Escape. That will give you a list of kernels that you can boot to. I've had luck with the "22" kernel (I believe that is the 7.10 kernel). Once your computer has booted, go to the terminal and enter:
sudo dpsk-reconfigure -plow xserver-xorg
This will walk you through a series of configuration options (on most the default is what you want). When you get to the end of it do the 3 fingered boot: Ctrl/Alt/Backspace. When it boots you will (most likely) come up with the highest res. your monitor is capable of...Then you can go on and address the rest of your problems. It's hard to do much when your screen is at something like 640X480. Good Luck!
