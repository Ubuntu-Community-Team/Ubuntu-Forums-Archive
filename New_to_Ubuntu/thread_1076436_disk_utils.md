### Post by drs305 on 2009-02-21
defrag is not really necessary in linux, and scandisk is replaced, more or less, by *fsck*, which is scheduled to run on your disks during boot automatically. (I think the default is every 30 mounts). You can learn more about a specific command/app by running "man <command-name>" in a terminal (example: *man fsck*)

Linux/ubuntu do not require near the maintenance of windows, so sit back and enjoy!
