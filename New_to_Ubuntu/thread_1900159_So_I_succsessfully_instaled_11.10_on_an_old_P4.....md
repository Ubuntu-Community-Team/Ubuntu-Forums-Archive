### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-12-25
I would say that all AGP cards are now cheap in comparison to the prices that they were when first brought out and in comparison with the cost of the very latest cards.

First, see if you can find one for sale. Note its specifications and then see if Linux supports that card.

I have just searched the web site of a company that I purchased the parts of my computer from. They list 118 graphic cards for sale but only 2 AGP cards. A Nvidia Geforce 6200 and a Geforce 8400. Are they supported?

Here are a couple of links to demonstrate what I am saying.

[http://openbenchmarking.org/s/NVIDIA%20GeForce%208400%20GS]("http://openbenchmarking.org/s/NVIDIA%20GeForce%208400%20GS")

[http://www.tldp.org/HOWTO/Hardware-HOWTO/video.html]("http://www.tldp.org/HOWTO/Hardware-HOWTO/video.html")

This is the sort of thing that I did before I purchased the parts for my self-build computer.
