### Post by TommyMac501 on 2009-10-22
Are you in the "Launcher" desktop?; a list of groups down the left, and clicking each brings up a selection of icons?.  If so, there are two ways (at least in Karmic).  First, there is the "Ubuntu Software Center" which gives you groups, then applications within those groups to install that have been tested and work. This is limited to programs that mimic the menu groupings i.e. Office programs, accessories and such.  I think it's in Accessories, but I don't remember, I moved it.

The other is "Synaptic Package Manager" which holds a list of all the software available (more or less, I don't want to confuse you).  Here you will find everything from applications to drivers to supporting files and everything in between.  There is a search bar up on top.  You'll find this in the "System" group.  When you find what you are looking for, click the selection box and choose "apply".  It will be installed, along with whatever additional files are needed.

I would look in the "Software Center" first before you mess with the Package Manager.

Good luck..
