### Post by QIII on 2009-11-18
Linux <> Windows, so you will have to be willing to accept somewhat of a learning curve.

There are basic similarities in that there is a GUI (if you choose to install it) that will allow you to perform operations on screen with the standard input devices.  And there is an operating system underlying that.

The similarities, from a general user standpoint, end there.

There is much to learn.

As for pen drives, they work fine for me.  If you are talking about running Ubuntu from one, there are many tutorials and there are a lot of bright people here who are willing to help. 

However, your title was too general to catch much attention.  I would recommend a new post with a very specific title like "How do I use a pen drive with Ubuntu?"

In general, ask only one question in any post, since it is easier for us to address one issue at a time.

### Post by gdonwallace on 2009-11-18
Hello and welcome to the Ubuntu Forums.

Concerning you question of Windows vs. Ubuntu.  As with changing from any one operating system to another, there are differences.  The majority of the learning curve will be in how Ubuntu handles hardware and installs software.  But a little pocking around and you'll get the hang of it quickly.  These Forums are the best place to find answers.  Also, in the Absolute Beginners forums, at the very top is a beginners guide.  I strongly suggest that you read through that to get an idea of how Ubuntu will work.

As for a pen drive, or any USB for that matter.  I have used them often and had no problems.  

Anyway, welcome to Ubuntu.  Peruse the forums to find answers to questions and if your not technically savy, you shouldn't have too many problems with making the transition to Ubuntu.
