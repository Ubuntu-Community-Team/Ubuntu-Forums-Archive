### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-06-22
> **NateTut said:**
> That's great but I don't live in the cloud.

Then you don't want an Android phone.  You also don't want any of the newer Palm phones, which run palms 'new' WebOS rather than the old PalmOS, because it also depends on cloud based storage through your choice of various cloud providers, including Palm itself.

Cloud based storage of PIM data can actually be a very good thing.  My smartphone is a Palm Pre.  I was a little apprehensive about switching to google or yahoo to manage all my contacts, until I realized a simple added detail: instead of replacing desktop sync, it just adds to it.

With my old Windows Mobile or Palm PDA's (before I made the jump to the Palm Pre last year) I had to sync my PDA to my desktop, which can be VERY difficult to set up on linux if you're using anything other than PalmOS.

Now, my phone ties to Google for contacts.  But, so does Evolution.  

Now, instead of having to plug my phone into my desktop to update back and forth, I can make the change one time, in any of three locations (my phone, Evolution, Google's website) and it will automatically update to the other two.  I still have everything stored on my phone and my desktop, just like I always did, but now it's also stored in the 'cloud,' and updating any one of them will update the others.  

If google looses my information, I just re-synce my desktop to it.  

If my phone looses it's info, I just remove and re-add my google account and it all comes back.

If my hard drive crashes and burns, and I don't have a fresh backup on my NAS, I just set my google account up in Evolution once I put in a new hard drive, and it's all right back.

In short, I have every bit of functionality and storage redundancy I had before, plus more, with less work.
