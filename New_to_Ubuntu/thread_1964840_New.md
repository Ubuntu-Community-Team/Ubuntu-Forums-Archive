### Post by Dngrsone on 2012-04-24
I am running triple-boot Win 7, Ubuntu 10.04, Ubuntu 12.04 (all 64-bit) on an HP G72.

A valuable resource for me when running Ubuntu on HP laptops (this is my third one) is [this](http://linux.aldeby.org/post/howto-ubuntu-linux-on-hp-pavilion-dv2000-dv6000-dv9000-series-laptops.html).

12.04 boots a teeny bit faster than 10.04 at the moment, can't say if that's due the newness of the install (not as much customization, etc.) or what, but both boot faster than Win 7 does.

Booting the CD takes *forever*, though.  The only real problems I have had, hardware-wise, was a lack of sound on the 10.04 side, which I solved by installing the latest ALSA drivers, which also requires me to reinstall said drivers every time the 10.04 kernel updates, so I wrote a script to do that.

Installation of Ubuntu goes much faster if you are plugged into your network when you perform the install.
