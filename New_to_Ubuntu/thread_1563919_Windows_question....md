### Post by diablo75 on 2010-08-29
I don't see why you wouldn't be able to use it on the laptop instead of the PC, so long as the laptop meets Windows 7 minimum system requirements (it probably does, and at most, maybe could use a RAM upgrade which is inexpensive).

Don't be afraid when you get to the activation stage; just do it online.  If they ask you to call an 800 number, just tell them the truth.  You're allowed to install the OS on one computer at any given time, and I don't think there's anything legally holding you back from moving that OS to another computer should you choose to replace it on the first one.

Edit:  By the way, your copy of 7 *SHOULD* upgrade Vista seamlessly...
