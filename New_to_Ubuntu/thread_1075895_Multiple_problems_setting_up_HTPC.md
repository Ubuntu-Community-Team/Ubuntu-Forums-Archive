### Post by aditeman on 2009-02-20
Hi,
I recently purchased an HTPC machine to hook up to my Samsung Plasma TV to enable me to stream HD content to my TV and to set up a DVR.
As I am fed up with Windows, and really hated Vista while I used it for a few months, I thought I'd try Ubuntu on my HTPC as a first step into the world of linux. I have a bit of prior Unix experience from work, but other than some basic shell scripting, I am a total Noobie.

So a few days ago, I got the computer with the following features:
 E5200 2.5GHz Dual Core
 2 GB RAM
 Gigabyte graphic card based on ATI Radeon HD 4350 chipset with HDMI video and audio output (sound card is ATI RV730XT HD 4670)
 AverTV Super 007 TV card
 RaLink RT2561/RT61 wireless LAN card
 Samsung 42A451 Plasma TV (connected via HDMI)
 TV stations are through the YES satellite company in Israel with an RF cable hooked up to the TV card.

Reading a bit, I decided to start with Mythbuntu, and so I downloaded it and installed it on my hard drive.
Here my noobie problems started, as I got low resolution and could only see part of the setup screeens. I hit the forums and found out about proprietary drivers and downloaded the ATI driver. After setting the resolution to 1280x720, I was able to see the whole screen. I thought all was well until I rebooted and Mythbuntu loaded all garbled. I have since learned that through the command line I can set the geometry one pixel shorter and I see the Mythbuntu frontend properly.
At some point I realized I couldn't hear anything, so I started trying to get the HDMI sound to work. I tried many proposed solution without success, including a guide that told me to change some settings on the sound preferences, including screenshots, but I couldn't find these screens on Mythbuntu or find out how to add them.
I finally tried to play a divx file through Mythbuntu Frontend, but failing, I got a little fed up and decided to try my luck with Ubuntu desktop first and add mythtv later.
So I downloaded Ubuntu 8.10 and installed it. Now setting the resolution was quick, and I was able to follow the sound card settings I couldn't before. Happily, I got the tests to play through my HDMI output. 
I now set out to see if I could get a TV stream through my AverTV card. After hours of searching forums and trying the different suggestions, I have almost given up. Nothing seems to work.
Raising my hands in failure, I reverted to the "may as well just run it on Windows" solution. I thought I'd at least try to play my HD streams through Ubuntu, so I decided to see how playing a movie would be. I tried to set up MythTV, but found out there was a good reason Mythbuntu was invented for inexperienced noobies like me. I decided to at least load a movie in mPlayer, but the screen flickered and there was no sound. I found myself searching and playing around with more configurations I have no clue about, and tried Totem too, but got the same results.
Finally, about three days after initially installing mythbuntu on my new HTPC, I am reaching out to anyone out there to help salvage my linux experience, before I revert back to the big brother with his system that hardware suppliers seem to write drivers for...(and that I speak the language a bit better...).
So, is it possible to achieve my goals of streaming HD and recording sports (NBA/MLB/NFL is on late at night in Israel...) on the system I purchased with linux?

Thank you,
aditeman
