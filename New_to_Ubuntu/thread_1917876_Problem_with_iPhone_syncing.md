### Post by Metaxa on 2012-01-30
iPhone user myself,

For you to use your iPhone with Ubuntu 10.10 and later you will need to install libmobiledevice, a quick google search will give you the info you are looking for. Now, if your phone is using iOS 5 you are currently out of luck as the team working on the previously mentioned libraries have not finished updating them to work with iOS 5. Consider donating to help speed up development if you can.

Hope this helps some

### Post by Huss on 2012-03-18
Not sure why this is marked as solved . . .

Two solutions:
1) Jailbreak following this guide [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1783715&highlight=iphone]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1783715&highlight=iphone"). I haven't tested this as I don't want to jailbreak
2) Use EZMp3 or FileApp (both free iphone apps) as a temporary solution until gtkpod bugs are sorted out. Follow guide here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=11774578#post11774578]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=11774578#post11774578")
Note: to use Ezmp3 youjust needed to create a folder in the 'managing folders' section of EZmp3 and then will be able to access through nautilus.

Hope this helps
