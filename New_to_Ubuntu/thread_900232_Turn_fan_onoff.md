### Post by gn2 on 2008-09-02
Laptops are simply not designed to be left on for prolonged periods.
Usually the handling of the fans is automatic and they are switched on and off as required depending on the heat.

You can add an applet to the panel to display the heat, it's called sensors-applet and it's available through Synaptic.

When the heat rises the fans should come on and reduce the heat, you can check how this is working by using sensors-applet while you do something CPU intensive like converting an .avi video to dvd .iso

### Post by john test on 2008-09-02
There is a CPU heat sensor that turns the fan on when the heat builds up.  If the fan never comes on you are either in a very cold area or your fan/controller is broken.

you can buy a chill pad to set your laptop on.  The chill pad pulls power from the USB port on your laptop and has fans that you can control with a switch.

I use the chill pad because the laptop internal fans don't do a good enough job of keeping the laptop cool
