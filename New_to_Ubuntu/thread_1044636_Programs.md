I was sort of in your position at one time. Here is what I did.

For the first 6 months or so, I browsed the forums, tried out stuff and generally experimented with ubuntu. I installed wine at some point, but found that my graphics card driver wasn't native to linux, and that reduced power, thus I wasn't able to play any games. In this period I was reinstalling ubuntu once a month or so, but I was learning masses. After that, I started buying machines, and learning more about hardware. Finally, I bought myself another graphics card, and installed all my games again. I found that in the end, I didn't start playing the again for quite a while. You should see ubuntu as a chance to learn more about computers, so I think the best thing for you to do is to try and make your ubuntu installation perfect. In doing so, you should find many gaps in your knowledge. Fill these gaps, and you are making yourself more aware of the world, and less likely to fall pray to the dangers of computers. If you want to, you could learn a programing language. This will make your life much easier in many ways, and give you a better understanding of how your computer works.

You do not want games necessary, but if you do, I suggest having a look at The Mana World. It's open source, so if you decide to learn a language, you could contribute, and feel that warm glow inside.

Best of luck,

Laurie
