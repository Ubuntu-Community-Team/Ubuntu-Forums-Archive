### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2008-05-08
OK, here we go!

1. Right-click on a vacant section of your taskbar.

2. Left click on [COLOR="Blue"]Add to Panel[/COLOR].

3. When the window opens, scroll down to Desktop and Windows.

4. Left click on [COLOR="Blue"]Window List[/COLOR].

5. Left click on [COLOR="Blue"]Add[/COLOR].

6. Close the window.

You should now have a window list section on your taskbar.

Roger :cool:

To the best of my knowledge, that is how it works by default. I know I had accidentally removed the window list from the taskbar once without realising what I was doing... 

You don't have to have the taskbar stretch across the screen if you don't want it to... Click on the taskbar>Properties> de-select "Expand" 

Some people, for whatever reason,(probably aesthetic) might not prefer to dock their open windows to their taskbar, but still want the taskbar across the screen. Possibly they are just used to the placement of the icons on the taskbar (recycling bin, calendar, etc) I think it is a very good thing that you have the freedom to decide this for yourself. Just spend 5 minutes in windoze and you'll see what I mean...just because you don't agree with another users aesthetically releated decisions, this does not make the desktop environment inferior...

IMHO, I don't think it's right to call gnome "stupid crap" just because you don't understand or appreciate the the versatility of the gnome desktop environment. But you know what they say about opinions...
