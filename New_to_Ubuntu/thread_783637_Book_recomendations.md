### Post by kwacka on 2008-05-11
Not included in the extensive list cited by SanjoEl is the 'Rute User Exposition & Treatise'.

Its in the 'multiverse' repository so (provided you have that enabled) a 'sudo apt-get install rutebook' will install HTML & PDF versions at:  /usr/share/doc/rutebook

I am a little confused by the comments regarding books specific to Hardy - I don't see what benefit these might bring to most users, over and above what is already available.

Any book that comes out in the next 6/12 months specifically for Hardy will be the current books with minor changes (e.g. a couple of pages on wubi) rather than written from scratch (consider writing time, re-writing, proof-reading, printing, distribution).

I can recommend Sobell's 'A Practical Guide To Linux' - I bought a copy when it first came out and still find it useful 10 years on.
