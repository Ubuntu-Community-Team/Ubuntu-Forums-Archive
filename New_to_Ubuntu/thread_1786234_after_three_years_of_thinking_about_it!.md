### Post by fooman on 2011-06-19
i don't own a canon printer so no help there...

but installing picasa should be pretty easy,  go here:

[http://picasa.google.com/linux/](http://picasa.google.com/linux/)

click on "download picasa 3 for linux".  on the next page,  click the link for your flavor (deb for ubuntu i386 or deb for amd64)

it should download to your desktop or the /download folder in your home folder

after it installs...move your mouse to the upper left of your screen, and the unity launcher should appear on the left if it is not there already.  click the icon for your home folder

when it opens, look on the left and click "desktop".....see if "picasa_3.0-current..." is listed,  if not...then again,  look on the left and click on "downloads".  

when you find the file in either place....double click on it to install.  the ubuntu software center will open and you will be prompted to install.  click the install button and follow along.

after it installs....click the ubuntu icon in the upper left,  and in the search bar that appears, type: picasa.  then double-click the picasa icon to open.

hope that helps.

There are generally 3 different download types for linux: **.[COLOR=Red]deb[/COLOR] .[COLOR=DeepSkyBlue]rpm[/COLOR] .[COLOR=Lime]sh[/COLOR]**

.[COLOR=Red]Deb[/COLOR]  is the package for [COLOR=Red]Debian[/COLOR] and [COLOR=Red]Debian[/COLOR] based systems (Such as [COLOR=Red]Ubuntu[/COLOR], [COLOR=Red]Linux[/COLOR] [COLOR=Red]Mint[/COLOR])

.[COLOR=DeepSkyBlue]Rpm[/COLOR] is the package for [COLOR=DeepSkyBlue]Redhat[/COLOR], [COLOR=DeepSkyBlue]Fedora[/COLOR], [COLOR=DeepSkyBlue]OpenSuSE[/COLOR] and countless others.

.[COLOR=Lime]Sh[/COLOR] is a type of "universal installer" as it's just a shell script (run from the Terminal) that downloads the files and installs them in [COLOR=Lime]virtually every distribution[/COLOR].
