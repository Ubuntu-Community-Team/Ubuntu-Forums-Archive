### Post by isecore on 2010-07-23
> **cascade9 said:**
> BTW, some AMD moterhboards, you can get a Phenom II X2 (dual core) and 'unlock' them to X4 (quad core). It worked here with the gigabyte above and a Phenom II X2 550.

Doing this is not recommended. Sure, all the Phenoms are essentially four-core CPUs, but they're thoroughly tested during manufacture, and if the cores don't pass the tests they are disabled and the CPU is sold as a dual or triple-core instead.

Unlocking the additional potentially unstable cores is not recommended, especially not for someone who is new to the hardware-scene.
