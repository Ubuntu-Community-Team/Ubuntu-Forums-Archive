### Post by itsols on 2008-12-05
I really don't know how to explain this. Here's what happened...

1. I use my Ubuntu 7.10 laptop in one city and check my google mail.
2. I unplug my RJ45 jack connecting to the ADSL router.
3. After coming back home, I turn on my laptop and Ubuntu starts with a different interface. From what I've learnt and observed so far, this looked (strangely) like a mix of KDE and GNOME.
4. Of course, there were ERROR MESSAGES whenever I tried doing something and everything indicated something to do with GNOME. I'm sorry I don't recall the errors and it never struck me to jot them down :( -
5. I also noticed that although my panels on the top and bottom were visible, I could not use the accelerators (shortcuts keys) like Alt+Ctrl+L, ALt+F1, etc. So I figured gnome was affected.
6. Everytime I tried opening Places > Computer, I got some warning (about gnome having an error) and something about another process may have prevented it from working correctly. What was worse is that I could not see any of my other drives - Windows partition, DVD Combo, etc. Just something that read "File System" and it contained only Ubuntu files.
7. I tried reinstalling gnome manager (I think this is what it was) but the system reported that it was already installed.
8. Finally I RESTARTED the system - a cold boot - and voila! - everything was back to normal. My gnome was back!

I really think this ought to be a bug on Ubuntu but here are my thoughts:

a. I think it is better if KDE can be kept away. Not like I use it or like it anyway.
b. I've posted this so that others new to Ubuntu (like me) will benefit as well.
c. Strangely, I encountered another issue (similar) where another person's gnome panels just went missing. Reinstallation did no good. And the last time he turned on his PC, he had a problem with his ADSL. Thus I just have a feeling that internet issues lead to conflicts with gnome and KDE. Just a thought and I hope a senior Ubuntu user up here has something to say.

Happy ubuntuing!
