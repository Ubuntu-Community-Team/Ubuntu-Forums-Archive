### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-04-11
Take your pick. All the official flavours will work fine on that machine. A lighter flavour, say Xubuntu, would run very well and snappy, but all of them should work fine.

It is always tricky with 'a friend of a friend needs ...' type threads. They can get long-winded and confusing because we ask you a question, you then need to go ask them the question and get back to the forum. Is there some way you can get them to post on this thread and ask the questions they want answered?

Anyway, there's a start. Why don't you download a few ISOs, burn them to create boot media, boot from them and 'Try *buntu without installing'. That way they can try them all and see what fits. When they find that, install.

Presuming you are fairly au fait and up for this? ;)

Final question: the friend's parents have asked your friend or you specifically to find a suitable Linux distro or you or your friend have decided they need a Linux distro? The latter can also be problematic. :)

Welcome. Either of those will give them something that *[I]_appears_*[/I] familiar. What is 'under the hood' and how it operates from day to day, though, is another thing.

Important to remember that *_Ubuntu/Linux is not a drop in replacement for Windows_*. Also important you don't give your parents the impression that it is. There _will_ be a learning curve and it will depend on the user how steep or easy that curve is. 

You should [all read this]("http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm") old chestnut, test Xubuntu and Lubuntu from the 'Try *ubuntu' option (live session that doesn't install anything) from bootable media and then have a think about if Ubuntu is where *they* want to go and if Ubuntu is the right way forward. Good luck. :)

... and be aware that Peppermint is [not an official Ubuntu flavour]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuFlavors") and not supported officially on the main fora here. Just sayin', but no reason not to try it.

Incidentally, the lxde desktop environment is used in Peppermint, the same DE as Lubuntu so not sure what's radically better about Peppermint as they would look very similar I'd guess, but there you go. ;)

[From here]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LXDE").

[QUOTE=Wikipedia]LXDE is the default desktop environment of Knoppix, Lubuntu, LXLE Linux and Peppermint Linux OS among other distributions.[/QUOTE]
