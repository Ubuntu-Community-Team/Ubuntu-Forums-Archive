### Post by DavidOfLondon on 2010-08-26
Full version for this reason - it has far more users and therefore you'll get far more help from others when you inevitably run into a wall.

The required specs to run the full version are so low you could almost run Ubuntu 10.04 on a 1970s calculator, so why bother with a netbook version which doesn't have the same level of support nor the same level of functionality.

A Dell Mini will have no problems running Ubuntu 10.04 whatsoever. I use a 4 year old HP 6730s with intel and no fanciness and my Ubuntu OS uploads in... no kidding here....28 seconds.

As for hyper-powering your Dell Mini...seriously, forget this whole "MORE POWER!" thing - it's a complete con. The obsession we have with upgrading today is based on completely overlooking our fundamental usage of a personal computer. Every single song I have for my iPod still hasn't filled my 8GB first generation, so why the hell would I want a 32GB one?! Same applies with PC specs - why in God's name would you ever want more than 250GB or anything above a 2Ghz processor unless you're designing wargames for the Russians?

Your Dell will play most games just fine (on your windows OS), it'll let you have all your music, all your text files, let you run powerful software, and it'll last a long time unless you throw it into the sea. Look, people upgrade because they have been told that's the thing that everyone is doing. I only have an iPhone (which I use around the house if I can't be bothered to turn on the computer) because I was given it for free - I've used the same nokia phone for 5 years now so why "upgrade"  - it's a phone! It makes phone calls!

The reason I left windows is I see no reason why I needed it on a daily basis. I have a truly ancient second laptop if I ever want to play games or online poker and that does just fine.

With Ubuntu I can do whatever I want knowing it'll never crash and never freeze and it isn't absolutely jam-packed with a billion files and programs I have never used nor WILL ever use.

28 seconds....

:)

David.
