It's not that you're too dumb. It's that these things don't work in Linux (yet). The Zune won't likely work with Linux because it's designed by Microsoft to only work with their (Windows) software.

Netflix is a little more hopeful. It uses Microsoft Silverlight (along with a special DRM plugin) right now, but there's no reason it can't work on Linux. Netflix just has to decide to allow it.

As for VBox, I've heard that it is capable of booting from a real disk, but that's definitely not standard procedure. I don't remember where I found the forum where somebody did it, but look around.

To ease the transition, consider dual-booting for now (Vista and Ubuntu on separate hard drive partitions). In the long term, you might want a more open MP3 player. Also, Amazon Prime streaming works on Linux, but I have a feeling Netflix will be here soon.
