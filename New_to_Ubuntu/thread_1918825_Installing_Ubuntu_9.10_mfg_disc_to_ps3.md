### Post by Double.J on 2012-02-01
Hi there and welcome to the forums!

When the PS3 was released this was possible as Sony allowed another OS to dual boot on the system. This feature was however removed in a security update and is no longer permitted by Sony. (I assume over fears of the feature being misused to circumvent licencing instead of any disposition against open source OS's!)

From a technical point, Linux and BSD will run on original PS3's as they were designed with compatibility in mind. However since the firmware update, installing the OS would require a procedure called "jailbreaking" (deliberately exploiting flaws in Sony's firmware to overcome the patch.) Jailbreaking is against Sony's EULA (and possibly illegal - continuing lawsuits) and as such we cannot discuss such matters on the forums sorry - Ubuntu does not officially support the PS3 platform. 

Sorry I couldn't be of more use
