### Post by enkay009 on 2011-10-27
The configuration for lightdm (the new login manager) is here: /etc/lightdm.

In that directory you'll find lightdm.conf.

This is what the my config looks like. It's more or less out-of-the-box except it logs me in on boot. If you want that, you'll need to replace the ME in "autologin-user=ME" with your username. 

```
[SeatDefaults]
autologin-guest=false
autologin-user=ME
autologin-user-timeout=0
autologin-session=lightdm-autologin
greeter-session=unity-greeter
user-session=unity
```

There's other configuration files in here too, including one to configure how the login manager looks (unity-greeter.conf). But one thing at a time, you need to be able to login first right? :)

### Post by enkay009 on 2011-10-27
Sorry mine was wrong. I previously had it to something else and changed it. Yes it should be "ubuntu" not "unity".

However just for clarification... the dots in dpi refers to pixels not the dots the greeter print. You probably should revert that 96.  

The choices you see when you click the wheel are other window managers that were installed on your machine. You can probably remove them if you no longer plan to use them.

Whirring of disks and long delays are probably because of your hardware. Increasing RAM and/or installing an SSD can help.

But if you don't want to upgrade your hardware, you can install lighter window managers that consume less RAM, use less CPU cycles and are therefore faster. XCFE and LXDM being two popular choices.
