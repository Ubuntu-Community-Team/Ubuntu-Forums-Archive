### Post by craig10x on 2013-07-06
Firefox uses the adobe flash that was installed on your ubuntu system when you installed the ubuntu restricted extras package...adobe isn't making newer versions of flash for linux anymore and all you get is security updates on that old flash version which is about 2 years old now, so you can't really get newer versions of that for firefox...

However, if you switch to google chrome (not the chromium in the software center but the one on google's chrome website) that has it's own flash plug installed called "pepper flash" and for that, Google has an agreement with adobe to supply the latest flash versions for the pepper plug in, so that's a good way around the outdated flash problem....

Firefox does not have such an agreement with adobe and i don't know if they have any plans to get around that problem but as i said, one way around it to switch to Google Chrome instead...it's what i use and it has become my favorite browser...:D

Chrome also has a nifty built in pdf reader for websites that have pdf files and also adds a ppa updater to your software sources so that you get the latest upgraded and new versions of Chrome automatically and very promptly in your software updater...
