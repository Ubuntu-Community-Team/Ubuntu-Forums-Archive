### Post by shaggy999 on 2008-10-29
There's a bunch of questions related to the release so I'll try to answer them all right here:

Q: What time will the new release be for 8.10 on October 30th?
A: The time is not set exactly, but they should start uploading to mirrors in the arly AM. Chances are by the time you wake up in the morning it will be available. **SIDE NOTE:** If you see early links to the isos before the official launch PLEASE DO NOT DOWNLOAD THEM!! The isos may change and the servers need maximum bandwidth for mirroring before the general public gets to them. In short, trying to download early can cause delays for others and may result in an incomplete or outdated iso!

Q: Can I upgrade Ubuntu from Hardy (8.04) to Intrepid (8.10)?
A: Yes. Make sure that you have your system set to upgrade to development distributions (not sure if that's the right word) and not just long term releases (LTR). You will receive a message something along the lines of "A new distribution is available! Upgrade now!"

Q: If I install the Release Candidate (RC) can I upgrade to the final?
A: Yes. You'll see package updates and possibly a message similiar to the one in my answer to the previous question.

Q: Best way to get the new CD images?
A: BitTorrent is the way to go. It relieves stress on the servers and will probably give you a faster download than downloading directly.
