### Post by I2k4 on 2011-03-23
They all have similar guts and most are reliable, but they differ quite a lot in keyboard layout and feel, and screen quality.  My first gen Acer runs XP and Lubuntu quite snappily (knock wood) 2.5 years on - can't speak to and don't want W7.  

Since you are used to a machine, I really suggest you go to a store and try to get a feel -  how about the screen and how about keyboard and touchpad (if you use it) and the location of the ports and sockets.  Those are more important than the brand I'd say.

### Post by sleepingdragon on 2011-03-23
I've been doing a lot of rework on Samsungs - N120, N150, N160, N210 and the old NC10. It mostly works pretty well, though some have problems with the screen brightness. The NC10 needs the lastest BIOS upgrade then every works with the samsung-tools PPA. the N150s and N160s do very well, and the N210 is a speedy little performer, but work is still needed on the brightness controls.

Samsungs are fickle things, but apart from getting the Fn keys to do their thing, Samsung make a very competent netbook. If you're into a lot of typing/big fan of the command-line, then the Samsung keyboards are very comfortable (93% size). 

One thing I did notice is that the Samsungs freeze up with the Desktop Effects enabled. It only happens after a few minutes, but disabling this feature keeps them stable.
