### Post by soupowl on 2008-12-06
If you are new to Linux/Ubuntu and want to buy a mobile dongle that works as a 'plug and play', I have just got a E169 from '3' Mobile, & had printed out lots of complicated instructions.  I was looking forward to the challenge of getting it to work, but hey presto!  Up came a dialogue telling me exactly what to do, and within 10 seconds I was on the internet.  Meanwhile my husband was setting his up with his Microsoft OS, and took 4 times as long - it suggested he go and make a cup of tea while he was waiting!!
So, if you're new to Ubuntu, and want mobile broadband, that's my recommendation.  Don't expect it to be as quick as normal broadband though, if you're using a website that it rich with pictures and so on, but it's fine for ordinary stuff....... I like Last FM, and the music from that seems to come in second long bursts which suggests that Mobile Broadband isn't suitable for listening to music on the net, but I'll try it in other locations first.  Good luck!
