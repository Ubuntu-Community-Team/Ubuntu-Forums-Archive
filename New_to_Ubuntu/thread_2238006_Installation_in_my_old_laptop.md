### Post by MoebusNet on 2014-08-05
I'm using Lubuntu 14.04.1 on my old notebook with similar specifications. I would recommend trying out the different versions of Ubuntu (Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, Lubuntu) as a Live-USB before attempting an install to see which desktop environment and responsiveness best suits your and your hardware. Just remember that Live-USB will be slower than a HDD install and to back up any data you want to keep. 32-bit may be slightly faster on older hardware, but the differences would be minimal.

### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-08-05
For the ACER Aspire One D150-1Bw, an ATOM based netbook, always 32-bit regardless of the flavor. 
You'll notice a knee-jerk reaction from many people here steering you away from the standard Ubuntu. However, let me first tell you it works and that's from experience. Many people without such experience will immediately suggest other because (quoting) "there's no way for Unity to work with such flimsy hardware, especially the RAM limitation" but the truth is they don't like Unity and they take every opportunity to steer people away from it.

That said, it's also true that you'll have a snappier OS with the less demanding desktop environments like the one in Xubuntu and specially Lubuntu. It's up to you to decide whether or not those 2-3 seconds delay when searching for an app or content is that important. Unity is the most productive DE of all, including those of Windows 8.x or MacOS. The current paradigm points to a focus on using the apps and access personal contents instead of the "old way" which is digging trough menus and submenus. It's up to you to decide whether it is important for YOU - and no one else - or not.
