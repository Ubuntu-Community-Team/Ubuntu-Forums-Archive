### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-13
Wifi security and web browsing privacy are completely different things for the most part. To secure your browsing TOR is a free proxy program that can anonymize your surfing by bouncing the traffic through multiple proxy servers on its way to where its going thereby blocking both your ISP from seeing where the traffic ends up and the end recipient from seeing where it came from. It will also slow down your internet browsing speed. As for Wifi just use the strongest encryption you can get to work WEP at a minimum and WPA if it works for you. Just remember to pick strong passwords and be sure to set your routers password if you haven't already.


Hope this gets you on the right track

PS Wifi signals only travel very limited distances to a AP (access point) at which time they become like any other hardwire communications. So I really don't think the "anywhere on earth" applies since your wifi at the very best transmits a few miles and that would only be with a high power wifi card and a directional upgraded antennae, and yours probably isn't visible more than 200-300 meters away. The rest of the Earth would be clueless as to your wifi transmissions :-)
