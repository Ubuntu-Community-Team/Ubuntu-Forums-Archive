### Post by pythonscript on 2010-06-01
For hardware, I would suggest looking here first:

[http://www.ubuntuhcl.org/](http://www.ubuntuhcl.org/)

As far as anti-virus goes, many Linux users, myself among them, consider it unnecessary, as the system is designed to greatly restrict default user rights so that any possible damage and/or infestation of a virus is quite difficult to achieve. 

I've found 
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index)
to be helpful in learning the basics about Linux, choosing which flavor of Ubuntu, to use, etc. Also, (I say this with complete sincerity) searching the man pages with

man -k *keyword* or apropos *keyword* are good ways to begin searching for commands (as well as google) and the man pages and the forums can provide you with more direct information from there. 

Hope this helps! Welcome to Ubuntu and the forums!
