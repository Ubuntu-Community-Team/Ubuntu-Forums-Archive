Clicking on the context menu item 'edit menus' of the default Main Menubar that comes with Ubuntu (Hardy) opens a window that shows only Applications and System. Why is there no way to edit the Places menu there? Is it a bug or an intentional thing or can I include it there somehow?

### Post by Bakon Jarser on 2008-09-02
I don't know.  I do know how to add things to it and you can get rid of a few things.  Open nautilus.  You can drag folders (possibly files too, I haven't tried) from the right side to the left panel and they will show up under places.  Anything you remove from the left side will not show up there, but not all things are removable.

### Post by sdennie on 2008-09-02
Right.  Places is a dynamic menu that is generated by various parts of the system (I think mostly nautilus and hal).  The closest you can get to editing Places is to open nautilus and then go to Bookmarks->Edit Bookmarks.
