### Post by Sandra Meredith on 2013-05-23
[COLOR=#111111][FONT=Book Antiqua]There are at least three OSCOLA styles for Zotero, which have been adapted by Virgilio Afonso da Silva (University of Sao Paulo), Thora Gylfadottir (Reykjavik University) and Paul Troop (Oxford University). The most complex style is that developed by Frank Bennett (Nagoya University), and provided in Multilingual Zotero (MLZ). The MLZ version allows reformatting from OSCOLA to American Law, McGill, New Zealand Law Review and Chicago, and extensive documentation is available to help users. For the latter see  [/FONT][/COLOR]http://[citationstylist.org/tools/#styles]("http://citationstylist.org/tools/#styles")[COLOR=#111111][FONT=Book Antiqua]. For more information about using OSCOLA with Zotero, see [/FONT][/COLOR][http://ejlt.org//article/view/190/284](http://ejlt.org//article/view/190/284)
