### Post by silver-grey on 2011-07-27
I've been running Ubuntu 9.10 for the past two years, and recently got around to updating to Ubuntu 10.04.  Since doing this, I have noticed a number of small glitches with my computer, including
-- cannot grab keyboard / malicious program may be listening messages
-- adblock plus filter subscription wiped after restarting firefox
-- appearance / skin altering randomly
-- file viewer unresponsive
These glitches normally occur during / after downloads, after inserting a jumpdrive, or after performing updates.  My computer is old (HP Pavilion dv6000) and I use it fairly heavily.  Because of these factors, I acknowledge that the problem may simply be my overloading the computer / accidentally interrupting processes.  However, my activity was similar while running 9.10 and I did not have such problems.  Despite running ubuntu for two years, I still consider myself a newbie.  Any advice would be appreciated, thank you.

### Post by redbikemaster on 2011-07-27
I agree. For some reason whenever I try to do an upgrade it wreaks havoc on my system. I mean, GUI is trashed, everything. Probably my combo of hardware or something. I always end up doing exactly what lkjoel said.

Also, a good thing to do is to keep your OS on a 20 GB or so primary (or master) drive and everything else on a nice, fat secondary (or slave) drive, and just configure your system (I believe you'd need to configure a file, fstab?) to automatically mount it on login. and direct your home, pictures, and everything else to the secondary drive. You'd have to ask around on how to do that if you don't know, because I don't know how to do all that.
