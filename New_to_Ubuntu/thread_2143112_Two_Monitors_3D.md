### Post by DuckHook on 2013-05-07
3D is the spinning icons, desktop cubes, transparent windows/title bars and other eye-candy that is associated with Unity in its latest incarnations. They add to appearance but not to functionality. 12.04 is the last version that supports good old 2D by default, if your HW is not really powerful enough to run 3D. All later versions only have 3D unless you completely change the desktop environment.

It is impossible to tell if your setup will run 3D because not enough info. The bottleneck is not your monitors, but your video card. A reasonably powerful video card will easily run two monitors of your resolutions. An old video card will not. In my opinion, there is nothing wrong with 2D and I actually much prefer it in my older boxes. It is fast, responsive and not distracting, and I am so happy to have my old HW running quickly again that the lack of 3D is not remotely a consideration. However, if you have the horsepower, then the 3D is admittedly nice.

If you want to pursue the 3D matter, then please provide all of the HW info that you can. We will especially need to know make model of video card, make model of CPU, amount of VRAM and amount of DRAM. Also, tell us if monitors are hooked up by VGA, DVI, HDMI or DisplayPort.
