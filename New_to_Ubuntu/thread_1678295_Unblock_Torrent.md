### Post by wormyblackburny on 2011-01-31
There are a few different ways that Universities block torrent traffic, generally the "easy" way is to block the commonly used ports for the popular filesharing programs like limewire. The other is to block access to the common torrent sites that you can find torrents downloads at. If you contact the University's helpdesk and provide a "legit" reason that you need to use torrents, chances are they can assign you a static IP and unblock the ports. I will tell you this: everything you do on a University's network is traceable. I have personally witnessed my network guys trace a stolen laptop within 3 feet of its location when it was connected to WIRELESS (regardless of whether you mask/change your MAC). If you are scared to ask for permission, chances are you are doing something you shouldn't be. Don't risk disciplinary action from your school....
