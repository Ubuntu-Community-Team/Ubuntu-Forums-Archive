Hello there :)

Zune, hmm. It might work on Linux. After all, the iPood does.

Though the iPod is much more popular hence pretty good Linux support. :)
I found this: (Not sure if it still applies)

[quote="Luinfana"]
he Zune is an amazing little media device. I'm an avid Linux fan and an avid Zune fan - that seems to be pretty rare.

Your Zune isn't going to mount under Linux at all (show up in /media/, etc), because it's not a normal mass storage device, It's designed to use Microsoft's Media Transfer Protocol to send and recieve data over its USB connection. There has been some progress using libmtp to communicate with the Zune, but files can only be read or deleted. Write-access (when the Zune displays "connected" on the screen) is not enabled until the Zune recieves a secret passkey from the Zune software, and thus Linux-based media managers (amarok, rhythmbox, etc) can't really manage the Zune and you can't browse it like a normal drive (for now, at least). 

[/quote]

### Post by jimmy the saint on 2009-05-02
I bought a sanza fuse for music.  Apparently the video is picky and I'm not sure if anyone figured that out yet.  It also works well as a drag and drop device.  Plus it has microSD support, so you can swap cards if you feel like it, bring some extra music or video (if you get video to work).  Definitely worth it for music, not so much for video. It will work with rhythmbox too if you a ".is_audio_player" to the device.
