### Post by steeldriver on 2014-05-26
I think the thinking is that since every directory has a . and .. the extra information provided by 'ls -a' over 'ls -A' is moot - I can count on the fingers of one finger the number of times I have been interested in whether a directory contains a .. (HINT: / and the answer is yes)

But there's nothing to stop you changing it if you wish
