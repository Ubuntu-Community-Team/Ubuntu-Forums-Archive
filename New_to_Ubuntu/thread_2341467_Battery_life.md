### Post by Impavidus on 2016-10-29
Hardware manufacturers don't always provide drivers for Linux and don't always give enough information to the open source community to write their own high-quality drivers, so on Linux you're sometimes stuck with poorer drivers than on Windows. These drivers don't always put your hardware into power saving mode when the Windows drivers would. So, depending on your hardware, battery life may be poorer, or it may be just as good. Usually the difference isn't very big. Drivers tend to improve over time, so the most problems can be expected on brand new hardware. Maybe someone has experience with your hardware.
