### Post by laidback on 2009-11-24
Within OpenOffice:-

You could try Ctrl+Shift+j (That is press and hold Ctrl button, now also press and hold the Shift button (Big up arrow just above Ctrl button) now press j (note lower case so make sure you don't have Caps lock on)), release all buttons. This is the OpenOffice instruction to View Full Screen and toggles back and forth, i.e. if you're in View Full Screen mode it'll take you back to normal view and if you're in normal view it'll take you to full screen view. 

I'm hoping that this will reveal the panel bars at the top and bottom of your screen.


Still in OpenOffice:-
You may also see a Box in the Top Left of your screen which says "Full Screen", press that and you go back to what I would call the normal view, the one I assume you are looking for.

On the other hand your problem could be something else entirely.

An idea:-
Have you a button on your physical screen which allows you to adjust the display, I have one called Auto which puts the picture nicely in the centre of the screen, sometimes things get out of sinc causing the display to be offset perhaps enough for you not to see the top of the dispay at all. By pressing Auto everything comes back in line.
