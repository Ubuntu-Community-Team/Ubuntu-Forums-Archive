### Post by Didius Falco on 2009-05-07
> **Yed Ied said:**
> as small as they are on a Mac.  I don't care for icons on my desktop, but when they are I don't like them as large as the smallest I've been able to make them.  Ubuntu 9.04- PC HP 1125- one Gig- two HDD, Ubuntu and XP Pro.

Open **Nautilus**. The easy way is to go to **Places/Desktop**. This will open **Nautilus** - on the menus at the top go to **Edit/Preferences** and change you **Icon View Defaults **Default Zoom Level to your desired setting.

This will change the Icon size on your desktop and in Nautilus if you routinely use Icon view for folders.

I hope this helps...

Regards,

Didius

On Edit: You can also right-click individual icons and select Stretch, which will put little squares in the corners that you can grab and use to resize. I routinely do this for PDF files I want to leave on my desktop for a while.
