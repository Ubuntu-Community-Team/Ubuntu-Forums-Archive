### Post by hellomoto on 2008-07-15
OMG - it finally happened!

I love ubuntu and part of the fun for me has been working around problems and the joys of when I get stuff working that "should only work with windows".


However today was the first ever time I had hardware Plug & Play out the box.

I saw an old bluetooth adapter in a old box of computer parts in my house today, I didn't have anything to do so I thought:

What the hell, I will plug it into my ubuntu box and see what happens.

Now I know ubuntu's slogan is linux for human beings (but if we are honest that isnt always the case).
Anyway I pluged in this bluetooth adapter and it was detected and installed in about 5 seconds. I was mega impressed!
Then I tryed to send a file from my phone and it did it first time! - I was amazed.
Knowing this works with linux it gives me alot more wireless options for connecting hardware.

In case people are interested:
OS: Ubuntu Hardy 8.04LTS
Bluetooth adapter: Belkin bluetooth USB adapter 
Model number: F8T003 ver.3
