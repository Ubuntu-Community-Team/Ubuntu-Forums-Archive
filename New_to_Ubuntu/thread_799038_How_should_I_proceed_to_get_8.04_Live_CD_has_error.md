### Post by RequinB4 on 2008-05-18
If you can wait, just burn a 8.04 CD when you can - less potential cause for trouble.  If you feel you must start now, it shouldn't matter bug-wise which one you pick, but you may have to go from 7.04 to 7.10 to 8.04.

### Post by Mythrandil on 2008-05-18
Always burn the ISOs at the slowest write speed, I've found that the live cd's are prone to errors at higher speeds.

---

### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-05-18
You could do 7.04, but that would take a lot of time to do.  If you do, make sure you install the distro upgrade and not the 7.04 upgrades.  There should be significantly more than 100 there, and that would waste a lot of time.

But the easier, quicker, and probably less likely to have problems is to just wait and burn another copy.
