### Post by Gitanes on 2010-11-27
This is something that I wonder about too; with regards to getting a new computer anytime in the future.  I'm now using Ubuntu as my daily OS on laptop and desktop.  Both have got ATI graphics cards in them, the desktop's card is older and that was the first thing I had trouble with when I installed 10.4. It now works fine, after upgrading the kernel to the latest.  But it seems that ATI doesn't provide very good support for their older cards when it comes to supporting Linux.  It seems this may be a company to keep away from.

Also, you might consider getting a computer sold with Ubuntu pre-installed.  I would expect everything to work right out of the box with companies that specialize in such computers.

### Post by WienerWuerstel on 2010-11-27

Most Blu-Rays should work on GNU/Linux too. But there could be some exceptions.

Also always remember to look at the Ubuntu Forums and Wiki for more Information on which Hardware is GNU/Linux friendly.
