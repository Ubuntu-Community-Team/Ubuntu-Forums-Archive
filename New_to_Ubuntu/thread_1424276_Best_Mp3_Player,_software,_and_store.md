### Post by veko on 2010-03-08
Amarok 1.4 gets my vote as the best music player. The latest version is 2.2.something and 1.4 is no longer supported by the developers, but it's still the best. There are couple of 1.4 forks though, Pana and Clementine, but I haven't tested these. I also must admit that I don't know how these work with music stores.

Exaile is rather similar to Amarok and it might be rather good too. 

The thing I like most in Amarok is the playlist: it contains only the files you drag and drop there. You can browse either through collections or through "physical" folders and files, and add files to playlist, but this does not happen automatically, as in many other players. In many players the playlist contains the results of the last search. It's automatically modified (messed up, in my opinon) by the last search, and it becomes difficult to select several songs that have different performer/disk/whatever. Of course you can make whatever kind of playlist you want, but Amarok avoids all this hassle.

### Post by Robertjm on 2010-04-03
Songbird has dropped linux development so you can scratch that one off your list unless you want to install a player that won't be moving forward.

Rythmbox has support for several music stores (jamendo, magnatunes and the new UbuntuOne). The interface is not the most polished, but you can drag items to your media device.

Amarok 2.x is the longest running one with a gui. However, when they switched from v1.4.x to v2.x the interface was radically changed and is really hard to use, compared to the older v1.4.x.

There are a couple of forks of Amarok v1.4.x (Pana and Clementine). When I read their wiki it made it sound like mtp device support in Clementine was "coming" which is funny since it was a fork of a program that already had support built in. Pana seems to pretty much took off where official development of Amarok v1.4.x stopped. However, I honestly, don't remember any music store support in v1.4.x, which could be a downer if you're looking for a program with integrated store support.

Good luck!

Robert
