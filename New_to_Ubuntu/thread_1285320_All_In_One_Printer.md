### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2009-10-09
One needs to remember that the reason why there are occasionally problems with printers isn't because Linux is lacking.

The reason is because manufacturers often don't release drivers for Linux.
The most notable manufacturer for this is Lexmark.

However, things are getting better all of the time.

What I suggest is that you go to this site and look up any printer that you may be interested in and see if it is compatible.

[COLOR="Blue"]http://www.openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi[/COLOR]

I agree with some of the previous statements that the benchmark for Linux compatibility is HP.

The reason for this is that HP actively support Linux and release drivers for their printers.
