### Post by JCollierDavis on 2010-04-27
I switched from Firefox to Google Chromium.  There's a feature that will sync your bookmarks across all instances of the browser across any machine.  I've installed and re-installed both the OS and Chromium multiple times and never lost any bookmarks.  Plus it's uploaded to your Google Docs and you can access them from any other computer.
I'm on the 32 bit / Dev channel located The Linux download is near the bottom of the page at: 
[http://www.chromium.org/getting-involved/dev-channel](http://www.chromium.org/getting-involved/dev-channel)
 
The feature may be avaliable in the Beta channel as well, but I've never had any reason to stop using the dev one as it seems to work well.  Get the Beta here:
[http://www.google.com/chrome?platform=linux](http://www.google.com/chrome?platform=linux)
 
 
On either the Page or Wrench menu in the upper right corner, you'll find an entry for Sync Bookmarks.  You have to log-in to a Google account for it to work.
 
Of course, I'd test it to make sure.  Go to Google Docs and check to see if it's uploaded; or run Chromium on another machine, or uninstall/reinstall it and see if it picks back up.
