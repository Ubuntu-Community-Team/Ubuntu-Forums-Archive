### Post by stderr on 2009-11-04
You'll need compizconfig-settings-manager installed 

```
sudo apt-get install compizconfig-settings-manager
```

Then goto System->Preferences->CompizConfig... 

Under "Desktop", enable "Desktop Cube" and "Rotate Cube". 

Finally, on your panel, locate your workspace viewer (shows all desktops), right click, Properties, and change columns to 4.

### Post by The Funkbomb on 2009-11-04
First thing you need to do is install CompizConfig Settings Manager.  It's in the repositories.

Once installed, go to System>Preferences CompizConfig Settings Manager.

A word of warning, the default settings don't play well with the cube.  If you follow these steps, you will most likely get a warning saying you can't have Desktop Wall enabled with the Cube.

Click on the General Menu on the left and then click over to Desktop Size.  For the cube, you need to move horizontal over to 4.  You now have four desktops to work with.

Click back and go to All.  Click on Desktop Cube.  Just click the check box for now.  If you don't like the settings, you can go back in and mess with the options.  I tend to like the default.

Click back and check off Rotate Cube.  I'm pretty sure I use the defaults on this too.  Again, if it's not working for you, you can click and go in to mess with the settings.

Click back and click on Cube Reflection and Deformation.  Check it off and go into the settings for the option.  Click on the Deformation tab and set Deformation to None.  This will give you a cube.  As you can see there is also a sphere and a cylinder setting.

Click back and close.  That should get you the basic cube.  There are three ways that I know of to spin the cube.spin the cube.  On the desktop, if you hold the middle mouse button and move to the left or right, it will spin.  Holding Control-alt and left mouse button will spin the cube even with a program blocking the desktop view.  You can also hold Control-alt and the left or right button as the keyboard shortcut but as far as I know, the cube will not zoom out.  It will just switch the face.

There are a lot of options in there.  You might also like 3d windows.  What this will do is when you spin the cube using the mouse methods, you'll see the windows you have open separated from the face of the cube.

Play around.  Not really anything dangerous in there.
