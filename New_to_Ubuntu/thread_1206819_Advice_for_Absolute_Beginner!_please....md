### Post by donkyhotay on 2009-07-07
> **sXeChris said:**
> 9.04 Jaunty Jackalope, get 32-bit no matter what because of compatibility issues. -sXeChris

I completely disagree, even back in the edgy/feisty era the only major problems I had with 64bit was flash compatibility. Thats been resolved with the past few versions and works 'out of the box' (as well as the 32bit versions at least). If you have a 64bit system with > 3GB of RAM use the 64bit version. I doubt you'll encounter compatibility issues (32-bit backwards compatibility works better on ubuntu then it does on windows) and no 32bit OS's can't access 4GB or higher of RAM (limitation of 32bit that affects linux, windows, osX, BSD, etc.). Using 32bit on a *new* computer these days is like buying a race car and then removing the ability to shift higher then 2nd gear. Yeah it works but you aren't using it to it's full potential.
