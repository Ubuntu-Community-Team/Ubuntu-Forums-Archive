### Post by CEB2nd on 2008-07-14
Before condemning the old CRT monitor to the dumpster
I would make certain the monitor is the actual problem.  Bad
video cards, bad drivers, loose or defective cables,
interference from other devices, improper setup can all
cause this type of behavior.  Try this monitor on another
computer to see if the problem follows it.

FWIW, do you know anyone with experience repairing
electronic devices?  The majority of problems with
CRT monitors are easy to repair, bad solder joints or defective
electrolytic capacitors - capacitors are a VERY common problem and
easy to find if the tech has an ESR (equivalent series
resistance) meter. 

Warning: Do not try to repair this
monitor yourself if you aren't experienced in this
field - an old monitor isn't worth getting killed for.

Good luck.
