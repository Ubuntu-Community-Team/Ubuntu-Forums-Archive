### Post by zealibib slaughter on 2014-02-15
You have your choice here. Thunderbird is pretty standard but can get heavy, evolution is nice, Geary is nice and light.  Any will do what you want. I suggest instead of taking the default (thunderbird?) email client thats installed in your setup google for a client with the features you want and compare. That way you will get exactly what you are happy with which is of course the linux way :)

You can search in (what ever they call the sidebar now) for your email client (thunderbird) and start that set up should be very close to what you would find in outlook.  If you get lost after you start it you can google how to set up email provider in thunderbird and find official and non official help guides which are really good. Maybe someone who uses unity will come by and tell you how to place an email notification icon somewhere but it should be really easy and done with a right click near the clock I guess.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-02-15
Open the Dash and search for Thunderbird. Or press Super + A and then under the Installed heading press See xx more results. Or Click Filter results and select Internet. Look for the Thunderbird Mail icon.

In Ubuntu we call the Windows key the Super key. Oh, by the way, do not expect Windows applications to run in Ubuntu. Not without taking special measures and then do not expect perfect results.
