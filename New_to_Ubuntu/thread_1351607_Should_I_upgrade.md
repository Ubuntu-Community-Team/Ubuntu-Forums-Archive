### Post by paradigm2 on 2009-12-10
Important or recommended updates are usually a good idea.  In my opinion I don't like to do distribution upgrades (9.04 -> 9.10, etc) and instead prefer doing a clean install.

One thing to keep in mind is that even in a worse case scenario where your entire system gets hosed due to an upgrade and for some reason normal recovery does not work, unless you are using encryption [and even then if you took proper precautions you can still recover things usually] it should be pretty easy to pop in a ubuntu live cd and copy all your important files to a usb flash drive (etc) and then reinstall.  Better yet would be keeping a separate backup of your /home ready to go.

That way you don't need to worry so much... The security and recommended updates often fix critical security problems as well as issues that may say do things like corrupt your data or cause crashes.  You generally don't want to avoid these.  At the most you might wait a day and look at the forums to see if people are reporting problems prior to updating.
