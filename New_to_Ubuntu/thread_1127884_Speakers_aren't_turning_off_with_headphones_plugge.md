### Post by amingv on 2009-04-17
I've had this problem before with many computers running Linux, and though I've searched quite a lot I haven't found a definite answer, but there's two things that can work:
When you open your volume manager there should be a section that says "Switches" and there there may or may not be an option called "Headphone sensor" (or something like that, I can't quite remember), if it's unmarked then mark it.

If it's not there (or if it happens to you as it happened to me with a VAIO laptop, that even with the switch activated the sound comes out), you can just mute "Front" and leave "PCM" unmuted, that way the sound should come from the earphones and not the speakers, though it's a burden if you plug-unplug them often.

On a much less common case, it could be a problem in the headphones/headphone jack.

I hope somebody can provide you with a better answer, but maybe this can help you in the meantime...
