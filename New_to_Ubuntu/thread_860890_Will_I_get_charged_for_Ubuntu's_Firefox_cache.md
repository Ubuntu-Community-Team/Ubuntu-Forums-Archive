### Post by anewguy on 2008-07-16
Regarding cache size, the speed of your connection actually has no bearing on the cache size, nor should it.  Cache is searched each time you access something on the net. The larger the cache, the slightly higher the odds the page will be found there, but with a trade off:  a larger cache means a longer local search time before a download of the page/picture/info will be started from the internet if needed.

If you shrink cache, or in particular clear it, your access will appear faster as it will go directly to the net - but then it has to download the entire page/picture/text from the net.

There used to be a general rule for clearing cache - a practical limit in terms of local search times versus actually accessing a page which has changed.  I don't know if such a thing exists anymore, but it is still wise to clear your cache regularly.  I try for a minimum of once a week.  Of course, that depends on how often and how long you are on the net.

### Post by Tony Flury on 2008-07-17
dgkalmegh - i think a lot of people have already answered your question.

to repeat and summarise : if you get a limited download from your ISP (say 4GB per month) then that will apply to any browser or O/S you use. 

All browsers need to download the content to your pc so it can be displayed, so if you watch a 1Mb video on You tube, IE7 on windows will download 1Mb, and Firefox on linux will download 1Mb, 
They will probably both cache that content, so your cache on both systems will grow by 1Mb approximately.

Both systems will still download information from the net that it needs, and your ISP will have no idea how big your cache is.

As other people have pointed out actually letting your cache grow might actually make you download less, if you visit the same sites repeatedly, as the whole point of the cache is to save you having to download content that you already have.

A smaller cache (especially if you have a fast connection) may make your browsing quicker, since your browser has less to search through on your Hard drive.
