### Post by The Real Dave on 2010-05-05
You'll need to rip them off on Windows using a ripper similar to DVD:Rip. Persoanlly I use DVD Cloner. After that, you need to encode the raw video folders into a suitable video file, such as an .m4v. Handbrake is the best tool for the job there. After that, you can throw the .m4v on a flash drive and watch it whereever you want. 

So it wasn't the worst advice.
