### Post by Frogbarf on 2008-12-13
> **mysticanonymous said:**
>  . . . ubuntu will not burn to a disc . . .

Are you sure you burned the disk correctly? When you download Ubuntu, what you get is a .iso file that has all the individual directories and files in one swell foop. If you burn that, all you've got is a CD with a single .iso file on it, not much use except as backup. Most burning software has an option to handle .iso files and burn a CD with the directories and files unwrapped out of the .iso file.

This is a very elementary mistake but it might be your problem. I know when I went to install Ubuntu on a new machine, I just used Infrarecorder on an old Win98 box, downloaded the .iso file, did a checksum thingie on it, burnt it to CD, and when I booted from the CD on the new machine, bingo! here came Ubuntu in all its glory.
