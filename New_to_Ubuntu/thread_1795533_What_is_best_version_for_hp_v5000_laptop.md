### Post by cariboo on 2011-07-02
Your best bet would be to create a Live CD of 10.04, you can get the iso here:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download)

Use a free program like [imgburn]("http://www.imgburn.com/") to burn the image to CD. Then boot from the CD to make sure everything works.

Once you've made sure everything works, use the install beside option. This will resize you xp partition to make room for the Ubuntu install.

### Post by nzjethro on 2011-07-02
Is a V5000 an older laptop with low specs? If yes (less than 2GB RAM), you could consider one of the lightweight DEs like Xfce (Xubuntu) or LDXE (Lubuntu). Check out those two variants for better performance.
As for which release, I'd say 10.04 LTS (Lucid Lynx). I'm currently using 10.10, with no troubles, but it goes EOL April next year, while 10.04 is supported until 2013. 11.04 needs some time to mature in my opinion, and with an older laptop your performance would be reduced.

### Post by lkraemer on 2011-07-02
I am running Ubuntu 8.04 LTS on my Compaq V5201US and it has the Broadcom Wifi
Hardware.  You most likely will need the Broadcom firmware to get your Wifi
functional.  The following posting will explain what I did to get the Firmware.

Here is my posting:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1470146](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1470146)

If I were you I'd have at least 1 Gig RAM (or 2 Gig) and a 250 Gig Hard Drive, and I'd Install Ubuntu 10.04 LTS or
Debian 6.0 "Squeeze" as my second choice...... depending on how the LiveCD's Boot and run during your testing.
Pick what you like......Mint, or Fedora........and on and on.

Other choices can be found at [http://distrowatch.com](http://distrowatch.com)

I created a system Partition of 10 Gig for Ubuntu, 2 times your RAM for SWAP, and the remainder for your data.
It's your choice..........

Burn the ISO's at a SLOW speed, and Verify the md5sum or sha256 hash after you have downloaded the ISO's, before you burn.

You will never be sorry.  Even my wife loves Ubuntu.........
