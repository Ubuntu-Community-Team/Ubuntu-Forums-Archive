Hello,

I've had Ubuntu 12.04 on my Aspire laptop for about a year.   It's worked great until about a month ago.   

The battery will not charge above 64%, no matter how long I leave it plugged in. 
It states that this 64% should last me 8:00 hours.  
When I disconnect from power, it takes about 20 minutes before I see a 'critically low' warning, telling me I have about 15 minutes of power left.  
If I do not plug back in, it will continue to give me these messages for about 1.5 hours.  

Obviously, it has something to do with the calibration.  The laptop is 3 years old and sees heavy use, so it is possible a new battery is needed, but I'd still expect it to charge to 100%, and just deplete much faster, and die when it warns its going to die.    The odd symptoms make me think it's not necessarily the battery.   

I have always and only used the OEM charger.  
I installed Jupiter but it does not seem to change anything.  

I've tried to look up ways to recalibrate a battery under Ubuntu, but all methods involve getting the battery to 100% first, which I can not do.  

Any ideas?
