### Post by icyest on 2008-09-18
The traditional Microsoft Windows XP has a "Remove Hardware Safely" icon button at the bottom right icon tray that enables you to remove a USB flash drive safely. When this option is selected, the computer waits 2 seconds, processes the command, and you are then given permission to remove the USB Flash drive in the form of a yellow message bubble. 
Those of you familiar with XP may note that this was a tedious task since it would sometimes not let you remove the USB even though all processes are shut down.
How does using a USB flash drive work on Linux? Are there different procedures to remove this? Is there a warning that tells you you can remove it? Anything I need to know?

### Post by swimb on 2008-09-18
In nautilus, or on the drives icon, right click and choose unmount. You should get a tooltip when its ready to be removed.

### Post by Delirium_Trigger on 2008-09-18
Unmounting a flash drive in Ubuntu isn't as tedious as it is in XP.
Simply open up your flash drive's location under places and then click File and click on Unmount Volume.
You'll know it's safe to remove as the folder will redirect to your /home folder.
Pretty simple.
Although rarely do I unmount; I just unplug the flash drive and hope everything's okay lol.
Hope that cleared up everything.
