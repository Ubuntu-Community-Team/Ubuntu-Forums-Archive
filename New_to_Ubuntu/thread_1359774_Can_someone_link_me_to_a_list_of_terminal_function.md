### Post by diablo75 on 2009-12-20
If it were me, I'd reinstall the OS.  Also, when it comes to ATI legacy hardware (aka, old hardware), the binaries that are posted on ATI's website (the drivers) aren't compatable with the latest version of X11 (at least that's the way it's been since 9.04 was released, I believe).  It's best to stick with the open-source drivers that come with Ubuntu and should already be enabled and working (to an extent) with your card.  My laptop has an ATI Radeon 1250 and I have to say it's performance is... well, could be a lot better if the drivers caught up.  I have to deal with random flickering from time to time and the 3D acceleration just isn't doing all that I know the card is capable of.  But I get by.
