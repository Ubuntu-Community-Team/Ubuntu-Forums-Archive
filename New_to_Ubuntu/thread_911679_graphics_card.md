### Post by alienexplorers on 2008-09-05
I know to move a graphics card you would disconnect any cables hooked to the rear panel.  Then open the case and remove the holding screw.  Slowly remove the graphics card.

Go to the other computer and open its case and insert the graphics card in the slot.  put a screw in to hold the card tight.  Attach the cables to the back of the card.  Start your OS and setup the graphics driver.

Be verrrry careful when handling the graphic board and make sure you were either an anti-static wrist strap or keep touching the case to ground yourself.

### Post by bobpur on 2008-10-01
At this stage my advice is a bit late, but we all know there's gonna be a next time. :)

I DO NOT dis-connect the power cord from the computer or the wall socket. the reason is that your machine is grounded. DO remember to turn of the power supply with the switch located near the power supply plug. This still leaves the computer grounded. In the time it takes to remove the side panel(s) any remaining electricity will have drained off. 

If this makes some of you a little squeamish, plug the cord into a surge protector and turn that off too. the ground circuit is still complete.

I don't wear anti-static wrist straps and I do wear short sleeve shirts so I'm touching the case at some point most of the time. I'm still here and I've never been shocked doing it this way.
