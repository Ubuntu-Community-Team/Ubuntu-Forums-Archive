### Post by Temposs on 2010-03-14
Well, the ideal way to back up is to have an external storage somewhere that isn't connected to the machine you're backing up. If your machine gets stolen, or there's a power surge that fries the whole system somehow, it doesn't do much good to have a backup within the same box. Backing up to the second internal hard drive is only good in the case that you accidentally delete files on the main drive, or only the main drive breaks.

I do it by setting up my oldest machine as a server and backing up remotely on a regular basis. Other people manually plug in external drives and backup and then put the backup drive in a safe place. There are also cloud backup services, like Rackspace and Amazon S3.

So, it just depends if you want a robust backup system, or if you just want to cover the two basic cases I listed above.

Dont count on 4Ghz.....and if your planning on running your system for 7 years, I wouldn't overclock it much, if at all. If you can only get your system with an ATI card, I think your builder/computer shop needs to get a clue....single-manufacturer shops suck in my expereince. 

The 300GB will probably be slower than the 1.5TB. Even if you get a 5400RPM 1.5TB. I'd run linux and win7 from the 1.5TB myself, and use the 300 for backups (either as an internal drive, or as an external). 

Dont forget that the 1st sector on a HDD is the fastest, and the last sector the slowest. The speed difference between 1st and last can be 100% or more. I'd run a small primary partition for win7 (as little as you think you can get away with, less than 100Gb for sure) then install linux (extened/primary, wont matter) then have a 'storage' partiton in NFTS for your common files.  

Blu-Ray is going to be expensive, and optical media can be a dodgy after any length of time. In the long run, BR-RW is probably more expensive than buying an 2 x extrnal drives- backup on drive #1, leave the backup at your partents/friends house, then when you do a fresh backup to drive #2 just swap the drives next time you go over.
