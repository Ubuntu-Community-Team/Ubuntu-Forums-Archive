Ubuntu will not have damaged your hardware, although those beeps do indicate a hardware problem.

Make sure your ram is plugged in properly - that is a common cause of problems - after that it may be worth finding your motherboards manual to see what the beeps mean.

### Post by kansasnoob on 2008-04-26
I don't think anything about any OS could destroy any hardware unless you forced a bad install.

My guess is that your CPU is overheating. That could be just a matter of dust-bunnies or a bad cpu cooling fan.

With Ubuntu I always go to synaptic and install "computertemp", and after closing synaptic I right click on an open space in the kicker panel (could be header panel, but I delete mine) and click "add to panel" and find the applet .......... then I drop the applet in the panel!

Then I always know if I'm starting to get warm! To me warm is anything over 30 degrees C. You should also be able to read the cpu temp by accessing the BIOS.
