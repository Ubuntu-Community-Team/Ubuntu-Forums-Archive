### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-04-03
A netbook with an SSD is likely to be using a cheap SSD; and you should avoid cheap SSDs at all cost. Trust me, I had a netbook with a cheap SSD and the write performance is shocking. Remember, whenever you perform system updates you're going to be writing a hundred megabytes or so... and that will take ages on a cheap SSD.

There probably won't be a huge difference in battery use between SSD and HDD. My old netbook with SSD used to last 2 hours and 10 minutes with the small battery. My new netbook with HDD and slightly larger screen lasts 2 hours 30 minutes with the small battery. Some of the difference in power requirements may come from the new netbook running Ubuntu 10.04 or having different hardware, but using an SSD will not magically give you another 30 minutes of run time.

I have a big battery for my netbook (an Aspire One). I got it off eBay, and it's a 9 cell battery; it lasts all day. Better yet, I bought it for my old SSD-based Aspire One and it works on my new one. It hasn't exploded yet so I assume it's a good quality battery.
