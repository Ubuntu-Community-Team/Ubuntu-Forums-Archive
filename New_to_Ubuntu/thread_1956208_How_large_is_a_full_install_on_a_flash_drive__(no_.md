### Post by Baldrick_NZ on 2012-04-10
> **snowpine said:**
> 15gb is the recommended minimum according to [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements)

Having said that, I use an 8gig flash drive with 5gig for sys files and 3gig as a home dir. Seems to work fine.

The one thing the OP will notice though, when done, is that it runs about 2-3 times slower on USB than when installed on the HDD itself. 

But if the OP's purpose is to use the stick to show others what can be done, and to use when travelling or while away from your usual puter, then I'd go for a 16gig min (with is more like 15gig in reality). Formatted into 3 parts: 6gig (sys); 2gig (swap) and the remainder as /Home.

### Post by oldfred on 2012-04-10
If installing to a hard drive I normally suggest 10 to 25GB as hard drives now often have lots of room.  The Arch site suggests you only use about 25% of a root partition on a SSD and I use that for all partitions. I do know I use 4GB more in /tmp when writing a backup DVD.

Install says it needs just over 4GB, but then you run out of space quickly with updates. I do have a full install of 12.04 in a 8GB partition on my 16GB flash drive, with no swap. I just looked and about 5.1 is used. But it is just a standard Ubuntu install with updates.
