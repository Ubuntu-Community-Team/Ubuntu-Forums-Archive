### Post by arvevans on 2010-12-04
During the first part of the install process you will be given a few configuration options for your install.  If you select "Use All Of The Hard Drive" this will re-format the whole drive for Linux use and will install Linux on that partition.

### Post by bug67 on 2010-12-04
**_Back up all your data_**!

Put the Ubuntu CD in your drive and restart your computer.  It *should* boot from the CD directly into Ubuntu.  Then, double click on the "Install Ubuntu" icon.  When faced with the prompt choose, "Install using entire disk."  Ubuntu will then erase everything on your hard drive during the installation and Windows will be a distant memory.  :D
