### Post by JohnGalt131 on 2008-08-12
I am a photographer and am moderately obsessive compulsive, especially when it comes to the organization of my documents.  I love the versatility and organizational power given to the realm of photography by computers.  Most notably the way in which programs like f-spot make use of the meta data in pictures to make finding the one you want instant and easy. What I am wondering is: has a similar program been made wherein the meta data of documents (.pdf, .doc, .tex, .txt, ...) can be indexed, rated, tagged, in a catalog of some sort.  The idea being if I need to find a scanned bank statement from 2003 containing the keyword "overdrawn" or something I open my program which has cataloged all my documents and searches a database of tags/keywords.  Until now I have seen a few products aimed at businesses but I will only use a free and open source product.  I have attempted to remedy the situation by simply using very long, descriptive titles, but it isn't as good as say f-spot with my pictures.

Any suggestions are appreciated
