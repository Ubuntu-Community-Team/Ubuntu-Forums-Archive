### Post by Hospadar on 2009-04-15
Depends on the game, just about every fullscreen game in windows uses directx, so windows can easily manage how that works.  In linux (and windows technically) there are more ways to get a fullscreen window, and some of them might not play nice with the window manager and let you switch windows and minimize and such.  Lately, I've found most everything to work fine and play nice with full screen, but there are some games that don't do this.

Generally if you alt-tab, it'll take you to the next window (i.e. some open window other than the fullscreen application).  Sometimes alt-F2 will get you to the program launcher and then you can do what you need from there.

I know of a lot of games and such that can't be minimized per-say, but you can switch to other windows and do other stuff just fine.

It might also make a difference whether or not desktop effects (compiz) is turned on, so try it both ways (System->Preferences->Apperance) if it's not working.
