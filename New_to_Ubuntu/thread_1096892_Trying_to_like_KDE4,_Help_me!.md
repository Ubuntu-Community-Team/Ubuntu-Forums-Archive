### Post by txcrackers on 2009-03-17
By way of preference, I've been in a fairly committed "KDE relationship" since 1.2 or so. I've had brief flirtations with just about every other "major" window manager that's come along in that time period (Gnome, Entlightenment, WindowMaker, BlackBox, FluxBox, FVWM, etc.) Here is my *opinion*, for what it's worth.

KDE has always reserved rather far-reaching changes for their major versions. 1.x to 2.x was quite a bit bumpy, as I recall. 2.x to 3.x was actually much worse than 3.x to 4.x (mostly due to major changes related to freedesktop.org). In all cases, it's always taken at least 2 or 3 minor versions to get "everything working again."

What we got with 4.x was a **major** architectural change and a number of new desktop paradigms. And, true to history, 4.0 sucked eggs, 4.1 was pretty good, and 4.2 is extremely usable - once you learn where everything got moved to. (I won't mention that it took me 4 months to figure out why my multimedia keys wouldn't work.)

My wife is going through the 3->4 transition even as we speak. She's moving from the "WHERE THE F___ IS IT?!?!?!?!" to poking around and just muttering "Huh - so **that's** where that is..." And she is not a "computer geek," just a fairly savvy user.

As I said, there were some paradigm shifts and you'll either like or not. I *like* the fact that KDE appears to evolve much more quickly than Gnome. My *opinon* is that Gnome is boring and clunky. KDE is slick...
