### Post by thecodergeek on 2012-12-20
Go to this link and read this:
[Best Linux For Old Computer]("http://jponline.hubpages.com/hub/Best-Linux-For-Old-Computer")

It has a list of the best linux for old computers.
The test were done on computers that were in fact pretty old. Ranging from 64mb RAM computers to 256mb RAM computers.
These distro's would all do well on a computer with 512mb. If I to say what the best was in terms of speed, ease of use, performance, I would say Puppy Linux (Lucid Puppy) and Slax. Slax is awesome! Slitaz is pretty bare-bones, fast, and good as well. AntiX is good as well.

Some of the distro's some of the others mentioned are ok, but still somewhat a little heavier in comparison to a lot of the linux distro's in the list in the above link. If your talking old computers, then that makes me think 64mb, 128mb, and 256mb of RAM. Obviously, if a linux distro can function well enough on 128mb of RAM, it's going to running pretty smooth with 512mb of RAM.

I disagree with everyone on the web saying you need at least 1GB. It is better to have at least 1GB of RAM, for doing multiple task and browsing more heavier webpages, but you could be amazed what you can accomplish with 256mb to 512mb of RAM.
