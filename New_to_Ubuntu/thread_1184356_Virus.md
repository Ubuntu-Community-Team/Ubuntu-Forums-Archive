From what I recall (someone correct me if I'm wrong). That purple tint is generally worsened by speakers being next to the tube and by keeping the monitor in standby and never switching it off completely. When left in standby mode (i.e. not completely turned off) current is still flowing through the monitor and speakers and as a result an electro-magnetic field builds up.

Monitors are cathode ray tubes (CRTs) which involve using an electron gun to fire electrons through a vacuum tube, the electrons then strike a surface that changes colour when hit by the electrons, resulting in an image! The previously-mentioned electro-magnetic field disrupts the way the CRT functions by altering the path electrons take within the tube (keep in mind that all electrons carry a negative charge and that as a result of this charge they are affected by the electro-magnetic field). A purple tint results because the electrons strike the screen in different positions than they normally would have had their paths remained unaltered.

By turning off your monitor (and possibly your speakers as well) you allow that field to discharge, this is generally referred to as degaussing and some monitors actually come with a degaussing button that eliminates that electro-magnetic field. Most of the modern CRTs make that loud "thunk" noise when they are switched on, that is actually a current surge that acts to degauss the tube.

Hope that helps you to understand it a little better.
