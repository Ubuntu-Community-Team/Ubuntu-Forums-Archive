From my experience (and I'm still relatively new), the initial configuration of the operating system (getting drivers installed and working properly, any hardware issues, etc) is the most difficult part about switching to Ubuntu coming from a Windows background. During the initial setup and configuration was when I used the most terminal commands, and really had to dig through things.

Now that my system is setup, all my hardware and devices are working, I can pretty much use the click-and-play methods to get things to work; using a graphical installer instead of installing via terminal, etc. However, there are still going to be times that require terminal use, from what I can gather, but it's not nearly as intimidating once you get over that initial hump.

My advice: don't give up. If you get frustrated, just log back over to Windows and take a breather for a bit. Any time you have a question, come here and ask. I've created various threads with what I thought were absolutely pointless and redundant questions and received nothing but quality assistance. The Ubuntu community really is great, and one of the only Linux communities I've tried to check out that I wasn't crucified as an absolute new user.
