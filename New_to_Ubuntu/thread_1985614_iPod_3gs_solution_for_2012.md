### Post by shaftesburyiv on 2012-05-23
A lot has been posted on this, I realize, but when I was looking for solutions nothing mentioned worked as well as this ..

NO "jailbreak" necessary ... you don't have to compromise the phone's warranty for this

on your iphone 3gs (probably others too?),

0. get the latest ubuntu 
1. download a song from itunes (get a free song)
2. download the app EZMP3 (free)
3. go to the app, under Settings, buy the full version ($2.99)
4. UNDER "mANAGING fOLDERS", create a new folder and name it (e.g. "Muzak")
5. plug in your iPhone to your computer
6. use Nautilus/the folder icon on the left side to see two different ipods, one is the phone, the other will say Documents.
7. Click on "documents"
8. Click on the blue icon for ezmp3
9. Now you are in a folder on your phone that will actually work!  Just drag and drop mp3 or m4a, etc. files (no wma) ... 
[add music]
10. when you are done, click the eject button next to the two iphone icons in nautilus/the folder icon/file manager
11. go to your icon for ezmp3 and play the music under "Folder Play" or "All Songs"
12: OPTIONAL: hold down until it wiggles and then move the "Ipod" icon by sliding it to some other place on the phone screen, and then put ezmp3 in its place. 
13: OPTIONAL: download opera and do the same with Safari ... 
 

You are good to go!

### Post by roelforg on 2012-05-23
I use oplayer lite because it's free.
 
The steps i do are (and mine isn't jailbreaked either):
0. connect device
1. go to the "documents" device entry for the phone
2. double-click on the oplayer icon
3. double-click on documents
4. open a second nautilus
5. find the mp3/mp4/whatever files (btw, oplayer uses ffmpeg for decoding so it supports a lot of formats)
6. select the files and right-click and select copy
7. go back to the first nautilus window (the one with the oplayer documents dir)
8. paste the files here and wait for the copy to finish (warning: The dir may seem empty but if you just reopen the directory the files show up, it's a weired bug)
9. disconnect the phone
10. open oplayer and press "My Documents"
11. press the desired music file and let it play
 
No jailbreak, no cost
