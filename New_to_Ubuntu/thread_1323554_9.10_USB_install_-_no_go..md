### Post by sgosnell on 2009-11-13
Both Michael and Richard:

My first guess would be either a bad .iso, or a bad burn to the CD.  Either is possible.  Check the md5sum of the .iso, using instructions from the wiki here.  If that checks, start to boot the CD, and when the initial menu comes up, select "Check Media for Errors", or something very similar to that, I don't have a live CD handy to check the exact wording.  That should tell you if you have a good CD or not.

When you burn a live CD, do it at the slowest speed you can stand, to minimize the likelihood of errors.
