### Post by IWantFroyo on 2012-03-05
1) Ubuntu and Linux in general is great for productivity. Really, it all works like smartphones. If you really want to send a lot of emails and get things done, don't use an iPhone.

2) As for replacing computers at home and work, it all depends on different things. I assume that you are completely in charge of the computers (no boss to yell at you?) here. I would advise to try Ubuntu on one home computer, and get some familiarity with it. Try setting things up and figuring out how you'll connect the systems, (I hear some good whisperings about kerberos, although I really have never needed anything like it). When you feel ready, slowly move the other computers over.
