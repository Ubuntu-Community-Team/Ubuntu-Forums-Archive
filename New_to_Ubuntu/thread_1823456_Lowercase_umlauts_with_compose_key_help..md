### Post by Wallyruss on 2011-08-12
I'm using Ubuntu 11.04.  I currently have my compose key set to right ctrl. I can do an uppercase umlaut by hitting ctrl+shift+"+vowel (=Ü), and I have found a way to make a lowercase umlaut character it is just a major hassle as I have to turn caps lock on before hand and off afterwards. I can't just do ctrl+"+vowel (=ú) like I've seen people say as this automatically defaults to putting an accent on the vowel as the ' and " key are the same.  I have to use shift in order to form an umlaut and this automatically capitalizes the character, too.  Any suggestions that would make it so that I could just make an umlaut without shifting?

---

### Post by CatKiller on 2011-08-12
You don't need to hold keys down. Release Shift before you press u.
