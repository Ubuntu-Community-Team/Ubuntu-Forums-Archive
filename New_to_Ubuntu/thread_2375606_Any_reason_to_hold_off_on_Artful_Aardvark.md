### Post by Autodave on 2017-10-26
Every 2 years, a long term service release is made. These LTSs have a service life of 5 years: security and software updates are available for 5 years after the release.  The last LTS was 16.04. 16 stands for the year of the release, 04 stands for the month (April). The next LTS will be 18.04. Releases in the interim will be 6 month releases and only supported until the next release.

I stick with only the LTSs and even then I usually wait a month after their release before installing them. Further, I do not upgrade to them but completely install the new release after copying all files that I need to save to an external HD.

### Post by drs305 on 2017-10-26
I agree with *howefield's* post. I've been using Ubuntu for over a decade. I overall like the new version, but the transition from Unity is taking a bit of adjustment. If you have a lot of scripts that involve window placement, use xmodmap to reassign keys, Compiz window settings/keyboard shortcuts, etc you will have some work to do to make Wayland work the way you want.

If you are more or less happy with how your system is operating, I'd not rush to upgrade. This does 2 things - it allows some updates to resolve lingering issues, and if the issues aren't fixed, gives time for workarounds to be posted so you won't have to search long and hard to find answers.

Happy Ubuntu-ing!
