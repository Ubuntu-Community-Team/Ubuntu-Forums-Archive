### Post by texla on 2010-10-15
You may be using these already but my favorite alternatives to Photoshop and Itunes are Gimp and Rhythmbox.  Gimp took me a while to get used to -  it is set up very differently from Photoshop in a lot of ways.  For ultra-professional work, it will not be adequate.  But for 99% of users and applications it will do a great job.  Just get ready to spend some time (vs. money) on learning the settings for resolution, working with text, trial and error and the like.  And try to always install from synaptic whenever possible.  Other cool programs (although you didn't list a need for them) are:

k3b (nice burner), brasero is nice to have too for some files that k3b seems to not like (mp3s?)
vlc (movie/media player)
cairo-dock (with open-gl option and compiz effects installed) amazing dock - very customizable and my #1 new tool for every program I use
Audacity (simple multitrack recorder and/or audio editor) 
Ardour/Jack and Jamin (for higher level audio work - takes some time to set up but these are very cool programs)
Kino (video editor), 
Evolution (Email and Calendar)
Ubuntu One - (cloud storage)

Again, you may know this already, but open-source alternatives to professional quality graphic, video and audio programs are hard to get used to and they are all works in progress with many workarounds, forum searches for help and individual customizations needed. No, they are not up to the quality of most pro programs for many reasons that you can spend years reading forums about if you wish. But they are always improving and they offer a lot to those who take the time to work with them.  They just are not a quick fix replacement like a lot of the other programs that I mentioned above are which, along with Firefox and Openoffice, can make you leave your windows/mac home and never look back.
