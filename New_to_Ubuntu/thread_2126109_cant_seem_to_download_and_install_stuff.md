### Post by varunendra on 2013-03-16
Hi aaron3uk,

You seem to have already gotten answers you were seeking.

While windows applications (normally) can not run on Linux, there are usually of plenty of alternatives available for doing what you want your OS to do.

It is best you decide whatever you want to do with your Ubuntu installation, then use that task as keyword in Software Center to search for applications that can do that. For example-

'**Presentation**' keyword suggests 'Libre Office Impress' among other thing which is similar to Microsoft Power Point.

However, if you search for 'Power Point', nothing relevant will turn up. Because it is the name of an application which Linux doesn't know about. But it does know about what it does - creating 'Presentations'.

After some fiddling with Software Center you'll know how to use it.

Alternatively, just create a list of what you want your Ubuntu to do, post it here and you'll most probably get an abundance of choices to choose from, along with suggestions about what will be possibly best for YOU.

Answers to two of your example questions -
[LIST=1]
[*]Best native Linux antivirus is considered to be ClamAV, not that you really need it. It, and other available antiviruses for Linux are meant to protect your 'Windows' part of machine (if you are dual booting) from windows viruses. They simply do not and can not affect Linux.
[*]For flash player *(what you meant by adobe player)*, while you can install it from Software Center, the best way is to install **Flash-Aid plugin** in your Firefox browser > Restart the browser and click the Flash-Aid icon at the top right corner of the screen. It will automatically scan your system and will install the best available plugin for flash, as well as optimize your system for it.
[/LIST]

For other needs, just search the software center (or net) and if unable to find or decide, just ask us here :)
