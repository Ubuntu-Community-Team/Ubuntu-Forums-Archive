### Post by Pas_sa on 2010-10-22
I've been playing [Sauerbraten]("http://sauerbraten.org/") since 2004. The main guys behind it are geniuses, and the game is a lot of fun on many levels.

Don't be put off by the sometimes incongruous art (which personally I quite like). It sometimes can feel like a but of a hodgepodge, but that's because it *is* one when it comes to art - it has a mammoth list of contributors. The netcode is amazing too - you're competitive even with 300+ ping.

The real draw-card for me however (besides destroying all competitors online in instagib and instagib CTF ;)) has always been the intuitive map editor - it's quite unlike anything else out there and is ridiculously easy to pick up and learn. The best user maps are included with the following release of the game (one of mine from 2007 is in there, "pgdm".. though I haven't had the time to make anything since :().

I personally haven't played a great deal of it anymore in the past two years, but it's definitely one of the strongest native Linux games.

Also [AssaultCube]("http://assault.cubers.net"), which is a total conversion based on Sauerbraten's predecssor, Cube. AssaultCube, on the contrary, has a very art focused team and can feel like a far more cohesive game than Sauerbraten - they both play very differently however.
