### Post by undecim on 2010-02-16
Ctrl+V is used as a shortcut key for some terminal apps, and so you can't use it to paste. However, when using a terminal, you can use Ctrl**+Shift**+V to paste (and similarly, Ctrl+Shift+C, to copy)

EDIT: Unless of course you are referring to virtual consoles (the thing you get when you press ctrl+shift+F1 and press ctrl+shift+F7 to get back from). I don't know of any way to copy/paste to those, because they are not connected to the clipboard that your graphical interface uses.

### Post by Flimm on 2010-02-16
On the terminal, you need to use Shift-Control-V to paste, and Shift-Control-C to copy.
The reason for this is that the normal keyboard shortcuts clash with historical keyboard shortcuts for the keyboard. For example, Ctrl-C interrupts and exits the current process. You can look at [this full list](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bash#Keyboard_shortcuts).
