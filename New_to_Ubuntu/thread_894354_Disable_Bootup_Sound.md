### Post by stropyboy11 on 2008-08-19
I want to go on Ubuntu at school, but there's one problem. I usually leave my volume on full blast when I power down, so when I boot up everyone in the room gets a healthy dose of tribal music. I've resorted to stuffing the laptop in my bag while it makes the sound, but those in my immediate area can still hear me. Is there any way I can just turn off the tribal music that plays when you boot up?

---

### Post by drs305 on 2008-08-19
System, Preferences, Sound > Sounds. You can select "No sound" from that tab for various events, including LogIn. You can also disable all system sounds from that tab if necessary.
