### Post by Werne on 2013-09-16
> **zuren said:**
> Case - Antec P182 mid tower
Mobo - Gigabyte EP45-UD3P ver. 1.1
Processor - Core 2 Duo E8400 3.0GHz
Graphics - HIS Radeon HD 5850
Optical drives - LG Blu-ray RW drive and a DVD-RW drive
PSU - Corsair HX620w
RAM - Corsair 4GB DDR2 1066
Well, even for today's standards, those are decent low-end components (except for graphics), C2D CPUs usually need little cooling effort and are quite energy efficient, make for pretty good HTPC material. Just make sure the PSU has a 120mm fan, that one is quieter. Card... problematic, those are usually not well known for being silent (I had one, it's a high-end gaming card), you could sell it for a pretty good price and go for a mid-range AMD/Nvidia graphics with passive cooling (maybe even make some money in the process). For a 1920x1080 output through HDMI, even the XFX ATI Radeon HD 4350 does a pretty good job.

> **zuren said:**
> CPU cooler - unknown but its big
Hmm, an aftermarket cooler, sounds like an issue. Judging by the fact that it's big, it's either made for performance (likely, according to the rest of the component), or it's fanless.

If it has a fan, that tends to be a problem in HTPCs due to the fact that being quiet is the last thing those coolers are focused at, the only noise requirement is that it's quieter than a jet engine. Turn it on and check it, if it's too noisy for a HTPC, you'll need a new cooler. For HTPCs I usually use Scythe coolers, mostly Big Shuriken and Kozuti since they're small (Kozuti is the smallest one I've seen so far), silent (lots of fan blades, low RPM) and support the 775 socket.

> **zuren said:**
> (upgrade to i5 or i7 when budget  allows)
Honestly, I wouldn't go with high-end components, it's unnecessary, those CPUs use more power and their fans make more noise. And I highly doubt a HTPC needs high-end components like those, I usually make them out of a Celeron G1610 and E8400 is about the same performance-wise.

> **zuren said:**
> - buy a SSD
Here's a suggestion - SSD, I'd advise only getting one for the OS (some 20-30GB should be enough), although even that's not too necessary. All else can be handled by 2.5" drives which are pretty silent and not so expensive nowadays. Getting a big SSD is pointless and a waste of money since it's not a gaming machine with 25GB worth of Windows on it.



All in all, there's a lot of potential in that machine to turn it into a cheap HTPC, good find.;)

### Post by Werne on 2013-09-16
> **zuren said:**
> Cooler - It is an Arctic Freezer 7 Pro ([http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16835186134](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16835186134)).  It's quite tall and has a fan.  I agree that going fanless would be the best approach.
You don't need to go fanless, even fanless systems need good case fans and airflow, which is sometimes even worse because those make more noise than a CPU fan if you don't have a decent (usually expensive) case. Usually the case fans are 80mm and spin at some 1800-2400RPM (you'd need two, one for pulling in air and one for blowing it out) while CPU fans on the coolers like the one on that CPU cooler of yours have larger fans and spin at 800-200RPM. Remember - the bigger the fan and the more blades it has, the less noise it makes because it spins at lower RPM, which is why I recommend Scythe coolers for HTPCs - big fans with lots of blades.;)

Honestly, that cooler isn't bad, I have an Arctic Cooling Freezer 13 and it's quite silent at some 1000RPM, acceptable up to 1600RPM (which is enough to keep an E4500 @3.3GHz on stable temps). If I recall correctly, you can change the fan speed using fancontrol so you can make it pretty quiet, my ASUS P5KPL-AM can control fan speed from BIOS, not sure if that Gigabyte can. Just make sure to replace the thermal paste first if you go fiddle with fan speeds so you don't fry the CPU, the paste might be old and hardened, I recommend Arctic Silver Ceramique 2 paste since it's cheap and good.
> **zuren said:**
> Graphics card - I'm researching fanless cards and what I may be able to get for this 5850.  Any thoughts on its ebay value?
I don't know, new ones are anywhere from 150-300$. It all depends, here in Croatia you could easily get 200$ but I got no idea how much you'd get elsewhere. So I honestly don't know what price you should aim at, maybe some 70-90$, maybe more, maybe less. As for fanless cards, the last HTPC I assembled a few months back used an XFX AMD Radeon HD 5450 which cost around 40$ here, I can see it's [30$ on Amazon]("http://www.amazon.com/XFX-Radeon-Profile-PCI-Express-ONXFX1PLS2/dp/B007B5YUMQ"), worked quite well in that thing (I used Ubuntu 12.04.2 as the OS).
> **zuren said:**
> SSD - My intent was to only run the OS and applications on the SSD drive.
That's good, SSDs are great when it comes to booting the OS, running programs, copy/paste speed and seek time but they are quite expensive, for a 120GB SSD you can buy a 1TB 2.5" HDD. Not to mention that you'd need a whole lot of SSDs to have enough storage for storing multimedia. 2.5" drives are pretty silent and no longer as expensive as they were 2 years ago, so they are becoming pretty good for budget HTPCs.

If you can afford an SSD, go for it, it will drastically improve reaction speed and boot time.
