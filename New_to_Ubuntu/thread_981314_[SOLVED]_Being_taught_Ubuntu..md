As far as Ubuntu goes, you have all the freedom you want, as mentioned above.

If, however, after you are done, you are needing to re-install the previous OS, there could be limits.  Some OS's only allow themselves a limited number of installs (3, I think) before another license is required to be purchased.  If the owner of the computer ends up having to purchase another license because of your "philanthropy" then there could a legal issue, if they choose to hold you liable.  I would recommend either using Wubi or LiveCD installs, i.e. not permanent, unless you have the owner's permission to uninstall the current OS.
