### Post by talsemgeest on 2008-10-08
Give quickstart a go: [http://quickstart.phpbb.net]("http://quickstart.phpbb.net/")
It is like a swiss army knife of tools for ubuntu, and has an option to image and restore a hard drive. Just boot off a live cd, install quickstart, and make an image, then restore it onto the other hard drive.

Also, please try to ask different questions in different threads, that way if one question gets answered you can mark it as solved.

### Post by bumanie on 2008-10-08
Need to know the specs of the old laptop, an alternate install cd of xubuntu may be the way to go. Xubuntu can work with as little as 128mb ram and I think 300-400MHz processor. You could also look at puppy linux or damn small linux as alternatives. As foe ghosting your present installation. partimage and clonzilla are options. Gparted can copy entire partitions/drives and there are also tools such as dd commands.
