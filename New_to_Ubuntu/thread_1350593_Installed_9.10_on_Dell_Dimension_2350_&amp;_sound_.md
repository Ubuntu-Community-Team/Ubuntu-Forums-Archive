I am a dedicated ubuntu user and have installed ubuntu on innumerable computers of friends. I say this by way of giving myself permission to express that, well, this perpetual sound problem does get a bit frustrating. Yesterday I installed 9.10 on a friend's Dell Dimension 2350--a 7 year old desktop computer-- and the sound worked perfectly out of the box. Today we started the computer, and no sound. I hate the thought of starting down that seemingly endless pathway down the [Ubuntu sound troubleshooting documentation]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SoundTroubleshootingProcedure"). It always ends up taking me hours, and often yields no results. But hey--the sound was working fine on this computer yesterday, so I know the sound card is ok with 9.10. In fact, I know everything was ok. So it should be an easy fix, right? Any tips, good insight on the quick fix? Thank you!!

### Post by Terl on 2009-12-09
The first thing is to check the volume and mixer controls.  I have had them mute themselves before or just turn the volume down; not often just every once in a while.
