### Post by 2hot6ft2 on 2010-03-17
Take the livecd out and wipe it off and blow it off just before placing it in the drive to remove any dust. After choosing the language Choose 
Check Disc for errors (if it checks out ok) then restart choosing
Try Ubuntu without installing (you can install from the desktop once it loads up). That also gives you a chance to try it out first.

It should have also been burnt at the slowest speed your drive will burn it at to help prevent errors.

### Post by irishrick on 2010-03-17
I ran into the same problem a few years ago and just running "Check Disc for errors" before I installed seemed to make a difference.  Found out later that the disk problem comes from the type of optical drive used to burn the disk. Some drives burn too quick and cause slight instabilities on the disk

Burn onto good quality CD media at 8x speed or as low a speed as you can manage. If problems persist, try a different CD/DVD drive to read the disc.
