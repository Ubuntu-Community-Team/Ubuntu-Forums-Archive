### Post by danny_galaga on 2008-08-28
i for one tend to forget some really basic things. my pc has been bogging down a lot, and it seemed to me to be related to firefox. but watching the system monitor, it didnt truly correlate. i dont know what made me think of it, but i thought i would have a look inside the case. sure enough, the CPU heat sink and fan were caked in dust and the case fan was pretty grotty too. so, out came the vacucum cleaner. i unscrewed the CPU fan (dont take out the CPU, they never seem to seat as well again) and with the vaccuum hose as close as i could get it, i used a brush to clean out all the crap from the heatsink fins and CPU fan. same with the case fan.

and guess what? everything is running fine now!

tip- make sure you have the correct sized screwdriver for the cooling fan.


so, kids. dont forget that our virtual worlds are still fixed firmly in the real world with its boring actual physics and something as mundane as dust can make a huge difference to performance.
