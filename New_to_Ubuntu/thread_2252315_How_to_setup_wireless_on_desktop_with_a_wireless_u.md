### Post by Topsiho on 2014-11-11
I don't think we need much more information here.
First you need a wireless USB stick, which is not so new that Ubuntu doesn't recognise it yet.
You put this stick in a (working) USB port in your computer.
If you are lucky you may see a light blinking on the stick, meaning that the stick is connecting to the computer.
If the stick is recognized by your computer you click on the icon, the appearance of it depends on the version and so, on this computer that I use it is two arrows next to each other, pointing up and down, in the top right of the screen.
You'll see a list of the names of wireless stations near your location, one of which is the one you want to use, if it is on.
Select it, and enter the password for it. Wait a few moments, and there you are: connected.

If your Wireless USB is not recognized, things are somewhat more difficult.

Topsiho
