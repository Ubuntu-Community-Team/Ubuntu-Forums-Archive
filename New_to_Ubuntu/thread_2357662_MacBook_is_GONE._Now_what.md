### Post by 1clue on 2017-04-07
I just did a side-by-side configuration on the high-end macbook pro vs the high-end system76 laptop. Both start at the same price.

The hardware advantages of the macbook are 2 that I can see:  The touch bar (Apple user interface rocks) and the multiple thunderbolt 3 ports. 

Aside of that, the system76 machine takes the cake. The processor can have an extra gigahertz, it can have more RAM, it has 2x m2.nvme drives vs the one pcie-based ssd, it has 2x 2.5" drive slots internal (making 4 drives, up to 12tb internal storage), the video card on the mac has up to 4g RAM vs the minimum one for system76 is 8g, usb3.1 ports, and the list goes on.

I really like Macs. I have one right now, although it's getting a bit old. I think their UI has driven the gui computing world since the Lisa came out in the 80s. With the passing of Steve Jobs, I can't say how they will compete going forward, but they have been fantastic.

What I hate about them is the closed box attitude toward hardware, app markets, you name it. I don't have the slightest problem paying for software when that software excels, I've bought software for macs and for Linux. Will do so again most likely.

Surely you won't max this out when you buy it, but just having the option to put that stuff in there adds value. I haven't checked the other sites being recommended but Linux people like to open it up and poke around. I imagine those other machines have pretty impressive expansion capabilities too.
