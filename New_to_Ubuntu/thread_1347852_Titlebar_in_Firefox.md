### Post by Anuovis on 2009-12-06
You could use several things to save the vertical space in Firefox.

>*Compact Menu 2* addon
It will make all your menus accessible from a single button, you can remove the menu toolbar then. 

>*Bookmarks Menu* addon
Bookmarks will be accessible from a single button. You can get rid if bookmarks toolbar.

>Remove Firefox status bar unless you really need it

Actually, I keep only one Gnome toolbar on the bottom. The upper one is on the autohide option, it keeps all my shortcuts and Applications-Places-System menu. This way you can save space even more

### Post by barnaclebill18 on 2009-12-06
What I did to gain more screen space on my laptop is delete the bottom panel and moved the top panel to the bottom, then added the buttons I wanted, like Window List and a couple of launchers.

I also use the Firefox add-on Compact Classic which shrinks the bars.

Between both of them, I gained almost an inch of usable screen.

Here is a how to:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SwitchingToUbuntu/FromWindows/Configuring](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SwitchingToUbuntu/FromWindows/Configuring)
