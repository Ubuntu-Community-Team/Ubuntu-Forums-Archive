### Post by Baldrick_NZ on 2010-08-14
Having struggled with this before, i settled on Songbird. Though it isn't supported under Linux anymore, you can still download the last stable release from getdeb [http://www.getdeb.net/updates/Ubuntu/10.04/?q=songbird](http://www.getdeb.net/updates/Ubuntu/10.04/?q=songbird). You'll need to follow their installation instructions if you're not sure how to install it.

Rhythmbox is good for simple functions, and has a music store. However, if you live outside of the UK, US and a couple of other countries you may find you're out of luck for downloading mainstream music. Songbird appears to have worldwide rights for music distribution through 7digital. I've used it, and it's every bit as good as iTunes.

It's worth a look anyway, eh?

Hope that helps.
