### Post by 2F4U on 2011-12-18
I would suggest that you look into the system log files, preferably after a freeze occurred. This may reveal the cause of the problem. However, if it freezes due to heating issues you could try a couple of things:
- clean the inside of the laptop from dust and dirt, so that the fans operate more efficiently
- buy a laptop cooling stand
- install a lighter desktop environment such as XFCE or LXDE

I am running Ubuntu 10.04 on machines from 2003 and Ubuntu 11.10 on another machine from 2007 without any problem.
