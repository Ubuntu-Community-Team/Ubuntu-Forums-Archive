### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-12-23
I like Compiz; I keep the cube semitransparent at all times so I can see when something has changed on a different desktop. Compiz also includes a zooming function which is handy for web images or videos that you want to view in full-screen, and functions for colour-blind users and users with ADD. The zooming also helps people with bad eyesight.

The "scale" window switcher is genuinely useful when you change it to show all windows from all workspaces. You can have the advantage of multiple desktops, without the disadvantage of having to remember where all your windows are. Fantastic.

Another thing I do is, if I want to watch a video in VLC while surfing the web, I use the "shelf" function of Compiz to make the VLC window really small, and have it stay on the top. Then I put it in an unobstructive space on the screen.

I'm sure other people have ways of managing their windows with Compiz that are useful and cannot be done with non-composited window managers.

However, if you're wary of stuffing things up, maybe stay away from Compiz, or work out what settings you like in a different user account.

### Post by Vignesh S on 2009-12-24
Well, I got compiz and all its cool special effects in Xubuntu. Yes, my desktop has a fair bit of power, but I'm pretty certain that there is a blacklist of certain graphic controllers so as to make sure you don't end up crashing your system, because before I installed the Nvidia driver, my integrated graphics chip wasn't on the white list of graphics chips/cards according to compiz. 
I know that on my old IBM Thinkpad, my graphics controller was blacklisted, and that was pretty old. But your graphic controller seems to be quite capable of taking compiz. 

Compiz also doesn't take that much system reosurces. At the moment, it's only taking up 28MB of RAM, and 1% of my Pentium Dual-Core 1.3GHz. 

Here are just some screenshots of compiz performing some of its eye-candy, though I had to use a camera because the computer wouldn't take a screenshot while the special effects were in operation, except for the live preview. Not sure how to get emerald working, which manages the customisable window borders, but we'll see about that later ;-).
 
One word of advice. If you are testing to see if compiz works on your computer, type it into the terminal. If you see something like "blacklist" or "whitelist", then its not going to work. There is a way around that, but I strongly recommend against that, since that is **very **risky. Otherwise, if you don't see your "username@computername $" after you tried that, and the cursor still keeps on flashing after an initial flurry of commands, then compiz is working :-).

By the way, you will need to install compizconfig setting manager in order to change/configure any of the special effects.
