### Post by halitech on 2009-07-01
the quality of the sound will depend on the format of what you are playing. If you are playing low quality (ie 64bit mp3) then it will suck no matter waht sound card you have. If you play a lossless format at 256bit or higher then you will get better sound with a better card. 

you say you are going from stereo to rca, are you using adapters or do you have a good quality cable that is all 1 piece? the longer the cable and the more connections, the lower the quality will be as well.

### Post by stinger30au on 2009-07-01
another trap people fall in to when running audio from the pc to a decent stereo system is they crank up the volume from the pc to over 85% or there abouts and wonder why the audio is distored

if you crank up the pc sound volume master to say 90% or there abouts your going to get distortion to start off with

then your going to feed your distorted signal in to another amp and your amp will make the distortion even greater and your then going to turn it up louder as well
 so its loud enough that any passing ufo's out near saturn can hear what your listening to


so, turn the volume on the pc down to say 50% and the  feed it thru your stereo system

now all you have to contend with is the original audio file in the first place

if that audio is distoered and recodred at a low quality, then theres nothing really that can be done to fix that

also run decent *QUALITY* cables, not some elcheapo 50 cent cables from your local kindergarten , do to a decent tv/stereo shop and spend some coin and get quality gear

### Post by Elfy on 2009-07-02
Most of the databases of compatible hardware I can find are rather old - this is a start [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsSoundCards](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsSoundCards)

You might be better of doing it the other way - start at the hardware and then search the forums/google for that card. For instance the Creative X-Fi cards have had problems - whether that is still the case I don't know. 

I don't use the forum search instead I use uboontu.com or you can add site:ubuntuforums.org to a google search. 

As an aside I am still using an old Audigy card - sounds fine to me through another old piece of kit - Denon amp and some old Tannoy floorstanders - but my ears are probably a little bit on the damaged side ;)
