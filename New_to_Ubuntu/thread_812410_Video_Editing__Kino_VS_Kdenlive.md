### Post by fizzybrain on 2008-06-05
Well, one way to find out would be to try them both ;-) they're both fairly small and easy to install.

But that's not what you wanted to hear, so here are my initial findings from using them both for a week or so (I'm using debian testing/unstable with KDE 3.5, so that gives me kino 1.3.0 and kdenlive 0.6) :-

Firstly, I think the whole "kino OR kdenlive" question is a false opposition - there's much to be said for using both in the same project.

Kino works well with grabbing dv off a camcorder, the keyboard shortcuts work well and I don't find myself waiting around for things - you can press play and then skip forwards and backwards, insert scene breaks, etc. all while playing (much nicer than presing play, stopping it, rewinding it, checking it, then inserting a scene break...). I use it for taking stuff off the camera and picking the scenes I want. If you only have one camera and want to pick good scenes from a tape and then just join them all together then kino is a good one to use.
AFAIK, KINO IS LESS GOOD IF YOU WANT TO DO AUDIO DUBBING, so probably less good for your project.

Most importantly (for me), kdenlive is a multi-track editor, which will allow you to synch and re-synch tracks, introduce delays, move/dub audio, etc. - so I can use a wide shot and a close shot and (hopefully) switch between the two. What it tries to do is really good. I used kdenlive 0.5 and managed to produce some good stuff and export it, but it tended to crash quite a bit. kdenlive 0.6 doesn't seem to crash nearly as much and I have found some extra features (which might have been in 0.5 for all I know, but only now have I found and used them).[edit: stuff about a problem removed - problem fixed]

I ran both on gnome for a while, but kdenlive seemed to work a lot faster on kde (kino might have been faster too, but that could be down to my imagination. Of course, the entire universe could be down to my imagination .... Cogito ergo est, etc.)

I think kino is more mature - it does everything it currently sets out to do, while kdenlive is still coming together (though it should be rex's gonads as the bumps get ironed out).

I'd strongly recommend given Open Movie Editor by Oracle a go, there's a quick demo on youtube somewhere. Oh, here it is
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ljEb1PdEdM](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ljEb1PdEdM)
It looks like it could be just right for you.

Other people recommend LIves, but I haven;t used it, and I have read that there have been stability issues (though of course, that might be all in the past).

You'll hate this though - I believe kino kind of wants all it's source files to be DV format ;-) It will convert them on loading, but it will probably be a pain in the neck. I think kdenlive is *much* happier with a multitude of formats.

Bear in mind that all the posts you might hear complaining about kdenlive's instability could well be from The Past (i.e. different versions of it and its dependencies). And while the number of synapses in the brain is finite, it's safe to say that the experience of playing with kdenlive for an afternoon is unlikely to displace anything important....
Oh, and the tips I would offer are:
- If you manage to break it (as I did, by undocking panels left right and centre in a fit of curiosity), and need to reset the configuration, removing and re-installing it will probably *not* work, as the configuration file is in a weird place.
You should find it in ...
/home/<username>/.kde/share/config/kdenliverc
I have deleted mine to reset the configuration with no apparent ill effects 
- turn down/off desktop effects if it is hanging while trying to load an existing project. I did this in Desktop Settings Wizard
- if you can't export your finished product properly, try making sure that the inigo path says
/usr/bin/inigo
and that the MLT path might need to be 
/usr/lib/mlt (though I'm still not sure about this)
- most of my crashes have been when trying to apply audio effects, I think; and even then they are infrequent
- I think the dependencies for 0.6 aren't complete, you'll need inigo and mlt (libmlt0.2.5), and kdenlive might not tell you that they are missing

I followed the installation instruction for debian and they were fine ([http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Kdenlive/Getting_and_installing](http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Kdenlive/Getting_and_installing)).
if the ubuntu ones don't work then the debian ones might be applicable (but I'm no expert, so ask someone who knows)

_cinelerra:_
How much time do you want to spend trying to install something when so many people find it overkill? Try the dyne:bolic live cd and that will give you a flavour of whether you're going to like it - I did and it started without issues (apart from the fact that, at 1024x768, my screen is too small to be useful)
