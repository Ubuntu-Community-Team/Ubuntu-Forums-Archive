### Post by drmrgd on 2011-08-03
I have Comcast and have been running Kubuntu for about 9 months now with absolutely no problems at all.  I just moved a couple weeks ago and needed to setup the service at my new place.  I booted into my Windows partition to do the online setup.  But, there didn't seem to be much of an indication that one could not do it in Linux.  

The only potential problem will be that they want you to install their proprietary Desktop Software thingy, which I usually uninstall as soon as the service is active.  I'm not sure if you will have any problems activating the service if you can't install that package, and I don't think it's ported to Linux.

That being said, if you're not setting up new service, I'm sure you can just plug your new computer in and go.  That I've done with no problems.

### Post by bringthenoise12 on 2011-08-05
Okay so I spoke with a Comcast tech on the phone and this is what he said, any PC you buy WILL work fine running Linux on Comcast high speed internet ... **HOWEVER** ... IF you lose your internet service for **ANY** reason it is **up to you to get it back up and running**, NOT them, as they only support Windows and Apple per their minimum system requirements.They do not do phone or in person assistance to Linux running computers, only PC and Apple, and it is up to the user if they want to take a chance on losing their connection or not.However he also mentioned he knows of people who have been running Linux on Comcast fine for months or years.

I also asked about recommended modems as he was a tech.He said if you buy a new modem definitely buy the **Motorola SB 6120**. Apparently it rocks according to his experience dealing with internet service every day.
