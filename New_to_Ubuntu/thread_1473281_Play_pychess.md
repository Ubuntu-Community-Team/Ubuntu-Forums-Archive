### Post by gmargo on 2010-05-05
If you just install pychess, you will only see the option to play against another Human.

However, if you install one or more chess-playing engines, then pychess will discover them and present you with a choice of whom to play.

I installed pychess on 10.04 Lucid, and got just what you did - only the option to play against another Human.  Then I used synaptic to install these packages: crafty gnuchess phalanx hoichess fruit glaurung sjeng toga2.  Afterwards when I started pychess, it presented me with the option of playing against a Human or any of those eight chess engines.  There are even more possible engines that are externally installable.

See also these links:
[http://sites.google.com/site/pychess/faq](http://sites.google.com/site/pychess/faq)
[http://code.google.com/p/pychess/wiki/ChessEngines](http://code.google.com/p/pychess/wiki/ChessEngines)

### Post by Lobais on 2011-03-13
Actually that is not correct.
PyChess is meant to include an AI by default. This is supposed to be a less strong and more human AI than crafty, gnuchess etc.
If you can't see it in the drop-down, you may want to try and reinstall the game, or if that doesn't work, report it as a bug.
