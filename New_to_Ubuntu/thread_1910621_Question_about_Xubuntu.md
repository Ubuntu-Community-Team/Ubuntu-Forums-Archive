### Post by Double.J on 2012-01-17
Xubuntu does come with Ambience, but it doesn't look quite the same as in ubuntu ;)

As snowpine says, desktops like XFCE and LXDE are Very configurable to get how you want. But they aren't gnome 2. Gnome 2 is sadly dead - not that I don't like Unity, loving it now I'm used to it, it's just a shame we can't have both! there are two projects mint have adopted/forged to continue the path - MATE, a fork of gnome 2 and Cinnamon, a fork of gnome shell by linux mint - at the moment it's basically the same, but looks interesting in the future.

I'd recommend trying a couple of live cd/usb's - xubuntu, lubuntu, xubuntu, mint etc to see what you like. If you do like them, there's not always need to reinstall your system; most can be added to your current system with little fus, choosing between them at log in - the only slight exception is KDE which adds lots of QT apps.. but still I have no issues using both!

I'll do my best not to get us bumped to recurring, but give unity a chance - now I'm used to it I like it so much I added it to Mint!
