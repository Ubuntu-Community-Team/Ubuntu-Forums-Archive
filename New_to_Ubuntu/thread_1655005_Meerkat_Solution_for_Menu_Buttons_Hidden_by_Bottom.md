### Post by Vot Musik on 2010-12-29
Hi all,

This is a newbie question that I don't quite have the right words for,  so I apologise if it seems a little fluffy.  I've been using 10.10 on a  Samsung NC10 Netbook with 1024x600 (standard) resolution for two months  now, and haven't had too many problems.

One frustrating issue that keeps reappearing is this: certain  right-click, 'options', and 'preferences' style menu dialogs (you know, the  ones which usually end with 'OK' or 'Cancel') are being cut off at the  bottom of the screen, and resizing is not an option.  In other words,  the menu interface is often too large for the screen.  

Sometimes 'OK' and 'Cancel' buttons are half-cut at the bottom of the  screen, which is ugly but functional.   More often, the are entirely entirely invisible, possibly taking a host of  further menu options with them.  The problem has occurred memorably with FreeCiv, Mupen64 Plus (the N64 emulator), MP3 Diags, and several Firefox extensions, rendering them completely unusable. 

I'm aware that the problem is probably oversight on the part of individual developers who don't think of netbook users, but is there a simple fix or work-around? Like, say, a program that forces a scroll bar onto these menu dialogs?  I'm trying to fix the tags on my mp3 collections, and it's becoming rather frustrating.

With thanks in advance,

VM

### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-12-29
I'm not sure you can resize those dialog boxes, but you can quickly move them with **Alt+Left mouse button ->drag** and the OK button should then become visible.  Apologies if you know that already, but many users don't.
