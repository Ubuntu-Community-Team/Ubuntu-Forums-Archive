### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-11-10
Hopefully you are using Synaptic to install. That is definitely the easiest and safest way.

When you open Synaptic, before you do anything else, click the **Reload** button. That will update the info in Synaptic. It may be trying to install something that is no longer in the repository, or the repository has been updated and your 'puter doesn't have the up-to-date info on what is available.

Hope this helps,
