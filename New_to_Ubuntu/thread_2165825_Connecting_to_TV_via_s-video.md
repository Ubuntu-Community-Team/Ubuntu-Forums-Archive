### Post by TheFu on 2013-08-06
On laptops, there is usually a Fn+key that enables/disabled multi-display support. Mine has little images that try to convey what they are for - vol-up, vol-down, brighter or less bright display, wifi-on/off, and if the video output should be replicated to an external display or not.  The svideo-out might need to be enabled in the BIOS too and you might need to drop the OS resolution to meet svideo standards.

I haven't seen svideo out on a laptop in a very, very, very long time, so I can't test.  Laptops today have HDMI, VGA, DisplayPort outputs and maybe DVI.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2013-08-08
I don't think you or your father will be happy with the appearance of the desktop on a TV set, even using an S-Video connection.  At best you'll get something between 640x360 and 640x480 depending on the characteristics of the TV.  I think your father would be much better off buying a decent monitor and connecting the laptop to it with VGA, especially given [today's monitor prices](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16824001542).

The other option is a new TV. My daughter picked up this [32" Samsung]("http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0074FGNJ6/") from Amazon and likes it very much.  That TV doesn't have a VGA connector, though [this one](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=9SIA2DY0YE1328) and [this one](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16889210095) do.  TVs with VGA inputs are getting harder and harder to find though, though there are [VGA-to-HDMI converters]("https://www.google.com/search?q=vga+to+hdmi+converter") that would work with most modern TVs.
