### Post by undecim on 2010-04-24
Sure, I have it done myself.

just right-click on each of the controls on the panel you want to remove and uncheck the "Lock To Panel" option and drag-drop them to the bar you want. Once it's empty except for any controls you don't want, you can right-click on the panel and remove it.

Also, check out the "Main Menu" control as an alternative to the menu bar, which consolidates all three menues into a single icon, saving space on your bar.

EDIT: Also, some controls are tricky. If you right-click on the wrong part, you will get a different menu. With the task bar for example, right-click on the space just before the window title where you see a sort of handle. You also have to drag-drop using that handle.

### Post by UltraMathMan on 2010-04-24
Absolutely. If you wanted to put the list of windows open, found on the bottom panel by default on the top panel, right click on the top panel, select "Add to panel..." then select "Window List" and click "Add". 

Once you have the top (or bottom panel the way you want, right click on the panel you want to remove and select "Delete this panel".
