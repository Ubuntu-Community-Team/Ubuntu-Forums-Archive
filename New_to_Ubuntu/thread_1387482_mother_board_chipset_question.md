### Post by anewguy on 2010-01-23
If you don't already know, a little background on chipset drivers:

Most people using this term are referring to the chipset drivers that come with their motherboard for use in Windows.  These drivers are not needed in Linux.  That's not to say that you may need a driver for say on-board video, etc., but in Linux you get these individually as needed.  Video and wireless networking seem to be the 2 biggest, though there are plenty of others.

So, as mentioned by the previous posters, we need to know what you are having a problem with so we can give you some guidance for Linux - is there a problem with video, sound, wireless?

For the most part, the drivers needed for on-board RAID, SATA, etc., controllers is included in Linux.  If you think you are having a problem with any of those please state what type of problem you are having.

If you were just curious about loading in the chipset drivers that came on the CD for your motherboard that you had to install in Windows, don't fret.  Unless you specifically have something not working, you don't need any of those.
