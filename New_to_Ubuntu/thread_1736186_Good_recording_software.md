### Post by nickleboyblue on 2011-04-22
If you want an entire audio suite, open up synaptic and download the ubuntu studio audio package, audio plugins package, menu, and ubuntu studio controls.  That should allow you to record, make drum tracks with hydrogen, do effects processing with various free LADSPA plugins and with calf, and use Jack for connecting programs together that normally don't work together.

Ardour and Audacity should work very well for you, but if you want a lot more control over your music, and if you want to have drum tracks without a drummer, synthesizer sounds, mixing tools, support for various sound cards, and essentially a lot of really decent and even professional tools, ubuntu studio packages are what you're looking for.
