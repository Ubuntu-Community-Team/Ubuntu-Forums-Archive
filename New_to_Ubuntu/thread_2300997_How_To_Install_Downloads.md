### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2015-10-29
OK, these type of files are like windows "zip" files.   They are just compressed files, using various compression formats.   So, they need to be unzipped or as Linux call it "extracted" first.   The Ubuntu file manager (right-click) will give an option of something like "extract archive here" or similar (there's more than one option to extract - - but that's the gist).   In the command line, there is a standard way to do the same if you prefer that.    But before getting into that, it would be helpful to know what the end game is . . . . are you trying to download files to install a specific printer?

Any specific info about your system, and the printer (or whatever) you're trying to do is necessary.    Also, just like a zipped file, not all compressed files are actually "executable" . . . . they may be support or library files.

EDIT:    what gives you the impression Google is going to do that ? (which is not true by the way - - why would Google even think to do that - Chrome can run on everything).

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2015-10-29
Just one more quick note . . . .  unlike Windows, you do NOT just go anywhere on the internet and download programs to install.    You go to a few trusted sources, starting with the Ubuntu Software repositories (like program databases).   The front end for that is the Ubuntu Software Center or a similar control program  . . . . . not the web or internet.    Unlearn those bad habits.

**EDIT:**  having said the above (which is generally true), there are a few cases where other safe software can be downloaded from . . . google chrome, OpenPrinting.org, Epson, HP or Brother are other sites.   These "stand-alone" software sources are learned from experience.   The official manufacturer sites (like Brother) are reliable.
