### Post by Lateralis on 2010-09-04
Short answer: no!  

10.10 is currently under active development and will not be ready until 10th of October this year.  10.10 is currently at Alpha 3 stage, meaning that it may well still be buggy and cause problems for your computer. 

I would also hold off updating for a few weeks after the release date, so that any unexpected issues that arise soon after the general release can be patched.  

For info, updating is a straight forward but time consuming process.  You will first need to ensure that your system is bang up to date before attempting the upgrade.  Then, to upgrade, press Alt-F2 to open up a dialogue window and type

```

update-manager -d

```

This will open a new dialogue window informing you that the latest distribution is available to download.  Once you start your upgrade I'd recommend you leave your computer alone and do nothing.

Addendum: The numbering system in Ubuntu is quite informative.  The first number is the year, the second number is the month.  So Ubuntu 9.10 was released in October 2009.  10.10 will be released in October 2010 and so forth.  Releases happen every 6 months in April and October, with a long term support (LTS) release every 2 years.  The last LTS release is the current version of Ubuntu - 10.04.  If you are interested in stability and long term support, then stick with 10.04 for now.  

Another addendum: You can only use this automagic update to go from release X to release X+1.  That is, you can only use this to go from 10.04 to 10.10.  You cannot go from 10.04 to 11.04 for instance.  The exception is the LTS version, where you can go from 10.04 to 12.04, which is the next scheduled LTS update. 

Hope this all makes sense?! :D
