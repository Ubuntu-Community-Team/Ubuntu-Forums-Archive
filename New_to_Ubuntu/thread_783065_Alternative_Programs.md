### Post by Xiong Chiamiov on 2008-05-05
First, [here is an excellent table of equivalents]("http://www.linuxrsp.ru/win-lin-soft/table-eng.html").

That said, some things depend on whether you're going to use Ubuntu (GNOME) or Kubuntu (KDE).  But I assure you, all those things have good alternatives, many of them available on Windows as well.

EDIT: eh, I guess I'll throw out a few options, rather than make you look for them.
Microsoft Office - OpenOffice.org or AbiWord
Web Development - personally, I use Komodo edit, but it depends on whether you need WYSIWYG or not, and many other personal prefs.  Text editors abound on Linux.
Media - VLC exists, but you really shouldn't use it (well, if you've got any Matroska files or anything with soft subs) - I'd recommend MPlayer, Totem, Kaffeine, KPlayer, etc...
Archive - zipping many formats is built in
