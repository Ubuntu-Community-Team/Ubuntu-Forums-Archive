### Post by Rasa1111 on 2010-09-11
> **thatguy93 said:**

Yes I would imagine so. lol
Ive used all things mentioned and theyve always worked great for me. 

To install AWN [the dock]
You just download it from synaptic package manager, or from software center. 
[or terminal]
To use terminal you would put in 
```
sudo apt-get install avant-window-navigator
```

 Once that is downloaded/installed, 
It will be in your **Applications> Accessories **~ menu.
Just click it to start AWN, and it will appear at the bottom of your screen. {when it starts for the very first time, it looks pretty plain.) 

 But to easily make it look as it does in the photo you posted,
You would right click on the little dock, 
click "Dock Preferences" 
and where it says Style: choose "3D'. 

then in the little box that says "expand the panel"
make sure it's unchecked. 

then you can play with the other settings there, and see all the different things you can do and what you like. 

 Then to further modify it to your liking,  
click on the "Themes" icon at the very top. 

 you can choose any of those themes, or install other ones you find and like. 

 then . while still in the same screen,
you can click the "customize" button at the bottom left, 
from there 
you can change the angle of the dock, 
the colors, the curves, etc..
 Pretty much everything. 
and its quite easy. 

Here are some more pre-made themes~ 
[http://wiki.awn-project.org/Themes](http://wiki.awn-project.org/Themes)
and
[http://www.linuxnov.com/top-10-avant-window-navigator-awn-themes-part-2/](http://www.linuxnov.com/top-10-avant-window-navigator-awn-themes-part-2/)

to add different applets, just click the "applets" icon at the top
and add anything you see you like to the dock.
and you can add anything from any of your menus into the dock also.

Here are some themes for your whole system
[http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=101](http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=101)
[http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=100](http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=100)

 and here is one like the pic you linked, for OSX look~
[http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/show.php?content=56577](http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/show.php?content=56577)

Applets for your desktop, 
like the clock, and calendar in your link, 
can be found in software center. [I believe called "screenlets"]

Conky is a little involved, 
and youd be better off searching these forums for info on it than hearing it from me. 

 There a few really great threads about conky here.

### Post by Rasa1111 on 2010-09-11
> **thatguy93 said:**
> if i click install, and try to click a theme, all i can do is open it.

easiest way to install themes I think, 

is to download the theme you want, 
(it will probably go to your "downloads" folder.)

 First open your appearance window, 

 Then open your downloads folder, find the theme you just downloaded,  and simply drag the package into the "themes" window. 

 If it works as it should, it will say
 "theme installed succesfully" , then it will be there with the rest of the themes.

So, say I wanted to install this OSX theme~[http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/show.php?content=56577](http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/show.php?content=56577)
I just click download, and it downloads, 
open the location the download went to {downloads folder, here}
then i just grab the osx theme.tar.gz file, drag it into appearance window,
and it's installed.

to make it invisible/transparent..
remember where i said to open the dock preferences,?
then go to the "Themes" icon at the top,
then click "customize" at the bottom left..
Once there, youll see the area where you can choose / change colors,
you click on each little colored box to open the switcher dialogue, 
then once that's opened, and you can pick whatever color you want,
you slide the little "opacity"?(ithink its called) bar down, and it makes it whatever level of transparency you want.
