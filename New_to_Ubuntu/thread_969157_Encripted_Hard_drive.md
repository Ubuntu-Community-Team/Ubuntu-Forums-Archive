### Post by lukjad on 2008-11-03
Well, the first thing to do is to defrag your hard drive. I don't think this will be a big problem since encrypting usually only affects those files that are already encrypted, though  in the case of an entire hard drive, the whole thing may be encrypted so as to prevent people from knowing how much space is used. If you want to play it safe, you could create a new, unencrypted partition from within Windows to install Ubuntu on. That would most likely be the best solution.
