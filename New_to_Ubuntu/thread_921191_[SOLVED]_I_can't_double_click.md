### Post by johanus on 2008-09-16
I'm finding my way in Ubuntu 8.04 and am booting Windows less and less.  Currently I'm realizing that I have no "double click" functionality at all on the desktop. I can open things only from the right click menu choice of "open".  I'm sure I used to be able to double click; don't know when it went away.  How can I go about getting it back?  I've looked through the configurations options in the GUI and don't see anything promising.

UPDATE!  Ahhah!  If I move the  click timing slider all the way to right and click extremely fast the system senses a double click!  What that tells me is that a  timing clock is running way over speed.  So it's maybe not coincidental that some of my media players  (video and sound) are playing at about 1.5 to 2 times normal speed! I've got a runaway clock???

UPDATE 2:   Yes! My system clock is counting 60 "seconds" in about 16 real seconds, judging by my watch.  WTF?

UPDATE 3: Found that my problem was solved by a BIOS update!  This appears to be one of those ACPI related kernel bugs that showed up a lot for AMD processors?
