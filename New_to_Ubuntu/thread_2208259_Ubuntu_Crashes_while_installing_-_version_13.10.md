### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2014-02-27
More information would be good; what error are you getting?  What sort of partition scheme are you setting up?  What kind of hardware?  

It's possible this could be due to a disk problem, bad RAM, bad optical drive.  The Ubuntu installer uses a lot more resources than the Debian installer, so if you've got a bad RAM sector or if your optical drive gets corrupt reads towards the outside of the disc (just for example), the Ubuntu installer is more likely to encounter this.
