### Post by alkh3myst on 2012-11-08
It's hard to tell without you pasting the terminal output after entering your install argument. If there's no error message, you may have had a package manager conflict (2 or more open). Did you have Synaptic or Ubuntu Software Center open when you tried this? This will disable installing software. If not, "sudo apt-get update" may clear this up. As an alternative, you can try Synaptic. I just checked, and have subversion available. A very detailed troubleshooting guide is available *here:* [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/PackageManagerTroubleshootingProcedure](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/PackageManagerTroubleshootingProcedure) 
Good luck.
