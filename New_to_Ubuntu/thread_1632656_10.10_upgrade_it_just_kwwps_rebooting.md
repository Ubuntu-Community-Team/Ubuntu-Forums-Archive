### Post by bwhite82 on 2010-11-28
One thing to try is at the GRUB screen, which lists all of your kernels, is to hit the 'E' key on whichever is your default kernel. Find this line:

GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT="quiet splash"

Change it to:

GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT="splash" 

That way you can find out exactly where it is rebooting at/getting hung up at. This is called 'verbose mode' and it allows you to see a bunch of boot up text on the screen.
