### Post by HotShotDJ on 2008-05-28
I am not aware of ANY routers, wireless or otherwise, that are OS specific.  Most have web-based interfaces for configuration, which are also OS agnostic.  My Linksys WRT54G (purchased for < US$50.00) works perfectly both wirelessly and wired no mater what operating system the computers are running. Its range is satisfactory for my home, and because it uses a dual-diversity system, there are not "dead" areas.  When choosing the right router for you, do not be lured into purchasing a router with more features than your wireless card(s) can support.  For example, if your laptop has a a/b/g card, you'll gain nothing by purchasing a router with "n" support (unless you are looking at future upgrades).  In other words, the more important question is this -- what routers are compatible with my hardware.
