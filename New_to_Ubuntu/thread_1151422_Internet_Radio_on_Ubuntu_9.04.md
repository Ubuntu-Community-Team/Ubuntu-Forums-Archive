### Post by freeman2000 on 2009-05-07
As zeroseven stated, you need plugins installed in Firefox.  I'm running Firefox as my browser, and in addition to having Java and Flash plugins, I have Mozilla plugins for every media player that I have installed in Ubuntu. You can download these from Synaptics.  I usually play Last.FM or ShoutCast or Screamer Radio in the background while surfing the web.  Sometimes there's a problem streaming the music because of bandwidth.  In other words, if the download signal is too low/slow, the music cuts out.  Due to your question I tried Iceberg and it didn't work for me at first.  Are you running NoScripts on Firefox?  I did a right-click on the NoScript icon and told it to temporarily allow Iceberg, and it reloaded the page and the problem was solved.  The music played.  Good luck.
