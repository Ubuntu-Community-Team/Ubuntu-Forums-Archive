### Post by Sef on 2008-08-17
1) Are you happy with how Hardy Heron is on your system?

2) Does everything work for you?

3) Do you want to risk something breaking that is currently working fine?

4) Do you need the latest software or is what you have fine with you?

5) What's more important: stability or newness?

> Should I expect hardware issues that have been solved in a current release to resurface in a future release?

Possibly.

Three Choices for Systems Still Getting Updates:

A) If you are happy with your system how it is and don't feel a need for the latest software, then stick with your current system.  

B) If you are unhappy with your system and feel a need for the latest software, then upgrade to the next system.

C) If you are happy with your system, but still want the latest software, then decide if it is worth the risk of upgrading and running into problems with the newer system.

Running the Live CD for a while can help you find if there will be any problems.

### Post by mhenry35 on 2008-08-17
Upgrading can be a mixed bag, so I'll share my experience with you:

I started on 7.04 Feisty, and although I had to do a few workarounds, it did work well on my Gateway Laptop with Intel Chipset.  It was very stable, never crashed.

When 7.10 Gutsy came out, I read about the new screens and graphics configuration items and I really wanted it, so I tried it out - and found out that the new release cut me off the internet (network drivers didn't work) and my old workaround for the former Intel widescreen display issue didn't work either.  I stuck it out for a while, but became very frustrated and reinstalled Feisty, then I went into a decision making mode - would I stick with Ubuntu, look for another distro, or go back to the dark side (windows.)

Finally, I decided to wait and if Hardy worked out of the box for me, then I'd stick around.  I was very pleased to find that Hardy did in fact solve the nagging problems, but the expense seems to be some stability issues, I think specifically with Totem.  

So, my advice to you regarding new releases is to download the live CD and test every piece of hardware on your system.  Write down any problems you find, and then start scanning the forums for others with the same problem and see if the fix would apply to you or not (like do you have the same hardware or application, etc.)  If you feel confident that you can fix the problems, then there is nothing holding you back.

Along with that, if you don't come across any problems, then the next step would be to scan the forums for anyone who has the same hardware as you, and see if they report any problems.

A best practice, in my opinion is to scan the forums for a couple of months after a new release, sometimes it takes some time for the problems to surface.
