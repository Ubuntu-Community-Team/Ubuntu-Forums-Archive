### Post by TheFu on 2018-02-22
It depends.  There isn't 1 MSFT Infrastructure.  There are lots of pieces.

Ubuntu can work with standards-based systems.  If **any** OS follows well documented standards, then it can be integrated to the point where those standards work.  Where those standards become proprietary, Ubuntu usually cannot be integrated without special, extra work, and sometimes commercial tools.

AD is pretty easy. It works.
CIFS is pretty easy. It works.

But usually there is a Unix-way to perform things that is more efficient and 100% F/LOSS.  Other systems that don't follow those standards often do not work well, at least without commercial tools used on the Windows-side.
