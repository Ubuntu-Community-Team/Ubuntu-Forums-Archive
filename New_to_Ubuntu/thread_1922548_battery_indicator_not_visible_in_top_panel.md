### Post by morhin on 2012-02-09
As a newbie I tried that and could not find "battery" anywhere. Even the search box doesn't find it.

It's there. 

Right click on the top panel (bar) then click "add to panel".
When the list comes up scroll down to the "indicator applet" (blue circle with the small letter "i" in it) and click on it.
The battery "indicator" should appear in the panel, along with a speaker and an envelop. This is a package. If you delete one you delete them all.

Another method (one that I use fairly often) is to google something like "add battery indicator to ubuntu". It will sometimes bring you back to the forums, but not always. I've had good luck with that.

Another helper is my reference book "Ubuntu for Non Geeks" by Rickford Grant with Phil Bull. Without this book I'm not sure I'd have made it through the first stages.

### Post by morhin on 2012-02-09
Okay, here's another one. This will put just the battery indicator in the panel. It will be stuck next to your wifi indicator, at least mine is. If I try to move it my wifi and battery and printer icon.... all move together.

System > Preferences > Power Management > (screen appears) General tab> 

I have mine set for "only display an icon when charging or discharging" 

Make as Default.

Restart computer. (Not sure this is necessary but I did it.)

If you have it set the way I do and your computer is plugged in, there will be no icon. Unplug your computer and the icon appears. 
Or change it to be there all the time. (Haven't tried this one).

Battery has a bar chart on it and you can left click on the battery for readout and preferences.
