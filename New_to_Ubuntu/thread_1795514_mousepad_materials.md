### Post by JKyleOKC on 2011-07-02
All of my mice here are opticals, and I use a variety of surfaces. The one on this machine is a fancy thing with a wrist-rest and a vertical spring that holds the cord out of the way. The cordless mouse used with my laptop runs on a sheet of white paper covered with scribbled notes. Another cordless used on my Email machine runs on a regular mousepad.

You'll have one thing to watch out for. I have a couple of cats, and the air always has a generous dose of loose fur floating about. Some of it settles on the flat surface of the gadget I use on this machine, and as I move the mouse it gets picked up into the sensor area. When that happens, the pointer movement gets erratic. I have to blow the hairs out of the sensor and then all is well again, until next time. You're quite likely to run into a similar problem if your surface sheds lint...
