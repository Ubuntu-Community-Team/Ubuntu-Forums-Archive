### Post by stephanvaningen on 2008-11-05
Kubuntu is more resource-wanting for it's UI. Xubuntu is ok if your machine has not a lot of power, or you want specific more CPU for child processes. 

Ubuntu-studio is just 'a' ubuntu with packages un-installed and other packages installed, a bit of re-wrapping and put on a new LiveCD...

The best you can do is find the packages on the market you need and install them on your (K/X)Ubuntu, depending on how performant your CPU/machine is...
