### Post by defaria on 2009-04-21
Don't do that! Those proprietary drivers are not ready yet. There are numerous problems with slowness, window manager aborts, it doesn't support dual monitors, etc. The open source drivers are more stable at this time but they don't do any of the fancy graphics (compiz and the like). At this time I'd rather a driver that supports dual monitors over one that does eye candy but crashes.

Oh and if you install via Hardware Drivers be aware you cannot back out via Hardware Drivers. You have to uninstall xserver-xorg-fgrlx, xserver-xorg-ati and xserver-xorg-radeon then reinstall xserver-xorg-ati and xserver-xorg-radeon to recover.
