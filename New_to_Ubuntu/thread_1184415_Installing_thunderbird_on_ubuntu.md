### Post by marshall.robert on 2009-06-11
Well, firstly what you downloaded is not needed, Thunderbird can be found and installed very easily by clicking on Applications -> Add/Remove... and then typing thunderbird in to the search box, waiting a bit, then ticking its box and clicking apply, where you will be prompted for your password. After you have done this then you will be downloading Thunderbird and then it will automatically install afterwards. When it is done you can launch it from the Applications menu in the Internet section.

For future reference, you may want to look in Add/Remove... first when you want to install something.
