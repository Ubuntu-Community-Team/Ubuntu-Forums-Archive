### Post by Mahngiel on 2009-12-26
It shouldn't cause any problems.  The reasoning is by defrag'ing windows first, you put all your files in a linear format, instead of displaced all throughout your drive.  I do imagine you had to shrink the partition first?  This idea was designed to have less file corruption due to fragmented files on a disk and then have them shrunk around and moved onto a partition.

Because really... it's only "virtually" tidy on your hard drive.
