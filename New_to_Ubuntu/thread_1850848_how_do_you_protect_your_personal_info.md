### Post by zeroseven0183 on 2011-09-27
Personal data security is a very broad subject. 

Just a small piece of advise.. Never trust a website that asks for your personal details (name, address, phone number, credit card number, and even password) unless you really trust that website or company. If possible, when registering to a public website, iuse a different name, address and any other details. That way you can hide your true identity.

Remember, it's no longer safe to stay public nowadays.

### Post by Lisiano on 2011-09-27
Most of the time, you can't correlate your location with a IP, most of the time it's only good up to a city. And as others said. Just don't give out any of your personal information, don't use your real name in a username. Use a handle like you see here. If you are concerned about how much the web already knows about you, google your name or your username. I found that some stuff was visible about me, had it taken down.
If you don't want your IP to be visible, you can always use a remote Linux server you admin (SSH), use VPN or just the TOR network.
For emails, you can use services like [http://10minutemail.com/](http://10minutemail.com/)

Also as someone once said
Don't put anything on the web that you don't want anyone to see.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-09-27
How much information did you give out when you registered for this forum? How much information have you put in your profile? Are you also registered on any social networking sites? What information did you give?

I once read a story of a German pilot shot down over England during the Battle of Britain. He amazed his interrogators by telling them that the German air force knew the location of every post office in the British Isles. How did you get that information he was asked. From the post office telephone books was his answer.

People are very free with personal information. We should not be surprised that it is easy for anyone to get hold of that information. As a test, think of the name of a well known person and google that name and see how much information about that person is freely available.

So, be mean with the information that you supply and do not store important security information on devices that are always on and always connected to the telephone system or internet. Do not trust your friends not to reveal information about you.
