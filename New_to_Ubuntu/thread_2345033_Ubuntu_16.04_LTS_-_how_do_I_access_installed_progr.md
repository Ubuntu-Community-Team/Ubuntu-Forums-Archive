### Post by howefield on 2016-11-30
Press the Super and A keys (the super key is usually the one with the Windows logo on it) and then start typing what you want to search for, or click on the top most button on the sidebar which will also open the "Dash".

If you search for "Help" you'll find some documentation worth browsing.

And here is a help page : [https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/unity-introduction.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/unity-introduction.html)

### Post by howefield on 2016-11-30
> **yodayams said:**
> But Ubuntu doesn't have menus showing installed programs?....

Not menus in the traditional sense, at least not in the Unity interface. Perhaps you'd be more at home with the interface of Xubuntu or perhaps Lubuntu, both of which have menu style trees.

It is easy enough the show installed programs in Unity though, Super + A and click on "*Installed see xx more results*", then scroll down the list.
