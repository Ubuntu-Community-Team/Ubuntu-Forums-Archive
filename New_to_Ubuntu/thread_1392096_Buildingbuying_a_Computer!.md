### Post by Bright_View on 2010-01-29
If you just want to compare prices, TigerDirect.ca is based out of BC as far as I know, and Memory Express (memoryexpress.com) is also excellent.  I go there for all of my parts (excellent prices - comparable to TigerDirect), and they have a handy online configuration tool.

Another thing to keep in mind - when you buy an already built system, the specs may be the same but generally the components will not be as good.  The best example is the power supply, which often in pre-built systems will be of lower quality, but will still have the same power rating (e.g. 430 watts).  I built my own system several years ago and I would probably not buy a pre-built system from anybody now because I think that a true cost/quality analysis would skew towards building your own, and the knowledge you gain in building it becomes invaluable later on in the computer's life when you may start having problems.

### Post by ovid9 on 2010-01-29
Four months ago I built a comparable computer for around $380.  I didn't have as powerful a chip, or as much RAM, but even allowing for an extra $200 for that stuff, you should still be able to get a computer for less.
 
Obviously if someone else is assembling it for you it will cost a bit more, especially if they have a warranty they offer.  
 
However, a couple things to be sure of whether you or someone else builds it:
 
1. Quality powersupply.  Quality is even more important than wattage.  On the newegg forums they have a wonderful breakdown of power supply quality.  I'd look into it.  A cheap power supply is just pain down then road.
 
2. A larger hard drive.  Sure, 250gig isn't small, but, you can buy a 640gig for about $50-60.  I see someone listed a 1TB drive above.  Storage space is so cheap, why not go bigger?
