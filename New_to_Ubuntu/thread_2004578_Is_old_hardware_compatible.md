### Post by sudodus on 2012-06-16
I think your specifications are good enough at least for some of the flavours of Ubuntu 12.04:

If you have a fast internet connection I suggest that you download and try several iso files. Ubuntu if you like Unity or Gnome and Kubuntu if you like KDE are nice and fancy desktop environments, but maybe you need something with a smaller foot-print. It depends very much on the graphics card or chip, which you did not specify. So I would recommend that you download and try also

Xubuntu with XFCE desktop environment has a smaller foot-print
Lubuntu with LXDE desktop environment has an ultra-light foot-print.

I don't know about those particular peripheral units, but in general, the linux support improves with time: It may be bad for new equipment if the manufacturer does not supply it, but after a year or two, someone might have developed or found a driver that works. So test if it works and or search for linux and the name and model of the equipment on the internet!

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-06-16
That hardware is perfectly fine for Ubuntu. I'm running Ubuntu on two computers; one is a lot less than yours, and the other is similar with a proper graphics card. Ubuntu flies on the better computer, although it is partly because of the graphics card (which is still a couple of generations old).

As for the scanner and 3G dongle, you'll just have to try them on the live CD. 3G dongles tend to "just work", from my experience. I believe Canon scanners have a track record of working well on Linux too, although I'm not really sure. Trying on the live CD will give you a definitive answer.
