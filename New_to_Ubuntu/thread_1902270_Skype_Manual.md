### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-12-30
Text someone: highlight the person (e.g. Skype Test Call) and press the blue button on the right. You can text Skype Test Call but it won't answer you.

Display a list of your contacts: That's already there on the screen. At the moment, you have exactly one contact, Skype Test Call.

Add a contact: Press the green button with a face on the very bottom left.

Set your options on the adjoining button, the blue S.

Toggle between your list of contacts and your history by pressing the little clock at the top right-hand side.

Press your own name ("hops") at the top of your screen to add a message than your contacts can see, or to edit your profile, including adding your name and an avatar.

To call landlines or mobiles, or to send SMS, press the long button near the bottom of the screen (you need to add credit to be able to do those things).

See your account or purchase credits (if you use the paid services) by  pressing the account button (next to the clock, currently showing "0,00"  on your screen).

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-12-31
> **mkornig said:**
> If I wanted communicate with that contact, do I have to know whether or not that contact is online?
The contact is listed with a Skype button that indicates he is on-line (green), on-line but unavailable (green with a white clock), not signed in (grey), and so forth. On your window, click on your own icon (to the left of your name) to see the various options.

> **mkornig said:**
> So in essence: there IS no manual for this version of Skype??
I suspect not. Skype supports Linux only because many people complain when it doesn't.

> **mkornig said:**
> Does my version of Skype support video communication?
Yes, it does. For some people it works perfectly (it used to with me [until very recently]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1900142")), whereas with others it is buggy. Go to the options > Video Devices > Test. Once you have it working, you will be able to use it. When you call someone on Skype, there is an option to start your video.
