Usually, I turn to google when I have a problem (and almost invariably, it leads to a topic on this forum's archive. Funny, that.), but this time, Google turned up empty :)

I just have a minor tactile preference when I use my mouse. See, coming from Windows, their default touchpad driver is no frills. Specifically, it lacks coasting. I'm not sure whether there is a technical term for the feature, but if installed, that's what the Dell Software calls it. (I keep thinking it's called the Synaptic Touchpad, but that can't be it, can it?)

_Anyhow, coasting: Whereby if I'm scrolling down a page via the touchpad, the mouse keeps traveling even after I have lifted my finger off of the touchpad._

With Windows' default no-frills driver, the screen stops scrolling after I lift my finger off of the touchpad. I can see how people might like the former over the latter, and I'm surprised that the default unix driver has that functionality, but _I was wondering if there was a way to disable it?_

P.S. Xubuntu automatically detected my cheapo mouse and assigned a driver in under a second. That's quite impressive. :)
