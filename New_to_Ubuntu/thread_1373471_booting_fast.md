### Post by mattbutsko on 2010-01-05
Best thing is to look into either an SSD or a fast RPM HDD (like 7200 RPMs). Best choice is for an SSD, which is like a flash drive, but it's your hard drive. They're expensive but since you only use it for internet, you can get a small 4GB one or an 8GB for pretty cheap.

I've seen SSDs boot Xubuntu in about 13 seconds and XP in 15 to 17.

In the event you don't know what an SSD is, it's a Solid-State drive. It has no moving parts which means it'll survive better if dropped (that is if you have a laptop or a netbook). They're generally much faster than HDD at reading, and writing. The only downfall is that they're much more expensive than Hard-Disk drives, but for a Xubuntu-powered internet machine, a small one will be fine.
