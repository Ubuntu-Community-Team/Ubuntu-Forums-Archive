### Post by TheFu on 2022-06-05
I've had issues with Canon and Epson. Ended up giving those printers away.
I've only used HP lasers and only at work.
I have a Brother all-in-one, but once the ink dried out, never replaced it.  I prefer to use laser toner which lasts years than spend $40 on replacement ink for a printer that was $55 new.  Inkjets are the original "just a taste" drug for most people. You'll be much better off getting a color laser printer in about 3 yrs just in the savings of consumables.

Bought my mother a Brother laser printer to replace a Canon IP-something that didn't work without $50 addon drivers from a 3rd party.

All my printers are USB connected.  I don't need network connections and certainly wouldn't use wifi.  If I need to print over the network, the system the printers connect into is a CUPS server and provides that network interface. That removes networking from the control of the printer company failures.

If you do connect a printer to your LAN, be certain to block internet access for it at your WAN router.  Can't imagine why anyone would want their printing habits sent to anyone on the internet.  IoT stuff is a security nightmare.

### Post by kurt18947 on 2022-06-05
I've stuck with Brother printers, getting them to work with Ubuntu has been trivial. I don't know about wifi; all my printers are ethernet connected. Your requirement for ink tank really rules out Brother inkjets. They have sort of a super sized cartridge but aren't really tanks. I recently purchased a MFC-J4535DW. I just set it up and it worked, no drivers needed for printing or scanning. I got the feeling when printer shopping that with Canon the answer is "it depends". There are Linux drivers available for some models not for others. If Canon is like my newest Brother - -  plug it in and it works I do not know. HP has had the best reputation for being Linux friendly but they have other issues related to their ink cartridges that eliminated HP from consideration. I got the impression from reading but no first hand experience that Epson was the least Linux friendly of the common brands. Epson can be made to work and it might be easy or it might try one's patience.

### Post by Impavidus on 2022-06-05
If the manufacturer supplies Linux drivers for your version of Ubuntu, it should work. But that doesn't mean it will still work with the next version of Ubuntu. If the printer works on open source drivers, I expect it will work on the the next version of Ubuntu too. HP printers usually work fine on open source drivers.

Look for reviews of the particular printer models you consider buying. Maybe somebody has tried them before on Linux.
