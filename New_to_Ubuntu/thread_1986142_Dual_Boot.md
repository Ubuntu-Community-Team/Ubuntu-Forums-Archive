### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-05-24
You can not legally use the same install of an MS Windows OS product on more than one machine at a time.  So, if you have Vista installed on your PC, you would have to uninstall that in order to reinstall it on your friend's PC.

If your PC came with Vista preinstalled, you can not legally move it to another PC.  That version is known as OEM and is licensed for a specific machine, not a specific user.

Also, presuming you have a retail version installed on your PC, when you reinstall it on your friend's PC it's very likely that it will refuse to activate -- since it's already been activated.

In that case, your friend will have to make a call to Microsoft to change the activation to their PC.

### Post by clive littlewood on 2012-05-24
Hi

My personal method for dual boot has always been as follows.

1       Format the intended drive using a live CD of ubuntu and gparted.

2       Install windows first on the full drive.

3       Install Ubuntu and you will be prompted to fix the size of partition using a slider.

After install windows might do a disc check, this is normal and you will be good to go.


Hope that helps
