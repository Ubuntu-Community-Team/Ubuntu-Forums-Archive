### Post by porchrat on 2008-10-04
Depends on whether or not you want Ubuntu to be a fully functional operating system.  I mean if you remove the bluetooth files you're going to have a problem getting any bluetooth devices to work.  If you remove the init.d files you're REALLY screwed as you won't be able to restart any of the daemons, if you remove xorg you won't have a x windows server...the list goes on.

I'm sure there are one or two that you could do without, as they will only relate to one or two pieces of hardware and/or programs, but it's not like it is going to save you a whole lot of space if that is what you are worried about.

Is there any particular reason as to why you want to remove these files?
