### Post by SirBartholomew on 2011-03-28
I wouldn't know how to bring all your files from wubi on to the new install. You could bring a lot of your files from it via pen drive but not all your settings.  

To dual boot I would create a partition in windows the size of the ubuntu installation you want. its pretty easy just google it. In vista it was in control panel under systems somewhere.It was under disk management I would have to be looking at it to know for sure. 

Then make a ubuntu install disk with an Iso burner you can google that and get one for free that is pretty good or if you have windows 7 just down load the ubuntu iso pop in a blank cd left click the iso and under one of the options it will have creat iso or something like that. Windows 7 burns isos easy and very well. You could also make a bootable usb stick if your computer will boot from usb. 

Once you have the install cd ready google partioning for ubuntu 10.10 I found 2 great easy walkthroughs for it with pictures and everything. Either write down how he is labelling the partitions or its much easy if you have another computer to be actually looking at it while your doing it.

Shut down your computer put in the cd or bootable usb stick if you are using that. turn your computer on it should boot into the ubuntu install from that. Just follow the directions and when you get to the part where it asks you if you are doing a whole system install or if your installing along side another operating system it will give you another option of manual partiiioning select that. Allocate the partitions to your liking then just follow the rest of the instructions from there. 

One thing is I would make sure your computer is hooked up to the internet with an Ethernet cable while your installing and when your asked if you want to update while your installing do it.

When your done installing ubuntu restart your computer you will be in grub 2 and you can just use the arrow buttons of your computer to select which operation system you want to boot into.
