### Post by Corrupter on 2008-09-17
Thank you to ALL who offer help to those who sometimes ask even the most obvious of questions.  Its not always completely evident the answer to some of us, and of course some days just being human means there are things we'll miss, and feel like we need to take a shortcut to try and find an answer.  We've ALL been there.  I want to say it again, THANKS.

I recently had some fairly revealing breakthroughs in understanding linux architecture and while i know that sounds a bit silly and a little like a good day at a group therapy session, understanding a new operating system to the point that you become a lot more independant means alot when you are on the road to being self sufficient.

Looking back i'm never one to take credit for something i couldnt have done otherwise without a bit of guidance and with that said feel that its sometimes the career linux users and professionals that go unnoticed, not to say that there isnt a significant number of those of us new to the OS that dont offer assistance through little tidbits we've discovered.

Just a final word of encouragement to those of you who stumble onto the ubuntu forums to offer assistance even though its very likely you dont need any yourself.  Its people like you that continue to make Ubuntu a great OS, not just because of its clever and intuitive designs but because there are people commited to making sure those who use it are not without some degree of support,  whether it be from peers or hired professionals.  Lastly, just remember that those of us who trapse into the forum asking questions occasionally forget to try and search the problem themselves, have a bad day, or just need a break from trying to work through what can seem like a complex and strange new operating system.

Thanks again to all those who help others selflessly and continue to make Ubuntu strong.
