### Post by crispy_420 on 2008-08-28
So dual boot is the easy answer. That way you get your Windows and your Linux. Most new laptops come with some pretty decent sized hard drives, so you should have plenty of room for both operating systems. A simple suggestion would be 4 partitions: 

Windows + Swap + /(linux) + FAT

But keep in mind you are limited to 63 partitions total per drive, so don't get too crazy. j/k
