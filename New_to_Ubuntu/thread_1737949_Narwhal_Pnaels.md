### Post by coffeecat on 2011-04-24
@TheManno1, as ivanovnegro points out, you can log into a classic gnome desktop but you need to enter your name first. Then the choice appears at the bottom of the screen - choose "Ubuntu Classic". Now enter your password.

But I suggest you persevere with the Unity desktop before you make a judgement. It will take a few days to get used to but is worth the effort. I much prefer it to classic gnome now. A couple of links for you:

[Ubuntu Wiki page for Unity](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Unity). The first few links are mostly for developers, but those under frequently asked questions are useful for ordinary users. Also, the power user's guide to Unity:
