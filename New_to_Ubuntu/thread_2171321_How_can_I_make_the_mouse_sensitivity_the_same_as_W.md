### Post by dhunt84971 on 2013-08-30
I agree with 3rdalbum, it may always be a little different.  Just depends on how often you switch back and forth and how sensitive you are to it.

With all that said, there is a CLI (Command Line Interface) command you can use to adjust the sensitivity.  This may give you different results than the GUI.  It's called xset.

Open a command terminal and if you use, ```
xset m 50 1
```, for example, the mouse will become very sensitive.  You can try different settings.  To restore your settings back to the default use ```
xset m m
```

Hope this gives you better results.
