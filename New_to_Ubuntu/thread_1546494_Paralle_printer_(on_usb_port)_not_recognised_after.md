### Post by johnfrith on 2010-08-05
I know this isn't a glamorous problem, but I would really appreciate help, as I'm out of my depth, otherwise.

I have a Samsung ML-4500 parallel printer connected to a USB port on my  laptop. Under Karmic the printer wasn't automatically recognised, but I  eventually got it to work by setting up an "Other" printer with a URI of  **parallel:/dev/usblp0,** using the "Printing" interface. On upgrading to Lucid, the printer stopped working, but it came back when I deleted and re-added the printer.

However, today there was a system upgrade, and the printer no longer works, even after deleting and re-added.

I think it might have something to do with [this bug]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/cups/+bug/436495?comments=all"), but that doesn't look like it's going to be fixed any time soon. It's a decent printer, so I'm reluctant to throw it out. 

lsusb doesn't see the printer. The printer 'state' is not connected. I've tried it in different USB ports, and tried rebooting the computer and the printer (that's about my level!).

Can anyone help me troubleshoot this? TIA.
