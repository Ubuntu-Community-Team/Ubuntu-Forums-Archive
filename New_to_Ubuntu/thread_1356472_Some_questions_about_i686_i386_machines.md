### Post by t0p on 2009-12-16
According to the fount of all human knowledge (Wikipedia not Google! :p ):

[**i386**]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I386") refers to computers with the Intel 80386 processor.Very ancient technology, launched in 1985.  The first 32-bit PC processor.

[**i686** ]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I686")refers to:

> 

[LIST]
[*][Intel P6 (microarchitecture)]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel_P6_%28microarchitecture%29"), as first used in the Pentium Pro and later adapted into the Pentium M and Core architectures
[*]In open source and especially Linux computing, it refers to [compiler optimization]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compiler_optimization") for [Pentium Pro]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pentium_Pro") and all later [Intel]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel") processors, and the [Athlon]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athlon") and all later [AMD]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AMD") processors[*[citation needed]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed")*], as compared to "i386" builds that are slower but more compatible with older processors
[/LIST]


I think the second part of the i686 definition is the one relevant to you.
