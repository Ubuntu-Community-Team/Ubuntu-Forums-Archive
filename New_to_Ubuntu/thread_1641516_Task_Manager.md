### Post by thinkinhurtz on 2010-12-09
From your terminal your can type top or htop for a command line system monitor. You may have to install htop first, but it is sometimes preferred over top. Or you can click on your System menu and look in the Administration tab. Find your System Monitor. This will give you a graphical version of your task manager. Sorry if its not in the exact spot specified, I use several different versions of Linux all day. But it should be easy enough to find knowing basically where to look and what to look for.
Hope that helps a bit.
