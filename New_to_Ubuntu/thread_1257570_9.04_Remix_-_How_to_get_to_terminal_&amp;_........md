### Post by Katalog on 2009-09-11
> **gccradioscience said:**
> Had the same problem too.  What is going on here?   Is Linux being like Microsoft?  Restricting apps and plugins that we need for our internet tasks ?

They cannot legally (in most coutries, anyway as is the case with Mac OS and Windows as well) just include these components as pre-installed in any of their releases. Aside from that, as with the majority of Linux distros, they offer them with almost nothing but free software out of the box as many people prefer not to have *any* proprietary software on their system at all, and have even spawned distros that take it a step further than that (case in point: gNewSense). In other words, they try and stick to the basic tenant of what Linux is all about in the first place - choice. If you prefer not have any patent encumbered software on your system, they also offer non-proprietary alternatives (gnash, swfdec, OpenJDK) as well, it's all up to you. They are not "restricting" anything per se, they are simply leaving it open to the individual's needs and beliefs/principles. I honestly don't want to create any anymosity here, but it just flat out kills me that a lot of people end up thinking Linux is screwed up simply because it didn't do things exactly the way *they* wanted it to, and end up in a jam. So instead of taking responsibility for not doing the things the way they should be done and screwing up their system, they take the easy way out by blaming the operating system itself rather than acknowledging the real problem, which is the user and either their impatience or unwillingness to learn how to do things the right way before just blindly charging right into it expecting it to behave like Windows (which it's not) and then complaining when application x didn't install correctly, when in fact Canonical has made it relatively easy to achieve what you desire with just few simple clicks. Hopefully in time, with a little help, research and patience, you'll eventually come to see that Linux is actually better than what your were previously using for one simple reason - it offers more of something that Windows does not, which is (again) choice. Choice in desktop environments, applications, customization, etc. all for free. There is a world of almost limitless possibilities waiting for you if you'll just take the time to adjust to it.
