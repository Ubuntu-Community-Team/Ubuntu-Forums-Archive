I'm not sure this will come out right.
linux has seperate things that can 'coexist'

There's copy, Ctrl+c and or highlight -> right click -> copy
There's paste, Ctrl+v and or highlight -> right click -> paste

And then there's highlight -> middle mouse click which pastes the highlighted text to where the cursor is, and doesn't remove any stored copy text from above action

### Post by estyles on 2009-02-02
> **hyperhopper said:**
> are you saying they are both active from the start?

Yes.  Ctrl-C/Ctrl-V works (although for some programs, you have to Ctrl+Shift+C to copy and Ctrl+Shift+V to paste, because Ctrl-C is already used for "Break" and Ctrl-V is used for something else).

The left-click/middle click is just a second shortcut that uses a separate clipboard and doesn't mess with your Ctrl-C'd text.  Kind of nice, actually.
