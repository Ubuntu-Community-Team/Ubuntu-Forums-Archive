### Post by lisati on 2008-06-18
Gmail and Yahoo have settings where you can direct spam and suspected spam into a separate folder which you don't have to download to your PC. If any does get through, you can always report it through services like [http://spamcop.net]("http://spamcop.net/") and [http://abuse.net]("http://abuse.net/")

(The abuse.net service is a bit easier to setup and use, but is prone to sending spam reports to the wrong place. Spamcop does a more thorough check on where to send the complaints but it usually requires you to log on to their website to double check spam you've forwarded to them and then actually submit the complaints.)
