### Post by Paddy Landau on 2008-07-19
For the ultimate portability, don't use OO or Word formats.

Instead, save your final document as a PDF (OO has a button by default to do that). That will allow almost anyone to open it -- Mac, Windows, Linux (which almost covers everyone).

Email the PDF to your potential employer, and keep the original ODT document so that you can make future changes.

I find it strange that OO won't allow you to save in Word format -- it should be there by default. What version of OO are you using? Perhaps you're trying to save from Calc (the spreadsheet), as you mention XML, instead of from Writer?

Incidentally, there may be a standard format in the near future. MS has signed up to the Open Documents standard, to which OO already adheres. In theory, that would mean seamless interoperability between various word processing packages. (In practice, I'm sure there'll be small inconsistencies.)

### Post by ugm6hr on 2008-07-19
> **le singe said:**
> and how would you know what the person you're sending it to uses?

This is the crux of the problem.

If you are sending job applications / CVs, I would recommend sending in PDF format for the following reasons:
1. OS independent (all OS have PDF support from Adobe or others)
2. It is pretty much an industry standard for electronic documents
3. The page formatting will be fixed at the time of PDF generation

I would recommend **not** using .doc or .docx because none of the above apply.

Specifically, having spent effort trying to make your CV flow from one page to the next, opening the same file in a different version of Word (e.g. Mac vs Windows, 2003 vs 2007 etc) will change the document formatting, potentially moving the page breaks to give your CV an unprofessional appearance.

So - my advice...  Stick with OpenOffice, and generate PDFs to attach for electronic applications.

PS: To answer your specific questions:
1. MS Word does not open .odt (OpenDocument) files, although I understand that the Office 2007 Service Pack is going to add support for that version.
2. OpenOffice cannot (yet) save as Office 2007 (.docx), but can save as Office XP/2003 (.doc)
