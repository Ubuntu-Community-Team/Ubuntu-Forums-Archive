### Post by JiuJitsu500 on 2010-01-22
There is a misconception caused by a switch from Nickel Cadium batteries to Lithium Ion in this past decade or more...

Nickel Cadium worked best, as I'm sure most people remember from toy cars and what not, to fully discharge the battery, then fully recharge... That's how it worked, and life was amazingly low when compared to nowadays.

Lithium works best and most efficiently by discharging to 92% or so, probably higher, and recharging... as the cells are more active and last longer, producing a longer and more powerful punch, and extending battery life in general.

That's just a tid bit though :) discharging means the battery is draining... since computer developers now pick up on the fact that lithium-ion is different, the computer simply lets the battery power the machine somewhat to it's optimal low (95%, 98%, whatever) and then AC power kicks back on :) All it's doing, in short, is keeping your battery at an optimal level and giving it extra life :)
