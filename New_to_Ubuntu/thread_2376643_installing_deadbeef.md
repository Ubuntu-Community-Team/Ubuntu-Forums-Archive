### Post by Autodave on 2017-11-04
You need to understand that Linux is not Windows.  I came to Linux about 10 years ago. At that time, I could do anything in Windows. After 10 years in Linux, I am still learning everyday and wish I knew a whole lot more than I do know.

When installing a program in Linux, there are often many other files needed. These files are called *dependencies.* They are files that are needed to make your program run properly. Whereas in Windows, everything you need to make the program run properly is already in there, such is not the case with Linux. Why? Because this fellow over here wrote a little program to deal with getting the sound to you speakers. This other fellow wrote a program to make the graphics of your program to display properly on your monitor. Another person wrote a program to make your particular CPU run the program properly. So on and so forth.

Why is it this way? Because these people did it because they love to program. They didn't get paid for it. Their joy or reward comes from making something work properly. 

Ubuntu is great in that most programs come in a .deb format. If you use the proper tools to install a .deb program, any and all needed dependencies are installed automatically. Any Linux distribution that uses .deb files is easy to use. That is *easy* once you learn how Linux works. Coming from Windows and expecting life to be the same in Linux is just not going to happen. There is a learning curve and that curve is quite steep at the beginning.

Fortunately, there are MANY great places, books, articles that are available for you to get through the steep part of the curve.  You need to start there. There is also this forum. All these people here are volunteers. And quite a few of them are VERY knowledgeable about Linux, computers, wifi adapters, graphics cards, oddball sound cards, etc. I answer quite a few posts here, but I read even more of them to help me gain understanding about something that I will probably run into someday. My list of Favorites in Firefox has about 120 saved posts from other people.  Many of those have been referred to many times and will be referred to again.
