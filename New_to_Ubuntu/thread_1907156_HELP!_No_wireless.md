### Post by TBABill on 2012-01-10
The key isn't the brand of the device, but the type of chipset. Intel, Realtek and Atheros tend to support Linux well. Broadcom as well but requires at least proprietary firmware, but some models can use either a proprietary or open source driver while others are only supported by the Broadcom proprietary driver...all depends on the model.
