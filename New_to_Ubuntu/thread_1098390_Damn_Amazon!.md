### Post by Temposs on 2009-03-17
e-book readers that use e-ink displays are a whole class of product. The Amazon Kindle is *not* the only one out there. I have probably the least costly one available. It's the Sony Reader PRS-500. Looks like some sellers on Amazon have it for around $230, which is considerably less costly than the Kindle. It does have somewhat less functionality, but it uses the same e-ink display as the kindle, I can read books on it and I got mine for $50 in a sony credit card deal a couple years ago.

With the Sony Reader, I can upload many types of formats of book, I can change the size of the text, I can bookmark pages, and because the display is so low power, the battery lasts 1 or 2 weeks on a single charge, which is awesome. It's about as heavy as a book, and I can hold it like a book. This device however does not have a keyboard or a wireless connection like the Kindle.

Readers like this are the future of dedicated reading devices. E-ink displays are a relatively new technology and the cost to manufacture devices with them is still pretty high, which is why these devices are so expensive. They don't use any digital displays like you're used to.

You should invest in one eventually, I think, though your budget should decide when and which one :-)
