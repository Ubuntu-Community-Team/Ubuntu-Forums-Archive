### Post by rg_stephens on 2008-10-29
If you delete to make more space on the drive, you should use *shift-delete*. If you press the delete button, it will go into a hidden sub-folder inside the current directory, like *.trash* and it will show up in the trash bin while the drive is connected. 

Try connecting the drive, wait a few seconds, and your files should show up in the trash bin. After you delete them from the trash, they're gone forever, and you should see your space go up.
