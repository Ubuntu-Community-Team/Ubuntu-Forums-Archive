### Post by damis648 on 2009-01-04
Are you referring to that applications can be found in the finder and not in menus like GNOME/KDE? It works like that because applications are installed diffferently in Mac OS. Installed applications are nothing more than folders ending with a .app extension. This is recognized as a package. These packages are installed by just copying them into the /Applications folder, where they are visible by finder.

### Post by sub2007 on 2009-01-04
In Linux nearly everything goes into /usr/bin, with launchers created in /usr/share/applications (although there are some anomalies). 

Programs like Tracker and Beagle can launch programs just by searching for their name (you can even search for "spreadsheet" if you want OpenOffice.org Calc) and if you have the deskbar then it works more or less identically to Finder.
