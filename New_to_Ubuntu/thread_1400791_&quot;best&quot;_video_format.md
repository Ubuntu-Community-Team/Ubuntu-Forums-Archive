### Post by Bachstelze on 2010-02-07
For PC use, H.264 video in Matroska container. Audio format would depend on your source.

*EDIT: However, if your source is already highly compressed, it's better to just keep it as is. Any further conversion will result in an additional loss of information.*

### Post by 311005901 on 2010-02-07
.mkv is a great format for video.
.mp4 is easy to burn to a DVD.
.mov and .avi can be viewed on almost all computers.
.ogg/.ogv isn't quite there in quality, but its open source so that's good.


But if your .wmv files have a low bitrate (sound/look bad), converting them won't make them look better. :-|

I suggest using PiTiVi for converting, editing, or joining clips.
Handbrake is also good for video conversion.
