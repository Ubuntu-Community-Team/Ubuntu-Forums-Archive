### Post by dfarrell07 on 2011-09-16
> **Frogs Hair said:**
> Make sure that you don't need IE

No one needs IE. It is the greatest security vulnerability you are likely ever to have on your PC. The only thing close is Flash, and various other Adobe projects like PDFs. Not to mention, it is total garbage compared to every other major browser. I have never seen a page or plugin of any kind that did not work with Firefox or Chrome (booting Ubuntu, I can't speak for Windows).

It sounds like you don't need to keep anything. I would do a full install of Ubuntu, and not worry about duel booting. I took the time to setup a duel boot of Vista and Ubuntu when I was switching to Ubuntu, and never booted into Vista after Ubuntu got iPhone support. I ended up forgetting my (long known, oft typed) Windows password, and removed the partition. 

It seems like you would be a bit more secure not to have a duel boot as well. Windows will be on a NTSF partition, which is vulnerable to some rootkits and such, unlike ext4 that Ubuntu uses. Other kinds of rootkits could still work on your hardware (your OS doesn't matter much if someone gets physical access and installs a rootkit), but not having NTSF is slightly more secure. Keep in mind, Ubuntu is very safe, even duel booting, and this is all quite minor.
