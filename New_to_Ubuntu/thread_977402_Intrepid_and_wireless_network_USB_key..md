### Post by ohzopants on 2008-11-12
A friendly piece of advice:
Start over with the 32 bit version.

From what I've heard/read, 64 bit is nothing but problems when it comes to drivers and certain programs.  I have been using Ubuntu for years now and I still steer clear of those headaches.

I've used ndiswrapper in 32 bits and it really couldn't be easier.  You may need to add extra repositories to locate ndisgtk (and I do recommend you find it, it's a great little utility).
