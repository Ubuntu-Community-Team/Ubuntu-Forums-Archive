### Post by mcduck on 2008-06-28
With that kind of simple effect (icons on a row with no visible dock) you could just as well use Gnome's own panel. Just put all the launchers and applets you ant into the panel, then go to panelmoptions to resize it, make it expanded to whole screen length and to make the panel itself completely transparent.

This would be much more efficient and resource-friendy than using some extra dock program, and works on old machines as well as new. But of course if you want fancy effects as well then AWN is the way to go..

### Post by appi2012 on 2008-07-15
empty your bottom panel (rightclick all the applets on your panel, select remove from panel)

rightclick your empty panel, and select properties. Change your height to whichever height you want. click on the background tab, and select solid color, and slide the slider to transparent.

Now just drag applications from your applications menu to your invisible panel. 

Rightclick the icons on the panel and select move to move them to wherever you want.
