### Post by yragrelluf on 2008-07-19
So quite some time ago I switched from Vista to ubuntu hardy, and loved it, except for how difficult it was to get my nvidia graphics card and my broadcom wireless card to work, along with some less important things. Well I managed to crash my system (hp dv6000 series laptop) the other day, and had to reinstall ubuntu, which I thought would take forever because of my hardware, but it was soooooo much easier. After the first boot, Update Manager popped up, so I installed all the updates, and rebooted. Then a window popped up and informed me that I would need to use proprietary drivers to get the most out of my Graphics card, so it walked me through step by step with a gui, and I rebooted. Then a window popped up about my Broadcom wireless card, and it walked me through with a gui to use fwcutter to get it working. Then after reboot, everything was working flawlessly without spending hours digging through the forums and messing with the terminal. I guess Im just trying to say that the newest version of Hardy is greatly improved and I would now recommend it to all my friends. Thank you to whomever thought about us hp users, and made our lives so much more easily open source!!!
