### Post by Kevbert on 2008-10-13
As soon as you've downloaded the ISO you should do a [[COLOR="Red"]checksum[/COLOR]]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM") as it's possible to download a corrupted or incomplete ISO.  The burn speed should be the lowest speed available for the drive to minimize errors.  Next the CD integrity should be checked via it's boot menu.  It's worth doing memtest as well at this stage prior to installing.

### Post by mjwhitfield on 2008-10-13
The general feeling in the forums is that lower is better, but, they're wrong.

Burning at such low speeds on a CD is suicide, most manufacturers don't even do quality control below 24x.  Try at 24x speed and make sure your PC isn't doing anything else while you're burning.

Also use [good media]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiyo_Yuden").

### Post by bumanie on 2008-10-13
All the answers here have aspects that are right and wrong. It depends on the quality of the burner and the quality of the media that information is being tranferred to. See below for more information:

> It is generally acknowledged as good practice to burn audio CDs at speeds no higher than 4x, but it is also important that you use good-quality blank media specifically designed for low-speed burning. Most computer media these days is designed for very high-speed burning, usually over 24x. Such discs may not give reliable results when burned at low speeds.

### Post by mjwhitfield on 2008-10-13
QUOTE WAR! WOOHOO!

Source: [http://club.cdfreaks.com/f57/max-speed-i-should-burn-audio-cd-best-sound-quality-54837/#post319232](http://club.cdfreaks.com/f57/max-speed-i-should-burn-audio-cd-best-sound-quality-54837/#post319232)> At 16x or below, the CD writer will use CLV (Constant Angular Velocity). This means that the writing speed stays constant from start to finish. At 24x or above, the CD writer starts at 16x and increases (in steps or gradually) to the final speed before it finishes.

Source: [http://www.hydrogenaudio.org/forums/index.php?showtopic=64090#](http://www.hydrogenaudio.org/forums/index.php?showtopic=64090#)> If i remember previous tests correctly, then very high burning speeds as well as very low burning speeds tend to negatively affect quality. So, choosing the lowest possible speed may not be a good idea.

Source: [http://www.hydrogenaudio.org/forums/index.php?showtopic=51849&st=0&p=591740&#entry591740](http://www.hydrogenaudio.org/forums/index.php?showtopic=51849&st=0&p=591740&#entry591740)> That being said there is no reason to believe there is even a rule-of-thumb that slow=better.

At the risk of sounding like my head is totally up my own ***, it boils down to this:
1. Make sure your burner firmware is upto date.
2. Use good media.
3. Don't be doing anything else while burning.

I've never had a bad burn in about 500 discs (DVD's, Audio, Data) following those rules and burning at half the rated speed of the media.
