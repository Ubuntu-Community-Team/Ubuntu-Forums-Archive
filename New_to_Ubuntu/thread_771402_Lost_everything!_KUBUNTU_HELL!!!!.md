### Post by meanburrito920 on 2008-04-27
Please do not reinstall next time you cannot access your data. There easy ways of saving your data from being overwritten, the simplest of which is to open a terminal and copy all your data to a seperate drive, or put the Live CD in and do the same thing. You should generally never need to reinstall to fix your problems. I would also suggest seperating your data into a seperate /home partition to avoid losing it with reinstalls. As far as the Kubuntu issue, I would first suggest trying out a different window manager such as gnome. If you really want to stay with Kubuntu I would suggest just staying away from changing the login screen. I dont know what you changed the login screen to, but unless you built your own KDM boot screen you probably got it from KDE-look.org or similar. You cannot simply set a picture as your bootscreen as KDM will not know how to handle it and will crash. Remember [GIYF]("http://www.google.com/") in cases like this.
