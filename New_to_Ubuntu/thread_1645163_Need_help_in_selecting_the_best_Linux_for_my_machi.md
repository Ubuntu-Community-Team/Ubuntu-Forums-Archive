### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2010-12-17
Someone mentioned this earlier, but I would guess the missing 8 MB of RAM is being used by the onboard video card. 

Be careful buying large sticks of RAM for a machine that old -- they might not support a full 256 MB stick.  You might be safer buying 128 MB sticks, unless you can google up some specs for the motherboard and confirm what it will support.

The other side to consider is that old RAM tends to go bad.  If you 256 MB stick goes bad, you have no RAM.  If one of your 128 MB or 64 MB sticks goes bad... you still have RAM.  Just a thought to consider, $4 isn't much to risk on it, I guess.
