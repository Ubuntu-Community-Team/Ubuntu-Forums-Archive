I'm not quite sure about what you mean with "screen moving away".

If you mean that you are moved to another virtual desktop, you are most likely accidentally using your scroll wheel over the desktop (and have, at some point, enabled the scroll wheel over desktop to do that). IF this is the case, you can simply use CompizConfig Settings manager to disable that feature.

If you mean that you only see a small part of the whole (single) desktop, and that part moves, you'r graphics drivers are badly configured and you are using a virtual resolution higher than your actal display resolution. Changing the resolution to something more suitable should fix the problem and allow you to see the whole desktop at once, without scrolling.

Still, both of these sceanrios depend on you actually moving the mouse cursor or scroll wheel. Are you perhaps using a laptop? Make sure you aren't accidentally touching the touchpad while typing.. Many laptops havea button that allows disabling touchpad, and at least Ubuntu 9.10 seems to also have an option for disabling touchpad automatically while typing on the keyboard..
