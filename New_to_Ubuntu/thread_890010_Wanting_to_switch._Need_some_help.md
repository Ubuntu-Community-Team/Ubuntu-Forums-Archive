### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-15
It fell off because these are all really device specific questions and unless someone uses that exact device they will not know, and with a system and 2 devices you want complex answers about there is little chance anyone who sees this will have this exact setup. I have 5 PC's and don't have either your exact mouse or keyboard, but I can give you some general tips. For games you should probably read up at winehq as they have lists of what plays and what doesn't and how well it plays. For the mouse & keyboard, the reason they have those neat little utilities is that the manufacturer provides them, they almost never do for linux. That said there are always more ways than one to skin a cat so to speak, so I am pretty sure if you investigated the hardware you could find a way to set your DPI ( how high I dont know ), this may not be possible with a simple GUI type program you may have to do some tweaks to a config file or similar trick to control it, I don't know though but anything is possible. 

As for your space shuttle looking keyboard ( man that thing looks like it could control the space shuttle :-) ) your in luck there as I quickly found this link where someone has already coded a linux driver for it including its lcd.

[http://www.memestreams.net/users/dagmar/blogid7625176/](http://www.memestreams.net/users/dagmar/blogid7625176/)

I am sure if you do your homework you can do the things you listed it just won't be as easy as it is with windows. But being free of windows and MS is worth it :-)
