### Post by gordintoronto on 2013-06-09
Make sure the printer is turned on. Run Printers. (This depends on what version of Ubuntu you are using, it might be called Printing.) Click on Network Printer and wait a few seconds. The Pixma should show up, select it and click on OK, Proceed, Yes, or whatever is offered.

This has worked for several dozen versions/distros of Linux with my networked Brother laser. In the past three years, I have never needed to search for nor "install" a printer driver.

If your printer is newer than your operating system, it might not work.

### Post by derek11 on 2014-01-31
I just joined-up to Ubuntu forums to provide an easy answer to this!

I purchased the Canon MP237 printer in Korea today, and after some frustration, did a search and learned that the MP237 is simply a re-badge (mostly) of the older Canon MP287.  So I choosed the MP287 driver from the list of Canon drivers that Ubuntu provided when I tried to install, and it works!

Very easy solution!  I hope others find this helpful.  Iaven't tried scanning yet, but on to that next.

Found the info here:

[http://driveprint.blogspot.kr/2013/10/downloads-driver-canon-pixma-mp237.html](http://driveprint.blogspot.kr/2013/10/downloads-driver-canon-pixma-mp237.html)
