### Post by Hansholz on 2011-06-01
I have a "no brand" touch screen that came guaranteed for Windows 7 only. I connected it with a laptop running on Ubuntu 10.10 and it didn't work. As soon as I upgraded to 11.04, it worked out of the box, with no configuration necessary (I did however have some resolution problems that the kind people here helped me solve  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1766261](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1766261))

Multitouch doesn't work yet (I can't pinch and zoom) and I'm not techie enough to go through all the suggested configs to get it to work. Personally I think the multitouch functionality on Ubuntu is still early stages and requires alot of tweaking from an end-user perspective. I also installed "onboard" to use as a virtual keyboard.

Regarding wall fixations, most flat screens come with VESA standard wall fixation holes. Mine is VESA 10 (100mm x 100mm).
