### Post by laurielegit on 2009-09-19
> **47tuc said:**
> The /etc/fstab file makes no mention of /dev/sdb

The /dev/sdb name for your device is not permanent. As devices are added and taken away, the sda,sdb,sdc,sdd names are reasined. Permanent names are stored in the form of UUID's, and that is what fstab uses. So it would be helpful if you could print the output of /etc/fstab.

### Post by 47tuc on 2009-09-23
I've fixed the problem.
I plugged the device in and waited for it to give the popup message that there was "an invalid mount option".
I then ran:
```
$ gnome-mount -vtd /dev/sdb
gnome-mount 0.8
** (gnome-mount:5056): DEBUG: Mounting /org/freedesktop/Hal/devices/volume_uuid_4638_AA97
** (gnome-mount:5056): DEBUG: read option 'rw' from gconf string key '/system/storage/volumes/_org_freedesktop_Hal_devices_volume_uuid_4638_AA97/mount_options'
** (gnome-mount:5056): DEBUG: Mounting /org/freedesktop/Hal/devices/volume_uuid_4638_AA97 with mount_point='GARMIN', fstype='', num_options=1
** (gnome-mount:5056): DEBUG:   option='rw'
** Message: Mount failed for /org/freedesktop/Hal/devices/volume_uuid_4638_AA97
org.freedesktop.Hal.Device.Volume.InvalidMountOption : The option 'rw' is not allowed for uid=1000
```So now I could see that the offending mount option was the 'rw'.

I re-ran the command forcing ro:
```
$ gnome-mount -o ro -vtd /dev/sdb
gnome-mount 0.8
** (gnome-mount:5383): DEBUG: Mounting /org/freedesktop/Hal/devices/volume_uuid_4638_AA97
** (gnome-mount:5383): DEBUG: read option 'rw' from gconf string key '/system/storage/volumes/_org_freedesktop_Hal_devices_volume_uuid_4638_AA97/mount_options'
** (gnome-mount:5383): DEBUG: Mounting /org/freedesktop/Hal/devices/volume_uuid_4638_AA97 with mount_point='GARMIN', fstype='', num_options=1
** (gnome-mount:5383): DEBUG:   option='ro'
Mounted /dev/sdb at "/media/GARMIN"
```And it mounted OK.
However all the files were owned by root:root
eg.
-rwxr-xr-x 1 root root 102804 2009-06-14 01:44 JUN-14-09 094452.gpx

I right clicked the Garmin Icon which had appeared on the desktop and selected 'Properties'. Under the Drive tab 'Settings' -> 'Mount Options' had a 'rw'.  I removed this and unmounted the device.  When I next mounted it the files are all owned by me and rwx for me.
eg.
-rwx------ 1 john root 102804 2009-06-14 01:44 JUN-14-09 094452.gpx

I can't explain exactly *why *the rw option stops the device being mounted. It upsets hal though.

Thanks to everyone who offered their advice.  You had me reading the right info which eventually led to the fix.

John.
