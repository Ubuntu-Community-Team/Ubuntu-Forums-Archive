### Post by andreasis on 2008-12-18
The only way that you will find out is if you try it for yourself.  I have tried both, over and over, and to me the clear winner is 8.04.1 which is also an LTS (Long Term Support) edition.

Pros Ubuntu 8.04.1
- faster
- more responsive
- more reliable

Cons Ubuntu 8.04.1
- older kernel may need additional tweaking of things that the newer kernel in 8.10 does by default
- you may want to manually update or download an updated versions of some programs... gparted 4.1 from sourceforge, as opposed to 0.35 that comes with 8.04.1 (Intrepid comes with 0.38 or 0.39 I think, but not even with the latest version), amule (hardy comes with 2.2.0 whereas the latest versions which include important updates is 2.2.2 and you'd need to download it manually


I believe that hardy's more effort for more efficiency, as compared to intrepid, imo.

### Post by Kayden on 2009-04-06
I honestly prefer 8.10 to 8.04. I will concede that it's not *quite* as stable, and causes my laptop to overheat quite often (Compaq Presario C700), but other than that I feel that Intrepid's the better of the two. Not to mention it actually supports my wireless out-of-the-box. ;)

But if you aren't having issues with wireless, and don't mind having to do tweak things a bit more, then I'd say go with Hardy. If you'd like to go, as stalkingwolf said, "bleeding edge" and don't care about sacrificing a bit on the speed/stability side for extra glitz, Intrepid will get the job done.

Then again, with Jaunty's release becoming more and more imminent, waiting it out could be your best option.
