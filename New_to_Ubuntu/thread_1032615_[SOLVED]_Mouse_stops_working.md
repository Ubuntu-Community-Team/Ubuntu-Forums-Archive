### Post by abhiroopb on 2009-01-06
Here is my current laptop status (the story may be a bit long winded but all of it comes into play at some stage of my issue)

I was using Hardy Heron for a while on my laptop (see sig). I had a USB hub attached to laptop with a Razer mouse and a Saitek Keyboard attached to the hub. I had a portable hard drive attached directly to my laptop. I also had a 19" monitor plugged into my laptop

Then I went on a holiday (leaving behind ALL my peripherals). During my holiday I installed Intrepid and everything was fine. Here I was using a cheaper wired mouse.

I recently came back from my holiday and obviously I connected up all my devices/peripherals and since Intrepid was newly installed I had to reconfigure nvidia to use dual monitors (xinemera).

Everything seemed fine and then I started having the following problems. I would be doing something on the computer and I would get up and leave and when I came back (sometimes after minutes sometimes after hours) the mouse would not be working. That is I could move the mouse around but I could not click on anything, and the mouse also would not highlight anything. I thought this had something to do with the Razer but my touchpad wasn't working either. However, the keyboard worked fine, and in general everything else seems to work fine (i.e. I can use the computer completely, just without a mouse).

So, I did CTRL+ALT+BACKSPACE, restarted X and a new problem arose, for some reason my laptop monitor (which is 1280x900) was configured to 1024x768. I would have to do a full restart before my monitor sorted itself out.

Does anyone know why my mouse is doing this?

The few theories I had include:

1. Razer mouse is giving a problem (although I used it fine in Hardy)
2. The USB hub is giving a problem (again I used it fine for ages, and it is just a standard 4 port 2.0 hub).
3. The monitor config (just seems strange that this would be an issue - but it may be).

The problem has started since I came back from my holiday and the above three things are the main things that have changed since I came back.

Any thoughts?

This is VERY annoying and I'd like to sort it out.

Thanks in advance!

### Post by donkyhotay on 2009-01-06
Sounds like a problem with your xorg.conf file since you also had a resolution issue at the same time. Xorg.conf primarily deals with graphics drivers and screen resolutions but some of them have sections for dealing with mice. Try posting the output of
```
cat /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```
