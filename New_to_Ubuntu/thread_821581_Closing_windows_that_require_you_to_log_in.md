### Post by hfp on 2008-06-07
Hey, everyone.  For a long time I have been completely closing internet windows after I log out.  For example, if I check my mail on yahoo, I will click log-out when I'm done, and then it will say "you are signed out now" or something like that.  At that point, I close that window and open firefox or explorer all over again.  I always have a fear that if I keep using a window that was used to enter a log-in name and password, that information could be passed on to someone else.  Is that possible?

Thanks a lot.

---

### Post by briwood on 2008-06-08
You don't need to close the browser window.  I'm a web app programmer.  The folks working at Yahoo are some of the best in the business. 

When you click logout your session will be destroyed on the web application.  No one else will be able to take over your session and masquerade as you. 

It's true that the web is inherently insecure, but this is not one of the things you need to worry about.
