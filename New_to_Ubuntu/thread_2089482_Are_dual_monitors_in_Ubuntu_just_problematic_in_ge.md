### Post by teacherjeff on 2012-11-29
Okay, this may be a strange post, because I'm not asking for specific advice for solving my problem. That's why I'm not going to include a lot of detail. But basically, I have been using Ubuntu for a good while, and I have constantly had issues arise when trying to extend the desktop to a second monitor. For example:

1. I had an HP laptop with ATI graphics. I never did get the dual monitor setup to work properly. (This would have been with 12.04 and 12.10). I tried with and without the proprietary graphics drivers. I tried editing assorted config files. I tried ATI's config utility. Finally I gave up.

2. More recently, I tried on a new Dell laptop. Got the second monitor to work, but strange things happen when I maximize windows (basically, they tend to jump unpredictably from one monitor to the other). I have googled, and this would appear to be a known bug.

3. I'm taking a Java class. Writing simple programs doing basic manipulation of images. Compiling and running from a terminal window. The images won't display (that is, the programs hang) if the second monitor is connected. I have tried this on two different computers. They work if the second monitor is disconnected, and they work with dual monitors under Windows.

Again, I'm not asking for specific advice on how to address these issues. What I would like to know is whether using dual monitors under Ubuntu is generally known to be a problem-prone thing. Or am I just cursed?

Thanks.
