[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4837798&postcount=6](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4837798&postcount=6)
			 			  			  			      
 			
  	 	 	 	 		 		 			 			 				 				 			
 			 			 		   		 		 		better solution all
hit Alt-F2 and run gconf-editor

when you run it navigate to 

apps->panel->global

under lock_down click the checkbox 

and restart gnome with Ctrl-Alt-Backspace

much thanks to ##gnome and #ubuntu on irc.freenode.net for help with this one

### Post by stinger30au on 2008-08-22
i have also found another handy utility will do it and also a few other neat tricks too

ubuntu tweak

[http://ubuntu-tweak.com/](http://ubuntu-tweak.com/)

if you look here it will tell you how to install it

[http://ubuntu-tweak.com/downloads](http://ubuntu-tweak.com/downloads)

once installed you can click applications->system tools-> ubuntu tweak

then scroll down to system and click on gnome

there is a few options. one in particular says

confirm message when remove panel

you can have this turned off or on

the other is

complete lock down of panel

and the other is 

enable panel animations

very easy

enjoy
