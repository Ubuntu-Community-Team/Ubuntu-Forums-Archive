Speeding up ubuntu to a point where you can feel a diffrence is about recompiling your kernel for your specific hardware, which speeds things up, and installing a light desktop, as gnome and kde eat system resources.  

Removing unneeded start up scripts, saves memory and and the system boots faster.  I supposed it could speed things up if the system services actually do something.  Installing Xubuntu or XFCE really speed ubuntu up on light systems, as its a slick lightweight desktop.

Ubuntu could be faster then xp and vista, and then again it could be slower than xp and vista.  Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu and all its variants is customizable.  That means you can customize it, slow it down, add heavy weight applications to it, change the windows manager, remove heavyweight application.  Its about freedom as in freedom of speach not freedom of cost.  With GNU linux, you are not just limited to the distribution, you can go gnome/ubuntu to kde/kubuntu ect, really quiet fast.
