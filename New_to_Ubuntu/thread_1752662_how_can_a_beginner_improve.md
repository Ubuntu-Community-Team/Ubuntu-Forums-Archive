### Post by Irihapeti on 2011-05-08
First, do regular backups. [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DriveImaging](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DriveImaging) and [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BackupYourSystem](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BackupYourSystem) tell you how to do that.

Once you have a good backup, you can try things with confidence, knowing that if you mess up you can restore your system easily. I've learned the hard way to backup before experimenting with something new, and it's saved me on a number of occasions.

If you have a spare drive, you could make an experimental installation to that and just wipe and reinstall if something goes wrong.

And it will - I think that you haven't lived until you've borked your system at least once. :) If you've learned from it, it's all in a good cause.

I learned a lot by exploring the "Help Center". (That's all the help files installed on your computer. It may have a question-mark or lifebuoy logo.) There's a wealth of information in there. I also got the Advanced Bash Scripting guide (which is good for beginners, too). There are also other Bash guides available.

Most of all, have fun!
