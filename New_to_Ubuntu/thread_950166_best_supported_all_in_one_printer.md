### Post by PierreDeKat on 2008-10-17
I've got an HP Photosmart C4480 that cost me about $69US, and it doesn't "turn on", but it does "wake up" when I print.

It's not fully supported by the version of HPLIP that's in the Hardy repos, though it prints just fine with that version of HPLIP, just the same.

However, the latest version of HPLIP -- that should be included in the Intrepid repos, I'm guessing -- shows me the approximate ink levels and everything.

Moreover, the ink cartridges are refillable, and that was a big factor for me, as well.

Whatever you do, **avoid any printers with cartridges that have a microchip in them**. 

Before I bought the HP, I had a piece of crap Epson running on Windows that was constantly telling me I needed to buy two new $35 ink cartridges.

It wasn't that I was out of ink -- these were brand new cartridges. It was the fact Epson had designed these printer cartridges to start sending you messages to buy new cartridges after approximately 10 prints.

I believe there's a class action lawsuit underway concerning these types of printer cartridges with the "expiration date" built into them.

I doubt I'll get any money out of the suit, but even if I did, I will still never, ever, ever buy another Epson printer for as long as I live. And I will warn others not to buy one either.

So there you go.
