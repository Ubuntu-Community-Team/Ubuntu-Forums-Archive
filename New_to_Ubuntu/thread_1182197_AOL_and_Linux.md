### Post by Dynaflow on 2009-06-11
> **jwaclawsky said:**
> Ok, I get it, the AOL client is Linux hostile. So my next question is: are there gaming web site my wife can use that work well with Linux? We'll leave AOL for some place that supports Linux.  Thanks.

Actually, just trying a several of the games at the AOL games front page, I only got one incompatibility message from the site.  Come to think of it, the game probably would have worked had I not had my browser specifically set to identify me as a Linux user.  You'd be surprised at the number of things that will magically start to work if your browser tells them you're running Windows.

I have to ask this just to make sure, since the last time I connected through AOL was in 1996, and I'm not sure how their plans and such work anymore.  Are you connecting broadband, or are you using a dial-up modem?  If the latter, you should be aware that the companies that make the cheap, internal "softmodems" PC manufacturers tend to toss into their machines generally have abysmal Linux support and don't seem at all eager to befriend the penguin people.

External dial-up modems, on the other hand, tend to have excellent Linux support and, as quality products rather than commodity components, tend to work a lot better, period.
