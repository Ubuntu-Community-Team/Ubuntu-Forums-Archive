### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-02-06
How are you booting the broken computer?
Does the disk spin up normally or does it make noises?
How are you mounting the corrupt disk?
Does the partition table look normal?
What file system is on the disk?
When you look at the smart data for the broken disk does it show bad sectors?

Personally I would take the hard drive out of the broken computer and install it as a second drive in a working computer.  There may be problem with the controller, the cables, or the power supply in the broken computer.  If there's no known reason for the disk to become corrupt then I'd suspect some sort of hardware failure.
