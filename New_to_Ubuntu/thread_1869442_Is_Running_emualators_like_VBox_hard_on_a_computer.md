### Post by dolphin194 on 2011-10-25
Running a Virtual Machine is no problem for the computer, that is unless you are running at 100% CPU load 24 hours a day. It will likely shorten the CPU's life, but most likely you won't notice.

**Think about gamers. **Gamers tend to have a 100% load on their computers for hours each day. Their computers run for a decent amount of time before breaking down. 

From what you say, the small amount of virtualization you do will hardly have an impact on your CPU. 

_Read the following if you want, but I'm warning you: **it may be complete nonsense!**_
Your computer is made to run Windows, which in my opinion uses a lot more than Ubuntu. Maybe this virtualization is making up for that CPU load...yeah I know, nonsense.
