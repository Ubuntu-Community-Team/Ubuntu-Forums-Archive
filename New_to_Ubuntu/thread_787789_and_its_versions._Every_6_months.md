### Post by ShodanjoDM on 2008-05-09
In the open source world...

Many things move fast. Bugs found and squished, security holes discovered and patched, new features keep coming and added all the time.

For example, slightly more than one year ago, open office launched and ran like a dying breath. Now it flies on this same machine.

New kernel also comes with lots of improvements, new drivers get in, obsolete drivers replaced. More companies opened their codes, like VIA which has just released 16.000+ lines of codes of their popular graphic drivers. I think we can expect the new VIA drivers will be included in the kernel in less than a year from now.

Most, if not all, major distros have similar policy about releasing new version of their own.

In case of Ubuntu, if you don't feel like "changing the version" so often, you can always stick with the LTS release that will be supported for 3 years.
