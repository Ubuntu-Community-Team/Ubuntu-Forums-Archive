### Post by Impavidus on 2017-02-09
An Ubuntu released in a particular year is lighter than a Windows released in the same year, so Ubuntu is good for slightly older computers. For computers that are even older, we have the lighter flavours Xubuntu (medium-weight) and Lubuntu (light-weight). Those two also have a more classical user interface, more similar to Windows before 8 (I actually never saw a Win 8 or 10 from closeby), so you may find them easier to use. All flavours of Ubuntu have much in common. The difference is the desktop environment and the default applications.

Nobody says your graphics card won't work. You just won't find drivers other than the driver already built-in in the kernel. For those older ATI cards, this driver may be good enough. It usually is.

I find Ubuntu (or Xubuntu now really) easier to use than Windows. Much easier. But of course, my main system has been Linux for over ten years now. And I know not everybody easily adapts to Linux.
