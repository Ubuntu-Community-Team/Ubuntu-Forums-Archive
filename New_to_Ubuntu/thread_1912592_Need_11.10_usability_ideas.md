### Post by GregA on 2012-01-21
Or, you could try Kubuntu, which uses the more standard PC-like interface similar to Win 7 (KDE 4.7x).   At this point in time, KDE's default file manager is Dolphin, which I like quite a bit - - very functional, especially if you enable the double-pane and detail views.

By the end of this year, another suitable alternative (for a PC-like interface) will be the much modified Gnome 3.3x series, but in my view, it's not quite as ready and stable (yet) as KDE 4.7/4.8.

BTW, you'll need at least 2 gb to run KDE with normal to fast response, although you can still get by with 1 gb of ram.
