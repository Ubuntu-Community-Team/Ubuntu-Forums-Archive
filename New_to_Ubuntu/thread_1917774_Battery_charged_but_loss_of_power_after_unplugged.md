### Post by Double.J on 2012-01-30
There is a good likely hood that the battery is on the way out, however this is not always the case - sometimes the sensors need re calibrating - you may have a feature in your bios for this; basically you charge the laptop up for a couple of hours to ensure it's fully charged, then the BIOS monitors the discharge to work out the min/max capacity.

A good tool for monitoring things like your battery state is System Profiler and Benchmark - available in the repos ;)

All the best!

### Post by LinuxFan999 on 2012-01-30
Lithium-ion batteries can be damaged, or even ruined completely, when fully discharged (down to 0%). You will need to buy a new battery, and this time, never let it discharge completely.
