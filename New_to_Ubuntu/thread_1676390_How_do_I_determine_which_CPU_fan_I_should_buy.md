### Post by cascade9 on 2011-01-27
There is no such thing as a 'socket a' fan. 

Not all heatsink fans arel screwed on, some use clips, some have the fan held on plastic tabs (not since the pentium MMX have I seen that) 

What one person thinks of as quiet another might descirbe as loud. Most 'user reviews' are very subjective. BTW, the 'quiet' fans tend to be slow and not move much air. 'Loud' fans tend to spin faster and move a lot more air. So replacing a quiet fan with a loud one could leave you with overheating problems.  

You migth do best to think about a whole new heatsink/fan setup. Gettign pretty hard to find new for socket a though. You could also do what I've done, use a bigger fan held on by only one screw (bigger fans tend move more air for any given RPM and noise level). Thats probably not the best idea of most user though. Or, if you can find one, this could fix your problem- 

[http://www.zalman.com/eng/product/Product_Read.asp?idx=226](http://www.zalman.com/eng/product/Product_Read.asp?idx=226)

You install the bracket, hang a 92mm/120mm fan off that so that its right over the heatsink.

About fans:
1) the smaller they are, the faster they spin
2) the faster they spin, the louder they are

So basically a 60mm fan will always be loud, probably you should search for a <4000 (less than) rpm fan with 3 or 4 pin connector, but you have to check that your motherboard support 3/4 pin fans.

Have a look around with Google and post here what you find.

By the way: 60mm is an uncommon size factor for fans.
And socket A is utterly old.
And a 60mm fan can be less or more noisy, however it will be noisy.
