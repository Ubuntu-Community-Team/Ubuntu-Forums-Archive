### Post by Redmage913 on 2011-09-01
Is your Comcast e-mail web-based (you have to use a web browser to access it), or do you use a program like Thunderbird to download your e-mail?  If it's web-based, it doesn't really matter what operating system you're accessing it from; I'd call Comcast and tell them what's going on, and ask if it's something on your side or theirs. If they say it's on your end, tell them that you wiped the machine, installed Linux, and are still having the same problem.  It'll give them a nice headache, and may even bump you up a level or two in their helpdesk.

### Post by 1clue on 2011-09-01
Another thing to think about is a network sniffer.

Especially if you're connected via wireless network, you could have some other computer on your local network that's infected, and sniffing for email passwords.  Most of the selections in email clients are sending passwords in clear text.  If you really want a scare, load up wireshark and watch it go while you check your email, surf the web or anything else.
