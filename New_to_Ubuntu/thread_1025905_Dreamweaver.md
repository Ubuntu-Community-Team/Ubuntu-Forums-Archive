### Post by igknighted on 2008-12-31
What parts of dreamweaver do you use?  I used it for a long time as just a really fancy text editor with great code complete and syntax highlighting... and in this case Bluefish was the answer for me.  Turns out I like bluefish more than Dreamweaver even.  However, if you use the WYSIWYG (what you see is what you get) part of dreamweaver, there are no really good options in linux.  Kompozer is in sorry state on Ubuntu at the moment, so your best bet is probably Quanta+ (it's in the kdewebdev package).  Of course, gedit and kate/kwrite make great html/css editors too.
