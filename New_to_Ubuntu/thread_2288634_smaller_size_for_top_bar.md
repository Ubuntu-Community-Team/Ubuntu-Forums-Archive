### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-07-28
The width of the Launcher in Unity, and the icons in it, can be adjusted in System Settings. I believe the icons default to a width a 48px, which is not especially small.

If you want a desktop *like* Windows, then Windows is the place to be. Linux is not a cost-free clone of Windows.

The KDE desktop environment, IMO, most closely follows (mimics?) the design patterns of Windows 7.  It's used in Kubuntu, and can also be installed from the Ubuntu repositories.

### Post by NathanRodriguez on 2015-07-28
These are called GNOME panels, and can be configured right-clicking on them and selecting properties. You can even delete them if you prefer a single panel desktop, but take care of adding the panel applets (windows, menus, indicators, ...) that you use back in the single panel.

### Post by claracc on 2015-07-29
As far as I see, you are using gnome flashback desktop.

You can configure your top or bottom bar by means of alt+right click in the bar, then press properties and there, in the general tab you can select the place to put the bar (it can be only bottom if you want), the size and the behaviour (automativally hide,...).
