### Post by Lars Noodén on 2013-10-12
In addition to Linphone, there are also Blink, Ekiga and Jitsi.  All four can talk to each other because they use SIP, but AFAIK Skype cannot.  So you will need to install one of these parallel to Skype at least at the beginning as you phase out Skype.  Your contacts will also need one of the four to be able to talk with you.  A further advantage is that they *probably* don't have the [surveillance that is built into Skype](http://www.theguardian.com/technology/2013/oct/11/skype-ten-microsoft-nsa).  

I made the transition from Skype a while ago and one of the improvements I notice is (usually) better sound quality.  There can be a bit of an echo sometimes, but some of that is dependent on how the other party is set up hardwarewise.  Skype always sounded distorted and SIP sounds clearer.  And the Skype calls often broke off, needing reconnection.

### Post by Lars Noodén on 2013-10-12
You can call directly to an ip address but the better way is to get an SIP account, that is mentioned at the [top of the FAQ](http://www.linphone.org/eng/documentation/faq.html).  

Linphone provides that service, so does SIP2SIP, Ekiga and others.  
[http://www.linphone.org/eng/linphone/register-a-linphone-account.html](http://www.linphone.org/eng/linphone/register-a-linphone-account.html)
[https://mdns.sipthor.net/register_sip_account.phtml](https://mdns.sipthor.net/register_sip_account.phtml)
[https://www.ekiga.net/?page=register](https://www.ekiga.net/?page=register)

There are others if you look around, but they are all free of charge.  You can call out to land lines on some of them, but to do that you'll have to register a credit card or some other payment system.
