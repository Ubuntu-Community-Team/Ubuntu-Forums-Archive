### Post by t.rei on 2010-03-24
In that case the BEST of all linux is really easy:

All of them :D 

Because it's really up to the individual. 

I like it for that: you make the computer assist the way you want to work. You are not forced to adapt to the way the computer works.

So yes, customisation - also know as 'fiddling around' by critics - is a major part of 'using a free user interface and operating system'.


So. Among those sites you should definately have this forum. It's rather indiscriminate regarding the distribution (even though it is called ubuntuforums) and a HUGE load of help and hundreds of helpfull people.

And you probably want to point out that it's all a matter of taste. 

Some like light, some like feature rich, some like option dialogs, some vi configfiles, some need all apps to look integrated, some need them to BE integrated...

You will need to focus. Pick a few 'typical ones'.

Like:
KDE
Gnome / XFCE4
LXDE
openbox / blackbox / fluxbox / compiz / metacity ...

emerald / gtk-window-decorator / kde-window-decorator / ...

how to:
install them, try them, get rid of them again, make backups of configs, playback configs, ...

depending on choice:
customisation of various parts...


oh dear, I hope you got lots of coffee in the house. ;) Also you might want to think about looking at [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/) . It's just about ubuntu and not quite up-to-date-in-all-ways (I don't think thats quite possible with the speed of development/improvement). But if anything has a chance to be up-to-date its a wiki or a million of blogs. ;)

### Post by andy1964 on 2010-03-25
Hi
 
Try [www.goinglinux.com]("http://www.goinglinux.com").
It has a podcast for linux newbies through to non-experts. You can go through their archives (about 95 shows) and select the specific topic you are interested in (e.g. installing apts, choosing the corect worprocessors, command line stuff: begineer and advanced), installing a printer, etc). 
It's done by two american chaps who seem down to earth and don't look to 'show off' by being too geeky. They also have a pleasant turn of humour. No adverts in the podcasts (except the odd one for other related podcasts).
 
This podacst is a consistent high scorer in podcast alley and similar. so I'm not the only one who rates it.
