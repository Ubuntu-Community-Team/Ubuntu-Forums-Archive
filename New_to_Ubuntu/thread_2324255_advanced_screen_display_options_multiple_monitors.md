### Post by Tom_Lee on 2016-05-12
Yes you can have a different wallpaper on each monitor, have them selected from a folder of photos you have compiled and have them rotate in whatever interval you want. 

I know that LinuxLite and Ununtu studio come with this feature built in with a a GUI right in the control panel. 

I am not sure if that is a native feature of the desktop they use or if it's an app built on to the settings/control center. 

Try searching synaptic or Ubuntu Software for Ubuntu Studio control center or "settings" and see if you can't just replace you control center. 

The one in Studio does FAR more and is far easier to navigate than any other distro I've tried. And my 3 monitors on my lock screen are in the proper arrangement which hasn't happened with any other distro. 

Also, your graphics card, got a Nvidia? If you installed the 351 (or 352) proprietary driver you now have the Nvidia control center which lets you do a TON of additional things when configuring your displays. 

Also you could search Ubuntu Software for "Tweak" and test drive the MANY options that may make this easy to do. Or search wallpaper and try some of those apps. Or search display manager etc. 

Also PinguyOS comes with a wallpaper switcher called Variety that downloads new wallpaper at whatever interval you set. Pick from a list of wallpaper sources, set and forget. Until it freezes. That app tended to do that. Like the Studio and LinuxLite option for wallpaper much better. Variety is in the "Ubuntu After Install" batch of apps. It's a slim and quick installer and you just tick on the apps you wanna have (VLC, Libre, Chrome etc). I highly recommend touching in on that if you haven't already. 

Maybe someone her knows the name of the one LinuxLite and Ubuntu Studio use. 

I like them. VERY lightweight and easy to use. 

Hope that helps.
