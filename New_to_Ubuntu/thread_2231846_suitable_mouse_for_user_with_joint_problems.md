### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-06-28
I had reasonably good luck with the Logitech Trackman Marble: [http://www.logitech.com/en-us/product/trackman-marble](http://www.logitech.com/en-us/product/trackman-marble).  Here's an Ubuntu wiki page about potential issues and tweaks: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Logitech_Marblemouse_USB](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Logitech_Marblemouse_USB).  

Linux drivers are typically included in the kernel, so don't assume you need to go looking around for something to download and install, as in Windows. Plug the mouse in, reboot just to be double-sure the kernel is aware of it, and see what happens. 

Good luck.

### Post by Nosphky on 2014-06-28
I have been using logitech trackman marbles for over ten years and never had a problem with the hardware.  When I changed from Windows7 to Ubuntu a couple of months back, Ubuntu just recognised the trackman.  I haven't had to do anything special - it just worked.I use the trackball version (not the mouse) because it is larger than the average mouse and my hand sits on it without the wrist bones touching the tabletop.  And you don't need space to move the trackball about.
