### Post by cbt on 2010-07-07

_[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1516239](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1516239)_ (Please take one minute to read this thread)

> **cbt said:**
> I am sure my backup does not contain any executable scripts.  
I deleted my entire home directory.  
I did a clean reinstallation of ubuntu.  
Now I use a different root password. 


These are sensible precautions, and will have eliminated any risk.

> Is it possible to install malware by only knowing my IP number (without my intervention)?

In short, no. Every time you access anything on the internet the machine at the other end knows your IP address. You're no more at risk of infection from that than you're at risk of being burgled because someone knows your home address. What attackers need to infect you is an exploit of some kind, be that a flaw in the software, or a social engineering attack.

Your "friend" sounds like a pretty clueless script kiddie, I wouldn't stress too much that his 1337 skillz have put you at any great risk. 

Giving anyone your password is obviously a bad idea, i'm guessing you've learned your lesson there.
