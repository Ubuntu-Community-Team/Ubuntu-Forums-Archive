### Post by Temposs on 2009-12-17
Since we know you're interested in programming, you might just open up the gedit text editor, which is the "equivalent" of Notepad in Windows. But, gedit is quite powerful, and I use it for most of my programming. Go to Applications->Accessories->Text Editor. 

You'll notice that gedit has built in automatic code coloring for most any language you can throw at it. There are also tons of plugins to enable more IDE-ish functionality. For instance, you can go to the Edit->Plugins in the menu and see a bunch of plugins you can enable. 

There are more available on the web and from the advanced version of Add/Remove, which is at System->Administration->Synaptic Package Manager. On the web, you can get more plugins at: [http://live.gnome.org/Gedit/Plugins](http://live.gnome.org/Gedit/Plugins)

Of course, gedit is just one way to do your programming.
