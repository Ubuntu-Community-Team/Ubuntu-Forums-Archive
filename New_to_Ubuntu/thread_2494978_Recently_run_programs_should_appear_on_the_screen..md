### Post by MAFoElffen on 2024-02-04
A lot of what was said, if new, might be going over your head. I think if you understood how Linux works (overall), this might help you.

A Linux distribution is a collection of Linux pieces and applications grouped together, sometimes with their own modifications, or flavors of how they do things. Those many pieces are interchangeable, to modify into your own liking.

The Desktop Environment, and what is displayed in the application chooser is just one of those interchangeable pieces.

The Desktop Environment for Ubuntu is Gnome Based, with a few Ubuntu modifications to make it appear as it's own. The Gnome Desktop is governed by the upstream Gnome Foundation and it's own developers.

Does that explain some of the above comments to you better?

Being the Desktop is interchangeable, you are free to change it... As was said above.
