### Post by illu45 on 2008-05-05
Agreed. Just go to the [Download Site]("http://www.opera.com/download/linux/"). Select Ubuntu from the drop-down, choose your version of Ubuntu (go to System->About Ubuntu to find your version), and click on download. Once it has downloaded, navigate to your downloads folder (Desktop by default) via Nautilus and double-click the .deb file and hit Install on the top-right. It is really quite a simple procedure, especially when compared to building from source.

EDIT: Well, it is not difficult to install Firefox 2 if you want more stability (I believe that it is even available in the repos). I think the reason for choosing FF3 was to stick to the latest available software. It isn't as stable as it could be, but its certainly not bad in terms of stability, IMHO.
