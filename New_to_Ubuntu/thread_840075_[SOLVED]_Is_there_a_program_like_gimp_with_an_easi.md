### Post by starcannon on 2008-06-25
I may be wrong, and someone please let me know if I am, but I believe Gimp is to Linux what Photoshop is to Windows and Mac.

If your wanting a full on, no holds barred image editing software The Gimp is it. There are lots of other graphics softwares for Linux, some very powerful and specialized, but as far as Photoshop like, gimp is the ticket.

I would likely encourage you to take some time to learn the gimp basics instead of looking for an alternative, heres an E-book and a few websites to get you started:

[Grokking The Gimp]("http://gimp-savvy.com/BOOK/")

Some beginners guides:
[Very Basic]("http://www.associatedcontent.com/article/420179/beginners_guide_to_gimp.html?cat=59")

[A great set of guides]("http://www.gimp-tutorials.com/tutorial/Beginners-Guide-to-The-GIMP-449.html")

[Learn the Interface]("http://glinted.angelskies.net/glinted/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=215&Itemid=47")

And as always Google is your friend:
[http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&sa=X&oi=spell&resnum=0&ct=result&cd=1&q=beginners+guide+to+the+gimp&spell=1](http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&sa=X&oi=spell&resnum=0&ct=result&cd=1&q=beginners+guide+to+the+gimp&spell=1)

GL, have fun, and give yourself time to learn your new software, I know life is busy, but 10~15 minutes a day of working at it, and in no time you'll be posting guides, advice, and how to's on the gimp forums.
