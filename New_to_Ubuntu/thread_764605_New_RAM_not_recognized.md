### Post by Tatty on 2008-04-24
It sounds like a problem with your bios not recognising your stick somewhere.  Out of curiosity, try taking out your other 512 stick and put your 2gb stick in its slot, so only your 2gb stick is in your machine.  See what happens with that...

### Post by nicedude on 2008-04-24
You can't just add one stick like that if you go to your pc or motherboard makers website and get the specs to see what all your pc does support.

### Post by kilroy423 on 2008-04-24
Some mobos are picky about what kind of memory you put in. ex: mixing different sizes / speed / slot / kind

Make sure you have the correct memory for your mobo.

Kilroy

### Post by Humph on 2008-04-24
I'd also check the documentation for your motherboard. It may not support 2GB sticks, or it may only support up to 2GB. You could also check the motherboard manufacturer's website for BIOS updates which address this issue. Are you mixing ECC memory with non-ECC memory?

But first off I'd follow Tatty's suggestion of just having the 2GB stick in by itself.
