### Post by JeQhdMD on 2015-01-07
Remember the launcher at left will expand as you move your mouse over it at bottom of screen.   Also, Ubuntu Unity desktop has a great tool at the top of the launcher called the "dash" (as in dashboard).   It will give you easy access to all your program icons (just goto bottom of screen and click the Apps symbol (the letter "A") . . . then click "see more results" of installed programs.   Couldn't be easier and visually appealling (it IT terms, "elegant").   Kinda like what you see using a tablet or smartphone which should be intuitive for most folks under 60 or so.

### Post by PondPuppy on 2015-01-07
I don't use the left-side application dock much myself.

I think what the OP may want is desktop icons. deadflowr gave you one way to do it; here are some other ways: [http://askubuntu.com/questions/450266/an-easy-way-to-create-a-desktop-shortcut](http://askubuntu.com/questions/450266/an-easy-way-to-create-a-desktop-shortcut) 

You could consider "classic menu indicator", which displays a menu in Gnome 2 style. Or CairoDock, which is a configurable application dock.

+1 to JeQhMD for mentioning the Dash -- just click on the "windows" icon on most keyboards, type the first few letters of the program you want to launch -- "gedi" for GEdit, for instance -- and hit "return". Hey presto, the program is running. Very fast as long as you remember the program name.
