### Post by flyznest on 2008-10-29
I have been using the k200d with Ubuntu 8.04 since august when I bought the camera...  works flawlessly.  USB recognizes it as soon as i plug it in...  I use gimp to process my images...  its been effortless.  Most of what I have been doing is long exposure wide field astrophotography.  the camera feels sturdy in my hand.  weather resistant seals, stainless lens mount, easy and intuitive menu navigation.  After a few months of playing around with it, I have come to prefer the AA battery idea over proprietary rechargeables...  I have a set of NiMH rechargeables that I normally use, I keep a pack of e2 lithium in my camera bag just in case...  and one time I was in a pinch and bought some cheapo alkalines at a 7-11..  worked just fine!!!

I have no problem with the image quality.  In my opinion, image quality is really about the glass, not the body, but even the kit lens that came with the k200d isn't too shabby for most things...  

as far as using your old lenses..  It will definitely take almost any screw mount or k mount lens you can throw at it BUT I have heard some nightmares of people with old chinon and ricoh k mount lenses getting STUCK on the body requiring a hacksaw and drill to remove..  not pretty..  you might wanna consult your camera shop before slapping some unknown lens on there.  I have tried a few older pentax branded lenses(screw and K mount) and have had no problems yet...  the older all manual lenses take a lil effort to setup but if you have the glass you might as well use it....  

The only real negative that I have found so far is userbase... not many people use the pentax yet so getting new lenses/accessories is still sort of a specialized market and most small camera shops don't carry anything.  ymmv.
