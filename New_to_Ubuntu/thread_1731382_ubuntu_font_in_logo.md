### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-04-17
I am not a legal expert but I guess that with fonts the issue is not so much how you use the fonts but do you have permission to distribute or even sell copies of the font file? With open source fonts I guess you have permission to make copies for distribution but you may not claim that you are the owner of the copyright. We certainly do not have permission to sell copies of open source fonts.

Images are completely different. Ubuntu images will be copyrighted by Canonical and you will need permission to use them. I know that you were asking only about fonts but I include this information as a comparison of the different approaches to open source products as I understand it.

As a legally ignorant person I do not think that you will be breaking any laws or even the spirit of any laws if you create an image that had words on it created using the Ubuntu font that is distributed freely in an installation of Ubuntu.

Regards.
