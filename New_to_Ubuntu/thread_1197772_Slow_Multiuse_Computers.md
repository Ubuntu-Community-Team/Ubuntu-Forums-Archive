### Post by Bölvaður on 2009-06-26
depends onhow much ram you have. but if you have 1 gig or more, then you shouldnt have much problem having **many** programs simultaneously open.

Linux has very good memory management and should multitask better, so no need only using 1 program at a time, also do your taxes while you listen to music, instant message, browse these forums and play tetris.

You will not get any good answers about what video editing program to use, some are good, many are not... you'll have to try them all out your self :)

### Post by darrelljon on 2009-06-26
Just having more software installed doesn't seem to slow Linux down in the same way as Windows.
I wouldn't worry about installed applications, certainly having four primary partitions is excessive. You also have far greater control over what applications you choose to run at the same time in Linux.

[There is video of Linux running 168 apps at once here]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LaGpRIKCWSE").
[Compare this to Vista only running 108 apps at gizmodo]("http://gizmodo.com/377255/vista-running-108-apps-bites-back-mac-os-x-butt").
