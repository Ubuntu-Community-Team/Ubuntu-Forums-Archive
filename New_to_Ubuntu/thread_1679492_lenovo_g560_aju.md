As an Eee PC user, I've got to say I really love my 900SD.  I have a 16" Toshiba that hardly ever comes out of the cupboard except for when I really need that big screen for presentations etc.  The Eee PC is the one I want to carry so it's there when I need it.  My 900 is a little small, especially the keyboard.  The 1015 would be just large enough to be really nice, but still small enough to carry.

My 900SD has G3 which I wasn't too worried about, but now find it really useful as I can connect anywhere anytime.

The G560 should run the 64 bit Ubuntu: it would really kick A*.  The 1015 is 32 bit and comes with 1GB RAM standard.  It's not a huge amount but sufficient for most things on Ubuntu as you know.  The suitability would really depend on how you want to use it.  The two machines are quite different.  Possibly the most attractive thing about the 1015 is the humungous battery life.  Even if I didn't get the claimed maximum of 13 hours I'd still be happy with 9+ hours.  One of the biggest limiting factors of laptops (apart from size) is the lousy battery life.  It's never long enough and you're always looking for a socket to plug into.

I can't comment on compatibility except to say that the ASUS can be considered more "mainstream" (ie popular/common) so if there are any problems you should be able to find them through searching.  Generally most big issues seem to revolve around graphics and networking problems, but with Ubuntu can include lots of niggly things like battery life indicators and problems controlling peripheral devices like wireless cards.

The 1015 comes loaded with Windoze 7 Starter which is apparently pretty awful.  I wouldn't plan on using it at all.
