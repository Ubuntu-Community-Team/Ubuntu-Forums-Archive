### Post by markbuntu on 2008-06-16
If you really need to adjust the opacity and color saturation of each individual pixel in your photos or any other picture/drawing then gimp is the thing for you. It has been around a long time and has a zillion extensions and plugins to do everything photoshop does and more, better and faster. 

Many large commercial shops and major networks like fox and espn use variations of gimp and other open source software for video editing and manipulation. Rendering with windows is just too slow and unstable for those instant playbacks and highlight shows.

Xara and Inkscape are the best for vector graphics. Each has its own strength so you need to try them out to find out what's best for you. They have also been around a long time. Xara I think has been around for 15 years or so. I use it to make moving and scaling titles and other text insertion and manipulation in videos etc.

The best thing is, they are all free and there are thousands of people around who will help you out when you get stuck on something, also for free. You can even talk to the people who wrote the programs sometimes.
