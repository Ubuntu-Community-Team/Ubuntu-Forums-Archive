### Post by blackbird34 on 2013-04-07
I like Firefox for the multiple profiles you can create, you can have one profile on your main computer sync'ed with, say, your office or university install of firefox, and another just sync'ed up to the Firefox Sync server. 
To get to the profile selector, type ```
firefox -P --no-remote
``` in terminal, as per the attached screenshot. You then untick the "Don't ask at startup" and then every time you start firefox you get that window; you can have as many separate profiles as you want. THis has the advantage that you can have a really heavily laden profile with dozens of addons, and a lighter one to offer to other users of your computer :D
Firefox just is way more configurable, and even the default bare browser has lots of useful features (like the infinitely extensible tab bar/ chrome just tries to squish all your tabs into the width of the screen, its hopeless when you've got 40 or so open)
