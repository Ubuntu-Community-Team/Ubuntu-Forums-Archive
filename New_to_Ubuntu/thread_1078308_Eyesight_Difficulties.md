### Post by Panzor on 2009-02-23
Well, if you go to the top panel and hit System>>Preferences>>Appearance, then hit the "fonts" tab, you should have a choice of what kind and what point font to use. To enlarge the top and bottom panels, right click them and go to Properties, adjusting the pixel width (Length?) from there. The panels may auto-resize when the fonts are changed.

### Post by bob-linux-user on 2009-02-23
In addition to what Panzor said. If while on the fonts tab you press details at the bottom right you can crank up the dots per inch settings. This will make the icons on the desktop bigger.You may need to log in and out to get the full effect. 

You might also to be interested to know there is a Linux distro called Vinux which is basically Ubuntu with some extras for partially sighted.

As an aside I always wondered why there isn't a high contrast NON inverse scheme included by default. My Father has macular degeneration and he has his Windows PC set to what would be the equivalent of the inverse of the high contrast scheme that Ubuntu provide. I.e he has black print on white background as much as possible.

Hope this helps.
