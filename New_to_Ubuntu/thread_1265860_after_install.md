### Post by earthpigg on 2009-09-14
> **themusicalduck said:**
> That's the one. The top option when you boot from the CD. It'll be slow but you can test everything there.

+1, and +1 again for "It'll be slow..."

it will be very slow. clicking on a menu may take you five seconds from the live CD -- the CD drive is not read _**nearly**_ as fast as your hard drive will be.

but you can test if it works at all or not, and that is the important part.

and the nice thing about learning how to set stuff up on the live cd: if you install the wrong stuff and mess it up, **none of your mistakes will be saved, either.**

which means that when it is installed on your hard drive, you will be able to make things work on your first try... you have already done it before, so you know how.

Ubuntu installed will look and work exactly like ubuntu on the live cd, except there will be no 'install' icon on the desktop, and any changes (*or mistakes*) you make will be saved.


even after ubuntu is installed, it is sometimes good to try things out that you aren't sure about from the live cd for that reason.
