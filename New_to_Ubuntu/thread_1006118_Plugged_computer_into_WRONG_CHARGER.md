Absolutely none...

The value for the amps is merely the maximum the charger can put out.
The load will determine how much of that maximum will actually be used.

The Aspire One draws less then 1.58 amps, and the 3.52 amp charger will put out that much (max of 1.58 amo), no more.

The Aspire 3000 on the 1.52amp charger will probalbly have charging difficulties; it should run OK, but the  battery may take a long  time to charge, or may not charge at all;
unless the charging circuit is marginal, then you may have over-heating problems... a well-designed circuit should just shut down.

And yes, I run 'bigger' chargers on my laptops all the time.
No problems in over six years. (IBM Thinkpads).

And one final thought....

Your home wiring is rated at some 200amps... let's split that for 120volts, say 100amps. You don't see  your electrical items going up in smoke, do you?

### Post by zxscooby on 2008-12-09
The only problem i could see is if you plugged the charger with the smaller amperage rating into the computer designed for
the charger with the higher amperage rating. It could possibly
damage the charger itself because it is trying to draw more
amperage than the circuitry can provide. Putting the smaller laptop on the bigger charger would only create a situation where
the charger is simply not being used to its fill capacity , that
is actually a very safe condition. 
 Plus or minus 2 amps is a very acceptable margin of error for
something like a dc power supply so i wouldn't worry to much about it. Any safe circuit design that is UL approved would allow for that much error and then some.
 If the voltage was different , that is another story because you would be forcing more energy into a circuit designed for less, possibly releasing the magic smoke from your laptop, but even then +/- 2 volts is marginal for a common power supply.
