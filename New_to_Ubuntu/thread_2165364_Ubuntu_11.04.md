### Post by The Cog on 2013-08-04
When you start the installer CD, you have the option of installing or just trying it from the CD without installing. So I suggest you download and create the installer and just try it. This should give you an idea if it works, and if you're happy then go ahead and install. 

Just avoid the option to erase the disk when you install - this would also erase windows. And make a backup first, just in case of errors.

Yes, you get a choice of which OS to load when it boots.
