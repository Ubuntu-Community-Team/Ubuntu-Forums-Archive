### Post by Rasa1111 on 2011-05-07
It may be better to install restricted extras from synaptic package manager,
as the "accept terms of service" when using a terminal confuses many new people. 

But using synaptic, you're given a simple GUI "check box" that is easier for most new people. 

So, if you want to do it that way..

 Go to your *_ "Administration"_* Menu, and open "*_synaptic package manager_*"

Then type 'ubuntu restricted extras' into the search bar at the top-right.
When you see it pop up in the list,  click it and choose "install", click 'OK' when asked, and then hit the "apply" button at the top. 

 When it's ready it will give you a little window that asks you to accept the terms of service, then just put a check in the box, and it will finish.

 This will give you flash, java, music and video codecs of all kinds..
So you should not have a problem playing any kind of media.

 Good luck.
