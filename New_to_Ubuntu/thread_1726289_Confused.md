### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-04-11
'Real' software jobs can create all kinds of software for all kinds of business needs.

* One engineer I know writes programs for chips that are embedded into helicopter control panels.

* Another I know writes video games.

* I write business software for Inventory Control & Point Of Sale systems.

Today, programmers are often working on specific 'layers' of complex applications:

* One may create database layouts and rules, and become an expert in SQL statements.

* Another programmer on the same project may create Data Entry screens (character based or GUI)

* Another programmer may just work on the Printed and on-screen reports for the same project.

The exact nature of what any programmer does depends on the project currently being worked on.

When learning to program, you will do all the steps from start (designing what you want to make) to finish (testing the code you wrote and finding the bugs to fix).

I hope this answers your question...
