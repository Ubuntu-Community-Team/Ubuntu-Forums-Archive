### Post by Roving Sign on 2009-01-21
Hi newbie here...just wanted to report a "solution" to some confusion I was experiencing.

I think a lot of us who are trying Ubuntu, start off on an older machine/hardware. My first experiment I used an old 32MB ASUS/Riva TNT video card. I just connected the experiment box to my everyday 19 LCD. In that setup, the TNT seemed to work just fine, and did everything I asked, resolution-wise.

Later, I pulled an old Samsung CRT out of storage and started out to re-build my sons computer using Ubuntu (was XP - which had a HD fail)

No matter what(using the Samsung CRT) - I could not get the TNT card to display the all the resolutions that I had before. I was limited to 1024 X 768. 

I did some Ubuntu forum searching, and found some posts where people were looking for resolutions they _knew_ the card supported, but weren't available in the settings. Various solutions were posed.

Wanting to get the computer back to my son - I just put it back on his 19 widescreen LCD...start...and viola! - All my higher resolutions are back!

My conclusion is monitors arent just dumb blocks, and some seem report some info back to the card/OS.

I didnt check (yet) to see if a different driver loaded...but I suspect its still the same.

So - If you are struggling with something like this - try a different monitor.

Thanks for this nice OS guys - I hope be windows-free by the end of the month!
