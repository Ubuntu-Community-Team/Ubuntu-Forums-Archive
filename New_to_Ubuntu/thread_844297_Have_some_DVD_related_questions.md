### Post by Canis familiaris on 2008-06-29
I am not  lawyer or expert in these matters but it is illegal in USA due to the Digital Millenium Copyright Act, patents, etc.
It is allegedly illegal to use such stuff in Linux/Ubuntu because Ubuntu being a free distro has not paid for the codecs. 
Microsoft had paid for these stuff for its Windows Media Player and the other media players available in Windows like of Cyberlink PowerDVD or Interlink WinDVD have paid seperately for the codecs.
Ideally a copy of WinDVD or PowerDVD should entitle you to play DVDs in your PC with any software but at least according to the laws you have to seperately purchase license for different software.
So even playing DVDs in VLC in Windows is illegal according to the laws.
But no body has been prosecuted yet.
But to say the truth, since nothing is morally wrong in installing these codecs, so go ahead and install them. No body will come searching to your computer. ;)
So go ahead install VLC in Ubuntu and enjoy those DVDs.

To purchase rights you might consider Fluendo.

### Post by Rhubarb on 2008-06-29
It's not just a codec issue (it is also a DMCA issue).
libdvdcss2 is the program that decrypts encrypted DVDs.
It decrypts DVDs in a very open way such that you could do more than just watch a DVD, you could then burn the unencrypted streams back on to a blank DVD / copy to your hard drive etc.

While I'm not a lawyer (I don't even reside in the USA), breaking encryption schemes is illegal (in USA).
But USA also has fair-use, in that I think you can copy DVDs / watch DVDs in Ubuntu so long as it's for personal / research use only.
So I'm not exactly sure on it's legal status, but I do know that it's not currently being policed.

I doubt if you just walk into a police station and ask them there :P
