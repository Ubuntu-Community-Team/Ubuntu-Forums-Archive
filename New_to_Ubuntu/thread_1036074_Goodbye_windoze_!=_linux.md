### Post by Kellemora on 2009-01-10
Hi Duds

If you like Fedora, there is a possibility you may like CentOS better, it's the full blown Red Hat clone!  So ANYTHING that's designed for Red Hat Enterprise will be the same in CentOS.

That's one I was using before finding Ubuntu when I was testing out Debian and Debian based Distro's.

In my case, a lot of things that worked just fine in CentOS, didn't work on Ubuntu, many still don't as Debian does not support a lot of the things supported by Red Hat, floppy drives for one, Meeces for another, Keyboards for another, etc.
BUT - Ubuntu is more LOGICAL for us olde geezers to handle, hi hi and I found little to NO support on the forums for CentOS.  I think they assume that most CentOS users do so because they use Red Hat under a paid support team at their job.

But I made my decision to convert my entire office over to Ubuntu and it's a decision I'm very glad I made.
I still run Debian and CentOS on a few dual boot machines, for when I need to do some things I can't do in Ubuntu and AVOID the DOZE at all costs if I can.

Like you said, the whole point is to get out from under the DOZE and BREATH some fresh air!
