### Post by Cypher on 2008-11-01
Most new CD/DVD burners include technology to do good writes at faster speed and also have a bunch of memory on board to deal with any buffering issues.

I usually burn my ISOs at the fastest speed and on my drive that's about 33-35x and the CD's work fine. I've also burned audio and data CD's at the fastest possible rate and never had any issues with them.

With the older drives and slower machines, it was needed to slow down the burn rate to ensure that the buffer was always available or you'd get underrun or coasters at the end of the burn process..
