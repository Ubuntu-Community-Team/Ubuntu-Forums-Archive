### Post by egalvan on 2009-04-07
Yes, those are fairly good specs...

The Sempron CPU is, of course, rather limited...
it has smaller cache and slower busses...

I had an HP with a 3200 Sempron, and replaced it with a 3000 Athlon 64, and the difference was quite noticable..
but it did cost me $50... it was a Socket 754..
a newer Socket 939 or Socket AM would be cheaper...
so it depends on your motherboard, and your wallet of course.

The video card is fair, I've got a Dell running that same card, and it handles video (DVD play-back) rather well.
Trying to upgrade it may be problematic.
If it's an AGP type, you should be able to find a cheap alternative.
PCI is much harder to up-grade.
Stick to nVidia if you go this route.

As for RAM,  you are doing rather well...
more RAM  will help when you have a lot of packages open at the same time...

Most likely, you have un-needed services starting at boot-time.
Scanning the list of running processes may be a good starting point in finding slow-downs...

System -> Administration -> System Monitor

will give you lots of info...
