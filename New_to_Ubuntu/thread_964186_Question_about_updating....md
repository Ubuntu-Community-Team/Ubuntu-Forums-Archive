### Post by nynoah on 2008-10-30
Notice all the problems with people on the board right now about updating to Ibex.  I would not upgrade over an existing install.  Yes it can be done.  But I would say that most people on here have stuff that is not part of the standard Ubuntu install.  Things like graphics drivers glich up when people upgrade.  If you turn off your proprietary graphics drivers reboot and then upgrade, you MIGHT be safe.  But really the best thing to always do is just do a clean install.

### Post by celticbhoy on 2008-10-30
An upgrade is not a clean install. Some software will upgrade to a new version, and the config file for the version you are using now might not be compatable. However on the whole the vast majority of your setting will work, assuming that there are no problems during download or upgrade. It is advisable to back up any all your settings and data beforehand just incase anything drastic goes wrong.

### Post by nynoah on 2008-10-30
after more than 2 years on Ubuntu, I would say yes.  It certainly will save you hassle in the long run.  Just back up your files you want to keep and do a fresh install.  After you back them up, IF you want to try an upgrade instead of a fresh install, try that.  But I would already have a 8.10 disk burnt ready to use if your computer goes kaput.  Turn of your graphics drivers and reboot prior to your upgrade that should increase your chances of a good up grade.
