### Post by Bullishonsilver on 2016-01-03
I very recently installed Ubuntu clean over a Windows 8.1 laptop. Firefox was slow & jerky. None of the recommended fixes worked. I am posting this what I did here because I have never seen it recommended, even though it is very obvious and something I just explored for myself without advice.

Edit>Preferences atop the Firefox browser page. Under >Advanced tab:

I changed the Connection to "Auto-detect proxy settings for this network" 
I clicked to Overide Auto Cache Management, and I set the Cache limit to 1000MB.  (I don't know why I went to 1000MB, just seemed like a cool number.)

________
Prior to this, I had gone into the Ubuntu System Settings (located in upper right corner of desktop, as dial gear icon). I changed the IPv4 settings (under Wired). This was a suggestion posted on the Ubuntu blog. Only thing is, the data entries I made I took from another computer I have, for which I recently migrated to Ubuntu and did not have Firefox problems; I used this data, rather than that recommended in the blog post. Yet, doing this fixed nothing. .... Perhaps it was when today I then instructed Firefox to Autu-detect (as mentioned above), that Firefox looked for the change I had made to IPv4 Wired connection.  

Bottom line is that Firefox is now working VERY fast and a lot better than when I had any version of Windows on any computer(s). This is what I had wanted from Ubuntu, and why I migrated here.
