### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-04-09
I think the OP raises a good point.

For the time being, I would suggest that the bank stays with Open Office.

However, be open and honest! Tell the bank that Oracle has not committed itself to leaving Open Office free of charge. The managers have the choice to make a seamless changeover to Libre Office, which is identical to Open Office apart from some minor error fixes, or to stay with Open Office. Tell the bank that Libre Office may, at some stage, start to differ significantly from Open Office, but that Open Office documents and Libre Office documents will always remain 100% compatible.

If you put it honestly like that to the bank, then the bank can make an informed decision. The bank will need to consider, at highest priority, reliability and continuity. From the bank's point of view, staying with Oracle probably would seem the best path, unless finance is a concern.
