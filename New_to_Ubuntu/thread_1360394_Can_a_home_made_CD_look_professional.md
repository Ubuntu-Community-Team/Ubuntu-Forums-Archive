### Post by Temposs on 2009-12-20
I think that this program probably creates images that can be printed out on standard form pages for different purposes, like CD-label stickers and book covers. It won't actually paint the image on your CD.

The only way I know of for an image to be painted right on the CD is to have a CD/DVD drive with the LightScribe technology built in, and to have the appropriate LightScribe disks to make them on. Here's the Wikipedia entry for it:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LightScribe](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LightScribe)

### Post by Vaphell on 2009-12-20
well, i don't think that burnable cds will ever look like the real thing. Pressed disks are created as physical negatives of a master disk and the data layer is made of metal. Burnable disks use chemical substances that are manipulated by light (laser) - that's why they are not metallic in look and detoriate faster - exposure to light affects the data layer and slowly destroys data.

### Post by reiki on 2010-05-31
stick-on labels are terrible abominations. Don't use them. If they are the slightest bit off center it can REALLY wreak havoc as the out of balance condition is exaggerated at the speed a CD spins. Go with either printable media and a disk printer or get a Lightsribe capable drive and media. Lightscribe is monochromatic whereas a disk printer prints in color. I've used both. For home use I prefer the lightscribe. I had an Epson inkjet printer that was made to also print on disk media. It worked ok, but I have grown to hate inkjets and their cartridges.

Oh and linux (Ubuntu) handled both lightscribe and the Epson just fine.
