Essentially - yes.  Except you can skip step 2.  Ubuntu will format the HD for you when installing (if you use the Guided - Use entire disk option).  And step 4 - it will offer to install, but there will be some options during that.

I second the [Psychocats]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu") tutorial to walk you through the process.

Lotus Smartsuite is now pretty aged, and I'd suggest you try out OpenOffice as a (free) alternative.  Lotus themselves have moved to an OpenOffice based product: [http://symphony.lotus.com/software/lotus/symphony/home.nsf/home](http://symphony.lotus.com/software/lotus/symphony/home.nsf/home) - but I gather OpenOffice 3 is still better.
