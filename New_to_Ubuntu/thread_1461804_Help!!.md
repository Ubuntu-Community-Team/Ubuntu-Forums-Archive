### Post by 2hot6ft2 on 2010-04-24
Turn it off and stand it on edge so the affected area is at the bottom leaning to the corner most affected and let it set overnight before turning it back on.
Most likely some windex got into the screen and will drain downwards.
When booting up it's trying to send a signal thru the windex making it slow because it's creating interference with the signal.
If it's still messed up tomorrow you may have to replace the screen, but not the whole pc, unless you got in inside the other part of it.

Windex is not for cleaning computer screens. A dry soft cloth would be better or one that is slightly damp. Never spray the screen directly with fluids.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2010-04-26
Screen wipes .. available at any big box office store and at some chain drug stores.  Alsop being the premier brand.

IF there is any (ANY!!!!!!) liquid that has penetrated the screen .. the best removal tool is bagged desiccant .. but hard to find in any normal store.

Second best is a large plastic bag .. put the unit in the bag and take a sock and fill it with soda crackers and another sock and fill it with uncooked INSTANT rice .. put both socks in the bag, seal it up and leave it for a day or so.  That will suck the moisture out.

### Post by steveneddy on 2010-04-26
When I do clean my TV, laptop or monitor, I spray the towel FIRST, not the screen, then wipe the screen with the wet towel.

Then turn it over to the dry side and soak up the wetness and remove the dirt.

NEVER spray your monitor or laptop with any cleaning agent, including water. ALWAYS simply wet the rag or towel first, _then_ apply it to the monitor for best results.
