### Post by KelinciHutan on 2012-01-11
I really hope this is the right forum for this question.  If not, my apologies to the mods.

Anyway, I switched from Windows last November to Lucid.  So I've been using MS Office for ages.  And I use my office suite primarily for creative writing.

All of which to say, I am really disappointed in OOo.  I was hoping it was just that it was new and I wasn't used to it, but after two months it's still awful.  The system for creating keyboard shortcuts is clunky, and doesn't allow you to actually make shortcuts for certain functions (like small caps text).  Navigational keyboard commands are not always used very well (for example, if I'm trying to highlight large sections of text, using keyboard navigation commands along with shift to highlight things sometimes drops the highlighting entirely, which sort of undermines the point).  Some text effects, like drop caps, either aren't always displayed or don't work in the first place.  Auto-correct works whenever it feels like working, and does not respond well to customization.  And do not get me started on my irritation with creating tab stops and things using the rulers.

Basically, I'm kind of hating it.

My question is, is LibO better at doing any of this stuff, or about the same?  Or, if it is about the same, is it more likely to improve on any of these things sooner?  I certainly like the politics of LibO better than OOo.  On the other hand, maybe it's me.  Have I just not found the right tutorials yet, and I'm doing all these things the hard way?

### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-01-11
If you want to moan about OpenOffice then go here:

[http://www.openoffice.org/]("http://www.openoffice.org/")

If you want help with OpenOffice or LibreOffice then you can ask and some of us who are using these products may be able to help you.

Another thing, OpenOffice must surely have improved since the almost two years since Lucid was released. Ubuntu now comes with LibreOffice as standard and it is being developed all the time.

I do not have the problems that you relate. I think the issue here is that you are expecting OpenOffice to be a kind of Microsoft Office without the price tag. OpenOffice and LibreOffice are their own programs. The developers have the right to do things their way. It has never been their intention to produce a cheap clone of a Microsoft product but to bring forward open document standards so users are not repeatedly paying for products from a company that has a monopoly.
