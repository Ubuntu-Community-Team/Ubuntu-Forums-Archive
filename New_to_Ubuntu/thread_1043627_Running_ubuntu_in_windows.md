### Post by Bölvaður on 2009-01-18
The boot menu you have is not GRUB (it is not installed from wubi the ubuntu installer for windows).

Because you are new on the forums I would like to take the opportunity to say welcome and here are some guidelines to think about before posting questions for support.

1. List your hardware if the problem may be related to that (like if I'd have black screen I would say I had xxx card and the xxx driver for it)

2. State what you have changed and when it began.

3. You can post as stupid and as many questions as you want (1 per thread) with descriptive name (no "I am a noob" type of thread name as people may be looking for particular type of support questions to answer)

Do not be scared of the terminal. It is the easiest way to answer people when they need support.

### Post by Bölvaður on 2009-01-19
Nah, it's not going to be very steep learning curve unless if you insist in doing things the Windy (yes it is my pet name for Windows) way. I think the best advice to get you away from Windy thoughts is to tell you to avoid installing programs from websites. It is ok to look for good applications on the internet, but it is best if you get and install them with system &#8594; administrator &#8594; synaptic package manager, or to lesser extend, applications &#8594; add/remove

.deb packages are files for debian like distros for installing files. They automatically prompt you for dependencies and may download them automatically.
