### Post by lkraemer on 2009-08-28
Yes, by all means boot from a LiveCD of the Distro you choose and
give it a test drive.  [http://distrowatch](http://distrowatch) has a listing of all the
distros.  You might want to select one the fits your needs over
the one you currently have selected.

My choices (in order) are Ubuntu, PCLinuxOS, CrunchBang, sidux, and
Tinycore.

And for your Gateway why not download the latest Gparted LiveCD and
use it to shrink your Windows partition (after defraggin it several times),
then move the recovery Partition immediately after the Windows Partition.
Then install your distro and make a linux-swap partition that is 2X 
your RAM.  (Max of 4 Primary Partitions....and you used them all).

Then use the Dual boot machine for a year or two while you hone
your Linux skills.  In a year to two, you won't be booting Windows.

Or, purchase a new Drive and install it, keeping your Windows as
a HOT Backup, and then install your distro.

Dual Boot Site:
[http://members.iinet.net/~herman546/index.html](http://members.iinet.net/~herman546/index.html)

Good Luck.

lkraemer
