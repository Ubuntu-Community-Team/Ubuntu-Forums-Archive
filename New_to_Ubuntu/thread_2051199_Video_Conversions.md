### Post by TheFu on 2012-09-01
You need to know exactly what format those files are in.  Some DVRs encrypt the data and there isn't any known way to break that encryption.  Other DVRs encrypt, but there are ways to gain access to the underlying media.

All of this is extremely DVR specific.

So, let's start with these questions:
* What make, model, and device version of DVR are you using?
* What TV content are you recording?  Satellite? Cable? Premium Cable (i,e. cableCARD?)
* How is the DVR connected to your PC?  Purely video/audio or via firewire connection or just over the IP network?
* Do you know the codecs used by the files?  mpeg2, mpeg4, AVC-1 ... there are many others.....
* What is the file extension?

Until you know this information **and tell us**, we can't help.
