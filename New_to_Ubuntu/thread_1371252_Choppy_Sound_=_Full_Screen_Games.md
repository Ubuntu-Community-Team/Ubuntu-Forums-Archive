### Post by warfacegod on 2010-01-03
If you are using nintendo emulators, try changing the frame skip rate and getting openGL support if possible. Because I have a weak video card (64MB), mine gets so bad I just turn the sound off and play music.

If your playing regular PC games the only thing I can say is check your video and audio drivers, make sure you have the right ones.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-01-03
Some games have the option to use "openAL" for sound (it gives you directional sound if you have 5.1 surround sound speakers). OpenAL seems to cause this problem for me. If you can turn it off in your game, then do.
