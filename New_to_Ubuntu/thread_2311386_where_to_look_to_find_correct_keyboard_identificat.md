I want to install Lubuntu on a laptop, but I don't know its keyboard.  

It was made in Japan, and it has a funky Japanese keyboard.  (For example, the "@" symbol is not above the "2", and there are some extra keys.)

When I do installation, Lubuntu asks me to choose a keyboard.  How do I know which one?  Is there a way to identify keyboards either by (1) looking at the hardware, or (2) looking at something in the computer (it is currently running Windows Vista, so somewhere in Windows...)?

Thank you very much!

### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2016-01-27
More than one possibility:

The easiest, surest thing is trial and error. Look at the available choices and pick the most likely. If that doesn't turn out well, reinstall. It doesn't take that long and you aren't going to affect the actual keyboard.

Alternately, there used to be some sort of "pick the most likely for me" option. You could try that. I doubt it will work, but it won't take terribly long to find out.

Thirdly, you could search for images of Nipponese keyboards, go to the webpages with the images, and maybe you'll find useful info on one of them.

Fourthly, you could use online translation and find and post the question with a picture of the keyboard at some online NIpponese computer forum similar to this one.

Good luck.
