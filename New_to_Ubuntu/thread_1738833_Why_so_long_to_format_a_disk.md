### Post by zipperback on 2011-04-25
> **Marc Blum said:**
> Thanks for your answer. Next question: How and where do I adjust the speed of of burning CD's?


Launch Brasero.

"Applications -> Sound & Video -> Brasero Disc Burner"


Now select "Project -> New Project -> Burn Image".

You will be presented with a small application window that says "Image Burning Setup" in the title bar.

Left mouse click where it says "Click here to select a disc image".

Select the .iso disc image you want to burn.

Now if you haven't done it already, insert a blank cd into your burner.

If you get a new notification window from Ubuntu telling you that it detected a blank disc when you inserted it, just close the window, because you already have Brasero running.

Now from the Brasero window, click the "Properties" button, now select "Max Speed" from the Burning speed selector.

Click  close, and now click Burn.

Brasero will now burn at the maximum speed available for your system.

I know this might seem a little confusing when you first read this message, but once you do it, you'll see it's really simple.
