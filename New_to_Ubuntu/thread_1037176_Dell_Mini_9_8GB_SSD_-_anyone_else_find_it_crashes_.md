### Post by sandyd on 2009-01-11
i guess by "crash" you mean that ubuntu crashes, not the hard disk. (because a SSD is physically un-crashable).

It depends on the SSD your using.

Lower cost SSD s are sometimes slower (even slower than your nice hard disk platters) so ubuntu may be waiting for an output from the drive, but the drive is terribly lagging from the instructions ubuntu gave it. 

Although its hard, and rare, SSD s may have hardware damage or manufacturing defects for example (cracked memory module, overheating).

Ill guess at the first one though because most SSD s that come with netbooks are the cheapest kind ( to keep costs down).
