### Post by ibuclaw on 2009-02-24
> **iamkain said:**
> what version of ubuntu...

That would mostly depend on what suits you the most, really.

If you would like to try something new, I'd say tryout the mainline Ubuntu distribution.
If you are accustomed to the Windows-style look, maybe Kubuntu.
If you are using an old Laptop that doesn't meet the recommended system requirements, then you could give Xubuntu a try.

As stated in the Ubuntu System Requirements page:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements)

The recommended minimum requirements for Ubuntu are:
[LIST]
[*]700 MHz x86 processor
[*]384 MB of system memory (RAM)
[*]8 GB of disk space
[*]Graphics card capable of 1024x768 resolution
[*]Sound card
[*]A network or Internet connection
[/LIST]
If your laptop falls under those specifications, you could give the vanilla version of ubuntu a try.

There will also be a marginal learning curve for you to learn how to use and administer your new operating system.
