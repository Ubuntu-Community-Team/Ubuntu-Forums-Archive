### Post by bumanie on 2008-12-18
You would probably be better off making an image of your drive onto an external hard drive and zipping it with gzip. A straight .iso of the whole drive will (probably) be too large unless you have blu-ray burning capability. If you have a external drive of greater or equal capacity to the present internal hard drive, you could clone your hard drive.
