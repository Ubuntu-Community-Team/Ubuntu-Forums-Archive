### Post by aa4bb on 2009-10-24
Ubuntu is seductive. 

It has everything to meet most people's computing needs, it's fast, and in my opinion is intuitive and easy to use. If 90% of users have Ubuntu and/or other Linux, and 10% had windows, I wonder how long windows would last. Other than the fact that there are programs written specifically for windows, it's hard for me to imagine that anyone would prefer it.

So, when I started with Ubuntu about a month ago, I was head over heels in love with it. I still am, but have some bruises to show for it. That's not unlike my experience with computers in general over a 40 year love/hate relationship. It's been worth the pain, but that's coming from a techie nerd. Most people don't have the skills and determination to work through problems. They want the computer to work without much effort on their part. So, take my love story for what it is - that of a commited geek.

So, the big problem that I've had - and mostly what I see in the forums - is hardware compability. There is such a vast array of video chips, sound chips, network chips, PCI, USB, WiFi etc etc, that it is a daunting task to have them automagically recognized and proper drivers installed. 

I'm amazed at how well Ubuntu does it out of the box. However, each of the four computers I've tried Ubuntu with - three notebooks and one ancient desktop - has had some issue with recognizing and installing devices. Most of those had to do with networking. Looking back, if I had made a wired connection to the Internet first (that is, direct Ethernet from my computer to my router rather than trying to establish WiFi in the initial installation), much of that grief would have been avoided. Ubuntu seems very good at going to get the packages that it needs to install wireless, etc, as long as it is connected to the Internet.

The other devilish issue is installing to dual boot Vista and Ubuntu. Vista is a jealous mistress with regard to sharing hard drive space by reorganizing partitions. If you have enough HD space and partitions, it can clearly work, but be prepared for the possibility of many hours of research and trying different tactics.
