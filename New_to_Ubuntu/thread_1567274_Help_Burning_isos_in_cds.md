### Post by LowSky on 2010-09-03
You will need a dual layer DVD... they can hold up 8.5 GB of Data

to get an idea of what is availible
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=100007591+600010559&QksAutoSuggestion=&ShowDeactivatedMark=False&Configurator=&IsNodeId=1&Subcategory=71&description=&Ntk=&CFG=&SpeTabStoreType=&srchInDesc=](http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=100007591+600010559&QksAutoSuggestion=&ShowDeactivatedMark=False&Configurator=&IsNodeId=1&Subcategory=71&description=&Ntk=&CFG=&SpeTabStoreType=&srchInDesc=)


make sure your optical drive can burn Dual layer disks. It may have a label on it that will say :**DVD-R DL** or **DVD+R DL**, DVD+R DL is more popular. They do make rewritable CD (CD-RW) and DVD's (DVD-RW) but not in the Dual layer format, they only hold 700 and 4.7GB repectfully.

If you are making an ISO, I believe you cannot burn other files onto the disk, an iso is basically an image file that will take over an entire disk. If you are just creating a plain old data CD, you can burn multiple times just so long as you do not finalize the CD.
