### Post by rg_stephens on 2010-05-27
My experience has been that for most common tasks, Ubuntu works much better than Windows, and it's compatible with *most* hardware. I keep a Windows computer around for a few specialized programs.

There are some good new-user guides out there, but the basic idea is pretty simple: Download Ubuntu, burn a CD (or DVD), boot your computer from the CD, (at this point you can check the disk for errors, test your RAM, etc) When you're running Ubuntu from the liveCD, you can to connect to the internet, and just make sure things are working ok, at least at a basic level. There will be an "install" button on the desktop. You can choose to erase your hard drive and install Ubuntu, or install "Windows and Ubuntu side-by-side", depending on your situation. If you have any problems or questions, come back to the forums.

It's always a good idea to back up your data before you do anything.
