### Post by thomas144 on 2010-04-29
If you don't have a Bittorrent client (like me), and you're doing the direct download from ubuntu.com, here's a tip:
 
If you start the Ubuntu download, and it is very slow compared to your Internet connection, try again. Cancel the download, refresh the page, and choose your options again. At least for the US, there are several mirrors. The maximum speed of my Internet connection is 728 kilobits/second, which is about 90 kilobyes/second. The first time I tried to download, it never got above about 13 kilobytes per second. I retried a few times, and I got a different mirror that ran at a little above 85 kilobytes/second. It's kind of like calling a busy phone number:  If you can't get through, try again!
 
Please, if you have a bittorrent client, use it.
