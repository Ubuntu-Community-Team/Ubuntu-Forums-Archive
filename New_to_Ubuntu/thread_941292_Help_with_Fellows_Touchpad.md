### Post by jivabill on 2008-10-11
OK< now I have solved the problem.  I did a bunch of research and found that IF you put two mice on a windows 98se or XP machine the OS thinks both the mice are the same mouse.  So you can have them both plugged in at once.

So I left my Fellowes Touchpad on COM2 where it is and added a M$ standard two button mouse PS/2 plugged into the mouse port.  I found that both of the mice work perfectly, that is the touchpad and the mouse.

Now I can run the touchpad under win98se and all its fancy scrolling and keyboard works fine.  When I boot into Xubuntu 8.04 I use the M$ mouse so everything works there on Linux.

And Xubuntu works very well on my older win98 machine.  It has an AMD 450 cpu, 384mb of RAM and two HDs each about 18gb.  Xubuntu is on the 2nd HD. The network printer Canon IP3000 is on one of the USB ports. (I have two other computers in my network).
