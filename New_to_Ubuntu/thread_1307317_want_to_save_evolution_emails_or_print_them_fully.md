### Post by callan79 on 2009-11-05
> **stinger30au said:**
> so far the closest i have got to achieving what i want is with thunder bird but the photos have been taken at such a high resolution they are not getting fully shown on the pages they are getting cut up and spread over the pages.


I think what we you need to do is look at the format of the email.

In the original email, are the photos INLINE (i.e. a photo inserted into an HTML email), or are they ATTACHED ?

If they are attached, you can't reasonably expect them to print, as they aren't part of the message - they're attachments.

But if you have an HTML email, with images inline, then you WOULD expect these to be printed.

Perhaps try emailing yourself and see what happens ... compose a new message to your customer, make sure this email is HTML. Then in your mail software click INSERT >> IMAGE. Then put the image, and some text, and another image.

Now do the same but this time ATTACH the image.

See if there's any difference.
