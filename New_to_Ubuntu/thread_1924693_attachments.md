### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-02-13
You can't effectively use email as a file storing service.  Email servers store emails, not files, and the best you can do is attach each photo or scan to an email.  But, some email servers have both email size limits and total space limits, meaning, some won't permit attachments larger than a certain size, and ALL (as far as I know) place a limit on the total size of your "mailbox".  It depends on what email provider you are using.

### Post by winh8r on 2012-02-13
The simplest way is to look for the "paperclip" icon in your email window it will allow  you to "include an attachment".

When you click on it you should see a box with a "browse" button on it.

Click on this and navigate to wherever the image or scan is located on your sysytem and click "open" at the bottom of the selection box window.

The attachment should then show up in the email window as a file name.

Complete your email and then click send.

Most email programs have a limit on the size of attachment you can send via email. If you need to send a lot of scans or photographs then you should consider using an alternative like a free file sharing site where you can upload a zip file with the data in it and send a link to it to the recipient in an email.
