### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-10-27
Ubuntu comes with Brasero by default (other flavors may come with another similar app) and you can also install the excellent K3B from the software center (default in Kubuntu). AFAIK Ubuntu MATE comes with Brasero.

That said, you need to decide what to do: 
1. Data DVD (with video files)
2. DVD-VIDEO

Option 1 is easier and all you need is one of the aforementioned tools, select the files and burn. However, a Data DVD is often not compatible with the old home DVD players. It all depends on the player itself supporting or not a) data DVD and b) the file formats and video codecs of the video files inside. 

Option 2 is compatible with PCs and DVD players but require different tools: DeVeDe, Bombono DVD, etc.. Also depending on the conversion needed and on how powerful your computer is, it can take several hours of processing for each DVD.
