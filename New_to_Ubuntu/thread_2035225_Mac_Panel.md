### Post by Lars Noodén on 2012-07-30
The picture you posted seems to show the dock at the bottom of the screen.  As far as I know there is no way to get Unity to move the panel from the left hand side of the screen.  To get that level of customization, you'll have to upgrade to KDE, XFCE or LXDE.  These are easily installed and can fit on your machine along side of each other if need be.

There is also Macbuntu, which claims to be able to set all appearances to be like OS X.  However, I have not tried it.
[http://sourceforge.net/projects/macbuntu/](http://sourceforge.net/projects/macbuntu/)
It's not in the repository, either.

### Post by Chdslv on 2012-07-30
Install Ubuntu 12.04 and then install Cairo Dock using the Software Centre and then log out and choose Cairo with Gnome+effects and you are without *any* other panels. :D

It works with the netbooks quite well, at least mine does, which is two years old..
