### Post by Inxsible on 2008-05-13
GTalk and Yahoo are not made for Linux...as you well know. So you can't use those apps per se. Also the protocols that they use for voice chat are not public knowledge since they are a closed source company.

So as for now, Skype is the only one which allows voice and video conversation in Linux. Kopete, Pidgin, Gyachi, Tapioca and others do allow voice conversations...but in a round about way and its never a guarantee that it will work on your machine.

Ask all your friends to get a Skype account as well...its free :) Thats what I have done.
