### Post by littletinman on 2008-09-30
**Synaptic** is like the unabridged text book. It will tell you all the different packages and dependencies that are available or installed. Very detailed, and specific.

**Add/Remove** is the like abridged strait to the point catalog. It shows you the main applications, without telling you all about the dependancies and what not. It's more user friendly.

### Post by matdombrock on 2008-09-30
I use add/remove for most installs. I use synaptic only when I have to because I believe that it puts you at a higher risk of installing (or uninstalling) something that could mess up your system. Add/remove tries to keep you from doing that. 

For instance:
Add/remove will not let you install something that will remove another package, it will tell you to use synaptic.

Also, you cannot edit software sources with add/remove, you can however with synaptic.

My girlfriend uses xubuntu on her box. I have told her not to use synaptic and just to use add/remove. if she wants to do something with synaptic she should let me do it (until she gets better at using Gnu/Linux.)
