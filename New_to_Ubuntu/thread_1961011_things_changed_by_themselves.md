### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-04-18
The "Windows List" applet shows in the bottom panel (by installation  default) or is available by right clicking on a blank area in the  intended panel and selecting "ADD TO PANEL". What apps you have open,  show to the right of the vertical perforated lines (almost invisible on  some displays). If you open an app it should appear in your panel as a  box with the name of the running app inside.

The only other  option that I know of for showing open apps is the "Window Selector"  (also available in the ADD TO PANEL) which is a menu list of what apps  are open.

When you open an application (VLC, Firefox, Rhythumbox,  etc.) they should appear in your panel. The more apps you open the more  running app boxes appear in the panel, with the app name or with what's  running in the app. If you open just one app, look at the panels and  see where the app shows up and just to the left of that box (if your  using the Windows list applet) should be two vertical perforated lines.  Should you right click on the lines and select about, it should say  "Window List".

I'm wondering if you got an update that moved the  Window List applet from the top to the bottom panel? All open  apps should show to the right of the perforated lines. If it's on the  bottom, all you'd have to do is delete it (right click on the lines and  select "remove from panel") from the bottom panel and add it (right  click on the top panel and select "add to panel" then select the "Window  List" applet from the listing of available applets) to the top panel.  Then move the lines (right click and select move) to the left leaving as  much blank panel space open to the right as possible.

I don't  know if there's a panel option in VLC's configuration, but think that  would only apply to a launcher not the panel running box. Unless the  launcher icon drops down with running info.

Good Luck
