### Post by XCan on 2009-08-15
Indeed most mud's websites provide a mean to connect through a java client, you could even connect to them using telnet. But the point is that mud clients are so much powerful, with macros and scripting possibilities. Anyway, I found a client called Mudlet [http://www.mudlet.org/](http://www.mudlet.org/) , and one of the devs is lurking around the forum. It's still in the early stage and I just gave it a short try, but I think it's safe to say that so far this client is the one that appeals me the most. It actually puts some emphasis on user frienliness, it's like zmud, only a lot faster.

### Post by bytesmythe on 2009-10-31
There is a really nice mud called Tera (teramud.com:4000) that has been around for years. It could definitely use some new blood if anyone is looking for a place to while away their idle time!

Lots of specialized races, classes, and a rune based magic system. For mud builders, there is a built-in javascript interpreter for giving real complexity to your areas. Give it a try!!

As far as clients go, I'm trying out one called Mudlet ([http://www.mudlet.org/](http://www.mudlet.org/)) that seems pretty nice so far. I've only been playing with it a short while, though.
