### Post by DuckHook on 2013-12-13
Hello niall.stronge and welcome to Ubuntu and the forums.

It is possible to chase down the possible issues but I am trying to think in terms of the *easiest* strategy for a one-day user with a completely brand new system.

Given the above, I would actually recommend that you just try installing it from scratch again. Make sure you check the ISO image (the DVD) that you are using to install with its md5 hash first. Instructions [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM"). We could try monkeying around with a Unity reset or adjusting Compiz settings, but by the time you figure those things out, not to mention the possibility of further mangling the system, you would have already completed a pristine install (takes 20 minutes on my system). Seems to be the best use of your time and resources. If you continue having the same issue, then there's more than enough time to try the arcane stuff.
