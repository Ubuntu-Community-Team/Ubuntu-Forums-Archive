### Post by newb85 on 2012-09-02
My experience is that this works automatically, but I know that a few years ago when I was using an Nvidia card, it didn't work automatically.  I guess I assumed the change was an improvement in Ubuntu, but maybe it's a difference of drivers.

The default way to check your displays through the GUI is to go to Displays under System Settings.  You can do this through the menu in the top-right corner of your screen or by typing "displays" into the dash.  Try hitting "Detect Displays" and see if your LCD comes up.

If this route is disabled (as it may well be with Nvidia), I would look for a similar option within nvidia-settings.  (If you're running Nvidia drivers, I would expect it to already be installed.)
