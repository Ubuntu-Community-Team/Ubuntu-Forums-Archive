### Post by bumanie on 2010-04-29
Occasionally a kernel will come out that has an idiosyncratic issue with particular hardware. Two choices - boot with the kernel that works or try to reinstall the newer kernel and see if that fixes the problem (probably use synaptic for this - type linux-image in 'search' and then right click the kernel to be reinstalled and click reinstall). If that doesn't work, just use the older kernel.
This is why most experienced users keep a known booting kernel on their system, just in case there is an incompatibility with a newer kernel.
