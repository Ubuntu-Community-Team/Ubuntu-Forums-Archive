### Post by xwizzard on 2009-05-04
Hello Ubuntu Forum community! 
Being an "initiate" of Ubuntu, I have begun my search for applications I use on my MS Windows desktop.
Open Office > MS Office; Brasero > Roxio Media Creator 9; GNOME Do > Rocket Dock; KTorrent > uTorrent; and direct drop-ins like Firefox, Pidgin, Thunderbird, and VLC Media Player.
I am still in search of a Daemon Tools (Lite) replacement. I know of the command-line "mount - loop" command, but like having a GUI preferably with support for bin, mdf, img, nrg, gi, etc. Of the near-replacements I've seen, AcetoneISO, CDemu, and kiso seem to have the most supporters. CDemu and AcetoneISO do not appear in the Ubuntu repositories, which deterred me from using them for the sake of apt updates and worrying about incompatibilities. I found kiso in the repositories, and found it a decent substitute, yet a bit laking in user-friendliness compared to AcetoneISO. I added CDemu by adding the repo to my apt-get sources (a puzzling project for any new linux/debian user), and found AcetoneISO only on the project's website (strangely in .deb format, yet no repository...?)
Of the three, I found AcetoneISO was the closest replacement. There are some features of kiso i'd like to see in Acetone, (and vice-versa). After adding CDemu repos and installing through the "synaptic" apt-get manager I've yet to see any signs of life from the thing. Installing daemon, client, and gnome applet has yeilded no menu icons or panel objects. I assume further steps are necessary, but frankly I'm content with AcetoneISO+kiso at the moment.
If anyone else has a suggestion for a Daemon Tools replacement for Linux please keep me posted. At the moment I'm undecided between AcetoneISO and kiso, but I'm easily swayed by shiny GUIs with extensive features.
