### Post by Blue Snapper on 2008-06-02
Hello Acadia,

I've had the same problem with Ubuntu 7.10 and 8.04 on my Inspiron 8000 with an ATI video card and am using the "Fn+F7" key combination as a temporary solution.

Give it a try: start your Inspiron 8000 with Ubuntu and when you see the double or triple screen problem hold down the blue "Fn" key while pressing F7 a couple of times.

Found it on the Ubuntu Forums site somewhere and it works for me for the moment (a 6 month moment).  It seems the easy way to go for the moment.
