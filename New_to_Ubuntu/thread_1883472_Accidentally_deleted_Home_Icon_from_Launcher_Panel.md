### Post by An Sanct on 2011-11-19
The *home folder* launcher/shortcut is just a normal shortcut to a folder, so if you press special key between the left Ctrl and Alt (normally the "Windows" key), and enter "home", the folder will be displayed. Run it and from the dash, right click on it and select "Keep in launcher".

If you are talking about the Dash Home (the gray one with the Ubuntu logo) then I cannot help here :) as I couldn't delete that icon/launcher and I tried really hard, it wouldn't go away.

### Post by koolkatkiwi on 2011-11-21
Just a further update to this thread. I did get the orange Home folder back by accident. I wanted to change the size of the icons in the Launcher panel, so I found this page:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/11.10/ubuntu-help/unity-launcher-change-size.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/11.10/ubuntu-help/unity-launcher-change-size.html)

I found I had to search for the CompizConfig Settings Manager by spelling out the whole name, not just using ccsm (as they say to do in this tutorial), which didn't work. 

After installing that, the Home icon changed to its rightful orange, and I was able to reduce the ridiculously large size of the icons, make the background transparent, etc. The icons in their standard size look like they are made for the visually impaired. Ah, it just occurred to me that in a larger screen with a high resolution, they may look just the right size.
