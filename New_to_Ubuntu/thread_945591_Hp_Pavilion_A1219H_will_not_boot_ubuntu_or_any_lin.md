### Post by handydan918 on 2008-10-12
I have a couple of suggestions. 
1) Try the alternative installer cd for Ubuntu. It is an installer only, not a live cd. If you are not familiar with Debian-style installers and manual partitioning, read up first.

2)Try a few other distros. There are a few that come to mind which are noted for very good hardware recognition. 

  1) Mepis 7. Also a Debian based distro (like Ubu) it is a KDE distro, rather than gnome. 
 
  2)Knoppix, the grandaddy of live cd's, always well known for amzing hardware compatibility and detection. It is not intended for installation to the hard drive, but may be useful for determining (via lspci, lshw, etc.) what your hardware really is.
