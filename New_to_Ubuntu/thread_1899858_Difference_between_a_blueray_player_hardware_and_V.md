The DRM  used on BluRay discs requires you to connect it through a HDMI cable (and both the player and display must support HDCP). Otherwise the image is scaled down to standard resolution. VGA is an analog connection and doesn't support HDCP, so you won't get HD image from BluRay discs with it.

(on the other hand the only way I know for playing BluRay movies on Linux requires ripping them to hard drive, in which case the DRM is meaningless and you can play the movie at HD resolution even with the VGA cable. I've nver tried this, though, way too much work just to watch a movie... )
