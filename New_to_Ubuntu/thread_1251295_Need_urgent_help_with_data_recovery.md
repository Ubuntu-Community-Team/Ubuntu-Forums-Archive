### Post by jrothwell97 on 2009-08-27
Welcome to the Ubuntu forums.

The first rule of disaster recovery is ***stop using the affected machine immediately***, lest the data be overwritten. (The computer will assume it's free space, and will therefore overwrite it whenever it pleases.)

Find another machine to use, *and turn off the other computer.* Then we can try and recover what's left of the data.

Good luck, and sorry about you losing your data :(

### Post by scratman on 2009-08-27
I would strongly advise creating a bootable version of Ubuntu on a memory stick (preferably on another machine) and then using that to scan the disk for any recoverable data.  Either that, or put your hard drive into another machine asa slave drive and do a full disk scan for deleted files.  To be honest though, if you are not sure what you are doing, I'd buy a friend who does know what they're doing a couple of pints, or visit your local friendly computer shop.
