### Post by squakie on 2012-11-03
I have a different approach to unity that some:  I right-click on items in that menu and unlock them - this removes them from the unity bar.  Basically I just have the Unity home and workspace icon on the left and that's it.  When you start an app it shows there while it's running, then removes itself when you close it.  in this way it is much like the left-hand side of the task bar in Windows.  Similarly, think right hand side of the top bar as the system tray on the right side of the Windows task bar.  I know - it's not Windows, it just works for me.  That leaves just the top left of the top bar - it's where I know the "menu" line for apps is going to show (file, edit, etc.).

I then just go into Unity via the Unity home button, click to tell it I want to see all installed apps, then go through them one at a time and drag the ones I want (Firefox, files, games, terminal, etc.) to the desktop.  That way the icons are on the desktop for all the things you normally use, and you won't have to use the Unity bar on a regular basis.

I thought I'd mention that since you are having the dislike for Unity that most of us have had at one point or another because it's such a change.  Just remember Unity is running on Gnome - things are just presented differently.

As far as your question about the software, take a look at MythTV - it might be what you are looking for.
