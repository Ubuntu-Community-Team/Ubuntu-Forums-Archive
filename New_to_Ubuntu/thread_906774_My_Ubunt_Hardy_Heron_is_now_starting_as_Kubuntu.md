### Post by cwsnyder on 2008-08-31
Are you saying your menu is still at the bottom left of the screen or that the screen after the sign-on says you are in Kubuntu.

Gnome is just a Desktop Environment, as KDE is a Desktop Environment.  You can use Xfce, for example, in either Ubuntu, or Kubuntu, as well as in the Xubuntu package.  You evidently did more than load just the Desktop Environment, you loaded all of the KDE packages to have your Ubuntu change to Kubuntu.

If your Applications Menu is at the upper left corner of the screen, Clock at upper right, Desktop revealer at lower left, and desktop switcher on lower right, you are in the Gnome DE.  If you are missing the top menu bar and the Application menu is on the lower left, you are probably in KDE.  If you are missing both menu bars, but an Application menu pops up as a context menu when you right click on the desktop, you are probably in Xfce.

cwsnyder
