### Post by blueridgedog on 2010-10-17
As another add to this, after using Linux since 94, I have found that apparent needs for office and photoshop may be over perceived by the end user.  I use open office and gimp and can generally get what I need done with them.  I use open office and gimp on my Mac as I have simply replaced them.  Think of your needs in terms of function and not software titles and things may work better than expected.  

As far as iTunes, the new libmobiledevices library allows Linux to interact with mac mobile devices completely, so I am also free to manage ipods and iphones from multiple Linux systems and have no desire to go back to itunes (don't even use it on the mac!).  From a functional standpoint, I have found Linux to be fantastic.  I keep the mac os on my laptop as a secondary boot option and I enjoy it, but I am much more productive in Linux on the mac hardware.  I bought a mac as I like the hardware and will probably go with a new mac desktop as well when my current box gets a bit long in the tooth.   

The future is more about software function than specific named packages...my daughter is more in to what she can do than the title of the software...that is interesting to me as it shows a way to the future.
