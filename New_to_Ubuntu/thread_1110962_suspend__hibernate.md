### Post by HavocXphere on 2009-03-30
It used to work as expected. But since an update ~ 1 month ago the option is gone...for me at least.

If you launch Kpowersave, then both are still available from the sys tray.

...But if kpowersave is running on my PC, then the PC doesn't shutdown anymore when I press the physical power button on the PC.

Basically you get suspend to Ram and suspend to disk.

Suspend to RAM --> PC still needs a trickle of power.  Fans etc power down. If you cut the mains then the session is lost.

Suspend to disk --> Similar to windows hibernate. Writes everything to disk. No power needed to maintain.

I wouldn't compare it to the windows "equivalent"...new windows version use Hybrid hibernate, which is a mix of Ram+Disk. And imo its superior.:-|

Whether it works on your PC will be hit & miss...it depends on the hardware. Just try it. On most hardware setups it does work.
