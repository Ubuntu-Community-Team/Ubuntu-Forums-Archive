### Post by ||0BL1v10N|| on 2013-10-24
The technical term for this is tethering and I advise users only to do this where there is a data plan with the cellular provider. Assuming this is the case, tethering is quick and simple. First step depends on the version of iOS on your iPhone. Once again, assuming the handset is iOS7 below is a step-by-step on getting the phone set up:

On the iPhone:

Go to **Settings** > **Cellular** **>** [B]Set up Personal Hotspot 
[/B]
You will then be prompted to set up a **wireless SSID/name** and  **password** (important for later). Once this is done we can turn our attention to your laptop.

At the top right of the screen will be a status bar, where you find time, battery power etc. Click on the wireless icon which should be grayed out if you are not connected to anything and select the wireless SSID/name you chose. Then type in the password. And your done.


Remember, a hotspot acts as a modem so will show up when in range of a device. Try chosing fun/interesting hotspot names. Mine is: it hurst when IP,

Hope this answers your question.  If there are any variations regarding software give me a shout.
