### Post by Darkness Des on 2010-06-14
Well, first. mount your /home directory on a separate partition. You can reinstall all day long and keep all your documents. For programs, you'd want to mount /usr and /etc on separate partitions. If you just want an honest fix that isn't a reinstall, I STRONGLY recommend... these forums. They work quite well. Google does too, I'd say about half an hour of failed help searches on there is good before you come here. There's also very good community documentation. Anything can be fixed if you're willing to take a few moments and copy and paste a few things into the command line. Oh, and I recommend Gnome (Ubuntu) instead of KDE (Kubuntu), as KDE can be very complex. My two cents on the subject.

### Post by TeoBigusGeekus on 2010-06-14
Welcome to the world of ubuntu - I truly hope you'll stay with us.
Bear in mind that your hesitations are totally justified: thousands of us have undergone these same thoughts.
My advice would be to create a partition from within windows and install ubuntu there (i.e. not wubi, as you've said you've already tried it, but dual boot).
Then, try to use your windows system as little as possible. What I mean, is try to only use ubuntu for everything that you used to do in windows. A time will come when the format of your windows partition will seem like the natural thing to do!
As for ubuntu breaking up, what do you think these forums are for?
Good luck!
