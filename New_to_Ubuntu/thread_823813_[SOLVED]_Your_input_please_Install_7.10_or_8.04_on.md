### Post by archer6 on 2008-06-10
It's done....Yay!

Here is the final configuration and outcome of my (dual boot) XP Pro / Ubuntu 8.04, ThinkPad T60 Project. 

1) Installed a second hard drive in the ultra bay.
2) Removed original hard drive with XP Pro from internal bay.
3) Used USB external CD drive to install Ubuntu 8.04 on ultra bay HDD.
4) Replaced original hard drive in it's internal bay.

Power up, and it automatically boots to Win XP Pro as normal.

Power up, press F12 and boot choices are displayed. Choose Ultra Bay HDD, and Ubuntu 8.04 Boots.

The Ubuntu 8.04 install went fast, smooth and everything works! 

I could not be happier, Thanks to everyone that responded to my questions and provided feedback and suggestions!

Cheers

The reason I have it setup this way, is it was a way suggested to me by an experienced Linux user. It provides a way so that I can remove the ultra bay hard disk drive if needed, without having to make any other changes, as Grub stays with Ubuntu. An additional benefit is that Win XP Pro SP3 and it's master boot record were untouched as that drive was removed during the Ubuntu install. The best of both worlds. Two completely independent OS's on their own drives, without being linked in any way. 

In the (unlikely) event I choose to sell the computer I simply remove the Ultra Bay hard drive, put the DVD burner back in the bay and it boots to Win XP just as it did when new. No further action required. Then in addition I also have a T60p. I can remove whatever peripheral I have in that Ultra Bay and Presto...Ubuntu in my T60p within moments.
