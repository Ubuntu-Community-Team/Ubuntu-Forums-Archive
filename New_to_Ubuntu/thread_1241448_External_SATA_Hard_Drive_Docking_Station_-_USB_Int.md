### Post by Bartender on 2009-08-22
I got a Vantec NextStar NST-D100 and a Seagate 1TB HDD.  The dock also supports eSATA, and includes an eSATA backplane bracket (and cables) so you could pop the bracket into a space on the back of a desktop PC if the motherboard has extra SATA ports.

The NST-D100 accepts notebook SATA drives or desktop drives.  The first one didn't work but Vantec was pretty quick about doing an RMA.

The latest Brando HDD dock looks pretty cool - it'll take 2 drives at once.

With Intrepid I had to do some screwing around to get the external to function, but I think it's pretty much plug-and-play now with Jaunty.

I want to emphasize one thing - in Windows it's important to properly turn off USB drives but you can get away with just unplugging most of the time.  In Linux, you definitely want to get in the habit of "unmounting" or "ejecting" the drive before turning it off or unplugging it.

You can experiment with different Linux distros by installing them to a loose HDD, then run it from the dock if you set up BIOS to boot from USB.

EDIT:  I was surprised to find out that I could watch video stored on the external HDD across the USB interface.  I thought I'd need eSATA for that.
