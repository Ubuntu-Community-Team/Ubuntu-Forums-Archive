### Post by chris282 on 2009-12-04
Hi Michael,

The following suggestion is provided by a first time Ubuntu user who put together his first home build about two weeks ago, still experiences a small thrill every time an application actually runs, and has very little business advising anyone anywhere on anything ever. However...

* Enter your BIOS setup when you boot up your PC.  On mine, that means pressing 'Delete' when the first boot screen comes up.

* Use the arrow keys to navigate, Enter to enter a menu, and Escape to come out of a menu.

* You're looking for something along the lines of "Power Management Setup".

* If you enter this menu there should, I hope, be an option called "Power On By Mouse", which can be disabled or enabled.  Enable it.

* Exit from BIOS using Escape or a displayed prompt, and save your settings.

* Allow your PC to boot up and see what happens.  My fingers will be crossed.

There may be an easier way to do this, but that's what I'd try if it were me.  Hope it works out, anyway.

All the best,

- Chris.
