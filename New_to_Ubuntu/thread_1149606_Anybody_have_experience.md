lmao. I also wouldn't buy a comp for two hundred dollars that won't even turn on! I'd make sure it is able to boot (to whatever OS there is if it had an HD), has passed a memtest 86, scandisked w/o errors, has a COA (important for dual booters; that WGA is annoying), and has a return policy (if it turned on).

### Post by Niniel on 2009-05-06
Power-related repairs can be costly.
I have an elderly HP Pavilion laptop (5 years or so old) where the power connector on the motherboard failed a while ago. Otherwise the box was, and still is, in good condition, apart from the USB ports wearing out.
Anyway, initially I sent it to a repair shop which attempted to fix the power connector on the motherboard for about 100 USD. Worked for a few weeks, but then it failed again. 
A new motherboard from HP would have cost me 400 USD.
I ended up buying a docking station on eBay that now provides power to the computer. That was about 120 USD (thanks to laptops being such lovely proprietary machines). And the machine isn't really portable anymore. :) But that's ok, it wasn't moved around much anyway.
