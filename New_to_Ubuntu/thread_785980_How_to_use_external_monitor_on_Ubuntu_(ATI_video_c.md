### Post by asrai on 2008-05-07
The screens and graphics menu doesn't seem to show in the applications menu by default now.  If you go to system>preferences>main menu you should be able to add it to your menus. It is under "other".  

I get best results by plugging the tv in to the laptop while the laptop is off. Then boot up the laptop, and you should be able to toggle between the screens.  Most laptops have buttons on the keyboard for this, on mine they are above F7 and F8.  If need be you can jump into the screens and graphics menu that you've now enabled and adjust the resolution.

Oops - forgot to mention that you can also adjust screens etc from the ATI catalyst control centre.  You may need to enable this in the apps menu the same way as for screens and graphics. It is also listed under "other"

I hope that helps, if you need any clarification let me know, I'm still just waking up!
