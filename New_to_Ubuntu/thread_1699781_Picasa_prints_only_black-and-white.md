### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-03-04
When I print from, say, Open Office, I get the option to change the printer properties. For example, I can choose the printed quality and whether to print in colour or black-and-white.

However, from Picasa, I don't get that option. It only prints black-and-white.

I have gone to my default printer settings (System > Administration > Printing) and ensured that the default settings are colour. But, still, Picasa prints draft-quality black-and-white.

I don't know whether this has any relevance: I notice that the default setting in Open Office is also draft-quality black-and-white. I wonder if these are the same default settings?

So, my question is...

How do I change the default printer settings so that the applications (especially Picasa) pick this up and let me print in colour?

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-03-04
I am confused. We have logged out of my account and into my daughter's account, and Picasa prints in colour from her account. But back into my account, and it still prints in black-and-white.

I've noticed that the default printer settings for OpenOffice also are black-and-white, but at least OO allows me to change it when printing.

As Picasa has no settings to choose between colour and black-and-white, this makes me think it is some account-wide setting; but where would I find such settings?

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-03-05
I found the answer.

I have an HP printer. I installed the HP GUI (hplipgui), and that allowed me to change the default settings.

It's a pity that Picasa does not use the default printer settings dialogue.
