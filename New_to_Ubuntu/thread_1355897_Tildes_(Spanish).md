### Post by arashiko28 on 2009-12-15
Go to System> Preferences> Keyboard, on choose layout and change your keyboard language to spanish -spain, tildes will be on the left of enter and next to it will be Ñ. Type the tilde first and then the vowel. 
á é í ó ú ñ @ 
your @ will be pressing right alt + 2.

Leave the keyboard model on generic 105-key (intl) PC.

### Post by squaregoldfish on 2009-12-15
The U signifies that it's a Unicode character - the international standard for assigning codes to different characters.

In most applications, you can insert the character either by copying and pasting, or by pressing SHIFT+CTRL+U followed by the code. So, to enter é, type:
```

SHIFT+CTRL+U e9 <space>
```and you'll get your character.
