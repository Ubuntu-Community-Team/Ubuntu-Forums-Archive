### Post by Hospadar on 2009-02-27
Firefox is just generally the default for non-kde distrobutions because it's arguably the most well developed browser around for linux.  Also most major web developers test their websites on IE, firefox, and safari, but not so much all the other more obscure browsers around.
That's not to say the others don't work, there's a lot of great browsers around that work really well (opera for example has bigger market share than firefox in eastern european countries), but firefox is the best choice for beginners (and a lot of other people) generally.

### Post by kansasnoob on 2009-02-28
For me Flash is a biggy! I love being able to watch flash videos in news clips and such.

What works best for me is installing Firefox using Ubuntuzilla:

[http://ubuntuzilla.wiki.sourceforge.net/](http://ubuntuzilla.wiki.sourceforge.net/)

And then installing the flash plugin - since this is Kubuntu it could be a bit different - but start by installing "kubuntu-restricted-extras". That also brings in the minimum java necessary.

Once Firefox is installed I like to install Flashblock, but not from the repos! Just go to Tools > Add-ons > Get add-ons and search for Flashblock. You should get 1.5.8 or later.

Firefox 3.0.6 + Shockwave 10... + Flashblock 1.5.8 gives me joy!
