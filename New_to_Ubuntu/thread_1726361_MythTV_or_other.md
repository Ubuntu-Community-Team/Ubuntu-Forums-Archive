### Post by mastablasta on 2011-04-11
What do you mean internet TV?
 
If you only want to see on TV what you now see on your computer then you only need a graphics card with HDMI out (nowadays they mostly have only HDMI) and a HDMI cable to connect it with TV (if the TV has an option for HDMI otherwise you might need some adaptor).
 
remote i think would work if the programme you are watching with supports remote control and if oyu can find one that works with linux.
 
 
If you mean tv signal comming to you via internet connection (optics, xDSL...) then you need to build a media box or normal converter. The Tv providers usually give these in their package.

Exactly, the issue that I've found with on demand internet TV is that it's all in different places. I.e BBC channels on iPlayer, ITV on ITV player, Channel 4 on 4OD so unless you're watching these through a browser then there's nothing that's really pulling them all together.
 
I'm running MythTV (with a tuner card) to record my TV - I then watch recordings through my XBMC frontend (or convert them to watch on my Android phone on the way work!). I've also got the iPlayer plugin for XBMC.
 
If you want to watch live TV (and you're in the UK) then [TV Catchup]("http://www.tvcatchup.com/") may work for you. There's also a [plugin for XBMC]("http://plugins.tvcatchup.com/~xbmc/"). I've not used either so your mileage may vary.
