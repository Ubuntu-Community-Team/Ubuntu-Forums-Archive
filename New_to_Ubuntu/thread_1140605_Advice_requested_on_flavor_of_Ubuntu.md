### Post by bennachie on 2009-04-27
+1 for trying the liveCD.

You should be able to run the vanilla (Gnome) version just fine, although a bit of extra RAM would be nice. Xubuntu would be the obvious alternative, but the 9.04 version still seems to have a few issues to address, judging by the most recent comments on Distrowatch.

I'm not sure that playing games under Wine will be a great experience, though, when you have such a relatively modest machine to work with. You might be better to set up a dual boot, retaining, say, a 30GB XP partition on your internal hard disk for that purpose. The remaining 30GB should be more than enough to do anything you could reasonably want to do on Ubuntu, and both operating systems should be able to access data on the external USB disk. If the liveCD runs fine in trial mode, the installer will offer to set up pretty much that configuration. 

Before you go beyond the trial stage, however, clean up your Windows system, deleting all unnecessary bloat, and defrag a couple of times to ensure that what remains can fit comfortably into a smaller partition.

### Post by jimmy the saint on 2009-04-27
I would go with either cruchbang or wattos.  Both are Ubuntu based distributions, so they will be familiar, but are pretty lightweight.  WattOS seemed a little faster to me, but it could have just been imagined.  Openbox is a little different than gnome or kde though.  You need to do a little work with text files to do a few things, but once you get the hang of it, its nothing to worry about. 

That said, if you are going to be using Linux for the first time, and can live with a little lag while you get your feet wet, I found the regular ubuntu to be easiest.  I have moved on to lighter varients/distros now, but in the beginning I found that most things could be done with the gui instead of the command line.
