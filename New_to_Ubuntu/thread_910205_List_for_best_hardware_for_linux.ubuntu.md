### Post by schmidtbag on 2008-09-04
just as a disclaimer i am NOT an dell supporter.  but... typically any moderately new dell product you buy will have all working hardware, including wireless.  i would not recommend you buy a dell, but research a dell with specs of about what you're willing to pay for, and then buy all the same parts yourself.  since dell now supports linux, they have made a good number of drivers, finally something good they did for once.

as for a processor, it really makes no difference but if you want the better speed:price on a dual or single core, go for amd.  otherwise go for intel.  nvidia used to have the best video support but basically as long as you have an ati 9200 or better, any ati card you get will work (thanks to amd pushing ati to make linux drivers).  for a tv tuner just look on newegg for something with what you want and check the reviews or specifications if people say they can get it to work in linux.  you'd be surprised how many ubuntu users there are on that site

### Post by Afkpuz on 2008-09-06
Here is what I've learned from my experience

Motherboard networking doesn't really seem like a problem.  The biggest thing is wireless problems, but cabled network cards have always worked flawlessly for me.

Video cards: Just buy an nvidia card and it will most definitely work.  Sometimes, the brand spanking new cards take some time to get drivers though.

TV Tuner cards: Go with Haupauge.  They have such good linux support and can be bought at newegg.com among other places.  I'm fairly certain that all haupauge cards will work very well

Printers: I use an hp laser printer and it is plug and play.  I hear that lexmark is not very good with their linux support.
