### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2009-03-03
Yes. You must of course make a partition on the HD for /home, you can make a partition taking all the space.

You can do this at the time of installation (use manual partitioning) or post install (let us know if you need a link on how to move your home partition).

### Post by neanderthalman on 2009-03-03
Yes.

You can mount your second drive to the /home folder.

You can also change the location of the home folder for a given user under the "users and groups" under administration to somewhere other than /home.

### Post by Temposs on 2009-03-03
I know it's possible to do. In the Ubuntu installer you'd have to do manual partitioning, and then you'll be able to choose what you want to mount to each drive/partition. I know you can do this because I have an asus eeePC which has two drives, and I've got the / on the small drive and /home on the large drive.
