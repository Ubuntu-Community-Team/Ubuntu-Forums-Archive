### Post by nitstorm on 2011-06-18
If you're trying to aim to being a power user using commands and shell scripts, this is the perfect link for you - [http://linuxcommand.org/](http://linuxcommand.org/)  Super awesome must-read for all wanna-be power users in Linux. I am going through it right now. Extremely informative and very useful.

Also there are other useful stuff like [askubuntu.com]("http://askubuntu.com/") which is a stack exchange site for Ubuntu Q&A. Then there are weekly discussions in IRC channels at #ubuntu-classroom where leaders and such of the community answer your questions. There is also IRC channel #ubuntu where Ubuntu help can be sought or things learnt by observation.  Also Jono Bacon, the Ubuntu Community Manager has a live Q&A channel [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/www.ustream.tv/channel/at-home-with-jono-bacon"). Also a lot of blogs and stuff concerning ubuntu and stuff out there. So... yeah, just look around :) There is no such thing as formal training :P (Atleast I believe so)


Hope that helped a bit at least. Cheers! and ofc, welcome to UF :)

### Post by trozamon on 2011-06-18
If you're up for a challenge, go to the Absolute Beginner forums and see if you can solve their problems. You don't need to post solutions, just see if you can solve problems. If you can't, try to figure out the problems.

Other than that, try setting up your own home network with a media server.

Generally, just try to do things in the most complex way(for example, configuring options for programs in the terminal) possible. That's how I learned.
