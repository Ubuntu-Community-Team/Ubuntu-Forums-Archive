### Post by hakermania on 2011-05-01
Unfortunately:
Applications don't go to tray anymore, because TRAY DOESN'T EXIST (complaints go [here]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/unity/+bug/752371"))
Also, it becomes extremely challenging (LOL) to switch between open windows because
1) You cannot know what is open at the current moment
2) Applications don't minimize when you click on the panel, you can only launch an application from the panel and focus on it, but not to minimize it
3) Alt-tab for switching between the open applications is horrible now, it appears slowly and when browsing with it the open applications is too hard to understand who is who (it doesn't provide window's title etc)
4) It is like searching your kid in the circus if you want to focus on an application through the panel, so Alt-tab is preferable IMO

As for your occasion
Or
1) the application has minimized normally and it is at the panel
Or
2) the application has minimized to tray and turns to be buggy with the new Ubuntu (for a reason this makes me angry)

### Post by New Linux Usr on 2011-05-02
If your not happy with the new effects of Unbuntu, you could always go to:
[INDENT]System->Administration->Login Screen
[/INDENT]Then select "Ubuntu Classic (No effects)" and hit close. Reboot, and there you have it, with the icon tray at the bottom of the screen, and back in appearance to Ubuntu before 11.04.

Kind regards,

New Linux Usr
