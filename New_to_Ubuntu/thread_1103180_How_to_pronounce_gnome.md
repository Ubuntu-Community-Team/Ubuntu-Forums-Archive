### Post by dwasifar on 2009-03-23
I think people are hearing "genome" in their heads at least two and maybe three different ways when reading this thread: "geh-nome," "jee-nome," and possibly "jen-ome."

None of those is a "correct" pronunciation of "GNOME" as we are discussing, though the first comes closest.  Its creators pronounce it "guh-nome."  I try to say it that way, but it's hard to bring myself to do it, so half the time I wind up saying "nome."

Personally I wish Stallman had kept the goofball wordplay out of GNU (and hence out of GNOME).  It causes needless confusion, as if he were to launch an open source soft drink and insist that it be spelled "cola" but pronounced "coll-a."  It's probably also part of the reason that his campaign to make people say "GNU/Linux" makes him look like Quixote tilting at windmills; people aren't sure HOW to say it, or if they DO know, they are afraid to look foolish.

(Oh, and FWIW, the correct pronunciation of genome is the second one.)
