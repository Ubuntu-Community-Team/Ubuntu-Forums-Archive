### Post by Didius Falco on 2009-05-16
Hi,

I'd consider 8.10 a "safe" upgrade, but your system specs, particularly your video card and if/how important 3D is to you will play a big part in determining if you should go to 9.04.

I'd read the release notes thoroughly to see if there any any known issues with hardware on your PC:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/releasenotes/904](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/releasenotes/904)

I'd also do some searching on the forum regarding your video card - both ATI and nVidia have just moved a range of older cards to their "legacy" lists, which means that there may not be a driver that will work in 3D for your card, and some Intel cards have had some problems, too, though most of those seem to have workarounds.

In short, I'd advise doing a little research first, before making the leap to 9.04.

I'd also advise a clean install, but that's just my personal opinion. Lots of people use the Upgrade function without any problems, but Upgrading to me always feels like being the second one to use the bathwater - cleaner than I was, but not as clean as I want to be. ;)

Regards,

Didius

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-05-16
Yeah, LTS doesn't mean that there are going to be fewer bugs. The LTS releases have the same development time and same bug-fix phase as non-LTS releases. They are just guaranteed to be supported with security fixes and a limited number of bugfixes across their lifetime. Also, the software is guaranteed not to change dramatically - a common source of headaches for people using computers in production environments.
