### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-05-04
Your hard disk already appears to have Ubuntu 10.04 on it; at least, there are Linux swap and ext4 data partitions. It's a 200 GiB hard disk and Windows probably has 68 GiB of this.

I honestly don't know why it's saying there's no other operating systems found, unless those partitions are empty.

If you click the "Something else..." button in the installer, you SHOULD (if it's anything like the 10.10 installer - sorry I haven't tried this with 11.04) be given a little partitioning tool where you can choose to put /dev/sda5 as "/" (the ext4 partition, as the root filesystem) and the rest should just work.
