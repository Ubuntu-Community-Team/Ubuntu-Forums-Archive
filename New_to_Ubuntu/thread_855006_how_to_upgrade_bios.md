### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2008-07-14
The question begging to be asked here is this:

**Why do you need to upgrade your BIOS?**

Is everything working OK with your current BIOS?

Unless you have a dual-BIOS motherboard, upgrading a BIOS can be a very risky procedure.

If something goes wrong during the upgrade, even the smallest glitch, your motherboard will become a doorstop. The only way to fix this is to usually return it to the factory for a BIOS chip replacement. A very expensive exercise, if they will do it for you.

If your BIOS is working fine, leave well alone.

What do they say about trying to fix something that isn't broken?
