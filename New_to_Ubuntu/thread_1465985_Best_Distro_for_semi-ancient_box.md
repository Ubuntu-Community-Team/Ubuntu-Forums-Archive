384M RAM is at the ragged edge of memory for a "modern" Ubuntu;
the system will do a lot of swapping, and that WILL impact performance.

But then Command Line version of most *any* distro will run in 64MB :)
I've seen CLI run in 8MB RAM.

### Post by snowpine on 2010-04-30
I knew this would be a lively discussion. :)

CrunchBang really is the best of the lightweight distros; I use it on everything from my 600mhz Pentium 3 with 384mb ram to my netbook to my 64bit desktop with 2gb ram. My only hesitation recommending CrunchBang to someone who's never used it before is that project is currently in development phase, so you have two non-ideal choices: The current stable release (based on Ubuntu 9.04, so it is slightly outdated and will reach end of life in October) and the "Statler" alpha release (based on the fantastic Debian Squeeze but a little buggy due to its alpha status).

I am also fond of AntiX and use it on my oldest computer (500mhz with 128mb ram). Sidux is cool too but maybe a little "bleeding edge" for your purposes; try Debian Stable with Xfce or LXDE if you want zero bugs. Elive is nice and sparkly but a lot of people are put off by its $15 price tag. Slitaz used to be cool (1.0 was totally innovative and unique, but now it is just another LXDE distro IMHO) and Puppy I do not like due to its lack of proper user management (though I hear they might be fixing this in future releases). 

And just because I didn't mention a distro here doesn't mean it's no good; there are just too many to try them all. :)
