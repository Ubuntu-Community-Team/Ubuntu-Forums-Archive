### Post by Paqman on 2012-11-06
Highly unlikely to be possible. Phones use ARM chips, for which support is still very limited on Ubuntu. The original HTC Dream also had very little RAM and would be unlikely to run a full desktop OS well.

Debian has better ARM support than Ubuntu does, try directing your inquiries that way.

### Post by dolphin194 on 2012-11-06
It would be possible to put Ubuntu on your phone, but it would be extremely difficult.

Let me re-word what Paqman said for you. **Ubuntu is not supported** on the ARM processors that phones use. You will not be able to install Ubuntu on your phone. You *might *be able to find some version of Ubuntu maintained by the community, but it's highly unlikely. Canonical *is in fact* looking into putting Ubuntu on Android phones (as seen [here]("http://www.ubuntu.com/devices/android")), but it will probably be a while before we actually see it.

The Android OS is actually based on Linux. It is also open-source, as stated [here]("http://source.android.com/"). I don't understand why you can't just use it, and I'm sure there's a way to run your regular Linux applications on it.

And finally, as Paqman said, Debian can be put on phones.
