### Post by Scott M. Sanders on 2009-11-24
Yes, ink does dry up. Try removing the cartridge and gently wipe its metal connector with a clean, soft, dry cloth, to the point where some ink comes off onto the cloth. We have to do this almost weekly with our Pitney Bowes mailer at the office, which uses HP cartridges. Home ones are a bit more resistant, but after a year or so, it could sure use a once over.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2009-11-24
I used to use an Epson printer, great if you do as lot of printing, as the cartridges are cheap, especially if you try compatible versions.  If you print seldom, as I do, they are very prone to drying out, and as the print head jets are permanent, and not renewed when you get a new cartridge, they are almost impossible to clean, and almost useless to a low level print output person like me.

I now use an HP all in one, where the print heads are part of the cartridge and so are renewed with a new ink cartridge.  I still seldom print, just the same, but also seldom suffer with the dry ink jets in the print head.

I suggest, therefore, that you avoid any printer with a permanent ink jet print head (most Epson and lots of Brother) unless you do a lot of printing, which is actually the best way to keep heads clean and usable.

### Post by lkraemer on 2009-11-24
Get an old cookie sheet from your wife, or a tin pie pan.
Fold a Bounty paper towel longways into fourths.  Lay in the pan,
and soak one end with finger nail polish remover.  Tap your
print cartridge on the paper towel easily to force the finger
nail polish remover into the print cartridge nozzles, enough that
it loosens the ink.  Then use the same method with a paper towel
with one end very damp.  You should be able to make a nice mark
on the paper towel when it drags over the towel.  I've used this
method on several HP cartridges for my 712 printer to break loose
the ink.  If you try to print too much with no ink available, the
cartridge will burn out, and you will have to replace it.

### Post by oldfred on 2009-11-25
I have had good luck with a q-tip and alcohol. Gently wipe the nozzles with the damp q-tip to clean the nozzles. Alcohol is a good solvent for many inks. At work we used to add it to printing ink to speed drying and reduce foaming.
