### Post by ibuclaw on 2008-06-04
If you dual boot Linux/Windows and you share files between both operating systems, it's usually a good idea.

Linux may not get infected by viruses, but that immunity can be often taken for granted and malicious content (usually Windows binaries, but Microsoft Word and Excel documents can be culprits too) are downloaded in the process. Where you don't find out what they really are until you reboot into Windows and run.

An Anti-Virus software for Linux also has a very good purpose too.
If your Windows drive is infected, viruses are easier to find and remove with scanners through Linux.

Since the virus isn't running, it can't defend itself from fix/removal.

But is it a necessity? Absolutely not.
You should download it/use it really when trouble arises for your Windows Account.
