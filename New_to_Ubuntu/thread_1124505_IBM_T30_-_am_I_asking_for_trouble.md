### Post by IdahoBackwoods on 2009-04-13
Canon doesn't provide Linux drivers for most of their PIXMA printers.  I had this problem with an iP5000 and now with an iP4500.  The solution I'm using is a German product called TurboPrint, which costs 30 euros.  TurboPrint has drivers for many of the PIXMA printers, but you don't get to check your ink tanks from the software, and you don't get on-screen info about the location of a paper jam.

I have written CanonUSA about this, and their answer is (paraphrasing) "tough luck."  So I told them that I won't be buying printers from them in the foreseeable future.  It's a shame.
