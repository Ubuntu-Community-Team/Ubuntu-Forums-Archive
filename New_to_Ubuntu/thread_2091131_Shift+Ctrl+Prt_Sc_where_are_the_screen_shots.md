### Post by evilsoup on 2012-12-04
Using SHIFT+PRINTSC (no ctrl) does exactly the same, except that it saves the screenshot to a folder of your choice (it defaults to $HOME/Pictures). Also, alt+printsc takes a picture of the currently-selected window, and printsc by itself, of course, takes a picture of the entire screen.

### Post by CaptainMark on 2012-12-05
I believe the standard gnome-derivitive shortcuts for screenshots are:
Printsc will capture the entire desktop
Alt + Printsc will capture the current window
Shift + Printsc will give you a target to select area

All of these will save the screenshot to the Picture folder but holding Ctrl will act as a modifier to the above shortcuts to send the screenshot to the clipboard instead of Pictures folder
