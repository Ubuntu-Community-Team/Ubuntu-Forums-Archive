### Post by django0909 on 2008-09-01
Hi Bob,

Assuming you are using Ubuntu, and your browser is Firefox, (orange bar at the top), you can do this.

Right click on your Desktop, click Create Launcher.

In the 'Name' box type 'firefox'

In the 'Command' box, type 'firefox'

Click OK, and an icon called 'firefox' should have appeared on your Desktop.

Click on that, and you should be back on the net in no time.


Sorry if this isn't the answer to your problem....!

### Post by Clippy on 2008-09-01
Also, do you have a "panel" (a thin bar across the top of your screen, that may say "Applications - Places - System" etc. on it)?  By default, the latest version of Ubuntu has a Firefox (internet browser) icon on the panel, and you may be used to using that.  

If this is the case, and you would like to have the Firefox icon on your panel again, this is what you can do:
[I]
Click on "Applications"

Go to "Internet"

Right-click on "Firefox Web Browser"

Click on "Add this Launcher to Panel"[/I]

and a Firefox icon should appear on the panel.

It's also possible that your desktop is configured differently than this, in which case the first solution posted by django will be a safer bet. :)
