### Post by cogier on 2010-09-05
Try [Ctrl] [Alt] [Delete]. The [Delete] must NOT be the one on the numeric keyboard. You will just need to press [Enter] when the dialogue box pops up.

My laptop will also bring up the close down menu by pressing the [On] key.

### Post by Kixtosh on 2010-09-10
> **cogier said:**
> Try [Ctrl] [Alt] [Delete]. The [Delete] must NOT be the one on the numeric keyboard. You will just need to press [Enter] when the dialogue box pops up.

My laptop will also bring up the close down menu by pressing the [On] key.I use the CTRL+ALT+DEL method also, for convenience mostly (it avoids navigating the mouse to the shutdown button on the panel). 

Otherwise, I used the power management options (System-Preferences Menu) to set the power button to shut down, instead of bringing up the dialogue box. The option is in the "General" tab. This can be useful if something happens to make the screen go blank (it's happened me when hot swapping the VGA cable, or messing with the display settings). The laptop shuts down cleanly even though I can't actually see what's going on.
