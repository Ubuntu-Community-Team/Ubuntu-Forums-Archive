### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-11-15
Apart from the devices already referred to the only other devices the OS (Ubuntu Phone) has official support for are the Nexus 4, Nexus 7 2013 & Nexus 10. Those devices are still being used by Canonical engineers as references devices for testing the Ubuntu Phone OS. But they are surely no longer top of the range.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

I think that the BQ Aquarius 4.5 has a screen with a touch button on it which the BQ Aquarius 4.5 Ubuntu Edition does not. So, buying standard BQ Aquarius 4.5 and flashing it does produce a BQ Aquarius 4.5 Ubuntu edition exactly.

The Ubuntu Phone does not yet have mass market appeal. I do not see why anyone would want to buy a Nexus device that has been re-flashed with Ubuntu phone. It is not as if you can buy them without an OS installed and at a discount price. And can then offer a good mobile device at a lower price.
