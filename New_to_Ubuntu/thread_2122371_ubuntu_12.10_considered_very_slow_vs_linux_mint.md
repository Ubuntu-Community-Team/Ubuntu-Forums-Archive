### Post by slooksterpsv on 2013-03-05
Unity does require a bit of a higher-end machine. Cinnamon is based on the older Gnome2 libraries and uses Compiz for it's rendering (I believe Unity does as well). Unity is faster cause Dash does do a lot of caching for apps, documents, music, etc. for "faster" searching. It may be indexing your home folder as to why Unity runs slower; if you're home folder is empty, I know that with Unity alone on my machine it ran too slow for me. I switched to Gnome Shell and it blazes vs Unity; compared to Cinnamon, the 2D version ran fast whilst the regular cinammon used a bit of my graphics chipset to power it. Either way you can try various others UIs such as KDE (which last I ran was too slow for me but is apparently fixed in 4.9 as the Window Manager ate up a lot of resources (not KDM)). It's all up to what you want and feel most comfortable with. Lubuntu uses LXDE which is super light, which is why I've ran it on computers with 320MB of RAM.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-03-17

You don't need to remember what programs you have installed. Open the Dash and click on the little bookshelf icon at the bottom of the Dash. Or right-click the Ubuntu icon on the Launcher.
