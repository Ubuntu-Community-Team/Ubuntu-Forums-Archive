### Post by jjex22 on 2011-12-29
I'm afraid nothing compares to SAGE in terms features functions and compatibility - after all everyone in the UK uses SAGE. It will run under WINE - though you may have to install the ODBC Drivers after install. I haven't used it myself on linux, only in my previous job, but it did seem to be an industry standard at least here in the UK and the reviews online of it under WINE seem to be very good.

in terms of open source, I've used GNUcash on mac, it's not quite as polished but very stable and linux/mac native... plus it's free - only downside is that it's designed for home/business use, not really large business/server use. you can do payroll stuff on it just fine - admittedly I only played with this to keep track of my own wages, and it's been 2 years since I had to use it regularly (no more money work for me!) but It was perfectly functional.

I'd say gnucash, unless you've already paid for a SAGE licence - then of course use that... one problem I had was that gnucash and sage used different fil formats - PAIN!, but this may have changed! It may be different where you are, but I only mention SAGE because it really is a standard in the UK - If everyone else wasn't using it, I wouldn't recommend it - A bit like MS Office - if Libre office couldn't read MSdocs, I would have to recommend ms office to everyone on linux - Can you imagine?!

### Post by audiomick on 2011-12-29
I've been using GnuCash for the last couple of years. I am a freelance sound engineer, and it does everything I need. I am changing to another program now, but only because the new program, a shareware program, is specifically tailored  to the needs of the German Tax system. The GnuCash Invoice blank doesn't have  a place for your own Tax number, or your Bank details. It would be possible to include them by changing the blank, but I don't have the inclination to learn the mark up language that it is written in. I have been posting the invoices in GnuCash for the book keeping, and then writing an Invoice to send to the client in Open Office.
