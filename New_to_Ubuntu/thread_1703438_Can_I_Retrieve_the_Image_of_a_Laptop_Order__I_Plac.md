### Post by sydbat on 2011-03-09
Well...retrieving the cached page all depends on how you have things set up in Edit > Preferences (assuming Firefox). 

If you have it set to remove everything when you close your browser, then it is gone.

However, if you have not changed anything in the defaults, then you might be able to go into History > Recently Closed Tabs > 'Page you were on' and open it. Also, you can go to View > Sidebar > History and go through your recent page visits (up to 30 days I believe).

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-03-09
You can go to Places>Home Folder. When the Nautilus window open go to View and select Show Hidden files. Then look for the .mozilla folder. Open it and then open the Firefox folder. You may see one or two folders with names that are a mixture of letters and numbers ending in .default. Try opening both of them until you see a folder called Cache. Open that and you will see all or most of all the images downloaded from your web browsing.
