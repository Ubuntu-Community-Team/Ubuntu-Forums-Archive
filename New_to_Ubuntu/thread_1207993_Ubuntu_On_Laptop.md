### Post by aysiu on 2009-07-08
The problems aren't for laptops in general... they are mainly for specific components. For example, Ubuntu 9.04 has support for certain Broadcom wireless cards, but 8.04 doesn't (out-of-the-box, anyway), nor does 9.10 (the alpha next release). Intel graphics cards will generally work better than ATI or Nvidia cards, which may require proprietary drivers to get working, and even then may be buggy.

Just run the live CD and see how it goes.

### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2009-07-08
I installed Ubuntu 8.10 on a 6 year old Dell Inspiron 5100 laptop after upgrading the RAM to 1 GB and the HDD to 60GB. On the Dell Inspiron 5100 laptop, it worked out-of-the-box. 

I installed Ubuntu Netbook Remix on an EeePC 701 netbook. On the EeePC 701 netbook, it worked out-of-the-box.  

I also installed Ubuntu 8.04, 8.10, and 9.04 on a Dell Inspiron 1420 laptop. On the Dell Inspiron 1420 laptop, I had to work around an Intel Graphics chip problem with 9.04; however, that only took a few minutes to fix by searching the Ubuntu forums. On the Dell Inspiron 1420 laptop, 8.04 had a problem with the Broadcom wireless adapter; but, that was easily fixed also. On the Dell Inspiron 1420 laptop, 8.10 worked out-of-the-box.

Try it out using the Live CD or Live USB disk. If only one device has a problem, such as wireless, then you can probably get it working with the helpful people on the Ubuntu forums with minimal effort. If you have more than one device that doesn't work out-of-the-box, and if you are a noob to linux, it could be frustrating.
