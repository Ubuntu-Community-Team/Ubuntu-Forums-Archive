### Post by mastablasta on 2012-05-24
in linux drivers are integrated into kernel (the system core). sometimes the drivers can't be integrated due to their non-free nature (they are proprietary software and closed source). in that case additional drivers applicaiton will help you find them and install them.
 
intel is supporting linux by opening the source for their drivers and giving other support to linux, so they can be and are integrated into kernel. you can read more here:

### Post by anewguy on 2012-05-24
An important thing to be mentioned here, that has been mentioned in other threads like this as well:

The driver CD that came with your computer is for Windows, not for Linux.  Those drivers will not even install in Linux.

Linux has its own set of drivers and usually installs them as needed when it boots.  Sometimes there are exceptions with some video and wireless cards, but these are normally easily fixed.

So, if you are coming from Windows to Linux and want to know what to do with that CD that came with your computer (or with your motherboard if you built your own PC) - you don't use it in Linux.
