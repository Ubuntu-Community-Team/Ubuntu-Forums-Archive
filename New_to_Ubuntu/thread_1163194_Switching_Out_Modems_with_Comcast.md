### Post by CompBio on 2009-05-18
We just got Comcast service last week.  The modem they gave me with the service has been working fine with my Ubuntu laptop, but I didn't want to pay a monthly modem fee, so I bought a Motorola SurfBoard 5100 on Craig's list.

I shut down my computer and disconnected the old modem, then swapped the wires over, powered up the new modem and booted the laptop.  It finds the network with no problem, but the only website I can get to is one that tries to do a Comcast auto-install of some kind.  It claims it doesn't support my operating system.

I figure this must be a configuration or driver issue.  I suspect I won't get far if I call Comcast.  Hopefully someone here has experienced this before -- if so can you point me in the right direction?

### Post by MikeEvans on 2009-05-18
They need to register your new modem in their system before they let you out on the net.  You can call them and tell them you have a new modem and they will have you read the MAC address.  Otherwise you should be able to do it online with windows.  Once the modem is activated windows and linux should have no problems getting online.  If you don't have a windows system laying around you can try getting internet explorer on your linux box with wine or virtualbox.
