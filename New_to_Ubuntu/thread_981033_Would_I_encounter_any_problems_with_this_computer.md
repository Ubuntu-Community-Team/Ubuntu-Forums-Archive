### Post by Titan8990 on 2008-11-13
If you are actually interested in gaming you should look in to a Core 2 Duo. Those 6000+ looks very nice at how cheap they are now but there performance will lack when it comes to physic intensive games.

I recommend a P35 chipset board with a e8400 or e7200 if you budget confines you to that. Not only do they outperform the AMDs at a lower clock rate, Intel has much better Linux support than AMD.


Edit: Also, there is enormous overclocking potential in the Core 2 chips.


What power supply do you plan to run this machine on? Also, 800mhz RAM is typically only a couple dollars more than 667. I would only get the 667 if you plan to get a 1333mhz FSB CPU and would like to leave it on a 1:1 ratio.

### Post by Linux user 66 on 2008-11-13
I agree with Titan8990, however, bear in mind that the newer motherboards based on the P45 chipset are also available. I'm using the Intel Core 2 Duo E8400 CPU and it gives you a lot for your money.

I'd also recommend that you buy 2 GB RAM/memory of the DDR2 SDRAM type at 800 MHz ( PC2-6400 ) minimum. Try to find modules with a CAS latency of 4.

Personally I'd rather have a Radeon HD 4870 (with 1GB GDDR5) VGA card or a Radeon HD 4850 (with 512MB GDDR3), but that's just a matter of personal opinion. However, on Linux it might be better to go for an NVIDIA VGA card (like the one you've chosen), since the drivers might be better. I'm using an NVIDIA VGA card, so I don't know how well Radeon VGA cards perform in Linux.

I hope you find what you need.

### Post by ECPCLINUX on 2008-11-14
Hi, I think that the Video card your suggesting is great, if you can afford it. The weak link in your suggested system is your memory, buy at least 800mhz. I have the AMD x2 6000 and is fast, but I also have the Core2Duo e6600 and it's faster than my AMD x2 6000. I also have the Core2Duo e8400 and thats the fastest by far. Plus Core2Duo can be overclocked nicely. For example my Wife's Core2Duo e4500 it overcloked at 2.5 and I have been successful in over clocking it as high as 3.0, but for her 2.5 was fine, plus I could use the stock Heatsink fan safely, since Core2Duo's tend to run cooler. The most I have been able to over clock my AMD x2 6000 is 3.2 from the stock speed 3.0 and that's with a decent Cooler Master Hyper TX2 CPU Cooler. To help you decide, Check CPU performances here: [http://www.tomshardware.com/charts/desktop-cpu-charts-q3-2008/benchmarks,31.html](http://www.tomshardware.com/charts/desktop-cpu-charts-q3-2008/benchmarks,31.html)
At that same site you can check out Video cards performances. I would personally stick with Nvidia for now as someone mentioned for a Linux Box.

As for motherboard chipset I love nVidia, I have had four systems with Nvidia chipset, nforce4, nVidia 6100 and now nVidia nForce 520 and they worked well with Ubuntu 7.10 up to 8.04LTS. I also have the P35 chipset working well in Ubuntu 8.10. The brands I have use range from Gigabyte, Abit, Foxconn, ECS, and  my wife's MSI. My only issue so far in Ubuntu is Skype, ever since Pulseaudio, which was included in Ubuntu 8.04LTS on. I just recently removed Pulseaudio and it serves my needs well.

 

Last but not least since you seem to live in the US, look up Newegg.com. They have the best online customer service and the prices are just great. I purchased for my AMD x2-6000 the Gskill DDR2 800 (PC2 6400) Dual Channel Kit and it works absolutely perfect, it overclocks like a champ. The rating for these at Newegg are excellent, they have earned the Customer Choice award x3. Dollar per dollar, Of all the memories I have these are my favorite and the best value.
