### Post by jpoRS on 2009-03-16
Ok, I will try and explain this using an elaborate metaphor involving a library.  It will leave a lot of things muddled still, but hopefully you will be able to understand what all these number mean.

[RAM]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAM"): If your computer is a library, RAM is your table.  The more RAM you have, the bigger your table.  This means you can have more books/programs open at one time.  4GB is a pretty hefty amount of RAM.  I have 2GB and rarely use more than 40% of that.  However if you are looking to do some serious gaming there is no such thing as too much RAM.  However for normal day to day use 4GB is more than enough.

[The Hard Drive]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hard_drive"): Think of your HD as the number of shelves in your library.  The bigger your HD, the more information you can store in your library/computer.  320 is a pretty good size, depending on what you plan on doing.  If you are an above average (if you are one you already know) hoarder of music/movies/whatever you will probably want to buy an external hard drive at some point, but for a laptop you don't want to go much bigger because it will start hurting battery life.

[Processor]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_processing_unit"): This is your Core2 Duo, and it is what is measured in GHZ.  Think of this as your librarian/research assistant (though I have never known anyone to have a Sexy CPU fetish).  Your processor is what does things on your computer.  You have a Core2, which essentially means you have two librarians running around doing stuff for you.  More librarians=your computer does things faster.  The speed of your processor is measured in Gigahertz (GHZ) and the bigger the number the faster your computer will process the information required to do whatever it is that you want to do with your computer.

Hope this helps, and welcome to the community,
jim
