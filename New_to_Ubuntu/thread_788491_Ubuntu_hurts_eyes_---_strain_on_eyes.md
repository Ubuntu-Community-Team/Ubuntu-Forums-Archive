### Post by rxchaos on 2008-05-09

I am an_ extreme noob _on Ubuntu but very proficient on windows, I have been fooling around with 8.04 Hardy Heron on a within windows install for about 2 weeks.

My eyes seem to _always_ bother me after 20+ minutes of use on Ubuntu.  First thing I noticed that bothered me was the focus in effect with wobbly windows (compiz), all text on the screen would be blurred right before focusing in when minimizing or maximizing (also the same effect when switching between desktops with 3D cube).  I have disabled the wobbly effect but my eyes still bother me regardless.  I do my fair share switching between desktops via 3D cube (a luxury that is new to a windows user)  Also, now with firefox 3 beta 5 I feel as though my screen is zoomed out a little (specifically text, sites like [www.engadget.com](www.engadget.com)) and websites do not look the same on the same computer when switching between windows xp and Ubuntu 8.04.

I have searched other posts about this and the only thing I could find was eye strain due to the type of font, I'm not sure that this is my problem.  I'm not sure if this is because of Ubuntu, compiz visual effects, or something else.  I stare at a computer screen 5+ hours everyday for many years and have never had any problems.  Is there anyone else who has encountered this problem and had a fix?  All suggestions welcome, please keep in my mind my limited Ubuntu knowledge.  

Some of my basic information:

-Dell 22" widescreen LCD Monitor
-ATI x1950 pro 512mb video card (believe drivers are installed correctly b/c compiz runs extremely smooth)
-Ubuntu 8.04 - Hardy Heron
-Resolution: 1680 x 1050 @ 60 Hz
-Theme, background, fonts everything basic that is installed with Ubuntu (no custom changes except compiz)

Thank you for your time
