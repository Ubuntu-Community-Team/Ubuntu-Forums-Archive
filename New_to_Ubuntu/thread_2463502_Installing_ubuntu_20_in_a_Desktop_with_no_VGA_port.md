### Post by scorp123 on 2021-06-14
> **aldcar said:**
> Acer Aspire XC-704G 

I own a similar PC:  Acer Aspire XC-605.  It also has 1 x HDMI port. And Ubuntu works tip top here. You said you have 2 x HDMI ports? Did you try changing ports, e.g. connecting the HDMI cable into the other port? Have you tried connecting that PC to a PC monitor via HDMI? If you don't have one: can you ask a friend or a neighbour to borrow you one so you could try what happens if you connect a PC monitor instead of the TV? 

Maybe your TV is at fault here, e.g. it does not properly detect the video signal? I have this problem too sometimes. In my case I sometimes have to switch sources via the remote control (e.g. select "HDMI 1" as a source, then back to "HDMI 3" where the PC is connected ...) before my TV will recognise that there's indeed a video signal coming from the PC.

### Post by scorp123 on 2021-06-14
> **aldcar said:**
> ... the tv turns blank and nothing happens

This might be a problem with your TV, e.g. it does not like the resolution and frequency the PC has switched into. Some TV's only want 1920x1080 at exactly 60 Hz when a PC is attached to it. Try any other resolution at any other refresh rate .... aaaand the TV screen goes black.

Can you try what happens when you connect that PC to a normal PC monitor?

Alternative idea: When starting Ubuntu there should be an option to select "Safe Graphics". Can you try that?
