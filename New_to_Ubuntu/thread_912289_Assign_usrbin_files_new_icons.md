### Post by scorp123 on 2008-09-06
> **Zopiac said:**
> i want to change the icon of the program itself... That's kind of pointless, sorry to say so. To my knowledge on Linux programs themselves don't have any icons (which is different from e.g. Mac OS X or Windows where binaries indeed can have icons embedded into their own binary code). So when you say "I want to change the icon of a program in /usr/bin ... " ... that's like saying "I want to change the warp-drive on my VW beetle ... " ... there is no such thing here, and whatever you mean to change: you most likely meant something else?

You'd have to take a different route and explain what exactly you want to achieve.

Do you ....

a) ... want Nautilus (= the file manager) to display binaries and files in general with a different icon? If so: Consider changing the icon theme then. You can download many themes and icon sets from web sites such as [http://www.gnome-look.org](http://www.gnome-look.org)

OR:


b) Do you want to change program's icon (e.g. on your desktop or on your panel) to something custom? If so: As someone else already mentioned: right-click on that icon, choose "Properties" and then click on the current icon and a dialogue will open and let you select a new one.

That's how Linux programs "get their icons" ... you simply assign one via the program's application launcher or menu entry (but you don't touch anything in /usr/bin !!)


So I hope my guesses are right? If not: My apologies then, but in that case I failed to understand what you mean.
