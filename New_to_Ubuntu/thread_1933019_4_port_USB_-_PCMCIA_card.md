### Post by audiomick on 2012-02-28
I'd be inclined to think that there was a running change in the production of the card that the manufacturer isn't mentioning.

It is possible that your system has had some driver updates or something, but if you haven't changed the system since the last card broke, that is unlikely. It would be an enormous but not impossible coincidence if the OS got a driver update between you breaking the old card and getting the new one.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-02-29
> **suomalainen said:**
> ... I broke this card and had to buy a new one. I bought same exact make and model.

It's not uncommon for manufacturers to change chipsets, even with the same make and model device.

Back when Gigabit Internet was in its infancy, I bought a Gb card from Linksys.  The same card came out in no less than three additional revisions.  The box didn't change, just the chipsets on the card.

And, since your chipsets aren't visible, there's really no way to see if they changed it in the newer version you recently bought.
