### Post by kemtnbkr on 2011-12-14
+1 for asifnaz.  As a rule of thumb, older (mainstream) hardware is generally supported.  If you were looking at brand-new components, compatibility would be more of a cause for concern, or if you had a rig with a PowerPC or SPARC64 or some other non-Intel or non-AMD CPU.

Also, in terms of graphics, I'm not sure what the minimum spec of graphics hardware you'd need for Unity.  Generally speaking, you can get away with pretty low-powered graphics hardware as long as you're not cranking Compiz effects up like crazy.  Unity ran fine on my ATI X300 128MB GDDR2 card, although I never messed with Compiz.  Also, if you want to save on hardware, you can always go cheap and run a lighter-weight distro like Lubuntu (my current) or Xubuntu, for example.  

However, its always good to check :)
