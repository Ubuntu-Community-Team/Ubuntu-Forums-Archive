### Post by CatKiller on 2009-05-28
Maybe, maybe not. Some control keys seem to work and some don't. The obvious way to set those keys to adjust the volume is to use the tool at System -> Preferences -> Keyboard Shortcuts and look for the Volume down and Volume up settings. You can set those keys to other values than the default. Just click in the box and then press the key(s) that you want to use to perform that function.

### Post by eMJayy on 2009-05-28
Hi. Does a black volume applet appear when you press the volume controls on your laptop? 

If it does appear but the volume still fails to be adjusted by the controls when you press them, you just need to go to System > Preferences > Sound and, under the Devices Tab, you'll see a section at the bottom marked "Default Mixer Tracks" - this is the section that lets you select which tracks the keyboard volume button actually controls (once it's recognized). 

Depending on the mixer setting being used, you may have more than one track listed for each mixer option. You just need to select the one that controls either master volume or 'front speakers' and your volume control buttons should start controlling whatever you select. 

If the black volume applet isn't appearing when you press the volume controls, you'll need to follow the instructions that the previous poster gave in order to configure the volume controls for use.
