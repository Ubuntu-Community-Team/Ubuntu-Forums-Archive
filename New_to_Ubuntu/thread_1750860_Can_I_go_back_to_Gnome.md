Archkidd, if you have 2 documents open and the one that is full screened ins in front of the other, hold down the Super key (usually the one with the Windows logo), this will bring out the, um, whatever the little doc bar on the side is called. To move something to another workspace, well, you can right click on the title bar go to "move to another works space" then tell it which workspace to go to. ### Post by Lateralis on 2011-05-06
> **3rdalbum said:**
> Gnome wasn't actually intuitive, it was just similar to Windows which is what you were already used to. If you look at any self-confessed "computer illiterate" person, you'll find that nothing in Gnome or Windows is actually "intuitive".


I have to disagree with this.  

Don't confuse intuitiveness with familiarity.  Something can be familiar but unintuitive, as well as intuitive but unfamiliar.  The two concepts are not the same.  

The Applications menu is a particular case in point.  For anyone, whether they are new to computers or just new to Linux, will find it a doddle to find a program they've installed, as there's a clear menu in the top left of the screen which lists every installed program in (broadly) logical categories.  The menu is clear and intuitive.  The same cannot be said of Unity, where you actually have to search for your programs or add an icon to the launcher menu, which is going to get terribly cluttered if you use a lot of different programs during the course of the day.   And on this point I totally agree with the OP.  In my personal experience so far, Unity just requires more clicks than Gnome 2 to achieve things.  This doesn't bother me too much as I actually use terminals an awful lot so tend to start programs from the command line than a Gui!  But there are plenty of others that do not do this. 

In time, increased familiarity might enable me to find shortcuts and to do things as quickly if not quicker than in Gnome 2.  But in my opinion, the new Unity menu is less intuitive and far, far less useful than the old Applications menu.
