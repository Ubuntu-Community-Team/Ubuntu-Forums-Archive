### Post by Kirk Schnable on 2013-09-05
> **Chant_Gras said:**
> Fn+F8
If you're having issues, the first thing I'd do is stop trying to use those key shortcuts.  Depending on the laptop, those key shortcuts may require ACPI configurations\drivers that aren't installed for you by default.

I personally have a ThinkPad, so the relevant information for my laptop is here: [http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_get_special_keys_to_work](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_get_special_keys_to_work)


Regardless, my recommendation to you is to use the Monitors\Display Settings to activate and deactivate your projector.  Depending on your UI (Unity, XFCE, etc) this may be located in a different place, but try searching for it on the System Menu if you have one.

If you let us know what desktop interface you have installed, we can help you find your display settings if necessary.

Kirk
