### Post by MG&amp;TL on 2011-10-29
Gnome-shell?

The workspaces thing isn't as well-defined in gnome-shelll, instead, in the activities menu, drag a window to the left and deposit in the empty window. This will make another workspace below it. You can then use:

Ctrl-Alt-Down: Move down a workspace
Ctrl-Alt-UP: Move up a workspace

Add shift into either of these, and it takes the current window with you.
