It's in the DMCA.
It is most famous for criminalizing the **distribution** of methods for circumventing proprietary locks. Many people confuse this phrase to mean that you cannot discuss it.

> "The Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) is a controversial United States copyright law. The act criminalizes production and dissemination of technology that can circumvent measures taken to protect copyright, not merely infringement of copyright itself, and heightens the penalties for copyright infringement on the Internet." --Taken from Wikipedia

Technologies that allow users to 'crack' the system to allow it to be installed on third-party machines are illegal, but the discussion of such methods is permitted.

Just like how you could learn how to shoot a gun, but it is illegal to shoot and kill someone.

Rhetorically, how do you think PGP and GPG keys would be allowed if no one was allowed to discuss the proprietary methods that are used to encrypt?
