### Post by 4Orbs on 2011-05-03
Xfce themes work a bit differently than gnome themes. With xfce, you can change the window frame independently of the theme. Xfce gives you a selection of pre-defined window frames to choose from located in the Xfce Settings Manager under "Window Manager". Some of these will change color to match the theme you selected, others don't. So switching themes does not automatically switch the window frame also. Personally, I use the G2 window frame because it always stays the same, dark gray color and matches most of the themes that I prefer.

As for the color mismatch on the Xfce panel, that has been a problem since the Stone Age and we Xfce users have learned to live with it or find a work-around.

### Post by 4Orbs on 2011-05-03
I should also add that you can fix the panel by adding a background image to it. Right-click on the panel and select "Panel Preferences" from the menu to see your options. A simple 2x30 pixel gradient image created in gimp, using colors that compliment the color of the applets can do wonders for your preferred theme.
