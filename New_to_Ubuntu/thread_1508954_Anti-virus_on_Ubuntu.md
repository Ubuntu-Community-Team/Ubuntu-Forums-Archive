### Post by nemilar on 2010-06-13
Linux A/V programs are [primarily designed to detect Windows viruses]("http://techthrob.com/2009/03/02/do-i-need-an-antivirus-program-on-linux/"), for example when you are running a SAMBA server for Windows client machines.

That being said, malicious things can still happen to any computer, Linux-based machines included.  Don't run programs or scripts from untrusted sources, etc....  Just use common sense and you should be OK.

### Post by lisati on 2010-06-13
Opinions vary as to the value of having antivirus on your Ubuntu machine. 

I have clamav installed on my server, mainly as a courtesy to Windows users who might wish to contact me by email, and as part of my ISP's terms and conditions for running an email server at home.

I used to have AVG free on my laptop, but since they changed the Linux-friendly version to not include a GUI, I stopped bothering.

Having your Ubuntu system hurt by malware and other nasty stuff is unlikely but not impossible. A significant portion of the nasty stuff is targeted at machines running Windows - it won't work on your system without help. 

Your defense against malware should, as with Windows, begin with being smart with what you allow on your system, what you open and what you look at.

As far as i know the Linux virus are very rare and old so you don't need anti virus on Ubuntu. I don't know anyone that has been infected.
