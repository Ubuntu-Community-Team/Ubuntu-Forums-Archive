### Post by Andavane on 2008-07-13
Seeing the Ubuntu Calculator in Applications, I was impressed by the display,
i.e. it would only give the product when you pressed "="

> 3 + 2 + 9, etc
This brought back memories of the early nineties in a wonderful product called
"Psion Series 3"

On this calculator the calculation was maintained, and when you pressed "Enter" you would get the product on the next line, whilst still seeing the original calculation. So you would see:

> 3 + 2 + 9
= 14

In fact you could make strings of calculations which would scroll up the pager if necessary, and you print out your workings and calculations as well.

Sadly that gadget was ahead of its time and was driven out by Microsoft etc. Still, I have always hankered after a calculator like that.
It seems so sensible: You need the original calculation as well as the result to check if the original was accurately entered.

I would have thought it was an easy thing to do.

Yet strangely it appears not been incorporated into calculators, or calculating software.

But there must be some, somewhere.

It feels like the sort of thing Ubuntu would do.

Does anyone know of such a calculator?

Regards

John
