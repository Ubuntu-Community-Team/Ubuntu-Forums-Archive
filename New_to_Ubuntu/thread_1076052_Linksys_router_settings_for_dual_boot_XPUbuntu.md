### Post by Hospadar on 2009-02-20
I would suspect nothing would need to change with your router.  As the router is outside of your machine, anything can connect to it regardless of what the client machines are running.  You may be confused in that often routers come with "installation cd's" which, in reality, are basically useless, perhaps at most providing some sort of electronic tutorial.

You *might* however have some issues with getting wireless to work in ubuntu, but probably not, wireless card compatability has gotten waaaay better with linux, and especially ubuntu, in recent years.

I think the thing to do would just be to boot off a live cd into ubuntu and see how it works.  Most likely, you'll find that everything works fine.  Just remember that you might need a new set of skills to get things working in ubuntu.  Be patient, and ask questions.  (Forum posts that start with "AAAAHHHGGGHRHRHH NOTHING WORKS" are typically not a good way to get help)

Good luck!

### Post by jimrz on 2009-02-20
No need to reconfigure, the router doesn't care about your os it justs talks to your network device. It pretty much just pays attention to the MAC address and sometimes the system name. If you like you can use the same sys name for both win and ubuntu. You may want to try out the ubuntu "live cd" and see how well it plays with your hardware. Also, find out the make / model / chipset used by your wifi card and search these forums to see what results others have had with it.
