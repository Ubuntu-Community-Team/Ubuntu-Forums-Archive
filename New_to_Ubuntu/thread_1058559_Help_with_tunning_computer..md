You can't make it a lot faster without upgrading your components.  Your memory isn't 5300MHz, more likely 533, which is pretty slow, budget memory.  Is that an Athlon 64 3800+ X2?  I guess it must be since you have DDR2 memory.

Anyway, you can overclock and may see SOME speed increase, but with only the stock cooling on your CPU, you won't be able to sustain healthy, stable temperatures, and will be forced to lower the speed again.

### Post by Gotaro on 2009-02-03
> **famous3warriors said:**
> Yeah I do have a 3800 +2.  And I do have to agree with you on about up grading my cooling system cause it is just standard.
Don't let that discourage you from seeing how far you can overclock and still maintain healthy temps!  I just recently got into OC'ing, and there is *definitely* a noticeable increase in speed when I hit 3.5GHz (it's 2.5 stock).  But I have to sit around 3GHz until I get a better cooler..  I have an Intel E5200.  I don't know anything about OC'ing 3800+ X2's, though, so you'd have to do some research to find out how much of a performance gain you could see.  A trusted temp monitoring program is [_Core Temp_](http://www.alcpu.com/CoreTemp/), and a good forum with a helpful community would be [_OCForums_](www.ocforums.com).  Core Temp is  Windows program, though..  I honestly don't know a good GUI app for Linux.  "lm-sensors" is the command line app used for checking temps.

Also, I agree that Xubuntu would be a good way to speed things up.  I use Kubuntu, and when I installed the Xfce environment, WOW it was fast!  Not too pretty, IMO..  but fast.
