### Post by bcbc on 2011-01-11
> **jimgd said:**
> Hey there -- thanks for the suggestion, didn't get it to go, but am pursuing other forum suggestions. How do you boot from the grub prompt?

You're already at the grub prompt so your root is (loop0). You can just enter the following. Should boot.
```
linux /vmlinuz root=/dev/sda1 loop=/ubuntu/disks/root.disk ro quiet splash
initrd /initrd.img
boot
```

There is this (but it's not always indicative of a problem)
> Mounting failed:
mount: wrong fs type, bad option, bad superblock on /dev/loop1,
missing codepage or helper program, or other error
In some cases useful info is found in syslog - try

### Post by j0hnnyv on 2011-01-11
I had the exact same problem doing the same thing you did. Its an easy fix. download the .iso and burn and create an ubuntu live cd. Put it in and reboot and it will load ubuntu. Choose the top option, not to install but to preview or however its worded.

Bring up Terminal..

Type this:

sudo apt-get install lilo
(when prompted press 'Y')

Then:

sudo lilo -M /dev/sda mbr


Once that is done, from top right click power button and restart...
pop the CD out and you should have the normal boot menu.  Choose ubuntu and you are all set :)

### Post by jimgd on 2011-01-16
Belatedly -- many thanks for the suggestions here. The solution was indeed under problem 2, in this very thorough thread, by Rubi1200
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1639198](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1639198)

For those who are also new to this, there's an important piece of information that you need to solve the problem, and once you have it, the solution unfolds very nicely. There's a slight trick to getting  that information, which Rubi1200 also explained and I will repeat below.

The information you need is from a boot script, which gives you a txt file, and that tells you where your bootup info is located. It is linked in the thread cited above, and I will relink it [here]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/bootinfoscript/"). 

The trick, however, is that after you download the script, you should move it to your desktop folder. 

Then, at the terminal, enter this command:
sudo bash ~/Desktop/boot_info_script*.sh
That will give you a txt file of information.
