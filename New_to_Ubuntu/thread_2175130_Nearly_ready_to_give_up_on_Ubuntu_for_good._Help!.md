### Post by squakie on 2013-09-17
+1 on what Mark Phelps said.  I also am a strong believer in using what works for you.  As they mentioned, normally this is not an Ubuntu problem - Ubuntu itself is not laden with problems as you seem to indicate.  PC makers do what Microsoft wants them to do in order to be able to license and support Windows and other Microsoft products.  In turn, the manufacturers create drivers for their hardware for Windows.  In a lot of cases, these drivers are in turn included in Windows or found automatically at new hardware detection.  The others are normally included on a CD or DVD from the manufacturer.  The biggest problem is that these manufacturers do not create drivers for their hardware for Linux, and sometimes the hardware technicalities are not open for others, so it's difficult at best to try to create a linux driver for them otherwise.  The vast majority of people who have "problems" with ubuntu or other linux variants are problems caused by this lack of linux support by the manufacturer.  These people assume that since Windows "did it automatically" (read: the manufacturers had to create Windows drivers and make them available, often invisible to the end user) that linux should as well and if it doesn't then there's something wrong with the OS itself.  Simply not the case.

Do post back your hardware specifics, anything about what you have tried (links?), etc..  It might also be helpful to know if what you are doing requires some special software in Windows or not.  I used to do some part-time work from home and it actually required some specific Windows software.

Please don't give up yet - let us have another crack at it.

### Post by squakie on 2013-09-19
You'd be surprised at just how manu people here are NOT out to destroy Microsoft.  But at the same time, they are responsible for most of this mess.  They force manufacturers to certain standards in order to license and support their products.  In and amongst that there are also some non-compete clauses.  Whether any of those apply to coding drivers for Linux who knows.  I find it intersting that some of the bigger players in the marketplace are the ones who do provide support for Linux - companies with more sales clout to deter manufacturer bullying.

Linux, and therefore Ubuntu, doesn't really make claims that "everything just works".  If you look at Microsoft products they don't state that either.  The difference is that Microsoft says if you want our approval (read:  ever noticed the message about unsigned drivers, etc.?) you must provide Windows drivers.  Linux is not a company - it is a community, and a community effort.  Such constraints are impossible to attempt and actually not welcome with Linux.  It's an OS - an OPEN OS.  You want to screw with it, make it something else, even break it to your hearts content, no one will stop you.

Just because manufacturers provide drivers to be compatible with Windows (and Microsofts ideals) doesn't mean they do the same for Linux.  That wireless that magically works in Windows?  It HAS a driver, and it's a driver provided by the manufacturer, NOT Microsoft.  All submitted drivers try to be included in Windows, and the add to hardware funtion (or update driver function) allow going to the net for it to search for drivers - again provided by the manufacturers.  People don't realize this because they either bought their PC in a box - where the manufacturer installed Windows AND installed the needed drivers, or they perhaps built their own and had to gather drivers.  Take a look at how many PC's come with images (be they actual disk or on a recovery partition on a hard disk) for all the drivers for the motherboard (again, manufacturer) and other hardware (again manufacturer).  They don't do the same thing for Linux.  Linux is an OS, and just like Windows, it needs to know how to talk to hardware.  If the manufacturers of that hardware do not provide Linux drivers, it obviously is not Linux's fault.  All this magic everyone *thinks* Windows does it doesn't - it's up to the manufacturer to provide that means in the form of a driver.

But of course I have stated this before in this thread, and believe me when I say the "Windows vs Linux" p****** contest has been going on for many years.  Most of here don't care - use what works for you.  Yes, there are Linux bigots, but there are a lot more Windows bigots.
