### Post by GregA on 2010-05-19
In general, Volundro, if your system was generally running slow before the login issues, it could signify a hardware component failure (drive errors, bad ram, controler issues, etc.). , , or that the Karmic install did not install cleanly in the first place.  Without more info about your system, these are general possibilities (a semi-educated guess).

But if you have important files on your system, the first thing I would do would be to get one of the better live linux distros downloaded (on your windows system) and burned to CD.  The best one is Knoppix, or one of the Slackware derivitives such as Slax, or Vector.   Then, you can use the default file manager application within Knoppix to access your hard drive and copy the files to a USB stick.

After that, you may want to try an install of Lucid Lynx, to see if it isn't friendlier to your hardware (unless of course, you have hardware issues as I mentioned above) . . . .
