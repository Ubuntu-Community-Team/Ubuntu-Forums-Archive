### Post by balaknair on 2010-03-20
@Carlee and quinnten83: From the page linked to, it seems to me the product you refer to isn't freeware, it's paid software, targeted at businesses that run Linux workstations.
I doubt if Kaspersky has any free antivirus suites on offer, for Linux or otherwise.


@getyourkarthick:
If you want to install an antivirus app just to scan your NTFS drives on your home computers, I'd advise trying one of the following

* BitDefender for Unices(free for personal use, free registration required)

* ClamAV(Possibly the safest and easiest option, available via the Ubuntu software centre, updates automatically since it's from the repos, Open source, free software)

If you want to try BitDefender you can follow this guide here
[http://tombuntu.com/index.php/2009/07/07/download-and-install-bitdefender-antivirus-on-ubuntu-with-1-year-free-license/](http://tombuntu.com/index.php/2009/07/07/download-and-install-bitdefender-antivirus-on-ubuntu-with-1-year-free-license/)

Of course you also have other options like AVG, Avira and F-prot, but the two mentioned above are in my opinion the most reliable and ClamAV is the easiest to install and configure in Ubuntu.
