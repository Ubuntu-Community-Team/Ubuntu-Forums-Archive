### Post by deadflowr on 2015-03-08
Thunderbird has to be running to be able to get notifications.
Running = open.
You can minimize it, and keep it running, but not close it.

### Post by QIII on 2015-03-08
In several DEs you can close the window rather than using "Quit" from the menu.  That closes the window, rather than just minimizing it.  That will leave it running in the background and avoid having anything extra on your panel.

When you do that, depending on the DE, an icon will appear in your notification area.  You'll get a notification when mail is available.  But you'll still have to restart the application to view it.
