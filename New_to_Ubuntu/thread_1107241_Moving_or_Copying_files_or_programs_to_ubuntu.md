### Post by mlentink on 2009-03-26
Why do you want to do this? No criticism, just curiosity. 

From out of ubuntu, this is fairly easy. If it is not already installed, install ntfs-3g (Add/Remove Programs, search for ntfs and you'll find it), and you'll be able to copy the files over. But without installing special support for reading/writing ext2/3 filesystems, you won't be able to read them in windows.
