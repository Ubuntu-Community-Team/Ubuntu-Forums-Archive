### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-12-05
Open the Dash in the Application lens. Super key + A. Super key = key with flying windows logo. Click on Installed See xx more results and scroll down until you see the Libreoffice icons.

There will be an icon for each component of Libreoffice installed + an extra icon called simply 'Libreoffice.' Click on that and you will get a page where you can select to open a new document for any of the components. If you use this method a lot, then lock it to the Launcher.

Regards.

### Post by vanadium on 2012-12-05
The icons that are installed on a stock Ubuntu 12.10 installation are "LibreOffice Writer", "LibreOffice Calc", "LibreOffice Impress" and "LibreOffice Draw".

Starting these gives you a blank new document.

You can launch the easily pressing the super key (the key with the Windows logo) and typing "writer" or "calc" etc.

You can locate them among the other applications: click the Ubuntu button. This shows the Dash. In the bottom line of the Dash, click the "Applications" icon (shorter: open Applications by pressing Super+a, as grahammechanical indicated).

Click, in the top right corner of the dash,  on "filter results". Now click the "Office category". This shows all office applications installed.
