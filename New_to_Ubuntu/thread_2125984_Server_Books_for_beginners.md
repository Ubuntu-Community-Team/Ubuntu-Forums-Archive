### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-03-15
Everyone has a different learning style.  I personally like the books from No Starch Press:  [http://nostarch.com](http://nostarch.com).  Once you figure out your learning style, then finding material becomes easier.

Do you learn better from:

1.  Reading
2.  Viewing video
3.  Listening
4.  Classroom/lecture
5.  Hands-on/breaking things

Or you could do a combination:  Pick up a book (read the reviews first!), watch some youtube videos on the subject, listen to a few podcasts, sign up for a class at your nearest linux conference, go to a Linux User's Group (lug) with an old laptop and resurrect it with linux and load the services that you want to learn.

For just plain documentation, start reading:  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NewDocs](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NewDocs)

Paper/Published books get of date quickly, so don't expect each lesson or tutorial to be correct.  Perhaps 90%.  But that is part of learning.  Figuring out what is wrong and then fixing it.  Google is your friend for that.

After 10 years, you will get the hang of it.

Really.
