### Post by chanyunkwan on 2009-04-02
It's a desktop pc or a notebook?

**_solution 1:_** if it's desktop PC, you can open the PC case and try to remove the battery for BIOS for a few seconds then put it back. The BIOS will load it's default setting again. everything should be fine again. 
(I don't really think what you've done could change the BIOS default setting.)

you can also use your xubuntu live cd to boot up your PC. Then choose boot from your hard disk. Then you can get in your vista system again.

If the first solution doesn't work, you can try to reflash your BIOS on vista.

**_solution 2:_** Go to the motherboard official website and download the BIOS update and flash a new version BIOS or the same version as your are using.

Or go to the company website that you bought your PC and download the BIOS update.

[COLOR="Red"]**( Try to call the company for technical support. If you are not very good at it.)**[/COLOR]
Reflashing BIOS is not absolutely safe. Be careful for your operation. Usually everything will be just fine. Take a deep breath and smile. 

 I hope this helps.
