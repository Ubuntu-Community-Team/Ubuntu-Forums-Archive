### Post by tcoffeep on 2009-09-15
Maybe filing a bug report might be useful. It could be a driver problem, but I've never used a netbook, so I'm not much help to you there. I've had the 3000bps, and I hated it. I just changed the mirror I was downloading from, and that made a -huge- difference.
And, if prior to the updates, you were fine... why fix what wasn't broke? Just stick to the default install, and see if that helps. Go a week like that, and it should be clear. Then check what updates are available, and mark them down, or save them to a text file. And try getting them one-by-one, instead of everything all at once. Scratch off or delete which ones update without giving you problems after a reboot, and then when you hit one that messes up you OS, you'll get an idea of what might be fsck'ing up.
