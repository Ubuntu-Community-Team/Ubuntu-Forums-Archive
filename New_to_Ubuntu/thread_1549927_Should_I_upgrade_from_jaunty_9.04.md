### Post by snowpine on 2010-08-10
You will have to sooner or later, as support for 9.04 ends in October.

I recommend a fresh reinstall rather than an upgrade in this instance.

Also keep in mind it is normal for eee PCs to be a little slow; it is not powerful hardware by any means. ;) Are there particular activities (boot time, disk access, videos, web surfing, etc.) where you notice the slowdown? "My computer is slow" can mean a lot of different things...

(edit) I just remembered something about the older eee PCs. Some models have 2 separate SSD drives, a small fast one and a slow big one for extra storage. If this is the case with yours, you **definitely** want to make sure the OS is installed to the fast drive. ;)

An alternative would be to use [Unetbootin]("http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/") (I don't know about Jaunty, but it is available in the Lucid repos).

One caveat...I've had a few bad experiences using the installations from the default menu.  I think it has to do with corrupted downloads.  All you need to do is download the .iso separately (I use the official torrent from [Ubuntu Website]("http://releases.ubuntu.com/lucid/")...scroll down the list and find the torrent for your machine), check the md5 checksum after download (the reason I use a torrent...error checking is inherent and the downloads are rarely bad), then use the option of installing from the iso you downloaded.  This has worked flawlessly for me every time.
