### Post by Old Jimma on 2013-02-10
Solution!

:P

This issue has been discussed previouly on the forums. See: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2054674]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2054674")

To summarize the findings of that discussion...

... the phenomena of the entire workspace 'not fitting' on the display of a large HTDV monitor is **[COLOR="DarkGreen"]not an Ubuntu problem[/COLOR]**. 

**[COLOR="Red"]Therefore, do not play with "settings" on Ubuntu to try to fix it![/COLOR]**

Moreover, to "fix" this problem users need to adjust the "picture" settings of their HDTV.

For example, for my Samsung 42" HDTV the following sequence solved the problem
[LIST]
[*]press "menu" on your TV clicker,
[*]scroll down until "picture" is highlighted... chose enter (or ok);
[*]press the up/down selector on your clicker until you find "just scan" and press enter (or ok).
[*]press on the "exit" button on your clicker. You are done!
[/LIST]

This is described on page 28 of my Samsung HDTV manual under "changing screen size."

For those of you who don't have a Samsung HTDV (or, possibly a  different model Samsung HDTV), refer to your owners manual to learn how to "change the screen size" of your HDTV to allow your TV to accomodate the entire Unity desktop.

I hope this helps somebody!
