Here are my answers to your questions:

1. Only you can decide for yourself but in light of my experience with Vista on the same Dell XPS m1330, it is worth considering along with a downgrade to XP Pro

2. I am a hardware guy that does better than average with software who has learned what I know from building and troubleshooting machines for 15 years.  Ubuntu has stretched my skills but it is learnable.  This site is a great resources!

3.  Ubuntu will work on the M1330... I installed it with WUBI and it worked great as installed, **except for the wireless.**  At least the one I have dealt with has a NEWER wireless chipset that does not have much support.  I did not fight it because it is my wife's machine and she does not learn new OS's well.  

My suggestions to you: 
Download the image for a live CD of Hardy Heron 8.04 and boot off the CD and try the live version.  If you want to play on the internet, plug into the internet with an ethernet cable.

If you like it, consider WUBI and install it in Vista.  If you get tired or frustrated, just uninstall like any other Windows program.  There will be a performance hit but you should not notice much difference on that machine.  

If you have any other questions related to my experience, feel free to ask.

### Post by kansasnoob on 2008-06-25
I don't know about your specific laptop compatibilty with Ubuntu but I always like to begin just by trying the Live CD, just "make no changes".

Then, as a matter of personal preference, if I have a working operating system (in your case Vista) I like to do a dual boot. 10GB is plenty for 8.04 and if you love Ubuntu you can make future changes to partitions quite easily (just always plan ahead), or if Ubuntu just doesn't do it for you it can be deleted and you can give the space back to Vista (again, plan ahead, you will need to restore Vista's boot loader).

I've had really good luck with this dual boot guide:

[http://apcmag.com/how_to_dualboot_vista_with_linux_vista_installed_first.htm](http://apcmag.com/how_to_dualboot_vista_with_linux_vista_installed_first.htm)

Just be sure to ask lots of questions before you start. Anything you don't understand is better resolved before it becomes a problem.
