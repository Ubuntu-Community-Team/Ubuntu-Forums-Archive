### Post by pararoly on 2010-03-13
Hello Gallifrey

I have been using Ubuntu for about 18 months now. I too have wanted to look-under-the-hood a little more. 

I really like [this]("http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/0470082933/ref=oss_product") book because it's not some huge ("Bible") volume which covers absolutely everything. But is accessible, compact and relevant. A _great_ starting place. Very cheap too.  

I have also purchased a [book]("http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/1430219971/ref=oss_product") about shell scripting, which admittedly I have not read in depth yet. It does look quite comprehensive and well written.

In addition, [this]("http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/0596154488/ref=oss_product") book seems to have quite a reputation in the linux fraternity. As well as loads of detail about linux commands (most of which you you could get form man pages) it also includes chapters on (from memory!), regular expressions, shell scripting, virtualisation and a heap of other stuff. It is no doubt a great reference book.


Online Resources I have personally found very useful:-
See the sticky threads at the top of this "absolute beginers" section of the forum.
[http://ftacademy.org/materials/fsm/2](http://ftacademy.org/materials/fsm/2)  -  Free 500 page, book - great!
[http://www.tldp.org/](http://www.tldp.org/)  [Masses of great stuff here - some great guides]
[http://linuxcommand.org/index.php](http://linuxcommand.org/index.php) - I found this site a _really_ good introduction to the shell. 

I hope all that helps! and does not overwhelm! haha! Should keep you going for a while.
Have fun!
Best Wishes
Roly
