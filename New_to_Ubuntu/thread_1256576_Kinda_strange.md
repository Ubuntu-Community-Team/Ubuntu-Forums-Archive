### Post by Rhubarb on 2009-09-02
You can use a bit torrent client application to automatically find the errors in the iso image, and download any parts of it that's needed to fix up the iso image.

The torrents for Ubuntu can be found here:
[http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/downloadmirrors#bt](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/downloadmirrors#bt)

Load that torrent into your preffered bit torrent client application, and point the download location to where your (corrupted) iso image is.

Bit Torrent has very good corruption prevention measures, and should fix up your iso image quickly.
