### Post by Sydius on 2009-01-15
My girlfriend just installed 8.10.  Most of the time, when she boots into Ubuntu, nothing weird happens and it just works.  But twice now, weird things have happened:

Once, whilst booting, the screen went nuts (hard to describe, but basically a kind of white gradient/trash) and it kept beeping until I shut it off.  Turned it back on and it booted normally.

Another time, whilst booting, it did quite a bit of beeping (the screen stayed black) and then booted into Ubuntu as if nothing was wrong.

It's a brand new laptop, and this doesn't happen predictably, and has only happened while booting into Ubuntu (but only twice).  I'm worried it is a hardware problem; any advice on where we could start tracking down the issue?

### Post by abn91c on 2009-01-15
You may have to check the manufacturer website for that problem, beeps during start up usually means bad hardware like RAM or video card. Take notice of how many beeps and how long they last, then google for POST beep codes for you particular laptop.

### Post by SteFrankie on 2009-01-18
This sounds very similar to a problem I've been having. Are you running 32bit or 64bit Ubuntu?

If you're using the 64bit version it could be usplash causing problems, you can disable it temporarily to check by following the instructions here: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DebuggingUsplash#Debugging%20procedure](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DebuggingUsplash#Debugging%20procedure)
