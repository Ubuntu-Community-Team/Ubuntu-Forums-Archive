### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-08-02
Firstly, phone manufacturers have to vet updates. Unless they've tested Android 2.3 and pushed it out as a phone update, you won't have the option to upgrade.

Secondly, if there is an update available for your phone, your phone is capable of downloading and installing it on its own. Go to Settings and About Phone, and then you can check for updates. If it says that your phone software is up-to-date, then you can't update your phone any further (yet, at least - your phone manufacturer might push out an update later).

If you really really want Android 2.3, your only other option would be to root the phone and install a custom firmware that is based on Android 2.3. The procedure differs from phone to phone and from firmware to firmware. A good starting place is Cyanogenmod - it's a very popular custom firmware for many Android phones and I really like it. Maybe your phone is supported? Note that Cyanogenmod will change the user interface and installed programs too (it will probably wipe your phone's data too, make sure EVERYTHING is backed up); it's not just like going from Ubuntu 10.04 to 10.10, it's more like going from Ubuntu to Kubuntu.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-08-02
Also ... understand that once you "root" your phone, you've probably voided any warranty and will no longer get tech support through your phone service provider.  This DOES depend on the terms and conditions of your service agreement -- so your ISP actually MIGHT continue to provide support.

Also, Verizon, among others, is working hard to find ways to disable "rooted" phones, so don't be surprised if one or more features don't work anymore.
