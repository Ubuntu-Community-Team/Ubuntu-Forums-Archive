Hi Russ

Just found out something you can do with Eudora you can't do with either Evolution or Thunderbird on a Linux machine.

On the Doze, you CAN force Eudora to run from a specific folder.
What this means is you can have one Eudora renamed Eudora A (name of service) and another one named Eudora B (name of a different service) and all the inbound e-mail from lets say the business POP3 server is in one folder and all your personal e-mails to a different e-mail address at a different POP3 server remains in that Eudora Folder.

Since most of the files used in Linux are shared files and/or must be stored in certain directories.  You cannot run two instances of the same program on the machine.  Or else I have just not learned how to do that yet.
