### Post by MoebusNet on 2013-01-18
I didn't see where you mentioned what OS version your HTC phone is running; mine is early Ice Cream Sandwich. Later ICS and Jellybean changed file formats, causing issues. If Ubuntu doesn't recognize your phone by just plugging-in via USB, perhaps this can help:

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/12/how-to-connect-your-android-ice-cream-sandwich-phone-to-ubuntu-for-file-access](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/12/how-to-connect-your-android-ice-cream-sandwich-phone-to-ubuntu-for-file-access)

Also, remember that *on your phone* you must select 'Mount as a USB drive' or similar instead of 'Charge only' when plugged into a PC. This will be under 'Settings'.
