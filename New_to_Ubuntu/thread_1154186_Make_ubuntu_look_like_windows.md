### Post by lavinog on 2009-05-09
I have been transitioning family members from window to ubuntu.
The main thing you have to ask is what do they use the computer for?
People that use the computer for basic needs like email, and web browsing have a much easier time transitioning.

People that use MS office frequently may not like the compatibility issues with openoffice, but you wont know unless you try. openoffice has the benefit of working on both windows and linux.

Gamers are out of the question

I usually find out what they will use most and make a launcher on the gnome panel.
This way they don't even have to do anything with menus...etc
over time they will explore different aspects of it.
My wife has an easier time with ubuntu than windows.
