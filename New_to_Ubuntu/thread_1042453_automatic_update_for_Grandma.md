### Post by Hendrixski on 2009-01-17
Cool,

yeah, I set up my family on Ubuntu too.  I used to go through hell when my mom would call up and complain that nothing worked because some virus wiped out everything... and she couldn't figure out anti-virus for the life of her...  Let's just say those calls have stopped now, and she loves Ubuntu.  it's easy for her :-)

I set the updates to only come up every two weeks.  And it's OK.  She seems to install them just fine.

I think forced auto-updates are bad because sometimes they require reboots for new kernel versions, or because they may slow down something... or you may turn off your computer while they're happening without knowing it and zap there goes your install.   So.  I wouldn't sweat it.  Maybe somebody can answer that part better. I dunno.

### Post by Elfy on 2009-01-17
You can set the update manager to only update and install security updates from software sources in the sys admin menu, you can alos tell it how often to check -= daily, 2 days, weekly, 2 weeks.

It's what I would do.

Then do other updates when you're available.

You don't have to restart with a kernel update - just next time is fine.
