### Post by bobpur on 2008-10-07
Soundblaster Audigy cards are usually pretty linux friendly. 

Remove the card and reseat it. Sometimes a little corrosion will screw things up a tad. While you have the card out fire up your machine and then power down. Then re-seat the card and let Ubuntu re-install it. It might just be a driver thing.

Someone, probably, has a slick little script to fix things but that wouldn't be me.

Good luck.

### Post by diablo75 on 2008-10-07
I've determined that the problem was actually [my stereo receiver]("http://images.google.com/images?ie=UTF-8&oe=UTF-8&sourceid=navclient&gfns=1&q=panasonic%20sa-xr50&um=1&sa=N&tab=wi").

All I had to do was shut it off and then turn it back on a few seconds later.  This of course was after I removed the card and switched over to the on-board chipset, and noticed that it too was "making" the bad audio.

Glad nothing actually broke.  O:)
