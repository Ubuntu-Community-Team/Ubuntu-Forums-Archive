### Post by HotShotDJ on 2010-05-16
There were some models of laptops that had major problems after a suspend-resume cycle during RC.  Developers decided to disable suspend for those models (mostly DELL if I recall correctly) until a fix could be released. The last I knew, the fixes are available in the ubuntu-proposed repositories.  Just enable them in Synaptic, refresh, and update.  That SHOULD fix your problem if it is the same bug as I'm thinking of.

### Post by poloshiao on 2010-05-30
**[[FONT=DejaVu Serif, serif]Hibernation may be unavailable with automatic partitioning[/FONT]]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LucidLynx/ReleaseNotes#Hibernation%20may%20be%20unavailable%20with%20automatic%20partitioning")**

 [FONT=DejaVu Serif, serif]The default partitioning recipe in the installer will in some cases allocate a swap partition that is smaller than the physical memory in the system. This will prevent the use of hibernation (suspend-to-disk) because the system image will not fit in the swap partition. If you intend to use hibernation with your system, you should ensure that the swap partition's size is at least as large as the system's physical RAM. ([345126]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/345126")) [/FONT]
