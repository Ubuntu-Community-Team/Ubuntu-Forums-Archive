### Post by Cypher on 2009-02-18
I'm not sure who you've been talking to in regards to VPN and Torrents, but your understanding of it is incorrect.

VPN's are primarily used to connect your home machine to a remote network (usually your work network) in a secure manner such that you can access your files without worrying about anyone snooping.

VPN's are strongly suggested when people use the Internet on public WiFi's because the connection is encrypted, you gain a level of protection.

Now with torrents, whoever you spoke to might have suggested something about security in relation to torrents and that's not entirely accurate or useful. Once on a VPN, you are essentially using your company's Internet resources and as such as being tracked/monitored and logged.

There is VPN software available for Linux, but as stated you actually need a network to join and that isn't free.
