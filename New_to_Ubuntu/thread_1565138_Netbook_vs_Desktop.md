### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2010-08-31
> **Jolicoeur said:**
> Does anybody have experience with both Netbook and the Desktop versions of Ubuntu?  Would Netbook work better on Laptop?  Does it have much less functionality?

IMO the only difference between the standard installations of Ubuntu and UNR is the interface.

The netbook edition is not otherwise optimized for laptops.

Install the interface (gnome, kde, xfce, UNR, other) of your choice.

See:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UNR](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UNR)

> Ubuntu Netbook Remix (UNR) is a 'remix' of the standard Ubuntu Desktop  release, enabling you to work better on devices with small screens, such  as netbooks and sub-notebooks.

### Post by johnny2m on 2010-09-02
I installed UNR on my HP Mini 210 netbook recently, but having used it a couple of times I now always boot into the standard Gnome desktop environment (as others have said, you can choose this at login).  The UNR desktop is designed to look better on small screens (different menu layout and everything opens in fullscreen), but I'm more familiar with the layout of Gnome and I have all my most used programs on my desktop, and the icons are just as big and clear as under UNR.

There's no performance advantage to using UNR that I'm aware of.

### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2010-09-02
I have experience with UNR and the desktop versions of Ubuntu.  I had three computers loaded with different versions, a desktop, a laptop, and a netbook.  The netbook was originally loaded with UNR and I have since loaded Lucid on it (desktop version).  I found that I preferred the desktop version.  It is really personal preference.  While UNR was designed (I guess) for the netbook with the smaller screens, the desktop version does work fine on it and I have found no functionality difference.
