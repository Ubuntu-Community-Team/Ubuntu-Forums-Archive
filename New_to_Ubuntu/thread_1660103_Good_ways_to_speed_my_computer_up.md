### Post by ram2500 on 2011-01-04
BleachBit is similar to Windows' CCleaner. It will clean up cookies, temporary files, apt (Ubuntu) temporary program files, cache, and more. Also, make sure you're not trying to fit a square peg into a round hole :p (add more memory if you are below 512MB). The good thing about Ubuntu is there is *usually* an apparent reason why it slows down, if it ever does. Windows requires a little thinking through to get to the problem.

### Post by scheuri on 2011-01-05
You definitively are speaking very very vague and general here. Any details (as in specs of the machine and what is going slow and what is slow to you) will help to determine what kind of issue you have at hand.

In general terms however, more RAM is always good. If you are using 32bit Ubuntu then I really suggest you do not get more than 4 GB (even that is too much).
A good average in my opinion to work with Ubuntu and usual things (such as surfing, word processing, playing music and videos, etc.) is at least 1 GB RAM up to 2 GB RAM.

When it comes to CPU a single core should already be fine (unless its like 10 years old). Having a dual core is always a win.

Do not forget the other peripherals such as harddisk! A slow hardisk (5400rpm) with less cache can not shovel that much data as a faster one. However, hard disks usually are the bottleneck all the time (unless you are using SSD, in which case the bottleneck is much less relevant).

But as I said...without details, we can not really point out the proper and useful advice for your specific issue.

cheers
