### Post by coffeecat on 2011-01-20
I'm not quite clear whether you mean a handheld mp3 digital voice recorder or a piece of software to use in Ubuntu. For the latter I agree with JohnHeikkila - Audacity will do what you want. But if you mean you need a cheap and effective MP3 voice recorder, then what is your definition of cheap? :) Staples [have a couple]("http://www.staples.co.uk/searchEngine.aspx")* but they're not exactly cheap. You'd need to be sure the machine has a USB interface so that you can plug it into Ubuntu and even then you can't be sure it will be 100% compatible with Ubuntu.

My [Philips Voice Tracer LFH0662]("http://www.amazon.co.uk/Philips-LFH0662-00-Recorder-Storage/dp/B002GWW9EK") works just fine with Ubuntu but it's not cheap either.

* **EDIT**: The Staples site obviously doesn't remember search strings. You'll have to search for 'voice recorders mp3' if you want to see what I was trying to link.
