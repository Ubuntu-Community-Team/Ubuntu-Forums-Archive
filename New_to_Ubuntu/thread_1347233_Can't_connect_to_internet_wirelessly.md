### Post by efflandt on 2009-12-06
How far are you from your wireless router (and how well does/did it work in Windows from same location)?  Sometimes moving the wireless router or your computer a few inches one way or the other can make a big difference.

My home is an old wooden home with steam heat, so I have no metal heating ducts or anything to block signals.  I get a decent signal on 2nd floor from DSL wireless/modem/router in basement.  My Linksys WUSB54GSC gets a stronger signal than wireless built into my Toshiba laptop (which usually works fine), which is stronger than a Dell laptop (which sometimes gets no signal).

At my boss' newer home, he cannot even get a signal in his kitchen right over his wireless/modem/router in basement office.  So we used Netgear powerline wireless range extender in bridge mode to get wireless on his main floor.  Although, they and other companies also have powerline ethernet where you simply plug a module into the wall at each end with 1 or 4 ports.
