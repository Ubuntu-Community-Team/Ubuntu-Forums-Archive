### Post by ajgreeny on 2008-06-15
Also have a look at digikam.  It's a kde program but runs without hitch on gnome as well, and I prefer it to f-spot.  Digikam with all the kipi plugins will do everything I want and more, and unlike f-spot it doesn't automatically copy files imported into another folder, which I nearly always forgot to turn off in f-spot, meaning I had two copies of all photos.  I accept that this may be down to my way of importing simply by copying from a card reader to the appropriate subfolder of my Pictures folder as I don't like f-spot's preferred way of putting everything in the one folder.  Perhaps I'm odd in this, but I think it is much easier to have appropriately named subfolders for my photos.  It means I can find everything so much easier in nautilus, for example, when I need to.

Anyway, enough;  have a look at digikam and see what you think.
