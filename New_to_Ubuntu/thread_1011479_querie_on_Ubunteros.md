### Post by Shhnap on 2008-12-14
Ok, so you want to become a core developer. Well you can, but imo it is likely to take you a few years. However, if you are really dedicated then I can recommend a path for you to take.

1) Learn C, fluently. (Any as many other languages as you can; though this is a good start) Without this knowledge you will never become a core developer. Lucky for you there are tutorials all over the internet so you can increase your skills easily.
2) Maybe in three or so months, when you have a good amount of C experience, you can try your hand at becoming a MOTU. Follow the tutorials and help fix bugs and make packages so that Ubuntu is a better place. Stick with it for atleast a year. At the end of this year you may have grown alot.
3) By this stage it should be obvious to you how much more you need to know and do before you become a core developer.

Please keep in mind that we would all love you to become a core developer; we want nothing more than good dedicated people working on the operating system we love. However, we are trying to point out that it will be alot of effort on your part. Don't let it stop you though. Plow on ahead anyway. I wish you luck.
