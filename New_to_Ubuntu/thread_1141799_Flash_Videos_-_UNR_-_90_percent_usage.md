### Post by MightySeb on 2009-04-28
Adobe's flash player seems to use a lot resources under Linux.

Since most online videos uses flash (like videos on Youtube), that would explain why the processor goes to 90% while playing an online video. On my laptop, the processor goes to 50% for a single Youtube video.

I know there are other flash player equivalent for Linux, but I didn't try any of these.

You can find more information about them here :
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats/Flash#Free%20Software%20alternatives%20to%20Adobe%27s%20Flash%20Player](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats/Flash#Free%20Software%20alternatives%20to%20Adobe%27s%20Flash%20Player)

And just for testing, have you tried to play a movie from your computer's hard drive (like an .AVI file) ?
