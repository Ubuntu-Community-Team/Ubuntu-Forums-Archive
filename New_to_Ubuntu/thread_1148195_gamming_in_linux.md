### Post by scheuri on 2009-05-04
Hi there...

The fact that you have 3D or not is not bound to the gnome desktop. It is bound to the graphics driver (and therefore bound to xorg and your kernel, at least to some extend).

If the driver delivered by your distribution does offer you 3D for that chip (what I actually doubt, because that seems to be a rather old chip) then you might just take that one...

There is not "right" or "wrong" here...at least not that I can see.

By the way: GTA Vice City does not work out of the box with Linux, you need WINE. That again uses a bit more ressources and your graphics driver needs to "understand" OpenGL.

Edit:
Ah, the intel x3100...maybe you are lucky, maybe not. Linux is not a gaming platform per se and intel chips are (not yet) the best choice for gaming. But GTA VC is a bit older, maybe yo are lucky.

cheers
scheuri
