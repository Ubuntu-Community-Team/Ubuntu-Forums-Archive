### Post by Truckie-D on 2009-01-20
It's probably something simple, so please don't yell at me - I'm new at Linux.

Here's the scoop:
I have a motherboard with built-in video, and an S3Virge video card as well.
I disabled the on-board card in the bios. I installed 8.10 as a dual-boot with xp. It came up fine, with the only problem being that my video card isn't being recognized.

The video card in question is a PowerColor C325DX4, which uses the S3Virge/DX chipset (86C375).

The card does function, but I'm unable to set the resolution higher than 800x600.  The "detect displays" button on "monitor resolution settings" doesn't seem to do anything at all.

Digging around the various forums, I tried using sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg.  This takes me through options for setting the keyboard, but doesn't ask anything about monitors or video cards.

Further investigation revealed that Ubuntu 8.10 relies on auto-detect for video settings.

I have a video driver that will probably work, but what I specifically need to know is how to override the automatic settings, and convince x11 to use the correct driver.

Thanks for your help.

Truckie-D

### Post by wolfen69 on 2009-01-20
try ```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure -*phigh* xserver-xorg
```  or
```
sudo xrandr -s 1600x1200
``` of course replacing 1600x1200 with what you want. or ```
gksudo displayconfig-gtk
``` reboot or restart x after trying each.
