### Post by Tatty on 2008-09-25
I decided to learn c++ on a whim not long ago and I have been using mono-develop to get me started, and this may be what you are looking for.

It was good at the time as I had not done any programming for years, and it enabled me to get straight into all the "fun stuff" without worrying too much about the foundations of project organisation, make files, command line compiling, etc.  Its all just click and go.

But now I am starting to feel a little boxed-in to mono-develop's ways so I am starting to look at trying to set myself up with simpler development tools.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=39](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=39)  There is a forum on here for programming talk.  Try browsing through the stickies on there.
