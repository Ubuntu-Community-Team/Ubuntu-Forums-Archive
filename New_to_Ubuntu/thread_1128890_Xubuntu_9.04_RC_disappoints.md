### Post by mesmith on 2009-04-18
My move to 9.04 RC was disappointing.  Fortunately, I had backed my xubuntu partition to a second hard drive.  This meant I was only 15 minutes from a restore to 8.10.  I ended up doing just that.

Let me say that I do understand an RC is just that, an RC.  I expected to find the odd problem.  What I didn't expect was to be disturbed by the overall look and feel of the release.  Niggles first:

My Gnumeric icon disappeared.  None of the others, just that one.  I looked around but couldn't find one.  I took a look at the new gnome-brave set, but found it incomplete (ending before the gnumeric icon would have appeared).  Odd, but no big deal.

My monitor settings were blown up to 1600X1400 (or something similar) so I reset to the setting I like.  That worked, but won't stick.  Every boot goes back to max screen settings.

Which brings up another point - the Settings Manager.  I liked it in 8.10.  Not totally complete, but very helpful (I'm a noob).  In 9.04 RC this exists but looks incomplete, worse - it seems that every item in the manager screen is replicated as a separate menu item.  Why?  I don't like long menus.

Abiword is fixed as regards the help system.  That's nice.  Why can't I have this version now for 8.10 without dealing with source code, etc.?  Makes no sense.  Same with Brasero.

Bottom line:  I love xubuntu.  My 8.10 installation has always been lean and mean with only minor tweaking.  9.04 just seems to me kind of clumsy and not as clean and well thought out.

This is personal stuff and in no way should be interpreted as a 9.04 failure.  I'm just disappointed.  Will try again with the final release.

Comments from 9.04 RC users?
