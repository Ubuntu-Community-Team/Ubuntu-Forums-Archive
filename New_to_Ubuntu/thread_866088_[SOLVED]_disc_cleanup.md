### Post by BoarderX2k3 on 2008-07-21
You do not need to defrag your hard drive in Linux because the Linux filesystem is much better organized than in Windows. For a more in depth explanation look [here]("http://geekblog.oneandoneis2.org/index.php/2006/08/17/why_doesn_t_linux_need_defragmenting"). 

However, there are tools out there you can download, but they are unnecessary.

I have never used any sort of disk cleanup in linux but I'm sure they are out there. I think the biggest source of free space may be uninstalling unused or outdated packages.
