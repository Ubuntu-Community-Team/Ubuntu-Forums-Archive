### Post by Ubunthree on 2008-09-19
I abandoned Windows at about the time Ubuntu Edgy was coming out, so I haven't used the actual AOL software since then. I have continued to access the 'net through AOL's server, however, using a fairly simple program called "penggy." Googling "penggy linux aol" should bring up plenty of information. It runs in a terminal window. You need to set it up with your dialup access number, screennames and passwords etc. by editing a couple of its config files. If you're stuck with dialup, AOL is probably not the best available service, but if it's what you already have, using penggy will keep you connected with a minimum of hassle until you find something better.

As for an AIM client, others have already pointed you toward Pidgin, which I am also using, and which works fine.

And incidentally... You don't necessarily have to be "feeble" or "not ready for Linux" to be using AOL, or dialup in general. Financial issues are the absolute controlling factor for me. If I get to a point where my budget loosens up a bit, and I can afford a bit of tech upgrading, then I will cheerfully dump the dialup (along with my land phone line). But until then, other things like food and shelter and bills have higher priorities unfortunately. Whatever highspeed service I get will have to be cable, *and* will have to cost significantly less than my land line and the nine bucks for AOL together. My online usage is not speed-intensive, and my needs are being met adequately. I may not like it much, but that's life at the moment.

I was using the ancient ancestor of AOL back in the 1980s, when it was called QuantumLink, and trickled in to my Commodore 64 through a 300bps modem, so I also may define sluggish performance a bit differently than others...
