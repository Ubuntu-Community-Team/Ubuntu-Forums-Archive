### Post by nothingspecial on 2010-09-15
Ok, I was trying to keep it very simple and tell you how to use the default Ubuntu ipod management app. The trouble is I haven`t used it for years. So I`ll tell you how I do it.

Open up the synaptic package manager again and search for gtkpod-aac.

Install it

Open it from your sound and video menu

Plug in your ipod

Click on the ipod bar on the left hand side once it has detected it.

To add one song, click "add files" on the top and choose the file in the window that pops up.

To add an album click "add folder" and choose the album foler

To add the whole lot click "add folder"and choose your music folder.

When your done click "save changes"

Right click the ipod picture and choose "eject ipod"

Right click the ipod icon on your desktop and choose "eject"

If you have a very new ipod and an older than current version of ubuntu, this could get very complicated
