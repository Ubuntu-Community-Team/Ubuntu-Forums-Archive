### Post by fabietto0102 on 2008-05-04
Hi Zmac_gr,

I have the exactly the same problem: I have a built-in WiFi card with chipset Ralink RT73 which is not recognized by Ubuntu and won't work. There are many threads and howto's about how to install the driver but I tell you it is not easy at all, and more, you might get it to work but then you have problem with WPA, HEX, etc..

The easiest and less stressful way to overcome the problem? Buy a new WiFi adapter which is supported by Ubuntu, end of the story. If you live in the UK, this is what I bought for a friend of mine and it spledidly works out-of-the-box and with £14.10 is the cheapest in the market: [www.faculty-x.net/cwd-854.htm](www.faculty-x.net/cwd-854.htm) .

I know spending money was not on the menu, but on the other side you ought to understand you can't expect Ubuntu to work perfectly on computer built to run Microsoft Windows. But don't worry, the day will come...
