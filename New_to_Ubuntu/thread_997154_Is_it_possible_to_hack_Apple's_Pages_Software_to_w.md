### Post by dr.frankinfurter on 2010-06-28
I know I'm digging up a relic here but is there anything that can [effectively] replace Pages? Not to my knowledge and if there was I'm guessing I would know about it. I really wish there was a good alternative to the apps on Apple. Pages is just.... sexy. I know word processors are not complex and that Abiword, OOo etc. will be able to replace basic functions but they are hideous and archaic. Also, [better] templates would be nice the ones in OOo suck. I'm tired of Linux being the 'third-world country' so to speak in the applications arena. Want something as robust and nice looking as Photoshop? Sorry, you get GIMP(ed) with a program that has less features, less eye-candy, and is un-intuitive with it's multiple Window interface. Want a video editor? Well, take your pick! Openshot, Kino, PiTivi... you got lots of choices! Ohhh, you wanted a *stable* video editor with *features*? Sorry, but we don't have any of those. SVG ed- no wait, Inkscape is still inferior to it's Adobe counter-part. I'm not even going to even touch the video game subject. 

Sure, we have *some* ground to stand on; Rhythmbox and Banshee are both fine-looking and feature rich. But, people don't do their jobs with media players - they do it with productivity applications. Until we get some decent quality ones Linux is going to be sub-par when compared to Windows (gasp!) and OS X for people who do anything other then go on the web. I'll give it to you that if they both stood on their own, Linux would take Windows to the cleaners. Putting corporate ethics aside, how is Linux a better OS then Mac OS X? Security... maybe... but I can't think of anything else. Until the open source community provides some applications, or a huge company produces something that's as beautiful as the iWork suite, Garageband, iMovie etc, then Linux is going to be second-class.

I hate the truth as much as anybody who loves OSS but it's a problem that really needs to be addressed in the long run. Can a group of people prove that open source can be something other then a replacement?
