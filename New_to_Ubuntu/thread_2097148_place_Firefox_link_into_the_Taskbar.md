### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-12-22
You need to add the launcher for FF to an **Application Launchbar** in the panel.

There is at least one **Application Launchbar **already in the panel, with the shutdown button on the extreme right hand end of the panel; just add whatever you want to that or make another **Application Launchbar** in a different place if you prefer by right clicking on the panel and using "Add to panel"

### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2012-12-22
Right click on an open space on the lxpanel.  Choose panel applets and then application launch bar.  That will get you to the quick launch applets.  Choose edit and then feel free to move the application icons around by moving them around or adding or deleting any or all of them.

### Post by Dennis N on 2012-12-22
> **Old-un said:**
> I have so far managed to get the Firefox launcher in between the time and shutdown icons. How can i move it over to the start menu?

There are two application launch bars on the panel. I think you are possibly adding Firefox to the wrong one. See:

(Right-click on panel) > Panel Settings > Panel Applets tab

The application launch bar on the bottom of the stack of applets in the default setup holds the shutdown only. The one higher up is where you want to put your application launchers. Highlight that launcher and press the edit button, then you can select from Available Applications (right), and add to the Applications (left) with the bottons.
