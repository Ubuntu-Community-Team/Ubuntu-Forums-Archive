### Post by diablo75 on 2009-12-08
Well my suggestion would be to make sure you have the drivers for your ATI card installed.  These can usually be found and enabled by going to System>Administration>Hardware Drivers (I think).  Then you just check the box off next to the driver and click enable.  You'll have to restart for the changes to take effect.

Once that's done Ubuntu should see the card, then see that there is a monitor attached, and I believe it will automatically attempt to extend out onto the second monitor all upon the next boot.  If it doesn't, then you can go into your Display settings to modify things, which should be in System>Administration>Display (could be System>Preference>Display, I'm not at my computer at the moment).

### Post by diablo75 on 2009-12-08
One last thing:  This might not work.  I've seen motherboards that are limited to displaying video on either the on-board adapter, or the adapter in the expansion slot, but not both at the same time.  Just mentioning that.
