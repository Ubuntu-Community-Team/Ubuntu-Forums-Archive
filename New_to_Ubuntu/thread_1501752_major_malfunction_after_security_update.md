### Post by cloyd on 2010-06-04
I don't know if such  a thing is possible, and a replly from someone more knowledgeable than me would be in order . . . but I you had been using windows, you'd probably be in trouble by now.

On re-reading your post, you said this happened after you accessed the internet. The site you visited may have been compromized by a malware user. Also, you rebooted and tried again. Did you go to the same website?

 I've had my Ubuntu machine hijacked and diverted to a phoney antivirus site while visiting an infected web site. I shut it down, and no further action was necessary.  There was nothing serious it could do w/o my password.  The fact that it was a phoney antivirus site trying to extort money from me because the page tried to mimic a page out of microsoft windows.  I don't use microsoft defender. When I opened up Mozillia again, it loaded the bad page again, and started all over again. I shut down, and then opened mozillia, and closed the tab that tried to load this particular page before it could load. If it would have loaded too fast to shut down, I could have disconnected from the net before loading mozillia, and then shut the tab as quick as mozillia started.

There is a force quit applet that you can install on the top panel. That is what I had to use to shut down Mozillia when the thing hijacked my machine.  It is easy to install, and it is handy. Every now and then, an application locks up. This will shut locked applications down.

Did this happen while visiting an internet site? If so, it was likely a similar thing. W/o you password, there is not much that they can do . . . though they might be able to steal stored passwords and such, which it sounds like this is what it was trying to to.  I think (not know) that sometimes these things happen because the site is infected, not because the site itself is designed to do it.

Linux has no known wild viruses (they are theoretically possible). But this kind of thing can still happen. When it happens, shut it down. Don't go back to that site.

Someone have more to say? . . . I've had some a few errors on updates lately, but nothing that seemed to try to open urls I'd been visiting.
