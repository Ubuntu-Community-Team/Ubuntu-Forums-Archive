### Post by crazy bird on 2012-01-02
There are no Linux drivers available for your scanner (check: [http://support-au.canon.com.au/P/search?category=Scanners&series=Scanners+%28Small+Office+-+Home+Office%29&model=CanoScan+8800F&menu=Download&filter=0]("http://support-au.canon.com.au/P/search?category=Scanners&series=Scanners+%28Small+Office+-+Home+Office%29&model=CanoScan+8800F&menu=Download&filter=0") and [http://www.canon.co.uk/Support/Consumer_Products/products/scanners/CanoScan_series/CanoScan_8800F.aspx?type=download&page=1](http://www.canon.co.uk/Support/Consumer_Products/products/scanners/CanoScan_series/CanoScan_8800F.aspx?type=download&page=1)) but SANE shows hat your device is supported: [http://www.sane-project.org/sane-mfgs.html#Z-CANON](http://www.sane-project.org/sane-mfgs.html#Z-CANON).
 
The site of SANE provides 2 websites on which a solution could be found:
[http://home.arcor.de/wittawat/pixma/](http://home.arcor.de/wittawat/pixma/)
[http://www.sane-project.org/man/sane-pixma.5.html](http://www.sane-project.org/man/sane-pixma.5.html)
