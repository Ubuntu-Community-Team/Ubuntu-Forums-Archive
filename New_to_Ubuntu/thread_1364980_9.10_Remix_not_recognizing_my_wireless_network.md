### Post by Tankerdog2002 on 2009-12-26
This is common and it's an easy fix.

We had the same problem.


    * Right click the nm applet on your panel.
    * Choose edit connections
    * Click the "Wireless Tab"
    * High-light your network connection
    * Click the Edit button on the right side.
    * In the new window that opens, look at the little box at the bottom left that says "Available to all users".
    * Check it
    * Close
    * Your done

### Post by Tankerdog2002 on 2009-12-26
Sorry, I gave you advice thinking that you had a wireless connection.

Well......I had this problem on my wife's Acer a coulpe of months ago, wireless worked while booted in XP but came up shot to hell in Ubuntu. Same as you, only had a wired and a VPN. When booted into Linux I had to manually turn on my wireless with Fn + F11, some netbooks have a manual button or switch. I don't know what you have or what chipset you are using.

I did get it working though. I checked the restricted drivers. Then I made sure I had all my wireless, networking, and lib files.........network-manager-gnome, network-manager, network-manger-vpnc, libnm-util1, libnm-glib0, libiw29, wireless-tools, pcmciautils, jockey-common, and I also installed network-manager-kde and knetworkmanager.

This is a known bug with remix and there are dozens of postings in launchpad. 

I also had to do all that Gnome keyring Bull-schitt for wireless; otherwise it disabled my connections and said: "you are now off-line".
