### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2015-05-19
Hello and welcome . . 

The best way to get help is to provide enough info for forum members to make an educated guess on what's going on, and what may fix it.    That starts by providing the specs for your hardware (and ditto for the specs on the projected workstations at the school).

Often, the kind of issue(s) you describe is connected to the graphics system, and whether or not you've completed the install correctly by installing proprietary graphics drivers.   This applies to nvidia and ati gpu's/cards.    Intel integrated graphics are mostly open source and work out of the box due to kernel-level support.

ALso, provide your make/model of PC, cpu type/speed, ram, and if any non-standard storage or other hardware connected (i.e., specialized gaming mouse etc.)
