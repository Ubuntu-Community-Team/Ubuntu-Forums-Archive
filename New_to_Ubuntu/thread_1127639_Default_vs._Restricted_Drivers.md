### Post by russward73 on 2009-04-16
Hi everyone! I apologize in advance if this should be somewhere else.

I was running Ubuntu 8.10 32-bit. I had the latest Nvidia restricted drivers and 3d acceleration activated.  I was having problems with streaming video being choppy and slow. I have a 64-bit processor so I did a fresh install with the 64-bit version of Ubuntu. I have not activated any Nvidia drivers yet or adjusted any accelerations settings and already streaming video looks 1000 times better.

My question is, why use the restricted drivers? Is there a benefit that I will miss in the future if I don't use them?

Thanks for your insight.

### Post by RetchingRabbit on 2009-04-16
The restricted drivers generally provide greater performance, i.e., hardware acceleration, 3d, etc.

### Post by ranch hand on 2009-04-16
If the drivers you are using are doing the job you want them to do - leave them alone.

You have choice in Linux.  if the kernal is handling your card for what YOU want leave it be.

Basically if you have to ask if the drivers you had before (and your performance was worse) are better than what you have now, YOU don't see the advantage.  There doesn't appear to be an advantage to YOU.
