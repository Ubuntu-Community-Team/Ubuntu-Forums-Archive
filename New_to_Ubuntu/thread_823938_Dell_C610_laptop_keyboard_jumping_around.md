### Post by zggame on 2008-06-09
I have Xubuntu 8.0.4 in my Dell Latitude c610 (PIII 1.2G/512M).  The keyboard works fine when I type slowly.  But whenever I get fast, the keys with "Fn" 1-9 (blue key for small number key in normal full-size keyboard) do not work properly.  It often push the cursor to upline, next line or back a word, or something like a shift-tab.  I am fairly sure I did not touch the pin-ball in the key-board center.  It likes the keyboard go wild when I type very fast.  I tried to set keyboard to the latitude series, but that did not help much. It happens averagely once every 50 words. (ranges from every couple of letters or nothing in a whole email. )

It is a fairly old laptop.  So it might also just the problem of the hardware.  But it has no such problem when typing slowly. So I suspected it is more related to software. 

Any suggestion is appreciated.

### Post by zalberico on 2008-06-21
It may be that the mouse is moving around by itself while you're typing, my friend and I struggled to get the mouse to stop jumping around on his c610 and found that plugging in an external mouse using a USB to PS/2 converter was the only thing that worked.  These little converters often come free with keyboards and mice.  Hopefully this is useful.
