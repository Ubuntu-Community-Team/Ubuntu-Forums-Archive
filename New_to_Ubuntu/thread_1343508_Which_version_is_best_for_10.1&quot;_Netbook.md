### Post by teward on 2009-12-01
To the poster of this thread:  You can use either Netbook Remix or the normal Ubuntu Desktop version.  Either way, though, you need to burn the image onto a USB stick unless you have an external DVD reader/writer.

If you want dual boot, I **strongly recommend** the normal **Ubuntu Desktop** version, 32 bit.

Yes, it is after install. A typical Linux system is made up of lots of different packages that can be installed independently of eachother; the Netbook remix only differs from the normal Ubuntu in the inclusion of a special launcher interface. This can be removed or just turned off. It can also be installed on regular Ubuntu.

There's also Kubuntu Netbook Edition, which is rather interesting. You might want to try it.

And then of course there's regular Ubuntu, Kubuntu and Xubuntu, all of which work on Eees.
