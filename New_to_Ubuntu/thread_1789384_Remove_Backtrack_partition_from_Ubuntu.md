### Post by jtarin on 2011-06-23
Do you have the Ubuntu Live CD (install disk) it has Gparted on it. You will need Gparted  to do this with. Boot from the Ubuntu Live CD and when you get to the desktop you will find Gparted under the System>Administration menu...(I believe.....it will be somewhere there) start it and after it launches and shows your partitions take a screenshot.Basically what you will be doing is deleting everything to the right of /dev/sda1. This includes the extended partition and everything in it. Now expand the /dev/sda1 partition to the right until it takes all of the unallocated space with the exception of about 2GB's which you will format as a swap partition. If your confused or not sure what to do post back with a screenshot of Gparted.

Quick easy explanation.

Swap space is hard drive space used as an "extension" of your RAM. If your RAM becomes exhausted swap space is used.  

This is very simplified and there are more aspects of it , but that is the basic concept.
