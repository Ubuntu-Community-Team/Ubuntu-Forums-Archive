### Post by fballem on 2008-10-04
> **uby54 said:**
> I have MUCH data I don't want to lose; no external CD to save it on though.  There wasn't anything mentioned about that in everything I read about trying out Ubuntu; I thought I read it all pretty thoroughly from the Ubuntu home page.

I'm sure that you read thoroughly. Nearly every computer user that I know learns about backing up data the hard way - and usually it is a really expensive lesson.

May I ask another question - do you do backups of your data to-day? If you do, could you please tell us how you do those backups?

Backups are a good idea, regardless of whether you use ubuntu, windows, or something else. Hard Drives do sometimes fail, and having a backup can be a lifesaver in those cases.

Backups are a _really_ good idea if you are making any major changes to your computer, such as installing a new operating system. Things do, occasionally go wrong and you most likely will not have a way to recover if they do, unless you have a backup.

I would recommend that you get an external USB drive. These can be purchased at BestBuy or similar store, or at an office supply store, like Office Depot. Not sure how large your hard drive is, but external USB drives come in various sizes. My suggestion is that you get one that is at least as large as your hard drive. For example, if the Hard Drive on your computer is 100 Gigabytes, then that should be the smallest external Hard Drive that you get.

Once you get the external hard drive, you should be able to plug it into a USB port on your computer. Please follow the instructions carefully, since some will require the installation of drivers under Windows. These drivers should be on a CD that comes with the drive.

Once the drive is operating, please format the drive under Windows to use the NTFS file system - many drives come formatted with FAT32, which won't store most of the information that you need.

When the drive is operating, and correctly formatted, you may copy the Documents and Settings directory to the external hard drive. When done, please safely remove the hard drive.

These instructions sound more complicated than they actually are to execute. If you have any questions, please post on this thread and we'll try to help you.
