### Post by JKyleOKC on 2014-09-03
Other programs you can use are "fotoxx" and "GIMP" and both should be available in the Software Center. The fotoxx program is designed specifically for photo cleanup and I've used it to eliminate perspective distortion in pictures of buildings, where the distortion makes it look as if the building is falling over backward.

The GIMP is far more powerful and to get the most from it has a fairly serious learning curve; it's pretty much the equivalent of Adobe's Photoshop and can do the same things. However it can also be used to do the things you list with no need to learn all of its bells and whistles.

Personally I keep ristretto, gthumb, and fotoxx on my systems at all times, in addition to GIMP. Of the four, I use gthumb the most for quick browsing through photo folders, and GIMP for anything more complicated than that -- but I'm doing all sorts of cleanup from not-very-good smartphone photos, for publication in an alumni magazine! The one I use the least these days is fotoxx simply because I've learned to use GIMP to take its place.
