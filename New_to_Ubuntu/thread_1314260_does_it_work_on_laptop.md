### Post by issih on 2009-11-04
The only way someone can try to honestly answer that for you is if you post as much information about your computer specs as possible

I mean everything - the model, the wireless card model, the graphics card model, the processor, everything. In addition you'd need to tell us what peripherals you are planning to use with it, what printer, what webcam, etc etc etc.

Then and only then could someone say that you will be 100% ok or not, and finding out would require them to do quite a lot of research to be absolutely sure, unless they happened to have the exact same subset of hardware.

The best way to find out is suck it and see, boot from a live cd and select the try without changing your computer option, and stay away from any links saying install until you are happy - be aware though, that running from a live cd is a sluggish experience compared to a full install, but it will let you test what works.

Secondly consider a wubi install from within windows.

[http://wubi-installer.org/](http://wubi-installer.org/)

this allows you to add and remove Ubuntu as if it was a normal windows program, you then just choose which to boot at boot time.

If it works then great, if not you uninstall it and walk away.

Once you are happy that it works and you like it, then you can consider a full blown install.

Just take these things one step at a time.

### Post by bovender on 2009-11-04
Hi,

as a relatively new Ubuntu user with a laptop I'd like to make two remarks:

1) Many people have problems with standby and/or resume on their laptops; see [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1307618](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1307618).

2) There is no out-of-the-box feature to switch screens. If you want to connect your laptop to a projector, you need to google a solution first. It will most likely involve using the terminal. No "Fn+F7" or similar feature as known from Windows & Mac. I use something called "disper" on my Thinkpad with an Nvidia GPU. (It'd be great if Ubuntu could support display switching out of the box, really. It is such a standard feature for laptops.)
