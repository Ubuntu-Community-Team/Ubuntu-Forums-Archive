### Post by t0p on 2009-04-04
I've noticed that most people who slag off gimp do so because gimp isn't photoshop.  You're such a person, and I'd suggest you use photoshop.  You could dual-boot windows with ubuntu, or run it in a virtual session.  But if the gimp doesn't cut it, that's because it isn't photoshop.  So use photoshop.

Or you could do as I do.  I use the gimp.  I don't moan about its inadequacies in Ubuntu forums.  If I have a major gripe (I haven't yet), I might complain on a gimp-related forum or mailing list or I might contact a developer.  But in the meantime I just use gimp to the  best of my/its abilities.  It isn't photoshop.  But it *is* a very good image manipulation program.  And it's free *and* Free, which makes it infinitely better than photoshop.

If you really truly want to use photoshop in linux, moan at adobe.  Or you could learn programming, and write a linux port or "improvements" to gimp.  Go for it!  That'll be a much better way of spending your time, rather than whingeing and moaning about gimp on a forum that isn't a gimp developers forum.

As for "good" image manipulation apps for linux...I recommend gimp.
