### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-11-18
Even the alternate installer for Ubuntu requires at least 128MB of RAM I believe, with 192 recommended.  For a computer as old as a pentium 2, Ubuntu may not be the best choice, you will probably want something more lightweight.  Damn Small Linux (DSL) and Puppy Linux are some very small distros that you might consider.  DSL is based on Debian, which might means it may be a bit easier to use if you are used to Ubuntu which is also based off of Debian.  Don't expect too many similarities, though, since DSL is *really* lightweight.

There is only so much you can do with hardware that old.

### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-11-25
> **heroidi said:**
> the hard drive was damaged

With a machine that old, I'm not terribly surprised.  The MTBF (mean time between failure) on hard drive is highly overrated by the manufacturers - I typically expect a hard drive to work solidly for 4 or 5 years (slightly longer than I plan on keeping a computer around as my primary machine).  Though I typically expect them to work longer than that, I wouldn't be too surprised if they fail.  Of course, it's quite easy for a drive to fail before that mark, or live for many years longer.
