### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2008-12-02
Just remember that Amarok may look a little out of place as its a KDE application and not a Gnome one.
Gnome and KDE are what are known as desktop environments, or for the windows user the main graphical interface.
Unlike windows where you dont have the option of customization without dangerous third party software, linux has choice.
If you dont like how one desktop interface works, use another free of charge without having to worry about addware/ viruses or annoying trials.
Its possible to use gnome software in KDE, and vice versa thanks to one not overriding the other.
But if you wish to make kde applications look better I suggest installing qtcurve, its not in add/remove but it is in the repositories.
Instead of using add/ remove or the terminal you can use synaptic, located under system, administration, stnaptic package manager.
Synaptic is sort of like a more advanced version of add/remove with far more applications and tools for you to play around with :D
