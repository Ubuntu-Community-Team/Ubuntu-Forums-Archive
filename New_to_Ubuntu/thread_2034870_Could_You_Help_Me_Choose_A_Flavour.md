### Post by Bartender on 2012-07-29
If you're just getting started, do yourself a favor and stick to one desktop environment (DE).  The DE is what you're looking at when the computer finishes loading the operating system.  The DE determines what the computer "looks like", and how you interact with it.  

A perfect example of how the interactions can be different: in Gnome or KDE you can right-click (oops - I mean wheel-click) on a file, then drag it to a new location and get the Copy or Paste option.  In XFCE you do not get the option to copy or paste after dragging.  XFCE is a more basic DE and certain "luxuries" are left out.

KDE and Gnome are the two mainstream DE's that overlay the Linux kernel.  They are the fully-featured DE's. Xubuntu uses XFCE.  There's also LXDE, Enlightenment, etc.    

Without some version of a DE you'd be staring at a terminal screen.  You'd need a computer sciences degree to check your email.

Pangolin, or Precise Pangolin, is simply the goofy name that applies to the latest version of Ubuntu.  You can pretty much ignore that once you understand that "Pangolin" and version 12.04 are interchangeable terms for the same thing.

The folks over at Mint start out with the Ubuntu OS but are working on their own DE's.  Cinnamon has gotten a lot of good press and is certainly worth checking out.

But to go back to my original comment, if your PC has the horsepower to run Unity 3D, and you don't despise Unity, I'd stick with Ubuntu.  Jumping back and forth between XFCE, Cinnamon, Gnome, etc. is just gonna drag out the time before you feel like you can get anything accomplished.

I've run into the same problem you describe when adding one or two other DE's so that I could log out of one session and use another.  The computer seemed to lose its way.  Might want to wipe it and start over, or at least delete a session or two...
