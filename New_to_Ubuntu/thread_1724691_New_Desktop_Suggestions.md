### Post by rosencrantz on 2011-04-08
In terms of Ubuntu compatibility or general hardware best-value-for-money recommendations?
For what purpose do you want to use the desktop?
I'd say go for Nvidia graphics (ATI has had driver issues in the past), but not the Optimus hybrid line (not supported yet) - or Intel on board, if you aren't a gamer.
I've had some trouble with WiFi chips in the past (I usually had home-assembled 'Frankenstein' desktops) - if there were only proprietary Windows drivers available, you could run into trouble with 64-bit Linux. Anyway, that was years ago. With 64bit-Windows, things may be different.  If you want to play it safe and out-of-the-box, make sure the chip is Atheros - or, once again, Intel on-board (no, they don't sponsor me).
Dell's Linux compatibility has been terrible in the past, but they've been embracing Ubuntu lately, so there should be no problems. I don't have any experience with the other two.

### Post by earthpigg on 2011-04-08
One of the problems faced by both ZaReason and System76 is that a large chunk of us experienced Linux users have long-since abandoned the notion purchasing pre-built desktops. That means that many of their customers and potential customers would essentially be doing charity by purchasing from them, regardless of how great their customer service and products are.

By the time I was ready to purchase a designed-for-linux desktop, for example, I was also ready to design and assemble it myself.

If I were being selfish, I'd vote for ZaReason because you purchasing from them would be putting money into my local economy. :)

Both companies have generally received high reviews from customers. Objectively, it would be hard to pick which is qualitatively higher. So, pick whichever gives you the most bang for your buck by comparing hardware configurations at your given price-point.
