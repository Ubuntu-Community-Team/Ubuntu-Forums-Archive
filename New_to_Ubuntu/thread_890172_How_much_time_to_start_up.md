### Post by starcannon on 2008-08-14
+1 to OutOfReach's comment.

I like your style Auspicious, I recommend doing the legwork first, and your already doing that.
Don't be in a hurry, don't put a deadline on yourself; that way, if a problem arises you aren't in a no win situation.
If you run into problems come here to the forums, we will help, we like to help, and you will have a good time in the process.


1)gather up a list of your hardware
2)post that list here
3)we can comment on compatibility issues you may encounter.
4)google a hardware piece with the word linux
5)start out with the 32bit Ubuntu, its just easier that way; 64bit works, but I wouldn't call it an easy install, I have 64bit hardware and still run 32bit Ubuntu, works perfectly, and is very snappy.

I will say that for Video Cards: Nvidia and Intel cards are the easiest to work with, followed by ATi. SiS and VIA are terrible.

Sound can be frustrating if you have something a little out of the ordinary, but most people have no issues with it.

WiFi can be a bear if you have broadcom or some other brand that requires Ndis, most will find that even wifi now days works with little effort on their part (do use an ethernet cable during install though, just in case you have a non-standard wifi card).

Thats the 3 areas where most people run into problems. Other than that, with exception of the rare motherboard here or there, things "just work".

I know you see a lot of help me posts here on the boards, and may think "wow lots of problems", but I keep in mind that for every problem we solve in here, there were probably 5 installs that we never heard about because they were problem free.

Welcome to Ubuntu!!! you've landed in the friendliest community on the internet!

### Post by halitech on 2008-08-14
