### Post by whatthefunk on 2011-12-30
I just did this...it was a lot easier than I thought.  First, you need to figure out what you want from the pc.  Do you do a lot of gaming?  Just internet stuff?  Do you use resource intensive programs?  Based on this you can figure out what hardware will suit your needs and start comparing different models.  There are hundreds of guides out there...here are a few:
[http://www.buildyourown.org.uk/](http://www.buildyourown.org.uk/)
[http://www.kitchentablecomputers.com/index.php](http://www.kitchentablecomputers.com/index.php)
[http://www.mysuperpc.com/](http://www.mysuperpc.com/)

There are thousands more.  The hardest part for me was choosing the hardware.  There are so many options out there that it can be a little overwhelming at first.  It took me a week of just reading online product reviews and running around to shops before I decided on what I wanted.

Once you get all the parts, its really just a matter of screwing things into the case and plugging everything in.  That part of it only took me a couple hours.

All in all Id say it was a great experience.  I leanred a lot about hardware and now have a super fast computer that cost a couple hundred dollars less than a similar pre-built pc would have cost.
