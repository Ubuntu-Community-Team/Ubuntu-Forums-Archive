### Post by Yashiro on 2009-03-15
The difference is mostly in look and feel. 
Kubuntu is probably the most different as it uses many alternate apps.

Ubuntu uses the Gnome Desktop Environmnent.
*Standard Desktop. Most common, looks a bit drab but it's ok.*
[IMG]http://www.pendrivelinux.com/wp-content/uploads/ubuntu804.jpg[/IMG]

Kubuntu uses the K Desktop Environmnent ("KDE").
[I]Uses a different toolset for the gui and is quite 'fancy'.
It's also quite slow and resembles the forgotten love child of Apple and Microsoft.[/I]
[IMG]http://gd.tuwien.ac.at/.vhost/www.kde.org/announcements/announce_4.2-beta1/desktop_thumb.png[/IMG]

Xubuntu uses Xfce Desktop Environmnent.
[I]Can use normal gnome apps, but runs alot faster than standard Ubuntu.
[/I][IMG]http://linuxos.sk/clanky/xubuntu/3m.png[/IMG]

If you are new. Start with **Ubuntu**.
