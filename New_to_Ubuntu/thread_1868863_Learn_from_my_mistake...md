### Post by Diametric on 2011-10-24
I thought I'd share this - I just purchased a python book and was looking to make sure I have python 3.x.  I was working on two different machines and not ***really ***paying attention to what I was doing.  I ran:

```
sudo apt-get remove python 
```

Despite the "are you sure's" and "this will remove 10 gazillion amounts of data" AND the log list of programs that were being removed - I didn't pay attention and about killed my entire desktop among lots of other things.  

I once read a quote in someone sig that said "Windows assumes you don't know what you're doing; Linux assumes you know exactly what you're doing" - or something very similar.  

Moral of the story and why I posted this in the beginners is: slow your roll and make sure you pay attention to what your doing and what your box is trying to tell you!

If you need me, I'll be at a tty prompt for a bit.

Cheers...

### Post by decoherence on 2011-10-24
Sorry to hear that! We've all been there. One doesn't truly appreciate the awesome, life changing power of the 'rm' command until they've forgotten what directory they're in and mistakenly clobbered their work files. It's not a newbie mistake -- it can happen to anyone who loses focus for even a moment.

I'll expand on this to say that if you ever get the following message

    You are about to do something potentially harmful
    To continue type in the phrase 'Yes, do as I say!'

Unless you're manhandling the package tree in Debian Sid, you probably really don't want to type in that phrase. I've done it more than once because I thought I knew better. Most times I was spectacularly wrong! :o
