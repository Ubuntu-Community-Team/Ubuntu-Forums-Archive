### Post by phidia on 2008-07-19
I would stay away from usb modems most of those are winmodems also. I don't know if you used [this ubuntu guide]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DialupModemHowto") for the internal modem but it might be worth a look.
External serial port modems are usually the easiest to get recognized and working. 

To this question: "Or, are there any stand-alone modems that have wifi built in to allow wireless connectivity to the modem(her wireless works fine in her laptop)" brings to mind the older apple airports but they might still be sort of pricey. 
From what I briefly looked at [here]("http://www.mwave.com/mwave/DeepSearch_v2.asp?scriteria=Modems+BEST+DATA+external+modem&All=y&DID=MDM&PID=MDM-BES") it looks like some pcmcia modems should work too.

### Post by t0p on 2008-07-21
Because you're talking about a modem for a *laptop* I assume that your friend would like something that gives her a wireless/mobile internet connection?

Cos, if there's 3G/UMTS coverage in her area, she could use a cellular phone as a modem and get good "mobile broadband" connection speeds.  I use a Sony Ericsson 3G mobile phone as a modem.  It's plug-and-play with its automagical USB datacable connection.  And I get speeds that, according to [the Broadband Speedtest on ZD Net UK]("http://resources.zdnet.co.uk/speedtest/"), are often on a par with 2MB DSL/Cable (2048 KBPS or more)!

Of course, connection speeds are very dependent on local conditions.
