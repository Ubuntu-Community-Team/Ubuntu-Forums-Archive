Should you delete Windows? That's up to *you*. If you are truly sure you can do all you need to without Windows, then by all means do it. But that's a decision only you can find the answer to by analyzing your own experiences and deducing what's best for *you*.


As far as how to delete Windows and keep Ubuntu, I've read several different scenarios online. All with varying degrees of success. Then insert your Ubuntu CD and choose to use the whole disc (which will wipe everything).

### Post by monkeybrain2012 on 2012-11-25
OP already said Ubuntu meets all his need, why still advise him to keep Vista? I say get rid of it! 

The easiest way is to save your /home directory (which contains your data and settings and then do a clean install, wipe everything. After install,  replace the new /home with the backup.

Another way, which is somewhat risky is to boot up from a live usb, use gparted to delete the Vista partition and then expand the Ubuntu partition to the whole thing, But since the dual boot configuration typically has Vista on the left and expanding the ubuntu partition that way may screw up your hard drive (I read on this forum somewhere that expanding to the right is ok, but to the left is somewhat risky. Others say it is ok, I haven't tried it, I used the first way when I got rid of XP, it was a really liberating feeling to be Windows free. :))
