### Post by riverguy99 on 2015-10-11
Is there a good reason to upgrade from 12.04 to 15.04?  We're running 12.04 on five office and home machines and are having no issues with it.  So what I am interested in learning is whether or not I should consider doing all the work to reconfigure any of these machines to 15.04.  Faster?  More capable, and if so, in what areas? When I upgraded to 14.04 some time ago, I went back to 12.04 because with 14.04 it had become a major mindwarp to configure dual displays.  There were countless posts about "all of the possible fixes" and 12.04 just found the displays and set them up all by itself.  **I LOVE Ubuntu**, but don't have much interest in experimenting with complicated ways to get things working.  But I sure do appreciate all the good folks who do, because that's what makes it possible for those of us who don't!

So, should I upgrade?  Also, I read a lot, now, about how it is not good to use WUBI for a side-by-side install with W7.  I need to keep W7 on one machine.  Is there a good guide somewhere on setting up 15.04 side-by-side with W7 without using WUBI?

Thank you!

### Post by howefield on 2015-10-11
There is a good reason not to upgrade to 15.04 and that would be the short support life it has left - January 2016, so bear that in mind.

You would shortly be looking at the choice of upgrading again or running an unsupported release, in addition you are currently having no issues and have no interest in getting "things working", I'd think you would be better thinking about this around 3 months or so before 12.04 hits end of life :)

### Post by tristan16 on 2015-10-12
If you have no issues with your current setup, stay with the version you've got. You'll get a notification when 12.04 goes EOL, and you'll be able to upgrade to the next LTS version, though you'll probably be better off with a clean install.

With the work you specified and the fact that you're probably not running brand-new hardware, you probably won't get much of a boost by upgrading. Personally, I noticed several performance improvements going from 14.04 to 15.04 because the newer version had better support for my hardware, specifically my graphics card. Since you're not gaming, you should be fine.
