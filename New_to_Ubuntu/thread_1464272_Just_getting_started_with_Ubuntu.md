### Post by Tom Collier on 2010-04-28
This will be a case of the blind leading the blind, so you are forewarned. With that caveat......

1. What you have downloaded is an *.iso file, or an image of a CD ROM. Your next step is to burn that *.iso image to a CD Rom.
2. You'll need:
    2a.) a CD drive with write capabiity on your computer
    2b.) .iso burner software. There are several available as freeware. Google it to find one that suits you, then install it on your computer.
3. Next, place an empty, writeable, blank CD in the CD ROM drive, locate the ubuntu iso file where you downloaded it, run the iso burner program, and burn the ubuntu iso file to the blank CD.
4.  When that task is complete, restart your computer with the finished CD in the drive. Your computer should then boot from the new *LIVE CD*.
5.  Once your computer starts up again, you'll see a series of menus, the first of which asks you to choose a language. English is the default.
6. Next, a menu offers you the opportunity to either run Ubuntu on a test basis directly from the CD without installing it on your computer's hard drive, or you can just go ahead and install it.

Once you are up and running, I **strongly** urge you to get a copy of every beginner's manual and tutorial you can get your hands on and ***read them***.

This forum can be of tremendous assistance no matter what your level of computer knowledge or aptitude.  But getting help here is like mathematics....if you don't have a basic grounding in arithmetic, an explanation at the calculus level does you absolutely no good.

If your computer is not running at all because of the fake Window 7 situation, you may want to consider borrowing someone else's computer that is working properly.  See steps 1-6 above to create a usable CD-ROM of Ubuntu, put that CD ROM in your crippled computer's CD ROM drive and then turn it on. That'll get you to steps 5 and/or 6.

Good luck! Enjoy using Ubuntu. I do.
