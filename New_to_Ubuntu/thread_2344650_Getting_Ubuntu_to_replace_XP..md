### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-11-27
Sparky,

Several of our PC Club seniors recently went through the same thing as you are now (within the last 18 months especially).

Anyway, to save yourself a bunch of frustration, my first recommendation would be to get the proper Linux DVD you need.   Do not get a USB Thumb Drive as they can be tricky to boot from old PC's (prior to 2006/2007 or so).

Below is a l ink to a reliable vendor that has been selling Linux OS disks for many years.   They have the "32 bit" version of Xubuntu available for $5.95.      

Suggest that after you get the disk, load it up and you'll see how it runs on your system.   Be advised any OS running off a CD or DVD is going to be MUCH slower than it is running off the Hard Drive.    But, you'll still get a feel for how Xubuntu looks and so on.    

When you want to install it . . . click on the install icon on the desktop.   Read the screens carefully, but, the simplest/best way IF Xubuntu will be the ONLY OS on the PC is to select the option to let it write to the whole drive, erasing all the content (data and XP OS) . . . . then just follow what the screen instructions ask.

BE sure to save or backup all the data you need to keep BEFORE installing Xubuntu.   You can reload it later into Xubuntu (for programs like LibreOffice, etc.)

Here's the LINK:   [https://www.osdisc.com/products/linux/xubuntu/xubuntu-16041-lts-install-live-dvd-32bit.html](https://www.osdisc.com/products/linux/xubuntu/xubuntu-16041-lts-install-live-dvd-32bit.html)

Please advise if ANY questions and how it goes . . . any hardware info you can provide will be useful . . . . especially info about your graphics system (does it have ATI or Nvidia graphics, or just built in graphics like Intel?).

Good Luck!  

PS:   if you do have a few $$ to spare, you may want to check out the link in my signature and get a PC with Ubuntu already installed!   Well worth it.    Takes about 10-15" to completely set up (or less if you're a keyboard whiz).
