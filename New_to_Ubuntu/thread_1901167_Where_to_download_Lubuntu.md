### Post by I2k4 on 2011-12-28
While I'm dual booting Lubuntu on my netbook, and think it's very good for that little machine, I'm experimenting with Mint 10.10 and 11.10 via Live USB on my main machine. 

 [http://www.linuxmint.com/oldreleases.php](http://www.linuxmint.com/oldreleases.php)

I found I didn't like most of the preinstalled software in Lubuntu, deleted and replaced nearly everything, which is time consuming. I do like most of what comes with Mint out of the box.  Mint also also has a nicer and more configurable interface, though it is more resource intensive. 

I strongly suggest experimenting with several versions via Live USB and Persistence before committing a new operating system to a hard drive: there are more differences than meet the eye.
