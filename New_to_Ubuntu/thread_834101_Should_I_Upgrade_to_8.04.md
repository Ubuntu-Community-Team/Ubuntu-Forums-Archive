### Post by bumanie on 2008-06-19
One can never say for certain that you won't have problems with the upgrade process or not have other issues after the upgrade. Personally, have found 8.04 to be far superior to 7.10, but that has not been everyone's experience. If you do an upgrade, rather than a fresh install, in theory all programs and settings should be enhanced by the upgrade, and nothing should be lost. You cannot downgrade, if you don't like 8.04. or find it buggy for your particular setup. I think it is fair to say that the majority have found 8.04 to be an improvement on 7.10.
