### Post by LowSky on 2009-03-13
When you power on the PC you will get the Dell screen, it might say press F2 or some other buton to enter BIOS, DO that, then search the BIOS for On Board Video or Grphics (sorry not familair with your laptop and they are all different),, next to that it will have an amount of RAM, it could be 4, 8,16,32,64,128, or 256 Depends on the PC when it was set up. Now following the directions for changing the setting (the menu will tell you how, sometime the up/down arrows, sometimes page up/page down keys... add more RAM as high as you like... 


Hope this helps
