### Post by okplayer02 on 2010-05-06
you can do that by going to Systems ---> Preferences ----> Startup Applications

then click add on the right side  then give it a name like empathy then under command you browse to /usr/bin/empathy then ok then comment if u like to enter one then add.

so empathy will start up automatically when u login to gnome.  Just make sure u have your passwords saved for the accounts in empathy you want to autostart and thats it
