### Post by DuckHook on 2019-04-09
Welcome to the forums, northsideglyde.

The matter of security is probably one of the most complex ones around. But reduced to a very rough simplicity, one can think of it as a sliding scale with "laughable" at one end and "impenetrable" at the other. Your answer will depend on where along that scale your comfort level lies.

By force of habit and good sense, we do not recommend unpatched installations around here. One of the reasons that Linux is not a malware sinkhole like other OSes is because its user base tends to be comprised of geeks who update religiously. Security holes are discovered in all OSes routinely. Updates close them off before they can be exploited. So, no, it's not a good idea to run without updates.

You've given us a lot of background, but the following clarifications would also be helpful:

[LIST]
[*]What do you mean by update? Is it from one version to another, or is it the security updates and bug fixes within a version?
[*]What version are you using anyway?
[*]What do you mean by "seems to go crazy?" Describe the behaviour in dispassionate and technical detail.
[*]You posted this in the Vittualization subforum. Are you running Studio in a VM?
[/LIST]
Last but not least, for critical data, it is imperative that you have backups. Your description of having years of important data on a single USB stick is scary. Electronic devices break down and USB sticks are especially fragile.
