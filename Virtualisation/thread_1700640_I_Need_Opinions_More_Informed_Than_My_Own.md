There is good news and bad news here.

_The good news_ is that virtualzation has become reasonably simple, and there are two free options to do it (VMware Player and VirtualBox).

_The bad news_ is that you are tying to stuff 3 quarts of water into a 1 quart jar.  A netbook simply does not have the horsepower to run Vegas in any reasonable way.  I use Vegas; and I have to say that Vegas it the lowest overhead video editing tool I have ever found. However, the minimums to get any real work done in Vegas just cannot be met by a netbook, no matter what operating system you run.

I have to dual-boot to get the performance I demand out of Vegas (I edit mostly AVC HD footage).

The other reality is that editing video requires a great deal of hard disk storage.  Most netbooks are designed to be 'very lean' with just enough storage to get by.

The quote Scotty of Star Trek: "You cannot break the laws of Physics, Capitan!".

You will need more hardware to run Vegas or Premiere effectively.  Vegas is the best beat for lower end machines; but it needs something to work with.
