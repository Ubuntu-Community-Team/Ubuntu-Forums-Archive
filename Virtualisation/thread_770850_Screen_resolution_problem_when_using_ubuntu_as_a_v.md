### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2008-04-28
Hi

I had a similar issue running Hardy in a VM on another Linux machine.

Here are the entries that made a difference for me - try them at your own risk, of course:

In the "Device" Section, Replace the line 
```

              Driver		"video"
```

with this:

```
        Driver          "vboxvideo"

```

That change alone gave me a default (and maximum) resolution of 1024x768.

(If you need more (and your monitor is capable) add a new "Display" SubSection to the "Screen" Section to enable higher modes.  Here's what my "Screen" Section looks like:

```
Section "Screen"
        Identifier      "Default Screen"
        Monitor         "Configured Monitor"
        Device          "Configured Video Device"
        SubSection      "Display"
                Modes   "1680x1050" "1600x1024" "1440x900" "1280x1024"
        EndSubSection
EndSection
```

Save xorg.conf, logout and log in again.

It will either be fixed or terribly broken.  That's apparently how xorg.conf works!:)

(I assume you have a backup)

Good luck.
