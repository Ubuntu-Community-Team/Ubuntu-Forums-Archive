### Post by lobo_blanco on 2009-01-26
Hi All,

Every once and a while we all need to hear a success story.
(Even though we know we'd be bored if everything were working properly)

Years ago I dabbled in Linux just to see what it was.
I've tried several times to shake the MS BSOD hold that they had on me over my many years but it always came down to needing some software that was only supported by Windows. (Or shall I say some software that was pandering to or only loyal to MS)

I'm a draftsman, so AutoCAD was my biggest issue, then came Intuit QuickBooks that I need for my business.

I have been dependent upon but not happy about having to use these two software any more than Windows.

QuickBooks is a bit of a preference thing but is the only software that I know that supports certain things that I do in my business.

Finally, I broke lose from Autodesk and purchased IntelliCAD.
What a great move that was. Same commands, works wonderfully and at 1/10th the cost. The biggest plus is that now I can exchange drawings between most if not all years of drawings made in CAD. (AutoCAD only supports several of the past years unless you upgrade/ IntelliCAD opens and saves to them all).

Neither AutoCAD or QuickBooks plays well with Linux as far as I could determine. Wine just didn't cut it. These software are too tied in to calling home etc.

I tried VMware's 30 day trial ... mistake.
It was nearly impossible to get off my machine.
I was working in Ubuntu 8.10 at the time and finally ended up using Gparted and starting over with Ubuntu 8.04. I went back to 8.04 for several reasons but I wanted the most stable platform that I could get yet still be able to migrate from Windows to Linux with relative ease.

Before I reverted back to Ubuntu 8.04 I tried VirtualBox - ose.
Had a few issues due to it's not being right up-to-date but could appreciate that this is what I needed to get away from my dual boot with XP. After a learning curve with Ubuntu, numerous package installs/ uninstalls etc I wanted a clean slate. 

I like Ubuntu 8.04 ... or 804 as I call it!
I'm a Ubuntu newbie but really getting into it and appreciating how much more stable and configurable it is than Windows.
I'm cautious about my terminal use but not afraid to follow documented, well written tutorials and invoke commands needed to get packages integrated properly.

I went the Sun VirtualBox route this time on a fresh install of 804.

VirtualBox Sun distro

Getting VirtualBox set up right was not terribly difficult the second time around and once I had the settings figured out and realized where the files all are kept and how it works in general ... It all makes great sense.

Currently I use 804 for all tasks except for my CAD and Books.
I have XP running lean within VirtualBox but do keep a firewall and antivirus up-to-date in XP as well as use ClamTk and Firestarter in Ubuntu.

All is working very smoothly now and the shift to use various programs throughout my workday is seamless and easy.

I'm sure happy and grateful for all the quality information that I've found here at the ubuntuforuums since this is where I've ironed out what would or wouldn't work under my particular circumstances. It really has helped learn from others who have done so much leg work and are willing to share what they've learned.

Sun's made VirtualBox a great product, I'm very impressed and confident that it will get ever better. The Ubuntu community is on one heck of a roll and will succeed beyond any of our wildest dreams, I have no doubt!

I just wanted to pass on a Success Story to any and all of you.

Thanks for what you do!
