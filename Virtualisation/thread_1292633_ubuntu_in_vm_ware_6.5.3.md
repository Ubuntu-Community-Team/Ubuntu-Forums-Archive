### Post by Kyluke on 2009-10-16
Try installing your graphics card driver inside the virtual machine if you have not done that already.
 
virtual machines do not recognise or have full access to your graphics card.
 
There should be an option just as in virtualbox to assign a certain amount of your GPU to the virtual machine. virtual machines are not intended for serious 3D apps but more testing and other apps that will not work under wine or other emulation software.
 
For more helpful information here are some links that might interest you.
[http://communities.vmware.com/thread/193243](http://communities.vmware.com/thread/193243)
[http://www.neowin.net/forum/index.php?showtopic=471347&mode=linear](http://www.neowin.net/forum/index.php?showtopic=471347&mode=linear)
 
 
Good luck and remember that virtual machines do not have the same power as an operating system installed on a PC natively
