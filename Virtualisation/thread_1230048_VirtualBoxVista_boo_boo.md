### Post by steveneddy on 2009-08-02
Saw a buddy today and showed him my Ubuntu laptop.

He liked it and DLed a copy and installed it in VirtualBox running on Vista Ultimate.

I ran off to do some other stuff and came back in the afternoon to check on him.

No full screen video so I told him to install Guest Additions in the Guest OS, explaining that he had to make it available in the VirtualBox CD drive before booting.

Well - he didn't understand file extensions and didn't know the difference between a zipped file and an .iso - I told him to find the .iso of Guest Additions and make it available to the guest OS before booting and call me over and I'll look to make sure it's OK.

Well - while I'm looking over his shoulder, telling him not to, he installs the Guest Additions software in the HOST Operating System - Windows Vista - and reboots - while I'm sitting there telling him not to do that - please stop - but he continues on.

Lesson - **do not install Guest Additions in the Host OS** of anything - it made the OS (Vista) crash, no mouse, keyboard buggy - pretty much he's gonna have to reinstall if he can't get it recovered.

I told him not to. I sat right there and said don't reboot - let's uninstall this software.

I tried to tell him that Vista will label an .iso file as an .iso file and NOT a zip file.

He thought otherwise.

This is pretty much my experience with all Windows users. They rarely know anything about computers, and really don't understand software, even on their OS of choice. The ones I run into that want to try something other than gaming or surfing end up borking their systems bad and blame free software and Linux, if they are messing with Linux.

That's my story for today.
