### Post by fatfox on 2008-06-10
Okay fellas - I feel this is a worthwhile issue to raise.

Re: All the rubbish floating around about it being 'illegal' to install Mac OS on anything but a bona-fide Apple Computer.

Let's look at the license, shall we?

"2. Permitted License Uses and Restrictions.
A. This License allows you to install and use one copy of the Apple Software on a single Apple-labeled computer at a time. This License does not allow the Apple Software to exist on more than one computer at a time,and you may not make the Apple Software available over a network where it could be used by multiple computers at the same time. You may make one copy of the Apple Software (excluding the Boot ROM code) in machine-readable form for backup purposes only; provided that the backup copy must include all copyright or other proprietary notices contained on the original."

Let's look at this closely - which people have clearly failed to do - it translates as ONE COPY PER MACHINE.

So far so good.

That Machine must be.................."Mac labeled".

There you go.

If you buy a copy of a Mac OS, and install it on your computer, it is NOT ILLEGAL as long as the machine is "Mac Labeled".

Now I've been around enough frivolous lawsuits to know that this element of the license agreement is wide enough to drive a bus through.

That means, even on a not-very-stretched construction, that as long as you have a Mac sticker on your box, the police won't be kicking in your door and dragging you away to computer prison.

It's clearly not prima facia illegal - we'll only find out the real legal position when a judge somewhere makes a ruling on the Apple license agreement and how far it stretches - and of course that ruling will only apply in that specific jurisdiction.

And I think I'm fairly safe in saying that day will never come - who is seriously going to grant a warrant to any law enforcement official on the basis that they suspect someone is running Mac OS on a non-branded machine.

Just think about that for a minute.

Enough of the paranoia about discussing the subject and closing threads etc. 

It's ridiculous, grounded in fear, not thought through, and inhibits the discussion of a perfectly valid area of interest.

Even using the word 'illegal' is going way to far - we're talking, in the very worst case scenario, about a technical breach of a license agreement, for which Apple would have to prove has cost them financial loss.

It's never going to happen. Period.

And it's not illegal, at least in my opinion - and I'm an IP Barrister so I should know - if you buy a copy of the OS and stick an Apple sticker on to box somewhere.

Note that it doesn't say "equipment purchased from Apple" or "Manufactured by Apple".

It says labeled.

Let the games commence.

### Post by dakira on 2009-02-21
Hi,

sorry for digging up this old topic, but I think I have something to contribute:

1. The license debate
License agreements as used by Microsoft, Apple and others are completely void in various countries (like Germany). In the USA ist might be possible that a company can dictate what you do with stuff you bought with your own money, in Germany it is not. When you (physically) buy something it is yours to keep and do with it as you wish.

That means in Germany it is perfectly legal to use Mac OS X on non-Apple hardware. It is not legal to crack OS X, though. But qemu has been patched to allow unmodified copies of OS X to run. Anyway, my point is: Not allowing this topic would be similar to not allowing discussion about restricted codecs because they are illegal in some countries.

2. OS X as a guest
It works with VMware and VirtualBox. For VirtualBox your processor needs to support hardware virtualization (like VT).. there are images floating around on the net for vmware which you can just as well use with VirtualBox (a few workarounds are necessary)
