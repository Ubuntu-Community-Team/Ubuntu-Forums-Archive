### Post by Ares Drake on 2008-04-23
I have good experience with Vmware Server. It is free (though not open source), works reasonably fast with virtual Windows machines, is rather easy to install and maintain (even across a network) and there are plenty of howtos around. It even offers limited passthrough of real hardware to the VMs. I am currently investigating Xen, as it seems to offer even more in this regard. It seems to be a little harder to set-up though. In case anyone has a good howto of xen with hardy or can tell me how to use virt-manager with xen in hardy (can't get it to connect to the local xend) please let me know.

Edit: Tried virtualbox as well, the closed source version is nearly as capable as vmware server I think. Afaik it doesn't do SCSI passthrough and you cannot manage it via network-GUI like vmware-console. But for other scenarios it might well suffice.
