### Post by Paqman on 2008-08-03
> **Dark006 said:**
> is it just better off to dual boot them?

Depends what you want to do. Virtual machines hog a stupendous amount of resources, since you're running two systems at once. So you'll need a fairly powerful machine, and even then you'll take a massive performance hit. If you're not trying to run anything too dependent on good performance then it's a really handy way to go. Otherwise dual boot is the best bet.

I use VirtualBox to run XP just when I need Office (stuff like school projects where I need 100% compatibility).  It slows stuff down some, but usually when I'm using something like office I'm not multitasking too much to notice the difference.  That is with 1 GB of RAM. If you need Windows every day, I'd keep the dual boot.

If you need it once a week or less, or only for one or two minor programs, use virtualbox.
