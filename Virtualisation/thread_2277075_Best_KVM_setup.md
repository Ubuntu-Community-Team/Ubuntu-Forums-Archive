### Post by TheFu on 2015-05-07
[http://www.linux-kvm.org/page/Main_Page](http://www.linux-kvm.org/page/Main_Page) - lots of great things there, but there is an assumption that you understand Unix administration with all the techniques that entails.  It is hard to jump into the middle of a book and expect to understand the story.  Perhaps I'm just dumb and slow. Took me about a year to even begin to understand Unix administration and I had an expert nearby to help. It was hard, frustrating. After all, why couldn't someone just make a GUI like I already knew to translate all that Unix-talk-crap into normal terms?

A few yrs later, things actually began to "click" for me.  Knowledge was building on knowledge and experience. Simple things actually became simple. Small issues were quickly corrected and I'd finally learned to understand manpages.  I learned new things almost immediately, because they made sense.  **For almost everything in Unix, there is an elegance.** I still uncover that daily these ... 20 yrs later.  For 40 yrs, people learning server administration have been humbled by the flexibility and capabilities of Unix. I don't see that changing. There is no simple way, except to practice and gain experience surrounded by others with similar goals. I've met people who jumped in and took LPIC training, then passed an exam, but realized they really had just touched 0.005% of what they needed to understand.  There is much to know and it is impossible for any single human to know even 50% of it.
