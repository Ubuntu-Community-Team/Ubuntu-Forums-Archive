### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-01-15
No problem!  Just do not try to share home as different versions of apps will cause problems.  You can either just use links from jaunty to karmic for your data files (docs, photos, music, videos, etc etc), as I do, or make a shared partition which is auto mounted in both by editing /etc/fstab.  You will be using grub2 by default if you install karmic after jaunty, but that is no major problem, as both OSs will show in the menu.
