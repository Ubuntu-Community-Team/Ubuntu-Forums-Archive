### Post by darlinsk on 2008-12-19
Like many on this forum, I've wanted to drop Windows and go completely with Ubuntu.  Unfortunately, there were some tools and functionality that I couldn't duplicate in Linux and was not willing to give up.

So, I tried vBox.  It was free, simple to install and got rave reviews.  It's cute, and worked for some things, but didn't resolve my problem.  I could attach my network drives, and run programs that didn't depend on external devices.  Unfortunately, I couldn't get my label printer, Sonos desktop controller, or Blackberry desktop manager working -- which made it a no-go for me.

So, I finally broke down and tried VMware Workstation 6.5.  What a great product!  It just works and is laden with features that allow you to really customize your configuration.  It comes with a Network Editor, a VMware Player, Workstation and full documentation.  I'm not sure why people say it is difficult to set up.  It seems quite straight forward.  In a fairly short time I was able to create the virtual machine, install XP, and get all of my "problem" apps working.

I like free software as much as the next person, but I won't blink an eye shelling out my $189 when the 30 day trial is over.
