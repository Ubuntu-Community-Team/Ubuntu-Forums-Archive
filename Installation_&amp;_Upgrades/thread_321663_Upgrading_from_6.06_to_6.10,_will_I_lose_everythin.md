### Post by jbus on 2006-12-19
Technically it won't remove or delete anything in your home folder if you do the upgrade correctly. Though, it is possible that you could have problems during the upgrade that would leave ubuntu in an unbootable state at which point you might have to use the live cd/recovery tools and decide what to do from there to ensure your files wont get erased. But it would be better to backup before doing this.

I have upgraded two Dapper machines to Edgy with no problems. Though these were both machines that were pretty vanilla and didn't have any third party repos to complicate things.
