### Post by Irvine_himself on 2010-01-19
As someone who recently migrated from windows, Macafee's SiteAdviser is one of those utilities I really miss.  I find that WOT is a very poor substitute. Since as Linux/Ubuntu users we are moderately imune to malicious downloads, its main use would be in detecting phishing scams and secure shopping.

A potential workaround is to add SiteAdviser to the search bar (there is a FireFox add-on to do this, see my signature.)

For a general web ratings visit [http://www.siteadvisor.com/sites/ubuntuforums.org](http://www.siteadvisor.com/sites/ubuntuforums.org) right click and add it to the search bar. 

Unfortunately,  [http://secureshopping.mcafee.com/](http://secureshopping.mcafee.com/)  doesn't seem to work from the search bar, but I am not giving up on that yet.

It is not quite as functional as having SiteAdviser installed, but it does allow one to easily check a commercial sites rating before visiting and/or giving out confidential information.
