### Post by slickymaster on 2013-05-15
> **nosmokenofire said:**
> Thanks for your help Slickymaster. I'm a bit of a novice, could you please explain what I do with the link you gave me.

Many thanks, Alistair.

You have to download the necessary files for the upgrade. Between the files inside the kernel directory you need just 3 of them. First get the _linux-headers file that ends with “all.deb”_, then get the _linux-headers file that ends with “i386.deb” or “amd64.deb” depending upon your computer architecture_, and finally get the _linux-image file that ends with “i386.deb” or “amd64.deb”, _again depending upon your computer architecture.

After downloading the 3 files you will _install them by order_, and **make absolutely sure that you install them in this order**:

1- Install linux-headers-3.9.0-030900_3.9.0-030900.201304291257_all.deb (ending by all.deb)
```
sudo dpkg -i linux-headers-3.9.0-030900_3.9.0-030900.201304291257_all.deb
```
2- linux-headers-3.9.0-030900-generic_3.9.0-030900.201304291257_ (headers ending by i386.deb or amd64.deb depending on your computer architecture)
```
sudo dpkg -i linux-headers-3.9.0-030900-generic_3.9.0-030900.201304291257_i386.deb
``` 
3- linux-image-3.9.0-030900-generic_3.9.0-030900.201304291257_ (headers ending by i386.deb or amd64.deb depending on your computer architecture)
```
sudo dpkg -i linux-image-3.9.0-030900-generic_3.9.0-030900.201304291257_i386.deb
```
After all being done, reboot your computer and run:
```
uname -r
