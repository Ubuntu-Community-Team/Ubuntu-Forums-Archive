### Post by Finch75 on 2007-07-11
one of the key questions is: How fast is your internet access? Are you still on a 56K modem? :-)
If you are, your plan certainly makes sense. If you're on 2 MBit/s or faster, a different plan would probably be better...

I'm also new to Linux, but I've noticed that many packages are updated quite frequently. By the time you have to do the next reinstall (? - which will hopefully take a long time!), most of your downloaded packages will already be obsolete again.

I've seen an "export option" in Synpatic which will export your list of installed packages. That will not save you the download time, but the "manual configuration time". You just download and install everything you like, then export the configuration. Next time you reinstall, you can just run that script. It will take a while, but install the latest versions of all your packages (if I understood that one correctly :-))
