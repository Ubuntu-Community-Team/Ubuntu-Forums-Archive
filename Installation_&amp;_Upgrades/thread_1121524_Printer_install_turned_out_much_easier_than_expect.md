### Post by AlanRick on 2009-04-10
I bought a fax,wlan printer from brother and installed it on Windows with no problems. I tried to find a ubuntu driver on the brother web site but they only had mac and windows.

Almost gave up.

Searched ubuntu forums and web. Eventually found [http://solutions.brother.com/linux/](http://solutions.brother.com/linux/)
with a driver but the instructions looked difficult (what is "cups", when to use "cups" and when "lpr"...)

Almost gave up again again.

As a last resort I leafed through my ubuntu book and read "In the old days.. CUPS... Things have changed".

Sure have - I followed the book's instructions "admin, printer" duh! and less than 60 seconds later ubuntu had found the printer on the wlan (God knows how), found a driver (not the exact driver but a best-fit) and installed it.

Printing works perfectly. 
:guitar:

Just goes to show - give the easiest approach a chance before even beginning to research a solution.
