### Post by prshah on 2009-11-20
> **rjt11890 said:**
> windows 7 doesnt give me the option to boot from usb. It just goes straight into windows 7

Booting options are controlled by the BIOS, not Windows 7. You can enter the BIOS with the F2 (usually, on eeePCs), or you can just keep tapping ESC as soon as your computer powers up (before the Windows 7 logo), and a menu will popup; you can choose your USB drive from that menu if it is recognized, and Ubuntu will boot up.

If it still doesn't work, maybe your USB flash drive is not correctly setup? What guide did you follow to setup the USB flash drive?
