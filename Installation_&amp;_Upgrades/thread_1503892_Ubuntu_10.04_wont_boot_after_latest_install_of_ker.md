### Post by arubislander on 2010-06-07
As you've updated your kernel.. do you get a screen at boot time that lets you select which kernel to boot? If you don't turn off your PC, then turn it on and press the Shift key (any of the two should do, but go with the left one, just to be sure). You should get a black screen listing kernel options. Try booting from the older one. Then at least you'll have your machine back in operation.
