### Post by Sef on 2006-05-06
Any way you update will result in the same system.  

First before you upgrade, back up your data.

Best way is to do a clean install. Burn Dapper and install it. You will need to reinstall your codecs and any other changes you made to Brezzy.

Dist-upgrade can work, but there is a higher chance than with a clean install to break your system. You can keep your changes.

(From another post of mine.)

### Post by aysiu on 2006-05-06
To add to Sef's post, if you do decide to reinstall, consider creating a separate /home partition.

That way, for future reinstalls--even though you may have to add in a few more programs and codecs--all your settings and files will remain intact.

If you're already prepared to reinstall, though, there's no reason to not at least try a *dist-upgrade*, as that... if it goes smoothly... will be easier than reinstalling.

For instructions on how to upgrade to Dapper, go here:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/dapper](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/dapper)
