### Post by floke on 2007-02-04
Open terminal and type:

sudo apt-get install kubuntu-desktop

This will add the KDE (Kubuntu) desktop to your ubuntu setup.
Logout, and when you are logging back in, select 'Sessions' fro m the login screen.
Select KDE - set as default or whatever you want when asked.
Enjoy!

This won't replace ubuntu, but will add the KDE desktop - i.e you can now choose between KDE and Gnome.

### Post by floke on 2007-02-04
Now now...there's no need to get rid of gnome altogether - at least not until you're really sure you only ever want to use KDE - which, if that was the case, then why not just install Kubuntu to start with? It's not like disc space is a major issue or anything.

When I first switched from gnome to KDE I thought - 'how could anybody not love this? why on earth would anybody want to use gnome?' - I just couldn;t understand it (having used gnome for about a month before the switch). Then, after about two months in KDE I switched back to gnome for a bit - just for fun you understand, loving the choice and the fact that I could (was also trying out other desktops like XFCE and E17). Then, realised that gnome seemed a lot calmer, didn;t crash for no reason (actually didn't crash at all), didn't bombard me with pointless apps that I had no intention of using, and didn;t make me wade through swathes of menus to get to what I wanted. I've never been back in KDE since (though will of course have a play on KDE 4).

The point of this is this: you should experiment and explore a range of desktops, and there;s no reason to go ditching any of them really unless really hassled for space. It's good to switch when you get bored. Variety is the spice of life and all that. I still have KDE on my system (K3b kis great and I love Konqueror from time to time), even though I'm a gnomer (for now).
