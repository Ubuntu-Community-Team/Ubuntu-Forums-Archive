### Post by mingus on 2005-07-07
Sure.  Here are the instructions.  Read and following carefully:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RecoveringUbuntuAfterInstallingWindows](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RecoveringUbuntuAfterInstallingWindows)

If your system can boot from floppy, it is a very good idea to make a grub diskette, too.  Include the optional step for menu.lst under #2.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RecoveringUbuntuAfterInstallingWindows](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RecoveringUbuntuAfterInstallingWindows)

Finally, also good to have an XP install CD handy (or a W98/ME/DOS bootable floppy), in the outside chance that your MBR gets hosed.  Easy to fix with this media.
