### Post by jtwing on 2007-05-24
I haven't used Xubuntu, but based on what I know about bash, and window managers in general, I can answer your question pretty definitively.  The first thing to realize is, you're asking the wrong question.

The bash shell won't be different...bash is essentially bash, no matter what version of Linux or Unix it is running on...if you notice any differences at all between one bash shell and another, I'd be very surprised.

What is different is the X-Window manager.  The X-Window manager is the part of the software that manages the windows, icons, menus, and pointers of the graphical user interface (GUI).  There are many, many different window managers available for Linux.  The default window manager for Ubuntu is Gnome.  Kubuntu uses the KDE window manager (hence the 'K' in front of the name), and Xubuntu uses (I believe) XFRE.

The main differences you will notice between Ubuntu and Xubuntu are the locations and contents of menus, there may be differences in the filesystem windows, and the icons may be different.  These are very customizable, so if you prefer a certain look-and-feel, you can probably adjust your settings without much difficulty once Xubuntu is installed.  I know that XFRE is designed to be a light and fast window manager, targeted at older and less-capable hardware, so you'll probably see a significant difference in speed between regular Ubuntu and Xubuntu.

Other than the look-and-feel, pretty much everything else should be the same.

Good luck!
