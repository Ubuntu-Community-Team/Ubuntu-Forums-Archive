### Post by Lux Perpetua on 2006-08-08
The IBM ThinkPad T43p also works quite well. The main drawback I've found is the ATI graphics card (relative lack of good Linux drivers), but that's only going to be a problem if you need to use 3D video capabilities (e. g., playing 3D games or using XGL). It's not even that big of a problem; 3D acceleration works, but the best drivers now are still somewhat buggy, which can lead to some aggravation. 

In all other ways, I consider myself lucky to have unwittingly chosen a laptop that is so Linux-compatible. 

Also, as a very minor point, this laptop does not have a "Windows key" on its keyboard. :-)
