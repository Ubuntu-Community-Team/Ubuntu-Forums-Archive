### Post by TheFu on 2023-07-04
I've seen that when I didn't choose a window manager/DE before login.  While that might be what you are experiencing, I'd put the likelihood around 5%.  

95% likely it is something else. I'd guess you'll need a Try Ubuntu flash drive to get into the system and review he logs to see what happened. [https://www.howtogeek.com/196740/how-to-fix-an-ubuntu-system-when-it-wont-boot/](https://www.howtogeek.com/196740/how-to-fix-an-ubuntu-system-when-it-wont-boot/) has lots of ideas.  You could also run the boot repair tool - have it create a boot-info log for others to review. Perhaps they will see something?

### Post by ubfan1 on 2023-07-04
Also try a virtual terminal (ctrl + alt + Fn3) to get a text login to try out your password.  Most likely it's a video problem.  From the text login, see if things like apt upgrade show things 9nvidia drivers?) being "held back".  Phasing sometimes gets in the way of a complete video driver update.
