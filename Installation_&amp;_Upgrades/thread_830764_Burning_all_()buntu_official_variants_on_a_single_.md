### Post by Metaleks on 2008-06-16
I would imagine that it wouldn't be possible.  When you boot from a cd/dvd, your computer searches for a specific file that's needed to do whatever the cd/dvd you have burned is supposed to do.  Having multiple files will more likely than not throw a wrench in that process.  That, or maybe just defaulting to a single installation of the many flavours of ubuntu.  Or maybe there is a way around this process!

So, give it a try!  You have nothing to lose, and everything to gain :)

(Also, can't imagine why you would want to do this, ubuntu, kubuntu, xubuntu, edubuntu, and studio are just a few synaptic clicks away from each other in the original ubuntu.)

Post back on whether it worked or not if you decide to go through with it.  It'll be interesting to see what happens.
