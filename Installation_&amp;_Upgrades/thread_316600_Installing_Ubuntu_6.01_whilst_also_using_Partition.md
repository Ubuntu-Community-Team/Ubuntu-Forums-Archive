### Post by jexxie on 2006-12-11
Also, since you probably have Windows installed on the entirety of the hard drive, just use Partition Magic to resize your Windows partition.  Resize it to leave enough space for Ubuntu (say 5GB just for playing around,) but don't create the partitions, let Ubuntu do that.

In the Ubuntu installer, create your swap partition (two times your RAM, unless you have 2GB ram, then you don't need swap), then use the remaining space as your /

Good luck!
