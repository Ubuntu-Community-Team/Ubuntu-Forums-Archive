### Post by rillip on 2007-05-25
There are a ton of guides out there for installing Ubuntu.  Here are two I've seen linked to often:

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing)

[http://www.futuredesktop.org/](http://www.futuredesktop.org/)

As for what you should have on hand, I would pretty much say all you will need to know is your partitioning scheme.  You'll need to be able to recognize which partitions are which by size alone, since you won't see the contents, and presumably want to keep their contents.

As for what to run, you computer looks very modern.  It should be able to run Ubuntu, Kubuntu and Xubuntu with no issues.  You don't mention the actual RAM for the system.  AS long as it's over 256MB you should be fine with Xubuntu, 512 recommended for Kubuntu and Ubuntu.

### Post by meng on 2007-05-25
Before you install, ask yourself why you're trying Ubuntu and whether you can afford some time to fiddle with things and optimize your system.
I believe you're more likely to enjoy Ubuntu if:
1) You have multiple reasons for wanting to make the change ("I hate Windows" alone just doesn't cut it.)
2) You don't mind spending (up to) hours to sort out (mostly minor) problems.
In other words, don't switch to Ubuntu the day before some important deadline, or examinations!
