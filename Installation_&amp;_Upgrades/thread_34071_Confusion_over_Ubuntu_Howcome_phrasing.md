Hi Nick,

Ubuntu (the Live CD, Hoary & Warty installation ISOs) contain everything you need on the CD to get going.  The beauty of Ubuntu, IMO, is that their distribution does not contain every type of browser, office app, media player, etc. (which is why some Linux distros are 3 CDs or more).  The developers at Ubuntu have selected a key set of applications (tweaked them for Gnome --- or KDE if you're using kubuntu) and made a very intuitive interface/menu system for the "MS convert."

So, just pick an ISO --- the Live CD, if you're not ready to dump Windows all together or Hoary 5.04 if you want to install it on your hard drive (btw, you can still keep Windows & dual boot if you wish) and then burn it to CD.  Set your BIOS to boot from CD, insert the ISO disk, power on and take that mighty leap of faith!

Be prepared to scratch your head, do a little cursing, do a lot of searching and asking of questions in this forum, but be patient --- there are many knowledgeable people here who will help you.

Before you begin, you may want to do a "system inventory" of your hardware to get makes & models of things like your video card, modem, NIC, sound card, etc.  I would say the biggest issue for many people is getting the sound card working, so being able to post make & model would be helpful.  A free program like Everest (formerly AIDA32) from [www.lavalys.com](www.lavalys.com) ([http://www.lavalys.com/products/overview.php?pid=1&lang=en&pageid=1](http://www.lavalys.com/products/overview.php?pid=1&lang=en&pageid=1)) will be able to grab all the info you need.  Run it before you start an make a printout as it will come in very handy if you run into any difficulties.

-Dave
