### Post by bulldog on 2007-03-07
If you're new to ubuntu and Linux in general,I would advice you to download the 32bit version of Ubuntu.
The 64bit version is a little harder to get to work properly.

But the main thing is to get a good copy of your .ISO which has the size as listed on the ubuntu download page.
You have to burn it on a low speed to decrease the chance of a bad copy.

You can install Ubuntu next to your windows without a problem.
But it's always a good thing to make a backup of your important files in case of something goes wrong.
