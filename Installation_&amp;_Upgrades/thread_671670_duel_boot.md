### Post by Zimmer on 2008-01-19
> **Pap3rbag said:**
> didnt get one

So, you have not downloaded an Ubuntu ISO yet?

I suggest you take this very slowly.

If you wish to try out Ubuntu then   [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download)

Download a Desktop Edition (aka Live CD)

and burn the ISO to CD.  Further details in this post (plus much much more)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=232059](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=232059)

The resulting CD (if you adjust your machine's BIOS to boot from CDROM first) 
will boot instead of Vista and you can explore a version of UBUNTU running from the CD and leaving your Harddrive and Windows installation alone.

The software will run more slowly than a proper installation or Dual Boot, but it will give you a chance to see if your hardware, sound etc.. will work..

Hold off installing/partitioning etc. until you have read enough to feel confident you are not going to do something to jeapordise your Vista installation (or until you are confident you have it backed up and could re install it.
 
Take your time...

Oh, and Vista Beta 2 was probably a name for one of the testing releases of Vista before it was launched to go on sale...

..and dual booting should not affetct the performance of your Vista install..

and you can never do too much reading..[http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu](http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu)

As someone who has never seen Vista I do not feel qualified or competent to guide you personally step by step through the  dual boot install.  But if you search the forums I am sure there are posts from people who can offer better  help and advice.
