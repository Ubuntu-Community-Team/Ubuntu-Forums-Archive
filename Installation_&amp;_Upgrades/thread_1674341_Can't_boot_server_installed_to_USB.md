### Post by f22buntu on 2011-02-10
I just got the board and am troubleshooting something else but I thought I might comment on something that I saw in the BIOS.  There was a setting to enable booting from usb apart from other settings in the BIOS version that came on the board.  I have since updated the BIOS and that setting went away and now it just recognizes usb drives and places them in the boot list.

If you have not done so up date the BIOS.  It's easy just put the .bio file on a thumb drive and press F7 on boot up to get in to their flash menu.

### Post by ezzy1980 on 2011-03-16
the problem is solved... ( or I should say there is a workaround intels bios bug)

[http://communities.intel.com/message/118448#118448](http://communities.intel.com/message/118448#118448)

the bios is looking for a fat partition as one of the primary ones before it allows boot to continue.

It full details are at the intel link.  The FAT partition is just there on the partition table.  Nothing else.

And lo and behold, all works.. :)

working with 10.10.

Good luck to all others......

Ezzy
