I recently switched a box from Debian sid to Ubuntu 5.10 - since the swap, my NFS mounts have gone to hell. Poor response all around, with I/O errors paramount.

The other boxes in the network seem to be fine. Just this ubuntu system is screwing up. I did change its hosts.allow, adding:

portmap: 192.168.0.0/255.255.255.0 
rpc.mountd: 192.168.0.0/255.255.255.0 

something I'd never had to do before.

What have I missed here?

---

### Post by emperor on 2005-10-28
The default UDP protocol has caused many folks problems lately. I added the "tcp" option to fstab on the client side and it seemed to help, especially in the mount time. TCP is just more sure-footed!
