### Post by darkod on 2013-01-23
sda or /dev/sda is your hdd. I/O errors would usually mean errors reading the disk.

You either have a corrupted ISO of ubuntu, or something is going on with the hdd. It might be just a loose cable, check the cables. Or it might be dying on you. Just because it worked fine yesterday it doesn't mean it will last forever. :)
