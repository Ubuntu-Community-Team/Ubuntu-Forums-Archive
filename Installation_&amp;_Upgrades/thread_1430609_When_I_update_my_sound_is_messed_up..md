### Post by thegod_slayer on 2010-03-16
hello

it was a problem which i have also experinced.
the case is that when we update our kernels get updated.
linux keeps a backup of kernels which can be seen while booting.
a list of kernels are shown on a black and white screen.
my problem was my laptop couldn't keep any backup of the older kernels.
and the new kernel had some problems or bugs
which uninstalled my alsa drivers.
so i stopped updating and now my lappy is working more than fine.

one more thing-----
do not install the proprietary hardware drivers in the system->hardware drivers section.

they may also be a problem.

everytime the the update manager comes just close it.
