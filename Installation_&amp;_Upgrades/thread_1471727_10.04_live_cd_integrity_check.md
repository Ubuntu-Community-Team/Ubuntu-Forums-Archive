### Post by eotakos on 2010-05-03
So, if you are new to Ubuntu, you want to install 10.04 and you decided that you want to do the "CD integrity check" (which is highly recommended), you most probably ended up reading this article  

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/CDIntegrityCheck?action=show&redirect=CDIntegrityCheck](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/CDIntegrityCheck?action=show&redirect=CDIntegrityCheck)

but you realize that the menu that is illustrated at the first picture doesn't show up. Instead, you are directly started with the live session, and two options, either to install, or to use the live session. (at least that is what happened to me)

the thing is that the menu is there, but hidden (for good-looks I suppose). All you have to do is press any key when the accessibility icon is displayed at the bottom of the page (early on at the boot-up process).

don't pass up the integrity check!  a faulty disc may prove to be way more time-consuming than the test...
