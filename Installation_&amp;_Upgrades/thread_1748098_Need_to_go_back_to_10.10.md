### Post by lechien73 on 2011-05-03
Hi,

If you boot from your Ubuntu 10.10 disk, you will be given the option to install Ubuntu - replacing your existing operating system.

Before you do, make sure that all your important files and data are backed up, since the installation procedure will wipe your hard drive.

For future upgrades/installations, it would be easier if you have /home as a separate partition (documented here: [http://uri.tl/e](http://uri.tl/e))

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-05-03
Then, your PC is not really booting from the disk ...

When it does that, you eventually get a screen with two icons on it.  One says Try It, the other says Install.  You should then click the Install icon.

You need to check your BIOS settings and confirm that you are booting from the CD.

### Post by lechien73 on 2011-05-03
When you restart your computer with the CD in the drive, do you get an option to boot from the CD? If so, that is what you should choose. If not, and your computer goes straight into Natty, then you need to check your BIOS - look at the very first screen when you switch your computer on to see how to get into the BIOS setup.
