### Post by raymondh on 2009-08-27
Are you putting/loading the Ubuntu LiveCD in WHILE windows is running?  IN that case, you are trying to do a WUBI-install which is dependent on a healthy, working Windows.

Decide whether you want to dual-boot (each OS in their own partition) or solely-boot Ubuntu.  And then, boot into the liveCD, manipulating your BIOS prior so that the cd-drive boots first (without windows loading up).

Whilst in liveCD, you may also TRY UBUNTU WITHOUT ANY CHANGES .... that gives you the chance to test out your system vis-a-vis Ubuntu (wifi, graphic card, etc).

If I understood your issue wrong, I apologize.
