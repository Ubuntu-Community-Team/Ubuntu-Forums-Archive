If you have wiped the partition, the data may still be there.. It sounds like you want help with data recovery. 

[http://debianhelp.wordpress.com/2011/07/05/how-to-use-photorec-for-data-recovery-in-ubuntu-linux/](http://debianhelp.wordpress.com/2011/07/05/how-to-use-photorec-for-data-recovery-in-ubuntu-linux/)

You can use the free Ubuntu software in the above link to do-it-yourself, or you can ship the computer to a data recovery company to do it for you. If you want to preserve your data that you have lost, turn off the computer. Boot from a live stick of Ubuntu (hopefully you have made a rescue stick of Ubuntu) as to not do any more read-writing to the hard drive that contains your missing data.  Use the instructions in the above link, or turn off your computer and call a data recovery company to ship out your computer directly to them for support. The more you use the computer that contains your missing data, the likelihood of it being overwritten accidentally FYI.

Reinstalling Windows will do more harm than good if you want to recover your data back that you erased.
