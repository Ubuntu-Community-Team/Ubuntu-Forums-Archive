### Post by az on 2005-12-11
That is a perfect description, but allow me to give you some perspective.

Debian is the reason why Ubuntu exists.  Debian is both slow-paced and stable and fast-paced and bleeding-edge.  Ubuntu takes a small subset of Debian developmental branch and puts work into them to make them stable.

The packages are maintained and stable when released, although their debian counterparts are still unstable.  All the work gone into those packages by Canonical/ubuntu gets back to debian.

All of the other debian packages are compiled and put into the universe repository (or the non-free restricted and multiverse repos).  They do not have an ubuntu maintainer, but they are overseen by a group of experts called MOTU (Masters of the Universe).

MOTU is a small band of packaging ninjas who make universe work.

My experience with universe is that it usually works fine, and when it does not, it is not something serious which will cause you data loss or anything.  Subjectively, universe is more stable than a lot of other linux distributions' stable release.
