### Post by aof_doomchild on 2006-05-02
I recently tried installing KUbuntu 5.10.  First, a slight amount of setup to the story:

I have an Apple USB keyboard and a Razer Copperhead USB mouse plugged into said keyboard.  The keyboard is plugged into one of the USB ports in the back of my machine.

When the installer started and presented me with a screen telling me to hit enter to begin installing, I noticed that the keyboard was having no effect, and the lights on my mouse were off (telling me there wasn't any power).  After trying a few things, I whipped out an old PS/2 keyboard and got the job done.  When told to reboot, I unplugged the PS/2 keyboard, thinking that maybe there was just some odd fluke with the installer.

After KUbuntu put me at a login screen, I realized that, once again, neither the keyboard nor mouse was working.  On a whim, I plugged the keyboard into the other USB slot, and everything worked like a charm.

I'm not sure if calling that a bug is right or not, since I got it all to work, but it seems odd that one port should work when the other doesn't.  Is this some shortcoming of the kernel, or just something I didn't configure properly?
