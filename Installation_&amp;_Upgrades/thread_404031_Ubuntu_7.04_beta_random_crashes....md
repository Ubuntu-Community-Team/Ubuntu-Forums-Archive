### Post by slayerboy on 2007-04-10
My suggestion would be that if you tried it in another computer and it's doing the same thing, then you might want to look at how you burned it/with what software).

I've seen a LOT of problems with cd's burned at max speed and end up as coasters even though it looks like verything wrote fine.  I burn CD's at slower speeds, especially Live CD's.  I usually go to the lowest the burner or disc will let me go.  It takes longer, but you pretty much are ensured and error-free burn everytime at a slower speed. 

Also, some Windows burning software is horrible on ISO's.  And, of course remember that this is still beta and is prone to bugs until the final release, even if it worked in VMWare.
