### Post by Qui on 2007-07-23
on switching from windows, i found that things did not go smoothly at first. however, what i did invest in was a second hard drive on which i installed ubuntu. 

dare i say this, but i had to return to windows to get to the internet such that i may troubleshoot ubuntu on several occasions during the installation and configuring process.

hence, my recommendation: making the switch? invest in a second hard drive/partition. it will save you many a sleepless nights. [being without an internet connection is terrifying - i think i might be addicted :(]

in addition, ubuntu automatically mounts the dos formatted hard drive for you after installation such that you may have access to the files in your old windows my documents folder.

finally, keeping windows around after switching to ubuntu can be useful especially for those hard to find windows based program on linux. [collecting odd, useful and useless things is as much an art as a sport-science]
                                                                                                                      Qui
