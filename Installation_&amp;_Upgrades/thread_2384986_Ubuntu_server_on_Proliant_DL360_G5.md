### Post by cruzer001 on 2018-02-15
I ran them years ago, no recent experience.

My advice is to stick with Xeon processors, the old Xeon's are Linux friendly, I had several.  Watch out for the really old ones, they had a really slow front side bus (FSB).  I believe the G5 could hold up to 4 processors, if you get one try to get it with 4 processors installed or you will need to buy the cage and old hardware is hard to get and parts can bring a high price.  Although old Xeon's should be dirt cheap.

Also look at the old IBM servers, they are Linux friendly and I did like them the best back then.

The biggest downside is high power consumption and NOISE.  LOTS of fan noise with 6, 8, 10 fans.

I now run a Lenovo Think Station, the older models are cheap, around $200 US and up on eBay (depends on what you get).  They have low power consumption and are very, very quite.  Again Xeon (4,6, or 8 core), three drive slots, can have lots of ram.  IMO a better investment.
