Video Card <> Monitor

Yes.

Monitor (hardware), resolution 1920x1200,1680x1200...  connected to 3 PC's.

Machine 1, Sandybridge Chipset, 1920x1440 and 1680x1200... So it never matches the aspect ratio between the hardware of the pc and monitor in the top three modes.  That is not an Ubuntu thing.  Running any Operating System on this box, with this video hardware, going to this monitor-- is going to get the same thing.  Another driver might be able to stretch or shrink it, but the *native* resolutions just don't match up between the two.

Machine x+, well they might have native resolutions (hardware wise) that match or come closer to the native resolutions and aspect ratio's of the monitor... or their drivers stretch or shrink the aspect ratio to fit the screen.

Let me explain it this way (since you also have nvidia)... Lets say I have an old nvidia card, a Geoforce2 (what about 10 years old or more...  Displays that existed then where somewhat square right?  Now I coonect it to a Widescreen HDTV... which is comparitively almost those two squares put together.  They are not really going to match up right are they.  And if the driver change the aspect ratio to do it, it is really going to look strange,

The sandy bridge is fairly new compared to nVidia and ATI .  People had had a lot of time to work out things for them in opensource and such.  There are some open source drivers, such as "[here]("http://intellinuxgraphics.org/2011Q1.html")" but I don't personally know how reliable the are, nor if they will adjust aspect ratios...

What will help you it go to [Panning Viewport]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Config/Resolution#Panning%20viewport") and follow those instruction to be able to use _**all of your usable screen**_.  That way when you move your mouse to that part of your screen, that the screen will pan, instead of your mouse disappearing.
