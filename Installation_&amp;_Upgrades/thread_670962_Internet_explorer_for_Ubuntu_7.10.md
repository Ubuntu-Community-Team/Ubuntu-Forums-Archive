### Post by seanmiller on 2008-01-18
I agree that ies4linux is excellent... I have IE5, 5.5, 6 and 7 running (IE7 isn't officially supported, but if you Google you'll find how to do it)... they are not 100% the same as Windows, though... some things, like transparency, don't work quite as expected and they're more for testing rendering than anything else so don't assume that they'll solve your issue.

I do wonder, though, whether you should be doing this homework at all... as a matter of principal I'd simply tell the school/college or whatever that I was unable to do it because it didn't work in Firefox... they really ought to be giving you work to do online that doesn't rely on a particular browser... you could end up spending loads of time getting IE working, then the homework, only to find that all your fellow pupils using Macs take a stand and get away with it.

Also, what, exactly, is this homework?  And what's the problem you're getting with Firefox?  Could it be a missing plug-in or something, rather than the browser?
