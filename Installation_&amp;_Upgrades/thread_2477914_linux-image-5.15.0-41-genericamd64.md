### Post by TheFu on 2022-08-11
Kernels come from kernel.org with light touches by Canonical.

There is a change history available for each kernel.

There are newer kernels available and we can pick any that we like to run, within compatibility of our hardware/drivers.  Proprietary drivers, say from certain GPU makers, often don't play nice with just any kernels. This is why we know to stick with the formally supported kernels released via APT repos by Canonical.  OTOH, 2 of the big-3 GPU makers have their drivers included in every kernel and those tend to "just work", providing much more freedom for everyone.  Someday, that last GPU vendor may finally let their drivers be included in the kernel, but I won't be holding my breath.
