### Post by renzokuken on 2007-05-02
1. they are both debian based and fairly similar but i would recommend Kubuntu as being easier. there is better support for kubuntu than mepis too IMO.

2. yes you can. you could have XP on one partition, Kubuntu on another and Mepis one yet another partition. the Grub bootloader allows you to choose which one to boot into when you turn your PC on. note though that they would not be installed on E:\ and F:\, they are windows naming systems and not used by linux. but it is fairly straight forward nonetheless.

3. I would say so yes, just follow the instructions. it is a whole lot quicker than an XP install as well
