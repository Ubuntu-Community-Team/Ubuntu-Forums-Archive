### Post by starcraft.man on 2009-08-22
A standard vanilla Ubuntu install requires about 2.5 GB. I generally suggest 10 GB for the lifetime of a standard user as root (excluding home partition, allowing a lot of room for installation and waste). Since your brother is just trying it, and I assume won't be downloading massive amounts of HD quality video to watch, he should be able to get by with 6 GB as an absolute minimum. If he wants to download a lot of extra pacakges like kubuntu-desktop or xubuntu-desktop to install alternate DEs, tell him to give at 8 or 9 GB. Linux requires a much smaller install compared to Windows over a lifetime. That's my 2 cents.
