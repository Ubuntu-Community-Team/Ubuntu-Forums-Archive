### Post by sikander3786 on 2010-10-23
Hi and Welcome to the community :-)

What is the source of installation? CD or USB?

Please provide hardware details specially RAM and Graphics Card.

Check the integrity of downloaded Ubuntu image.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM)

If the MD5SUMs match, check the disc for defects. There is an option on the first page where you choose to "Try Ubuntu" or "Install Ubuntu". If you don't see that page automatically, hit Enter at the black screen with a man in circle and a keyboard to see it.

If the disc is defected, burn a new disc at the lowest possible speeds.

### Post by sikander3786 on 2010-10-25
Your RAM is not quite upto the mark to hold regular Ubuntu desktop.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements)

You can try some other light weight flavors of Ubuntu. The one I will recommend is Lubuntu.

[http://lubuntu.org](http://lubuntu.org)

Some people will recommend Xubuntu but I don't find it that much light weight.

You can also go for a minimal install and later add LXDE as your desktop manager but Lubuntu will be an easier choice.
