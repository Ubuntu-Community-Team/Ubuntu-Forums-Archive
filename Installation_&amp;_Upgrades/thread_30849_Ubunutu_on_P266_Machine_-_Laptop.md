### Post by az on 2005-04-30
You have enough disk space and enough ram.  It may be slow, but that all depends on what you like and what you expect.

You can always install the default system and add Icewm, fluxbox or XFCE4.  You will still have all the ubuntu utilities available, you just do not need to boot into the whole gnome environment all the time.

You can run things like the gnome-volume-manager to autodetect and mount usb drives and cdrom and stuff in an Icewm or XFCE session....

You can also use abiword instead of openoffice and xterm instead of gnome-terminal.  Rox-filer, mc or xfe instead of nautilus....

Endless, I tell you!
