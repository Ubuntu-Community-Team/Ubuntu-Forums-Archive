### Post by ruggersway on 2007-10-18
You can download and resize your vista partition using gparted [http://gparted.sourceforge.net/download.php](http://gparted.sourceforge.net/download.php) , or if you have partition magic you can resize the windows side.

I usually use partition magic, but if you don't have it gparted should do the trick.  I have had gparted fail a few times - which is why I tend to stick to partition magic, but gparted usually works.

--you could always just blast vista...I've been dual-booting vista and ubuntu since vista released and I still haven't configured my windows account....I doubt you'd miss it, I know I don't.

Good luck
