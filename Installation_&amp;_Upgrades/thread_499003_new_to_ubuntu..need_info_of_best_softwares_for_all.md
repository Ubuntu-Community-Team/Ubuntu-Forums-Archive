### Post by AAM on 2007-07-12
you are new to ubuntu!

there are something like 18,000+ programs in the repositories (along with Debian). Different people swear by different software. The only (repeat ONLY) way to find out the 'best' is to try them out for yourself. I use Rhythmbox instead of Amarok because I don't really want to mix my KDE and GNOME applications if not necessary. Others will tell you that there are better things than Rhythmbox and Amarok, and others will howl them down.

Linuxland has this nice (bad?) feature that you can try everything and make up your own mind. A more reasonable question is to ask what programs are used for each of your categories so that you don't miss anything.

PS I happen to agree with K3b & VLC
