### Post by Herman on 2007-11-16
In any case, regardless of whether you can fix your Ubuntu install or not, you still need to know how to make a backup or you are wasting your time using a computer at all, sooner or later you will lose all your files somehow if you don't have a backup.

About how to make a backup of your Ubuntu, you need at least your ordinary files from your /home/username directory, (of course! ), and also your .evolution files and your firefox bookmarks.
Here's a link about how to do that, [GUI method of backing up]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p13.htm#overall_plan").
Sometimes there are some extra files that you might want too. For example, I use 'password manager', so I also need to find the file for that in 'hidden files', and make a copy of the file for 'password manager' as well.

If you are in a hurry you can copy your entire /home/username directory, it makes it faster to make the backup and you can just get the files you need from it later when it's time to restore.

As for installed software, here's another link about that, [Back-up and Restore Added Software]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p13.htm#Back-up_and_Restore_Added_Software").

Regards, Herman :)
