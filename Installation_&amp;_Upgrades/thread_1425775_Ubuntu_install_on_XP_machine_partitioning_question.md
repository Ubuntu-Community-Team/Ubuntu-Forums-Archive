### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-03-09
It's always worth having backups, of course, of all your important personal files, but when you install Ubuntu and get to the partitioning page, choose to do it with manual partitioning, I can't remember the exact wording on ther screen, but it will be obvious.

You will be presented with a listing of all your current partitions on the disk(s) and when you click on any one of them you can choose to edit that partition.  Leave all other partitions untouched and all the data on them should remain as it is when you start.

There is a very good web page on doing all this at [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installseparatehome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installseparatehome).  This page is for a making a separate /home partition, which is definitely worth having, but it does also explain all the procedures of manual partitioning at this stage of installing.  If all your data is already on other existing data partitions and will remain there, you may not want a big /home as it will only really contain configuration files and folders.

Have a good look at this and come back again with any other questions.
