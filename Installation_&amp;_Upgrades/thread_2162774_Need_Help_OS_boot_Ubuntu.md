### Post by fantab on 2013-07-16
First of all, what Hardware does your "old computer" have? Like CPU, RAM, Graphics...? 
Have you checked the integrity of the downloaded Ubuntu.iso with [MD5Sum Check]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM")? It can be case of BAD BURNING. Try burning it again to the USB, (by the way how did you Burn the .iso to the USB?) Try [Unetbootin]("http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/") or [Win32 Disk Imager]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/win32diskimager/") to burn your .iso to USB.
If you have RAM less than 2gb then Ubuntu is not good, you must try **XUbuntu**. Or if your RAM is less than or equal to 1gb then **LUbuntu** is your best bet. 

Some 'old computers' cannot boot from USB drive, even though they can read and write to them.
It is important that you give us as much info about your 'old computer' as you can.
