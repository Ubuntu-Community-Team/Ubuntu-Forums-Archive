### Post by linuxbastard on 2008-12-26
Hi, as previously mentioned, we would need a better idea of what you're machine is.

If you are getting a "no signal" message on your monitor, you could check first that your video cable is plugged in tight and that your video card is also securely seated.

There are no interactions between the OS and your bios during the POS stage of your machine, so you should still get to a video test and then a motherboard test even before the OS boots.  If you don't get even the video test usually it's an issue with cable or card.
