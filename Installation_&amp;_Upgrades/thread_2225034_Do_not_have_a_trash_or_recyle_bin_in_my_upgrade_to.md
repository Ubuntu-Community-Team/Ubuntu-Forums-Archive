### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2014-05-19
I believe there is a setting in gnome-tweak-tool for that. But here is something better, open Nautilus, go to Preferences > Behaviour and check include a delete command that bypasses trash. IMO Trash or as you called it "recycle" is one of the most stupid design, probably taken after MS's recycle. When you delete something you want it gone, not to move it to another folder where it will be forgotten.
