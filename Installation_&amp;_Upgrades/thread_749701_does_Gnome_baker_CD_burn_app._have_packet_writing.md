### Post by Perkins on 2008-04-11
A simple breakdown for you then:

CD-R:  Writable once.  If you make it multi-session, you can keep adding to it until it's full, but data cannot be erased from it.

CD-RW:  Writable multiple times.  You can use it like a normal CD-R, but you can erase it and start over.  Generally more expensive than CD-R.  Less stable for long-term storage, more susceptible to fading and such.  But they can be cheaper in the long run than CD-R if you make use of the erase and reuse feature.

Packet writing is a scheme for writing the data to the disk that allows one to add and remove files.  On CD-RW / DVD-RW it lets you delete specific files from the disk without having to erase the entire thing.  I have heard several people swear by it, and several others say to stay away from it if you value your data.  It would not seem to be strictly necessary for what you're wanting to do.
