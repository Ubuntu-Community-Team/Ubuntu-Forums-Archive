### Post by tht00 on 2008-03-16
> **Petrock6 said:**
> Turns out I made the dumbest mistake, turns out it was the i386 disk but it's a AMD64 computer.:lolflag:

Actually, I would recommend i386.  It's compatible, simpler, and the performance gains of a 64-bit OS is minimal.


Your other statements through the thread make it obvious you don't know what you're talking about.

Debian is not out to get you.

Graphical errors usually signify a graphics card driver problem, but it could be more complicated.

Partitioner crashes are unfortunate, but I've had them when dealing with Ubuntu liveCDs for some reason.  Try another liveCD to get your partitioning done, like this: [http://gparted-livecd.tuxfamily.org/](http://gparted-livecd.tuxfamily.org/)

If Debian works and Ubuntu has problems, the obvious thing to do is to use Debian.  If they both have problems, then you may have a hardware conflict or it could just be a bad configuration setup -- to get help, make specific and in-depth threads for each topic and resolve them one at a time.  Just a few words of wisdom when looking for tech-support on forums.

Happy Linux hunting. :smile:
