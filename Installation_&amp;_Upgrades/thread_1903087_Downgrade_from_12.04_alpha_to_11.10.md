### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-01-01
> **maddyui said:**
> Hello,

I am a big fan of ubuntu OS and has been using it for a long time for my commercial and personal use.

Then, you should do the following (to prevent this problem in the future):
1) Change your Update Manager settings to NOT show pre-release versions.  Basically, any "long time" user should know better than to upgrade to an early Alpha version of anything.  Guess you did not.  At least this change will prevent such future problems.
2) Understand that, unlike with MS Windows, there is no simple "roll-back" capability between OS versions.  Once you upgrade, you're basically stuck with the new version.  In future, you can prevent this problem by using Clonezilla to image off your current (i.e., WORKING) version to an external drive, and if the upgrade causes serious problems, you can restore the old version in a few minutes.
