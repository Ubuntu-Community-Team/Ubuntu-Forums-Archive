### Post by Doobie9 on 2010-08-04
Not that I know of--at least on the hardware side. I've taken hard drives out of Linux machines and booted up on different machines with no problem. With Windows, IIRC, the hardware detection is some kind of anti-piracy feature--Windows wants to 'lock' itself down to a single computer or something. I've actually hosed a few Windows partitions just by resizing them; since they were just for games, I just reinstalled :P. Windows is always the 'disposable' OS because of this, hehe. [Yes, I get a demented pleasure out of it.]

There are several 'remastering' options for Linux: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_remastering_software#Linux_utilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_remastering_software#Linux_utilities)

While I haven't done this myself (I plan on getting around to it), it may be helpful to investigate some of these utilities. I've heard remastersys being mentioned a lot.
