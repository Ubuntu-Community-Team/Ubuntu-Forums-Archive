### Post by linuxslate on 2013-01-25
I also have one of these (Toshiba u845W), and I have exactly the same problem.

12.04 install went fine (momentary problem getting the SSD and HDD set up the way I wanted).

1st update got me to the 3.2.0.36.43 kernel, and .... Screen distortion.

I have just been picking the old kernel (...0.29, I believe), in GRUB every time I boot, and it is fine.

Any other Kernel should be OK.

I like the machine.  Everything else works fine (12.04 is the only OS on it), and I like the ultra wide screen.

WiFi is very good "out of the box",   3D acceleration works fine.

I am using only the 32G SSD, and even cold boot times are in the few seconds.

I am having some problems with errors from the synaptics touch pad control panel, but the touch pad is still usable.  I have a mouse for intense work, but that is the nature of touchpads, nothing to do with this machine or Ubuntu.

I have also largely "fixed" many of my complaints with Unity.  I have normal (useable) scroll bars, Menus in each window, and Window control icons on the Right.  See this page:
[http://www.techsupportalert.com/content/tips-and-tricks-ubuntu-after-installation-ubuntu-1204.htm]("http://www.techsupportalert.com/content/tips-and-tricks-ubuntu-after-installation-ubuntu-1204.htm#Create-Advanced-File-Manager")

Not only that, but when it dies, I'm going to bolt some wheels to it, and I'll have a cool skateboard.
