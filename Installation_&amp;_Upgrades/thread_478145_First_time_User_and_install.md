### Post by jrev on 2007-06-19
The guide you mentionned is no good.
The best is to resize your harddisk beforehand with a Gparted downloaded live CD 
You start your computer on that CD and you can visualize your Hard disk beautifully

Of course before partitionning you have to defragment the hard drive from Windows itself until all your data are gathered on the beginning of the C partition (usually the only partition of the disk )
