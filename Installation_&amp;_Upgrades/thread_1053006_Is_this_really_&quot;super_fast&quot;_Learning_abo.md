### Post by snowpine on 2009-01-28
Hi there, Xubuntu uses the same "core" system as Ubuntu, so it is approximately the same speed. There is a small performance boost (I would estimate 10%) over Ubuntu due to the Xfce desktop environment instead of Gnome, plus some of the "heavier" applications (like Openoffice) are not included in a default install of Xubuntu.

Ubuntu and Xubuntu are great, but they are among the slower Linux distros in my opinion. You definitely owe it to yourself to check out other options if speed is your #1 concern. I personally find SliTaz to be the fastest "usable" distro on my hardware. Puppy and DSL are also quite nice. (Fluxbuntu is based on Ubuntu, so it doesn't fall into this "lightweight" category by my definition.) The reason these tiny distros are so fast is that they are loaded entirely to ram at boot time, which minimizes disk access.

Just my 2 cents.

(edit) ps Windows XP is almost 10 years old; the current version of Xubuntu came out in October of last year. They are designed to work on very different hardware, which explains why XP may be faster on your computer.

Also, to answer your question, being a dual boot does not degrade performance at all. Only one OS is booted at a time. :)
