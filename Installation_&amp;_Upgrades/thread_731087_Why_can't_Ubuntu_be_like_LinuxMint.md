### Post by gfahey on 2008-03-21
Don't get me wrong, I love Ubuntu and been using it since Breezy but, LinuxMint has it all over Ubuntu.

Now, I realize LinuxMint is Ubuntu but, the install and everything else just blows Ubuntu out of the water

Case in point (and my biggest gripe) is wifi setup. I managed to get it working in Gutsy but only after hours of work and forum searches. Even then, it was "iffy". It never worked right and I had to constantly sign into my home network. Tried Hardy Beta this morning thinking they surely have made this easier but, I was disappointed. I'm using a Dell Vostro 1000 laptop and while I realize Hardy is still in beta, you'd think this issue would have been front and center fixed. Nope.

I got fed up with Hardy and installed Linux Mint 4.0 back onto my Vostro. 15 minutes to complete install. I updated and then the Restricted Drivers pop up box appeared. I chose to install the ATI driver and also the Broadcom driver. Reboot and my wifi connection was working 100%. Total time? Less than 30 minutes!

Now, I don't know all that goes on with this at Ubuntu but, I for the life of me, I cannot figure out why this can't be as easy as it is in LinuxMint! Even installing Google Earth was a HUGE pain with Gutsy ( I never tried with Hardy Beta) but, in LinuxMint, their own software portal page does this in a snap and it WORKS! Sure, I love terminal and command lines as much as anyone but, LinuxMint seems to be on top here. Not a slam against Ubuntu either. I guess what I am trying to say is that I WANT to use Ubuntu but, LinuxMint, to me, seems to be Ubuntu done right. I mean, I have NO issues with it and it never freezes up. 

Come on guys, can't we get the same out of Hardy when final comes around? Is the wifi issue being dealt with?
