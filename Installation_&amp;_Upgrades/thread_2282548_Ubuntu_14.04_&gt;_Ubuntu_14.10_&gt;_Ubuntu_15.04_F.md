### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-06-15
Do a fresh install.

As you have proved, upgrading to a newer version does not repair a faulty system. It is not designed to do that. Upgrades work best if we are upgrading from a default installation to a default installation.

The problems on your system could have any number of causes. Memory, overheating, video driver conflicts. Those are just three that come to my mind. Create a new partition. Install 14.04 in that partition and if the problems remain then you know that they are hardware related. If they are gone, then you know that a fresh install will fix things.
