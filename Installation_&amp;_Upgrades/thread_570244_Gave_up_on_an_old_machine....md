### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-10-09
256 MB DIMMs can be hard to find.  They have double-thickness, so sometimes they don't fit in the sockets well.  You may be able to put 2x256 and that would give you 512 MB.  They're not that expensive, but they are hard to find.  I orded a few for some old HP machines a few years ago at upgradenation.com in Chicago.  I think they were $22 each.

I would get 1x128 and 2x256.  Try the 2x256 first and if that doesn't work then add the second 128 as a backup plan.  Do some research on your motherboard and you will see what it's max capability.  If you have an Intel 440BX chipset then you can normally take 256 per slot.

[http://kmandla.wordpress.com/2007/04/22/howto-set-up-feisty-for-speed/](http://kmandla.wordpress.com/2007/04/22/howto-set-up-feisty-for-speed/)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems) (mentioned above)

You could try the minimal installation of Ubuntu (i.e., just the base system + xorg) and then add the **xfce4** metapackage. It's just the basic stuff for Xfce. You can add more packages later. I'm not sure it will be fast enough though.

As kerry_s said, it will take some work and experimentation. Xfce is not the lightest of environments. You might be better off with just a light window manager, such as Fluxbox, Openbox, IceWM, etc.

If you don't feel like putting it together yourself, then you might want to wait for fluxbuntu, but personally I would see how well the base system + xfce4 OR a light window manager performs.

If Ubuntu isn't fast enough for you, then maybe try Arch or (as suggested by kerry_s) Debian.
