### Post by jchapman on 2007-10-24
My admittedly lowly Pentium III Compaq N160 laptop was running like a top with Feisty - appropriately named. Audio worked, on-board video rendered 3D desktop effects nicely. Everything worked well.

Enter Gutsy - bye-bye audio, bye-bye 3D desktop effects. 
Mix in a few hours across a few days looking for and following users recommendations for what appeared to be suitable fixes for my new issues.
Bake for a while and face the resolve that nothing short of dumping Gutsy altogether is going to get full functionality out of my laptop again.

What did I do? XP (I know, I know) has found its way back on the hard drive. No 3D desktop effects there either - but audio works (kinda important) and my PC card for wifi actually lights up now (there's actually lights on the card which indicate connection and activity). They never lit up under ANY version of Linux (sigh).

This leaves me in a very embarrassing place - having to explain to all the Windows users I've been bashing these past many months why I'm running XP again. Ubuntu, you were the ONLY Linux distro which worked fully with my laptop - now you've let me down.
