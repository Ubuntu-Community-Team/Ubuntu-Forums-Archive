One of the greatest additions to Gutsy (or maybe it was Hardy?) was the default inclusion of pdf printing.  A great little feature to show people who just decided to embrace linux.
Now, cups-pdf is once again somewhere in the repositories, but not installed by default.  I'm not going to judge before hearing other opinions.  In mind, however, the disc space saved by removing this package is negligible by most people's standards.  Further, pdf-printing ought to be default on any operating system.
I'm not saying this is disaserous, by any means.  It took all of about 30 seconds for me to apt-get install the package.  I just feel that making the effort to remove cups-pdf on upgrade and from the Intrepid disc image is nonsensical.  If you perform a search you'll notice plenty of individuals on these forums happen to agree.
Possible justifications?
