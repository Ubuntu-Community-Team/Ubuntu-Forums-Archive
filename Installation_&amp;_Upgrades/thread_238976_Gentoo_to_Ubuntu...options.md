### Post by &#12465;&#12452;&#12488; on 2006-08-18
I wouldn't run Ubuntu without at least 3 GB space for the root partition, or it'd run out of space pretty fast.

This is the reason why you should set up a separate partition for /home; when you format your system, or even switch to another distro (like you are going to do now), all files and program settings are kept with no hassle.

I don't really see the problem with deleting Gentoo, if you are able to copy all your files over to somewhere else, because the downtime is minimal. I quite recently bought a new laptop. After starting the thing up, I put in my Ubuntu 6.06 disc, and booted up the live system. The installer used about 14 minutes, and then I could reboot into my brand new Ubuntu installation!
