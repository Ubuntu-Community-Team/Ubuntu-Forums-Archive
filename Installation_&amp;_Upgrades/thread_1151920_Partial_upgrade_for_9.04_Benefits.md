### Post by sgosnell on 2009-05-07
It should list a new kernel choice, but you can fix that by removing old kernels.  The easy way to do that is via Synaptic - just mark the old kernels for removal, then click Apply.  I usually keep a couple of kernel choices available, just in case.  I currently have the .28, .29, and .30rc4 installed.  If one becomes unusable, I can still boot from one of the others.
