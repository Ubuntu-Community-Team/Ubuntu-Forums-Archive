### Post by oss_monkey on 2006-07-08
Greetings, everyone.

Right now I'm in the middle of updating the software packages in my newly installed Ubuntu. I write for others who, like me, want to get Ubuntu up and running in their laptops (or desktop, really) but may encounter trouble, especially if things are going wrong.

**These are the things I recommend each new convert do:**
[LIST=1]
[*]**Search the forums and other websites for technical problems you might encounter (especially with hardware).** I learned a lot about my Toshiba on this forum mainly, and the common errors that happen.
[*]**Download and burn the appropriate CD correctly.** This was the problem, and I didn't know it. 
[/LIST]
First I got the regular ISO, then when I booted up, I wondered why it never got to the desktop. I then saw that a system needs 192MB of RAM, so I got the alternate CD. When I burned it, it was worse: it didn't want to boot up at all!

Upon inspecting the CD, I found that there might be an error with the burning, so I did it again, this time on another brand of CD-R. It booted, yes, even installed, up until it hung at 6% when it looked for findutil. I ran the "Check CD for defects" item, then it appeared: a file corrupted. It dawned on me that there might have been damage on the CD itself, since the MD5 checked out fine for the ISO.

I tried burning again, this time on a perfectly clean CD, with not even a single shallow scratch.

Now, I'm writing my first post in the forums in the Ubuntu OS, with Firefox. Wahoo! :razz: 

Just to summarize:
[LIST=1]
[*]Read & search the forums.
[*]Download the **appropriate** ISO.
[*]Burn correctly at *slowest* possible speed.
[*]Run "Check CD for defects" at bootup.
[/LIST]

Enjoy your cup of Ubuntu!
