### Post by rajdoctor on 2008-03-11
Dear New Ubuntu users,

Greetings! 

I use Microsoft (Basic - Word, Excel etc.) I wanted to try Ubuntu. My company's IT told me that install Ubuntu in your home PC / laptop or an external drive. I got a second hand 20 GB external drive to install and try Ubuntu. My laptop is quite new - IBM 2007 model.

The steps I followed were:

1. I wrote an email to Ubuntu to send me a ubuntu software CD. ([http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu)). I got the CD in 2-3 weeks time. 
2. I selected a holiday for installation - so that I can install and try it out.
3. I attached the 20 GB external hard drive in my usb. Good also to keep the online internet cable attached.
4. I formatted the 20 GB external hard drive. (Start->My Computer->(right click on the external drive) Click Format-> Quick format.
5. Opened the CD drive, and offed the laptop.
6. I removed the internal hard drive of my laptop. It is very easy - just unscrew and slide or drop it out.
7. I put the Ubuntu CD in the drive and started the laptop. The external drive is already attached to the laptop.
8. The Ubuntu screen came, and I followed this link ([https://help.ubuntu.com/community/GraphicalInstall](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/GraphicalInstall)) Take print out and keep with you beforehand.
9. I did not create any partition on my external drive as suggested in the link (for beginners this is best), meaning I selected the option - Erase entire disk (Step 5 of 6 on the link) and installed the ubuntu.
10. It took exactly 30 minutes to install the software. It was fast. 
11. On top right corner is the off switch of Ubuntu. I clicked it and shut down my laptop.
12. I removed the Ubuntu CD from the CD-drive.
13. I retarted my laptop. Automatically the laptop booted from the external drive - Ubuntu. It was working. I clicked on Mozilla Firefox icon on top left bar. Internet was working. Great!
14. I switched off my laptop. I slided and screwed my internal hard drive again.
15. Re-started my laptop. I pressed F12 to enter BIOS setup - to change the booting sequence. I went to boot, selected USB, and pressed F5/ F6 to bring it as top option of booting. Save by F10 and came out.
16. Laptop started with booting from my 20 GB external hard drive. Worked fine.
17. Shut the laptop down again. Removed the external hard drive. Re-started the computer again to check the internal booting.
18. Everything works fine - as usual.
19. So now, whenever I want to work on Ubuntu, I shut my computer, attach my external drive and re-start the laptop. 
20. It works GREAT! Nothing of my internal laptop is disturbed, and still I can learn Ubuntu. WOW!

If you are as NEW as me, try this steps and if your laptop is new, the external hard drive is more than 10 GB - I think there is no reason why it should not be so easy...
