### Post by groggyboy on 2006-12-27
i remember reading somewhere that making an upgrade jump like that is bad.  personally, i'd suggest you download an iso of kubuntu 6.10, make backups of any important data, and do a clean install.  it's the best way to avoid any issues/breakage.  The upgrade from dapper to edgy is especially touchy, because a number of packages have changed their names, and won't nescessarily be brought along for the update.

if you still want to upgrade, follow these instructions on the wiki:
[Dapper Upgrade Instructions]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DapperUpgrades")
[Edgy Upgrade Instructions]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EdgyUpgrades") (these are for gnome/generic)
[Edgy Upgrade Instructions]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/KubuntuUpgrade") (these are for KDE)
