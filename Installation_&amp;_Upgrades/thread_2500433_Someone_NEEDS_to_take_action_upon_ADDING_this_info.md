YOU are that "someone".  Open a bug in the bug system with your complaints.  You care. You need to speak up.  [https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/report-ubuntu-bug.html.en](https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/report-ubuntu-bug.html.en)

BTW, nobody here works for Canonical.

Also, there are lots of download methods that support restart and incremental updates.  **zsync** and **bittorrent** are two.  Many http download clients support restart as well.

The upgrade is less than 8GB.  Does that help?  For most people, their smallest flash storage devices which are used to do a fresh install will be 8GB if they are 15 yrs old. Newer flash storage will be larger.  I use microSD cards for this sort of storage inserted into a USB adapter to make booting easier.  If my home internet isn't working, I visit the local public library and use their connection to download stuff.  A fresh Ubuntu ISO usually takes less than 30 minutes to get from their connection.  I pull the ISO from the local University Linux archive which is "network near" to me. They have huge pipes.

Just some options to consider.
