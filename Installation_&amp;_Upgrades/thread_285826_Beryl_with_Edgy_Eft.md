### Post by sloggerkhan on 2006-10-27
I got beryl to work in my EE install. It seems a little buggy. If I ever log out to the switch user screen and then log back in, it's pretty much sure to crash... but as long as I stay logged in it's fine.

My experience on installing it was that this guide worked perfectly:

[http://wiki.beryl-project.org/index.php/Install/Ubuntu/Edgy/XGL](http://wiki.beryl-project.org/index.php/Install/Ubuntu/Edgy/XGL)

even if not quite as clear as some. (It will tell you need a custom file for an xgl session as opposed to a gnome session. It's hard to tell what you've tried from your post, but that could be the missing custom file.)

Beryl really has way too many options... a bit overwhelming.
That said, aside from the the logout issue, there are only a handful of other issues I have with it:

1. Most annoying issue: I can't seem to change my icons. I am not sure why... If I change them, they will not show up changed unless I logout and login in a non-xgl metacity session, which will probably crash unless it's the first session I log into after booting.

2. The fact that the interior of windows is still controlled by GTK 2 makes for some pretty funny looking combinations.

3. No mater what you do, it seems to only work with 4 desktops... no matter what you change the number to.

4. Fonts look bigger.

5. TOO MANY OPTIONS without enough explaination.
