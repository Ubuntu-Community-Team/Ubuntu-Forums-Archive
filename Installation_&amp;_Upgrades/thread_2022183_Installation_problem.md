### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2012-07-10
If you checked the md5sum and burned the CD at a low speed and cannot boot from the CD, I still suspect that you did not get a good burn.  
What are the specs of your computer?  Lubuntu doesn't take a great deal to load, but it does require a minimum of 192 Mbs of RAM to install.
I just reread your posts.  You said that you could not install but you didn't say that you could not boot from the CD.  Can you do that as a minimum?  If you can boot to Lubuntu, try it and see if all of the components work.  If you had two versions of Ubuntu installed you already know how to do that.  Lubuntu has a much lighter desktop and file manager.  If you cannot boot from the CD, you probably need to burn another CD or try to burn the ISO to a flash drive (if your computer will support that.)
