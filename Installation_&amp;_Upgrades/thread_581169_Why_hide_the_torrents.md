### Post by Eric the Grey on 2007-10-19
I've finally gotten started on my Gutsy download using Bittorrent and I have to ask...

Why on earth is it so difficult to locate the torrent files for the ISO images?  I would think that they would be placed on the main download page for easy access, not to mention taking some of the strain off the direct download mirrors.

Here are the steps I had to go through just to find them:

The main Ubuntu page at: [http://www.ubuntu.com/](http://www.ubuntu.com/) has the download link/graphic which takes me to this page: [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download) which lists the main downloads, and allows you to choose a location.  

You have to click the link below this to get the whole list of download locations here: [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/downloadmirrors](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/downloadmirrors) and then scroll all the way to the bottom of the screen to find a link to torrents.

From there to: [http://releases.ubuntu.com/7.10/](http://releases.ubuntu.com/7.10/) where I had to scroll down through a large list of files to pick out what I needed.

It seems as though using bittorrent to download the ISO images is a bad thing and the webmasters are hiding them to avoid people using it.  There should be a list of torrents right on the main download page.

Note:  I did try to find a mirror overnight while at work, but none of the ones I tried had decent speed, if they allowed a download to start at all.  I finally gave up.
