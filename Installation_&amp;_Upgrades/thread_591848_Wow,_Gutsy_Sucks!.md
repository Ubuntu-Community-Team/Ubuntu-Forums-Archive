### Post by davarino on 2007-11-08
Well, I guess I'll give my tuppence worth about Gutsy. (And Ubuntu.)

I am **not** running Gutsy on any machine except as a live CD: I do not believe in upgrading until it's utterly necessary.

I run Dapper Drake and probably will until its support time runs out or I get a new compu. My reasoning is (to me) impeccable. Ubuntu upgrades in the past have always given me grief - so why disturb the little peace of mind that I have been granted, simply to have the newest, niftiest system?

A few observations gained from considerable pain: [SIZE="1"](Learn from them if you wish)[/SIZE]

If you've never loaded up Ubuntu before, you'll probably have an easy time of switching over from Windex... possibly you'll need to fix a few hardware incompatibilities and learn a few things, but it is likely to be less painful than an upgrade from one Windex version to the next.

On the other hand, if you are *upgrading* Ubuntu Linux, you'd be wise to get your home directory onto a different partition, and then upgrade your present installation from the alternate CD. (I have never had any good luck with the online upgrading in the past.)

Even if you take those precautions on an Ubuntu upgrade, you quite possibly will have grievous problems - especially if you are one of those people who likes to add this piece of tinsel and that bangle and another little program all the time. (Remember, if a program didn't come from the Ubuntu repositories, or even if there isn't a "Package Supported" icon for it in Synaptic, your house of cards is *quite likely* to come tumbling down. It will probably be fixable, but it may take a *lot* of work to fix it.)

Another thing to bear in mind (which often isn't) is that ***every upgrade assumes that there is a slightly more powerful hardware system to run it than the previous version**.* It's true with Windex; it's true with everything Linux except for possibly the kernal. If you are getting "adequate" performance with your present Ubuntu version, expect things to slow down a bit (or even to gork [as in "God Only Really Knows"]) if you upgrade. Likewise if you decide to move to Kubuntu from Ubuntu, or Ubuntu from Xubuntu.

Upgrades are very similar to brain transplants... the body they control should be able to handle them - you don't want an 80-year-old running around believing that he's really only 17. Too many broken bones, moments of confusion, frustrations, and even unexpected death can result. (Though it can be interesting to watch.:))

I guess this is simply the old codger in me talking, but I don't see much advantage in upgrading Ubuntu every 6 months or every year. If you **really** want to add a certain piece of software to your system, consider downloading the tarball and installing it to your present system. It's much more dependable than any upgrading process that I have seen. (I did this with OpenOffice.org and I am roseate with happiness about my results.)

I _use_ my computer - it's really not my substitute for a hobby hotrod... so I can't afford for it to be down for more time than an evening. Because my experience has shown me that upgrading Ubuntu can take a week or more to get a functioning system going (even if it happens only rarely), I'm not going to take that risk.

I'd advise anyone else not to take the risk, either. Change when you need to, not because it's the fashion to.
