### Post by TheBuzzSaw on 2007-11-05
Well, I decided to try Kubuntu since I had virtually no experience with the KDE system. At first, I was very impressed. The desktop is very aesthetically pleasing, and the system runs very smoothly. I was convinced I would make the leap permanently until I played around with it for a bit.

First off, when I first installed Kubuntu, the sound worked fine. I had full audio support. In one update, it informed me that "libportaudio0" is no longer supported and will be removed. So, it removed my audio support entirely without replacing it with something similar. That was silly. Not to mention, it claimed it was upgrading to Gutsy Gibbon, which is what I had just installed. >_<

Second, Firefox felt unusually "detached" from the system. It did not function entirely. For instance, I could download files, but I could neither open them nor open their folder through the download window. My Google Toolbar bookmarks menu would never function. I suppose these could be Firefox-specific problems, but I had no problems with those in GNOME. Also, the text/fonts looked weird and oversized in various browser windows.

It seems that KDE has much potential, but it just did not serve me as well as GNOME. I will continue to tinker with KDE (because I am not closed-minded to new things), but GNOME just seems to get the job done better for me.
