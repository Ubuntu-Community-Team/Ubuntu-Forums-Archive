### Post by stchman on 2008-03-20
Hello all, I am creating this thread as to tell everybody that the Nvidia 8800GT cards works perfectly with Ubuntu.

I just built a new system using a Core 2 Quad and an 8800GT 512MB video card and Feisty works perfectly.  The mobo I used had a P35 chipset with a SATA controller and it too was supported out of the box.

To get the 8800GT working I merely installed the latest legacy Envy 0.9.10 and told it to do a manual install of the Nvidia driver.  The 169.12 was there.  From what I have read the 169.xx series were the only drivers to support the 88xx cards.  I let Envy do its thing and rebooted.  I had to change the xorg.conf to use my widescreen LCD ("1920x1200") and all is well.  I triple boot with this machine (XP for games, Vista for DX10 games, and Ubuntu for everything else) and even ultra modern vista needed a lot of drivers to get it to work.

So to all those folks out there wanting an 88xx nvidia card, they work with Ubuntu.
