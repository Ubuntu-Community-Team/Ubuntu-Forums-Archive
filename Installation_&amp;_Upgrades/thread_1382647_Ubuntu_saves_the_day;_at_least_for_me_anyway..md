### Post by wizarddrummer on 2010-01-16
I won't go into the extremely long story of how I got to this point; but the short version is that I have been having computer woes for months now. My little EVGA 780i SLI w/ 4GB RAM; 2 500GB SATA HD's; 2 EVGA 8800 GTX SLI Graphics cards; Intel Core 2 Duo four fans and some hefty power to drive all of it sits collecting dust because of a problem that I can't diagnose effectively because of my location and funds.

I purchased a budget computer; this one has had its share of problems mysterious reboots; freezes etc. That problem is being fixed and I am using a loaner. Its a Dell Optiplex that has no room to speak of; pretty tiny. 

I put my SATA from my broken machine drive in it and booted up with the version of XP that was already on the disk; Downloaded the drivers for the Dell and I was on my way. 

Well within a few hours that disk cratered due to some XP malfunction. It could not find some a pci.sys file. 

You do know that the old .sys files is a throw back to CP/M the crapola OS that Bill bought and converted it to PC-DOS.

So I was dead in the water and I remembered that I had just downloaded the Ubunto install CD. 

So with a 2 GB USB drive and a CD-ROM, I was able to get up an running thanks to some brilliant people working on this. Ya can't do this with XP!

It's been four years since I used Open Office. I haven't pushed it to the extreme (I have some wicked complex ACCESS databases, where local data is entered that gets uploaded to servers I wrote and stuffs the data into an on-line MySQL and then downloads and synchronises data that was entered on-line) yet so that will be interesting.

good stuff!
