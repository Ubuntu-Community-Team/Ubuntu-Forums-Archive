### Post by louieb on 2007-09-11
I put Ubuntu 5.04 or was it 5.10 on a P1 200MHz 128 MB ram. It was slow but functional. That PC was fairly picky In addition to Ubuntu I got Slackware, Puppy, DSL, and xUbuntu 6.06.1 installed on it. Slackware 11 with the 2.4 kernel probably worked the best. 

That machine maxed out at 128MB ram. If you have 256 I bet you have a slot 1 P2.  If so look  around for a P2 400 or better. the mom and pop shops around here give them away for 5 or 10 bucks. 

Right now I have a P2 400MHz 384MB ram. Its dual boots XP and PCLinuxOS. Its faster that the P1 was but nothing to brag about. I'm kinda old and slow myself so we kinda fit:).  

Whatever flavor of Ubuntu you try to  install go with the alternate CD. I haven't got a live CD to work on a PC w/256MB ram since version 6.06.
