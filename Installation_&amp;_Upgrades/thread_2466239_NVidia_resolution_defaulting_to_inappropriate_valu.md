### Post by moodog on 2021-08-23
I have been running a mythbuntu system for quite a few years now. It has an nvidia GeForce GT 430 in it. The computer goes through a Yamaha amplifier to a Samsung tv. A long time ago, I remember having to mess around with EDID to set the right monitor characteristics to ensure it ran at full resolution, however a year or two back that was no longer necessary, and it would automatically go to 1920x1080@60Hz.

A few weeks ago I nervously bit the bullet and decided to upgrade to 18.04. This went quite smoothly - no noticeable issues, and I was also able to upgrade mythtv smoothly.

I didn't leave the system running 18.04 for long before deciding to upgrade to 20.04, which also went smoothly. I removed the mythbuntu packages, and everything has been running fine, except for this issue.

I open the NVidia-Settings app, configure the resolution to 1920x1080@60Hz, and save the config to file. However after some time, or some activity, the monitor resets to Resolution: Auto. Unfortunately, this is a very weird resolution of 2880x576 - which is not good for watching tv. I then reset it, and it stays at the right resolution until, I believe, the TV is switched to (eg.) playing netflix or disney+, or blanks due to timeout or similar. 

What I've tried:
- editing xorg.conf to remove any auto modes, and only leave the required mode - 1920x1080@60 - this doesn't seem to have any impact - the problem still occurs
- disabling screensaver and screen blanking
- downgrading the NVidia driver to an older model (I read on some post that this solved a similar problem for someone) - max resolution is not the same with the older driver, but the problem still occurs.

The settings can be changed whether by the system Display settings or the NVidia-settings app - not sure if there's any difference here, or whether these two settings methods could be conflicting with each other.

Any advice welcome! this has definitely dropped the WAF on a system that's been running smoothly for many years now...

### Post by not-published on 2021-08-25
It's not really an "installation and upgrade question":  it's a MythTV question and an other-OS question.  I will say briefly:  both Xorg (i assume your using) and nVidia proprietary drivers (i assume your using) sense DISCONNECTS and RE-CONNECTS and take action.

Ubuntu, Gnome rather (i'll assume that too?) is likely not "triggered" to reset preferences when "new hardware" is present.  It likely has no idea the one hardware and the other are the same and might not even be notified.  That is a guess.

I've not tried mythtv but i assume it may have triggers on changes (hdmi device changed sensing).  So really:  one cannot say "X12 or Gnome or MythTV" without more information.

/var/log/Xorg.foo.log  will have "device dis-connect / device-re-connect" listings sometimes (which originated from the nVidia driver support)

2880x576   is absolutely ridiculous.  you can bet nVidia didn't do that* - and your TV likely doesn't support the mode and would have negotiated it last or never - so it's probably selected from a list of available resolutions Xorg spewed out - and some software chose it because it was "first on the list".  (new vesa, hdmi modes are very well engineered and nVidia drivers know them - they simply don't spew out bad resolutions randomly they are much more sophisticated than that)

sounds like either Gnome or MythTV did it.  i am now wondering if you ever edited your MythTV config files to give preferences.  also it may have worked on your past setup due to LACK of support (it never disconnected because of lack of ability to do so).

but of course  you may have found a bug in a new compile of MythTV.  however - i would say - continue your threat in the HARDWARE section

The Connected Monitor Option there is the one from the Xorg log that says your Samsung is located at, and will use that port explicitly for ouput and intput of EDID...

Deleting the "metamode"....because that is sometimes treated as a suggestion as preferred, instead of explicit, so still allows for auto-select.

Adding the modes to Subsection Display makes it more of an explicit call, and is supposed to use that mode, even if nothing is detected as being connected (your blanking screen). But you need to use whatever xrandr -q says the name of the mode is... Don't trust my edit for that name, as I can't see what is says on "your machine."
