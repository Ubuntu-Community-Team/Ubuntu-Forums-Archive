### Post by tal007 on 2009-08-28
I am assuming that you have burned your iso image properly. Merely copying it on to your CD or DVD will not not work. You have to select the "Burn image" option when you burn your iso image. Select a low speed option with verification if you have that on your program.

Before you try to install, you have to change the setting of your BIOS. You may have a "Boot option" in your BIOS menu. Things like:

Boot options( 1st, 2nd  and  3rd boot option ): 
         
           1. Floppy      [on / off / auto detect]
           2. CD/DVD      [on / off / auto detect]
           3. Hard Drive  [ on /off / auto detect ]

You have to set your "CD or DVD" to "on" or "auto detect" depending on whichever one you have. Mind you though, it takes time for things to show up on your display. It is not that fast with Ubuntu installation. It scans devices for several minutes. You will see some frightening messages on your display.  So, be patient. Other things that I noticed you can not auto launch a program from a DVD burned on CD drive. So, be aware of what kind of media you are using to burn and run from.

If it does not work, could be a hardware too. Ubuntu is not a mature system yet. Still evolving. Hopefully, in the years to come we will see better performance.
