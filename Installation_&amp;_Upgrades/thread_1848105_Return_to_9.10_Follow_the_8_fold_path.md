### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-09-22
There are a few points to consider before you think about going back to 9.10, the most important of which is that the version is no longer supported, so there will be no more updates, not even security updates for you and therefore the OS will be at some risk.  You must make up your own mind about the sense, or not, of going back that far.

I suggest you use 10.04 Lucid, the last LTS version which is supported until April 2013 on desktop and 2015 for servers.  If it is just the unity desktop that you dislike, have a look at Xubuntu instead, which is great in the 11.04 version.

However if you really decide after all that to use 9.10, here are answers to your two questions.

> 1) Return to 9.10. Does this involve just putting the CD I used to  install in the CD drive? I think that all I'll lose will be a few random  files on the desktop... any advance on that?Yes that is right, more or less.  You can salvage and backup those desktop files to a USB from either your current installation or using the live CD.
> 2) Follow the 8 points on the sticky at the head of this forum.
Again the answer is yes, but bare in mind my comments above about support.
