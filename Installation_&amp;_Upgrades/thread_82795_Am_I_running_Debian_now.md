### Post by duffman25 on 2005-10-27
Ubuntu is based on debian sid, so in MANY aspects they are very similar (system administration & manteinance). It's not advise to mix repositories because of binary incomplatibilty. So, if you question is related to administration & use, yes. Although you will be safer using ubuntu's wiki howto's because they are written specifically for your operating system. If you are thinking of miing binary deb's, then don't. It could lead to a unstable system.

And of course you can ask for help in a debian forum if you're polite & ask good questions. Debian people won't throw you away because of that (or at least they shouldn't!).

FYI I've just bought "The debian system" by martin krafft. It talks about the whole debian system & community & many things in there also apply to ubuntu eventhough the book is written for debian sarge (current stable release).
