### Post by angryfirelord on 2007-06-15
Are you sure that you have "fglrx" as your driver in your xorg.conf? If you installed the xorg-driver-fglrx packages, then that's the only thing missing.

If you did enable it and it still doesn't work, then run:
```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```
Select fglrx as your driver, accept all defaults until you hit the resolutions. Select the ones that are supported by your monitor. Then, on the next page, select Medium and the resolution you want to use. Log out and log back in.
