I am having trouble with the installation cd, which may very well just be a hardware error. The file hash of the download matched the official one posted. After burning onto cd and rebooting, I ran the feature for checking defects. It resulted in 2 errors. 
 
 I then switched to a different cd-rw in case it was a bad disc. First boot up stalled. Restarted and ran the check again. It gave me 1 error. The second check on the same disc/burned iso came up with 3 errors. 
 
 So I decided to make an iso image of the disc to compare against the downloaded image, to see if maybe my cdrw drive was having any read/write problems. The file hash matched the download one, which I confirmed to be the correct hash.
 
 Perhaps the drive's reading quality comes and goes, I don't know. But the burned disc seems not to have any errors in it (obviously read the disc right also.) On a side note, before taking a look at ubuntu I gazed at the live cd of kubuntu. I burned that one on a separate, external usb cdrw. The drive was relatively new (as in not used a great deal.) Having worked perfectly on every use, it broke after booting into into the kubuntu live cd. Even though I was quite upset, I figured it was just a nasty coincidence. It may still be a coincidence, but now I am wary.

 This isn't the first time trying ubuntu. The previous times went really well, just decided to stick with windows for the time being. I also used linux moderately back in college while doing research. All good until now. :(
