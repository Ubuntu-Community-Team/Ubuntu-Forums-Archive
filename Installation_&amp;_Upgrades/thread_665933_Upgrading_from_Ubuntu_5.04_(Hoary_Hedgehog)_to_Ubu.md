### Post by Crenfinkle on 2008-01-12
When you first turn on the computer, before it starts to properly boot, there should be a prompt which says something like "Press <F2> for setup." Look for that and press whichever button it tells you to. In the BIOS menu that comes up, look for something like "boot priority" or "change boot order" and move your CD drive to the top. Save changes and exit. Once it's all done, you can come back and change the order back to normal, or just leave it. 

What is happening here is that your computer is not configured to boot from CD before it boots from the hard drive. Or, you have a faulty CD.
