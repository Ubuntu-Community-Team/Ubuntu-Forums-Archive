### Post by crushdance on 2007-03-08
It's a bit rushed I know. But after installing linux(Ubuntu) and giving it a try I can say this:

Pros:
1) It is very clean and well organized. Much better so than windows and I like the way it run pretty well on any configuration, even though my computer is an up to date one. I can see why its popular because not everybody wants to or can afford to upgrade so much. I really love the low resource use.

2) Fast. Once you get past the boot screen and log in, theres virtually no moment of lag or downtime. I really admire the people who worked on this. Its very nice.

3) Getting help can be frustrating when you start off. Good idea with the forums it saved my life(literally) I almost panicked that I lost all my windows files.

4) The features are awesome. I love how you can take a screen shot and poof its right there on your desktop or whatever and the files automatically rename themselves if you have a similar file name. Awesome.

5) Installation is faster than windows. Way to cool.
Cons:
1) Most of the problems(the only) problem I encountered with Ubuntu is installation when it just wouldn't work until I realized that I had to reformat the previously made ext3 partition and recreate it. This is something that should be implemented on the live cd's and other distro's in the help section. It would really make trouble shooting easier. If it's a known problem then why not update the help file?

2) I don't know much about how drivers work. However some things that should be there are just not availible and that can turn people off big time. I for one was disappointed that it only had two display modes availible for my video. One was too high and the other was to low.

3) Compatibility...Oh I can't say this enough. It can't play MP3's outside of the box? er CD? I was kinda "O.O" 
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Overall I really like the open source community. But I just feel like it's not really for me at this time. I heard fiesty fawn or what not is coming out soon so I might check that out. I don't expect it to change overnight though but its still an option. Before I installed this my windows friends told me it'd be a waste of time and I have to disagree. Its a very slick and streamlined OS. Really if it could have some more support and "simplicity" out of the box I really think the open source community can easily pose a challenge to the big companies.

Anyways, thanks for the help and the chance to try it out. Have fun!:lolflag:
