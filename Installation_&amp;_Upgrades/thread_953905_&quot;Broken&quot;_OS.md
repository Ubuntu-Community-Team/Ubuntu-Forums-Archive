### Post by caljohnsmith on 2008-10-20
I believe your Firefox bookmarks will be in your firefox folder: ```
/home/<username>/.mozilla/firefox/<random>.default/bookmarks.html
```
Where <random> is just a random sequence generated by Firefox. From the Live CD, you could mount your Ubuntu partition and copy your bookmarks and other important files.

I would definitely just save your important files and reinstall if I were you. You should be able to go to the Places menu on the desktop, open up a file browser (Nautilus), and access your Ubuntu partition with it. But if you need help with it just let me know. :)

Assuming your wireless interface is wlan0, the essid (name) of your network is "mynetwork" and your router acts as a dhcp server,
```

sudo ifconfig wlan0 down
sudo iwconfg wlan0 mode managed essid "mynetwork"
sudo ifconfig wlan0 up
sudo dhclient wlan0

```
