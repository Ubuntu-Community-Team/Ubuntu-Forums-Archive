### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-03-28
You could try going into System --> Preferences --> Screen Resolution and selecting a lower resolution.

If that doesn't fix it, Ubuntu isn't loading the correct video drivers for your card or chipset.

In that case, you would have to find and install the proper video drivers -- which is why they provide the LiveCD to try this stuff out BEFORE you install it.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-03-29
It's your call, of course, but I wouldn't give up on Linux just yet.

I've experimented with installing different distros on several machines and found that the newer versions of Ubuntu have far more display problems than the older versions.

In one case, for example, I couldn't get anything newer than 7.04 to yield full screen resolution.

You should consider trying PC Linux OS or Mint.  I've had experience with both of them yielding better display results.  You can find them on the distrowatch.com website.
