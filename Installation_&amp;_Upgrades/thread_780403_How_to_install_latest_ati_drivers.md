### Post by esteckis on 2008-05-05
First check and see that your ATI driver is enabled, go to System-Administration-Hardware drivers and click on it. A window should open up showing the restricted driver that you are using.  The ATI should be listed with a green light and checked off (meaning in use).  If you have that, then go to System-Preferences-Appearance and click on Appearance. A window will open up and click the top tab marked Visual Effects.  Now this shows 3 items.  Now if you have a black dot by Normal or Extra that initiates Compiz Fusion which in my lame term understanding gives you special desktop effects. The ongoing problem with ATI and Compiz is that Video playback/ OpenGl screen savers/ ( I do not play games on my computer, but games might use OpenGl processes. I know Google earth crashed on me if I use normal or special effects and Google Earth uses OpenGl.  This is a compatibility problem with ATI and Compiz. To see if it is that problem, change you Visual effects to "none". Then try your game, if you do not get screen flickering, then you know it is that.  There was a slight work around I found where you can switch back and forth using a tray icon. here is the link  

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=780755](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=780755)
