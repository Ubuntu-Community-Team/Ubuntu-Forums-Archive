### Post by Gzus666 on 2007-11-28
well, no matter what you pull out of it, it still has to boot everything else. removing things and tweaking is a trivial speed boost. if the people you are demonstrating to don't understand that a major bottleneck in PCs is hard drive read and write speeds, kind of a waste of time anyway. plus OS booting speed isnt really because linux is superior in that sense, its just smaller than windows, with less to boot up. the real advantages are things most people dont understand, cause Microsoft hides them well enough where people think they are normal. 

think about it this way, remember how fast DOS used to boot up back in the day? nothing really to boot, so BAM, there very quickly. i would explain the advantages more so about memory management being better in linux, the kernel being better designed (this is more related to windows kernel being so poorly designed, all so he can preserve the source code) its more so that windows is so poorly made, not so much that linux is the best thing made. not knocking linux, i love the kernel, its great, just making an observation. once you show people the stability and speed of the actual OS when its been booted, most people with any computer knowledge will pick up on it. if you really want to show them something quick, i would go with gentoo, or sabayon since they lack the bloat.
