### Post by ddrichardson on 2007-09-12
Stop - do not do anything else.

Download disk imaging software - [System Rescue CD]("http://www.sysresccd.org/Main_Page") fits the bill. Make a disc image. This way you will not make anything any worse.

Next decide how valuable the data is. If its very important then consider professional data recovery. This is very expensive but involves techniques that simply are not available without specialist equipment - there are even systems that can recover a lot of data from smashed drives.

If not that cost effective then research the many recovery programs available online. The chances are that the data is still there but the decriptors and file tables are now on a different filesystem.

This may be beyond the capabilities of all but professional recovery I'm afraid - I take it your wife kept backups?
