### Post by aysiu on 2006-05-26
The advantage to a clean install is the 99.9% chance of it working successfully, as opposed to the 85% chance of a dist-upgrade working with no roadbumps. I'm pulling these percentages out of my ***, but I think they give you a sense of what you should do... especially if you're thinking also of switching to Xubuntu.

Create a separate /home partition, though, as that'll make future clean reinstalls easier on you.
