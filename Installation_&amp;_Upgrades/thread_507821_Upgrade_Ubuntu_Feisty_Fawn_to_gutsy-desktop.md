### Post by MrFSL on 2007-07-23
I agree whole-heartedly. Some time ago I was desperately trying to get Debian Testing or Unstable working and appealed for help to a different forum. I explained how I really wanted to "UPGRADE" to these newer versions. One of the moderators corrected me and said that using testing or unstable was not an "UPGRADE." A higher release number does not always mean an UPGRADE. Upgrade means moving UP in the sense of getting the new and IMPROVED. However, in many ways changing from a stable to an unstable version of your software can often be seen as a DOWN-GRADE.

Get it?
