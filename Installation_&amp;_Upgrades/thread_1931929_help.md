### Post by baumanno on 2012-02-26
It's totally up to you: whatever you feel most comfortable with!
Start off with ubuntu as it is, play around with it and get a feeling for it; then you can always get another window manager such as lxde or xfce.
On my part, I got tired of Unity and switched to xfce, which is mor lightweight, albeit a little less "shiny". Simply by going

```
$ sudo apt-get install xubuntu-desktop
```

I guess its the same with lubuntu (use lubuntu-desktop instead (please confirm, can't check atm)). Then you can choose, on startup, which WM to use just by clicking on the cogwheel-icon. Just google for "linux window manager" and you'll be spoilt for choice.

But your machine will cope with any of the available options, if that's what your afraid of.
