> **Autodave said:**
> Just gifted this by someone who didn't use it anymore.  Is there a Linux version that I can put on here and how can that be done?  It shows a 26G (?) storage, so I have plenty of room.

Not really.  The Amazon tablets have a very old version of Android. Think my 7th-gen Fire 8 was Android 4.5. You'll need to know the android version of your device.  Knowing the "generation" is a shortcut.  7th-gen means the tablet was released in 2017. You may be able to run a linux userspace under a chroot, but there are better android tools for CLI stuff.  A GUI would likely need to be run under a chroot and accessed using a VNC client from Android.  Last time I did that, it wasn't on a Fire Tablet and the keyboard mapping was off so much that it wasn't worth my time.  That's a bunch of added overhead on an already slow device mainly designed to hoc Amazon stuff.  I only used my Fire 8 with amazon to check on package delivery status and the "thank my driver".  I removed all the Amazon stuff I could and moved the other apps into a drawer that was buried on the last app page, so I never needed to see it.

OTOH, there are lots of Linux-like tools for Android that will run on Fire Tablets.  I hook up **f-droid** and selectively choose which APKs to install.  About a yr ago, one morning, my $50 Fire 8 just stopped working. It never worked again - no boot. No BIOS. No reset ever worked.  6 yrs is a long time from a nearly daily use tablet.  I lasted less than 2 days before I ordered a replacement.

Used that failure as an excuse to get a new Samsung A7-Lite tablet for $120.  More RAM, faster CPU, same screen resolution, accepts larger microSD cards and has a real GPS, unlike all Amazon Fire tablets made the last 10+ yrs.  GPS completely changes what I use it for.  Instead of a 5in phone or small Garmin, the 8inch tablet is much nicer while driving. Just which Navii wouldn't require the google app store to install, so I'm using less good OSM navigation tools for offline maps.

I'm completely addicted to the tablet and for me, 8in is the size. 7in is too small. 10in is too large. 

Think I posted a list of the apps I used in these forums a few years ago.  Mainly they work off-line with the ability to sync local cached data.  With wireguard as the VPN, I can connect over the internet back home to my systems to get anything I may have forgotten, assuming internet is available.

I do not connect the tablet with any financial data or even email.  I don't trust Android enough to do that.
