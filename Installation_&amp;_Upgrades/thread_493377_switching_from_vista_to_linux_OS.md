### Post by ninique on 2007-07-09
I've been using Kubuntu for a month or so, and yes it is pretty easy to use and install. Also, it's good because it's part of the Ubuntu family so you can find a LOT of support out there, since most of what you'll find for Ubuntu will also apply to Kubuntu. 

I suggest you take a look at this if you're unsure of what distro is good for your needs: 
[http://www.zegeniestudios.net/ldc/](http://www.zegeniestudios.net/ldc/)

This site also helped me a lot when switching to Kubuntu:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php)

Also, I have the same laptop as you (except it has 2gb ram), and although it's currently working on Vista, I plan on downgrading (or rather upgrading depending on the way you look at it) to a dual-boot of XP and Kubuntu. I've done a bit of research and I've found that the webcam doesn't work at all on Linux, and there are some problems with the sound in ubuntu (you have to fiddle around in the volume manager to get something to come out of the speakers) but I dunno about any other incompatibilities.
