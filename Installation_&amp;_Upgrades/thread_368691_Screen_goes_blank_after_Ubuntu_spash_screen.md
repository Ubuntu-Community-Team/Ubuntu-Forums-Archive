### Post by RHTopics on 2007-02-24
It is a good possibility that your /etc/X11/xorg.conf does not have your monitor setup correctly.  The standard "nv" driver should work fine for you.

I did a quick Google on your monitor specifications.  From what I found, it is capable of producing a resolution of 1600 x 1200 and should have the following frequency ranges:

H-Freq 30-85
V-Freq 50-160

From your description in trying to solve your problem, it appears you have already modified your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file, although you did not specifically state that you have.

If you have modified your /etc/X11.xorg.conf file and you did not make a backup, try to change it back to the original state.  And then check your 
monitor section in the file to see if your monitor has been correctly setup.

I have included my xorg.conf file with the monitor changed to match your monitor specifications so you can see what I am stating:
