Antivirus is a bit like a precaution against harm: there are rascals out there who will try to trick you into loading something onto your system that could do some mischief, ranging from a simple practical joke all the way to damaging the data stored on your computer. A good antivirus program goes some way to knocking it on the head before any real harm is done. The catch is that you will still need to use some good sense (e.g. don't open attachments or follow links in emails from people you don't know). Opinions vary as to its usefulness with Ubuntu, because Ubuntu is designed differently to Windows.
