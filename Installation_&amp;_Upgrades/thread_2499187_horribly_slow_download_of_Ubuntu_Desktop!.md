### Post by TheFu on 2024-07-18
> **retiredat44 said:**
> horribly slow download of Ubuntu Desktop!

There are so many details missing, that nobody can help.

What is "slow"?  Slow to someone on a dial up connection is different than someone at a govt site with 100 Gbps connectivity.

How fast do you expect?

Which mirror are you using? How close is it to your location - and I mean NETWORK location, not physical location.  Sometimes people 80km away from the mirror servers get routed through another country if the routing tables (outside our control) are messed up.

**Exactly which URL are you using?**  I prefer to get my Ubuntu ISO files from a local University mirror because then the link distance is minimized (about 35km).  However, that University throttles downloads for people outside their network to 2 Mbps, so I'll never see more than 2Mbps downloads.  That's fine. I let the download run while I do other things.  I think the fastest I've gotten any ISO the last decade is about 20 minutes. It can take time.  Is that slow?  I remember waiting overnight to get a few images files in the early 1990s over dialup, so no, I don't consider 20 minutes to get 700MB - 1.5GB slow. Expectations.  I remember how much faster my ISDN connection was, compared to what everyone else had.

Wifi is always slower for me than wired connections, so I don't use wifi if I can avoid it.

Is your storage nearly full?  Whenever storage gets over 90% full, everything starts to slow down a bit.  For really large disks, 95% full might be where the slowness starts.  YMMV.

Are other downloads of similar stuff (other distro ISO files) also slow?

Anyway, lots of questions that will help people figure out the issue.  It may be that you have unrealistic exceptions.
