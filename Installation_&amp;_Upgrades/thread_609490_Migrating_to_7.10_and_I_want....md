### Post by Herman on 2007-11-13
Apart from the obvious files to back up, you can also back up your firefox bookmarks and you email , including contacts, calendar, memos and tasks.

To back up those files you just open your /home/username folder and click on 'View', and click  'show hidden files. 
Then you should be able to see a folder called .evolution, and inside it are five directories you will want to save a backup copy of. Those are: addressbook, calendar, mail, memos and tasks.
When you re-install all you need to do is paste those into your new .evolution directory and you'll have all your email, contacts, calendar dates, and reminders.

For Firefox, you open .mozilla, and then open the folder in it called firefox. Inside that there's on with a funny name, like a password, everyone's is different. You open that folder and you'll see a file called bookmarks.html  and you can save a copy of that and transfer it to your new install too if you want.

There might be a few other things you'll want to make a backup of in your /home folder too, I use 'password manager', so I save a file of that to paste into my new installation, so I'll still have all my passwords.
You may have other specail things you want to keep.

Apart from the obvious files you keep in /home/username, as long as you back up those ones I just mentioned, you'll probably be pretty  much ready and able to re-install Ubuntu and when you restoer those files, you'll have a clean re-install, and you'll still have all your old files.
