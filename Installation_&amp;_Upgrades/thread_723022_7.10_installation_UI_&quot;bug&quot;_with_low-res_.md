### Post by VulcanTourist on 2008-03-13
While installing Ubuntu 7.10 on an IBM Thinkpad A20M, which (apparently) has a maximum screen resolution of 800x600, I encountered considerable problems with the installation process because of poor choices made with the UI:

On 800x600 and smaller displays: some of the installation windows/dialogs are larger than 800x600 AND ARE NOT RESIZABLE, even with Maximize, nor are they scrollable, resulting in the buttons at the bottom of the windows not being accessible, or barely so, except by keyboard [Tab]-bing and guesswork. At 800x600,  I was able to BARELY see the tops of the buttons in several windows, but ONLY by setting both the top and bottom desktop bars to "autohide"; otherwise even at 800x600 the buttons were completely truncated.  I was able to get through that by virtue of experience, but most people faced with that situation would not.  I found it very disappointing that such a thing has been overlooked by so many people.  Those windows need to be resizable and/or scrollable!  There are still systems and situations for which this will be a major headache.

Yes, I know a similar scenario can occur during the installation of Windows while relying on default VGA resolution, but is that really the bar that the Linux dev community wants to set for itself?  No?  I thought not!
