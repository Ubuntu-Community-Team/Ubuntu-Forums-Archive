### Post by jenova_skill on 2009-06-19
Well I decided to upgrade my computer. New Motherboard / cpu / ram / Powersuply. 1st I tore apart my desktop salvaged Hard drive and gave away parts to others that needed. So I put together the new box being 1st time doing something like this I was nervous as hell. The moment of truth I booted up ...... NOTHING! !...... No system beep Nothing!... Hours later... I Bent a pin on the Cpu!!!!!!!!! When i say it was bent ... It was a pin on the edge and it was bent around to the side under the cpu. Devastating blow but at least I knew what the problem was. I googled the problem and i came across a technician Attempting to bend back the pin on a cpu with 2 pin needles. He broke the pin in the vid after 15 min's of trying to bend it back. Of course I said I could do it. So i got some small plyers and a mechanical led pencil (7mm) *bitting nails** taking off shirt*** sweating I used the tip of the pencil to pry the pin slowly but surely I got the pin back into shape enuff to fit back on the board. Fired it up and WHAM it worked!!! I was completely Excited. Ubuntu picked up on the new System with ease no problems at all.... I had old OEM copy of xp insalled but i did a clean wipe with ubuntu. I wanted to run Wintrash 7 just to play some games however I found out all 3 of my cd rom drives ( old ) don't work so Trolling boards and whatnot I got a crash course on Virtual box Wow i like it and I am impressed  now i don't need to boot nothing other than Ubuntu. I'm now Installing a game as we speak in virtual box and writing this post.  LOL I hope someone gets a laugh at this.
