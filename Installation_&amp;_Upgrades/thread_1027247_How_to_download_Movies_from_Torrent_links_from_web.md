### Post by Zack McCool on 2009-01-01
Keep in mind, downloading copyrighted movies or television shows is illegal, and you risk significant fines for doing it.  But, not my place to preach...

Downloading .torrent files is the same, no matter if the content is legal or not.  You need a bittorrent client.  Ubuntu comes with Transmission pre-installed, and it is a popular client.  I prefer kTorrent, and many people use Azureus.  Once you download the .torrent file, you open it with the client, and it makes all the connections to the seeders to grab the torrent chunks.  You also may need to open ports on your router, if you are using one.  The ports are configured in the torrent client.
