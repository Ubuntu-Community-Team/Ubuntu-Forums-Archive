### Post by CPL2010 on 2010-08-09
You might want to check and make sure that the WiFi antenna in the lappy are on nice and tight.  On the 1505 it's easy enough to check.  Google cleaning a Dell 1505 laptop, the guts of it are pretty similar to a 1525 and I end up cleaning the wife's laptop monthly, the wifi antenna though runs up and under the screen hinge and can get pinched thereby pulling the antenna off the wireless card which has happened before to me.

Dust and heat can also cause wireless to turn into a an awful experience so pick up a can of air for good measure.  

Don't forget to ground your self btw before touching anything in the lappy.

When is the last time you cleaned the heat sink?
