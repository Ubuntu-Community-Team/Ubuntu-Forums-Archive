### Post by wkulecz on 2009-12-07
AMD came up with the x86-64 instruction set.  Intel expected everyone to buy into Itanium for 64-bit.  When it was clear Itanium depended on compliers nobody could write, they joined the x86-64 bandwagon.

SO the amd64 moniker is to insure AMD get credit for a very useful innovation and enhancement of the x86 architechure.

So yes, amd64 is what you wnat for your 64 bit Intel CPU unless you actually do have an Itanium.
