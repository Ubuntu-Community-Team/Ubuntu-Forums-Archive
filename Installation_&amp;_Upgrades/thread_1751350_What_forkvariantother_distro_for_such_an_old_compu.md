I also brought back an older computer into service, or at least to play around with.  Mine was an HP XT919, originally a Celeron 800 with 128 MB of RAM.  I recently bought 512 MB of RAM (The max HP says it can handle with two slots), and put in a Socket 370 Pentium 3 processor from a dead machine I had laying around.  Remember, this machine was designed to run Windows Mistaken Edition (Win ME).  At first I tried Ubuntu 8.4 with Rivendell radio automation packaged with it (RRAbuntu), and while I had sound with the trial of Rivendell, I lost all sound capabilities with other programs, no matter what I tried.

I then tried Ubuntu Studio.  Whether it was a bad disc or operator error, it wouldn't totally load when put on the hard drive.  I then tried Ubuntu 9.04 and upgraded to 9.10.  While I had sound, it was clunky, and got blank squares where the cursor was.  Another problem with all other versions was my screen resolution would only go to 800 x 600.  No higher

After all that, I went to Xubuntu.  MUCH better!  It still hickups once in a while, but I accept that because it's 10 year old machine.  Resolution is MUCH better, runs a bit quicker, and gives new life to this old beast.
