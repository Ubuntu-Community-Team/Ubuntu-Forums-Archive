### Post by Grafixx01 on 2009-05-27
I had that problem with my OQO when I installed Ubuntu 8.10 on it. Since I didn't want the Bluetooth  due to not wanting people to hack into my system through the Bluetooth connection, I just disabled the radio on bootup. The OQO can do it in the BIOS as well as during the bootup process with the "FN" key and the "Bluetooth Radio" key. Maybe that'll help if you can't get into the actual Ubuntu system?
