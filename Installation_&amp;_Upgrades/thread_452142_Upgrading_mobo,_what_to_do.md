### Post by Big Ed on 2007-05-25
> **stingerssx said:**
> I have Ubuntu running with Beryl on an older computer. It freezes after a little while. It has a PIII with 600 mhz. So I'd like to upgrade it to an AMD K7. What do I need to do, (software) to upgrade to this mobo/CPU?

   Thanks.

You might not need to do much.  The only thing that will likely give you a problem is the video card.  If you normally run in high resolution, those drivers are pretty specific.  One way around this is to drop to a low resolution (like 640x480 or 800x600) before taking your old mb out.  Those are pretty generic to all video cards.  Then it should boot up in the same resolution and give you a chance to see what resolutions will work on the K7 mb.  You may have to use Synaptic to load new video drivers.  

If that doesn't work, you can always download and install Feisty.  Then you will have the newest versions of all the software.

Ed
