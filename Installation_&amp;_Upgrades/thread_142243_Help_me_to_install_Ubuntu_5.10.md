### Post by aysiu on 2006-03-10
A good first stop is this PDF guide:
[http://ubuntu.xgn.com.br/ubuntu_user_guide.pdf](http://ubuntu.xgn.com.br/ubuntu_user_guide.pdf)

Before *installing*, you may want to try out the live CD--that runs off the CD and your computer's temporary memory (RAM) and doesn't affect your hard drive. It gives you a sense of how well Ubuntu will detect your hardware (sound, video) and what the interface is like.

It will also run significantly slower than a normal install.

If you wish to dual-boot, this is the best guide to setting that up:
[http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone](http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone)

Other than that, ask questions as they come up.
