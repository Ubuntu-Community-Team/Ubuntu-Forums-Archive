### Post by foresthill on 2011-08-30
If the live CD works OK for you, I would pull the trigger and install. But if you get a black screen like I do on my Gateway (with Intel 4500 MHD graphics) and numerous error messages while trying to install, that's a bad sign and you might want to hold off.

I have one laptop that runs 11.04 pretty well. No black screens and I use the Ubuntu Classic desktop with effects turned off. Other than some printing problems with PDF's, I'm reasonably happy. 

The other laptop hates 11.04, so I'm sticking to 10.10 on that one.

A lot of it depends on how much tweaking you want to do, which is really up to you. If you're OK with that, you can probably get 11,04 running on just about anything, but is it worth the effort? Only you know the answer to that.
