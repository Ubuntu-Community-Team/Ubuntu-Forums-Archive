### Post by sticksnleaves on 2010-02-16
I agree with darkod. At work I make it a rule that no one complains about alpha/beta software not working properly. You're always taking a risk when using non-stable software. You'll be compounding the risk by using an alpha release of such an essential piece of your system. Early releases of software are generally targeted at developers so they can either help fix bugs or begin integrating new, unfinished features into their own software. If this is just an everyday machine you will probably be getting in over your head in terms of buggy functionality. You'll be wishing you stuck with 9.10.

As for Fedora, that's up to you. I don't like Fedora and would rather downgrade or put up with any issues I'd be having with Ubuntu. That is just my opinion. If you don't mind learning a new OS or you enjoy that sort of thing then maybe it would be fun. If you want a RedHat system you might also want to look at CentOS.
