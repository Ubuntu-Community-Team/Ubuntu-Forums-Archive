### Post by pdub on 2006-08-06
Wow! You may wish to consider re-installing.

You can get rid of the wacom errors be removing all references in your xorg.conf file. Remember to remove them from the DEVICES and SERVER LAYOUT sections.

sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg,conf.bak

sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf

I really think that a re-install is your best bet though.

### Post by pdub on 2006-08-07
You still have wacom related items listed in your xorg.conf file. Remove all Device sections that refer to wacom.

If you post your xorg.conf file I will fix it for you.

Your ServerLayout should look something like this:

Section "ServerLayout"
	Identifier	"Default Layout"
	Screen		"Default Screen"
	InputDevice	"Generic Keyboard"
	InputDevice	"Configured Mouse"
EndSection
