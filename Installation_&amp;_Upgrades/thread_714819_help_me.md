### Post by DrMega on 2008-03-04
Go to the System Menu, then choose Administration->Synaptic Package Manager.

In there, do a search for "ubuntu-restricted-extras"
Tick the box and then click apply.

It will now play your music.

### Post by gpilkay on 2008-03-04
Also, some video files and DVDs require codecs that Ubuntu can't legally distribute.  Medibuntu offers them if they're legal where you are.  Here's their page:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu)

After installing their repositories you go to synaptic and get libdvd, the w32 codecs, and more that handle DVDs, RMVB,a and others.  From add/remove, VLC and Mplayer are also a bit help in the odder formats.
