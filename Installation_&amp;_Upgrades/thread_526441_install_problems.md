### Post by jnorthr on 2007-08-15
Have you burned a dvd  with a copy of ubuntu or kubuntu from the download site ? Go here: [http://www.ubuntu.com/](http://www.ubuntu.com/) and try learn more> to the desktop edition. At the bottom of that screen is the download button. That takes you here: [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download) so you can click 'choose a location' then click start to copy it onto your current box. If you've done it correctly you should have an .iso image of ubuntu stored on ur local box. Burn that .iso image to a blank dvd at the slowest possible speed to avoid burn errors. When you've done that, you will have a 'live cd' . You can put that into either of ur 2 boxes and try a reboot. If that fails, it's ur box, otherwise you'll be into ubuntu. You can test drive the live cd without doing an install and when you are happy, you can do an install.

It may be that your burn process has introduced problems on the dvd copy, that's why you want to burn them very slowly. Did the machines work before ?

Also, don't forget to check the md5 checksum number on your downloaded file, against the md5 number in the Ubuntu repository where the ISO file came from.
