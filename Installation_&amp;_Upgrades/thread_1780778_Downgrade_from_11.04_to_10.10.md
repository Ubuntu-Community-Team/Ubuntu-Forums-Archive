### Post by coffeecat on 2011-06-12
I find it ironic that you don't like Unity, yet you want to theme your desktop so that it looks like a Mac! :wink: A lot of the complaints about Unity are that it's "too Mac-like", buttons on the left, global menu, dock and so on.

Anyway, I don't know whether MacBuntu works on 11.04 classic gnome. In that YouTube video, cheese is the webcam viewer - it's in the repositories - and Rythmbox has been nicely themed to look as though it's a Mac app. As far as I can see - the glimpse was very brief - they've just made the nautilus file browser look a bit like the Mac Finder. When he clicked on the desktop, the file browser menu appeared in the top panel - **Which is what you get in Unity!** :p

Good luck with Mac-ifying your gnome desktop, but don't give up on Unity just yet. There are many people who took a dislike to Unity at first (myself included) but who have come to prefer it. You can always log into it occasionally for brief periods and get used to gradually. You never know - you might end up liking it. The two links in my sig might be useful.
