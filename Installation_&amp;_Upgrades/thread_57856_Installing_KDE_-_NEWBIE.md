### Post by solarcontrol on 2005-08-23
I find KDE to be a great tool for pushing linux on the unfamiliar or less computer-savvy.
It's visually more impressive and more intuitive.

That said, Gnome is better once you know your way around linux a bit (and on slightly older boxes where KDE is too much).

I personally use Gnome, but I give my customers KDE when I can.
That way, they can learn to be an "advanced" KDE user in short order (which makes them a linux novice lol) and they are ready for Gnome.
Then, once an "advanced" Gnome user - which makes one a competent linux user (something I have yet to achieve heehee) - they are ready for the lighter WMs (Enlightenment/IceWM/Flux/etc)

To start out "slim" with linux takes some determination lemme tell ya.
I started last year with Deli Linux on a 133mhz P1 (the BEST distro for boxes that old btw - I have 2 like that and theyre both fast and stable and have all the office functions I need plus xmms - the distro tests on a 486 with 16mb ram but has IceWM)
It was a chore and I had to add ALSA from source to have sound as well, but when I was done I had turned an old throwaway into a useful little machine.

Had I not been so intrigued though, and if I werent a pack rat that collects old boxes to play with, I would have blown it off.

Most people arent that interested.
I figure get em hooked with the pretty/easy stuff (KDE), then reel em in with the more efficient stuff (Gnome).
Whether they keep going deeper into geekdom from there is of no concern, because at that point they are as addicted as I am.
:}

Thanks for giving me an easy choice.
