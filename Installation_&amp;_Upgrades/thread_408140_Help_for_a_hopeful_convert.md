### Post by Herman on 2007-04-14
Probably no-one who's available right now knows for sure. You might have to wait a while before someone else with the same hardware as you have happens along and notices your particular thread. 

I wouldn't wait that long, I'd jump right in and try an install and find out for myself.
If you have some spare room on your hard disk why not resize one of your existing partitions to make some free space and go ahead and install Ubuntu in it? :D
Back up your data first. You don't need to delete your other operating systems. Just install Ubuntu beside them. If it doesn't suit your hardware just delete it, resize your other partition and re-install Suse's grub to MBR again.

Regards, Herman :D

### Post by CourteousCucumber on 2007-04-14
I'm in a very similar situation, AMD Sempron 1.8, Nvidia GeForce 6600, Dell 1440x900, but I am a way Linux noob.  I installed 6.10, my first linux install, two days ago and was stuck with 1024x768.  I tried several suggestions to get the 6600 up to 1440x900, but borked the machine badly.  Finally I downloaded drivers from the Nvidia site and "sudo nvidia-xconfig".  BOOM 1440x900!  Perfect!

Good luck.
