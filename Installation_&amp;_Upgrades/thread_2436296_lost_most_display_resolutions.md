### Post by burnsmicro2 on 2020-02-03
On booting up my Ubuntu 18.04.4 PC after a 2 week vacation, my display no longer filled my screen properly: the top of the display was mostly hidden and the sides were not filled. On checking Settings/Devices/Screen Display, I could only find 3 display options and I could no longer find the 1360x768 (16:9) that had worked so well for so long. 

I followed [COLOR=#000080]_[[COLOR=#127622][SIZE=3]How 	to Set A Custom Screen Resolution in Ubuntu Desktop[/SIZE][/COLOR]]("http://ubuntuhandbook.org/index.php/2017/04/custom-screen-resolution-ubuntu-desktop/")_[/COLOR] which almost worked, except the display was offset so the left of the display was off the screen, leaving a blank strip to the right.

I updated the display drivers and rebooted, to no avail. The display controller is Radeon HD 4250.

How can I set up my display to fill my 1360x768 screen?

---

### Post by Autodave on 2020-02-04
Have you checked monitor / PC connections?  Make sure that they are tight....maybe take them apart and reconnect.  If you have a bad connection, the info from the monitor might not be making it to the PC.

---

### Post by burnsmicro2 on 2020-02-04
Brilliant!! Solved the problem.

Swapped the VGA cable and the display now fills the screen as it should.

Thank you very much!
