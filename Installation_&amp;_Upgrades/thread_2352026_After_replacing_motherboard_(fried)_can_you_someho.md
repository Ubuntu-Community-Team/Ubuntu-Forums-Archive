### Post by wildmanne39 on 2017-02-08
All your data, files, apps are saved on your hard drive so there is no need to do anything about the bios to get to your old installation just install your hard drive. There may need to be some bios settings changed but not so you can access your old installation unless it does not recognize the hard drive.

There should be  directions for setting up the new motherboard in the box it came in.

### Post by geeksmith on 2017-02-08
As long as your BIOS is set to boot from the hard drives, they should boot up just like before. This assumes that you don't have an exotic boot configuration, of course. I've upgraded my CPU, RAM, and MB before. Booting the already-installed OS was as simple as attaching the drives and powering on.

---

### Post by wildmanne39 on 2017-02-08
Yes just like before, just plug the drive in and it should boot, if you have integrated graphics on the new motherboard and it is not the same as the old one then you may get a black screen or something like that and have to boot into recovery mode and remove the old graphics driver. 

I have installed ubuntu on a harddrive then taken it out and put it in a different computer and it worked flawlessly.
