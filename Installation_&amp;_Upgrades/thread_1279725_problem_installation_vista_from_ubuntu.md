### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-10-01
Answer depends on what "solution" you want ...

You can install Vista from any DVD medium you can find (that's one "solution") but it won't bet the OEM version that was installed on your machine from the vendor -- and they will easily determine that once they get the machine.  Which means, there's a likelyhood that they will say you voided your warranty and, either refuse to fix it without some payment, or refuse to fix it in general.

The only "solution" that they will find acceptable is the one you trashed when you wiped the recovery partition.  

An alternative, if you want to pay the money, is to see if they will send you a new recover disk.  If that can reinstall Vista by itself, that's probably going to be cheaper than what they will charge you for fixing it.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-10-01
When you contact ACER, tell them that you're having suspected virus infection problems and need to start over afresh with a clean install -- and that you've misplaced the disk that came with the machine.  

You need to confirm that the disk they send you will be sufficient to do that.  If all it does is boot you into a recovery mode, and that goes looking for a hidden partition on the drive -- you're no better off than you are now.
