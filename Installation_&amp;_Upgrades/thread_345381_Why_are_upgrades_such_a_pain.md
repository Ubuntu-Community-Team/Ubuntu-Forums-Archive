### Post by erico on 2007-01-24
Each time I do an install with Ubuntu or any other distro of LINUX, my wifi card,which is the only primary internet interface in my system stops working after the initial update. Now the wifi interface works fine until the initial upgrade, which, by the way, the OS pushes hard for.  After the update or upgrade, wifi  (ath0 or Wlan0) breaks virtually without fail. 

Is there not a more painless way that the update can be done, perhaps with warnings that prompt the unwary user and say, for example, " Caution!! Your WIFI card might not work after this update/upgrade" ?

I know this sounds just like another complaining Nooby who doesn't write down what kernel he started with and doesn't make sure that he has the appropriate  wifi .inf file,Ndiswrapper and utils ready to intergate into the next bigger, better, more reliable kernel. But in reality, I believe this is one issue that keeps many individuals who really want to use Linux (in whatever distribution) away from Linux. 

So what is the LINUX community going to do about it? Universal acceptance of LINUX surely will not happen until issues like this are in the past.  This is an issue where Windoze shines.
