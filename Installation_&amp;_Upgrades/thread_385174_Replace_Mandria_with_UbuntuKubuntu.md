### Post by buuntuu! on 2007-03-15
don't quite understand what your problem is... but here's the way to do it:
1. get into your BIOS (when starting up you should press Esc or F1 or F10... varies with the system, you'll have to try
2. there you set CD as your first bootdevice, meaning that the computer tries to boot from CD if it finds one
3. save and restart.
4. at that point you put your cd in and it should boot from it
5. the install can be done from here.
read some howtos first! [psychocat's]("http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/") is good.
have fun with ubuntu

EDIT: you can get rid of mandriva during the install, piece of cake
