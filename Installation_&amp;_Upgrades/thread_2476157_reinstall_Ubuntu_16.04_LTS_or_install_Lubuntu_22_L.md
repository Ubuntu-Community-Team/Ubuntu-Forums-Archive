### Post by TheFu on 2022-06-17
Lubuntu 22.04 LTS
or
Xubuntu 22.04 LTS
or
Ubuntu-Mate 22.04

Beware that LXQt, XFCE and Mate DEs running on 20.04 have support until April 2023.  Those same DEs running on 22.04 are supported until April 2025.  If you need a very stable system and can commit to upgrading to 22.04 THIS YEAR ... say in the fall, then I'd go with 20.04 versions.  Nearly all the big bugs have workarounds by now. 22.04 has so many changes and many can break your current workflows. Best to ease into a new OS.

Since 16.04, there are many, many, many, changes.  In 20.04 and 22.04, Canonical has inflicted a few of their wishes onto us that just aren't ready for use.  There is another way to avoid those alpha-quality choices - Linux Mint.  Get the latest, probably with the Mate or Cinnamon DE.  I think you'll like it and won't need to learn about snap packages or Wayland.

Disclosure: I'm not a Linux Mint user, but many people in my LUG are, so I've seen their desktops and heard the types of issues they run into.  Mint is based on Ubuntu, but a little different - in good ways.
