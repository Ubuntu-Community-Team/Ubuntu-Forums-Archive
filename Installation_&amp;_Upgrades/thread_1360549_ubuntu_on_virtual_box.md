### Post by elite_angel on 2009-12-21
Those are boot selections.(GRUB- GRand Unified Bootloader)

Its your bootloader.

[http://www.gnu.org/software/grub/](http://www.gnu.org/software/grub/)
[http://grub.enbug.org/](http://grub.enbug.org/)

Displays your present kernel version.

You can boot from your previous version of kernel if you want. ( As i can see, your kernel isn't updated yet.)

If you have windows installed first on it (on the same drive), you can see it there, select, and boot on your windows partition.

Please specify what is the problem. 

Its normal in Ubuntu.
