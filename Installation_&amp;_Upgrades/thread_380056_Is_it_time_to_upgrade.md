### Post by Vexed Arcanist on 2007-03-09
The only reason to upgrade is/are:

1.  A feature only available in the newer version that you simply must have.
2.  Performance increases you simply can't live without.
3.  Just cause you are bleeding edge and .... well .. just cause ...

Since I am continually breaking things to a point I end up reinstalling I just decided to run Feisty Fawn.  I noticed it had a nice performance increase over edgy (for me, my system, etc.).  However if you don't like fixing crap continuously and have no need for something you don't even know exists, stick with what works.
