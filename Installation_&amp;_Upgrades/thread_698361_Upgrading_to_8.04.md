### Post by bizzy on 2008-02-21
Upgrading will work correctly for most people most of the time.

However, I have 5 Kubuntu systems that started mostly around 6.06 and are now 7.10. That must be betwen 10 and 15 upgrades. About 5 of those failed. I didn't bother to diagnose. I did a fresh install.

But because an upgrade can brick a mission critical setup - I would always counsel that you take a partition image before upgrading. Pretty simple with something like G4U on the Ultimate Boot CD (provided you have somewhere to back it to ... ). Then if it fails you are 20 minutes away from being restored with a fully operative system while you figure out what went wrong.

And its a well known IT lore that people with backups have far less failures then people without!!!
