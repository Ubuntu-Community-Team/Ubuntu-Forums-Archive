### Post by phillw on 2010-03-21
Hi and welcome to the forums,

The only issues that seem to have been flagged up is that of the broadcom wireless adapter and the touch-pad being offset.

Boot using the LiveCD in 'Try without altering my Computer' mode and check that both are working.

Enabling the wi-fi is quite easy, the touch-pad may present a problem as the work-around was to use a mouse, but that was in 8.04 - the author reports it works in 9.10

Possible WiFi -->[http://answers.yahoo.com/question/index?qid=20090930055111AAqkf6Z](http://answers.yahoo.com/question/index?qid=20090930055111AAqkf6Z)
and here --> [http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-newbie-8/need-help-install-wifi-drivers-on-my-compaq-presario-v6000-using-opensuse-10.3-634475/](http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-newbie-8/need-help-install-wifi-drivers-on-my-compaq-presario-v6000-using-opensuse-10.3-634475/)

There is a sub-area in these forums for Wireless where you can get help if you have problems getting your WiFi to work --> [http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=336](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=336)

Possible TouchPad --> [http://kimbriggs.com/computers/computer-hardware/compaq-v6000-laptop-linux.file](http://kimbriggs.com/computers/computer-hardware/compaq-v6000-laptop-linux.file)

I understand your computer uses a broadcom WiFi adapter.

Welcome to Ubuntu!!
