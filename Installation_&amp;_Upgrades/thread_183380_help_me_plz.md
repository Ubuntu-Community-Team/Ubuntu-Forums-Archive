### Post by confused57 on 2006-05-28
Try going into your bios at bootup and see if your HD is recognized, set the bios to autodetect the hd and enable "SMART" drive, if you have it.

Make sure you don't have a floppy in the floppy drive & you said your jumper setting on the drive was set to master?  Are you hearing any hard drive activity when you're booting up(check indicator light).

Try running a LiveCD on your computer(if you have a CD drive), mount the hd, and check the partitioning on it.  I'm not familiar with Edubuntu, but you may be able to install Grub if the LiveCD tells you anything.
