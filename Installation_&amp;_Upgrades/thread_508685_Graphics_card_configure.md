### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-07-24
youre definitely using the vesa driver per your xorg.conf. You'll want to use the i810 driver for sure! I can't say that's the reason it's crashing but that will give you much better resolutions and graphics support than vesa.

Have you checked out your syslog for any errors that stand out?
sudo nano /var/log/syslog
then you can scroll down with the arrow down, then to exit, hit ctrl-x. OR you can just use your favorite text editor like Kate, VI (VIM), Mousepad, or Gnome's Notepad which the name escapes me right now.

Also, enter this command to check out any messages that occur at startup

dmesg
