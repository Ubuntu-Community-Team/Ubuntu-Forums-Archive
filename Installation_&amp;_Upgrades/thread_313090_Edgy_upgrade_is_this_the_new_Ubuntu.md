### Post by incognito on 2006-12-05
My first linux installation was a single disk dual boot Breezy Badger / XP setup that worked beautifully. Over the next many months I upgraded without a single significant problem until... Edgy ](*,)

After seeing the initial troubles of others in the forums I waited weeks before presuming the receeding tide of complaints meant the initial glitches had been repaired. Following the official upgrade (gsku "update manager -c") I was rewarded with 2 crashes during package loading and then a black screen of death (new Ubuntu TM?). After multiple identical apt-get and aptitude updates, dist-upgrades, and xserver installs something finally just worked (as bleary visions of Windows 98 danced in my head). This repair advice was gleaned from the now 11 page long upgrade forum sticky and a duggmirror FAQ; not from any official Ubuntu documentation. After this terrible experience I must note:

a.) Edgy 6.10 was, as others have noted, *not* advertised as an unstable beta.
b.) Despite prior beta testing and presumed in-house testing what  happened to allow all of the python and xserver component incompatibilities and failures during an upgrade? How will (or just will) this be prevented in the future? Should "upgrading" be discouraged over more cumbersone clean reinstallations?
b.) When widespread problems abounded why were Edgy update manager recalls and official fixes not released?
c.) What does Ubuntu / Canonical expect of current or future users with new real concerns about ease of use, stability, and support?
