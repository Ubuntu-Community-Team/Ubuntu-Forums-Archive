### Post by wieman01 on 2006-08-17
I had a very similar problem with Bluetooth on my laptop (Sony Vaio). Then I figured it had to do with the Linux Power Management which is installed by default and is controlling the CPU performance. When the power cable is unplugged in particular, the "powersave" mode was activated and caused my Bluetooth connection to become unbearably poor. 

Either disable Power Management or change the settings of your profile. That did the job for me & Bluetooth works fine now.
