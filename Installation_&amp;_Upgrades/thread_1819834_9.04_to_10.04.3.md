### Post by coffeecat on 2011-08-07
@SlyBattery, have another look at the link that Old_Gray_Wolf posted. You haven't followed it correctly. Your system is still looking for packages from the us.archive.ubuntu.com, security.ubuntu.com and archive.canonical.com servers. Your /etc/apt/sources.list file **must** only have lines for old-releases.ubuntu.com when you do your aptitude/apt-get update. Remove, edit or comment out any lines that refer to any server other than old-releases.ubuntu.com. Also - comment out any lines starting with "deb cdrom:". You don't want a CD as a repository.

Next - that link is out of date and doesn't take into account the fact that Karmic is also end-of-life and you have to upgrade to Karmic before you can upgrade to Lucid. You have to trick your system into thinking that Karmic is still supported. This is what you do. Save the following text as /var/lib/update-manager/meta-release.
