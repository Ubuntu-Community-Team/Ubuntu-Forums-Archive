### Post by lechien73 on 2010-10-12
Welcome to the forums, first of all.

In answer to your question - you're better off doing a completely clean install, rather than upgrading.

The Ubuntu 10.10 ISO is available from here: [http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download)

Regarding Debian vs Ubuntu...I wouldn't call them competitors. Ubuntu a fork of the Debian project, so is built on Debian architecture. Ubuntu's release structure is different as well. As one blog post said:

> When you are choosing between them [Debian or Ubuntu], the decision is not a case of right or wrong, or of superiority or inferiority, but of what matters to you.

### Post by lechien73 on 2010-10-12
If your processor has 64-bit support, then you will likely notice a small performance benefit over 32-bit. The real plus comes from the fact that 64-bit works better with memory sizes over 4Gb.

The benchmarks for Ubuntu 9.04 are here:

[http://www.tuxradar.com/content/ubuntu-904-32-bit-vs-64-bit-benchmarks](http://www.tuxradar.com/content/ubuntu-904-32-bit-vs-64-bit-benchmarks)

The "Windows" key in Ubuntu becomes a "Super" key that can be used in other key combinations, or separately. Particularly if you're using compositing, the "Super" key is often used in combination with another key to switch features on or off.
