Just which options are you trying to achieve this?
Ub 11.10 normally selects a sensible option (even if it isn't as high a res as you'd like.
Ub 11.10, in common with the last few releases, doesn't use an xconf file - it's all done 'live' by interrogating the graphics chipset and the monitor declared parameters.

+1 with dandnsmith

Additionally, that being said, sometimes while probing that hardware it ends up being out of sync ecause it couldn't understand the mode it tried to probe with. Other times it probes the hardware, like an older monitor, that doesn't have data to return... and puts it into a mode it doesn't understand.

Sometimes this is just a driver issue. Sometimes just a grub edit to put it into a mode closer to what it can do. Sometimes more is needed. 

Back to fixing your problem- You said with 9.10 you didn't have problems. Do you have a 9.10 LiveCD?  From being booted on a Live Image of the 9.10 LiveCD, look for the /var/log/Xorg.0.log from your installed filesystem and post it.

Then get to a terminal session and post the results of 
```

lspci -vnn | grep VGA

```
