### Post by auburn on 2005-10-13
I was having soooo many freezes, apps dying and segmentation faults. I was about to install debian stable to see if it was my hardware. But I had a recent problem upgrading the headers or some other part relating to the 2.6.12-9 kernel and I got a: 
```
I would strongly advice you to either abort 
and fix the errors in depmod, or regenerate the 
initrd image with a known good modules.dep file.
 I repeat, an initrd kernel image with a bad 
modules.dep shall fail to boot.
```
When I rebooted the 2.6.12-9 kernel wouldn't boot anymore and I found that my problems disappeared when I began using the 2.6.12-8 kernel.

My personal lesson: stay away from cutting edge kernels, especially 2.6.12-9.

I just saw where someone referred to that error as a "'not for this architecture' error". Maybe I somehow got hold of a kernel or a kernel element that wasn't compatible with me?  Maybe I've been upgrading too aggresively? Maybe I've been doing one too many dist-upgrades? or Maybe I should be using apt-get instead of aptitude install?

duron 900mhz or 1.2ghz Athlon
chaintech skt600 mobo
512mb ram -memtested fine

an nvidia or an ati card
