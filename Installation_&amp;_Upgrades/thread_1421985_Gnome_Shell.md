### Post by Thomas Garman on 2010-03-04
So, supposedly Gnome Shell is available through synaptic.  I just downloaded and installed it and then ran gnome-shell --replace...

It doesn't work at all.  I hit the windows key and get nothing and there is no application launcher in the upper left hand corner and Alt-f2 produces nothing at all.

Alt-tab gives me the option of choosing the windows that were running when I ran gnome-shell --replace

but nothing else works at all.  Just a vast expanse of digital nothingness.

Does anyone know why this might be?

I have an nvidia video card (see sig below) and two monitors running from "twin view"...

[UPDATE:  I read other threads and googled it and learned that the problem I was having derived from the fact that compiz was running in the background.  So, I killed compiz and then ran gnome-shell --replace in Alt-F2 and it worked just fine.  I liked gnome-shell so much I used synaptic to uninstall compiz.]
