### Post by borgward on 2009-11-12
I was prompted to return to the forum after reading Hey "Ubuntu, Stop Making Linux Look Bad" last week. I made some constructive comments to that article, and was criticized for never contributing to or asking questions of Ubuntu. 

I first tried Ubuntu with Version 5.10, or maybe earlier. I have tried it since, and recently Xubuntu 9.04.

My biggest complaint has been that Ubuntu is very slow to boot and open applications. I have tried using it on a variety of computers. The slowest being my Dell Latitude CPi, and the fastest being the tower I built using an ASUS A7N8X-X Delux mobo w/ Athlon 1.8 gig processor and 1 GB RAM.

I am currently running Debian Lenny.

Last summer I had trouble connecting to a wifi hot spot with Knoppix HDD install while on vacation. My nephew handed me Xubuntu 9.04 and to it's credit, I was online. The downside was that Xubuntu was very slow to boot on my CPi laptop, even slower than XP.

At home I did an experiment using the Dell Latitude CPi 400MHz PII 256 MB RAM.I have XP on one HDD and Xubuntu on another. Both HDD are 6GB IBM Travelstar. 4200 RPM. I forget the exact data, but Xubuntu HDD install took about half again longer to boot than XP.

Ia m curious why Ubuntu is always so slow to boot, especially since it is Debian based. Debian is pretty quick to boot and open apps.

I applaud Ubuntu's effort to build a Linux distro that works for the average Joe.
