### Post by Dale61 on 2008-04-07
Yes, it can be done.

I've just spent a very long weekend upgrading from 6.06 LTS to 8.04 LTS.  Most of the time was waiting for the downloading of the new packages, and as I'm on dial-up, this took the best part of 100 hours.  Yes, it's not a typo - 100 hours.

I started at 1am Thursday morning, and the last of the packages was downloaded at 5.20am Monday morning.  The installation then took just over an hour, and everything went according to how I assume the developers expected.

I'm encountering a few minor hiccups - I can't get Firefox 3 to default over Firefox 2, I can't yet get the spinning desktop cube, and a few of the other bits of eye candy are not functional even though my system indicates all should be fine.

I'll keep searching through the forums to try and find a fix for the few problems I have, but all in all, the upgrade was pain free.

Unless you are extremely patient, I do not recommend upgrading this way if you are on dial-up, but if you are willing to take the chance, it is well worth it.
