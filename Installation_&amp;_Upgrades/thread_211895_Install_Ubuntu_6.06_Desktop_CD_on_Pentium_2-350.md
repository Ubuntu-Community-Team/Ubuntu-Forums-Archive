### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-07-09
You have the option of a fresh installation with either the "desktop" or the "alternate" CDs; the difference is the process.

The "desktop" CD boots to a live Ubuntu environment, with a nifty installation option. It's quicker and cleaner, but some people like a little more control when they install.

The "alternate" CD is a text-based install, with mega-control and plenty of tweaks. 

For a machine that old, I would suggest downloading Xubuntu. It's lighter than the standard Gnome desktop, and might prove a little more responsive on your hardware. But that's up to you. :)

Good luck!
