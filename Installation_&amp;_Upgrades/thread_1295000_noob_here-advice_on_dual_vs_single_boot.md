The entire advantage of dual booting is to retain the ability to use XP programs you need and can't easily replicate.  If there are no programs you need, then it probably does make more sense to just wipe your old disk in favor of Ubuntu.  As far as image burning, I don't really have the expertise to make a personal assesment, but if you're looking to spend a little money, you might want to actually buy a new hard drive, so that instead of worrying about your old OS, you can simply remove and store the hard drive containing it while loading a larger hard drive to put Ubuntu to work on.  Hard drives are pretty cheap after all, as well as easy to swap.
