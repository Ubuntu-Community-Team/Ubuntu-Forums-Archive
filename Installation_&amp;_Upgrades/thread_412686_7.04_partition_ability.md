### Post by mjitkop on 2007-04-18
[FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]1/ it will partition  the drive so as to share space with XP?[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]The CD is a Live CD  as you may already know so technically you can run Ubuntu without installing  anything on your Compaq, it can run off the CD. [/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Having said that,  Ubuntu comes with a package (or program, if you prefer) called "gparted"  installed by default. This package gives you the possibility to partition all  your hard drives on your system as you want. [/SIZE][/FONT]
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]When you choose to  install Ubuntu on your system, gparted is launched automatically to offer you  several options for your installation. You can just accept the default option if  you want and Ubuntu will partition your drive automatically and share it with XP  as you described. You can also take full control over your installation and you  can partition everything on your own as you wish. It is very convenient! In any  cases, if Ubuntu detects another operating system on your system (XP, in your  case), you will have a dual boot system after Ubuntu is installed (see your  question 4 below.)[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]2/ no need for  additional software or partitioning procedures?[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]That's correct. As  explained above, Ubuntu comes with gparted that gives you the possibility to  partition your drives. No need for additional software.[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]3/ [/SIZE][/FONT][FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]I have about 30gigs  unused space for it to work with.[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Perfect. That's  plenty of space![/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]4/  Also, if it succeeds at this partitioning, what will I see on boot-ups  thereafter -- some sort of display allowing me to choose XP or Ubuntu startup?  [/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Exactly. Ubuntu will  use a boot-up manager called GRUB and everytime you will start your Compaq  afterwards, you will be given the option to boot to Ubuntu or  XP.[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]5/ Or  must I secure some software to manage the startups?[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]No need for  additional software.[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Isn't that great?! :)
[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Don't forget to  backup your important data from XP before the installation, just in case  something goes wrong during the process. I do not anticipate any problems but  better be safe than sorry. 
[/SIZE][/FONT]
  
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Good luck and have  fun! :popcorn:
[/SIZE][/FONT]
