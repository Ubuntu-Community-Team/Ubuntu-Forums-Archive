### Post by Frogs Hair on 2013-01-13
Hello and Welcome 

The first step is to create a DVD  or USB the latest Ubuntu(12.10) is over 700 Mb that is why a DVD will be required. 12.04 may be a better option because it is a long term support release and has support for 5 years.12.04 will also fit on a 700 Mb cd. [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto)

Once you have a disc created test Ubuntu by choosing try ubuntu without installing to see how it works on your hardware. (Running a live disc can be slower than an installation) I dual boot and use the installation method at the link.  [http://www.liberiangeek.net/2012/10/dual-booting-windows-7-and-ubuntu-12-10-quantal-quetzal/](http://www.liberiangeek.net/2012/10/dual-booting-windows-7-and-ubuntu-12-10-quantal-quetzal/)
