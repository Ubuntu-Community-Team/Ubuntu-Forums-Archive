### Post by YannBuntu on 2010-03-17
Upgrades often cause problems, it is very important to test in live-CD before.

First of all, save your data (/home ..) on an external drive.

Then try to boot on earlier kernels to see if there is one ok.

If not, reinstall Ubuntu9.04 (as it worked before), and put it back your saved /home to restore your settings.
