### Post by coffeecat on 2011-05-20
Hi, give Unity a few days and you may find you prefer it. I now prefer it to the classic gnome, but it did take me some time to find my way around.

But to your questions. Here's a clear basic guide:

[http://omgubuntu.co.uk/natty/](http://omgubuntu.co.uk/natty/)

Something for if you want to do a bit of tweaking:

[http://castrojo.tumblr.com/post/4795149014/the-power-users-guide-to-unity](http://castrojo.tumblr.com/post/4795149014/the-power-users-guide-to-unity)

**Preferences**: click on the shutdown button at top right (yes, weird, I agree!). Choose "System Settings" and you get the gnome control centre. Most of what you want there.

**Nautilus**: The top-most icon in the launcher opens a nautilus window in your home folder. The equivalent of the Places menu is in the left pane by default. If you want a second Nautilus window, middle-click on the icon, or ctrl-N, or from the nautilus global menu, File > New Window.

There's also the Files and Folders launcher icon near the bottom of the launcher bar with some of your folders.

**Unison**: If you've installed the unison-gtk pacakage, press the super/Windows key to open the dash and start typing unison in the search bar. A unison icon will appear which you can click on. Once unison has opened you can pin its icon to the launcher with a right-click.

**Firefox**: You can start a second instance of Firefox with the same middle-click trick.
