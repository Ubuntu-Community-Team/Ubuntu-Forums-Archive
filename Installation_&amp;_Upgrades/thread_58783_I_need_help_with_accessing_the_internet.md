### Post by j.stagner on 2006-06-16
[QUOTE=earobinson]beaten: ndiswrapper

That would be fantastic if "try a search next time" worked, but really it doesn't. 

The fact is, setting up Ubuntu (or in my case Kubuntu) to use a wireless network card, communicate with a WPA access point, and get on the internet is apparently beyond the ability of anybody to describe in any meaningful way that will work for a very large number of beginners.

There are literally tens of thousands of forum or blog posts that utterly FAIL to describe the process well.  While the OP did not ask for help well, he was asking for help and the responses he got are simply horrible.

Even once you get ndiswrapper to work, there are possibly even bigger hurdles to overcome, such as the fact that most of the posts even on this forum about getting a WPA-PSK connection to work reliably and without constant user interaction are simply useless.

Thank you friendly Ubuntu community member (I realize not everyone in this community is such a jerk), for so gallantly helping us beginners discover the wonders of this mysteriously popular Linux thing and be able to finally leave the supposedly horrible world of Windows, with which even my 76 year old grandfather and my 10 year old son are able to set up a brand new machine.
