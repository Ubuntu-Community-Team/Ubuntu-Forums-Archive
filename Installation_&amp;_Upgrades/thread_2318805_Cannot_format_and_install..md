### Post by yancek on 2016-03-29
Having a partition on disk which isn't being used isn't going to have anything to do with "filling it up".  I can't see any reason why he would have deleted it.  Also, did you find out what partition he deleted, where it was on the drive, was it a logical or primary partition, was it a root filesystem partition or a boot partition and was your Ubuntu the only system installed?

Not knowing what "some stuff" is, there is no way to do anything but guess.

In any case, modifying partitions isn't going to prevent you from booting a CD/DVD, totally unrelated so I guess you need to find out what "some stuff" was.
