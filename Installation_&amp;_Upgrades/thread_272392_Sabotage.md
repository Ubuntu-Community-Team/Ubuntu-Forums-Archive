It is not as harmful to me as it might seem. I normally have three linux systems on my computer. Most files are recoverable by mounting one linux system from another. The real nuisance is the work involved when one of the linux systems fails. On the other hand, each system has its own merit. Until now, Ubuntu was the most stable. pclinuxos has newer features faster and kde and more flexibility in logging in. With a few additional features, Puppy will be the real star of linux. While it does not have the security of other systems, most home users do not need "root" and the possibility of adding other users. Where I do get into trouble is with grub and lilo. It is the malfunction of one of them that led me to install Edgy EFT instead or replacing Dapper Drake. I thought that as long as I had to reinstall, I might as well try the new one. The other problem is in restoring bookmarks in browsers, especially in Ubuntu because you can't just log in as root in order to make the necessary restorations.

With Windows, you must do things Microsoft's way. With linux, there is variety and its programmers are better. Moreover, there is a little bit of the adventurer in most users of linux. And, unlike Microsoft with its $3 a minute help, you can usually get out of trouble in linux by going to a forum. I like to think that by posting my troubles, I help others with the same trouble.
