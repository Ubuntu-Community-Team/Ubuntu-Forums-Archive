### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-11-29
Unless I misunderstand what you're saying ... you can't boot from a blank drive, and I presume you know that.  So, selecting the blank drive as the first boot selection is not going to work.

IF what you're really doing is booting from the CD, and the installer is not seeing the drive, then reboot, select the option to get into the BIOS screens, and confirm that the BIOS sees the new drive.  IF the BIOS doesn't see it, the installer will not see it, either.
