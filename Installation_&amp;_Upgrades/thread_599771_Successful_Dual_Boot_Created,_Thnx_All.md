### Post by Chappy7777 on 2007-11-01
Just a quick note to thanks all those who helped me do a successful job of creating a dual-boot out of my existing XP and 7.04  (Somewhere in here there is a post asking for that help).

I thought it would be good if I posted how I managed to do this, since it went so easy and seamlessly. I have to give credit to Richard Grant, whose book "Ubuntu Linux for Non-Geeks" helped me install and get 6.06 working on my older PC. 

Here is his method. 
1) Defrag Existing XP partition. 
2)Put in the Ubuntu CD (this works w/6.06 and 7.04, so it should work for others).
3)Click on Install (After using it as a Live CD to make sure there are no major hassles).
4)When you get to the partition page click the top choice 
   "Resize IDE1 master, partition #1 (hda1) and use freed space.
5) Click forward and wait. The time depends on HD size and PC specs (CPU and Ram, duh).

That's it. Once it's done, click restart and remove the disk.

umm, as my "name" and signature may suggest, I was at this point doing some intense prayer. It didn't hurt as I have been into MS-XP and from System in the CP, I reset the waiting time of the dual-boot choice. It defaults at 1 second. Not enough time. 

I don't promise this will work but since it did for me, and I am only anything due to my faith, it should work for you. try it 1st. I had read all the stuff about NTFS and FAT 32 hassles, Swap Files, etc. I ignored all that stuff and Richard Grant gets the nod.
