### Post by jdong on 2004-11-30
I've been using Linux, believe it or not, for only a bit more than two years... Before then, I was an all-out Windows nerd before that -- able to recall from memory the exact names of all the IIS configuration menus, etc etc etc.


Back then, I viewed Linux skeptically- thinking that it's some weird OS that couldn't accomplish any Windows tasks, is completely incompatible with Windows, etc etc.

Then, there was Knoppix... Within a day of discovering Knoppix, I installed it to my hard drive. I started to piece together knowledge-- and being the computer nerd I am, I primarily focused on the command line aspect of Linux... heck I even started reading UNIX administration manuals, which amazingly provide some of the most clever tricks!

I stayed with Knoppix / Debian-SID for a year or so, and then started trying other distros. First, I went to RedHat 9 at the time, which I didn't like too much. Then, I found Mandrake -- which I really liked and stayed with for half a year or so... Then Mandrake got boring, and I decided to take the Gentoo plunge...


Gentoo (to me), was actually quite simple to install and manage -- just time consuming! After a year with Gentoo, I got absolutely fed up with the slow compiling of every update (and how every other week you have to backcompile everything else, because of a libc update or something of that degree!), so I moved back to Debian-SID.

It was around this time that Reiser4 was released, and I started toying heavily with that. My testbeds were Gentoo and Debian-Sid for that phase... Then, it was October and I found Ubuntu...

I love Ubuntu -- I've tried almost every distro at some point in time, and i have to say that Ubuntu is and will be the best choice.

In fact, just yesterday I decided to [switch from Ubuntu back to Fedora Core 3.... ](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=6553) That lasted about 2 hours before I reformatted back to Ubuntu!

And here I am today...  When I started using Linux, I dual-booted it with Windows... At the beginning, I fell back to Windows on a regular basis, treating Linux as a toy. Now, I primarily use Linux -- my computers don't have Windows period. And I have not looked back ever since.
