### Post by rodh on 2010-06-08
I am getting ready to do a fresh install 10.04,  I have been reading many post about seperation of root and home partitions,  I have a 300G SATA drive and a 250 G IDE drive.  would it be best for the OS to be on the faster drive or for the /Home

---

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2010-06-08
OS and /home on the fastest! You are not going to need much more that 10-15Gb for you OS (it installs on less than half that, and you /home can then be the rest of the drive, with a 2Gb swap after that (slowest part of the fastest drive). 

Other drive for storage and backup of your /home in case fast drive dies. ;)

### Post by oldfred on 2010-06-08
I do not know. You cannot type faster. Internet will not be faster. And those are the biggest speed bottlenecks. More memory always is faster.

I prefer to have entire system on one drive and different systems on every drive. that way if one drive dies I still can boot the other. In your case if either drives die you cannot boot.

I think system is used more, but do not know for sure.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2010-06-08
I kind of agree with oldfred. For day to day, ah. But if you're doing high demand processing and data shuffling like video editing, multi-track audio, and other AV tasks, you need to be particular about these things. I would have my OS on a super-fast twenty Gb drive if poss (read: affordable!).
