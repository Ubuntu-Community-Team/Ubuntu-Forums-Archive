### Post by SIlvio77 on 2008-11-17
Hi

So, I've managed to resolve the problem. This might not be the best way, but i changed the hard drive on which Ubuntu was booting from and now it works fine. 
I tired re-installing and it wouldn't have it, so i figured maybe it's the hard drive, and it was.
I simply took a hard drive that was lying around, which had a copy of Ubuntu on it, and it just worked.
Like I said maybe not the best way but it works. You might want to check the integrity of your hard drive.
