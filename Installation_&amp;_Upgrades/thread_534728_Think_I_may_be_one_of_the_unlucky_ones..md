### Post by liquidfunk on 2007-08-25
Magically I acquired an NVIDIA graphics card off of one of my friends. Its not the best card, but compared to my old Onboard Intel, its glorious. 

 I burnt off a CD of LinuxMint, after hearing that it is so good with everything be able to work straight away, codecs there, wireless, and desktop effects out of the box. 

 After a rather short bootup I was greeted into a really attractive GUI, one of the main reasons I chose Mint. Straight away I noticed that wireless wasn't connecting, I'm no stranger to this. 
 
I installed Mint, thinking it may have been just a LiveCD thing. Hoping to try again after the install.

Luckily NDISwrapper was preinstalled, which, having used it successfully before. It was looking up. I copied over the drivers from the Install Disc, (the USB wireless was plugged in from the start). Pressed install after having selected the .inf file and it told me the card was not detected.  I wondered if the computer needed to reboot before it would detect my card. I was wrong, it didn't detect my card at all. I tried numerous tutorials off of the Mint Forums, which led me nowhere. 

 I gave up on Mint.

 I thought seeing as I've had so much 'happiness' with Ubuntu, I may as well start using 7.04. I did the install, got NDISwrapper from Synaptic, after discovering it was not detected automatically. Still it told me it was not detected. 

 So, in the end, I switched back to 6.10, which is where I am now. Long day. 

 Its annoying how in 6.10, I installed NDISwrapper, installed the .inf, and it detected it straight away. 7.04 and Mint, didn't detect it at all.

 Shocking.
