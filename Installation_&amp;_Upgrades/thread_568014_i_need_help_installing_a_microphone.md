### Post by PaulFXH on 2007-10-05
I presume you want to record stuff with the microphone, right?
Then right-click on the Sound Volume icon in the top tray and click Open Volume Control.
Then click Edit>Preferences and check everything.
Next, under the Playback tab, make sure everything is unmuted (left-click to toggle mute-unmute) and turned up
Then do the same under the Recording tab.
Next, under the Switches tab, make sure everything is checked EXCEPT FOR the Mic as Output. Under the Options tab, choose Mic as Input.

Now go to Applications>Sound & Video>Sound Recorder. Here choose Mux in the Record from input box and Record your own voice for a few seconds.
Now click File>Sava As and give the file a name. Now click Play.
Can you hear your voice?

This works for me on my MacBook. YMMV
Good luck
Paul

### Post by PaulFXH on 2007-10-05
Please don't worry about the fact that your microphone doesn't show up. This is really, genuinely and truly NOT a problem.
I'm using a Gamma headset with microphone in Ubuntu right now and it doesn't show up anywhere. But guess what....it works absolutely perfectly!

As for the directions I gave in my first post, you really have to do ALL of them precisely as written. Half-*ssed doesn't work here.

As for your question about where Sound Recorder is, do you see in the top panel (usually on the left hand side) something like this: Applications Places System?
If so, click Applications and then click Sound & Video in the box that opens. Get the idea?
If Applications Places System is NOT there, you can add it as an applet by left clicking on the panel and choosing this applet from the available list.
Good luck
