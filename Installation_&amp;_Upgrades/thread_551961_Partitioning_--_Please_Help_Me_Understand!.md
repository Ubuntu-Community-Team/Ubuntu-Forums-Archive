### Post by Pumalite on 2007-09-16
MSchenker:
Lets stick to what we have for the time being. Desktop, 1 internal hard drive that we are going to partition to use Ubuntu.
First defrag XP several times, erase all temp files, then use Gparted: [http://gparted.sourceforge.net/download.php](http://gparted.sourceforge.net/download.php), to re-size your XP partition.
Download the Gparted iso, burn it to CD as an 'image' not 'data', boot from it (you have to make sure that in BIOS, CD is first in boot order), one in, at the boot promp, hit 'Enter', you will see the XP partition; right click on it and 'resize'.
