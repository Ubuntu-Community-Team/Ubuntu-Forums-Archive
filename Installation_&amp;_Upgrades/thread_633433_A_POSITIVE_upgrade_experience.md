### Post by gpilkay on 2007-12-06
I admit I'm a member of the “two month” club, I wait till two months after a release to upgrade.  Much MUCH longer for Windows upgrades (even my brand new Dell Vostro runs XP, not Vista) but anyway, I just wanted to put in a positive upgrade experience so people ill know it's not all bad.  

After a disastrous install on the Laptop (I only use Linux on it with a Live-CD now, due to the gross incompatibility...I hope Hardy fixes it) I was hesitant about taking my desktop 7.04 to 7.10, but after my school semester was over and I had nothing to worry about if I had to suffer any down-time, I backed up all my files from my Ubuntu disk (I have two hard drives on the desktop, one Ubuntu as MASTER and XP as SLAVE so Ubuntu handles the duel-booting) and then closed all applications in Ubuntu.  I clicked through the menus, and left it connected to the DSL while I used my laptop to watch a DVD.

When my movie finished, my installs were largely done.  Word of advice:  Even if you have no idea what you are looking at, click the little 'details' arrow as the updates are being installed, it's fascinating.  Plus you will have a good idea of what is REALLY happening.  As the updates are applied, you can actally watch as your 7.04 icons change to the 7.10 version.  You can't see that in Windows!  

There were a few weird things as the updates installed.  It did try to access the XP drive once (I unmounted it, and it got on with the install) and afterwards, I lost my little startup music ditty, which is a known bug and is beign worked on.  Other then those very minor things, the upgrade went flawlessly, and I have a brand new 7.10 system ready to go.

Afterwards, I did run through and clean up a lot of orphaned packages, the upgrade seemed to leave quite a few all over the place, but I'm very pleased with the results.  

For reference, I have an E-machines, with two 80 gig hard drives, 2 gig of RAM, and a Celeron D processor.
