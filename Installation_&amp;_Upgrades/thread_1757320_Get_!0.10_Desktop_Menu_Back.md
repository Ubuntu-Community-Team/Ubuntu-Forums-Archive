### Post by BruisedQuasar07 on 2011-05-16
FOUND IT! For users experiencing my problem of not having available to them, the suggested way of getting the "Ubuntu Classic" desktop (menu) back.

Here is what worked for me. I make it wordy, step by step so absolute newbies can follow what I did. (Ubuntu used to be as user friendly as my instructions try to be)

At the top of the crazy Unity Ubuntu 11.04 desktop (your screen), look to the right top corner. Find there what users call the power button. It's the last icon found in the top right corner. Click it.

In the drop down menu look for "system settings" (should be the last choice). Click it.

look for "System" heading. Under "System" find
"login screen" click it.

In the box you get, find and click "unlock".
find and click "show list of users". You can then "select ....as default session". Select
"Ubuntu Classic" Close the box and reboot.

Warning. Be patient. When you boot, it will take even longer than 11.04 already takes (my Windows 7 takes less time to boot on both laptops that also have Ubuntu 11.04)and you might not see the cursor you are accustomed to seeing. You will get a login box. after that,
there will be delay booting. Just wait and you will eventually hear the familiar music and the desktop will appear, the one that is user friendly and modern Linux, not the new Mac wannabe desktop "unity".
