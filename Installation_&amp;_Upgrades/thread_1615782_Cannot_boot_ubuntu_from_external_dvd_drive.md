### Post by oldfred on 2010-11-07
Does your BIOS support USB boot. You should be able to see USB options when in BIOS. Have you updated BIOS to newest version? If so perhaps CD may work better than DVD.

My portable is about 3 & half years old and boots from USB devices. I have changed to flash drives as I can keep updating them.

If not you can either replace CD or remove HD and plug into another computer that you can use to install. Just keep generic drivers, if offered to change until reinstalled into your portable.

### Post by oldfred on 2010-11-07
I found my USB flash under hard drive choice with my BIOS you may also want to look there. You can then also try the usb flash drive.

Also instructions for CD or USB flash drive
[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download)

Using another computer is just like another install to a second drive. The most important thing is to make sure grub is installed to the second drive's MBR. You have to use manual partitioning to get the combo box that lets you choose.

Maverick partition by hand to use free space:
