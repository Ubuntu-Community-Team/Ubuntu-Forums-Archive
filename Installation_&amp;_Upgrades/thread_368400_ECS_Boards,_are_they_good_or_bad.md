### Post by hipnerd on 2007-02-23
I think, that ECS boards are generally considered poorer quality than many of the bigger-name boards. But, the reality is that they are generally aimed at the budget market. They are often slightly slower, or have reduced feature-sets, but they are also less expensive. 

I don't know of any current manufacturer that makes boards that have unacceptably high failure rates or bug-ridden BIOS. But everyone kicks out a lemon occasionally. At any rate, I don't think you can say "All ECS boards are bad" or "All Abit boards are good."

So the question becomes: Does a specific  ECS board have all the features I want at a decent price point? (And is it well-supported in Linux?)


I use [Tom's Hardware Guide]("http://www.tomshardware.com/") to compare motherboards, and have bought many different manufacturer's boards over the years depending on my needs and who had the best power/price ratio at the moment.
