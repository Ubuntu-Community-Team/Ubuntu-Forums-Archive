### Post by Bert Berlin on 2007-12-29
YOu didn't mention how old the Sansa E280 is, nor how much use it has had.  Generally, that problem of the sound erratically switching from one ear to the other occurs when the jack (the hole you plug the headphones into) gets damaged from stress - such as pulling the headphone wires from one side to the other.  This spreads the contacts inside the jack so that it can't touch the plug properly, and, as the plug moves around, when you turn your head, for example, the contacts in the jack touch first one side of the plug, then the other instead of both at the same time.
If the Sansa is still under warranty, try to get it replaced under warranty.  If not, Sandisk can probably repair it for you for a fee, but, if you bought the Sansa at a discount, you may find the repair fee to be more than the replacement cost.
