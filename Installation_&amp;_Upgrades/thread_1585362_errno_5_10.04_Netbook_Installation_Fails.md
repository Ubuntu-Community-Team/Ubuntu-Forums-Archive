### Post by mercadop on 2010-10-01
Just wanted to follow-up that I resolved the issue.  Initially, I had used a 2GB PNY USB Flash Drive to perform the installation. As a last resort, I went to the computer store and exchanged it for a 4GB Kingston USB Flash Drive.  I re-created the installation files and the install completed.  I don't know if the resolution was the 4GB or the maker, but I will leave that interpretation to others.  

On a separate note, I was not able to get Wireless to work after the install, but I did install the drivers through Synaptic software manager (needed ethernet connection at first), and after rebooting wireless worked.
