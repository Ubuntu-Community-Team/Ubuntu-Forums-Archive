### Post by Soybean on 2007-06-14
Yeah, dual monitors isn't a feature that's automatically configured. If you had a dual-head NVidia card, I believe their drivers can set it up almost automatically now... but that's not very helpful if you don't.

There's a guide on the subject that covers a few different techniques here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=221174](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=221174)

One thing I'd recommend is making a copy of your current xorg.conf (which works with the Intel card), then setting up a similar one that works with just the ATI card, and making a copy of that one too. THEN move on to trying to get them to both work together. By doing it in pieces like that, if you make a typo or misunderstand a setting or something, it's a lot easier to tell where the problem is.

> The only thing left to figure out is how to get the taskbars to extend across both instead of just the one desktop. Should be a bit easier though.
I'm not sure if you can actually stretch one bar across the two screens, but what you can do (it's almost the same thing) is add another bar on the other screen. Just right click on an empty space in one of your current bars and click "New Panel." Then just drag the new panel to where you want it.

What I do is actually drag one of the two original bars to the other screen, so I've either got one long bar along the top or the bottom (I alternate a bit). A dual monitor setup is already a lot wider than it is high, so I don't like to waste any extra vertical space.
