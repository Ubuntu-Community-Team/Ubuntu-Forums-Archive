### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-04-25
Here is a screen shot of the options in 11.04 from the LiveCD/LiveUSB:

[IMG]http://img848.imageshack.us/img848/874/upgradetonatty.png[/IMG]

You can try an upgrade first, and if you don't like the results, then do an clean install.

If you have a dual boot system, you may have to select the 'Something Else' option, then choose your current Ubunut 'partitions and check-mark the 'format partition' to wipe out the old data.

If you have a separate '/home' partition, don't format that; just format the '/' (root) partition.

### Post by oregonbob on 2011-04-25
You can install VirtualBox, download any ISO of Ubuntu and quickly build an experimental 11.04 as a virtual machine. I installed 11.04 on a USB hard drive for a test. I found I had lots of special configuration with my multimedia equip, etc. I have it all just perfect in 10.10, but I would have to apply all that configuration on the new Beta2 version. My Hauppagge TV card didn't work on new version. So I have returned to 10.10 for a while.

You should try live CD, virtualbox or USB of 11.04 before upgrading.
