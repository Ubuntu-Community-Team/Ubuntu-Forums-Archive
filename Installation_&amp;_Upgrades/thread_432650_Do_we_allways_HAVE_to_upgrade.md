### Post by Chonnawonga on 2007-05-04
t_ras,

I'm no expert in these things, but I believe the idea with the releases is to have one whole set of packages that works together, ensuring (as much as these things can ever be sure) a coherent, stable system. You can, of course, upgrade or change one thing on its own. If you couldn't, Ubuntu wouldn't be much of an OS. (I think we've all had enough of that locked-in feeling elsewhere.) But by having a stable, universal starting point, it makes everything more likely to work and easier to troubleshoot.

...or so goes the theory. It doesn't always work that way in practice, of course. ;)
