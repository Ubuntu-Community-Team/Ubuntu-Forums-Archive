### Post by g3r41d on 2006-01-29
I had this problem recently. And i believed alot of the people here also encounter the same fate.. After downloading so many copies of the iso and burning countless copies of ubuntu, kubuntu and live cds , we still could not get it to run. The installation would continue fine to mounting. And at copying files, somewhere it would hang. 

Here is my solution.
- First try to burn the iso at the slowest speed possible. I did mine with nero @ 14x.
- Do installation with expert mode. Do not do the default installation of just pressing enter. During installation. When they prompt for parameters for cdrom.. enter '-d1'. To enable dma which is required for most cdroms.

Continue all the way.. It should work. At least for my case.. Try it out
