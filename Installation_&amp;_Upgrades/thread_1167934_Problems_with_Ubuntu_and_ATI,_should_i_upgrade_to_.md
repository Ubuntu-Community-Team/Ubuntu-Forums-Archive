### Post by ALIENDUDE5300 on 2009-05-23
I had no problems with upgrading with an ATI Radeon 4850HD card installed. Everything works fine, even the proprietary drivers. However on my other computer which I also upgraded on, which has an ATI Radeon X1950 PRO card, I'm forced to use the open-source MESA drivers. Depending on what card you have, you should either upgrade or not upgrade. I think anything 2100HD or below will not work with the new proprietary drivers, but I'd double check before making a choice.

### Post by topcat5 on 2009-05-24
Generally, expect a lot of issues if you have an ATI card.  I've got two laptops with ati graphics and both have had just constant issues with every Ubuntu upgrade.  One of the laptops is fairly new and worked with Ubuntu for just one release, 8.10, and now is unsupported.  

Ubuntu is free to the user so I have no expectations that it will work at all but the lesson to be learned here is to simply avoid any purchases of ATI gear in the future.   I absolutely will not purchase another system with an AMD processor or ATI card in it.   It's really a shame as I once was a believer in AMD.

For now I guess I am staying on 8.10.

I think there's a few threads that go on the 'have you had problems with jaunty upgrade'. Maybe you'll just have to find out what suits you.
