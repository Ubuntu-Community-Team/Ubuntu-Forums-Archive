### Post by quixote on 2010-05-06
Unfortunately, afaik, you can't skip versions when upgrading.  You can go straight from Long Term Support version to the next one up (eg Dapper > Hardy > Lucid).  With the short term versions (eg jaunty 9.04 or karmic 9.10), you have to go through each step up.

Which in your case sounds like a showstopper. :(

I had a situation like that with a fussy laptop.  I ended up backing up my /home, doing a clean install, and trying to remember all the customizations I'd made.  It actually wasn't as horrible as I'd feared.  Synaptic makes reinstalling stuff about as easy as it can be, and if you have your /home on a separate partition then all your config files are preserved.  Themes, settings, and so on are all there.

Test Lucid out with a livecd first though!
