### Post by fflarex on 2007-07-02
Just insert the disc and turn on your computer. If for any reason nothing happens, you might have to mess with your BIOS settings to make your system boot from a disc drive. This is usually only necessary on older systems though.

If it *does* load from the disc, but you get an error, you'll need to make sure your hardware (and especially your video card) is compatible. A lot of times there are workarounds. You may have install from the alternate install CD, which is not at all intuitive like the live CD is.

Basically what I'm trying to say is that if everything goes right and all your hardware is compatible, installation will be so easy you won't believe it. Otherwise, you may be in for some frustration. My own laptop took 2 weeks to get everything working correctly, and the only reason I didn't go back to windows during that time is because I didn't have a backup disc. Don't regret it at all though.
