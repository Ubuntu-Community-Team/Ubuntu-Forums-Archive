### Post by Circus-Killer on 2009-01-15
well, its basically stability vs latest'n'greatest.

if you stay with hardy, which is an LTS, it will be far more stable. if you upgrade, your applications will be at newer versions, some may have new features, etc.

but a word of warning, back-up all your important information before upgrading. upgrades sometimes don't go as plan. a lot of people, including myself, prefer to do a clean install when we upgrade. the problem with this is that it will wipe out your whole harddrive, so backup your info and stuff, do a fresh install, then just restore all your stuff back.

but as one poster said, if you happy with hardy, and it does what you need to do, you might as well just stay with it for now, until you get the hang of things a bit more.
