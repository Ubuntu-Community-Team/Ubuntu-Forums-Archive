### Post by bobpaul on 2008-04-19
The 8.04 LiveCD has been updated for clarity, to suit users who were scared to try Ubuntu because they thought "Start/Install Ubuntu" might format/erase their HD. That's good, but one of the MAJOR benefits that Ubuntu had over other OS installs was that one could minimize the installer and run Firefox while they're waiting for it to finish. With other operating systems, one had to wait 20-30 minutes while the computer was essentially unusable, while Ubuntu gave you a working system and THEN did the install in the background.

In my mind, we've reverted with the new behavior, even if users can still click Try Ubuntu and double click the Ubuiquity icon on the desktop, just like before. Most users, even previous users, will probably select "Install Ubuntu" only to find the computer is unusable for the next 20 minutes.

Is there a reason other than "this is what users expect" for the change? Why not have 2 options, as now, but have BOTH boot into the desktop environment. The "Install" option could launch Ubiquity automatically, perhaps even before Gnome-session. Perhaps even Ubiquity could load as currently on 8.04, but with an icon present on every page to "Continue loading live environment" or similar. This would just launch gnome-session, allowing the panels and desktop to load up behind the Ubiquity wizard. 

I've seen little comment on this issue other than, "It's new! It's great!" Am I alone?
