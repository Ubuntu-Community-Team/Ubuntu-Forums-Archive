### Post by KCPokes on 2007-10-17
There are a bunch of threads recently about some of the lightweight distros that might work for you.  You might want to check them out.  On a personal note I have an old Tecra 8000 with a 266Mhz Pentium II and 128MB of RAM.  This used to be my old test box for all the different distros.  As of late I've ran Xubuntu (still too heavy), Zenwalk (dug it but didnt like my ISA sound card), Mepis (same issue with sound), and now I'm on Arch Linux and loving it.  You get what you choose to install on Arch, nothing more.  I'm running Arch with OpenBox and I'm impressed how well it is running.  

In regards to what you've attempted, I've ran into the same issues with Puppy and DSL; neither would install on my machine.  Puppy would just hang during the graphics setup, regardless of the choice of xorg or vesa.
