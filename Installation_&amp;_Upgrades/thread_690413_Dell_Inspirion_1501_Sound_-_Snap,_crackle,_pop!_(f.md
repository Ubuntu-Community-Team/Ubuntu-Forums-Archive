### Post by lil0tik on 2008-02-07
Hey All - Wondering about a fix for apparently widespread audio problems on Dell Inspirion 1501.

I have a new Dell 1501 laptop (AMD Duo 64, dual-boot Vista & Gutsy), and - like many others -  have been having nightmarish problems with audio.  From the time of purchase, the audio (through headphones) was terribly distorted by loud pops, static, and crackling...  After chatting for a bit with a Dell tech-rep, I sent the computer in for a motherboard replacement.  It did absolutely nothing to help, and I'm still troubled by audio issues.

In Vista, the audio issues are solved (solved! after a month and a half of working on it!) simply by installing the newest ATI Catalyst drivers.  ATI makes the motherboard for the 1501 as well, so they sneaked in a MB update into the video-driver update...  This version is NOT available via Dell Support, and one must go to the ATI site to get it.

SO...  I am wondering if anyone has had any luck solving the audio issues in Ubuntu.  Using the OSS audio driver instead of ALSA seems to help a bit (I still haven't gotten Jack working correctly, but that's for a different post), but the problem still seems to be with the video drivers / motherboard...  I haven't tried installing the ATI drivers via Envy, etc., and am just using the ATI flgrx drivers.

Any insight into this issue is appreciated!  I know there are more people than me out there who are / have had to deal with this...
