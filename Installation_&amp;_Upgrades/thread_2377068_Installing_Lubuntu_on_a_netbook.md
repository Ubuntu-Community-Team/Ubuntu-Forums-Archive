### Post by DuckHook on 2017-11-08
> **jacatone said:**
> I'd like to install Lubuntu on a little netbook listed below, but haven't a clue how to do this.
The following instructions are for Ubuntu, but the process for Lubuntu is very similar. This should give you a starting point: [https://tutorials.ubuntu.com/tutorial/tutorial-install-ubuntu-desktop#0](https://tutorials.ubuntu.com/tutorial/tutorial-install-ubuntu-desktop#0)

Another more general guide: [https://rtechsupport.org/kb/linux-installation/](https://rtechsupport.org/kb/linux-installation/)

Your choice of Lubuntu is a good one. Ubuntu won't run well on your netbook. I'm not sure what IOB means either but you can download a Lubuntu LiveUSB and test run it first. Lubuntu downloads are available here: [http://lubuntu.me/downloads/](http://lubuntu.me/downloads/)

If you know how, it is better to download a torrent using the magnet link. Torrents are self correcting and won't stress Ubuntu's server bandwidth.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2017-11-09
I have what appears to be an almost identical spec netbook (I think it's a re-badged MSI Wind U100) which is running Lubuntu 16.04 without a problem.

It's not the fastest in the world, but you would not expect it to be with that spec, but it is very usable for a bit of web-browsing, emailing, and watching video or listening to music (with headphones; the speakers are hopeless).

Try it with a live USB (F11 in my case to bring up the boot device menu and boot from USB) and you may find the same as I do, that is too good to throw away just because it's 7 years old.
