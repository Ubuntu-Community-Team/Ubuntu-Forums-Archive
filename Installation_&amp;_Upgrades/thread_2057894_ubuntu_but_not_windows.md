### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-09-14
Not much to go on regarding details ... so you're asking us all to guess ...

As to why you could get Ubuntu to work after it sat for months, my guess would be that the spilled liquid dried out and, fortunately, the residue was nonconductive.

As to slow booting of Win7, that typically indicates hard drive faults, but since that is sealed, your spill would have nothing to do with that.  Maybe during startup, it was running keyboard checks and parts of your keyboard circuit pad were shorting out.  Who know -- lots of guessing, here?

Have you tried installing Win7 since you got Ubuntu loaded? If so, rather than saying "to no avail", if you really want help in that, you should tell us what DOES happen -- how far it gets, what you see on-screen, what error messages are shown, etc.
