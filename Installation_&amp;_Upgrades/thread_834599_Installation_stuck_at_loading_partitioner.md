### Post by matty-K on 2008-06-19
Hey everyone...

This is the first time I've experienced this problem installing Ubuntu and Kubuntu.

Basically I have been trying to get my WMP54GS installed so I decided to do a clean install. I've done several clean installs in the past while in attempts to make it work. I've tried Ubuntu and Kubuntu and have had both installed correctly. However, this particular time I cannot seem to install Ubunutu OR Kubuntu (both 7.10 Gutsy)

After selecting my keyboard layout, the bar that says "Starting up the partitioner" fills up quickly up to 46%. That's where the text says *Scanning disks...*. I knew it took a couple of minutes before, but today it is not doing anything. I can still click buttons, so the program is not completely frozen. I have rebooted, I have gone into Windows Recovery and went fixmbr to fix the dual-boot loader (in case that was the problem), but no matter what I do I cannot get past 46% at this particular stage. Clicking 'skip' on the bar does not allow me to continue the installation.

Any thoughts?

---

### Post by matty-K on 2008-06-19
Well, I figured it out by accident! Turns out it was getting stuck because I had my iPod plugged in! I just unplugged it (forgot to eject it, oh well) and restarted the installation and it went no problem. 

Hope this helps other people out.
