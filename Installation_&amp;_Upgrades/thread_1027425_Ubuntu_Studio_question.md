### Post by secondstage on 2009-01-01
It is not recommended to do a system upgrade, because there is a chance that a configuration file, or something else will not be removed, thus causing problems later on. But if you still want to...

Because the 8.04 release is long-term, Ubuntu expects that you would stay with the long-term. To change the default, open Synaptic (System > Administration > Synaptic). Type your password, and hit enter. Once Synaptic is open, go to Settings, then Repositories. Click on the "Updates" tab. Look for the release upgrade option, and change it from "Long Term Support Releases Only" to "Normal Releases".

I do not know whether or not it will prompt you to upgrade after you close Synaptic, or after a reboot.
