### Post by martalli on 2007-04-27
Hello,

I was on the irc channel a day or two ago when a user asked about upgrading from kubuntu dapper to feisty.  He had been asking another question, when we discovered that he was on dapper (lsb_release -d), making helping a little more difficult.

I googled a bit and seemed to understand that he would have to upgrade from dapper to edgy and then to feisty.  Is that correct?  The standard recommendation for updating wasn't immediately open to him, requiring installation of several gtk programs and dependencies, but I couldn't find any other kubuntu-specific recommendations.

Well, to add to the problems, he was using a modem, so a dist-upgrade would have been a exercise taking days.  Could it be achieved with kubuntu edgy and kubuntu feisty install disks?  Apparently, he had a fairly generic install, with few extra programs installed, but what if he had lots of extra stuff installed?  Actually, this last part is mostly a question for me, since I was considering an upgrade of the edubuntu edgy installs at my son's latchkey program, and anything to shorten the download times would be nice.  Which disks should be used - alt installs and dvds?  Are desktop install disks also OK? 

I figure these questions are pretty succinct, but I couldn't find exact answers out on the web.  Thanks!

First, you are correct in that one can't upgrade from dapper to feisty.  Got to make a stop at edgy on the way.  On dial-up, that could take quite a while.  In his position, I'd likely download a new install disk and just save what I wanted to keep from my home directory (mail, etc).

For offline upgrades (or even just to save bandwidth) you need the alt cd.  (The install cd is set up a little differently.)  I don't know if you would need the dvd or not, but there wouldn't be that much to download afterwards this early after the release date.  Depends on whether the computer has a connection or not.
