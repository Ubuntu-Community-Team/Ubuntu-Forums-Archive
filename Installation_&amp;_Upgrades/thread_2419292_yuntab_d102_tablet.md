### Post by DuckHook on 2019-05-19
What you are proposing to do is far from trivial.

AFAIK, the one attempt at producing an Ubuntu mobile was Canonical's now abandoned Ubuntu Phone project. If you are determined to proceed, look through the [Mobile Discussions]("https://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=426") forum for further discussion/suggestions.

You may find that the barriers to success are just too high. If so, try installing *Blokada* on that android device. It is the best ad blocker I have ever found for Android. You will first have to add the FDroid repo. A web search will give you plenty of instructions on how to do this. Since FDroid and Blokada are FOSS, you will be getting the next best thing to a native Ubuntu environment.

Blokada runs as a quasi-VPN service so it blocks not only in-browser ads but all of them, including in-app ads. You may find this enough to make the tablet usable again and avoid the need/trouble/headache of monkeying around converting it to Ubuntu.
