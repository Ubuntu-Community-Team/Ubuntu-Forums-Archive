### Post by iaculallad on 2008-04-26
Try this one, this might fix your resolution:

In your terminal, copy the line below:

sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf

then find the sections located in your xorg.conf file

*Section "Monitor"
*	Identifier	"Configured Monitor"
	HorizSync 30-110
	VertRefresh 50-160
*EndSection

*Section "Screen"
*	Identifier	"Default Screen"
*	Monitor		"Configured Monitor"
*	Device		"Configured Video Device"
	DefaultDepth 24
		SubSection "Display"
		Depth 24
		Modes "1280x1024"
	EndSubSection
*EndSection

Copy and paste the lines which does not contain asterisk to your config file. Then restart your graphical display.
