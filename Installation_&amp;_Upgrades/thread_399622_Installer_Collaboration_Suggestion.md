### Post by mweichert on 2007-04-02
Hello,

I just wanted to post my two cents on the Ubuntu installer. I think all the major linux distros, Ubuntu, Redhat, Novell, have accomplished to provide an easy-to-use installer for Linux. 

I like Ubuntu's approach, featuring a live environment to work while the installer works in the background. It reminds me of the BeOS installer in some ways.

Anyhow, it's disappointing to see less collaboration between the distros in this regard. Each distro is recreating the wheel instead of collaborating together. We now have ubiquity, debian installer, anaconda, and yast. Also, we have "autoinstall" methods for each: preseed, kickstart and autoyast - each with different tools and configurations. 

I would love to see more collaboration in this department so that sys admins can easily deploy linux in heterogeneous environments. 

IMHO, ubiquity and kickstart are the best foundation - but both could learn from the other efforts as well I'm sure. 

Mike
