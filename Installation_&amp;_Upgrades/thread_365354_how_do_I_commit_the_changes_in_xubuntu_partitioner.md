### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-22
The partitioner won't let you continue if there is a fundamental flaw in the partitioning scheme.  Sometimes it won't give any clues as to what the problem is.  A smart user can spot it and correct it and then the partitioner will let you write the changes.

New forum members always post lots of detail when they ask questions.

From your post it is difficult to tell the following:

If you have windows already on the disk . . .
How big the disk is . . .
How big the Ubuntu partition is . . .
If you created a swap partition . . .

This information wil give insight into how the partitioner may be behaving.
