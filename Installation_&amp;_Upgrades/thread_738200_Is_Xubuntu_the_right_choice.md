### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-03-28
I do not know if the spec's for Xubuntu go that low or not, but I do know the Puppy Linux should work fine on it. I keep Puppy on a gig USB stick, so 3 gigs is actually a fairly large space for Puppy, and it is designed to work for older computers.

All I can say is try Xubuntu, and if it doesn't work, try Puppy. You may have to use ndiswapper for the wireless, though, so be prepared for it not to work right away. Have a cable ready to plug in to access the net with. If you have the drivers that came with it, Puppy should be able to use them without the need for the net. Just pop the CD and browse it with ndiswrapper till you get the right file.
