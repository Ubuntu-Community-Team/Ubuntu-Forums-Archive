### Post by zvacet on 2009-08-18
Did you checked [md5sum]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM")? If it is not correct you will not install.You can download same iso with torrent and point download to the folder where your existing iso is.Torrent will just check iso for bad files and replace them with good ones.After that run md5sum again and if it is O.K. burn at lower possible speed.Check disc integrity and i its good you are ready for install.

### Post by presence1960 on 2009-08-18
do as zvacet says. When you burn a new CD if your software won't let you choose burn speed look [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/9.04/switching/installing-burning.html")
Slow speed is better, faster speeds work great on data CD/DVDs, but on burning an image slow speeds are best- they seem to produce very few errors.
