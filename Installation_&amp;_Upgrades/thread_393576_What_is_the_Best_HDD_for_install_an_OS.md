### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-03-25
It depends on your budget.  I assume this is a desktop machine.

You can get a cheap IDE, 5400 rpm, consumer grade drive, 250 GB for around $80 US.

You can get a better, IDE, 7200 rpm, business grade drive for about $120.

You can get a server-grade, 10,000 rpm drive for even more money.

I did a google search for prices and found this piece of advice:

[http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/archives/000800.html](http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/archives/000800.html)

Basically:  small 10,000 rpm drive for boot and OS, second larger 7200 rpm drive for data.

Some other points to think about:

Put a /swap partition on it (1-4 GB).  This is helpful for testing any live distros.
Extra Linux partitions (4-20 GB each) for newer Linux Distros.  For instance Dapper, Edgy, Feisty are three in the Ubuntu family alone.  This way you always have a stable environment to go back to when the latest distro takes a dump.

Break in your drive before doing a final install.  You can use one of the utilities from Ultimate Boot CD to shake up your drive to make sure it is up to snuff.

Good luck.
