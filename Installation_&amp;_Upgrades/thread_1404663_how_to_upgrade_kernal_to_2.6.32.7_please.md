### Post by khelben1979 on 2010-02-12
1. Open up [Synaptic Package Manager]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synaptic_package_manager").

2. Search for Linux kernel in Synaptic (will give you a list).

3. Choose the one you want and click on install.

4. Reboot the system.
