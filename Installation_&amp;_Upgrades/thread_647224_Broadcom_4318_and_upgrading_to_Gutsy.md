### Post by rewrittenfromscratch on 2007-12-22
I'm currently running Feisty on my laptop, a Gateway Solo 1450, and it took me a serious amount of magic to finally get wireless up and running with my Broadcom 4318. In the end, although I tried every possible Broadcom solution I could find, the one that worked was using Ndisgtk. Now my wireless is up and running just fine, though it literally took me weeks, during which time the only connection I had was my wireless in my dual-boot Windows, forcing me to reboot every time I wanted to try a new fix. It was torture.

More to the point, though, my first question is whether upgrading to Gutsy will force me to potentially reinstall the drivers for my wireless. For some reason, I have a bad feeling that after the upgrade, something or many things are going to be overwritten, and the fixes I have now will cease to work.

Second... What do I gain from the upgrade? The only major reasons I've seen are the GUI for multipe monitors (which doesn't concern me, because I've tried Gutsy before, and no amount of tinkering could actually make this work) and the new repositories. Of course, the shiny new repos make my mouth water.

I think it's possible that the only reason I want to upgrade is because I'm upgrade-addicted. More specifically, I'm update-addicted. Some of the programs that I'm using now are backports or were installed from outside the repos (like Pidgin), and I like to keep all my packages updated as best I can. For this reason, having the new repos is a pretty big deal.

So, just to recap, will I be risking my miraculously-working wireless if I upgrade to Gutsy? And what would I potentially gain from risking it?
