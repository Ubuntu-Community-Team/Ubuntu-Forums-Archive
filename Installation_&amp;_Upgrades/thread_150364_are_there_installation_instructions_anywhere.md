### Post by Herman on 2006-03-25
[Installation - Ubuntu Wiki]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Installation?highlight=%28dual%29%7C%28boot%29%7C%28install%29#head-42fa62c81b02633b39beaf29de47aced9077733e")
The link above is the install instructions from the official Ubuntu Wiki, and below on the left (my sig link)  is a website with some examples of installs with illustrations for you to see how it's likely to go. There are three examples to look at on different pages, you can pick one and do something similar if you wish. There are some other pages with extra info there too just for a little extra help.
In no case is anything there meant to contradict anything in the official Wiki instructions, if any of it does, follow the instructions in the official Ubunu Wiki.  (Except where the official Wiki says the Ubuntu installer/partitioner can't resize NTFS, that info has been obsolete since 'the 'Warty' release was superceded by 'Hoary', I am sure, but they still leave that there in the Wiki. I guess the people who edit the Wiki  are extremely busy, they do a great job but have a lot on their plate.)
Anyhow, good luck! :D (Herman)

P.S. If you don't want to dual boot you can still do the same thing as the Ubuntu + (WIndows with) FAT32 install, but just choose 'Erase Entire disk' at [!!] Partition Disks (illustration no. eight), and skip the partitioning section. Except for that, a Ubuntu-only install is quite similar to the dual-boot install, but even easier! (You'll see what I mean when you look at the links).
