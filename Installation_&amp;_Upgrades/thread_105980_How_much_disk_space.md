Just over two gigs for files, filesystem and swap.  Go for at least three to five gigs, though.

1 partitaion for Ubuntu

Sure, if you want.  The shared partitions can be formatted as vfat (fat32) so that it is readable by both OSes.
You can do the partitioning by hand with the installer.

### Post by Koybe on 2005-12-19
It depends on your needs.
I would say 5Gb is more then enough for installing, but then if you add many apps and/or files, it can fly higher. You don't even give your HD capacity?

Just go for something like 15Gb for Windows, 10Gb for Ubuntu and the rest for you "both file partition". Don't forget to use FAT32 for that one.
