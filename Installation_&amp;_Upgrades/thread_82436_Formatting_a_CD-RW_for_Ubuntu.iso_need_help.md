### Post by Efwis on 2005-10-26
most burners use the same structure. i.e. if it see the disk it will burn to it.

I recently ran into a problem similar to yours and it turned out that the disks were bad when they were made. no rhyme or reason to it.

Have you tried to jsut burn the iso to the disk using any program??

The reason I ask is because most cd-rw's aren't formatted in the real sense of the term. CD-rw disks are blank disks with no info on them. its not like a floppy disk from back in the days of 5.25 floppies,where you had to format the disk to work on a specific machine.

I would recommend just trying to burn the ISO onto the disk, if it doesn't work then contact the manufacturer of the disk and tell them what happened. thats what I did and they sent me an entire replacement pack of disks without a reciept.
