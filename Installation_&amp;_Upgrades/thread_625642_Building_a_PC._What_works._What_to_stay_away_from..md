### Post by him610 on 2007-11-28
I recently finished a homebuilt; my first in several years. The technology has advanced, but it is still familiar. I thoroughly enjoyed it. 

Any one of the dual-core CPUs from either AMD, Intel, or even VIA will probably do just fine so long as it is compatible with the motherboard you choose. You might want to check the cpu charts at tomshardwareguide.com ; choose alternative solutions that fit your budget. 

Fortunately, most of of the motherboard vendors sell models designed to accept AMD or Intel with similiar specifications. They also have motherboard models with different chipsets from nVidia, Intel, or AMD/ATI. Which chipset or GPU do you want? 

1.  How much do want to spend? Choose a dollar limit. 
2.  Select alternative components from those highest rated by the greatest number of raters within your budget.
3.  Read the reviews; see what others had to say about the components you are considering.
4.  Research the product support websites to determine if they have driver support for the operating system of your choice.
5.  Make sure your choices are compatible with your previously acquired components.
6.  Order the components you have chosen.

Other thoughts:

Occasionally, even the best, highest-rated models by any one of the manufacturers will have a defect.
Ignore the CPU (or you name the component) evangelists. 
It's all about your choosing what you need or want.

Have fun, and good luck. :)
