### Post by seuaniu on 2006-12-09
The reason that most security tools require a high level of networking knowledge is because they are built by network admins, for network admins.  Snort, in particular, can be a bear to work with - I ended up giving up on it.

A couple of tools I've used:

snort
chkrootkit
nmap or nmapfe (graphical front end)
nessus


Check out [http://sectools.org]("http://sectools.org") for their top 100 tools, most of which will run on linux, and are available in ubuntu.
