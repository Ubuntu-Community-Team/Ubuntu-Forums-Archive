### Post by coffeecat on 2011-03-29
Grub and ext4 were special cases. I'm not aware of anything that will not be upgraded when you upgrade from 10.10 to 11.04. If you were thinking of upgrading now and that is your only Ubuntu installation, I would advise waiting. Natty is still (just) in Alpha. Beta 1 will be out on Thursday. The general recommendation is to use alphas and betas for testing purposes only, not as your main system and only if you have a spare machine or spare partition to test them on.

### Post by Rubi1200 on 2011-03-29
I agree with coffeecat that you should wait unless you like testing and don't mind things crashing, getting deleted, not even starting etc.

In fact, I always recommend people wait at least 2 months after final release so that any last minute bugs, stability issues, and so forth, are taken care of. 

I did this with 9.10, 10.04, and 10.10.

Consequently, I have never had any of the problems seen so frequently on the forums.

Oh, and if you do plan on taking risks make sure you backup all important data first.
