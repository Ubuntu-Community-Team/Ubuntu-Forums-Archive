### Post by nsche on 2008-04-01
Most everything you need should be in /home.  Copy that to a dvd/cd or whatever you use for backup.  Before you do that you ought to figure out what you have installed in addition to the basic install.  Install the same version of Ubuntu on the other computer and restore /home from your backup media.  You will then have to install/remove packages to get an equivilent system.  This all assumes you cannot physically remove the disk and exchange it with the other computer.  You can then do the same when you get your system back.  Back up the target system before you start!

My experience has been that the system will probably be blank or maybe have XP/Vista when it gets back from HP.
