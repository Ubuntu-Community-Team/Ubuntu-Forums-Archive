Hi and welcome to the forums,

"Help me", okay. If you have 10GB of hard disk space available I'd strongly recommend that you switch from Wubi to dual boot.

Win7 and Wubi just do not seem to get on well together. As wubi has been around for a long time and Win7 has not, I would not like to say if it is a problem with Win7, or Wubi. All that matters is that there have been some people saying that they do not get on well together.

From Win7 you can make some free space and pop ubuntu on there, doing so has several advantages.

As to the way Win7 is behaving --> [http://www.sevenforums.com/installation-setup/2557-dual-win7-ubuntu.html](http://www.sevenforums.com/installation-setup/2557-dual-win7-ubuntu.html)

May give you some idea as to why Wubi is having problems.

You may also find this of help --> [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1052602](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1052602)


But, just go for a dual boot :-)
