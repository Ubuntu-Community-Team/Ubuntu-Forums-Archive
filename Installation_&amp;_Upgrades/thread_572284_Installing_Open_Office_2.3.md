### Post by gregladen on 2007-10-14
If I may chime in on this question of upgrading:  Using the upgrade manager to move from one version of Ubuntu to the next, for me, has worked very well on a few occasions, and once was pretty much of a disaster.  In the case of the disaster, I was left with a non-functioning system.  I was able to easily move my data and stuff to a safe location (it was backed up, but moving it this way was easier than a restore) and then carry out a fresh install, then move the data back.  But it was a bit messy.

One technique many people swear by, which I am now doing on my main machine ,is this:  Have two partitions on your hard drive, one for the system, and one for the /home directory.  There are various tutorials on the Ubuntu forums and elswhere for doing this.  

Then, apparently (though I have not tried this yet myself) you can do a full upgrade to the new system without messing around with your data (meaning, your emails, files, etc.) instead of using the upgrade manager.  This should be much cleaner and safer. 

With Gusty coming out very soon, I'm looking forward to trying this.  But I do have a second hard drive in place to which I'll move all my /home directory stuff first!
