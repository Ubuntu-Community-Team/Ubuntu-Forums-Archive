### Post by 5nak3 on 2009-04-24
unfortunately as far as i am aware there is no way to upgrade via the cd. If i am not mistaken the cd is used as a clean install method whereas the upgrade manager is the method to upgrade without a cd.

Now while the cd may lead to a clean install, you could actually save yourself a lot of effort and time with fixing settings, and what not by having your /home on a separate parition to your root.

See here maybe this is of use:

[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome)

[http://www.sysresccd.org/System-tools](http://www.sysresccd.org/System-tools)  - use this is you want to make a backup of your hard drive contents, so if things do go wrong you can restore and be right back where you are right not. Use the partimage function of the if I am not mistaken.

If I were you I would wait for someone with a bit more knowledge on the matter, but as I say as far as I am aware there is no way to upgrade by cd
