### Post by lisati on 2008-08-07
Some people recommend a "dual boot" where you have both Windows and Ubuntu co-habiting your computer while you find your way around Ubuntu. This gives you a choice of whether to run Windows or Ubuntu when you turn your computer on, and gives you a chance to get on to the forums if something happens that you need to ask a question about.

But back to your question: yes, it is possible to have a complete "Ubuntu only" computer. When running from the install disk, you will be asked how you wish to "partition" your system (your computer's disk drive(s), actuall). To have it Ubuntu only, choose "Guided: use entire disk.)

**_IMPORTANT:_** Backup your important data before making major changes to your computer

**_Important point #2:_** Ubuntu isn't the same as Windows: you're always welcome at the forums asking questions if you need help.

### Post by snowpine on 2008-08-07
Hi Armen, welcome to the forums! There are several different ways to install Ubuntu--including from within Windows--but here is what I recommend. I am assuming your computer has enough ram (384mb minimum). 

Download and burn the Ubuntu Live CD ([http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download)). Pop it in your CD drive and turn on the computer. It should start right up, but if not, reboot and look for something like "Press F2 for boot options" and choose CD-ROM. When you get to the Ubuntu screen, choose Try Ubuntu With No Changes To My Computer. Use it for a while and make sure it really works (no video problems or anything). Then, click the Install icon on the desktop, and when you get to the partition options, choose "Guided install--use entire disk". That's it, post back if you have any problems.

### Post by semitone36 on 2008-08-07
Make sure your boot menu has the CD-ROM option at the top of the list.  I recently learned how to dual boot myself.

When your comp first turns on, look around your screen for anything that says something like "press (function key) for BIOS menu"... on my comp it was F2.

Once youre in your BIOS look for a BOOT menu and, within that, something along the lines of BOOT PRIORITY.  This will give your computer a list of options of where to look for an OS. Normaly the harddrive is at the top of the list but to install ubuntu you need to rearrange the list so that the CD-ROM is at the top.

After you do that put the ubuntu disk in your drive and exit BIOS. This will open up ubuntu in live cd mode (basically a "try it out" mode). Once youre into the OS just click the install icon and follow the directions.

Good luck with it!
