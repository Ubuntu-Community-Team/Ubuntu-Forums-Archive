### Post by vexorian on 2012-08-20
"Bloat" is the word people use when they don't really have a real thing to complain about.

I really, really recommend you to stick to 12.04 LTS, and just remove the bloat. Newer software versions are better. You would need too many work arounds to work out getting a new firefox or chromium version, because the stuff that was out in 2010 is embarassing by now.

And I say this as a guy that was very "comfortable" with 10.10, but I did a clean install of 12.04 and I am not regretting it. I can assure you that your life will be easier if you take 12.04 and remove the stuff you don't like as opposed to trying to stay with 10.10 or 10.04 and trying to get things up to date. If you liked old gnome, just install "fallback" gnome. It is almost the same and it even feels faster (probably due to GTK3). On it you can install mutter, metacity or compiz (without unity) and have desktop effects (if you are into that stuff). Latest LibreOffice is also so much better than the stuff in 10.10
