### Post by rcc on 2007-02-08
I have a Logitech TrackMan Marble trackball that I find convenient for my arthritic hands.  I installed Ubuntu 6.10 on a Pentium D 3 GHz system with 2 Gig memory.  This system is dual boot, with Win XP in the other partition.  I use a KVM switch which toggles to a Win XP machine of my wife, and a RedHat 7.3 machine.  The trackball is a PS-2 connector, which fits with my KVM switch.

After working in Ubuntu for a couple of months, it has developed a tendency to crash with movement of the trackball.  Sometimes, movement of the Trackball immediately after first booting Ubuntu 6.10 causes the system to crash and reboot.  Sometimes I can go a  minute or so,  with mouse movement, and then the system crashes.  The system only crashes on mouse (trackball movement) with one exception, which was with a left mouse click. This does not occur ever in Windows on the same dual-boot machine, and never occurs on either of the two other machines on the same KVM switch. 

Since the trackball works fine in the other OS, and in the other systems on the KVM switch, I strongly suspect the Ubuntu driver.  I have thought about a reinstall, but haven't been able to save files and projects that I created.

Suggestions please!   This is not a cordless trackball, it is not a USB trackball, it is hardwired with the older PS2 connector.
