### Post by clegends on 2011-10-17
Am having a lot of issues myself, and am also considering going back to Natty, and reinstalling Oneiric on a separate partition to help troubleshoot bugs... feels a bit undercooked to me.

Anyway, if you're not able to sort out your issues (though I recommend hanging around and trying first), you need to wipe and reinstall. Are you familiar with the Linux filesystem? All of your data is under /home/user. You can back up this data to an external hard drive, then restore it to a *folder* on your new install. You can then manually restore things from there. The best way I found to install Linux is to have a separate /home partition, so that if you ever need to wipe your system (omg! hearsay!) you can do it and (more or less) restore everything back the way it was. I.e., your settings are mostly saved. By default, Ubuntu tends to make you install on one partition, no idea why.

Anyway, good luck. If you can't figure out your problems, and need to reinstall post again if you get stuck. Cheers.
