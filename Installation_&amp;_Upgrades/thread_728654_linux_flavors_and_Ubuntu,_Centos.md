### Post by scottro on 2008-03-19
What he said.  :)
For a desktop, CentOS can be lacking in some things.  While they can be found and installed, Ubuntu makes it a bit easier.

For servers, CentOS is basically RedHat Enterprise Linux with  logos removed, and a few other things.  It's used in many places where for cost, or other reasons, people decide not to go with RedHat.  (RH doesn't, as far as I know, have an official stance, unofficially, they approve.)

Of course, there are over 200 others out there, but those two are good representatives of their respective classes.  

This doesn't mean Ubuntu is no good as a server or Centos is no good as a desktop--it just means that each is designed with the respective typical user in mind.
