### Post by CD Baric on 2007-10-19
It is my professional experience that CD/DVD burners are often subject to failure after a very short lifetime - sometimes under a year.

You may try booting from a different CD like a Windows bootable disc and see if that works.

Just for fun, check your BIOS and see what is reported.

If the drive is truly trashed, just spend the $30 and replace it. By the way, the reason you are only paying $30 for a brand new drive is because they are crap!
