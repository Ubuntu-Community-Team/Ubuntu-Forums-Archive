### Post by jdong on 2008-04-23
As most of you probably know, Hardy is in its finalizing stages imminent for release.

As some of you may further know, for several architectures there release-like ISOs already in hidden directories of the main mirror. On behalf of the forum staff and archive managers, I'd like to ask for people to **refrain from posting such URLs, advising the use of such URLs, or using such URLs themselves**, for several reasons:


(1) It's inconsiderate to everyone who wants to get a copy of Ubuntu on release day: Overloading the central archive mirror causes delays in pushing out these images to release mirrors all around the world.

(2) It's a big annoyance if an error was found in the release: It's not out of the question that at the last minute a serious bug can be found, forcing a re-roll. Since these CDs are marked as finals in every respect, it would be very confusing to determine "good" or "bad" ones.

Again, we ask for users to wait for a formal release announcement before downloading the final release.
