Whilst I understand your frustrations, removing Wubi is not the answer. There is, plainly, an issue with updates on it - these will be being looked into. 
Just as some people decry moving over to grub2.
Progress has to be made & that does involve releasing software that has not been tested on every computer configuration in the world.

Whilst a lot of people just want it to work, for many - the change over to Ubuntu (or any linux) is to actually learn something about how their computer works.

I'm currently playing 'try and break my computer' with the alpha of 10.04 It is only by testing the releases can problems be flagged. Rest assured - people are working on the problem, in the mean-time, you'll know the rescue script off by heart.

A sense of humour is always a useful trait at times like this ;)
