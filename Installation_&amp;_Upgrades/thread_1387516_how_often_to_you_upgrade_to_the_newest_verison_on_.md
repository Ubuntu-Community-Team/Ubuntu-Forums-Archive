### Post by SPazzo on 2010-01-22
It varies.  Some people will upgrade immediately, some will wait, some will BETA test.  If it's hard to upgrade every six months, try jumping from Long-term-support to  Long-term-support releases.  You don't have to back up, reinstall, and start over as often.  Long-term-support releases are released every two years.

### Post by kansasnoob on 2010-01-22
I never know until I try.

Gutsy (my first Ubuntu) was absolutely great for me.

Hardy had some serious pulse audio problems for about the first four months but has otherwise been great.

Intrepid was OK but Hardy was very stable by then so I just passed.

Jaunty was my best Ubuntu experience yet, just stable as a rock. No issues whatsoever.

Karmic's Xorg and my hardware just don't seem to play well together, so I've somewhat passed on it (although it's still installed for testing).

So far Lucid looks like it's going to be great, for an Alpha 2 it's been amazingly stable, but all development releases are prone to out and out breakage so shouldn't be used on production machines.
