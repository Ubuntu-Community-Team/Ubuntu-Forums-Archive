### Post by fritz_monroe on 2006-06-15
I know from other forums that I participate in that often the only posts about products are about problems.  I wanted to post about my upgrade from 5.10 to 6.06.

I have a Sony Vaio VGN-FS742/W laptop.  I've been running Ubuntu on it for about 6 months and have liked it.  I decided earlier this week to upgrade to 6.06.  I had to wait a couple days because I still had a couple finals to complete.  I didn't want to risk messing up my system and not being able to get online.  That worry was unfounded.  My upgrade went seemlessly.  I used the Upgrade Manager and a little over an hour later, the upgrade was complete.

Now for my initial thoughts about Dapper Drake.  I tried out hibernate and it seems to work just fine.  I put it to sleep last night and after work today, it woke up no problem.  I did notice that my touch pad now has a scroll bar at the side and that will take a little while to get used to.  I also understand that the wifi support is a bit better.  I'll try using WPA now and see how things go.  One thing that I wish was straightened out is the support for the Sony function keys.  It sure would be nice to be able to adjust the brightness with a simple key stroke.

Over all, it's awesome.
