### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-03-14
I would make sure you have the latest motherboard firmware.  I have an old Dell Optiplex, PIII, 500 MHz with an added Promise 100 MHz controller.  It works fine under Dapper.  The card is detected by the BIOS and the drives are enumerated and listed on the screen during the BIOS boot.  If the BIOS doesn't enumerate the drives, Linux will have a hard time finding them.

You might turn off Plug-n-Play operating system off in the BIOS, that may trip up hardware detection.  Check lspci for interrupt conflicts.  Try turning off serial, USB, and parallel ports (temporarily) to see if the drives show up.  All it takes is one conflict to ball up a system.

Good luck.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-03-26
To oldcotton1:  Since this is an older, 600 MHz PIII, it's time to rethink your configuration.  Put both optical drives on the same IDE ribbon.  Put your boot drive on the second IDE.  Get a working Ubuntu system.

Then add the Promise controller and a data/media drive.  See if the system detects it.  You may have to upgrade the BIOS of the Promise controller.  I had to do this using a windows machine (making a bootfloppy with bios flasher).

All I can say is that my Promise ATA100 controller works under Dapper,  Your 133 controller should be similar with the clock  speed increased.  I can boot off of this configuration, but then my BIOS enumerates the drives right after POST.


To rbarrimond:

I'm not aware of a way to fool Linux.  It pretty much relies on the BIOS to find the drives.   I would also try to find newer firmware for your tri card.  That's an interesting combination.  I have gotten firewire/usb pci cards to work under Ubuntu that I have pulled from macs, but not a trio card.  Hey, 2 out of 3 ain't bad.

You may have to insert a Sonnet boot loader.  This basically announces to the BIOS that you have additional drives.  Search the web for Promise enumerators.  

The other thing that I noticed in your dmesg is that DMA was turned off.  Perhaps the card will only work with DMA.  Perhaps there is a DMA conflict.  Try unplugging your optical drives.  Make sure you have no conflicts on IRQ6.  Check /proc/interrupts.  The kernel is finding the card and knows that it provides 4 ide channels, so that is a start.  I didn't see any "hardware unknown" messages.

Let us know what works.

Good luck to both of you.
