### Post by baltimark on 2007-04-16
This weekend I was trying to install ubuntu on an older laptop. . .a Dell Latitude with 128 MB of RAM. 4 GB HD. 

First of all. . .I tried to install 6.10 and it got to the yellow/orange screen and then nothing appeared. I'm referring to the screen which, during a normal install, should have the little icon that says "install". The 6.10 image was from a download which I already successfully installed on a different PC. 

I didn't know if the monitor settings were wrong, and perhaps it was off the display. As a shot in the dark, I tried a re-install with 'live vga=771' (saw it as a troubleshoot) but that didn't help.

So, I tried to install a 5.10 disk that I had, and the installation from CD seemed to go all right, but after installation, when it tried to reboot from the hard drive, it got to the line that said "installing hotplug subsystem" and just hung (I let it sit for about an hour). . .it seemed to stop reading the hard drive at all. 

Any ideas about either of these? 

The laptop had been successfully running XP. It's always been pretty stable.

### Post by baltimark on 2007-04-18
In case anyone is interested, I SORT OF solved this problem. 

Most of you who are more experienced probably know this, but I was fishing around and noticed that **X**ubuntu said it is, "ideal for old or low-end machines". (yes, I'm quite the noobuntu)

I went to the Xubuntu download page, and further noticed that the primary .iso file indicated you might need 198 MB RAM for an install. 

So, I used the alternate Xubuntu file, and now my old, crappy laptop is running slick and sweet with Xubuntu on it. And, I think that's a pretty nice package. . .that's going to be a nice little box for just surfing the web and maybe doing a little python work or something. 

Case closed.
