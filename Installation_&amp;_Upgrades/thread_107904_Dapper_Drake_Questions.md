### Post by az on 2005-12-24
... But seriously, You will have to do a lot of downloading, since updates happen every day.  You will find that something will stop working and you may or may not want to track down the problem.

You will then keep track of wheter this new problem is known and is a symptom of some packages transitioning to another version/framework/solution or whether it is somthing new that no one else knows about and you can help fix.

So, it will be just more work to do to keep your OS running.  But you will be helping out the development of Ubuntu by doing that.


You can take half of a plunge by just adding breezy sources to your list and instaling certain packages that you want.
