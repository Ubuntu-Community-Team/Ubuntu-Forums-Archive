### Post by ericartman on 2008-01-04
Ditto on Nero, they used to have a trial version you could get for free, maybe they still do. Of course when you get into Linux the burning options get immense, I use K3b for my ISO burns but there are many options. I would back up everything that is important to you before putting Ubuntu on your drive. I have never had dual booting screw up my windows install but I may just be lucky. BTW I recommend for you to get a copy of Super Grub [http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/SuperGrubDiskPage.html](http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/SuperGrubDiskPage.html)
 it has helped immensely when grub has become a problem. Good Luck!  BTW burn the cd at the slowest speed possible to limit problems and the type of cd only depends on if your system likes it or not.
