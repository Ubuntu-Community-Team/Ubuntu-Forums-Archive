Yes, in wine. But be warned, not everything works. In fact, it's quite limited list. Their website is:
[http://www.winehq.org/](http://www.winehq.org/)

And you can click on AppDB to check if the applications you need are supported.

If you depend on specific windows app, or lots of them, dual booting ubuntu with windows is much better solution. In fact I am dual booting myself just because few apps I need in windows have no way of working in linux, and there are no equivalent apps.
