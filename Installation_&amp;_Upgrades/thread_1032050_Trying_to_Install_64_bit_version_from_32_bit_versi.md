### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-01-06
You mention DVD-R but the desktop and alternate Ubuntu ISO images are for CDs, not for DVDs.  If you burned the ISO to a DVD, it probably won't work -- which could be why it won't boot.  You need to burn the ISO to a CD-R disk.

Also, you said you can't boot off a CD-R either.  This gives the impression that your optical drive is bad, or you don't have the boot from optical drive option set in your BIOS.

Finally, if you're trying to "upgrade" an existing 32-bit install to 64-bit, you can't do that.  You have to do a fresh install.
