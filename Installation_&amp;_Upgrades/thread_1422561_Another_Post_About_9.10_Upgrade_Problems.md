### Post by GenePayne on 2010-03-05
This is not intended to be a rant, but rather to be another data point for anyone considering upgrading to Ubuntu 9.10 (Karmic Koala).

I've been running 9.10 for about 3 months now. My upgrade from 9.04 to 9.10 was buggy, but a format & fresh install of 9.10 didn't fix the problems.
Fortunately, I upgraded only at home. At work I'm still running 9.04 (Jaunty). I couldn't have afforded the delays and productivity hit of 9.10 issues at work. I am very pleased with 9.04; it has been rock-solid. 
There are some things I like better about 9.10, but compared to 9.04, far more problems than benefits. I'm not looking forward to spending the time to switch back to 9.04 at home.

_A list of (most of) the problems I've had:_
[LIST=1]
[*]Firefox has noticeable delays in loading pages, flash video has random pauses. I get similar delays in other apps too, but mostly Firefox
[*]Virtualbox "freezes" my windows XP guest when doing anything processor-intensive. Screen goes gray, and keyboard/mouse inputs are ignored.
[*]Compiz effects are slower. Where are the compiz controls?
[*]Can't get sound to work properly. I fixed sound problems in 9.04 but have been unsuccessful in 9.10.
[*]gscan2pdf breaks when trying to save a scanned document.
[*]some programs like eclipse can't access internet sites when they try to connect directly (e.g. trying to download packages for eclipse).
[/LIST]

I've spent some time trying to find fixes for these, but haven't had much luck. If anyone has fixes for these, I'd welcome suggestions.
