### Post by tommcd on 2010-04-29
I always do clean installs of Ubuntu; and I never have problems. I use the alternate CD for all my installs.
I can do a clean install in 20 minutes or less. It then takes perhaps another hour, at the most, to get the updates, reinstall the software I use, and set things up the way I want them. I do not consider this to be a problem at all. Many people spend more time than this trying to do a dist-upgrade, since the Ubuntu servers are known to run a bit slow when a new version of Ubuntu is released. 

Note that it is recommended to remove third party repos and packages before doing a dist-upgrade:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/CleanUpgrade](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/CleanUpgrade)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes)
Almost no one does this though. This is likely a a big reason why so many people have problems with dist-upgrades imo.
