### Post by wpshooter on 2007-03-20
My suggestion would be to make sure you have anything that you can not afford to loose BACKED UP and/or have an IMAGE of your pre-installation of Ubuntu.

My second suggestion would be that if you have another computer that has nothing on it that you need to perserve (either O/S or programming or data) that you take that computer and WIPE it clean and then PRACTICE installing Ubuntu on it a few times before you try on your production computer.

Having said the above, I do **NOT RECOMMEND **having more than one O/S on a computer at any given time !!!

### Post by tommcd on 2007-03-20
See this site:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index)
Work your way down the menu list on the left side of the page. It explains everything very well. The easy way to dual boot is to select "resize IDE master" (or whatever it says) at the partitioning section of the install. This will allow you partition your hard drive with a pertition for windows and a partition for ubuntu, plus a swap partition. Just don't select "Erase entire disk" this will wipe the disk clean and remove windows.
Hope this helps. You can safely dual (or triple, or quad boot) many OSs. Just be careful with partitioning.
