### Post by howefield on 2015-12-04
No Ubuntu version is released as unstable although the LTS releases tend to be built a bit more conservatively so may be more "stable" in that sense.

The main difference is the support each release gets, Long Term Support releases (currently 14.04, the next being 16.04) come out every 2 years and get 5 years of security updates from date of release - to be more accurate, 14.04.1 gets 5 years of updates. Installing from the current point release of 14.04 may be different depending on the kernel used, yes it's all very confusing :)

Interim releases get 9 months of life support, so your 15.10 goes end of life next July. A big consideration for your "newbie" friend. If by "Ubuntu 14" you mean 14.04 or 14.0.1 you are fine, if however you mean 14.10 you are already past end of life.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/info/release-end-of-life](http://www.ubuntu.com/info/release-end-of-life)
