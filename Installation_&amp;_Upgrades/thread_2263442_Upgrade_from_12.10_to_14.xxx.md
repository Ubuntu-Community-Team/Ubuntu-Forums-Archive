### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-02-01
To upgrade from 12.10 to 14.04 we would first need to upgrade to 13.04 and then to 13.10 and then to 14.04. Now, here is the problem. Actually it is 3 problems. Ubuntu 12.10 has reached end of life and its repositories are now closed. This is why you cannot install applications, update or upgrade.

There is a way around this problem but once you get to Ubuntu 13.04 you will find the 13.04 has also reached end of life and its repositories are now closed and the same applies to Ubuntu 13.10. Now, as  I said, it is possible to work around all this but the risk of breaking the OS becomes greater with each upgrade we do when upgrading in a sequence like this. It is also a lot of data to download and takes a lot of time. And that is why we are recommending a new install of Ubuntu 14.04.

But it is your choice.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EOLUpgrades](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EOLUpgrades)
