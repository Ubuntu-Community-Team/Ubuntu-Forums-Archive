### Post by lemming465 on 2008-11-02
You are yet another victim of some X video driver problems, at first guess.  What is your video chip?

You can probably get at text console with [Ctrl-Alt-F2], log in, and check /var/log/Xorg.0.log for error messages.

Booting in failsafe mode with the vesa video driver might work.
If you have an ATI chip, converting to the xserver-xorg-driver-flgrx driver might help.
If you have an Nvidia chip, converting to the xserver-xorg-driver-nv driver might help.

Specifying *driver "vesa"* in /etc/X11/xorg.conf is the equivalent of the failsafe boot.
