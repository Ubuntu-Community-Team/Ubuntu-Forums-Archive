### Post by corncob on 2011-02-17
In the system menu go to Administration.  Then open "Startup Disk Creator, select your source, either an ISO or CD, and your target -- your Kingston pen drive in this case.
If you want to be installing Ubuntu from your pen drive rather than from a CD, go to the Ubuntu Download page -- [http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download) and in step 2 check USB stick instead of CD and click on "Show me how".

### Post by oldfred on 2011-02-17
Welcome to the forums.

corncob suggestion is for putting the liveCD onto a USB. With 4GB that is porbably the best solution. You can also turn on persistence to let you save some updates, but it is not a full install.

If you have a flash drive 8GB or larger then you can do a full install which is just like an install to any second drive with maybe a few differnt settings for flash to reduce writes.
