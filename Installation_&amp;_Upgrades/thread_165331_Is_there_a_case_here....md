### Post by jazzmuzik on 2006-04-24
If you install Ubuntu chances are it'll work 99.999% correctly. Most times when people get into trouble it's because they are tweaking their systems or attempting to tweak them and not really knowing what they are doing. These people push forward, ask questions here and other places and eventually get past the problems. Ubuntu is a great Linux distro.

My suggestion is to try the Breezy verion. It is stable. Dapper is still beta and problems are being worked out for its upcoming release.

If you want a conservative approach, download one of the "Live" Ubuntu iso files and burn it to a CD with your favorite burner (in iso mode of course, not data mode). Then reboot your computer via the CD and you can experience Ubuntu without even installing anything, no changes are made to the HD. Note that Ubuntu runs slowly this way because everything is being retrieved from the CD, but if you like what you see, download the "Install" version and try an install. Then it'll run at fast speed.
