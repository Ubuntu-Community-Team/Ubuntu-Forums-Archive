### Post by aysiu on 2009-08-09
There's nothing to study about UNetBootIn. It's pretty straightforward.

Here are the steps, though:

1. Download the disk image (.iso) of Linux Mint from the Linux Mint website. I would highly recommend using BitTorrent, as it is more likely to leave you with an uncorrupted download.

2. Download the UNetBootIn for Windows .exe file.

3. Plug in your USB drive and make note of its location (D:, E:, F:, etc.). It should have at least 800 MB of free space.

4. Once #1 and #2 are completed, double-click the UNetBootIn .exe file, which will launch UNetBootIn.

5. Select the option to choose the .iso (ignore the top bit about selecting a distribution to download--you've already downloaded it). Browse and find the Linux Mint .iso you downloaded in #1.

6. Select the option to choose what drive to "burn" the .iso to (this would be the D:, E:, F:, etc. that identifies the USB stick you have plugged in. Click OK.

More details here:
[http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/#install](http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/#install)
