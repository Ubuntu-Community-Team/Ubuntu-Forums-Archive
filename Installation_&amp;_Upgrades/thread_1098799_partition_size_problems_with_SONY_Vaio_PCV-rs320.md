### Post by zvacet on 2009-03-17
You can use [Gparted live CD.]("http://gparted.sourceforge.net/")First defragment few times partition you want to shrink (D in your case),and then shrink it.On unallocated space expand your C.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-03-18
If you have Partition Magic, recommend using that. But, do it in two stages.  First, resize and move your D partition to the right.  Then, resize your C partition.  All should be OK after that.
