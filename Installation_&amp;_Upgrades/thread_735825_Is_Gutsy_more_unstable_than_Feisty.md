### Post by dstew on 2008-03-26
Unless you have a clear reason to upgrade from one distribution to another, I wouldn't do it. There really is not much of a difference between Feisty and Gutsy overall. The new distributions are not really meant to be "better" than earlier ones in terms of stability. Mainly they add features, usually new GUI front-ends for configuration programs, and some new applications. They also add new drivers for hardware that was not supported before. But, because the new distributions often use new kernels and drivers, you can get some hardware issues that you did not have before.

As you probably know, once you get your Ubuntu system configured, it will be very stable. The updates (not up**grades**) that you get with the update manager help with security issues and bugs. These updates make your system better. But up**grading** is installing a whole new system, and you often have to do all the configuration stuff again because of the new drivers.

To sum up, the only reason I would upgrade is to get some new application I wanted, or to get some hardware working that was not previously supported. If you notice, the names of the distributions reflect to some degree the boldness of the developers, and what they had in mind. Hence, Dapper Drake is stable, Edgy Eft a little "edgy", and Gusty is a bold new distro. Hardy Heron, however, is being designed with stability in mind. You might wait for that.
