### Post by spudley on 2006-12-02
I recently installed Ubuntu again - its on a P4 2.8GHz, 512Mb RAM, 64Mb video card (on board), 40gb hard drive.... nothing too unusual or complex.

Running the basic install gave me a 1024x768 resolution - something I could live with, but wasn't overy thrilled at being maxed out at.

Anyway, after playing with Ubuntu again (I've tried to make it a full part of my office for a while now) I realized it just isn't running very fast... things are taking a long while to 'redraw' on the screen.

I found the Ubuntu install gave me basic VESA video drivers, I made the change to SiS but still had speed issues (screen redraw most notibly).  

I tried switching from gdesklets to adesklets to nothing, closing Firefox very regularly and still no help.... almost ready to give up, I decided I just wanted to atleast see Ubuntu at high resolution -- I played around with the configuration and figured out how to bump resolution to 1280x1024.

A quick reboot got me my screen change - but you know what else - a faster Ubuntu!

How? why?  I have no idea... but it worked - I have adesklets, gdesklets running at the same time, Firefox with lots of tabs open over the course of a couple days... no speed issues!  I even took the time to get my network printer working (Brother 420CN) so it looks like I'll be integrating Ubuntu into my office after all.

As per the subject, no reply is needed - but if increasing screen resolution speeds things up in Ubuntu, I can handle that ;)
- maybe it'll work for others with a similar problem?
