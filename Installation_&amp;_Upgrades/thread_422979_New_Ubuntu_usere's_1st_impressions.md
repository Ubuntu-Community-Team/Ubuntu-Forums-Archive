### Post by jeepfreak2002 on 2007-04-25
To start, I am a bit of a noob with linux.  I installed Fedora 5 on a Shuttle nForce 2 pc a year ago for a terminal at work.  The machine works flawlessly, Internet is peppy, email is fine, and every day I seem to find a new little feature that I like.  I got frustrated with Fedora over a couple of issues on a new machine, and began to look for other distros.

I installed Ubuntu 6 (i386) on a new Shuttle nForce4 AMD64.  The install went absolutely flawlessly.  I only began to get frustrated when I attempted to install nVidia drivers.  It just seemed like there was no concensus on the method.  I then saw that Ubuntu 7 supported it as a restricted driver.  Thank God!!!!  It's about time a distro gave some simplified way to install the most important driver on a machine.

I updated to Feisty Fawn, and blammo - there was the "restricted driver" that installed perfectly.  So I had 4 other identical machines that I figured - might as well download 7, burn a DVD, and avoid having to update 6 to 7.  

Well... Let's say that I am glad that just by chance my first experience was installing 6, and upgrading to 7!  7's live CD defaulted to either 640x480, or 800x600.  The install menus were so large that I could not even click on the buttons on the bottom of the windows because they were off of the screen.  Luckily, thanks to this forum, someone had the same problem, and I learned of the "alt-left click" trick.  No prob, right?  Install finishes, I remove the DVD, reboot, and CRASH - the x-server (or whatever the hell controls the window graphics) just bombed out.  Back to 6, then upgraded to 7, and no problem.

So what is the story with 7?  Other people are complaining about the video issue on the install as well.  Over all, I like Ubuntu.  I will have 3 PC's at work, and my main PC at home all running Ubuntu.  I will continue to tinker with things.  (and probably break them in the process!) I'm looking forward to seeing what this community has to offer! 

Jeepfreak

### Post by g8m on 2007-04-25
The install cd give an option to boot in safe graphics mode. The 2nd if I remember correctly. The default tries to install the drivers for your videocart. These work mostly, but give effects like small screens. Booting in safe-graphics mode uses the vesa-drivers. It is more reliant and will probably give a larger default resolution. For a normal install you dont need the fancy driver stuff, that's just for showing off, if they work.
