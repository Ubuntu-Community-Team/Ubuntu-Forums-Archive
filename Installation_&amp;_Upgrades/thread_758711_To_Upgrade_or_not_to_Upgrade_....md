### Post by Kwag on 2008-04-18
Here's the situation ... I am running Ubuntu Edgy Eft.  People laugh (I am so out of date) but I have everything I need working.  I have all the applications I want installed, I have tweaked the system for performance, added my personal touches and settings, etc.  The result is a lightning fast, stable operating system.  Here is primarily how I use my computer:

Internet:  Firefox, mPlayer, Google Mail
Music:  Amarok
Photos:  Google Picasa for Linux
Office:  Abiword Processor, GNumeric, Tomboy Notes, gLabels, Scribus (I don't like Open Office)
Windows:  I virtualize this OS because I choose Quicken and Turbo Tax.

I am hesitant to upgrade because ...

1.  I don't think I can upgrade to Feisy, upgrade to Gutsy, then upgrade to Hardy.  What is the chance that will work?  I have done lots of tweaking to my current system.  (Automatix, Easy Ubuntu, etc.)  Plus, that would take forever.

2.  I could do a new install.  I have / and /home on separate partitions ... but since Hardy is so much more current than Edgy ... will my configurations in /home really continue to work if I leave it untouched and just install Hardy to / ?

3.  I could back stuff up and wipe out / and /home, install Hardy from scratch and copy over what I need.  

Well, the question is.  Is moving to Hardy worth it really?  What does Hardy offer that really benefits me?  Human nature dictates that operating systems tend to get bloated over time.  Why do I want to slow down my system, and spend hours setting everything back up again?

Reasons I can think of for upgrading are the following:

1.  The support life for Edgy is ending.  I will no longer get updates.  Firefox will become out of date and I will have unplugged security holes. 

2.  Improved security (of the new operating system itself).

3.  More recent software (but maybe I can live without)

4.  Maybe if I do the work to get Hardy installed, and don't mess around too much.  From then on, i can just go through the normal upgrade procedure ... and then I can easily always stay current!  But  I've heard from many ... don't upgrade Ubuntu, you will run into problems..do a fresh install each time.

5.  Hardy will give me 3 years of support.

So, I am looking for a small handful of opinions.  People who can tell me things that I don't know, that might convince me to either upgrade or stay put.     I am quite torn right now.

Thanks.
