### Post by dabl on 2007-08-02
I went with Intel motherboard -- the D975XBX with integrated HDA audio, on the theory that the higher price was justified by avoiding a separate audio card.

With the LGA 775 socket, you can choose any of the high-performance CPUs.  Don't use the Intel (included) CPU heatsink -- it's a piece of crap.  Buy something like my Arctic Cooler Pro, and Arctic Silver thermal paste.

I bought 4 GB of RAM.  I think I wasted my money on the last 2 GB -- I've never seen it used, even though I've pushed my system with audio encoding and stuff like that.

Buy an Nvidia graphics card -- I got an EVGA GF 7900 GS, but if I were doing it today I'd get an 8000 series.

Spend the money on a couple of WD Raptor WD1500 10,000 rpm SATA hard drives, if you want FAST.

I got the last Samsung Syncmaster 1100MB, but they still have 1100s that are 21" displays -- beautiful! (and HEAVY!)

I put my stuff in a big Antec case and used an Antec TruPower Trio 650 like this: [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817371001](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817371001)

Make sure your case has the big 120mm cooling fans included.

Microsoft Comfort Curve  keyboard and mouse (that's what they're good at!).  It runs Kubuntu, Ubuntu 64-bit, and e-live Linux, no problems.
