### Post by houseworkshy on 2010-01-25
Have you tried going into the bios on bootup?  On my machine I do it by tapping the del key as it boots, this varies but most machines tell you.  Once in check to see that the machine looks to it's cd/dvd drive for a boot record before it looks at the hard drive.  the convention is that the disc/usb at the top of the list is checked first and the one at the end last.  Be cautious in bios, remember what you see( such as what the drives are called ) then change the boot priority, if it needs it, but leave every thing else alone.  If it is that then you will be able to boot from the cd/dvd again.
