### Post by aysiu on 2007-11-18
A full installation process is a lot easier than a dual boot.

With a dual boot, you have to worry (as you are doing now) about partition sizes.

With a "full installation," you don't have to worry about anything. Just install and erase the whole hard drive.

By the way you don't have to "see out the G-parted." GParted is included as part of the installation process as the partitioning tool.

I still think you're better off with a VirtualBox Ubuntu, especially since you're still reliant on Windows-only programs. Do you really want to reboot your computer every time you want to switch back and forth between Windows and  Ubuntu?
