### Post by Scunizi on 2006-09-17
The 386 kernal is the default because it "just works" on the vast majority of machines.  On the DEV email list there seems to be an ongoing thread about the benefits of 686 over 386 kernals and the speed factor.  The general answer is "it depends" on a lot of factors and which program you're running.  I switched to the 686 kernal to get smooth DVD playback in mplayer.  It was a little jerky with the 386.  I also have hyperthreading which in enabled in 686.  For those that know the differance it's easy enough to install.
