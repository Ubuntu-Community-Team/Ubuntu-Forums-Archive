You hould make sure the MD5 checksum is correct. Now, to the other issue. It is true that sometimes your hardware will not work out of the box, a bit of tinkering would be needed.

I think you can take this from people who use Linux every single day, you certainly don't need to be a programmer to make your system work, but you musy be open to learn. Linux works and is configured in  different way than Windows, and this is not a bad thing, it's just different and sometimes beginners will find it a bit more difficult. Other times it can be the other way around.

Don't be afraid of the command line, it is an extremely powerful and efficient tool. It takes a learning curve, of course.

In any case, I would recommend you to give Ubuntu a try on the live CD, on a virtual machine, or better on a separate disk or partition.

Then you would be able to choose based on your own experience and opinion and not on what other people say. You should use wichever operating system you prefer and feel more confortable with.
