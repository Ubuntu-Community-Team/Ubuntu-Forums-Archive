Hiya folks...

Like a few of you, I too am looking forward to the next release of Ubuntu (version 8.04) in just a few short days.

Being a novice to Ubuntu, I'm an **Ubuntu info. junkie**, in that I seek as much info. (tips and tricks mostly) on this OS as I can (in small, easy to understand doses, of course). Anyhoo....asides from this  awesome resource (Ubuntu Forums), I also utilize Ubuntu.com & I have a copy of "The Official Ubuntu book : Second Edition". The book is more geared towards 7.04, while I run 7.10 Gutsy Gibbon but it really helped me understand some concepts.

With the new release of 8.04, I decided to visit Ubuntu.com and clicked on the link at the top that says "The new Ubuntu. 3 days to go". I figured I'd take a few minutes and read over what was being offered, and whether or not the upgrade was for me. On the right hand side you'll find a heading that reads "Contents" and it lists some information about the next version & what it will include, some known bugs, etc.

I was a little disappointed however at the lack of version numbers. For example, if you go to Chapter 4, Section 2, Subsection 2 (entitled : Brasero), it tells you how Brasero is now included in the OS upgrade. What it fails to mention is the version number.

Something else that was lacking in the documentation was an FAQ section. I didn't see one on that list. Did I miss it? Has anybody seen an FAQ about 8.04? I'm wondering if I missed it, or if they haven't posted one up simply because it's not released yet and are waiting for the flood of questions.  :-\"

Would've been nice to know if performing the upgrade required me to format my drive and/or whether or not I'd be losing previous data. What about my existing software? Would that have to be reinstalled?

I'm a little anxious because I'm in the middle of tinkering around with some software (I.E: Win4Lite). Being somewhat of a newbie, I don't want to waste too much time on an installation that will be undone because I'm definitely going to be upgrading.

I guess I'll have to wait and see.
