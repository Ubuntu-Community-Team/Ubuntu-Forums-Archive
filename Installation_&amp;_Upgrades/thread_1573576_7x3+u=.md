From the way your drive is partitioned (at least what you posted originally) the only way you could get what you want is to shrink the 160GB partition and then create an extended partition in there with logical partitions for Ubuntu (I am pretty certain that is what howefield was actually thinking of).

You need to use the Disk Management tools in Windows to shrink the partition and I also recommend rebooting at least twice and running a chkdsk before installing Ubuntu.

If you need more help or have any additional questions, feel free to ask.

One final note: backup all important data before you start!
