Asking people to spend £20-£30 just to try out Ubunutu is not going to win over many people.

My Ubuntu 6 live CD sat on my shelf unused for a year after I tried it for five minutes because I couldn't connect to the internet.

My Ubuntu 7.04 CD again went on my shelf for several months.

The Distro that finally persuaded me to install Linux was PCLinuxOS, which has no problem connecting via a USB ADSL modem. I switched to Ubuntu after finding Steven Harper's utility.

I'm now using Windows because my Ubuntu partition is useless without an internet connection.

I suspect that a lot of Ubuntu CD's will end up on the shelf as people try them and find that they have no internet connection, people who previously could connect with Steven's utility, if they happened to find it.

Money is tight for some people and spending it unnecessarily is difficult to justify. Not everybody has the spare cash to pop down to PCWorld for a modem just to get Ubuntu working.
