### Post by upalm on 2005-05-06
try modifying this section below in xorg.conf by changing DefaultDepth to 16 or maybe less depending on your screen size. i had this exact problem a couple of days ago and this is what i did and it worked after a reboot.

Section "Screen"
Identifier "Default Screen"
Device "Intel Corporation Intel Default Card"
Monitor "Generic Monitor"
DefaultDepth 24

if this doesn't work then try the Fn+F? key, where ? is whichever F key controls monitor output & display for presentations etc. on your laptop.

hope it works out!
