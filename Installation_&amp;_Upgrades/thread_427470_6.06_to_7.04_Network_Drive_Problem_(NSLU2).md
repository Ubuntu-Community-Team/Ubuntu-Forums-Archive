I ran into this problem after the 6.06 to 7.04 upgrade so I have posted it here...not totally sure if this is the right spot.

Running 6.06 I was able to log on to my Linksys NSLU2 drive by using the following line in my fstab:

//192.168.1.10/disk0401  /mnt/nslu2-1  cifs user,rw,username=luckystrike,password=lsmft 0    0

The IP of the Linksys is 192.168.1.10 and the disk share is disk 1  with a space between the k and the 1.  The 040 (ascii space) took care of the blank between the k and the 1.

Under 6.06 everything was fine...however...I upgraded to feisty (7.04) and what I get is:
mount error 6 = No such device or address

sudo mount //192.168.1.10/disk\ 1 /mnt/nslu2-1 -o username= etc......  works fine.  I had to use \space here 'cause 040 didn't work

Summary:
The original line in the fstab worked...after the upgrade to 7.04 it does not.  I've tried what little I could think of and I have not been able to arrive at a solution...I've searched and googled and have not been able to find anything that fixes this...any suggestions would be appreciated.
