### Post by MikeBuffer on 2007-01-13
When you begin a Ubuntu installation, it's useful to know whether your processor is 32-bit or 64-bit, because the 32-bit version of Ubuntu will run on both types but the 64-bit version will run only on 64-bit processors.

Intel's recent processors are confusingly named: the "Core Duo" processors are 32-bit whereas the "Core 2 Duo" ones are 64-bit. Incidentally, the "Duo" bit means that there are two CPU cores on the chip. Core Solo processors have a single CPU. 

Another confusing point is that the Ubuntu 64-bit distros are referred to as "AMD64" even though they're equally usable on Intel 64-bit processors, since Intel have implemented extensions originally developed by AMD.

If you're unsure about your processor's architecture, find out its number and Google it on the intel website: "T3350 site:intel.com". [Here is a PDF document]("http://www.intel.com/products/processor_number/proc_info_table.pdf") from the Intel website that summarises the features of a wide range of their processors. The second-last column indicates whether the processor is 64-bit or not.
