### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-03-11
Your SATA drive can't share a port with your IDE drives, so leave your IDE setup as is and plug your new SATA drive into the SATA header on your motherboard (or expansion card).  I am told SATA only allows one connection per cable, so you don't have to worry about master/slave settings for it.

As for which edition to use, what are your computer specs?  A computer that came with XP should work great under regular Ubuntu.  Xubuntu provides the Xfce desktop environment which is lighter weight and Kubuntu provides KDE, a heaver desktop environment which some people prefer.  GNOME is the default that Ubuntu comes with.
