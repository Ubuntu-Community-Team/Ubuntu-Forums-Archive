### Post by meng on 2007-02-17
> **AndrewTheArt said:**
> So you recommend DSL over Puppy Linux? Why so?
It's not a super strong recommendation of one over the other, it's just that DSL strikes me as more "standard" /flexible in terms of package management and is also designed to allow more than one user (whereas Puppy Linux runs essentially as a single-user OS). Bottom line is, depending on your needs and preferences, Puppy may be better for you, perhaps you'll like it better because among other things there's a version of Puppy that comes with Openoffice (but on the other hand, 64 MB and openoffice could be stretching it!). There are other lightweight distros too. Fluxbuntu, for instance, but that also runs fluxbox (which is the same window manager as DSL).

I don't mean to tell you what you should or will like; only you can come up with the correct answer after sampling the alternatives.
