### Post by TBABill on 2010-11-01
As far as getting money back, perhaps, but they may be more willing to just fix the issues and give it back to you as it should be without any further cost. That would be up to you to negotiate.

I think your friend needs to do some homework and not just want "that free one" as far as operating systems goes. Yes it costs money for MS products and failures can be costly and disappointing, but you or she may suffer similar failures in Ubuntu at some point. I am certainly not saying Ubuntu will fail like Windows will, but the upgrade process is a potential for breakage so she would be best served with a LTS version, currently 10.04 for the latest LTS.

She should prioritize what she wants the system to do, what she needs as far as apps goes, and what her level of willingness to learn the OS is. Relying a business on an unknown OS just because it's free is a setup for failure. Personal use is one thing but business relies on things working and staying out of the way.
