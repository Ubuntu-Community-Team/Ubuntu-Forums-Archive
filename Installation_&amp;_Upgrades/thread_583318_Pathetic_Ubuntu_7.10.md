### Post by haseeb on 2007-10-20
Previously I have the 7.04 version installed and during the installation of that version I didnt have any problem except some irritating problem related to my graphics card and using dual monitor. Anyway, I was happy with my single monitor display with a nice operating system. 

Today I have installed Ubuntu 7.10. I want to share some of my experience with you. 

1. When I boot my pc with the live cd. I saw my screen resolution is 1280x960. I don't have any idea why Ubuntu developers assumed everybody in the world is using wide screen monitors. Do they have any statistics or they dont want to care about the normal poor-pocket users like me?

Anyway, thats not really a big deal anyway during the installation. However, after installing the os I found my resolution is like the same before. OK FINE, i thought, let it be changed. But alas, after changing my resolution my mouse pointer vanished: Like a blind person I was moving my mouse and after some time may be I pressed something by my invisible mouse pointer. Then the waiting animation comes and I got my mouse pointer back.

As a second step, I tried to activate my vendor graphics card driver and the result is it doesn't allow me to use resolution 600 something in my 19 inch monitors! Anyway, I have decided to give the default generic driver a try. May be my 256 mb ATI Radion 9600 card is so lousy that mighty Ubuntu guys dont want to consider it anyway.

Then I was trying to install some of the drivers and software by installing Automatix2. However after one hour of so I got installed AcroReader 8 only. Most of things show some errors and the PC hangs. So, I understand, like the previous Ubuntu version, there is no easy meal here for me.

After getting restarted my pc again goes back to single monitor wide screen resolution. I again restarted my pc and it shows the same result. 

Now I am writing the message in my lousy windows. 

Thanks anyway for the developers for this kinda operating system. Live with your pride.

Bye.
