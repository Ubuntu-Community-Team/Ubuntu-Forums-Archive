### Post by snowpine on 2013-01-07
Welcome to the forums!

Did you check the md5sum of your downloaded Ubuntu .iso image? If there was any kind of download error and the file is corrupt, then you can burn a thousand USB/CD/DVD and they will all fail. :) Here are easy instructions to check the md5sum:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM)

That's Step 1. Then Step 2 is to run "Try Ubuntu" in "Live" mode (not install). Play around with it like that for a few days. This will give you a pretty good idea of whether you like Ubuntu and whether it runs well on your hardware. Make sure to test all of the hardware features (wireless, graphics, webcam, suspend, etc.) as well as any software applications you need.

Then finally Step 3 is to install. Too many people jump directly to Step 3 in my opinion. :)

Two final thoughts: 1) There is a Search box at the top right of this page; you can find existing threads on your specific model netbook; 2) These netbooks usually have pretty low hardware specs (I am not familiar with your specific model) so you might try Lubuntu or Xubuntu instead.

Good luck!
