### Post by running_rabbit07 on 2009-06-22
I am using Ubuntu and it installed with no problems. After the install was complete Ubuntu immediately offered to load the NVIDIA drivers to fix the resolution then restarted the PC so Ubuntu could configure the driver settings itself and the resolution is actually set better now than it was through microsoft. The only other thing is getting all of the add-ons for the net. I found it helpful when I signed into yahoo and was immediately offer 3 choices of media add-ons. Most of them could be found via the add-on finder under the firefox tools menu.

If you only have 4gigs, you may consider Xubuntu being it uses a little less hard drive space and uses less RAM. I have Xubuntu on an old laptop that had 2000 pro and now it runs much faster. After I loaded Edubuntu on it, it slowed down a bit though.

I haven't tried Kubuntu, so I can't tell ya anything about it.

Good Luck and Happy Ubuntu Hunting!
