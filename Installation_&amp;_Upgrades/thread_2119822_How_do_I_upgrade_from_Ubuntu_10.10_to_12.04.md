### Post by SuperFreak on 2013-02-24
You might do better to do a complete install of 12.04 rather than upgrade. I may be mistaken, but I believe unless you are presently at a LTS such as 10.04 you have to upgrade stepwise through each version of Ubuntu (11.04, 11.10, 12.04) and this may lead to an unstable system
Before either upgrades or install do a back up of important files(email folders,Firefox settings, data files etc)

### Post by deadflowr on 2013-02-24
being that you've been running pre-unity versions of Ubuntu, I'd suggest several things to do before a re-install :
1) Take a quick peak at the new desktop layout:
[http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/take-the-tour]("http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/take-the-tour")

2)If the old gnome layout is something you prefer, there is a classic gnome option:
[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-install-classic-gnome-desktop-in-ubuntu-12-04-precise.html]("http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-install-classic-gnome-desktop-in-ubuntu-12-04-precise.html")

3)If neither of those are helpful you can try the other flavors like:

[xubuntu]("http://xubuntu.org/")

[lubuntu]("http://lubuntu.net/")

[kubuntu]("http://www.kubuntu.org/")

As with the posts above, backup anything you wish to keep, as running a fresh install is really the best option, as it is untelling what will happen if you try updating from 10.10 to 11.04 to 11.10 to 12.04.(Aside from 10.10, at least one other release is dead ;11.04)
