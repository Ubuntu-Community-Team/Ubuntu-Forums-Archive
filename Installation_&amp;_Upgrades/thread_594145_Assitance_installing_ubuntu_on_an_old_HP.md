### Post by Herman on 2007-10-27
Hello cjnkns,
Usually, CD/DVD drawers opening and closing seemingly by themselves would make me suspect your computer is under the control of a cracker who has network access, or by scripts or programs (malware) installed in your operating system, as can happen in Windows computers.

It is normal for the CD/DVD tray to eject the Ubuntu install CD at the end of an installation, but that depends on what CD you used. The Live CDs ask you first, if you want to reboot or keep using the Live CD. The 'Alternate CDs' should self-eject.

Other than that it could just get down to plain old faulty circuitry. 

The error messages would be another matter. Can you tell me more about your computer and exactly what error messages you're seeing? 

It could be that you have a faulty old CD drive and if you can try exchanging it with one that is either a new one or an second hand one that is known to work, that might fix all your problems, but I'm only guessing.
Often, a good hardware cleaning will fix all kinds of problems too. The lens in you optical drive can be cleaned with a Qtip (cotton bud), and some denatured alcohol (methylated spirits), and that might fix your error messages. The problem there is getting your CD drive out of the computer and taking it apart and getting everything back together again correctly. For most people would probably be easier to buy a new CD/DVD drive.

Once you have Ubuntu installed you'll be immune for crackers and malware as long as you are reasonably careful about what programs you install.

Regards, Herman :)
