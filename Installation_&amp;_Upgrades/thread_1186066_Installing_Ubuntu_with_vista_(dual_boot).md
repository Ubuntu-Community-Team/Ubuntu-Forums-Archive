### Post by raisinglinux on 2009-06-13
If you want to utilize your new hard drive, your only choice would be to swap the drive out depending on which OS you want to run. It sounds crazy but some users have a hot swap drive bay and have one drive per OS or config. Then simply insert the drive you want to boot with.
 
If you don't want to do that, you will need to partition and install.
 
But you still have a choice...if your new drive is larger than your old vista drive, simply clone the vista drive to the new hard drive and then partition and install Ubuntu. Here is a link on one way you can clone: [http://raisinglinux.com/2009/utilities/ultimate-boot-cd-free-computer-repair-toolkit/](http://raisinglinux.com/2009/utilities/ultimate-boot-cd-free-computer-repair-toolkit/)
 
You can also install both drives and just choose a drive on each boot if you still want to utilize both.
 
Good luck...
