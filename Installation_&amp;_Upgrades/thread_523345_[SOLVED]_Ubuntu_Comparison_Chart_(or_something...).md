### Post by Herman on 2007-08-12
'Dapper Drake 6.06  ' is an LTS (Long Term Support) edition of Ubuntu, and is more for people who just want to use Ubuntu for something (like work or something other than playing with the software itself) and who want stability and security and peace and quite. If you have a business to run, you can keep Dapper Drake for a long time.
Dapper Drake was released way past the normal release date because they wanted to make sure it was pretty near perfect before they finally released it.

As soon as 'Dapper Drake was released or probably even before, work began on 'Edgy Eft 6.10'. Edgy Eft was released pretty much on time. Naturally there are quite a lot of people who love to have the latest 'cutting edge' software,and it doesn't bother them if it isn't quite perfect yet. If you know how to back up  your files, added software and settings, (which everyone should do all the time anyway), and how to restore everything again quickly, it's really nice to be right up to date with the newest, nicest software even if it can give a problem or two for some people once in a while. These forums get very busy each time there's a new release out. After a few weeks things get quieter and maybe even a little boring again. In the meantime, many of us actually enjoy the challenge of helping iron the wrinkles out of new software and helping others cope with any hiccups.

Edgy Eft was followed by 'Feisty Fawn 7.04'  about six months later, and most of the same people who rushed to install Edgy Eft then rushed to install Feisty Fawn. But ultra conservative safety lovers who just keep using 'Dapper Drake' just kept on quietly plodding along almost unnoticed in the background, like old turtles. Probably they are getting a lot more real work done than all of us rabbit-like software junkies, (me included).
By now Egdy Eft and Feisty Fawn have both proven themselves to be safe, fast, reliable, easier to use, and can do more things than 'Dapper Drake', 
Things are starting to get a bit boring again.  I guess that means everything is working well, just like it is supposed to.

I think the next release, 'Gutsy Gibbon', is supposed to be another long term support release. I wonder if it will be released 'on time' or if it will 'sleep in' like Dapper Drake did. Whatever happens, I think for sure that 'Gutsy Gibbon' will be another great Ubuntu release, and will again be the best yet! 

I think by now I would not bother installing 'Dapper Drake' if you haven't installed Ubuntu before. I would install 'Feisty Fawn, it's much more modern and better. I wouldn't hesitate too long to replace it with 'Gutsy Gibbon' either, when that's released. If you're conservative, wait two or three weeks after Gutsy is released. After that Gutsy should be good for a while unless you want to be adventurous and go for the next release. 

That's what I think anyway,
Regards, Herman  :)
