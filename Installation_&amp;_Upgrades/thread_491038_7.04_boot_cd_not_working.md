### Post by kyphi on 2007-07-04
If your optical drives are connected to the motherboard via IDE connection you will not be able to boot any Linux distro successfully.  The 965 chipset is notorious for that.

Your options are to either use an IDE to SATA adapter which has to be powered or replace the PATA optical drive with a SATA optical drive (the board should have 6 SATA ports).  While the adapter is cheaper, the replacement of the optical drive I would consider the preferred option.

There may be other solutions but this one works.
