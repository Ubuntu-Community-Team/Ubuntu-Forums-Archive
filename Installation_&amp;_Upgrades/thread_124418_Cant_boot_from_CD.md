### Post by ysse on 2006-02-01
Well, it's either your disks or your computer (or both) that's the cause.

If you just look at the boot CD contents, you should see a few folders like **dists, doc, install, isolinux, pics, pool, and preseed**. If you do, there's a good chance that the CD is OK. If not, there's no way it could boot.

If you can, try to boot another computer with your disks. Then you would know that at least one of them is OK, and the trouble is in your computer.

> Boot from disk is set to number one in BIOS
Shouldn't that be **CD-ROM** as number one? 

A few more details about what happens when your boot process fails would be helpful. Does the drive activity LED flash? Do your hear the CD drive working?
