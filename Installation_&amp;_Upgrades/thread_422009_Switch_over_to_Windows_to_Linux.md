### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-04-24
I wouldn't trash Windows just yet.  Can you get to the web?  Try running security.symantec.com and go through the the online virus checker.  Open notepad and cut and paste the results or print out for reference.  Then go and delete all of the offending code.

I would do some generous housecleaning first.  Delete old photos, music, work files, anything else that you don't need or have backed up somewhere else.

Go through your software manager and delete programs (using the uninstall procedures that come with most software).  This will make room for Ubuntu to fit on the disk.

We need more specifics:  What kind of laptop  (processor speed, RAM, harddisk size)?

What will you use the laptop primarily for?
