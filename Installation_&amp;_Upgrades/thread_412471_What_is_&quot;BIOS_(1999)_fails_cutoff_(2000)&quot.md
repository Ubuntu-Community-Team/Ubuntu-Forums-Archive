### Post by BoneKracker on 2007-04-18
**HEY!  Don't take my Avatar.  That's not cool.**

Get your own.  Be an individual.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BIOS is software.  You download it and install it onto a special chip
on your computer. 

You can get it from the web site of whoever made your computer (or
whoever made the motherboard, or whoever made the chipset used on the
motherboard). 

They also give you a little utility program that is used to do the
installing. Installing (updating) a BIOS is often called "flashing" the
bios, and these little installer/updater programs often have the word
"flash" in their name. If the computer is a PC, this program is
typically a .exe.

They will have instructions to follow, but is basically goes like
this:  

1. You download the bios update file and the updating utility
program.  Be very careful to get a bios that goes with your computer
and not some other computer.

2.  You grab a bootable DOS or Windows disk, and copy those two files
onto it.

3.  You reboot the computer from the floppy, and then run the update
utility program.  It will probably ask you to select the file.  It will
take a minute to flash the chip, and you should leave the computer
alone until it finishes (above all, don't try to reboot it or turn it
off).

4.  That's it - you're done.

You can find articles and other instructions about how to update your
BIOS on the Web.  Read one of them, then go to your PC's website and
look for a BIOS update.  It will also probably make your machine run a
little better.
