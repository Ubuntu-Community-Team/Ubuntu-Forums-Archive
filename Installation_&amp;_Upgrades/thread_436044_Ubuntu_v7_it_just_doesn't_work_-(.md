### Post by DoctorO on 2007-05-07
I'm sorry, but whoever came up with that slogan was asking for it, I just couldn't resist after all the time I have wasted trying to install first Ubuntu v6 months ago, and now v7. Using my freshly burnt Ubuntu cd, I tried to install it on a p3 500Mhz with 4GB HD and 256MB of RAM. No go: it just gave I/O errors. Funny I previously had win 98 & xp dual booted on that pc. Isn't Ubuntu supposed to be old pc friendly? Apparently not. Okay tried it on my p4 1.8 Ghz with 500 MB RAM and win xp loaded already. Inserted the cd and from the Live CD desktop hit Install. Nice GUI came up and wanted me to partition the disk. The wording is kind of confusing "new partition" means what? Actually it means old partition re-sized and Ubuntu installed in the free space. Finished the steps. The window, oops box, asks me to reboot or continue from live cd. I click on Reboot, get the multi-boot screen, click on the first Ubuntu option, get the words Starting up, then Please wait, see the first small portion of the progress bar and...nothing else. Reboot, same result. I tried re-installing, following the same steps as above but this time at the end of the install I don't get a box asking me to reboot. I reboot anyway and same result. Hmm. I'm grateful for the fact that I still have my xp intact. Add that to the frustrating experience I had with Ubuntu v6 and its text only installation "guide" I'm starting to wonder if this Linux is just a joke started by Microsoft people. I'm sure it works for some people, but not for everyone judging by the amount of requests this forum has. And I am a computer tech, go figure. Oh yeah by the way, my old p3 500 Mhz easily re-took win 98 and xp on the 1st try. Funny eh? Ubuntu made for humans? The dream goes on. I just wasted 2 cd's and a couple of hours of my time :confused:
