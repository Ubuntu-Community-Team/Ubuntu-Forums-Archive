### Post by caljohnsmith on 2008-08-18
If Hardy 64-bit Ubuntu works for you, you could just install that, and then "add Kubuntu on top of it" by simply installing the "kubuntu-desktop" package. That will give you Ubuntu + Kubuntu, and you will have the choice of which desktop to log into at the login screen. That's actually how I do it, so I have the best of both worlds so to speak. :)
