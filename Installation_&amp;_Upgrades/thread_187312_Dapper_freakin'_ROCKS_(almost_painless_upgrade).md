### Post by baldass_newbie on 2006-06-02
Holy smokes.  I have an ATI RADEON 9200 and having installed the AMD64 and then the 32-bit (got tired of a lot of the 64-bit headaches) I just hate the ATI setup voodoo.  

After install, I could tell right away I was where I wanted to be.  Sweet.  

It wasn't all roses, however.  I tried just replacing "Breezy" with "Dapper" the /etc/apt/sources.list (after copying a backup, of course) and then apt-get update && apt-get dist-upgrade, (per this [thread]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DapperUpgrades")) but was having source issues.  

I then tried Upgrade Manager but was having similar source issues.  Basically, I had some bad source servers that I commented out.  In the process of figuring it out I came across this thread: [warning / take a look here prior to upgrading]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=185467").  

I ran through the setup a few times to make sure that things were clean and then, whammo.  I only wish I had started in the console first.  

When finished, I did the old reboot (it is a version upgrade, after all...) and there you are.  Beautiful res 1600x1200.  Can't believe it.  

Now if I could just figure out DirectX on wine...
