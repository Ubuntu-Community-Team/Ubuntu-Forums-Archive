### Post by giutax on 2007-10-24
Hello. I am not an expert but you dont have that resolution even listed in you xorg file. you can run this in terminal:
gtf 1680 1050  75. You may need to play with the 75 number as it is the refresh rate of you monitor. I dont know what that is.
My output looked like this:
 # 1400x900 @ 75.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 82.20 kHz; pclk: 188.07 MHz
  Modeline "1680x1050_75.00"  188.07  1680 1800 1984 2288  1050 1051 1054 1096  -HSync +Vsync
 
then you need to add the Modeline under the Monitor section of the xorg.conf

only this part though  Modeline "1680x1050_75.00"  188.07  1680 1800 1984 2288  1050 1051 1054 1096  -HSync +Vsync. 

I had to run gtf 1680 1050  75 at 4 different refresh 72 70 68 rates before i got my monitor to work with 1440x900. I added all the the Modelines to the xorg.conf until i found one that worked. 

modeline "1440x900@72"  130.75  1440 1536 1688 1936  900 901 904 938  -hSync $
  modeline "1440x900@70" 126.98  1440 1536 1688 1936  900 901 904 937  -hSync +$
  modeline "1440x900@68"  122.20  1440 1528 1680 1920  900 901 904 936  -hSync $
