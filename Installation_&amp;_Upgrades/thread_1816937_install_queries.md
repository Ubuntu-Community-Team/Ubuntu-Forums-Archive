### Post by new_tolinux on 2011-08-02
Normally, you'll download the ISO, and burn it on a blank CD, and then boot that....
Almost any burning-program can do so, Windows 7 even can do it without any burning program (right-click the ISO, then choose "Burn image to disc").

Although, you should really read well what's on your screen when you boot from the CD, since it's quite possible that you'll overwrite your existing windows-installation instead of installing linux to your external harddrive.
