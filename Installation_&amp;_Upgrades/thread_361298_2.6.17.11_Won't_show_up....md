### Post by Onimae on 2007-02-20
I can't remember which one I had to add, but to get rid of the "faulty kernel" just look for it in your /boot folder. Then use this command:
```

gksudo gedit /boot/grub/menu.lst

```
find the line of the offending kernel, and delete it. That should do it.

**Onimae**
