### Post by Bartender on 2009-09-19
If, as you say, the two OS'es are on separate partitions and that all seems to be correct, I'm thinking the first thing you want to do is at least get Windows working again.  To do that, fix the MBR.  There are about a million posts on this.  If you don't have a genuine Windows installation disc (who does these days?) then Super Grub Disc might be one of the easiest ways to go.

Get Windows working first, then post again.  

My guess is that your installation disc was burnt too quickly and it had a flaw.  Only takes one tiny error.

Once you get Windows running, breathe a sigh of relief and decide how you want to proceed.  You can try to fix GRUB, but don't do it with the disc you used before!
