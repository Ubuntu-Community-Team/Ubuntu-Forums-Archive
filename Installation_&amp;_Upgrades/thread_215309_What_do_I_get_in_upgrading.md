### Post by ezsit on 2006-07-14
> What's new

Ubuntu is released regularly and predictably. Since our October 2005 release (Ubuntu 5.10), we've made a lot of improvements to give you the best Linux experience possible. Here's what's new with Ubuntu 6.06 LTS:
On the Desktop CD

A new, very fast, graphical installer based on the Live CD

Faster system startup and login

Graphical shutdown process

Improved support for video playback

Optional NetworkManager for convenient roaming between different
wireless and wired networks

GNOME 2.14.1, OpenOffice.org 2.0.2, X.org 7.0 

1. I like the old installer, it worked perfectly and never gave me problems. It was flexible and easy to use. The old installer did not get in the way of what I wanted to do and was a model of elegance and effeciency. The new installer sucks (when the first bit of advice a newbie is given is to download the Alternate CD and try that to avoid installation problems, why would anyone want the new installer?).

2. On my system (Celeron 2ghz, 512mb ram, older Nvidia card, intel 845 chipset built-in sound and ethernet), Breezy boots just as quickly as Dapper, and tis prettier too. Dapper's boot screen is distorted and ugly compared to Breezy's. Dapper startup has a stupid popup box up with useless progress indicators and awful fonts. Breezy just looks elegant.

3. Menu organization is dumbded-down ala WinXP. The new icons and buttons all look ripped from XP. Ubuntu is becoming the XP of the Linux world. True, I like my Linux to just work as much as possible, but Breezy does just this without the toy-like look and feel.

4. I don't need power management, especially features that do not work reliably. I have a desktop tower and most power management does not apply.

5. I installed all the codecs and video playback is just fine.

6. As a desktop tower, I don't do much roaming. I have a direct ehternet line to the router. Wireless settings don't apply to me.

All the software that came with Breezy works fine for me. I have no need for a new X server with a 5 year old video card. No need for a new version of OpenOffice since Beta2 works great for me. No need for Xgl since it rarely works well anyway.
