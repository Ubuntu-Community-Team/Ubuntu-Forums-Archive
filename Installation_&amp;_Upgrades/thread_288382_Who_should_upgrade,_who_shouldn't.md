### Post by aysiu on 2006-10-29
If you need a real performance boost, you shouldn't be upgrading to Edgy for that--you should be using a lightweight window manager (IceWM, Fluxbox, Windowmaker) instead of Gnome, KDE, or even XFCE (which is pretty lightweight in its own right).

That said, the great thing about Linux in general (and Ubuntu specifically, too) is that you can have the latest and greatest, even in old hardware. Unlike Windows and Mac, where the hardware requirements just go up and up and up with every release, Ubuntu Edgy doesn't require any more than Ubuntu Warty did two years ago.

But... you should probably use IceWM or Fluxbox in Edgy if you have an older computer (I'm guessing it has 128 MB of RAM or less).

Here's my list of users who should upgrade:
[LIST=1]
[*]those who are still running Hoary or Warty.
[*]those who want the latest packages and have a spare partition to backup their current Ubuntu install via a disk image.
[*]those who have very little "alien" packages installed.
[*]those who are willing to sit through an hour and a half watching things fly on the screen during upgrade :)
[*]those who are willing to solve their own problems and not rant at the Ubuntu developers or those who are not afraid to use the command line.
[*]those who have physical access to their machine in case problems arise.[/LIST]That said, here are my list of people who should **NOT** upgrade:
[LIST=1]
[*]corporate users using Dapper.
[*]those who are using their machine for production use or very important work. Are you running a server for mission-critical tasks?
[*]those with very short attention spans :) sorry but upgrade requires that you pay attention to the process, especially during the package configuration stage.
[*]those who are impatient.
[*]those who are unable to find and correct their own problems or those who are unfamiliar with the command line.
[*]those who do not have physical access to their machine. Do you need SSH to access your machine?[/LIST] As for your 5 year-old laptop, install XUbuntu instead of the Ubuntu or KUbuntu.
