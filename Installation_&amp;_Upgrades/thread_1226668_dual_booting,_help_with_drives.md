### Post by stlsaint on 2009-07-29
well first ensure you have a backup image of that recovery drive...unless you dont want vista anymore...then like merlin said dont go thru wubi...use partitions...ensure your d drive has enough gb to hold vista and install that first then burn a ubuntu iso to disc and boot from there...when you get to the partitioner table select to do manual selections of partitions and put ubuntu on your c drive or whatever you desire! grub will upload itself into vista bootloader and at the boot screen you will have the options of which os to boot! any further issues i recommend using a bootloader manager like easybcd for windows...my personal fav! for booting trouble shooting!
