### Post by caffienefree on 2007-10-25
I believe your issue may be related to this bug... [https://bugs.launchpad.net/envy/+bug/107915](https://bugs.launchpad.net/envy/+bug/107915)

Without being able to replicate the exact steps, I'd say remove the nvidiaglx driver, and then do another sudo apt-get update&&sudo apt-get dist-upgrade (or update-manager -p -d). If that doesn't give you the remaining packages, then sudo apt-get update&&sudo apt-get upgrade.

I know there's a command dpkg --configure something, it usually tells you to enter it when your upgrade goes wrong, but I can't remember it... will keep looking, though.
