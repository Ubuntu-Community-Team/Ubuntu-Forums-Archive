### Post by ashdezign on 2007-11-10
I upgraded to Gutsy when it was first released and since then have upgraded my secretary, wife and son's computers to gutsy as well.

What's different: (From my experience) overall Gutsy seems more stable (at least for me).

My logitech camera works plug n play on Gutsy when I couldn't get it working at all on Feisty.

Printer support seems improved, More printers detected automatically.

Gaim has finally been replaced by Pidgin (the new name for Gaim and the newest version of it).

New software available in the repositories and newer versions of them.

New Linux Kernel

Compiz Fusion is installed by default and you can use as little or as much of the special effects and functionality as you like. Limited only by your desire and your computers capabilities. And if your computer cannot handle it it will disable compiz by default leaving you with a normal desktop experience.

Most default install programs seems to have newer versions than were available in Feisty and while I have seen some complaints about Open Office on the forums, so far I have had no issues.

Better (imo) desktop search integration with tracker. (It finds a lot more of my files than beagle ever did and supports tagging)
