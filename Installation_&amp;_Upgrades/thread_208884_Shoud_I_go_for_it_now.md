### Post by ezsit on 2006-07-05
After reading the posts I noticed that most of the problems were happening on laptops and newer computers (especially ones with SATA hard drives). 

If you have an older desktop computer without SATA hard drives you will probably be fine. If you have a new computer or laptop, I would stick with what works.

I am reminded of a old saying, "if it ain't broke, don't fix it." This seems to apply more and more to Ubuntu. 5.10 is still my choice because it just works and 6.06 doesn't offer anything worthwhile.
