### Post by ffxr on 2007-04-09
Your choice should be made by considering how many programs & your going to install on each os; i would consider putting ubuntus' home directory on the shared FAT32 drive.., but its not really  that important, your file & os structure might appear more logical if its kept on the ubuntu drive...   its really upto you.. theres no hard and fast rules.. as long as the ubuntu partition is not going to be tiny <2gb which is definately not recommended...

id probably go with 20gig for each OS.. remembering that its easier to add an extra hard drive as additional storage space rather than to expand a partition with an OS already on it..

swap isnt really important unless your choked for RAM.. 512mb is probably more than enough..
