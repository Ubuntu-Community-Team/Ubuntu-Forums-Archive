### Post by taurus on 2007-01-11
You can use gparted on the LiveCD to resize your harddrive, leaving some space at the end to install Ubuntu.  You need to do that first before you can install Ubuntu on your machine.  And of course, always backup your important data in Windows but if you just follow the instructions on the screen, you should be fine.

### Post by Coelocanth on 2007-01-12
I resized my Windows install almost a year ago now. Back up your data. As noted, make sure you defrag your hard drive. I recommend doing this several times beforehand (I did mine three times). Back up your data. A good idea is to go through your windows install and uninstall anything you don't feel you need and delete any files you feel are useless. Basic disc cleaning. Back up your data. Empty your recycle bin, and use disc cleanup. Then defrag. Then back up your data. Everything should go very smoothly, as long as you follow the instructions in the tutorial.

Good luck.

Oh, and did I mention you should back up your data? ;)
