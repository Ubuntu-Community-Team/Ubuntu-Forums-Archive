I'd say with that computer Gnome would be fine but with Ubuntu, Fedora, Suse it would be on the edge as they tend to pack everything in.  I use Gentoo, so I may be a little bias with the potentials.  Gentoo's awesome but it's really made for the Linux hobbyist as the user is responsible for most of the setting up.  Debian is the parentage of Ubuntu and is well known for it's hardware support, a little more difficult to install than Ubuntu but nice and solid once it's running, and would probably the next best bet.

Those are all pretty good distros though and look to be kept up to date.  Most if not all of these distrobutions will have the ability to install XFCE once the initial desktop is installed.

md5 sums are used to measure the length of the download and check for basic integrity.  A bad md5 may not reflect a corrupted image and rather show a poor job of maintenance on the download site.  Nevertheless it's probably wise not to use that iso.

Footnote: If there is pretty limited bandwith limit from the ISP, keep in mind that updates for Ubuntu or the other distrobutions will be done from their servers and that these updates at times can be rather large.  The ISP that is being used does have a snapshot of the Gentoo portage tree in it's repository that would eliminate this problem.
