### Post by RiceMonster on 2008-06-26
So I've installed Arch for the second time after installing Slackware, not liking the package management, not getting my resolution to work right, and having the Gentoo 2008 beta 2 live cd not even boot. This time I'm pretty sure I'm going to stay. It was actually the first distro I used and I ended up installing Ubuntu out of frustration, as I had no idea idea what I was doing. This time every thing's going smoothly and I remember why I wanted to install it in the first place. I'm glad I decided to switch to Ubuntu originally as it gave me a good chance to learn about Linux, so I can get proper enjoyment from distros like Arch.

So anyway, what I love about Arch:
1) Pacman is a really good package manager. I like it more than apt-get
2) The repos have pretty much everything, and they're far more up to date than Ubuntu's (in my experience).
3) Rolling release is a superior model. It keeps the repos up to date, and I don't have to reinstall or do a whole distro upgrade every 6 months.
4) I have full control over my system. Now I don't have to discover what's holding everything together, because I put a great deal of it together myself. Plus, Arch is way faster than an "out-of-the-box" distro.
