### Post by K.Mandla on 2009-01-02
I mentioned this on the Arch forums and I thought I'd post a note here too ... the [Lowarch home page]("http://lowarch.org"), which was an effort to extend Arch Linux to include i586 machines, expired at the end of the year. 

I still have a copy of the ISO; I posted it to a [file hosting service]("http://hosted.filefront.com/kmandla/"), and I'm [seeding it]("http://linuxtracker.org/index.php?page=torrent-details&id=7ca348467875268f1c1dff1d9e77005e22d6ac7d") on a high-speed line (8Mbps up, although I cap torrent traffic at 240Kbps ;) ).

There are a few people who are interested in i586 rebuilds as a central repository; if you are one of them, you might post a note [here]("http://bbs.archlinux.org/viewtopic.php?id=62176"). Short of something like Crux or Gentoo, Arch is the best possible answer for old or outdated hardware. A proper repo for i586 would be a godsend. ;)

Cheers!
