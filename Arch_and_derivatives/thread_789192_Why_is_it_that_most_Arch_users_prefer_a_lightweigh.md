### Post by handy on 2008-05-12
I think that the nature of Arch being *"Arch Linux is what you make it"* has created the situation where Arch shares it's potential for lightness with the few other Linux distro's that also require building from the ground up.

When building your distro' in such a way it will always have what you require/desire & be largely free of what the builder considers to be extraneous, so these types of distro' will imho always be lighter.

There are then of course, those people which whether due to the limitations of their hardware, or due to personal desire *(**for whatever reason**)* pursue (in the extreme) the lightest system they can manifest.

To answer the thread's question further, one could look at the variety of motivational forces ranging from the purely practical to the psychopathic!

If so, I would think that people who require speed for an undertaking have a practical reason.  Those that enjoy the challenge of building a light & fast system surely do exist.  Those that for whatever reasons dislike GUI's & enjoy using the terminal also exist.  There are also the elitists that consider their terminal knowledge & computing expertise a reason to consider themselves superior to mouse clickers! :lolflag:  These people are related to those that consider themselves as Linux users to be superior to Windows users.  They are both members of the huge & diverse human family that are unfortunately afflicted by & suffering from chauvinism.

### Post by smartboyathome on 2008-05-13
I got enlightenment up and running on arch. Just use a combination fo the previous poster's Elightenment wiki page and the [Beginner's Guide]("http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Beginners_Guide") to do it. I recommend gtkPacman if you want a gui for it, and to install yaourt (see the portion in that guide on it) for unsupported packages. To install firefox 3 (which I prefer over firefox 2) run this code: "yaourt -S firefox3-bin" (without the quotes) and Firefox 3 will install. For a GTK theme switcher I recommend gtk-theme-switcher2 (see gtkPacman). After that, everything else should be at your fingertips or in guides.
