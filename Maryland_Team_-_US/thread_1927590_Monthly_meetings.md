### Post by Nopposan on 2012-08-28
I wasn't aware that there was a Maryland Ubuntu user's group until today. I learned about it after e-mailing Canonical customer support and asking about local Ubuntu.

I recently started a group called Linux Neighbors on Meetup.com

[http://www.meetup.com/Linux-Neighbors]("http://www.meetup.com/Linux-Neighbors")

We're in Montgomery County. The whole purpose is to give Linux the advantage of face-to-face user help. (Thereby, we may successfully overcome some of the hesitancy or discomfort of new users, and this should help us to keep PC's from being needlessly "upgraded," or rather exchanged for faster PC's running the latest proprietary OS.) Our first meeting is at 3:30 pm this Saturday, the first of September. I'm going to advocate that we recommend Ubuntu and focus our newbie user-help efforts on it. I'd also like us to discuss a mechanism by which new members agree to be an installation assistant and recruiter for Linux Neighbors. Perhaps Linux Neighbors could become a sub-group or project of the Maryland Ubuntu Team.

Also, I've just learned of and joined a brand new LUG, it's Montgomery County Linux Users' Group, or MoCoLUG, and it is hosted as a group on yahoo.com. MoCoLUG hasn't met yet, but it's an interesting development. To join MoCoLUG, check out Yahoo Groups or e-mail the administrator at [email]mocolug@yahoogroups.com[/email].
