### Post by ian-weisser on 2016-12-05
Required reading:
- [Trademark policy]("https://www.ubuntu.com/legal/terms-and-policies/intellectual-property-policy") for using the 'Ubuntu' name
- [Ubuntu design guide]("http://design.ubuntu.com/brand/ubuntu-logo") for the graphic layout and colors
- Also see the available [graphic element and pictogram library]("http://design.ubuntu.com/downloads?metadata=element-pictogram").

I think you are within the guidelines for use of the Ubuntu trademark, as you are merely identifying an official and supported distribution channel...but I am not a Lawyer, and I don't work for Canonical.

Consider donating your graphic to [Spreadubuntu]("http://spreadubuntu.org/"), one community website for community-created graphics like this

The Ubuntu Software bag logo may confuse a bit, since it represents debs instead of snaps. Perhaps the more generic circle-of-friends may be less confusing.
Could the background color perhaps match the design guide a bit more?

12.04 and 14.04 users cannot easily add snaps, so be sure their click-throughs fail gracefully.
