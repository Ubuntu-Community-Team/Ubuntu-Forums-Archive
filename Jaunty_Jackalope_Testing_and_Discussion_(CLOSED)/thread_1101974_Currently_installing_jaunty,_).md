### Post by Skripka on 2009-03-23
> **Bart_D said:**
> Is that all ext4 does?  Bootup and shutdown faster?  Or is the system itself(opening apps, folders, etc.) faster as well?

Disk check operations are roughly 10X faster here.

I can check all 3 partitions on my 250GB drive in less than 30 seconds.  Eat that Ext3 ;)


BUT-due to delayed allocation-you MUST be wary of improperly shutting your system down.  Most apps and DE config files are not written with delayed allocation in mind--ergo improper shutdows can cause data corruption/loss.  This is not a problem with Ext, but with the apps people run on it.
