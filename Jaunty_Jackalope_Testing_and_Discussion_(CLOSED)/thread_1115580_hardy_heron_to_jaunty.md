### Post by Melk79 on 2009-04-04
It depends on your needs/wants.  I suggest reading the release notes and press releases of Jaunty to get an idea of what's coming and see if it's of interest to you.  If you just want a stable computer that you can use, you might be best with Hardy.  If you're wanting to try the new ext4 file system, new notification system, or other new features of Jaunty, you may want to upgrade.  If everything is working great now and you know nothing about those things, just stay where you're at.  It'd probably save some headaches.
