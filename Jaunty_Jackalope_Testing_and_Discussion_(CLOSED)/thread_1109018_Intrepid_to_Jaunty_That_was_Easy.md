### Post by Steve1961 on 2009-03-28
Over the years I've done many dist-upgrades from one version of Ubuntu to the next, and most of the time they've gone OK, albeit with a few minor glitches that needed working out here and there.  However, I recently bought a new desktop and did a clean installation of Intrepid on it.  And when the beta of Jaunty was released on Thursday I thought I might as well upgrade the system as I normally do when the new release gets to the beta stage.  I have to say that this was the most problem free upgrade I've ever done.  It took about an hour, and following a reboot I was expecting to have to sort a few glitches out.  But no drama, no glitches, the box just rebooted and that was that, I was running Jaunty.  Ubuntu really is getting better from release to release, and I've run every version since Hoary.  So congrats to the devs.
