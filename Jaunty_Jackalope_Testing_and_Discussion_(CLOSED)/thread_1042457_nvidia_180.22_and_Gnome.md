### Post by jfernyhough on 2009-01-18
When you say "blank" do you mean it goes black but remains powered, or that the screen powers off? Have you ever configured display settings via nvidia-settings? Possibly it's trying to output to a display that's not connected.

Secondly, try running
$ sudo dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg

and re-add the IgnoreABI option. This will reset xorg.conf back to its initial minimalist state so we can narrow down the options.

As a final check, log in via a console, 
$ sudo mv /home/username /home/usernamebak
Log out, go back into GDM (CTRL-ALT-F7 if it doesn't automatically switch) and log in. This will recreate your gnome settings from scratch - if this works then it's an error somewhere in those and we can mostly recover by logging back out, removing the created /home/username, moving the original back, then rm -fR ~/.gnome* and ~/.gnome2*

### Post by Nullack on 2009-01-19
Those xorg confs are not what nvidia recommends. The move is toward x auto setup not extended conf setup. Heres what works on 180.22 after I manually installed it from nvidias website:

Section "Device"
	Identifier	"Configured Video Device"
	Driver		"nvidia"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"Configured Monitor"
EndSection

Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Monitor		"Configured Monitor"
	Device		"Configured Video Device"
EndSection

Section "ServerFlags"
	Option		"IgnoreABI" "True"
EndSection

If that doesnt work you have some sort of incorrect install. Some diagnostics would be has the module compiled, is the module loaded, what does the x log say about why it fails to execute etcetc
