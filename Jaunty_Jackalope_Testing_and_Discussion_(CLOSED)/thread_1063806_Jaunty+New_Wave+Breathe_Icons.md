### Post by GepettoBR on 2009-02-15
> **AlexBellisBrown said:**
> I think Jaunty should come with a "theme selecter" on the install CD, or when your upgrading, and the themes should be pulled from the Gnomelook top 10 most popular, providing of course they are released under the appropriate licenses. If all you people as complaining about the default, instead of giving the OS looking like something, have the option to MAKE it look like something. *I understand what the appearences thing does, im mearly saying that it should come up automatically so people can choose on the first time they boot the OS*

At first your idea is good, but I see two issues with putting it into practice:

1) The issue with licenses that you mentioned yourself. It's complicated to manage that (as opposed t a script that grabs the Top Ten, if licensing weren't an issue) especially since many of the most popular themes are based on other themes and thus partially inherit their licenses

and

2) Looking through the first two pages of Gnome-Look's Most Downloaded and Top Rated, I noticed that the overwhelming majority of themes is either very dark or OSX-inspired, so a method that simply harvests the top picks would lack in variety.

Most of the top themes in Gnome-Look are either GPL, public domain or CC so the first issue isn't, currently, that important, but the second is. If a new user sees that the automated tool only lets you choose between variations of a small number of ideas* this person will have the impression that GNOME's theming capabilities are as limited as Windows' and might not even look further for a theme he or she likes.

If there is a way to bypass these issues without having to resort on a randomizer that would pull up unfinished themes as well as complete, polished ones, then this might be a very good solution. I should point out that this would be a first as well. I don't know of any OS that does this.

*I don't mean to imply that the creators of these themes didn't put work into making them stand out (if they hadn't, their themes wouldn't be in the top of such a large list) but what stands out the most at first glance are the color schemes and the window control buttons, which in general are very similar in themes based on the same idea.
