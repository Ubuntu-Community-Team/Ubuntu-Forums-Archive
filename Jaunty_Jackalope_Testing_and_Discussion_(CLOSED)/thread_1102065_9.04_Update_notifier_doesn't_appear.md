### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-03-23
This thread has way too many duplicates, but I agree I don't like this change. It took me a while to decide, but I have caught myself opening up the update manager directly, telling to check for updates, punching in my password...
As a security-conscious user (and one who loves updates of all shapes and sizes), I care about having my updates as they happen. This breaks that use case. If I ran a server, I would now have to keep up on the security notices or I couldn't guarantee the safety of my server within seven day periods.

In fact, a good half of Canonical's NotifyOSD stuff is misguided. I love the bubbles, I love the new capability we gain for undemanding notifications; it brings a whole new layer to interaction here.However, I get the feeling that someone just does not understand the purpose of the system tray, or, as it is known in GNOME, the ***_notification area_***. They've gone on some bizarre rampage in which they claim that all things which dare make contact with the system tray are dirty and must be purged from existence (even when they are doing the right thing). Instead, they prefer woefully desktop-specific panel applets and windows which get to be managed by Compiz; the most fancy decorator of windows, yet most inept manager of windows, available.

If you want declutter the notification area, work upstream with the FreeDesktop folks and see about updating the system tray spec with support for categories. Down the line, everyone will be grateful.


As for the update manager stuff, it won't break any laws to have both solutions concurrently. It would help if the update manager window had the same icon as the update notifier uses to teach users the visual cue involved.

### Post by allCuExpMa on 2009-03-27
Yeah i found that as well quite annoying, no notifier anymore, but at the other hand, it's a beta version, so it's suggested to run or look for updates daily anyway (or maybe more than ones a day)....
here is the solution, at least to get he "sys-tray" icon back :) 
gconftool -s --type bool /apps/update-notifier/auto_launch false 
and then kill and restart update-notifier and will get the icon for update-notifier back. (to check it straight away, run: sudo apt-get update)
