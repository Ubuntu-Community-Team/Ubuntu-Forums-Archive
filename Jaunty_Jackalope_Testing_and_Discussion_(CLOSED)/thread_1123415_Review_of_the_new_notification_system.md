### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-04-13
I agree part-way. notify-osd could implement timers and action buttons within its current general design, but doesn't. While I agree with getting apps to be coded defensively, and making notifications more passive, many things it does seem needlessly complicated.

As for the applications throwing many notifications at once problem, notify-osd's composited goodness could solve this elegantly along with a sharpening of the current kludge that puts confirmations (volume adjustment) apart from others. Notifications from the same application should be queued, but each application should get its own independent queue. The different queues of notifications should be stacked vertically at the same time, to a limit of two or three or so. Urgency should come in to play.
Doing that also means that the hard-coded timer for bubbles (which I agree is way too short for Jaunty to be taken seriously as an accessible release) need not be hard-coded, since they don't get in anyone's way but themselves.

On the BRIGHT side, I'm loving the new features this adds that are more specific to notifications. For example, attaching a progress percentage. Appending notifications has recently been fixed up. The feature is basically invisible since the things disappear too quickly, but still pretty neat.
And I still think killing the short-lived click responses on the bubbles to be a worthwhile step. Those of notification-daemon also harmed accessibility, sucked for timed notifications since users had to click things urgently, and sucked for untimed notifications since users had to ultimately close the notification because it got in their way.

I also like that this change has brought out some thinking towards improving the GNOME desktop in terms of consistency. Downstream, volume control and brightness adjustment now use libnotify for feedback, which absolutely makes sense. Hopefully this will be able to go upstream some day. This exposed to me some absolutely bizarre UI design for sticky and slow keys sanity check dialogs (which previously were notification bubbles. And still are. But shouldn't be since I gave them a bloody patch!)
While this leaves a hole where I agree we need another API or another arm of the existing one, the *simpler* notifications encourage further use of notifications where they make sense. For example, I was working on a really cool visual notification for when keyboard layout is changed, made doable by the fact that notify-osd is completely unobtrusive.

With many eyes at GNOME turned towards gnome-shell and compositing window managers, I think the big fix to a lot of qualms people have making normal dialog boxes (as opposed to the glorified dialog boxes of notification-daemon) can be solved by improving how window managers present them in the first place :)
