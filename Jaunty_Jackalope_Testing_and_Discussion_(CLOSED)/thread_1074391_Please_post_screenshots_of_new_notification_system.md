```

cd notify-osd
bzr unbind

### Post by MacSlow on 2009-02-28
> **Yes_It's_Me said:**
> Has anyone else noticed that suddenly the notifications are much larger and consequently, easier to read? Maybe someone took note of my bug report:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/335383](https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/335383)

Good improvement I say, but I would still like the ability to change font size myself (though this new size fits me perfectly!).

The size and face of the fonts - used for notifications - is picked up from gconf (your system-wide settings for GNOME). It would be unwise to provide a separate settings-dialog just for that. Right now it's just picked up at startup of notify-osd. This means if you change your system-wide font-settings after you logged in (read: notify-osd was started) this change will not affect the text rendered in the notification-bubbles until you restart notify-osd or log out and in again. But this behaviour will be changed to be runtime-dynamic for 9.04.

Best regards ...

Mirco "MacSlow" Müller
