### Post by Polmac on 2009-03-11
> **MadsRH said:**
> So, no one knows?

The notifications are probably going to be included in GNOME; some Ubuntu members that are also GNOME members are trying to do that and it seems it's likely that will happen. Same thing was tried with KDE, but for the moment it seems there's no consensus on that matter.

In the usability hackfest where the features of GNOME 3.0 where discussed, one of the new ideas that were considered likely to be implemented was a notification icon that would collect the notifications received, and show them when clicked. This is very similar to what is being done for Jaunty: notifications shown for a few seconds, and they can be read later clicking on an icon. So GNOME's and Ubuntu's ideas on this issue are definitely in the same direction.

But of course, everything will depend on the response of the users to the new notifications.

### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-03-17
I'm *really* starting to love this now after helping with a bit of bug fixing and whatnot, seeing how many things used notification bubbles for no good reason. (Recent example: The slow keys and sticky keys messages).

What is happening here is cool: Ubuntu is sharpening the concept of a notification, making it very specific in what it does and how it behaves. It almost feels like this is a fresh invention because it's such a departure from the mere glorified dialogs that notifications were before.

When this is finished with, notifications will be *more powerful*, not less, because they will have a real, unique and important place on the desktop.
