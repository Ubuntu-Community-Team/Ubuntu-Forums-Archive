### Post by tlois on 2009-04-04
I would try to run it live first to see how your equipment works with it.  I tried it on a few computers (installed it completely on my work computer, which the IT cats have not figured out yet! Can I get fired for that?!) . The only problem I had was with connecting to my WPA encrypted wireless network at home.  

But it did at least work with my drivers on both of my eee machines, which was a major pain before. It also looks pretty rockin' with the new boot screen, theme choices and a neat little program called Janitor or something like that that helps get rid of trash floating around.

The only way I could get it to work with WPA on my asus little guy was to hardwire into the router, then wpa seemed to work when running purely wireless.  

Other than the WPA issue, it looks great and I doubt you will have too many issues with it.  But do try running it live first just to confirm.

editing to say-there are bug reports about the wpa issue, so hopefully will be fixed soon.  Editing again to say that I tried something I meant to try earlier with the WPA- I set my router to g only and it now works with WPA.
