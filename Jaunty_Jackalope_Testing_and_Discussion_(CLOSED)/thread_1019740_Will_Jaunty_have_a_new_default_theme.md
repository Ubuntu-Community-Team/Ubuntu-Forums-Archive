(I find it strange that people keep saying the Fedora artwork is beautiful. As a Fedora guy I really wished they would stop changing it all the time and for once perfect something over a few cycles like the Ubuntu and openSUSE guys).

XUL has actually made vast leaps and bounds forward - Mozilla have made countless changes to their underlying architecture to accommodate GTK+ integration, and I now find that only very exotic widget themes have any problems with it - and they can be fixed with a custom stylesheet or a few source patches (if you don't want such a hackish work around).  The default theme will adapt to the user's icon and GTK+ theme as it stands, more effectively than many native apps.

Frankly, OpenOffice is a glacial juggernaut, and there's no real motivation to bring around significant UI changes.  If somebody like Canonical were prepared to fund experienced developers to sort out the UI toolkit and switch the rendering engine to Cairo, it would look infinitely better for a fairly modest amount of work.  There's no point waiting for theses sorts of changes if there's not even any effort being made to bring them about.  Oh, and only OpenOffice plugins are written in Java, the core UI is C++ - it's not an issue with Java, although that isn't perfect.  I urge you to actually give Dust a go - it works very well with all of these applications.

Frankly, I wouldn't consider our current desktop to be a "stylish golden brown" - let's be honest, at the moment it's mostly orange.  I'm not saying that architecture improvements aren't necessary - merely that there's no real timeframe for them and UI appearance is very rarely of significant concern to upstream developers.  If there's no roadmap for these improvements, surely it makes sense to make the best of what we have?  The amount of developer effort to switch to Dust (or any of the other themes out there) is minimal to none - it's simply a policy position that's standing in the way for the time being.  Distinction should be in a colour palette, artwork style and a logo - not in garish window borders or bland UI controls.

If Canonical stated that a new theme would be introduced once specific upstream targets had been met and put some resources into meeting those targets, I'd understand.  As things stand they keep promising a UI refresh while making no visible steps towards it.
