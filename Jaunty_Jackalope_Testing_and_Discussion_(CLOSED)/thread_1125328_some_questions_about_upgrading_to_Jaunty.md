### Post by eeeandrew on 2009-04-14
I personally prefer a clean install. If you partitioned your HDD on install so as to have /home and / on seperate partitions you can upgrade the OS on the / partition and leave you /home partition which stores all your documents and settings intact. You will need to reinstall your programs but their settings will be preserved.

Hope this is useful,
Andrew

Yup, the process to upgrade is very easy. I have had my laptop running Jaunty since December, and my Desktop running Jaunty since Alpha 4. As the other's have said, it's basically the same as just upgrading a bunch of packages and changing your repos. Luckily the new upgrader program in Ubuntu does this all 'automagically' for you, and all you have to do is reboot when it's done. Very simple and straightforward.
