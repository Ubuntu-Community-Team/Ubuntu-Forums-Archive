### Post by SushiR on 2009-04-19
I don't know if and how much compiz will be integrated into Gnome3. Some features of compiz are quite useful, as well as some features of metacity, gnome-panel, applets in the current state make working on a desktop a lot easier. I hope they'll combine the best features of both PLUS some new stuff to make Gnome3 a pleasure to work with.

Customization is a really important thing. Users won't give up "their" way they'd been used to work, to organize application windows, files, eyecandy. You can well observe it with KDE4. I gave up on KDE because I can't stand the way it acts (slow, not intuitive, a lot of useless stuff IMHO...). So the thankless task of Gnome developers is to keep the right balance between new stuff, usability (most important), eyecandy and the possibility to customize every tiny bit of the desktop. I know that is a hard job to do and it's a long way to get there.

I hope the Gnome staff will not be as ignorant as the KDE guys appear to be. Constructive criticism must be heard and accepted as part of the road to Gnome3. New ideas must be argued and not be rejected on principle. Both users and developers must learn from each other. And I guess this is the most important rule to create a powerful, easy to use and feature-rich desktop environment.
