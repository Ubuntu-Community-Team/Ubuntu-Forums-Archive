Your sound problem could be something very simple.

double click on volume icon and make sure PCM volume is turned up to max.

If using surround sound speakers, go into the preferences after you double click the volume icon and enable:
Front
Surround
Center
And turn them to max.

I could solve it by changing the output plugin in Audacious from PulseAudio to ALSA and back to PulseAudio.

The Master volume was turned up to 100% but the music output was nearly mute at the Audacious sound level of 50%.

Ha! I could reproduce it by changing the Audacious sound level to 50%, then opened the Audacious preferences and changed PulseAudio to ALSA and back to PulseAudio. The Master Volume is at 100% and the music output again nearly mute because PCM stays at 50%!

Audacious with ALSA changes the PCM volume by turning up/down the Audacious sound level.

Audacious with PulseAudio doesn't change the Master or the PCM volume.

To change that again turn the Audacious sound level at 100%, reduce the Master sound level as a precaution to maybe 60% and change PulseAudio to ALSA and back again to PulseAudio. Then reduce the Audacious sound level from 100% to 50% and after a short "sound break" the music output gets much louder while reducing the sound level ... or use ... :mrgreen:

```
alsamixer -D hw:0
```
