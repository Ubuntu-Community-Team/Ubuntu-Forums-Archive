### Post by teddybairs1 on 2009-03-06
I had never been really happy with Intrepid Ibex. It always seemed slow, a resource hog, and something never seemed quite right. After a round of installs to Debian Lenny, back to Hardy, back to Intrepid, I took the plunge and installed Kubuntu Jaunty (it seemed the safer bet from Ubuntu because the KDE 4 desktop is past stable release, as opposed to Gnome 2.26 which hasn't hit release yet). Aside from one or two graphical glitches, it does seem a lot more stable than Intrepid did with either KDE or Gnome. It also seems to use less resources (up to 100MB less RAM-wise) than Intrepid. It just seems ironic to me that the Alpha is more stable than the previous release.

### Post by DarkOx on 2009-03-06
On Hardy, I kept hearing my hard disk spinning, which didn't happen on my Windows partition. I'd heard that Hardy/Intrepid had a bug known to eat hard drives and that it'd been fixed in Jaunty. I don't know if that's actually true, but it was enough to make me nervous. So I've upgraded to Jaunty Alpha 5, which seems to be working perfectly -- no audible spinning at all; everything else works fine.

So far, it's been really good. Jaunty feels faster, both on boot-up and in general use. The new notification system looks slick, though it's annoying when someone messages you, and clicking the message doesn't do anything. I haven't had any problems with stability, but that may because I haven't updated the system since I installed it (worried about breakage, and all that). And, of course, your mileage will no doubt vary.
