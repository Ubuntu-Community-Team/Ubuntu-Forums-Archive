### Post by sgosnell on 2009-03-01
I put it on my EEE 900, and it worked out of the box.  One caveat - I installed it to an SD card, not the SSD.  If it blows up, I can just remove the SD card and be back in Intrepid.  That's part of the beauty of flash memory, and especially SD cards - they're cheap and everything is pretty much reversible.  I also have Intrepid on another card, so I could theoretically install Jaunty to my SSD and be able to revert via the SD, but installing to an SD card makes more sense to me.  I would never install an OS in the alpha stage on an computer I relied on for production, but it's fun to try out new stuff when it's not affecting anything critical.  If you don't have an SD slot, get a cheap USB drive and install to that.  You can try it out and just unplug it if things go south.

That's the problem isn't it. It's not funny it just shows that you have absolutely no idea of development. That's what makes people say 'Yeah go ahead go alpha runs great on my machine.'
