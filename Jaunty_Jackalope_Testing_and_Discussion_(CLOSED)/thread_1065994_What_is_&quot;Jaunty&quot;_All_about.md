### Post by kestal on 2009-02-10
Jaunty seems to have made a lot of improvements. I like them a lot. For testing purposes it seems to have a solid base ready for improvements prior to release.

I noticed that ext4 is faster on my system and boot times are good. The new advances in nvidia/ati drivers seem to be starting to catch up [COLOR="DimGray"](though, if your not careful, you can easily just bork your current build)[/COLOR] and Alpha 4 was much better than Alpha 3 [COLOR="DimGray"](at least on my box, where it gave me many system beeps).[/COLOR]

Is Jaunty going to be considered anything close to 'mainstream'? or would it still be more so considered for those with a prior and convenient amount of programming/terminal experience? Don't get me wrong, the terminal gives me a thrill of power over my system tht I never experienced in Windows.

In the last 2 releases of Hardy/Intrepid, I have noticed things being worked on, but then in others areas I did not.

- Default Theme/Icons
- Seamless Boot
- Included alternative boot logins which crash every system I tested them on.
- Screen Savers from the 90's
- A Wallpaper folder that isn't even connected to the wallpaper selection UI (and mind you, although I know the space pictures are sentimental, its a bad selection for a linux desktop anyway)

Now, I know, Intrepid had the dark theme included, but it wasn't default. Ontop of that, though it was well done, I am glad that it wasn't default. However, shouldn't Ubuntu, if they are focusing on being released on laptops for the general public (with no prior knowledge to what Linux can mean to them) also try to focus on the default visual effects?

With this said, do NOT start flaming me about being a Windows fanboy or any of that. I am just saying that Linux is easily overlooked because of the simple and basic things you'd expect to see from a modern OS. Its icons and theme does look like it comes from somewhere in the 90's while its GDM and loading menu seem very rigid.

Mind you, Ubuntu has done great things and is sincerely one of the best operating systems out there, but while working on those advanced features and backends, it also seemed to forget about the basics.

I mean, isn't Ubuntu supposed to be a 'newbie-friendly' Linux distribution, among other things? Its sad that facebrowser wont be in for 9.04 release, and its a shame about Plymouth. (would have been nice additions). I know it has Compiz like all the others, and that is great: I often impress many people I know with that.

If I knew exactly where to go and that my voice would be heard to change these UI's and default theme, I would. As I have a lot of graphics design experience and have developed programs with sleek UI's before; however, it seems that all the art themes and icon themes that were uploaded and brought up for consideration were not really considered much at all, because nothing was really uploaded or changed.

I am not saying for the development team to lower themselves to Windows Standards [COLOR="DimGray"](ie: the perception of a quick boot up even though things keep loading after the desktop is up)[/COLOR], but the default themes/icons do look like any other linux distro out there. Shouldn't Ubuntu, as one of the Linux OS leaders, try to stand out more than just the current default gnome theme and icon set?

Anyway, before any of you go all defensive and counter-attack all my points with an almost limitless amount of fanboyism, just remember that I do have valid points. Just because YOU dont care about the UI or how it is perceived does not mean that any Joe Shmoe on the street will want an OS with icons that look like they belong to step up from Windows 95.

[/rant]
