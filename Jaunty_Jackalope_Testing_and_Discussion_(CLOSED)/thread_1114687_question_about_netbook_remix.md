### Post by bennachie on 2009-04-03
> **pspsampsp said:**
> does the netbook remix differ from normal ubuntu or can i use it to install ubuntu on any pc or laptop that supports booting from usb?

I can't offer a definite answer, but the essential framework of the remix is clearly very similar to that of any other Ubuntu version (as set out in the blurb at [http://www.canonical.com/projects/ubuntu/unr](http://www.canonical.com/projects/ubuntu/unr)), so the odds would be in your favour. The NBR GUI works well on small screens, but might be a bit tiresome at a larger scale, and there would seem to be little point in using the remix on anything other than a pretty basic system. Note, however, that you need at least 512MB of RAM.

Since straight Ubuntu Jaunty works very well indeed on my own eeePC 4G (with 1GB of RAM, but otherwise standard) and on several other computers around my place with varying ages and capacities, I'm not sure that I can see much point in NBR from the perspective of a computing enthusiast.

Canonical, however, probably have their corporate eyes on a series of downstream commercial applications that might be addressed by a - no doubt heavily disguised - netbook. Indeed, such devices could provide an almost perfect low-cost, low-power-consumption solution to a range of common problems (note the reference at the above URL to point-of-sale operations). I can only wish them well. Anything that helps to support the continuing development of an already very useful and attractive OS deserves our support.
