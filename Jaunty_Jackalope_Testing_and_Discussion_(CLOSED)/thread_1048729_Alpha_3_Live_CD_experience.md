### Post by Yashiro on 2009-01-23
I became a fan of Ubuntu around 8.04.
Had alot of problems setting up my ATI4850 but after that was fixed, it's been OK.
I still run Hardy and it's good.

I'm not much into testing pre-release software in my free time. 
I have done it as a profession for years and really don't want to put myself through the ordeal at home too. 

Anyway, being ill recently left me at home with nothing to do I decided to download various distributions.
Basically to see what the state of play is in 2009.

I'll not try and get into technicalities. This is just list of what I encountered while I was pretending to be a noob to the distro.

[SIZE="1"]The Good
- The distro booted.
- The LiveCD install was fast compared to others I have tried.
- Detected my sound card. (SB Audigy ZS Pro, external box still doesn't work though)[/SIZE]

[SIZE="1"]The Bad
- It boots into a plain desktop. That's it. No Welcome notes. No mention of 'What's new'.
- The wallpaper is very ugly.
- LCD Font smoothing is not enabled.
- Font is OK, if a little big.
- It still cannot detect Radeon cards properly. Jockey of no use.
- What IS new or cool in Jaunty? Nothing detectable to me really. This was really disappointing.[/SIZE]

Mostly trivial issues to me, and most nerds. But to a new user?
The stark uselessness of this bleak install was somewhat off-putting. As was the lack of cool stuff to play with.

There could be:

[SIZE="1"]- more shortcuts on the desktop?
. to change my wallpaper, 
. to enhance my fonts, 
. launch a device profiler/manager as seen on OpenSolaris.

- a setup panel/wizard to do all the above and more
. The panel approach is a bit microsofty I guess, but most switchers will be Windows users.[/SIZE]

[SIZE="1"]- more cool stuff!
. better power manager interface and function
. a new display control panel 
. display driver install that works and explains what its doing
. a less cryptic network manager
. remove some cruft apps
. gnome schedule with a cronjob setup wizard?
. a full system backup for nerds/server
. a time machine clone for users
. fix the management of smb shares! it's too hard for non nerds.
. actual working webcams
. non flickering video under compiz
. bootloader management and recovery via a gui
. universal mouse panel (like btnx), assign buttons, better control of mouse speeds
. wacom/touch improvements
. Iphone/pod connectivity improvements
. DNLA server for non nerds so they can share media in the home[/SIZE]

Ubuntu is rapidly gaining momentum and more users. As such it has the opportunity innovate.
The Jaunty install experience left me pretty flat.I was sad that it's become nothing more than another distro. Maybe I was expecting too much.
