### Post by RAOF on 2009-04-16
Yes, mostly.  It's almost exactly the same as running Windows on bare hardware - that's kinda the point of decent virtualisation systems.  Depending on how Virtualbox handles networking you might need a firewall slightly less, but apart from that, it's regular XP in every way.

Barring serious VM exploits (or sharing drives), your XP VM won't affect your Ubuntu install, though.
