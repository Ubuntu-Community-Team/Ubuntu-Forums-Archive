### Post by Nullack on 2009-02-19
I had another machine in for awhile so I figured I'd have a look at the Fedora 11 alpha. Generally it made me appreciate how I feel Ubuntu is a stronger desktop OS. Thinking it through the only real advantage I can give Fedora from my point of view is better security with a fairly tight selinux profile. The negatives:

* RPMS seem to take a long time to install. Certainly much slower than apt. Installs and updates take too long.
* General usability hassles. Like I elect to do an upgrade, and all the upgrades fiddle around for ages with the poor performance of RPMs, then it finally says, oh, due to an unmet dependency Im aborting the whole upgrade. With Debian and Ubuntu, it would hold back problem updates by default but still do the OK ones. You have to manually specify to not abort the whole thing, as if you now in advance that it will fail.
* The lack of a hardware drivers like applet just adds hassle. As does, having to add an external third party repo just so I can have a working opengl video driver.
* Silly decisions that make the experience non intuitive. Like, they release an alpha, and yet on their Alpha sources there is a choice between Fedora 10.91 or Rawhide without any real explanation of what repo is what. I found out later that the 10.91 is just an image of packages at the time the Alpha was released. For testing purposes you'd think the only repos worth having in the default list would be rawhide.

In the end I was pleased to learn a bit about Fedora but more so, I was pleased to understand the inherent wisdom behind Debian/Ubuntu.
