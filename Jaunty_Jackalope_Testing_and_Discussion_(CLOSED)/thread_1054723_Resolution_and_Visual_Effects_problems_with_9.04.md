### Post by Ng Oon-Ee on 2009-01-30
Alphas of any software are not recommended for beginners. Even betas should be steered clear off, unless you like the challenge of figuring out exactly where the bugs are coming from.

Please try installing 8.10, if graphics are still low-res you may want to take a look at various guides for NVidia/ATI/intel depending on what your graphics card is.

### Post by Spr0k3t on 2009-01-30
First time Linux users should generally use a standard release or long term release.  The video display will be as you have described if graphics drivers are not installed for your specific card.  I don't know which graphics chipset you have, otherwise I could point you in a direction to fix the issue.

For the time being... pull down a torrent of one of the latest stable releases, and see if that fixes the majority of your problems you might be having.  As it stands, 9.04 has at least two months before I would recommend it for daily use.

I do hope you find the answer to the issues.  If not, feel free to ask for help.
