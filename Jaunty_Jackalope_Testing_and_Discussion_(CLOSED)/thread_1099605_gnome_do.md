### Post by scaine on 2009-03-18
It also pre-populates it's icons by default, so when you first start it, it "guesses" what you use, but over time, if you keep running certain apps, it'll add icons to them.  Or, even if you start something using ALT-F2, or a terminal, you can just drag the icon of the running app onto docky to resize.

It's also very intuitive (compared to Cairo-dock, that's for sure).  You resize by dragging, add apps by using/"Do"ing/dragging, remove apps by dragging.

Hell, you can even just drag to resize left/right and it'll add guessed apps to fill out the space.  Plus, you get all the awesomeness of do right there at your fingertips - stock quotes, google calendar lookups, music player control, etc.

I know, I sound like an advert for Do, but you really have to try it believe how useful it is.

Developer blog entry : [http://jassmith.wordpress.com/2009/03/13/gnome-do-081-released/](http://jassmith.wordpress.com/2009/03/13/gnome-do-081-released/)
