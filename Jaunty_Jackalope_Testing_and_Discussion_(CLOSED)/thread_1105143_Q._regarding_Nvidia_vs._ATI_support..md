### Post by RAOF on 2009-03-24
> **Nullack said:**
> ...
Who cares if AMD released doco - both companies dont have anything close to a good FOSS driver for their gpus.
...

This is a bit disingenuous - nvidia doesn't really have an open-source driver *at all, for any card*[1], while many old AMD/ATi cards have fully-featured open-source drivers, and the newer r600/r700 cards are getting support right now.

The open-source radeon drivers have traditionally had slightly worse 3d performance than fglrx, and sometimes fewer features.  That said, the closed-source drivers don't necessarily have all the features of the open-source drivers, either.  For example, the nvidia binary doesn't support xrandr 1.2, which I really miss, and nouveau's 2d performance, particularly XVideo, is better & less buggy for me.

All three of the major players - Intel, AMD, nVidia - have HD video decode hardware and support some form of API in their drivers.  The main difference here is that mplayer has a patch which uses nVidia's API.  Eventually, one (or more) APIs will be widely supported, and you won't have to care.

It's true that the nvidia binary blob is generally better than fglrx (again, not always - I think fglrx supports xrandr 1.2 now, which is nice).

[1] The nv driver, while open-source, is obfuscated and doesn't really accept patches from anyone outside nvidia - this is the reason why the nouveau driver exists at all.  The nv driver also doesn't intend to support 3d at all, and is generally feature-anaemic.
