### Post by scheuri on 2009-01-21
Stop it!
It makes me sick!!

And I am not talking about the linux-windows discussion (because that does not bother me at all), I am talking about the "when I use alpha can you assure me that it will only have some bumps and nothing major".

FIRST RULE for computer science:
Never ever use an alpha version on a computer which is not intended to be for test purposes only and is not allowed to stop working longer than just a couple of minutes or hours.
Expect it to break EVERYTHING and render your computer useless.

An alpha release is ALWAYS buggy and will always have bumps. And it will always have MAJOR issues!
Even if it turns out it does not have such things as major issues, you have to assume it does! Every time!

So, no one in this forum will promise you that it will only have some minor things which will be fixed with patches (whenever they are available).

It is on your own risk that you use an alpha release which is intended to be used by people knowing this and who want to TEST the system (and maybe even contribute with bug reports or fixes).

Wait until you have at least beta (however, even then there is no such thing as garantuee it works properly without major issues).
You are surely better off waiting until release...chances are very good it will have only minor issues by then which will be fixed with patches.

Sorry...but I see soooo many posts like yours and those people tend to come bank whining* about a broken system after using alpha or beta.
