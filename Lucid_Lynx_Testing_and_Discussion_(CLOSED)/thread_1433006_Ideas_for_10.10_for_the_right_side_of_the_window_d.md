I thought people might be interested in this considering it would change the way we work with Ubuntu considerably.  The idea showed up in the Ayatana mailing list as a way to utilize the space on the right side of the window border. ### Post by Chenzo on 2010-03-30
In my opinion, the *idea* is good, but the suggested gestures are mostly useless; we can already maximize by double-clicking, and we can already close by clicking a button or using compiz scale. We can also move windows to new workspaces simply by dragging them to the edge of the screen. We should think of features that we want to do, but that we cannot currently do easily. 

However, there are some options for useful gestures here. for example:
-middle-clicking the sphere closes the window (just like tabs in firefox)
-Moving the sphere right or left does a right-maximize or left-maximize (similar to aero snap or compiz grid)
-Dragging on top of another application's sphere 'tabs' both windows on the title bar ([similar to KDE 4.4]("http://www.freetechie.com/blog/kde-4-4-beta-1-tabbed-windows-review-opensuse/"))
-scrolling on top of the sphere grows or shrinks the window proportionately (it can either re-size the window or actually scale it down for un-cluttering the desktop purposes) 

These last three actions that I would love to do, but currently cannot do (as) easily. For this reason, I am voting 'other'; even though there is no option for other yet.
