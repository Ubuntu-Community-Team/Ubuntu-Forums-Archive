Abiword and Gnumeric are perfectly fine applications, however they fall quite short of OO.org.  For starters, OO.org contains not just a word processor and spreadsheet, but also presentation program, database, formula application, drawing application, and more, all together in one package.  Not including OO.org means you lose all that functionality.

Also, nearly all Linux desktop distros, including Ubuntu, use OO.org as their primary office suite.  So someone using Ubuntu on her desktop is used to OO.org, then gets a netbook with Abiword and Gnumeric??

This seems like a big mistake to me-- the same office suite should be standard across all flavors of Ubuntu.
