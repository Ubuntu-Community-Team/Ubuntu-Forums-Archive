The application indicator spec specifies that applications should expose their indicator facilities through D-BUS through a specific transport protocol library (dbusmenu), and libappindicator is provided as a convenient way for applications to provide that information over D-BUS. The main benefit of the work is standardizing the way this information is exposed and treated, not necessarily drawing it as, say, GNOME 2.x menus and widgets, the way they are now (that's what the indicator applet does).

With GNOME Shell or other future technology adopting the spec, the work already done in this department would continue to be useful within a more "modern" environment.

On Mac OS X, the left side works, because 1) the close button is on the outside 2) window menus are separated into the universal toolbar and 3) applications generally don't quit when you close the last window of the application.
