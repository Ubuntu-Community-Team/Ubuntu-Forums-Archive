### Post by ALIENDUDE5300 on 2010-03-09
The only thing that really bothers me (other than the title bar buttons being on the left) about the new theme is the non-standard ordering of the title bar buttons. Instead of the (almost always standard) Minimize, Maximize, Close, or on Mac, Close, Minimize, and then Maximize, we have an unusual order of Maximize, Minimize, Close. There is no real reason for this, and it's ridiculously easy to change, but it breaks most existing themes, and it's EXTREMELY confusing on theme like "Dust Sand" where the buttons are not labeled at all, and they don't have icons, or even different colors when you hover over the buttons. Also, speaking of hovering effects, we have none in either of the new themes. It adds an _excellent_ touch when the buttons light up or show some visual indication that you are hovering over them. In addition to that, it's almost *necessary* to have some kind of visual indication when you hover over the buttons if we leave them on the left, as I explained in an earlier post, it's easy to accidentally click on, for example, the close button, when intending to hit the Edit menu. A visual indication similar to what is on almost every other theme would at least slightly reduce the chances of accidentally clicking one of the buttons. I really do feel that the buttons belong on the right side of the title bar, and so does the majority of Ubuntu users...

Some polls about title bar button location:
[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2010/03/poll-do-you-want-ubuntu-window-controls.html](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2010/03/poll-do-you-want-ubuntu-window-controls.html)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1422422](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1422422)

At the time of writing, in both of these polls, more than 75% of voters prefer the title bar buttons to be on the right of the title bar.

If Canonical does decide to keep the icons on the left side of the title bar, please at least move the close button to the right side of the title bar. This button is the only "dangerous" one of the three, and, from a functionality perspective, it actually matters if you accidentally bump the close button and lose important work or progress in an application. It's much harder to accidentally bump the close button if it is on the right side of the title bar. Also, if Canonical leaves the buttons on the left, it would be great to have an easy way to change them back to the right. :)

Edit: In addition to the above suggestions, I would also like to request that the text be in the center of the title bar, similar to in themes such as New Wave. This doesn't matter that much to me, but it would be nice to have. It's also more similar to Mac OS X, as that seems to be what Canonical is trying to copy from... ;)
