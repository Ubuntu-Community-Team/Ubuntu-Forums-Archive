### Post by Madspyman on 2010-03-02
Ubuntu will be switching to Yahoo as it's default search engine for Firefox in the upcoming Lucid Lynx release. While it's no big deal for those who will switch the default back to Google, they're are many who will just keep using Yahoo. 

Depending on who you trust your information with, this could be considered a security risk.  

"On July 29, 2009, it was announced with a 10 year deal that Microsoft will have full access to Yahoo search engine to be used in Microsoft future projects for its search engine Bing." - Wikipedia 

"Microsoft and Yahoo were sharing detailed information about their customers before 2008. This should be a red flag for Ubuntu about handing over their users to Microsoft via Yahoo. If things were that bad two years ago while Yahoo was fighting the take over, imagine how bad they are now." - Boycott Novell

Sounds to me like Microsoft has access to most of Yahoo's search data, this most likely includes the user information Yahoo stores to aid in search results. All of this used to help improve Bing, so it'll most likely find it's way to Microsoft's servers.

Whether or whether or not you consider this a security risk is up to you, I just feel it necessary to point this out to those who switched to Ubuntu to avoid the politics of a Microsoft.
