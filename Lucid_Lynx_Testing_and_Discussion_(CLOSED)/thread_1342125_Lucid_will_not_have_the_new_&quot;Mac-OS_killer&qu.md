### Post by davisouzarj on 2009-11-30
I have just read today, in the Michael Forrest's Ubuntu Blog, that no major change in the theme and/or gnome desktop is in the Lucid release schedule. In other words, Lucid will be pretty similar to Karmic, at least in the appearance. For those who don't know Michael, he is the "Canonical Design Team GUI Prototyping Guy".

Just to remember, in July 2008 Mark Shuttleworth made a promise that "Ubuntu would be more attractive than Mac OS X within 2 years". Well, when Lucid is released, 20 months of these 2 years will be past. 

I know many people believe that Canonical should forget this "silly appearance discussion", and focus on the improvement of the system stability and reliability.

On the other hand, I am really convinced that break promises like that is very bad marketing, when we talk about revert bug #1, and also in many ways a disrespect to the community.

For me, until now, Karmic is being a regression when compared to Jaunty, mainly considering mobile internet support. But a more-of-the-same desktop for Lucid, for me, is a major and extremely important regression. 

I am not an Ubuntu developer, but considering me as part of the Ubuntu community, I would like to propose that the lack of a new "OS X Killer" desktop theme for Lucid is a potential major bug, and a serious regression, that should have its importance marked as "high". Otherwise, the consequence could be the lost of our confidence in Canonical, and in the seriousness of the Ubuntu Project.

I would really like to know if I am the only one here that thinks like that, or if there are others there those think the same.

Rgds,
Davi.
