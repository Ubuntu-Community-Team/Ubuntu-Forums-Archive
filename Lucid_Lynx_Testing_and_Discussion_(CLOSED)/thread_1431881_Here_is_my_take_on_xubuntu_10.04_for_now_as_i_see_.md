### Post by Calmor on 2010-03-17
Is that seventeen years of linux knowledge or seventeen years of Windows knowledge?  The two are not really equal.

Several of your comments point to the latter.  No networking tools?  NetworkManager, for starters, is pretty good.  There are alternatives (names escape me because NM works for what I need to do).  Look through synaptic for network and your head will spin... what are you trying to do that isn't working?

As for printers - you need to configure Samba correctly.  I haven't had much experience in Xubuntu, or anything past 9.10, but it wasn't that difficult.  It did require a little manual configuration (editing a text file), but I've been able to do it.  The fact that HP doesn't support networking my printer (on Windows machines only) is a different story.  Depending on the printer model, even if your Windows machine can see it, it might be impossible to print to anyway.
