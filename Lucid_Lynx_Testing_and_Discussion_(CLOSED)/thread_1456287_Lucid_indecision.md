### Post by bennachie on 2010-04-17
Kubuntu Lucid is coming along well. The KDE 4.4 variant seems pretty stable and capable. While the resources allocated to the project have not allowed the team to match the increasingly elegant artwork incorporated in Ubuntu, the things that really matter are now working much better than in previous iterations. The development team deserves some serious congratulations - until now, I have always seen openSUSE as the gold standard of KDE distributions, but Kubuntu is getting better every time.

To pick a few nits, Kmail seems to have been a bit troublesome for some reason, and has always had a few strange omissions (no easy "backup and restore" mechanism being the most obvious). However, Thunderbird 3.0.4 runs very well, and looks good. The Firefox installation process is now properly integrated with KDE, which is great - I know that Konqueror has its fans, but you wouldn't want to rely upon it for all purposes.

More seriously, I don't really understand why KNetworkManager always seems to lag a generation or so behind its Gnome counterpart. If you plan to use mobile broadband in more than one country and/or with more than one carrier, you'd definitely be better off staying with Ubuntu or, if KDE is a must, transferring to Mandriva.
