### Post by Robin Nixon on 2010-03-09
If Ubuntu really wanted to be ground-breaking it could take a much bolder step than simply moving some buttons about, which has ended up just being an annoyance to over 3/4 of its users (as per the poll on this site). But a bold and new strategy would, I think, be accepted much more favourably.

Something like, I don't know, maybe dropping the maximize button (because you can always double-click the title bar), and combining the close and minimize buttons into a single button (because they are similar: one makes the window go away to the bottom, the other makes it go away completely).

That way there'd be only a single button remaining, which could minimize the window if left clicked, or close it if right clicked. How about that? From three buttons down to one - Now that *would* be a bold move.

Maybe it would work, maybe it wouldn't, but I think that some inventiveness in that arena would earn Ubuntu some brownie points, especially when so many people are demonstrating that they are prepared to learn new hand gestures for touch devices.

You see, people *are* prepared to learn new ways of doing things, but only really take to it if they perceive it to be an improvement.

If Ubuntu made such a move it would then be seen as the leader, not the copier (of OS X) that this button moving episode makes it appear to be.
