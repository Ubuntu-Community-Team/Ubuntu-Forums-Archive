### Post by Rob2687 on 2010-03-14
> **rottentree said:**
> So what's the point in making it more similar to OS X?
OMG free Mac! Get it while you can ?

It all goes back to the early days of OSX. When it first came out the GUI was lightyears ahead of it's competitors. Not only was the interface polished from the top down but it also had sexy graphics accelerated desktop compositing... and so the obsession with cloning the OSX look began.

People wanted to duplicate many components of the OSX look with things like the brushed metal or aqua look to the dock and genie effect or expose. With each successive release Apple continued to refine and update the look while Windows and Linux remained virtually the same for many years. People were craving something new, different and better. Thus perpetuating the obsession with copying the looks and functionality of OSX.

Now Windows and Linux have finally caught up with the fancy desktop compositing, desktop widgets, docks and what not that don't look like they came from 1992... and so the next step is now to create a look good enough for people to drool over without people crying that it's a copy of OSX.

The problem is that OSX was really the first OS in recent times to really impress people with it's interface. Sure there are indviduals who will reply to this and say that they never saw what was so great about it but you can't change the fact that it did have a certain wow factor visually that put it on the map. Just look at Gnome-Look.org. That piece of crap OSX Aqua theme still reigns king at 1,617,424 downloads. The next one down with half a million downloads is yet another OSX theme.

It set the bar for certain aspects of a modern desktop OS interms of the interface, the look and it's functionality. Especially for the joe user market so it's inevitable that people will be making comparisons back and forth.
