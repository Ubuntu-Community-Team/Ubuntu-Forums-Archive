### Post by andrewabc on 2010-02-25
Others are correct.

However for people who installed early in development, there are most likely left over files and stuff that got changed. Not a big problem, although a clean install when final is out will make sure everything is as it should be (you don't have to though).

I remember when I was testing Hardy, there were like 10 installed kernels taking up 1gb of hard drive space. I only need 1 (final version). Which of course you can manually remove them. Not sure if they now auto remove old kernels. Each kernel I think takes 170mb.

Search synaptic and see how many different versions are installed.
