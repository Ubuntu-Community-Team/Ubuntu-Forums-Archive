Phill.

The alpha is on 2.6.33:

[http://koji.fedoraproject.org/koji/buildinfo?buildID=160238](http://koji.fedoraproject.org/koji/buildinfo?buildID=160238)

It is though very likely that the kernel will be upgraded at some point during the F13 lifetime to .34, the kernel maintainers normally do this to keep the bugcount under control and to add new support.

Fedora doesn't do long term support (or support at all), a release is maintained rather than supported for 2 cycles + 1 month, meaning 13 months. If you want longer term maintained release CentOS is the place for you.

There is also a team of volunteers working on keeping at least certain releases alive longer than 13 months, but doing this on volunteer power is hard work, and ungrateful work at that.
