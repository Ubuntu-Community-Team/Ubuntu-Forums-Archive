> This is an update on the [New  Ubuntu Theme(s), Boot Splash, Logo Revealed (And More!)]("http://www.webupd8.org/2010/03/new-ubuntu-1004-theme-and-logo-revealed.html") post which  I'm sure will make many people happy.

Dustin Kirkland, a Core  Developer of the Ubuntu Server for Canonical [posted]("http://blog.dustinkirkland.com/2010/03/ubuntus-new-themes-and-byobu.html")  some screenshots with Byobu and the new Ubuntu Light and Dark themes.

So  what's so interesting about those screenshots? Well, the window buttons  are in the top right corner:


[CENTER][[IMG]http://lh4.ggpht.com/_1QSDkzYY2vc/S4-L1L1gIgI/AAAAAAAAAcw/nUb9cqUkO5w/s400/light.png[/IMG]]("http://lh4.ggpht.com/_1QSDkzYY2vc/S4-L1L1gIgI/AAAAAAAAAcw/nUb9cqUkO5w/light.png")

[/CENTER]


As you can see, the window controls are  placed on the top right corner by default.


I'm not sure what  was the reason to place the window buttons on the left in the initial  screenshots, but it seems that was something custom for those  screenshots only. This is definitely good news for most people who saw  the initial screenshots (since 90% of the comments were arguing about  the window buttons placement).

And a final note: I must say it's a  bit of lack in professionalism to publish such custom screenshots with a  lot of non-default elements (besides the window buttons, there is also  no bottom panel - that is also something customized for those  screenshots only).
