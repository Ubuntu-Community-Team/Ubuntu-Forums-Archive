### Post by Sophont on 2010-03-10
Yeah - right.

We don't (usually) pay a cent for a rich system with thousands of free apps, which gets regular major updates every few months.

There's 2 kinds of devs. One is paid by interested companies (IBM, Sun, Oracle, Red Hat, etc...) who will work on what is important to them (the company and their clients).
The rest is volunteers investing their free time to work on programs that we get for free and they rarely even get a thanks from us.

Stuff like "less chatter, more debugging" sounds very ignorant and ungrateful to me.

Forum reps? What's the plan here? Giving orders to people who donate their free time to work on apps? If there is something that's very important to you - write a bug report or find the appropriate mailing list and make a polite suggestion. If you can't be bothered to invest a few minutes to do either then it wasn't that important after all.

You don't have time to do proper bug reports - no worries - somebody else will likely do so sooner or later - the price you pay then is patience.

Every OS has it's price.

Windows costs money, has lots of malware, is unfree and got plenty of DRM and other restrictions.

Mac OSX is even more expensive, much less malware, other mix of restrictions and bound to a limited set of hardware.

Linux is mostly free, has very few restrictions - but lacks good high end gaming support and some features you still have to wait for.
There's always a price - take your pick.

We already have "representatives" with a "direct link" to the devs. These are the people who bother to write useful bug reports.
