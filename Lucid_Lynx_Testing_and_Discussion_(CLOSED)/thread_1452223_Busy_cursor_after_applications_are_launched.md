or are people increasingly realizing it as a badly designed and/or implemented "feature"?

I mean the wacky behavior when you start an app, or the app starts doing something and the mouse becomes a "waiting" cursor and problem is - it stays so not only for this app's window but also for the whole desktop (with which it certainly has nothing to do!) and sometimes affecting other windows' cursors too, confusing the user into thinking he needs to stop working with the computer until the cursor becomes normal.
Second "weirdity" (or "wackity") is that the cursor stays in "wait" state even **after** the app already started and ready to use. If you don't know what I mean please don't bother. For the rest - do you think this will ever be fixed (removed) or is it still viewed as a virtue, or, just fend this issue off by saying that it's the particular app's fault?
