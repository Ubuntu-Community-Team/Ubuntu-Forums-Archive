### Post by samh785 on 2010-03-15
Today while at my girlfriend's house, I overheard her mother talking about how she was having troubles with her iPod. I asked her what the problem was, and apparently her son (in college with a mac) put about 800 songs on an iPod nano (3rd Gen.) and sent it to her so that she and her husband could hook it up to the speakers and play it when they don't feel like watching TV and nothing on the radio is any good. The trouble arises when she plugs it into her windows computer and it tells her that it needs to be reformatted so that she can use it with the windows version of iTunes. This would of course erase all the songs on the iPod and defeat the purpose of the whole ordeal. I told her that if I take it home, I might be able to bypass it on my computer and get her songs for her. She gives me the iPod and I take it home. I plugged it into my new install of Lucid Lynx (I'm not sure of the native compatibility of this model of iPod on previous versions of Ubuntu) and it instantly shows up on the desktop and asks me if I want to open it in Rhythmbox. I just wanted to share with you all how absolutely perfectly this all went for me, and how much I am totally loving my OS of choice right now.
