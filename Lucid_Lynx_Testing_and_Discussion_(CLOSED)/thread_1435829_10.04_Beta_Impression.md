### Post by Psumi on 2010-03-21
For starters, I love the quick boot time, it's very pleasing to see it be so fast even on my IBM T41 laptop.

Next, I would like to point out... mini.iso still installs plymouth, people said it wouldn't, but apparently has.

furthermore, I'd like to point out that everything works, and works very well. One thing I noticed, is that alsa will no longer grab the microphone input when capture is set to mix (In karmic, it did this for some strange reason.) Another thing I noticed is that pidgin is the newest version, making the settings dialog look much nicer than it ever was.

Also noted is the quick response time with gtk-recordmydesktop:

-- Karmic --
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzLNK8h9f0U](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzLNK8h9f0U)

-- Lucid --
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xROONapdNAU](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xROONapdNAU)

In lucid, I also show htop. In Karmic, recordmydesktop would take 100+% of the CPU even with the two cpu-lowering options on. In Lucid, as shown, it only takes 2 to 10 percent.

Flash still takes up half the CPU depending on what I am playing though... would love to see this fixed in the future (if possible.)

My camera works with lucid just as it did in karmic, so that's another plus.

In all, lucid is a lot better than Karmic. I haven't seen any of the new rebranding, because I use a mini.iso xfce install, gnome-icon-theme, default xfwm theme, etc. I don't mind dull looks though. as long as everything works!

Good jorb!!
