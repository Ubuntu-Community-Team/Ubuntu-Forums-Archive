### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2010-03-17
> **floborg said:**
> Everybody's forgetting something...sound themes are broken in Karmic.  See [_[COLOR="Blue"]Bug #324700[/COLOR]_]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/gnome-media/+bug/324700").

They are not broken. [Sound themes]("http://www.freedesktop.org/wiki/Specifications/sound-theme-spec") are just built (customized) like icon themes; in /usr/share/sounds with index.theme files, so you can choose the overall theme you want to use.

What is missing for you is the GUI to easily change every sound individual. I, for one, am not terribly upset it is gone; this should encourage more cohesive alternative sound sets to emerge, as we see with icons. (Granted, it hasn't happened, probably because it's more difficult to distribute these things than icon themes).

It's good, they took a startup theme from another pack and made it more echoy, which brings me to one thing I have to mention.

Windows 6/7 (Vista/7) both add reverberation to all desktop sounds unless you turn it off.  This can sometimes be really annoying and should most likely just be limited to button and other sounds, but it does help do one thing which is to take the edge off everything.  The whole reason for fade-in/fade-out I think is that it sounds nicer to remove hard edges.  Some might say they like certain things to retain their edge and I would agree, but I feel sound themes which aren't so harsh will be more popular.  That's my main complaint about Ubuntu's default sound theme.  Yeah, of course drums are harsh usually, but do you really want your *desktop* to be harsh too is I feel a legitimate question.

I would bet a more relaxing desktop experience would be the most desirable, and so feel themes like these are a little more in the right direction.  Perhaps this theme's reverb is a little too long but I would take it over Ubuntu's harsh default.  The crackles in the welcome sound need to be cleaned out of it though.
