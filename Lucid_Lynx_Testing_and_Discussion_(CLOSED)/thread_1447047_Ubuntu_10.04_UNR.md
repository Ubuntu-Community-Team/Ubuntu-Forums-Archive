### Post by strobe on 2010-04-05
You could have kept the Netbook UI and used the 2d version and you can add panels as well as stuff to the panel.  And (at least on my laptop) luckily for you, the Netbook-launcher crashes randomly so it won't even get in your way.  Or, you can just add the Ubuntu Desktop through Synaptic located in the Meta packages.  That should allow you to use and login with the gnome desktop and will give you the "normal" panels.  
 
I too have tested out the Netbook version of Lucid and it seems even more restrictive for a smaller screen than it is a fix.  No multiple workspaces and the "name bar" uses all the real estate.  Tiny screens need more options to configure... not less, imo.  And removing the workspaces is a big loss for a screen that only has limited area.
