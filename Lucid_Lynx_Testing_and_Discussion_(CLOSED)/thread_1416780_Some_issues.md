### Post by marcopolo1981 on 2010-02-26
Upon installation, it automatically installs nVidia Riva/TNT/GeForce driver, which is better than the low graphics you normally get. But they aren't exactly brilliant and offer no effects. Unfortunately, the latest nVidia drivers that Jockey has to offer are version 173. Considering that the latest stable drivers from nVidia are 190.53, this doesn't quite add up. The 173 drivers are over 12 months old. This wouldn't be quite so bad if it would allow me to install the latest version from nVidia's website. But it gives me an error saying that the error sometimes occurs if they have been compiled against the wrong kernel. I have no idea what that means, only I ended up reinstalling the 173 version again.

Then there are the random log-outs. I can be happily surfing the web, then all of a sudden, I am logged out and have to re-enter my password. There are no warnings and I am not doing anything to warrant a crash. 

The Network Manager applet is not showing any signal, even though it says full when I click on it.

There are the strange half-coloured blocks that appear when logging in that obviously are a glitch somewhere.

Sometimes - not always - I control-alt-f1 and get nothing but a flashing underscore. Then I go through f1 to f6 to try and get a working prompt, but find none. Only by restarting do I get one to work.

These are quite minor for a alpha flavour. I like it a lot, and these are nothing more than annoying, but I wanted to bring them to somebody's attention for the final release. Obviously, there are going to be hiccups at this stage, and I am not flaming it! This is not a production machine and I know the risks involved. 

If anyone wants to try and fix some of these issues, I would gladly test them on my laptop for the developers.

I have only had it installed a couple of hours, and I am bound to find more issues.
