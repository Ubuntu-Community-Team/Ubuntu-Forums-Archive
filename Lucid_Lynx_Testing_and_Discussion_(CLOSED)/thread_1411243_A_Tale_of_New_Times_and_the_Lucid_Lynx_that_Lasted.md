### Post by christia on 2010-02-19
Hello 

I just wanted to share my Ubuntu experience, and make a few statements on what i think Ubuntu has accomplished and what it should accomplish.

I bought a Sony Vaio Z in Jan09. It came with Vista - even though i never really understood why, I hated Vista as everybody else did. I felt like trying something new - and was told about Ubuntu.

I am no tech-wiz, nor am I a tech-idiot - more about this later. I like fuddling around with hardware/software, but don't have much time (working 16 hours a day in a top investment bank). In a new operating system i wanted something that gave me stability, but more important i wanted something that worked. I hate having to find weird drivers, and I hate using unintuitive systems.

I installed Jaunty Jackalope. To my big surprise, everything went smooth. Everything worked, and everything went quick. 

BUT - i quickly ran into "trouble". The "FN" keys on my computer didn't work. I couldt adjust sound/brightness with the click of a button. Also, my "Stamina/Speed" button didn't work, so i Couldn't use the HDMI output. Last, i found it a challenge to download and install a theme - i had to google how to do it, and even then i found it strangely unintuitive. Yes, every new thing comes with a prize, but Windows got a huge plus in my book as i didnt have to learn anything new. So, when windows 7 release candidate was offered for free, i jumped on that ship - not because i didn't want to give ubuntu more of a chance, but i was in a place in my life where i didn't feel like "wasting" time on this (was graduating, and then moving to first london, then new york, then london again, all for 2 months at a time, and then finally to hong kong). However, my W7 didn't make the FN buttons work - i had to install weird drivers and dig on sony's webpage. but at least everything was as it used to be, and the only challenge was to find the right drivers.

As my W7 was coming to an end, and as i, when i bought my Vaio, bought the cheapest HDD i could get to change to an SSD later, i did the following:

1) i bought an SSD (Intel X25-M - can HIGHLY recommend this)
2) i cracked my Vaio open, and installed it
3) i put in a cd with Ubuntu Lucid Lynx, and turned it on

Why lucid lynx for such an Ubuntu noob as me? well, i ahve always been a sucker for newest versions, plus my experience with alpha/beta versions are normally good.

The computer turned on, i installed ubuntu and sat down. But something weird happened.

All my FN buttons now worked - so here Ubuntu was even MORE "plug and play" than W7??? now, i don't understand much about deep hardware issues - maybe the drivers i installed back then were somehow saved in the BIOS or something, but in case Ubuntu actually was able to find these themselves, this was a huge plus. I have now installed all the stuff i use - Skype, Citrix client for doing office work from home, and my email etc. everything went extremely easy! If Ubuntu keeps going like this, i will never ever change. Only minus is that i still find it hard to install all that "theme" crap (maybe there is a problem with themes for 9.04 when running 10.04? i dont know).

What i want from an operating system is:

1) Plug and play - i want to be able to put in the disk, turn on the computer, and have everything working, just like windows. Have often read "things dont work for ubuntu because programmers are lazy and haven't made the programs smart" - might very well be, but as a user, i don't care. same reason i used IE until very recently, as i had problems with many work related webpages (and still have in firefox, but now tolerably)

2) slickness - i don't use many advanced applications, so want something that is stable, and runs FAST (which is why i bought the X25-M), and looks GOOD. 

3) user friendliness. i like having ONE program that plays videos, ONE program that plays music (happy to combine the two) etc. I HATE having to download codec/new players etc to watch a movie

4) cost-efficient - well, ubuntu is free which is pretty cool, but if i have to spend hours finding drivers, learning how to install themes etc, i don't mind paying for windows. take my hourly wage for the time i spent doing this initially, and i asure u Ubuntu cost me more than windows. 10 times more.

Room for improvement:

1) WHY, oh WHY is the first background screen still so but-ugly? for christ sake, make it look better. also, the default theme is not as nice as some of the ones available - why not use them?

2) i would make (maybe it is allready made, but then ANNOUNCE IT) a 10 step introduction to Ubuntu - what are the main difficulties for a windows user to switch, how to overcome them? for example, how do i install themes/programs? what do i do if things don't work? where can i expect problems?

3) seems like ubuntu is pretty good at not having me find new drivers/codex/players all the time when i watch movies, but still seems to be quite many programs pre-installed. i never saw an opportunity where i could prechoose which programs i wanted.

4) techiness - still get error messages once in a while, and still don't know how to handle them. for users with my medium techsavvyness, it seems that right now ubuntu is fine, but for less savvy people, still too much hazzle (also refer to 2))

5) small point, but why does an open software OS come preinstalled with a specific browser? even windows will offer you a choice now! same for other types of programs. see also 3) for this
