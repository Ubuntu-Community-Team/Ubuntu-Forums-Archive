### Post by djchandler on 2010-04-09
AMD64 version .iso downloaded, burned on my mythbuntu 9.10 box, and booted to choosing between install and test screen with zero problems. I must say, the artwork *is* striking.

The only issue I have is with some of the low contrast scroll bars and buttons being difficult to see. The screen was fine-tuned perfectly to see the 9.10 install that I overwrote with the Lucid Beta 2 installation. Windows 7 looks great on the same screen and video card too. So it must have at least a little to do with the color choices. But the rest of it looks amazing.

Although I didn't time the install, Lucid Beta 2 is by far the fastest OS install that I have performed. Considering how much software beyond the bare OS gets installed, it's nothing short of amazing in terms of the speed. There's less babysitting than with Windows 7 besides being faster with more useful software than the OEM version of Windows 7 RTM puts on a computer when you consider OpenOffice and Evolution being a part of the Ubuntu Lucid installation. This is a fair comparison from me, not just malarkey. I have an MS TechNet Plus Direct subscription and have clean installed Windows 7 RTM versions 3 times on 3 different computers of mine. I also installed the RC and Beta before those. Ubiquity is *fast*, period, The Live CD is slick and done to the highest of professional standards, and looks every bit like a product worthy of charging at least $100 US per copy (and more with OS support provided).

Great job, everybody.

Due to this already being a dual boot system I manually partitioned the disk. But for some reason I couldn't get it to triple boot as I couldn't create a third logical partition inside of the extended partition where Karmic and its swap already were.:confused:

Could be PEBKAC, but I couldn't get it to install despite having prepared an unused disk area for the Lucid install. Since I had already backed up any data from Karmic that I needed, I went ahead and removed Karmic's partitions. After I did that, everything went smoothly.
