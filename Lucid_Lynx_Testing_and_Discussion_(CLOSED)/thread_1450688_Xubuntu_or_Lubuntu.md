### Post by julianb on 2010-04-09
Here's my experience with Xubuntu, Lubuntu, and standard Ubuntu:

Standard Ubuntu (9.10 and 10.04beta) "just works" with the largest number of things ... no problems with "double click a file to open it with appropriate application" etc etc. Lucid Beta is fast but not bug-free.

Xubuntu 9.10 - most of the time it "just works" like Ubuntu, BUT when I used it on a few different Asus netbooks it wasn't any faster than Ubuntu. Tweaks like auto-login, changing the desktop appearance, etc., may be difficult and getting support may be difficult too, with the smaller user base. Xubuntu is supposed to be light, but in my experience its performance is no better than standard Ubuntu. 

Lubuntu 10.04 beta - runs noticeably faster. still needs some bug fixes, for example, I wanted it to automatically log me in to my housemate's encrypted wireless network, but it would not save the password. every re-start, I would be prompted to create a password for my "keyring", and the wireless network key was never stored, nor the "keyring" password.

### Post by phillw on 2010-04-16
Hi
> Please do not read my glowing praises of Lubuntu as a smear on any of the members of the family. One of the nice things about the family is that we all share the same heritage and you can easily try the various flavours out on a "try before you buy" scheme. Once you have decided on the flavour you want, you are then required to pay exactly nothing.

The last update I had on Lubuntu is that it was and is very _unlikely_ to be a 'main' for 10.04. It will remain at 'RC' level. There are various reasons for this, I try to keep a quick 'news' on Lubuntu updated (It's in my Sig) with the emails via the lubuntu mailing group feel free to subscribe to it. [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Lubuntu#Joining](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Lubuntu#Joining) the Team & Participating

As you will see, some of us have done some pretty crazy things to it :lolflag:

Yeah, there are still some bugs, but these are not so much stability bugs (it uses *buntu) more things that don't quite work as they should and some things still on the 'To Do' list.

Indeed you can pop lxde onto different linuxes, it is just a desktop, but Lubuntu is its most popular flavour. Adding the Lubuntu desktop to a minimal installation of Ubuntu has been reported as being slightly larger than just putting lubuntu on from the beginning (in terms of disk space). It is currently the only route for those who cannot run Ubiquity because of RAM limitations (Ubquity is the GUI installation system), and need to put on the command line mini installation with no GUI at all. You would need to add the lubuntu desktop ppa.
And, yes, it is on the 'To Do' list, but that will be after the 'RC' for 10.04 launches and the poor devs have a bit of time.

Regards,

Phill.
