### Post by djchandler on 2010-04-04
The best thing about Thunderbird is it works in three major OSes, *nix, Mac and Windows.

What I don't like about Evolution is it gives the impression there's no easy way to export your data to another email client. It can be done, but would be a challenge for the average computer user. 

There's problems setting up recurring tasks still in Evolution. If you use the Lightning plug-in for Thunderbird, you encounter no such problems. It's as easy if not easier to maintain the calendar's appointments and tasks with Lightning as with Outllook. IMHO anybody who has used Outlook has a much easier time transitioning to Thunderbird than Evolution.

Keeping in mind Evolution is the default upstream (Debian has issues with Mozilla), there's an implied dependency of Gnome to Evolution. So removing it, as the previous and many other posters have indicated, is not for the faint of heart. Of course if you truly understand what's going on, it's no big deal.

Because Evolution is the default in the upstream, I voted that it should be kept. But let me qualify that there should be an easy means of removing it without the appearance that there are still remnants of it left behind. But that's probably something that needs to be worked out between Debian and Novell.

Another issue with changing to Thunderbird is that you must start the entire application if you want task and appointment reminders from the Lightning plug-in. You could use Sunbird if memory is an issue (how much difference could that be?), but then you lose the ease of turning emails into appointments or tasks as you can using the Lightning plug-in with Thunderbird.

BTW I have replaced Evolution in the 10.04 beta with Thunderbird just to make sure it's doable in the same manner. My actual email is on another computer.
