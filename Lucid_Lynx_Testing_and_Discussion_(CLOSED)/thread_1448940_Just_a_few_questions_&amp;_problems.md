### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-04-07
Here's my answers to your questions and comments, as far as I can.

Q1.  This effect is surely down to the animation plugin in compiz, so play around with the settings of that and you may be able to get back what you want.

Q2.  The work is being done on Indicator applets instead of notification area applets, because many applications and utilities eg evolution, empathy, are no longer supporting or supported by the NI.  Therefore the only way to get an icon in the panel is to use IA.  How Gnome Shell will deal with these things, I have no idea.

Q3.  I have no idea.  I didn't like the grey/brown login screen for 9.10 very much, and changed it to my desktop background image, but it did work very well and quickly.

Q4.  No idea, and it's one thing I have no interest in.

P1.  I agree that plymouth at the moment is not working, on my machine at least.  I get the same black screen and cursor flashing for several seconds, then about 1 second of the purple "ubuntu" logo with five orange dots, and then the resolution of that goes wrong, the screen goes pink patterned with the left hand end of the word ubuntu just about visible (the letter "u"), and then the login screen appears.  From then all is OK.  It is still quick, however; probably quicker than 9.10.

P2.  I have no idea, as I have not bothered to do that.  Having used Ubuntu for 5 years, I don't need to read that any more.

I am using lucid on a test partition that I use for just that purpose, and still have karmic as my main OS.  I agree that lucid looks good, and apart from these minor glitches I think I shall move over properly when the final version has been around for a week or two.
