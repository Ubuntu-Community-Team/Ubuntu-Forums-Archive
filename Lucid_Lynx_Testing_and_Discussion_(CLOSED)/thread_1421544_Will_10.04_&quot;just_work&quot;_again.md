### Post by reiki on 2010-03-04
I started using Ubuntu back in (I think) 2005. I've always been interested in linux and tried several flavors, but none of them was really a suitable everyday working desktop OS.... until Ubuntu.

Right now I'm up to Karmic (64-bit). It's a nightmare. The rear sound jack on my machine isn't detected. I can no longer manage users and groups through the GUI. Flash is hit and miss. I haven't tried burning video DVDs again yet so I'm not sure if that's working again or not.

It just seems as each version comes out, more stuff gets broken as we "advance" to newer and supposedly better pieces and parts. 

I want to get back to "stuff just works". 

Just my 2 cents as a long time user. I'm not a programmer. I try to get people switched over to Ubuntu. In many ways it is so vastly superior. I can't keep recommending it if the analogy is going to be similar to people having to be auto mechanics to own a car. I can't keep sending them under the hood to tweak stuff that should work out of the box.

I guess I'm just getting a bit frustrated with all the shiny new stuff taking the spotlight while long-standing issues remain unresolved.
