### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2010-02-27
I downloaded Alpha3 of the next Ubuntu release and I am currently trying to break it :D  I wanted to share some exciting news about nice features, but 
in reality, this Alpha3 just looks like an Alpha3.

The boot process and login screen are quite similar to Karmic, but look unfinished.  I am not really paying attention, as I am sure they will radically change on the official release.  Boot time is fast, but have not noticed any difference when compared to Karmic, which was installed on the same machine up until 2 days ago.

GNOME 2.29 is loaded, and I can see OpenOffice 3.2, Firefox 3.6, and a number of more up to date applications, which again, will be updated as the final release is launched.  Gwibber joins empathy for this release, and both can be easily controlled from the new MeMenu.  It is OK, not sure I would have invested the time in such feature (absolutely worth nothing unless you are into social networks).

As expected, GIMP is gone, and the whole installation only takes a very impressive 2.6 GB of disk space!  Even without GIMP, Lucid is leaps and bounds more complete than Windows in terms of applications preisntalled, so it is surprising that it can do it taking such low disk space.  What was not so pleasing was to find out that Lucid takes 400MB+ of RAM memory with no applications loaded.  My Karmic and Jaunty installs boot to 220-260MB on average, so finding twice as much in use for no apparent reason was not nice.  I hope this is just due to its Alpha quality and is solved before release date.

I was also surprised to see Synaptic still under the applications menu.  My understanding was that the Software center would take on all of the installation/uninstallation duties for Lucid.  That's not the case in Alpha 3, and I personally hope it stays that way.  The software center could be perfect for new comers, but it is still a bit limited in functionality and I think it will take a little longer before it can fully take over from Synaptic.

Lucid was working very well on my HP NX7400 laptop.  It recognised all hardware perfectly, and although I have seen some dodgy things here and there, but nothing that can be reported as a bug.  

If you are looking for some exciting new features, stay away from this Alpha release, which is solely meant for testing and bug fixing.  Personally, I cannot really tell if I like the direction Lucid is going, because it is still unclear from this Alpha.  Let's wait and see.
