### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-12-22
> **altonbr said:**
> They REALLY could put more time and energy into this instead of setting up a group on Flickr (which has a mandatory sign up for a Yahoo account).

Why not this forum? Or a version of brainstorm.ubuntu.com but for the wallpapers? Just something more accessible to the Ubuntu community at least.

A deadline and more communication would also be nice.

This topic was discussed at UDS, and definitely is in peoples' minds, including those that are close to the project. (Using Ubuntu One was one such solution). Please remember that this is a very new project.

The choice of Flickr at the moment is a practical one; people who like to share really good photos tend to have Flickr accounts, since it is probably the biggest photo sharing site out there, and they do a great job respecting peoples' individual content licenses (including Creative Commons). So, using Flickr means they're making the process really, REALLY easy for a particularly large audience. That's the case for now, anyway ;)

A rating system like on Brainstorm, as you suggest, doesn't quite cut it in this case, since the entries here should be judged relatively equally. Having a site where each entry gets voted up or down by an arbitrarily large or small group of people is not reaching that objective.
(I didn't follow the process for Karmic myself, but from what I hear the people doing the judging did a good job of that, all things considered, going so far as to make prints of piles of pictures so that they could be looked over more comfortably).

That's not to say one couldn't be a wonderful help and a beautiful aide to the process, especially with something to even the tables like a "help judge the entries" interface that presented (weighted) random entries based on how many times they have been judged - maybe actually setting them as the user's wallpaper - but that takes considerably more work than simply adding <img> to Brainstorm.
