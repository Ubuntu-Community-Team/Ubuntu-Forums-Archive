### Post by Ellipsis on 2010-03-21
In Ubuntu it is easy to change anything (or at least find a guide to help you change it) which is great for the user. But when people get put off by choices which damage their work flow they can quickly switch back to more familiar routines. This is bad for testing. 

This rebellion against work flow-interruption happens on all platforms-just look at Facebook. Every time Facebook undergoes a UI change a band of users will rally against the change and be very loud... and then disappear. They don't have a choice- they have to adapt and grow to like it (Though Facebook does at times give in a little). With Ubuntu you have choice and that is a problem. But only if people don't test and complain after not testing. 

Recently, the work flow break was the button positioning. People have become very loud on that issue. Other work flow breaks will happen- Gnome 3 for example. Which is the reason for this thread. There will be many examples of people switching back before properly testing. 

Of course there are MANY valid comments about problems with a new workflow/UI element. But these MAY be drowned out in a sea of "I don't like it change it to something familiar" and "logical deduction" (you don't test a kernel through logic, you should not test UI elements like that).

So to check this my own line of reasoning I am wondering: How many of you "tested" before switching back in regards to the "Button Issue"?. For those wondering: Depending on your usage/workflow testing will take different amounts of time from a day to a few days but certainly not a few failed mouse clicks.  

So have YOU: Tested the Buttons? Other than it not being familiar what is your objection? Has a scenerio _occurred to you_ in which the left sided buttons have caused incidents like closing an application instead of opening one from the menu? Has it happened multiple times?

As well I would like to ask: Are you someone who has yet to try the latest builds but already have an opinion on the issue? Why do you have that opinion?

Edit: If you are still in the process of testing or have not used the latest builds please do not vote. Instead join the discussion. Once you have tested or switched back please vote AND post about your experience.

### Post by djchandler on 2010-03-21
> **And1945 said:**
> Perhaps I am stupid, but how does one go around changing the buttons to the right side again?

Run /usr/bin/gconf-editor

Go to /apps/metacity/general/button_layout

Change the key from **maximize, minimize, close:** to[B] :maximize, minimize, close

[/B]All that needs to be done is move the colon from one side to the other. I also added back the left side drop down menu button that is on the title bar in the last version of the Humanity theme.

Ex. **menu:maximize, minimize, close**

See my full screen shot on my previous post (#9) in this thread.
