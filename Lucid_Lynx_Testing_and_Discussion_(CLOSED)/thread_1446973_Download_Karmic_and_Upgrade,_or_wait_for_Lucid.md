### Post by FatalChaos on 2010-04-04
At least for me, I would rather do fresh installs since I have had random problems when upgrading in the past, especially if I tweak the system a bit. However, I've heard that Ubuntu's gotten better about upgrading, so either way it should be okay. 

I guess the real question is how urgently do you want to use Ubuntu again? the safe answer is just wait to do a fresh install, but you'll probably be okay using karmic and then upgrading, especially if you don't tweak your system a lot.

### Post by Didius Falco on 2010-04-04
With just three weeks to go until the release, I think I'd just wait until after the release to upgrade, unless there is some compelling reason to do so now. Actually, if you have the time, I'd wait 6-7 weeks from now until the post mass-install rush is over and any new bugs are squashed. 

If you have a spare 15-20 gigs, you could simply install it in a separate partition and give it a test drive until the release. That way you'd have your "productivity" install but still be able to test out Lucid.

Of course, this is one of those things where the only "right" answer is the one you decide on. ;)
