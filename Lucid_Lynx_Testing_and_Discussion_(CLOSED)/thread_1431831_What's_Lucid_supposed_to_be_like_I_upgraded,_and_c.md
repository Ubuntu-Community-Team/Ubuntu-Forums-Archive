### Post by Lucky. on 2010-03-18
I dunno if I would call this 100% trollish.  Reason being:

One of the most annoying things I hear when somebody alpha/beta tests software is "OMG THIS DOESN'T WORK YOU NEED TO FIX IT!" when it's blatantly obvious that the part they're complaining about isn't anywhere near completion.

On the same token I've tested alpha/beta stuff and said "Yeah this is broken, you need to fix this part right here," only to hear some pompous dev idiot say "Oh yeah totally we know about that and it's getting fixed in the final version."  Then the final ships with the same bug sitting there, and people are like "We had noooo idea that issue was there, nobody told us!"

If the user knows what's **supposed** to happen and not happen, at least he/she can determine what needs to be improved / fixed / etc, and give good feedback.

As for kelvin.illa, I'd say "sorry man, get more involved with the development process" rather than mentioning anything here.  Start by learning how current Ubuntu builds work.  Learn more about the internals of the OS.  When you're at that point, pay attention to the dev team messages & reports.  Then the alphas and their problems will be as clear as day...and you may find yourself working on them yourself.  Until then, we're just the dudes on the sidelines rooting for the team and hoping they give us something nice when its time.
