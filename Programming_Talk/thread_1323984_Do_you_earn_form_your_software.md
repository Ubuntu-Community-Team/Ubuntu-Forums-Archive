### Post by dvlchd3 on 2009-11-12
It depends.  Some open source projects will receive government funding because they are "researching" something involving technology.  Other open source projects receive money from donations only.  Others form a model that allows users who "pay" for the software to get better support, or more features, or both.

Most Open Source projects are created just because someone realized there was a problem with no solution for it.  They then look to the open source community to help them develop such solution.  Sourceforge is the best example I can think of for this.  ([www.sourceforge.net](www.sourceforge.net))

I found a few good articles on the Open Source business model.  There are several theorys on how to make money from Open Source software, but some developers are motiviated simply because they feel they are offering a viable, great solution for the world.  (The money is just a bonus :))

[http://www.opensourcestrategies.org/](http://www.opensourcestrategies.org/)
