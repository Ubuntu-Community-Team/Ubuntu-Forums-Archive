### Post by orphanlast on 2011-10-18
I am about to learn how to use blender simply because I've seen what it can do and it's free. However, I've read the Wikipedia page about it and it's a tragic tale about a man who created a company called NaN that planned on selling Blender as its flagship. When it wasn't selling, they eventually kept moving more and more open source. When they realized that they had to close shop for good, they were barely able to squeeze out Blender 2.59.

But... with NaN gone, what do you, the open source programmers out there, see as the future of Blender? To me, even though I haven't used the program much, it seems like the Linux crown jewel... along side with Gimp... only Blender seems better executed, in my opinion. 

Do you guys see people just modding the crap out of it (which will inevitably happen, and that's cool) and that's it or do you think that there'll actually be a group of people that will adopt it as their pet project and continue to refine and broaden its use and functionality by making other releases of the program?

It'd kinda suck if that's it for this poor little thing.
