### Post by Paul Miller on 2009-06-23
> **Penguin Guy said:**
> 
**Removing the letters:**
Apparently sandpaper works fine but it sounds like a lot of work and will leave scratches.


You'd have to experiment on a few keys made of the exact material you're going to be using, but I wonder if there isn't some kind of solvent that will take the lettering off the keys and leave the key itself.  Barring that, the sandpaper idea seems good.  To avoid having the appearance or feel of scratches on the keys, you'd just have to use a fine grit.  You can start the sanding with a coarser grit just to get things going, being careful not to scratch too deeply into the plastic of the keys, but you'll need to finish with what's called (oddly enough) a finishing grit. This will increase your workload proportionally, but the final result will be worth it.

Edit: OTOH, if you're going to be using decals for the symbols, anyway, why not just make them cover the whole key and not bother with removing the original letter?  I'd at least try this and see how you like it, because it has the potential to cut your workload *wayyyy* down on this project.

You could also start with a blank keyboard.  Google "das Keyboard" and you'll find what I mean.
