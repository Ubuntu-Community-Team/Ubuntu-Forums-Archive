Just because you don't have to learn it doesn't mean you won't find it very useful down the road (which requires you to know it!). Like lisati said, having a generalized sense of programming languages can be very useful.

Do you have to? No. But I'd recommend you do at some point, because it has become very instrumental in making pages, and websites. For example, on my pastebin website, when you go to the main page, it autofocuses on the textarea where you paste your code. I don't need to click it. I just enter the URL and press ctrl+v. Why, in fact, my site even has the ability to resize the textarea dynamically, in case you need more room to see the code as you're getting ready to paste it. I can't do that without Javascript. So it has it's time and place. Other examples are Ajax, which can decrease bandwidth for your site, and increase responsiveness for users, plus it looks nice.
