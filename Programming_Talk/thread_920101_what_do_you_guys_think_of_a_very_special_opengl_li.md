### Post by Tony Flury on 2008-09-15
I may have completely misunderstood you - I hope I have, but the whole point about libraries is that they encapsulate common activities into simple to use code, which can be re-used over and over again - and the become a standard method that everyone understands, and that you can move between projects. Well written libraries that everyone uses are the examplar of good quality design - to be embraced and re-used not thrown away.

If you train people not to use the libraries then they wont have the skills they need on the next project they undertake - which will almost certainly use the libraries.

Also - why throw away all the hard work that other people have put in, and re-invent the wheel ?

Unless a particular library does not do exactly what you want, at the speed you want it, there is no good reason not to use teh standard libraries.

Can i suggest a better course of action might be to see what you can do to extend the exiting library of your choice, to provide either a more specific functionality, or provide some higher level function that combines and co-ordinates a number of the low level library functions - that way you build on the work already done - and at the same time give practical skills on the use of the common software.
