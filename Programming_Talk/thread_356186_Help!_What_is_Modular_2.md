### Post by samjh on 2007-02-08
Modula-2 is a revised version of Pascal.  Designed to be even more structured and with additional language constructs.  These days, it's hardly mentioned anywhere, although Pascal still remains a reasonably popular language.

Pascal is a fantastic language for teaching structured programming.  It was extremely popular in education and computer science back in the late 70s and throughout the 80s and early 90s (used extensively on Apple for developing the MacIntosh operating system until version 9, and a popular choice for computer magazines, university and high school courses, etc.).  Modula-2 tries to make it even more suited to computer science education, so I can understand the choice, although I've never used Modula-2 (I was originally taught in Pascal).

A Modula-2 compiler for Linux: [http://floppsie.comp.glam.ac.uk/Glamorgan/gaius/web/m2fabout.html](http://floppsie.comp.glam.ac.uk/Glamorgan/gaius/web/m2fabout.html)
