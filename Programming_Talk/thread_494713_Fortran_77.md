### Post by xtacocorex on 2007-07-07
Well I can't tell you about FORTRAN 77 books since the ones I had were instructor books for courses that I got rid of.  I do have a numerical methods book that has code snippets in 77, but doesn't talk about the fixed-format rules.

For FORTRAN 90, "Introduction to FORTRAN 90 for Engineers and Scientists" by Nyhoff and Leestma was my second book on FORTRAN that I used in college.  It has kept be on the golden path for years now.  Another book that was required for class was "FORTRAN 90/95 for Scientists and Engineers" by Stephen Chapman.  I didn't use the latter book until I graduated.  

I was given "FORTRAN 95" by Martin Counihan as a graduation present from one of my high school teachers.  This book is pretty in depth and requres that you know some programming which is why it was hard to look at until I became a proficinet programmer.

I learned FORTRAN 77 by looking at a NASA program called PMARC and the internet.  I don't know how I would relearn it if I had to, but going from 77 to 90 required some backward thinking.  The fact that you can't cross column 82 and can only start on certain columns for certain things messed me up in the beginning.  There isn't much syntax difference as anything in 77 can be used in 90, at least from what I've seen.

Hope this helps some.
