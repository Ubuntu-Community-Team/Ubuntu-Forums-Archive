### Post by zadehas on 2010-11-27
if you have lots of memory, go with netbeans ([http://netbeans.org/]("http://netbeans.org/")). You can also find it in the repositories. Netbeans is heavy weight, but great for complex projects.

Geany ([http://www.geany.org/]("http://www.geany.org/")) is a lightweight editor, still packing a lot of features.

Otherwise, basic editors will probably be enough (gedit in Gnome or Kate in KDE for instance).
