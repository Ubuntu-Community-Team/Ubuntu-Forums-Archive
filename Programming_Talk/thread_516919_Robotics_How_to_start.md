### Post by hod139 on 2007-08-03
Robotics is a huge cross disciplinary field.  It's a combination of Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Computer Science and Mathematics (especially mathematics :) ).

Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) is the first college to actually start offering an undergraduate degree in robotics. This is a big experiment, because now WPI has to define what a robotics major is.  If you want to get an idea of what courses WPI considers a robotics major should have, you can check out their website: [http://www.wpi.edu/Academics/Majors/RBE/courses.html](http://www.wpi.edu/Academics/Majors/RBE/courses.html)

As for a good place to start, I suggest linear algebra.  No other math will be more important (maybe differential geometry but lets start at the basics :) ) to assist your learning and  understanding of the basic principles.

I'm getting my Ph.D in robotics (technically computer science), so if you have any questions feel free to pm me, or stop by the #ubuntu-programming channel on freenode, I hang out there a lot.

### Post by pmasiar on 2007-08-03
[vex](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vex) - easy, cheap, with great guide how to build your own remote-controlled robots. If $300 is too much for you, try [meccano](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meccano) builder - I bought it online via FatBrain, for like $80. meccano has mechanic part (better than vex), but no remote control tho.

WPI in MA has [competition](http://users.wpi.edu/~savage/) for high school students using robots based on this kit. Great fun!
