### Post by els on 2010-05-06
I was in pretty much the same situation until about 2 years ago when I was hired by a small local company to build a series of very customized ecommerce sites for them.  I found that while building the dynamic catalog/CMS for them was somewhat tedious at times I learned so much more than if I was working with something prebuilt like Zen Cart.  So even though there are many great applications already out there (i.e. Wordpress) it can be pretty beneficial to take the time to make your own blog/catalog/whatever from the ground up.

I also found that using a framework (Codeigniter in my case) really helped me to start to understand how to structure a larger application.
