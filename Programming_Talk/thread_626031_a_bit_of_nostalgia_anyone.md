### Post by pmasiar on 2007-12-02
> **CptPicard said:**
> This one is going to be hard to beat 

Let me try:
My first computer I was allowed to reboot myself  was IBM/360 clone, which, after using for a year nightly  alongside with PhD astronomy student working on his thesis, I was allowed to run myself alone for couple of nights - to run Star Trek game competition of course! :-) 

It had 1MB of RAM, was build from separate transistors (no IC), has 2 disks [2311 clone](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_IBM_disk_storage#IBM_2311) as big as dishwasher, 6 plates about 20 inches across, storing 7MB.

After that, I was allowed to "admin" and use for my master thesis PDP/11 clone, what has 64K semiconductor memory, and "upper" 64K was [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferrite_core_memory](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferrite_core_memory) when accessed, you can hear the clicking noise as ferite core were flipping and flopping, and feel your program slowing down.

Floating processor was broken, they could not fix it for couple weeks, but I rewrote my FORTH routines around it, so I was the only one able to use it (it helped on my thesis) :-) It has also the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colossal_Cave](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colossal_Cave) game. Written in Fortran (in those days, [Real programer did not used Pascal](http://www.pbm.com/~lindahl/real.programmers.html)) of course, but without floating point arithmetic, so Colossal Cave run! :-) 

> **samjh said:**
> Oh the days when floppies were really floppy, and hard disks were hard to come by!

PDP/11 floppies in my ancient times were [8 inches big](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floppy_disk#Origins.2C_the_8-inch_disk), and stored around 250KB. We cut holes into envelope of single-side medium to convert it to double side, doubling the capacity. :-)

My first PC clone I used was original IBM PC XT, 4.77 Mhz CPU. It has 1MB of RAM, no hard disk (expensive 10MB hard cards in those times) and only one of two floppy drives worked. I booted from one floppy with installed PCTools, made PVT resident (TSA, wasting 150KB of RAM), then switched floppy to another, with Borland Framework II.

First PC I bought myself has 1MB RAM, 12MHz CPU, and everyone thought I was joking when I said I splurged on 100MB harddisk: Who needed such ridiculously huge disks? In those times, 10MB was fine, and MS-DOS could not handle partitions above 32MB. Of course it was before UFO-XCOM: Then is was me who laughed, when then cannot install 10MB game on their pithy 40MB disk, formatted with single partition  32MB! :-)

Yes, those days single person could understand OS written by MSFT, and many did!

I bought myself a Commodore 64 with two 1541 floppy drives and the 1702 monitor (which actually had better video than my 19" tv) and taught myself how to hack the programs on it.  I later gave it to my dad (once I had a fancy 486DX machine), and kind of regret doing so... it got sold or donated when my dad died, I guess.
