### Post by maximinus_uk on 2009-09-24
From having worked on a few projects myself, I can tell you most people who ask for information on the programming side end up never contributing. This means that most programmers will end up pointing you at the doc's, forums and code for more info, because they've answered most of your questions already. And it's rare to find a project with flow charts for code (can't say I've ever worked on one).

A better approach is to focus on one area. Look at the current TODO, or current bugs, or even ask where you can help. Concentrate on this one thing and then ask questions about that. If a project has 10K LOC nobody expects you to understand all of it before you help out, especially if you're having to learn a new library as well.

Another good avenue is to try the site's IRC channel, if they have one. This is often a far faster way to get good information - and it lets you get to know the team a let better as well.

Good luck!
