### Post by DougB1958 on 2007-11-23
Sounds to me like what you're looking for is the sort of stuff that would be in the first couple of chapters of a good compiler design book. Unfortunately, my impression is that teaching compiler design has somewhat gone out of fashion (at least in the US), and with it much of the demand for good compiler design books. Or maybe it's just that I haven't thought much about teaching compiler design in a while. But for whatever reason, the books I really like in this area are old -- but still available at places like Amazon.com, or so a few quick searches claim,

The one I think of as the classic is Aho, Sethi, and Ullman's "Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools," published in the mid-80's. But I have a feeling that there's a brand new update by Aho, Lam, Sethi, and Ullman, so you might look for a similar title by that set of authors first.

Another book that I remember as being very good at the practical aspects of writing compilers (including parsers) is Fischer and Leblanc's "Crafting a Compiler," and it's later edition "Crafting a Compiler with C." Again, these are late-80's-early-90's vintage, but I remember them being pretty good.

Hope this helps, and good luck.
