### Post by pmasiar on 2008-01-25
> **Lord Illidan said:**
> I am wondering, can we link to other pages? ... Should I link to them, copy and paste from them, or just make my own content?

In my experience (and my wiki :-) ) other people created excellent content and is much smarter to link to it that trying (and failing) to one-up them. Writer's contribution is still important: finding the links, evaluating them, and commenting them. Not all links are good, and not all top-rated pages on Google are the best. There are many "hidden gems". :-)

But there are many different ways to link, not all equally good. Good link suggests where it goes and why will be for the reader beneficent to follow the link. Paradigm is "information hunting", and good link has good "smell", so hunter will follow it. It also should "deep link" - directly to the article, not to the site homepage.

Copy-paste content might be against the license, and also less effective, especially if you copy-paste from another wiki: it is like forking the code, any code improvements will be lost unless merged. Link with good "smell" is preferable, IMHO.
