### Post by ajackson on 2009-07-14
A lot depends on what area you want to work in. For a lot of companies a degree will be sufficient to show your skills in a favourable light. Getting Network+ would be a nice add-on but jobs that require that level of competence should accept your degree (if you show how hardware/network related it is).

If however you are applying for positions where they mention the CISCO certificates (the names of which I can never remember) then your degree might be a bit too general for that employers liking but again different companies might accept a heavily network orientated degree as a substitute.

Since you are still at university you should be able to talk with careers advisers who should be able to give you better information than I certainly can.
