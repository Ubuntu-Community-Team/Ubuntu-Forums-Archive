### Post by ZylGadis on 2008-07-18
Story writing (the industry calls it storyboarding, which is incorect IMO) is a very valuable skill to have as pertains to building games. In certain genres (e.g., RPG) stories are a fundamental part of the game. So if you are a good story writer (and I do mean good, as opposed to writing about "Young hero fights blue-skinned orags to retrieve Bling of Power and save the world") you can be a valuable asset to a programming team.
Sadly, programming and creative writing don't mix. They require completely different skill sets, parts of which are contradictory. Very few people can do both. This has nothing to do with dyslexia.
