### Post by Vaphell on 2011-02-01
You are right about the marriage of hobby and work.
I'd advise against getting into game development business. Unless you are a crème de la crème, most probably you will be underpaid, overworked and will lose a love for games in a blink of an eye :)
I used to be an aspiring 3d modeller and got a job in a small, B-class game company. After mere 40 days of extremely mindnumbing 10-12h/day work with only 3 days off in that period i gtfoed from there and never looked back. There was always some deadline to meet, every week. What's worse, it totally killed my hobby, I never launched 3dsmax again.
So my advice is: if you love games, do it without pressure in your free time, but don't plan a career in an industry that exploits the naivety of countless young people who think that if playing games is cool, then developing must be too.

### Post by Moloch85 on 2011-02-01
Programming in the game field teaches you how to create efficient data structures, how to manage timing and threads, how to control the user input and use graphics at its best.
They are extremely important issues in many non-game programs, so it's not a waste of time to apply in this field.

Sure your hardcore gamer background will help you, if games are your passion i suggest you to cultivate it at its best, maybe starting from simple projects in simple frameworks, then progressively going to the point you want to reach. You're plenty of time to do other things. A friend of mine has recently attended a course about videogame programming, realizing a musical game called Xenophonic:
