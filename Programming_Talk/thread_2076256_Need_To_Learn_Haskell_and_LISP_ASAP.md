### Post by trent.josephsen on 2012-10-25
What is he going to do when he gets a real job? "Sorry, boss, I don't know much about Rails. Let me ring my buddy Majorix..." :-P

Seriously, I gave out way more homework help than I probably should have wasted time on during my 4 years in college. I know you want to help out your friend, but you don't want to cultivate dependence (which is easier than it sounds, trust me). Besides, if his professor won't let him use the assistance of other people in the class or even Web resources, you can bet that "my friend who knows X helped me with this" *definitely* isn't acceptable. Homework is often assigned to teach discipline and study habits, not just course content.

On top of that... as a sophomore software engineer, where are you finding the time to help other people with homework in classes you don't even have? I found helping other people cutting in to my own homework time even when we were studying the same material. Not to mention that, especially right now, you should most certainly be spending your spare time looking for software engineering internships and co-ops to improve your chances of a job on graduation, not fooling around with other people's homework. Internships get harder to find after your sophomore year and it's difficult to get experience without them.

This is the advice I wish someone had given me when I was in the fall semester of my sophomore year. I regret not following it.
