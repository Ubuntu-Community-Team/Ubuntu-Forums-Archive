### Post by meng on 2007-01-14
I'm not sure how much you know about python, but one of it's charms is the concept of "batteries included", that is, libraries/modules for the things you are most likely to need come by default. There are multiple choices for GUI programming: tkinter, wxwidgets, pygtk, pyqt. But if you haven't grasped the basics of python yet, don't get sidetracked by trying to learn GUi programming simultaneously. Unless your a programming genius, that's unlikely to work.

Of course, feel free to tell me that I'm wrong and you're the next Stallman/Torvalds.

If you want a nice environment for editing and building your Python programs then try Stani's Python Editor. It's free and nice and easy to use without being a ridiculous over the top IDE that does everything for you.
