### Post by vcardona on 2007-07-12
Its been a while since I used EMACS, but I will try to answer your question.  The best advise I have is to do the following.  Read the documentation that comes with EMACS.  Specifically, you will want to learn about cc-mode.  That is the major mode that handles C, C++, Java, and Objective C.  The help documentation that comes with EMACS should have a lot of information about configuring EMACS for those languages. 

My second bit of advise is to google for .emacs files.  There are a lot of examples on the web, and many are really good.  Pick and choose the parts you need.

Finally, get a book.  The GNU Project has some books about EMACS.  Oreilly used to have one as well.  I own the Oreilly one and it is pretty good at getting you started at least.

Good luck.
