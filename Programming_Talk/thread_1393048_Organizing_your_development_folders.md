### Post by Hellkeepa on 2010-01-29
HELLo!

The type of language, and type of application*, are determinators that could change quite easily, or you could even end up with a mix of languages. In either case, they're not important at all. Except maybe for language tutorials, which could be organized by language with success.

The name of the client/employee is a good way to separate projects, especially if you are working freelance and have a lot of clients. You might also want to separate them by year, if you are working on many small projects.
Another way you could organize the projects, is by "usage"/design type. For instance web projects in their own core folder, used as the root folder for your development server. With Desktop applications in another, and daemons/server applications in a third.

* I've gone on the interpretation of "type of application" to mean "application classification", like in browser, finance, IDE, and so forth.

Happy codin'!
