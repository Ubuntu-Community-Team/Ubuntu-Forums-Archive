### Post by trent.josephsen on 2010-12-04
Sounds like a fit for software engineering.  "Computer engineering" usually (but not always) refers to something more hardware-centric, but if I understand you, you're more interested in a general survey of the low-level stuff and moving quickly into the realm of software.

You might want to try putting your hands on an evaluation board and learning some kind of assembly language.  My knowledge is all 68k, but ARM looks interesting.  (x86 is notoriously hideous.)

I don't have the series myself, but nobody can give you more information than [Knuth](http://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~uno/taocp.html).
