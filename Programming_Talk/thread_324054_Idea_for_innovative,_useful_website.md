### Post by kidders on 2006-12-23
Hmmm.... learning things *together*... interesting idea.

Three possibilities strike me (Hopefully you'll get more responses before long!) [COLOR="DimGray"]**Edit:** Yay Wybiral![/COLOR]

**1. Something academic**
Using the web as a learning resource is pretty hard work, because current search engines are ... well ... a pile of crap. There's a lot of work going on at the moment in academic circles that has to do with organising information semantically, so that when you go looking for information about, say, Spiderman, or the reproductive cycle of the praying mantis, you actually get what you ask for. An ontological search engine would be a real asset to students.

**2. A proper ELE**
Current educational learning environments are absolutely awful. Sometimes I wonder about how tough it would be to write a proper, smart learning environment that could adapt meaningfully to a person's intellectual ability & prior knowledge. Say you wanted to learn Japanese. Chances are that you would never find an online resource capable of teaching you the way you like to learn, at the pace you like to learn at, without frustrating the hell out of you, all while taking account of how good you're likely to be able to get, at the end of the day.

**3. Something fun**
You could opt for trying to offer people ways of learning about more ... well ... frivolous things, like maybe what it's like to be colour blind, how to train your memory to remember extremely long numbers, or how people go about inventing new flavours of ice cream. Something like that would rely completely on presentation, but there must be *some* kind of innovative way of presenting information that someone hasn't thought of yet.

These ideas are probably utter crap, but exactly *why* they're crap would be good to know ... the perfect concept is out there somewhere ... we just have to find it!!
