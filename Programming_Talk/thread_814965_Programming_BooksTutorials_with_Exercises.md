### Post by thornmastr on 2008-06-01
> **Majorix said:**
> Oh some misunderstanding between us then. Forgive me.

[QUOTE=Majorix;5091877]There SHOULD be at least a few "exercises & solutions" books, and I will hopefully find at least one for each of those languages.

The elegance, beauty, and indeed the practicality of programming is that the solution which works is the correct solution. The solution you derive as a beginner is not, guaranteed, the solution you would pick in a year, or five years, or.....etc. 

Therefore, most good programming texts do not provide answers. They do, much more importantly, provide questions which in some way impact greatly on the subject under discussion.

The best example I can give you is _Accelerated C++_ by Koenig and Moo. That text will either help you to "see" what is happening within the language particle(s) under discussion or will discourage you to such an extent it quickly separates the "programmer" from the ever quagmired "wannabee". It takes time and effort to do the questions, but what you will learn very quickly is the right solution is the one that works best. 

Therefore, solve the question, and know that if the solution is correct, you do have the **Correct** answer. If you have questions as to the correctness of your approach, post them here, and people will guide you as they can.

Best of luck in your endeavors.

Robert Berman
