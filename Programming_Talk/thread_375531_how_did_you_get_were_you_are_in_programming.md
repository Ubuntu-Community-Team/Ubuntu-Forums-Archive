### Post by s1ightcrazed on 2007-03-03
Interesting post. 

I'm only 28 (yes, ONLY!), so I don't pretend to have all the answers, but here is what I will impart to you based on my life's experience, so far:

Do not think of your future in such narrow terms. You obviously have a desire to do something with programming, so keep it simple. Learn as much as you can, and be as good as you can be with whatever you learn. 'Paying the bills' is as much about making yourself unique, and indispensable to your employer as it is about any sort of ideal or preplanned career path. Do what you love to do, and don't compromise. Period.

### Post by tbodine on 2007-03-04
Well, I'm nowhere special in my programming venture, but I guess I can try to share some insight.
I too am 16 -- 17 in 8 days, whoo! -- and sometimes I kinda feel like people wouldn't really want someone so young trying to fix their bugs and what-have-you, but I'm wrong! Programmers don't even ask my age, ever, unless it's just a user poll or something like that, so they wouldn't really know. If I submit a bug fix, or show someone a program I'm writing, they treat me just like any other programmer, even if they know my age, it's quite cool actually.
Anyways, if you really want to get into programming, I'm part of a really cool community that goes by the name of Nonlogic ([http://www.nonlogic.org)](http://www.nonlogic.org)), now I'm not trying to plug them or anything, but they have a really great programming community going on there. I won't get into what they do or anything, that's not what this post is about, but being around these other programmers -- quite a few of them about my age-ish -- really gets me wanting to learn more about programming, and it really motivates me to actually go out and keep coding. However, not everyone in the community uses GNU/Linux (on their own system, anyways), some use Mac, and yes, some use Windows, but it's not a GNU/Linux community, it's a free software development community, and it's really helped me with my programming, I thoroughly enjoy the time I spend there.
That's my spiel on developing free software, being in a programming community, and being a "young" programmer.
