### Post by SuperMike on 2007-09-30
As a fellow Ubuntu Server programmer, you may be in a startup company and want to grab lucrative USA Federal Contracts (if you are USA-based). There are two avenues of which I am aware:

[LIST]
[*]FAR - Federal Acquisitions & Requisitions. A catalog where the USA Federal Government solicits RFPs (Requests For Proposals) for federal contracts.
[*]GSA Schedule - A catalog put out by the Government Services Administration of the Department of Defense. It lists businesses and services that are authorized to be purchased by agencies of the Federal Government.
[/LIST]

Of course, you're probably thinking you don't want to support the war, or perhaps that you do, and being listed on GSA or submitting a proposal out of an RFP in the FAR doesn't mean you have to be involved with that at all. For instance, you could design software to help the Fair Housing Authority be more efficient at satisfying home loan requests for low income families, or a system to track health problems for the National Institute of Health. Along the way, you can spread the good word about Ubuntu, and especially Ubuntu Server, which needs a boost in large enterprises and government agencies.

Anyway, that's about all I know about it, and I've had a friend (of whom I can no longer find) who once came into an office meeting we were having to shake our hands, tell us he was leaving, and the reason was because his startup company just landed a national contract that was proposed via an item in the FAR. His startup partnership did extremely well and we never saw him again.

So if any of you know of pitfalls with doing any programming contracts and services via FAR or GSA, or want to share the steps, please let me know. I'm interested in getting involved with this with my company, Volo (volosystems.com).

Thanks in advance.
