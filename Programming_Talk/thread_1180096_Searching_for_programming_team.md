### Post by rjack on 2009-06-07
Don't start a new project, follow an existing one.

Yes, follow not join.

Choose a $project you like.
Subscribe to the $project-devel mailing list and read things.
Follow upstream and try to figure out bugs and missing features.
Try to understant the development method and how the other developers behave.

When you find a bug or something that you think you could hack yourself, write code.
Test it and submit a patch to the $project-devel mailing list.
Be prepared to the replies, they can be not what you expect, but this is the part where you really learn something.
Don't flame. Never.
Follow suggestions, rewrite patches, hack and re-hack again. Try to have some fun while doing this.

goto 1 :D

A group of newbies can't go far. Actually, it's a miracle if they get to the point to really start a project.

(don't get it bad, I don't want to be rude or offensive, I'm just trying to be realistic)
