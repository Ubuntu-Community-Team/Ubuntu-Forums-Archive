### Post by samjh on 2009-05-06
C++ won't disappear to even diminish.  Although it's losing market on the desktop application side, it's stealing the embedded systems market away from C.  Where it loses out on the desktop, it will make up in embedded systems and mobile devices as they become powerful enough to not rely on assembly and C.

As for games, C++ will still be king for at least another five years.  C# and Managed DirectX is still in the realm of hobbyists and indie game producers.  It hasn't yet broken into the mainstream gaming market, let alone AAA game-makers.  It is gaining traction in tools market though (map editors, etc).  The thing about games is that demand for features - especially visual effects and physics - increase in proportion to available computing power.  So unlike other applications, games will continue to require highly resource-efficient programming languages even though other applications don't need them any more.  C++ is very ideal: object-oriented, very strong support from libraries and development tools, and resource-efficient.
