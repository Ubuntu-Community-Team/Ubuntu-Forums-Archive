### Post by Abras on 2008-09-19
If you mean the Atari 2600 (aka the VCS, their most popular game system) then yes, the games were coded in assembly. I believe their later systems (the 5200 and 7800) as well as their line of computers used assembly of some kind or another. Although, their computers probably had BASIC for hobbyists.

I really don't know too much about those things since they were before my time, but I  highly recommend you check out [Atari Age]("http://www.atariage.com/") if you want to learn more. That site has a ton of information and their forum members are very knowledgeable. It's pretty much the internet's epicenter for all things oldschool Atari. They even have active homebrew community that turns out at least a dozen releases each year. Many of those games make it on to carts and the site sells them at their store.

Personally, coding for the Atari seems like a lot of work for not much in return. Still, I can kind of understand what draws people to it. Nostalgia is part of it, but mostly I think it's the challenge it presents. Making an enjoyable and interesting game experience is difficult enough, but the challenge really ramps up when working with a machine with such limited specs. And they've definitely succeeded since many of their games are miles ahead of the old commercial releases. Even then, I say no thanks! I'll stick with Python.
