### Post by JupiterV2 on 2011-06-10
Wanted an ERP system for our very small at-home business. Wanted something simple my wife would use. Wanted something scaleable as the business grows. Wanted this feature and not that. To get the exact features we wanted, the fee for a commercial product was exorbitant. So, as all programmers do, when it doubt: Write one yourself!

So I started to design my DB schema, which got more and more complicated. I worked on some flow charts and what-not. And one day, while staring at my work, I came to the heart-sinking conclusion: I could do this in a spreadsheet in 15minutes. And I did. And it works great. /sigh

I still plan on creating a Small Business/Entreprenueral level ERP program but I way over-complicated the issue. Please, tell me I'm not the only one who falls victim to their own ideas of grandeur!

### Post by kjuergen on 2011-06-10
yeah, I learned that the hard way too but I really learned from it...... Today I start looking for an existing solution first and if I can't find one I look even harder! 

Even for simple use OpenERP (previously tinyERP) is a great solution and you only have to use the stuff you need. However, if your business grows (who doesn't want that) you won't outgrow it until you are a multi-national company and at that time you will have people to install your SAS system.

Opposite to a spreadsheet solution OpenERP will take care of all the database crap and if you want to add or change things it will modify/re-arrange the database with a few simple lines of code.
