### Post by Vyoma on 2008-11-29
I have this idea in my head, and am just brainstorming. As a part of brainstorming, I was mulling over where I should be hosting this particular project?

Being an avid Ubuntu user, I headed over to Launchpad and it seems to offer all that is required. :)

But then, there was another question that popped - what is the state of upstream push of these Launchpad projects?

From what I have been reading around, Ubuntu community seems to be doing good in terms of tracking bugs on different applications, and working on getting them upstream whereever appropriate. 

But what about projects as a whole? For instance, I am trying to build a project which should run on any GNU/Linux distribution that has Gnome as its desktop environment. Should I limit it to just Linux audience? Or should I try to get it into Debian repositories first?

No experience on that, but my common sense tells me you should first think on getting a stable staff of developers, have a product and then try to get it into a GNOME-based distro. If it happens that several distros adopt it into their default GNOME install, then maybe the GNOME developers may have a look at it...

And forget Debian repos for a while, you'll need to stick to a lot of standards, policies and such to get it accepted.
