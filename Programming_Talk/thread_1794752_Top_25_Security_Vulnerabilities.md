### Post by Dangertux on 2011-07-01
Good stuff. Just from the brief look I gave it, it is very informative for CSO's , CIO's, CTO's and developers alike. Some of these are very easy to fix, and in the end they all come back to weak coding. 

I think devs and corporations alike really need a wakeup call. SQL Injection, XSS , and CSRF , are killing corporations. When such "trivial" security holes are left unchecked and married to already weak security policies namely , weak password policies, underprotected root level assets (IE: failure to properly implement DEP/ASLR) , or just plain un-updated applications or poorly coded applications , it is a recipe for disaster. I'm looking at you Sony.

EDIT : A further thought on this subject. When looking at corporate risk management matrixes (usually for smaller companies) you will quickly see that they do not perceive the threat in a proper manner. For instance they tend to itemize these threats, as opposed to looking at how they impact eachother. As I stated above, these security threats all can have an escalatory effect on eachother. Something that many corporations don't take into account.
