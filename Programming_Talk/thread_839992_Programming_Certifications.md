### Post by raf952 on 2008-06-25
> **slavik said:**
> well, my job won't cover it at all, at this point I am trying to decide if it is worth the hassle at all. I do plan to ask some professors at my college.

You might consider joining the [Association for Computing Machinery]("http://www.acm.org") (ACM). For US$99/year (US$19 if you're a student) you get full and unlimited access to 1,100 online books from Safari and Books24x7 and 2,500 online courses from SkillSoft.

My regimen is to work on a CBT during my lunch hour. In just the past month I've completed a dozen (btw, I'm studying for the PMP certification).

Certifications by themselves are of arguable value. The key advantage they can provide is to broaden or deepen your exposure in technical or professional areas. It exposes you to concepts and terminology and demonstrates you've learned enough to pass a test, but doesn't guarantee you actually can apply what you've learned.

As an IT manager I always ask during interviews, "what do you do to keep your skills sharpened?" Responding that you regularly take CBTs and have gotten xyz certifications in areas you have passion about generally says a lot more than that you surf blogs and occasionally post to technical forums :)

Definitely check out ACM. For just the online book and course benefit it's an incredible bargain--especially if you're a student.

Good luck!
