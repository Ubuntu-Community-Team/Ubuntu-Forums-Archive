As of 3 December 2011, I am considering the purchase, via eBay®, of a used Logitech® Y-UW92 encountered overseas and not on the United States domestic market; it packs a modification of the United States layout with two Backslash/shift-Vertical Bar keys, narrow left shift, and tall/narrow NewLine (per ISO standard layout).  As I understand things, LinUX uses two programs to load keymaps: the Keyboard Daemon (/sbin/kbd) and X Keyboard Mapper (/usr/sbin/xkb).

Which files will need modification to amend the keymap(s) for an ISO, rather than ANSI X4.22 standard, 'board?  (I can find reference data online for the keywords necessary for program-command and cursor-control keys and so forth, which I anticipate needing for future installations involving semi-custom 122-key (U. S. International) and full-custom 127-key (JIS, improved from specific International Business Machines® 5556/5576 subtypes) Unicomp® On The Stick&#8482; Emulator&#8482; models.)

**Update:**  The actual keybpard is a Logitech® PID# SC81115009W, up and running as of 11 December 2011.

**Update 2:**  All key characters correspond to the keycaps this unit, with the exception of the "Backslash/Bar" key alongside the Left Shift; the assigned characters for this key are Less/shift-Greater (as used with International Business Machines® P/N 1397000 and similar, for PC's doubling as EBCDIC smart terminals using X3270, TN3270 and similar applications).
