### Post by nathan.the.sane on 2012-01-03
For some reason, I find that I can't focus on programming for more than a couple minutes before compulsively alt + tabbing over to Firefox or Banshee and killing time. I'll be in the middle of writing a function and find myself checking Slashdot even though I know nothing major could have happened in the 10 minutes since I last refreshed it. It's not like I'm trying to let an algorithm work itself out in my head or something; I know what the code needs to be doing I just can't seem to buckle down and get it written out. Also, I have a hard time finishing projects in general; I get tired of them and move on to something else. Does anyone else have this problem, and what are some ways to fix this and focus on the task at hand? I only program as a hobby so I'm not about to lose my job over this or anything, but I feel like I have the skill to produce a large-scale app or something if I could only find some sort of motivational spark to push it to completion. :???:

### Post by Zugzwang on 2012-01-03
Here is an idea: listen to some music that you really know well. Intuitively, this way, you keep your brain cells that are not needed during programming busy, but at the same time you are not distracted by something that is new to you. Of course this reason does not make sense from a scientific point of view. ;-)

### Post by CoffeeRain on 2012-01-03
I sometimes have the same problem. I assume because you program as a hobby that all of your projects seem fun. Are you working with any particular language or languages? If the language does not suit you or your project, that could be part of it. I have started many projects as hobbies, but not finished them just because I didn't feel like they were important. I have a Gmail app I'm trying to make in Python, and I think that if I use it all the time, it would become useful. You might try making smaller apps or programs and use them a lot.
