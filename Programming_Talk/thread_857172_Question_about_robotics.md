### Post by hod139 on 2008-07-12
There is currently only one school in the US offering an undergraduate robotics degree, Worcester Polytechnic Institute.  This is a very new program and many other schools are watching it.

If you are coming for graduate school, then there are many schools that will offer a graduate degree in robotics (e.g. CMU).  However, most schools will still grant a computer science or an engineering degree, even when studying robotics.  Some of the best robotics labs in the country are, of course, associated with some of the best schools: CMU, UPENN, Stanford, UC Berkely, Harvard, MIT, CalTech, UMICH, etc. 

I suggest you find your interest in robotics (CS, EE, or ME) and then look for a program that specializes in that.  You can also always post back here, as I am involved in robotics community ([http://www.cs.rpi.edu/~sberard/](http://www.cs.rpi.edu/~sberard/)) and can try and offer some advice.
