### Post by Moonbuzz on 2006-04-27
Depends on what you need. For a full blown, fully featured, fully supported compiler/environment, you'd need to buy one of the major players' (i.e. MicroFocus, AcuCOBOL, Fujitsu, etc.) software. For learning and practicing purposes, you can use [TinyCobol]("http://tinycobol.org"). It's hardly the best thing out there (as it's only implementing the 85 standard, and isn't complete), but it's the best free (as in [sugar-free]("http://gnu.org")) GNU/Linux compiler out there.
