### Post by NovaAesa on 2010-04-06
I'm surprised that you are getting to choose between them rather than having to do both. The equivalents at the University of Newcastle are both compulsory for CompSci.

Having done the equivalent of both courses, I can say that I enjoyed algorithmics more. I would say that algorithmics would be harder than OS though because there is much more maths involved (maybe you are good at maths though).

They both sound pretty important. Your major is application programming, so IMO it's pretty important to know things at the OS level (such as multithreading, synchronisation, deadlocks etc). But then again, with application programming you would also have to have a reasonably idea of what is happening with all the different data structures and algorithms that work with them.

### Post by Some Penguin on 2010-04-06
Both are rather useful, but in somewhat different ways.  Understanding the whole stack (such as the operating system and the architecture of the hardware you're operating on) is useful for understanding why your system is behaving as it is (and thus detecting otherwise non-obvious bugs or performance issues) -- but understanding algorithms and having a good analytical mind are probably more immediately useful, since if you're having difficulty figuring out how to sanely decompose what it is you want to do into reasonable pieces and how to approach them, you probably should worry about that first.
