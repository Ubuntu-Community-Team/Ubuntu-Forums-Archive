### Post by Zugzwang on 2008-02-11
Accoding to this [compatibility table]("http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-faq.html#compat-matrix-footnote-7"), he has to switch to GPL to use your code. 

That's by the way one of the major "properties" of GPL: It's very "virulent", so you can't publish any work based on GPL under some different license (except for later versions of the GPL if the individual project allows this).

### Post by pmasiar on 2008-02-11
You should contact that coder, and explain (using provided link) that code released under GPL can be switched to LGPL only by original copyright holder (you), and ask (1) for a compensation (fee) to switch to LGPL, if you want it; or (2) require that other program be released under GPL only, because it is based on your code; (3) completely recode their code, not using any of your program.  If that rogue coder will not agree, you can escalate: notify devel list, notify users, contact groklaw website to help you find a pro-bono lawyer to explain them that they are wrong.

Let us know what was the result.
