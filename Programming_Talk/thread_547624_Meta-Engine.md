### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-09-10
Why do you need the word 'engine' in the name? It's clunky and annoying. 'Google' is incredibly easy to say, as is 'Yahoo'.

How about 'MetaSearch' or 'Lookout' or something just plain random? A name is only a name, it doesn't have to be descriptive. Make it memorable, and people will keep coming back. 'Meta-engine' just doesn't sound all that great, to be honest.

I don't think many websites would object to you using a short section of text as a 'preview' to go along with the link. If you copy the whole thing, though, I think it will still count as infringement even if you put a link to the source. You can't copy an entire research paper, and then put a link to the original web-page it was featured on, for example - without the copyright holder's permission.
