### Post by TheFu on 2021-07-28
Part 2

VPNs that only use usernames and passwords are not very secure.
SSL-based VPNs aren't usually secure against state-level monitoring, unless they use TLS 1.3.  Earlier versions, including all SSL, have all been cracked.

---

### Post by TheFu on 2021-07-28
Part 3

Security professionals suggest using IPSec-based VPNs with 2FA.  IPSec is part of the IPv6 standard and has been available and working for over 15 yrs. Android and iOS clients have native support for IPSec VPNs.
