### Post by rplantz on 2006-10-29
I started programming (in FORTRAN) when I was an undergraduate in electrical engineering at Berkeley in 1961. After graduating in 1962, I applied my skills to a program to design part of the (analog) guidance system for the Gemini space capsule.

I have been programming ever since, mostly in (several) assembly languages. I wrote programs as part of my research in grad school. Then I worked as a programmer in industry for ten years. My final career was a CS professor at a university.

I've been retired for a couple of years and now program for fun. One of these days, I hope to be able to contribute some of my programming skills to the Ubuntu community. But so far I've been very busy with other projects in my retirement. I'm beginning to wonder how I ever found enough time to work!
