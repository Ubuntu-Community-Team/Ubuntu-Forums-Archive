### Post by TheFu on 2018-06-04
18.10 hasn't been released.   Someone new to development should probably stay on 16.04 and I'd suggest the KDE or Gnome GUIs. There are enough surprises with development. You don't need fresh OS surprises in addition.  Even 18.04 is a little new.  Customers (the sort that pay) care about support on LTS releases that they actually use.  Businesses won't move desktops to 18.04 for at least a few months, some will not for another year.  They will be on 16.04 and some will be on 14.04 still, planning to move to 16.04 this year.

New is the enemy of stable.

I would look at other systems which do what you want and see how they did it.  Coding a GUI isn't the easiest way to learn a language.   Ubuntu has a developer website. Google should find it easily.  They have simple ways to install developer tools and some how-to guides.

Ubuntu forums doesn't allow cross-posting.  Pick 1 subforum for a post. Anything development related belongs in the development subforum.
