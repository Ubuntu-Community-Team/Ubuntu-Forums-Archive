### Post by Abras on 2008-09-28
Hi guys,

A few months ago I came across this really neat little site called [Cymon's Games]("http://cymonsgames.retroremakes.com/"). It's devoted to little type-in games written in C/C++ (many of them rewrites of old BASIC games but there are a few originals).  Now, those old magazine type-ins were way before my time, but I always liked the idea. Actually I'm pretty surprised that no one had had the idea of bringing type-ins back with a modern language before now. So it's good to see, but I'm a bit worried...

The site is run by one guy and he puts a lot of time and effort into it, releasing one program a week and with a number of side projects to keep him busy. Naturally he's really hoping it takes off, but so far there don't seem to be too many interested. Which makes me wonder how long he'll be able to keep this up before he inevitably throws up his hands and abandons the project completely.

For my part, I guess I'm one of the few who people who actually enjoys the site, and I decided to help it out a little by posting here. I figured the open source crowd here would especially appreciate the project since all the source code is open and ready to be fiddled with. And he openly encourages submissions (in any language) and suggestions from other people. At the very least you can reply here and tell me what you think of the site. Really, I just can't understand why it isn't more popular... :confused:

Note: Just to stop any speculation before it starts, I have no connection with the site or the guy that runs it. I just like the site and want it to get more traffic and maybe a few helping hands. So I hope you don't think of this as spam...
