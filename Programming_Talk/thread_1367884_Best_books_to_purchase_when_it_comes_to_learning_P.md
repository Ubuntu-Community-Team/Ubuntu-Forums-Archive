### Post by matsuzine on 2009-12-30
If you truly are a beginner, I might recommend "Programming Python for the Absolute Beginner".  ISBN  978-1592000739.  It will give you a very gentle learning curve and all of the examples are games, which is fun.

Otherwise, for something completely different, try Mark Lutz's "Learning Python" ISBN 978-0596158064.  It's a pretty big book, and will serve you much further along the learning curve.  It might take a bit more investment than the first book, though.  

If you want a great introduction to basic programming concepts ( not in Python, though, all of the examples are in Ruby) try Chris Pine's "Learn to Program".  You can buy the book, but an older edition is also available online at [http://pine.fm/LearnToProgram/](http://pine.fm/LearnToProgram/).

Have fun!
