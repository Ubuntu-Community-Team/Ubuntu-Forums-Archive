### Post by Tony Flury on 2009-10-06
It really all does depend on what you are interested in. Databases and GUIs can be used for *almost* anything - if you want to spend the time developing it.

You may as well ask for a wood-working project that uses a hammer and a screwdriver.

However some ideas - in sort of ascending order of difficulty (i think) : 

1) A simple system to track your CD and DVD collection.
2) A simple system to track your books.
3) A simple system to track something else you collect
4) A system to track, report and compare the results and performance of individual member in a local sports team (bowling is relatively easy).
5) A simple library system for a local club.
6) A system to maintain a sports league creating a schedule of matches, recording results and producing the league tables
7) A Genealogical database so that you can record your family tree.
8) A Gui that makes creating databases easier

And anything else that takes your fancy.
