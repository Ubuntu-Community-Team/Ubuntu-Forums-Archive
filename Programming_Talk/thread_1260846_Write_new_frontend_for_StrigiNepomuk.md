### Post by haTem on 2009-09-09
Definitely check out kde's techbase, it has a lot of good resources. Once you've got a decent grip on python, you should probably take a look at PyQt so you can build the gui for your frontend (if I understood you correctly).

[http://techbase.kde.org/Development/Tutorials#Python](http://techbase.kde.org/Development/Tutorials#Python)

There are also a bunch of articles on strigi/nepomuk that you may find interesting. They all seem to be in C++ though, and I am not sure if there are python bindings around. You'll probably have more luck asking around on irc or the appropriate mailing lists than you would in this forum. Try #kde-devel on freenode.

[http://techbase.kde.org/Development/Tutorials/Metadata/Nepomuk/Resources](http://techbase.kde.org/Development/Tutorials/Metadata/Nepomuk/Resources)

I would recommend starting small. Don't jump into this project directly, or you might get lost and lose interest. Learn how the various frameworks work by making small, simple test apps that are relevant to your goal and you'll eventually get there. Good luck!
