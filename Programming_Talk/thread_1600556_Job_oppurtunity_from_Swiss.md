### Post by dwhitney67 on 2010-10-19
The term IT, of Information Technology, is overused, and over broad.  It could mean anything related to the use of computers or the transmission of data between computers.

If a person is interested in developing their skills as a programmer, the first thing I would recommend is that you attend a reputable university or college where you will learn the fundamentals of Computer Science.  Programming language skills amount to squat if you cannot convert project requirements into a viable design.

Now as far as financial institutions are concerned, some reputable ones do hire software engineers.  I know that Bloomberg (sp?) in NYC is looking for C++/Linux/Posix developers.  Conducting electronic trading is in high demand, and well, servers, software, etc all make it happen.

As for taking the job in Switzerland, why would you not take it? It would offer you good work experience, a cultural experience, and maybe even provide you with the opportunity to attend a university where you can obtain a degree in CS.
