### Post by Unterseeboot_234 on 2006-09-29
I share what has struck my interest thus far...

[http://www.blassic.org/](http://www.blassic.org/)
Is a society with a special compiler, thus some of the add-ons are not relevant for the BASIC in OOo. They list definitions for about 80% of they keywords.

[http://www.pitonyak.org/book/](http://www.pitonyak.org/book/)
Author's website lists places to buy his book. The publisher of his book has two chapters for .pdf download. The author has links for a very rough draft for a Base book he may or may not finish in .pdf. If you buy Andrew Pitonyak's "OpenOffice.org Macros Explained" and register the copy you get a password for the entire .pdf download. Book reviews of people that bought it on Amazon.com said it was dated, however the macros haven't changed much.

If I google the newsgroups, there were groups that started up with the 1.0 and 2.0 OOo. Less than 25 dead maillists. Funny, one group wanted $ collections to advertise in New York newspaper about OOo as a FREE alternative. 

I did note there are more German books and academic downloads about OOo. I want English text for sanity.

I'll finish by saying: the macros look like a feature that could customize OOo so that whatever project appears to be a standalone application without all the memory overhead.
