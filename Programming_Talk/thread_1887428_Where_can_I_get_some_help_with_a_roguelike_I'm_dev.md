### Post by DangerOnTheRanger on 2011-11-27
I personally think the best course of action is to *not ask for any help at all* at this stage, and just develop it by yourself for a year or two. It sounds weird, I know, but here's why:
[LIST]
[*]You have very little (if any) project planning skills; adding more people to your project at this stage would merely slow you down
[*]Your project probably doesn't require more than one team member at this stage; I developed [this]("http://openblox.sourceforge.net") for around a year before someone else started to help me out (and that was with testing). I speak from experience - you should be able to do %99 of your roguelike without any help whatsoever
[*]Handling a team requires setting up an infrastructure that can coordinate them; this also requires time and effort. I'm not sure maintaining an issue tracker and/or mailing list is something you want to do at this stage
[/LIST]
At this stage, I predict a lot of would-be helpers won't chip in because they cannot see you've done any work on it yourself; you say you've written a map generator, but you haven't posted it for download. I'm not saying you didn't write one, I'm just saying your basically asking people to trust that you'll see this project to the end and won't bail out on them halfway through, that's all. Because of that, I recommend developing it yourself for 1 or 2 years, and *then* requesting assistance; you'll find once people see you actually have something tangible to show them, they will ask *you* to join on their own accord. This is what all those places you visited were trying to tell you.

I know, having a team working on a FOSS project you created feels great - I can relate to this - but I don't think you need one at this stage.
