### Post by amoeen on 2009-05-21
[ScrumEdge ]("http://www.scrumedge.com")is a collaborative tool that allows teams, scrum masters, and stakeholders to manage the Scrum lifecycle at the product, release and sprint levels.

[ScrumEdge ]("http://www.scrumedge.com")enables agile development teams to better organize and manage project deliverables, allowing them to deliver high quality products in short time-frames. 

With [ScrumEdge ]("http://www.scrumedge.com")you'll no longer need to deal with shared excel-sheets or walls covered in post-its! The icing on the cake is the ease with which Scrum Masters and Team Members can access their data and view a project's progress from any location; office, home or even while traveling!

Scrum Masters can sign up to [ScrumEdge ]("http://www.scrumedge.com")and create their scrum teams and scrum projects. Once a scrum team member has been assigned tasks for a sprint, they can sign in and view their sprint log, their burndown charts, budget charts and burn rate charts. 

Give [ScrumEdge ]("http://www.scrumedge.com")and shot and let me know what you think.

### Post by amoeen on 2010-08-10
Carlin, have you seen the new version of [Scrumedge]("http://www.scrumedge.com")? It seems that the developers read your comments and decided to change the navigational elements :)

In the new version you start off by selecting a project (if you have added one) and then working inside that project i.e. building the product backlog, creating sprints, adding tasks, etc. 

The project level dashboard also allows you to view all tasks for a given sprint with one click. Progress bars help gauge how well (or poor) your sprint is doing. Similarly, you can view progress on a particular story in your backlog with one click.

I would recommend that you take the free online demo to see how the features and navigation scheme have evolved. You can also read about this update at [http://blog.scrumedge.com/2010/05/new-version-of-scrumedge-goes-live.html](http://blog.scrumedge.com/2010/05/new-version-of-scrumedge-goes-live.html)
