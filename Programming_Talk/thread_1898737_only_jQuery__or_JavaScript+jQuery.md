### Post by Frak on 2011-12-22
I don't see anything wrong with learning JQuery "first". In essence, you'll be learning the two in parallel, since JQuery isn't a replacement, nor a superset, of Javascript in the way Obj-J is. JQuery is a *compliment* to Javascript in order to ease the development of commonly repeated tasks.

So, yeah, go ahead and take a dive into JQuery. Over time, you should be able to explore the inner workings and figure out the "JQuery way" on your own. ;)

Diving into JQuery is a good book on JQuery. In terms of learning Javascript, I would happily say that Envato's Nettuts site is an excellent source for its "Beginning Javascript" series of video casts (for those who are visual/lecture learners).

Cheers to your studies!
