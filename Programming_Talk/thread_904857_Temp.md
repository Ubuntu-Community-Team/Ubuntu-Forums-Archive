### Post by pp. on 2008-08-30
The basic premise in the OP is somewhat incomplete. 

The point of many of the discussions we see here in the OPP does not consist of preparing well-researched definite truths for future seekers of advice to unearth and peruse.

Rather, I perceive those discussions as attempts to find out how currently active members of the forum relate to certain topics and reply to certain more or less thought out statements.

Seen in this light, every new thread about - say - abortion, gun control or the sanity of inhabitants or the leaders of this nation or that does indeed create new insights. The insight, however, is not about those topics but about the people posting in the thread. That's a good thing, for two reasons. 

One: People are the most interesting and the most important topic there is.

Two: Most people joining those discussion are utterly unqualified to contribute anything whatsoever to the topic being discussed. Most, I said, not all.

As a side effect, it possibly could occur that someone fetches a genuine answer to something she posted here which is indeed food for thought. Since the mind does not easily suffer from obesity, food for thought is a fine thing to have.

Hence, suppressing similar topics or lumping them into megathreads would get rid of many threads including some possible flame wars. It would also get rid of the main purpose of the OPP that I can see.

Staff becoming so entangled in a thread that they won't be accepted as neutral parties isn't unheard of. This does not restrict itself to the OPP forum, either. If I remember correctly, the Programming place also has some heated debates at times.

I have no solution. I can see that the role of staff can drift slowly and imperceptibly from contributing (to the subject matter) to becoming the target of aggression or to trying to moderate the thread.

Not having a solution is not a problem for me. I think that situation enlivenes the place. Also, I see no particular point in barring staff from joining the fun. They spend enough time in this forum trying to be useful and helpful, mostly with success. Don't bind the mouth ...

And my heartfelt thanks to all staff who spend untold hours here.
