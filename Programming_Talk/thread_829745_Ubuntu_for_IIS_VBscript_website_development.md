### Post by Mickeysofine1972 on 2008-06-15
Well first off welcome to a whole new world of opportunity!

I made the switch from ASP to PHP a few years back as I found whilst there is ASP support in the for of chillisofts asp modules for apache, they are not very easy to get hold of.

Also, I did find that I now have more job opportunities as a result! (in the UK anyway), as there arent that many people asking for 'Classic' ASP anymore. Most want VB.NET if they ask for a microsoft based website.

However the jump from Classic ASP to PHP isn't that harsh as alot of the things you do in ASP, like creating database connection and doing queries are actually easier under PHP/MySQL, (Well thats my opinion and a lot of others too).

If you do decide to to give PHP a try I recommend the gPHPEdit program from the Ubuntu repositories which is what I use at work and home.

I think in terms of self employment and dealing with customers requests, they generally favour a PHP/Apache/MySQL based site as it generally is cheaper for them as the only thing they have to pay for is the hosting and your development costs.Using MS stuff you may have to buy thing like dreamweaver etc. Thats only my experience from when I first switched over though. 

All in all it is possibly one of the best things I ever did career wise and also on a personal level I really like the freedom you get as a developer from using Open Source development tools.

Hope this helps you in some way

Mike
