### Post by aks44 on 2008-01-19
[OSS Nokalva]("http://www.oss.com/asn1/") looks like a very good resource. Amongst other things, it offers a free download of [Olivier Dubuisson's book]("http://www.oss.com/asn1/dubuisson.html") (one of the leading french ASN.1 engineers) which looks quite suitable for beginners:
> Mr. Dubuisson's book explains ASN.1 and its encoding rules in easy-to-understand terms. It addresses the subject at both an introductory level that is suitable for beginners, and at a more detailed level that is meant for those who seek a deeper understanding of ASN.1 and the encoding rules.


[http://asn1.elibel.tm.fr/](http://asn1.elibel.tm.fr/) has plenty of resources (even tutorials). Don't be frightened by the .fr domain, the site is available in english. ;)


The [ASN.1 Consortium site]("http://www.asn1.org/") may also be a good place for finding information.


Hope this helps.

(FWIW, please keep in mind that I have absolutely no practical experience of ASN.1, I just got interested in it a few years ago only to realize that it wasn't what I needed at that time. I still have my own [IANA]("http://www.iana.org/")-assigned Enterprise OID namespace though :))
