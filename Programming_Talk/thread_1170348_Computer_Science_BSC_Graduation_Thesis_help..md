### Post by stefangr1 on 2009-05-26
Can't you first pick out a very broad subject, and ask for a professor you like? This still gives you some options to choose your subject later on. Choosing a topic is something different than choosing a specific research question. In fact, on my university the initial choice of the students is only meant to make a match with the specializations of the professors. As the work proceeds, and you become more familiar with the subject, you probably find out what's really interesting about it and then rephrase the topic of the paper a little.

My experience is that people (actually especially those higher in the tree) find it flattering to be asked by a student, you can choose a supervisor that you like, and it shows that you are the active kind of student.
