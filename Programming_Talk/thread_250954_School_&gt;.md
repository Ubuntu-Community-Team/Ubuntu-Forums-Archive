### Post by sysops on 2006-09-05
Put yourself in the shoes of a curriculum designer, you want your students to be apt programmers at the end of the course, do you start them off with an object oriented langiage like python or a procedural language like basic or pascal/c. In most cases course designers know that the latter are better aligned with a novice's way of implementing simple algorithms. So you start them off with what's easy for them and as they progress you put them on with what's obviously on the next level and current with today's requirements. Also, procedural programming is the basis for other programmming paradigms like functional, structured and object oriented programming. Makes sense to learn it first.

To answer the question - pascal is a breeze but it gets tougher as with any other languages as you progress on from the basics but you can be on top of it all as long as you do your homework ;).
