### Post by pmasiar on 2007-10-30
This forum is great, but lacks karma: sometimes for a newcomer, especially a beginner in the field, it is not easy to distinguish good advice from a bad, expert from a random advice of passer-by. No way to **establish credentials**, prove (or at least suggest) that someone's advice can be trusted more than other random person.

Everyone has an agenda (if someone says not, it's a lie right there :-) ). So I think we should have sticky, where "experts" (or plain regulars - anyone who cares to comment) can write about who they are, and what is their area of expertize in which they can be trusted, and possibly mentioned other regulars whose opinions they value. it does not mean that "experts"/regulars are always right, or forum is closed club. It is just that some regulars, by repeatedly posting mature posts with insight and good information, established credentials to other people - network of trust id you wish. You, newcomer, may or may not trust - or "expert" may have bad day and be completely wrong.

**This is not discussion thread**, please keep thread on topic: regulars explaining what they are. If you want discussion about merits (or foolishness :-) ) of having such thread, please [go to discussion thread](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=597643) 

Every poster could also start own thread about discussing own "credentials" if s/he chooses so, I did.

Also, this is not to establish classes of members or some elite/dictatorship. Sole reason for this post is, newcomer to this forum can browse and have idea who regulars are, and how relevant given advice might be. So regulars (spending way much more time that they should :-) ) can avoid shouting matches, and have central place to post good links explaining their POV. Please understand, regulars invested lots of time to establish credentials, and are interested to maintain that, but random posters do not care about the misunderstanding their sometimes wrong posts might cause.

If someone has no entry here, it does not mean his or her advice is automatically wrong. It just means that s/he did not bothered to comment here. It might be Linus Jr.! :-)

Uff, such load of legalese disclaimers! But as a consequence to Murphy's Law: any thought expressed is also misunderstood, and you can read flamewars in discussion for comment #2. :-)
