### Post by dbd on 2009-08-20
If you do want to get into programming professionally then one thing which will really help out your CV is being able to show that you really care about, and enjoy programing. A great way to do that (presuming you have some spare time) is to contribute to open source projects. That will give you valuable experience programming with people, working on other people's code, on projects that are not just toy projects but have actual users, and you'll get to help the open source community at the same time! 

When I had an interview for my first (and only, I've left the industry, but why isn't relevant here) programming job the interviewers were very impressed by the fact that I'd done open source work. Nothing says that you are dedicated to something better than the fact that you've been doing it at a professionaly standard for free, and for fun! 

But when looking for your first job you will be restricted to employers who are happy to look past your lack of a CS major, and I'm not sure how many of them they'll be. Perhaps you should go for programming jobs which require not only programming skills, but specialised skills in another area which you also have experience in (say finance, or if that isn't an option, perhaps even a hobby of yours which has given you useful experience).
