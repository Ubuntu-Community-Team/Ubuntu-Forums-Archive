### Post by Dangertux on 2011-11-23
Seriously, if you want to become a TRUE expert. Figure out a way to get sysadmins to pull their head's out of wherever it is they have them stuck. Why is it they are afraid of Lulzsec? For that matter, why is it anyone is afraid of SQL injection. This is 2011 not 2003. SQL injection and similar attacks that have been utilized by Lulzsec and our favorite Guy Fawkes mask sporting teenagers are NOT new. 

I can tell you why it's happening too. As always sysadmins and everyone else are looking for security in a box, they want a device that sits on their network with a little display that says "you're safe". It doesn't exist. Most networks have several different IDS, IPS and firewalls. They use web application firewalls or things like cloud flare. You know what? I'd be willing to bet a considerable amount of money that every single one of them who has gotten "owned for the lulz" did not do anything but leave those devices in their default configuration.

Everyone acts like infosec is cutting edge. Sure there are some bleeding edge attacks. But the truth is the average attacker doesn't have to be a genius, he just has to be smarter than you. In the case of most sysadmins that's not very smart. Stop trying to buy security in a box. Understand the threats that apply to you, educate yourself, write meaningful policies, educate your users.  You know what the biggest threat to the network of whatever office or school you all go to is? You are. Because you like facebook, twitter, and click every single thing someone you 'trust' sends you.

There are all sorts of devices for securing your network. However there isn't a single device or software package on the market that will secure you from laziness or outright stupidity.

Hope this helps.
