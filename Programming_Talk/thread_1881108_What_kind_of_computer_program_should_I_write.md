### Post by epicoder on 2011-11-15
If you are looking to make money, then Ms. Daisy is right. There is plenty of money to be had there with the large user base. You'd be surprised what people pay money for. (See the beer app on iTunes)

Another big market is games. Windows obviously is the biggest market there, but again, phones/tablets have a huge share as well. Something as simple as a tower defense game with good art (see Plants vs. Zombies) can make you a killing in this market.

You could also write a utility for Windows (registry cleaner, virus removal, etc.) However, this market is largely dominated by free (as in beer, not as in speech) software such as CCleaner and Avast antivirus. People also tend to shy away from paid software in this category because of the large amount of [scareware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scareware") and [rogueware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rogue_security_software").

I'm no expert on this field; take my advice with a grain of salt and do what you want to do. You'll find someone to buy it.

### Post by 11jmb on 2011-11-15
There is an unbelievable amount of luck involved in making it big.

Games are always a simple, popular way if you want to make money. Just look at angry birds as an example. There is noting spectacular about that game. It is simple, addictive, and worth millions now. Developing a game towards mobile platforms is almost a must, though, if you are looking for profit.

Another way would be to look for a small market gap and try to exploit it. Everybody is on the social networking bandwagon, so that gravy train might be full. A bit of foresight may help your apps make it, but again, there is an unbelievable amount of luck involved.
