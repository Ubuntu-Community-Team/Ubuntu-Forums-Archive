### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2010-03-03
Pro Tip:  When you are looking for the right IDE for *you*, you should be specific about what your needs and preferences are.  Otherwise you get a thread of people sounding off on their favorite text editor, which evolves into a vim/emacs/geany/gedit/kate/eclipse flamewar.

So answer this:
 - What kind of projects are you going to work on?
 - What have you used before?
 - What tools do you need available?
 - What toolkits/API/databases are you going to work with?
 - What attributes are most important to you (speed, stability, features, appearance, etc)?

Then you'll get useful responses.
