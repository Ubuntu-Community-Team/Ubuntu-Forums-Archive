### Post by VforVincent on 2014-12-08
I'd like to get into [Rust]("http://www.rust-lang.org"). But frankly, I find learning languages on my own to be kind of ineffective. So here's a proposal for anyone who shares my interest in Mozilla's new language.
 We find a group of people (anywhere up from three would be okay) who  can still pick something up from doing simple projects. We each work on an implementation of the same basic concept for two  weeks max and then submit a source code package. It doesn't have to be great - it doesn't even have to work - but it has  to be something the other participants can give feedback on. In the third week, we each study the work of two other participants  (code, but also project structure and approach to testing,...). We come up with a list of questions related to the implementation (how a  particular language concept works, why it was preferred,...). The idea is not to correct the other person's work, but to find out why  they did it in a particular way. We send that list to the others, and in the fourth week everyone answers  the questions they were asked.
 For now, I suggest we all pick *one* topic from the list below and tell us how to contact you (can be "in the current thread" or "via PM on this forum").

 
[LIST]
[*]static site generator
[*]simple 2D game
[*]text adventure
[*]Lisp/Prolog interpreter
[*]elementary 3D renderer
[*]game of life
[*]text editor
[*]mini-minecraft
[/LIST]


 The reason why I would like everyone to just pick one topic is to  avoid the overhead of organizing an open, cross-website poll. If the idea is a (moderate) success, we can formalize it by setting up a  centralized website with a project voting system and what have you.

 Voting closes on December 15, GMT+1.
