### Post by doorman on 2009-02-01
apart from being much more lighter than an IDE, vim lets you have full control over the coding & compiling process - in an IDE you write the code, and then click "compile" or "run" without fully comprehending what is going on in the background. On the other hand, if you write your code in vim, you have to compile it yourself, thus having more insight in the compiling process. Ofcourse, there are plug-ins that facilitate the compiling and/or testing task, but in general, when starting off in vim you have just the plain editor and that's it.

I think vim is a much better road to take when starting off programming as it gets you full control over your programs.

Also, when you feel confident enough in vim, check out their website, as there are a lot of plug-ins that make your life & coding easier. What's more, you can create a fully blown IDE inside vim itself, as there are plug-ins with all the features a modern ID has (code competetion, project management, compiling routines, etc.)

### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-02-01
I like Vim because I'm easily distracted by shiny buttons and boxes in full IDEs. My coding speed has increased significantly since I started using Vim, though not noticeably at first. The plugins and ability to customize Vim are major selling points for me as well. I also like to compile and debug my code myself.

It really comes down to preference. There are still some things I prefer an IDE for (Java), but do most of my projects in Vim. 

In general I feel that Vim/Emacs users are often misunderstood, especially when people focus on the "editor wars". From my experience, most Vim/Emacs users don't use their editor to feel superior to IDE users, they use their editor because it works for them.

In the end, choose what works for you.
