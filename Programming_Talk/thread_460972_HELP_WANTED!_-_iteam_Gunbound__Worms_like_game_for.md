### Post by taiyo on 2007-06-13
I would like to clear some things up...

As far as I perceived it, the original idea was to create a Gunbound(=! Worms)-like game.
Worms and Gunbound do co-exist, I played both and I do like both, but they differ from each other in a few points:

Worms
1-8 avatar per player
many different weapons
wind has effect on weapons
no items (the weapon you use never has an effect on how long it takes until you can make your next move)
no clothing which gives bonuses
normal health only measure of life
no respawn

Gunbound
1 and only 1 avatar per player
different vehicles, but each with one weapon which has 3 attack modes (each with a different delay)
wind and special "time of day" have an effect on weapons 
items which can be activated to give a temporary bonus (double-shoot, leech, teleport; these affect the delay until your next turn)
clothing/accessory which change damage/delay/armor...
different armor types: health/armor/shield ; penetration power of weapons relate to the enemies armor type
respawn possible

Even if they, theoretically, would directly compete, it wouldn't really matter. I wonder myself why there are thousands of distributions and dozens of editors with open source... but each of them serves a specific purpose... you get a choice... and this is what -free software- is about ;)
