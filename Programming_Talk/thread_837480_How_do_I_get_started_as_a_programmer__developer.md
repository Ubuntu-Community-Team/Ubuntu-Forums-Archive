### Post by pmasiar on 2008-06-22
I have to respond, you leave me no choice :-)

As usually, before giving advice, I'll prefer to ask questions, so my advice will be relevant to your needs, not to my bias :-)

OP: Do you want to become professional programmer, paid to develop apps? Or becoming hobby programmer, helping in free time to advance cause of Free Software (which is much easier) is all you want?

In either case, you want to learn basics of programming, and yes, Python is what I recommend. You need to become fairly proficient programmer before you are able to help with ubuntu packages.

But just FYI, you don't have to be expert code hacker to help FOSS: you can help beginners with installation issues, test beta versions, report errors, contribute to docs and FAQ. All this you can to without being expert hacker.

And you don't have to hack on ubuntu - with old hardware of yours, maybe some distro focused on old/limited HW could be better choice - and they need help more badly than ubuntu :-)

### Post by nvteighen on 2008-06-22
But, no matter what your preferences/goals/etc. are, the best way to learn programming is to write code.

What I (a middle-beginner, not an expert as the people you may meet here) recommend you is, whatever language you finally choose, stick to it with discipline and avoid "jumping" from language to language at all costs. You have to first learn how to program; then, after having developed skills, different projects will lead you into learning the most suitable language for that particular task.

Good luck! And never stop asking questions! Here you'll find very kind advice whenever you need it.
