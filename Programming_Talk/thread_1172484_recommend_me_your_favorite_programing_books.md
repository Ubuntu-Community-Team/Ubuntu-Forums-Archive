### Post by dandaman0061 on 2009-05-29
I highly recommend C++ Primer 4th Edition.
[http://www.amazon.com/Primer-4th-Stanley-B-Lippman/dp/0201721481/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1243572710&sr=8-1]("http://www.amazon.com/Primer-4th-Stanley-B-Lippman/dp/0201721481/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1243572710&sr=8-1")
I have read through many cpp books (including previous editions of this one) and this one seems to be the clearest, teaching you the proper/standard ways of programming with the standard libraries and then proceeds to teach the older (but still useful sometimes) ways of programming.

It has also been very, very useful as a reference.
