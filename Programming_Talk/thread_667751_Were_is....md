### Post by CptPicard on 2008-01-14
I am not too convinced of your hypothesis that the traditional corporate structure is neccessary for creating awesome things. Microsoft is actually awfully lame in innovation regardless of its size and both financial and human resources. If you insist on a corporate example, look at Google or Apple.

You admit that Vista isn't as good as Ubuntu and the latter is developed outside of Microsoft... on what basis is the bang for buck ratio better for Vista then, if Ubuntu's price is zero and Vista's quality is worse?

I'd recommend you read [http://www.amazon.com/Cathedral-Bazaar-Musings-Accidental-Revolutionary/dp/0596001088]("http://www.amazon.com/Cathedral-Bazaar-Musings-Accidental-Revolutionary/dp/0596001088")

As soon as Linux's market share increases significantly, we'll see incentives for corporate development of things like games, which tend not to be represented well in OSS circles as their development is intensive and they do not fit well into the picture of "commonly shared durable goods" that most large OSS projects are and where sharing benefits the  whole -- OS kernels, servers, compilers...
