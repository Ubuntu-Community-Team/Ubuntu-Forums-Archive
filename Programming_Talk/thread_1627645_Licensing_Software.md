### Post by shadylookin on 2010-11-21
GPL is a viral license that requires derivatives to also be GPL licensed. When people license under the GPL they want to make sure that anyone who uses their work as a base also has to release their work. This effectively drives the price of any GPL software down to zero rather quickly.

You could however release your own software under your own license. You could even release it under a license that allows people to look at the source for non-commercial uses

The GPL prevents anyone from taking your software, improving it, and then keeping the improvements secret(assuming they actually release it, they could in theory keep improvements in house and not distribute it). BSD/MIT style licenses are specifically written to not require others to re-release it as open source. 

Most people do no make money selling open source software, but instead by selling technical support for that software. Even more likely you'll work for a company that wants a certain feature added to an open source project and you'll be paid to implement that feature.

### Post by worseisworser on 2010-11-21
As a copyright owner of the software you've created you can dual-license it:
  [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-licensing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-licensing)

edit:
The reason this works is because companies often have no intention of giving back code, but are instead more willing to give back money. So either way you get *something* in return; code (their improvements and patches) or money.
