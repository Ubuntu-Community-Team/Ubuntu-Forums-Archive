### Post by Senesence on 2010-06-24
I read a few OpenGL books (ok, "skimmed"); I think they make better reference material than study guide.

OpenGL tutorials would probably be more helpful, because you wouldn't have to read over 300 pages before you can start putting something of substance on screen.

Also, OpenGL is just a graphics API, and games are so much more than just graphics.

Take a look here:
[http://lazyfoo.net/SDL_tutorials/index.php](http://lazyfoo.net/SDL_tutorials/index.php)

Only the [last lesson]("http://lazyfoo.net/SDL_tutorials/lesson36/index.php") is on OpenGL, but all the tutorials cover more than just simple rendering.

There is also a [very nice article]("http://lazyfoo.net/articles/article10/index.php") on OpenGL in general.

I hope that helps.
