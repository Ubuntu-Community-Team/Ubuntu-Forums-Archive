The most practical way to get experience before one finishes their university studies is to get hired as an intern.  For many companies, this is a chance to hire someone and pay them a minimal salary, yet at the same time groom them for possible full-time employment once the individual graduates from the university.

Most individuals that are hired for positions such as the one you saw listed in Craigslist, probably have most of their strengths in one or two particular languages, and glancing knowledge of the rest.

I find it comical when some employer wants to hire someone with a boat-load of skills, 5+ years experience, in an expensive metro area, and is offering a paltry salary of $60-70K per year. Seems to me that such employer is more interested in hiring an H1-B person.

Other, perhaps better, job boards for IT professionals in the US include:  Dice, Monster, CareerBuilder.

P.S.  If you have a squeaky clean background, no criminal record, etc., try to get a job (or internship) that will offer you the potential to get a security clearance.  Jobs that require clearances to access sensitive materials/information will never be shipped to a developing country.

### Post by NielsBhor on 2009-06-06
I agree with Slavik. Most employer realized a long time ago that their candidates are highly unlikely to meet all the skills they have requested. This is why a bachelors degree is need. Not only does it show you specialization, it also show that you're capable of learning other languages as well. 

They post those required skills to provide the candidate of what is expected. It's up to you if you'd like to work in those positions. Employers know you don't know all the languages posted or even had experience in such. They just want to know your motivation for the job. In fact, through experience with selecting candidates for a position, my company look closely at their enthusiasm for the position. They don't necessary meet all the skills requirements. It's common practice to list skills desire. However, it's impractical that the candidates knows all of them.
