### Post by nvteighen on 2010-10-09
This is about propierty. I remember an ISP that explicitly stated the router was licensed and was their propierty, not my family's; it featured a big sticker citing all possible laws and contractual stipulations... Not sure whether they were applicable or not, but they claimed that.

Check your contract and avoid having a nasty issue with your ISP.

Another possibility is that you buy a new router and play with that one. That router will be of your propierty and the worse you can do when modifying it is to void your warranty period.
