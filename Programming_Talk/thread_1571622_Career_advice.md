### Post by GenBattle on 2010-09-09
Finding jobs in software is a bit of a catch 22. If you go for the abundant jobs you have more to choose from, but you're competing against a huge pool of experienced developers. If you go for rare jobs (like electrical/embedded engineering you'll have trouble finding a job, but when you do you can assure you will be one of a very limited number of candidates.

I don't think Java or C# are on the decline yet, but they are both very mature. They are both used by a huge range of businesses and organisations for a range of reasons, but as other technologies improve in the next 5-10 years, java use may decline.

5 years out this dominance won't change unless Oracle catastrophically screws Java or something. Both are business languages made to suit business problems. If you're more interested in the pure web end, it pays to watch some of the newer frameworks like Ruby on Rails, etc. PHP is still reasonably popular for web development, but languages like Ruby and Python are making some progress. That said, this is all my opinion and made up of at least 70% speculation, so take it with a grain of salt :-P At the 5 year mark we're probably looking at a continued movement to web/cloud based services and program distribution through channels such as the the App store and Android market. Personally i think massively parallelized languages and tools will be the future by this point. Already things are becoming more about how much you can do in parallel, and i think this will only become more true as we approach the 20nm quantum tunneling limit of current chip manufacturing techniques. Languages and tools like Google Go and OpenCL will help to lead the expansion of mainstream parallel programming.

10 years in the future, who the hell knows what the world of computer software will be like. Personally i would put some time into languages like Ruby or Google Go which are just emerging now, but show great promise. Really though, you can't hope for any sort of accuracy when predicting what will happen this far out. 10 Years is a long time in the tech industry.

Opportunities arose not from being a coding monkey, but from taking the Accounting/Financial understanding and being able to build tools/reports/etc. on top of it to enhance what my co-workers are able to do.
