Our sticky, while trying to be simple, is making information hard to find by being too minimalist.  It creates an information bottleneck, as the one sticky contains headings and explanations far removed from the question that the beginner wishes to ask.  The more layers there are in FAQs, the harder it is to find what a person wants.  The more links someone has to click, the less inclined they will be to follow them.  They're just simple principles in information science and usability.

I propose that the one sticky be divided into four separate sticky-threads:
[list]
[*]HOWTO: Compilers, Editors, and IDEs (eg. gcc, vim, eclipse)
[*]HOWTO: Libraries and Other Tools (eg. GTK, Glade)
[*]COLLECTION: Books, Tutorials, and Challenges
[*]OTHER FAQs
[/list]
This arrangement will mean that guides to two most popular types of questions: how to compile and which editor to use, will be found easily.  The second very common questions: GTK, Qt, Boost, etc. are also easy to locate.  We can relegate links to books, tutorials, and our programming challenges in the "Collection: books, tutorials, and challenges".  Anything else can go in Other FAQs, although I strongly suspect it won't be looked at by most beginners.

There are some frequently asked questions unsuited for stickied FAQ threads, so we should be mindful not to become cranky librarians when they're asked repeatedly.  Examples include vim settings, A vs B polls, and questions about "which language/library?".  These will be asked even if we had a FAQ sticky with a title as big as our screen.

The "read the bloody FAQ" mentality needs to go.  The Unix/Linux community is blemished by RTFM screamers.  As much as it might stroke someone's ego to think they're somehow smarter than a novice, the attitude is discouraging and turns people away.  The more experienced members should cut some slack and not shoot someone for asking a question already in a sticky-thread.  Instead, just give a link to the appropriate thread.

Feedback and opinions welcome.

[EDIT: I think the Homework policy sticky should be a separate one too.]
