### Post by Max Luebbe on 2006-07-03
I agree with Asimon.

I'm an AI nut, and the Norvig book is one of my favorites.
I got a first edition on amazon not long ago for about $10 hardcover.
It has excellent coverage of a lot of topics.
(Norvig's a hotshot at Google now, too, he knows his stuff.)

The Russell book is also good, but I like Norvig's better for a coverage of the field.

If you are interested in cookbook stuff/algorithms, check out AI Application Programming (I forget the author). I think I got it on Amazon for $4 or something equally cheap.

I reccommend having a rock solid knowledge of discrete structures to try this stuff, especially trees, depth/breadth first search. Wouldn't hurt to memorize Dijkstra, Prim, Kruskal and as many other classic path algorithms either.

Hope this helps.
