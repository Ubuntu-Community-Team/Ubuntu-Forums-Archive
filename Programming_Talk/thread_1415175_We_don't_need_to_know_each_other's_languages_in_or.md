### Post by smmalmansoori on 2010-02-24
Hi everyone, I am thinking of developing this sometime in the future. Please provide your feedback.:P

Imagine a website is made where different people have certain questions in programming that needs to be answered, the only problem is that some may know English very well, some poorly, some not at all.

This is an example of what the website will provide.

1. Arabic user comes to the website with a question in mind, he/she finds a search field to write the question in.

2. As the user types the question, suggestions of related questions already asked and answered show up.

3. If one of the suggestions is what the user wants, it is chosen and the answer shows up, if not then the user submits the entered question.

4. The question is transferred to the "Q: Arabic Translation" section, any person who knows Arabic and another language can translate the question to the other language. 

5. Another user visits the "Q: Arabic Translation" section and translate the question to English. A copy of the question is made in the "Q: English" and "Q: English Translation". The question in "Q: English Translation" gets a star beside Arabic, and the same question in "Q: Arabic Translation" gets a star beside English, indicating that the question has been translated into those languages.

6. Another user visits the "Q: English Translation" section and translate the question to Spanish. A copy of the question is made in "Q: Spanish" and "Q: Spanish Translation", a star is added beside Arabic and English in the question in "Q: Spanish Translation", and a star is added beside Spanish in the question in "Q: Arabic Translation" and "Q: English Translation".

7. Another user visits the "Q: English" section and answers the question. A copy of the answer is made to "A: English" and "A: English Translation".

8. Another user visits the "A: English Translation" section and translate the answer to Arabic. A copy of the answer is made to "A: Arabic" and "A: Arabic Translation". The answer in "A: English Translation" gets a star beside Arabic, and the same answer in "A: Arabic Translation" gets a star beside English.

A lot of projects bet on collaborative editing and won:

[LIST]
[*]Wikipedia
[*]Google Maps
[*]etc
[/LIST]

### Post by CptPicard on 2010-02-24
Well... the rewards for translating other people's questions back and forth are few. Not sure if people would go for it, and it would take a long time before that would reach any kind of a critical mass to actually have any kind of meaningful knowledge base.

The far more important problem for someone who strives to become a programmer and doesn't speak English is documentation. All documentation tends to be in English, and it is almost impossible to be competent in any sense as a programmer without reading that documentation.

There already are educational materials in other languages in educational institutions, and programmers who do not speak English can form their own communities where they can share information using their own language. If a situation such as you're describing arises, I am sure some there is some English-speaker around who is able to find and answer and translate, even doing essentially what you're suggesting. There is no need for centralization.

### Post by nvteighen on 2010-02-25
The issue I see with this is that translation works under market rules. There are languages more "valued" than others because they have more relevance to a certain topic... As an extreme example, if I happen to post a question in Basque, I'll probably have to wait a lot longer to get someone translate that into English and someone else back to Basque than if I posted it in German or even Spanish. The result you'll get is that finally English, Spanish, German and *maybe* Chinese will end up being the sole competitors...

Take a look at Launchpad translations ([https://translations.launchpad.net/](https://translations.launchpad.net/)). Take any project using that service and observe to which languages the program has been translated completely or nearly to completion. Then look at the ones which have been uncompleted or even not even started for years... There you have a very accurate image of what languages are more valuable in programming and CS and which are less.

At the end, having an international language is the best way to go. Ok, I won't get into the political reasons of why English is the current one, but there you have it... use it. Please, also notice that I'm a Spanish native speaker that doesn't know any Arabic and you're an Arab native speaker that surely doesn't know any Spanish... and we still can communicate because of English ;)
