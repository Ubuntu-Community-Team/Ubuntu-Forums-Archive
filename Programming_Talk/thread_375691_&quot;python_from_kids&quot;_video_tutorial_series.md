### Post by HorstJENS on 2007-03-04
Sorry if this is the wrong list but i doubt that non-programmers are interested:

At [www.showmedo.com](www.showmedo.com) is my newest video tutorial series ready to watch: "Python from kids" 

The video series consist of 3 very short videos so far, about 

[LIST]
[*]**Rur-Ple** (Python Learning Enviroment)
[*]**Easygui** usage and customizing (Easygui is a simple GUI for python)
[*]using** pydoc** and **drpython** to browse the help text of **Easygui**
[/LIST]

All videos are made by 10-11 year old kids from my class. The videos are very short, have German voice and English subtitles.
The kids and me would be very exited if you could make coments about the videos in the form below the video at [www.showmedo.com](www.showmedo.com)

Hint: All videos where made with 100%M$ free software (Ubuntu Edgy).

### Post by HorstJENS on 2007-03-05
*@ketilkn*: Thanks !
**special:**
Well, it's a private afternoon class organized by [http://www.profikids.at](http://www.profikids.at) . I got the kids in October last year, and all of the 3 kids had visited computer classes before (i don't know how long). I think Lexi and Leo also visited a class about "programming", but with Flash. They had no prior experience with Python, that' what i know for sure.  The staff at profikids could tell you more.
**to what Level:**
My goal is to teach enough Python -more exactly, to make them read "a byte of python" themselves- so that we can finally begin to code something in pygame. (I want to use the wisdom gained from working with the kids for my pygamebook: [http://pygamebook.sf.net](http://pygamebook.sf.net) )
Sadly i have the kids only one hour per week and not so much can be archived in this time. At least the time fly by very fast when working with the kids :-)
