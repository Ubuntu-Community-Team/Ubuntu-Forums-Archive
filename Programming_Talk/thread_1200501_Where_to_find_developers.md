Hi Everybody,

I'm not entirely sure this post belongs here but it seems to be the most fitting place to put it.

I hope this questions isn't too vague but at this point in time I can't really be any more specific.

I have spent years in the taxi business working with all sorts of different dispatching software. Recently, myself and some co-workers have started our own taxi company and are not entirely happy with our "out of the box" dispatch software. It will do for now but I think we will be shopping again in a couple of years.

As a possible alternative to this I would like to investigate the possibility of having our own linux based software developed. This is my question - Where would I go to start asking some questions to see if this is viable? Who would I need to talk to specifically? Is there a place where developers capable of handling such a project post ads etc?

It would likely be a fair sized project.. involving many end-users in the office and dispatching trips using a G.P.S system to many vehicles probably equipped with cell phones. So an app of some sort would have to be developed for the phones as well.

If anybody has an idea of how I could start such an inquiry please let me know.

First suggestion: Google for "Linux dispatching software open source".  This will give you a list of existing OSS projects that may be able to fit your business.

Assuming that none of them are exactly what you want - contact the maintainer(s) of the software, and find out how much they'd charge to customize it for you.

That way, you get a basic "starter package" for free, and you can easily find the people who are familiar with it to do the customization.
