### Post by simeon87 on 2011-05-23
There's no standard procedure for doing this. It depends very much on the software and the end-users. Are they technical or not? Do they need an easy-to-use installer? Some programs, such as Python applications, have their own way of distributing software. Otherwise you can just release the source with instructions on how to compile it.

### Post by DangerOnTheRanger on 2011-05-24
On Windows, apps normally take care of automatic updating themselves.
On Linux, though, each distro's package manager takes care of this.

Sometimes, languages themselves have packaging abilites. Perl has its CPAN facility, Python has PyPI.
Other than those two, I'm not sure.
