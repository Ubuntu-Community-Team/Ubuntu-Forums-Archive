### Post by Nevon on 2009-06-20
I use a number of different social media services on my blog - Digg being one of them. The traffic Digg generates depends on how good the content of your site is. If it's newsworthy and a lot of people Digg it, you'll get awesome traffic. But if only <10 people ever Digg it, you might only get a tiny bit of traffic.

So far Stumbleupon has been the most successful social media service for me.

### Post by jinksys on 2009-06-21
Are you looking to increase hit rate, or advertising dollars?  From what I've heard, while you may get an insane amount of traffic if a page gets 'dugg', few readers will click ads and most have anti-ad software, such as adblock.  Members of social sites like digg and reddit have internet ADD.  Most see a submission as a single page and once they consume that content they are back to digg for more content.  Sure some will click through your webcomics, or maybe click a couple links, but the majority won't stray far from the warm, familar bosom of digg/reddit.  However, like Nevon, I have been more successful with StumbleUpon to create more long term readers.
