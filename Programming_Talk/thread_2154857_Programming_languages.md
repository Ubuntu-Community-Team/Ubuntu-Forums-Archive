### Post by mickeelm on 2013-06-24
I believe you've gotten some answers here regarding languages and IDE, a general tip from my side though would be to do some basic examples with a simple text editor before using an IDE and work against/on the command-line when compiling and running programs. You will fall into some pits in the beginning but in return get a better understanding of how the language mechanism works and in it will benefit you in the end, I promise :)

So go with what you feel like, the opinions differ on all this of course. I myself work as a programmer in java and use Eclipse (which I think is great, but I saw that opinion wasn't shared earlier in the thread :D ). When you get a bit more comfortable with development and your IDE I would also recommend you to take time to explore shortcuts, tips & tricks etc. for your IDE. That will probably save you huge amounts of time and possibly help you write better code. At least it has for me!

Best of luck!
