### Post by Olav on 2017-02-23
For a super quick solution on a remote server: [Adminer](https://www.adminer.org/). phpMyAdmin is nice, but Adminer is about as capable and it works with more than just MySQL/MariaDB. And I like its minimalistic appearance.

On the desktop I often use MySQL Workbench, mostly because of its diagram editor and reverse engineering feature.

Within Eclipse I sometimes use the tools from Data Tools Platform, but they are by no means a favourite. But practical enough to quickly look up a column name or so.

For Eclipse there is also the [DBeaver](http://dbeaver.jkiss.org/) plugin which look like it offers quite a lot of features, but I haven't tried it yet.
