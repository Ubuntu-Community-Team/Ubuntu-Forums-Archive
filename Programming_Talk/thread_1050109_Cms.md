Go for Drupal, and, if you don't like that, try Joomla. Both are extendable - your biggest challenge will be finding a theme / template which is close to what you want. Most themes are freely modifiable.

It seems like you're not that far down the planning route at this time though - more often than not you will run into problems which your current plan doesn't solve. This is why I was asking 'what you need' :P. You may very well find that a CMS you're using doesn't have an extension to (for example) allow users to create their own mini-reviews to supplement your own reviews. There is a difference (to developers or designers anyway) between 'what you want' and 'what you need', so it's got to be asked :P

To save yourself future headaches, do yourself a favour and get a written plan together outlining what you want. Then, for each 'want', think about it and work out what each of these 'wants' requires (or 'needs') in order to be implemented. So, as a very basic example:

1: You **want** a content management system
2: You **need** a server with php enabled, and a database.

EDIT: For most of the things you've listed as features for your website, I think there's a very strong likelihood of them being available as extensions to both Drupal and Joomla, so I imagine you won't have too much trouble. You might have to spend quite a while trying out different extensions to find the best for the job, though.

That's what I'm talking about - I don't know if such a thing exists as an extension for any CMS, but if you want it, you'll have to find out how to go about it. Content management systems usually have seperate roles for users - authors, publishers, administrators etc.

I guess you COULD just make every user an author and sift through submissions, cherry picking the best ones for yourself, but it really depends on how you would like the whole thing to be implemented. That's why I suggested you write a plan :P Until you know what you want, you don't know what you need - and until you know what you need, you won't know what to do!
