### Post by asmiller-ke6seh on 2007-12-01
> **LaRoza said:**
> That is StarOffice, a Sun Microsystems product. MS didn't design word processors and other office software, they just have a popular one.

HUH?

Okay, let me approach this from another angle: What are the OOS design standards? How do we provide for consistency across applications? When a novice computer user (and there will ALWAYS be novice computer users) approaches a new application, how do we provide a consistent experience?

Macintosh always had design standards. We call this the Macintosh Interface. There is a high degree of consistency. One of the points of the David Pouge presentation at TED is that consistency is one of the keys to successful design.

Would there be a benefit to the potential user community if there was an Open Source Software Interface Standard (OSSIS)? Especially if that standard was superior to Apple's and Microsoft's? The Standard would be offered for use, but not prescribed. It would include standards for both character-based and graphical user interfaces, and also describe methods for inter-application communication. But, best of all, it would be OPEN, in every respect of what we mean when we say OPEN.
