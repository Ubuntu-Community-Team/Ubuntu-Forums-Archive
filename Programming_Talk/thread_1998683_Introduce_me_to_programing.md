### Post by trent.josephsen on 2012-06-07
> **na5h said:**
> If you're into web-development, [W3Schools.com]("http://www.w3schools.com/") is a good place to start. They have some great step-by-step tutorials. 

HTML and CSS for layout + JavaScript and PHP for the programming should give you a pretty solid ground.
You may not be aware of some of the problems with W3Schools -- see [here](http://w3fools.com/). W3Schools makes only the most half-hearted attempt at standards compliance, their instructive content is often misleading, and their $95.00 certifications are not accredited or recognized by anybody. It's not the worst resource you can use, but there are much better options available.

@OP, I suggest picking a language (I and many other people here recommend Python) and getting to know how to use it before trying to switch to something else. You don't yet have the skills or experience to evaluate languages and choose which one to use.

Also, if you're only just "at the level that i understand the html code more or less", you want to make a decision here:

(1) Learn to write concise, elegant, standards-compliant HTML, CSS, and Javascript, without CGI or anything. Once you're comfortable with that side of Web development (I'd suggest spending a good 6 months before you even consider yourself ready to move on), consider learning a language like Python, Perl, or PHP to automate the process; or

(2) Put Web development on the back burner for a while and learn a general purpose programming language. Check out a copy of *Learning Python* from a nearby local or university library if you can (most people don't have or like to spend the money but there's no substitute for good books IMNSHO). Once you're comfortable with programming, then pick HTML back up and learn to make CGI scripts.

I suggest (2) but I'm not a professional Web guy, thank goodness.
