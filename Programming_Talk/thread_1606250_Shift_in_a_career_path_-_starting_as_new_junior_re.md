### Post by StunnerAlpha on 2010-10-26
If I were you I would try to be a master at C++(know templates, unions, etc). Even if in the job you won't be using all of the language's features. Just knowing everything to the point where you can code it on the fly and use each feature successfully will only increase your chances of out competing your competitors and secure the position.

It may also be wise to look into optimizing code for speed and efficiency(know algorithm amortized cost[big-O notation]?-couldn't hurt).

### Post by dwhitney67 on 2010-10-26
Look into "Embedded C++" [here]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embedded_C%2B%2B").

It's like C++, but most notably without *templates*, and hence without STL.

I had to use this style of C++ when developing flight s/w for a satellite.

As far as what to expect on the job, don't worry too much about it... you will learn as you go along.  Maybe each night you will have "homework" to do so that you can come back the next day armed with more knowledge.
