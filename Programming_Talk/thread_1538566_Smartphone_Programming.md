### Post by scratman on 2010-07-25
To be honest, there's no requirement to buy a handset that's already on T-Mobile, you could buy and handset with any provider, call the provider and request your PUK/PAC codes, and you will be able to transfer the phone to T-Mobile with the new number. You could even buy an unlocked phone and get a SIM from T-Mobile to put into it, if you wanted.  That being said, a smart phone is likely to use a lot of internet bandwidth, and is probably not a wise way to go, you can get contracts in the UK with unlimited data included from £15 per month, so it may well be worth looking into that.

### Post by soltanis on 2010-07-26
> **shadylookin said:**
> Every android based phone I've run across you can put your own apps on it. Though I can't say for certain that there aren't any super locked down ones.

Not that carriers haven't tried, but most stupid Android restrictions are with the bootloader. Telecoms don't like people having root access to the phone, so they've been pulling more and more jerk moves to try to keep people from getting it; the Droid X, an upcoming phone, will have a hardware fuse that will brick the device if it detects anything other than the factory loader, so yeah.

On the other end of the spectrum there's the Nexus One, which Google practically encourages you to mess with. I think it's safe to say that most Android phones are much more permissive than other smartphones, such as Nokia or Apple products.
