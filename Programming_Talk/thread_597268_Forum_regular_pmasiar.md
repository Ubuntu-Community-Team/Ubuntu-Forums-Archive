### Post by CptPicard on 2007-10-30
Disclaimer: I do not particularly fancy myself any kind of an expert here and I do not feel like I am after the distinction, but yes, it's probably true that I am more competent than the "average bear", to cite another Uni. Helsinki CS alum. I'm not doing this off ego (got better things to do in life -- I used to inflate my ego on the net when I was a teenager), but I agree with pmasiar's idea.

This is not a mutual admiration society. I have personally witnessed enough threads where a n00b gets just *horrible* advice, and each time, the more competent posters need to really establish from ground up that they know something. I really don't think that we need to establish any kind of special procedures for "membership" or anything -- just some vouching for the pretty obvious group of more serious people around here so that the immediate newcomer can have some idea of which paths to follow. This is just fair; the learning curve in learning computing is steep as you need to typically learn a lot of stuff at the same time. I can't even imagine how it would feel like going back 10 years to the time when I was trying to figure out Slackware 3.4, C and Java *at the same time*....

This stuff folks come for to here is after all pretty objective. The "there are multiple truths" approach does not really apply to our n00bs... they need "a" truth, and pretty much any one of our more knowledgeable people will be able to provide an answer that the majority of our other knowledgeable members can subscribe to. I am perfectly willing to debate the remaining finer points  after the obvious bs has been cleaned out of the way from creating total confusion. This is the essence of the "thread-hijacking"-phenomenon here -- a satisfactory answer is arrived at, after which the "experts" just duke it out to the end. The n00b doesn't care anymore at this point.

IMO the "karma system" should not be seen as something that excludes people or establishes some kind of academic pecking order... it just notes noteworthy people as they have been helpful to the forum. Not being one is not yet an insult. You should always remember that "you are only a guru when other people tell you so"... and some reference material to that could be useful.

Mind you, "I am on the experts list so I am correct" is never an argument.
