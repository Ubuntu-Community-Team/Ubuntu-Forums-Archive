There are lots. They used to be listed on the Ubuntu article at Wikipedia but some eager-beaver deleted all of them.

Of course, I'm partial to

Ubuntuguide.org (at [http://ubuntuguide.org](http://ubuntuguide.org))

and

Kubuntuguide.org (at [http://kubuntuguide.org](http://kubuntuguide.org))

Both can be printed/downloaded in PDF or XML format, if you choose.

Occasionally I have found some arcane material buried somewhere on

Ubuntu Geek (at [http://www.ubuntugeek.com](http://www.ubuntugeek.com)), but it is heavy slog finding solutions there.

Much of the time I end up finding solutions at the Ubuntu Community wiki

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community](https://help.ubuntu.com/community)

and for server stuff refer to the Ubuntu Server Guide

[https://help.ubuntu.com/10.10/serverguide/C/index.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/10.10/serverguide/C/index.html) 

There are many, many other wikis, but few are comprehensive.

The problem with printed books is that they are usually a year out of date by the time they are on the shelf, and computers and software evolve quickly.

Videos? No way. It would take a lifetime of watching videos to learn everything you can do with Ubuntu.
