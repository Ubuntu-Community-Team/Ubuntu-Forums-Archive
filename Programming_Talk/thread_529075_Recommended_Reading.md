### Post by Max Luebbe on 2007-08-18
Patterson and Hennessy will give you a good grounding in how a cpu works (I like this book a lot)

[Computer  Organization and Design]("http://www.amazon.com/Computer-Organization-Design-Revised-Printing/dp/0123706068/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1/002-1693514-3081667?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1187483476&sr=8-1")

This was my digital logic book at university:
[Fundamentals of Digital Logic]("http://www.amazon.com/Fundamentals-Digital-Logic-Verilog-Design/dp/0077211642/ref=pd_bbs_sr_3/002-1693514-3081667?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1187483594&sr=1-3")

Likewise for Discrete:
[Discrete Mathematics and its Applications]("http://www.amazon.com/Discrete-Mathematics-Applications-Kenneth-Rosen/dp/0073312711/ref=sr_1_5/002-1693514-3081667?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1187483702&sr=1-5")

To start off using good programming practices, I highly recommend Code Complete 2nd edition by Microsoft Press.

To inspire you in your studies, you might also pick up 
[Soul of a New Machine]("http://www.amazon.com/Soul-New-Machine-Tracy-Kidder/dp/038071115X/ref=ed_oe_p/002-1693514-3081667?ie=UTF8&qid=1187483863&sr=1-1")

With any of these suggestions (esp. the textbooks) you can usually get low cost paperback versions (International Editions) if you look for them - same book, usually a fraction of the hardcover cost.

Hope this helps, and best luck with beginning your education!

### Post by pmasiar on 2007-08-20
One more reading: [http://lwn.net/](http://lwn.net/) - Linux Weekly News. This is the best of IT industry ezines, with most insightful comments on articles. This is the one what real hackers read :-)

Main editor, Corbet, is often speaker at linux conferences, because he has rare capability to understand kernel, **and** write/talk sensibly about it for people interested, but not deeply immersed in the code.

Other informative articles about distros and applications, linux tricks, with insightful comments.

Quite often, **discussion** to an article is more informative that the article itself:  [How Linux became a mobile phone OS](http://lwn.net/Articles/245847/) explains **why** flash, differences between flash memories, all by [Bruce Perens](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Perens). It is paid, but cheap at the level of "student/hacker", and well worth the money.

I cannot recommend it highly enough!
