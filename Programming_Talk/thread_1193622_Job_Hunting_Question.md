### Post by dwhitney67 on 2009-06-21
Out of curiosity, which university did you attend?  Were you able participate in any internship programs?  If so, I would first seek employment at the company where you have a "foot in the door".  It is never taught in school, but the best time to look for a new job in when you already have a job.

As for the job postings, generally they are posted by hiring agencies (pimps as I like to call them), that are looking to place you (ahem... the whore) at some company.  In most cases it is a win-win scenario, but on occasion, it can be you that is stuck with a bad deal.

When you see what appears multiple listings for the "same" job, apply to the firm that you believe will represent you the best.  Often times, it is not possible to know which is best; thus just go with your gut instinct.

Send me a PM; maybe I can put you in contact with a company if you need assistance; the job may require you to relocate.

If you are open to relocation, just peruse the job openings in the Washington Post.  You will not be disappointed.
