### Post by memories on 2008-08-26
I can't make out what you mean by 'design' in your post. Do you mean design as in nice graphics and layouts, or 'design patterns', like OO and etc? 

I also suggest picking up another language. I personally prefer rails, and now we have rails 2.1, mod_rails and the next update of Ruby is said to provide a major speed boost because it will begin compiling ruby into byte code (like python's .pyc). 

Anyway, for graphics, first learn CSS. Then just come up with a design on paper and try to emulate it in CSS and Gimp. 

You also need to read about color, standard web conventions and other design related topics. I suggest reading "Dont Make Me Think" - a book by Steven Krug about usability.
