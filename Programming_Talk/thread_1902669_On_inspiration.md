### Post by DangerOnTheRanger on 2011-12-31
> **hakermania said:**
> Well, most of the times it is quite simple. See what you do everyday, see what you don't like on the things you do.

That's all, see what you can't do or what you would like to be better. Make it or fix it :)

Quite right - I'm pretty sure this is probably the primary way FOSS projects come into being. The secondary way is probably creating an alternative to an existing proprietary program. For example, my primary FOSS project came about because I wanted a FOSS alternative to the proprietary games Blockland and ROBLOX. 2 years later, [OpenBlox]("http://openblox.sourceforge.net") is finally getting close to what I originally imagined.

That's probably how you'll have to come up with an idea: Find some problem domain (that you're interested in, of course) where FOSS doesn't exist yet, and then bring FOSS there. Trust me, there are ideas out there - you just have to find them :)
