### Post by BenAshton24 on 2009-12-29
I am personally a massive fan of learning by example and the tutorials that I would have liked when I started programming are the ones that focus on making something useful. I would then adapt what I had learnt and make my own things.

Here Is my idea of a good tutorial.

1) Tell the viewer what the program that you will be making is going to do
2) Suggest ways in which the program could be adapted
3) Jump straight into programming, if you want to explain how your IDE works and which buttons do what, then explain as you are doing it and make sure that it does not become the focus of the video.
4) Show the viewers what the program does at each major stage in order to keep them interested.
5) Conclude by showing the program working and then maybe show some simple adaptations.
6) Talk about the ways that the program can be expanded
7) Anything extra that you want to add including the boring bits (by this time the viewers will be either satisfied with what they have seen or intrigued enough to continue watching).

When I fist started programming, I didn't really care about how everything worked, I just wanted to know what to do to make things happen. The interest in how everything pieces together came later.

I like the videos that you have done so far, but I think that they cover then more basic topics but with an advanced user in mind.

These are just my opinions and, after all, they are your videos so you should do them in a way that suits you best.

Good luck.
Ben.
