### Post by kalaharix on 2008-06-11
I have put together a small Gambas tutorial is response to two posts in this forum.

The first was from someone who said Basic languages were rubbish and then used  QBasic  as as example. Now I am old enough to know that QBasic predates Windows. In fact I am so old that I cannot remember whether QBasic came out with Dos5 or Dos6. I doubt you'll get much support in this forum if you say Vista is great but to say Vista is rubbish and use Dos5 as a reason is nonsense. 

The second was from a Python proponent. He advised a newbie that Python was great because you could ignore any GUI and just learn programming with a command line. IMHO that is soooo wrong.

Programming is a language: it is about communication. The modern idiom is the GUI and we can't ignore that. Probably 60% of a modern program is spent coping with the variability of the GUI. The program logic itself is often quite a small component of the end product. A programming environment where the GUI is not tightly integrated with the language is, to put it mildly, not helpful. The level of integration between wxWidgets or Glade and Python is awful. If website design can move beyond the unpredictable table model why can't Glade move beyond its restrictive tramlines?

Anyway I think Gambas is great and I am hoping that a short tutorial will break the ice for those with some VB experience who are new to Linux. You can find it at [http://www.kalaharix.wordpress.com]("http://www.kalaharix.wordpress.com").
