### Post by cwaldbieser on 2007-10-13
Potential employers will be looking for a way to differentiate you from other candidates.  Since the job is entry level, you are probably not going to find a bunch of Java experts competing for it (they will be looking for positions where the salary is more in line with their level of experience).  

Try to find out more about what the employer does before the interview.  What kind of software do they make?  As a new employee, what kind of role do new employees take on in the company?  How can you use your skills to fit in and benefit the employer?  Be as specific as you can.  Everyone is going to be saying stuff like, "I'm a team player." or "I enjoy working with technology".

If you have any non-technical skills or experience that are relevant to the employer's field, make sure to play them up.

### Post by pmasiar on 2007-10-14
Because you are entry-level, don't worry too much about technical questions. They either accept what you know and teach you what they need you to know, or not.

But go to your local library and ask them about book about interview process. They may even have video to watch. It is about body language, about different ways to conduct interview, about being on time, properly dressed (better dress up than down!) and groomed, making good impression.

Remember, you can make first impression only once, and as they say, there are two kinds of people: those  who say openly that they judge people according to first impression, and those who lie about it :-)

Smart employers ask about how you handle stresfull situation, conflicts, and will ask you for examples from your life. If they don't ask, they probably don't care about them - which might be red flag for you.

Also think about questions you want to ask (books mentioned above might get you idea). If they will not offer it themselves, ask for tour of premises, just to see if you will like it there. I once interviewed in a shop where they did not have cubicles, but people were so close as almost touching elbows. I got offer but rejected it :-)
