### Post by trent.josephsen on 2012-07-30
I wouldn't pay a cent for any course unless it came with a well-recognized and useful certification. You can self-study for cheaper. Especially popular, common skills like the ones you mentioned.

I don't know of any worthwhile certification in HTML5, PHP or Javascript, although Google might bring something up. Red Hat certifies Linux system administrators but it costs a lot. So does Novell. CompTIA has a Linux+ certification that I think is a bit more broad. Specific server software like IBM Websphere and obviously Microsoft IIS will have training material provided by the vendor.

If you just want to learn stuff, then pick something you want to learn and ask how to learn it. You'll get better answers if you ask for recommended materials and advice rather than paid programming courses.

### Post by trent.josephsen on 2012-07-31
In that case, I would suggest getting Linux sysadmin certification. Good for your career. If you use Ubuntu at work, Canonical has some kind of Ubuntu Certified Professional cert that I don't know much about.

I don't think you'll find much in the way of training material for "very advanced" PHP or JS, though. Experts don't get to be experts by taking lots of more advanced courses; even in academia, that's only how you start your career. You become an expert by doing, not by being taught.

Also, if PHP and Javascript is the extent of your coding repertoire, maybe you should look into broadening your experience rather than continuing to specialize. Python, Perl, C, Java, Lisp and maybe an assembly language will make you a better and more valuable programmer than spending thousands of hours learning the more obscure details of PHP.

Disclaimer: I know exactly nothing of PHP except what I have been told by others, which is mostly to stay clear of it. PHP experience would be a liability for me.
