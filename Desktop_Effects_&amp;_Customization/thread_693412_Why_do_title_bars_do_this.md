### Post by NIT006.5 on 2008-02-11
Generally, if problems occur related to desktop effects, it would "normally" mean that your hardware has "issues" with effects, from my limited experience. I have had one or two problems with "certain" effects causing unpredictable behaviour, in which case I just disabled those particular effects. On other machines though, where effects generally didn't work properly or caused unexpected side-effects, I just disabled the effects.

So unfortunately, it might just be your hardware that doesn't "like" desktop effects. Of course, I could be completely wrong and someone else might have a simple solution, but generally I've just had to accept that effects don't work well on some hardware and if that's the case, then there isn't too much you can do about it without replacing hardware.
