### Post by BobCFC on 2007-06-25
If you right-click on the diamond icon near the clock and choose Beryl Settings Manager

Then in the **General Options** section on the **Main** tab is an option called **Horizontal Virtual Size**.

You can increase this to make you cube into a pentagon or hexagon etc.   Also set back to 4 to turn into a cube again.

If you want 3 sides like a triangle you will have to turn the caps off because it looks weird.  To turn the caps off  click on **Visual Effects** at top then **Desktop Cube** on the left.  There should be a **Caps** tab where you can untick the first option '**Draw Caps**'

### Post by BobCFC on 2007-06-25
To force an application to a specific viewport click on **Window Management** at the top then tick **Set Window Attribs by various criteria** on the left.   You will also have to click on this name to see the options.

Click on **Initial Viewport** and the box should drop down.  Use the + button to add a new rule.  You will need a bit of trial and error from this point on as programs behave differently.
I have not done viewport but Opacity works in the same way...

When the form pops up you can pick options on left from Window Title, Window Class etc then click Grab and your mouse will change... then you can click on a window you want such as the Terminal and it's name will appear.

The trouble is programs such as firefox change their title depending on the current page.  You will have to experiment.
