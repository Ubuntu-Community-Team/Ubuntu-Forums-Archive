### Post by o7a on 2007-04-28
Hi,

Svg format is more and more used for software icons. Notably in those docks that everybody want.

Have you already try to find the official icon of a software in svg format ? I have and when it isn't impossible, it's very laborious (even for the open source software)...

Most of the time, designer who made the icon doesn't give the vectorial file (in the case he made it in vectorial, which is often the case for icons). So when you search, you find a lot of icons named software.svg, cool, you download them, then you realize it isn't the official one but a remake for a specific theme... It's quite boring.

In my case, I didn't want one dock but two, for my dual-Screen ; So I needed for many many svg icons and spent much time to get the expected ones.

For the icons unobtainable, I rolled up my sleeves, and plunged my hands into inkscape. I touched up several icons and vectorized 3 ones : Skype, Google Earth, and Beagle.

I'm quite pleased with my work, I was finespun and when you convect the svg file in a png one, you obtain with the exception of few pixels the same png icon than the official one.

Becoming aware of this lack on the web, I decided to create a small web site listing the software svg icons existing, [**http://svgicons.o7a.net/**]("http://svgicons.o7a.net/") was born. That's now you get involved, I'm short of resources and time, whereas such a community abound of them.

So give me your opinion, suggestions, and of course svg icons ;).

Thanks for your attention and sorry for my bad English (which you can correct if you want),
