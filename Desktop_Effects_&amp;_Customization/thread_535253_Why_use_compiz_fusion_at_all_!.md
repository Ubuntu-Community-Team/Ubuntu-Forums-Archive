### Post by hyperair on 2007-08-26
Controversial title isn't it. There are a lot of people who, like me, prefer to use Compiz Fusion not just for the effects, but for the increase in the level of productivity it offers (I'm talking about plugins such as "Scale", "Scale Add-ons" and "Desktop Cube", not the effects purely for eyecandy purposes). Those are things which Windows XP + Object Desktop can never give me, nor Windows Vista. 

Then there are those who firmly insist that Compiz Fusion is a useless piece of software that should be banned to oblivion because they deem that it doesn't follow the Unix principle: KISS (Keep It Simple Stupid). Anyway their arguments ALWAYS revolve around the same thing (useless plugins such as fire distract them), and their arguments ALWAYS have the weakness in that they ALWAYS fail to address the issue where Scale and Desktop Cube increase productivity. 

Personally, I am not a hardcore gamer, and any games that I play run perfectly on Wine. Also, in my free time I just surf the net or mess around with some programming. Occassionally when the need arises, I'll bring up Virtualbox equipped with Adobe Photoshop CS3 (I'm head designer for my college newsletter, and GIMP sucks for the job), Microsoft Visual Basic (I hate it but as a Cambridge A-Levels Computing student I have no choice) and Microsoft Offce 2007. I see the need in Compiz Fusion over Metacity not for the eyecandy (though I do appreciate a good looking desktop), but for ease in which I can multitask.

Also, while Deluge rocks, KTorrent rocks even more. I'm no big fan of KDE, and if Deluge can ever reach the level of functionality KTorrent provides I'll be glad to switch over.

As for Nero, I see no use for that as K3B serves me well.

### Post by Rupertronco on 2007-08-26
If you fail to see the added versatility and functionality of your desktop, (especailly if you're writing webpages, doing any programming, even writing a thesis and browsing the web at the same time) you haven't played with compiz enough. The cube is great for multi-tasking, scale is great for finding what you want while multi-tasking, and window tabbing is great, not too many people realize how awesome that plugin is, it saves me a ton of space when i have a bunch of things running. I group similar windows like GFTP+Bluefish+Firefox(for the site im working on) or Mplayer+Xmms+Ktorrent and then another firefox with thunderbird on the back, it keeps everything organized and provides great productivity enhancement. 

The bonus is that it looks cool, but the argument that it provides no benefits other than it's nice to look at is pretty off-target.
