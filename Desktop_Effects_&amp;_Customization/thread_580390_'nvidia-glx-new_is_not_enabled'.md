### Post by DavidTangye on 2007-10-18
It might not be installed at first, but the Restricted Drivers Manager will advise you of that via a popup, and you can get it to install it. This all works nicely. Just follow the pop-up help.

Once you have you 3D driver installed, it gets you to reboot. After that you can configure the 3d stuff via Preferences->Appearance, and then Preferences->Advanced Desktop Effects Settings.
