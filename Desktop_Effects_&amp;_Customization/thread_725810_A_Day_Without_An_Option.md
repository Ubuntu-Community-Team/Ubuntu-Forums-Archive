### Post by mocoloco on 2008-03-16
I find it highly unlikely, at least not for many many years.  One of the  great benefits of Ubuntu (and free software in general) is running it in developing countries where older systems and slower connections are the norm.  They know this and try to cater to that demographic, for example that's why they're sticking to one install CD when so many other distros have multiple CDs/DVDs.
I'm wondering how long it will be before 3D becomes more useful than basic eye candy, when there's a genuine advantage to a 3D GUI.  Perhaps as new input methods like multi-touch screens become the norm?
