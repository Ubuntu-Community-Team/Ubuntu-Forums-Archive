### Post by zero244 on 2007-05-22
If I had a dollar for every hour Ive spent trying to get beryl or compiz working I could take a cruise to Tahiti.
I dont think Beryl will be ready for prime time for many months or years. I know some people have it running ok.
But the majority do not.
I had it running halfway stable with a ATI card but since I upgraded to a Nvidia 7600 I have not had it running for more than a minute before it freezes me solid.
It has to be the most problematic feature to install with wireless close behind. At least wireless problems dont usually freeze your computer.
And all the talk about Nvidia cards being more compatable and easier to setup up is a bunch of hogwash.
With my ATI card I downloaded the drivers.......installed beryl........made some changes to my xorg.conf and it ran just fine.
The bottom line..........beryl has become a nightmare. There are dozens of instructions to install it.
So if your newbie I would pass on Beryl and save yourself a bunch of headaches.
I will use Ubuntu with the Standard Gnome interface until they work out all the massive bugs with the current video drivers.
Or until someone comes up with a install routine that actually works for most people.
