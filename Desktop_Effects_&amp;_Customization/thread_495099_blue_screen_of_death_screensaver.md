### Post by llamakc on 2007-07-07
Why not just use the native one that's provided by the package xscreensaver-data-extra? I figured that out by searching for "bsod" at packages.ubuntu.com. Here were the results.

[http://packages.ubuntu.com/cgi-bin/search_contents.pl?word=bsod&searchmode=searchword&case=insensitive&version=feisty&arch=i386](http://packages.ubuntu.com/cgi-bin/search_contents.pl?word=bsod&searchmode=searchword&case=insensitive&version=feisty&arch=i386)

After you install that, you choose BSOD in screensaver options.
