### Post by Dryer Lint on 2007-07-12
Here's my setup:

*fresh Kubuntu Feisty installation
*Beryl 0.2.1
*GeForce 7600 GT (PCIe)
*nvidia-glx-new 1.0.9755

Beryl runs rock-solid and hasn't caused me any problems so far, but the framerate seems way too low to me!

On an idle screen with a few simple windows it's around 40FPS.
I have pretty much all of the effects enabled, including Blur and Water.
However, if I deactivate ALL the plugins, effects and fancy things, even the window decorations, the FPS only goes up to 47.

I would like to have 60FPS because I'm running on an LCD clocked at 60Hz and I think my card should be more than capable of achieving that! A friend gets over 200FPS with his old GeForce5, so what's wrong here?

If you could help me out with this, I'd be really happy! I just love Beryl! :)

### Post by gavintlgold on 2007-07-12
What I used to do (when I used beryl) was turn off the "Detect Refresh Rate" option in General, and turn up the refresh rate to 200 (not that that actually happens, but it helps) Try that, see what happens.

### Post by Gausian on 2007-07-12
you can also try adding this line to you xorg.conf along with your other options to enable hardware accel.

```
Option         "RenderAccel" "True"
```
