### Post by Bashed on 2007-04-30
I'll be honest and straightforward. I think the default linux interface is poor, including fonts. You need to tweak this, enable this, disable that, tweak another this and that. While its all a matter of preference, let's be fair. MAC OSX and Vista are by far more crisp, cleaner and sharper in its interface. Its more professional and clean. I want to know why linux is not like that. I've already paid several visits over to kde-look.org and gnome-look.org but nothing comes close to default sharp interface of MAC or Windows.

Perhaps you can prove me wrong. **Let's see how honest even the linux gurus can be about the quality of the overall interface.** If you read my many posts/threads asking for help in this forum, you'll see I'm not lover / hater of one side for another. I like Windows. I like MAC. I'm trying to like linux more and have not quit after trying various distros.

This is what I've experienced in Ubuntu Feisty (same for various other linux distros I've tried including Ubuntu Edgy, Kubuntu, OpenSuse):

**1.** Firefox menu is stretched, too wide for whatever reason (where the toolbar is). Possibly due to custom theme, not sure yet but its odd that it doesn't do that in Windows.

**2.** Fonts are disgraceful, even after installing msttfonts and using Tahoma or Arial or Verdana,  on every possible setting (hinting, smoothing, etc) on 96dpi / 1280x800 resolution. I've seen so many others in this forum complain about the same problem. MAC or Windows fonts look incredibly sharp and and crisp, so why linux doesn't I'm honestly not sure.

**3.** General interface is not crisp, not sharp like MAC or Windows. Why, I do not know. Its actually shocking considering all the effort put into linux, yet it seems not much effort to make the default interface really sharp.  I've tried Beryl (Nice) but the interface is still confusing.  The theme manager and emerald manager do not mix at all, so you really have two themes at once which is really annoying.  Also, look at the toolbars for examples in any OpenOffice program.  They are huge for whatever reason, taking up much real estate on the screen.

**4.** My sound card won't work (awaiting a resolution on 2 threads, with others having same exact problem).

**5.** By default, 5 button mouse does not work.  Have to modify system files to get it working to a fair degree, but not 100% with no customizable options. I know its ultimately because MS must provide the drivers, but we won't wait for the biggest competitor to make linux drivers at all, so one would think linux (by now after all these years) would have working 5 button mice drivers by default.

**6.** What's with the form fields / radio buttons?  They look horrible.  More system file modifications needed to get them to look fair, but not sharp enough.

**7. **It takes nearly 3 minutes for Ubuntu to fully boot.  Others face the same issue here:
- [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=416719](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=416719)

**8. **Unstable battery usage.  Tried on two different fully 100% charged batteries (one regular, one extended, both Lithium Ion) and Ubuntu claimed low battery usage after 10 minutes or so. See my thread here:
- [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=419231](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=419231)

For sake of not breaking hearts for hardcore linux users, here are my compliments ;)

Free software, community effort and generosity, security, performance. For this I'm thankful for. That is why I have not given up on trying linux. This is the same in the included documentations as well.  Its a bad way to welcome newcomers to the linux field.
