### Post by staticvoid on 2007-11-21
Here's my first howto.
So, lets say I have a picture I want to make my login screen to have as a back drop? Can I do this?

Yes!

1. Download a simple GDM login screen .tar.gz file from[ gnome-look.org]("http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=150&PHPSESSID=9a626ee79dd563ac2b0cc01277b2b1d1") or[ art.gnome.com]("http://art.gnome.org/themes/gdm_greeter/")

2. extract the file and look at the contents of the folder

3. nottice the "background" image and the "screenshot" img. those are the ones you can change.

4. find a picture, open the screenshot and background images and Gimp away!  
   a. if its an SVG file use Inkscape to edit it.

5. right click on the folder and choose "Create Archive..." and compress it to TAR.GZ format. now go into the GDM login manager. System>Administration>Login Window

6. choose the "Local tab" and then just drag your tar.gz file into the area with the visual login screens. Install, and switch to it.

7. Switch Users to check it out :)

ok, hope this helped someone. I had a fun 2 minutes writing it :) Its cool stuff.

Viva la ubuntu!!!
