### Post by c0mp13371331337 on 2007-11-17
Ah yes, the panels.  In my eyes, one of the only downfalls to Ubuntu.  I've spent a bit of time tweaking and working with panel configuration (only a bit of time, as there isn't much to do) and I dunno, I'd like to be able to do a BIT more.

My advice would be to check out some panel alternatives.  If you're using a compositing window manager like compiz or beryl, Avant is a decent choice.  Otherwise, simdock does basically the same thing, just not quite as developed or feature-rich quite yet.

There are also alternatives out there, if you don't mind getting your hands a little dirty, to the entire gnome-panel system.  I haven't taken it to that extent and I'm pretty much just 'coping' for now, but it may be worth looking into if you're really all about revamping the way the panels look.  Course you're also looking at probably not as tight of an integration between the desktop/window manager/programs/panels if you decide to go with something else, but it's certainly an option.

Also, have you tried creating a simple image in the Gimp that goes with your color-scheme and just using that image as your panel background image?  Depending on how fancy you make the pic, you may be able to get it looking the way you want.  Good luck!

Yeah as c0mp said there isn't much you can do.  You can however right click on the panel, go to properties and change the color, opacity settings and add a picture for the whole bar.  I use Avant Window Navigator for my bar.  Very slick and stable.
