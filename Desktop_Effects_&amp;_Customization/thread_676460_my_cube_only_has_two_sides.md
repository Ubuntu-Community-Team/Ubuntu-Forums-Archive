### Post by Promaster91 on 2008-01-23
Go to system>Advance Desktop Effects Settings. Then check Desktop Cube and Rotated Cube. Then click on Rotated Cube then actions and then General. Set initiate to what ever shortcut you want. Then close out of the window. To move the cube press <Ctrl> + <Alt> + Left or <Ctrl> + <Alt> + Right. If that doesn't work do to General Options in Advance Desktop Effects Settings. Then go to desktop size. Then enter in 4 for Horizontal Virtual Size.

### Post by Achtung on 2008-01-24
In the "Rotate cube" dialog, set your "initiate" action to a key+mouse click combo (Mine is set to "Super"+left click, which means the "Key" is set to "Disable", and the "button" is set to "<Super>Button1").

Press your key+mouse combo to get the zoomed out cube and start rotating it. Without letting go of your mouse button (In my case, left click), let go of your keyboard keys (In my case, "Super"). You will be able to move the cube with your mouse without touching the keyboard. 

This allows you to take pictures of the cube by simply pressing "PrintScrn".
