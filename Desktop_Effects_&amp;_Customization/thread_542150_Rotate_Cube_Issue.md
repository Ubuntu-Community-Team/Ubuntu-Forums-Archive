### Post by jimbo99 on 2007-09-03
After using Beryl for a long time I was eager to see the merge between it and compiz.

Unfortunately that transition has been rocky.  They introducted tons of new bugs and they carried over a huge number of old bugs.  I think the main issue is that they based the new merge on Compiz instead of on Beryl.  I believe the transition would have been better if the transition was the latter.

Dealing with the various issues I found that the rotate cube has quite a few annoying issues.  I'm posting this to hopefully help others that might encounter the issue and not know how to overcome it.  The issue is not a major one but it an annoy.

If you select icons--a multiple group--from on your desktop by holding down the mouse cursor and dragging it the selection is very smooth.  What may bother some is that after you rotate the cube and then select groups of icons in the same fashion the selection process is choppy at best.  It continues this performance forever until you restart Linux or reload X or reload the desktop manager.

The quick answer to this is to reload the desktop.  If you have the compiz fusion icon you can do it quite easily by right clicking on the icon in your system tray and choose to reload.

Another annoyance is that--well, the 3d desktop windows used to be a nice feature.  Customers would come into my store and I'd demo Ubuntu with Beryl to them.  Eveyrone went apeshit over it.  They all loved it.  Showing them 3 full screen movies playing at the same time and then music and opening windows and tabbing between them gets everyone realizing this is a wonderful OS with potential beyond what Windows could even consider offering.

Well, the 3d desktop was nice as I could put a window on the corner of the cube and rotate it and show them that it played and that it was transparent.  What happens now is that the window is split into two different objects and looks piss poor.They really let us down on this.

It used to be that you could rotate the cube with transparency and look at the window from behind and see the contents.  This is no longer an option.  You can't see the window contents from behind by looking through the transparent cube.

You use to also be able to rotate the cube by holding down the middle mouse button.  Now you are forced to use two hands to accomplish the same thing. You have to hold down the alt+control and click the left mouse button.

On top of that the program uses much much more cpu utilization to accomplish the same thing.  it used to be you could rotate the cube and it would be very smooth and still take nearly no cpu cycles.  Now the amount of cpu utilization is very noticeable--even on an Athlon X2 6000+ (yes I have a 6000+).
