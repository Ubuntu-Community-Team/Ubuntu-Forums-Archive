### Post by lotacus on 2007-04-23
1. do you get to the login screen? select the link that says "sessions" then click "Gnome session" on the window that pops up. Now enter your username and password to get to the desktop. Once at the desktop, beryl manager should be in the menu launcher, not sure what linux folks call the "start menu" equivalent, but it's in that menu under system tools. Click on the beryl manager and it shoud 1: give you a context menu by your curser, and 2: load in the systray. The rest should be familiar to you in order to set it all back to "automatic"
