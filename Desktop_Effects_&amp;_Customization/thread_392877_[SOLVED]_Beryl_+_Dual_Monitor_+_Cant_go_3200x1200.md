### Post by flimflam on 2007-03-25
I haven't used Envy.  I have to explain my previous issues to give you a heads up:

I have a Core 2 Duo, Nvidia 680i board (Asus p5n32-e), and 8800 GTX.  With this setup, 6.10 and the Feisty Herd's hard problems with ethernet, sound, and the xserver.  Sometimes a forcedeth change worked for the ethernet, and you had to install the alternate cd with an 8800.  

trying to just get the xserver running with this card entailed finding the nernel, and matching the source, installing the libc header files, and then installing the Nvidia Linux drivers from cdrom.  But even when I did this on 6.10 or on a Herd, the xorg.conf file would not be configured correctly, and I could not get Beryl working.  Then I had issues with Grub since I have Vista on 1 drive, Mandriva on another,  and XP and Ubuntu on another disk - getting them to place nice with each other was a pain.

So two days ago I downloaded the desktop and alternate version of the Feisty Beta.  With the Beta everything went smoothly with the build, updates, driver install, and Beryl install.  So I have everything jamming out now.

So , my point is, I would seriously try the alternate install with the 7.04 Beta.  It is the most refined and up to date version of Ubuntu yet, and the most stable.  the partitioning manager, which I think is Ubiquity or something is different though.  Nonetheless, it was the most streamlined install and configuration I have done yet - most of my issues though stem from having bleeding edge hardware.
