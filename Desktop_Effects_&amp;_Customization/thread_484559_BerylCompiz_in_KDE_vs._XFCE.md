### Post by mojoman on 2007-09-01
I'm running Xfce and I've been looking around a bit, thinking about trying out Beryl or Compiz and so far I've decided that the Xfce+Compiz combination is not a good idea. Why? Well, Compiz uses Metacity WM and I *hate* Metacity (Anyone who uses a dual monitor setup does). Beryl seems to be the way to go with Xfce but I haven't taken the plunge yet myself. Now, if you don't use dual monitors perhaps Metacity won't be such a pain in the ...

Now, as for switching to Xfce, I for one love Xfce. Compared to Gnome, I think Xfce wins by miles. I haven't used KDE much so I can't really compare them but Xfce looks like windows? :shock: (I've actually heard people say the same about KDE though I don't agree with them).

With your hardware, you might very well see a noticable increase in speed when you run on Xfce BUT travel light if you want to get the most out of it (i.e. ditch all those Gnome-applications and the ones with the silly K-names, otherwise, you'd be using the same libs as before and would probably notice less difference).
