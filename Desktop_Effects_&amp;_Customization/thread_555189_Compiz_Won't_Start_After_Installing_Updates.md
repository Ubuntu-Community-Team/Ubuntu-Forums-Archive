### Post by kroenecker on 2007-09-19
I was encountering the same kind of problem.

Solution:

sudo apt-get install xserver-glx

Now this will slow down your glxgears by about HALF its usual rate!  Just a warning.  After installing, your best bet is to restart your computer (although you might try restarting xserver with ctr-alt-del).  Upon logging back in you should see a little pop up info bubble that says that with the next xserver restart you will have glx enabled.  You might want to take note of how to disable the automatic loading of glx.

That should do it.  I was pretty frustrated to have compiz suddenly crap out on me.  But I am running Gutsy so I probably shouldn't complain :D

### Post by kroenecker on 2007-09-20
Nah.  I'm a digger.  I like to figure things out.... This is from our good friend ButteBlues who apparently spreads kernels of knowledge in other threads as opposed to the unhelpful comment left in ours.

But I'm a nice guy so I'll give credit where credits due:

Quote courtesy of ButteBlues.
> 
It's a bug in the compiz wrapper.

Do this to fix it:

Code:

sudo gedit /usr/bin/compiz

Find: "GL_MAX_3D_TEXTURE_SIZE"

Replace with: "GL_MAX_TEXTURE_SIZE"

Save and close.


Alternatively:

Code:

tmp=`sed s/GL_MAX_3D_TEXTURE_SIZE/GL_MAX_TEXTURE_SIZE/ /usr/bin/compiz`
sudo echo "$tmp" > /usr/bin/compiz

I fail at sed - that's not the best way to do it, but it'll work for now.


Muchas gracias ButteBlues!
