### Post by Gagle on 2007-07-04
Hi,

First of all, are you talking about Gnome-panels ?  Did you remove the top and bottom one ?
If so, I wonder how.  To quote from the official documentation (gnome.org) :

"You must always have at least one panel in the GNOME Desktop. If you have only one panel in the
GNOME Desktop, you cannot delete that panel."

Now I don't mean if this means you SHOULDN'T or you CAN'T .

Either way, try issuing the gnome-panel command in the terminal and post the output it yields.

Also, if you still have one panel left, you can still add another panel by right-clicking on it and choosing 'Add Panel' . 

A drastic measure would to re-install the ubuntu-desktop and gdm packages.

Anyways, I doubt this will help but at least it's a start.  :(
