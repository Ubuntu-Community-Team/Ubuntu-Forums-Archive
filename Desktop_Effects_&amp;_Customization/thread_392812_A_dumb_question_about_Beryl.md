### Post by old_geekster on 2007-03-25
> **diablo75 said:**
> How do I get it to run automatically right after I login?  I don't want to even have to select a session...just turn it on, login, and it's running right off the bat.

And here's a bonus question:

Will this ever be used to make the booting of Linux more, shall we say, "sexy"?
Go to System/Preferences/Sessions/Startup Programs/ +Add and type "beryl-manager, then OK - Reboot (Ctrl/Alt Backspace).  Now, Beryl should run when your computer boots.
