### Post by Fjatle on 2007-10-07
What triggered me to try Linux/Ubuntu was some videos on youtube which showed this fanzy panzy desktops with all kind of cool effects. 
After the install, I got this 'boring' brown desktop with nothing special in it. To make my desktop as cool as these youtubers, I was bound to google my way to make it the way i pictured it.

Now, I am not an expert PC user, but still i am the "neighbur thats fixes the PC issues" kind of guy :)
So to ME, it didn't take that many weeks before I got somehow comfirtable with this new interface.
For a more regular PC user, I guarantee that the process would take much much longer, and probably he/she give this whole thing up before they get comfirtable with it.

So here is my suggestions:
----------------------------------

After the first install, I think there should pop up a wizard (with of course extremely easy to use interface) that let the user customize their desktop in any way they want.

Example:
*would you like to use the default setup?
*would you like one panel at the bottom with only one main menu (start) button?
*Change the look (color, size) of the panel (pre-installed bg-images for panel)
*Use a dock bar instead of panels?

...Ok, you see where I am going here. This could be a long step-by-step guide that i think a newbie would appreciate very very much. 

And why stop at desktop customization? Why not take care of screen resolution and stuff like that along the way?

Windows has this "Take a tour of windows" on the first install. A bit annoying for the most of us, but for Linux (which most of the people is not familiar with), this should also be implemented.  A simple guide to easily guide a total newb to do most of the basic tasks. 

---------------------------------------------------

If Ubuntu want to be the easy to use distribution of Linux, the Ubuntuforum is simply not enough to help out people. 
In fact, when I help my neighbors with their problem, I most often do a simple search on google or a forum. This is not something everybody everybody is able to do, due to several reasons.
*not knowing the existence of such forums
*Not able to describe what their problem actually is
*...and then describe it in English?? uhh, now you're asking for much
*..many other reasons

I think I have made my point of why I think there should be a newbie-friendly step by step guide at the very beginning after an install.

And wouldn't it be nice if some of the most common  new to Linux questions could take a little brake at these forums, simply due to a wizard wich answer many questions before they even knew they didn't know? :)


Anyone agree?
