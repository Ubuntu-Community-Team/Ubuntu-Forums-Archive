### Post by irish_flu on 2007-05-22
Beryl is indeed "not ready for prime-time"; not even close.  That said, I run it on my box with no problems (though I had some problems in Edgy).

There is a project (in the back of my head, Beryl may have been born of this project) called Compiz.  If you're a Fiesty Fawn user, I believe Compiz is there and ready to use.

take a look at this thead:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=434406](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=434406)

Now, on to your other questions:
I'm not sure (the only way to know is to try) that your machine would like Beryl or Compiz much.  I would say that's a minimal amount of RAM for Gnome you have, and don't believe I'd try running a resource-heavy desktop effects program (if it were me; your opinion of necessary performance might be different from mine, of course).

Also, your machine does have a "graphics card", it's just built onto the motherboard (and not a card).  Otherwise, you'd have a hard time using a monitor.  ;)

That said, given the age of the machine (you say it's from year 2000, right?) I'd imagine the video has either 32MB or 64 MB of RAM for itself (and might even share with your 256MB of system RAM).  If it has 32MB, or if it shares your system RAM, I definitely wouldn't run those programs.  If it has 64MB, then the choice is less clear (but I would still shy away, personally).

As far as "system specs", Beryl is so new that I doubt anybody has hashed out anything like that.  I don't think your processor would be a problem (though I don't know that for a fact) but I do believe that 256MB of RAM would be a problem, and I believe you'd want a better video card.

Now, as far as which video card you should buy we could talk all day about NVidia vs. ATI.  If you search this forum, you'll find all sorts of folks espousing both (and calling both crappy, heh).  My personal preference is for NVidia cards, as they offer 3D-accelerated Linux drivers (I don't believe ATI currently does this).

However, if you do choose to buy a video card I'd make sure I saved some money to double (at least to 512MB) that machine's RAM. 

Here are a couple of other things to think about:
-How much RAM can your computer handle?  Check out the slick "memory selector" at crucial.com to see what sort of RAM you need and how much your computer can take (and then buy that memory wherever you want, heh).

-What sort of Video Card slot does that machine have?  It might be PCI, and it might be AGP (which is a lot faster).  When you're looking at new cards, many of those use a third (and fairly new) bus called "PCI-E".  Make sure you get the right one, or you won't be able to install the card into your machine (it physically will not fit into the slot).

Beryl works really good. Emerald too. I have had Beryl installed since last fall, and I've been through the early versions of 1.1+, until using the SVN builds all the way from 1.5 to 2.0 for most the winter and spring. I had to change back to a regular build of Beryl when I upgraded my xubuntu to Feisty Fawn because of the merger.

The SVN was usually the version that you sometimes had to fix if they changed something, or added something new. And also, the new plug-ins sometimes made things break, but the Feisty Beryl works fine and is very stable and very usable so don't believe the hype unless you try it.

As for your specs, I only have 512mb RAM, and 1.60GHz.

Beryl AiGLX works really nice for me on my embedded Intel graphics card. 

-but don't expect all of the fancy plug-ins to work with a low grade system, most of that stuff is tacky and really slow anyway. Beryl does have some really good plug-ins that are useful and don't slow the system much at all, so I would say search the Beryl forum archives for any users with a similar computer.
