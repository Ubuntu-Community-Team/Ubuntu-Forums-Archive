### Post by psyopper on 2007-07-25
That's a pretty heavy question. In reality you need to learn a LOT about image manipulation. In Linux the best tool you could use is The GIMP, if you are a convert from Windows and Photoshop or PaintShopPro I might suggest GIMPShop (a version of The GIMP mad to function more like PS or PSP).

You default Ubuntu installation includes a copy of The GIMP, in your main menu go to Graphics, GIMP Image Editor. For some tips, trick and tutorials [check out the T3 post at my blog]("http://psyopper.wordpress.com/t3/gimp-and-gimpshop-tips-tricks-and-tutorials-on-the-web/") to get started in learning how to use it.

Also take a look at [www.deviantart.com]("http://www.deviantart.com") and [www.gnome-look.org]("http://www.gnome-look.org") for ideas and inspiration.
