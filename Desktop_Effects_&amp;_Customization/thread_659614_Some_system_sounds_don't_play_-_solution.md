### Post by oldsmobile_mike on 2008-01-05
Hi!

I'm a new Ubuntu user (although I do have a little Solaris experience), just installed it on my Sager 5600P laptop today. For my first hair-pulling experience, I couldn't get some of the system sounds to play. The logon sound played, and sound was fine in all the media players I've tried, but the usual sounds for things like selecting menu items and logging out wouldn't play.  After reading all the forums and checking all my settings (making sure nothing was muted, installing Synaptic-esound, etc.), I managed to fix it by selecting the sounds I wanted, then disabling system sounds and ESD within sound preferences, then logging out, then logging back in and re-enabling system sounds and ESD, then logging out again, and logging back in one more time, and now all my system sounds are working.  Yay!

Hope this solution helps someone else.  :-)
