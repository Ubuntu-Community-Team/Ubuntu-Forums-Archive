### Post by dld on 2008-01-30
I ran Fedoras for a number of years; window geometries were always available with a right click.  Under Ubuntu I cannot find a way to get a listing of the geometries of all open windows.  I would like to size and position a window, look up its geometry, and then enter that geometry in devilspie, rather than trial and error editing of a .ds file.  Any ideas??

---

### Post by Fraktyl on 2008-01-31
Not sure if you can enable it with the window manager to display it, but you can do the following in a terminal window

xwininfo

Then click the window you want information on.  It'll give you the geometry.
