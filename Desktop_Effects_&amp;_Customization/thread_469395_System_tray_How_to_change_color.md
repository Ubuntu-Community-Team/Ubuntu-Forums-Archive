### Post by Vil on 2007-06-14
> **lsutiger said:**
> I have attached a picture for reference...I lined the area ( horribly) in red.

How can I have the system tray (if that's what it is called) take on the color of the panel?

The entire bar at the top (and the one at the bottom) is called a panel. Gnome panels do not have separate parts like the taskbar does in WIndows; instead, they use applets which can be moved anywhere you like on any panel . The trick with Gnome panels and transparency is that some applets can be transparent and others are unable to be transparent... That is what you are having difficulty with right now. KDE can do (afaik) full transparency for just about everything.

You can see in the following screenshots of one of my panels what I am talking about.
Transparent:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=35355&stc=1&d=1181828564](http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=35355&stc=1&d=1181828564)

Not Transparent:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=35356&stc=1&d=1181828564](http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=35356&stc=1&d=1181828564)

As you can see, in the first screenshot, just about everything is transparent, but the character palette applet is very much not.
