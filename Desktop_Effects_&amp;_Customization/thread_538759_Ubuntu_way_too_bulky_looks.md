### Post by Twilight in Zero on 2008-06-23
I know exactly what you mean. The screenshot up there has all the widgets way too HUGE. And the spacing is no better.

It was weird to think about it today; KDE's panel is HUGE and doesn't size down well, but qt is small and sleek (at least from what limited I've seen). But Gnome's panels are small and sleek while GTK is HUGE. ...by default.

Fortunately, there are compact GTK themes!

Clearlooks Compact: [http://martin.ankerl.com/2007/11/04/clearlooks-compact-gnome-theme/](http://martin.ankerl.com/2007/11/04/clearlooks-compact-gnome-theme/)
Human Compact: [http://martin.ankerl.com/2008/05/13/human-compact-gnome-theme/](http://martin.ankerl.com/2008/05/13/human-compact-gnome-theme/)
And my favorite, Compact-Colors: [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Compact-Colors?content=78182](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Compact-Colors?content=78182)

All the gtkrc files in Compact-Colors have comment documentation, so you can tweak them to your liking, and know what you're doing. It's actually necessary if you want to use colors other than green, red, blue, or orange. :P

It's too bad that GTK is so bulky by default, though. It's really off-putting. :|
