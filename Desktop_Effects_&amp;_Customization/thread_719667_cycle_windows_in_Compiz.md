### Post by wanchai on 2008-03-10
What do you mean by alt+tab? This is just key combination that Compiz allows you to assign to different functions. Even in Metacity you can assign these things. I think we have to go back to Windows in order to find a fixed meaning for alt+tab. And yes, these basic switchers as they are implemented in Windows and Metacity are what I have in mind.

Why? Many times I work in a way where I am reading something in one window and typing in another, for example reading a HowTo in Firefox and hacking something in a console, or reading something in a fullscreen PDF and typing in a fullscreen spreadsheet. In these cases I need to switch back and forth between these two windows frequently, and then I find it annoying if the whole screen dims and the windows shrink and dance in circles or whatever. What I'm looking for is a function to simply raise the window  that had the focus before the current one, nothing more, just that: raise the previous window. All the window switchers in Compiz are great if I have to go deeper into the stack, but for toggling the last two, they are visual overkill.

### Post by raul_ on 2008-03-11
Just the regular application-switcher that is mapped to alt+tab by default. Disable the ring switcher and the Shift Switcher, go to Application Switcher, map it to alt+tab, set the brightness and opacity setting to what you want.
