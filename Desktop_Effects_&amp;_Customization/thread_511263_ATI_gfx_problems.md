### Post by khughitt on 2007-07-30
I normally have to have composite on to get compiz working myself. How are you logging into your desktop? Are you using a custom start-up script, or just loggin into regular gnome?

Post your **/etc/X11/xorg.conf ** file.

The fact that fglrxinfo still lists mesa means that your drivers probably aren't loading correctly for some reason. First I would check your xorg.conf and make sure everything looks okay there, another option is you could try re-running aticonfig --initial (after backing up your current xorg.conf), and then re-enable compiz in the new config file and restarting x-windows. Finally, if none of this helps, you can try a slightly older version of the driver. For me, using the one from april or may of 2007 worked great, but then upgrading to the newer version from june broke compiz.
