### Post by picopir8 on 2007-10-23
I like the default panels but I want to spice them up ever so slightly and make them look a bit more 3D.   I also want to avoid installing something like AWN which would be overkill for what I want.

My temporary solution was to create a move my panel and put a blank panel in its place, then adjust the color and transparency to my liking.  I then took a screen shot, opened the screen shot in gimp, and cropped the panel.  I then masked the top half of the panel image and lightened it, inverted the mask and darkened the bottom half.  I saved the resulting image and then modified my panel to use it as a background image.  It gives the appearance of a semi-transparent 3D panel.

The only problem is I will have to repeat the process if I change my wallpaper.  The whole process takes <5 min so it is easy enough to do but if there is an easier way to make existing panels appear less "flat" then I would much rather do that.
