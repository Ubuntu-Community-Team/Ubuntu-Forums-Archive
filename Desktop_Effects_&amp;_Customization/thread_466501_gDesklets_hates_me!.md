### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2007-06-06
I've installed gDesklets many times over the last few years, and every time has unfortunately resulted in a nearly immediate removal.

I won't turn this into a rant (although I would like to know: Am I the only one who is driven crazy by the thing?), but I found its interface unintuitive with the presence of controls and desklets; the process of adding desklets slow; and the desklets available by default via Ubuntu's repos redundant, often unstable and often hard to configure (and the fact that I always think the word "configure" with them proves how much work these need: A desklet should by a tiny program; an applet; something that requires no configuration and works out of the box! Yes, that includes RSS readers. I should not have to right click any of these and go to a huge configurator. If necessary, a little button would work fine).

So, as you can see, I am not very impressed with gdesklets. Maybe I'm missing something (lots of people seem to get fine stuff with it!), and I'm not saying it is an unworthy project (I do like the idea of desklets), but I really do not want to try it again unless I am certain that I was just using it wrong or installed the wrong package.

Thus, my question: What other well supported and mature desklet systems are available for Gnome?
