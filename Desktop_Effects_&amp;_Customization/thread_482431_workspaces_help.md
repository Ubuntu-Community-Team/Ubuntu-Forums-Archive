### Post by drakos on 2007-07-19
Yeah, I found that desktop effects in general are pretty bugging on some systems. Mine would remove the panels on the other workspaces too. If you don't care about the wobbly windows, and the cube, just turn off desktop effects all together. I would recommend, if your interested, that you install beryl-manager. It does everything that standard desktop effects claims it does, with many more options, and fewer bugs. But I digress. In summation: turn off desktop effects and that'll stop your disappearing panels.
