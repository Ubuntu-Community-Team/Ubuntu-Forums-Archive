### Post by ZeldaFan on 2007-08-28
Well most people are probably thinking that Beryl is obviously going to slow down my computer and take up more resources than without it. But usually that is negligible to me, since I have a rather new computer with nice specs (core 2 duo processor 2 gb RAM). However after an hour or so of usage, beryl oddly becomes extremely resource intensive. The change is significant and sudden. All of a sudden I notice considerable lag in the desktop cube and dragging windows. However, it's nothing wrong with my computer, because if I log off and back on, beryl goes back to normal. For some reason beryl abruptly slows down my computer after some time, and I was wondering if there was any way to fix it.

Also this a side note, but at least for me, beryl in a way usually makes my computer feel faster. For some reason, without beryl, dragging windows in ubuntu causes window lag when dragging them over other windows that are more resource intensive like openoffice and firefox. Shades of the window movement remain for fractions of a second. But with beryl such lag is eliminated, weirdly enough. It was one of the main reasons I installed beryl. While it took more resources, it oddly made my computer "faster"...
