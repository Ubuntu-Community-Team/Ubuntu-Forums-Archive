### Post by urukrama on 2007-10-25
You can basically use any panel you want with Openbox. Pypanel seems a favourite of Openbox users, as is fbpanel, lxpanel, perlpanel, [tint task manager]("http://code.google.com/p/ttm/") (just a task list) and visibility (and icon box/task list). You can also use gnome-panel, xfce4-panel or kicker. Just play around, test them out, and use the one you like best.

I either use no panel at all (when I use pure Openbox), or I use xfce4-panel (when I use Openbox as a window manager within Xfce). Occasionally I use pypanel as well.
