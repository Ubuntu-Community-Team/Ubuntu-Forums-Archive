### Post by Cresho on 2008-01-23
well, what can I say.

GTK is the user interface
Metacity is the window border equivalent to emerald but emerald takes it to a new level.

You can choose many many many themes.  MANY.

[http://art.gnome.org/themes/gtk2/](http://art.gnome.org/themes/gtk2/)

[http://www.gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=100&PHPSESSID=9b1bda332217373c81ea551069a03226](http://www.gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=100&PHPSESSID=9b1bda332217373c81ea551069a03226)

easy to install.

just right click on screen click on change background and them choose theme and choose install and look for where you put the files at.  YOu can make it look dark, 100% vista or windows xp, or mac....or choose your own poison.  I spent weeks looking at all of them.  after install, you may need to choose what you want under the tabs to get the theme.  Lots of mix and match as well.

### Post by Cresho on 2008-01-26
to uninstall emerald, open synaptic package manager under Administration and do a search for emerald and right click on it so you can mark for uninstall on these files.:
emerald
libemeraldengine0

Reboot and now you can use metacity with gtk again.

here are a few examples of a full metacity and gtk theme.
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/T-ish-Brushed+Pack?content=30972](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/T-ish-Brushed+Pack?content=30972)
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/MacOS-X+Aqua+Theme?content=13548](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/MacOS-X+Aqua+Theme?content=13548)
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Neos?content=24229](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Neos?content=24229)
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Truth+for+linux?content=45987](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Truth+for+linux?content=45987)
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/OrangeLiNstaBlackPlastic?content=62434](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/OrangeLiNstaBlackPlastic?content=62434)

Metacity is default of ubuntu.  just rightclick on screen and select change desktop.  Choose theme and custom button.  another window will pop up and you can choose controls, colors, window border(metacity, icons, fonts, etc..  YOu can even change the mouse cursor.  I use one called "grounation" from gnome-themes.  It is the best cursor theme in my opinion.  Very animated and high details.  do a xkill in terminal and you can see the best xkill cursor in my opinion.(be carefull what you kill on your desktop).cancel xkill with mouse right click.
