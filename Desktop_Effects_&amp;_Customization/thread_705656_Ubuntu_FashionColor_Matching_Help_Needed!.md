### Post by anoc on 2008-03-02
Hmm, this is kind of a loaded question. Are you looking for pixmap themes?

If not, you should know some default themes that exist have color customization. Clearlooks, Glider, & Glossy all can be changed to have whatever colors you choose for the windows, input boxes, and selections (and their associated text).

If you're using compiz-fusion/emerald for your window frames, most of those have a much more detailed level of customization. You can then blend the frames into the windows based on whatever colors you used, or keep the frames separate. Play around with different colors and opacities to see their effects.

You can use the eyedropper cursor (similar to Gcolor2) to get exact colors from any wallpaer you have.

Many of the themes you can get on gnome-lok.org and other sites have that same color customization enabled once installed.
