### Post by Inxsible on 2007-08-30
I made the switch and I am pretty happy with it. That is not to say that it will work for you. There are a number of issues with Compiz Fusion, because it is still in development phase.

But with Linux, you can always try it and if you don't like it, get rid of it :)

Make sure however, that you uninstall Beryl completely before installing Compiz Fusion. I have heard people have had trouble having both on their systems.

### Post by jbysmith on 2007-08-30
Yea it *is* pretty slick... but be prepared for breakage or other weirdness.

I'm using the one from Trevino's repository, which is pretty much bleeding edge on updates.  Currently, it has issues with the latest Wine (not from Buntu's repo, the 0.9.44 version), and the 3D effects are a little wonky as well.  But thats the nature of bleeding edge.. sure it'll be sorted out soon. That aside it's working pretty well.  Seems faster than Beryl once you tweak it, and the new effects are downright awesome.  (I'm in love with the shift switcher)

And as the previous poster suggested, get rid of Beryl beforehand to prevent problems.

If anything, give it a spin.  If it's too problematic for you, you can always remove it, remove the repo, and reinstall Beryl rather easily.
