### Post by FoolsGold on 2007-04-01
I've seen screenshots of people's desktops where they've been running beryl, and I often see the top gnome-panel cast a shadow onto the desktop. I've got beryl running, and I don't see any shadow from the panel. Windows have shadows, menus/tooltips have shadows, so why doesn't my gnome-panel cast a shadow?

Is there an option I've missed, or have they just added a fake shadow to their wallpaper via GIMP or something? Ta.

---

### Post by FuturePilot on 2007-04-01
Open Beryl Settings Manager. Then go to the Visual Effects tab then click on Window Decoration. Expand the Appearance menu and check the box that says Draw shadows on panel-type windows. That should do it:)
