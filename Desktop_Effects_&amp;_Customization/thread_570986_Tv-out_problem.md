### Post by damianfrancis on 2007-10-21
The short answer to your first question 'how do I fix this problem' is to make sure you have the Twinview option on, and commenting out crap that might cause a problem while removing lines that almost certainly will.

Google "linux install nvidia drivers" or read the install instructions from nvidia's site and you'll see several things wrong with your config file/files

> **magikx21 said:**
> I'm not showing a grey tv in nvidia-settings, my pc boots up the tv flicker and it shows a grey screen.

What have you tried so far? And what do you mean "nvidia-settings?" 

Here are the issues I see with getting the tv-out working (and working may not mean flawless).
1.Be aware that the "nvidia" module will not be installed on your system by default.
I don't know enough about the nv (open) driver to know if it can control the card to do what we want, and all the conversations on the net about it assume your using the nvidia driver.  
-If you have a new setup you'll already have the newest one, but if your just now trying to start using tvout on a card that used an older version of the driver go ahead and update Ubuntu and install/activated the nvidia module.
2. As far as I can tell or have seen the little app that Ubuntu has in their update tool for controlling the tvout on nvidia cards, is crap.  I've only gotten it to load once or twice and it didn't seem to have any instructions on what to do to get the tv-out to opperate propperly.  Which brings me to the third issue.
3. Understand that there are two things that could be keeping you from displying on the tv.  The module or the hardware.  You can see pretty easy however that the hardware works is you plug it in before starting your box--with or without the other port active--and see the screen initialized.  
4.If you have the newest modules installed and know the hardware can initialize the display move on to modifying your x-config file.  It should work with the right config settings.

But  **magikx21** where are you getting these x-config files?  

You need two basic, no three, things to make this work.  The card, it's driving software, and the right confige file.

Don't just try to load someone elses x-config file!  I've read from several different peoples discussions (at least two or three on Ubuntu forums) the modifications which must be in place to activate the tv-out of a one of the card's  covered be the nvidia unified open driver.
I don't know of a software util supplied though Ubuntu that will help you set up X.  So your going to have to either read some or all the installer instructions from nvidia's site to understand what options to comment out, add in, and why; or you can blindly try mixtures of other peoples config files hopeing one works.

I'm not trying to be a jerk I'm honestly trying to help you and anyone elsewho is asking the kinds of questions you have.  If your questions aren't specific you'll either get short cut off answers or ones that try to cover all the bases steering you towards undersanding and better questions.    I'll try to get back and give you some better pointers and links when it's not the middle of the night but here are a couple how-to's that will help.
