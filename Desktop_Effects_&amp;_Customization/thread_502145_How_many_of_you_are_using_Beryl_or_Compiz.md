### Post by pbaehr on 2007-07-16
I run compiz-fusion and I'm very happy with it.

Whether or not you decide you're ready to install it depends on what kind of new user you are. Are you interested in learning or are you just looking for a computer that works no matter what?

If you like to tinker and you're not afraid of spending a few hours on the forum repairing the damage if something goes wrong, go for it.

If you are going to become frustrated if you hit a bump along the way, it may not be worth the trouble.

### Post by maestrobwh1 on 2007-07-16
I am using beryl.  I have tried compiz as well, but oddly prefer beryl. Most people seem to like compiz, although from what I understand, it looks like there is a fusion project that takes the best of both.  I tried fusion compiz also, but did not like the white outline some of my menus had and could not figure out how to get rid of them.  I also lost my theme manager.

I use KDE (Kubuntu) rather than Gnome (Ubuntu) and these things are trickier for me.  It is easy enough (if you don't put a shortcut in your Autostart menu) to recover if you launch it and it fails.  If you get it installed, you can configure it to fall back to your normal window manager.  I only once or twice had it bail to the point of falling back to the login screen. 

My experience with Beryl
It is unstable ESPECIALLY when running firefox, swiftfox, or any graphical wine programs, at least for me.  Otherwise It does indeed look very cool. It NEVER broke my system though.  I drop down to my usual windows manager when doing anything important.  At worst, it just won't work... and that might just be until you do some research to tweak settings for your particular graphics card.  If you have an integrated card that shares your system memory, forget it.  It it is a SIS graphics integrated video, as if found on many laptops, REALLY REALLY forget it (I should have researched this first).  I tried compiz on my laptop with one one these cards and the worst thing that happened is is that I had to hard boot the machine and the settings didn't take effect.

Again, don't follow any directions to autostart anything until you know it works, at least to your satisfaction, and don't type a term paper or do some major project with it enabled unless you are good at saving every few minutes;-)  

Side notes:
1. However, if you are running Kubuntu feisty (I have installed all of the kde artwork and themes using adept/synaptic), I noticed that KDE 3.5.7, if you right click the top of any window, and select "configure window behavior" the top option "window decoration, if it is set to crystal, you get some very similar options under "window decorations" tab.  if you choose the sub tab background, you can get some transparency options.  

I have also tried the bottom option for this KDE options menu: "translucency," and it does work, but it REALLY slowed down my desktop computer and although not top of the line, it is a 2.2 GHZ athalon XP with 2 MB of ram.  With it activated... it does some really nice shadow 3D-ish looking effects, but so will beryl and compiz and those seem to alt least run "normally" in this regard.  I think the KDE feature is still experimental.  I did not have beryl running with this particular option... just seemed to be a bad idea.

Out of the box, KDE 3.5.7 is really nice these days even without these extra things.  I don't really use Gnome, as I could not get used to it being that I was a Microsoft user for so many years.  KDE eased my linux transition:)  Gnome however has been reported to work better with beryl or compiz.

I am running Kubuntu Gutsy Gibbon (experimental) on one of my partitions and my screen is even sharper still.
