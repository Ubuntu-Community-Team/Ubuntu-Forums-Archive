A standard functionality in X windowing systems for as far back as I can remember (10 years) is "snap to edges," meaning that when two windows are brought into proximity their edges "snap" together.  This default behavior prevents you from overlapping edges unless you want to and saves you fiddling them, trying to get them "as close together as possible."  It's very handy.

I had this enabled in XFCE via a checkbox in the desktop configuration menu.  I can't find a corresponding item in Gnome.  Does it exist, and if so, where?  

Also, while I'm thinking about windows, is it possible to enable in Gnome the ability to drag windows from one desktop to another?  It doesn't seem to work OOB.  I find that handy sometimes, as opposed to clicking the title bar icon and selecting the 'move to another workspace' option.
