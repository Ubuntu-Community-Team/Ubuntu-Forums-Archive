### Post by applehead on 2008-03-25
A good way, is to get emerald.  If you type

sudo apt-get install emerald

in the terminal, and then 

emerald --replace

you will have emerald theme manager in the system - preference menu.  Then, if you go to 

[http://www.gnome-look.org/index.php?...d5cad1e715c34c]("http://www.gnome-look.org/index.php?...d5cad1e715c34c")

there's loads of emerald themes, download one, then go to your emerald theme manager and on the 'themes' tab click 'import' then point it to where you downloaded your theme. when you see your theme in the theme manager just click on it.

Happy days.:)
