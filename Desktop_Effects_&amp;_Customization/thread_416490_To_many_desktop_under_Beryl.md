### Post by CameO73 on 2007-04-21
Go to the Beryl Settings Manager --> General Options (actually the first tab you see when it opens)
The options that are important here are 'Horizontal Virtual Size' (default: 4), 'Vertical Virtual Size' (default: 1) and 'Number of Desktops' (default: 1). This gives a default  desktop that is split over 4 screens. 

Btw, watch out when you use the mouse wheel to scroll through the options; you're not the first to inadvertently set some options that are very hard to find again!

### Post by kvonb on 2007-04-21
To change the number of desktops, look on the Beryl settings manager under "General options", "horizontal virtual size" is the amount of desktops on the cube.

I have no idea what "Vertical virtual size" does, I leave it at "1".

"Number of desktops" is an interesting one, it seems to give more than one "set" of desktops, ie sort of like more than one cube, you get 4 desktops on the first, then another 4 on the second and so on.

If you get too messed up with your settings, you could always delete or rename your .beryl folder.

That will set it back to defaults, maybe quit Beryl-manager first, delete the folder, then start Beryl-manager.
