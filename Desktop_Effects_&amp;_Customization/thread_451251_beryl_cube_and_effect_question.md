^In that video there are two questions I have, how do I make my cube work like that (it seems like you go further from the screen and the windows are shown and u see the background and stuff) and after wards he seperates all the windows to a list or something on screen, whats the command for that?

It's all set up in the Beryl options.  Look at the section on the 3d cube, and there's a setting that controls how far to zoom out when in the cube view (I'm not on my linux box right now, maybe someone else can give the specific name/location of this setting?).  There are countless things you can change in the options: how far from the desktop windows raise when in cube view, a background color/gradient/image, etc.

As for viewing all windows, hit F8 or F9 to view all windows on the current desktop or all windows on all desktops (respectively).  By default, I believe moving your mouse to the lower right corner of the screen also brings all windows up like F9 does.  These keys can also be changed to your liking in the options.  You also have alt-tabbing abilities.
