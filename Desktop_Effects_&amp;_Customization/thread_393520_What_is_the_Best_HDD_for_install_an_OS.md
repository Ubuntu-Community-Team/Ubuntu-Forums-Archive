they have been having some growing pains with SATA, but i think if you do a new install and not a upgrade, things have been okey.  The SATA is the new interface for commercial computers, and its biggest asset is the 4pin cable as apposed to an 80 pin cable... the performace improvement is present, but not huge.   eg. its not worth upgrading a motheboard for SATA

### Post by AtrejuT on 2007-03-26
Basically the faster it turns (so the more rpm's it has) the quicker the seek time which is probably more relavant then the transfer rate. I doubt you'll feel the difference very much, though.

You could theoretically consider a RAID 0 if you're building a new system, but this is rather fragile to data corruption - if one of the disks breaks all data is gone!! - so be careful!

If I was you, I'd just buy a disk of the shelf basically (as mentioned above, have a look at the compatibility list first) - the outcome of something more complicated won't be worth the hassle.
