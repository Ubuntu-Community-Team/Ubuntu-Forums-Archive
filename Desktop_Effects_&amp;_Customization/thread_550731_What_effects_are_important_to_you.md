### Post by pointone on 2007-09-14
Transparency on my terminal is great for copying commands and whatnot from websites without having to switch back and forth between windows a dozen times.

Zoom plugin is really great for when I'm showing stuff off to co-workers and friends, so they don't all have to crowd in around my screen.

Wobbly windows is pretty much a must have for me now; whenever I use another computer and move the windows around, it just feels really strange. Guess it's grown on me... :D

### Post by smartboyathome on 2007-09-14
Desktop Wall makes things pretty easy and smooth for switching desktops. I disable it and enable the Desktop Cube when showing it off. Also, I use the Widget Function a lot, as it helps me clear away all the widgets when I don't want to see them. The Grouping plugin is great for when you want to group several GIMP windows (all the pictures, not the control) into one. A cosmetic plugin that help me are the shift switcher plugin (it lets me see the windows and is bigger than the Application switcher included by default).

### Post by Rupertronco on 2007-09-14
The transparency is also an excellent tool to use in gimp for comparing images, I love it. The most useful tools are definitely the multiple switcher plug-ins (ring, shift, and scale) makes finding everything you have running a snap. 

The wall and cube are excellent for managing multiple desktops, I prefer the cube to the wall (i have it bound to the mouse it makes navigating the desktops on the cube 10x easier)

The zoom is great if what you're trying to read is small, or if you use it on a laptop/notebook. I have all my textbooks for this semester in .pdf and the negetive windows (white on black background) makes reading them much easier on the eyes. 

The majority of the other programs are for showing off, or just asthetic appeal.
