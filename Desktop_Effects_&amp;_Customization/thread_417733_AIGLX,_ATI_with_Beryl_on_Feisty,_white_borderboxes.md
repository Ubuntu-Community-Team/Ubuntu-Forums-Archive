### Post by Abdi110 on 2007-04-22
I found a fix here...

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=293002&highlight=option+name%3Dallow_large_textures+value%3D2](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=293002&highlight=option+name%3Dallow_large_textures+value%3D2)

Sadly this seems to drop performance down, to the point where other have switched to 16 bit color. After reading the treads linked there, and talking with a friend who's got about the same machine, I'm getting the impression that there's some magical way where even if set your xorg.conf to 16 bit color, all of the applications and everything still display in 24 bit. Either there truly is a magical way to do this, which I haven't figured out, or people aren't as quick to spot a machine running at 16 bit color when viewing photos.

I gave compiz a go (with that fix I just linked to in place) and in the performance department it seems to work a whole lot faster then beryl. Conky on my desktop isn't too happy right now, but I'm sure I can fix that. The two things that are bugging me about compiz is it's not playing nice with Emerald for windows decorations, and it doesn't have all the nifty effects that beryl has.

The only reason I'm running AIGLX right now is because power management seems to function correctly under Feisty. With Edgy going into suspend would still leave the GPU running, which in turn would kill the battery far quicker. Back then I ran FGLRX and beryl ran fine. The system would eventually get bogged down and X would restart without any sort of errors. A friend recommended I stick with AIGLX due to it being open sourced and FGLRX not.
