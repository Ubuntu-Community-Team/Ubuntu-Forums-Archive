### Post by strumluff on 2007-05-04
pappo Beryl will work fine on an ATI card, just follow the wiki guide to installing Beryl with an XGL session as ATI drivers do not currently support AIGLX, although I hear it is possible on certain cards, same goes for Compiz really so its your choice which you prefer to use. Compiz has less features but is more stable, Compiz and Beryl will be merging again in the future though so bear in mind it is still early days.

Select the guide you need according to your ubuntu version and card:

[http://wiki.beryl-project.org/wiki/Install_Beryl_on_Ubuntu](http://wiki.beryl-project.org/wiki/Install_Beryl_on_Ubuntu)

AIGLX is out-of-the-box in Ubuntu; this is why the ATI guide for Beryl is complex, because it instructs the user to grab the XGL packages and configure a special session for running it (which allows Beryl to work).

Compiz is what Ubuntu uses for the "Desktop Effects" preference pane, yes, but its functionality is limited, especially for us ATI users.
