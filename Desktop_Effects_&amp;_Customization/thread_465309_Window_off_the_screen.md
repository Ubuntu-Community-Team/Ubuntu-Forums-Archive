### Post by Hendrixski on 2007-06-05
you don't have to uninstall beryl, just change the settings to be less extreme.

if you bring an application to the front you can press ALT+SPACE and a little menu pops up (it's OK if you don't see it) then if you hit m ,  the lower case letter M you can move that application around the screen with the left, right, up and down keys.  What you can probably do then is wiggle around with that until you get it in the main window.

then Beryl should remember where you moved it to and that's where it'll open the applications in the future.

hope that helps.  P.S. beryl is beta... I hope you're not using it at work.
