### Post by mcduck on 2007-07-02
They all work. But GDM themes are for the Login Screen, not for your normal applications. They are installed from System/Administration/Login Screen (or whatever it's called).

For the desktop you'll want GTK2-themes, andMetacity themes for window borders.THese can both be installed from System/Preferences/Theme or by extracting into ~/.themes or /usr/share/themes

edit Some themes may need you to install additional GTK2-engines, like pixbuf or Murrine. Pixbuf at least is in repositories, but I think Murrine is not in repositories so you need to get it from Gnome-Look.
