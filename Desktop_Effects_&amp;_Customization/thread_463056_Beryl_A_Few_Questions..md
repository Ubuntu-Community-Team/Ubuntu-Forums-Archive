### Post by BKonkle on 2007-06-03
1.) Beryl is a Window Manager program that will give your windows transparency, animation, and 3D effects.  It allows you to place your desktop on a cube, so that you can hold down CTRL+ALT on the keyboard and move the mouse to rotate the cube and pick the workspace you want to work with.  It makes your windows wobble when they are moved, and gives them nice animated minimize/restore open/close effects.  Some of them make the windows spin down to the taskbar when minimized and spin back up to your screen when maximized.  Others give them a genie-in-a-bottle style swirl down to the taskbar and a matching swirl back up.  I use an effect that allows windows to "Glide" in and out when I open and close them.

2.) I think you may have difficulty running Beryl on the setup you described below.  I tried it on my wife's laptop, which has a AMD Mobile Athlon 2000+, 512 MB of memory (128MB of which are shared with the graphics), and an ATI Radeon Mobile graphics card.  Beryl was rather laggy and seemed to moderately slow the desktop down.  I tried the Desktop Effects (System-->Preferences-->Desktop Effects), and it performed much better.

3.) If you go out and buy an Nvidia card, expect the installation to be quick, easy, and pain free.  Anything else, and expect to spend a few hours ironing out all the kinks.  I used an ATI Radeon 9200 previously, and it took quite a bit of troubleshooting to get it to work.  I finally had to settle on the open source driver and AIGLX before it worked.  Now that I own an Nvidia 7300GT, I installed the nvidia-glx-new drivers and the installation was a simple copy&paste maneuver.  I had the new nvidia drivers and Beryl both running within 15 minutes.
