### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2007-12-01
ahh I see.
I think that user is either A: using KDE4 that is still in development and not recommended right now
or B: he is using superkaramba.
It definitely looks like KDE4 but ehh you can make KDE3 look pretty good too.
I have made KDE look fairly decent myself:
[vistalike 1]("http://www.linfx.com/coppermine/albums/userpics/10174/vistalike1.png")


if you like how that looks there is a easy way to make your panel look wonderful, just right click on the panel and under "panel menu" select "configure panel"
under "appearance" you can start to customize how the panel looks
now the choices are limited initially but you can get panels from [KDE look]("http://www.kde-look.org/")

they have a section called "kicker background" where you can select a vast array of backgrounds.
most of them come in zip files and some of them are pretty direct.
after you are done getting a panel you like you can use it as your panel background
as I said to make these panel backgrounds your background just unzip them or just pop them in a directory somewhere
I personally have a folder called kicker backgrounds (kicker is the name of the panel in KDE), you can make one yourself.
you can just take whatever kicker backgrounds you unzipped or downloaded and pop it in there.
Now some of the applets I have in my screenshot are not available to kde in ubuntu or kubuntu.
there are many ways to configure KDE, in its looks, its icons and all that stuff
most of the icons and kicker backgrounds are available on kde look
but styles and window decorations are another matter, most on kdelook are source code only so it might be best to download some themes from the repositories.
in synaptic or adept or whatever search for styles like qtcurve, polyester and thinkeramik, these themes are considered some of the best.
