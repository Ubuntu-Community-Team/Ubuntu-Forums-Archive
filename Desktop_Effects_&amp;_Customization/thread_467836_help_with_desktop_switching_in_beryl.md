### Post by Nythain on 2007-06-09
this would be a good time to point out that beryl doesnt use "workspaces" or "desktops"

it uses whats refered to as viewports
not to accurately but easily explained would clones of your desktop/workspace rendered again in different locations, like the sides of the cube.

for this reason, keep your gnome "workspaces" or kde "desktops" at one, anymore seriously confuses beryl and your desktop environment
