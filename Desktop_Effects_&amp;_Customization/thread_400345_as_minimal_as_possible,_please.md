### Post by justin whitaker on 2007-04-03
> **bostoys said:**
> which is the most lightweight DE? and what services can most people generally turn off?

Well, the lightest ones that I know of are aewm++, wmii, ion3, and twm. These are not for the faint of heart: they are heavily console and keyboard centric, and require quite a bit of knowledge to configure. 

The next step up are the *boxes: fluxbox, blackbox, openbox. These give you are really low memory footprint, but are still functional, and look really slick when configured. The menu is a mere right click away, and is fairly sane. 

You can also look at windowmaker, fvwm, and a host of others.

A good general resource is here: [http://xwinman.org/](http://xwinman.org/)

Most of these are in the repos. 

As for services...I don't have a printer, so I ditched CUPs. If you installed any servers, you can shut them off until you need them. 

There is a great guide here (use at your own risk...may be dated):

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=89491](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=89491)

And more performance/customization tips here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=189192](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=189192)
