```
sudo /etc/init.d/gdm start
```

### Post by Victormd on 2008-01-10
In my case, I installed the restricted drivers and could not get back either. What I had to do was to edit my xorg.conf file:
sudo su /etc/X11/xorg.conf

and add the line:
Option		"NvAGP"		"2"

as the last line under: Section "Device" in the xorg.conf file

Furthermore, you might have to disable your ATI card in the BIOS...

### Post by salefish on 2008-01-11
Excuse me if this is off topic, I'm a real noob, but I had the same problem and it was so easy to fix on mine it was actually stupid.
I installed in low graphics mode, the only way it would load, and could not change my resolution from 800x600. I enabled my NVIDIA restricted driver, or I thought that had enabled it. I now have my doubts. I restarted, nothing changed.
