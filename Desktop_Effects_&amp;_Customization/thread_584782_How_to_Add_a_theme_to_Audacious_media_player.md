### Post by Lozz on 2007-10-21
I will admit this is probably one of the simplest how to threads ever but I've only just found out Audacious supports themes & was quite impressed with them. 

1. Find any XMMS theme, there are quite a few on GNOME-look.org under the XMMS subheading

2. Extract the theme's main folder to your home folder

3. run the following in terminal 

>  sudo mv _name of your theme folder_ /usr/share/audacious/Skins/ 

4. Right click on the Audacious player & go down to the preferences option. In preferences select appearance, hit the refresh button in the top right hand corner & select your theme from the list.

As I said before this is fairly simple stuff but I thought someone may like it. Enjoy.

### Post by kostkon on 2007-10-22
I am not sure, but I believe that *Audacious* can also use *Winamp* skins (i.e. themes). Why I am saying this?

Because, *Audacious* is a fork of *BMP* which in turn is a fork of *XMMS* (the *BMP* devs actually just wanted (mostly) to port *XMMS* to *GTK2*). 

*XMMS* supports *Winamp* skins and, thus, there is a possibility that *Audacious* can use them too.
