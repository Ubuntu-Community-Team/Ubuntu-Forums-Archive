### Post by 67GTA on 2007-11-25
Assuming you have ```
compizconfig-settings-manager
``` installed, open the advanced desktop effects settings in SYSTEM>PREFERENCES, check the box next to animations. Then click on the animations button(the part I was missing). Then click on the close effects tab. There will be three lines at the top; glide, fade, and another fade. Double click on the glide entry to open the advanced settings. There you will see a drop down menu where you can select whatever effect you want to happen when you close a window, along with the duration and color. The other settings in Advanced Desktop Settings Manager work much the same way.

### Post by 67GTA on 2007-11-25
Compiz Fusion is installed already. I'm using Linux Mint, and the main settings manager(compizconfig-settings-manager) was left out by default in Mint. It may be installed already in Ubuntu. Open your menu and look in SYSTEM>PREFERENCES. If Advanced Desktop Effects Settings is there, then you have what you need, provided you have a 3D graphics card and driver installed. Just follow my instructions in the above post to enable the effects. If you don't have a supported 3D graphics card, Compiz won't run.
