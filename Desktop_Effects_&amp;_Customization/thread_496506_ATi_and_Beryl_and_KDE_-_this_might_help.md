### Post by maestrobwh1 on 2007-07-09
I have an older ATi card, Radeon 7200 with 64 MB.  It was quirky when running Beryl and I came across this and it seemed to have smoothed out a few things, and Beryl seems more stable, at least when using Firefox/Swiftfox.  I had issues where stuff would just start behaving oddly.  One thing is my scroller on my mouse would stop scrolling the page and would enlarge text instead.  Also, no shortcuts on my desktop would work, unless I right clicked them and chose open.  

[http://forum.beryl-project.org/viewtopic.php?f=36&t=4493&p=25526&hilit=smooth+beryl+radeon+driver#p25526]("http://forum.beryl-project.org/viewtopic.php?f=36&t=4493&p=25526&hilit=smooth+beryl+radeon+driver#p25526")

Follow the directions exactly, except I used Kate rather than nano to do my editing.  For one thing, the first instruction tells you to move beryl to a new filename, and it looks like beryll, but it is actually beryl1 (a 1, "one" not an extra l, "L").  Use cut and paste and just change the editor name to your choice.  I also saved all of the altered files first, including the one in /usr/bin called "beryl-settings," to another place... just in case I could not boot back into KDE.  Knoppix-live CD is a wonderful thing to have if you don't do things from the command line well, as you can just boot using that, and move the originals back easily.  I didn't have to do this however, and everything worked right the first time.

EDIT: I am using Kubuntu-Feisty.  I am not sure what the result will be on other distros, and I don't think these tweaks are needed in Ubuntu with Gnome
