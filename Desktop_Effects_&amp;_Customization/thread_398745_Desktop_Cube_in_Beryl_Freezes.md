### Post by garybrlow on 2007-04-01
hmm you're using SVN software which is very much unstable. You may have encountered a bug in that current version. All SVN software shouldn't  be used in normal circumstances unless you are trying to help the software development process by testing, looking for and filing bug reports. Beryl 1.0 and 1.1 are stable so use them instead. Most Beryl instability is more or less and most often than not caused by Kernel, X and video driver issues. If you recently had Kernel updates, Xog core updates, and or video driver updates its usually best to either reinstall or recompile the video driver especially if you're using the NVIDIA/ATI official binaries and reinstall Beryl  as well. For SVN versions it is mosty like caused by bugs. Search the Beryl forums for similar problems. 

So far, even on Feisty Beta Beryl 1.0 and 1.1 are generally stable. Occasional Kernel, Xorg, and Video driver updates tend to break it and can be fixed by the above solution.  I haven't experienced that kind of bug yet. I experienced White Screens, constant crashing, and outright Desktop Freezing in relation to what I mentioned before but It could also be introduced by unsupported plug-ins that are a try at your own risk basis. Try updating to the current SVN version in some cases updates tend to fix existing bugs/problems but in some cases break it even more as well.
