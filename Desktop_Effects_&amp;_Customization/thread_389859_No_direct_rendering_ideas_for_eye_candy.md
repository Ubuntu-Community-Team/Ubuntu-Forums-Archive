### Post by reclusivemonkey on 2007-03-21
"Pretty" is a very subjective term; if you think minimalist is pretty then you will most likely love Fluxbox. However, I suspect you want a high degree of control over what you see on your desktop which is only possible with window managers like Gnome and KDE. You could try adding widgets to either of these two if that's your thing. gdesklets and adesklets for Gnome, I think Super Karamba is the one for KDE but I've never used KDE so I can't be sure on that one. There are enough widgets and alternative docks/launchers for you to pretty much make your own desktop. If you prefer a leaner/text based look, you can use conky rather than widgets.

If you really don't like KDE or Gnome, as I mentioned you can try Fluxbox for a very minimal look, XFCE for a slimmed down Gnome look, or even Enlightenment which gives you a great deal of control over what you see on the desktop.

[http://gdesklets.org/index.php](http://gdesklets.org/index.php)
[http://adesklets.sourceforge.net/](http://adesklets.sourceforge.net/)
[http://netdragon.sourceforge.net/ssuperkaramba.html](http://netdragon.sourceforge.net/ssuperkaramba.html)
[http://fluxbox.sourceforge.net/](http://fluxbox.sourceforge.net/)
[http://www.xfce.org/](http://www.xfce.org/)
[http://www.enlightenment.org/](http://www.enlightenment.org/)

Have a look on gnome/kde-look; you may find inspiration there. Good look.
