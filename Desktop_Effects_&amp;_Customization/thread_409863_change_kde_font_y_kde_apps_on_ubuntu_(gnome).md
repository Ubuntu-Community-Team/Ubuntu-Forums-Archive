### Post by deanjm1963 on 2007-04-15
The easiest way to do it is to install kcontrol. It also installs quite a few kde libraries as well, but if you're not short on diskspace, 100mb or so won't make any difference. once installed, just open up a terminal and type kcontrol and you have the kde control panel, you can change the font and the kde style of your kde apps, as well as adjust the colors to your liking. I'd recommend you install kdeartwork-style as well.
