Yeah, I can see why. Every Linux project is not about maximum configurability; every developer has his or her goals for the project and if you don't like them you're free to use something else, or fork the project. You can't say that any project is against the spirit of Linux, or whatever, because there is no such spirit, at least not in this sense. Why are you even using Gnome? Gnome is certainly not the most configurable DE in existence.

The other major problem with your request is that a fully themable application would no longer fit into the desktop. Pidgin looks like a Gnome app because it's meant to look like a Gnome app, and it should remain looking like one for the sake of consistency, familiarity and ease of use. And if Trillian is anything to go by, also stability and memory usage.
