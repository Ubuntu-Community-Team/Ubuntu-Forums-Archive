### Post by Zorael on 2008-04-15
Okay. What's happening is that you don't have any available resolutions defined in your xorg.conf, which makes it try to autodetect which are available and fails. Likely this is what happened when your xorg.conf was generated, too. So you'll just need to add them manually.

You need to open your xorg.conf with superuser permissions. Enter the following in a run box (Alt+F2).
```
gksu gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

Scroll down to where it looks like the following, and add the part highlighted in bold (hard to see in Firefox though).
```
Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Default Screen"
    Device         "Configured Videocard"
    Monitor        "Configured Monitor"
    DefaultDepth    24
[b]    SubSection     "Display"
        Depth      24

```

Add wanted resolutions there, into that Modes field. You should also delete any higher resolutions than the one you want; once you have logged in, it will change to the resolution you've set it up to use in your preferences, but the login screen will default to the highest resolution. There's also the possibility of the highest resolution being outside of your monitor's supported range.

So, for a random widescreen you might want to add "1440x720" and remove "1600x1200". You get the idea.

[Use this tool to generate a modeline for your resolution.](http://www.bohne-lang.de/spec/linux/modeline/) Since you seem to want 1280x1024, you just need to know what vertical refresh rate your monitor supports.
