### Post by hyperair on 2007-09-28
I'd say don't bother yourself with it, really. Defending GNU/Linux's name against some petty kid (or teen or adult) who thinks that Vista's the best OS in the world is one thing, but getting into a fight over it.. That's just going overboard. You'll be seen as an extremist, and that displays immaturity and insecurity over the prowess of GNU/Linux over Windows. Not a very good image to portray of GNU/Linux users actually.

A heated debate is always healthy though, and I'm willing to accept one any day ;) In fact, I've managed to get a few clasmates of mine hooked on Ubuntu already, and the ones using Vista are the ones dissatisfied. Those currently using Vista who are not switching to Ubuntu are considering switching back to XP.

As for Microsoft taking our ideas and using it, that's their choice. It's good to take existing ideas (even from competitors), improvise on them and make them better. That's how competition thrives, and makes products get better. Think about it. Compiz Fusion borrowed a few ideas from Apple (Scale in CF<=>Expose in OS X) didn't they? Is the Apple/Macintosh community complaining? Perhaps some, but not many. Then why should we complain when Microsoft borrows our ideas? Even we have borrowed ideas from Microsoft already. Think of the Shift switcher from Compiz Fusion. That's the Flip3D (from Aero) and Cover Flip (from Itunes) on steroids. 

Now I'm not defending Microsoft or anything. I hate Microsoft. But what you are doing is going against the free software philosophy which is to make the software free so that you can take it, modify it however you wish, and redistribute it, sell it, etc. If you are going to complain that Microsoft is getting their ideas from GNU/Linux, then you might as well close-source it, patent it, and make it the same proprietary blob that Windows is.

One way to see this is that by taking our ideas and implementing it onto Windows, Microsoft is in fact acknowledging the superiority of the GNU/Linux platform, among other things.
