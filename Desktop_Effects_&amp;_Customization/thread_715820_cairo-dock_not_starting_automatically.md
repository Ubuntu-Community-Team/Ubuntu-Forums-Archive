### Post by larryfroot on 2008-03-05
Hi,

Go into system / preferences / sessions...then click on add to add what is essentially a launcher in all but name. Put in a name for the thingy - Cairo Dock springs to mind for some unknown reason, then 'cairo-dock' (without the ' marks) goes into command and then you can put whatever you like in the description....there's no law against it. Click OK and make sure that the box is ticked next to its name in the list. That way cairo-dock starts on a fresh (and subsequent) logins without terminal input..and you can close as many terminals as you like afterwards without cairo-dock going down in flames. Or something. But you have to reboot to experience the joy of it all working nicely for the first time. 

Sitting at the bottom for cairo-dock means going into the configure menu...you can get to this by right clicking on a bit of space between icons and going to the top of the menu and into 'configure' if it seems like you only have two tabs in the configure menus, press the button on the bottom left hand corner to show you the whole bloomin lot. In the position tab there is a drop menu...go intop that and choose bottom. The relative alignment slider simply moves the dock to appear left right or middle to various degrees. Auto hide is on the same section...can be useful...happy experimenting!
