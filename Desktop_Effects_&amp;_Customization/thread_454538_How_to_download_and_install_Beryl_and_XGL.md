### Post by dabl on 2007-05-25
At the top of your screen, you see "System"?  Click that, choose "Administration", and then "Synaptic Manager".  in Synaptic, scroll or search to find "beryl" and "beryl-manager", and perhaps "beryl-ubuntu", if you want it.  Mark them for installation, then click "Apply" at the top of Synaptic, and 'twil be done for you. :)
