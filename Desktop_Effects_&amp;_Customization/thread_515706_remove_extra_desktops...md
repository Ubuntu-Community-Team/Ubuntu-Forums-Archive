### Post by cookieofdoom on 2007-08-02
Well, first of all, they are not taking up much of your computer's resources at all. If they're not logged in and doing something then they occupy (correct me if I'm wrong) only a few k of RAM that is already cached anyway.

Their being open is a result of how Linux (possibly UNIX?) operates. To remove them you would have to enter a lower level of system operation (again, correct me if I'm wrong). This would in fact hinder your computer's performance as it wouldn't have a lot of the services that help it to do things more efficiently (processor throttling to name one of them).

Maybe an *nix expert can come in and elaborate more. I really don't know that much about what I'm talking about. I can tell you, though, that having all of those logins there is using less system resources than running notepad or calculator on Windows.
