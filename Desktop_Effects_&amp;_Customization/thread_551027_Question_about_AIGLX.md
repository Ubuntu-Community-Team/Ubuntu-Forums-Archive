### Post by Rupertronco on 2007-09-14
I recommend the sticky. [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=272104](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=272104)

Basically it's going to tell you that depending on your graphics card (ATI requires XGL, nVidia and Intel can use AIGLX, but it's no longer required), that AiGLX and XGL allow for Open GL 3-d acceleration (required for compiz and beryl).

The latest nVidia drivers, and I believe Intel as well(although not 100% on the intel) no longer require the use of AIGLX or XGL.

It's not default with Ubuntu, and XGL and AIGLX essentially do the same thing, XGL is older, but is the only way to get ATi cards working right now.
