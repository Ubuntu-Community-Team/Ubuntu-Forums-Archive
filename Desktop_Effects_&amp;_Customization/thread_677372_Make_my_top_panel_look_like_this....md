### Post by Casual Fan on 2008-01-24
Well, the arrows and panel width are easy. Just right click on the panel, and on the "General" tab, uncheck "expand" and check "show hide buttons" and "arrows on hide buttons."

As for the icons, they're just part of a theme, but who knows which. Better e-mail whoever posted that pic and ask 'em.

### Post by Number_6 on 2008-01-24
Right click on your top bar (I'm assuming you're running GNOME) and select properties. Deselect 'expand' and select 'show hide buttons' and 'arrows on buttons'. You can adjust the colours on the 'background' tab.

### Post by Tenken on 2008-01-24
Many gtk themes will give you a black panel, couldn't give you a specific theme, but [Murrine Tango Gray]("http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Murrina-Tango-Gray?content=74144") is an example.
