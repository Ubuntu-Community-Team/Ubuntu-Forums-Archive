Hi, and welcome to the forums.

First, there's no such thing as a stupid question, but you can always use the forum search or google.

Now, that bar is pretty much a standard gnome-panel. All you have to do is rightclick on a bare area of the bar, and chose properties.

To make the bar smaller, uncheck expand.

Then, to make the bar transparent, select the background tab, check Solid Colour, and adjust the transparency. Note that is fake transparency, so it won't show icons or windows.

### Post by ppatalano on 2007-03-21
1. Go to Applications-Add/Remove.
2. Install gdesklets
3. Run gdesklets (should be in Applications - Accessories)
4. Use manage desklets and navigate to Toolbars/Launchers and double click on starter bar to add
5. When its on your desktop, right click and choose configure desklet to manage its appearance and use the new starter option when you right click to add more applications to the starter bar

I hope this was simple enough for you, so that you can have an OS X style icon dock.
