### Post by jpeddicord on 2007-03-06
This was one idea mssever sent me before the team was officially set up:
> 
Triage team. This would be the main group of people. The triage team would regularly check for unanswered posts (older than some threshold, maybe 2 hours, to allow regular forum processes to run their course before we kick in) and handle them in one of the following ways:[LIST]
[*]Answer the question, if possible.
[*]If the question is unclear, politely request clarification.
[*]PM one or more experts with a link to the thread, then post in the thread that the unanswered posts team has referred the question to an expert.
[*]If we don't have any experts in that area, post a reply saying so and recommending some other place to look. In this case, we need to try to find one or more experts in that area.
[/LIST]
The main point of his idea is the third bullet down, and it could be a key technique if done right. We would essentially have a database of team members and expert areas. When a thread is encountered that would require someone from that area, we would either:[LIST]
[*]Add the thread to a queue for that particular expert group
[*]PM/mail someone in the expert topic area
[*]Ask on the IRC channel for any experts around
[*]Defer a post to the Beginners or Hardware teams (only with old posts; the teams will see the newer ones anyway)
[/LIST]

How does the idea sound to everyone, and does anyone have any suggestions?

Jacob

### Post by Richard Kut on 2007-03-11
Hello fellow team members! I would like to share with you how I have been approaching these issues, in the hope that my comments spark some ideas.

What I do is that I enter one of the forums, say "Absolute Beginner", and then I click on "Unanswered Posts". Then I click on the "Last" page button, and this usually brings me to the oldest unanswered posts. Then I hunt around for something that I feel that I can answer. If I cannot find anything on that last page, then I will go backwards from there towards the newer posts on the next-to-last page, and so on.

My reasons for using this approach are (in no particular order):

- I was shocked to see how many folks have unanswered posts even after two weeks or more.
- I know what it feels like to wait for an answer to what I feel is an urgent problem.
- Someone who has already waited for two weeks or more should not have to wait one minute longer, even if it is free support. 

Maybe some kind of canned response after a certain elapsed time could be implemented for this last point?

Again, this is just my personal approach, so take it for whatever you feel it is worth. I am not advocating that anyone else adopt this method. However, I would be interested to read what others are doing to tackle similar issues.

On that note, best of luck to us all in this endeavor!
