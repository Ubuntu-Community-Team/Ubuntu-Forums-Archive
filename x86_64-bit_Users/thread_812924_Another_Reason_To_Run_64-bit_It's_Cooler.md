### Post by linux4me on 2008-05-30
I just upgraded to 64-bit Hardy from 32-bit Hardy, and as usual, I installed lm-sensors and sensor-applet to keep an eye on my CPU and GPU temperatures. 

The idling temperature for the GPU is unchanged, but since upgrading to 64-bit Hardy, my idling CPU temperature has dropped from 34C to 29C!:confused: 

I have double-checked to make sure I'm looking at the same sensor, and I am. I also verified that the temperature I'm seeing in Ubuntu is the same as the one reported by the BIOS for the CPU. It is.

I can't explain it, but I'll enjoy it until someone bursts my bubble. :)

Has anyone else noticed something similar?
