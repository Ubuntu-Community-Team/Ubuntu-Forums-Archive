### Post by lemming465 on 2009-10-09
When you say "crashing" it's not clear if you mean "X died" or "kernel paniced" or "keyboard froze", or ...

At a guess (from the "tainted" and "nvidia") bits you probably have an Nvidia video chip and are using the proprietary close-source restricted vendor driver?

Unfortunately, I'm not a whiz at intepreting kernel dumps.  However, I can suggest a couple of things you might try:

* use the slower but open source "nv" driver and see if you still get crashes.  The obvious way would be by disabling the restricted driver and rebooting.
* try a newer kernel, perhaps from 9.10 beta "karmic koala".  Heck, maybe try upgrading to the beta - its been pretty stable for me.
* revert to a 32-bit install, where it wasn't crashing
