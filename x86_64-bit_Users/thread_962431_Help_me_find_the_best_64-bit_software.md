### Post by Thelasko on 2008-10-29
Well Maya is really cool, and really expensive.  An open source alternative is [Blender]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blender_(software)").  It's not as feature rich as Maya, but it's a good place to start for a hobbyist.  Blender can be installed from the [repositories.]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installingsoftware")  The repositories have a great selection of software designed for Ubuntu, I recommend having a look.

Open source:
[Cinelerra]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinelerra")
Probably anything on [this page]("http://opensource.arc.nasa.gov/")
[Stellarium]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellarium_(computer_program)")
Lots of university projects.

Proprietary:
[NX]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NX_(software)")
[CATIA]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CATIA")
[ANSYS]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ansys")
Star-CD

Most of these programs will utilize more of your graphics card than your processor, but I think it's what you're looking for.

If you just want to kill some clock cycles, try [BOINC.]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_Open_Infrastructure_for_Network_Computing")
