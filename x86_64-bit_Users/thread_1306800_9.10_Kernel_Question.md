### Post by Ayuthia on 2009-10-30
The -14-generic portion is the Ubuntu revision numbering scheme.  The 2.6.XX part is the actual kernel.  For each Ubuntu version that comes out, it will stay with that kernel number throughout the life of that version (Hardy 2.6.24, Intrepid 2.6.27, Jaunty 2.6.28, and Karmic 2.6.31).  Ubuntu will make updates to that kernel and send out their -YY-generic update during the life of that version.

So in short, each time a new kernel is used, the Ubuntu revision number will start again.
