### Post by ajgreeny on 2009-08-02
I suspect the change you made needs no action at all from you; the OS has sorted any differences it found on boot-up, and is using whatever is needed.  In the past the time when problems appeared was after a change of video card, when new drivers were often needed, and some user input was required of the user, but now even this is often sorted with the use of the hardware drivers utility.

Your perception of the lack of extra speed is probably a result of the old system being very fast before you made any changes.  I run a sempron 2400+ with 2GG ram and think it is pretty fast, so maybe you have simply reached the limit of speed available from your hard disks or ram, or some other bottleneck, but I doubt that a reinstall would help. And if the system still runs it means that the hardware is obviously still supported.
