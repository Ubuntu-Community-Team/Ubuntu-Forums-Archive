### Post by 3Miro on 2009-03-22
Any Intel video card is generallu bad for gaming. For games you want NVIDIA or ATI.

As far as I recall, AA is a Linux game that comes from the repos, therefore open source, therefore already compiled for 64-bit. If you can play the game in 32-bit, then it must work under 64-bit, there is no difference from that prospective. If it worked under 32-bit and is not working now, then you have a driver/setting issue. If it doesn't work under 64-bit then it wouldn't run under 32-bit.

I am playing a number of 32-bit windows game sunder Ubuntu 64-bit with wine and I have no trouble.

### Post by 3Miro on 2009-03-22
It depends on the Laptop. In general Desktop hardware is very easy to upgrade, but Laptop hardware is very specific made to fit in the little space and use as little resources as possible. Thus Laptops are hard to upgrade. Contact your laptop manufacturer to get details on possible upgrade.

### Post by jespdj on 2009-03-23
In most laptops, the graphics card is not interchangeable. Some high-end gaming laptops have a separate graphics card in an [MXM slot](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile_PCI_Express_Module), but your laptop most likely does not have this, so there's most likely no way to upgrade the graphics card in your laptop.
