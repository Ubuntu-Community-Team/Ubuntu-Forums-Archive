### Post by 3Miro on 2009-05-01
64-bit doesn't mean 2 times faster. Lookup CPU register and word size.

For average use (according to some random studies that I have encounterd on the web) 64-bit is about 10% faster. However, some apps (such as video editing) you get up to 50 or more percent improvement in speed.

In general your game is 32bit and 32-bit apps need emulation to run on a 64-bit system. Your CPU (as all modern Intel and AMD CPUs) has the so called compatibility mode, so when you are running a 32-bit app under 64-bit Linux, you are actually running it in 32-bit mode. 64 vs 32 has no effect on game speed.

In general, games with wine run slower than under windows, however, that is not a rule. Long time ago (8-9 years) there was this company Loki Games that made the so called Loki installers. Those were something like wine, however, they were optimized on a game per game basis and also they were commercial. Loki games went out of business, however, with those installers, games under Linux ran much faster than on windows (on the same hardware).

Also I have seen apps be more stable under wine than windows, I have also seen the opposite.

P.S. FPS depend very little on the CPU, those really have to do with the GPU and all GPUs are 32-bit anyway.
