OK: hardware and OS are 64-bit. That's good.

However, the BIOS is not offering any RAM above the 32bit 3.2/4GB barrier. I guess your computer is not brand new?
Standard recipe to try to solve this:
1) update your BIOS to the newest version
2) search your BIOS settings for something like "memory (hole) remapping" or "memory hoisting". That option should be on.

If the above does not solve the problem, search your hardware sellers website what the maximum amount of RAM is in your hardware: it could be limited to 3.2GB which is often called 4GB. Then you're out of luck.

The physical nature of RAM hardware makes it almost impossible (or at least, highly impractical) to manufacture devices that do not end on set address boundaries.

The complexity of manufacturing something that is 1000 Mib versus something that is 1000 MB (just so you can "save" the cost of the extra actual memory) would mean that these slightly lesser capacity memory devices would cost 10X (100x?) more than the easier to make - and larger capacity - ones.

For hard drives it is different as the platter capacities are far more linear and the marketing strategies that use the misleading capacity definitions come into play, but for solid state memory the increments are essentially fixed.

A 1GB RAM stick is a 1 GB RAM stick, there is no such thing as a 1 Gib RAM stick.
