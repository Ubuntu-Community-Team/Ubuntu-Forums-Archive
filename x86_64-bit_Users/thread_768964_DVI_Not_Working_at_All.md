### Post by Aldanga on 2008-04-26
I have one very odd story to tell.

Once upon a time I built a PC. It was a decent PC, but with integrated ATI graphics. That caused me trouble with Ubuntu 7.10 64-Bit, but those were resolved with help from the wonderful community on these forums. However, one day I turned on my PC and, to my dismay, my monitor displayed a simple message: No Video Signal. To be candid I was horrified. 'Have I destroyed my motherboard?' I wondered. 'I must go get another graphics card and find out.'

I went and bought another card, an nVidia 7600GS, to be specific. It worked flawlessly with the vesa drivers, unlike my IGP which would no longer display any video. Then I went to install the restricted drivers. My desktop was promptly split into many pieces and pasted together in a seemingly random order on my screen.

After much thought and a couple reinstalls, I decided to switch to a D-Sub cable instead of DVI. Voila! It worked! I was disappointed my DVI didn't work, but glad to have figured it out.

Then came a time when I got an extremely good deal on an 8800GT, which has only DVI ports. It worked for a little while, LiveCD and all, then randomly stopped working, just like my IGP before it. I was frustrated and decided to wait for 8.04 to see if that would fix my issue.

In my impatience I downloaded the 8.04 64-Bit Beta two weeks before the final release; and it worked! It was a beautiful release and I was quite pleased to finally be able to install it. However, a week later I put the CD back into my drive to edit some files on an Ext3 partition and 'No Video Signal' graced my screen once again. I have yet to solve this problem.


I must ask: Is this an issue with my video card/monitor or Ubuntu itself? How can it recognize and use an output one week and literally a week later give no video signal? I cannot even install 8.04 right now (though I am working on downloading different versions of the installer). I continuously get the error, "Your configuration could not be detected. You're in low graphics mode... Configure it yourself..." yada yada yada. I can't get it to work. Does anyone have advice as to how to solve this DVI issue?
