### Post by Malta paul on 2009-07-30
DDR2 is faster than DDR, DDR3 is even faster but is more expensive.
I would check your motherboard manual to see what you can fit. Despite having a quad core CPU your motherboard may be limited to DDR2. Also note that memory chips come in different speeds. Use the fastest speed your MB front side bus will take.
8GB of the fastest DDR2 your MB will take would be the best option. (I'm using DDR2 at 1066 bus speed)

### Post by bobince on 2009-08-01
> **Zaytoven said:**
> Does stuff like this have to do with the processor or the RAM or both?

Audio/video encoding is CPU-bound. Video editing (as in sequencing clips) can benefit from more memory to load and cache clip data.

As dcstar said, check System Monitor, but I personally would be surprised if you could get any benefit from 16GB. 8GB is already a staggeringly enormous amount of memory; I have 4GB and even running virtual machines seldom pushes me below 3GB free.

16GB also typically means 4x4GB modules, which are currently not so easy to get hold of.
