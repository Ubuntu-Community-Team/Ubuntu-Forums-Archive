### Post by keepitsimpleengr on 2009-05-05
I run both 32 & 64 bit Ubuntu on separate hardware.

The upgrade from intrepid to jaunty on the 32 bit was a blissful dream.

The upgrade from intrepid to jaunty on the 64 bit was a terrible nightmare.

Possibly part of the reason is multiple monitors on the 64 bit.

The upgrade from the alternate CD for 64bit failed to upgrade Nvidia drivers to the 180 version, required for jaunty.  In addition, it failed to properly use the 180 version already in use in intrepid.

Dealing with trying to get the system working required extensive reboots, which was an inadvertent stress test - in which marginal hardware failed - precipitating even more problems with the upgrade.

The upgrade failed to update compiz fusion, perhaps eye-candy but a useful and fast desktop switcher for me.

Furthermore, bugs with nvidia-settings and gnome panel create big head aches for me when using the 64 bit jaunty.

The point is this: why the discrepancy in upgrade experience for the two versions?  Possibly reduced testing of the 64 bit versions?  Scant resources for 64 bit work?  Bad luck?  

I expected some problems with the more complex 64 bit system, but not this.

I take responsibility for the marginal hardware, but do not believe an upgrade should be a hardware stress test.  

I do not mean this as a complaint, but a notice that perhaps some small changes could be made.  40+ years in the computer business given me tremendous respect for the people who undertake such a complex and difficult task as Ubuntu. :) I thank them for all their hard work.
