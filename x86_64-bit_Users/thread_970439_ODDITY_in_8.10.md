### Post by phlembob on 2008-11-04
I spent the weekend checking out some common problems listed with 8.10.  1. Grub is inconsistently correct, and most of the time, the errors are variable.  In other words, for some reason grub is not written with the same repeated problems (error).  2.  NVIDIA proprietary drivers do not default within usable video resolution.  Blank (black) monitors without video are very difficult to work for correction.  3.  The open video does default within usable picture, but is not adjustable above 800 x 600.

I would recommend for people working from their computer or with only one HDD to stay with 8.04 LTS at this time.  The first round of updates haven't eliminated any problems mentioned above.  Next week these problems could be gone, but not as of 11/04/08.  There is a problem in the update manager when it goes to clean-up.  It will not finish with either choice KEEP or REMOVE.  ):P
