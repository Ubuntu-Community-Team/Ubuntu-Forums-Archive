### Post by PGHammer on 2009-07-01
> **Spaceman7o said:**
> i would go for 32 bit if you can im 64 but it aint a problem 32 is better if you can use that everything about installing is easier lol .. and drivers.

Drivers are the largest issue with deciding whether or not to go 64-bit (with browser plug-ins close behind), and that is regardless of operating system.

Once you get past those two hurdles, I would say go 64-bit regardless of how *little* RAM you have.

I originally did my 32 vs. 64 face-offs on systems with 512 MB of RAM (and converted personally with just 1 GB of RAM).

Though I have 3 GB today, I didn't do the upgrade because I had to *personally*.  I did it because of one persnickety application (a Web application) that wanted more RAM (and the reality that DDR2 is Seriously Cheap).

The driving factor for my recommending 64-bit?  Not future-proofing or to access lots of RAM (remember, I didn't have anything close to lots of RAM), but *stability*.  Given equal driver support and amounts of RAM, a 64-bit operating system whacks a 32-bit version of the same operating system in terms of stability.  (Doesn't matter if the OS in question is 'Buntu, Solaris, OS X, or Windows 7/Vista/XP; 64-bit *always* trounces 32-bit, drivers and RAM being equal.)
