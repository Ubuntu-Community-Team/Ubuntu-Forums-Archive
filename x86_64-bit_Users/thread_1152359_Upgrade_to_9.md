### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2009-05-07
It really depends on your personal philosophy. Some people say, "If it ain't broke, don't fix it." and others say, "If it's not broken, you haven't tweaked it enough."

Upgrades can definitely foobar things (whether you're running 32-bit or 64-bit), especially if you've installed 3rd-party .debs, messed with repo sources, or done something "n00bish" 

If you have Intel graphics, you'll want to do your homework and test out a LiveCD before upgrading. ATI dropped proprietary/Catalyst support for all but the newest cards in Jaunty as well.
