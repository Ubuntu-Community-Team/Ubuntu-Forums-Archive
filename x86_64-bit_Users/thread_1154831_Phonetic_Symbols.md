Yes, they are a group of fonts (Ipa-sams Uclphon1 SILSophiaL,Ipa-samm Uclphon1 SILManuscript, Ipa-samd Uclphon1 SILDoulosL you can get them for free from [http://www.phon.ucl.ac.uk/home/wells/fonts.htm](http://www.phon.ucl.ac.uk/home/wells/fonts.htm)) though which you can type Phonetic symbols straight from your keyboard (e.g. you type t you get a symbol, you type shift + t you get another, extremely useful if you have to use them a lot). No need for setting-ups and if you take any of the fonts with you, any computer from the office, college, or home can be used. 
As you can see, they are very easy and quick to use!
Thanks anyway, with your help at least I can work with phonetic symbols on my home computer!!!
