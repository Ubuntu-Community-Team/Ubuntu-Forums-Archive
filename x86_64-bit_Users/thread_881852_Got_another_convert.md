### Post by opr8ive on 2008-08-06
After playing for a few weeks with my new laptop (trying to get ubuntu all working and such) I have learned an awful lot in a short time.

My nephew is staying with us, and has his computer in his room, alas the 40 GB drive he had in it with his M$ install finally pooped out..

I gave him an oldish WD 120GB hd I had lying around, and a Ubuntu 8.04 live cd. 

He putzed around for a bit, then I went in to help.

Needless to say, withing about 45 minutes, we had all the drivers in, video acceleration working, restricted-extras all installed, and even showed him how to poll the HD for errors with smartctl.

His Ipod worked right off the bat, as did his USB wireless network dongle..

He was pretty impressed. (so was I.. it took me about 50 hours to get all my stuff working, but I digress) I think he's going to stay with ubuntu for a while. :)

I did have one question tho...

copying MP3s off his ipod onto the computer results in the file name changing from Someartist_Somealbum_Somesong.mp3 to an odd (random) 4 letter filename. (ATOL.mp3)  The Song still plays, and all the tag info is correct, but the filename is changed.. I know the ipod does that to the files when you import them, but is there a way to copy files from the ipod to the computer, while keeping their original filename intact?
