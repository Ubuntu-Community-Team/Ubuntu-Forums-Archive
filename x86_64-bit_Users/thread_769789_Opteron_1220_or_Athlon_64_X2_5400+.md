### Post by old_geekster on 2008-04-28
As you know, Opterons have been developed specifically for servers.  Basically, the same speed Opty is exactly like its brother in the Athlon lineup.  The difference being that the best of the best chips are made into Optys.  This is because of the stress that is put on them.  At least this is the story that I have been given on many other forums.

Personally, I would go for the Opty for that reason.  Allot of enthusiasts use them because they seem to overclock better than the Athlon, though they are not supported for desktop use by AMD.

### Post by Em-Buntu on 2008-05-16
Opteron is always > than Athlon.

Opteron is Server class chip while Athlon is desktop.

More low cost motherboards are available for Athlon, but Opteron has larger cache, higher performance HT links, ECC memory and scale to multi chips. 
Server motherboards are also usually more reliable and design in higher performance components.

Noise level is determined by fan type, not chip.
My Quad Opteron setup is as quiet as a single chip Athlon. Perhaps a few db more, but not at all loud.
