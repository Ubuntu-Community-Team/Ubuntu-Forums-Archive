### Post by Jouke74 on 2008-07-30
You can use to completely different manufacturers as well, as long as the amount of RAM in both channels (consisting usually out of 2 RAM banks/slots) is identical. E.g. 2x 256 MB in one, and 1x 512 MB in the other will give you 1 GB dual channel memory theoretically.

However, you are making your life quite complicated because the BIOS settings of all RAM slots(!) need to be equal as well e.g. CAS and other latencies, the speed of the RAM etc. If you're buying RAM with different RAS/CAS/Mhz etc, the BIOS parameters of the slots need to be put onto speeds on which both can work. Of course this is much easier to do when you have a matched pair.

Please also check your motherboard manual for this, as of course the motherboard may have additional "wishes" for dual channel to work. Here you will also find a list with qualified vendors and RAM that is supposed to work.

PS. this has nothing to do with 64 bit OS, goes for 32 bit OS as well.
