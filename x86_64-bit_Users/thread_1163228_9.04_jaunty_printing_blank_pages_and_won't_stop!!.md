### Post by ShadowTek on 2009-05-18
It seemed like 8.10 worked with my current printer without any extra effort, but 9.04 did not recognise the printer, and I needed to select the proper driver manually and install it before my printer would be recognised.

So, assuming that my memory is correct and that I *did not* have to fool with driver installation on 8.10, it would seem that there was a bit of a regression in printer detection and driver installation in 9.04, at least in my case.

With that in mind, perhaps your 9.04 also failed to install the proper driver for your printer. Maybe it auto-installed the wrong one?

### Post by aysiu on 2009-06-22
Wow. I just experienced this problem. It was weird.

The printer was automatically added when plugged in. Ubuntu kind of guessed what driver to use (used the 2060 driver), and with that the pages all cut off in the middle when printing (the top half of the page was printed, the bottom half was blank).

So I installed the HL-2040 drivers straight from the Brother website:
[http://www.brother.com/cgi-bin/agreement/agreement.cgi?dlfile=http://solutions.brother.com/Library/sol/printer/linux/rpmfiles/lpr_debian/brhl2040lpr-2.0.1-1.i386.deb&lang=English_lpr](http://www.brother.com/cgi-bin/agreement/agreement.cgi?dlfile=http://solutions.brother.com/Library/sol/printer/linux/rpmfiles/lpr_debian/brhl2040lpr-2.0.1-1.i386.deb&lang=English_lpr)
[http://www.brother.com/cgi-bin/agreement/agreement.cgi?dlfile=http://solutions.brother.com/Library/sol/printer/linux/rpmfiles/cups_wrapper/cupswrapperHL2040-2.0.1-2.i386.deb&lang=English_gpl](http://www.brother.com/cgi-bin/agreement/agreement.cgi?dlfile=http://solutions.brother.com/Library/sol/printer/linux/rpmfiles/cups_wrapper/cupswrapperHL2040-2.0.1-2.i386.deb&lang=English_gpl)

After I did that (you have to make the /usr/share/cups/model directory first, I tried to print a test page, and it kept printing page after page after page of blank pages. When I Googled that, I got this thread but also a lot of other posts from non-Linux users (at Yahoo! Answers and the Brother forums). The only answer given was "You're out of toner."

Yes, our toner light is on, and we are *low* on toner, but we are not out of toner.

Here's where it gets really weird, though.

Even though the *test page* printed page after page of blank pages (the only way I could stop it was unplugging the printer--cancelling the print job did not stop it), once I plugged the printer back in and started printing normal stuff (not the test page) from Firefox and Thunderbird, it worked just fine.
