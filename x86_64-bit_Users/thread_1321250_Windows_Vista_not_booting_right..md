### Post by picopir8 on 2009-11-10
A little more info might be helpful.  Its kinda like saying sometimes my car wont start but sometimes it will.  Expecting a diagnosis without any details is asking a bit much.

So far here are are few possibilities:
1. The computer is not getting power, make sure it is pugged into an outlet with power.
2. Your computer has a loose power cable, reseat all cables.
3. The power button on your computer is broken, replace it.
4. You might have a stuck key (or something is pressing on a key) causing your bios to select an alternate boot device.
5. Grub is misconfigured for windows.
6. your windows partition is on an external drive which may not be plugged into your computer.
7. That same external drive may require power, verify points 1 and 2 for you external drive.
8. your windows partition may be corrupt, get windows to boot into safe mode and repair the partition.
9. your windows install may be corrupt, get windows to boot into safew mode then repair the installation.
...
