### Post by steeleyuk on 2009-08-30
Type **uname -m** at the terminal. If you see i386 its 32-bit, if you see x86_64 then its 64-bit.

---

### Post by wingnut59 on 2009-08-30
you may also want to try uname -a to see more information about your installed version of Linux. Depending on the actual cpu, you may get i686 instead of i386
