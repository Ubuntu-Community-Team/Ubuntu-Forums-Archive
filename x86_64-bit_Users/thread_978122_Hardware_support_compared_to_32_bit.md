### Post by Arup on 2008-11-10
You have no issues as majority of hardware are supported due to Linux's kernel based driver design. I am as of now running 14 PCs on Ubuntu x64, few of them are old and some new cutting edge with no issues. Wireless works out of the box, so does cams, digitizers, bionics etc.

### Post by lavinog on 2008-11-11
The gap has closed pretty quickly since dapper. 64 bit proprietary drivers seem to have more issues than their 32-bit counterparts, but they tend to be updated more frequently to address this.
One example is the proprietary drivers for my ATI card...sometimes a newer version has more bugs than the older.
