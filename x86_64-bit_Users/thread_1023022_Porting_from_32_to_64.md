### Post by cariboo on 2008-12-27
No, you will run into problems if you just copy your 32bit programs from one drive to the other. Unless you've compiled programs yourself there should be 64bit replacements for all your 32bit programs. You may want to copy the configuration files for your programs, as it shouldn't make any difference between versions.

### Post by rbmorse on 2008-12-28
Depending upon your mail program, you may be able to "export" stores and settings from the 32-bit installation to a file, and then "import" those into the 64-bit installation.  Evolution does that via the file > backup settings and file >restore settings menu items.  The term "settings is a bit misleading as it actually carries all of the mail store, address books and calendar entries.
