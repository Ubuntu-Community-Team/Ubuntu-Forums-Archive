### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2009-07-01
That's like asking for an instruction manual to life. Ubuntu and GNOME focus on providing sane defaults, while leaving customization to the individual.

> Flash works fine in 64-bit, and has for months.
Some people wouold disagree with that. In my experience, the native Flash 64-bit plugin works wonderfully with open-source/Mesa drivers, but has issues (e.g. segfaults) with the proprietary ATI drivers. I also see some people reporting issues with nvidia proprietary drivers, such as with full-screen video.
Of course, the 32-bit plugin isn't absolutely perfect either, so I don't think the Flash plugin is a good reason not to at least try 64-bit.
