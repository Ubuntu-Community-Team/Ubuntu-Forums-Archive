> **Grant A. said:**
> How can I make it to where þ shows up after I hold down alt gr + t on a U.S. QWERTY keyboard?

I don't know what alt+gr+t is supposed to do, because even though I have a US QWERTY keyboard, I don't have a gr key. I thought the gr key occurred only on certain European keyboards.

However, I do know how to enter a thorn &#447; into text in Ubuntu. That is, I assume the character you want is the thorn (used in English up to the middle of the Middle English period). The Unicode number for the lowercase thorn is 01BF, and the uppercase is EEF6. To enter the character in almost any program on Linux type Ctrl+Shift+u, then the Unicode number, and then a space.

Having said that, the character will not appear unless you are using a font that contains the thorn character. Very few fonts do. Personally, I prefer Junicode, as it has all the characters for the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA), plus it has an extensive set of characters for classicists. It even has the thorn in two different types of runes.

For further information on how to enter characters for classical languages and for linguistics, go to:

[http://www.ipa4linguists.pbwiki.com]("http://www.ipa4linguists.pbwiki.com")

On that site there is a page for free fonts that you can download. There is a link to the Junicode font on the fonts page.
