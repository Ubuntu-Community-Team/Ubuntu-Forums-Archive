### Post by MystaMax on 2008-10-20
I'm not sure this belongs in [x86 64-bit Users]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=343") forums. You may have better luck in the [Multimedia & Video]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=334") forums.

The short answer to my fix is: I had to download the drivers from [nVidia's site]("http://www.nvidia.com/object/unix.html"), to get my resolution to work properly, but compiz wouldn't work. So I uninstalled the drivers from the nVidia site, and used EnvyNG to install the drivers. Again, everything worked but compiz. By chance, I launched nvidia-settings w/ sudo, and made sure my resolution was correct, and saved the xorg.conf file using nvidia-settings. After another reboot, compiz was working!

Posts that helped:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=948301](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=948301)

I tried almost all recommendations in this thread, and [post #6]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=5968985&postcount=6") allowed me to get my native resolution back (no bulletproof X). 

I'll repeat: EnvyNG wouldn't work properly @ first. It did work after uninstalling the nVidia site drivers. I then used nvidia-settings as root to update my xorg.conf, and after compiz started working.
