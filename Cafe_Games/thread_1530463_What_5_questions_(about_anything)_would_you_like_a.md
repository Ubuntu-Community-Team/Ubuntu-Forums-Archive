### Post by jwbrase on 2010-07-13
> **gnomeuser said:**
> 1) What is dark matter

"Dark matter" is anything that doesn't give off enough emitted or reflected light (or block enough light from something else) to be seen from Earth, but can be detected by its gravitational effects on other things. So most planets known outside the solar system qualify as "dark matter" (other than those that have been detected when they passed in front of their stars), and even Earth, and you yourself could be considered to qualify if viewed from very far away. However, "normal" stuff can only account for a certain fraction of the dark matter we've detected, and the vast majority of dark matter has to be made of particles that have properties that don't correspond to anything we know about. (Otherwise, certain things we observe in the universe would be different).
