...I'm more of a Superman fan than Batman, though I am now much *more* of a Batman fan than I used to be...

...went to see the opening night (midnight) showing of Batman Begins when it came out, showed up about 5 or 10 minutes before it started, there weren't even lines, the seats in the theater weren't full, though that was before the general public was aware that the current cast and crew of the Batman are the best that the silver screen has ever had (in my opinion, that's all)...

...went to see the opening night (midnight) showing of The Dark Knight when it came out, showed up about 5 or 10 minutes before it started, there were mile-long lines (literally) pouring out - going outside - through the parking lots - and as far as the highway! That theater ended up reserving *every* auditorium that night just for Dark Knight. We didn't bother watching it that night. We saw it the next day, mid-afternoon, and it was still packed, there were still lines, though we managed to get great seats.

:D

I believe The Dark Knight is the #1 "comic book movie" of all time, so far at least. I could say it's hard to admit to that when I'm still more of a Superman fan, though there's no denying the fact that I was more impressed with this movie - as a comic book to film adaptation - than any other previously produced. I look forward to seeing the next Batman movie. Even if it's not as good, as long as it has at least most of the same cast/crew, then they'll have another powerful hit.

Though, I also look forward to seeing the next comic book movie - of another comic book, produced by different cast/crew - that will be even better than The Dark Knight. It's not about "oh it's the best because it's this/that character"... it's about how, over time, the movies are gradually improving in their capacity to adapt a comic book to film.

That's all just my opinion... I know people will disagree. ;) That's okay. :D
