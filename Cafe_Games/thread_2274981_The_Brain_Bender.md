### Post by robsoles on 2015-04-24
'flat' and 'smooth' may as well ambiguously be each other.

I'm no physicist but I have done a little reading; the way gravity works in our Universe is a huge contributor to the shape of our planets and other bodies formed by gasses, dust and debris, to the best of my understanding.

As mass increases the gravitational effect of the mass increases, the gravitational effect just attracts from a periphery distance to the center of the mass in all directions reasonably evenly with exceptions made by other significant masses in (not too) near proximity - if the Earth stopped travelling at a right angle to the Sun quite so rapidly it would simply fall into the sun. If nothing more cataclysmic happens beforehand the Earth will eventually fall into the Sun imho, but I am digressing.

Due to the reasonably uniformly 'spherical' effect of gravity; colliding dust, debris and gas, which happen to form a mass of any significance has a tendency of settling into a spherical shape - note tho, movement and interaction with other significant bodies (even without colliding) can make odd shapes, even a cuboid - to anybody on the cuboid crossing from one face to another face will just be a matter of stepping past the crest of a mountain.
