### Post by almigi on 2008-11-30
Question: I do I get my new HP Deskjet Printer to work with my Computer?

Windows answer: Once your printer is set up, and connected to your computer via the USB port, Windows will recognize the new hardware.  After which it will ask you to insert the CD that came with the printer so it can search for drivers.  Doing so will also install several programs from HP, such as the HP Advisor, and a digital photo management system which probably isn't any better then the one that came with your camera.  It will also put some shortcuts on your desktop so can easily access the online HP store.  After which you'll be prompted to reboot your computer. Do so, and then, while not necessary, you'll be encouraged to register your new product, so that you can receive even better support.  You'll also be able to sign up to receive "special offers" via email and/or snail mail.  Of course this is just going to be unwanted clutter in your inbox/real life mailbox so you probably shouldn't check those options.

Linux answer (assuming a modern, user friendly distro, like Ubuntu or Mandriva): Your system should recognize your printer automatically.  Just connect and print.  Worst case scenario you may have to do some manual configuration, but that's probably going to be the exception more then the rule.  Chances are if your printer isn't recognized automatically, you're not the only one who had the same problem.  Just Google "<distro> <printer model>" and you'll probably find some easy step by step instructions. Again, this is really more the exception then the rule.
