### Post by verb3k on 2008-05-09
> **LaRoza said:**
> In Opera, you just right click and block the content you don't want to see. No need for an extension.


There is a big difference. In ABP you subscribe to a long list containing all advertisements sites like doubleclick and adsense. You don't need to click on every banner you find to block it. Also, do you have the patience to block all advertisements on the web? In opera if you block one ad from doubleclock you're not blocking doubleclick itself, just blocking that particular ad.

In Firefox, ABP blocks all ad sites from their roots without your interference at all. Using regular expressions, ABP blocks all objects whose URL contains the regexp ".doubleclick." that will match al ads from doubleclick... and so on.

See, I told you there is a big difference :lolflag:
