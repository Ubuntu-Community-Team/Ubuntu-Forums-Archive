Simple rules/guidelines -

1. Be orderly! One sentence per post (you may make multiple posts if you want).

2. Be concise! Try to make it no more than 25 words per sentence (and no less than 4).

4. Be spontaneous! There doesn't need to be any order to these sentences. They will be rearranged in the end anyway.

5. Be appropriate! Keep it PG-13.

6. Be creative! Anything can happen! You write it, I'll make it fit into the story. (Somehow...)

At that exact moment, the Internet died. Twisted nematic liquid crystal began to bleed through the silver coated glass plates of the monitor lipping down onto the keyboard. Linus did not understand why this occurred, since he was writing in C,  little realizing that this was caused by the mother and father of all electrical storms creating an EMF of such magnitude that every chip in every device fried. Linus started to switch from C to Java to avoid compatibility issues... This caused him to accidentally hit the backspace key. And he kept on pressing it until every line of code was canceled. And once the code was all gone, time began to go backwards. And he muttered to himself under his breath, "where would I get Linux tech support, anyway?" as he started searching for time's man page. Then he typed into the terminal, "man time." He found nothing, so he got extremely mad. Followed by a sudo date -s "15 MAY 2009 18:00:00" making the time re-run from there in the right direction. He breathed a sigh of relief, then started to cough.

Indeed, in May 2009 he had had the swine flu.

He really needed a break, so he packed up a few things and went to the beach. &#8220;Rhodey is my best friend - his ancestors came from Rhodesia, and as a result he loved basking in the sun. Sadly the Portsmouth/Bilbao ferry becomes defunct soon and no amount of really hot sweet tea can revive it.&#8221; Sandra watched the waves lapping against her Yagi-Uda array and wondered if the source was within the milky way. But nobody answered because Julio was the only one who knew Sandra, and he was angry and wasn't speaking to anyone. To her surprise when she got there it was already broken, which proves it is better to travel in hope than to arrive, even at a beach.

Linus created String named msgTemp8 and linked her to a spot on memory. But the link was suddenly reset and removed by a garbage collector. At least Tove was there to make him soup. But she suddenly saw Sandra and shouted "Who is she? What is she doing here?" Regression had taken such a hold that she did not know whether it was decomposition or entropy bringing us to this deja-vu. It became obvious her 4x4 was no match for my WR450 and even the Ténéré was struggling to keep up. But she suddenly jumped on a brand new R1 and started to chase her prey. 

Tsunami comes, using his laptop, he emulates him self to the virtual world. Few seconds later, Linus realizes that he has an assignment to do. "I really need an SDRAM today, just to recover my old machine... I'm so desprate to do my homework in java!", Linus said.

Tux sighed as he read the various wanderings on the thread. &#8220;I have told you a million times not to exaggerate.&#8221; 

Julio thought he misunderstood something, so picked up the newspaper and read. Pricetech felt foolish after being shown the article by Julio. Even foolisher when he discovered that 6 days had passed since his last post and that no one else had posted forcing him to single handedly revive the thread. Which he did very well, giving a new life to the story. &#8220;Only twelve more after this to discover if there is a discernable theme or our valiant efforts are lost in the ether.&#8221; But wait ... what was this in the editorial - a disclaimer!

&#8220;Items featured here and elsewhere in this publication are the responsibility of those submitting them and not the responsibility of the publication, its owners, or the lady on the corner.&#8221;

Everyone was angered by the length of the disclaimer, especially since the thread was getting slow, probably because many had forgotten the original story line.

After a long day of work, Linus finally rested his head. Having reached the first milestone Linus had now to decide if the opening moves are a classic gambit for a chessnerd to arrange. Before he fell asleep he had an idea to have several different people write a story one sentence at a time. Unfortunately he had forgotten where he had put his head to rest. Then Spot bit him on the leg.
