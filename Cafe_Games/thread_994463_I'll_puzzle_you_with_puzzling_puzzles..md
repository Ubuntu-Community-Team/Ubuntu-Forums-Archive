### Post by pp. on 2008-12-01
The expression in the right hand bracket is a number followed by the digit 0 (zero) which is printed as a superscript.

Assuming this time the expression to follow the conventions for mathematical notations, that means "the number raised to the zero-th power". Since I have no easy access to superscripting here, I will use the text-based notation y^0 to express the same.

Now, we all ought to know that y^1 = y.

Also, we ought to know that y^(n-1) = (y^n)/y.

Hence: 

y^(2-1) = y^2 / y = y^1 = y  and
y^(1-1) = y^1 / y = y^0 = y / y = 1

Any real number raised to the zero-th power just gives one. (I'd have to consult my books to see what 0^0 is, though).

Your pretty large number in the right hand bracket raised to the power of zero is not zero but one.

We know that the number is at most 6 away from other numbers. This immediately rules out 39, 49, and 50 which are 10 and 11 away from each other. It also rules out 47 that is 8 away from 39.
