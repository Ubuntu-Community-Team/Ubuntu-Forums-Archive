### Post by thomas144 on 2010-11-19
Here's the deal. We make a story of Tux. Not much else to do. These are some recommendations. Not rules, but recommendations:

1. It's good to end your post in mid-sentence. For example:
And then Tux booted up his PC and was shocked to see...

2. When I post to stories, I like to write posts that are fairly long, like:
And then Bill Gates snuck into Tux's house. He found his main desktop and popped in a CD. Two hours later (yes, two hours!), it had Windows Vista on it. Bill Gates was very satisfied that he just did this, so to celebrate, he clicked on Tux's radio. But, it was the middle of the night, so Tux heard it and woke up. He walked downstairs, saw Bill Gates, and said...

I don't like:
And then Bill Gates snuck into Tux's house....

But these are just my ideas and in no way mandatory. It just seems that stories flow better in larger chunks. Just my suggestions. Also, let me note that every once in a while, I'll compile the story into one piece. This way, we can all see what we put together.:D

Now, I'll start the story.
