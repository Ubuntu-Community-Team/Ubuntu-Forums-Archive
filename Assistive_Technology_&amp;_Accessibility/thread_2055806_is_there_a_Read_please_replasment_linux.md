### Post by tartalo on 2012-09-10
If this is for a visually impaired person you probably want a screen reader, I believe that Orca and emacspeak are the most popular choices.

Orca is a screen reader for Gnome and it's available in Ubuntu's repositories. 

There's also an Ubuntu based distribution called Vinux that includes Orca and other accessibility tools for blind and partially sighted people on top of a graphical interface: [http://vinuxproject.org/](http://vinuxproject.org/)

Blind people have another option called emacspeak, which is a complete audio desktop based on emacs, it doesn't need a graphical environment and it's available in Ubuntu's repositories. More info here: [http://emacspeak.sourceforge.net/](http://emacspeak.sourceforge.net/)

If what you want is just a program that reads aloud, a text-to-speech program might be enough, festival and espeak are popular choices, both are available in Ubuntu's repositories, they are command line programs but there are several front-ends available for them.
