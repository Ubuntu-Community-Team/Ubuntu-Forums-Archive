### Post by QIII on 2021-06-07
xpra is in the Universe repository.  That indicates that it is an application maintained by a MOTU (Master of the Universe) -- a volunteer who packages that *community-maintained* package.  That is, it's not included in your Ubuntu installation by default.  You have to activate that repository and understand that everything in there is a roll of the dice.  If that package does not work, you will have to contact the MOTU or those who develop and maintain that package.

I do not even activate Universe or Multiverse. 

And again, I will re-state what is said above:  We are all volunteers.  We are not employed by Canonical, LTD, and cannot, therefore, affect changes to Canonical's policies or the software provided.  Making demands such as yours is not the surest route to avoiding Staff attention.
