### Post by T700 on 2006-08-02
233Mhz is not going to provide a happy experience with Ubuntu.  How much RAM and disk space do you have?  I'd strongly suggest Xubuntu using the alternate (text based) installer.  It is lighter on resources and you would at least have a chance of it working.  

The basic steps are:  

1.  Download the ISO.

2.  Burn the ISO as an image.  **Do not copy it, install it in Windows, or make a bootable disk--burn it as an ISO image!**

3.  Verify that your PC is set to boot from CD.

4.  Insert your CD in the PC and reboot.

5.  Follow the prompts.
