### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-02-13
> **UnixBASHparty said:**
> I never used a tiling wm; how does it work? Mouse keyboard both?

wmii is primarily keyboard driven, you don't need to use the mouse to execute apps, or to move apps around the screen.
It's very good for computers with small screens (ie netbooks), as it doesn't bother drawing window decorations, and generally makes applications full-screen or 1/2 screen if you have 2 applications open.

There's a few youtube videos of it in action, which gives you a good idea of it.  It's even better to use once you get the hang of it.

[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o8o7Y0LFZGw](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o8o7Y0LFZGw)
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQWk3iW1Vno](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQWk3iW1Vno)
