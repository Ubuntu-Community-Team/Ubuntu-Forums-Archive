### Post by _DD_ on 2008-06-10
> **superbenny said:**
> "Ubuntu is a very nice alternative to Windows computers..."
I don't really like that one. It just doesn't sound right. Its something to do with how it kinda implies that the computer hardware is also Windows, and Ubuntu is an alternative computer, not just an OS you can install over Windows on (virtually) any x86 computer.

Some people don't understand what operating systems are - they just think that the stuff you see on the screen *is* is the computer (I've talked to some people who haven't even heard of Windows before or don't begin to understand what it is), so a statement like that would just confuse things further.

The classic place to see this in play is if you watch the CNN news report on the release on Windows 95 (I think its CNN, you can find it on youtube); whoever wrote the commentary obviously didn't know much about computers, as they seemed to question the hype because it was only an operating system that was made to sound like it didn't actually do much for the computer (or "the program that talks to other programs" as I seem to remember it said).

Yes, I know its Windows 95 and it probably didn't do too much to help the computer it was installed on (:lolflag:), but this demonstrates how easily people misunderstand what an operating system is.


Much better would be simply to say "a powerful and viable alternative to Windows".
Another thing you could touch on is that its derived from the same roots as Apple OS X and shares OS X's stability and security, yet its free and far more flexible. [size=1]Before anyone says; I know that OS X is Unix-based and in the BSD family, blah blah blah, but its a valid simplification.[/size]
