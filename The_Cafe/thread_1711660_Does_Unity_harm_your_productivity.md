### Post by blueridgedog on 2011-03-27
This thread is interesting as a long time Linux user (outside of the Ubuntu specific elements).  I recall using xwindows when it was more or less a method of having multiple terminals open at a time and the number of graphical applications was limited.  We ran a variety of window managers at first (sawmill, enlightenment, xwm, fvwm and others).

Over time we have developed as a community and edged towards desktops that run a given window manager as part of a broader environment.  The Gnome desktop with metaciy/beryl/compiz as its manager is an example.  KDE, CDE and XFCE are others.  These desktops environments have taken the place of some of the prior system configuration tools (you don't really need an xwindows configuration file anymore with gnome, you don't have to configure your networking interfaces and a whole host of other things are now done by a "desktop environment" that were once done by the user through the course of setting up and managing a system). 

The fact however, is that you can run a variety of window managers and a variety of interface tools.  If you are a Linux user and you have not tried other window mangers, then you are missing out on part of the fun and part of the freedom.

What I see at play is the fact that we now have a large userbase who are really happy with a very advanced product that has a high degree of functionality.  We FINALLY have something so good that people are starting to be resistant to moving the development forward.  For almost two decades we have pushed and pushed and pushed to evolve the Linux desktop and we have now crossed the point where some are willing to say "good enough...what I have works, I like it and I can do everything I need with it".  They may not have been around or do not recall the days when we did not have a graphical web browser or did not have any desktop environments. 

With Linux we have always been striving, developing and growing.  It is thrilling to think that we are to the point where it is so good that some are ready to setup camp forever.  But we never will do that.  We will always keep  moving forward.  Regardless of what you call it, Unity, Gnome3, KDE 4, Gnome Shell or ???? the point is that the state of the art of Linux is always a moving target.  Those of us who started when there were no choices are pretty excited.  This is the same level of inovation that got us to where we are, so just imagine where it will take us!

So, the two things to keep in mind is that Linux has a history of mixing your own window manager and desktop tools and a history of growing and developing.  If you don't like something, odds are you have ten other options and if you just wait long enough, it will change!  

Be diverse, move forward and love using Linux on your computer.
