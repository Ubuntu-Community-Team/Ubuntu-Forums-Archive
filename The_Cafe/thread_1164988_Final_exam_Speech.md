### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-05-20
Using a projector would be almost essential to get their attention. It is kind of boring to look at someone demonstrating stuff on a small screen.

Do you have a TV card with remote?

You could setup the remote to activate several compiz features. For example I rotate the cube from the remote to check some stuff while playing videos on full screen. You could open media files and other programs on different desktops using the compiz "Place Windows" plugins and switch desktop using different effects while making your presentation.

You could also run Windows inside a Virtualbox. If you could make Windows crash and give you a BSOD, then would be freaking cool :)

Another thing that you could also do is to log on the Ubuntu IRC channel and select a couple of questions from the other students to ask to the community. This will give them an idea how the community works and how important it is. You could also create a thread on "Absolute Beginner Talk" forum and invite your school mates to join and ask questions later.
