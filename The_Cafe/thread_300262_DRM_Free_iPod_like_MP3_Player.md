### Post by skymt on 2006-11-15
Take a look at the [Sandisk lineup](http://sandisk.com/Products/Catalog(1014)-MP3_Players.aspx). Specifically, the e200 series. Linux and Ogg Vorbis compatible, decent capacity for a flash player (2-8 gigs), stylish, and cheap. It's not DRM-free (it supports Microsoft PlaysForSure), but nothing is these days, and you aren't forced to buy DRM-ed music.

If you want something completely DRM-less, try one of the [iAudio players](http://www.cowonglobal.com/product/product_iaudio_flash.php) from Cowon. I don't like them as much as the Sandisk players, but only a few have any DRM support.

### Post by angrykeyboarder on 2006-11-18
You can play any DRM-free mp3 file in any mp3 player or iPod. What you can't do is play a file protected with DRM unless the player supports it.

DRM applies to the copy-protection on the files.  It doesn't apply to the player itself.

So as long as you purchase music from places such as Naptster, URGE, Rhapsody, Yahoo! and the iTunes Music store, it will be DRM-protected (encrypted) regarless of how you listen to it.
