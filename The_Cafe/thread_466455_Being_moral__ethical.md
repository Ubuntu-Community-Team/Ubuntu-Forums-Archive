### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-06-06
Ok, I'll bite...  Mind you, during my role-playing days, when I took a test that tried to categorize a person's mentality per AD&D alignments, I ranked as Chaotic-Good (meaning I would do whatever it took, regardless of Laws, to achieve the greater good.  Wow, set dork factor to warp factor 5!) 

> If a band releases an album in 2007 and by 2009 you can't find copies of it anymore and nobody even knows of the band anymore, is it wrong to copy the songs from a friend's CD that you just met?Sure.  In fact, you might end up spreading the music around as mp3's, causing a revival of said band, and letting them go on a come-back tour.  (See, karma can split either way).

> If your husband/wife needs to receive some antidote to survive, only you can make the antidote (only you have the equipment), and the antidote's ingredients are patented / copyrighted so that making the antidote is illegal, would you make the antidote to save him/her (which may entail stealing the recipe?)Yes, absolutely.  While I don't mind folks profiting from innovation, I'm a firm believer that medicine and business shouldn't mix.  My reasoning is that eventually, the bigger a company gets, the more its focus will be on profit and less on providing quality product / service.  When medical corporations (hospitals, HMO's, pharma-corps, etc) focus more and more on profit, they find ways to get people sick and keep them sick, because they make money off of it.  You may laugh at that, and I'm not saying they do it directly.  However, as an example, studies came out showing fructose can induce diabetes in rats.  (In fact, today, researchers feed rats fructose to induce a diabetic state so they can do more diabetes research).  So, those studies come out, and you'd think the USA FDA would start banning or rationing the amount of high-fructose corn syrup that could be used in food.  No.  Food and Drug lobbyists made sure nothing like that ever happened.  So, food companies continue tossing tons of high-fructose corn syrup in foods, and the rate of diabetes in the US has continued to rise (along with obesity and the other problems that come along with it).  The pharma-corps get to make a nice profit off their insulin drugs, and the hospitals get a nice profit from all the over-charged heart-attack patients that get wheeled through the doors.  I'm not saying it's a conspiracy.  I'm saying it's a food chain, and people are the algae...  So, by all means, if someone is dying, and I had the power to concoct something to save them, I sure as heck would do it.  The Hippocratic Oath states that anothers well-being comes above all else.  That's why the doctor that treated John Boothe (the guy who shot Abraham Lincoln) wasn't charged with aiding and abetting a criminal...he was simply following his Hippocratic Oath to help a person in need above all else.


> If your dad promised you $100 to go to summer science camp and at the last minute took it to go on a fishing trip, should you be angry that your dad broke his promise or should you accept the fact that he needed the money?I would be very angry.  If my father made a promise to me about something, then turned tail on it at the last minute for a personal whim, then he's setting a very poor example as a father.  However, he's teaching me a very valuable lesson...you can't trust anyone in life, even your own parents.  Of course, my come-uppance would arrive when he's old and decrepit, and needs to have money to live in a retirement home.  I'd say I'd pay for it, then at the last minute go out to Vegas and gamble it away.  If he complained about that, then I'd simply recall the tail about the summer camp trip and say "unfortunately for you...like father like son...."

> If you and your friend agree to meet at 8 oclock at a restaurant and a man desperately  asks you at 7:50 on the street to help him, do you help the stranger or go to see your friend?I help the stranger, but first I ask enough questions to make sure he really does need help.  If my friend doesn't understand that this guy needed help, and that's why I'm late, then they wouldn't be my friend to begin with.

> If you had to choose between using Windows with no penalty versus using Linux and having two birds killed per hour of use, which would you choose? :O I hope this is a no-brainer.This is a really stupid question.  Ok, yeah, to save the lives of the birds, I'd go back to using Windows.  But, when you say "no penalty" what do you mean?  Free, no licensing, open source code, etc?  That's very open-ended.

All the other questions were interesting derivatives of business and social ethics.  That last one makes me want to strangle you because its so loaded.
