### Post by Wolki on 2005-08-27
[QUOTE=Cool_dude_Prav]
XFCE - lightest and most Eye candy (except KDE)
Gnome Tools - such as GIMP and stuff, only the best...
KDE Tools - such as K3B and stuff, only the best here again...
[/QUOTE]

Well, that would mean that both Qt and Gtk libraries will have to be included, along with libraries for each DE. So unless you remove apps it won't fit onto a cd.

Also, the best of each DE is a highly subjective thing. For example, I don't like XFCE because the panel is very unflexible and i don't like the default config. Some people like Amarok for playing Music, some Rythmbox and some Muine. Others want the simple old-school xmms or its newer incarnation beep. Which one gets on the CD?

And, while it's usually not a problem during daily use, I doubt mixing DEs for default configs is a good Idea. Default should be that every program has a similar Interface, and KDE/Gnome really differ here (button order. configuration, look&feel ...). I think it would be confusing for new users.

Since installing programs is easy thanks to modern package managers, I don't see the reason for such a project.

### Post by darkmatter on 2005-08-28

Though I find your idea an interesting one, I must disagree with the above quotation... ;-)

I'm actually planning an Ubuntu-based distro, so trust me, there are many considerations to deal with other than the applications (correcting dependancies for example. as some packages built for Ubuntu havew dependencies other than those of their source package). What makes it unique? What new features can I offer end users? How can I improve on what has already been done?...

Anything of lesser consideration isn't a distro - it's a monstrosity.... 

My advice, find others who are willing to asist you and start planning what you want your distro to be, rather than just repackaging. Although, admittedly, repackaging is no small chore. ;-)

I like all of them (except Rhythmbox, Muine suits me better).
gnome terminal can do everything I want it to, looks nice and isn't too complicated.
