### Post by requeth on 2010-06-18
I'll start first with Ubuntu. To describe the logo I'll sum up what the African word Ubuntu means. A good translation is "the belief in a universal bond of sharing that connects all humanity." ([http://www.easy-ubuntu-linux.com/ubuntu-african-word.html](http://www.easy-ubuntu-linux.com/ubuntu-african-word.html)). The Ubuntu logo is three people of different races holding hands together. Shuttleworth's dream is to make a linux that everybody can use. Since it's now sold in walmart, I think that's mission accomplished. ;)

Linux's penguin comes from the founder of Linux Linus Trivolds. In a discussion about mascots he said he liked penguins, which caused the discussion to switch to what position the penguin should be in. The penguin was named "Tux" after a while and all was right in the world. No real official reason was ever put out that I know of justifying Tux. ([http://www.sjbaker.org/wiki/index.php?title=The_History_of_Tux_the_Linux_Penguin)Unofficially]("http://www.sjbaker.org/wiki/index.php?title=The_History_of_Tux_the_Linux_Penguin%29Unofficially") I have heard the story that penguins huddle together and when attacked all attack as a group and can take down even the largest walrus. Linux people do tend to huddle together on the internet and we generally do complain a lot about Windows...so...

Anyway, think that answers your questions.
