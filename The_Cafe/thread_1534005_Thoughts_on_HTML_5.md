Don't worry! Asking questions is always good :)

The Video stuff is definitely not what html5 is all about by any stretch. However, for that in particular, take a look at the img tag. At first, HTML didn't have <img>, and at some point lots of weird browser-specific extensions started to emerge in order to fill that gap. The standard later evolved to fill it properly.

Now we have the <embed> tag so Flash can live within valid HTML, but in terms of functionality it is still a client-specific extension. The <video> and <audio> tags do for those types of media what the <img> tag did for video.
(And, because it's properly integrated with web standards, we get awesome goodies as well).
