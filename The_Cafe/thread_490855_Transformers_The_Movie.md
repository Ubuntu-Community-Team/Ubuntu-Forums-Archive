### Post by Calash on 2007-07-06
In all I liked it.  They did a retelling of the story, and in that they did a good job.

Some of the dialog was very VERY cheesy, to the point of being funny.  The military guy was just so over the top that I could not help but to laugh.  The stereotypical romance between the two kids was a bit much as well, however did we really go to a movie called Transformers to see the humans?

No...we went to see the robots.  And they were SPECTACULAR.  A bit over complicated as noted above, but the confusion wore off after a bit.  They each had a new look, but held some tie to there cartoon origin.  The faces were very detailed, and it was obvious that they use them to convey the emotions of each character.  Megatron had the evil teeth, Prime the more noble and strong features.  Would have been happier if Prime had his blast shield up all the time...it is just a better look.

The Decepticons did not get enough air time, especially Megatron who did not even get to take an earth form (Tank, gun, whatever).  I do see an opening for a sequel though, with Galvitron.  If they do go that route I hope they expand on him more, and the constant conflict with Starscream.

Prime was just about perfect as far as personality.  Rambling inspirational speeches, self sacrificing and expecting his troops to be the same, and overly caring for the humans.  The end with Jazz did not sit well with me though...seemed a bit too emotionless for the normally caring Prime.

There was way to much product placement though...the Mountain Dew machine and XBox 360 being some of the worst.  Guess you have to pay the bills somehow.

In all I really enjoyed the movie.  It was a different take on the story, but well explained and entertaining.

### Post by MonkeyBoy on 2007-07-13
I read an interview with Alan Moore recently where he pointed out that when something is good in one media it doesn't necessarily mean it should be translated into another.  Transformers is a fine example of this.  It was always toys and anime until now and that was fine but this movie really alters the whole Transformers style to a point where it is unrecognisable.  What a load of rubbish.

There have been a lot of examples of cool things being ruined by movies recently:  V for Vendetta, Spiderman, Batman, Fantastic 4, Lord of the Rings, War of the Worlds, Dune, League of Extraordinary Gentlemen, Hulk, Constantine, and the list goes on.
