### Post by DBO on 2008-09-05
Hey all,

Most of you probably don't know me, but i am the primary (not the original) developer of GNOME Do right now.  David is on break right now while he gets his new job and life squared away.  In light of this I have been given a lot more freedom to really get GNOME Do looking its very best, performing better than it has ever before, and generally doing more.  All is/was in fact going quite well.  The skinny on the new features that are, or are almost, done:
[LIST]
[*]Faster Searching
[*]Bezel HUD Clone Interface (from quicksilver)
[*]Animated Interfaces (fading icons, animated expand, animated child in/out)
[*]ShowCase Interface (inspired from quicksilver, but radically changed)
[*]Text mode that allows for complex text entry.  Compose an entire formatted email in Do
[*]Better crash protection/screen stealing prevention
[*]Improved multiple item selection support
[*]Custom theming/keybindings (still highly experimental)
[*]Overall polish and finish that has thus far been lacking
[*]a11y work
[*]Much more...
[/LIST]

However, today my laptop has gone belly up.  The only development machine I have is my work machine which runs windows and is not possible for me to dual boot (I don't really own it).  This leaves GNOME Do high and dry until I get a new machine, which as a poor college students is likely to not be very soon.

So I am not here to ask for donations (yet).  What I am here to ask is your opinion.  What should I... Do?

The way I see it, I have two options:

1) Beg and plead the community to help me get a new development laptop (it really does need to be a laptop with how much I am on the move).  I am afraid the comminity will see this as a selfish move however, so I want to ask you guys first.

2) Wait until I can afford a new machine and restart development then.

What do you guys think?

Jason "DBO" Smith
