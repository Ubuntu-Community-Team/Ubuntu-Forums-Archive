### Post by stmiller on 2007-11-12
It IS perfectly legal in many parts of the world .... except the US. However, it is somewhat of a grey area. The US copyright law allows copies of purchased items for fair use, in the same way you can legally make a copy of your software CDs and software DVDs for personal backup or use, or rip a CD with iTunes to put the audio onto your iPod, etc. All fair use and A-OK.

But when you purchase a DVD, technically you are purchasing a license to privately view the DVD content in your home- you are not purchasing the DVD contents, in the way that you purchase and own an audio CD to rip in iTunes.

SO, that DVD license that you purchased is the MPAA's way of saying that you are NOT entitled to fair use, because you don't own a personal copy of the content in the first place, blah blah blah... grumble grumble.

FWIW it's also not 'legal' to listen to MP3s in Linux and so forth. So, I mean, ultimately it's up to you and what you feel comfortable with doing with your purchased content.

Checkout the program HandBrake. It works quite well.
