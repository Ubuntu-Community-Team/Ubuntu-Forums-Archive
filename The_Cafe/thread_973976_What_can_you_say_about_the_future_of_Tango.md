### Post by wersdaluv on 2008-11-07
The [Tango Desktop Project]("http://tango.freedesktop.org/Tango_Desktop_Project") is a very influential to the look and feel of the GNOME desktop. It sets the standard of what fits the rest of the desktop's look and feel or not. Projects from Pidgin to OpenOffice.org depend on its guidelines for their icons and the rest of the theme.

As for the KDE desktop, it evolved from [Everaldo's Crystal]("http://www.everaldo.com/crystal/") to the new [Oxygen]("http://www.oxygen-icons.org/"). This fact makes me think of how a desktop evolves its look and feel. It makes me wonder what GNOME is going to do next. KDE's look managed to let go of its old standards and go to a different direction.

The same is true for Windows. Window's XP's look and feel is very far from Windows Vista's [Aero]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_Aero"). They would not have achieved that if they did not let go of the standards of the Windows XP look and feel.

What do you think about Tango? How much more can it be improved? How much room for improvement does [its guidlines]("http://tango.freedesktop.org/Tango_Icon_Theme_Guidelines") allow? This could be subjective but, could it match the beauty of Oxygen, Aero, and OS X icons? Should GNOME's look and feel move on and ditch it [like some of the icon themes on gnome-look.org]("http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=120x121")?
