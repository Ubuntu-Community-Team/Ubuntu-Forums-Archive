### Post by psyopper on 2007-11-19
I find Compiz-Fusion indespensible for productivity. Sure, there are some really useless "wow" plugins like snow and water but with some configuration most of the effects really do help productivity. For instance:

1. Cube, as pointed out, gives a separation fo workspaces. Sure, most other WM's have workspaces, but how many other WM's allow you to preview a workspace with a single mouse click? I have desktop cube set to rotate on Button2 (middle mouse button) and Transparent on Mouse Rotate, 40%. With a single click of the center mouse button I can see through my cube and look at both my current workspace and the one opposite it to see what's going on on the other side.

2. Blur Windows. I'm new to Linux and am learning the command line. Instead of blindly cut/paste-ing into the terminal I have my terminal set to transparent, about 80% so I can lay it on top of a browser and actually see what I need to type. Sure, you can do this with other WM's, but with the blur I can add a "frost" effect to the transparency to help the terminal stand out more and keep the text layers from bleeding into each other.

3. Task Switchers. There's a plethora of task switchers available for C-F, each of them offering a different style to match mental workspace management. I find that Ring Switcher works best for my brain. Unless I go to a Mac this type of switching is not available to me.

4. Animations. While it may seem like bling I think separation of what actions you take with a window being visibly reinforced by the effect gives a user a sense of confidence that they did the "right" thing with the window. Is Burn a little extravagant? Sure, but Burn on closing a window and fade on a minimize does a good job of visually separating the activity.

5. Enhanced Zoom. Ever come across one of the myriad Flash players that dont do "full screen" in Linux? No? Try the videos at Revision3.com, they don't work right. Which is a shame because R3 is a tech-video-website by the ultimate geeks who brought you TechTV and Digg. With Enhanced Zoom Desktop now you CAN watch those videos in something other than a postage stamp.

Take a look at the [Compiz Fusion Wiki]("http://wiki.compiz-fusion.org") and you will see just where/how most of the effects actually help with workspace manipulation and podictivity optimization.

### Post by reacocard on 2007-11-19
scale and expo are both very useful. quite frankly, that's about all I use. The real benefit of compiz and the like isn't the 'bling' that you see on youtube, it's the enabling of applications to use compositing and the moving of much of the stress of drawing onto the GPU.  compiz makes possible things like AWN and screenlets that otherwise would either not exist or not work anywhere near as well as they do with compositing. Also, in environments such as real-time audio processing, moving the rendering of the desktop onto the GPU frees up the CPU to do the important work with much less chance of being interrupted.
