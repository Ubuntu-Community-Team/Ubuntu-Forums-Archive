### Post by Portmanteaufu on 2009-10-22
I've been closely following the upcoming release of the Motorola Droid smartphone, and in a lot of the articles I read I notice that a common discussion point is how well the iPhone and Android do or do not support Microsoft Exchange.

I find this peculiar because my company (which has tens of thousands of employees) like many others has a relatively locked-down e-mail setup that happens to feature an exclusive arrangement with RIM's Blackberry. In order to collect your work e-mail, you would have to be on the company network either by VPN or by being on-site... or you could have a work-issued Blackberry that the company has granted special privileges.

So! With that in mind, if I were to buy an iPhone or any Android phone, I wouldn't be able to connect to Exchange anyway. Since I suspect that most sizable companies have similar e-mail access rules and that very, very few people are running a personal Exchange server, why does this matter as much as it seems to? Is my company more of an oddity than I think it is? Are there people out there with iPhones/Android phones that have been allowed to connect to their work mail? If so, could you describe your company's size and which industry it falls into?
