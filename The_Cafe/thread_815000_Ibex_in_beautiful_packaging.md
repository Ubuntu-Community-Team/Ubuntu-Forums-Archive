### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-06-01
> **MONODA said:**
> also make sure that the packaging is creative on the inside, the zune packaging has always looked awesome to me.

If you want awesome packaging, check out how Wacom does it with their Bamboo drawing tablets. Instead of horrible plastic, they use a light papery material that can just be unfolded from around things rather than needing a pair of scissors and a chicken sacrifice to get open. As far as I am aware, just about everything in there is recyclable and the only plastic I saw (besides the hardware itself) was around the USB cable.

If Ubuntu was to have a real life shelf presence, having a good package that does not encourage becoming a useless hunk of plastic would be quite important. As an example of environmentally unfriendly packaging, we have Microsoft's crazy hard plastic cases. Another thing that can make software packaging bad, though, is the issue that license keys cannot be verified without actually *using* said keys. For example, a person could buy some software, then decide he doesn't want it and return it to the store. The store cannot just resell the software; they must return it, in which case we can be nearly certain it gets thrown out (since the material price is tiny). Why? Because of a little license key written on a piece of paper.

I think a saleable Ubuntu package would really good, but it would need a few things beyond the software to be successful:

-A printed manual. Not huge, but with enough information to get someone started. As a quick example stepping above and beyond the norm, the Help Center has an article on basic computer skills, though it could do with some pictures. Trouble with it is a need for basic computer skills to get there! A printed manual solves that.

-64-bit and 32-bit CDs, alternate install CD. "Software you may need" CD (for example, restricted drivers in the event that the user lacks an Internet connection).

-Real support services for at least 6 months. Indeed, this means big cost (maybe best as an add-on), but I think it would be a really important detail.

-A sticker :)
