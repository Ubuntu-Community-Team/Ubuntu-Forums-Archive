### Post by Jussi Kukkonen on 2006-10-18
> It seems that people are all about open source... but when it comes to actually doing something to get more people involved... they just don't do anything...
If you build it, they will come... In these circles just announcing something is worth nothing: if you want people to participate, there has to be something that people can start playing with. I your case that would maybe mean getting an issue of your magazine out, even if it's just on the web. If it's good (somehow better than the alternatives), people will hopefully want to work with you.

Practical advice for the forum: Get rid of the subforums. Start with one forum and start creating subforums when/if people seem contribute a lot on   a specific subject -- the current  design just underlines the small amount of contributions, actively prevents discussion and seems bureocratic.
