### Post by Lightstar on 2011-05-02
While this title is really vague, I'm posting about Operating Systems here.

For awhile now, whenever I received an old computer, the first thing I would do is wipe it clean, install a version of linux and give it out.

Now comes an uncle, who never really used a computer beside his ugly DOS looking program at work to track inventory.  He has no experience with anything else.  He wants a computer.

My values tells me that I should put linux, set it up safe and fast.  Knowing we wont have to deal with stupid spyware and virus which will save us both frustration.  Also the OS being free.

My logic tells me that I should put Windows with a good antivirus.  The reason for it being the most common OS out there.  I think I would feel pretty bad if whenever he went to a friend's house and got on their computer, he would scratch his head and look stupid, computer illiterate, because he never used windows and only knows linux.

So here I am, fighting with myself.
Should I force on him my own preferences, or give him a tool that should help him on a much larger scale.

Now that I'm done writing this, I'm thinking it might be better for his sake to put windows, maybe a small partition of linux, in the hope that his own curiosity pushes him to learn it later on.

What's your opinions?
