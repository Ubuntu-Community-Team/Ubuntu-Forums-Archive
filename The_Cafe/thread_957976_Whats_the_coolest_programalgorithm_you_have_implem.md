### Post by yabbadabbadont on 2008-10-24
I don't know about "cool", but I was always a bit proud of a DOS version of the tar utility I wrote for properly spanning multiple floppy disks.  The man pages on SCO Unix (yes, I know) were incorrect/incomplete when it came to properly describing the file format, so I ended up reverse engineering the format.  Then I discovered that the DOS interrupt 21h function calls *did care* about the the disks not containing a FAT filesystem, even though the docs said that they didn't.  (Abort, Retry, Fail anyone...)  I ended up directly using the BIOS interrupt 10h functions to do the low level reads and writes.

I also wrote a pretty cool program for displaying PCX images.  (which most of you are too young to remember :D)  It would even properly detect a Hercules display adapter and make use of the "enhanced" graphics resolution it provided.  (VGA didn't exist yet and I didn't have access to an EGA card)
