### Post by DirtyPC on 2011-06-11
> Bad connection is possible, but I'd rather expect that either the inverter or CCFL tube is gone. Did you get a red-ish (or maybe even flashing) display before? If so, your CCFL died; otherwise, if the problem just appeared out of nowhere, it is most likely because of a bad inverter.

Try to find a service manual for the laptop, it should describe how to replace the inverter. At least for the devices I had to deal with, this is some small PCB than can be exchanged quite easily; i.e., not built onto the mainboard.

If it turns out your CCFL is bad, you have a real problem, as these are usually not meant to be replaced. Even if sent in for warranty, the service center will usually replace the whole display unit. It is possible to replace the CCFL yourself, though, but I recommend not to even begin trying unless you have a very steady hand and quite some experience with a soldering iron.
