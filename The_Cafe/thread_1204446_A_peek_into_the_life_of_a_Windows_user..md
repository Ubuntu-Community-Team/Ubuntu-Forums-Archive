### Post by monsterstack on 2009-07-04
One of the first things I noticed after switching over was the sheer difference in the way Linux software sites present themselves. To find freeware Windows applications you have to include the word "free" somewhere in your Google search, and that will lead you through a maze of ad-infested sites offering trial versions of professional pro software (always advertised as being in a box). Everyone's just trying to make a buck. The claims are more often than not dishonest, too.

To illustrate my point, compare [this]("http://digitaldump.wordpress.com/projects/rednotebook/") with [this]("http://www.davidrm.com/").

> **Linux sites:**
RedNotebook is a graphical diary and journal helping you keep track of notes and thoughts. It includes a calendar navigation, customizable templates for each day, export functionality and word clouds. You can also format, tag and search your entries. [Download link.]

> **Windows sites:**
Start today! If you have ever wanted to keep a personal journal, start a diary, or simply organize your many notes, now is the time. The Journal offers a Free 45-day Trial. So Download The Journal today! (Registration required)
