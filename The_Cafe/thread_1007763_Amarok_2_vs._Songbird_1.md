### Post by wersdaluv on 2008-12-10
I choose Amarok 2. I've been trying Songbird 1 for days. The features pwn and it looks like it has a good future but it's a resource hog and it still needs some polishing.

Amarok 2, on the other hand is more resource efficient, more stable, and fits better the look and feel of my GNOME desktop because I'm using qgtkstyle. There's the native bird plugin for Songbird but it doesn't work right for me. 

Overall, Amarok 2 wins the departments of efficiency, Linux desktop integration, and stability. As for plugins, Songbird is better because it's easier to customize but Amarok 2 isn't doing bad either and it is definitely better for KDE4 users because it's using Plasma.

### Post by PsyWolf on 2009-04-27
i'm seeing alot of people critisizing amarok for being too busy.  claiming that they don't need a full screen for their music.

i totally agree which is why once i start my music playing, i minimize amarok to the system tray (default on close) and then i use the amazing system tray icon.

 Using the tray icon's tricks

 Hovering your mouse on the icon will display a nice popup with current track information.
    * Clicking the middle mouse button on the icon play-pauses the current track.
    * Scrolling the mouse wheel changes volume.
    * Holding Shift while scrolling the mouse wheel seeks through the current track.
    * Holding Control while scrolling the mouse wheel will skip through tracks in the playlist.
    * Dragging and dropping items into the tray icon opens a pop up menu allowing you to choose from three options, Append to Playlist, Append and Play, and Queue after Current Track.
    * Tray icon is also a song position indicator. 

if are on a laptop or just prefer your keyboard, you can alternatively configure and use the global shortcuts to do all the same things without ever touching the mouse.  

until songbird an addon for similar "global" functionality, i'm sticking with amarok.  plus, i'm not a big fan of resource hogs.

for more amarok tips, check out [http://amarok.kde.org/wiki/Tips_and_Tricks](http://amarok.kde.org/wiki/Tips_and_Tricks)
