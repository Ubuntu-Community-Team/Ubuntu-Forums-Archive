### Post by rucadulu on 2009-04-15
I was chatting with a friend the other day and we got to talking about Ubuntu and various other Linux distro's. The subject came up about Ubuntu changing its base from Debian to another base Like Slackware or just going it on its own and building Ubuntu form scratch. We agreed to disagree. I thought Ubuntu should build form scratch where as he liked the Slackware path. Either way we both thought Ubuntu should leave Debain behind.  

Just curious what others think about this topic.


Added later in thread in separate post:  

I think that Ubuntu's, Mandriva's, and Fedora's Development pace is far greater then Debian's. And I worry that if Ubuntu is to dependent on Debian that it could fall behind Mandriva and Fedora in the Development and implementation of new code.This is my reason for suggesting that Ubuntu create its own base from scratch. I am really not a big fan of Fedora and would hate to see Ubuntu fall behind Fedora. Mandriva on the other hand I think is a well put together OS and it is good competition for Ubuntu. 

As for the Slackware idea like I said that was my friends, his reason was that Slackware is a more stable base from which to grow. And for the record I do not agree that Slackware is more stable than Debian. They are both rock solid and slow to change.
