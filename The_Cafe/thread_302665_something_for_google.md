> About Flamingo

Flamingo is a simple interactive mapviewer, no more no less.
But this limitation is also the strongest point of Flamingo. With only a handful of toolbuttons users can easily use a map. Flamingo offers only the basic tools as zoom in and out, pan, identify and search. From experience we know by now users like to use Flamingo.
So, with Flamingo organisations are able to share their geo-information as user friendly interactive maps.
Besides, Flamingo is very flexible, available for free and open source.
Flamingo is developed in Flash and requires Flash Player version 7 or higher. Nowadays a suitable version of Flash Player is integrated in most browsers.
By using Flash Flamingo is platform independent. So Flamingo runs on Windows, Linux, Apple and Unix systems and with almost all modern browsers.
Flamingo is tested with Internet Explorer, Firefox en Opera. Users also report that Flamingo runs succesfully in Safari.
The Dutch provinces developed Flamingo for their own use. The Change Advisory Board consists of nine members from eight provinces. For the Change Advisory Board the Dutch provinces remain the most important target group for which they manage and control Flamingo.
On April 27th 2006, Flamingo was released as GPL licensed Open Source software.
Unfortunately time is limited to support the use of Flamingo outside the Dutch provinces. As a result all documentation is available in Dutch only.
If you are willing and able to assist in translating these documents to English we would greatly welcome your help!
