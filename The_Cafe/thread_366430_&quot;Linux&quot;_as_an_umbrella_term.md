### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-02-22
Saying "I use Linux" is plenty of information for someone who has never used Linux, and saying "I use Ubuntu Linux" is too much information for someone who has never used Linux. Trying to explain the subtle distinctions between distros to someone who barely knows what Linux is will get you nowhere.

An analogy could be calling Macs, HPs, Dells, Gateways, Toshibas, Vaio's, etc. "Computers". In some circumstances, it may be useful to know a make and model, but in general you're not hurting anything by calling it a computer.
