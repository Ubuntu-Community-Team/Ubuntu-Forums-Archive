### Post by Kvark on 2005-07-18
The titles/ranks have been bothering me too.

The only truly meaningful titles are those tied to a specific role the person plays in something people heard about or a specific thing the person has done that many heard about.

People who can get titles like that are forum moderators, wiki moderators, op in irc channel #ubuntu or #ubunutforums, cononical staff, creators/maintainers of specific programs, celebrities from other distros and people who do projects like ubuntuguide and backports.



Which means most people are left to automatically generated standard titles. If the standard titles are ranks based on post count only. Then they are a straight one dimensional ranking ladder. The only meaning of such a rank is how high up the ladder it is. If you can't compare the rank to a random other rank and figure out which one is higher up, then the rank holds no meaning at all.

Perhapas there could be alternative ladders. If someone answers questions or writes howtos he/she could be moved to a helpful/tutor/mentor sounding ladder. Someone posting artwork could be moved to an artistic sounding ladder. Someone  writing for example auto configure scripts could be moved to a scripter/coder ladder.....
