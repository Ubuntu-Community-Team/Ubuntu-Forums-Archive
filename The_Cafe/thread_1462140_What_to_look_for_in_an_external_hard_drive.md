### Post by blueturtl on 2010-04-25
Do you have a preference in internal hard drives?

If you do, find out what that Buffalo or LaCie actually has inside it before buying. Might be a bit difficult though, unless the it's an actual hard disk manufacturer selling the thing like Seagate.

Do you have a preferred interface? Practically everything is USB today, but you might also have eSATA or FireWire to choose from. Both should be more trouble free than USB with higher transfer rates.

Watch the reviews for people's comments. For example my Seagate FreeAgent had a power saving feature which caused it to go offline (it wouldn't properly tell the OS it did this, and so would just spill read errors after falling asleep).

Also, if you want the drive to be secure there are some models that have built in RAID.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-04-25
Just to spell this out- there are only a few mechanical HDD manufacturers out there- WD, Hitachi, Seagate (also includes maxtor, sold 2006), Samsung, Fujitsu (sold to toshiba 2009). Every hard drive enclosure will have a drive made by one of these manufacturers.  

The quality of the enclosures vary, and sometimes just a tiny difference in model names can make a big difference, so be careful. 

Reviews can help a lot, but I'd trust a proper review on a hardware site far more than a 'review' you see on the product page on newegg, etc.
