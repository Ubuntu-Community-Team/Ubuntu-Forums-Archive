### Post by earthpigg on 2009-12-03
Here's an idea that just popped into my head...

currently, google search works via a popularity contest. except the voters are websites themselves, and more popular websites get more of a vote.

ie: wikipedia linking to your website will do a helluva lot more to bring your site to the top of a google search than some random website that no one links to.

this works ok, for the most part. 

the way digg.com works is that users submit links, and other users can give it a thumbs up or down, which determines how high it is on a given search result.

this works ok, for the most part.

what if we combined the two approaches? 

our new website, tentatively called diggoogle.com, has spiders that run around indexing websites and ranks them using [the same method ]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PageRank")google does.

from there, users giving thumbs up/down to specific links on the search results influences where a given result ranks.

users would have the option to tweak how much weight is put into the thumbs up/down via a simple slider.

user A wants user input to matter more, he can set his little slider to be 70%/30%.

user B does not care at all about user input, and just wants unmodified PageRank search results... his slider can be at 0%/100%.
