### Post by Paqman on 2012-02-01
> **Simian Man said:**
> It's great that we can run KDE and Unity on touch devices well, but it seems of limited use to me when Linux applications, by and large, wouldn't work well on these devices.  Running OpenOffice or Gimp on a touchscreen would be an exercise in vexation.

Most touch devices are going to be ARM, so the existing apps would have to (at a minimum) be recompiled for the new architecture. As you point out, there would be little point in simply porting the existing Ubuntu repos over to ARM as they'd be little use.

That's why I'm somewhat skeptical of Ubuntu trying to enter the tablet or smarphone market. It would divert vast amounts of developer and community effort to develop a competitive application ecosystem, and would face the battle of taking on massive entrenched juggernauts (Apple/Google/Amazon) with huge budgets, armies of manpower and impressive existing user bases. I don't really see how Ubuntu can match that, and we'd exhaust a lot of effort trying.
