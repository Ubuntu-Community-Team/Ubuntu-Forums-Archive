### Post by TheFu on 2016-03-27
That is why reading AND understanding what you are paying for is so very important.  Most "content" purchases aren't really purchases **like a book,** they are a license with restricted use and Amazon can remove access to that content whenever they like.  It isn't just on Kindle devices, but for all "content" purchased - even with physical media.

Do you think you own the DVD/Music CD/BluRay  content?  Nope. You own the media, but not the content on it. That is licensed with specific terms.  You cannot have "public displays" with the retail DVDs sold, for example.
