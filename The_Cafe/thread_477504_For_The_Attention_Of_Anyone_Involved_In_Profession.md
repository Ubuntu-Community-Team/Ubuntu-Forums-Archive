### Post by cunawarit on 2007-06-18
It really depends on the company that's doing the hiring. 

You won't be considered for a highly paid job in a hedge fund, going against red brick University graduates any time soon... But you still have a good chance on the long run. 

You CAN get a programming job, your first job won't be highly paid, but it will allow you to start building up a CV. If you can get a job with a company that provides plenty of training opportunities then all the better. After a few years, once you have gone a few steps up the ladder somewhere, have a few more qualifications, not having a degree won't matter anywhere near as much... Specially not when you have your own company :D

### Post by ComplexNumber on 2007-06-18
it will be just as effective as a degree for getting in to the job in the first place. of course, some companies can be a bit more snobbish than others, but they are very few, it seems. having a degree merely means that you get promoted faster.
in many cases, employers want someone who has lots of enthusiasm for programming, can fit in with the rest of the team, and can satisfy much/some of the criteria for the job...in that order of priority. the first 2 are interchangable. the last  one is of much lesser importance.
