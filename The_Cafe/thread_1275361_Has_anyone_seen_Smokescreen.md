### Post by MattBD on 2009-09-25
Has anyone seen this online game, [Smokescreen]("https://www.smokescreengame.com/")? I heard about it on BoingBoing, and I've quite enjoyed playing with it.

Essentially it uses browser-based mini-games to teach you about the potential risks of social networking websites and how to mitigate them. It's all based around various fictional sites and services, and also includes IM sessions and phone calls to make it seem that bit more interactive. It's pretty eye-opening.

In one of the missions, you have to help someone stalk a girl online so he can get the best line to chat her up, and it's downright scary how much information you can actually obtain about her likes and dislikes, where she's going and what she's doing etc. In another, one of the characters has bunked off college to go to a demonstration, and someone's posted a picture online so you need to help her disguise this so she doesn't get into trouble. So far there's 7 missions on there, with another 6 to come.

At times some of the names of the sites are a little cringe-worthy (MSG Chat? Fakebook? Gaggle? Tweetr?), but it's good fun. Although it's primarily aimed at teenagers, there's no reason why anyone else can't enjoy and learn from it, and it might help a few people behave a bit more cautiously online, which can only be a good thing. And being browser-based it runs OK on Linux!
