### Post by fwojciec on 2007-10-06
This is a tough one - all in no particular order though...

Solos (I'll go with "classical" choices here):
* Gilmour/Pink Floyd - Time - the whole song is just an excuse for the awesome, epic solo, IMO
* Page/Led Zeppelin - Since I've Been Loving You - my fingers always hurt when I try to bend strings like Jimmy does... hurt in a good way though ;)
* Hendrix - Come On (part I) - an unorthodox choice as far as Jimi songs are concerned but the whole song is just so good.  It may sound simple, but it's so hard to play with such a clean/exposed tone, and his sense of rhythm is just out of this world.
* Knopfler/Dire Straits - Brothers in Arms - just perfect in all possible ways...
* Fripp/King Crimson - Sailor's Tale - the whole song is an instrumental, but it kinda has a solo, and it is probably the most unique/original solo I know of.

Riffs (OK, trying to come up with some of the non-obvious ones):
* Jones/Tool - Rosetta Stoned - one basic idea permutated in so many different variations throughout the song.  I think Adam Jones comes up with some of the best riffs ever - he's not a technical genius but such a great, self-aware artist/composer! 
* Slash/Guns and Roses - Nightrain - don't laugh at me, hear me out, it true! (awesome solo in this song as well)
* Hetfield/Mustaine/Metallica - probably my favorite of the many great Metallica riffs.
* Bolan/T-Rex - Children of the Revolution - "I drive a Rolls Royce, cause it's good for my voice"
*  DeLeo/Stone Temple Pilots - Vasoline - simple means often produce great effects.

### Post by lespaul_rentals on 2007-10-08
Favorite Riffs:

1. Anthrax - "A.I.R."  The breakdown riff in this song is simply amazing.  I can't help but headbang when I hear it.

2. Eric Clapton - "Layla"  The lead riff in this song is great, it carries a lot of feeling, at least for me.

3. Megadeth - "Take No Prisoners"  The dynamics in this song are amazing.

4. Psycroptic - "Cruelty Incarnate"  This entire song is a riff-gasm.  There are so many changes and dynamics in this song but every single riff played is incredible.

Favorite Solos:

1. Children of Bodom - "Children of Bodom"  The neo-classical solo in this song simply blows me away.  Sure, it's hard to play.  Sure, it's technical.  Sure, it's fast.  I like it because it brings tears to my eyes just hearing it.  It's so emotional and beautiful while still having that metal feel.

2. Necrophagist - "Fermented Offal Discharge"  I know it's cliche to mention this song as Muhammed's finest work, but I must say that the solo in this song is perfect.  There are sweeps galore, scales played so fast they could shatter a metronome, and every single note played is perfect.  There aren't any forced phrasings or anything.  Plus, it's one of the most emotional fast solos I've ever heard.

3. Dream Theater - "Panic Attack"  Fast, heavy, mindblowing.  Hey, it's some of the finest musicians in the world rocking out together.
