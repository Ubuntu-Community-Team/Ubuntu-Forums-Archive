### Post by maniacmusician on 2007-06-06
What's your budget? Here's what I generally recommend (since I've used this hardware and trust it).

**Mobo:**
Two choices. High end: [Gigabyte GA-965P-DQ6]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128323") or, if you have more of a budget, Mid-end: [Gigabyte GA-965P-DS3]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128012"). Those boards are very similar...the high end one uses the ICH8R chipset whereas the mid end one has the ICH8 chipset. Incidentally, I own the mid end one, and it is pretty nice, but if I had the cash, I would have gone for the DQ6. Love that board.

**RAM:** 
Most RAM is pretty good, and it really just depends on your needs. Corsair is probably my favorite manufacturer, but their RAM is very pricey as well. After Corsair, I prefer OCZ, Kingston, G.Skill, Crucial ; in that order. As for speeds and whatnot, I would go for 2GB(1GBx2) at DDR2 800 for the sake of looking to the future; DDR2 667 is good as well if you want to save some money. 

You would probably be okay with 1GB of RAM, but I like having RAM available for various tasks. It's quite useful, for example, if you like running virtual machines. so 2GB is my recommendation.

**Processor:** 
Any Core 2 Duo processor with 4MB of L2 cache should do you fine. My personal recommendation is the E6600. It's in the middle of the spectrum as far as performance, and it's pretty much the best bang for your buck. If you choose another Core 2 Duo, Make sure it's Conroe based and not Allendale. But, I wouldn't stray outside of the Core 2 Duo line.  Fanboyism aside, they're really the best option right now.

**Hard Drive:**
Here are some basic rules for buying a hard drive; SATAII (aka SATA 3.0 Gb/s) interface, and buy from the following manufacturers: Western Digital, Seagate, and maybe Samsung (that's the order that I would prefer them in). 

I recommend getting a 320GB hard drive, as they actually seem to have the best storage and performance for the price you pay (you can get a good one for $90. If you want something bigger, look in the  500GB range. By the way, I recommend that you use newegg to research and look for products...you dont have to buy from there in the end, but it's very useful for looking at parts and whatnot. 

**Video Card:**
You can't really get intel cards for the desktop (at least I dont think so), so go with an nVidia card. I always look for the best price/performance first, and in this case, you find that with the nVidia GeForce 7600GT from EVGA. One of my favorites. If you want to spend a little extra, go for the 7900GT. If you want to spend even more, look at the GeForce 8 series for some serious performance...but you probably dont need this unless you're a hardcore gamer or 3D artist. 

That's all I can think of for now. Oh, and use this [PSU calculator]("http://www.extreme.outervision.com/psucalculator.jsp")(the Lite version is free, the Pro version costs money to use) for sizing up what kind of PSU you'll need. Usually, a good thing to do is take the value it gives you and add 50-100 watts to that for padding. And [here's a basic guide to putting together the various parts that you buy]("http://tools.corsairmemory.com/systembuild/report.aspx?report_id=12472"). Your experience will be a little different because you'll have different parts and a different case, but it's still useful.
