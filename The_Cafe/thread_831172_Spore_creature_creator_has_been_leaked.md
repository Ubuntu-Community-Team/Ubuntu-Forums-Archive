### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-06-16
Yep, I'm impressed. Seems that it doesn't exaggerate animations as much as in some tech demos, sadly. For example, there seems to be less wobbling when I shake the spine and less of that tipping over effect when I try to build a tweety bird creature. I must say, I miss that a little bit, but this is really impressive.

I also have noticed a distinct lack of animation blending which Will showed off when he first demoed Spore, which I dearly hope is not a lost feature since it was a very impressive technical feat. I can see why the creature editor would not have such a thing presented on its end, so I remain hopeful for that one. I love the way they've fleshed out the creature design gameplay with special powers and the like. Really cool :)

Particularly interesting is that you can actually block a creature's own legs with other poorly positioned limbs, leading to slower walking. Very interesting...
Suffice it to say, I have built all manner of abominations.

A shame that Wine doesn't run it immediately, but it's great that the Wine developers will have so much time now to get it (and thus Spore itself) working.

### Post by nicesocks on 2008-06-18
lies!

you just have to intuit where the ui is supposed to be.  when you get to the spiraling galaxy (the main login screen) you will notice that upon mouse over -barely to the right of the center of those spiraling arms- you will hear a different sound.  click that spot and it will load up the creature editor.  the little blob will float there and you can make vain attempts to click where different parts are, hover over them, grab them and go all mr. potato head on the blob.


however, it likes to render "behind" images "infront."  so trying to make a creature can be done, it just won't render correctly at all.  for starters, the grass blades render floating in the foreground.  eyes that should be on the far side of the head will appear, but the close eye won't.  the inside of the mouth will show, the outside won't.  so yeah, it works, and from spinning the camera around you can get a sense of what your abomination looks like, but don't hold out for awesomeness.

and don't even think about using the painting tool...  that's impossible without the ui, and just guessing can get you rather lost.

i've even managed to make a baby appear in the walk-around feature.  must've clicked on the egg icon.  that was by randomly clicking the bottom of the screen to test emotions and actions that wouldn't look right anyway due to the improper graphics.

so yeah, it works.  but it works like it's broken.  ~_^
here's hoping someone smarter than i works that out.
but hey, now the WINE background people have two months to get this going, so that spore will be a "platinum" or "gold" for them.  'cause this game will be hot.
