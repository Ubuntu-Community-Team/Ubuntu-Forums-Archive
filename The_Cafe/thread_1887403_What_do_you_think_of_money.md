Money was created as a place holder of an actual item, like gold or another highly regarded item. Paper money was originally issued as basically a promissory note of ownership. Promissory notes gained popularity in trading environments, like Stock Exchanges, at the end of the day the traders would then cash in the notes for the actual items. Governments print a promise that the note is worth something. Its only up to the government's populace to accept it as currency. Money is needed in a industrial world. It would be hard to buy a car by trading a few goats, or a new PC for a few sacks of corn. Its easier to trade a universal currency that can be used to buy anything instead of finding someone to accept your trade.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-11-27
> **brawnypandora0 said:**
> Why doesn't the government simply print more bills and distribute this to the population? Then would everyone in the world be richer?

No, everyone in that country would be poorer. Currency has the value it does because of its rarity. If you print more currency, you devalue every single piece of that currency ('virtual', i.e. "in the bank" or real).

In other words, if they print too much money, then it's worth less. Eventually, as what happened in pre-Nazi Germany, your entire life savings become only worth enough to buy a cup of coffee.
