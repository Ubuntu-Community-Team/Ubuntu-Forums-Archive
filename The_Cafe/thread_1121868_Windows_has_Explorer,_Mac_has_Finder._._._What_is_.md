### Post by Pasdar on 2009-04-11
I remember installing Mandrake many years ago and during installation it gave me the option to install some eight shells. It's not a problem to me but for 99% of world they will be confused.

The first step in becoming a success as an OS is to apply a certain standard that you keep, so that everyone recognizes a set of programs to be part of your OS. Even though "free choices" sounds nice, it will only confuse them. What you want is to limit their choice to the maximum during install, and then if they figure out how to change stuff, have to choice to change to anything they wish. This is what Ubuntu is doing, and with success. I had no choice to do anything during install, except for partition and time/date/place.
