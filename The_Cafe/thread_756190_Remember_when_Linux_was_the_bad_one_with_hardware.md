### Post by VChief on 2008-04-15
When I first started using Linux back in 1999, hardware could be an issue. Playing with X was fun and it took me about a few months to get a modem to actually work (looking back, I'm still not sure why I didn't just go by a non-win modem...think I was looking for that sense of accomplishment...I knew it could be done and I was determined to do it). Windows was still the "Plug n Play" OS and I remember reading about how one of Linux's greatest weaknesses was that it was a pain in the neck to make most hardware work. Seems to be the opposite now.

I still have XP for the sole purpose of Flight Sim, and I only boot XP to play about once every couple months...I hate seeing single digits on 'uptime,' not to mention shutting down for no good reason. So, recently, I got a few new toys. I got a better graphics card than the one I had, I got a wireless mouse and I got a new monitor. My new monitor was the first. It had DVI, so I decided to use that. Plugged it in...what do you know? Linux immediately recognized it, I set up my resolution and I was good to go. Windows? Not so much. That was a pain in the neck. It didn't like my DVI. I installed the monitor's drivers. Reinstalled them. Reinstalled the graphics card drivers. Finally, after a few reinstalls and countless reboots, XP finally decided to play nice. My mouse. It took about 5 minutes for XP to realize there even was a wireless mouse. Then I had to reboot. With Linux, I just unplugged my old mouse, and plugged in the receiver. Took 3 seconds. My graphics card. That took the most work with Linux.  I had to uninstall the nVidia drivers. Then install them again. Windows. Another torrent of reinstalls and reboots. 

Linux even recognizes my joystick without a problem and any other hardware I throw at it. Windows seems to take a while to get around to seeing that I have something new then figuring out what it is then telling me to reboot. My joystick was particularly fun, though not as much as the monitor.

Anyway, just thought I'd share. Maybe even see if anyone else has any similar stories.
