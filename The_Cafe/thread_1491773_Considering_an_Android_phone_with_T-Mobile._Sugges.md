### Post by aysiu on 2010-05-24
I know you say you don't like the touchscreen keyboard, but it's actually quite nice the way it's set up in Android. You start typing a few letters of the word you want to type and it'll list several possible words that could complete what you're typing. You click on the appropriate word, and it fills in the rest (including the space before the next word). So you do have to be a bit extra careful on the first two or three letters, but you can get a message out pretty quickly without typing out the whole thing.

I have the MyTouch 3G with T-Mobile, the original one (not the Fender, Slide, or 3.5), and it's pretty good, except for the 192 MB of RAM (a pittance compared to what's out now). Given that Nexus One was the first to get the Froyo updates, I would think going with Nexus One would be a safe bet, since it is the official Google phone.

Even though I like T-Mobile (both the customer service and the affordable plans), it's coverage is just not as robust as AT&T and Verizon. You might want to consider getting a Droid from Verizon... the Incredible, perhaps?
