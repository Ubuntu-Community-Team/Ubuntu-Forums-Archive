### Post by Shakz on 2010-05-12
Howdy! 
I must have read "ugly brown" on these forums a 1000 times since I started using ubuntu. 

I like it though! Perhaps it was the way I was introduced to Ubuntu. 
My sister-n-law got her laptop ate up with viruses a week after I had just reloaded it for her. She called me and when I said it would be a couple days till i could clear it out again she said she was getting a mac. 

I was shocked as she is a broke college student but hey whatever floats your boat. I asked her why a mac...she said she cant handle all the viruses and she needs a reliable laptop for school. 

I thought "man it sure would be nice to be immune to windows based viruses myself." Granted I have never got a virus but thats only because I am VERY cautious. I used to spend a lot of time on the dark side of the net (not porn....stealing) so I learned very quickly to be careful of what you download and install.  

Anywho...I was at work when the thought occured so I said what the hell and did a search on "Fedora Vs" to see what other distros were like fedora as its the only one I have ever used in the past...although it proved to be a pain in the *** every time. 

Fedora vs Ubuntu was the first one to come up so I went to the ubuntu website and read this. 
[http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory](http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory)
I read a part about  the name...what it meant and why Ubuntu was named so. 

Went home burned a disk...fired it up....
"Holy crap! Brown....why?" Then I thought....hummm Africa...the savannah....Giraffes, Lions, Tribes.....International Scouts....deserts.

The color scheme seemed fitting...after that I loved it. It felt....natural. More organic than other colors. In a time when everything is made out of or to look like plastic...even our flippen OSs...it was refreshing. When did plastic start looking cool to people?

I already know a lot of folks hate this color scheme, I have read it here, on reviews, vlogs, blogs, you name it! 
I would like to hear (read) why other folks like it/miss it. I know I am not alone. 

I miss the human themes....I think I am going to put them back on Lucid tonight. They can keep the black and purple.
