### Post by irrdev on 2010-10-11
If you are going to use Google Bookmarks, understand that the "Google Bookmarks" service is entirely different than the "Bookmarks Sync" provided with Chrome. In fact, "Google Bookmarks" is basically dead from what I understand. If you sync any bookmarks within Chrome, the bookmarks will actually show up in Google Docs as part of a read-only "Google Chrome" folder. You cannot edit them or rearrange them online ATM, so this feature definitely needs more work. On the upside, though, Chrome is excellent at instantaneous synchronization, and also syncs all my extensions and preferences. In fact, I switched over to Chrome's Bookmark Sync even before Xmarks announced their demise. It's definitely worth checking out if all of your browsers are Chrome.
