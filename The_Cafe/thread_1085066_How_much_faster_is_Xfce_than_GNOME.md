I'm only personally familiar with one E17 distro-OpenGEU, E17 with a mix of Gnome/xfce elements that is Ubuntu based.  It looked great-and ran quite well.

[http://opengeu.intilinux.com/OpenGEU/Home.html](http://opengeu.intilinux.com/OpenGEU/Home.html)

I used it for several months and was happy with it....until Intrepid Ibex came along-and it took until sometime in the last month for Ibex packages to get out (That is 5 out of 6 months since Ibex came out and 1 month until Jaunty comes out)--I got sick of waiting for packages, and b0rked packages, and I got back to KDE where I've been since (Now KDEmod under Arch).  

The rain on the parade for e17 really is the lack of dev/packaging activity that goes on-due to the tiny amount of manpower put into e17 distros.  There are only a handful of people that dev e17 distros, that do put in a great deal of personal time--but it is miniscule ot the resources of Gnome/KDE/etc.  OpenGEU probably has the largest crew of all the e17 distros.  E17 is a great environment-capable of things KDE4 and Gnome have yet to do....it is a true shame it doesn't get more dev action than it does.
