### Post by Inodoro Pereyra on 2011-08-23
What I like about Ubuntu is...well...about everything.
I've been a reluctant windows user for a looonngg time (since 3.1), and pretty much hated every minute of it. 

The first thing that caught my eye (besides the boot up speed) was the  cleanliness and general organization of the desktop. I love not having  icons all over the place, as well as having all my apps easily  categorized in the drop down menus, instead of having everything bunched  up together in an endless "programs" list.
Then, I found the community. I have participated on dozens of Internet  forums, on several different disciplines, from general science to  homebrewing, and, so far, the only forum that, IMO, can hold a candle to  this one, is mx-3.com. But the sheer size of this forum alone put it on  a whole different level, as any time you can ask a question, and  literally expect to have an answer within minutes, which seldom happens  anywhere else.

Now, I'm slowly starting to learn a bit more. Yesterday I found a link  about scripting on another thread, and spent most of the day reading it.  It almost brings me back to my TI99 days...

Finally, now I'm exclusively running Ubuntu, as my main hard drive  cr@pped out on me, but I usually have to use windows, as I need  Solidworks, which brings me to the 1 thing I hate about Ubuntu: its  spotty compatibility with windows programs. If I could run Solidworks on  Ubuntu, I wouldn't touch windows with a 10 ft. stick.

### Post by ninjaaron on 2011-08-23
I haven't used windows for a few years now, so I don't have as much basis for comparison as I might anymore, though I did play with Windows 7 for a couple of weeks when I bought my new Laptop. I liked it, overall, but the updates were such a pain in the ***, trying to shut you down every five minutes and then taking several minutes to turn off and reboot after the update.  That was super annoying, but the the last straw was the ad-ware that wouldn't go away no matter what I tried.  Other than that, the system seemed pretty good.  It was fast and stable.

I'm just more used to Ubuntu now, and it can do certain things that Windows can't, and I like doing those things. Same goes for OSX.  It's a wonderful system, but I'm used to Linux now and it's particular strengths.  I'm also used to the particular weaknesses of Linux now, so they don't really bother me now either.  I'm like a Windows user.  I just keep using what I know :p

I guess the thing I really like about Ubuntu is the way it shines on low performance hardware.  Since I started using it, I just buy the cheapest computers I can (usually with a RAM upgrade).  Ubuntu always works great on these, and there tends to be minimal bloat from release to release (in comparison with Windows and OSX), so upgrades almost never cause a lapse in performance, and many times they offer improvements, especially when they hit a breakthrough on the kernel.  My friend just "upgraded" to Lion on a PowerBook that is several years old and it's caused some real regressions in performance.

I showed him how I had Arch booting in 12 seconds on my atom netbook. He was just kinda in shock.  Now I've installed Ubuntu on his roommates old Windows Laptop, and it's faster than his PowerBook.  This hasn't really been a good month for him.
