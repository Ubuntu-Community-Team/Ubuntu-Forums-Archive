### Post by ashickur.noor on 2011-09-13
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]Open  Source movement is a social movement to ensure the rights of the  computer users. To fulfill this objective Free Software Movement  encourages to develop or create free and open source software, its uses  and to improve the quality of them. On [/FONT][/COLOR]September 27, 1983,[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial] Richard Stallman [/FONT][/COLOR]announced[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial] the GNU project to achieve this free  software movement philosophy.  For that&#8217;s why he is known as the founder  of this movement. To make this movement speedy from 2004 every third  Saturday of September is celebrated as the &#8220;Software Freedom Day&#8221;. In  2011 it is 17 of September. For more details please visit -  [http://wiki.softwarefreedomday.org](http://wiki.softwarefreedomday.org)[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]To know who are celebrating this day all around the globe please visit -- [http://wiki.softwarefreedomday.org/CategoryTeam2011](http://wiki.softwarefreedomday.org/CategoryTeam2011).[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]&#8220;Foundation  for Open Source Solutions Bangladesh (FOSS Bangladesh)&#8221; will also  celebrate this day as the "&#2488;&#2475;&#2463;&#2451;&#2527;&#2509;&#2479;&#2494;&#2480; &#2478;&#2497;&#2453;&#2509;&#2468;&#2495; &#2470;&#2495;&#2476;&#2488; - &#2536;&#2534;&#2535;&#2535;" for all the  Open Source and free software lovers in Bangladesh. The program will be  organized at Presidency University at their Banani Campus.[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**_Program Details: (8 hour 30 minute, Its a day long event at will be Open for all):_**[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**1. **[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]At  10:00 am in the morning the event will be opened for all (Students and  Enthusiasts from all over Bangladesh may join us). Some of the Hosts and  the invited Guests will give their speech to the participants.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**2. **[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]From  10:30 to till end their will be banners and festoons showing the  history and moments, developments information etc. of various Free and  Open Source Software and Linux.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**3. **[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]Their will be video shows representing Software Freedom and the FSF movements and many more.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**4. **[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]Various Linux distro will be available for all (in ISO or CD/DVD format).[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**_Special Attraction:_**[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]For the first time in Bangladesh there will be a &#8220;[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]**Linux Installation Booth&#8221; **[/FONT][/COLOR][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]in  the event. Where our volunteer will install the Linux distro&#8217;s and do  the necessary tweaks after installation among the participant as they  prefer.  (Those who register in Online will get the priority most.)[/FONT][/COLOR]

### Post by ashickur.noor on 2011-09-18
[SIZE=3][COLOR=Navy] Event updates
[COLOR=Black][SIZE=1]
[/SIZE][/COLOR][/COLOR][/SIZE]As scheduled the event started at 10:00 in the morning with the National  Anthym of Bangladesh and the Free Software Song. After that the video  speech by Richard Stallman for Software Freedom Day (dubbed into Bengali  by FOSS Bangladesh) was played. After that the main show began. There  was a whole day seminar and sessions for the participants on the event  such as -- 


[LIST]
[*]@ 10:30 -- 11:15 Installing GNU/Linux based OS and configure it for desktop use was presented by MD. Ashickur Rahman Noor 
[*]@ 11:15 -- 11:50 Configuring various type of internet connections and usb modems was presented by Shazedur Rahim Joardar (ring) 
[*]@ 11:50 -- 12:50 Using GIMP for image editing in a professional way by Shohanur Rahim Joardar 
[*]@ 12:50 -- 14:00 Lunch break. 
[*]@  14:00 -- 15:30 Use of Libre Office and Libre CAD in profeessional needs  was presented by Dr. Miah Mohammed Husainuzzaman Shamim 
[*]@ 15:30 -- 16:30 Using GNU/Linux and Free softwares in daily life. 
[*]@ 16:30 -- 18:00 Questions and answering session for the participants on the event.
[/LIST]

For the first time in Bangladesh, in such an event we  have an arrangement for the participants as "Linux and FOSS  installation and support booth" where the participants installed  GNU/Linux based OS and Free Softwares to their laptop/netbook as their  own choice by our volunteers. The whole event area was covered by 3MBps  bandwidth under a wireless (Wi-Fi) network. There was a CD/DVD/ISO  distribution booth too. From where the participants had collected their  choosen GNU/Linux distros. 
To view the photos of this event [click here]("http://picasaweb.google.com/toshazed/cgBlXE?authuser=0&authkey=Gv1sRgCNWBxsqhtb7IGQ&feat=directlink").
