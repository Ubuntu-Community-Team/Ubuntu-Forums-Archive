### Post by poofyhairguy on 2005-07-16
My favorites:

Some gdesktlets (I don't like what seems to be the most popular though- the one that is a rip off of the OSX dock).

Luminocity is fun but useless

xcompmgr is the best. I bought an Nvidia card just to get the full effect. drop shadows are fun and fading is very pretty.

Too bad the last one is a little buggy in Gnome because the Metacity composition manager fights with xcompmgr. I have tried to gut metacity's compositing manager, but its hard to do. When I try to get some advice from the gnome wizards, the won't help because "why do you need xcompmgr when Metacity has a compositing manager? In the future Metacity will be able to do most transparancies and more."

Of course the answer is that:

1. Metacity's is boring right now, I can't wait.

2. Even if I could wait....it might be a while. It seems to take longer for stuff like that to get into Gnome (look at how nice KDE's compositing manager is). Might be gnome 2.14 or whatever before Gnome gets fun.

3.  It might never do fading, only transparancies (I care little about those alone).

4. Metacity in my opinion is the worst program in the Gnome desktop environment, so I want to depend on features coming from it 0%.

5. Metacity might just wait for glx-or whatever the new accerated x manager is- so more people can use it (just buy an Nvidia card TODAY, easy and cheap I say) and then it won't be two or three years before I get eye candy.

I'm a brat when it comes to eye candy.
