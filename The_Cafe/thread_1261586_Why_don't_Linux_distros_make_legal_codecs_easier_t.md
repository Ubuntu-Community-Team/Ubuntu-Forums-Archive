I took the idea from the following Blog:
[http://blogs.zdnet.com/hardware/?p=665](http://blogs.zdnet.com/hardware/?p=665)

I now that many of you will say: Why do you care?

Well, I don't care about the legality really, but I believe that the owners of those patents made a choice, they decide not to license their invents as free softwares. I disagree with their decision, but at the end is their problem.
 

 Is my believe that go against the will of an owner over his property is wrong. No matter how rich a person is, steal from a person is always wrong.
 

 If I'm not happy with the abusive terms of a company, I just stop using their product and start looking for other alternatives. That was one the reason I started using Linux in the first place.
 

 All what I want to ask to the Ubuntu's developers is if they could extent the explanation in the message box that says:
 

 [B]Restricted Software
[/B]The use of some of the software may be restricted in some countries. You must verify that one of the following is true:
1. These restrictions do not apply in your country of legal residence.
2. You have permission to use the software (for example, a patent license).
3. You are using this software for research purposes only.
 

 If I wouldn't have looked for more information, I would have been totally lost and disconcerted with  meaning of this message. It wasn't easy for me to find further information about the matter in the Internet. If Ubuntu's developers could add a link to further information, that will be great.
 

 Some information they could include is:
 
[LIST]

[*]How can I use codecs, where     royalty rights are paid. Like the cases of Fluendo or Quick Time for     Linux.
[*]What does it mean by researching     purposes.
[/LIST]
