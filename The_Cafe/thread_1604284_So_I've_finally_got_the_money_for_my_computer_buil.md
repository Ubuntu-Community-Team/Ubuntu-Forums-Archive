### Post by nirvana21 on 2010-10-23
> **Dustin2128 said:**
> 
PSU: 500W minimum, shooting for 20-30$ range.


I would suggest getting a decent power supply. Besides the motherboard, it is perhaps the most important part. If you get a poor power supply, your components won't last as long, it will be less efficient$, it will provide less stable overclocks, and even more.

Don't worry about the wattage as much as the rails and how many amps are distributed on each rail. Good brands are Seasonic(most good units are re-branded Seasonics), Silverstone, Corsair, Antec (Earthwatts series), and PC Power and Cooling.

Expect to spent more like $40-$50 for a decent unit.


Since you are going AMD, make sure you get an 8xx series chipset so that you have USB 3.0 and SATA 6 capability. If you want SLI capability on the other hand, you are going to want an Nvidia chipset which might not include USB 3.0. I would suggest getting one good graphics card. I personally have crossfire right now and it is good, but I should have got one good card.

> **Dustin2128 said:**
> 
RAM: I'd like 4GB, anything more would be epic. no more than 100$ preferably.


You can get good 2GB x 2 sticks for around $70 if you look for sales.


Lastly, if you really want to water cool this system, the case layout is very important. You will want a hearty power supply for the overclock and pump.

Building a computer is lots of fun. Enjoy!
