Background:

I have proposed Edubuntu to a local school and have been asked to provide an overview written presentation. (I run a small IT company, my children also go to the school, and my wife is on the PTA).

My objective is to provide enough information for the staff to request a full proposal, or at least a demonstration. The target audience then, is educated but not technical, teachers and potentially governors. I want to keep it simple, as the majority will be low-level Windows users.

I would really appreciate some feedback on what I have so far, please. Any constructive comments at all please (even spelling or grammar mistakes :) )

Obviously, lots of this is ripped from websites - all references are given at the end.

Very sorry if this makes for a long post. Thank you for taking the time to read it.

Presentation:

Overview of Edubuntu &#8211; a FREE operating system for schools


About Edubuntu

Edubuntu is a free Linux-based operating system, designed specifically for use in education environments. It provides a centralised management of configuration, users and processes, complete with collaborative working facilities. The Edubuntu package comes complete with some of the very best free software and digital materials for education.

The principle of Edubuntu implementation is that an educator can setup a computer lab, or establish an online learning environment in around one hour! Then administer that environment without having to become a computer geek.

Edubuntu has high quality support provided by an active community of enthusiasts and professionals.


The Edubuntu Manifesto 

The team behind Edubuntu makes the following public commitment to its users:

&#8226;	Edubuntu will always be free of charge, and there is no extra fee for the "enterprise edition", we make our very best work available to everyone on the same free terms. 
&#8226;	Edubuntu includes the very best in translations and accessibility infrastructure that the Free Software community has to offer, to make Edubuntu usable by as many people as possible.
&#8226;	Edubuntu is released regularly and predictably; a new release is made every six months. You can use the current stable release or the current development release. Each release is supported with security updates for at least 18 months.
&#8226;	Edubuntu is entirely committed to the principles of free and open source software development; we encourage people to use free and open source software, improve it and pass it on.

 
About Ubuntu

Edubuntu is Ubuntu, customised for classroom use. Ubuntu is currently the most popular Linux distribution. Ubuntu its creators, developers and users have won multiple awards in the industry for Best Distribution, Best Community, Outstanding Contribution to Linux, Linux Journal&#8217;s Reader&#8217;s Choice, and many more.

"Ubuntu" is an ancient African word, meaning "humanity to others". Ubuntu also means, "I am what I am because of who we all are". The Ubuntu Linux distribution brings the spirit of Ubuntu to the software world.


User

The Edubuntu desktop provides a child friendly (but easily customised) intuitive environment, which is similar enough to other major operating systems (in particular Windows) that it takes just a moment for most users to orientate themselves and begin working.

Edubuntu comes with many hundreds of pre-installed applications to enable almost all users to perform whatever task they please immediately. The Edubuntu repository (a massive store of trusted free software) also contains over sixteen thousand further free packages, which can be installed easily and quickly if desired.

The pre-installed software includes a full office suite that is fully compatible with Microsoft Office, so enabling use of pre-existing files and exchange of files with Windows users, with no compatibility issues to worry about. Also included are many educational games, graphics design software, desktop publishing applications, course-planning software, SchoolTool (for course planning, student management, calendar and timetabling, etc.) and hundreds more.

The installed educational software includes games to practice maths, spelling, reading and advances through to include typing, chemistry, languages, geography, etc. even astronomy, and computer programming!


Administrator

Edubuntu has been designed throughout to ensure it can be administered by teachers, rather than requiring specific IT personnel. This means that the skill set required is no more than that of an interested, informed and competent teacher.

The Ubuntu (and related) websites provide comprehensive documentation regarding all aspects of installing, administering, maintaining and using Edubuntu (and other products in the Ubuntu range).

The vast and active Ubuntu community provides rapid and effective free support through the forums; however, professional support is also available from many companies, in particular Canonical, a company created specifically for providing professional support for Ubuntu, by Ubuntu&#8217;s creator.

By using Edubuntu&#8217;s terminal server system to create a network, only the server for each group needs software maintenance. This means only one computer to apply security patches, only one PC to update virus software, only one PC to reconfigure when anything changes (e.g. policies, colour schemes, pupil names, teachers, the theme of the week, or term, etc.)


Security

Linux is widely regarded as the one of the most secure operating systems available today.

Some simple facts demonstrate this:
&#8226;	Number of known viruses for Windows = 60,000 (approx)
&#8226;	Number of known viruses for Linux = 40 &#8211; yes FORTY! (Approx) and most of these are confined to research laboratories!
&#8226;	The German military have banned the use of Microsoft products due to concern over &#8220;back-doors&#8221; built-in to the operating system.
&#8226;	The Chinese government use Linux as their official operating system, due to similar security fears.
&#8226;	The USA&#8217;s National Security Agency (NSA) developed Secure Linux for its own use.
&#8226;	All open-source software (where the developers publish the code) is under constant scrutiny from peer review, something that can never happen with closed-source applications.
&#8226;	To install applications using Linux requires an administrator password, so a pupil cannot bring in a disc from home and break the system, similarly a virus infection cannot actually do anything without admin privileges.


Hardware

Edubuntu and Linux in general are very effective at using resources efficiently. When compared with the more popular operating systems, Linux requires lower specifications to perform the same task, uses them more economically and can be customised more easily to produce even sleeker operation.

However, where Edubuntu really excels is the built-in terminal server implementation. This enables just one powerful machine (the server) to provide a pre-processed feed to many &#8220;thin-clients&#8221; (low powered computers, often recycled, obsolescent machines, with no hard drive). The powerful machine does all the work for the thin-client, which just has to render the graphics on the screen and feedback the mouse and keyboard operations to the server.

This enables users to continue using (and often take out of retirement) older computers which were considered useless.

Side Note:
It may be possible to &#8220;cluster&#8221; a couple or a few mid powered machines using free software to create one &#8220;super-computer&#8221;. This groups computers and shares resources as though they were all in one enclosure.
For example, four single processor computers with 512Mb RAM each, would &#8220;cluster&#8221; to produce, in effect, a four-processor server with 2 GB RAM.

When upgrading the cost is minimal too, any upgrade to the server has an immediate impact on the performance of all the client computers!


Teaching

The effect of implementing Edubuntu is to allow teachers to take control of their computers, their software and thus their classrooms, rather than relying on some, often-absent, IT specialist. They will better understand the process of deploying the equipment and software. They will have masses of fantastic, pre-installed, educational software at hand, all free; and they can get on with the job of teaching children, rather than fighting with their computers.


Cost

The cost is one truly astounding part of Edubuntu - it is free!

Edubuntu comes with hundreds of free applications!

Edubuntu provides access to hundreds more educational packages free! These alone would cost thousands of pounds when purchasing equivalent software for proprietary operating systems.

When using the forums, support is also free!

By recycling old computers to create a terminal server network, most of the hardware is free!

If required, to serve the network, the purchase cost of just one high-powered server is far less than many single mid-powered machines, one for each student or classroom.

By using Edubuntu&#8217;s terminal server system to create a network, only the server for each group needs software maintenance. This means only one computer to apply security patches, only one PC to update virus software, only one PC to reconfigure when anything changes (e.g. policies, colour schemes, pupil names, teachers, the theme of the week, or term, etc.)


Security

Linux is widely regarded as the one of the most secure operating systems available today.

Some simple facts demonstrate this:
&#8226;	Number of known viruses for Windows = 60,000 (approx)
&#8226;	Number of known viruses for Linux = 40 &#8211; yes FORTY! (Approx) and most of these are confined to research laboratories!
&#8226;	The German military have banned the use of Microsoft products due to concern over &#8220;back-doors&#8221; built-in to the operating system.
&#8226;	The Chinese government use Linux as their official operating system, due to similar security fears.
&#8226;	The USA&#8217;s National Security Agency (NSA) developed Secure Linux for its own use.
&#8226;	All open-source software (where the developers publish the code) is under constant scrutiny from peer review, something that can never happen with closed-source applications.
&#8226;	To install applications using Linux requires an administrator password, so a pupil cannot bring in a disc from home and break the system, similarly a virus infection cannot actually do anything without admin privileges.


Hardware

Edubuntu and Linux in general are very effective at using resources efficiently. When compared with the more popular operating systems, Linux requires lower specifications to perform the same task, uses them more economically and can be customised more easily to produce even sleeker operation.

However, where Edubuntu really excels is the built-in terminal server implementation. This enables just one powerful machine (the server) to provide a pre-processed feed to many &#8220;thin-clients&#8221; (low powered computers, often recycled, obsolescent machines, with no hard drive). The powerful machine does all the work for the thin-client, which just has to render the graphics on the screen and feedback the mouse and keyboard operations to the server.

This enables users to continue using (and often take out of retirement) older computers which were considered useless.

Side Note:
It may be possible to &#8220;cluster&#8221; a couple or a few mid powered machines using free software to create one &#8220;super-computer&#8221;. This groups computers and shares resources as though they were all in one enclosure.
For example, four single processor computers with 512Mb RAM each, would &#8220;cluster&#8221; to produce, in effect, a four-processor server with 2 GB RAM.

When upgrading the cost is minimal too, any upgrade to the server has an immediate impact on the performance of all the client computers!


Teaching

The effect of implementing Edubuntu is to allow teachers to take control of their computers, their software and thus their classrooms, rather than relying on some, often-absent, IT specialist. They will better understand the process of deploying the equipment and software. They will have masses of fantastic, pre-installed, educational software at hand, all free; and they can get on with the job of teaching children, rather than fighting with their computers.


Cost

The cost is one truly astounding part of Edubuntu - it is free!

Edubuntu comes with hundreds of free applications!

Edubuntu provides access to hundreds more educational packages free! These alone would cost thousands of pounds when purchasing equivalent software for proprietary operating systems.

When using the forums, support is also free!

By recycling old computers to create a terminal server network, most of the hardware is free!

If required, to serve the network, the purchase cost of just one high-powered server is far less than many single mid-powered machines, one for each student or classroom.

### Post by BlackZen on 2008-02-01
I hope it won't be perceived as bad netiquette to resurrect a long dead post.... BUT...

I received an email this week asking about how my proposal went, from a reader planning to propose Edubuntu to a Local Education Authority.

My reply to them is below, just in case it is of interest to anybody else:

The school chose not to go with Edubuntu. The best explanation they offered was that their technical support person only worked with Windows machines!

A pretty pathetic explanation in my opinion.

We have actually chosen to remove our children from the school, for unrelated reasons, and so do not have the desire to push the matter further.

However, it may benefit you to know how we would proceed and how we would have done things differently, if we were to do it all again.

How we would have done it differently:
[INDENT]
&#8226;	Approach the Ubuntu community MUCH earlier! They came up with some great ideas and useful resources, some of which we didn&#8217;t know existed. Read the whole thread for more information. 
&#8226;	Only make your presentation face-to-face (we had to do it on paper), and take a working Edubuntu PC with you. We would have taken a server and small terminal network, but they wouldn&#8217;t accept that. 
&#8226;	Make certain key people will be at the presentation. We suggest for a school: head-teacher, IT teacher (if there is one), ALL IT support staff, some parents and pupils, possibly even governors, etc. This may be different for an LEA, but not too much, we think. Try to have enough equipment for them ALL to get hands-on! 
&#8226;	We are used to dealing with industry managers. They are pretty predictable types, in that, they can usually be persuaded by large money or time savings. In education, we found that this did not sway people the way we expected. You will likely encounter staff who have been co-opted into roles for which they have little or no aptitude or interest. You will find &#8220;jobsworths&#8221; who simply object to change, as they believe this will mean more work for them. You will find people who are actually afraid of anything new! You will find people who just want to teach (admirable, if narrow-minded), and will object to anything that takes them away from that. Possibly worst of all, there are people in charge of decision-making for IT who have absolutely NO IDEA what they are talking about! Be prepared for all of this and try to anticipate the objections they will raise. 
[/INDENT]

How we would proceed if we so wished:
[INDENT]
&#8226;	Arrange a private meeting with the IT support person (who in our case killed the whole idea). In this meeting, we could explain and demonstrate how easy it would be to administer the system and save money compared to proprietary systems. We would do this in private so we didn&#8217;t embarrass them, in particular the fact they had huge holes in their knowledge and were totally unwilling to learn! 
&#8226;	Arrange a demonstration to staff, parents, pupils and governors. Again, this may be different for an LEA. 
&#8226;	Ask very directly, what alternatives they had to the enormous money savings we were offering. 
&#8226;	Involve the local Ubuntu &#8220;LoCo&#8221; team (Local Community team, if you have one), or even the nearest LUG (Linux User Group) to support your cause! 
&#8226;	Petition the parents and governors individually and directly, not via the school. 

[/INDENT]
We truly believe in Edubuntu, we use it at home. We believe that our case was clear and honest.

However, it was stopped dead in its tracks by the fact that the school relied on ONE individual to provide ALL of their IT support and that person was afraid to try something different, or even to consider it.

That is truly a shame!

If we can provide any further information, please do not hesitate to ask. We would also love to hear how your presentation turns out! It would be great if you could add it to the thread on Ubuntu Forums, or start your own thread (please, send us a link if you do, so we can follow your progress).

Best Regards and Good Luck


BlackZen
