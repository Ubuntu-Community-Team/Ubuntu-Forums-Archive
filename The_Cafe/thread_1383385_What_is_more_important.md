### Post by humphreybc on 2010-01-17
O.o guess I'm going against the trend.

In my opinion, for the foreseeable future (next 5 years or so) I think that until Linux becomes more mainstream, there will never be equivalent apps to that on Windows.

Using Adobe as an example:

It would require a lot less effort, time, and money to port across something like the Adobe Creative Suite, than for a heap of volunteers to create something just as good.

Besides, big companies like Adobe will realize that money can be made off Linux when it becomes more mainstream. They will port across their software before anyone else releases an equivalent package for free - and when they do, the work on the free software will die.

For professional, quality software, Open Source will never be able to compete with the giants who have funding behind them, and industry support. They're already established and I find it very difficult to imagine a free alternative to compete with the Creative Suite - as awesome as it would be, it's not going to happen.
