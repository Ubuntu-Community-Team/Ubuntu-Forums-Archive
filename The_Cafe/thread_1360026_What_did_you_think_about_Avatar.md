### Post by NCLI on 2009-12-20
SO, as the other thread is completely ridiculous and biased, I'm starting a new one.
**_[COLOR="Red"]SPOILERS[/COLOR]_**
You've probably noticed that the first option sticks out(In more than one way), and that's because that was my experience. No, the plot wasn't new, so what? It was told better than I've ever seen that type of plot told before, the characters and world were believable, partially thanks to the 3D, but also thanks to great writing and characterization. 
The entire plot hanged on the premise that you accept Pandora as a living thing, kind of a gigantic brain, but that's really not too far fetched the way it's presented. If you accepted that, everything in the movie made sense.

The camerawork was great, and some scenes were so beautiful I got a lump in my throat. 

All in all, I loved it, 10/10.

Anyway, some of the criticism I've heard:

**Bah, it's just a load of environmentalist bullsh*t, mixed with anti-Americanism:** Actually, the Americans never really did much colonization, so if anything, the movie is anti-European.  Please notice that the soldiers were hired by a private corporation, and had nothing to do with the government. They were probably American because Cameron is American. And what could you possibly have against wanting to protect the environment? It's what keeps us alive for crying out loud.

**It's just Pocahontas in space:** Yes, and Star Wars is just the old knight-saves-princess tale, the Godfather is just a gangster flick, etc. All fiction imitates other works of fiction to some degree, so what?

**It's just the usual criticism of the Iraq/Afghanistan-wars:** No, no, no and NO. Avatar is an obvious criticism of the European colonization of America and Africa. For gods' sake, the natives have flat noses, and obviously African accents! It has absolutely nothing to do with the Iraq war! In fact, Cameron wrote the script 15 years ago, long before the current American conflicts in the Middle-east.

**The plot was predictable:** All of it? Really? Well, that's personal opinion. It sure kept me hooked, isn't that what matters? Die Hard was pretty predictable, I sure enjoyed that, same with many other great movies.
