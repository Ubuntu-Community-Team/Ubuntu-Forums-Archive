### Post by DeadSuperHero on 2009-03-30
As the title suggests, I'm thinking of constructing a Free Software/Free Culture social network specifically for everyone: Ubuntu users, Debian developers, BSD advocates, etc. I'm willing to pay for a server, set up the CMS (currently, Elgg looks really sharp), and basically provide a Free alternative to Myspace and Facebook, with free web APIs for developers to code web applications, as well as desktop tools to manage their profiles if they wanted to.

The problem, however, is that I can't think of a domain name that isn't taken! I'm currently using the domai.nr tool to come up with ideas, but a lot of great names are usually bought in bulk by other hosting companies for ad space.

So, I'm turning to the community for help. This is going to be a hobby project of mine, but it needs a name.

A few guidelines when suggesting it:
-I would like the name to emphasize the importance of both community and freedom.
-I want the name to "pop", I want it to be simple and catchy.
-When proposing a name, please try and explain the meaning behind your name, and why you proposed it.
-If you want, try and show a simple logo to go with it.

**Update 11-28-09:**
Although necroposting in topics is usually discouraged here, I would like to bring this old thread up for air once more due to the fact that I am saving up money for a domain and hosting. Unfortunately, I still have yet to find a good name for the site, but I would love to see some more suggestions from any members that may have missed this thread before.

The main goal of this network is to center around two things: a network built WITH FOSS tools, and a social network catering to FOSS enthusiasts and developers. It would be a FOSS-community alternative to Facebook and Myspace and cater more towards geeks than average joes, but would maintain a different sort of functionality than identi.ca. (This hypothetical site is to Facebook as identi.ca is to Twitter, so to speak)

I'm also happy to report that the site will most likely be based on Elgg and will likely undergo a usability-testing alpha in about four months or so if everything moves along according to plan. 

I am still greatly in need of a network name, but a rough skeleton of the site is being built on one of my private servers at home to test. It's actually going somewhere.
