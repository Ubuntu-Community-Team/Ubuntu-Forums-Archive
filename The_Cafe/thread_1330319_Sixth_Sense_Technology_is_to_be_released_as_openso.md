### Post by Xbehave on 2009-12-15
Wasn't there another thread about this? anyway i said it before and i'll say it agian. **MEH**, it's overhyped to hell and far from a 6th sense or something original it is just augmented reality, this tech has been around for literally years.
1) major problem is that image recognition is hard, expect ti carry round an 8core machine with 32gb of ram if you want it to recognise anything outside of obvious objects.
2) There is no metadata with a physical object, so even when it is recognized it will not be able to do what they show in the demo outside of a few testcases.
3) There is no metadata available for info on your display, so if you drag an image off your screen all you have is the image you've lost whatever the image represented
4) Even if you get the metadata, information about them is rarely available in a standardized format, bus times in london != bus times in new york.

Now there are ways around these, but they will probably be gradual and come from the spread of AU on phones rather than this. Finally the interface sucks, a HUD inside your glases is much more stable and private, if you want to share then an a projector in your phone offers a much more stable UI the something you are holding.

tl;dr
1) Meh
2) This is nothing new
3) It addresses none of the problems with existing tech
4) It adds new problems
