### Post by ve4cib on 2010-02-01
> **&#20170 said:**
> It would be nice to see Opera take an innovative step forward and develop their own Operating System.

Right now we have Windows, Apple and soon Google Chrome...it would be nice to have Opera step up to plate and offer their own brand of innovation.

What do you think of the possibilities of an Opera OS?

I'm afraid that I, like many others here, just don't see the point of this idea.  Unless the goal is specifically to create competition with Google in the thin-client/web-app-centric OS market I don't understand why this is a good idea.  But then it's not "a new brand of innovation" it's "Opera's flavour of Google's implementation of the thin-client innovation of 25 years ago".

Opera makes a decent browser.  But to turn that into an OS is a LOT of work.  In the end it would probably be exactly the same as Chrome OS (Linux kernel + browser UI + a few local libraries), but without Google's deep pockets and OS-writing experience.  This to me does not spell success.

Opera is better off, in my opinion, sticking with its current application focus.  People need browsers, and Opera is one of the better ones out there.  Especially for mobile devices.  I could see Opera creating other web-focused applications, but I can't see them succeeding at building their own OS.
