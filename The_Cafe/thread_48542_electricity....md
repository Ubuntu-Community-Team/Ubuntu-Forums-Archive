### Post by bgstratt on 2005-07-12
[http://www.answerbag.com/q_view.php/17770](http://www.answerbag.com/q_view.php/17770)

To get the exact answer you need to know how many watts your monitor and tower are rated at.  Just like the link, take the 2.5A multiplied by the 115 volts and that will give you 287.5 watts an hour.  Multiply by the 720 hours in a month for 207,000 watt hours a month, or 207 kilowatt hours, then multiply by the average cost per kwh, which is generally 8-12 cents US$, or say $20.70 at 10 cents an hour, if you pay less for electricity it will be less, or if your watts come out lower it will be cheaper.

### Post by WildTangent on 2005-07-13

a 250W PSU is what its maximum rating is, doesnt neccesarily mean it drawing that. but if thats what it is drawing, well use that in the calculation. well assume you pay $.10/kWh so we take 0.250 kW (250W) and multiply it by the cost per kWh, .10
```
0.250x0.10=0.025
```
so, that costs you 2 and a half cents per hour. multiply that by 24 and you get 60 cents per day
