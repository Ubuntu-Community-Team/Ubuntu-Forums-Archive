### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-06-09
The difference between Linux and Windows is that, with Windows, the entire platform is developed by a single company with complete control.
On that topic, the whole Windows software ecosystem is poisonous because Microsoft is in direct competition with practically every developer that uses their platform (indeed, it's completely backwards, given that they built the software platform to be usable for developers). Corel makes a decent office suit for Windows... Microsoft makes one that swallows theirs whole. Netscape? IE. Flash? Silverlight.
This battle of not-invented-here is now being foolishly moved to the web, which is going to be Microsoft's downfall. They won the previous battles because they controlled the platform from all sides, but they do not control the web like they do desktop computers.

That cannot happen in the Linux ecosystem, because there is no one company with the power to *own* the entire ecosystem. Sure, Novel could distribute nothing but Novel stuff, but then it wouldn't really be Linux. Even core systems like Xorg or Dbus, or important language interpreters such as Python, are key to the platform yet coming from all different directions.

"Free software built by the community at large" is not just shiny paint. This is different.
