### Post by GuiGuy on 2008-03-21
[SOAPBOX]
About 12 months ago I sneakily moved "Her Indoors"'s PC to Kubuntu. She hardly noticed and now she doesn't care. It works. 

Last Christmas I "upgraded" her PC with an ASUS eee pc. She loves it. The way it boots up so quick is a real plus. She likes the icons. She likes the way she can carry it around the house.  She doesn't give a fig what the OS is. 

And I should mention that friends and acquaintances are still inviting themselves around to our house to look at her eee pc. These, the same people who were telling me only a few months ago that Linux will never cut it, are now seeing the light.

[/SOAPBOX]

It's all good. 

[And today I feel extra good after reading this]("http://www.smh.com.au/news/articles/why-small-is-beautiful/2008/03/18/1205602389600.html?page=fullpage#contentSwap2").

So, it does seem linux didn't need a "killer application" after all. It was lusting for  killer hardware, but not in the way most of us think of killer hardware.
