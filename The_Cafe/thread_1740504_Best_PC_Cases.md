### Post by ade234uk on 2011-04-27
I bought an IBM Desktop PC with P4 3.4 processor from Ebay about a year and a half ago. Got to say, the build quality of this case is fantastic. The case does look pig ugly and people have asked what this thing is with the IBM badge sitting on my desk. But I absolutely love it. Its built like a brick s&*t house.

Unlike other cases to get inside you just press the tabs in both sides and the lid slides straight off. 

To get at the drives is even easier. The drive bay is on a hinge so you just pull up the whole drive bay, and then slide the drive out.

I have not come across a case that comes close to this at the moment apart from a shuttle I once had. 

But whats most impressive, this IBM case needs no screws to install new PCI cards, Graphics Cards either. I can see why they cost a lot of money.

I think if I upgrade my PC anytime soon, I will just mount the new motherboard in this case.

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-27
There are plenty of very nice cases on Newegg. I recently got a used Cooler Master HAF 922, you don't need a screwdriver for PCI cards or HDD or CD/DVD, you can install/remove those very easily. Nice cases are hard to come by, the shipping form Newegg is large, so either try to get a used one or if you get a good one, stick to it.

I used to have a very simple case, but I had made extra room for more fans, it worked great, had no trouble with heath and it was rather cheap. Although, it looked horrible, it was literally taped with duck-tape. I didn't care, I just wanted something that works. The case is better though, it has some looks, but more importantly, it has great air-flow.
