According to the latest leak, this device is a very sturdy and well-built tablet, one that is also quite thin, being about the same thickness as an iPhone. The surprising bit is that the tablet will indeed feature full phone functionality, though you'll need to rely on a Bluetooth headset, or the P1000's (as it's called) speaker.

Additional details include a front-facing camera for video-calling, a 3.2-megapixel camera at the back, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, 3G, GPS and Internet tethering over USB or Wi-Fi. Finally, it was also mentioned that the battery can't be removed and the tablet seems to be running Android 2.2. It will be powered by an A8 processor running at 1.2GHz.

The 370-gram tablet is expected to feature a 4,000mAH battery, 16GB of on-board storage, up to 32GB of microSD expansion, integrated eBook reader software and “exclusive” magazine content.

“The 7-inch panel is said to be of Samsung’s Super AMOLED variety which should make it readable outdoors judging by its smaller sibs,” wrote Engadget’s Thomas Ricker.

The Samsung Galaxy tablet is slated to launch “no later” than the third quarter of 2010[http://androidforums.com/tablets-mids/103318-samsung-galaxy-tab.html](http://androidforums.com/tablets-mids/103318-samsung-galaxy-tab.html)

This looks like a very nice option, the next two years will be nice for new technology options, I look forward to the future.
