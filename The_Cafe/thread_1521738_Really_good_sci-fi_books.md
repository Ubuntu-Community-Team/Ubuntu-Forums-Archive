### Post by donkyhotay on 2010-07-01
Robert L. Forward wrote some pretty good "hard" sci-fi stuff. Stephen Baxter can be worth reading as well but his books seem focused on some sort of "gimmick" that is interesting by itself(like universes where gravity is 1,000,000 times stronger then ours, or concurrent versions of the same person existing together due to time travel) while the story itself just doesn't grab me. I think "enders game" is probably one of my favorite non-series books of all time and thats sci-fi(ish), some of the sequels there were decent but everything written after "children of the mind" (especially the shadow series) seemed... contrived... and thats not counting all the retcons involved (especially the shadow series). Of course nothing really compares to the classics, especially asimov's work.
