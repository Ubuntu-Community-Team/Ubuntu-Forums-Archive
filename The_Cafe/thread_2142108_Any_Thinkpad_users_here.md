### Post by rewyllys on 2013-05-05
I'm writing this on a Thinkpad T420, which I've had for about 18 months now.  It's the fourth Thinkpad in a row that I've owned, starting in 1993.  I love especially the excellent feel of the keyboard, and I would never buy a laptop that lacked the little red button cursor control.

I've experienced excellent service with all of them.  One of them failed after about 11 months; I've forgotten what went wrong with it. What I do remember well -- and happily -- is IBM's service after the failure.  They overnighted a prepaid UPS shipping box to me; I sent the failed unit to them; and within 48 hours they had examined it, fixed it, and returned it to me, all at no cost to me!

The failure of that unit was the only problem I''ve experienced with any of my Thinkpads.  And that unit worked perfectly for the next 4 or so years that I kept it, till I decided to update to a newer model.

I've been running Linux on them for the last 4 years, and I'm currently running Linux Mint Nadia.  It installed flawlessly.
