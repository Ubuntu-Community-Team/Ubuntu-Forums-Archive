It is not just that. If you sell a Linux option, you also have to support it. Not just customer support (extra personnel), but also compatibility checks, assembling the "master copy", and so on. It is for a niche market (relatively, compared to Windows), which makes it even more costly.

I do understand why large, non-specialized, mass-market companies prefer to support just one OS. It is cheaper.
