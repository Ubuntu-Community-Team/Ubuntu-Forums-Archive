### Post by earthpigg on 2011-05-11
The class was entirely lecture based, with a few handouts. This is how we prepared for the essay final:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=User_talk:Earthpig&oldid=428543129](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=User_talk:Earthpig&oldid=428543129)

About 20% of the class contributed, and some number approaching 100% viewed it.

Of those that contributed their notes, I was actually the smallest contributor - I initiated it and got the ball rolling by typing the questions in, and not much more. One of those "[if you build it, they will come]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field_of_Dreams")" moments.

My studying was mostly comprised of merely reading and studying that Wikipedia page.

The test was 1.5 hours long, and the student was presented with three of those seven questions -- two of which they had to answer. That was today.

The professor was fascinated by the notion when I revealed our secret weapon to her after class, and I've emailed her that link. She said she may encourage students in future classes to continue that for their own purposes.


The biggest lessons learned for me:
[LIST=1]
[*]It worked.
[*]My decent public speaking abilities sold the class on the idea. This was crucial.
[*]The hybrid google docs thing resulted in some ugly <nowiki> gray boxes, but got folks to contribute that otherwise would not have. A more permanent solution needs to be created that is accessible to folks that love their MS Word paradigm but is compatible with wiki formatting. 
[*]For a super permanent solution, it may be worth hosting outside of Wikipedia... I skirted the guidelines for user_talk pages with that one.
[/LIST]
