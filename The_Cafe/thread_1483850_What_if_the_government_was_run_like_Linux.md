So how about it? If the government was run like linux, what would like look like? Do you think it would be practical? Could it compete with the more traditional authoritarian models? Would it resemble John Lockes "Panarchy". 


It would have to be a federation....I mean, you have the government 'kernel'....maybe a basic constitution of principles and way of networking. You would have lots of 'participatory communities' or cities that would pop up with their own ways of doing things. This would be organized in a way that is highly democratic and from the bottom up, yet it would be a decentralized democracy where people are free to deviate from the norm on a participatory basis within their own communities....more mainstream communities would exist as well of course.

You would probably have a lot of people moving around for the sole purpose of jointing a cutting edge community in development...I imagine some of these would be in rural areas since there is more room, but urban districts can also be inventive with the participatory democratic format.

Its also possible that you would get stuck in a community that doesnt fit your personal tastes....but the same is true right now, though the effects might be more obvious. At least within your community you are free to further organize collectives within the community along the lines of industry or trade or civil planning. Perhaps these unions could pan the entire country, and yet be networked locally via a federation of semi-autonomous regional chapters.
