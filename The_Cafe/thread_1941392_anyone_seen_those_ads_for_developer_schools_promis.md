### Post by JDShu on 2012-03-15
I'm going to put my economics hat on.

This is basic supply and demand. When there aren't many developers around, the salary is going to be very high. In particular, in today's environment where there are new startups left and right and Google is willing to pay 100k for (crazy smart) people straight out of college, then chances are that competent (maybe even some less competent) developers are going to be paid a good amount. From what I have seen, people in my CS department have no problems getting employed... I know a guy who has a job at Microsoft now, and another guy got 3 job offers without applying. A friend of mine only knows COBOL and he has no shortage of work either.

Maybe this is a tech bubble like in the 90s. I could certainly believe that the mad rush to go "social" is a fad. But even if it was, the IT bust in early 2000 was not that long ago, meaning that if the current bubble went bust now, it would be back in a couple years.

Yes people are cutting costs, but only where they can afford to. Right now, they can't afford to skimp out on hiring developers.

The most important thing though is to do what you love, anyway :)
