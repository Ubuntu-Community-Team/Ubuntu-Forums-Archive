### Post by justinjstark on 2007-06-17
I have been looking at buying a digital camera.  I am vacationing next week and would like to order one in the next few days.


What I am looking for:

1) It must work well with ubuntu.

2) Something in the $200-$300 range.

3) While I don't plan on doing any professional photography, I want something that takes nice pictures.

4) Something with a rechargable battery.

5) I would prefer if it was black.

6) Edit: Image Stabilization would also be preferred.


What I don't care about:

1) I don't care about size.

2) I don't care about resolution:  I would rather have a 4mp that takes great pictures than an 8mp that takes mediocre pictures.  I don't plan on doing any prints.


I have been considering the [Canon S3]("http://www.amazon.com/Canon-PowerShot-Image-Stabilized-Zoom/dp/B000EMWBV0/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1/102-0986867-0459347?ie=UTF8&s=electronics&qid=1182112143&sr=8-1").  I've read a lot of good reviews about it.  But, it uses AA batteries.  I would rather have something that I could just bring home and plug in at night.

I have also been looking at the [Panasonic FZ8]("http://www.amazon.com/Panasonic-DMC-FZ8K-Digital-Optical-Stabilized/dp/B000MWVMRG/ref=pd_bbs_sr_2/102-0986867-0459347?ie=UTF8&s=electronics&qid=1182112603&sr=8-2").  It has a rechargable battery but from what I have read, it doesn't take as nice of pictures as the S3.


Do you guys have any other suggestions or recommendations?

### Post by Leftblank on 2007-06-17
I can't quite help you with specfic recommendations, but are there currently any camera's that do not work properly with Ubuntu? So far I haven't seen too many that do not work 'plug & play' similar to USB sticks. 

Personally I'd go for a Nikon as I'm very happy with the D40 (DSLR) we've got at home, but I wouldn't be able to name you any specific models. Another suggestion I do have is not to mind too much about the battery usage; especially when you're on a vacation it actually became useful to me to be able to switch the batteries while shooting a lot of pictures, despite the less easiness of just plugging the cam in to charge it up again; get two pairs of batteries and a turbo charger and usually you should be fine!
