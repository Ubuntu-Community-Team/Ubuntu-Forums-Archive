### Post by gn2 on 2009-04-09
[This discussion]("http://www.theyworkforyou.com/debates/?id=2004-03-08.1229.2") might interest you.

If that letter is to TV Licensing, all you need to do is advise them that you do not possess any equipment capable of receiving a television broadcast. 
Anything more is irrelevant.

They need a warrant issued by a court to enter your home and you are perfectly entitled to refuse them access if they do not have one.

You can't take someone to the the small claims court for harassment.  That is not what the small claims court is for, the small claims court is for civil matters.  Harassment is a criminal act and you would have to involve the Police and take them to crown/county court.  The problem here is that the number of people without any broadcast equipment is minuscule, these letters are sent out by a computer.  With TV licensing it is the address that is licensed and not the person, anyone can operate a TV within a licensed address.  What has obviously happened in this case is that the previous occupants of this property have held a license that they have moved to another address, leaving this address unlicensed.  All the computer knows is that this address is no longer licensed and the computer will keep on sending out letters until it is told to stop.
