### Post by julioneander on 2012-03-13
From an audiophile perspective, anything that changes the sound causes distortion (distortion is any deformation caused on the original waveform).

If you want a HiFi sound (in the real meaning of high fidelity), you must turn off every processing* on the audio.

I have a studio monitoring class system plugged to my Ubuntu, with no processing at all, which I use to listen to my .flac collection. You can feel how natural the sounds and timbres are.

*Of course, some "corrective" processing can be used, but even these are discouraged.
