### Post by QIII on 2016-10-17
Hello!

You clearly have some significant misapprehensions about the nature of the Linux community.

Canonical, Ltd, the developer of Ubuntu, has no control over OCR software developers or the developers of GRUB.

Canonical certainly has no control over those who develop other Linux distributions.  Red Hat, for instance, is a $1B, publicly traded company.  Canonical is a small, privately-held company.  Red Hat could crush Canonical like a worm and never notice the bump.

Ubuntu is a Linux distribution released by Canonical, Ltd.  Ubuntu is a small subset of all that is Linux.  Linux is not contained by Ubuntu.

### Post by ventrical on 2016-10-19
> **jcostas-madrid said:**
> **Dear community**, I have three suggestions to improve ubuntu.

Ubuntu needs to bring all the people that work in different distros under the Ubuntu's name.

Ubuntu means , basically, "humanity and compassion" or , simply , "humaness". In a sense, all those who choose to practice these virtues can be already considered under the 'ubuntu' name. The many different desktops produced by Canonical already cover most of what is out there in the Linux community. Linux is not centralized or "owned" by anyone. If it was I would be a millionaire for all the testing I did for Linus Torvald's first publication of a boot disk (which BTW did not work and it was probably my fault). When we do work  out of humaness and compassion we usually do it for free without expecting anything back in return. It is all done for the greater good of the community. So when you make a contribution  in the forums you are, in effect, working with the ubuntu community and the linux community at large. You are working 'for' the greater betterment of the ubuntu and linux distributions that are cycled out in the various spins. 

 There are , however, enterprise editions of ubuntu and linux which of course cost money. Canonical is the main group which produces, develops and distributes the ubuntu brand. Canonical owns the brand but they do not own the ubuntu ideology or original translated concept. Red Hat also owns it's own brand and sells  a commercial brand of a custom linux kernel based software.  The most popular brand of linux-kernel based software is , of course, ubuntu. However .. to put all the different linux distros under  one group, under a centralized brand  would be contrary to the whole of the opensource concept and any new diversities would be quickly and quietly muted. I will not name names but there are distros out there which are .. uhh ,very stable but they are endentured and after time you would be able to tell that you are playing on a singular ping-pong table with rules. This is a result of what happens when people try to centralize linux. It looses it's diversity.

  Just my opinion but I think it is best to leave  the Linuxes the way they are. But I do understand your quest for stability.

Regards..
