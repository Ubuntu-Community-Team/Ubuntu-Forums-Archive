Can't help you with the GPU issue as I run a completely AMD shop here and know nothing about nVidia. My only comment would be to consider altogether new components. You seem to have a lot of stuff hanging off of limited base power and new stuff can be far more energy efficient than old stuff (depends on what you buy). GeForce Now.  It's free if someone wants to wait and play for an hour at a time.  But $9.99/mo makes it so you don't have to wait.  Then if you really want to kick it up, $19.99/mo is the equivalent of running a GTX4080 and lower wait times.  Since I signed up for the "Ultimate" ($19.99/mo), I have never had to wait to play a game.  

This helped solve quite a few gaming (performance) problems I have in Kubuntu.  There is a Snap for GeForceNow at the Snap Store.  It also runs right in the browser (Chrome or Chromium). In Kubuntu, I can get 60fps on my heavy hardware requirement games with all of the options on.  (1080p Ultra Settings on games like Marvels Guardians of the Galaxy, Horizon Zero Dawn, Shadow of the Tomb Raider, etc.)

Hardcore gamers claim there is a problem with lag... but I live in Alaska, and my "ping" is anywhere between 75-80ms.  And I can barely notice any lag.  At least, not enough to hinder the gameplay.  If I was hardcore, and needed 240fps... I might have an issue.  Also, the picture is fantastic.  It supports HDR (if the game does), and there is very little compression artifacts in the picture.

The requirements are:
15 Mbps for HD resolutions at up to 60 FPS (e.g. 1280x720)
25 Mbps for FHD resolutions at 60 FPS (e.g. 1920x1080)
35 Mbps for UW QHD & QHD resolutions at 120 FPS (e.g. 3840x1440 or 2560x1440)
45 Mbps for 4K resolutions at 120 FPS (e.g. 3840x2160)

Also, It doesn't support all games, so my advice would be to use the free option first, to see if it supports what you have.  Sync your libraries (Steam, UbiSoft, XBox) game libraries, and it will list which games you have that are supported.

My thought process was... for $20/month, or $240/year...  Is much more cost effective than buying my own GPU.  Plus, they keep upgrading their service.  When I started it was a GTX3080, now it's a GTX4080.
