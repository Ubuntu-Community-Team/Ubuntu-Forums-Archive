### Post by Nixarter on 2012-07-15
It helps them trouble-shoot.  What I would do is backup the drive with DD, and then use the system recovery that came with the computer, or find a windows disk (you implied it had windows to begin with) and install it with the key on the bottom of the laptop.

When it comes back, restore the drive from the backup.

No worries.

And when they say they "don't support" linux, that doesn't mean it voids your warranty.  It just means that their staff aren't specifically trained for Linux.  So if you have problems... come here instead, because they don't know how to help you.  But, it might make it hard because they have to test it afterward. Once it charges for a bit, they will unplug it and make sure it is discharging correctly.  They might now be able to figure it out on Linux.  So I would either do the backup thing, or get another hard drive and  install windows on it.  They will turn it on and make sure it is  charging.
