### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-06-28
I have been following the Lubuntu project for a couple of months now, and I read a transcript of a recent IRC "meeting" in which it was very clearly stated that "lightweightness" is *not* a goal of the project. Unquestionably an LXDE-based Ubuntu will gain *speed* and use less resources, but making it "lightweight" is not really what they are pursuing. And rightly so, I think, since there are already about a zillion distros already achieving or aiming to be "lightweight."

LXDE-based [U-Lite]("http://u-lite.org") is one of them, it's ranked at DistroWatch (rising from obscurity and still climbing) and being lightweight (but still graphical) is it's main objective. [Crunchbang Linux]("http://crunchbanglinux.org") is very lightweight, having no desktop environment at all (I added LXDE to it and made my own "U-Lite" before I had even heard of it). You've got Puppy, Slax, LXDebian, and probably dozens more all going after the same thing. Lubuntu is quite correct not to duplicate the others.

-Robin
