### Post by forrestcupp on 2007-04-23
I love Linux, and for me it is a better choice than Windows.

But one of my major pet peeves is how so many people use derogatory names for Microsoft, such as micro$oft, microshaft, windoze, etc.  It seems pretty childish to me.  We like the Linux way of doing things better, but as they say, there's more than one way to cook a goose.  Do we really need to treat people disrespectfully?  I know that when I was a Windows user, these terms were offensive to me because I saw it as an attack on my competence as a user of Microsoft products.

If we really want to convince or woo Windows users, the way is not through offensive trash talk about their current choice of an operating system.
