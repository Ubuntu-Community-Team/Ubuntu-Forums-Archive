### Post by beercz on 2006-09-14
A bit of light relief.

Euphemisms are words or phrases that are used to make something sound better than it actually is. See [http://www.berghuis.co.nz/abiator/rdg/euphemismhelp.html]("http://www.berghuis.co.nz/abiator/rdg/euphemismhelp.html")

Quite often these can be applied to job titles.  Here are a few amusing examples:

Dustmen are 'waste disposal operatives'
Ice-cream men are 'travelling sales persons'
Sales representatives are 'account managers' or 'business development managers' 

And my favourite (so far) is:
'Vision technician' - a window cleaner!
