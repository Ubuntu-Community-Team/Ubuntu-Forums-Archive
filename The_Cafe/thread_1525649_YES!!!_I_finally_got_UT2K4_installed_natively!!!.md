### Post by Legendary_Bibo on 2010-07-07
I've had issues getting Unreal Tournament 2K4 installed on my computer because I didn't have the freaking linux-installer.sh file on my disc. I tried the Loki installer and it would always crash, but tonight was different. I whipped that installer into shape! I was bored and decided to try one last time before selling it. Loki always froze at a screen asking for my CD key after I put it in. Killing it from top or even the Ctrl+Alt+F2 way with the default signal wasn't working. Well I tried killing it while it was running from top again (normally I would have to restart my computer to get it to stop), and I don't know how the kill signals work, but I figured I would try something new, so I just put '1' in and boom it stopped it, but it did something I didn't expect. It came back up to the screen asking for my CD key so I said what the hey because the menu looked kind of different. Entered the CD key in, and up came a EULA, and the installation process happened, finished, and plays without any issues. I don't even have to turn off Compiz or any thing that uses my audio while I play! :D
