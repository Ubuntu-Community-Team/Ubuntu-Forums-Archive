### Post by Mateo on 2009-09-25
The idea of openID is great.  Universal log-in for sites, who wouldn't love this?  There was 2 major problems, ignorance and lack of incentive.  Ignorance: Users likely won't know about openID unless they actively look for it.  Lack of incentive: Unless a user is *planning ahead*, there is no incentive to sign up for an account.

These 2 problems *seemed* to be resolved when large sites such as Yahoo and Google started to become openID providers.  They supposed automatically make their account owners have openIDs.

Great.  Except it was implemented poorly.  Instead of assigning an openID to its users or asking their users to create one, they've left their users completely in the dark.  Which means that if you have a Google or Yahoo account, the only way to use it with other sites is for the developers of those sites to create "Sign in with Google" or "Sign in with Yahoo" code.  This is both 1) Added work for the developers and 2) glut.

So that means if you have a Google or Yahoo (or many other similar) openID account you either are restricted so a small portion of openID supported sites, or you have to create a second (and therefore redundant) openID account for those sites.
