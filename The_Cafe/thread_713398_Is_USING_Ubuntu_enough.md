### Post by forrestcupp on 2008-03-02
Well, there is one big way that you can help by just being a user.  You can file bug reports.  Any time you use something in Ubuntu and it isn't working quite right, you can go to [Ubuntu's part of Launchpad](https://launchpad.net/ubuntu) and file a report about it.  Problems can't be fixed if they don't know about it.  All you have to do is tell them what program had the problem, what the problem is, and how you got it to happen.  Anyone can do that.

It is important to try searching first to see if the bug has already been filed.  If it has, you can add your input to that bug report.

### Post by Tyche on 2008-03-02
There are many ways to help.  Not all of them are glamerous, but they can be fun.
1.  Join a LoCo Team, and help where you can on IRC or in the forums.  Participate in the various events that the Team sponsers.
2.  If you have language skills, there are various ways to help by translating documentation and etc, including the Ubuntu Weekly Newsletter.
3.  If you have artistic skills, there are Art teams that would like help.

Whatever you have skills in may be helpful, if you just look around and ask others.  LoCo Teams are a great place to start asking, since they are often made up of a wide cross-section of society.  Some of the members may be so advanced as to make your head spin, while others may be just starting out and totally lost.

However, you shouldn't feel that you HAVE to contribute.  This should be something you do for fun, not like a job you HAVE to go to.  Relax, enjoy, and Welcome to Ubuntu.
