It would be far too impractical, so I seriously doubt it exists. I would just say "no", but technically there's no reason it could not be done...

As to external processors, how do you think it would plug in? CPU's are interconnected in such a way that they can exchange data at incredible speeds (think gigabytes per second, though I don't think throughput is the objective so much as extremely low latency); you can't exactly get this with USB. A decent graphics card would have a similar issue- you simply can't push enough data through most peripheral interconnects fast enough to make them useful. I think there are standards for external PCI devices, but I don't think anyone has even considered building something like this.

So, no. The closest thing to actually solving your problem is logging in remotely, but you'd have to have an extremely good connection to do anything remotely graphical. (Plus sufficient graphics capability on the client to quickly update the display- something like playing a fullscreen movie at 60fps, I guess.)

The limitations of netbooks are inbuilt: expandability is limited by design, even moreso that traditional laptops.  They're meant to be cheap, small, throwaway machines.  Nothing more.
