### Post by Kyuukei on 2011-07-29
So, after getting fed up with lolVista, I got some friends to help me make the transition to Ubuntu. It was wonderous. Everything was so... smooth. Streamlined. Gedit is badass, the multiple workspaces are awesome, I can scroll without clicking to switch programs (!!!), the bootup process is ridiculously quick, the emphasis on command line is very intuitive. I could go on and on. But I won't.

So then I accidently opened Banshee when I was adjusting my volume, went to close it quickly, and it crashed. Didn't respond at all. I was all "huh" and went to close it. A prompt came up saying it was not, in fact, responding, and that I could either force-quit it or wait for it to get its **** together. I chose to force-quit it.

And it just closed.

No going white and ineffectually searching for a solution I didn't need. No having a panic attack before bringing up the prompt in the first place. No wasting several seconds of my life with a needlessly bloated closing systemic.

It just closed. And it was *awesome.*

THANKS GUYS. I LOVE UBUNTU.
