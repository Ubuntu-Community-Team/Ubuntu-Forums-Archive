### Post by HavocXphere on 2009-02-19
Cool is whatever the mainstream is not doing at the moment. Take T-shirt fashion: e.g. suddenly vertical stripes are seen as totally cool. Then the distribution channels get flooded and then suddenly everyone has them and then they go from cool->common. Following this line of logic, Windows is not cool. Its common.

Cool is anything rebellious, insane, anarchist and non-conformist. Taking Apple: Everything needs to be Apple branded and bought from an official Apple Store or else it won't be compatible (and/or frowned upon as a Mac Clone). Apart from the price, there isn't anything insane about the Mac Eco-system. The Apple legal eagles have declared that Mac OS X may not be used with anything except Apple hardware. That is neither anarchist nor rebellious. And of course its got the trying-too-hard-to-be-cool problem.:rolleyes:

True Coolness emanates from the personal style of the person under review, not from the expectations of the outside world. So anyone who tries to emulate all the trends that are currently "cool" is bound to fail. It'll just end in the trying-too-hard-to-be-cool problem or constantly being one step behind the current flow. The only real & permanent way around this is to impose your own idea of cool onto the outside world. The *nix eco-system (Or any FOSS) gives the user the ability to customise it in any way he/she sees fit. So the coolness of the OS is largely dependent on the user personal style & coolness factor.
