Most likely that is because GNUstep **is no window manager** and therefor cannot be run as one. In fact, GNUstep cannot even be *run*, because it is just a bunch of libraries, see below.

You are probably referring to Window Maker, which does indeed look NeXT'ish, but has **absolutely nothing** to do with GNUstep.

GNUstep isn't even a desktop environment, like GNOME or KDE -- it is a development framework, see the first like over at [GNUstep's website]("http://www.gnustep.org/") :

> 
GNUstep is a cross-platform, object-oriented framework for desktop application development.
