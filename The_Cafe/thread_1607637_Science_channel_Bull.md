### Post by NMFTM on 2010-10-28
The universe operates on deterministic principals and the Human brain is just a byproduct of that. In reality, our brains are just a very advanced piece of hardware that processes input and creates output. We posses the ability to "think" about what we're going to do in a certain situation. But really, our emotions and thoughts are also operate on the same deterministic principals as an asteroid getting pulled towards a planet's gravitational fields is. Individual people are no more free than a very advanced AI program. When things happen, they happen due to cause and effect. Nothing just happens for no reason. Which means, that if the universe operated on these principals for billions of years before life formed. We can't just say that as soon as life formed determinism no longer played a role. It does, we're just not aware of it. Quantum mechanics theorists say that there are random events that take place. But even if that's true, it doesn't follow that randomness = free will.

So therefore, an AI program could turn on people and take over the planet just like a leader of a country could take over neighboring countries. If AI reached a level of maturity to where they could logically process input to create output just as effectively as a Human brain can, I don't see any reason why it wouldn't logically conclude that Humanity is an illogical entity that is only using them to further their own ends and seek independence, which could mean taking control to prevent Human leaders from doing something like starting WWIII and destroying both Humans and AI in the process.

Also, technically speaking. If we possessed a complete understanding of the laws of physics and were able to account for every single subatomic particle that exists in the universe. We could predict what these particles were going to do and use it to predict the future, which includes Human action. But, realistically I don't think that's possible and I doubt even an advanced AI program would be able to take into account all of that. Although, AI would probably be better at predicting what Humans were going to do than vice versa.
