### Post by Deiz on 2010-07-28
Your link points to [http://ubuntuforums.org/www.logicsupply.com](http://ubuntuforums.org/www.logicsupply.com) rather than [http://www.logicsupply.com/](http://www.logicsupply.com).

Logic Supply has a good reputation, but the feasability depends on what you're looking for. If you want a low-powered, durable system for embedded use, they may well be your go-to.

If you don't explicitly need something for embedded use, you can do better building a system yourself. For a desktop I'd sooner look at [ZaReason](http://zareason.com/) or [System 76](http://system76.com/).

### Post by earthpigg on 2010-07-28
Checked out the 'nettop' and 'low-cost' offerings they have.

I've built more powerful desktops for similar price points. [example from a few months ago]("http://dl.dropbox.com/u/1832211/cheapbuild.jpg").

For the power & price of those machines, you can get a netbook.... which will function just as well as a small form-factor desktop when plugged into a display, keyboard, and mouse. Except it will be more flexible for the same price. 

I like that they offer Ubuntu 10.04, but cannot honestly justify purchasing anything I am seeing - unless you have some specific niche use that they target. 

for small & cheap, though... nothing they have seems to defeat self-assembled desktops on one hand, or mainstream netbooks on the other.
