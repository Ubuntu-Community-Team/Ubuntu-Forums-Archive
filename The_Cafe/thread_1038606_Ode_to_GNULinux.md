### Post by lu6cifer on 2009-01-12
Surprisingly, there aren't very many Linux poems out there, so I made one:

Copylefted under the GFDL

Ode to GNU/Linux, in 100000 lines

A kernel created by Torvalds in ninety-one,
Add in Stallman's GNU for extra fun.
Oh, GNU/Linux, how shall I boot thee? 
Let me name the ways:
Debian, Fedora, Slackware, Gentoo,
Down with Microsoft, Free Software stays,
Mandriva, Knoppix, openSUSE, Ubuntu,
The distros will thrive while Windows decays. 
Interested? Well, here's what to do:
Boot from a disk,
There's really no risk.
Just try it, it's free,
No registration or product key.
Ubuntu's a good start, find salvation in its forum,
But conduct yourself with the utmost decorum.
When you're experienced, give Gentoo a try,
After compiling begins, just try not to die.
For old computers, use DSL,
Which can run on a flashdrive as well. 
For web servers, run Apache,
Cause IIS goes Kamikaze.
It's a programmer's haven, and you can tell,
that you won't get caught in DLL hell. 
Time to pick a text editor, emacs or vi?
Oh wait, real programmers would use a butterfly*. 
What about programs? How do I get those?
No applications--what a problem that would pose,
Well, in Gentoo, just compile from source
With Ubuntu, sudo apt-get, of course
Yes, GNU/Linux really does have it all
It's a one of a kind system, a real hall-of-famer
Using it among your friends will make you stand tall    
Oh wait, you're a gamer? 

*xkcd.com/378
