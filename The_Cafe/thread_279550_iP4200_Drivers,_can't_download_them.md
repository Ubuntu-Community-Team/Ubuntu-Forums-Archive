### Post by schurtek on 2006-10-19
I work very closely with one of Canon's Distributors here in South Africa who have agreed to give me hardware backing for our open source projects... no other distributor would agree to that. Pinnacle Micro however stated that if any of my solutions prove popular, then obviously their hardware would be first choice as it has been designed developed and tested on their hardware...

Also more than half Pinnacle's staff use Ubuntu at home... :D

They told me about Canon releasing code for linux... i phoned Canon up and after several hours of trying to explain that Linux is a desktop operating system I got to speak to a guy somewhere in Canada who works in R&D and confirmed to me what I was told. YES! They are busy with it! Hence all my Inkjets have been replaced with Canon...

I just bought an Oki Laser c3400 but that was because it prints like a photo in draft mode... and it's cheap to run... and hey... there are enough brains out there to develop and Oki port for Linux.

The point I am trying to make is... that if I can get enough people interested in developing and open source project for Canon Product Drivers in Linux... I might be able to get Canon to approve the project and work with us...
