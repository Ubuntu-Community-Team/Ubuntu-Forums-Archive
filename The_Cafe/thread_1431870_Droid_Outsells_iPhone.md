### Post by Tristam Green on 2010-03-17
The thing about the iPhone sales was that, at the time, there was nothing like it.  No smartphone could do what the iPhone did.

Now, the Droid surpassing iPhone v1 sales is impressive, but let's face it.  Except for it being an Android device, it's another "smartphone for the masses-iPhone killer".  Far more people have data plans on their cell lines now than they did in 2006/07, and many of those users are switching from iPhones to Droids, be it either by interest in Android, "looking cool", or aggravation from AT&T.

Those points should put the numbers into some perspective.
