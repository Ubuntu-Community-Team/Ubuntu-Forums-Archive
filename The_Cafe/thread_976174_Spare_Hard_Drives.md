### Post by handy on 2008-11-09
Separate drives are perfect for distro' hoping, that is how I do it.

If you get into it, you might like the convenience of using drive drawers; putting your boot drive in a draw means you can easily swap it out to install & use another distro'.

I have 3 draws in my test machine, which is left over from when I used to maintain customers systems.  I was always using Norton Ghost to clone & cast images of drives as it was the quickest way for me to do much of my work.

Drive draws are quite cheap, 5 years ago they used to cost me less than $20- Australian, each.
