### Post by Fire_Chief on 2008-11-26
It's a bit broad to just say I want a good magazine for Linux. For example, some readers love magazines that get very technical and offer a lot of cmd line or scripting articles. Others like magazines that focus on software reviews and productivity. Some look for both or like magazines that  include CDs regularly or share recent news in the Linux community. It's kinda up to your tastes. Having said all that...

For a print magazine, I would pay a visit to Barnes & Nobles or Borders Bookstore and have a look at the Linux magazines there. They typically have at least 4 different ones all the time. You can look at each and get a feel for the style of magazine it is and pick your favorite.

Many if not all of those print magazines have websites that you can visit too. Have a look at these.
[http://linuxmagazine.com/]("http://linuxmagazine.com/")
[http://www.linux-magazine.com/]("http://www.linux-magazine.com/")
[http://www.linuxjournal.com/]("http://www.linuxjournal.com/")
[http://www.linuxformat.co.uk/]("http://www.linuxformat.co.uk/")
[http://www.tuxmagazine.com/]("http://www.tuxmagazine.com/")
[http://linuxgazette.net/]("http://linuxgazette.net/")
[http://www.linuxidentity.com/us/index.php]("http://www.linuxidentity.com/us/index.php")

Cheers!
