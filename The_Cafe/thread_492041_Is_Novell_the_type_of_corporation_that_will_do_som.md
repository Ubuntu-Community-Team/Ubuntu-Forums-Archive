### Post by bonzodog on 2007-07-04
No, not many people realise what type of corporation Novell really is. Not just that, but the Patent FUD stops at the US borders, and so only concerns those who would foolishly trade inside the US.

Novell, lets just say, that they have a hold or two over MS - MS have blatantly infringed on Novell Patents that were established up to 15 years ago, and Novell could sue them almost into non-existence. 

But should a Patent war start, which MS know full well would be foolish, it would be signing their own death warrant. The *very* first thing that IBM/Novell would pull (yes IBM would automatically be there -- they have "vested" interests in Linux), is a worldwide Cease and Desist on distributing Windows with *any* PC, until the case is resolved -- which would only take about 7 or 8 years. That would more or less kill MS, and change the shape of the home computer market forever. IBM would also then counter-sue MS for infringing their patents as well, so the patent thing is a kind of cold war situation - it's all about politics, and the basic background threat of it. 

Actually suing for infringement is effectively the nuclear option, and not really viable - there would be no winners. 

Except of course, those in the EU - none of this applies outside the US, and so Linux could go on being freely distributed around the rest of the world with whatever desktop they want. MS could never effectively issue a cease and desist and have it honoured outside the US for anything in linux as, well, the rest of the world just doesn't give a rats *** what the US says or does, and Software patents just don't work here.
