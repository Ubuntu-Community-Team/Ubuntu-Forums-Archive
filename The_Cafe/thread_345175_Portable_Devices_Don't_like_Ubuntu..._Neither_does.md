### Post by banjobacon on 2007-01-24
> **Johnsie said:**
> Gaim is text based.... that was 1997. We're living in the 21st century now. Check out video conferencing on Skype for windows and you'll see what other software can do.

Skype is a closed-source program that runs on a closed protocol. If you can't do Skype video conferencing on Ubuntu, that's Skype's fault, not Ubuntu's. As long as you use Skype, you're locked into Skype's product. That's why it's important to support open protocols, like Jabber and SIP.

The same holds true with Flash. Adobe chose to release the latest version for Linux months after it had been out for Windows, and they've chosen not to release a version of Shockwave. 

Yes, you have the freedom to buy whatever hardware you'd like, but you should use that freedom wisely and purchase from companies that actually show some interest in being compatible with Linux. Ubuntu can't magically support every piece of hardware ever created; the manufacturers need to play along, too.
