### Post by YosefKaro on 2009-11-23
Hi all,

I remember waiting with great anticipation to receive my Wave invite.  In the interim, I watched a number of videos about google Wave's features and I was thoroughly impressed.  When I finally got my invite, I sat around pouting for half-a-day because I was all alone on Wave (For those who don't know: When you first receive your invite, you have no invites to send to others, so the only contact you have is the one who invited you and, if you are lucky, your other gmail contacts who also have Wave).  

I was all by myself until I discovered that there are public Waves that anyone can join in on.  So I 'caught' and I 'rode' a number of the Waves that I happened to be interested in.  My first impression was that of awe: I was blown away by this 'IM on steroids'.  However, as I 'rode' more and more Waves, the more I realized that they lose their momentum and die off.  The Waves just get longer and longer: they become very cumbersome and difficult to navigate.  

At this point, after using it for just one week, I have to say that I like my gmail account, my IRC client, my Skype, and my google docs many times over more than I do Wave.  

Don't get me wrong: I think that google Wave has tremendous potential.  I just feel that they are making their target audience way too big.  Their target audience is the entire world.  My bad, just the portion of the world that is currently using email.  They seem to have this notion that if email were to be invented today, then it would look something like google Wave.  However, I have my reservations.

Please tell me what your impressions of google \/\/ave are.  :D

-Yos

### Post by michaeldt on 2009-11-23
As a university student I find it useful to be able to collaborate on coursework with GW. Of course it helps that I had enough invites to get people onto it. I think it has potential, I'd especially like more of my family to use it as I prefer it's method of conversations to just the usual email. Often I email my parents and my dad sends photos. Being able to comment on particular ones and discuss various things, each with their own thread, is quite handy.

I think it comes down to two things:
More users
More gadgets

Some of the existing gadgets are useful, but more gadgets would certainly make things better. At the moment one robot I love (provided externally) is a latex robot which allows me to input formulas into GW using latex. 

I also think it would be useful for projects where people on the project can discuss ideas separately whilst still keeping the whole discussion in one place, particularly since you can revisit the entire discussion at any time. Of course, this can all be done using email, but I think GW in these areas certainly provides a better interface for it.

All it really needs is a better way of checking it, so some desktop gadget or email addon.
