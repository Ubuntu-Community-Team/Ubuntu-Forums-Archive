### Post by Mighty_Joe on 2009-08-06
> **sydbat said:**
> My question is - if USB flash sticks have a limited amount of read/write before going t*ts-up, what is the lifespan of these solid-state (flash) hard drives? Have they figured out a way to overcome the read/write limitations?

It's not nearly as bad as some people make it. Most drives sold now implement some form of wear leveling to maximize drive life. [This guy]("http://www.bress.net/blog/archives/114-How-Long-Does-a-Flash-Drive-Last.html") got 90.5 million writes (reads don't cause wear) out of a 1Gb drive.  One thing to consider: the larger the drive, the more memory cells the wear leveling has to work with, the longer the drive's life, so buy big.
