Turning the question around, plenty of people have seen me using Ubuntu on my EeePC 701 while out and about, and it often attracts a bit of attention.  But then along comes an idiot who says "Ah Linux, that's the cheapie system that doesn't work properly.  I try to put such fools right, but it's so often a losing battle.

One time I was on my EeePC in the library, using the terminal for something or other, and someone reported me to the librarians for "hacking"!  It took me a while to convince them I was actually doing nothing wrong.  For some reason the sight of a command line interface makes some people believe you're up to no good!  When will these fools get a it of education?  Grrr!!!

Incidentally: over the past few months I've noticed that when a character in a movie or TV show is using a laptop, 8 or 9 times out of 10 it's an Apple!  This isn't an accurate reflection of how many Apple users are out there, so it's got to be product placement, right?  I wonder how much Apple are paying the studios?

Some Apples are very nice machines though.  An Apple store opened in my home town a few months ago, and I went in to see what I'd been missing.  Some of those laptops: they're so thin, and the GUI looks so nice... I'm seriously starting to think that when my current machines die I may get a Mac of some description.  Those Mac Books (I don't know the model name) look so nice: they're real thin and light, and the display is just so gooood!  So I might go for a OSX/Ubuntu dual-boot, just for the pose.
