### Post by shearn89 on 2007-06-15
Hey all, just thought i'd share something with you - my network at home/school (i live on site as my dad is a teacher) has restricted internet controlled by RM. They also provide the filtering for many other schools etc....

Recently, whilst trying to get my wireless card up and running, i tried to access the Sourceforge page for ndiswrapper, only to find its filtered!

On emailing RM (very politely) asking why it was blocked, and if it would be possible to unblock it, i received this reply:

> 
Thanks for your message regarding our filtering of Sourceforge.net.

The reason why this site is filtered is that it appears to provide 
articles on how to create proxy servers that will allow users to 
bypass our filters. We are obliged to take all steps to prevent this 
happening, and as such the site must remain filtered for the time 
being.

The site was added to our pornography list because it is our highest 
ranking filter list that all our customers sign up for.

Kind regards

IFL Filtering Team


Now i know this is a fairly acceptable reason, but surely its a bit indiscriminate to filter an ENTIRE open source project-hosting website, simply because they "appear" to show users how to get round filtering. Could they not just block the specific project pages?

Thoughts/opinions/sympathy/people saying i'm wrong are all welcome!
