I own the hardware upon which the software has been fixated; in terms of copyright law this means I am the owner of a *copy of the copyrighted work*. The copyright holder retains *some* exclusive rights to what is done with that work, but his rights of "ownership" of the software -- at least the instance of that software fixated on the hardware I own -- are not unlimited. 

In particular, copyright law does NOT grant the copyright holder the right to prohibit an owner of a copy of the software from "utilization" of the software, nor is the copyright holder entitled to prohibit the owner of a copy of the software from "adapting" the software in order to "utilize" it ([USC Title 17, §117]("http://www.copyright.gov/title17/92chap1.html#117")).
