### Post by potrick on 2006-01-11
You see, to me what Apple does with the Apple computer is much worse than what Microsoft does.

Microsoft is seen as being a bad company because it bundles software with its operating system to destroy competators. For example, IE killing Netscape or Windows Media Player making it less likely that anyone buys a dedicated media playing program. So they control a lot more of the market than they should.

To me, part of having a monopoly is using your power to sell unrelated products. So, to use the classic example, if I own all the worlds cheese and then start including crackers with every purchase of cheese, I'm going to pur a lot of cracker companies out of buisness. 

So yeah, what microsoft does is essentually a monopolistic practice. 

But now think of Mac. Not only are they bundeling a web browser, media players (one of which you need to register for 50 dollars just to get full screen, I might add) and even audio editing software with their operating system, but they even go so far as to say to even use this software you need to buy their hardware, which is very well marketed and "hip". They are selling all the worlds cheese along with free crackers in a very expensive and trendy box that costs way more than it should. And you can't get no cheese if you don't buy the box. 

And yet they are seen as the good guys, just because they are not Microsoft and they occasionally use open source software. At least Microsoft has the decency to pretend we Linux users don't exist, but Mac builds their software using open source technology (even their much hyped ability to network with windows computers is based on something Ubuntu users know very well--Samba).

In conclusion, I agree with everyone when they say that Mac is no better than Microsoft.
