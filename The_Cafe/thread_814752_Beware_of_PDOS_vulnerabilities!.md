### Post by Unix_Slayer on 2008-06-01
New Denial of Service attacks out there. Called PDOS = Permanent Denial of Service. Network-enabled systems firmware are susceptible to a remote. Called "phlashing". Unlike distributed DOS attacks-which sabatoge a service or Web site, but also can be a cover for malware delivery-PDOS is a pure hardware attack. It's a logical and highly destructive extension of the DDOS criminal extortion tactics. 

These attacks result in a costly recovery for the victim, since it means installing new hardware. But they cost attackers much less than a DDOS attack, which requires renting botnets to overwhelm a site with data requests. Make sure your flash update mechanisms have authentication so not just anyone can update them, and that they're designed with attacks in mind. **_[Meaning your firmware in Wireless routers most importantly]_**
