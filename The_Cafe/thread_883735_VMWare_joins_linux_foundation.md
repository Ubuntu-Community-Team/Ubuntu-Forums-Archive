The well known virtualisation company has become a linux foundation member.

Press release:
[http://linux-foundation.org/weblogs/press/2008/08/06/vmware-joins-the-linux-foundation/](http://linux-foundation.org/weblogs/press/2008/08/06/vmware-joins-the-linux-foundation/)

News story:
[http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2008/08/06/AR2008080602006.html](http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2008/08/06/AR2008080602006.html)

It's not really earth shattering news but it requires a little financial commitment and is surely a statement of some intent to continue to support the platform.

The rest of members list is here:
[http://linuxfoundation.org/en/Members](http://linuxfoundation.org/en/Members)

Platinum members pay a $500,000/year membership fee.
Gold members pay a $100,000/year and Silver between $5000 and 20,000.
