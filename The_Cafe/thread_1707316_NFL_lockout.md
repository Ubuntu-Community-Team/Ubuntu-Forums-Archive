### Post by TBABill on 2011-03-15
No idea if it will happen or not, but imagine the toll that will take on the economy. Regardless of whether someone enjoys the sport or not, without it there will be further job losses, local economies hurt wherever hotels, tickets, restaurants, transportation, etc. are in place to support the games. Plus vendors, suppliers, airlines, and on and on. Then add onto that the network coverage, advertising and other things that would not happen, or at least would not happen for the intended purpose of those time slots. The impact would be pretty large in a fragile economy already hurting from unemployment, housing foreclosures and losses to critical positions such as fire, police, teachers and other necessary services.
 
I enjoy the game and hope there is a season, but more importantly, I hope there is a season for those who work so hard to support it and count on the incomes to support their own families.
