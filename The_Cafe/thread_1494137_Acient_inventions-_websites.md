[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_historic_inventions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_historic_inventions)

There are issues with all those lists, however. They almost always use the 'correct' archeaological dates, which can be a long way out. They will only take 'hard' evidence, and once you go back to pre-3,000 BCE its getting very hard to find a lot of the things made from perishable materials. 

so you can have situations where there is evidence for a fairly complicated technology, but no evidence for simpler, related technologies. 

Example, from that wiki list- 

60,00 years- bow. 
6,000 years- bow drill. 

If it took 54,000 years for someone to 'get' the bow drill, then I'm a monkeys uncle (and not his nephew LOL). 

Also, sometimes there are problems with people being given ceredit for inventions yhey 'tradionally' made (eg  [FONT=Arial]pythagorean theorem to Pythagoras, even though there is hard evidence that the babylonians knew about it at least 1000 years before Pythagoras was even born)
