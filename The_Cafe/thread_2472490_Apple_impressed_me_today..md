The hardware AND OS as paired are highly secure. Probably the most secure that 99.9999% of use will ever use.

When I looked at Apple's automatic sharing between iOS devices (part of the family is Apple-only for phones). I couldn't figure out how they did it securely over bluetooth or wifi-direct. Two sisters were automatically setup to swap photos from their phones whenever they were nearby, doing nothing. We were in remote parts of the world, no internet (no TV either), yet the transfers still worked. Just because I couldn't figure it out, doesn't mean all the proper PKI exchanges weren't happening. Some user interaction was necessary to setup the initial exchange between the devices. Trusting that forever is what scares me.  Hopefully, Apple is better at owner wipe and transfers than every vehicle maker which seems confused that people rent cars.

My whataboutism is high. Sorry.
