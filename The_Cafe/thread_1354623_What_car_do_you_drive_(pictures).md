### Post by kevinatkins on 2010-01-02
First car - 1976 Austin Maxi.. white with brown highlights. OK, it was a rustbucket. 5-speed gearbox, if you could find the gears. Decent ashtray.

Then a 1972 Reliant Regal - bought for a laugh, and at least it wasn't rusty like the Maxi. Worst car I have ever driven - only safe / stable in reverse. The whole car was the ashtray.

Then a 1982 (wow, modern) Leyland Princess - surprisingly good, very comfortable. Ashtray excellent, very well-positioned just by the steering wheel.

Discovered Italian cars.. fell in love. Next up was... a Fiat 126 (OK it was made in Poland). Very slow, but still dangerous. Terrible in any conditions other than dry, on the level, in a straight line. Not ideal in the UK then. Still not as bad as the Reliant mind. Good horn - very loud.

After that, I had at least five Minis... brilliant, but high maintenance. Not safe when a bus hits you. A little bit uncomfortable on the longer journeys - like, anything over 5 miles.

Fell in love with Italian cars again. Next car - 1986 Alfasud Sprint 1.7 QV. Seriously quick, beautiful to look out. The loudest horn of any car I've ever owned. Unreliable electrics. Horn used to go off at random. Tailgate fell off.

Next up - 1992 Rover Metro GTi 16V (the multipoint injected one, yay).. would have prefered 205GTi but they were twice the price for similar year. Metro was more than half the fun though, when it went. Which wasn't often. And when it did go, had a job stopping the thing - brakes appalling. Good litle go-kart though, very quick.

What else.. ah yes, the 1974 Rover P6 3500 V8... great to look at, great soundtrack but it was a complete nail.. Often to be seen with bonnet up on motorways.. 

Back to Alfas.. 1999 Alfa 156 next up.. To my eyes, still one of the most beautiful saloon cars ever made. Wonderful engine - powerful, lusty yet economical, too... Pity the chassis was so average - arthritic round corners, ride too firm... 100% reliable over two years - how un-Italian.. got bored, so got rid..

Replaced with a couple of Alfa 33s - lovely boxer engines, lots of rust, collapsing interiors, gear selectors that fell apart.. back behind the wheel of some proper Italian machinery again. Bliss.

And I currently drive.. a 1998 Peugeot 306 diesel. Hateful smoky thing but it drives nicely, isn't rusty, it's comfortable and it looks pretty good, even today.
