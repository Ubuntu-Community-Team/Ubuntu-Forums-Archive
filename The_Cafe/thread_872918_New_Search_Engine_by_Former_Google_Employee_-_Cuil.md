NEWS: New Search Engine Crated by Ex Google Engineers/Developers

A Company made up with ex Google employees created a new Search Engine that they hope will be the leader in all search engines. Unfortunately, they will have to fish against the custom of most of the internet users.

[Cuil]("http://www.cuil.com/") offers from Starting from Sunday, July 37 Cuil is offering a new search services in which the company proclaims to be able to offer a faster and less costly than Google which is believed to be best search engine.

The aspiring rival of Google points out that their services goes further than being a Techniques of dominant searches that concentrates in the links and audience's traffic, **analyzing however the context of each page and the concept to which it alludes to the search of each user.**

"Our significant advances in the search technology have allowed us to index much more of Internet, being placed almost all the Network within reach of the fingers of each user” affirms Tom Costello, cofounder and delegated advisor of Cuil.

Danny Sullivan, analyst of searches in Internet and Chef Editor of Search Engine Land, thinks that [Cuil]("http://www.cuil.com/") can try to exploit the complaints that the consumers have of Google, mainly by the results that favor to the most popular pages.

**The privacy of the users is another one of the strongpoints** of its strategy, according to the company. Concentrating in the content of the pages instead of in the history of link connections, the company does not need to store personal information of the users or their browsing history.
