### Post by mohrolsen on 2009-03-26
The following has been kicked around for a while between friends of mine, mostly as a zany type utopian idea that would never really be possible. However, I&#8217;ve decided to run with it &#8211; at least to the extent of seeing what people think of the idea.

Obviously, everyone reading along here is aware that what can be accomplished with a strong community and a shared sense of creating something useful and beneficial. Without this sense, there would be no Ubuntu, Linux, GNU, Open-source or Creative Commons &#8211; this goes without saying. But can this mentality be transferred to a full length documentary about that same community?

A lot of people are aware of and have seen two of the better features on the free software initiative ([http://video.google.com/videosearch?q=linux+documentary#](http://video.google.com/videosearch?q=linux+documentary#)) available on the web, but is it time for an update?

Would it be possible to collaborate online, create and manage all the logistics around creating a documentary that gives a more modern and complete picture of what the whole community is about? With the community, I am not only referring to the Ubuntu community &#8211; but rather the entirety of the open-source movement; from individual hackers and programmers to initiatives such as the OLPC, commercial uses and all sorts of related movements that have come into existence as a result of this type of collaboration and paradigm shift inherent on the net.

Do you think the community would be able to come together in this manner to create a film that in return would be freely available on the net for free (in the sense of stealthisfilm.com). A film that celebrates altruism and true community. It would of course be a movie planned and created on open software only and no-one would benefit commercially, save for the possibility of donating any profits to charity.

What say ye! Is it at all possible, or just zany and undoable? How would you guys go about it, what would you call the movie, would anyone be interested in giving it a go, have an idea or critique ... let&#8217;s hear it!

### Post by uc50_ic4more on 2009-04-02
For what's it's worth, I do audio and video production for a living, and would be happy to film anything within a reasonable drive of Detroit, MI, USA or Windsor, ON, Canada.

I think it might be a good idea to produce something along the lines of documenting the Ubuntu or wider GNU/ Linux experience from the *user* perspective. By that, I mean that it could be an engaging experience to show how casual users (your grandma, neighbour, *you*), educators, business people, web developers and others are using Ubuntu; both as a testimonial to how wonderful Ubuntu and the surrounding community is, but how they dealt with what hardware and/ or software issues they may have had.

I have seen documentaries which explain the underlying history, theoretical and ideological themes of Linux and the broader open source or free/ libre software movement. For those of us that are interested in that, it can be quite interesting. For casual users, however, it is very, very boring; at least these are the results of my informal focus group consisting of friends, family, neighbours and those unable to flee. ;)

Perhaps, then, showing real people (not to say that us computer folks are not "real") whose experience - or lack thereof - with computers may relate more closely to that of an non-geek viewer might inspire people to 1) take something of value from the video; either in the form of an elevated understanding and involvement in Ubuntu or a general inspiration to become more deeply involved in the community, and 2) to spread the video virally or in person to the people around them.

I wonder also if it'd be appropriate to profile some of the people behind Ubuntu as people more so than developers. This may underscore the concept of *community* behind Ubuntu.

In summary, I think it'd be cool to produce a film that features people talking about how they use Ubuntu, and why they love it. I think that video should provide real-world users with a good grasp of the benefits of F/LOSS in practical, everyday terms, and should even, if it could be done so without turning the "nerd" knob up too high, feature some screen cast-looking footage of an installation and basic usage.

If that seems like a good idea, chime in her and let me know. I could begin by talking to the converts I have enlightened to get some comments and short interviews from some computer illiterates. I could also perhaps talk to some members of the local UNIX User Groups in the area to see if there are any other people who'd make good viewing. I could even post the full resolution footage somewhere to get us rolling.

Thoughts?
