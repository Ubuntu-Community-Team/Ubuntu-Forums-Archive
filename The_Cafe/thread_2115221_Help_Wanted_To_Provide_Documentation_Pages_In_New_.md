I think most people land on the individual wiki pages through google searching for their problem, so most people will never see the alphabetical index.  I agree completely, it takes a long time to load 4,000 links, but most people will not see that page.  You have to go to the very bottom of the community support page and click on Title Index.  So, although it takes a long time to load, rarely will people go there.

Your NewDocs should include the top 10 that I have listed--and probably alread does, but also the Quick versions--which don't have as many page hits, so some searching is required.  So weeding out the old pages--which marking for deletion is already an option, but including the Top 10 pages on your NewDoc's page would be helpful.  We have the data, we might as well use it.

Here's another example of Official Documentation and 14 clickable elements:  (It could be simplified some, but it's well laid out.)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/12.10/ubuntu-help/index.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/12.10/ubuntu-help/index.html)

If nothing else, the Alphabetical Index page needs some simplification so that you don't have to load all 4k links at once.  This can be simply handled by grouping letters such that you have 12 groups, then each group will have about ~330 elements, still a huge number, but quicker to load.  The real question is to group the existing 4k links into an 20/80 split, taking into account duplicate links across distros and categories--for instance the USBboot that I highlighted earlier.

One could take the Page Hits, pull them into a spreadsheet, identify the Top 20% (based on simple page hits divided by total page hits), then search the pages for those Top 20% and group the duplicates.  That might be useful.

Then you could make a page with the Top 20% that would capture 80% of the user's search needs.

It's interesting, when doing a google search for someone else's problem on the Ubuntu Forums, I often find private wiki's and blogs or linuxquestions.org as the top hits within google.  I often have to go to page 2 or 3 before I find an Ubuntu wiki page--Official or Community.  And I don't know why that is!

### Post by BlinkinCat on 2013-02-13
Hi all,

Well this morning the Wiki went though a major change inasmuch  that I completely changed the numbering system from a total page system to an individual page numbering system. I consider that this action changed the Wiki from being a white elephant into a very user friendly  system. For those who haven't caught up with the latest Wiki it may be found here -  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NewDocs](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NewDocs)

There are a number of advantages in having the pages individually numbered. Not least of which is the fact that renumbering potentially hundreds of pages just for the sake of a small number of changes was ludicrous. I had envisaged myself doing this updating but it  is I believe unreasonable to expect others to do it. And after all it is expected that Wikis should be able to be updated by anyone. 

There is also a further advantage that I believe allows the Wiki to be a good referral tool. For example it is so much better to send an end user to page V9 rather than V624 as it was before the change.

There have only been a couple of other changes such as adding the "Forum Wiki Team" page  and also adding page "AAA" this being an alphabetical list of the top 20 page hits. The latter was suggested by tgalati4.

So there it is, the current database I believe has been established to be adequate, however if there is anyone who has any deletions, alterations or additions just let me know.  It is now a breeze to only alter one alphabetical page at a time. (lol) 

Well I have rambled on enough so thank you and cheers - :)
