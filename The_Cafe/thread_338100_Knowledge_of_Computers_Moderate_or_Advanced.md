### Post by marx2k on 2007-01-14

I then went through DOS, Windows 3.11, 95, 98, etc...

I would say my main computing history has been on the Amiga 500 and DOS 6.22

The thing I really love about Linux is its' community base and it reminds me of the closeness I had with my Amiga as being the 'alternative'.  These forums, and Linux obsessiveness in general reminds me of forums for the Amiga and AmigaOS and titles like "Where have you spotted the Amiga?" and it would be spotted rendering weather forecasts on some news channels, giving airport listings, etc.  It's always fun not being the mainstream and having a feeling of constantly 'coming up' to it.

It becomes really intuitive in a way that I didn't think it would since when you first come into it, the file system and everything going on within it seems really archaic. 

I think the main point is to spend a LOT of time tweaking, configuring, compiling from source, fixing, etc... then one day it just... clicks.  Of course, many people don't have the same obsessiveness about it as I (and most of you) do.
