I'm downloading Openoffice.org 3

...So I got to thinking - big business plus opensource - maybe they're stronger together than they can be apart. OpenOffice is an open-source project that was begun by and is supported a large business. 

Chromium OS is an open-source project that was begun by a large business. 

Ubuntu is supported by a private company too. 

What does the open source community offer to big business? Lots of free code and product support!

What do large businesses offer to the open source community? A practical approach to "let's get this software in the hands of as many people as possible", and a way to fund developers who otherwise might quit making open source software and, well, do something else to support themselves.

What other helpful or not-so-helpful things come out of the connection between big business and free open source software?

You can read my sig and get a gist of what Oracle noted. Of the leading Open Source projects, most (around the 96% and higher mark) of the code implemented is by the corporation itself. As an example, here's the commit chart for OpenOffice.org

[http://www.gnome.org/~michael/images/2008-09-29-overall.png](http://www.gnome.org/~michael/images/2008-09-29-overall.png)

In reality the leading projects like Firefox have a paid staff that performs most of the programming. Community code contributions are in fact minor.
