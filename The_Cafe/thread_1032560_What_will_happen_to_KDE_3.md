### Post by Erik Trybom on 2009-01-07
It seems to me that most distros, for the time being, either ship with 3.5 by default or let the users choose. This list includes (but is not limited to):

OpenSuse
Debian Testing (soon to become stable)
PCLinuxOS
Mandriva
Gentoo
Slackware
Arch (via KDEmod)

The only major exception I can see is Kubuntu, who recommends KDE 3.5 users to continue using Hardy. 

I think KDE 3 will be around for quite a while. There are many distros out there who prefer stability over bleeding edge, and those will continue to ship KDE 3 until KDE 4 is good enough. I don't expect that to last more than 1-2 years however. A fork would certainly be possible, but I think by the time this becomes necessary KDE 3 will not have enough supporters to maintain it.
