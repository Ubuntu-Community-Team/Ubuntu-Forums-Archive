### Post by earthpigg on 2010-07-04
i never really "troll", as i understand the word to mean.

but i'll sometimes assume a set of beliefs that are not my own. not to elicit emotional turmoil (which is trolling), but to make logical fallacies more apparent. 

eg:
someone else - "ubuntu is better at everything than windows."
me - "yup, i agree. i thought i liked CoD4 when playing on windows, but playing it with WINE on Ubuntu... wow! better framerates, less buggy, and a heck of a lot more fun! ubuntu is indeed better at ***everything*** than windows."

i like satire.

not to get anyone hot and bothered, but to point out that "everything" is a pretty strong word to be using in this context, in that example.

do we/you-the-reader consider that a valid distinction, or no?

and, generally, the less serious the forum the more likely i am to do that. never in the support area, for example, and a strong 'from time to time' here in the cafe.
