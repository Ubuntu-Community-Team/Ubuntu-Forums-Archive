### Post by handy on 2010-08-03
If the notebook is old enough to have pulled in plenty of dust, you might want to remove the keyboard & give it a clean inside. I find compressed air to be a brilliant way to do this. Be sure to stop any fans from being spun by the air though as you can cause damage if you don't.

That could solve your problem. Alternatively, if you have a fan that is not spinning as fast as it should (which can be impossible to tell by observation) you may need to replace it, or a good trick is to remove it & soak it overnight in very light machine oil (sewing machine oil is ideal, diesel fuel will do the job too).

Best I can do for you at this distance.

Good luck. :)
