### Post by trinux_bc on 2011-08-09
I'm just finishing that degree so if you've got questions you can pm me. You should really consider UBC, in Vancouver Canada unless you're looking for an online program. Lots of Americans in the program, about a quarter to a third of the students, and they all came up because the tuition is way cheaper. It's ALA approved and well known in the US, a good chunk of the job postings through the school are in the US.

I wouldn't say there are no jobs, though if you're looking to work in a traditional library you should be prepared to live in smaller towns, that's where most of the job openings are. On the other hand, there are lots of jobs in Records Management and Information Management which is my focus, it's just public libraries that aren't so good these days. Everyone I know who has graduated in the last couple years from a library program has found full-time work within a few months of grad, all over Canada and the US.

trinux_bc

### Post by trinux_bc on 2011-08-09
If you're looking for online programs than my advice would be to go with which ever one is cheapest. LS isn't like most fields, it means absolutely nothing where you go to school as long as it's ALA approved. Also, most library schools have access to many of each others online courses, right now I'm in one based on UBC that has two Texans, one from Portland, one from Pittsburgh, and I think someone from New York. 

I know you said you're not really into computers, but I will say that one of the few high demand areas in libraries is for systems librarians. Basically they need people with really good tech skills who are willing to work for librarian salaries. You can see why that would be hard to find.

trinux_bc
