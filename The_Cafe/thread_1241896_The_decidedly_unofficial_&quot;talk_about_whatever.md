### Post by thatguruguy on 2010-02-15
Speaking of apples, here's a tasty recipe of pork chops with apples:

Ingredients: 
4 pork chops (I used boneless) about 1/2"-3/4" thick. 
1 large or 2 small-medium onions (I used 1 large Vidalia onion) 
1 large or 2 medium apples (I used 2 small-to-medium yellow delicious) 
Dijon mustard 
seasoned salt 
fresh-ground pepper 
cooking oil 
vermouth (or dry white cooking wine, if you prefer it) 
 
Season the pork chops with some fresh-ground pepper and seasoned salt (just a pinch; you don't want them to end up too tough) and coat them with dijon mustard. Then put them in a zip-lock and place them in the refrigerator. Leave them in there for at least an hour. 
 
When it's time to cook, heat some oil in a large pan over medium-high heat until hot. While the oil is heating, cut up the onion and the apples, leaving the skin on the apples. (I cut each apple into 16 pieces, because it's easy.) Then dump the onions and apples into the pan and cook them for 2-3 minutes while stirring until the onions start to look cooked (by which I mean they start turning translucent; you'll know it when you see it). Remove  the apples and most of the onions from the pan, place in a bowl and set them aside. 
 
After adding another tablespoon (give or take) of oil to the pan, put the pork chops in. If you want, you can season them a little more with some more pepper as they sit in the pan. Cook them until done (approximately 4 minutes per side), turning after 4 mintues. 
 
When the chops were done, turn the heat down to low and put the apples and onions back in. Throw in a splash of vermouth (if desired), cover the pan, and let it simmer for about 10 minutes (turning the chops and stirring everything at the half-way point).

Serve with mashed potatoes. Seriously good chops.
