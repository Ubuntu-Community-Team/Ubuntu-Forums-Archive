### Post by MaxIBoy on 2009-12-02
This has been discussed to death. +1 means "I agree." If no one is allowed to say something that hasn't already been said, no  one will say much, because a big part of a functioning community is  agreement. If we're not allowed to agree, everything will grind to a halt. It also defeats the ability to reach consensus. 

Imagine this: Person A expresses his opinion, person B expresses her opinion. 99% of people agree with person B, but because agreement is frowned upon, the thread ends right there and both people get an equal say. Even though clearly person B deserves a greater say. 

So unless you propose that I plagiarize posts whenever I agree with one, "I agree" is here to say. 

I think it's a good sign that something like "+1" exists. It means we agree enough that an abbreviation needed to be invented. That's a sign of a healthy community of more-or-less like-minded people.
