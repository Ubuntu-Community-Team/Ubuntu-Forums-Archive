### Post by fifth on 2008-09-08
I'm not a huge fan of Lewis Hamilton, but think the ruling made to penalise him is wrong and very unfair. Stumbled across this petition (don't think these petitions make the slightest bit of difference,  but I've signed anyway :) ) if anyone else cares to sign.

[http://www.petitiononline.com/belgp08/petition.html](http://www.petitiononline.com/belgp08/petition.html)

> To:  FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile)

We the undersigned insist that the FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile) reconsider the decision made to add 25 seconds to the finishing time of Lewis Hamilton (McLaren-Mercedes) in the Formula 1 Belgium Grand-Prix (07th September 2008). We believe the decision that has been made to be wrong and strongly urge you to revert back to the original final classifications. The rules state that Lewis Hamilton had to give position back to Kimi Raikkonen, which he did. He then freely, and through driving skill alone got position one back.

Sincerely,

### Post by TomMK on 2008-09-11
Not wishing to be apathetic, but I don't think it'll have any effect. The FIA won't reverse it's decision.

The problem is the difference between "gaining an advantage" and "gaining a position". They are usually taken to mean the same thing, but in this case they were not, which is why everyone is outraged about the penalty.

I cheered louder than anyone when he won the British GP, I was there. But I agree with the fact he gained an advantage (but not a place). Now we need to sort out the rulebook, and clear up exactly what "advantage" means.
