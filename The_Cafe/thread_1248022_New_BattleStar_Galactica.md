I liked the original BSG and I had an open mind about the new BSG; I wasn't too bothered about changing the character's gender.

I thought the new Battlestar Galactica stunk in several ways. It stinks as something with the BSG name, and it stinks as science-fiction, and it stinks as a TV show, and it stinks even as a set of CGI sequences. So many things in the show made so little sense, especially the way the characters behaved (completely unbecoming of their role on the ship, usually).

The more hardcore original series fans didn't start disliking the recent series when they heard of Starbuck's gender change. Richard Hatch (from the original series) had campaigned for years to get a Battlestar revival on the TV or at the cinema. He even produced a trailer, and I believe either a draft script or a plot synopsis. These were shown to limited audiences and had much critical acclaim. A studio bought the rights and buried it.

Then the TV studios decided to make their own Battlestar Galactica, with no input from Richard Hatch and using none of his ideas. The man who'd kept Battlestar alive all those years was treated with utter disrespect. Can you blame the fans for wanting to dislike the new series?

Good luck to the movie, let's hope it progresses faster than the Battlestar Galactica fanfilm. Glen Larson == good sign. Bryan Singer == bad sign. The word "re-imagining" == bad sign too. But I hold out some hope.
