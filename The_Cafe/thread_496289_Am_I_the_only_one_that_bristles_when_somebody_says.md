### Post by Atomic Dog on 2007-07-09
OK.  I'm a 38 year old guy...I'm a network manager by trade.  I drink beer, I love sports, have been married for 17 years, have a nice house, pets,  ride sportbikes and dirtbikes, camp -lets face it, I do a LOT of outdoor activities.  I'd like to think that I'm about as regular as they get...

...yet people that I work for will say "you're a computer geek!"  I really bristle at that.  A computer geek, and I have a good friend that is, is somebody that would rather sit in front of a computer all day and night instead of getting out and doing something.  I have nothing against that, but I hate it that people think all people that know more about computers than how to hit a power button is a geek.

I find most computer enthusiasts and experts are just the opposite!  Most are quite outdoorsy and have lots of hobbies.  I find software engineers, programmers, network admins and the lot (the ones I know and hang with) really like to get away and do stuff that doesn't involve a computer or wireless connectivity.  Am I wrong or did I just happen to fall into a unique crowd that does?
