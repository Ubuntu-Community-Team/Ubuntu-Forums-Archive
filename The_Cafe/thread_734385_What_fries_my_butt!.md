### Post by Nathan_M on 2008-03-24
What often irritates me is when a newbie asks a question, and gets scared off by loads of responses that use technical terms they're not going to understand. Newbies don't know what aptitude is. Or where to find xorg.conf. Or anything like that.

When I answer questions for newbies, I do it click-by-click ("First, click Applications > Accessories > Terminal", then next time I can say "Open a new terminal window as you did in step 1", and so on). Don't worry too much about explaining why you're doing each step unless it's a really simple explanation... most people will follow the steps and learn as they go along.

I think a lot of people get scared away from Linux because they try it out, ask a question in the linux community (in general, not just here), and then don't understand the responses they get. And it frustrates me when I see this happen.
