### Post by H.E. Pennypacker on 2007-05-06
I am not so sure this case does all that much, because the obviousness test has always been around.  That does not stop patent holders from suing others.  

A former professor of mine is a patent attorney, and he was perfectly clear about there being companies that exist only to sue others for patents.  The patents they hold may not actually be worth that much, but suing someone will generate considerable money.  This, of course, has nothing to do with genuine protection of patents.  Generally, these companies will buy patents from an inventor, and they maintain them for as long as they need, as long as they need to find someone to sue.
