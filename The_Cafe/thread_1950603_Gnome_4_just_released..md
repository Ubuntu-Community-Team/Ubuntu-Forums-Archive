### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-04-01
Eh, Unity is being popularized that way more than Gnome 3 is, but it's fairly clear that Shell was made to be tablet-friendly. (Bigger window borders with only one control, huge click targets, etc.) And Gnome *is* looking to reduce menus in future applications. The bit about the shut down procedure seems to be a reference to holding Alt to shut down the computer in one step in Shell. I don't know what he's on about with the keyboard accelerators, since they're still "power user" features most people never notice in either system, but both Shell and Unity require typing. I don't think there are any clear swipes at Unity specifically.

Incidentally, the HUD is meant as a complement to the menus until it's redesigned to absorb the other menu functions it doesn't provide. They're not "getting rid of the menus for HUD." They'll coexist until (or unless, rather) the HUD becomes a very different thing than it is now.
