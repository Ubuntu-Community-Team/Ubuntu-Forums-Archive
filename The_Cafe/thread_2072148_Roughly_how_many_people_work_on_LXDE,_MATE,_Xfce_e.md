### Post by ssam on 2012-10-17
the site ohloh gathers stats from revision control systems.

[http://www.ohloh.net/p/mate](http://www.ohloh.net/p/mate)
[http://www.ohloh.net/p/lxde](http://www.ohloh.net/p/lxde)
[http://www.ohloh.net/p/xfce](http://www.ohloh.net/p/xfce)

note that the figures should not be taken as absolute truth. i am not sure if those counts include the applications or libraries associated with those desktops (or whether you would want them to). they count based on who commits a patch not who actually wrote the patch. in some projects every commit is public, in others devs do large amounts of work in a private branch, and then commit it in one go. ohloh may not realise that jbloggs and joebloggs are the same person, and so may count as multiple developers (developers can create an ohloh account to fix this)
