### Post by aysiu on 2008-05-24
I think you should break down the costs for yourself at least.

[list][*]How much is the domain name registration per year? [*]How much is the web hosting per year? And if you're not a reseller (i.e., your host and maintain your own servers), how much money do you spend on server hardware and how many hours do you spend maintaining the servers? [*]How many hours did you spend on the initial design of the site?[*]How many hours do you spend updating the site?[/list] Ultimately, in this sort of business, time is money.

Whether you decide to be a loss leader or not, you should have a basic flat fee for the cost of the domain and hosting and then charge per hour for changes made to the site.

### Post by nexxus07 on 2008-05-24
While we are just starting ourselves we sell mainly Typo3 websites. The initial site creation is usually quoted in a range based on our evaluation of the size of the project in hours. We charge 30 CAD per Design/hour and 40 CAD per Typo3/hour. I know that prices for Typo3 of 85$ are normal but we live in a small community where people cannot spend a lot on web design :-). Usually we spend quite some time on our quotes and they are very detailed but it seems customers really like transparency. It also is to your advantage if the scope of the project all of a sudden changes. The last quote we presented they showed us the other quotes they got from another big agencies which had a list of 3 points and just round numbers. They definitely felt more comfortable with our detailed quote.

The hosting subject is more difficult at least in our case. Most customers know about cheap hosting at bluehosts or with the big ones so don't expect to make big bucks on the hosting alone but rather explain your price because of the services you offer. If you have relatively simple web sites with php html it's probably not worth managing your own server if you want to stay profitable. While it is fun it's a lot of work and you have the responsibility. I would take your real costs of the hosting add a one time installation fee + the anticipated hours of maintanance. In your case a 25 p.m. that would be 300 a year, minus real hosting costs of lets say 60$ which would leave you with 240 divided by your hourly rate.

In our case we were not able to find a good host locally for Typo3 since we are using advanced things like ImageMagick which are not present on mass hosting solutions, so I'm currently trying to setup a dedicated server myself. What a pain and good business for O'Reilly. The thing is you got to think about mail server, dns, backup, security, hw failure. I don't expect it to be profitable for the next two years so personally I would advice against it unless you are sure to have more than 30 clients a year or you are really adventurous.
