### Post by diablo75 on 2010-02-26
I'm kinda of surprised the center of the conversation about a Darknet (which I'm assuming you're meaning a *wireless*, ad-hoc/mesh network/IEEE 802.11s network) is about the causes of piracy and concern over porn trafficking.  Granted, these are valid concerns, but you already have the same issues to be dealt with on a much larger scale with the Internet as it is today.  Darknet's, at least in America, aren't really necessary because we have a Constitution that grants rights to free speech and privacy.  There are a lot of nations that are forced to use filtered, government sanctioned access to the Internet and the people don't like it that way.

So a Darknet, if you were ever to see one crop up en mass somewhere, it wouldn't happen because people suddenly wanna share child porn and pirated copies of Windows but more likely because they're trying to bypass dubious government censors.  Or because it's a cheap way to build a network from scratch if you don't have one already in place to work off of (see the One Laptop Per Child project; all their laptops create a mesh network.)
