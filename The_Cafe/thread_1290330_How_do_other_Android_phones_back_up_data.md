### Post by skillllllz on 2009-10-13
Android phones store all of your photos and music on the SD card. You can simply copy all of the files directly off of the SD card on to your PC. When you plug the USB cable directly into the Android phone, Rythmbox and F-Spot also allow you to transfer your data to/from the PC.

As far as updates go, the devices receive updates over-the-air (aka OTA). The device downloads any available OS updates in the background and then prompts you to apply the upgrade.

Apps are typically downloaded and installed OTA via the devices built-in "Android Market": [http://www.android.com/market/](http://www.android.com/market/)

Updates for apps are also provided  via the Android Market; the user receives an alert that there are updates available and chooses whether or not to install them.
