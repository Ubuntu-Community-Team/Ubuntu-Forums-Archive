### Post by Georges_Bouchard on 2015-05-25
Another fiasco trying to find a replacement for gmail is Startmail.  It's a paid service that seemed promising.  The problem I had with it is that first of all it is very slow.  Perhaps it is a browser app like Tutanota.  I don't really know.  What I do know is that I had extreme difficulties with the Contact List imported from Google.  For one thing, the list contained around 800 email addresses Google had captured over the years.  Somehow Startmail would merge a User Name with an incorrect email address.  Gmail did not do this.   Another issue was that that if an original sender had just used an email address, but no User Name, Startmail then assigned the name, "Unknown" to that particular email address, and there were between 300 - 400 of these "Unknowns."  It made using and managing the contacts list completely unmanageable.  The list of "Unknowns" was long, and Startmail was slow.  Supposedly you could search, but over 300 Unknowns made that somewhat impractical. Moreover, Startmail uses two Contacts Lists.  One for outbound, and another for inbound.  Each has to be edited separately. I think possibly contacts in the outbound list were being intermingled with the inbound list.  It's hard to say as I could never really identify the exact problem as the merged names never appeared on both lists in the fashion they were merged in the actual email.  I wish Google had more respect for privacy and security.  It's difficult to find a viable replacement that is private, secure, and genuinely easy to use.
