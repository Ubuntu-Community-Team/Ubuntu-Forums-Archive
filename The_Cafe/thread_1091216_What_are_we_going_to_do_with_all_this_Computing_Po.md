### Post by MaxIBoy on 2009-03-09
> **Ioky said:**
> But why you need to care about any of that as long as the games run perfectly? Daikatana would run perfectly on my computer, but that doesn't me it's not a crap game. Similarly, Quake 3 runs perfectly on my computer, and it's loads of fun, but it still doesn't show off my computer at all. > Plus you can't really compare thing just by their clock rate. A 256bit 500MHz CPU will run much faster than a 32bit CPU running at 3GHz. Untrue. Assuming otherwise identical architectures, a 256-bit CPU clocked at 500 Mhz will be just about 1/4 as fast as a 32-bit CPU at 3 Ghz (not 1/6, but 1/4, as the extra registers would have some benefit.) However, it would be able to access 2^224 times as much memory. In other words, a 256-bit architecture would be a little bit faster because of the extra registers (ultra-fast RAM built right into the CPU,) and it would have a theoretical maximum RAM size of 105312291668557186697918027683670432318895095400549111254310977536 terabytes. It would also run much hotter and be difficult to construct, plus it would have latancy isssues due to having to be constructed in a larger size.


Clock speed is not an accurate measurement of CPU power (unless you're comparing the same CPU at different clock speeds,) but RAM is standardized enough that clock speed is accurate in measuring its performance.
