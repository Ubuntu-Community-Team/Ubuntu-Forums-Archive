> **t0p said:**
> I don't really understand what is the target of the OP's tirade?

I got the immediate impression from the rant that the target was "Linux snobbery." The "geekier than thou" crowd looking down their digital noses at simple folk who prefer to run applications rather than running the operating system.

It is the snobbery that accuses us simple folk of "not contributing" to Linux, because we "merely" *use* our computers rather than become Linux-certified Master Technician/Engineer Supergeeks.

[SIZE="6"][COLOR="Purple"]Contribute?? I thought Linux was free![/COLOR][/SIZE]

But I *do* contribute, in every little way that I can. I share Linux with family and friends, I blog about what a difference Linux has made for me, hugely *simplifying* the computer experience (I'm a simple boy. I need simplicity. Simple is awesome). I donate money when and if I can. I bought some Ubuntu and Linux swag and bling for my 'puter and I wear my Ubuntu hoodie and participate in these forums. But I'm not a geek and don't really want be a geek (although I must admit it has been surprisingly easy and fun playing customizing and experimenting with Linux).

I place a very high premium on ease of use and simplicity and beauty. As I and others have found ourselves saying over and over again, **I prefer to run applications. Not my operating system.** And that is why Ubuntu is so awesome. It lets me just do the things I bought a computer to do.

Supergeeks have every reason to be proud of the awesome work they do! I admire supergeeks. But supergeek, wonderful though you are, please don't be a snob. Not all of us have the same gifts and skills (and the world would be a dull place if we did). And y'know what the funniest thing about all this is? **It took the work of many Supergeeks to make the user interface simple enough for us ordinary common folk.** Well Supergeeks, you've succeeded! Thanks! 

Instead of chiding us for *using* it, you should be proud. We're certainly proud of you.
