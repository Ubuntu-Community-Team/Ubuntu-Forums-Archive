Apparently a chatbot called Cleverbot has passed a Turing test. The test version was more powerful than the online version (did more runs over it's conversation database) but you can test Cleverbot out here
[http://www.cleverbot.com/](http://www.cleverbot.com/)

An article about Celverbot: [http://www.newscientist.com/article/dn20865-software-tricks-people-into-thinking-it-is-human.html?DCMP=OTC-rss&nsref=online-news](http://www.newscientist.com/article/dn20865-software-tricks-people-into-thinking-it-is-human.html?DCMP=OTC-rss&nsref=online-news)

From my personal experience Cleverbot produces funny (yet inconsistent) answers, a little excerpt:

(this is from two separate conversations)

> User: You know Linux?

Cleverbot: Yes.

User: What is Linux?

Cleverbot: It's mouthwash that you use in your computer.

User: You know Linux?

Cleverbot: No?

User: Really?

Cleverbot: Yeah...

User: What is Linux?

Cleverbot: Linux is a free operating system designed by Linus Torvalds.

User: So you do know!

Cleverbot: Know what?

User: What Linux is.

Cleverbot: What is god?

User: You are asking me?

Cleverbot: What is my prize?
