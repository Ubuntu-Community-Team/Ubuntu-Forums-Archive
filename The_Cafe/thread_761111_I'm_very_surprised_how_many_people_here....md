### Post by BlaAccount on 2008-04-20
...actually BUY (or used to buy) software instead of just pirating.

Basically, where I work and even with regular folk I deal with, very, VERY few people actually buy software - they're much more inclined to torrent it or copy it or obtain it elsewhere. I'm talking commercial stuff like Windows, Office and Photoshop, and in particularly a willingness to pirate the software instead of using free alternatives when appropriate.

My surprise is how, at least in the Ubuntu forums, how many people talk about how Linux allows people to avoid paying for Windows plus extra software. The thing is -** people weren't going to be paying for the software anyway!** If I ever mention about having purchased a game, I'll get funny looks as if I was a retard for not having just downloaded the game instead.

How does one change a society's perception and redirect them towards either demanding sensible pricing of software, or TRULY free software, if piracy is considered not only acceptable, but perfectly normal? How can Ubuntu break into the market if such perceptions are so ingrained?

Now I can make my own change in the way I deal with software of course, and I have, in a morally and legally appropriate fashion. But whenever people in the forums say how expensive Vista or Office is, I shake my head because I can't understand how people are so naive to believe others are actually BUYING software still. Cost has no merit when the damn thing is going to be pirated in the first place.

BTW - I'm referring to end-users here, not businesses. Business still has to deal with legit version of applications *cough*forthemostpart*cough*, but the folks with their own machines aren't gonna care. I don't see what can be done to change this perception.

I guess my point is that whenever people here say how they saved money by not having to buy software they might have had to get when using Windows, I wonder where these people live. It's just not normal behavior to BUY software where I am. You'd be mocked if you did.

(Disclaimer - I'm a regular poster with a temp account since I don't want to admit to my own piracy on my rather respectable account. Call me a coward if you wish. :) )
