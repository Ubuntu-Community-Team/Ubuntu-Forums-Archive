The line is "blurry" at best. Usually only Gnome, KDE, XFCE and LXDE are referred to as DE. They all come with WM and then a ton of extra apps. The rest are called WM since even if they come with extra apps, those are too few and/or very basic.

Unity is Compiz WM + special UI (User Interface). You can still use Compiz without the Unity UI. Gnome-shell is WM and UI that are inseparable.

Most of the WM are mix and match (except Gnome-shell).
