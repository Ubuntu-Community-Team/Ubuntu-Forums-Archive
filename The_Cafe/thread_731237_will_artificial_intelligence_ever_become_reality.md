### Post by Darkhack on 2008-03-22
We'll have AI in some simple forms; much like today but it will be improved upon.  AI will be for specific tasks.  For example,

* Weather software to help predict the weather even farther ahead in the future and more accurately.  

* Programs that use cameras to watch a sporting event and be able to determine the winner based on the players movements and strategies that have been used so far.  So a computer can watch a sporting event and then by half-time determine the winner and score at the end.

* Software that can listen to music or the user can input notes and the computer will be able to rank the music.  Random notes that are all over the place would get a low score, while music with a consistent rhythm will get a higher score.  It may even be able to suggest chords or notes.  You play 60 seconds of a song in front of a mic and a computer generates the last 60 seconds for a two minute song that sounds good.  Could also be used for poems or paintings. 

* Improved voice and handwriting recognition as well as language translation that reads more naturally.

* Lie detectors that can be used with just a camera and microphone.  Look for visual cues such as facial expressions.  Pick out suspicious behavior in a crowd.

* Software could monitor an IM conversation and be able to tell whether or not two people are connecting well.  Could be used on dating sites.

* Video games that learn a players behavior and adapt.  Forget easy/medium/hard modes.  The game will adjust itself so that a player will be up against a challenge without throwing the controller at the screen in frustration.  If there are two enemies, X and Y, and the player can defeat X very easily but has more trouble with Y, then X might be put on 'hard mode' while Y on 'easy mode'; thus the two difficulty modes are being merged.

* Software that helps kids learn.  The student can do math problems and if the computer sees that a student is doing well on multiplication but struggling with long division, it will increase/decrease the number of problems for each type on a worksheet.

Most of these examples already exist in the some form, but I think we can see improvements in this area.  The point I'm trying to make though is that AI will be designed to perform a specific task.  You will not see a computer smart enough to fool a human and try to simulate a whole person.  The human mind is too complex.  It would take billions of lines of source code to get anything close.

The best AI program I've come across is named [Alan]("http://www.a-i.com/show_tree.asp?id=115").  If you find anything better than Alan, please let me know.  Despite Alan's impressive amount of knowledge and conversation skills, you very quickly discover that he still doesn't seem life-like.
