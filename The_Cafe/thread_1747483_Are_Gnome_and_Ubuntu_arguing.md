### Post by earthpigg on 2011-05-02
Money is not the only medium of exchange.

Ego is a *vital* component of open source economics.

:D

edit: 

and i do not mean that in a negative way, just as an acknowledgement of what we must consider when we observe this stuff from the outside -- just because some people are 'volunteers' does not mean they aren't acting out of rational self interest. They absolutely *are* acting out of self interest and a desire to be compensated.

That compensation can be 'thank you' emails, acknowledgement on a mailing list, or it can be a sense of self-fulfillment, but self interest is still at play regardless.

If one feels that he or she is being denied just compensation, it is possible one will get upset and emotional - and possibly do other things to *get* that due compensation.

edit2:

another component of transparent product development is that sensationalist commentators see most/all of the dirty laundry. to put it in perspective: if this is ***as bad as it gets*** in the production of the great software we use, lets call ourselves lucky and be grateful. no cancer clusters, for example, that come out of producing industrial chemicals.

Listening to feedback is essential, Shuttleworth should know that most of all. I always saw Linux as a community project pushing the boundaries of computing to further our knowledge and ability. Variation and choice is healthy. It's not healthy however when it involves taking away the end-user's "right" to choice. I can't support a DE which does that.

That's the reason I mention the example of the left-hand menu bar being unmoveable by Shutterworth's own admission. It is philosophically wrong in the face of Linux development. Why should the left-hand menu be under *the Ubuntu logo*? It's pompousness gone wrong. Big egos, small penises?

Lastly, people here are taking offence to my use of a Hammer and Sickle symbol. Clearly it must have different meanings to different cultures. In my mind it's associated with 1984 style control, and the sentiment to it. It's not meant to be derogatory, it's meant to humorously convey a point: Microsoft tried to kettle everyone into a certain way of using their PC's, don't, Ubuntu, go doing the same! The joke says nothing more or less, so don't misconstrue it as such. Freedom and variation are two of the best things about Linux, take that away and you make Linux suck.

But to put this in perspective, I like Ubuntu and I can sympathise with Shuttleworth's position. I would hate to deal with a disorganised organisation and would possibly just do it myself. All I'm saying is, don't let it get to your head, Mr Shuttleworth.
