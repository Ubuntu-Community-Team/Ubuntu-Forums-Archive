### Post by t0p on 2009-09-23
As you're a blogging newbie, I suggest using [Wordpress.com]("http://wordpress.com").  You get your blog hosted for free, a good user interface, and your blog will be in the Wordpress.com "community", meaning that people will be able to find it pretty easily.  Also, Wordpress.com blogs seem to get indexed by Google pretty well, so if someone does a Google search for keywords that you've used in your tags or blog descriptions, your blog will appear in their results.  To see this in action, do a Google (or [Scroogle]("https://ssl.scroogle.org")) search for "i hate hate" or "spider girl of bihar" - [ihatehate.wordpress.com]("http://ihatehate.wordpress.com") will appear in the top results.

So, start off with Wordpress.com. And register the blog with [Technorati]("http://technorati.com"), which will make it even easier to find. If you find that it takes off, and that you get into the whole blogging experience, you can then buy your own domain name and get some paid hosting.

### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2009-09-23
Note: don't self-advertise too much on Fark/Digg/Reddit. The users will eventually come to resent you and everything you submit will get downmodded into oblivion. This happened with a few sites. Once a week is really the MOST you'll want to do for self-advert.

Secondly: if you're serious, REALLY put effort into it. I can't tell you how often I see blogs by people who complain that they can't get any readership that post articles that contain no original ideas and look like they were cobbled together in 10 minutes. If you're a news-oriented site you can get away with that (see: Atrios), but for actual content you need to look at each article like you're submitting it for a college paper.
