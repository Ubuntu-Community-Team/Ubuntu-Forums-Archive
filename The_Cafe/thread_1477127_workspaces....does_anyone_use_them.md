### Post by arvevans on 2010-05-08
My setup is routinely configured for 8 (or more) workspaces.  one runs email, one runs a firefox browser, and one runs the chromium browser.  Beyond that the other workspaces are rather dynamic, depending on what projects I might be working on at the time.  The radio transceiver design group that I participate in is usually up on one workspace.  schematic and PCB editors are on another, and one supports a group of Google Gadget links to on-line tools for electronics and for microprocessor code development.

One thing that makes all this useful is that I label the desktops so switching between tasks is just a matter of clicking on the proper label.

I tried doing all this in a single desktop and using the application names as clickable links, but the names are too long and rapidly ate up the available display space on the application bar.

So, to answer the original question...YES, I do use multiple workspaces, but NO, you don't have to use them.  It is a matter of what you need, what works for you, and whether they are productivity enhancers or detractors for how you use your computer.  Maybe it all comes down to how many balls you can juggle at once (multitasking) without dropping some of them.
