### Post by Bazon on 2011-05-06
Hello!
Since I tried Natty Beta, I spent more time than ever on Ubuntu related sites as this forum or launchpad *(and my girlfriend is getting angry about that... ;-) )*: So many things that bother me in Unity. 

As a good Linux user, I tried to find solutions by myself or report bugs, if there is no solution. And more and more I get the impression, that many things have no solution, as Unity is simply lacking options. Worse than that, this lack of options seems to be not bugs, but "design decisions".
More and more I feel: **Canonical is following a "less is more" paradigm!**
*(Mark Shuttleworth prefers an iphone compared to android btw...: [http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/05/mark-shuttleworth-talks-windicators-changes-for-unity-in-oneiric-and-whole-lot-more/](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/05/mark-shuttleworth-talks-windicators-changes-for-unity-in-oneiric-and-whole-lot-more/) )* 

So as I don't want my girlfriend to get even more angry, I start to think whether Ubuntu is becoming the wrong place for me.

So I ask you:
**What might be a good Linux distribution for someone who can't stand that "less is more" paradigm?**
*(and is user friendly in a way I don't need to spend too much time on it, you know, my girlfriend... ;-) )*

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-05-06
Is your issue with the distro or the DE. Ubuntu has probably the largest repository out there, so this is definitely not "less". If you have trouble with the DE, then check KDE and especially XFCE (as it is the closest thing to Gnome2).

If you want another distro, Debian will carry Gnome2 for at least another couple of years (and who knows what Unity and Gnome3 would look like by then).

Then there is Fedora, easy with large repository.

Then there are more exotic options like Arch and Gentoo, but for those, you will have to pick a DE first.
