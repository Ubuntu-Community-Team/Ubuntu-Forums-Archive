### Post by cariboo on 2009-10-04
I had a Lenovo laptop in the shop a couple of weeks ago, it would shut down for no reason, sometime after 5 minutes, sometimes it would run for hours. The battery was no good, and even on AC the laptop would try to run off the battery. The fix in this case was to remove the battery before plugging in the ac adapter. After doing this the laptop would keep running as long as you wanted it to.

I have to admit, at first I thought it was a thermal problem too. I cleaned the heat sink and replaced the thermal paste just as a precaution.

It isn't very hard to do, there is usually a cover on the bottom towards the left side. there should be about 4 screws holding the cover, remove them and you should see the heat sink. There should be 4 screws holding the heat sink to the cpu, remove them and carefully lift up the heat sink, apply the paste and tighten every thing back up.
