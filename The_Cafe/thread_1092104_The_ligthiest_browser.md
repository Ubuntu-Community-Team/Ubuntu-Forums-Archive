### Post by dannybuntu on 2009-03-10
You know there is a trade off - you get more speed, but less features. The fastest ones have very little features which you will feel in websites with lots of javascript. In other websites, the appearance all looks to weird.

In my experience:

1. 
a. epiphany - mostly compatible with lots of websites, relatively faster than ff
b. kazehakase - also very good, but interface looks 1990ish
2. dillo - very very fast but websites will look like they are from 1990s
3. w3m - terminal web browser, really fast - but all you get is text
4. arora, netsurf, etc - are baby browsers
