### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-01-14
By leaving out other tablets entirely, the graph doesn't even tell us what percentage of the tablet market Apple actually makes up. You can't make claims about Android on the basis of a graph that specifically excludes it.

Admittedly, some iPad owners really do make them their only PCs, but that is, again, not something the graph speaks to. If 50% of the arbitrary category, "PCs and iPads," were iPads, that still wouldn't tell us how many actual users, if any at all, didn't own a Windows PC.

And frankly, Linux doesn't matter. What makes Android special is that it's an open platform on repurposable hardware, unlike an iPad and probably unlike most Windows 8 ARM devices, which will undoubtedly have a similar vendor lock.

What matters about iPad is that, while being a closed platform, it nonetheless isn't Windows. I'd really hoped that Android would take off, but taking on the iPad in tablets isn't really any easier than taking on Windows on desktop PCs. I really *thought* it was going to be easier earlier on, but the same problems are coming up.

Android is still *the* alternative, and for now, that's fine; I'm *much* more worried about the odds of Windows becoming the non-Apple platform of choice for tablets. That's a scenario where Android becomes like desktop Linux at best and locked out of the game entirely at worst.
