### Post by hobo14 on 2010-03-02
Pretty sure that in the US a personal backup of something you own is perfectly legal, but the DCMA makes breaking encryption illegal, which can create some conflicts.

Recording industry execs have said though that they would not pursue instances of breaking encryption for personal backups, only for large scale distribution (can't find a link, sorry).
You decide for yourself whether record industry execs are to be trusted! ;)

So I think the DCMA makes the encryption breaking technically illegal, but morally perfectly acceptable, and the chances of being caught (let alone sued) are nil.
