Not sure why everyone's heated about this comment.  It's true in anything you do in life...if you don't make your own choices in life...someone will make them for you.  Plain and simple.

I personally think as Gates has gotten older, he's gotten a more sobering perspective.   Originally, it was all about making the bucks, and he cut some corners to do so.  But once Microsoft was entrenched, he realized the impact it has, and he really would like to go out with a bang...getting Microsoft to really help forge more future technology.

The reason I say this is because Gates is and always will be a nerd / programmer person first, and a business person second.  Ultimately, after all the bucks are made, he still wants to crank out something cutting edge.  And, he gets the company to do such.  But, they focus so much on cutting edge that they fail to patch up the holes in the dams they've already built.

 
Now, Ballmer...THAT guy you have to worrry about...  He's MBA business-man, through and through.  When Gates wakes up in the morning, his first thoughts are probably "I wonder how the folks in R&D progressed with that faboo new idea they had?"  When Ballmer wakes up in the morning, he probably thinks "I wonder what we can squeeze more money out of?"

A lot of those Ballmer quotes sound like pure, cut-throat business thinking.
