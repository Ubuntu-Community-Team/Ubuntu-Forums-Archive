### Post by Erunno on 2007-08-08
Two reasons I can think of at the top of my head:

1. Mark Shuttleworth is a strong proponent of synergetic realeases. He was one of the speakers on this years aKadamy and pleaded for simultaneous realeases of GNOME, KDE, Kernel, OpenOffice, X and some other projects for maximum marketing.

2. Even if Kubuntu would align there release schedule with KDE there's probably zero chance that KDE 4.0 would become the default desktop environment as even the developers of KDE suggest that it will be mostly a framework release with possible problems concerning stability and completeness (some applications and frameworks will slip the 4.0 release). KDE 4.1 or 4.2 will be most likely more suitable for widespread deployment.

### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-08-08
IMO, staggering the release (especially as much as dragonbite suggested) would hurt Ubuntu as a whole more. Releasing a new version every 6 months across the board allows for a large push and promotional effort in one shot making a big splash. Having the same effort diluted over 4 releases a year (if Kubuntu got it's own 2 releases after Ubuntu) or 12 times (if it was every month) makes it a lot less special getting a new release. I say stick to the 6 month schedule. 

Not to mention there will always be a new package released at X time shortly after a release you can't always keep waiting else you'll be doing so indefinitely and never get to release. The solid 6 month schedule makes it easy to plan around upgrades for folks.

### Post by Jucato on 2007-08-08
Hm... Hasn't this question been asked numerous times in the past, with the same answers?

1. Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, and Edubuntu are all "products" of Ubuntu. It wouldn't make sense if, for example, Novell released its Enterprise Server months after its Enterprise Desktop. And like what has been said, Mark Shuttleworth is a big fan of "synergy" and syncronized releases. So from the corporate and administration side, releasing separately is not likely.

2. All the *buntus have a 6-month release schedule, which was made to sync with GNOME's 6-month schedule (I think Ubuntu is released 2 months after the latest GNOME release). KDE does not have such a consistent, regular, time-based schedule. And even if it did, it's highly unlikely that it would coincide with GNOME's. Releasing Kubuntu separately would cause a very big shift in development schedules and processes, which would hurt Kubuntu more.

3. KDE 4.0 is not reason enough to change a whole release schedule for Kubuntu, if you look at the big picture. We can't always be delaying releases for special cases like these.

4. Kubuntu is known for releasing packages as soon as a new KDE release is announced. So technically there is no problem with getting the latest and greatest KDE versions.

Again, as what has been said in other threads as well, Kubuntu will release KDE 4.0 packages as soon as it arrives. There might even be separate re-spinned CD's for it. Users can just upgrade to KDE 4.0 from their Kubuntu 7.10 systems. It won't be until Kubuntu 8.10 that KDE 4.x (4.0 or 4.1) will be distributed by default, since 7.10 is too early for KDE 4.0 and 8.04 will be an LTS (and KDE 4.0 is not promised to be completely stable or complete).
