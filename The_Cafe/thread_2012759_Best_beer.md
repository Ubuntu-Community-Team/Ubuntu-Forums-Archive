### Post by bouncingwilf on 2012-07-01
Having quaffed beer all over the world for many decades I've come to a few conclusions;

1. With the exception of micro-breweries, none (or very little) was brewed in N. America.

2. So called global brands such as Guinness, vary enormously depending on which continent you buy it (the Dublin stuff is great but Hong Kong's variant tastes like it's been brewed with a a Marine's boot).

3. Good lager, if that is your taste, predominantly originates in the Germanic/Nordic countries + Belgium.

4. For me nothing compares to a traditional English bitter ale such as Young's or Shepherd Neames's  - And serve it at the correct temperature without all that added fizz!!

Bouncingwilf
