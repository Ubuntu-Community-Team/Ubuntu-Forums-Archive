Hi Kevin,

Not too many years ago I called Dell to see if I could purchase a system without Windows pre-loaded, and I made it all the way up to some senior manager before they told me Dell had a contractual agreement with Microsoft that they couldn't sell computers, or at least that line of computer, without Windows preloaded.  Even telling them I had a credit card in hand ready to by a few dozen systems without Windows they still wouldn't do it (I didn't, but figured that was the only way for them to humor my request).

I then contacted another smaller vendor (/me looking through emails to see if I can remember who -- this is about 4-5 years ago) who sold both Windows and Linux systems.  I asked why they had on their site that they "Recommend Windows" when they also sell Linux.  The response was that that they had an agreement with MS that stated to get the discount for Windows they had to put that on their website and ads for the systems though their systems ran well under both Windows and Linux.  

After this I started noticing that most vendors who sold Windows preloaded, even to this day, have "CompanyX Recommends Microsoft XXX".  

So with this it's obvious that Microsoft has agreements with LOTS of vendors to not only sell, but also falsely claim that the vendor or PC would run better with Windows.

I personally stick with System76 (as do you I see) and other FOSS friendly vendors.  I just wish more computer shops in my area (Central Texas) did the same.
