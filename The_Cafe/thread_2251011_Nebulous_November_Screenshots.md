### Post by coldcritter64 on 2014-11-13
> **moebob24 said:**
> I've decided I am going to switch to XFCE. Show me what you guys have to give me some ideas!

> Xfce4 is extremely customisable. Oooooh yeahhh ! So true. :D

A split personality DE with both xfdesktop and Nautilus desktop running off a xfce4 session in Debian Wheezy.

Both Desktops are showing their desktop properties dialogs. Both Nautilus and Thunar are open with their "about" option showing.

The blue xfdesktop is shifted across by half to expose the nautilus desktop below by an easystroke gesture and a bit of scripting.

I use "wmctrl" in scripts in my home folder to manipulate both the desktops. 
A toggle switch script is used either from a panel launcher or an easystroke gesture to switch between desktops, for anyone interested in trying 2 desktops at once the next script will switch between xfdesktop and nautilus desktop if installed. You need both "wmctrl" and "xwininfo" installed to use it.
> ```
#!/bin/sh
#
#
#

TOGGLE=$HOME/.toggle0

if [ ! -e $TOGGLE ]; then
    touch $TOGGLE 
    wmctrl -ia $(xwininfo -tree -root -all | egrep '"Desktop":' | egrep 'xfdesktop' | sed -e 's/^ *//' | cut -d\  -f1);
else
    rm $TOGGLE
    wmctrl -ia $(xwininfo -tree -root -all | egrep '("desktop_window" "Nautilus")' | sed -e 's/^ *//' | cut -d\  -f1);
fi

exit 0
```I like to have the xfdesktop available, love that context menu set up with the applications there as well, then I also love nautilus scripts, so running both works well for me. The xfce4 panels are the clincher though in my decision making, I love how I can access nearly everything I ever use from the nested launchers around the screen edges. You'll notice most of the icons on the three panels have an expansion arrow beside them ;).
