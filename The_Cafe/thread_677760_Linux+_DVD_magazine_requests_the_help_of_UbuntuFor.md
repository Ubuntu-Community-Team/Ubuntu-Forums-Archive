Hello and thank you foremost for wanting to help spread the word on Ubuntu/Linux more.=]

There are multiple how-tos posted on the web w/ screen shots that show your basic LiveCD installation.  ie. [howtoforge]("http://www.howtoforge.com/the_perfect_desktop_ubuntu_gutsy_gibbon")

Samba should be included, as most users will likely still be using Windows in some shape or form(especially converts or people testing). This will give them access to their files w/o having to move/lose their files.  ie.[ubuntuguide]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Gutsy#Samba_Server")

Some may even wish to dual-boot their Windows install along-side Ubuntu until they can make the decision whether Ubuntu will work for their daily needs:
vista: [nighthacker]("http://nighthacker.net/how-to-dual-boot-vista-and-ubuntu-gutsy710/")
xp: [apcmag]("http://apcmag.com/6101/dualboot_windows_xp_and_ubuntu")

novice users may not wish to get into iptables, as it can be very intimidating for someone who doesn't know what they're getting into, even fire starter may be enough to overwhelm them.  I would also mention that Linux in general has very few to no effective viruses that target home users(especially in a more mainstream distro like Ubuntu) and that port-related attacks are few and far being the only ports that are open are the ones currently being used and need to be open by default.  

I would also include the wants that most users are used to on their Windows boxes like [DVD Playback]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Gutsy#How_To_Add_DVD_Playback_Capability")

You may also wish to mention that alot of users won't have access to their Windows applications and may need to look into alternatives.

MS Office ---> Open Office
Nero ---> k3b
Windows Media Player ---> Amarok

etc.
