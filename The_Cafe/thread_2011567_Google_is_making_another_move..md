### Post by Dragonbite on 2012-06-29
Hey, here is one idea regarding Google's entrance to Tablets with Nexus 7:

Apple has been suing Samsung and other Android vendors for a while and these vendors don't necessarily have the means to fight pack with the same ability.  

This is hurting the Android business as well as stressing the partnership with Google.

Now that Google is in on it, they "could" be sued, but Apple isn't going to want to do that because Google has the means and, after watching the Oracle case, good lawyers to take them on (it WOULD be epic though...:lolflag:).

So by getting into the Tablet market they are effectively positioning themselves as a deterrent against Apple and these silly lawsuits.

This is good for Google because if vendors are more willing to sell Android devices readily, they may also be able to keep them up to date or improve Android as well as making some money for Google.

Google can also make money off of their own devices and have real-world feedback and data to improve upon the design with.

Not  to mention, it helps get their view of what the future (cloud) should be like in the hands of more and more people.
