### Post by Tundro Walker on 2008-06-07
You might want to pass on the job.  

a) working on Ubuntu Server is quite different than working on Ubuntu Desktop.  There's more command-line, and usually way more networking tools, config scripts, etc you need to know.

b) if they're just getting going with some servers, they're going to need them up and running quickly.  If you don't have much experience with Linux servers, it might take you longer than they expect.  If they find out your "learning as you go", it might make you look bad, or you could end up setting things up in a bad fashion.  Either way, you could damage your working credibility.

~~~

In the future though, if you're transitioning to other jobs, the most important thing is to talk about how your actions impacted the previous company you worked at.  Too many folks write up resumes or do interviews where they say "I built a database.  I maintained a server.  I walked a dog."  Ok, and...?

It's important to focus on Problem, Action, Resolution.  You want to explain how you solved problems in a proactive fashion which greatly impacted the company.  But don't embellish the truth (too much :))
