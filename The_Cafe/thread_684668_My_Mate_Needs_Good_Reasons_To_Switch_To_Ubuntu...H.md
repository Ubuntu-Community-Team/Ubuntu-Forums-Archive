### Post by k2t0f12d on 2008-02-14
With regard to Photoshop v GIMP, I have found more useful features in GIMP then Photoshop merely by experiementing.  The box selection is more versatile, and shrink wrapping window size to photo size is invaluable for preventing desktop clutter.  Those are the top two that come to mind, and Photoshop may also have those features, but I never saw them.

Besides, GIMP is licensed under free terms and Photoshop isn't, who needs a better reason then that?  GIMP updates with the rest of the system, and isn't always connecting to the internet by itself for updates.  Discounting virus definitions, I have never seen any other Windows programs updating themselves more then Adobe's do.

EDIT: Forgot to mention usablility.  I noticed no difference whatsoever between the two.  Except for the features that I found easier to find and use in GIMP, they might as well have been the same program for how easily I can switch between them.
