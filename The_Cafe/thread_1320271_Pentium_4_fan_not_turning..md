### Post by cascade9 on 2009-11-09
Brushless has no effect on bearings. Apart from Maglev (magnetic bearings) they all have bearings of some sort. 

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_fan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_fan)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brushless_DC_electric_motor](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brushless_DC_electric_motor)

'Seized' doesnt mean that the fan wont turn, its just the the amount of power required to turn the fan is less than its input power. Sometimes you can get a seized fan going again by spinning the fan, quite often its inertia and friction that stops the fan from spinning. Its still a good idea to replace it, even if a push starts it spinning.
