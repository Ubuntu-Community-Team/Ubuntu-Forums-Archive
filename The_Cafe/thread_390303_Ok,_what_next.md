### Post by ciscosurfer on 2007-03-21
> **slibuntu said:**
> So would that offer a whole heap of new packages and programs?It would offer the chance to learn how another package management system works.  In terms of different apps you could try out, yes and no.  It simply depends on the distro, what they use to package their apps, and what developers decide to use to package their apps.  Here's a good link to find RPM apps: [http://rpm.pbone.net/](http://rpm.pbone.net/)

Btw, you can also convert a good amount of RPM packages to the .deb format Ubuntu and Debian-based distro's use via the Alien command.  More on that here: [http://ubuntu.wordpress.com/2005/09/23/installing-using-an-rpm-file/](http://ubuntu.wordpress.com/2005/09/23/installing-using-an-rpm-file/)
