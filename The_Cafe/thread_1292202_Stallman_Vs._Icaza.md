Be careful.

The Microsoft CP does not actually provide legal protections for users uniformly across the world. One should definitely not consider the CP as a form of contractually-binding agreement.

Another problem is the protection offered by the ECMA standard.  A lot of people seem to think this is some kind of get-out-of-jail card.  It isn't.  The EMCA's code of conduct regarding patents is the patent holder of a ECMA standard technology/product should be prepared to issue a "reasonable and non-discriminatory" license.  This must be applied for individually for each licensee.  Funnily enough, Microsoft has not provided details on what this license is or how it may be issued to applicants.

I'm not anti-Mono.  I like what they've done.  But don't believe all the pro-Mono garbage you read.  There are always two sides of the story, and unfortunately in the case of Mono, the issue of protections against patent actions by Microsoft is fraught with uncertainty.  I think it's unlikely that Microsoft will take legal action against Mono users or developers, but there is no firm guarantee that they won't (unless they happen to be Novell's users, thanks to the MS-Novell agreement).
