### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-02-24
Sorry, I should have explained. My understanding, and the BBC's take, was that Canonical is pitching this for very mobile business users. The idea is that your phone is your work PC, so you can plug in "anywhere" and have your PC with you; bring up your desktop PC down in accounting, or over at the branch office in NY, or at the presentation podium in a client's boardroom.

But "anywhere" requires that there's a docking station wherever you need one. Otherwise, you can plug in at your own desk and at home, but you're not guaranteed anything else, and the advantage of having your PC in your pocket is diminished by the fact that you can't actually access it in any of the interesting places you take it.

Motorola's Atrix tried to remedy this problem by including a netbook-style "webtop" dock, but then you've lost all the portability benefits, because the "webtop" dock doesn't well fit in your pocket.

The dock *itself* is irrelevant, really. It's Bluetooth input and HDMI out, so you don't need anything special. But you'd need to have a keyboard, mouse, and display handy.
