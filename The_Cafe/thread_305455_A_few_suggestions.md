### Post by eddie57 on 2006-11-23
First I want to say how much I enjoy using Ubuntu. I have a 4 year old pc and it works vewy well on it. All the progs that i have run work as they are supposed to and i have not had a single system crash:=). Having said that It seems to me that one of the biggest issues trying to get someone to try something new, in other words trying to get someone to come from windows to linux.the act of getting a linux machine online can be a bit of a chore. First off Ubuntu wouldn't recognize my internal modem "which i was expecting". I didn't want to compile a driver for it so i bought an inexpensive external serial/usb modem. In order to get the modem working initially, first I went through system administration/networking clicked on the dialup modem button and tried to configure it with no success. Then i ran the pppconfig utility from the terminal and got it configured through the serial port, but it was awkark getting online that way. Anyway i finally figured out how the repository worked and downloaded the gnomeppp utility which gave me access to the internet through the menu on the desktop. the gnomeppp prog configured my modem through the usb port,and wouldn't recognize the serial port.

The point i am trying to make is that i was successful only because i was paitient, and i like tinkering with the pc. and also i started using a pc when there wasn't a gui. Most people, when it comes to using a pc don't have the patience to take the time to figure things out. If you want people to use Ubuntu, or any flavor of linux for that matter, the act of getting online for the first time has to be made simpler. Ubuntu is a great os, much better for me than xp. I am afraid a lot of people are driven away because certian aspects of Ubuntu are too complicated, mainly getting on the internet, which is what most people use the pc for in the first place.
