### Post by hsweet on 2008-01-23
I've fallen in love with cms made simple lately and have used it for a couple of sites.  [http://misucatskills.org](http://misucatskills.org) and [http://congregationbethhillel.org](http://congregationbethhillel.org).

The menus take care of themselves, it comes with some decent templates you can modify(or roll your own)  and best of all, once I get the thing set up, I can assign local folks to do updates without buggering up the layout:)

There are all sorts of modules you can add as needed.  Photo albums, streaming media, user registration and on and on.

The thing about websites is you don't want to be responsible for every last bit that's on there for free.  There are volunteers willing to update stuff so long as it is not too hard.  You can take care of getting the thing off the ground and teaching it new tricks and let the day to day stuff take care of itself.
