[LIST]

[*] It might just be me, but Ubuntu *feels* more stable than windows somehow...

### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-07-02
I'll cite a personal example.

**Windows**

I downloaded some .mp4's at home on my Ubuntu computer, but didn't have time to mess with them.  So, I flash sticked them to work, and tossed them on my Windows computer to listen to.  I highlight several, and some mp3's, in a file directory, and hit "enter" to play.  Windows Media player opens up, but notices the 1st file on the list is an mp4.  So, it makes a single file play list out of the mp4.  If I want to listen to the other mp4's, I can't just add them to a play list, I have to play each one individually, and let it loop.  I play another mp4, and Windows Media Player proceeds to crash and lock up my computer.   I Ctrl+Atl+Del, but no response.  I reboot.  I assume the file is corrupted, and with a few other mp4's and no way to tell if their corrupt or not, I try each one, locking up my computer 3 more times in order to find the good from the bad.  For the ones that will play, I look for a way to convert the file to mp3.  I don't have anything native on the computer, so I have to get some 3rd party freeware online to do so.  However, because a) my internet is blocked from reputable 3rd party freeware sites (Tucows, Snapfiles, etc), and b) sites I CAN get to are less reputable and have spyware and such in the software, I decide it's not worth messing with.  I decide to open up Windows' native sound recorder, in an attempt to manually record the mp4's that work.  I start recording the first mp4...Windows' native sound recorder stops recording after 1 min, because that's the max limit it will record (presumably to prevent folks from manually recording music off CD's).  I see what file format I can save as...WAV...so it's not even in a format I want anyways.  I decide to scrap the whole idea until I get home to my Ubuntu computer.

**Ubuntu**

I downloaded an .mp4 music file.  I select the .mp4, along with a group of other .mp4 & .mp3's, and hit "enter" to play all of them.  Totem opens and plays everything as a play list.  I want to convert it to .mp3 format so I can play it on my portable player.  I google "ubuntu sound convert", and find out there's 2 options.  1) One of the Ubuntu dev's created a bash script that will convert all mp4's in a directory (and recursively convert into sub-directories) into mp3...just gotta get 2 support files from repo's.  2)  I can get a sound converter from the repo's and do it with a GUI instead.  As an experiment, I try the BASH script, and it works, but the corrupted mp4's don't convert.  No crash, no lock up, instead the CLI just says "corrupted file, can't convert".  I d/l the sound converter from the repo's to see if it can do it, but it can't.  Again, no crash, no lock up, just a message stating it can't recognize the format.  However, since I can play them in Totem, I just play them with the sound recorder going and manually make an .mp3.  No big deal.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

It's little things like that, day in and day out, that continually make me appreciate Ubuntu more, and Windows less.
