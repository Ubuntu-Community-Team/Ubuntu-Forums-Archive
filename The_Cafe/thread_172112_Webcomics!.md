### Post by endersshadow on 2006-05-08
Admittedly, I love webcomics.  I've always been a comic strip fan, and there are some great ones out there.  I'd love to see if there are any new/exciting/fun ones out there that I haven't stumbled across yet, so I ask you: What are your favorite webcomics?

My personal ones are (in order...):
1. [Questionable Content](http://www.questionablecontent.net) - my personal favorite.  You need to read the entire backstory (it's long) to get the new stuff, but it's incredibly intricate and well developed.  Jeph also has a great sense of humor and an uncanny ability to make great dark humor.

2. [Ctrl+Alt+Del](http://www.cad-online.com) - Because every geek loves Ctrl+Alt+Del.

3. [Dr. McNinja](http://www.drmcninja.com) - As the title says. It's a doctor...who's a ninja.  This comic hasn't been around for too long, so it's easy to get into (takes about a half an hour to read all story lines).  Incredibly well written.

4. [Dominic Deegan](http://www.dominic-deegan.com) - A little DragonballZ-esque sans cuteness and a lot better humor and more mature themes.  A great story line, too!

5. [Bilo y Nano](http://tira.escomposlinux.org) - It's a comic vaguely about Linux, and it's in (Castillian) Spanish natively.  There's an [English translation site](http://comic.escomposlinux.org), but some translations lose the meaning of the native writing, so if you can read Spanish, read it natively.

Those are the ones that I check every day/week.  How about you?
