### Post by gnomeuser on 2010-03-21
Ground Control, the first game was life alteringly good, I never tried the sequel since I switched to Linux entirely by then and none of the wine solutions supported the game.

However I am sad to see it never be more fully explored, it was in my mind one of the best and most underrated RTS experiences available with real innovative ideas for it's time (and some which remain).

I am also sad to see the Fallout series having ended. No, Fallout 3 is not a Fallout game, it's the unholy love child of Quake and Might and Magic with none of the charms and touches that made Fallout 2 the best game ever created.

Baldur's Gate I was sad to see stop, I had such a great time exploring the first game. I preordered the second one but having not yet entirely explored the first game I didn't play it when it arrived. Then a friend borrowed it and I haven't seen it or him since... come to think of it that was around the time my Planescape Torment disappeared as well.

I also do quite miss the charm of the original Leisure Suit Larry games. I played the typing versions of the first game when I was 6 with a dictionary in my hand. I learned more English that way than I ever did in any class afterwards. I think this is an underrated learning experience for young children to have such a typing adventure in a second language available. It challenges and lets them explore. I certainly invested more time in playing those games than I did in doing homework (I used to play Space Quest 3 on time - roughly 3h, slightly obsessive I know). I know some people consider Leisure Suit Larry unsuitable for children, but I found it to be a rather unimportant part of it. Still maybe a good murder mystery or something.. go make it.
