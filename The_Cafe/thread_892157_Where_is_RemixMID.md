### Post by SarahKH on 2008-08-17
As it stands Netbook Remix is just a collection of applications that sit on top of a bog standard 8.04 install.  There's no secret source behind them.  Saying that I am running 99% of the stuff in launchpad (minus the xorg fiddling bit) it works pretty good on my tweaked install running on a 4G EEE PC.

I'm not 100% sure what Netbook Remix will end up being personally, as most of the netbooks out there (EEE, et al) have pretty perky processors (Celeron's or Atom's) in them they just lack screen resolution.   I'm also not 100% convinced that a OpenGL based launcher would give more battery life than a 2D display, but hey, I like eye candy and I'm keeping it :)

So on one hand Netbook Remix is out (kinda), on the other hand you can't download an ISO of it and go.  But then if I remember the press releases Remix is aimed at OEM's looking to customise it for their machines rolling off the production lines; so you'll have to play OEM and rip bits off/add bits too 8.04 to do your stuff.
