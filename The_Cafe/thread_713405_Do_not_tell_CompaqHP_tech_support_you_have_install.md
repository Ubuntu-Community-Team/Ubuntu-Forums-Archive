### Post by nutz on 2008-03-02
It will lead to the worst experience of your lifetime.

I just called up Compaq/HP tech support to get information about my laptop because I couldn’t find the information anywhere on their website. I wanted to know what mini PCI-Express cards are compatible with my laptop so I could switch it out for a different one that is more Linux friendly. The AR5007EG that it came with is a great card but it still doesn’t have driver support with Linux 64 bit and I really wanted my wireless back.

The tech support lady asked why I wanted to change the network card and I was foolish enough to tell her it was so I could run Linux and have wireless networking. As soon as I told her that the entire tone of the conversation changed instantly and she informed me that the warranty was now void and I would no longer be able to receive support for it. I asked her why that voided my warranty when my question was about hardware and she said it was because Linux was not supported by them. So I asked her if they would support it if I put Vista back on there and she said that they would. So I told her I was running the recovery software as we spoke to restore Vista on the laptop and then she agreed to provide support. This of course was not true but I had to fib a little it seemed since this made all the difference in the world and I was screwed on support unless this laptop had Vista on it. The whole time she and I spoke she kept trying to find angles as to why I wasn’t deserving support because I had installed Linux on the laptop. I kept reminding her that Linux was now gone and very soon there would be the original OS loaded back on there.

So with all of that out of the way we got back to business and I asked her again which network cards were compatible with my laptop. In order to answer this she had to put me on hold while she asked a senior support technician. After about five minutes of being on hold she came back on the phone and told me there were only two cards it would support. She said one of them was from Verizon and the other was from AT&T…

Haha…

At this point we clearly had a misunderstanding since I was asking about the mini PCI-Express wlan card and she was clearly talking about the 3G Air card. So I spent about 10 more minutes explaining to her what the wlan card was and how it was not related to the 3G Air card. The whole time she kept arguing with me and trying to tell me that they were in fact the same thing. Finally after going very in depth and describing the actual use of each one and where each one was located on the underside of the laptop she conceded that they were in fact different and she had gotten information for the wrong cards. So with that she put me on hold again because once again she had to ask the senior support technician about this.

After almost 10 minutes of being on hold she came back on the phone and informed me that the wlan card was built into the laptop and it was physically impossible to remove it without permanently damaging the laptop. Of course I corrected her on this also and even directed her to a webpage that described the process of changing the wlan card in a laptop almost exactly like mine. I also went into great depth in explaining what a mini PCI-Express card was and how it was a standard slot type used my many laptops for their wlan cards. At this point we were back to square one and she said that Linux was the reason why I could not change my wlan card. So I had to remind her that Linux was now out of the picture and the recovery CD was nearing completion on restoring Vista as the only OS on the laptop. With that all said she fell silent for a few seconds and I could tell I had properly backed her into a corner that she wasn’t going to be able to get out of without the correct information. So she put me on hold again in order to ask the senior support technician about this latest ground breaking development.

I was quickly beginning to lose my temper because it was painfully obvious this lady didn’t have the knowledge needed to answer my question. But wait it gets better!

After more than 10 minutes of being on hold she came back on and explained that she now had the senior support technician on the line with us and he would be taking over the phone call. I felt a wave of relief rush over me after hearing that since I was near breaking point with this lady.

The man introduced himself and informed me that he was the senior support technician and her supervisor. I desperately wanted to ask him why his non-senior or whatever she was support technician was so ignorant as to the product they were charged with supporting but I held back because I needed this guy in a good mood.

So I spent another 10 minutes recapping the entire conversation I had with the previous person and explained in great painful detail exactly what I was looking for. He then informed me that they did not support Linux and my warranty was now void. I then responded by telling him that Linux was gone and the original Vista OS was back to which he responed by saying there were no other cards that were compatible with my laptop. He said the AR5007EG was the only mini PCI-Express card that would work in my laptop. Period. I’m serious, he said that! I asked him why that was the only card that would work and he said because that is the only card that it comes with…

I explained to him once again that I could not use the one that it came with and needed to switch it out for a different one. At this point he reminded me that removing it would permanently damage the laptop and void the warranty. I could clearly see at this point that he was stonewalling me and I wasn’t going to get anywhere with him.

So I asked to speak to his manager and he informed me that he was the manager. I said fine I understand that you are a manager but I want to speak to your manager or whomever it is that you report to. He informed me that he didn’t report to anyone! Yeah he actually told me that. I’m serious! This guy actually expected me to believe he was as high as the totem pole went at Compaq/HP technical support.

I gave up at this point since it was clear this man wasn’t going to be any help either and after being on the phone with these people for almost an hour I was done. So I asked him to identify himself and he gave his first name. I won’t go into detail but let’s just say it wasn't a very unique name. So I asked him for his last name because I wanted to be able to identify this man when I wrote a complaint to Compaq/HP. He said he was not allowed to give his last name. Then I asked him for some sort of agent ID or something that uniquely identified him and he said he had none. I then asked him if there was any piece of information I could use to identify this call because I intended to launch a service complaint. All he gave me was the same number I originally gave to him when he came on the line which he indicated was some sort of call record number. With that the entire call had taken up too much of my day and I was done dealing with these people so I thanked them for…nothing, wished them a good day and hung up.

So in reality there is only one real lesson learned out of this and that is never tell technical support at Compaq/HP that you use Linux! As for the crappy support they provided that was nothing new since they are all ready world renowned for that. Their cheap prices lured me in though so I guess I have my own cheapness to blame.

Just wanted to share that, I also sent a copy of this to their bit bucket/suggestion box.
