### Post by UniverseA7X on 2008-10-07
I'm pretty sure this has been discussed before, and if it has, then this belongs in recurring discussions. 

I see many people here talk about why linux isn't ready for the desktop, why it can't top apple or microsoft in the market, why the upcoming version of ubuntu is so terrible. 

I feel that people may not understand that real people put their time and effort into creating this operating system free of charge and free for everyone to use. Shouldn't we be a bit more appreciative of that? Yes there are problems that need to be addressed, but how many have already been solved? How far has linux come since it's inception? 

Those people who work on this make it possible for people like me to enjoy life and have things easy. Things like browsing the web and checking my email, and getting a paper written on OpenOffice. These tools are provided to me without any strings attached. I feel that these are the most selfless people out there today. These are the people that not only want change, but create it. I know we can't ignore the problems, but why focus on what can't be done and instead, make it done?

And now, your opinions on this. :popcorn:
