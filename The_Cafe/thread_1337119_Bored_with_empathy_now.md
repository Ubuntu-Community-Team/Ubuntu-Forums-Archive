### Post by vlad_novak on 2009-11-25
As a recent migrant from KDE, I found Pidgin as less intuitive and somewhat poorer in options compared to Kopete. I view Empathy as one huge step backwards in the sense of intuitiveness of the interface and richness in its options.

It changes my status randomly.

I cannot change font face and colour in conversations.

I cannot invite other contacts to opened chats.

I cannot figure out anything, I have to google for everything. It is not clear to me how to add or change smiley themes or is there even such a thing. I can't figure out how to install themes. 

Occasionally Empathy just go buggy. Everyone from my list disappears, I have to log off and back on for them to appear. Sometimes, it reports me a account problem that won't stop even after quitting and restarting I found on a forum that 'killall telepathy-butterfly' solves it.

When adding a new contact that is offline at the moment, I get absolutely no info, only my connection is restarted and the account not added. That happened a few weeks ago, I don't know if it was fixed by now.

I use MSN and almost all of my contacts are on MSN or connecting from Windows. I don't want to change the messenger or the protocol, I just want the default messenger that comes with the installation to be advanced and functional. All of these and everything mentioned earlier in the topic seem like minor things that can easily be made or fixed, I don't know why they aren't.
