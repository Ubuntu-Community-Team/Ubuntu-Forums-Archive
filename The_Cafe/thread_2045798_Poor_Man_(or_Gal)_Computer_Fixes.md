I'll get the ball rolling with my no cost cooling solution for my laptop.

[IMG]http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7259/7835508652_b6ffb7f9ff_n.jpg[/IMG]

I scraped off the rubber feet off an old Packard Bell computer tower ( circa 1998 ) which was headed to the dump and stuck them to the bottom of my laptop.  Surprisingly, after all these years the glue holding the feet to the tower was still sticky enough to adhere solidly to my laptop with out the need of additional glue.

My computer now stands a half a cm higher and runs about 7 degrees Celsius cooler than before.

### Post by mips on 2012-08-22
nx9110 laptop was missing 3 of its 4 L shaped rubber feet which are surround by raised plastic so if you take the feet off the raised plastic would scrape whatever surface it was standing on.

I pulled of the remaining foot, removed any glue residue & washed it with warm water and soap. Next I placed the remaining foot on some wax paper and poured candle wax over it until it was covering the entire foot properly. I repeated this process 4 times so I had 4 new moulds. The molds I filled with silicone sealant, let set and now have 4 brand new feet.

### Post by Face-Ache on 2012-09-08
A friend's computer was running really badly, with a CPU fan that constantly ran at full speed. I took a look at it, and noticed that the whole fan/heatsink module was totally loose due to one of the Support Retention Module motherboard clips being broken.

It should have been firm enough for you to virtually be able to pick the whole case up via the fan/heatsink module!  So i replaced the thermal paste, and used a small cable-tie to ratchet down the corner of the fan/heatsink module to the CPU chip.

I hear that the PC has been running totally fine ever since. Cable-ties are awesome  :)

Its possible you are just in an area far away from any broadcast towers, and/or wooded or hilly perhaps, with a weak signal.  In that case amplifiers are required (however, they tend not to work very well in my experience).  Also, having the rabbit ears on the ground floor severely interferes with the signal.  Cars driving by, or people sitting in the room, can interfere with the signals on the ground floor.

By installing them in the attic you can get much better reception and avoid signal interference from passer-bys. I was battling poor signal strength until I moved the antenna directly under the roof (just make sure they are "pointed" in the general direction of the broadcast towers).   I also think its interesting that rabbit ears are actually oriented in a poor direction by their default design, it much better to have them horizontal instead of vertical.

It takes a little effort getting everything set up, but when considering the yearly price of cable or satellite, it's well worth it.  (We're Linux users, putting in some effort to get things how we want them shouldn't be a problem, right?)  I find the major network channels are more than enough for my needs.  I can always rent new movies for a couple bucks from a local grocery store if I want to see something really new (by the way, spell check doesn't like my spelling of "movies", hmm?).
