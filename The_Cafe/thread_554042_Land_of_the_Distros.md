### Post by justin whitaker on 2007-09-18
For me (from my personal experience):

Losing popularity: Mandriva. Used to be up at the top, but several bad choices, and releases, do wonders.

Hardest to Install: Gentoo, at least if you are new to Linux. It is actually very straight forward, just time consuming. 

Easiest to Install: Linspire. Seriously: 4 clicks. Even Ubuntu can't top that. I would say that second would be PCLinuxOS, then Ubuntu. 

Least User Friendly: Gentoo. The community is helpful if you are asking an interesting question, but there is alot of RTFM over there. 

Runner up: PCLinuxOS. Either they are sniping about other distros, or they are telling people to roll a package because it's not one of the 4000 in their repositories. Yeah, that's noob friendly. 

Most User Friendly: Ubuntu. 

Has the Least Amount of Community Help and Support: Mandriva. You don't get alot of help unless you are a Club member...then the stock response is "check the wiki." 

Most Amount of Community Support: Ubuntu. Not really a fair question. 

Best Distro To Learn Linux: Slackware. 

Best Case for Linux is Better than Commerical OSes: Any Ubuntu distro with Beryl running, or Puppy Linux on a fast machine.

### Post by auberginepop on 2007-09-18
I've had have Ubuntu running on a few machines for some time and am more than happy with it.

A couple of times in the past I have given a few "lightweight" distros a look as I have some old hardware and the idea of a stripped down, fast os is always appealing. Although some of these have proved interesting (eg damn small linux) I did not find any of them to be more than a curiosity and certainly nothing I'd install and use regularly...

...until I gave Puppy Linux a go. I downloaded it 10 mins ago. It is already up and running (I am writting this from it) and it even got wireless with WPA running in seconds something even Ubuntu struggled to do on this laptop.

Running from RAM it is lighning quick but the desktop and general feel is far from primitive like some of the other small distros.

I am certainly not going to replace Ubuntu on my main machine but this old laptop, which is used almost exclusively for the web, may well be barking for a while.
