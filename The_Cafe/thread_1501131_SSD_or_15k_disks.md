### Post by Shining Arcanine on 2010-06-03
Practically all of the things computers do today are random in nature and SSDs (especially Intel's SSDs and SandForce based SSDs) excel at random reads and writes. SandForce SSDs are unproven at the moment and while they outperform Intel's SSDs, I recall reading some thing about them possibly getting into a state where their performance is degraded below that of Intel's SSDs.

I think you would be happiest with Intel's G2 SSDs:

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820167023](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820167023)
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820167024](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820167024)

Keep in mind that these are pricy, so you will likely want to use a conventional hard drive for long term storage and only keep the things that you need in the present on the Intel drives.

### Post by andrewabc on 2010-06-03
If looking for pure speed you need SSD.

If doing intensive writing to SSD (you say server/enterprise), then you may want an SLC SSD. They cost ~twice as much as MLC SSD.

As for MLC SSD there are 3 types.
Intel - fast read and random 4k read/write. Slow sequential writing.

Indinlinx (OCZ Vertex) - fast read, ok random 4k read/write, medium sequential writing. Mature firmware as it went through ~10 revisions in 1.5 years.

Sandforce (OCZ Vertex 2) - fast read, fastest 4k random read/write, fast sequential writing. New product a couple months old.

If you want to learn about SSD read "anantech SSD anothology" series.
