### Post by Delever on 2009-06-25
I think we give too much praise to intelligence. Research seems to be moving to understanding that intelligence is precise arrangement of brain functions carefully polished by evolution over millions of years. Is fly intelligent? In it's environment, yes. Near candle or glass - no. Human seems to have much better and more adaptive reality simulator, however, we still can act like flies.

So, it seems, computer hardware is up mainly against time - we gain more ideas how things work by studying, our hardware is getting faster and more parallelized, and we can even construct simulations which can evolve ([genetic algorithms]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_algorithm")).

I think we are up to disappointment. Our created AI is more than likely going to be more logical, rational, and without all those tiny human flaws we are so accustomed to love :)

### Post by monsterstack on 2009-06-25
Supercomputers are supposed to surpass the processing power of the human brain in a couple of years, if I recall. But a lot of people seem to think that you need more than just raw power to achieve intelligence. Maybe we humans are so smart because of the way our brains arrange all of that information. Gosh.

With that in mind, IBM are currently building a fully working model of the human brain. Dubbed [Blue Brain]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blue_Brain") [wikipedia.org], obviously. It's going to take quite a few years to complete, but it will be pretty cool when it's finally done. The real deal will be when it comes to testing the intelligence of such a machine. To do this, they will pass the machine a whole bunch of equations to solve, and then monitor how it goes about doing that. A cold, unemotional logical machine will churn out the data exactly the same way every time. If, however, the machine computes the data differently each round, that suggests conscious decision-making is actually happening. It would be indicative of sentience. Of course, it's much more complicated than all that, but that's the gist of it as far as I can recall. It's ever so exciting, though!

I, for one, welcome our new robot overlords.

### Post by lethalfang on 2009-06-26
> **Delever said:**
> I would say human brain is good at simulating different realities quickly (in other words, good at imagining). Decisions are not so difficult once you have that :)

A mind is capable of making decisions without facts available, i.e., gut feeling, taking a chance, etc. 

Telling a computer to make a decision while missing the slightest data, and it will not do so. Every so-called decision by a computer is absolutely deterministic. Every random number generated by a computer is also absolutely deterministic.

A computer does not make mistakes, and it does not make decisions. Those two traits go hand-in-hand. Computer as a device is named exactly as it does: to compute and nothing more. To build something that will function like a brain will require a radically different device. We cannot even begin to imagine building such a device. The first requirement is to understand how brain works, and we are nowhere close to that. We do not even understand how the simplest organism (bacteria) works, let alone the most complex organ ever evolved.
