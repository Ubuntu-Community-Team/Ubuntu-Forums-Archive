### Post by Dixon Bainbridge on 2008-11-20
> **wmcbrine said:**
> On the one hand, I can see the logic behind this decision, a decision that a lot of formerly-print publications are making. On the other hand, I wonder if they aren't slitting their own throats. In print, just by virtue of being in print, you gain an aura of authority, earned or not; on the web, it's a level playing field between PC Magazine and some guy blogging from his basement.

Print media is bought by one person and usually read by another 20 (on average). So the ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) for one copy of a printed magazine isn't great, compared to printing and distribution costs per issue. People that pick up the magazine to read second hand from the original purchaser don't generally then go and buy a copy for themselves, as they've already read it.

(I used to work in media monitoring and evaluation, so I've seen all the studies done on this. Print media as a business model is dying).
