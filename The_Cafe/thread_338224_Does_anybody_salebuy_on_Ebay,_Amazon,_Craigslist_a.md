### Post by Bezmotivnik on 2007-01-14
> **meng said:**
> Perhaps we differ on the meaning of private then. I consider almost all of eBay "powersellers" and eBay "storefronts" to be privately owned small businesses and therefore private sellers.
Legally, a "private party" is a *non-business*, an individual doing "casual sales on an occasional basis," like someone having a garage sale or liquidating an estate or selling his motorcycle.

At the point your sales are a regular thing and a significant source of income, your status as a private party ceases.  You have become a business and are compelled to file a DBA, get a business license and go through all the rest of the bureacratic headaches involved in starting any small business.

One of the allures of eBay is that it helps people illegally evade the local, state and federal business laws that apply to them.
