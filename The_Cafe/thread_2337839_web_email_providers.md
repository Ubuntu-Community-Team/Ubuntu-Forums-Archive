### Post by mikodo on 2016-09-25
> **mastablasta said:**
> is the free account encrypted?

The free accounts allow for all of this.

                         Protonmail uses a similar to GnuPG encryption with public and private keys, though automates  the process for users. 

I as a user of Protonmail could send you a non-user of it an email un-encrypted or encrypted. My choice there. 

If I send an un-encrypted email and you respond then it remains an un-encrypted communication transmission between us. The servers always though, where the data is held, is always encrypted and not available to Protonmail employees so, it remains secure regardless.

If I send you an encrypted email, and you respond to me, then it is encrypted in transmission both ways.

If two people both have Protonmail accounts, they can choose to always have their communication transmissions encrypted by a one time choosing and never have to make the decision each time again if they want it encrypted always. 

I like and use Protonmail mostly for knowing that my email data is not being mined, like what happens with other Web-based ones.

I choose to use the .ch (swiss) domain as it has more security, rather than .com. At least for now. Swiss and German regulations (laws) may be changing to a more relaxed security model, like much of the rest of the world unfortunately. See the link below. Even if this does happen, the data still is secure as it is encrypted in storage with no way to access what is there. If the Swiss courts do become able to subpoena the data centers data, it is still encrypted and unaccessible.

Hope this helps.

### Post by TheFu on 2016-09-25
From what I've read, never trust encryption that happens inside a web browser.  Sure, it is better than nothing, but if you use gpg on your trusted, controlled system, you don't have to worry about the browser or server-side screwups. 

[https://tonyarcieri.com/whats-wrong-with-webcrypto](https://tonyarcieri.com/whats-wrong-with-webcrypto) - some lite reading on when browser-based encryption makes sense and when it doesn't.

For me, the belief that something is secure when it isn't is much more dangerous than not using encryption and knowing it isn't secure.
