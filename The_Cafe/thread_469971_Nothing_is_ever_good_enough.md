### Post by djchandler on 2007-06-10
Have you considered Darwin, the FreeBSD branch on which OSX is based? You can download it from Apple after signing up for the Apple Developers' Network or some such nonsense. It's free, but I've only tried it under QEMU, which emulates a PII, before I realized you need an **SSE2** compliant processor for it to run. Otherwise, even though it installs, when it tries to boot, you get a kernel panic. :o

At least that way you can get a taste of what to expect before investing in an Intel Mac to run OSX, although I've heard of people running it patched (illegally) on some pretty generic Intel-based platforms, but with SSE3 cpus, including some of the newer Athlon 64 processors. It especially seems to like Pentium D or Core 2 cpus and Intel motherboards of course. You may still be able to find the specs for the hardware reference platform the Intel-based OSX was developed on (pre Core 2). ;)

[www.intel.com/support/processors/sb/cs-001650.htm](www.intel.com/support/processors/sb/cs-001650.htm)
