### Post by exemplarstudent on 2009-07-11
Just throwing it out there...

What if one were to rework a computer down to a low level to be based cheifly on sound instead of on pictures.

At the very least, one could develop a 100% sound-based operating system, which had no monitor, keyboard or mouse, but only a microphone and a speaker.

Then one could make programming language that was totally in sound, everything, totally sound. No visuals whatsoever.

For interface, one could use clicks and grunts and various human sounds in particular order, and when the language got high level, some spoken words.

I think it could work. People become reliant on sight and forget the merit of sound. theres a lot more to sound than people may think at first glance. people are able to memorize hundreds of thousands, perhaps millions of sounds, all carefully filed away, when they learn to speak a language, and a computer which takes advantage of human-sound affinity could prove useful.

any takers?
