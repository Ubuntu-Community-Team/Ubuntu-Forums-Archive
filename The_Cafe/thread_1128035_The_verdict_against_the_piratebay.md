Everyone knows it's not right, and they should pay for your book, BUT, unfortunately, the way our economies work they never will.

It's the way our economic system works and as long as we have this system, we will always have shoplifting. You set a price for your good/service and people have a threshold in which they will pay for it; this is called their utility. If the monetary value for your product exceeds the utility gained by a potential customer, they won't pay for it. 

So, if the customer wants your product, but is not willing to pay say $20 for your book, you have consciously forfeited this market. So to say that you have lost potential sales is bo llocks. In theory; If the total utility underweighs the amount he has to pay, but outweighs the chance of prosectution, they will steal it. And since chance of prosecution on the internet is low, you get a lot of downloading and P2P, HOWEVER, you lose no market because you've already forfeited it; But as we know theory doesn't always play out perfectly, companies do lose market, how much they lose, I believe is blown a little out of proportion.

If you didn't want to forfeit this market, the entire economy would have to run by auction, you'd gain the amount of money the person that has the SECOND highest utility is willing to pay for it. each buyer would pay just below the gained utility for the product, up until the point where the expense to you is not worth the revenue gained from the sale. However, an economy could never run like this for many reasons.
