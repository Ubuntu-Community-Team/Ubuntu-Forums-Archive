### Post by Delever on 2009-06-01
It detects that special shape of that object on your paper, then maps 3D model on top of it. Such technique is called Motion tracking or _[Match moving]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Match_moving")_, and is very common in movie industry to map 3D models to the frame in scene in automated way, even if camera is shaking a lot. Shape can be anything that software is designed to recognize - in this case it is shape on white paper, but it can be colored dots or some other kind of markers.

It is amazing to see all this technology becoming available to everyone.

Compiz also have this technology to track user's head position:

[Compiz : head tracking]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5ODz3MNd6s")
[Compiz Webcam Headtracking #2]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5ODz3MNd6s")

Also, another project based on similar idea:

[Johnny Lee: Wii Remote hacks]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QgKCrGvShZs")

Fun :)
