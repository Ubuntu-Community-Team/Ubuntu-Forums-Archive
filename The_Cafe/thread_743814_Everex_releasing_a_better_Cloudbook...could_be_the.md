### Post by IsawSp4rks on 2008-04-06
> **ghindo said:**
> 

Also, I'd prefer a solid-state drive over a traditional HDD.  



I wouldn't.  SSD's are cost prohibitive and so you end being artificially restricted to sizes that turns your UMPC into a cheaper end MP3 player; 4 or 8GB of space doesn't leave much room for photos, movies, tv shows and games and I doubt many people could really argue that UMPCs are decent workhorses due to their inherently low specced CPUs.  With suspend/resume supported a regular HDD is fine as the only slow bootup experience will be the first time you turn your UMPC from a cold boot.
