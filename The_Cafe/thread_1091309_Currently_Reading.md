### Post by JackieChan on 2009-03-09
I just finished Watchmen a few weeks ago, and now I'm reading *The Call of Cthulhu and Other Weird Stories* by H.P. Lovecraft

It's a collection of short stories from pretty much the greatest author who ever lived. These stories are just Sci-Fi/fantasy/horror stories. This guy takes Edgar Allen Poe's essence, modernizes it, makes it more confusing, and adds a Sci-Fi element. Right now, I'm reading the short story The Call of Cthulhu. The plot is kind of hard to explain, you would have to read the story, but I'm going to try to summarize it as best as possible. It's about research collected over the years about cosmic God named Cthulhu. Cthulhu is worshiped by a cult, the Cthulhu cult. He's a cosmic God with an appearance that leaves you on the brink of insanity. He's as big as a mountain. He resides in the underwater city of R'lyeh. One day Cthulhu will rise up from this city and destroy the world. Cthulhu, along with the other Great Old Ones, came from the sky before mankind was manifested. 

[IMG]http://www.summeroflovecraft.com/images/cthulhu-6.jpg[/IMG]

I've read a lot of these other stories, like Dagon and The Last Test, and they talk about the other Great Old Ones. It's very interesting stuff. These stories are some of the most creative and interesting things I've ever read. I highly recommend this book.

My next book I plan on reading is going to be about Japanese folktale and Japanese mythology. Those stories have an thrilling horror aspect to them, and seeing them in this culture is really cool. The yokai stories are very intriguing to me. I'm very interested in the Yamawaro (from Gazu Hyakki Yak&#333;), Wany&#363;d&#333;, tons of other yokai, and tsukumogamis. I'm also thinking about reading Akira and Batman: The Killing Joke, I could use another good graphic novel.

I'm interested to see what you users are reading. Let me know if you have any recommendations for me. :)
