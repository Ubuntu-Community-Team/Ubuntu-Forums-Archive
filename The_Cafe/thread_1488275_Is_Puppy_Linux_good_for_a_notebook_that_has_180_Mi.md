### Post by Ibidem on 2010-05-19
Something other than Ubuntu with GNOME, at least--a rather slow combo for that little ram (Recommended RAM: minimum of 256-384 MB).  Lubuntu  is the fastest/lightest semi-official version; Xubuntu is supposed to be 192 MB +, and is slower than its reputation.
I suspect Lubuntu may be the best way to go.

Puppy should run very well on that as it's well over 128 MB; maybe try Lupu (Puppy linux, built from Ubuntu Lucid binaries), or perhaps Quirky or Lighthouse Pup.  There must be well over 100 different "puplets", so I'm sure something will be the right fit--provided that a distro with large repositories isn't desired.
[http://puppylinux.com/download/](http://puppylinux.com/download/)
[LuPu]("ftp://ibiblio.org/pub/linux/distributions/puppylinux/puppy-5.0/")
Then there's NimbleX, and Slax, and TinyCore--but be a little cautious about the last, since it's bare-minimum, rolling release.
