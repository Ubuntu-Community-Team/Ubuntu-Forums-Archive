### Post by Hexydes on 2007-10-21
Oh, and additionally not in my post:

- On an older laptop that I threw 7.10 on, which has a TRENDnet TEW-2655PC card, the Internet worked almost right out of the box. It wasn't lit up when I first started. I wasn't sure what to do, I tried to install NDISWrapper from Synaptic, but it said I needed the Ubuntu install disc (which was busy at the moment on a third computer). I went to the network settings, and the wireless option was actually listed there. I went through and filled out all the information, but it still didn't work. I finally ended up restarting the computer for something else, and when it came back up, I had a light, and the Internet was working. So that was cool, though it would have been nice to have some additional prompting from Ubuntu about what to do.

- Dual displays is not working. :( It's better than 7.04, where it wouldn't even see the external display. It actually sees the external display now, but I can't use both the laptop and external display at the same time; this may be a driver issue, I'm not sure. At the very least, the external display should be working, but it isn't, not properly. I instructed it to use the proper driver (ATI Mobility 128), and set it up with the right monitor (Dell 781p), but it just reverts to VESA and 800 x 600.

- I put it on a third computer, with two sound cards, and it is able to switch between them much better. Not sure if ALSA was touched at all, but it seems to be a lot more consistent now.

- Flash isn't working, not the sound anyway. :( Didn't have any problems in Feisty, but I can't get it working in Gutsy. I've tried both Flash and Gnash, but no luck. I'm going to keep screwing with it (this is a computer I'm putting together for my Father to use, because I want to see how he does with Ubuntu, and he is a YouTube junkie). :)
