> **McRat said:**

Sorry, I had to pick up on this in order to say, not always. Some of my key texts at the moment are >£50 and others have been close to £100.
I appreciate that some jobs do require extremely expensive tools, but education isn't always a free ride either. ^^

For the main topic, I never hated school, it was somewhere I went and had fun and learnt things as we went along. I did always scoff when people told me that school was "the best time of your life" and that school was a picnic compared to work, but they had a point.

School as a lad was 9am to 3pm (give or take 15 minutes) with an hour and a half of break spread over the day, not to mention all the time between classes, slowly walking to and fro. Homework such as it was took little time, and the school holidays that flew by really lasted forever.
College was similar, slightly more free time and more work expected out of hours. Then I bottled it, dropped out of college and started work full time.

8 hour days, 30 minutes of break, 5 days a week with 5 weeks holiday allowance (quite generous really) and no more teacher training days. Gone was the 9-3, in came the 6-2, or the 8-5, maybe the 2pm til 10pm, or 10pm til 6am. Then comes Christmas, the work gets to insane levels and there are minimal new staff to cover, then we get to 14 hour shifts, remember those 30 minutes of break? They're gone, if I was lucky I'd get 5 minutes to go grab a cup of tea. 
Then there were the shift patterns, finishing work only to go back in 8 hours later.

After 6 years I bummed off and went to Australia for some months, the traveling was good, but then the money ran low and work was needed again. We did fruit picking, harvesting Oranges, the hours were good, 5 hours a day, maybe 3 days a week, the rest of the time it wasn't suitable picking weather. But then we didn't make enough, though we did get all the oranges we could eat. :D
Then it was back to Sydney where I found grunt work working dispatch for a warehouse, 8 hour days again, short breaks, no entitlements, no sick pay. Did get to work out in the sun though.

After a while, I came back home. Time passed, I went back to my old job then left again and came to Uni. 
I'm back at the good life, I'm scheduled for 2 days a week, 6 hours a day. My holiday is even more impressive than it was as a school kid. The stress is far more than back then as well, but, it's worth it.

To be honest, by now I've completely lost the point of what I was originally going to say. 
So instead I'll say this. With hindsight, nothing is terrible, school was a better experience than I gave it credit for at the time. The grunt work I did had its fun times, even the chaos at Christmas would see me leaving with a smile on my face as I walked home at 10am after a 14 hour shift with a satisfaction of a job well done. 
Uni life, sure, it's good, but it's not quite school again, even as a student once more I'm expected to be grown up. ;)
