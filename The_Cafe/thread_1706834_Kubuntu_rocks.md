### Post by Dry Lips on 2011-03-14
I have Kubuntu installed alongside Ubuntu, (just to check it out).

**This is what I like**:

- KDE is indeed very polished. If I was going to show
Linux to, say, my sister, I would probably show her KDE.
I think Kubuntu is stylish in a way that Ubuntu isn't. 
- I also like the widgets, (the Screenlets manager that you can 
install in Ubuntu doesn't come close. And I don't like the 
concepts of docks, so no Cairo-dock for me...) 
- The interface of Kubuntu is also quite customisable, 
which is a good thing.

**This is what I dislike**:

- All the animated stuff in KDE (menus etc), is impressive
at first, but actually becomes a distraction when you use it.
If you have many windows open at a time, and has to rapidly
switch between them, you get a little dizzy. But then again, 
you can easily turn this stuff off. (Which I did)

- Also I thought the menus and everything were a bit sluggish
on my computer. My processor isn't the strongest one out there
(AMD Sempron 145), but even with the proprietary drivers for 
my graphics card installed, the frame-rate of the interface seemed
to be a little low.

- Moving from a Windows environment to Ubuntu, the concept of 
having several workspaces was a pleasant surprise. 
When you have many windows open at the time, this makes
navigating a lot easier. Unfortunately, although you have different
workspaces in Kubuntu, you still only have one taskbar, and 
switching workspaces doesn't affect what programs you see
at your taskbar. But isn't the whole concept of having workspaces
then defied? 

- Kubuntu didn't manage to remember the resolution of the screen,
and I had to change it again every time I rebooted. 
(Probably there is a way of fixing this.)

- I managed to freeze Kubuntu by adding a panel and changing the
size of it until it was so small it came right beneath the button at the
top right side of the screen. This ought not be possible.

**All in all**, I'll stick with Ubuntu/Gnome for now... But I'm not saying 
KDE is a bad idea, or that Kubuntu is a bad distro. It's just not my cup 
of tea at the moment...

### Post by murderslastcrow on 2011-03-15
Gnome can be heavier than KDE if you add on all those packages like Elementary OS is doing, for example. KDE 4 was much more 'hungry' when it came out than it is now. I've also found that KDE on Ubuntu seems to be slower than most other distros with KDE 4. So if you've only tried Kubuntu I'm not all that surprised at your distaste.

I've found that, although it's best to give KDE some overhead above 512 MB of RAM, it tends to utilize the resources at its disposal much better than Gnome. This may be an issue with Qt, KDE itself, or simply the fact that KDE users usually don't get a ton of third-party add ons like Gnome seems to come with these days.

Anyway, while people have their opinions on what's ugly or not, I just hope they don't ignore KDE just because of their GTK loyalty. It's still incredibly light for what it does. I've personally never met anyone in real life who didn't think KDE looked better than Gnome, but I guess those 'noobs' don't know anything. XD

I think the surprising amount of functionality is what tends to bring me back. On Windows, OS X, or in Gnome, I tend to notice all the things I can't do anymore.
