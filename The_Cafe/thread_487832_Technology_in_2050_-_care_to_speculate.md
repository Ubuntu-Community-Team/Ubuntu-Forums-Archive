### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-06-29
My predictions are...

1) there won't be a 3rd World War.  Countries are merging into a global society, and it's less reason to attack each other.  Instead, we'll keep seeing an increase in socio-political "terrorist" groups, some of which want their own country, some of which just have some statement to make, etc, etc.  After 2010 or so, one of these groups will finally get ahold of a nuke (not just a dirty bomb, but an actual nuke) and set it off in a populated area.  At that time, there will be a pretty severe, world-wide crack-down on  socio-political groups, and it will inevitably draw the world closer together.

2) Corporations will keep getting larger, spanning more countries, and eventually affecting politics in some countries so much as to make them puppet governments.  We will essentially have mega-corps, and while a World War 3 is highly unlikely, there will start to be more corporate wars.  They'll start by providing aid to situations, then provide security aid to their aid convoys, then folks like the United Nations will give their security forces the power to retaliate with deadly force.  Then, corporations will find ways to loop-hole this and turn their security forces into personal armies, which they'll use to leverage control over politcal bodies and such.

...but, enough about the world

As for technology, remember that in the 1950's, cartoons used to make fun of future technology by having robots come out with brooms to sweep up a broken glass on the ground.  In 50 years time, while we don't have robots to do our chores, we do have higher tech things to make chores easier...like a vacuum.  In the 1950's, they didn't even think about vacuums.

Knowing this, a lot of predictions we make today will be based on the limitations we currently live with.  We may think that in 50 years we'll have eye-glasses that project the screen into our eyes, and our hands will type on virtual keyboards.  However, as seen in the matrix, in 50 years we may just have jacks in the back of our heads and bypass virtual keyboards and monitors all together, instead hooking our brains right into computer networks.

Schools as we know them today may not exist...IF science can find a way to write information directly into the organic hard drive we all have between our ears called a brain.  Before a child is even born, doctor's may fit the kid with a chip sensor that pipes information right into their head.  When they're born, they already have all the basic knowledge needed to be a productive member of society (Reading, Writing, Math, Computer Skills, etc).  

In addition, aging will have been conquered.  As we unlock the secrets of genetics, we'll be able to speed-grow children (mentally and physically) into the prime of their lives (20's) and "freeze" them there.  With this, population explosion can occur (since no one would die from natural causes).  However,  nature finds a way to balance itself.  If the pendulum swings too wildly to one side, nature balances it with an oppositely wild swing.  So, we'll be able to live forever, but we'll find more radical diseases cropping up killing us.

Unfortunately, all of this greatness will be based on whether you can afford it or not.  The "rich get richer while the poor get poorer" divide will widen as children born into privilage are genetically enhanced, cyber'ed, etc, while poor children will remain normal (which, even if they're a gifted normal person, will still make them a sub-normal person compared to a genetically gifted person).  This will cause more caste system sub-division amongst people.  Racism will no longer be race-based, but genetics-based.  This will help fuel some of the socio-political groups I spoke of earlier, which will lead to skirmished violence around the globe.

These predictions are all based on 2 driving human principles..

1) our ability to appreciate what we have has actually reverse-evolved the faster our technology & science expands.  (Meaning, the faster we grow technologically, the less we appreciate what we've accomplished...power corrupts and we get egos...we are human.)

2) Humans are nothing more than advanced pattern-matching software (brain/thought) housed in a biological analogue machine casing (flesh) and an overriding program to find cost/benefit in everything.  Our main objective in thinking is to look for patterns, find differences and similarities, and judge them to be beneficial or un-beneficial, and how much it will "cost" us to gain or avoid them.  From that, we end up creating caste systems, as we judge others, categorize them desirable or undesirable (for whatever reason), and try to strive to be in the more desirable categories ourselves.  With this, we have formed large economic systems as the foundations for goods transfer.  This system of economics is not dependant on what is best for the whole of humanity, but rather what is best you can afford.  With that objective in place, as technology expands, and costs more and more to gain better technology or more technology for your use, it widens the gap between the have's and have nots, the desirable and undesirable.  Thus, the caste system along with elitism grows.  

History has shown this always causes turmoil, and eventual physical conflict / violence.  In the past, conflicts have usually involved majority human components, so the morality of someone can act as a governor on the severity of backlash.  But, as more and more AI or robotic constructs replace humans as war machines, the few will end up being able to control or even wipe out the many without having to worry about the troops feeling remorse and stopping before the end goal is reached.  IE: Imagine if Hitler controlled an army of robots instead of soldiers.

Oh yeah...and we'll finally have jet-packs like they promised us so long ago...

### Post by Alterax on 2007-06-30
I am not so big on the WW 3 scenario; my view is actually very optimistic.

I think Open Source software is fully going to take off from a consumer point of view in 3-5 years.  This is going to lead to computers being affordable by even the poorest of households within eight to ten years, combined with a massive restructuring of our current Internet backbone.  We're already seeing indications of Open Source doing that--not too long ago at all, something like Ubuntu wouldn't have been thinkable.

We'll see a lot more consolidation of data between computers (in whatever form they exist then) and personal items.  Because of current research into using human brainwaves as a means of communicating with machines, it's safe to say that this will become integrated into how we interact with our computers, much like using a GUI and mouse was once sci-fi but now we've become used to the idea.

Lifespans will have increased to about 120 years average, due to increases in nutritional and medical sciences, and it will not be uncommon for people to take advantage of this time by going through two, possibly three or four very different career focus phases during their lifetimes (now two seems to be standard).  Diseases will be more effectively treated by using the study of proteomics to check many medicines' effectiveness and side effects well before administering them to the patient.  (These technologies are already showing promise in current research).

Understanding of terraforming and genetics will lead us to finding new ways to grow and harvest crops effectively.  We are talking about more nutritious food that grows in climates we wouldn't even think of as being arable now--the end of hunger and poverty as we know it.

So, yeah, it's pretty much optimistic; say what you will but that is the future that I see, and the one that I am eager to bring about every day.
