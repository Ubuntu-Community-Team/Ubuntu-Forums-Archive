### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-09-30
Well, it's strange that people say the Linux of TODAY is hard, as it's finally easier than Windows and OS X in most respects outside of the whole 'running software never even made for this OS' thing (Wine). And really, the fact that the option is there is amazing enough.

But really, it's just the fact that I know people who use OS X, and they think it's basically an updated version of Linux, and even with so many of our friends and family using KDE and Gnome, they still doubt the merits of Linux, as if we're all dumb freeloaders without any class.

I like Linux, not only because it excels at everything except for perhaps running every Windows game and productivity application out there (it does most), but because of the community it inspires. Allow me to explain.

Without Linux, the world is just Mac and Windows, and this is how many people see it. In this world, you either have the choice of getting a cheap computer with crappy software, viruses, slowdowns, headaches, and all the horrible side-effects of using Windows, or you can pay out the wazoo for a high quality computer with a secure, stable, beautiful, fast-enough OS and sacrifice the program compatibility.

With Linux, you can run most Windows software, get tons more for free that will be continually developed and improved by the people for the people (open source), it's just as beautiful as OS X (I think KDE is more beautiful, but for the sake of being unbiased let's just admit they're at least equals), it's fast as Hell, it's more secure than OS X, it runs on more computers than any other OS, and you can get hardware even cheaper than you could with Windows.

So it's like the best of both worlds and then some, unless you happen to depend on an application for which there is no suitable replacement (unique game), or you are forced to ignore the replacement by your work, or technical limitations (Adobe CS/AutoCAD).

Of course, there are options to do anything you want in Linux, for free. That's just it- it's beautiful, clean, professional, and extremely functional for a large variety of uses.

So yeah, hearing people talk about Linux from 10 years ago as if that's how it is today is pretty bogus. You just shouldn't judge anything you don't have experience with to back it up. That's called bigotry.
