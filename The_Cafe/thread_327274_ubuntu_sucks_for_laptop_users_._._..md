### Post by prizrak on 2006-12-28
I have an nVidia based laptop without any fuss, not sure why you can't find one. What I found difficult is finding a non widescreen laptop with nVidia as they tend to produce higher end mobile video cards that are normally found in more powerful laptops. 

Battery life with Centrino and integrated Intel video is better on Linux mostly because of how the CPU scaling is handled (I won't get into it just trust me on it). Most mobile ATI and nVidia cards have GPU throttling (the video card's central processor can run at slower speeds to save battery life) unfortunately none of the drivers for nVidia do GPU throttling on Linux and only some of the ATI cards have it under Linux. 

Laptops are difficult beasts because of their reliance on ACPI functions. While ACPI is technically an industry wide standard each manufacturer implements it differently. This translates into undocumented features and quirks that can stop Linux from working properly. For instance on my laptop (Acer) when Windows is hibernated it will absolutely not boot from the CD. Some laptops may run at full CPU and cooling because some ACPI function is not implemented as expected. 

From experience best brands for Linux.
Centrino based Acers
Toshiba 
Thinkpads

I also hear ASUS is very good but I never used it.
