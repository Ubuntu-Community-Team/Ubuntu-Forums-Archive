I find it interesting that people think that because it may not be on the most systems within a company that its not mainstream.  FreeBSD and OpenSolaris, I would say, are not mainstream desktop solutions in the enterprise..  I'm willing to bet all of the top ten companies in the US have linux **_desktops_** deployed in some capacity, and its usually in the places that can afford to be interrupted the least; the desktop solutions that are closest to that companies payload.
