### Post by nickleboyblue on 2011-04-03
I believe that people are happiest when what matters most to them are their relationships with other people and not how much they own, how rich they are, or how educated they've become.  Essentially, I believe anyone and everyone has the ability to choose to be happy, but in order to successfully make that decision, you need to care more about how happy other people are than how happy you are.

The problem of today arises when people are more concerned about how well they perform in their next FPS tournament than they do about how their parents, siblings, friends, and neighbors are doing at the moment.  Most of us are too stubborn to REALLY care about others more than we do about ourselves, our money, our gaming time, how our girlfriend makes us feel, etc.

The best way to make people happy?  Live the life of the happiest person on earth, the guy who couldn't care less if his computer is stolen because whoever stole it probably needs it more than he does.  If you somehow manage to become that person, make sure other people can see how happy you are.  If they do, and you tell them why, they might just join you.  It's highly unlikely to make it very far, but at least you helped one or two people to become happy.

### Post by Pogeymanz on 2011-04-03
I think the biggest thing keeping people from happiness is fear. Whether or not a particular fear is legitimate or not is a whole other question.

But studies have shown that, here in the states, happiness increase with yearly income until about $80,000. After that, more money doesn't seem to have any effect on happiness.

The reason, in my opinion, is that a person making $80,000 doesn't have to worry as much about food, housing, car exploding, etc.

Obviously, this thread can get very political, very quickly. But I think as a general principle, if you try to minimize your fear and anxiety you will be more happy. This is, of course, easier said that done. Especially for people who are in tough financial situations.
