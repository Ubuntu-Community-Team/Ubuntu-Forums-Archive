### Post by vidwarren on 2011-09-15
Thanks for the feedback I'll definitely edit the CMS/Database part when I get a chance.

I have to say that I'm generally dubious to sites like Jamendo. It all depends what you want from music and what you want to gain from joining the site.

If you're looking to make money through the site, the aspects that would put me off are that:

- You must confirm that 'neither you nor any other rights holders are members of any collective rights society'.
  This means that you can't be a member for PRS For Music. It is essential to join PRS For Music to collect royalties generated from your music being played on radio, TV, online and even your own gigs! I.e. if you gig or if your music is played by anywhere with a PRS for Music license, joining Jamendo and opting to collect money sacrifices this. Is it really worth it? - N.B. I'm based in the UK; different countries will have different licenses and societies.

- You'd be putting you music out there for free voluntarily.
  Now let's say that a sample library, TV show or something similar do like your music. Having it on a site like this can cause complications and their policies may prevent you from using tracks that you've already made publicly available. It also makes it harder to justify charging money for CDs at gigs or when busking if you have chosen to allow downloading.

People tend to find new music organically, accidentally or on personal recommendation from friends etc. Sites like Jamendo often face a difficult position being too busy for your songs to stand out and not busy enough for a large enough number of people searching for one thing and then accidentally stumbling on your page. That said, perhaps I'm making that assumption because I'm based in the UK and it's not too popular here. I don't know what Jamendo's connectivity is like with social networks but I never even see links in status updates and tweets, let alone embeds (if that's possible) SoundCloud allows you to embed into a Facebook status update and on a lot of vBulletin forums (if the owners have allowed it) meaning that you can 'unobtrusively' share tracks without making a user leave the page.

On first glance, Jamendo looks to me to be a bit like ReverbNation without as many fancy widgets.

Of course, every site has trends and patches of people with certain tastes and preferences. If you think that the 'Jamendo crowd' fits with your music, more power to you. Of course, if you're not trying to 'make it' or to sell you recordings, why not share your music with a wider audience; that often takes a lot of the complications out of your decisions.
