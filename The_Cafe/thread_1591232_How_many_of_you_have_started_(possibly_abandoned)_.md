### Post by aleska on 2010-10-09
If you are like me, over the years you have started a blog or two or ten, only to abandon them later as your passion fizzles - or more likely as you become far too busy to maintain it effectively. 

I'm curious, who here has, or had, a linux or ubuntu oriented blog?  I'd love to check them out!  

I've started tinkering with one again, this time with an eye toward sharing ideas, suggestions and examples of using linux at home.  The objective is not to bash any other OS.  Nor is it to make converts.  Rather, the goal is to create (or link to) helpful content that showcases interesting, innovative, or simply practical ways to use Linux to enhance ones home computing experience.  

What I would love to find is a group of folks from here, that would be interested in participating in this...being regular contributors.  I believe that a group blog of this nature would have lots of benefits.  For me, it would help keep me jazzed about it - always interested in what's new.  For the blog itself, it would help to ensure that it *stays fresh* with new and frequent content.  

If this is something that you would be interested in, and you feel you could contribute to a blog with this specific focus, let me know.  I'd love to see a sample of your writing style - maybe a link to a blog you've kept (whatever subject) just to get an idea of how it would meld with others participating.  

As for my writing style, I'm not suggesting that I'm something that special.  But if you'd like to see the nascent posts, you can find them [here]("http://linux4home.wordpress.com/").

Thanks! Aleska (Alex)

### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-10-10
I used to have a blog but I didn't separate my Linux rants from all my rants about *other* things (dance rants, school rants, political rants, and even religious rants). I did *categorize* them so people who were only interested in one category didn't have to wade through all the other "irrelevant" stuff... but I find that most people don't bother to search by category. And lotsa people posted snarky comments about "I don't want to find this kind of garbage on a Linux blog" (which it never claimed to be a "Linux blog" anyway), and they didn't like the fonts and colors I used, and whatever like that. Moderating comments became a full-time job (okay, that's a bit of an exaggeration), lol. I got ticked off one day and just killed the whole blog. If I ever start blogging again, I'll make separate blogs! One for my Linux rants, one for dance rants, one for school, one for political and religious rants, etc. Since people can't be bothered with the category search.

-Robin
