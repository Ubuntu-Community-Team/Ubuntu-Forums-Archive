I've got the Asus eee pc 1000HA and WhenIi purchased it I did my fair share of research to find out what would run best on it and also support all of (or most of) standard Ubuntus features. I soon found out that EasyPeasy is horrible (in my opinion) and so is the whole Netbook Remix style desktop environment. I found it to be overly crowded and run very poorly. Easy Peasy was basically a watered down version of Ubuntu. It barely was usable for anything besides Skype, Internet Browsing, Instant Messaging, and other standard things.

After being disappointed with Easy Peasy and the Netbook Remix I looked into [Eeebuntu STANDARD]("http://eeebuntu.org/index.php?page=standard"), not [Eeebuntu NBR]("http://eeebuntu.org/index.php?page=nbr") (Netbook Remix). I tried it out and loved it. It is basically a full installation of standard Ubuntu, it just has the Array kernel for all of the eee pc hardware support. I **LOVED** that.

_***Now-a-days***_ I am using the standard Ubuntu 9.04 Beta on my eee pc and even though it is "beta" it is running better than any of the custom builds that are *built for the eee pcs* (i.e: Eeebuntu, Easy Peasy, etc...).

*I would highly reccomend installing Ubuntu 9.04 on your eee. You will love it. I guarentee it.* :)

### Post by RandomJoe on 2009-04-16
