### Post by slayerboy on 2007-04-01
I have never seen in any software that I have used in Windows or Linux a warranty that the software will work.  Now that said, in the interest of customer service, the company/person creating the software might try to help make it work for your situation, but you can't sue a company/person because their software didn't work on your computer and they won't do anything about it.  99% of the time on the Windows end, the software is going to work unless you don't meet the system requirements.  Linux is a different story because most of the software is free as in beer.

Case in point, I played a game called World of Warcraft for a while in Linux and when they released an update it would not work on my system under Linux.  Now this is a monthly fee for the game, but they still had no guarantee that it would work on a person's system.

The main reason is because computer systems can vary in design and specifications so much that they can't possibly test every possible situation that their software may see.
