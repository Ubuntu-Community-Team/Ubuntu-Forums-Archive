### Post by HermanAB on 2011-07-18
Howdy,

First figure out whether the key is really broken, or whether X is broken.  Boot a live CD and check the key.  If it works, then you have an X problem and could probably fix it by redefining the key to be itself with xmodmap, which will effectively repair it by fixing the X key definition.  

I have had this happen some years ago, with the T key on a laptop and have seen another complaint about the same kind of thing, so this happens and no-one knows why.

### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-07-18
Sometimes is is just a matter of popping off the key cap and putting it back on. If the hinge under the key is damaged you may have to replace the keyboard. Replacing a keyboard doesn't require opening up the laptop in most cases. You can Google on the model for instruction on how to replace a key cap or a keyboard, and sometime you can find a video showing how to do it. I have reseated key caps and replaced keyboards on several laptops for the first time when I was 62 years old. It really isn't hard to do. The instructions will also help you identify what key cap or keyboard you have. If you order it on-line they are not expensive; however, make sure the company has a good return policy. You might find you ordered the wrong part or the replacement may have a manufacturing defect. One keyboard replacement I bought had a malformed switch rubber boot.
