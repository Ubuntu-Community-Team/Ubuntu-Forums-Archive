### Post by Six_Digits on 2007-11-16
OK, Started my Linux adventures almost exactly a year ago. Needless to say, coming from windows wasn't exactly easy. Lucky enough for me, at 21 I've been using computers longer than they've been making useful GUIs. Windows was my main platform until 2004 when I had a Redhat machine and a Windows 2000 professional less-than machine. Although I have to say, that was MY FAVORITE Windows release. Quite stable and fast, but I always felt as if I didn't have complete control. My Redhat machine lacked any sort of internet adapter, so that was a slightly useless piece of machine,

     Anyways, I found Ubuntu last year and have been dual booting ever since. I tended to give up in the beginning when I run into a "serious" problem. It seems the more I learn. the more powerful Linux becomes. Actually mostly anything I want different - I can achieve, as long as I follow instructions. 

    So... My point - All my computer life I wanted something I could control. I wish I started with Linux - Would have saved me $1000 and taught me much more than clicking "OK" and using a control panel. Completely MINE and completely free, LINUX. It's almost euphoric the way I feel about Linux freedom. What can't you change?
