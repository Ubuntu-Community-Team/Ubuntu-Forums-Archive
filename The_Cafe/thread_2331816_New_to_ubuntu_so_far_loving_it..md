### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-07-26
> it is still not a good solution as they are all in the same room (risk  of fire). but at least there are 3 copies of each file. ideally the  server should be at least 30km away.

That statement reminded me of the time I worked for a major high street retailer in one of its stores. All the data on the server was backed up every night to a tape drive. As a warehouse man I would sometimes be required to change over the tapes because us warehouse men started work much earlier than the office staff.

These backup tapes were not put into a fireproof safe. They were stacked on top of the server. A fire that destroyed the server would also destroy the backup tapes. A business could replace hardware in a couple of days but without backups of customer and supplier accounts and the stock inventory that business might find it very difficult to get back in business after a fire.

Regards
