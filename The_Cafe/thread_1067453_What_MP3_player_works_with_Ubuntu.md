### Post by GregA on 2009-02-11
I've had good results from a Best Buy brand media player - the "Insignia NS 4v24".  These are similar to many media players in playing video and audio.  The rechargeable lithium battery is renewed by connecting to your standard usb port or hub.  Ubuntu sees the player as a flash drive, mounts it, and so you add or remove mp3 files using Nautilus or any file manager.  The other thing I like about the Insignia player line - - they fully support the ogg vorbus standard.   Ogg is Open Source, and more compressed than mp3, offering as good if not better playback (it's easy to convert mp3 to ogg or the other way around).  The player has fm radio, and a nice color screen about same size as Ipod.   Best Buy also has speaker docking units, etc. or you can hook up to most players if they have a usb port.
