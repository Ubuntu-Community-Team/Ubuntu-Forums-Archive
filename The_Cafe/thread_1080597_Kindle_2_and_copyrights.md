The author basically says that Kindle should not be allowed to offer "read-out-loud" ebooks without paying extra royalties,

a) What is the difference between a screen reader, and what Kindle does?

b) Amazon's best strategy would be to add voice input, then sell it at a discount to individuals who meet ADA criteria, and/or market it as an accessibility tool.   (This throws all copyright issues out the window, because of the exemption copyright law grants to creating derivative works for blind, and/or otherwise disabled individuals.)
