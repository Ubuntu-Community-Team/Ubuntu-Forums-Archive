### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-10-18
Well then folks. 1983. **The Police** - *Synchronicity*.

Recently "remastered" and reissued in this century with far better graphics - and far worse sound quality. Technology improved but mainstream "industry standards" seem to have lost whatever taste and intelligence it had. I've kept the original and for even better sound quality, the original vinyl. Shouldn't be that way, but it is.

Even sound quality sticklers have been driven from buying CDs thanks to the "loudness" trend and other symptoms of apparent deafness that set in the music biz around the late '90's. Just in time for the internet to provide a "free" alternative for the increasingly disposable "product" the corporate media still expects everyone to pay billions for and... we know how that's all going. 

Some older CDs were actually of quite high quality - whether or not the cheap packaging was - and many were crap of course, but now it's a rare one indeed that delivers the resolution CD promises. That may play a part, whether or not most people consciously notice. So one major reason less to pay for a CD, which by the way never did go down in price to the extent that the industry was initially promising when folks were balking at $14.95. And here you'd hope we'd be way ahead a quarter century later, right? Gah.
