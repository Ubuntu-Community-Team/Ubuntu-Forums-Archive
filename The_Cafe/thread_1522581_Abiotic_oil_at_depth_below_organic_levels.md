### Post by libssd on 2010-07-13
As is so often the case, there seems to be a confusion here between "hypothesis" and "theory."

**Theory**: a *well-substantiated* explanation of some aspect of the natural world; an organized system of accepted knowledge that applies in a variety of circumstances to explain a specific set of phenomena.

**Hypothesis**: a tentative insight into the natural world; a concept that is not yet verified but that if true would explain certain facts or phenomena.

I'm willing to accept abiotic oil as an unproven hypothesis, but there is nowhere near enough evidence for it to qualify as a theory. On the other hand, there is more than 100 years of geological evidence that oil exists in sometimes vast underground reservoirs (really, permeable rock formations, not lakes of the stuff, as many people imagine), and that as oil is removed from these reservoirs, they become less and less productive. New techniques (directional drilling, hydraulic fracturing, water and gas injection) generally extend the useful life of these reservoirs beyond initial estimates, but I have yet to see any credible evidence that they are magically recharged. The abiotic oil hypothesis predates modern geology, and has pretty shaky underpinnings.

**Occam's razor** applies here:  the simplest explanation of a problem is the preferred explanation, unless it is known to be wrong. Which is more likely:
