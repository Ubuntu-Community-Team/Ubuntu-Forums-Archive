Really.

You including Jimmy Hendrix amongst the people who "don't have enough talent to do original songs".

Or did you not know that 'All Along the Watchtower' was written by Bob Dylan?
[Source]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_Along_the_Watchtower")

A few more people who are considered to have some talent, but have 'remixed' others original work:

"You Really Got Me". The Van Halen cover was bigger than the Kinks (superior) original.

Neither are really famous but "Hanging On The Telephone" was originally done by the Nerves and Blondie has the better known version.

Tiny Bradshaw, Howard Kay, and Lois Mann wrote "Train Kept A Rollin'" but the Yardbirds version is more famous than theirs, and Aerosmiths' version is more famous than that.

The Jam's version of "David Watts" originally by the Kinks.

It's just barely a cover but Led Zeppelin's "The Lemon Song" is much more famous than the song it's a reworking of, Howling Wolf's "Killing Floor".

Lynyrd Skynyrd's version of "Call Me The Breeze" is better known than J.J. Cale's original, but I could go on and on about covers of his songs, but I'll suffice it to add "Cocaine" and "After Midnight" done by Clapton.

The Clash have the most famous version of "I Fought The Law". At least more famous than Sonny Curtis's. But Bobby Fuller's version is up there too.

The Clash's version of "Police & Thieves" is also heard more often than Junior Murvin's.

The Leaves' version is by far the best version of "Hey Joe".
