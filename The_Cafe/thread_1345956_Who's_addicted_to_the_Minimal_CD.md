### Post by ajgreeny on 2009-12-04
I am seriously considering using it for my next install of ubuntu.  It does have many advantages, as you say.

1  It is very customizable.
2  You can use it without the installation of many outdated and unwanted packages that quickly appear in a new version of the full distro CD.
3  I have used it a couple of times to install just the LXDE desktop and the few apps that I needed on an old, slow laptop.  Brilliant!

The only problem I can think of, is that many people who have not used linux, or specifically ubuntu, in the past would find it very odd to be faced with what looks like a strange version of msdos (if they even know about that) on their screen, and would probably be very frightened by it.
