### Post by Rob Sayer on 2014-03-05
I'm think newbies here ...

- for novices (& probably many intermediate users) stick to lts unless you have hardware support issues.

- ubuntu tech support is second to none.  But there are good and bad answers to your questions and it may take a little while to figure out which is which.  This is endemic to the Web anyway, and ubuntu support isn't nearly as bad as a lot of other web topics.

- just because someone maintains a blog doesn't necessarily mean their advice is good.

- just because someone has a large number of forum entries doesn't necessarily mean their advice is good either.  But it's probably better than a lot of blogs I've seen.

- if you want better suppport when you're asking questions on here or askubuntu, you'll get much better results if you can phrase your question clearly and specifically.  Eg. asking "<pci id here> wireless is intermittent" is better than "wireless on my <computer model name.  Note this is just as true when you're taking your car to get fixed ...

- linux, like unix, will tend to need more configuration at first than windows.  But once you've done that  it'll work so much better it's ridiculous.  I've had ubuntu crash ("internal error") a few times.  All that happened was a notification appeared in the panel.  Everything else, including stuff running in the background, kept working normally.  Once I didn't even notice the little orange splat for maybe 5 minutes.  Try *that* in windows ...
