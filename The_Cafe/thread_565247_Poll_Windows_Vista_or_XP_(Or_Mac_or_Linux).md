### Post by Sisophon2001 on 2007-10-02
If you only have one computer, then you should keep open the option of duel booting to windows so that if you buy a new gadget that has drivers only for windows, you can at least use it until some bright spark makes it work with Linux. And if you are paying for a new windows OS it might as well be Vista, which should be standard now on new computers, and will give you five or six years of working life for your money. If you went for XP, I very much doubt it will last as long as Vista. You don't have to use it every day if you don't like it. Think of it as a backup OS.

I see little point in purchasing a Mac unless you are into luxury at the expense of cost. Correct me if I am wrong, but that is still my understanding of the trade off. I used Mac's first years ago and switched to a DOS machine when I first had to pay for my own computer. I think the trade off still stands. Is it worth the extra money? If it is to you, then get a Mac.

I use free apps on my WinXP laptop, the exact same apps as I use on my Linux desktop. I think a Linux/Windows mix gives best all round value.
