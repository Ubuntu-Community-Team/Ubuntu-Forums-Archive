> **Lead is rarely found in source water, but enters tap water through corrosion of plumbing materials. Homes built before 1986 are more likely to have lead pipes, fixtures and solder**. **_However_**, **new homes are also at risk: even legally &#8220;lead-free&#8221; plumbing may contain up to 8 percent lead**. The most common problem is with brass or chrome-plated brass faucets and fixtures which can leach significant amounts of lead into the water, especially hot water.



This is a general answer for the questions... :)


How Does Lead Get Into My Water?

Lead enters the water (&#8220;leaches&#8221;) through contact with the plumbing.

[B]Lead leaches into water through:
[/B]
Corrosion* of

Pipes
Solder
Fixtures and Faucets (brass)
Fittings
The amount of lead in your water also depends on the types and amounts of minerals in the water, how long the water stays in the pipes, the amount of wear in the pipes, the water&#8217;s acidity and its temperature.

***Corrosion is a dissolving or wearing away of metal caused by a chemical reaction between water and your plumbing**.
