### Post by RAV TUX on 2007-06-24
The problem with Distrowatch and Wikipedia is they do not include all Linux Distros for what ever reason. This is why I have launched a new site:

Distropedia
[http://distropedia.com/](http://distropedia.com/)

I have started simply by mapping all the distros by country of origin, I will need a collaborative effort to develop the site.

Rule #1 if a Distro exist it will be included regardless of notability(unlike Wikipedia).

Rule #2 All Distros will be included with NO waiting period (unlike Distrowatch).

Rule #3 You will not be required to buy ad space to waive the waiting period.(not a rule set in stone but there has been at least one incident of this happening, at least where the Distro was taken more serious and the wait time waived)

I am sure I can add more rules...

If anybody would like to work on a collaborative effort to develop the site please contact me directly....

In the beginning right now when it is new it will need a lot of loving care. Right now there is no content beyond a small article on Wolvix.

Two or Three years from now it will become the industry standard, competition is always good to improve the content of these sites anyway...

What I visualize is a Wikipedia like site that is devoted to Distros, Linux, BSD and others(Haiku for example). With no ads of any kind, and  no bias to  older developed distros.

This will require I suspect installation of mediawiki, which I have to figure out....or sign a new contract with a host that includes mediawiki already installed,...which could be as low as $71 a year....not much more then the $60 a year I currently pay the only difference is my current host is unlimited. So it would be easier to install mediawiki instead. I also like MoinMoin Wiki (what Ubuntu, Debian, & Fedora use)

Anyway, take a look at the map....which is still in heavy development....and let me know what you think.

If anybody would like to help please contact me. If you have any development ideas that you would like to see incorporated let me know.

Another CafeLinux.org Developers site. 

Remember I am one of your own. I got my start with Ubuntu, and ubuntuforums.org, this will always be my online home. ;)
