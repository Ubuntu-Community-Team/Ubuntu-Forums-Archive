### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2014-12-18
Welcome to the Forums.

You'll find, as Dragonbite has said, that there ARE an AWFUL lot of different distros out there that work extremely well on older hardware, and help to keep them useful & productive.

I use Ubuntu myself on my 'big' machine, a 2005 Compaq Presario with an AMD Athlon 64 and 3 GB of DDR1 RAM. I've only been using it since May, but I'm hooked.

I tried it on my other machine.....a 2002 Dell Inspiron 1100 laptop, with an Intel Celeron and 1 GB DDR1 RAM...but it was a bit too much for the graphics. I then tried Lubuntu, the lightweight version, but the graphics problem persisted. I soon came to realise that the graphics card is one of those 'awkward' ones!

She's now running Puppy Linux 'TahrPup' 6.0.....which works perfectly. And it's based on Ubuntu anyway.....so I'm QUITE happy..!

Which just goes to show that in the Linux world (to paraphrase dating agencies!), there's a distro out there for EVERY computer...

Have fun.
