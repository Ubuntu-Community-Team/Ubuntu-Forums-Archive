### Post by t0p on 2013-12-03
> **david98 said:**
> What type of photography you into top nature landscapes building's. I think most if not all photographers are using digital. It's amazing how much technology has changed in the last 10 years. Look at the Wright brothers taking there first flight in 1903 now 110 years later look what man kind has achieved I cant even begin to imagine what sort of technology will be around in another 110 years when all of us are long gone.

I like doing landscape, buildings, street photography.  Also covert pet photography: I have a big, bouncy American bulldog (another pastime) that is my most frequent model; but she *hates* being photographed, if she sees me so much as point a camera in her direction she glares and growls, so it is a real challenge to get decent shots of her being lively and happy.  Sometimes I just hold my smallest camera and take pics without looking at the display.  A bit hit-and-miss, produces complete garbage most of the time.  It'd be a real money burner if I did this with a film camera so let's say hooray for digital.  Hooray!

Incidentally there's still a fair number of photographers using film.  I (very rarely) use a film camera, and the photo lab I use will develop the film then scan the negatives/slides and send me a CD instead of prints. Very good quality scans, and I can print the good ones using a computer printer or I can send the negative to the lab to get a nice large print done.  So film photography is still alive and kicking.  Just not kicking as hard as digital photography!
