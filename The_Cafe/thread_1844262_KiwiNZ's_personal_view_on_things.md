### Post by KiwiNZ on 2011-09-14
I wanted to take a moment of your time to clarify how I feel about open source and in particular ubuntu. I have received emails and notes indicating that it appears that I am more pro Apple and Microsoft than I am 
Open Source and ubuntu. Let me state here that simply is not the case and I will explain my position.

First a bit of history about KiwiNZ. My background is Military, then Central Government the later as a Senior IT manager. I am also a Volunteer Search and Rescue/Urban Search and Rescue.I and 55 years old.

Now I have been using  Linux in various forms and uses since the early 1990's and for a time owned my own Company selling and servicing PC's and Servers with Linux or Windows or Unix installed, due to
my health issues I sold that business and also retired from full time employment to concentrate on my rehabilitation.

_Ubuntu_

I have been involved with ubuntu since early 2004 and started using pre release versions easily that year. I joined ubuntu Forums on the day it started. I have been an active member of the ubuntu community since.
I have advised incalculable numbers people to move to Open Source over the years, but I do not preach.

I am totally behind ubuntu and believe if managed correctly it will continue is rise in popularity.

Ubuntu can be a very good system if Developed and managed professionally.

_Other Operating Systems _

I am Operating System agnostic and this I believe is way some folks feel I am more supportive of Apple or Microsoft. I believe that an Operating System is simply that, it is not an entity  or some great Social Crusade.
An Operating System should do what is required of it , it should be the "grey man" and work in the background.

Considering the above, I will recommend to those who ask the best Operating System that meets their set of circumstances bet that Windows, OSX or Linux. If not asked I do not preach.

_The Companies _

The main players I believe do what they do in main very well. Apple, Microsoft, HP, IBM, Oracle, Google all produce very good products and not so good products. They also contribute greatly to the Industry as a whole.
The emerging companies, Redhat, Canonical, Samsung etc also produce very good and not so good products.

I am not particularly supportive of any one company but support products that I feel are worthy and I also not in  the habit of not supporting any one company based on myth or mis-information.

_Conclusion_

Do I support Open Source? absolutely
Do I support Proprietary ? absolutely

I believe both have a place and role to play in the industry. To put it simply, Operating Systems and Software should be purchased or used based on their feature set and how well they meet the requirements of
the purpose to which they are to be applied.

By excluding OS's or software on the basis that they are Open source or proprietary is at best flawed at worse stupid. Use what fits best.
