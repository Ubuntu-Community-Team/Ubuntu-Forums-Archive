### Post by DuckHook on 2023-02-25
Hands down, a desktop.

Mainly because I'm a tinkerer at heart and changing the guts inside a laptop is a high wire act. Piece of cake on a desktop.

Not to mention, far more power, big monitor, loads of disk space. Maybe the biggest factor is that I'm an old retired geezer who doesn't have to lug my work from place to place.

*All* of my computers, desktop or laptop are multiboot. Learned that the hard way years ago when components were less reliable and I was more foolish, routinely performing brain surgery on the OS. Being able to boot into another partition, then chrooting into the broken one to fix it, was priceless. Saved me on many occasions.

But what ended up being the best solution was the discovery of containers/VMs. Now, I do my brain surgery on the containers/VMs and leave my host system alone. But the multiboot habit has persisted.

### Post by TheFu on 2023-02-25
Server with sufficient power, CPU, RAM to support multiple virtual machines running any OSes needed. I stopped dual booting about 15 yrs ago. There's little point in dual booting today. The CPU should have an iGPU built-in to avoid 99.9% of driver issues.

Then I'd borrow someone else's interface device and have a flash drive with a full OS installed and running on it, perhaps MX Linux running under a Ventoy boot manager.

While I have a few raspberry pi computers, they are running media center player software, so they aren't really used as "computers" and more than a Roku or Fire stick is.  Generally, we use a cheap tablet to control what gets played where for entertainment. That can be music controls or video. The media comes from the "server" in a different room.  That can be photos, videos, audio, or ebooks.

While I'm at it, 1 computer isn't sufficient. A mirror computer is needed for failover and the hardware needs to be very similar, capable of running everything on a single machine.

If I lived and worked in a big city and spent 10 hrs away from home daily, perhaps a tablet or cellphone would make more sense.  Actually, I'd love to have an 8 inch cell phone/tablet device, but nobody makes them in my price range.  If my cheap tablet had a real GPS, I probably wouldn't need a cell phone at all.  Hummmm.
