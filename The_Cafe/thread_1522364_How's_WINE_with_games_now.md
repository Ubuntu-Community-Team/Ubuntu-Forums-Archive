### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-07-02
To be honest, it must be my taste in games and productivity applications, but everything Windows game I've ever played installs and runs well in Wine. So, as far as I'm concerned, Wine is perfect for my needs thus far.

However, a good practice to have is GO TO APPDB FIRST!! Don't just go through the whole installation and then get all angry when it doesn't work. That's not good procedure. These posts of people saying, "I installed this, it didn't work. I installed yet another thingy, it didn't work," are probably taking a less practical approach, no offense.

If it says it doesn't work, or does work with tweaks in AppDB, and it's under a recent version number, try it. If it's under an old version number, try it again and submit some new test information to AppDB.

Like the original responses, just don't come into it with high expectations- it's not a perfect compatibility layer just yet. But, if it works with a few of your games and not a few others, that's still some time you won't have to spend in Windows.
