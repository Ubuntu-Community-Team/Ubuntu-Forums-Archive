### Post by samjh on 2008-12-22
I don't know how many of you are aware of the plans by the Australian government to filter internet content at ISP-level.  It's quite significant, as Australia will be one of the very few countries - perhaps the only "western" democratic country - to implement such a scheme.

First, the internet service providers who are partaking in the pilot trial has not be disclosed to consumers.

Second, the government wants to extend the filtering scheme to include peer-to-peer traffic, in addition to normal HTTP traffic.

Protests over the scheme:
[http://www.news.com.au/technology/story/0,,24795948-5014108,00.html](http://www.news.com.au/technology/story/0,,24795948-5014108,00.html)

Consumers in the dark over participating ISPs:
[http://www.news.com.au/technology/story/0,,24823359-5014239,00.html](http://www.news.com.au/technology/story/0,,24823359-5014239,00.html)

Scheme to be extended:
[http://www.news.com.au/technology/story/0,28348,24833959-5014239,00.html](http://www.news.com.au/technology/story/0,28348,24833959-5014239,00.html)

Beware.  Your country may be next. :(

A national broadband infrastructure is being planned at the moment, but there are big issues about who will be building the infrastructure.
