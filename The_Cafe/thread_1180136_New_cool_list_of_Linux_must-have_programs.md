### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-06-06
Nice work. Nevertheless, I do have some comments.

In the image section I would strongly suggest to include digikam and remove F-Spot. Digikam is far more superior and feature rich than F-spot, it includes editing tools, raw editing, catalog tools with EXIF and IPTC support, geo-tagging and much more. Digikam has everything you need to organize and edit photos. You don't even need to touch Gimp.

I was kind of disappointed to see aMule and Vuze and not Deluge on the p2p list. Ok, vuze is fancy and has options for everything. I wouldn't be surprise if it could make some coffee too. But it is kind of bloated and a resource hog. Well I don't like java applications. Anyway, if you want just a torrent client, with everything you need to download torrents, with several options (webui, remote gtk and cli) for connecting to a remote server, a nice interface an easy configuration, then Deluge is the killer application.

I think smplayer deserves to be side-by-side with VLC, instead of Totem and Mplayer. It's a frontend for mplayer, whith excellent support for subtitles and lots of nice features. Is the killer player in my opinion.

In the security realm, I would suggest taking a look at [moblock/mobloquer](http://moblock-deb.sourceforge.net/) and [iplist](http://iplist.sourceforge.net/). 

An application that I consider a must have is easystroke. I also like screenlets, specially because the terminal applet. I have several of this on my widget layer, for monitoring connections, iptables, moblock logs and other stuff. I also like the terminal applet of awn dock. I don't like, but there are lot's of people who use gnome-do.

Just my 2 cents.
