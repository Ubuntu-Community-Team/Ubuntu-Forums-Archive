### Post by LinuxFanBoi on 2009-11-17
I just got done reading some rather unsettling articles criticizing Ubuntu;

1. [Two Simple Suggestions for Ubuntu]("http://www.linux-mag.com/id/7607")
2. [Hey Ubuntu, Stop Making Linux Look Bad]("http://www.linux-mag.com/id/7600")

This got me to thinking.  We know that you can get "beta" releases of an upcoming distribution before it goes "live."  That's how we get a list of known bugs when we are offered the option to upgrade.

What I suggest is this.  Add a feature to the upgrade process that will search your configuration to see if it is likely to encounter any of the known bugs in the current release.  Then give the user a recommendation.  If the test proves to show a low potential for bugs,  recommend that the user upgrade,  If there is a high probability that the user's is going to encounter the known bugs,  recommend that the user not upgrade,  and retest again at a later time.

An example would be a dialog window that appears saying "Based on known issues and you're current configuration,  it is highly probably that upgrading will produce a negative experience." Then give three options,  One simply expands to show the user where they are likely to see issues,  the other two would be for the user to choose to either upgrade and accept the risk,  or to wait and try again after patches and fixes are released.

Comments, suggestions, flames welcome. Flames will be ignored. ;)
