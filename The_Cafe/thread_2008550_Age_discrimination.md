### Post by QIII on 2012-06-23
There is a very simple reason militaries throughout history have recruited the young and passed over the old except in dire need:

Youth are more impressionable and therefore more malleable.

The Soldier's (Sailor's, Marine's, Airman's) life is one of privation and sacrifice.  It is a life that requires early, nearly monastic conditioning of both the psyche and the body.

By the late 20s, the time has simply passed for most people to be trained into the mental rigors of that life, whatever their physical condition.

If your friend is 45, he would be a Private like all of the 18 year olds if he enters, unless he has a college degree and would be an E-4 with the 22 year olds.  If he enlists with an OCS option and fails, he's back to Enlisted.  If he succeeds, he's a Second Lieutenant with the 22 year olds.  If he gets a Direct Commission because he is a doctor or a lawyer, he would be a Captain with all the 26 year olds.

Maybe he can keep up physically.  But he'll be sitting at the kiddie table at supper.

There is also the fact that you can't get in unless you can retire with 20 years by age 55.  

There were recent allowances for age because of end-strength requirements, but those have gone away.

I'll be brutally honest.  In my several commands I would have found a 45 year old private to have been more trouble than he was worth.  My First Sergeants and Sergeants Major were that age and I turned to them for counsel based on their years of experience.

### Post by jockyburns on 2012-06-23
Dunno how things are in other countries, but here in the UK we have legislation to cover all sorts of discrimination. However, our government are well into the process of raising retirement age. Now there's no statutory requirement to actually retire at all. We have over a million young people unemployed and a lot of experts use the argument , that if the retirement age were lowered to , say 60yrs, this would have the effect of creating jobs for the unemployed youth. 
I have personally seen age discrimination work against me (I'm almost 56yrs old now) I applied for a job working on railway coach building. A carpenter by trade, at the interview, a youngish chap (couldn't have even been 25yrs old) looked at my CV and said "Oh, your a carpenter! Ahh well might as well go through the motions.":rolleyes:
First question,,,, "Can you read drawings?" WTF, As a carpenter, I've spent most of my working life reading and working from drawings. I explained this to the young man and he replied "Well these drawings are very technical you know. I responded by mentioning that I have Advanced Level Engineering Drawing (passed at school in the 6th form) His reply was "Ohh well that was almost 40 yrs ago."
Needless to say, I didn't get the job. 
A few weeks later I was speaking with a friend in the local, who told me he'd been successful getting a job at the same place. I asked what he did, and he told me he places the carriage seating into position, then places 8 or so bolts through the pre-drilled holes and holds a spanner on the bolts , whilst someone underneath tightens up the nuts using an air wrench. I asked what his background was and he says "I'm a printer by trade."

But he was only 32 yrs old. :lolflag::lolflag:
