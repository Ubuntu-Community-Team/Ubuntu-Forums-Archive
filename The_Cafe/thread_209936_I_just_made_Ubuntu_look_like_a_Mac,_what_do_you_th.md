### Post by aysiu on 2006-07-06

I have to admit, every time I use my wife's Powerbook I'm at once wowed by the slick  good looks as I am repulsed by the user interface.

A few things that bother me about the Mac behavior:

1. When I Cmd-Tab to a minimized application, the application's windows do not get brought forward--they stay minimized. And there are a lot of applications for which there are no keyboard shortcuts to bring them forward once you're focused on the application.

2. F11 is a handy way to show the desktop, but it's too easy to click on the wrong part of the desktop and have all the windows pop right back over your mouse cursor.

3. The inability to maximize applications. Sometimes that + button's behavior makes no sense, especially when I'm looking at files and folders in that multiple-column view.

4. It's too easy to accidentally drag and drop an icon off the dock (thus removing it) when trying to simply move the icon to another position on the dock.

5. Keyboard shortcuts aren't easy to assign for launching applications.

I think I'll just leave it at five.

If you can tell me theming Gnome or KDE to look like Mac also enables those five annoying behaviors, then, yes, I will just get a Mac instead.
