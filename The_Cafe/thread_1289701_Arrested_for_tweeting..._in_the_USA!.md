### Post by t0p on 2009-10-12
Have you seen[ this]("http://rawstory.com/2009/10/g20-protester-arrested-for-twitter/")?  Elliot Madison, a social worker, was arrested for tweeting!  To be precise: he has been accused of "hindering apprehension of people who were engaging in criminal activities... "The criminals identified in the warrant are protesters against the G20 [in Pittsburgh]. Their crime? They were demonstrating in the street without a permit."

Earlier today I listened to last week's ["Off the Hook" radio show]("http://www.2600.com/news/view/article/11879").  The presenter, Emmanuel Goldstein, interviewed Madison's lawyer, who explained that Madison has been specifically accused of warning demonstrators not to go to a particular area because the police had issued an order for people in that area to disperse.  The lawyer says: how can that be illegal?  Madison was actually *helping* the police by preventing demonstrators to go there.  The lawyer also asserted that Madison's tweets were First Amendment-protected speech: if he was on the street and said to a buddy "Don't go down there or the cops will arrest you," there wouldn't be any problem.  But because it was done over the medium of Twitter, that somehow makes it a crime?  The lawyer thinks not.  The police obviously disagree.

The authorities have been doing their best to paint Madison as some sort of anarchist criminal.  They raided his home in Queens, [they told newspapers]("http://ubuntuforums.org/they%20also%20confiscated%20from%20Madison%27s%20home%2011%20gas%20masks,%20five%20pairs%20of%20goggles%20and%20test%20tubes%20and%20beakers.%20They%20said%20they%20also%20took%20away%20anarchist%20books%20and%20pictures%20of%20Marx%20and%20Lenin."), and seized "evidence" including "11 gas masks, five pairs of goggles and test tubes and beakers. They said they also took away anarchist books and pictures of Marx and Lenin."  As if pictures of Marx and Lenin are somehow proof of Madison's evil nature!  Unfortunately, the press have been "parroting [the FBI's] claims". Which Vic Walczak, legal director for the Pennsylvania ACLU, sees as pure "intimidation," and part of a "[much bigger war on demonstrators" in Pittsburgh]("http://rawstory.com/2009/10/g20-protester-arrested-for-twitter/").

What's really ironic is the US authorities' double standards over these matters.  In Iran, after the disputed election, demonstrators used Twitter to help organize their protests - and the USA called on the Iranian authorities to not interfere with the service.  Twitter was seen as a tool of the righteous.  But when it is used for exactly the same purposes in America, suddenly it's wrong.

I urge anyone who would like to know more to listen to last week's "Off the Hook" - mp3 download available [here]("http://www.2600.com/offthehook/mp3files/broadband/off_the_hook__20091007-64.mp3").  It's interesting to learn how First Amendment-protected speech suddenly becomes sinister when it's done online.
