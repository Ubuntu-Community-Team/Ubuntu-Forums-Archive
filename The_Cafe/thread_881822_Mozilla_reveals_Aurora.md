### Post by Mazza558 on 2008-08-06
This is what I just posted on the [Digg article]("http://digg.com/software/Mozilla_reveals_the_Firefox_of_the_future"):

> What about people who just want to do something simple, like find out the weather/news/sports results? Will they want to go through a maze of thumbnails? It all seems like designing something extremely complicated to suit the needs of a minority.

The mobile interface further down, on the other hand, is something I'd want to use. It doesn't let the UI get in the way, and looks fast and easy. It could work just as well on a desktop.

When designing a UI, people need to ask themselves:

1. What do people want to actually do? Will this new UI get them to their destination quicker?
2. Is this new UI confusing? Could almost anyone sit down and use it?
3. Are the new features worthwhile? Would people actually use them?

Some interesting ideas in those videos, but they definitely need streamlining and refining.
