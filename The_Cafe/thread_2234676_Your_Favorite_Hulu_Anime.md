### Post by SeijiSensei on 2014-07-16
What especially annoys me about Hulu is that even if I pay for a Hulu Plus subscription, I still have to watch all the ads on the shows not specifically on Plus.  What's the point of subscribing?

Like deadflowr I rarely watch dubbed shows, but if you want one suggestion on Hulu, then I'll throw out the zany [Oh! Edo Rocket]("http://www.hulu.com/oh-edo-rocket"), a satirical look at political repression via a sci-fi story set in 1843 Edo.  It has an especially good "localization" by its R1 licensor, Funimation.  I'm told the English dubs for [Baccano!]("http://www.hulu.com/baccano") are pretty good as well.

Another good dub, though not on Hulu, is Aniplex's release of [Nodame Cantabile]("http://www.crackle.com/c/nodame-cantabile") on the Crackle site.

If you're serious about watching anime, you should try watching shows with subtitles.  I'd start by trying the hilariously over-the-top [Kill la Kill]("http://www.crunchyroll.com/kill-la-kill") at Crunchyroll and browse around the hundreds of titles on that site.  For suggestions I'd try the forums at [Anime Suki]("http://forums.animesuki.com/forumdisplay.php?f=15") and [MyAnimeList]("http://myanimelist.net/forum/?board=16").
