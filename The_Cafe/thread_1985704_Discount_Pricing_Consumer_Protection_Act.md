### Post by Lucradia on 2012-05-23
**Note:** This thread will be locked by forums staff (likely by me reporting the posts) if the discussion gets too heated.

Discount Pricing Consumer Protection Act, a name, was brought up in an email by Wisconsin Senator Herb Kohl:

> According to a report in today&#8217;s Wall Street Journal, Sony and Samsung - two of the world&#8217;s largest television manufacturers - &#8220;have begun trying to force retailers to rein in discounts on televisions, threatening to cut off those who sell TVs for below the manufacturer&#8217;s minimum prices.&#8221;  Sony and Samsung have implemented this new policy to reap higher profits &#8211; from the wallets of consumers.  The same news story also cited other electronics companies that have &#8220;tough policies restricting the prices at which their products can be sold&#8230;&#8221;

Until a few years ago, that kind of vertical price fixing was against the law.  Unfortunately, the Supreme Court narrowly overturned the century-old ban on retail price maintenance in 2007.  Since then I&#8217;ve been working to restore it.

As the chairman of the Senate Antitrust, Competition Policy and Consumer Rights panel, I wrote legislation to overturn the Court&#8217;s decision.   Justice Breyer, in his dissenting opinion, estimated that if only 10% of manufacturers engage in vertical price fixing, the volume of commerce affected would be $300 billion, costing the average family of four an additional $750 to $1,000 for retail goods every year.    

My bill, the &#8220;Discount Pricing Consumer Protection Act,&#8221; will allow retailers to sell goods below a threshold price set by manufacturers.  I&#8217;m pleased that it was approved by the Senate Judiciary Committee last November, and I&#8217;ll continue to work to bring it before the full Senate for debate.  Until then, I&#8217;m hopeful that Sony and Samsung and other manufacturers who have policies against discounting find that consumers will look elsewhere for affordable products.

As a businessman, I appreciate that retailers welcome flexibility in their business with customers.  As a Senator representing the people of Wisconsin, I know that discounting and discount stores play a vital role in family budgeting.

Want people's thoughts on the subject.
