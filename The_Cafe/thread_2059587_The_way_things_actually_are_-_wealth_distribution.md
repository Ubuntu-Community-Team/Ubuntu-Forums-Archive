### Post by yeehi on 2012-09-18
I saw an illuminating chart on a website looking at wealth [here]("http://www2.ucsc.edu/whorulesamerica/power/wealth.html").

The chart shows you the way people *think *wealth is distributed, the way wealth in fact actually *is *distributed and the way people believe it *ought *to be distributed.

Clearly, these three things are very different from each other. The author mentions that the way people think wealth *should *be distributed is in fact even *more *egalitarian than in a country like Sweden!

The website contains a lot of other interesting information.

The chart is below.

[IMG]http://www2.ucsc.edu/whorulesamerica/power/images/wealth/Actual_estimated_ideal_wealth_distribution.gif[/IMG]

It would be nice if a python guru could create an on-line, interactive similar chart that would let us use sliders to first ask us how we *think *wealth is distributed, then show how we think wealth *should be* distributed (or redistributed). Results could be anonymously recorded and compiled into new charts, displaying the aggregate of what viewers think
