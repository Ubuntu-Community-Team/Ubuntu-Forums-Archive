### Post by linuxforartists on 2011-01-13
I was surprised at how much I liked it.  When Columbia Pictures announced they were making a Facebook movie, the idea seemed ludicrous at the time.  

Reminded me a little of "There Will Be Blood" and "Citizen Kane."  A man can achieve everything he wants, but at the cost of his soul.  

The director, David Fincher (Se7en, Fight Club) has really matured and mastered how to direct actors.  His earlier movies were more visual.  There was only one Fincher-style swooping camera move in "The Social Network," at the beginning of the scene where Mark Zuckerberg and Sean Parker are at the nightclub.  

And the music.  Can't go wrong with Trent Reznor doing the score.
