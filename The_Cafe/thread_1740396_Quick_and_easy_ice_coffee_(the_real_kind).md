### Post by Foxcow on 2011-04-27
For all you coffee lovers out there that like a nice tasty, warm-weather, caffeinated drink, I bring to you cold brewed coffee.  

I don't know about other coffee drinkers but I really enjoy the taste of a nice hot coffee but in the summer time, I get really hot after drinking one.  Ice coffee is, well, nice and cool and you can control the strength of the drink.


**Things you will need:**
-Two pitchers that can be sealed
-Metal strainer
-Coffee.  If you get beans (recommended), you will need a grinder too


Pitchers

Strainers
[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/mQKLY.jpg[/IMG]

Cofee.  I LOVE this stuff!!  The best I'd ever had.
[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/hjyy4.jpg[/IMG]


First things first, you want to use a 4:1 water to beans/grinds ratio.  I usually do about 1 cup of coffee to to 4 cups of water.  Mix your water and coffee in jug number 1:

[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/yTe5r.jpg[/IMG]

A funnel makes it easier:

[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/B1Vzg.jpg[/IMG]

The mixed result:

[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/ty2UU.jpg[/IMG]


Next, the coffee needs time to brew.  The mixture goes in the fridge from anywhere from 8 to 14 hours.  I usually go for ~12 to 14 hours.  Prepare it in the evening, its ready in the morning.


Straining the concoction can be a bit tricky.  Some people like to strain twice with the metal strainers and once with a paper filter but I think the metal strainers do a good enough job.  I don't have a pic of the strained result unfortunately.  This is how I like to do it:

[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/UV7mA.jpg[/IMG]

Be aware that this stuff is **STRONG**.  1/4 to 1/3 of a pint glass of the "brew" is about the strength of a cup of coffee.

-Pour about 1/3 pint of the brew
-Add cold water but leave enough room for some heavy cream or milk.  Personal preference will dictate how much you add.
-Stir in a spoonful of sugar and a few ice cubes.

Voila!
[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/Eaq6Q.jpg[/IMG]
