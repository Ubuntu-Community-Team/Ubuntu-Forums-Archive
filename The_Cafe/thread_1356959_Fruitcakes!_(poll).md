### Post by handy on 2009-12-16
They aren't that hard to make, the following recipe is good for xmas or weddings, it should be made at least 2 weeks before eating & will keep for 4 months.

**Rich Fruit Cake**

Ingredients:

375 g (12 oz) raisins
375 g sultanas
125 g (4 oz) mixed peel
125 g glace cherries (I leave them out)
125 g dates (stoned)
3 tablespoons rum
3 tablespoons sherry

Cake Mixture:

250 g (8 oz) butter
1 cup brown sugar
grated rind of 1 lemon
1 tablespoon golden syrup
2 tablespoons marmalade
5 large eggs
2 cups plain flour (I have used wholemeal & it works fine)
1 teaspoon mixed spice
1 teaspoon cinnamon
1/4/ teaspoon salt
125 g blanched almonds, chopped
extra almonds
extra rum

Prepare fruit. Chop the raisins & place into a bowl with the sultanas & finely chopped peel. Cut the cherries & dates into small pieces & add the other fruit. Sprinkle with rum & sherry, cover & leave overnight.

Grease a 20 cm (8 in) round or square cake tin & line it with 2 thicknesses each of brown & buttered greaseproof paper.
Beat the butter until soft & creamy then beat in the brown sugar & lemon rind. When the mixture is creamy & light in colour add the golden syrup & marmalade. Beat well.
Add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition. Add 1 tablespoon of flour with the last egg. Sift the remaining flour, spice & salt together & stir into the creamed mixture alternately with the fruit & chopped almonds.

Spoon the mixture into the prepared tin. arrange the extra almond in a pattern around the top. Bake in a slow oven (300F-150C) for about 4 hours or until cooked. remove the cake from the oven & immediately sprikle with about 1 tablespoon of extra rum. Remove the cake from the tin leaving the paper on the cake. Wrap the cake in a tea towel  allow it so cool. Store in an airtight tin or wrap in foil of plastic wrap.


Age & eat, almost as good as a fine wine...
