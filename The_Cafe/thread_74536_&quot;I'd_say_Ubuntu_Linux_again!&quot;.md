### Post by newbie2 on 2005-10-12
[COLOR="DarkRed"][FONT="Book Antiqua"]"For this, I highly recommend getting  Ubuntu Linux.[/COLOR] A sleek live CD implementation of Linux with a lot of  bells and whistles that make it very attractive to first time users.  The geekiest thing you need to do here is to enable your computer to  boot-up from your CD/DVD drive. This is quick and painless.
The next method requires more technical competency than the first one  above. This setup allows you to install two operating systems on your  computer. This is called dual-booting. Dual-booting does not mean  running two operating systems at the same time (well, that is for  another article on virtual computing environment such as VirtualPC  and Xen) but it means being able to select from two (or more) OS at  boot time. Before you do this, make sure that you know how to  partition your computer's hard drive and also be able to figure out  what MBR stands for (Master Boot Record). For dual-boot systems, I  often recommend having a separate data partition that you can share  between the installed operating systems. What is the recommended  Linux distribution for this?[COLOR="DarkRed"] I'd say Ubuntu Linux again!"[/FONT][/COLOR]
