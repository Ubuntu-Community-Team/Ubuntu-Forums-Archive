### Post by tseliot on 2006-07-15
> **slugkilla said:**
> Have you guy herd about how vista will have 6 versions? And one of them is made to support 16GB of memory!! Is that even possible on Linux?
16GB is nothing ;) 

If you enable the support in the kernel (in Linux) you can use up to **[COLOR="Red"]64GB of RAM[/COLOR]**.

There are 3 possible configurations in the kernel:
1) using up to 900MB of RAM (that was used in Ubuntu Hoary by default)
2) using up to 4GB of RAM (that was used in Ubuntu Breezy and Dapper by default)
3) using up to 64GB of RAM
