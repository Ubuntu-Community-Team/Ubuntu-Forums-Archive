### Post by Ray Hamilton on 2009-02-10
TBOL3 you don't say WHY you want to learn Spanish.  In my experience, motive plays a great part in motivation which leads to increased memory retention and learning. So when you HAVE to do something there is more motivation. Interest also plays a large part in learning - if you are not interested in a subject, it is much more difficult to learn.  So you should have some success in using your interest in technology combined with learning Spanish - however you may only be able to communicate on that subject e.g. you might find a problem ordering a meal if you can only talk about bytes and nibbles!! You could try hanging out in the Spanish forums.

### Post by el_itur on 2009-02-11
I learned english on my own and yes, being it the dominant language in the tech world helps a lot... I suggest you to try to catch up on spanish communities of things you like (the argentina loco team is quite active!).

For reading, I don't know what you like but I would try to pick whatever it comes to my hand, Jorge Luis Borges wrote his first things in english and his way of writing is quite influenced by that, he writes tales so it's quick to read. I would take that.

In that line other writers I enjoy are Juan José Saer or Ricardo Piglia. The spanish translations of José de Saramago are great and it's also very spoken-like. I think the spanish literature is the vastest and richest of all (and I'm not the first to say it), so it souldn't be hard to find anything you like.

Movies... I think that is quite another level, as spoken spanish has so many accent and jargon depending of the country or region you are talking, and there are just too many spanish speaking countries, Spain spanish is the hardest to catch IMO. I would recommend you some argetinian movies just for you to try and see what happens: Moebius ([http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0117069/](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0117069/)), Hombre mirando al sudeste ([http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0091214/](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0091214/)) are the ones that I like the most. But again, the production is quite vast.

Anyway, after you try all of that, the best way to learn a language is by talking a lot on it. Having english-speaking friends boosted my knowledge to almost native (I still find hard to pronounce a lot of thinks but... :)

hope it helps
