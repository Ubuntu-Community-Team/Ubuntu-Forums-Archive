### Post by drawkcab on 2009-07-14
I've bought two laptops from powernotebooks.com and am very pleased.  Basically they buy decent whitebooks from overseas (Compal, MSI, etc.) and then put together a custom build for you (you pick the parts on their webpage).  You can select no OS if you do not want a MS license.  Their in-house brand (rebranded Compals and MSIs) is by far the best deal because they offer a free 3yr warranty.  

An even cheaper way to go about it is to buy a whitebook from a retailer like zipzoomfly.com and then order the components.  You kind of have to know what you are doing, not just in terms of assembly, but in terms of what hardware will work with the whitebook.  A lot of people have saved tons of cash this way.
