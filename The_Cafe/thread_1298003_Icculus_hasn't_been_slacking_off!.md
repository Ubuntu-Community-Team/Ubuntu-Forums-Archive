Have a look at this:
[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=news_item&px=NzYyNQ](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=news_item&px=NzYyNQ)

In summary, Ryan Gordan, the guy who has so far not completed his port of Unreal Tournament 3 to Linux after years of work, just came out with preliminary work on a "universal binary" for Linux, similar to OS X. His "FatELF" design would allow binaries for 64-bit, 32-bit, powerPC, ARM, and whatever other CPU architectures, to be bundled into one single file. Possibly even binaries for other OSs like FreeBSD, bundled with Linux versions.

I hope this works out well. It wouldn't be well-suited for packaging in distro repos, but for software vendors it could be great!
