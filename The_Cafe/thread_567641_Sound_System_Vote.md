### Post by BuffaloX on 2007-10-05
Sound in Linux is far from on par with windows.
Missing features for M-Audio Revolution 7-1

No control of bass, like phase shift, redirection to/from sub - front/center.
No adjustment of distance to speakers. (Which is crucial).
No control of frequency range.
Lacking master/front volume control.

On the positive side, audio quality is actually OK-
Surround is supported.
If audio is routed through JACK, all kinds of weird stuff is possible. :)

I am quite optimistic that as Linux is gaining more acceptance with hardware vendors, drivers will improve.
