### Post by scheuri on 2009-05-07
Technically (AFAIK) you are not allowed to sell GPL-Software (mind me, I am not saying opensource, I said GPL).
You are allowed to charge for the CD/DVD and "your work" to press/burn that CD/DVD.

However, you are not allowed to charge for the software on it.
On the other hand, you may charge more if you support your Linux Group or a charity (but well, then you need to give the money to them, of course ;))

### Post by GeneralZod on 2009-05-07
You can charge any sum you like for GPL software, and don't have to hide behind the "I'm just charging for the price of the CD!" excuse, either :)

[http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/selling.html](http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/selling.html)

However, when providing the *source code*, you may only charge a small fee (i.e. - the cost of the CD + shipping, say), which is where I guess the confusion comes in.
