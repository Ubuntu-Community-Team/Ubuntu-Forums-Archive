Although French, I have never drunk wine. However, I can tell you that the container (agreed, it's not a definite or accurate indicator) is a decent heuristic of the quality of the wine. Expensive wine tends to be better and also tends not to come into "cubis" (boxed wine). Cheap wine tends to be not as good and also tends to come into boxes more often. So boxed wine has a higher probability (*) of being bad.

(*) Yes, this means some boxed wine will be better than some bottled wine, but on average bottled wine is of higher quality than boxed wine.

This is of course a purely probabilistic analysis that does not take into account the aging of the wine. I'm way better at math than oenology after all.
