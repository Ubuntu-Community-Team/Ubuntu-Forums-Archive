### Post by QIII on 2012-03-09
That sound and heat is unused compute cycles not costing you anything.  :D

Um...

I have a lot of machines running a lot of distributed science projects.  The CPU intensive ones use about what a bright light bulb or two would -- say 100 to 140 watts 24x7.  Even with power as cheap as it is in the hydropower PNW of the US, it adds up.  The ones that use GPUs suck up a lot more.

While your CPU is being used for those projects, the "CPU cycles" are used, not unused.

There is a reason why I use Noctua coolers on those machines.  They get hot.  Which means watts.  Which means money.

The whole "unused cpu cycles" thing is subterfuge.  Your CPU is certainly being used.  Hard.

There seems to be a general misunderstanding when it comes to power consumption.  People think that because a computer is more efficient it won't draw as much as older ones.  Sorry.  That is "more efficient" per unit of work.

But if you tap a 145 watt cpu, you are using more power than a very old one cranking like the little engine that could at 40 watts.

And power supplies only draw what is being asked of them, plus a bit more for inefficiency.

Your 850 watt power supply isn't drawing 850 watts from the wall when your machine is idling.
