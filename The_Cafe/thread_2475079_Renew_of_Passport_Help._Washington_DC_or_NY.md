_**Update: This only applies to US Citizens. Foreign citizens don't get US passports.**_

Let me check some dates.  These are pre-pandemic.
I mailed the renewal on Aug 30 and received the new passport  on Sep 22.
My first passport forms were sent overnight Apr 19 and I was on a plane to Japan April 26th.

Passport renewals aren't the same as Visa renewals. Might help if you were clear about that.  The Philippine Embassy wouldn't have anything to do with US passports ... unless a US citizen was going to Philippines for non-tourist reasons or if staying over 30 days.

When I needed a passport renewed, it wasn't an emergency, I just filled out the forms about 90 days before my trip and hoped.  I didn't pay any extra expedite fee.  Took under 30 days.

When I needed my first passport, I needed it quickly.  The company paid a service to hand-walk the paperwork through the system and used overnight deliveries each way.  The key was to visit a local US postoffice that has passport processing support so they can validate your signature. I think that's only needed with the first passport.  Around here, only 1 -in- 10 post offices do passport stuff.

What is required is all listed online. Renewals should be easier. I don't remember going to the postoffice at all.  [https://www.usps.com/international/passports.htm](https://www.usps.com/international/passports.htm)  That link is really good. Lots of caveats are explained.

This link [https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/passports/how-apply/processing-times.html](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/passports/how-apply/processing-times.html) says the expected timeframes for passport processing.

A passport holder for a non-US country can always leave (unless they are in jail for other reasons).  The visa in the next county, if required, has nothing to do with the USA.  Citizens of X country can always return to X country. No VISA needed. They are citizens. Of course, if country X has laws they've bent/broken, or if their passport shows they've visited countries forbidden by country X, that's completely different.  

Hopefully it isn't a "family emergency" due to bad health.  I've had to travel for those reasons a few times. Be certain to check with the airlines for a fare reduction. They won't be fantastic airfares, but they won't be the typical 3x cost that a last minute trip usually runs either. Just need to ask and provide a little paperwork to the airline.
