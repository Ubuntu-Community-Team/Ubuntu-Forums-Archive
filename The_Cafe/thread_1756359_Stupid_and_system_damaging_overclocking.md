### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-05-12
The CPU might burn out if you play with the voltages, but anything strong enough to burn the CPU will probably take its toll on the motherboard too. If you have a low-end CPU, you've probably got a low-end motherboard as well that won't be able to take this kind of shock.

If you want to play with the clock speed and get a modest improvement, go ahead. But if you play with the voltage to get a much higher clock, you could do damage to the motherboard. Also note that higher clocks also mean higher temperatures, and I'm assuming you only have the stock Intel cooler on your CPU.
