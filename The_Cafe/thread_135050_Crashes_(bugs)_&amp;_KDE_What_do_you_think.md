### Post by aysiu on 2006-02-23
I wouldn't say Mac OS X is the model of stability. I've seen my fair share of rainbow circles of death. I know some people are going to disagree with me on this, but the most stable OS I've used is Windows XP--no blue screens of death, no deathly lockups... Control-Alt-Delete gets me out of any bind.

That said, KDE is buggy if you play around with it too much. I keep it pretty vanilla of late, and I've yet to see it do anything weird (freeze up, crash). A lot of times, though, it's not KDE itself (as a desktop environment) but KDE applications that have a tendency to crash. I've seen those same KDE applications crash as often in Gnome and XFCE as well.

While the Linux kernel is stable, the desktop has too many variables in play. You have multiple desktop environments, packages that may have been aliened in from an RPM, some packages from source, some packages from DEBs, some commercial software (think Firefox), some written-in-someone's-basement software (think SharpMusique). With all of those elements at play, it's easy to screw up your system if you play around too much.
