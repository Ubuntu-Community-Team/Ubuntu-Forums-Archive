### Post by monsterstack on 2009-05-26
Yes, you read that correctly. After the Pirate Bay verdict, you may recall that the Judge, Tomas Norström, was [investigated]("http://www.mcvuk.com/news/34045/Pirate-Bay-owners-could-face-re-trial") [mcvuk.com] for being biased. Specifically, he belonged to a couple of pro-copyright groups affiliated with the Prosecution. And now the Judge (Ulrika Ihrfelt) put in charge of deciding whether or not the first Judge was biased has been [removed for being biased]("http://www.mcvuk.com/news/34385/Second-judge-removed-from-Pirate-Bay-case") [mcvuk.com]. You just can't make this stuff up, sometimes.

> 
This is the second time that the Swedish legal system has been accused of a pro-copyright agenda. After the conclusion of the first trial, it emerged that the judge Tomas Norström was a member of both the Swedish Association for the Protection of Industrial Property and the Swedish Copyright Association.

Accusations of bias followed, and subsequently fellow judge Ulrika Ihrfelt was appointed to examine the claims. However, now it has been found that Ihrfelt herself is also a member of both groups – a discovery that has lead to her removal from the process.
