### Post by twisted_steel on 2005-02-19
A kernel upgrade would not affect your current system as you see it - your programs, settings, and directories will remain intact. What is does upgrade is the "heart" of the operating system - things like drivers and schedulers.
   
 In Hoary, there are a few additional apps that will be included in the next version of Gnome. It has newer versions of applications and I imagine the kernel as well. If you are new to Linux and don't want to run into upgrade problems, I'd wait for the final release of Hoary to come out. The preview is scheduled for release on March 9, with the final slated for release on April 6. If you'd like a peek at Gnome 2.10, check out [Davyd's screenshots]("http://www.gnome.org/%7Edavyd/gnome-2-10/").
