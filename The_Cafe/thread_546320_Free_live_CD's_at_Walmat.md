### Post by Darkhack on 2007-09-09
> **izanbardprince said:**
> To be honest, have you ever TRIED installing Windows from a CD?

Yes.  How many people reinstall Windows?  People buy a computer from the box stores and the OEM version of Windows that is already installed includes the drivers as installed by the manufacturer.  Most normal people won't even reinstall Windows when it goes to crap.  They hire people like us to pop in the CD and wait an hour or two while it reinstalls.

The kind of people whom are capable of installing and using Ubuntu have already heard of Linux and most likely have heard of Ubuntu.  If someone wants to try it, they will come to us, just as we all did.

Besides, how many people want to get rid of Windows?  They might be sick of viruses and spyware, but Ubuntu is not "Windows without viruses and spyware".  They want to run their current applications (If you think they can figure out WINE, then you've never talked to normal people about computers).  People hate change.  Even if it is minor.  The lack of a Start button will cause them to freak.  Yes, "Applications" is actually more explanatory than a Start menu, but most people will still ask how to run their programs upon seeing this.

Now what about people who have never heard of Linux?  Put yourself in their mind.  They have used Windows for nearly a decade.  They use Windows at work or school.  Their friends and family use Windows.  All software they see at a box store is designed to run on Windows.  The whole world is Windows.  Do you really expect someone to change something as crucial as an operating system to an alternative they have never even heard of before?  How would you feel if some manic nerd came up to you all frantic about how superior Leenix brand automobiles were.  They use three wheels (two in back, one in front) and a joystick to steer.  Oh, by the way... the car is free because it is worked on by hobbyist car enthusiasts.  Would you be willing to give up your Ford, Toyota, or Honda for a brand of car that you have never heard of that uses an interface you are unfamiliar with?

Price does matter too.  How does a normal person expect a free operating system supported by hobbyist computer programmers to compete with a giant corporation like Microsoft with their $400 copy of Windows Vista, when they have never even heard of Linux, and everyone they know uses Windows?

In summary... Anyone who thinks that Linux can succeed by giving out free CDs to Joe and Jane Average has no grasp of the real world or how normal people think and use computers.
