### Post by pfeiffep on 2015-04-25
I had a need to upgrade my Dell Laptop from 4 GB to it's maximum 8 GB. I went to Amazon and found a Crucial brand kit of 2 4GB chips advertised for my Dell. 
I installed them both and I couldn't get Win 7 to run correctly. I took on out and replaced it with one of the original 2 GB chips - everything worked just fine.
Amazon sent me an advance replacement which had the exact same symptoms. I used the memory test available in GRUB's start screen and had instant failure.

Wellllll ... one set of bad chips is sorta ok, but the second probably not. When I loaded BIOS hoping to 'fix the problem the new chips were identified, but their speed was not.
Calling Crucial for help at this point proved extremely helpful - they recommended a slower ram speed than what I purchased.
[LIST]
[*]Original chips 1333 Mhz
[*]Crucial chips  1600 Mhz
[/LIST]
Their explanation was that the combination of 2 speeds enable the pair to work at the 1333Mhz rate, but the system wouldn't work with both chips at 1600Mhz. They provided Amazon part numbers for their suggested replacement.

Kudos to Crucial their help also Kudos to Amazon who is refunding my money.
