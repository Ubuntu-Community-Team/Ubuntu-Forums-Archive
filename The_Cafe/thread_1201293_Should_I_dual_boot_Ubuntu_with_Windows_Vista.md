### Post by Leed on 2009-07-01
Dual Boot is the best thing you can do, you don't want to lose the windows that you otherwise would have to buy, so make sure you don't delete it when installing ubuntu, I always manually make partitions. 

Any operating system can suddenly cause a problem, that's when dual booting comes in handy, you can still get all your files, in most cases you can also solve the problem. I also recommend making a seperate NTFS partition to store your files on, that way any operating system can mess up its partition and you still have your stuff. 

Even if you primary use Windows, having an Ubuntu will come handy (not only when your windows dies from the viruses it collects). Any time you need a program for something and don't want to go and buy it, you can check in Ubuntu for a free alternative... in most cases you will find one. 

Just do it, be open, that's how I started, that's how I eventually abandoned windows... and I still have it on Dual Boot just in case.
