### Post by The Cog on 2019-09-27
I would interpret moving it forwards as making it earlier. From Wednesday to Monday (or to the preceding Friday if only counting weekdays).
Equally, moving it backwards would be delaying it, making it later.

Here in the UK, we talk of events that are scheduled in the future, and of bringing it forward (i.e closer to you, earlier) or pushing it back (away from you, further away in time). Often in hours, not days, such as bring a meeting forward from 3pm to 2pm.

If you're not familiar with the usage, then it's not obvious what it means, especially if you omit the old and new times.
