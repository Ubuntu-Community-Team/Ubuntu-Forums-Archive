### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-05-29
Local Community. Ours has different projects for people in my home state. One of our projects (Qimo4Kids) was to build some computers and load them with kid-friendly Ubuntu and donate them to needy schools and kids' charities. We even sent a bunch to another state.

We have "distro release parties" and "installfests" to introduce people to Ubuntu Linux, we have a booth at the Linux exibit, stuff like that.

It's a cool way to get involved and give a little something back to the folks who work so hard to give us this awesome distro without charge. And it's fun!
