### Post by Legendary_Bibo on 2010-09-03
I work as a math tutor at my college, and so far I've worked 8 hours between 2 days (I only work 13 hours a week on account of being a full time student), and the first day I didn't do anything, I just worked on homework. Today I was supposed to be in there for like 3 hours, and I only had one appointment for tutoring. All my sessions are an hour long, and the contract they put the students under is really strict, and if they don't uphold their part (not being more than 10 minutes late twice, etc.) I can drop them so the spot is filled. My only client was half an hour late, and when I started helping him he had to take a phone call that lasted the whole time (it was a family emergency, and my boss wanted to drop him from the appointment list, but I was nice enough to give him a third chance (I was supposed to meet him my first day on the job)). They then let me go home 2 hours early and they still paid me the two hours I didn't work. I get paid $10/hr for this job which is the highest wage I've had for a job, and the lowest amount of work I've had to do. Next week I'll have more clients though so the fun won't last much longer. :(

Also since how the department is right next to the cafeteria, the higher up staff will go to the cafeteria to get food for the underlings if we want it.
