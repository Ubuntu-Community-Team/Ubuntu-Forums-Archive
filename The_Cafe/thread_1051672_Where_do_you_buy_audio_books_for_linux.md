### Post by jxn on 2009-03-11
emusic.com has a pretty good selection of audiobooks, which it sells as mp3s.  There are only two drawbacks: 
[LIST=1]
[*]The initial download is some kind of zipped thing, a .emz file or something, which requires the use of a special app to decompress.  Luckily emusic supports their own (experimental, and somewhat buggy) download manager for linux, and a superior download manager called emusicj also exists.  See [http://www.kallisti.net.nz/EMusicJ/HomePage](http://www.kallisti.net.nz/EMusicJ/HomePage) 
[*]Additionally, their model is pseudo-subscription-based.  You buy monthly credits, and you only get so many credits a month, then they refresh whether you've spent them or not.  This hasn't been too troublesome for me, though.
[/LIST]

So far, I've had mostly positive results with emusic.
