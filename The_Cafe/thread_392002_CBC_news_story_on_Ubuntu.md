### Post by aysiu on 2007-03-29
[quote=Luggy]I understand your point, that it over simplifies the process of installing Ubuntu.[/quote]Well, actually, I don't mind the article oversimplifying. An article's job is not to go in-depth on all the ins and outs of the installation process. It should, however, examine generally the pros and cons of adopting the new product *and* warn people about what extra steps they might have to take in order to get things up and running.

Since the ISO part has been done to death, how about this? Instead of saying: > Either way, reboot your Windows machine with the disc in the CD drive, and rather than starting up Windows, the computer will run Ubuntu directly from the CD. You could say > *After you make sure your computer is set to boot from CDs,* reboot it with the disc in the CD drive, and rather than starting up Windows, the computer will run Ubuntu directly from the CD. Instead of saying: > Ubuntu comes with Open Office, a full (and free) office software suite that works with Microsoft documents, such as spreadsheets, text files and presentations. You could say > Ubuntu comes with Open Office, a full (and free) office software suite that*, despite some minor compatibility issues,* works with Microsoft documents, such as spreadsheets, text files and presentations. Small phrases like those make the reader aware of potential trouble down the line or the need to research an issue. Someone who reads the second phrasing about booting would *know* to do a Google search ("Oh. I have to make sure my computer is set to boot from CDs? Let me research how to do that... Ah, it's in the BIOS settings, and I have to press Escape or F1 or F12 to get into that."). That's much better than someone putting in the CD, seeing the computer still boot into Windows wonder "What's wrong with this? The article said I just had to put the CD in..."

Likewise, knowing in advance that there might be minor compatibility issues will more likely leave you with someone saying, "Compatibility issues? It seems to work just fine" (that's a happy user) as opposed to "Works with Microsoft documents? Why won't the formatting preserve in this Word document, then? What a load of BS!" (that's an unhappy user).

At the risk of sounding arrogant, I'm going to offer my little write-up on Ubuntu ([Is Ubuntu for You?](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=63315)), which isn't going to show up in Google News or get "Dugg," as far more likely to prepare people for both the benefits *and* the hardships of migrating from Windows to Ubuntu. [quote=Luggy]Like it or not, the CBC loves us and there is no such thing as bad publicity.[/quote] We're not in Hollywood, so I don't believe this to be true. I'd much rather have two users come of their own accord with open minds and stay than have ten users try Ubuntu, one being successful, and the nine others feeling lied to and then spreading the word to all their friends that "Linux is still just for geeks" and then posting a long rant on these forums about how Ubuntu and Linux are not "ready for the desktop."
