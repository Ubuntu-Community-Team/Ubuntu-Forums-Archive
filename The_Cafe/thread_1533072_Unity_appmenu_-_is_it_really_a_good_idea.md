### Post by x-shaney-x on 2010-07-17
As most of you know, the unity netbook interface will include a Mac style unified menubar amongst other things.

I'm interested in people's thoughts on it?
I would like this to be discussion on the usability aspects of the appmenu, NOT a discussion of whether or not Mr S is trying turn ubuntu into a Mac rip-off.

The appmenu is supposed to be a space saving initiative and may or may not include window controls in the menubar also.

My thoughts:
Having worked with Macs I have to say I hated the global menubar. I found it un-natural and it just makes sense to have application controls within the application window.

It may well save some space but that same space could be saved in other ways:

Having google chrome/firefox/opera style compact menu.  It would save space but keep the controls within the window.

Removing the un-necessary extra padding around the current Ambiance/Radiance themes would save pretty much exactly the same amount of space as removing the menubar.

I am testing unity on my maverick install, which currently has BOTH the appmenu and individual window menus.
I have now disabled the window menus because I found it just seemed more trouble to move the mouse to the menubar.
Now I am forced to use the appmenu so I can see if it's something I can get used to.

My other concern is that if the menubar is also going to contain window titles and window controls along with the menus, it is going to fill up quickly, especially with the increasing number of indicator menus.
And if unity also gets the upcoming "windicators" as well, the menubar is going to end up a cluttered mess and be very confusing to use, especially for new users.

Although netbook interfaces need to save space for usability, if everything is crushed up too much it all merges into each other and becomes harder to use.

There is no doubt an appmenu makes for a much slicker, nicer looking desktop but I don't see any other advantages at all.

So in short, I would like to see ubuntu have a rethink on this (not gonna happen) or at least give an option to switch back to regular menus (I imagine that can be done by removing the packages or running regular gnome anyway).

### Post by DoubleClicker on 2010-07-19
Personally, I really like the global menubar, because it is consistent with my 26 years of mac use.

But from an objective viewpoint, there is a main argument for each of the pro and con positions:

For the pro position: that it is quicker to jump to  a fixed point at the op of the screen, than it is to navigate to variable point in the middle of the screen.

For the the con position, that on a large screen you have to move the mouse much farther to reach the menubar.

Though it is rarely discussed, the truth both positions are really about mouse acceleration.  With acceleration set low , you have to move the mouse far to reach the top of the screen, 
but you can move fast to reach a near point.  With acceleration set high, you can get to the top of a large screen, by moving the mouse a short distance, but you have to move the mouse slowly, to get to a precise point in the middle of the screen.  I can move from the bottom right, to top left, on a 30" monitor, without lifting my wrist, and I slow down for fine point navigation, without thinking about it.

So for the most part, your preference, will most likely be dictated by your habits, and your mouse acceleration settings.
