### Post by Bezmotivnik on 2006-11-12
Yes, MySpace is a bad website machine.  Everything that was hideous about amateur HTML ten years ago is still flourishing there:  Blindingly ghastly backgrounds (animated, perhaps), kitschy pictures, mismatched colors, play-on-load music, things that flash and move, epic bad taste and poke-in-the-eye ugliness.  And that's before you even try to read the subliterate copy about the site's nitwit perpetrator.  

And yep, Linux sites for some mysterious reason tend to be the same way, only geekier:  Six frames with exposed scrollbars (my favorite!), dogs-dinner layout, inane graphics...like they haven't been updated since Clinton's first term.

Still, there may be *something* there, hopeless as it is.  I can't tell you how many people (including many here!) have links to their sites in their profiles, but there's nothing but a shell "under construction" site attached to the link, with little or no content -- and they stay that way forever.  Why even bother? :confused:
