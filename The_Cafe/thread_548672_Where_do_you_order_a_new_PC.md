### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-09-11
I build mostly. There are lots of local shops that have surprisingly good prices depending on where you live, though usually mostly on parts.

If your in Canada or even the US you can try [NCIX.]("http://www.ncix.com/") They are based in Canada (Vancouver, B.C.) but they offer buying of individual parts and building systems (go to Buy a PC in the bar). Then pick a section and model. After that you can customize the machine with ANY part in their site/inventory (even if it's out of stock) and they'll build it and ship. They are a god send in this world of overpriced/bad OEMs (and their prices are decent). The only downside to NCIX is to remember the onus is on YOU to ensure the parts all work together and are good, if you customize a lot from their pre built rigs.

They have the standard RMA/return policy though I don't think it's quite as lenient as Dell. They won't give you the longterm tech support though (don't think it's as expansive as Dell or HP), but if your geek ya know you got more brains than the folks on the other end of that line anyway. Check that before buying (especially return policy on systems), I'm mostly speaking on recommendation from others but I'm planning on getting a good lot of parts from these folks and their customer care seems to be good overall from the forum posts/people I know. 

That's the best solution I can think of, I can't recommend any large OEMs they have mostly bad support (usually lenient return policies like Dell are the only plus) and often overcharge/have price discrepencies on their sites.
