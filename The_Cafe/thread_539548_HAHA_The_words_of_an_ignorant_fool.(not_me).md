### Post by Celegorm on 2007-08-31
I don't see where his comment about graphical design needing a GUI comes into play.... It's the *application* that needs a GUI for graphical design, not the OS. As far as trying to convince him that linux is awesome, steer the conversation more towards particulars and facts which can be substantiated rather than vague past experiences and opinions, and give him references. For instance, "my friend's linux server has been up continuously for the past 2 and a half years" or "There are only about 30 known linux viruses in the entire history of the operating system, most of which exploited vulnerabilities which were quickly patched, and none of which still exists 'in the wild,' compared to the approximately 140,000 known windows viruses. If you don't believe me look [here]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virus_statistics")." Also, realize you are never going to convince someone "linux is awesome" just with good arguments and logic since that is, after all, an opinion. It's more fun to make them look at the facts and realize how unsubstantiated their biased and prejudiced opions are, anyway :biggrin:
