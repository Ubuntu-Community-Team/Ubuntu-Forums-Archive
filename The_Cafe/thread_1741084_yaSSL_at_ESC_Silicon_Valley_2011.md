Hi!

We wanted to let our Ubuntu users know that yaSSL will be exhibiting at ESC Silicon Valley, May 2-5, 2011 at the McEnery Convention Center in San Jose, CA. 

We'll be talking about several topics - including securing your applications and devices with SSL (using the CyaSSL embedded SSL library) as well as the yaSSL Embedded Web Server. If you are a CyaSSL user, customer, or fan and are attending ESC Silicon Valley, please stop by and visit! We'll be at Booth 2322.

CyaSSL has many advantages making it ideal for running in both embedded and non-embedded environments, including:

[LIST]
[*]Minimum size of 30-100kB depending on build options and operating environment
[*]Runtime memory usage between 5-50kB
[*]Support for SSL 3.0, TLS 1.0. 1.1, and 1.2
[*]Support for many progressive ciphers, including RABBIT and HC-128 stream ciphers and NTRU
[*]Extremely Portable (with OS, I/O, and C Standard Library abstraction layers)
[*]Open Source
[*]Support Available
[/LIST]
