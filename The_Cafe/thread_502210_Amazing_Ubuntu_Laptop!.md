### Post by bonzodog on 2007-07-20
I got myself an Acer Travelmate 4200WLMi, with all intel chipsets including wireless and graphics.
It's an Intel core single (centrino), it said on the box that it came with an 80GB HDD, but on looking at the disk partitioner on it, I found it to be 120GB (!???!!).

It has 512MB of RAM, and they had put Vista Home Premium on it, but I used an Acer backup tool to back up the factory default image, then just left a restore partition on the drive of 6GB. This was for the 'hardware warranty'.
Vista ran like a dog on it, and it took ages to do anything -- apparently, this is default behaviour for systems with less then 1GB of RAM. So, it had to go -- it had taken it 3 HOURS to merely complete the set-up and install of Vista and the Acer toolkit. 

So, I decided to scrap Vista altogether, and put feisty on it, which is really fast on it, and the wireless card actually works better under Feisty than it did in Vista (it has a better signal strength.)

*Everything* worked out of the box in Feisty -- that was one of the reasons I went for an all intel laptop, as Linux is good with Intel gear. That included the Synaptics touchpad and Widescreen 1280x800 display. Even the function-key brightness settings work for the monitor.
