### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-01-02
Ubuntu phones have Over The Air updates (OTA). Canonical take responsibility for keeping the phone OS up to date and not the OEM. It means that the phone models released last year will have the same OS as phone models released during the coming year. And as the Ubuntu phone OS is a work in progress this is a good thing. The OS is not fractured as with Android.

But I do not think that the upgrade is forced on anyone. Neither are upgrades to the applications forced on anyone. People upgrade through system settings.

I think  that the origin of this "rumour" is an attempt to create Fear, Uncertainty & Doubt (FUD) to put people off buying rival products.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-01-10
> Please explain your criticism of Android, I'm looking for a reason to not upgrade.

My reference was to the fact that OEMs modify Android to suit their taste. And its then up to the OEM to send out an upgraded version of Android. So, Google may release a new version of Android but not all Android phone users will get the upgrade. They have to rely on the OEM being willing to do the work. And they may be more interested in advertising the latest models with the latest Android.

I was contrasting this situation with the situation on the Ubuntu phone where Canonical takes responsibility for keeping all Ubuntu phone models up to date with the latest version of Ubuntu phone OS.

I have heard Canonical developers speak of pushing out an upgrade. That could be misunderstood, perhaps intentionally misunderstood, to then be used to make an accusation that upgrades will forced on the user.

The updated version gets "pushed" from development channels where it is tested into the regular (stable) update channel for the Ubuntu phone. And then it is up to the user to accept the update/upgrade.

Regards.
