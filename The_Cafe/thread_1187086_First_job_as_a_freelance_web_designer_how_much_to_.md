### Post by ade234uk on 2009-06-14
The job can be a pain in the ****. 
You need to set an hourly rate, because I can gaurantee you 9/10 the customer will want change. I have done websites in the past that have taken 6 months, due to the customer changing things. If you charge a set fee, you will be out of pocket and your enthusiasm for the job will subside. When I first started out, I used to do this, and I also used to do favours, then companies will start using you for more favours.

Just like their business needs to make money so do you, so don't become a soft touch.

Also think about how you are going to market yourself. There are so many bedroom webdesigners out there, the whole market place is saturated. Having a proper office to work from with a proper business address sets up apart immediately from people doing websites on the side.

### Post by cariboo on 2009-06-14
The correct way to figure your hourly rate is to use a formula called the bottom-up forumla.

you add your expenses + how much profit you want to make and divide it by billable hours

If your expenses are $2500 per month, and you want to make $1000 profit per month then divide it by the number of hours you actually spent working on the web site. Multiply all numbers by twelve to get  yearly totals eg:

[list]
[*] $2500 x 12
[*] $1000 x 12
[*] 60 x 12
[/list]

($30000 + $12000)/720 = $58.33

round up to $60 per hour

Now that you know you need $60 per hour to live, estimate how long it will take to do the job eg:

40 hours X $60 = $2400

This assumes you actually spent 15 hours a week doing nothing but working on the web site, not answering the phone, not surfing the interweb, not talking to your girlfriend/spouse nothing but actually sitting in front of the computer working.

I use dollars because I'm Canadain, substitute your own currency for whereever you are.

Edit: I forgot to add that when totalling up your expenses, don't forget to add in your wages. Add up your personal expenses, what you need to live each month, and add 10% for a bit of a cushion.

### Post by mr.propre on 2009-06-14
To give you an example of what could happen.
A few years back, around Christmas, I received a cheque from "Rijksdienst voor Pensioenen". Just a cheque and nothing else, no letter, nada.
A little bit strange because I was 19, still a long way from my pension, but I thought it probably was because I paid to much taxes or something. So I went to the bank and got the money without any problems.

A few days later I received a letter that there was a bug in the software and I received a cheque by accident. But I didn't need to pay back because the software company was responsible.

Now, it's perfect legitimated to work without any TAX-number or what so ever. But you have no right on any unemployment insurance and need to indicate all your "extra" income in your personal taxes. What would be stupid, knowing it takes just a few days/weeks to get a TAX number.
