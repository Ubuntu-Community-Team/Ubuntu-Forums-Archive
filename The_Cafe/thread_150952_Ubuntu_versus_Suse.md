### Post by nickle on 2006-03-27
Suse is a good distro to start with  (IMHO), particularly as mentioned above if you don't have a fast internet connection. For your first attempt, I might even recommend buying the boxed set, because the manuals are a great introduction to Linux in general. Also you  get several months free support from Suse/Novell when you buy the boxed set. These guys were great on several occasions when I had hardware problems. They stuck at it patiently over several weeks until I got things up and running.
I like YAST because it has all the admin stuff in one place and laid out nicely. However, I am not keen on the RPM update system. I have found apt much better and easier to use. Also the SUSE install is a breeze and very elegant. The current Ubuntu release installation procedure is a little scarey (but not necessarily more difficult) in its rawness.
Having "broken my teeth" on SUSE, I have now moved on to Kubuntu. It is my impression that using Ubuntu requires more "knowledge" of Linux. But, Ubuntu also has some nice 3rd party stuff like Automatix (tx Arnieboy) which simply sets up many of the packages you need for day to day fun stuff. Such packages are simply priceless for the uninitiated and lazy like me.
I also find the range of packages available for Ubuntu is larger than for Suse. In Suse I hand to to much more searching to find certain packages ( or do source builds). I have never had to do a source build in Ubuntu.


However, Ubuntu wins hands down when it come to the forum. Me not being very technically adept, I found the suse forums were sometimes very snotty and did not tolerate what they deemed as "ignorance". I used to hate when they told you to go and do a google search when you were not even sure what you should have been searching for. The Ubuntu Forum is probably one of Ubuntu's greatest assets.

### Post by shuttleworthwannabe on 2006-03-28
Just installed SUSE 10.1 )openSUSE 5 dics) beta 8. GNOME 2.12.2

1. GNOME is very responsive
2. Package manager is broke..at least on mine
3. ATI driver installation is PITA to get working (make sure when u installing OS you choose a VESA drievr and a suboptimal resolution, at least u can get in tot he GUI mode)
4. Power management on laptop is fabulous
5. The look ad feel of skin (Gilouche theme) is nice and appealing (but can't beat the new Ubuntu theme!)
6. ALSA sound mixer is configured ou tof the box to handle multitaskign of soundcard (this is not the case in Ubuntu Dapper, we still have to configure GNOME to do this, wiki has excellent instruction on this..see my thread on this issue)
7. It is a heavy install; approximately 3G of base installation (GNOME, OFFICE, INTERNET, MULTIMEDIA, GAMES, and thats it)
8. OpenSUSE still needs installation of restricted media formats (w32codecs, mp3, mpegs, etc). I could not install as my YAST is not working well.
9. Generally, nothing new, still using old GNOME (which we had when we tested Flight 2/3)
10. Documentation is pathetic: nothing beats Ubuntu documentation and help projects (wiki, forums, links to other user friendly site, and i see Novell is looking to set up their own official forum..but is facing some challenges..see this: [http://suseroot.com/blog/blog.php?postid=58](http://suseroot.com/blog/blog.php?postid=58)

But the best thing is the power management on my laptop: fan has never been so quite, not even in WinXP

Just my 2c.
