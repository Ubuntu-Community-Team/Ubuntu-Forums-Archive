Its probably emphasis on open source collaboration (version control software, standards, etc.) which I understand is a but different from what people might do within companies. I'm sure its easy to make it different than a regular CS degree.

That said, I think a regular CS degree is enough for everybody - if you want to go into open source development you can do that. On the other hand with an Open Source Degree, it seems dangerous because your options are more limited after graduation. i.e. at best its equivalent to a CS degree, at worst it you spend all this time and money and are unable to get a job in a smaller market.
