### Post by DrMega on 2008-04-17
The English is nearly fine. Just two points from me:

1. When using abbreviations it is customary to give the full version when used in the first instance. E.g.

> STEP 1. Structure the DMP into goal, criterion and alternative levels to form 
a hierarchy of the DMP.

I would rewrite as:

> STEP 1. Structure the DMP (Decision-making Problem) into goal, criterion and alternative levels to form 
a hierarchy of the DMP.

Notice how the first time DMP is used the it is written in full in brackets, but for subsequent instances the abbreviation alone is fine.

2. The only other thing I would change is where you say:

> 
STEP 3. According to the obtained judgment **matrixes** each linked with a certain criterion 
and the contributions of each criterion to the overall goal, quantify every alternative's 
advantage to the DMP. The alternative with the highest rating will be chosen as the
 DMP solution.


The plural of "matrix" is "matrices", not "matrixes".

Other than that it is fine.
