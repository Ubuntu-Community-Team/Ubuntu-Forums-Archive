I've never got into this discussion, since at those numbers I just start using scientific notation (1 million = 1E6 or 1 *10^6). But according to this: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_scale](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_scale)

> 
**Long scale** is the [English]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language") translation of the [French]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language") term *échelle longue*. It refers to a system of large-number names in which every new term greater than [million]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Million") is 1,000,000 times the previous term: *[billion]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1000000000000_%28number%29")* means *a million millions* (1012), *[trillion]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1000000000000000000_%28number%29")* means *a million billions* (1018), and so on. **Short scale** is the [English]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language") translation of the [French]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language") term *échelle courte*. It refers to a system of large-number names in which every new term greater than [million]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Million") is 1,000 times the previous term: *[billion]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1000000000_%28number%29")* means *a thousand millions* (109), *[trillion]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1000000000000_%28number%29")* means *a thousand billions* (1012), and so on.

So a billion for me is 1 000 000 * 1 000 000 or (1 000 000) ^2 and a trillion is 1 000 000 * 1 000 000 * 1 000 000 or (1 000 000) ^3.
