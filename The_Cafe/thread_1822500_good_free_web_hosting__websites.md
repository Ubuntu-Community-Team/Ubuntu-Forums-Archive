Hi there. 

I am the guy that Dry Lips mentioned earlier. That lil' project of mine has accumulated almost 1000 messages, so that's a lot of context in the "Too Long - Didn't Read" department! 

The key point at the beginning is the underlying model I used. It is primarily a *longevity* test, filtering out fly by nights. It was based on a sort of competition that is now largely over, with the simple theme "put your host where your mouth is and let anyone who wants to look at a live uptime dashboard". There was a scoring model that allowed a few bumps but I did my job and those hosts are still here after about 2 years. In general, I *purposely* ignored "managerial decisions" to avoid getting wrapped in political games of who offers what etc. All I cared about was either the host was still alive, or they croaked. Really, it was stunningly simple, but I had my part in a big shakeout of newbie hosts over at FWS.  Anyone could enter and "all they had to do was not croak. They croaked anyway." The part that shocked me was that I "only" got about 30 entries out of (quick estimate) 500 (!!) hosts over at FWS. And of those 30, only four made it.

Now. Dry Lips is right, the good ones are businesses, and by definition of "free as in beer" they have to do something with themselves to keep the bills paid. Your original question of "what is a good free host" begs the reply - "define good"! It really is almost as complicated as "tell me a good Linux Distro"! (And for the record I do lazily keep an eye on the Linux scene, but I am a humanities guy so save your laughter at my newbiness in later posts.)

Now. "Good Host". Now that we solved the problem of the host not croaking on you (at least this week!), how do you choose? Turns out that there are sorta "flavors & specialities" among those 4. By accident if I read you right as an OSS guy, CWahi isn't your deal. They got semi-booted off FWS for the legendary "Unlimited Bandwidth" claim. (Cue the usual responses.) Thing is - no one has yet busted them for it! So FYI I specifically asked a ways back if someone could host a Linux Distro Mirror site and he said yes. HOWEVER - they are like the scary dude on the wrong side of town who will save your a$$ in a knife fight, when the dinner table niceties aren't in play. In return for that bandwidth, they DO have the most aggressive ad package of the four. I did a signup just now, but I kinda forgot I have a lot of Ad-block stuff on, so I didn't see your antivirus item, (was it a popup?) but I am not 100% surprised either.  Treat them as if you're a smart cookie who can take care of yourself if you decide to call on them for the mega bandwidth. 

But if you have more modest needs, examine one of the others. The nicest admin service is at Seraphim Labs, while Decker Services is a tad fierce if you get on his bad side but he might be the most knowledgeable combined with good response time availability. IsMyWebsite is middle of the line. 

Hope that helps,

### Post by _f_0_c_u_s_ on 2011-08-13
Selecting a free hosting company depends on your needs. I have been building web sites for four years, and have never used paid hosting for any project.

If your site is fairly simple and not resource-intensive, I suggest [Zymic]("http://www.zymic.com/"). I used them back when my sites were all HTML-based. They are fast, reliable, and have a decent amount of space that is provided with your account as well as having several excellent subdomain choices (if you choose to go that route).

If you are looking to get a bit more complicated, for instance building a forum, blog, or community site, or anything that's not written using HTML, you might want to look into  [TigerServe]("http://tigerserve.com/"). I am hosting several web sites with them at the moment, and they seem very good so far. Unlike Zymic, they are cPanel-based. Another nice feature is the inclusion of the [Softaculous]("http://www.softaculous.com/") auto-installer, which is a good tool to have if one is not comfortable manually installing scripts such as Drupal or WordPress.
