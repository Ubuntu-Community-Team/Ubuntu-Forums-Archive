### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-08-23
There's other progressions not listed in the poll.

In starting with Ubuntu, I personally found the folks helping out in the Absolute Beginners section to be the MVP's for the longest time.  I had a few issues with Xubuntu, and they were either already answered via a quick search, or someone was prompt and courteous in responding.

Overall, I think the person who tries Ubuntu, regardless of being a Linux noob or veteran, and sticks with it to help evolve it in some way (via Loco team, Development, forum help, or even word-of-mouth marketing) helps the most.

I think the folks that don't help at all are the ones that try Ubuntu, and leave it because of some issue, but they never express what that issue is.  You can't resolve an issue from a customer who won't tell you why they left.  Not to say I want them leaving in a huge flame war, but if that's what it takes to get them to express what they're dissatisfied with, it's that much more feedback that can be used to tailor the distro.
