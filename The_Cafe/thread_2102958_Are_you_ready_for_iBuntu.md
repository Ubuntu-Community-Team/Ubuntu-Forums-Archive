### Post by jwbrase on 2013-01-10
What I'm really wondering is why Google isn't doing everything they can to help Canonical market Ubuntu for Android to OEM's. The one thing the mobile OS market is lacking is exactly what UfA provides: A means of running desktop productivity apps in more-or-less a traditional desktop environment when docked. For the user, it has the advantage over Ubuntu Phone of having pre-existing Android apps for the mobile side. It has the advantages over a hypothetical homegrown dock interface that Google might develop that: A) it already exists, and B) it can use existing Linux desktop apps.

And Google needs a UfA-type system yesterday. Microsoft already has the components they need to deploy such a system (which, though they've been floundering in the mobile market, could bring the weight of their dominance in the desktop market to bear), all they need to do is get their head out of their rear and reverse what they did with Windows 8: instead of trying to kludge their touch interface onto the PC, they just need to offer Metro on mobile devices when undocked and a traditional Windows desktop when docked. If they do something like this for Win9, Android could still lose the mobile market and Microsoft's dominance could continue for the foreseeable future.

And even if Microsoft doesn't wake up, Apple could. Whereas Microsoft tried to build a system that could bridge mobile and the desktop and failed, Apple doesn't seem to even be trying yet. But they're a big enough force in the mobile market that if they woke up, they could absolutely annihilate Android.

But if UfA ships before that, it will solidify the dominance of Android, not to mention quite possibly having substantial positive ramifications for Linux on desktop hardware.
