### Post by TheFu on 2021-12-22
I suspect LineageOS is best for privacy. Anything tied to either Apple's or Google's or Amazon's app-stores doesn't have any privacy. Not all devices can run LineageOS.
[https://www.reddit.com/r/LineageOS/comments/75wzpw/best_burner_phone_to_install_lineage_on/](https://www.reddit.com/r/LineageOS/comments/75wzpw/best_burner_phone_to_install_lineage_on/) suggests a cheap moto g4, but that is a 4 yr old article and device is even older.  A sister just got a new Moto phone a few weeks ago.  It is huge - over 6in long, with a 20x9 display but under $150. She won't run Lineage on it and it seems the main developers are all using $700+ devices now. There's actually an Asus phone costing over $1000 on the recommended page.

[https://wiki.lineageos.org/devices/](https://wiki.lineageos.org/devices/) has the supported devices.

Or the PinePhone: [https://www.pine64.org/pinephone/](https://www.pine64.org/pinephone/) for $150.

It is times like this I truly miss my N800 device. A modern version of that device, with modern battery, modern CPU, graphics, storage, running debian and Maemo would be fantastic.  I traveled central and south america for a few months with just that and a keyboard. At the time, I couldn't get a full day use from the swap-able battery and the 240p display was pretty bad, but for the time, it was an amazing device with very few privacy considerations. Web surfing and email and skype all worked.  Sadly, it didn't have an "airplane mode" - once on a flight an alarm started going off DURING take off. This was a time when no devices were allowed on during take-off or landing and the entire cabin was staring at me. It didn't help that I was the only person in the exit row - odd rules about speaking excellent English to get a seat on those rows - that didn't make any sense to me. The entire plane was packed with people, but 2 rows were empty.  Whatever.
