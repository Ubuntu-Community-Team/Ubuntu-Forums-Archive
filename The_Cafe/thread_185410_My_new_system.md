### Post by nickle on 2006-05-31
I though I would share my thoughs on my new system. It might hel as guide for those considering buying new hardware.

My kids had been bugging me for a long time for their own computer at my ex-house. So I am going to give them my trusty amd xp300 based system.
So I decided to splash out a bit:
[LIST]
[*]Nexus Breeze case 400w which is supposed to be real quiet
[*]ASUS A8N5X MD
[*]GeForce 7600 silent
[*]WD Raptor 10000 RPM (Expensive!!!)
[*]1 Gb fast memory[/LIST]The rest was made up with various bits and pieces.

My aim was to have a fast and quite system that would last me a few years. Here are my thoughts:
Performance:
[LIST]
[*]While there is an improvement in speed, it is not as much as I might have expected especially with dual core and dual channel memory.
[*]DVD ripping and Video encoding is MUCH faster, but audio ripping and encoding is not much better.
[*]For mutlitasking where one of the tasks is intensive, the dual core is nice, but the old XP was a pretty good performer. For example I can now listen to music, rip, encode, download torrents and play BZflag without any problem! However, note I don't do this kind of stuff every day!!
[*]The biggest improvement in terms of responsiveness seems to  be the Raptor. It really seems to make things move.
[*]My old graphic card was an GeForce MX500. For the limited games I play (Bzflag, netpanzer, slune with my kids), I see no difference. In fact, it is my feeling that unless you are using modern cutting edge games or specific graphics applications an older graphic card will suffice. I will be investing in a bigger display later, so it may still make a difference.[/LIST]Noise:
[LIST]
[*]The Nexus is not cheap and touts its quietness. While it is quieter, it is not that quiet! I do wonder whether the extra expense was justified. My guess is if you want real silence you have to splash out on a fanless system.
[*]Well the slient graphic card is clearly adequate for my purposes.
[*]I guess I would gain some more peace by going for a CPU cooler that is quieter than the AMD boxed one. The boxed cooler is still the loudest component.
[*]The raptor is silent when not actively and persistently searching. When it gets going it rattles quite a bit, but surprisingly quiet for the speed. With a HD silencer, it would be perfect. Be careful here though becasue it does get hot.
[*]I also have a big samsung HD. Although not nearly as fast as the raptor, it is really quiet. I would highly recommend samsung spinpoint drive for those looking for peace, size and value.[/LIST]I hope these comments will be useful to some of you folk.:)

### Post by polo_step on 2006-05-31
[QUOTE=nickle]
Noise:[/QUOTE]
[FONT="Courier New"]Having hyperacusis, that's everything to me and one reason why I rarely use my desktop computers.

I've done a lot of experimenting and have found that an awful lot of "low noise" stuff simply isn't.

One of the greatest drawbacks on my Linux computer is that it's the most gawdawfully noisy box ever designed.  Despite having a nasty-loud cheap CPU fan, which I replaced, the real source of the problem is the case itself.  Some cases just resonate like crazy until they are damped with something.  If it looks like I'm going to be doing more work with Ubuntu 6.06, I'm going to do some serious sound deadening of this case with dense rebond-rubber foam and coldpatch -- which if done properly will not materially increase interior heat in a properly ventilated case.

One big plus is having large, relatively slow-moving fans.  I've managed to get two higher-end Ultra power supplies free-after-rebate and they have 120mm fans.  These do a great deal to cut down noise.  

On my main office machine that runs XP, I have an absolutely huge [Zalman cooler]("http://f3.yahoofs.com/users/434e1f65z465b6960/b35e/__sr_/3763.jpg?phQahfEBGkURHB8Q") (yes, that *is* a full-size momboard).  I think this would work with the fan turned completely off, as even at lowest adjustable speed it keeps the CPU at barely about ambient temperature.  It's not really all that that quiet, though, when the fan is running.

The AGP card has a **very** large heatsink that takes the place of another noisy fan -- with perfectly adequate cooling.

I think that seriously damping the cases will produce the largest drop in noise of all, though.

[/FONT]
