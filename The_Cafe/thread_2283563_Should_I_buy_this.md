### Post by 1clue on 2015-06-30
I've worked with hardware like that some years back, but current for the time.

I know this is a joke thread, but seriously people do use this sort of hardware.  The thing you need to know about it though is that most buyers of this sort of thing don't just buy one of something.  They'll have a few racks of it (or a lot of racks of it), and some other equally impressive hardware tying it all together.  They buy N+1 of a box of a certain category, where N is the number of boxes they need and the +1 part is a spare.  Or if N is a large enough number they'll get +some other number.

And also, chances are the buyer is a business and not just some guy looking for stupid-fast Facebook access.  Normal user tasks are not generally any faster on this sort of hardware than they are on a medium-fast desktop or laptop.
