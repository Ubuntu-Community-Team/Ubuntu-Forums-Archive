### Post by ve4cib on 2010-09-28
New show's I've watched and am enjoying:

**Boardwalk Empire** (HBO) - It's a drama about corrupt politicians and gangsters in Atlantic City right at the beginning of the prohibition era.  Martin Scorcese was involved as a producer, Steve Buchemi stars, and it's generally a well-made, well-written, well-acted show.  I'd definitely suggest checking it out.

**Lost Girl** (Showcase) - A Canadian urban fantasy show that was probably made to capitalize on the Vampire/Werewolf mania out there, but succeeds where most others fail.  The main character, Bo, is an orphan who discovers that she's actually a Fae -- the catch-all term for non-human creatures -- but refuses to align herself with any of their light-versus-dark traditions.  She's an outcast among outcasts, trying to stay alive and discover who her parents were.  The show is a nice, dark, gritty version of the urban fantasy genre, and reminds me a lot of the World of Darkness RPG (for anyone who's into pen-and-dice RPGs).

**Nikita** - Another show based on that infamous French film.  This time the title character is Asian, but the rest is the same.  Girl falls in with bad crowd.  Girl kills cop.  Girl gets recruited to secret government agency and trained to be an assassin.  Girl goes rogue.  Girl vows to take down secret government agency.  It's not a stellar show, but it's a fun turn-your-brain-off-and-watch kind of show.


The only other new show I've watched any of is **The Event**.  I saw the pilot, and was not impressed.  It could maybe have potential, but nothing in the pilot leapt out at me and said "Watch this or you'll be sorry you missed it!"  I have the second episode on my computer, and I'll watch it eventually, but I'm in no hurry to get to it.  As a replacement for FlashForward I found the pilot to be very lacking.


Finally, **Rubicon** (AMC) isn't exactly new (it's currently in its 11th week of its first season), but it's quite good.  It is pretty slow-moving, but most consipracy/thriller shows are.  It's about a guy who works for a secret, autonomous intelligence-analysis agency within the US government.  He stumbles into an as-yet-unrevealed conspiracy that seems to involve his boss, several corporations, and members of the CIA, NSA, and other agencies.  In typical conspiracy/thriller fashion he doesn't know who to trust, but has to keep digging for information under the radar.  James Badge Dale (of The Pacific) stars in it.
