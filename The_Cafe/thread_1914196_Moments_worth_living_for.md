### Post by JDShu on 2012-01-24
The moment when I finally realized what it was that [I loved to do.]("http://www.paulgraham.com/love.html") It was a mix of "I have gone completely nuts" and "I can't believe I never realized it"

Out of college with an economics degree, I didn't have FT work.. it was a bad year and I suppose my job hunting skills were lacking. One day, I realized that I had been learning to program for three days straight instead of looking for work like I was supposed to. That was probably the first time I had a true epiphany and took a real risk: I switched fields to CS with a real aim to become a software developer. I haven't reached that goal yet, but I am enrolled in university now and recently landed a paid position as a research assistant.

What I really want to emphasize is that having a plan that you believe in, no matter how crazy, makes all the difference - and the moment I realized it is one I won't be forgetting.
