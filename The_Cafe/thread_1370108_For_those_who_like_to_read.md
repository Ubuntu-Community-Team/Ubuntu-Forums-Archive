### Post by t0p on 2010-01-02
The Gutenberg site is great for books that are in the public domain.  Unfortunately, nearly all the books in the public domain are *old*.  So it's great if you like old books.

Of course, there are ways of finding newer works.  [Archive.org]("http://www.archive.org/index.php") is good, but it can be tricky searching that site. Then there's [www.u2u.cc]("http://www.u2u.cc/") for searching Rapidshare, Megaupload and the like.  And of course, Google - a search like 

```
<book title> download filetype:pdf
```

should turn up some decent results... along with loads of dross, unfortunately.

I think the current copyright laws in the USA and the EU are skewed too much in the author and publisher's favour.  Copyright protection lasts for far too long.  It would be better if books that have been out of print for a certain amount of time (say 20 years?) automatically entered the public domain unless the author applied for extended protection.  Then sites like Gutenberg.org would become truly useful.  As things stand, publishers want to be paid for a download when they haven't done anything to deserve payment.  If no one can be bothered to continue publication of a book, why should they get paid?  It's a license to print money, but without having to print anything!
