### Post by etnlIcarus on 2009-02-07
> **PhoHammer said:**
> I feel like a teenage girl.Who doesn't? >.>> Compiz Fusion has blown my mind and I ask why anyone would choose to live without it?

Lots of reasons. Usually when I get bored with it, it's because of it's lacklustre performance when it comes to *basic window management*. Middle clicking the titlebar drops the window to the bottom of the stack maybe 50% of the time (it's meant to happen 100% of the time, FTR). Using the scroll wheel *anywhere* only results in the correct behaviour maybe 75% of the time (again, should be 100%). Focus model options come in the form of a slider, ranging from windows unshading/unminimsing and jumping to the top of the stack with every hint (annoying as all hell), to windows not doing that, but new windows then do not receive focus (even more annoying than all hell). 

I mostly use compiz for two reasons:
- Makes using workspaces much more natural and less cumbersome.
- With much of the graphical overhead being offloaded to my GPU, my CPU is much more able to handle tasks like playing Flash videos because Adobe doesn't support acceleration on my card.
