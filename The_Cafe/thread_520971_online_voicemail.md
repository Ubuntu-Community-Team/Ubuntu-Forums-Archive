### Post by komputes on 2007-12-05
There are two good FREE providers of on-line voice mail/fax but it's not a local number, and only one works with Ubuntu.

###1###

J2.com - this is a good one and an annoying one at the same time. 

The Good: They don't cancel your account after 30 days (i've gone a year and the number is still up)

The Bad: Since it send me a weird message.gsm file I can't open it with any audio app. I've tried VLC and downloaded Sweep because it says it supports GSM. I don't know if they encrypt it or what, but I had the same problem on XP, without their proprietary software. If you want a test file, just message me.


###2###

K7.net - This one is OK and works on Ubuntu.

The Good: All the files are standard documents (WAV and PDF or EPS, i think). It work with all operating system and no software to install.

The Bad: They delete your number if you don't use it for 30 days, so don't go on vacation without calling yourself. 

I guess this could be good for someone who uses it regularly, but if you only need it once in a while, your number will most likely be temporary.

Hope that helps! and please, if someone has a method to listen to J2 JConnect GSM Files please let me know!


###3###
Fax only - [https://www.efax.com/en/efax/twa/signupFree](https://www.efax.com/en/efax/twa/signupFree)
I never use this account and it's still active, I'm not sure what kind of file they send. But I think it was PDF.

### Post by willdeans on 2009-07-18
Greetings,

I called up jConnect at (888) 718-2000 and asked them if they offered a free service which allowed me to receive voicemails.  They claimed they did.  I said terrific, I have already signed up for the service but I am having trouble using the free service for under $100.  How do I use it without purchasing a copy to Windows?  They claimed that I would have to purchase a copy of Windows but then went a step further and UNBELIEVABLY made fun of me for not having Windows.  They further falsely stated that I didn't own a PC  because my personal computer didn't have Windows installed.  Based upon this attitude I urge you to use any service at all but jConnect.  jConnect also provided other factually and technically incorrect information.  I later discovered that you could use audacity to access your orphaned .gsm voicemails after switching off of jConnect.

Audacity Instructions
    sudo apt-get install audacity
    "File" -- > "Import" --> "Raw Data"
    "GSM 6.10"
    "Little-endian"
    "1 Channel (Mono)"
    "Sample Rate 8000 Hz"
