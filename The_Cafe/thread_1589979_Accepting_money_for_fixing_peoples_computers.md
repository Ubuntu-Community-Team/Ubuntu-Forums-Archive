### Post by undecim on 2010-10-07
I do a little bit of this, mostly I fix laptops for college students, so let me lend you some of my experience

First of all, people really don't care about certifications. I don't have a single paper to my name aside from a high school diploma. If you can do the work, then people will keep coming back to you for their technology needs, they will tell their friends about you, and you will start to get more customers and return customers.

That being said, make sure you can do the work. Usually, this is just a matter of removing malware from a computer, but can sometimes be more complex. Sometimes you have to be able to take a laptop apart (earbuds result in lots of laptop screen cracks), and being able to solder is a must (it's easy, really), because people leave their laptop charger plugged in when putting their laptops into their bags, and that results in a lot of broken charging ports.

When you take malware off of someone's computer, offer to set up Microsoft Security Essentials and File Hippo (after explaining that MSE is the best anti-virus you can get, and that most viruses attack via outdated applications) at no additional cost.
