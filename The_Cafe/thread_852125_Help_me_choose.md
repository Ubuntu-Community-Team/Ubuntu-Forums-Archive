### Post by kuja on 2008-07-07
The T5XXX are the lowest end of the mobile intel core 2 duo's. T5xxx is basically a T7xxx with half of the L2 Cache disabled and a slower FSB (667MT/s instead of 800MT/s). T5xxx and T7xxx are also referred to as Merom - 
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel_Core_2#Merom](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel_Core_2#Merom)

The best processors available in the mobile laptops currently, are the T8xxx and T9xxx (also known as Penryn). Advancements of these include (potential for) faster FSB speeds, faster clock speeds, larger L2 Cache, SSE4 support, longer battery life, and the ability to support DDR3 memory. [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel_Core_2#Penryn](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel_Core_2#Penryn)

Just a piece of info, not a discouragement ... [color=red][size=3]the Low end merom's are still far better than their precessors (pentium 1/2/3/4/M/D, Core Duo), and likely much cheaper than their successors (penryn) also. It just depend what you're comparing them to how good they are. :)[/size][/color]
