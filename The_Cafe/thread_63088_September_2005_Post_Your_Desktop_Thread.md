### Post by matthew on 2005-09-07
[QUOTE=Strangerdave]Kind of a newbie here, but it looks as if you have a bar up top, then one underneath with all the icons on it.  Then you have a bar at the bottom of the page.  How did you get that?[/QUOTE]

This is done using two different things. For the extra "menu bar" and icons I am using Gnome Applets. You right click on an existing menu bar (called a panel) and you can make a new panel or add applets, which are small programs which do specific things like monitor the weather, your processor activity and lots of other cool stuff. Play with it a bit and you will have some fun.

The floating bar near, but not at the very top is a desklet from gDesklets called "starterbar." I got it along with the latest version of gDesklets from their web site at [http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org/](http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org/) Look around there, lots of eye candy and resource-using unnecessary fun available. The icons are links I made to various programs I use often.

Oh! I also made a transparent png that I use for the background on the panels and for the starter bar. I'll attach it at the end of this post for anyone that wants to use it. Nothing special, it will just save you the time of firing up gimp for yourself.

> Did you get all that at gnome-look.org?  Also what do you mean by controls and window border?

Go to System->Preferences->Theme and click theme details. There are three tabs there allowing you to mix and match parts of themes. I used the controls and border from one theme and chose icons from another. I will often change and use the window borders from one theme and the controls from another. Play with it and you will see what it does.

### Post by matthew on 2005-09-28
[QUOTE=YourSurrogateGod]I have a question. Some of you guys are modifying your desktops, I was wondering, would you suggest an online tutorial to someone like me who's pretty much a n00b to this whole process?[/QUOTE]
I don't have a link to online tutorials, though there might be some stuff at [http://www.gnome-look.org/](http://www.gnome-look.org/) and that would be a good place to find some wallpapers and themes.

Wallpapers are easy to change. right click on the desktop and choose change desktop background. If the wallpaper you want hasn't been added to the list yet, click add wallpaper and browse to it and voila.

Themes can be downloaded from the site listed above. To add them, System->Preferences->Theme and drag the tar.gz file into the theme dialog box. You will be asked if you want to add the theme, select install and it will be available. Note that some themes are complete, and some only include the window frames or icons, etc. so you may find the theme in the main dialog or you may have to click "edit" and look in the various tabs to find the theme bits that exist. I suggest playing around with it and having fun...it really is the best way to get comfortable and learn how it all works.

Icons may be added in the same way and place as themes, but sometimes the icon package may not install well. In that case, just decompress the tar.gz file into /usr/share/icons and you can choose the individual icons using smeg or via a right click on the icon and selecting properties when it is on the desktop/in a folder.

Um...being entirely self-taught on this through trial and error, that's about the extent of my knowledge, but it should be enough to really get you going. Have fun!!
