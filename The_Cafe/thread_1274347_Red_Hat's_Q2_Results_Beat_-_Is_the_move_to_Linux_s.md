Some of my assorted thoughts:

Red Hat has weathered the recession well, a fact that is common knowledge by now. My understanding is that part of this is due to their lock-in subscription model, but the fact that they seem to be increasing sales of subscriptions as well suggests that they are a viable solution for many businesses. I guess we'll have to see how long Red Hat's success will last and whether its the real thing. For sure, more market penetration of Red Hat in the business world would translate to a better experience for Linux users overall.
