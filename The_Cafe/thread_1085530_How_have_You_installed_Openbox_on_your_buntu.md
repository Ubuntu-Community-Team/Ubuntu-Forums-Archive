### Post by Phil Urich on 2009-06-28
On my main computer (**Athlon 4400+, 4GB RAM, 8.04LTS**) I mainly use KDE 3.5.10, but I have pretty much every other big WM/DE installed, and Openbox is a frequent contributor to my computing routine.

On my Acer Aspire One (**8.9" netbook, Jaunty**) I actually run Fluxbox most of the time since PyPanel doesn't work on Jaunty, alas.  I've been thinking about getting Openbox to just auto-start stalonetray and not bother with anything else; my heart is always torn betwixt the two, heh.  Maybe I'll just run fbpanel on Openbox.  Or lxpanel.  Oh, the permutation are endless!  Every once in awhile I boot into KDE 4.3, and it's always so shiny, but if I leave it running for more than about 15 minutes it starts chugging at a snail's pace, and it's a quick reboot (nothing else seems to work) and back to Openbox or Fluxbox!

On my 13" laptop (**Core Solo, Jaunty**) I...uhh, actually I tore it apart recently and I haven't gotten around to putting it back together.  But while it was together I primarily used LXDE, which is pretty much just a customized Openbox after all.

On my previous projector computer (**Athlon 1800+, 512MB RAM, 8.10**) I first had installed a full Kubuntu, but for running XBMC Kwin seemed to screw up something and XBMC ran at below 10 fps!  So I fell back to good 'ol Openbox.  When XBMC mainlined VDPAU support (so I could do all the video decoding on a graphics card, thus allowing me to finally do 720p or 1080p on a spare computer) I went and bought a PCI Geforce 8 series card (since there aren't any AGP ones, and you can get entirely passively cooled PCI ones).  Strangely though the computer wouldn't even so much as [POST](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power-on_self-test) with the card in no matter what I did, I even went through the trouble of upgrading the BIOS, but I gave up and chucked it into the corner, pulling a different computer out instead. Thus:

On my current projector computer (**Pentium III 600mHz with a GeForce 8400GS PCI card haha**) I just installed Jaunty from minimal and put Openbox, and only Openbox, on it.  I didn't even bother with a DM, I just have it auto-start XBMC which I drop out of into Openbox if I ever have to do other computing things on that box (which is rare).  So right as we speak I have an Openbox session running, within which XBMC is running and a 720p video is playing on a computer built mostly in 1999 :)  And whose graphics card, at 512MB, has twice as much RAM as the rest of the computer has!

Or to sum it all up (leaving out a few other cases, which believe me also include Openbox), **to one degree or another Openbox is installed on ALL my *buntus** :)
