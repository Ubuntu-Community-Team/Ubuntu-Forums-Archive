### Post by klange on 2008-05-28
I always tend to keep my drives clean and clear of any trash or other unused files. I commonly find myself using the Disk Usage Analyzer to find where random, unneeded files may be lurking. One cool thing that the Analyzer does is make a graphical representation of all of your disk space with a different block for each folder, the larger the block the more space its contents take up. They actually make for some pretty interesting abstract art pieces, as well. So, let's see how your drive's organized, post your Graphical Usage Map!

In mine, the massive purple box on the left is a 10.9GB directory on one of my partitions containing all 169 English-dub episodes of Naruto in OGG format (I have my reasons...). Total drive usage is 24.9GB out of a 120GB drive.

**Oooh**: I just cut another 380MB of unused files in the form of a ARM rootfs (bootable through NFS) and an old all-in-one LAMPP set up (I have a server for a reason...)
