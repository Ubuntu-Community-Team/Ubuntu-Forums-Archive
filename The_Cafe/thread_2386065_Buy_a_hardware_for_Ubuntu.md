### Post by DuckHook on 2018-02-28
Welcome to the forums klaxmi361,

If you are looking for a prebuilt desktop machine, unless you either build it yourself or buy a custom configuration from a small computer shop, they almost all come with Windows preinstalled. There are Linux vendors who preinstall your choice of distro on their desktop builds. You can easily Google for their names and websites.

The usual method for testing HW compatibility is to run a LiveUSB session on the machine to put it through its paces and see if all components are supported. Just make sure that whatever you buy can be returned with no restocking charge, then make sure you test it thoroughly before the free return period runs out.

Other than the above, it really is irrelevant what anyone here might recommend. None of us have any idea what your needs are, your budget, your desires, your pet peeves&#8230; it's a lot like asking where you should go for vacation, or what car you should buy. There are too many variables to provide anything other than pointless subjective opinion.
