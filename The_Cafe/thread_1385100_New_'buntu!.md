### Post by neu5eeCh on 2010-01-19
Just wanted to add my 2 Cents.

I love Ubuntu - just installed it about 3 weeks ago. Wish I could congratulate all the developers and individuals who have contributed to it. In fact, I've installed it on three out of my four laptops (Mint for my wife).

I'm so lucky to have missed the Windows 7 family pack - which is what prompted me to try Linux.

Wow!

I've dipped in and out of Linux. In the late 90s, I tried RedHat, but eventually gave up when I couldn't successfully edit - what was it called? - the xconfig file? Then booting up XWindows was always a little like Russian Roulette. Some drivers worked, some didn't. I could never get my sound card to correctly pronounce Linux.

Later, through the aughts, I dipped in and out of Linux. I remember the early days of KDE, with its radio buttons that went nowhere (like a highway that just stops). Half finished dialogue boxes would pop up, drivers didn't install, and there were always the warnings that if you didn't know what you were doing, you might blow your 46 pound CRT to kingdom come.

I always, sadly, returned to windows.

But Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Mint, Xubuntu, Ubuntu Remix. Linux has grown up and I'm having fun.

As much as I love the variety of distros, I have to say, I hope the Linux world (let alone the rest of the world) begins to coalesce around Ubuntu. This might encourage Windows and Mac-side software and hardware developers to start developing for the Linux-side with the expectation that they're investing in a distro with a future. **Then** there will be no looking back.
