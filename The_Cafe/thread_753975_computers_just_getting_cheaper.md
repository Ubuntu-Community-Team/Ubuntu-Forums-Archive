### Post by Tundro Walker on 2008-04-13
Unless you're doing gaming, or some hard-core video/sound editing, you can hop on CraigsList and buy someone's old p4 2+ghz for ~$100 cheap.  Usually comes with a burner, decent sized hd, etc.  It's more than enough horsepower to do day-to-day stuff.

Computers are the modern version of the hoola-hoop, where supply has just outweighed demand, so folks are practically giving them away these days.  But, like the previous posters said, the price quickly escalates when you get to more modern, quality hardware.  Most folks don't need a modern computer, though, unless they're trying to use Vista.

If you're sticking with Ubuntu, you can easily get old p3 & p4's to sing pretty.  It's almost a crying shame how these perfectly good comps are getting kicked to the curb, because they can't run Vista well.
