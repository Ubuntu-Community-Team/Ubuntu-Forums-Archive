### Post by samalex on 2010-08-03
Online suggestions:
- Kill some time on IRC... there's always good conversation on there.
- Visit archive.org and see what your fave websites looked like in years past
- Google yourself and see what you find
- Visit Groups.Google.com and search for topics in the 80's and 90's.  Always great for a read, plus for techies the entire Linus Torvalds vs Andrew Tanenbaum debate is still out there among other awesome snippets of history.

Offline suggestions:
- Visit your local library and read through newspaper articles from like 100 years ago or just browse the books and magazines.
- Do genealogy research on your family (also at the library, not online, unless you're online at the library).
- Do you have a Goodwill Computer Store and search for awesome deals.
- If you have a university in your area, go walking around it as if you go there -- and to get more looks wear a T-Shirt from a rival school.
- Go peruse the used book stores or comic shops

Just some suggestions of things I used to do when I was single... but now with a family and kiddos there's never an idle moment when there's not something around the house that needs to be done.

Sam
