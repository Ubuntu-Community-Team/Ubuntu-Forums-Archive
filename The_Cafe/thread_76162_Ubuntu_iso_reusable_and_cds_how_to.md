### Post by Omnios on 2005-10-14
Found a little trick with the Ubuntu iso that I use. I only have a few CDRW and though I just bought another 10 im still going to use this trick. I found the Ubuntu iso to large to put on a cdrw or was it cause it wouldnt do it anyways so I burnt a iso as a regular image on a cdrw and found later that I can bulk erase it and reuse it. This allows me to have a burnt copy of the Ubuntu iso but more importantly it allows me to bulk erase it and reuse it to make an image of the new release. I useualy order cds to hand out and keep one for myself so having a burnt back up copy when the cds come in is also handy. 

 This can also work for other things that frequently change expecialy for big files. Cheep cheep *chuckle*
