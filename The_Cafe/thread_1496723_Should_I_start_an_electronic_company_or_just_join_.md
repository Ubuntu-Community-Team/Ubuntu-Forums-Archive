### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-05-30
There's plenty of scope for ideas in the consumer electronics space.

I'd agree with the other posters - if you have to ask whether you should start a company, then you don't have the ability to do it (not without studying business at university).

I've always wanted to start a consumer electronics brand where the emphasis is on making TVs, DVD recorders etc very easy to set up and use. For a number of years I worked as a TV installer and found that some tasks were difficult to explain to the customer, and that the interfaces on some CE items were very unintuitive.

However I simply don't have the business training to start such a company; and if I did, there would be the danger that I'd end off like Ruslan Kogan: An importer of cheap crud from China with a warranty that's barely worth the paper it's written on. Who seems to take delight in seeing mass unemployment occurring after an electrical retail chain go bankrupt.

Still, if you've got the right ideas and are willing to do relevent units at university or TAFE, you could climb up the corporate ladder at an existing company and become part of a product visionary team. You wouldn't become the CEO through ideas.
