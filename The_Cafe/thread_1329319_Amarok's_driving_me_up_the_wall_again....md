### Post by seeker5528 on 2009-11-18
> **Tipped OuT said:**
> Why don't people use Rhythmbox? It's a nice simple player, with some cool plug-ins available.

Rhythmbox is nice if you just want a simple player that will show all your music and give you different filtering/browsing options and random play, without having to create a playlist, but being more oriented to that style of use it doesn't do particularly well when you start to get into the playlists and advanced features, depending on what you actually want out of them.

For my personal usage, going from Amarok to another player is like taking a backward step.

I had some issues with some tracks not showing, but these all seemed to be related to the gstreamer phonon backend and all seemed to be the result of directories or files with spaces in their name, never had a problem with the xine backend.

I never did like the way the storage of cover art was handled, usually I save cover art into the directories where the music lives, for the cover art I didn't have I had the copy cover script installed that would copy the cover art from where Amarok kept it into the directories where the music lives, have not seen a similar script for Amarok 2.x yet.

Forgetting the cover art that Amarok downloads and stores, if it's forgetting on an upgrade, it might be because the source of the cover art has been switched from Amazon to Last FM.

### Post by seeker5528 on 2009-11-21
> **tom957 said:**
> Do banshee, exaile, etc., do iPod scrobbling?

According to the FAQ at Last FM:

> You can scrobble your iPod with our software. Last.fm supports all iPods, Firewire and USB, including iPhone and iPod Touch. However, Linux iPod scrobbling does not yet support iPhone or iPod Touch.
> Linux: You can scrobble with Linux using the &#8220;scrobble iPod&#8221; item in the &#8220;Tools&#8221; menu in the latest version of our software.

I don't have an iPod so I have not really looked into this stuff, but the Last FM client available in Lucid has the 'scrobble iPod' option and I am pretty sure it's been the same client more or less with only minor changes over the last few releases. The client in Debian was recently updated, but I expect that was probably more about updating it to use the new API for interacting with the web site, which may result in some streaming that was working, not working if you don't have a paid subscription, even if you live in the US, UK or Germany, but I think not much else relating to functionality. I have not actually tried to use it yet, but some discussion in the Amarok forum hinted at that being the direction things seemed to be headed based on their discussion with people at Last FM relating to what the API allows clients to do or not do, nothing that affects features relating to scrobbling.

You may not view it as the ideal solution, but it's an alternative, there may be others.
