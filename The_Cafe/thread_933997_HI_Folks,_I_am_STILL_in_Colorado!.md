### Post by Tamlynmac on 2008-10-10
I live in Salida, Colorado and still enjoy John Denver's music. Guess music like many other things is a personal preference. Cripple Creek is very pretty, but you haven't been in the mountains until you've gone to North Fork or Tin Cup.

My job took us to Missouri (Platte City) for 15 years and we missed the Rocky Mountains sooo much that I finally left that position and returned home. "Theres no place like home" just ask Toto... Clean air, clean clear water, very low humidity and of course beautiful views. The Aspens are about done turning and winter is right around the corner. The area we live in is known as the banana belt, summers rarely get above high 80's during the day and 50's at night. The winter averages in the 40's during the day and drops into the 20's at night lower on stormy nights. Snow almost never accumlates above 12 inches and is usually gone within a few days except in the mountains or shadow areas. Sunshines everyday on the average of 50 weeks a year. No air conditioning (swamp coolers) and depending on the winter and house size a woodstove and solar heat is all that's needed..

Many people vacation here, but few live here. Population of approximately 5K. The Arkansas river runs through town and is great for rafting and trout fishing (trophy waters). It truly is a paradise and we love it. Both of us are natives and have traveled all our the US, Mexico & Canada. But as I said, There is no place like home - where ever that may be....
