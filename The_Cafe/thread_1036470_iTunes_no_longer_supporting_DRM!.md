### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-01-11
CORRECTION: iTunes still supports DRM. If the record companies start insisting on DRM again, then iTunes would not require any client-side updates to start using DRM on music files again. Also, all videos are still DRM-encrypted. Apple has said on many occasions that it believes in DRM for video files. Note that music videos are still all DRM'ed, despite being freely available from the record companies.

Until the video DRM is dropped and videos can be converted into other formats or burned to DVD, and until Apple actually removes the DRM that attempts to lock iPods to iTunes, I will not re-open my iTMS account. Oh, and I may require Apple to apologise for telling me to buy an Apple TV in order to watch my legally-purchased videos. :-)
