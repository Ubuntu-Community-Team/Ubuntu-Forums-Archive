### Post by ihavenoname on 2006-06-04
[QUOTE=isotonic]ubuntu rocks!!!:cool:[/QUOTE]
My first Distro was Fedora, but the Ubuntu Community is the one that got me going in Linux. Despite that I was a die-hard fedora fan, and for some reason i dispised Ubuntu (the distro not the community) But I continued to track the development of Ubuntu ( I tried many of the Flights) I have slowly begun to become an Ubuntu to user. I must say, it is the most amazing distro ever IMHO.
I say this keeping the following things in mind:

**Ease of Installation**: Ubuntu Fedora SUSE all fit the bill

**Ease of Setup Post-Installation**: Ubuntu Fedora and Suse do fine on this as well

**Package Managment**: Ubuntu Fedora (Suse 10.1 is horrible in this reguard u NEED SMARTpm or you not going anywhere)

**Number Of Available Packages in repositories**; (Ubuntu...nothing else comes close and this is perhaps what holds SUSE down, all their (useful) repos are third party. Fedora extras is great but things such as katapult, monodevelop, and chromium arent in there...not a big deal but it makes a diffrence.)

**Quality of Packages in the repo**: Ubuntu (Many of Fedora's packages after a release are horrible (at least with FC5) Fedora Eclipse would not let you create Python projects even with pydev installed, Open Office Calc had a glitch where "-" signs did not appear. The kernel on the ISO did not support non-oss video drivers (thats what did it for me in the end, I mean come on all that work on a distro and then you release it with a bad kernel...the greatly decreases the level of "polish" that a user feels. When you install an OS it should be at its peak performance not need updates to cirtical things such as the kernel) I would use Fedora as a server where extra apps are not really neccessary, Fedora is possibly the most Stable distro I have ever used, the Desktop is where Ubuntu regins King (a benevolent King mind you!)

Trust me on what I say I have distro hopped like crazy this past year I know what I am talking about (not to sound too cocky) Ubuntu truely does rock, I can't tell you how many times my mouth has dropped after discovering a new feature in Dapper, and this was supposed to be a "conservative" release....I cant wait till Edgy, which is supposed to be a more feature centered release.


P.S. Other notable distros that recieve little credit

Blag: It's a slimed down Fedora Core w/ mp3 support IMHO it makes Fedora a lot better. It even uses the Fedora repos. (Debian wants this kind of relationship with Ubuntu it seems, in Blag's case it's a plus but I dont want Ubuntu to follow suit)

Arch Linux: It allows for complete control of you system, think Gentoo except binary. It has some of the best tools around for making packages (in the Arch format) and also for recompiling packages that are in the Arch repos with custom configs. IMHO it's one of the best Distros out there (goes to show how much The HPD list on DIstrowatch is not the end all is all place to go when looking for a distro  some of the smaller distros are fantastic. The default package manager "pacman" is the best package manager Ive ever used,(or will be with a few more features) It's ridiculusly simple and easily the best feature of Arch Linux. (Did I mention that Arch is i686 optimized?)

Frugalware Linux: Slackware with atitude. (hahaha) It's i686 optmized Slackware with the Arch's package management tool (Pacman) Amazing distro! The Devs are Very Fast and keep Frugalware at the bleeding edge! (more so then even Fedora) it's easily the fastest distro I have ever used. The only problems are the size of the repos (it's getting there...at least they have mono-develop) and also their community is almost non-exsistant (from my experiance)

After try all those distros (and many more) I have come to agree withj you isotonic
Ubuntu does truely rock! (and  "Just Work" seems to imply that it barely does what it has to do (which it does) but it does not give credit to the fact that enables the User to do what they Want to do with it. It doesn't cripple (unlike Linspire and Xandros) and yet it does not overwhelm (SUSE) I could go on and on...I'll stop here.
