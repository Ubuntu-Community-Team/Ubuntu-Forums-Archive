### Post by zero-9376 on 2008-01-26
I haven't played ETQW but I will say that I was very disapointed with crysis. I was able to run through the openning sequence (up to the guy dangling by the parachute) on medium plus a couple of high settings at 1280x960, from then on as it got more intense I had to progressively turn down the settings till i reached 800x600 with everything low. The game still looked pretty damn good but I was very disappointed considering that I dont think my system is bad and it was supposed to be scalable. For me HL2 EP2 came pretty close to crysis (although without the awesome destructable environment) and I was able to run that at 1680x1050 and its apparently optimised for ati where crysis is for nvidia. Whats more I found HL2 the better game for the same reason that i enjoyed farcry more than crysis, it had an engaging and interesting storyline. To me crysis was VERY boring although it may be partialy because I played it as a standard shooter where others have commented that it is better when you use the environment more but the underlying storyline is the same and I found it lacking. The entire level inside the 'ship' was an excellent example of how this game is both good and bad, it looks fantastic (especially with higher settings) but it was infuriatingly boring to play through. 

The technical excellence of crysis can't be ignored which is why I went back and watched/played a couple of bits again with higher (and by that i mean medium) settings including the end sequence but for me it wasn't one of those games that I will be playing through again, well until the sequel is released.

Sys Specs: XP Pro 32bit, P4 3.2Ghz, 2GB DDR, 8600GT
