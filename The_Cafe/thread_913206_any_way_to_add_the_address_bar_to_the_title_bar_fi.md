### Post by ~LoKe on 2008-09-07
That's not the title bar.  Right click on one of the bars and choose "customize".  A window will come up, but move it out of your way.  Click and drag the address bar and move it up into the menu bar.

I think [this](http://icedloki.deviantart.com/art/Desktop-as-seen-on-11-05-07-69083030) is what you're after.

### Post by smartboyathome on 2008-09-08
Google programs the window borders into Chrome (not that hard on Windows anymore), and Windows just sets the window border to "borderless" for that window. They also make their own toolkit. Because of this they can do whatever they can specialize it for what they want/need.

Similar could be done for Linux, but it would combine the window manager and toolkit, not a good move imo since not everyone likes the same window manager, and this would take away that choice.
