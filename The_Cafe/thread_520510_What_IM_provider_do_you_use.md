### Post by dreadlord_chris on 2007-08-08
> **super breadfish said:**
> Just make an MSN account. Trying to convert people would be a waste of time.

Slightly off topic, is it possible to have more than one IM account on the same address? I'm getting fed up of having a bunch of different addresses to remember, it would be nice if I could rope them into one.

That would be Jabber + Transports:
[http://www.jabber.org/user/userguide/ar01s12.html](http://www.jabber.org/user/userguide/ar01s12.html)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabber#Connecting_to_other_protocols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabber#Connecting_to_other_protocols)

i.e.
You have 1 Jabber account: [email]somebody@jabber.org[/email] - anyone on the Jabber network can reach you at that address. But, you also have several *legacy* IM accounts: Yahoo, MSN, AIM, ICQ, etc - and you'd like everything sorta merged. From your Jabber account you subscribe to *Transports* (think gateway or proxy) - there's one for each of the *Legacy IMs*. Sometime you might have to search a bit to find a Jabber server that has the transport you need - but generally speaking, the 3 "Big Boys" (AIM/Yahoo/MSN), can be found just about everywhere
