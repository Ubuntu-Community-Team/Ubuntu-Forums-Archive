### Post by TheFu on 2022-03-18
> functioning humidity:Below 80%;

I don't know where in the world you live, but I've lived in places where 95% humidity (95°F) was common 6 months of the year. We had to over-use air conditioning to wring the water out of the air there. High humidity can destroy electronics.  I live farther north now and away from the coast, but we still have high humidity here. Fortunately, the A/C doesn't have to cool nearly as much to be effective.  I would think that only mountain regions would never get to 80% humidity, unless there are storms.

I've worked in a place where they had rotating, scheduled, power outages daily. The country didn't have sufficient power generation for all the needs.  People had generators and alternators which fed into deep-cycle batteries. At each change-over of power, there was always a spike in the lines. Mainly, the power was used to re-chill the refrigeration devices and to re-charge all the house and compound batteries.  When we were under battery only, a TV would never be turned on. Just the networking and laptops would be used.  If power was out in the evenings, we'd start the generator for a few hours - mainly for family entertainment after dark.  When I arrived, they'd just replaced the deep cycle battery banks and alternator. Those things wear out.  I'd be more concerned about the UPS.  Power output from the UPS should be clean.  I have a few here, but only for computer equipment. They automatically perform tests daily and set off alarms when the tests fail.  I try to use less than 20% of their capacity steady-state.

I may seem odd, but the power issues really didn't bother me nearly as much as non-potable water from all the plumbing and cold showers (except in late afternoon from solar water heating). Not being able to get any shower water in my eyes or mouth after a lifetime of doing that was very hard to un-learn.

Can't say that I've ever seen 280V on any appliances anywhere in the world.  They are usually 110-240V.
