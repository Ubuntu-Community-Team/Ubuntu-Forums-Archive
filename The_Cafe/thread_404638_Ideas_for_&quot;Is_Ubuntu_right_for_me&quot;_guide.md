### Post by steevk on 2007-04-10
Please don't take this criticism as bad, or personal. I really like this guide overall...here's my thoughts on the revision:

Who is this guide written for? By your little glossary at the top, it seems to be written for the absolute beginner. Is that true? Than maybe parts like this need to be re-worded:

> There are virtually no viruses on Ubuntu, if you keep your firewall up and don't run potentially unsafe scripts. There is the occasional security vulnerability, but those are rarely, if ever, exploited. Just remember to not open a whole lot of ports on your router/firewall and avoid using the root account, even if it saves you typing in your password every time you do something system-related. XD

My concern with this is that a complete beginner doesn't need to hear words like 'ports' and talking about 'root,' a concept which doesn't even really exist with Ubuntu. If I were writing this section of the guide, I'd write this more along these lines:

> There are virtually no viruses to worry about with Ubuntu. While there are security vulnerabilities, they get patched quickly, thanks to the thousands of people worldwide who work on Linux. These patches are also easy to apply. Whenever a new security patch comes out, Ubuntu tells you via a little pop up menu that lets you click a few times to install them. In addition, every time you do something that could affect the system, Ubuntu asks you to put in your administrator password. So if you're not doing something that should mess with your system, you don't have to allow it to run. Browsing a web page should not change your operating system, for example. This feature allows you to still do what you want with your computer, but just makes sure that you have full control of your own system, and that a malicious program isn't changing things without your knowledge.

Do you see what I mean? Keep your audience in mind. If you're not shooting for the beginners, then what you wrote is fine...it just seems to me that you're going for complete newbies. In that case, as soon as they see 'root account' their eyes glaze over.
