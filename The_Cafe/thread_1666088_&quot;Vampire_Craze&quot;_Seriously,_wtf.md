### Post by luceerose on 2011-01-13
Wow. I was reading this article (posted: November 22nd, 2010);
[www.geekosystem.com/buffy-movie-no-joss-whedon/](www.geekosystem.com/buffy-movie-no-joss-whedon/)
about the upcoming 'Buffy' movie.
And in the article (I kid you not), are the words "the now five-year-old vampire craze"

Seriously ?!?
Is this article actually suggesting that there has been a 'vampire craze' from 2005 to 2010 ?
It might just be a case of me being ahead of the trends yet again but, as far as I'm concerned, 2005/2006 saw the END of the vampire craze. Certainly not the beginning to say the least.
Of course, you could argue that there has been a 'vampire craze' since Bram Stoker's popularization of Dracula in 1897, but I'm just talking about (fairly) recent movies here.
For me the turning point was Underworld:Evolution. Didn't this movie start progressing onto the Werewolves side of the tale because we were all sick of vampire movies by this point ???
I know I was.

e.g.
1994 - Interview with the Vampire
1995 - From Dusk 'til Dawn
1997 - Buffy TV Series
1998 - Blade
2000 - Dracula 2000
2002 - Blade 2
2002 - Queen of the Damned
2003 - Underworld
2004 - Blade Trinity
2004 - Dracula 3000
2005 - BloodRayne
2006 - Underworld Evolution
2008 - Twilight

Ummm... "five-year-old vampire craze" ?

Although, in hindsight, looking at this list - it may have actually been Dracula 2000 & Dracula 3000 that made me sick of vampire movies. Talk about flogging a dead horses carcus, I can't believe someone even decided to make a sequel to that Dracula 2000 VOMIT.
No wonder I was so happy to see more of the Werewolves in Underworld.

But what is this recent vampire craze everyone is talking about ?
Twilight ?
Does the box office have an audience full of emo teens too young to remember what was popular 10 years ago. causing the regurgitation of old trends ?

Opinions...
