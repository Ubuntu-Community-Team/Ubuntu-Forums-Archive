### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-09-09
The "Steve Ballmer" syndrome is starting to bite them in the butt.  When Gates stepped down to let Ballmer run the show as CEO, I thought...

**"Crap.  MS has gone from being a company that makes software to simply a company that makes money."**

So far, the prediction has been pretty accurate.  Gates used to keep the company ahead of the game by innovation (or, buying up the company's that were innovative, and also by hiring really innovative, smart folks).  Ballmer's trying to keep them ahead of the game by simply adding road-blocks to what they currently have, then trying to charge folks money to remove the roadblocks(EG: Trusted Computing Platform, Windows Genuine Advantage, Windows Anti-Virus, etc).

With Win95 and 98, MS went to great pains to make sure old software was compatible on it.  But XP marked a turning point where MS was all about forging ahead and forgetting the past, which happens to include some of the old software you were running on Win95/98.  I think folks were shocked by that, and they see it happening again with Vista.  Not exactly conducive to getting customers to upgrade.  Plus, having to get new hardware just to run the monster.  A $199 copy of Vista ends up costing you $1199 by the time you pop $1000 on a computer that can actually run it (along with anti-virus, spyware zappers, and all the other crap that has to tag-along just to keep it going).

Really, with all that going against it, who's the marketing genius that thought folks would bail on XP to go snatch up Vista like it was the cure for Cancer?
