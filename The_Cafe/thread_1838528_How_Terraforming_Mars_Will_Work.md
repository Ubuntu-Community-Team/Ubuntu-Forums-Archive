Exactly. We don't have the technology to do geoengineering on that kind of scale, and aren't likely to do so any time soon.

Even if we did have the technology, the economics don't stack up. What possible payoff would there be for expending that kind of energy and resources? Planet-sized magentic fields don't come cheap. Who would pay for it, and why? I've never heard a decent economic argument for the idea of terraforming. 

Why change a whole planet to suit us? It seems like doing it the hard way. A permanent outpost on Mars could be established without changing the whole planet, and for planets further afield it'd be much more elegant to change ourselves to suit the local conditions than vice versa.

Mars is a white elephant anyway, we'd be better off colonising Earth's Lagrange points than Mars.
