### Post by jflaker on 2007-12-08
GPS is based on maps and known signal speed.  Being sufficiently far away, a satellite triangulates your position by listening to the coordinated signal arrival.

All 3 (OR MORE FOR BETTER ACCURACY) sats send a beacon simultaneously from known geostationary locations and the reciever, based on arrival time can put your location on the map.

SO, with LPS, your distance is extremely small and with near light speed radio signals, would require extremely fast (much faster than we have today) radio and computing equipment to locate you.

Here is a better option using RFID.
Using a floor grid.  The person can have RFID tags on their feet with receivers in the floor** or** RFID on the floor and RFID receivers on the feet.  (figure out which works better by trial/error).  The LPS receiver will pull the RFID tag ID and based on your known map, display your location.

I am thinking RFID on the floor and the receivers on the feet so the receiver can be wired or bluetoothed to the LPS device.
