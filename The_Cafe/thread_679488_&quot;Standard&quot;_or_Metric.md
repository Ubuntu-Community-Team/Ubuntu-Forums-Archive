### Post by mssever on 2008-01-27
The Marshall Islands (or RMI) also use the standard system (to use the US term since US English is the dialect in use there). I believe that the other countries of the former UN Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands also use the same system, but I'm not positive.

When I spent ten months in the Marshallese capitol, Majuro, buying food was an interesting experience. Apparently, the RMI has no labeling standards of its own, so food is labeled according to the standards of the country of origin (nearly all food other than fish and coconut products is imported). So food from the US is dual-labeled in standard and metric (usually in round standard quantities) while food from, for example, Australia is labeled exclusively in metric units.

That was the trip that taught me the practical value of the metric system. Comparison is a whole lot easier in metric. I wish the US would switch over to metric. Didn't the Brits switch in the 1970s? We should study how they did it and learn from them.
