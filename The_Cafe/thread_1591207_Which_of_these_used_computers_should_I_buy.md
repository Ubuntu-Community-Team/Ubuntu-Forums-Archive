### Post by chessnerd on 2010-10-09
It depends on your needs. 4 looks best to me, because for $5 more you get a powerful dual-core processor. Next best #5 because of the hard drive. After that, #1 because of the dual-core processor and price. Then 3, then 2 (unless you need a floppy drive).

40 GB of hard drive should be fine unless you know you'll need more. I had a 20 GB hard drive with Windows 2000 installed on it until Windows 2000's end-of-life in July. I also had a laptop with XP SP2 on a 40 GB hard drive that served me just fine for a couple of years.

You're lucky you have the option of getting such cheap hardware. I'd probably grab a #5 to upgrade my family's communal desktop (it has a 2.0 GHz P4, 768 MB RAM, and an 80 GB hard drive) or even a #4 and then swap out the hard drive.
