### Post by t0p on 2009-08-26
> **Mr. Psychopath said:**
> I really don't know what to think of the FSF anymore. They go around doing awesome campaigns such as supporting ODF, XMPP, and open protocols. They fight DRM.

Then...they treat corporate products like the ultimate inherent evil, whereas I believe just greed and hatred are roots of evil instead rather than physical manifestations.


You, and some other posters in this thread, misunderstand what the FSF are all about.  They are *not* mere promoters of open source software.  They are a political movement.  They believe that the way intellectual property laws work in relation to proprietary software is antisocial and fundamentally wrong.  Some of you scoff at the use of the word "sin".  You need to realise that Stallman is not alone in his belief that proprietary software is an evil.  He may be more extreme in the strength of his convictions, but these are basically the principles of the FSF as an organization.

ES Raymond believes that the "bazaar" model of software development leads to a better product.  Stallman is different.  He does not necessarily believe that Free software is technically better than non-Free software.  His argument is that you should use Free software because non-Free software is morally wrong.

You may not agree with what the FSF stands for, but to say they should stop believing in it is plain wrong. It's like criticizing the Communist Party for being communist.  If you want to promote the technical superiority of Free software, join the Open Source Consortium.  If you want to stand for Freedom in computing, the FSF will welcome you with open arms.
