### Post by Maheriano on 2009-05-22
I thought it'd be fun to post the times we had to use Windows temporarily for things in our journeys with Ubuntu.

I was at my friend's house and we were getting ready to go to Dairy Queen (ice cream chain) to get some Blizzards (like a McFlurry). I had a coupon emailed to my Blackberry for 2 for 1 and I had to print it out before we left. So at my disposal I have my Blackberry, her Vista laptop and a printer that her roommate's dad left there when he moved back home. My plan was to email the coupon to her Hotmail, download it, set up the printer and then print it off, all in 30 minutes before we had to meet our realtor.

Here goes.....I emailed it fine and picked it up out of her Hotmail only it was a link to the coupon which can be printed directly from the browser and not an image as I had previously thought. So I followed the link and it was displayed on the screen. I tried to right-click but they had it all cut up into individual images displayed on the screen so then I hit PrntScrn to crop an image of it but nothing happeend. Oh yaaaaa.....Windows doesn't automatically bring up a SAVE dialog for the image. So screw it, I'll try and print it right from the browser.

So open another tab, go to the company website for the printer, input the model number and it brings up a Google search related to "drivers". WTF? I sort through everything and find what I think is a driver. I install it, plug in the printer, check Device Manager.....nothing. Except Windows has now installed its own proprietary driver as soon as I plugged it in. I unplug it, remove the software I just installed, unplug the printer, remove the proprietary drivers, go back to the website, finally find the right driver and install it. Hook up the printer again, print out the coupon, took me 25 minutes total.

I could have downloaded Ubuntu, burned a liveCD, booted to that and printed it faster. I need to start carrying a thumb drive install with me.

As for the screenshot:

An average user question: How can I take a screenshot of my sektop?

In Linux:
Hit the "Print Screen" button.

In Windows:
1. Hit the "Print Screen" button
2. Open MS Paint
3. Go to Edit menu and hit Paste
4. Go to File menu and hit Save as and choose .png format cause in .jpeg it will look like sh*t and in .bmp you will get a huge file.

That should take you about 3 seconds to do as it means you have to press about 5 keys. Snipping tool is available for people to use and you can even set a shortcut in Windows to use it (mine is CTRL+ALT+3), pin it to the start menu, put it in your quick launch or have a shortcut on your desktop.
