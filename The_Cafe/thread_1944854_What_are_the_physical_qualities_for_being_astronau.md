### Post by BeRoot ReBoot on 2012-03-22
Basically, the only fundamental physical requirements are perfect eyesight and huge muscle mass - because both are likely to get ruined after a few months in microgravity.

Also, astronauts/spationautes/cosmonauts/taikonauts are primarily selected from military aviators and scientists. If you're neither, it's likely the only way you'll ever go to space is as a tourist.

### Post by ccrs8 on 2012-03-22
I know two astronauts.  Both were mission specialists - picked as a specialist for a specific task of a specific mission.  In both cases, they were scientists selected to run experiments.  The physical requirements for a mission specialist are not too extreme.  You must be under a certain weight, have a certain cardiovascular health, and make it through some tests, but you don't need to have superhuman strength or eyesight (but correctable to 20/20).

To be a pilot or commander, on the other hand, requires a lot more, including tons of flight training.
