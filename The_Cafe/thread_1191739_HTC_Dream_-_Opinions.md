### Post by rfdeshon on 2009-07-17
> **abhiroopb said:**
> does the HTC have a word processor or can it access google docs and edit files?

You can access google docs through the browser. They have a mobile version that seems to work well (for me at least). There is talk that Google is working on a native app for Android but I can't confirm that for sure. I believe there's third party solutions (or are ones in the works) for editing MS Office docs on Android.

> 
Does the dream have multi-touch? I know the g1 does not, in my opinion that's a deal breaker for a touch phone 

Sort of. Last I read the code for multi-touch has been in the kernel since v. 1.0 but was commented out due to veiled threats from Apple that they would sue if Android had multi-touch. It seems that Apple, being the litigious jerks that they are, took out a patent on the entire CONCEPT of a multi-touch interface. That said, I've read that people have uncommented the code, recompiled the Android kernel and had it running with multi-touch on the G1 (apparently the hardware is capable of it). Since it's not officially supported though, basically no apps have support for multi-touch. It's something that will come once the Android developers are reasonably sure that Apple won't sue them I'm sure.
