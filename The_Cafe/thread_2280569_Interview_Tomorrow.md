### Post by mastablasta on 2015-06-09
oh internal movement? those were even weirder for me.

they asked me to come to the big boss office where they basically asked me if I would be willing to be promoted. it would mean extra work but they saw me as best candidate for the job due to my background. the interview wasn't too long. I found out what was interesting me and they got some confirmations as well. anyway I took it. soon after that I was instructed to whom my current work is to be assigned. I took care of that. started on my new post. organised it a bit start setting up the project. when suddenly all top management went to seminars (external consulting - got to love those) and suddenly no one from them would speak with me. when I told them I have things prepared but needed budget approval or at lest to discuss them and the options they told me to wait for another month or two. in the mean time I keep preparing the projects. I had many ideas at the time. some were good, some not so much. I also found some new and better suppliers. I got the products tested in the lab where they were so excited with them that they wanted me to get a few for them to be used at home. since I didn't get the budget approval because apparently they didn't have the time due to all consulting, I used some of my own money to get the samples. that's how enthusiastic I was. I was doing it all for the good of the company.

a few months later they told me another colleague would be taking my place and I get the old work place back. hmm I said "ok, but can I ask why"? I mean it was all already agreed and setup. their response: this is "how we now decided". they also asked me to give all my current project I was asigned and any other ideas to that colleague. I deleted most of them and gave only what was there already when I started. 

those products I found were a good idea, so they decided to get some. they had to find a different supplier who offered worse products and more expencive. I don't worry much about that since I am not responsible for those. 

anyway took/got my old job back - this was just after the economy collapse. so while most people managed to reach about 80% of their yearly sales plan. I got 130%. When others started reaching 100% I got 200%. I know I should move elsewhere. I tried (mentioned above), but didn't succeed. so I decided to lower the tempo a bit, get some more time with family, go on vacations AND push for bonus every once in a while. might not be a dream job, but I can usually work 8h a day. rarely I do overtime. and if I do it I make sure I use those hour to take time off as soon as I can.
