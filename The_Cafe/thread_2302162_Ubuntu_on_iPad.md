### Post by mystics on 2015-11-08
According to the Ubuntu Touch devices page ([https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)), only two tablets have official support. Those are the Nexus 7 and Nexus 10. All other tablets would be unofficial, and all devices on that page appear to be Android-based.

With that said, there might be ways to get Ubuntu desktop onto an iPad. At the very least, it's possible to do that on Surface devices. Whether or not it's possible an iPad is something I'm not sure of. 

However, I will say that as someone who has run Ubuntu Touch on a Nexus 5, it is not just a simple root device then install Ubuntu Touch. You replace Android, wiping virtually everything associated with it except the bootloader. The state of Android (e.g. rooted or not) prior to that is practically meaningless. Surface devices would be like any other Windows device as far as I know. 

Basically, what iOS does as a jailbroken device doesn't really dictate whether or not Ubuntu runs (reliably) on it. It depends on what Ubuntu itself can do on the device independent of iOS, and from what I know, that still hasn't been done and has been quite a challenge for a (compared to Android) very small number of people even trying.
