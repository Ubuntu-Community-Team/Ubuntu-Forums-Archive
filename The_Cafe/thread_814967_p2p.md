For games check out [Ubuntu Gamers Arena]("http://ubuntu-gamers-arena.org/"). There are excellent native games like Glest, Warsow, Flight Gear, Beyond The Red Line, Planeshift etc. For music I suggest [Jamendo]("http://www.jamendo.com"). There are excellent bands like Cool Cavemen, Poxfil, Jammin-Inc, Whiteroom. And if you want to download live performances of famous artists you can check out [FurthurNet]("http://www.furthurnet.org/index.php") and [etree.org BT]("http://bt.etree.org/"). For more legal music there's [Grooveshark]("http://www.grooveshark.com/"). For movie downloads you can find a lot of good stuff on [Public Domain Torrents]("http://www.publicdomaintorrents.com/nshowcat.html?category=ALL") and [Legit Torrents]("http://www.legittorrents.info/").

### Post by alwiap on 2008-06-06
I've been using Ubuntu since 6.06, and I used to use KTorrent for everything, which I loved, but I've downloaded less and less, and decided I would just use the default 'Transmission', and honestly, it's not that bad, and pretty minimal, which was what I was going for.  Also, I like the fact that by default the icon stays in the status bar (or whatever it's called) right next to Pidgin, with the same looking logo.  The only reason I didn't like KTorrent was because it was a native KDE program and the fonts looked a bit weird on GNOME.  However, I still believe KTorrent is an excellent piece of software.

I, too, download a lot of ebooks, and that's about it (an occasional distro as well).  I have some friends that do a lot of downloading, and they are hooked on [www.scrapetorrent.com](www.scrapetorrent.com) for searching.

I listen to a lot of obscure music that is sometimes out of print, and on a few rare finds I've been able to find a torrent of the music that I couldn't get my hands on any other way.
