### Post by crimesaucer on 2007-12-02
I've had to save a person from drowning two different times when I was out surfing. 


The first time (I was 16 years old), it was a little redneck girl who was swimming with her brother in Nags Head NC, and she had gotten pulled out by the undertow and then got caught in a strong riptide that swept her all the way out to where all of us surfers were sitting in the outside lineup... 


Her brother wouldn't leave the beach/shore-break to save her because he couldn't swim good either. 


I paddled over to her and put her onto my surfboard, and then swam my board in to the beach. 


It was in the late late afternoon and the sun had already gone down, so the lifeguards were all off duty. Actually one lifeguard was still hanging out on the beach with his friends, parting after his shift had ended, so he was sort of drunk... 


He saw it all happening, and ran down the beach like David Hasslehoof... then he ran out into the surf and did that lifeguard dive into the shore-break, and when he finally made it out to where I had already rescued the girl onto my surfboard, his face was bleeding from where he had dove straight into a shallow sandbar and had scrapped his face on the bottom of the ocean.


She and her brother did not thank me (or the bleeding lifeguard) at all. Neither did her redneck parents watching.




The other time, the exact same thing happened with some old European dude that didn't speak English... he was clawing and scratching me as I put him onto my surfboard. When I swam him in to the beach, he didn't say thank you either. I think he was more shocked and embarrassed to say thank you, or he didn't speak English at all and the whole drowning in America sort of freaked him out, so he just walked away.
