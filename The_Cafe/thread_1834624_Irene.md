### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-08-27
Hurricanes themselves are not a problem when they are Category 1 like Irene. It is the rain, flooding, and the tornadoes that they produce that is a problem. Those tornadoes can be more devastating than the hurricane itself.

The scariest hurricane I have lived through was Carla in 1961. I was living on the Texas Gulf Coast at the time. Carla was a Category 5 with winds of 175 mph (280 km/h). I think it was a Category 4 when it came through the area where I lived. Only 43 people died.

I remember how dark it was. The storm was so violent. It was as dark as night even during the day. The wind and rain were battering the house. You didn't go outside in those winds. We had to use candles because the power went out. We had to use water from bottles we filed beforehand; because, the water supply was either not working or contaminated. We stayed in our houses and hoped for the best.

I won't go into details about not flushing the toilet. That tank of water can come in useful for hygiene when you haven't had a supply of water in a few days :).

I hope things go well for people on the east coast of the USA.
