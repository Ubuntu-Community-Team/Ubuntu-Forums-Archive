### Post by TheMono on 2007-07-17
OK, I work in retail selling these things. Here's my opinion:

Samsungs are nice televisions, especially for the price. The only thing that lets them down is they tend to be too bright, reproducing blacks as greyer than other makes. If you are into sport as the main reason for it, you shouldn't find this a problem though and you will probably like this screen.

The Toshiba screens are extremely reliable products. Other than that though, they are pretty average pictures. Toshiba used to be great, but they aren't keeping up with the pace.

Sony's are beautiful panels but expensive. You will notice a smoother motion on the Sony panel than the Samsung, and the greens should be reproduced a little more faithfully, but not so much for reds and blues - they are much the same in quality.

I have to confess to being a panasonic fan - have a look into this panel if you haven't already (from the first UK website I found): [http://www.nextag.co.uk/Panasonic-TX32LMD70-32-LCD-539867519/uk/prices-html?nxtg=9eb40a240524-B3937BD53799DBDC](http://www.nextag.co.uk/Panasonic-TX32LMD70-32-LCD-539867519/uk/prices-html?nxtg=9eb40a240524-B3937BD53799DBDC)

The only other thing is that if I was buying a sport TV, I'd be buying a plasma as first preference, but they are hard to come by in the sizes you're interested in. Try this:
[http://www.nextag.co.uk/Panasonic-TH-37PX70B-540503195/uk/prices-html](http://www.nextag.co.uk/Panasonic-TH-37PX70B-540503195/uk/prices-html)

Plasma does not have the response time issues that LCD does, so you won't notice motion blurring while watching football on the plasma which you would on an LCD. Problem is that they basically start at 37", and as such are more expensive than a 32" panel.

Edit: Hence I voted other, but in the 32" range I'd probably buy the samsung if you're mainly a sport watcher.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2007-07-17
I work at an electrical store that sells Samsung and Sony TVs. Both my boss and I, who know the browngoods section, say that the Samsung LCDs are the best we've seen.

They have a beautiful picture, they look great, touch controls on the TV itself, easier and quicker to switch between AV inputs than the Sony, they have a 3 year warranty, and they are very well priced. I must say I haven't noticed any contrast problems or motion problems like the above posters have mentioned. In fact, Samsung's Full HD TVs have interpolation for unbelievably smooth motion, and a glossy screen for very deep blacks.

I'd rate Sony as "average" - I can't tell the difference in picture quality between a Sony KDL40V2000 and a Conia LCD of the same size. And Sony's integrated HD tuners seem to be rather fragile; our floor model is always the first to develop artifacts during bad weather, and sometimes running a boom box from the same power point can cause image degradation on the Sony TV. Not just our floor model either - I've observed problems when setting them up in people's houses.

I can't comment on Toshiba; we don't sell Toshiba and I don't think I've seen one of their screens.

I don't work for Samsung and I don't get any bonuses from them - I just think they're fantastic TVs. The only problem with them is that the built-in speakers on them are a bit tinny, but that's easily solved by running the sound out into a sound system.
