### Post by mediax on 2006-10-13
FWIW, I'm one of those people who chooses a trackball ahead of a mouse.

I do find it a lot more precise - and it certainly reduces the number of times I accidentally move the mouse when clicking a button.  The biggest advantage for me, however, is that they have a small footprint on my overcrowded desk.

The main drawback I find is that for some applications (particularly games) it's a lot easier to move the cursor quickly over long distances (e.g. one side of the screen to the other) with a mouse.  The solution is just to tweak the sensitivity settings, but personally I can rarely be bothered. :roll: 

The other "problem" with trackballs is that there is no standard layout - different models are designed so that the ball is moved with the thumb, first finger or second finger.  The choice is great, but it does mean that the advice to "try before you buy" is well worth following.

I think it's one of those things that comes down to personal preference.  Personally I prefer trackballs to mice and absolutely hate the touchpad systems on laptops!

### Post by jonrkc on 2006-10-13
I've used a Logitech corded mouse, then a Logitech wireless mouse (loved it and still do!), and finally have gone to a thumb-operated Logitech "MarbleMan" trackball because of limited desk space and a faint nagging suspicion that it's better for my wrist, though in many years of daily computer use to the tune of hours a day, I've never suffered any kind of RSI.  I'm careful to keep my wrists straight; as a former cyclist and a current guitar player, I cannot afford to ruin my wrists.

Anyway.  The trackball works with minimal tweaking, and I find adjusting the cursor speed easy using xset.  I seldom have to change it from the setting I've found easiest for me, but it only takes about five seconds if I do want to change it.  

Some environments such as Xfce will highjack your xset settings but you can override the environment's setting using xset--again.  Normally I use IceWM which nicely lets you do as you please with about everything.

I will probably switch back to the wonderful wireless mouse someday just for change's sake, and then back to the trackball.  There's room for diversity!  :)
