### Post by gutterslob on 2010-12-29
> **matty-guy said:**
> How do I apply the slightly darker versions of colours in MOC? I tried dark blue and stuff like that but couldn't get it to work.
Your .Xdefaults have 2 values for each colour, right?

In your MoC theme file, the first variable is for your foreground text colour, the second is for your background colour (specifying "default" gives you your standard BG), and the third is sort of a modifier that you can set to bold,reverse,underline..etc your first variable.

Lets use blue as an example;
"selected_file = blue  default" gives you the first blue (color#4) with the default term background for your selected file.
"selected_file = blue  default bold" gives you the second blue (color#12) with the default term background for your selected file (it will also bolden your font if you've enabled bold fonts in your .Xdefaults)

Hope this helps.

### Post by hhh on 2010-12-30
> **Rasa1111 said:**
> wow!
 that is just beautiful hhh!! 

Can you tell me how you customized the color yourself? 
Thank you, sir! Navigate to a template in the src folder in Clarity, template_canus.svg for instance. Open it in a text editor and save it to the name you want, template_Custom.svg for instance. Near the bottom of the file are these two lines...```
<stop id="start" style="stop-color:#dbdbdb;stop-opacity:1" offset="0" />
<stop id="stop" style="stop-color:#757575;stop-opacity:1" offset="1" />
``` Those are the start and stop hex colors of your icon gradient. The line a little below that...```
<use style="opacity:0.45;fill:#000000;fill-opacity:1;stroke:none"
```is the color of the icon's shadow. So for my icons, I did```
<stop id="start" style="stop-color:#B2C8BC;stop-opacity:1" offset="0" />
<stop id="stop" style="stop-color:#445743;stop-opacity:1" offset="1" />

<use style="opacity:0.45;fill:#051002;fill-opacity:1;stroke:none"
```
Now go up a level so your back in the Clarity directory. Click the change-theme script and choose your new theme, hit OK when it's done, change to another icon theme and back to see the changes.

I use Gcolor2 to pick my hex codes. Happy tweaking!
