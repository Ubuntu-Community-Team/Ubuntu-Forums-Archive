### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-11-08
Worse thing is to sit around and be glum about it so good to see you are looking on the brighter side. Are you mobile at all? Can you still stay involved in some small way? 

I played volleyball for many years and when I was at the 'height of my powers' I broke the ulna and radius in my right arm severely and was out of action for about ten months. Once the reality of the situation hit me and the tears and moping were over, I coached the team for nine months! 

Who knows, there may be a role for you with the team behind the scenes that you haven't noticed before because you were concentrating elsewhere ... :-k :)

PS: Did the medicos identify why the pain killers had that effect and have they found any that do work? If they have, don't feel like you need to be a martyr and suffer through it drug-free. The ones I had for my arm were quite pleasant and I used to wait until I couldn't handle the pain any longer before taking them and enjoy the relief of some pain free hours.
