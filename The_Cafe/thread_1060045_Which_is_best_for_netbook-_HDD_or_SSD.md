### Post by Skripka on 2009-02-04
SSDs have a faster boot time, use less battery.

HDDs are cheaper to replace, and have higher storage capacity.



Your odds of having either fail are quite small.

### Post by Johnsie on 2009-02-04
HDD has moving parts so is more fragile. Can be slow sometimes.

SSD has no moving parts so can take a good beating. Can also be slow sometimes (depends on the hardware).

I use an EEEPC with an SSD and am very happy with its performance. My main problem is disk size. I've had intrepid running on it since about june last year and haven't had any problems. The SSD in am Acer One I configured seemed a bit slower though

[http://www.fudzilla.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=9291&Itemid=1](http://www.fudzilla.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=9291&Itemid=1)
> 
**Intel claims 5 years SSD life.**

...

There's also a lot of talk about wear leveling, something most people aren't aware of when it comes to SSD drives, and as long as the manufacturer of the SSD drives has incorporated this properly, there shouldn't be any major concerns for drive failure or data loss, as there is spare capacity to handle some worn out data blocks. One interesting point is that if you would manage to wear down your SSD drive, in other words manage to wear out the memory chips, then **the SSD drive will go into read only mode and no data will be lost**.
