If 667 Mhz is okay for you right now, I wouldn't upgrade. But it would make your system faster. First though, check out the maximum ram speed your motherboard can handle.

### Post by undecim on 2012-01-22
Higher clock speed alone doesn't make RAM faster, because RAM with higher clock speeds have higher CL.

To get the real speed of your ram, multiply CL (the first number of your RAM timings) by 1000, then divide by the clock speed in MHz. This is how long milliseconds takes for the RAM to return a value when after it's been requested. For most sticks, it's around 6 or 7 milliseconds.
