### Post by popch on 2008-03-27
I have owned a number of canon ink jet printers, and have them left standing most of the time, some of them quite a bit more than a year. They all went on printing just fine.

The main thing is to shut them down properly, not just pull the plug or switch off the power strip. If done properly, the printing heads will go into parking mode and position, thus conserving your precious ink.

### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2008-08-16
It is a change in the business model. A long time ago a printer cost close to $1000; however, the ink cartridges cost $1. As the price of computers dropped they had to find a way to make the price of a printer go down as well. When the printer manufactures dropped the initial cost of the printer they compensated their revenue by raising the cost of the ink cartridges. They also made the ink cartridge smaller.
