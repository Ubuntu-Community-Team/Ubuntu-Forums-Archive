### Post by bonzodog on 2007-11-23
xorg is far from antiquated, in fact it is one of the most advanced windowing systems currently in development. It has roots originally as part of the XFree86 project, which is years old, but they forked the code a long while ago.

Xorg is under very heavy development, and Ubuntu has a tendency to use beta/testing versions in it's stable releases, which is why we get crashability. 

Some of xorgs instability is also down to how it was built and packaged by the devs -- everyone makes mistakes, and it's very easy to screw up a package compilation of X, as there are so many individual parts.
