### Post by dbbolton on 2007-08-14
i like to use docks sometimes.

i think that docks such as avant and xfce-panel are more convenient for window management than the gnome/kde panels because avant and xfce-panel allow for an "iconified" window list, whereas gnome/kde-panels rely on a tiny icon beside text, or a near-useless "window selector", which only shows one window's icon. for me, it's faster and takes less brain power to find the window i want based on an icon rather than a string of text.

the launchers on all panels and docks are virtually identical. end of story.

two of the chief initial complaints dealt with screen area. afaik, all docks and panels can be automatically hidden when not in use. even if you don't automatically hide the dock in os x, you probably have more free screen area thanks to the universal menubar.
