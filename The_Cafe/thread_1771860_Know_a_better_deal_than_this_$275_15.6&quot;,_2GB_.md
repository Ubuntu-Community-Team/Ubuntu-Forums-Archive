### Post by LowSky on 2011-05-31
I wouldn't spend less than $400 on nearly any computer. If I'm buying a laptop I would only buy new. The reason is the battery, most only last 2-3 years at best. The next is the size of the battery, anything smaller than 4 cells is barely going to last 2-3 hours on a charge. Best to get a laptop with a 6 or 9 cell so you get 6-12 hours of play. I wouldn't buy a laptop with a screen over 15 inches. The bigger the screen the more the PC will weigh and use more power. Processor speed means nearly nothing these days. For best results search for their current line ups. A  new processor might only say 1.6 Ghz and yet it will run circles around a much older 2.5 GHz model, make sure its at least a dual core chip, if not walk away. Hard drive size should be over 120GB by this point. 250-320 should be standard if it uses a standard hard drive and not an SSD, expect less than 120GB for those, but faster read times and much higher cost. Ask yourself if you really need a DVD drive, models without are becoming the norm and should cost a few dollars less. Also I like to use business oriented models over consumer lineups, like Lenovo's Thinkpad, or Dell's Vostro. The business lineups usually better built to withstand movement and falls. They may cost more but usually last longer.
