### Post by solitaire on 2008-03-06
Think the FAA (or your regional Flight agency) will say that ALL electronic equipment need to be off during take off and landing.

During flight you can use a laptop but make sure the wireless connection (if you have one) is turned off / disabled before you get on the plane.

There are strict rules about radio transmitters inside a plane. Although there is no evidence of them interfering with the plane, the FAA tend to err of the side of caution and ban all transmitters during flight. But double check with your flight operator a few days before you travel.


But in my experience if there is no wireless on the laptop you can use it during a flight.
