**Dell to sell laptops at Sam's Club**

Move expands on PC-maker's agreement to sell desktops at Wal-Mart.

June 8 2007: 2:17 PM EDT

SAN FRANCISCO (Reuters) -- Dell Inc. said Friday it will start selling consumer notebook computers at more than 500 of Wal-Mart Stores Inc.'s Sam's Club stores starting next week, its latest move to sell computers in retail chains.

Dell (up $0.17 to $27.16, Charts, Fortune 500) said last month it would start selling desktop computers for less than $700 each in more than 3,000 Wal-Mart (up $0.24 to $50.00, Charts, Fortune 500) stores in North America. Friday's announcement expands Dell's retail push to notebook computers, which are typically more expensive and more profitable than low-end desktops.

The Inspiron 1501 notebooks will have Turion dual-core processors from Advanced Micro Devices Inc. (up $0.44 to $14.12, Charts, Fortune 500), two gigabytes of memory and 120-gigabyte hard-disk drives, Dell spokesman David Frink said. They will feature Microsoft Corp.'s (Charts, Fortune 500) Vista Home Premium operating system, he said.

Frink said he did not immediately have information on the notebooks' prices.
