### Post by toupeiro on 2010-04-10
My Daily-driver is a 2007 Piaggio MP3-500.  I dare say its a scooter (that goes ~ 120mph on the speedo but I don't take it that fast), but its pretty much in a class of its own.

My biggest recommendation is to NOT buy one of those 1,000-1,500 scooters. You will not get 3 years out of it.  We bought one for my wife and she only put 300 miles on it.  In that time, it broke down 4 times.  2 electrical problems, 1 mechanical and 1 body.  Some of those parts are near impossible to find, if not impossible, when they do break.  It's just not worth it. We got rid of it, and I refuse to buy her another one unless its a known, better crafted bike.  

Keep one thing in mind when buying any scooter or motorcycle. YOUR BODY IS WORTH MORE THAN $1,500.  If they're selling it to you brand new at that price, there is a good reason for it, and its universally applicable.  You get what you pay for!

You may not think 20-35mph is fast, but wait until you have no chassis, seatbelt, or airbag to absorb an impact.  Even a small fender bender, or going over a mild pothole or uneven railroad crossing can be pretty traumatic if you don't know how to absorb it, or if the bike is made too poorly to absorb it at road-safe speeds. 

You're better off finding a used vespa, honda, or something mainstream that is built with quality parts that you can replace with quality parts should they need it.

With bikes, never skimp on tires, oil changes, plug changes, and manufacturers maintenance regardless of who makes it.  You'll do chain/belt adjustments, throttle adjustments, plug replacements, and brakes, which can mostly be done very easily by yourself with a manual.  Personally, I don't do brakes anymore.  I want to trust whats going to save my life to someone who knows how to calibrate them regardless of the type of brakes they are.

My scooter goes new for about 8K I got mine for 7K with 200 miles on it, but gets almost 60MPG.  I bought it when gas was at about $4.25/gal.  the amount of money I would have saved after 1 year on gas at that price would have paid for over 65% (closer to 70 I believe) of the cost of the bike itself, and my bike is made as well as my cousins ninja motorcycle.  Braided cables, water cooled, dual-shock rear suspension, VERY well made.

### Post by handy on 2010-04-10
I've owned 14 of the things, large & small, road, off road & compromises of those two.

I'd recommend a 4 stroke trail bike for a variety of reasons:

You sit up higher & therefore have better vision.

You have wide handlebars which give you more leverage/control if/when something unexpected happens.

You have suspension that will cope with any kind of pot hole & will allow you to ride up a gutter if you have to, to avoid trouble.

The upright riding position & wide handlebars are easier on your back, wrists & entire body.

A Honda XR 200 2nd hand costs about $1200- here, just to give you an idea.

I'd buy Japanese.  The Chinese bikes have engines that are copies of Honda, Yamaha & such, but they aren't made to the same standard & will not last as long or be as reliable.

Most of the bikes made since the early 1980s have electronic ignition now, which means that changing oil, filters, chain maintenance is usually all you have to worry about.

When the tyres wear out you can put tyres on that are more suitable for sealed roads too.

Everything about owning & running a bike is cheaper.

Wear good protective clothing. It always freaks me out when I see people riding a bike in shorts, sandals, t shirt & no gloves.

Gravel rash is a horrible thing, & when we fall we put our hands out first.

That said, riding a bike is an enormous amount of fun, I hope it all works out well for you. :)
