### Post by popch on 2007-11-04
As far as I know the placement of the gap or notch indicates physical parameters of the RAM stick, such as speed or voltage. The gap in the one shown with the OP is not quite in the center but a bit offset. This prevents you from inserting it the wrong way round as well as from inserting products which may damage your computer or be damaged by it.

See if there is an inscription (on the board or a label) on speed (measured in MHz) or voltage (measured in V).

I think that if it fits into the slot in your computer while accomodating the notch in the board it will work.

DDR400 is the highest standard for 184pin DDR RAM.  you can get up to DDR500 speed modules in 184pin (i'm running DDR500 modules in a DDR400 standard motherboard) but they are only used when overclocking.

you can run any DDR speed modules (DDR200, DDR266, DDR333 or DDR400), DDR400 is the fastest, since it has a bus speed of 200MHz, where as DDR333 has a buss speed of 166MHz, DDR266 has a bus speed of 133MHz and DDR200 has a bus speed of 100MHz.

he could have a DDR400 module in his motherboard, but his motherboard could only support 166MHz bus speeds.

either way, if you had a DDR400 module in there, you can use DDR400.

> **hanzj said:**

Probably the difference goes to integrated video?

RAM speed is how fast the processor can access what is on the memory.  In many cases, this is as important as how much you have available.  4gb of slow ram is no good, because the bottleneck will be the ability to access it in a timely manner.  Likewise, if you can access the ram quick enough, making it faster really doesn't help without having any more to access in the time saved.  So it is a balance.

In your case, what are the rest of the specs of the computer?  If you really are only using 192mb of ram, I highly doubt you would see any practical gain going over 1gb of ram.  Also, consider getting 2 sticks of 512 rather than one 1gb.  It tends to access faster, plus if your motherboard is capable, it will run in dual channel, for even more speed increase.
