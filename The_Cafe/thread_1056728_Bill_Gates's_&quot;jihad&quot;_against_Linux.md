I don't know if this is posted here before, but I found it rather interesting:

[http://boycottnovell.com/2009/01/30/microsoft-intel-anti-linux/]("http://boycottnovell.com/2009/01/30/microsoft-intel-anti-linux/")

This gives you an idea of how the upper management in Microsoft viewed or attack Linux.

Executive summary:
- This is based on evidence gathered during the Iowa vs Microsoft anti-trust case, a case where they eventually settled for something like $500 million dollars in cash.
- Bill Gates uses the word "jihad" to describe the companies activities against Linux.
- E-mails between upper management in the company suggest they are trying to push Intel away from supporting or investing in Linux (
- E-mails between upper management suggest that Microsoft has plants in OEMs (in this case Compaq)
- "On the OEM side I am thinking of putting hitting the OEM harder than in the past with anti Linux actions" <-- quote says it all

This is from the information released to the public. On top of all this, a ton of other evidence used in this case is confidential. I guess somehow in American justice system it's possible to hide really damaging evidence.
