### Post by chucky chuckaluck on 2009-06-07
i don't think they're intending a british accent, but i can see how you would be under that impression. one of the three essential differences between speaking and singing is that it frequently takes longer to say a word while singing than it does during speech. most people are at a loss as to what to do about it. in this case, they're extending the vowel length (which is the right thing to do), but in the case of diphthongs (the english language is full of them), they're making all parts of the vowel equal in length rather than making the diphthong the same length as a consonant. this distorts the words and can give the impression that another accent is being employed. 

in the case of british singers imitating an american accent, that's more a matter of british singers imitating their favorite american blues singers.
