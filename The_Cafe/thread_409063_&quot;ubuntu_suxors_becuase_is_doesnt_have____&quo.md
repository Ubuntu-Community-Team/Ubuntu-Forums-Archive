### Post by dspari1 on 2007-04-14
Totem-gstreamer now does a very good job detecting and doing automatic downloading of codecs that are needed to play files. As long as players do automatic detecting and downloads of codecs, the average users won't even remember that it wasn't installed by default.

With that said,I still get some sites with vidoes that doesn't work even with the proper codecs installed. One site may work with totem-gstreamer, but doesn't work with totem-xine and vice-versa.   

kubuntu on the other-hand has the advantage of having KMplayer(konqueror). KMplayer for konqueror does automaticly switches between mplayer and xine until it plays (in more cases than not). I actually get more compatibility using konqueror than I do firefox because of this. The only problem I had was finding and installing all the codecs manually to get everything working.

Another point that I want to make that is bothering me is that the Adobe Flash for both Konqueror and Firefox are not the same. For example, abcnews.com and foxnews.com videos(they both use adobe flash) does not work with Firefox, but they work great with konqueror. However, msnbc.com (also uses flash) works with firefox,  but it doesn't work with Konqueror.

I find it annoying that I have to switch between browsers to get 99% compatibility  with the sites I visit. I hope this is something that will Iron out in the long run; thus I'll be able to just pick one browser and expect everything to work.
