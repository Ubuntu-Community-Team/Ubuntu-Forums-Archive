The Apple II/II+/IIe/IIc/III/III+ never came with a GUI as standard. You could load GEOS on to it though just like you could with a Commodore 64 and others but none of them came with a GUI as standard.

The first computer to come with a GUI was the Apple Lisa in 1983. This was a totally different architecture to the Apple II machines. The Apple Macintosh followed in 1984. In 1986 Apple produce the Apple IIGS (16bit) which also had a GUI but was a different architecture to the original Apple II machines but offered backward Apple II compatibility. The machine was to little to late and was pretty much a financial failure when compared to the more powerfull 32bit offering from Amiga & Atari which came out a year earlier.
