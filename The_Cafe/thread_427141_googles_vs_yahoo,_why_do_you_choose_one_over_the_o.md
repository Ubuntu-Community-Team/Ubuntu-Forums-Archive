The subpoena is for broad data on search habits, not personal information. But the request has raised alarms among industry observers and civil libertarians who wonder what kind of data search engines have about their users -- and what other, more sensitive data the government may seek next.

**Google, Microsoft's MSN, Yahoo and AOL received subpoenas for a random sampling of millions of Internet addresses cataloged in their databases, as well as for records for potentially billions of searches made over a one-week period. Only Google refused to comply.** The Justice Department wants to use the data to support its argument that Web-filtering software doesn't work.[/quote]
