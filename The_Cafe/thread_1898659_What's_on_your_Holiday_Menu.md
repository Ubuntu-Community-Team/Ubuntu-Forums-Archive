You don't hear the words "tasty" and "carp" in the same sentence very often. Hopefully English carp are a lot different than American ones. ;)

well... to my knowledge, the most "popular" carp specie in USA is so-called "silver carp" [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qfG4vsJ5_xI](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qfG4vsJ5_xI) which isn't really good /so i heard/... the carp i cut few hours ago is a mirror carp (aka king carp [IMG]http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2f/Cyprinus_carpio-King_Carp.jpg[/IMG] - they are kept in UK mostly for "sport", they aren't recognised as edible fish. but, i wasn't born in UK, and in the country that i originated from, carps are traditional christmas meal /like turkey here/.
btw, for "clean" the meat from it's muddy taste /if carp was kept in muddy water and/or its big/ it's good to cover it's meat in kefir /basically: fermented milk/ overnight in a fridge... it usually helps /although it won't make any miracles: that's why you should usually choose rather small carp /no more than 2 kg ~4.5 lbs/.
if in doubts, you can always find someone from central/eastern europe and ask about it /maybe get a bit for free?/ :)

after a bit of googling, i've found a solution for silver carp edibility: you have to eat no longer than 1 hour after it's killed. freezing etc won't help
