### Post by dca on 2007-09-07
Not to sound like a stinker but Ubuntu is technically nothing new...  It uses the Linux kernel orig developed by Linus Torvalds in 91.  The Linux kernel is POSIX compliant as most other *nix kernels: BSD, Unix, Solaris, etc.  Because of the ideas behind open source software a lot of enterprises are using Linux distributions in their data center because of low acquisition cost and TCO...
 
Other distributions using Linux kernel:
 
SuSE
Red Hat
Debian (which is what Ubuntu is based on)
Slackware
 
I would say because of GNU and the portability of the Linux kernel (GPL licensing) is the cause of why there are so many distributions (OSs) based on it...

### Post by EvilMarshmallow on 2007-09-07
> how does it help people make a living when theres not really any body making money out of it

Computers, in and of themselves, are not necessarily a source of revenue.  Many IT people work in companies that don't do anything computer related, but they make their living because they make the systems work.

I work in a foundry.  Cast Iron has VERY little to do with computers.  But my company needs me because they want to have services like email and a website.  So I make my living by the fact that I'm able to manage their systems for them.

Not all the money in the software development industry is made by selling a program on retail shelves...
