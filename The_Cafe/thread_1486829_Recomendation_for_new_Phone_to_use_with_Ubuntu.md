### Post by McRat on 2010-05-18
I hate cellphones, and while having owned a dozen or more over the last 14 years, I seldom carried one.

My wife bought me an iPhone and I laughed at first.

Well then I started to use it.  Now I carry a phone everyday.

Why?

I have man fingers.  I can type a number on the iPhone, the first time, every time.
I do not need detailed instructions on how to check my voicemail.
I can see the display, and hear the phone ring from across the room.  

The other features are simple to use, and don't need instructions.  

I know a lot of people complain about this, but I can go 3 to 8 days between recharging.  I'm not much of phone person perhaps.  I use minutes a year, not hours.  If I can't say it in 5 minutes, I should write a letter instead.  But I turn off Bluetooth, 3G, WiFi, and Locator Services unless I'm using them.  That has more to do with it than anything.  They use a lot of power even when idling.

But the bad news.  AT&T in 2010 is absolutely the worst managed phone company I've ever dealt with.  This was not always true.  If anything causes me to switch phones, it will be AT&T.  There customer service is terrible.  It can take hours to make simple account changes.  Even at their best, they don't match Sprint's worst.

PS - There is a COOL network tool for $4 for the iPhone that helps you analyze LAN and WAN issues.  Compass is very handy when setting antennas.  And you can "jail-break" it for free and enable Tethering which permits you to use the phone as a mobile HotSpot against AT&T's will.
