### Post by MikeTheC on 2009-03-06
I remember when the Mighty Mouse first came out. I didn't need one and hadn't actually used one until quite a while into their established presence in Apple's product line up. Eventually I did use them when playing around with displays. Then, perhaps a couple years later, I bought one, desiring something "new" in the mouse department.

Let's just say I returned it within several hours of purchase, as I simply couldn't stand how it tracked (no matter how I adjusted it), hated the side-grip buttons (which I turned off, but then what's the sense in having buttons that you could, ostensibly, make use of, turned off 100% of the time?), and found that it had surface compatibility issues which reminded me of first generation optical mice from about 2000.

Ultimately, I've switched over completely to "laser" optical mice, and can't even conceive of going back to an "optical" mouse, which is what Apple's so-called "Mighty" mouse still is to this day.

Right now, I use a Logitech cordless MX620 on my eMachines system. It's without a doubt the best wireless mouse I've ever owned, and even if you forget about the wireless part, this series of mice are great, they're contoured to fit your hand, have excellent ratchet/free-spin scroll wheels, no buttons in the way of the grip area, etc. It works perfectly in both Linux and Windows.
