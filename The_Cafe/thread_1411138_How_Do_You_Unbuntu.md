### Post by BETATEST on 2010-02-19
Just wondering how people deployed Ubuntu on their systems.  

Me, personally, I now have it as a VMWare session on my Windows 7 laptop.  

Not that I have anything against Ubuntu (or Linux in general) being the Primary Host OS ... but there were just so many quirks between 9.10 and my laptop that I never experienced on my desktop.  

I know, it's due a lot in part of the rigid proprietary nature of laptop hardware vs. a more flexible nature of desktop hardware.  On my desktop, I could swap out easily to a video card that Ubuntu loved ... whereas that's not really possible with my laptop.  So, on my laptop, I just leveled the playing field with VMWare.  

Some people complain about lag, overhead, how it requires to have a really beefed up machine and the cost of that, how Ubuntu just doesn't respond as fast in a Virtual environment, and how they want off Windows completely.  I've never really encountered slow problems myself and I only have a 2 gig system ... but the way I see it, VMWare made Ubuntu "Just Work" again on my laptop when before ... Ubuntu 9.10 just had me and others banging our heads.

Where I work, it's a Microsoft Only shop.  I have a company laptop.  Even if Ubuntu had installed peachy keen on the laptop, new corporate IT policies would have required it to be removed anyway.  (In all fairness, their equipment and their rules.)  So, I boot up Windows 7 - and then start my Ubuntu 9.10 session.  I can work in both environments simultaneously.  I'm happy, company is happy, Ubuntu is happy - it's a win-win-win.

Now if I could just get a OSX on there too - I'd be happier than a Koala Bear with an entire Eucalyptus forest to itself.
