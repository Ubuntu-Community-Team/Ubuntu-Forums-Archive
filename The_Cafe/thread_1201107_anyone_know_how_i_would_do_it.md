### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-06-30
If you don't want to get your hands dirty with wiring a phone plug, you could always get a pair of Homeplug networking units. They look like those bulky power adapters with an Ethernet socket in the bottom. They transmit and recieve Ethernet signals through your home's electrical wiring, up to 85 megabits per second.

They are simply plug and play, and are completely network-transparent (your computer and modem will think that they are connected through a long Ethernet cable).

I'm going to get a set today, because I'm sick of my wireless disconnecting and being really slow.

### Post by cariboo on 2009-06-30
It did basically the same thing to connect to the network in my shop, I put RJ45 connectors on the phone line that runs through conduit 4 ft underground, one ends up in the external phone box on the side of the house, and the other end connects to the phone connector box in the shop. I then used two inline ethernet connectors (why do these things cost $20CDN). On one end the cable connects to my router, and on the other end it connects to my SMC access point, and then to an 8 port switch.
