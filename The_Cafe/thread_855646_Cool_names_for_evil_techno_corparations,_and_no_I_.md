### Post by popch on 2008-07-11
Some names which I rather like but have been taken:

R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots)
General Products
Fokker-Mueller

From off the top of my head, I can think of four categories of names for corporations:
Founder's name (e.g. Miller's, Roche)
Name of the location where the corporation resides
Name descriptive of what the corporation does (see my first example, Precision Gears, or BASF)
Arbitrary words or made-up words


Some strange effects occur in the course of time when a corporation changes the owner, the nature of its business or when it merges with other corporations. The interesting cases are
Name remains unchanged
Names are concatenated

The third case (name is adapted or replaced) is not 'interesting' because it then becomes just another name.

Hence, I thought my original suggestion (Precision Gears) slightly amusing because the name was wildly inaccurate for what the corporation actually does, but it might suggest some strange periods within the history of the corporation.
