### Post by ve4cib on 2009-11-10
> **SuperSonic4 said:**
> RE: Point 2: The Fall of the Berlin Wall is of infinite more importance and that happened 20 years ago yesterday

"Importance" is entirely subjective.  I don't deny that the fall of the Berlin Wall was a major event in world history, and I agree that it's probably more "important" than Sesame Street.

But I can easily see how someone could place Sesame Street on the same level of importance, albeit in a different arena.  Sesame Street represents a major milestone in popular culture, and provides one of the relatively few multi-generational pop-culture reference points in society.  Almost everyone can relate on a personal level to Sesame Street.  Comparably few people can relate personally to a wall being torn down in Germany.

Some might argue that it's more important to be easily relatable to the largest number of people.  Others (like ourselves) might argue that a major socio-political event of the 20th Century is more important than a pop-culture icon.  And none of us is wrong; just different.
