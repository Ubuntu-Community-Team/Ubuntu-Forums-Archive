### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2009-07-12
For me the best movies are not always the movies with the best plot.
Take the first Star Wars film, compared to the others (and yes that includes the prequel trilogy even though episode I sucks big) it has a very paper thin plotline... but its how it was done is what matters here, A new hope took old dungeons and dragons style storytelling and flung it into a futuristic setting and made legends of its own.
In fact movies with too much plot can be bad too, look at the lord of the rings movies for example.
I might be lynched for this but honestly I disliked the Lord of the rings films, too long and sometimes too dull.
If I can film three movies that were five hours long with a majority of said film be people walking I would get booed out by many.
But because this is a "epic saga" LOTR gets away with murder.
But the LOTR movies suffer the same weaknesses the books have, having so much time getting to know every last detail of the story but at the same time making it overwhelming in terms of how many plots to take care of.
Now I am not knocking Tolkien, I love his works but sometimes yeh I felt he overdid it.
Its possible to develop a good long story with a beginning and an end without too much strain for the reader, instead of having just three large books in LOTR maybe 5 smaller books would be more appreciated in a time where attention spans are small.
Now lucky for me I have great attention spans when getting into a sotry but even for me LOTR can be a tough read.
