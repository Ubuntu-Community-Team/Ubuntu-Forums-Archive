### Post by CSMatt on 2008-02-24
It must have been a year or so now since BitTorrent Inc. officially stopped developing their BitTorrent client and re branded uTorrent as BitTorrent 6, and so far no one has bothered to pick up development of the original client.  Why?  I know it wasn't the most popular of clients after Azureus and uTorrent came out, but it was elegant and simplistic.  Sure there's libtorrent, but that won't run in Windows or Mac.  And I tried installing the last BitTorrent client in Ubuntu, then Fedora, and it was so old that it couldn't find the dependencies anymore.  Even compiling from source didn't work.

The only thing I find myself using BitTorrent for anymore is for occasionally downloading free software ISOs, and I'd like to not have to launch a client that has all sorts of bells and whistles that I don't want or need.  I have a hard time believing that I'm the only one who misses BitTorrent and wishes that someone would resume development, so why hasn't it happened?
