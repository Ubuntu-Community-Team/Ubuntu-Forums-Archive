### Post by 50words on 2008-08-15
I just installed Windows XP Pro on my ThinkPad last night after using Ubuntu exclusively for months. While I still prefer Ubuntu, it has been nice to get a few things back.

(I'm not a hater; I still use Ubuntu--with Windows XP in VirtualBox for Acrobat and my ScanSnap--on my office desktop and have a small Xubuntu partition on my ThinkPad.)

Here is what I can do easily in Windows that I cannot do easily (or at all) in Ubuntu:

[LIST]
[*]Use my webcam to capture video. In Ubuntu, Cheese did not work for me at all.
[*]Edit video. Could not do this at all in Ubuntu (yes, I tried every video editor in the repos, and yes, I realize this could be hardware-related).
[*]Use the middle-button scrolling feature of my TrackPoint (could do this in Ubuntu, but only if I did not mind pasting a lot of stuff randomly into my documents).
[*]Use the buttons on my USB Skype handset.
[*]Use iTunes and my iPod Touch.
[*]Sync my Blackberry Pearl.
[*]Edit PDFs (the Ubuntu solutions are clumsy and therefore not very useful).
[*]Use Miro.
[/LIST]

What I prefer about Ubuntu:
[LIST]
[*]Bluefish, my favorite text editor.
[*]Easy SSH.
[*]Easy updates with few restarts required.
[*]The network manager applet.
[*]Add/Remove Programs
[*]F-Spot
[*]Virtual desktops
[/LIST]

I realize that some or all of the things that are easier in Windows are possible in Ubuntu. They are just easier in Windows. And there are good alternatives to most of the things that I prefer about Ubuntu in Windows.

But I am not interested in spending all my time entering terminal commands to get things working or just to edit PDFs.

And I realize that some or all of the above are not important to many Ubuntu users. And hey, I get by without them just fine at work. But they are areas in which I would like to see improvement.

So that's my list. What would you add?

### Post by eragon100 on 2008-09-01
I have just switched to vista ultimate yesterday (had the cd around, wante to try it...) and so far it's about 4 to 5 times as fast as ubuntu with everything on the desktop (starting programs and such), and the frame rate in games is about 20 % faster (nexuiz, ET:QW, savage, warsow...). For some reason, sauerbraten runs twice as fast on maximum settings at 1680 x 1050 res in vista then it does on the same hardware in ubuntu at medium settings with 1280 x 1024 res. I realize that's probably just because the NVIDIA windows drivers might simply be better, but the performance difference is there anyway, so why not take advantage of it?

On the desktop, Vista just manages your RAM better. (But I do have 3 GB of RAM :)). In task manager it may say it uses 900 mb while idling, but it doesn't use that RAM. About 400 to 500 mb of it is reserved for starting applications, which is why it runs circles around ubuntu on the same machine. Ubuntu only uses 350 mb of memory... until you start programs, and that takes way longer. IMHO, ubuntu is a great OS, way better than xp, and should be installed on every computer with less than 2 GB of ram and / or by everyone who doesn't have the money to buy vista or wants to spend it on something else, but vista is better for computers with 3 GB of ram or more, a fast CPU, and a good GPU.

Just my 0.02 $ :wink:
