### Post by djm227 on 2009-10-13
What I wrote here is strictly my opinion based on a little bit of fact...and I kind of wrote too much, so don't read it if you don't want to.
---

Now I am extremely new to the Linux world, I'm in about a week, but ever since I began using it I have been thinking the whole time "why the hell don't more people use this system?  It seems just as good, if not better, than any OS Microsoft has ever offered".  

Now I know that all distributions of Linux have been struggling over the years to grab a decent chunk of the home OS market share.  However, from the recent trend of events, and predictable future events, I can really see a huge jump in the use of free/open OS. Some examples: 

1.) Vista was a huge failure.  Apple saw an increase in their market share by a little bit, and I'm sure Linux did as well.  That whole thing started getting people thinking that Microsoft could let people down....and even turned some people off permanently.

2.) Google Chrome.  There is a gigantic buzz about this...even people who aren't "computer people" know that Google is developing an OS.  What many average people don't know, however, is that it will be free and a Linux distro.  This is huge, because not only will many people try out Chrome, but it will get them interested in Linux in general.  Google is attempting to go toe to toe with Microsoft....and the competition will force innovation from all sides, something we have not seen for a long time.  

3.) Microsoft is getting scared.  I don't remember how long ago it was, but they FOR THE FIRST TIME filed Linux as a competitor.  They used to blow it off like Linux couldn't touch them...now they are feeling a little bit human.

If you think about it logically, home computing has only been big since the early 90's (roughly 19 years).  Even though it is well established, it is still a very new industry, one that has not yet fully adjusted into the market.  There is virtually NO competition for Microsoft, which is a bad thing....but fortunately people are starting to realize that.  Competition and demand are the only things that will force innovation in the OS industry, and I think the start is not to far off.

I, for one, am going to do all that I can to help.  I will be switching my family to Linux this weekend and my roommate will find a Wubi install on his laptop when he gets back in town (don't worry, he's cool with it).  I'm not saying Microsoft is bad, they just desperately need some competition.


Edit: and by the way...I'm new.  I'm looking forward to being a member of the community.  Thank you in advance for putting up with my stupid questions.
