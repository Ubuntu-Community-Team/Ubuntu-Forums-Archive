### Post by esodin on 2007-04-01
Well, call me pragmatic because that's what I am. I have no emotional attachment to Windows nor penguins.

I have mainly focused my computer time the last few years around getting as much performance as possible out of my hardware - over clocking and  water cooling all the way. When Vista was released I decided to give  it a shot and figured, that while I was at it, I would also give Linux a shot - I had - after all figured out that much of the reason for the slowdown over time came from the endless MS security updates.

I installed Vista on a system with 4 disks already running XP. Well here is a little ammunitions for those of you that like to rag on MS: It took my 20 hours (and 4 activations) to install Vista - turns out the driver for my nvidia SATA controller (on a pretty new ASUS top of the line mobo) was non-existent. After endless hours of searching the web; involving memory sticks and having to swap to the secondary SATA controller i got the Beast up and running. By boot loader was of course all messed up, so I ended up having to install XP again - on the bright sight; I guess it was time for a fresh install anyways. Vista is pretty responsive, eye-candy is up from XP, security is many times better, and the update system makes more sense (Thought: Funny, now that I have dipped my to in Linux I realize that all those good "new" things in Vista already exist in Linux)

I then installed Ubuntu 6.10. Had a hell of a time getting it to boot, but after a few evening's on the prowl for info I figured GRUB out (God, how I now love GRUB - best thing since Copernic Desktop Search). Took me another few evenings to get my dual monitors on nVidia to work right (thanks, Automatix), and with wine running those Windows apps I can't do without worked great - well almost: I still cant get CS:S and TeamSpeak to play nice at the same time. 

I have poured hours into getting Ubuntu to work with all my stuff: NAS drives, Media centers, LAN's etc - and i have loved every minute of it! This stuff is great. 

So, can I throw out Windows? - No, not quite yet. 

For most my admin stuff I am almost there. I still need to find a way to convert 10 years and 15Gb, of emails from Office 2003 pst files to Mbox or some other "universal" format. My app for keeping track of my DVD's, Movie Collector from Collectorz.com, works great through *wine*, but I have a hard time giving up Copernic DT Search. While Beagle is on the right track, it still has a way to go both in regards to usability and speed. 

For gaming, sadly, XP will stay the preferred OS for quite some time. There may be great games native to Linux, but I play CS:S and Battlefield - thats where my buds are. Graphics and sound are left wanting under *wine * and I found it to be quite unstable. 

Sorry about dragging this out - my point is: In many aspects Linux is superior to Windows, for some uses XP is still king. We don't live in black and white world - it's all shades of gray. 

PS 
My next "project" is to put Ubuntu on my work PC, a Sony VAIO SZ - that should give me hours of fun - can't wait.
