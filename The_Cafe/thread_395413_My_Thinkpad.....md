### Post by Aetherius on 2007-03-28
Recently got myself a little Thinkpad R60e, and last night I installed Feisty Beta on it, and I'm incredibly impressed. EVERYTHING WORKS OUT OF THE BOX.

Was running feisty from herd 2 on my desktop but it got neglected and was never updated, so I was out of the loop as all the fantastic improvements came to be.

[LIST]
[*]Wireless Roaming (with WPA!!!) OUT OF THE BOX
[*](Broadcomm!!) Bluetooth OUT OF THE BOX
[*]All the special little function keys (hibernate, sleep, lock screen) fully functional OUT OF THE BOX
[*]And the most impressive point: Desktop-Effects only a single click away. God bless my GMA 950
[/LIST]

One thing I really liked was immediately after I installed and booted Feisty, a little pop up in the notification area informed me that I was using a restricted driver for the wireless and asked me if i was ok with this, giving me the option to remove the closed driver.

ThinkPads are extremely Linux-friendly. Too bad you have one with the Windows key on it...I think that when production switched over to Lenovo, they found a cheaper keyboard manufacturer that insisted on including the key. Or, they said "well, you'll get this key too" and the dummies at Lenovo thought "well, if it's free!"

Wireless, with the Atheros card, is the only thing that at times doesn't work flawlessly.
