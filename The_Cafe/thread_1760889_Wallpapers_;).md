### Post by DZ* on 2011-05-17
The main attribute of a good wallpaper, IMO, is redundancy.  The theme or "story" should not be lost if you block a substantial part of a wallpaper. This can be accomplished by multiple presence of similar objects. For example, to have a single cute kitten in the center is not good. One may even be tempted to place applications around it, trying not to block the kitten. OTOH, a group of kittens scattered throughout the screen might work.

Another important attribute is a relatively low level of detail: it shouldn't be distracting, and it should not be too complex. A group of kittens is Ok, but not on a background of a Where is Waldo poster.
