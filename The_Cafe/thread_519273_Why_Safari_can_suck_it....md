### Post by izanbardprince on 2007-08-06
1. Fails to respect the Windows user interface norms, like every other Apple product, it has to force it's own UI on you.

2. Can somebody explain to me why Safari needs 2.5 times as much RAM as Firefox?

3. The only reason Safari had any access to plug-in's was because it raided my Firefox plug-in's, the official Flash installer won't detect Safari or install a plug-in for it, so if you want flash, first you have to install Firefox, then Safari.

4. No extensions, I like my Adblock Plus and Flash Block and all the other tools you can bolt onto Firefox, with Safari you're stuck with whatever Apple decides to give you, I wouldn't even mind that so much if they didn't suck as bad as they do at writing software, the Safari team needs a crash course from Opera.

5. Firefox renders pages significantly faster, Safari has issues where it sometimes gets "stuck" loading pages.

6. While Webkit is open-source, Safari itself isn't, which means that user improvements to Safari are limited to Webkit, and thats if Apple takes the patch, if the user submits a patch to Webkit, they've essentially developed software that Apple will sell for profit, but receive no pay for their work.

7. The license for Safari is long, convoluted, and heavily padded with non-relevant clauses, such as the user is not allowed to use their copy of Safari to develop or proliferate nuclear or biological weapons, because making nuclear or biological weapons "violates United States law", somebody better tell Apple that sovereign nations are not subject to US laws in the first place.

Anyway, how is that going to look on a physicist's diagram? "Plutonium...check...detonator....check....copy of Safari Beta 3 for Windows.......check"

8. Default homepage is advertisements for Apple products.

9. Does overloading a system's RAM pool and effectively "nuking" the operating system's ability to function put them in violation of their own agreement?

10. It's rigged so that it won't work well with plugins from any other media player than Quicktime, and I hate Quicktime slightly worse than Safari, and if you grab Quicktime with Safari, it automatically grabs iTunes, which I hate worse than Quicktime, therefore Safari installs foistware.
