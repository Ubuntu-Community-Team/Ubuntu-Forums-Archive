### Post by Tadaen_Sylvermane on 2022-06-11
I've always had trouble getting internet where I live. For the longest time the best I could get was fixed wireless. Way to expensive for way to little bandwidth. I signed up for the Starlink thing 2 years ago. After setbacks with pandemic and such it finally arrived a week or two ago.

This thing is great. The bandwidth bounces around. My worst speedtest was 80mbit down. Best was 260mbit down. Uploads have been between 10 and 28mbit. The latency and consistency though is rock solid. My wife has had zero trouble with working at home on this connection. This includes her vpn, voip stuff, everything. We can stream, game, work all at the same time with no problems. Worst ping I've seen thus far is 110ms. Average is 50.

All hail Starlink. Internet for rednecks has arrived!

### Post by TheFu on 2022-06-13
In the US, some of the wireless internet (old cell-phone) companies have made 50Mbps connections available for $50/month.  So it comes down to having a good antenna for your remote location.  

A friend has remote property in a few different states. Both are 100% off-grid.  In the mountain location, he uses a $20/month addon to his normal cell family plan to add unlimited data in the cabin there.  The cell tower is across the valley on the next mountain over. He built a little parabolic director for the router cell antenna to get better signal.

At a ranch property where he leases pasture land to some neighbors, he has a different setup, but also uses cell data.  You never know until you try.  [https://www.t-mobile.com/home-internet](https://www.t-mobile.com/home-internet) is one. This is not special 12-month only pricing. It is their no-contract price.
