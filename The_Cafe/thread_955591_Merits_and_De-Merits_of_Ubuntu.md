### Post by Dragonbite on 2008-10-22
Not sure if you are basically asking for a Pro/Con opinion of Ubuntu or not.

Merits:
[LIST]
[*]Easy to install with a smart starting selection of applications
[*]The option to varying starting points (alternative, server, kubuntu, xubuntu, etc.)
[*]Frequent update schedule
[*]Stability of Linux
[*]Good hardware recognition
[*]Easy package management and means to keep system updated
[*]Helpful and active community for questions
[*]Marketing recognition
[*]Drive towards the enterprise market (so if I wanted to try and do side-work or convince a company to go with Linux can use Ubuntu, which I am familiar with, for server, desktop and other)
[*]Easy process to add multimedia codecs or proprietary applications such as Flash at user's option
[/LIST]

De-Merits:
Interface is good, bu[LIST]
[*]t not as "WOW" or shiny as others (openSUSE, OS X, etc.)
[*]Some packages are not as up-to-date as others
[*]Ubuntu is not the "rebel" of Linux distributions anymore (it's becoming *gasp* mainstream!!)
[*]Does not include codecs out-of-the-box
[*]Forums are too active (can't keep up with them!)
[*]Does a lot for you, so there isn't a lot of tweaking involved ;)
[/LIST]

### Post by 1cewolf on 2008-10-23
Ubuntu's strengths:

+Mainstream...and that's not a bad thing!
+Outstanding marketing
+Big-time financial backing
+Relatively easy to use
+Uses Synaptic, arguably one of the best package managers out there
+Best forums of any Linux distribution, bar none
+Launchpad rules!
+Brainstorm rules!

Ubuntu's weaknesses:

> 4. Too many packages tied into others with no logic (e.g. try to remove evolution or printing system.) 
-Most people seem to use Thunderbird instead of Evolution, but Evolution is left as the default.
-Lacks polish in many ways. For example, the Human icon theme isn't attractive and there's still not a clear distinction between Preferences & Administration.
-Not as innovative as it should be. Ubuntu needs to pick up on some of what Linux Mint is doing, especially in regards to their menu.
-GNOME is stagnant. I'm sorry, but when the ability to restore from the trash is FINALLY added in **2008**...that's not a good thing. KDE & Qt are running circles around GNOME and GTK right now. I think most of Linux's "killer apps", like Amarok and K3b, are on KDE.
-Could use a lower sudo timeout.
