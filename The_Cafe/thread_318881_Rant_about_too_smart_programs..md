### Post by Engnome on 2006-12-14
I hate programs that go "But I know better than you therefore this won't work right now, sorry"  Maybe not the best example but anyway:

Yesterday I decided to start making notes in tomboy, nice app.
10 minutes ago I tried removing the "Start here" note. But guess what? The delete button is grayed out. Why? I only have one note right now, I don't need a note just to keep track of my other note. I don't know why I got so mad (maybe cause I'm hungry?) but I got to the "steam coming out of my ears" stage. There's no key for "Don't show the ****** Start here note" in gconf. Renaming it doesn't work either, it's now called "Untitled 3" but I still can't delete it. ](*,)  I bet there is some way to remove it but why not with the delete button?

This was one of the biggest reasons I switched from windows. No OS or program should assume it knows better than I do. Period. I see a dark future for my sanity if GNOME/Ubuntu starts going down this path. I understand that some people feel GNOME makes/assumes their users are stupid and sometimes I agree.

Thanks, feels a lot better now. If someone feels the same it would be even better, knowing I'm not alone.

btw when typing in this rant I got a red underline under "Ubuntu", our spell checker doesn't have ubuntu in it's dictionary? :mrgreen:
