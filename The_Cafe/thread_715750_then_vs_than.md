### Post by p_quarles on 2008-03-05
"Then" indicates s sequence of things, either in time or in logic. That is, "first we went to the store, then we went to see a movie," or "if that's true, then I must be late for my appointment." The first example is a temporal sequence, and the second example is a logical sequence. 

"Than" compares things, and so always comes with an adjective. I.e., "that movie was worse than Gigli," or "a chain is no stronger than its weakest link." In both cases, you'll notice that "than" directly follows an adjective -- "worse" and "weakest" respectively.
