### Post by Crowchild on 2010-03-09
Just need to vent a little...


So my warranty on my dell xps 1330 is about to end... that means everything is starting to fall apart. About three months ago the gpu burnt out and Dell convinced me it was better to pay for a three month extension of my warranty so that I would be covered for the repair rather than pay for the part. Now that extra 3 months is up, the cmos battery won't hold a charge. 

I called customer service and told them this. They said someone would call me in the next few days to do an onsite repair. I asked them to double check this as I live 7 hours drive from the nearest town with more than 2000 people and couldn't see the possibility of someone coming from there just to fix my laptop. They said no problem. It would be taken care of... 'it's covered in your warranty.'

A week later, when nothing had happened I call to see where this mysterious travelling repairman was. They told me the computer had been fixed. This surprised me because I would have thought I'd notice someone coming into my house and opening up my computer. Still, they insisted it had been fixed. I insisted otherwise and eventually they told me that it hadn't been fixed (Surprise!?!?) but the work order had been cancelled do to my location. I asked why no one bothered to contact me when the work order had been cancelled. They went back to saying that it was because the laptop had been repaired. 

I gave up on trying to figure out why nothing had been done and started onto how to get something done. They told me that because, of where I lived I'd have to meet the repairman half way (!?!?). Incredulous, I mentioned again that, if said repairman was coming from a city, half way would be 8 hours drive for the both of us. "No problem", said customer service. "Big problem" said I. But, I had to know... I asked "Where is this person coming from?" The response, "Canada." I might have yelled a bit after that one.

Eventually, they agreed to have me ship the laptop to a depot where it could be repaired. When it came back there was a note saying there was nothing wrong with the laptop but that they had replaced a faulty cmos battery that they had noticed in their inspection.

In the mean, time technical service had told me that I could get a refund on my warranty but needed to talk to customer service to get my money back. Customer service told me I had to talk to technical service to get the okay to refund the warranty. Technical service gave me the okay and sent me back to customer service. Who accidentally hung up on me. When I called back the switchboard operator sent me to Dell Ubuntu customer service, who had no idea about anything, so they put me through to tech service who put me through to customer service. Who told me that they couldn't do anything. I agreed. (Each time, they forwarded me there was a minimum 30 min wait to talk to a human.)


If I was getting paid for the time I've spent on the phone with Dell at the same rate I do for my work, I'd have the laptop paid off already.

I loved my xps 1330 and would have bought one again if it hadn't been for this experience. No wonder Dell's stock price/market share is dropping. If the can't keep the customers they've already got, how are they going to get new ones?

### Post by earthpigg on 2010-03-09

unlike laptops, where the OEM has a near monopoly on parts for that laptop, and ***_will_*** get a piece of the pie. i have a busted HP laptop, for example.... the hard drive has connector things are ***_just_*** different enough that the only place i can get a hard drive that will fit is HP.

IMO: get a nice desktop, and for mobile computing needs... get a dirt cheap netbook unless you really need computing power. assume that whatever you buy, it's components will start to fail 3 years from now.

### Post by Objekt on 2010-03-10
That's weird.  I thought Alienware were the poorest laptops.  In 2008, we bought 11 of their m15x model where I work, so we could run 3D apps on the road.  Worst Purchase Ever.  Complete pieces of junk.

At least 7 out of the 11 broke in some way during the first year, despite very gentle treatment.  Due to their huge size & weight, people didn't often take them on the road.  Many of the hardware casualties never left our office (until being shipped in for repairs!).

People got tired of them breaking REAL quick.  2 years later, only a couple of the Alienwares are still being used.  One of those is my officemate's.  He'll leave it running overnight and come back to a bluescreen (running Vista Enterprise).  Bluescreens often mean a hardware fault, so I guess his Alienware is going in the dumpster next.

Interestingly, Alienware did not go out of their way to make repairs impossible.  The drive is a standard SATA 2.5" HDD.  My Acer Aspire One netbook also uses a regular 2.5" SATA hard drive, not some proprietary Acer-only part.

FWIW, I have hardly ever seen a Dell business-line laptop (Latitude) go down for any reason.  We've had dozens in the 4 years I've worked here, with only a few that broke.

I think it's best to consider laptops disposable items, and plan accordingly.  This may mean buying an extended warranty.  Computers have an annoying tendency to develop problems just after the warranty expiration.
