### Post by genti on 2008-12-09
I wrote something about this a while ago ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=332668&page=4](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=332668&page=4)) but I think now it deserves its own thread.

1. I buy an Acer laptop ~ end December 2007.
I was waiting for a job answer that gives me a laptop so I did not open it yet. (I got the job by the way).

2. I send an email to Acer in January asking about the Windows refund. Acer in US. They reply that I need to call. Ok.

3. I call. The tech support was friendly but she did not know anything about the Windows refund. After explaining to her and after she asked her coworkers she told me that I need to send it back. Refund is going to be less than $50 for Vista premium.

4 I send it with Fedex the next day.I paid ~$9.50. I ship it in its sealed original box.

5. I receive the computer after 2 weeks. WINDOWS IS STILL ON!! They had just opened the computer and sent it back. They had agreed to the terms for me. I delete their Windows version and install Ubuntu + Vista Business (I will switch to XP Pro, maybe VMware if I solve the suspend issues).
A nice picture of how I received it, check the RAM bay cover.
[IMG]http://www.setupmypage.com/vmware/acer/_MG_7344.jpg[/IMG]
It was simply not closed properly, nothing damaged(so far).

6. I wait almost 2 weeks and call Acer to ask about my check. They say the tech has screwed up but still I HAVE TO SEND IT BACK, For the 2nd time. OMG!
Ok, i thought about it. The previous effort is considered sunk cost and this time they sent me a Fedex prepaid slip via email(after me insisting, initially they wanted me to pay). Next day I ship it back.

7. The kicker, ~10 days later they call me back.
They say there is a crack on the computer and the **motherboard is damaged.** It is not under warranty and it costs ~$445. WTF.
[IMG]http://www.setupmypage.com/vmware/acer/tada.jpg[/IMG]

I packed it with their original box and I was 100% sure that there were no cracks when I send it. I barely used it for 2 weeks. I asked whether the package was damaged. The rep says "I will need to check that sir..."
Before I saw the damage I asked them on the phone whether the damage could have been because of shipping but they insisted that it was inside, from what the customer representative was telling I was expecting a big hole and missing keyboard.
You can see from the picture that the damage is clearly on the SIDE. To have a better idea of where the place is have a look at the next picture, it is on the left side, near the Intel logo (same laptop but I found this picture from Google Images, Hardwarezone.com)

[IMG]http://www.hardwarezone.com/img/data/articles/2008/2591/Acer_4620.jpg[/IMG]

Well after talking on the phone with them again they refuse to fix it. I also told them that I had a witness that can verify that I shipped the laptop intact but they would not care. (The witness is my wife, she was making fun of me the whole time for going through this for a lousy refund). Well, it is the principle, isn't it?

8. I refuse to pay. I bought the laptop during a sale for $499+tax. They ship it back after the 2 week limit(they can keep it in their "repair" facility for up to a certain time).
When I receive the laptop I open it, I say what the hell, why not, I don't have much to loose.
**IT WORKS! **The freaking laptop works fine. No motherboard BS.

I decided to contact the Legal Services at my university. They helped me write a letter where I explained the situation and demanded some action from Acer. Of course Acer did not respond.

Then I paid a very small fee and had the office send a pre-lawsuit letter. Here I made my second mistake, I did not ask 
Acer specifically for the fee I paid. I guess I was dreaming, I thought that once somebody who has even the slightest customer service skills will read the letter they will fix the laptop, send me an appology letter or instead of fixing it they would send me a new laptop.
That did not happen of course.

Acer goes back and fourth and finally we agree that they would send me a new prepaid Fedex slip and I would ship it back for the 3rd time so they could fix it and remove Windows.
