### Post by dgrafix on 2008-09-10
I think the image of the unwashed boring geek who rants endlessly about open source is an unfortunate and hard to shake stereotype, but times and opinions are changing. Not that talking about open source is bad, but it really does sound like a religious tirade to those who do not understand, are totally uninterested or just want to use a computer rather than mate with it (like in the video that chilli bob posted). Radical ideas tend to need a slow and timely introduction!

Most of the people I know who use it are tech savvy, but far from being total geeks in the usual sense. 

As linux becomes more refined, easy to use and if people could prove that linux IS a commercially viable platform for developers (ie the many using linux dont mind paying for games and 'special' software on occasion) then linux would be unstoppable. Until they do though, linux will rarely attract true professionals for specialized use in areas like film & media, photography, game designers, musicians, artists etc. While open source apps are excellent and good enough for 80% of people, they are usually not of industry standard grade. I know many professional artists and photographers who love using linux but cannot simply because there is no photoshop.
