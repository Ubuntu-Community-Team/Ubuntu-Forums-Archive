The role of AV is not to just "keep the bad stuff out". That is the sort of magic app thinking that we are continually trying to educate people against on these forums.

The role of AV is more overarching than that: at the very least, it is supposed to be about good computing hygiene, which starts with user awareness and promotion of good user habits. If we allow ourselves to get all Star Warsie about it, they are supposed to be on the light side; not the dark.

But when a platform ostensibly dedicated to security suddenly starts enabling dangerous and/or foolish user habits, then they are well on their way to the dark side.

The same principles are at work on these forums: discussions of aircrack and system penetration are off&#8209;limits, not because these are inherently bad (pen testing is a time honoured and necessary practice) but because such knowledge can be easily subverted to do bad things. We would not be discharging our duties as good community citizens if we were even seen to be condoning it.

What is Norton's stance if not outright condoning of a practice far more sketchy? How does blurring the line between self&#8209;instigated crypto mining and the malware sort contribute to good computing hygiene? How does this enhance user awareness? Promote good user habits? And if they don't feel that they are gatekeepers for such values, then they should stop calling themselves a "security" company and admit to what they really are: an "exploitation" company every bit as flippant about our security as the social media giants.

In Kafke's *Die Verwandlung* (the Metamorphosis), Gregor, a travelling salesman supporting his sister and parents, is turned into an *ungeheueres Ungeziefer* (monstrous bug might be a good idiomatic translation into English, although the German is a bit nuanced).

I've always interpreted this bizarre bit of nightmarish surrealism as not only a story of Gregor's plight and his family's growing disgust to the point of Gregor's death, but also of his sister Grete's transformation in the eyes of her parents to be worthy of finding a husband.

Two metamorphoses:  Grete rises to the top on Gregor's demise.  That is just one of many possible interpretations, but I like it.  If you ever have the chance, learn German and read it.
