### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-05-04
These forums are the best way! I tried to join the _Beginners' Team_ but couldn't. I jumped through all the hoops they told me to (creating a Wiki and Launchpad account and stuff), and the next thing was to join them in their IRC chatroom. But you have to "register a nickname" in order to participate, and several attempts at it failed. But I was able to converse with others in the Beginners' Team chatroom, so I asked for help and did exactly everything they instructed. For nearly *an hour and a half*. No luck. But it was very entertaining for all the folks in there _watching the newbie struggle_.

I tried again two days later for about 30 minutes and got the same result, again much to the delight of the Beginners' Team members who got another 30 minutes of entertainment watching the newbie struggle with something that's supposed to be so simple.

I finally posted a goodbye in my "Introductions" thread in their forum. "I'll just go over to the Dark Side," I wrote. "It's the quick and easy path anyway, right?"

I dunno what possessed me to look in something called "Beginners' Team" to help me out as a beginner.

But I successfully joined the Florida LoCo team! I was all excited about meeting some other "Ubuntuans" and learning all I could and helping spread the word about this awesome, wonderful Linux distro! So I got this e-mail from the team about a "Release Party" for Jaunty and couldn't wait to go. I drove 60 miles to get there - and no one showed up. Turns out they changed the date to the next day, but forgot to include the newbie in the notification - even though the Florida LoCo team has it's own forum right here on this site.

So... no more release parties for this Florida Ubuntu fanboy. And no more Beginners' Team, and no more attempts at getting an IRC "nickname."

Just stick to the forums and contribute that way. It's fun and maybe you can help someone else along the way too.

-Robin
