### Post by youbuntu on 2010-10-03
These are the things I detest about other geeks, being one myself:

1/ Many, MANY geeks seem *totally* incapable of trying to translate code in a SIMPLE analogous way, so that at least the *basic* principles can be understood by the man in the street - it's a skill, but it isn't that hard to do!

2/ Why do all these screencasting nerds INSIST on using TINY terminal fonts, and then uploading to YouTube in 360p/480p, where you couldn't make it out anyway, due to YouTube compression/crap resolution?! The same applies for lecture presenters - they more often than not have their terminal font set to a TINY size - how are the audience supposed to SEE?

3/ My pet hate, is the rebellious "SMACK" that "showoff geek" will give the return key, after having hammered in 3 lines of code, to show the folks something; some guy just did it on a lecture in Google video, and I felt the urge to laugh! Dude, if you hit **my** keyboard that hard, I'll smack you in the plums!!

4/ All of the above. I am far from perfect, but as well as being a geek, I am an EXTREMELY sociable and empathetic person, and I know how hard it can to understand code, **without** further obstacles being put in my way by thoughtless tutors

Kinda a rant, but in a light hearted way :)
