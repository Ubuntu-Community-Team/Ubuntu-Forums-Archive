### Post by ScionicSpectre on 2011-08-26
Saying Ubuntu's built on GNOME 3 is almost as bad as saying Xubuntu or Lubuntu is built on GNOME 3. If a file manager, a settings dialog, and a daemon or two are enough to qualify it as GNOME 3-based, I think the line just got ultra blurry.

Most non-KDE environments will be borrowing heavily from GTK 3 based applications in the near future, so I think it's fair to refer to GNOME-specific applications and the GNOME environment as GNOME, first and foremost.

In other words, if Unity didn't exist, no one would feel any pressure to blur this line or make this distinction. GNOME is a lot more than what's included with Ubuntu in 11.10, and most people will be thinking of the whole package, including GNOME Shell, when you mention GNOME 3 or GNOME.

Also, Arch is the best GNOME 3 distribution ever. D: You simply cannot defeat it! (actually, Fedora isn't bad, and they're nice enough to include Aldabra in the repositories). Then again, Arch is the best <insert any DE but Unity here> distro, if you're looking for up to date, vanilla packages. Oh baby, yeah.
