### Post by JustinR on 2010-07-26
Hey everybody,

I've been using a Dell Inspiron 1720 for the past two years, and while it's been a great laptop - and it's still running strong, its getting old. And because of Dell's lack of customization on their laptops now than when I bought this laptop I can't find a worthy successor to this laptop.

So I started looking around on HP's website and found the HP Pavillion dv8t series laptop.

Here is it's customized configuration:

> 
Intel(R) Core(TM) i5-520M *(Better virtualization options than the baseline)* Dual Core processor (2.40GHz, 3MB L3 Cache)with Turbo Boost up to 2.66 GHz *(Already faster than my current computer, and speed was never and issue so this will be fine)*

6GB DDR3 System Memory (2 Dimm)

640GB 7200RPM SATA Dual Hard Drive (320GB x 2) with HP ProtectSmart Hard Drive Protection

Nvidia GeForce GT 230M

18.4" diagonal High Definition HP Ultra BrightView Infinity Display (1920x1080p)

Lightscribe Blu-ray ROM with SuperMulti DVD+/-R/RW with Double Layer Support

Webcam and Fingerprint Reader

Intel Wireless-N Card with Bluetooth

SRS audio + Altec Lansing speakers


And then everything else from there is generic and should work fine with Ubuntu.

So my main concerns with this laptop are if the specs above are compatible with Ubuntu?

I've investigated the graphics, even though its an Nvidia, some people complain that is doesn't work correctly.

So does anybody else have this laptop and could they recommend it for Ubuntu?

**Edit:** Everything seems fine except Turbo-boost (Doesn't matter if it doesn't work just that I get the baseline of 2.4 GHZ on both cores without a performance loss) and the Finger-print reader. I wouldn't use the fingerprint reader anyway so I'm good.

But the SRS + Audio seems to be iffy still.
