### Post by machiner on 2005-01-24
Wanted to comment on your phrasing...forgive me, but...

"But what can take you aback is the extreme amount of configurations required."

Has negative connotations.

Psychologically speaking - you just scared away a big portion of your demo.

It's important (while still maintaing honesty in advertising) to not bring up anything negative in the initial contact with potential "customers" regarding a product.  People will read that line and get turned away - they will remember what they had heard about linux -- all of it - linux is for geeks
linux is terrifically hard to set up and configure (we know differently, don't we)

As well, Ubuntu is completely NOT hard to configure, not does it require such from the end-user. In fact, I would remove that phrasing and replace it with the following:

No longer is Linux hard to configure...or call it a NEW thing -- hey now - Linux is now easier than windows to install and configure.

NEVER use wording that would potentially scare away a (large or small) portion of your intended audiance.

peace
