### Post by Tuna-Fish on 2008-01-28
> **Kow said:**
>  Lets say you're entity X owned by Nokia. You need a QT license for whatever product it is you are releasing. You purchase a QT commercial license for say $20. You lose Nokia $20 but Nokia gains the $20 back from the QT entity. Whats the point?

The point is that nokia won't be the only entity making programs for nokia phones. Building a more 3rd-party developer friendly platform has been a bit of a holy grail for all the phone makers for a while now, because they want the same kind of network effects that made microsoft rich. that is, if they can manage to get enough software to run on their phones, that software alone is going to tip the scales and bring them more customers. Then, those more customers mean that more software is written for their phones. You get the idea.

Trolltech is a good idea for nokia because,
1. Writing software for symbian sucks. Writing software for Qtopia rocks. Nuff said. (maemo is still only a small part of what nokia would need, qtopia is a full platform.)
2. By owning qt, nokia can sell licenses to the closed-source software makers for their phones, and get another revenue stream that is not dependant on the carriers.
3. This means ocular penetration for motorola. And nokia knows the best time to kick them is when they are down.
