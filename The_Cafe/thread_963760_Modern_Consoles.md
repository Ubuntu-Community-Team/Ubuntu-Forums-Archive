> **aeiah said:**

Hahaha...If that's a freak, I'm almost afraid to say what kinda stuff I've got running around here!

I think the biggest reason they add these features is the same as is true with any product.  The more people you can attract to your product, the better.  All of those features, while some may be overkill or unnecessary to you or me, may be the only reason others bought it.  I can name at least 10 people I know who bought the PS3 as a Blu-ray player.  They thought it was cool it played games too, and has the potential the PS3 has, but the price of it compared to stand-alone Blu-ray players was worth it.  Same as the media center features they are both improving constantly - just an added feature that may snag a few more buyers.  Not to mention the media center side is fairly easy, since they are basically smaller versions of a PC.

I don't know that PC gaming will die out completely, and I doubt Microsoft would abandon the PC gaming side completely in favor of the Xbox, but the consoles do have their advantages.  How often can you buy a computer and 2-3 years later buy the latest and greatest game available and be sure it will run flawlessly?  On the other side, how many things can you do on a PC that would be nearly impossible via a console?  

They both have their pros and cons, but honestly the more they add to the consoles, the more selling power they have which means more money, so the big question shouldn't be why they are adding all these things, but why it took them so long...
