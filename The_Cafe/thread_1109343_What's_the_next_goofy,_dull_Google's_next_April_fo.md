They actually did quite a few, though, I'm not sure if all of them took place on April 1.

- **Google** **Adsense** **for** **Conversations**
- **Google** **Weblogs**
- **Dajare** (Japan): organize the world's laughter
- **gDay** (Australia): search web pages 24 hours before they were created
- **GMail Custom Time**: allows user to set the time they want the recipient to think it was sent.
- **Google Book Search Scratch n' Sniff**
- **Google Calendar Is Feeling Lucky**
- **Google Dialect Translation** (Korea): allows user to translate text among regional dialects of Korean to and from Standard Korean
- **Google Docs Paper Airplane**
- **Google Manpower Search** (China): powered by a team of 25 million volunteers who search through internet and paper documents whenever a keyword is entered, promising to give a result in about 32 seconds.
- **Google Summer of Code "WTF Public License"**
- **Google Talk Conservation**: promised to shorten Google Talk conversations by April 22nd in order to reduce the energy used to transmit chat conversations, therefore reducing Google's carbon output.
-** Google Wake Up Kit**
- **Virgle**:  joint project with Virgin Group to create a permanent human settlement on Mars
- **Yogurt**:  Google Orkut's name was changed to Yogurt for a day.
- All Featured Videos on the UK and Australian version of YouTube linked directly to Rick Astley's "Never Gonna to Give You Up"
