### Post by cascade9 on 2011-02-24
A detail you made a nasty mistakes with this....You have a i7 LGA 1155 CPU, but you've put a LGA1366 and LGA1156 motherboards onto your list. LGA1155 CPUs will only work with LGA1155 motherboards. 

 1200watts is far, far more than that setup would need. 

2x SSDs? Nice for 'pose' value, but its not going to help gaming that much (even in RAID 0) 

Unless you have a SATA DVD drive around, get one of them as well, no matter which way you go. (personally, I like the pioneer drives) 

If you do decide to get a prebuilt system, dont go to faclon northwest. Big name, overpriced. Not as badly overpriced as say alienware, but still overpriced. Check ibuypower 

[http://www.ibuypower.com/](http://www.ibuypower.com/)

I'd build one myself, but I actualy like building. Dont take this the wrong way, and laugh if you want...but can you build a lego kit and have it look like it does on the packet? If you can, building a computer should be do-able. If not, get somebody else to build it. 

I'd decide if you want LGA1156 (older, but cheap and still good for gaming), LGA1156 (new, still having 'teething problems'), LGA1366 (nice, but expensive, and wont really give you much more than LGA1155/1156 for gaming) or AMD AM3 (stable, mature, cheap).

In the end, for windows gaming its best to decide how much you have to spend, what games you play and then go looking for what fits best, rather than the 'linux' approach (get a stable, supported card with decent drivers with good price/performance). 

BTW, what games you play can make a huge difference.....some games love nvidia, some games love ATI/AMD.
