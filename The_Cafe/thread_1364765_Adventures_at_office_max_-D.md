I just got my new HP officejet 8500 pro Printer and it by the way come up flawlessly in Ubuntu and yes I love it, not just like it LOVE It :). However this would not even be note worthy to mention because the support for HP printers is well documented and well done (unlike my old lexmark I dumped on my brother in-law muuhahahahahahaha) but there is a bit of a twist to the story. I was at office max where I purchased the printer (it was on sale for $150.00 it is normally $300.00) the sales person asked me why I was not going to purchase the wireless version ($349.00 not on sale). I simply smiled and asked if he has a wireless network at home....he did....I said so do I. I mentioned to the young salesman that the printer is a network printer, he stopped me there and said but the wireless printer dose not require me to plug it into computer and leave it on to share the printer any where in my house. At this point I laughed, and mentioned again the printer was network and I had a wireless network in my home. He did not do the math again and said but its wireless (as if continuing down the path of trying to get me to pay almost $200.00 more for the exact same printer with wireless in it was going to work) so I had to break the bad news to my sales person, the printer is network, I will be plugging it in to my wireless router, therefore it would be a wireless network printer. The sales person at this point (still confused) tried to sell me the extended warranty. 

The rest speaks for its self (I wonder if he would have told me that it was not Linux compatible too if I told him I used that...now that would have been a fun conversation)
