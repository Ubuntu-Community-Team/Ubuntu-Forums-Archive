### Post by SonicSteve on 2010-04-25
In eager anticipation of 10.04 I decided to visit the Ubuntu.com website this morning. While there I checked the little countdown counter and looked around a bit. I then asked myself this question; 

If I were a new visitor does this site explain properly what Ubuntu is and who can use it? 

What I noticed is that it has  strong focus on Netbooks and Servers. However there was no proper mention of Laptop and Desktop usage. One thing I know from experience is that inexperienced users will not necessarily make the leap/connection in their minds. It's quite possible that some people who visit out of curiosity might visit and leave thinking "OH well I don't have a netbook, and I for sure don't have a server."

I'm sure some of you will think I'm making something out of nothing but I think it should be addressed. 

Since there are 3 main versions Laptop/Desktop, Netbook, and server and since there are 3 prongs to the Ubuntu Logo perhaps that could be a nice graphical splash page directing users to the version they need.
