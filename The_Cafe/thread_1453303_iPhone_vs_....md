Firstly.

Secondly, my N900 is better (for me) than my old iPhone 2G because:
 - Can sync with my Ubuntu PC either by USB or OTA with rsync
 - Can use xvid video files
 - Don't need to Jailbreak to get everything I want on it
 - 32GB is bigger than 8GB
 - Can insert a MicroSD card to get more storage (potentially 64GB total memory)
 - Can overclock it
 - Supports widgets on the desktop
 - Is based on Debian!
 - 3G
 - Bluetooth A2DP (something Apple left out of the original iPhone)
 - Native Skype support
 - Front facing camera
 - Multitasking like Apple's Expose
 - Application manager and extras-devel repository is great
 - Speakers are much better than the iPhone 2G
 - Video Camera
 - It takes exponentially better photos
 - There isn't a choice of 80 Fart applications
 - Emulators are great
 - Screen Resolution
 - It has flash (but flash is awful, so I try to use the html5 version of sites more)

It is worse than the iPhone because:
 - Hardware keyboard is cramped
 - Screen NEEDS a protector, whereas the iPhone had no scratches on the screen at all, the back was mullered though.
 - No major developers for apps or games
 - Ovi Store is the worst, only one good game developed for it.
 - Not quite as smooth as the iPhone

Why my N900 is better than Android:
 - It doesn't use Java
 - It is real Linux
 - My data isn't watched by google
 - Did I mention it doesn't use Java? :)

Why my N900 is better than most phones:
 - It doesn't support crapp MMS out of the box. I hate that technology, it's dated, expensive and pointless. Seriously, who DOESNT have an email address!?

### Post by maflynn on 2010-04-13
The iphone has a lot going for it.  I've had one for a couple of years now and basically I can say it just works.  Of course, I own a Mac, and I've bought into the whole apple eco-system.  I use Linux for work stuff, OSX for personal stuff.  So its a snap syncing my contacts, music, emails etc with the iPhone.

I recently tried a Nexus One phone but found it subpar compared to the iPhone.  The apps pale in comparison to the iPhone, and not just volume wise, but the clear majority of iPhone apps have a higher polish, quality and performance then the android apps. 

The screen was less accurate, but by too much, the keyboard was a bit tougher to type and the battery life was worse. I had to turn off 3G, wifi, gps and at times background processing just to get through the day.

Just my opinion but for me, the iPhone just works.
