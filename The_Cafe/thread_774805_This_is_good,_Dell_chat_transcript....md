### Post by notwen on 2008-04-30
When purchasing a N-Series model you are offered support packages from Canonical.  Dell only offers hardware support w/ Linux, if you need software support, you'd be buying it from Canonical(through Dell).  Check [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=762770") for more info on the Dell models that come pre-installed w/ Ubuntu.

Dell has no obligation to support Ubuntu on all of their makes/models.  I personally was very excited to partake in their pioneering the market for pre-installed Linux by a major manufacturer.

Were you personally interested in getting one of these machines or just investigating to see if Dell would give you support for installing a third-party OS on a machine purchased from Dell?  Despite numerous rants regarding Dell and there lack of Ubuntu support, I believe they took a pretty big risk in being the first of the major manufacturers to offer pre-installed Linux machines.  

I purchased my Inspiron 1420n right after they began offering them and have zero complaints.  If you're in the market for a pre-installed Ubuntu system, please consider Dell.  Should you need software support w/ this new system, be sure to purchase it from Canonical through Dell.  The more users support pre-installed Linux from the bigger companies, the more likely it will become a standard option for your future machines.  =]
