### Post by der_joachim on 2006-08-20
Depends on your definition of geek, I think. I am certainly not the scruffy, overweight parents' basement type, but I prefer gaming / tinkering with my linux box over watching TV. I have a number of other hobbys with varying degrees of geekism. And I am a programmer, which I started to do after failing my linguistics degree. 

I prefer a somewhat broader definition of the term 'geek'. For me, a geek is somebody with a certain a certain hobby, and a strong, almost obsessive urge to feed that desire and to share their knowledge and experience on that subject with others, even if those others do not really care for the subject. It can be any hobby, be it programming, role playing games, embroidery, motorbikes or some kind of sport. Geekism is also a kind of typecasting. I get different reactions from people when I wear one of my commemorative marathon shirt (Eindhoven, Netherlands 2001) than when I wear my C3PO/R2D2 shirt.

Judging by this definition of the term geek, I most certainly am a geek. My main hobby is running. I try to run 40-50 kilometers a week, which requires a certain amount of discipline on my part (I have to eat healthily, sleep regularly, skip the alcohol, et cetera). I also bore my girlfriend, my colleagues and my friends with running trivia and stories. So yes, I am a running geek. :)
