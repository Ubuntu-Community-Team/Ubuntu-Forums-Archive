### Post by Forrest Gumpp on 2008-03-06
Is the 'multi seat' capability of Linux of potential interest to your school?

I do not know the actual layout in which you school's computers are used, but the 'multi seating' capability of the OS may offer an opportunity to the school to economize on the number of computers it might otherwise have to purchase in the future.

It would seem your proposal to put Linux on older computers would be a good test bed/demonstration upon which the school board could base future decisions, without it having to outlay any money.

The prospect of making future savings in hardware purchasing might provide the 'justification', if any is needed, for the school's IT administrator to approve the 'trial' of Linux.

Expect opposition from unexpected quarters if it looks like you'll get your proposal taken seriously.
