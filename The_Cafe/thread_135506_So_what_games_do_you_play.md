### Post by Master Shake on 2006-02-27
[QUOTE=megamania]:-) When I received my brand-new Yars' Revenge cartridge, on my *first game* after 20 years I accidentally managed to find an easter egg (the programmer's name popped up and the game stopped)...

I'm pretty sure it will never happen again...[/QUOTE]

Its actually pretty easy to uncover...

1) You must hit the Qooutile with the Zorlon cannon while the Quotile is in his fireball form.  Wait until he launches it.
2) NOte the thin black line going down the middle of the screen.  If a certain part of the yar touches that line when the explosion ends, the game ends, and the programmer's initials show up.

The autor of Yar's Revenge, one Howard Scott Warshaw also programmed three other games for the Atari 2600

-- Raiders of the Lost Ark
-- ET
-- Saboteur (A-Team)

Saboteur was never released.

ET and Raiders however both have easter eggs in them.  In Raiders, you can uncover the yar and the  programmer's initials, and in ET, you can uncover the Yar, Indiana JOnes, and then the programmer's initials.

Off the top of my head, I don't remember how to do those, but do a search at the forums at [http://www.atariage.com](http://www.atariage.com) and you can find them.

Other trivia about Yar's Revenge..

1) It started out as a port of the arcade game "Star Castle".  The programmer deemed it impossible to port to the 2600, and so he altered what he had to the current form.

2) Yar is "RAY" backwards.  At the time Atari's head was "RAY Kassar".  He named it YAR as a little suck-up to Ray in the hopes that the game would be released.  It worked. :D
