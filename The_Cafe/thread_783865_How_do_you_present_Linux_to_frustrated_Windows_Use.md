### Post by inportb on 2008-05-06
I share your frustration. I want so much to help my friends out, but it can be difficult. I have a small circle of openminded friends who were willing to try Ubuntu; most enjoyed the experience. We tend not to actively advertise the OS, opting instead to passively show peers the power of the opensource platform. It's successful because those who take notice are usually more likely to accept new ideas.

Personally, I am committed to helping my peers debug their systems, even if it means staying up a bit later than usual. Instead of just telling them what commands to execute, I try to explain the process and get them to think for themselves... at the risk of looking like an a$$hole who would not just give a straightforward answer. =D
