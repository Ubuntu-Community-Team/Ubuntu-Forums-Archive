### Post by fak3r on 2007-10-11
> **Incense said:**
> I think this may be what you're looking for, but it isn't free.

[https://www.google.com/a/]("https://www.google.com/a/")

Actually Google Apps for Domains is free, I know because I have it handling mail for about 5 domains, though only about as many users. 

Google innovation. Powerful solutions. No cost.
[https://www.google.com/a/org/](https://www.google.com/a/org/)

I think there's a limit on how many mail accounts you can have, but it's still pretty slick.  I went this route last year when I got tired of trying to keep up with the latest spam attacks on my mailserver - was just taking up too much time, and I've been very happy with it.  You can create a default landing page for your domain (a start page), you get the email, cal, and documents (word, excel and powerpoint like apps).  You can share docs between you and another person (within the domain or not) to have them edit it and much more.  It's a nice setup, if you don't mind Google having so much of yr stuff.

The only option I recall was an app that tried to mimic Gmail that you could install to your own server was duroty: [http://duroty.blogspot.com/](http://duroty.blogspot.com/) but at the time I didn't want to get back into having my own mailserver.  

The easier option is Gmail-lite: [http://gmail-lite.sourceforge.net/wordpress/](http://gmail-lite.sourceforge.net/wordpress/) basically a front end you host, but it talks to Gmail to get all of it's data.  Would be great for me since I can't check Gmail from work (but can check things on my home server) but I couldn't get the latest one to work.  May try again now that you've jogged my memory!
