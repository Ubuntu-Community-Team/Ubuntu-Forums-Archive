### Post by Mazza558 on 2008-06-01
Reading a forum thread (different forum, in an off-topic section), I found a
post about someone wanting to dual boot Ubuntu to give it a try. Some people were positive about the change, others had different opinions. Here's a quote that stood out to me:

> It's a no win situation for Linux until it gets a killer app that is worth switching for. As long as Linux remains 'not as good as Windows' or even 'just as good', I don't see the point in making the effort.

someone replied with

> Right on about the killer app thing. Ubuntu has so much potential of becoming better than Windows though, it's getting there, but killer apps is what is needed most indeed.

another quote:

> It's fun to try out, but there's really no reason to switch unless you're a programmer.

Taking this into account, and ignoring the benefits of the linux system itself (e.g repos, security), what applications are available on linux only that you think are "killer"? What apps would really want you to switch?

I don't think there are actually that many. The most popular apps available on Linux are cross-platform (e.g firefox), and so I don't think Linux will ever have a popular app that would cause people to switch. People will probably switch if the apps they enjoy also work on Linux.
