### Post by Lucradia on 2011-11-13
Popcorn that is un-used for that day first goes through the process:

1. Sift through for any bad portions (Mold, etc) and throw that part out if any.

2. The good leftovers is then bagged up in a huge bag (which is easily carried)

3. The bag then sits while staff see if they desire to have the bag.

4. If no staff desires the bag, it is promptly thrown out.

I should know, a high schooler in one of my old classes came with a huge bag of popcorn for a friday movie we had in class.

### Post by whatthefunk on 2011-11-13
The production of ethanol requires that the corn kernel be ground into flour and then fermented.  Once the kernel has been "popped" and made into popcorn, you wouldnt be able to make flour so you would have to use a different production process.  Also, a popcorn kernel is cooked which could complicate the fermentation process.  

A much better solution would be to eat all your popcorn and not waste food.
