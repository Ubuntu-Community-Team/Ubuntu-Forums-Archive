### Post by _outlawed_ on 2011-01-14
Grounding only works for alternating/direct currents.

It will NOT protect your computer from static electric shocks that form by friction of materials rubbing on each other like rubbing your socks along the carpet floor then touching metal such as the metal screws or components inside your computer.

Most computers nowadays are able to withstand static electric shocks without damage.

### Post by ErikNJ on 2011-01-14
> **_outlawed_ said:**
> Grounding only works for alternating/direct currents.

It will NOT protect your computer from static electric shocks that form by friction of materials rubbing on each other like rubbing your socks along the carpet floor then touching metal such as the metal screws or components inside your computer.

Most computers nowadays are able to withstand static electric shocks without damage.

While you are correct in stating that grounding does not protect against damage to the computer, it's the root cause of the grounding issue that can.  The ground issue may be due to a short between the current running through the machine and the screws on the chassis.  In this case, a static discharge to the screws on the machine can affect the components within the laptop.
