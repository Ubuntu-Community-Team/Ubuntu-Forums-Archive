### Post by GrouchoMarx on 2008-06-10
I highly recommend the [Cowon D2]("http://www.anythingbutipod.com/archives/2007/04/cowon-iaudio-d2-review.php") (just got one recently). There is a 16 GB model but D2's also have an SD-card slot, effectively giving them unlimited memory capacity. It's really small (a little thicker than most flash audio-players), with 52 hours of audio battery life, and 10 hours of video life. It has a really high quality touch screen, although the screen itself is not particularly big. The audio quality is fantastic. The D2 is officially Linux compatible (as a USB mass storage device it's not hard to be Linux compatible :P), supports MTP file transfers, ogg, and most other audio file formats.

The only drawback is the firmware: it is certainly not as sophisticated as what you'd find on a ipod, and the user interface is not very intuitive. Luckily, there is not a whole lot to learn on the D2. You also have to be careful to sanitize your mp3 collection, because malformed audio files can corrupt the internal database, requiring you to wipe the memory and reload all your mp3s.

On the whole, it's really a fantastic little player.
