### Post by SergeiFranco on 2010-09-06
Hi,
I am not sure if this is the right forum to post this into, mods please move it to the right place if it is wrong.

I am calling all ubuntu, ubuntu derivatives and OSS users in general to spend a little time and mail a complaint to the [email]product.support@garmin.com[/email] (or better use this link: [e-mail garmin support]("http://www.garmin.com/garmin/cms/home/support/searchsupport?src_url=http%3A//iqc.garmin.com/eCustomer/KODSelfService/request.do?create=kb%3Agarmin%26forward=escalate") ) regarding their, what I call, negative support for Linux.

Here is the story:

I am the owner of two garmin products (the so-so Nuvi 250 and good unit Oregon 450). I wanted to register my newly purchased Oregon 450 as well as update firmware on it. Previously (some time ago) you could go to garmin site and download gcd files and place them in appropriate directory on sdcard to update the firmware. Garmin removed that mechanism in favour of webupdater that only works in Windows or OSX. 

I also tried to register my Oregon 450 and they require me connecting the unit to the PC to register it, for this to happen again I need either Windows or OSX. This is very stupid, as I cannot register the product and in garmin's eyes I am not a real customer.

When I e-mailed to garmin support regarding the issue they responded with this:

> 

Unfortunately what you are asking is not possible. You must have one of these operating systems to register as well as do updates and there is no work around. 



Please remember that it was not required to have Windows/OSX to register products and it was not required to have Windows/OSX to update the firmware (there was manual method of placing gcd files on the unit).

I plead, please contact Garmin support ([e-mail garmin support]("http://www.garmin.com/garmin/cms/home/support/searchsupport?src_url=http%3A//iqc.garmin.com/eCustomer/KODSelfService/request.do?create=kb%3Agarmin%26forward=escalate")) even if you are not garmin customer, because even though you are not, this issue probably affects your decision on purchases later. Please complain to them regarding their negative stance of linux support and (if you are not customer) say that this negative stance will actively affect your purchasing decision in future.

Please remember garmin is using Linux for their units, so they have Linux engineers, also they provide support for Mac OSX, which has similar mechanisms to Linux.

As far as I am aware all webupdater does is downloads said gcd file and places it into the directory on the unit (that is mounted as mass storage device). I have tried to run it under wine, and unfortunately it does not.

Your help is greatly appreciated.


Sergei.

### Post by SergeiFranco on 2010-09-12
Here is the final update:
After me and garmin had a e-mail war, I got this sorted:

They registered for me the unit (even though it was "impossible" to register without Windows or Mac, as they stated initially).
I have updated both of the units on linux. 
I used the oregon wiki to update my oregon and using the information, that you can in fact update the unit without Windows or Mac, as leverage I escalated the issue, until I got what I wanted.

I could not find the gcd file on the net for Nuvi so I asked them directly for the file, which they did send it to me.


So what is the moral of the story?

If you want to update and register your garmin product you can do so, you will have to be very persuasive and keep pushing garmin support until they cave in.

If you need gcd file you can ask garmin support to e-mail it to you, using me as a precedent (I will happily e-mail them supporting your cause).

What I don't understand is why they make it so difficult for us Linux users?

But for greater good keep bugging them for Linux support. Eventually they will consider cost of answering your e-mails/calls vs cost of supporting linux, in favour of supporting linux. So linux users don't have to jump through hoops and sift through the lies to get their GPS units updated and registered.
