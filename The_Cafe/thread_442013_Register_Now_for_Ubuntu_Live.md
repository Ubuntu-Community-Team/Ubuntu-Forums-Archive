Forum folks, here is an invitation to you from Canonical's marketing manager: (Updated: Tuesday, June 19)
[QUOTE=Gerry Carr][B]Ubuntu Live
July 22-24, 2007
Portland, Oregon
[http://www.ubuntulive.com](http://www.ubuntulive.com)[/B]


If you'd like to learn how to herd cats and influence people, how to
deploy Ubuntu in government, or why Asterisk loves Ubuntu, you can't
afford to miss Ubuntu Live 2007, July 22-24 in Portland, Oregon.
Co-sponsored by O'Reilly Media and Canonical Ltd., it will be the
largest gathering ever of the Ubuntu community.

Whether it's the humor expressed in the topics of so many of our
speakers or the lofty ideals inherent in the spirit of Ubuntu Ð "I am
what I am because of who we all are" Ð the appeal of this amazingly
versatile open source platform continues to grow exponentially. In
creating a venue for the exploration of Ubuntu in enterprise, education,
government and community, Ubuntu Live 2007 emphasizes the
interconnectedness of the work and the platform's accessibility for all
users.

FOUR-TRACK SCHEDULE OF SESSIONS SUNDAY AND MONDAY
With so much going on and so many great options, you'll be hard-pressed
to decide which sessions will do you the most good. To make your choices
easier, we've provided a comprehensive at-a-glance schedule, with
options for each track--Technical, Ubuntu in Action, Community, and
Education--clearly shown hour-by-hour. Find it all at:
[http://www.ubuntulive.com/ubuntu2007/schedule/](http://www.ubuntulive.com/ubuntu2007/schedule/)

CATS, RABBITS, CLONEZILLA AND OTHER UBUNTU MARVELS
Here are just a few of the thought-provoking sessions you can attend:

"How To Herd Cats and Influence People"
Jono Bacon, Ubuntu Community Manager, Canonical

"Astrisk Loves Ubuntu"
Federico Musto, Chief Cook and Bottlewasher, Dog Hunter Inc.

"Catch that Rabbit: Using Ubuntu To Control Patch Management and User
Interface for AMQP-based Virtual Appliances" Yvette Chanco, yentlsoup

"Clonezilla: It's Better Than Ghost"
Kristian Hermansen, Security Researcher/Developer, Cisco Systems, Inc.

"Myth TV: Live on Ubuntu"
Mario Limonciello, Iowa State University, and Andrew Oswald, Java
Architect, Chariot Solutions

"Why Do We Need a Ubuntu Women's Project"
Belinda Lopez, Senior Learning Architect

"Ubuntu Tools: Now and Next"
Steven Smith, Founder and CEO, FiveRuns

"A Sandbox for Every Application"
Gordon Hopper, software engineer, Motorola

"Linux Meets the Firmware"
Rolla Selbak, Intel Corp

"Thin Clients, PHAT Results"
Jon "maddog" Hall, Executive Director, Linux International

See the packed schedule of sessions and speakers at:
[http://www.ubuntulive.com/ubuntu2007/schedule/](http://www.ubuntulive.com/ubuntu2007/schedule/)

MASTER THE POWER OF UBUNTU IN TUESDAY TUTORIALS
Led by Ubuntu experts, three-hour tutorials (for which there is an
additional fee) will focus on collaborative learning and exploratory
participation. Tutorials will coincide with OSCON, the O'Reilly open
source conference taking place at the same time and venue, so you can
profit from even greater exposure to the open source community. See the
complete schedule of tutorials at:
[http://www.ubuntulive.com/pub/w/60/tutorials.html](http://www.ubuntulive.com/pub/w/60/tutorials.html)

EVENING NETWORKING EVENTS
Evening events include Birds of a Feather sessions (BoFs) on Sunday and
Monday. If you'd like to provide the spark for a BoF session, you can
submit a topic proposal at: [http://www.ubuntulive.com/pub/w/60/bof.html](http://www.ubuntulive.com/pub/w/60/bof.html)

There's also an eye-opening Exhibit Hall, showcasing open source proucts
and services, plus a lively "hallway track" supplying plenty of
opportunity to socialize and share sucessful stories and cautionary
tales from the trenches with colleagues and new friends.
So, are we gonna see you in Portland?
