### Post by Coelocanth on 2007-03-04
I don't have a subscription to any, but I've just recently started picking up some from the local bookstores or newsstands when I can find them. A couple I've thought were pretty good are Linux Magazine (a quite complimentary article about Ubuntu in the latest issue)  and Linux Pro Magazine (an article on Edubuntu in the latest issue). The latter includes a DVD of a different distro each issue. However, from my understanding, they usually only concentrate on the most popular distros.

### Post by ComplexNumber on 2007-03-04
> **aysiu said:**
> I don't subscribe, but I agree with ComplexNumber that *Linux Format* is a good choice.
maybe i should have explained why i favour linux format. linux format tend to cater for the beginner to intermediate user in _every_ topic that they present. the other 2 magazines that i mentioned tend to cater for the just below intermediate level  to advanced level, but they always seem to assume that the user has at least some experience in _every_ topic that they present.
although i have a reasonable level of experience with linux, that doesn't mean that i am going to have a reasonable knowledge of every topic that they present. for example, when linux format presents tutorials about inkscape, gimp, or blender or whatever, i want them to start from the absolute basics so that i can easily digest it. linux format does that, whereas the other 2 always assume that the user has had some degree of knowledge of each of those topics. therefore, i don't really learn anything from the other 2 magazines, and i find the way they present everything to be a bit coarse.

### Post by Lord Illidan on 2007-03-13

Linux Format is the best, imho. Tutorials are great, the staff have a friendly chatty writing style, and their dvds are jampacked with 2 or more distros and loads of software.
