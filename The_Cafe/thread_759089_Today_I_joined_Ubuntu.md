### Post by NE Key on 2008-04-18
When I moved to a country where you have to pay for Windows and my computer went "phut" I looked at linux and chose the simplest.

So I started with Linspire. Then I moved to Freespire.

I tried Ubuntu on the laptop but it failed; I could not get on-line without going on-line. The installation paradox. "Download these drivers and you will be on-line in seconds" Errrrrr !

Freespire was "insert disc, install, enter key" and I was on-line.

I have now moved on to Ubuntu - only because I found a "How To" in the forum that gave 20 or so steps which I followed and got me on line.
(Not totally perfect - had to improvise and if I had not been slightly knowledgeable from my previous time with *spire/linux I would have been lost.)

But I won through. Another Ubuntu  user.
