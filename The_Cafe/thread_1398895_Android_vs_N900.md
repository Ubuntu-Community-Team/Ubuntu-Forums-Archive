### Post by steev182 on 2010-02-05
One of my friends recently got an N900 on contract, and after a couple of times using it, I have (hate to some degree, because Nokia was so complacent before the iPhone/Android) to say that it is *the* most impressive smartphone on the market.

I put a couple of divx files on it, they played smoothly and well, logged into Skype, it gave me the option to call my contacts using skype or on the phone, I wanted to log onto msn, I couldn't...

But that's not where it's impressive, because just going to the app manager, the repository has a pidgin/libpurple contacts/conversations plugin and it let me log into nigh-on any IM protocol I wanted to. I could also get apps from the Ovi store (less impressive) and by adding repositories to install some others.

The multitasking is nearly as good as that of Palm's WebOS and definitely borrows from Apple's Expose and Compiz, but the Clutter interface is well worked and enjoyable to use and the touchscreen isn't nearly as infuriating as I was expecting although I would like it if you could use the directional arrows on the keyboard for navigation.

The firefox based browser is nice, and it's media player is the best of any Nokia I've used. The keypad takes some getting used to after being very used to a touchscreen, but it seems nice.

Anyway, now it is making me think hard about selling my Samsung Galaxy and finding the rest of the money to buy one, it's quite fast, which is surprising for a non snapdragon based device, and it actually looks like Nokia worked their butts off to make this as good as it is (even if it does still have the slight feel of a beta product...)

The only problem is it's £450 and a lot thicker than the Galaxy, but Maemo's media aspects are so far ahead of Android and probably the potential of the Galaxy, that I'm tempted to get it!

I both agree and strongly disagree with this post.

I too own an n900, and the placements of the connectors is annoying.  But it's not a deal-breaker; you just need to hold it with a slightly different grip than other slider phones.  It's a little awkward, but certainly still usable.

As for the bugs and crashes, I've never experienced those in over a year of using it as my full-time mobile.  Updates have always been smooth, and calling it "an expensive brick" seems like a gross over-statement.

Yes, the software has glitches, and it's not the greatest user experience.  I wish there was a decent on-screen keyboard, support for portrait mode, etc...  Maybe I've just learned to work around the major issues, lucked-out and never encountered them, or maybe your standards are just way higher than mine, but I'd rate Maemo as average.  C+ to B range.  Definitely not an A, but it works reasonably well and does what I'd expect a smartphone OS to do.

The hardware itself I'd rank a B or B+.  The connector placement and single-touch screen keep it out of the A range, but the keyboard is good, the camera is great for a phone, and the device has handled all of the abuse I've thrown at it over the last year of being banged around in pockets, used in sub-zero temperatures, dropped, and rained on.

Are there better phones out there?  Probably.  Are there worse phones?  Yes.  The n900 isn't some kind of miracle technology, and people shouldn't praise it as such.  But at the same time it works just fine if you're willing to put up with its quirks.
