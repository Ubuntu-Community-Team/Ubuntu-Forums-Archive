### Post by m4tic on 2010-02-17
wow, i'm still finding it hard to believe its possible. Intro first, i hate being in small spaces, the furniture in my living room is spread away from each other, and every release of mandriva and opensuse kde left me feeling mad and crazy, still to this day i don't know how to open other partitions with them. I thought that's how kde was until yesterday i burned Kubuntu 9.10. i have never liked kde before and did not expect anything different and thats what i got, kde, but somethings were implemented perfectly for people like me. since installing it i've been happy with everything about how it works and up to yesterday windows xp was still my number 1 os then ubuntu but now kubuntu is my favourite. i like how i can access other drives without passwords, installing a lot of my offline .debs is easy just as in ubuntu but faster. i like how it greets me with how i can install restricted formats and its detailed that even first timers get it. well linux mint is easy but kubuntu is even better, from my experience that is. in ubuntu and others i had to get automount partition programs but i haven't had the need in kubuntu. i've also noticed how my resolution has doubled now and how many icons can fit on my screen, its a good feeling for me since i get the impression of being in a big space. i will continue trying kde ubuntu out and maybe it will be my default in sometime
