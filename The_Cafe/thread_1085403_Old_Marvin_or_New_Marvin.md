### Post by MikeTheC on 2009-03-03
Except for simtaalo, none of you folks picked a distro or otherwise described what kind of OS Marvin would run.

I've been waiting to get some responses here before putting up anything else myself, so since that's happened, here are my thoughts:

I prefer Alan Rickman's Marvin. He's got a terrific voice for this kind of part, and I think he got the interpretation correct as well. Now, that being said, I agree that the movie wasn't as good as the TV series. On balance, how can you make a single movie from a series of multiple full-length novels and do it justice? Heck, even the TV series didn't do the novels justice, but at least there was more there, they could spread things out, and so forth.

Given that Douglas Adams was one of the strongest proponents of the Macintosh, I'm guessing he would probably have picked something *other* than Mac OS. He'd have probably picked Windows ME, if I had to guess. However, it's easy enough as it is to start a Windows vs. Linux flame war on this board, and as that's not what I was trying to achieve, I'm going to limit this to Linux and say Marvin runs Gentoo, but with his Genuine People Personality modules mis-compiled or at least badly configured.
