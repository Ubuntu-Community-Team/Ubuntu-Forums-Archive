### Post by -deadcats on 2006-07-17
GNOME desktop has gotten a lot of push from for-profit enterprise companies like Red Hat and Novell. Supposedly easier for SysAdmins, I dunno...

Coming from a KDesktop Environment, I miss some of the somewhat easier functionality of it. 

GNOME is different, but GNOME is changing. I'm presently downloading SLED 10 final on DVD, and I LIKE some of the changes SUSE has made to the GNOME look and feel. I don't like to feel that GNOME has been practically shoved down my throat by Novell with SUSE 10.1 (KDE used to be the top-choice on the installation screen, now GNOME is), because KDE has historically had the best support in SuSE. Now, with Novell's ownership, the powers-that-be are insisting on change. (Hence a lot of the shake ups lately between Novell and former SuSE heads?)

My $.02.
