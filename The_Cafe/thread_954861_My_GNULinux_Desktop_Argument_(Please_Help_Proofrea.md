### Post by david_lynch on 2008-10-21
You wrote: **_Linux was created by Linus Torvalds in 1991 and is based on the Unix-like operating system, MINIX._**

Unfortunately this is completely false. The design of linux was so radically different from minix that Andrew Tannenbaum, computer science professor and creator of minix, flamed Linus over his design approach, saying he "would flunk Linus" based on the dsign of linux. Minix was a microkernel, while linux is a monolithic kernel, a fundamental difference. So, linux doesn't contain even a single line of minix code, even though Linus was inspired in part by minix, and in the very beginning, linux did support the minix file system.

So where did linux come from? Linus referred to a book by Maurice J Bach, called "The Design of the Unix Operating System" while he was writing the initial Linux kernel. It's no surprise, since Unix has been taught in universities all over the world for some years, and such books are widely available.
