### Post by JDShu on 2011-06-24
There are some big differences. Game AI is usually pseudo-AI.

For example, game AI usually cheat. For example, in most strategy games, AI will get more resources or produce units faster because it just isn't smart enough to compete with a human. Often their learning capacity is limited, so it might just calculate the player's mix of troops and then create the counter units on the spot - no real AI there.

For another example, in The Sims, the characters do not really think for themselves very much. Most of the clues are taken from the data stored into the environment. Items you buy will modify the data of each square, and the Sim will use that data and its own stat data to figure out what to do next.
