### Post by Screwdriver0815 on 2009-05-08
my opinion: Kubuntu Jaunty rocks!

I started my Linux experience with Kubuntu Hardy as KDE 3.5 was something like windows. So the switch to Linux was easier for me.

But I got to a stage were it annoyed me too much, so I switched over to Gnome. 

But I always followed-up the development of KDE4 and with the release of KDE 4.2 I decided to give it another try. And I have to say: its really amazing!

KDE 4.2 is not slow, it is stable enough to work with it. And (this is the main thing) it looks really good! The applications are nice, they have most likely more features and a more professional appearance compared to the gnome apps. The desktop stuff and the options for configuration makes the whole system providing a really nice desktop experience for the user. 

some downsides: some apps are not installed by default. For example:

- no picture modification app like Gimp (has to be installed manually)
- no picture organisation app like F-spot (the for manual installation available app Digikam is much better than F-spot)
- no scan-app like XSANE (but skanlite which is available in the repos does the job as nice as XSANE)

the package installer KPackageKit crashes sometimes but if so you can use the terminal anyway. 

So for me Kubuntu 9.04 is worth a try. Its really nice and the integration of KDE into the system is much better compared to Fedora 10 (for example).

Maybe for the next release of a LTS I switch to Kubuntu on the Laptop too.

Edit: just a question: why do so many users use Firefox in KDE? Is Konqueror not good enough? I didn't have any problem with it, except the flash on youtube (no sound) but I got it fixed. And it renders the websites much nicer than Firefox (but I have to admit that I am not a friend of Firefox as it really annoys me with its bugs and stupid default settings)
