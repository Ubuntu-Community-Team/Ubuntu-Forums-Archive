### Post by Onyros on 2008-05-07
The album's pretty good, but I agree, nothing like NIN from the 90's. My favourite album is still a remix album: Further Down the Spiral. And that's even though my favourite song from that album may not be a NIN song, altogether. At least it's attributed to Aphex Twin (At the Heart of it all).

Even so, tagging NIN as pop music is a pretty limited qualification. Makes no sense whatsoever to label NIN the same way you'd label... let's say, in an extreme, Celine Dion.

And it also makes no sense in tagging NIN as supposedly limited music just because of their use of tones. In the realm of "industrial" music (I know NIN doesn't particularly fit, probably never did) which makes use of time signatures and noise (yep, noise) to create sound, music isn't just the use of tones. Plus, talking about NIN and then bringing Miles Davis to the equation... come on!

NIN's songs are as much about the production as the creativity behind them. They're dense, layered, absolutely not simple. Even though they may seem so, but that's just when you don't really bother to listen.

There's more to music than just "tones". If you for instance listen to some Eyvind Kang and then tell me that because of his limited use of tones he's a worse musician than he is, than I'll totally understand where you're coming from. But I'll never agree.
