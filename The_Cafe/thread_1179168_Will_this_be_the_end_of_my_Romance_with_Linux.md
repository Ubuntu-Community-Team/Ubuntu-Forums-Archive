### Post by HereZiz on 2009-06-06
> **anuj.pathania said:**
> Technology without ideology no matter how advance, will only doom the human race in long term.

Proprietary technology is not without ideology. The ideology is just different from FOSS'. It revolves around writing quality software for consumers and get paid for that. After all, we live in a monetary system. There is nothing wrong with wanting to get paid for time and knowledge invested.

Vast majority of software users do not care about FOSS ideology though. They use the computer and the software as a tool, to get certain job done, commercial or otherwise. The "freedoms" involving the ability to modify the code and distribute modified code is interesting only to some developers. Most still want to get paid for their time and knowledge which would be impossible within the "freedoms" of a FOSS.


> Why is that Linux which is the best out there, don't dominate the world outside university lab and **true** coder's home.


"True" developers is a vague term, and most certainly wrong in the given context. Most expect to get paid for their work. They won't get paid if they develop for Linux. The only devs that get paid for developing for Linux are the ones working for companies which benefit financially from Linux one way or the other. Novell, Canonical, Red Hat...
