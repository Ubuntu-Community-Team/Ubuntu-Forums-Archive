### Post by 50words on 2007-10-04
A good calendar, a place to collect the ideas, tasks, and other things that pop into your head, and good lists. Give David Allen's Getting Things Done (GTD) a try. It isn't perfect, but using the basic principles has been really beneficial to me.

On my computer, I can use pretty much anything to manage my lists of tasks, calendar, and contacts, but I prefer Outlook. Evolution is fine, but the interface is not quite as efficient, and it is missing some features from its task, calendar, and contact management that are very nice to have.

Sunbird is good, as well, but suffers from the same problems as Evolution.

I sync my Treo with Outlook and keep a little notebook with me to collect tasks as they pop into my head.

### Post by notwen on 2007-10-04
If you'd like to avoid a large email application like Outlook, Evolution, Thunderbird you may be interested in [Rainlendar]("http://www.rainlendar.net/cms/index.php").  It's a small calendar app that includes events and a todo list along w/ timer popups, etc.  Very useful if you're around your PC a lot during the day.  Let me know what you think. =]
