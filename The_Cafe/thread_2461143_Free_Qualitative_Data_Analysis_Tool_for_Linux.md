That's exactly how and why one of my former employers used Apache Hadoop on an IBM-built computer cluster, running Red Hat Enterprise Linux. That former employer is a news + media + publishing company. They spent millions on expensive IBM hardware and support contracts to find out which of their various online activities (ads, campaigns, various portals, social media, etc.) were appealing to different parts of the population (gender, age group, income class, etc.) and what needs to be changed to optimise said online offering for the target audience they want to attract, or if there was a change in an offering they'd want to know how much each age group or income class liked or disliked it, and they'd analyse social media interactions and reader feedback.

They hired a special team of data analysts just to deal with the mountains of data this thing collected.
