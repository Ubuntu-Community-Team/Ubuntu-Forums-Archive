### Post by vexorian on 2012-09-12
I remember hibernation, it at first seemed like such a great idea. You save RAM to disk, and shutdown the PC with a special option, so it can later be turned on again and it loads all the RAM from the disk!

In practice though it didn't work that well in ubuntu and we had tons of drama.

But lately, it just does not feel so useful anymore. It all began with OSes making boot so much faster than it was. Now with 25 seconds boot, the time you would save is not worth anymore. Worse, RAM became much larger and it turns out that loading 4GB from disk takes a lot of time...


And it is that suspend works so much better. It is much faster to wake up when you don't have to read 2GB of data out of your hard drive. And suspend has far less hardware support issues. Also, suspend barely uses more energy than hibernate due to ghost drain. 

The only use for hibernate is quite hypothetical - You are about to completely lose energy and you don't have any time to shut down. *Assuming* you had time to hibernate, then this is a good use for it. But shutting down does not take that long time. Just save your work!

I've been wondering if anyone else has this feeling, that hibernate was just a fad.
