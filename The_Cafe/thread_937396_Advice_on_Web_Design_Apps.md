It depends how far you want to go with HTML design/development, but when you really know the language your most of the time better and faster of using an non-WYSIWYG.

When I design the layout of an website I still use Dreamweaver (under windows) out of habit and because its got allot of small tools that speed up your coding, however I don't use the WYSIWYG editor. I tried allot of other editors under Linux but none really satisfied me, most of the time when I need to do some makeup coding under Linux, I use gedit or geany because they are light and fast.

Again, it all depends on how far you want to go, doesn't see the point of becoming a die hard (x)HTML/css coder when you just planning to make one (personal) website, though you should always know the basics of HTML, even if you are planning only to use a WYSIWYG editor. And if you are planning to make more than one website (even as an amateur) it's best to learn designing websites without a WYSIWYG editor. Never ever say you are a website designer when you only use WYSIWYG, because you are not.

See it like this: "It doesn't mean because you can play one song on a guitar that you can call yourself a guitar player. It doesn't mean because you know 20 numbers of PI that you are good at mathematics. It only means you can do a need trick that everyone can learn without knowing what they are doing."
