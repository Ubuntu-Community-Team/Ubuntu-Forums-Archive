### Post by mcduck on 2009-10-11
It's for those living in a country where software patents apply (mainly USA), to provide them with a *legal* way of acquiring those codecs.

The rest of us can freely use the codecs available from Ubuntu's Universe/Multiverse and Medibuntu repositories. Not that it's likely to get sued for using those unlicensed codecs even in the USA, at least on personal use. But OEM's and corporate sers & such will definitely need a way to get those codecs without any legal issues.
