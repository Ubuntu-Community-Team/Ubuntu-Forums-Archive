### Post by Capt. Mac on 2008-12-27
I have most compiz effects disabled, but my favorites are as follows:

Expo - lets you zoom out and see all your workspaces easily
Desktop Wall - just because it allows you drag windows from one workspace to another
Maximumize - resizes a window to take up all available space
Snapping Windows - I have edge attraction disabled (bugs the hell out of me), but I have a slight edge resistance (because my windows are usually at the side of my screen or edge-to-edge)
Application Switcher - This lets you alt-tab into different windows, showing previews of windows that are not minimized. Not sure if this function is available without Compiz, but if it is, it probably wouldn't have the window previews anyway (makes it quick & easy to distinguish between two instances of the same application).

Shelf is a nice feature, but I don't normally have it enabled. It allows you to scale-down a window you're not actively using, so that you can see the whole thing without taking as much screen space. I occasionally find it useful for 'always on top' applications.

I feel like your poll is missing... well... everything, really. My favorite Compiz-Fusion 'effects' are more about adding functionality and not about eye candy (Not that there's anything wrong with eye candy. It is always nice to be able to make your computer look good). I do have Window Previews enabled, but it's hardly useful enough for me to consider it a favorite. Animations are nice, but I prefer an instant response when manipulating my windows. And wobbly windows just make me feel sick to my stomach :-&
