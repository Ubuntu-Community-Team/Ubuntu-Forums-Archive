### Post by ve4cib on 2010-04-07
> I really can't think of a single reason to own a pad. What do people use them for, apart from looking like tools in cafes?

I can see a few potential uses for slates/pads.  The first one that comes to mind is bulk book transportation.  No, reading on an LCD isn't as nice as a real book, but if you need to take a metric ****-tonne of textbooks or other reference materials from point A to point B, and be able to actually read them (and maybe make annotations) something like that will be useful.  The only solution to the problem?  No.  But one possible solution, yes.

Secondly if you want a nice coffee table photo/web/video viewer a slate is a much better option than a laptop.  The supine "couch computing" is just awkward with a laptop.  But something small, flat, and light would do that very well.

I think the bottom line is that a slate is not the be-all and end-all of computing options.  It's another niche gizmo that people who own them (or want them) will find uses for.  Those who do not own/want them will get along just fine without them.


Anyway, back to the HP vs Apple tablet debate, I'm in the wait-and-see category.  I don't think HP will move as many units as Apple simply because Apple has the "buzz," a huge viral marketing campaign, and legions of devoted users who will buy anything that comes with fruit-shaped stickers.

Is HP's device the superior of the two?  Probably.  But Cowon makes better mp3 players than Apple, and yet the iPod sells exponentially more units every year.  Quality and features really doesn't equate to sales.

My biggest concern is how well Win7 will actually run on something with specs that low.  I've heard varying reports; some people saying it runs great on low-spec machines, other people who say that it's on-par with Vista on similar hardware.

Win7 is also not necessarily the best touch-oriented UI out there.  Despite iPad's lack of many standard OS features it is designed specifically to be touch-friendly.  Win7 is designed to be a laptop/desktop OS.  It'll be interesting to see what kind of tweaks HP implements to address those kinds of (potential) problems.
