### Post by toupeiro on 2008-01-06
I'm not so sure how much making a sticky -- or having moderators be harder on people are going to help matters any.  Attitude is a point of view and in all cases reaction is voluntary.  There is always going to be a time when someone is going to say something seen in a negative light, whether the poster deliberately intended to do so, or if the reader is being overly sensitive.  It is human nature for such things to happen.  The answer to this here is the same as it would be in real life.  learn how to conduct your self accordingly.  Don't expect anyone else to change the situation for you.  

As a suggestion: If someone is steering entirely off topic, or being overly abrasive, ignore the post and continue on the topic at hand.  There are so many things WE can do to assist in these problems, but many of us, admittedly myself at times, feel the need to comment on someones negative slander..  This hapens.  We can also choose to step back, recognize what it is and whats happening, and choose another way to respond.  All things considered, we can't expect such Utopian conditions in this diverse of a setting.  This community will succeed by learning tolerance and understanding, not by getting the last word in edgewise and asking moderators to close their fist tighter on the community.

### Post by Praadur on 2008-01-06
This is something I often worry about when posting on forums.

I haven't seen anything mean, but I have seen things which *might* be construed as mean as another poster pointed out, and likewise I've often posted things myself which I realise might be seen that way too, and that bothers me to a degree.

I've been told in the past that I can come over as a particularly cold individual, and that's not intentional.  I simply don't know how to convey emotion over the internet, and I don't think that the use of smileys really cuts it to express what I'm feeling at any given time.  So often my approach is a little analytical, cut and dry if you will.

It's because of that that I've come to realise that mean-spiritedness is often subjective, as I do try to be helpful and get into the spirit of things myself, going with the flow, enjoying given discussions or just forum silliness, what have you.  But I do worry that often I'll come over the wrong way and one of these days I'm going to end up with an infraction for saying something just a little bit the wrong way, being a tad too cold... or just accidentally riling someone with a different opinion, whichever.

That's how it's hard to know how to perfectly conduct myself in an online environment, what the best form of internet etiquette is and so on.

It's because of this that I'm always worried about what I'm going to say, or have said, because someone might jump at shadows and read me the wrong way, assuming without asking and so forth, it's happened before and the possibility of that is something that stays with me frequently.

So if I, for one, have come over as a bit... flat, I apologise, it's not intentional.  I certainly support a friendly forum atmosphere but I just hope that this'll never turn into one of those forums where being somewhat socially awkward is a crime.

Just my (long-winded, I admit) two pence on the matter...
