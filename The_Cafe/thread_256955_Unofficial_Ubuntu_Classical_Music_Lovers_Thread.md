### Post by henriquemaia on 2006-09-14
Almost all of my musical listening time is spent in classical music. It has been like this for more than 12 years now. I'm always trying to get deeper and deeper into the musical works.

I tend to listen the same composer for long periods of time, trying to assimilate and interiorize (as well as appreciate even further) its musical language.

I went through different stages in my discovery of this genre. In the begining, I heard a lot of symphonic music. As the years went by, I started discovering the joys of listening to chamber music. My most recent discovery was Schubert's chamber music. His 3 last quartets are very touching and profound.

X being a sound that, when muffled by the singer's head and diffused by the space between the singer and the audience, equals the sound the singer wants the audience to hear.
