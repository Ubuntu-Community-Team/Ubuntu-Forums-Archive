### Post by p_quarles on 2007-11-08
Ahem [puts on pedantic hat] -- actually, in poetry two words "rhyme" when their final syllables sound the same. So "orange" rhymes with "exchange," and and "silver" rhymes with "cantilever." 

Words in which the final two syllables sound alike are known as "feminine rhyme" (don't ask me why). A lot of nursery rhymes use this method, which is why it's familiar to so many people. Orange and silver don't have any feminine (i.e., two-syllable) rhyming words. [puts pedantic hat back in box)
