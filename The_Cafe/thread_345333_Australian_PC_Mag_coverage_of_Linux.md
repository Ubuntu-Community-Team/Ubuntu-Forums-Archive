### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-01-24
**Linux and Ubuntu in Oz Media** (A report done because I am both bored and can't concentrate on the work I am supposed to be doing;)  )

Both the dead tree and online version of Australian Personal Computer (APC) mag have had quite a lot of Linux and Ubuntu coverage over the past year.  This is a real break with history as it was very much a windows world for more than a decade (and it is published by a company with strong business links to MS and the web site carries quite a lot of Vista advertising)

They have just published a series of articles online about dual booting Linux and Vista (and XP).  See the [main article here]("http://www.apcstart.com/5162/the_definitive_dual_booting_guide_linux_vista_and_xp").

There has also been a veritable flame war going on about the advantages/not of Vista with duelling reviewers at 10 paces (a circulation booster, I'm sure). See [this article]("http://www.apcstart.com/5049/10_reasons_not_to_get_vista"), and [this one]("http://www.apcstart.com/5082/10_reasons_you_should_get_vista").

The journo dissing Vista and boosting Linux (Ashton Mills) has also had a series of articles in the hardcopy mag under the series title of "Living in World Without Windows" in which he compares Ubuntu with XP/Vista and gives a rating for a series of tasks or functions on ease of use, stability, etc.

I'm sure this is happening elsewhere, but I for one am pleased to see "mainstream media" taking a real and generally positive interest in Linux and Ubuntu in particular.
