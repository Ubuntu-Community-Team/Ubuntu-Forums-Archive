Which makes a difference to me as a user because?  IM integration might be the future, but until Empathy gets some *very* basic functionalities working first, I'm going to stick with Pidgin.

> 
Empathy had voice and video before Pidgin.

Given that they both now support voice and video, it's better to have voice and video "before" because?

> 
Empathy is probably smaller on disc than Pidgin (the backend for Empathy, Telepathy, is already included in Gnome anyway).

Empathy needs libpurple just like Pidgin does.  Telepathy is an advantage, but "smaller on disc" is a thing of the past with 500GB+ hard drives selling for less than $70 and 1TB+ drives going for less than $100.

> 
The Empathy developers are easier to get along with than the Pidgin developers.

It's totally my guess, but they're easier to get along because they're not in a dominant position like Pidgin is.  It's a universal phenomenon not unique to Pidgin.  Whoever is more popular also has more attitude.

> 
Empathy might be easier to use.

I find Pidgin infinitely easier to use, because Empathy is lacking a lot of *extremely basic* functionalities (that Ubuntu integrators consider as "advanced" or "hard core".

For example:

Merging contacts or "metacontacts" should be a basic functionality of a multi-protocol client - I have multiple friends with multiple accounts, and they should be grouped as a single contact.

Whoever thinks it's an "advanced" functionality must have had their heads buried in sand for the past 10 years, because they did not see this feature implemented in most multi-protocol clients.

Also, where's the Pidgin log import?  It can import contacts, but I consider my chat history at least equally important, if not more.  Losing chat history is like losing all read messages in your inbox when you switch mail clients.  It's totally unacceptable.

Where is file transfer?  Logging preferences?  Idle time settings?  User groups?  Blocked users?  Custom emoticons?

I can go on for quite a bit if I want to.  Fact is, Empathy, except for some promise for the future, is extremely lacking in terms of functionality.

Web sites like this: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/EmpathyVsPidginUsability](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/EmpathyVsPidginUsability) that attempts to compare "usability" without taking into account functionality is totally ridiculous - yeah it's EASY!!  Who cares if it's not working??

Not implementing features in the name of making it easy to use is a bad excuse, because Microsoft MSN chat client manages to give users more control behind the "Advanced..." button than Empathy yet everybody and his dog can easily learn it.

End of rant.

> **AICollector said:**
> Notification icon support; Pidgin has a flashing icon when it wants your attention in the system tray. It's also possible to bring up the window from the same icon. Empathy dosent.

In Ubuntu, this functionality is bundled in the indicator-applet, the little envelope icon next to the panel clock. When you have new messages, the icon changes and you can click on the applet to either bring up the main Empathy window or directly bring the conversation window from the person who has sent you the message.
