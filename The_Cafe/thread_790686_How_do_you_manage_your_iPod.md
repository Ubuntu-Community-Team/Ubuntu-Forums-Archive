### Post by Dapilot1 on 2008-06-04
> **Neurotripsick said:**
> Dual Boot with windows, so, iTunes on windows

To free your music from iTunes use doubleTwist. It will convert all your protected songs into mp3. It kinda sucks how you can't really control it, but it the only thing out there that can do it that I know of after qtFairUse 6 was stopped.
As for your album art, your can drag the art from the bottom left corner to folder outside iTunes and it will copy over. You might want to do this first even though I put it as the second because it won't bring the art.

The Path to Freedom: (In Order)
1. Copy all Cover Art to a folder.
2. Use doubleTwist to convert m4a's into mp3's.
3. Delete away m4a's and move the entire music folder hierarchy and cover art to linux.
4. Reconstruct with EasyTAG or what ever music program you use.

P.S. I use floola because Amarok and Banshee can't handle videos to my knowledge.
