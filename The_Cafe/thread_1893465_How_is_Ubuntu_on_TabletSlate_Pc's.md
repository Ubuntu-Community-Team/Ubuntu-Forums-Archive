Check out the ATOM N450 based ExoPC.   It had been $600 but is on sale now on Microsoft's website for just $400.

[http://www.microsoftstore.com/store/msstore/en_US/pd/productID.224518200?siteID=5bPSTcPGJTo-vpDpS0IS1Ndp4WrVKR99Ow]("http://www.microsoftstore.com/store/msstore/en_US/pd/productID.224518200?siteID=5bPSTcPGJTo-vpDpS0IS1Ndp4WrVKR99Ow")

Since its Atom based Ubuntu is easy to install.

The above website's ExoPC comes with Windows pre-installed, 64G of storage, HDMI port, micro-HDSC card slot, 2 usb ports.

It supports bluetooth and WiFi B/G/N

Its a nice size at 11" tablet.   

I have it and it works great.

There is also a good writeup on the Intel AppUP website about how to create a Multi-Boot environment for it:

[http://appdeveloper.intel.com/en-us/blog/2011/07/07/creating-multi-boot-exopc-tablet]("http://appdeveloper.intel.com/en-us/blog/2011/07/07/creating-multi-boot-exopc-tablet")

You can also get alot of info from the ExoPC website in the Community forum:

[http://exocommunity.com/forum/]("http://exocommunity.com/forum/")

The ExoPC is made by Pegatron which is a division of ASUS.
