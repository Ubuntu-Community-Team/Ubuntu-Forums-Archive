### Post by Brazilian Joe on 2009-03-06
I am a software developer/graphic artist, and I have a rather long experience with Windows, Linux and Mac OS.

Currently, I own a macBook 17' (the one before the unibody). And I am happy with it.

I have grown to hate Microsoft for its bugs, and love Linux for its freedom.
But since I got in touch with Linux, a few years ago, I noticed it lack the polish of a commercial system. I wanted to steer clear of buying supported version and kept with the free ones.

As I got older, either I got grumpy, or more pragmatic (I'm 33). I liked Linux's freedom, but got tired of its fiddlyness. First it was RPM hell. Then I found in Gentoo a renewed energy and the hope of the best performance, but as time passed the community got into a crisis of internal bickering, and the packages I needed to get my job done were basically unsupported. Finally, I started hearing about Ubuntu and finally gave it a go. it was much, much ahead in user-friendliness compared to the other at the time, and is improving since (other Linuxes have too I am sure).

But we have this cursed vendor lock-in, in that our company has an Exchange server, and that works with Outlook better than anything else. Having lots of files exchanged with clients, Ditching MS Office for OpenOffice was unthinkable too.
Besides, I was not the one to call the shots in the server side.
I actually like a few things of Outlook, and the Office 2007 works for me. I think it's actually much better than OpenOffice, which does not seem to innovate much interface-wise on each iteration. The OOO button icons look so... 98-ish. It could use some interface love. I couldn't propose such a drastic change.

The company I work for used primarily Windows, but XP was long in the tooth, Vista was/is a pig, and we needed upgrades. We chose the mac, since we had the possibility to try something new, a good, stable OS which looked better than Windows usability-wise. And it had Microsoft Office too! And Adobe suite, which we also needed.

I fell in love with mac OS initially. The interface was so much more polished than Windows, I really got along with its paradigm. Others liked it too. Although it was not such a love relationship with Office. Office For Mac is like, a kick in the groins. Especially if you have tried Office 2007 and like it. Entourage is utter crap, leagues behind Outlook. No ribbon interface for Mac. All programs are slower to respond. Of course, it was microsoft apps.

Still, it has its limitations. i tried macPorts, but never grokked it that well. 
I am loving using Mac OS as my primary OS, doing my graphic stuff in it.
My second OS is Kubuntu on a VM, because it's relatively hassle-free to use, and I do my website development (PHP/Apache/PostgreSQL) on it.
I ended up using Vista SP1 on Boot Camp, mainly to play games and test web sites on IE.

Mac OS is great, is fun to use, and has many of the commercial apps present in windows. The iLife Suite which comes with it is incredible for home made DVDs, managing images, that kind of stuff. That software suite is worth a lot of $$$. Apple Mail works very well for my email needs (a few GB using IMAP in several accounts).

Ubuntu Linux has many attributes of Mac OS, but not quite the compatibility with commercial apps I would like, despite Wine and stuff. Wine is not yet compatible with many Corporate needs, despite running Office 2007.

The Ba$tard is Windows, but if you throw a ton of memory and kickass hardware, it performs adequately. And it has GAMES. ;-) 

To get the best of the three worlds, your only option is a Mac. Except if you try to do a hackintosh, but that's fiddly.

I am happy and i recommend it, if you can shed the dough to buy one.

If you care a lot about corporate evil-ness, then you might want to skip Apple stuff though, as it is as much as  Ba$tard as Microsoft is (if not more). My excuse is, "at least they build good products".

YMMV.
