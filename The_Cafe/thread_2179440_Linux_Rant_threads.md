### Post by RichardET on 2013-10-08
Whenever I read these types of threads, and I admit, I find them  sometimes entertaining, I am generally awestruck by the level of  expectation many people seem to place on whichever GNU/Linux  distribution they have used to reformat their formally Windows based  equipment, completely disregarding issues surrounding patents or  copyrights, and somehow they never understand why their new open-source  OS which does not have the same level of licensing with respect to DRM,  failed to operate as seamlessly as their old Windows install did.  I  like to use SAS JMP;  Guess what?  The past 3 versions don't work with  Linux!  So what should I do?  Complain or run a 64 bit Windows install?   Is this a fault of Linux? No!  But it is why I use VMware.  So my  answer to all these types of threads, always remains the same - either  invest upfront with proper hardware & software, or be realistic  about what you can do and be happy!  When we fix our house, we generally  have many tools but when it is our OS, we expect one OS to do every  job.  You see my point?

Down the road, within 20 years or so, I  doubt Windows will be the main OS on the majority of systems;  most  likely it will be some type of light weight OS like Android, or perhaps a  Ubuntu derivative, or other Linux variety.  Already Windows is  crumbling, on 10/17, all Windows 8 people are promoted to 8.1; Amazing,  in less than 1 year, MS has already begun dumping version 8.  If MS were  smart, they would learn from Sun and open up the OS, because now even  Oracle is pushing Linux.  To me this is all very humorous, because I  recall in the 2002-2003 period when Sun publicly announced they would  never go the way of an Open Source Solaris!  Really?

I first installed Ubuntu back in 2006, and it wasn't until the last year or so that I had got to the point where A) I knew how to do things the "linux way" or B) I had learned to just get along without stuff that can be a hassle under linux (flash, stuff like that).
