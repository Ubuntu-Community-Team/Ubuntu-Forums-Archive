### Post by lyceum on 2007-05-24
I am currently working on a website to market FOSS and help new users get acclimated quickly with less effort. The idea is that they will go to a search engine, type what they want to do and a list of programs will pop up, in order based on popularity. When they click on one to check it out they will see information from the program’s webpage and/or Wikipedia. There will then be links to tutorials (with lots of pictures) on how to use the programs and how to install them. 

That said, here is my question: when you want a tutorial/walk through/how-to do you want all on one long page? Or, would a page for each segment of the walk through be better? Or, with lots of pictures, one for each picture?

For example, I created a how-to for logging into the IRC for the Ohio LoCo group, all on page, rather sloppy. I am now organizing it into sections, setting up GAIM/Pidgin to use the IRC (page 1), then signing in to Ubutnu Ohio (page 2). If people do not want to scroll down, I could just make a page for each picture. 

I really want this to look professional and be easy to use for new comers. Any and all thought are welcome.

Multi page and organized by section is definitely a lot better. I'm actually, at the moment, writing a book that will assist and enable users in their efforts to switch from Windows to Linux, so in that regard, it's similar to your project (there was a thread about it probably a few pages back now)

If there's anything I've learned, it's that users don't like to get overloaded. They want small paragraphs (even if there end up being more paragraphs at the end), and neatly organized sections. They like a table of contents, so that they can jump to a specific page should they need to. 

So it was a good idea to divide your howto into sections.

Good luck with the project!

edit: just saw the poll. I chose the last option. Makes the most sense, logically, especially if you combine it with the 3rd option; always start a new section on a new page.
