### Post by Paqman on 2013-01-12
> **ugm6hr said:**
> But Macbook Air's have Intel inside too (just minus the sticker).


They do, but the PC market in general has been in a pretty dire state lately. Sales of PCs have been getting a kicking while tablets and smartphones have been flying off the shelves. Macs however, have still been selling nicely. So Intel decided that if you can't beat 'em, join 'em, and created the "Ultrabook" brand to try and steal some of those Air sales.

It's not in Intel's long-term interests to let the desktop PC market get hammered from all sides, even if they do still make some money from one of the attackers. IMO Intel need to stop trying to flog a dead horse and produce some chips that actually do rival ARM, because their attempts so far have been pretty lame and clinging to their core desktop mega-chip market is going to hurt them in the long run. There will always be demand for powerful desktop CPUs and chipsets, but it's not going to be the majority of the computing market like it was.

### Post by Copper Bezel on 2013-01-12
Touchscreens for ultrabooks are actually a good thing. They're not absurdly expensive, and while the touch interaction itself is basically useless on a machine with a keyboard (not just because it's redundant, but because the angles are wrong and touch controls near the bottom of the screen are awkward,) they automatically mean better display quality than a basic LCD panel. Apple uses glass screens for a reason. If touch is the reason that'll get other manufacturers doing it, that's fine with me.

WiDi is a neat gimmick, but could drive the price upward. I understand why ultrabooks can't offer a huge VGA port, but I don't know what's wrong with micro-HDMI. Yet another new spec to keep up with and yet another source of battery drain to worry about.

The antivirus bit is weird. It does seem like a move to shovel a redundant product to consumers. MSE is fine.
