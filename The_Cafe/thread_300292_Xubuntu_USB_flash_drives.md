### Post by Old Pink on 2006-11-16
Search for the how to (not my own) on how to create your own distribution/flavour of Ubuntu, and apply it to Xubuntu, simply removing EVERYTHING unnecessary, until it's no bigger than your flash drive.

Then, install to the flash drive. Once you've got an install on the flash drive, you can simply clone it, over and over again. :) 

Or, you could try just install Xubuntu, cut it down on your hard drive, by using Synaptic to remove any unneeded packages and move the entire root directory onto a hard drive.

Obviously, there may be some problems you'll need to work around, the one that instantly springs to mind is GRUB. If it were to work on every computer, GRUB must be updated each time it boots, or somehow before MBR.

It's all trail and error, really.
