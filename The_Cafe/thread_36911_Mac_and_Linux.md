### Post by HungSquirrel on 2005-05-25
[QUOTE=sonny]Well now that we're ontrack, in these hardware topic, I must ask, what is the REAL difference between the hardware (in english, not in some alienish words), because as I understand (just a little) there's some hardware compatibilite; once a read something about audigy and mac, I can't recall now.

 The reason Mac programs won't work on PCs is because the processors understand different instructions.  Just as if you were to say "Saludos desde Veracruz" to us, most of us would not understand what you were saying.  However, if you were to say "Greetings from Veracruz", most of us would understand you perfectly.  The concept is similar between Mac and PC: Macs only understand instructions given in a certain way, and PCs only understand its own instructions.  Hardware is not interchangeable between the two because each piece of hardware only knows how to send Mac or PC instructions to the processor.

### Post by MuckSavage on 2005-05-26
You can upgrade/swap out quite a few components of a mac with off-the-shelf "pc" parts. All Macs use ATA/SATA drives, nearly any optical drive, and any memory. And things like pci cards, video cards, just need mac specific drivers. I can go into any compusa type place and buy most hardware, and not think twice.

Obviously, you can't switch out motherboards, NIC's and so forth, and things that don't have mac drivers. But I've found that most things will work. Plug and play at it's finest.

### Post by matwyn on 2005-05-26
[QUOTE=wbeck85]I think that there are other computers out there that run on the powerpc archetecture. But i dont't know of any off the bat. but I THINK there must be....[/QUOTE]
 A lot of IBM's HPC (High Performance Computing) servers run the Power 4 (soon 5) range of processors, which are equivalent to Apple's G5 - traditionally that's why most Linux distros had a PPC version. Using them on a Mac is a side benefit.

Personally I prefer Ubuntu for PPC (I think it must be the calming colours!) but for the most compatible (hardware wise) distro have a look at Yellow Dog ([http://www.yellowdoglinux.com)](http://www.yellowdoglinux.com)).  They only do a PPC version (it's a Fedora based distro) and are the only company licensed by Apple to install and sell Linux on a Mac.
