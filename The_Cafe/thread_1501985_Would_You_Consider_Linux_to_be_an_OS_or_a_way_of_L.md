### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-06-05
Well, Linux, even for people with very distinct computing needs, is all they need. You know, other than gamers. Not perfect for everyone, but it's certainly not in the hands of so many people as it is perfect for.

That is to say, it is a very good OS, and given the software availability that Windows enjoys, I would have a hard time calling it anything but the best. Best is subjective to your needs from a computer, however.

Linux works perfectly for everything I need, and for that I'm grateful, since I've dealt with the other OSes and I don't like them very much.

When it comes to the ideology, I must admit that freedom of information was big for me long before I considered that kind of transparency in regards to software. I just accepted it since it didn't seem that important. But the more I used computers for work, the more I realized it was important that I have control over that part of my life. I didn't want to build a business on something unreliable or subject to vanishing or suddenly losing support. I also recognized that having a system that has endless amounts of bugs, crashes often, and is susceptible to viruses, or a system that is full of DRM and exclusivity didn't suit my digital interests.

With my music, movies, shows, documents, image formats, etc. I didn't want to build all of this up in a cage.

Also, my personal freedom is paramount, because it means that, even if I choose to use closed-source software, it was my choice. Not someone forcing me to choose between success by sending them money or failure by investing it elsewhere.

And, well, the community makes things seem much more cheerful and optimistic here, anyway. Windows and OS X feel so cold, despite their efforts. Even if I had a single reason why using Windows or OS X would make things easier, I wouldn't be able to settle for anything less than a dual-boot.

  Even thought Ubuntu is just an OS and Linux is Just a kernel, what has come out of it is so much more and I am proud to be a part of it as I boot up my systems in the morning and smile when I hear that sweet music flowing from my speaker to welcome me home. ):P
