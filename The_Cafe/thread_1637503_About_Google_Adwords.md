### Post by czr114 on 2010-12-04
> **Doctor Mike said:**
> Not true, Facebook requires an account that is not restricted/prohibited in terms of personal information, Google allows privacy control for its advertisers...

Google and Facebook are both atrocious violators of privacy, even though the specific methods of collection differ from service to service based on the scope of each.

How many people even understand what Google is collecting, how it retains their information, and how that is then cross-linked for maximum intrusion, or how [Orwellian clowns like Eric Schmidt view privacy to be an antiquated notion](http://www.theregister.co.uk/2009/12/07/schmidt_on_privacy/)?

In many ways, a Google account, combined with the ubiquitous presence of AdWords, Google Analytics, and other services makes it much more pervasive than Facebook, whose collection is limited only to Facebook-related activities.

The privacy paradigm present in the commercial, ad-laden, social web is simple: users have none; users are a product to be mined and profiled.

Other large ad networks also have no problem defaulting to eternal cookie tracking for every user every time he visits any infected page or performs any action with an advertisement object, all without informed consent.

The only difference with Facebook is the level of cooperation required.
