### Post by lyceum on 2009-02-01
My wife has an old PPC that I have been trying to get her to put any Linux based OS on for a while now. She finally gave in and decided if she was going to put a distro on it she had to have Ubuntu. So I told her she would have to install it herself. (Puting Ubuntu on a PPC is a pain.) She said that was fine thinking it would be easy, having seen me install Ubuntu on many a machine. To make a long story short, 5 hours later she finally has it up and has even registered here on the forums (I helped her with basic stuff but told her she would have to come here for the hard stuff so she would know where to go if I was not home). She wanted to use my ID, but I told her no. Now, after all that work she is an Ubuntu user. I told her to give up 3 hours in, she refused and kept at it. I am very proud of her. I really think it is the work and the feeling of satisfaction when you get it right that draws you in and keeps you here. That is (IMHO) the joy of learning Linux.
