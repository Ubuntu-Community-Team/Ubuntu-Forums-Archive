### Post by The Real Dave on 2009-07-14
Nice :D 

I get alot of old unwanted PC's, but usually, their Pentium III's or slow Celerons, sometimes I'll get a 4 or 5 year old PC though. People tend to give me their old PC's as opposed to paying to get them dumped. I rip out the parts that I want, keep a few of the better ones and then, every couple of weeks take whats left to my local recycling centre to get rid of em for free :D 

This is my most recent one, from a friend who bought a new laptop, and didnt want the old desktop. Its a 2.4Ghz Celeron, with 512Mb DDR2 RAM, and a 40GB IDE HDD, a CD RW and DVD ROM drive. Replaced the 1.7Ghz Pentium 4 as my bedroom PC (the PIV is now my file server, replacing a 451Mhz Pentium III :lolflag:) 

The best way to learn about how to upgrade, repair or maintain computers is to get a few old ones to mess around with :D 

> P4 is plenty fast for most things.
Thats true, I'm currently running on a 3Ghz PIV and it works pretty well, though I do max it out with VMing and disc burning. One thing to remember about a PIV is that their a hot CPU, so install lm-sensors, (sudo apt-get lm-sensors , then run sensors-detect) and monitor your core temp. Keep your computer and heatsink clean to maximise cooling. My CPU used run @ 50C idle, spiking up to 65C, now its running at a much nicer 35C idle and even after an hour maxed out. My CPU fan (which used run constantly at 5500rpm) is now around 1000rpm idle, and 1700rpm under load, thanks to a good cleaning and a new fan. Canned air is a godsend :D :lolflag:

Oh, happy birthday BTW :guitar:
