### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-10-23
Tell me if/why this idea would sound retarded to you.

So, Microsoft's Halloween Documents would imply that they see Linux as a threat, and they don't believe FUD works.

Now, since Microsoft has a really tenacious 'copy the good ideas' itch (which they should not be blamed for, if you lack innovation you can at least catch up with it), I think they could've done something awesome with 7 (or even Vista) to become a really firm competitor in the market, which according to everything I can see in the public eye where I live, they aren't now and no one's all that excited about "another Windows!? ALREADY!? Jeez."

So, here's my plan. We need to get rid of those Linux freaks somehow, and get on a level playing field with Mac OS X.

Well, rather than creating a DOS-based Vista with a compatibility layer for XP applications (instead of just building on XP), why not just throw it out of the Windows? Get a kernel that's based on Unix, add a driver database with the collaboration of vendors you already have on a tight string, and add and XP compatibility layer to the system so you can use the old Win32 APIs even for new programs. Re-implement the APIs for the user-space programs.

Optimize the Hail out of the interface, since you're so into wrapping the interface and kernel around each other, kinda' like Haiku. This could result in a very fast and stable system, to rival the speed of Windows XP, and if they worked really hard, it could actually be faster than XP (that would be a feat!).

Then you'd have the benefits of UNIX on your side, along with the DOS-freaks who can't live without it. You'd have the same disapproval accompanied with Vista, but if you implemented these ideas well, you would eventually gain market share and perhaps even oust Apple from its position in the marketplace.

Does this sound stupid? Retarded? I know it may have taken a while longer, but as we can see right now, people have been fine with XP for almost a decade anyway. It would've been better to please customers, I think.

However, I am aware that hardware vendors would've been angry that they couldn't charge more for more powerful hardware, and they aren't planning to back off with this stuff even with 7's lowered requirements.

I'm also aware that our economy works with planned inefficiency for lower-cost products, etc. Tell me what you think. I know we're not all really Microsoft fans, here, but I'd love to hear your ideas for what you would do if you were Microsoft.
