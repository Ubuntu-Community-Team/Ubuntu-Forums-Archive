### Post by Jon Monreal on 2010-04-18
Sometimes I get such a sense of "unwashed masses vs us" here in these forums. While both Facebook and Twitter have a bunch of crap such as advertising and useless groups and updates, they can both serve a purpose even if you aren't so interested in some of the kinds of interaction they offer. For developers, they can offer you a chance to interact with your users and get feedback in a more relaxed setting than a bug tracker, which might overwhelm an inexperienced user.

Twitter especially seems to be a great platform for businesses and organizations interacting with users. A 140 character limit means you aren't overwhelmed with giant feature requests and the like, but instead short honest statements.
