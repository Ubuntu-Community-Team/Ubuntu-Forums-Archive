### Post by McRat on 2010-06-19
The best place for tested good used computers USED to be IBM, but it seems that they off-loaded to TigerDirect.com.  This way, if you buy a busted one, you can get your money back.  Some computers are "retired" because they are broken, so never pay more than 50% market for anything at an auction.

TigerDirect has systems (no monitor) starting at about $109 each.

For a AMD64:  [http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/SearchTools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=6078305&CatId=5139](http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/SearchTools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=6078305&CatId=5139) is $139 each.

They often have huge numbers of these, so you can have matching boxes.  The IBM lease systems are very well designed and last forever.

### Post by McRat on 2010-06-19
One thing to be aware of with auction computers.  I've went to two live auctions with pallets of computers in them.  In both cases, all the computers were missing their HDD's.  Some were missing other components.  It's faster and cheaper to remove and destroy HDD's than it is to erase them.

But one was DoD (Army) and the other was McDonnell Douglas.  Schools might leave them in.
