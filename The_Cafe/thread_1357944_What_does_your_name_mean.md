### Post by Fire_Chief on 2009-12-18
Nickname from a major blunder at my old job. I used to work with a small IT company and we were moving in to an old Sunguard facility for our new offices. We had to remove 2+ dump truck loads of old CAT3 network cable and such that was zip-tied up in the ceilings (drop ceiling style). The space was about 40,000 sq ft and the process of moving a ladder every 3 feet to remove another tile and snip a few ties  quickly got old. At one tile, I found a large pipe (8") that fed the building fire sprinkler system. It was certainly big enough to hold my weight and made it 10x faster for running down a stretch of cabling snipping the zip-ties and yanking out yards of cable. This went on for about 2 weeks without a single hitch. One night we were clearing another part of the building. I had become progressively more and more confident in the strength of the sprinkler lines to hold my weight (with my help). This fateful night I ventured across a 1" line feeling it bow while I held myself up by the roof trusses. I stepped on a T-junction, heard a nice *crack*, followed by a constant hissing....  It took all of a second to realize what I'd done. 20 minutes later after someone finally heard my calls for help to shutdown the water mains for the building, I got out of the ceiling. Called a 24 hour commercial plumber to come fix it (blah blah fire code blah blah). My co-workers got some beer and retold their blunders of days past. From that night on I was dubbed Fire Chief. If you read this far...I would love to give you those few minutes back but alas I'm fresh out. :)
