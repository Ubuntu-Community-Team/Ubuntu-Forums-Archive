Hello Ace!
First of all thank you for the feedback and for using your time to post.
I must say for starters, that you got your concept of what uppy is all about wrong. The whole point of the service is not to compete with dropbox or Ubuntu cloud service.

While people can still safely store all the images they want in our storage, it's not it's main goal.
Uppy is a image hosting service, without the bloat. There is no registration and no cluttered pages, making us the ideal solution for sharing images on forums, blogs, social networks, online auctions, emails and other websites.
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=arial]
Our main role "online" is for people to have a website which can almost instantly give you an adress so you can send or share with anybody you want. This service already exists, there are serveral websites such as imageshack, the main difference between us and imageshack are the [/FONT][/COLOR]excessive [COLOR=#000000][FONT=arial]advertisement they have, and our extreme simplicity.
[/FONT][/COLOR]To conclude my reasoning, we do not want to be a dropbox service(despite the fact all your images are completely safe), we want to be a bridge for you to cross instead of making a turn when it comes to sharing.
When it comes to trust, i think that lack of trust happens to every business thats starting, at least until you gain some certain fame and credibility.
