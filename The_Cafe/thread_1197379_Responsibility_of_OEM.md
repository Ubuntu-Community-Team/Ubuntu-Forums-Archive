### Post by Jestersage on 2009-06-26
Recently, I found out that a certain shop apparently choose not to OEM install DVD codecs due to the entire DVD-copyright fiasco (Though since it's Canada, they MAY change it due to high demand). As a worker there, they gave me my choice as to whether to install the codec or not, and in the end I choose not to.

Still, considering that one of the most common inquiries from the users is issues with playing DVD (there's none, and most have NO internet), I have to ask myself: am I doing the right thing or wrong thing by choosing not to do an OEM install of the codec? While Canonical choose not to support those in medibuntu officially, does this imply an OEM should not either?

I guess the reasoning I choose not to install the DVD codec is more due to concern for the shop (Considering how the copyright lobbies in Canada is gunning "Harper the Furher" to make it worse than DMCA), but as of now there are no limitation in Canada. Still, especially for a laptop, if they bring it from Canada to United States, woudl the user or even the OEM be in trouble?

So my questions is thus:
a) How much should OEM do to its machines? Should it achieve all known functionalities, or should they only install/configure features if it is "legal"? (I am not only asking in terms of Canada, but also of United States, considering Canada is one border away)
b) if some kind of deal similar to the Microsoft-Novell is reached between a Linux distribution and those that hold such DRM, such that it allows them to legally install mp3 and DVD codecs (and anything I may have forgot), woudl you still use such distribution?
