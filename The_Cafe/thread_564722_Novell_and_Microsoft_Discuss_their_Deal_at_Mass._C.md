I came across this recently and I think it's important people hear it (you may or may not want to read Groklaw). Many people in the community got upset at Novell for making the deal, here they talk in defence of it. I won't try to summarize, it's about 1:30 long. Even so I think it covers an important topic and should be listened to and then discussed.

As for my comments on it, I have to quote PJ's article because I believe she pointed out one of the most telling statements. 

> Novell is getting money, they claim, market share, although you will note Andy Updegrove challenges that story, pointing out that sales levelled off after the initial spike. But the main takeaway, as they might put it, for me is that this is an anti-Red Hat deal, and Novell is thrilled about that. **Justin Steinman reveals that to market their SUSE Linux Enterprise Server against Red Hat they ask, "Do you want the Linux that works with Windows? Or the one that doesn't?"** It's just appalling. Let me ask you developers who are kernel guys a question: When you contributed code to the kernel, was it your intent that it be used against Red Hat? How about the rest of you developers? Is that all right with you, that your code is being marketed by Novell like that? I also have questions about antitrust issues, with Microsoft being Novell's partner in such deals and sales pitches. Nothing speaks louder about Microsoft's true determination never to be actually interoperable than this conference.

Anyway, make what you will of it and discuss. I'm certainly not any happier with them after listening to it all. I think I got a clear read on where their priorities lie (as opposed to reading all the press releases and blogs) and they don't jive with my view on things.
