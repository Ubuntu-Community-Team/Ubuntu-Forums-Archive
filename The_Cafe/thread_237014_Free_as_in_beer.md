### Post by kebes on 2006-08-15
Like aysiu said, the point is to differentiate between freedom (doesn't cost money) and Freedom (provides liberty, choice, etc.).

So sometimes people say "it is free as in 'free beer'" which means that it doesn't cost money (although it may or may not provide you with Freedom). Other times people will say "it is free as in 'free speech'" which means that it provides ideological freedom (although it may or may not cost money).

Linux (and Ubuntu) is both 'free' and 'Free': it costs no money, and it provides you with all the choice and liberty you need. Some people care more about the 'free beer' part (don't want to pay alot of money for software) and some people care more about the 'Free speech' part (they want to be able to analyze and modify the software they use). Most of us like both aspects!
