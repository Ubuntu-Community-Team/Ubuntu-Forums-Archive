### Post by Nyromith on 2011-06-10
I didn't use the beta, but I installed 11.04 the day it released as final. Despite the crazy (and even evil) experiment to turn computers into toys (Unity as a tablet interface), the developers have done a great job. I installed Xubuntu (although I have Core i7 and 6GB RAM), and it was super fast and bug-free.

Now the desktop has much better hardware acceleration - the windows resize and move much faster, and I have an option to eliminate the  annoying tear problem I had before (through the Catalyst driver).

Plus, the XFCE guys added a nice applet that shows you a particular folder as a menu (like the main menu). I set two of them - one for '/' and one for the home directory, so now I can access any folder in my computer much faster. For me it's a prove that hierarchical menu is still the fastest and most logical way to find things in a GUI.

So please don't say that people who don't like Unity can't appreciate the hard work developers do.
