### Post by Mazza558 on 2009-01-17
I've devised a simple way of testing your Ubuntu PC for normal, real-world situations - simply attempt to do these tasks using a GUI, installing no additional programs during the test, and you can safely say that normal users (e.g casual users who just want to get something done) would have no trouble using your PC. The test takes no longer than about 20 minutes. Then rate your PC based on how many tasks were achievable. 

[SIZE="5"]**(If a task can be obviously done, you can ignore it and move to the next one)**[/SIZE]

- Find an image on the web and insert it into a word processor, into the middle of the page, with text below it.
- Resize and crop an image
- Watch a Youtube video with sound, full speed
- Listen to music whilst watching a youtube video
- Open 3 tabs with youtube videos and pause them all without your browser crashing
- On one youtube video, watch it full screen with no flickering or crashes
- Play a 3D game fullscreen with no flickering
- Watch a streaming, non-flash online video with sound, and seek (e.g drag the progress bar)
- Watch a DVD with no flickering, fullscreen
- Watch DVD extras with no flickering and no problems 
- Go to [Onemorelevel]("http://www.onemorelevel.com/random.php") and try one flash game, fully functioning, and then play another with the same conditions (just refresh that page and you'll get another)
- Scroll down a content-heavy page with little to no lag (like [this]("http://digg.com/linux_unix/Update_Girl_drops_out_of_college_because_of_Linux") page)
- Locate a file manually, and then see if you can find it by searching your PC (no terminal :P)

___________________

Why would you do all these things? Well, sometimes I think Ubuntu users get too caught-up in tweaking and not actually making sure their PC functions on a basic practical level.

My practical score, according to the above, is 8/13 - failing at the page scrolling (firefox crashed) and trying to seek a non-flash online video from gametrailers, DVD playback and 3D games having flickering. This is after upgrading to jaunty - on intrepid it was only 6/13 - failing on flash-based sites.
