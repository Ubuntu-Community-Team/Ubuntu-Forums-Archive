### Post by sharkinfested on 2008-06-18
I know many of you are too young to remember the oil crisis of the 1970s but those of us that were there have seen these types of stories before.  They are hoaxes.  
While it is possible to breakdown water into hydrogen and oxygen the amount of energy required is enormous &#8211; far greater than the potential energy that the hydrogen would yield.
In fact we already have such an engine that is being used every single day &#8211; we call it a nuclear powered submarine.  Some of the nuclear power in these state of the art vessels is used to turn turbines to create electricity that in turn power the submarine.  However MOST of the power from nuclear plant is used breakdown water into hydrogen and oxygen.  In this case it is the oxygen that is collected since fresh oxygen is needed for the crew to breathe.  Nuclear power allows these subs to stay submerged for extended periods.
Cars running on tap water is, and always will be, an urban myth &#8211; the bonds between oxygen and hydrogen are stronger than the potential energy of the resulting hydrogen.  It&#8217;s basic chemistry.

### Post by Seren on 2008-06-18
It is physically impossible to run a car on water only.

The following reaction give you an amount of energy E ( that can power your car). 

2H2 + O2 -> 2H2O (+E)

But first, you have to get dihydrogen from the water. The reverse equation is

2H2O -> 2H2 + O2 and it cost exactly E in energy to break the bonds between H and O.

So basically you are only able to get the same amount of eneryg that you had to invest first to break H20.


So if you see websites about water car, they are either :
- hoaxes
- a distortion of the truth : it is a car running or using water, plus another energy source. But it is not a car powered only by water.
