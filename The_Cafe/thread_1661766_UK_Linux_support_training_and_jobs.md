### Post by slackthumbz on 2011-01-07
The market is actually pretty good. Bear in mind that any organisation that needs to do large amounts data crunching; e.g banks, anywhere that does research, etc is pretty much guaranteed to be running linux/unix for their backend server infrastructure. Salaries are good too, often ranging from £35k - £50k. 

I'm a web dev at a charity funded research institute that runs primarily on linux. We have a 2,000 node server farm for cluster computing and pretty much all of our servers and many of our desktops run debian. Interestingly when I started here 3 years ago I didn't know a great deal about web development but they took me on because I have extensive experience with *nix, can write perl (which we use for our CGI) and I rock hard on the CLI :)

I got my 'qualification' as it were through sheer experience and having been running and administrating *nix systems since I was 14 but there are companies that specialise in training and certification.

I'd recommend looking at the Linux Professional Institute ([http://www.lpi.org/](http://www.lpi.org/)) because they offer non-vendor specific certification. I'd also recommend having a go at Linux From Scratch ([http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/](http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/)) as a learning exercise.
