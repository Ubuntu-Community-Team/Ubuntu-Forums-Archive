### Post by Arathorn on 2007-04-23
> **H.E. Pennypacker said:**
> I'd suggest you use OGG.  I am pretty sure it is lossless.
Ogg is a container format, but when people refer to Ogg they most often mean Ogg Vorbis, wich is lossy. Because of the slow development of Ogg Vorbis, codecs like Lame MP3 and AAC have caught up in quality, but Ogg Vorbis is off course still open source. But to return to the original topic: Don't encode from one lossy codec to another, so no MP3 to MP3 with higher bitrate, no MP3 to Ogg Vorbis etc, because it will degrade the quality of your audio.
