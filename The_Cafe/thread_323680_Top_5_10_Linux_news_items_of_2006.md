### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-12-22
I'm a journalist by trade*, so the turn of the year means an irrepressible urge to revisit newsworthy items of the last 12 months.

In that vein, I'd be curious to hear what suggestions you have as the top 5 (10?) Linux-related news items or events of 2006. I'm sure there will be lists posted by other sites elsewhere, offering their definitive list of newsworthy items, but I'm more interested in what actual front-line users -- and not pundits -- are saying.

If we collect enough, it might be fun to throw together a poll and see what comes out as the top five (10?) among forum membership.

Remember, this is Linux-related, not just Ubuntu-related. And let's try not to debate the topics or their newsworthiness, but rather keep a list of ideas and see how they compare.

For starters, I would suggest (and these are just my opinions) ...

[LIST]
[*]The Dapper Drake release, which signaled a maturation of Ubuntu;
[*]The release of Java into the open source arena;
[*]The Beryl fork, which to me meant more than the advent of XGL/AIGLX/Compiz, since it became a project driven by the community;
[*]The Novell-Microsoft juncture, which should be self-explanatory
[/LIST]
Feel free to suggest something. Don't forget to tell us why.

*Yes, that is why I occasionally cannot resist correcting the grammar in the titles of your threads. Call it a compulsion. :twisted:
