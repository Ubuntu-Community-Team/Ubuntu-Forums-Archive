### Post by TheFu on 2020-04-13
For that price, you can't get what you demand.

I&#8217;d build a desktop system for $500 that does all of it, then buy a cheap, used Dell laptop for $300-ish to use for remote access.  A $500 desktop reusing parts will be faster than a $2000 laptop. if you don't have used desktop parts, add $300 to the cost. The cheap Dell should have more than 8000 passmarks, but to get that battery life, you can't have discrete GPU needed for that gaming performance.

"full stack development" is just a marketing term for "i know javascript on Node", BTW.

Windows will eat up over 250G before you know it.  As a dev, you'll need lots of storage for different versions of code, different dev environments, VMs, containers, and backups. A 2TB desktop HDD is about $40.

13in versions cost $2000+ usually, but won't get close to the gaming specs.

Don't get hung up on cores.  Worry about passmark totals.   For $80, you can get a 12,000 passmark 6 core Ryzen CPU. Add a $100 MB and $80 for RAM, then a bunch of used stuff --- case, PSU, HDD, keyboard, monitor, mouse, GPU and you have a crazy fast desktop for $250-ish AND you know exactly what components are inside.  Plus you can do C/S programming w/ multiple systems since you'll have 2.

BTW, be certain to get intel GigE networking. Avoid Realtek or other brands. wifi alone, w/o wired too is a mistake for a developer.

Few people here will be current w/ the models of laptops on sale today.  14 months ago, i was up on models but with a much lower price point. The last 10 yrs, every laptop keyboard I&#8217;ve had died after about 18 months.  But the Dell laptop from 2010 is still working w/ terrible battery and a discrete GPU.  A few keys on my current laptop stopped working in the last month. it is  and asus.  Had acer, toshiba laptops before.  But my trusty ibm 101m keyboard from 1996 is still going strong.
