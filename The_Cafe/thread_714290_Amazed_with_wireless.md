### Post by forrestcupp on 2008-03-03
I just hooked up a Dynex wireless card with an Atheros chipset to my desktop.  I expected it to be somewhat of a headache to get working.  I knew Atheros chipsets were good for Linux, but I still thought I'd be doing some forum searching.

Well, I plugged it in my PCI slot, booted up Vista, put in the drivers CD and never could get it to work.  So I booted up Ubuntu, hoping it would be easier, and it just worked immediately.  As soon as I booted up, the wireless just worked.  I didn't even have to go into the RDM and enable it.

I'm truly amazed.

So now I'm downloading the Vista drivers from Ubuntu, if they have them.  I don't use Vista too much anymore.
