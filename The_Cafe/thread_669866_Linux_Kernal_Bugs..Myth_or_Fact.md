### Post by tbroderick on 2008-01-16
> **Black Mage said:**
> It was said that Linux Kernals have a large amount of bugs and errors, and they are increasing because Linus Torvalds is pushing more towards development and optimization of drivers rather  than spending time trying to fix old bugs.

[Here's what Andrew Morton had to say to InformationWeek on the subject:]("http://www.informationweek.com/news/showArticle.jhtml?articleID=202600515")

**InformationWeek**: Which is the goal these days in developing the kernel... rapid development of new features or minimizing possible bugs and defects?

**Morton**: We try to do both, of course. It's not a direct trade-off. If you apply patches faster, you can find bugs and fix things faster... There are many layers of back-up behind us. There are hundreds and thousands of testers who help us find new bugs. As the kernel goes into the leading-edge products, [Red Hat] Fedora and [Novell] OpenSuse, it goes out to many thousands of additional testers. It's a year old or more by the time it gets added to an enterprise version of Linux.
