### Post by AndyCooll on 2006-10-10
While it would indeed be great for Ubuntu to have such codecs installed by default, as has been repeated on these forums many many many times it isn't legally possible and it simply won't happen (see my signature on restricted formats). The mp3 format (for instance) is patented, it ain't a "free" codec and free distros cannot distribute it.

For those who don't have an internet connection and want a working "out-of-the-box" version of Linux there are alternative distros such as Linspire and Xandros.
