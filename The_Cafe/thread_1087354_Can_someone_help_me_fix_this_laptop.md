### Post by issih on 2009-03-05
Check if theres any output on the external vga/dvi out by plugging it into an external monitor. If the computer works on that screen then you have a working motherboard and a non functional (for some reason) screen, otherwise the chances are that the motherboard is shot.

As for fixing the screen you currently have (assuming the motherboard is functional), the chances are not good. If its just the backlight not working, it might be the inverter board, but chances are it is the actual light, and those are pretty much not replaceable. Realistically you are looking at a new lcd panel, there are no magic fixes I'm afraid.

Oh and bumping more than about once every 24 hours will get you shouted at...just so you are warned.

Hope that helps
