### Post by handy on 2009-12-22
I use Arch & the Openbox window manager.

Openbox, as is Ubuntu, is very happy to use the GTK-1 or 2 themes.

I personally use (meaning I copy it there too) the *Aero* theme on any distro I play with, as it is GTK-2. (I don't use the KDE desktop)

I have modified Aero somewhat so as to suit my taste (eyes).

Aero is a darkish theme, which is the way I like it, I just had to tweak it somewhat to suit my personal *sensitivities*... :lolflag:

You won't have any trouble finding Aero if you search for it.

I highly recommend Aero to anyone who prefers a screen with less brightness.

I am however totally peeved with how much stuffing around it takes to modify a GTK theme though.

One day it won't be so bl00dy time consuming to modify the *gtrc* file as it is now.

As it is now... It is an absolute total pain in the RRRs.

SO, what I've learned from that, is get your theme just the way you want it, then back the baaastard up, so you never have to waste so much bl000dy time again.

Some things just should NEVER have to be done twice!
