### Post by handy on 2011-03-16
I have a Yamaha RS 40 set, which is entry level pro. It has a huge range of sounds & 32 inbuilt kits. It has memory for more that you can put together yourself.

One of the things that I noticed from using it, is that it has helped my brain learn to multi-task a bit better. I can now read when people are talking around me, where previously I could not.

Very interesting I find.

I don't have lessons, I have very strong natural rhythm & have owned hand drums for years. So I just worked at integrating first my two arms, then I bought in the base drum, then the hi-hat.

There are courses available for free on the web if you have a search.

Many pro drummers use only electronic kits in their performances. They love them, so easy to transport, set up & pack away for transport again. Oh, & no need for microphones & all the hassles that EQing them can bring.

Apart from the whole new world of sounds & effects that are at the drummers disposal & not only in the hands of the "sound engineer", which is a name that many consider to be an oxymoron. :lolflag: They are so often half deaf & have bigger musicians egos than any of the band members! (Speaking from experience here as both myself in the past & my wife who still has to deal with these oxymorons can attest.)

Compared to all of the hassles of a full skin kit, digital drummers now just laugh at those problems.

The sampled sounds are capable of being so good. The only problem I've found is that you can't use brushes, so if you need to do that you need to buy a snare drum & very likely a genuine hi-hat, depending on what you want/need to do.

Many never desire to use brushes as they just aren't part of the styles that they play.
