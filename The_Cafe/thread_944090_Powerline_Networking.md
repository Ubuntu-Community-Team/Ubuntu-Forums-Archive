Your neighbor and your networks won't be on the same circuit (unless, of course, you are in an apartment building or condominium and you and/or your neighbor are on the same side of the transformer). Even if one of those scenarios happens, you can still program those powerline ethernet plugins so that they are a separate network from your neighbors. I have a pair of plugins because I need to run my wireless router on the opposite side of the house that my cable modem is on, and it had a description of how to do it in the instructions.

If you are looking for these, I would go with Netgear's 200mb/s version. Even if you are just connecting computer-to-computer, I find it faster than Wireless. :)
