### Post by Fatal Toenail Infection on 2009-06-26
I can't really speak for categorizing different laptops in that manner, but check out [www.notebookreview.com](www.notebookreview.com).  It's one of the best unbiased sources of information on new laptops (and they have a massive forum too).  My advice would be to get a laptop with solid build quality and a good reputation.  If you're running linux, you'll never need to "upgrade" to a new computer as long as your current one is still in one piece.  And plus considering how cheap laptops have become, it's really not necessary to spend a lot on a high spec laptop.
