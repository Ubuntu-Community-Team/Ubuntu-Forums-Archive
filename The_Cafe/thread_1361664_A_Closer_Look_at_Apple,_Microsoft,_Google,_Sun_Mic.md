Small Design Firm Sues Microsoft Corp. Over Bing Name-DJ
Thursday, 17 Dec 2009 11:22am EST 
Dow Jones reported that a small computer-based design firm in Missouri is suing Microsoft Corp. for trademark infringement over the name Bing. Bing! Information Designs LLC is alleging trademark and unfair competition against Microsoft in its suit filed in a St. Louis circuit court, saying Microsoft's use of the name dilutes the value of it and confuses the public about the companies' relationship to each other. The company is seeking actual and punitive damages including having Microsoft pay for corrective advertising to remedy the confusion it caused. Two more Latest Key Developments from Microsoft:

> European Commission Settles With Microsoft Corp. Over Browser Antitrust Case-DJ
Wednesday, 16 Dec 2009 05:45am EST 
Dow Jones reported that the European Commission accepted to settle its remaining antitrust issues with Microsoft Corp., ending a decade-long battle over the software giant's monopolistic business practises. The European Commission, the EU's executive arm, agreed to abandon its case against the Company over whether the Company is illegally abusing its dominance in the Internet browser markets without a fine, in return for a legally binding commitment from the Company to start marketing rivals' browsers alongside its own Internet Explorer. The commission had accused the Company of crushing rivals and stifling innovation by including its own Internet Explorer browsers in its dominant Windows operating system. 

Microsoft Corp. Suspends Juku Amid Plagiarism Charges-DJ
Tuesday, 15 Dec 2009 11:22am EST 
Dow Jones reported that Microsoft Corp. has suspended MSN Juku, its new microblogging service in China, amid allegations that much of the code was lifted from a Canadian startup, Plurk. The Company said it was suspending MSN Juku, which was developed for MSN China by a third-party independent vendor, while it investigated the charges. The move followed allegations by Plurk that much of the basecode used in MSN Juku was plagiarized from Plurk, which claims to be the microblogging service in Taiwan.[http://www.reuters.com/finance/stocks/keyDevelopments?symbol=MSFT.O](http://www.reuters.com/finance/stocks/keyDevelopments?symbol=MSFT.O)
