### Post by Frogs Hair on 2010-03-21
Hi,
I come across many threads that mention a need for faster boot times, so I did an experiment .  I set the lap timer on my watch an did a cold boot. The result was one minute and 4 seconds for Ubuntu, and most of that time was spent waiting for mother board splash
screen, loading  the Asus express gate, and the bios info screen.The time from Grub to to full log in was very fast indeed.

I felt no anger about this, nor the the inclination to throw objects across the room at my computer. I could see this being an issue for laptop or notebook users that need to be up and running asap in a work environment . No mater how fast I get from grub to full login,
I still have to wait for the other functions so Ubuntu can boot.

For me , waiting one minute is not worth getting upset about.
