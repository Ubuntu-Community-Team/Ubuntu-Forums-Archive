### Post by kepardue on 2008-08-30
Been reading more about Ogg Theora since Mozilla announced native support for it in 3.1.  Although I haven't had much luck with the encoder so far, I'm extremely encouraged by Chris Montgomery's recent encoder improvements.  There's so many different formats for the preservation of data, but it seems that the open community and others have standardized behind OpenDocument for word processing/office data.

I have to ask, do you guys think that Theora will become the OpenDocument of video?  Will we be able to preserve critically important video (such as government or NASA archive footage) in high quality, HD or near HD quality?  Will Xiph perhaps adopt some sort of lossless video format (or is there one that I don't know about now) that would be suitable for editing?  For instance, with things like home video of my family, it would be very bad (TM) if my video were in a format that nobody supported in 30 years or in a format that preserved it in a less than ideal quality.

### Post by Bachstelze on 2008-08-30
> **Polygon said:**
> im not sure about this, but i thought that x264 (OSS equiviliant of h.264) was meant for high def stuff

More accurately, H.264 is just a format (also known as MPEG-4 AVC), and x264 is a free software library to produce H.264 video streams. Both terms are not interchangeable (there are other H.264 encoders like Nero Digital AVC for example).

And I agree that Theora is stil far, far, far from being a viable alternative yet.
