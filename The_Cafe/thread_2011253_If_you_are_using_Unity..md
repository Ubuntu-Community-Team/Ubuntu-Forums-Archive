I really think it all comes down to what you're used to.

I used gnome shell for over 6 months and recently returned to unity for a go. The initial week was very irritating. I would not call the inability to minimise open apps through unity's launcher a feature.

Right now I'm going to say I don't think Unity is more productive (though I prefer the term comfortable). I plan to stick with it a few months and then move on to KDE for a while maybe. Once I'm done with that cycle I'll no doubt come to the same conclusion, it's all about what you're used to.

I voted _no_. I used the Unity Desktop through all the betas leading up to 12.04 and for about month after 12.04's release. The very things which the writer likes about Unity are the aspects that I *don't* like.  I switched back to XFCE/Xubuntu.

He writes:

"The general public are used to a desktop metaphor that dates back to  Windows 95. They open up programs only to minimize them so that they can  open up more programs. "

Huh? I don't get this. Is it because I don't have a screen the size of a football field? I don't "minimize windows". I simply switch between them, just like *he* does. Is he saying he prefers Unity because it _forces_ this behavior?

"But what you don&#8217;t have is what Linux-land calls a Winlist &#8212; a small applet, in a panel, that houses all minimized windows. "

Huh? I have that in my XFCE panel, but it houses all my **maximized** windows. It does everything that the launcher can _and_ minimize _and_ it doesn't add a secondary "panel" to the desktop (euphemistically called a "launcher"). I just don't see the difference between my "win-list" and the launcher, except the launcher has less functionality?

> **Version Dependency said:**
>  When I login to my dad's Xubuntu computer, I keep hitting that damn SUPER key and wanting to type.

Install Synapse. You don't have to choose between Super or Alt-F2. It does both. You also don't have to arrow-key through a wall of icons. (I rarely use my menu in Xubuntu.) On average, easily half the keystrokes are used when invoking Synapse as compared to Unity's launcher or lens. 

One of the reasons I felt as though Unity was *less* efficient was that I was always having to reach for the keyboard (for a keystroke) or cycling through a variety of mouse gestures to choose between desktops and apps. One is either hunting for the launcher (which may or may not pop up) or the launcher is always up and burning real-estate. Anyway... just explaining why I voted no...
