### Post by B0rsuk on 2006-06-26
[http://slashdot.org](http://slashdot.org)
Reading slashdot is like holding a leash of a large, excited dog. This is because news stories come and go so fast. It posts short summaries of articles and links to them. The real treasure is user-made comments; there are some clueless people, of course, but among  several hundreds of comments its very easy to find real gems. Traffic here is very high, and it's often enough to link to a page at slashdot to put heavy strain on other server, occasionally bringing it down.
As some people will point out, slashdot is not without its faults, but it's still a great site.

[http://digg.com](http://digg.com)
A suplement to slashdot. Can't say too much about it, because I rarely visit it. Both sites have ther pros and cons. Digg prides itself to be community driven, but one thing that  turns me off is that it has merely dozens of comments on each story, instead of hundreds (slashdot).

[http://happypenguin.org](http://happypenguin.org)
Probably the best news site about linux games.

[http://www.phoronix.com/](http://www.phoronix.com/)
high end hardware/linux games type of news site.

[http://freshmeat.net/](http://freshmeat.net/)
About new software/new wersions etc.
