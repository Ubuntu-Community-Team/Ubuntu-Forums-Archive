### Post by teet on 2006-08-26
> **Kethinov said:**
> Hi all. I'm looking to throw a TV Tuner in my current box. Here's my list of needs:

- Linux compatibility
- Ability to record as well as capture
- Remote control
- Good picture

And of course, I'm looking to go as cheap as possible. One thing I specifically don't need is HDTV support.

I bought a Leadtek Winfast TV 2000 XP RM several months back.  Newegg was having a special and after all was said and done (shipping and handling, rebates) I got the card for $14.  Not too shabby.

It uses the bt878 chipset, so it was pretty easy to setup (I have a guide on my site [http://www.missouri.edu/~datcnc/articles.html](http://www.missouri.edu/~datcnc/articles.html) ).  The only real complaint is that the remote doesn't work that well...I got it halfway functioning in MythTV (it was a real pain in the arse), but since I sit in front of my computer most of the time, it's really not an issue.

My Leadtek card is what is called a "software-encoding" card.  Right now I'm recording a TV show using MythTV (it's running in the background) and it's using about 40% of my CPU time.  I have a 3.06 Ghz Celeron D processor (picked up for $50 a few months back) and 512 mb of DDR 400 memory.

If you wanted to spend a little more money, I would suggest going with a Hauppauge (spelling?) PVR 150 or PVR 250.  These are "hardware-encoding" cards.  Basically, instead of using up CPU time, the tuner card itself takes over part of the work (this would be really good if you had a slower processor).  I think the PVR 150 is like $90 with a remote.

The best thing about the PVR 150/250 is that there is a TON of documentation on them.  There are 2 or 3 **EXCELLENT** guides on how to setup these cards and get MythTV up and running with them (with full remote control support).

-teet
