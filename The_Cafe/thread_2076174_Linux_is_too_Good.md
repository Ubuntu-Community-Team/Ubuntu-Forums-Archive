### Post by Artemis3 on 2012-10-25
There are many factors and that is one of them. Heavy marketing affects buyers and the oems most always will want to keep offering that "popular choice". Thats where the problem starts.

Also when they pick parts, a vendor will often offer a cheaper piece with... non released / NDA only specs which only works in... you guessed.

Those committing to linux and other free alternatives are usually all or nothing shops, and usually a bit more expensive, which is why you see system76 and zareason that way. Lack of regulation allows for heavy bending towards the benefit of the few controlling the market.

If there was a law requiring all computers to be sold with a free (as in freedom) OS alternative, and only allowed proprietary OSes as optional, opt-in choices; perhaps we would see things a bit different.

But that would mean thinking for the benefit of consumers (and the environment), not corporations...

As for planned obsolescence, there are various documentaries about it, one by Cosima Dannorizer is very good, but i think its in spanish and french only.
