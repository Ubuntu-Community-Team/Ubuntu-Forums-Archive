### Post by 1clue on 2015-12-17
While my reply doesn't talk directly to your topic, it does reference the first sentence in your post.  I don't have significant speech recognition software experience, but I know how to speed up your typing.

Most people I know look at their keyboards when they type, and between that and "hunt and peck" typing, they're pretty slow at it.

[LIST=1]
[*]Take a picture of your keyboard and print it off on a full-sized sheet of paper.
[*]Find a cardboard box that can enclose your keyboard, mouse and your hands.
[*]Cut the flaps off of it.
[*]Put it in place over your keyboard and mouse.
[*]Cut enough of the "user side" of the box away that you can get your hands in to comfortably type/mouse, but where you can't see your keyboard.
[*]Lay the picture of your keyboard on top of the box and tape the top edge of it to the box, right at the edge closest to your monitor.
[*]Flip the picture over the box so you can't see it.
[*]Don't remove the box for any reason for 3 weeks.
[*]Keep your fingers on home row and type properly.
[*]If you get stuck on a key's location, hunt for it without looking, and learn where it is based on your home row hand position.
[LIST=1]
[*]If you can't find it in reasonable time, flip the picture back over, locate the key, flip the picture back and then find the key with your fingers.
[*]Never operate with the picture in easy view.
[/LIST]

[*]Practice, practice, practice.
[*]Use control key combinations rather than the mouse where possible.
[/LIST]

Most people who type regularly know where all the keys are but are not confident in that knowledge.  By hiding the keyboard you stop thinking about key location and start thinking about what you want to say.  You type in words and sentences, not characters and punctuation.

Also while there are some activities where a mouse is a benefit, most things you do with your word processor and/or computer in general can be done with a key combination much more quickly than you could reach for a mouse and find a menu item.

I don't type as fast as I can talk, but it's close.  Getting the distractions away from you is the main thing.

Sorry if you consider this to be off-topic, but I see it as relevant.
