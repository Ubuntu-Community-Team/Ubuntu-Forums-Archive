### Post by king minger on 2007-04-19
Feisty has ended my love-hate relationship with linux!

I have been messing around with linux on-off since 1998 and only now, with feisty, can I truly say i'm going to be using it more than 'doze for non work stuff.

I started with SUSE back then, but never got up and running on my P-200 MMX. HD space was needed for games so guess what went...

then tried Red Hat a year or 2 later, but never really got on with it.
Mandrake was my distro of choice after that, and I even set my mum up with it and she still uses (no spam-bot headaches for me!) howeve, something catastrophic usually happened to my installs, so were removed and forgotten.

Warty looked interesting from day one, and i've been advocating ubuntu since breezy, but never fully made a switch myself til now (with a SUSE 10.1 install in the meantime)

I recently got a new laptop (for me anyway) and decided to dual boot it with both ubuntu and XP. the feisty beta was just out so i thought why not:) 

so far, the XP boot has next to no time actually running and ubuntu doing pretty much everything.
the only problems i've had were with the broadcom WiFi and an underperforming mobile Radeon 9000 (now legacy!)
the wifi sorted with ndiswrapper and the radeon by tweaking the ati driver (not supported in the fglrx driver) all of which came from this forum:KS 

now my linux-fu is nearing a comfort zone for me and i'm really enjoying the view!

Thanks for such a great community and here's to the best version of (k/x)ubuntu ever:guitar:
