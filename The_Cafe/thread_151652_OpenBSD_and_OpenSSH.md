### Post by fbscarel on 2006-03-28
Hello there guys, this is my first post on this forum. Actually I am an OpenBSD user, but I have used Ubuntu for a while so I appreciate both. I am not starting any flamewar, by the way.

Recently, Theo de Raadt (and some other lead developers, such as Marco Peereboom and Damien Miller) have posted some messages to public forums explaining the difficult financial situation OpenBSD and one of its most popular projects, OpenSSH, are exposed. Links follow:


[http://marc.theaimsgroup.com/?l=openssh-unix-dev&m=114316163313701&w=2](http://marc.theaimsgroup.com/?l=openssh-unix-dev&m=114316163313701&w=2)
[http://marc.theaimsgroup.com/?l=openssh-unix-dev&m=114316224627520&w=2](http://marc.theaimsgroup.com/?l=openssh-unix-dev&m=114316224627520&w=2)


Short story is that a lot of vendors use and abuse OpenSSH (RedHat, IBM, Sun & friends) and do not give a single dollar back. The userbase strives to donate as much as possible, but it still doesn't seem enough for the severe costs to maintain the project.

I'm here to invite Ubuntu users to donate or buy merchandise (CDs and T-Shirts), if possible, from OpenBSD. The project not only develops OpenSSH but also a whole bunch of security features that both the BSD and Linux community have been benefiting for years (such as the recent X.org bugs found by the new free() and malloc() features).

Just a request: do not reply to this post if you are going to give the new super-duper idea to OpenBSD. Some "not-so-smart" people from Slashdot have done it, and it doesn't add anything after all. Since the Ubuntu/Debian guys are so much cooler than the Slashdotters out there, I don't expect that to happen anyway.

Thanks for your time, OpenBSD webpage is [http://www.openbsd.org](http://www.openbsd.org) , and if you want to donate [http://www.openbsd.org/donations.html](http://www.openbsd.org/donations.html) .


Most regards,
Felipe Scarel.
