### Post by Simian Man on 2010-08-09
Yes I do know that.  That's why whenever I'm making a recipe that calls for one of these metals, I make substitutions.  Chrome and nickel can be substituted for aluminum provided you reduce the amount of liquid in the recipe slightly to preserve the consistency.  Cobalt can be substituted with either Copper or Pyrite (fool's gold).  Copper is cheaper and easier to find, but does have a slight acidity you may have too account for.
