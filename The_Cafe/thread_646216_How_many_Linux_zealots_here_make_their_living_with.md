### Post by maljaros on 2007-12-20
I set up the network for my company when we we got wired ten years ago and gave some thought to using Linux, but didn't know enough to cut the umbilical.  We had a legacy issue with most of our files being in Wordperfect and Quattro-Pro formats.  I did speak to the Oop guys and they agreed that Open Office (which was quite primitive back then) would not cope with our (very technical) spreadsheets.  Also our DOS-based accounting package and CAD system could not port to Linux.  The joke is that five years later a merger with another company forced us to convert to MS-Office which is where we are today. The growth from a five workstation peer-to-peer (remember "Windows for Workgroups"?) to thirty workstations and two dedicated servers has been a very expensive nightmare that would have been so much easier in Linux.
Some of my tears over what might have been were wiped away today when I realised that my dual-boot workstation, which has seen successively pre-Fedora Red-Hat, Lindows, Suse, Mandriva and one of the early uBuntu releases, has not run MS-Windows since I upgraded to Xubuntu 7.10 a month ago.
Freedom at last!
