### Post by t4thfavor on 2011-03-04
Hosting your own site entails installing and maintaining a web server. I have mine on a really small, and efficient atom 330 system, which is more than I will ever need. It runs Apache2 which is easy to configure, and fairly secure.

It also takes a bit of firewall knowledge to keep it secure, you shouldn't just plunk your web server in the DMZ unless you really know what your doing. 

I prefer to host my own because I already pay for commercial internet service, and I don't make any money on any of my sites (and therefore can't justify another expense). Plus I learned a lot about Linux and how to configure services, and firewalls which was worth every minute I spent.

If you let someone else host it, you don't get to really understand how things work, and in my opinion thats what tinkering in general is all about.
