### Post by havchr on 2007-11-19
If you want to do programming, you should try and figure out what kind of software you like to write. Then look for jobs in papers/internet etc. For programming, I would guess there are specific pages for it-jobs as well.

If you have done a complete working system of something that's always a good thing to have on your cv. It would get you a long way. Also networking, try and meet up with people who can offer you work in non-akward situations. For example, for game programming, a trip to GDC (game developers conference) is excellent for meeting people in the game industry.
