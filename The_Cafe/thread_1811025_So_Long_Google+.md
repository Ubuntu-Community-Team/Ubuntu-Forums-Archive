### Post by DeadSuperHero on 2011-07-24
I'm perfectly happy with my Diaspora account instead.

I think what really bothers me, though, is the whole principle of the matter. I have some friends that enjoy being under a pseudonym or a persona online, and those reasons are actually far less malicious than just "being a part of Anonymous". Some of them are prominent in their place of work, and don't want certain aspects of themselves shared. (For example, let's say someone had different religious or political views than most of their co-workers, and didn't want their views to cause a conflict in the workplace). For this reason alone, I really have no problem with pseudonyms or anonymity, and in fact, I rather encourage it. 

The biggest problem I've had with Google+ is that it seems to give a false sense of security to many of its users. Sure, you can post things privately to Aspects, but the fact of the matter is that Google really is just a great big indexing engine with advertising attached. They're search and advertising, and throwing myself and my identity with an entity that so heavily reasearches search and has numerous privacy issues year after year really makes me uncomfortable with dumping all of my private data in there.

Is that a paranoid assumption? You bet. It's not nearly as bad as Facebook, but the fact of the matter is that Google+ runs the same risk as Facebook does: becoming a huge data silo of personal information. The principles behind decentralization and federation could alleviate this problem greatly, I feel.
