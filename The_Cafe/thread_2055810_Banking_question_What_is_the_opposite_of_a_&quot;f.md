### Post by vasa1 on 2012-09-10
We've had something called a flexi-deposit or multi-deposit in which an initial contracted interest rate is fixed for the tenure but withdrawals any time before maturity are permitted. In such cases, interest on the withdrawn amount is at computed at a lower rate while the balance continues earning the contracted rate.

If you want, you could avoid the deposit question by going for "financial assets" and grossly dividing them into "debt" and "equity".
