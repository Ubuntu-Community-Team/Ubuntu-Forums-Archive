### Post by handy on 2009-01-14
The only advice I have, is that if you get an AGP graphics card that has had a PCI-e GPU grafted onto it, you could be in for some fun & games getting it to run under Linux.

I have an XFX GeForce 7950GT card, which took me 20 hours to eventually work out that it needed AGP to be turned OFF in xorg.conf before I could get it to work properly.

So what you are doing is a good idea; find out what works for other people doing what you want to do, before you spend your money & potentially lots of time.
