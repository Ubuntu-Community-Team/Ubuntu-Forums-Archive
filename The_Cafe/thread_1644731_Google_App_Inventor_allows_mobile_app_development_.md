### Post by linuxforartists on 2010-12-13
This article appeared in *Wired* magazine:

[Coding for the Masses]("http://www.wired.com/magazine/2010/11/st_thompson_wereallcoders/")

Personally, I think it's a great idea.  Naturally, you'd end with a glut of bad programs, the same way cheap video cameras resulted in a lot of bad home movies.  

Still, empowering people through technology is a good thing.  The article singles out Google's new tool for creating Android apps.

The App Inventor for Android sounds like an awesome tool.  How many times have you wanted to build your own mobile app, but didn't have the necessary coding skills?

The cynic in me wonders what angle Google has.  The obvious one is that they're trying to accelerate development of their app store to compete with the iPhone.  

Also, does Google own any app you build with this platform?  I checked the FAQs for more information.  They say you can't upload your app to the Android market yet.  You can't export the source code to a text editor or IDE either.  

Of special interest to coders is that App Inventor uses the [Kawa Language Framework]("http://www.gnu.org/software/kawa/"), which I'd never heard of before.  Worth looking into if you hate WYSIWYG editors and want to work on code directly.

In any case, this is one more point in favor of getting an Android phone over an iPhone.  The other is avoiding AT&T, but that's a whole other rant. ;)

What do you guys think?
