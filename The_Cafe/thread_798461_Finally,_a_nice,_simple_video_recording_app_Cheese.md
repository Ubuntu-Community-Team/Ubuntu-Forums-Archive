### Post by 50words on 2008-05-18
I just discovered Cheese, a nice and simple video recording app. It lets you take capture photos and videos from a webcam, apply some cheesy effects, and save them (or export photos directly to F-Spot).

Finally!

It is working fine for me with pictures. Video capturing is a little spotty. Videos do not show up in the gallery for me, but they are there if I restart cheese, and I can save them in OGG format, which I can (theoretically) edit in something like PiTiVi (except that neither PiTiVi nor any other movie editor I have tried in Ubuntu works at all, so I am still stuck with videos I cannot edit, making them relatively useless.).

My only real complaint with Cheese is the low quality of the videos. Cheese only turns out a 320x240 video, which is pretty low quality for my 2 megapixel webcam. I don't see any option to change this.

Still, finally a step in the right direction. Hopefully the Cheese devs will keep up the good work, and the video editors will improve so that we Ubuntu users can finally join the Web 2.0 fun.
