### Post by Copper Bezel on 2011-05-04
One thing I've found useful is aggressive use of symlinks. Using them as shortcuts, you can organize all your data by logical classification, then create a separate organization around how you actually find yourself interacting with the information (so that, for instance, the handouts and information for my current classes are way down in a directory structure under Documents along with previous years', but the present semester is backed up via Dropbox and immediately accessible from my catchall folder.) That way, too, you can pull in different elements of the same project and copy that whole spidery creature off if you need to port it somewhere, all without breaking the rules of your "real" organization method.
