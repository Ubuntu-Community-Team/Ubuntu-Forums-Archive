### Post by lovinglinux on 2010-02-13
Please don't do it. Collecting random e-mails is the most basic method used by spammers. If your client wants to build a database, then create a newsletter system on his site that will allow his clients to subscribe/unsubscribe willingly.

Take the opportunity to teach him how bad spam is.

> From: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spam_%28electronic%29](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spam_%28electronic%29)

Spam in e-mail started to become a problem when the Internet was opened up to the general public in the mid-1990s. It grew exponentially over the following years, and today comprises some 80 to 85% of all the email in the world, by a "conservative estimate".[3]

I totally despise those e-mail addresses databases. I often receive e-mails offering to buy those lists and several of my e-mail addresses are on them.

I hate spam so much!
