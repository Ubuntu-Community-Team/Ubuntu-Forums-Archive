### Post by Puppy fam on 2007-08-02
Sorry to double post.

I did some research on editing the BIOS, and here's what I came up with:
> It is possible that your computer is rude and completely ignores the CD. That’s OK. All you have to do is tell the computer to check the CD drive before booting up your regular operating system. This is a kind of complicated process, so if you don’t have a problem, just skip down two paragraphs.

You can tell your computer to check the CD drive by shutting down the computer, turning it back on, and hitting a key or combination of keys right away. Unfortunately, the key(s) you need to hit vary based on your computer. Some common ones are: F11, Del, Esc, Ctrl+Esc, Ctrl+Alt+Esc, and Ctrl+Alt+Del [Source: SysOpt.com]. Just try each one until one of them works (if one of the combinations doesn’t work but the computer hangs up, you can just hit the power button and try another one).

OK, once you get a key to work (if you can’t get any to work, try asking at the Ubuntu Forums), you’ll see a rather old-looking menu. Use arrow keys and enter to navigate through the options. You need to get the computer to check the CD drive before booting to your hard drive; different computers will have different menus, so I can’t help you too much. I know this is confusing, so if you run into trouble, please ask at the Ubuntu Forums.

Is that accurate? Thanks!
