### Post by neu5eeCh on 2013-02-11
Good luck with that.

Just for perspective's sake. I have a fairly successful blog and was asked to contribute articles to another site. I wrote two articles. The first question I asked myself was this: Why am I contributing to the success of someone else's web site when I could be using the same energy to further the success of my _own_ blog?

Secondly, why am I spending time on this and not getting paid? If I were getting paid, then the distraction might make sense. Right? I already have a hobby, and it's _my own_. I'm not going to spend time on somebody else's hobby unless I'm getting paid. When it's somebody else's hobby, and I'm contributing my time and effort, I call that a job and I expect to get paid for it.

But that's just my perspective.
