### Post by jsmidt on 2008-10-22
> **mintochris said:**
> A multiverse is a very different thing to the many worlds interpretation. 

    Again, I mostly agree with your statement.  The mechanism which creates a multiverse in most modern day theories is different then the branching ideas of the many worlds models.

    However, in many cases the end results are the same.  Both lead to arbitrary numbers of universes where everything which can happen in some universe does happen.  In this way modern multiverse theory is very similar.

   Moreover, in theories with moduli spaces, you have an even more diverse outcomes for each point of the moduli space leads to a universe with different low-energy physics.  M-Theory has a moduli space able to generate 10^500 or more different types of universes with different low energy physics.  

Again, I agree the mechanism is completely different, but the end result is very similar.  I have only been stubborn in pointing this out because it really could be that everything which is possible does happen in some universe.  Very similar end result to multiple worlds theory though the mechanism is very different.
