### Post by Dimitriid on 2007-07-20
I got an average of 30% of all my calls of customers that WILL require a repair. You wouldn't believe how many Laptops die, lets face it Hard Drives die, LCD monitors die, motherboards overheat, etc. But is not even that you know all computer products can fail early and most definitely WILL eventually fail.

The thing that you gotta remember is that on what, over 90% of the consumer laptop market many vital parts are propietary for the particular company that makes the laptop, so the parts really add up. $400 for motherboards, $400 for hard drives, $450 for a new processor, $700 for a new screen.

In most of the cases you get any problem after 1 year and it will be more cost effective to just get a new one. Not if you extend your warranty. Just dont over do it, 1 extra year or 2 at most, 3 years we really crank up the price of the extended warranty because we expect it to fail to be perfectly honest and the cost of laptop+3 year warranty on 3 years down the road is not worth it and it will be better to have a new one anyway.

### Post by macogw on 2007-07-20
I got the extended warranty on my laptop, and I did have a problem, but the idiots at Gateway didn't fix it.  They reinstalled Windows and called it "fixed."  I snooped a bit inside the laptop later (while I was at work at a computer repair shop, so "it was opened by a computer repair tech" :) ), and it turned out the thermal paste on the processor's hotsink was completely hardened.  Hard thermal paste is a baaad thing, so once I put some proper thermal paste on there, it started running quite a bit cooler and not locking up.

EDIT:  I got the 3 year warranty (with accidental damage coverage just in case a root beer falls over or I have a clumsy day), Dimitriid.  This laptop's for school, and I'm going to be in school for more than 2 years.  Besides, I certainly don't expect any computer to die within 3 years.  I don't expect total death (dead mobo or cpu would qualify) within 5 years, even.  Maybe it's because I'm used to desktops.  I know hard drives on laptops are expected to die more due to all the jostling around, but what about other parts?  Really, I'd expect most laptop issues to stem from misuse or poor handling, things which are only covered by the accidental damage warranties.  If it's a "notebook" but is a "desktop replacement" and just sits on a table, it shouldn't have much in the way of issues.  Dropping it, banging it into a wall while carrying it around, spilled drinks...those sound like more likely sources of issues on a laptop.

I'm asking because I tend to have a very different idea of "maximum lifespan" on hardware than other people.  Others say 3 years is maximum on a desktop.  I say 8 years should be expected.  I was surprised when my brother and sister said the desktop hadn't turned on in months and didn't say anything when you tried.  I got my dad to try turning it on and it said fan error.  I'd hardly call replacing a $8 CPU fan the end of the "maximum lifetime" on a computer, but if you want to call "first time it's opened for repair" the end, well that computer was 5 years old.  For the first problem to occur after everyone says it should have been dead & buried makes me think something is wrong with how others either treat or perceive their hardware.  I don't even do anything to it for "upkeep," (when I replaced the fan, I cleared the dust out for the first time in those 5 years) so to kill a desktop faster must require a physical beating.  And no, I don't get nice "high end" hardware that lasts forever.  My dad's a cheapskate.  It's a $350 HP with 128MB PC100, 40GB HDD, and a Celeron from 2002.

### Post by teet on 2007-07-28
I don't purchase extended warranties **EVER**. 

I purchased a few of them when I was younger and realized what a waste of money they were.  I like to look at the big picture...over the course of my lifetime, I figure that I'll come out ahead if I NEVER buy an extended warranty.  

I bought a new laptop ~5.5 years ago.  The hard drive died after 18 months and it was out of warranty.  I spent around $150 buying a new hard drive and replaced it myself.  Other than that, I have had no problems and still use the laptop semi-daily.  I could have probably spent $150 and got an extended warranty.  But I like my way better.  If I bought the extended warranty, I would have been out $150 no matter what.  By not buying it, I at least gave myself the possibility of saving $150.
