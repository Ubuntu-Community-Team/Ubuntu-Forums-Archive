Why do people say 'Pop' or 'Soda'?  It's the generic term.  I don't say "Let's go to McDonald's so I can get a Big Mac, large fries, and a baked apple pie."  I say I'm going to McDonald's to get dinner.  Then when I get there, I decide on the specifics.  To some people "Coke" doesn't mean "Coca Cola"; it's a generic term for a carbonated soft drink.  So it's really no different than saying, "Let's go get a soda" and then deciding what type of soda we want when we get there.

I'm sure if you think about it, you can figure out how this came about for some people.  Coke was really the first drink of that sort to be mass produced at insane levels and gain such crazy popularity.  So some people just started generically calling those types of drinks "Coke" just like Th3Proffesor's "Xerox".

And for the record, most people don't call it Xerox anymore.
