read the review:

[SNIP - a tetris implementation]("http://snip.sourceforge.net/") [[sourceforge.net]]("http://www.stumbleupon.com/url/snip.sourceforge.net/")
I know what your going to say: "not another tetris-like game!"
well yes it does have tetris implementation but this is far more then just a game. Gone are the "Disney" world color and animation-like look. This game is much more then a game: Stark black and white clip art leads to an erie almost scary feel that will move you. The sound that goes with this is perfect. I find this one of the greatest works of art ever. This game belongs in the Smithsonian. You will be intrigued, worried, and touched by the art  that encompasses the completeness, the emptyness of this artwork.

from: [http://azu-reus.stumbleupon.com/]("http://azu-reus.stumbleupon.com/")

...dare to be moved by this erie and wonderful work of art:

System Requirements:

    * DirectX 8.0 or better
    * DirectDraw-compatible video card with 4 MB+ VRAM
    * Pentium 200
    * 16 MB RAM (32 MB recommended)
    * Windows 9x/2k/XP OS (implies reset-button)

**News**

 **Nov 11 2004**

 xavierpayne's new code is in CVS - snip is themeable and can play background music now.
 The new features are not documented in detail so far, though.
 **Oct 27 2004**

 Though I thought snip was pretty dead by now, it seems some people are still interested in it; we've got more than 1.200 downloads so far (which is more than I ever hoped for). Many thanks to xavierpayne, a new dev who sent me some patches that should be in CVS soon and will continue improving snip in his free time. Since I don't have Windows anymore, this could speed up development somehow.
