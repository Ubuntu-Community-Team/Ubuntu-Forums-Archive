### Post by llua+ on 2012-01-16
I would still look at the Red Hat certification route like CharlesA said if i was you. Linux+ and LPI certifications are multiple choice whereas Red Hat's are practical exams.

From a employer viewpoint a person; Picking a person who picked the right answer from 4 choices with a automatic 25% chance of being right, versus a person who was dropped into a environment being told to fix it/Make it happen and actually _did_ it isn't a hard choice to make. 
But i am not a employer so take it with a grain of salt.

### Post by haqking on 2012-01-17
If you take the CompTIA linux + exam, you will also get the LPIC and NCLA from Novell.

So you get 3 for the price of 1 as long as you pass.

The Linux + is easy and cheap so worth it just to get the 3 qualifications and is good base for further study in the levels above LPIC and also onto RHCE.

The Linux+ and LPI are vendor neutral of course, RHCE being vendor centric of course
