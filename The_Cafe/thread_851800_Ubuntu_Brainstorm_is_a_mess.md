### Post by nand on 2008-07-07
(biais warning: i'm the brainstorm.ubuntu.com developer :) )

IMO, the number-of-ideas argument is just wrong. Having 800 or 10000 ideas does not change the fact that a portion of them is not exploitable, another portion is bad, another portion is good, and a few are "wow - never thought of that". And as pointed out by gunashekar, this number is likely to go higher and higher. In the long run, even in the forums, the high number of ideas will become a problem.

The real problem to tackle is : Since our time is limited, how could we automatically get the best ideas out there without reading *all*? How can we get the cream without spending all your free time in it?

Forums do not tackle this problem. The only metric that could help to sort ideas is the number of replies, is not that good. They could be replies of controversy, of hate or of support.

The solution used in brainstorm is simply natural selection : bad ideas are buried, ideas which are likely to be good goes up in the "most popular" listings.
=> If you only want to look for ideas likely to be good, just stick with the most popular listings.

Coming to the "mess" feeling, I have the feeling this is triggered by the fact that ideas concern a *very* large area. Ubuntu is huge, and, like it or not, for some people, Ubuntu represents all it contains. The consequences is, instead of posting upstream ideas on upstream structures, people post them in brainstorm. And on the overall, we get ideas for *lots* of different areas.

The problem is then : "ok, I'm interested in good ideas, but only a given subset of them." That's the more likely usecase for developers looking for the most popular ideas concerning its software, or a prospective developer.

To tackle that, we put more or more ways to classify ideas. Ideas can be linked to a category, but now also upstream software, Ubuntu software, Ubuntu derivates, Ubuntu websites. And still more is to come.
You can then search by this fields.


Now, some of you feel that it could be organized even better. Please tell me how, I'm open to suggestions and criticism. Submit an idea about the website. Discuss it.
A good part of the things that were implemented after the initial release were suggested by users (see [http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/category/11/most_popular_ever/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/category/11/most_popular_ever/) ).

### Post by gunashekar on 2008-07-09
I think that an idea should be discussed for all its pros and cons before putting up to vote. The initiator of the idea can be given an opportunity to polish his argument, modify his views and decide when it it is time to ask for a vote or withdraw his idea. The mere present of a - or + and a number of votes can influence a lot of casual voters to vote without applying their mind (called Bandwagon Effect or Counter bandwagon effect by pollsters). Live voting is therefore not recommended even on non-political technical issues. Secret ballot is better. Live voting is more suited to less important issues and when online polls serve to add superfluous interactivity keep the viewer interest in the issue and on the webpage.

By putting a premium on voting and forcing more applied thought and discusion before calling for a Vote, we can reduce the number of ideas being voted upon at any point of time. The voting period should also be limited to a fixed number of days so that the results can be published. An idea voted down in the past can be pulled up and modified at any future date due to changes in the environment and thinking among the community members.

### Post by 23meg on 2008-08-06
[QUOTE=nand]Basically what could be done to make moderator work more efficient?[/QUOTE]

When a moderator or developer marks an idea as a duplicate, rather than enqueue it in the proposed duplicates list, the software should mark it as a duplicate right away. The current process of proposing the idea as duplicate and wading through the duplicate pages to find it and actually mark it is a pain. 

I'm also puzzled as to how exactly the proposed duplicates are ordered, and would welcome a way to adjust the number of ideas shown per page, and be able to jump to any page (as opposed to links to 9 pages being shown at a time at the bottom). Showing the page number links on the top as well as the bottom would also help.

Also, duplicates must, and I mean it, *must* recurse. The current situation with endless duplicate branching is a mess. If I've marked idea B as a duplicate of idea A, and idea C as a duplicate of idea B, idea C (and all its further duplicates, if any) should be marked a duplicate of idea A as well, and all ideas except idea A should either be closed to comments, or a big "You're commenting on a duplicate idea" warning should be shown when commenting on them, similar to the one in the Launchpad bug tracker. This should apply in a retroactive manner.

I should also be able to re-mark as a duplicate an idea I've deliberately or accidentally unmarked as a duplicate.

I can't check whether any of these have been implemented in the development snapshot, since I can't log in.
