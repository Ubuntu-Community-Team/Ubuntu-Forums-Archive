### Post by nwillettjeffries on 2009-01-16
Google just ended development on their notebook web-app and so I'm now on the market for a new notes program.

I was wondering what other people use for notes? What are the must-have features of a note taking program for you?

For me a good notes program has:
-tagging
-handles various text formats, pictures, and links seamlessly
-easily clips a webpage into a note or takes a screen grab of some sort.
-syncing or persistence in the cloud so I can access my notes anywhere/anytime.
-some sort of assurance that I won't be left with a bunch of notes in an odd format on a program that's no longer in development...in other words, persistence is key.

Tomboy is too simplistic for my needs, although the wiki-like style has obvious utility.
I briefly looked at Basket. It's really feature rich. The only downside is that it's KDE and it has dependencies on kmail, kontact, korganizer, etc so it's a bloated install for any gnome user...

Sorry in advance if this is posted in the wrong forum.

### Post by nwillettjeffries on 2009-01-17
Hopefully google will open source the plugin so that someone will continue to develop it. Having said that, I can see which way the wind is blowing, and in the long term, I'm not entirely comfortable staying with something that's permanently going out of development.

I took a look at zim. As far as I can tell, it's a more feature rich version of tomboy although maybe I didn't spend enough time with it. The biggest reason why I didn't stick with it was the lack of any sort of tagging functionality. Lack of web integration was also an issue but I could work around that with some copy/paste work...

Also installed dropbox. Not a note taking app *per se* but it's still an incredible way to sync and share files. It's one of those things that you look back and wonder how you worked without it. Definitely highly recommend to anyone who uses multiple machines or moves around a lot.

Haven't tried notecase yet but it's on my list. The webapp Ubernote also got my attention today as they've released a client to automatically move a google notebook into ubernote. I'm hesitant to put this stuff into the cloud again but there are obvious advantages there as well.

I think evernote has a webapp part to it as well though I don't know how good it is. I guess the ideal, at least from accessibility is to have notes stored locally and automatically syncing between something in the cloud.
