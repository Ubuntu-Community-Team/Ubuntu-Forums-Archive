### Post by TheFu on 2023-01-16
[https://www.theregister.com/2023/01/16/basecamp_37signals_cloud_bill/](https://www.theregister.com/2023/01/16/basecamp_37signals_cloud_bill/)
37 Signals is a cloud based business. They spent $3.2M last year in rented, cloudy, systems from multiple suppliers.  The CTO and CFO ran some numbers for what that money could have bought in Dell Servers and paying another company to management them.  It was less.  Plus, they'd own the systems with an expected 3-5 yr lifespan, so they'd save 20-30% over renting compute and storage from cloudy providers.

Cloud services are expensive.  They make sense for quick trials and for tiny companies, but businesses are quickly learning that there is a premium to put your applications and data on someone else's hardware, at the end of someone else's network, inside someone else's buildings.

The excitement in the industry to push everything to the cloud to remove headaches, is overdone.  My guess is with 100+ servers in the cloud, a company would be better off owning the equipment, hosting that equipment in multiple data centers run by someone else, behind locked doors, where your people have access, but not anyone else beyond the DC service techs.

Next year, 37 Signals has promised an update for the changes and costs involved in moving most stuff out of cloudy service providers.  It will be interesting to see what they've learned next year.

Do you think they will actually spend more, since new expertise will be needed? Are they rushing too fast in the opposite direction?

### Post by Skaperen on 2023-01-20
too many businesses or business-wanna-bes are using the cloud as just a hosting provider with a fancy name.  cloudy this, cloudy that.  that is just going to cost the same plus all the fancy features and name plus that little cloud icon.  now it could be twice as much.

instead, using the cloud should be about what you cannot get from hosting (self or rented).  the best feature is dynamic scaling.   you put up 100 servers and in a couple months one morning someone famous tells everyone to try out your service.  it's 10:00 AM and you run the numbers on your current traffic and realize you'll need 600 more servers and 5 more internet connections ... by noon!!  properly programmed and configured, the cloud can provide that and automatically detect the need and set it up.

sadly, when i go over the help questions of so many "cloud users" (sometimes i think they see it as vaping on servers) it become obvious they still see it as a small gang of pets rather than a herd of cattle.
