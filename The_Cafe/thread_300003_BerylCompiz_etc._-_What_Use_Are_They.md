### Post by angkor on 2006-11-15
The point to all this beryl/compiz stuff is that in a couple of years you'll have a fully hardware accelerated linux desktop. Meaning the cpu no longer draws the desktop but your graphics card, leaving your cpu to do more important tasks. 

In the meanwhile it gives you a beautiful desktop with some useful effects, e.g. scale, zoom, transparency.

---

### Post by mushroom on 2006-11-15
Yeah, Beryl/Compiz are pretty much implementing what Quartz Extreme did in Mac OS 10.2, that is, moving all window drawing/compositing to the video card. Not only does it leave your CPU free for other tasks, it accelerates window drawing massively while allowing for some neat effects. Right now I'm using Beryl with a very OS X-like, minimal setup with only Scale and dropshadows enabled. It's quite nice, stick with it; it may be pretty beta, but it's fairly solid.
