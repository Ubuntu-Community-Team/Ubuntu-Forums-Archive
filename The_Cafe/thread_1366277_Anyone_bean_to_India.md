### Post by Duncan J Murray on 2009-12-29
I went two years ago for two months, where I travelled around Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Kerela, Ooti, Varanasi, and various places inbetween.  I was visiting leprosy organisations and looking at work done elephantiasis and HIV as well.

I absolutely loved it.  But I know some of my friends who've been have had completely different experiences, and have not enjoyed it so much.  So here are some tips:

1.  Be a man rather than a woman.  As a woman on your own, you can be at risk of physical attacks.  I didn't hear anything really bad, but you will probably have to fend people off.

2.  Only eat in the top restaurants you can trust, or home food cooked fresh.  The non-Indian stomach is not used to the various bugs that the Indians are used to, and you can end up with some nasty gastroenteritis if you're not careful.

3.  You _will_ need loperamide for occasional use.  There is some awesome food you can try out there which your nose and your tongue will love, but you stomach will have problems with - mainly, I think, due to the spices used.  There's nothing wrong with a bit of loperamide if all you've got is slightly loose stools.

4. Avoid the tourist areas.  Sorry to say that I didn't enjoy the typical tourist areas much - most were traps.

5. Stay with locals who know the area.  This is what I did, and it really allows you to see the country from the inside, rather than from the perspective as a tourist.  It means that you avoid all the hassle of those people who will try to rip you off or give you bad advice.

6. India isn't really one country.  There is SO much to see, and each state is quite different.  There's jungle karnataka, kerala is tropical, tamil nadu is subtropical, ooti is mountainous, gujarat is more deserty, and the north is completely different too.  Not sure how to advise - maybe go for a long time?!

7. I recommend mountain walking shoes - they are very helpful for walking over the often broken roads and pavements.

8. Jet airways is probably the most reliable way of getting around India on time.



I really enjoyed the hospitality, the great food, the chai, the scenery.  There was something about the place.  It was bizarrely familiar to me even though I hadn't been before.  I also enjoyed the chaos of the place - it's like a complete free-for-all.  There's an excitement and liveliness of the people there.  Everyone seems to be full of energy and, bizarrely, happy.

Hope you have a great time.

Duncan.

### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-12-30
Decide how long you want to go for and what type of journey you want.  India can be done very cheaply as a back-packer using public transport overnight trains etc) and staying in local places, or with a lot of cash.

India unfortunately has a "foreigner" rate for everything remotely tourist-related, which tends to be about 100 times more expensive than for locals.  So be prepared to spend US-type prices for hotels and tourist attractions.

As others have mentioned, be aware that there are plenty of scam artists in India; just have your wits and a decent guide book with you at all times.

Not wanting to redirect you, but would like to suggest considering Sri Lanka as part of your trip too (depending on the security situation at the time)...
