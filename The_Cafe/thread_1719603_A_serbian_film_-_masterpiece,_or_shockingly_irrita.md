### Post by Legeril on 2011-04-02
OK so due to the nature of this movie I'm not sure if the mods will let this post survive, while movies classify as entertainment I'm not sure this one really does. We'll see, but I'd like to know some other peoples down to earth views on it as the 'hype' is just pointless.

I watched this film with no preconceptions, I had heard nothing about it nor had I seen or read and reviews. But I have to say the film didn't really shock me in the least, in the directors attempts to be shocking I feel he made what could have been a good film overly predictable. Not everything about this film sucked though, the imagery is great and I feel it was shot really well, a really self-aware film.

At times I felt like there were deeper themes being touched upon, is this a metaphor for the wars and torture that the people had endured for so long? Is this a message about media desensitization? A reflection of corrupt government in an isolated country? Every time I felt like some issue 'may' have been touched upon then it was followed by irritating 'try to shock you' crap. I giggled most of the way through it, because I found it mostly absurd - much to the disgusted annoyance of my girlfriend. I have seen movies that are solely trying to capitalize on shock value - read *August Underground's Mordum* - but this movie missed the point in my opinion. I find watching Sex and The City with the Mrs more entertaining than this movie, *Irreversible* it is not. My friends are raving about it, in an obvious attempt to be 'sick' and 'twisted / cool', the only thing I liked about this movie was the music.

I'd like to know your opinions? Does this movie deserve such hype? Or the threats of arrest under 'child porn' laws? Why do you like/dislike it?
