### Post by justin whitaker on 2007-03-29
France:

Went to Paris, and my parents moved to Nice. Paris is timeless, and there is always something interesting to see, from a museum, to some random neighborhood that you stumble on as you walk around. 

Nice combines the best parts of Paris, with a beach and some Italian flavor. The countryside around Nice, from Mento to Cannes, is as varied and as striking as any that I have seen. I think everyone should have a chance to wander around Peillion. 

El Salvador:

I have gone 4 times...my wife is from there. The first time I went, I understood why my wife said "There is no poverty in the United States." Yes, we have poor, but not "living in an aluminum sided dirty floored shantytown beside a BMW dealership" type poor. The first time you see that sort of dichotomy, it is a shock. 

That said, San Salvador was much larger than I imagined, and varied between really ugly to strikingly beautiful. Costa Del Sol is as nice a beach as I have seen. Fantastic weather, even with a few tropical downpours, and great people.

Guatemala:

Just came back from a destination wedding there, which was my 3rd visit. Guatemala City, from what I have seen of it, is like any other major city in the world. The people seem a little more conservative, a little more reserved than in El Salvador, but everything is "para servirele". 

Chichicastenango is a real contrast to the city. Bustling market, crowded and narrow streets, mayan rituals in a Catholic church...and about 80 French tourists travelling in a pack...followed by groups of merchants trying to sell their wares. 

Antigua is unique. Spanish colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, and odd mix of tourist centric restaurants and ruins from the 1770s earthquake. Somehow it all works. If you get a chance, stay at Casa Santo Domingo. 

Greece:

Went to Kalamata for a wedding, then spent 5 days in Athens. Beautiful countryside, too many cars. Very touristy in parts, which somehow takes some of the charm away from the ancient ruins...maybe it's just wandering from the Temple of Zeus and seeing a Starbucks that threw me. 

Mexico:

Went to Cabo San Lucas for my Honeymoon...would not go back. Not that I did not have a good time, it just seemed like an extension of Southern California. Too many gringos, maybe.
