### Post by l109421p on 2008-08-22
I have researched over 3,000 alleged 'work at home' opportunities - most of them warranted further investigation due to frauds. Of these overe 90 % were shown to be fraudulent and offered nothing at all to anyone who paid up to $200 to work. Though conviction of such persons running such companies is high, reimbursement of fraudulently gathered funds is zero. 

The monies demanded were not necessarily 'up-front' in nature - often a token payment was given, for 'work' done, such as stuffing envelopes or similar. Then the fraudulent company would suggest that if a sum of money was paid, further contacts would be forthcoming. 

Be very careful of all online offers of work, and pay NOTHING at all to do it. There are a very few companies that offer genuine work, but they are getting very few and far between - most genuine companies have been let down by internet contacts, and no longer wish to employ them.
