### Post by zami on 2010-02-19
"Hacker" only bothers me because it's almost exclusively used to imply illegal or nefarious activities.

I consider "hacking" anytime you are altering a program to work "not as intended", or "opening" something not intended to be open.  And honestly?  I think the idea that all "hacking" is nefarious is actually born of a Windows/Mac mindset.  That's not meant as a negative Windows comment just...

Well, say in Linux, I want my gnome panel to be be blue instead of brown (or whatever).  I just change a setting within the program, in a manner that's intended by the software.

Now lets say I want the gnome panel to look like a giant butterlfy that flaps when I move my mouse.  That would involve some serious coding/programming skills, but because the program (gnome panel) is transparent and open, I'm not "hacking" into the program/code.  And I'm only likely to piss myself off if I fail or muck something up.

But lets say I want to make the Windows XP panel green instead of black.  I have to hack open the code/programing/registry files/whatever, to do that, then apply coding/programming skills to make my changes.  Windows assistance is now void.  You're not supposed to do that.  It's hacking.  It's bad.  It's dangerous. And so forth.  

So I think just because hacking IS "a bad thing to do" on so many PCs, it naturally follows the term has a negative connotation.

I don't *often* use the term "cracker" (um, on people I mean) as it's also a racial slur, as blur xc pointed out.

-zami
