### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-06-15
What war? Ubuntu is a Linux distribution. It cannot go to war. And I doubt very much if Canonical has been at war with Microsoft. The mismatch between the two is so great that any war would last all of 30 seconds. No time to put a steel helmet on.

And then there is the Ubuntu code of Conduct to consider.

> We invite anybody, from any company, to participate in any aspect of the  project. Our community is open, and any responsibility can be carried  by any contributor who demonstrates the required capacity and  competence.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu/conduct](http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu/conduct)

Microsoft Azure is a partner in the Ubuntu Certified Public Cloud program. Businesses buying cloud space off of Microsoft can install Certified Ubuntu images for that cloud space. Canonical will partner with just about anybody if it allows it to make further progress in its mission.

> It is our mission to make open source software available to people  everywhere. We believe the best way to fuel innovation is to give the  innovators the technology they need.

[http://www.canonical.com/](http://www.canonical.com/)
