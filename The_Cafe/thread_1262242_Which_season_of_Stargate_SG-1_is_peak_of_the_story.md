### Post by petrus4 on 2009-09-13
In terms of the series which was based on the first movie, that ended when Daniel ascended, which I think happened at the end of Season 4/start of season 5.

It's fairly obvious that the show's producers knew that, too; they actually have Daniel and Jack O'Neill re-enact how those two characters said goodbye to each other in the film.  The scene was a literal tear jerker for me, and it has a very final feel to it.

The show very nearly did end at that point, and IMHO it should have, considering the way it went after that point, but the Sci Fi Channel decided to keep it going.

Vala Mal'Doran was the single worst thing to ever happen to SG-1.  The character simply should not have been introduced at all; a lot of people disliked her, and with good reason.  The Jonas Quinn arc was also a mistake, and was probably unfair to the actor.

The Ori/Replicator threads are good for the most part, but for me, the single main thing that made SG-1 worth watching, was the dynamic that existed between the original six characters; Daniel, Sam Carter, Jack O'Neill, General Hammond, Teal'c, and Janet Frasier.  

Even science fiction, at its' best, is primarily about human relationships.  The high concept stuff refers to what **happens to** the characters, but we aren't going to care about what happens to them, unless we have an emotional investment in them.  SG-1 is at times a fairly emotional show; the producers I think understood that that was important.

From that point of view, the show should have ended when it was originally going to, at the end of season 4.

The original *Stargate* was, in a lot of technical aspects, an extremely flawed movie, and from a purely academic cinematic point of view, it probably didn't deserve the legacy that it ended up having.

MGM however, found a genuinely warm, sensitive group of actors; and the concept of course allowed for a virtually limitless canvas, from a writing point of view.  The writing was also good; substantially ahead of post-DS9 Star Trek, most of the time.

As well as the series, however, the original film is, from a purely emotional point of view, one of my own all time favourite films; which just goes to show that when it comes right down to it, heart always means a lot more than polish.  As science fiction, for me it ranks second only to Star Trek itself.
