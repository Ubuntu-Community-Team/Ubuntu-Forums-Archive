First and foremost: Thank You! for the well wishes. It does my heart good that there are in fact people who see the good in my service and valuable time I freely gave to ubuntuforums.org.
This I did out the goodness of my heart without need or want for any kind of return.

Keep in mind that I have only resigned from volunteering as a moderator, I am still here as a member of the community.

Please do not hold Adamant1988 or maxamillion responsible for my decision or any other member of this forum, I have been contemplating this for some time now, I have made my decision independently of this or any other factor. As many of you may know ubuntuforums.org is the largest and most successful online community that is Linux based. I have worked hard to help preserve this trusted stature of ubuntuforums.org within the community. With this volunteer position comes the investment of a great deal of time, as stated before I have sacrificed much precious time that I should be giving elsewhere in my life, specifically this time is best invested into loving my wife and the expansion of happiness in our marriage, and yes if you are wondering sacrificing precious time to freely give to the ubuntuforums.org board does take away time from a marriage. Just the other day a Spammer had posted 16 spam post after issuing infractions and requesting his IP be banned in the staff forum, nearly 30 minutes of time had been consumed. This is snapshot of one sliver in time of many needed duties in moderation. If anything for me personally I am relieved of this "privileged burden of time" and now free to enjoy ubuntuforums.org to it's fullest extent as a member of the community.

Also I most note that after I resigned and posted my public resignations I had found an e-mail sent to me by Ryan that the FC did in fact decide to de-mod me but that they had not yet had a procedure in place to do this, and they asked me to do what I had already done. To step down and say my goodbyes. Please do not judge the FC harshly as I had previously stated I stand behind their decision 100% and support it completely. What is most important here is the greater good of the community as a whole, and I trust in their judgment that they have made the right decision. Keep in mind that their decision had no bearing on my decision because I had already resigned and posted my resignation in the staff and public forums. I am still Thankful for the privilege and opportunity to serve this community, freely as a volunteer.

I will still be here as a member of ubuntuforums.org, keep in mind that I still serve as a staff member of sabayonlinux.org and help them mod their forum. The Sabayon forum community is much smaller and only takes a fraction of my time commitment.
