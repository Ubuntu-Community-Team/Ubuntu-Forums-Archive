It might also be called the "virus" effect - one person I had contact with a few years ago, long before I discovered Ubuntu and these forums, didn't take kindly to me dropping him a note to the effect that a virus had sent out an email in his name "It can't have anything to do with me, I use Linux". Another time I mistakenly (stupidly?) did a "reply to all" on a viruse-infected message, and unintentionally sent a message to ALL the users of the ISP I was using at the time. The backscatter (including some which wasn't very polite) kept coming in for at least a year. The ISP has since plugged a hole that allowed that to happen.
