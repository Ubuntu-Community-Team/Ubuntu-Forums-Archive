What do I mean about the law of diminishing returns? I mean that 2 cores are not twice as good as one. 4 cores are not twice as good as 2. 8 cores are not twice as good as 4. 

You might get 50% to 75% increased efficiency on your total desktop with a dual core.....thats an estimate because it depends on what you are doing. You might get 80% or even higher for certain things like media conversion going from 1 core to 2, as dual cores REALLY helps with video conversions....but is 4 cores going to give you a 160% improvement? Nope. Cut the benefits in half before adding them. Even time you add a set of cores the benefit is not only less than a true doubling, but the overall gain is less per core the more cores you add.....Say you got 60% improvement from the first core for YOUR typical workload. You might only get a 40% improvement on YOUR typical workload going from dual core to quad core. You might only get another 20% improvement going to octo-core, and you might not even notice the difference going from 8 cores to 10. 

With our current architecture, we hit a sweet spot between quad core and octo core. For many benchmarks however, the quad cores are only 20% faster than the dual cores. We very quickly ran into the same problem as we did with clock speed, only clock speed really is 'all that' when it comes to gaming, where dual cores is more geared for office and multi-tasking and multimedia. We actually hit that wall a lot faster.


I think the Bulldozer actually brings something new to the table, above and beyond the tweaks that were made to increase efficiency of the i7. Its not just the same old architecture with more cores slapped on. Its really something new and innovative.
