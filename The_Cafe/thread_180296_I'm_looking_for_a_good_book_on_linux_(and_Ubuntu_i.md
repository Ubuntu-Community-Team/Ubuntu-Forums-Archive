### Post by confused57 on 2006-05-22
[QUOTE=batholith]Everyone, thanks for the suggestions!  Although it's unfortunate that the Unbuntu Linux book is getting "bad" reviews...  But, I probably would have loved it 1.5 years ago!

Thanks again.[/QUOTE]

I agree,  I believe it would be very helpful to the "Absolute Beginner", most everything you need to get started and learn a little about Ubuntu & Linux is covered in this book.  The main advantage is that everything someone needs to know to get started is in this one resource "manual" rather than doing forum searches, googling, etc.  I've found it has answered several questions that I wasn't able to find by doing forum searches.   The forum is great for more advanced or system specific solutions...there's usually always someone here that has the answer.
I wish I had the book when I was just beginning Ubuntu & Linux, it would have saved a lot of time & effort; and in my opinion, I think it would have made my learning curve a little "less steep".  If someone has the resources to purchase it, I recommend it for the novice; if you've been using Linux for awhile, maybe not...
Note:  It is written for Breezy, I think you may be able to extrapolate a lot of the material to Dapper?

The book does mention Windows crashing and anyone who's used Win98 will have experiences with that; WinXP has been rock-solid for me...most answers to questions in the forums still give instructions with "sudo apt-get" this or that, but by reading through the forums I've realized "gksudo aptitude" is a much better method.   My point is that the book should give the beginner a pretty good overview of how to setup & configure Ubuntu, introduction to commands, troubleshooting,etc...but the forums are indispensible for learning more advanced or better methods not covered in the book...just my opinion.

### Post by aysiu on 2006-05-26
[QUOTE=elijahclarity]Don't know why Begiining Ubuntu Linux is getting not-so-reat reviews here! I've donr reading 3 chapters and I must say THIS IS THE BEST LINUX BOOK I'VE EVER READ!! The first few chapters give u such a beautiful introduction to the world of linux and how it compares to windows that I'd recommend ths book to EVERY newbie Linux user....HIGHLY RECOMMENDED from my side:)[/QUOTE] I'll give you my reasons for not recommending it:

1. False advertising: it's not for novice to professional--it's for absolute novice only. If you've been using Ubuntu for two weeks, you won't learn much from this book.

2. False information/propaganda: I love Ubuntu as much as anyone, but claiming that Windows is unstable and will freeze your mouse cursor for no reason and Ubuntu is stable and will never do that... BS.

3. Bad practice: *sudo* in front of graphical commands--that's a no-no.

But... for absolute beginners who know nothing about Ubuntu or Linux, it may not be such a terrible thing.
