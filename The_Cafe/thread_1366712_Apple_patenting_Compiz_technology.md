### Post by klange on 2009-12-30
What's this? A thread linking to my blog about my rant? Oh dear.

Where do we begin... I guess I'll give an overview of the real story:
Back in December of 2007 I saw the famed Wii Remote head-tracking video by Johnny Lee. It was awesome, and I popped into #compiz-dev as I usually did back then and said "I'm porting this to Compiz". I didn't have a wii remote at the time, but SmSpillaz (Sam, a fellow Compiz developer, and now one of the head guys for community management two years later) did, so I wrote up the code, which took a few weeks of poking around with other plugins. This was my first functional plugin for Compiz. Eventually, we got it working and SmSpillaz recorded what is one of the worst video demos ever. This is all in January-February of 2008. A few months later, Apple files this patent. Two months after that, with the help of a forum-goer / programmer, we had it ported to a webcam with OpenCV using the OpenCV facetracking demo. It worked great, and we have videos of it in action, clearly doing its job. Eventually, I got a system with a webcam (yeah, finally, I can use my own plugin!) and recorded the last video we ever took of the thing, and that was it. It's been idle since February of this year. Eventually I moved to another (completely unrelated) community and my Compiz work was a good conversation piece. A few days / weeks before the patent went public, one of my friends on this community was rather obsessed with my work (...), and the day before this news release came out I took the time to get everything squared away to continue work and try and get my tutorials fixed up so they could actually use it. I did a git-pull that night. Now, like any other Internet denizen, I read a lot of Gizmodo / Engadget / what have you, and as with any other morning I made the rounds. And found this news article. And I got *pissed*. You see, I'm mildly bi-polar, and I'm a bit cranky in the morning to begin with, so this definitely isn't helpful. The blog post the OP linked was what resulted from an entire day of link hunting, after blowing off steam at a chemistry final (which I got a B on, and this probably didn't help that, but you won't see me contacting my college about it any time soon).

And I'll answer some questions / respond to some comments:

Sorry I didn't link that myself, I went to the MacRumors article on the matter and thought that would suffice. I was also trying to cover all of our work on the subject, not Apple's side.

> **Gizenshya said:**
> so why did you post a link to a blog instead of reputable sources?

Probably because I'm the only source for this? My code, my idea, my implementation, my rant made the same day as the patent announcement. Pretty hard to get a reputable source when no one else cares about you.

> **danielrmt said:**
> they are not patenting "compiz", but a feature compiz devs have started to implement, but was not even released yet.

I did [release it](http://cgit.compiz.org/~klange/headtracking/). Compiz has open development, all of the code is available as soon as someone hits commit, and that goes for third-party plugins that are developed in-house. Headtracking / Wii remote tracking has been available from the Compiz Git repository for quite some time. Just because we don't package it doesn't mean it hasn't been released (I can't package it anyway because you'd still need to compile the right version of OpenCV, and it's just bad form to package third-party Compiz plugins).

> **Giant Speck said:**

Hey... It's not just any useless feature, it's *my* useless feature!

> **yester64 said:**

Our code is available and the effect achieved is identical to what they describe graphically and in words in the application. For the first time ever I actually bothered to get all of the TIFF images for the patent and look them over.

I leave you people alone for a month or two and look at what happens to my blog posts. Looking forward, I'm going to eventually port the plugin to Compiz 0.9.0 (oh boy... I had just gotten everything ready to compile the day before, and had been talking to some of my friends and colleagues about it. Seeing one of the "big-bad"s has patented something you thought of and implemented when you wake up in the morning is not fun.

For the record, I really don't hate Apple so much. I own an iPod Touch and iPhone, both of which I use quite frequently. I just don't like it when they try to patent my stuff. The reason we don't patent things in the open-source world is because as soon as we release something all attempts to patent it (by someone else) are void - we want everyone to have the same chance to use the technologies we create.

This is the email I'm sending to the SFLC unless someone points out a glaring error I need to correct before I hit send:
> SFLC,

I am a part-time developer for the Compiz composite window manager, and was recently informed of a patent application by Apple Inc., filed June 17th, 2008, made public on December 17th, 2009, which covers technologies my colleagues and I designed, developed, and implemented through open-source extensions to the Compiz window manager during the January-February 2008 time-frame. The technologies in question use "head tracking" to change the perspective of a user interface to match the position of the user, specifically in order to make the stacked windows of a desktop environment appear to be three-dimensional. Our project, which began initial development in December 2007, makes use of a Nintendo Wii Remote infrared camera rig to track the position of the user, and then performs various OpenGL operations on the 3d environment provided by Compiz to produce results which we believe this patent describes.

The patent in question is available here: [http://appft.uspto.gov/netacgi/nph-Parser?Sect1=PTO2&Sect2=HITOFF&p=1&u=%2Fnetahtml%2FPTO%2Fsearch-bool.html&r=1&f=G&l=50&co1=AND&d=PG01&s1=20090313584&OS=20090313584&RS=20090313584](http://appft.uspto.gov/netacgi/nph-Parser?Sect1=PTO2&Sect2=HITOFF&p=1&u=%2Fnetahtml%2FPTO%2Fsearch-bool.html&r=1&f=G&l=50&co1=AND&d=PG01&s1=20090313584&OS=20090313584&RS=20090313584)
Our relevant open-source code developed and released before the patent application was filed is available from the Compiz Git repository: [http://cgit.compiz.org/inactive/users/klange/wiitrack/](http://cgit.compiz.org/inactive/users/klange/wiitrack/)
Video evidence of our implementation in use is also available upon request.
Though not necessarily relevant due to its development after the filing date, further updates to the project which used a webcam for tracking the user's location in place of the Wii Remote are available from this separate repository: [http://cgit.compiz.org/~klange/headtracking/](http://cgit.compiz.org/~klange/headtracking/)
