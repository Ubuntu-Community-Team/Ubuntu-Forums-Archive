### Post by DuckHook on 2023-02-14
Imagine that we live in a society where 85% of cars are manufactured by one company. You can't buy cars from this company; you can only lease them. When you do lease one, it comes with all sorts of strings attached:

[LIST=1]
[*]You cannot change any of its fundamental parts. Oh, you can add bling, change the seat pitch and maybe even choose your own wheels, but if you try to change anything in the engine compartment, you could land in jail.
[*]Never mind change—you are not permitted to even *look* in the engine compartment. This car maker has become so powerful that it has convinced lawmakers everywhere to make it illegal to lift the hood. The secret engine may not even be studied.
[*]Upon signing the lease, you surrender any right to privacy or anonymity. You must agree to their tracking where you drive, how you drive, how often you drive, why you drive and who you drive from, with and to. To grandly compensate you for this baldfaced theft of your private data, they give you free floor mats and and a monthly copy of their magazine: We Pwn You.
[*]This tracking is done via their omnipresent tracking device. It cannot be turned off and you cannot even start the engine without having the device active.
[*]It has unreliable safety gear: seatbelts are ill fitting, airbags sometimes fail to inflate and crumple zones are designed so poorly that don't protect you from side impacts.
[*]Even though they charged you money, they will take no responsibility for injury or death resulting from their poor yet secretive design.
[*]On the topic of money, the basic model is restricted to 50 kph. If you want it to go faster, you have to pay more. The engine is actually capable of greater speed but is arbitrarily throttled back to what you pay for.
[*]It drives like a wallowing swamp barge. It is heavy, clumsy and unresponsive.
[*]It is a senseless hodge podge of jumbled components. The transmission technology is several decades obsolete but is used nonetheless for the sake of "backwards compatibility".
[*]This manufacturer maintains its market dominance by making sure that the entire transportation infrastructure will work only with its car. The roads, refilling stations, street lights and even the destination parking lots are discouragingly unfriendly to any car but theirs.
[*]More recently they require you to pay them an additional fee every year to listen to the radio, turn on the heat or use the air conditioner. This too is tracked and reported back to mothership. Headlights and turn lights remain free for now out of the goodness of their tiny little hearts.
[/LIST]

There are alternatives though. In particular there is a car maker that makes an amazing Delorean&#8209;like vehicle which:

[LIST=1]
[*]You can own outright, with no strings attached.
[*]Permits you to change anything you want on it. There are no secret parts and no constraints on what you can do with it.
[*]Actually invites you to poke and prod under the hood, learn how it works and tinker to your heart's content.
[*]Even permits you to copy the car and give those copies away to your friends.
[*]Does not track you and, in strong contrast, offers all sorts of tools and options to defeat any sort of tracking.
[*]Has a good safety record and all sorts of safety gear, although it cannot protect you from your own foolishness.
[*]Has unlimited speed and power. Corners beautifully if you know what you are doing although this speed, power and nimbleness can be dangerous in the hands of the inexperienced.
[*]Is so scalable that it can run anything from motorcycles to Batmobiles to Millennium Falcons.
[*]Is absolutely free. Requires no payment of any kind. Never has and never will.
[*]Has no additional or hidden fees. No subscriptions. No surprises.
[*]Is produced and maintained by a whole community of loyal enthusiasts. You are not beholden to the whims and wiles of a single monolithic entity.
[*]Is not soiled by association with past shady conduct. You can drive it without feeling that you have to take a shower afterwards.
[/LIST]

There are some significant differences:

[LIST=1]
[*]The engine is a Back&#8209;to&#8209;the&#8209;Future fusion job. If you run into maintenance issues, all of that stuff you learned about internal combustion engines won't help one bit. May even be frustrating.
[*]The new learning curve is pretty steep.
[*]The car is built by and for car enthusiasts. Therefore, it has some bleeding&#8209;edge components that aren't as stable as those of the swamp barge.
[*]Component manufacturers build their components for the swamp barge and don't much care whether they work on this car.
[*]Help is mostly from fellow car enthusiasts. Such help is sometimes laden with jargon and an unfortunate cliqueish attitude.
[*]Your friends will give you funny looks wondering why you drive a car that must take side roads and can't meet up with them in their favourite parking lots.
[/LIST]
