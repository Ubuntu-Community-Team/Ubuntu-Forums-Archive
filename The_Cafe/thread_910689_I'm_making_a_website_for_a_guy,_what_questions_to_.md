### Post by LaRoza on 2008-09-04
[list]
[*] Who is the target audience? (Make the answers as specific as possible)
[*] What is the purpose of this site? (Again, get specific answers)
[*] What sort of maintence if any will you need?
[/list]

If any of answers to the above questions are not suitable for you, then don't take the project.

Also see if you can get a general mockup (have paper and pencils ready. Perhaps even something with colours for more specific drawings). People often underestimate the value of paper when it comes to design.

### Post by cpetercarter on 2008-09-05
Get the guy to focus on content. Content is the most important thing. Written material needs to be clear and relevant to the target audience. Pictures need to enhance the text, not detract from it. This may sound pretty basic, but I have seen many sites where all the effort has gone into slick design, and no-one has ever thought about what the site was intended to say.
