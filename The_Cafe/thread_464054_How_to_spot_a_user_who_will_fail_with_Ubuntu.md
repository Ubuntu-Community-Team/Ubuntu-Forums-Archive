### Post by lazyart on 2007-06-04
After thumbing around here for a few months and playing with Ubuntu I've noticed some signs of a user who's probably not going to make it in Ubuntu.  At least, not this time around because I think the majority of us made multiple attempts to move to Ubuntu before landing here.

**Hardware challenged.**  Has real old hardware.  They've heard that with Linux you can breathe new life into old equipment.  So he dusts off a 233 mhz system with 32 megs of ram ("It ran Win95 just fine") and wonders why the LiveCD doesn't work, if Xubuntu is really Ubuntu and if he can do the spinning cube thing after installing Puppy Linux.  Not to be confused with...
**Hardware execution challenged.** Really wants to see what this Linux thing is all about.  Downloads the CD and burns it.  Then asks "How do I make my computer boot from the CD drive?"  An hour later still doesn't know why, even after he made the change in BIOS that it still boots from the HD.  Finds out later that he burned the .iso as a file not an image.
**WINEr.** Thinks he hates Windows but loves the games he plays on it.  Burns the CD correctly, probably because he's burned a bunch of CDs he's downloaded over the years.  Within the first 5 posts he's asking "How can I run my Windows games in Linux?" or "I've heard about Wine and I want to play (insert game here) on Linux."  Within a week he comes to the stunning conclusion that "Linux will never replace Windows" before heading back to Counterstrike.

I just wonder sometimes if Ubuntu is being perceived as something that it is not. :(

### Post by buuntuu! on 2007-06-04
i think your 3 points can be boiled down to
**lazy/impatient.** (no offence intended) anybody with a *slight* understanding of how a pc works can succeed with ubuntu. the main problems that will arise are well documented (thanks to [psychocats)]("http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php") and others).
 if you take the time to read and learn:
how to burn an iso
what is partitioning all about
what are the apps i can use under linux to do what i did under windows before (i am deliberately avoiding the term "replacement" here)
...
it is possible to get your computer up and running and enjoy it more than ever with windows!

i was a complete noob when i decided to make the switch to linux and consider myself a typical end-user still, but even with limited knowledge ubuntu is a can-do!

### Post by capecove on 2007-06-04
Well, I am new to Ubuntu, and really to all distros of Linux (I had Mandrake 6 and Corel Linux for a bit but gave up on those soon after installing (LILO dual boot with Win98SE is not such a good idea)). In fact, I just installed on Friday. However, I have been in IT for 16 years and I can tell you that I believe Ubuntu is a real competitor to Windows XP/Vista. 

There is some real falling out on M$ lately. Ubuntu is a nicely built project with actual home desktop possibilities. The sad part is, like others have been saying, the main bulk of computer users out there don't have the savviness (sp?) to burn a cd. Plus, there is a real feeling out there that the command line is inferior, the GUI is king and kicking out to a prompt of any kind is evil.

I may struggle getting to know just how Linux works and may not yet know everything about the commands out at a prompt, but I know that I am willing and able to use Ubuntu 80% of the time at least. If I could only explain how excellent it is to turn a regular 5 minute power on to usable state in Windows XP SP2 into a 45 second process. Plus, and you guys will think I'm a freak I am sure but I know I have doubled my network bandwidth speed. There will be times I must use Windows, but it is possible I may not purchase another piece of software written only for Windows.

I think that those who have been using it successfully need to just keep  walking the walk. Enjoy the ride and make Ubuntu as good as you desire. Believe it or not, sometimes just positioning a piece of software to take over after the fall of a competitor is wise.
