### Post by waldopepper on 2009-05-31
I actually watched the opening episode of "The Goode Family". Some of the posters here have missed the theme of this show. It is satire, mocking the do gooders in American culture. The show opens explaining that Ubuntu was adopted 16 years ago as a baby from Africa by the Goodes, a white American couple. They did this thinking that they would give a poor black African baby a chance for a better life in America. They "forgot to check a box" on the adoption form, and they received a white baby from South African Dutch heritage. He was named Ubuntu, perhaps (this is just a guess on my part) as a spoof reference to Mark Shuttleworth, who is also a South African white person.

The show has many other stereotype references to do gooders. They drive a Prius. It has a bumper sticker WWAGD? (What would Al Gore do?) They ride bicycles everywhere. They recycle everything, sorting it all very carefully, and then we see the garbage collector dump it all in the same place.

They are vegans, and feed their dog meatless dog food. (The dog compensates by catching and eating birds and cats in the neighborhood.)

It's all pretty funny, particularly if you are not one of those do gooders.
