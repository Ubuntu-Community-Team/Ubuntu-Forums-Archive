### Post by cascade9 on 2010-03-19
Fake chips are not a new thing- I've heard of fake intel 486s, 586s, P2s, P3s and P4s, AMD K6s, K6-2s and athlons. Normally they were just 'remarked', meaning that somebody took a lower level chip and then changed the markings to a higher level (eg 486 SX 25 into 486 DX 33, 586 166 into 233 MMX, etc)

These fake i7 were around before newegg shipped them. IIRC, they started appearing a few months ago. 

Its not as nasty as the faked P4s that were around a few years ago, and possibly still are, though the poster on this thread appears to have gone quiet-

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1427257](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1427257)

At least the i7s are obvious fakes. The old 'celeron into p4' trick was nastier, they ran, and with the motherboards they were shipped with you needed to actually check with CPU-z or other tools to even have an idea they were fake...and provided you believed the BIOS over the software, you would think that everything was fine.
