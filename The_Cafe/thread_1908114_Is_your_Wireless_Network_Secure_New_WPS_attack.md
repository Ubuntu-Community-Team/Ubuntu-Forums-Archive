### Post by hackertarget on 2012-01-12
Hello,

Many people have been running WPA / WPA2 for years and are under the impression that their wireless security is pretty good.

And it has been up until a couple of weeks ago. A new type of attack that brute forces something called WPS is public and it works.

I have an Asus Wireless Router that is less than 6 months old, it came with WPS enabled by default and I was able to successfully break into it.

Have a look at this article that covers it pretty well and then take the time to check your router and see if you have WPS enabled using a PIN.

[http://www.businessinsider.com/how-to-hack-wpa-wifi-passwords-by-cracking-the-wps-pin-2012-1](http://www.businessinsider.com/how-to-hack-wpa-wifi-passwords-by-cracking-the-wps-pin-2012-1)
