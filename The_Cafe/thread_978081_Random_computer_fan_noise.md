### Post by Kernel Sanders on 2008-11-10
Ok, I was wondering what a few of you thought of this.

When I turn on my computer my power supply fan starts up as normal, in fact, everything does, and then it get's to the Windows Vista login screen, and my power supply fan starts making one HELL of a noise. It's almost screaming DO NOT WANT :lol:

So, I jab a pencil in it, stopping it from moving. I then remove the pencil a few seconds later, and all is well. The fan performs normally from then on. In fact, the whole computer does.

So:

-The power supply fan only goes crazy when the computer is booting up after being switched on.
-This only happens at the vista login screen (the sole OS on my desktop)
-Manually jamming the fan and then unjamming it returns everything to normality
-This has been going on for months now

In short therefore, would you say I need a new power supply? Since the fan is 100% awesome apart from when it's booting up after being switched on, and ONLY when it gets to the Vista login screen, I am at a loss whether to blame the fan tbh?

### Post by mips on 2008-11-11
I suspect the PSU has a variable speed fan that somewhere during boot up goes into high rpm and at that rpm it vibrates due to bad bearings or whatever.

1. You could replace the PSU

[COLOR=Red]**WARNING:**[/COLOR] Next options are **[COLOR=Red]DANGEROUS[/COLOR]** if you don't know what you are doing,

2. Remove the fan, pull the sticker in the centre covering the bearing/sleeve back, add a drop or two of penetrating oil.

3. Replace the fan with a new low noise fan.

Options 2&3 are hazardous to your health if you are not familiar with electronics/electricity. Always disconnect the mains and always bare in mind that your PSU is of the switch-mode variety that contains high voltage (400V+) capacitors that retain their charge after being disconnected from mains.

### Post by ciscosurfer on 2008-11-11
+1 to mips

So, Vista likes to load all sorts of crud *ahem* processes at the login screen, which is undoubtedly setting your fan to crazy mode.  Many Vista users complain about this.  However, I have to agree with Mr mips on this one, at least in theory.  It is quite plausible that the PSU fan is off-kilter.  While it may be cheaper to simply replace the fan inside the PSU, it's also a fairly dangerous task to an untrained professional.  In my opinion, you may as well just buy a new PSU (yes, reconnecting the cables might be a pain, but at least you'll be free from shock therapy, at least potentially...be careful either way ;-)).
