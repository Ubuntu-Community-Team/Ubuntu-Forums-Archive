I don't mean to bash OpenOffice.org in any way...  but in my humble opinion the project has become stale. Why do I say that? ### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-01-22
Office suites are developed, surprisingly enough, for office workers in enterprise.

Enterprise doesn't want something "progressive" in terms of interface, because then it costs them money for retraining and for taking support calls from people unfamiliar with this progressive interface. That's why Microsoft Office 2007 is such a shock to businesses. That's probably the biggest thing in favour of OOo, that it uses a bog-standard traditional interface that doesn't trailblaze. Microsoft Office 2007's "ribbon" is singlehandedly hampering the adoption of OOXML!

In a choice between switching to a new MS Office with new interface, and switching to something else with also a new interface, businesses will switch to MS Office 07.

In office suites, boring is where it's at.

### Post by Darkhack on 2008-01-26
The thing about the Mozilla development platform is that everything is rendered in Gecko.  In Firefox, everything, even the UI is nothing more than [XUL]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XUL") and [Javascript]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Javascript").  You can even [open Firefox inside itself]("chrome://browser/content/browser.xul").  Would XUL and Gecko make for a suitable office UI?  I don't know.  I have the feeling it might be a bit slow.

I'm all for competition, but can someone think of a practical reason why XUL would be better than a native toolkit for such development?  GTK+ and Qt are both cross platform.  There is also wxWidgets, Mono, Java, and a bunch of other solutions.

Both OpenOffice and KOffice are receiving lots of updates.  It would make a great pet project and something to do for fun but I don't see a lot of developers jumping on board because there isn't any major problem with OpenOffice nor KOffice that would inspire anyone to create a new project or fork.
