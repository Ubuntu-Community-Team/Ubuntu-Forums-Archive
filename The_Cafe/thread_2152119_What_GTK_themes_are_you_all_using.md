### Post by Copper Bezel on 2013-06-11
One of the things I really like about a lot of the Mac-type themes, though, isn't uniquely Macish or anything to do with the chrome and the button styles - it's just the way the titlebars blend smoothly with the GTK theme with no edge. I've always been a little picky about that; it's sort of a pet peeve, one of the reasons I really liked Orta, and one of the reasons I think Shell is more attractive than Unity at defaults. I don't see the need for the extra UI clutter, and it looks awful on some apps, particularly GTK2 ones, since GTK3 at least matches the toolbars to the titlebar color, which tends to make a smooth transition. For some reason, something like Ambiance, where that big, thick, heavy grey title bar just *stops *on its bottom edge without any shading, really bothers me, and there are light themes that have a titlebar that's shaded to be "rounded" on both edges, or flat in a way that looks like a "card" behind the rest of the app, which looks better, but it still seems like clutter. (Not the *package *clutter, which is fine of course. = ) And unclutter is nice, too - go thine way, silly mouse pointer getting in the way of crap I'm trying to read.)

I'd thought that OSX had a hard edge between the titlebar and toolbar - it doesn't, of course, so this *is *just another often-imitated feature of Aqua. But it's a nice one.
