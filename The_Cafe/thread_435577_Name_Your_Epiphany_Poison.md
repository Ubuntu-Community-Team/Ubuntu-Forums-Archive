### Post by kanem on 2007-05-07
> **karellen said:**
> when I open a new tab it should be blank, no with the current homepage in it (the only way I can achieve this is to use blank as homepage, which I don't like)
I also used to want this.  But think about the first thing you do when you open a new blank tab. For me, it was to either enter an address manually, or use a bookmark. For the former you can enter a new address in your *current* window, hit Ctrl-Enter, and it opens a new tab at the new address. Your current window stays where it is and the original address reappears. Or, if you are using a bookmark you can just middle click on the bookmark, and a new tab opens with at the new site.

The only thing I miss is the ability to order my  bookmarks arbitrarily instead of having to have them in alphabetical order.
