I understand the issue.  I have similar problems.  My frustration comes from certain things not quite working well in Linux, and there not being a good solution.

First is Games.  Proton has really helped with this, as most of my games now work without a hitch, and run great right from Steam.  But there are a few that don't work as well in Linux, or don't work at all, and I am forced to reboot into Windows just to accommodate one or two games.

Secondly is hardware acceleration (in the browser specifically).  This again, is a gaming feature.  I am using Google Stadia, and Nvidia GeForce Now for some of my titles that require a little more power than I currently have available.  These services work in Linux... but the browser (even Google's Chrom) doesn't support hardware accelerated decoding... so my lag is increased to an unplayable level.  However, in Windows... Edge, Chrome, Brave, etc.  They all work flawlessly, with Hardware Accel.

Third, is controller support for the Browser.  I have an 8BitDo Controller that connects via Bluetooth.  And connectivity is fantastic in Linux, but the browsers do not recognize this as a controller.  So again, I have to use Windows to use my controller.

Finally, there are some little glitches here and there.  Handbrake doesn't turn off the PC when an queue is finished... it will however go into sleep mode.  Kubuntu seems to not be able to recover from sleep mode... well...  It does, but then the compositing needs to be reset, by restarting the computer.  So, that's a no go as well.

That being said... I always struggle with the "Windows Data Collection" crap.  It actually goes further.  Telemetry is built into many aspects of Windows, and not entirely Microsoft itself.  Nvidia has telemetry built into it's drivers for Windows... so, no matter what I want to use it for, I cannot turn it all off.

There are some utilities for Windows, like "ShutUp10," that will allow you to turn on and off a lot of the background processes that cause your computer to run slower, and constantly upload information.  So, that's a plus... but you can't ever get it all.  This is why I prefer Linux.  I think.  Do I?  What's easier?  What's more functional?  What does what I need it to do?  AAAARRRRGGGGHHHH!!!!
