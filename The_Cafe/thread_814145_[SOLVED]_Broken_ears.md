### Post by Sinkingships7 on 2008-06-01
Wow. Well, I have very sensitive hearing, and would definitely consider myself a growing audiophile. Here's my story:

Once upon a time, I had all of my music at 48kbps. Yes, you heard me. 48. That is the borderline for stereo sound and mono sound. 1kbps less goes into the mono sound realm. Anywho, I had all my music at that bit rate (mp3, I should add) because I could fit more music onto my 512MB MP3 player, at about a megabyte per song. I quickly progressed out of that range and into the 128kbps range, and noticed a huge difference. I was content for a while, but I had default harmon/kardon speakers. 2.0, nothing amazing. Didn't sound great, but were okay. Then I got new speakers. A nice set of [Logitech Z-2300's.]("http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/speakers_audio/home_pc_speakers/devices/231&cl=us,en"). Boy, did that sub rock. As a result, I noticed a certain "airiness" from the towers, yet a crisp 'n' clean sound from my sub. Strange. I experimented. I graduated to 160kbps, and only a day later tried 192kbps. I noticed the difference every single time. But was this mediocere bit rate and mid-class speakers enough for me? No, I should say not. I upgraded my speakers again, this time to the [Logitech Z-5500's.]("http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/speakers_audio/home_pc_speakers/devices/224&cl=us,en") These are the speakers that I'm using right now, and have been for a half a year or so. They're gorgeous, and sound better than they look. I tried 256kbps. Still found the difference and started to find 160kbps too airy sounding. I still notice the difference between 256kbps and 320kbps, but I have no problem with 256. I CANNOT, however, hear the difference between 320kbps and Lossless format, thankfully. I'd rather my songs not be 25MB each.

And that is how I slew the dragon and saved the town.

### Post by RandomJoe on 2008-06-01
Depends on my setting.

I have a huge stack of songs on CF cards for my old MP3 player that uses them.  Since the only place I use it is while riding my bicycle down the road - and most of the time only have a single earpiece anyway! - I went for max capacity on the relatively small CF cards I had, and everything is 64kbps mono.  They don't sound all that bad, really, but there's definitely a lot missing.

Especially now it doesn't matter much, as my current laptop has absolutely hideous speakers in it.  My old Dell C840 sounded pretty darn good (for laptop speakers, being listened to in a mechanical room, that is!), but still higher quality didn't matter much.

Once upon a time, there was something called SACD and DVD-Audio.  Both HIFI audio format fought a minor battle to be the replacement of CD-Audio.  Then MP3 came along and slaughter both.


Seriously, if you got a HIFI system that cost at least a couple of grands, then you might be able to hear the benefits of the HIFI audio formats.  Better if you got this [6 millions hifi system]("http://www.likecool.com/Jeremy_Kipnis_6_Million_Home_Theater--HIFI--Gear.html"). ;-)

One thing you might notice once the sound quality went beyond CD is the "depth of field".  Those who have a good HD display might know what I'm talking about.  Between DVD video quality to HD quality, there is suddenly a realism to the picture and you can preceived an image depth.

Same thing happened in audio.  Beyond CD quality, you will start to be able to hear sounds coming from different distances.  E.g. the singer will be closer, the drummer will be farther back.  It's subtle, but noticeable.
