### Post by stalker145 on 2008-02-04
Yaaay paintball!

Probably my favorite sport. A tad chilly lately to play since I despise being hit with marbles, but it'll be warming up here soon enough :D

I'm one of those cheap guys that paid ~90USD for my kit at WalMart. It's a Spyder Xtra that the only thing done so far is a 14" rifled barrel in place of the 10-12" that came with it. I'm looking forward to throwing an HPA tank on there.

I will always believe that a good player with a cheap gun will almost always outplay a guy with all the bells and whistles who shoots their hopper in 30 seconds. Well, they're fun to bum rush when they're reloading ;)

There's a guy who plays almost every time I go out that is hilarious. He's running with an old 10-shot pump gun. That and he's a big, beefy Samoan. Runs for the dorito (we only have speedball around here) at the 50 yard line *every stinking time*. He's always my first target 'cause he's good with that gun... I guess you have to be.

### Post by aroth87 on 2008-02-08
I used to play a lot back in high school. I had a pretty decent set up, ICD BKO, Egg II, and a Crossfire HPA tank. I was pretty good too...
But I got to college and couldn't find anyone to play with and was tight on cash, so I sold everything a little over a year ago because I needed the money and didn't play anymore.
Every-so-often I long to get back on the field and play. It is a lot of fun just a little spendy.
My best story: My friends and I weren't all that bright and used to play what we called 'Civil War'. We would all fill our hoppers, go out into a field and get into two lines facing each other. The rules were you couldn't run from where you were standing and you had stand there until everyone's hopper was empty. Tons of fun, right? Well, you did gets lots of welts to show off at school for the next week. Anyway, we went and played at a smaller field one day and the refs and management were pretty laid back so we asked a group of people if they wanted to play 'Civil War' with us. No one did but the refs offered to let us play it so they could get some entertainment. So Civil War on a speedball field commenced. We had fun and everyone else had a good laugh. 

Adam

### Post by lespaul_rentals on 2008-02-10
It's a shame it's so expensive.  The thing is, markers, hoppers, nitro tanks, etc. aren't the greatest expense (maybe 500 dollars for a decent setup).  It's the balls, the field fees, and all the crap you add to your setup but know you really can't afford.

I've always been a speedball kind of person myself.  Unfortunately, the only field out in my area (Boise, Idaho) is a great place but expensive.  A day of 'ballin' will cost you around 40 dollars.

The best way to save money is to join a league that plays tournaments, and get sponsored.
