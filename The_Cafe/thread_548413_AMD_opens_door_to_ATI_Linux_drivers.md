### Post by igknighted on 2007-09-11
The community was absolutely right to be skeptical, ATI hasn't lived up to its word in the past.  But wow, I am blown away by the commitment AMD is shown to open source right now.  I almost want to sell my nvidia GPU in order to get an ATI card to show my support.  I think it is very fair to say that the ball is in nvidia's court.  They are now the least open source friendly of all the major graphics chipset manufacturers (intel of course has used OSS drivers for some time).

Note that AMD is not completely, 100% open sourcing everything.  To achieve the very top performance of 3d hardware you will need their proprietary driver.  This is understandable.  They do not own all of the code in the driver, and therefor cannot legally open-source it.  But to get 3d that can run basic 3d stuff like Beryl in the OSS driver and then having excellent (and in a month AIGLX supporting) proprietary drivers for gamers and such, AMD/ATI has clearly taken the lead.  Bravo.
