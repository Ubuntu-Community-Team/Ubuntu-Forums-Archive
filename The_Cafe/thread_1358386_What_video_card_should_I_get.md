### Post by Nerd King on 2009-12-20
ATI hardware has better build quality, but at this point NVIDIA has MUCH better drivers. As in, they're much more usable. The NVIDIA drivers are pretty much perfect in that they handle everything glitch-free. The downside is that they're proprietary which means you have to wait for them to update to match new kernels etc. Not such a problem on ubuntu as they tend to synchronise things, but on a rolling release like Arch it's a pain in the bum. The open drivers aren't that great yet.
ATI's proprietary drivers have come a long way, and are pretty usable, but many glitches exist on them that don't happen on NVIDIA or INTEL. The interesting development is with the open-source drivers, which have come on leaps and bounds. They lack the glitches of the proprietary drivers but at this point they're a little slow. That's improving, and will get better over time. The .32 kernel + Xorg-edgers gives coolness.
So.. NVIDIA for right now, ATI for longer-term.
