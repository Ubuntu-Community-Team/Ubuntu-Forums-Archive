No, it isn't that simple.  Anyone who is 20 yrs or older and hasn't seen the Star Wars movies isn't enough of a fan to be saved from spoilers.  If you are a parent, you have a duty to provide the opportunity for your 10 - 20 yr old children to see the movies.  Just like we don't spoil the "Easter Bunny" or "Santa Claus" fantasies for children, we shouldn't ruin the main plot twists in Star Wars - at least not in the first 6 movies - for younger people.

But at age 20, expecting everyone in the world NOT to reference key pop culture references they don't know is asking too much.  There are lots and lots of pop culture referenced used daily that help people recognize who is a fan of the same types of TV/Movies as they are.  "*Hey, hey, hey, hey-now. Don’t be mean; we don’t have to be mean, cuz, remember, no matter where you go, there you are.*"  This isn't not a meditation reference, it is a pop culture reference.  If you don't know it, that's fine.  You aren't a fan.
