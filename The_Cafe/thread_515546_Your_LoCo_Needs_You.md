### Post by Spr0k3t on 2007-08-02
LoCos are a way to get involved in the education and proliferation of Linux in general.

More about what a LoCo is: ([https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoTeams](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoTeams))
LoCos are not just groups of Ubuntu users... and they are not just LUGs, instead they are an extension of the LUG (Linux/Unix/BSD User Groups) to help build the awareness of Ubuntu.

The number of LoCos are massive: ([https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoTeamList](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoTeamList))
Just the other day an article was posted on The Fridge ([http://fridge.ubuntu.com/node/1063](http://fridge.ubuntu.com/node/1063)) discussing the mass movement in these LoCos.  I was online with someone just last night who started a LoCo and saw 14 new users in a matter a 24 hours.  I believe this momentum can be harnessed globally.  It's a way of giving back to Ubuntu and helping those around you become aware of.

If you want to get involved but don't want to start one... be the first to have something available for your area.  Natural leaders will help push the team to a better front.  If you want help finding a team, don't hesitate to ask!

### Post by nickburns on 2007-08-02
I can tell you why I am nervous about attending / joining.  It seems that many enthusiasts talk in acronyms. ( like "LUG" and such)  It can be a bit intimidating to meet with people that have a intimate knowledge of something that you are just learning about.  And some (not all) are not the best at treating you like a new guy should be treated.

A good example is a acquaintance of mine, he will rattle off something like, "your not building your kernel from scratch every day", then stare at me like I'm an idiot.  Then follow it up with some new 50 character command line that I should be using before brushing my teeth each morning.  It is hard for a new guy to fit into a room with people like this.

Don't get me totally wrong, I love the forums and have professed my love and thanks for this great learning environment.  This is a safe place to learn, especially the absolute beginers talk.

Either way, I think that what stops new guys like me is the "I'm scared of someone intimidating me while I learn this operation system".
