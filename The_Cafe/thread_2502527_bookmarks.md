### Post by TheFu on 2024-11-19
Sorry, but I don't see any question or tutorial to help.  Maybe I'm just missing something in the post that is perfectly clear to others?  IDK.

A friend stores his bookmarks with notes in **neovim** text files.  He uses fgrep to search them fast.

I don't use bookmarks very often. Only have about 10 total. 

For things I want to save as reference, I'll use "**wallabag**" to grab the webpage and save it into my wallabag server.  [https://github.com/wallabag/docker](https://github.com/wallabag/docker) is the easiest way to get it running, I suppose.  I used the normal install, which was pretty easy and light.  [https://www.howtoforge.com/how-to-install-wallabag-on-ubuntu-22-04/](https://www.howtoforge.com/how-to-install-wallabag-on-ubuntu-22-04/)  

After it was initially setup, updating is just 3 easy commands, once a month.  
```
cd /var/www/wallabag/html
sudo -u www-data /bin/bash
make update

```That's it.  In fact, it has been a about a month since the last time I updated.... Just did another update. It worked and took less than 1 minute. Now on Wallabag v2.6.10 (released Nov 3, 2024)  I'm using SQLite, not mariaDB/MySQL.  The sqlite DB file is just 11MB in size for the 1,346 of articles in the system.

Articles in Wallabag can never disappear or be updated, until I remove it from MY server.  Remember Google's "Read it later"?  That's what wallabag is, just self-hosted. Keep articles you want for reference. Delete those that were interesting just once.  Annotations and notes are supported in the system too.  

Did I mention that Wallabag is multi-user, so other people in your household have their own accounts, their own stash of saved webpages, their own annotations?  

Anyway, no need for bookmarks anymore for static information. Can't help with dynamic pages, sorry. You'll just get a snapshot.  Also, some websites can't be grabbed, but most can.
