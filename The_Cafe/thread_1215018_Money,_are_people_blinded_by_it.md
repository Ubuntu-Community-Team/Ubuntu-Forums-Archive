@OP:

Meaning absolutely no offense nor anything personal, but you're still a kid. I know you've probably heard this a zillion times, but you're not yet old enough to fully understand and grasp the world around you. It also doesn't help that your school system, just like ours over here, has an agenda of their own that's to the political benefit of other groups, and not yours as a student.

I'm not trying to suggest in any way that we should not care about the planet we live on. Far from it: if we really were to screw it up, obviously we'd all be up you-know-what creek. However, the problem is that you have to pick your battles. I mean, if we kill ourselves off due to starvation caused by abject poverty, then none of us will be around to have this discussion in the first place.

Admittedly that's a bit of an extreme, but I'm just trying to illustrate the point.

I don't personally believe we realistically have the wherewithal to harm this planet (short of a prolonged massive nuclear war many times the size of one we could actually wage at this point). I do believe we have the ability to harm ourselves, and have very demonstrably and provably done so (workplace chemical hazards, contaminated drinking water, etc.) in specific instances. That being said, we also need to understand what we're actually doing and respond to that, and not some perceived "Oh, we're going to destroy the world!" kind of scare mongering which many various different government and multi-national agencies are very good at ginning up.

Moreover, consider the instructions you're given by the attendants when you board an aircraft. They tell you if you're with a small child to put the breathing mask on yourself first *and THEN* help your child. That's not because airlines hate children, but rather to help ensure the passengers have their heads in the game and their priorities straight. Likewise, that's what's happened with your parents.

What you should really be asking is why the "not friendly (so-called)" choice costs so much less. Was it made in your country or imported from somewhere else? If it was imported (and that's a fair bet) then from *where* was it imported? China? India? Bangladesh? And do you realize what folks in those countries get paid? So, would it not make more sense for you to be concerned about -- and ostensibly seek to rectify the situation -- exploitation of workers instead of merely myopically focusing on possible or potential harm being done to the environment?
