I know it's a lot harder to find a job fresh out of college in these economic times.  But my advice to you is to look in the field of your expertise.  If you know absolutely nothing about the line of work you're applying for, then you probably won't get the job.  There are too many people competing that already know all about it.

If you really graduated from college (which spelling must not have been your major :) ), then you obviously have to be somewhat knowledgeable in some field.  That's where you need to be looking.  There is too much competition out there to try to get a job for which you have nothing to offer.

If you're looking for a job, and the interviewer has to be a psychologist or philosopher to figure out what you're trying to say, that's not going to do you any good.
