### Post by Gizenshya on 2010-01-10
Well, first you should competely master your kana.  There is a nice program called kana test available from the repositories that quizzes you on them.  It isn't really intended to teach you kana, only quiz your knowledge of them, but it is a good guage for where you are.  It's certainly worth a download.

I don't know about kanji, though-- to quiz or learn.  I'd be interested in one if there is.

I don't know where you are with your kanji studies, but it is best to start with the simple characters.  They come up very often (as they are usually the simplest concepts), and they are also often the radicals that form more complex characters.  And if you don't remember a compolex character, you can usually gets hints to its meaning from the radicals that might refresh your memory.

Make sure you memorize both the onyomi and kunyomi.  And you'll never actually commit them to memory unless you start writing using them.  You can write papers, notes, or whatever, just use them!

Also, don't fall into the habit of just seeing meaning when you look at them.  Make sure you 'read' them so you commit the readings as well.  Plus, it comes in handy when you want to type them :p

### Post by virusiidx on 2010-01-10
I highly recommend the book, "Remembering the Kanji" by James W. Heisig. [http://www.amazon.com/Remembering-Kanji-Complete-Japanese-Characters/dp/4889960759](http://www.amazon.com/Remembering-Kanji-Complete-Japanese-Characters/dp/4889960759) The first book goes though about 2000 of the most used Kanji from Newspaper, Books, Sites, etc... It also teaches the stroke order as well (which is also very important). The 2nd book goes through pretty much the 2nd half of the Kanji (which isn't used as often). He also has a book on the Katakana/Hiragana as well, but those are nothing compared to Kanji. :P

If you're serious about learning the Kanji, this is the best method by far. Do yourself a huge favor and check it out. You might even be able to find a PDF of it somewhere. ;)

Basically, it uses a technique by creating a story and pictograph of each Kanji and goes in a spcific order to make it easier to remember. You have to go in order, or the story won't make sense, so you can't just jump to a later page. It doesn't teach you the Japanese hiragana associated with the word, but it teaches you the meaning. Once you learn the meaning of them and the hiragana translation, it's no problem to link them up. 

Once you get the book, sign up for this site: [http://kanji.koohii.com/](http://kanji.koohii.com/)
It's tied to the book to help study the Kanji so you can see others' story/pictographs they came up with to help strengthen your memory. 

You can also download and print some Flash Cards associated with the book as well. Link: [http://www.polarcloud.com/kanji](http://www.polarcloud.com/kanji)

I've been using this book to learn about 25-50 Kanji a day and I can recall 95%+ with ease with only having to review them no more than a few times. 

Anyways, hope that helps. Good luck!

Edit: For those looking to learn Japanese words in general, Smart.fm's Japanese Core 2000 course is pretty good. Teaches the main core 2000 words that are most often used. [http://smart.fm/series/3318](http://smart.fm/series/3318)
