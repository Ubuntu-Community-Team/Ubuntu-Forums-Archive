### Post by nabl on 2008-09-03
I know that there are some computer manufacturers, such as System76, that make custom Linux machines, but from what I have seen, their products aren't very unique (or aesthetically appealing in general). In addition, other manufacturers' products, even those that are capable of running Linux, still lack the uniqueness that would come with an entirely custom Linux computer. Therefore, I was thinking of what a great laptop would look like, and here's what I came up with...

I am a huge fan of Apple's minimalistic design approach, so I used the Macbook as a template for the quick mock-up that I made. I think that the Ubuntu logo lends itself well to this kind of branding, and that the much-debated color scheme looks great on a laptop. Even for those who don't solely use Ubuntu (I am one), this computer would be very attractive--the Ubuntu branding doesn't take away from it, as most people would likely appreciate the Ubuntu philosophy, whether or not it is their distribution (or OS, for that matter) of choice. Obviously, this wouldn't be a direct ripoff of the Macbook, but I do think that it is certainly a good place to start.

So, without further ado, here is my (very quickly made) mock-up. What do you think about such a laptop? Would you buy one at a reasonable price? What design changes would you make, if any?
