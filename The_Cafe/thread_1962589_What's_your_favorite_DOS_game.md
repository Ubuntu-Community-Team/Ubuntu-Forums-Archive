### Post by Myrddin Emrys on 2012-04-22
I go back to the Sinclair ZX81 and 3D Monster Maze, the nearest thing to Quake in 16k:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_Monster_Maze](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_Monster_Maze)

Later it was the Spectrum, and games like Fairlight:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fairlight_%28video_game%29](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fairlight_%28video_game%29)


On DOS, I have fond (but now rather vague!) memories of Sierra's Space Quest III, the earliest decent PC game I recall playing:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Quest_III:_The_Pirates_of_Pestulon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Quest_III:_The_Pirates_of_Pestulon)

Back in the 386 days I liked Frontier, the sequel to Elite:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontier:_Elite_II](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontier:_Elite_II)

and one of the Microprose Stealth Fighter slight sims, F117a:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Night_Hawk:_F-117A_Stealth_Fighter_2.0](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Night_Hawk:_F-117A_Stealth_Fighter_2.0)

Then it was the Wing Commander series. The first game was installed from floppies and ran on the 386, but by the time IV was released, it was an elaborate multi-CD, multimedia production starring Mark Hamill and Malcolm McDowell in cutscenes, but still running on DOS:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wing_Commander_IV:_The_Price_of_Freedom](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wing_Commander_IV:_The_Price_of_Freedom)

Rowan's terrific WWI flight sim, Flying Corps, with physics that seemed realistic at the time and nice 3dfx graphics (remember them?), also deserves a special mention:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flying_Corps](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flying_Corps)

After that, major games actually started to run under Windows...
