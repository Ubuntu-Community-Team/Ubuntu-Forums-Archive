### Post by Kingsley on 2007-09-20
> 
Long expected, Google has officially taken the wraps off of its latest enhancement to its web based [Google Docs]("http://docs.google.com/"): presentations. For those not keeping score, this means that Google (GOOG) now has a web-based word processor, spreadsheet, and a presentation tool.
 Seems like only yesterday when they claimed to not be going after Microsoft’s (MSFT) Office monopoly…. Judging from the crowd here at TechCrunch40, Google’s web based “suite” has made significant inroads into the early adopter set - several dozen in the crowd of about 500 here raised their hands earlier admitting they were users. No, the new presentation tool can’t do everything that PowerPoint does (no drawing of geometric shapes, for example.)[http://thebrowser.blogs.fortune.com/2007/09/18/google-adds-presentations-to-google-docs/](http://thebrowser.blogs.fortune.com/2007/09/18/google-adds-presentations-to-google-docs/)

This is very nice. Kids in middle school can now work on Powerpoints together without actually having to be next to each other. I wish this was around 7 years ago.
