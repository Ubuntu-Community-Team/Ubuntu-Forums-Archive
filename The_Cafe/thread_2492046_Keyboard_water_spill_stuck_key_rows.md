### Post by grahammechanical on 2023-10-29
I suggest buying a USB keyboard. If you take this laptop out and about you could by a portable - flexible USB keyboard. They do not cost much. I suggest doing that before you take the laptop apart.

There are dissolved solids in water. When the water has evaporated those solids are left behind on the circuit board and between the connections. They also are a source of short circuits. Years ago I used to fit car alarms. Sometimes water would get into the box hold the electronics. This was in the days of transistors. Well before the invention of integrated circuits. Even with the the alarm circuits dried out there would still be a short circuit due to those residue solids. Usually, calcium.

Regards

### Post by TheFu on 2023-10-29
> **1fallen said:**
> instead of guessing here, A repair shop is my suggestion, and not what you wanted to hear.
If it turns out to be just the keyboard they aren't that expensive any more.

For Apple, I can't imagine them charging US$1000 for a replacement keyboard.  Perhaps $200, thanks to the "Apple Tax".  It might be possible to connect a USB keyboard and use that instead.  I saw a $30 88-key USB mechanical keyboard recommended just a few days ago.  I'm not a fan of using bluetooth stuff, since security of those things is terrible.  I suppose that's something each person needs to decide for themselves.

While my laptop needs a replacement keyboard and that was $33, the time to replace it and attempt to fix the plastic "rivets" holding in was an additional $100.  So, to replace a keyboard in a laptop that is worth $200 total would cost $130.  They do sell an entire keyboard/touchpad assembly for $90, which would prevent breaking the plastic rivets and should result in a like-new situation, but the replacement labor is $50 (rather than $100) because everything has to be removed inside the laptop (battery, storage, radios, and motherboard BEFORE the keyboard can be replaced.

Very few of the "thin" laptops have easy keyboard replacements. By thin, I mean sub-1inch (2.54cm).  That's the price we pay for "thin", it seems.  I miss the days of unscrewing 6 screws and popping the keyboard out from the front like my old 1.25in thick Dell laptop. Less than 5 minutes to replace it.  Plus the replacement keyboard was $35 and the touchpad wasn't included or needed.

I-Fix-It probably has a video to show a full teardown for all the popular laptops.  They often show just the teardown ... and if it takes over 15 minutes to get to the keyboard, expect $100+ in labor.

I have washed and cleaned and dried a full mechanical  IBM 101M keyboard (not a laptop) a few times. The trick is to completely ensure there's ZERO liquid left anywhere. 

Use alcohol on the electronic parts, not the removed keys.  Rubbing alcohol is fine, but any isopropyl alcohol over 70% will work.

Using a drop dish washing liquid in the sink and old toothbrush to get into the crannies after removing all the keys was important.  When I think it is try, I give it another day.  This is all before reassembling the keyboard.  For drying, I use towels the first day, a hair drier the second day and leave a fan running over the keyboard electronics for 3 days straight after drying it. If there is any chance there's liquid remaining, don't connect it.  

Laptops each have slightly different membranes and contacts. With an Apple, there will be 500 videos for cleaning it on youtube.
[https://www.gadgetreview.com/how-to-clean-under-macbook-keyboard](https://www.gadgetreview.com/how-to-clean-under-macbook-keyboard)
Of course, Apple says to use just compressed air to blow out crumbs. Not really a solution for sticky things.
[https://www.wikihow.com/Clean-a-MacBook-Pro-Keyboard](https://www.wikihow.com/Clean-a-MacBook-Pro-Keyboard) has multiple steps from banging out the crumbs, blowing compressed air, using a damp microfiber, removing the small, medium, then larger keys and soaking those in warm dish water, using isopropyl alcohol on the key switches. Be gentle and don't use too much liquid.
