### Post by Kale_Freemon on 2015-11-25
[COLOR=#141823][FONT=helvetica]This is what happens when you're a computer enthusiast, have spare parts laying around, and are bored: you make a homemade WiFi extender ***powered by Ubuntu***. lol
[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#141823][FONT=helvetica]Pretty simple. Used Ubuntu Linux on my old Gateway laptop to bridge the WiFi connection from a USB WiFi adapter to the Ethernet port on the laptop. Plugged into the laptop Ethernet is an old wireless router that was laying around, being useless.
[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#141823][FONT=helvetica]DD-WRT does not support my old Belkin router, and the default firmware doesn't support WiFi repeating, otherwise I'd have gone that route.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=#141823][FONT=helvetica]
So, now, there's two WiFi networks in the house. But, so long as I have a good connection upstairs now, I do not care. lol
