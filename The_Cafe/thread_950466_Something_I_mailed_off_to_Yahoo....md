### Post by bigboyman2 on 2008-10-17
So, I was in my yahoo inbox (not my main e-mail address), and I came across a messenger all-star letter. For those who are unfamiliar, Yahoo messenger all-stars are users that have been with a yahoo e-mail address and profile for X amount of years. I had become an all-star when I still had vista on my laptop. And even though I'm using linux and the chat client kopete, I'm still considered an all-star. Though, I'm kind of irked at the fact that we don't have a full (or partial build) of native Yahoo support. So I sent an e-mail off. There's the text in quote form, sorry for it being so long.

[quote=bigboyman2]If this is not the correct e-mail, please forward to the appropriate
person or department. I just didn't see a generic "feedback"
button.
Or, my apologies if I missed it. That being said, I just have a few
questions regarding the messenger. And please, read the whole e-mail.
(In my experience with dealing with companies, the e-mail is scanned
for keywords, and a canned, copy-pasted response
 is sent off)


I have been using this yahoo account for quite some time. I'd bet
money that you would know better than I on how long I've had this
account. When I first signed up, I was using Windows XP, and all was
well. I was enjoying talking with my friends, and a buddy of mine who
had left for the Navy. I had a couple different desktops, and it's
always worked better and better with each iteration (release).

When the summer of 2007 rolled around (if I recall correctly) I got my
first laptop. It was pre-loaded with windows Vista. Now I didn't like
it at first. But I tried it, it seemed to work okay. Until about 2
months later. I had done all the appropriate steps, defragging,
running anti-virus, anti-spyware, keeping the firewall up, but the
laptop would have none of it. It just refused to work as it once did.
I eventually got fed up with it all. I took some advice on the Ubuntu
forums, and
 installed that distribution of Linux. I am amazed and
overjoyed on how well it works. If anything goes wrong, it's fixed in
a global update, overnight, or I can fix it myself by going into the
system.

One thing I regret about that, is that there is no native linux
support for Yahoo messenger. True, I can use a chat client like Kopete
and send/receive messages, but not all functions work. I can send
files, but I can't receive them. This is where you may come into play.
I know it can't be done overnight, and I halfway expect this to go
directly into the trash. But it can't hurt, eh?

Basically, me and a lot of other users in the open-source community
would love if you released a linux native Yahoo messenger. If you
provide the source code (not to me, I don't code) to the general
public, you can have many improvements made, even if it isn't perfect
on the first release. The open source community is a great
 thing.
Instead of invisible engineers who provide little feedback on how a
system works, you can have many citizens and potential investors
helping out and potentially make Yahoo, as a whole, better.

I'm not going to provide a thesis on why it should be done, and I'm
not going to cite everything I've said. These are my words. I'm pretty
sure someone has said them before. But I'm saying them to you.
I still have Vista on my new laptop, and I keep yahoo messenger on it
when I do need all the functions. But it is a pain booting into vista,
just for a chat application. Many people would appreciate the gesture.
I'll leave it to you to decide what to do

Best regards

-Joel (a.k.a bigboyman2), Yahoo Power User, and Messenger All Star
[/quote]

I got a response back. Not much, but better than "We will never support linux due to so-and-so reason"

[quote=Melissa]Joel,
Thanks for your feedback! I'll pass it along.

-Melissa[/quote]

Thoughts? I enjoy criticism, so feel free to nitpick my e-mail. Just wondering how many of us would like this as well

:)

Also, if this is in the wrong forum, feel free to move it to somewhere more appropriate. I'll learn, I promise =p
