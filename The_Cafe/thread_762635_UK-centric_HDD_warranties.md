### Post by gn2 on 2008-04-22
The reason Seagate have a five year warranty is to try to retain customers.

Ready made external hard drives are usually poorly designed.
Hard drives are designed to be used lying flat and the right way up...

The common design practice of having them standing vertically to save space is a major contributing factor to them failing, as is leaving then on 24/7 and on a desk in sunlight.

My advice would be to buy a Raidsonic Icybox (aka Nanopoint) and fit a Hitachi or a Samsung hard drive in it.

Better yet, get a network drive enclosure.

### Post by jken146 on 2008-04-22
If I remember correctly, the Sale of Goods Act gives you the right to return your drive to Amazon, even though you opened it.  Within the first six months after purchase, if you return an item for being faulty or defective, the onus is on the retailer to demonstrate that the fault was not present at the time of purchase.

You might get somewhere complaining more to Amazon.  I suspect the manufacturer's warranty may be voided because you opened the dirve's case.
