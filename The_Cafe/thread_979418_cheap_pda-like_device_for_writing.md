### Post by joninkrakow on 2008-11-12
This may sound outrageous, but what about an Apple Newton Messagepad 2100? You can add BlueTooth to it to move your text files back and forth. It has awesome handwriting recognition (just print, don't do cursive--although, it _can_ learn your cursive, and it's good at it, most people don't have the patience to teach it). It also has great battery life, and its notepad app is as simple as they come, yet powerful. It has the ability to create heirarchical outlines, including the ability to move each point both in and out and up and down. I used my Newton for years for taking notes during lectures and conferences. Back then, I used a computer that could sync with Newtons, but with BlueTooth, you can transfer your files back and forth. Just do your homework (tons of info on the web, and an email list [www.newtontalk.net](www.newtontalk.net) for asking questions) before you purchase anything. You will also want a computer with a serial connection and a serial "dongle" to connect your Newton the first time, so you can install the necessary software (or buy one already tricked out with the software--and hardware). If you need, you can also find a keyboard for the Newton, and type, but I tended to just write on the screen, myself. ;-)

-Jon
