### Post by toupeiro on 2008-10-15
First off, I am a big Ford fan, and I have several reasons for that.  


If you're driving a late model car, many parts today that are Honda, Toyota, and Mitsubishi are Manufactured at the same plants, using many of the same materials that Ford, GMC, and Dodge parts come from.  So, for the sake of example, if I am going to pay 100 dollars for a Ford starter, and 500 dollars for a Toyota starter, and they were made at the same damn plant, which do you really think makes more sense?  The whole "american cars can't take the miles" was applicable from about 1972 - 1993.  I'd take a Ford Fusion and put it up against a camry for longevity any day...

Why are most domestics cheaper than imports in the US.  Don't fool yourself into thinking it has everything to do with quality.  Why is the ford starter only $100, and a toyota in the same class closer to $500? Because, most of Fords parts have longer manufacturer lifecycles, whereas many other companies engineer parts much more frequently, even expendable parts like Starters, therfore it costs more to supply so many different variations of a starter.  

Ford will have 1 type of a starter for 4cyl engines, 1 type of a starter for 6 cyl engines, 1 type for 8cyl, and 1 type for Diesel, and run those designs anywhere from 5-8 years before re-evaluating.  They may revisit engine design, firing system, fuel delivery and distribution, and exhaust more frequently, but they only re-engineer what they have to, that is how they tend to keep their costs down.

What I am saying is, just because its cheaper in cost doesn't mean its any lesser in quality anymore.  If you want something exceptional, you have to get into price limits most people cant, or don't want to afford.

Older fords are great because parts are cheap, they are made very well, and they are very easy to work on and everything is very accessable.

My non-ford history?

1993 Mitsubishi Eclipse:  Threw Engine rod through the block over 1000 Miles away from home.

1969 Torino GT: Numbers matching car!!!  Doing performance restoration for the last 5 years.  Original Engine, transmision, brake system and electrical system were in perfect working condition before starting restore.  No major incidents.
