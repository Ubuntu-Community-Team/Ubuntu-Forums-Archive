### Post by uraldinho on 2008-05-15
I don't get it.... I know indexing is processor intensive, and grows exponentially with the amount of data being indexed, so the indexing part of google is probably very energy intensive. But the query itself shouldn't consume much, probably a very small fraction of the 11 watt is the query itself. So the more we query the lower the energy per query ratio. 

What I don't get is, youtube is a simple storage based retrieval. There is no processing involved in youtube video retrieval. The client sends a request and the servers fetches the video from the file system and sends it to the client. No complex operations. So I would expect youtube to use a fraction of a google indexing. I do admit, a video will consume network resources rather than server resources, but the routers are implemented in hardware and should be extremely efficient. 

I think the 11 watt claim is based on estimates and not on actual facts. It's rather a large number. The article starts with 11 watts, then it says between 2 and 8, and finally it says let's assume the average is 4.5 watts. In my opinion even 4.5 watts is a large number.
