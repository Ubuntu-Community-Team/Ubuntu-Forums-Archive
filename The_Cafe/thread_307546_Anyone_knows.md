### Post by junglepeanut on 2006-11-26
All my opinion below. So sorry if it feels like I am saying bad deal becasue that is not the case, I want it to say I would not do that. 

I do not consider that cheap, yes, it is not expensive, but you would be paying more for something that may not be worth it later. 

In fact I have seen laptops similar to that given to people for free to learn Linux on. 

Considering the range you are looking at, tell me this, what would you like to do with this laptop? School work? Surf the web? Program? Your answer to what you want to do with it may answer more whether this is the right laptop or not.

I think the 700MHz is not so bad but definitely add more memory if possible for the same price. Also make sure it has the power cord, and the battery has a warranty. Often older laptops batteries will not hold a charge, then you have a laptop that has to be plugged in all the time, that is not fun.

Also does it have a CD drive? A DVD drive? Does it have enough USB plugs? Does it have ethernet? Is the modem real or a winmodem? Try to figure out all these questions before purchase to make sure you are happy with what you get.
