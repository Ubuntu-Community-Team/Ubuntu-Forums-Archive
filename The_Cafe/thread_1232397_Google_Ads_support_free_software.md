### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-08-05
How many people have noticed that Google Ads appear on lots of free / open source software websites?

Not just application home pages (e.g. [FreeNAS]("http://freenas.org/")), but general info (e.g. [Distrowatch]("http://distrowatch.com/")) and particularly documentation projects and news blogs (e.g. [Ubuntu Geek]("http://www.ubuntugeek.com/"), [nixCraft]("http://www.cyberciti.biz")) too.  The examples above are all sites I personally frequent fairly regularly (and software projects I use).

The issue of monetizing the web and creating an internet economy is now old news - subscription services generally failed, with ad-based income taking its place.

Unfortunately for people commiting time and money to these projects, free software advocates are likely to be more concerned about security, and use NoScript, or be aware of Adblocker etc.  I have recently been browsing without either of the above addons (after a fresh Jaunty install), and have decided to support sites by at least looking at (i.e. having them in my field of view) the ads.

How many people here pay any attention to Google Ads?  How many block them?  Or would you rather be free of the ads and pay a subscription or donation etc?
