### Post by ticopelp on 2009-12-06
Focus on Runescape and try to come up with something unique that adds value for fans of the game. 

An unfocused forum / chat board has almost nothing to offer the average user. There are hundreds of thousands of places to discuss "whatever comes to mind." Unfocused blogs aren't going to draw traffic either, unless the people blogging are working on something interesting. Blogging about your day is of interest to almost no one beyond one's immediate circle of friends. Everything that's irrelevant, throw out. It's noise. 

Give people a reason to come to your site that they can't necessarily get at any number of other sites. Generate interesting content and try to get the word out about it. One viral video or popular meme with your name on it can draw thousands to your site. Most will leave, but a few will stay.

Promote where you can, but don't spam. If you go to a community and try to poach their users without meaningfully participating or demonstrating that you want to also be a part of *their* community in return, they will sense that you're just shilling, and will usually reject you. Work on trying to find people who share the interests and goals of the site, rather than "more users" -- more doesn't necessarily mean better, especially if you're not really selling anything.

Just my two cents.
