### Post by Tucatts on 2007-05-21
Well, lets see. I have two dual booted machines in my office. XP on both and Dapper on one and Feisty on the other. To be honest they both work fine. I had a bunch of xorg problems with Dapper but have not had that in a while now and besides it broke so much I got to be a bit of an expert in fixing it, lol. Feisty has been very stable, all the multimedia stuff works, cd and dvd burning, printing, etc. I have had Dapper for over a year and it is also very stable now. I can't tell a lot of difference between the two. I might mention that both are on a router but wired. I have not used Ubuntu for a wireless connect yet but Feisty seems to have had better press on that and I bet it would work better. The only big problem I have with Ubuntu is I need to use a scanner in my sales job. Not often but man when I need it I need it, so not being able to get my Brother or visioneer scanners  to work is a problem Also need active sync for my pocket pc phone and while Feisty seems to be set up for a PDA dock it does not seem to work with win mobile 5.0. I am about 95% there with Ubuntu but not 100% yet. Still I do most of my computer work with Ubuntu and only reboot into xp when it is absolutely necessary.
So, I guess you can give Feisty a try or go with the old saying" if it ain't broke don't fix it" lol
Have tried freespire, linspire, pclinuxos etc and always come back to Ubuntu.
Tucatts
