Topic:- New album on the way, But how would you like to pay? if at all?

Like the topic says I've started work on the new album and have been toying around with alternate ways of generating income for the band and different licencing and business models.

The current way we work is, -and some folks might prefer it -is that you buy an mp3, or a CD, and thats that. You personally cannot copy that (legally) and we take a one-off fee for that music, which we licence to you to enjoy as you see fit.

However, this model is no longer working for smaller bands such as us so I've been thinking about some alternatives.

Alternative one.

The Creative Commons Licence.

We give away all our music for nothing,- we retain all the credits to the songs, but people can copy, share and generally spread around the music as much as they like. The payback we hope is that more people come to gigs, buy t-shirts etc. Essentially the music works as an advert for the me and the band.

Alternative Two.

The Creative Commons Licence + subscription.

This is similar to the first alternative except there is an on-going yearly fee to people who want to download our music. I'm currently thinking a small fee of around two quid, for access to the entire library.

This would at least help with the cost of hosting all this music and also the hope is enough people would get to hear the music and like it that there would be a surplus at the end of the year which would fund the band to do further stuff.

Of course at it's creative commons the music could be spread around, copied, shared as much as you like,- you wouldn't actually *have* to pay,- but the hope is that you enjoy the music and appreciate it's worth and feel that a few quid a year is a small price to pay to ensure it continues.

I'm open to other suggestions, please post them if you have them. :)
