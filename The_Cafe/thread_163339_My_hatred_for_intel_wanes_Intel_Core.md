### Post by Mathias-K on 2006-04-20
Indeed, according to Anandtech the upcoming Conroe will be miles ahead of anything K8. A whopping 40% in gaming. Have you read the AM2 tests? I have seen gains in performance of more than a few percent.

In my logic (as a hardware reviewer and frequent reader of various hardware buzz) Conroe seems to be it in 2006. I would not be so keen about combining Intel CPUs with nVIDIA chipsets. The stability under pressure from the nForce4 IE was poor compared to that of Intels own 955X chipset.

I think the hottest combo will be a lowly clocked dual core Conroe, a half-cheap board and 2GB value RAM.
