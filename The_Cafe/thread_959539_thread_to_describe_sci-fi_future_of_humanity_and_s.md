### Post by Paqman on 2008-10-26
> **Martiini said:**
> In japan there are fullsize factories with robots and only with 5-10 engineers that maintain the robots.


There are also a lot of factories that have got rid of their robots and put humans back on the assembly line. Humans are much more versatile and smart than robots. 

A line using robots can generally only produce one thing. One using humans can produce a whole range of similar products (as long as you get the supply side nailed, that is) Plus, human workers can spot problems and adapt much more easily than robots. So some factories have found they work much better with meatbags on the line instead of robots.

Bottom line is that some tasks suit automation, and some just don't.

AI is an interesting subject though. Some people (such as Roger Penrose) have argued extensively that digital computers will never be able to "think", as human thought is non-algorithmic. If you believe all the "singularity" nutters we're going to find out just how smart machines can get within a generation or so. Personally, i'm not that worried that my toaster will be outfoxing me any time soon. Moore's Law is not, as some people seems to think, an actual law of physics.
