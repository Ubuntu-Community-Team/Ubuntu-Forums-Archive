Most people think that centered ball is easier to control. I agree with them. Unfortunately most of the centered trackballs don't have a scroll wheel (ie: marble mouse). BUT I must say there is a much better solution than a scroll wheel: If the centered trackball has >=3 buttons, X server lets you configure the 3rd button as a scroll modifier. Meaning, when you press and hold the 3rd button, the ball works as a scroll wheel! You can't believe how fast this is. If you scroll down a page with 5 normal scroll turns, it only takes one spin with this method.
Right now, I'm using a Trackman but I'm changing to a centered trackball when I have money.

### Post by Kvark on 2009-01-25
I have a Kensington Orbit with the ball in the middle and 2 buttons, it is very comfortable to use.

1) It needs no drivers or configuration, just plug it in an click away.

3) It's not good enough for competitive gamers for 3 reasons:
3.1) Trackballs are 'sticky', when the ball is still and you start moving it you jump a millimeter but once it's moving you have perfect precision. When you're sniping and almost on target it's annoying to jump over the target and have to move the sight around to gain precision. Mice also have initial friction but it's easier to avoid starting with a jump when you start moving a bigger object.
3.2) Back when I was looking around I couldn't find a single trackball with both an acceptable DPI and an acceptable price, maybe there is one now.
3.3) The Kensington Orbit has only 2 buttons.

3.4) That being said I find it effortless to play FPS games with a centered trackball. I beat bots on high difficulty in most games and perform just as well (for a casual gamer) in multiplayer as with a gaming mouse. I use high acceleration on the desktop but low sensitivity in games. Fine fingertip motions plus low sensitivity gives good accuracy yet I can also turn around quickly by sending the ball spinning for an instant.

No scroll wheel) I never used the scroll wheel for scrolling or changing weapons anyway. I've always scrolled with Page Up/Down or the arrow keys to scroll. On Linux I click with both buttons at once to do middle click pastes all the time but on Windows that doesn't have "both buttons = middle click" I really miss having a middle mouse button.

This is pretty old.  This and another thread like it... Someone's shopping for track balls.  I will use my experience to answer your questions to the full extent of my knowledge.

1. Other than the hardware itself, a trackball operates just like a regular mouse.  It should work fine with standard human interface drivers.

2. Absolutely get one where the ball is operated by the thumb.  These are far easier to operate -- in my opinion -- than center mounted trackballs.  That is, unless you prefer to move the mouse cursor with one of your opposing digits and would rather click the left mouse button with your thumb.  I still say go for a thumb trackball as this operates more like a regular mouse in practice (as far as button pressing is concerned).

3. You will most certainly get used to it.  Some of my best FPS gaming was done on a trackball mouse.

4. Maybe?  Depends on the ergonomics of the whole package (trackball + your hand = whole package).  Less energy is going to be involved in movement, though instead of moving your wrist you will be moving your thumb.  Take that for what it's worth.

The only reason why I would not get a track ball now is that they are hard to find in local stores to try out for comfort, and also they are not well suited for laptops (because they're bulky).
