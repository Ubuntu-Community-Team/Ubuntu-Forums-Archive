### Post by aeiah on 2010-08-10
now we're talking about WPA2 etc, this is where aircrack becomes useful for innocent things. WPA hashes are salted with your essid. you can download salted tables for the most common essids (things like 'netgear', 'home', 'linksys' etc), and can download password dictionaries with millions of passwords contained within.

you could use aircrack with your WPA handshake hash and the correct pre-compiled salted hash table, or compile your own table, or just brute force it on the fly. you'll find that doing any of those will take ages if your password is complex enough, and will never result in a positive result if you have a password not contained in your password dictionary. if you're using some kind of random password generation instead of a password dictionary, the crack could take years if you have a complex enough passphrase.

so there you go, a useful reason to use aircrack. it'll prove that either your network will take ages to crack or is virtually uncrackable (if you have a strong enough passphrase).

now, who's gonna bother doing that just for a quick way to connect to the internet? they're just gonna crack your neighbours WEP network instead.
