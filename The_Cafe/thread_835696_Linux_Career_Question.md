### Post by Prospero2006 on 2008-06-20
Check out a company called Rackspace. They are expanding right now and need people who are Linux certified.

I'm a teacher myself. I have re-imaged a few computer labs in my school so that they run Ubuntu, and we've developed a whole curriculum for our middle school students that is based on Ubuntu/Linux. It's pretty cool to be able to work with Linux every day and have complete control over what we do and how. --It's a dream job.

If you are into starting your own business, you might want to look into selling your local school district on a cost-saving Linux lab. There are plenty of district that are actively looking for ways to get computers in the classroom, but just can't afford to do it. (Or at least they think they can't afford to do it.) 

Just as an example: 
   -In the lab down the hall from me. The Windows image with hardware costs about $1500 per seat. In a 30 room lab that's $45,000. --No Bull.

   -In my room, the computers cost about half that because I don't have any licensing fees. Depending on how much hardware is reuseable, the price point could drop to as low as $200 per seat. 

Anyway, my point is there is definitely a market for cheap technology in public schools. If you have the time to invest, it would be worth pursuing.
