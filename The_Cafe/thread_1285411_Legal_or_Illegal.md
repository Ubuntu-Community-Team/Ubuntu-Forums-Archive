### Post by koenn on 2009-10-07
usually, when you're on a network that is not your own (school, work, public library, ...), you do so by the rules, terms, conditions, ... of whoever gave you permission to use that network.
So it's not about legal or illegal - it's about what the owners of the network consider "acceptable use".

You can either argue that you didn't know and had no way of knowing that port scanning was unacceptable on this network (if that is indeed the case),
or argue that you interpreted the rule as being meant to keep people from using that network as a means to anonymously do port scans - and that it wouldn't apply to running a check against your own network / computer.

In the end, it'll be up to your school to decide.
