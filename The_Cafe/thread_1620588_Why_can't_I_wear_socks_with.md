### Post by koenn on 2010-11-14
> **laurenbanjo said:**
> The main reason for wearing socks is to keep your feet warm. The main reason for sandals is to keep your feet cooler. So why would you do both?


socks have many purposes, keeping your feet warm is only one of them.

the purpose of sandals is to keep your feet cooler. So obviously, you wear them when it's relatively warm. When it's warm, your feet tend to sweat. Feet are among the most perspiring parts of the human body.

Without socks, the sweat, your skin and the shoe form an excellent environment for bacteria, which will soon start to produce a rather unpleasant smell -- even in sandals. Sometimes even more so in sandals because the open shoe lets in all sorts of dust and dirt that adds nicely to the mix (from a bacteria's point of view), producing the typical sandal sludge. 


Sock absorb a great deal of this sweat and help it to evaporate (they act like a wick). The eveporation adds to the cooling effect. 
What doesn't evaporate remains absorbed in the sock  - which you can (and should) change regularly - your feet and your sandals are far less affected by it.

So wearing socks in sandals makes perfect sense. It keeps your feet cool and clean, and prevents your sandals from becoming old and smelly before their time.
