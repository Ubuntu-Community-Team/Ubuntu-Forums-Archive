### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-06-05
In my experience, the newer ATI drivers are pretty good.
I have a laptop with a mobility x700 128mb and a desktop with an nVidia 8600 gt 256mb. The nVidia card is better at mode switching and MUCH better at gaming at 1680x1050 res, while overall the ATI card actually has better video playback. I can only imagine this is improved with newer ATI cards which probably close the gaming gap, not to mention that the ATI installer from the ATI website will actually build .deb files specifically for Ubuntu.

ATI was pretty lame, I'll admit up through last fall, but starting with their release this spring, maybe around April, I'd say their drivers are giving nVidia a really good run for their money. Plus, ATI drivers are clearly improving, whereas nVidia's don't seem to be going anywhere.
