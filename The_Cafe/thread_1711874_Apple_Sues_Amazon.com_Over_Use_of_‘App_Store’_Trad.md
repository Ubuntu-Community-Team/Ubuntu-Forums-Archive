### Post by Dr. C on 2011-03-22
It is more the case of a genericized trademark [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genericized_trademark]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genericized_trademark") such as kerosene, escalator, heroin, zipper etc., all of which were former trademarks of different companies.  When word marks are used as a noun or a verb as opposed to an adjective or an adverb that is what happens. 

Amazon has a lot of legal precedent on their side.
