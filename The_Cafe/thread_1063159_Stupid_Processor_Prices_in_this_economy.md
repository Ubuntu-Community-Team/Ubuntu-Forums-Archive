### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-02-08
> **mamamia88 said:**
> i was gonna call you crazy but then i checked newegg and it seems intel is consistently at least $50 hire for similar processors.

<disclaimer> I own AMD and Intel systems. </disclaimer>

Intel processors are worth $50 more in terms of better performance. A 3GHz Core 2 Duo will defeat a 3GHz Athlon, and the same story applies when you look at Core 2 Quad versus Phenom 2.

In short, don't compare clock speed.

@chris200x9: When looking at Core i7 systems, you need to look at the whole picture. To adopt Core i7 you need a motherboard with the new socket (which are all enthusiast-grade mobos - great but expensive) and DDR3 RAM. And you really need multiples of 3 sticks of DDR3. It's much more expensive than DDR2 because it's relatively new and faster. Most CPU coolers on the market don't come with mounting brackets for the new socket, which means an added cost.

It is a very expensive exercise to adopt Core i7. That's why there's so much interest in the Phenoms, and I'm glad to see people supporting AMD when they really need it. But at the moment, a Core 2 Duo is still the better proposition, especially as AMD are changing their socket in a few months (so they've just destroyed the argument of "when a newer processor comes out I can just put it into my existing computer"). Who knows, the AM3+ platform might require DDR3?

AM3 AMD processors will work on AM2 boards with DDR1 and DDR2 RAM types. When installed on an AM3 board, it will only support DDR3. AM2 processors will not work on AM3 boards (RAM restriction), but AM3's (processors) in AM2's (mobos) will only work at peak HT speeds for an AM2 that the board will allow.
