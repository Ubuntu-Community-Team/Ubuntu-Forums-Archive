### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-06-07
Read the details of what you have won very carefully. 

I have money so I can afford the so-called free trips. They usually cover the flight on the cheapest route to the destination. You may have to change flights going from one airport to another taking up hours because of the indirect route. The free lodging may be a condominium/resort share arrangement where you have to spend time listening to them preform their high-pressure sales pitch to purchase shares in a condominium/resort you can't afford. They may describe the entertainment in the area; however, if you read carefully you will realize that it is not included in the free trip. I had one to Las Vegas and another to Disney World, although the brochure described the entertainment in those areas, none or it was included; however, I knew that but had the money to make the best out of the offer. I also have some basic principals; such as, I will not buy anything when pressured, nor will I buy from someone that contacts me first. I have to contact them first before I consider buying anything from them. I basically got 4 day vacations for half of the price I would have spent if I had paid the whole thing myself. You also have to travel when they offer the trips; which, are usually off-season times when a lot of the things you expect to participate in are not occurring. In your case, **winter** in sunny California, HAHAHA.

If you are creative, you can make it into a decent vacation. You just need to know what is included in the so-called free trip.
