### Post by nothingspecial on 2010-05-11
So I know this bloke (man, guy).

He has no viable qualifications.

About 10 years ago he joined a wholesale seafood company. He progressed to run the wholesale fresh division. Basically, he bought fresh fish in bulk, and sold it (very quickly) for (in most cases) a profit.

He did quite well.

He got disillusioned. 

Making money is great. Providing for your family is great. But what use is a man that simply generates money?

Especially at the expense of an important, dwindling natural resource?

So, he has sold his share in the business. And has embarked on a degree in "Environmental Science" (of which he knows nothing), with an intention, to somehow, find a job conserving fish stocks.

He is 35 years old. He has a wife and 2 children, age 6 & 8. He raised enough money to pay off his mortgage and not need to work for 6 months.

The degree will take 3 years, with no guarantee of a job at the end.

He was deeply unhappy in what he was doing. While he still has money, and is studying he is very happy.

The money is going to run out. But, whatever happens, he cannot lose his house.

Mad, visionary, or completely stupid?

Any thoughts?
