Quantum computing is not about making "fast" machines using conventional hardware. It is about exploiting  peculiar features in quantum mechanics like quantum entanglement to do things that classical machines cannot do no matter how fast and powerful they are (as permitted by physics), an example would be cracking RSA encryptions (which depends on being able to factorize large number) within reasonable time. The limitations of classical computation is set by the laws of physics and clever engineering cannot circumvent it anymore than you can make a perpetual machine in defiance of the second law of thermodynamics.

Read the book by Simon Singh to get an idea what this is all about.
