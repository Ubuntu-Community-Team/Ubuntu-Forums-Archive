### Post by handy on 2009-02-07
By emailing the spammer's (which is usually very difficult to do), you may have made a mistake, as now your email address has been confirmed to their systems, systems where the content of your email is undoubtedly irrelevant.

You may find that you will receive a lot more spam in the near future, that is of course if the various spammers actually received your email anyway.

If your ISP doesn't provide an anti-spam service by default? Which the customer can usually configure to suit themselves then you may want to consider finding an old PII & setting up your own standalone IPCop/Copfilter box, being a multi-talented firewall.  This will give you the ability to use various services, including Spamassassin & Rules De Jour to protect your system.
