### Post by LaRoza on 2007-07-19
You mentioned most of the common word processor, and some of the best. Although you might not find them any better, you could look at KOffice and StarOffice. StarOffice is what OpenOffice is based off of, but it isn't free.

> 
-Something that can handle large text documents. 300 pages of text are still only a few kilobytes, so I don't understand why any application would have difficulty with them, but AbiWord and Microsoft Word slow down to near-unusability and even OpenOffice lags some. Anyone know the reason? Any solutions?

A few kilobytes? A single letter is one or two bytes, depending on encoding. Formatting adds more. I would suggest breaking up the large documents into many, instead of a book, do chapters for example.

> 
-Good UNIVERSAL images and tables. All three can make tables and integrate images, but they won't look the same when you move from one program to another. Solutions?
Use an open format, like OpenOffice, and read it in OpenOffice. There is no such thing as "universal". Even HTML which is a limited standard renders differently on modern browsers.

It looks like you want PDF.
