### Post by MikeTheC on 2009-04-18
It's funny you should pose this question. The other day in my Intro Algebra class at college, our professor was talking about this exact thing. There's an organization (I don't remember the name right at the moment) that hires people for a number of jobs at resorts, lodges, parks, and so forth all across the U.S. Lots of really scenic places, interesting people to meet, etc. Typically your only expense will be in traveling there and back. Your time there (room, board, etc.) is frequently picked up by the employer.

The concluding point my professor made was that doing this sort of traveling and/or work experience after high school is a very good thing, because if you don't do it *now* you may never get around to doing it later in life. Arguably, as you go into adulthood, enter the workforce, start a family, etc., it becomes progressively harder and harder to allocate either the resources or time to do this. He said that it was when he was on a trip to a camp in Maine over the summer about a year or two into his schooling that he discovered he'd rather teach than have a job crunching numbers, simply because he became a camp teacher.

You never know where life will take you.

Just my 2¢.
