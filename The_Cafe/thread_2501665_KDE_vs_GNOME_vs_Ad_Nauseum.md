### Post by Shibblet on 2024-10-16
I am a huge KDE fan.  Ever since I found out about Kubuntu in version 10.04, I have jumped on the KDE band-wagon, and haven't looked back.

However, I have looked sideways... and I see many different types of DE's, like Gnome, XFCE, Budgie, Cinnamon, Unity, etc.

Now, I don't want to start a trolling flame-war on this thread... but am I the only one who thinks KDE is a much more "professional" desktop environment?  I mean, I'm not saying any of the other DE's are no good, I think they're fine.  But KDE seems to have a more professional edge to it's style and design.

Gnome, to me, feels more like a tablet interface.
And then there's Cinnamon, Unity, Cosmic, etc...  Why does every distro feel the need to re-create a current DE, and make it look a little different, just to call it theirs?
Ubuntu has morphed Gnome 40 into the "Ubuntu Desktop."  Cosmic doesn't even really look any different than Gnome.  Solus's Budgie looks like a hybrid Gnome 2 (MATE) / Gnome 3 concept.

But, to me, KDE stands out above all of the rest.  It's professional, it's clean, it's customizable, and it just looks good.

I'm not trying to say anything bad about the other DE's.  They all server their purpose.  And more importantly, "Different strokes for different folks."  If you prefer Gnome over KDE, that's fantastic.  If it does what you need, even better.
