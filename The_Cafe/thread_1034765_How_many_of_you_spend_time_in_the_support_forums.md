### Post by bigbrovar on 2009-01-09
Ubuntuforums can be quite addictive. but most of the addiction as more to do with hanging out in the community discussions section.often i spend time here just going through the various interesting threads and point of views about FOSS and technology in general. however i always try to at least pay a visit to the support forums to see if there is any problem i can help with. yeah it can be boring and frustrating a times esp for someone with Linux knowledge of Linux (like me) but one thing i discovered is that no knowledge is too small. you would always find someone who has a problem you have come across and solved before. and its also a great place to learn by reading solutions provided to people you can learn more about Linux and how to trouble shoot. I always see it as more community duty to Ubuntu, so everyday at least once or twice i try browse the support section to see if there is any problem that i can help with. and where i find one i give my advise to the best of my knowledge and stay with the thread till i feel the problem is solved or if i cant solve it try keep with afloat with my post... this goes a long way in making the forum a great place and helping new users stay. beside it has a good feeling esp when you get to solve the problem. and get thanked for it. so i would encourage us to always spend some times at the support thread believe me it would mean a lot to the person in need.
