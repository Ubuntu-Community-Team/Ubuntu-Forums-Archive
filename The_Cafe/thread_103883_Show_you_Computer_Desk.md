### Post by futz on 2005-12-16
[QUOTE=MetalMusicAddict]Very nice job. Im looking into all the tools in the morning.

Again, very nice.[/QUOTE]
Thanks MMA. This set of tools, though much, much harder to use than the ultra simple Canon PhotoStitch, does a much better job. I just finished having a real close look at the pic and can't find any hint of the stitch lines at all. With PhotoStitch, you can almost always find em if you look hard.

Let me give you a bunch of links to save you a ton of time:

[http://rbpark.ath.cx/articles/compile-hugin-ubuntu](http://rbpark.ath.cx/articles/compile-hugin-ubuntu)
This is the install tutorial. It has one error. The Panotools CVS source code download doesn't work as advertised. Probably the filename at sourceforge got changed or something. I ended up just grabbing the tarball and compiling that. It worked fine.

[http://rbpark.ath.cx/articles/create-panorama](http://rbpark.ath.cx/articles/create-panorama)
[http://blogs.linux.ie/xeer/2005/02/03/autopano-sift-making-panoramas-fun/](http://blogs.linux.ie/xeer/2005/02/03/autopano-sift-making-panoramas-fun/)
[http://user.cs.tu-berlin.de/~nowozin/autopano-sift/](http://user.cs.tu-berlin.de/~nowozin/autopano-sift/)
[http://blogs.linux.ie/xeer/2005/08/03/autostitch-stitching-photos-made-easy-even-in-linux/](http://blogs.linux.ie/xeer/2005/08/03/autostitch-stitching-photos-made-easy-even-in-linux/)

NOTE: Autopano.SIFT totally refuses to work when I let it downscale resolution. The default setting just won't work for me. I set it to "keep input size" and then it works. YMMV

NOTE 2: It needs a LOT of RAM on a bigger stitch job. I'm running a 12 pic stitch now and the box has slowed to a crawl. I looked at top and it has used up all my RAM (1 gig) and a gig of swap space on the hard drive too. Who knows how long it'll take to finish now? Guess I'll go to bed and see if it's done when I wake up.

NOTE 3: Gave up on that last stitch. The 12 files were directly out of a 4 megapix camera, and were like 870kb to a meg+ each. It was totally out of space to work and would have taken weeks to finish and wore my hard drive out.

So I resized each one down to 1024x768 (around 85kb each) and ran it again. That worked fine. I watched it in 'top' and it used almost all the ram, but never touched swap. But the results were bad. It did a bad job and you can clearly see the ugly stitch lines (diagonal!). I assume that's because of artifacting introduced by shrinking the jpg's. My camera outputs jpg's. What I should have done is immediately convert those to tiff or something and then shrink. Alternately, I'll need more RAM. Lots more.
