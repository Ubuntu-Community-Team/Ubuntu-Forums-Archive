### Post by darweth on 2007-04-12
Sometimes I let Linux consume my soul and spend waaay too much time attempting to customize crap.  This almost always ends in failure and sometimes even breaks a thing or do.  Repeatedly installing fluxbox, openbox, enlightenment, kde, xfce, etc.  Spending hours trying to learn or adapt to them only to realize I am a GNOME man and that GNOME's utter simplicity and convenience are a dream to use.  It might not be the prettiest or the fastest, but it certainly has all the bells and whistles.  Why do I keep trying other DEs and WMs?!?!  No idea!  I just feel like I am missing out. :)  I feel as though GNOME has spoiled me though and that perhaps I am too dependent on a GUI or snazzy simple applets.  It is nice to just have a trash bin and volume control right there in the panel which gives GNOME a step up over fluxbox.  I am sure there are ways around that too, but it involves learning, configging, perhaps even *GASPS* hotkeys!!  

I am just rambling. :)  I should just learn to accept that I am a very casual computer user anyway and need not seek beyond GNOME.  All I do is listen to music, surf, chat, torrent, and watch videos!  Do I need to become an openbox master?  Not really.

Because 4 hours later when I decide to switch back to GNOME... I end up thinking, "Damn, another wasted night.  I should have watched that movie!"
