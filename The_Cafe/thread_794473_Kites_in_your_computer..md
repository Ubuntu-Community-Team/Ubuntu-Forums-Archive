### Post by TorqueyPete on 2008-05-14
Are screen savers cool to mess around with for hours? Some would say not. ;)

Did you know that one of the Ubuntu screen savers can be perfectly reproduced and flown as a kite??

[IMG]http://i81.photobucket.com/albums/j226/chashugh/Tetrahedron/Screenshot.png[/IMG]

The Seirpinski 3D screen saver may be just some moving shapes, and not the most spectacular of screen savers. But you could actually build a physical version as a kite, as big or as small as you like, and fly it!

[IMG]http://i81.photobucket.com/albums/j226/chashugh/Tetrahedron/fig14.gif[/IMG]

[IMG]http://i81.photobucket.com/albums/j226/chashugh/Tetrahedron/9429.jpg[/IMG]

[IMG]http://i81.photobucket.com/albums/j226/chashugh/Tetrahedron/tetra_new34-1.jpg[/IMG]

Alexander Graham Bell was enthusiastic about this shape, and basically 'invented' it as a kite form. Even making a tetrahedron kite so powerful that it could lift men off the ground!

[U][IMG]http://i81.photobucket.com/albums/j226/chashugh/Tetrahedron/Bell-tetrahedral-kite.gif[/IMG]


[/U]And here is the [Tetralite]("http://tetralite.com/") website offering proper plans and full instructions for diy kitemakers. Or anyone with some thin plastic or paper and a few sticks. I'veeven seem some made with drinking straws! Very light weight, and flying in virtually zero wind.  B)
 
How cool is that as a screen saver now?
