### Post by suomalainen on 2012-03-14
Ubunteros,

When a typical thread is created, usually the poster is seeking to remedy a problem they face. I think we can all agree that for the most part the OP usually understands the basic of posting and what is required when seeking help/advice. For example, what version of Ubuntu they are running, make & model of their equipment etc... etc.. All that pertinent info that is needed to understand the problem they face.

In turn, the community offers assistance. This may be in the form of running terminal commands, offering links to online resources or simply building a solution based on the problem from the ground up... In the end, working together, the community has strengthened itself and the products they represent. A solution to the problem was created and the OP is a happy camper.

But how about those folks that are so "clever" that they seek out problems and create solution for the community on their own. Is there a standard to be followed?

For example, when offering such solutions, whatever they may be, should they be presented with good written instructions followed with detailed illustrations or photographs?

I'm interested in picking the thoughts of others and seeing how you view such a question.

Thanks for your feedback,

Suomalainen
