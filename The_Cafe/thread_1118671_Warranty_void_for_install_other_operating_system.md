### Post by michaeldt on 2009-05-03
In the UK you need to remember that you have rights beyond their "warranty". In fact, most of the time they offer a 12 month warranty so that you don't come knocking in 18 months time or to sell extended warranties. However, the Sale of Goods Act provides more protection than manufacturers warranties. So just because they won't repair the hardware under their warranty, doesn't mean they have no legal obligation to do so, because they do (depending on how old the hardware is though). Of course getting them to make the repair is another matter, but it might be worth mentioning the Sale of Goods Act if they do refuse.
