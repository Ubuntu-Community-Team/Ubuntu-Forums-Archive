### Post by Maupertus on 2008-08-10
I've been following these discussions for a while now, and there never is an easy answer. I really wanted to get rid of Amarok because I want to go all GNOME and I thought it was a bit heavy. It's a great audioplayer.
I've been looking at Exaile, Banshee, Listen and have settled for the old love that I've felt for Rhythmbox.

What I really miss and (to my knowledge) all of them lack is a decent database supporting it that organizes my collection for me (as in, makes folders by letter, artist, album, song in my music directory) has the power to let me edit tags in a very easy way (God I love the: "re-write Artist to xxx for selected tracks-option). 

Now I'm using Rhytmbox, but still keep Amarok to organise my music and tag my songs, which Rhytmbox just can't do. 
Also, it's handeling of podcasts is horrid, and the Last.fm integration is a lot better, but Amarok's is so much more refined.

So everytime this discussion rages, the answer always is... find the thing that suits your needs, use all of them for a while. And for most of us the answer will be a choice to use Amarok ;)
