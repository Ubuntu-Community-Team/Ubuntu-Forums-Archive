### Post by gnomeuser on 2010-03-22
I am battering hard for the Nokia N900

[img]http://www.mobil.nu/ArticlePages/200908/27/20090827134435_MDK914/n900_6.jpg[/img]

I really like it's hardware keyboard, the interface is smooth and nicely augmented with Tracker in ways that really enhances the experience. It has some nice subtle visual effects that work well and makes the interface easy to learn and enjoy. Everything works consistently and they use expected behavior to make the tablet effective and pleasurable in use. 

The interface does feels a bit rough in places, there are places that need obvious polish and it's mail application is a 90's reject that desperately needs to be replaced. They should look at the media experience and the excellent web browser experience to see what they should go after and they should remember that they have tracker as well. 

The cable plugs are placed oddly for use of the keyboard. When the datacable is attached using the keyboard is entirely impossible to use comfortably and efficiently. When the headset is plugged in it is also impractical to use the keyboard which is a shame since that seems a likely use case, listening to music while texting, browsing or in my case writing a blog post.

Also in the hardware department the back latch feels bridle and removing it is hard work at least the first many times. It makes the device feel cheap and seeing as this is the first impression out of the box due to insertion of the battery being required this is highly undesirable.

The camera is great, it has a high quality optic for a device of this type. The lense is protected by a cover, which when opened automatically starts the camera applications. All your photos are managed by Tracker underneath which means the application just works when it comes to managing the data and presenting it. You can even easy add tags, though the default collections is in English only.

I am having some problems getting the device to behave correctly on Lucid but these are being worked on. For the time being the experience here is subpar.

Updates for the N900 are done using a standard Linux package manager, in this case dpkg with a usable pretty UI resembling a good appstore and OS management center. This is really useful however Nokia have a fairly poor trackrecord issuing updates. Hopefully this will change as MeeGo gets off the ground and one could potentially install any spin of MeeGo one wanted in case of need or preference.

It's a fantastic device, I highly recommend it. It has flaws and improvements can certainly be made. One misses the centralized contact and account approach from Android and the way that makes things like calendaring just work. I hope this will be possible in the future for now expect a lot of flexibility but mired in hard to understand configurations dialogs for everything. Before this is fixed, perhaps by Ubuntu One and Ubuntu SSO, the device is for experienced users which is a shame because so much of the experience is just right.

With the exceptions of the few hardware issues, all of the N900's beauty flaws are software related and the system is nearly the same as what is used on any Linux desktop. The benefits of solving them for these devices will have ramifications and improvements on the desktops and likewise. 

I am very happy with it.
