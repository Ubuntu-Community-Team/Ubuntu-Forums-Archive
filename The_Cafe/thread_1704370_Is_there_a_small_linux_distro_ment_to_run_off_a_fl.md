### Post by Hur Dur on 2011-03-10
Slax is made as a live CD. They actually recommend against installing it to the flash drive.

Puppy isn't really a featherweight anymore, as it crawls on very old computers. However, it's good for a flash drive Linux distro.

SliTaz is actively developed, and they have a live CD available.

Feather Linux and DSL are dormant, but they are good Live CDs to have if you have an older computer.

There's always Finnix, although it is a pretty big distro. 

You could try a distro that is the bare minimum (i.e. Just a working system, no X.)

For extremely small, look at: Blueflops, and Devil Linux.
