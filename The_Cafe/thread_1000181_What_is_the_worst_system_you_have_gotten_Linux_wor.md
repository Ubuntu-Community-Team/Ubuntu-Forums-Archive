### Post by Toe on 2008-12-02
Two weeks ago I installed Intrepid on a dual Pentium2 400Mhz system with 512MB Ram, with root on a software-RAID1 comprised of two SCSI drives. I had to tweak the rootdelay boot parameter. Otherwise the system wouldn't wait the necessary 50 seconds for all its drives to become initialised. Apart from that all went well.
I'm using this machine right now, by the way. (still need to get repair parts for my regular PC)
Even Skype calls are possible, although I can only receive but not send a webcam video, since the P2 doesn't seem to know the necessary instruction sets.
Surfing works quite well (including flash videos), as does OpenOffice.

A few months ago I also tried Xubuntu on a 300Mhz Pentium 2 Sony Vaio laptop, but its 64MB of Ram made working with it barely possible.
But I'm sure it would fly with Damn Small Linux or something comparably small.
