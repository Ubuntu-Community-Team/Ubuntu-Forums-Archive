in '99, senior year of high school, my teacher got me interested in linux and the idea of free (as in freedom) software (don't remember if the term "open source" was in wide use yet).  my first attempt was mandrake.  that lasted about as long as the time it took to install and reboot.  found myself at a command prompt because my video card wasn't supported (out of the box at least).  tried red hat and, later, fedora core 1.  both didn't like my modem (i lived in a rural town in arizona with no high-speed provider at the time).  tried ubuntu about 5 months ago and love it.  no driver problem that couldn't be fixed by a google search and the copy & paste feature, except my laptop's s-video out which isn't a big deal for me.  now, ubuntu is installed on all my comps.  also convinced my dad and my sister to use it on a couple of their comps.  my dad loves it and my sister uses it for her guest comp at her house.
