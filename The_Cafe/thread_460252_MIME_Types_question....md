### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2007-05-31
The Linux desktops have lots to do with MIME types, recognizing files' types with those instead of by arbitrary extensions pinned on to their names. This is indeed excellent :)

I am right now planning a project that will make use of this quite extensively, but I am having trouble figuring out how exactly they fit in!

MIME types (content types) are "Internet media types", and they are to be registered with the [Internet Assigned Numbers Authority](http://www.iana.org/).

With files on the desktop using the same scheme, this seems really weird! Should MIME types be registered with the IANA regardless of whether they have anything to do with the Internet, or do files on the local machine go by a different scheme? (Or how many people bother, anyway?)
