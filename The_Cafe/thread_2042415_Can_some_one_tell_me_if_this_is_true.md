### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2012-08-17
I have been using on-line content for quite some time. The way we used it and what capabilities were available changed over the years. I am sure it will continue to change.

When I started, there were dial-up message boards at some universities. I had a 240 baud modum to make the connection. Everyting was text of cource.

Then I heard about the Internet. I subscribed to an Internet service. I think it was Delphi. I used that until the mid 1980's. I think I had a fast 16Kb/s modum at the time. Later, I started to read about something called the World-Wide-Web. 

I can't remember what company I used to get my WWW service.  I set up my own message board at that time. The message board didn't exist very long. I got tired of checking it every day to moderate the posts. I found it intolerable that some people would post p0rn on it.

Eventually, newsgroups were becoming less popular, and Yahoo groups emerged. 

Now we have Social sites; such as, Facebook and Google+. I do not use any of them. I don't think the decline in the number of message boards is a result of bandwidth.

I think the number of message boards has decreased; however, I'm not sure that is bad. I just don't like the current trend of Social sites that try to make money from it every way they can.

I will be watching to see what evolves next.
