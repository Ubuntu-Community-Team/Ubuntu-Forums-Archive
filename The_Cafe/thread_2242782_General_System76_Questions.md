### Post by MoebusNet on 2014-09-05
This may not directly address your questions, but if I may I would like to make a suggestion:

Try out Ubuntu Linux before you spend your money on a notebook with Ubuntu or any Linux preinstalled on it as the only Operating System:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop/create-a-usb-stick-on-windows](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop/create-a-usb-stick-on-windows)
[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop/try-ubuntu-before-you-install](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop/try-ubuntu-before-you-install)

You should be able to run Ubuntu from your present computer by booting from a USB external hard drive or flash drive or a DVD to get an idea of it before you spend your money. Ubuntu and Linux have a different philosophy towards computers than Windows and MacOS, it takes a little while to get used to it. Gaming on Linux in general is improving rapidly, but for casual gamers Windows seems to still offer better hardware support at present (YMMV).
