### Post by toupeiro on 2008-06-04
I'm not overly impressed to be honest.  I mean its a nice laptop and all, very powerful, but not really a "sweet spot". when compared to other high performance laptops out there for the price.  How many threaded applications to you really crunch on a laptop that take full advantage of quad core?  

I'm not saying quad core doesn't have its place in the consumer market, but the vast majority of consumer software has not caught up, especially in the mobility market.

I can get an HP 8710w with a T9500 dual core, 8GB of RAM and a 512MB quadro-FX 1600M for a few thousand less..  I'll likely use the 8GB of ram far before I perminantly tap out both cores on that T9500, and if by some chance I do its cheaper to put that power in a desktop and use VNC or SSH if I am not in front of it. :)  The 8710w also gives me on board HDMI and a blu-ray player.  If I were going to spend my own money on something, I'd sacrifice the quad core bragging rights for the full package offered by HP.  I have a 4GB model of the 8710w with the T8300 and the 512MB Quadro card that my work provided for me.  Its a monster laptop with no shortage of power and resources.  I think they paid in the ballpark of $2,600 - $2,800 at the time. (beginning of the year)  I could probably get 2 in that configuration today for the asking price of the quad.
