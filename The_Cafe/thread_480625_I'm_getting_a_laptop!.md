I have the same laptop. I paid $550 cash for it, in person, so I hope the condition of the laptop is good. The product page does mention that it may have scratches, scuffs, etc. Mine came in mint condition: every part was immaculate, and the seller mentioned that he'd recently replaced the keyboard and the plastic around the keyboard. The article doesn't mention SVGA or SXVGA, but mine doesn't have that either, although it would be very sweet if it did (1450x1024 max res, or something like that). Mine came with 1.5GB RAM and an 80GB harddisk, plus an extra hardly-used 9Cell (extended life) battery. Needless to say, I think I got a great deal. At 200 pounds, I think your deal isn't too shabby either, although I hope the case and screen are in good shape.

This laptop is very Linux-friendly because of the Madwifi driver, ATI Radeon Mobility 7500, and generally vanilla hardware. You'll get crummy reception though with the wifi because the ath_pci module has [this bug that I filed]("http://madwifi.org/ticket/1343") with Madwifi, and has been successfully repeated by many users.
