What itches me mostly about Ubuntu's success is that the other major distributions pay for a lot of upstream development by sponsoring developers in a multitude of trans-distributional projects while Ubuntu develops mostly stuff for themselves or invents software like Launchpad and refuses to make the sources available so others might use it without Canonical's participation. Canonical is the only major Linux cooperation (major defined by user base) I know of which does very little (close to nothing) on the upstream front.
