### Post by zerhacke on 2006-04-22
You are to heed The Rule Of The Greater Geek. If you find yourself unable to perform any duty with your computer, you are to seek out a geek of higher magnitude than you. This geek is referred to as the Greater Geek. If the Greater Geek is unable to help you with your problem he will refer you to HIS Greater Geek. The process repeats itself ad infinitum untill either the Greater Geek supply is exhausted or your problem is solved. Should you exhaust Greater Geeks, you are not to stop. You are to find a geek who was not in the chain of Greater Geeks you used the first time and begin the chain again. Repeat as necessary until you have the solution to your problem.

ALWAYS TIP THE GREATER GEEK. It does not have to be with money. In the scope of the Greater Geek's desire, the order of preference of tip is as follows:

* referrals - refer more friends to the Greater Geek. This gives him new boxes to play with and new people that admire him. Greater Geeks REALLY like to play with new boxes. This is probably the greatest tip you can give a Greater Geek

* praise - Most Greater Geeks are socially inept and cannot curry almost anyone's favor. They're generally socially outcast and don't get out a lot. Praising the Greater Geek gives him something he almost never gets - kind words and a sense of friendship, even if temporary. If you really want to make your Greater Geek happy, praise him to other people in his presence.

* techincal toys - Computer hardware is always welcome, but it doesn't necessarily have to be computer hardware. Spend some time at [http://thinkgeek.com](http://thinkgeek.com) and find something really cool to a geek. Get him this thing. You don't have to spend much to make him happy.

* software - This is a very hard way to tip your Greater Geek. Most likely, he has what you want to give him already. Also likely, what you want to give him does not work on his operating system. However, if you CAN find something your Greater Geek does not have AND it runs on his operating system, he will be very happy with you. I suggest looking into DIVX compressed AVI movies or MPEG 1 compressed movies - they work on almost any operating system.

* dinner - This works off of the praise tip in a way. By taking your Greater Geek to dinner, you're not only giving him food, you're giving him a social outlet. You may even make friends with him, which to him is a rare, rare treat. Again, you don't have to spend much, it's not the money that counts.

* money - If you cannot tip your Greater Geek with other means, money is nearly universally accepted. WARNING - SOME GEEKS REFUSE MONEY AND ARE INSULTED BY IT - these geeks are generally the ones you hear talking about the GNU/GPL a lot. They do it for the love of the machine. You probably will not meet many of those kinds of geeks, though. If your Greater Geek will accept it, money is fine... it's just that there are better ways.

* those of you who think that you shouldn't tip your Greater Geek for such reasons as the Greater Geek is a friend should call your local computer shop and ask them how much one hour in the shop is going to cost you.
