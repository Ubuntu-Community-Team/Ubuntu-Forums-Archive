### Post by Paqman on 2011-07-11
Well, Android's not a desktop OS as such, but it's the closest Linux has come to making an impact on the desktop, yes. It's running a lot of tablets.

I've always suspected Linux was going to need to change tack to make inroads into the desktop market. There's just too much inertia around Windows, even Apple struggle to do more than sell around the edges of the desktop market, and they've got a good product with an immensely strong brand and huge amounts of marketing dosh. 

Linux is likely to gain ground only where new markets are opening up in computing and where it can move quickly and compete on a level playing field. I suspect it's not going to be the standard desktop distros like Ubuntu or Fedora that ever make it big, but new types of Linux like Android.
