### Post by joepie91 on 2010-11-21
I've set up an old hardware collection project a few weeks ago... and I've received quite a lot of old computers, parts, and peripherals already. I try to refurbish things, throw a light-weight Linux distribution on it (Puppy Linux mostly...) and give them away to people who can't afford a computer through schools, for example.

This thread sort of has two purposes... first, I'm curious if anyone else has been doing this? And I specifically mean collecting old hardware from everyone, not just companies... I just have a large poster on the window, and fairly often people bring stuff here.

Second, maybe this thread gives some nice ideas to people who haven't thought of anything like this yet, but would like to set it up.

I know this post is terribly badly written, but it's late and I'm tired :P

EDIT: For the curious people... most hardware I get is fairly old... Pentium 2 or 3 computers with between 64 and 256MB RAM. Sometimes, however, I get slightly more modern computers. Fairly recently a small webdesign company dropped off three computers that could easily run Windows XP. I haven't checked the specs yet though.

### Post by The Real Dave on 2010-11-22
I did a refurbishment project with my school once. My school has over 170 active computers at any one time, and there's always old machines hanging around.

We took 20 1Ghz Pentium III machines, each with 64-128Mb of RAM and a 20Gb harddrive and installed Windows 2K Pro, along with some basic applications (Firefox, Openoffice etc)

They were then donated (along with screens, mice and keyboards) to two local nursing homes, with whom we were doing an IT project (basically where the young taught the elderly how to become IT literate, a project designed to close the age divide).

The IT project worked great, with quite a few out of the group getting very comfortable with what they wanted to do.

However, for the most part, the computers went unused. Earlier last year, the class after mine (who were continuing the project)  fundraised and bought laptops for some of the best "students".

Personally, I didn't see the point. They were not going to use their laptops anywhere other than at home, and even though the little Dell machines weren't powerful, they were quite quick. 

At the end of it, most of those desktop have been recalled. A few came home with me. I often get donated/unwanted computers, usually so that people don't have to pay to dump them. 

I should however start advertising for them :twisted:

### Post by joepie91 on 2010-11-22
In general I aim at people who really don't have a computer at home, but need one (kids going to school, for example) and can't afford one. I simply won't tell them it runs Linux (most people don't even notice) and give them an A4 sheet with a short overview of what the icons do. That'll probably suffice for most.

Currently I'm talking to several schools, and they're going to make an inventory of kids that don't have a computer at home. I try to avoid giving them out to individuals because it attracts a lot of freeloaders who just want an extra computer and monitor to run MSN while they're gaming. But the people who need a computer ARE there. Since I aim at people who really can't afford a computer, and basically only need a word processor and internet browser, there's not a lot they can demand when talking about specs. It's this, or nothing.

If kids at schools have computers and there's nothing to give away there anymore, I'll just aim towards elderly people, and give them a PC with a really simple idiot-proof operating system, and teach them how to use it.

Add to that I am currently working on a non-profit (free) internet cafe here, so even if noone wants computers, I can use them here.
