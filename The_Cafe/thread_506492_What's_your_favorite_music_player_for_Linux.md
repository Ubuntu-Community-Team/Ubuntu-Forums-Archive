### Post by euchrid on 2007-11-29
I can't believe no-one's put [cmus]("http://cmus.sourceforge.net/") up here! Far and away the best for just playing mp3s on your computer.

For those not acquainted with it, cmus is a command line only player. No graphics, no drag and drop, no covers, just a text display and keyboard controls. (You can, of course, use Nautilus or Thunar context/right click menus to add to the playlist, if you need to). It'll play mp3s off a CD or any recognised device, as far as I can tell. It's near instant to load, and being command-line, very easy to adjust to your needs with scripts, etc.

I used to use Amarok, but all I was doing was playing mp3s - not using any online services, or whatever. Amarok is brilliant, but really, I don't need a GUI and loads of extra stuff just to play music. All I want is the music - and cmus gives you that; no fuss, no rubbish, and very easy to add to playlist or library. This also frees up system resources and is less of a distraction! Quick to load

Nothing against Amarok, but if you don't need anything except to hear mp3s, then try cmus. Get the *minimum* you need, not the maximum!

I used to use Sound Juicer to listen to CDs, but now use mcdp, for the same reasons as above... Give them both a go!
