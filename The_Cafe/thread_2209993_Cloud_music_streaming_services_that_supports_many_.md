### Post by Unanimated on 2014-03-13
Google Play Music does exactly this, for free. You can upload 20,000 songs, and for $9.99 a month you get a Spotify-like subscription service (Google Play All Access) to listen to whatever you want.
I used to use this, but I recently switched to Spotify. I miss having all of my music in the cloud, but I'm adjusting to the way Spotify handles your "library," and Spotify has much more music than Google Play All Access. The downside, however, is that if Spotify doesn't have a song you want, you're pretty much out of luck. Spotify can handle your local files and sync them to your devices that share a wifi connection with the computer Spotify is running on, but I have yet to test this because I'm at a college that uses enterprise security on the wifi and it apparently doesn't like that. Also, Spotify's Linux client isn't the best - it's in beta and behind the Windows and Mac versions, but apparently the Windows version of the application runs fine under Wine. Spotify also has a web player, but you won't be able to use your local music using their web player. Google Play takes place completely in the browser - the only application you download is used to upload your music to their servers.
So yeah, that's the basic rundown of both services. So far, I've been really happy with my Spotify subscription compared to my Google Play All Access subscription, but that's because of my individual needs from my music and because their iOS app is much better than Play Music's.
