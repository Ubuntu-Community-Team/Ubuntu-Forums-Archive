### Post by TheFu on 2020-12-05
To recall people, places, events years later.  
To pass on to our kids and their kids information about where they came from, what life and people were like before they were around.
I've lived in 13 different cities and 9 different states. Photos help to remember those places, people, friends, events.  Of course, I have far too many photos of rural Nebraska from my bike too. Not every photo is great ... though looking back through those of "nothing", does show something.

[https://blog.jdpfu.com/2011/01/05/tips-for-digital-photo-organiz-storage-and-archival](https://blog.jdpfu.com/2011/01/05/tips-for-digital-photo-organiz-storage-and-archival)

I tried using organization software, but every few years, incompatibilities break things. Ended up with a good directory structure that has worked over a decade. I store metadata in the file headers. Lacking that, in text files in the same directory. Don't trust organizing software beyond using it temporarily to move photos into good directory structures.
