### Post by tigerpants on 2008-02-05
Life of Brian was their most *precise* work, as in, it lampooned the stupidity of religion very well and it still holds up now. Grail was just 6 men pratting about in armour that lost its way towards the end so they just gave up altogether.

As the Pythons themselves have admitted, most of their stuff didnt really work at all - alot of it wasnt very funny, and was nicked largely from Spike Milligans series in the 1960's. Terry Gilliam's animations still amuse, but Python were more influential than actually funny. Their impact on British comedy was huge. A classic case of influence far exceeding actual comedic value.
