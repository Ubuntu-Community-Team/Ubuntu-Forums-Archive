### Post by igknighted on 2008-04-13
I've played with it, and frankly I wasn't impressed at all.  "oooh, I can flip through things and use 2 fingers to manipulate stuff"... oh wait, you mean aside from the gimmicky UI stuff it's just another locked up phone like my razor?

I'm holding out upgrading to a "smart" phone until data plans (a) come down in price to a reasonable level, and (b) coverage is truly fast and seamless.  In all honesty, I think cell phones are a dying breed, at least in their current forms.  The carriers like verizon and att think they own this lock on the whole flow... but once technology like wimax makes high-speed net access available, who needs an old fashioned cdma phone?  Wimax (or similar technology) capable devices using voip for talk are the future.  I would gladly pay for broadband through wimax and get access to my house and phone, this whole separate services for the same features on different devices is dumb, and as soon as the technology catches up, the cell companies will be sunk.
