### Post by vexorian on 2007-06-15
> **juxtaposed said:**
> Ok.

That just sounds stupid to me. Sure, it's true, but I personally value my personal files (music, movies, documents, etc) much more then my system files/operating system.

Something deletes my OS? Annoying, just install it again.

Something deletes my personal files? That's really really bad :P

You can always backup your important files.

It is easy to restore an operating system, the problem with viruses is not really when they destroy your computer:

If a virus acquires root access (which happens by default on any pre-vista windows) it is able to do much more nasty things that are very... bad.

A root virus can install itself to the kernel and make itself invisible, so you would never know that it is in your windows installation, That is what allows it to actually infect other computers exploiting yours, its invisible status won't let you know it is sending emails to your contacts or copying itself to some disk (it can just hid the files it is copying) It can also take over your network to DDOS some site, you wouldn't know.

A root virus can modify system files, a non-root virus can just wipe your /home/ folder, if that happens you would detect it immediately, and restore a backup copy of /home/ (if you are doing anything important in a computer you should always have one anyways) thus it won't be able to infect other computers, that is way better.

Quite the opposite, you are the one who doesn't know what you are talking about. Try opening the /bin folder and renaming anything in it and see what happens. Software (malware is also software) runs at the same level of access that a user that started that software has.
