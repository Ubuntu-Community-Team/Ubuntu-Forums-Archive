We haven't signed up (think it costs about £2K per year) although we are compliant.

We also have around 25pcs.  I think they target all companies.

I think there would be more compliance and therefore less need for the likes of FAST if:

1. Software licensing, particularly in the business/corporate world, was not so confusing and complicated;

2. Software was cheaper;

3. More open-source/GPL software was used in a business environment.

### Post by toupeiro on 2007-08-24
well, with your numbers its pretty easy to do this yourself.  If you are a microsoft shop, simply download [This Tool]("http://www.microsoft.com/resources/sam/msia.mspx") and let it collect information.  Then compare that with what your purchase records and physical licenses.  

If you are a Linux or solaris shop, you might be using something like flexLM to manage your application licenses.  there are a TON of scripts out there to collect license info routinely with flexLM.  You can download an open source monitoring tool called Nagios, and build some modules to do lmstat commands on your various linux / unix based licenses.  Nagios also does a TON more.

Its only a big deal when you are dealing with hundreds of servers and potentially thousands of clients.  If you just want to do some due-dilligence you can do it yourself pretty easily and save yourself / your company a lot of money.

Just because they say they do, it doesn't mean it is true.  The only people a right to enter your property without your explicit consent are Customs and Excise, a Bailiff of the Court, The Police (and only in situations where they have evidence or genuine belief that there is a crime being committed which places members of the public in imminent danger, otherwise they need a warrant).

Using unlicensed software is not a crime, it is a civil tort.  Distributing that software for commercial gain is a crime, but that is a different ball game altogether.

That is the law.  You can refuse to allow them onto your premises irrespective of what they try to make you believe on their website.
