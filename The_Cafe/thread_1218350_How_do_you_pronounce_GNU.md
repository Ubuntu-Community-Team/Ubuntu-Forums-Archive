### Post by HermanAB on 2009-07-20
Actually, a Gnu was a (now extinct) South African Wild Beast from the Western Cape region.  The FSF Gnu logo is a pencil drawing of the beast.  There are other similar wild beast still in South Africa, notably the Blue Wild Beast of which there are many thousands.

As for pronunciation, Gnu was a Koi (also now extinct!) word and was pronounced with a silent 'g', so the pronunciation should really be similar to 'knew', although the FSF has a different pronunciation guide.

If you are wondering, the Gnu were very tasty and were hunted to extinction by members of PETA (People Eating Tasty Animals).  The Koi mostly died of small pox and influenza a few hundred years ago and the few remaining stragglers were assimilated into other groups.

### Post by Grenage on 2009-07-21
> It's an acronym so I pronounce it as G-N-U, whether RMS wants it like that or not

Well said.

> I thought the difference between acronyms and abbreviations was that with abbreviations, you said the letters individually such as...

Abbreviations are simply any cut-down words (not to be confused with contractions), GNU is an initialism/acronym.  If it's an acronym, you pronounce it as a word; if it's an initialism, you pronounce it according to it's components: G-N-U.

In this case, I'd say it's an initialism, so Gee Enn Yuu (G N U).  Others may see it as an acronym, so they'll pronounce it as a word.  Either way, nobody is 'really' wrong.
