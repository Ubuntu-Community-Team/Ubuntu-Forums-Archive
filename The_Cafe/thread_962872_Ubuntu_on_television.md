### Post by rasmus91 on 2008-10-29
Hi everyone

This evening i watched a program on television (In Denmark, small country 5.5-6 million people) which was about The danish people using way to much money each year on Software. Approximately 20-30 billion Danish [Kroner]("http://http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danish_krone") on software, especially because the only company which sells computer without windows on it is the company Zepto (i think there are some small shops too, but nothing everybody here know about). Well the programs called "penge" (directly translated "Money") and they did a VERY INTERESTING TEST. They took a normal danish family, which had three computers. They have normal computers with windows, and M$ office, plus a (normal charged around 400 kroner) fire wall.

They got 3 Linux Nerds from Denmark to buy new computers for them, computers which were pretty equal to those they already had. The Linux guys made sure Ubuntu 8.04 was pre-installed on the computers, and then the familys' old computers were packed away. they were given a couple of internet pages to check on if they should need help, and also the number for the nerds. for fourteen days the family used the Ubuntu computers.

Well, before we get to the result there was some interviews. only one of them is really interesting:

I think it was the danish spokesman for opensource, and the "chief" of M$ distribution in Denmark or something. 

This was interesting. I'll start out by telling you that its only like 1-2% of the danish people who knows about Linux. so almost everybody uses M$ products. 

So the hostess of the program asks The open source guy if he thinks that Linux is fulfilling for peoples needs:

His answer is something like: "Yes. and i think you'll see with this family also that they are very satisfied. The quality of open source programs is rather high"

Hostess: "HM, lets just wait a minute for their response. now, (what ever th M$ guys name is)" 

Do you think that the open source is as good as he says: "Well, it has evolved in the later years, but i'd like to know what this family's opinion is"(I'm not sure that was what he said)

Hostess: "so what did you think about this free software?"

M$ man: "people buy our software because its the best. We use about 20 billion kroner each year at researching. The open source community copy everything they have from our programs, but they are always around 2-3 years behind us."

Hostess goes to the couch where the family is sitting and asks: "so are you satisfied with this free software?"

Father (using computer each day at work): "yes absolutely, its fantastik something free can be this good. its just as good as the thing we're paying for"

Mother: "it can do everything i need"

Son: "easy to use"

daughter: " yeah"

Hostess: "so if you were about to buy three new computers, would you buy it with windows, or without and install Linux?"

father: "sure buy without, cheapest and just as good"

Mother: "yeah, it's great."

Then they just told us that you could save around 3000 kroner on a laptop if you buy it without fire wall, windows and office.

What i got left is to say, this is a documentary, they did give the family 3 computers with this on. its not a act.


Please feel free to leave any commentary, in fact I'll start:

I hate that M$ Dude for telling us that opensource copy everything. i don't think that i cannot find something in every windows version that isn't copied from another system... 

hope you enjoyed hearing that Ubuntu Advertised on television, even in this small country

Ejoy your fantastic Linux                      -Rasmus
