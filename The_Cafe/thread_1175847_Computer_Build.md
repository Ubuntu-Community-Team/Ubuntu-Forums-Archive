### Post by Skripka on 2009-06-01
> **-gabe-noob- said:**
> Would: 
APEVIA X-Gear ATXB5KLW-BK Black Steel ATX Mid Tower Computer Case 420W Power Supply - Retail work? 


Also I would downgrade to 
AMD Athlon 64 X2 7750 Kuma 2.7GHz Socket AM2+ 95W Dual-Core black edition Processor Model AD775ZWCGHBOX - Retail 

For the sake of making the computer cheaper

That box will work, and be cheap.  But, the trade off for cheapness, is that it will be hard to work on/in, and will in all likelihood make for a computer that runs loud due to poor heat dissipation and 2 small (loud) fans.

NOTE-on the NewEgg site, they picture at LEAST 2 rather different cases in their pics.  That alone raises my suspicions.

When I build machines I buy Lian-Li boxes, and buy PSU separately.  I've had only bad experiences buying cheapo case/PSU combinations.  Yes it costs more coin, but it makes for easier build/maintenance and cooler and quieter running machines.  I bought a Raidmax case/PSU box once.  The crate was cheap, and looked swank...was a PITA to work on, and came wired completely wrong (wrong connectors, wrong wire coloring-everything).

To each their own.  If you're out to build cheap, that box will do...but it might start to grate on your nerves after a while.

### Post by gn2 on 2009-06-02
What will the computer be used for?
No need to spend money on components you don't need.....

If you are going to do it, get a good case and a good PSU, they'll last through several modifications and upgrades and money spent on them is money well spent.

I would suggest a Seasonic or Corsair (made by Seasonic) PSU and an [Antec Solo]("http://www.antec.com/Believe_it/product.php?id=MTk=") case.

Look at the [TDP]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_Design_Power") of a CPU, if you like a quiet PC lower is definitely better, look to be 65w or less.

Graphic card... if you don't play games or do 3D modelling etc, just get a motherboard with Intel on-board graphics.

These are just my opinions and preferences.....

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-06-02
The GTX260 is a good choice as long as you get the newer model with 200+ stream processors. But it requires a reasonable amount of power, and I don't think a 430 watt PSU will cut it. I'd advise 500 watts or more; I think Nvidia advise that amount too. Make sure it's a brand-name PSU too, otherwise it probably won't be able to sustain a good wattage.

I'm also wondering why you're getting an IDE DVD burner. Go for SATA, they are just as cheap now, and in particular you should be buying a Pioneer as they are fast and reliable. A little more expensive ($10 maybe) but it's worth it.
