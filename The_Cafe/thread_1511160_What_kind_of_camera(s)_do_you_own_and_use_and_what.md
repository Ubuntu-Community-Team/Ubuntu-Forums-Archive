### Post by t0p on 2010-06-16
**Cameras I own:**

**Pentax SF7**
with a SMC-Pentax-M f1.7 50mm, SMC-Pentax-M f3.5 135mm, and a Pentax PKA-fitting 35-80mm zoom.  Autofocus body, manual focus lenses.  Go figure.

**Zenit TTL**
with a Helios-44M f2 lens.  55mm I think, and a Helios f2.8 135mm.  That Zenit is built like a tank, and is about as heavy as a MBT too.

**Polaroid Impulse AF**, 600 system.  Some Dutch outfit has started making Polaroid-compatible film again recently, I might buy some to play with.

All the above are film cameras, and I haven't used any of them in years.  My current camera is a **Fujifilm S5700**, one of those "bridge" cameras (a glorified compact).  It isn't too bad I suppose, the lens is fairly nice and it has a decent zoom range, but what can you expect from a sensor the size of a pinhead?  I really do want to get myself a DSLR - preferably a Pentax or Samsung so my lenses will fit and I won't have to buy a load of new ones.  But I'm currently too poor to buy even a used DSLR that's any good.  Bah!

One thing I love about digital photography: you can snap away like a madman then go through the thousands of pix later and pick out the good 'uns.  That's how I worked with film too, but that was an expensive game!
