### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-10-08
I'm pretty much a hack when it comes to Inkscape, but I figured an icon for Iceweasel was a decent way to learn.

(EDIT: If you don't know what [Iceweasel]("http://www.gnu.org/software/gnuzilla/") is, start [here ]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=272791")and [here]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=270631").)

I used the Tango globe and colorificated it (so it doesn't look like a sparkly blue dot), then traced a picture of a weasel I found on Google. I wanted his head to peek around the globe, with the tail around back. The tail is supposed to look icy, but at 48 pixels square, everything gets so small that it doesn't really look like anything, i guess.

Anyway, you're free to pick this up and improve upon it. And if you don't like it or you think it's awful, don't tell me. I know it already. :rolleyes:

**UPDATED: 10/10, 0915 MST**

First, yes there are Debian folks who are watching this thread, so in that sense, this is a good place to make comments regarding the icon.

Next, in case you hadn't heard, we (actually, I think it was gstubbs or maybe .t. -- thanks, whoever :) ) started a page in the wiki for posting variations on the icon, or new versions if you want in on the fray.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IceWeaselIcon](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IceWeaselIcon)

Next, the best way (at present) to make your opinion known on the icon(s) is to post your comments on that wiki page. I think I could add a poll to this thread, but to be honest, my fear is that if I add a poll, and then we get a real winner of an icon later on, I don't think I can add an option for it. Maybe one of the moderators/admins could help with that, just so a really good one doesn't get left out.

Lastly, a word of note, we have a digg page, just to get attention and hopefully bring some quality talent into the fray (I still count myself as a rank newbie with Inkscape):

[http://digg.com/linux_unix/Iceweasel_Icons_in_the_Ubuntu_Wiki](http://digg.com/linux_unix/Iceweasel_Icons_in_the_Ubuntu_Wiki)

Cheers, and remember ... *sudo aptitude install inkscape*!

**UPDATED 10/10, 2155 MST**

I put two new pages up in the wiki -- one for IceDove (the Thunderbird equivalent) and IceApe (SeaMonkey's equivalent). The Debian folks are keen to get icons for those as well, so if you feel artistically inclined, jump in. Remember that you can start from scratch if you like.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IceDoveIcon](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IceDoveIcon)
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IceApeIcon](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IceApeIcon)

I put up an IceDove icon, and I'll post the svg here in case you want something to start with. I don't think I'll start new threads for them. (Edit: I replaced the IceDove ones here with a dove-plus-envelope. I'm not sure why I had a globe on there. :-k Thanks, manaicmusician.) 

IceApe is a real challenge. Good luck with that one! :rolleyes:

**UPDATED 10/11, 2220 MST**

I've given it a solid attempt, but the IceApe icon eludes me. I'll put my version here and in the wiki, in hopes that someone might receive a burst of inspiration -- perhaps even *divine *inspiration -- and come up with a winner.

I think, for once, the mascot looks icy. but aside from that, I'm having a little trouble getting the gorilla to look like I want. (As I understand it, an [ape]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ape") is collective noun for a group of primates -- which incudes gorillas and, curiously, humans. Maybe I should have put a person there. ;) )

I can't upload the svg, since there's a five-file limit for uploads. I'll try to tack it on in [a later post]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=1607610&postcount=121") in this thread. (Follow the link if you want it.)

Maybe I'll try it again with a stuffed-animal ape. That might be cute. ... :-k

### Post by ericesque on 2006-10-12
> **.t. said:**
> Yeah. Me too. As I wrote on the Wiki, I wonder, psychologically speaking, why that is...

It's because we read left to right.  Our brain is conditioned to expect more information as we move to the right.  When you look at the icon (with the head on the right) it feels balanced because you have negative space on the left, a bit of the globe as you move to the center, and the weasel's head when you end up on the right.  It is sequentially more complex.

If the head is on the left, you start with the most complex imagery and end up with negative space.  This causes our mind to feel lost when we end up in the negative space area.  There's nothing at the right of the icon to help our eye move down and back to the left of the icon again.

Edit: although it looks like Cookiebearo already explained the concept similarly on the wiki...
