### Post by stuh84 on 2007-10-04
I have had a few problems with my HP laptop for a couple of reasons, I wouldn't recommend going with them to be honest. The DVD-RW drive works intermittently, and when I consulted HP they said reinstall the driver (how will that make a difference if it does it when it had XP on, Ubuntu, and did it before it had booted an OS?). 

Also, the on board sound they use, its very strange, it takes over the system yet has no CPU usage. I haven't listened to music on it for a long time like I used to, but it use to stop my system from doing anything else for a while.

These two issues aside, for Linux its not great, but not bad either. Everything worked alright, apart from the wireless card, but the Broadcom BCM4318 is notorious for that anyway. The onboard ATI graphics were a pain too, only just in the past month got compiz working after owning it over a year.

From what I've noticed, very little has changed in HP laptops, so I'd still be wary of going with them, still use the same chassis in most cases, so can be quite iffy.

IBM/Lenovo seem to have a good reputation around here, so I'd say you are likely to be alright with them
