### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-11-05
Gnome and Windows 8 also both take a lot of design cues from Android - Gnome more openly than Windows. Now that Gnome has added the app drawer icon to the Favorites bar, which itself behaves much like the Android hot seat, they're being fairly straightforward about it. The transparent black panels and dialogues are an Android cue, too, and the simplified app front-ends for the core Gnome apps are mobile-inspired in a general sense. The tiling is just Win 7, not 8, snap, and app switching is a bit of Android and a bit of Compiz with no Win 8 involved that I can see.

I kinda wish Gnome had taken a few *more* cues from Android's notifications, although the new clear-all feature is a nice step in that direction.
