### Post by kurt18947 on 2018-04-22
I've been a trackball user since dinosaurs roamed the earth. Well maybe not quite that long but just about:). I've been through a few models, had a Microsoft Trackball Explorer on Win 98 I think. I threw it out thinking it was defective when it was probably just Windows/drivers but I didn't know any better at the time. Given what those things go for on Ebay I sure wish I had put it in the junk cabinet instead. SWMBO has had a Logitech Cordless Trackman for years but it's getting tired so I began looking at alternatives. These are both on desktop machines so wireless is just another point of failure. I came across Elecom and said hmmmm. At least one reviewer felt the Elecom "huge" was a successor to the Microsoft trackball.  

Amazon has easy returns so what the hey.  I got the "huge" even though I have pretty small hands, I preferred the larger ball diameter, about 50-55 mm. Initially I thought getting the larger size was a mistake but after using it a few minutes was happy with my bargain. Fingers to manipulate the ball, thumb to scroll and press the left button. The main buttons work as expected and there are 3 additional buttons that have no function in Ubuntu. XEV recognizes them in 18.04, I believe there is a module required in kernels earlier than 15.xx for the 3 extra buttons to be recognized. The usual buttons - left click, right click, middle click, page forward and page back work fine in 16.04. I'm not sure what I'd do with the 3 buttons so for now they're decorative. SWMBO got the smaller trackball, it works just as well but is well, smaller. The ball is about 35 - 40 mm and my hand sort of drapes over the smaller trackball and sits more on top of the larger device. The larger device has a padded rest for the heel of my hand so it's comfortable and not fatiguing. Both are comfortable, it depends on your preference. Unique to Elecom AFAIK is a 'speed selector' switch. The larger trackball has 3 'speeds', the smaller has 2 'speeds'. Lower setting = more precise, higher setting = less ball movement for a given travel distance. For me, the high setting made the cursor act like it had chugged 3 cups of high octane coffee. Thing was pinging all over the place but middle setting is fine. I think I'm done with Trackball shopping for a while.
