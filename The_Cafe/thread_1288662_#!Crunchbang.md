### Post by K.Mandla on 2009-10-16
> **Grifulkin said:**
> Yeah I have worked with those before, so would you say you like Crux better than Arch, I'm always looking for new distros to check out might be worth while.
Yes and no. Crux's package selection is slim -- dare I say anorexic? -- compared to Arch-plus-AUR. You need to really, *really* know the guts of your machine before you can ever get started in Crux. And there's the whole compile-time issue, which annoys a lot of people.

But I knew Crux was better for me when I started rebuilding and recompiling entire Arch systems, because I was disappointed in Arch's speed. The difference people talk about between Ubuntu and Arch is just as dramatic between Arch and Crux. My 1Ghz machine booted in half the time between Ubuntu and Arch and half again between Arch and Crux -- as little as 16 seconds from Grub to desktop for Openbox on Crux.

Yeah, I like it, but no, it's not for everybody. 

Now -- back to the thread topic. :oops:
