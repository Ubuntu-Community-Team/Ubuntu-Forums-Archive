### Post by junior aspirin on 2008-04-04
this is what i understand, i may be wrong::

UK ISP's are not allowed to check what you are downloading because of privacy laws, so the work has to be done by a 3rd party by obtaining your IP address when you download something, which they then pass on to your provider.

the general scheme of things is 3 strikes and they cut you off, which IMO is much better than being faced with a massive fine.
this scheme at present is voluntary, but unless ISP's embrace it it is said that legislation maybe passed to enforce it.
Virgin Media are the 1st to announce their support, but i read today that the director of the Carphone warehouse (owns talk talk i believe) objects to the plans.

that is a very basic overview, an im sure it is alot more complex than that.  

The are several flaws though. an ISP will not want to shun its customers, cutting them off means loosing out to competition.  In my opinion i doubt that this will be heavily enforced, but they will just show support an make it look as if they are doing something to keep the uk recording industry happy.

I cant see how this will work anyway, peerguardian etc already block specific IP addresses, so what it to stop that. and how will they prevent music blogs like the hype machine or search engines like SkreemR.

the main point is that legal P2P will still be ok, since they are not targeting Bittorrent/limewire/etc themselves, just the content.



Like i said i dont understand it fully, I just feel that it's almost impossible to prevent.
