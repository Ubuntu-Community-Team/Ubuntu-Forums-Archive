### Post by Ric_NYC on 2011-08-30
> **Facebook patents messaging and viewing private profiles**

Summary: Facebook has patented a system for viewing different people’s profiles and messaging them, based on the permission they have set.

Facebook has patented a “System and method for managing information flow between members of an online social network.” That’s the official title for patent number 8,010,458. Of course, Facebook as well as other social networks has been using this technology for a long time now, although Palo Alto’s is arguably the most complex.

In summary, this patent describes whether you can view a given person’s user profile and whether you can view his or her full profile or a mini version of it. If Facebook decides to fight its competitors with this patent, it could be difficult for other social networks to offer the basic feature.

For the most part, software patents exist only to stifle competition, whereas the original intent of patents was to give the creator an artificial monopoly to cover the R&D etc of developing the product. Software isn't tangible in the same sense, and software patents should rarely be validated. Most tech companies have a portfolio of software patents, not because they are necessarily producing anything useful with the patent, but to stop legal challenges by other companies (as they won't know how many patents they themselves are infringing on).

Anyway, for the OP's topic, social networking sites that existed before FB should be fine, you know, prior art and all. Also, the features described are trivial at best (just because the patent examiners don't understand doesn't mean the patent should be accepted), and I would be surprised if any claim actually held up in court. But they'll probably use it to heavy-hand small players that start to take off. I don't think they'd be silly enough to target Google....
