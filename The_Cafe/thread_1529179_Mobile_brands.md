### Post by gnomeuser on 2010-07-13
I've owned several phones, most recently a Nokia N900 and an HTC Hero.

Nokia are bleeding marketshare, they underestimated the importance of touch interaction in Symbian and were thus left years behind Apple, Microsoft and Android. Their MeeGo bet will likely prove unsuccessful, to little, to late and using a number of technologies that simply aren't suitable for this century (C++ and QT, you have got to be kidding me. Android just just an application development environment that runs solely in a webpage and requires no knowledge of programming).

HTC spew out phones at a pace that is unbelievable, however they tend to put them out with may glaring software errors and then provide poorly QA'ed updates, famously the Hero update was delayed more than 6 months and even then it had bugs.

I believe Apple currently makes the best hardware package, the best OS is by far Android (sadly with exception of the Nexus One nobody actually deploys Android correctly). The most desirable phone to me at the moment is by far the Motorola Droid 2. I am a sucker for it's near vanilla Android deployment and hardware keyboard. Next to that the Evo 4G looks really sexy but after having experienced the Hero I have made a sacred oath to ever give HTC money again.

I personally have sworn to stay with my N900 despite hating it's guts in several ways till such a time as the Android 3.x phones start rolling out. I am looking forward to the new UI work and I am also hoping that the battery life issues of this generation of phones will be less an issue in a years time.
