### Post by handy on 2009-11-11
I think of chronic fatigue syndrome to be something akin to what a mother feels like, when she is woken multiple times during the night to feed/change or whatever her babie(s), & then has to carry on through the day looking after the child(ren) & doing all of the housework, clothes washing, shopping, preparing food for not only the child(ren) but the husband too (when he comes home from work).

She is so often stuffed; incredibly tired, & quite often unappreciated.  The amount of work that she has to do in the day is multifaceted & easy for the uninformed observer to miss, as in not appreciate at all. 

People suffering from chronic fatigue syndrome can feel absolutely worn out, all of the time.  Some can adapt to the situation & function much better than they would have, if they ever felt so tired in the past (before the disease).

In the end, feeling like your head is full of a mud & feeling physically nauseous due to tiredness, is a totally personal experience, that I hope no one else ever knows about on this planet or elsewhere.
