### Post by Koori23 on 2008-05-25
I just finished a great book. Well, E-Book actually. It's 254 pages of pure geek bliss. It's a first hand account of how the author was put to task finding a $.75 cent accounting program error and ended up helping catch a international hacker and exposing elements of the Chaos Computer Club.

This guy literally out hacked the hacker by some rather crude ingenious methods.

Granted, the information is dated (We're talking mid 1980's).. Some clues to this? Well, the word "Internet" doesn't exist, ARPANET does.. 

The Author is Cliff Stoll [http://www.ocf.berkeley.edu/~stoll/](http://www.ocf.berkeley.edu/~stoll/)

He helped nail this guy [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markus_Hess](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markus_Hess)


Now comes the tricky part. If you type in "Cuckoo's Egg" into Google, you can literally download the entire book  off of the 5th or 6th link down. His own website hints that it's free, however it wasn't released BY the author as far as I can tell.

[http://www.ci.ulsa.mx/~elinos/docencia/herseg/cuckoo_egg.pdf](http://www.ci.ulsa.mx/~elinos/docencia/herseg/cuckoo_egg.pdf)


Anyway, I loved reading it.
