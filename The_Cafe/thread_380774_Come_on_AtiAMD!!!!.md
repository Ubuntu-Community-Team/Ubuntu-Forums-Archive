I doubt it. They feel that by doing that, they'll be revealing their trade secrets to their competitors. They don't want to go open source.

> What do you think about ati's support? How far is it going? Do you think they will support AIGLX? And, whats your experience with their products?
Their support can truly be expressed in one word: **crap**. It's absolute, disgusting, crap. IMO, ATI is pretty much in bed with Microsoft. All the signs are there; They support DirectX more than OpenGL, they contributed their experimental R600 chipset to the XBox 360, etc. Nvidia, on the other hand, supports OpenGL much more than DirectX. If you're going to be running Linux, they're really the optimal choice. Even if you disregard that ATI's drivers suck, Nvidia is still better for OpenGL. 

As for my experience with their products; I had an X200 chipset (something like that; I've forgotten) that was supposedly supported by fglrx. However, I could barely get DRI going on that thing, much less compositing. It was so pathetic that the open-source drivers, that were reverse-engineered, worked better than the ones provided by ATI. Suffice to say, I'm never buying ATI again.

edit: Since my fiasco with ATI drivers a few months ago, I've sucked it up and bought a GeForce 7600GT. It's a great card, and pretty cheap for the performance that it offers. I'm more than satisfied, and looking forward to getting another Nvidia card in the coming months.
