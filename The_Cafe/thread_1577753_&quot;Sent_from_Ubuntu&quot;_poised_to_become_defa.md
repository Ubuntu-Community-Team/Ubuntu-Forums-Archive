### Post by NCLI on 2010-09-19
[QUOTE=OMGUbuntu][s]A case is being made for a ‘Sent from Ubuntu’ signature to be appended to all mail sent from Ubuntu’s desktop e-mail application ‘Evolution’.

Seriously.

Whilst the signature is not enabled by default right now ‘that should change before the end of the cycle” [according to this bug report]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/evolution/+bug/615300").

Apple append a similar signature to mail sent from their mail clients on the iPhone and the desktop.

I’m not sure what relevance informing a contact that a message has been sent from a specific operating system does other than sit there waiting to be ignored.

“This is not classy. It actually feels pompous and rude.“ ~brettcornwall

This may come across like ‘positive’ spam but it’s still spam nonetheless. Certainly [readers of Lifehacker agree.]("http://lifehacker.com/5580178/the-worst-email-habits-and-annoyances-you-should-avoid-or-at-least-be-aware-of")[/s][/QUOTE]
[s][Source]("http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2010/09/sent-from-ubuntu-signature-to-be-added-to-all-mail-sent-from-evolution/")
[Bug Report calling for this "feature" to be removed.]("https://bugs.edge.launchpad.net/bugs/642839")

What do you think? Personally, I think it's a very, very bad move. Add your voice to the bug report if you agree.[/s]

The idea has been canned.:guitar:
