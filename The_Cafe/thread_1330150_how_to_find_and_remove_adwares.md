### Post by P4man on 2009-11-18
> **Grenage said:**
> If Linux ever really took off, I don't see any reason why such things couldn't exist in some form.  I imagine that a large chunk of users will run almost anything with root rights if the app tells them to.

The problem here is not permissions, its how you typically install stuff. 95% of the software is downloaded through repositories, these things are managed, built from source, checked and frequently rebuilt as new versions come out. Most of the rest is compiled from source by the end user. Where are you going to hide your malware?  How will you distribute it?

You'd have to obfuscate the source and trick all PPA maintainers into believing the app is valid. Or infect the binary of a valid app which will cause the signature verification to fail. Or you'd have to hack the PPA and modify the source of a valid app and then it will take perhaps 2 days before someone spots it.

Agreed its not impossible, but its like with biological virusses. If reproduction rate < death rate the virus dies out. In a world of managed PPA's and open source code, its gonna be really hard to make something that reproduces faster than its discovered and squashed.
