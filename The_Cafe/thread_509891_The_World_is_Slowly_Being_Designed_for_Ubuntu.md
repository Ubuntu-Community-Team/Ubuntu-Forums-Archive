### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-07-26
> **forrestcupp said:**
> But what if MS starts turning out stuff that's *not* crap like Microsoft Surface?
Great. As much as I like Linux and specifically Ubuntu, I use it because I really think it is the best operating system available. While I like open source, the thing I really think is important about open source is the idea of open standards.

From a consumer standpoint, I will be quick to drop a little bit of functionality (and possibly pay a little more) if it means I'm not going to be at the mercy of a single software vendor. It is important to me that a company cannot stop innovating and simply rely on vendor lock-in to keep bringing customers back - and that interacting with the large already locked in customer base will keep bringing new customers.

If Microsoft ever started publishing their specs (not necessarily their code) so that other companies could write competing software using the output files from Microsoft products, *and* starts turning out high quality software, I could see switching back to Microsoft products.

Before I go back to Microsoft, they're going to have to make significant changes in their business model. I'm not counting on that happening any time soon, but I won't say it can never happen.
