### Post by uberdonkey5 on 2008-09-09
> **Redptc said:**
> Can you dual boot on these eeepcs or is that not possible? I was planning to get one for 6 month trip to the UK.

Possible, but obviously have to do it in order of XP, then linux (whatever version you want). Also, need more than 4GB (need SD card or USB drive, but then its just like running ubuntu or xandros or whatever off your USB drive). Also why would you?

Asus did a wonderful job with 4GB eee pc. I LOVE mine. I would go for this rather than more expensive ones (I even went for 7 inch not 9 inch screen) partly cos you always worry about expensive computers being stolen, but bottom of line eee pc is cheap (250 euros here i.e.about 3 US dollars*)

As far as linux version I would DEFINATELY try to get that. I find it hard to believe that you cannot get linux version in australia. Indeed, it may be cheaper to ship out linux version to oz rather than buy windows version. As it is pre-installed it works seemlessly. With wireless, webcam, internet browser, skype, office, games etc its just perfect as it is. You just need to realise that ctr+alt+T is needed to get into terminal and access synaptic package manager.

I use mine for work whilst travelling (store data on SD card and backup regularly) and for up to date surf forecasts (have vodafone connect pen plugged in so I am completely mobile). This thing literally gives me orgasms its so cool. Indeed I use it more than my 2GB duocore dell laptop because from pressing on to connecting to internet is about 30 secs and it turns off even quicker.

Indeed, I have ripped loads of my DVDs onto a 4GB USB drive which I watch (seemlessly) on my asus when I travel.

Biggest problem with asus is that you need to download software to get into advanced mode (i.e. to work as a proper, completely alterable linux operating system). Indeed, I considered installing ubuntu on it, but now I think I will leave it in simple mode (and keep the Xandros OS) because it is stable and it works. If it was my ONLY computer I would put ubuntu on, but its fully compatible with my files in ubuntu. Only problem with installing another operating system is that Eee PC has this cool 'factory reset' feature which you won't then be able to use: Basically, if things bugger up, remove your SD disk (where you should store your data), then press factory reset button or (F9 I think) on boot up, and the system is restored to original OS.  I must stop before I burst a blood vessel.

Basically, I REALLY REALLY recommend asus eee pc 4GB (maybe you want bigger screen though) and get like a 16GB SD card to put in it. It is the perfectly designed computer. My friend uses it at work to connect to the most powerful computer in portugal (he he) - just connects a remote keyboard and monitor.
