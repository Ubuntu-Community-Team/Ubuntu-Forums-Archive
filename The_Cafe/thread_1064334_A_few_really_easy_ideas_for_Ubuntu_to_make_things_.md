### Post by cwortman on 2009-02-08
There are a few tweaks I always apply to Ubuntu. I know a lot of people who use Ubuntu who do the same in our local LUG.

There are 97 of us and I think we represent a particularly large number of Ubuntu users. We recently switched to Ubuntu from other various distributions. When installed, the following was done almost immediatly:
100% of us installed "Restricted Drivers"
95 removed Open Office
93 installed Wine
100% installed Flash 10
100% added AdBlock Plus and Element hider
100% installed either VLC player or the Ugly set

So I propose the removal of OOo, inclusion of flash, inclusion of Restricted Drivers, inclusion of Wine, inclusion of adblock and element hider, and include VLC Player. If you want to further make life easier get rid of Totem and Rhythmbox. I have made a rehashed CD and it cuts off ~230MB of information. It would give room for improvement of the OS and let the user add what they want and need from Add/Remove.

Thoughts? Love it, hate it, ideas of your own please. We would love to hear from you.

Posted from Christopher Wortman during the first half of this meeting we would love to hear responses for next week. We might even build our own iteration of how an OS should be done. Minimal as possible allowing the user to add what he or she wants. We here are of the mind of if you can't include what a guessed 80% of users add, remove some unused pointless MB and allow the user to add it later. Focus on usability, discoverability, and minimalism. The more simplistic you make it the less can go wrong.

### Post by MaxIBoy on 2009-02-08
Imagine there's a guy in an Italian food restaurant, and he's yelling at the manager, saying, "Why don't you serve Chinese food instead? I like Chinese food better! It's not as if it would be very hard to stop making Italian food and start making Chinese food!" Now imagine that there's also a Chinese restaurant right across the street from the Italian one.

This excellent post by Ayisu is a very worthwhile read, and really should be stickied:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=58017&](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=58017&)



VLC and the Ugly set cannot legally be handed out within the United States (and a few other countries.) Technically, it's not legal to install these codecs in the United States without paying for them (you can buy them legally in the Ubuntu store.) 

No one really cares, everyone installs them anyway, but they can't be included in the default install or else Canonical (the company that sponsors Ubuntu) would get the everloving crap sued out of them. (Other distros that are based around a loose organization of people don't have to worry, because there's no one person or company to sue.) 

With things like Flash 10, even though Canonical has permission from Adobe to put it in the default install, a lot of people wouldn't like that for philosophical reasons. See this article for a few of those reasons:
[http://www.fsf.org/about/what-is-free-software](http://www.fsf.org/about/what-is-free-software) 
Here, Free Software does not mean "software that doesn't cost money." It means "software that gives you **freedom**." One of the main goals of Ubuntu is to create a distro that gives you as much freedom as possible. Other distros, like Linux Mint, have different design goals, and they *do* include proprietary software by default. 


As far as removing Open Office goes, allow me to quote from [this page]("http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu") on Ubuntu's official website:
>  **Everything you need comes on one CD,** providing a complete working environment. Additional software is available online. 

  The graphical installer enables you to **get up and running quickly and easily**. A standard installation should take less than 25 minutes. 

  Once installed your system is immediately **ready-to-use**. On the [_desktop_]("http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu/desktopedition") you have a full set of productivity, internet, drawing and graphics applications, and games.
