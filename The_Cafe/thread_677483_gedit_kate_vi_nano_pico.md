### Post by michaeljt on 2009-06-26
joe is a great (and very small) console text editor.  I first tried it out at the time when emacs seemed too big for my 120MB Slackware 3 partition (I was not used to needing 20MB for a text editor!)  It's very best feature is its brilliant online help (like nano/pico, but better), which makes learning it a doddle, and it is much more powerful than those two, although less than the two giants of course.  And it has grown over the years, now it weighs in at over 300KB :)

Although I personally hate vi(m), it is still well worth learning, as it is the only editor apart from ed which is pretty much guaranteed to be around on any *nix system you find yourself at.
