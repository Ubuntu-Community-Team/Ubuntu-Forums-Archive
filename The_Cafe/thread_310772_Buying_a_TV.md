### Post by rsambuca on 2006-12-01
If you must have "thin for cue stick room", then you will be looking for either a flat-panel LCD or Plasma.

Which one you want depends on the room conditions.  LCD's do not have as good contrast as plasma displays, but are better if you have a lot of light in the room.  ie bright windows during the day.  

Plasmas, on the other hand, have much better contrast than LCD's, but due to the gas-filled screen, all have a shiny reflective screen that isn't as good in certain lighting conditions.  Some of the higher end  plasmas have pretty good anti-glare coatings, though.

Most plasmas today have a special anti-burn-in feature that shifts static images by a pixel or two so you don't get logo's burnt into your screen coating, but if you leave it on a channel with a news ticker or something, it will definitely burn in after a while.

If you are getting a 42' TV, I definitely would NOT bother with 1080p, as for television viewing, you would have to be sitting very close to notice any difference between that and a 720 or 768 TV.

I also happen to agree with eriefisher, if you can afford the space, I would look at a Rear Projection DLP as they have great contrast, and you can either get way bigger for the same price, or save a ton of cash for the same size as a flat-panel display.

A few months ago, I was originally looking for a 42" plasma or LCD because of all the hype, but after a ton of research and sampling a few, we ended up getting a 65" 1080p DLP and we couldn't be happier!

Good luck with your purchase!
