I just caught wind of a new music file format that's supposed to change the recording industry again...it's called MusicDNA.  It's essentially an MP3 compression with extra layers of information that link the file with other music (based on things like style, aggressiveness, mood, etc.) and can also be regularly updated by the artist/label to include updates such as lyrics, concert ticket offers, and other promotions from the artist.  Also, from what I gather, the software to get the most benefits from this new file type will be free to download.

Now, my question is, do you think this will be embraced by Ubuntu with its philosophy?  I know that .mp3 isn't for some reason embraced by the philosophy, which is why a lot of GNU programs use .ogg instead...  But this revolution in technology will be difficult (impossible?) to replicate with an alternative, but since Ubuntu tries to stay at the cutting edge of technology, how could we afford to fall behind the times with something this major?

Essentially, I guess I'm asking the more seasoned fellow Ubuntu-ers how you think the community will respond to this.  I'm curious to see how it's going to pan out.
