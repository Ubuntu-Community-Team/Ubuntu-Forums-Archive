It's the second most used browsers, more users than Chrome and Safari combined, and since the first most used browser (IE) isnt' involved in this yet, I think sites will be reluctant to ignore Firefox, considering that's the biggest HTML5 supporting browser. I'm not sure what to think personally, but I know that h.264 is a good codec, and it has the advantage of hardware support (iPhone, VDPAU, etc.), and I know Theora can still be a good codec, but it's honestly hard to compare, since there's a lot of encoding options, etc. that can make a huge difference. All I know is that things are a mess, and something needs to happen that will allow a standard codec to work on all the browsers (or have both codecs somehow work on all the browsers).
