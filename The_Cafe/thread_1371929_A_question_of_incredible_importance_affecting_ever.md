### Post by thatguruguy on 2010-01-03
I've been an English speaker for my entire life.  By which I mean I speak English, the language.  I am not, myself, English.  I am, and shall remain for the foreseeable future, a citizen of the United States of America. That being said, I have noticed that some English speakers on this forum occasionally use the expression, "That works a treat."  I presume, without any particularly compelling evidence, that such poster use the Queen's English.  Here in the States, we occasionally use the expression, "That does the trick."

As anyone who has ever spent a night out as a child on Hallowe'en knows, there is a distinct difference between a "trick" and a "treat".  They are, in fact, polar opposites.  However, it appears that a thing which "works a treat" may, in fact, "do the trick."  Is it possible to resolve this dilemma in any meaningful way?

I look to you, my fellow Ubuntu users, to resolve any potential conflict.  Thank you for your time, and best of luck.
