### Post by mendhak on 2011-07-25
What about the other way around?  GMail has a POP 'download' feature.  Go to Mail Settings, then click on 'Accounts and Import'.  There should be a section for checking mail using POP3.  You can specify your POP3 server and credentials there and GMail will regularly import your emails into your GMail account.  All of those emails will be labeled with your POP3 account email address so that you can spot it easily.

### Post by mendhak on 2011-07-28
It should still work - I'm describing the 'mail fetcher' which isn't the same as mail forwarding.  GMail acts as an email client (just like Thunderbird/Outlook) in this case and simply requests emails.  Basically, if you can use Thunderbird or Evolution or some other email client with your ATT mail, then the GMail 'mail fetcher' should work too. 

Try it in the mail settings as I've posted above.  The first time you do it, it'll take a long time if you have a lot of emails and after that it should be regular-ish.
