### Post by 47of74 on 2010-04-02
> **szymon_g said:**
> congratulations!

do you still remember answers and number of questions ;)?

but seriously- how long does it take? did you take course + exam or exam only?

The company I work for gives us access to SkillSoft and I took the CBT courses provided - but those were for the old LPIC-1 objectives and covered a lot of stuff that's been deleted or moved to LPIC-2.  I then got a copy of [LPIC-1 in Depth]("http://www.amazon.com/LPIC-1-Depth-Michael-Jang/dp/1598639676/ref=pd_sim_b_3") by Michael Jang.  That book reflected the current requirements.  I read through that book over the course of the next couple weeks, writing a bunch of notes on each chapter.

The exams are 60 questions and you are given up to 90 minutes to take each exam, but it didn't take me even an hour to go through the exams.  Jang says sometimes people are given more than 60 questions and more time if LPI has beta questions that they want to test out before putting in the pool of regular questions.
