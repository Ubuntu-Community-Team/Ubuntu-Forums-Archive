### Post by arvevans on 2009-12-29
Blogs are problematic for discussing an on-going project, because the information orientation is backward (LIFO) and thus does not represent the order in which you did the work.

As the Internet evolves, we old geezers have seen it change from an email and data retrieval system (WAIS & Gopher), to data storage & retrieval (WWW), to Social networking (Facebook, etc.), to cloud-based computing.  Blogs were one of the many fads along the way, but now seem to have been replaced by new usage methodology.

### Post by t0p on 2009-12-30
"Are blogs a waste of time?" - what a sweeping question.  You might as well ask "Are books a waste of time?" or "Is conversation a waste of time?"  There are so many different types of blogs, of such varying quality, that it's impossible to characterize them all as useful or useless.  You have to consider each one on a case-by-case basis.

I think [my blog]("http://ihatehate.wordpress.com") isn't a waste of time; and my very small readership might agree.  I don't follow many blogs, but the ones I read regularly are certainly *not* a waste of time.  In my opinion.  I certainly wouldn't bother subscribing to a blog that I considered a waste of my time.  Even if a blog just gives me an amusing way to kill 5 minutes, that's not a *waste* of time.  Whereas picking through 100 posts per day to find just 1 or 2 items of interest amongst the detritus certainly *would* be a waste of time. Well, a waste of *my* time anyway.  Maybe you find it a useful way to spend your time.
