### Post by JA2 on 2007-04-30
While I agree with most of maniacmusician's points, I think everyone has a choice in the OS they want to use. 

One point to note is that people don't always have the options laid out in front of them when they first enter the world of computing and so whatever they begin with tends to be generally accepted as the standard for everything going forward, however mediocre and flawed that option may be (in my opinion, of course).  Depending on how much time they spend on that first OS - and the level to which they educate themselves about other choices, it might be quite difficult for them to migrate away from the environment they've always been comfortable with to something that's almost entirely different in look, feel and control. 

The question at hand is about choosing between Windows or Ubuntu, but it could just as easily be a debate on Windows over MacOS or Windows over Solaris.

I've been one of the fortunate few with the opportunity to evaluate many different OS platforms between my high school days back in the 80s and my present day job as a Network Administrator, and in the end have found the Unix/Linux platform to be the one that best served my computing interests.  Someone else may say the same about Windows or MacOS, but I can't knock them because it was their choice.  All I can hope is that they made an educated choice, rather than accept what some sales ad or store clerk told them.
