### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-11-07
Sometimes I think I'm really strange.

The other day I was calling up a business and got put on hold. It was one of those phone systems where, instead of playing the radio or digitized music loops, it has a monophonic or duophonic rendition of Greensleeves.

I don't know why, but I realised that this music was probably being generated in real-time by a Motorola 68000 CPU. It made me think "What a waste - many of my childhood memories are of sitting down in front of a whole computer powered by this CPU... and someone's making it play crummy 'hold' music 24/7."

I felt sorry for the CPU, having so much potential, but instead being worked for menial tasks in this demeaning fashion.

Has anyone else thought about this and felt bad? What other insignificant tasks do classic computer CPUs perform?
