### Post by rkh on 2008-02-07
I have one. Rather, my kids got one as a Christmas present from their grandparents. But, I must admit that I have spent a whole lot more time playing with it than they have.

Sugar (the desktop interface) takes some getting used to. There is nothing really wrong with it, it just is very different from what I'm used to. 

Physically, the thing feels solid. Moreso than the Dell Latitude I use for work. The keyboard is small and rubbery, but looks like you could spill coffee on it without damaging it. Battery life is very good. I haven't done any testing, but you can run the thing for several hours on a charge. 

Usage wise, I find myself using it to causually browse through stuff, check mail, goof of while sitting on the couch. I wouldn't try to do any major work on it withoug hooking up an external keyboard. 

The wireless internet stuff works well. Public hotspots sometimes take a couple of tries to connect, but that's not unusual. WEP is supported out of the box. WPA requires an upgrade to the latest version of the software. Instructions are here: [http://wiki.laptop.org/go/WPA_Manual_Setting](http://wiki.laptop.org/go/WPA_Manual_Setting).

All in all, this is a really neat little device. Small and light, perfect for travelling and solid as a rock.
