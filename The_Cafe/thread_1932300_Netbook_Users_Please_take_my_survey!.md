### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-02-28
Yeah, I don't really see the benefit of netbook-specialized distros, particularly as regards storage space. I have a 32 gig SSD. The system, apps, and swap take up six of those gigabytes. I could have fewer apps and take up four instead; great. But the difference between 26 gigs and 28 for my personal files is not really significant. The same goes for 10 and 12 on a 16 gig drive. 

And yeah, not all netbooks even use SSDs. But the ones that don't have slower performance, and not all netbooks have two gigs of RAM, so some other machines are better off with a lightweight environment, which I *don't* need. There just aren't a lot of unifying factors even within the netbook class to make a special distro meaningful. (The only real, across-the-board concern that netbook users have in common is the tiny, standardized 1024x600 resolution, but that's addressed, as much as possible, by Unity.)

Using a modified distro just seems like asking for extra problems. I'd rather just use vanilla Ubuntu and make what tweaks I'd like to make, which I'm going to end up doing anyway.
