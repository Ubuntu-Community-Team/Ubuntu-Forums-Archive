The "Standard" interface is for computer users who are used to working in a normal Linux desktop environment.  It's what you'd get if you installed Xandros on any PC. ### Post by aysiu on 2008-04-08
I'll probably post a longer review of this on my Ubuntucat blog once I've had a chance to extensively test it (use it for at least two weeks), but here are my initial impressions from just opening the box and fiddling with it for five minutes: [list][*]**It is really small.** Yes, I know I was supposed to know that. I knew the measurements before I bought it. I'd read other reviews saying it was small. I'd even seen YouTube videos where they would compare it to people's hands or larger laptops. I guess the hand next to the Eee was supposed to make the Eee look small, but the videos just made the hand look like a huge hand. The thing is delightfully small, and you won't really know just how small it is until you see it in person. [*]**It feels solid (except the keys on the keyboard).** It doesn't feel like a flimsy piece of plastic. It feels like a solid laptop that you don't worry too much about breaking. The keys, on the other hand, feel like cheap plastic, but I guess they do the job. [*]**It really does start up quickly.** I'm not just talking about the boot-time. I'm talking about the whole process. You open the box, pop in the battery, press the power button, and almost immediately you answer your username and password, time zone, etc., and you're off and running. [*]**The screen isn't that small**. Well, yes, of course, it is that small. We all know it's 7" and only 800x480 resolution, but it's usable. I did have concerns that it would be *so* small that I would curse myself for not waiting for the 9" screens Asus will be releasing later. It's usable, and that's what counts. [*] **There is no warranty sticker**. In the initial release of the Eee, there were a lot of complaints and confusion about whether removing the sticker over the RAM door voided the warranty. The sticker said it did, and Asus said it didn't. Well, I just bought this thing (it arrived today), and there is no sticker... so there should be no more confusion. Removing the door to the RAM alone does not void the warranty. [*]**The simplified interface takes getting used to** It appears to be using some kind of mix between KDE and IceWM, but it doesn't behave like either one. I was trying to find a way to get the file manager up so I could make a ~/.icewm directory, but there was no easy way to do this. I'll have to do some research and fiddling around. I don't mind the simplified GUI, but the SilverXP theme is killing me, and I need some ThinBlack goodness.[*]**The small keyboard isn't that bad**. Maybe it's because I don't have monster hands (my fingers are pretty slim), but I find the keyboard usable. Some people have said in their reviews that it would take hours to touch-type, but I felt pretty comfortable after a few minutes. The right Shift key is awkwardly placed, but it took only a few misses before I was able to incorporate that into my touch-typing.[/list] Well, that's it for now. I'm very excited about my new computer and will write more once I've had more time to really get to know it.
