### Post by RobertSwipe on 2010-06-25
I've now had a Streak for a couple of days. The display is terrific, but the pocketability size is going to take some getting used to. It does fit into a shirt top pocket, but I keep getting told off by my SO for ruining it (the shirt that is). Not sure that's true, though... It also fits in jeans pockets but it somewhat awkward when going upstairs or slobbing out on the settee in front of the TV.

It ships at present with Android 1.6, with a promise of upgrade to 2.2 sometime soon (I've seen September on one site).

As someone who generally uses a phone as a phone, it's far more than I would have chosen, but I think if I can alter my lifestyle slightly, it will become an essential tool.

Web browsing is great on it, and scrolling seems responsive. The only issue I've had with responsiveness is a few screens where touching an edit box or the "save" button doesn't seem to work always. I suspect this sort of thing may be fixed in 2.2. The edit box should bring up a keyboard, but you can always get around that by holding the "menu" button - it forces the keyboard to be displayed. When the "save" or "edit" buttons don't seem to work (not very often), it seems to have done the action anyway, and backing out of the screen hasn't lost anything.

The apps are generally good, and the apps store (called "The Market") has loads of stuff available, much of it free, and a proportion priced from £0.99 through to £7.99 (hmmm - the ol' 99p trick is a common feature).

It works with all my bluetooth gizmos, but I haven't yet linked it up to the car (a job for the weekend).

Sound is good, videos (the bits that I've tried in two days) seem good, and photos & videos taken on it are very good (compared to my old N95). Wifi works well, and the changeover from wifi to 3G seems smooth in the one situation where it was necessary.

I don't know yet if I can make VoIP calls. I haven't tried using the Dell PC Suite, and although there's no specific Linux synching software supplied, it works as a USB drive quite happily.

Lots more playing yet to be done!

### Post by Shibblet on 2010-08-19
I think I had to send it back.

Dell has released two versions of the phone.  One for $299.95 with a 2 Year AT&T contract, and an unsubsidized version for $549.95 without the AT&T contract.

I ordered the one without the AT&T contract.

Now, I was a bit disheartened when I opened the box and saw an AT&T Sim Card inside that needed to be activated.

"Oh, that must be for people who get the $299.95 version, and need to sign up with AT&T."  I said to myself.  "They could do it quickly over the phone."  

WRONG!!!  The unsubsidized version is still locked for use only with AT&T.

I called Dell immediately.  "Can I get an unlock code?" I asked.

"Certainly." replied the nice lady on the phone on Monday the 16th of August, 2010.

Today, August 19th, 2010; a Thursday, I received a phone call from Dell, and I was ABSOLUTELY POSITIVE that they were calling with my unlock code.

WRONG AGAIN!!!

They were calling to let me know that they were unable to get the codes from AT&T, and that they weren't sure when they would be getting them.

Oh well, looks like the Streak is going back to Dell.  One day, when they have an unlocked version, I may jump back in, but until then...
