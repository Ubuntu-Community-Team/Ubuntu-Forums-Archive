### Post by 1clue on 2009-10-09
Personally, my income is protected by copyright law.  I'm a software developer.  People who steal software (or music or whatever) hold a low place on my scale of respect.

FWIW, I know somebody who has an older laptop.  For several years, half the hardware has not worked on that box, he figured it was just broken hardware.  He installed Ubuntu on it, and all of a sudden that old hardware started to work again.

Assuming that Windows gives you hardware compatibility is naive.  Assuming that Linux does NOT give you hardware compatibility is not quite so naive, but getting to be so.

In general, the companies who don't cooperate with Open Source for drivers (either by making them or by helping others make them by providing hardware specs) usually don't have good hardware anyway.  The companies who build good hardware have something to be proud of, and the software is just a thin veneer over a nicely built machine.  The companies who build junk let the software do all the work, and by cooperating with OS driver programmers they expose the cheapness of their junk to the world.

On top of that, if you buy a PC completely built, you've paid for a Windows license anyway.  If you build your PC from scratch, then you can pick your hardware.  Don't pick crappy hardware.  It will haunt you for the life of the machine, and cost you more in the long run.
