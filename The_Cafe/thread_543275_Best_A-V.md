### Post by arsenic23 on 2007-09-05
Last year we had a virus busting test using hard drive images from customers that had horrible cases of multiple infections.  Each virus program got to try and remove the viruses two ways, first we let it remove them with the infected hard drive mounted as slave and then we reimaged the drive and installed the app directly on the drive.  We tested everything on your list except Mcaffee, as well as a few other programs.

**Kaspersky** came in first place, having removed everything save for one or two files.
**NOD32** came in a close second.  It was able to find much more when the drive was a slave then when it was installed in the active environment.
Surprisingly **Avast** was third, but failed to detect and remove more then I'd want.
**Norton** and **AVG** both failed to remove large amounts of the infection, and Norton failed to install on the infected drive itself.

So as of last year I'd say Kasperksy was the best Windows antivirus solution, and that out of the ones on your list AVG and Norton come in last.  I've only been using Kaspersky and NOD32 in our shop since this test.
