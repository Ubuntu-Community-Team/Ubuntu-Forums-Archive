### Post by ugm6hr on 2013-02-10
This is obviously all nonsense.
However, the suggestion of running dual OS on a phone is not so ridiculous:
[LIST]
Work phones and Personal phones are often held by individuals
People like to be able to choose their personal phone (and OS to a certain extent)
Businesses like to be able to isolate their work IT (and phones to a certain extent) from security risks due to operator issues
[/LIST]

At present:
[LIST]
Blackberry allow separation of office and personal functions, albeit only on 1 OS
Dual SIM phones allow separation of call functions
[/LIST]

So a dual SIM / dual OS phone is appealing for some. Whether it is appealing enough to be running simultaneously, and thereby slowing both OS down, I'm not so sure.
