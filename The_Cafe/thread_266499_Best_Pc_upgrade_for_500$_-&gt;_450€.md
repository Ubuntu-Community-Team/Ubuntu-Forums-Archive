### Post by onioneater36 on 2006-09-27
In windows, I know you can dual head even if the 2 screens are different sizes.  Not sure if this is the case in Ubuntu, but if it is, I would recommend the 20" screen.  You could use the 17" screen to launch apps, keep a terminal window running, etc, and then use the 20" for applications/artistic work.  That would be pretty cool.

With the bling left over, you could upgrade to 2GB dual channel.  I am not sure, but I don't think having 4 dimms (4 x 512 mb) works as well as having 2 dimms (2 x 1GB DC), so you may want to try and sell the 1GB kit and get something like the Corsair 3500LLPro 2GB DC kit for that mobo ([http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16820145032]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16820145032")).

Also, don't discount upgrading to a dual core and then...

sudo apt-get install linux-686-smp

to setup the kernel to support the multi-core.
