### Post by TheFu on 2018-04-05
During some travel last year in a huge metro area, I found that google maps includes walking directions with ETAs. They assume high-speed walking, I looked up their assumptions.  Appears I'm more of a moseyer than a walker.  They also have bus and subway information in their directions.

Stoplights around here change dynamically based on traffic conditions.  Sometimes they are 10 seconds, other times they are 90 seconds with timing varied during the say.  The perform optimized timing to let the most vehicles through. The lights are all timed with others nearby, like a grid to move the most traffic possible in the primary direction necessary.  Nothing about the traffic light timing is public ... except that yellow lights cannot be shorter than 3 seconds, by law. On streets with higher speeds allowed, yellow lights last longer.
