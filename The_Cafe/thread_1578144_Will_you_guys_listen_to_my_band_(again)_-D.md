### Post by eriktheblu on 2010-09-22
Overall, I think it's good stuff. 

Good points:
High quality recording. You can always tell the home studios by the drums; you obviously went above that. 

Horns, despite the fact that it isn't popular, horns are a fairly essential part of a ska band. You not only have them but they are employed quite well.

Solid drums. The tonal characteristics are not the signature ska drums, but that's OK.

The following might come off as nitpicking, but I subscribe to the philosophy of always striving for perfection (despite the fact that we can never reach it). 

Points for improvement:
The string section is a bit bland. Some lead guitar work can a lot of flavor to a song. I'm not saying you need full on verse length solos, but a little flare occasionally. Oh, and the phaser/flanger effect on 2012 didn't really seem to fit for some reason.

The vocals seemed a bit rushed at times, so I would suggest working on the lyrics a bit more. It just didn't seem to flow right. If you have your own songwriter/lyricist, perhaps some classes in composition or literature might help. This also may be a contributing factor to the few times when your singer almost sounded out of breath. 

Vocal harmony was quite simple and a little boring. If there was a 3rd part, I couldn't make it out. I'm guessing your bass player doesn't sing?
