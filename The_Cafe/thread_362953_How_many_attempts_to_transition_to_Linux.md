### Post by Bob Sugar on 2007-02-16
I tried a couple times with Red Hate, then I tried Fedora whore, then I tried Scruse, then I went to Ubuntu, and then I tried Ubuntu again - and I'm keeping it this time.

The reason I never kept any of the other distros, and the earlier Ubuntu attempt was that everything was such a pain in the azz to manage, install, update, keep track of...  really, who needs 8 different apps for music, 4 word processors, 2 email programs, etc and so on. I'm keeping it on a laptop this time more as a learning tool and test bed, and will continue to use XP for serious work until a time comes when Linux and it's developers can come to consensus in terms of a common installer, media player, single editing tool, terminal interface, etc etc. (seriously - gnome, KDE, X so on and so forth? - For Christs sake, just put together a package that just works, with apps that just work, with utilities that just work, with support and drivers that just - well - work.)

Plus all the naming of all the programs is a night mare, the lack of GUI on a lot of programs - like Wine for instance, (duh, you want windows users to switch, make wine work for everyone. Simple) and the myriad of other user-unfriendly this and that's are what will always keep me from using it as a primary, and will also keep Windows at the forefront.

Just my two cents.

Now, having said all of that, I will say that Ubuntu is a great OS, albeit one that needs a f-ton of simplification and updating, but overall it's pretty functional. For the average Windows user looking for a change, it won't work, but for others, like myself who happens to be a MS sysadmin - or another more advanced user, or simply someone who is highly adaptable, it can be figured out.
