### Post by rhcm123 on 2010-10-07
As a user who is fond of both, I must say all 3 major OS's have their advantages and disadvantages: (I know this will create a minor firestorm)

*Windows:*
-Somewhat unstable, but I have had only 5 crashes in the >1 year life of my PC. Programs run great out of the box, espescially gaming, which I do a lot of. I don't like having to shell out $60/yr to Norton, but it comes with the package at this point. I don't use the tech support so I can't comment on that. My biggest problem with windows is**VERY SLOW BOOT**. And ram hogging. Im on 64 bit and have 6 GB so its ok, but with norton, a little bit of rainmeter, and various other background programs I easily use 2x what ubuntu uses. Some programs, like Microsoft office, are a lot better than their linux equivalents.

*Linux:*
-Stable. Period. Has never crashed unless I've done something completely idiotic to it. No support for windows programs, and WINE just dosent cut it yet. Alot of problems with media. Music is the only one that runs acceptably well. Program library has been greatly increasing though. Graphics are great once restricted drivers are enabled. To fix things (e.g. my sound didn't work on install) requires a little time and a cool head.

*Mac:*
Idiot-proof. That being said, it's not idiot-resistant. Mac viruses are springing up now that the average user has super-user capability and thinks they're completely immune. No program library besides the overpriced mac crap. A computer for begginers, and very overpriced.


I think the problem with people and computers nowadays is they have no desire to go and see 'oh i got an error message, lets google it and see how to fix it', execute the fix they find on the net, and then blame the OS for their ineptitude. (that's why macs are popular; they accept the average user's an idiot and built their computers/support around that concept.)
