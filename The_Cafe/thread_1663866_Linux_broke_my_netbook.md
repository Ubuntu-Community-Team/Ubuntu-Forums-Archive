### Post by Johnsie on 2011-01-10
There have been examples in the past of when badly designed drivers have permanently damaged hardware. These included drivers which put too much load in hard disks, drivers which didn't control the cooling fans properly, battery management toos which caused the battery to burn out quick than it should have, and drivers which bricked some networking cards. If you use Google you can find out more about this.

If a bad programmer is making a driver then the chances are that the software can have negative effects on the hardware. I'm not saying the article is true or false, just that it is indeed possible for Linux to break hardware.
