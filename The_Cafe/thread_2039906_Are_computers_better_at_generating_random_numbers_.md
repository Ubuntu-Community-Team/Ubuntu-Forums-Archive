### Post by vexorian on 2012-08-09
Computers are terrible at generating random numbers. They use pseudo random number generation. 

Hence why serious uses of random numbers use the environment to generate them.

As for your second question. The numbers themselves are not helpful in deciding. What is important is the method used. If you pick 5 numbers uniformly at random then any subset of 5 numbers is equally good as the other. 

If you are talking about passwords, then 1,2,3,4,5 are not bad for being "less random" but because it is more likely humans would pick them, but not because they are bad at thinking of random numbers, but because they are more likely to think not on random numbers but on numbers that are easier for them to remember.

### Post by JDShu on 2012-08-09
Human's are extremely bad at generating random numbers. We're chock full of [cognitive biases.]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_bias") The example that immediately comes to mind is the [clustering illusion]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clustering_illusion") (I had to look up the name) but basically when you tell a person to give you a sequence of random numbers, there will be less strings of repeated numbers than a truly random series would suggest.
