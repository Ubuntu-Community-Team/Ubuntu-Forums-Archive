### Post by FuturePilot on 2010-09-04
I don't think so. Windows treats flash drives differently than hard drives. In order for it to see more than one partition on a flash drive you basically have to flip the RMB bit on the drive itself. [http://www.ghacks.net/2009/04/17/partition-usb-flash-drives/]("http://www.ghacks.net/2009/04/17/partition-usb-flash-drives/") I looked into it once and decided it was more trouble than it was worth and ditched my multi-partition flash drive idea.
