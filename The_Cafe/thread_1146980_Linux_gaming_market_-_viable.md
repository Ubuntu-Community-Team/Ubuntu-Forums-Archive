### Post by samjh on 2009-05-03
An often-encountered subject on these forums is about why game producers don't develop games for Linux.  An often-said answer is that the market for Linux gaming isn't big enough.

I was interested to find this poll on the ETQW (Enemy Territory: Quake Wars) [official community website]("http://community.enemyterritory.com/"), about the platform they run the game on.  ETQW is available natively on Windows, MacOS, and Linux, so I think the results are quite credible, although its relatively small population of players (in comparison with giants like Counter Strike) might have skewed it somewhat.

**Which version of ETQW for you?**
Windows PC
65%
Mac OSX
4%
Linux
21%
Xbox 360
5%
PlayStation 3
4%
Total votes: 17521

So approximately 1-in-5 ETQW players use Linux -- more than the figures for MacOS, Xbox 360, and Playstation 3 combined!  That's pretty huge, and a very significant portion of their player market.

Does anyone know of other polls like these?

Could this be enough to signal game makers to take Linux gaming seriously and dedicate resources to port their games to Linux (in ID Software's case, they have one developer, "Timo", who did the Linux port for ETQW)?
