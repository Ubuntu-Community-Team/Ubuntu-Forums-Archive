### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-05-23
So I was considering making a switch to openSUSE. While I'm relatively cozy with Ubuntu right now, I do enjoy their design and many KDE tweaks that make it very usable. So I went to the IRC to ask some questions about the roadmap for 10.3, how upgrading will go, when KDE4 would come out, etc. As I was asking these questions, I realized that I would never have to ask them in Ubuntu or Debian, so I explained that I'd changed my mind, and wished them luck. Specifically, I wished them luck with 10.3, weathering this MS-Novell media storm, and said that I'm sure openSUSE is ready to take a big step (just being polite). Mistake!

Suddenly, a flurry of messages came out (as opposed to the two lone answers I got for my more than two questions) that I was "a nobody" and had "no f****g clue", etc. Keep in mind that I'm the one censoring those words; the chatters weren't so shy. I'm sure happy to be with the lovely Ubuntu/Debian community!
