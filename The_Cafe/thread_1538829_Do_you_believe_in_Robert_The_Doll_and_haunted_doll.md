### Post by gnomeuser on 2010-07-26
> **TheNerdAL said:**
> Either I'm really stupid or you're really smart! :P

I would recommend reading Carl Sagan's Demon-Haunted World as an introduction to thinking governed by reason, logic and evidence. It's a great book and an easy read.

One should keep an open mind, though it is foolish to let ones brain fall out in the process. That is what skepticism is all about, not naysaying. Demanding evidence to back any claim.

Also for clarity placebo is a level used to determine if something else has effect. A simple example would be if you want to test if a new medication is effective in addressing a problem. You take a group of people, then you have someone else divide them in two groups. Being double blind is important as a mere blinded test is apt to bias from your assumptions. One group gets the medication, the other group is the control who get placebo (typically some form of sugar pill). It is important that you do not know who is getting which treatment. You are looking for the natural variance in known non-treatment as the level and your medication has to be better than these results in a statistically significant manner to be considered effective.

There is no magic involved at all, it is all fairly simple, yet placebos have been a constant source of confusion for people who mistakenly have the impression it is real medication. 

Placebos can be useful in treatment as well but this is considered unethical and even dangerous. Such as the practice of giving out antibiotics for viral infections. This is known to not work and yet the patient will swear it did as they got better. There is no relation between the treatment and recovery, and this is what placebos are all about, expectations, yours and the doctors.
