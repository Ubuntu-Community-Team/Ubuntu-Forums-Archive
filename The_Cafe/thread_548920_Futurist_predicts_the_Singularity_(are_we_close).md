### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-09-11
This goes under the "RAV TUX" category of cafe posts.

[A futurist]("http://www.kurzweilai.net/articles/art0134.html?printable=1") has made some pretty in-depth, lengthy predictions about where we might be in 50 years, and how close we might be to the Singularity.  He has a fascinating take on a lot of subjects, one of which is that the rate of progress is not linear, but rather exponential over time.  IE: All of our discoveries compound on each other so rates of progress in everything double each year.

He thinks that if you can just live long enough (say to 2050 or 2075), Science will have probably perfected genetics to the point of immortality.  Or, technology will have gotten to the point of us merging with it to expand our life-spans, our brain capacity, etc.

Pretty interesting stuff.

There a difference between scientific innovation and wild speculation.  One leads to results, the other only serves to entertain future generations, like the link you posted.

It's one things to take careful observations from a telescope and deduce the Earth orbits the Sun, it's another thing entirely to make up terms like "Singularity" and speculate about something we know nothings about.  Frankly, we don't even know if it's possible to create non-biological, human-level intelligence or grant immortality, so speculating on what might happen if that happens is fiction, not science.
