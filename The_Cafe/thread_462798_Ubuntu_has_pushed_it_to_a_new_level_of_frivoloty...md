### Post by Lucifiel on 2007-06-03
That you know, it was actually able to install the drivers for my screwed-up onboard lan during the installation process. That I did NOT have any problems: Ubuntu uninstalling it, my pc misbehaving, etc.

If I had known about Ubuntu a few years ago, I would have not bought my lan card. 

And that, I was able to do word processing out of the box(though now, OpenOffice Word is broken 'cos of severe font issues) and that I had a variety of programs pre-installed. What was the best was that well, there was Synaptic. 

And well, it was damn great that drivers were ALL pre-installed. Man, I used to spend hours organising and labelling my programs in sub-folders and I'd have to write up lengthy .doc files as I often forgot what programs were for what. And under Windows, you know, you had to install drivers in a certain order or things could go out of control.  

Well, those last points are universal among almost all or all of Linux distros but I was pleasantly surprised.
