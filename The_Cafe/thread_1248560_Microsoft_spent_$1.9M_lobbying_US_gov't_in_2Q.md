> **Microsoft spent $1.9M lobbying gov't in 2Q**

Microsoft spent $1.9 million in 2nd quarter lobbying on intellectual property, other issues

    * On Monday August 24, 2009, 10:52 am EDT

SEATTLE (AP) -- Microsoft Corp., the world's largest software maker, spent nearly $1.9 million in the second quarter lobbying on intellectual property rights, cybersecurity and foreign-worker visas, among other issues, according to a recent disclosure report.

Redmond, Wash.-based Microsoft makes most of its profit -- $17.7 billion in the 2008 fiscal year -- selling the Windows operating system and Office and other desktop software. A rise in piracy is enough to throw off the company's earnings in any given quarter.

Microsoft employs scores of lawyers and former law enforcement workers to track down illegal software copying operations and engineers to make it harder for people to distribute unauthorized copies of its programs to unsuspecting PC buyers.

The software maker also lobbied Congress on anti-piracy and intellectual property protection issues, patent and copyright matters including funding for the Patent and Trademark Office...
