You'll be welcome!

Ubuntu (and Kubuntu) work fine here on the Gold Coast. The Gold Coast is about half an hour south of Brisbane. I spent a year working in an area just south of Brisbane called Logan, which was a comfortable drive along the motorway.

If you enjoy English winters, you should have come in winter, although the weather has been relatively mild so far this year. You might want to make sure the place you are living has an air conditioner. My home doesn't! After almost six years in Queensland I'm almost starting to acclimatise! Sweating's good for you, isn't it? (Unless you drip all over your new Sony laptop . . .)

Well, I hope you're move goes smoothly, and again, welcome!

### Post by The Mekon on 2005-12-02
I am another Pom who came to Australia in 1960 and lived in Adelaide for 38 years before retiring to Queensland.

We now live in Tewantin which is in Noosa Shire, a very popular - if up market resort area 130 km north of Brisbane.  Away from the coast it is quite rural and there are problems with getting Broadband internet connections.  We were on a waiting list for two years before getting access to one of the available/updated line connections.  My next door neighbour has been told that she is on a waiting list of about six months!

Apart from that Queensland is a great place to live but can have some extreme climate episodes.  In Noosa, last Thursday, we had 264 mm of rain in 2 hours which caused some problems!

Brian
