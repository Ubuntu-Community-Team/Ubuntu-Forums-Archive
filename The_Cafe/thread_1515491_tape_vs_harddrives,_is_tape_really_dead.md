### Post by McRat on 2010-06-22
Hmmm...  The drive that commercial refers to is $2500.  Retail on the media (800GB = 1.6TB compressed) $113 (fair market $40)

Speed (actual) is 80 MB/s, 160 compressed.

Throw away the "compressed" stuff.  Any data can be compressed on write, and that's not how data rates or sizes are measured.

RAID5 is perhaps cheaper, or close.

Certainly faster.

Lifespan?  That's hard to say.  Tapes stretch.  You can't run a tape indefinately.  I had a lot of trouble with cartridge drives, but that was 15 years ago.

### Post by Frak on 2010-06-22
Tape backup is probably the most reliable backup. When I worked for a school, we done daily diffs with one full backup each month. A set was kept on hand while another was transported to the State Dept. After that, I worked for a mid-size business, and it was nearly the same. 6 diffs + 1 month full.

Thing is, you can back up tons of data with it. I've never lost a tape for any reason other than misplacement. Very, very slow, but very, very reliable.

EDIT
Also, I am over-simplifying it. It was a little bit more complicated, but the redundant diffs made it safer in case an incr. failed.

### Post by alphaniner on 2010-06-22
The company I work for deals in backup solutions, tape and virtual tape with RAID storage.  We sell far more of the latter, but tape is still very much alive.  Mostly we push tape for D2D2T.  The latest generation of the most common technology, LTO, is LTO 5: 1.5TB native, 3TB compressed.  I think McRat was talking about the previous generation, LTO 4.  The stuff you see on Newegg for a few hundreds of dollars are literally decade plus old technology.
