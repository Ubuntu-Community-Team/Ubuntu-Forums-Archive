### Post by forger on 2008-10-16
I think no derivatives is too much
cc by-nc-sa license (as mentioned above), limits people to use it, read it, share it non-commercially and make modifications to it that could attach it to a similar project.
[http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/3.0/](http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/3.0/)

But be warned, this is a very restrictive license and it's not compatible with GPL :)

I suggest something else:
- Use Gnu FDL: [http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/fdl.html#SEC4](http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/fdl.html#SEC4)
- Make your wikis, make a docbook formatted copy as well
- Find a publisher with which your team could make a printed copy
This way, you would fund the non-commercial project to further expand :)
If you already have a product/book out, I believe that no-one would use it for commercial purposes.
And as always, your work is strictly protected with a GNU copyleft ;)
