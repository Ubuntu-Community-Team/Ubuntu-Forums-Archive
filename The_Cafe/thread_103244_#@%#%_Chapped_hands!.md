My friend swears by drinking lots and lots of water.  I've been doing this recently, and it seems to have helped quite a bit with dry skin on my hands, feet, face and lips.

### Post by almahtar on 2005-12-14
I have that problem, times a million... I have ictheosis vulgaris... gives me very dry chapped skin, especially in temperature extremes, especially on hands and feet.  I just use lotion (not using it is just NOT an option), and keep socks on my feet and gloves on my hands as much of the day (and at least all night) as I can.  It somehow helps to retain moisture.  Drinking lots of water is good for it too.

It doesn't solve the problem... nothing does, but at least it's livable if I use shower time to exfoliate a lot, lotion immediately after, lotion frequently, drink lots of water, and keep socks/gloves on at night.
