### Post by t0p on 2010-01-27
I listen regularly to:

[LIST]
[*][The Geekcast]("http://www.geekcastonline.com") - general computer/tech stuff;
[*][Linux Outlaws]("http://linuxoutlaws.com") - Linux, free software, computer/tech;
[*][Pauldotcom Security Weekly]("http://pauldotcom.com") - computer security;
[*][Security Now]("http://www.grc.com/securitynow.htm") - computer security
[/LIST]
You mentioned Linux Outlaws, so I assume you know what it's like.  The Geekcast is pretty informative and entertaining, but it's not focused on Linux or free software.  The other two are good if you're interested in computer security.  Security Now is one of Leo Laporte's productions, but it's a tad better than TWIT and TWIG - probably because of his co-presenter Steve Gibson who is pretty clued-up.

There are also a couple of "internet TV" shows I watch: [Hak5]("http://hak5.org") and [Systm]("http://www.revision3.com/systm").  Hak5 features a lot of Linux and free software stuff, but not exclusively.  Systm is more hardware oriented; it's big on hardware hacking and stuff like building flamethrowers and Sumo robots.  Very entertaining, plus you're in danger of learning something.

I've been a fan of Leo Laporte since The Site on MSNBC back in the mid-90's, and TWiT is one of my faves.  I do like FLOSS Weekly, though sometimes their topics aren't of interest to me that much.

As for Steve Gibson, I think he knows his stuff for the most part, but as some noted he doesn't do his homework that often before reporting on something.  And I don't think he's the best for a weekly radio show. Maybe an occasional consultant or corespondent, but not much else.  He used to be good when Leo had him on The Screensavers, but those were small clips with a focused topic.

Outside of that, I don't know of any good Linux or FOSS podcasts off hand.  Linux Link Tech Show is okay, but I think they push that crappy L33t Hax0r thing way too much, like the now gone BinRev podcasts and early Systm episodes used to do.  The only folks who can get away with that are Emmanuel Goldstein and his crew at 2600 in OTW and OTH because they are true hackers in my book.

And for Leo's commercials, they are annoying but it's a necessity to keep the show going.  He's said many times he doesn't like doing it, and early on he said he wouldn't unless it was absolutely necessary.  I do contribute financially to the show when I can, but he's basically recreated what I loved about ZDTV/TechTV, and I know that's not cheap.
