### Post by Paqman on 2013-04-15
They're acrylic, so you thin them with water but they're not water or oil based. Just search for "miniatures paint" Legolas, there are plenty of different brands. You'll probably want to try a few and find what you like. Quality is variable in my experience. I've used Citadel paints (the Games Workshop stuff) a fair bit because they're easy to get, they're ok but nothing special. I am tempted to try out Army Builder, I'm not entirely convinced that it's really as easy as they claim, but it's worth a crack IMO.

If you're just starting out then the quality of your paint probably won't make much difference, but make sure you get decent brushes. They don't need to be sable or unicorn fur, but don't buy nasty cheap ones. Get lots of different sizes and look after them properly. Always, always wash them out, pick out any stray hairs, and always stand them upright with the bristles uppermost. I like to keep the little plastic sleeves on them that they come with. Don't ever paint straight from the pot either, you'll just dry up lots of paint. Get an old tile or plate and use it to mix only as much as you need at a time.
