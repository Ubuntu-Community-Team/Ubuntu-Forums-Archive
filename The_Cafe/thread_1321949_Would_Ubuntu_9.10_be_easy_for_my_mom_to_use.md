### Post by plurworldinc on 2009-11-10
Well here is a fun way to look at if you should give you mom Ubuntu or not. Last month I was faced with the same question but my mother wanted to install Ubuntu on her new machine that was running Vista. At first I was really on the fence about converting her, because or random hardware and software issues I had in the past with Ubuntu ( which I must say is getting better with every new distro).

   In the end my mother converted to Ubuntu for two main reasons, I myself and a good number of my friends use Ubuntu as or primary system or dual boot them with XP (not Vista) when it came to helping my mother with any of her computer problems if its something that happens with Windows I may not be able to help because I have been use to the Ubuntu and I can help.

   The second reason was just a stroke of bad luck, with in 20 min of plugging her new laptop onto the web for updates she got some weird malware/spyware virus that made her new laptop a nightmare for the rest of the evening. 

   So my advice would be let her test out the LiveCD, dual boot if you have enough space for she can really see what Ubuntu can do for her. Explain it is not Windows and in the end it can help her save money in the long run...

   One more fun thing to do is, when I installed Ubuntu on top my mothers system she began doing a little research on her own for fun and the last time I saw her she was dual booting Ubuntu and MoonOS because she loved the feel and look of the OS. 

  GOOD LUCK !!!!):P
