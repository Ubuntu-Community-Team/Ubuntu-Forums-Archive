### Post by banjobacon on 2006-09-18
Also, installing MP3 support in Ubuntu is about as easy as adding Vorbis support to Windows.

I really don't think this is as big idea as a lot of people make it out to be. Windows also does not come a variety of common codecs (Real Player and Quicktime are not part of Windows, unless your computer's manufacturer installed them). Ubuntu gives users a central, trusted place to install codecs from (apt-get, Synaptic, etc.), while Windows users have to install 3rd party applications to use some of these codecs.

Neither of these situations is ideal, but I don't think the Windows situation is that much better than Ubuntu's.

Maybe an effective way to cut down on complaints about poor multimedia support would be to better communicate *why* MP3s and other media won't work. I know most people avoid reading documentation, so this could be difficult. One way inform new users if to have new installations of Ubuntu show a screen saying something like, "Welcome to Ubuntu. Before you get started, please note some of the differences between Ubuntu and other operating systems you may be familiar with: blah blah blah." If people better understand why MP3s aren't supported by default, maybe they won't mind the one or two extra steps. Heck, maybe they'll be inspired to use open media formats.

Go to help.ubuntu.com, choose the Ubuntu Desktop Guide, and look for Multimedia Codecs.

Something that people don't really realize is that most of the time they've grown up on Windows, so to speak. You've had years of learning how to perform certain tasks (installing software, drivers, upgrading the OS, and so on) with this operating system. When starting out with a new operating system, it's naturally gonna be a bit different -- and those people expecting Linux to do everything the exact same way as Windows are going to be disappointed.

A lot of times, comparable tasks under Windows and Linux (in my mind) are equally "easy" or "difficult" -- just different. You already know how to do it in Windows with your eyes closed... now you need to learn how to do it under Linux :)
