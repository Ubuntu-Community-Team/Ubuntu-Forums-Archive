### Post by Dustin2128 on 2010-06-07
I did a little more searching for distros, and I realized: I don't want straight minimalism; just purism. So obviously I came to slackware, I'd already tried it in VMs several times, seen those epic youtube videos showcasing its performance ect. so I gave it a try. My first thought is a compliment on the fact that they finally adopted kde 4.x series (didn't like 3.5) Wireless was initially a pain ](*,)...:-k..........](*,) (it took slightly less than a half hour to install, almost 4 hours to get the wifi setup) but I eventually got it running with one of the /extra packages, and it even solved my range problems, I can be 400 feet away from the router and still have broadband speeds :guitar: slight annoyance having to compile most programs from scratch but only slight. Flash is giving me largish problems but that's going to be gone in 9 years anyway :P Plasma widgets are pretty awesome and I like the compiz effects after 3 months of using flux. Also a question: who invented the 'snap' feature, was it KDE that windows copied or was it windows that kde copied? kind of leaning towards the first seeing as how conservative the slack team is with new KDE releases.... 
Overall I have enjoyed the slackware experience greatly and am planning to swap 100% to slack as my main O.S.:guitar:
