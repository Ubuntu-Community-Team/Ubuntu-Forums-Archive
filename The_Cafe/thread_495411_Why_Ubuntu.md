### Post by bonzodog on 2007-07-08
**1. When were you first introduced to Linux?**
1996

**2. What was the first distro you used?**
Soft Landing Systems, later to become Slackware. Had it on a web and mail server in a Cybercafe, which used BSD running a very basic X for the desktop machines. (ahead of its time? Maybe.)

**3. What attracted you to ubuntu?**
I had been running Slackware since 1999 on my home machine as the Sole OS, and had just built myself a brand new AMD64 3000+ machine, and it was at about the time that Pat stopped bundling gnome with Slackware. 
I decided I wanted a 64 bit distro to get the full power out of my new machine, and one that came with Gnome. 
This was late 2005, and Ubuntu was just coming into its own, so I tried it. I have since stopped using Ubuntu as it seemed..bloated, and some of the packages were very badly built. 
I missed the Slackware did things, and Zenwalk was on the Rise as a Slackware equivalent of Ubuntu. Admittedly, it's only 32 bit at the moment, but it's light and fast, and has suited me perfectly.

**4. Why do you use it today?**
I don't. Zenwalk is now my sole distro of Choice. I continue to be a part of the community though, as this is by far the best Linux community on the planet, and also the biggest.
 
**5. How old are you (to see the age of ubuntu users)?**
34, married with children almost as old as the OP, and Live in Ireland.
