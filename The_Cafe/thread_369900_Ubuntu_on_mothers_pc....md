### Post by H.E. Pennypacker on 2007-02-25
You'll probably want to disable automatic updates.  Why does she need an up to date computer if she gets everything done with a current configuration?  Unfortunately, some updates should only be performed by those who know what they're doing.  Your mother would be better off not updating anything.

To skip all the configurating, having you thought of Linux Mint (which is now codenamed "Bianca")?  It comes with certain proprietary software (e.g. Flash, and I believe the audio codecs).  It also now has a single panel which goes more in line with Windows.  Even better, Linux Mint is based on Ubuntu (something you're already familiar with).  She won't have to mess with Automatix, and there's a solid Ubuntu base.

### Post by Praill on 2007-02-26
Ubuntu and its open source ways is ideal, but can be a head-ache for the regular user with an M$ mindset. And consequently an issue for you with setup and configure time. So you might want to try one of the proprietary hybrids like Freespire.

Freespire supports nearly everything your mother will need "out of the box" except DVD playback (which can be fixed quickly).
There can be sound problems with freespire though, which are easily fixed by disabling the Jack server, and issues with wireless WPA encryption that you should be able to fix with a quick google of the problem (if you need it).
Also you might want to remove CNR so she cant be coaxed into buying anything from the linspire people.

I know most people will tell you freespire sucks, but its what I always recommend for the average user. It has a flawless install, a KDE-inspired windows-type gui, and complete out of the box support for all proprietary codecs (thanks to its $$ version Linspire) that makes the email and web-surfing user completely comfortable at no cost.

Good luck.
