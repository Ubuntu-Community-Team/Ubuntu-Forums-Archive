### Post by lovinglinux on 2010-09-13
Canonical already started to provide paid software through the Software Center. Now Mozilla is starting the development of a Marketplace, in order to allow developers to charge for their extensions, like Apple does with their App Store.

[http://www.theregister.co.uk/2009/12/11/mozilla_add_on_marketplace/](http://www.theregister.co.uk/2009/12/11/mozilla_add_on_marketplace/)

(The article is from last year)

Looks like there is a kind of pressure from many developers to create such marketplace, but Mozilla wants to know the opinion of all extension developers and from users too. To do so, they created a survey for each target audience.

The user survey was included in Firefox 4 Feedback extension (aka Test Pilot) and I think is over now. Nevertheless, you can leave a comment at [https://testpilot.mozillalabs.com/testcases/addon-marketplace.html#respond](https://testpilot.mozillalabs.com/testcases/addon-marketplace.html#respond)

Developers already received their survey by e-mail this week.

As a developer and an avid extension consumer I have mixed feelings about it. I tend to agree with the opinion of the author of [this article]("http://www.ghacks.net/2009/12/12/mozilla-considering-app-store-for-add-ons/").

What is your opinion on the subject?
