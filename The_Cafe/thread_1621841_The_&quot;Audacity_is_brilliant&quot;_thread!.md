### Post by Schrute Farms on 2010-11-14
I agree.  I use it to digitize vinyl and cassettes so I can put them on Cds or my MP3 player.  Audacity works better than a music studio program I purchased a few years ago.

My favorite part is the export multiple function.  In the past, after  recording something, I had to individually select each song, copy/paste  it into another track or window, then export it.  With Audacity, all I  have to do is select the beginning of each song, and it will export each  track for me.  There is also a silence finder that will find the  beginnings of songs automatically, if you choose.  That saves me so much time, that I'm finding it fun to break out all of my old records, or buy new ones, and recording them.  Before, it almost seemed like a chore.

The only bug I find (and it might not even be Audacity's fault) is that my sound output in preferences has to be set every time I play back.  But not a dealbreaker, by any means.
