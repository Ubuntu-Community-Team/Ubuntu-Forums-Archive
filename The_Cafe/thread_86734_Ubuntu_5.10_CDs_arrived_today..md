### Post by Qrk on 2005-11-08
I think its worth waiting. People equate burned CDs with pirated software, when really Ubuntu is about as far as you can get from pirated software (is it even possible to pirate Ubuntu?)

Linux already has the stigma of a hobbiest OS; giving someone a professional looking CD really does wonders when it comes to them giving the software a good try. And if you take an hour to install it for them; give them Java, Flash, and mp3 support; show them synaptic; open a .doc on openoffice; and set up their email with evolution, you've made a friend and a Linux user. 

Thats the only thing really holding Linux back in the vast majority of the market. Its not the lack of games; most users don't game or have machines that can. Its not the lack of support, Ubuntu has much more free support than windows does. Its not the lack of hardware detection, Ubuntu supports a ton of hardware to the point where a problem is rare. Its not the usability, Gnome and KDE are more than competetive with Windows Luna. 

No, the thing holding Linux back is that 90% of the users out there are afraid to give it a try, or they don't even know that it is an option.
