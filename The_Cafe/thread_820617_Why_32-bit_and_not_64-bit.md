### Post by Superkoop on 2008-06-06
> **ghindo said:**
> I don't really *entirely* understand the appeal behind x86_64.  From what I understand, the only real advantage is the ability to recognize 4+ GB of RAM.  Other than that, there's no real difference.  Could someone enlighten me?

Well it's a bit more than just the memory, although I believe 64bit can manage more than 18 exabytes of memory at max.
2^8 = 256
2^16 = 65,536
2^20 = 1,048,576
2^24 = 16,777,216
2^32 = 4,294,967,296
2^64 = 18,446,744,073,729,551,616 

Also, 64bit allows for better audio work:
16-bit = 96 dB
24-bit = 144 dB
32-bit = 192 dB
64-bit = 384 dB
Since 64bit can hold so much larger values, it also allows higher audio precision. You can hold more true fidelity even when a lot of audio processing is going on.

Basically it's like having a fatter pipe. Where 32bit can process 2 16bit words at once, while 64bit can process 4 16bit words at once. 

I think the best article I have read on the issue is [_***this one***_]("http://www.bit-tech.net/bits/2007/10/16/64-bit_more_than_just_the_ram/1").
