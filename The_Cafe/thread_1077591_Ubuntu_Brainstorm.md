**Category 1:**
"I'll propose something that essentially boils down to 'something does not work on my computer, fix it'. Of course, I'm aware that a lot of people does *not* experience the same problem, but I'll add over-general statements like 'this is a severe problem and must be fixed' and over-dramatic warnings like 'Ubuntu must solve this if they want to increase market share' anyway. Oh, and just for fun I'll add ungrateful and rude statements like 'the fact that this is not fixed yet is a clear sign that the developers don't care about the users', despite the fact that the problems I'm complaining about are incredibly complex and demand knowledge of countless different hardware configurations, despite the fact that the developers *are* working on it but a solution does not come in one day and despite the fact that more and more hardware *is* supported with each release."
Examples: "make suspend work", "solve bugs"

**Category 2:**
"I'll propose something that essentially boils down to 'do this because that's what Windows/MacOSX do'. I won't give any logical arguments or serious usability studies about what I'm proposing, mostly because I'm completely incapable of knowing the difference between 'intuitive' and 'what I'm used to'."
Examples: "install ejecter by default", "trash icon on the desktop"

**Category 3:**
"I'll propose the improvement of something in a completely vague manner, without giving absolutely no concrete idea on how to achieve it. To compensate for this lack of substance, I won't have any restraints in criticizing the current situation and sometimes I'll even curse. Of course, none of those criticisms will actually *say* something and will essentially boil down to 'this sucks', but it's good for my ego. Oh, I almost forgot, when someone asks me to do something, I'll simply brush it off saying 'I'm just an user', despite the fact that there are *plenty* of things a non-tech person could to to help improve the issue I raised, including things that take less time to do than the time I took to write my rant."
Examples: "make a beautiful default theme", "improve usability"

**Category 4:**
"I'll propose the default install of something I use. I don't have any arguments in favor of it other than things that essentially boil down to personal opinions, but to compensate for that I'll post intimidating statements like 'it's CLEARLY better than the current default option' and a long list of features that the current default option does not have. Of course, I won't have any evidence about how much those features are seen as an advantage, but the important thing is that *I* use them and I'm the freaking center of the universe."
Examples: "make banshee the default player", "make exaile the default player"

**Bonus category:**
"I'll propose something and I won't bother using the incredibly simple search feature of Brainstorm to see if someone has proposed something similar before. Of course, if someone did I'll pollute the site and mess up the votings, but my idea is so unique and brilliant that the odds of this happening are zero."
