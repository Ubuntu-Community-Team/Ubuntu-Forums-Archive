Aye maties this be the day. Was twelve score years ago today when we sighted the Horn loomin like a great monster out o' the sqaullin flurries and endless fog. An' this after six weeks of pure hellfire and damnation, scuttin back and forth, lookin for safe passage with half the royal navy close behind. Men froze up solid in the riggin, no food, no sleep, pumpin like madmen we was just to keep er afloat. Aye matie, was tryin times they was.

Them's that died was the lucky ones I tell ye. 

But us Pirates have perculiar views on luck.

So this is the day we remember them you ask?

No, this is the day we spit on their watery graves and curse em for takin the lubbers way out, abandonen us in our despairin hour of need. For usin their luck to save themselves from the hardships while heapin their own share of the burden upon us all.

Is this the day we speak fondly of their rascally ways you ask? 
Fond rememberence of them we have, yes, fondly we remember the sharin out of the booty from the dead men's lockers.
