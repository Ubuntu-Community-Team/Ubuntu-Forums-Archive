### Post by Roger Mudd on 2007-11-10
I'd have to disagree here. File formats are much more important to me than applications (although it's often hard to separate the two concepts). The file format is what holds the data/information and the data/information is the valued part of the equation. If data/information is "held captive" by proprietary formats, so to are users (See ODF, BBC, etc.).
I don't have any issues with the existence of proprietary formats like WMV, Quicktime or Flash. Just their utilization at the expense of perfectly acceptable open standards.
Finally, any marketing department worth their salt will attempt to reach as many customers as possible. By not including their trailers in open formats they are excluding a segment (probably small in relative terms) of their potential market. On this project, however, I would expect that segment to me substantially larger due to the use of OS software in the production process. OS people get excited when they hear of projects like this and *Elephants Dream*. In my view, they're selling themselves short by abandoning the OS ethos in the distribution process.
