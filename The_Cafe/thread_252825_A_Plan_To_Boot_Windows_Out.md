### Post by Brendt2 on 2006-09-07
Hello,

I just had a realization as I was reading through the linux/Ubuntu VS windows debates.

I was thinking about how the home computer market is made up.  There are the professionals, the small buisness owners, the gamers, the power users, ect.   My perception is that the MOST common computer user is the super casual computer user.   People that fit in this group ( For me ) are Grandma, Parents, siblings, and so on.   These people do not game.   They do not program.   They use the computer to surf the internet, use email, use "office" apps, listen to / download music, and occasionally want to make graphic designs (and have no need to all of the features that adobe offers).

I have been transitioning all of the these people in my life over to Ubuntu (using automatix to get all of the apps they might be used to seeing, and of course the "clip art") and have had no complaints at all.   These people know nothing about how the system they are on works, and they need no knowledge as it relates to their computers.   I showed them an example update procedure ( you click here and presto! ) and showed them where their apps are.   For each user / computer it takes me approx 30-40 minutes to get them setup and ready to go on their own (depends on if they want their digi cam or iPod explained also =) ).  

My "service" calls have all but disapeared as of now and have been replaced with "My computer is so much faster!" calls.

Since the response has been so grand, I have started to get the small buisness owners that I know as well.  To them the idea of getting rid of all the costs of windows (licensing,apps) would definately help bring them over from the darkside!   Of course the small buisness owners are probably one of the hardest markets to crack.  These people do not want their day to day operations to be in any way stopped.  I start this project this coming weekend so I guess we shall see how the transition goes!

Does anyone think that with Ubuntu, we might finally have a weapon to win over the majority of people?  (Realizing that computer gamers are not in the majority!)   Due to the ease of conversion that I have had, I can't imagine why we all can't start evangilizing this switch over (basically you switch them over and tell them that this is for their own good! (note this is just for the super casual users!)).

Anyone else had this level of success?  What if any road blocks?

-Brendt
