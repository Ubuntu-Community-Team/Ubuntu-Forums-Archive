### Post by empty_spaces on 2010-03-27
I know it's still very much in active development, and only offers a few basic features at the moment, but I still find myself switching over to it quite often lately. It also seems pretty stable.
I do use the latest version of AWN to make it more usable, and the Applications are not classified by type, so using the keyboard helps.(See screenshots)
I haven't seen too much love for Gnome-Shell out here, but that's most probably because it's such a big departure from the traditional Gnome desktop.
I believe that as long as they make it as customizable and feature-rich as Gnome 2.X, it could turn out to be quite good.
Just my thoughts.
