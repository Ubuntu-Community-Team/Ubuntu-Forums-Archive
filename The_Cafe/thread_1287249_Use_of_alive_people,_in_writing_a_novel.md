### Post by earthpigg on 2009-10-10
have you seen Revoultion OS?


on another note, i strongly suggest renaming the characters and putting the standard 'all stuff fiction, any resemblance to reality purely a coincidence bla bla' thing in.


perception is reality.

the perceptions of your readers are THEIR reality.

so, if you start writing about Ballmer or Stallman in a way other than what a reader 'knows' to be true, it will be interpreted as if you wrote that trees are generally pink in color and that Joseph Stalin invented the light bulb...


if you give them other names, then you can attribute whatever personality traits to them you wish _***without***_ inevitably turning the majority of your readers off because your book reads to them like a poor imitation of Tolkien.


(all of the 'them', of course, are the 99% of your readers that don't already have strongly held pre-existing convictions about all of these folks you want to put in your book *that match your convictions exactly*.)

### Post by t0p on 2009-10-10
That boilerplate disclaimer "All characters are fictional, any similarity with actual people is purely coincidental" will *not* protect you from a defamation/libel lawsuit.  That disclaimer appears on just about all novels, movies, TV shows... even ones that claim to be "based on a true story".  It's all very well claiming that your characters are fictional: but if you write that "Richard Smallman", founder of the "Software Freedom Foundation" , secretly does coding for MacroSoft, you *will* get sued! :p
