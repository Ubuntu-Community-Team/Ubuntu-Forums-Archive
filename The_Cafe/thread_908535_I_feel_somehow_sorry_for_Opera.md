### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2008-09-02
Why Opera didn't catch on:

1) It didn't originate tabs (I don't believe). The first browser I saw using tabs was Neoplanet about 10 years ago.

2) Extensions > widgets. End of story. I don't want a "widget" that gives me a tiny program that sits in its own window, I want to download something that alters the browser.

3) As a result, except for things most users don't care about (shaving RAM usage, millisecond speed boosts), Opera offers almost nothing useful to users. Hell the only reason I give it a try now and again is in the hopes that it'll massively shrink its memory footprint so my battery lasts longer, but it never quite does. What does it honestly offer the user?

Opera had a time, but it got surpassed.
