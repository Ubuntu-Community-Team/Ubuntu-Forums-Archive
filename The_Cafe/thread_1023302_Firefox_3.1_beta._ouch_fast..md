### Post by akahige on 2009-03-13
> **pt123 said:**
> Yeah just saw that on Tombuntu, shame this package doesn't exist for Ibex

You don't need a special package for Intrepid. All you have to do is download firefox-3.1b3.tar.bz2 from the [official download page](http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/firefox/all-beta.html) and extract it somewhere in your home folder.

Then go into the "firefox" dir and run the "firefox" script. (I did it from a terminal with "./firefox". It will automatically pick up your existing Firefox profile, so you'll have all your bookmarks, logins, &c.

Since I'm on an AMD64 system running the  64-bit Flash alpha, the 32-bit Firefox spits an ELF class error and won't load the plugin. There's a simple fix, though. Go to Adobe's [Flash download page](http://get.adobe.com/flashplayer/) and get the  .tar.gz version. Pull out libflashplayer.so and rename it (I just added "32" to the end of the name), then dump it into ~/.mozilla/plugins and you're good to go. You can leave the  64-bit .so in there in case you want/need to go back to FF3.0.x.

When everything goes official, all you have to do is delete the beta directory from your home folder and all's good.

Cheers.
