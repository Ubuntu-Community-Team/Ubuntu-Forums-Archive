Ubuntu is something that is not needed imo. Debian is quite easy to set up and once it is up, anyone can take care of it. See, the funny thing is I use arch and it's not on that list. I'm going to Debian or gentoo but last I checked, gentoo was at a halt. If it isn't anymore, I'd like to know.
Gentoo is the only OS I have installed on this machine right now. Give it a try and decide if it's in the state you would consider "halt". ;)

It's kind of a strange animal, but it's the only distro that I can keep around for any length of time. It's not perfect, but then no distro is. I enjoy the ability to customize everything in a hassle-free way and it has been rock-solid stable for me. Everything works just fine on my system. As long as you read the docs carefully, pay attention to the warning messages Portage gives you, and follow the forums reasonably closely, Gentoo can be a great deal of fun.

If you decide to try it, don't use the graphical installer since it's buggy and no longer supported. Instead, you can use any LiveCD (I like Gentoo's Minimal CD and the Gentoo-based [sysresccd]("http://www.sysresccd.org/") is quite popular) and follow the stage3 installation in the [Gentoo Handbook]("http://www.gentoo.org/doc/en/handbook/"). You'll want to use an up-to-date stage3, either from the /experimental directory on the mirrors (see [this]("http://forums.gentoo.org/viewtopic-t-719732.html")) or from [funtoo.org]("http://www.funtoo.org").

By the way, the technical support areas of the Gentoo forums are active and friendly. 

**Edit:** I just wanted to add that new ebuilds are being added daily to the Portage tree. You can see some "signs of life" on the new-look [Gentoo homepage]("http://www.gentoo.org/").

If you don't save Debian, and Redhat almost every other distro will fall on it's face.

There are more base distributions than three... Slackware, Debian, Red Hat, SuSE, Mandriva, Gentoo, GoboLinux, Enlizy, Source Mage, Sorcerer, Rock, Puppy, Arch, Knoppix, Pardus, Crux, Lunar, SliTaz, and I am most likely missing a few.

These are all independent of one another (note that while SuSE and Mandrake both use RPM, neither is actually based upon Red Hat). There are so many distros and as soon as one of them leaves the picture, the others pick up the slack. You really need to look at a more broad picture, research, and try things out. You may be surprised. In the realm of ease of use, I have never seen better than Mandriva and Pardus, and in the realm of power-users there is little more intense than Feather, TinyCore, or TTY Linux. Tomstrbt and HAL91, are the most minimal, though without a compiler it is hard to do anything on them (though one could easily be added...).
