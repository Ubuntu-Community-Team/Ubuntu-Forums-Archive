### Post by chessnerd on 2010-04-29
Heres a little something else to talk about beside Lucid (although there's nothing wrong with Lucid talk...):

It seems like laptops actually have a surprising number of advantages for a home server, but they also have some problems...

Advantages:

1. Size - laptops are small and light compared to desktops, especially the 13" and smaller ones, so they can be stored anywhere.

2. Energy usage - laptops generally use less electricity than desktops, so this can help keep the energy bill down and help the environment.

3. Battery - power backups are expensive, sometimes even over $100 for just an hour of power for a desktop. Laptop batteries can last hours and even if they are old they typically outlast a backup power supply, so if power failures regularly occur in your area this will keep it running.

4. Performance - laptops, even old ones, typically have more than enough memory and processing power for a server, which doesn't need much at all.


Disadvantages:

1. Hard drive - laptop hard drives are usually smaller than desktop ones. This is especially true of older laptops.

2. No customization - unlike desktops, laptops are not very customizable and shouldn't be taken apart unless you really know what you are doing

3. No RAID - laptops usually don't have or allow for multiple hard drives and are unlikely to support RAID.

4. Poor cooling - laptops can run hotter than desktops if they are pushed too hard and you can't add a second fan to it like on a desktop. This could lead to overheating if there is a high sustained load.


What do you guys think? Do laptops make good servers?

The way I see it, it depends. 
Depending on what you would use the server for, the advantages of using a laptop could outweigh the concerns. If you don't need a lot of hard drive space and are fine with the hardware as it is then a laptop should work well, but if you need a beefy, upgradeable, super-server, a laptop won't cut it. 

Also, if you feel I left out any important advantages or disadvantages, let me know and I can add them to the list.

1. Small (nintendo wii size)
