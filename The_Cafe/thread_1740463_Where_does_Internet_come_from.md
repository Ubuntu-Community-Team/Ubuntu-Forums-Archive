### Post by Paqman on 2011-04-27
> **brawnypandora0 said:**
> Who runs the Internet?

No one organisation runs the internet, although there are organisations who're responsible for things like maintaining important pieces of the infrastructure. Obviously ISPs at various levels are involved too.

Basically it's a network of networks. All people have to do is provide the infrastructure for the data to move along, and all operate using the same standards. They can also influence how the traffic flows to a certain degree.

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-27
Internet is just a bunch of computers connected into a global network. When the idea was first conceived, the main advantage was that the internet will be decentralized so that any portion of it can be turned on or off without affecting others (I think the original design was a cold war military idea, even if the enemy nukes one part of the country, the rest will keep connected, i.e. there is no central server to nuke).

Various companies own the cables and the DNS servers (the ones that let you type Google.com as opposed to 74.125.113.103). Companies sell people access to the network, but at the end, nobody "owns" "runs" or "powers" the Internet. It is a large collection of companies/people doing working together.

### Post by tigerplug on 2011-04-27
Well the cable that you see assuming that you are using DSL over PoTs, joins the telephone line outside back as far as (what you Americans call) a CO / Exchange.

Here, we connect to a DSLAM - [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Subscriber_Line_Access_Multiplexer](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Subscriber_Line_Access_Multiplexer) 

From there it is transported over an aggregation link back to the core of the service providers network and at difference NAP's(Network Access) / Peering points, your service provider will advertise these routes to the world (its kinda like a big room where all the providers meet and "talk" to each other").

The type of connection doesn't matter a network is a network. If you hook two computers together so you can share files, printers, etc... you have a network.

[http://www.linfo.org/network.html](http://www.linfo.org/network.html)

When you connect to your internet provider you are just connecting to a bigger network, their network may only cover a city, might cover a state, might cover a country, or they could have an international presence.

To form the internet, you have a bunch of companies and cities in some cases, that connect their networks together to form an even bigger global network.

Your home network would be pretty simple and the internet pretty complex, but the basic idea doesn't change, the internet is still just a network. The internet comes from any place (every place) anybody has set something up that you can access from any other place with internet access, it's a collective.
