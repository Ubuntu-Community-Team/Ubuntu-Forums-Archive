### Post by spdl on 2005-12-12
Hi gang,

I have been purchasing goods via the Internet for quite some time.  I usually use Amazon, NewEgg and Memory Ten.  They all provide excellent service.

The other day I decided to give Overstock.com a shot, they had a product that was a little bit cheaper and they offered free delivery.  So I added a few more things to the purchase.

Well, giving them a chance turned out to be a big mistake.

The website stated that my products were shipped.  But only 2 out of 5 products showed up at my door in two seperate boxes.  The one box contained a taped package, as if someone already opened it.  When I took it out of it's packaging, the two joint pieces in it were broken off and just sitting there loosely.  So I requested a return for this and a refund.  And I also asked why the other products did not ship.

I received an email a day later stating that I need to give more details to my request, even though I already did.  So I did give them more explicit details.  So a day later, my account on their website is now updated showing that only two items shipped, one is on back order until after Christmas (it was intneded to be a Christmas gift), 2 were shipping now and one in a week.  Today is Monday, I will contact Overstock later today to get an update on the refund of return shipping of the damaged product and a full refund.

That is the risk you can pay when you give someone a shot.  And that is the risk you can pay when you order online and not going to a store.

What did I learn from this?  I'm not ordering from Overstock again.  What will I do now?  Continue to order from Amazon, NewEgg and Memory Ten.

I just wanted to let you all know of the expereince with them and that it wasn't cool.
