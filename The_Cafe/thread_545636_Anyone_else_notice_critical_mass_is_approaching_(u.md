### Post by triptoe on 2007-09-07
This year we have seen a few great leaps in desktop linux.... and while I would not call it the year of the desktop.. it seems to me like it is approaching.

Dell is now offering preinstalled ubuntu hardware.. a major OEM.. and you can get a copy of ubuntu for less than an windows

ATI is open sourcing their drivers.. which will compell nvidia to do the same or pull something from their sleeve...

migrations by the thousands are happening left and right in different countries. I believe France's parliament just changed for instance.

The only thing I see atm holding linux back is software... namely the Major apps (photoshop, autocad, ms office) and games. With virtualization the apps can be taken care of.... if not through wine, crossover, or open source alternatives.

As for games... this year we will see two great titles that for some... will be enough to switch completely over.

Unreal 3
Quake Wars enemy territory

So the question is.. is there going to be some must have feature that will make users want to switch?

Will it be lack of spyware? 
Will it be better performance on the same hardware in comparison to vista...?
or will it be xp users switching over who don't want vista because its slower and expensive.. but want the graphics/ eye candy?

Will there be a critical mass at all? Open source seems to me like it has an exponential effect. this means that as we accelerate there will be a point when there will be a big leap.. kind of like how firefox leapt up out of nowhere all the sudden.

I personally feel that the time is coming soon..... My prediction is Gutsy +2 will start the ball rolling

### Post by treis on 2007-09-07
No, we are still a ways away from critical mass.  Ubuntu isn't ready for the mainstream yet.  There are cosmetic issues such as the boot process and lack of desktop effects.  Other issues include poor system configuration.  Editing xorg.conf to change your resolution or whatnot just isn't going to cut it.  Things are getting better, and Gutsy will go a long way to fix the software issues.

Hardware support is still lacking.  The move by ATI is promising, and hopefully we will see better drivers.  Wireless is still a big problem.  Dell selling systems help, but I don't think to can click to them from the front page.  You have to specifically search for Ubuntu to find them.  I think you are looking at least at another 18-24 months before I'd say Ubuntu is a viable contender on the market.
