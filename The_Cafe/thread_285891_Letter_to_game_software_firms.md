### Post by cunawarit on 2006-10-27
It won’t achieve anything; to a business it is a pure numbers game. Games are getting more and more expensive to develop and profit margins are already very tight. 

The main consideration for games companies is profit; I can’t picture many games firms rushing to support a platform than has such a small percentage of the desktop market. That’s the same reason Mac OS X doesn’t get that many games either. 

What’s in it for the companies? Why should they support Linux? Do they care if people migrate to Linux? 

Linux and Apple have less than 10 percent of the desktop market; to top it off the Linux market is fragmented into many distros. If Linux and Apple are to get support from games companies they first need to capture a sizable chunk of the desktop market, not the other way around.
