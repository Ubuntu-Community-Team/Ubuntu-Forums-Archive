### Post by plb on 2005-04-12
I first started using *nix systems in 1999 with Redhat and have since tryed many distros inbetween, Slackware, Mandrake, Suse, Fedora, Debian, FBSD, OBSD, Gentoo. And I would just like to share some of my initial impressions and suggestions:

Ubuntu is probably one of the best "Run out of the box" distos I've seen and would recommend it to any person interested in trying out linux, everything worked for me right after the installation was complete.

Apt is a great pkg management tool, although I must confess I prefer FBSDs over anything I've used. You get the best of both worlds...source and binaries.

I think its rather silly to have kubuntu and ubuntu...why not just make an installer option of which desktop you want KDE or Gnome? Or perhaps nothing ( I myself like a minimal setup consisting of fluxbox as my WM).

I would like to see an installer option of which services you want installed and or running at boot time.  

Anyway these are just some things I can think of at the moment I'll post some more after tinkering around a little more with hoary  :wink:

Either way great distribution, look foward to watching it grow

### Post by MasonM on 2005-04-12
I've been using Linux since 1995, Slack, Debian, SuSE, Mandrake, Gentoo, SimplyMepis, Libranet, and others. I have to admit I'm pretty favorably impressed with Ubuntu so far.

As for having both KDE and Gnome included on the CD, that would mean leaving a bunch of other stuff off to make room on the CD. I believe Ubuntu is trying to keep the distro down to a single CD, therefore something would have to be traded off.

It's not like it's a big deal to install KDE once the Ubuntu installation is completed. The greatest thing about Linux is having choices. If you don't like what's included, remove it. If you want something that isn't included, install it. Isn't it great? :)
