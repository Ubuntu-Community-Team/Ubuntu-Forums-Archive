### Post by Vinton90 on 2012-09-12
One thing that I have learned to do before jumping in and signing a contract, is to ask people who live in the same area what provider they use, and how consistent their signal/network is. You may sign with a provider that sounds great in theory but then find that in the areas you most frequent, your service is terrible. 
Where I live we have Virizon but Sprint has the majority of the market. My father (who uses Virizon) has to drive to the next state in order to get in person help. I don't use Sprint but T-Mobile and I'm stuck in a contract for the next few years with a company that doesn't provide the greatest service for my area and or my needs.

As for phones, I'm currently using a Sasmung Gravity with qwerty keyboard. It's alright but after nearly a year it is starting to slow, and I can't keep many of the apps on it that I would like. The only thing keeping me from upgrading is that many of the phones out now have stopped using the qwerty keyboard and I'm not ready to let it go.

Hope that helps.
