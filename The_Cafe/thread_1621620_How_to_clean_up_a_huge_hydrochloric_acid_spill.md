### Post by sprintexec on 2010-11-14
How you 'deal' with such a spill will depend on what has become involved along with the HCl and what else might become involved. If you have no specialist chemicals to hand to neutralise the HCl an initial approach would be to call the Fire Service (in UK), keep people out of the hazard area. If anyone has become contaminated they would need to be urgently washed down with copious quantities of water. You could if circumstances permitted create a temporary bund using dry earth, cement or sand. If its a huge spill you might need access to a mechanical digger. These are just a few primer ideas....

### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-11-14
Set up rubber dams around the spill--you would need to research the appropriate type of rubber that is resistant to hcl.

I would raid the local newspaper of all of their rolls of newsprint.  Drop the rolls into the spill.  The acid will attack the cellulose in the newsprint creating viscose.  Not enough newsprint--look for a large source of sawdust, that will work as well.

You can then pump the viscose into tanker trucks to a processing facility--such as a fabrics plant.  Viscose can be spun to make rayon.  The rayon can be woven into T-Shirts that are then sold to the townsfolk--"I Survived the 2010 Acid Bath".

That's worth at least a "B".
