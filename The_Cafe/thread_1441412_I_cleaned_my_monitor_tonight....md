### Post by sisco311 on 2010-03-28
For my 10+ year old CRT monitor I use a cotton wool saturated with rubbing alcohol (or aftershave or [pálinka]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palinka") :)).

The screen of an LCD or plasma monitor is more fragile. For cleaning LCD monitors you can still use rubbing alcohol or a mixture of vinegar and water, but for plasma monitors _always_ use a special fluid designed for plasma monitors.

[http://www.wisegeek.com/how-can-i-clean-a-computer-monitor.htm](http://www.wisegeek.com/how-can-i-clean-a-computer-monitor.htm)
