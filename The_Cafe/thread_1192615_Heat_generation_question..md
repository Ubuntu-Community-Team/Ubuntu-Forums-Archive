### Post by gn2 on 2009-06-20
To answer the specific question posed, if all three hard drives are the same, the flat out 1500 plus two hard drives will produce more heat.
The 1500 has a TDP of 60w, the 5200X2 is 65w.

However, when assessing the overall heat output of the two PCs you need to consider the heat output of all the other fitted components. 

What form factor are the hard drives? 
3.5" ones generate much more heat than 2.5" ones.

get a watt meter (the plug in to a wall socket, and have a socket on the front for an electrical device, they show the power usage on a little screen).
