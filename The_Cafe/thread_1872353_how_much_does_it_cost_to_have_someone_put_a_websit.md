A price you charge for personal work is only related to the time it takes you and what your time is worth not based on a web site stat.

If you are inexperienced then do a good job, take it on the chin and use it as a portfolio and reference for future work where you will have a better idea of how much your time and effort is worth ;-)

### Post by Paqman on 2011-10-30
$100 is pretty cheap if you're having to do the Wordpress theme from scratch, but I don't agree outright with the prices on the site you linked to. There are too many variables, and as haqking said if you're just starting out then your should be offering a hefty discount as:
[LIST=1]
[*]You're inexperienced, so shouldn't be charging full-on professional rates
[*]You want to build a portfolio
[/LIST]

Are you going to be supporting the site? If so, what are you charging for that? Could be worth taking a beating on the price to get yourself ongoing maintenance work.

### Post by alphacrucis2 on 2011-10-30
I spent a lot of time setting up a web site for a charity. My fee was $0.00. A commercial job is a different story and you should probably charge based on the time you spend. I suspect that companies that do this as their normal business wouldn't deliver much if any of a web site for $100.

I would say follow Paqman's advice here as undercharging could be worth it if you intend to use this site as a reference for other jobs.

### Post by satanselbow on 2011-10-30
As a UK based professional web designer (soletrader, not wage slave - my choice!) I would be charging £200 to install a out the box Wordpress install. No customisation, no hosting fees yada-yada included... and I'm cheap (again - my choice!)

Hey - I did plenty of work for friends/family and didn't get paid a bean before the money coming in could even be considered "regular income".

Take it on the chin - do them a good job and then retrospectively decompile it to work out what you would actually need to charge to make it pay - you may be surprised ;)
