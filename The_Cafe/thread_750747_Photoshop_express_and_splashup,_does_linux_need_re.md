### Post by Foster Grant on 2008-04-10
There are some cludges out there for CMYK support, but I can't say I'm impressed so far. What really hurts The GIMP is its execrable interface.

Which is too bad. The other various publishing tools on Linux &#8212; and here I'm including CinePaint, Inkscape and Scribus (*especially* Scribus) &#8212; have almost matured to the point that a freelance graphic designer could have his or her entire workflow (and billing, thanks to Gnumoney) in free-as-in-no-money open-source software. Buy an inexpensive modern PC (even a laptop) and install Linux rather than dropping a couple grand on a Mac and then another grand on Adobe Creative Suite.

Photoshop Express is not worth talking about at this point, and possibly never. I am not pleased with the license terms. I'm not sure what the license terms are for Splashup.

Honestly, web apps ... I don't trust 'em. I prefer to run trusted binaries on my computer rather than take a chance on remote code.
