### Post by Mikes80 on 2008-06-07
I recently started to learn Java, and am currently reading through the official Java tutorial. Upon reading the [_Code sample license_]("http://http://developers.sun.com/license/berkeley_license.html") in the tutorial I came across what I thought was a slightly odd and humorous line.

The first part of the license is the usual 'You can use this, but must redistribute with the license blah, blah', all very open source. It's the final line of the license that made me laugh and left me feeling slightly puzzled;

> You acknowledge that this software is not designed, licensed or intended for use in the design, construction, operation or maintenance of any ***nuclear*** facility.

*Nuclear facility?* Why nuclear facility? :confused:. When you take into account that most of the examples in the tutorial are related to creating a Bicycle class this makes this statement even more strange!

Perhaps some one out there in Ubuntu Land can explain this specific and odd statement.

Also does anyone else have any examples of similar funny license clauses whether software related or not?

### Post by klange on 2008-06-07
It's a liability thing. If you use X software from Y company in Z occupation that is excluded in the EULA, and X software causes a major disaster - your nuclear power plant goes into meltdown, your life support system fails, etc. - Y company is not responsible.

It's not nearly as funny as iTunes + nuclear weapons, which is just a plain old "wtf?".
