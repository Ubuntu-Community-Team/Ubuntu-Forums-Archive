Living in Canada and having worked in the U.S., I am confirming that in Canada, Debit Cards are not the same as Credit Cards. I remember in the U.S. that I had a debit card that had a Visa number and a Visa symbol on it, which allowed it to be used like a Visa Card, including making internet purchases. In Canada, Debit Cards are issued by individual financial institutions and have no association whatsoever to either Visa or Mastercard.

As for why the charge from Canonical would not be authorised, that is a conversation that you need to have with your bank, or with Visa if your Debit Card has the Visa symbol on it. A lot of 'credit card' companies, like Visa, are very conscious of not allowing foreign charges. This can usually be cleared up with a call to the bank to indicate that these transactions should be allowed. They will put a note on file for your card.

Hope this helps,
