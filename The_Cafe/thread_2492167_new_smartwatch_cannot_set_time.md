### Post by Automat2 on 2023-11-04
1. You may be able to set up a virtual SIM card (eSIM) to the smartwatch, so it would use your cell phone's SIM account - with your network provider, without the need to have a BT connection available at the time. In some ways it would be similar to what Google Pay/Apple do with our bank details when we use our mobile device rather than the bank card to pay in shops, etc. ESIMs allow to receive and make phone calls, texts, emails, receive notifications independently from the phone.
Please note that this is different from the smartwatch being able to record and collect data away from the phone, as it understands that it will collect the data, store it and eventually connect to the phone and sync.
2. If there is one, I don't know.
3. Smartwatches/fitness trackers sync date/time from the network, either independently (eSIM) or via connection to a phone.
