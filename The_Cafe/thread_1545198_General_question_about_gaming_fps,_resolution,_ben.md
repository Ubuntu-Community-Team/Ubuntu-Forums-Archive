### Post by Zorgoth on 2010-08-03
I have a laptop (described in my sig) with a good CPU and a weak video card.  I am trying to figure out what games to buy for it.  I am considering Starcraft 2 in particular but there doesn't seem to be a demo and I'm not shelling out $60 if I don't know I'll get getter than 10fps on 640x480 or something.

So I have been looking through posts of people's performance of the game under wine and am trying to compare the results with what I'd get with my own card.  Most linux games have the good fortune to not be saddled with ATI and I presume I'll take a hit from that, which I'm hoping to estimate by comparing the performance/benchmark ratio of my WoW to nVidia cards (in directx mode).


And the main question is, when the GPU and definitively not the CPU is the bottleneck, how does resolution scale to frame rate?  I mean, if someone was playing at 1680x1050 and I plan to play at 1024x768, I assume I'm probably not getting a 2x frame rate boost, but what I am I going to gain?  And fps scales ~linearly to benchmark numbers, yes?

### Post by handy on 2010-08-03
The [**_Phoronix Test Suite_**]("http://www.phoronix-test-suite.com/") is a large download, but it gives a very thorough test of your machine, which you can easily upload at the end of the lengthy process to the Phoronix site. Where you can compare your results with others which could be very helpful in your circumstances.
