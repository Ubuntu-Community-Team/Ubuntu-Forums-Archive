### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2008-03-31
> **chucky chuckaluck said:**
> obviously, this fuscia character and i are polar opposites.

It depends, really. There are two kinds of people in Linux, I've noticed (naturally I'm exaggerating the divide)::

1) The performance hounds. These are the people who love whittling down every last megabyte of RAM they can, seeing if they can get it running on a 1994 PC with 16MB of memory and a 300MB hard disk and still have it blazing. They aim for top-notch performance and have no qualms sacrificing some eye candy for it. To them, the beauty of Linux is in the ability to tune it like a race car, like modding a Geo Metro to outrun a Lambo.

2) The aesthetic nuts. These folks are the ones who love how much you can modify the appearance of Linux. Any app that gets installed and any icon placed in a folder has to be made to fit in with the overall appearance. Sure they might use a few hundred more MB of RAM and it takes a half minute longer for the system to start up, not to mention things just don't move as quickly, but it looks as slick and polished as can be. This camp is willing to sacrifice a little performance to make it look better.

Now obviously there's overlap, the tweakers want it to look good at the eye candy crowd still wants the system fast and easy on the resources, but I think that's a fair observation regardless.

Me, I'm firmly entrenched in the second camp. Most of my time spend modifying stuff is on gnome-look, and I'm avoiding Opera because I'm having a hard time getting it to look as good as I want it (even if it's faster and renders pages better), and my music player choice was partly due to GTK integration (Audacious, damn you...).

As a result, my wallpaper is a HUGE part of my experience. Even though it's on the bottom, I never have my windows maximized so I can always see a fair amount of it, and as a result a change in the wallpaper makes a palpable difference in my experience. To folks more concerned with performance, it likely wouldn't, but to me it does.
