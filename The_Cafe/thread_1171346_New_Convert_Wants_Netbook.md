### Post by Johnsie on 2009-05-27
EEEPC or MSI Wind (clone) is the way to go if you're looking for a good price.

Don't go for anything with a small hard small hard disk though or you will spend alot of your time trying to manage space. Screen size is important too. My previous EEEPC 701 had a 7 inch screen and it too a while to get used to. Having only 4GB meant that I needed to compress /usr to create space and every once in a while that needs some maintenance(compressed folders are read only so I had to create an overlay that needs compressed every once in a while). My newer more current Advent netbook is 10 inch and the improvement is vast (I still love my 701 though which has and continues to serve me well).

Both my 701 and my Advent run Ubuntu pretty well and were cheap. The keys are important too... Some keyboards on netbooks are really hard to type with. Make sure the buttons are in the right places and if you touch type that you can actually feel the indents on the home keys. On the EEEPCthat was fine, but my Advent netbook has very flat keys and is hard to type on. Also some of the keys are in weird places and it's hard to feel the home keys.

BTW alot of newer netbooks have mobile broadband modems built into them. For some people that is more convenient than using a dongle when you want to surf on the move. Not sure how well they work with Ubuntu though as I'm using XP on the one that has that.

### Post by starcannon on 2009-05-27
I think this is a fairly honest article; it's short, but to the point.
[http://buzzdroid.com/linux/ubuntu/is-ubuntu-right-for-your-small-office/](http://buzzdroid.com/linux/ubuntu/is-ubuntu-right-for-your-small-office/)

I would add that I think the cost saved in virus/malware/etc. fighting and removal is an excellent economic factor to consider when weighing the pros and cons. If MS Office is key there will be a need for Crossover Office; but, if Open Office will meet your needs then you have even more money saved. If one is dealing with a database, it may be prudent to research that compatibility as well, and perhaps even consider transitioning to a MySQL environment at some point.

Don't forget to factor in the time required to get everyone up to speed with the new OS. Most people in an office environment are mouse clickers and button pushers, so there will likely need to be some in service training sessions to get everyone going.

Wishing you and your office a smooth and successful switch if it is what you all decide to do.
