### Post by Dimitriid on 2007-10-09
I own a 6131 ( basically the same exact thing but Nokia changes models when they change countries ). It is an outstanding simple phone, a lot of the functionality of a "smartphone" but easier on the pocket. Having it with 1 or 2gb microsd card means you can use it as an mp3 player easily ( honestly, when do you really need to listen to more music than that? And even if you do this phone can hotswap microsd cards anyway )

But my gf recently bought a similar but fancier model, the 6300 series:

[IMG]http://www.about-nokia.com/blog/media/1/20061128-nokia6300.jpg[/IMG]

If you prefer a slim model with a better camera ( 2 megapixels ) you might want to go with it.

About bluetooth well, I dont have a working bluetooth card at the moment (Acer claims my laptop has one but it does not work even on the preinstalled, soon to be gone Vista.) I did get a DKU-2 USB cable though and the phone easily lets you choose between "phone" or "storage". Storage works great to transfer without removing cards and such ( or if you do not have a working linux sdcard reader, mines are a bit of a hassle and the linux hal daemon is a real hit and miss with em, with different results literally each time you try to automount em, manual mount slightly better but similar results ) 

Phone functionality however...I only got 3 programs to recognize this phone: Wamu, KMobiletools and gnome phone manager. Wamu appears to work with the DKU-2 Cable but fails to download my messages from the phone ( after looking for Troubleshooting info the best I could find is "oh yea its a program error wait for a new version" which who knows when will be available since its a frontend to another app ). KMobileTools or whatever its called also had similar problems: It wouldn't let me get my messages to the computer, and when I tried to send a message it sent nothing but my phone company charged me as if it did.

Gnome phone manager its really, really minimal: it literally only has 3 options. Phone was not being recognized so I had to manually set up my mobile device location. After that it said "connected" but I later found it likes to lie too: It would not open incoming sms messages and when I tried sending one it sent it to the wrong contact even after doublechecking the number and all.
