### Post by qBaz on 2006-06-04
So I'm writing a paper about technology users, experts, and the distinctions between them.  I'm posting these questions on a couple of different boards around the 'net, and hoping that a person or two might take a moment to muse on how they regard technology.  I really appreciate anyone who responds!  Come on up to New England, and I'll buy you a beverage!  :)

(When I say "technology," I'm mostly thinking of computer technology, for the purposes of this paper.  If something else comes to mind for you when you hear the word "technology," you could certainly go with it.)

######################################################################

What is the first piece of technology you remember using?  Did it work the
first time?  What, if anything, do you remember feeling about it?  Do you
still use it?




Growing up, did your use of technology bring you closer to your peers, or
distance you from them?  What did the distance or closeness, if any, feel
like?



What technology do you use habitually, i.e. you would miss it if you didn't
use it?  Have you, in fact, ever gone without it?  How did you feel without
it?  When you resumed it?



Ever stayed up late (or early) solving a technology problem?  Why did you
lose sleep over it?  Was the payoff worth it?




Are you different from someone who simply _uses_ technology?  Do you generate new tools, tricks, or technologies?  If so, does that make you regard "users" differently than "creators" or "experts?"
