### Post by Zimmer on 2006-10-13
> **Bezmotivnik said:**
> OK, but what does that actually mean?  What are they for?  What is their intended significance?

Thanks for any clarification.

GCE  General Certificate of Education

O  Ordinary level  , usually taken at age 15-16 

A  Advanced Level    usually a two year course exam taken at @ age 18

Students would choose several subject options for O level study (usually about 7 different subjects) and spend around two school years preparing for the exams.
Then if their grades were good enough they might go on to what is known as sixth form to study for around 3 or maybe 4 A - level subjects.

To gain entrance to a University in the UK a student would usually require 3  good A -level grades , one of which would be in a subject relevant to the intended degree course. 

The GCE was replaced by the GCSE (General Certificate of Secondary Education) and many of the exams were modified to include coursework spread over the two year study period.
*Secondary = the period of education from age 11+ 

Over simplified, but hope that clarifies the situation
