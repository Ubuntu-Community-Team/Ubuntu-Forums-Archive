### Post by aysiu on 2006-05-26
Well, obviously, a lot of what's a "novice" or an "expert" is self-defined.

I always refer to myself as a "newbie" (I've been using Linux a little over a year now--Ubuntu a year and two days), but some forum members think I'm crazy for identifying that way.

I don't know what labels go where, but I'd say these are some distinct tiers I've seen:

1. I have no clue about Ubuntu or Linux or anything not Windows or Mac. In fact, even on Mac or Windows, I had very little clue. You can't assume anything with me.

2. I know a lot about Windows or Mac. I understand what RAM is, what partitions are. I get the concept of how computers work. I know next to nothing about Linux, though.

3. I know a little bit about Linux. I've read a couple of books about it, and I've tried a distribution or two. I dual-boot or use live CDs only. I don't know how to troubleshoot any problems.

4. I know it a bit better. I can actually help out other people. I still ask for more help than I give, though. Things are actually starting to work!

5. I'm no seasoned veteran, but I can troubleshoot most simple problems for people. I have a working installation and, better yet, I have an installation I can intentionally break... and then fix again!

6. I intentionally break and fix my system just for fun. In fact, I have more than one system, and I play around a lot. I'm thinking about learning how to program for Linux even though I've a) only programmed for Windows before or b) never programmed at all.

7. I know a lot about Linux even though I've been using it only a short time. I develop. I recompile kernels. I really tweak out my system. If someone (a friend or relative) wants me to install Linux on her computer, I feel pretty comfortable installing just about any distro she wants just about any way she wants, and I'm confident I would know how to remotely administer the machine or troubleshoot it. I know the perfect parts to put together a Linux-compatible computer from scratch.

8. I've been using Linux since twelve years ago. I live and breathe Linux. I'm heavily involved in the community and development.

9. I'm practically Linus Torvalds.

10. I *am* Linus Torvalds.

With my own criteria, I'd say I'm at about #5.
