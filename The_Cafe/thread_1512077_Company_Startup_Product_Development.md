### Post by samalex on 2010-06-17
In our community, there's a big need for local mom-and-pop computer shops because most have closed in recent years.  I think many made their bread and butter from home users doing upgrades, building new systems, etc, but anymore most home users order from Dell, Best Buy, Apple, etc, and buy new instead of upgrading.  The few local shops who still are around do more work with businesses, which there's a huge market for.

If you can get a good grasp of networking, setting-up corporate networks, configuring servers (Windows, Linux, and Apple), and also possibly get into VoIP with Asterisk, you could really bring allot to the table for small to mid-sized businesses.  Get certified to work on systems by Dell, HP, etc and you can do warranty work.  Get your CCNA and you'll be able to roll-out some awesome network layouts depending on corporate need.

Unfortunately too many people, like me, enjoyed working with home users but that market just isn't there anymore.  I used to do work on the side upgrading hard drives, adding new hardware (modems, network cards, etc), and installing software... but anymore most home users only need virus and spyware clean-up which I don't care to do.  I know several people who make house calls and honestly rake in the $$$ by helping people after hours do just this, but it's just not for me.

So I say if you have the capital, skill, and come-up with a good market plan, plus if you can find some clients right off the bat, go for it!  

Sam
