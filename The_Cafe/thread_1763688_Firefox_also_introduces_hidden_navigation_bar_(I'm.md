If you want the icon, you could move it down to the Add-on bar, and when you need it, just press ctrl+/.  Also, you can access the noscript options through the context menu too.  The menu that comes up with the keyboard shortcut you're using is better than these two, since it doesn't go away after you've whitelisted one site.

A little tip: when you hover the mouse over a link, it just gives the title: to see the full url, hold shift.

### Post by Larkspur on 2011-05-20
> **nrundy said:**
> Is there a way to activate the dash via keyboard? ctrl + L opens URL box. but is there a way to just display what tabs you have by tapping a keyboard shortcut?

Well, there is a box showing previews of tabs to the right of the Url bar, but that takes a little explaining (it's actually the bit that makes some people dislike Home Dash) but to see all your tabs you can do two things:

-Use Panorama (Ctrl+Shift+E)
-Move your mouse to the top left hand corner (or hold down a mouse key anywhere): the screen will darken and the FF icon will appear with favicons for all your tabs below it; hover you mouse over each one to see previews in the right half of the screen.  This is also how you get the navigation controls.
