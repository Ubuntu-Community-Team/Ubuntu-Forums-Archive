### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-11-20
> **PondPuppy said:**
> 

With respect to privacy...user data...

Back in the primeval days of the web, pre-Google,  it wasn't unusual to find commercial sites that expected people to pay. Not surprising, since authors and publishers have used that model for a few centuries. 

People made it abundantly clear they weren't interested in paying. That motivated commercial sites to look for other ways to make money, and they've all pretty much settled on massaging the user data the web's architecture makes dead simple to collect.

So, today, most any semi-literate stumblebum can stand up a click-bait site and try to make a few bucks.

While people like to complain about the tracking, etc., going on across the web, I haven't heard anyone say they'd pay Google $50 per year to stop tracking them, and pay Facebook to do the same, etc. Most of us seem to think we're entitled to get the web free of charge. As long as that holds, corporations will find other ways to make us pay.
