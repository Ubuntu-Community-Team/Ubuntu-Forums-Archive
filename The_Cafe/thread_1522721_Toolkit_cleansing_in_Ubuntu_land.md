### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-07-02
Ubuntu's great, and it's done a lot to popularize Linux-based operating systems among noobs, common consumers, and the technologically aware. It also seems to have done a lot to popularize Gnome.

I went back to a few polls from around 2004 and they all rated KDE above Gnome in popularity. I'm not sure if anyone else who has been in the community for a while has noticed this, but it seems that the big names (Mandriva, openSUSE) used primarily KDE for a while.

I really like Gnome, don't get me wrong, but I seem to be running into a lot of hostile comments lately. Nothing like, "KDE sucks," and "it's bloated Vista-ware," kind of stuff. No, this time it's mainly based around QT, and ETK for that matter.

Whenever people bring up a really nice application like rekonq, the older Opera, Kopete, minitube, etc. I tend to hear mostly comments of, "why not GTK!? This sucks!" and "who uses qt? It just makes the interface look ugly."

They seem to be blissfully unaware of the millions of KDE users who have to deal with GTK every day, and who rarely give these kinds of complaints, at least in Ubuntu-related veins (probably due to minority more than politeness).

I guess having round menus in Gnome really unsettles some people.

But really, I hope that people can just accept the minorities in our community rather than face them with mere annoyance and ignorance. I find it extremely ironic that people who use an open source OS can be so close-minded to something so minute. I guess we just learn to be picky, and it leads us to be picky at the expense of others.

Anyway, I hope we don't breed KDE and QT out of the open source ecosystem, since it's very nice. T^T I guess I'm just expressing my uneasy feelings of discrimination among a community of Gnome users. If you're a Gnome user who doesn't loathe KDE, I'd like to hear your opinion on this.

And please, tell your other Gnome-using friends about the problematic nature of toolkit discrimination. :O It's become rampant in our society! </sarcasm>
