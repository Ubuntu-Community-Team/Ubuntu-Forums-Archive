### Post by dkaddict on 2007-05-23
I doubt I am alone in this dilemma. I haven't looked back since switching to Ubuntu some 7 or so months ago. In fact, I can safely say that making the transition to a fully open-source OS has been the best thing I have done since first logging on to the net many years ago. The biggest pull for me has easily been the ideology behind the OpenSource communities movements in the world of Microsoft et-al. I absolutely agree with everything OpenSource stands for and am firmly of the belief that 'free' software, by its very nature, will outperform its proprietary counterparts in the vast majority of cases.

Now my dillema is as follows.

I have a few friends who, upon my teling them of the virtues of installing Ubuntu from a live CD, hassled me to burn them each an Ubuntu Edgy live CD. I have done so and they have the discs. The problem is, they are too scared to even boot the live CD let alone install it. I have offered to tak them through the process on the telephone (they are far flung friends and family), sent them links to pictorial, partition>livecdboot>install web pages, sent them all of the how 2s I could have really done with when I first started out with Ubuntu, and generally told them that Windows has an alternative. They remain, alas, too scared. Yep, scared. That is the word which is coming across.

Has anyone managed to help people to take the plunge who have this fear that anything except MS Win will render their PCs and Laptops unusable? I've tried. Tried hard. No joy.

I may have said this somewhere else before, I can't remember where so here it is. 

This 'fear of all but MS Win' is, I feel, one of the biggest obstacles which the Open Source OS community faces when trying to get people to try Linux. If anyone gets past that little (huge??) psychological barrier and actually gets Ubuntu booted on their machine (that bit, the 'their machine' bit is crucial as some of my friends have seen Ubuntu and Kubuntu on my notebook) by way of the live cd, I am pretty sure that that's most of the battle won. Common sense took over when I tried out Ubuntu first of all and I couldn't get rid of MS Win as my main OS fast enough. Exscuse my french, but Ubuntu, Kubuntu are **** hot and make that known to anyone with half a brain who tries them for long enough.

I can't really think of trying anything more than I already have to get my friends to progress to Open Source. They are intelligent people who take matters such as the environment, politics, etc seriously. Linux and Open Source fits their outlook upon life yet MS has somehow gotten right under their skin.

Any ideas are welcome. Bug #1 remains.:sad:

Peace

dkaddict

### Post by Spr0k3t on 2007-05-23
My whole family has made the switch to Linux (not just Ubuntu).  What I did was start by "upgrading" the office software for the family.  I gave them the choice of moving from Office2000 to Office2007 or OpenOffice.  The choice was a one sided OOo.  After that, I started giving them applications such as Gaim, Thunderbird, Firefox, Inkwell, Gimp and the likes.  They loved it... eventually I had everyone working with free or open source applications for their computer usage.

So one night at dinner I made the suggestion that everyone should switch to using Linux instead of Windows.  The kids didn't give me much huff about it, but the rest of the family was totally freaked.  It came down to the applications in the arguments... mainly compatibility issues and loss of personal data.  When I explained to them the software wouldn't change, just the underlying operating system... they agreed to try it for thirty days.  When they saw that most of their applications ran faster in Linux, there was no holding back.  Going on three months now... and they all sound like total geeks.
