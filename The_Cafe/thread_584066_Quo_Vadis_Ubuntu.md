### Post by Hillerd on 2007-10-20
Hi, 
I just downloaded the live-CD of gutsy and am enthusiastic, but looking at the release notes [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/GutsyGibbon/ReleaseNotes]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/GutsyGibbon/ReleaseNotes"), I wonder where Ubuntu is heading for.
Some history. About 1 year ago I had a look at various Linux distributions via live-CDs. I decided for Ubuntu 6.06, installed it as 2nd operating system with the scope to "play a bit around with Linux". Well, 3 months afterwards I realized, that I was not booting WXP any more and had converted completely (apart of some astro software I bought that does not run with WINE). Then there were the usual upgrades: 6.10, 7.04 and my enthusiasm ever increased. But with 7.04 the trouble began. Having a laptop with the kernel update to 2.6.20-16 it was not able to control the battery any more. There were several updates, but none to fix that issue. I am back at 2.6.20-15. Ubuntu simply did not care for laptop users.
Now I'd like to upgrade to 7.10 because of plenty of improvements (e.g. Gnome, Evolution, Gimp). But having an ATI graphics card (I need fglrx mostly for its reduced power consumption and better visual display) with bugs like black screen, no session manager, no suspend (did not work before anyway, so no real problem) this is simply not possible (anyway, thanks for the release notes to make me aware of it!). I am using my laptop daily and cannot afford having a non-functional operating system.
So Gutsy is nice but not for every user. Ubuntu seemed to be able to attrack "normal" users but my impression with the last 2 releases is, that the quality assurance is not working sufficiently. Maybe instead of releasing every 6 months a shiny new version it would be better to increase the period of development but to have functional new versions.
So my question: where are you heading for Ubuntu? Quick shiny features for hackers or an operation system that is suitable for every-day users and that really can compete with the nearly monopolist? I'd rather preferred the second.

PS: For now I will stay with 7.04, I would not even consider converting back to WXP (as some people mention in their posts), Linux is simply too powerful to give up. But maybe breaking up the distribution and installing the improved applications separately could become an option...
