### Post by bashveank on 2009-04-13
#1 tip: Build up a following. Once you do this Twitter becomes very interesting, you can throw out a question like "What's the best microphone to buy" and you'll get 10 or 20 responses in 5 minutes. You can link to an article and blow up it's hit count. You can poll people for opinions and get a very engaging discussion going.
There are many ways you can do this - It's not easy, but it's very rewarding:

Saying useful things, When I attend press conferences and lectures I tweet anything of significance that goes on during the event and add the appropriate hash tag. I get about 6 new followers each time I do this. Also, whenever I can give out random tips (for me, it's photography tips, graphic design tips, linking to articles I find useful, etc...) I don't have any tangible follower increase based on these tips, but I believe the reason for my steady follower growth is my helpful tips.

Develop a distinct style. I don't limit myself to a particular field, but I probably should. Some of the most followed Twitterers are the most followed Twitterers because their followers know what to expect. Whether it's the humor Twitterers (Fake McCain, Darth Vader, etc...) or the serious ones that only post things on a specific topic (Digg on the top stories on Digg, AdoramaPix on Adorama coupons, Scott Bourne on Photography tips)

Plug the hell out of your Twitter account. Put it in your signature, tell all your friends to follow you, add yourself to wefollow.com, etc...

Most importantly, once you have a following, connect with them. Reply to lots of posts, try to answer people's random questions, participate in Twitter memes, ReTweet things you find interesting, etc.... 

Oh, and unprotect your status updates if you want to be found.


P.S.
follow me! twitter.com/bashveank

I follow about 80 people, of which only around 20 are regular tweeters, but they talk about things that are interesting and relevant.
