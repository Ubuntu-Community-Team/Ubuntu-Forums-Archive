### Post by bwallum on 2007-10-01
Hi, firstly welcome. I did the transition starting 6 months back and now wouldn't use anything else. It is different, you have a lot more control and that means you can screw up as well. The community are brilliant however and have got me out of any trouble I got myself into. It just needs a little patience.

I am running Gutsy (7.10) but it has some eccentricities that need to be overcome before it runs absolutely great. So I suggest you load up 7.04, wait a few more days, then upgrade to Gutsy when it is out of beta and released for production work.

If you are really keen and adventurous Gutsy will work. Just be careful of the video setup screen when it first boots. Leave all as generic and don't press the OK (GUI) button. Instead switch off the computer by the power (REAL) button.

Then start afresh, when grub comes up, press esc for the menu. Then select the kernel version 2.6.20-16. Gutsy will work after that with possibly some eccentric behaviour when you try to adjust your monitor and graphics card to suit your actual setup. It is not a big problem and disappears after a couple of boots. Everything else works fine. in particular things like BBC News streams are much easier to get going in Gutsy. Use VLC player for dvds (utterly painless) and MPlayer for steaming (still a bit clitchy but workable).

Remember you can load Fiesty then upgrade through the update manager quite easily when Gutsy is finally released for production. I wouldn't put too much effort into personalising Fiesty though.

Regards
Bob
