### Post by fuscia on 2006-02-26
django reinhardt was one of the best jazz guitarists of all time. his hands were burnt in a fire in 1928 ('29?) so it's pretty astonishing what he was able to do.

there's a whole pile of miles davis (trumpet player) you can choose from. the guy went through even more styles of music than he went through styles of clothing.

eric johnson might be to our liking. amazing technician who favors tube amps.

blues rock bands with great guitarists are numerous: cream, allman brothers, johnny winter, ten years after...there are so many i can't even think of them.

pat metheny (jazz guitarist) might be a little too spacey for you, but worth a try.

### Post by matthew on 2006-02-26
Okay, I looked this list over a few times and they're not all blues/jazz, but I couldn't bring myself to erase anyone here. I'll annotate a little as well

***Stevie Ray Vaughan!!!!!!!!!!!!!***
My all time favorite...I wish he was still alive.

[I][B]Al DiMeola
[/B][/I]***Wes Montgomery***
Straight up jazz and very good at it

***Les Paul***
The man is a stellar guitar player and at 92 years old still holds down a weekly gig in New York!! He also invented most of the non-distortion effects in use today as well as multi-track recording and had a hand in the invention of the electric guitar.

***Eric Clapton (especially Blues Breakers, Cream, Derek and the Dominos, and Blind Faith eras, but I still really like him)***
Again, a personal guitar hero. He's not as fiery or flashy as when he was younger but he's still better than most of us will ever be. He also seems like a great guy since he got clean and sober in the 1990's.

***Django Reinhardt***
Gypsy jazz recorded in France in the 1920's-40's

***Eric Johnson***
Highly skilled, great tone. Sometimes he lacks in emotive qualties to my ears, though. YMMV

[I][B]Gary Moore
Jeff Beck
[/B][/I][I][B]Roy Buchanan
[/B][/I]***Joe Satriani***
Wow. That's all I can say. Check them out.

[I][B]Henry Garza from Los Lonely Boys
[/B][/I]My favorite currently-recording and touring band and guitarist. The band is made up of three Mexican heritage men from Texas who are all stellar musicians and have a chemistry that is just amazing. I saw them live in Phoenix and they had no set list. They just huddled together after each song and chose something. At one point they shocked some of the members of the opening band by inviting them onstage to help perform an impromptu performance of Stevie Wonder's _Superstition_. Henry Garza plays like a combination of Stevie Ray Vaughan, Carlos Santana, and I'm not sure what else, but he's kept me in awe since their first CD came out in 2004.

[I][B]Rev Gary Davis
Blind Willie Johnson
[/B][/I]These two are from a similar era to Django Reinhardt. They were unabashedly Christian so if that might offend you look elsewhere. However, they were STELLAR guitarists in a country/acoustic/rural blues sort of way and they have been cited as influences by all the big "British Invasion" guitarists like Clapton, Page, Beck, the Beatles, the Rolling Stones and lots more. The surviving recordings aren't always of the greatest quality, but the music is awe inspiring.
