### Post by johnjust on 2007-09-18
> **GeneralZod said:**
> I don't know what a "column-editing mode" is, but most KDE apps (including Kate) have FTP open/ save (plus a load of other protocols) "built-in", as it were.

I didn't know Kate had an FTP client built-in, that's definitely good news.  Column-mode editing is like this: say you built a table in html:

<table>
  <tr>
    <td></td>
    <td></td>
    <td></td>
  </tr>
</table>

Uh-oh, now your cells are all blank, let's say you wanted to put a link tag in there.  You could type it in once, then copy/paste it, or you could use what's known as column-mode.  In UltraEdit, you would place the cursor inside the first <td></td> tag, turn on Comun-mode, and shift+drag it down to the other two cells.  Start typing, and it types in everywhere the cursor lies (I suppose row-editing may be a little more accurate, but since it's at Line X, Col Y, it kinda makes sense.)

To LaRoza, I just checked out the Crimson Editor, and I noticed it also has the column-mode AND FTP built-in
