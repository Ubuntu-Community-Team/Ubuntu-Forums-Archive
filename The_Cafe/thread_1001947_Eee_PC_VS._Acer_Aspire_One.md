### Post by Onyros on 2008-12-04
I'm surprised there aren't more people considering the N10JC (Asus). It has a discrete graphics chip which can be toggled/alternated with its integrated chip, comes with two batteries (3 cell and 6 cell), a 160GB HDD, an external slim dvd player, and a 10 inch screen @ 1024x600, which is the best I've seen on a netbook... and is priced at around 510 euros / 650 dollars / 450 pounds.

This might be a specific package for Portugal, but if it is the same out there, just for the added HDD, external DVD recorder (DL), the two batteries and the discrete graphics chip... it's one helluva package. Just imagine if you wanted to buy an additional battery for an EEEPC 1000, and an external DVD recorder... you still wouldn't have the dedicated graphics.

I have one, and it finally dethroned my Thinkpad X31... which I still believe is an incredible laptop after all these years, so you can imagine how much I like the N10JC.

The only thing that isn't seamless for me is the transition from discrete to integrated graphics - I'm running Arch - but I have a script that takes care of that at boot time.

### Post by gnomeuser on 2009-01-02
A friend of mine has the Aspire One and it has now been in for repairs twice. Broken Wifi and motherboard, this time they have kept the machine for over a month so far. Acer definitely does not score bonus points for this.

I played a bit with the Aspire but found that the touchpad felt like rubbing my fingertip on sandpaper and the button placement was all wrong.

The Eee PC is much nicer, personally I favor the S101 model for style and slickness. Their touchpad is also of much superior quality.

You may also like the Lemote YeeLoong, it's a bit different in approach to the netbook market and you will not be able to install Ubuntu on it (due to the lack of a compatible ASM port). It comes with Debian preinstalled, Coreboot for the BIOS.. everything is supported by Free Software from A-Z.
