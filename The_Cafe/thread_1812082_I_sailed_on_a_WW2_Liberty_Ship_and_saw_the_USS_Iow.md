### Post by earthpigg on 2011-07-25
The SS Jeremiah O'Brien, from Pier 45 in San Francisco. We visited the US West Coast mothball fleet and saw (among others) the USS Iowa. It was rad. 

Some of the pictures are here:
[http://ctmason.wordpress.com/2011/07/25/ss-jeremiah-obrien-ghost-cruise-sighting-of-uss-iowa-bb-61/](http://ctmason.wordpress.com/2011/07/25/ss-jeremiah-obrien-ghost-cruise-sighting-of-uss-iowa-bb-61/)

Some I contributed to various Wikipedia articles (including the [port-a-potty]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port-a-potty") article :P ).

We also saw the Chilean Navy training ship [Esmerelda]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Esmeralda_%28BE-43%29") that may have been used for some very naughty things by the Chilean gov't in the 70s that we cant talk about here.

I haven't updated any of that to include images of the fascinating engine room of that 70 year old ship, but you can see them (and video) by looking at the file names of the otherwise unorganized images [here]("http://dl.dropbox.com/u/1832211/ss%20o%27brien/index.html") (shh, don't tell dropbox I've got an index.html in my public folder). That engine room is significant in pop culture because sound samples from it where used in the Titanic film.

### Post by wrtpeeps on 2011-07-26
Nice.

The view from my office window is an old WW1 warship of the Royal Navy docked in Alexandra Dock at Harland and Wolff Shipyard.

Also have the slipway the Titanic was built on (the gantry's are gone) and the dry dock (The Thompson Dry Dock) the Titanic was fitted in. 

Game of Thrones uses the old H+W Paint shed on site as it's filming location too, and just 30 seconds down the road the SS Nomadic (the last White Star line vessel still afloat) is dry docked.

Every day there are tonnes of tourists in the area to take in the whole shipyard and see all about the Titanic and where it was built etc.
