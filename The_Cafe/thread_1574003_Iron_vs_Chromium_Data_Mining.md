### Post by Taiji on 2010-09-13
I have been wondering about peoples thoughts about this....One thing I do not like about Chrome is that it is helping Google track our every move......yeah, I know Google has some fans since they have been supporting open source, but I am sure that is for their own benefit rather than ours. Its a lot cheaper and more practical to use Linux then make money off optional proprietary extras than to build a competing OS from scratch. Honestly, I dont entirely trust google. I think their level of data mining is excessive. I have also heard people arguments about how there isnt a reasonable expectation of privacy on the net....well, I dont expect total anonymity but I see no reason to assist others in data mining my activity or to make their job easier. 

So I tried Iron....This is the Beta (Alpha?) for Linux. Its almost exactly the same thing as Chrome, only with the google data mining removed.....Honestly, unless you feel the NEED to be data mined I can hardly think of any benefit of Chromium over Iron, besides that you have to compile Iron from source (for now).

I have also heard some criticism about Iron being redundant, that it doesnt bring anything new to the table, that you could accomplish the same thing by modding Chromium, and that the features dont have a lot to do with web surfing privacy......all true, except it specifically removes the GOOGLE data mining software. \

I didnt do any scientific benchmark testing, but Iron seemed a little faster than Chromium.....maybe because it didnt have to wait for google to document your browsing activities, doesnt have built in redirecting processes linked to the google search bar, or whatever....but Iron might even be faster than Opera let alone Chrome. 

I still like firefox for its addons, which are still better than Chromium addons in my opinion, but if you need speed I think Iron is where its at. Its almost the exact same thing as Chromium, not a whole lot new to the table besides a built in add block and the removal of data mining.....none of which will make your browsing more secure in regard to overbearing websites and general analytics (other addons can be used to somewhat combat that), but you get all the benefits of Chromium without the data mining, and a few small tweaks that are nice.

I cant really think of any advantage that Chromium has over Iron besides that we need a .deb file for Linux and that it has not been added to the repositories yet. 



Lunascape is probably one of the best browsers around right now, due to its ability to use software from several major browsers, but I dont believe anyone is porting to Linux right now.
