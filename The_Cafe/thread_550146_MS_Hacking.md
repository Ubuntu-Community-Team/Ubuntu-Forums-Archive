### Post by robert_pectol on 2007-09-13
> **Kingsley said:**
> The writer is crying over nothing. Who the hell prefers to test and manually install their updates on Windows?
Although it may appear to you that nobody would ever elect to manually install and test updates, the fact is that many corporate IT departments do in fact test updates before widely deploying them on their networks.  The chances that an update will conflict with even proprietary software is small.  But the risk is too great to leave it to chance.  It can end up being a costly setback.  I remember a few years ago this happened at my workplace.  One morning the IT department was suddenly flooded with phone calls from the Engineering department.  Apparently an overnight update was pushed out to all the hosts on the network before being tested properly.  Many man hours were lost because we couldn't use one of our software tools until they fixed what that update broke!

> **Kingsley said:**
> The files that he said were updated are required for Windows Update anyway. I hate when people try to make mountains out of gopher hills.

Well, what can I say... one man's gopher hill is another man's mountain... I guess.  But the facts are the facts and if an update ends up costing a company money and/or time, suddenly the gopher hill seems much larger!
