### Post by schauerlich on 2010-10-21
This is an apple discussion, so y'all probably expected me. I've been out of the Cafe for a while, but, here I am.

The App Store makes me uneasy. As much as I dislike fear-mongering and the "OMG CORPORATIONS WANT 2 TAKE UR FREEDUMZ!" mentality that's all over the anti-Mono camp, I can't help but think the App Store is a step in the wrong direction.

I don't think that Apple will specifically lock down OS X to the extent it has iOS. At this point, it'd simply be too much effort for the potential return. iOS and Mac OS X, while sharing a common heritage, are very different OS's for very different tasks.

Instead, I think the danger lies in developer's reaction to the App Store: What? I can come out with an app today, send it to apple and they'll make it available to EVERYONE who has a Mac? Free distribution, free hosting, and a large userbase that can easily find my app! That sounds great. Sure, I could keep doing what I'm doing and put a download link on my site, and keep paying hosting bills for all of those downloads, and keep relying on word of mouth to attract new customers. Or, I can let Apple take care of it all as long as I give them a cut! But selling 100 copies of my app at $10 each is still a whole lot less than selling 10,000 copies at $7 each. Sounds great!

On top of that, I think users will love it: There's one central place to get just about any app I could want. Just open up a program, type in a few words and I get 12 different options, half of them free! And they stay up to date automatically? Awesome! It's just like Ubuntu's (or most linux distro's) repositories. Fun stuff.

I believe Apple when they say that the App Store will not be the ONLY way to install applications on OS X. However, I also believe them when they say it will be the best. Developers will all move over to the App Store and submit their apps, and users will all move over to the App Store to get their apps. The App Store will be the main way of installing things, and as far as most users are concerned, the only way.

Now, what happens when Apple doesn't want to put an app in the App Store? Sure, the developer can do it the same way we all do now, by putting a DMG up on their site and waiting for users to come to them. But if users are all hooked on the App Store, who's going to bother with that? You're going to have a hard time getting anyone interested in your app unless you're a big publisher like Adobe, Microsoft, Google or Valve. And even then, people will complain about your app and how it works weird and they'd rather just use that one app that's in the App Store that's only $4.99. In effect, the App Store will be the only viable distribution method for small to mid sized developers, whether Apple actually locks down OS X or not.

**tl;dr Everyone will love the app store and indie devs will have no other viable options.**

> We would've liked to see Apple do the right thing and remove these limits, but it looks like that's not going to happen. Apple has removed GNU Go from the App Store, **continuing their longstanding habit of preventing users from doing anything that Apple doesn't want them to do.**

> ...complying with the **FSF's demand** that they not allow users to use GPL'd software acquired through their software distribution system.
