### Post by Onyros on 2009-02-13
I've recently watched Gran Torino, The Curious Case of Benjamin Button, Slumdog Millionaire, Choke, Rock'n'Rolla, Religulous and Pineapple Express just for kicks.

Of those, my favourite was Benjamin Button, but I must say I enjoyed them all, probably apart from Choke which disappointed me somewhat. I was expecting something a little different, probably something more intense. Sam Rockwell, well... rocked. Anjelica Huston is Anjelica Huston, you can't go wrong there. I just expected more, especially because the book's so good.

Gran Torino won't please everyone, but it's masterful, apart from a few amateurish performances by first time big screen actors and actresses. If you want to take the movie seriously, though, try to avoid the final credits, if you don't want to laugh your butt off. That's because Clint Eastwood decided to sing "Gran Torino", and it's hilarious. It's incredibly anti-climatic, I don't know what the old man was thinking there.

Slumdog Millionaire... I love Danny Boyle. I liked the movie, but I also think it's a little overhyped. I'd still give it an 8 out of 10, which is pretty good by my standards.

Rock'n'Rolla has high entertainment levels, and a few classic performances, one particularly enlightened monologue by Toby Kebbell (look out for him, he'll make some "noise" in the future). Gerard Butler is one of those characters we just can't help but like and has a brilliant dancing scene with (hoot hoot) Thandie Newton.

I just don't like the setting-up-of-franchise thingy they've done there. Other than that, it's a typical Guy Ritchie movie. People who liked his previous efforts will like this one, but it won't overwhelm.

Religulous will also not please everyone. Personally, I gained an incredible respect for Bill Maher, especially due to the fact that he managed to dissect his views and religion in general without being abusive, and sometimes even garnering a few laughs from people with strong religious beliefs themselves.

As for Pineapple Express, it was kind of a guilty pleasure. Superbad changed my view of teen movies, and I'm a fan of Evan Goldberg and Seth Rogen, even though I think the latter always plays the same part.

I've seen others, but that'd be too much, this one's long as it is :P
