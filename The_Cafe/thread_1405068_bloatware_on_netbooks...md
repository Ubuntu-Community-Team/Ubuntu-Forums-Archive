### Post by xpod on 2010-02-12
Our girls Netbooks(MSI wind`s)came with Windows and there was a surprising lack of excess crud, even on that. It had the obligatory Norton 30 day(?) trialware and some kind of MS Office free trial but other than that not too much else if i remember correctly. Not sure i`d count the third party wireless app as bloatware but all that trialware junk and Norton, in any way shape or form are just silly on an average Netbook.

Those minor problems were solved on a temporary basis by removing Norton & the MS office trialware and then the rest of the unwanted(by them) software was dealt with a couple of weeks later when i eventually sat down with them and helped get *buntu on the things.
I encouraged them to keep Windows for the time being in case we had any hardware issues within the Warranty period as it would save a wee bit of hassle re-installing it if that time came. The 160G drive was already split 75%/25% so we left Windows on the 40G as was and stuck *buntu on the unused 120G`s.
Full blown Ubuntu installs probably carry more excess requirements to be perfectly honest so as far as our laptops & netbooks are concerned we just stick to our own preferred minimal installs with only the stuff we want.
