### Post by SemiExpert on 2012-03-26
> **Copper Bezel said:**
> [_ARM-powered Android netbooks_]("http://www.engadget.com/2010/06/21/toshibas-ac100-8-hour-smartbook-runs-android-2-1-on-a-1ghz-tegr/"), essentially. It's even less meaningful than the word *netbook* itself. I don't even know whether something like the ARM-based Transformer Prime would count as a smartbook, or if it has to be a non-touch-based, low-cost gadget. (Prime apparently runs Ubuntu now, though. That's cool.)

 The term Smartbook applies to ARM clamshell devices, not just Android.  As to whether or not an ARM Android tablet with a keyboard dock is a Smartbook is a matter that is open to interpretation.  The term Netbook seems to currently apply to 11.6 and smaller notebooks with Intel Atom and AMD Brazos (both C and E-series) processors.  Confusingly, if an 11.6 inch notebook has a Sandy Bridge processor, it's not a Netbook.  So a Thinkpad X120E with an AMD Fusion E-350 chip is a netbook, a Thinkpad X121E with an Intel Core i3-2357 CPU isn't a netbook.

As far as the Asus Transformer Prime, it's not a product that I'd buy, or advise buying, even if I could be confident of running Ubuntu without bricking it.  Not a big Asus fan, not all that confident about Asus products or Asus support.
