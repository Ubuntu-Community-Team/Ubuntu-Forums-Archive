### Post by Tuxoid on 2009-04-12
firstly, I am sorry if this is not in the correct section of the forums. I checked, and seems to be fine to put it here.

Anyway, a while back I had created a mock-up for a potential desktop environment (posted [[COLOR="Red"]here[/COLOR]]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=840734") for reference). As seen in the thread it had received some mix thoughts. Given the input from those who had commented, I made several revisions to the mock-up, focusing on a better sense of clarity. After about a year of revising, to ensure its ease-of-use, I am presenting my current revision in this thread for the sake of feedback.

I would like to know how well the community here understands the design - firstly - and secondly, I would like to know whether the community thinks this is a reasonably ergonomic and comfortable-looking design that they'd being comfortable using in many uses.

On an important side note, I am not a programmer. I am focused on designed, and regardless of the generous technical requirements of developing a desktop environment, I plan to focus on design prior to any code development. I will eventually develop code, but being a desktop environment, this will need to be designed first.

I am purposefully omitting any description of what each component is or does because that information should be obvious, if I have properly designed it that is. If any are confused about any of the components, I will, of course, revise those components.

I'm hoping this revision proves to be helpful in producing some feedback.

### Post by PacSci on 2009-04-12
It does seem intutive, but there are a few comments I have.

First of all, I was a bit confused about whether the penguins were icons or not. After inspecting closer, I saw that they were distro logos, but others might not catch on so quickly and try to double-click them.

Second, the font just doesn't quite seem to mesh with the rest of the design. You might want to select something a bit softer.

Third, though the launcher pizza in the middle has an obvious function (selecting applications), the pizza in the lower left is not very obvious. Is it a window switcher? That's what I originally thought, given its position, but considering that there is a globe icon with no visible window with a matching globe, that is out.

In the end, it seems like a very unique and innovative design, but it's not the kind of thing that I would want to work in every day - I'm too used to the traditional paradigms. Similarly, people who are already used to the Windows, GNOME, KDE, or Mac OS ways would probably have a hard time getting used to the idea. Maybe I'd pick up on it, but maybe not. There are probably people who would like it, though.

On another note - have you considered how hard it would be to get existing applications to "fit in" to the rest of the DE?

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-04-13
It's good that you're thinking of new ideas for Linux.

But from the looks of it, this is not something that I'd use or advise others to use. It looks like this whole desktop is built solely around making it "easy" to launch programs. There are already a dozen different interfaces for netbooks that make it "easy" to launch programs, but make it less convenient to access disks, shares, files and settings.

Launching programs on Linux is not difficult or inconvenient, especially if you use a dock.

I don't want you to become discouraged, but I think the idea needs a massive amount of reworking as it appears to be trying to make a trivially-easy task a little easier. The payoff is minimal for the investment of time.
