### Post by aljames2 on 2023-09-29
I was born at night, but not last night…

How is it a “service” to consumers when the deal is:  

1) Give them ownership of all your bookkeeping data + 
2) Pay them $500+ a year indefinitely +
3) No option to back up your data files to your computer +
4) BUT you get to “look at” your data as much as you want, as long as you keep paying the ransom.

I can maybe live with #2, but then rest is a deal breaker.

All accounting software solutions are now no longer “software solutions”, and instead are online cloud services.  This is what I have found.

CPA’s are getting incentives to keep clients in line with the technology, and to make their jobs easier.

I can’t think of a single thing one can do to release protection of sensitive personal information than to follow suit.

I am looking to migrate to a “non-cloudy” bookkeeping software.  The one I use today is non-cloudy, but is legacy & the mother ship has disabled certain features that are disruptive.

Suggestions or just chatter on the topic may be fun :)

### Post by ian-weisser on 2023-09-29
It's a negotiation:

When you find a tax accountant who...

1) You have a good working relationship with
2) Is willing to work with your strong desire for non-cloud bookkeeping software

...then you will likely have a narrow choice of software that they are familiar with.


There is nothing preventing the community from adding great bookkeeping features to existing software.
There is also nothing preventing the community from providing great support so those accountants are willing to recommend the software to their clients.
