### Post by original_jamingrit on 2007-12-15
My setup is usually four, first is a super user terminal and a web browser, second is pidgin and xchat, third is whatever I happen to be working on, text editors and terminals, and fourth is e-mail and torrent stuff.  Whenever I use gimp, or a word processing program, that usually occupies either screen 1 or 3, and games/fullscreen stuff normally occupy screen 1.  There's no paricular strategy to it, I just kind of arbitrarily choose a workspace to work on something, and then tend to choose the same workspace for that particular program.

Of course, you really don't have to use them, I find it easier to focus on one thing at a time; it's less distracting when all of the immediately important stuff are on one workspace and let everything else run in the background.
