### Post by fatality_uk on 2008-05-12
Fees for digital rights broadcasting have risen for Internet based radio stations. This increased dramatically about 2 years ago which forced many "internet stations" to close up shop.

While I like the idea, some background. The costs for running even a small show of just a few hours per day are about £40-50,000 per year. This is for an "off the shelf to air" package, which means that you receive a broadcast stream into which you have to insert your content, such as news, talk and announcements. This reduced the need to manage content but also means that you have little control over it. They might have Johnny Cash on one track and the next track is Def Leopard :D If you wanted to create your own playlist, you need about 3 or 4 agreements and each costs a lot. If you were to play **ANY** music, you would **HAVE** to have the license to play else you would be shut down so quick.

I think a podcast would be the way to go. Lets say you have a phone in, were it live and someone said something defamatory about, ohh er lets say Microsoft, you as the broadcaster **COULD** be held liable for the content. There are precedents for this.

Would love to see this happen but I don't think just yet there is a big enough market to make it viable. :( Which is sad :(
