### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-11-13
Maybe you can also start a petition to get hardware manufacturers to stick a Linux logo alongside the Win and Mac ones when they know a component works 'out of the box' in Linux. I send an email to this effect whenever I purchase something that works with Linux but there is no mention that it does anywhere on the merchandise, website or in the manual.

This perpetuates the myth that you need Windows or Mac to use that new DVD optical drive (because that's what's on the box) and there are no alternatives to following that trail when Linux users are aware that optical drives, pretty much all of 'em, work on anything ... it is actually in the manufacturers best business interests to include as many options for their hardware as possible, unless of course it is more lucrative to just not sell to or encourage a particular section of the market because of exclusivity rights promised.
