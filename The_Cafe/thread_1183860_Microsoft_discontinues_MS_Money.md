> Microsoft plans to stop selling Microsoft Money, its venerable, but not market-leading personal finance program, CNET News has learned.

The software maker has been notifying financial institutions and plans to announce the move to customers over the next 24 hours via a posting on its Web site and a notification in the software. Although Microsoft will stop selling the product in at the end of June, it plans to support the product through January 2011.

### Post by skillllllz on 2009-06-12
QIF files created by Quicken are flawed in that respect and sometimes cause that to occur. I've even seen this when importing from one version of Quicken to another (ie Q2004-->Q2006). It's not exactly Moneydance or GnuCash's fault.

I know that with Moneydance, you could try creating accounts with the same exact names as they appear in Quicken. Then export each account in Quicken to its own QIF file, and import them into Moneydance one-by-one. That should help a lot. Some of the balances might be incorrect, but this can be fixed by changing the "starting balance" under the account properties.
