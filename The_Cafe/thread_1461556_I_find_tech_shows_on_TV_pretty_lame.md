### Post by samalex on 2010-04-26
You're right, there isn't much on TV anymore for the computer geek, or at least not after TechTV was eaten-up by G4.  Now'days as many others I catch many of the TWiT podcasts and enjoy watching [TWiT Live]("http://live.twit.tv") when I can, plus I like the other Linux-based shows like Linux Link Tech Show and Linux Outlaws.  I used to like Geek News Central Podcast, but anymore it's like an hour advertisement for itself and its sponsors plus cheesy contests.

But in days past I have to say I was a HUGE fan of Computer Chronicles, which I watched for years, even before I got into computers as a kid.  But they still have the [archived shows on Archive.org]("http://www.archive.org/details/computerchronicles") which are great.  

Also MSNBC used to have [The Site]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Site") which was a weekly computer show that was amazing!  It was the precursor to ZDTV which became TechTV, plus for any Leo Laporte fans, Leo was DevNull, the animated co-host to Soledad O'Brien.

CNet used to also have a few weekly television shows that were pretty good, and they even had what I think was one of the first 'tech networks' though they tried to do it online and in the days when most people had dial-up.  It's still around, but honestly CNet could've been what TechTv became if they would've played their cards right.

Now'days I think the spin-offs from the TechTV guys have the best content, namely TWiT, Revision3, DL.TV, and others along with the various Linux-themed podcasts.

Sam
