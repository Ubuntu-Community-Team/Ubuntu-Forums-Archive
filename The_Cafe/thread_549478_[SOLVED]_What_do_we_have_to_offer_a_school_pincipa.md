### Post by bigbearomaha on 2007-09-12
I am part of a local group in Omaha,NE that accepts donated PC's, installs and configures a variety of Linux Distros on them and re-donates those to local small schools, community centers and students/families.

I don't know about other districts, but the schools here do indeed pay for Windows and there is often a discount, but not always.

So far we are working with two small catholic schools in the area and are implementing Linux servers to provide internet access to all the classrooms and other communications and education oriented apps to the staff and students.

The machines are all donated but sometimes the accessories such as cat5 and hubs/switches etc are hard to get donated so schools have to look for donations and grants to acquire them.

Linux is a great way to help local, small schools so long as there is someone in the community to help admin the setup.

Also, even though there is so much Linux can offer to these schools and community agencies, sometimes the principals and administrators are leery of it because they may have existing service contracts which may not support the introduction of Linux or often, they are just under-informed or misinformed about the issues, legal and otherwise revolving around Linux.

 I wish you luck helping the school out.  If there's anyway I can help, I am always around somewhere.  Here, FBK or Linux Geeks United.
