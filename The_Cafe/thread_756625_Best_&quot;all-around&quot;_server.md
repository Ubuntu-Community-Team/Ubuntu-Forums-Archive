### Post by toupeiro on 2008-04-16
Anyone who's been working in IT support for a while has had enough time to form a bias, for one reason or another for or against a particular hardware vendor.  Be it support, or failure rate, or cost, or performance, or any combination of the factors, Its a fun trivial (although sometimes heated) debate amongst IT professionals about which server is the best server.

I've always been a Proliant loyalist until recently, not because I've been let down by the proliant line, but I found a line I feel surpassed it in several areas.

My best "all around" server is the Sun X-series servers, particularly the X4200 and X4600 series for their scalability, performance and cost.

The 4600 offers a configuration no other self-contained server can by being able to scale up to an 8-way quad core configuration with 256GB of RAM in a 4U footprint.  They also have the concept of true power redundancy by giving you 4 power supplies and letting you configure them in a 2+2 failover.  4x 1GB Ethernet ports are standard equipment on both the 4200 (2U 2-way quad capable system) and the 4600, and even when scaling down the 4600 to a quad chip system, it still beats the Proliant DL585 4 way server by thousands of dollars, with the DL585 completely maxed out from a scalability standpoint.  Plus, if you've ever had to work with Sun support over the phone, it was by far the best support experience I've ever had, even in off-business hours.  Finally, its an X64 server, which means it can run any x86/x64 OS out there.  They are microsoft certified, VMWare certified, and Linux certified for multiple distributions.

So lets hear it.  Whats your favorite server, and why? :-)

edit: so this hopefully doesn't go over the top, I'm not talking about compute clusters like Crays, Altix's or multiple infiniband connected 1U's.  Self Contained  single chassis that are Rack Mountable or stand-alone :)
