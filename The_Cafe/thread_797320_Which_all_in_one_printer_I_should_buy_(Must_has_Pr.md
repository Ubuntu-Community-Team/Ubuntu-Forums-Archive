### Post by kevinatkins on 2008-05-17
I'd second the suggestion to go with HP - I use HP stuff exclusively, their HPLIP (HP Linux Imaging and Printing system) is designed to work with many of their printers, and also supports the 'All-In-One' functionality of printer-scanner-copier models. Do check before buying a specific model though - odds-on it will work, but check to make sure first.

Epson are pretty kind to Linux, too, so it might be worth examining what they have to offer.

One of the worst for Linux support is Lexmark (and Dell, too - they're rebranded Lexmarks) so avoid...

### Post by vrangforestillinger on 2008-05-17
I have an Epson RX560. Althought I think the RX560 costs more like $300, this might give you a clue on other Epson models:

Works in Linux. Printing out of the box with CUPS, scanning with SANE when installing libsane-extra from (Hardy) repositories. It seems Epson actually have hired a company (Avasys) to write linux drivers.

A big downside is that I think the black inkcartrigde is too small. And my feeling is that the cost-per-page (text) is quite high.

Edit: Copyfunction and printing from memory-sticks (photos) works independantly of the computer.
