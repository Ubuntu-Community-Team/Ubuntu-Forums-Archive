### Post by fabricator4 on 2011-06-26
> **doas777 said:**
> I have no idea of what you speak. since I have a decade and a half worth of experience with these devices, I feel like I;m missing out on something. care to elucidate?

It think this is the ferrite device on the data cable.  Normally, the cable runs though the Ferrite bead, or it might take a loop around it.  The function of this device is to act as a choke for low frequency noise on the cable that might interfere with the operation of the device.

Low frequency noise can get onto the cable because it or the device may act as an antenna for low frequency EMR.  Most modern devices do not need this as the internal electronics are designed sufficiently to filter this noise out.

Some computer stores sell (or used to sell) these ferrite beads so that you could add them to cables that were not already equipped with them.  Unless you have a specific problem cause by EMR then these devices are probably not particularly useful as an add-on device.

The bead is not normally obvious in factory fit cables, but looks like a plastic housing over the cable, towards one end or the other.  Those with no background in electronics would probably not give it a second thought.

Chris.
