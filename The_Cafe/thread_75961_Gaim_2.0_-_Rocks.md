### Post by Kimm on 2005-10-14
I just downloaded and installed Gaim 2.0 from cvs. Even though its still in developement its a great program. The new sound theme is not as "high-pitched" as the old one, and works realy well with the ubuntu system sounds.

Even though the GUI isnt all that different there are some things that have changed. The conversation window has changed and the... "status manager" or whatever you like to call it, is rather different.

The conversation window has smooth scrolling when you resieve a new message, not an important one, but it looks and feels good.

I noticed a small change is transfer speed, its not big, but its noticable, in the old versions I had an average speed of around 4 kb/s it not averages around 10 kb/s, as I said, still not fast, but its better.

### Post by UbuWu on 2005-10-14
Gaim 2.0 is going to be great! they benefitted a lot from the google summer of code. These are the highlights from their summer of code page:

> 
* Rendezvous Plugin will be in the next release of Gaim.
 UPnP NAT Traversal This should greatly improve file transfer success rates. Will be in the next release of Gaim.
* Gadu-Gadu Support will be in the next release of Gaim.
* SIP/SIMPLE We now have a working protocol plugin for doing IM over a SIP connection, compatable with kphone, iptel.org and sipgate.de. Will be in the next release of Gaim.
* ICQ File Transfer will be in the next release of Gaim. Some of his changes also made it into earlier releases of Gaim.
* Music Messaging Music Messaging is a Gaim plugin that allows collaborative musical score editing. Will be in the next release of Gaim.
* Doodle a "whiteboard" system for Gaim will be in the next release of Gaim.


See [http://gaim.sourceforge.net/summerofcode/](http://gaim.sourceforge.net/summerofcode/)

And on the news page:
> 
Gaim-vv

On a related note, the gaim-vv project—which aimed to offer a framework for voice and video support in Gaim—is being merged back into Gaim proper for hopeful incorporation into Gaim 2.0.0. This will be used to support Google Talk's voice as well as MSN and Yahoo! webcams.
