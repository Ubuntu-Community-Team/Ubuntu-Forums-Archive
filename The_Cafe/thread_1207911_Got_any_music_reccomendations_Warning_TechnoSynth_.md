### Post by BoyOfDestiny on 2009-07-09
Well, you should probably take a look at some of these c64 mixes (c64 came from Commodore, same folks that made the Amiga)
[http://remix.kwed.org/](http://remix.kwed.org/)
EDIT: This your style?
[http://remix.kwed.org/download.php/2624/DjUnz%20-%20Foreign%20Girl%20%28Platones%20McCheese%20Areolas%29.mp3](http://remix.kwed.org/download.php/2624/DjUnz%20-%20Foreign%20Girl%20%28Platones%20McCheese%20Areolas%29.mp3)

If you are demoscene fan you should probably know of [chiptunes]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiptunes"). There are plenty of rips, and homebrew tunes, in a plethora of formats across various platoforms.

First I'd recommend grabbing [audacious]("http://audacious-media-player.org/"), then at the very least installing [uade]("http://zakalwe.fi/uade/").

On the uade download page, they offer an rsync archive of Amiga chiptunes. (So it's just cut n paste, and you've got an instant archive that can easily be kept up to date, it's pretty great.)

Other formats that audacious supports include adlib (DOS), c64 (the wikipedia entry I linked has a link to the High Voltage SID archive), various Nintendo formats, look for 2A03.org or Famicompo... 

Well just have fun searching through all that, you won't find things more authentic when it comes to electronic.
