### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-07-15
And use Gmail's web interface every so often and mark them as spam/junk there so Gmail moves them to spam/junk as they arrive.

Also, spam filters may allow through mail from an address you have previously sent mail to.  So, if you've sent email for any reason to a business, including registering and verifying an email address, you will probably need to be sure to mark mail from them as spam.

### Post by bashiergui on 2014-07-15
Clicking on the "unsubscribe" link on an unsolicited email can land you on a malicious website. Never log in to a site that an email link takes you to.

Your address has apparently made it on the spammers list. Once on, there's no getting removed. I generally solve this problem by having an email address I use to sign up for mailing lists & websites. This one invariably gets mega spammed. I use a separate email Address for emailing friends and associates. That one typically remains spam free. ### Post by lisati on 2014-07-15
Others have mentioned logging into webmail every now and again and marking offending messages as spam. This helps the providers tune their filters to suit your preferences. I'm not sure about gmail, but I have encountered situations where doing so with Yahoo results in a compplaint being sent to the sender's provider.

I usually file a spam report via services such as Spamcop. They analyse the offending email, try to identify the true source of the email, and fire of complaints to the appropriate people. It also helps feed the monitoring system that feeds their blacklist.
