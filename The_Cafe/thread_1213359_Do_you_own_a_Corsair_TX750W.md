### Post by starcraft.man on 2009-07-14
No, I own a [1000HX series.]("http://ncix.com/products/index.php?sku=28797&vpn=CMPSU-1000HX&manufacture=CORSAIR") That said, from my experience of my Corsair PSUs I don't think you'll have any trouble, good quality. They are quiet enough IMO, much better than anything an OEM will ever ship but not without noise (when ya got music or something else going, unlikely to be noticeable).

I think it's solid. I haven't had any voltage issues and they are pretty reliable, even when operating near peak. If your gonna leave it on forever, just make sure your case has good air flow. Also would help if ya can get cheap electricity, that's another matter though.

### Post by gn2 on 2009-07-14
Corsair don't make PSUs.

Some of their previous models were made by Seasonic and were excellent.

The one you're looking at is not a Seasonic made unit, it's made by Channel Well Technology.

A quick Google reveals that it's not really a top-notch piece of kit.

If it were my money I wouldn't buy one.

If you're looking for reliable PSU information and well researched reviews, look at [SPCR]("http://www.silentpcreview.com/article699-page1.html").
