### Post by audiomick on 2010-02-04
Hi Psumi,
funnily enough, I think your chances of getting what you want are actually better with a budget price camera than with a name brand.

In a low price camera there is less likely to be a proprietary rechargeable battery pack, because the camera is cheaper to build without it.

Likewise, there is less likely to be any whizz-bang proprietary software / memory access solutions, because, once again, the camera is cheaper to build without them.

You will, of course, have to live with the lower picture quality and that the camera has a lot more trouble with difficult lighting situations than a more expensive one.

If you find that the camera doesn't talk to Linux, the solution is to simply get an appropriate card reader, if your computer doesn't already have one, take the card out of the camera and read it out with the card reader. My Pentax K10D works perfectly well with my Ubuntu computer, but I prefer using the card reader to transfer the pictures. I think it is a bit faster, and some books that I have say that a card reader may be more reliable.
