### Post by MikeTheC on 2009-07-06
This forum and a good friend of mine who is not a member here often sound the same... Droning on and on about how Apple is evil, how they're destroying the computer world, trying to control and end user choice, blah blah blah...

In the first place, when have you seen such a widely supported and developed-for platform as the iPhone? When in the history of cell phones have you seen the degree of independence from carrier control that the iPhone has brought? For that matter, when you've got 8 and 10 year old kids who are able to make money writing software for the iPhone -- which oddly enough somehow manages to get approved despite Apple's awful app censorship process -- how is it that Apple is doing harm to us? (That is, the general public at large) ? For that matter, given the nature of the iPhone, it's caused this rising tide which, as the expression goes, floats all boats. Look at the choice of smart phones out there now which didn't exist before.

I own a Palm Pre. Do you honestly think Palm would have gotten anywhere without Apple having made some substantial headway? How about Google and their Android product? Both of these have benefitted from Apple's leadership and spearheading this level of refinement and feature capabilities on the ultra mobile (ergo smart phone) platform. Nobody else -- with the possible exception of the Blackberry -- has done this.

Believe me, I'm all for choice and competition. These two things keep everyone -- including Apple -- honest and very rightly so. No, I don't think Apple would have done some of the things they've done which are benefits to users without a healthy sense of competition. But that being said, to try and paint this as Apple having done nothing for us... that's a load of crap.

At the end of the day, as GeekyGirl and Swarms have said, if the iPhone isn't the product for you, by all means take your business elsewhere. That's the whole point of competition. Quit b*tching on UF and go actively *DO* something about the situation.
