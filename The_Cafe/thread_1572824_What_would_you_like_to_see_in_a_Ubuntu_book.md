### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-09-11
I honestly think that if we have an interactive tour, as is being worked on, as well as the Ubuntu Manual Project for people who want to dig a bit deeper, that's good enough for the majority of people.

Compared to the OS X and Windows documentation, we've got a really good start. The main issue here is that you can't explain a lot to people in dumbed down language, as they won't be the kind of people looking for that information anyway. Either a simple tour that points people to stuff and makes it all obvious, or a simple guide.

So if I were to write an Ubuntu book, I would make it in between these two. Use basic English and screenshots that make it very click where to go to do certain things. I would like to keep it under 20 pages long and explain just what people need to get a good start with Ubuntu 10.10. As dead simple as possible without actually being a tour.

The thing is, most people are fine with the language and terminology used in the Ubuntu Manual Project. People are far less stupid than we expect them to be, even in America.
