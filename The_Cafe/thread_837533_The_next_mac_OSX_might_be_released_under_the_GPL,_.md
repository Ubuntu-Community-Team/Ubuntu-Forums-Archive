### Post by FranMichaels on 2008-06-22
> **Freddy said:**
> Doesn't Mac OSX use a kernel licensed with the BSD license? If so, they can't release it under GPL even if they wanted to, GPL restricts to much, BSD is free as in total freedom.

You are confused, it's the other way around.

BSD is GPL-compatible. Meaning you can take BSD code and make it GPL. You can take BSD code and make it proprietary as companies like Apple and Microsoft have done. It is in this sense that BSD is "freer". Use it for whatever, open source or closed source, no requirement to give any sourcecode back, or release the sourcecode.

Now the GPL, you cannot take it and make it BSD licensed or put in a product without the [4 freedoms]("http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html") being there.
This is why some Microsoft fans (and Microsoft itself so to speak) and BSD license fans say the GPL is restrictive/viral. It restricts others from taking those 4 freedoms away.

[http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-faq.html](http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-faq.html)
[http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.html](http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.html)

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BSD_licenses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BSD_licenses)

### Post by Rhapsody on 2008-06-22
Well, joke or not, Apple probably could do this. Stuff under the BSD license would stay under it, but they could GPL the proprietary stuff and the whole package would only be distributable under the GPL. Of course this only applies to the three or two-clause BSD license, anything under the four-clause BSD license would have to be relicensed or replaced.

Also, if Apple actually wanted to try for world domination, this would probably be the only route. The mature, stable Mac OS X could make one hell of a competitor to Windows.

But they won't do it. Apple make most of their money off hardware, and sacrificing the Apple lock-in for an expansion attempt like this would be madness, Apple would have no way of knowing if they could make up the lost revenue from their new sources. They'd lose control of Mac OS X too. Support requests would skyrocket as people use the OS on non-approved hardware, and they couldn't ensure that people would be paying for their OS either, as free forks could (and would) be made.
