### Post by issih on 2008-06-12
Google for motherboard bundles returned a fair few results, for example.

[http://www.novatech.co.uk/novatech/bundles.html](http://www.novatech.co.uk/novatech/bundles.html)

Not saying you should buy from any of the people I've suggested. In the past I've only posted ones I have used and found reliable, but everyones experience will vary. Most important, look around at a few sites to get a feel for how cheap (or not) something is. Some websites (and nearly all retail stores) take the proverbial **** when it comes to the price they ask for components.

If you do opt to go this route, a motherboard bundle sounds like a good bet. Unless you keep fairly well up on your knowledge of hardware you can get caught out in terms of the type of bus and speed needed for the memory and indeed the compatability of the processor with the motherboard etc. Getting the board processor and memory together will take most of the pain away. After that Just make sure you buy sata drives if all you have is sata connectors, and ensure your graphics card matches the slot (almost bound to want pci express now - frankly you shouldn't buy a board with agp on now).

After that get a case, take some precautions to avoid killing anything with static, read a couple of guides about what to do and plug it all together. Honestly its very very easy :) its basically grown up lego, and we all like lego don't we.
