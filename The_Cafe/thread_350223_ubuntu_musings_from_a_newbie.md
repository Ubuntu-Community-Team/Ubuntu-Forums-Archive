### Post by Brunellus on 2007-01-31
The first step to wisdom is this:  admitting that you dont' know.

In migrating from one OS to another, the biggest problem is simply not knowing how things are done in the new OS.  The biggest mistake is simply assuming that your experience in one carries over to the other.  Most of the time, that's simply not true.

Having looked over your post history, I can say that you're not atypical as far as new users go.  I was the same once.  The difference was that I either had to make Linux run as my primary OS or have no computer at all.  Working without a net was a great motivator!

The "standard" reference for progam installation is [how to install anything in Ubuntu](http://cutlersoftware.com/ubuntuinstall/).  Alternatively, the relevant wiki page:  [InstallingSoftware](http://help.ubuntu.com/community/InstallingSoftware).   Those pages should cover all but the most specialized needs.
