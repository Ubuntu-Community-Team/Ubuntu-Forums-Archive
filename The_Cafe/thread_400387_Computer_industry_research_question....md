### Post by Bigbluecat on 2007-04-03
If you look at the brand name PC sellers then the "lack of competition" as you call it is less about technology innovation than it is about logistics, brand and supply chain management.

The underlying technology is available to all at roughly the same time. OK large customers may get some advanced access. But Intel, AMD, ATI, nVidia, Broadcom, displays, hard drives - all the components of a PC are available to all.

But still there are only a few major PC sellers. This is because they have created a brand and customer base, very low cost of manufacture, large buying power and efficient logistics control.

Now as an interesting point around 80% of the worlds laptops are manufactured by 4 Taiwanese companies - Compal Computer, Quanta, Inventec and Asus. They do a lot of the design work also.

So when considering your question you may have to look beyond technology to the commercial aspects of why there are only a few large PC manufacturers.
