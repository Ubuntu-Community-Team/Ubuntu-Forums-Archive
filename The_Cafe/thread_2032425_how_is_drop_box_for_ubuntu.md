### Post by Dragonbite on 2012-07-24
Dropbox works great.  I have it on my desktop and laptop (both running Ubuntu).

When I try a different distro, or switch to Windows or do a clean update love the benefit Sync-over-LAN provides.

On the new/refreshed laptop system I can install dropbox and put in my credentials. At this point I make sure that my desktop, also with Dropbox, is on and logged in.

So when the new dropbox connects to the cloud server, the server tells the laptop to get the list of files from the desktop computer on the same LAN.

This makes my 1.5 +/- GB of files take 20-30 minutes instead of 4-6 hours if I had to download it over the internet!

I just wish additional space was cheaper (like $0.50-$0.60 per GB per year as SkyDrive and Google Drive cost).

I use Dropbox for it's cross-platform (Linux & Windows for me) and cross-distro (Ubuntu, Fedora, openSUSE, etc.) and rapid Sync-over-LAN.
