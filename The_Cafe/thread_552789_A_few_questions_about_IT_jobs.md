### Post by koenn on 2007-09-17
I'd recommend some sort of degree, Bachelor level, in computer science or IT or whatever has enough subjects that interest you. 
The advantage over certifications is that you get a broader view. You may not be a fully qualified Oracle dba but you'll have a working knowledge of rdbms'es and SQL, enough to deal with simple database issues and learn the rest as you go. You won't have enough programming experience to land a programmer job fresh out of school, but at least you'll understand the basics of programming and be able to  have a sensible discussion with programmers. You may not have learned how to configure a Cisco router blindfolded, but you'll have enough background to be able to design and implement a small network, and pick up the rest as you go, if network administration should turn out the way you want to go. And so on. 

As for jobs, consider governement jobs (any level). At least in Belgium, they often don't require formal degrees, instead they organise their own exams to test your knowledge and skills, and if you pass, you qualify for the job, regardless of whether you learned your stuff at school, or at home from a book.  
If it's the same where you live, it certainly is an option, if not as your ultimate career then at least to get you some experience in the field.

### Post by thatswhatshesaid on 2007-09-17
I was/am in a similar situation.  I went to college for a degree in Computer Science (and had and IT internship/job on the side during summer/winter breaks).  I made it though 4 years of school, getting 3 years of credits (infamous party school - my fault not theres though).  I ended up leaving school and signing on as a full time hire at the place I had my internship (I had experience -- but no degree).

Life happened, I got married, had a kid.  Two years later the company decided to merge us into an outsourcing company.  Everyone got nervous and the fact that I had experience but no certifications or degree was a big concern.  I ended up getting an entry level QA job on my technical background (where I am now) but now I still need to get a degree and going back to school fulltime isn't an option being the breadwinner of the family.

I didn't see anyone else mention this: when I was in IT just because you had an MCSE didn't mean you knew what you were doing.  Two guys I worked with that had the cert. knew there stuff inside and out, the other 3 guys got theirs in a boot camp where everyone that went passed (no matter how incompetent they were).  Because of things like this, not everyone places a lot of faith in certifications (including my former tech-savvy bosses).

My advice: if you can, go back to school full-time or part-time, and get the degree.  Even if you have a bunch of certifications, you never know when you might want to switch careers and if you do, at least you'll have a degree to fall back on.  Anything is better than nothing when it comes down to it though.  

// my 2 cents
