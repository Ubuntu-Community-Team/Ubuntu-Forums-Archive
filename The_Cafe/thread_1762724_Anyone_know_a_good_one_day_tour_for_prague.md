### Post by linuxforartists on 2011-05-21
If you stay in a youth hostel, you can ask the reception about tours.  You'll meet other backpackers too, which is nice.  You could probably even get away with staying in a hotel, then popping into a hostel to book one of their tours.  Should be cheaper than what hotels offer.

The link odiseo77 posted is a good list of places to start with.  The Charles Bridge, the castle, the main squares are all excellent.  Then you could just wander around and stumble onto more cool stuff.  I enjoyed the maze-like Ledebour Garden, which I hadn't planned on seeing.  Reminded me of those M.C. Escher drawings of endless stairs.

If you want my personal perspective, here's a link to my travel blog, from when I went there: [Marcus Goes Global - Prague]("http://www.marcusgoesglobal.com/2004/04/marcus-meets-europe-czech-republic.html").  My photos really don't do justice to the city, it's one of the most beautiful in the world.  Like a "greatest hits" of architectural styles.

I got the chance to backpack around Europe years ago, when I was studying abroad in England.  Great times.
