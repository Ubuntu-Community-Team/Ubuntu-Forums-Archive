### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-10-20
This is just to be silly.

Last time I upgraded from Dapper to Feisty, the update manager didn't go so well, and I had to do a full install of Feisty.

This time, I'm giving the Update Manager another shot, to see if it wigs out or not.

So, just to be silly, I decided to keep using my comp while it was upgrading.  So far, I've played about an hours worth of Tremulous, I surfed the net for a bit (until my Macromedia Flash driver's got disabled due to restricted drivers *pout* ... guess I have to reinitialize restricted drivers after each upgrade?)  And now I'm posting on the forums while it's updating packages (sorry, "Installing the Updates" ... about 18 mins to go).

Just thought I'd be silly, and see what others were doing while they're comp upgraded.

Actually, it would be a two-part question...

1) Did you clean-install, or Update Manager upgrade?

2) If upgrade, did you keep using your comp while doing so, and if so, what did you do?

I think of all the things I've thought were cool while using Linux...being able to use my comp while it's doing a full upgrade ranks pretty high up there.

So far, so good, but one beef I have with it is that it disabled WICD, which I'm using for my wireless card.  It'd be nice if it would retain the 3rd party apps and Restricted Drivers during the upgrade.

EDIT: 

So the update / upgrade to Gutsy went off fairly smoothly.  

* It re-initialized my restricted drivers (so Macromedia Flash works again).

* It got rid of WICD, which left me w/o wireless.  I was initially annoyed by this.  I did "iwconfig" in terminal, and it was recognizing my card, though.  So, I went to "System > Admin > Network", and noticed it had already found my wireless card there, filled in the pertinent info (network name/password).  All I had to do was switch it from WEP to WPA for it to find my network.  And *ta-da*, 1 minute later I'm back online!  Awesome!

* I'm using Tilda (quake-style terminal dropdown), and I noticed after the first time you drop down its terminal, it shows up screwed up from then on (the text doesn't display).  I'm not sure what's causing that.  I'm just gonna reinstall Tilda, and hopefully that will take care of it.

Anyways, just letting you know, I screwed around on my comp the whole time it was upgrading to Gutsy Gibbon, and it upgraded just fine... a lot smoother then when I tried it from Dapper to Feisty.  Two huge thumbs up to all the Devs and folks who worked on this!  Ubuntu keeps getting better and better!
