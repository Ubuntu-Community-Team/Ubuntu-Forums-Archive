### Post by falconindy on 2010-02-25
Hi there. You seem to be unfamiliar with Reg CC, aka the statutes governing your institution's Funds Availability policy. Perhaps you should pick up one of the pamphlets that are required to be available in each branch next time you visit.

Moreover, just because **your** account is in good standing does **not** guarantee that the account that the check was drawn off of is in good standing as well. You should be thankful that they hold the money. If the check were to bounce and you already spent it all, you'd find yourself with a number of NSF fees and risk closure of the account (which will happily appear on your credit report).

Banking is a business of calculated risk, and holding checks for a **reasonable** period of time is part of what goes into reducing risk by an extremely high margin.
