### Post by The_Pirate_King on 2010-01-12
One of the things that I recently discovered I was able to do with FF3.5 is type in "wiki John Hancock" or "youtube rick astley", and often the highest rated Google page would be pulled up, and it would be the wikipedia article on John Hancock or a youtube video with Rick Astley. 
But this wouldn't happen all the time, and it relies on google's "I'm Feeling Lucky" method to bring you to the right page. 

Why not take it to the next level? 

It would be easy enough to create a script that would allow you to type "wiki something" into the address bar and get [the wikipedia result for "something"]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Something").  The same goes for any site with a working search function.  
You could even make it really advanced by allowing users to create their own shortcuts.  There could be a menu, with text fields "URL of Site" and "shortcut".  Put in "http://www.mysite.com" and "mysite", and whenever you type "mysite something" into the address bar, you'll get the search results for "something" on "mysite".  I suppose there might be some difficulty creating custom searches, but firefox could easily enough pipe your search into wikipedia, youtube, twitter, or any search engine of your choosing. 

Just an idea... if there's already an addon like this, tell me...I'd like to use it.

### Post by Xbehave on 2010-01-12
Firefox has had this since forever, they are called keywords:
1) right click in any searchbox and select add keyword for this search
2) edit any bookmark and add a %s where the search term goes
3) there is a way to edit userchrome.css to show keywords under tags in the add bookmark dialog
