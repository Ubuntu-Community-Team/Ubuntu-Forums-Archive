### Post by ntowakbh on 2008-03-10
Going by the question "Do we really need computers?" Yes and no. Originally, computers didn't exist, so we obviously got along just fine without them.  Now that is a different story, because nearly everything is controlled, in some way, no matter how indirect, computers.  Packaging for nearly any food? Printers.  Cash registers, etc. So now, because it has become so common place, it would be impossible to remove technology from our present day world.  

So do we need them? Last I checked, the basic needs for a human to sustain life are: food, water, clothing, and shelter.  I don't see computers there in that list.  So in a short answer no.  However, this response only applies if you take the word "need" in the literal sense, as I have done. Do we want computers?  The vast majority of people do.

### Post by original_jamingrit on 2008-03-10
There are some people that need them more than others.  I don't have any hard statistics handy, but I bet if you took a handful of people, at least half of them wouldn't mind going on a week-long vacation without a computer or Internet access.  I also bet that in large enough group of people, a small percentage of them would have been much happier with life if they were living in a different time-period without computers.

So I guess it depends on what you mean by need.  If you mean "need" as in you need them to get by in this world and do whatever your current occupations and/or hobbies are, most people would say yes.  If you mean "need" as in couldn't live a satisfying life without one, most people would say no.
