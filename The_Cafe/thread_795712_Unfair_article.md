### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-05-15
Oh, I remember this. Here, you just need to look at it [from another perspective](http://dylanmccall.blogspot.com/2008/01/ubuntu-linux-606-red-hat-enterprise.html).
;)

Interesting timing to resurrect this. There is much talk about that SSL fiasco being a black eye for Debian. I agree in that it's a sign of something in need of change (I suggest a new kind of web-based discussion system specific for code / usability review), but I think the handling of that situation was pretty remarkable. The day I learned about it, a patch made it downstream in Ubuntu to fix the issue. The timing was rather amusing, too: The day after Patch Tuesday. That gives us a sharp contrast to what would happen if such an issue was divulged at the same time on a Microsoft system: Users would be waiting a full month before the automatic update came through.
Furthermore, the problem would have probably been discovered via a working exploit of compiled code by people with nasty intentions. In our case, it was discovered from the source, without the need to create an exploit; the possibility of it could be logically determined in a tidy, nondestructive way.
