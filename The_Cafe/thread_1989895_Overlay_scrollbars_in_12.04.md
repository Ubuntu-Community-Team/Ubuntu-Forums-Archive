### Post by vasa1 on 2012-05-29
They still seem to be a novelty to many users. Quite often, mention of overlay scrollbars involves removing or disabling them. I removed them in 11.04 and in 11.10 but have kept them in 12.04 and now like them a lot.

It's not that much about freeing up "real estate"; they just are very, very convenient once one gets familiar with them.

I couldn't find a video on the subject on YouTube. It would be nice if someone would put up a video (with voice) showing how they work (even when there's a lot of scrolling involved). They even work in an Xfce session. (I know because I installed Xfce 4.10 onto "vanilla" Ubuntu 12.04.)

I'm looking forward to when things like Firefox, Chrome, and LibreOffice will be able to use the overlay scrollbars.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-05-29
I still can't quite get used to them (because I've been using normal scrollbars since 1991?) but I do like them. Being able to catch the scrollbox anywhere on the edge of the window is convenient. Also, on a laptop with a trackpad, if you are scrolling slowly through a document you can just "click, click, click" on the down arrow; and then if you need to go up you just move the cursor an inch upwards and click.

I suspect they could be implemented in a way that feels more natural to existing computer users, but I do like them.
