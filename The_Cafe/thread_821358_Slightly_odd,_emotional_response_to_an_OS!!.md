### Post by fatality_uk on 2008-06-07
I have seen a few posts over the last few weeks that caught my attention. Many are in the usual stream. "I am leaving Ubuntu, but this isn't a troll thread".

It seems from reading these threads that a mindset has built up in these users denoting a slightly deeper emotional attachment to Ubuntu than perhaps is warranted for an Operating System?

I have seen posts that almost read as though when people even try another operating system, MacOS, Vista or another distro, there seems to be a "guilt" switch enabled somewhere in the subconscious. 

I am guilty of this as well. I spoke to Microsoft Platinum/Gold partner (Can't remember which type) who is looking to supply us with an new ERP system and during that conversation, I heard myself defending Linux probably more than was rationally necessary.

It's not logical!!! I don't give such an emotional response to my headphones, my monitor, dishwasher or DVR!! Not even to applications that I really enjoy using, Inkscape, Assault Cube, Urban Terror.

Why Ubuntu?

Having tried many distros over the last 6-7 years, none quite gives as much of an emotional response as Ubuntu! I think the marketing people call it something silly like "stickiness". The ability of a product/service to quickly build a loyal customer base.

Any thoughts as to what it is about Ubuntu, of all the distros, that has the x-factor that leads to these responses?
