### Post by funkatron on 2009-11-30
> **BenAshton24 said:**
> I don't have twitter or know much about it but "Quit" looks good.

Also "Spaz"? Not a very P/C name is it, I'm all for screwing the system but tht is just offensive :P

"Gimp" anyone?

Regardless, the usage of "spaz" as an epithet towards those with cerebral palsy was common in the 1980s UK, but wasn't common outside of that area, and it is even less common now. I certainly didn't intend it as such when I chose the name, and I believe that common usage on Twitter and beyond doesn't intend that either. I do monitor people posting about "spaz," and while it gets used a lot (which makes finding the posts about my app a bit of a pain), I've never seen it used as anything more than a playful jab.

My use of "spaz" is very much a reference to Twitter and other microblogging services' tendency to inundate one with information, and how that can make us a bit ADD. It's also in reference to the nerdy, geeky, "spazzy" characters of 1980s US teenage comedies like Meatballs (which has a character named "Spaz"). I'm proud to be quite nerdy, geeky, and somewhat hyper myself.
