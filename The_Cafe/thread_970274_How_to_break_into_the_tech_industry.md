### Post by handy on 2008-11-04
I advertised in the local paper, the paper had a policy of giving new businesses an interview & photo (half page) for free.  I wrote my own interview. 

The weekly advertisements were in the form of a computer hints & tips column, which was half the price of the same space if it were a pure advertisement.

Do good work & word of mouth is your best advertising.  I only advertised for the first 3 months of my business which ran for over 10 years.  I operated out of a converted bedroom/office in my house with its own phone line & answering machine.  I have never owned a mobile, I'm sure that is what others like (personally I don't like to be interrupted by a phone call).

Be careful how you charge people;  private customers who are poor you be kind to, business customers you charge for all of your time.

Initially have a very competitive (low) hourly rate, as this builds your client base quicker.  Put your price up a little every year (if the financial climate can support it).  Many small price increases are far more palatable than big jumps.

The first year is usually the toughest.

All the best. :-)
