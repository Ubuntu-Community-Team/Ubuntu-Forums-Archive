### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-04-24
Apple writes products for Windows to reach out to a large user base, and potential convince windows users that Apple products are better than Windows.

Microsoft writes products for Macs to continue to make a profit off of people who had left, and possibly get them to come back.

There's not much profit to be made writing programs for Linux. Linux users have typically made a decision about their operating system, and Apple isn't going to be able to win them over. Microsoft doesn't want to actively help Linux become a viable alternative to WIndows.

There simply isn't much of a market for proprietary applications on Linux.
