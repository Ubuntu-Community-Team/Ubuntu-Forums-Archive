### Post by blueshiftoverwatch on 2010-01-13
It seems like there aren't enough hours in the day much of the time. I just sat down and started to mess with some PHP in Netbeans and it's already time for bed.

Does anyone think that at some point in the future people won't need to sleep or will need to sleep much less than we do now. Without having to transfer our consciousness (if it's even possible) to a computer?

Nobody knows what exactly the brain is doing during sleep, or more specifically REM sleep. But do you think it's possible that a drug will be made that negates the need to sleep? The way I see it, the Human brain is awake for about 16 hours and than needs 8 hours to recharge at night. During those 8 hours (assuming your not waking up) your lying there not doing anything. What if a drug were made that instead of needing to devote 100% of your time for 8 hours to sleeping that your brain was constantly in a state of recharging it's self? Letting you stay awake indefinitely.

The downside to that would be that because your brain was busy working to solve every day problems AND recharging it's self it would probably operate at a reduced capacity, probably around 66%. Because during the normal Human sleep schedule 33% of your time is spent doing nothing, but with the use of these possible drugs that 33% would be spread out over a whole day.

Do you think it would be a good trade-off to let your brain only operate at 66% of the capacity it would if you had a normal sleep schedule but never have to sleep again? Of course, if you needed to do some really intense stuff you could always stop using the drug and return to a normal sleep schedule. But if you could, can you imagine yourself doing it on a regular basis or most of the time?
