### Post by drawkcab on 2009-09-30
My hypothesis is that Microsoft is trying to create a win/win scenerio by limiting features on basic ed.  From their angle, the problem is that they can't expect to take as big of a cut from each netbook sale because the price is so much lower.  For example, if you are selling a $1300 laptop, the windows tax of $100 or so makes sense.  But making $100 on top of a $270 netbook isn't going to fly.

So one option is to try make the netbook craze go away by offering a crippled OS that frustrates users (they've done the marketing research and know that not being able to switch the background will frustrate some 80% of windows users).  If the netbook market collapses as a result, that will work in their favor.

More plausible is the second scenerio where they will equip netbooks with basic for a reasonable fee and then, as users get frustrated, they will command a nice upgrade fee to make up the money they would have otherwise made.  So you pay the $50 windows tax when you buy and then another $50 to upgrade.
