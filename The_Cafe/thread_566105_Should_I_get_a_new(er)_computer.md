### Post by Zero Prime on 2007-10-03
I see no reason for a (new) computer.  My E-Machine fried a few weeks ago.  I could have bought a brand new PC, but instead picked up a new mobo, power supply, and video card.  I already had 2 hard drives in my old PC.  The RAM and Soundblaster Live were still good as well as the AMD Sempron 3000+.  It cost less than $100 to give a nice upgrade to my system and even though I still use the same stick of RAM and CPU the system runs loads better.  If I decide that one day I want or need a newer system I'll just install another new mobo and CPU.  I'll have no choice but to upgrade the RAM then, but I'll cross that bridge when I get to it.

One other thing to note.  Upgrading a system is very easy and cost effective.  You don't need the latest hardware.  1 or 2 generation old hardware flies with Linux.  And while a generation sounds like a long time it's only about 3 to 6 months for computer hardware and as the latest and greatest come out, the slightly older drop drastically in price.
