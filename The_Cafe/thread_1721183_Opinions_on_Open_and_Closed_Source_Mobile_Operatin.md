Glad I could help you out. I've tried to answer as accurately
 as I could, but honestly I have no idea which OS a Nokia 1200
 with a monochrome screen runs. Perhaps I'm a bit different
 to most when it comes to cell phones, but for me the main 
selling point for a cell phone, is that it is *no* smart-phone at
 all. Basically I just want to be able to make phone calls and 
send SMS. The reason for this is that most cell phones nowadays
 actually are mini-computers loaded with plenty of distractions,
 from access to social networking, to games, built in cameras,
 internet access, etc. I'd really like less distractions, as I think
 my life is hectic enough as it is! A smart-phone would be a 
constant source of distraction as your likely to bring your cell
 phone with you at all times.  
 
 But sure, it would delight me if my old Nokia 1200 ran an open
 source OS, however I have my  doubts about that ;)
 
 A final couple of thoughts:
 I know that old people often struggle to use even simple cell 
phones. Age is very relevant to this, as I'm sure you're aware of.
 I don't think there is, or should be, a one size fits all cell-phone.
 A final thing to consider is that the cell-phone market is rapidly 
growing in the developing world. When defining the typical consumers,
 remember that in a couple of years, the typical consumer isn't
 going to be a wealthy European or American, but a person living 
in Kenya, India or Bangladesh.
