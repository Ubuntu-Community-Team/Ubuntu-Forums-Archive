That's about right.

It is against UF rules to post for homework assignments. But since you were honest and didn't try to disguise it, I feel compelled to give you a few pointers (without actually doing the work for you, as this is actually an easy assignment).

-The dependent variable will (as it's name indicates) always depend on the independent one. In your case the price is dependent on what day it is.
-You could easily guess every value of the dependent variable, but put some more effort and write a function (equation) that for every value of day(x) calculates price(y). Make sure the function takes a value of day in order to produce a value of price, if it doesn't take any value of day it's probably not right. Think carefully how price changes in relation to day, know that on day 0 your function must return 20 as the answer.
-Imagine how the graph will look before you draw it, will it be a straight line? a horizontal, vertical or diagonal? a parabola? to what side will it lean? will it start from the origin (0) of the graph? compare your expected output to the facts you already know and see if they fall into the same logic.

It may also be a good idea to talk to your teacher (or to a counselor, or ultimately your parents), explain to him that you and some other friends cannot understand the assignments, if your teacher doesn't get feedback then there's no foreseeable way he'll improve his teaching.
