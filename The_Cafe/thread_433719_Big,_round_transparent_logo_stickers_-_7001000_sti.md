### Post by Jenda on 2007-06-25
And a quick thought on why I sell big packs at a loss... best shown on examples.
When somebody orders a pack of 200 stickers, it means the total amount of stickers bought can be higher by 200. For example, that person increases the entire batch from 800 to 1000, assuming 800 will be sold to other people.
If I wanted to buy just 800, it would likely cost $550. 1000 stickers cost me $600.
So I can either turn down this person who wants to buy 200 stickers for $110, because they cost me $120, and buy only 800 stickers. Or I can take his order, and buy 1000.
If I turn them down, I'll have 800 stickers and be $550 in the red. If I take it, i'll also have 800 stickers, but only be $500 in the red, because I paid $600 for the stickers and got $100 from the guy who got 200.
This way, he saved me $50 by buying stickers I lose money on :) It's a win-win.
