### Post by samjh on 2007-07-10
A lot of indie game programmers who aim for multi-platform markets tend to use multi-platform programming languages.  The most common language is Java at the moment.  This tends to rule out resource-intensive games with high-fidelity graphics that sell appeal to the mass market and sell large volumes of copies.  Although some Java games are almost AAA-class, they are not quite there yet.

But for an indie developer creating a AAA-class resource-hungry game, such as something along the lines of Half Life 2, the cost and man-hours of porting to multiple platforms may understandably be prohibitive.  Highly optimised code such as those found in AAA titles tend to be platform-specific, and efforts to port them across platforms are usually non-trivial.  Definitely not the kind of task a small indie development team with maybe half-dozen programmers would want to do without significant promise of profit.

I think that for the time being, native Linux games from the mainstream AAA market are only likely to come from big-name developers and publishers.
