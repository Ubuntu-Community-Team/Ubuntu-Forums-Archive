### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-10-15
The GUI they propose WOULD be fairly quick to implement, though, relative to most other crazy ideas. I'm impressed by how practical it is :)
(Although the GUI toolkit / included applications need to be all about single window interfaces, maybe with dialogs rolling down from the top like MacOS does).

Heck, Linux even has stable multi-pointer support now. That doesn't necessarily mean multitouch, but with their approach (which specifically does fingers) MPX should be perfect.

I am not a fan of their insanely complex gestures, though. Personally, I think present multitouch interface design fails once designers start trying to make use of those extra fingers. It should be fluid and natural, so arbitrarily saying "5 fingers means you're pulling away the workspace" does not fit!
A better approach would be if the Global area of the touch pad could used to control zooming.
