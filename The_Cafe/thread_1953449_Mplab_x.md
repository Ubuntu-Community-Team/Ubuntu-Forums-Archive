### Post by lz1dsb on 2012-04-06
It's old news anyway but some of you might be interested. For quite some time Microchip is providing their new IDE under Linux/Windows/OS X
[http://www.microchip.com/pagehandler/en-us/family/mplabx/](http://www.microchip.com/pagehandler/en-us/family/mplabx/)
 
I think that it's great that they give you the freedom to develop under the OS of your choice. And they're even moving to a new compiler which will also be available for different OSs:
[http://www.microchip.com/pagehandler/en_us/promo/mplabxc/](http://www.microchip.com/pagehandler/en_us/promo/mplabxc/)
 
And also when you think about it, when you runn the IDE (which is free) on a linux distro, it's perfectly legal without spending any buck for licences ;)
 
I think it's about time to get my old programmer out of the closet and get back to my old hobby which gave me so much pleasure in the past... And now I should be able to programm and develop the firmware natively under Linux, without any tweeks or VMs, or whatever :guitar:
Maybe the Arduino Project has paved the way for this to happen...
