### Post by matthew.ball on 2009-10-29
If you're interested in earning money, go the route of the BITS.

If you're interested in learning, do Computer Science.

At least here (Australia), Computer Science is *very* theoretical, more often than not it's essentially just mathematics (programming languages, formal methods, algorithms, AI, computation etc) - this is what I do, and it is awesome.

A BITS does some stuff like project management and resource management/requirements gathering, as well as a wider range of IT stuff (networks, databases etc). I started in a BITS, but I am more of a software developer, so the computer science was more appealing.
