### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-11-19
Sorry to hear about the macular degeneration. I had cataracts that have been corrected through surgery. While I had cataracts I was able to use my laptop with a 14.1 inch wide screen at 1280 X 800 resolution. The 14.1 inch widescreen is about the lowest I would recommend, and forget about a 10.1 inch netbook. My desktop computers all have 20 inch or larger widescreen monitors. I learned to use "Ctrl +" and "Ctrl -" to adjust the zoom in the Firefox or Chrome browsers when I was almost blind before the surgery.

I prefer HP desktop computers, and Dell or Toshiba laptop computers. I and my family have used several of each. They all work well with Microsoft Windows; however, if I am planning to install Ubuntu/Linux I am **very careful** about researching the specs of Toshiba computers as some of them don't work well with Linux Distros. HP and Dell computers work well with Linux Distros with minor tweaking. I have a Lenovo desktop that has worked very well, however, my wife has a Lenovo laptop that has been disappointing because of braking keyboard keys. So, my experience with Lenovo is limited with vastly different experiences.
