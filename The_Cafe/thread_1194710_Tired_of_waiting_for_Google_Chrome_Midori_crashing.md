### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-06-22
Since about 2 months ago, I discovered the WebKit rendering engine, and tried a few browsers. Midori was unstable, and even if you use the PPAs, it seems to have performance issues and still crashes frequently in Ubuntu.

Of course, there are the dev builds of Chromium and Google Chrome, but we all know heading to a page with flash just to know you can't use it is a pretty big drawback.

Also, Arora's pretty good, but uses Qt rather than GTK, and we want something that's seamless with our Ubuntu desktop, neh?

Well, surprisingly obscure option of Webkit browser, Kazehakase is very fast, stable, and let me say it again- FAST in Ubuntu. Much faster than Epiphany with Webkit, and of course much easier to install.

Just go to Add/Remove to find it- just one more note, though. On all other webkit browsers, there were certain sites that had artifacts or lacked some functionality, but all of those sites have regained their former gecko glory with Kazehakase. Some examples- IMVU.com doesn't have a white space next to the shout-out, imeem's player plays, and animated gifs don't go crazy.

No crashes yet, and I've been using it for a day so far. So please, try it out and tell me if you would also suggest it.
