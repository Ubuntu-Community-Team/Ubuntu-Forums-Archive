### Post by mystics on 2015-10-15
Here's a few dates to think about:

ENIAC came out in 1946.
UNIX came out in 1973.
Windows came out in 1985.
Linux came out in 1991.

Something about all those dates is that they occurred less than 100 years ago! In other words, we're dealing with technology that hasn't even hit a 100 year birthday yet! And if there is one thing we've learned in those less than 100 years it is that you can't guarantee the technology you're using will even be relevant in 20 years, much less 100 years. Basically, we can't even guarantee that Ubuntu will be around in 2104, and if it is, there's no guarantee it will use the same numbering system (how many time has Windows changed the way it names stuff!) or that anyone but historians will care about Trusty Tahr. And if it does survive, keep the same numbering system, and people do care, it will have weathered enough that a simple turnover to 2104 will be relatively easy to address.

So while it might be a nice little thought experiment, I don't think that it warrants much concern 90 years before our concerns may be realized.
