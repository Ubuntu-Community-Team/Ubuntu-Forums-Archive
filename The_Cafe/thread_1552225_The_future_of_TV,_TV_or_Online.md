### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-08-13
Samsung and Philips are coming out with YouTube enabled televisions. I think it's pretty obvious that, if we DO use TV, it's going to be internet based. A lot of the cable bandwidth sent through OnDemand services uses internet protocols to run the content.

Also, services like Vonage are really taking over with home phone plans. I think 4G and High Speed internet will be the main ways we gather information in the next 10 years or so.

Seriously, just look at what the desktop Operating System has done to video production, audio production, office work, graphics and multimedia design, content delivery, etc. etc.

The internet is the same way with audio and visual communication, but this way it's interactive. And, in many ways it's becoming more collaborative, which is what the creator of the World Wide Web (Tim Berners-Lee) had envisioned in the first place. It wouldn't surprise me if it continued in this direction and became ubiquitous. It's just too convenient and well-priced not to.
