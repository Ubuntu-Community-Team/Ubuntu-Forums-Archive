### Post by undecim on 2011-04-11
I think you're confusing "cloud" with either "p2p" or "magic"

p2p technologies in an MMO could be handy.

There are security and privacy issues though. For example, with BitTorrent, you can see the IP address (or the proxy address) of anyone who is downloading a file.

Also, I could be sending false data. Generating random numbers between two people is tricky, and an MMO takes a lot of random numbers. To pick a random number between two people, both sides need to pick a random number, then use cryptographic methods to prove that one side didn't change their number. This requires additional communication and will introduce additional lag. So random numbers still need to be generated by a trusted 3rd party (i.e. the central server) to keep lag down.
