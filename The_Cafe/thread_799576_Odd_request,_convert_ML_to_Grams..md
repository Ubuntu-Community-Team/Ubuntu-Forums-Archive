### Post by SupaSonic on 2008-05-19

if density is roughly ~ 1, like water, then

mass (gramms) = 1(g/ml) * 100 (ml) = 100 gramms

### Post by 505 on 2008-05-19
Take a bottle of vodka, put in on scales. Drink the bottle of vodka and put in on the scales again. Subtracts and divide and you have your answer.

Or the answer without the added benefits of being drunk:
Say vodka is 40% ethanol (0.789 g/mL) and 60% water (0.9970 g/mL) then the density of the vodka would be 0.914 g/mL. Thus 100 mL is about 91 grams.

### Post by yssida on 2008-05-19
First, what does 40% mean? Does it mean 40% of the total volume?

Then, you have 40 mL of alcohol, and assuming everything else is water, and some other stuff negligible, then you got 60 mL water.

alcohol (ethanol) is 0.789 g/mL, and water is 1 g/mL.

Multiply alcohol's density with the volume. So 40 mL * 0.789 g/mL = 31.56 g.

Multiply water's density with volume. 60 mL * 1 g/mL = 60 g

add 31.56 g + 60 g = 91.56

A general formula would be
Given:
ratio/percentage of alcohol to water
total volume of the vodka

Constants:
water's density
alcohol's density

solution:
[((alcohol's percentage)*(total volume of vodka))*(alcohol's density)]+[((water's percentage)*(total volume of vodka))*(water's density)]

EQUALS whatever weight you come up with...

It's quite easy actually
