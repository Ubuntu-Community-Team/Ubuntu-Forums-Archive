I am a Mac user whose tired of Apple's premium for their name. Back before the Intel switch, when Macs had hardware features like the three finger salute was coded in ROM/Firmware and not in the Mac OS Software for force a reboot (and the keyboards had power buttons), Target Disk Mode, Firewire ports... all of this has been eroded away by Apple.

Now for $1100 you get... a crappy Intel with Mac OS X.

Sorry, kids, but the Mac OS GUI isn't worth the price to me. So I moved to Linux. Only to find the without flash, a PPC Mac is basically dead in the water! So.. I've sold that hardware and moved to cheap old x86 Intel PCs running Ubuntu or Puppy Linux.

Onto the car analogy. I googled Linux VW... why, I don't know. Regardless my car -- a 2006 VW Jetta TDI -- runs on open source fuel: biodiesel. The local places make it out of veggie oil (used of course) and chicken fat. It smells like fries, has a lower CO2 output (and the jury's out on if the NOX is higher or lower, depends on the feed stock). Most importantly, anyone with time on their hands can turn oil or fat into fuel via well documented processes online.

This is VERY much what I like about Linux. It just works on most any hardware, might need some tweaking, but it works. I have an old Celery 1Ghz with 256MB running Puppy in RAM... with GUI and networking... it's just fine. Ubuntu on my EEE, just fine.

I don't ask a lot: just long uptimes, web support, and being able to read any formatted hard drive I stick into the thing (even HFS+). Linux does that. I can make it look like Mac, Windows, or neither. Hell if I want to be old skool I can work out of a terminal or three. For the car I ask 575 - 750 miles driven on less than 15 gallons of fuel consumed (42-50mpg), and a 500,000 miles lifespan (my dad's 86 Golf Diesel just turned 430,000), and heated seats :)

In the end there are no wrong choices, only the right ones for you.

Oh and to the guy who said he'd not been in a VW, while I'd trade the 06 for a Subie Diesel, you should give the Jetta SportWagen a try, thats if you like wagons. No 4x4, but it's still a nice FWD vehicle. Look for the Audi A4 Avant TDI in the coming years if you need 4x4 and a wagon. The A3 and VW Rabbit diesels should be here by end of 2010!!
