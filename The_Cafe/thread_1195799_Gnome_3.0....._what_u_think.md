### Post by joey-elijah on 2009-06-24
I actually like gnome 3! 

People are always resistant to change - KDE users hating KDE 4, users boycotting facebook everytime it changes it's layout etc

People need to realize that progress doesn't come from standing still. 

If you don't like Gnome Shell - get involved and tell them why, suggest alternatives, mock them up in the gimp, etc

Gnome3 -needs- to move away from this 15 year old "desktop" metaphor we currently use - the way we use computers and what we use them for are different now and we should have a DE that reflects that. Gnome 3 or KDE 4 may not be the best representations of this, but they're steps along in the right direction and can be built on.

### Post by froggyswamp on 2009-06-24
Focusing on fixing those tens and hundreds (little) usability problems Gnome is notable for - must be top priority, also more user friendly and sophisticated desktop (items) handling and enhanced drag-n-drop experience. Skipping these issues and focusing instead on "big programs" instead is not the optimal way to go.

The Ubuntu project "100 paper cuts" should have been started several years ago - I wrote about the need to fix the myriad of little usability bugs and more in a blog of mine and Canonical started doing just that. Nonetheless Canonical is not doing enough in this direction, i.e. they can't simply rely on usability bug reports, they need to do serious research themselves.
