### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2010-05-12
> **ubunterooster said:**
> That's creepy!

It's nothing!
[https://panopticlick.eff.org/](https://panopticlick.eff.org/)
Now *that* is creepy. It's also the nature of the web; it's why it works so well.
(Well, to a point. Future standards may deprecate a few features being used there).


With regards to a lot of Chrome processes being responsible for a lot of network traffic, this can be caused by a complex web app with Javascript that regularly queries a server. Extensions also get their own processes, so some of them may be doing the same.

With regards to the "Chrome extensions aren't good enough" argument, I've found precisely the opposite; I'm in love with a [certain Chromium extension]("https://chrome.google.com/extensions/detail/okanipcmceoeemlbjnmnbdibhgpbllgc?hl=en-gb") and can't use Firefox until something similar comes to be for it.
(The others I love have Firefox equivalents, though Chrome has consistent icon placement and "new messages" badges to its credit as well).

So, let's not start that debate in this fellow's thread; everyone is equally entrenched in his own side, and equally wrong about the other ;)
