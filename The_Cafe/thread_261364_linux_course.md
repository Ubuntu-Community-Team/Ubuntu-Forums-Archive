### Post by mips on 2006-09-20
You basically have 4 certification options:
Linux Professional Institute - [http://www.lpi.org/en/lpi/english/certification](http://www.lpi.org/en/lpi/english/certification)
CompTIA (Linux+) - [http://certification.comptia.org/linux/default.aspx](http://certification.comptia.org/linux/default.aspx)
Red Hat - [https://www.redhat.com/training/](https://www.redhat.com/training/)
Ubuntu  - [http://www.ubuntu.com/partners/certification/pro](http://www.ubuntu.com/partners/certification/pro) (Based on LPI with extras)

As for pricing you will have to call local IT training vendors in your area.

All the above would be good except for the CompTIA, my experience with CompTIA is that thier training is not to great. The Ubuntu & LPI are basically the same except Ubuntu has a few extra modules. So if you do the Ubuntu one you could also get LPI certified. Red Hat is probably the most well known one but pretty vendor specific. Red Hat is used in a big chunk of enterprises so will probably be the most welcomed cert from an employer perspective.

The above are my opinions and not necesarily correct.
