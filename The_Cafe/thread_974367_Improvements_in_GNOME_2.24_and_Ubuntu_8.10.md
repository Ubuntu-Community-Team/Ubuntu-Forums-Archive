### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-11-07
Sorry, this should be a comment to your article but Blogspot's OpenID stuff ate my post. I just need to point out that you are incorrect about the Shut Down dialog not counting down. Sit there for the full 60 seconds and you will see!

It just doesn't count down on a 1 second interval at first because that would be unnecessarily distracting. It doesn't matter whether the system will shut down in 60 or 58 seconds; both are big numbers. So it starts off counting down at 10 second intervals so that the user isn't distracted (and can thus read the dialog in peace), then it turns into 5 second intervals and ultimately 1 second intervals to give us that satisfying "7... 6... 5... 4..." thing.

It actually makes a lot of sense.

As for everything else, it's a nice article. Thanks for pointing out the changes. There are an astounding number of people who think GNOME is stagnating, when really it's just being cleaned and polished under the hood. One must build up to a leap ;)
