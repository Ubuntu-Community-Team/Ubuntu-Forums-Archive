I believe the English translation makes it a bit confusing:

> &#39853; Sake: [Salmon]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salmon")[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sashimi](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sashimi)

While the English words are the same notice the difference in Japanese.

> 
This beverage is called *sake* in English, but in [Japanese]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language"), sake (&#37202;) or [o-sake]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honorific_speech_in_Japanese") (&#12362;&#37202;) refers to alcoholic drinks in general. The Japanese term for this specific beverage is *Nihonshu* (&#26085;&#26412;&#37202;), meaning "Japanese sake".[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sake](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sake)
