### Post by LowSky on 2011-11-14
if your in the US, go to [www.newegg.com](www.newegg.com) and look at their monitor section. All products have reviews from users so it takes some guesswork out. The check a few stores in person.

My newest monitor was well under $200, its a 24" LED backlight LCD ASUS with 1920x1080 display. Pretty decent for normal day to day.

IPS is usually used by people who do a lot of photo/video editing and need perfect video coloration from any angle. TN displays are your run-of-the-mill display. You can tell the difference by standing to the side of the monitor and you notice the screen dim. 

Usually IPS will be more expensive but at the same time not so good for gaming as their refresh rates are higher. TN are cheaper unless you look for a model that can do 3D because of the higher refresh rate.

Also if you can afford it get a LED backlight monitor. The cost a lil bit more, but the trade off is a thinner monitor and lower electric bills.
