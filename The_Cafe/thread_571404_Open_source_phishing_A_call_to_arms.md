### Post by SonicSteve on 2007-10-09
The article isn't so much condemning Linux for being vulnerable to phishing attacks. It's suggesting that a Linux machine makes a good attackers platform for a phishing attacker to phish from. 
We all know that phishing will succeed because there can be nothing done to secure the user from themselves, and really we don't want that to happen anyhow. 

Here is the opening paragraph of the article

> Recently, consumer auction giant eBay announced that nearly 1,200 registered eBay users’ information was stolen via phishing attacks. That’s not shocking. **What’s shocking is that it’s very likely the phishers were using rootkitted Linux boxes.** They expected the attacks to come from Microsoft boxes. This time that’s not the case.
It was only a matter of time. As Linux becomes more and more popular, the spread of more nefarious uses for the operating system will only grow. And grow. And grow. And what will more than likely happen is the threats will only get worse. Because of the flexibility and power of the OS, the criminals creating the attacks will be able to push the boundaries even farther than what we’ve seen when the Windows OS is used

Its a compliment as well as a potential problem.
