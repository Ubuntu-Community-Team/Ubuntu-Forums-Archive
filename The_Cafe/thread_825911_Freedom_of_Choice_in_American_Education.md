### Post by picopir8 on 2008-06-11
I must admit I did not watch the video but I live in an area where people can choose their schools.  Here are the problems with our system:
1) Parents have be below some income threshold.  If a parent is above that threshold, even by $1, then they do not qualify at all.  So it encourages people not to work, or work part time so they get the government handout. If it works as well as many proponents claim, then it should be available to all.
2) Private schools and charter schools do not have to meet the same state/federal criteria as public schools.  Public schools must have licensed teachers, must test their students and meet certain goals to retain their funding. This costs money and takes time away from actual teaching.  If these schools get my tax money for taking choice students (whether directly or in the form of a voucher), then they should be required to live up to the same standards as the public schools.  If those standards are not worth enforcing, then then public schools should not have to follow them either.
3) Along the same lines as #2, it is a slippery slope allowing students to go to religious schools or schools with certain social/political slants.  While there is nothing wrong with these schools (and I plan to send my kids to a religious school myself), it is the state sponsorship that is a problem.  If you allow one group then you must allow them all.  While most people in the US (primarily Christian country) would not mind their taxes going to a Christian school, tax money must also be allowed to go say a satanic school.  Hate groups that survive by claiming to be religions, social organizations, or political groups could also set up schools and collect tax dollars.
4) Allowing people to choose their own schools is a bit of an illusion.  Prestigious schools who may not want choice students to attend their schools simply raised their tuition above what the government is willing to pay.

If the government can solve all of these issues with choice schools, then they have a chance at working.  The present implementation though just wipes many of the problems under the carpet.

### Post by shadylookin on 2008-06-11
In the US the education system is largely run by the state and not the federal government so it's not really fair to paint the entire country with one brush when there are 50 different systems. 

For most people you can either pay money to have your child attend a private school(assuming there is one) or they can attend public school for free. Creating a voucher system is a slippery slope because if you let any voucher be used for a religious school you have to accept all religious schools. I for one don't like my tax dollars going to teaching children religion. 

Comparing us to Belgium is fairly pointless. Belgium is a small country with a fairly high population density. For me I would of had to travel 2 hours there and back to attend another high school in that time they probably could have traveled to the other side of their country.:lolflag:
