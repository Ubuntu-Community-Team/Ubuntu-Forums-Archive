### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-08-09
I know it'd get a LOT of people to finally make the switch, like companies and classrooms, which are in many cases the big starting grounds for new users.

But how many of you guys already using Linux would like to have an official port? If the need is big enough, I think we could do something interesting to get Adobe's attention.

For a while I've been thinking, "well, the reason people say they don't port Adobe CS is due to market share, and there aren't enough media professionals using Linux already to justify it." Of course, there's no way Adobe or anyone else can know how many Linux users there are, while we do know there are quite a few.

So, I've been wanting to ask Adobe just how much money it would take for them to port it, and see if we can't start a fund raiser like Diaspora did with Kickstarter (although not on that site, of course).

I mean, it would be ideal for us to build functionality and compatibility with Adobe formats into relevant open source packages, and that will come in time, but some people just need the interface and the name to feel comfortable. If people are going to try to use Wine or VirtualBox anyway, we might as well have a native port for those people until open source is in the right position to stand up right next to Adobe (I think it is, but most don't agree with me).

After all, if even Linux users could use Adobe products, that might hinder the need for open source projects like GIMP and help their business (probably not, but they could feel like they're digging in for the long haul this way).

So, I suppose what I'm asking is,

[B]1. Do you want it?

2. What would it take to convince Adobe to do it.

3. Can we pull together to prove it to them and do what it takes?[/B]

I mean, it's the customer's voice that decides, in the end.
