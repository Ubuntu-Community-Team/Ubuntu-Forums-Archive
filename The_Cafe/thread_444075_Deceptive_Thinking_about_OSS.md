### Post by DoctorMO on 2007-05-15
I was reading an interesting article (NewScientist 05/05/2007 p36) about choices and preferences and was thinking afterwards about weather some of the colouring applies to software and more specificity open source software.

For those without a subscription I'll post a brief overview: How to Make Better Choices

1. Don't fear consequences - people are more likely to make a choice that makes us happier by choosing things with the least amount of pain or potential loss, to such an extent that the gain must be twice that of any loss; but after a loss the pain is much less than expected with most people rationalising away the loss to make the world bearable.
2. Go with your gut instinct - where the choices are too numerous it is better to do some other task while letter your unconscious mind process the choice and then make it. with simpler choices is worth more to rationalise it.
3. Consider you emotions - Anger can make you impetuous, selfish and risk-prone; emotions of past choices effect your current rationalisation and sway your mind.
4. Play the devil's advocate - most people argue only on the evidence that supports their position and ignore any evidence that counters it. the best way to prove something is to disprove it's falsity not to show it's truth.
5. Eye on the Ball - when evidence is unavailable, seemingly random numbers can provide anchor points such that your more likely the move a figure towards the anchor point, even given it's randomness.
6. Don't cry over spilt milk - Ovoid becoming sucked into investing time and money (throwing good money after bad) if you consider that you would not have done it had you the choice now.
7. Look at it the other way - the presentation of options leads us to pick the option that spins on the positive gains, and we are less likely to choose options that include the negative data.
8. Beware social pressure - it's very difficult to ignore what other people think, so if you find everyone in a group agreeing play contrariety.
9. Limit your options - The more options you consider, the more dissatisfied you will be with what ever choice you make, fearing that you have missed out on something.
10. Have someone else choose - People are happier having their choices done for them when they are uninformed and don't like choosing for themselves; it's better to take personal responsibility in everything you do.

Now the parts I see relating to our talks on these boards are 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8 and 10; we seem swayed by so much it seems amazing that we can become better people from talking to others given these factors.

What do other people think about these factors?
