### Post by AlbinoButt on 2007-09-14
> **MissionImpossible said:**
> Why do many Linux users stubbornly continue to refer to Notebooks PCs as "Laptops"? Is it because MS calls them Notebooks, as well?

When I bought my Notebook, it was advertised and documented as such and nowhere did I see "Laptop" mentioned!

"Notebooks" weren't commonly known as such until recently. Marketers decided that they should be called notebooks for two main reasons:

[LIST=1]
[*]To convey the message that it is very small
[*]To prevent people from claiming that the companies endorse people using them on their laps
[/LIST]

Nowadays, portable computers run so hot that you can really hurt yourself just by operating them on your lap. Companies didn't want to be liable for this, so "laptops" needed to be called something else.

And why buy a laptop when you could get a *notebook*? Doesn't it sound so much smaller? Marketers sure think so.

But to the point, people like me call them laptops because that's what they were commonly called since the late 1980s/early 1990s. Before then, suitcase-sized computers were called "portable computers" or "luggables".

Also, if I say, "I just bought a *notebook*," this is pretty ambiguous. I could be talking about a paper notebook, which the name originated from. If I say, "I just bought a *laptop*," there's absolutely no confusion.

I haven't bought into the relatively recent marketing trend that renamed them to **notebooks**.
