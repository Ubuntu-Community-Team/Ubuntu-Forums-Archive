### Post by Sheinar on 2006-04-08
[QUOTE=BWF89]Kind of like how in a socialist/communist country everything is controled by the government and the average person doesn't have the ability to speak out against the government or try to improve it.[/QUOTE]
It's unfortunate many people seem to have this impression of both communism and socialism. It's mainly from bad communist governments in other countries, and people seem to throw socialism in as the same thing. They may be tied, but it's not.

I can understand why people have a bad taste in their mouth when the word communism is uttered, but I can imagine many socialists have just had enough of them being thrown in as the same thing.

I see the arguments for Free/open-source software being socialist in nature and capitalist in nature. Both the "it's not communist/socialist because that's where the government owns everything and are against companies, money and free speech" and "it's not capitalism because capitalism is all about evil corporations screwing over the little guy" are talking out of their backsides. Both systems can be tied to Free/open source software. The capitalist part about having good competition where people can improve on the software and resell it, making competitors have to keep up with their competition and keep improvements coming, to keep their customers. Then the socialist part where the means of production (the source code in this case) is shared/can be used by everyone.

I think they both have important roles and I don't particularly think FOSS swings one way or the other.
