### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2010-09-12
The Pong video game from the 1970's before there were home computers. I got mine out of a dumpster behind a Tandy (Radio Shack) store. I plugged it into a TV and it worked. It was in a dumpster; however, it was working fine. I guess the new technology was more than some people could handle. :)

The TRS-80 CoCo computer with 16 KB of RAM, 900 KHz clock rate, 8 bit 6809 processor, and a cassette recorder for storing programs. It didn't have a floppy disk; that was an upgrade when they became available. When the floppy became available it was 80 KB of storage. The computer actually worked for 9 years.

What was interesting about the CoCo computer was that it had a color display capability. **Co**lor **Co**mputer = CoCo.
