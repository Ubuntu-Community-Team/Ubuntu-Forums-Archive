### Post by doas777 on 2008-10-25
just put a '.' at the beginning of the file name. that'll hide it.

---

### Post by LaRoza on 2008-10-25
Ctrl + h in most file managers will toggle between showing and hiding.

There is no special hidden attribute, just the . in front of the name. ("ls -a" will show them in the terminal as well)
