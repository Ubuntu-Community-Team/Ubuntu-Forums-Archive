### Post by LaRoza on 2008-05-13
> **KaliVoid said:**
> when lifting heavy loads with squat like
exercise you need to elevate the heals to
avoid injury to the achilles tendon

No, that is the worst thing you can do.

For heavy squats, the shins should be almost verticle with the weight on the heel.

Most people don't squat correctly. They try to use their quads. It is entirely (in principle) a hamstring and glute exercise. The action is like sitting back on a seat that is too far away.

The pistol is an excellant way to learn how to squat: [http://hubpages.com/hub/Need_Help_with_Pistols_Single-Leg_Squats_-_Part_1](http://hubpages.com/hub/Need_Help_with_Pistols_Single-Leg_Squats_-_Part_1)

That image is a little misleading, as he is holding no weight. The knee should never extend past the toes with a load.

A squat with a bar should have the bar low (not on the neck!) and the action (going down) should be mainly a flex at the hip (not knees) the knees will bend on their own. A tight abdomen and tensed hips/legs will ensure safety. The weight should on the heels and the head should be looking straight or slightly up. Coming up, is an action of pushing the heals against the ground (not straightening the legs, that makes you fall forward) and straightening the hips.

Not much, I know, but it's enough for me to say that I'm not sedentary. 

      In addition to work I do other stuff.
