### Post by The Keeper on 2009-04-06
I hope IBM does buy Sun. In my opinion they have more to gain from the merger than not. I'd say merger would also be better to us too.

On the hardware stage I could see IBM making use of Sun's SPARC related technologies by improving their own line of SPARC CPU's. There's quite a bit of potential on workstation and server market for SPARCs. If they compete with ARM on the netbook/notebook market, they may gain enough popularity for RISC CPU's to invade desktop market eventually. There hasn't been a proper competitor to Intel's and AMD's stranglehold on CPU market with their CISC x86 CPU's. I certainly hope RISC would gain more popularity.

As for software, I believe merger would only improve ZFS, Java and MySQL. Even though IBM does have its DB2, the base target audience is a bit different. Well, I doubt they would scrap MySQL given how huge market it has. ZFS could finally be licensed under GPL and be included in the linux kernel. Java7 JDK and JRE development might kick off as fully open-sourced development. IBM would also get Solaris. Sun failed to market Solaris properly, but IBM might yet succeed. Then Sun has StarOffice and xVM virtualization software.

So yeah, IBM acquiring Sun has much potential on both hardware and software stage. The only big question is MySQL, but I doubt IBM would just kill it off. More likely to build a new business model out of it, while keeping it open-sourced. Sun has much more to lose in this if the merger doesn't happen. At least IBM is one of the better partners that would be willing to buy Sun. If the merger happens, the future will certainly become slightly more interesting.
