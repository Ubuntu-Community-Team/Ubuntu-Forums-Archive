Question...

In 'the South' (US), tea generally comes in two varieties; sweet tea, and unsweet tea.  This is tea that is sweetened with sugar, and is defaulted with ice (unless they specify otherwise).  We even have a rare third variety called "half sweet, half unsweet," which actually means they prefer their tea with fresh mucus.

Out west and up north, people give me very confused looks when I ask for sweet tea.  They say... uhh... "iced tea?"  Then we're both confused.  Unless the waitress has exceptional diplomatic skills, a compromise of "coke" is usually agreed upon.

I've even tried saying... iced tea, with sugar in it.  However, the confusion persists.

Is iced tea defaulted sweet, just as sweet tea here is defaulted with ice?  Or, is it just straight tea with ice?
