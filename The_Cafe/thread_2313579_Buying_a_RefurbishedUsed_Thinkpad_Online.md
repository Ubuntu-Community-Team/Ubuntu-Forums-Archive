### Post by sudodus on 2016-02-14
I have good experience from buying refurbished enterprise class computers for my family and friends, but in another continent (Europe). One of these companies has a one-year guarantee, that the computer should work. In 2 cases of totally around 10 (3 Thinkpads), there were problems, that could be fixed, so I think those companies are reliable.

- one HDD had bad sectors, and they sent a new one.

- one Windows system's graphics was failing. The computer was returned and fixed. They said it was a software problem.

All these (around ten) computers have worked well with linux.

-o-

Find companies in your country that refurbish computers from companies and other organisations.

*Laptop Closeout* seems to have various length of guarantees for different classes of computers: I found 3 months, 6 months and 1 year. Three months is probably enough to detect hardware problems with a computer. (If it works when you get it, it will probably continue to work until it is too old to run a modern operating system and browse a modern and bloated internet ;-) But of course, if you have bad luck, something might stop working the day after the guarantee expires.)

If you cannot find a company that looks better, I think you can buy from this company. If you are not sure, maybe you should buy a rather cheap model.

### Post by kurt18947 on 2016-02-14
I've bought 3 laptops (ThinkPads) off Ebay and had no major issues. The one I'm typing this on was one and it did have an issue (fan failed message) but a few minutes on Google produced a fix -  very quick shot of compressed air in the heat sink plumbing. Be sure there's a reasonable return time. My local Craigslist also has a guy that always has laptops for sale so I suspect he buys off-lease laptops, cleans them up, replaces hard drives if necessary and resells them. Buying a machine without a hard drives presents an opportunity - factor a nice SSD into the price 

I prefer brands that post hardware manuals online. If I do have a component failure down the road it's nice to know how to remove and replace. It seems there's always a screw or clip where I least expect it. Youtube is another resource but I prefer official and printed given the choice.
