### Post by mcduck on 2010-04-13
Speed of hard drives depends on many things, and changes depending on where your files are located physically on the drive, the file system used and many other things. Because of this any single number would automatically be wrong. Detailed information is usually only given for drives intended for professional use, but if you really want to know then try to find a review of the drive from some hardware site, they usually run performance tests and produce nice graphs that will give you an estimate of the drive's performance.

On the other hand, the values given for SSD drives are usually just as wrong, especially on cheaper drives the values are more like an optimistic presentation of ideal situation, then boosted a bit for marketing purposes... How misleading the values on the box are varies insanely much, so I'd say taht if you consider buying a SSD drive then reading through some tests & reviews is even more important than with HDD's, it's pretty much the only way to find a good product (unless you are lucky enough to pick the single, good product from a shelf full off bad ones that all have equally good-looking numbers printed on the box).

In short:

* If it's a HDD; the things to look for are speed (5400RPM drive is slower than 720RPM drive) and size of the cache. After that the performance differences between the drives are pretty much too small to be noticeable in real-life use.

* If it's a SDD, read a review. Ignore whatever the box says.
