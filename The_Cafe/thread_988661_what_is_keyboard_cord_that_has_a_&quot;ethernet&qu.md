### Post by Bungo Pony on 2008-11-20
You either zapped your keyboard, or you zapped your PS/2 port. I'm not sure about newer PCs, but older ones used to have the keyboard port fused on the old DIN style connectors. The fuse was mounted on the motherboard and is usually green in color. To replace it, you would have to solder a new one in.

I'm using an old Model M keyboard myself. It's not a PS/2 model, it's an old DIN style model plugged into a PS/2 adapter.

You may want to try plugging another PS/2 keyboard into your computer to make sure that it is indeed the keyboard that has failed.

Also, would it be possible that you broke off one of the pins inside the PS/2 connector coming from your keyboard? I've done that once myself.
