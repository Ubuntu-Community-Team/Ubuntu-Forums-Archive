### Post by kanem on 2007-05-14
So, we've all probably heard about the alleged 235 Microsoft patents that we are said to be violating. This got me thinking about the following hypothetical situation: what if the worst happend? What if MS said exactly which patents they were and the courts upheld them? I know this isn't likely and that this FUD will eventually blow over like the SCO fiasco, but lets play what if.

I see two choices:

1.  Developers could change the code. Re-writing large amounts of code in the kernel, GUI and other software. This would be a huge amount of work and would take time and effort away from progress. Also, since some of the patents are undoubtedly vague and cover general ideas instead of specific code (like [Apple's spring-loaded folders](http://ufies.org/archives/000536.html) patent) it would actually be impossible to take it out without crippling the OS.

2. Alternatively, the rest of the world could say to hell with MS and their patents. Development on all code integral to the GNU/Linux OS could go on as usual outside the US. There are huge numbers of FOSS developers outside the US. And among those in the US, many are not American, and so probably wouldn't mind much going back to their respective countries, as long as they still got to do their job. The large corporate entities who back Linux also have large divisions outside the US.

I'm probably missing some pros and cons of these two choices. Or possibly a third choice. What do you think of this hypothetical situation? Could the international fork be done? Would it be done? I say yes, it *could* be done, but would not be.
