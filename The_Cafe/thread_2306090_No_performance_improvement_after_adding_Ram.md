### Post by jeff127 on 2015-12-12
Upgrading RAM is cheap and it won't always work - the slow-down may be the CPU. Most systems (including windows) don't need much more than 2GB of RAM anyway. If you were running a game it's possible that there'll be an improvement, but your best bet is a CPU or hard drive upgrade. I've got 8GB of RAM (more than I asked the shop for) and I only use 1GB at most.

### Post by sudodus on 2015-12-12
+1

When RAM is the bottleneck, the amount of it makes a big difference. Otherwise you will hardly notice the difference.

You will probably notice a difference if you have many tabs open in your web browser, or if you edit huge pictures or video clips. The system will be very slow, when it starts swapping, and you decrease the need for swapping with more RAM.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-12-12
If it is a decrease in application loading times that you are looking for then the answer is a storage device with a faster data transfer rate such as an Solid State Drive.

Applications are loaded from physical storage (hard drive) into RAM. So, doubling the amount of RAM will not reduce the loading time of applications but it will reduce the amount of swapping of data out of RAM on to the hard disk and back again when too much is going on with the OS.

Regards.
