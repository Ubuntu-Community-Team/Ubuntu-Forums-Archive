### Post by briancurtin on 2006-05-16
i have thought a bit about that same thing, kabus. when i first heard of arch, the speed is what brought me to check it out. everything you listed is what has kept me there. 

pacman is a great package manager, and packages themselves are easy to work with in building them and such. PKGBUILDS are available for pretty much everything, and if you cant get one working or dont know how to make one, you can ask on the forums and get some help or someone might even make the PKGBUILD for you. ive never had instability problems with anything either, except for things that are my own fault. packages show up in the arch repos maybe a day after they come out, so everything is very up to date. if something is out dated, you can just flag it out-of-date and the maintainer will get an email to bring it up-to-date. the package maintainers and devs really do a great job working with everything. 

arch systems are meant to be kept up to date for the most part, so those looking for a "install and leave it alone" system might want to look elsewhere. due to how often things are upgraded/updated, if you don't 'pacman -Syu' every so often, once you finally do, you will have a big buildup of upgrades and you never know whats going to happen. thats just how it is though, and i 'pacman -Syu' at least once a day. 

the community forums are very good as well. problems are tackled pretty well and not too many things go unfixed. the people who are involved in the distro on the inside are pretty active in the forums, such as the maintainers and trusted users (users who manage the AUR), so sometimes they chime in with their deeper knowledge of a package if thats the case. everything ive asked got fixed, except my one work in progress XGL-based thread. im pretty sure it will be fixed soon though.

all around it really is a great distro, something judd worked hard on and should be proud of. its definitely worth a shot to anyone looking to try a new distro, or those who are looking to see what all the fuss is about over at [www.archlinux.org](www.archlinux.org)
