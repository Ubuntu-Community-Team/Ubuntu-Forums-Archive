### Post by michaelzap on 2007-12-16
Hmm... I'm not sure whether I like it or not. I'm used to Amarok, and I'm actually not finding the interface as intuitive as that is.

For example, how the hell do I add files to the playlist? I can play albums easily enough, but what about when I want to play multiple tracks from different artists? There must be a way (since there is a playlist), but the online documentation for this is blank and I can't seem to find it by clicking around.

I also prefer Amarok's flexibility in how the collection is organized. I don't always play entire CDs, so I like being able to open CD subdirectories in the collection browser and see the individual tracks there. With BMPx there are three panels - Artist, CD, and tracks - all of which are handled much more elegantly in Amarok imho.

Plus I can drag and drop files in Amarok, and that doesn't seem to be the case in BMPx. And it refuses to use my local cover art, which is annoying when it doesn't find something online (I'm seeing mostly placeholder covers). And its tag editor, lyric and artist info browser, and overall display/interface options are nowhere near as nice as Amarok's.

On top of this, it's currently using 36MB of RAM on my system, whereas Amarok uses 21MB (with a collection of about 12,000 tracks).

OK I guess I don't like it. Or maybe I just like Amarok better. A lot better.

In general I'm happy with Amarok, but I keep trying other music players only because it's KDE and I use Gnome, so it looks a little bit out-of-place and requires some extra libraries. But I guess I have those anyway for K3b, which I don't plan to give up. I may try BMPx again in a few months to see if any of my complaints have been resolved, but for now I'm sticking with Amarok.

### Post by geoken on 2008-05-27
It's good at what it does, but it doesn't do enough to be the best IMO.

First off, any player that only lets me browse music through a small number of predefined parameters (2 to be exact) is almost useless to me. The point of media players with library management features is to help you find your media in ways that a simple file manager can't. With rhythmBox all my tracks are sitting there and I can sort them by artist, genre, album, length (to hear my dj sets), year (when I'm in the mood for old stuff), rating (when I just feel like listening to my best songs), etc. If all the app lets me do is sort by artist and album I may as well just browse my music library with nautilus and use a minimal player to play/que tracks.

It also needs device management.
