### Post by lespaul_rentals on 2008-02-27
I don't know if you're a fan of energy drinks, but a large Rockstar, Bookoo, or Monster can keep me up for a good three hours a piece (I also have a caffeine tolerance, as I drink at least one caffeinated drink a day, and they still keep me up all the same).  If you choose this approach, choose a drink such as Vault, Rockstar, Bookoo, Monster, Red Bull, etc., one that claims to be an energy drink.  Sodas like Coke and Pepsi have a higher sugar to caffeine ratio, so your body will sugar crash and you will be tired as hell if you do not have enough caffeine.

Also, I don't know if you smoke, but a grit or two or maybe a cigar is always good.  You said that you intend to do work while staying up.  Nicotine makes you very aware and thoughtful, which can help keep you motivated and maintain creativity.  It also helps you stay awake, becuase it gets your heart rate up.  (Don't complain about the health consequences of smoking, I know what you're going to tell me anyway.)

But remember, drink water!  Drink lots and lots of water.  Drink a Nalgene bottle of water every 30 or 45 minutes, at least.  Drink even more if you are using caffeine to keep yourself up, as you will need to replenish your fluids to maintain health and energy.  Avoid foods that are high in protein and fat, as these will make your digestive system work harder.  Don't eat a can of tuna or a bowl of macaroni and cheese.  If at all possible, eat small snacks throughout the night instead of large meals.  This will keep your metabolism ticking, but it won't tax your digestive system too much.

May I also recommend that you listen to some good music?  Energetic stuff, of course.  Also, I've found that radio talk shows (my personal late-night favorite is Loveline, [http://www.lovelinearchive.com](http://www.lovelinearchive.com)) are good to listen to, because they keep your mind thinking, thus discouraging sleep.

Good luck!  Check in every couple of hours or so.  Remember that once you pass the "Oh man, I'm soooo tired" mark, it gets easier.  I usually go to bed at about 11 PM, and I find myself reaching critical exhaustion at about 1 AM.  If I push myself past that and make it until 3 AM or so, it gets considerably easier.  Just the other day, I stayed up until 5:30 AM.  The only reason I went to sleep was because I knew that the next day would most likely be hell.
