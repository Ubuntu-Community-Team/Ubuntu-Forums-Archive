### Post by RAV TUX on 2006-05-19
Just got back from Niagara Falls with my wife, just wanted to say I still love the "Waste Basket" icon!

I am a very detail orientated visual person, I have a high appreciation for aesthetics,  I also happen to prefer the Gnome desktop.

I have to say my post goes beyond an appreciation of a single icon but how this icon flows with the Ubuntu Human Theme, and the default Gnome desktop. Beyond this it incorporates the existential realization of the personification of Ubuntu; Ubuntu expressed in visual beauty and it's philosophical completeness.

I realize it is a rendering of the tangerine 'dream' theme, but presentation is everything, believe it or not but it is important. Especially when I present the live CD of Ubuntu on someones Apple or XP computer. Small visual cues like this are most important to first impressions to the first time observer of any linux distro.

To miss this type of detail in the marketing perspective of Ubuntu when trying to overcome bug #1 is not wise or reasonable.

The "Ubuntu Wastebasket" is symbolic of the success that Ubuntu has found in the international market, this attention to detail and the success which proceeds it is more then just a a guy going goo-goo over a trashcan.

It is my way of congratulating the overall Ubuntu team, and the Ubuntu community on a job well done. You are all beautiful. As beautiful as a Ubuntu Dapper wastebasket, and that says a lot. 

:mrgreen: 

(my apologies for not explaining my appreciation of the wastebasket more in depth to help lead to intellectual discourse on the artistic design of the Ubuntu default desktop theme)
