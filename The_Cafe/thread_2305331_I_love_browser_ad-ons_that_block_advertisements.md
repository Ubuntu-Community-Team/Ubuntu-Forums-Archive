### Post by night_sky2 on 2015-12-04
> **michael-piziak said:**
> Please explain further my friend.
You don't need to install an extension just to enable Firefox Tracking Protection.

It can easily be done in about:config

1. Go to location bar and type about:config

2. Skip the warning and then type: **privacy.trackingprotection.enabled** -> True

3. restart Firefox

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-12-05
I use Adblock in browsers because ads often slow page loads and, because few sites prepare and monitor their own ads, can be a source of malware when an ad syndicator is compromised.

The ads are much less of a privacy concern for me. I'm not especially concerned if Site B knows I was at Site A (something inherent in the net's design anyway).

However, the use of adblockers and anti-tracking techniques combined with the public's expectations that web content be provided free of charge calls into question the ability of the web to deliver content that rises above lowest-common-denominator dumbed-down rubbish. If sites can't find a way to make money, they will disappear, or become as useless and doomed as Yahoo's or MSN's main page. Web content will be reduced to user-generated nonsense on Facebook, Twitter, etc.

In the early days of the commerical web, after Netscape and IE became available on Windows in the 1990's, many sites did try to sell their content.  Web users responded with a wave of contempt and derision. That's why we have ads today.
