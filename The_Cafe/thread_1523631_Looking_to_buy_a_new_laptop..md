### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-07-03
I just went through that process a couple of months ago.  Here's what I can share:

1. Hardware compatibility really isn't an issue these days.  There is so little hardware out there that is incompatible, that it's hardly worth worrying about.

2. There are literally thousands of laptops on the market.  You need to put some serious thought into exactly what you want to be able to do with it, what your personal hardware requirements are, and your budget before anybody could give you any real advice.

3.  There is always System76.  They sell very nice laptops with Ubuntu pre-installed.  They're not cheap, but I've yet to see an unhappy review of the hardware, or the service.

### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-07-03
> **mamamia88 said:**
> all i can say is make sure your wireless card works properly before buying.

Good advice, and one of the few pieces of hardware that has less than universal linux support.

However, WiFi support has been improving at an incredible rate.  I read reviews and forum posts about the laptop I ended up purchasing that were as recent as 6 months ago, and many of them had lengthy discussions about downloading and manually compiling drivers for the WiFi.

However, 10.4 installs with perfect support for my WiFi chipset, right out of the box.

So, do check WiFi, but don't sweat it.  As long as some people are able to get it work, you'll be able to as well given a bit of patience and the ability to read and follow directions carefully.

### Post by chessnerd on 2010-07-04
> **Mr Nemo said:**
> I've checked out the system76 website. They are nice laptops, but I feel they are a little expensive for what they offer. I'll have to look into them a little further.

Another Linux computer vendor is [Zareason]("http://zareason.com/shop/home.php"). You might want to check there as well.

My laptop gets occasionally kernel panics so I understand your concerns. I haven't gotten a panic in a couple of weeks, but I'm sure it'll happen again at some point.

As far as hardware support goes, I have only had issues with three things: backlight, wifi, and sound.

The backlight issue was easily fixed, although now Plymouth looks crappy.

Wifi strength was an issue back in 9.10 and earlier, less so in 10.04, although I think that the wireless card might be the source of the kernel panics.

Sound was an issue in 9.04. Speakers or headphones plugged into the jack wouldn't work. It took a while to fix. The headphone jack issue went away in 9.10, but microphone support is still crappy.

If you do end up accidentally buying a laptop with a few problems, don't fret about it. They will probably be gone in a future release.
