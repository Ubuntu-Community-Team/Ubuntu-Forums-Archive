### Post by zekopeko on 2010-07-15
> **Cam! said:**
> 
[*] Native option to turn off Keychain/Password alerts.

There is a specification for this somewhere in the Ubuntu wiki. Maybe under the new Network Manager indicator but I'm not sure.

> [*] Native .7z support.

Depends on the space on the CD. Plus it's not a popular format.

> [*] Application Ballot during Install. During installation, you get to choose between FireFox, Opera, and Chrome, as well as choosing between Empathy IM and Pidgin.

This will never happen thank God.

> [*] New Screensavers.

What's the point? Screensavers had a specific purpose before. Today they are just a power sucking nuisance. 

> [*] Animated Wallpapers.

Nice but not really in Ubuntu's eyesights as of now.

> [*] Better Notification. Perhaps Panel items would flash if you get an update.

The notify-osd specification is still incomplete. No morphing windows and parts of the notify-osd aren't yet implemented.

> [*] Screensaver and Monitor options in the Appearance menu.

What do those two have to do with the Appearance window?

> [*] Support for directly dragging something from an Archive to the Filesystem.

This still isn't supported?!

> [*] A shortcut for the Default Filesystem in the "Places" menu.

Why? Most users don't care about /usr, /etc, /root, etc. They care about their drives and cameras, iPods, USB sticks etc.

> [*] Support for Transparent windows and panels.


Only if the make blur work on the majority of drivers. Transparency without blur sucks big time. It's a usability nightmare.

What I would like to see is a stable, polished and working Ubuntu One.

Round corner on my menus. Generally more theme polish. There are some great mockups from Canonical floating out there.

Banshee as the default media player. Drop Totem and develops a QuicktimeX-like interface to Banshee to replace it.

Kill the old notification area completely.
