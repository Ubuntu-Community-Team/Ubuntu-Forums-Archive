A port can be one of two things. Either a physical connection point for components (i.e. USB port, monitor port, etc) or a software port (i.e. Port 80).

Both uses are derived from the concept of a shipping port where the computer is an island and the only connection to anywhere else is through ports of entry. For hardware, it is a physical dock where components connect (think a ship tying up).

For software ports, it is more like the customs control at the shipping yard that screens and allows goods and people to pass. A closed port will not allow anything/one through. An open port is receiving traffic.

### Post by popch on 2008-01-08
> **barbedsaber said:**
> I suspected the port thinng, but now I want to know, why dosnt eveyone overclock, i mean, i'm itching to make my 2.2ghz procseser run at 999ghz, for projects that do NOT involve world domination ;)

The 'rating' of the processor speed is done by the processor manufacturer. He can apply quite different criteria such as the speed you have paid for, the maximum speed at which the device will run reliably over longer periods or time or even the speed at which the device will activate the smoke signal feature (i.e. will burn out or melt down).

The faster your processor runs, the more power it will consume. All the power it consumes leaves the chip as heat. There are limits to how fast you can drain the heat.

Also, the periphery of the processor must be able to keep up with the demands of an accelerated processor. It is of little use if your processor runs much faster but has to wait much longer for the data to arrive from RAM.

Thus, overclocking at more than a few percents usually is done only in conjunction with some cooling equipment. Overclocking by factors or by orders of magnitude is not usually possible.

If your 2.2ghz computer is dual core, you really have no reason to overclock unless you know why you need to.

Ports are a little more important to learn about if you are using the internet for more than just web browsing. HTTP:// is generally on port 80. IIRC the first 500 ports or so are reserved.
