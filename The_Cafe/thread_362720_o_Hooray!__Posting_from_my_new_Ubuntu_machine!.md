### Post by Wartooth on 2007-02-15
I'm pretty stoked.  Minor accomplishment to most, but a great deal of fun for me. 

My boring story:

Got two free emachines, installed Ubuntu on the lowest-performing one, everything went very well.  Only problem was that the video card couldn't give me the resolution I wanted with my big monitor.  Regardless, once I confirmed it was working, I promised that machine to a friend who needs a computer and she'll be running a 17" CRT, so won't be an issue for her. 

Tried Ubuntu on the second one, but it had some hardware issues. I figured I could use the second one for parts, and was right.  

Ordered a barebones, swapped over the HDD, CPU, RAM and DVD drive. Oh, and I snagged a fan from it, too.  Hehe. 

I now have an AMD 64 3400+, 1 GB RAM, 40 GB HDD, nVidia GeForce 6100, DVD drive equipped rig, running 6.06 LTS (no, I wasn't bold enough for 64-bit, sorry :( ) for less than $100.00.  Free machines and having spare parts laying around RULES! 

Using my previous experiences wrestling with the other machines, I quickly got the resolution I wanted (1920x1200).  I am having a couple of oddities, like the very subtle waves I'm seeing and a flicker of something on the right hand part of the screen when a page loads, but I'm assuming there may be driver tweaks necessary at this point.  Not too worried just yet. 

I'm very excited as THIS is the machine I get to keep and really try to learn on, but the previous two certainly helped me.  I've wanted to do this for YEARS and am finally getting the opportunity.  

Yes, I know this is a bit pointless, but I'm sleep-deprived, happy, and needed to tell SOMEBODY. :D
