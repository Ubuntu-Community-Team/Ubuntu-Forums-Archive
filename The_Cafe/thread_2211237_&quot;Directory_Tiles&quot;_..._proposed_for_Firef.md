### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2014-03-15
I have *no problem* with a FOSS organization earning revenue to maintain itself.  In fact I'm happy to see such businesses and organizations thrive.

HOWEVER...

I find it profoundly dissapointing that we seem to be reaching a point where major FOSS projects are injecting ads into the experience.  To me it's a basic failure if we can't find better ways to sustain these projects than turning them into adware.  

Don't forget, Mozilla is a non-profit, not a business.  Yes, they need revenue to survive, but they also need to stay true to their mission.  Namely:
> 
The Mozilla Foundation is a non-profit organization that promotes openness, innovation and participation on the Internet. We promote the values of an open Internet to the broader world. 


In the end, it's their call, not mine.  I just find it a little sad, that's all.
