I thought the Nexus One distribution model was cool, but a bit idealistic.  The reality is that if someone is with a provider already and not planning to go anywhere for a while, they want the option to get the phone at the discounted price.  My wife tried to buy the Nexus One and renew her T-Mobile contract for 2 years.  They were offering that option, but you had to ditch your current plan and sign up for the voice/data plan that comes with the phone.  We have an older family plan with a great rate and some nice features grandfathered in.  For her to get the phone she would have had to break up our family plan and take the voice/data plan that was required for the phone.  We could then ditch that plan and re-form the family plan, but it would have been the new version and our monthly cost would have gone up significantly.  So, she held off and got the MyTouch Slide when it came out (she loves it, by the way).

I think it would have been wildly popular if they had done more of a hybrid plan.  Sell it unlocked directly through Google, but allow T-Mobile customers to buy it through the normal channels if they preferred.
