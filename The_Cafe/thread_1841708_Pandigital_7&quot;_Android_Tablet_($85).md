### Post by earthpigg on 2011-09-09
I searched for Pantigital, and saw nothing more recent than a tangential mention from several months ago so I thought I'd make a new thread.

I told myself that once a basic Android tablet was under $100, I'd pick one up. And, I have:

[http://www.amazon.com/PanDigital-72-70FW-7-Inch-Tablet-Computer/dp/B004QRIUOG](http://www.amazon.com/PanDigital-72-70FW-7-Inch-Tablet-Computer/dp/B004QRIUOG)

-WiFi only. No GPS, no 3g, just wifi.

-The ones they ship *now* don't have the locked down "e-book reader only" crap, and so there isn't a need to root. Plain old Android minus the Google Apps (all of which have alternatives, anyways). Don't get a used or refurbished one, though, or you will have to root to make it useful. Ignore all mentions of software and interface in reviews more than a few months old.

-If you really want to root it for w/e reason, z4root is free and does the job with a click.

-No Android market, but between Amazon Appstore, Opera Mobile Store, downloading .apk's manually, and the included "SlideMe" market I seem to be covered.

-B&N and Kindle apps work fine - Had to get the Kindle app from Opera Mobile Store, Pandigital seems to be buddy-buddy with B&N, and Kindle doesn't officially support the device so it wasn't on official Amazon Appstore. Between B&N and Kindle, your source of books is covered and anything over 100 years old is of course free. I started "The Prince" by Machiavelli.

-Not nearly as good as any of the $500 options.

-Resistive display, not capacitive, which takes a little getting used to - you have to push a bit harder than a gentle feather touch that capacitive displays have made me used to. Biggest downside is that typing on it is a pain. As far as resistive displays, though, this seems to be best-in-category.

-Upside to the resistive display is that, if you really want to, you can grab any object around you and use it as a stylus thingie. 

I just want to emphasize, before anyone drops $85, that when I first used the resistive touchscreen, I completely hated it and considered re-boxing it and sending it back.

It took about 30 minutes for me to get used to it and now it isn't a problem, but expect to have a similar initial RAAAGE moment that I did because "O noes, my feather touch isn't recognized!"

And it is a bit heavier than a kindle or nook. 

The USB cord is USB Mini-B, but doesn't seem to actually charge it.

[http://www.partsreadyonline.com/images/usbTypes.jpg](http://www.partsreadyonline.com/images/usbTypes.jpg)

It comes with a USB Mini-B chord for communicating with your computer, and a separate power chord that plugs into your wall.

No SD is included, but the included apps seem to have been hacked so as to not need one for downloading stuff. Most apps you will end up getting (Opera, etc) require an SD card to download anything, so dig one out of your drawer. 

I haven't recorded battery life under use, but I let it do it's sleep mode thing last night and woke up to it still being nearly fully charged. Once I popped the back, I saw this written on the battery if it means anything to anyone:

> Li-Ploymer Battery
Model NO: MLP385085-2S
Value: 1600mAh 7.4V 11.84 Wh

If you have one of these (or any Android device) and want access to the various markets, open this thread on the device and click the following links:

Amazon Appstore: [http://www.amazon.com/amazonappstoreapp/](http://www.amazon.com/amazonappstoreapp/) This one seems to be the best-in-breed.

Opera Mobile Store: [http://mobilestore.opera.com/](http://mobilestore.opera.com/) This one is the most lax about caring if an app "officially" supports your device, which is beneficial. 

Soc.io Mall: [http://m.soc.io/](http://m.soc.io/) I haven't used this one much.

Conclusion:

Is it an HTC Ultra Mega Tablet Universal Conquest Dream? Nope. Is it an iPad? Nope.

Do you get what you pay for at $85? Absolutely - an e-book reader suitable for casual web browsing and reading of e-mails.

### Post by earthpigg on 2011-09-11
> **Ric_NYC said:**
> Too cheap. It cannot be a good thing.

Having played with this and done a bit of homework on the device...

Unlike [these things]("http://www.vkamobi.com/computers-laptops-netbooks/tablet-pc/google-android/?sort=20a&disp_order=3"), it wasn't *manufactured* to be $85 and run Android.

They where *manufactured* to be sold for $200 with a locked down bastard-offspring of Android with a custom UI that forces the customer into the B&N store.

When that approach failed, they put more-or-less vanilla Android on it and are now dumping them to recoup some of the costs of the production run. Hardware wasn't the problem, the software was.

The $85 represents the company's failure, and our opportunity to capitalize on that as consumers. And they've even been kind enough to save us the trouble of having to put a custom ROM on it.
