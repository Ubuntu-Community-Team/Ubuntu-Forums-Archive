### Post by starcannon on 2009-08-05
One could do some digging around the local area; newpapers, law firms, hospitals, and other institutions with large computer networks associated to them may have or be willing to open a part time spot for an apprentice. Dig around a bit, you may be able to get involved with a program that gives them a tax incentive, or a wage matching incentive, or something like that.

If your folks can carry you through school, then your already a step ahead of many; this relieves the pressures of every day survival, and will allow you to concentrate heavily on academics. You can always join a suitable club and get involved with a project to pad out your resume when dealing with the college years, you'll have more fun, and learn a lot in the process.
