### Post by fletchoid on 2010-10-02
I have been dabbling with Ubuntu (and/or the Mint variant) off and on for a couple of years.  Occasionally, I have run into trouble with an installation, a game, or some aspect of Ubuntu.  I go online and read a zillion forums, seek advice, and eventually solve the problem.  However, I have noticed a problem with much of the advice.  A lot of it is totally unhelpful, and could end up turning a noob completely off Ubuntu forever.  Here is an recent example.

I was trying the Live CD of 10.04 on an older computer, and got a totally black screen.  So, I booted into Windows, and searched Ubuntu black screen.  I got almost half a million results, so apparently the problem has occurred a few times with older equipment.  Here is a summary of unhelpful suggestions I got:

"Just edit Xconfig"   HELLO!! BLACK SCREEN!! CANT SEE ANYTHING!!!

"Edit Grub to include nomodeset"
Fine, if you know how to edit GRUB2 and where to put nomodeset, and HELLO!! BLACK SCREEN!! CANT SEE ANYTHING!!!

Having used Ubuntu quite a bit, I had figured out how to do all these things, but the black screen is a problem that a first time user of Ubuntu is going to have, and they will give up almost instantly, and never try Ubuntu again.  This would be a shame.  I have left Ubuntu running on my computer while I have Thanksgiving and Xmas guests in the house, and most of my guests used Ubuntu without even realizing that it wasn't Windows.  They would ask me how I got the cool interface, and where could they download it.  If Ubuntu is going to recruit more users, we have to be very careful to give COMPLETE instructions on how to fix a problem, not assume that the person asking the question is a Unix programmer.  New users need to have ALL the steps listed for them, otherwise, when the "solution" fails, we lose them forever.  Anyone else have thoughts on this?

### Post by Bradtek on 2010-10-02
Some simple do's and don'ts for getting help

**DO** Ask as specific as possible questions

**DO **Include System specs (as much as you know)

**DO** look at how long the person answering has been here.
Someone that joined yesterday is probably also a newbie
and someone that's been here for a few years may just have better information (not always).

**DO** wait for others to reply with other options / opinions

**DO** be patient, forum members are from many different time zones and occasionally are asleep when you need that instant fix/answer

**DONT** blindly follow any advice, check other forums / google to see if others have had success with a particular fix.

**DO** use google's advanced search to limit search to more recent pages (ie last 6 months)

If you **DO** Blindly follow the first thing posted

**DO** have your important files backed up

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-10-03
Few comments on this thread ...

First, the OP has yet to response to ANYTHING anyone has said, so saying anything here intended for the OP is most probably a waste of time,

Second ,regarding responses, I've been posting here for a long time and often see posts that jump right into a detailed solution without any way of getting there.  That's not tuxtosterone, that's just bad practice.

On the other hand, we DO expect folks to be able to do some of their own work here and most of us are NOT willing to spend HOURS writing a thread that details step-by-step input-and-response kinds of instructions for those who have never even opened a terminal.

So, what I'm claiming is that the best response is a combination of the following:
1) Asking questions of the OP -- if there are a variety of solutions.  This will help narrow down the choices of answers.
2) Expecting the OP to do so research of their own -- and to work with us.  They can ask if they don't understand a response -- and we'll explain.  They can also do some of their own forum searching to find out more details about a response.

To illustrate 2), the "nomodeset" response is often the correct one for black screens upon boot, so I did a quick forum search in the General forum, and the SECOND thread listed had step-by-step directions on how to add the nomodeset parm.

This took me less than 2 minutes to do! I'm sure it took the OP more than two minutes to type their diatribe.

If folks aren't even willing to spend an extra 2 minutes trying to solve their problems -- then (IMHO) the fault lies not with the forum contributors, but instead, the fault lies with the problem presenters.
