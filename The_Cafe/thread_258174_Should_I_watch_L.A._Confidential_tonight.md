### Post by atrus123 on 2006-09-16
L.A. Confidential is a really good film, especially if you have any interest in noir as a genre.

Back in college, I used to spend a bunch of time watching noir and reading hardboiled detective fiction.  L.A.C. definately fits the mold.  I'd like to read Elroy's novel at some point as well.

For those who like the genre, I recommend:

(films) Maltese Falcon, Laura, A Touch of Evil, Bladerunner, Brick

(books) Red Harvest & Maltese Falcon - Dashiell Hammett, the Talented Mr. Ripley - Patricia Highsmith, The Postman always Rings Twice - James Cain, most stuff by Raymond Chandler

Oh, and just to alert you all, the film Black Dahlia, also based on an Elroy novel, is getting really bad reviews.  This looks like one to miss.
