### Post by kutjara on 2008-03-29
....but here it is, for what it's worth.

A couple of years ago, the first-gen Intel MacBook Pros caused a bit of consternation among users for running CPU temperatures in the 70s. Much of the discussion centered on the way Apple had applied thermal paste, which by all accounts was fairly messy.

The "experts" on the Apple user forums recommended using very small amounts of Arctic Silver, less even than that recommended by either Apple or AS themselves. Just a tiny thin line, was the general consensus. Any more, and the paste would itself absorb heat, trapping it against the CPU, instead of efficiently transferring it to the sink.

A number of users reported 10-15 degree temp drops after following these guidelines.

Perhaps this could be related. Hope it helps.
