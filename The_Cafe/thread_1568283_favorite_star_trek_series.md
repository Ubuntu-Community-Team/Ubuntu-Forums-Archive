### Post by neu5eeCh on 2010-09-05
Never, ever, ever, ever liked TNG.  Reasons:

1.) Turned the bridge of the Enterprise into a cruise ship. Carpeting? I mean... CARPETING?!? OK, listen up folks: Real men don't cruise interstellar space on wall to wall carpeting. No. And what's with all the touch screen technology? No. The whole bridge was just too sanitary, clean and plastic (the whole ship was that way); lost the feeling of anything like a "real" working bridge (think the last Star Trek movie).

2.) Roddenberry's dumb-*ss philosophy (as stated by himself) that human beings had evolved past personal conflict by TNG. The beauty of the first series, such as it is, are the personality conflicts between the different characters - the conniving ambassadors, the mad Admirals, Kirk's obsessive jealousy and pettiness. That's what drama is all about. Take that away and what are we left with? - the crew of TNG - flavorless, bland, predictable, etc.. 

3.) The original series was about Exploration (where no man has gone before). And what was the second series about? - an over-grown Crown Victoria cruising the UFP's seedy neighborhoods. (Keep the immigrants out.)

4.) Riker. (And just what purpose does he serve? None.)

5.) Geordi and those idiotic visors - a character about as exciting as a Mean Spiral Three Subject Ruled Notebook. Who would you rather have at your pool party - Geordi or Scotty? Game over.

6.) The uniforms. Need I say more? Footies anyone?

7.) Communicator badges. When Kirk **flipped** open his communicator... man, that was just coolness. When the TNG cast waved their hands over their breasts... well... um... I won't go there. 

8.) The Klingons. Emasculated.

9.) What's with those 1970's-style-TV-remote phasers? Give me a *real* phaser I can wear like a six shooter - not a fey little remote.

10.) Can't we all just get along? That really seemed to be the motto of TNG. The original series? Hell no. TNG was gutless, bloodless and flavorless.

Did I mention this is all just my opinion?

### Post by ve4cib on 2010-09-05
I'm a fan of Next Gen when it comes to the TV series.  I think the original movies (especially 2 and 6) were the peak of Star Trek, but overall TNG had the best writing, the best stories, the best acting, and the best direction.

DS9 was really just a rip-off of Babylon 5, set in the Star Trek universe.  See JMS's opinions on that particular subject.  B5 did a better job of blending the supernatural/religious elements in with sci-fi than DS9 did, and the wars in B5, while rendered with inferior CGI, I think had more puissance behind them.  B5 also had the distinct advantage of having at least one toilet on the station.

The original series is campy and fun, and very much a social commentary of the 1960s.  Taken in the context surrounding its production it's a very good show.  But sadly it just doesn't stand up to time very well.  But the original films (as I mentioned above) I think were the peak of the franchise.  Seeing Starfleet organized and behaving like a proper interstellar navy, with all the nautical jargon, NCOs running around the ship, and uniforms that actually looked like something a quasi-military organization would invent was pretty refreshing.

Voyager started off with some modicum of promise, but it completely failed to live up to it.  Chakotay and Paris apparently had some kind of history, but they never elaborated on it.  Every other week was "Oh!  We've found a way home!  Harry, don't get excited.  Oh, it's not really a way home.  Sh*t.  Well, maybe next week."  Janeway could never stay consistent on whether or not the Doctor was an independent member of the crew, or if he was a piece of ship's hardware.  The writing was mediocre-to-poor, the acting was okay-ish, and the stories were mostly rehashes of stories already covered in TOS, TNG, or DS9.

I've never really watched much of Enterprise.  I tried getting into it at the beginning, but that theme song just threw me off.  It looks like it has potential though, and I'll reserve more severe criticism about it until after I have a chance to watch the entire series.
