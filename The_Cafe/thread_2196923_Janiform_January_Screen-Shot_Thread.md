### Post by Copper Bezel on 2014-01-04
My Asus Vivobook and my desktop (a generic Gateway hooked up to a 23" Dell HD display.) Thanks to Synergy and Dropbox, the arrangement is like a Xinerama display that I don't have to plug in (and with the advantages of having the "external display" have its own CPU.) I've tweaked the Dash to remove shopping and music results (but not other online sources) and always include my Tomboy notes. Software and configuration between the machines is largely identical (and of course I have Tomboy syncing configured through Dropbox.) It's an absurdly comfortable arrangement. 

[[IMG]https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/17749392/Image%20Hosting/Discussion%20Boards/UF/Screenies/2014-01-04/leftt.png[/IMG]]("https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/17749392/Image%20Hosting/Discussion%20Boards/UF/Screenies/2014-01-04/left.png")  [[IMG]https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/17749392/Image%20Hosting/Discussion%20Boards/UF/Screenies/2014-01-04/rightt.png[/IMG]]("https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/17749392/Image%20Hosting/Discussion%20Boards/UF/Screenies/2014-01-04/right.png")


[[IMG]https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/17749392/Image%20Hosting/Discussion%20Boards/UF/Screenies/2014-01-04/deskt.jpg[/IMG]]("https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/17749392/Image%20Hosting/Discussion%20Boards/UF/Screenies/2014-01-04/desk.jpg")


Ubuntu Unity on both machines with minor tweaks. GTK and window themes are from the [MediterraneanNight Series]("http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/?content=156782") with minor color tweaks; icons are Faenza with Moka actions (which I think blends fairly neatly.)

Having a clipboard manager that updates across both machines thanks to Synergy is absurdly nice as well.

metaf=file browser
