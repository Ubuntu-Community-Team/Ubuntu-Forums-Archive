### Post by Artemis3 on 2011-04-20
A couple of years ago, Floppy (native drive) support in Ubuntu was broken, it appears to be something about udev getting replaced, etc. Thing is, they don't work like they used to with mount or your Desktop Environment (like an USB drive); mtools can still use them fine tho.

Floppies came to replace something much much worse, the magnetic tapes, but of course most ppl wouldn't know :P

Of course the magnetic tapes felt like godsend coming from punched paper/cards, but thats getting really ancient ;)

I am waiting for the replacement of both optical discs and flash memory. Discs are easily scratched, and they self destruct on their own, even faster than floppies...

Flash memory becomes damaged with writes; and some cheap brands get damaged only months after you buy them, for no apparent reason.

At least with a floppy, you knew you had to either put your finger on it, or place it near a strong electromagnetic field (such as your power bar, regulator, whatever).

The replacements i see in the horizon: magnetic ram, actually an old concept, IBM was supposedly refining it, would allow a very fast non volatile memory, which only needs power to change its state.

Non moving optical media, sort of a multilayered disc that won't move... Think of a transparent polymer rectangle with many many many layers for a laser to read or write. You could call them isolinear chips if you like ;)

I didn't hate floppies, and actually apple was the best performer, with Steve Wozniak thinking outside the box; made them much faster and cheaper (drives) than anybody else in the 80ies.
