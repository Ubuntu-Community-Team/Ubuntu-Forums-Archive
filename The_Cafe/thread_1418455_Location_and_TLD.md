### Post by t0p on 2010-02-28
What advantage/disadvantage is there in a resident of the UK choosing a **.org.uk** or **co.uk** TLD rather than **.org** or **.com**?  And looking at it from the other end of the pipe, is there something that stops (or ought to stop) someone who lives in the UK or USA from choosing,say,  **.de** or **.fr**?

### Post by Barrucadu on 2010-02-28
When I see a gTLD website for a business/whatever, I generally assume that the business/whatever is international, whereas if I see it has a ccTLD, I assume it operates in (perhaps not exclusively, but mostly) in one country.

As for stopping people registering foreign ccTLDs, I know some registries require you be an inhabitant of or work in the country to get the domain, but not many do that.

### Post by Bachstelze on 2010-02-28
> **t0p said:**
> And looking at it from the other end of the pipe, is there something that stops (or ought to stop) someone who lives in the UK or USA from choosing,say,  **.de** or **.fr**?

.de or .fr require the Administrative Contact address of the domain to be in the relevant country (Germany or France). I believe most ccTLDs have similar rules.
