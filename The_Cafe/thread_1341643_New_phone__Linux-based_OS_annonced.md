### Post by phrostbyte on 2009-11-29
The OS is called Access Linux, originally made by Palm Inc. for Palm phones and PDAs before they went with WebOS (the OS which runs on Pre and future Palm phones). The UI uses a radical menu layout (is that a pun?). This allows it to be used with one hand. The UI seems to me to be Minority Report-like. This OS is a Japanese/Israeli collaboration.

What's good about it is this:

1. It's one handed operation fits more with how I use a phone, namely with my right hand, using my thumb as the primary way of interacting with the phone.

2. The intuitive menus (such as scrolling to a contact and being able to call or text with the push of a button, something I wish my Samsung Eternity would do) mean that I don't spend as much time pressing on icons and trying to get to functionality, it's at my fingertip.

3. The phone presents you with the things you do most often at the top of the stack of menus. Thus, I don't have to press three buttons to get a text message started. Instead, I flick my thumb once, and I'm set.


That's what excites me about the phone anyways.
