There really isn't one - but that's freedom for ya! Canonical sells DVDs to those with slow/restricted/no internet connections. 

Why does Canonical sell Ubuntu? Why does Red Hat sell its own brand of Linux and yet support the Fedora project? Open-source companies aren't selling the software, they're selling the expertise and customer support they can provide with that software. (After writing this, I see that this is relatively irrelevant to the discussion. I'm not deleting it.)

In the case of just davo11 selling Linux distros to people - why are you selling them? Are you adding your own modifications to those distros? Will you offer personal support for these distros to your customers? Or are you trying to make a quick buck off suckers who don't know any better? (Joking. But seriously, now.) If you're selling to suckers and they find out they could get it for free, they will feel cheated. You may be following the GPL in letter but not spirit, and ultimately (I believe) that hurts what Linux and F/OSS should be all about.
