So, I assume you know what rudiments are, as far as drumming technique go that is one of the keys. There are only 86 of them, hehe that will keep you busy well for the rest of your life really.

Alan Dawson is well known in this area.
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eOX_kXFRqF8](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eOX_kXFRqF8)
[http://www.amazon.com/Drummers-Complete-Vocabulary-Taught-Dawson/dp/0769265243](http://www.amazon.com/Drummers-Complete-Vocabulary-Taught-Dawson/dp/0769265243)

As a Jazz musician, and really many types of genres, and being a multi-instrumentalist I would say keep practising. Don't hesitate on using a metronome, and spending large amounts of time with it. Omar Hakim former drummer for weather report and others has shown grooving with this is possible.

Here is one of my favourite drummers, the only musician to ever audition for Frank Zappa, may he RIP, and play the book perfectly.
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J1xmRQAUOr8](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J1xmRQAUOr8)
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EgRTcVELa4](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EgRTcVELa4)
