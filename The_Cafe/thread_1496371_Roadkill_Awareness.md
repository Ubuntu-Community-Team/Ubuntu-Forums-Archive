### Post by HappinessNow on 2010-05-29
Awesome art project by an artist:

> Description:
Recognizing roadkill, one poor critter at a time....During this time of year there is a higher population of roadkill on our roads. We may find them interesting, we may find them disgusting... but when it comes down to it we don't give them enough recognition. 

My goal is to raise awareness to these animals who are decomposing as we drive by. I will be outlining them with chalk or chalk powder and then constructing a burial mound near them as a reference to ritual symbolism that we associate with death and remembrance. 

Please join me in seeking out and commemorating roadkill... [http://www.facebook.com/group.php?gid=120336697987650&ref=mf](http://www.facebook.com/group.php?gid=120336697987650&ref=mf)

It scares the crap out of me to drive through West Texas at night on the 10 fwy.

You count several roadkill deer each mile.

At the last minute, half of them bolt across the highway in front of you.  Exciting if you're towing heavy.  They aren't big deer, but they are enough to ruin your day.  Haven't hit one yet.  Just raccoons, opossum, coyotes.
