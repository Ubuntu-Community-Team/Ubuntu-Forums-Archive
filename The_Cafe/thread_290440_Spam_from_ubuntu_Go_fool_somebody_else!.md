### Post by I, Brian on 2006-11-01
Just to clarify, the issue raised is with the various email lists run by various linux distros.

When they send out emails to subscribers, unsubscription information isn't clear, and the mailman software can be especially unclear on how to unsubscribe if you do click through to a link.

I've also sent "unsubscribe" replies to various lists, and even though they have been received, acknowledged, and "moderated" - nothing happens.

I think a key point to make is that the distros themselves aren't sending out the emails - my apologies, as the wording is confusing and I accept that - it's that the mailman software is user-unfriendly as used.

I also want to make it clear that the article isn't trying to accuse Ubuntu or others of spamming - however, the failure to easily be removed from email lists *may* be perceived as "spam" is the point I was trying to underline.

I appreciate that there may be some confusion over the matters raised, and I have added a correction - but if anyone feels that further corrections are required, I'm happy to consider them.
