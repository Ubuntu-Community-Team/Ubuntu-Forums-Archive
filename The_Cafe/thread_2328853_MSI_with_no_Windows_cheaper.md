### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-07-01
Final chapter: To report that Xubuntu-core 16.04 LTS works 'out of the box' on the MSI CX62 6QD201xAU laptop. 

M.2 in, created one large EXT4 partition on the 1Tb hard drive called /data and a 2Gb /swap space. Created 20Gb / partition for the Xubuntu-core install on the M.2 and got to it. Installed in literally a couple of minutes after hitting 'Install now' button. Wifi, mouse, keyboard, graphics (on Nouveau by default) all work fine first boot. Additional Drivers gives me the correct NVidia driver for the 940MX GPU and that's it. 

I installed using Legacy mode. No Win, no matter. 

To sum up: Intel i7 6700HQ CPU, Nvidia 940mx GPU, 8Gb DDR4 2144 (I think is the number) RAM, 1Tb 7200 HDD, 120Gb M.2 SATA SSD. (As a side note, somewhere on the MSI website they invite customisations and suggest that if you want a different hardware configuration in one of their machines, ask the shop where you're buying it and they'll be happy to oblige. In my case, this proved to be absolutely correct in the case of the HDD upgrade and adding the M.2.)

I'm happy with it, wife's happy with it, case closed at this end, but fire away if anyone wants to know anything about the machine or install. :)

[PS: Just thought of it. This machine can also take a PCIe M.2 instead of a SATA one. Absurdly fast apparently, but I for one don't have that kind of cash. The price is currently stoopid expensive.]
