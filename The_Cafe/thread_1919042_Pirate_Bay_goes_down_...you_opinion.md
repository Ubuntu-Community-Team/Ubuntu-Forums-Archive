### Post by Docaltmed on 2012-02-02
This thread won't last long, but while it's here...

The technological advances which manifest themselves in services like Pirate Bay are simply evidence that the laws and morals surrounding intellectual property are not keeping up with the technology. Not surprising, this is true for a lot of areas.

I think the smartest way to quelch pirating would be for the content creators to dramatically reconstruct their business models to reflect the new technological reality. I'm quite willing to buy reasonably priced content that I actually own, that I won't lose when I change my hardware, and only from companies that aren't threatening me every time I go online. 

Until such a time as the media companies wake the heck up, the Pirate Bay and similar sites serve as an incredibly useful economic goad to force modernization of a hidebound industry.

And then, there will always be those people for whom the common man's unfettered access to ideas and information is anathema.
