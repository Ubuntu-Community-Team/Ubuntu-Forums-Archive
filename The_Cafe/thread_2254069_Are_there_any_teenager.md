My interest in computers got started back in the mid 1970s. I once even had a job during the school holidays in the computer room of a well know car manufacturer.

Back in those days, although remote terminals were beginning to appear around the place, punched cards, magnetic tapes, and humungous printers were still quite common. Disk drives, when present, were usually physically larger but with a somewhat smaller storage capacity than commonly available for your laptop or PC these days.

I've heard it said (citation and verification needed) that I'm probably holding more computing power in my hand when making a call from my smartphone than was available to NASA during the moonwalk.
