### Post by az on 2007-08-22
GoDaddy is the cheapest with one exception.  Some hosting providers will register one (or more) domain manes for free as long as you use their web hosting.  In the case of hostmonster.com, this is advantageous for me (by a big margin).

That being said, if all you want to do is register a domain name, use GoDaddy.  Be careful about agreeing to anything else from them.  I have heard accounts of how people have accidentally paid for features they did not need (they were misled into clicking for them) and it like pulling teeth to get their money back.

Their linux hosting seems to be fine; apparently it used to not support a lot of features that many php-based web applications needs.  I guess they are making an effort to improve.  

I use their free, ad-supported hosting for http;//rescubuntu.info.  

You get a free hosting package every time you register a domain.  It seems to work fine.
