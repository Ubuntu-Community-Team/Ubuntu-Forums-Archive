The whois that 1fallen provided is for a site thats just 1 letter off, its not fdmedia, its f_***V***_dmedia. Im sure the whois is just as un-interesting (or interesting, depending if your trying to track down the devs of this addon).

As far as what fvdmedia *is*, its nothing harmful (as far as i, and millions of others can tell, theres no such things as 100% security so im adding that). But its an addon, for firefox, chrome, and probably opera, maybe vivaldi, um, pale moon..?.. , waterfox, etc.. Have you ever heard of 'Video Download Helper'? Because its the exact same sort of plugin. When videodownload helper is having problems, eh hem, copying, youtube (eh hem, YOUTUBE only i swear) videos, then FVD will work, and vice versa. Download helper is sometimes breaking when youtube and others update their api, and when that happens, fvd will work. Honestly FVD and Download helper are great addons, tons of people use them. You can download tutorials about Linux from youtube to save/offline viewing, A+/Net+/Sec+ video, of course music  songs/albums, documentaries, literally anything thats able to be played on youtube can be downloaded with these addons.

Oh and its not just youtube either, which leads me to some speculating as to why you didnt KNOW this addons was installed already (not taking into account possible addon devs getting hacked, and turning legit addons malicious for awhile, its rare but that has actually happened). Whats more likely, is either you downloaded the addon at some point, or someone else that used the cpu downloaded the addon.  Maybe this person forgot to tell you, or perhaps they wanted to download videos other than youtube videos, for later, offline 'viewing' (lol). The old downloadhelper addon used to actually give you a giant list of 'compatible' sites with content that was downloadable, and like 90% of them were porn sites. In fact, for someone new to the porn game, you could simply look at downloadhelper and have a giant, curated list of legit porn sites at your finger tips. You know they had actual, working/playing videos, not just links to links to links (oh i remember those days, getting tricked so many times, click here FOR REAL this time to watch, SIKE, another jpeg, another affiliate link, lol).

Anyway, long story short, its a legit addon, go to your browser, go to the 'plugins/addons/extensions' page, and you should see 'Flash Video Downloader'. FVD. Now its crazy, i know, when you type in 'FVD' in the firefox addons market, the addon does not pop up, im not sure why they dont add that tag when thats what they call themselves (maybe dont want to be connected too easly by youtube, theres been drama with both addons and youtube, people be hatin).. but if you actually type in 'flash video downlaoder', you see fvd, as well as several clones.. fvd also has a great speed dial app (this was before the days when FF had speedial), with neat settings that allow you to have entire speed dial folders, and actually hide them and lock with a password if you want (i think we've all wanted the ability to lock some of our bookmarks, now i just export to html file, good enough).. All that said, i dont use the FVD speed dial anymore, because i dont trust the company that much, i think they send data back to their servers about whats in my speed dial, whether that setting is on or off (much like im guessing ghostery does). So your cool knucklehead, its not a security risk, the reason the webpage popped up all by itself is because the addon was updated. Happened to me too. Its a great addon, try it out if you want (always choose one of the bottom options to download, the others will fail), or just remove it if you want. Has nothing to do with a linux vuln, and even if it was malware, as was mentioned, it would be a browser vulnerability, which is cross platform, but not necessarily fatal to the OS itself (unless your running windows of course).

And coldraven, i just have to say, uBlock Origin (the 'origin' part is important) is better than ghostery. It does everything ghostery does, blocks all the same trackers, and no lie, plus more. Its has options that allow other cool stuff too. Now im not saying this is a deal breaker, and this arguement can be debated, but ghostery is indeed an advertising agency. While they do give you the option to turn off the 'ghostery' tracking, if i had to choose out of trusting a advertising company, vs. a very well known and seemingly cool guy (gorhill), i trust the individual human, opposed to the corporation/ad agency, anyday.

Ghostery and ublock (dont forget to install umatrix from gorhill too, its not hard, just click to allow what you want, similar to noscripts) both use 'lists' to block ads/trackers. The main difference is, ghosteries list is proprietary, and uBo's lists are *community maintained*, and *open sourced*, you can view the actual list. Now one could of course ague that since ghosteries lists are indeed proprietary, isnt there a chance ghostery can block a tracker every now and again that ubo misses? yes that argument can be made, sure, but thats like arguing that Microsoft is more secure than an open source and community maintained OS like linux, because Microsoft has 1 or 2 features, that MAYBE, are more secure. That doesnt make the OS as a whole more secure, and in fact, we have no way of knowing if ghosteries lists are more secure or not even *sometimes* because again, proprietary. We would just be giving an ad agency the benefit of the doubt at this point, and it still doesnt win the arguement that ghostery is overall more secure than ublock, it only assumes ghostery is *sometimes* more secure, while ublock is most of the time more secure, because anyone can add to the list when they find and ad, or malicious website.

And if i was a betting man, i would bet that ghosteries 'proprietary' list is nothing more than the *same* lists that uBo uses, just repackaged for ghostery. I cant imagine them letting all that hard work by the community just go to waste, and NOT use the fanboy/easylists/etc. Im almost sure thats all ghostery is doing.

I started off on ghostery, and i loved how there was a little ghost icon on everything it blocked, it made me 'feel' safer, but then i started to use ublock, and the ability to add way more lists, for way more strict blocking, proved to me ublock is without a doubt, better than ghostery.
