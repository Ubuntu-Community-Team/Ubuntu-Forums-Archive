### Post by MikeTheC on 2009-08-05
> **cptrohn said:**
> Why can't I just pay a small monthly fee and subscribe to the channels I want to watch online?

Because cable companies aren't exactly offered cable channels that way. Some channels are known to be less popular, so they're automatically stuck into the bundles with more popular channels. You're basically forced (bot as the cable company and as the end customer) to subsidize the less-popular channels.

Imagine if they let you pick your programming line-up a channel at a time. You know all those channels you want? Well, guess what: those are probably the ones (plus or minus a couple) that all your neighbors want, and everyone else in your city wants, and everyone else in your state, and so on. What do you think would happen to all the channels that got below a certain minimum subscribership? They'd go bust!

So, this has nothing to do with how to serve you best and *everything* to do with propping up junk content channels and feeds into the political machine that *is* Hollywierd.

Mind you, I happen to like the idea of being able to subscribe a channel at a time to just the stuff I want to see. However, I really don't watch TV, so at this point I would be subscribing to exactly 0 channels anyhow. If they gave me free unlimited everything-included cable tv I promise you it wouldn't change my viewing habits one single iota. I just am not interested in watching TV. I have other things I'd rather be doing instead.
