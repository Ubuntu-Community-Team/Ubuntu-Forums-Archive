### Post by Shibblet on 2023-11-29
The older I get, the more I am not interested in most modern released games.  The ones that I like are very few and far between...

I always like to ask this question, because I like to know what parts of games really interest people?

I will go ahead and start:

I'm a huge fan of Skyrim... primarily for the lore and exploration.  Plus, it's one of the most modded games ever, which has really helped extend it's life for over 12 years now.
There is so much depth in the "Elder Scrolls" universe.  There are books in the game that explain the history, myths, legends, and lore that really open up the "wonder" of the world.

I was also talked into playing a game that looked a little childish, called "Hollow Knight."  And I am really glad that I did.  It's very stylistic, from the graphics, the music, the exploration, and, of course, the lore.  Plus, I have never played a game that presented so many different challenges... some are necessary to complete the game, others are just for the pure challenge of it.

I've played a smattering of other games over the years, but since 2010, nothing really "exceptional" besides the above two.

What games did you find "exceptional" in recent years?
