### Post by aysiu on 2007-05-04
I'm glad it didn't bash Ubuntu or idealize it, but I have a bone to pick with this paragraph: > But for someone just getting his feet wet on a new operating system it's what psychology professor Barry Schwartz famously termed " a tyranny of choice."

Want to play music? There are 11 music players to choose from, including Audacious, JuK, and Quark. Need a text editor? Would you prefer GTKedit, Kate, KEdit, KWrite, Leafpad, Mousepad, or xae? All are available with a click or two from the gigantic repository. Are there not as many music players available for Windows? Don't Windows users ever download something besides Windows Media Player (Foobar, WinAmp, iTunes, etc.)?

Ubuntu gets rid of this "tyranny of choice" by installing only one application per task by default. You can always add more players later if you wish, but you aren't bashed over the head with choices. Use the default until you get curious about other choices. I don't see how it could get better than that.
