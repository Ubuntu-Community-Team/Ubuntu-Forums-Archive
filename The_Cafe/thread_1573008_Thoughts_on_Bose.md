### Post by hhh on 2010-09-12
A long response...

You got a good sounding pair of $100 (when they were introduced) computer speakers for free... be happy!

What makes a speaker good involves so many variables it's ridiculous. Let alone what the original source was and how well it was recorded and how good is the player it's being reproduced from and what amp is driving it and what distortion does the amp produce and what's the overtone series of the distortion the amp produces, etc etc etc..., there's what are the speaker drivers (tweeter, mid, woofer, sub) made of (paper, neoprene, Kevlar, silk, aluminum, copper, etc...), how many drivers are there, how are the drivers arranged, where are the crossovers set, is the speaker ported (vented), what's the cabinet made of, what's the design of the interior of the cabinet, what's the resonance of the cabinet and in what frequency range(s) does it resonate and how much, what's the build quality of the cabinet, what's the room they're being listened to in like, where in the room are the speakers located... and on and on and on.

Bose gives the consumer almost no information about their products. Here's what they say about your speakers...
[http://www.bose.com/controller?url=/shop_online/digital_music_systems/computer_speakers/companion_2/index.jsp](http://www.bose.com/controller?url=/shop_online/digital_music_systems/computer_speakers/companion_2/index.jsp)
Your speakers seem to be a ported self-driven 2.0 system... 2 speakers, the speaker has a port to increase bass response through bass-reflex, and there is an amplifier built in to one speaker. Additionally, they are magnetically shielded so they won't mess up a CRT monitor or your hard drive data and they have some digital processing included to adjust the tonal balance depending on what frequencies are going through them and to give greater stereo seperation.

Here's what they don't tell you... how many drivers (probably 2, each speaker has 1 driver to produce all the frequencies it can manage), what are the drivers made of and what size are they (probably a metal alloy at 2 or 2.5" in diameter), what's the frequency range and response of the drivers, what wattage is the amp, what distortion does the amp produce, etc, etc, etc... That's where audiophiles have a problem with Bose... what are you buying for your money? Back when I was more into audiophile equipment (10 years ago) Bose used paper cones in all their stereo speakers, while better companies were using more accurate and/or longer lasting materials like silk, aluminum, neoprene, Kevlar, etc... So you have to look to independent reviewers to help judge Bose products, and those can be hit or miss. Here's a review that seems favorable but doesn't give many technical details and goes off the complete deep end at it's finish by comparing a pair of mass produced $100 computer speakers to an outrageously unaffordable-but-awesome $1,700,000 (yes, 5 zeros) limited production supercar...
[http://www.techtree.com/techtree/jsp/article.jsp?print=1&article_id=110501&cat_id=536](http://www.techtree.com/techtree/jsp/article.jsp?print=1&article_id=110501&cat_id=536)

Ha ha, the lulz.

Here's the best review I could find (though it's a little too enthusiastic, and they imply that these speakers go down to 20Hz which i guaran-freaking-tee you is impossible, 100Hz would be more realistic. They say "Great speakers should be able to hit somewhere in this wide range of sound." Wrong. All speakers hit somewhere in this range of sound, it's the range of human hearing, great speakers should hit the entire range with equal volume and minimal distortion, and for that be prepared to pay thousands of dollars)...
[http://computer-speakers-review.toptenreviews.com/bose-companion-review.html](http://computer-speakers-review.toptenreviews.com/bose-companion-review.html)

Some other reviews...
[http://www.dkszone.net/review-bose-companion-2-series-ii](http://www.dkszone.net/review-bose-companion-2-series-ii)
[http://wirelesssurroundcomputerspeakers.com/bose/companion-2-series-11-multimedia-speaker-system/](http://wirelesssurroundcomputerspeakers.com/bose/companion-2-series-11-multimedia-speaker-system/)

To be fair to Bose and their lack of technical specs, they go by the "it's what the consumer hears" sales approach, and that has some merit (though it also might allow them to overprice a poorly made speaker). My computer speakers are a pair of no-name paper-coned speakers that came with their own tiny paper-coned subwoofer that I paid $30 for brand new at least 8 years ago... and they sound fine. Clean across the range with some decent bass response. I just had a friend over, we were listening to lastFm through my computer and he commented on how good it sounded. Stereo equipment, it's hit or miss.

I heard a 10K Bose installation in the exact same room with a 5-6K Klipsch installation head to head.  The Acoustics in the room were designed to compliment the Bose system.  There was no doubt, in that comparison, the Klipsch sounded so much better, and not just in my opinion, but in the opinion of everyone that was with me at the time.  

I don't claim to be an expert technical sound guy, but I know I am capable of hearing a breadth of detail in various venues and in different types of live and recorded music.  I can't read music, but I can and do play by ear.  I've never had a voice instructor, but I knew how to use octaves in my singing and harmonise before I knew what either of those words really meant.  I couldn't sit here and tell you that Bose from a technical standpoint, is inferior to Klipsch or any other manufacturer for that matter.  

What I can tell you, is that The quality of the end product produced by Bose was in no way near as full and clear in detail than the Klipsch system, and without seeing the products, I would pick the Klipsch all day long for better sounding gear.

Removing a competitor from the picture...  I've gone to a few different Bose theaters in different cities in the U.S. and even it "built for bose" venues, and I have just really not been *that* impressed.  I'm not going to say it sounded horrible, but for the money, I've definitely heard better.

More conjecture:  In my opinion, they've ridden the coattails of key points of innovation they did a long time ago, but they haven't done anything in quite some time thats a knock you on your backside breakthrough.  If you want to carry the pricetag you carry, you better still be leading with innovative designs and products the the little guys will catch up, and not be so little anymore.
