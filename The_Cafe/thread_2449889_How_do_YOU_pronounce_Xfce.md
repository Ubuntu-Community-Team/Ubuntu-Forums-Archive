### Post by Cetus on 2020-09-03
Hi all,

I noticed there was an old thread from 2011 in the archives here about this topic but I wondered if the general lexicon had changed since then.

It's the pronunciation of "Xfce" or "XFCE" as in the desktop environment. Many argued on the old thread that the question itself was beyond ridiculous but just because one set of people call something one thing doesn't mean the general usage of it is the same thing. 

I personally say to myself "ecks-face". For no reason. It's just what I said in my mind the very first time I ever saw "Xfce" written somewhere. However, that I know of, I have never said that out loud to anyone. The Xfce Wikipedia entry clearly says (as the first 8 words of the entire article) "Xfce or XFCE (pronounced as four individual letters)".

Several of the people on the old thread said that for years they had pronounced it "X-force" to themselves, the reason being unclear.

Another person I've heard say it out loud who should probably be considered an authority is one of the developers incorporating Xfce into MX Linux as their default DE. It's an introduction video and he says "X-F-C-E" several times. (all the 4 letters individually, the same as the Wikipedia article says).

Anyway, what're your 2 cents on it?
