### Post by OrangeCrate on 2012-04-26
> **shizz said:**
> This was my first proper release date so I was very excited for the build up. Made myself wait for the final ISO :) **I might alpha and beta test in the future though.**

Good for you. A lot of people around here will tell you to wait for the final, but, I don't buy that. How do you know not to pet the "nice doggie" without getting bit. Trial and error are the only way you're going to learn Linux completely.

Just be sure you backup your Home folder (just your personal stuff, not anything that has a "." in front of it), and prepare yourself to read a lot about what to do (and not to do), and also be prepared to reinstall often at first, until you start to get the dev process down.

To avoid vapor lock, please do be sure you have something that works for everyday use. Either another partition with Ubuntu or Windows, or another computer.

My suggestion is to jump in at the Beta stage, rather than the Alphas. They are not for the weak of heart, or IMHO, users new to the development process.

Good luck, and have fun.
