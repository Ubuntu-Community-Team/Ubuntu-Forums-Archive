### Post by GameGod on 2006-04-29
[QUOTE=vinbuntu]I was just hoping someone would enlighten me on why they mono applications are so incredibly polished after being developed in such a short amount of time!
[/QUOTE]

Since everyone's just been bickering about integration so far, I'll actually answer your question for you. :)

Mono apps end up being polished quickly because C# is a killer programming language. It's got C-like syntax (which most programmers know) without all of the quirks of C/C++. Using C#/.NET makes it very easy to develop a quality application quickly because programmer's don't have to spend time dealing with all sorts of nitty-gritty C problems and headaches. Simply put, it's just much easier to use Mono than C/C++.
(MonoDevelop is also very very good, which isn't surprising since it's written in Mono.) :)

Edit: Oh yeah, and yes, the trend of quality Mono apps being developed WILL continue because C# isn't going to get any less useful. :) (A word on integration: It seems Novell is definitely pushing the integration thing, and it's likely that Novell's OpenSUSE or their SUSE Enterprise Linux will "feel" like it has integrated Mono apps before any other distro... although Ubuntu will reap the benefits as well.)
