### Post by JeffS on 2005-03-03
I've been away from this forum for a while.  I've been a bit hot and cold over Ubuntu.  Right now, I'm red hot, as I've re-installed it (after tyring a bunch of other distros, to varying degree of satisfaction).  I'm having a great time with it again.  It is such a smooth, solid, stable, easy, fast distro.  

Two things I was cold about (which I've gotten over now):

1.  Single disc, with limited software installation:  Generally, I prefer a larger package set, as I don't have the bandwidth at home to apt-get everything I want.  But I have T1 access at work, where I have an extra PC, and I can bring in my laptop, and apt-get everything I want, easily, quickly, and seamlessly.  I just installed KDE easily and quickly, and it worked beautifully.

2.  Dial up set up:   I had to do a lot of tinkering to get this to work to my satisfaction inititially, but I can get it working fine now, now that the learning curve is over.

So once again, I love Ubuntu.   Synaptic and the Ubuntu repositories are such a great feature.  I haven't had such great luck with other Debian based distros.  With those, installing something with apt-get would work maybe 30% of the time.  With Ubuntu/apt-get/Synaptic/Ubunut repositories, installing something works 100% of the time.  No fuss, no muss.

Anyway, now that I'm back using Ubuntu in earnest, I'm back to wanting to frequent the Ubuntu forum.  I've noticed some nice changes.  I like the new user rating system (based on amount of posts), with the coffee cups, and the little phrases.  I just think it's cool and clever and appropriate.  So, good job Ubuntu Forum admins! =D> 

And, oh yeah, great job Ubuntu Devs!  =D> 

Sorry, this is a boring, pointless post/thread.  I just wanted to express my satisfaction with the Ubuntu experience.  :D
