### Post by KCPokes on 2007-12-23
It always depends.  Best case is they can activate the phone, at which time the phone only will work but none of your other features (internet, text messaging, etc..) will work.  This is due to how your phone is provisioned on the network.  IMO, I'd call up Sprint and ask to speak with Tech Support.  Normally the reps in the store aren't going to be able to do much for you, but it doesnt hurt to try if it is convenient.  

This is something that Sprint is trying to get away from with the Open Handset Alliance.
