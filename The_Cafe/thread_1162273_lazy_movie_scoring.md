### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2009-05-17
Another thing you have to realize about guys like Zimmer and Elfman -- it's not just them sitting at a keyboard with some staff paper hammering out the score.  They have teams of composers that they farm things out to.  Now think about the psychology of this.  If I'm mister big-name movie composer, and you're mister no-name guy who depends on me for his big break, if I ask you to help on some kind of epic movie, you're going to give me standard, solid, run-of-the-mill "epic movie" music.  You know if you get too far out, I might think you don't get the genre and not ask you for any more work.  You know also that anything you give me is going to end up with my name stamped on it.  So you're not going to hand out your most inspired piece of work.
