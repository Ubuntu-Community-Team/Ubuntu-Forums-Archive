### Post by HermanAB on 2009-08-02
Concerned about Freedom?  Use the Mandriva Free Linux distribution.

The One and Powerpack versions may have some non-Free stuff, depending on what you install.  Bear in mind that what is Free and what isn't, also depends on the laws of the country you live in. So, with Mandriva you can get the best of all three worlds and your degree of Freedom is in your own hands.

### Post by Firestem4 on 2009-08-03
> **Shibblet said:**
> That is a tad bit confusing.  But if Mandriva One is "free" as in money, why wouldn't it let me install the broadcom wireless driver?

I'll try to explain it differently because i don't know if you grasp the idea behind it yet.

You keep thinking of free in a monetary sense. Non-Free drivers as in you have to pay money.

in the Linux world free takes on more than one meaning when explicitly talking about software. 

There is Free as in Free Beer (Hey bud i'll buy you a drink on me)
And free as in Freedom (Viva la Revolution!)

Free (as in Freedom) means that there are no proprietary drivers/software  or etc on the system (by default, you can always install these later). Proprietary means *not* open source.

Mandriva One includes non-free (freedom) drivers, which are Proprietary (closed-source) (ie: *non*-free (freedom)

None of these cost (as in free-beer).

Hope this helped some.
