### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-05-12
I think F-Spot does the job pretty well, with an importer wizard popping up automatically. It does not demand the user wrap their head around moving or copying files between disks (or between anything), which is a concept I have found people to be terrifyingly confused by.

It also is one of the simpler photo managers out there, with one single technique for managing photos (hierarchical tags!). Conveniently, that single method is powerful enough to do anything the others do, except with a much simpler interface. Quite empowering, actually, while still very natural to use.

As for moving photos around, F-Spot is nice enough to integrate with the rest of the desktop, permitting drag & drop where necessary, which should work in most cases. (Where it does not, File -> Export or Copy Photo Location does...)

Anyway, it could be worth a shot if she hasn't tried it already.
