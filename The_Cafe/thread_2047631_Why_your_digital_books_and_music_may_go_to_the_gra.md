A friend sent this to me yesterday and it really does hit a nerve. Like most I have many digital copy of books, music, apps, etc that I have on my laptop, Nook, Desktop, and server. Now that I turn 74 last Tuesday, it looks like I will take it all to the grave with me. To me this is just not right.

I encourage you to read this article and see what you think about this. 
[http://www.marketwatch.com/story/who-inherits-your-itunes-library-2012-08-23]("http://www.marketwatch.com/story/who-inherits-your-itunes-library-2012-08-23")
Years ago I got into recording music from disc  to tape, but back then it was 8-tracks. I had 10,000 songs and I ended up giving them to a friend. Now legally I can't do that with my digital stuff because of licensing. You don't own it you just have a license to use it.

EDIT: I forgot to mention, if you don't use open source all your programs and OS's will go down the toilet with your music and books.

### Post by cariboo on 2012-08-25
Actually your point about sharing taped music is wrong too. Back then, everyone knew that the quality of taped music was not the same as listening to it directly from vinyl, so nobody really cared if you shared your music or not, and taped music doesn't last forever as tape deteriorates over time, no matter how careful you are in storing it. 

Since music has gone digital, in theory, your copy should sound and be the same quality as the original, this is what originally brought out the licensing issues we are seeing now.
