It was a bit of luck, but I made sure the MIS department knew of my technological improvements (such as taking my job and building an Access database to make everything easier, quicker and accurate).  When the person in the MIS department left, my name came on top of the short list!  And I originally got the job by temping there.

One of my roommates didn't finish college because he was hired in Silicon Valley by a company his senior year and he left.  He's the exception, rather than the norm.

You do have a powerful advantage over some people, though; you know about Open Source.  So many people think they can only get valuable experience from getting a paying job (the 'ol catch-22!).  

Experience trumps education, the problem is when starting out all you have is education and no experience.  Get yourself involved in open source projects and you'll get your experience.

Don't just look at Canonical, too.  Red Hat, despite this economy not being the best, has been doing great and is hiring!  One of their job requirements, or want-to-have experiences, is experience with an open source project!

Novell may be for sale, but nobody knows what's going to happen.  Keep an eye out for them and submitt.  Worst case scenario, they make an offer and you say "No".  Best case scenario, they get bought by somebody who believes in them, and they start hiring and they already have your resume sitting in front of them!
