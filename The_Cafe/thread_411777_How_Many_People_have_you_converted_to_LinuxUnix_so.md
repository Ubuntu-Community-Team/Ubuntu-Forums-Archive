### Post by aurelm on 2007-04-18
The biggest issue with converting people to Linux is that the vast majority of computer users run some flavor of Windows, and the vast majority of them are computer illiterate. Sure, they can turn the computer on, log in, check email, play a game of solitaire, surf the web, write something in Word and then go do something else, but that's pretty low on the scale of computer literacy IMO. Most folks have shortcuts to everything they ever use on a computer on their desktop. People generally seem to hate computers except insofar as they can entertain themselves with them. I know these things because I worked tech support for a while and I've seen more of these people than I care to think about. 

You try to put these people out of your mind, but at some point you need to face the facts: these people are the villagers and you are the rude fisherman. (I just read the FAQ on the beginner's thread the other day. Check it out if you don't know what I'm talking about.) This is their world. Converting them would be a great thing because it would mean software companies would actually give Linux ports a shot, among other perks. Unfortunately, right now they regard Linux in general as a scary thing and an elitist attitude only makes things worse.

To convert these people, there needs to be an effort made to reach out to them on their terms. Check out windows.com and look at how they market to people. Everywhere you look, it's 'which version is right for you?' not 'is Ubuntu right for you?' Microsoft doesn't ask, it tells. People listen, especially when somebody knowledgeable (you) tells them about something they know nothing about (computers). How many times have you seen somebody click those ads that say 'click me'? If it didn't happen, those ads  wouldn't exist. That's step one. 

Step two is to make them comfortable once they get Ubuntu installed. Installation is no problem, but if a special build could be created that streamlines installation even further, that would be ideal. No more questions about how you want your HDD partitioned. Just format the whole thing and install Ubuntu. Once this build is installed, every item that somebody could want to use should be dumped on the desktop as a shortcut. I know, I love my clean, empty desktop too, but most of the users we're talking about here break out in a cold sweat if their desktop is empty. That's my two cents anyway.
