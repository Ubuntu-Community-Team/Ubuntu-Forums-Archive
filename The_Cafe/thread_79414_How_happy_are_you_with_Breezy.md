### Post by Riverside on 2005-10-20
[QUOTE=jbcard]Problems:  - DNS problems with internet[/QUOTE]What problems exactly?  There aren't that many issues that one can experience in terms of DNS, and almost all of those are more likely to be related to the customer facing caching resolvers provided for customer use by one's ISP.  Many (particularly in the case of primarily residentially focused ISPs) caching resolvers provided for customer use by ISPs are under specified and/or overloaded, the ISPs in turn occasionally employ hacks with undesirable side effects (overriding default and specified TTLs in queried external zone files with longer values for example), and the whole thing can quickly become a bit of a mess.

There is thankfully though a remedy for Ubuntu (and other Linux) users, which works very well.  First, install Bind 9 using apt-get install bind9.  Once installed, edit /etc/resolv.conf to read:

nameserver 127.0.0.1

Your machine will then be running its own caching resolver, and will be independent of any caching resolvers supplied by your ISP.  If the caching resolvers supplied for customer use by your ISP are causing the DNS issues that you are experiencing, this should resolve those issues.
