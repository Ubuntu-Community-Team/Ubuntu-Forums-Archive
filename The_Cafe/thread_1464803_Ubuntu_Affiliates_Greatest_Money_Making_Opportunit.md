### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-04-28
Here's what I suggest- buy Ubuntu CDs, merchandise, or donate to the project, if you have some left over from supporting the other projects you explicitly donate to.

This way you can at least help them stay up a little bit. And if this isn't substantial, I think if they just asked people who like them to go to a website with ads, rather than force them to, it would probably bring a lot of people out to support them with a few minutes out of their day.

There are many better ways for them to make money than shoving it down peoples' throats. Ubuntu's done a lot for the image and modern viability of open source, even if it's technically just a fork with updated software. It stands for much more than that, and I'll support them with a few dollars to keep the important work going.

The best way you can support Ubuntu is to buy devices that run it or support/use it in some other explicit way, if you need to get a new computer. Go to Dell's website and buy their selection- it'll expand, show up in stores at lowered prices, and do a lot more than just using Ubuntu at home. This way people will know Ubuntu can make you money, not just make Canonical money, and more people will support Linux. This is already happening, but we can speed it up! Spread the word- don't preach, just show it off a little, think about it with your next hardware purchase.
