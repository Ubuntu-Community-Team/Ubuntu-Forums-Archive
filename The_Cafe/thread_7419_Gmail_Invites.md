Yeah, I was able to keep an address on a mail server at my former school from '88 to '98, when I finally killed it off. Email, USENET, UN*X talk command and zephyrgrams were pretty much the only way to have a useful conversation then so my address was well used. 

It was funny, back then it was illegal in the US to sell individuals Internet access. You got it through your school or if you were very lucky through work. Spam was completely unknown. Once in a rare while someone would post on USENET an advertisement and most likely they'd lose their access the next day. If an admin was too slow to boot the user **and** post an apology it was possible he'd find a few people trying to email him a 2g tar file containing VMS. =) Punishment was swift and harsh.

The Internet has totally changed since then. In some ways for the better (like my 3mbit down access for $40USD a month) and some ways for the worse (spam, trolls, phishing, scams, AOL, free iPod Ponzi schemes, etc.).
