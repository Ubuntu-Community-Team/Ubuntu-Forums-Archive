### Post by aysiu on 2006-12-09
Well, it all depends on just *whom* you're emailing. If you're emailing random people, it's spam.

Then there are what we call at my job (I work in admissions) "prospects," which are people who express a desire to be contacted by certain types of colleges. Finally, there are what we call at my job "inquiries," which are people who express interest in our particular college.

I'm coming at it from an admissions perspective, but it's pretty similar to a business one. For example, you may notice when you download some software or create accounts for banks or businesses, you may have a checkbox asking if you want to receive news about special offers and whatnot. If you check that box, what they subsequently send you is not spam.

If, however, some random company you've never done business with sends you email... that is spam, of course.

### Post by Soarer on 2006-12-10
If you have some budget (doesn't need to be much) and a web site you could try Google Adwords (adwords.google.com). Make sure your site is optimised for your selected keywords first, as this will have an impact on traffic even before you start paying.

If you are a business serving the local community, as opposed to an on-line business serving the world, I would get some leaflets printed and give them out yourself, or put them up in shop windows (after asking permission of course).

Both of these are cheap though they require some effort & fine tuning to get right.

Email marketing requires a list of 'opt-ins' - people who have agreed to accept advertising emails. Many of these are around. Response rates vary wildly though.

### Post by Soarer on 2006-12-10
Yes - whenever people sign up for something on line, there is usually a tick box that allows 'specially selected partners' or somesuch to email to them. These companies will let you, for a fee, email their list.

One magazine I subscribe to here in the UK - Precision Marketing - has lots of companies advertising lists with various demographics. Many magazine publishers will allow you to buy access to their subscriber lists, and on-line newsletters often sell ads in their (free) newsletters.

### Post by Soarer on 2006-12-11
I think it is unreasonable to lump all these email activities together. I get lots of email marketing which I have opted to receive. Much of it provides information I find useful as well as the adverts. True opt-in email must have an unsubscribe link at the bottom as well, which is why a lot of spam copies this requirement.

Most advertising we are exposed to is not opt-in at all - I can't elect to watch TV programs without the adverts, or insist the newspaper or magazine I buy is advert free. These at least cover the cost or subsidise the price of the item. Billboard advertising gives me nothing, but I STILL can't opt out of it.

I think it unreasonable to compare legitimate email marketing (which, incidentally, I don't send, I only receive) with spam. And I would urge you Peter, for the sake of your business, to have a marketing plan, even if it's only 'mention it to everyone I meet, and put it on the Ubuntu forum as requested'.

As the adage has it 'Nothing happens, until something is sold'.

Good luck :)
