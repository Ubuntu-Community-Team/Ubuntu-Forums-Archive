### Post by tgalati4 on 2009-08-12
What you want is automatic power logging.  A google search will turn up whole-house power measurement systems.

Basically, you have to put a loop of wire around both of your power phase legs and the current measured is sent wirelessly to a USB dongle.  Then a simple logging program keeps track of the kilowatts used.

Some folks have used a video camera focused on the meter and using numeral recognition can "read" the power meter.  

There are tutorials for how to read a power meter.  You can simply use a notebook and keep a log.

I have a kill-a-watt power meter that meters one device.  You plug in what you want to measure (120 VAC only) and it integrates the power.
