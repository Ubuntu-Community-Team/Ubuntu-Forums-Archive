### Post by Joeb on 2005-06-25
I was listening to NPR this morning (National Public Radio) and they were talking about the Stanford Forgiveness Project and the need to forgive.

In the course of the discussion, the speaker talked about the African word "Ubuntu" and the circle of friends and how it related to the need to forgive and more importantly, how forgiveness through Ubuntu brings the community together, causes it to be stronger and grow.

Anyway, I know he wasn't talking about linux, but on the otherhand, most of what he was saying is applicable to the community built around Ubuntu (linux).  

I just thought it was interesting to note that in the course of a five minute interview, the word Ubuntu was mentioned about ten times, even if it wasn't in regards to linux.

The relationship between the word Ubuntu and community is poignant, since the purpose of this linux endeavour is to build a community which feeds the distribution, as opposed to other projects which just focus on packages.

It is a different approach to building a linux distribution in that it "enriches the soil" if you will instead of just harvesting it.
