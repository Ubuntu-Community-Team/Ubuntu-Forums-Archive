I wouldn't say fanny packs are controversial :) But you know, whatever. If you like them, wear them. If someone makes fun of you, don't lose your cool, act unfazed and say "What's the big deal? they're useful."

I find that the more intelligent you are, the harder it is to try to "fit in", and the harder you try to fit in, the more miserable you become. So I've resigned myself to relative solitude. I do the things I love to do, and act in the fashion that is most natural to me, and I generally stay away from people that I'm not going to like. I'm comforted by the presence of the few that I do like and depend on, so it's all good in the end. 

I mean, the whole point of life, as I see it, is really to enjoy yourself. It's not about who's the most productive and it's not about reaching some kind of redemption at the end of it. If your idea of lifelong enjoyment is to get stoned out of your mind, then great, have fun doing that. Other people love working with their hands. Some enjoy writing, some enjoy thinking, and some enjoy doing just plain nothing; they gaze at the sky and are filled with contentment. So, you know, do whatever makes  you the happiest. Make sure you stay aware of constraints like money and whatnot, so that your happiness is not suddenly halted due to lack of funding :) So, by this design, it all seems relatively simple to me.
