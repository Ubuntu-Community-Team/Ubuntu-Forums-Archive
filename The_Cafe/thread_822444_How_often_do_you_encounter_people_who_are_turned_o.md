### Post by wdaniels on 2008-06-08
> **WindowsSucks said:**
> While fire and wobbly windows are most definitely eye candy, the cube is not. The cube is a visualization tool that allows true noobs to the general Unix-like world to understand the concept of workspaces.

I don't find it to be of any practical use to put the workspaces on a cube, and I struggle to see how it is really helpful as an aid to visualise and understand the concept of workspaces - I've never come across anybody who can't grasp that idea immediately.

But it was the first clear and impressive demonstration of window compositing in Linux, and one that was uniquely so given the lack of multiple workspaces (as standard anyway) on other platforms, and that way it came to represent this technology in Linux. When the videos of movies playing in real-time folded across the edge of a rotating cube first appeared, a lot of Windows people thought it had been faked :D

I've never met anyone who was not impressed by the desktop cube, and for me that is it's real "practical" function - as a simple demonstration to others that Linux is not just emulating other graphical desktops - it has it's own architecture and special features. This is mostly why I still keep the cube, even though I got bored of it months ago. I love showing Windows users the cube on my 630Mhz 512MB Eee PC when their fancy expensive laptops slow to a crawl at the mere suggestion of enabling Aero.
