### Post by blueturtl on 2010-05-01
Compaq has not been Compaq for a good while.

When shopping for a new computer people have these options:
[LIST]
[*]High power specs
[*]High build quality
[*]Low price
[/LIST]

From the above list, you can normally only have two. If you want the top two, you'll pay a good deal for it. Look at Apples and Lenovos for example. There is a reason those machines don't usually sell for less than a grand (as new anyway).

I realized this when we bought my sister her new Toshiba. It was one of those notebooks you can get for practically the price of a netbook. End result... actually it's a decent machine, but the build quality is a bit questionable. The mouse button gets stuck and the machine will shut down from overheating unless you regularly vacuum clean the entry holes for the CPU fan. My mother-in-law on the other hand has one of those top-of-the-line Toshibas that must have cost closer to two grand when she bought it. It's fast, it's light and you can bet the mouse buttons won't fall off.

You can't really depend on a name alone any more. Few brands hold their own name to such a high regard that they refuse to sell poorer quality in the lower price segment (the alternative is to not sell anything, because you can't compete).
