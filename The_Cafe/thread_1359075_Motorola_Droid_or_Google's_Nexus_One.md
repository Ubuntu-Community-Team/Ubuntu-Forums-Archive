### Post by vgrisham on 2010-02-05
> **vgrisham said:**
> I've just ordered a Droid. I thought about waiting for the Nexus One for Verizon (no T-Mobile where I live), but two things steered me to the Droid. First, I want a physical keyboard and second, the Droid screen is larger (and I loathe having to wear reading glasses to use a phone).

I've had my Droid for four days now and I love it. 

Physically, I like the heft of the phone and the slightly rubberized feel of the back. The screen is big and vivid, and the physical keyboard is really nice. I still don't quite know what possessed Motorola to intrude into the keyboard with the fingerprint reader, but the keyboard is still way better than my old Palm Centro's. 

Android is really intuitive, customizable, fast and stable. The Car Home feature is truly innovative. The turn by turn directions are spot on. The Android Marketplace is teeming with quality apps, free and pay. The music player sounds great, but unlike Apple, there are others that may be installed via Marketplace. Android = choice.

So far I've only found a few annoyances. The onscreen keyboard is going to take some getting used to (or not since I'll probably use the physical keyboard 99% of the time). The default system sounds are dorky, especially the robotic, "DROID" exclamation upon bootup and notifications (remember the Cylons from Battlestar Galactica?), but they are easily replaced. I also don't like the fact that I have to tell the phone to make the SD card available via USB (why else would I dock this with my computer?). 

Overall though, I love the Droid. I'm sure the Nexus One will be cool too. I don't think you can go wrong with Android (well, there is always Motorola's Blur variation).
