### Post by Ewingo401 on 2010-04-09
I've done my share fair share of distro hopping over the last few years.  I always end up back at Ubuntu, but every few months I get bored and want to try something new.  In my travels I've discovered that the only distro that has a "disable tap to click" option by default is Ubuntu.

I personally hate the tap to click option.  I always end up accidentally clicking things when I'm trying to scroll through a webpage, and it's generally a pain.  In every other distro I've tried there has been no default way to disable it...and I've tried many.  Fedora, Sidux, Debian, Mint, PCLOS, Mepis, etc.  Sure, most of them have work arounds to get the job done but a lot of them consist of steps that your average newbie would might be scared to take on, such as editing config files.

Anyway, I just find it interesting that Ubuntu does this where no one else does.  Anyone have any idea why that is?
