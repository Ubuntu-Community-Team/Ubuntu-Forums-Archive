### Post by earthpigg on 2011-04-13
***I LOVE THESE QUESTIONS!***

Geography is defined by lines on maps.

Culture is defined by values, norms, beliefs, and social practices. 

The two have very little in common, except by coincidence.

Something worth noting:

Look at Finland and Japan on a map. From the Human Geography perspective, they have little in common.

Yet, while living in Finland, I heard several Finns tell me of some sort of fuzzy connection to Japan that exists in legend and pre-dates the written word in Nordic Europe. Some spoke of language, some of pop-culture, but all spoke of some connection that any Human Geographer would have immediately discounted.

Just goes to show, both are valuable social sciences, each with its own value.

Who but an Anthropoligist would say that Japanese and Finnish have more in common than Japanese and Hindi? :P

### Post by user1397 on 2011-04-13
Although what earthpigg said is true, if you're looking at the two subjects as college courses they can be strikingly similar.

Cultural anthropology centers around the different cultures around the world, how those cultures evolved, and how different cultures interact with one another.

Human geography focuses a lot on the human aspect of geography, which entails human migration patterns, differing cultures, language migration patterns, etc

They can be quite similar, or quite different.  At my university there is a course titled 'social geography,' a title which can also look similar to the two mentioned before.  This particular one at my university focuses on urban patterns and trends in 20th century USA, so I think they could have used a better title.
