### Post by poster_nutbag on 2005-05-26
The computer industry has always been that way. I remember reading complaints about the amount of movie tie-ins, rehashed sequels and so on way back in 1983!!!

My pet hate - and call it trivial if you will - is economics code in games. It has been universally crap since the dawn of computer games. They all have the damn same cycle:

Money from nowhere -> given to player -> spent and dissapears nowhere

Think Civ 1,2,3 and 4 here. And probably 5,6,7 and 8 as well. You see, the people who develop games like this because THEY DON'T ACTUALLY CARE IF YOU LIKE THE GAME. They are only interested in sales. Hey, you liked the last one, right? So now computers have moved on a bit we can have EVEN MORE EYE CANDY, and keep the same old game logic 'cos artists are cheaper than programmers, right?

I remember CIv3 coming out. Civ2 being my favourite game, I had it lined up for a purchase: especially since all the game mags went 9/10, best Civ ever... etc. What they didn't tell you was that exactly the same tactics in Civ2 worked in Civ3!!!! So, nothing new to learn then. Didn't they damn well test it beforehand?

Sorry about the rant, it's just how I feel. Not that I'm a defeatist: I'm writing my own game, and it can be found at [http://www.spqr-game.co.uk/](http://www.spqr-game.co.uk/). And yes, it will have a *real* economics engine :-)
