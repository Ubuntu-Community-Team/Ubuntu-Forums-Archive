### Post by perspectoff on 2010-12-31
I use Namecheap, which has .info domains for $3/yr or something like that, and .org domains for $10/yr, and I think now has .com for $9/yr.

Don't know how that compares to GoDaddy, but Namecheap allows URL forwarding, Dynamic DNS, and they have always been pretty reliable for me. (I think GoDaddy just dropped their prices ($2/yr for .info, $10/yr for .org, and $12/yr for .com) in order to compete with Namecheap...) GoDaddy uses extensive scripting on their sites, however, which gives my security monitors fits and makes navigation quite slow and tedious. Namecheap's website is much quicker and easier to navigate, and doesn't set off my security censors.

I have no interest in them other than this (and I have 5 URLs registered with them).
