### Post by DoctorMO on 2006-12-20
The BBC have no choice, I know a number of programmers at the BBC from various sides and it's a bone on contention with in the organisation to be honest.

The BBC must provide content to the British public which pays the licence fee, this move will make it more likely the BBC can ask the government to instigate a tv or computer licence instead of just a tv licence which is how the bbc is currently funded.

Now Zudeo has a linux version, the question is do these files work in Linux too? because the BBCs charter explicitly forbids it from entering into agreements which restrict how users can access content such as requiring ms windows or internet explorer. this is why you will find linux help pages on the bbc.

So the likelihood is that the BBC will want a DRM format which works on all platforms and uses torrents. I hope that this is what they have found.

On the other hand BBC Worldwide have no such responsibilities and they may just consider this venture to be non public BBC in which case even british people would have to pay to get this content.
