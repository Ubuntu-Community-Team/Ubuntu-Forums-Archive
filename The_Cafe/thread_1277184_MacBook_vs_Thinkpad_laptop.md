Um. That's the entire point of their business model. They provide the software and hardware together to make a seamless "user experience", so that the user doesn't have to worry about incompatibilities. It's mentioned many times in the "Get A Mac" (aka "Mac vs. PC") ad campaign.

True, much of the lower levels of the userland is pretty similar to the BSD and other various Unixes from whence it came, but the kernel is a heavily modified version of Mach and 4.3BSD's kernel, together with Apple's own I/O Kit. You'll get used to them quickly if you do decide to get the MacBook. If you like OS X, and think it's worth the cost, then go for it. Otherwise, you're probably better off with the Thinkpad.
