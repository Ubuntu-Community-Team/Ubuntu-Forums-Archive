### Post by QIII on 2018-02-11
I have my doubts about curved monitors.  My basic misgiving is that for it to be effective (not distorted in terms of your perception), you have to have your eyes pretty much at the center of the radius of the curve.  Think of IMAX or the big trend towards curved movie theater screens in the 70s.

IMAX makes some people sick if they are not dead center and curved movie screens (for that reason) have largely disappeared.  This seems like a marketing re-hash.

But say you can find that perfect sweet spot in the center of the radius and work at your desk.  It that a good position with regard to your personal visual acuity?

Honestly, I think it's a gimmick.  Give me three flat monitors that I can angle for best viewing at a comfortable distance from my (admittedly tri-focal hobbled) eyes.
