### Post by aysiu on 2006-08-01
I do believe people should be pro-active about solving their own problems, but asking for help can also get you *better* solutions, as you have the input of numerous users and of experienced users who can correct other users.

When I set up my first dual-boot, I tried some complicated guide with using a *dd* command to copy Grub to a floppy and then try to add it manually to my Windows boot.ini file. I was a little annoyed when I found out that installing Grub to the MBR automatically sets the dual boot up for you.

In some ways, I would rather a new user say, "Hey, what's a **good** dual boot guide?" and have me or another forum member point her to one than have her just search and find a crappy one. What bothers me more is the constant handholding--for example, if someone asks a question, you post a link in response, and she doesn't even bother to read the link (and asks you further questions that indicate not having read the link).

If people take the time to post you a link that solves your problem, you should take the time to read that link.
