### Post by mo0nykit on 2009-10-12
Hello!

I'd like to confirm an observation about the Microsoft Windows licensing scheme. At some point, I had planned to purchase a copy of Windows XP Professional. So off I went to some computer shops and asked around. From what I knew, Windows has to be activated over the Internet after installation (and IMHO you can have 3 reformats/installations before the activation process totally brands your copy as illegal or something)

Per my experience with Windows, the computer becomes sluggish over time. So, somehow, you have to reformat your PC regularly (probably every year or so). Well, nobody can be perfect with using registry cleaners, cache cleaners, uninstaller cleaners, etc. So, let's consider it a given that you have to reformat a Windows PC regularly.

At the shop, I asked what might happen if would reformat my PC. "Do I need to reactivate it?" "How many times can I reformat and reinstall Windows while still having my license intact?" The sales staff couldn't give a definitive answer.

So my observation is: **Is Microsoft doing something similar to *selling fattening fastfood and slimming drugs at the same time?***

[LIST=1]
[*]Knowing that Windows has an inherent flaw (you have to reformat regularly)
[*]Requiring that you activate after every re/install
[*]Imposing a limit on the number of times you can re/activate
[/LIST]
Well, that's my observation and opinion. Guys, what do you think? I hope to start a healthy discussion on this :)
