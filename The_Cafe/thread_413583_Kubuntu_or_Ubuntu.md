### Post by canadianwriterman on 2007-04-19
Kubuntu has a KDE GUI with the associated KDE applications, and Ubuntu has a GNOME GUI with its associated applications. They differ in look and feel and the availability of system settings (KDE offering a somewhat more complete set of settings). As far as the applications so, some people feel that some KDE applications are more powerful than those that come with GNOME. That said, I am an GNOME user and prefer it. It really boils down to personal preferences and I would suggest trying each and seeing which one you like better. Hope that helps.

### Post by pppetter on 2007-04-19
First of all, they look different. They also use a different set of standard applications(the applications that comes with the CD - you can later on install apps used in the other distros). 

Take a look at some screenshots, that's one way to start. Then maybe you should download the corresponding LiveCDs and test the differences yourself, so you have an idea of what you get before you install.

It's really a matter of taste. I have both kubuntu and ubuntu installed on my computer. and I personally like ubuntu better.
