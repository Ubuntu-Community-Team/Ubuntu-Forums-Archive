### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-02-20
As a general rule, you should only put the PC on the UPS and the rest on a power surge strip.  Your PC can get by with 500 VA (watts).  With everything on an UPS, you will probably need 1,200 to 1,500 VA (watts).  The challenge is that the extra equipment listed has a surge when powered on, but uses much less power once running--it's these other devices that make the UPS shutdown prematurely--when the goal is to protect the hard disk on the PC.

The printer and TV use a lot of power, yet either can be protected with a surge protector strip.  It makes little sense to compromise UPS operation by placing your printer and TV on the same UPS as your PC.
