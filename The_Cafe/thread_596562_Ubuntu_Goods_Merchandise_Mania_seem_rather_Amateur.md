### Post by OzzyFrank on 2007-10-29
I have to say, I've never seen an online company as pathetic as the one handling Ubuntu's official merchandise, Merchandise Mania in the UK. OK, so maybe I should have enquired when the 2nd confirmation email didn't show up "shortly", but after ordering and receiving the first one, I assumed all was OK. Anyway, 3 weeks later I decided it didn't look like anything was happening, even though I had the amount deducted from my credit card pretty much instantly.

So it turns out that while my shopping cart had been processed and the total amount deducted from my credit card, they had not received my order. Which is weird as my first confirmation listed the goods as well as acknowledged payment. They've refunded my payment, but this has cost me a few bucks in credit card fees and conversion from AU$.

I don't mind starting all over again (even though it would be a bit of a pain, to tell you the truth), but if it means losing more in fees and basically with no other outcome but wasting my time, I have to admit I could do without it. I'd love to spread the word more by being in public with my Ubuntu apparel, and to of course help its makers with the commission they'd receive, but now admit I probably won't be going back. As one can see, this means less for Canonical/Ubuntu, in money and exposure, which is unfortunate.

### Post by OzzyFrank on 2007-11-16
I ended up getting a reply eventually and it was that I should let the web pages finish loading to ensure that the order goes through (which I assume I had done the first time, since the company that billed me on their behalf sent a receipt, hehe). Anyway, decided to try again, and at first looked like I was gonna have the same hassle, as the 2nd confirmation email is supposed to come "very shortly" but actually took a few hours.

Delivery was pretty quick from the UK to Australia. The merchandise looks good enough. The backpack/day-bag isn't too flimsy, and the logo is embroidered (likewise on the baseball cap, which seems to be fairly good quality). The cup/mug is of medium quality/thickness, but mine came with a chip which had obviously happened before transit. I emailed them about this and the reply was a confirmation that a replacement mug was being shipped.

So, I would recommend this company and their Ubuntu merchandise, but would of course suggest that if you haven't got a 2nd confirmation email within a day of buying, email Merchandise Mania and tell them.
