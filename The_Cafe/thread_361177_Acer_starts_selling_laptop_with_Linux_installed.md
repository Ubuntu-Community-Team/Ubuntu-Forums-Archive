### Post by Onyros on 2007-02-14
I wouldn't advise anyone on buying an Acer laptop... They've turned into quite problematic machines, and I've heard too many a horror story regarding their laptops and, especially, their customer support (after the problems arrive, let's just say they won't work too hard on solving them; unfortunately they're not few and far between).

I think that with the advent of Vista, if they want to sell lower-specced laptops with good, recent OS' they'd rather turn to Linux than to old XP. And that's where we can get some market momentum going on.

I've had a lot of people asking me whether they should get Vista, some people with laptops that aren't even one year old, and I always tell them that rather than installing a crippled Vista, which won't work well to start with, and won't work well with their hardware because it's not powerful enough, they should try Linux. I've offered to set up a few, and I've had great success.

As a final OT remark: as these are mostly people that work in a corporate environment, you wouldn't imagine which has been the killer-app that has tilted them definitively towards Linux (Ubuntu, in particular): EVOLUTION! They love it. "It's like Outlook, just better". Go figure...
