### Post by handy on 2010-02-28
Cary a harp in your pocket always, when no one is around play it.  The more you do it the better it gets.

You should start to have a few riffs that you can play; as time goes by you keep on accumulating new ones.

When you get to the point that you want to start playing some with friends who play guitar, get them not to change key in a piece, & to play simple chord progressions.  In my day, this meant that Bob Dylan, Neil Young, America & the like were some of the most likely candidates.

When you want the blues sound, you need to use what is called the crossharp technique; So if the song is in A major, you count up 4 letters (inclusive) & you play in D major. (Actually when you are using the blues scale it is the Pentatonic (5 note scale.)

If/when you get to be really good at playing the harp you can have 3 or 4 harps in your hand & be switching between them in the same piece of music that is NOT changing keys, as there are multiple crossharp positions, where again you just count up another 4 key letters. Of course, for each crossharp position you extend into, there are a more notes that you can not play if you want to sound good. ;)

Anyway, I expect that I have given you far more than you want to hear.

The bottom line is, put in LOTS of time, when no one else can hear you. The time will come when you can play along with recordings which can be a great deal of fun, & also continues your preparation to playing with other live musicians.

I am an improviser, which means I make it up as I go along, which is a great way to play, as when you are good enough at it, you can't make a mistake. :D

Have fun. :)
