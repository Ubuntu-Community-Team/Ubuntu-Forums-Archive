### Post by Paqman on 2010-10-06
Poor fault reporting.

I'm an engineer for a train company. I get fault reports through for "door trouble", with no further info. What sort of door? A passenger door? Gangway door? Cab door? Does it not open? Not close? Has it caught fire and fallen off? Without a decent fault report I have to send a bloke to check every door on the whole train, which is a waste of time and often results in them not finding the fault. Which means delays and red faces when it fails again. Argh!

It's usually drivers and guards that report faults to us, but if you ever want to report a fault as a passenger, give the train company the vehicle number you were sitting in (trains in the UK usually have this printed on the wall at the end of the saloon, it's a five digit number) and as much detail as possible, in writing. Without this the chances of it getting fixed are low.

For this reason I always put as much detail on Ubuntu bug reports as I can. Too much info doesn't affect anyone's ability to respond effectively, too little info does.
