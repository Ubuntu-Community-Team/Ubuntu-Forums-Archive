### Post by daynah on 2007-01-30
For real. If you're a geek, it may seem odd to do something weird like get out of the house and take a walk or just go somewhere new. But if you have a laptop, why not go do your computing at a park, in the sun? Or, find some hole in the wall indie-hippie computer coffee place to do your computing. You'll be doing something you're used to, but be pushing your comfort zone some, ya?

Also, you could volunteer. There are a lot of schools that would love someone who's knowledgable in technology to help teach it. Yeah, you'd be teaching Word probably. But maybe it'd be to an after school program to a poor school that only has one set of 15 computers, and that's the only time those kids are gonna get to use them, so let's not get  picky. Word it's exactly the program they need to learn to be able to check in the little box in their future job application... if they can't, they may not have a job in the future. You have plenty of skills to volunteer and you can be as fickle in your time while volunteering as you'd like.
