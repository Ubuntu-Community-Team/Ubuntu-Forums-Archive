### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-08-18
Depends why you want a different distro.

Mepis, Mint, Fedora, Suse, Mandriva and PCLinuxOS will give you a similar experience to Ubuntu - all are fully featured, very accessible for newbies and come with heavy-weight apps and integrated desktop environments. The differences lie in package management and utilities (mainly).  Rav Tux's OZ distros are also nice and fit well with these heavyweights.

For something different, especially faster and lighter, I like Antix (Mepis based), Zenwalk (Slackware based) and Damn Small Linux (Debian-based).  

Test yourself and amaze your friends with eLive.

March Linux dares you to learn Linux.

If you want bleeding edge or specialist stuff, go Sidux, Sabayon or Ubuntu Studio.

If you wish to be a Linux God, its slackware, arch or gentoo.

Check the link in my sig for info and links on these and more.

### Post by bonzodog on 2007-08-19
It really does depend what you want out of your distro install.

Do you want some thing where everything Just Works(tm)?

**OR** do you want to *learn* Linux at its core, learn how it really works, have the confidence to rebuild a broken system without a major re-install, knowing full well that you can confidently handle the system in the console mode with no X for a few hours?

If you want the first choice, go for PCLOS, Mepis, Mandriva, Linspire, or Fedora Core.

For the second choice, try Slackware, Zenwalk, Arch Linux, Gentoo, Debian, or even something like Crux Linux.
