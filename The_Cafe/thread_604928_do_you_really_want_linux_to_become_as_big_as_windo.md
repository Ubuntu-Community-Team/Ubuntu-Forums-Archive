### Post by svtfmook on 2007-11-06
think about it.

if a linux distro was to become as widely used as windows, i guarantee that the open source world would disappear.

why?

#1. developement.  why has ATI spent a total of 10 minutes developing drivers for linux?  because there's no monetary retribution in it for them.  and for an OS to become widely used, it has to be supported to the fullest.  which means everyone would have to spend big dollars on development to ensure full compatibility.

#2, support.  bug tracking and support for millions will not be free.

#3, advertising.  you can't sell something that no one knows about.

i do believe that more systems need to be sold OS free, but then companies like HP, compaq, toshiba, sony, etc, wouldn't make their kickbacks from companies like AOL, compuserve, netzero, etc for bloating their OS's with those company's advertising within the system.  but still, an OS free system would cost less for the consumer and the manufacturer and give the consumer a choice.

### Post by RebounD11 on 2007-11-06
I'll put in my 2 cents, since I am a 100% Linux user.

I liked the idea of Linux, free software, community, sharing ... and every other thing that Linux and Open Source fight for in the software world. I've seen it grow to the point where it could challenge the monopolistic Microsoft or the ever-more-bragged-about Mac OS X. The Linux communities have gained more and more members lately, some who made the switch because of certain frustration (my case, frustration meaning viruses and lack of money to use 100% legal software) or just out of curiosity (example some friends of mine after seeing Compiz Fusion clips from YouTube). Anyway more users implies more potential software buyers which attracts developers to the Linux area (I personally don't think that people use Linux just because it's free or that Linux users don't want to pay for certain softwares if they're worth the effort). And if developers start giving Linux as much attention as they give Windows, and Linux keeps improving as fast as it has in the last couple of years (and I don't doubt it will) then we might live to see the end of the monopoly era (and I don;t mean Monopoly the game :D) and the beginning of a great competition (hopefully ,and pretty much wishfully, a fair one) in the OS world which could only benefit the users (that's us).

Personally I'd like every one of the major OS (Windows, Mac, Linux, BSD etc) to have a pretty much equal market share so that the developers can't choose which to have a better support, and the OS development to be aimed at stability, security and interoperability be it an Open Source  or Non-Open Source OS.

PS: Choice is a basic freedom, and should be so in the software world, this being said I could add Windows is not "evil" or "wrong", it's just another choice, MS policy of imposing it is wrong, and the way they do things is not only bad for the users, but it's bad for them too. IMO if MS would manage to get Windows on every computer in the world the OS development would stop and no real or significant progress would be made, while the Open Source has a philosophy of constantly improving the quality of software through personal and joint efforts from the community and their members.

EDIT: I guess my 2 cents where a little more than I've expected when I started writing :D
