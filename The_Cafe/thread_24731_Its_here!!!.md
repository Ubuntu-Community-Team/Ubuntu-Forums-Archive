### Post by jase on 2005-04-08
It's been a pretty happy day here, too.

I've been suffering with Windows for a while on my old Dell Inspiron 2650 laptop.  I use and love Gentoo on my desktop at home (and a Mac Mini too).  But I dreaded trying to configure Gentoo on a laptop.  The laptop gets used mostly at work, so I needed a distro that "just works", rather than a project and hobby like Gentoo.

Based on the positive reviews, I figured I'd give Hoary a shot.

Wow.  It "just works"!  My wireless network works.  My external USB burner works.  My digital camera works.  Speedstep throttling on my P4-M works.  All stuff I dreaded trying to configure.

And even though there's been some gripes about mp3 support not working by default, there was nothing to adding it.  It's a complete non-issue.  Piece of cake.  

If anything, it was TOO easy.  I feel like I cheated.  It's supposed to be more work!  I'm supposed to sweat a little...  struggle a little!

It's not often the hype is justified, but this time it is.  This is a heck of a nice distro.  I'd like to give a huge pat on the back to those who worked on it!
