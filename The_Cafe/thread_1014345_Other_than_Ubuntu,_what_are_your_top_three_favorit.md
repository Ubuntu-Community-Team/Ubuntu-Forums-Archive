### Post by ubi-wan on 2009-02-04
As far as the mainstream is concerned:
[LIST]
[*][Vim]("http://www.vim.org/"), the devil of a fellow's editor, being the same in the command line as in any graphical user interface, be it Ubuntu or Windows (for the latter, it even serves as a replacement for sed and awk);
[*][Open Office]("http://www.openoffice.org/"), if I need to do office work, again, on any platform;
[*][The GIMP]("http://www.gimp.org/") which can be considered as a capable replacement for Photoshop, allowing to even use many plug-ins of the latter.
[/LIST]
Besides, the following applications are really handy for everyday's usage:
[LIST]
[*][Rubrica]("http://rubrica.berlios.de/"), a contact database for Gnome which offers a pleasant interface by allowing you to initiate by a simple mouse click a contact, be it of type email, phone, chat, or messaging;
[*][Sunbird calendar]("http://www.mozilla.org/projects/calendar/sunbird.html"), a less known program from the Mozilla suite, which I find nevertheless very useful;
[*][GQview]("http://gqview.sourceforge.net/"), an image browser that, with the help of a couple of bash scripts, outperforms F-Spot and iPhoto altogether.
[/LIST]
