### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-02-26
AMD is aware there are issues with it's (closed/locked down) drivers.

Consumers and business users in the Linux world have complained/requested for AMD to open their drivers so Canonical and the other companies/communities can debug and improve the code.

Looks like they finally got through:

Per Ars Technica article on 12/15/2015:    

"AMD is today announcing GPUOpen, a comparable set of tools to GameWorks"

Further . . . 

"GPUOpen will, when it is published in January, be open source. AMD will  use the permissive MIT license, allowing GPUOpen code to be used without  any practical restriction in both open and closed source applications,  and will publish all code on GitHub."

Now . . the GPUOpen drivers will take care of the kind of issues the OP (A.R.B.) complained about (and falsely attributed to Linux versus the Management & Engineers at AMD).    But, it won't "compete" with the still-closed source higher end drivers that will drive high-end gaming graphics (understandable).
