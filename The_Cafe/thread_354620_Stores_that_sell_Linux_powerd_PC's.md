### Post by adamklempner on 2007-02-07
Linspire has a pretty good locater for companies that sell Linspire stuff.  Some of these companies also sell other distro's too:

[http://www.linspire.com/featured_partner/featured_partner.php?](http://www.linspire.com/featured_partner/featured_partner.php?)

If you happen to be interested in Linspire computers (probably not,  but bear with me) you might be surprised to know that you can pick them up at big stores like:

[Sears]("http://www.sears.com/sr/javasr/search.do?BV_UseBVCookie=Yes&keyword=linspire&vertical=ELEC")
[Walmart]("http://www.walmart.com/catalog/product.do?product_id=3762913")
[TigerDirect]("http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/searchtools/item-Details.asp?EdpNo=1511696&sku=N52-3038")

Also, [http://www.koobox.com/](http://www.koobox.com/) makes some cute systems.

While Linspire may not be the best Linux distro for everyone, they have made some pretty good progress in getting Linux based computers on the shelves of some of the larger retail outlets.  Hopefully this at least helps pave the way for other Linux distro's to get on the market.

A year ago or so I played with some Linspire PC's on display in MicroCenter in Boston.  I think they were made by Systemax, were very cheap, and worked reasonably well.  The salesman there new next to nothing about Linux other than you don't get viruses and you can't run windows programs.  I played it dumb just to hear what he had to say, and it wasn't much... lol.
