### Post by Vadi on 2007-09-06
Hi all,

This idea just spawned to me today as I was thinking of how, well, inefficient the linux counter website is. Because far from everybody registers themselves there (I did, yep)... fast forwarding half-hour ahead, I got this idea:

What if we figured out the "approximate", because it's still really hard to get the real figure, percentage of linux machines? I have a statscounter.com tracker on my website, that tells me everything from what search engine was used to reach the site / what resolution it was viewed at / what OS viewed it. The last part is the important one, since it sort of gives a rough idea.

So in this thread, if you own a site and have a stats counter in it, feel free to post the numbers (just for curiosity purposes, nothing else), and I'll keep a tally at this post of the total numbers.

```
**Total numbers % percentage so far:**
*OS name*               *# of OS's*          *total %*
Windows XP/2k/...         1,098,987       ~76.1%
Windows Vista             18,766           ~1.3%
Linux                     15,078           ~1.0%
Mac OS                    145,763         ~10.1%
Unknown                   164,026         ~11.4%
  ---
Total                     1,442,620

Data last updated: 10 Sept, '07, per post #22.

```

Note: All windows OS's below xp are counted in the xp/2k/... category, SunOS and others are counted as "unknown".
