### Post by nothingspecial on 2008-11-24

Well if your going to get any more my recommendation would be Sliding Gliding Worlds - 
from back in the days when they used to travel the free festival circuit and finance themselves selling cassettes (remember them) of their self recorded music. Similar philosophy to Ubuntu - Anyone could turn up and listen to them play for free but you could contribute if you wanted.
 
It`s a nice mixture of trippy tracks (Sliding And Gliding/It`s A Hup Ho World), multicultural flavours (Mae Hong Song/The Green Island), Reggae (The Dusty Pouch) and other Ozric Weirdness. Oh yeah and the classic rocker Kick Muck.

I`ve shared a cigarette;) around the campfire with them on a couple of occassions. Really nice guys who cared about what you thought of their music.  Of course live their tunes were totally different because every tune is an improvisation around a theme (as are the recorded versions) so you never hear the same thing twice.

There`s no one really similar although you could try Eat Static who are an offshoot of the band but more dance music orientated and completely electronic (I think). And there`s always the mighty Hawkwind but they`re a different thing all together (same difference if you know what I mean)

All this being said, I`ve no idea if they`re any good now or even if they have the same line up. My wife and I are all grown up now and have kids and a mortgage and all that sort of stuff so we can`t just decide to go and watch some bands in a field in Shropshire any time we feel like it, although we do sometimes make planned excursions.
