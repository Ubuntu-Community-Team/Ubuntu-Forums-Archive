### Post by aysiu on 2010-11-29
Clothing is communication, and like all forms of communication is based on consensus of meaning.

I think the point about con artists wearing suits does not invalidate the value of suits any more than con artists lying invalidates truth. In fact, con artists feeling the need to wear suits to con people only reinforces the notion that suits convey seriousness of purpose.

If a con artist can say "I love you" and really mean "I don't care anything for you," does that then mean "I love you" can never truly be meant sincerely?

Same deal with suits. Just because someone can wear a suit as a means of trickery doesn't mean suits suddenly can no longer show respect, formality, or seriousness.

There are very few funerals people wear shorts and tie-dye t-shirts to, and that's because suits still convey seriousness and formality.

First impressions matter. That's part of life. It's the reason a loud fart on a first date is likely to be taken differently from the same loud fart twenty years into your marriage.
