### Post by Happy_Man on 2007-11-25
Unfortunately, yes, I do think a lot of hopes will be dashed and angry blog posts written when KDE4 final comes out. But that's only because the majority of users don't know wo the KDE development cycle works. 

When they say "alpha", they mean "uh.... look, there's something on the screen now. It may or may not do stuff, but the fact is, our code at least *works* on some fundamental level. Thank God."

When they say "beta", they mean "alpha". They mean to say that "It's somewhat getting close to not crashing now. Yay."

When they say "RC", they mean "beta." They mean to say that "look, it's finally coming together as a whole now. It's getting there. Some stuff may not work, but most of it does."

And when KDE 4.0 Final comes out, it's an RC. Most of it is production-stable, but there are some things that will be fixed and polished over the course of 4.0 --> 4.1. I'm thinking big things like Plasma.

So... when the good folks developing KDE 4 finally release KDE 4.1, *that's* when everyone should start using it, because then the magic will finally be happening. 

Just saying.
