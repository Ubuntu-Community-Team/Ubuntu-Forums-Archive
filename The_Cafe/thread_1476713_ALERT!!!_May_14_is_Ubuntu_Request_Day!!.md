### Post by undecim on 2010-05-08
If this is all done on one day, I don't think it will make much of a difference. Think about it:

> Sales Person: 3 different people came in today asking about something called lee-nucks. Do you have any idea what that is?
Manager: Probably just some kids joking around.

Rather than this, every time you buy a computer, do some shopping in the store and ask the sales person if they have any laptops that are filly Linux compatable. When they say they don't know, ask if there is someone who does. Stress the importance of Linux compatability, give them your top 3 decsion-making factors in buying a computer with Linux compatabilty as #1.

Bring a pen and paper (or use a cell phone) to make a note of a model number of a computer that looks like one you want and tell them you may be back to buy it after you've checked to see if it works with Linux.

You can also ask them about rebates for the softare that come preinstalled.

EDIT: You can even bring in a live CD and ask if you can test it. I don't think a lot of stores will want you messing around with their computers, but if you can explain that it doesn't mess with the hard drive and they believe you, you can spark the sales person's interest in Ubuntu.
