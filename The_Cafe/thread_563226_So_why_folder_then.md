### Post by popch on 2007-09-30
It's either about the user paradigm or just plain sloth.

The authors of the CLI based OS were thinking of a list of entries which made finding files easier (or possible).

The authors of at least one of the OSs hidden behind a  GUI thought that this concept was too challenging for the user (if they thought at all), so they pretended that directories 'contained' files which then became some kind of containers. Be thankful they didn't name them 'envelope', 'cassette', 'cabinet', 'box' or some such.

It does not appear to matter, one way or the other. 

Until the developers started to get really creative and invented ***soups*** containing ***folders.***
