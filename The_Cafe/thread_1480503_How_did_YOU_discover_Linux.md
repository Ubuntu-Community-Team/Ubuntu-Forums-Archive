### Post by silkworm2.5 on 2010-05-11
So, Just a little thread for you to put your Linux story into.  If I can figure out how, I will place a poll here for us all to vote in. I'd be interested to see how many f us found it because windows F****d up:)

So here's my story.
I went out and bought myself an issue of PC world magazine one day because of the DVD which came with it with over 100 programs. Among these were 3 flavours of Linux. Kubuntu, DSL, and... some other one that was about 20 Meg.
Interested, I tried burning Kubuntu to a CD and tried it without installation.
After about 20 minutes of using the test and seeing all the apps, an customizability of it, I decided to install it to my secondary HDD. That was a mistake of my own. I didn't have the internet back then and so I could not get any software for it. So, I left it for about a year or two.I only tried using it again to install on my memory stick, but that failed and had a long story surrounding that which I wont bore you with.
Soon after getting a school issue netbook and bypassing the extremely hypersensitive web filter, windows failed. With my un-backed up school work and more than 10GB's of music that I no longer had CD's for, I needed a way to access the HDD short of returning it to have the disk formatted. (The problem was that after the log-in prompt, nothing would open. Not even Windows explorer or task manager.)
I remembered about Parted Magic which I had used to restore my memory stick after attempting to install Kubuntu to it for a portable OS.
I dug out the disc and wired up my USB CD Drive. Booting was no problem and I was stunned by the speed, features, and usability of Linux once again. I moved all my files to a USB drive and onto my dads computer.
After that, I returned it to the school Tech's and they formatted and re imaged it (or as they said "Fixed" it) after 2 months. After its return, I started looking around on 'stumble upon' for the topic Linux. Soon after I got the Ubuntu netbook remix installed as a Wubi for my netbook.
So there it is. My joyous journey from windows to Linux which was caused mainly by windows dying.
So, there is my story. What's yours?

### Post by AnneTanne on 2010-05-11
Years ago, my husband (who is teaching 'beginning computer and internet' to elderly people) was doing a course himself about webdesign (with Frontpage :confused:).
And one day, one of my collegues gave me a book about XHTML, and told me to give it to my husband... who just looked at the 'frontpage', and gave the book back to me.
And since I hadn't an unread novel at hand, I gave the book a second glance, and than a third and... Before the evening was over, the first ideas for a website were emerging.

And then, that collegue of mine then showed me something even more interesting... His PC had not Windows installed, but that geeky thing called Linux, which I thought to consist only of a fullscreen terminalwindow, and it didn't!
Instead is had a largely customizable, slick looking graphical interface.

So he sent me home with the latest version of SuSE (must have been 6 or 7 something).  I installed the OS in dual boot, and was an almost immediate convert.
But a few days later, I still don't know how it happened, something went wrong, and the PC didn't boot anymore....
I had already found a LUG, but those extremely friendly people weren't able to help me out either.
So at the end I decided to do a fresh install of Windows and then Linux.  (After having found out that I at least could make back-ups of both our files.... with a Linux live-CD! That was the most amazing thing for my husband: that Linux could 'see' the Windows partitions, and Win seemed completely blind to Linux.)

And it was really hilarious...  It took me a few hours to have a 'naked' Windows installation, without additional software, without the drivers we needed.... And after less than two hours I had a fully equipped Linux install.
It has taken me a few days more to find all the software and drivers hubby needed...

But I must admit... Somewhere in 2006-2007 I think, I had several succeeding versions of SuSE that were not very good: even with the help of some 100% geeks, I couldn't get a single sound out of my computer, and then I have abandoned Linux for some time.

But a few years ago, I had some problems with my hosting provider. And at that very moment, a reader of my blog let me know he would like to offer me webhosting for free, if I would just put a small link to his company in my sidebar.  That company offers - apart from 'green hosting' all kinds of ubuntu-based computer-solutions.
And after hearing him about Ubuntu, I was ready to give Linux  another try, although most people of the aforementioned LUG told me that Ubuntu was just a Linux-distro for people who can't think themselves.
And they are right, I'm not of the kind that compiles her applications all from source, only do that when I don't find another way to obtain the program...

But I do use linux, and hubby and I are raising a child that is aware that Windows is far from the only OS in the world.  (Even better: he prefers the Ubuntu laptop over the Windows PC).


And using Linux has another advantage: When hubby runs into a computerproblem, he always asks me for help... But I'm so tired of ttrying to fix Windows problems... And after so many Linux years, I can say now: sorry, but don't know enough about that machine...
