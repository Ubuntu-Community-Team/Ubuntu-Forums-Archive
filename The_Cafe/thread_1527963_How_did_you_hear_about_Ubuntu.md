### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2010-07-10
I learned about Ubuntu from the Internet.

I started using computers in 1974. Most computers ran UNIX or the vendor's own version of the UNIX OS. DOS was released on the IBM PC in 1981. Microsoft didn't release Windows 1.0 until 1985. Apple had the Apple I in 1976 and the Apple II in 1977.  For a period of time I actually saw more Apple II computers in the workplace than Microsoft Windows based PCs. However that changed with the introduction of IBM PC &#8220;clones&#8221; that could run Microsoft DOS or Windows. The &#8220;clones&#8221; were much cheaper than the IBM product, or Apple's computers.

In the early days of computing, people wrote software and freely shared it with everyone. We didn't have the WWW, at first. We used Bulletin Boards with dial-up modems :). We had no concept of patents or &#8220;Intellectual Property&#8221;. When that concept arose, I started looking for &#8220;Freeware&#8221; and &#8220;Shareware&#8221; programs. In the process I found Debian, SuSE, Mandrake, and RedHat. These quickly exploded into an every increasing number of distros.

I am attracted to the FOSS community; because, that is where the roots of the computing infrastructure originated. Companies want to patent and protect their Intellectual Property; however, that is not how it all got started.
