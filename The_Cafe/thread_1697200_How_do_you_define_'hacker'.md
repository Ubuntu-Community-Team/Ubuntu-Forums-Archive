### Post by red_Marvin on 2011-02-28
When something breaks, a hacker will try to fix it instead of buying new.
When a hacker needs something s/he will try to build it or improve something to fit the need instead of directly buying it.
Not (only) because it is an economical choice (depending on the components involved it may be not), but because it is the right thing to do.

Because working technology, or technology that could be working, is a terrible thing to waste.

(I would also restrict hacking to the digital electronics and/or software world, eg fixing your car instead of buying a new one is not so much hacking imo)

### Post by kn0w-b1nary on 2011-03-01
Hacker: Someone who tweaks and modifies things to suit his purposes. Can be good or bad. Patching the uxtheme.dll or shell32.dll in windows is a hack, but it's more of a tweak and usually is done by someone who wants something not given straight to him.

Cracker: One who breaks into things with malicious intent. This includes cracking Facebook and Myspace accounts. Not only is this illegal, but IMHO morally wrong.
