Er open source is the base line, anyone doing economics will know about social base lines. the trick to earning money is to build on top of the base line.

In programming the act of building on the baseline it's self is where the money comes from. So more people in the community need to be investing in serious paid for development and more businesses need to be set up for such money to be useful.

### Post by fatality_uk on 2008-08-11
> **DoctorMO said:**

Economics is great in the classroom. In the real world however, a product or service needs a USP to move into a market that already has established players. Windows & Mac OS.

Linux on it's own isn't a unique product for the average PC buyer. For the average user, it's on OS. It's has no brand awareness and no history to trade on in general terms. Geeks know it and love it. Hell there isn't even ONE Linux. There's lots.

What's the baseline in terms of the desktop market? Windows. What compelling reason does a user have to switch to Linux if they have Vista installed and working on their PC? None.

Would they consider a PC that's £50($100) cheaper than a Windows PC if promoted correctly and did what they wanted, i.e. just the same as a Windows PC. Yes! Do they know what Linux is? Probably not. Have they heard of the Ubuntu brand? Maybe.

I find it laughable that some people get so bent out of shape about a Linux desktop company trying to make money (you know there are some out there) and yet the same people want more than anything to see Microsoft removed from the World's PC's. 

You can put forward economic theory from Adam Smith onward, the fact of the matter is that Linux needs brands, not baselines. Novel or Red Hat don't go into a large multi-national selling Linux, they sell Novel or Red Hat. Hopefully Ubuntu will be the desktop equivalent.

The fact is there currently isn't a business model that allows average end users to be exposed to Linux in am meaningful way and allows the Linux distro maker to earn cash. that hopefully will change, but it will need the support of hardware and software vendors to make that happen.
