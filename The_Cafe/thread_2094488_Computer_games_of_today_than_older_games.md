### Post by weasel fierce on 2012-12-13
This is generalizing heavily as there's innovative AAA titles, and derivative indie titles, but generally there's certain games you are only going to get in the mainstream (big games like Skyrim) and certain games you are only going to get in the indie market (FTL for example).

Indie developers can afford to play to a niche, and take chances, while the big developers need to hit something that is guaranteed to sell (and even then, plenty of games never break even).

On an individual level, I mostly don't care about mainstream gaming, since it's not available to me. I check out new titles on a buddy's 360 and we have fun. Then we play some turn based indie titles or some two player games on the old Amiga, and those are fun too.

### Post by drawkcab on 2012-12-14
I usually wait a few years before buying games for a number of reasons:

1.  They're cheaper.  I never pay more than $15 for a game.  Most of my games I've picked up on steam for $5-$10.

2.  I don't need a $2000 rig to play the latest and greatest.  Anyone can have fun playing TF2 on a $300 laptop.

3.  Gameplay > fancy graphics and hype.  A 2-4 year period will generally separate the wheat from the chaff.  If people are still enthusiastically playing a game years later, there must be something compelling about the gameplay.  Most of these games that are released, however, are moderately entertaining grinds that you forget.  You'd be better off picking up a sport, reading a book, going on a date--anything other than wasting hours of your life on every mediocre game that gets released.
