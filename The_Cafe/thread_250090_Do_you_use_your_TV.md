### Post by jISh on 2006-09-03
I've been stuck in a mental debate about this for some time now. 
For me, it seems useless having a computer monitor and a TV.

Okay, so recently my old CRT monitor fried and I bought a new 19" widescreen LCD monitor. In my living room I have a 27" CRT television. I live by myself.

The monitor has such better picture quality (nice high resolutions). Also, I want to move my movie collection from DVD to pure digital. I've thought about making one of my PCs into a media center PC, but it just seems useless if I could just use my monitor for all that stuff. Thinking about moving my 5.1 receiver into the computer room, so I can enjoy my surround sound still, and even with PC gaming.

I don't see a point in owning an expensive monitor and a television, does anybody else feel the same way? I can understand for people who have families or roommates with them why they would need both, but for people flying solo, it seems pointless. I'll be keeping my TV around, just to play Xbox on, but that's about it. (And no, I don't actually watch TV)

### Post by teet on 2006-09-03
I feel the same away about the whole monitor AND television thing...they really should be the same device.

However, here is the problem as I see it.  All televisions that I have tried to hook my computer up to have crappy resolution where the text is barely legible (I've tried it on my old school 53" bigscreen and my newer 55" HD widescreen TV with DVI input).  Computer monitors have much better resolutions, but are costly after you get out of the 19" range.

So...I still have a regular TV in my living room because I want a bigger screen.  In my office/play room I just have a plain old 15" LCD that I use for normal computer tasks and MythTV stuff.
