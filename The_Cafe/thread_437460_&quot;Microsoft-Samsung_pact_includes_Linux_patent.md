I noticed this article today, and I didn't find it mentioned here, so I posted it. It's less than a month old though;
[http://www.computerworld.com/action/article.do?command=viewArticleBasic&taxonomyName=operating_systems&articleId=9017120&taxonomyId=89](http://www.computerworld.com/action/article.do?command=viewArticleBasic&taxonomyName=operating_systems&articleId=9017120&taxonomyId=89)
> **April 18, 2007 **  [(Computerworld)]("http://www.computerworld.com/")  -- Microsoft Corp. on Wednesday signed a broad cross-licensing agreement with [**close partner**]("http://www.computerworld.com/hardwaretopics/storage/story/0,10801,101330,00.html") Samsung Electronics Co. that includes a controversial provision granting the Korean electronics conglomerate rights to patents that Microsoft claims have been illegally borrowed by the Linux operating system.

It's a bit scary; it seems Microsoft is building bases from which to attack Linux from, while at the same time benefiting from Linux.
