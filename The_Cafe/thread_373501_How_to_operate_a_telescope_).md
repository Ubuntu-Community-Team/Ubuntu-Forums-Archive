### Post by muguwmp67 on 2007-03-01
It is normal that the image be upside down.

I found the manual for the telescope:
[http://www.meade.com/manuals/TelescopeManuals/Telestar/TelestarRB60.pdf](http://www.meade.com/manuals/TelescopeManuals/Telestar/TelestarRB60.pdf)

Page 9 of the manual has some information on viewing tips.  It looks like the best option to get started with it is to use the 25mm lens first.  Its been a long time since I've used a telescope, but until you get used to working with it, I think this is the best place to start.  I think you isntalled the exension eyepiece, which is probably so magnified that locating objects without some sort of sky map would be difficult.

A beginners guide to astronomy, one that features some sort of atlas for locating interesting stars (locating a binary star and zooming in so you can see both is pretty cool) will be most helpful.

### Post by tenshi-no-shi on 2007-03-01
If you are looking at things in the night sky, you first want to have a good star map and be able to find things with your naked eye.

But one thing to remember always use the eyepiece with the largest number first. It's a low magnification and allows for a greater field of view. Once you center the object you want to see in the viewfinder switch it out with the next largest viewfinder. and repeat the operation till you get to the desired magnification.
