### Post by Kvark on 2009-02-22
After a quick look at the current prices I'd build a desktop with a Phenom 2 quad core 2.66GHz, 4GB DDR2, 1TB HDD, integrated graphics, cheap DVD burner, cheap case with good reviews and high quality PSU for £500. Then spend the other half on a few really sweet accessories, perhaps screen, speakers and printer.

Ram that isn't even used for caching does exactly nothing so it's a pure waste to get more than what your ram usage including cache peaks at but when DDR2 is so cheap why not take 4GB anyway just in case you ever want to work with huge files. I don't believe the difference DDR3 makes is noticeable to the user. If you want to play the latest demanding games you'd have to add a dedicated graphics card. If not then integrated graphics would do as it (in my experience) handles all other activities perfectly with ease.
