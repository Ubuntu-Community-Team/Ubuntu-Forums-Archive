### Post by Marlonsm on 2009-12-04
There is a poll running in the Brazilian news site Folha about Windows 7 and the results are much different from the ones I've seen in other countries:

The question is "Do you plan upgrading or have upgraded to Windows 7?"
And the results so far are:
30% - Yes
9% - No, I prefer Vista
28% - No, I prefer XP
4% - No, I prefer a Mac
28% - No, I prefer Linux

I know that as the poll was in the "Tech" part of the site, it's more likely that people there use other OSs.

But even though, I didn't expect such a result, specially here that all computers sold with Linux and the cheapest ones and have a bad distro.

And I also didn't expect Mac to be down to 4%. Its market share here is very small, but I thought people would vote for the Mac even though they don't have the money to buy one (which is a lot of money here, the cheapest MacBook is around R$3500, about 2000 dollars).


Now I'm starting to think that if it's true for most developing countries, Linux actually has a bigger marketshare than we thought...
