### Post by ComplexNumber on 2007-03-08

don't start off by reading a heavy manual. unless its a superbly written book thats on your wavelength, that will just kill your interest in programming dead. if you are completely new to programming, i would  probably start off with just having a look at a really really basic program(eg "hello world"), then seeing how it works. then set yourself a project. a typical project for you may be something like hangman.
setting yourself a project really is the best way because you learn loads about the language and how to program along the way. starting off by reading manuals is just duplicated effort.
always keep lots of reference books and tutorials at hand whilst you are writing the program. sometimes if one book doesn't explain what you need to know in such a way as its on your wavelength, another one might.
there should be less procrastinating from you, and more doing ;). just get stuck into the project.
