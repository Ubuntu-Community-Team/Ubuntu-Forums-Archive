### Post by koenn on 2011-06-07

Technically, you're right, although the workspaces are more like having multiple screens, without having multiple monitors.

there is, however, at least one window manager (that I know of) that actually did what you descibe : provide a screen (or "desktop") that was larger than the actual display (or monitor)  - you'd have eg a 1024x768 desktop on a 800x600 monitor - so you could see only part of the actual desktop, and you'd sort of slide the visual part to another region to see what was there. 

[http://www.fvwm.org/documentation/manpages/unstable/fvwm.php](http://www.fvwm.org/documentation/manpages/unstable/fvwm.php)
> Fvwm provides both, a large virtual desktop and multiple disjoint desktops which can be used separately or together. The virtual desktop allows you to pretend that your video screen is really quite large, and you can scroll around within the desktop.
