### Post by sydbat on 2009-04-02
Unless you are turning off your box for more than 24 hours at a time, you won't notice any real decrease in power consumption. Even then, it is negligible. And, unless your box is really old which *might* increase power consumption if running all the time, turning it on and off constantly can create less than favourable hardware conditions.

It could be different elsewhere, but I have never found an up or down spike in our power bill. When we go away for a week or two (sometimes more...don't worry, we have someone come in at least once a day to feed/play with our cat), and I turn off the computer, the subsequent power bill has no discernible change from the ones when the box is running 24/7. And the person coming in everyday does not use our computer, or watch TV, or do anything that uses lots of power.

Same for the other utilities (gas, water, etc), which you would logically expect from reduced usage.

Therefore, I believe it is a myth that turning off your computer every night (or whenever you are not using it) really "saves power".

### Post by RandomJoe on 2009-04-02
I used to be in the "never" camp, but have recently become more aware of power consumption.  Not for the typical "green / environmental" reasons, but from a _production_ angle.  It costs a LOT of money to produce my own power!  Much easier to conserve in the first place.

Not that I'm completely off-grid.  I have a small subset of things in my home (ham bench, small computer, lighting) that I run off-grid with wind/solar power just because it's a fun challenge.  And it provides some backup should we have another long outage.  But I started looking around and watching how much I really do use.  A large chunk of my consumption was in always-on but seldom-used computers!

Now I just fire up whichever machine I want to use when I want to use it, otherwise leave it off.  My only always-on system now is a mini-ITX style Atom-based system.  Pulls all of 25W at full tilt and has plenty of horsepower for what I have it doing.
