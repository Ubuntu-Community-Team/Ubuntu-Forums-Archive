### Post by koenn on 2009-02-22
voted meaningless.
As others have pointed out, "intuitive" is relative and subjective. 

"difficult to fix" has a couple of problems too
- what if something would be extremely difficult to fix, but you hardly ever have to fix it because it just never breaks. How meaningful would a "difficult to fix" score be then ?

- how do you define "fix" ? 90% of problems in windows are solved by rebooting, and the remainining 10% by reinstalling  :)  That's not difficult. 

- can you really say that an *OS* is easy or difficul to fix ? doesn't that vary with the problem you're trying to fix ? 



lastly, your linear representation doesn't work : where would you put an os that's both "intuitive" and easy to fix ? 


On the side, assuming the premise of your graph is correct : what gives you the idea unix is not hard to fix ? would you, if you had to fix a networking or disk partitioning problem, even know where to start looking ?
