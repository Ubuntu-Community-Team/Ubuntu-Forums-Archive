### Post by WalmartSniperLX on 2010-08-08
Audacity should be perfect. It allows simple editing and works with high sample rates (up to your hardware maximum). You may have some problems with peaking leading to sound loss if you don't mix properly if you're doing multi-channel recording. I used it a lot for voice and musical instrument recording. Honestly, if you want the best in 'quality' sound, invest in an audio mixer (can be purchased at most online music stores such as music123.com, musiciansfriend.com, americanmusical.com). All you need is one that has audio out thru stereo RCA, run an RCA to jack cable to plug into the back of your sound card, and a decent XLR mic. 

Even for basic recording with a PC mic, audactiy should prove to be good enough.
