### Post by rvndmnmt on 2011-03-07
I have been with linux for a good amount of time.  About 1997 or so.  And, perhaps, I have a few epiphanies about this wondrous OS.

Back in the day times were tough with this OS.  I used SuSE and Mandriva simply because they were ahead of the curve at the time with the graphical user interface.  However Mandriva was more of a power users OS while SuSE took the route of user friendliness.  Not that they were without their quirks.  In SuSE I always had the problem with NIC interfacing and Mandriva always had sound card issues.  I got away from Linux because at the time I simply could not foresee a future for it.  It was a tinker toy and not much more than that.

Before I receive judgment.  I have written about a half a dozen guides for various distros (Mobiln being my last one) on how to do things.  I am not a linux guru, but I have made my contributions.  I hope I can do the same for Ubuntu at some point in time.

Fast forward to today.  I got into Linux again about two years ago.  And now I am Windows deficient because of it.  I was shocked.  What was early alpha phase was now in late beta phase and very usable.  To prove a point I installed it on my sisters computer at ubuntu 9.10 and she is still using it.  She has absolutely no computer skills.

Well, with smartphone sales out pacing PC sales.  One OS is standing out on top and one OS is rapidly gaining ground on the IPhone.  And that OS is Android, a Linux based OS.  

I want to stress that Android is Linux based.  Android is a separate distro these days.  But I feel that more attention has to be spent on smartphones.  There is this myth that Linux is completely immune towards viruses and malware.  Yet android is always having problems with this.  To date about 220 or so viruses have been written for the Linux PC.  That is not because it is impossible.  That is because it hasn't been as effective to write one for Linux.  As of current Linux controls about 5% of the desktop world or so.  In the future, if Android finally topples the IPhone,  the viruses are going to be much more of a problem for Linux OS'es since now that Linux is worth a hackers time.  

Android is taking the route of Ubuntu on the more common device.  I feel that if there is a future with Ubuntu (which I would prefer over Android BTW) then it is in these handheld portable devices such as tablets and smartphones.  And I feel that Ubuntu could, quite possibly, take the forefront since I hate Google's philosophy of "Don't be evil".  I don't care who it is, government or corporation.  But what I do is my business.   This is more of a suggestion than anything to Ubuntu.  But get into the handheld portable market and there you will make your name.  PC's and laptops are going the way of the dodo.   And those of us in the IT area know that this is exactly what is happening at this point in time short of servers.  And Linux controls more than 95% of the server area.  

Handheld portable devices running Linux seem to be the wave of the future.  And this is one area Microsoft has ignored for the large part.  Take advantage of it.  For gods sake.  Put both Google and Microsoft into the ground.  You have the resources in personnel.  But if you can get into the portable market with much more reliability than Android.  I think you might have it.

### Post by Lucradia on 2011-03-07
You should also make sure you have the hosts file edited to re-route malware sites to 127.0.0.1, so that non-browser programs cannot connect to them. You can use this site's hosts file as a base: [http://www.mvps.org/winhelp2002/hosts.htm](http://www.mvps.org/winhelp2002/hosts.htm)

View it as a text file, and open up /etc/hosts with sudo in your text editor so you can paste stuff in if needed. Unlike Windows, the hosts file in linux is only regenerated once. The one in windows is regenerated every so often in newer versions of windows, and thus requires a special bat file. Using the fixit on windows to reset your hosts file too much will permanently fix it so you can't re-apply your anti-malware hosts file.
