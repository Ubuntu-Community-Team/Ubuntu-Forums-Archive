This. Although I wouldn't have worded it quite so heatedly it sums up the fact pretty nicely.

Nokia is still (by a very wide margin) the biggest phone company in the world and their yearly show is a big event and attracts a very large amount of media coverage, especially here in Europe. And more importantly, this is event is not just covered by the usual tech press, but also by traditional media like newspapers and TV stations, something that normally only happens with Apple events.

HTC on the other hand might be a growing player in the US and has managed to gather quite a bit of attention over there with devices like the EVO 4G and thanks to the largest American carrier, Verizon, promoting them heavily. But in Europe they are practically non-existent. Both in terms of marketshare and general awareness of the HTC brand with the "Average Frank/Jacque/Heinz". 

To put things in perspective [1]:

> Smartphone marketshare (UK, DE, FR, ES and IT)   -  July-2009 July-2010

               July 2009 (in thousands) July 2010                 Change

Nokia          | 28,260   -   65.6 %   |   31,169   -   51.2 %   |   -14.4
Apple          |  4,398   -   10.2 %   |   11,677   -   19.2 %   |   + 9.0
RIM            |  2,746   -    6.4 %   |    4,886   -    8.0 %   |   + 1.6
Samsung        |  1,976   -    4.6 %   |    3,121   -    5.1 %   |   + 0.5
Sony Ericsson  |    498   -    1.2 %   |    1,495   -    4.8 %   |   + 3.6
LG             |    197   -    0.5 %   |      487   -    0.8 %   |   + 0.3
Motorola       |    180   -    0.4 %   |      388   -    0.6 %   |   + 0.2

HTC doesn't even make the chart, meaning their share is below 0.6%, while Nokia has over 50% of the market.

So when HTC then announces to hold a conference in the same town at the same time as the Nokia World, whose date had been set month before that, then it becomes pretty clear who is profiting from the others attention. An HTC event at some other time here in Europe would have never managed to attract even a fraction of the over 5000 accredited journalist of the Nokia World. HTC clearly profits from the fact that all these journalists are already in town. So HTC schedules to get an event at the same time as Nokia World to get some more attention in Europe and then Nokia responds. As Kwiniz already said: "Ad agency games".

To bring this whole post to a conclusion: What bugs me, is just the tendency of major US tech sites to label anything concerning Nokia as bad, futile & negative and to project the market situation and dynamic of the US onto the rest of the world. On both Engadget and Gizmondo the roles were clearly set from just reading the headlines.
