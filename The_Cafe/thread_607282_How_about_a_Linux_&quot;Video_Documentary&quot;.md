### Post by klange on 2007-11-08
I've been thinking about this since I started recording my C-F set up a few weeks ago. If there is one thing that keeps most users from moving to Linux is that they have no idea what Linux really is, where it's used, and why it's so much better for them.

I propose that a "Video Documentary" be made covering key points as to why users should make the switch.
Things I think need to be shown in such a video are:[LIST]
[*]**Replacement Software** - OpenOffice, etc. Things we have that cover the basics of computing.
[*]**Productivity** - How many tasks do you know can be done faster and easier on Linux than on Windows? I know I can make a lengthy list.
[*]**Stability** - And none of that "Linux never crashes" ******** some zealots are throwing around.
[*]**Eye Candy** - I've seen plenty of great videos on YouTube of people showing off their C-F set ups, but how many people outside of the Linux world look at these?
[*]**Non-PC applications** - Everything from phones to routers, very few people know how many products run on Linux.
[*]**Freedom** - The word has been thrown around everyone, but most average users still don't understand what it means to be free.[/LIST]And that's just my list, I'm sure we can think of more.

On the more general scale of things, I want more than just "this is what you can do on <distro>", I'd like to see a "this is what you can do on *Linux*" sort of thing, representative of as many distros as we can get participants from.

Once all the sections are filled with videos, they'll be pieced together using a free video editor (Probably something like Cinellera), and upload to a website for linking as well as cut into sections and uploaded to video sites like YouTube, etc.

I'm looking for ideas, and of course people who want to help out.
