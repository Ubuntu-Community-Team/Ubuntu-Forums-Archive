### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-06-11
The look will stay very similar. The intention is to take the Unity user interface and turn it into a platform that will work with phones, tablets and TVs as well as the desktop. Canonical is not designing a look for a phone that is then going to be pushed on to the desktop. Things are moving in the other direction. Obviously the screen size of various devices will have an effect on how the workspace works and how the apps look but, speaking generally, expect the desktop work space to look and work more or less the same but with the added value of being able to slide a phone style work space out of the left side of the screen and use it as a phone. Some of the community developed apps may well replace some of the apps presently installed.

As you are using 13.04 expect to one day get the option of setting a PPA and then switching into Unity 8/Next at the log in screen. It is possible even now to install the core apps from the phone on to a desktop and keep track of how they go from a design to a fully working application.

Most of what you see here could happen on a desktop if the monitor was a touch face monitor. If not then dragging the mouse from the sides and top and bottom would work apparently. But the desktop would still remain very much as it is.

[http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/06/07/ubuntu-phone-video-demo/](http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/06/07/ubuntu-phone-video-demo/)

Regards.
