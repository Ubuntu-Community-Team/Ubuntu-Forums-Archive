### Post by DoctorMO on 2007-05-16
The deal is most parts of the world apart from the EU have laws for software patents or no laws restricting them.

In the EU, the EPO will issue software patents but the courts are compelled to dismiss them because they violate the EU patent agreement (as such) in this way Microsoft has the same patents with the EPO but is unable to enforce them since courts would simply invalidate them.

As others have said, US patents do not apply to other countries.

The EU, in particular, do not recognise software patents generally.  However, each member nations in the EU have differing laws in the matter, and some may indeed recognise software patents although with varying degrees of validity and enforceability.

Software patents are recognised in Australia, but the qualification of patents are tougher in Australia than the US.  It is likely that any Microsoft patents in Australia are probably more substantial than their patents in the US.
