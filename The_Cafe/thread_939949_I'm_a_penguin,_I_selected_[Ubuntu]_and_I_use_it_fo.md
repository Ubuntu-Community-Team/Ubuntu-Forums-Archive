### Post by patrickballeux on 2008-10-06
I am actually following the new trend of Apple and Microsoft.  I am not trying to do something different.

And most important, we have to be positive about Linux (Ubuntu) and not negative about Windows/OSX.  

The only way we can "demonstrate" that Linux is worth a try is by showing,telling,explaining the great features of any X distributions.

Like we hear often: Linux is better because it cannot get infected by virus for Windows... This is not a feature for the end user, and actually, they almost cannot believe it.

But show Compiz-Fusion, F-Spot, Open Office, Evolution, any great games, customization, the help forums, etc...  Those are features worth talking about.

The hardest part of this action won't be convincing windows users to try linux but to convince linux users to share their experience thru a small video clip.

It is not about winning or loosing, it is about catching the wave while we can surf on it...

It is not flattering by imitation, it is surfing the wave while we can catch it.  The current marketing trend is about showing real people and their lives...  It's everywhere (Big brother, American Idol, etc...)!

For years, Linux fans tried to impress with cool features, and nobody cares.  Did you see how many compiz-fusion there is on youtube?  But nobody is switching in mass..  (there a a few, but nothing that we can be proud of...).

So let's try it the other way, let's do it as what is currently talked most!  Microsoft ads and Apples ads...  Let's put Linux (Ubuntu) in people's mind...  

Windows users are always relating to Windows when it comes to computer, so let's use this at our own advantage.  The current marketing trend is "I am something...".  We should stand up and get on the wagon as it goes.  It is a way of getting out foot in the door.  Once done, then we can show our stuff, our way.

We have to stop thinking that if Microsoft does it, it's bad and evil.  It is working for Microsoft...  So it will work for us also!  We are talking marketing here, not religion.

So come on people, do your part, and help create that buzz and make it our own.  To be noticed, we have to take space and not just be passive.  We have to react, to show, to get together!

Why do you think that Apple is so trendy... They created a buzz around their product.  Are they better?  I don't know but everybody knows about Apple.

Think about it:  If every Linux user would post a 30 sec video clip on youtube, spread it in Facebook,MySpace,Digg, etc...  Then we would get that foot in the door...  After that, who knows?

Let's start with "I am a penguin..."  and after that, we'll find something else...

Come on!  Get up and share!
