So I was talking to this who I sits next to in my library studyhall. He said "Hey BWF, why do you always visit all these Linux sites? What is Linux" I was like "Linux is an OS kinda like Windows and Mac. Only better because you don't get viruses and all that nasty stuff". He thought it was cool so I decided to burn him a Ubuntu live CD. I went to give it to him in Social Studies (first period) But he said he didn't have a computer. So I decided that I had to give the CD to someone because I didn't want to carry it around all day so during my Visual Basic.NET class I tried to give it to my friend Shawn who sits next to me but he didn't want it either. Then this kid (forget his name) who sits near us overheard me talking about Linux and said "Wow!, is that Ubuntu? I tried to download SuSE a few days ago and left my PC on for 3 days but it wouldn't work. Then I tried to download Ubuntu but my connection is too slow. Could you hook me up with one of those?' So I hand it to him and he knew everything you had to do to get it working. So the next day in VB.Net class I asked him how it worked and he said it was really good only he coulnd't get online because he has dial up and just clicking on the browser woudn't do anything...

Now I know what Richard Stallman was talking about when he said with free software you can "help your neighbor" and it feels pretty good. You coulnd't do this with Windows or OSX. That's for sure...
