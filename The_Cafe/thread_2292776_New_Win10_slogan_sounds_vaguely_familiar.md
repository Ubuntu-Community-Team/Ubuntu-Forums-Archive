### Post by Petro Dawg on 2015-08-30
Just saw a Win10 television commercial and was curious how they came up with the new Windows ad slogan; "**Windows 10, a more human way to do**".  Does anyone else think that sounds vaguely familiar?  Perhaps something like, "**Ubuntu, Linux for human beings**".

Personally, I think "a more human way to do" is a strangely worded phrase anyway.  To do?  To do what?  Is Windows 10 a more human way to do... laundry? 

Perhaps they had to word it that way to keep from blatantly stealing the Ubuntu phrasing.  To differentiate the slogan a bit more from the Ubuntu one (while keeping to a 5th grade reading level and 8 words in length), I might have gone with something like "**Windows 10, a more natural way to connect**".  That phrase is slightly more descriptive, applicable between all devices upon which Win10 might be installed, and sounds more like a complete thought to me.  Perhaps that slogan is already copyrighted, however a quick Google search doesn't pull up that phrase.
