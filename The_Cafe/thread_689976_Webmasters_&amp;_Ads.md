### Post by Dr. C on 2008-02-07
It really depends on the arrangements between the webmaster and the advertising company The common ways are:

Pay by number of impressions usually a rate for each 1000 impressions
Pay per click
Pay for a lead. For example sending a inquiry for a product or service, downloading a piece of software etc
Pay for a sale

### Post by macogw on 2008-02-07
Depends on the advertising contract.

With Google Ads, I get a few cents per 1000 "impressions" (times the ads are shown) and a bunch more cents for each click-through.  One day I got 70,000 hits because I was on the front page of Digg, and that earned about $30, so that'd be about 50 cents for 1000 clicks, except that doesn't sound right.  Usually I only get like 3 cents, so I'm guessing it's probably kind of tiered.  Once it was clear I was getting a ton of views at that time, the value of the views probably went up.
