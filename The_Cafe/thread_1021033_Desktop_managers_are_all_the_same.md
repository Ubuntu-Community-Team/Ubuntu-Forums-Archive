### Post by BGFG on 2008-12-24
Aren't they ?

Thanks to another post on the forums, i took a look at some enlightenment based distros and what struck me was the touted revolutionary new interface that is basically the same line across the board.

Nothing against Enlightenment, KDE, Gnome or any other environment but there is nothing revolutionary about any of them. Do they work extremely well ? yes. They all behave differently and look different but all offer the exact same tools in slightly different formats and presentation.

I admit that some are glossier than others and have different items stuck around the desktop, but there are a multitude of tools to achieve the EXACT same look and feel no matter what your WM.

I haven't said all i'd like to say, and honestly i love all our WM's and their devs, but until we achieve a breakthrough in hardware and software that finally takes us away from the key input and selection devices that ALL proprietary and open source window and file managers are based on, then i see no revolution.

I think that will be achieved with AI. when we can simply sit at a screen and dictate spoken commands as our only mode of interface with our machine, then speak of revolution.

### Post by smartboyathome on 2008-12-24
Enlightenment isn't meant to be a revolutionary interface. What's revolutionary about it is how it acts more like a desktop shell in Windows than a Window Manager in Linux, as it uses modules to build stuff into it. Also, it gives you a very eye candied desktop that can be customized how you want, without all the bloat that stuff like Compiz provides.

If you want "revolutionary desktop", then check out stuff like 3DWM, even though you probably can't compile it anymore (if you can even find it ;P), it still is very revolutionary even today.
