I aimed to keep superfluous indicators at a minimum. I am thinking of adding other cues (such as a sound effect for the plasma cannon charging up, or maybe some smoke puttering out of your ship once it gets fairly damaged). But, for the most part, I hate onscreen indicators that obstruct your view of what's going on. They can be a real distraction.

I also wanted the game to be brutal, yet playable. Once you've played it a few times (or several, like I have through the course of developing this game), you get pretty good, and learn how to deal with mobs and such more effectively. As for world boundaries, I can't think of an elegant way to do such a thing... unless, of course, I have it block your path with a fleet of bogeys when you get too far off in any given direction. :P

Thanks again for the comments! The more feedback I get, the more I can tweak the design for great justice. ;)

### Post by theraje on 2010-11-14
Hey guys, thanks for your comments! :D

(@GabrielYYZ) Yeah, most of my friends had trouble lasting more than 30 seconds during testing. I usually last closer to 2.5 or 3 minutes, and my best time is just shy of 6 minutes. (My high score is 43,550 - that ought to give you something to "shoot" for!)

The asteroid idea could work. It would be possible to give a "cushion" of a certain distance from the origin to be free of asteroids, and once you leave that "cushion" area, the asteroids start showing up - the farther you go, the more frequently they spawn.

I have also heard some others complain about the turning speed of the ship. I'm not sure the best approach to handle this. I could speed up turning, but then you might have trouble aiming in certain situations (like when your aim is just *slightly* off and you need to adjust). I could also make the rotation start off at the current normal speed, and then get faster as you continue to turn (with a cap on turning speed, of course).

(@simpleblue) Thanks for commenting on the help page! Good ideas for making things more readable. :)

I was having trouble deciding on the weapon control scheme. It does seem a little "off" to shoot one weapon with the space bar and the other with the C key. I am wary of using "Alt" because of the nature of that key setting focus to the menu bar. I could always change the space bar to another key, but I would imagine some people ramming the space bar and nothing happening, and be unable to figure out how to shoot at all. :P

I *could* add a "setup" page that allows users to choose which keys control each weapon. The user chooses his key setup, saves the settings (using a cookie or whatnot to store the data), and uses those for play. It would take a small amount of work to complete, so I might end up doing something like that.

Re: maneuverability; see my thoughts on that above, in my address to GabrielYYZ's feedback.

It would be cool to embed a more "appropriate" font. I was thinking a terminal-style font, but a more pixelated font would be good too. It would be easy enough to change the way end-game data is presented.
