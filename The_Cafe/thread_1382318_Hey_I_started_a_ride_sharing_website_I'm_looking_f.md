### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-01-15
It's missing something.  I'm not sure what it is.

Perhaps break it up, craigslist style.  You could use the "tag cloud" feature to create a list of cities for origin and destination.

Also, you need a section for local travel--within a given city, especially when there is a trade show in town.

Marketing happens when the website works and engages the user and then that user shares his/her experience with others.

Craigslist is a good example of simple design that works.  I've never seen advertising for it, yet everyone knows what it is and how to use it.

In a way, your website competes with craigslist's bulletin board.  So, you have to distinguish your site with additional functionality.

Perhaps you could do a mash-up with google maps and your ride-finding service.

You could follow [http://fossevents.org/](http://fossevents.org/) and then ask the event sponsors if you could include a link for your site to help coordinate rides for various FOSS events around the country.  After a year of doing this, you will get both exposure and usability data.
