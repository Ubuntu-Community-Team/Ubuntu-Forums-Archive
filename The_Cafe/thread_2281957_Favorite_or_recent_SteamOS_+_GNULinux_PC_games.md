### Post by Welly Wu on 2015-07-24
So far, I think that Sid Meier's Beyond Earth has potential to become my second favorite SteamOS + GNU/Linux PC game, but I have to take a wait and see attitude until the Rising Tide DLC is available later this Fall 2015. The problem with Beyond Earth is that it feels alien and it does not have enough legs to stand on its own yet. Civilization V feels like a complete world rooted in history. This is why it is still my top favorite SteamOS + GNU/Linux PC game of all time. It's a huge world with layers of details to delve into and there are enough civilizations to keep it fresh and interesting which strengthens its' replayability factor by several magnitudes of order. Fighting over scarce luxuries, resources, and gold keeps it challenging and the other civilizations go out of their way to defeat you using science, culture, war, or natural wonders of the world. It is hard to stop playing this one. The best "win" is a draw with another civilization in a given set of turns. Beyond Earth goes beyond Earth and it explores space and the galaxies, but it needs more expansion DLCs to flesh it out. It feels like an unfinished software product right now. The Starships! PC game is a joke and it was poorly reviewed, but it is interesting to go Beyond Earth in a simplified board game style of PC gaming. I read rumors that it will be ported to SteamOS + GNU/Linux later this year, but 2K and Firaxis have not commented on this yet. If it becomes available, then I will purchase it. Beyond Earth has huge potential to change the game for Firaxis because it fixes the years old complaints about the previous Civilization PC games. It adds logical structure and easier management of the technology web and it adds new challenges to survive and thrive on a hostile alien planet. Where Firaxis missed the boat was to complete Beyond Earth and the modding community stepped in to make it more usable. Sometimes, I think that Firaxis is shooting for the moon when they publish a brand new Civilization PC game and I feel that the SteamOS + GNU/Linux port needs to be optimized for better performance as it is very resource heavy at ultra graphics settings which I don't think that it should be in the first place. It goes beyond loading screens and loading saved games. I'm talking about 3D hardware accelerated graphics performance here. It needs to be optimized for multi-core and multi-GPU hardware configurations and it needs to scale up more smoothly. There should also be a more detailed manual and a short video to teach new Beyond Earth PC gamers how to do basic tasks. There is too much of a steep learning curve involved and it winds up becoming a frustration riddled with trial and error type of learning from mistakes which can destroy a space colony or your entire civilization. Firaxis made the alien planet too hostile and inhospitable and some assets and resources are not apparent which results in missed opportunities to take advantage of each one each turn. It is still my second favorite SteamOS + GNU/Linux PC game so far, but I want much more and I am willing to pay for more features and content in the foreseeable future.
