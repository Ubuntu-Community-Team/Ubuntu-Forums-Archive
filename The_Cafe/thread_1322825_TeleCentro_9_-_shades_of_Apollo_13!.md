7 Nov 09 - Got up and plugged the modem in and see strange things; of the six lights on the front: Power, DS, US, Online, Link & Tel, the first four lights were blinking!  Aldo came a little late as he had car problems, but as soon as he saw the modem he knew it was toast!!  He plugged in a new modem, without connecting it to the computer, just the cable and power, in less than a minute it was set up and ready to go with solid lights on Power, DS, US & Link.

This supposedly will go up the line to some supervisor, like I'm holding my breath here.  Anyway the person on the phone said that they have specifically said in the work-order that the technician must bring a new modem, as this one is not working. Great, now as long a Wednesday isn't another Friday and the &%#ing guy shows up!

-----
10 Nov 09

21.30 - OK, tomorrow they come, or I should say they are scheduled to come yet again! If you hear what you think is English swearing and can't figure out where it's coming from it means two things, you have NOT read this and that was me cursing loud enouth that every TC employee can hear me no matter where they are!

----
11 Nov 09

08:31 - Victor came!  What a guy.  First he checked the modem. OK, it didn't work, then he tested the cable, it had NO signal. Up to the roof where he checked everything.  This building has 120 apatments in two wings and on the roof there are "nodes" the same as on the street because of the number of apartments each feeds (60), not the regular type "box" a building would have.  One had NO signal for Internet one (ours had a weak signal).  Victor did his magic and boosted the signal somehow.  Then he came back to the apartment, and hooked a new modem up directly with the cable from the roof. In less than a minute it had the lights working and we have a new modem online.

However when he hooked it up to the computer it didn't work.  Here's where "Apollo 13" became "TeleCentro 9".  Remember when they had to "steal" amps from the Lunar lander to get the Space Capsule up and running, Victor did the same here, he started "stealing" signal from TV to power Modem. First he changed the splitters that divided the signal equally for one that sends more signal out of one exit than the other.  He needed to change two of those. The original cable comes into the living room and is split in two for TV's in the living room and bedroom.  On the way to the bedroom there is another splitter to add the modem.  Now it's wired in such a way (2 extra wires on the floor) so that the modem gets the signal first out of the strongest side of the splitters, then it feeds the living room TV and the Bedroom TV.

The problem however, is not fully solved. While we have Internet, it is slow.  They are, or will be, working on a "node" on the street today or tomorrow, as that's where the problem originates, and again on the roof.  Apparently they have 48 hours to change that and they were advised of the problem yesterday.

So --- after 9 days, we have Internet again.

I hope you didn't think I deserted you.

Have a GREAT day.
