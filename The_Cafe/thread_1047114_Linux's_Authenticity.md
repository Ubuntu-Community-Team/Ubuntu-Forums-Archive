### Post by jespdj on 2009-01-22
> **dividenot said:**
> Oh, maybe I misunderstood the article.  I'll have to do some more research on the topic.
Wikipedia contains a lot of information on this topic: see for example [Unix](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unix) and [Unix-like](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unix-like).

Linux is not a real Unix system, in the sense that it is not based on the original Unix source code. Instead it has been written from scratch by Linus Thorvalds and thousands of other developers. That's good, because it means that people who claim to hold the rights on the Unix trademark or original source code can't make claims against Linux (at least not based on those two things).
