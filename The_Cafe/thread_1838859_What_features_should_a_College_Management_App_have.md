This software will help all staff members in managing their data relevant to the subjects they teach, track the syllabus completion, manage student attendance, handle student data (term work marks, practical etc.) and many more with the ease of a click. ### Post by keithpeter on 2011-09-04
> **iampriteshdesai said:**
> I want it to work out of the box. Colleges can install it and they are good to go.

Good luck with this huge project. You mention

Online coursework vaults, Syllabus completion tracker, Online library, Study notes, Assignment notification, Online results, Student Dashboard, Messaging system

Those functions could be provided using a system like Moodle (moodle.org), an open source Virtual Learning Environment (there are others). With luck, you will be able to pull student identities in from your registration/timetabling/fees system.

There are major differences between countries in the way the educational systems work. Best thing is to spend some time making a 'user story' for made up students that maps their progress thru' the system in your territory. 

User stories can help get an idea of what is needed (and help with marketing the system when completed). A couple I used in staff training some time ago (relevant to UK Further Education).

[http://bodmas.org/blog/ilt-ideas/user-stories-teacher/](http://bodmas.org/blog/ilt-ideas/user-stories-teacher/)

[http://bodmas.org/blog/ilt-ideas/user-stories-adult-student/](http://bodmas.org/blog/ilt-ideas/user-stories-adult-student/)

My fellow teachers began to realise how complex the data flows became after brainstorming a few of these...
