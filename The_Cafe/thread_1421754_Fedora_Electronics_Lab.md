You can have them in Ubuntu, it's just a remaster of Fedora with all the apps included.

Geda applications are available as .deb files:;
[http://www.gpleda.org/index.html](http://www.gpleda.org/index.html)
[http://packages.debian.org/search?keywords=geda](http://packages.debian.org/search?keywords=geda)

**What is gEDA?**

   The gEDA project has produced and continues working on a full [**G**PL]("http://www.fsf.org/copyleft/gpl.html")'d suite and toolkit of [**E**lectronic  **D**esign **A**utomation]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_design_automation") tools. These tools are used for electrical circuit design, [schematic capture]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schematic_capture"), simulation, prototyping, and production.  Currently, the gEDA project offers a mature suite of [free software]("http://www.gnu.org/") applications for electronics design, including schematic capture, attribute management, bill of materials (BOM) generation, netlisting into over 20 netlist formats, analog and digital simulation, and printed circuit board (PCB) layout.
