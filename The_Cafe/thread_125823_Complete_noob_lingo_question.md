"free as in freedom" means that you have access to source code, etc. - the various freedoms you have that are associated with the product.
"free as in beer" means that the product is free of charge.

you can have software that is one of those two freedoms, both types of freedoms, or no types of freedoms. just because you got a product free of charge doesnt mean you get the source. sometimes you pay for a product and the source for it is free (i think that cedega program is like that). sometimes you pay for it, and get nothing in terms of source (ie. windows). i think you already knew that, but didnt know it in the terms of beer and such
