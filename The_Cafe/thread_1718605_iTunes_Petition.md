### Post by cafejunkie on 2011-03-31
The reason it more than likely won't work is simply because of Apple's nature. Apple likes to have full, 100% control over all of their software and devices. As you can probably see, all of the iTunes services will eventually conflict with the licensing schemes of most Linux distros. 
Rather than petitioning for a port of iTunes (which would include the store, etc) you should be petitioning for I/O drivers for iDevices so that they can be used with Banshee, Rhythmbox, Amarok, etc. 
I recently convinced my fiance to go 100% linux (with Ubuntu) despite her using an iPhone. I set her up with Windows inside virtualbox and it works flawlessly.
