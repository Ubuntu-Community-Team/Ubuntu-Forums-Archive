### Post by ian-weisser on 2023-09-23
> **Autodave said:**
> Can you create your own app?

Sure, of course you can. Tutorials on how to do so are readily available any Search Engine.
However, it's easy only if your friend has prior experience developing software.

  > **Autodave said:**
> Does the app have to be in the "app stores" for someone to get it?

In Ubuntu? No, not at all. The system owner has control. But that's where most folks look.
In other ecosystems, like iOS or Android, it's different and the hardware owner has less control.

Most "create-an-app" folks are looking at it as a job. An income-producing opportunity. Ubuntu and the rest of the Open Source ecosystem are generally bad choices for that. Your friend should be looking at Apple and Android for "app" opportunities.

### Post by TheFu on 2023-09-23
Hey, if you're going to create an app for free for your friend, perhaps you'd also create these 10 apps for me, for free too?  Obviously, you have the skill to do it in 1 day each, since you are asking questions about it.

Do you see the point?  Your friend doesn't understand that people go through years of training and practice to become app developers.  They don't see that effort and since they didn't offer to pay you, I doubt they appreciate the effort either.  On Android, most "apps" are written using a funky version of Java.  Do you know anything about Java?  Have you already written some non-trivial java applications?  Do you understand how programming for a phone is vastly different than programming for a deskto even if both use java?

iOS programming is completely different.  They don't use Java.  There are specific languages and tools required if iOS is the target.  Last time I looked, you had to register with Apple and use a MacOS computer to build iOS applications.  Do you have a MacOS computer already?

There are many tools that claim to make app development easier.  These tend to be bloated and have all permissions enabled.  MIT made an app prototyping tool for this purpose a few years ago.  There are logs of youtube videos on it.  Perhaps these tools would work fine for a quick DB lookup tool.  [https://appinventor.mit.edu/](https://appinventor.mit.edu/)  There is a huge difference between an app created by one of those tools and one created by a professional.  Mainly bloat and security issues with the app-builder stuff, but it is a quick way to get success, which can be important to people new to software building.

Around 2011, my company built an app that showed videos and played local music.  It took about 6 months for the tablet-side of the work. I spent about a month on the server-side.  At the time, I was making about $20K/month ... so that little prototype would be around $100K for 2 people to built.  Or you could have them out-source the development and perhaps someone would agree to build a very simple app for $5K.  

When we take our cars into to the shop to have 3 days of work performed, generally, that work will be about $1200/day ... so $3600 total and that would be a real deal on the price.  It isn't just about the replacement parts, but the tools and knowledge to do the work.

See how putting a price tag on things completely changes the idea for how easy it is?

Before you agree to anything, I'd ask your local community college if they have a phone-app development program of courses you could take.  See what the pre-requisite classes are and work out how much background you'd need before actually programming for the target platform.  And don't forget to determine which android platform version you will target.  If you use the latest version, then older devices won't be able to run the program.
