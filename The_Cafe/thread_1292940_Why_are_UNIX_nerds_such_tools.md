### Post by openfly on 2009-10-16
Now, most systems guys are a grand lot ( I am one myself ).  I find myself friendly with just about every person in every shop I walk into... except for the guy with the thinkgeek addiction.  I love think geek as much as the next online store with gadgets, but you know the type of person I mean.

He's the guy who doesn't bathe proper, sits in his cube wearing a black t-shirt with a psuedo-witty comment and sneakers... when everyone else has a collared shirt and shoes.  He's a jerk to everyone, and thinks that any disagreement with him is an act of petulance.  And these guys cling to the open source community like maniacs.

Most people who work day to day with linux, aren't "hackers" or "geeks".  They are regular people doing their job, usually fairly well.. at least in my industry.  In fact the people I've met in tech who have impressed me the most have had insane beginnings and hobbies that share almost no commonality.  But there's like this subculture of complete tools who think that just because they work in IT or know how to shell script that they are suddenly the worlds authority on everything.  And, these are the same people who turn rabid on the internet when anyone addresses them with anything less than awe.

It pisses me off.  To no end does it piss me off.  Freenode is in my opinion filled with these people.  I once had a guy ban me from freenode's #perl because I told him to stop recommending POE for the problem I was confronting in the sockets module.  I wasn't even a **** about it.  I just told him that's not a good design choice for what I am doing... boom banned, not using POE apparently means you don't belong on freenode.  Hell I got banned from #flood on freenode for not flooding properly... I mean talk about stupid as hell.  And it's never "hey could you try this..."  or  "we setup some rules here, because..." it's always "YOU HAVE TREAD UPON THE MINI FIEFDOM OF RANDOM TINY THING THAT I WILL DECLARE MYSELF EMPEROR OF BECAUSE MY LIFE IS EMPTY AND I MUST FILL IT WITH SELF INFLATED EGOTISM!"

Recently, I had like three threads on the UNIX.com forums regarding some weird python behaviors... great stuff nice back and forth I think me and others involved learned a lot.  Then I up and get banned for quoting a line from "hackers" back to someone as a joke.... why?  Well simply put, because the admin is a tool.  And more often than not this stuff doesn't transcend the digital divide.  Hell you wander outside of the open source community and this stuff virtually disappears.  It's like ubuntu cds are being handed out with a license to be a jerk to everyone you meet.

You wander into the hardware hacking / electronic design community and everyone is just plain incredibly chill and excellent to each other.  But you wander back into the software community and there's flags flying and people screaming bloody murder.  

Now, maybe I'm just out of my mind here, but I'm curious if anyone else has noticed this.  It just seems to me that this radicalized fsf / open source / info sec / yada yada elitist bs culture that's been built up in IT over the past decade has just plain gotten olde ( with a ye olde english e ).  I'm curious if anyone else sees what I am seeing and feels the same way.
