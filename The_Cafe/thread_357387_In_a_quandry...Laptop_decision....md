### Post by prizrak on 2007-02-09
I would suggest getting a bit more power in the R40 (if it's a decent enough bit) to make it a little more "future proof" (there is no such thing obviously but you catch my drift). You should post which ATI they use because some of the ATI cards will work out of the box with the community driver and even have 3D support. Others will work with FGRLX (or w/e the hell the name is) driver and might take a bit more time to get working. 

If you are not gonna run Beryl and general 3D stuff just about any card will have a community 2D driver and will work OK out of the box (tho some will run slower). 

Also try looking around the forum to see what issues people normally have and how they are resolved.

Most ThinkPads play very nicely with Ubuntu and Linux in general. I have 2, older 600X and my more current T42 (Pm 1.8,1 Gb RAM, 80Gb hdd, a/b/g wifi, ATI 9600 mobile 128 Mb video card). If you can find one with the features you need in your price range, I highly recommend the T42. Everything works out of the box on either dapper or edgy. My vid card is not supported for 3D acceleration under fglrx pkg, but the "ati" driver from the initial install does give me hardware acceleration (not all that the card is capable of but enough for my purposes, since I do not use it for gaming and don't realy want beryl or what ever eye candy on the lappy. It has an excellent display, bright and crisp + videos look excellent on it.
