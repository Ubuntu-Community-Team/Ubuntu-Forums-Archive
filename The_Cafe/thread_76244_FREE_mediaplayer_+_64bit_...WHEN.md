### Post by JuanC on 2005-10-22
Would be Free Flash media player instead of free media player , there is a lot of free media player for 64bits , for example [VLC](http://www.videolan.org).

Another issue is that Macromedia uses VP6 codec for videos in Flash and there is no free media player that can decode VP6 video , yes you can use VP6 windows dll to decode , but this only works in 32 bits no 64 bits and there is no the best solution.

And also there is a thread that explain how to contact Macromedia for a request of Flash player in 64 bits and another questions of Flash in 64bits :
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=51211](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=51211)
