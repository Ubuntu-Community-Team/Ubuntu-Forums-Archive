### Post by brunovecchi on 2008-05-21
I also use tags, six to be precise. Awesome Windows Manager allows the display of more than one tag at a time.

Maybe this will clarify or add to the general confusion:

Think of tags as "labels" that you impose on the apps that you open. Let's say you have only two tags, named "1" and "2". You then open four applications; the first two with the tag "1", the third with both the tags "1" and "2", and the fourth with the tag "2". Now, when you tell the window manager to show the apps with tag "1", it does just that, and you can see the first, second and third window. Then you switch to tag "2" and you see the third and fourth applications.

In this simple example, tags are equivalent to virtual desktops, the first two applications lay on the first one; the fourth on the second virtual desktop and the third one would have the "show in all workspaces" option set on. But it can get much more elaborated than that; you could try and see some (or alL) tags simultaneously, and also you can assign certain applications to certain tags by default. You can also assign meaningful names to your tags, such as "Work", "Web", "Music" and "Administration". Then you classify some of your applications to each of this four tags, so that when you launch them they get their correct tag. In any moment, you can fuse one or more tags and see, for instance, "Work" and "Administration" applications at once.

### Post by brunovecchi on 2008-05-22
> **popuptarget said:**
> brunovecchi.....Now I know I am a drool dripping idiot....how does one assign a tag to an app and how do you invoke the tag?  If I am frustrating people blame LaRoza as laRoza brought up tags to begin with.  :)

I suppose that depends on the window manager at hand! With awesomewm, you switch tags with the Mod4(coughwindowscough) key + number, or Mod4+left/right. It's configurable, though. If you want more than one tag, it's Ctr+Mod4+(tag numbers you want to see at once).

As of assigning tags to applications. The easy way is to just launch the application, it will open in the current tag. You can then change this tag pretty much the same way you move a window through workspaces, by pressing Mod4+shift+(tag numer). Again, if you add Ctrl to the mix, Mod4+Shift+Ctrl+(tag numbers), you manually assign more than one tag to that application.
The most elegant way to do this though, at least for some commonly used applications, is to configure a very simple dot file, and pre-assign a tag or tags to them. This way you just launch the program and it will pop up in  its tag/s.

You may want to check out some screenshots:

**[Wmii window manager]("http://www.suckless.org/wiki/wmii")**
**[Awesome Window Manager]("http://awesome.naquadah.org/screenshots")**
