### Post by Evil-Ernie on 2011-03-02
Depends on the job role and the interviewers. I would say always dress as smart as you can for interviews as even if the company themselves have a relaxed dress policy it shows effort and professionalism. To some interviewers it doesn't matter but the question is, is it worth the risk that you may be faced with someone that it does matter, or even it might come down to you being pitted against a candidate that have equal skills, qualifications and attitude as you so may be used as a splitting hairs point.

Of course it shouldn't really matter as I don't think how you dress for an interview makes you and better or worst for a job, but then I am not interviewing you!

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-03-03
Since this was 11 hours ago, you're likely back from the interview now and have answered your own question ...

First, to comment from my experience, if you don't have "dress clothes" for an interview (at least, "business casual", or better, a suit), then you need to ask BEFORE the interview if certain attire is expected.

If they balk at you asking, then even going to the interview is most probably a waste of time. IF they won't hire you for asking a question, then how are you going to do any work there??

And second, the only honest answer anyone could have provided you to your question is "We don't know". As some have said, maybe you'll get someone who doesn't card about attire; but on the other hand, maybe you'll get someone who really DOES.  We simply don't know.
