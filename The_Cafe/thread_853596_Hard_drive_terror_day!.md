### Post by len1x on 2008-07-08
What's up,
I'd like to share a story with all of you guys... I had my case open from both sides today, and I had a big *** bottle of water over my desk.. Forgetting that it's there, I hitted it while standing up with my elbow, it fell near my case and spilled some water over my 500 gigas hard drive.. my pc hanged.. no water touched the mobo, but one of my hard drives took most of the water.

Got the hard drive out, tried to dry it etc with cool air.. no luck! I thought that I have to bite the bullet. I thought it died.

I hanged the hard drive like laundry infront of some big *** fan, went out for some coffee etc...

Just came back home, tried to plug it in, and guess what.. It got recognized in bios ( and the best part that ubuntu booted :P )... Now I'm back and running.

I'm really happy my disk didn't die, thanks god :)

### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-07-08
The flash drive will probably be all right for a while. Those things can usually take a pounding in my experience. I've run one of mine through the wash several times by accident and it's still working.

The Standard hard drive though....   keep an eye on that. The motor in it may short out, but you may have saved it by drying it out immediately rather than trying to restart it.
