### Post by MikeTheC on 2009-06-29
Wow, that HannsG sounds nice.

Of course, the proof is in the pudding, as they say.

As long as your video card has enough oomph to push that kind of resolution, then I'd say "go for it", assuming the monitor does not, in fact, suck.

It's really weird where we've come from in terms of displays, size, shape, quality... all of that.

Even the s***tiest of monitors these days is worlds apart in clarity and quality from *any* monitor of yesteryear.

Really, about the only thing that picture tube displays are good for now are high-end color correctness. To this day you'll still find people paying $1000, $2000 or more for super-high-end picture tube monitors if they're pro photographers or print houses, etc.

Other than that... LCD pretty much owns.

Personally, I don't think you should base it on numbers provided by the companies. There really isn't a good standard for measuring contrast ratio, and often they use the best possible settings to inflate it.
