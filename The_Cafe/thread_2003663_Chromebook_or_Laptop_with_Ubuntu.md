> **irv said:**
> **About the Chromebook:**

I feel for you. It has taken my wife many years to get any comfort with using computers.  It also didn't help I kept changing things on her :lolflag:

The Chromebook is basically a browser with a few extra pieces for managing the hardware/OS (connecting to networks, login pictures, etc.)

I would suggest while you look at which way to go:
[LIST]
[*]Get her going on Chrome/Chromium NOW, so that whatever you decide she will have the same experience.  Even if this means getting her online on your system.  This will go a long way to making her comfortable with any of the systems.
[*]If she doesn't already, set her up with a Google account and turn on Synchronization (of EVERYTHING; passwords, cookies, bookmarks, etc.) in all Chrome browsers she uses.  This will copy over the same extensions and apps (which are basically glorified bookmarks).
[*]Add the "AdBlock Plus" extension!  It will keep confusing advertisements from cluttering up and confusing her.  I am reminded of how much good this extension does every time I have to use IE!
[/LIST]

If 99.9% of everything she does can be done in the browser and without local apps then the Chromebook does offer a fair degree of simplicity.  If she receives a lot of attachments (Word, PowerPoint, etc.) see whether they would open in Google Docs; Gmail has a nice option to open a file in Google Docs from reading the browser.

The screen of the Chrome/Chromium browser can house custom bookmarks (not the apps that you use Google Play to get) but I don't know if those are synchronized between browsers or not.

I have a Chromebook (Cr-48) and Ubuntu (Unity) and Windows.  I find when I am surfing I do like to use Chrome because added bookmarks, apps and extensions are synchronized between all of my systems and I have it remember passwords too.

If you can get your hands on a modern Chromebook before having to purchase it, work around it a little. They are making some changes that may reduce the simplicity benefits for her, or may make it more comfortable for her.
