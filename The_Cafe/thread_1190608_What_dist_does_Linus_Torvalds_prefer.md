### Post by dspari1 on 2009-06-18
He has stated that Fedora is his primary Linux distro, but he would use any distro that installs easily, quickly, and has a package manager that solves all dependencies for him, and keep itself up to date with recent packages.

His description pretty much puts Gentoo, Arch Linux, and Slackware out of checklist.

As for Desktop environment, he has stated that he is currently using Gnome as KDE 4 was a disaster. In the past he has had tough words to say about Gnome since it wouldn't let him configure his right mouse button to what is normally the middle click button. 

> "Without tip-toeing around the matter, Linus Torvalds made his preference in the GNOME vs. KDE matter quite clear on the GNOME-usability list: "I personally just encourage people to switch to KDE. This 'users are idiots, and are confused by functionality' mentality of Gnome is a disease. If you think your users are idiots, only idiots will use it. I don't use Gnome, because in striving to be simple, it has long since reached the point where it simply doesn't do what I need it to do. Please, just tell people to use KDE." Also, "Gnome seems to be developed by interface nazis, where consistently the excuse for not doing something is not 'it's too complicated to do', but 'it would confuse users'.""
 
Couldn't he just switch to a distro that still supports KDE 3.5? (PCLinuxOS, Mepis, or even Kubuntu KDE 3.5 remix) 

I don't know about you, but him switching to Gnome is the biggest foot in mouth that I've ever seen.

It's almost popcorn worthy. :popcorn:
