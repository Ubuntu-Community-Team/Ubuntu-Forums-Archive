### Post by Welly Wu on 2015-08-07
I bought a 2015 ZaReason Zeto desktop PC and I concur with the statements made by oldfred. Notebook PCs including desktop replacements are overrated and overpriced because hardly anything except the SO-DIMM RAM and perhaps the internal disk drives can be upgraded by the user himself. If you stay at home most of the time, then a desktop PC will offer you more power with a desktop grade CPU and perhaps a nVidia Geforce GTX GPU plus you'll get the capability to upgrade almost everything internally over time at the same price or significantly lower prices. You can get desktop hard disk drives for mass storage which you can not do with a notebook PC. I also have a Lenovo IdeaPad Y510P notebook PC which is still fairly high end and modern, but I only use it to keep my software up to date daily and when I travel to the Nutley public library, Starbucks coffee shop, or a friend's apartment once per week. If you plan to play Microsoft Windows or SteamOS + GNU/Linux PC games, then a desktop PC will be a far better investment of your time and money as you will get desktop grade performance and upgradeability over time. Desktop PCs are not dinosaurs and they are not extinct. There are good usage case scenarios where a desktop PC will trump a notebook PC. Remember, once a single PC hardware component malfunctions or fails with a notebook PC, it is very difficult to get it repaired or replaced under warranty. If you buy a Microsoft Windows notebook PC and install GNU/Linux, you will void the manufacturer's warranty. If the warranty expires and something breaks, then you have to deal with it or repair it out of your own pocket. A desktop PC offers room to expand and grow over time and the performance alone should be enough to justify the purchase of either a System76 or ZaReason product model. I would recommend the ZaReason Zeto desktop PC system as it is a small form factor mini-ITX design that is compact and light enough to be transportable to LAN parties. ZaReason products are pricey, but you will be supporting another GNU/Linux PC OEM/ODM and by doing so you'll vote with your wallet in favor of supporting GNU/Linux.
