### Post by FoolsGold on 2007-03-18
I had a somewhat positive experience with tech support via email.

My ISP hosts a repository of useful software for customers who are members of said ISP, which includes (funnily enough) Linux ISOs. To limit the repository to ISP-customers only, the repository web site checks your IP to see if it's a connection established from the same ISP; if it is, you go though, if it isn't, you're blocked with a message saying so. Recently I tried connecting to the site, yet despite having worked fine in the past, this time it blocked me even though I was still a member.

I emailed tech support (didn't bother with phone, wasn't in a hurry) explaining the issue, and just for completeness I mentioned I was using Ubuntu Linux. The respose I got from the techie was that they didn't have any experience with Ubuntu Linux, BUT they still suggested ways of resolving the issue, which included disconnecting and reconnecting the net connection to establish a new IP, which ended up fixing things. I mention this because even though the techie wasn't familiar with Linux (or at least Ubuntu), they didn't flat-out say "You use Linux, no tech support for you!"; they were still willing to suggest ways to fix my problem, help which ultimately proved useful. That, plus a few prior uses of their tech support, reminded me of one of the reasons I'm still with the ISP. :)
