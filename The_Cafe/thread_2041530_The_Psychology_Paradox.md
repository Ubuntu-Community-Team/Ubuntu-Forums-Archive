Psychiatry - The study and treatment of mental illness, emotional disturbance, and abnormal behavior. [Using psychology]

Scientific study/method - The principles and empirical processes of discovery and demonstration considered characteristic of or necessary for scientific investigation, generally involving the observation of phenomena, the formulation of a hypothesis concerning the phenomena, experimentation to demonstrate the truth or falseness of the hypothesis, and a conclusion that validates or modifies the hypothesis.

The principle difference between psychiatry and psychology is that psychiatry focuses on the brain and psychology focuses on the mind.  So psychiatric treatment will tend to be physical; involving operations and drugs whilst psychological treatment will tend to involve counselling and behavioural therapy.  In practise these boundaries are blurred so members of either profession may prescribe physical or behavioural treatments.

One should distinguish between empathy, which is a measure of how well a person perceives the feelings of others, and compassion, which is what a person feels motivated to do about that perception.
Empathy, selfishness, compassion, and the part they play in stimulating behaviour is really part of social psychology
