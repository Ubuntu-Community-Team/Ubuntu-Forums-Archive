### Post by Paqman on 2011-11-21
> **F.G. said:**
> 
A while ago a kid got arrested and had a encrypted HDD, he refused to tell them for ages what the password was, and all they could do was hold him till he gave it up.

Er, certainly not in the UK. Refusing to give up an encryption key to the cops is a serious crime, punishable by several *years* [jail time]("http://www.theregister.co.uk/2009/08/11/ripa_iii_figures/").

Which is (IMO) as it should be. The purpose of encryption is not to be able to hide evidence of a crime from the police. Of course, if you're actually guilty and the crime you're trying to defend yourself from carries even higher punishment than that then a couple of years in lockdown might be quite easy time in comparison, in which case you'd be smart to withhold your keys.
