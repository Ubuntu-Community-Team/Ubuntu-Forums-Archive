### Post by TBABill on 2010-02-25
Having worked for both a Telco and a Cable Operator, ADSL is an old technology that is not planned for upgrades, at least with Verizon. They'll support it and add in areas they can get a little extra profit by increasing the number of subscribers able to get DSL, but they are not expanding the service or implementing the technologies to get the 7Mbps+ speeds a cable provider can sell.
 
In an old building you have issues with both phone wiring and cable wiring, depending on when each was updated (or if it has just been maintained over the years). There's no way to know unless you suffer issues with telephone or cable service, but that's not a good gauge either. You can have metallic faults on telephone lines and not even know it (short, ground, etc.) My suggestion is to ask some neighbors what they have and if they are happy with it. DSL is probably slower in most places you go and you already have slow service with it. You may pay more for cable, but most times it's worth having reliable service with high speeds. The cable providers generally sell digital phone service and you can cut the ties completely with the phone provider to save a few bucks (no, not like Vonage...just phone svc through the cable network back to a telephone switch and onto the phone network).
 
My money would go to cable, but your circumstances, location, condition, etc. will determine if it's better or not. No way to know via the forums....check with neighbors or catch a tech out working in the area if you can. They know where the problem areas are.

### Post by Objekt on 2010-02-25
I don't know if the OP is stuck in ISP hell like I am, but I recently switched from ADSL to cable, and I'm not looking back.  

At least in the US, the available speeds are usually higher with cable than DSL.  In my area, the fastest DSL is a pathetic 1.5 mbit down/768 kbit up, but with cable you can get up to 16 mbit down/2 mbit up.

Cable is also not as vulnerable to accidents of geography & cheap construction.  With DSL, distance from the nearest telco equipment can limit your maximum speed, as can wiring quality in your house.  We did not have to consider these things when ordering cable Internet.

I don't know why, but here, latency is also much higher with DSL than with cable.  It's still higher than it should be with cable, but I now get around 50 ms less latency than when we had ADSL.  That is a big help for gaming.
