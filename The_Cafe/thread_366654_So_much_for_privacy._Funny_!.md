### Post by Zuuswa on 2007-02-21
I believe that this is inevitable (if not in some form or another, already existant).  The important issue that we can address, however, is *who* can see our information.  For example, in the UK, what if everybody were allowed to see the video from allof the cameras mounted on the lightposts or streetsigns, instead of just police officials?  You could see around that dark corner, and see if anybody was waiting to jump you.  Or you could check and see if your date is waiting at the park for you, or watch your children walk home from school, etc. etc.  But then, anybody could be watching you, including those who wish you harm.  Most importantly, in this scenario, is that theoretically by allowing the freedom of information, those who choose to abuse the system are caught by the system, including malicious police officers and government officials.
