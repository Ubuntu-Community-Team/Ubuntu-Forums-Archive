Some of those look photo-shopped.  Plus, WinXP is a very stable system.  I would attribute these to...

1) some third-party writing some crappy program that some other third-party used for their automated whatever, and it had a loop-hole in it that allowed a crash that WinXP couldn't handle for some reason other than dumping out debug message.

2) Some smart-*** hacker running around with a wireless laptop, hacking into the system and creating a BSOD (either by faking it or by causing a system crash) just to perpetuate the "Windows is totally unstable" philosophy everyone seems to assume.
