### Post by forrestcupp on 2008-12-17
I hate to say this, but you're just going to have to get used to it, or don't hang around here.  Not everyone here is an MS hater, but the ones that are haters are vocal.  I personally use Vista a lot, as well as having Ubuntu installed.  

The Linux world is a FOSS world.  It exists *because* of FOSS, so you're not going to talk anyone out of supporting it.  That doesn't mean that proprietary software can't have its place, too.  But if it weren't for Free Software extremists, FOSS wouldn't exist, therefore Linux wouldn't exist.  You just can't completely take that out of the Linux community.

I somewhat agree with your sentiments, but you're fighting an impossible battle.  You'll save yourself an ulcer or stroke if you just learn to accept that there are always going to be FOSS extremists who hate MS and proprietary software, but that not every Linux user is like that.

And remember that some Microsoft haters are that way because of a bad experience, and they'll calm down after they've had some time to heal.  We just cycle through people on the forums at such large masses that there are always people fresh out of a bad experience.
