### Post by handy on 2011-07-07
The various possible flashing colours & the beeps are actually NOT Sony's equivalent of post beeps from a computer motherboard. Which means that the variety of things that the PS3 can do before it gets to the flashing red light are not something we can use to help us understand what exactly has failed in the PS3. The flashing red light means that the PS3 has a problem that is stopping it from being able to boot. 

Apparently for many, this problem can be solved by turning off the power to the PS3, then removing the HDD & then reinstalling it.

If that doesn't work do it a few more times & it may.

If not it will require more time to fix.

[I]**[edit:]** After more reading, it would seem that this problem is often caused by the HDDs connection to the PS3, so cleaning the connection on both the HDD & the PS3 with compressed air is a good idea.

Also, if it is due to a poor connection with the HDD, it makes sense that some people find that by inserting & removing the HDD multiple times, all of a sudden it starts to work again, as by removing & reinstalling they are cleaning the connection contacts to a certain degree.

**[edit2:]** If the above doesn't work then its the slower more difficult fix as shown on video here:

[http://playstation3ylod.info/?p=89](http://playstation3ylod.info/?p=89)

Thanks for having this problem OP, I too own a PS3, & now I have the above videos Bookmarked for future reference. Hopefully I won't ever need to dig into the guts of my PS3, but time will tell.

[edit3:] I've watched the video instructions & what you have to do if it isn't your HDD connection, is dismantle (all but completely) your PS3 so as to remove the motherboard, clean all the heat sink paste of the two chips. Then you put the motherboard in an oven to re-float the board, which means you are melting the solder enough to remove any dry joints (bad solder connections) then you replace the heat sink paste & put it all back together again.[/I]

> Each SPE is composed of a "Synergistic Processing Unit", SPU, and a "Memory Flow Controller", MFC (DMA, MMU, and bus interface). An SPE is a RISC processor with 128-bit SIMD organization for single and double precision instructions. With the current generation of the Cell, each SPE contains a 256 KiB embedded SRAM for instruction and data, called "Local Storage" (not to be mistaken for "Local Memory" in Sony's documents that refer to the VRAM) which is visible to the PPE and can be addressed directly by software.
