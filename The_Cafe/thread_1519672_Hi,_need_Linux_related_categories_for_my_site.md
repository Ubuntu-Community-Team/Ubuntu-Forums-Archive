### Post by koenn on 2010-06-30
your question is silly - for a number of reasons

1. Noone here knows "evrything there is to know" about Linux
2. There is no "definitive" set of categories. Every field of knowledge can be categorized in multiple ways
3. categories are overrated, especially for large fields of knowledge, such as "everything there is to know about Linux"

to elaborate on #3 : in the 90's, people tried to "categorize" the information on the web (eg [http://dir.yahoo.com/Computers_and_Internet/](http://dir.yahoo.com/Computers_and_Internet/)). Then came google's search engine with a way to find stuff without needing to browse through categories, and redefined web search. 


That's an example of the limitations of "categories'. It's also rather approriate for your site: If 'your' content is going to consist of other peoples web pages, Google will give far quicker access to that info than any category-based filing system such as your site.



There, helpful.
