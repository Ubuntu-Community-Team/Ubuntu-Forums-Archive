### Post by cartman640 on 2010-09-21
I wouldn't bother with liquid cooling these days. Years ago, when air coolers were the largest block of copper you could find with the fastest (and loudest) fan possible attached, liquid cooling was a much more efficient method.

However with todays heat pipe based heat sinks, and better combinations of metal for heat absorption and dissipation, they are nearly on par for efficiency (and noise) with liquid cooling. Also a good air cooler will be hugely better than a cheap liquid cooler.

My advice for hot systems would be to move them or buy decent air cooling for them. Of course if the room is hot already, adding more cooling won't really help, and moving them would be the best option.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-09-21
When CPUs cost a fortune (> $1000 US), high clock speeds (> 3GHz) were all the rage, and a slightly faster CPU cost a LOT more ... liquid cooling got you the advantage of a faster CPU at a lower cost.

But today, with multi-core CPUs being the mainstream, CPU clock speeds not being very high, and really GOOD air-cooling high-speed fans, unless (as others have said) you're overclocking, and even then, pushing the limits of what the CPU can do, liquid cooling is probably NOT worth it.

### Post by LowSky on 2010-09-21
Liquid cooling today is basically a coolness factor. LC is great for showing off parts where huge heatsinks would generally cover up. Also LC allows you to use colored coolant and parts that add an extra touch of DIY.

Like everyone has said you really need to change the airflow in the room. If its heating up like an oven, then you need to push cold air into the room by opening a windows or using A/C.

I would move them to the basement, as they are just servers, the only issue would be the basement's humidity, or if it floods. Keep them away from piping, the water heater, and off the ground. If its like my basement you might need to clean them more often for things like spider webs and check for case rusting which can mean too much humidity.
