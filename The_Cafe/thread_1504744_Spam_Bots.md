### Post by McRat on 2010-06-08
> **philinux said:**
> Looks like they are real people to me.


Yes they are.

Mostly from China or Russia with English as a pre-requisite for hire.

There is no way a bot can get through some of the user-verification schemes.

What we switched to was a "tech question".  This is a question that is easy for somebody with interest in a board's content to answer.

In the case of this site, it would look like:

Ubuntu is a:

A) Political chat site.
B) Talk about Africa.
C) An operating system.

Usually the spammer doesn't know anything about the site, they are paid by the hits for their links.  While they could easily search to find the answer, it's faster to go to the next one on the list.  The list is very long.  Mostly it's blog or magazine sites.

It reduced our spam members over 90% after the "human test".
