### Post by Meep3D on 2008-01-08
Reading the threads here pretty much half are about MS, and pretty much all of them involve bashing MS.  

How about this for an idea:
Ignore MS entirely, don't even dignify them with your thoughts.  If you're tempted to write a rant about how crap Windows is, don't bother.  There is really nothing new to say that's not been said before.

I find it a bit sad that Linux defines itself so much not by what it is, but rather by what it is on a scale of 'vs Windows'.

Your time is precious.  Why spend so much time focusing on something everyone dislikes so much?  Why does Linux always have to be viewed in comparison to Windows?  Why is it impossible to have a conversation without bashing MS?

Surely Ubuntu has reached a point where it no longer needs to be in MS' shadow.  Superiority does not need to be proven - it just is. 

Lets all just ignore MS, apart from maybe the occasional topical egregious security or trust violation.

Lets have a Linux forum, rather than a MS bashing forum!  Wouldn't that be so much nicer?

### Post by TomMK on 2008-01-09
I agree with the OP, but also with post #2, namely; it won't happen. It is human nature to boast. Many new Linux or Mac users feel they have achieved something by moving off the dominant OS to an underdog system, and can not resist taunting Microsoft, whom they feel they have beaten. This only wears off after using the new OS for a significant amount of time, until it is considered normal, rather then novel. Given the constant influx of new Ubuntu users, this is not something that will go away any time soon.

If the Linux community wants a larger share of the OS market, this is something it will need to put up with.
