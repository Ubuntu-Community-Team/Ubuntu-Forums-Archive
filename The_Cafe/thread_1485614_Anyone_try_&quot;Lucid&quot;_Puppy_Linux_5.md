### Post by Ibidem on 2010-05-20
Remember that Lucid Puppy is based on the Lucid Lynx.
It's basically Lucid with a bunch of files ("cruft") removed, a different window manager, configured to run from RAM, etc.
The binaries you can blame on Canonical and other contributors; it's not compiled from source.

### Post by Ibidem on 2010-05-26
Well, now I have tried LuPu 5.00. Puppy has long been a favorite of mine, but this is probably the best yet (first that really works with my wireless).
I just added IceWM and Seamonkey to it, and did a manual "frugal install" that boots from grub4dos...so I've got an interesting chainload now:
Grub1--->ntldr--->grub4dos-->Puppy
It seems more stable with IceWM than with JWM (graphics-wise; intel graphics are trouble); I needed to download a browser in Ubuntu because PuppyBrowser is inadequate for authenticating on campus internet here.

And 5.01 is out (since yesterday), bringing back the pre-boot configuration.

One thing though: 
LuPu kills Ubuntu's wireless if I don't have an ice-cold start (ath5k vs madwifi I installed; 30 seconds down isn't enough, though overnight does it).
