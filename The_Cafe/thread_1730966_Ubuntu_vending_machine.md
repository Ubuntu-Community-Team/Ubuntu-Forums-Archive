No? Yes? :)

That is a cool idea; however, I don't think you can be profitable with it.

I had some vending machines a long time ago; before, cell phones had cameras in them. My vending machines sold disposable cameras. 

You have to find places to put your vending machines. The stores, amusement parks, pubs, whatever, will want a piece of the sales for allowing you to put it in their establishment. They will look at your idea and say something like, "What the $%^&* is Linux". They have a valid point. What is the demand for this product? In my case the amusement parks looked at the amount of space my machine would use and the space a video game machine would use, and found the video game machine more profitable for them.

There is also the cost of operating the vending machine; electricity to power it; and the cost of the raw materials, in your case, CDs, DVDs, or USBs; sales or VAT tax; and so forth. 

Maintenance of the vending machines may be greater than you expect at first glance. Do you realize that every time the government changes the appearance of the paper currency you have to update your vending machine's software/firmware?

If you analyze your business plan and determine you can make a profit, or at least live off of the income, then go for it.
