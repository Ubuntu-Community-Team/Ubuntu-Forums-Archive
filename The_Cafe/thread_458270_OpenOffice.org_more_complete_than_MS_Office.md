### Post by Hex_Mandos on 2007-05-29
I've often seen discussions on whether OOo is up to MS Office. Most of the time, the main points are "OOo Eriter doesn't have a grammar checker or a way to switch to Title Case", or "OOo Calc doesn't include some obscure mathematical formula" vs. "OOo can save PDFs". Today, I realized something: OOo includes the Draw component, which I've found is quite nice. MS Office, on the other hand, only includes Visio in the most expensive bundles. I suppose most people end up using PowerPoint for that kind of stuff. For this reason, today I instructed three friends to install OOo: otherwise, they won't be able to open the flowcharts I sent to them.

So, while business users might find Visio better than OOo Draw, most people probably won't know that such a tool exists, as Office is just the big 5 (Word, Excel, Powerpoint, Access, Outlook, maybe Publisher?). That makes OpenOffice.org better than most "Home" edtions of MS Office, IMO.
