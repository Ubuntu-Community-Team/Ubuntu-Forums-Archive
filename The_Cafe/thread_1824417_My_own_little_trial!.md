### Post by -gabe-noob- on 2011-08-13
So about a year ago my dad was arguing with me that the reason linux hasn't taken off/ isn't widely used is because its "unintuitive and way too confusing for non-tech geeks" 

 Anyway, without telling him I "fixed" my little cousins "broken" laptop with a Xubuntu install and showed him the ropes (it was a laptop running vista that was WAYYY too underpowered to run it at any reasonable speed, 512 ram and a 1.8 ghz single core processor) 

I'm with the cousin and he LOVES the laptop, he likes all the "cool sweet free games" (I showed him the ubuntu software center) and says its running super fast. 

Anyway, what I was more impressed with was he kept the computer updated and has had virtually no issues in using it daily, he has an email and uses word processing (I did have to show him how to save in word friendly formats and admittedly he isn't doing anything that would have formatting issues between LibreOffice and word. ) and it works fine.

Anyway I think my little trial proves that non-super nerds can use Linux just fine. Note: I'm not saying its easier/better than anything else out there, but I think for someone just starting out with computers, they're able to catch on to it just as fast as they would with other OS's.

Edit: The purpose of the thread is to see if any other forum goers have had any similar experiences.
