### Post by Mikecore on 2008-01-25
I have spent a day or two going through the unanswered posts and have noticed many silly posts. New users are posting question that have already been answered many times over. I don't think they are even trying to use the search tools ( forums/google ), or wiki, or tutorials. Most of their problems have already been explained and answers how to fix them posted. I love to here more and more new folks using linux, however I'm not sure I care for the fact that they want everything spoon fed to them. I guess this comes with the territory when a OS starts to become more main stream. Its just the linux community works so hard at providing great forums, wiki, tutorials and man pages. and from the looks of these post most of that stuff is flat out ignored. My first linux distro was Gentoo and as most know thats all about reading and learning. I think Ubuntu is amazing in the fact that 30-40 min after booting the install cd/dvd that you can have a fully functional linux box. After spending ( sometimes days in the beginning ) installing Gentoo and editing config files by hand ( xorg, fstab, samba, etc ). I can appreciate why Ubuntu is so great.
