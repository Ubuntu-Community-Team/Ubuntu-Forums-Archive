So, I know a LOT of people who know of only two things: Windows and Mac. They've never even heard the word "Linux." If they've never heard of that, they've probably never heard of Ubuntu, either.

Can we start a thread about ideas for how to push Ubuntu? The site [http://spreadubuntu.neomenlo.org/](http://spreadubuntu.neomenlo.org/) needs to be far more visible because I didn't know about it until the Lucid release. Marketing is key in getting your product out there, and Ubuntu doesn't do much of it outside tech circles. If we're shooting for a perfect 10, I think it's time to start attacking Bug #1 a bit more directly!

The cheapest thing to do is online ads in places like Facebook. Target the younger users - they're the biggest and most tech savvy, anyway.

I know there are a lot of digital media/cinematography folks out there. Perhaps at the UDS, something could get recorded if there is a solid idea.

Here's an idea I've had for awhile for a commercial. Have it set in a mall-type area. Inside, there are two groups of people: the stereotypical "PC" users in suits and the "Mac" users in casual clothes. The PC users are browsing stores that sell glass and window panes while the Mac users are looking at displays of fresh fruit (okay, I know, corny, but people will get the analogy). Focus on someone who is dressed differently, looks lost, and just doesn't fit in. (preferably a female - we need to break the Linux-is-for-guys stereotype). Then, have her walk up to a small bin and pull out a stuffed penguin. Give a close up of her face as she smiles, and then zoom out to an Ubuntu logo with the slogan "Computers for Human Beings" and a link to the website. I assume everyone is laughing at this idea, but it looks great in my head . . .

Lastly, Canonical needs to try to get companies and individuals that will help fund this initiative. I think that advertising is ESSENTIAL - we need to start, because Ubuntu is finally ready.
