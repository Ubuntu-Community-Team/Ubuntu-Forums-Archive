### Post by wersdaluv on 2009-10-03
I'd rather be loved and spread love like Mark Shuttleworth than be as rich as Bill Gates.

I just realized that money is overrated. To have a desirable human experience, as a geek and UX enthusiast, I would be satisfied by using well-designed technology. Yes, money can buy that but it's not the only way to acquire it. I am inspired by Mark's vision of improving the User Experience of the free desktops. I dig his idea of the free software process being the right way of building software and his belief in its potential. That proves that you don't have to be as filthy rich as Bill to do what you want. What's more important is the love that Mark is getting and spreading. I would definitely choose to hear the words that Mark hears over Bill's. Also, knowing that your life is dedicated to such a cause is very very fulfilling. Mark isn't the only rich guy in the world but guys who spend money, time, and effort for such things are harder to find. 

It seems that money can be too much. When I was younger, I told myself that I have to be the richest dude in the world. Now, it appears that that wealth isn't always positively related to life satisfaction. You only need enough wealth to satisfy the few things that only money can buy. If you can acquire your basic needs, other things than money are in charge of making you happy. If we are going to refer to Maslow's heirarchy of needs, physiological needs are followed by safety, love, esteem, and self-actualization needs. You think money is the only thing we need for those? It may be important to satisfy those needs in some settings but it can be largely irrelevant. A good example would be Gandhi (yes, happy birthday to him!). He changed things without formal authority or money. 

To simplify things, my point is that the important things that make up a desirable human experience are not always the most popular ones. I love what I'm doing now and it's not exactly very conventional. My goal now is to influence and be influenced like Mark.

I know, this isn't my personal blog, but I hope, I somewhat inspired some of you. hehe :)
