### Post by davidgypsy on 2005-07-27
Lately I have been giving Mepis CD's to people new to Linux because just from a usability point of view it is super easy. Once installed there is not much to do... kinda boring actually. It has the ability to play MP3's and DVD's straight off, has Acrobat Reader 7, etc. so it is a no-brainer for Windows users to come to grips with.

It has Synaptic, and uses the Debian repositories, so it should be easy to upgrade actually. It really sucks at opening usb devices, including cameras, but Ubuntu does this GREAT!

Ubuntu is really good at a lot of stuff, which is why I use it, but purely from an ease of use point of view, Mepis is ahead.
