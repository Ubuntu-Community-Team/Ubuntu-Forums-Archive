### Post by igknighted on 2007-06-08
[http://www.ubuntu.com/](http://www.ubuntu.com/)

> Ubuntu CDs contain only free software applications; we encourage you to use free and open source software, improve it and pass it on.

Does this strike anyone as a little misleading?  On the Ubuntu CDs is non-free drivers and firmware for several wireless cards (intel, atheros, etc.) and now they are promising a "free" version, as opposed to what?  The current version?  They claim it is already free software.  Other distro's that are recognized as free software distros (Debian, Suse, Fedora, etc.) do not include these things, except on separate media labeled as "non-free".

I do not care if Ubuntu is strictly non-free or not, what bothers me is the misleading nature of it.  The quote above implies that what you get is free software, which to a linux user, that means something very specific, and does not appear to be what we are getting.

### Post by the_darkside_986 on 2007-06-08
The kernel modules that interact with the binary crap are GPL2 for obvious reasons. Most people who try Ubuntu either don't understand GNU/Linux or assume that Ubuntu is an easy-to-use system without consideration of licensing issues. OSS purists are usually more knowledgable and would choose something like Debian. So if it were misleading, the only people who would care are people who would rather not use Ubuntu in the first place.
