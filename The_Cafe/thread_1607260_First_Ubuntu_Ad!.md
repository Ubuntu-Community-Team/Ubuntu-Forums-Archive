### Post by 3Miro on 2010-10-27
It is not bad, but it is far from good.

There is nothing about "freedom", just that it is free. While "freedom" rhetoric may be confrontational and probably shouldn't be put on the first TV ad, simply mentioning that Ubuntu is "free of charge" will lead most people to think of it as "free soup for the homeless". Why not have something that lists large Linux servers that people know about (like youtube) and then tell people that they can have "Server Power on Your Desktop ... and It Is Free".

Also, the shape-shifting orange dot is catchy, but by the middle of the ad I was wondering "what are they trying to sell again?" People will have no idea what this is about. Why not, instead of funny orange dot animation, show actual Ubuntu desktop with some screenshots of people using office applications, games, internet and so on. Better yet, show a desktop with fully pimped Compiz, now that will catch some attention (besides will probably cost less to make, since it will be mostly recording of the regular desktop).
