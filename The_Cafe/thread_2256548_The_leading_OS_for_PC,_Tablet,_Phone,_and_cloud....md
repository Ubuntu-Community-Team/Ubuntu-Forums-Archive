### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-12-13
MIs-leading is also "leading?" Is it not? ;)

It all depends upon the definition of the word "leading." In  my understanding of the word "leading" I see nothing to suggest that the reference is to the OS with the most market share or the most users. There are many ways in which an organisation can rightly claim to be "leading" in this and that field of endeavour.

I note that the reference is to Ubuntu and not to a corporation. Let us compare like with like and not compare Ubuntu (a Linux distribution) with corporations like Microsoft and Apple. Doing that is another kind of misleading. What market share of the desktop does the Apple OS have when compared with the Microsoft OS? What market share of phones and tablets does the Microsoft OS have when compared with the Apple OS? Out of those two which is the market leader in PCs, phones and tablets?

Being market leader does not necessarily mean either of those two corporations are leading in other areas. They are certainly not leading in FOSS on PCs, phones and tablets are they? If Canonical can complete its convergence strategy, then Ubuntu will certainly be leading in having one code base that runs on phones, tablets and PCs with a UI appropriate to each form factor and suitable applications for each form factor. Ubuntu mobile devices will never have the market share of Apple's OS or Android or even Microsoft's phone OS. But that does not mean that Ubuntu is not leading on phone, tablets and PCs.
