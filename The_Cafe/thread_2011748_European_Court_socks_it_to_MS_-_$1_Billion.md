### Post by Dr. C on 2012-06-28
This case is very significant becasue of who the main competitor was namely SAMBA. To make a long story short the EU basically forced Microsoft to license certain patents in a manner compatible with the GPL v3. The very same GPL v3 that was too radical for even Linus Torvalds! When Microsoft dragged its feet the EU responded with fines that came to approximalty $1 Billion. The history of this is also very interesting. The SAMBA project raised the ante, by moving to GPL v3, very early in the game ahead for example of most of the GNU project. 

This is very different from previous anti-trust cases against Microsoft where many of the competitive products such as browsers or media players were propriety. 

The real bitter pill for Microsoft here is not the $1 Billion but the GPL v3 and what it represents.
