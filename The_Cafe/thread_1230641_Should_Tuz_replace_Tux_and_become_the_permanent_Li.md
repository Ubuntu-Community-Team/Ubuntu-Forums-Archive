### Post by Grant A. on 2009-08-05
> **reyfer said:**
> You do know that at the rate the ice caps are disappearing, penguins will be on the verge of extinction soon too, right?

This is actually a common misconception.

1) Only **2** species of penguin inhabit Antarctica.
2) Many penguins live in Africa, South America, and the islands in between there and Antarctica.
3) Antarctica isn't **purely** ice, there's a whole 'nother continent underneath the ice.
4) Tux isn't even either of the species of penguin living in Antarctica.
5) Global warming does have a balancing point. Fresh water has difficulty mixing with saltwater in the oceans, due to the fact that fresh water is less dense than saltwater. The fresh water floats above the saltwater and disrupts valuable ocean currents that help to push warm air over certain regions. Most notably, the Gulf Stream in the Atlantic Ocean keeps Europe temperate, and less icy than Canada. If the fresh water were to build up in high-enough amounts, it would result in the Gulf Stream's, and various other ocean currents', failures. Thus causing a new Ice Age, until things work themselves back out.

While I applaud your concern, please read up a bit more on the situation. When things are properly explained, and less jumbled up together, people tend to take notice a lot sooner. Especially when they live in an area soon to be affected.
