### Post by handy on 2010-12-30
MS, Apple, Oracle & ECM have teamed up to have a go at Google & Android. Though Google is also an Open Invention Network member & is so licensed to use these Novell patents already.

Though virtualization & office suites could also be in the mix with this gang, as would be the fight for the cloud. It should be a good blue, I put my money on Google. 

The opening up of the AMD GPU tech' info' & the great deal of help that AMD have contributed to the AMD/ATi FOSS driver stack (including the kernel) has been wonderful & could eventually cause nVidia to do the same thing. 

Eventually we will have AMD OSS drivers that will compete with the nVidia closed ones which is going to make the AMD GPU's a real threat to the top end commercial graphic companies that use Linux based systems. :)

The alternatives to OpenOffice.org & MySQL will flourish, Oracle can jump in the lake where they will eventually drown themselves. 

I can't put a number in the poll. Overall I consider it a great year for FOSS in general, there are no insurmountable problems in front, only a broadening of the devices that FLOSS will be used on. 

Ubuntu server is going from strength to strength in the corporate user market which is great for Canonical & therefore Ubuntu, as they will be making money.

The Linux desktop just keeps on slowly, steadily & unstoppably gaining more users. As Russia, China, India & Brazil keep on ramping up there Linux usage the percentage of Linux users is going to start accelerating leaving MacOS way behind & shrinking the the MS OS, hold dramatically.
