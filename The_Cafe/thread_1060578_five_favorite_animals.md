### Post by Michael627 on 2009-05-26
> **chucky chuckaluck said:**
> i love cats. my cats have been the best thing that ever happened to me. gorillas are second, bears third, followed by dogs, then otters. and you?
 
I would mention, yes, cats and dogs. But also rabbits, both wild and domestic, monkeys, and my favourite wild animal is the hare, which resembles a large rabbit and to North Americans is known as the Jack Rabbit. I've just finished reading a terrific book called **Bad Hare Days** by John Fitzgerald, about a campaign to save the endangered Irish Hare, an animal under threat in Ireland both from modern farming methods that lay waste to much of the countryside, and the so-called "sport" of coursing in which captive hares are forced to run for their lives in a confined area or enclosure from hyped-up greyhounds that savage or kill many of them in the process. 
 
According to the book, people who take a stand against this hare coursing activity have a  mountain to climb in their efforts to protect the long-suffering Irish Hare. Some have been badly bullied and others have suffered nervous breakdowns as a direct result of their campaigning. 
 
Though they've got lots of public support for their campaign, the minority who support coursing are so powerful with top politicians and catholic priests backing them that it seems it could take many more years before the Irish hare gets a better deal.
 
I know someone will reply to this and make the usual jokes about rabbit stew, "bunny huggers", and say "ah they're only silly animals, who cares?"...but I wouldn't knock the sincere efforts of people who just want these animals protected from some pretty horrific- and I would say **UNSPORTING** practises.:p
