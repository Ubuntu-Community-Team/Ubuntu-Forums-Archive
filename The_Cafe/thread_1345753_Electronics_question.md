### Post by LinuxFanBoi on 2009-12-04
[IMG]http://imagehost.vendio.com/bin/imageserver.x/00000000/pedroauctions/ROBThumb.PNG[/IMG]

For those who don't know.  R.O.B. was included in the premium Original NES package that sold for $199, but was a very short lived device.  To my knowledge there was only 2 games designed to use it. The original NES packaging was so massive it had a carrying handle so you could cart it around to your friends house.   Though you rarely had to take it anywhere because if you had one, your friends would often come to you. and sometimes at unreasonably early hours of Saturday mornings so they didn't have to wait for their turn to be player 2.  and BTW $199.99 was an outrageous amount of money to ask of your parents back in '84/'85. so until the price came down a lot of kids still got an Atari.  If you don't now what and Atari was,  that's another post.  

In the Image you see above the second controller was placed into a cradle connected to the red and Blue platform levers.  You had a motor to spin the gyros and a rack to stage spinning gyros until you needed them.  A fully spun up gyro would continue to spin for about 2~3 minutes.  When you needed them you could manipulate ROB to retrieve them and place them on the red or blue platform depending on which button on the second controller you needed to activate.  Keep in mind if the gyro stopped spinning there was no way for rob to retrieve it, and retrieving it by hand was considered to be cheating.

When I served in the Army,  it was amazing how many of the 18 year old kids had no idea this thing ever existed, but then again a lot of them where born around the 1990 era and have no idea what what life was like before 16 and 32 bit gaming.  They never got the experience of blowing on the edge of the card slightly protruding from that giant game cartridge and ever so gingerly sliding the cart into the machine and delicately pressing down else the game wouldn't load properly.  And even then sometimes it took a good sharp smack on the top and side of the machine to get the game to load.
