### Post by sgosnell on 2010-01-15
Nokia Internet Tablet.  The N800 has no keyboard, touchscreen only.  N810 is a little newer, has both a touchscreen and small keyboard.  Neither has any phone options adt all, but do support wifi well.  They run Maemo, a variant of Linux, optimized for the platform.  They do internet browsing very well.  The N900 is the newest model, and has a built-in cell phone.  I haven't seen one, but there are reviews and videos of them on the net.  I have an N800, and it works fairly well, but text input is tedious at best.  A bluetooth keyboard would probably help with that, but I haven't bothered to spend the money.  I mostly use my Asus EEE-PC 900, which is bigger, but has a usable keyboard and much larger screen.  It's not as portable, but it's more versatile.  I carried a Palm for years, but finally gave up except for some specialized uses.  The Nokia will run GarnetVM, which is an early version of PalmOS running in a virtual machine on the tablet.  

If you're looking for something very portable, and compatible with Linux, the Nokia is it.
