### Post by kpatz on 2015-01-10
Depends on the motherboard.  Some require equal-sized pairs to do dual-channel.  So, you could have 2 512 MB modules in a bank and 2 1 GB in another bank for a total of 3 GB.

But with a motherboard with 4 slots and a max of 4 GB, why not just fill them all with 1 GB DIMMs?

Most probably the worst will be that you could miss out on the added benefit of some more speed that you get running RAM in dual-channel as mentioned above.  Other than that what I have found the only other downside doing this is that all of your memory gets the run at the same speed as the slowest DIMM you have installed (which sucks if some of the RAM is very fast and one of them is very slow).  There may be a point where one gig less RAM but higher speeds is preferable, or visa versa.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-01-10
The sockets might be arranged in 2 banks of 2. That means you may be able to the 1GB DIMM in the first socket of bank 1 and the 2GB DIMM in the first socket of bank 2. Or the 2 500MB DIMMS in socket 1 and 2 of bank 1 and the 1GB DIMM in socket 1 of bank 2 or the 2GB instead.

I doubt if you will be able to use the 500GB DIMMs in the first bank of 2 sockets and the 1GB and 2GB DIMMs in the second bank of 2 sockets.
