### Post by wmcbrine on 2009-02-06
This is a gross overestimate. The bulk of it is transportation costs -- which he figures at "current rates", using early-21st century Earth technology, at $95 million per ton, to lift it from the Earth to orbit. But that's silly -- first of all, you couldn't even gather all that material on Earth, and you wouldn't try; you'd mine it from asteroids, etc. Second, for a tiny fraction of the total cost he suggests (although still an astronomical sum in absolute terms), you could construct a far more efficient delivery system, such that the cost per ton could be brought almost arbitrarily low. He isn't properly accounting for economies of scale, which would be vast in such a project.
