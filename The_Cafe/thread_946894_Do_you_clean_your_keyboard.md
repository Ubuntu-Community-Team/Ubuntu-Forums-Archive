### Post by Therion on 2008-10-13
A really cheap, soft, two-inch paint brush works wonders for getting between the keys and sweeping out crumbs and crud.  Turn the keyboard upside down, shake it a little then hit it with some canned air.  Then brush, then a few more puffs of canned air and you're good to go.  

I don't really recommend removing the keys unless you reeeeeally think you need to.  A paper clip is fine I guess, but I find a table knife (e.g. NOT a "steak knife") is easier and more substantial to work with.  Be sure you know your keyboard though, so you know where to put the caps back on when you're done.
