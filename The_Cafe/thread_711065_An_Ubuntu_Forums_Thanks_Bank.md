### Post by Forrest Gumpp on 2008-02-29
With the exercise of a little anglotextuphonic* agglutination applied to the respective African and Latin roots of the new first word in the phrase, let me introduce the as yet unrecognised principle of **'ubuntuberrima fidei'.**

The Latin component of this new first word is 'uberrima', meaning, in free English translation, 'in the utmost good' (faith).  It is the underlying implicit principle binding all parties to insurance contracts.  The African component, ubuntu, of course needs no further explanation in this place.  The agglutination of the two words accurately represents or describes the position every member of this Forum voluntarily adopts when, as a condition of membership, they accept the Code of Conduct.



[SIZE="2"]Now we Ubuntuans all have a problem.[/SIZE]



Our Forums are getting very busy, and that's good in the sense that it means more people are using Ubuntu.  With this 'busyness', however, comes congestion of the forum boards, increased workload on a largely volunteer workforce, and probable greater delay in getting answers to our questions, and that's all bad.  This situation is exacerbated when Forum members fail to read stickies and FAQs before posting, and that's happening a lot.

It has been suggested in some quarters (in the Forum Feedback and Help sub-forum) that a sterner response on the part of Forum staff and moderators to this neglect is required.  Indeed, I myself may have have been one who suggested it.  To be fair, however, it has been suggested in even more quarters that such a response or approach by moderators would be self-defeating of the ethos of Ubuntu and the point and purpose of the Forums, if I may put words into peoples' mouths.  (And why not?  That's where words belong, in peoples' mouths.)

[SIZE="2"]So what to do?[/SIZE]

**I suggest that members be asked to lodge an Ubuntuberrima Fidelity Bond with an appropriately constituted Ubuntu Forums trusteeship fund, a 'Thanks Bank'.**

Every member benefits from these Forums, whether they be posters or just viewers.  Even long-inactive members benefit, for the resource is growing despite their absence, and the day they do have a problem, the Forums are there to fall back upon.  You could regard the Forums as a form of informational insurance.  So whilst most contributions of knowledge and/or services are freely given, it is not IMO unreasonable to suggest that those who are able voluntarily forego a small 'premium' to enhance the quality of this 'insurance'.

**Specifically, what I suggest is that the preservation of a Forum member's Ubuntuberrima Fidelity Bond deposit be conditional upon that member periodically having visited, and lingered for a deemed to be sufficient time upon, the FAQ and Stickies pages relevant to both the Forums at large and any sub-forums within which they post.**

The assessment of a member's having sufficiently fulfilled this condition I feel sure could be engineered in such a way as to be an automated feature of the Forums.  Fail to sufficiently fulfil in accordance with the published rules, and part or all of that member's Ubuntuberrima Fidelity Bond deposit becomes forfeit to the 'Thanks Bank', and thus becomes Ubuntu Forums revenue that can be applied to such purposes as improving the functionality of the Forums or funding free software projects.  We all know there will be plenty of forfeits!

On the other hand, should members jealously keep their deposits intact by zealously fulfilling the FAQ and Stickies conditions, then the Forums will have benefitted not only by way of interest earned on deposits, but through the better discipline of members, reduced board clutter, and better quality and/or quantity of advice.  Maybe less cash flow, but better information flow.  The real result will, of course, lie somewhere in between.  It's proposed to be a fun, honour, and respect thing, not a deadly serious financial transaction.  Undertaken in that spirit, deposit forfeits could be acknowledged as being under, apart from the published rules, conditions like "the referee's decision is final and binding" and "no correspondence will be entered into", and such like 'no litigation' clauses.  Sport, not avarice.

Whilst being mindful of the membership demographics in all respects, a large (500,000 +) and growing membership with each depositing, say, $1, creates a fund of $500,000.  A deposit of $10 or $50 for many may be quite bearable.  You simply have to think of what the Forums are worth to you in self-assessing an 'honourable' or 'appropriate' deposit.  If an average deposit of $10 across the present membership of 500,000 + was to be achieved, a fund of $5,000,000 would come into being.  Some of the financially better off may well be more slack in guarding their deposits, and won't that just be too bad for them!  It won't necessarily be bad for Ubuntu Forums, however.  The whole idea really is that members sportingly just write their deposits off:  quickly if they insist by infraction and subsequent replenishment, or very slowly by guarding them well but leaving them in the 'Thanks Bank' virtually indefinitely.  But, come the worst and they wish to leave, members can just as sportingly have their deposits, if still intact, returned if they request it.

I give you **The Ubuntu Forums Thanks Bank - Just for Fun!**  And good Forum behaviour.



*'Anglotextuphonic' means English first written before ever having been spoken.  Much as in "In the beginning was the word...", if that helps in this Canonical context.  I don't think you will find anglotextuphonic in the dictionary just yet, if my Google search is any indication.  (I will let somebody else have the last word here, however, just on principle.)

### Post by Forrest Gumpp on 2008-03-04
Het_Irv

That's doubly big of you to say that you may have got the wrong idea about the 'Thanks Bank' proposal.  I noticed the first time and intended to respond to that post, but just didn't get around to it. Sorry.


The Outline Idea


Such members as wish to financially support the Ubuntu Forums lodge such amount of money in a properly administered UF fund as they are prepared to 'risk', interest free, repayable on demand.

The UF may earn interest on the voluntary deposits of members.  This interest constitutes income for the UF, and is presumably used as the UF Council sees fit in running these Forums, and/or such projects as the UF membership at large (and, yes, that includes voting rights for those who prefer not to support financially in this way) desires.

The 'risk' undertaken by members who choose to lodge deposits, of 'losing their money', will be one of their own creation.  By agreeing to a set of as-yet-to-be-written rules, depositors will agree to visit the various Forum pages and sub-pages containing such necessary background information as will allow them to best use and participate in the Forums.  The FAQs, the Stickies, the CoC, and such like to each be visited (and lingered on) for some as-yet-to-be-determined time with some as-yet-to-be-determined frequency.  Fail to do it, and you do your dough!

The recording of visits and the accountancy for failures to maintain the frequency and completeness of visits to these pages is of course the challenge to site design.  Such software to some extent obviously already exists (eg. the recording of the fact that 'you have already voted in this poll' and, dare I say it, the 'Thanks' feature.:)).  Some software may yet have to be written or adapted.  The important point is that it will all be an automated process.

Deposits of members voluntarily agreeing to these rules become forfeited to the fund if the member fails to maintain the agreed frequency of visits to the relevant Forums pages.  Such money then becomes income for the fund to dispose of as it will in fulfilment of its objectives.


The General Objective


The whole idea of this 'game' played with real money is to provide a good humoured incentive for members to maintain familiarity with the FAQs and Stickies.  If it works, the UF makes a little bit of money from the interest it can get on deposits.  If it doesn't work in terms of achieving greater overall familiarity with the FAQs and Stickies, the UF makes a lot more money with which to deal with all the forum static.

Nobody is forced to play.  Everybody retains full Forum privileges

That's the general outline.  There is lots of scope for members suggestions for making it an even more fun thing.
