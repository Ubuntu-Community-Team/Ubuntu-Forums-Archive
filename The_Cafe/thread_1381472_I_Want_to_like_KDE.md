### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2010-01-15
> **Vignesh S said:**
> I used KDE 4, and I loved the see-through glass effects on the desktop, but the default theme didn't do it for me. I'm just too used to Gnome to turn back now :-). I didn't have a problem with the apps though, though yes, it was really annoying that Firefox wasn't installed by default, but rather, Konquerer was used :-(. That didn't quite make the cut for me, and I went back to Gnome. Maybe for another day?

I don't mean to pick on you specifically, and you can certainly use whatever software makes you happy; but I see comments like this all through this thread and all the time on this forum whenever someone brings up KDE.  So I just have to point out two things I wish everyone trying KDE would understand:

 - You don't HAVE TO use KDE applications in KDE.  I've been using KDE for six years now and I've always used firefox and thunderbird because I liked them better.  I use Emacs instead of Kate, OpenOffice instead of Koffice, and Synaptic instead of whatever poo package manager Kubuntu shipped.  I can't understand why people say "I went back to GNOME because the web browser in KDE wasn't good" or something like that.  Use the browser you want to use.

 - Second, and more importantly, half the point of KDE is, and has always been, customization.  The reason KDE has always had a reputation of being big, bloated, and confusing is because there is somewhere a dialog to configure just about any aspect of the desktop you want to configure.  

With KDE 4, the whole point of Plasma is to allow you to reconfigure the desktop any way you want it: don't like the menu?  there are at least two alternatives.  Don't like the panel? Remove it and use a dock.  Don't like the task bar, the clock, the layout, the WHATEVER?  Change it!  They're all just plasma plug-ins, nothing special.  

I'm not saying the implementation is perfect yet, but once you understand what they're trying to do with the desktop it's really cool.  It's sad how many people take one look at the default desktop and say "it looks like Windows, I'm going back to GNOME."  It makes me wish some distro would ship a default desktop that really makes plasma stand up and dance rather than the same tired old Windows layout.
