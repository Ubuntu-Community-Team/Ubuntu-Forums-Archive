### Post by Jucato on 2006-08-23
The GIMP had something going for it and had the chance of becoming a good alternative to Photoshop. Unfortunately, I think it has failed in some aspects: UI (debatable), native CMYK support, an image format (.xcf) that is well-documented (no, source code doesn't count as "well"-documented) and can be used for exchange between other graphics apps, etc. I hope the OpenUsability project will change at least one of these things ([http://www.gimp.org/announcements/open-usability-gimp.html)](http://www.gimp.org/announcements/open-usability-gimp.html)).

I think one of the reasons why people in Linux still stick to the GIMP (if they can't use Photoshop) is because there is currently no other "good enough" alternative available. Pixel is non-free, Krita is a different type of program (more paint than image editor). But if the GIMP doesn't get its act up soon, there's a chance that something better would come around and unseat it. It's a situation that occurs frequently in the open source world.

One thing that puzzles me is that GTK is making a lot of progress, but the GIMP isn't, considering that GTK is the GIMP Tool Kit.
