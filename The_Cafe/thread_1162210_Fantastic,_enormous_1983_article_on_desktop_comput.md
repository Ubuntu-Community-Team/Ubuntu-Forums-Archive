### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-05-17
I discovered one of Google's awesome toys: [Google News Timeline]("http://newstimeline.googlelabs.com/")... which led me to an incredibly fantastic article on the doubt and promise surrounding computers in 1983: [http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,953632-1,00.html](http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,953632-1,00.html)

Some nice quotes:
> "The assertion that all human knowledge is encodable in streams of zeros and ones&#8212;philosophically, that's very hard to swallow..."

> "The desktop revolution has brought the tools that only professionals have had into the hands of the public. God knows what will happen now."

> It would have been possible to single out as Man of the Year one of the engineers or entrepreneurs who masterminded this technological revolution, but no one person has clearly dominated those turbulent events. (Ah, the days when Microsoft did not dominate the entire market)

There's even stuff in there about the possibility of a global network of computers and what that could mean.

It is surprisingly true to how things are *right now*, and lots of bits remind me of free software in particular... which really should get you thinking about a lot of things :)
