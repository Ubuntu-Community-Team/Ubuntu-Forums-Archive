### Post by southernman on 2007-08-30
Hello there 

First thing you should understand is this... take all you know about Windows based OS and toss it out to trash. Your in a different world now and sometimes in that world things will not work as planned. When that happens to you and you can't find the solution on your own, post a thread in one of the support forums and be as concise as possible with the errors you get... along with a descriptive thread title.

Linux isn't meant to be a Windows replacement... it is merely an alternative for everyone to try on for size. Not everyone can use Linux... but I highly suspect you won't be one of those.

One of the only things Windows and Linux have in common is this:

When things go wrong (and it's a given it'll happen sooner or later) you have to do research to fix the problem! It's been my experience (before dumping MS all together) that detailed fixes for Windows problems are buried so deep inside bs you most times just throw your hands up and say wtf! That doesn't mean Linux docs aren't obscure, it does mean that there are a plethora of documentation available at your fingertips.

Providing your going into this venture expecting to have to do a lot of reading... you won't be disappointed with the overall Linux experience. If you expect things to "just work" - your going to hit snags along the way that will make you try to blame the OS, when everyone knows a computer is only as good as the one behind the keyboard.

Post back after you've ran Ubuntu a couple of weeks and let us know how it's going for you so far... if you have time and care to share that is.

Best of Luck
