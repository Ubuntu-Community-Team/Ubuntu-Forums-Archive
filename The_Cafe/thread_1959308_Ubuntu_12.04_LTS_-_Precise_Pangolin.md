Sorry to hear you're having all of the problems. A couple of suggestions to help...

Error reporting. Apport, the error-reporting software, is basically cranked up to high prior to the production release, and then, as I understand it, it is turned off, or at least turned down, so it doesn't yell so much, when the production release comes out. With any OS, you have stuff crash from time to time, but you never know it because the system recovers without injury. I noticed that in my upgrade to 12.04, apport remained on high alert for a few days.

When an upgrade comes out, even the production release, updates come fast and furious, and I find myself updating daily, and sometimes twice daily for the first week or so. The iso you downloaded is an older image, and must be updated again after install, possibly multiple times.

So I would bet if you made sure you did your apt-get dist-upgrade, you would have fewer problems.

I also noticed that on 12.04, there were some driver issues, for both my ATI and Nvidia graphics cards. I had to manually update ATI's catalyst, and a fully-working Nvidia update for my card just came through the repos today, so again, making sure you have updated video drivers might help.
