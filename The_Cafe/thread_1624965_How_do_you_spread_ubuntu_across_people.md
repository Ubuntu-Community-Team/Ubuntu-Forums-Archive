### Post by TBABill on 2010-11-18
I usually don't suggest it to people unless I see they have a need that Ubuntu can fulfill better than their current system. For instance, one person I know has viruses and trojans often so I am asked to clean the computer and get it running normally again. After a few times I suggested he use Ubuntu instead. He has been a Linux user for about 6 months now and enjoys it. But others I have mentioned it to did not receive it so well because they perceive it to be something negative due to a lack of understanding what it is and what it can do.
 
I keep it handy when other users get into a jam with their OS, then show it to them and try to teach them what it can do and how it differs.

### Post by cprofitt on 2010-11-20
I actively evangelize in my community by:

[LIST]
[*] Giving hands-on labs at local educational technology conventions
[*]Holding hands-on workshops in conjunction with my LoCo and the local Hacker Space
[*]Holding Installfests with my local LUG, LoCo and Hacker Space
[*]Talking to people about moving away from Windows after I clean their system after a malware infection
[*]Talk to people who complain about how 'much it costs' to move away from Windows right after they look at Apple computers
[*]Talk to Apple users who complain about the Apple eco-system and tied down nature of their computers
[*]Giving classes at the local senior center on Open Source applications
[*]Discuss Ubuntu on several social media websites that I am a member of
[/LIST]
