I installed ubuntu on my kids' computer because it was old and did a poor job running Windows. I ended up buying them a used computer off ebay which didn't have an OS installed, and ubuntu was a logical choice.  I put it on my computer after a particularly nasty virus.  I put it on my wife's computer because facebook chat wouldn't work on her powerpc mac, and she couldn't update her mac anymore.  I put it on my sister's computer when it would no longer boot (presumably, because of a virus that one of her kids had thoughtfully downloaded).

Of the people mentioned, the only one who is a "geek" (in fact, the only one really computer literate) is me.  Once I got my sister (plus her kids), my wife and my kids set up, however, none of them have had any real problems with ubuntu.  To me, the best way to get linux "out in the world" is to set it up on the computers of people who need it, and show them how it works.  It's the easiest way I've found to make "converts". None of the people I've "converted" care about the fact that it is FOSS or anything like that; they just want something that works.

### Post by plurworldinc on 2010-02-15
I thank it's a wonderful idea, one of the biggest problems with Linux is no one know about it. They may know Linux is out there doing something, but they have no idea of what that something is. 

   Now if you are trying to make a poster to promote Linux, i would recommend adding these points that may help you get started...

1. Since Linux most of the time can run off of a LiveCD make sure the people know they an test out a new OS with out harming there system in any way shape or forum.

2. Mention Dual booting as an option ( just remember dual booting is also something that can scare the average user)

3. Mention it's a great way to bring life back to an older computer that is running WindowsXP or Vista with very little cost to the user.

4. Install the OS for free, but charge for tech support to get them up and running. When ever i helped out a friend the install part was easy, but it does take a little time to get everything just right for.

5. Remind them this is "NOT WINDOWS" no windows problems, but also no Windows software native to the system.

  That should give you a little help to get started, i know it is hard asking other users on this forum because half would like to see Linux grow and the other half just wants to keep it close as a geek tool. Linux is wonderful, but there comes a time when you just have to prove it, tell the world and get the message out , Linux is here, and here to stay.....;)
