### Post by I-75 on 2008-11-08
I think the biggest threat to Microsoft's profits is not the Linux netbooks. But rather the open source mandates by governments, government entities, school systems, cities, corporations, the military. 

Russia is the latest example in mandating open source for all schools in all regions, once a mandate has been made its pretty much set in stone. 

Open source mandates just doesn't mean Linux, although these mandates fall nicely into Linux' lap. But also includes popular programs such as Open Office and Gimp.

Regarding the Linux netbooks, they are exposing many to Linux for the first time. I just don't think the numbers are going to put all that much into the dent for MSFT's bottom line. The problem is for Microsoft, is that if new users see how well Linux runs with the inherent protection from viruses with Linux. There could be a good chance people may not return to Windows. Its not bashing, it is reality.
