### Post by bartos on 2009-01-14
Just wondering why people worry so much about things they can change and something that takes a small amount of time compared to having the machine running.

Boot time.- Booting usually takes about a minute or so. Not worried as my machine will run for the next four hours. So 1 minute out of 240 to load the OS. I suspend so my boot time is about 4 seconds. No big deal either way.

If people have a problem with any boot time then find something to do while it boots. If I boot from cold then it is in passing as I go to do something quick. Clean off your desk or something( admit we all have cluttered desks).

Backgrounds, icons, etc.-These are the easiest things to change and some people will not try a disto because it is ugly. I know first impressions count, but you might be surprised at the OS.
Splash screens are useless as they hide all the good info you can watch also. Good way to find trouble early. I see see windows folk look at my machine booting and ask what all the text is. I tell them that their nice bar going back and forth is hiding it

When I look at an OS, I need it to boot and and work properly. Detect my hardware and use that hardware properly. You don't want BSOD or black or polka dot screens of death. The software should run and stay running. Configuration should work and be easy to adjust.

That is all I ask in an OS , as everything else can be changed. My OS of choice is Mandriva 09 on my machines and Ubuntu on the wifes.

What do you look for in an OS and why?

### Post by Sand &amp; Mercury on 2009-01-14
I agree with the hysteria about boot time. One minute out of the numerous hours my lappy is running per day is no skin off my nose. Shorter boot times would be better for sure though, and I guess there's not much else for us to complain about right now. Ubuntu notoriously boots a bit slower than Windows and Mac, never enough difference to be a dealbreaker, but people always want the best.

As for the default look, it can be important for the very first impression. Especially now that we're seeing laptops sitting in stores with Ubuntu running on them. Eye candy certainly shouldn't be the focus of a distro or OS in general, but having a default look that is eye catching and functional too can go a long way to leaving a potential user with a good first impression.
