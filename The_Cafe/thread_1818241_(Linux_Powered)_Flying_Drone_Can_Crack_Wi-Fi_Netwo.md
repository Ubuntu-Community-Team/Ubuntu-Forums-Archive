### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-08-04
For the Ubuntu enthusiasts, this [[COLOR="Navy"]article[/COLOR]]("http://news.cnet.com/8301-31921_3-20088180-281/wireless-drone-sniffs-wi-fi-bluetooth-phone-signals/") said in the fourth paragraph that the onboard computer is running Ubuntu.
> Their prototype Wi-Fi drone, which was brought on stage yesterday but not flown, is made of reinforced foam and can carry 20 pounds. They added landing gear, a 2.5 horsepower motor powered by lithium polymer batteries, a telemetry link, an onboard computer running Ubuntu, and a payload of wireless sniffers and network-cracking tools. 
The telemetry link probably lets them access the Ubuntu onboard computer that has things like aircrack-ng running on it. The unique part is that it can fly anywhere that can not be accessed from a car while war-driving, and it can track moving signals to anywhere it can be flown.

The prototype has one significant lack of capability:> The drone--which they dubbed WASP, for Wireless Aerial Surveillance Platform--can stay aloft for about an hour. 

I can imagine that governments already have Unmanned Areal Vehicles (UAVs) that can do this; however, this prototype shows that a criminal organization could do it as well. I can imagine that a government's UAV can support flight times much greater that one hour. A government's UAV probably has a lot of other things on them as well.
