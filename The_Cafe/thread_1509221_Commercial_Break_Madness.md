### Post by lovinglinux on 2010-06-14
Yesterday night I wanted to see a good movie. Although is pretty hard to find a good movie being broadcast on TV that I haven't watched yet, I was able to find one on the Warner Channel. The movie was "The Shawshank Redemption" (1994), which has 142 min (2:22) including credits, [according to IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0111161/). 

So, I would expect to finish watching by 2:22, because it started at midnight. But the movie finished at 3:30! It was unbelievable. They stretched the movie by 50% using 15 useless commercials, that only showed other programming of the network. There was only two sets of commercials, almost identical. It was painful to watch. 

This is unacceptable, specially considering that I pay for that channel. There should be a law against this, but...

Does this happens on your country too? Do you think cable/satellite TV networks provides a good service?

My personal opinion is that they are robbing us. Here cable TV is very expensive (even when not HD), they do a lot of reruns, they start broadcasting TV series that have already been canceled in the US and has no ending, move popular series to premium channels without warning, switch from subtitled content to dubbed content in the middle of the series without warning, stretch the content with useless commercials, repeat popular movies over and over (hundreds of times) and so on. When we sign-up for a cable service we have the illusion of buying a good product because they have hundreds of channels 24/7 in the air, but most of the time I want to watch something I like, I can't find anything new. Most channels are crap anyway.

### Post by bash on 2010-06-14
Concerning the original post:

At least in EU the maximum amount of advertising that can be shown is reulated by the most recent revision of the directive covering media and television in general. It is limited to a maximum amount of 12 minutes of commercials per hour. Excluded from this are news casts and shows aimed at children. For those special rules exist that are even stricter.

So at least here in Europe (or more precisly the EU) there are some rules in place to limit this to some degree.
