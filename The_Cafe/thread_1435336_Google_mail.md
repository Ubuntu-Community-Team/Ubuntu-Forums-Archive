### Post by PhilGil on 2010-04-24
I'm going to interrupt the usual Google lovefest for a bit...

I use both Yahoo and Google, and they both have strengths and weaknesses.    I've been with Yahoo for at least 10 years so I still use them as my  primary email.  Yahoo services vary by region, so I'll be referencing the US version of Yahoo mail.

1. Yahoo mail offers unlimited storage, Gmail is 7 GB

2. Google scrapes your email to target your ads, Yahoo scrapes your contact list to promote their social networking services to you.

3. Yahoo has horrific banner ads (which can easily be dealt with using adblock plus), Google does not.

4. The Yahoo web interface is more Outlook like, the Google interface is <ahem> unique.  I personally hate, hate, hate not being able to preview my Gmail before opening it.  It's so much quicker to read emails in a preview pane than to have to click to open each one.  I know this is personal taste, but I much prefer the Yahoo interface.

5.  The Yahoo spam filter seems effective to me, the one complaint I have is that it can sometimes be overly-aggressive.

6.  SMS and Yahoo messenger are seamlessly embedded in their email interface.

7.  The huge win for Google is that they offer free IMAP access.  I think one one of the big reasons Gmail is eating Yahoo mail for lunch is Yahoo's insistence on retaining their walled garden in many of their markets (including the US).  There are a couple of workarounds available, but you still only get POP access, not IMAP.

8.  Yahoo just seems to have lost their will to be innovative in email and calendaring.  When they purchased Zimbra, it seemed like there were going to be some great features on the way.  Instead, they introduced a lame social networking space and some useless embedded apps.
