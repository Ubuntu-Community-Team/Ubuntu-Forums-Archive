### Post by rhardie on 2007-04-23
I am an avid photographer, having taken over 30,000 photos in 2006, and shoot in RAW format vs JPG.

Adobe Labs has  a new RAW imaging software, Lightroom, that was released in February and it appears they won't be supporting Linux.

HOWEVER - an Austin, Texas based Bibble Labs produces a very nice product and they support Linux.

[www.bibblelabs.com](www.bibblelabs.com)

Some of the fine folks on the Lightroom forums believe that anyone running Linux doesn't have time to take photos because they are too busy trying to get their Linux operating systems to work.

The Adobe folks think it's too much trouble to support Linux due to the diversity of the distros and the 1% market share; however, I notice that they have a multitude of software products with overlapping functionality - or redundancy - that requires a great deal of support.

If you enjoy digital photography in a serious way and need a professional application for RAW work flow, consider Bibble.

I'm not any way connected with Bibble  (I use Lightroom at the moment) and receive no benefit from my remarks.  I'm new to Ubuntu and Linux, love it, and want to find as many mainstream ways of doing my professional work within Ubuntu.

### Post by Dixon Bainbridge on 2008-11-18
Don't use any software for RAW conversion other than the bespoke software produced by you camera manufacturer. If thats Nikon, for example, buy Capture NX2, if its Canon, use whatever Canon flog. The difference is just crazy.

I've converted Nikon NEF (RAW) files in LightRoom, Bibble, LightZone and RAWstudio and UFRAW and none of them come any where near Capture NX for quality. Basically, your camera manufacturer made the camera, it created the algorithm for the RAW, it knows how the picture should look. Its especially evident with high ISO RAW images - in lightroom and bibble, I had noticeable noise, but when I convert the same RAW files in Capture NX, there is hardly any.

If you're not that serious about image quality, then yeah, use Bibble or whatever for conversion.
