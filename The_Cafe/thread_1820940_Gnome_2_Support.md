### Post by ScionicSpectre on 2011-08-08
There are incompatibilities among some of the GNOME elements that would have to be handled delicately by packagers. For the most part, gnome-panel is the significant element of GNOME 2 that has changed in GNOME 3 (that is, a refined applet system to which all previous GNOME applets haven't been ported). The new gnome-panel in GNOME 3 is quite good and worth taking a look at in Fallback Mode. Aside from this, Xfce is quite good, but if you really want the old-fashioned GNOME panel and perhaps some of the older applications, take a look at the MATE desktop environment. It already has quite a few users, and it may be worth paying attention to as an alternative. I wouldn't be surprised if there were a PPA for it soon, since it's being actively used on Arch and has its own repository just for Arch.

However, I'd recommend you look at the new gnome-panel or XFCE first to see if they suit your needs before going through the trouble. It's also quite likely that Linux Mint will port their specific applets to gnome-panel 3 and continue to use it for the foreseeable future with the GNOME 3 platform.

### Post by ScionicSpectre on 2011-08-09
Danyc, while GNOME 3 is already quite customizable (with project Sweet Tooth the extensions framework will become official with a solid API), the customizations that originate will not likely be the same kind that were available for GNOME 2, since users of GNOME 3 are a far different audience. While there are extensions available for almost anything you could do with applets in GNOME 2 already, the Activities menu is a feature that most GNOME users are sticking with 3 love, and endorse fully. It's not going away by any means, although there is an alternative menu similar to the Applications/Places already there.

Of course, you can always use gnome-panel as has been mentioned before. However, I think XFCE or KDE would suit your interest in diverse and flexible customization quite well. There aren't a lot of things you could do in GNOME 2 that you can't do in XFCE.
