### Post by troymcdavis on 2007-03-29
I have a confession to make. I've been slacking off.

When I got here, all I did was hang out in the cafe, not really contributing much at all. It wasn't long before I changed that.

It was two months ago that I decided to begin a vigorous "no thread left behind" program. I pledged to answer one unanswered plea for help in this forum everyday. I missed days, for sure, but that didn't bother me. It's just now that I've realized how badly (and how long ago) I fell off the wagon.

I wanted to write something up, but I'm not one for melodrama, so I operated quietly on my own. But I saw daynah's sig, and, sadly enough, my own with its quote from WalmartSniper, and realized I needed to reinvigorate my mission. This is all about community, right? Why not call on the community?

I realized today I have nothing to lose by appealing to the community, and so much to gain. We can encourage and challenge one another. Together we can achieve far more than anyone can alone. Best of all, an experience like this can bring us a little bit closer together.

Here, standing at the turn of the month, with the release of Fiesty on the horizon, I want to pledge to answer at least five unanswered request threads every week for the month of April. Over 31 days, that's about 20-21 threads. I feel up to the challenge.

So, for the benefit of the community, I encourage everyone to pledge what support they can give for the month of April. It's easy to find questions that just require a little googling, or you can ask some follow up questions; at the very least you can bump a thread up. Think about goals you can meet: one thread a day, one a week, etc. 

For me, I plan to make April a commitment to "leaving no thread behind."
