It is my understanding that Ugg is short for Ugly or Fugly:

**Ugg boots**, or simply **uggs**, is a [generic]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generic") term for a type of [sheepskin]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheepskin") [boot]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boot") which has been made in [Australia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia") and [New Zealand]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand") for nearly 200 years. They were originally called *ug boots* or *ugh boots*, which is short for "ugly"[[1]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ugg_boots#_note-0"), and sometimes referred to as "fuggs" or "fugly." Due to [trademark]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark") issues ([see below]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ugg_boots#Trademark_issues")), uggs are often known in the retail world by the more generic term **sheepskin boots**. Other popular brands of sheepskin boots are [Warmbat]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warmbat"), Emu, Ugly, [Uggs-N-Rugs]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uggs-N-Rugs") and Aussie Dogs.[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ugg_boots](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ugg_boots)
