### Post by samjh on 2008-06-04
Net neutrality?  It's a mere illusion and nothing more.

The Chinese government can censor the net so that the majority of Chinese net users haven't got a clue about what goes on in the wider world of the internet - and by extension, the world at large.  Some technically proficient users manage to find ways around it, but on the whole, the fact that a government institution can censor an entire country's internet access is a big hint that net neutrality is easily violated.

As for companies violating net neutrality, ISPs are traditionally fiercely opposed to outside attempts to influence their services.  Any type of access control is a cost to the ISP, and adds to user inconvenience, and therefore user frustration.  ISPs are also acutely aware of the fact that its technically savvy clients, who have great influence over the entire internet market, place heavy value on privacy and freedom of access.

The only realistic way for businesses to influence net neutrality on a global scale, is to lobby governments to introduce access controls by legislation on the argument of better "security".
