### Post by aaaantoine on 2008-10-02
I am of the belief that you should buy the Dell with Ubuntu preinstalled (as opposed to a Windows Dell) for two reasons:

1. It shows Dell that there is actually a market for computers preinstalled with Ubuntu / Linux, and they will continue to market it.  This will help to raise Linux awareness.
2. Letting Dell test for 100% hardware compatibility takes the pressure off the consumer to do likewise.  This grants them a small leeway in pricing. They have to make up for R&D costs, do they not?

It doesn't make good business sense to sell Linux PCs at a loss, unless you are really adamant about promoting free software.  Maybe Dell is waiting for further improvements to Ubuntu before they begin actively marketing it.  Have you considered this possibility?

### Post by notwen on 2008-10-02
There's a horrible generalization going around that Windows Machines offered by Dell are cheaper than the same machines offered w/ Ubuntu.  It's called promotional deals.  There was a huge ruckus revolving around free hardware upgrades when Dell first began offering Ubuntu machines and this promo was not available w/ Ubuntu machines.  There was enough demand for it and Dell corrected this.  

I purchased a Inspiron 1420n when Dell first began offering them.  I will eventually pick up one of the new Inspiron Mini 9s or the 10in counterpart that is due out sooner or later.  

It bothers me to see people constantly complaining about this issue, if you're wanting to support a large OEM offering pre-installed Ubuntu machines, then don't let a price difference deter you enough to complain about it.  Buy the Windows equivalent machine, do not accept the EULA, get re-imbursed your difference and install Ubuntu or your distro of choice.  Otherwise help support Ubuntu being installed by large OEMs and do what you had intended to do in the first place.  =]
