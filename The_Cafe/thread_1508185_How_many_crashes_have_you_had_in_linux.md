### Post by dtfinch on 2010-06-12
I chose over 20.

There were a couple years when I first switched to Linux where my video chipset (intel 845G) wasn't very well supported, so it'd crash after a minute or two of anything 3D. The problem had actually been fixed in XFree86 for some time, but this was after the big licensing fork and it took a while for X.org to get a similar fix. Also had a day of crashes when I tried to install a semi-supported wireless card.

I've witnessed two production server crashes, both due to physical hardware failures. One was a raid that lost both drives (maybe heat). The other was a ram failure, with no spares handly, though I managed to bring it back up temporarily with the kernel option "mem=128M" (out of 1gb), which seemed to avoid the damaged area until the new ram arrived. I also had a server not come back after a reboot, as though the MBR had been wiped out, which shouldn't have been possible. I was able to use a livecd to reinstall grub, after which it worked again.

On my current system, I haven't had Linux itself crash, but occasionally Portal on Wine crashes X as it exits and I have to re-login.
