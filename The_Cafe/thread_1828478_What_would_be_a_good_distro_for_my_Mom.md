### Post by neu5eeCh on 2011-08-19
Don't use Ubuntu/Unity. I, personally, would never unload Unity on a novice. 

As others say, use Ubuntu LTS or go with an Ubuntu respin that uses Gnome 2, like Mint or Elementary OS. I would probably avoid KDE. It's generally sluggish and its settings are byzantine to a novice.

In fact, for some Windows users I recommend LXDE. These are the users who couldn't care less what their desktop looks like. They don't fuss, fiddle and faddle like the rest of us. They have better things to do with their lives - like actually use the software. For them, something like Peppermint OS, WattOS4 or Lubuntu is ideal. They'll love it because it's fast as hell, simple to navigate, and they'll never notice how non-configurable LXDE can be compared to the boat, the Cadillac Sedan Deville, that is KDE, or the high strung iPhone OS that Unity has turned into.

Seriously, consider an Ubuntu based LXDE respin. I've had great luck with these.

### Post by forrestcupp on 2011-08-19
People who say GnuCash is a good alternative for Quickbooks have never really used Quickbooks. It might be a decent alternative to Quicken (which is nothing like Quickbooks), but there are no native Linux programs that are a good alternative to Quickbooks.

Also, Wine is not a good solution for Quickbooks. People who use Quickbooks have very sensitive information that shouldn't be screwed around with. Also, a lot of 3rd party addons will definitely not work with Wine.

Your best bet for that would be to run it in a virtual machine. The problem with that is that the information is probably very important, and if you accidentally delete your virtual machine file, you lose everything.
