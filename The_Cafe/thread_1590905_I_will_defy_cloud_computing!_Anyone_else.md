### Post by oldsoundguy on 2010-12-04
adding a line or three:
Most responses seem to indicate an erroneous interpretation of the cloud. It is NOT storage, although that is part of it.
If you used Intuit on line this last year to do your taxes .. you were using the cloud
If someone you know uses the new Windows 7 photo processor, they are using the cloud.
The long term intent of cloud computing is to have the software program held on a site and for you to visit, launch the program and do your work, and leave .. and get the bill for usage next month.
Adobe is moving that way right now.
The only thing that will eventually be REQUIRED on your computer will be a browser (and an OS to fire up the machine, sound and video).
Cloud computing will be totally cross platform.  You will be able to run Photo Shop in the cloud with your Linux based computer and browser without jumping through the hoops of an install in Wine, for instance.  But you will be charged for usage.  How that is to be done is yet to be determined.

That means no more "buying" programs and installing them on your computer.

Will be quite a bit of time before all of this transpires, but that is the way the software makers want it as that will KILL piracy and put the profits into the originating companies instead of the Chinese Tongs.

How the FOSS outfits are going to cope with this is TOTALLY unknown.

Most responses seem to indicate an erroneous interpretation of the cloud. It is NOT storage, although that is part of it.
If you used Intuit on line this last year to do your taxes .. you were using the cloud
If someone you know uses the new Windows 7 photo processor, they are using the cloud.
The long term intent of cloud computing is to have the software program held on a site and for you to visit, launch the program and do your work, and leave .. and get the bill for usage next month.
Adobe is moving that way right now.
The only thing that will eventually be REQUIRED on your computer will be a browser (and an OS to fire up the machine, sound and video).
Cloud computing will be totally cross platform.  You will be able to run Photo Shop in the cloud with your Linux based computer and browser without jumping through the hoops of an install in Wine, for instance.  But you will be charged for usage.  How that is to be done is yet to be determined.

That means no more "buying" programs and installing them on your computer.

Will be quite a bit of time before all of this transpires, but that is the way the software makers want it as that will KILL piracy and put the profits into the originating companies instead of the Chinese Tongs.

How the FOSS outfits are going to cope with this is TOTALLY unknown.

Free/libre software gives you the opportunity that proprietary software doesn't  and that is to control what is executing on your computer because you have insight in the source code. Proprietary programs might be doing *anything* with your computer and you wouldn't know it.

And now people want to make perhaps the only bigger mistake which is to totally take away their freedom to control what gets done with their information by using programs that not only are closed source, but they aren't even on your computer. And you want to hand your information to such programs?

### Post by Paqman on 2010-12-07
"Cloud" is one of the vague terms that means so many different things that it means almost nothing.

[LIST]
[*]If you're a big company like Amazon or Google, your "cloud" is your millions of VPSes, and storage that you rent out to your clients.
[*]Or maybe the cloud is the online apps that you use, like Gmail, Dropbox, Twitter and Flickr.
[*]Or maybe it's a future operating system that acts like a thin client
[/LIST]

If you're thinking of the first two, the cloud is nothing new, and has been mainstream for a long time. It's just got a new name now. Big organisations virtualise everything these days, for ease of management. Even small organisations looking to scale do it.

The last one on my list seems to be the one people most freak out about on this forum. Nobody is going to force you to use a cloud-based OS, so why worry? You use a guerilla OS already, you aren't locked into anything.
