### Post by t0p on 2011-03-22
OP: Good luck!  (You're gonna need it!)

As ikt mentioned, for your forum to survive amongst all the others that have gone to the wall, it needs a USP (unique selling point).  Linuxproforum.com needs to offer something that other forums don't.  You can look at Ubuntuforums.org and think "Well if they can do it, so can I!"  But Ubuntuforums.org has survived in large part because it's the "unofficial" user support forum (I say "unofficial" bcause, even though it's now hosted on Canonical servers, it still maintains a large degree of independence).  Also, lots of Linux users are willing to help out - not just admins and mods, but also average common or garden users who log on regularly to see if they can help someone.  Also, Ubuntuforums.org has a wonderful Community Cafe, which really helps with community building.

So you need to look at other forums, figure out what they are failing to provide, then provide that "thing" by the bucketload on your site.

Also, it doesn't help your publicity when you get the url wrong in your announcement post!  ;)
