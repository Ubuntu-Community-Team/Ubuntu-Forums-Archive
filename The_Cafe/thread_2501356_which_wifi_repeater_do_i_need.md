I recognise your need. I also am ignorant in networking. I did find this from an internet search:

[https://forum.dd-wrt.com/wiki/index.php/What_is_DD-WRT%3F](https://forum.dd-wrt.com/wiki/index.php/What_is_DD-WRT%3F)

> **DD-WRT** is third party [firmware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware")  released under the terms of the GPL for many Wi-Fi 4 and Wi-Fi 5  wireless routers based on a Broadcom or Atheros chip reference design.

That will limit your choice of router/modem. Does it limit the choice of WiFi repeater/expander? I am not sure. From only a little reading it seems to me that DD-WRT is used to replace the firmware of a router/modem. Would you need to replace the firmware of a WiFi repeater/expander? I wonder about that.

You must already have a router/modem. Is it already running DD-WRT firmware? From what I have read replacing the router firmware carries the very real risk of bricking (making unusable) the router. Are you not confident with the standard encryption methods already in use.
