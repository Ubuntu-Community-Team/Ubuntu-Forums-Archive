### Post by arvevans on 2011-03-21
This "AT&T" is not the same company that was called AT&T prior to Judge Green disbanding it in 1984.  The assets of that AT&T were subsequently purchased by Southern Bell Corporation (SBC) who changed their name to AT&T in 2005.  At the same time Judge Green disbanded AT&T he also killed Bell Telephone Laboratories, Western Electric, and the interface standards agreement between AT&T and all US telephone operating companies, including the local Bell Operating Companies.  The original AT&T was formed by US government mandate in 1886 for the purpose of bringing and enforcing standardization to the nation's myriad of then incompatible telephone systems.

### Post by jwbrase on 2011-03-22
> **tommywright said:**
> It's Capitalism out of control I tell ya.

Out of control, yes. Capitalism, no. Companies like AT&T want you to *think* that they're all about capitalism and free markets, because free markets are good for the customer. But they don't really want a free market, because that makes business more dangerous for them. Some upstart competitor might make a better, cheaper product and steal their market share. They just want a market that's unfree in their favor.

> **arvevans said:**
> This "AT&T" is not the same company that was called AT&T prior to Judge Green disbanding it in 1984.  The assets of that AT&T were subsequently purchased by Southern Bell Corporation (SBC) who changed their name to AT&T in 2005.  At the same time Judge Green disbanded AT&T he also killed Bell Telephone Laboratories, Western Electric, and the interface standards agreement between AT&T and all US telephone operating companies, including the local Bell Operating Companies.  The original AT&T was formed by US government mandate in 1886 for the purpose of bringing and enforcing standardization to the nation's myriad of then incompatible telephone systems.

Well, it's a bit more complex than that. SBC had been a child company of AT&T before the breakup. They've now bought out their former parent company and many of their sibling companies that the original AT&T spun off when it was broken up. A lot of what's left has been lapped up by Verizon (another former part of AT&T under a new brand).

So the current AT&T isn't entirely the original AT&T, but it's not entirely *not* the original AT&T either. It's more like the original AT&T cut into pieces, stuffed in a blender, and then randomly half-glued-back-together.
