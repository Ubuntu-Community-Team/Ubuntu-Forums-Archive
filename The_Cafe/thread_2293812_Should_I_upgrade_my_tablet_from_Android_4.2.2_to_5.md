### Post by mystics on 2015-09-07
I run a custom ROM (specifically CyanogenMod), so I'm not sure what things are like for more official versions. I do remember that, before flashing a ROM, Android 5 had absolutely horrible battery life, and memory leak issues made the phone unusable at times. However, I think Google fixed those by 5.1.0. At the very least, I know that it hadn't fixed them by 5.0.1 and 5.0.2, and when people starting suffering seriously on some devices (some issues with 5.0.1 specifically caused overheating issues that bricked some Nexus 7s), people were advised to upgrade to 5.1.0 as quickly as possible.

All that aside, though, Android 5 wasn't exactly a huge step forward. It introduced Material Design, which gave some aesthetic improvements and made the interface easier to interact with. It also improved the whole notification system, such as adding them to the lockscreen and giving user control options in the notification bar. Beyond those reasons, I've seen little reason to upgrade to Android 5. If you don't like the aesthetics and can get the better notification system with third-party apps (which I'm sure you can), then I wouldn't advise an upgrade unless you need it.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2015-09-08
I now have both an LG Stylo phone and an LG tablet running Android 5.  I don't know about the battery life issue because I haven't compared 4 and 5 on the same hardware.  A full charge on either of my LG devices lasts at least a day, but I'm not using my phone constantly like some people do.  Otherwise I'm happy enough with Android 5, especially since there were a couple of apps that I couldn't load on my Galaxy S3 because they required at least Android 4.4 while the S3 was running 4.3.

On the other hand, I generally subscribe to the adage, "If it ain't broke, don't fix it."  Unless you can identify specific features of Android 5 that you might use, why bother?
