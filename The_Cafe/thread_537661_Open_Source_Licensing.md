Let's not be too hasty to disregard open source licenses for two reasons.

1) The case has not even been decided yet. Its precedents at this point are not nearly as important as the final decision in the case. Moreover, the current injunction could stick, or the monetary damages could be high enough that it warns off other violators.
2) As the article pointed out, there is little precedent. So while this sets precedent, there are many other kinds of licenses to be tested under other circumstances in other jurisdictions where judges can decide differently.
