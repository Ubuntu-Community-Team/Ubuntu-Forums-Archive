### Post by Erik Trybom on 2007-09-17
I lean more towards topic-specific searches:

Google for finding web sites or solutions to problems.
Wikipedia for finding information or explanations about a subject.
Imdb for anything movie-related.
Ultimate Guitar for guitar tabs.
Ubuntu Forums for Ubuntu-related problems.

...And so on. Best thing is, Opera makes it very easy to search in this way. Typing "g elephants" in the address bar will make a Google search for elephants. "w elephants" does a Wikipedia search instead. You can associate whatever search you like with a letter or keyword. (Firefox's search tool is also nice of course.)

### Post by t0p on 2007-09-17
I have used 3 search engines with reasonable regularity - enough to judge which one is "better".  The 3 are google, altavista and yahoo.

Yahoo is crap - is it even a real search engine?  It seems full of "sponsored" sites, and "directories" that have been constructed according to one person's preferences, rather than lists created by "spiders".  I hate Yahoo... but I use Yahoo! Messenger (through gaim of course), so I'm just a hypocrite maybe.  I did use Yahoo Groups for a while too, but lost interest with them.

Altavista is a better search engine than Yahoo - but that's not saying much, considering how crap Yahoo is.  Altavista's translator "babel fish" can be handy, but it can also mangle all the meaning out of sentences.

The best search engine I've used is Google - hell, I even have [www.google.com](www.google.com) as my homepage sometimes!  There are some great sites that explain the syntax that can be used in the search field to really narrow down your results.  The old "intitle:index.of. mp3 my favorite band" is a famous one.  But the syntax can get very complicated and very precise - and that, combined with the way the lists are produced "mechanically" by the spiders, apparently guided by site popularity, makes Google the most accurate engine.  Of course, you get offered lots of inappropriate results too, but I guess that's what happens when you try to give intelligence to an advertisement machine - which is what Google is really.

The best way to search, as said above by "Erik Trybom", is to use appropriate sites rather than an engine.  Wikipedia can be good (but you must always remember that what's written there may be pure BS).  And ubuntuforums.org for ubuntu related searches... and the other distros sites if you use them...

Maybe the "randomness" of stumbleupon.com is really the best.  It's worth installing the toolbar anyway, as stumbling can keep you amused when you should be working instead!
