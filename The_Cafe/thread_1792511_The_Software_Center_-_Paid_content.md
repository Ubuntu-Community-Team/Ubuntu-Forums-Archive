### Post by ZarathustraDK on 2011-06-28
No this is not a rant against paid content :) I've just been wondering about a couple of things in regards to the Software center:

1. If you've previously purchased an app (before USC), is it then possible to "authenticate" the USC-version of the same app if the package-name is the same, using the old programme? Asking because I bought the Humble Indie Bundle, and now games like Braid are popping up in USC; was thinking that it'd be nice to have ones purchases validated through Ubuntu-One, instead of a gazillion different game-specific pages that you've forgotten the login-password to.

2. What keeps people who have purchased a copy of a game through USC from simply installing it on all their friends pc's by logging into their Ubuntu-One account on their friends pc as admin? Saying it because if Canonical wants to attract major players to USC and have them port their stuff to Linux, then I'm sure they'd want some sort of authentication that's a bit more strict.
Not that I mind lack of DRM as a user, but it might prove a deterrent to some of the powers that can get the ball rolling our way...
