### Post by Chibone on 2008-01-11
If you're bored, you could:

- browse Add/Remove Programs and try new applications
- wander aimlessly around Ubuntu Forums, or aim to help beginners
- install (Edu/X/K)ubuntu and try out another desktop environment
- find some free entertainment on Newgrounds/Kongregate/JayIsGames/etc.
- read the news from Digg/Slashdot/Reddit/other social news aggregator, or read some actual news websites
- learn a real life skill by reading online tutorials

Or you can think "What did people ever do without computers?" and then go do it.

### Post by some_random_noob on 2008-01-12
- Read the news (Digg, BBC or whatever local news you can get).
- Try out new applications/games.
- Try customizing the look of Ubuntu (An excellent time waster :) ).
- Work on some sort of project (Try programming, try making a news/community site or something useful).

The best way to avoid being bored is to have hobbies; It doesn't matter if it's real life or something on your computer. But preferably have a mix - spending all day on the computer isn't healthy. 

Trying something new is always a good idea. If you take up programming or themeing then who knows what kind of cool stuff you could come up with. If you want a non-computer hobby then I would suggest life-skills (i.e. grow a blueberry bush and make yourself a crumble :D ).

Remember people: 
[SIZE="3"][COLOR="Red"]"Time wasted is life wasted"[/COLOR][/SIZE]
