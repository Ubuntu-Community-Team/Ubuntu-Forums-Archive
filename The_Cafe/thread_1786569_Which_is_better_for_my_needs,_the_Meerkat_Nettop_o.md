### Post by kaldor on 2011-06-21
Nettops are not meant for "real" work. Think netbooks; cheap desktop PC for simple tasks.

> I'm looking for a computer that serves the purpose of music editing, photo editing, video editing, media entertainment, word processing, internet browsing, social networking, and document storage.

The nettop will be perfect for the document/web part. But photo editing, music editing, video editing, and multimedia you will *not* enjoy such a low powered machine. If you want the best option for your money and needs, grab the Wildebeest or Wild Dog and get a minimum of 4 GB of RAM. That should be fine and will last a long while.

Also, Ubuntu isn't the best choice for Video editing. Openshot, Kdenlive, etc, are not very reliable and were nothing but trouble for me. I'm well aware that Pixar uses Linux for their production, but it's not like you'll be using that same expensive software. It's a sad area that Linux just lacks in. As much as I support Linux, this really is a setback for a lot of people. Aside from Video, Linux is a great OS for everything you've mentioned. Just don't expect much on the video editing side.

### Post by forrestcupp on 2011-06-21
Let me tell you from experience that nettops are not suitable for what you want. I have an ION nettop, and it does great at some things, but not others. The limitations are in the small number of PCIe lanes. What that means is that you can do one lane-requiring thing good, but not more than that.

A good example is that my nettop does a great job of displaying video that is on the hard drive, even at 1080p. But the wifi uses up one of the lanes, so if you're trying to stream video from the internet and display it on the screen at the same time, you have serious limitations. You can't watch any videos from Hulu in full screen, even in low res, without it being way too choppy to watch. Aside from that, their general performance is pretty slow. They're not made for hardcore work, like editing videos.

[Here is a good article explaining what I'm talking about](http://www.anandtech.com/show/3702/zotacs-zbox-hdid11-review-next-gen-ion-better-worse-than-ion1). I wish I never would have bought mine.

You should be able to find a good Intel Core I3 laptop with HDMI in your price range that would do much better.
