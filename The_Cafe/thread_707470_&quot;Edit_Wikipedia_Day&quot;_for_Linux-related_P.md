[SIZE="4"]**Edit Wikipedia Day for Linux-related Projects**[/SIZE] -- *March 12th, 2008*

My proposal.

_**Why?**_
I'm a heavy believer in Wikipedia's impetus of making knowledge freely available.

Why do I believe we should have a Linux-related Wikipedia day? Because like many others, I do a lot of research on Wikipedia (even if I take it with a grain of salt) because there is such a wealth of information available on one website. Many Linux-related articles however, seem to be lacking rich content that can be found in many other articles. Ubuntu's article however, is an exception and was a former featured article.

_**Personal efforts not enough**_
I've done a lot of editing on the Ubuntu article by adding content (features in Ubuntu's release history, download links) and screenshots (by downloading and install every single version of Ubuntu in VMware), fixing spelling and grammar, creating redirects for Ubuntu versions, creating colour templates for tables, etc.

In fact, I sometimes [overload the Ubuntu article]("http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ubuntu_%28Linux_distribution%29&diff=prev&oldid=161917900#Releases") with information.

I still feel though, that my efforts are not enough.

_**Other articles need help**_
So I recently went around to various Linux and Ubuntu related articles and noticed they are in dire need of help.

I was hoping we, the Ubuntu community, could help the software we know, love and use everyday in Ubuntu by adding any resource we can to their articles.

**_Hopes_**
Specifically, I'm advocating that we all log on to Wikipedia on March 12, 2008 and edit five articles regarding software used on Ubuntu linux.

I'm hoping we can blog about this day and push it across most Linux distributions.
