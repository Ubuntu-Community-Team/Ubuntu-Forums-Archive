### Post by Rhubarb on 2009-03-27
> **Firestem4 said:**
> Thats really cool. I bike ride quite frequently and I ride through a lot of backroad area's down here in Southern California. 

Any more info on how that site works or how I could contribute? I unfortunately don't think I hav ethe trnasfer software for my GPS anymore :*(

To edit / upload traces into openstreetmap.org, you can use potlatch - which is the online flash-based editor.
Or you can use a program called JOSM - [http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/index.php/JOSM](http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/index.php/JOSM)

There's a few other apps that can be used for editing, it's just a matter of personal preference.

I'm not too sure about transfer software for GPS devices - As I only buy GPS units that are easy to transfer (usb mass storage device).
If you can manage to get a GPS trace from your GPS unit, then you may need to translate the file into something that JOSM can recognize better - like .nmea or .gpx or .osm.
A really application for doing this is **gpsbabel**.

You'll need to sign up to [http://www.openstreetmap.org/](http://www.openstreetmap.org/) to get yourself a username and password first.

Hope this is of help
-Rhubarb
