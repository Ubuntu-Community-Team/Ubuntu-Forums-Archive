Those articles focus on Q3, the first quarter in history in which PC sales have shown any decline, yet last I read for the year as a whole growth is  largely flat.

That's pretty significant, because we have to keep in mind we're talking about growth rates.   Folks aren't throwing out their PCs.  Instead they're buying tens of millions of new PC every year, added to the tens of millions of PCs they already have.

Sure, some rare cases will find tablets completely satisfying as a replacement, but so far the most adamant of such arguments were written on PCs. ;)

I rarely quote MS execs, but one (wish I could remember his name) said last year, "The tablet of the future is your phone."

I think that says it all.  Even the 7" tablets won't fit in a pocket.  The phone is the one computer you can have with you all the time.

As the portability of computing increases, it allows us to be reconsider our options when we're back at home or at the office.   While this is anecdotal, I've been finding a surprising number of friends tell me that as their phones got more powerful, they've ditched other portable devices and buy good desktops to get work done and play games.

Tablets are a fun "also", but you have to hold them and give up half your screen when you want to type.

When you add a keyboard to solve those two limitations, what then is the difference between that and an ultrabook?

Ah, the i5. ;)
