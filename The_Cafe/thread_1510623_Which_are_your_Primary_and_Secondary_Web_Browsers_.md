### Post by dtfinch on 2010-06-18
On Linux, Firefox is pretty much the only browser I use.

On Windows, until a few weeks ago, Chrome was my primary browser, and I'd occasionally use Firefox. Now it's the other way around.

Lately, I've gotten tired of the Chrome bugs still open since the first release. The big one is the middle click bug where Chrome fires the left onclick event when your intention was to open a link in a new tab. Rather than try to fix it themselves they decided to wait for a fix from Apple, which after 2 years you know isn't coming because most Mac users don't middle click.

There's also Chrome's poor history management, causing it to eventually start slower than even Firefox after a couple months, with the only option being to completely clear ALL history, lacking any sort of "clear older than X days" feature.

And Google has no respect for user choice when it comes to automatic forced silent upgrades.

My big reason for switching to Chrome in the first place was because it originally started in 1 second vs 20-30 seconds for Firefox after a cold boot. Now they both start in about 5.

Firefox extensions:
bug489729 - prevents accidentally creating a new window when clicking on a tab.
Flashblock
No Fade In Google
OmniBar - combines address bar and search so it's more like Chrome
Smart Stop/Reload - merges them into one button
Unsorted bookmarks folder - makes new bookmarks appear in a submenu under bookmarks, like they should have in the first place

And I have my Firefox layout arranged to closely resemble Chrome. All on one bar, buttons on the left, menu on the right, with the omnibar in the center.
