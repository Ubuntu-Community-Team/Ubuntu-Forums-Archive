### Post by bonzodog on 2007-03-04
Ubuntu is developed by full time paid Developers, who work under the Ubuntu Foundation, which is part of Canonical. 

We are not privy to their development methods, but I believe that when they make changes to a piece of software they upload their changes to a server somewhere, and each developer has set tasks that they work on alone. 
The developers are all over the world, and work from home, and are given Laptops by Mark Shuttleworth to do the work on.
It seems most of their work is bug-fixing, and they tend to use basic tools such as Vim or Geany to do the coding with.
The only other distro that has full time paid developers is Suse linux, which is part of Novell, and as such, the developers work for them.

Remember, most of the Software in ubuntu IS NOT actually written by them, it's written by some hobby-coder-geek at home in his spare time. The Ubuntu devels just bring this together, and do bug fixing coding, which they then upload to 'upstream', which is often Sourceforge.  They also sometimes fork others projects, to provide an extra feature for Ubuntu users. A lot of the developers for Ubuntu also work with the Gnome Foundation, and help build and develop Gnome apps, which will actually be used by all the other distros.
