### Post by The Flying Penguin on 2010-05-21
I have to say portability is the main thing I look at when I shop for a new notebook (aside from Linux compatibility). I bought my first ultra-portable, a Dell X300, a few of years ago off Ebay and immediately fell in love with how easy it was to carry around. From that point on I knew I would never carry around a bulky laptop on a daily basis.

Since then I've been through an Acer Aspire One, MSI Wind u210, and finally an Asus ul30vt. I know I've been through too many notebooks in the last few years but now I've finally honed in on something that is powerful enough to be used as my main machine but still is portable enough to carry around everywhere. The great battery life is certainly a plus too!

Btw, I also have a Fujitsu P1610 convertible that I use when I really  want portability and just want to bring something with in case I need to  quickly look something up or if I am going to have limited surface  space to work at like in a bar or something.

I know some people who are only interested in the guts, others who want big screens, and some who expect to have Blueray so they can watch movies. For me, optical drives are just extra bulk since I generally just use ISOs or my USB DVD drive.

So what style do you prefer?

I'm interested to see what most people here use.
