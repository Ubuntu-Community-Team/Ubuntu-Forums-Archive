### Post by gnomeuser on 2010-03-16
Must haves:

Firefly
Dollhouse
V
Journeyman (while not that sciency, the characters and stories carried this show far, to bad it was cancelled)
Futurama (while a cartoon it has very good science in it at times and it is hella funny, glad to see it return.)

I am also currently watching Charlie Jade, only watched the pilot but it looks promising. Sorta Film Noir meets Dark City in look. Not quite sold on it yet.

Generally I find Battlestar to be uninspired, while their science advisor keeps them generally on the straight and narrow, the storytelling is pointlessly illogical and boring. 

Fringe is what Battlestar is but without a science advisor at all, I watched the pilot and have since utterly refused to waste more time on it. I was hoping it would be as dark and interesting as the X Files or Twin Peaks was (both excellent, but again not really sci-fi) but it really wasn't either.

I do quite enjoy the first few seasons of Stargate SG1, it is funny, the story ideas are generally good but the science is way off the deep end which is distracting. I am not really ready to consider it science fiction. To me science fiction is about taking what we have now and extrapolating it to the near or far future. 

In the same vein, a fantasy show that has magic in it really isn't science fiction, it has nothing to do with science.. it's frikkin magic. Magic isn't a bad thing to pretend is real but it ain't science.

I have been wanting to see FlashForward, I know the author of the book it is based on knows his science and I have high hopes for the show. I haven't had time to invest in seeing it yet though. I would wager this would be a good show if done right.

One thing I would like to see, the old roleplaying game Deadlands had an interesting steam punk wild west setting. I think that would make a great TV show, with a wide range of subjects to deal with. I am really hoping someone will take the time to do it right. As a game it isn't that interesting to me but the setting would be fantastic for a character driven show that slowly unfolds the many threads the setting goes down.
