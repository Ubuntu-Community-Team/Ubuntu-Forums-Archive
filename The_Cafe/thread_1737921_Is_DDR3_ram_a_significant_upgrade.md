### Post by undecim on 2011-04-24
It's not that big a performance difference. Almost all DDR2 and DDR3 have the same latencies (about 10 ns; High end ram you can get down to about 9 ns), and from what I've seen (i.e read about and/or subjectively experienced), the bandwidth doesn't make a significant difference.

If it were me, I would rather have more DDR2 ram than less DDR3. Or perhaps spend the difference on a processor with a large cache. That will give you better performance in most cases.

EDIT: I should note that motherboards that support DDR3 generally have better memory controllers, being more recently manufactured. That will have a significant impact on performance, even if you only use DDR2 ram.

### Post by rJ~ on 2011-04-24
As far as I know, going from DDR2 to DDR3 is not really a big upgrade, unless you're doing specific tasks that require high memory bandwidth. For gaming and everyday use you wouldn't notice the difference by just upgrading motherboard and RAM (assuming your current motherboard can't support DDR2 and DDR3, most motherboards don't support both).
