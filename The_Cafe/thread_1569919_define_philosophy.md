### Post by koenn on 2010-09-07
philosophy is a rational search for truth, for an answer to the question "why are things the way the are'. 

That's a rather broad field, and over the ages, specialized subsections emerged -- these are now what we call 'science'. That's why all (or most ?) academic studies can still lead to a title of PdD - Doctor of Philosophy. Another side effect is thet philosophy, in a more narrow sence occupies itself with subjects not (entirely) covered by other sciences (such as the nature of knowledge and the validity of ways to acquire it, the nature of language and meaning, ... ) or by addressing them on a higher level of abstraction.
