### Post by chessnerd on 2010-01-06
I have one that's 14 years young.

It's my favorite for four reasons:

1. It came from my family's first computer, which I will always have a soft spot in my heart for.

2. It has the old "Gateway 2000" logo with the gold "G" and full name, which is retro and way cooler than the "cow box."

3. I found it and nursed it back to health:
I needed a keyboard for my Linux desktop and I had recently found this old keyboard. So, I decided to clean it off and use it. The problem was, it was REALLY dirty (I'm talking dust, grime, split pop from nine years ago, etc.). But, that didn't stop me. I took it and cleaned off every individual key with alcohol and cleaned out the area behind the keys and blew it off with compressed air so now it looks like new.

4. It is incredibly hard to push down the keys compared to modern keyboards, which means that whenever I use it it is a unique experience and makes me wonder how I didn't break a finger when I was using it at age six...

There is but one issue other than really tough keys: The "M" key doesn't work too well. Otherwise, this old reliable keyboard should serve me well for years to come. ;)
