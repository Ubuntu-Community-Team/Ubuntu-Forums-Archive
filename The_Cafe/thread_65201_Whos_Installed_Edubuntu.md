### Post by rajsarkar on 2005-10-05
I still have not done the full installation of Edubuntu. However, the workstation installation gives a fair idea about the softwares installed.

My observations are as follows:

1. Many more educational softwares and games are required to be installed. Another educational distro KnoSciences ([COLOR="Blue"]http://gistlabs.homelinux.net/Knosciences/index.html[/COLOR]) contains many more educational sw and games

2. The educational softwares and games seems to be heavily skewed for students of lower classes (kids) and higher KG classes( K-9 to K-12). However there are hardly any software or games for students of middle level (say from K-5 to K-8 )

3. Atleast one inrnet based e-learning or centent-management software requires to be included in the final version of Edubuntu with full localisation support. There are many such sw like ATutor, Dokeos, Moodle etc.

4. Most of the educational softwares and games don't have the help file installed. The softwares should come with help files. 

More feedbacks will be provided later.
