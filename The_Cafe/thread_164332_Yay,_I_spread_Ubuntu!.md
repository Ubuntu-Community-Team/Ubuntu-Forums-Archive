### Post by zerhacke on 2006-04-22
A little background information is in order so that I can tell this story.

I'm disabled.  I have schizoaffective disorder, which is much like being a bipolar schizophrenic.  Before you think I'm all nuts, I'm pretty well medicated and respond well to the treatments offered.  I'm almost normal.

In the US, there's a government agency called the Bureau of Vocational Rehabilitation.  They work with disabled people, trying to get them jobs.  If the person needs anything special to work, they provide it.  There's a lot of hoops to jump through to get the help though.

Now, I want to be a *Nix Systems Administrator.  One of the hoops I had to jump through was to go take a test on what I knew computerwise with this agency in town.

So, I went to the agency.  I brought one of the Ubuntu sleeves that Canonical kindly sent me in the mail.  Didn't know if I would need it, since I had no idea what the test was going to be on.  I figured it would be better safe than sorry.

So I get there, and the short and long of the test was that they couldn't test me.  They had no Unix boxes set up.  So, I gave the guy the Ubuntu CD.  At first he didn't even know what it was, but after some explaining he understood.  Turns out they used to teach Red Hat 9 at this little agency but dropped it a few years back due to lack of interest in it.  When I left he grasped that Ubuntu was a Linux distro like Red Hat is.

I got a call about twenty minutes ago.  The tester guy LOVES Ubuntu and is in love with the apt-get system.

Since they couldn't test me the other day, I have to go back in to be tested after he can get a *Nix system setup.  He'll be testing me on Ubuntu, and at the same time he'd like me to teach him the nuances of the system.

I'm not the best guy at Ubuntu as my posting history can attest.  However, I have the secret weapon in this - I get internet access while I test and teach, so I'll be able to come here during the big ordeal.  :) 

Happy Ubuntuing!  And spread the message people!
