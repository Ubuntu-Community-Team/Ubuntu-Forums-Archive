### Post by meldra on 2006-09-17
Greetings,

The feedback I received from various sources has encouraged me to take the idea of the counter to the next step.

Without further ado, I introduce the [Ubuntu Surveys]("http://surveys.geekosophical.net")

Please spread them around the net as much as you can. 

Get people who have absolutely no idea what Ubuntu is to take it. Explain that the surveys will provide developers of free software with information to help make software better.

Get your non-ubuntu 'friends' to take it as well. Try avoid the 'anti' ones though. If you need help convincing them, explain that the information will potentially help the development of lots of Free and Open Source Software, as the results will be released under the Creative Commons. 

Last of all, [Digg it]("http://digg.com/linux_unix/Ubuntu_Surveys_for_Software_Freedom_Day") and whatever else can help spread the word.

Thanks in advance :)

Edit: forgot to mention before, the project is [documented on the Wiki]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MarketingTeam/UbuntuSurveys")

### Post by meldra on 2006-09-17
I'll be brief with this, as i'm just about to try sleep.

I plan for the survey to be an annual event. As this is the first time it's been run, there will be issues with the structure and so forth, they will be worked out by next year ;)

The timeline is at the moment to run the surveys for a month, ending on the 16th of October. I'll then sort and analyse the results and publish them openly under a creative commons licence. Not sure when this will be, as I have no idea of the scale of the data that will be gathered.

At the moment there is mainly current user surveys done, with only a very small handful of potential and previous users. Spread the word of the survey through all the other communities, technical or not, that you are involved with.

Thanks in advance, guys and gals :)
