**What kind of audio format do you use?**

There are several different types of audio, the main two are *Lossless* and *Lossy*. Lossy being mp3,wma and aac; and lossless being flac,ALAC and mp4. Lossy uses greater compression with a loss in sound quality and some frequencies. However, Lossless uses less compression and retains all of the audio frequencies of the source audio. In theory, mp3 files that are 192 kbps are sound equivalent to flac audio files to the human ear. However, on high end sound hardware some "clam" to hear the difference at 320 mp3 audio. I have always riped audio cds at 128 mp3 but since I have the space and the time, I am considering re-riping all my audio to 1024kbps flac audio. What I want to know, is what fellow Ubuntu users use for their music collection? Do you value high sound quality or space?

I merged ogg and mp3 since both are lossy audio codecs, wma can actually fall under this category but I decided to leave it as a separate options since its a Microsoft format.
