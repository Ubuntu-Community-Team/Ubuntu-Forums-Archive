### Post by mykalreborn on 2007-04-03
you can allways try [ubuntu studio]("http://ubuntustudio.org/"). it's an ubuntu derivative for artists. it hasn't been released yet, but it should be by the time feisty gets launched. until then there is a meta-package in the repos called "ubuntu-studio".
it's usually best to download distros made for media editing and not separate apps as some of them are a bit tricky to be installed. 
if you don't want to wait for ubuntu studio, you can download [64studio]("http://64studio.com/"), musix, dyne:bolic. there was a distro which you paid for, but i forgot it's name. 
but be careful that it's not yet that easy to make music in linux. at least it wasn't for me, and of course it depends on what kind of music you want to make. i suggest you dual boot with win xp and use a windows app instead.
