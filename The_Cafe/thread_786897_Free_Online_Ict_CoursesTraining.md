### Post by billgoldberg on 2008-05-08
I don't know which country you are from, but I guess most countries will have something similar.

Here in Belgium, well actually Flanders, we have a government body called "VDAB".

This translates as: Flemish Service for Work Conciliation

Basically, they help you get jobs and offer **free** courses in all fields you can imagine. You can take classes going from a few weeks to a year or use online courses, you can access 24/7. Hell, for some courses you even get paid.

It might be worth looking if there is a similar service in your country.

If not, google is your friend.

### Post by Tundro Walker on 2008-05-08
Sheesh, America could use something like that.  My Dad and I were talking the other day about how a lot of the "traditional school" curriculum could just be computer-based courses online with 24/7 access.  Sure, some students may still need a teacher for somethings.  But if done correctly, we should be moving kids more towards self-paced education.  At least, that's my opinion.  The gov't could work out a standardized course-work (which, there-in lies a problem, since different states, even cities, have different standards sometimes), and students could do courses online then test to pass.  A national database would pass/fail the students.  They could then show up for like 1/2 days for classes that mandatorily would need classroom environment, like band, art, etc.

Seriously, English, Math, even Sciences should be CBT courses.

And, expand on that with college-level courses provided by the government for worker-retraining initiatives.

I know at one job I took CBT courses for MS Office, and in about 4 weeks I had enough under my belt to get certified in all MS Office programs.  Helped me get jobs at later places, too, and also expanded how I do things.

I think in America, most folks are trying to make a buck by selling this type of training.  Come on Barrack or McCain!  Do something about this!
