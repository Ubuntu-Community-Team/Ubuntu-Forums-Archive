### Post by FuturePilot on 2007-06-21
It depends on what kind it is. If it's a static cling one, it might come off pretty easy. If it's got some kind of adhesive on it, it will probably last longer, but it will probably be hard to get off and won't be reusable.
The one thing I never liked about those is that the corners start peeling up. But that's usually a sign it's pretty much lived out it's life expectancy.

### Post by Yoooder on 2007-06-21
I use clear protective plastic on my Moto Razr, and they work great.  I get them in 2 or 3-packs for about $4 off of eBay whenever I need them, and I always look for the kind that has the protective plastic wrapped with 2 layers of protective plastic for shipping (redundancy is great).

The film on my external LCD tends to last about 6 weeks before the corner lifts up enough to let some dust particulates in and cause some bubbling.  After that it usually has about a week or two until it bubbles to the point that I just pitch and replace it.

One thing to note--at least with my screen protectors--is that you need the screen to be absolutely immaculate for it to be a good application.  I usually scrub the screen clean with some windex and a towel, then go at it with a lint-free cloth and compressed air until NASA would be proud of it.  This way there are no bubbles at all under the protective film, and that really helps make the film last.


btw:  I've never had to replace the film on the main inside screen :)  It's going on 6 months or so and is still perfectly adhered
