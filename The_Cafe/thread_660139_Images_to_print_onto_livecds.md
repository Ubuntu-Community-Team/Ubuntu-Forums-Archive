### Post by saulgoode on 2008-01-06
You might wish to investigate the [LightScribe CD/DVD burner]("http://www.lightscribe.com/index.aspx?flash=no"), which will print your label directly on your CD/DVD. Not only will the end result appear more professional, but you will avoid the problems that some front-loading units (such as those found in cars, entertainment centers, and Macs) have handling CDs with thicker, printed labels.

The units themselves are not that expensive, but you will have to pay about twice as much for the specially coated blank disks. There is a vendor-supplied Linux version of the labeling software and though this is not Open Source, there is also a software development kit for writing your own or including in an existing project.

There may be similar offerings from other companies but I haven't checked.

### Post by Spike-X on 2008-01-07
DON'T USE STICKY LABELS!! 

The glue seeps through the disc over time, and does affect the information recorded on the disc.

I've found a better option to be discs with a printable surface. Inkjet printers that can print on these aren't at all expensive, and the results are excellent.
