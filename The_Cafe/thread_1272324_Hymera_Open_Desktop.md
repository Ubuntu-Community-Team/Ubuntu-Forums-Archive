### Post by xArv3nx on 2009-09-22
Has anyone heard of this? They've had multiple ads on DistroWatch, and they recently launched an English version of it.

I'm trying it now. It came on a 3GB+ DVD, and (obviously) has a good bit of software on it. The first thing I did was boot it up and it was astounding, it booted the installer in <30 seconds (off a live cd?!). The installation took a total of 5-6 minutes, maybe less (for 3.3 GB worth of stuff? no way!!). I thought at first the DVD didn't boot up because it sat at a blank screen with a blinking underscore for a good 5-6 seconds.

The first boot was average, but it did come with a nice splash that faded out and started the desktop -- I didn't see a grub menu, just that blinking underscore until the splash started (no text, either). The default DE is Gnome. It's very nice, very nice theme. it seems to be a very nice out of the box experience.

Desktop effects work out of the box (including NVIDIA (and apparently, ATI) drivers). 


these are just some things i've noticed after using hymera open desktop for a few minutes. has anyone else tried it?

Absolutely, everything works out of the box and yes, English is fully supported. Remember, this is a new distribution so there will be at the least some minor quirks to work out, as with any OS, open source or commercial. I only noticed 3 minor oops after install.

1. Adobe reader is in Italian [COLOR="RoyalBlue"](uninstall, add debian-multimedia repo, install english version of 8 or get 9 from Abobe)[/COLOR]
2. Compiz Fusion icon is in Italian [COLOR="RoyalBlue"](uninstall and add english version from repo)[/COLOR]
3. Browser start page defaults to the Italian Hymera site page.[COLOR="RoyalBlue"](change it by hand to your preference)[/COLOR]

This is a really nice to look at Debian based distribution, which comes packed with oodles of functionality, including some of their own preferential applications such as the mail application and custom browser. The entire install, from front to back is easy and based on the Debian GTK installer front end but with their own complete graphical rewrite. When I booted, I was astonished at almost the latest Nvidia drivers (185.x.x) and compiz/emerald already active. Also worth noting was the deskbar and desklets.
Just remember, if you can't do better, do not complain! Especially for free! =P

### Post by Cubby on 2010-07-13
A bit late for this thread, but I appreciate the information on how to change the Compiz Fusion icon in tray from Italian to English.  Thanks I.T. Guru! 

I've been using Hymera for the past ten months, and so far I am very pleased.  I use their forum in the English section (Hawkins) and am always given quick answers to questions.  I also use google translator to use the main forum that is in Italian.  Easy enough.  

One thing I hope to see in the next Hymera release is during install, a selection for keyboard layout.  I found out the hard way that it is in Italian layout and my passwords wouldn't work, as it didn't accept the underscore mark.  As long as you use upper and lower case letters, numbers, and commas and periods, it goes well.  So, when you first log in, make sure to change your keyboard layout to your language.  Hymera will also find Grub legacy distros and Grub2 distros when you install the Grub menu.

I'm usually a KDE desktop fan, but I use Hymera when I want to burn a disc, as I like Gnome Baker much better than K3b.  And yes, Hymera folks did a great job on their desktop theme.  It's eye catching, and 3D works on my nVidia gForce 8500 graphics card.  They were even thoughtful enough to install kppp internet tool as I use dial up. 
Cubby
