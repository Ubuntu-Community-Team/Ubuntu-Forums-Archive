### Post by samalex on 2012-04-18
Wow, I didn't expect this kind of turnout :)  Granted sys admins do live in the command line world, but I was curious if leaving the GUI for a week wouldn't be a fun experiment or challenge for some to tackle, both newbies and seasoned Linux users, on their primary box.

And yes, though I said WWW wouldn't be all that usable, it was due to Java, AJAX, Flash, HTML5, etc, which a majority of sites use anymore, and none of these load well or at all in lynx or other text-based browsers.  

Why do this?  Why drive a classic car... why go camping... it's a throwback to the way it used to be I guess.  Nostalgia.  I don't see it looking back, but rather remembering how we got to where we are today.

At any rate I'll probably try to take a Shell Challenge after school is out next month, and I'll finally have a chance to learn emacs :)  I already have my system setup to use Alpine to get into GMail and Usenet and I have a few telnet BBSes I frequent for news and other info.  Also being an amateur radio operator and getting into APRS this might be fun to tinker with as well in shell.

LJ had a neat article a couple of years ago listing some amazing CLI apps - [http://www.linuxjournal.com/article/10854](http://www.linuxjournal.com/article/10854) - so for anyone new to shell this might be a good place to play with shell that is :)  I personally already use many of these apps, but there are some I haven't used.  

Anyway, just something fun...
