### Post by hizaguchi on 2006-07-12
When I introduce somebody new to Gnu/Linux, it's usually because they needed an application to do something in Windows and I showed them a free alternative.  Firefox, Gaim, OpenOffice, and the Gimp are all great gateway drugs.

When you say, "Hey, I want to dump your whole computer and reinstall a completely new OS without any official support and that doesn't work well with some formats you normally use," most people will laugh at you.  But when you show people that free software is often much better than proprietary alternatives it eases the transition and opens opportunities to talk about open source and the Gnu project.  This is why I wish more Linux apps were available for Windows.  There is already a GTK2 runtime environment, so at least the rest of the really good Gnome apps could be made cross-platform, right?  And then people could gradually replace their applications with free ones, and eventually only the underlying OS would be different.  And at that point, you've eliminated the need for official support in most areas as well as the problem of unsupported formats.  So then you can say, "Hey, let's go ahead and take that closed source OS off your system and replace it with a free one that doesn't get viruses and spyware and that will work better with all the applications you're already using anyway."  Then they're alot more likely to go for it.
