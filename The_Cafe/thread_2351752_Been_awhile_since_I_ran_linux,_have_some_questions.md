### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-02-06
No suggestions for second-hand laptops. Lubuntu is the lightest if you're looking for light (you can go lighter with the minimal install or one of the -core releases, but that may be a bit of a learning curve). There are LTS releases (long-term support) and interim releases. The former has three years support in the case of Lubuntu and the interims have nine months, any flavour. Go for 16.04 LTS if you don't want to be upgrading to a new release every six months.

Any Ubuntu flavour will run HD YouTube without issue. It is the machine that will cause any issues. For Lubuntu, a laptop with 2Gb of RAM should be fine to do what you want to do by the sounds of it. You'd probably get one with 4Gb for not much more.

Good luck and hope that's of some help.
