### Post by pantone186 on 2009-10-28
I have a old sony vaio sr11k with a udma2 / ata3 interface. With a maximum of 33mb/s throughput.

What do you think would be the best choice out of the following disk drive upgrades to push every last drop of performance out the machine?


**Conventional HDD e.g Samsung Spinpoint 160gb**
+cheap
+spacious
+fast enough to saturate the ata3 interface
-noisy
-slower seek/access time than SSD
-consumes more power then HDD or CF
-heavier than SSD or CF


**PATA SSD e.g. 32gb MMC King Spec**
+fast enough to saturate the ata3 interface
+silent
+consumes less power than HDD
+fast seek/access time
-expensive
-limited capacity


**Compact Flash e.g 8gb SLC x300 0r x366**
+fast enough to saturate the ata3 interface
+silent
+consumes less power than HDD or SSD
+fast seek/access time
+very light weight
-expensive
-very limited capacity

### Post by ssam on 2009-10-29
i have a mini-itx server/jukebox that has / and /home on a CF card (music and shared files are on rotating disks). never had any observable data corruption on the CF.

all the top end DSLRs use CF cards, and photographers tend to get upset with corrupt photos.

the only dodgy flash cards I have seen have either defective on arrival, so a quick run with badblocks identifies them. or a 512MB CF bytestor card that lasted for about 5 years of taking photos. so probably worth sticking to the big name brands.
