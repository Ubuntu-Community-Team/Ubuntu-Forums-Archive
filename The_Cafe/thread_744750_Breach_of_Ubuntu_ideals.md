### Post by Keyper7 on 2009-02-27
> **HammerOfDoubt said:**
> Like Linux, Stallman assumes you know what you are doing, and that you read beyond the title.

Blame people who don't inquire or desire to learn about what exactly this thing of ours is all about.

Very weak analogy. Misconceptions about Linux come from wrong descriptions ("it's like Windows, but better"), not from the word itself. The word "Linux", alone, has no intuitive meaning or common usage. Therefore, there's no ambiguity involved.

"Free", on the other hand, has two possible meanings: "free as in beer" and "free as in freedom". The first is commonly associated with a product, the second is commonly associated with people. Of which of those the definition of "software" is closer?

The bottom line is: assuming that "free software" is "software free of charge" is wrong but *perfectly reasonable*. If you simply write "I'm gay" in your Facebook profile, it's not very reasonable to complain later that those people thinking that you're homossexual should've realized all by themselves that you were using the original meaning of the word.

People can only inquire about something or desire to learn about something *if they are aware that there's something to be inquired or learned*. Blaming people for not going after the non-intuitive meaning is arrogant and elitist, and the kind of thing that drives new users away.
