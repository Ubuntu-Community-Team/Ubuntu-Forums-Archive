### Post by koenn on 2009-03-02
> The hacker mind-set is not confined to this software-hacker culture. There are people who apply the hacker attitude to other things, like electronics or music — actually, you can find it at the highest levels of any science or art. Software hackers recognize these kindred spirits elsewhere and may call them ‘hackers’ too — and some claim that the hacker nature is really independent of the particular medium the hacker works in. [http://www.catb.org/~esr/faqs/hacker-howto.html#what_is](http://www.catb.org/~esr/faqs/hacker-howto.html#what_is)
Although you can find people with "hacker personality" in other fiels than software (and hardware/electronics), it's mainly in the computer-related fields that hackers an be seen as a community or a culture.

While I don't doubt that there are musicians with 'hacker qualities" or people who 'hack' music or musical instruments, I doubt there is actually a a community culture of 'music hackers'

A music hacker, I have never heard of the term before now in the context you describe. Could you have invented a completely new genre of human creativity?

Stating the obvious, this seems to apply more to electronic music rather than rock or classical. Can you hack an acoustic guitar or a trumpet?

Could you label people that modify the firmware and software of their chosen electronic instrument to increase it's capabilities beyond that which it's manufactures originally designed as hackers? Interesting.

If you compose (some may consider this the wrong use of this expression) electronic music via samples etc, are you not hacking music?
