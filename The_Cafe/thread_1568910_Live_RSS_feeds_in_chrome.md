### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2010-09-06
Nope, Chrome doesn't do live bookmarks and probably won't. They're very much a Firefox thing.

There are a few extensions that try to mimic the feature through various funny hacks:
[https://chrome.google.com/extensions/search?itemlang=&hl=en&q=live+bookmark](https://chrome.google.com/extensions/search?itemlang=&hl=en&q=live+bookmark)

Maybe we can find something that works. What has you wanting live bookmarks over a more information-rich feed reader?

It isn't what you think you want (:P), but have you tried 
[Feedly]("http://www.feedly.com")? It is a really, really great RSS reader / service that gets the absolute most juice it possibly can out of your feeds. It creates sort of magazine-style pages using them, with some really cool stuff that promotes certain articles by their popularity, age and other data.
It's only an online service (connects to Google Reader with no offline mode), but that one flaw didn't keep it from winning my heart.
