Don't take this the wrong way, only with a grain of salt...
 
If you're serious about getting into Linux, in the US most of the enterprises are using RedHat.  They offer a certification also:    [https://www.redhat.com/training](https://www.redhat.com/training)
 
It may be a little more expensive, however.... 
 
See, as an example, a lot of the data rooms in larger enterprises have rack-mount 1u, 2u, 3u servers.  Sometimes stacked maybe six on top of each other. Towards the center of the rack is a KVM drawer.  A real thin dealie that pulls out and you flip the monitor open and you can access any of the headless servers by clicking which one you want.  The type on those monitors (there's no GUI) is pretty large so there's always hope.  The problem comes when you're talking about the larger servers where there's nothing more than a terminal (keyboard/13" green screen monitor) connected to it.
