### Post by Mercury_Alpha on 2009-02-20
AOL purchased Netscape, who were developing and releasing Netscape Navigator. Eventually, AOL decided to open source Navigator. This was called "The Mozilla Project." This project created the [Mozilla Application Suite]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozilla_Application_Suite").

The suite was commonly referred to as Mozilla, and Mozilla contained a Web browser, email client, and I think a Usenet client too.

This project was eventually perceived as bloated, so the Suite was broken up. The browser portion became Phoenix, which changed its name to Firebird because of legal entanglements. Then they changed it to Firefox.

The email portion of the suite became Thunderbird.
