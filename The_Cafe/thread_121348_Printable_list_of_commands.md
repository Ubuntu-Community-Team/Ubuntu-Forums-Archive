### Post by Iandefor on 2006-01-25
Check the man pages as you need to use commands. You could also, for a listing of commands for investigation, print the output of dir ```
/usr/bin
``` and investigate whatever pops out at you. 
Also, you could check out [http://www.ss64.com/bash/](http://www.ss64.com/bash/), which has a complete listing of just about every bash command. Not all are available for Ubuntu, but the majority should be.
