### Post by nayab on 2009-05-23
In many years of professional support, I have encountered lots of anti-virus products. What I use at home however is AVG, simply because it is free. It has never let anything past.  But to be honest, it is not because it is the best product out there. The simple fact is that user-disipline is the best defence. I do not click on suspicious links, and I never accept files from untrusted sources.

Viruses are spread through ignorance. Where I work now is an exceptionally restricted IT environment. No aimless surfing, definitely no IM programs and certainly no social-networking sites. A computer is still in essence a mathematical tool (that can still be used for fun). As such, 60 servers and 300 desktops/laptops have shown in the past 12 months only 2 minor viral infections.

Try and educate your folks as to why clicking on suspicious links is not good, and never to accept files from other people. And I promise you, clicking on the link promising you are the millionth visitor to the website and you have just won a sports car is complete garbage.

Be well.:D
