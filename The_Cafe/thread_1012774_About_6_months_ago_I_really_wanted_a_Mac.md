### Post by MikeTheC on 2008-12-16
Speaking as a Mac user, without a doubt there is a certain degree of "fit and finish" Mac OS X has which Linux still does not, on both an OS level as well as an application level. Now, that being said, you pay quite the hardware price penalty to have legitimate access to the Mac OS X environment/platform. I think one of the biggest "barriers to entry" for many folks (in particular, those who are not "long-timers" such as myself) is the price. I don't think there's too many who couldn't pop for the $129 for the OS, but since you have to pay a premium for the hardware (and unlike years ago, the alternative hardware is good, cheap, and plentiful) it's a disincentive, particularly for Linux-originating folks.

Windows folks are probably less unwilling, since they're starting from a point where there's an in-built desire *not* to keep using the platform they have, ***and*** they don't even have a dog in the "F/OSS vs. Commercial-Proprietary" fight. x86-using Linux folk (and that accounts for the super-majority) don't *need* the Mac OS X platform for anything (other than if they want to run Aqua apps such as the iLife or iWork suites, Final Cut, etc.) and are already "not using Windows", so where exactly is the incentive?
