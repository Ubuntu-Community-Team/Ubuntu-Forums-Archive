I think I'm going to build a computer instead of buying one. But I can't decide what case to buy.

Basically this is what I want:
- Around $100 (preferribly under $100)
- Good cooling (very important, I want my components to last)
- Something that I'll be able to keep using for years to come
- If possible, have the front I/O ports and power button near the top of the front

So far I think the max length of Nvidia desktop graphics cards is 10.5 inches. Other companies currently have longer graphics cards I think. Do you guys think that graphics cards are going to continue to get longer in the future? I want to buy a case and have it be "future-proof" so that I can keep using it for future upgrades and builds. But if the case bottoms out at slightly over 10.5 inches I might not be able to upgrade my graphics card in the future.

What do you guys think?

### Post by blazemore on 2009-10-26
> **pwnst*r said:**
> sure there's a contest, but i wouldn't part with my 1200!

[http://www.antec.com/Believe_it/product.php?id=Njkz](http://www.antec.com/Believe_it/product.php?id=Njkz)

Of course the 1200 is better, but not for the price. Unless you want water cooling, or have a seriously massive graphics card, the 300 is fine.

Generally, don't skimp on a case. Go for a reputable brand like Antec, even if it means paying a bit more. Aim to spend between 10%-15% of your budget on your case. For example, a decent mid range gaming machine should set you back around £400-500, so aim to spend around £40 on a case.

Another tip: don't buy a case with a built-in PSU. They tend to be cheap, noisy, inefficient and unreliable. You're better off again, buying from a well known brand. Don't sort by price and go for what looks like a good deal, because it probably isn't. You can get a case with a PSU in it for about £15, but do you really want to plug your expensive components into that?
