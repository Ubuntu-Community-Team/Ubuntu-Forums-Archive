I am new to Ubuntu but not new to Linux.  I am now 62 years old and am  getting close to retirement after 34 years as a software engineer. 

I started doing software a long time ago as a machine language  programmer (TMS 9900) for a company that did custom testers for the  contact manufacturing industry.  Then was thrilled with my first  symbolic assembler ( Intel 8080 )!  Went from there to C, then years  latter to C++, even taught OO for a while.  What I am doing now if Java  development for Aircraft Systems Simulations that run on Linux system.   Along the way there was some Fortran, Pascal, Basic, PLM, RLL languages.   It has been fun.  I actually started in the High Tech industry as an  electronics technician and I have a dozen years of doing exrething from  design, build, test and debug complex hardware designs.  But that was  years ago.

Total hip replacement two weeks ago.  In a few  months I will get a knee replacement.  Then after some convalescence I  am planning to retire early next year, or after the next really bad  meeting :grin:

Focused on the Junior high and above and do  things like teaching Java, maybe writing apps for smart phones, maybe  some hardware stuff using VoCore one one of the other embedded computer  designs.

I have a niece there  that is an English teacher and she tells me that in her school they are  starting to provide an evening meal in addition to breakfast and lunch  because of the level of poverty.

Thanks, and I look forward to setting this up.

I've been contemplating a means of providing linux laptops to disadvantaged children at our local jr/sr high.  I live in a very poor rural county in S. ### Post by poorguy on 2016-05-30
We do a computer camp through my local Church as we receive donations from local businesses that are doing system upgrades. 

The donated computers are complete systems that ran Windows XP and are still very usable. We clean and upgrade and install Linux on them and give them people who can't afford a computer. Along with the donated computers we get all sorts of extra needed hardware which is used for the necessary upgrading. 

We have several computers set up in the Church basement so that people can come in and learn how easy Linux is to use. 
This event happens four times a year and is a good cause. Everyone involved really enjoys doing it.
