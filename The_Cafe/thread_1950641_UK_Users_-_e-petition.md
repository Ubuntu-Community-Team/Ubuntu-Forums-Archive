### Post by D-Dan on 2012-04-01
We all know that the UK Government has dallied over using Linux for government business, but they appear to stall regularly and only ever include a small subset of functions in their considerations. So, I've started a e-petition on the government website calling for a full review with a view to migrating all systems to open source, and request that you put your name to it.

Sorry everyone else, AFAIK it's only available to UK residents (possibly UK nationals).

[http://epetitions.direct.gov.uk/petitions/32255](http://epetitions.direct.gov.uk/petitions/32255)

I understand your sentiments completely. I worked in the Civil Service for 20 years, and was a representative, building to a senior official in the PCS (and it's predecessors) for 18 of those years. I saw how poorly computerisation projects were handled and how expensive private companies made it.

I also know that, to date, the Government has adopted a very piecemeal approach, which is why I've phrased the petition as I have, in a bid to force a more unified review across all Government services. It's unlikely the Gvt will ever rid itself of private contractors, but maybe we can at least force a change in the way funds are spent. The less that is spent on unnecessary license costs (not to mention hardware savings that could be employed owing to thin client solutions and the generally lower requirements of Linux based systems), and the increased security offered by such systems (thus avoiding the monstrous cleanup costs when they get it wrong) and there are definite savings (and not a footnote on a spreadsheet either) to be made.

Incidentally, it seems I've sparked a minor genesis, and for US users someone who saw me post in another forum has made an equivalent petition on US Gvt.
