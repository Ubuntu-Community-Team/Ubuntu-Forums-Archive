### Post by nicolasdentremont on 2024-09-29
I'm not sure how those shocks are constructed but they usually have a piston with rubber seals. Chances are that the mount of heat required to loosen rusted parts will damage the seals and the paint also. But I guess if they're rusted tight like that the seals are probably already gone. I'd spray them with something like WD-40 or penetrating oil then move them back and fourth to help loosen them. clamping everything in a vice would help. Depending on how many tools/materials you have kicking around you can improvise a simple slide hammer to hammer the shock tubes out rather than in.

### Post by nicolasdentremont on 2024-09-29
[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slide_hammer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slide_hammer)
It's basically a metal cylinder that slides along a rod. The rod usually has a stopper on one end and bolt threads on the other. A long bolt sometimes works. You attach the threaded end to the object you want to remove and hammer the cylinder against the stopper. I use improvised slide hammers to hammer out things that I can't access from the back. Bearing and pins, stuff like that.
