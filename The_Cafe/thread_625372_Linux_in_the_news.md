Exactly. What was the biggest complaint Mac users made against Vista (beyond the bugginess)? The number of versions. Apple ads ripped on the fact that there's the home version which comes in two flavors, professional, ultimate, etc. I think Penny Arcade even made a joke about it.

Think about that. People complained that there were too many versions of Vista to install, and that was only as complicated as going "will I use this at home? Do I need networking capabilities?"

Now imagine your average user trying to find out what version of Linux to use? There's three versions of Ubuntu, which has multiple derivatives (Mint, Geubuntu...), all of which come from Debian which is full of other offshoots, if you don't want to use Debian itself. And that's just for starters.

One huge reason Linux isn't taking over and will have a damn hard time is that there's no box labeled "Linux" at the store that they can pick up. They can't just call up a computer store and say "I want a copy of Linux".

Most computer users don't want that much choice because they see an OS as a path to use the software they want. Linux doesn't exactly fill that massive void.
