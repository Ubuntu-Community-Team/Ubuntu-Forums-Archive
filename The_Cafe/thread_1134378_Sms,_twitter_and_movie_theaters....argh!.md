### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-04-23
I started to hate cell phones a couple of years ago, when the damn thing started to wake me at 7 am Sunday mornings, with sms messages about new products and services.

I have terminated my account on Orkut, which is more popular than Facebook in my country, because the damn thing only served to pollute my inbox with spam and stupid comments. I never used Twitter. I really don't get it.

Now I'm simply amazed with the new method of visual pollution a company created to induce keyboard-happy people to spend money. I'm talking about [MuVChat](http://www.muvchat.com/faq.html). The service allow the attendants of a movie theater in Charlestowne, IL, to send sms messages to the screen while watching the movie - [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b1DuXr_lTxg](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b1DuXr_lTxg)

The people behind this stupid idea says that each person sends an average number of 40 messages per movie. Considering a session with 200 people, they send a total of 8.000 messages per movie.

Would you go to a movie session where 199 teenagers would be in charge of the movie subtitles?

I hope they use Windows to do this, because I would love to see a BSOD on the movie screen. :popcorn:

The original article is [http://www.switched.com/2009/04/20/onscreen-text-message-commentary-coming-to-the-movies/](http://www.switched.com/2009/04/20/onscreen-text-message-commentary-coming-to-the-movies/)
