### Post by p_quarles on 2008-05-06
Sun also recently purchased MySQL. 

Making the software available for free does not necessarily equate to making no money on it. After all, many of Sun's products (Java, OpenOffice.org) are already available for no cost. This is also true of Microsoft, Apple, Adobe, and other major proprietary software vendors. I assume that Sun intends to make money on VBox by selling solutions rather than the software.

On the other hand, they may begin charging for it as well. VirtualBox OSE is a branch of the main project, which is under a proprietary license.
