It shouldn't be in any persons nature to be lazy and not use the resources at there disposal, a simple Google search for "FUD meaning" gives a nice explanation on the first link provided...

> ***Fear, uncertainty, and doubt*** (**FUD**) is a tactic of [rhetoric]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhetoric") and [fallacy]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fallacy") used in [sales]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sales"), [marketing]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marketing"), [public relations]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_relations"),[[1]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear,_uncertainty_and_doubt#cite_note-LeadRefs000-0")[[2]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear,_uncertainty_and_doubt#cite_note-1") [politics]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics") and [propaganda]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda").  FUD is generally a strategic attempt to influence public perception by  disseminating negative and dubious/false information designed to  undermine the credibility of their beliefs. An individual firm, for  example, might use FUD to invite unfavorable opinions and speculation  about a competitor's product; to increase the general estimation of [switching costs]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switching_barriers") among current customers; or to maintain leverage over a current [business partner]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_partner") who could potentially become a rival.
 FUD techniques may be crude and simple, as in claiming "I read a  paper by a Harvard professor that shows you are wrong regarding subject  XXX", but the paper does not exist. (Were the paper to exist then it  would not be FUD but valid criticism.) Alternatively FUD may be very  subtle, employing an indirect approach. Someone who employs FUD cannot  generally back up their claims (e.g., "I don't recall which professor or  which year the paper is from"). To dispel FUD, the easiest way is to  ask for details and then provide well researched hard facts which  disprove them. For instance, if it can be shown that no Harvard  professor has ever written a paper on subject XXX, then the FUD is  dispelled.
 The term originated to describe [disinformation]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disinformation") tactics in the [computer hardware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_hardware") industry and has since been used more broadly.[[3]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear,_uncertainty_and_doubt#cite_note-Elliott000-2") FUD is a manifestation of the [appeal to fear]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appeal_to_fear").

Right I've spent some more time on Suse Studio this morning and have since realised that it allows you to create your distribution based upon openSuse 11.3 which means that most if not all packages you can choose in your build are open source.

I'm currently creating a build to work with my old desktop pc that has 256MB of RAM and 40GB hard disk so the build at the moment is going to be based around IceWM.  When I've finished the build I'll create a live CD and if anyone would like to try/test it let me know.

Since coming back to Linux last year after an absence of 5 years by using Ubuntu 9.10 I was a bit disappointed that with Ubuntu I could not do a custom install and choose the packages I wanted.  With Suse Studio it gives you the tools to build your own distro of openSuse by selecting the packages you want.

I think that other distributions like Ubuntu should have something similar or failing that allow users to do custom installs.
