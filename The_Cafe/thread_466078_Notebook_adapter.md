Explanation:

The current rating (Amps) basically specifies the amount of current the adapter(PSU) can deliver to the device(laptop). The device laptop on the other hand will NOT draw more current than it is specced to. Even the current 3.16A the adapter is specced to is slightly above what the laptop needs.

So if you get a 19V@5A adapter the laptop will only draw what it needs, probably about 2-3A. The rest is of no significance. A 5A adapter only providing 2-3A will probably run cooler & last longer than your current fried 3.16A

So go ahead and get that adapter, you have nothing to worry about.

Edit: Even if you go slightly over the voltage, say 20V you should be ok. The laptop has it's own internal regulator circuitry which conditions the power and steps it down to 5V & 12V. A higher voltage though would mean the circuitry is going to work a bit harder and take more strain & generate more heat. So if you can stick to 19V but don't worry about the Amp rating as long as it is above 3.16A, dont go lower.

If you doubt me ask any electrical engineer and they will tell you the same thing.
