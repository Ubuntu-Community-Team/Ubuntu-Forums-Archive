### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-06-03
You'd hear a lot of people saying "Ubuntu included Tomboy just to let Microsoft in!" or "They took out GIMP because they don't want us to have children competent in visual arts," or, "they chose Rhythmbox because Banshee has a mono dependency- they're trying to get rid of Microsoft!"

But no, seriously, I think Google's done a great job of 'not being evil,' and they deserve a lot more outward respect. I'm sure all of us who DO use their services (probably most of us here, not all I'm sure) are grateful for the quality of the services they provide, whether it be Gmail, Android, or just plain old Google.

I personally sent them my 'story' to how glad I am that they are prioritizing openness in YouTube with HTML5 and Web-M, as well as Chromium which is an awesome browser.

Of course, this is all to their benefit, but I have an inkling that they also felt good about it, since many people at Google use Linux in the first place and embrace open standards.

For someone in their position, they've done a lot less evil than I could imagine any other company doing. So let's not just criticize them for the bad things they possibly could be doing all the time. If you don't wanna' use it, that's fine, but every other search/email/OS provider has the same power over your data as Google, as far as I know. Might as well just stop using the internet or support Diaspora if you want a more privatized web.

So, for those of us who are still using something other than our own servers for these things, and using Google, let this be a thread where we talk about what we LIKE about Google. I suggest if you guys are proud of them for some of what they've done, you submit your own story here. [http://services.google.com/feedback/share_success](http://services.google.com/feedback/share_success)

Of course, if you still want to bring some constructive criticism into this thread, I'm sure everyone's fine with that. :D

and I've been generally happy with these services.

Not too long ago I worked for Google for a year- through a temp agency- in a field I dearly love, and they sent me to live in India for two months. After the people of the area had resoundingly told all the air freight companies they were not welcome to fly in and out of our quiet little Federal airfield, all of a sudden it was OK for Google to do so. A different scale of traffic, I know, but an ugly and disheartening precedent. Left a mighty bad taste in my mouth. I wondered- and still do- where all the people who hassled Larry Ellison over flying his jet into San Jose are now that we have a real problem.

Overall, I have to say that Google has made many important positive differences, but is by no means inherently or thoroughly un-evil. They are only as strong as their weakest links, and they will have to struggle more than most of us to stay honest, just because they are so big and so powerful.
