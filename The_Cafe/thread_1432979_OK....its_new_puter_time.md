### Post by Eldera on 2010-03-18
Why not make a list of the things you do and then start looking for a machine that does those things best? 

Different strokes for different folks. What do you do with a computer? 

For example a person that wants to carry his computer out to the restaurants (and school) needs something different from a person who needs a big screen to compensate for eye problems and a big keyboard to compensate for arthritis. Battery life can be very critical to someone traveling and irrelevant to some who is always on AC. Gamers need something different from someone just doing e-mail.

### Post by markbuntu on 2010-03-18
You can spend a fortune on the latest greatest stuff right now and watch the price drop like a stone over the next 6 months or..... There are price points with components like mobos and cpus and gpus that will get you a great machine with not much money, It will be last years latest greatest but still better than 98% of existing machines and give you very good performance for many years.

If you are going the BYO route get a big box with lots of fans.

If you are a gamer fanatic spending $1,000 a year on new components will let you keep up, barely. If you know a fanatic gamer ask him for his old stuff, he probably has a closet full of really great stuff.
