### Post by zmjjmz on 2008-08-27
Ok, this is **_*not_*** my invention. It's Cory Doctorow's.
In his book _Down and out in the Magic Kingdom_, money has been replaced by something called "Whuffie". The idea behind Whuffie is that whenever you do something that earns respect, your Whuffie score goes up. The more Whuffie you have, the more respect you command. In the book, Singularity has been reached in the form of HUDs, so knowing someone's Whuffie score takes less than a second.
(And so has immortality in the form of backups, which would owe to the disappearance of crime)
I discuss this because the book only deals with Disney World, and the effects of Whuffie there.
If we were to suddenly go through a complete conversion to Whuffie, this is what I see happening.
Buying products could be done like so:
Person A has x amount of Whuffie. Salesperson has y amount of Whuffie. If Salesperson gives Person A the product, then Peron A respects Salesperson, and Salesperson's Whuffie goes up.
If Person A doesn't respect Salesperson, then Salesperson resents Person A and Person A's Whuffie goes down, while bystanders feel sympathy for Salesperson, and Salesperson's Whuffie goes up.
If Person A like the product, then Salesperson's company's (be it him/herself, or a gigantic corporation) Whuffie goes up.
Service would work much the same way, and employees would not be workers, but people providing services for a company.

Crime would die, as would war. Governments would be much encouraged to either stop existing (why do you need one if crime dies out?) or do a much better job for their people.
Innovation would be encouraged because the innovator would obtain a large Whuffie boost if millions of people like their product. People would make a living of viral videos, GPL developers would be far "richer (the term is a misnomer regarding Whuffie)" than proprietary developers, etc.


I assume there are a lot of problems with this, would anyone care to point them out?
