### Post by Tibuda on 2010-02-10
> **madnessjack said:**
> Promote != preach
You are right.


Promoting is fine, as long as you make sure the user is aware that:
- Not every application out there have a Linux version. Engineers need AutoCAD, designers need Adobe.
- Not every peripheral out there supports Linux. Lexmark and Canon printers are a nightmare on Linux, you need iTunes to sync Apple iGadgets, etc.
- Not everybody out there would be able to help him. Non-tech people rely on friends for support. Off course you could help him, but you'll not be always there.

This may scare the user, but if you don't tell him, he may find by himself the hard way, and will be just another unsatisfied user. You can blame software/hardware vendors for not supporting Linux, but you could have warned the user before he buys a Lexmark printer.

I'm pretty sure everybody switching to Macs are aware of similar limitations. Except for Apple iGadgets off course.
