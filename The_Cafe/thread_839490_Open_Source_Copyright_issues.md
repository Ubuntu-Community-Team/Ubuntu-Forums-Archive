### Post by saulgoode on 2008-06-24
> **lisati said:**
> I vaguely recall hearing something back in the 80s about MS taking someone to court (possibly Apple or Xerox) for "stealing" the idea of a GUI. Sadly I didn't pay too much attention at the time, writing it off as chutzpah that would quickly get tossed out of court on account of MS not having invented it in the first place.

I believe that to which you refer is Apple suing Microsoft in the late '80s for Windows allegedly violating copyrighted Mac GUI elements. 

Apple ended up losing the case but little could be concluded from the court's decision because the vast majority of the GUI elements being discussed were considered part of a license agreement that MS legitimately obtained from Apple. 

If I recall, there were about 150 different aspects of the GUI being argued and all but about a dozen were properly licensed. The dozen that weren't licensed were considered trivial and obvious by the courts -- but that is not to say that the others were not considered trivial, just that being covered by a license obviated the need to make such a decision.
