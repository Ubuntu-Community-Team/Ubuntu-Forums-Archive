### Post by Giant Speck on 2009-09-16

However, xylitol has been shown to have many health benefits.  It's good for the teeth because it cannot be fermented by mouth bacteria and also lowers the pH value of saliva, which makes it difficult for certain types of bacteria to survive.  Because of the lowered pH value, remineralization of teeth can occur much faster, which can repair cavities and weak spots in teeth.

Because xylitol is absorbed more slowly than sugar, it does not cause high blood sugar levels, making it an idea sugar substitute for diabetics.  Xylitol can also be used to treat osteoperosis by improving bone density and prevent ear infections by preventing the growth of bacteria in the Eustachian tubes.

And while laxative-like symptoms my occur with sugar alcohols, the body can adapt to consuming the substance in as little as a couple weeks.
