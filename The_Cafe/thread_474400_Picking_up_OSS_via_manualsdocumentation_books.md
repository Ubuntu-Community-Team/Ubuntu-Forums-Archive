### Post by SoulinEther on 2007-06-15
Instead of trying to sell OSS software by directly selling it in a nicely packaged box and released by the main developing team / company behind the software, why don't users (professional, for the most part) make books and bundle OSS with them?

The money then goes to the person that truly deserves it: the author who is spreading OSS by making it accessible to the average user.

Books would have titles like: "GIMP: From Installation to Image Perfection" or ... "Firefox: From Installation to Web Utopia".. so that the user gets the feeling that they are getting bundled software or something... along with that feeling that somebody is there guiding them every step of the way.
