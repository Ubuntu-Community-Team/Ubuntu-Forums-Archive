### Post by JKyleOKC on 2011-11-18
A few years ago, I did an AltaVista search (it was in the days before Google) for my name, just out of curiosity.

I got some 500 hits, only three of which were actually me. Of those three, two listed addresses I had not had since 1962.

My point is that few of us have absolutely unique names, so the odds are great that our names are shared with others around the world. One of my "shares" is a physician in the UK and another is a senator in Tennessee, for both of whom I've received misdirected e-mail. Another is an artist in Chicago.

Bottom line: if privacy is that important to you, use a fake name or meaningless label. Otherwise, suit yourself!
