### Post by YoungQuiz on 2009-05-04
According to NetApplications, Linux usage share on client devices has, for the first time, passed the 1% mark.

Here&#8217;s how the web usage share breaks down for April 2009:

    * Windows: 87.90%
    * Mac: 9.73%
    * Linux: 1.02%
    * iPhone: 0.55%
    * iPod touch: 0.15%

Over a period of 12 months, this is how the market share for the top five OSes has changed:

    * Windows: -3.68%
    * Mac: +2.35%
    * Linux: +0.39%
    * iPhone: +0.40%
    * iPod touch: +0.12%

Even if you disagree with the numbers, there&#8217;s no doubt that Linux is on the up and growing at a rate similar to that of the iPhone.

How significant is this 1%? Very? Not much? Not at all?
