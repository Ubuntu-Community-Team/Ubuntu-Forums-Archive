### Post by BslBryan on 2010-04-22
I own a Linux computer store based in Chattanooga, Tennessee.  We support Ubuntu and other Linux operating systems as much as possible, and we always hold some sort of release party and give out tons of cds.  They are available regularly.  We are not making much money, as we want to be competitive and have the best prices, but really that just means we don't have money to support ourselves or Linux.  The thought is that either we start doing really well or we close down the shop.  We even had to trade in our very professional website for a basic one (that I really dislike) because it was a bit too expensive to keep up with.  If you are interested, PM me.  I can promise you that it's not a joke, and that we won't just spend the money on ourselves.  Anything, even a dollar, can help us to live longer and help more people discover Linux.  On top of that, we are the only shop in town that works on pre-XP computers.  I understand (and promote) the need for advanced hardware and software, but for some people, it's all they need, and it's all they can afford.
