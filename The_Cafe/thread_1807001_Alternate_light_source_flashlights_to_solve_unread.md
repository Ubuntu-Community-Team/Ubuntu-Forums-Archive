### Post by Roasted on 2011-07-18
I have a dilemma. There are some closets here where the ink has faded off of the tags which notate which port in the patch panel is to what room. Example, 106-5... Room 106, port 5.

This is of course a slight issue when it comes to wire management and knowing what cables are going where, why, how, etc.

So I have this idea... I watch a lot of forensic type shows, and I know they sometimes use alternate light sources to determine if a note was written in different ink. With their forms of light sources, which seem to be some sort of overhead projector with different lights being on a swivel, seems to really make even the most unreadable ink pop out in broad daylight.

That said, I began to wonder if there was a more portable (and more affordable) item similar to that. Maybe not something that's forensically driven, but something that could make faded ink appear easier, as least so I can re-tag everything with proper tags/ink this time around.

Any thoughts?

### Post by mips on 2011-07-19
That is why you keep floorplans with clearly labelled outlets.

It's easier to use a labelling system that goes by patch panel & port numbers instead of room numbers etc.

It's not to late though, get/draw floorplans and give them to a cabling company asking them to remap & relabel your outlets & patch panels and update the the floorplans with the information. Any subsequent new work or changes will also be noted on the floorplans.

If you only have like 20 points you can do it yourself but anything more than that and it becomes a pain in the *** so rather get someone else to do it.

Nows a good time to start a structure cabling policy document that contractors must adhere to.

For now try a black/uv light.
