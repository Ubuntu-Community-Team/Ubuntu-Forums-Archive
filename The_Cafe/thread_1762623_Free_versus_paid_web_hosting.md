### Post by linuxforartists on 2011-05-21
The free hosting I've used are for "hosted services" like Blogger and WordPress.com (WordPress.org requires finding paid hosting).  If you're doing a blog, using a free hosted service is okay.  

The only thing that might be a problem is offering e-pubs for download.  If you're going to charge for them, you'll need shopping cart software, an SSL certificate, and more tech support.

If you're doing anything fancier like that, you really should get paid web hosting.  Shared hosting plans are a great way to start, I'm only paying $3.95 a month.  If your traffic picks up, you can trade up to bigger plans.

The main advantage of paid hosting is branding.  For example, you can buy a unique domain name and call your website "www.desktoplinuxreviews.com" instead of "www.desktoplinuxreviews.blogger.com," which looks more amateur.  (By the way, DLR is a real site and it's great.)  

Really depends on your goals and budget.  A great resource is [WebHostingTalk.com]("http://www.webhostingtalk.com").  Ignore the web hosting reviews you find on Google.  Almost all of them are spam.

Good luck!

When I was looking at web hosts a year ago, here were the ones that made my shortlist.  This was after I did extensive research on WebHostingTalk.com

**HawkHost** - I went with them in the end.  So far great tech support.  Whenever I've sent them a support ticket, they've always been fast and responsive.  Some extras that I liked were that they use LiteSpeed for their servers and offer Softaculous as a script installer.  Not many web hosts have those features.
[B]
HostGator[/B] - One of my client's websites is on them.  Very big and established company, a solid choice.  A tech support guy once spent an hour with me on the phone to help me fix a botched WordPress install.  Now that's "support"!
[B]
