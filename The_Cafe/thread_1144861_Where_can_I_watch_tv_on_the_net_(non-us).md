### Post by t0p on 2009-05-01
I live in the UK.  My TV self-destructed last year (I was watching the Eastenders omnibus when smoke started to billow from the back, the screen went black, oh it was great!).  I'd spent all my money on booze, drugs, computer equipment and the like, so I couldn't buy a new TV.   So I decided to watch online TV on my computer instead.  Wonderful!

Thing is, the TV channels in the UK are wedded to advertising revenue, so development of TV over the net has been pretty poor.  There are the "catch-up" services (BBC iPlayer, ITV Catch-up. Demand.Five, 4.OD), but they have various limitations.  Plus they don't all work so great with Linux because Tux doesn't give head as satisfyingly as Bill Gates.  Bah!

But I have been pleased with the sites that enable you to watch TV shows and movies streamed in flash.  I regularly use [Surf the Channel]("http://www.surfthechannel.com/"), [tv-video.net]("http://tv-video.net/"), [Project Free TV]("http://www.free-tv-video-online.info/"), [watch-movies.net]("http://www.watch-movies-links.net/") - they don't always have the latest shows, and the flash video players aren't of the greatest quality, but they ain't half bad!  I've been planning to get a DVB-T card for my computer so I can watch "real" TV on the machine, but I'm in no great rush to do that now.  More money for drugs!  :)
