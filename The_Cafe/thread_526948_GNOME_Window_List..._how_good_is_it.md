### Post by tehkain on 2007-08-16
I would like there to be a toggle like in xfce where I can make the list display icons only. The visual reference focuses on a central circular point, while the word reference(and small icon) requires the comprehension of multiple characters. I much prefer documents written in the English alphabet when faced with a single symbol alternative(some Asian scripts), but Icons have a distinct advantage in a window/dock.

This is a subject that has many different ideas but none universally correct or perfect(or even close). It needs more focus as it is a great issue. All of the work going into window listing and management today lacks innovation, new ideas, and puts way to much focus on the visual.

The window list is the standard on most PCs(os independent) because it is the greatest common single solution. For most existing users it makes perfect sense. Now the Apple Dock is nice but (note I am talking about the people who do not like to learn) some people might have to take a moment to get used to a icon driven launcher/window list.

Some times Scale, Switcher, Vistathingy, Expose, alt-tab like,  and window previews are the future, but not always.

I just wish a good team would try to tackle this issue and think outside the box rather then making X-like apps. Also I wish distros would consider these things for inclusion because we have seen that(as much as I love them) gnome is staying independent of a good number of things like Compositing.

So here is to the future. Replace the gnomepanel, window list, and notification area - good riddens.
