### Post by orlox on 2009-09-27
> **entr3p said:**
> Agreed. There's already **enough** dead, half finished video editors.

+1

Also, the number of programs being currently developed is not an issue (though there are many dead right now...)

Just take openshot, kdenlive and PiTiVi, the three being actively developed, and with more than one developer. These are getting interesting functionality at a fast pace. And there are others being developed, but these three are getting most of the attention right now.

So, if you want to make the exercise, you're absolutely free to do it, no matter the opinion of anyone else. But, if you want to contribute to open source, you must really think if it's worth starting a completely new project. To begin with:
[LIST]
[*]Does your project adds anything new to the current list of projects??
[*]Are you really going to invest enough time to make this project compete with the current alternatives?
[*]Do you really need to start from scratch?? All three projects I mentioned are based on other multimedia frameworks (MLT for openshot and kdenlive, gstreamer and gnonlin for PiTiVi).
[/LIST]
So, if you are going to invest a lot of time on this, be warned, unless you think it VERY well, all your work could end up as just an exercise.

And in any case, I think a more useful exercise on open source development would be to become a part of an existing project. There are high quality products already out there, and it's very hard to get to that point by solo-coding.

But once again, if you want to try it, you're absolutely free to do so. I would have said the same thing to the guy who started openshot (with kdenlive and PiTiVi already out when he started), but he managed to pull out a well thought design, put a lot of effort in the project, and has gotten very far.
