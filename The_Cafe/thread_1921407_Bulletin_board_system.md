### Post by t0p on 2012-02-06
There's a kind of BBS thing going on at freeshell.org.  You connect by ssh or telnet, then you're in a white text on black background milieu, with message boards and MUDs and a whole other bunch of that "authentic internet before the web destroyed it"-type stuff.

If you point your browser at [freeshell.org]("http://freeshell.org"), you'll find full info on how to join in.  Oh, and they have webmail too, as well as mail, mailx mutt or whatever  cli email client floats your boat.  Oh, and there's pico *and* nano!  There's a vi tutorial too, if you really wanna get down and dirty.

### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2012-02-11
If you are wanting to host it on your own computer you may want to look at "Linux/Apache/MySQL/PHP" and "phpBB". "phpBB" [screenshots]("http://www.google.com/search?q=phpbb+screenshots&hl=en&prmd=imvns&tbm=isch&tbo=u&source=univ&sa=X&ei=Etk2T8mILYHs2QXywNX0AQ&ved=0CDQQsAQ&biw=1105&bih=639").

"Linux/Apache/MySQL/PHP" install instructions at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ApacheMySQLPHP](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ApacheMySQLPHP). "phpBB" ([http://www.phpbb.com/](http://www.phpbb.com/)) is in the Ubuntu software repository.

It took me about 4 hours to install and configure.

I installed the default LAMP stack using the first two commands on the page ApacheMySQLPHP I linked to. Then I installed phpBB from the software repositories. The rest of the time was spent seting up the bulletin board the way I wanted it.

Once you have a LAMP stack you could also look at a Wiki; such as, MediaWiki ([http://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/MediaWiki](http://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/MediaWiki)) which is also in the Ubuntu software repository.
