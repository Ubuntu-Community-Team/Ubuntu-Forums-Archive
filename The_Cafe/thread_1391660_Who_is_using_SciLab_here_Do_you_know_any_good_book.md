Not really. Octave itself has no gui. Qt-octave is a frontend for it written in Qt. That does not make it in any way affiliated with KDE. One reason a lot of people like writing in Qt is that Qt is much easier to port to Windows, while GTK+ is sometimes very difficult to get working on Windows.
