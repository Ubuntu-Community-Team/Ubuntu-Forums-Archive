Main reasons for not liking the iPhone:

[LIST=1]
[*]Difficulty to develop (must use Mac OSx on an intel chipset, must register with apple, must pay yearly fee, must submit software to Apple for approval). Native language is Objective C, my background is C/C++ and Java. While it's not a big ask to learn a new language, I do find it annoying how different it is. Not going to get into the whole ideological flame war about this, just IHMO its a limitation.
[*]Can only being able to install apps from iTunes.
[*]iTunes only being on Windows and OSX.
[*]Sealed unit, cannot replace battery.
[*]No flash.
[*]Various other issues but not really significant.
[*]No USB/MicroUSB/USB on the Go/Full USB
[/LIST]

Basically every single point above I can find an android device that doesn't have such a limitation. I should note however that I've yet to find Full USB for an android device and I've only found USB on the Go on a Android tablet. It's not a must have, but I really would like it.

End of the day it's really about your needs. The phone that best suits you really depends on what you need. There are a lot of reasons why someone likes one phone over another.

I should say in defence of the iPhone is that Apple have done something good for people who are not technically minded. When it comes to a simple interface with simple application management and music management, then the iPhone is what I would recommend.
