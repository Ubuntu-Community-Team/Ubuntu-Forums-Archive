### Post by james_xxx on 2008-12-22
In my opinion, Emusic is great. It is where I get most of my new music. 

However, if your tastes tend to be more in the 'Top 40' type category, you will probably hate it. 

If you are into world music, or a LOT of other non-Top-40 tunes, you will really appreciate it.

By the way, there are several downloaders available for Emusic Linux users. Emusic has their own, which I have not tried, but I use Emusicj, which works perfectly for me.

Link:
[http://www.kallisti.net.nz/EMusicJ/HomePage](http://www.kallisti.net.nz/EMusicJ/HomePage)


I do, however, wish that Emusic had support for free formats, such as .ogg.

### Post by enigmaingr on 2008-12-23
Consider giving Lala.com a chance.  It started off as a CD swapping site but has now moved on to DRM-free downloading.  It also allows you to upload all your music files (even from ripped CDs) to their site so that you can listen to your own music on any internet-connected computer.  You also get a 50-song credit that allows you to store 50 songs in your profile.  You may play these songs as many times as you like through the website.  If you choose to buy the song, you can download them for $.79-$.99 each.  

Perhaps the best thing about lala is that you may listen to the complete song (for about 75% of the inventory) before purchasing.
