### Post by NovaAesa on 2008-08-17
Well I'm doing software engineering (first year atm) at the University of Newcastle. And money things are fairly easy for me due to the way that tertiary education and the government works in Australia. I don't actually have to pay for any of my uni fees until after uni is finshed, due to a student loan that any Aussie citizen can get. Fees are cheap here to if you are an Aussie citizen. I think it's about $18K at the end of 4 years. And then we have to pay if off when we get a job, a bit like tax (it gets taken out before you get a pay check).

Here's the really screwed up thing about Australia though (it works in my favour so I'm not complaining). I get free money from the government because my parenst are poor (neither have jobs) and I'm studying away from home. I get about $430 a fortnight, which easily covers my rent, food, other expenses etc. On top of that I've got an equity schollarship FOR BEING POOR AS WELL! That's $4K a year extra. I get a few other schollarships as well, $1K here, $2K there. These were from achieving acedemically well in high school though, which I had to work really hard to get. I think one of them was for getting dux of my year.

And this is where it gets really unfair. One of my friends who is doing EE is in super financial touble. Her family is middle class (compared to mine -very low class), so she doesn't get free money from the government. She's smart, but not super smart, so no scholarships. So she's having to work in all her spare time (she has 3 jobs) just to pay for rent and food.

So I don't think it's fair. I get rewarded because my parents don't work and come from a dirt poor family, and she gets stuck working many many hours each week because she comes from a better off family. Seems mixed up to me :S

Although I'm university educated (it was free in Australia when I did it, and I got paid living expenses, lived at home and had a part-time job too) I don't believe a higher education is essential. I think western society has "over bought" the tertiary education myth and that it is now a business built on false promises.  I know many people who are very smart, successful and happy who never went near a university.  I've also met a number of post-doctoral fellows driving cabs.

The sort of mass-produced vocational degree education offered by most universities (up to and including doctorates) fits people to stereotyped jobs in industries and bureacracies that may or may not exist a few years after they graduate.  I now employ many graduates and I often think I'd give up three of them for one street-smart kid with a fast brain and a willingness to do whatever it takes.

I've begun to believe that mass tertiary education is basically a con.  They have convinced governments and industry that certain jobs require certain qualifications (and have gone about "professionalizing" jobs as diverse as nursing, surveying, graphic design, journalism and "hospitality" - all of which used to be learned on-the-job via apprenticeships).  We really risk undervaluing a whole range of skills and talents that have nothing to do with studying text books, sucking up to lecturers and passing exams.

I do think that if you are driven to learn and have talent, university is the right way to go (whatever the cost) provided you choose a "real" degree and select a top institution. I just don't think that describes 80% of the people I see doing it.  

And that's all I have say about that.

Grumpy Old Man

### Post by clash on 2008-08-18
Don't know about anywhere else but in Ireland all education is free. (unless your filthy rich). 

Way it works here is as follows ....

Level 1 -> Low income people. If your parents earn below a certain amount of money a year, you apply to your local government authority and they will pay all your fees and give you a small grant during the year. (usually just enough to pay for rent, food and maybe a few drinks, of course dependant on the price of rent where you are ....)

Level 2-> middle-relatively high income people. Again going on your parents earnings, the government authority will pay all your fees and usually give you some sort of grant depending on your income. 

Level 3-> high - very high income. These people don't need to worry about money so the government doesn't pay their fees. :) If you are in this bracket paying for university is not a problem.

Oh and btw I know its different in other Countries (USA) but here it doesn't matter the university etc you go to. e.g > You can go to our best University or our worst. The government will still pay your fees. The Universities basically go on highschool results. i.e > The best universities only take the best students, it has nothing to do with how much money you have/can pay them. 

I honestly don't know 100% but I'm fairly sure its the same scenario in most EU countries. I'm also pretty sure that if you can speak French in a fairly fluent level that you can go to any of their universities for free but you have to pass a French language exam first. 

So basically I didn't pay a penny to go to University. :guitar: I actually got money from the government (very very little, enough for a few pints of Guinness and a couple boxes of fags a week) but I don't owe anyone any money for it. Oh except I had to pay for rent which I had a part time job for, well rent and beer.
