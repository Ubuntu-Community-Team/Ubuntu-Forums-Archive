### Post by samjh on 2008-06-11
The thing about X.Org is that it is a low-profile project (ie. most end-users don't realise what it does or notice it in action), so it gets very little attention from the less savvy portion of the Linux user population.

Even among the portion of users who know what it is, X.Org deals with features that delve deeply toward the "hard" end of coding, close to the metal, failure-sensitive stuff.  This means that even among programmers, contributing to it can be a somewhat scary prospect.

Hopefully as more vendors jump on the Linux bandwagon (I'm looking right at Acer, Dell, Lenovo, etc.), hopefully some of those vendors will contribute in the form of monetary support for X.Org developers or even assign some of their developers to work on X.Org.

It needs a good amount of tender loving care, but it isn't getting much, if any.
