### Post by jpeddicord on 2010-09-09
IANAL:

While you are technically downloading the content, "ripping" it would violate this statement: > You may access Content for your information and personal use solely as intended **through the provided functionality** of the Service and as permitted under these Terms of Service.

YouTube downloader apps aren't covered under "provided functionality" -- only viewing the video in the page is provided.

For applications that let you view YouTube things get a little different; as far as I know you can **view** videos in third-party applications -- I'm not 100% sure on this though, so please don't quote me.

And then there's fair use -- I'm not even sure where this fits into the picture.

[QUOTE=fiyer]Violating the TOS of a website is slightly different than violating the law.

Youtube's TOS is an agreement between you and the company. [/QUOTE]This is true. If you violate their TOS you aren't directly doing anything illegal, but they're free to block you and you could be considered trespassing. Or something like that.

It's a little bit like restaurants. Smoking in businesses isn't illegal everywhere (though in Ohio it is), but if it's a restaurant's policy to not allow it they can legally ask you to leave. If you don't, you're trespassing. Weird analogy, but I think it applies.

### Post by Dr. C on 2010-09-10
IANAL

There are a host of issues here: 

1) Under which country's laws are we talking about here? The OP's question is totally vague since it does not identify the jurisdiction.

2) Once we agree on jurisdiction. The next questions become:
a) Is the contract enforceable? Are there other rights for example in copyright law that override certain clauses in the contract? 
b) Has the user actually agreed to it?
c) Is breaking the contract illegal or merely a civil matter?

3) The response of Ubuntu forums. One must understand the difficult situation the Administrators and Moderators are placed here.
a) Under which country's laws is Ubuntu forums governed? 
b) How do you respond to a situation where something is: Perfectly ok with no civil liability in country (a) a civil matter in country (b) and illegal in country (c).
