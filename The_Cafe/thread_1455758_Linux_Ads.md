### Post by Penguin Guy on 2010-04-16
I've just taken a look at some of the Linux ads [here]("http://video.linuxfoundation.org/"). Before you read on, I would like to say that I mean no offence to the people who made the videos, I realize that all the videos took an enormous amount of time and effort to make.

However, all the adverts seem to be saying either that 'Linux is free!' or, 'Hey look - we have a cool mascot!', neither of which is honestly going to persuade anyone to switch (freedom just makes us sound like hippies and no one really cares about the mascot). We should instead be putting across the facts that Linux:
[LIST]
[*]Is faster
[*]Crashes less
[*]Looks better
[/LIST]

Also:
[LIST]
[*]I want to say that (in my opinion) the [standard mascot]("http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=153470&stc=1&d=1271430379") gives a rather bad impression of Linux whereas the [crystallized mascot]("http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=153471&stc=1&d=1271430379") has a far more professional look to it.
[*]Take a look at  [this video]("http://video.linuxfoundation.org/video/1262") - very epic but couldn't help thinking it looked a bit like a zombie film trailer, speaking of which, [these penguins]("http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=153474&d=1271431176") look more scary than friendly.
[/LIST]
