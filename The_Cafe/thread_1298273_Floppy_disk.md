### Post by Gwasanaethau on 2009-10-22
Just a couple of years ago, we used to hand in college assignments on 3½" floppies. I still have a few floating around, though I rarely use them. I use one as a Windows 98 startup diskette, which I may need in the future so I've kept it. I added a floppy drive to the last computer I ordered (only four years ago). It only cost me &#8364;1,21!

I also prefer PS/2 mice and keyboards to USB, they just seem to work better for me. Besides, it frees up USB ports for other things. I had my printer connected to the parallel port too, but that is definitely something that's been made obsolete by USB and ethernet &#8211; parallel connections are painfully slow (and I couldn't check my ink status via a parallel connection either).

As for serial ports, my desktop has two, but I've never used them. I don't even know what they could be used for, either.

P.S. The disc inside a 3½" diskette is 'floppy', even if the case was not. I believe that's why they were named as such.
