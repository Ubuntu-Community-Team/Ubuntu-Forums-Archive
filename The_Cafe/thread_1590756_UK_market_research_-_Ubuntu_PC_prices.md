### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-10-08
I tried the same thing earlier this year selling through eBay and I made a small profit, just not enough to replace a steady job. Most of the buyers were new to Ubuntu and I'm happy to say they all were satisfied with the OS. What are your aspirations? There is definitely a demand out there for Ubuntu PCs in the UK.

The large volume sellers can afford to undercut you on price (I'm guessing you are operating small scale?) so you will have to offer something extra to get people interested. I offered free support and hardware guarantee for 6 months. Here is a similar spec PC to your test model, on sale for £100 less than your price:
[http://cgi.ebay.co.uk/Desktop-PC-ATHLON-II-X4-Quadcore-4-x-2-8Ghz-2gb-250gb-/150503359873?pt=UK_Computing_DesktopPCs&hash=item230ab30581](http://cgi.ebay.co.uk/Desktop-PC-ATHLON-II-X4-Quadcore-4-x-2-8Ghz-2gb-250gb-/150503359873?pt=UK_Computing_DesktopPCs&hash=item230ab30581)

It depends on more than the price of course. Obviously you will make  sure the hardware is 100% compatible, but what about OS upgrades? With a  new version wireless adapters and GPUs can often decide not to play  nice. I'd suggest you stick with LTS releases, unless the customer requests otherwise.

Plus other headaches, I had 2 brand new GPU failures in a month and Royal Mail managed to "lose" one of my PCs on route to the customer.

Good luck is what I am saying. :)
