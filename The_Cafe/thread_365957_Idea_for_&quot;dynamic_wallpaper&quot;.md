### Post by rubycon on 2007-02-20
I had this vision of a desktop wallpaper some time ago that I wanted to share. Now that we have a more or less 3D-powered desktop it would be cool to use the effects to get a more dynamic experience. My idea is to have a simple minimalistic landscape picture (vector art would probably be best suited), say a picture of a savanna for Ubuntu. Just that the sun would rise and set along with your clock giving you a wallpaper that would actually reflect the time of the day with all that it would imply, such as a changing color scheme and stars at night. Maybe also throw in some silhouettes of animals moving in the background without bloating it too much would be cool. 

I haven't looked into this very much yet (maybe there's something similar available?), I just wanted to hear your thoughts on this and maybe get some ideas about how something like this could be done.

### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-03-17
No need for those programs anymore! A fellow known as Søren worked on timelapsed background support for GNOME. You can see it going in Fedora 8. Just not a very prominent feature...

[http://mail.gnome.org/archives/desktop-devel-list/2007-September/msg00530.html](http://mail.gnome.org/archives/desktop-devel-list/2007-September/msg00530.html)

I definitely agree with you, background images like that can be very cool. Fedora's changes depending on time of day, and it has a great impact. A scene like the one you describe would definitely look wonderful in Ubuntu :)
Besides that, it's rather unique in terms of being default in an operating system. MacOS does have changing wallpapers, but its implementation is rather primitive; just images changing set to a timer. No fun time of day stuff.
