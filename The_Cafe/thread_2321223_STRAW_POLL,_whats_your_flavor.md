### Post by TheFu on 2016-04-24
> **izznogooood said:**
> I had no Idea there where so many MATE users, why are there BUSes against unity?
Could it be hardware related?

Flexibility and choice. Gotta love Linux!  My 80+ yr old Mom happily ran LXDE before she died. She would probably run Mate.  It wasn't a hardware thing for her - she had a Core i7 with a high-end, CAD, GPU.

Unity didn't work for years inside virtual machines, that was my problem with it.  Almost everything I do is virtualized.  Plus, I don't have/buy GPUs and the eye candy in Unity just isn't worth it for me.  I'm running this "desktop" under KVM, inside a VM, over the network right now.  My systems are all reasonably fast, but eye-candy isn't something I like. Don't want to waste the power on it.  Plus, compositing DEs were always less stable for me.

I want a system that gets out of my way and uses few resources - that isn't unity, IME. I could list 50 reasons by myself why Unity isn't for me, starting with full-screen apps. Hate those. This VM has 1 vCPU and 1.5G of RAM. Minimal so the system it is on can handle other useful tasks like running a blog, CMS, email server, document server, VPN, slash-clone, and a few other things.  

At my LUG (weekly meetings on Sundays), almost everyone new coming from Windows runs Linux Mint. But there are some folks who do run Unity and like it.  One of those guys does it simply because most people do use Unity and he wants that "stock" experience.

I always stress that the OS is not the GUI.  Swap in what ever GUI you like and be happy. ;)
