### Post by Paqman on 2012-03-22
> **BertN45 said:**
> 
I am seriously considering to buy some solar panels to feed my 4 inverter batteries and disconnect from the net completely. I just put another motor in the fridge for the second time this month.

If your inverter is compatible with PV that might be a good idea, although you'd almost certainly need to beef up the number of batteries. Fully off-grid PV is generally a major (ongoing) investment. 

Another option is to switch some of your loads to 12V DC and run them straight off a battery bank with a simple solar charge controller and a PV array. Charge controllers are much cheaper than inverters. You can get 12V DC power supplies for computers, they use them for in-car computing systems. Lighting can also be run directly off 12V DC (eg: spotlights). It'd be impractical to run it right round your house, but you could certainly do something small-scale.

Just a thought ;)
