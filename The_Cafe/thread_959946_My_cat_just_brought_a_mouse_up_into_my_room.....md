### Post by Thelasko on 2008-10-28
FYI: Mice in the house are a symptom of another problem, AIR LEAKS!  If you let your cat outside, or live in a temperate climate, this doesn't apply to you.  Those of you that live in parts of the world that experience a cold winter, pay attention, you will not only stop the mice but save money on your heating bill.

When it starts getting cold outside, like right now, mice start to look for a warm place to spend the winter.  This is usually a house.  They crawl in through gaps in siding, doors, or where pipes pass through walls.

[Here is an article from the US government]("http://www.energystar.gov/index.cfm?c=home_sealing.hm_improvement_sealing") on finding and addressing air leaks. [ Here is another article from Popular Mechanics.]("http://www.popularmechanics.com/home_journal/home_improvement/4199453.html")  Edit:  [CDC mouse control]("http://www.cdc.gov/Rodents/prevent_rodents/seal_up.htm")
