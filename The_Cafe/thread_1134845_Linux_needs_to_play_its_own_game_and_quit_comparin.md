### Post by samjh on 2009-04-24
She has some good points, but makes strange remarks.

I agree that the Linux community need to stop comparing Linux to Windows.  Yes, people do it.  Just take a look at the Recurring Discussions forum here.  There are many threads which try to compare or contrast Linux with Windows.  It's not a phenomenon particular to Ubuntuforums.org, it happens in other Linux discussion boards too, although it seems more prevalent here than elsewhere.

I also agree that Linux has little chance of ousting Windows in areas where Windows is dominant.  Especially on the desktop market.  Yes, we should aim for Linux to be competitive on the desktop, but that is a very long-term goal.  Let's not kid ourselves - Microsoft isn't exactly trembling from head to toe because of Linux.

But her statements about the absence of Linux in data centres and back office is just wrong.  Same with her comments about the instability of Linux applications in comparison with Windows applications.  I find Linux applications generally more stable (although not by much).  And no, the Linux community doesn't compare "inflated" figures about Linux popularity and compare it to Windows - she must have pulled that one out of thin air.

Overall, a 5/10. :|
