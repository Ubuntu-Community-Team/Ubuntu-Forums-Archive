### Post by HappinessNow on 2009-08-25
> A **troll** is a member of a race of fearsome creatures from [Norse mythology]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norse_mythology"). Originally more or less the Nordic equivalents of [giants]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant_%28mythology%29"), although often smaller in size, the different depictions have come to range from the fiendish giants – similar to the [ogres]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ogre") of [England]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England") (also called Trolls at times, see [Troller's Gill]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troller%27s_Gill")) – to a devious, more human-like folk of the wilderness, living underground in hills, caves or mounds. In the [Faroe islands]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faroe_islands"), [Orkney]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orkney") and [Shetland]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shetland") tales, trolls are called [trows]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trow_%28folklore%29"), adopted from the Norse language when these islands were settled by [Vikings]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vikings").
 Nordic literature, art and music from the [romantic era]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanticism") and onwards has adapted trolls in various manners – often in the form of an aboriginal race, endowed with oversized ears and noses. From here, as well as from Scandinavian fairy tales such as [Three Billy Goats Gruff]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Billy_Goats_Gruff"), trolls have achieved international recognition, and in modern [fantasy]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fantasy") literature and role-playing games, trolls are featured to the extent of being [stock characters]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stock_characters").


The usage of the word *troll* has developed over time. It might have had the original meaning of *[supernatural]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supernatural")* or *[magical]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magic_%28paranormal%29")* with an overlay of *malignant* and *perilous*[*[citation needed]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed")*]. Another likely suggestion is that it means "someone who behaves violently"[*[citation needed]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed")*]. In old Swedish law, *trolleri* was a particular kind of magic intended to do harm[*[citation needed]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed")*]. It should also be noted that [North Germanic]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Germanic_languages") terms such as *trolldom* ([witchcraft]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Witchcraft")) and *trolla/trylle* (perform magic tricks) in modern Scandinavian languages does not imply any connection with the mythical being. Moreover, in the sources for [Norse mythology]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norse_mythology")[*[citation needed]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed")*], *troll* can signify any uncanny being, including but not restricted to the Norse giants (*[jötnar]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%B6tunn")*).
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troll](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troll)

It is interesting to see how the word troll has devolved to be used in forums.

Edit: trolling is actually a fishing term, of dragging bait on the bottom of a river/lake hoping for some bites. much like posting in response to an OP. :P
