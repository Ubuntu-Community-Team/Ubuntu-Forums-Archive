### Post by V for Vincent on 2011-12-02
I don't know exactly how the American system works, but I can relate to your situation. I have a European bachelor's (which is a three-year degree) in literature and linguistics, but I switched to CS rather than pursuing a master's degree in LL. So all in all, I think it'd be a good decision to switch if you're in a good financial position to do so.

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-12-02
My advice would be to first finish your masters in humanities, you have made such a big investment in time and effort and burning bridges behind you is a bad idea.

AFIK you cannot go from BS in humanities to MS in Computers. You will have to go back to BS in CS and then MS.

You can find a computer job with any degree form high-school to Ph.D. However, it is worth mentioning that the different levels would give you a much different type of job (i.e. the kind of work that a MS graduate would do is completely different from someone with High School + Certificate).

Some people prefer to get some education and then go out and find a job. I am proponent to the idea of getting as much education as possible even if it means living as a student for a few more years. In the end, the expected value of your salary goes higher the higher your education is. But then again, you will have to pay money for school and you will have to consider your financial situation as part of the equation.
