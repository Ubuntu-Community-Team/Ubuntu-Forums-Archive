### Post by Freddy on 2007-08-22
I mostly use Audacious (which is a fork of BMP, which is a fork of XMMS) to play my music, before that I used BMP and before that the old XMMS. Audacious uses a small Winamp like interface and it does what it's suppose to.

I really can't understand these new "improved" audio players, who really needs to browse the wikis and read the lyrics when playing you music, if you must read up on the artist just open up your browser, how hard can it be :).

If you want a small clean audio player I would recommend Audacious, since all development on the classic BMP is over and XMMS has been really old for many many years. You'll find it inside the ubuntu repository if you want a newer version you have to compile, the development version can be a real hassle to get installed and in my opinion not really worth it yet.

Mplayer takes care of all my needs of playing moving pictures.
