### Post by toupeiro on 2008-08-30
This actually happened to me a few years back.  And no, I am not afraid to drop names, because I think this type of action shows 100% lack of business ethics.

CSC (Computer Sciences Corporation), in an attempt to be the solid outsourcer of of Downstream refining IT in our area, laid off all sub-contracts to bring in their own, inexperienced employees.  We were not offered positions, but we were told we would have to stay on to train our replacements, while all the time they were buttoning down our security in fear that we might sabotage the systems.  Having some stitch of business ethics, I stayed and retrained my replacement, then got re-dispatched to our central office.  3 months later, CSC calls me with a sweet offer to come back and do what I was doing because .. guess what..  they couldn't do it.  I had supported that site for 5 years.  The mentality that they could bring someone in in three weeks to learn what we've been doing for 5 years is absurd.  Anyhow, I decided to take the offer, because it was a good one, and they had full request of their customer's IT manager for me specifically to come back.  9 months later, while our IT manager was out on business, they decided to tell me my services were no longer required.  The IT manager, when he found out about this, was infuriated, but to CSC I was just an employee number, I didn't matter, and they filled me with an inexperienced, less expensive employee number, and that was all they were required to do to meet quota.

Happily, I am not in that world anymore, but I absolutely applaud you for walking away.  Employers need to remember that "At-Will employment" swings both ways!  You can drop me without notice for any reason, and I can leave you high and dry for exactly the same reasons, so why don't we both just agree to be above that and you pay me for the value I provide, and I stay because I enjoy providing it and you pay me well for my services...  IT people provide value!  If you have to backfill 3 people to provide the same value as one, you've lost...
