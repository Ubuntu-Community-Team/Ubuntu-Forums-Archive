### Post by Helkaluin on 2013-04-22
If you haven't heard about it, Geary is a new open-source email client written by the Yorba team, who also wrote programs like Shotwell.

Geary is currently the default email application of Elementary OS, which builds upon Ubuntu.

Geary aims to be a simplistic, no-fuss, just works alternative to all the ageing email solutions on Linux.  It is written in Vala, supports conversation view natively, and as all elementary applications, has a distinct focus on its æsthetics.

Yorba is pushing a funding project for the next release, which includes quite some new features that will make Geary a valid alternative for everyday emailing.

[http://www.indiegogo.com/projects/geary-a-beautiful-modern-open-source-email-client?c=home](http://www.indiegogo.com/projects/geary-a-beautiful-modern-open-source-email-client?c=home)

We would *love* to do Exchange support!  That said, with a staff of 3 it would take at least a year or two to get right.  There's more than one protocol, there's calendering, contacts, etc. etc.  It's a lot just to spec out, let alone implement.
