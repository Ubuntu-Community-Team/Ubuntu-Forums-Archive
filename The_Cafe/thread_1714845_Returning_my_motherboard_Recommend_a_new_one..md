### Post by wizard10000 on 2011-03-26
If you have to bend the motherboard to get the heatsink to mount you're doing it wrong.

"High mounting pressure" is completely unnecessary.  What *is* necessary is good contact between processor and heatsink and it sounds to me like the heatsink was mounted improperly and/or somebody's using *way* too much thermal paste.  The amount of thermal paste you should be using is about the size of a grain of rice, spread evenly across the top of the processor with a single-edge razor blade.  The layer of thermal paste should be translucent, not opaque.

Thermal paste is used to fill the gaps between processor and heatsink.  Any more than that reduces your cooling efficiency pretty dramatically.

Asus makes some of the best boards in the business.  What's more likely is operator error.
