### Post by samalex on 2010-06-10
Hey guys,

I just saw [this article posted on HowToForge]("http://www.howtoforge.com/installing-apache2-with-php5-and-mysql-support-on-ubuntu-10.04-lamp") giving information on how to install Apache, MySQL, and PHP on Ubuntu 10.04.  It's basically just installing everything from the software repositories then testing things to verify it's configured property.  Pretty simple, and honestly anyone could follow this and get a full web server up in no time.

What makes me kinda laugh is I did a presentation around 2002 at one of our LUG meetings showing this exact thing, installing LAMP on Red Hat 7 or 8 (can't remember), and it was pages of documentation with downloading source code, compiling, changing configuration files so things would play well with each other, plus some common 'gotchas' which normally cropped up with missing packages (anyone remember RPM Hell?) or libraries.  The entire process took a while to run, and it often required some work to get things ironed out.

To me this just proves that Linux and Ubuntu specifically have made things so much simpler than days past whether you're a home user, budding network admin, or full fledged IT pro.

Pretty cool :)

Sam
