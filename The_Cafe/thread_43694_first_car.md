### Post by oddabe19 on 2005-06-22
This review is my own opinion, I actually have owned many of these brands of vehicles. And it's mainly based off of my experience..
One of the first things i would recommend is a manual tranny. They're more fun to drive, cheaper, better gas milage, and when your clutch fails (assumingly never if you don't tear your car up) it's generally cheaper then automatic transmission to replace.

I know everyone else in the world owns them but there's a reason:
Hondas are great cars. Especially if you get a manual transmission. They run forever.  And they're cheap.  And don't really need much outside of normal maintenance. I'm looking for anew one myself, but my current 1990 Civic has 273,000 miles on it and still runs strong (many original parts).

Subaru, I've never had one personally, but many of my friends have owned them and they've been just as happy as anyone else. They're very reliable aswell, downside: rust.

Toyota is another great company. for many of the same reasons.


Ones to stay away from:
Kia, Daewoo (remember them), and Hyndai.  They may look attractive with 10 year 100,000 mile warrenties, but Hyndai nearly went out of business in the mid 90's due to shotty cars.

Cars that are... meh....
GM vehicles (Hummer, Chevy, Saab, Pontiac, GMC, etc...) short of their trucks, their cars aren't manufactured with 'quality' in mind. Which is why they just canned 25,000 jobs.
Volvo, safe as hell, but rust like crazy.
Ford vehicles (lincoln, mercury, Mazda). same as GM.
Chrysler (Mercades, Dodge) - more expensive usually, but quality isn't great.

### Post by WirelessMike on 2005-06-23
My first was a 1977 Ford LTD II.  It was a boat.  4 doors, vinyl seats, white exterior, red inside.  It served it's purpose.  It taught me the value of gas mileage, vehicle maintenance and defensive driving.  I turned a corner on a dirt road to quickly and slammed into a 4' deep, 5' wide ditch.  Barely dented.  Finally scrapped the car when a gasket went out.

I've driven GM vehicles ever since.  Never had any major problems out of them, and I don't change the oil as regularly as I should.  My chevy truck has been nothing less than 100% dependable and trouble-free for over 57k miles so far.  It's a 2000 model.

The REAL reasons GM is having to make drastic cutbacks involve GM's major drop in sales due to their ignorance of the public's desire for better gas mileage and just plain better looking or innovative concepts in vehicle design, as is apparent in the increased sales of hybrid vehicles and the success of Ford's and Chrysler's newest design concepts like the PT Cruiser, Magnum, 300M, F150, Ford 500 and Mustang.  GM's CEO admitted this publicly in a press conference just this month.

Overall, I would suggest the best FIRST car would be exceptionally safe above all else.  That would suggest Honda, GM, Ford, Volvo, Toyota or Nissan...  Not necessarily in that order and not every vehicle they make.  I've no experience or research regarding Volkswagens so I can't speak to how they rank.
