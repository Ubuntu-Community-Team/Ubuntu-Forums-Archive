### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-01-06
Do you really want to unplug it from the TV and take it into your computer room every time you have new files to add to it?

No?

Then you might prefer a networked media player that can browse Samba shares hosted on your computer.

It also has the added advantage of being very scalable. Even if you don't mind unplugging the HDD media player from the telly and plugging it into your computer to transfer new videos, what happens if you get another media player for another TV? With a networked media player you can add as many players as your network's bandwidth will allow. I've got one in my lounge room and I've just recieved one for my bedroom.

The networked devices usually have a USB port anyway for memory card readers, flash drives and HDDs. Oh and if you don't have Ethernet cabling, you can sometimes use an approved Wlan dongle, or you can definitely use Homeplug networking that works over your home's power cabling.

Make sure you buy one with good format support. There are some lemons out there that will play Xvid and MPEG-4 in MPEG and AVI containers, and nothing else. The ones I have are made by Astone and they play almost anything you can think of (including Vorbis, but not Theora); and they can even read Ext3-formatted hard disks.
