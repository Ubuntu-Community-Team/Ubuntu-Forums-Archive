### Post by The Producer on 2005-05-13
Well, eversince the beginning of this week, i've downloaded and tried out the SuSE Professional 9.3 (yes, downloading it from Torrent or P2P is legal according to Novell themselves), to see how much has improved since 9.2.

Overall, I was impressed.  During my time in Ubuntu, i've developed a great appreication for GNOME, and in SuSE it appears as if they put more time into it than in 9.2.  Compared to Ubuntu's GNOME however, SuSE's doesn't come close when it comes to speed and stability.  However, apps seem to open faster, especially OO2, which, IMO, is terribly sluggish in Ubuntu 5.04.
I also was now able to try out the Linux versions of XSI and Houdini, which I was never able to try because of some dependancy issues.  I also was introduced to Jahshaka, which seems to be a very interesting video editor for Linux, and I hope to maybe check it out on Ubuntu (if I can in the first place), because compliation in SuSE failed for reasons beyond my knowledge level.  The pre-compiled binary wouldn't stay open for 30 seconds.

YAST is good, but i've been spoiled by Synaptic Package Manager...so.. :-)

There are things I like to see Ubuntu take on as well...like "kdesu", which allowed me to run a super-user mode of Nautilus without having to open the terminal or log out.  Also, a way to install RPMs or DEBs that are outside of Synaptic without having to resort to the terminal would be nice too, since most of the apps i'm interested in are not in the repositories.  Or maybe a automatic GUI for ./configure, make and make install?

After now having used all of the major distrobutions available for Linux, I can say that I think Ubuntu is the best one out there.



Now...i've been asking myself, since i've pretty much seen what Linux has to offer me (from the standpoint of a (desktop/animation and a/v editing user, not from a experienced programmer), why should I stay?
Ever since I started in Linux 5 months ago, my Windows time has dropped TREMENDOUSLY.  At the samet time, my attempts to do my usual monthly animation project have failed...either because I can't get this or that feature working, or because I put too much trust in OSS... and having them fail, or not produce the quality of output i'm looking for (ex, Cinerella, Blender, without support from Anim8or or my C4D R7...).


Why Should I Stay?
------------------------------
1.  It's free (as in cost...the open-source part really doesn't mean much to me)
2.  It's stable
3.  I Love GNOME
4.  Some apps that I use (3d animation mostly), run better, and more importantly, faster, on Linux.
5.  I can do 80% of my standard compunting needs (web browsing, streaming, a/v playing, etc)


However, there are alot of things that I do not like, or that I need that Linux doesn't offer me, which is why i'm contemplating switching most of my workload back to Windows (Windows2K...until I skip up to WinXP Pro).

Why should I leave Linux?
----------------------------------------
1.  The majority of apps I use are Windows only, and refuse to run, or run properly in WINE.
2.  Video editing is sad in Linux
3.  I don't have to do 1h of pre-purchase research on new hardware before buying it...it will work 95% of the time.
4.  I love RTS games (Act of War, Command and Conquer), and cannot play them without having to run them through some 
emulator.
5.  I don't have to use the CLI in Windows...ever
6.  I can do 100% of my standard computing needs in Windows...including playing the silly little MSNM games that my friends love to play.
7.  Windows is stable...to me...maybe i'm lucky...
(and 8.  I can't encode WMV9 Professional video files  :oops: 


There are some other things that I've noticed about Linux that have completely fasicanted me...to the point that I refuse to recommend Linux to any of my friends...and that's the attitude of some members of the Linux community.  
I don't know if it's a small or large majority, I just know its exsists and it irritates the hell out of me, and I think it's what has turned off similar people like me from taking Linux up in replacement of Microsoft.

1.  I'm frequently insulted for still being a Microsoft (Micro$oft) user, and for supporting "The Man"

2.  When I suggest things that may help make desktop Linux more of a reality, i'm accused of being lazy because I won't do it the "old fashioned way".

3.  Some people forget that i'm not a programmer...and those who do know this are under the belief that it's my responsibility become one if I want to get my problems fixed...

Here are some ways of thinking i've noticed within the community as well.

1.  Microsoft and "Winblows" are evil, and Bill Gates is "Satan"
2.  All software and hardware companies owe Linux, and must bow down and support the communities every request.
3.  Linux is always right, regardless of what the facts say.
4.  Those who "migrate like sheep" from Microsoft are lower in intelligence and brain capacity than the "l33t" saviors of the Linux camp, and basic human respect and curtousy do not apply to them.

It's almost like this Linux movement, to some people, is like some sort of sick religious movement, where the primary objective is to destroy Microsoft completely and absolutely...while other things like trying to create something that can even [i]equal[i/] MS in a desktop sense goes on the backburner.
It's what made my early days of moving over to what they said was, "a Home Users Desktop Replacement that was JUST AS or MORE capable than Winblows!", a living hell.  I would post on forums, and either get no repsonse, or I would be told that 
"it's because you still have Winblows on a partition", or to RTFM, or read MAN pages.

Then when I would RTFM, it would be full of technical language that flew way over my head, especially in the MAN pages.... :-S

The largest negative experience i've had isn't with dealing with hardware or software problems with Linux, believe it or not.  It was with dealing with the completely uncalled for "i'm better than you" attitude that i've seen. 

UbuntuForums has been the greatest experience with a Linux user group i've ever had...not to say that it's perfect, because it's not.

So now...i'm pondering whether or not I should continue to use Linux as a main desktop.  Will the things that really help people like me transition over completely actually be implemented?  When it seems so many people I run into about 
them seem so dead set against making it easier?  It's said that Linux will get better...but, I don't see anybody wanting to deal with the community if the loudest people seem to be anti-social, fanatical, self-obsessed, "communists, who seem to worry more about fighting the capatalist Satan, Bill Gates, than with actually trying to improve the usability for the common user"  (that's basically what i've heard some people describe a portion of the community...there is also one article I read that suggested that they were also quickly crossing the boundries into terrorist territory...).

I know that isn't true... or at least I hope it isn't true, since frequently I see improvement in Linux daily, especially here at Ubuntu, but to tell you the truth, nearly 3/4ths of everyone I talk to about Linux either don't know about it, or say that it's only for light-deprivated nerds.  The guys i'm working with on my animated movie idea either tried and came back to Windows, or simply refuse to use it in the first place.

Personally, i'd like Linux, and I like Windows (half because it doesn't screw with me, and half because I have no choice but to).  

Well, i'd like to hear your suggestions for me, and for those who want to come over and happen to be reading this.

I'd like to want hear from those who have managed to get rid of Windows completely...and when you think, if ever, Linux will replace Windows in mainstream use?

This weekend i'll be making the finial decision...whether to continue to make Ubuntu the primary OS in GURB.

Thanks
