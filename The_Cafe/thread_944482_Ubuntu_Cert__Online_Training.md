### Post by germanix on 2008-10-11
I have not actually taken this course, but I did look through the course content some time ago. I was trying to decide whether to take this course, or attend a Ubuntu course personally at a venue here in Germany where I live, at the time. The course here was based on the contents of the online course. Once I studied the contents of both of these courses I came to realise that it realy was very basic stuff. It covers downloading the Live CD, installing it, configurations etc, and then takes you through the various desktop functions and applications. It also covers add/remove and the synaptic package manager etc, and I believe touches on the use of the terminal. If you have no knowledge of Linux Ubuntu it will surely  help you to understand some basic stuff, and give you certain skills to use your Distro better. I realised however that although at that time, I myself had only used Ubuntu fot approx. 2 months, I already knew all of the contents of the course and then some, purely by having read the how to`s, the Wiki, and by spending some time in the forums. If you read the community documentation, the 8.04 specific documentation, and look at sites like psychocats.net etc, and in addition follow the posts and threats on this forum, you will realise very soon that this course can really teach you very little that would be new to you. If however you want a structured course where you can learn step by step in your own time,starting from basics, you should take this course. Once you have, it would be nice if you posted your experience with it, here on the forum.
