### Post by Paerez on 2006-11-02
Grab banshee, and click Edit->Plugins. There, enable Smart Playlists. Then create a new smart playlist and set its options. A good idea is "Rating > 3 stars". Or you can give it a Genre to play from (or a selection of genres). Or no restrictions at all.

### Post by maxim99 on 2010-09-26
I don't know how it works, but Guayadeque has a "Smart Playlist" feature that definitely works. Just from the little I've used the program I like it a lot better than Amarok. Thanks for the recommendation. I'm quite surprised that such a little known application works so much better than some of the more renowned ones.

I also found that Banshee has a plugin called Mirage which analyzes your music files, and finger prints them, and has the ability to create a play list based on similar tracks it finds in the library. Using this method is accounts for different styles from an artist, as well as style changes through out an album, and doesn't limit selection based on genre. Instead is uses the acoustic properties of the songs to make selections without the other components coming into play.

Here's an article about it:
[http://ubuntumanual.org/posts/252/mirage-banshee-plugin-miraculously-adds-acoustically-similar-songs-to-your-playlist](http://ubuntumanual.org/posts/252/mirage-banshee-plugin-miraculously-adds-acoustically-similar-songs-to-your-playlist)

Here's a video about it:
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vmST5-NSITg](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vmST5-NSITg)

Here's the home page:
[http://hop.at/mirage/](http://hop.at/mirage/)

I started this quest looking for a replacement for MusicIP Mixer (MusicMagicMixer), and found two great applications with many great features. I'm quite happy with my bounty.
