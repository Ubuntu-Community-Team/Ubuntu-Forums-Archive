### Post by sisco311 on 2008-07-21
no/yes:
> GNU GRUB developed from a previous package called the **Grand Unified Bootloader** (a play on [grand unified theory]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_unified_theory")). It is predominantly used on [Unix-like]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unix-like") systems; the [GNU operating system]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_operating_system") uses GNU GRUB as its boot loader, as do most general-purpose [Linux distributions]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_distribution"). [Solaris]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solaris_Operating_System") has used GRUB as its bootloader on x86 systems since the Solaris 10 1/06 release.
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_GRUB](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_GRUB)
