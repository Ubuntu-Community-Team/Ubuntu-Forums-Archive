### Post by Superkoop on 2011-05-27
Personally I would prefer not to see most books I enjoy as movies, because the things that make a good book do not make a good movie usually. Books are so detail oriented, with twisted plots and side plots and lots of things that need to be developed throughout hundreds of pages and hours of reading. Movies need to entertain more quickly, there is much less time to become acquainted with the plots and side plots, the characters need to have their personalities established and developed in a fraction of the time, and it has to evoke emotion through visual effects rather than emotive descriptions.
Thus books and movies are entirely different media, and both need to be changed to work well in a different setting. So unless you can actually watch a movie based upon a book you enjoyed as an entirely separate entity, you probably won't enjoy it. I have a tough time doing this because I am first and foremost a book person, movies hardly ever actually move me in the same way books do.

To answer the OP though, I suppose the YA book series Pendragon by D.J. MacHale would make neat movies. I read them when I was in high school, I enjoyed them then, and they have fairly simple plots like the Harry Potter books.
