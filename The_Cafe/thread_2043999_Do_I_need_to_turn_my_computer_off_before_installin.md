### Post by Petro Dawg on 2012-08-17
Yes, you must turn it off, there is a good chance you can fry other components on your board if try to make connections while power is on.  It would also cause a fatal error if you remove the old RAM while the OS is running.  The computer probably wouldn't recognize the new RAM until restarted anyway.

What makes you think your computer might not restart?  Is it just really old, or do you have other specific issues?  Why upgrade RAM in a computer you are worried might not start up?  Sounds like you are throwing good money after bad.  Just save up for a new computer.

> But would it damage my body/nervous system?

Probably wouldn't kill you if you have a good home electric system (and don't have some other health issue).  But if you accidentally touch something it could give you a good "bite", maybe causing an electrical burn.

### Post by anewguy on 2012-08-17
As everyone has been stating, DO NOT try to do this with the power on and/or plugged in.  In addition, if it is a laptop/netbook/whatever other portable device, be SURE to remove the battery as well.  Not doing so is almost a guarantee of a dead device.

Can something in there hurt you?  Yep.  Kill you?  Not unless you start messing with the power supply or the AC mains line.

But here's the deal - you don't have to worry about whether or not it can hurt you IF you just power it off, unplug it, remove the battery (if applicable) do you?

So be smart - just turn it off, unplug it and remove the battery if applicable.

If you have some problem with it not booting after installing/swapping memory, take it back out.  Always be sure the memory matches the type the manufacturer says to use.
