### Post by wersdaluv on 2009-09-12
GTK+ is a great toolkit. However, Qt feels more advanced to many Linux users because of the very aesthetically pleasing themes it has. The popular GTK+ engines we have now (Murrine and Clearlooks) are good but they do not create a desktop that is as beautiful as Oxygen. [KDE 4.3]("http://kde.org/announcements/4.3/images/kde430-desktop.png") is probably the most beautiful Linux desktop I have ever seen. 

In fairness to Murrine, it served as a foundation to great themes like [Shiki-Colors]("http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Shiki-Colors?content=86717") and [Sonar]("http://forgeftp.novell.com/opensuse-art/openSUSE11.2/metatheme/"). Those are probably the best GTK+ themes that I have ever seen but that still isn't the look that I'm looking for. Murrine feels like glassy plastic. It works for some but I don't like that feel. Clearlooks, the default GNOME theme, looks good too. However, it doesn't seem to be moving forward. It was the best when it was released but it stopped growing. GNOME is to Clearlooks while KDE is to Oxygen. Mismatch, right?

Some themers attempted to do ports of Oxygen. I want to see a GTK+ theme that looks as modern as Oxygen but I'm not looking for a ripoff. Clones are nice but they do not seem to work. Attempting to copy every Oxygen element would lead to desperately adding elements that aren't meant for GTK+. 

A great example of a modern GTK+ engine is [Aurora]("http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=56438"). However, I'm not settling with Aurora because of some usability issues. The arrows are too pronounced and are distracting, Firefox tabs look odd, font color on Firefox right click menus look odd, etc. Also, there's a possibility that it will no longer be updated. The last time ECHM logged in to gnome-look.org was Jan 4. He may or may not log in to post an upgrade again.

I'm thinking of making a new GTK+ engine based on Clearlooks or Aurora that looks as modern as Oxygen but doesn't try too hard to rip Oxygen off so high quality would be maintained. 

Who's with me? Let's put this baby on Launchpad!
