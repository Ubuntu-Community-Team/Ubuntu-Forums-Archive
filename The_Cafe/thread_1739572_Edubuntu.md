### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-04-26
I am trying out Edubuntu 11.04 beta right now. My original intention was to examine the educational side of the OS so that I could recommend it to some friends who are educating their children at home. But at the moment I am using it to become familiar with the Unity desktop in preparation for the release of standard 11.04.

As well as some educational programs for all ages there are also programs that can be used to restrict what students can do on computers, programs to record the progress of students. I have not tested these programs but I am very good at Tux Maths and Tux Typing. Perhaps that is because they are designed for very young children. Also included are LibreOffice and other standard Ubuntu programs.

I can only guess at the usefulness of these programs to Chinese teachers and students but some of the programs might be helpful in becoming familiar with the English language or some other languages that are better supported by Linux developers. How good is support for Chinese in Ubuntu? I think that Edubuntu can be used to customise a network of computers so that students have a different desktop and programs to the teachers.

Regards.
