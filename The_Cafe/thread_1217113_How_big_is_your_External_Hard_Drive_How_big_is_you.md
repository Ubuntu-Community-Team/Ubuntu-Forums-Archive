### Post by handy on 2010-05-10
I used to use FreeNAS in a multi-purpose tower, but I have fairly recently gone to a ReadyNAS Duo, which I've currently got a 1TB WD Green drive in. It takes 2 drives in hot-swappable drive drawers. It is set up for RAID-1 (which sucks imho) but you can trick it into having 2 separate RAID-0 config's which basically gives you 2 JBOD HDDs.

There is a tiny motherboard in the thing that runs a cut down version of Debian. It has a gigabyte NIC built in too. I use NFS, but other protocols are available for OS X, & windows. 

256MB SODIMM, which can be upgraded to 1GB.

It also has a Transmission add-on that allows it to be an autonomous torrent slave. Which is great because it uses 4W at idle & 35W if there are 2x500GB drives in & working.

It's kind of a bridge between an external drive & a sophisticated NAS server.

It's also cool & quiet.

The only use I have for a flash drive is when I rarely stick the SD card out of my camera into an adapter & plug it into the computer.
