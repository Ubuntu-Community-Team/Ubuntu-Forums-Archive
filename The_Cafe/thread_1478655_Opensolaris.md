I would not argue that if Oracle abandons OpenSolaris that the project would be difficult to sustain and might die on the vine.  That is very different; however, from saying that Oracle _has_ abandoned OpenSolaris.  In fact, that last statement Oracle made about OpenSolaris was a pledge to continue supporting the project.  Oracle has been fairly involved in the Linux community, as well as partnering with proprietary interests and it may well be that the Company is attempting to determine where best to spend its resources, in terms of community projects, but I have neither seen nor heard any statement indicating Oracle plans to sunset OpenSolaris.  My other point was simply that the GPL is not the only open-source license; most Linux distros either ship with or provide access to software under a variety of licenses, including the BSDL, the MIT / X11, the MPL, Apache, and others.  I have used Ubuntu for a number of years and have been reasonably impressed with the quality of the distro.  It is only recently that I have started exploring other open-source OSes.  Part of the catalyst for that was what I view as irresponsible behavior on the part of Canonical in forcing adoption of Pulseaudio server (or at least making it very hard to avoid), in spite the general outcry against it, as well as adopting a beta bootloader and moving to a new service management system with what seemed like little warning or forethought.  I am running OSOL now, because it is interesting, but I may well find myself running a GNU-based OS (more likely Debian than Ubuntu) at sometime in the future.
