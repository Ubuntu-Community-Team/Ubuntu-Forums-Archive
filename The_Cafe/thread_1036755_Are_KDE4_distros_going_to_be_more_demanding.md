### Post by BigSilly on 2009-01-11
I'm having a bit of a distro hopping session at the moment. I always use Ubuntu as my main; that's a given, but I usually use the latest Mandriva as my dual boot option simply because I like it so much. However of late I've found it a little slow on my PC. My specs are very basic by today's standards - AMD Sempron 2200+.768Mb RAM, Geforce 6200 256Mb. Nothing stellar, but usually very good with any Linux.

I decided to try out a number of other OS's - chiefly Fedora and OpenSuse, and I'm noticing the minimum requirements now for KDE4 distros is 512Mb RAM, with 1Gb recommended. Both Mandriva and OpenSuse with KDE4 are a little sluggish on my hardware. Workable, but not anywhere near as fast as Ubuntu and Gnome.

Can we expect to see those minimum specs for KDE4 to climb upwards now as it improves? I'll be honest, I was quite surprised to see the minimum for KDE4 distros so high - I thought it was supposed to be less demanding than KDE3. Obviously I'm going to have to buy some RAM, but I didn't want to spend money on that and then find my processor needs upgrading. I may be better off just buying a new PC altogether.

Is KDE4 simply out of my league now?

### Post by GeneralZod on 2009-01-11
> **BigSilly said:**
> 
Can we expect to see those minimum specs for KDE4 to climb upwards now as it improves?

If KDE3 is anything to go by - and there's no reason to assume otherwise - after a certain point the resource consumption of KDE4 should, on average, begin to decrease with each successive release as things get more optimised and hard-coded functionality/ dependencies get spun off into optional plugins.  I suspect that Nokia's acquisition of TrollTech will lead to an even stronger focus on Qt performance, which will benefit KDE automatically.
