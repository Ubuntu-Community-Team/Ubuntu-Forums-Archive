### Post by Firestem4 on 2009-02-15
I am highly interested in learning Information Security, Specifically where it applies with networks. So I'm in the same boat as you. 

A friend of mine recommended a wide variety of good books that I want to get into. (It covers a broad array of subjects regarding computers and networking, programming etc). I'll list them, and you may or may not need them but i kept the list for personal future reference.

(In not a very specific order)

Andrew S. Tanenbaum - Modern Operating Systems
Andrew S. Tanenbaum - Computerarchitecture
Rick Lehtinen and G.T. Gangemi - Computer Security Basics
Cyrus Peikari and Anton Chuvakin - Security Warrior
Jon Erickson - Forbidden Code (If not available where you are substitute with Hacking: The Art of Exploitation instead)

Programming: (I have no programming experience so much of this is for me to get started)
Firstly he recommended for me learning Psuedo Code.
And he said to pick up a book on logic and regular expressions.

And if I can. Try to get a set of Rainbow Books. [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_Series](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_Series)

And here is a possible cert you may be interested in when you are further in your schooling/career. 

[http://www.eccouncil.org/ceh.htm](http://www.eccouncil.org/ceh.htm) (Ethical Hacking certification. While not industry standard is rapidly gaining notoriety and value).

Hope some of this helps!
