### Post by IYY on 2007-06-07
When it comes to game graphics, do you prefer 2D or 3D?

2D games include platformers like Super Mario, RPGs like Diablo and Fallout as well as Chrono Trigger, fighting games like Mortal Kombat and Street Fighter, strategy games like Warcraft II and Age of Empires I-II as well as Heroes of Might and Magic II and III.

3D games have pretty much replaced 2D games in every field: instead of the platformers we have shooters (and 3D platformers), we have 3D RPGs like Morrowind, all the strategy, RTS as well as turn-based, are in 3D...

Personally, I prefer 2D in all cases except for where the 3D is actually meaningful for the gameplay (like in shooters and racing games). Here are my reasons for liking 2D better:

1. Sharper, cleaner, more detailed visuals. They may be less realistic, but they just look much better to me.

2. No need to worry about the camera. This really confuses me in 3D strategy games: the game takes place on more or less flat ground... so why the 3D? The tilt often makes it required to spin the camera around just to get a good view. None of this was required in games like Age of Empires.

3. Work faster, smoother on older hardware.

4. They tend to be more artistic. With your average 3D fantasy game, for example, you see a whole array of transparent halos, explosions, huge glowing spiralling runes, but the overall effect just seems messy and tasteless to me.

5. They are cheaper to make, so smaller companies can develop them, and take more risks. I guess for this reason they also tend to have better plots and gameplay.

For me, the love for 2D games is more than just nostalgia: I actually think they are better. However, I realize that it could be because of my age (for example, people tend to believe that the "golden age of cinema" was around their highschool years) so I'm including age in the poll.

### Post by pmj on 2007-06-17
Look, there is no reason you can't have a 3D game that plays *exactly* like a 2D game. You can lock the camera and have 2D movement and it would play exactly the same. 3D only has benefits over 2D in such a game; it would mean much less work for the artists considering the extremely high quality of the animation, better hit detection, better feeling of actually being inside the world, better physics, better lighting, better graphics, better everything.

It's a shame the platformer pretty much died out as a genre a decade ago, in favor of games with full 3D movement, but that's not a fault of the technology but rather the game developers.
