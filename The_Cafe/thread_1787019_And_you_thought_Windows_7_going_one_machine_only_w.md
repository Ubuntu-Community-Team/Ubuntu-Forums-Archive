### Post by Macskeeball on 2011-06-20
See the [Redmond Pie](http://www.redmondpie.com/windows-8-build-7989-leaked-ships-with-new-virtual-keyboard-references-to-sms-support-per-feature-licensing-geo-location/) article referenced in that article for a little more commentary.

Currently Windows 7 does this with editions (starter, home basic, home premium, professional, ultimate, and enterprise), and any edition can be upgraded to a higher edition from within Windows for a fee. This seems like a way to simplify that with something akin to in-app purchases, for OS features. This way, Windows users could pay for the specific additional features they actually want rather than paying for bundles of additional features like they do now.

From a Windows user's perspective it sounds like it could be an improvement, but from a Linux and Mac user perspective, it sounds like a hassle compared to simply getting the full version from the very beginning. It also goes without saying that Linux distros are generally free. OS X Lion will be $30.

It's worth noting that even though MS may be thinking along these lines and working on this internally, that doesn't necessarily mean it will end up in the final product.
