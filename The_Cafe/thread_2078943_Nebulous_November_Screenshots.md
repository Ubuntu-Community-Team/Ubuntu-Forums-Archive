[http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v245/viperdvman/Screenshots/ScreenshotKubu_1.jpg](http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v245/viperdvman/Screenshots/ScreenshotKubu_1.jpg)

This time, instead of the pokemon themselves, I thought I'd make a couple of wallpapers of the Unova gym leader, Elesa, from Black & White *(both the anime and the games)*. It took quite a bit of effort, especially when it came to hunting for some of the images I used in making these wallpapers.

I've decided to stick with ROSA Launcher for a while. I'm finding the updated versions much less buggy, and working much more smoothly with my proprietary NVidia drivers. And I've set it to activate from Docky, just like I had done woth both Takeoff Launcher and GNOME Activities. This post ([**here**]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11823588&postcount=67")) illustrates how I accomplish it. The other screenshots show off my open KDE apps *(showing off Oxygen Transparent with full blur effects)* and my open ROSA Launcher.

Despite upgrading from Ubuntu 12.04 *(with KDE built on top)* to Kubuntu 12.10, I', pretty much sticking with the Mac OS X style  interface and most of the same Plasma and Window themes. So far... not bad. New distro... though mostly an artwork and icon change.
