### Post by SeijiSensei on 2016-06-17
Apparently Microsoft intends to build connections to the LinkedIn database into its Office products.  Today's [New York Times]("http://www.nytimes.com/2016/06/17/opinion/why-linkedin-will-make-you-hate-microsoft-word.html") has this *cri de coeur* from a dedicated Word user.

> [Microsoft CEO] Satya Nadella supplied one explanatory clue in an email that he sent to Microsoft employees. &#8220;This combination will make it possible for new experiences,&#8221; he wrote, such as &#8220;Office suggesting an expert to connect with via LinkedIn to help with a task you&#8217;re trying to complete.&#8221; He went on to predict that such experiences would &#8220;get more intelligent and delightful.&#8221;

&#8220;Delightful&#8221; is not the first adjective that comes to mind here, or even the 10th. If I&#8217;m working in Word, I can&#8217;t see why I&#8217;d welcome the intrusion of even a close friend, let alone a bot telling me about a stranger pulled from LinkedIn&#8217;s database.

Sounds like a good marketing strategy for Open/LibreOffice to me.
