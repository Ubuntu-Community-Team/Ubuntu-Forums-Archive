As a society we are slowly evolving. What your kids experience in school is probably going to be quite different from what you remember from school, just like what you experienced in school was probably much different from what your parents experienced. Things are different.

Times when it was acceptable for young boys to fist fight to resolve their disputes. Times when it was acceptable for seniors to paddle freshmen as high school initiation. When my parents were in high school if you knew how to use a computer you were a nerd. When I was in high school everyone used computers and you were an outcast if you didn't know how to use one. My point is society is constantly changing. Geeky things become less and less geeky until finally they are accepted as the norm. There is quickly coming a time when by isolating your child from computers for fear of him turning into an outcast will actually cause him to become more of an outcast. Something like "Hehe, thats the kid who doesn't even know how to type right" or "haha, you don't even know how to use the internet what a loser".

Ive witnessed this first hand. I knew a couple kids in elementary school whos parents wouldn't let them wear baggy clothing because that wouldn't have been acceptable when they were in school. The only thing this succeeded in doing was isolating these kids from other kids their own age who had the "cool" cloths. They were dressed different from other kids their age and they got picked on heavily for it.


As far as the eyes, that is something every computer user should to look into. The computer monitor itself has no negative effects on eyesight. The problems arise from sitting infront of the screen for hours on end. Your eyes are designed for hunting, and focusing on something two feet away from your face all day long can ruin your vision and cause nearsightedness. Also poor lighting conditions and placement of equipment is know to cause eye strain. These problems can all be avoided by proper conditions and taking frequent breaks from the screen.
