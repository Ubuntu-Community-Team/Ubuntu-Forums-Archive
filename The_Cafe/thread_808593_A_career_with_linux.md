### Post by Prospero2006 on 2008-05-26
Hello there,

I'm a school teacher in Texas, and over the last two years I've converted several computer labs at my school so that they run free software.

[http://www.neisd.net/imak/computerlabpage/](http://www.neisd.net/imak/computerlabpage/)

Now, the horizons for this kind of thinking are expanding every day. From Google services to staples like Open Office, it shocks me that school districts still feel it is necessary to spend +700 dollars per seat in school computer labs for software and licensing. As a tax payer, I'm really upset.

So, maybe school software suite setups and support?

There's a huge need out there. School systems need cheap alternatives.

### Post by tcpip4lyfe on 2008-05-27
I'm a linux networking admin for a startup.  When I started we had 3 ubuntu servers and 20 desktops.  Now we have 18 linux servers running ubuntu and red hat and over 100 workstations. Basically I just sit around and configure LDAP directories, Samba, Asterisk, and write shell scripts.  I went to school for Cisco and wound up doing what I'm doing right now.  If you get lucky, you don't really need a linux degree as long as you have a good personality and can prove that you know what you're doing.
