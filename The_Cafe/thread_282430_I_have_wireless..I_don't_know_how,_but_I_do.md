### Post by Jellicletrb on 2006-10-22
I'm quite new to Ubuntu, I got my first CD Wednesday.  I installed it on my desktop and with the help (ok, lots more than help) of Automatix, I had everything up and running by Thursday evening.  So while I was posting my hoorays and "look at me's" in the beginners forum, someone mentioned the KDE desktop to me, so I decided to put it on the laptop and take a look at it.  However, after reading all the scary posts about wireless networking on the boards, I thought that I'd use the ethernet card and a 25' "ribbon cable" and leave the wireless for a later date, when I know a bit more and get a bit braver.

So after installing Kubuntu on the laptop, dual booting with XP, I started exploring, and found an application called "wireless assistant" and thought, well, it can't hurt to look.  The assitant detected my integrated wireless AND my wireless router.  I right clicked on my network and it said, "You are about to disconnect from ****"

Disconnect????

So, I opened firefox and shazamm...there is Google.

hehehe...this is cool...look at me, having so much fun.
