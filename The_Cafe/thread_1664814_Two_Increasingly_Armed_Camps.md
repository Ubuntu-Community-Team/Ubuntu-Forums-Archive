### Post by 3Miro on 2011-01-11
>  Yet, while in an ideal universe, both sides would realize that their differences only benefit their proprietary rivals, in this one there seems little chance of that ever happening. 

Totally disagree.

Competition is the main source of trust for new ideas and new development. The strength of the FOSS community is that competition is internal, thus creating a perpetual driving force without danger from stagnation.

I also disagree with the author's negative view of Canonical establishing control over projects. The community is completely indifferent to company's profits or success, so for a company to prosper, they need a measure of control. Unless Canonical tries to close the project and make it proprietary (which is highly unlikely), there is no problem.
