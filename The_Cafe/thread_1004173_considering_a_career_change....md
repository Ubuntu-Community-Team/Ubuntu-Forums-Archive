### Post by BobLand on 2008-12-07
hardyn,
Try looking for an entry level windows server admin position.  Respond to classifieds in your local papers (if any exist) and hit all the web hiring sites like Monster.  

Post your resume with as much information as possible.  Often, recruiters use these sites for potential candidates and will fit you into an organization in a position possibly much different from what you would expect.

Stress your positives and never say anything negative about yourself.  Don't fill the page with useless words.  Stick to the facts.  Don't exaggerate too much as that can come around and bite you in the ***.

Keeping the resume to a single page is fine if you don't have much to fill in.  Worst thing is to babble on and on.

Remember, this is not a great time to be looking for a job but as an entry level candidate you may have a shot at companies that are downsizing and looking for cheap hires.  

Sometimes, the best strategy is to get your foot in the door so if you get offered something that is less then ideal, think if it can lead to something.  Most do.

Good luck,
bobland

### Post by sunoccard on 2008-12-07
if your going to try to do some work involving networking or any major technical work, i suggest you get a degree or certification like CCNA or A+ it applies very well. A+ is more dealing with hardware, and CCNA deals more on the technical networking side. I've personally taken the A+ before, it's not extremely difficult, but you'll need to be well versed in all aspects of hardware.
As for the CCNA, all i can say is good luck, that test is on a whole other level, ( i'm currently studying for it) but if you make that you won't have much trouble finding a job as a network admin or technician
