### Post by jespdj on 2008-01-09
I would absolutely not want to have a touch screen instead of a touchpad and / or mouse. The screen would be completely messed up with finger prints in no time.

A small, built-in projector would be cool if it works well (good enough resolution, doesn't eat too much power).

There are already many laptops with built-in Bluetooth. My Dell XPS M1330 has built-in Bluetooth, and I have a Logitech V470 Bluetooth mouse. It's very convenient to have a cordless mouse and not need any special receiver (especially since this laptop has only two USB ports).

The XPS M1330 also comes with a multimedia remote control, which you can store in the expansion card slot (it's small and flat).

Many laptops also have a fingerprint reader for logging in (it's optional on the XPS M1330, mine doesn't have it).

I hope that laptop batteries will get dramatically better in the coming years. Every now and then there's a story in the news about new, revolutionary battery technology. Just yesterday I read a story about superbatteries which could be charged to 90% of their capacity in just 5 minutes. Unfortunately most of those stories don't seem to lead to a real revolution. I'd like to have a small and light battery that lasts at least a day instead of 2 or 3 hours.
