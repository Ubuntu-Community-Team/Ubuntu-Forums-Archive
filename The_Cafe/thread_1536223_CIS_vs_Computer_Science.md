### Post by Simian Man on 2010-07-21
It's kind of like the difference between a mechanic vs. a mechanical engineer or a pharmacist vs. a chemist.  CIS is more about being really good at using systems that exist in a work environment, while CS is more about designing those systems in detail.

Both are important, but I think CS gives you a bit more options.

### Post by Chronon on 2010-07-21
I view information science as more abstract.  By my understanding, it deals with general methods for encoding and manipulating information.  On the other hand, I don't have much experience in either a CS or CIS department.  I am more familiar with information science from a physics viewpoint.

The CIS department at my previous school taught classes in general algorithms, operating system design, compiler design, etc.  It certainly did not seem geared toward applied CS, as stressing particular protocols or programming languages did not seem to be a big part of the curriculum.
