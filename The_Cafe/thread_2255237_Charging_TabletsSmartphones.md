How well educated on this subject are sales assistants? How well educated am I, on this subject? There are lots of myths around.

It used to be that if the battery was not completely discharged and then fully re-charged it would somehow have a "memory" of the power level at which it was recharged and when it was discharged through use back to that level it would show as being low in power and in need of re-charging. That was not a myth. But is it still valid?

I once read an electronics article on how to build a battery charger that automatically completely discharged the battery before recharging it. This was decades ago and the battery chargers for these modern devices must surely do the job right. Besides, I am not sure if this problem is valid for the type of batteries in these kind of devices.

There is one thing that I do accept as valid and that is the instruction that we get with these devices to fully charge the battery before first using it. It also stands to reason that if we are using the device as it is being re-charged then some of the power from the charger is being diverted from re-charging and so it must take longer to re-charge.

These devices are designed to be run on mains power as well as battery power. Is this not true? I would not want a tablet that only ran on battery power and then forced the user not to use it for the length of time it took to re-charge the battery. In that case I would expect the tablet to be supplied with 2 batteries, one to use and one to be on-charge.

### Post by carlwsnyder on 2014-12-03
On NiCd batteries, you can get 'memory' set if you don't fully discharge the battery before recharging it.

On Li-ION batteries, you never want to fully discharge the battery, as it [s]will[/s] may kill the battery, but you don't set up a memory by charging/discharging without fully discharging before fully charging the battery.

Oh yes, on your specific question, it depends on your charging current being greater than the discharge current while leaving the device powered on.  If you have a weak charger (low current capability), you CAN kill the device by leaving the device on while charging.  In other words, if your device doesn't show that it is charging while running and connected to the charger, shut it down!

### Post by user1397 on 2014-12-03
I will say, I'm by no means an expert, but I did work at a smartphone/tablet retail/repair shop for 2 years and I dealt with thousands of tablets/smartphones during that time.  From my experience, it does not matter at all to plug the charger in when the device is on...this is actually quite normal and many people use their device while it is charging.  One thing that used to be truer but not so much anymore, is that if you leave the device plugged in while it is already at full charge, this will negatively impact overall battery life.  Unless you excessively (and I mean *excessively*) do this, this won't affect you.  

Now one thing you should keep in mind when charging devices, is that even if your device has a universal charger port (like say, micro-USB), some chargers might not work with it and some could actually be damaging.  This I don't fully understand myself...I just know from my experience.  Sometimes I would use another manufacturer's micro-USB cable to try to charge another manufacturer's device, and the device would maybe say "charger not compatible" or it would not charge the device, or something it would make the device shut off or worse.  So if you can, stick to the original manufacturer's charger (but also don't freak out about this because most chargers aren't going to create a problem, this is rather rare.)

### Post by PondPuppy on 2014-12-04
Great site, help_me2! Apparently, with lithium-ion batteries, recharging well before the battery is depleted can prolong life. A table on the site indicates that a lithium-ion battery which is allowed to discharge completely before recharging will deliver only 300 to 500 cycles before its ability to hold a reasonable charge is compromised, while a battery which is recharged when it is only 25% discharged will deliver 2000 to 2500 cycles.
