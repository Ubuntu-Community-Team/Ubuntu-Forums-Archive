### Post by handy on 2007-08-01
I hurt myself badly 24 years ago, after a few years of recovery, I thought, what can I learn that I can never know everything about, which won't be hard on my body?  

As I had been a turf farm operations manager, & could never work like that again,  I thought I'd have a look at computers, which I really knew absolutely nothing about.  After some research I chose an Amiga over a Mac, & found that the thing that interested me most of all was trying to understand how these things work?

Cutting a long story short, I ended up writing software reviews & AmigaDos tutorials for Megadisc an Australian magazine on disk.  Commodore killed the Amiga, I moved on to windows, & was shocked at the mess it was; continuing with my obsession of having to teach myself about OS's, I diversified taking on the hardware side too, which I found was much easier than the various windows, or any OS for that matter.

I ended up as a sole trader, self employed computer tech', with some good business customers & many private ones too.  10 years of mostly dealing with other peoples windows problems was all I could take.  

I've been retired for nearly 2 years, doing what I like on my computers, usually between 12 & 16 hours a day.  My body is not capable of doing too much physical activity, so computers have been perfect for me, I get huge amounts of mental stimulation from the various forums on the web, I also enjoy playing around with different Linux distro's with occasionally a different OS thrown in for good measure.  Oh, & of course there is Guild Wars. ;-)

These days I don't have the drive I once had, which would have turned me into a Linux guru, I know bugger all about Linux really, just enough to get myself in & out of trouble; it's interesting how we change as the years go by.  I do find the community very comfortable to be a part of though, for that I thank you all. :-D

So, I'm not proud to be anything, though, I am very happy to be a geek, a nerd & a dork, I won't try & work out the percentages though, as I find it near impossible to be objective about my subjective! :)

A really interesting thing about this little story, I find, is that prior to my accident, I *lived* outdoors, I would happily work in the rain, if anyone had told me I was going to turn into a computer geek, I would have laughed myself silly & thought they were mad!

You never know what's around the corner, so make the most of every moment I say. :KS
