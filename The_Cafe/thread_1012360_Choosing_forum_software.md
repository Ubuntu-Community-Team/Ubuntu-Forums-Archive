### Post by Vadi on 2008-12-16
> **Vadi said:**
> 

The following are out of the race currently:

vBulletin
phpbb
simple machines
bbpress

vBulletin: Looks rather horrid, and the only good implementation of this forum I ever saw was these. I don't have the time to customize the forums so much to get them to this state. 

phpbb: Same really, except the thing has so many spambot issues it's not even funny. I don't feel like forcing my visitors to post in a topic either. Same goes for the look - the "styles" they have are just very basic skinnings, the layout and everything remains in place - so I'm assuming altering that would be a pita and break when there is a forum upgrade as it's the core code. It's also unable to do proper RSS apparently.

smf: I've seem them at use @ [http://forum.ubuntu.ru/](http://forum.ubuntu.ru/), wasn't impressed. I don't like their license either.

bbpress: This is what I went with originally for the forum. However, bbpress is just too minimal. By default, you barely have any markup, no editing buttons, no PM's, no avatars except for gravatar (who's going to register on a 3rd party site just to be able to upload an avatar? Nobody did.). No image uploading either, no, no, no... the forums turned out to be way too minimal. Yes, there are plugins - however they are community-made, half of the time don't work as needed, and some are plain poorly made. bbPress just isn't the same quality as Wordpress right now.

Vanilla is looking pretty good currently, and thanks for the list, I'll check them all out.
