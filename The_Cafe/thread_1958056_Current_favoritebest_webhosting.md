### Post by sandyd on 2012-04-14
[Stablehost]("http://www.stablehost.com/shared-hosting.php") and/or [MDDHosting]("http://www.mddhosting.com/")
Used to host with them (in fact, all of my sites were), before I shifted all my sites to dedicated.

Though they both are smaller companies, they have over-the-top support, and help beyond what a normal host would help with. (Stablehost uses Litespeed over apache, and I wanted caching, so they messed with APC. When that fried Litespeed, they got me an xcache plugin that I could put into my php.ini)

The most useful thing ive found is that you can customize your own php.ini (i.e load your own php modules).

Oh, and if your looking for high performance, stay away from 'unlimited' shared hosts. 
If your lucky, you get a box with good people. 
If your not, you are out of luck and get stuck with abusers, and will likely experience problems such as huge slowdowns or inaccessablilty. (i.e. [http://openubuntu.com/index.php/topic,273.0.html](http://openubuntu.com/index.php/topic,273.0.html))

P.S. If you want nice reviews, check out the [offers]("http://www.webhostingtalk.com/forumdisplay.php?f=4") and [reviews]("http://www.webhostingtalk.com/forumdisplay.php?f=1") section of [http://webhostingtalk.com](http://webhostingtalk.com) . Lots of legit reviews and discussions there
