### Post by 23meg on 2009-11-15
> **RiceMonster said:**
> Just because it's free doesn't mean it can't be criticized.

I agree with that fully. But this thread doesn't seem to be about the increasingly rare phenomenon called "criticism".

Moaning, bashing, making snarky comments to stir debate, perpetuating learned myths about people or projects one doesn't side with, complaining to get attention to one's problems, nowadays seem pass as "criticism", but are actually not.

People are welcome to use Ubuntu for free, that is, free of both monetary cost, and of the cost of contribution in terms of time and energy (in whatever form, including the simplest such as helping fellow users), but those who do are *not* welcome to detract from the community by introducing the social cost of actions in the above paragraph. Whenever they do, people who don't use Ubuntu for free in that sense have every right to take issue with that.
