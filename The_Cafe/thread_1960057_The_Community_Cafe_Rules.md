### Post by winh8r on 2012-04-17
Religion and politics are a personal matter and should not be forced on anyone **anywhere**.

With regard to the Cafe, there could be some benefit in less moderation where a topic is generating lively debate. Members who are likely to be offended when directly challenged in a debate should think before getting involved in a heated debate in the first place. Members are responsible for there own actions when deciding whether or not to get involved in a debate.

There is a world of difference between moderation and policing.

Obviously the forum needs to stick to the image that Canonical dictates if it is to remain affiliated. However it would be sad to see the forum turn into a "quick answer" shop like AskUbuntu, where people post a question, get an answer and the post is then locked. With no feedback, no sense of community and quite often rather overly technical answers given to both beginners and more experienced users alike who often would prefer  it to be broken down into understandable chunks

Feedback should be welcomed everywhere both positive and negative, and members who discuss and debate such feedback are generally able to conduct themselves in an appropriate manner.

Maybe a little trial period could be implemented where posts are unmoderated. provided that the post is generally in keeping with the flow of the thread, and is an opinion rather than a personal attack aimed at an individual.Regardless of subject matter, although anyone who resorts to using political and religious references in a discussion about Apple being an elitist brand, really should not be getting involved in the first place.



If someone does post something which receives complaints from other members then they are given a chance to edit it and make amends, or the post is simply removed. People will soon get into the way of it.


The obvious alternative is that things remain as they are in order to prevent , as has been discussed, mayhem and extra work for the moderators and staff. In which case it might be an idea to implement an open warning system where offending posters are publicly warned in the thread about their posting style / content and therefore everyone else sees where the boundary is being drawn by the moderator.

All of that said, as KiwiNZ has pointed out in the past , this is not an open forum, it is a moderated forum, and members have agreed to the terms when joining, so anyone who feels that their freedoms are being restricted has many other options , there is no padlock on the exit here.

As is often said on this forum with regard to many things:

"Use what works for you" 

 and if that happens not to be the Ubuntu Forum then so be it
