### Post by akast on 2006-11-01
I am Anton Kast, the guy who maintains LightZone for Linux at [http://sonic.net/~rat/lightcrafts](http://sonic.net/~rat/lightcrafts).  There's an active email list for this project at [http://kast-dev.com/mailman/listinfo/lightzone](http://kast-dev.com/mailman/listinfo/lightzone).

LightZone for Linux is not pirated.  It is an authorized port of LightZone for Mac and Windows, given away for free by Light Crafts Inc., which is the copyright holder.  I work at Light Crafts, and I maintain the linux port on my own time.

The discrepancies you've seen in displayed colors are at least partly be due to the fact that the linux port does not use display color profiles.  On Mac and Windows, you can ask the operating system for the color profile for each display device, and LightZone uses these to correct images displayed on your screens.  On linux, there is no standard for discovering display profiles, and so LightZone just uses a default.
