### Post by perce on 2008-05-22
> **Stochastic said:**
> 

The Nokia 810 (and 800 and 770) look interesting and stable, but will they work as cellphone replacements?  



They are not cellphones: you can't make phone calls with them. You need to connect them either to a wifi network, or to a bluetooth cellphone with an internet subscription.

> 
I think in the end I may attempt to get a regular cell phone that uses linux (if not merely for the ethical reasons)


If you're interested to ethical reasons, you should also check if your future cellphone uses Linux in a GPL compliant way. I read an article that was saying that many Linux mobile devices don't, even if things are improving. You will also want to avoid hardware locked platforms: there is no gain in using Linux, if you can't do what you want with it.
