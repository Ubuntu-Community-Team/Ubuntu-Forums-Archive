### Post by pfeiffep on 2015-02-06
I purchased a new 2013 Toyota Avalon at the beginning of model year 2014. It's the limited edition so it has all the bells and whistles I could ever want. 

The navigation system had incorrect, incomplete, or bad map data loaded. We asked about new map data when the car was new and were told to wait until the next map update. 
Well we just took the car in for service and asked for the map update; we were informed that this is a charged update ($169.00 and a $50 installation fee). 
I agreed to the part charge but challenged the installation. The dealership rose to the occassion and offered free maintenance on the nav system for as long as I owned the car. 

I decided to call Toyota and register my concern about paying for a premium navigation system (~$2000) I should not have to pay for a map upgrade to fix a data issue. Well ...
I worded my concern carefully (also saying how much we really liked the car) and was rewarded with Toyota sending me $200 in Amex checks.
