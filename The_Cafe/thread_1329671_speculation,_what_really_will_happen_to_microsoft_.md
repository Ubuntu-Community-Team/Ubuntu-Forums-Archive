### Post by ZankerH on 2009-11-19
Extrapolating from the Moore-like progress acceleration, the amount of technological advances that will happen between 2030 and 2040 is roughly equal to the progress that happened between the first man-made fire and today. That close to a technological singularity (2045), you can't possibly make an accurate prediction of anything. We're likely to have the technology to upload a human mind to a computing machine by then. We're also likely to develop a strong AI by then. Once that happens, everything we did up to that point becomes irrelevant. What does matter is the way we get there - either through the Free software philosophy, leading to a Free and open future, where the SAI is benevolent and human uploads have human rights, or, through corporate closed-source software, where the SAI works for the sole purpose of benefiting it's corporate overlords, and average humans (and their uploads) are treated inhumanely, with as much rights as any other string of ones and zeroes.

I'm a singularitarian, and I can't stand these kind of topics where people speculate decades into the future without applying the slightest bit of math to extrapolate approximately where our technology will be by then. Predicting 30 years into the future today is like predicting a thousand years into the future in the year 1000 AD. Impossible.
