### Post by Simian Man on 2009-12-01
If you want to narrow it down to two or three, I'd go with Fedora, OpenSuse and Mandriva in that order. ### Post by MisfitI38 on 2009-12-01
> **slumbergod said:**

For example, a lot of Karmic adopters have had wireless and graphics issues. Have the other distros had the same issues or is it the way Ubuntu is engineered?..

The GNU/Linux development model breeds these types of issues. Users of other operating systems must modify their computing habits in order to avoid malware, whereas FOSS users generally try to step up their competence in order to solve system upgrade breakage.

All distros have issues. Some are more annoying than others. 

However, some are inherently higher quality than others. Debian, Slackware, Fedora and Arch are all very high quality distros with active communities, but tend to be a bit more for intermediate-advanced users than Ubuntu. 
If you're looking into a more beginner-oriented distro, you might want to try Mandriva (as was mentioned) or PCLinuxOS, a derivative.
