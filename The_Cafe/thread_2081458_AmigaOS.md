### Post by mips on 2012-11-07
I used it years ago when they still made real Amiga computers. Was an awesome OS at the time and lightyears ahead of anything else available on the market (in conjunction with the hardware). I still think it was the best OS ever created.

AmigaOS as it stands today will only run on a limited hardware platform [http://www.amigaos.net/content/72/supported-hardware](http://www.amigaos.net/content/72/supported-hardware) so if you were thinking about running it on your x86 based hardware forget it. The hardware is all PPC based and very exapensive.

If you wanna get a feel for it you might wanna try one of the alternative projects.

Have a look at at AROS/Icaros Desktop

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AROS_Research_Operating_System](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AROS_Research_Operating_System)
[http://aros.sourceforge.net/](http://aros.sourceforge.net/)
[http://www.icarosdesktop.org/](http://www.icarosdesktop.org/)

I downloaded it last month and it's really cool not to mention lightning fast. When I get some time I'm gonna install it on a spare 20gb laptop drive.

### Post by TenPlus1 on 2012-11-07
I had an Amiga 1200 years back inside a tower case with a PPC card and BVision 8mb graphics that ran really well and was quite fast...

The Os itself is very simple with everything in it's place (C, LIBS, DEVS, Prefs, Utilites) and the Workbench is very easy to use...  It has a single menu bar at the top which gives you Menu's (kinda like Unity top menu) and Memory information...

For more information check out: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Workbench_(AmigaOS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Workbench_(AmigaOS)) and if you wanna know anything specific ask away...

Code written as it should be.  Lean, Mean and VERY keen.
