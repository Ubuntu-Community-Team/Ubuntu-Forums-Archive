### Post by blueturtl on 2007-12-16
If I were you I'd be going with a FireWire webcam instead of a USB one. That's because the USB ones rely on manufacturer dependent protocols to transfer video, none of which are standard. It really is a hit and miss to buy a USB webcam unless it clearly states support for a given operating system right on the box.

FireWire webcams have adequate bandwidth, low CPU utilization and a standard transfer protocol so they can be used in practically any OS without installation of drivers! [[source]]("http://www.litech.org/spook/whichcam.html")

Now you may be ticked off by prices, but it's all about which you value more: your own trouble and time spent vs. money.
