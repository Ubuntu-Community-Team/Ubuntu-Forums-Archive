### Post by Docaltmed on 2012-02-01
I'm not in IT, but I'm the president of a small company, and I have done all the hiring and firing the past 16 years.

If I were looking for an IT guy (I could only wish!), if you had the skillset I needed, I would not be terribly put off by your background, *especially if you were upfront about it.*

Here's why: As you explained it here (and would to me), you screwed up when you were young and dumb, did your time, and you've kept your hands clean ever since. That last part is key, because it tells me that this probably really was youthful stupidity. But if you *don't* mention it, and hope I won't find out about it, you're wrong and you won't even make it past the doorway.

Second, the type of crime tells me that you're enterprising and clever, but not too clever or you wouldn't have gotten caught. I like those traits in an employee. (The enterprising and clever parts, not the stupid).

Finally -- and this is critical -- I know, because of your background and inexperience, that I can hire you for substantially less than your competition. For a small businessman, that makes all the difference in the world. Yeah, I'm taking advantage of your long-ago history. Tell me I'm not already the 700th person to do that to you.

But, if you work out, and work well, I would probably bring you up to par at some point to keep you.

Hope this helps.

It even allows a person to respond to police, courts, prospective employers, upon any questioning about convictions, that one does not, have any.

There are few caveats, but only a few. You can guess which crimes they may be, I am sure. 

I suggest you check this out.
