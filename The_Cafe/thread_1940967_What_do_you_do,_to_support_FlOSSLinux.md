### Post by winh8r on 2012-03-14
I try to make financial contributions to projects that are useful to me when I can, and also try to help out the smaller developers with little things like improving translation, spelling, typos etc in their programs, all the little things that get overlooked when they are busy working on patches and new releases.

Also spreading the word about open source is a valuable thing too, there are people in the world who still believe that unless you pay money for it , it is not the "real" thing!

I like your way of thinking though, contributing what you would have spent on mcrosoft products to the open source projects.

I usually say to people , even if you can only afford to contribute a couple of dollars/pounds/euros , it all counts, especially to the really small developers who are often the only member on the project "team"

A few dollars a month can mean the difference between being able to pay for hosting or shelving the project.

### Post by Paqman on 2012-03-14
I track down all the newbies on the forum and reply "rtfm" to all their posts. In this way I keep traditional Linux values alive.

Seriously though, I don't do much. I try to help out here and on places like askubuntu.com, I try to make time to install the testing branch and file some bugs, and I've written some scripts to help other Linux users out. Big up the people who do more, there are some really dedicated folks out there keeping the big machine running.
