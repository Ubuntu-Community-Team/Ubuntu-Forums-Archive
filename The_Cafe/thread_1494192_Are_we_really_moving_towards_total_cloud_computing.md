### Post by chessnerd on 2010-05-26
I don't think so. I think the world will come to a balance closer to cloud computing than we are now, but I think that we won't go "total cloud" for three reasons:

1. The cost of portable storage is dropping rapidly. You can now get an 8 GB flash drive for $15. That's a good amount of mobile storage for less than some online services cost. This time next year, expect similar prices for 16 GB models. Sure, flash drives can get busted or lost, but you might also go somewhere with no Internet connection (like on a plane) and be without your files.

2. It will never be as fast as local computing. The Internet will never reach a speed that will be faster than the speed of writing to/reading from a hard drive. Uploading a 3 GB file takes hours on the average Internet connection, downloading is faster, but not by enough. Storing and reading to the hard drive can be done in minutes.

3. People will always hate Big Brother. There will always be those who never use online services for personal files because you can't trust that Google, Microsoft, or any of those companies will keep all of your information private. These people will never go for total cloud computing.
