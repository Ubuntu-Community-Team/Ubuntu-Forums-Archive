### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2010-09-16
What is wrong with journalists these days? No, the study didn't say 29% of US Internet users are okay using the Internet while having sex.

Ars communicates this properly, as they do:

> What the companies found was that 29 percent of Americans are OK with being "plugged in" while honeymooning (a descriptor that other news outlets seem to interpret as "all sex, all the time"), while six percent say it's OK during a wedding. Eight percent are cool with checking the Internet during a religious service&#8212;hey, gotta set those fantasy football lineups before the benediction&#8212;while 41 percent think it's acceptable while having dinner with family and friends.
[http://arstechnica.com/gadgets/news/2010/09/internet-proves-too-irresistible-during-weddings-church.ars](http://arstechnica.com/gadgets/news/2010/09/internet-proves-too-irresistible-during-weddings-church.ars)

Even described without a stupid headline, though, that study strikes me as entirely pointless.

*&#8220;Study finds that 99% of US Telephone users are okay making phone calls while working! 80% use telephones while using the Internet!&#8221;*

In other words, the Internet is a dumb pipe that feeds all manner of things. Studying when people use &#8220;the Internet&#8221; is completely vague and unnecessary.
What may be useful is studying when people use various services, like social networking sites or email. In the latter case, for example, it's quite possible for one to read / respond to an email message without presently using the Internet. So, I'm responding to an email while sitting in a bus, but I'm not &#8220;using the Internet.&#8221;
