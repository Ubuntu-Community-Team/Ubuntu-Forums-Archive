***WARNING:** shameless plug ahead!*

I've been working on a browser-based RPG in my free time for several months now. It's definitely in a *playable* state right now, but it's quite unfinished. There's quite a bit of development yet to do, but registration is open (and of course free). I'd love for people to go ahead and check it out and provide some feedback. 

[Pioneers of Aethora (http://aethora.com/)]("http://aethora.com/")

From the [About Page (http://docs.aethora.com/about)]("http://docs.aethora.com/about"):
[INDENT]"Pioneers of Aethora is a web-based turn-based strategy role-playing game. Feel free to call it a lame attempt at a rip-off of Final Fantasy Tactics, Might and Magic, Battle for Wesnoth, etc, etc. Well, those are some influences, anyway. Also, it&#8217;s web-based, so &#8230; there."

"I like the idea of a game that you can pop into for a few minutes, play a little, and walk away from at any time. The combat system in Pioneers of Aethora is completely turn-based, and because of that, you can walk away from the game in the middle of a battle, go do something else for a while, walk up to another computer, log in, and pick up where you left off."[/INDENT]

What works:
- character/party creation
- combat (pretty much fully working)

Experimentally Working:
- player-based market 
- missions/quests (although I still need to write more content, the system is in place)

Planned:
- player crafting/labor/etc
- player-to-player communication/collaboration

(more details here: [http://docs.aethora.com/about/future-features](http://docs.aethora.com/about/future-features) )
