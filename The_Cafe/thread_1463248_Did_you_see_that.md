### Post by jetsam on 2010-05-01
Yeah I'm all for bringing the other OS forum back, and would support such an effort by publicly posting on the forum that I'm all for bringing the other OS forum back.

I think it went away as a means of limiting the attractiveness of the forum in some ways because the servers were running into capacity problems, but I think the forums lost in a lot of ways when it shut down.  Open source in general gains by spreading knowledge around, not by encapsulating it into camps...  People that have tried more than one flavor of Linux are usually better able to address support issues, so it does benefit the forums in pretty direct ways as well.  You can learn more about Ubuntu by installing Debian, or Slackware, or Arch, or Gentoo, or Puppy, or Fedora, or that one in the 97th place on Distrowatch-- once, than you would learn by clean installing Ubuntu for the 9th time.

Also, good ideas come from nostalgia.  I never had an Amiga, but the die hard Amiga remember-ers are something else in the potency of their yearning...
