### Post by tslawinski on 2008-12-14
> Cyan Worlds has decided to release all of Myst Online URU Live's assets to the public as open source. This includes the client and server architecture. Cyan will also hold one Myst Online server shard open for players to have a centralized world, while others can now freely put up their own Myst Online servers.

This will be an interesting experiment to see if a development community forms around the game. Unlike many open source projects that solve developer problems (and therefore have a direct audience) a game casts a broader net to a non-developer community. I also wonder about the operational aspects of a online game--like how many people actually know how to build and maintain the servers?

The URU Live game itself has seen some tough times, having been dropped by Ubisoft and GameTap until Cyan got the IP back. Now Cyan is throwing it over the fence to see if an open source approach makes sense.

I'm not aware of any other efforts to open source an EOL game--let's hope for the best.
