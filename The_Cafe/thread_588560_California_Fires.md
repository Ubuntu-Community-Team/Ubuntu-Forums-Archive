### Post by NT4usB on 2007-10-23
> **bikeboy said:**
> Is it normal to have bushfires this late in the year over there? We're heading into severe bushfire season over here now. I wouldn't think you would often see high danger in Northern and Southern hemispheres at the same time.

This time every year.
350 days of the year we have an onshore flow with heavy moist air.
In the fall, storms start brewing offshore and the cold air/water pushes down from the north sucking what's left of the summer down off the mountains in what we refer to as the 'Santa Ana' winds.
The air compresses and heats up as it roars into the basin.
Single digit humidity, 60 mph (100kph) winds and 80-90°  temperatures (36° C) combined with thousands of square miles of dry brush. One small spark or careless cigarette and the fire season is here again.

### Post by bikeboy on 2007-10-23
Thanks for the explanations guys. In (most of) Australia we see most of the fires from about November through February, that's the last month of Spring through to the end of Summer. The Summer air is always dry in the South East, so we're in particular trouble if we get good Spring rains that cause growth to accelerate.

We haven't had much Spring rain this year (or for about 7-8 years), but as you said a cigarette or even lightening strike can easily trigger a major fire, especially when it's ~35-40°C for a week. Last year there were a couple of really big fires active for about a month, the smoke was thick around here for about 3 weeks despite the fire being about 45 mins from where I live.

I know what it's like, friends lost houses in local fires when I was at school, so best wishes to all of Calif., hopefully fortunes will change really soon.
