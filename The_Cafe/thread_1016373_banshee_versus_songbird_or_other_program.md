### Post by igknighted on 2008-12-19
If you want to play DRM'd music which you purchased from itunes, you will need to strip the DRM somehow.  Most common method (lossy) is to burn the songs to a CD and rip them non-drm.  Any app can then replay these.  There is no way to play DRM'd itunes music on linux.

The only way to purchase from itunes on linux is to use an old itunes version via wine.  If you really want to buy songs, your best bet is to use amazon.  They sell drm-free music, which you can then put on your ipod with any app you choose.

Which ipod do you use?  Many new ones are as yet unsupported on linux, so be careful there as well.  Apple is not a friend of open computing, to say the least.

### Post by Zeedok on 2008-12-20
But to answer the original poster's question . . . I'd like to hear what people think of Songbird vs Banshee, specifically with reference to iPods and podcasts.

I have an old 5G iPod which fortunately is supported by native Linux apps and I have used almost all of the programs (Banshee, Songbird, GTKpod, Amarok and GPodder for podcasts) to get music (including podcasts) on and off my device.  They all have their benefits and drawbacks and I'm still searching for the app that meets all of my needs.

I don't really use the "media player" functionality of these programs much - I try and keep all of my media on my iPod rather than on my laptop - so the iPod/media device management functionality is really important to me.  Banshee works pretty well with older iPods, but still has a few irritations (eg you can't yet manually edit the album artwork and "podcasts' do not get added to the "Podcasts" menu on the device).  Songbird on the other hand does allow you to edit the album art, but I'm not a fan of their take on podcasts (I get it they want to "Play the web" but I still like having a feed that shows when new content is available).  Also, Songbird is multiplatform (which is a plus in some ways) but IMHO doesn't look quite as nice in the gnome environment.  I'm happy also to give away the "iTunes" look that ships by default with songbird.

What I really hope is that with Android getting some momentum and SD cards and other flash memory formats getting larger in capacity, we end up with half a dozen open source options from awesome mainstream phone (maybe media device as well??) manufacturers (Sony Ericcson Phones look nice:p) with large removable storage and an attractive modern touch interface and web browsing.  Being open source the linux support will be really easy to implement (they have **already** added G1 support to Banshee). 

> A disclaimer - while I agree that apple is anti-open (does that mean closed??) source, I like their devices better than what else is available and I also like the iPod touch interface.  My music library is 30+GB and I can't think of and media players that ship with that much memory anymore apart from the iPod classic and the Zune (I am **_not_** buying a microsoft product, plus it's linux support is even worse from what I have read).  I like being 'alternative', I'm an Ubuntu user afterall, but sometimes popular products are popular for a reason (??).
