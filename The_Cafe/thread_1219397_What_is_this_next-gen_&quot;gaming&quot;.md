It highlighted the fact that with the gray looking colors you can get more detail into the game without having to go into too much lighting stuff.  Has to do with the (I forget the technical term) light that bounces off of other things.  For example, if you have something bright red in real life, it will reflect a reddish light onto other things around it.  To get that same kind of effect in games would be very time consuming and i think most PC's and consoles arent really up to the task.  So what happens is that the more vibrant the colors in your game, the less real it looks.  The more greyscale stuff they use, the less (ambient?) light needs to be reflected off of objects and therefore the whole scene looks more real.
