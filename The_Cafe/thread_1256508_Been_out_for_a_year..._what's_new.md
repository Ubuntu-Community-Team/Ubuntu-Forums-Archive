### Post by akiratheoni on 2009-09-02
Due to some issues with Intrepid and the like, I stopped using Ubuntu for about a year. I had installed Jaunty around April but when I encountered the same problem as I did with Intrepid, I stopped using Ubuntu again.

Now that I'm in college, I figured that the downtime between classes could be used to fix Ubuntu. Well, I booted into Ubuntu a couple days ago and voila! Mostly everything that had been problematic was fixed. Keep in mind that I didn't update Ubuntu at all, and it just magically fixed itself.

I used to be really into Ubuntu before I phased out of it; I even used my big English project to write about free software, and I got the highest grade out of all the people I know. 

But obviously things change quickly in the open source world. What are some the biggest improvements in the past year? I want to catch up, and reading a years' worth of articles on Digg isn't my cup of tea.

I know that VirtualBox added basic support for DirectX 8 and 9, so that's a big leap for virtualization. Also, I installed Amarok and to my delight, saw that it was version 2, instead of the previous 1.4 or whatever release came with Hardy. What else?

Thanks guys.
