### Post by DoctorMO on 2007-04-30
No the DVD specification has not changed there ARE a couple of variations on the DVD specification all of which are not always supported on all devices:

DVD-ROM Single Layer, Single Sided - Classic DVD (normaly Data) 4GB
DVD-ROM Double Layer, Single Sided - Most Video DVDs 8GB
DVD-ROM Single Layer, Double Sided - Unusual, older dvd videos (such as Contact)
DVD-ROM Double Later, Double Sided - Most uncommon, 16GB of Data
DVD-RAM - A DVD set inside a case, completely different media
DVD-RW - A Rewritable DVD
DVD-R - A Blank DVD-ROM Disk (WORM)

Now if your talking about video DVDs then the formats haven't changed much, still mpeg2 video and aac sound with VOB menu formats normally encoded with CSS encryption and stored on DVD-ROM Double Layer, Single Sided media.

It's possible that you just have an unforgiving DVD player or one that is breaking.

Yeah, if you're not talking about video DVD's then there may be a problem.  There are 2 different types of DVD-R's and DVD-RW's.  You can either get DVD +R/RW or DVD -R/RW.  Some of the older DVD drives only supported + or - but not both.  The newer ones support both.  If that is the case, there's nothing you can do, but buy the right blank DVD's or get a new drive.  But if you make a video DVD and finalize it, it should work in any drive/player no matter what type it originally was.
