### Post by Valiante on 2007-04-02
**Keep Music Alive**
 
The government has introduced new legislation which forces all live entertainment venues to require a licence, imposing all sorts of restrictions on the music industry which could eventually kill it off altogether.
 
*The recently introduced changes in licensing law have produced an environment where music and dance, activities which should be valued and promoted in a civilised society, are instead damaged by inappropriate regulation. We call on the Prime Minister to recognise this situation and take steps to correct it.*
 
See [**_[COLOR="Blue"]my website[/COLOR]_**](http://valiante.co.uk/keepmusicalive) for more information.
 
[**_[COLOR="blue"]Click here[/COLOR]_**](http://petitions.pm.gov.uk/licensing) to sign the e-petition now.
 
Please take a few minutes to consider this issue - it may affect you more than you first realise.
 
Also please forward this on to anyone you can think of.  Not wanting to state the obvious, the more signatures we can get on this petition, the greater its chances of success.
 
Thanks for your time,
 
Marc.

### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-04-03
Even though I can understand the idea of 'noise pollution' or whatever (eg, you wouldn't hold an open air heavy metal concert right next door to an old people's home), the fact is, that kind of thing just doesn't happen anyway. This government absolutely refuses to trust people to make rational judgements. By forbidding spontaneous performances, or even planned performances at unusual locations, they're taking away our human rights of expression. Yes, 'noise pollution' can annoy people - particularly if they don't like that particular noise, or the band sucks, or whatever, but to force the venue management / owner to pay for a licence to hold performances is just disgusting.

Anyway, I'll sign, even though I don't think one of these petitions has had any impact at all on the government.
