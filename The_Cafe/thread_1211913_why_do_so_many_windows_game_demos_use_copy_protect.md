### Post by zugu on 2009-07-13
> **Gizenshya said:**
> BioShock, Steam... very good copy protection.  Yet promptly cracked.I beg to differ. Bioshock cracks were very unreliable (crashes, freezes) and a lot of time was needed to engineer a proper crack.

Steam protection hasn't actually been cracked, some groups just spent a lot of time to remove the tight Steam integration from the games (i.e. Team Fortress 2), but the result was crippled games, missing a lot of features. Steam is a distribution platform and a service at the same time. People get certified, secure servers (VAC), a place to download their purchased games from, a community, Steam Cloud etc.. So no, Steam hasn't actually been cracked to this day, only some games had the Steam protection removed. Steam being cracked is like World of Warcraft being cracked. You can't crack WoW, but you can try (and fail) to perfectly emulate Blizzard's servers.

Of course, any decent player can tell you that in the case of WoW and Steam you get what you pay for. I would gladly pay money for a game that I like, to support the devs and to have access to value added services, not only to the feature-complete games.

Some game demos for Windows include copy protection because the developer decides the demo should be playable for some limited amount of time, such as one hour or one day. The DRM makes sure you're getting kicked out of the game after the timelimit has expired. I guess it's easier and cheaper for game developers to provide the whole game and strap some DRM on it when people ask for a demo. This way, the developer doesn't spend time and money on removing levels, textures, sounds and other data from the game. It's also easier for the player to buy the game: just insert the key and voila, the whole game is available - no download required.
