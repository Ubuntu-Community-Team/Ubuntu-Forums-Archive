I have a Fujitsu P1610 that is giving me major problems. It crackles and pops when you move it (kind of sounds like its coming from speaker) and constantly freezes. When I press down on the eraser style mouse or if I put too much pressure on the keyboard it starts crackling. Usually, it freezes as the live cd loads and if I'm lucky enough to get into the live cd, it will freeze during the installation. Sometimes, after a lockup, when I restart the machine the monitor is completely black but the lights on the unit indicate CPU and hard drive activity!

My dad has one of these so I tried swapping in his RAM and hard drive but still had the same problems.

I opened it up once and could not find anything wrong. Nothing seemed loose.

Is something shorting out? Am I going to need a new motherboard or something?

### Post by theraje on 2010-08-04
Sounds like something may be up with the power... most likely the grounding. Of course, it's hard to tell without actually seeing it in person, but with the steps you've taken that would be my first suspicion. If you have a similar power cable, try hooking that into it and try it then. 

If it still does it (or does it on battery power) then it's got to be something in the laptop itself. Try making sure the speaker wires are all seated properly and the solder joints haven't become damaged, etc.

By the way, do you have any idea what may have caused the laptop to start behaving like it has been?
