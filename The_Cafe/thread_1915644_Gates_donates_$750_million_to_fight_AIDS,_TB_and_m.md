### Post by Mikeb85 on 2012-01-27
> **Ozor Mox said:**
> Perhaps bouncingwilf is talking about how it is very difficult to buy a computer (especially a laptop) without paying for a Microsoft product, even if unwanted. Just a thought.

I don't know about all companies, but Lenovo gives the option on just about every PC they sell on their website to be shipped without Windows.  

Besides, if Windows comes with a PC, it's at a very discounted rate, so you're not paying anywhere close to retail price.  The ONLY people who pay full price for Windows are those who buy it off the shelf.  

Speaking of philanthropy, Bill Gates has also made it a policy to overlook piracy in the 3rd world, and allows other countries (China for instance) to buy licenses for merely a few dollars each.  Now, while some may say this is to increase their monopoly, many other corporations would not allow such a thing.  

And Mr. Gates is also not being 'forced' to donate money, the tax rate on capital gains is not that high, and the tax break for donations doesn't exempt you from paying capital gains except on the shares themselves being donated.  

Finally, how hard is it to believe that someone can separate their personal philosophy from their corporate one?  Whatever we make of Microsoft's practices, Bill Gates has been very charitable, and seems like a decent enough guy.
