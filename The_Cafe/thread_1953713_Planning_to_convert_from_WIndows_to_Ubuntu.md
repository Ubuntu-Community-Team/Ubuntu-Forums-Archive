### Post by jockyburns on 2012-04-07
I "Converted " from Win XP about 7 months ago. Someone said there would be a steep learning curve involved. So far, I'm not actually sure what steep learning I've done. Apart from the close window button on Google Chrome being on the opposite side from XP, the shutdown button being elsewhere and the user panel being on the left hand side of the screen, there's practically no difference. Someone asked me what I look for when I buy a car. I told them, as long as it starts when I turn the key, what else should I look for? 
Same with a computer OS, just as long as it does what it says on the tin, I'm happy. Win XP did have it's problems (most of them being malware and viruses). With Ubuntu (touchwood) I have no such worries. Ubuntu has worked for me. The only downside being that I can't now play my favourite game Eve Online. (don't get me started on Wine though, I had real problems getting it to even load in Wine and when it eventually did, it was so slow, a noob could have taken out a battle cruiser) ;) ;)
