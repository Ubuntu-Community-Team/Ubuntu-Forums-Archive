### Post by luceerose on 2010-06-04
I saw a couple of threads over in the Maverick Meerkat section yesterday about gnome-shell 3,
-mainly this thread- [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1476241](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1476241)
, so I decided to try it. As such I have decided to start an opinion thread on it here.

I installed gnome-shell3 on Debian Sid the easy way by installing it through Synaptic.
I don't know how much more will be added to it by the September release, but at the moment I'm not finding the interface greatly accessible.
To me, the most disagreeable thing was having to cycle through my tremendously long list of applications in one alphabetical menu. I'd much rather have things categorized like the current [Applications, Places, System] menu.
The Favorites is pretty good and in a decent place, but with the Applications menu so non-categorized, I think the Favorites would fill up all too quickly.

The only thing I found easy about the new gnome-shell interface was how very simple it is to add new Desktops. (Just hit the plus button).
A functionality that's altogether useless to me without a pretty little cube attached :wink:

I'm just glad gnome-shell3 isn't going to come standard with Meerkat. And I sincerely hope this isn't the direction the standard gnome releases are heading in. I love the current 2.x versions of gnome, mainly because I can put things wherever I want. On the other hand, gnome-shell-3 has almost no configurability.

I'm interested to know everyone else's opinions.
Include which distro you tried it on, which method of install you went with & what you liked & disliked.

### Post by yossell on 2010-06-04
Gnome-shell? I quite like it, though I've probably not played with it enough. It works pretty well on both my laptop and desktop, and I like the way it represents workspaces. I find it quite elegant and easy to work with - there could be some tweaking on the top bar appearance, but that's all. 

However - it's a pity it doesn't seem to work with compiz and I don't feel it offers a great number of advantages over traditional ways of working. And I tend to prefer lightweight systems - Openbox - with my own shortcut keys, so I'm not sure I'm the right target market. 

It hasn't yet made me rethink how I interact with my computer, and I'm not sure why people would *want* to change, but I certainly don't hate it and I like the fact that people are trying to develop something new and different.
