### Post by mcduck on 2016-10-15
That's about 10 years old hardware, so anything like i3/5/7 wouldn't even be an option. You'll have hard time finding anything else than possibly some used processors.

Anyway, assuming you find something that the motherboard can deal with, it's probably going to be pretty cheap by now, so just go for the best it can handle. Try finding a Core 2 Duo. E8400 would work, if you enable the overclocking mode on the motherboard. 

...and since you are limited to used processors, there's no way to tell if whatever you can buy comes with a heat sink or not. Depends on what you can find.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2016-10-15
No doubt there's someone selling bare Intel processors, but everyone I've see and/or purchased has a fan.  CPU's need fans for temperature control.

To start your search, check your motherboard specifications to see what kind of CPU is compatible with it.  That will narrow things down considerably. 

Performance improvements can also come from using a better video card, replacing a slow hard drive, or adding additional RAM to a system with minimal RAM, e.g., less than 4 gigs.  For example, if a system is swapping frequently because not enough RAM is available, adding a faster CPU will not impact that. Video cards do not need to be expensive.  Cutting edge cards aimed at gamers are expensive, but many cards at lower prices could handle your requirements.
