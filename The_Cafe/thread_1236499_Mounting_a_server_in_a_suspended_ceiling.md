### Post by hambone79 on 2009-08-10
I know this is a crazy idea, but has anyone every heard of mounting a server in a suspended ceiling? Let me explain why I'm asking this...

I have a home office and two rack mount servers. The servers are currently sitting next to my desk, but I would like to put them somewhere else since they generate alot of heat. I have looked at several options and as of right now, I only have three places I could possibly store them:

1. In the garage.
2. In the utility closet.
3. In the suspended ceiling in the office.

The problem with the garage is that it stays fairly hot and dusty so I'm worried about the servers getting damaged. The utility closet would make an ideal data closet except for the fact that there is a hot water heater sitting in there. Speaking from experience at my parent's house, blown hot water heaters can throw water everywhere so storing a server next to one is a bad idea.

The suspended ceiling seems ideal because it is hidden from view, it would require a shorter run of cables to all of my other equipment, and the suspended ceiling should help keep the heat out of my office. It is also free of any plumbing that could bust and spray water all over the servers.

I have an 8U rack and it fits up into the ceiling very nice. I figure all I need is a few lag bolts to hold it in place and an extra power outlet run into the ceiling.

Any thoughts?

### Post by tgalati4 on 2009-08-10
As an installer, I know it's against code in most jurisdictions.  If your server catches fire, then it will immediately spread to the roof without notice.  The ceiling tiles help slow the spread of the fire by keeping the gases contained in the immediate space.

You can buy a small wall-hung equipment rack and place your server in there, or on top of a bookshelf.  Be sure to leave 18" of clearance above the server and the ceiling.

Since you don't need a display (headless server), you could place it in a closet.  It's a good idea to install vents in the door at the top and bottom.  I would also put a smoke alarm nearby.
