### Post by CraigPaleo on 2010-10-22
I was barefoot as often as I could be when I was a child. My feet were so tough I could run, full speed, on gravel. 

Heal-toe running doesn't absorb any shock but I think the tendency is not to run that way when barefoot. 

Running on a soft, sandy beach will really work your calves. Also, I live on a golf course so when all the golfers are off I'll run on the grass. it's very refreshing. I don't do it nearly as often as I should.
