### Post by igknighted on 2007-05-01

> They say is costs thousands to create decent looking fonts and this why Fonts in Linux look different to those on Mac and Windows. I know some people say that fonts in Ubuntu look just as good but I can never make them as good know matter how much configuration I do.

Why has this not been done, or is it more complicated than this?

Because windows/mac come tweaked for high-end systems.  In ubuntu, it is set for lower end systems and to enable fancy features like anti-aliased fonts you must turn them on in gconf-editor (or the kde control centre or whatever the xfce equivalent is)

Also, try DejaVu Sans, its like the standard font but renders better.

EDIT: If you have an nvidia card you can also turn up anti-aliasing on your desktop with the nvclock program.
