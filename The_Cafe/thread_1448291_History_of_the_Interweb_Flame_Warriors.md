But if I had to write my own flame warrior, mine would be something like.

> 
[B]
 [SIZE=5]Troll Hunter[/SIZE][/B]

[IMG]http://policelink.monster.com/nfs/policelink/attachment_images/0050/4731/Troll_Hunter.jpg[/IMG]

Troll hunter hates trolls, like a Ninja Assassin he doesn't post a great deal preferring to conserve his strength for important battles. He does not make an appearance in warrior mode on many forums, preferring to lurk in the shadows, or occasionally posting low key messages on various parts of the forum. However one thing you can be sure of is should a troll present itself, Troll Hunter will attack with all the stealth and speed of a black panther on a moonless night. He delights not in being provoked or feeding trolls, but in winding trolls up so that they are banned. So for example Troll Hunter will not flame, resort to ad hominem or respond to anything except to make the troll look ridiculous and his arsenal of weapons is prodigious; experienced, deadly and seldom bested by any but the cleverest most pernicious troll. Should the Troll Hunter himself be banned, he will return better armed next time to continue the fight against insidious parasite that are forum trolls. For the Troll Hunter the price of ignorant trolling is death or perma bans. Troll hunters antithesis is obviously Troller although he has little or no interest in most other denizens, warrior or not. He is usually unable to be coaxed into battle unless his prowess or skills as a hunter of human flotsam is challenged.Thought it'd make an interesting thread for my first one. Those who don't feel like exercising their creative juices feel free to post anything of interest about the history of the web and the evolution of it therein.

I've been on internet forums for 8 years now, and they never cease to amaze me, whether good or bad, if that concept means anything to a free change of ideas and a subjective medium.
