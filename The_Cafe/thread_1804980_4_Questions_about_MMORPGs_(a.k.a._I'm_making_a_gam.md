### Post by Dangertux on 2011-07-15
Well I haven't played many different mmos but I played WoW for a couple of years. 

The biggest improvements I would have liked to see in that game are as follows. 

- realistic quests/missions : For example if I have to travel all over the world gathering rare items fighting dangerous opponents the reward better not be 10 gold pieces. Make rewards relevant to quest difficulty. This wasnt so bad at low levels, but at high levels it was noteable. 

- leveling : IMO fewer levels with more time in between is more fun. So long as the quests aren't too repetitive to the point where it turns into a grind fest. 

- Tie places and characters together. In wow if felt like each time you got to a new zone it was completely oblivious to the rest of the world around it. This led to a very sterile feeling. 

- pvp - if you have it, please balance it. I played 2300+ bracket arena in wow before I quit , it was massively imbalanced. If you're going to try to implement pvp make sure your classes are well balanced

- Crafting : crafting is a great idea in mmos but I think the high level crafting equipment should be on par with high level pve/pvp gear. It never was in WoW however it was also easier to obtain. This was fixed toward the end of BC. I didn't play in wrath or cata so I can't speak for those 

Overall be creative, alot of MMOs seem to copy wow I assume because of its popularity. However, I personally would be more apt to try something new over a wow clone.

Hope this helps. Good luck with your project.

### Post by 8_Bit on 2011-07-16
I've played quite a number of MMOs, both F2P and subscription-based ones.

These are the things I consider important

[LIST]
[*]Auto-route to NPCs. Eden Eternal has this and it's very useful for those long walks back to an NPC to turn in a quest.
[*]An uncluttered, easy to understand, consistent interface.
[*]Creativity. I don't want to play the same game again and again but with a different name. What I mean is, make your MMO stand out somehow. Have a killer feature.
[/LIST]
FlyFF did this by allowing fully-3D flight anywhere in the game, and for quite a while it was the only MMO that had this. (Of course WoW and others eventually caught up). Another example is Rose Online, which has a go-kart system in which you can build, customize, and drive your own go-kart. It's getting harder and harder to have a unique feature like these nowadays, because MMOs constantly copy each other, unfortunately.

And most importantly
-The ability to have FUN. A lot of free-to-play MMOs fail in this regard, because the cash-items end up unbalancing the game dramatically. In order to win in PvP you end up having to shell out $100++ of real-world currency in return for item scrolls and uber weapons. Many of those item scrolls are just to protect your weapon from breaking permanently if you try to upgrade it, and the scroll just disappears after one use, forcing you to keep buying more until your weapon is finally upgraded. In some F2P MMOs it can take hundreds of dollars of scrolls to fully upgrade an equipment set, whereas in a subscription-based MMO the user would have been able to do that for just $15-30 a month...

In F2P MMORPG's, it quickly becomes very boring and discouraging to play for people who are poor and do not purchase from the item store often.

Some companies allow players to complete surveys in order to win cash points. That is a great alternative to make available for your players who can not, for whatever reason, support your business with real-world money.  Another alternative is to include advertisements within the game. It's a win-win situation for you and your players - they get to play for free, and you get to sell advertisements and stay afloat as a business.
