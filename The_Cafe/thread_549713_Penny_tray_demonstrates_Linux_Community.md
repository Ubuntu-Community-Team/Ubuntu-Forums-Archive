### Post by PoHandle on 2007-09-12
Today at work, work being a cashier job at a liquor store, my boredom grew to a point where I had to do something productive to keep myself sane.  An idea popped into my head: I'll make a "take a penny, leave a penny" tray out of a #12 bag and empty cigarette carton.  I assembled it in little time, and thus decided to decorate it.  I labeled it "OpenChange:  To promote Linux and Open Source (take a penny, leave a penny)".  (I did not have in mind the Open Source project for an exchange server while labeling the tray.)  I figured I was killing two birds with one stone, because there was a place to put all the change that customers would leave about the counter, and I would advertise Linux.  I then placed 5 pennies in the tray to get it started.

[IMG]http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v303/PoHandle/0911071542.jpg[/IMG]
(Picture taken at the birth of the tray)

Throughout the day, customers would drop change into the tray or take change out.  This got me thinking "Hey! This tray is like the Linux/Open Source community!  People contribute what they can so that others may benefit."  As my shift progressed, I watched the amount of change grow in the tray.  At the end of the shift, I was astounded at how much more change was in the tray.

[IMG]http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v303/PoHandle/0912072128.jpg[/IMG]

This analogy made me feel great pride for the Linux community.  It appears (from this unlikely association) that the Linux community is self-sustaining and its contributions are greater than its deductions (deductions being what is taken to benefit members of the community).

I hope to donate the tray's contents to Open Source developers if the amount ever becomes significant, though, I'm not sure if that would be possible with such a small tray.  Perhaps I will assemble more to place around.

Thanks for your time. :)
