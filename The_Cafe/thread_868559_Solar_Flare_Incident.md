### Post by dhughes on 2008-07-24
I put #30 sunblock on all my Hard Drives :D

 But seriously, I'd be more worried about lightning, a flash of lightning sends out a huge electromagnetic pulse, a bolt of lightning has millions of amps and that carries quite a wallop!

 But if for some reason the Sun went crazy and sent out such a huge pulse, the fist thing to go would be satellites in orbit taking out critical pathways for communications.

 If it got to the Earth's surface it would probably cause a surge in high tension power lines. It's happened before in Quebec with only a mild solar flare. The EMP generates a current in the wires as it moves past. That would knock out power or send a surge to some unlucky people first before it burns out.

 Then if finally hard drives are fried that would mean the loss of many family photos and sensitive data. Businesses such as banks may lose record of how much money is in your account!

 The biggest problem would be the loss of communication between people worldwide, we've all grown used to a world where we can talk to anyone, anywhere at any time. That would be the biggest loss.
