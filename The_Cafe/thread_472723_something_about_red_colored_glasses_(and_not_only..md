My "weed" analogy was actually me referencing personal experiences.

1) (someone who seriously needs to have their pot taken away because it's skewing their reality).  I knew this couple through a friend of mine.  The guy was a big-time pot-smoker, and it seriously affected his life.  He regularly decided to stay at home "sick" to smoke pot instead of go to work, to the point of losing several jobs.  He was paranoid about everything.  Etc, etc.  I ran into him a few years later, and he was a changed man.  After his g/f dumped him (and they were really serious..thought they'd get married), he realized he was relying too much on pot to carry him through things.  It blunted his response to things, which made him not care much about things, causing him to lose a serious job he had, and his girlfriend all within the same month.  He gave himself an "alcoholics ultimatum", telling himself he'd stop using it for a week to prove to himself he wasn't like some crack junkie who had to have it...he was just a regular person who like to smoke occasionally to lighten his mood.  He told me that after that weeks time, things kinda cleared up, he found another job, got on speaking terms with his girlfriend again (but still not married), and things started looking up.  For him, he realized that he was smoking pot to help him cope with all the "bad stuff" happening in his life, but he didn't realize that his pot smoking was *causing* all the bad things in his life...until he gave it up.  He told me now that he still smokes occasionally, but its not the main priority it used to be.  Guess he just needed that epiphinal "moment of clarity".

2) (someone who NEEDS to smoke pot).  A girlfriend I dated had bi-polar disorder.   I say "had", because when I was dating her, she kept it under control by smoking pot every day.  She was already a smoker, and just worked the joints into her usual smoking routine.  In the past, she had been to doctors who put her on various pills, but they all had wacko side effects, like keeping her up for days, or causing huge headaches that would put her out for a day.  I remember the week that she stopped smoking pot, because I noticed a change in her pattern.  The week prior, she wasn't calling me as much, and she seemed kinda distant.  I met her for lunch at her work on Tuesday like we usually do, and the person at the front-desk told me she couldn't go.  I thought it was odd, because if she didn't want to go in the past, she'd call me and tell me instead of wasting my time showing up.  I asked the front-desk person (who she was friends with) if she noticed anything different, and she said that my g/f was acting pretty odd.  Not like psycho, but just kinda moody.  Said she was running hot and cold.  IE: She'd be in a really great mood, then suddenly go off on an employee for the littlest thing.  I thought "uh oh".  I stopped by her place later that night, and she didn't answer her door.  I called, she didn't answer.  I finally camped out by her place during the weekend (yeah, yeah, call me a "stalker"), and caught her as she was taking the trash out.  She looked like hell.  She was really pissed off at seeing me, called me a stalker, told me to F off, etc...not exactly the sweet, charming girl I was dating.  Anyways, this all leads up to me finally getting her back in her apartment, where she breaks out in tears and tells me she stopped smoking pot.  I had a hard time understanding her, but from what I make of it, she was tired of having to rely on it, so she wanted to kick the habit.  She had gone cold-turkey for about a week..and it showed.  Her apartment was a mess.  She was a mess, and I, for the first time in my life, was arguing with someone to try to get them to TAKE drugs.
