### Post by Corfy on 2008-10-02
> **StitchJAcket said:**
>  Question though what all goes on there.

The bulk of the event is taken up by speakers talking about various aspects of Linux and/or open source software. Topics range from those targeted to beginners (Home Suite Home, Can you Do That in Linux?, etc.) to topics targeted to advanced users (OpenAFS: How Becoming Open Source Changed AFS, and Everything You Need To Know To Start Using It Today, etc.) to issues that anyone can understand (Sustainable Computing, Blender: Open Source Fundraising Success, etc.). You have your choice of one of three topics for each time slot during the day (except for the opening and closing keynote addresses, which are the only topics in those slots).

Also there will be many booths set up from a variety of Linux, open source, hardware, and computer-related vendors and organizations. You can usually pick up a lot of free stuff at these booths.

You can also hang out with more than 1,000 people who have all at least heard of Linux (most of my friends, if they have heard of Linux, they heard about it from me... gets a little lonely at times).

There is also an after party, but I have not attended that yet, so I don't know what that is like first hand.
