### Post by ZarathustraDK on 2010-03-15
Going full HD 1920x1080 :)

Really, if you want bigscreen sweetness be sure to pick a somewhat known brand. My old Mirai was a pain, the resolution was odd and there was scanlines-galore, no modeline ever worked.

Tip on the Samsungs: the later series have a lot of unneeded cruft (network, youtube (wtf?), etc.) stuff you wont be missing if you are going to hook it up to a pc anyway. All you want is a bigass display in good quality with a HDMI-port, and those usually come quite a bit cheaper than the "full-feaature" ones you see in the commercials.

For instance, my Samsung UE46B6000 cost me about $2.200 (which is the afore-mentioned bigass-screen with a HDMI-port), the fully equipped version, in comparison, cost about $3.500 for a lot of stuff I'll never need (mind you, I don't live in the US, it's probably a lot cheaper there).

### Post by MaxIBoy on 2010-04-06
Once you get that extra room, you find uses for it real quick. Eventually, if you need to go back to a smaller display, you feel cramped and confined. 

But in my opinion, having multiple monitors is more important than having one big monitor. Whenever my desktop machine is working (ahem,) I have a 19-inch [s]wide[/s]shortscreen LCD plus a 17-inch CRT. Neither of these monitors is anything special, but together you get a **lot** of room at a reasonable price. 

Using the 27-inch iMacs at the local University library seems vacuous and empty to me. I really don't like having multiple windows visible at the same time (no idea why, it just doesn't feel right,) so with a bigger monitor, past a certain point, I actually get less information per square inch. 

But with two monitors, I can maximize **two** windows at the same time. For me, this is great, and it literally doubles my productivity. Also, check out this video:
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1DWzuIreDGA](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1DWzuIreDGA)
