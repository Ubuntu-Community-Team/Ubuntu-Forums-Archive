### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-03-24
To my bicycle, get on and go for a ride. Or go for a walk. Or, now footy season started just tonight over here in Australia, [HERE]("http://www.afl.com.au/"). Watch a replay of a game and those nutters throwing themselves around and you're soon forgetting your blues.

But not everyone is as sports crazy as I am. I'm also a musician so I find grabbing one of my instruments can cheer me up some. Shifts the mindset.

Depending on why I need cheering up, sometimes I just have a cry and then I feel better. Try it sometime. Very therapeutic. :D

(PS: But if you find yourself doing it on a daily basis, seek professional help and advice, please, and if it seems specific to winter, or any other season, do a little research into [seasonal depression]("http://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/seasonal-affective-disorder/basics/definition/CON-20021047"). The very middle of winter is invariably a bad time for me. About two weeks either side of the shortest day of the year.)
