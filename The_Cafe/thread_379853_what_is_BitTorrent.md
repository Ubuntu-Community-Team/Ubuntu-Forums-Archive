### Post by me1on on 2007-03-08
From the [Wikipedia article]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bittorrent"):
> BitTorrent is a peer-to-peer (P2P) file distribution protocol. The protocol was originally designed and created by programmer Bram Cohen, and is now maintained by BitTorrent, Inc.

BitTorrent is a method of distributing large amounts of data widely without the original distributor incurring the whole of the corresponding costs of hardware, hosting and bandwidth resources. Instead of the distributor alone servicing each recipient, under BitTorrent the recipients each also supply data to newer recipients, thus significantly reducing the cost and burden on any given individual source as well as providing redundancy against system problems, and reducing dependence upon the original distributor.

Unfortunately, BitTorrent is usually used for illegal purposes. It does have some good uses, however, and is especially good for downloading large (legal!) files like the Ubuntu CD image. It has some other advantages too, such as the ability to resume downloads and having a smaller chance of the file being corrupted during the download process. It's not that great at downloading smaller files, though.

### Post by Quillz on 2007-03-08
BitTorrent is in of itself nothing more than simply a way of downloading any given file. Unfortunately, like the Internet itself, it is occasionally used to share illegal material. (Of course, this is just as common as it is to download something illegal via HTTP or FTP.) BitTorrent is generally slower than a direct HTTP download, but if you have many seeders, like for a Linux distro, for instance, then you will get very quick speeds. When I was downloading Herd 5 of Kubuntu, I got nearly 900 kb/sec for a constant 30 minutes.
