### Post by gingermark on 2006-04-29
I think a comparable QT program to the GIMP would be Krita, although The Gimp is generally superior I think.

I use both Gtk and (obviously) qt apps in KDE, and as an end user, I'm more worried about how a program works that what toolkit is used. Gtk apps look fine with the gtk-qt style engine anyways.

The most important thing for me is configurability (via the GUI). So whilst I prefer K3b over Gnomebaker, KAudioCreator over Sound Juicer, Kaffeine over Totem and amaroK over Rhythmbox, I'm more that happy to use Gtk programs that actually have the options to do what I want, such as Grip, Gaim, or The Gimp.

I will say one thing - QT has the more consistent apps. Interface wise, Gtk apps are all over the place, with a wide spectrum in terms of how customisable or "rigid" each GUI is.
