### Post by bryncoles on 2008-10-19
> A PC is a tool.

i used to think that too, before i started using ubuntu in january this year. since switching though, i know this is wrong. a 'tool' can only perform a limited number of functions, depending upon what / how it was designed. 

a computer should not be thought of as being a tool. it should be conceived as being a tool kit. a tool can only perform one function, for which its been specifically designed. a hammer can drive nails into a wall, a ceiling, bits of wood. but when you want to turn a screw, you'll find your hammer no longer fit for purpose. 

my computer can surf the net. why should i need extra, different software to word-process (quiet, google-docs, no-ones talking to you!)? 

a computer offers a range of options for completing a range of different tasks in a range of different ways. i only understood this once i started using a system which offers this range gratis, always and at my finger-tips. 

most people don't want a tool-kit though. they don't want to have to think about the best way to approach their problem. they don't want to have a problem. they just want to do one task and move on. 

problem is, they want to do many 'just one' tasks on the same computer, with the same limited tool-kits. 

anyway, why are we pushing up this guy's hit-counter?
