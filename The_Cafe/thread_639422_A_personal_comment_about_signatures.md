### Post by PartisanEntity on 2007-12-13
This is my **personal** opinion, and I post it here as just another member of ubuntuforums.org.

Many of us use signatures and often it is a nice and convenient way to know a little bit more about fellow members. It also gives a nice personal touch to the otherwise somewhat cold nature of text on a computer screen.

However, these signatures can lead to a cluttered appearance and the use of text sizes, colours and special characters can give it all a somewhat confused appearance. Of course this is all a matter of personal taste.

I recently tried to make my signature as unobtrusive as possible, I selected size: 1 and gave the font a grey colour. This way, the personal touch can remain, yet it stays in the background and allows the actual thread contents to remain in the foreground.

So, if you happen to agree with me, perhaps you would be interested in doing the same or similar to your signature so that we can make it all a little easier on the eyes and aesthetically pleasing.

If you don't agree, well then sorry for wasting your time, but thanks for reading this far :)

p.s. I am also open to any other suggestions or ideas.

### Post by 23meg on 2007-12-13
I meant setting it in your browser and/or desktop environment. If you have problems viewing small text in this forum, you most likely have the same problem elsewhere too, so you need to make a system-wide adjustment. Even if a font is too small after making that adjustment, Ctrl + Mousewheel (or "View -> Text Size") allows instant global resizing in Firefox, so you can temporarily make fonts larger to see that signature, and decrease the size again. There are also accessibility tools such as screen magnifiers that may be used for momentary size enlargement.

A relatively smaller font size, without anything in bold (which is interpreted as emphasis) conveys the fact that what's written in the signature is of lesser importance than the message body, and that's good design practice.

### Post by popch on 2007-12-13
My position appears to be a bit between those of PartisanEntity and RAV TUX.

I appreciate the thought that signatures (and 'such') should not drown the forum's contents. Thus, the post bodies should be rendered in a manner which allows for easy and fast reading. Headings and possibly author data should stand out, and that's easily accomplished by using bold typefaces or even larger fonts.

Anything else not contributing to the thread should be as discrete as possible. It should be left to the reader's discretion whether he wants to take notice of that 'other' stuff. That makes grey preferrable to black, smaller preferrable to large, uncolored preferrable to colored and static much preferrable to animated or flashing.

As the OP knows, I ***do ***take notice of some signatures from time from time. But not always.

I am all for a good signal to noise ratio, especially when I want to read a thread in a hurry or when I have to concentrate on the text just in order to understand the contents.

On the other hand, as I am not as young as I used to be and wearing bifocals, reading minuscule lettering with poor contrast or scanning long lines of text can quickly become frustrating for me.
