### Post by parker13 on 2006-12-22
Wordpress is the obvious choice ([http://wordpress.org/)](http://wordpress.org/)). It's themeable and very customisable with some HTML/PHP knowledge. It has all the necessary features, such as comments, pingbacks, RSS etc. Anything that doesn't come as standard (such as Google Analytics) can be added as a plugin. The Ubuntu SE site uses wordpress: [http://parker1.co.uk/satanic/](http://parker1.co.uk/satanic/)

You need a MySQL/PHP available on your hosting service, or you can blog for free on the Wordpress site.

### Post by teet on 2006-12-22
A lot of people seem to like wordpress.  I messed around with it this summer a little bit (I just installed it on my local "webserver" in my house) but finally ditched it.  There are literally hundreds of different themes to choose from, but I didn't like most of them.  I tried to customize the few that I did like (the default theme is rather nice actually) but it ended up being too complicated for me.  The final nail in the coffin was the fact that there wasn't a good way to put up a photo album and have it integrated within the wordpress theme.  I tried several different photo album plugins but none of them worked the way I wanted them too.

With that said, the actual blogging system in wordpress is great.  It did exactly what I wanted it to do.

-teet
