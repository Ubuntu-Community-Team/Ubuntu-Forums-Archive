What you describe is not the question being posed. There is no need to download the WIN32 codecs because they already exist on the computer's harddrive. 

Like Jrusso2, I have searched for any indication from MS that using their codecs is restricted to their operating system; and I could find none. To Microsoft's credit, they are rather generous in the licensing of their codecs for use with their OS (i.e., no royalties or commercial-use restrictions), but they make no mention of any restriction against using their codecs with another OS (if you can find one, please provide a link). 

I am not really sure they *could* make such a restriction, at least not without having it challenged in court. Consider that any non-Microsoft program has the "right" to use the codecs which are stored on the harddrive; why shouldn't Linux -- which is in essence merely a non-Microsoft program -- have that same right? 

I imagine one would have to be pretty creative in writing a license so that WinAmp could legally use a codec but Linux could not. I'm not saying such licensing is beyond reason WRT Microsoft, but I don't see any evidence that they have attempted to address it.
