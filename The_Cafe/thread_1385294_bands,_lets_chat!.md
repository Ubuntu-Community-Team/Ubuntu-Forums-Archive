### Post by steveneddy on 2010-01-20
I am a former professional musician.

It is hard keeping bands together. You are married to the other members in a manner of speaking and to be successful there has to be lots of compromise.

If you start your own band, you are in control, and you can hire whomever you like to play in your project.

I kept my own band for times when we could play together, but mainly worked for others and learned to be a team player (no pun intended).

Very few people make money playing their own music. To make money, have fun and get your music "out there", get a cover band together, get some gigs, make a few bucks and then start adding in your originals to the mix.

Get enough songs for a CD, use what the band earns to go to a little local studio and cut a CD and sell it at the gigs.

I still come off of the road to jam with friends and I have an open invitation with other band members that still play to come sit in a whole night or just for a few songs.

I have friends who play in local cover bands and make enough just working Friday and Saturday nights to make a house payment.

I've been around the block in the music business. The key is to have fun and enjoy what you are doing. Otherwise it isn't worth it.
