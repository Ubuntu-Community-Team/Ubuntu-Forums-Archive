I can tell you that the first computer bug happened in 1947 at Havard University to the US Navy's Mark II computer, housed in a large un-air-conditioned room. It was brought to a standstill by a moth getting itself jammed in a relay switch.
The operators removed the battered corpse of the insect and taped it next to the entry in the log book before restarting the machine.
Generally, the mechanical nature of early computers made them particularly vunerable to insect interference.
Though this was the exception, as most other early computers at the time, such as the ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer) at the University in Pennsylvania were electronic and used moth-proof vacuum tubes...

Georg and Edvard Scheutz were the first to build a programmable engine, although Charles Babbage came up with the concept years before, but never got past a prototype.
The first computer programmer was a woman - Ada Byron. She was inspired by Babbage's concept. So much that she actually wrote the first plans for the programmable computer and give it freely to Babbage, of whom boasted about for years after. And it was the etchings of these plans that gave Georg and Edvard the leverage to invent it.
Amazing, GPL at work 200 years ago!:lolflag:
