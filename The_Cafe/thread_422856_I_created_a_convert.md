### Post by ssodhi on 2007-04-26
I think it's great that Ubuntu is making its way onto a broader base, and that people are actively marketing it. With that said, if we made a thread for every conversion some people might get the wrong idea. The last thing that you want to do is encourage the evangelism that has haunted Linux since it first went Desktop, or so to speak. I mean, I hate to be the one to crash the party, but try not to put too much weight on 'conversion'. I dislike the term due to the dogmatic overtones it implies. Ubuntu isn't about a war with Microsoft. Ubuntu is about Ubuntu! By no means am I accusing you of being a preacher, I know nothing about you, your approach or anything else to that effect. The more we focus on 'converting', the less we are focusing on providing a solid operating system to convert *to*! Besides, you'll feel much more satisfied knowing that you converted sixty people without a word of praise than having vocalized a hundred of them.

Don't take this personally, by any means, I was merely making a broader commentary. Your heart's in the right place but in the broader scale of things, don't worry too much about converting. As long as you do your bit for community in one way or another, you get your cookie.

-Sanjay Sodhi
