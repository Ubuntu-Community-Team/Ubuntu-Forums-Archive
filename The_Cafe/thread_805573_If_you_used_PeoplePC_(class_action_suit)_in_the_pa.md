I can remember trying to cancel PeoplePC when I got a cable modem:

Me: "I want to cancel my dial-up service. I got cable-modem broadband service installed today."

PPC: "Great. We have special plans for people who can't afford $14.95 per month. We'll put you on that."

Me: "No, you misunderstood me. I want to completely cancel my entire service. I no longer need dialup, as my cable service was installed today. Please cancel my service and my account."

PPC: "No problem. We'll just put you down for the special $6.95 per month plan. Can I do anything else for you?"

Me: "Yes. You can CANCEL MY ENTIRE EXPLETIVE ACCOUNT! I DON"T EXPLETIVE NEED IT! I HAVE EXPLETIVE BROADBAND! EITHER YOU CANCEL MY ENTIRE ACCOUNT, OR I"LL REPORT YOU TO MY CREDIT CARD COMPANY!"

PPC: "I am authorized to offer you a special plan for as little only $3.95 per month."

Me: "Give me your name, then connect me to your Supervisor. I wish to get from him the name of your Corporate Attorney so that I know whom to send the paperwork to. My family is pretty much the entire law-enforcement and prosecutor in this neck of the woods."

PPC: "No problem sir. Just give me a minute to cancel your account..."

### Post by Tundro Walker on 2008-05-24
My Dad decided to sign up with them for a while.  They use some kind of annoying POS dialer for their dial-up service.  It was slow, unreliable, and when he finally cancelled service with them, it took me 2 hours to track down all the components to uninstall the little ... well, the little pain in the neck.  It didn't have a simple uninstall ... the thing was embedded in the registry & OS like a troja or virus.  After snooping on the net, it turns out their dialer acts like spyware, tracking user activity so PeoplePC can sell the info to marketing companies.

I'm really getting tired of businesses taking advantage of customers these days.  What, it's not enough that someone wants to do business with you?  You have to go the extra step and take advantage of them?  Seriously, is this what they're teaching these MBA grads to do these days?
