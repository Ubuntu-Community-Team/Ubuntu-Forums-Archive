### Post by Brynster on 2008-11-14
Water cooling is fairly straight forward if you wanna go down that path i recommend [http://www.thermaltakeusa.com/Products.aspx?C=1160](http://www.thermaltakeusa.com/Products.aspx?C=1160)

If you buy a complete kit for your specific processor it will cover everything you need such as the pump, reservoir, heat exchanger, pipes, connectors and fluid etc.

They are no more difficult to fit than a standard fan assembly for the processor. The difficult comes from making the pips look tidy and mounting the pump, heat exchanger and reservoir within your case or externally if needed. 

Usually after you have got all the bubbles out of the system your processor will run up to 50% cooler however in real old the heat saving is around 20-30%. The noise levels as a rule are quieter than a standard CPU and cooler fans although that will vary depending on if you have used hi quality low noise or silent fans.

The best advise is buy the best kit you can afford, as cheap ones usually are the ones that fail first or perform worse.
