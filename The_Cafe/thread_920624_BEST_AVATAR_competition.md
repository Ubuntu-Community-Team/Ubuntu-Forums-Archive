### Post by Corfy on 2008-09-15
I want to toss mine into the ring, even though I don't think it is the "best".

Several years ago, I was redesigning my personal website and decided I needed a logo. I happen to like raccoons, and there aren't many raccoons used in logos, so I took a picture of one of the toy raccoons I have, worked with Photoshop to convert it and give it a cartoon look, and then placed it over two different colored "C"s that were embedded one inside the other (the "C"s were the initials of my site, Corfy's Corner). Voila, instant logo. I saved it as a JPEG in a fairly large size and resized it smaller as needed, and I started using it as an Avatar on the web.

Over the years, of course, that original logo began to degrade, especially after I lost the original, and what was large in 1999 is pretty small by today's standards.

Last year, I updated the logo by recreating it in Inkscape, so now I have a clean SVG version of it.
