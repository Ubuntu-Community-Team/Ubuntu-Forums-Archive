### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-02-05
When an organisation publishes its intentions people expect it to deliver the goods in a reasonable time frame otherwise detractors start talking about vapourware and broken promises. That puts pressure on the organisation and unwise decisions might be the consequence.

This is what I like about the arrangement that canonical has with its OEM partners.

1) A purchaser will not be stuck with a version of Ubuntu until the OEM puts out an upgrade or the user buys a newer model.
2) The OEM is responsible for producing commercially viable hardware that is not rushed to market otherwise its reputation will suffer.
3) Canonical has to coordinate with the OEM to prove that the OS does what it is meant to do to the satisfaction of the OEM.
4) The OEM and not Canonical makes the decision on when to release the product to market.

If Canonical was buying hardware in bulk quantities and then re-badging the devices with the Ubuntu logo we might be getting a rushed to market device. But the method of development chosen gives me some confidence about these products.

Regards.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-02-11
Carriers are just that, a carrier for the data. As I understand the set up with an Android device is that the OEM has responsibility to ship any updated version of Android. With Ubuntu Phones it is Canonical that takes responsibility. It is explained here

> "The (hopefully) final OTA-7 re-spin is in progress with the two  blocking issue fixed and changes released. Big thanks to everyone  involved," said &#321;ukasz Zemczak. "Once the image is built QA will perform  sanity and delta testing on the generated images and if everything is  ok, the image will then go to manufacturers for a final check. ETA for  release: first half of next week."

> If the testing results are OK, the Ubuntu Touch OTA-7 images will be  sent to phone manufacturers, in this case BQ and Meizu, for the final  check. Then, Canonical will start uploading the OTA-7 images to the  official mirrors and send the update to all Ubuntu phone users, which  will happen sometime in the first half of next week.

[http://news.softpedia.com/news/ubuntu-touch-ota-7-will-arrive-in-the-first-half-of-next-week-says-canonical-494582.shtml](http://news.softpedia.com/news/ubuntu-touch-ota-7-will-arrive-in-the-first-half-of-next-week-says-canonical-494582.shtml)

All Ubuntu mobile devices will get the same Over The Air update over a period of 24 to 48 hours from "official mirrors." It saves the OEMs a lot of work. And a device does not become out of date because a new version of the OS has been released.
