### Post by aysiu on 2011-06-10
Saying something isn't for you because you're a power user isn't belittling you. It's simply stating a fact.

If someone tells me "This meat dish isn't for you because you're a vegetarian," I don't feel belittled. I feel grateful. Now I know not to eat that meat dish. I'll find another more tasty dish instead.

Some things will appeal more to power users, others to average users. Saying something designed for average users won't appeal to power users isn't an insult to power users.

And who is a power user? Well, I basically think of it this way--if you tend to give more tech support to others than you ask for it from others, you're most likely a power user. If not, you're most likely an average user. Power users are also more likely to tweak and customize things.

### Post by Peter09 on 2011-06-10
When I WAS a power user.

I cut my teeth on a machine called a PDP11/34. We were building a command a control system, programming, using Vt100 terminals and using macro11 assembler (the machine ran RSX11m for anyone interested).

A PDP11/34 in this configuration had a frontage which was about the size of a door and was as deep as a large fridge. The bottom half of the cabinet contained the power supplies and on top of that the processor and control panel. The top half contained four disk packs. Each pack was about the size of a small car wheel (although not as deep) and each fitted into a draw in the machine. Each pack held 60MB? (or was it 10MB).

The machine was in a separate room and I sat the other side of a partition, which was half glassed, so I could see the machine.

Man - when I did a large build on that machine it physically swayed - that's what I call a power user.

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-06-10
In my definition a power user is one that wants to get maximum performance of their machine, does something that benefits form that performance and is willing to spend the time to learn how.

If you only keep two browser windows open, this isn't "power". If you keep several windows open while doing research + several .pdf paper + taking notes about the papers or writing your own one, then you have at least 10 windows opened on different workspaces. ### Post by FreeTheBee on 2011-06-10
It's true the term is sometimes used with a condescending tone. I don't think though that a phrase like 'mint is not for me because I'm a power user' necessarily has to imply that. 

I consider a power user someone who likes to tinker and get things exactly the way he/she likes it. For such a person, installing a full fledged distribution like mint or ubuntu will probably mean spending a lot of time ripping out or replacing unwanted things, and fixing possible breakage due to the changes. A more bottom up approach, for example by installing a basic system with Arch or Slackware or something and then building it up entirely to your own taste, seems more convenient to me in this case.
