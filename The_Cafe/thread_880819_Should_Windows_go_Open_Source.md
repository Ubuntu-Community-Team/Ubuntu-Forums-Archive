### Post by estyles on 2008-08-05
> **RiceMonster said:**
> Where's the "who cares?" option.

1) I took "What?" to be the "who cares?" option.

2) I disagree with lumping Apple together with Linux as much as most people in this thread.  At least the Windows OS is something I can deal with, and not just because of familiarity.  The Apple/Mac business model of locked down hardware, locked down software, and the philosophy of their programmers (you can only do things one way - so don't look for alternate ways of accomplishing things - they don't exist) is, to me, sickening.  And their attack marketing is only palatable to us because it attacks the big boys.  If they ever felt threatened by Linux, what do you think their advertising team would do?

3) Contrary to what others are saying, I think that opening up their source would be good for MS, to a degree.  They will never do it, at least not anytime soon without a major paradigm shift, but I think it would be good for them.  As it is now, very, very few end-users ever purchase a copy of Windows.  Either it comes pre-bundled on their computers, where they don't even realize there's a cost involved, or they get a pirated copy without even thinking about it.  It's actually in MS's best interests to allow the piracy to continue, because it keeps people away from Linux (full disclosure, that opinion is stolen from an article I read that someone posted here - I fully believe it though).  Their money is made through OEM deals and corporate clients.  As long as they still charge for their software, corporate clients will still pay for it even if it's open source - they can't afford to be caught in a lawsuit.  And the OEM's will still pay for it because they can pass the cost on to their customers, and they (perhaps wrongly) believe that there is an element of safety and trust in going with an MS product - even if someone copied the source line-by-line and released a Windows distro that was half the price, would it be worth the risk to an OEM?  Not likely.

So I think that going OS wouldn't affect MS's bottom line much at all.  Certainly not in the short run.  But it might allow them to improve their OS with the cooperation of the open source community, and their actually being part of and adhering to standards would probably help them in the long run.

Not gonna happen, of course, but I don't think it would be bad for them.  Of course, open source doesn't necessarily mean free (and when I say free, I mean without charge, I'm sick of people trying to change the meaning of the word - for 99% of people if you're talking about a product and not a bird, free means without charge) - they certainly would hurt their bottom line if they offered Midori *gratis*.
