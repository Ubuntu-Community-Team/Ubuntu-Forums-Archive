### Post by TheFu on 2018-12-18
The "FROM" in any email can be anything.  It isn't validated any more than the return address on a letter or postcard.

**Nothing to see here. Move along.**

For fun, I've sent emails as [email]god@heaven.net[/email] before.  It is harder to do for most people since ISPs should block outbound email from any domain that they don't know about, but if you buy a $5/month VPS, you can setup SMTP and send anything you like with any "FROM" that doesn't have DKIM or SPF protection.  Email was the wild-west for a long time. Parts of the internet, still is.
