### Post by ian-weisser on 2022-10-27
> **Sceiron said:**
> *In general, are help/support forums dying out? It seems like reponse-times are getting slower and supporting people are getting fewer.*

Ubuntuforums, AskUbuntu, and IRC all seem about as busy as they were five years ago. To me, those three seem steady-state and highly responsive.

We are currently in the weeks after a fresh release, and still in the months after an LTS-release. There is always a spike in support requests during those times as folks encounter (mostly self-created) problems with their release-upgrades. I see lots of new folks trying their hand offering support on IRC and AskUbuntu...and lurking here awaiting an opportunity.

UF was originally intended to be much more than support. It was supposed to be a complete-community site, where developers could collaborate, announcements would get wide distribution, local groups could coordinate and share, folks needing help could get support, specialty groups could form, new contributors could get orientation and advice, all mixed with a bit of positive social interaction.

All except support eventually dried up. Many of those non-support uses were chased away to other sites by a flood of bullies and fools when Ubuntu became immensely popular and won the distro wars. Hence the current proliferation of many sites and the crickets chirping in many sections here. Like a tree, some branches have withered, but the tree itself seems healthy to me.
