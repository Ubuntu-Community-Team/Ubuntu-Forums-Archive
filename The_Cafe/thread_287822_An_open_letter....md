### Post by aristotlewilde on 2006-10-29
**The following views are my own.  They do not in any way reflect any particular Website, Linux Distribution, Developer, Politician, etc...

I have to get this off my chest.  You may see it elsewhere as well, as I am not just posting it here.  It's a topic that needs to be addressed.  Feel free to weigh in below.

Ok, here we go...

I know it's Trick or Treat season, but please, lay off the “EYE CANDY” if you are new to Linux.  If you just freshly installed a new distro, you got the equivalent of a Range Rover that gets 99 miles to the gallon.  AND you're leasing it for $1 a month!!  Don't F' it up by putting spinning rims on it!

Yes, this is a little tongue in cheek, but have a look at the official Ubuntu Forum today.  All you will find are THOUSANDS of posts begging for help on installing Ubuntu 6.10.  THOUSANDS.  

A large percentage of these posts are from brand new users who screwed up their xorg.conf trying to install Compiz/Beryl.  They haven't even used their new distro for a day yet, and they are mucking it up with spinning rims!

With the new distros, just getting things to work initially isn't that hard anymore.  It used to be that people would get to the partitioning stage, get frustrated and give up.  Nowadays, we can get a functioning distribution that looks damn good out of the box with almost no hiccups (perhaps a video driver issue here and there, but nothing 2 minutes in a support forum can't cure).  It feels like getting a clean install is no longer a brick wall to hit, so we've artificially created a new one in the NEED for Compiz.  Once these eye-candy effects are included in distros, I'm sure we'll create another.  Open Source is always going to be cutting edge as far as giving the end-user things they actually want, since these apps are created by the very users that need/want them.  

If you have been using Linux for a while, I assume you'd like to see as many people happily using Linux as you can, spreading the word about it and being productive with it.

If you are new to Linux, I assume you didn't download that ISO so you could screw something up, pull your hair out and go running back to Windows/OSX.  

Please, once you get that new distro up and running play with it for a while.  You can do endless things with a stock install of Ubuntu/Fedora/etc.. that you could not do with your old OS.  Look around and explore.  Get used to how it works before you go and mess it up. 




p.s.  On a personal note, as a Linux user for over a year now, I was able to get my own install of Ubuntu 6.10 Edgy Eft running in record time (with all necessary audio/video codecs, ALL drives mounted and my Nvidia driver properly configured).  ALSO as a Linux user for over a year, I KILLED that install in record time by making my first attempt at getting Compiz running.  Luckily, my next install went even faster.  Who needs spinning rims anyway?

### Post by cmost on 2006-10-29
While it's true that a lot of people are screwing up their newly installed systems trying to install Compiz/Beryl, I believe the majority of the fault lies with Ubuntu itself.  Why on earth would AIGLX be enabled in X.org by default if Ubuntu's devs didn't want people to enable the cool effects of Compix or Beryl?  Secondly, this begs the question:  Why wasn't a little utility to easily configure Compiz or Beryl included in the default install?  This could have been something similar to SUSE's Yast configurator which  simply checks hardware compatibility and fetches the required packages from the repository.  People love eye candy and many distros are beginning to include Compiz or Beryl as part of their default install.  Not everyone runs a back room server.  We love our bling.

On a related note, after reading the many, many upgrade horror stories on these threads the past several days, i've decided to sit Edgy out and await Feisty fawn (stupid name by the way.)  Ubuntu devs screwed up a lot more than X.org for a lot of people and this release is already being hailed as the worst ever by several notable reviewers as well as the community at large.  I hope Ubuntu gets its crap together for the next release.  To others, I recommend sitting this one out or trying Fedora Core 6 (which offers a nifty little applet to enable Compiz or Beryl.)  Just my $0.02.
