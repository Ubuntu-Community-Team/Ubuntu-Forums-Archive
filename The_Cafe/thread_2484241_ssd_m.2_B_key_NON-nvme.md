### Post by TheFu on 2023-02-21
I have a few m.2 SSDs that aren't NVMe.  They are SATA and come in a few different standard sizes. 2242, 2260, 2280.  Those are width-length numbers.  22mm wide and 42mm long is what my laptops use.  Desktops usually support all 3 lengths by having screw holes in each location.  2280 is the most common.  I've seen mention of 22110, but I've never seen this.  Perhaps that's for the 4TB+ sized SSDs?  IDK.

SATA SSDs tend to be in the 450-550 MB/s, which is 2-3x faster than spinning disks. Very worth the effort. 
Anyway, if you search for "m.2 SATA SSD", you'll find plenty.

### Post by Dennis N on 2023-02-21
an m.2 SSD drive with both M and B slots (like those mentioned) will plug into a Socket for type B devices. You also have to be sure the socket actually provides a correct interface type for the drive. Check the motherboard specifications to find out.

Type B socket is mostly for SATA, but type M can also be SATA, so that's why the 2 slots.
