### Post by kaldor on 2011-08-01
Anything with an Intel or AMD graphics card will usually work fine these days. In fact, I'd actually recommend AMD graphics over NVIDIA and Intel these days. AMD and NVIDIA cards might have heat problems if you use the open source drivers. If the laptop runs too hot, install the proprietary drivers. 

The only thing to worry about when getting an Ubuntu-ready laptop is the wireless. Newer broadcom cards work pretty well with Ubuntu 11.04. Realtek may cause problems.

### Post by linuxforartists on 2011-08-01
My general advice for Linux compatibility is go Intel everything: processor, graphics card, and wireless card.  Unless you're a hard-core gamer, they should be powerful enough for everything you need.

Another thing is to pick up a machine that's been out for a year.  Chances are, the community has reverse-engineered all the components by then and solved the compatibility problems.  So the computer should work straight out of the box.  Cutting-edge machines are more likely to have issues.

I've heard mixed things about Toshiba.  Elsewhere on the forum people have recommended HP and Lenovo for Linux.

Speaking for myself, I've only used Ubuntu and Ubuntu-based distros on Asus machines.  Asus is my favorite brand.

Good luck choosing a new computer.
