### Post by aesis05401 on 2009-08-26
I feel this is a condemnation of the current laws surrounding EULAs than anything else.  How can this even happen?  Easy, our technology markets are built on non-standard and legally questionable contracts between vendors and consumers.  

There is a reason that things like leases and insurance agreements require initials on all pages, and often on each clause on each page... because that is what is required for the contract to be considered legally sound substantiating evidence if any court case arises.  

While it is possible to go to court with an unwitnessed contract written on the back of a used paper plate, the aggrieved party shouldn't have relied upon an unwitnessed contract written on the back of a used paper plate in the first place.  Or an EULA ;)

Legal precedents trump EULAs any day.
