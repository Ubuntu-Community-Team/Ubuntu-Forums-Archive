### Post by bonzodog on 2007-06-09
Currently, Ubuntu pays its developers and funds the Ubuntu foundation through Canonical, and it has £10 Million set aside. 

Mark made his fortune through the sale of Thawte to Verisign for something like £100 Million.

Ubuntu is currently a 'philanthropic venture', but he has plans to make it profitable.

Also, Mark cleverly set-up Canonical on the Isle of Man, which is a tiny island between Ireland and the UK. It's a crown dependency and is popular with companies as it has no Corporation Tax.

As it's also not a part of the EU, it means it is *totally* protected from any future software patent laws that Europe might introduce.
