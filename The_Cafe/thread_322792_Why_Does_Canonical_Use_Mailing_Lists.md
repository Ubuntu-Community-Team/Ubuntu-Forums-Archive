### Post by SuperMike on 2006-12-21
Why does Canonical use[ mailing lists]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/mailman/listinfo") for Ubuntu when a perfectly decent forum like you're using right now will do just fine?

There are many problems with mailing lists:

* They're highly inefficient because everyone gets emails on topics that may not pertain to their interests.

* It's a good way to get spam because your email account can get harvested because you shared it with a mailing list.

* It's a good way for flame wars to erupt. I mean, you can't delete the message once sent, or edit it, etc. 

* And if the emails are put into an archive and stored forever, they're kept for a good long time, continuing the confusion. Let me give you an example. Let's say Linus Torvalds wrote into a forum and said that something wasn't possible when it was obvious that it was and he had his contraction wrong, such as using "can't" when he simply meant "can". So then this gets archived and a year later, someone sees this mailing list archive, sees this email, and may erupt and send nastygrams to Linux over saying something he said a year ago that was a mistake.

* Some ISPs have policies against enrolling in them and may even drop your account or put a block on it for 30 days. They may also detect which hosts are sending the mail and block the hosts on the firewall.

* They're a holdover from the birth of the Internet. It's time to move on.

In my opinion, Canonical would be far more efficient to use forums. I mean, it's not like it's the birth of the Internet or something.

### Post by Hobbsee on 2006-12-21
Reasons why I like the mailing lists:

*  Filtering - it's very easy to filter based on subject/body/headers/etc, giving fine grained control of what you want to see.  It's very easy to delete multiple messages.

*  Moderation.  Anyone not on the list gets their mail moderated.  ie, no spam.  Anyone can read what's been sent to the list via the archives, even if they dont have permissions to post to it.

*  The subject may not reflect the body.  Mailing lists let you see the body at a glance, rather than the subject only, unless you click to view.

*  Bugtracker mail is either via mailing list, or via straight email (if you're subscribed to a bug)

* Email can be used by almost anyone.  If you dont have a graphical web browser, (ie, lynx or something), you cant read the forums easily.  Especially when blind or something.

*  Forums are quite convoluted, at times, getting to the section you want - unless you bookmark the sections you read.

That'll do for a start?
