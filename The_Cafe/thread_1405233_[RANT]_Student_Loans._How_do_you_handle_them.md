### Post by Maheriano on 2010-02-12
I came out of school with a 9.5 year (approximate) loan at $444 per month.
The interest rate is prime + 2.5%.

When interest rates were high I was paying $7.50 per day in interest alone and only about 30% of my payments per month were going towards my principle. Now that the interest rates are low I get about 80% - 90% towards my principle and I'm only paying $1.80 per day in interest.

Plus I pay $500 per month instead of the required $444, this puts another $56 per month directly on my principle and over the course of the year will actually remove a few months of payments at the end because interest on that portion of the principle has stopped compounding. This can be bad though because I don't actually pay interest because it's tax deductible so no matter how much interest I actually pay on my student loan, I always get it all back at tax time. But I still want to pay this thing off as soon as possible.

And since I graduated I've made about $200,000 in income thanks to my education so I think it was a pretty good investment. About 100% return on investment (ROI) per year and will only increase in time.

Consolidating a student loan is a bad idea because its interest rate is almost always lower than what you could get otherwise. 

A $40,000 loan on a 10 year term is going to be about $450 a month, whether its through a government program or through a bank. Doesn't matter. Only way it will be lower is if you pay it off slower. 

At 10 years, your $40,000 loan costs you $55,000 in total payments. The *only* way to concievably lower your payments any considerible amount is to lengthen the term. A 30 year term loan at 40k would be about $250 a month, **but** you'd pay back about $95,000 over the course of the loan. Double what you took out. 

If someone, ever, told you that you could take out $40,000 in debt and pay it back at $140 a month, they were **lying** to you. Even a 50 year loan would be more than that on that amount.

The simple fact is that if you were allowed to pay $140 a month, at any reasonable interest rate, you would die in debt. It would not be paid off in your reasonable lifetime.
