### Post by gnomeuser on 2009-02-03
Pretty much all computer based "rpg" games are pointless hack and slash scenerios where all problems are solved with a sword or if sofisticated a fireball.

As an old school roleplayer, who used to make his living teaching pen and paper roleplaying, I am somewhat offended they use the RPG label for these games. It has nothing to do with playing a role, you are handed a 2d dimensional premade character and a sword. Typically the storyline is more linear than a Michael Bay movie, no depth, nothing. 

The absolute best in the genre in my opinion, if you must, is Fallout 2. It's >10 years old but it still has an interesting world with lots of side quests to let you explore. One of the best computer games ever made.

offtopic: any pnp roleplayers, especially those with d&d experience should definitely check out the movie The Gamers: Dorkness Rising it's a hilarious take on our subculture which I can only recommend highly.
