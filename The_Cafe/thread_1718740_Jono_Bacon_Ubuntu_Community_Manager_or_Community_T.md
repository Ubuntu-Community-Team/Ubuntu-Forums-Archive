### Post by perspectoff on 2011-04-01
Rambling April Fools rubbish. The Ubuntu / Canonical folks have to be careful because Google / Android is now the 800-pound gorilla. Google has always had some of the best April Fool's gags, and anything second-rate and self-indulgent, even for April Fool's day, is going to make Ubuntu look amateurish.

(For example, I understand that this year Google China offers teleportation services, as a workaround to Chinese censorship proxies.)

For better or worse, Ubuntu is built on marketing and hype, much as Apple, Google, and Microsoft are.

Some argue that without the marketing and hype, Ubuntu wouldn't be where it is today.

After all, it is the Debian community (whose marketing and hype are minimal) that develops most of the software that Ubuntu uses.

If the world has to put up with a bit of foolishness from Jono Bacon (or "fake avatars" of him) or other Canonical or Ubuntu folks, is that insufferable? Well, probably not. 

Canonical / Ubuntu has to be careful, though. There are a lot of Linux distros (Red Hat/Fedora/CentOS, OpenSUSE, and Google OS/Android, for example, in addition to Debian itself) to which serious Linux business (and other) users can turn should Ubuntu become too self-impressed and foolish.

Serious Linux users don't want to be part of another cult (a la Apple), nor do they wish to be part of any organisation with the appearance of amateurish fools at the helm. 

Give us chocolate fish!
