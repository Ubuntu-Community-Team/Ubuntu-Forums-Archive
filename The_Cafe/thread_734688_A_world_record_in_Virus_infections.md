I have come across infections yielding 20 - 30 threats from machines running anti virus programs -  see below.

These days, if I have to clear off a machine then (including spyware and adware) if I find 20-30 threats that is bonus day.  70 - 120 is light infection.  300 or so is moderate infection.  Above that it is getting serious.

Just in case anyone else out there has to deal with this sort of problem I have found the following
:(Norton is heavy, it degrades performance seriously, it does not clear everything off all the time, it is specifically attacked by some viruses, and uninstalling is a pig.  I know of no other program where the developer had built a 'removal tool' just to get its stuff off a machine.  Every Live Update leaves previous entires in the registry clogging every thing up.
:(McAfee is heavier than Norton.  In my experience even greater performance loss.
:confused:AVG is OK, the free version is great but is AV only so once you have added anti spy and anti ad you then have performance issues again.
:)I have shipped Panda for around 3 years now.  It is light, effective, fixes spy and ad ware.  In a recent magazine review it took editors recommendation ahead of Norton and McAfee.
:):)The nice bit about Panda is there is a downloadable active scan which means you can clear the machine up without having to pay out rakes of money first.  You do of course have to be able to access the Net so a DOS attack means you cannot use it.
