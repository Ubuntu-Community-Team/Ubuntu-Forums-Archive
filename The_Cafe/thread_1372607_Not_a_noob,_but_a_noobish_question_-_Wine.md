### Post by forrestcupp on 2010-01-04
In Wine's configuration folder in your /home folder, it actually makes kind of a virtual C: drive.  When you install Windows programs with Wine, it puts it right there in your C: drive folder right in your /home folder.

You don't ever need NTFS or anything from Windows other than a dll once in a while to work around issues with problem apps.
