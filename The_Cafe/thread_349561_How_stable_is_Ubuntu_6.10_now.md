### Post by daynah on 2007-01-30
I upgraded to edgy on my desktop last night and it was a breeze and everything is going great. But I understand your concern, I tried to upgrade Edgy on my laptop and it was a disaster... wifi didn't work and I had to be plugged it at all times. I might as well had a second desktop. Except I only had one desk.

I don't think Edgy is unstable as in it's going to crash and you're going to lose you're data. But Edgy, especially on a laptop, has had many stories of people getting so  frustrated with it that they go back to dapper. I haven't read of people losing data.

I wouldn't upgrade on a laptop. I tried it on my desktop, and everything was okay. But go ahead and make a backup. I find it easier just to impliment the back up than to downgrade. :)

The differences you're going to notice as an end user (I'm assuming you're not a dev) are pictures. Edgy is very pretty. Also, two programs in particular come with Edgy and are integrated in the search, TomBoy and FStop. TomBoy takes Wiki-style notes and FStop organizes your photos similar to Flickr.com. You can have these two programs downloaded on Dapper, they just wont be integrated into your search.

By the way, I think I've used Ubuntu's search funtion three times in the year I've had it.

In short, you ain't missin' much. Having edgy, as an end user, is really for the novelty "not feeling behind" because there aren't many overwhelming reasons for us to upgrade. Fiesty is when we're going to want to upgrade bad. :)

So, in short, I think it's a okay for you to upgrade if you have a desktop, but you don't really need to.

### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-01-30
It's kind of funny to read the wide range of responses.

While I won't be upgrading my primary workstation anytime soon (everything is just the way I want it, why mess with a good thing) I did download and try Edgy this past weekend on a spare laptop.  And was frankly quite pleasantly surprised.  

Suspend and hibernate worked out of the box - first time I've _ever_ had either one work on my Dell laptops.  (I'm usually using the nVidia binary driver though - the laptop I tried has just basic Intel integrated graphics.)

The wireless PCMCIA card I plugged in (that I previously used ndiswrapper with) was recognized, native drivers loaded, and when I installed Network Manager it pulled up a nice long list of local APs.  This wasn't perfect though - for some reason it disassociates after a few minutes of inactivity.  It'll connect back up when I resume network traffic, but there's a delay that's annoying.  Haven't spent the time to figure that one out yet.

I did have a bit of an issue with the cooling fans - that seems to be common.  Until I loaded i8kutils the fan wouldn't do anything until the laptop was H-O-T.  With that installed, though, it stayed nice and cool.

Otherwise, it felt pretty much like I was running Dapper but with a bit of a speed boost.  Even on a relatively punky 1GHz P3M laptop things ran quite smoothly.
