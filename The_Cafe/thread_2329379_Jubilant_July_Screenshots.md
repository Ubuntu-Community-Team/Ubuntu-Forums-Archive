### Post by QIII on 2016-07-01
Not exactly your average screenshot...

This is a project I did with my Odroid XU4.  I took the mirror frame from my late grandmother's vanity, built a small case (with vents!) behind it out of matching red oak, cut a piece of plywood to fit the frame after removing the mirror, inset a 22" monitor, matted around the monitor with black posterboard, put the Odroid, a wireless AC1200 dongle, a 500MB hard drive, wireless keyboard/mouse dongle,  the necessary power, HDMI, USB cables, etc, in the case, installed fbi to rotate every 5 minutes with a random selection of one of about 50 images of Oregon locations/sites directly to the frame buffer without worrying about X.  Effectively, a large, really "antique" looking digital picture frame to hang on the wall.

The Odroid still acts as a headless server that I ssh to from my main machine, it is still my website development platform, serves some files on my LAN, etc.
