### Post by Paqman on 2011-07-02
> **Dustin2128 said:**
> Technically most rocky planets in the solar system are inhabitable, you just have to build the habitat. 

Technically the only planet in the solar system's "habitable zone" is Earth (habitability being defined as a temperature range conducive to liquid water). Any outpost of any size we built on a planet outside the habitable zone would need heavy and continuous support from Earth.

I think it's an exciting time for space exploration. Our robotic explorers are getting very capable, so we're able to do more and more interesting exploration (I'm personally of the opinion that manned exploration is over, it'll all be machines from now on).

Opening up private investment in launch and orbital operations will drive down the cost of getting mass into orbit and make a whole lot of stuff that's currently too expensive viable. The space industry is maturing now, and the big government projects are only going to be a part of the picture now. Faster-better-cheaper was a hugely positive move from NASA, as it showed investors that space missions didn't have to be massive, scary and risky. Smaller, cheaper, lower risk missions with more tightly defined goals are an easier sell to investors, and it's changed the industry for the better.
