### Post by David D. on 2011-03-21
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Usenet is a worldwide distributed Internet discussion system. It developed from the general purpose UUCP architecture of the same name.

Duke University graduate students Tom Truscott and Jim Ellis conceived the idea in 1979 and it was established in 1980.[1] Users read and post messages (called articles or posts, and collectively termed news) to one or more categories, known as newsgroups. Usenet resembles a bulletin board system (BBS) in many respects, and is the precursor to the various Internet forums that are widely used today. Usenet can be superficially regarded as a hybrid between email and web forums. Discussions are threaded, with modern news reader software, as with web forums and BBSes, though posts are stored on the server sequentially.

Anyway, this is a short answer (sort of) as to what it is.  If anyone is interested in more information, Google is your best option.
One notable difference between a BBS or web forum and Usenet is the absence of a central server and dedicated administrator. Usenet is distributed among a large, constantly changing conglomeration of servers that store and forward messages to one another in so-called news feeds. Individual users may read messages from and post messages to a local server operated by their Internet service provider, university, or employer.

Most IPS's have cut access to Usenet (Newsgroups), so users have to connect through their ISP to the Usenet service provider.  Most Usenet providers charge for the service.

### Post by samalex on 2011-03-22
I'm shocked so many people haven't heard of Usenet...  It was the bomb for so long, and you can still read some very historical posts on there like RMS announcing the GNU project, Linus Torvalds announcing Linux, and even the Torvalds vs Tanenbaum debates about Linux vs Minux.  Years ago DejaNews put Usenet archives online going back to 1981 though it has since been gobbled up by Google.  Personally I love reading people's points of view on movies and events right after they happened.

Now'days I still use Usenet for lots of things, but most of the groups I once frequented have been taken over by spammers or pr0n which sucks.  It's because of the pr0n that it got such a bad wrap and why so many ISP's have pulled the plug on their Usenet servers.  

I will say NNTP is still a VERY viable protocol and I wish more services (like Ubuntu Forums) would support it on their backend to give users the ability to use a unified NNTP client to read content.  The web front ends are great, but when someone (like me) monitors a dozen or more forums it'd be nice to have all that in one application which NNTP would make possible.

Sam
