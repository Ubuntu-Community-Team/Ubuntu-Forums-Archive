### Post by Ironi on 2005-05-29
I got started with MiniLin, a Slackware-based dist, back in 1995 or so. I used it on a 486 with the umsdos filesystem (a nasty, easily corruptible fs that used an existing FAT fs). I even tried running the X Window System back then, but everything was too slow and Netscape always died with a Bus Error whenever I tried to run it.

After that, I moved to Debian around 1997 and stuck with it through several generations of hardware and software upgrades. Around 1998, when Enlightenment was big, I made my first attempt at marginalizing Windows and using Linux more often. I didn't really succeed though, and I continue to dual boot to this day.

I added the Ubuntu repos to my apt sources late last year. At first, I only used Ubuntu's Xorg and stuck with Debian for everything else. Then I decided to go all-out and make hoary my primary upgrade source. Of course, I got a bit miffed a short time after that and tried to revert to Debian packages.

Presently, I'm still using both Debian and Ubuntu packages in a careful balance, but I intend to either go back to Debian or move on to another distribution soon. Debian seems to have been permanently slowed down by Ubuntu and by its own problems, so the likelihood of my going back to it isn't very high. Sticking with Ubuntu isn't an option: to be blunt (when am I not?), its community sucks. Hard. It's long past time for me to expand my horizons, anyways.

BTW, I'm 25 and I run Ubuntu with my first language, English.
