### Post by aysiu on 2008-03-20
There's nothing terrible that's going to happen if you switch to KDE, but you may experience minor problems or annoyances. For example, if you log into KDE, and then log out of KDE and log back into Gnome again, a file called *desktop* will now appear on your desktop. A bunch of KDE icons will fill up your Gnome menu and vice versa, sometimes with missing icons. And you might have to reconfigure some things  (as you noted--printing/networking).
