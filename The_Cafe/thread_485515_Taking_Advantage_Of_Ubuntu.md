### Post by Alterax on 2007-06-27
I admit, sometimes I do take it for granted.  It's easy to, considering it's what I use at home, on my PC at work, and on my laptop and home servers.

I don't mean to bash Windows, but there are so many times I deal with problems on the servers at work or the users' workstations that absolutely boggle me--not only how they happen to begin with, but also how convoluted the solution to the problem can be.  Linux--Ubuntu in particular--has absolutely spoiled me, and it has even made the boss comfortable enough with Linux to start requesting we use the Desktop version for our new servers (Not server, you ask?  He'd rather have a GUI until he gets the terminal down.  It's no major loss.)

So am I taking advantage of Ubuntu?  Absolutely.  I intend on taking it for all that it's worth--and to the multitude of people that contribute to it, I have to make it quite clear that it is worth a lot to me.  As a home user.  As a businessman.  As a network/database admin.  As a value-added reseller.  And even if it were NOT free, I actually would gladly cough up an MS-priced license fee to use it because it is reliable, stable, and highly versatile.  That it's free makes me even happier to use it.

--Alterax
