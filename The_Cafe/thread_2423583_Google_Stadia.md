### Post by Shibblet on 2019-07-25
There's a lot of buzz going on about this,

However, I am finding that most people aren't really enthused about the concept, because it is cloud-based, streaming, gaming.
Many people are upset about the premium service not having a library of games to choose from.  Others are upset about having to pay $10/month for 4k Gaming with Surround Sound...   Some are complaining that you have to pay full price for games, and you don't get a physical copy of the game... etc.

Other issues are latency, image quality, and 

Am I the only one that sees the benefit to this service.

First of all, it's OS independent.  Bonus for Linux users.
Secondly, it's hardware independent.  Saves you money on video cards and fast processors.  Even if you pay $10/month for the premium service, it would take you 4 to 5 years to spend as much as you would on a 4k 60fps, Capable video card.  All you need with Stadia is a decoder, preferably hardware, to do 1080p 60fps.  This means you can game on your phone, underpowered laptop, tablet, etc.

Yeah, you have to buy the game, but you do on GOG, Steam, XBox (Microsoft), iPhone (Apple), Android (Google), etc.  And if one of those companies goes out of business, then you're screwed... but come-on... of all of the companies named, GOG and Steam are the "smallest" financially, and they've been around for a long time.  Google is not about to go out of business.

Plus, the 1080p 60fps version of Stadia is free... as in no cost.  So, all I would have to do is buy the game I want to play, and then I can play it for no additional fees.  Even if the game is $59.95, I can stream it anywhere.

So.  Linux gamers, especially Ubuntu ones, please chime in on this.  Is this a good idea, or am I just looking at this through rose colored glasses?
