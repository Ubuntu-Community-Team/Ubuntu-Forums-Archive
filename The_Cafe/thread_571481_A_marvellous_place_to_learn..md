### Post by marxman47 on 2007-10-09
Hello everyone.

I've not been a forum member here for very long but I have, in that time, received an enormous amount of help from all sorts of good people.  

I have recently and successfully installed Ubuntu on a separate Hard Disk Drive and removed my old Ubuntu install from my Windows Disk.

It now seems that I have the best of both worlds!  I have been a Windows user since 3.1 in the days of 386 SX computers with 40 MB - not a typo - 40 MB Hard Disk and have learned the little I know from Windows and a little bit of DOS.

At the risk of controversy, I always thought that XP was the best thing since the last best thing, but I didn't feel like buying a new PC just to run an OS which seems to have all its teething troubles to come, so I tried Ubuntu and I was amazed at the OS and its applications.

I am using Windows less and less and Ubuntu more and more, but I won't trash Windows, here or anywhere else.  Comparisons are, after all, odorous - or something.  For example, I like to tickle my digital photographs and I find The Gimp too much and F-spot and gThumbs too little and I'm getting too old to learn too many new tricks, so I use PSP 8 in Windows, just as I did before.

The Open Office suite is a wonderful piece of software, feeling and looking not unlike a MS product from a year or two ago and much better than the Works which came with my machine.

I am glad to have found Ubuntu, I have a lot to learn and I would like to say hello and thank-you to everyone here.

Best regards, marxman47.
