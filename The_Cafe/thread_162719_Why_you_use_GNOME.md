### Post by aysiu on 2006-04-19
I use both Gnome and KDE, and I like them both. I find myself often switching between the two at whim.

I could go on and on about what I like about KDE, but since you asked about Gnome specifically, here are some things I like about Gnome:

I'm an Aqua-phile, and I have to say I prefer the Gtk and Metacity Aqua themes to Kwin-Baghira.

Theme installation in general is simpler (drag-and-drop to theme manager window or click "install" button for GDM themes) than in KDE.

It recognizes my multimedia keys (yes, I know there are things you can do to get them recognized in KDE, too, but I don't want to do those things--Gnome recognizes them straightaway).

I love *gnome-volume-manager*--it's no muss, no fuss. I've had all sorts of weird adventures with *ivman* and whatever KDE 3.5 is using for automounting devices.

Plus, this isn't inherent to Gnome as a piece of software, but you do get better support for it on these forums. Most of the HowTos and tips and tricks tend to be for Gnome, not KDE.
