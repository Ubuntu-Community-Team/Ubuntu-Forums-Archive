### Post by Spr0k3t on 2007-06-05
I have a huge list of these.  However, I will only do three.

**Lock Stock & Two Smoking Barrels** ([IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0120735/) | [Wiki](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lock_Stock_and_Two_Smoking_Barrels))
This movie is the reason Guy Ritchie is even on the map.  Four guys go in on a sure shot round of high stakes poker.  What they don't realize is 'Hatchet' Harry is a cheat and removes them from their earnings, and then some.  As with any Russian mafia, the four stiffs have a week to come up with the lost bankroll.  Brilliant plot and building points throughout.  Each of the actors are perfectly fit for their roles in this film.

**Empire of the Sun** ([IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0092965/) | [Wiki](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_the_Sun_%28film%29))
This is my favorite Spielberg movie.  To me, this movie was brilliant in comparison to E.T.  It has all my favorite elements in it... and the story gives a very realistic detail of a British youth just after the Doolittle strike on Japan.  Very powerful acting by your favorite Batman (Christian Bale) when he was a kid.  Make sure you have a good theater room to enjoy this one... P-51 Mustang... Cadillac of the sky!

**Vanilla Sky** ([IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0259711/) | [Wiki](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanilla_sky))
Yes I know, it's got Tom "I'm a space man" Cruise in it... but it's by far his best acting he's ever done.  The story of a playboy who has it all only to fall to the reality of who he really is... or is he?  There's one major plot in this movie, but it pans open to five other hidden plots.  If you enjoy shows like Lost, check this one out.  Cameron Diaz plays a fantastic insane ex-girlfriend in this one.  For those who have seen it and hated it, should watch it again and pick apart the intellectual value of it.  I did good by finding three major plots prior to the movies end... I had to have some "tech support" to figure out the other two though.  You can sum it all up in one line: "How can you taste the sweet, if you haven't had the sour?"

Other movies I would recommend are Donnie Darko, The Usual Suspects, Trainspotting, A Clockwork Orange, Boondock Saints, A Scanner Darkly, and of course El Mariachi.

### Post by urukrama on 2009-01-02
And here are some European (and Russian) gems:

**Festen** *(The Celebration)* -- [IMDB]("http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0154420/")  [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Celebration_(Film)") 
Very gripping and realistic Danish drama. The acting is fabulous. This is the first [Dogme 95]("http://www.dogme95.dk/") movie, and follows [its rules]("http://www.dogme95.dk/menu/menuset.htm") very strictly.

**Ostrov** *(The Island)* -- [IMDB]("http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0851577/") [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ostrov_(film)")
A very remarkable Russian movie, with a deeply spiritual theme. Very strong acting, wonderful cinematography. This movie is somewhere at the top of my top ten list.

**Lilya-4-Ever** -- [IMDB]("http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0300140/") [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lilya_4-ever")
Swedish director Lukas Moodysson's best movie so far. A very tragic story, based on a true case, but so beautifully acted and filmed. This movie will stay with you for quite a while.

**La stanza del figlio** *(The Son's Room)*-- [IMDB]("http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0208990/")  [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_stanza_del_figlio")
What happens to you when your son dies? The best movie on grief I know. Real and raw emotions. Great acting, especially of Nanni Moretti, who also directed the movie. I get the impression the movie is not very well known outside Italy (see the nearly non-existing Wikipedia page), which is a real shame.
