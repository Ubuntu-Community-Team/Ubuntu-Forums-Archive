What is more important is the frequency and timing of the new RAM. For example, RAM 'A' runs at 800MHz. If RAM 'B' runs faster, it will likely be scaled back to 800, which means you paid for RAM that you are not using. If it is slower (say 600), then your motherboard will scale RAM 'A' back.

Then there is the voltage stuff. That shouldn't have as much effect, but it is best to keep things close.
