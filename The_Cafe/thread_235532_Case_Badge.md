### Post by em3raldxiii on 2006-08-13
It depends on which Windows logo you've got.  Is it the bubbly one, or is it the hard flat one?
 
In either situation, your best bet will likely be to use a razor blade and carefully pry it up until it pops off.  Once it's off, you should be able to use some iso-propyl alchohol to clean off the adhesive residue.
 
Sometimes the logo is "recessed" into the case a little bit, which makes removing it a real pain.  In this situation, use just the corner of your razor blade and SUPER carefully pry a corner up.  Once you have a corner, the rest should peel up without too much fuss.
 
Let us know how it goes!
