### Post by am-expat on 2016-10-01
i This is an interesting thread and up my ally. Traveled a lot in the 70s and 80s, less in the 90s and seldom now because I have too much fun where my adopted home became.  After 87 countries visited, and liking them all, the one place I visited most often since 1976 has been St Petersburg Russia. Finally in 2002 I moved here from just above San Francisco California. Despite the western press accounts, I found it much less stressful, relaxed and highly cultured, very social, friendly and now with the drop in ruble value, it is the best bargain in Europe. I thought about New Zealand and some Central European countries as possible destinations since I enjoyed my visits so much.  Italy was a nice place to visit but like France, is overrun with tourists and that brings a lot of street crime. Street crime here is very low, I can and do walk into all neighborhoods day or night without concern which is very different between my native US where every city has regions which a stranger is best advised to stay clear of.

There are a lot of opportunities and low barriers to starting a business, although the unemployment is very low, about 3% it is still easy to find really good employees, mainly due to it being the most educated large city in the Russia which is the most educated country in the world. Every night is a difficult decision, which drama, concert, ballet, opera, jazz group, or exhibition to see, or just do dancing until 6am or meet strangers in the many English style pubs. There are 354 museums within walking distance of my home in the city center, and 11,000 restaurants.  I have never really been a city person, preferring rural areas far from the nearest neighbor on the coast or high in the mountains of California but this city feels like home. 
I would have to add that the woman are enough to make a guy forget about going anywhere else. My GF is crazy about international travel so we do go but I would rather stay "home" because is it more fun to hang out in one of the most, if not the most beautiful city in the world. 
There is a lot of interest in moving to other countries, people write to me everyday asking for advice. The number of expats spreading out all over the world from stagnating developed countries is amazing. The net migration across the US border has been outgoing for the last 6 years. A lot of Europeans and North Americans have been moving here, particularly since the cost of living has decreased so much. A pensioner on SS could live like a king. I know a lot of people who moved over the last 20 years to Thailand and other developing countries but they are not cheap to live in anymore. Spain used to be a bargain but the prices for housing increased as many foreigners moved in in the 90s and the depressed economy has reduced services, at the same time a new wave of foreign home buyers have raised prices again. This time it is not the UK buyers but now Middle Eastern and Russian vacation home buyers are driving out the cheap living options.
 The best quality of life is generally in Central Europe which combines reasonable cost of living with beautiful cities and countryside that in western Europe have been overrun, crowded and with lowing quality of life. Croatia,  for example, is beautiful, low cost, relaxed, safe, clean and stable. The fastest developing economies in recent years has been in Africa. 
I think the world would be a lot more peaceful place if everyone lived in a foreign country at least part of their life.
