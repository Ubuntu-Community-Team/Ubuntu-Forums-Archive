### Post by Luke has no name on 2010-01-31
Listen has replaced Exaile as my super-awesome Linux music player of choice. It has dynamic playlists, album info, all the cool things you'd expect from a player, BUT it has a much better library browser than Exaile in my opinion. I'm using it to manage my 7000+ song collection and it's doing well. Crashes sometimes, but as far as feature set, I like it very much.

It's a little buggy, and until Lucid comes you're stuck with terrible 0.6.2.

SO! 

```
sudo add-apt-repository ppa:listen-devel/ppa
```

To get the updated version. Crashes a lot less often.

### Post by Luke has no name on 2010-02-01
Listen does crash too often. In the 0.6.3 release, it's gone from hard crashes to simply locking up. Haha!

It is such a bother to me that I am truly, really thinking about digging through the code and figuring out what is locking to fix it. The feature-set and the UI of Listen is absolutely top-notch in my opinion; it is unfortunate it has such bugs.

I do not like Rhythmbox as much as Listen. 

1)Listen, when working correctly, feels more lightweight and clean than Rhythmbox.
2)Until Rhythmbox allows me to play music in the play queue WITHOUT POPPING THE TRACKS OFF THE QUEUE, I will never use it as my player. What a stupid thing for B not to have an option like that for the queue.
