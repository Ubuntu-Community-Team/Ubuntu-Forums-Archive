### Post by ssam on 2010-09-19
i am not sure there are any commercial available arm laptops yet.

also the even the top end arms would probably fall short of your description. ARM is a low power chip. they are mostly single core 1GHz or lower. also they are 32 bit. however this is enough for many uses, such as ebedded, servers, most web browsing. ARMs are usually paired with a DSP (digital signal processor), to do fast decoding of media codecs and basic 3D.

### Post by formaldehyde_spoon on 2010-09-19
An 'equivalent' ARM chip will not be as fast as an x86 chip (and I say that as as an unashamed ARM fan), but yes, it will be far more efficient. (and it's 32 bit BTW)

No, I'm sure what you're asking for doesn't exist.  There are a variety of poor quality, low powered ARM netbooks from Chinese manufacturers, and two decent ARM netbooks that have made it to retail: the HP Airlife and the Toshiba AC100. 
There are also a plethora of prototypes from other big-name companies, but nothing else for the public yet. 

There are some of us here hoping for similar products, but while there isn't an ARM port of Windows, and while the general public stays stuck on Windows, we aren't likely to see many ARM machines very quickly.  Unfortunately.
