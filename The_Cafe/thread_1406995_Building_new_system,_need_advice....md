### Post by Icehuck on 2010-02-14
The first thing I would do is say, "What am I going to use this machine for?"

Are you going to use it for playing video games? You mentioned you have kids, so will they use it to play games? 

Will you be doing any type of video editing?

Is this machine going to be used for random web browsing and occasionally doing word processing?

Figure out what you want to do with it and then set a budget if needed. Without that information it's tough to say if they hardware is sufficient for your needs or not.

### Post by DownTown22 on 2010-02-14
My thoughts...

Motherboard: I've never really been a fan of Gigabyte (again, this is purely personal opinion), I've always like ASUS boards until I bought a DFI last year for my media center.

RAM: Might as well go for at least 2GB, and as was previously suggested, even 4GB because it is quite cheap these days.  My favorite brands (so far) are OCZ and G-Skill - a lot of people like Corsair, but I've never given them a go.

Video:  I've always like nVidia cards - a 9500 or higher series will do the trick.  And a 256Mb or 512Mb card should be just fine.

CPU:  Intel..AMD...both have treated me very well.  My desktop runs an Intel Core 2 Duo and my media center runs an AMD PhenomII X3 with is very slick!

Power:  I really like OCZ and CoolerMaster power supplies.  They've always given me lots of air flow and run very quietly.

Hard Drive:  I like Samsung drives...again, they run quiet.

Cases:  CoolerMaster and Thermaltake make very good cases with excellent air flow.  The CoolerMaster Centurion is a great case!

After I've typed all this, I think I might have made a similar post a few months back...let me see if I can dig that up.
