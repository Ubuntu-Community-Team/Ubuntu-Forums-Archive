### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-05-22
I've just been trying Fedora 15 on a live CD and I have to say I'm very impressed.

Not with Fedora itself, but with Gnome 3.

The animations are slick and smooth even on Nouveau's experimental 3D drivers. There's a minimum of clicking involved to look at installed programs and look at your workspaces. I also don't mind missing the minimise button, because you can just middle-click a program to open it in a new workspace. Sure, I don't like the notifications system, but I've been trying for ten minutes to find something else that could use improvements to finish this sentence and I really can't find anything else I don't like.

Frankly, Unity looks like an early prototype compared to Gnome 3 and its shell. I know Gnome Shell has been in development longer than Unity, but its rewrite was started around the same time as Unity's first version was started. The level of polish is astonishing. It's really something Unity's developers need to take notice of and implement, urgently. Gnome 3 is so very slick and smooth.

Okay, I finally found something I don't like so much: The Alt-F2 run box, which is even more basic than the Gnome 2 box! Unity's is better. I can also see how I'd get a burnt finger when using Gnome 3 with a trackpad, because there's a lot of mousing to get to the workspaces. Apart from these things though, Unity has a lot of catching up to do.

EDIT: I would switch to Fedora on my netbook tomorrow, except that I can't get its package manager to work from the live CD; and I'm a bit worried of being stuck with a dud package manager and not being able to install anything or have a good selection of software.
