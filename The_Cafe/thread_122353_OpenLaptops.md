### Post by Burgundavia on 2006-02-01
In order for a project like this to really take, it must also bring in the developers. The reason you need developers on board is that it is ok to have a place that lists how well a laptop works. It is critical that something is actually done about those issues, because most users are not going to play with their laptop to get their hardware to work.

Currently the DCCA and Ubuntu are starting to work together on laptop support issues. (Amongst others). I suggest that you might want to speak the Ubuntu laptop people and their counterparts in the DCCA and see if openlaptops might be a nice neutral ground for all distros to colloborate and share code/solutions.

As a final note, if you have a piece of hardware that doesn't work/partially works in Ubuntu, please file a bug. Don't walk, run and file that bug.

Corey
