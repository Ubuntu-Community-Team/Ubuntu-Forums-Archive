### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-05-18
> **debd said:**
> Well, am a little sceptic about upgrading my system to 11.04 and have stayed back with 10.10.....

What stood out in your post were three concerns:

[INDENT]1) Security issues with Natty.

[INDENT]Every new release has some security issues. They get worked out over time. That is why some people wait a month or so before upgrading.[/INDENT]

2) Stability issues with Natty.
[INDENT]Every new release has some stability issues. They get worked out over time. That is why some people wait a month or so before upgrading. Your system may work better or worse than others with the new release. You need to do a trial run on your system to determine compatibility using a USB, Live CD, or dual booting the two releases before making the decision to upgrade.
[/INDENT]

3) Upgrade path methods.
[INDENT]You can upgrade using the net upgrade method; however, for me that has not always been successful. [/INDENT]
[INDENT]I always backup my /home folder before an upgrade no matter how I choose to upgrade. [/INDENT]
[INDENT]I tend to do a fresh install and use the /home folder backup to restore some setting, email, data files, and so on. Or set up a dual boot and copy the /home setting, email, data files, and so on, from the old release to the new release partition.[/INDENT][/INDENT]

The advantage and the problem with Linux is that you have the choice of what you do. You also pay the consequences of your choice. You need to find a way to use USBs, Live CDs, VMs, test partitions (dual boot), or test machines to test a new release to determine if it is right for you. By right for you; I mean, does it work on your system, do you like the new features, are those features mature enough for you, and so on.

For example, Natty works with most (6 out of 9) of my systems and is stable on most of my systems; however, I don't feel Unity is mature enough. Customizing Natty to use some other desktop is inconvenient or not worth the effort involved for my needs. I have chosen to stay with 10.04 LTS. That is my choice. However, I will continue to monitor the evolution on Unity and Gnome 3 by using VMs.
