### Post by sdmike on 2007-02-21
I just wrote an article to help linux beginners cross over to the linux/ubuntu world. So if any of you could read it and give me some feedback I would definetly appreciate it.

Here it is:

Linux
This thread is for people who want to learn Linux. Usually when people think of Linux they think of a technical operating system that is very demanding that requires excess coding. However, this is not always the case. Thanks to distros like Ubuntu it is now very easy to switch over to Linux. Anything you can do on Windows you can do on Linux.

Why use Linux?

Are you sick and tired of having to put yourself into debt by buying software to get the job done? Are you angry about viruses destroying your computer making you have to reinstall windows, which can be very aggravating? Are you annoyed of spy ware making your computer and internet connection slow? Are you constantly worried about Windows security? Lastly are you tired of having to buy new hardware or computers just to get performance out of your OS? 

If you answered yes to all of these then Linux is just the OS (operating system) for you. It is fast, secure, and will never let you down. You don’t have to worry about viruses and spyware because most spyware is designed for Windows.  When using Linux, you are never logged in as a Master account so you never have to worry about your system being attacked or being modified accidently. Ubuntu gives you a firewall and antivirus, so it’s comforting they are there and updated. Games, software, and tools are given to you plentifully. In Ubuntu you can add games, software,  tools using synaptic, and can also add or remove programs by installing a program called automatix. 

If you need certain programs such as Microsoft Office for work, you can still use Linux. There is a company called Open Office, which is almost exactly the same as Microsoft Office with Word, Excel, Powerpoint, Access, and more. You can even save your projects so that they can be compatible with other OS of any type or year. So your office experience will be relaxing knowing you didn’t pay $400 for it and it the files are compatible with MS Office.

Why is there more than one Ubuntu Distro?

There is Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, and Edubuntu. Ubuntu works off the gnome GUI (Graphical user interface), which is different in every version. It is basically the look and feel of the OS. Kubuntu is very popular among people who are switching from Windows because its GUI has the same look and feel to Windows without the blue screens of death. Edubuntu is more for kids which comes loaded with tools for school and a stable OS that keeps them safe but quick. Xubuntu uses the Xforce GUI which is crisp and clean. Xubuntu is a light version of Ubuntu which is designed for people who use their computer for word processing, email, and internet and that’s it. So now that you know about the different distros you can chose which one best suits you.

How do I start my Ubuntu journey?

Go to [www.ubuntu.com](www.ubuntu.com) and download the latest distro which will be a ISO file then you burn the ISO file to a disk. Then put the disk in and follow the directions on the screen. If you can read English you can install Ubuntu with ease. Once you have that done and you are connected to the Internet go to [www.getautomatix.com](www.getautomatix.com) . Once you download and install automatix, you can load important software and inputs to make your computer the ultimate media/entertainment pc. 

If I need help where do I go?

It all depends on what Ubuntu distro you chose but the main forum is [www.ubuntuforums.org](www.ubuntuforums.org) . There you can get extra information or help that will make your Linux transition smooth and painless. This is also because you have a ton of people who are willing to help you and down the road maybe you will return the favor and help other new people out too.

Well I hope all of this information was useful to you and if you think I left any information out just email me at [email]mike@urinsane.com[/email] .
