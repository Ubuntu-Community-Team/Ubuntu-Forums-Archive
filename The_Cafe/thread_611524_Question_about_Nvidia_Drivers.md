### Post by samjh on 2007-11-13
Use the method recommended by each distro.  So for Ubuntu, you should stick to the ones in the repo.  They are the most reliable: tested and packaged specifically for the Ubuntu system.

It's only when the recommended method doesn't work, that you should investigate the binary installer from NVidia.

Graphics driver updates are usually designed to fix bugs, not introduce new features.  Optimisations are few and far between.  Even when features are added, they tend to only apply to particular games or platforms, which more often than not, will not apply to your situation.  So if the driver in the repo works, use that instead of trying to get the latest one from NVidia.

### Post by FG123 on 2007-11-13
I cannot stress enough how damn nice it is to use a distro where the NVIDIA drivers update along with the kernel as they get updated through the repos. I've had too many bad experiences trying manual installs of the drivers and X complaining after a kernal upgrade that they don't work, they I try something else and I get a version mismatch and... uhg, I hate that crap.

Stick with the repos unless you have a really specific need to go manual, and you know what you're doing.
