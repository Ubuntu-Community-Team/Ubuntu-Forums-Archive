### Post by steve_pearce on 2008-06-12
Hi,

I started attending LUGs around six months ago. My LUG is based in the east of the UK. The Anglian Linux Users Group (ALUG). We are split into two groups due to Anglia being a large place. I attend one of these groups every second Thursday of the month. Its great.

The meets were not quite what I was expecting to start with. I imagined I would see people setting up machines and installations etc. These days though however, people can get a GNU/Linux up and running with little or no effort and so in most cases the "Install Fest" probably does not happen anymore.

The group do however have "kit meets". I have yet to attend one of these meetings but I am guessing that would be the kind of event in which installs take place.

Our group I believe consists of around 40 active members. We tend to meet in a good corner of a pub in Norwich. Talking about free software technologies and random items of discussion also. 

I have found the meets and general community IRC discussion to be of great benefit to my learning experience. Both in general userland knowledge and boosting my knowledge on the programming side. 

If you feel comfortable attending a local event then I would highly suggest doing so.
