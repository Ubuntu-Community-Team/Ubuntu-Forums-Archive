### Post by jackpetrilli on 2008-02-25
Absolutely yes... provided it was priced right (about $50 - $80).

My reasons for this?

1.  The Evil Empire needs a viable alternative to bring their pricing down to realistic and fair levels.

2.  Linux needs to standardize around a single standard if it is ever to move from a niche market.  Goobuntu would do that.  I'm not against the hobbyists' use of the many different Linux distributions.  They could continue to do that.  But Linux needs a "main standard" to which the general public could identify.

3.  Linux needs to appear a viable alternative to the general public, not as a power users hobby OS as it is perceived today by the gp.  The name and prestige of a big company like Google would do that for Linux.
Currently MacOSX *mostly* just runs on Macs.  Linux has to start to grab a share of the PC market, before Apple "wakes up" and makes its OS usable on *all* PCs.

Also Goobuntu would be well-served to incorporate an improved version of CrossOver, Wine, or VirtualBox, so that the migration path would not be so painful for those Windows users devoted to some of their Windows software.

- Jack
