UBUWIKI FOR AFRICA

SOFTWARE FREEDOM DAY LAUNCH

Ubuwiki Offline, a South African, open-source software package geared towards the Ubuntu operating system, has been launched. The release of the package, &#8220;Ubuwiki for Africa&#8221;
coincided with Software Freedom day (20-09-2008).  Software Freedom is celebrated around the world as a result of Richard Stallman and the Free Software Foundation&#8217;s call for free software: "When we call software &#8220;free,&#8221; we mean that it respects the users' essential freedoms: the freedom to run it, to study and change it, and to redistribute copies with or without changes. This is a matter of freedom, not price, so think of &#8220;free speech,&#8221; not &#8220;free beer.&#8221;"  - Richard Stallman.

Ubuwiki Offline is an intermediate technology solution for Africa where low-bandwidth and high-cost of internet make communication unaffordable for the majority. Ubuwiki contains a digest of information &#8211; Ubuwiki Live - to get Ubuntu-users started in the absence of broad-band Internet. It is an African solution -- a derivative of the XHTML platform created by the open-source Wiki on a Stick (WoaS) project, which exists entirely in a self-contained file, allowing users to edit and change live content to suit their individual needs.

The Indlovu Project responsible for the South African distribution, agrees that  WoaS-type digital bundles are the way to go in terms of offering computer users an alternative format that respects the needs of human-beings. Think of Ubuwiki as an MP3 for files and data -- If the only digital music available was via streaming audio, computer users would naturally rebel and find a way of saving the information offline. We believe Ubuwiki is the answer to capped and shaped data, since it allows users to edit and build pages &#8216;offline&#8217; in situations in which there is no internet readily available, and in keeping with the spirit that is the basis of Ubuntu, the application is shared by the community.
