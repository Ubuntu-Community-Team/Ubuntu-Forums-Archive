### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2011-08-11
I doubt it.

These groups seem to be effective against single servers; such as, police departments, various low priority websites, and account management sites (gaming website account managers). However, I haven't read where they are very successful in tacking down multiple servers behind load-balancers that are designed for high traffic. I would imagine that Facebook uses multiple servers behind load balancers.

They might find some Vendor Software vulnerability that would allow them to hack Facebook, or they may find that Facebook uses a centralized account management server that they could take down. 

The employees of companies and government organizations do not seem to be very good at identifying socially engineered attacks either, based on what I have read. They might be able to socially engineer an attack via an email Trojan, for example.

Nov. 5th, gives them a long time to find an attack method. Are they up to to challenge? I'll wait to see what happens if anything.
