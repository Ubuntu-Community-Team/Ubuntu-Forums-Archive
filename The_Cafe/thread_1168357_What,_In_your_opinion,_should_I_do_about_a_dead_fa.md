### Post by gn2 on 2009-05-24
There is no monitoring of the heat within your PSU, which is supposed to have a fan.
Until a fan is fitted, you should not use the PSU.

So long as you're very careful and know what the hazards are, PSU fans can be changed, but get one that is the same as the one that comes out.
By that I mean one with the same speed/airflow characteristics.

Proceed at your own risk, there are potentially fatal hazards lurking inside a PSU.

Also have a look at your house insurance, fire damage from unprofessionally modified electrical equipment may not be covered.

### Post by tom66 on 2009-05-24
If you're worried about killing yourself because of the potentially fatal charge stored in the capacitors, get a screwdriver and short out all the big blue/green radial things (the small ones are fine and can be left alone).

Replacing a PSU fan shouldn't be too difficult. It might even be easier just to attach it to the 12 volt rail of your PSU and feed wires back in to the PSU.

You can't discharge the capacitors by trying to run the computer on them, because a point will be reached where the power supply controller won't work which means that there will be nothing going to the PC and no/little discharge.
