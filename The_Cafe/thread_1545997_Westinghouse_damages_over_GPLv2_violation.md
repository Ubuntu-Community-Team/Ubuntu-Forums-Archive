### Post by kwacka on 2010-08-05
Happened all over the world, mainly in mainland Europe (e.g. D-Link in Germany), but this is the first time that a US court has tried a case involving the a GPL - as such the license now has legal standing in the USA.

Previous cases in the USA have 'reached a settlement" before reaching courts - e.g. Cisco (regarding LinkSys routers).

### Post by kwacka on 2010-08-05
Further information - 14 companies are involved in this case, all were previously contacted by the 'Software Freedom Law Center' and all either ignored them or did not participate fully. 

As Westinghouse has gone into administration, and so no longer takes part in the discovery process, this is a default judgement. Westinghouse failed to respond to either the court or plaintiffs' lawyers since march 2010

The damages awarded are 3 times the amount allowed for a single copyright infringement.

Maybe this finding will cause the other companies (including Best Buy, Samsung America, JVC America, Western Digital, Bosch, GCI Tech) to get on the phone pretty quickly to Eben Moglen. 

The Judge has stated that Westinghouse 'knowingly & willingly' violated copyright.
