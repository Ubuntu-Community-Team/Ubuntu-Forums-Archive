> **forrestcupp said:**

A non-profit foundation asking for your *support* to develop a free (in every sense) product that you can use on as many computers for as long as you want vs. a giant corporation cornering you into paying for per-install licenses for a proprietary product which you can only use on as many computers as you've paid for, only in the manners specified by its license. The direct comparison is completely off the mark.

Asking for an "ongoing" donation of $10 for a year is just another way of asking for a one-time donation of $120, the only difference being that it may be easier to pay in piecemeal for some people. If $120 is too much, you can donate less. It's not a subscription model, since it doesn't grant you a time based privilege (other than a t-shirt). Maybe that needs more emphasizing.
