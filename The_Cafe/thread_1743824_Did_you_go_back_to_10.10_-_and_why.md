### Post by oldskool on 2011-04-29
I love Ubuntu and thank all of the extremely talented and dedicated people involved in every aspect of the Ubuntu project for their time in putting together one of the best OS's available. 

Unfortunately I didn't get on with 11.04. 

I have just re-installed 10.10 because I couldn't get on with the new interface / Unity. 

There are bugs with any new OS and these will be reported multiple times. I went back to 10.10 because I can access most things in 1 or 2 clicks. With Unity, I had to scroll over the dock, wait, scroll down (which is slow) to say, the terminal, click to open it, then i would have to repeat to go back to firefox etc etc. If i needed to configure something, i had to click the top left menu and type what i needed, hoping i got it right. For a new user this is very hard, the only alternative is several clicks navigating through the more apps icon which isn't particular obvious that is the gateway to system settings etc. 

There is no logical grouping of programs in unity, you need prior knowledge of the names for a text based search. Why re-invent the wheel, menus are logical, group logically and are easy to navigate. If you want quick access to something just provide a shortcut key to a search facility or just pin the programs to a dock/bar. 

I used unity for a day and my workflow slowed right down. As explained above you have to hover over the unity dock which sometimes doesnt work. Wait for it to show, then scroll the dock for your program, open it and repeat to switch programs. 

Alternatively, in 10.10 i have macbuntu installed. I know some purists wont like macbuntu but i like the interface and it sets up ubuntu tweak and compiz nicely. 

I have a visible dock at the bottom of the screen at all times, providing instant access to all open programs. The top bar contains my workspace switcher, which i can switch between with the mouse wheel thanks to compiz? again instant access to anything. I even have the windows widget so i can gain instant access to a specific window and for anything non standard i have logical menus to navigate to. 

I'm worried about the future of Ubuntu now they are dedicated to unity. I found 11.04 made me think and slowed me down on my daily routine. The bugs will get fixed but the usability is separate. That's what worries me, hence this post. 

It's almost like there is no OS, it's all hidden behind a search box. It's like someone looked at google chromes omnibox and thought that's a good idea, lets make an OS like this.
