### Post by gnomeuser on 2009-11-18
> **RaZe42 said:**
> Well what about Maemo then? ;)

Only used, developed and funded by Nokia. HTC alone has 150 employees working on Android development, Google has who knows how many - every major vendor is doing something with Android and it's reach is now beyond phones. It's in ebook readers, on netbooks, it is slated to go in settop boxes, home entertainment systems and many other places.

The question is how do you compete with that level of development, sure Bada and Maemo can benefit from some of the advancements brought about by Android. Primarily the kernel would be a target here and all of the platforms appear to be open for developers. But still the fact remains that there is just a lot more push behind Android and they will be fighting to keep up.

Then comes the question of how easy the platforms are to develop for. Maemo just switched their gui framework from GTK to QT, a major adoption hurdle for developers. Bringing with it porting, and your target for this investment currently is one phone - not an investment that is likely to give you a great return. Sure Nokia might put out more Maemo devices but outside of them who will? Bada is unfinished but faces some of the same problems.

As for Android, it's a well defined and documented platform, you write your applications in Java (as opposed to the C/C++/python which seems popular in Maemo). Cutting down development time, and giving you a deployment surface across multiple devices (and an ever increasing number of them at that).

Add to that Nokias notably odd bi-polar attitude towards Maemo in that they also push Symbian - thus splitting their development efforts and forcing developers to write for both their stacks. 

I believe Maemo is without a future.
