### Post by Eisenwinter on 2009-10-07
I don't know what my reaction would be.

In the last ~70 years, we (the western society) have become virtually completely dependent on electricity. In the last ~30 years, computers have emerged, and slowly but surely we became dependent on them for many things.

Seeing a world with no computers or electricity at all, for someone who was born into western society in the last ~30 years, would probably be a completely shocking experience.

Computers, nowadays, run virtually everything we know in society, such as banks, stores, and even dairy farms, as ridiculous as it seems. (I'm a dairy farmer)

Going back to a world with none of these things in it would be "a very steep learning curve" to someone who has grown up with all of this technology available.

I look at my 9 year old nephew. He was born, as you can probably figure out, in 2000.

He was born into a world with fast internet, fast computers, automated "everything", and so on.

Everytime he visits, the only thing he does is play video games on my computer.

We are scared of what we don't know or understand.

We (most of us) don't know what it's like to live without computers or electronic technology.

Most of us would be very scared of this situation.

I'd have to be put in this exact situation though, to know what my actions will be.
