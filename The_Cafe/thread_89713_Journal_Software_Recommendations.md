### Post by blastus on 2005-11-13
I'm not sure if there are any programs, but I think you could use a spreadsheet. If you want to identify patterns you could devise your own classification and attributes scheme. The issue is that dreams may fall under many topics and some topics may be correlated or be subtopics of others. For example, you could have a general topic called *nightmare*. Under nightmare you could have death and then under that murder, suicide etc...

```
nightmare
   death
      murder
      suicide
```
So any dream that involves suicide involves death and is a nightmare. The issue with this is that you would need a transitive reasoner to programmatically determine that a dream about suicide is a nightmare. 

As for attributes, you could have all kinds of things like relevance (how relevant the dream was to the topic(s) you assigned it under), intensity (how intense the dream was), reality (how realistic the dream was), actor (where you an actor in the dream, where you you or where you someone else or did you observe it in third-person?) etc... You could even go further than that and try to tie it up to dream interpretation--certain symbols and subjects have certain interpretations and stuff like that.
