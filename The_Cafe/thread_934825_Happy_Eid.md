Halloween isn't pagan, it is christian. It is derived from Hallowed (or holy) evening. The pagan festival which falls on the same day is Samhain.

The "celebration" most people do these days dates back to the christian strategy of discrediting and ridiculing the pagans by hijacking significant dates. Another difference between halloween (christian) and samhain (pagan) is that while in halloween the tradition is to dress in silly costumes and demand treats as a bribe to prevent pranks, the samhain celebration traditionally involves the nice sentiment of the entire community getting together around a comunal bonfire, the flame from which is traditionally used to light torches to light relight the fires in each home, thus reminding the whole community that we are all warmed by the same flame.
