### Post by TRoe on 2010-06-24
I spent 12 years in the US Navy.

I hated it and I loved it. It's long days and low pay, but it's an experience that lasts a lifetime, and there's something to be said about the fraternity of brothers-in-arms (no matter which service you elect to join).

If you do decide to join, take everything that your recruiter tells you or promises you with a grain of salt. Your recruiter will always tell you what you want to hear just to get you to join. So, do your own research and continue to seek advice from those who have been there.

### Post by eriktheblu on 2010-06-24
Been Army for 13 years now. There are good and bad parts. It's definitely not for everyone, but I couldn't see myself doing anything else (well, I could see myself as a rock star too).

This is probably the worst time in recent memory to enlist since the forces are pretty much over-staffed. Standards will be higher, and incentives are reduced.

It's not worth doing it just for the pay, but the pay is actually quite acceptable. As a man with minimal higher education I probably make more than my peers. When I first joined, there would have been no way that I could have earned that much and had those benefits in the private sector.

Here's a little test I developed a while back:

If you really want to be in the military, join the Marines.
If you don't really want to be in the military, join the Air Force.
If you want to see the world, go Navy.
If you need money, go Army.
If you're looking for excitement, join the Coast Guard.

### Post by Sporkman on 2010-06-24
One thing that people don't realize is that you forfeit certain rights when you enlist... For example:

- You **can** be imprisoned for certain political speech
- You **are** subject to unreasonable search and seizure
- You are denied certain rights free citizens take for granted, such as freedom of movement or the freedom to wear your hair a certain way. You can be imprisoned for not showing up somewhere, or for refusing to cut your hair, for example.

These aren't a big deal for most people, but if you're the sort of person that takes these liberties seriously, you may want to reconsider. Also, you need to be able to deal with authority figures in a respectful way, even if they're jerks (though that's a good skill to learn in any context. :) ).
