### Post by xmastree on 2006-10-16
So, a friend of my dad's asked my advice about his computer. It was given to him and is currently running a messy installation of XP. Icons everywhere, a right mess.
He doesn't use it much at all, but his wife does. For typing the odd letter and playing silly games like spider solitaire when there's nothing on the TV. :rolleyes: 

They want to go online so they asked at a local shop. He told them they need an upgrade, and that since it wasn't a legit copy of XP it probably wouldn't work once it contacted MS' server for authentication or whatever.

The first bit is bull (athlon 1800/256/40GB), the second, I don't know. You can guess what I suggested, can't you?

So yesterday I went and showed them a Dapper CD running live, and explained that it's free, secure from viruses, and infinitely more configureable than windows. Shower the wife some of the silly games and she was hooked, especially when I put her windows wallpaper there (a pic of her dog!).

Now, luckily she knows very little about windows programs, so she won't have to re-learn everything. She didn't even know about cut&paste.

I think they're ideal candidates for ubuntu linux.

With that in mind, I suggested that he finds out from the isp what lind of connection they require, usb or ethernet. I don't want the hassle of trying to get a usb modem working when I know an ethernet will just work.

He needs to buy a NIC, but they're cheap these days.

Next step is to back up all her letters onto CD and start over with a fresh install and no trace of XP at all.

Wish me luck...
