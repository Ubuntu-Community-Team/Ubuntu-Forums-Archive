I'm playing around with a homemade electromagnet, made from a permanent magnet and some wire I bought. I was unaware that having uninsulated wire is an issue. Would having a short circuit cause the magnet to be any less strong with 60 turns?

---

### Post by Thelasko on 2009-04-24
> **FraggedLocust said:**
> Would having a short circuit cause the magnet to be any less strong with 60 turns?
Yes it will.  The electricity must [go around in a circle]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right-hand_rule#Direction_associated_with_a_rotation") to get a linear magnetic force.  The short will prevent the electricity from going in a circle (technically a helix, not a circle).
