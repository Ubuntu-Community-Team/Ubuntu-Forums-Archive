### Post by 23meg on 2010-09-06
Merged with a recent thread with the same topic.

Even though different people use it to mean different things, "stable" is not a word that goes with pre-release software in general. To quote myself from an earlier thread:

> Ubuntu is only "stable", in the sense that it doesn't change significantly over time, once it's *released*. And whether it's going to be "stable" in the sense that it doesn't crash, doesn't lose data, etc. at any point in your experience is very hard for people with random unknown configurations, sets of packages, needs, goals etc. to advise you accurately on.
