### Post by dagrump on 2010-06-30
It does by default enable effects if the hardware supports them. Emerald is not part of the default install, & is not worth the disk space.
The idea is to get the software out to the most folks, & to to give them a functional system, the bells & whistles are for later.
The reason I suggest working out the issues on a stable install are that we are playing with a moving target here. Even if you work around a problem today, tomorrow an update may break it.
Some times it takes the cutting edge to support some hardware,I ran into that with a mobo during edgy's days. Chipset wasn't supported till very late in development.
The long term support model allows you to find the work arounds that actually allow you to have a stable workable system, that should stay that way.
ATI is a nightmare, I just gave my ATI cards away as they are a pain in the tail to put up with. I now make sure anything I buy is either Intel or Nvidia. I still have boxes running 9.04 as they seem to work fine, it ain't broken, no need to fix it. 
I don't mean don't play with the new toy, but it is just that, a new toy that is going to break, & it will happen at the least convenient time.
