### Post by recluce on 2010-09-15
As much as I like Ubuntu, if you are looking for an extremely stable OS, you should not look at Ubuntu. Ubuntu is more about the "bleeding edge" and all releases I have used so far (Intrepid through Maverick) have been released with significant bugs still present, some of them most certainly show stoppers for some people.

If you really need ultra-stable, I would look at Debian Stable for desktop or notebook computers (even though Debian Stable is boring, compared to Ubuntu). For servers, my choice is FreeBSD (and has been since the late 90s).

I do agree though, that while LTS only stands for "Long Term Support", these releases should focus on stability beyond anything else.

What it boils down to: Ubuntu is my desktop/notebook OS of choice for the balance of "enough stability" blended with usability, nice looks and a huge number of available applications. But even so, I feel no reason to do each and every distro upgrade (I skipped Karmic and will skip Maverick on my production machines).

### Post by Sophont on 2010-09-15
Current state of Maverick feels like almost every Ubuntu version looks 3-4 weeks before release.
And there's always a batch of package updates with noticable improvements (or just debug flags removed) during the last few weeks.

There will always be some issues even after release. Not only because of Ubuntus 6-month cycle - but also because that is *always* the case for *all* platforms.

There's never been a bug-free release of any OS *ever*.

The only real question is if there's a particular problem that bothers you on your hardware on release date.

Please check with live CD before you upgrade - or wait a while and read post-release reviews.
Unless there is a killer must-have feature or some desperately needed bug fix in the next release - you don't *have to* upgrade on 10.10.10.

If you have any troubles with Maverick on 10.10 feel free to wait a few weeks or until 11.04. That version will not only have 2-3 weeks of extra polishing but 6 extra months worth of improved drivers, new features and bug fixes.

I don't get why people argue every 6 months for extra delays when they can pick any multiple of 6-month delay intervals they like. ;-)

Lucid will not stop working on 11.10.
