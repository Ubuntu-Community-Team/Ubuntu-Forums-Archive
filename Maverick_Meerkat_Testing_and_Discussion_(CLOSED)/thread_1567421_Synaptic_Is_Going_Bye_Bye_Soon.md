Example 2:

Doing that with a GUI is less than desirable and sometimes not even manageable or possible.

Just because there is a new pretty way of doing things, doesn't mean it should trump innovation in having other ways to do things.  *That* is the linux way.  Having multiple ways to do things is flexible, not embarrassing. There are definitely reasons I would use one of the methods I outlined in my day to day work with linux over the other.  If your position is the one of represented positions of the community making these decisions (because the way you are putting yourself out there right now, it sounds like it is), then I would wonder to what lengths you've really tried to push Linux tools if you think one awesome tool is ever good enough.
