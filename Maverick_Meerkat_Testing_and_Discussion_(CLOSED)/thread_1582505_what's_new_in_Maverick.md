### Post by recluce on 2010-09-26
> **Chris1274 said:**
> I've been playing around with the Maverick live CD just to see what's new, but there doesn't seem to be much of anything significantly different from Lucid. Besides the newer kernel, is there anything really interesting to look forward to that I'm not noticing?

I guess you just have to take a look at the list of new features and decide if it worth the effort to upgrade.

The new kernel is important, especially if you use SSDs (TRIM support), but can be added to Lucid through the Ubuntu kernel ppa.

My **personal** decision: add the kernel ppa to Lucid machines that have SSDs, otherwise skip Maverick on production systems. That said, my test installation of Maverick runs fine and I look forward to play around with the next alpha versions of Nutty Narwhale (or whatever 10.10 has been christened).
