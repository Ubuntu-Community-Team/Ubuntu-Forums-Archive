### Post by x-shaney-x on 2010-08-14
Well it has been said this is a recurring discussion which must mean a number of people has asked about it so doesn't that make it something that should be considered.

At the moment, the ways to get mintmenu in ubuntu:
1. Use a little known PPA that may not be up to date.

2. Find the mint repos and manually download and install the deb, get the complaints about missing dependencies and have to go back and search for them.

3. Add the official mint repo and install from that and as most people who would like mintmenu have been mint users at some point I would guess this is the most likely route people would take.
That of course leaves the danger of forgetting to disable the repo afterwards meaning the user will end up with a hybrid mint/ubuntu install when they upgrade.
and since the mint repos won't be pinned this could lead to more serious problems at some point.

I know at least one dev mailing list I was following some time ago where someone was proposing mintmenu and the other people in the list dismissed it out of hand because "we don't need another menu".
Looking at synaptic I don't see that many different menus in the repos yet how many web browsers, media players, photo managers, email clients etc are there?
