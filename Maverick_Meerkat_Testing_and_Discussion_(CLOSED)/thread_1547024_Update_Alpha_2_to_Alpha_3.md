### Post by jerrylamos on 2010-08-06
Yes, but you don't test any of the changes in the install process.  

If you just want to run, do the update.

If you want to see if there's been a bug in install, then do that.

My general impression is a fresh install uses less space than an update, but I haven't tested that.

Over the various Alpha's and Beta's and RC's, there have been LOTS of problems in install.  Better we find them than the general public.

Jerry
