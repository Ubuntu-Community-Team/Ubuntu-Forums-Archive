### Post by seeker5528 on 2010-07-17
On a fresh boot, KDE session, only KDE system monitor open. System monitor show 310 MB of RAM being used. 

There is no reason for a KDE session to require more than 1 GB or RAM unless you have really piled up on the stuff that starts in your session, I would guess it would take more than your normal widgets and things you would add to the desktop or the panel.

Generally speaking, the desktop you run seems to be less of a factor than the applications you run.

My test case is to open Synaptic, open Firefox, start some updates or other things installing in Synaptic, browse the net while the installations are in progress. If the system bogs down so much that browsing becomes really slow, it's not enough RAM.

For my own use, 512 MB is what I consider the minimum, but anything less than 768 and it's too easy to hit the ceiling, even with Lubuntu if you are not limiting yourself to the stuff that's part of the stock Lubuntu installation. 

Later, Seeker
