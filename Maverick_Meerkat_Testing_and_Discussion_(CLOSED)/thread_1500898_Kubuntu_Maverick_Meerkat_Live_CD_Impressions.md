### Post by PGHammer on 2010-06-03
I'm actually going to (for only the second time that I can recall) recommend an *alpha* of a 'buntu (and Kubuntu, at that) for daily use; and it's Maverick Meerkat alpha 1, at that.  (The only other alpha of Kubuntu I ever recommended was alpha 2 of Karmic Koala.)

First up is my impressions of KDE itself.  It's not 4.4.4 (which just came out for Lucid), but the 4.5 beta (which is going to be the base of Kubuntu MM).  Apparently, it's taking some pointers from the Netbook Remix (and GNOME), because there is now both a top and bottom bar by default (again, if you are familiar with Ubuntu or the Netbook Remix, you'll recognize it).  Unlike GNOME, the two bars are identical (except that there is a ShowDesktop widget on the bottom bar, which didn't load with alpha 1).  I happen to think this is a nice touch (especially for those that have multiple users, and/or multiple 'buntus; the learning curve is now greatly eased).  I'm typing this from Konqueror (while the x64 live CD is installing in the background); the mere fact that I can do so speaks volumes about how stable Konqueror is.  (Previous versions of Konqueror had serious issues with stability; so much so, that before Firefox was fixed in Lucid, I retreated all the way to *Opera*.  Yes; Konqueror was that bad.)

Another un-surprise; unlike Lucid, MM *is* a bit slower at this stage with my AMD HD5xxx GPU.  In fact, partition formatting is much slower.  However, the jury is just compiling a preliminary finding (this is alpha 1, after all).

### Post by bennachie on 2010-06-04
Kubuntu NBR doesn't really have two panels - it would be closer to describe the NBR setup as a single traditional KDE panel at the bottom of the screen and a MacOSX (and Windows 7 copy) dock near the top of the screen, which conveniently disappears when you need the real estate. This is actually a very good arrangement, much better than the clunky Ubuntu NBR setup.

However, I can't really see the point of the duplication evident in my recently installed Kubuntu 10.10 Alpha 1 system, especially when screen real estate is limited. Actually, until I read your post, I assumed that the decision had been taken to move the panel to the top of the screen, for the kinds of reasons you mentioned, but that, given the very early stage of development, the bottom panel was still hanging around. I've no particular objections to the switch to a top panel if that's what's intended - maybe the grand plan is to reverse the Kubuntu NBR setup and make life even easier for those transferring from MacOSX!!

Apart from this oddity, Kubuntu 10.10 Alpha 1 does indeed seem to run well.
