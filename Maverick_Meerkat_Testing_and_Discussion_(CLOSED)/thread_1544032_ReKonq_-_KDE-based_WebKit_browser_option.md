### Post by PGHammer on 2010-08-01
And we have yet another WebKit-based browser for KDE (as if Arora wasn't basic enough); ReKonq looks like what it sounds like (a WebKit-based version of Konqueror, which is KDE's default browser, and the basis for the Dolphin file manager/browser).  It has actually replaced Arora (but not Konqueror) as the KDE optional browser.  If anything, *Konqueror* is in trouble (however, Arora was a threat to Konqueror); all Arora and ReKonq hammer home is just how needlessly slow Konqueror is (especially when Firefox 3.6.x waxes it, even when loaded down with add-ons and plug-ins).

How many of you testing Maverick have tried ReKonq?

I'm curious about your impressions (in particular, how it compares to Arora, Konqueror, and to Firefox).

I happen to like ReKonq (and Arora as well) as both are good basic browsers (and being WebKit based, they are fast as heck).  While neither is a threat to Firefox (plug-ins and add-ons) both have banished Opera firmly to also-ran status.  (I'm not ReKonq's author; merely a surprisingly-pleased *user* looking for opinions from other users.)
