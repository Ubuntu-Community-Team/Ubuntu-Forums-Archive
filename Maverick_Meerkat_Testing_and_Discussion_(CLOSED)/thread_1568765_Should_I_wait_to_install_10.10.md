### Post by JohnElway on 2010-09-05
I would check out some of the Ubuntu blogs and see if 10.10 is bringing any new features that you would really want. To be honest though, new releases almost always have some bugs in the beginning so if you were to upgrade to 10.10 as soon as it comes out, you might run into some issues. I'd go with 10.04 (or 9.10) for now and upgrade to 10.10 once the wrinkles are ironed out. I've heard some people say that they always stay one release behind the most current for the sake of stability. In that case you wouldn't upgrade to 10.10 until 11.04 comes out next year. 

I also purchased a netbook a few weeks ago and tried out Ubuntu Netbook Edition 10.04. It works well and has an interesting UI compared to regular Ubuntu, but I didn't really see much of a point to it. The UI optimizations weren't as great as I thought they would be (BTW my netbook has a 10-inch screen) and there are certain features that are missing from Netbook Edition. For example, workspaces aren't enabled by default which I found very annoying. I was able to get them using the info on this site --> [http://www.wains.be/index.php/2010/06/03/several-workspaces-on-ubuntu-netbook-remix/](http://www.wains.be/index.php/2010/06/03/several-workspaces-on-ubuntu-netbook-remix/), but still why wouldn't workspaces be there by default? For fear of performance hits? Either way, I decided to get rid of Netbook Edition in favor of regular Ubuntu 10.04. To my surprise, Ubuntu was no less "compatible" with my netbook than the Netbook Edition was and all the hardware issues I had with Ubuntu were also present in the Netbook Edition. Also, speed and performance on each of them were basically the same. Unless I'm missing something, I don't see much of a reason to go with Netbook Edition unless you really prefer its UI.
