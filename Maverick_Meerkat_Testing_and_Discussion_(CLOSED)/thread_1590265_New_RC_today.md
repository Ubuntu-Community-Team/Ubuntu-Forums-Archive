### Post by 23meg on 2010-10-07
No. "Release Quality Iteration", which covers the entire post-beta period, means that period is basically dedicated to quality assurance.

Iterations, work items, sprints and burndown charts should be familiar to people who have experience with the Agile methodology, which some Ubuntu teams make use of in their own way. Work items are listed in each active blueprint, and progress is tracked with weekly reports / meetings and burndown charts, which are essentially hacks on top of the Launchpad blueprints functionality to make it useful for this process. [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WorkItemsHowto](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WorkItemsHowto) has more information.

In other news, [ISO testing is well underway for the final release]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1589593"), some post-RC images already got new builds, and you can expect more.
