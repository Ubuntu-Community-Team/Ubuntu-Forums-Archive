### Post by arpanaut on 2012-09-05
Then you will need to make the choice of of staying with the LTS or riding the cutting edge, which may bring other instabilities,

LTS are released to provide Long Term Support for users that require stability, support and predictable results through updates. A lot of this is directed towards enterprise installations which demand such constraints. If your needs are better served by the "interim" releases by all means feel free to use such releases.

Personally I always keep a LTS install in service, while using the "interim" and development releases available for most of my day to day usage. Your Choice... I prefer to ride the cutting edge, but have fallback to stable when needed.

Sometimes it is a pain in the hind-quarters, especially early in the development cycles, but is very satisfying when one finds a bug, reports it and it gets fixed. 

You need to gain an understanding of the Ubuntu development cycle... new things are generally NOT brought to the LTS.  Although with the 5 year support cycle recently implemented this may not be written in stone.
