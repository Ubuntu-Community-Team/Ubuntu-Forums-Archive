### Post by Stinger on 2012-09-16
Well folks.. Let me tell you a little story. :popcorn:
Way back in time there was a Linux OS called Ubuntu, it was based on the Linux kernel and all the nice goodies from another Linux OS called Debian.
Debian was a bit difficult to handle for the end user (me) but that all changed with Ubuntu. Ubuntu used the Gnome 2.xx DE, a well tested and powerful DE.
The Ubuntu developers developed new apps within this DE to make Ubuntu more solid and even easier to use for their users and made the changes available for Debian too.
There was a lot of good energy between Ubuntu Gnome and Debian.
Some decided to give the users even more polish and goodies, and that's how Mint came to live as an unofficial spin.

But..
One day the Ubuntu leader had a vision "I want to make an UI that unites all apps and hardware" the idea seamed brilliant but what about the Gnome DE ? Well the plan was to use compiz and make "Unity" as an extension.
Those where not the plans of the Gnome3 or Debian developers, the good energy was lost between the three and they are now going in each their direction.

Gnome seems to survive after a rough period, because many of the Ubuntu fans felt that they where let down by Gnome, that was not the case, but Ubuntu didn't give their users the complete Gnome3.

Unity is having a rough time too because it's a UI and not a complete DE therefore it relies heavily on Gnome components, but the Gnome components don't fit so good anymore and lot of good energy is lost patching up the Gnome components.
The same goes for the Mint spin.

I don't know the plans for Debian but they are not using Unity and therefore good energy is lost there too.

I wish we could make all three work together as in *old' days and not let Unity or Cinnamon dictate the way of development.
The users do not always know what they want in the future, that's why we, as users, rely on the developers ;)
Cheers !
