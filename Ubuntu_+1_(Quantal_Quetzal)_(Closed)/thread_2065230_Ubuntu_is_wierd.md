### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-10-01
A quote from this web site posted on 20th September

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2012/09/unity-update-introduces-animated-dash-price-ribbons-visual-tweaks]("http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2012/09/unity-update-introduces-animated-dash-price-ribbons-visual-tweaks")

> The workspace icon can now be repositioned on the Unity launcher; it is no longer &#8216;locked&#8217; in position. (You can even remove it too via dconf, but we&#8217;ll talk about that another time&#8230;)

To put it another way, the option to remove is a recent addition put into 12.10 beta 2. Which is just about 10 days old.

Linux has had multiple workspaces as standard for many, many years. I think that is wise that the workplace switcher cannot be removed by a simple right click and a selecting of 'unlock from launcher.'

Too many people would remove the switcher and then complain that Ubuntu only has one desktop.

Some options are for beginners. Others are intermediate and still others are experienced users only. Ubuntu has always been oriented towards the beginner. Which means limiting options.
