### Post by BobCFC on 2007-06-25
If you do get a separate graphics card I would definitely go with Nvidia not ATI because Nvidia brings out regular Linux drivers.

Office work should be fine with on-board graphics though.  It depends what you mean by multi-media but most video uses the CPU anyway.

If you have any spare cash I would get more ram but you will be fine for now, I am just a ram whore.

Erm, something is a tad wrong there as socket am2 requires ddr2 ram and you have ddr ram? That will definitely need changing as that ram is totally incompatible with that motherboard. You won't even be able to plug it into the board.

Get some cheap half decent ddr2 pc 6400, such as [this](http://www.ebuyer.com/UK/product/114721/rb/0) and you are laughing. A cheap nvidia card would be good as well, something like [this](http://www.ebuyer.com/UK/product/106578) will take anything you are likely to throw at it in linux.

course, your idea of cheap may differ from mine, but it should give you something to think about ;)
