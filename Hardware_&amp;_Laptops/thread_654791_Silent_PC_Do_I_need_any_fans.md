### Post by bonestonne on 2008-01-01
need? no.

you should still have fans though, because heat will not just leave the case magically. most conventional cases are based upon having at least an exhaust fan, a fan in the power supply, and a fan on the processor.

while you're running a 45W processor, you don't want to leave it be.  i'd suggest at least enable Cool 'n' Quiet in BIOS, it'll take down the fan speeds and CPU clock rate when not needed. you should order nice case though, Antec makes great products. you'll want to swap out the Antec Tri-Cool fans, they're terrible. you may want some Nexus fans, however Noctua came out with a new set of great 120mm fans that may be worth looking into.

since you're against noise [seeing as you don't want fans] you may as well get a better heatsink for both the processor and graphics card, but its your computer, you do the research on it. Thermalright and Scythe both make products that can help you.
