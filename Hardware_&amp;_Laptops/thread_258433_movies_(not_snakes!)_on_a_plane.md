### Post by korny on 2006-09-15
Hi all.

I'm planning to go on an overseas trip very soon, and have been copying some DVDs to my laptop (Thinkpad X60 dual core) - before you ask, yes, they are all DVDs I own :)

My question is, what are peoples suggestions for maximising my battery life while watching movies on a plane?  I know the obvious ones:
- enable laptop-mode
- minimise screen brightness
- disable wireless etc.

However, there are a few options I've thought of, but have no idea which are better or worse:

1. Put the movies on a ram disk - I have 1.5G of memory, so I should be able to copy a movie onto a ramdisk, and then play it with minimal hard drive activity.  The only problem I've found so far is that I can't seem to make a ram disk greater than 512M.  I've seen a discussion of "tmpfs" - is making a tmpfs mount as efficient as a ram disk?  What are the pros/cons?

2. Put the movies on static RAM - I have a 1G sd-ram card; I could copy a movie onto it, then play the movie from the card, thus saving some hard disk life - is this a real power saving or not?  I assume it would be worse than a ramdisk though.

3. Manually lower processor speed - somewhere I saw a suggestion that the cpu frequency scaling applet could be configured to *set* the cpu speed, in which case I could manually lower the speed of my cpus - is this a good idea?  Is this any better than the default linux power saving?

4. Booting Windows - I hate to do this, but I've seen in some discussions that Windows still has better battery management than Linux on these laptops.  Is this true?  I have a windows boot on the x60 (only for games!) so I can do that if necessary.  Even if I did this, would it give better battery life than one of the ramdisk/static ram options above?

5. (unlikely option) - I could play the DVDs from the DVD drive.  I'm assuming that this would be the worst option, but I'd be happy to be corrected.

Any other hints and tips would also be welcome.  I have about 20 hours of flying time, so the more hours of movie watching I can manage, the better :)  I know my best bet would be to *try* all of the above and benchmark the results, but sadly I'm running out of time before I leave...

- Korny
