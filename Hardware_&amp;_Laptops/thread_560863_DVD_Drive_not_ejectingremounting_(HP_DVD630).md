### Post by Kickboy on 2007-09-26
I just recently built a new computer, and I have 2 optical drives installed. One is a CD Burner/DVD Reader. The other is a CD Burner/DVD Burner. The issue I am having is with the DVD Burner (Model HP DVD630).

When Ubuntu boots, the drive is mounted and recognized perfectly. I can view the disc that is currently in the drive, and read the data. When I eject the disc (either by pressing the button or ejecting through Nautilus), the drive is unmounted, but the tray is not ejected. I know the DVD drive works because I can eject the tray during boot, but not once Ubuntu has loaded. Additionally, once I have hit eject and the drive is unmounted I am unable to remount the drive unless I reboot the computer.

I have done some intensive googling on this issue, but have turned up nothing. If it matters, my system specs are in my signature. I am running the 64-bit version of Ubuntu.


Any help would be greatly appreciated.

Thanks

### Post by Kickboy on 2007-09-26
Nevermind, I fixed it. Figures, I spent hours working on this problem and then I fix it 10 minutes after I post about it.

For those curious I simply had to force open the tray with a paperclip while Ubuntu was running. Still not sure why it broke in the first place, but stranger things have happened.
