### Post by futz on 2007-01-23
> **carverj said:**
> Am attempting my first CPU upgrade. 
I have a Compaq Presario (assuming ATX) with a 450 MHz power supply.
Of course you mean 450 Watt.

> Q1. Is it a straight-forward process for someone who has upgraded everything but mboard & power supply
Not too difficult. Be careful about static. Don't wear static producing clothing, don't work on carpet, ground yourself (wrist strap if possible), leave the box plugged in with power supply switch switched off so box is grounded but power is off to mainboard.
Take your time. Go easy on the cpu/heat sink. Don't force things if they don't want to go. See why they won't go first before applying more force.

> 2. With a new dual core AMD 4200+ , will I need to install a bigger power supply?
No. That should be adequate, assuming the box ran good with it before the change.

### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-01-23
> **carverj said:**
> I have a Compaq Pressario (assuming ATX)
I'd make VERY sure it's actually ATX before you buy - unless you are also prepared to purchase a new case & power supply if it turns out not to be.  Many vendors will use nonstandard configurations for their mobos, cases and even power supplies.  They'll be *close*, but not the same, making it difficult or impossible to use another motherboard with that case/PSU.
