### Post by #Reistlehr- on 2007-07-21
Yeah, buying a laptop new, will save you a lot of money. Building them is a really bad idea. The main point in reason is, every motherboard is specific to a shell. For example, in desktops, you can but an ATX motherboard and put it in a ATX case. Or an mATX mobo, and put it in an ATX case. With laptops, this is not the case. The errors that can be sought after will lead you into a 6 month loop, while you try to figure out what's not working. I've been in the IT feild long enough to know just to buy one, and not build one.

But if you wish, we can stop you. Make sure your video card, if not embedded, fits the motherboard, along with the CPU, RAM, Wireless. if you want a PCMCIA or a Express34/54 card, you need to decide. you need to choose how many USB ports you want and then find a shell with just that amount that will fit the mobo. Also, dont forget it will probably lead you on a wild goose chase, trying to find a working/compatable power supply/ac charger and battery after you're all done. (And it all add's up to the point you should just buy one).

But now to linux. Best bet would be either a i386(Intel), or an AMD64 (AMD Turion64 X2's are nice laptop cpu's). Make sure that the ACPI is compatable. Read around. The luckiest card i've had work right out of the box for wireless, was an Intel Chipset, or an Atheros card. Broadcoms are nice, but they take work, but then again it is linux. (i could go on and on for days). Synaptic scroll/track pads are usually 99% plug and play. A SATA 5400RPM hd is a hellofa lot faster than a PATA 4200RPM. (With SATA your /dev names will now be sba,sbb, etc, instead of hda,hdb...)

If i can think of anything else ill send you a shout.

Oh, and don't expect Standby, or Hibernation to work.
