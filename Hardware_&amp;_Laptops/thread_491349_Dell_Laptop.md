### Post by Sunforge on 2007-07-03
It would be worth posting the spec of both if you want more advice.

Sticking to general princinples:

Do you absolutely need a laptop? They tend to die more quickly than a desktop PC and have less raw power to boot. If you're 50/50 on this one, get a desktop.

If it's an ultra light laptop - check your peripheral requirements and make sure that you can fit that printer, USB keyboard, mouse, travel thingy and all the other options you were planning to get/use into the darned thing.

If it is a powerful laptop with all the bells and whistles it'll weigh a bit more. Are you planning on carrying it around that much? Heavy laptops can drive you nuts if you have to live with them on the move.

If you're convinced you need a laptop I would definitely go for the longest warranty on offer and see what you can get on accidental cover. I run a relatively big network and the number of dead laptops that come back is quite surprising. I often wonder what people do with them (apart from working on them that is). There are always exceptions to the "laptops die early" rule and you could get lucky but if you're not and it does break getting spares for laptops when they're more than a few years old is very tricky.

Oh and one last thing - the case. People always forget that a laptop needs a decent and hardwearing case.
