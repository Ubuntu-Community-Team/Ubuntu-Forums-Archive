### Post by aysiu on 2008-04-11
> **DianeHelen said:**
> Oooooooookkk sorta makes sense

so, the new Asus EEPC I just ordered comes with Debian, and I "think" its Xandros. Since Ive been playing the last day or so with Ubunto, will the Xandros feel pretty intuitive on the new lil PC?
It's quite the opposite, actually. You think it's Debian, but it's really Xandros (which is just based on Debian).

The customized Xandros that comes with the Eee is fairly intuitive, but experience with Ubuntu won't make the interface seem familiar at all. Asus created a specialized interface just for the Eee.

The frustrating thing about Xandros is the lack of reliable and large software repositories. In other words, adding more software than what comes with the default is not as easy as in Ubuntu.

### Post by aysiu on 2008-04-17
First thing I did was boot up and use the Eee for about five minutes to make sure it was working. Then I turned it off (probably should have taken the battery out, too, for safety's sake), unscrewed the two screws for the panel on the bottom, removed the panel, pushed the two holds off the side of the 512 MB stick (making it pop up), pulled out that stick, put in the 1 GB stick and pushed it down until the two holds locked in, screwed back in the cover, and then turned the Eee on again, and it was recognized.

If you've done upgrades in the past, you should be fine.
