### Post by Bezmotivnik on 2006-09-08
OK, so...the raw idea of USB-HDD enclosures seems attractive:  You can have separate drives that are semi-portable and that are used only when you need them, instead of having them running all the time in internal installations, adding to heat, noise and power consumption.

I've always felt that they must be hard on drives due to restricted cooling, however.  I bought [one of these]("http://images.amazon.com/images/P/B000AJO4J4.01-A1NDBS7YGOPBD6._SCLZZZZZZZ_.jpg") on sale last year and was surprised to find that (like most of these things) it was essentially just a tightly-sealed, unventilated aluminum box with leads and a circuitboard.  The advertising claim for this sort of device is that the heat is dissipated through the  case (presumably even when it's cheap thermoplastic) through the air and directly through the metal mounting rails which make a mechanical connection to the case.  Truth, or mainly elaborate fantasy?  I don't know, but the case at least gets **[COLOR="Red"]HOT[/COLOR]**.  Who knows how hot *the drive* gets? :confused: 

So, when it came time to find a place for a second drive, I bought [one of these]("http://www.cintreusa.com/CI-330U2-BK.asp") because it had a fan, and the drive that was going in was a new hot-running 200G Maxtor.  A fan, even one like this with a blade assembly the size of a nickle, will get enough throughflow  to cool the drive, right?  Plus it has a black aluminum casing which should dissipate a bit more heat.

[Here's a rear view.]("http://us.st11.yimg.com/us.st.yimg.com/I/directron_1911_928312398")  See those seven pinholes?  That's ALL the intentional openings in the entire case, and they're obstructed by the circuitboard.  Those ventlike slots *aren't* slots at all, but are purely cosmetic.  While the fan works, I can detect virtually no airflow nor cooling through anything but the case, which gets as hot as on the other one without the fan.

What's going on here?  Are these things just bogus drive-cookers or am I missing out on something?

Thanks for any clarification.  I've tried another tech forum and haven't received any really knowledgeable information.
