### Post by spiregrain on 2007-01-05
Well, option 1 is only really going to work if you want both screens to have the same image.  The other two options will let you have the same image, or two different images (like a double-width desktop or something) depending on how you set it up.

If you want to use a high-ish resolution with option, a high-quality splitter device will help.  In my limited experience at 1680x1050, a normal analogue cable looks horrible, and even one sold as "High Quality" produces a slightly shimmery image.   I dread to think what a cheapie splitter would look like, although maybe at 1024x768 or 1280x1024 you'd be OK.
