### Post by sergiodlc on 2006-02-14
Hi:

I have an ASUS P4P800-VM (with CPU P4 2.8 GHz FSB 800 512K) motherboard with a Maxtor 80 GB hard disk. Since I had at hand a Hoary install CD, I began installing this version. After that, I changed the repositories to point at the ones they keep in the intranet at work. I upgraded to Breeze and set up kubuntu desktop perfectly.

Once I did all this, I restarted my PC and everything went fine (with the exception of the soundcard, but this doesn't matter to me right now). I worked on this newly installed PC for the whole day and everything went fine.

When the time came to go home, I logout and left the computer on as usual. When I came back next morning the computer was off. When I turn it on it didn't recognize the hard disk. All other PC's connected to the same UPS where up and running as usual.

I have searched the forums but nobody seems to have had the same problem. I've read about some problems with ASUS motherboards but all of them regarding secondary issues. I have upgraded the BIOS to the newest available version and it keeps with the same problems.

Please, help me 'cause I'm desperate now.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2006-02-14
In the BIOS do you get to the part where it says "detecting IDE disks" or something like that, and then the freeze?  If so it may just be that the ribbon cable that links the drive to motherboard needs reseating at the disk or board end.  Give it a try if you can and it may all start up again OK.

I don't understand if it's your BIOS that doesn't detect the hard disk or only Ubuntu.

If it's your BIOS you could try to unplug the cable from both your harddisk and motherboard and then plug it in again. 
Or change cable. 
Or swap it with another hard disk and see if it makes any difference.
Or plug the cable in another port.

In other words make sure your motherboard isn't the problem otherwise you will have to replace it.
