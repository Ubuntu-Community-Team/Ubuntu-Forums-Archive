### Post by robenroute on 2007-01-07
The nForce 600 chipset is brand spanking new and I wouldn't count on proper Linux support; you might get a few things working, but no guarantees. If you want to run Linux on a computer, it's most advisable to pick your hardware in such a way that it will work, trouble free. Check the forums, not only Ubuntu, but also Mandriva, Fedore and SUSE; you'll see what I mean.

Just ask yourself one sinmple question: "what do you want your computer to do?" That's all. Do you want it to run Linux? Then don't go the cutting edge hardware route (NVIDIA 600, Intel 965); you'll be in for a few disappointments. But then again, it's your choice, but a choice you'll have to make!
