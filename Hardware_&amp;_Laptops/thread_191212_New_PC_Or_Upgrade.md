### Post by jml on 2006-06-08
While I haven't built any systems for years, the one principle I truly believe is that you can't have a power supply that is too big.  There is really no down side to a bigger power supply, and you reduce the risk of having system problems due to inadequate power supply capacity.  Problems that can potentially damage you system or cause the loss of data.  You can then keep the old power supply around as a back up unit.  Or the basis of your next construction project.  :wink:

### Post by Patsoe on 2006-06-10
Note that there is a downside to getting a fat PSU: at lower current draw, PSUs lose efficiency. So while you don't want to overload a PSU, you don't want to oversize it either... this will cost you on the energy bills.

As to the DFI board, while it is very popular and a safe recommendation, I would think it is quite expensive for these purposes (unless you plan to do a lot of overclocking, and running two videocards? - well, a nice thing about the second PCI-E 16x slot is that it could take a storage controller in the future).
