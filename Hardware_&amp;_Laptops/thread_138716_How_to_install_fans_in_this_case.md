### Post by glug101 on 2006-03-02
Screws and nuts my friend!  I did something similar when I modified an ancient 486 server to accept my super socket 7 motherboard I had.  The hardware store should have the right length and diameter of screws and make sure you find nuts that will accept them.  (Hardware store staff are usually pretty good at helping you with that)

For the electrical, if the fans do not have proper connectors (the fan I used didn't) then you'll have to know a bit about electricity.  Get an extension for the molex power cords and splice into the 12v or 5v wires (depending on fan requirements).  However, I suppose that most fans just clip into the motherboard these days...

### Post by wrtrdood on 2006-03-02
Long screws that will extend through to the far side of the muffin fan mounting hole is the best way.  Most all muffin fans have the same mounting requirements.  There is an important point to be made here as well.  Be sure to purchase the right voltage fan.  As mentioned they come in different D.C. volts but they also come in 120VAC.  I'd avoid those just because of the noise issues of A.C.

### Post by ekarni on 2006-03-02
Several things to consider:
1) Just to confirm, you're planning to install two fans side by side, right?  I see a set of holes for the "left side" fan in the picture as well, so that's good.
2) Measure the diameter of the vent hole to determine the fan size to get.  You should come out with something close to a standard size - 80mm and 120mm are the most common, but other sizes are out there too.  Measure the hole spacing and diameters.
3) Are you planning to power the fans off of motherboard fan headers, or "system power" (i.e. the 4 pin molex style that also gets used for hard drives, cd-roms, etc)?  Shop accordingly.
4)  If you're buying the fans from a physical store, measure the hole spacing on the fan to make sure it matches what's already installed.
5)  I agree with the other posters, machine screws are probably the easiest way to go.  The easiest thing to do is just bring a fan to the hardware store and try different screw sizes until you find one that fits the holes in the fan.  Get ones long enough to go clear through the fan, through the case material, and add an extra 3/8" or 1cm for threading the nut on.  Buy a minimum of 8 screws and nuts (4/fan) and 16 washers (2/screw).  
6)  Screw the fans in place, using the washers under the screw and nut.

Trust me, this is way too many words for a simple task!
