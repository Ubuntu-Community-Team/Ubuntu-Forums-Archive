### Post by neurostu on 2008-02-09
Essentially how it works is your power supply is rated to put out so many watts of power.  If you demand more then it is rated for the supply will still work just fine but it will wear out faster.  

You will not notice a performance decrease? It won't slow down or shut down if your power demands are too high, it will work until the supply just dies.

I wouldn't worry about it as a new supply is only 20-30 bucks. 

The way I tell if power consumption gets too high is if I run out of power plug on my power supply ;-)

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2008-02-10
> I wouldn't worry about it as a new supply is only 20-30 bucks.
I would spend a little more than that on a decent PSU. There's no reason to skimp on one of the most critical components of the PC.

> how do I know if the power consuption is not too high? would it simply stop turning on or would it turn on but give me a lack of performance?
It depends how much power you're lacking. If you're a little bit underpowered, your computer will start acting "funny", giving you inexplicable errors, shutting off under heavy work load, etc. And if you're really underpowered, you will have problems starting your computer. It may start some components and hang there, or just not start at all.

> i'm going to add a new sata 320Gb hd with fans to my desktop pc hasAdding a hard disk should only add avery modest power demand during normal use ( ~10W) It's the startup  surge you have to watch out for.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-02-10
If you start getting weird untraceable failures while underway, it can SOMETIMES be traced to an overloaded power supply .. the more active cooling you add, for instance, can put a drag on a power supply.  They are not that expensive when it comes to upgrading, but just watch the load.  And remember a really cheap power supply will be rated at MAXIMUM .. not cruising wattage.
I changed mine out as I added all the extra goodies as they just wouldn't hack it.  BUT, I bought higher end Enermax units and their wattage is rated at CRUISING level with the MAX being almost double their rating! (to top that off, they are really QUIET!)

BUT, if you are ok now, changing is not needed as long as the PS stays together and is viable for your work load. IF it starts running really HOT during and after a session (the Mark XX hand will tell you that), you should start shopping!
