### Post by irv on 2007-10-16
There are many people using Windows who are afraid to switch to Linux. Some questions I see are, “What if my motherboard goes out and I have to replace it? Will Linux recognize it after it is installed?” Well, just like Windows there is a chance it might fail, but with Linux I don't see much of a problem.
	Before any hardware change just use the Simple Backup, Simple Restore that is built into Ubuntu and save all your setting in your home directory and if the changed hardware give you a problem just re-install Ubuntu and restore your setting and you will be up and running again. When I do a backup I include the “/var, /home, /usr/local” and I exclude “/proc, /tmp, /dev, /var/tmp, /sys”. 
	One thing to remember is, always look for hardware that is supported by your OS. Question the people you are buying it from and also find out if it can be returned if it doesn't work. You don't want to buy something that does not work.
Irv
