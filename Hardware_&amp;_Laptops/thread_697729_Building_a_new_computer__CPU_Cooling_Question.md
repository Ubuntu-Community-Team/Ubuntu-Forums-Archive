### Post by YoungJim on 2008-02-16
There are several companies making turn-key liquid cooling setups.  I'd personally go with the Swiftech kit, as I've never had trouble with any of their parts:  Consider that I have my CPU, NB/SB, and two video cards with Swiftech waterblocks on them, and they've never been any trouble of their own fault.  

If you're going to do it, make sure you use the coolant additive (HydrX in Swiftech's case) as your system will probably get all scummy if you don't.  I don't know about any other manufacturers, but all of Swiftech's waterblocks can be disassembled with provided tools for cleaning if necessary.  I got what I wanted - peace & quiet - as with two Eheim aquarium pumps (a 1046 for the GPUs and a 1048 for the CPU & chipset) and a 120mm fan on the [radiator]("http://store.summitracing.com/partdetail.asp?autofilter=1&part=DER%2D15902B&N=700+4294924500+4294888847+115&autoview=sku") the noisiest part of my computer is the power supply..  Good times.

Truly, the whole apparatus is a big bag of overkill, I'd suggest one of the kits just because they have everything you need, and will fit into an ATX case without complaint.  -Jim

PS I've left my computer on - with an overclocked FX-55 - running F@H and it never got above 48C, even in a warm room.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-02-16
just an FYI as I just ran into this problem.  Keep your audio card as far away from any internal fans as you possibly can.  Fans generate RF (radio frequencies) and you can pick that up with an audio card and SWEAR it is fan noise, when it is NOT.  Some of the stock fans on NVidia cards are really prone to this fault!
