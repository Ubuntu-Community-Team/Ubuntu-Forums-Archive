### Post by Hop-Frog on 2006-06-08
To check for problems with a hard drive or the connection, you could seek out the manufactur's Web site and see if they provide some form of diagnostics tools for download.  The manufacturers for my hard drives (Maxtor and Western Digital), provided ISO files that I burnt to CD and booted from, that were independant of whatever operating system I was using.  They also had a version of their software that ran from a floppy disk and a version that ran on Windows or from DOS.

You should also double check that the jumper pins are in their proper places as I don't think these hard drive diagnostic tools were able to tell me when I had them in the wrong place. 

Oh, and back up any important files.
