### Post by meustrus on 2008-03-23
I'm struck by what people are saying.  Right now I can tell you that Intel integreated graphics chips are not as horrible as people make them out to be.  They can and do run Aero and compiz/beryl just fine, and will even play some games if you're not going for the cutting edge modern ones.  You shouldn't be avoiding or searching for a specific graphics chip in your price range.  Getting an ATI mobility Radeon or an nVidia Geforce Go7000 or higher might do you better, especially an 8800M (m stands for mobile), but these are pricy options only found in pricy PCs.  The X3100 is the best Intel graphics chip around right now, and it's been designed as a midrange graphics chip with power management options to keep battery life high.

What I would recommend you find is a nice Core 2 Duo computer with 2+gb RAM and as large of a hard drive as you can get.  160gb would be great in your price range.  Try to avoid the 3945 wireless card if possible, only because it uses restricted drivers, though it works fine in Ubuntu and Windows.  If you're curious about the AMD processors though, here's the difference.  The Turion processor, which is what's mostly on notebooks, is not as good as the Core 2 Duo, but it's probably on par with the lesser Intel chips.  Running an AMD chipset in this day and age will be more difficult for compatibility though.  I just managed to get the Ubuntu live CD to even load on my new AMD computer, and that's only because I used the Hardy Heron beta disk (though this probably has to do with the graphics card, nVidia geforce Go, and that's why I recommend the Intel graphics, because getting anything else to work in Ubuntu is a pain in the ***).

Hope that was helpful.
