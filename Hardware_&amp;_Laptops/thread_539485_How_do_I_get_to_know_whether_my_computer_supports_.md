### Post by fredj on 2007-08-31
We don't know any more than you do. You need to give a lot more information before anyone
can help you with this. What sort of hard disk is it, serial or PATA? What computer do you have?
Do you have space to install any more disk drives? What disk drives do you have in there at present?
Given your seeming lack of knowledge about disk drives and your computer I think you need to get
some help form someone who knows more than you. Start by going to the Seagate website and
get the specifications for this disk drive.

### Post by daveshields on 2007-09-01
Why bother with an auction to acquire a drive when you can get a brand new drive from Newegg? Visit [http://newegg.com]("http://newegg.com"), look for "hard drives," then "internal hard drives." Pay particular attention to those listed under "Top Sellers" and "Free shipping." For example, you can get the  [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822144122]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822144122")"Western Digital Caviar SE WD800JB 80GB 7200 RPM 8MB Cache IDE Ultra ATA100 Hard Drive - OEM"  delivered to your door for under $50. For about $75 you can up the capacity to 250GB.

You want items with lots of customer reviews.Read the reviews, though almost any modern drive should work well with Ubuntu.

See my recent blog post [http://daveshields.wordpress.com/2007/08/27/on-buying-and-building-hardware-break-a-leg-with-newegg/]("http://daveshields.wordpress.com/2007/08/27/on-buying-and-building-hardware-break-a-leg-with-newegg/") "On Buying and Building Hardware: Break a Leg with Newegg" for some tips of buying hardware from Newegg.
