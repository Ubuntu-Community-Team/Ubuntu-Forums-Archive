### Post by mjwood0 on 2007-10-01
This is really a subjective question.  Ubuntu works well on most current hardware but it's always best to check the compatibility list (there is a post about this stickied to the top of this forum) just to be sure.

With regards to processor and memory -- I know there are some people running quad core intel chips and they seem to work great with the 64-bit version of Ubuntu and would provide some serious computing power!  I'm currently running 512MB ram, but depending on your applications, this may be a little slim.  I would like to upgrade to 2GB and will in the immediate future.

As far as video cards, this gets a bit tricky.  In the past, NVIDIA was more supported under Linux.  Now, it seems that both ATI and NVIDIA are experiencing good driver support.  Personally, having used NVIDIA for so long, I'm reluctant to switch away from them -- but there's really no reason why you can't make just as many things work under either.  

Good luck with your system build!
