### Post by Twintop on 2007-07-23
DDR2 533MHz runs at 4.2 gbps while DDR2 800MHz runs at 6.4 gbps, or 52% faster. If you're going to be doing photo/video/audio editing, then you'll definately notice a difference. My suggestion would be buy a 1GB stick of DDR2 800 and try it out. If you don't notice a difference, then put the other 1GB stick of 533 in and have 2GB of RAM running at 533MHz.

The thing that makes a BIG difference is Dual Channel memory. It effectively doubles your throughput and doesn't cost much more than 'normal' RAM, but you have to get 2 sticks of it. I have 4GB DDR2 800 Dual Channel in my AM2 Dual Core Opteron system and even with Windows XP in a VirtualBox machine (with 1GB memory dedicated), I rarely if ever use my swap space.
