### Post by diJenerate on 2007-08-07
My company just bought three UX280p units for lab testing some mobile linux apps we are developing, and I have had a chance to play with one of these with Kubuntu Linux. First off there is a lot less not working than actually working hardware wise:

-Fingerprint reader not working yet, but I have read that some people have it working.

-Cameras work but the 'cameras' are really one camera with 2 CCDs. Right now no one has successfully figured out how Sony's windows utility switches between the two CCDs so only the webcam CCD can be used so far.

-Extra buttons (Sony Menu, Zoom+/Zoom-, Capture button) not working yet.

-WWAN module not yet working, we haven't figured out how to power it on.

-Issues with Kpowersave and the backlight. Have not been able to get Kpowersave to control the backlight levels even though the KControl display can switch it on/off so it works just have to tie it in.

Everything else works, touchscreen, sound, BT, CPU speedstep control, etc...

You can find more info [here]("http://www.micropctalk.com/forums/forumdisplay.php?f=23"), [here]("http://www.linux.it/~malattia/wiki/index.php/Vaio_VGN-UX50") and [here]("http://mozy.org/vaio/").
