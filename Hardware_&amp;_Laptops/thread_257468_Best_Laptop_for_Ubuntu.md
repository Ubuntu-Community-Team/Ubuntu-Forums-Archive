### Post by jml on 2006-09-15
I also like HP laptops.  If you order one of their business laptops, (go to the small business section on their web site,) you have the option of ordering your laptop with FreeDOS instead of Windows.  That is an OS that can easily be deleted when you install Ubuntu.  

A couple suggestions when you do purchase a laptop.  Try to get a laptop with something other than the Broadcom Wireless chip set.  Even though Ubuntu has drivers for this card, these forums are full of postings about the problems encountered getting it running.   

Also, if you plan to use a PC Card device (wireless card, modem etc)  be sure that the laptop you are considering still has a PC Card (formerly PCMCIA) slot.  Many new laptops from Dell and HP (consumer devices) come with an express card slot which is not compatible with PC Card based devices.

Also, try to avoid bleeding edge hardware, I generally look at hardware that has been out for at least 6 months.  It usually takes that long for most mainstream distros to support it.  Hope this helps.

Joe
