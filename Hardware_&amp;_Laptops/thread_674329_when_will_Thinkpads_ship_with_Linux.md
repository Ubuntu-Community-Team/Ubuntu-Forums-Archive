### Post by mperry on 2008-01-21
Its funny that this comes up and evolves all around every few years.  I remember the commercials a few years ago about the Thinkpads with Redhat Linux on them and before that Dell preloaded any of 4 different linux distributions you could want on their workstation and desktop lines and even on the Inspiron laptops.  I worked for a certain linux company that ended up doing work for IBM's Thinkpad group building out the support and also worked with Dell back then on a number of options around support.  You could go to the LInuxworld Show in New York in 2000 orr 2001 and see the dell systems running suse, caldera, redhat, and turbolinux.  We even got them to add debian back then as a supported distribution in a few ways; but their primary offerings never included debian.  

Seems that the vendors do a flirting thing every so often with Linux. When I was involved with Dell it was back in the dot boom/bust times.  About the same time with the thinkpads like the T22/23s.

Interesting memories...
