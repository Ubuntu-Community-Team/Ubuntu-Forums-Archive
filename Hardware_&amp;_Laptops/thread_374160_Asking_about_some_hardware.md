### Post by ArtInvent on 2007-04-03
I have this hardware except for the exact brand of RAM. I used two banks of 1gb each, brand is OCZ I think, no problems. Running Ubuntu Edgy 32 bit. 64 bit also works but there are enough issues with other 32 bit apps that I decided to go back to the 32 bit and everything pretty much works. Sound is good, disk drives, usb, etc. 

The motherboard: could not get the DVI connector to work which is a bummer. No matter how I plugged and unplugged, fiddled with the BIOS, etc. Also note that you cannot use the TV out while using a computer monitor, it's one or the other. Apparently people have gotten dual monitors to work with this using both VGA and DVI, but since my DVI connector doesn't give me anything, that isn't going to work for me. Also, getting the TV component out to work, you are pretty much left guessing as to what the xorg.conf settings ought to be. I have not quite got it yet.

You might look into a new board for the Athlon X2 made by Gigabyte that is one of the few boards that can run Linux BIOS. Gigabyte M57SLI-S4, available at Newegg.com. I would think that this would pretty much be guaranteed to work with the Linux of your choice, though I haven't seen any actual reports of builds with it yet. It's pretty new. Discussion at -

<http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=348418>
