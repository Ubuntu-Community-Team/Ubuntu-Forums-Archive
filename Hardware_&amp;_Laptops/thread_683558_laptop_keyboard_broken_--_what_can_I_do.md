[Not that they're cheap]("http://www.computercare.ca/product.php?productid=16510"), but in my job I am forever replacing laptop keyboards. They're pretty easy and are usually just a matter of undoing a couple of screws and pulling the board off. No need to disassemble the laptop. I work primarily on Dells, so I can only *assume* that HP's are similarly easy.

As for the external keyboard fix: if typing on the bad keyboard causes it to go squirrely on you, then an external *shouldn't* have those problems. I'm not sure how to disable the internal keyboard, but if you're typing on the external, it should be OK. Just a matter of plugging it in, I'm sure.
