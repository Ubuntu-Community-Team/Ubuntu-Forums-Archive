### Post by ltmon on 2006-10-12
I doubt you will get that to work.  I've heard about people trying it (for use in home built LCD projectors) and it involved soldering and external electronics.  It generally rendered the laptop useless for any other purpose.

As a creative solution you could either install an OS on the laptop also and use FreeNX (desktop remoting) or use the laptop as a thin client to your desktop.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FreeNX](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FreeNX)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ThinClientHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ThinClientHowto)

Note that neither of these solutions will give you any decent graphics performance, but both are quite usable for web browsing, email etc.
