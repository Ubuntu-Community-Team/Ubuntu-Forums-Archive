### Post by jml on 2007-04-24
I agree with bodycoach2.  If memory serves me, (pun intended,) the 1150  integrated graphics used shared ram for both system and video.  In that setting Ubuntu's Gnome interface may suck up quite a bit of resources and slow down your system.  Your Processor is not the rate limiting factor.  If you can add another 256 meg stick of RAM that would make a world of difference.  A site like crucial.com sells memory at competitive prices.  If you check out this link:

[http://www.crucial.com/store/listparts.aspx?model=Inspiron+1150](http://www.crucial.com/store/listparts.aspx?model=Inspiron+1150)

They are selling a 256 meg stick of memory for your laptop for less than $35.00 US.  If dollars are tight, then Xubuntu might be a better choice.  Just remember as you test the live cd's, they run much slower compared to when they are installed and run from your hard drive.

Good luck on your journey.  Hope you enjoy it.
