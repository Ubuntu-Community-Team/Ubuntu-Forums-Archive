### Post by jespdj on 2008-03-06
I have a Dell XPS M1330 and I love it! :) For the specs, see my signature.

*Does gutsy work smoothly with it (all desktop effects work, suspend and hibernate)?*

Yes, everything works out of the box, except the internal microphone. There's a patch for ALSA available to fix it. The link can be found on [Luca Gibelli's page](http://www.nervous.it/2007/11/linux-dell-xps-m1330/) about Ubuntu on the M1330. Suspend and hibernate work fine on my M1330, but I've read that other users have some problems with it. The only problem I have is that the volume of the sound is set to maximum after resuming. This is a known bug.

Note that Dell sells the M1330 with Ubuntu pre-installed. So Ubuntu is officially supported on the M1330. See Dell's [Ubuntu on the M1330](http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Clients/Products/XPS_M1330n) wiki.

*How long does it take to start up (from the time you type your password to the desktop showing up fully)?*

I don't know exactly, maybe between 30 and 45 seconds.

*Do I need the Dell version?*

It's not absolutely necessary, I'm not using the Dell version.

[i]Can I buy it and same money if I...
order it without the web cam?
without an OS?[/i]

I don't think that you can get it without webcam or without OS, but you can get it (in the USA at least - not here in The Netherlands) with Ubuntu pre-installed.

*Is there any special offers that I can take to advantage and save money?*

I don't know, you'll have to look at the Dell website in your country.

[i]How easy is it to take apart the cover? 
I have a bunch of blue LEDs and thought I'd have some fun with that.[/i]

I don't think it's easy to take apart the cover; it's not made to be taken apart by the end user. I'm certainly not going to try!

### Post by Limbic on 2008-03-06
I got an HP dv2715nr about a month ago that I'm loving with Ubuntu.  Was a great deal at Best Buy; $800 normally, picked it up on sale for $650.  Runs Vista acceptably but a little slow, does a fantastic job with Ubuntu.  As far as the compatibility end of it goes, after all the reading and such I've been doing it seems like it's much more a matter of particularly parts than it is overall brands.  The two big things that seem to have the most compatibility issues are video cards and wireless network cards.

I'd try to stick with an Nvidia video card for sure, although Intel video cards seem to work fairly well.  From what I've read, ATI still has a lot of issues with Linux but I can't speak from personal experience on that, so I could be wrong.

Broadcom and Atheros network cards both seem a bit finicky.  There are lots of guides out there, though, and just be sure that if you end up having to use NDISWrapper that you're using the correct drivers.
