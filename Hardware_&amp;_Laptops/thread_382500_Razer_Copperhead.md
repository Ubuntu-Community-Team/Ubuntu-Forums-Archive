### Post by Knapra on 2007-08-19
Even though it's been a long time since it was posted, I thought I could make a reply in case other people come across this thread.

The problem with the razer copperhead needing to be replugged upon boot is due to faulty firmware. Windows or linux (mac OSX and BSD too?) doesn't matter, the razer won't be useable until a replug most of the time.

The problem is posted [here]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/linux-source-2.6.17/+bug/74069").

To make a long explanation short:
1) Go to [Razers download page]("http://www.razersupport.com/index.php?_m=downloads&_a=view") and download the firmware of your choice. Do NOT use the firmware from the bundled CD, rumor has it that it can break your copperhead, razer also discourage it.

2)Get the firmware flashing program from the [Razertool sourceforge]("http://razertool.sourceforge.net/") page.

Note that it might be a smart thing to backup the original firmware from the mouse in case the flashing goes bad. An improper flash might ruin the mouse.
