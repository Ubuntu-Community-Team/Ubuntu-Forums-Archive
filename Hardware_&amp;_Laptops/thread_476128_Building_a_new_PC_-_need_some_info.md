### Post by gnirts on 2007-06-17
Usually, problems with Linux compatibility are with the peripherals and not the parts you listed. That being said, it would pay to search around Google with queries like "M2N-MX ubuntu" (no quotes) just to make sure. The hard drive and the mobo are the only two I'd worry about though. I'd be surprised if there were any issues however.

Although it depends on what you will be doing, it doesn't really matter anymore with the whole FSB/Memory speed matching thing. That was a bigger deal on the old P4 chips. It's a safe bet to stick with the PC2-6400. In fact, depending on the quality of the RAM you get, you may be able to overclock it to PC2-8000 speeds anyway. Just make sure you run MemTest86 for at least a few hours to ensure stability at the new speeds. You can usually run MemTest by pressing <Esc> while grub is loading.

You especially won't notice the memory speed difference if the machine you are using now is the one in the signature, its ancient! I know I went from 512MB of PC3200 to 1536MB of PC2700 on my computer a few months back, the increased capacity made it a huge net gain in performance.

Just out of curiosity, why aren't you going with a Core 2 processor? If you get a chip like the E4300 you can overclock past the performance of a X6800 (which costs ~$1000 compared the E4300's $115).
See: [http://www.computerpoweruser.com/editorial/article.asp?article=articles/archive/c0707/25c07/25c07.asp](http://www.computerpoweruser.com/editorial/article.asp?article=articles/archive/c0707/25c07/25c07.asp) (you won't be able to read the full article but you should buy the magazine anyway, well worth the price)
