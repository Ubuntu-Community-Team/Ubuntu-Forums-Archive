### Post by daynah on 2007-03-29
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesLaptops?highlight=%28CategoryLaptop%29](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesLaptops?highlight=%28CategoryLaptop%29)

Mine (a toshiba M35X) hasn't been updated since, I think, Hoary (I believe the first stable released?), but it works great in Edgy (the most recent not-beta). Remember that a wiki is not updated as a writer's job, it's updated when people feel like it; no one's job is to update that thing.

I suppose now that I'm thinking about it, I should update my laptop's section...

Chances are if your laptop used to work with an old version, it's not going to stop working. :)  And don't look up the list by EXACT model (My Toshiba M35X-S329 just happens to be on there, but I'm sure just looking at any of the Toshiba M35Xs would be fine. They all have basically the same problems).

Your laptop isn't going to have everything work. Don't expect it to. My laptop doesn't have it's acpi work, that is to say, it has no idea that it has a battery. Yup, Ubuntu knows it's on a laptop, but magically it thinks it's on without a battery and not plugged it at the same time. Common with toshibas. I fixed it with Dapper, but when I upgraded, Ubuntu got dumb again and I gotta remember how the heck I fixed it (oh yeah, I tacked my friend who works linux for a job and sat him down for an hour). So for now, I just watch the little lights on the front of my laptop. It doesn't bother me because I'm addicted to ubuntu.

Maybe it'll bother you. It's thinking of marrying someone. You think you love her. But she snores. Do you love her that much? Maybe there's a fix at the drug store... but maybe the fix wont work for her.

In the end, just try a live CD. If it works, it's gonna work on your laptop. If it doesn't work, it's not going to work on your laptop. :)

And remember, install windows first THEN ubuntu to clean up the mess. :)
