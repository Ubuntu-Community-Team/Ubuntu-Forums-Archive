### Post by futz on 2006-09-25
You should be able to just overwrite the non-working setting with a new one. 

In System/Preferences/Keyboard Shortcuts you just click on the shortcut you want changed and hit the key (or keys) you want bound to that command.

As far as I can find out there is no easy way to just click a button and have them all put back to default, so think about what you're changing and maybe even make notes so you can change it back if it doesn't work out.

When you make a change that uses an already defined key combination, there is no warning. The other shortcut just stops working. The little program that does keyboard shortcut settings could use a bit of idiot-proofing for people like me. :mrgreen:
