### Post by noctiphile on 2008-04-02
I just bought a new Samsung LCD monitor to replace an old CRT.  I found that Samsungs got a lot of top reviews.  They have a huge line of models to choose from.

The only real thing to worry about is compatibility with your graphics card.  Find out what the resolutions and frequencies are that it supports.  I just ran into a problem with an old card (about 5-8 years) not supporting the best resolution of my new monitor.  If you upgrade your card too, I wouldn't worry.

Another thing to consider is the connection interface.  There is VGA and DVI.  DVI is better and is the future, or at least the present.  Make sure your card supports DVI.

DVI and VGA have separate resolutions and frequencies you need to check, for compatibility between your card and your monitor.  An LCD monitor has *one* native resolution and a corresponding frequency.  That is the most important one to have support for.

Being green is about as simple as getting an Energy Star rating.

LinuxHardware.org doesn't even have a section for monitors.  I believe I read once that monitor compatibility isn't really an issue with Linux.  Someone please correct that if I'm wrong.

If you are interested in using the monitor for HD video from an external source (not the PC), make sure it comes with extra input jacks for that.  If you want to play videos on the PC, the graphics card is the key piece of hardware.

Hope I was some help.
