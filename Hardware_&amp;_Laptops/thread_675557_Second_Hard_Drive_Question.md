### Post by CheShA on 2008-01-22
Maybe you could get an external USB caddy for the drive to replace the one which broke? Here in the UK you can pick one up for around £10.

The alternative is to try to get your drive working internally.  It sounds as though your PC is trying to boot from the newly added drive instead of the existing drive.  This is probably because of the socket you have plugged it in to (compared to how the drive is set up).  Try unplugging your CDrom and plugging the extra drive using the cable/socket where your CDROm was, a "normal" configuration would allow you to boot like this and get the data aoff the drive.

Beyond this, you will need to learn about how to "slave" a hard drive using bios and jumpers.  [Check this video]("http://www.ehow.com/how_6030_install-second-hard.html") and [this one]("http://www.ehow.com/how_6031_change-master-slave-designation.html")
