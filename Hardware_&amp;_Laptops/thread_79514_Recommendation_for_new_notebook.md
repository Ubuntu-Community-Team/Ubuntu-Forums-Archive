### Post by Wermut on 2005-10-20
I'm planning to purchase a notebook in the very near future.
It should:
- work flawlessly with Breezy (ACPI, Hotkeys, WLAN etc. fully supported)
- be mobile and quiet (not to heavy and big)
- allow to work with many applications at one time in GNOME / KDE (so quite reasonable performance), i.e. mostly Web, Email, Open Office, Programming, DVD, Music etc. (no gaming etc.)
- cost around 1000 EUR

Basically something like: Centrino processor, 15'' / 15.4'' LCD, 512 MB RAM, DVD-RW, WLAN ...)

Whenever I find a nice piece of hardware after doing some research I find out that there are troubles with Linux.

I've spent hours searching for a suitable model; now I'll give it a try and just ask whether someone has some suggestion for me.

EDIT: Do you recommend the IBM Thinkpad R50e? According to linux-laptop.net there are problems.
