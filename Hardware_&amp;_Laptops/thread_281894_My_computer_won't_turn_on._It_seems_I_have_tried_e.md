### Post by kvonb on 2006-10-21
Firstly I would recommend testing your CPU, maybe take it to a computer shop and ask them if they can test it, just to rule that out.

The P4 Prescott you are running is a real pain in the arse, I have one.  They need a WHOPPING power supply, I run a 500W!

I recently had the same problem, the computer just wouldn't turn on one morning.  I had to replace the mainboard as that had blown, and also buy a bigger power supply.

When you buy a mainboard, make dead sure that it supports the Prescott CPU, this is very important, it MUST say that it supports the Prescott on the box, not just "should support Prescott CPUs"!

They are getting very hard to find these days!

If you were given/bought the wrong mainboard you will have the symptom you are having now, ie it won't post.

If you are 100% sure that the board is good and DOES support it, look at the power supply next, you will need AT LEAST a 400watt.

I would recommend the ASUS boards, they are still readily available from e-bay, I bought the P4S800MX, not flashy but it works and is reasonably priced.

Good luck, Kev :)

### Post by kvonb on 2006-10-21
OK, take all the components out of the box and put them on a clear (non-conductive) work bench, setup the P/S - Maniboard - 1xRam chip - keyboard - Monitor and see if it posts.

Make sure that the CPU fan is working, you did use the correct amount of heat-sink compound when you installed the CPU I'm hoping.  Prescotts run extremely hot (as you are probably aware) but I have never come across one that fried under "normal" installation yet!
