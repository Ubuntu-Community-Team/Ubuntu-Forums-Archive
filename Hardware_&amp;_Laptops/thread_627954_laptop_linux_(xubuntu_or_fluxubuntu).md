### Post by DJiNN on 2007-12-01
TinyFlux (Based on PCLOS) is nice & fast, but small & fairly lightweight.  Also, check out antiX (Which is based on Mepis - which is itself now Ubuntu/Debian based). It's really good, lightweight, fast, but has all the features that you would want. 

Puppy is good, but just doesn't do a lot for me. Fluxbuntu is also a great lightweight distro that's worth a try.  I tried all of the above, and on my old PIII laptop (600 Mhz Celeron) i'm currently running antiX M7 release and also TinyFlux. 

You could also do a Minimal Debian/Ubuntu install and just install the packages that you require. That way, you can keep it REALLY light and hopefully speed things up a bit. There's LOADS of info on these forums about Minimal installs using either FLuxbox/OpenBox/iceWM etc..... well worth a look, and not that difficult to do.  

If you want to try something that's not Debian based then Zenwalk is also worth a go. It's based on Slackware, and although it has a lot of bells & whistles, it's still quite fast even on older/modest hardware. It's also incredibly fast to install.

Just remember.... the more that you want it to do, the more it will cost in CPU etc.... :)

Have fun
