### Post by Keith_Beef on 2008-01-03
OK, so nobody reported anything bad... in fact, nobody reported anything at all.

So I went and bought one.

It works, straight out of the box, with kernel 2.6.22-14-generic x86_64 GNU/Linux.

[This card]("http://www.newegg.com/product/product.asp?item=N82E16815201008") has two USB connectors on the back plane, and a USB header on the card (note, a USB header, not a standard USB connector like on theback plane).

I bought this to connect a [Rosewill internal mount memory card reader]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16820223076"), since the USB headers on my mainboard are used up by the connectors on the front of the tower case.

So far, I've tested the Rosewill card reader with SM, SD and Memory Stick media: all work, with no extra configuration necessary. I insert a memory card, and a little icon (cute little representation of the physical card, not a generic device) appears on my desktop.

The reader also has a USB connector on its front panel; I connected an external 2.5" hard disc to it and that worked perfectly well.

All in all, I'm very happy with the hardware.

The box for the Rosewill card reader states that the device will work with Linux, that for the Koutech card says nothing... 

The documentation (well, what little of it there is) is, as is ever the case with these cheap devices, *abysmal*.

But hey! for $22 (plus shipping and tax) I got an extra 3 external USB connectors (two on the back of the tower, one on the front) plus an internally mounted memory card reader.

Not to be sneezed at.

Beef.
