### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-04-21
There are two common failure modes for laptop power connectors.  The one mentioned above is when the plug in the laptop has a problem - frequently a broken trace or solder joint - and by wiggling the plug around you are getting that break to make contact.

However, I have also often seen where the cord entering the plug gets bent/twisted/strained too much and either shorts a couple of wires together, or one conductor just breaks and wiggling it around makes the connection again.

Can you tell exactly where the problem is at?  Often, if the power jack in the laptop is the problem it'll visibly wiggle a little bit.  If the problem is the jack, then yes the only fix is to open the unit up and resolder / replace the jack.  If the problem is the cord, you might be able to cut off the old end and install a new plug.  (Assuming they used a standard plug you can find somewhere.)  If you try this, be sure to leave a little wire on the old plug so you can strip it back and use a meter to figure out which wire goes where on the plug!

Every manufacturer seems to have thier own creative way of assembling the cases, I can't help you with opening it up.  You may not want to do it yourself anyway - the power connector is usually on the motherboard, which usually means stripping the laptop _completely_ down (a royal PITA) and unless you're good at soldering, you might make the problem worse as everything is so small and packed together on these things now.
