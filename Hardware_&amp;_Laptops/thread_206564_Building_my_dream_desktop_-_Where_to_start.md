To play those games and to have a wicked crazy time, you could go with some slightly older hardware.  I don't know what your specs are now, but i can say that my system that's starting to "age" runs most games of that age bracket maxed out, with little to no noticible effects.

My system:
480 W Power Supply
ASUS P4C800-E Deluxe (800 MHz FSB, SATA Raid, 1Gb Eth, and more)
3.0 GHz P4 + HT 1MB L2 Socket 478 (just missed the LGA switch with pci-e, *kicks self*)
1GB PNY Verto 2.5-4-4-7 (lower numbers = faster response)
2x120 GB Samsung Raid 0 (Quiet as hell, love the sammy lines)
2x250 Maxtor IDE (for my movies and music)
NEC 8x Dual Layer DVD-/+R/RW (Freaking amazing)
The floppy drive
Creative Audigy Pt Pro ZS 2 (sounds great even in linux, and you can plug your guitar into it)
ATI 9800 Pro All-In-Wonder.  Runs WC3 all out, HL2 with better settings around 50fps.  (speaking of which.. where'd my direct rendering go?)

Case is personal preferance.

Cooling:

If you're planning to squeeze every drop of life out of it, use a liquid cooling solution.  I don't have that much experience in that area, ask another poster.
If you're just casual about it, fans work nicely.  Though i suggest a 3rd party video card cooler... the fans on most cards suck.


Something a little better than that would suit you more than well, upgrade to an x series equivilant nVidia (7600 perhaps) (ATI+Linux = crying), a PCIx motherboard, some bigger HDs.  Buy some older (read: not profusely bleeding edge) hardware, save some cash, buy a beer.

My computer flies nicely.  Also flies off the back of my truck too.  And still works*.

*I make no warranty that a computer can fall off a truck and still work.  Even if your sister did it.

### Post by Roger Mudd on 2006-06-30
Motherboard and process are different. You'll need one of each.
I won't recommend any specific hardware because I haven't been following developments in technology very closely in recent months. I will make these suggestions, however:

1) Take airflow and temperatures into consideration. I have a P4 2.8 Prescott. Great processor, but it gets very hot. This necessitates the need for more fans/cooling. This, in turn, ultimately leads to higher noise levels. I'd focus on getting an efficiet processor that won't bake the innards of your computer during everyday use. Also, when it comes to fans, bigger is better. 120mm fans move more air at lower RPMs, which means less noise. My office sounds like a server room as a result of my inexperience/bad decision.
2) I think most people will probably tell you to go with Nvidia over ATI in terms of a graphics card. I personally have had little trouble getting my ATI 9800 Pro up and running. But it has been a sticking point for others.
3) Check out these sites for some good information:
[http://www.tomshardware.com/](http://www.tomshardware.com/)
[http://www.ocforums.com/index.php?s=](http://www.ocforums.com/index.php?s=)
[http://www.hardforum.com/](http://www.hardforum.com/)
[http://www.anandtech.com/](http://www.anandtech.com/)
[http://newegg.com/](http://newegg.com/)
Knowing that you're overseas, you may not buy from Newegg. But you should take advantage of their site. They have a large inventory, excellent search engine and a good system of user feedback on items that have been purchased.

### Post by patrickfromspain on 2006-06-30
If you have the money, and you think you could take advantage of it, I'd go for an Asus (or Abit, or gigabyte, or whatever) mobo with the nforce4 chipset, an AMD athlon 64 x2 sAM2 (s939 wouldn't be bad, though, but "old"), nvidia 7600  (if you have the money, you have the 7900 :D) and 2 gigs of ram (or even more ;)). 

If you want something less expensive, but still fast and with an excelent price/performance relation, you could go for an Asus mobo with VIA KT890 chipset (I have one of these) and an AMD Athlon64 starting at 3500+ s939, 1gig of ram (don't go for less than 1gig) and Nvidia 6600GT or 7600.

It's all about money! Anyway, I'd pick an AMD Athlon64 over any Intel pentium, and a Sempron over any Celeron.
