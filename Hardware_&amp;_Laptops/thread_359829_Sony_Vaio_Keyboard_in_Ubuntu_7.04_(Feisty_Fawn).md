I'm placing this thread here toward the prospect that someone else might have the same problem I did.  


If you are using a Sony Vaio notebook/laptop and you are having trouble with your keyboard, here are some of the things that helped me to (finally) get the correct configuration.  I had much trouble finding the correct setting, the installation not performing it correctly.  


=======================

On your desktop, in the panel, go to ... 

SYSTEM>CONTROL CENTER.

A window will pop up.   Under the HARDWARE heading, click on  ...

KEYBOARD

A window labeled "Keyboard Preferences" will pop up.  Select LAYOUTS tab.  (Second from the left.)

The first line will read "Keyboard Model".  Click the "Choose" button directly to the right and select "Korean 106-key" from the scrollable screen.  Click "OK".

Within the opened window that is titled "Keyboard Preferences" (this window ought to automatically be on the top when you click "close" from the previous screen, but maybe not) under the heading "Select Layouts", look for the "U.S. English" layout, and check the box to the right of it to make it default if it is not already defaulted.  

If you do not see the "U.S. English" layout, click the "Add+" button which is immediately to the right.  

Select "U.S. English" from the scrollable screen.  [Note:  the "U.S. English" line is expandable.  There is no need to expand it to select the correct layout (unless you're just curious what else there is; not advisable).  Simply select "U.S. English".]  Click "O.K.".  

You will see that this layout has been added to the list of layouts under the heading "Selected Layouts".  Click the box the select it as the default setting.  

Open a text editor to ensure that all the keys work.  


IMPORTANT NOTE:  If you toggle the Num Lk key on your keyboard, you will be encoding the special characters (the "third" character on certain keys) such as the "*" on the "p" key  or the "+" on the "/" key or the 4,5,6 on the u,i,p keys.   You can encode these characters either by toggling the Num Lk key (which on my model causes a display light beneath the monitor to light up, indicating that this option has been LOCKED just as toggling the Caps Lock will do) or you can encode them by holding down the "Fn" key (to the left of the "Windows" key, to the right of the left Ctrl key) and pressing your desired character on at the time.
