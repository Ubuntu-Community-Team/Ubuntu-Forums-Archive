### Post by hardyn on 2007-04-06
what kind of yeild are you expecting?

it would be a better use of your time to find a used XP1600, they have got to be going  for change now.

over clocking is sort of dependant on your motherboard, and if it will allow you to alter the front side bus speed and processor voltage levels, either through bios settings or jumpers... name brand computers such as dell, compaq etc. will not allow you to do this... and forget it for a notebook.

The idea of over clocking is to overdrive the processor though an increase in the front side bus speeds, as the actual multiplier is fixed.  As the front size bus increased in speed, the quality of the signal is degraded, often requiring an increase in processor voltage.  As the ram is synchronized with the processor, by increasing the front side bus speed, the ram will be over driven as well... as ram is often cheaply made, it will be the first thing to let you down in an over clocking situation.

with normal cheaply made computer parts... a good overclock might be 10 - 20% so 75-150mhz more... for a whole 900mhz  and a huge loss in stability and increased heat production.  

its really is fun to play with... but you are not really going to make a slow computer any better though overclocking... if you can afford any kind of update, try to find an athlon xp or P4 motherboard processor and ram... you will have a better machine in the end.

... my opinion.
