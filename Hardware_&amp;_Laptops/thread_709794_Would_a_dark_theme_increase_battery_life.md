### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-02-27
No, the laptop's LCD screen backlight will still be on, and this is where the power drain comes from, so rendering darker colors won't save you anything.  I think this may have been more true with CRTs, but even that seems more like a myth or illusion than a useful reality.
However, dimming the screen usually saves on battery (except on my dell laptop it seems - but really, this is one of the best ways to save power).

### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-03-10
The flourescent backlight uses ~70 Volts, the actual LCD panel uses ~2.2 volts.  If you can turn down the brightness of the backlight, that will save battery life.  Whether LCD pixels are switched on or off (light or dark) results in minimal difference in power draw.

Of course, when you turn down the backlight, it makes the screen impossible to see.
