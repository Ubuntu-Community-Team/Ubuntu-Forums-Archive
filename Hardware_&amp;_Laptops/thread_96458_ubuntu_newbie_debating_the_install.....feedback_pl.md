### Post by xtacocorex on 2005-11-28
I started off with RedHat 9 and then progressed to FC2 and then FC4. I switched to Kubuntu on a whim when I got worried about my home partition size, so I put Kubuntu on, found that it recognized my 802.11g wireless card out of the box, but I put FC4 back on 7 hours later. I haven't figured out why, but maybe I was scared of change. So, the next afternoon after realizing that I have been unsuccessful at getting that wireless card to work in FC2 and FC4, I put Kubuntu back on. I don't regret that decision at all. 

The only major difference is the package management system, which I now prefer apt over yum, and the location of some files.

As for the 1 CD, once you enable the universe and multiverse repositories for apt, you have access to over 17000+ packages to install (I just got that number from Synaptic which is a GUI for apt).

Also from my check in Synaptic, the Sawfish packages are right there for installation. One question I have for you is will Sawfish work under KDE? I don't think KDE can switch desktops like you described and I'd like to try that out.
