### Post by pegasus_oz on 2008-02-21
I recently (3 weeks) purchased an Acer 7720G laptop/notebook.  I found the following
[LIST]
[*]irregular screen size - sometimes all screen is used, other times renders as 15"
[*]no driver support for DVD write - Solaris 10 there is
[*]wireless unable to establish at start up
[/LIST]

We also purchased another Acer 5720 notebook/laptop. This however uses Windoze only.  The 7720 is configured for Solaris, Linux and Windoze.

With Windoze, we have experienced problems with auto updates and re-installation of windows on both machines.  At this point, Acer Australia have actually refused any responsibility for the problem (before any changes were made on the 7720 which would be fair enough) and also refused to escalate to a manager.  

Acers point of view is that there is no problem with the hardware (diagnosis over the phone).  Our point of view is Acer sold both systems with Windows and are thus resposible for the system they sold.  The 7720 is three weeks old and the 5720 1.5 weeks old.:confused:

So be careful buying an Acer product.  Acer, in our experience, do not support their warranties in Australia.  One only hopes they do in other countries.

As for the drivers for Linux, I intend to uninstall Windoze and run with Linux and Solaris only.  I figure I can deal with two OS's with driver issues but a third is simply toooo time consuming.

Best of luck in your search.
