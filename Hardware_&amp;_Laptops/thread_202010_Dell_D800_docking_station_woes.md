### Post by sovietfunk on 2006-09-11
I installed kubuntu with my Dell undocked, followed the Twinview/Xinerama/MergedFB triple howto somewhere on this forum (Went with Xinerama flavour) and only then hooked up the monitor and did the final tweaks to my xorg.conf. 

The guide is here: 
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=221174]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=221174")

There is a command in there to regenerate your xorg.conf. I guess you should run this command without the external monitor connected, so you get a conf which is optimal for laptop use (which is of course most important). And then you can follow the guide, which means you'll have to do some plaintext editing. And possible multiple X restarts. 

If you have LCD external, I think you'll have to follow the Xinerama method. The others depend on identical resolutions on your monitors, and for most laptops nowadays I just can see this happening...  And if your hardware is relatively recent you won't have any problems with 3D performance. (The guide says that DRI won't work with Xinerama, but my Xorg log says nothing about DRI failing to load or function).

At work I have an lcd panel above my laptop, and elsewhere just the internal LCD. Works just plain excellent so far. I have not dared to try Hot Un/Docking yet, though. 

kevin
