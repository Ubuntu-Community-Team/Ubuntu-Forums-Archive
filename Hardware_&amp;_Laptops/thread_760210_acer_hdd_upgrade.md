### Post by em3raldxiii on 2008-04-20
Switching to your new HD should be totally simple, as long as your motherboard supports SATA.  I assume your existing HD is an IDE (considering it's a 5400rpm 80gb), so just make sure your motherboard has the appropriate SATA port.  

As far as heat goes, I don't forsee anything terrible - the new drive will definitely produce a bit more heat, but it shouldn't be enough to cause any harm in your case - assuming you have anything bigger than a microATX low-profile horizontal case.  If you've got a decent sized tower, and if you make sure there is a little bit of airspace between the drive and anything around it, you won't notice any severe heat issues.  Make sure to route any cables you have, and maximize airflow ... but again, this is most likely not much of an issue.

Now, if you are overclocking stuff, cramming a lot of hardware into a small space, and if your vidcard has a crappy heatsink on it, then you COULD run into some surprising temps.

Under low-load conditions, though, I don't think you'll even notice.
