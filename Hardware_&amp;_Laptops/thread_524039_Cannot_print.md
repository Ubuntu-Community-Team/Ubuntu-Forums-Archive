### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-08-13
If it won't print from either Windows, or Ubuntu, it sounds like you may have a hardware failure.

First, unplug the USB cable from both the printer and computer and plug it in again.

Next, completely power down the printer, by unplugging it from the wall power socket and the plug it back in again. This does a full cold re-start.

Also are the when the printer is powered, are the normal indicator lights on?

Can you plug another USB device into the port you are using for the printer? This will test your USB port for correct operation.

It is just a matter of elimination.

As a last resort, you could try using your printer on another PC to see if it works OK.
