### Post by Existentialist on 2008-04-18
Everything looks pretty good.  Couple things though; do you plan to overclock?  The FSB of the Intels are 333Mhz stock, so DDR2-800 would be more than enough even with an overclock of the FSB to 400Mhz+.  That might save you a little money over the 1066Mhz RAM.

Last, if the 8800GTS you are looking at actually cost $269.99 I would look at a different brand.  They should be around $220-250 now.  You can get a 9800GTX for under $300 now, which is a better deal than 270 for a GTS.

### Post by Existentialist on 2008-04-19
SLI and Crossfire are the same idea.  With 2 (up to 3 with some nvidia boards) of the same graphics card, you can run them simultaneously for better performance.  With lower resolutions like 1280x1024, the difference is nowhere near 2x the performance, and even on higher resolutions the efficiency is not 1:1, but it is a great option for combining two lesser power cards for good performance.  I have 1 8800gts now, and plan to get another soon now that prices have come down so much since these cards were introduced.

Here's a line to a review done on the new 9800GTX, it includes a single GTX and SLI'd GTXs in a few games and at different resolutions.
