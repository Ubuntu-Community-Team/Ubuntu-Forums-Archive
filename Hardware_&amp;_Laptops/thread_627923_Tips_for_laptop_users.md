I just thought we could start a thread for offering  and requesting (distro-dependant and -independant) tips for the linux laptop user.

I will try to post as many as I can think of, but support from others would be awesome.

First off I would like to say, why out of the 100s of distributions out there can't there be one specifically tailored for use on a notebook??? I understand many distros have excellent support, but why not take one, and tweak the hell out of it to maximize its battery life, customizeable touchpad shortcuts, superior wifi card support, suspend/hibernate support, etc.  It would seem only logical, but is practically nonexistent.  I would even be willing to help as much as I can in starting and developing that project if anyone is interested....

Anyways on to the tips...

-Try a lightweight window manager. less resources used = more battery life
-Compiz-Fusion has some very nice features that may not be processor-light, but can help you out when all you have is a touchpad
-Configure your touchpad. somewheres here on ubuntuforums are a few guides on disabling accidental clicks, easy turning on and off of touchpad, etc.
-Hibernate instead of suspend when laptop will be not in use for an extended period of time.
-Setup performance options to allow you to scale down processor when unplugged

Here's a few websites that contain information and software to download that may help you out:
[http://people.freedesktop.org/~hughsient/quirk/](http://people.freedesktop.org/~hughsient/quirk/)
-laptop HAL suspend quirks etc.  only for fedroa and mandriva i think
[http://samwel.tk/laptop_mode/](http://samwel.tk/laptop_mode/)
-laptop mode tools. its supposed to help increase battery life when not plugged in. may or may not be included in your distro by default
[http://www.linux-laptop.net/](http://www.linux-laptop.net/)
-find out if if your laptop is listed and guides for setting it up.

thats what i got for now, anyone else, feel free to add (or subtract if it suits your fancy)
