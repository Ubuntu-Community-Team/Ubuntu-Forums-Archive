### Post by jml on 2006-10-24
HP corporate (as opposed to their consumer) laptops are not only Linux friendly, they can be special ordered (from the web site) without Windows if you wish.  If you go that route, select a laptop configuration with an Intel wireless card and not the Broadcom card.  Broadcom cards can be really tricky to set up in Linux.

If you want a turn key solution, go to this link:

[http://www.system76.com/](http://www.system76.com/)

They sell both laptops and desktops with Ubuntu preinstalled and all the hardware configured.  I've read several posts on this site giving them good marks for product quality and customer service.  A question I sent to customer service was answered within 24 hours and I'm not even a customer yet.  I plan to buy one from them in the near future.

As for desktops, once you price out what an upgrade would cost, you may want to compare it to the cost of buying a desk top unit from the same company.  I like the idea of supporting hardware vendors that support Linux.  Just a thought.
