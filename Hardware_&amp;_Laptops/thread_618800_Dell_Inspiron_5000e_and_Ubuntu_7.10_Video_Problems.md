### Post by Eric Lander on 2007-11-20
I'm still working to get my Dell Inspiron 5000e and Ubuntu 7.10 happy together.

The trouble, as many threads have pointed out, are attributed to video issues -- in particular with the Rage Mobility 128 M3.

Here's the current situation... I boot up the machine, and I get some funky video errors on the right hand side of my screen.  Specifically, I get four vertical bands on the far right side of the screen of distortion and flickering.  In all, these four bands probably consume 1/6th of the width of the display.

So, I press Fn + F8 and change from LCD to VGA-Out.  System goes blank, and I hit those keys again.  Now, my machine is without the bands -- but it's with major margins as if the screen is a floating window on a black workspace.

I've heard about drivers from XIG -- but this is a secondary machine... I can't afford to buy drivers that *might* help me out... Hence the open source switch.

So now I'm looking for some help from the community here...  My mind keeps going back to the Horizsync and VertRefresh -- or something else in the xorg.conf.
