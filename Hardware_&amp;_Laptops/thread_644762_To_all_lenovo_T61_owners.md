### Post by jerone on 2008-01-06
I have a T61 14.1 WSXGA+ 2GB DDR2, 120GB HD 5400RM , & x3100 with bluetooth

To answer your questions:

1) Yes had the same problem with the right palm rest poping up and down. Made click sounds and jst felt cheap. This is a design flaw in the T61. What I did to fix it is I ran to home depot. Got thin double sided wall mounting tape. I then removed the palm rest and the hard drive and placed several pieces of the double sided tape under the hardrive cage where it connected and the right palm rest. Closed everything up with tape aligned and worked perfect. Doesn't flex anymore.

2) The speakers on the T61 suck. Sorry. They are not powerful at all. I guess the idea was for you to be using your headphones or extrenal speakers more. They are not very loud at all.

3) Yeap the latch is a little loose. As long as you don't play with it the battery will stay. But sometimes in when my laptop is in my bag it will hit the latch and come loose. This is yet another design flaw.

4) Not sure what you mean by expander case. Maybe you mean docking station. The docking station for Thinkpad T61 works for both 15" & 14" models. I borrowed my friends recenty and it worked fine with my 14" T61 (he as a 15").

Overall T61 is about 95% there with full Linux support. I've been kicking out bugs in the community in hopes to have the next Ubuntu release ready with 100% support right out of the box without any major issues.

### Post by nowhere@cox.net on 2008-01-25
Just to chime in, I got a couple days ago a T61 14inch widescreen. A small plastic tab that holds the right side of the palm rest to the front of the base was sheered off during installation. This cause my right palm rest to lift slightly from the lower base at the right corner. Pressing slightly would cause it to "tap" on the cage below as you describe.

I called support and they overnighted a new palm rest to me. (The new one has the IBM branding on it as opposed to the plain ThinkPad branding). I replaced it, being careful to slide the front in towards the screen as not to stress the little tabs, then pressed down from above on the clasps at the left right and center and now there is no play in the right palm rest. I also took the opportunity to replace my 1GB RAM with 4GB I bought in a hurry from Newegg (Kingston value RAM btw and it works perfectly).

To see of you have the problem, invert your notebook carefully with the screen open looking from below along the front edge.  There is a seam where the palm rest meets the base below. The left side (now on the right since you are inverted) will be very snug with no gap but as you move across, the gap will widen and be loose at the corner. 

It seems very easy to sheer these off if the installer does not get all tabs seated in their slots, continues with the installation anyway and presses down on that corner to try to get the tab in the slot. 

Have a look.

As for the Ubuntu install, it went very smooth. I have other nvidia machines so I started with the Desktop 64 Gutsy disk, booting in safe graphics mode, did the guided resize of my Vista partition and in about 15 minutes, Ubuntu was up and running. I installed the nvidia restricted drivers with no problems.  I have tweaked a bit now and everything works except suspend/hibernate, and the screen brightness. Brightness function keys bring up the OSD and change it properly but it does not affect the screen brightness. I can ctrl-alt-F1, adjust with the function keys, then ctrl-alt-F7 back and it sticks however. I have not played with Twinview yet but I hear it is bugged.

Cheers,
Eric
