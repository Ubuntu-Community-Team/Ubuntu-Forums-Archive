### Post by maxamillion on 2007-02-06
Most desktop hardware from major manufacturers in the mid-range market is supported by linux but here are a few pointers that might help:

-If you want a graphics card, buy a nVidia and before you do check the linux driver compatibility site to double check the one you want with what is supported ... [http://www.nvidia.com/object/IO_18897.html](http://www.nvidia.com/object/IO_18897.html)

-Network cards: every desktop ethernet card I have found to date functions without problem with linux, but if you want to be sure: D-Link, Intel, and Linksys are a safe bet.

-Asus, MSI, and Abit are motherboard manufacturers I have had good experiences with.

I'm sure there is plenty I haven't thought of and other community members will help fill the gap, but thats all I can think of off the top of my head.

If you want a pre-built machine that will run linux, almost all mid range pcs on the market will suffice with little or no hassle ... HP/Compaq, Sony, eMachines/Gateway, Dell, etc.

..... hope that helps
