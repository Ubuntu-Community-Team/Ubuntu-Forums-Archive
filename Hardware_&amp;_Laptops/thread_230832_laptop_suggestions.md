### Post by valnour on 2006-08-06
I picked up an older IBM Thinkpad A31 on ebay about a year ago for like $550. It's got a Pentium 4 Mobile 1.6Ghz, 256mb RAM, and a fried HDD that I replaced with a 40GB out of an old Sony VAIO I had. 

Anyways, it's a great little machine, and I've been using it constantly since I got it. I've used several different distributions on it (Slackware, Debian, Fedora), but by far the easiest to install was Ubuntu (which of course I'm using now). All hardware was automatically detected and just worked, including my PCMCIA wireless card (an IBM 802.11b card). I've heard nothing but success stories with the IBM Thinkpad line of machines, and would imagine that extends to Lenovo's Thinkpads. So yea, I'd recommend picking up a Thinkpad on Ebay that's a couple years old. Just be careful not to get screwed on shipping :wink:
