### Post by w4ett on 2007-09-16
Overclocking is a function of adjusting CPU core voltage, and clock multiplier in addition to memory and bus frequency.  You accomplish this by adjusting the motherboard setting with jumpers and bios settings.  This cannot be done by using Ubuntu...it is controlled by hardware.

In other words, your motherboard has to be capable of adjustment outside of the normal stock settings.

I recommend doing a google search for your particular motherboard referencing  overclocking, and you will probably find dozens (or hundreds) of websites providing guides to do the tweaks.

Good Luck
