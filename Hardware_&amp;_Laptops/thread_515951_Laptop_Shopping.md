### Post by digeratess on 2007-08-02
I took a Feisty Live CD to Circuit City, and they let me try it on all their laptops. (I was kind of surprised.) It didn't boot on a single one. I tried a variety of graphic controllers including another Intel x3100, Intel 950, and two different nVidias. I believe one was 7800 and I can't remember the other one. Not one of them would start the live CD. For the record, I know the CD to be good because it works fine on two older  laptops I have at home.

After I got home and read the two above replies, I did indeed check out the configs of newer machines on the LaptopTestingTeam page as well as that on the Dell laptop for sale with Ubuntu. Several of those reporting working fine, including the Dell, have the Intel x3100 video. I also checked out the Ubuntu Dell support forum here at UbuntuForums.com and was surprised that the pre-installed Dell laptops do not even appear to be hassle-free. Combined with the fact that the only pre-installed Dell laptop on offer has a 14.1" screen when I'm looking for a minimum 15.4", I don't see that Dell as being a good value for me.

My conclusion is that this is a bad time to buy a new laptop if you want to run Ubuntu without a big headache. My next idea is to go ahead and buy a Vista laptop with hardware similar to the Dell's except a 15.4" screen and plop down $250 on a year of Ubuntu support. I'm going to have to sleep on whether that's a good idea or not.

FYI, I almost cried when the FireDog guy (CircuitCity's version of BestBuy's GeekSquad or Apple's version of the Genius) asked me if Ubuntu ran on Windows XP. When I told him Ubuntu is a type of Linux, he said he didn't think Linux runs on regular computers and that I might need a Xeon chip for that. (Note to self, don't buy the FireDog support contract.)
