### Post by Roasted on 2006-09-05
Do you have another hard drive you can test? Do the usual things, such as making sure all of the cables are properly connected, blah blah. But if you have doubts on your hard drive starting to die, the only way I can think of a solution to confirm or dismiss that would be to use another hard drive and see what your results would be. 

I think of hard drives like the engine in your car. Sometimes, you just can't tell it's about to die. Other times, like in the case of my 93 Pontiac Grand Am GT (RIP), it puffed black smoke and idled very rough among its last few weeks of life. In other cases, such as my girlfriend's, her engine seemed perfectly fine. Then one day it slowly came to a stop after coasting into an ice cream parlor parking lot and never turned on again (crankshaft went). 

That's the best I can help with. Good luck!
