### Post by mike_f on 2007-02-21
I've always preferred AMD first for price-performance reasons. Check sites like anandtech for CPU comparisons, then look up the price on pricewatch.com. If you are a hardcore gamer, your strategy may be different. I've bought K6-2s, Durons and Athlons. When I bought my first Duron, I was astounded how good it was (and today, how little power it draws :).
Oh, yeah, I HATE Intel for purposely not implementing Firewire anywhere. For high speed, peer to peer data transfer like digital video or hard disk access, USB SUCKS! BTW Ubuntu NEC firewire drivers are way better than the ones that come with Windows 2000 - thanks, team!
I've installed Ubuntu 6.1 on three different systems with no CPU and little other issues - amazing compared to a few years ago.
I prefer Nvidia for the GPU, mainly after being screwed repeatedly by ATI's lack of backward and forward compatibility with important features. BTW I'm thinking mainly of the All-In-Wonder series with TV tuners but also some basic cards. I haven't bought ATI in a while so I can't speak to their 'universal' drivers like Nvidia has had for a long time.
Hope this helps....
