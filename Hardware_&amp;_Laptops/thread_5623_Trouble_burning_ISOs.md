### Post by williamroddy on 2004-11-20
Typically, I burn the ISOs I download from the lists on DistroWatch. This time, was using Ubuntu, which had been converted to the Hoary branch, with which to download and burn ISOs.

With Warty, I had been able to left-click on the ISO, select "Write to disk..." and achieve consistently-repeatable good results. This time, I had need of an additional copy of Ubuntu, because my respiratory therapist was coming to the house to adjust the myriad of breathing equipment required to keep me alive, and I wanted to repay his frequent kindnesses with one of my own: an introduction to Ubuntu Linux.

I set out to burn the CD in the same manner, on the same machine, with an ISO I'd used before a few times. When I was finished, I wanted to check to see if the disk would boot, but the disk was unbootable. I tried another CD, but got the same results.

I tried to burn another copy of another distribution I have one my hard drive, but it failed. I tried another distribution I'd also burned before and it failed.

I thought perhaps something had happened with my CD burner, a Sony internal that is not very old and has given me good service.

Since I have other systems installed on the computer -- part of the only hobby I have left, at age 63, disabled -- I rebooted into KANOTIX and used KDE, K3B, to burn an ISO of Ubuntu that I then laid aside, while  I burned a copy of SimplyMEPIS.

I booted the SimplyMEPIS live CD and it worked. I rebooted and began the process of booting into the Ubuntu CD I had made for the therapist, who was soon to arrive, and it began to perform as it should, though I stopped it before it changed my hard drive.

I'm certain my respiratory therapist now has a good working copy of Ubuntu (and doesn't know a thing about my adventure!), and I'm hoping he enjoys the experience as much as I am.

I still get the same results, with the same ISOs, when I try to make disks with Ubuntu. Though I'm running out of disks, I am undeterred and, not to make light of my serious situation, Ubuntu is now my distribution of choice . . . till death do us part.
