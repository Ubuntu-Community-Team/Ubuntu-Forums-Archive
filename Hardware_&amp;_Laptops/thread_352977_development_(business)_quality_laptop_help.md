### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-02-04
I don't do software development, but I do have to use my laptop in just about every possible environment - including very dirty, very hot and very cold.  We've used Dell Latitudes for years, the company leases them for originally three and now four years and they hold up well except for a couple of techs who abuse everything they own anyway...  (I even have some now-4-year-old Latitudes at home that I got after the lease was up, still going strong - even the batteries.)

And in the case something does break, the corporate warranty support is lightyears better than the home - I can call up, say I have a bad whatever, and it (and a repair tech if necessary) just shows up the next day no BS with troubleshooting scripts.  Can't say if that's _all_ business support, or just because I'm with a huge corp who leases thousands of machines. :rolleyes:

That said, I liked the feel of the C-series Latitudes a lot better than the current D-series ones.  They've been doing well so far, but just don't feel as robust.  I guess all the companies are getting cheap...

As to Linux support, I haven't had any major trouble with anything except the WinModems (but I gave up on them a long time ago as I haven't used a modem in years, haven't tried recently).  The built-in wireless cards require I use ndiswrapper but it works fine for me.  And I had to use 915resolution to get the proper screen res on my D610.  Dell also uses their own weird version of temperature control, so be sure to install the 'i8k' utils to be able to control the temps the fans run at.
