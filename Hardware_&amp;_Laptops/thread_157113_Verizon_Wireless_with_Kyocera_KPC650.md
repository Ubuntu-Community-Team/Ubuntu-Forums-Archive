### Post by Jim March on 2006-11-23
I got so fed up I went and bought a Kyocera KR1 external router box.  For $250 it turns the KPC650 into both Ethernet and WiFi.  Works damned good, absolutely no drivers needed and it gets that high-powered cell signal away from...well...some fairly important parts, if you know what I mean.  So call that a bonus and be done with it :).

To me $250 was a small price to pay to completely ditch Bill Gates.

See also:

[http://booster-antenna.com/index.php?main_page=product_info&cPath=35&products_id=99](http://booster-antenna.com/index.php?main_page=product_info&cPath=35&products_id=99)

(I'm only a customer of 'em, no financial interest...but the CD they create with different KR1 ROM sets and custom instructions makes it worth dealing with them.  Also: the KR1 has an advantage over it's D-link brand slightly cheaper cousin: the KR1 has GPS hardware in it which will be usable with upcoming firmware.)

I've been able to get it working with and without external antennas/booster on the KPC650, speed is unchanged from previous WinXP/KPC650 direct, and I can share the connection with others.  AND it's a quality router with firewall functions.  I've had it running with both the 1002 and 1006 firmware, no changes noted other than a nice signal strength meter in the 1006 ROM.  Router config is via any browser, go to [http://192.168.0.1](http://192.168.0.1)

Probably one of the more expensive solutions to an Ubuntu-related problem, but it gives you enough other bennies that it's worth it.  No more mutating nuts for starters...
