### Post by tristan on 2005-08-23
If you want to play recent 3D games at a decent frame rate then a 5200 might be a little underpowered. 

I've got an FX5900XT which is nicely supported (as are all Nvidia cards) easy to install and gives 40+ FPS in Doom3 (800x600 2xAA 2xAF, high detail). You can also overclock it using the latest (7667) driver to crank a few more FPS out of it.   

Since 6x00 and 7x00 cards are out, 5x00 cards are pretty much superceded so you should be able to pick up a 5900 cheaply off eBay or even at a local computer shop.

### Post by Lord Illidan on 2005-08-23
The 6600 gt is pci express. This guy wants agp.

In my experience, nvidia cards work quite well with Linux, ATI is improving support, but NVIDIA remains the best in Linux...

Now, an fx5200 is similar to what I had before.. It depends on your choice of games to play and your motherboard. If your motherboard is agp 4x, then it is useless getting a high-end graphics card, unless you want to get a new motherboard too. In that case an fx5200 will be sufficient, providing you stay away from Doom 3, UT 2004, etc.

Otherwise get an FX 5700, or FX 5900.. If you want some more power, try a 6800 gt, and you will not regret it... but you need an AGP 8x motherboard!

BTW, if I were you, I would stay away from e-bay... the risks of getting a dud in my opinion far outweigh the few pounds extra cash you spend getting the equivalent in a computer shop..
