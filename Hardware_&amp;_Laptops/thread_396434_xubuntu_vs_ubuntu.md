### Post by Gek.uk on 2007-03-29
My laptop is a Compaq 1.33 ghz Athlon XP job with 384 MB, ATI onboard graphics using 32 MB.  I tried several flavours of Ubuntu and it just wasn't very responsive.  However for the past couple of months I've been using XUbuntu.  The difference is very noticeable, in the sense that XUbuntu is much more responsive.  My advice would be to stick with what you have.  I think Xfce is much more sensible than Gnome anyway, having extensively tried both.  I'm not even going to mention KDE, I tried that as well and still preferred Gnome.  Bear in mind I'm no die hard Linux user but I would really like to be.  I'm trying to get away from Windows, not just for me but for my customers (I build PCs) who could do without the £75 Windows tax.  :)
