All ATX PSUs, provide the same voltages...the only differences are their physical size and the motherboards they are designed to be used with and the output current measured in watts and/or amps  (not voltage).  They come with a 20 or 24 pin molex mainboard connector (and sometimes both) and may or may not have SATA power plugs.

The bottom line is to examine the current PSU and buy  a replacement with the required power connectors.  As far as the wattage, I always recommend the highest rated unit that is within your budget.  The more heavily built PSUs (and consequently more expensive) have a longer life cycle and are less failure prone.

Yes antec does make a good PSU and a lot of their units are re-branded for sale by other vendors.

Hope this answers your questions.
