So I wasn't sure where to put this. It isn't a complaint or a question.

We needed to get a memory card reader for our (windows based) desktop pc- The kids mostly use it, and they have a variety of devices with a variety of card formats. So as a result, generally about 10 USB cables laying around the computer. I suggested to the teenager that she should get a card reader, so she went to Walmart and got the following:

Stratitec USB2CR25 25-in-1.

I was very impressed. Yes, it's an utterly generic card reader with one of those strange almost faux-velvet type finishes that wears off and leaves shiny spots. But not only does it mention on the packaging that it supports Linux, it specifies Linux +2.4.25 (I'm guessing this is when pnp type usb support was enabled?), AND it provides extensive instructions for mounting the disks etc if you have trouble.

Nice one.
