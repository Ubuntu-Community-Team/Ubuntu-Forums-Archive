### Post by xyphur on 2006-10-14
When your machine first powers up, head into the BIOS setup utility. Depending on your machine type, this may require pressing DEL, F1, or something to that effect (it should say it somewhere on-screen when you can press it).

Once in the BIOS, browse around and look for memory settings, they'll most likely be somewhere like Chipset or Advanced. It is possible that it will be listed in it's lesser-known format - such as 'PC3200' rather than DDR400 - but both mean the same thing.

On the other hand, and/or if that fails, and you know that your system is in fact DDR (and not DDR2 or something else), then you can still go out and buy the fastest DDR memory you can find and it will still work, it will either just fall back to the fastest speed supported by your machine, or at the very least to the speed of your existing RAM.
