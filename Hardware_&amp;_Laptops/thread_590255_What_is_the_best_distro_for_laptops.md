### Post by jml on 2007-10-24
Don't want to start a flame war because I really like Ubuntu, but I do have a bit of experience with other distros.  The one thing I found is that hardware support for laptops among the various distros is a bit hit or miss.  I would suggest that you download and try the following distros.  Kanotix 2007 Thorhammer RC6, and Linux Mint-Celena.  They are combination Live CD's and install CD's.  See if the hardware support is better with either of them.  Kanotix is based on pure Debian Etch with a few "tweeks" and Mint is based on Ubuntu 7.04, but with multimedia support and restricted codecs included.  Hope this helps.

Joe

### Post by hardyn on 2007-10-24
i have make lots of posts about this... even today.

but no, ubuntu does not get great battery life out of the box, but there is lots that can be done... well a little anyway.  enable laptop-mode, it will spin down the hard disk and govern the cpu while on battery.  try to use the proprietary video drivers, i have found the open source drivers to have truly horrible power performance.

and i cant really speak for the media keys... although i have seen guides on this web site for manually mapping the keys.

I don't think it is just ubuntu that suffers from these problems.
