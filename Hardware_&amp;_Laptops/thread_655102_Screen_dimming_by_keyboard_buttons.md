### Post by emptythevoid on 2008-01-07
I suppose laptops can handle the backlight issue differently, but in my experience, the backlight percentage is preset within the laptop itself.  Here's why I think this is the case:  I have a Dell Inspiron 6000 and if I go into the BIOS Setup, there's a page there that'll say, here's the level you've set for brightness under AC and for Battery.  There are (I think) seven different levels of brightness on my laptop (which I've always controlled using the Fn keyboard shortcuts).  I'm not saying this is definitely your case, but this has been my experience.

As an aside, this also means that whether I'm booting Ubuntu or Windows, the backlight rules apply.  So if I'm running on battery and I'm using Ubuntu and I use the keyboard shortcut to lower the backlight all the way down, and then restart the computer into Windows, the backlight should still be all the way down (if I'm still running on battery). 

That being said, there may be a program to override the backlight controls, but I cannot say for certain (and it may be dependent on your laptop model).
