Hey everyone,

to improve the portability of my Dell C600, I have a "Latitude C/Port Advanced Port Replicator" ([http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/acc/latcport/en/index.htm](http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/acc/latcport/en/index.htm)) in school and at home. This C series dock features a headphone jack, which unfortunately does not seem to work on Ubuntu Dapper.
This is how it should behave:
1. By default, use the laptop's speakers to play music.
2. In case headphones are attached to the laptop, disable the laptop's speakers and use the headphones (duh).
3. If headphones are attached to the dock, disable the laptop's speaker and the laptop's built-in headphone jack.

Until now, I was not even aware that this behaviour is software-conntrolled -- I figured some small sensor-like metal pieces take care of what jack is used...

Anyways, on Win XP everything works the way it is supposed to, while Ubuntu does not seem to "know" about the Dock's jack... (it only takes care about the first two steps that I mentioned above).
