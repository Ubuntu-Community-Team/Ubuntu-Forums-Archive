### Post by Existentialist on 2008-04-11
Will you be doing any gaming with the machine?  And by 2-3 virtual machines, do you mean at the same time?  Or do you just need enough hd space and ram to support 1 at a time?

If you want multiple virtual machines running at once, you will probably want to look in to a quad-core with at least 4gb of ram.  Otherwise the dual-core intels like the e8400 are a very good value.  

As for the motherboard, anything that supports the new intel 45nm cpus and ddr2 is probably the best option.  With ddr2 prices as low as they are now, you can easily get 8gb of ram for less than 4gb of ddr3.

For the graphics card, it really depends on whether or not you intend to do any gaming or graphically intensive stuff.  The gpu will not affect the virtual machines, so since this is all you mentioned, it's hard to recommend anything.

### Post by Existentialist on 2008-04-11
Well for the cpu, since the new 45nm Intel quads have been released recently, the price on the older q6600 have been dropping to the low $200 range which is a good deal.  These cpus will be supported in just about any LGA775 motherboard out there.

As I have always liked nvidia's chipsets and graphics cards, my recommendations usually are for boards using there chips.  Don't let this deter you from considering the other options though.  For boards, I think the 650i chipset boards are a great price/performance value.  They use DDR2 which is very cheap right now.  They all support up to 8gb of ram so you can run quite a bit if you need it for your virtual machines.  I was actually looking at newegg and found this EVGA 650i board on sale:
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813188017](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813188017)
Great value for a good company if you are considering purchasing soon.  No need to go above the 650i unless you are looking to overclock, or run multiple gpus.

As for the graphics card, anything from the 7 series on up from nvidia will do dual displays fine.  The 8 series will give dual dvi rather than a dvi and vga (a few of the 7 series do have this I believe), and are a better value.  I would look at an 8600gt or 8600gts.  Both are under $100, and passive models are available without the fan if you want a quieter pc.  On the other hand, if you would like a higher end card, the 9600gt is as high as I would recommend for your build.  The 8600's will run dual widescreens with all the compiz features on, so I would imagine they would provide you with enough performance.

Since I don't know what price range you are looking in I tried to suggest good price/performance values.  Let me know if you want other suggestions more fitting to a different price range.
