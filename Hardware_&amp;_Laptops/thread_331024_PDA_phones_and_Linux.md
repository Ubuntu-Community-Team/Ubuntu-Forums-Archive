### Post by VMan on 2007-02-10
There are several linux based PDA's.  Most (that I know of) will sync well with linux.  Some even run Kalendar as their scheduling app, so syncing is as easy as syncing the two file (computer & PDA).  Look for Sharp Zaurus models.  They pulled out of the US market, but are still selling strong in Asia.  They are imported through a company called Dynanism or something similiar.  I have not dealt with this company so can't say anything about them except that I've heard good things.  I have a Sharp Zaurus 5500SL.  The design was ahead of it's time.  It had a slide out keyboard.  The battery like was good, but not great.  The screen brightness and saturation really sucked compared to an IPaq though.  The really bright spot was the amount of software that was ported to the platform.  I could do almost anything with it that I could do with a desktop.  Most of the software was free, but some (video viewer and textmaker) were commercial, but well worth the cost.  You could even "roll your own OS" for it.  Everything from custom kernel and apps, to just changing the apps included in the ROM.  I remember seeing some custom ROM's that were specially designed for medical uses.  They even included some good medical stuff in the ROM.  I never tried them since my job involves putting people in the hospital (just joking; kinda) not fixing them.  Unfortunately, since Sharp pulled out of the American market, I can't easily get a new battery and the PDA will only worked while plugged in.

You might look for the 800(?) series.  They looked like a mini-laptop.  If I could justify the expense, I would have gotten one.  They run around $800 US and I got a full size laptop for that (which suits my purposes better).

Hope this gives you some hope.
