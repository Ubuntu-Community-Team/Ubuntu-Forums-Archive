### Post by wankel on 2008-01-01
Hi GS,

Maybe you found out by now, or else I did maybe :-)

First of all I was curious what circular scrolling was anyway. I found your post, and this page: [http://www.physics.uq.edu.au/people/alexei/synaptics.patch](http://www.physics.uq.edu.au/people/alexei/synaptics.patch)

There is not too much of an explanation, but it works for me as advertised. The only difficulty is getting used to actually make the scrolling engage. Once it engages (I can make it do so by left clicking on a scrollbar, and then sliding my finger along the top of the touchpad), it will stay active until you take your finger off the pad.

The possible advantage over "normal" scrolling might be, that it works continuously for any scrollbar, instead of many little vertical scratches to scroll down a page.

Good luck!

Edit: I do not think it needs adapted hardware, since it is just some motion registration along the configured edge.
