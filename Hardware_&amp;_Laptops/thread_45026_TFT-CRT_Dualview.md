### Post by sonny on 2005-06-29
[QUOTE=Labonte]do someone have an example of xorg.conf file that i can check, i'm a little newbie in the linux world, and are therefore a little uncertain to change to much..  :grin:[/QUOTE]
Well, before you change your xorg.conf file make a backup of it, type this in a terminal:

sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /home/username/xorg.conf

I always keep those kind of files in my home directory, just in case I blow the sistem off. If your changes gets you out of the X session, then you just have to type this in a terminal:

sudo cp /home/username/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf

Now, you don't have to be afraid of changing your xorg.conf file, I'll post mine, as soon as get home, in the meantime you should give this a go.
