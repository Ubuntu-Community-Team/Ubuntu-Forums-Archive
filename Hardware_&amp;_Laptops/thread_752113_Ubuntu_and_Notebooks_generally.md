### Post by wsclark on 2008-04-11
I am *not* by any means a Linux or Ubuntu expert, but I bought a laptop for college recently (Dell Inspiron 1405) that came with Vista Home Premium.  I became frustrated with Vista after a few months and installed Ubuntu (Gutsy).  It was incredibly easy to do, and I was surprised at how compatible it was with the laptop hardware without even having to install any drivers. My RF keyboard and mouse were automatically detected (and even the auxiliary media buttons worked!) and all of the shortcut keys on the laptop still work.  Long story short, it's not a hassle at all to install Ubuntu on a laptop, and I would recommend it.  You can also consider dual-booting linux and xp or linux and vista if you're worried about software compatibilities.
