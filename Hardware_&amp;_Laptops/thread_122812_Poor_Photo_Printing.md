### Post by SteveF on 2006-01-28
Hi,

I'm getting poor results printing photos from the Gimp and I'm hoping for some ideas on how to improve the print out.

I have an Epson Stylus Photo 820, which I understand is supposed to be well supported by gimp-print.

When printing photos from the Gimp, they appear over saturated and too dark.  In Gimp's print screen, I change the printer to the Epson and set the paper (either matte or colorlife depending on what I am using) and DPI to 720 (which I was told is optimal for the Epson 820 for photos).

I have tried changing gamma, brightness, dithering and contrast.  None help (all make the image look washed out).

I have removed to -raw option and chosen postscript 2 as the print device.  That produced much better colors, but the quality was terrible (banding was very visible as were dots).  I did modify the printer setting in CUPS knowing the postscript version would use those, but that did not help.

I tried downloading TurboPrint.  That produced good colors but terrible quality as well.

The quality of the output just isn't acceptable as a photo and I've run out of ideas on how to get good photos out of Linux.

Thanks for any help,

Steve
