### Post by fyrestrtr on 2006-04-22
I will second the Thinkpad recommendation (I am writing this on a Thinkpad).  IBM is one of the few companies that provide Linux drivers for their laptops.  I am currently using a Thinkpad T43 2668-71G.  Mine has 1GB RAM, a 2Ghz Centrino processor, 60 GB HDD, and a ATI X300 video card with 64 MB dedicated RAM.  It also has bluetooth and wireless, however -- I have yet to use them on Ubuntu.

Most things on this laptop work out of the box, including the Thinklight and the volume control keys.  It comes with two pointers (the tradition "rubberhead" that sits between the G and H keys, and a Synaptics touchpad). I disabled the rubberhead one from BIOS, and only use the touchpad -- which was detected fine and worked as expected.

It has has some IBM-unique features such as an embedded security chip and a fingerprint scanner.  These can be activated if you do a bit of research on the net and download the drivers; the fingerprint scanner works great with authenticating with programs (example -- instead of logging in with a username and password at GDM, just swipe your finger).

About the only gripe I have with this laptop is that ATI makes crap drivers. It took me a few tries to get acceleration working properly with the binary ATI drivers -- and even then, it only half-works; but it works good enough that I can play World of Warcraft without problems.

There is also a very lively community around Thinkpads and Linux.  The [ThinkWiki](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/ThinkWiki) is a great resource for everyone that is running Linux on Thinkpads.

If you are going to a shop, grab a livecd with you and boot each laptop with it to see what is detected and what is not.  If I was going to buy a new laptop now, I would probably get one with the dual core centrino processors.  Try to get one with an nVidia video card as their binary drivers for Linux work *extremely* well.
