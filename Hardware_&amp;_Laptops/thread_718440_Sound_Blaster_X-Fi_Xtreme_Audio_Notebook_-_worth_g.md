### Post by benjaminmorris on 2008-03-09
I dual-boot Ubuntu Studio 64-bit Gutsy and Vista Business on an ASUS G1S with a Core 2 Duo. The processor is supposed to be 64-bit, and it must be, because it runs my 64-bit OS. The beta drivers are for 64-bit systems only, and for some reason mine was not detected as 64-bit. The creative website lists several desktop x-fi models that the driver is designed for, but the notebook model is not on the list. When I by-passed the OS check, it exited saying it couldn't find an compatible device in my computer, so my guess is that it doesn't support the notebook version (which I've read here and there is just a re-branded SB Live! 24-bit).

In Vista, though, it's fabulous! It's a dramatic improvement over on-board sound, especially on my Logitech X-540 5.1 speakers. I think it's worth it to get the add-on module that allows it to support up to 7.1 (sold separately). The Creative Console is feature-packed, allowing pretty flexible customization. Some have complained of less-than-exceptional support in Vista, but I've had absolutely no problems with it. Overall if you really enjoy listening to music (as I do) you will find yourself booting back into Windows to enjoy the much-improved sound experience with the card and some good speakers (mine are good, but not incredibly amazing like some other, more expensive, logitech sets).

Hope this helps! I don't know if I can provide any more information, but feel free to ask. I'm not often on this forum, but I'll try to remember to check back on this, since I too would like to get the x-fi card working in Ubuntu. Let me know if you find anything else out!

### Post by kleft on 2008-03-21
This sound card is actually quite good but to be honest, it really isn't meant for playing media back through your on-board laptop speakers. It's meant to be plugged into something external. It is also great for improving performance of headphones. I use it on XP and it's great for recording and playing music but I nearly always play back through external speakers or headphones using the card.

You can play sound through the laptop speakers but there is about a hal a second delay. That means nothing for music but for videos, it's unbearable. I wouldn't recommend getting it if you are only ever going to play back through the laptop speakers. It also doesn't look like it's got a Linux driver at the moment and from my experience with creative, you could be waiting a couple of years for them to release one!
