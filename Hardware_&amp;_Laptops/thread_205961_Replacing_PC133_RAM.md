### Post by blkish on 2006-06-29
if the RAM already in the system is PC133, it is most likely you can only add more of PC133 (168 pin DIMM) type.

Some motherboards have slots for both PC133 (168 pin DIMM) and DDR (184 pin DIMM) RAM types. You might be lucky and have one of these.
However, you will probably only be able to use either PC133 or DDR at the same time.

An easy way to identify the RAM you have is by looking at it. 

164 pin PC133 DIMMs have two sets of gold pins with a gap in between the sets. One set will be larger than the other.

184 pin DDR DIMMs have three sets of pins, with gaps between each set. The sets are small, medium then largest.

A good guide to visually identifying your RAM type can be found [here]("http://www.dewassoc.com/performance/memory/how_to_ID_memory.htm").

DDR is faster then PC133, but PC133 is still perfectly serviceable and can be picked up cheaply (check ebay or local ad papers). 

An upgrade to 512mb would likely see a major performance increase in your machine.

good luck
