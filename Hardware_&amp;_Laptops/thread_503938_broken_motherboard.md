### Post by information_entropy on 2007-07-18
1.check the cable, make sure it is plugged in properly
2.look at the cable connectors, make sure you have not broker or bent a pin
3.disconnect the monitor from the computer.
   does the monitor manufacturer screen show properly?
   do the screen setup buttons work?
4.if possible try the monitor on a different computer. does it work?
5.try the cable with a different computer and monitor. does it work?
6.try a different monitor on the problem computer.  does it work?
7.[COLOR="Red"]make sure the problem is not the monitor or cable before you start tearing apart your computer.[/COLOR]
8.if you have onboard video try disabling it and using a different video card.
9.if your onboard video has failed you can often disable it in the BIOS and use a separate video card.
