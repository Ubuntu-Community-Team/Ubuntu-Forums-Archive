### Post by jml on 2006-03-24
I agree about the Thinkpad.  I have installed Linux on two of them and they are very Linux friendly.  If cost is an issue, you might want to consider buying a refurbished ThinkPad.  I bought a really clean Thinkpad R31 from 

usanotebooks.com

They refurbish many brands of laptops, but have a very good selection of Thinkpads.  There is a pretty good warrenty, and they have good support.

Another brand of laptop that supports Linux rather well is the HP nx6110.  Its a business model so it does not have accelerated graphics, but it is solidly built, (I know because I recently bought one,) and if you dig down very deeply into the HP website, or talk to a sale rep on the phone, you should be able to order it without Windows installed and save a few dollars.  It will then come with another open source OS called FreeDOS.  Not very useful, but easy to delete.  That way you can get a laptop without the Microsoft Tax.

Joe
