### Post by hardyn on 2007-10-09
well i think you have the right idea...

basicly if you are playing in a linux relm, you want to look at driver support, that will basicly dictate your purchases.

for example:
i don't know if you want to wait for ATI... otherwise your looking nvidia.  the nvidia-glx-new support starts at 6xxx series boards; thus you have a decision made.

there has been alot of trouble with new embedded audio chips, thus staying with a little older hardware will probably be to your benefit.   there should be compatibility list for the ALSA driver.  stay away from bells and whistles in motherboards, they almost certainly have windows only support.  check network cards using NATIVE driver support the same way.  you do NOT want to use ndis wrapper if you can avoid it.

hard disk, power supply, dvd devices; almost allways work... and don't require much thinking. 

my little machine, which works perfectly is:

an AMD XP2400 w/ 1gb ddr ram
nvidia 6200-128mb
asrock socket A motherboard.  with SIS lan and audio which works perfectly.
pata hard disk and dvd burner.

good luck.
