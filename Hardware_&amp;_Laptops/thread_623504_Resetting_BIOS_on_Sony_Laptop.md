### Post by swj on 2008-02-26
I do not know if this will help and / or lead you in the right direction...in any case, it seems like your out of options except to pay someone to do it.

I recently worked on a toshiba laptop.  If your lucky your sony may be **similar**.  This method worked and cleared the BIOS password.  Apparently, newer laptops (all?) have a security chip installed and the old methods just don't work anymore--the security chip is suppose to add...security to the laptop.   The only other option was to pay $475 for Toshiba to do it since the laptop was out of warranty.  However, no tech support person will ever tell you how to bypass a security feature.  I personally have a Lenovo T61 and I have not checked to see if my laptop is similar.

*I found this on some forum(s) and do not know who originally posted it*

1)Remove Laptop Battery, i.e., all power to the laptop
2)Take off Memory Cover
3)Using two thumbs spread spring clips to outside and pull out RAM memory.
4)Remove clear vinyl label opposite RAM memory slots in upper left corner
5)Using a magnifying glass, in the upper left corner you will see JOPEN1 with
a white silkscreen square next to it . Inside the square you will see what looks like the letter T over a U.  Using a metal paper clip short the U to the Silver 1/4" post connecting the motherboard to the laptop case(this is you ground, it is located above what looks like a
silkscreen outline of a chip)hold for 25 -30 seconds.
6) Place back your RAM memory, put in the laptop battery and test start laptop. you should see a selection of F1 or F2 for setup. Using the F2 gets you into the Bios menu where you reset the time and date. Then Tab over to the SECURITY setting tab and change your passwords. Now
hit F10 key ,yes to save and your done!  Note: if your having trouble your most likely grounding
the ground.
7) I added this step: In my case, the original BIOS had a security chip bug causing the BIOS password to kick in.  I updated the BIOS.

The above did not damage the laptop in any way and I got the instructions down the first time.

Reply back if something like this works--I am curious.

Good luck!
