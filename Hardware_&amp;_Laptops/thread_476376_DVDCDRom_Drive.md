### Post by ddrichardson on 2007-08-24
This is indicative of a laser failure but there are two other possibilities - has the tray worn on its guides (this causes the laser to missalign very slightly) or that there is a build up of dust. As the laser ages its power drops and small flecks of dust that weren't a problem become one.

The higher level of reflection on CD-R often affects this as well, if you can clean out the drive using a non abrasive cd lens cleaner or compressed air (make sure and let the can warm up a little to prevent moisture and do it with the power off. Also don't aim at the laser as it may cause problems).

If you can get hold of some of those plastic adhesive cd repair things you may be able to access these cds.
