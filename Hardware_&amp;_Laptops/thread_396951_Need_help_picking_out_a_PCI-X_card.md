### Post by azemute on 2007-03-30
A few things to keep in mind:

1) judging from that processor, I truly suspect you're looking for an AGP graphics card, which is good... you should be able to get a decent one for fairly cheap
2) PCI-X isn't the same thing as PCI-express. PCI-X is an old server architecture with a 64-bit databus, but is still painfully lacking in data-rate compared even to AGP. [That, and I don't think they ever made graphics cards for them]

That being said, Nvidia [as you said] is defiantly the way to go... personally, I'd look at the 6600GTX or such, as you can get them for dirt cheap, though you can also pick up a 7600 for less than $100 USD.

It all depends on what you want to do with it. Compiz/Beryl will be a breeze with either, and most native-linux games should run just fine on both. If you're looking for something somewhat more future-proof, I've heard good things about the 7600GT series.
