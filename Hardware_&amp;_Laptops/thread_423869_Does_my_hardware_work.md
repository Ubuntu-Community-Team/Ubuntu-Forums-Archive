### Post by jespdj on 2007-04-27
Just download and burn a Ubuntu Live CD and boot it on your computers and see if it works or not.

Your hardware doesn't look unusual so I think you most likely won't have any problems.

I'm running 64-bit Ubuntu 7.04 on an Intel Core 2 Duo E6600 / Asus P5B Deluxe motherboard (onboard Intel HD Audio) / Asus ATI X1600 video card / 2 GB RAM / 320 GB SATA-II harddrive / Sony IDE DVD writer.

Everything works out-of-the-box, except for one little problem: it didn't automatically recognise the resolution of my monitor (a Dell 2405FPW, running at 1920 x 1200). That was easy to fix.

Just try it out. Note, if you want to use Linux you must be prepared to be looking for solutions to lots of small problems. That's what the forums can help you with... ;)
