### Post by renzokuken on 2007-04-30
yeah, you could disable the onboard wireless and buy a USB wireless key that is known to work with Ubuntu . you can get them now for £10-20 ( $20-$40 i think).

it is usually possible to get most chipsets working though using ndiswrapper and a windows driver. 

the biggest linux issue with laptops is wireless, but support and compatibility is improving very fast and there is usuallly a way to get it working.

search the wiki for compatible cards [http://wiki.ubuntu.com](http://wiki.ubuntu.com)
