### Post by mips on 2006-01-09
I think you need to analyse your customers needs first before you go out and buy anything.

What will he be using the laptop for ?
Portability, size & weight he's happy with ?
What resolution does he require, XGA, SXGA, SXGA+ or UXGA ?
Display size, 12", 13", 14", 15", 17", Widescreen ?
Does he need wireless ?
What is his budget ?

If he has the budget go with an IBM/Lenovo T42p or T43p. 
I could also recommend the HP range but it depends on the model, business range is nicer.
MAke sure the laptop does not use Broadcom wireless, Intel has native support, so does the Ralink.
