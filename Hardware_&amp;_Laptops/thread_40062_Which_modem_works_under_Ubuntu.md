### Post by jonny on 2005-06-07
Some winmodems work OK.  The built-in one in my Dell laptop is fine, for example.  But it's really hard to know what chipset a modem will be based on.  I tried researching modems when I built a PC for my mother in law, but finally gave up and bought an external serial modem.  These always work (don't get confused with external USB modems; these are fussy with Linux) and, in my limited experience, seem to give better results - fewer dropped connections and slightly higher connection speeds.

In the UK - we call it rip of Britain because our prices are always higher than anywhere else in the world - they cost about £20-25, more than twice the cost of a soft modem.  I imagine a British or European supplier would ship one out to you if you're desperate.
