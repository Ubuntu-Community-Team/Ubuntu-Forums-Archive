### Post by Henry Rayker on 2007-01-31
The biggest hurdle I think you'll face will be getting WoW up and running. There was a situation a few months back where Blizzard flagged a very large number of users who happened to be on Linux based OSes...after I believe a week, they decided they made a mistake and fixed it; there's no guarantee that they won't "mess up" again.

Surfing the net is no problem with firefox; watching DVDs, and listening to music may cause some small issues...you may need codecs (not too rough to install, though); openoffice should let you get your office fix.

Dual booting will help your WoW issue; I'd recommend just booting into Windows to play WoW, then boot back to Ubuntu to do everything else.

Your wireless may or may not work out of the box - it will depend very heavily on the chipset it uses.

Wine isn't installed by default, so you'd need to install this. Linux software will run on any Linux based OS, but not on Windows or OSX; Windows and OSX software won't run on Linux based OSes either.

The live cd is basically an entire operating system runnable from the cd-rom. No harddrive is necessary but, due to that, you can't save anything. Any and all changes you make, files you create etc. will be gone when you reboot.
