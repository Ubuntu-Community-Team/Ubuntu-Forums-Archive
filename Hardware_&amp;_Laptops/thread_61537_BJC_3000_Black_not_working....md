### Post by taryneast on 2005-09-01
I've been scratching my head over this one - it's kinda wierd. The printer isn't using the black ink. It makes all the right movements and even sounds like it's trying to print, just in invisible ink!

It started when I changed over to Ubuntu, but that doesn't mean it's definitely a problem with ubuntu, of course - I just figured it'd be worthwhile asking.

There doesn't seem to be any problem with the colour ink catridges - when I print a test page it happily does everything except for the black bits... 

Some details of the printer:
It's a Canon BJC 3000 with a USB connection. The cartridges are all Canon-brand and definitely have enough ink in them (buying a new cartridge was one of my things I tried).

I've tried:
- switching on/off the printer
- unplugging/replugging the USB connection
- reinstalling the printer/driver etc (it wouldn't detect it as a local printer, so I set it up by the "specify a port" method - using USB printer #1)
- I tried moving it to USB port 2 - and changing that over.
- I tried all three of the drivers that are available under the BJC3000 (the two that are not the recommended one just print with different dot patterns, but still no black).
- I tried printing stuff from various different programs - gimp, Firefox, gvim just in case it was something in between the application and the printer driver...
- I replaced the ink cartridge with a new one (Canon brand just in case they have those dodgy DRM wierdness - which I don't think they have, but hey, it's one more thing to be certain about).

Now, for all I know it could be a dodgy cable... or some clogged whatever - but right now I have no way of checking that - there's nothing obvious to my untrained eye...

If anybody has any idea of something else that I could try (apart from buying a new printer), I'd appreciate it.

Cheers and thanks,
Taryn
