### Post by Kadin2048 on 2006-09-29
While as a particular purchase-source, this may not be helpful to those of you in the U.K., but I have had great luck buying corporate-surplus equipment from [www.retrobox.com](www.retrobox.com) .

I looked at the cost of building myself something from bare-bones, and then I looked at what I could get on the surplus market, and the surplus market won.

I picked up an HP xw5000 "workstation" which is certified to run Linux (RHEL, but whatever, it means that it won't have any really crappy hardware on it) for under $300 last year and it's been great. Saved me so much trouble and worry with buying parts and hoping they'd all work and configuring everything.

Anyway, I just thought I'd throw that out there as an idea. If the people you're building for don't need today's latest-and-greatest, maybe starting off with a used system rather than a bare-bones one is the way to go? It'll give you a jumping-off point (you can still spruce them up for them, install and config SW) but it makes the project a lot less daunting.

Good luck!
