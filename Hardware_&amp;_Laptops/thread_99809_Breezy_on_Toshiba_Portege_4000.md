### Post by nikkkko on 2005-12-06
This just worked first time out of the box and I am really, seriously impressed.

Having dumped Windows a year ago for Suse on my desktop, I was dreading installling linux on my Toshiba. I decided to give Ubuntu a go and was immediately put off by the installation screens which are less elegant and communicative than the Suse Yast experience.  I managed not to partition my hard drive the way I wanted as a result, but that's my only gripe because from there on in, everything just worked.  Wifi, for example, worked first time with no tinkering. Switching from internal to external monitor worked first time with no tinkering. Sound too. Everything. (I have to admit I haven't tried ir or bluetooth yet).

One minor tweak was running xorg config again with my external monitor connected.  This picked up the ability to run higher resolutions which appeared when I restarted the x server. Perfect.

Two big pluses over Suse:
1. Synaptic is simply nicer to look at and easier to use than Yast
2. Automatix saved me literally hours, (probably days), of downloading and tinkering to get my multimedia sorted out.

Nice work and many thanks. (Now, where's that donation page...?)
