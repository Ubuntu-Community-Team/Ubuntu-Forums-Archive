### Post by bobpur on 2008-04-05
If I read your post correctly, you should shut off your computer and remove the sound card.
Try your onboard sound card. You may have to install drivers. IF drivers are already installed for your onboard sound and it is activated it may be conflicting with the X-Fi card. You should only have one in use at a time.
BTW-(To the best of my knowledge)-Linux doesn't like X-Fi cards at all. So, if you dualboot, you will use the X-Fi card in windows and switch to the onboard card for sound in linux. You do not have to install onboard drivers. they come with the distro. Just switch the speaker jack from the X-Fi card to the onboard jack.

Good Luck!
