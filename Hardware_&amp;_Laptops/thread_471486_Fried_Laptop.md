### Post by buggybug on 2007-06-12
hi.

I have a vaio PCG-K215S and have taken it apart just 4 weeks ago the first time because my fans were extremely loud and I concluded that they were dirty. I recommend you to take a lot of time for this because vaios are very stubbern to be screwed apart (lots of different teeny-weeny tiny screws) ;)

notice #1: your warranty might expire when taking apart your laptop
notice #2: make sure you are earthed (non-charged hands)
notice #3: make sure your hands are not greasy
notice #4: do NOT touch the PCB's (printed circuit boards)/ electriconical parts, just touch the casing

First of all, take out the battery ;)
I guess you want to go for the fans. You get there from the top. Its best to take away the sound panel right above the keyboard. You do that by flicking away the plastic extensions that attach the sound panel to the screen. There are no screws, so be careful that you don't break the little hooks. Then you can unscrew the keyboard. Then you can take away a metal panel (lots of screws again) and there you are at the fans. Then again you can take away the bottom panels that hold the harddiscs. And you can see right through the fans. Take a vakuum cleaner and hold it right on the fan. I furthermore tried to get rid of the really fine dust by staggering into the fan using cotton buds slightly damped... worked fine. 

It worked for me... fan is much more silent now.

Ah and screw it back together :D

### Post by IntuitiveNipple on 2007-06-12
Here is the tar+gzipped PDF **service manual** you can download (2.8MB) for the [PCG-GRT series](http://tjworld.net/sony-laptop/Vaio_PCG-GRT_service_manual.tar.gz). I hope it helps you.

Don't be afraid of taking it apart, I do it frequently with my Vaios - it takes about 3 minutes. Make sure you've got a decent set of jewellers cross-head screwdrivers. Use a soft mat or towel to lay out the screws in the positions you removed them from so you know where to return them to. It is not much of a problem because most screws are identical size.
