### Post by ErikTheRed on 2006-12-10
The iPod is one of the most supported mp3 players under linux. Amarok has native support for the ipod and I'm sure the gnome equivalent does as well (I use Kubuntu, so I'm on KDE all of the time). Also, you could put the rockbox, an open source firmware, on it which supports nearly any format under the sun. There's also ipod linux, but I don't believe it's stable on the newest ipods yet.

As far as alternatives, if the player just acts as a usb storage device where you can drag and drop files through your file manager, it would be fine too.

It's pretty hard to find a player that won't work, there's even been progress made for the players that use a lot of Windows Media Player.
