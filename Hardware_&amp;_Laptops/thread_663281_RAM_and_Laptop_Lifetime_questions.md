### Post by feistybird on 2008-01-10
1. The more the better, no matter if it's Windows or Linux.

2. Yes, more RAMs generally cause more heat, but it isn't a big deal.

3. Yes, Battery last about 2-3 years, or maybe a bit longer,  Hardware should be fine, (any well-known brands.) I've a 7 year old Asus NB, and it is still working perfectly except battery... but I'll recommend DELL as they've Ubuntu preinstalled products.

### Post by tylerspaska on 2008-01-10
1. ram is the cheapest way to boost performace on any OS. i would buy the laptop with the lowest ram configuration and then buy aftermarket ram (it's easy to install and super cheap these days)

2. i have no idea, but i can't imagine it makes that much of a difference

3. i've had my dell for 4+ years. it still works great and has held its value well (it still goes on ebay for about 50% of what i paid). the battery still caries a 2 hr charge. i have some friends that have compaqs that have only lasted 2 years. i think it really has to do more with the user rather than the brand in most cases, but that's just an opinion.
