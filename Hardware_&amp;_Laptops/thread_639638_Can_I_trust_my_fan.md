### Post by martinrandau on 2007-12-13
I am experiencing very high temperatures on my Znote 3415 laptop with Nvidia 8600GT graphics and Intel Core 2 Duo 64 bit processor.

When doing processor intense processes such as playing World of Warcraft the temperature quickly rises to up to 85 degrees until the fan kicks in and lowers it to around 70. These are ridiculously high temperatures! 

When the laptop is idle, it is around 50 degrees.

The question is, can I trust my fan or is something really wrong here? I do not want to destroy my processor.  

I have applied the fixes to the Cycle count bug, and some of the effects of that might be increased temperature of the hard drive, but since playing WoW is not a very intense process for the HD, I don't think this is the reason.

### Post by Existentialist on 2007-12-13
I would try looking here:
[http://processorfinder.intel.com/List.aspx?ProcFam=2643&sSpec=&OrdCode=](http://processorfinder.intel.com/List.aspx?ProcFam=2643&sSpec=&OrdCode=)

Find your processor and click the left column to view its detailed specs.  Intel says most of the mobile C2Ds are designed to operate up to 100 C.  As long as you are not getting in to the 90-100 range, I wouldn't get too worried, the fan seems to be keeping it at an acceptable temp.
