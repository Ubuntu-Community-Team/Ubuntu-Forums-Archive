### Post by spd106 on 2005-10-31
Try this [wiki]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsWirelessNetworkCards") page for starters.

I would suggest you find a card that has native drivers. The atheros based cards seem quite good. You will hear people moan about how you need to make sure that you get the right card version and revision number, with the most up to date firmare. The good news is that support is getting better all the time. Just be a little careful.
 
Often it boils down to how easy it is to obtain a good card. If you can afford to be patient and order online from someone who will guarantee that it will work. Or if you need to pop down to the shops and get one straight away. Also when you've identified the one you want, you then find out it's obsolete and/or unobtainable. It also seems a bit pointless to settle for a 802.11b only card when the b/g cards and routers are so cheap.

Another good place to look is the [linux-wlan]("www.linux-wlan.org/") project (Warning: It's quite a long page).

I can't say i'm familiar with your machine, but the PCMCIA slots haven't seen much change in a while.
