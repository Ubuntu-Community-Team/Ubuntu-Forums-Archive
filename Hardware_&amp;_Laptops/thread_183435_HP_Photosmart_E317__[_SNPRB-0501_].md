### Post by uberlinux on 2006-05-28
I went out on a limb and bought this before checking to see if it was Ubuntu-friendly, here's what I've got to report:
1. DigiKam sees it as a 'USB PTP class camera', but does not actually grap the photos.
2.  It does work in Ubuntu, at least as a USB mass storage device.  You need to change the default settings to get this to work, here's how.
Go into the camera's menu, then scroll over to the picture of the wrench, thats the config menu.  Scroll down to where it says USB, and switch the option to 'Disk Drive', then plug it in, it should just pull it right up.

Hope this helps someone.  I like the camera so far, BTW.  Can't really go wrong spending $90 on a 5MP digicam from Ubuntu's #1 supporter, HP.
