### Post by Ken_Lewis81 on 2007-05-27
The popular hardware site [tomshardware.com](tomshardware.com) has ad a few articles about Intel 965/975 mainboards (back in [October](http://tomshardware.co.uk/2006/10/11/four_975x_motherboards_for_core_2_duo_uk/)/[November](http://tomshardware.co.uk/2006/11/13/shootout_at_the_core_2_corral_uk/)), compared them to nVidia 680i boards ([http://tomshardware.co.uk/2006/12/19/intel_or_nvidia_uk/](http://tomshardware.co.uk/2006/12/19/intel_or_nvidia_uk/)) and Intel are lining up the next-generation 'Bearlake' platform with the G33, P35, G35 and X38 chipsets ([http://tomshardware.co.uk/2007/05/21/intel_intros_3-series_chipsets_with_fsb1333_and_ddr3_uk/](http://tomshardware.co.uk/2007/05/21/intel_intros_3-series_chipsets_with_fsb1333_and_ddr3_uk/)).  I don't think a single Intel board is mentioned, because you get better features and a lower price -- I'd be surprised if they were less reliable -- from OEM's, so I can't help you on that score.  The Asus P5B series are 965/975-based and are rated highly; the Asus P5K is based on the newer P35 chipset.

Take care.
