### Post by julian67 on 2008-02-22
I recently bought a 9 cell battery for my Asus M5N (1.6 GHz Pentium-M centrino, 12" display, Intel 855 GMA, 768 MB RAM). The laptop is a little over 3 years old and the 2 batteries originally supplied (2 batteries! Asus give a good deal) were on their last legs. My new battery is an original Asus part so it certainly isn't only Dell who offer these. I can't even remember the last time I used Win XP which was pre-installed, these days it boots into antix running on Debian Sid and it seems fine to me, CPU scaling works and power management seems the same as on Windows. I haven't ever exhausted this battery but from the info the system offers I should get over 5 hours from it. I do recall that when I ran Feisty  the battery was probably exhausted a little quicker than in XP.  This laptop runs the fan all the time, whatever OS is on there (it's normal on these) and is noisy (Asus were even replacing them under warranty due to complaints) but at least in Linux it is possible to control the fan [http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~reynolds/m5n-linux.html](http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~reynolds/m5n-linux.html) withoiut further expense. I assume there might be 3rd party utilities to do the same in XP. 

One thing I suggest is to avoid cheap 3rd party batteries. I've gone this route before to try and save money on expensive camera batteries and the cheap ones are awful. Spend the money on the real thing, it will last several years and hold a charge properly.

### Post by Wulfrunner on 2008-02-22
I have a T60p with a 9-cell lithium ion battery and I have to say one of my biggest disappointments has been the battery life in Linux compared to Windows. Yes, there are things you can do to reduce power consumption in Ubuntu (tickless kernel, cpu scaling, gpu scaling, lower screen brightness, suspend USB, blah blah blah), but I can never reliably get the power usage below 20W. 

How does this translate into battery life? If I am CAREFUL -- not doing anything that calls heavily on my CPU or GPU (browsing, coding, e-mailing, etc.) I can get 3 hours in Gutsy. If I am taking full advantage of the core2duo 2ghz, the firegl ~400mhz graphics, and all the nice sound and video (essentially, playing games), I can get 1.5 hours in Ubuntu. 

Compare this to 5.5 hours in Windows when I am being careful, and 2.5 hours when I am pushing it. When I want the power, I just plug it in anyways; but when I am not doing anything I want the 5.5 hours of use. 3 hours is not worth it when I have to lug around a battery that is as heavy as the laptop itself!
