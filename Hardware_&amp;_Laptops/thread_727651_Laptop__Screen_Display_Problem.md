### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-03-18
You need to make sure the wires are connected to their proper headers inside the laptop screen and on the motherboard.  You should also check that the wires have not been stripped of their protective covering - use electrical tape to cover up any exposed wire.  
This may or may not be possible without tearing the laptop to pieces (especially inside the screen, though you should be able to do it by pulling back the plastic around the LCD itself - not like you have much to lose).
I honestly don't know how much it would cost to repair, but the chances are it wouldn't be cheap and may not be worth fixing.

### Post by ygolohcysp on 2008-03-18
When checking it out, first see if you can tell which cable/cables would've had tension on them first with the way it almost fell.  Check both ends if you can, and anywhere they would have been strained by bending around the plastic case.  If it's too much trouble to get to the back side of the connectors, and you're absolutely sure you won't get shocked or short something out, then you could try turning it on and wiggling the cables lightly to see if the screen flickers or changes.  I'd use this only as a last resort, and I mean last.  If you end up deciding that you'll get it fixed, and are okay with whatever the estimated cost is from a repair shop, then don't turn it on and wiggle the wires.  If you haven't found a problem with the wires themselves, or the connectors, check the headers on the display and or circuit boards where they connect.  Look for broken solder connections or traces lifted off the printed circuit.  From what it sounds like, it most likely is just connection failure.  No guarantee though.
