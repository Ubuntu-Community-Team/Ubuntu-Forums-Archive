### Post by tetrafuran on 2008-04-12
Based on a tutorial ([http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal)) I installed a cli version of ubuntu. My goal was to end up with an OS that uses as little resources as possible. I'm using an old laptop with 400 MHz and 128 MB of RAM.

At the moment there are still a few things to solve.

1) As the computer starts it says something about ACPI not working and it suggests me to use ACPI=force. When I close the lid it is supposed to beep twice and suspend to ram. At least that's what I think DSL and other distros do. Just two beeps and then there's silence and very low energy consumption.

2) I chose to install fluxbox. When I start it, it doesn't show me the menu when I right click the background. Middle click is successfully simulated and the mouse works otherwise as expected. Either the right click doesn't work or the menu doesn't exist.

All in all it seems that cli ubuntu didn't solve any problems, but it created more. Not a surprise there... I've used enough computers to develop a certain degree of pessimism. So as for my original question: is it worth it? It seems that it wasn't unless these problems can be solved without an expert. I haven't yet made extensive research on the issues at hand, but it has become obvious that not just anyone is capable of setting up a system like this. I got this far by relying on logics and reading what's on the screen. Usually there comes a point when possessing knowledge becomes imperative and the lack of it inhibits further progress.
