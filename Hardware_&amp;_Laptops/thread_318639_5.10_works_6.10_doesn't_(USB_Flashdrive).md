### Post by disabled_20220313 on 2006-12-14
Have you tried finding out what the partitions are called?

You can find out by  plugging in the drive going to System --> Administrator --> Device manager. Bury your way through the left hand menu and find the USB disk.

<There must be a quicker command line method to find this out>

At a guess the device names for them would be /dev/sda1 and /dev/sda2. I'd guess that your personal files would reside on the 2nd partition

You could manually ```
sudo mount  -t vfat /dev/sda2 /media/usbdisk
```

I know this isn't a permanent solution, but will do in a pinch.

If no one comes up with a solution, I suggest you file a bug report, so atleast it might get sorted for feisty.
