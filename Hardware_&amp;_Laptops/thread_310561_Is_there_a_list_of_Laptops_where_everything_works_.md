### Post by jml on 2006-12-01
Ubuntu really does have good laptop support, with in my opinion, one very big caution.  Wireless networking.  If you look through the networking/wireless forum threads, you will find a lot of discusion of many users experiences.  Not all of it good.  

Ideally get a laptop with either a Prism or Atheros chipset, ( or buy a laptop without built in wireless, and buy a PCMCIA wireless card based on those chip sets.

Another issue with wireless is encryption.  If you do not need encrypted wireless, then many of the mainstream laptops will do fine.  WEP encryption seems to work ok as well.  WPA can be a real pain, especially if you are new to linux.

If you are planning to buy a new laptop, you may want to check out System 76.  They have a sub-forum on this site.  They sell several very nice laptops with Ubuntu pre-installed and they make sure everything works.  I've written them and they also make sure WPA encryption will work out of the box.  Since you are planning to buy a laptop anyway, I would suggest supporting a company that seems to want to be a good member of the Linux community.  For the record, I have no financial interest in the company, but I do plan to buy me next laptop from them.  Just my two cents worth.
