### Post by steveneddy on 2007-06-17
I have had lots of luck with Intel processors and off the shelf hardware.

Lots of RAM will make a big difference in the speed, especially in Linux. Ubuntu only requires 512 MB but with somewhere between 1-2 GIG of memory, if one can afford it, will make a difference no matter what the OS.

My server runs well with a seven year old PIII with 512 MB RAM. It runs VNC, Samba, Apache and it is my music server for the house and file share PC server for the family. Never any hiccups or downtime on over a year.

Just check the hardware compatibility list and I think you will do fine. 

FYI - my laptop runs a Core 2 Duo @ Ghz processor with 2 GIG RAM and an nVidia PCI Express graphics card and this thing rocks.
