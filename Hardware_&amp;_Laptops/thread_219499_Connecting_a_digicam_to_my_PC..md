### Post by swamytk on 2006-07-20
In Ubuntu by gThumb is a photo manager. But in Xubuntu have you installed gThumb? If you have installed gThumb, ensure that gnome services are running to enable gThumb to automatically popup when you connect camera. gnome-volume-manager is one such service which takes care of this. There is an option in the XFCE settings to start GNOME/KDE services at startup. Enable this setting and enjoy.
