### Post by hangman_jdf on 2007-08-06
Here's an answer to the question I posed to tech support about the usb converter.

"The PS2 to USB Adapter is designed to operate with devices which have
integrated hardware support for USB. There are mice and keyboards who
 support both USB and PS2 connections. They usually ship with an USB/PS2
 converter. 
 In your case it seems likely that the keyboard does not "speak" USB and
 when your system tries to communicate via USB with it, it fails. As a result
 you get an "unknown device" in device manager.
This product is only a adapter (connector conversion), but not a
 converter (signal conversion)."

I think that I might just go get a usb keyboard and see.
