Microsoft MS4000 wireless mini-mouse - Works.  It's a mouse, nothing fancy but it works just in case you wanted to know.

Power Management Other Than Suspend - Just works.

Summary:
As long as you are willing to use xfce or other similarly light interface then this model of laptop can still be used for another year or so.  Good hunting everyone.


### Post by professor fate on 2007-10-28
Through the following combination of activities, I was able to get the sound card working on a Dell Vostro 1400 with an Intel Corporation 82801H (ICH8 Family) HD Audio Controller (rev 02)
.  Also, I am making no assumptions on your level of skill with Ubuntu, so I'm providing a step by step approach.  As of this writing, I'm on day 4 of my Ubuntu experience. 

1.I don't know why, but the 32bit version of Gutsy caused me grief to no end with respect to sound, so as a last attempt I installed the 64bit.  Life is better.
2.After installing Ubuntu, go to Applications > Accessories > Terminal
3.At the prompt (e.g,, flashing cursor) type in: sudo gedit /etc/modprobe.d/alsa-base
4.This will bring up a text editor full of entries.  At the bottom of the page copy or type in the following:  options snd-hda-intel model=5stack
5.Save the file and close it.
6.Restart computer

At this point I had sound.  I actually found this info on another thread but it was buried among many other posts.
