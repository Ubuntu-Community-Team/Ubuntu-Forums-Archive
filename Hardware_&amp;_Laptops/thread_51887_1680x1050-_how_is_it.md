### Post by caporaso on 2005-07-25
Hi:
Yes, the Inspiron 6000 can be configured with the 1920 x 1200 resolution -- it is the WUXGA screen option -- beware though, I have not been able to get running at that resolution in Linux yet, and I've been trying on and off. If you're going to go with it, I think there is a configuration option to get an ATI video card and people with that option seem to have had better luck than those with the Intel video card that comes by default.

The pixel size is definitely smaller that a 20 or 21" monitor at similar resolution (1600 x 1200) but I haven't used anything but my laptop screen in a while so it is hard for me to compare.

-Greg

### Post by vayu on 2005-09-18
Since your post was 6 weeks ago, I might be a little late, but here goes just in case. 

I have the 1680x1050 model.  I also have the ati video card with 128mb ram instead of the integrated intel driver.  For $100 extra it's well worth it.  

When I first got it I was bummed at the smallness of the fonts.  Then I found I could set it to 130dpi instead of 96 and all is groovy.  I love this computer.  It's fast (I have the 1.6 ghz M730)  The wide screen display is great, I really like having two full windows open side by side.  I don't think I could ever work on a normal dimension display again.   I would not get the 1900 something resolution screen.  That would be way too small, I'm at the edge now.  

The 6000 has worked great for me with Ubuntu right from install.  I installed the fglrx video driver instead of the standard one from the install to get 3d acceleration and opengl working.  No issues.
