### Post by jpkotta on 2006-03-15
I checked out the website, and it looks like either Asus or Linksys.  My friend had a Linksys (wireless) router and had no end of troubles with it.  He said there are all kinds of similar reports on the internet for this particular router.  Maybe their NICs are better, but I am wary of Linksys after what he went through.  

I have a TrendNet something or other.  Cheap as hell from newegg.com, and once I got it working, it worked great (better than in Windows, which for some reason throttled it somehow).  No Linux driver for it, had to use the Windows driver with ndiswrapper.  I've never used it with Ubuntu, but I'm sure it would work.

### Post by Bagnaj97 on 2006-03-17
Cards with Ralink chipsets work well, and they also have 64bit windows drivers if you need them. Some versions of the Belkin F5D7000 have linux compatible chipsets but some have broadcom. There is a list of compatible wireless cards here [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsWirelessNetworkCards](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsWirelessNetworkCards).
