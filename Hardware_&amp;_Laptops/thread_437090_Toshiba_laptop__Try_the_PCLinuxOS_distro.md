### Post by jupiter66 on 2007-05-08
I will probably be flamed for this post but in my experience, Kubuntu 7.04 is a horrible distro
to try to get working on a Toshiba laptop. (Mepis 6.5 has the same problems)
 It's like oil and water, they just don't seem to mix.

The broken sound issue was not fixable on my laptop (A105-S2141) but worked perfectly
with PCLinuxOS TR4.  The wireless worked fine as well, but I had to point wireless driver installer
GUI to my Windows files to load the Windows driver.  (I dual boot XP and PCLinuxOS)

In summary, I found the PCLinuxOS distro to be:

1) Faster
2) Way better looking
3) Works without hours of fiddling and "tweaking"

Give it a try, you might like it.
It is also a live CD and you can boot and test it to see if it will work with your hardware.
I am still testing it, but so far, it is very impressive.

I thought I would miss the apt-get of Kubuntu, but since I use Synaptic, it is a non-issue.
