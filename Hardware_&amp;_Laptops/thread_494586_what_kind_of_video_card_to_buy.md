### Post by HumbleGod on 2007-07-07
Here's what I got from some quick GIS checks.

AGP port:
[http://www.si87.com/Products/Videocards/agpvspci/agp-or-pci-or-isa.jpg]("http://www.si87.com/Products/Videocards/agpvspci/agp-or-pci-or-isa.jpg")

PCI-E x16 (top port):
[http://images.tomshardware.com/2005/11/23/pc_interfaces_101/pcie.jpg]("http://images.tomshardware.com/2005/11/23/pc_interfaces_101/pcie.jpg")[http://images.tomshardware.com/2005/11/23/pc_interfaces_101/pcie.jpg](http://images.tomshardware.com/2005/11/23/pc_interfaces_101/pcie.jpg)

Check your motherboard to see which one you have. Judging from the specs you mentioned, I'd expect your wife's computer handles AGP.

As for RAM, from what I've heard as long as the pin count for the new RAM is the same as that of the old, it should be fine. However, the RAM will only be able to run as fast as the highest speed the motherboard is capable of. If that's 2700, then even a 3200 will run at 2700 speeds.
