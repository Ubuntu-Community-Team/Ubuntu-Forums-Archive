### Post by eklitzke on 2007-05-10
Hi everyone,

I am a student in college, and I just got a job offer for a sysadmin internship at a company that uses Ubuntu heavily internally.  This will be a big pay increase for me, and consequently I'm looking to get a new laptop soon :-).  I'm looking for something approximately $2000 (USD) or less.

Right now I am using a Lenovo Thinkpad X40.  I chose this laptop because I knew the hardware was exceptionally well supported in Linux -- namely, it has an Intel graphics chipset, and an Intel IPW2200 wireless chipset. As most of you probably know, the Intel graphics drivers are completely open source and DRI works out of the box, and the IPW2200 wireless driver is mostly open source (the firmware isn't technically open source, but it's shipped in the default Ubuntu install).  Consequently, _all_ of my hardware has worked on this laptop from day one with no fiddling whatsoever, and I am very pleased about this.

Things I like about my current laptop:
[LIST=1]
[*]Intel graphics
[*]Intel wireless
[*]Small and quiet
[*]Good hardware support!
[/LIST]

Things I don't like about my current laptop:
[LIST=1]
[*]Only two USB ports
[*]No DVI output
[*]No CD drive!
[*]Slow hard drive (1.8" 4800 rpm)
[*]Relatively low resolution (1024x768)
[/LIST]

From what I've seen, the Apple Macbooks look nice. The "regular" Macbook models (i.e. with a 13" screen) have Intel graphics.  I'm not sure what they're shipping right now, but in the past it seems like they've shipped either Atheros or Broadcom wireless.  I haven't really followed these closely, but my understanding is that getting Atheros wireless working is pretty straightforward, and as of the latest upstream kernel (2.6.21) it appears that Broadcom wireless chipsets will be well supported.  Is this accurate? The Macbooks also have DVI out, which is nice.  I've also looked at the offerings from System 76.  It looks like the Darter Ultra is about the right size, and has Intel wireless/graphics, but I think it looks kind of ugly and AFAICT there is no DVI out.  Lenovo and Dell also have nice offerings, but it seems a bit hard to find the combination of features I want (it especially seems hard to find laptops with Intel graphics/wireless that aren't low end bulky laptops).  Actually the newly-announced Lenovo T61 looks very nice, but I can't find any tech specs on it at the moment, beyond the short summary they have listed in the Lenovo store.  I haven't really looked at HP or Vaio laptops yet.

Anyway, does anyone have any newly purchased laptops that they love?  Can anyone comment on the observations I've made about the laptops I've considered?  What is the state of Broadcom wireless (esp. as of the latest upstream kernel)?  Are there any laptops I might have missed?  None of the likes/dislikes I've listed are absolutely mandatory, so if there are any great configurations out there right now that are off in a couple of areas that's probably ok.  And thanks in advance for the comments!

-- Evan
