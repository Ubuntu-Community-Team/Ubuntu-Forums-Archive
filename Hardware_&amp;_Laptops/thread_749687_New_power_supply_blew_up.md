### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-04-08
once you get your new supply .. take everything OUT of the computer and set the motherboard up on a flat wooden surface or cover the surface with a towel.  I could be that you have a grounding issue. 
 At present, there is absolutely no way to determine if the processor or ANY of the plug in items has sustained damage.  You can run the board bare bones to test the components.  It will boot with nothing but the processor and a video display hooked up if the processor is OK.  Then you shut down and plug the memory in, and re-boot.  Repeat the process for each component individually.

To prevent static electricity, I usually do my assembly on the kitchen table and do it BAREFOOT!  LOL
(having good light helps. too!)

When you finally test and verify everything, THEN you start by re stuffing the case.  And to be on the safe side, go through the individual procedure AGAIN.

I know it sounds time consuming, but it the only way for a newbie to do it right.  That was how I began many years ago, and I STILL install using the single component method.  Amazing what you will find (like a cd/dvd reader that has gone south .. things like that!)

Oh, and having Vaseline on hand to dip the end of the Phillips driver into to hold on to those screws that you have to put into an awkward  place also helps.
