### Post by zodium on 2005-11-13
I wouldn't go with an x2 4200+ (if you must, go with an x2 4200+ or x2 3800+ if you don't want to spend the money for a 4200+; the 4400+ and 4800+ aren't really worth looking at). As suggested, a 64bit 3500+ or even 3200+ would be perfect for you.

Forget about 10k RPM Raptor disks, those things run hot as heck and make tons of noise. Instead, get a RAID-capable disk like the Western Digital Caviar RE series as they run significantly quieter and last significantly longer (34dB for their non-RAID disks versus 26dB on the RE - that's Raid Edition. Since the dB scale is logarithmic, that means it produces just short of half as much noise). The price difference is negligible, but it should be noted you'll generally be forced to go with 8MB cache instead of 16MB, but in my humble opinion the tradeoff is worth it.

The 6600 is an excellent choice, no comments there. Make sure your motherboard supports PCI-E though, or you could get screwed over short term and will *definitely* be screwed long term as AGP is becoming obsolete very, very fast. You won't see any new cards coming out with AGP support. 

For RAM, get a gig of PC3200+ Corsair Value Select and just buy two 512MB of the exact same module. "Dual Channel Kits" are more or less a marketing sham and as long as your motherboard support dual channel, you just need two RAM sticks of the same size, type and model.

To summarize: an AMD64 3500+, 2x512MB Corsair Value Select RAM modules, any GeForce 6600, a 200GB Western Digital Caviar RE (or equivalent model from other companies) and your motherboard of choice as long as it has PCI-E support. That should be a very decent computer for a surprisingly small amount of money. :)
