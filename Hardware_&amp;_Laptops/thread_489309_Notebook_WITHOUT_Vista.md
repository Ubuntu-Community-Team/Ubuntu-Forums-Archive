### Post by gborzi on 2007-07-02
Actually, Jason's reply isn't so much wrong. Big laptop vendors, such as Toshiba, HP, Acer are able to get OEM windows licenses at a very small price, because of their large sellings. I've read that some people have managed to get the Windows license reimbursed from some big vendor, but what they got was about 40-50 euro. On the other hand, small assemblers pay the OEM license in full, so the difference in the final price can be on the order of some hundreds euro.
As for Dell, I don't think they are an option, those with the preinstalled Linux are sold only in the USA. I have looked at [http://www.tuxmobile.org/]("http://www.tuxmobile.org/"), which seems to be a little outdated, and I have found a French vendor, [http://www.xbook-computer.com/]("http://www.xbook-computer.com/"). Their Lapilli notebook is very similar to my Compal HEL81.
