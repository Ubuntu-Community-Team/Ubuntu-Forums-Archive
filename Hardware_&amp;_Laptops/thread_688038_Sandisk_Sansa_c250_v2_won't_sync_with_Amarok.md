### Post by Patness on 2008-03-26
Before I say anything, I should mention that I own a c240 v1.

I don't use Sandisk's original firmware - I got mine in early September and dealt with a massive driver rollback fiasco that caused me to lose faith in their firmware completely. They don't even support their own HCSDMicro memory with their firmware, which [Rockbox]("http://www.rockbox.org") does. Since you're on a v2, you can't support that either.

So, bearing in mind that we are using two very different devices:

Connect the player using MSC mode. MTP mode tends to be stubborn.

Eject the player from within Ubuntu itself (you should just be able to right-click the device shortcut on your desktop) when you want to write everything to the drive - not everything is written simply because you've copied it over. Give it several minutes (until it says its done).

Whereas it's freezing up, I'm not sure if that's intentional or not, as, while these players do work with Linux, they aren't designed to and Sansa has refused to offer any support for them. What support they do have is from users like me and you.

I've had my own problems getting Amarok to synch my device with a playlist. I've copied everything over manually, but this is time consuming work that could be done with less effort.

If you ever want further help, look up my contact information on here. Get a hold of me as you see fit and ask away.
