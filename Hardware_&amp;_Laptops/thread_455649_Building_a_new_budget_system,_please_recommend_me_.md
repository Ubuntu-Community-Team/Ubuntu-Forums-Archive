### Post by Cows on 2007-05-26
Hmm I could say to get the same hardware I have.. It's not the cheapest but it's not the best. You can also just downgrade some parts of my specs. Example:

**My Basic Specs**
- Intel Celeron D 3.06 Ghz
- 2x 512 DDR 1 RAM PC 3200 ( 1024 DDR 1 RAM in Total )
- nVidia 7600 GS
- PC Chips Motherboard

Now you can take some parts and downgrade them as I said before.

**HPC = Uncle's HP Computer**

- Intel Celeron D 1.3 Ghz would be good since I installed Ubuntu 7.04 successfully on HPC with this processor.

- HPC has 2x 256 SDRAM. Ubuntu still runs fast.

- I think HPC has a cheap onboard card since when booting up there was black and white flashing which is actually hard to describe. But once it gets to the login screen it is good. Perfect vision.

- Now for video card: I had a ATI Radeon 9200 PRO before I bought my Nvidia 7600 GS. my ATI worked perfectly and I would recommend you to get it if you are on a low budget. But if you can get a Nvidia card that is pretty much the same price and supports beryl ( wobbly effects ) then get it. My ATI card supports it and its approximately about 40-60 bucks atm. Nvidia does have better support for linux so I would say get a Nvidia 5600 If you can.. Any Nvidia that supports basic beryl effects would do.

- For the motherboard .. I have a $55 or $65 motherboard and it works fine. It has 8x AGP support and 2.0 USB w/ 4 ports for usb , onboard video , ethernet , PS/2 , onboard sound. Everything about this motherboard is good. This motherboard uses AGP Video card and Pinless Intel Processor. I would recommend to get intel over AMD. That's just my opinion since I used AMD and Intel.

- You should get a Lite ON dvd/cd combo burner. There about 40 bucks and are extremely good and reliable. I never had a problem with it.

Hope that helps :).
