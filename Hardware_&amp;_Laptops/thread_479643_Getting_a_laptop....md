### Post by javag33k on 2007-06-20
$1500 can buy 3 HP/Compaq Presario (c552us) laptops running Vista that you can subsequently blow away and put Feisty on (trust me, those laptops don't have the horsepower to run Vista so you really do want to put Ubuntu on it - i.e. 512MB and celeron chips). ;)

However, if you do want something more powerful, I'd have to agree, the new T series from Lenovo or older IBMs have seemless installs for Dapper and Feisty.   The bluetooth and wireless cards (both the standard and upgraded versions) are recognized after install, so you'll be able to get going in 30 minutes and not have to search forums/google for how to get those things to work.  Since I have both a 500 dollar laptop and 1700 dollar laptop running Dapper and Feisty, I have to say I actually like the cheaper laptop a lot sans a limitation on the resolution.  For me, the big thing with deciding which laptop is whether or not there is also a dedicated video card.  Resolution above XGA generally needs a dedicated card with associated vram.  Many of the T series (but not all) run the X1300, X1400, or even the NVIDIA 256MB ones.

So I'm looking at one of the links posted above (the zareason.com).  They allow you to configure just about everything on the laptop AND the memory upgrade is at an awesome price (note: buying a laptop at other places and choosing 2GB sometimes commands an additonal $200 while they charge a competitive price).  I'd probably go with them on my next laptop purchase.

I buy about 3-4 laptops a year and I have some level of Ubuntu on a Thinkpad, Dell, and Compaq laptop.  Feel free to message me if you'd like more info on my thoughts about buying a laptop.
