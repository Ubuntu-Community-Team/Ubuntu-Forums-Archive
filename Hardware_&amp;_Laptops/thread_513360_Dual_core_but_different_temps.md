### Post by HarshReality on 2007-07-30
Somebody will jump in but here is my 2 cents. Dual core does not mean 50/50. It does however mean that when one core becomes loaded to a certain degree (I have no idea what determines this) the other core kicks in to take some of the work load off the primary.

Despite them both being in the same chip it would seem logical that one core which is doing a majority of the work and the secondary which is 'occasionally kicking in' would not share the same temp. As for what reports the temp... cant say... how they would differ as they are in the same CPU, also cant say.

But in the end... its intel so, nuff said.
