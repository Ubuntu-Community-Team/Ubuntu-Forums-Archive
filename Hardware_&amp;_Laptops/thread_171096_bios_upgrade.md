### Post by confused57 on 2006-05-05
I'm on Ubuntu, but don't know if you can check bios information from the OS.

Why not just press "F2" when your computer is booting up and enter your bios setup menu, this should tell you everything you need to know.

Your bios may be set for  silent mode, or something to that effect, which means it doesn't display the bios info during a routine startup.  Lot depends on your system.  The info is usually displayed for only a few seconds during bootup, press scroll-lock to read it.

Once you find out about your bios version , check the manufacturers website to see what the latest bios version, and what it upgrades(support for hardware,etc).  If your system is working OK for you, I would give it serious thought as to whether you want to "flash" update your bios.  I would read and find out as much as I could about flashing bios, because if done incorrectly it could render your computer unbootable.  Flashing the bios is not for the "faint of heart".
