### Post by bergersau on 2007-05-18
I'm using and ASUS p5b wi-fi Delux.
The onboard sound is well supported out of the box on Feisty 7.04. -  'Just Works'
In fact the out of the box was better than for Windows Vista - I had to download and manually install drivers to get proper support under Vista.

As for quality, onboard sound cannot compete with an external sound device.
The electrical interferance from other devices will always degrade an onboard sound devices performance.

I find the onboard more than good enough for me as it is easily better than my current speaker setup.

### Post by miceagol on 2007-05-19
It's a long time since I bought the motherboard now. ;) The onboard card is called Intel HD Audio in Alsa, and surround doesn't work with it. It only duplicates front sound into rear speakers, but no 5.1 surround in movies etc. So I bought an M-audio Revolution 5.1 dedicated sound card, and it works really great in Linux. You can read about my research [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=344681") and [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=400268").
