### Post by Dynaflow on 2008-05-09
Ironically for a company that manufactures Linux laptops and PCs, Dell only offers Windows drivers (such as the one to which you linked) for that AIO.  The Linux Foundation's OpenPrinting database rates this printer (as of 9 May 2008 ) [as a paperweight]("http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Dell-Photo_AIO_926").

Give it time.  It's inevitable that -- eventually -- the right amount of talent will collide with the right level of table-banging frustration in a Linux-using, Dell 926 AIO owner somewhere, and a penguin-friendly driver will be born.

### Post by Dynaflow on 2008-05-26
[Device drivers]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Device_driver") are generally operating system-specific, and if a company is unwilling to release the technical information needed to write alternative device drivers for other platforms and OS'es, end users are pretty much stuck with whatever drivers the manufacturer deigns to release (or that clever people are able to reverse-engineer).

You may find a perusal of [this thread]("http://www.ideastorm.com/article/show/62329/Provide_Linux_Drivers_for_all_your_Hardware") at Dell's IdeaStorm feedback site enlightening.  You might also want to add your 2¢.
