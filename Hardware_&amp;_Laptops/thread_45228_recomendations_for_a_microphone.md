### Post by irusun on 2005-07-02
I just discovered skype a few days ago... so I'm still exploring all the hardware options.  I can't suggest any specific models, but here's a couple of pointers...

Any microphone that plugs directly into your sound card's microphone jack will work (their should be a microphone jack right next to the jack where you would plug speakers/headphones on the back of the computer).  These kind of microphones don't need any special drivers - I picked up a cheap one for under $5 - it works fine.  I'm sure there are nicer ones that will pickup the voice better.

They also make combination headset/microphones that work the same way.

It's get's more complicated if it's a USB microphone - i.e. it bipasses the sound card 's input/outputs and goes through the USB port only.  These types are usually listed as being for Windows (and maybe Mac as well) - you'll need to do some research on the web to find out if there's a driver for Linux (the Linux kernel includes driver support for some USB microphones).  Be weary of these types unless you know Linux support exists.

You may also want to look into a converter that lets you hook up a regular telephone to the soundcard - e.g. [www.chat-cord.com](www.chat-cord.com).
