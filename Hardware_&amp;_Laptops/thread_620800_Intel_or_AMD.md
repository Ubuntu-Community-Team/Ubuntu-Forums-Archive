### Post by jespdj on 2007-11-23
An Intel or AMD processor does not make any difference for Ubuntu.

Generally, the nVidia video drivers for Linux still work better than the ATI drivers, although ATI has promised to improve the drivers (really good ATI drivers are not there yet).

I've heard bad things about Broadcom wireless chipsets, so I would avoid them as well.

Intel support for Linux is universally good....

Having said that, my Acer5051AWXMi has an AMD processor, Atheros wifi and ATI graphics card, all of which worked flawlessly with Gutsy (not so easy with Feisty).

Wifi chipset is probably the biggest issue to bear in mind.  Other things to consider, if they are important to you, are card reader and webcam compatibility.  My card reader now works in Gutsy, but only with SD cards (supposed to be 5-in-1).
