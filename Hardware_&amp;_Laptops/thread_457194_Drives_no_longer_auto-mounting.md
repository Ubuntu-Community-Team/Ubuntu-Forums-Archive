### Post by merlinus on 2007-05-29
> **tomio said:**
> I just updated the kernel by using the automatic updates and now my fat32 volume won't mount, but my windows volume still mounts.  The fstab hasn't been modified.  I tried sudo mount -a and it tells me:

mount: special device /dev/sda6 does not exist

any ideas?

The kernel upgrade probably changed sda6 to hda6.  You will have to edit /etc/fstab -- at the very least -- to reflect this change.

You can check in a terminal by entering:

sudo fdisk -l

(l is a lowercase L)

Good luck!

-merlin

BTW, the same process may work for newagelink.
