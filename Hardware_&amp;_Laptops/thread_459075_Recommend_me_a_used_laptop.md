### Post by ali4949 on 2007-05-30
I have been using IBM Thinkpads for a long time and when it comes to robust hardware nothing beats it. Best bet in my opinion would be buy a refurbished T40, 41 42 43 depending on budget and buy a new battery. Spend some extra on upgrading the ram to system max or in some cases like my T30 you can go up to 2 GB even though the original specs say 1GB.(after a bios update). Also if you want to run compiz/beryl make sure you get a laptop with atleast 32 MB video memory if not more although that would exclude some of the older Thinkpads out of consideration. Try tigerdirect, there Thinkpads are actually IBM refurbs, not some third party.

### Post by drummingpariah on 2007-05-31
> **lazyart said:**
> Compaq N620c is a good unit, though it may or may not have wifi builtin.  I use a PCMCIA card.  Battery life is in the 3-4 hour range. 1024x768 is standard, though some will do 1400x1050.  All have Mobility Radeon 7500 and run Desktop Effects smoothly.

Video modes are the same on the Thinkpads.  1280x1024 is very very common as well, with 1400x1050 being more rare (only with the 64mb video cards with the fancy-schmancy screens).  If you have 1280x1024 you won't be hurting for resolution, and the brightness on my 8-year-old T22 is pretty darn good, even in the sun.

Don't assume that because the Thinkpad is my first recommendation that it is necessarily the best.  For everyday stability and ruggedness, they're pretty top-notch.  The LFEC computers (laptop for every child) are coming out soon, and are estimated to be under $200, and can do everything that the average person would want out of a computer, in addition to being able to be powered by hand and having a built-in stylus for basic drawing.  They're cheap, rugged, water-resistant, and all-around a good cause.  The day they come out, I plan to get 3 (one for me, one for my little sister, and one for the cause).
