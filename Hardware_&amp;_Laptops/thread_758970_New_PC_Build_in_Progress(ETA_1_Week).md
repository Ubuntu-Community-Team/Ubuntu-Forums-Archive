### Post by cubeist on 2008-04-18
Looks great.  I am doing a build at this moment as well, however I went the AMD route to save some money.  A couple things I researched for my build you may find interesting...

Regarding ram speeds check out this article
[http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/ram-speed-tests,1807.html](http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/ram-speed-tests,1807.html)
I actually went for cheaper, slower ram after reading this article.

Re crossfire: Does not work with linux and as far as I know there is no plan in the next while to support crossfire on linux, so paying extra for parts that are crossfire compatible is not necessary right now!

I personally think ram cooling is a bit redundant... most ram sticks come with decent heat spreaders built in, and as long as you have adequate case cooling things should be ok... Ram is fourth on the list of hot things in a computer (after cpu, vid, hd).

I also went with a WD HDD.  I have been a Seagate user my whole life, but the price and performance of the SE16 class looks great. (Tests faster than a raptor in a couple categories)

Good luck!
