### Post by em3raldxiii on 2008-02-27
Traditionally, GeForce (nVidia) cards have been supported to a greater degree than ATI (Radeon) cards.  That's starting to change, but I am under the impression that you are still better off with a nVidia GeForce card.  As far as which one specifically, you'd probably be fine with anything not super brand new (even those might be okay).

And if you have the option to pick a brand, I would recommend Asus, Gigabyte, and EVGA ... others may recommend some different ones, but most people will agree on those three.

You'll be safe spending anything from 100 to 200 bucks ... cheaper and you won't get the features you want, and more expensive is going to be a waste of resources for your purposes.

Here's an example of one that would probably suit your purposes very well:
