When you give a monitor a refresh rate or resolution it can't handle, one of several things happen, depending on the monitor:

1) it tells you so; you get a frequency out of range message on the screen.

2) you get a less helpful message, telling you that the monitor works, but to check your computer.

3) screen goes blank, and the monitor goes into sleep mode. The status light turns amber.

I have never seen a monitor be killed by this. Ever.  It just isn't that easy to damage PC hardware with software, no matter how poorly written. People have tried. 

I believe you that it died while changing resolutions, but i believe it very likely that the monitor would have died very, very soon anyway. Even if you had left it alone.

I can see how a physically bad video card might kill a monitor, but I've never seen, or even heard of that happening either. For events less than a lightning strike.

> i have very large files on my master drive that i cannot burn to disk b/c i don't have a dvd burner, and im hoping that this uninstall doesn't impact my xp os.  any suggestions on this would be greatly appreciated.

Your Master Boot Record will be destroyed if you delete the linux partition, but If you have a CD with Windows XP it will be easy to fix it. Just enter in the Recovery Console after you boot from the CD and type ```
fixmbr
```.
This should do the trick. After that you will be able to boot normally into Windows. Then use a partition manager to recover the destroyed partition that you used to install Ubuntu. Suggested software: Partition Magic or Paragon Partition Manager.
