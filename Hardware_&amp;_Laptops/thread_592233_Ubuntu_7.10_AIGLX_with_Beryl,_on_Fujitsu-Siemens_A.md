### Post by natty_c on 2007-10-26
Hey!
I've installed the Ubuntu successfully for the first time. At the startup, after selecting the os, everything went black, and I figured out, that I had to push the Ctrl+Alt+F6 buttons, to switch to the text based interface.
I was in.. :) After selecting "Enable" to all the restricted drivers, with a plugged in UTP cable, and that I enabled all the mirrors in the System -> Administration -> Software Sources, it downloaded the required video driver, and the broadcom firmware cutter, than the firmware.
with [this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=488385&highlight=x700+aiglx") guide, I managed to have 3D support, and Beryl is working fine now.
The conclusion is that this system is able to run the Ubuntu 7.10 well, but there are some machines, where you don't have to mess with anything, like the IBM R32.. :)
Bye
