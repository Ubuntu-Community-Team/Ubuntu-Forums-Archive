### Post by ciscosurfer on 2006-10-12
You can install Ubuntu on just about every computer out there.  Preference is up to you.  Just be sure to check the hardware compatibility database before you buy anything.  System 76 looks like a good place; haven't check out Linux Certified, so I can't speak to that.  What's the Laptop wiki?  Anyway, if you buy a modern-enough machine, the latest Ubuntu versions will work just fine.  Any other quesitons, please feel free to post.

EDIT: Linux Certified looks good.  The reason these sites came around btw, other than there not being in existence before-hand, is b/c they do the hardware testing for you and certify their machines to work.  I know for sure System 76 works with Ubuntu, b/c that's what they preload as the OS.  Just keep in mind, most manufacturer's computers will work as well.  Remember, you can even install Ubuntu on PPCs and Macs.
