### Post by alfonz on 2006-02-16
hmmm, unless you are also planing on buying a new motherboard for that cpu, I wouldn't. The board you have now will not support that Celeron D.

Also, the video card that you have now is probably 1x/2x AGP, the new Celeron D board will probably be 4x/8x AGP or if you get the PCI-E. That means that that vid card will not be compatible with your new board. 

I am only going by assumption here and it is by no means entirely correct. If you can provide the hardware info of you current setup I migth be able to tell you better what you should do  or what you can do so that you can weigh your options and make your descision that way
