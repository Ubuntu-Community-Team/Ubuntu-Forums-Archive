 There is a not-so-well-known trick with these computers that perhaps you are not aware of: to make the graphics a little better you can increase the amount of RAM available for video display from the default of 1mb to 8mb. This option is in the BIOS and is a very minor thing to change with impressive results.

 Details of the GX270 can be seen on the[ Dell website]("http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/opgx270/en/ug/index.htm"). I suspect that you may be in a little trouble with a PCI Express card as the site specically says of the video adapter:

> Integrated chipset video or expansion AGP card

 I too have been hunting for a half-sized AGP card but as you know they are hard to come by, and of course there will then be the problem with the heat these cards generate. Mind you the best addition I made to this machine was to upgrade to 1gig of RAM.
