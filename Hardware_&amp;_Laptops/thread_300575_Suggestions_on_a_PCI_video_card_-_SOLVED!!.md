### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-11-30
Well, I have good news and bad news.

The good news is, if you have a machine that needs a strictly-PCI video card, that 6200 is a sweet card. DVI, VGA, 256Mb on the board ... enough goodies to last you a couple more years of solid gaming. I don't have frame rates to compare, and I haven't used it under Ubuntu yet, but I'm fairly sure you'll appreciate it. And best of all, the 'egg dropped its price another $15 from what I paid for it. (Jerks.)

The bad news is, it doesn't work in the machine I wanted it for. There's some sort of incompatibility between my motherboard and the card itself, and the system never even reaches the BIOS check with that card in it. I don't mind, since it works fine in another machine, but it kind of defeats the purpose of originally buying it.

Either way, I intend to keep mine. It's working great, and it has turned my old K6-2 550 into a screaming Armagetron Advanced madman. So the fun is definitely not over ... even if the card is probably running faster than the CPU in that one. :)

Cheers all, and have fun.
