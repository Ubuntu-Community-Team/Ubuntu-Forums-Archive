### Post by cilynx on 2007-01-14
I'm currently on integrated audio (laptop..go figure).  However, the last desktop card I bought was an SB Live.  It worked quite well, including sound fonts, 4-point surround, and independent speaker volume.  I know the info is four years out of date, but maybe it has a bigger brother that still uses a supported DSP.

According to Wikipedia, there will be support for your card second quarter of this year:

*As of January 2007, there is no Linux support whatsoever (since its release in August 2005); Linux users are unable to generate any sound from the card. Creative, on May 18th, issued a statement that a closed-source Linux driver that works with the Advanced Linux Sound Architecture will be available in the second quarter of 2007&#8212;one full year later. The corresponding thread on the Creative website forums has grown to hundreds of posts, some angry and indignant. However, Creative did not mention any Linux OS support in the minimum system requirements that are printed on X-Fi packaging.*
