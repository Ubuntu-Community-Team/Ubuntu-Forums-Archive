### Post by Mike'sHardLinux on 2007-02-18
The SATA 1.5 drives are becoming less common. From what I've seen, SATA 3.0 drives are actually cheaper than SATA 1.5 drives. For example, you can get a WD 320GB SATA 1.5 for about $105 at newegg, and a comparable WD 320GB SATA 3.0 is $85.

I'd go for the SATA 3.0, unless you can find the SATA 1.5 a LOT cheaper.

One thing, though: You're not  going to see much real performance improvement with the SATA 3.0. Even though the SATA 3.0 interface can achieve a max of 3Gbs, there are other limiting factors, such as the speed at which the drive can actually deliver data.

I am using two (normal config, not RAID) Samsung 250GB SATA 3.0 drives in this desktop, and they have worked fine in Dapper and Edgy. Abit AL8-V motherboard, Pentium D 820.
