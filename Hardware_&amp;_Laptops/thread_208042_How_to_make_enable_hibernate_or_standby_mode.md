### Post by the_blue_pill on 2006-07-03
Both wifi and acpi standby are available BUT it is almost impossible to tell whether or not they will actually work on a particular system. Your best bet is to try out the live CD before you install ubuntu on your harddrive and see for yourself.

In Kubuntu standby/hibernate options are controlled from System Settings > Laptops and Power > Laptop battery in the K Menu.

If you're really lucky your wifi will be automatically detected by ubuntu. If not, there are many ways to go - if nothing else works you may probably use ndiswrapper to use your windows drivers in linux.
