### Post by karmik on 2006-09-04
As a general rule..no you can't.
I'm not familiar with those model but can you be a bit more specific? 
Anyway that's really unlikely. They probably are for 2 different sockets , with absolutely no compatibility both at mechanical/electrical and logical level. 

In the very unlikely case that they both are from the same manufacturer (you didn't specify whether they are amd or intel, the cpu family etc etc) you still would have serious power consumption issues by putting such a powerful cpu in a system built for a cpu very much less power demanding. So my answer is more or less, no you cant :D

You can scavenge a laptop of the same model family but a better revision, something probably with just a bunch of mhz more than yours and you'll have some more chance to upgrade but anyway it really isn't worth the effort, not mentioning the fact that operating on a laptop is quite harder than on a common desktop pc and judging from your question you don't seem to be capable of that either.

Be careful with electronics, they're expensive and they can be harmful if not handled with care and knowledge ^^
