### Post by tcpip4lyfe on 2008-03-22
I've found that with recording to a computer, good cables and a good mix level does wonders to eliminate group loops.  If you keep the amp at a higher level then the input level without distorting your sound card you can eliminate a lot of the ground loop but you won't be able to get rid of it.  I used to use a windows program called cool edit that works awesome for eliminating buzz.  Before I got a good amp and sound card, I would record the amp with for a couple seconds after recording a track and then you can take those AC frequencies out of the recording with only minor disturbance to the track.

Edit: I use this to record my MicroKorg and my guitar.  [http://www.m-audio.com/products/en_us/Delta44-main.html](http://www.m-audio.com/products/en_us/Delta44-main.html)  Works amazing and its only a couple hundred bucks.
