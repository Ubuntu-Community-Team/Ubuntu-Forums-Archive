### Post by ugm6hr on 2007-06-12
> **Enverex said:**
> That Acer has a much better processor in it than all the rest but everyone seems to be against that one. You need to try and find something other than a Celeron to be honest...

I have no idea why everyone is slating the Acer.  I bought it from the same place as linked above (when it was £350 :( and have had no problems with it (apart from Vista ;) 

From the link - go to customer reviews - I've posted my Xubuntu experience as Anonymous.

It was easy - LiveCD Xubuntu7.04 (i386 - not AMD64) worked out-of-the-box (with the base 512MB RAM - I now have 1GB).  Even identified suggested "Restricted Drivers", so all I had to do was tick some boxes and click OK.  A 5 second adjustment on alsamixer required to fix sound (unmute surround and mute front speakers).  The media card reader will not work (ENE don't do Linux).  Ethernet was easy - plug in and go.  Wifi was easy - and I recommend Wicd rather than Network Manager (which isn't part of Xubuntu anyway) if you're planning on WPA.  I haven't tried the dial-up modem (haven't needed it for years) or the external monitor option.  The Synaptic Trackpad works out of the box too, but has extra options if you install gsynaptics and edit /etc/X11/xorg.conf (lots of examples here - but I'll post mine too).

I have heard that Acer will not honour your warranty if you don't maintain a Microsoft OS, so I would recommend a dual-boot.  The HD has 3 partitions (1 data recovery, 1 Vista, 1 empty), so is perfect for dual-booting (no need to resize partitions - just delete the empty and use Guided - Largest Free Space install option).  Also, there are some reports of them taking several weeks to fix problems - but I'm not sure that's much worse than anyone else.  Anyway - it is an excellent laptop, with **good** Ubuntu compatibility.  As mentioned - it has a better processor than the Celeron.
