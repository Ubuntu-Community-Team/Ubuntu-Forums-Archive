### Post by Achetar on 2008-02-09
Alright, I just bought a Cooler Master Mystique case, a BFG Tech nVidia nForce 680i SLI MoBo, and a 650W Antec TruePower Trio PSU. My mobo has four 3-pin connections for fans, and I have 2 fans on the case. The case fans also have 4-pin connectors to hook up directly to the PSU. Which should I use? I believe the mobo fan connections would allow fan control (like when the computer is on, but cool and not doing anything), but the PSU would power it better. Any help?

---

### Post by neurostu on 2008-02-09
If you are more concerned with noise then I would plug your fans into the MOBO so they can turn off when things cool down, otherwise just plug them into the PSU.  It really doesn't matter that much.

### Post by neurostu on 2008-02-10
Any of them will work, If you don't have a fan on your CPU then you should mount one on the heat sink and then plug it into the CPU fan slot. This way the MOBO can configure the fan speed, minimizing noise. If you already have one then just plug it in where ever is most convenient.
