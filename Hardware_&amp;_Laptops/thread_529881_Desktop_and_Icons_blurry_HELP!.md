### Post by Soybean on 2007-08-20
Hmm... I'm not that familiar with LG products. With most LCDs I've seen, though, there's a button on the monitor that brings up an on-screen menu, and that menu often has some useful information. For example, on the Dell monitor I'm using right now, at the bottom of the menu, it says 
> 
Resolution: 1280x1024 75Hz
Optimum: 1280x1024 60Hz

So I can clearly see that I'm running at the correct resolution, although my refresh rate isn't optimal (it doesn't usually matter).

You should see if your monitor has a similar feature, before ruling out resolution problems. It's possible that I was wrong about the ideal resolution for your monitor, or perhaps it didn't change like it was supposed to when you tried to adjust it.

If you can confirm that the display resolution matches the monitor's native resolution, and it's still fuzzy, my next guess would be that there's something wrong with the monitor. If you could test the monitor with another computer, or even another OS if you dual-boot, that would help in narrowing down the trouble.


Edit: Just thought of something else. Sometimes those on-screen menus have options for sharpening the image or otherwise making it look better. In my experience, the default settings are usually pretty good, and my fiddling just makes things worse... but it's something else you could try.
