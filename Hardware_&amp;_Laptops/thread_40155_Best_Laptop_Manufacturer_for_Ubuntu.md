### Post by costoa on 2005-06-09
I'm with bored2k on this. For your price range (~$400USD) an used IBM Thinkpad is the way to go. TPs IMO are some of the best built machines and, more importantly, they rarely make a bad machine. It's worth paying extra for one.

Guessing from your icon you're in the Bay area. If so, you have a wide selection of places and people to buy from. Check out UC Berkeley students that are upgrading.

If you can make sure you test out the machine before buying. Run memtest86 off a live distro. Look for dead pixels on the screen, major cracks in the case, a loose power plug or boot errors that might point to a dying HD. If it can't run for more than 10 or 20 minutes without locking up it might have a bad motherboard.

You need to decide what kind of screen you want: active or passive lcd. Active lcds have a great look, work well in low light, a more uniform screen quality and fast updating. They also use a lot more power than passive lcds. If you're always going to be near an AC outlet 90% of the time then an active lcd would be nice. If you need to stretch battery life than I would choose passive. 

Chances are the battery is going to be "tired" and will hold less of a charge than a new one. A new one will be about ~$80 but worth it if you plan on being away from an AC outlet.

Finding an used TP with a bad HD might be a good opportunity for a little hacking. Replacing the HD for a CompactFlash card(s) (1G for $75, 2G for $125) and IDE adapter ($20) would add a fair mount time towards battery usage. Some of the adapters support dual CF cards. Since a $400 TP will most likely have <1G HD a new replacement 20G can be found for ~$60. IMO I might get the machine with a bad HD and fix with either suggestion.

While more than you want to spend I just bought a Sharp PC-MC24 for $900 at my local BJ's. It's ~5 pounds and small with a 12" screen. Runs Ubuntu well. The built in 802.11b/g sets up quickly with a bit of tweaking. I'm still working on the power management though (user error not Ubuntu's). It's a great little machine for, IMO, a great price.

Good luck and post the model information here for some feedback.
