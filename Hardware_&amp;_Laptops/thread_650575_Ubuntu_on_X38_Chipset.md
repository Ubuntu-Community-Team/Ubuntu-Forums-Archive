### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-12-27
Your best bet is to try a beta of Hardy Heron as it will have the latest (or near-latest kernel).  Intel is pretty good about providing specifications for their chipsets.  

It's amazing how motherboard manufacturers (such as Aopen) completely ignore them when designing small form-factor motherboards and enclosures.  They ignore simple thermodynamic principles.  Intel says to provide a 2" by 2" by 2" 17-fin heat sink for it's graphic chip.  Aopen:  "Let's put in a 1" by 1" by 1" 12-fin and call it a day."  

Although not as flexible as other motherboards, I like Intel-built boards because at least they follow the guidelines that they publish.
