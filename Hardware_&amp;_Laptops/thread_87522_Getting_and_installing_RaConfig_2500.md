### Post by Who on 2005-11-08
Well, I would say there is no harm in getting the drivers and the utility - as the ones from the rt2x00 prject site are likely to be more up to date than the Ubuntu ones.
The website is
[http://rt2x00.serialmonkey.com/](http://rt2x00.serialmonkey.com/)
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com//Rt2500WirelessCardsHowTo/](https://wiki.ubuntu.com//Rt2500WirelessCardsHowTo/) is the howto for installing the driver and utility.
However, my _guess_ is that Ubuntu will have set up the driver as 'normal' and in a very similar way to the way howto suggests - thus, you _may_ be successfull just installing the utility, as per the section in the howto...

Good luck. You were lucky to get a laptop with a Linux compatible chipset!
