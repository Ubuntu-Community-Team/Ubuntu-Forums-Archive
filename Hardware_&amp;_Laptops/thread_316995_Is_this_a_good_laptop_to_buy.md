### Post by mcduck on 2006-12-11
Sempron is rather under-powered CPU, so even if it might do the job for you I'd recommend trying to find something with Core CPU..

Also, HyperMemory is just Ati's nice way to tell you that the graphics card uses (partly or only) your RAM. I suppose that card has max 128MB of dedicated graphics memory and the rest is taken from system RAM. It might even have no dedicated memory at all..

### Post by stanjam on 2006-12-13
I build my own laptops now.  The latest one I built about a year ago.  The nice thing about building your own is you decide what the hardware is (Linux compatible) and you don't have to spend money on a Windows Operating System.  The system I bought was around $1500 about a year ago on directron.com.  Here are some of the specs:

Asus Z71V laptop
Intel Pentium M 2.0 Ghz
Nvidia go 6600 Vid card with 128 MB mem
2G DDR2 memory
internal Intel IPW 2200 ABG wireless card
PCMIA DLink BG wireless card (got for the boost at home)
free laptop case (not a bad one either- ASUS threw that in)
15.4" Widescreen LCD display

Saved myself some cash and was VERY easy to build.  The bonus came when I installed Ubuntu edgy the other day.  Went on without a hitch and everything worked out of the box!  Works even better with a modified xorg.conf file!  Well, I haven't tried the smart card reader yet, so no idea on that one.
