### Post by jml on 2007-04-25
Right now the only laptop I am aware of as being completely Ubuntu compatible out of the box is one purchased from System 76. (there may be other vendors supporting Ubuntu, but I'm not sure.)  

[http://www.system76.com/](http://www.system76.com/)

That is what they have built their business model on.  The computers cost a little bit more than a Windows based laptop with similar specs, but in my mind, the value they add making sure everything works out of the box,  the fact that they give fantastic aftermarket support for both their hardware and Ubuntu, and that they sponsor a sub -forum here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=158](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=158)

Makes the slightly increased cost well worth it.  I purchased a Darter from them and am very pleased.

If you want to "roll your own" so to speak, I agree with the others who have recommended ThinkPads, and HP laptops.  In fact HP offers laptops on their small business web site (as opposed to their consumer website) that can be ordered without Windows.  Good luck on your search for a laptop.

Joe
