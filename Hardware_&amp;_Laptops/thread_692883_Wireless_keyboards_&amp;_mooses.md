### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-02-10
waiting for a device to get added may take a bit of time, meanwhile you still have as you stated "you get what you pay for".

Not saying to run out and spend the money, but next time you consider buying hardware, you should look and ask first.  Cheap is not necessarily GOOD!
I have wireless on EVERY ONE of the 5 computers I have running (two share a D-Link KVM with audio switch).  I use ONLY Logitech pointing devices ( three marbles and one actual mouse) (found that MS units have a very limited range) and I have three Logitech ergonomic keyboards and one Microsoft (smaller footprint) ..  I do not CARE if all of the extra buttons work, but most DO (since I can accomplish the same tasks from the desktop).  I DO care that the keyboards and pointers are dependable and WORK in all of their basic functions.  
AND, I got every unit on eBay for a BIG savings over buying retail.
