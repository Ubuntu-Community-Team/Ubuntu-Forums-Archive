### Post by shadov on 2007-12-10
I'm building something similar. I think I'll get something like this:

[Antec NSK2480-EC (with 380 W EarthWatts PSU)](http://www.antec.com/ec/productDetails.php?ProdID=00280)
[ASUS P5K-VM Intel G33 MicroATX motherboard](http://www.asus.com/products.aspx?l1=3&l2=11&l3=542&l4=0&model=1690&modelmenu=1)
E2140 CPU
2GB RAM
500 GB HD

I can get that for about 380 EUR here in Finland. The G33 chipset on the motherboard seems to be quite well supported ([http://gentoo-wiki.com/HARDWARE_Intel_G33,_Q35,_and_Q33_Chipsets](http://gentoo-wiki.com/HARDWARE_Intel_G33,_Q35,_and_Q33_Chipsets)). It has GMA 3100 integrated graphics for which Intel provides open source drivers. The G33 also supports next generation Penryn CPUs so you can upgrade later. I haven't really looked into other chipsets yet so there might be better ones out there.

So my advice, if you want small form factor with integrated graphics, would be to find some G33 motherboard you like. But look into other chipsets too because I'm not an expert and haven't done enough research.

AMD vs. Intel is a good question when buying low end machine. I think AMD might give you slightly better price/performance ratio with less energy consumption especially when idling. Though if you are into over clocking Intel gives more bang for the buck. If you plan to upgrade your CPU in the future you need to look at todays or tomorrows high end CPUs. There Intel is clearly the winner.

For case choose whatever pleases your eye, then google for a review. Repeat until you find one you like. Hardware enthusiasts would tell you to buy a powerful and reliable (and very expensive) PSU separately. I'd say use whatever that comes with the case but avoid cases that come with bad PSUs. Or get a very cheap case without PSU and buy some decent quality but not very powerful PSU separately.

Antec has some quite affordable, decent looking and reasonably quiet cases that come bundled with a PSU. The EarthWatts PSUs that come with the cases have good reputation. At least at the moment. Last time I bought an Antec case with PSU that had a good reputation on internet forums, the reputation didn't last long. :) Haven't had any problems with mine though.
