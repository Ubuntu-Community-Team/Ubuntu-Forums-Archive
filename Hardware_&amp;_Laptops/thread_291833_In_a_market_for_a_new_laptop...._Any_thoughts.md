### Post by tturrisi on 2006-11-03
How to buy a laptop:
1. know ALL the things you want to use it for. (www,developement, gaming, etc)
2. is it a desktop replacement or just for travel?
3. wireless a must? (research best wifi chipsets)
4. weight
5. battery power life
6. keyboard (nothing beats trying it prior to purchase)
7. comfort (see 6)
8. screen quality (some are crappy compared to others)
9. video (own memory is better than shared memory)
10, upgradability (max ram, etc.)
11. burn cds & burn dvds?
12. available ports for accessories & external hardware (printer, mouse, spkrs)

Do research (reading) & then go to various stores to try out the laptops so as to get an feel for everything.  Take a live cd w/ you & boot the store demo laptops using the cd & see how it responds to Linux.  Most laptops come w/ bios setup to boot from cd before hd.

Dell sucks unless you plan on wiping the XP install.  Dells come preloaded w/ spyware, useless apps and AOL bundles, and the quality of the hardware they are using has dropped in the last few years.
