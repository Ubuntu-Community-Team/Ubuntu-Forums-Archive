### Post by bodycoach2 on 2007-06-04
I've had it. You've had it. You know you have. The FEISTY CRITICAL TEMPERATURE issue. I think I found a solution!

Install Feisty without the battery in. It worked for me. From playing a little too much with my system, I mucked it up. Bad habit, but it was my own fault. Not Linux, Ubuntu, or Feisty at all. Mine. That, and I love taking credit for disasters. Makes me feel powerful.

Anyway, I had the same 'critical temperature reached' issue when trying to install Feisty. I tried all kinds of cooling solutions. Nothing worked. Then I though, "*Maybe, it's not the processor temperature!*" I took the battery out, powered it up, and installed perfectly. Put the battery in after getting everything installed. No problems.

Anyone getting this critical temp error on a laptop, try taking out the battery to install. See if it works. Can't believe I didn't try it before. Would have been a whole lot easier than installing Edgy, then upgrading.
