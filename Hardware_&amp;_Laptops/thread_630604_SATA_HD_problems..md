### Post by JNowka on 2007-12-03
I just recently got a Seagate Baracuda 320GB SATA HD with 16MB cache and 7200 RPM.  I installed it into my computer, and loaded up Ubuntu.  I created the partition, formated the whole thing as ext3, and checked out the new drive.  
 
Apparently the freshly formatted HD means that 5GB of HD space is already taken up.  I thought, what the hell probably just the file tables, then I copied over 300MB worth of data.  I suddenly had used 14gb of HD space.
 
To tell the truth I got a SATA 300 and my port is a SATA 150.  But I made sure that the jumper was set correctly before installing the HD so that it could be used with SATA 150 controllers.
