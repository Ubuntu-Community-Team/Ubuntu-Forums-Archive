### Post by davehein@theriver.com on 2007-12-26
The ASUS eee PC is very popular, and the eee PC user community is busy trying to develop a customized version of 7.10 that installs and runs without hassles or user tweaks. A current Ubuntu runs just fine from an external CD/DVD reader through a USB port, after tweaking the boot sequence in the BIOS. However, there are problems. Wi-fi doesn't work (and wi-fi is one ofthe eee PC's major charms). Some screens don't work right with the ee PC screen resolution (800x480). Etc.
If other community members (either community) want to play with custom releases, that may be the way Linux works. For me, the real issue is the following:
Will Ubuntu itself release a version that will directly install on an eee PC and be fully functional? I wouldn't play with a version from anybody else, but I would love to run real Ubuntu on my eee PC, if it comes directly from the Ubuntu project.

### Post by chilinski on 2008-03-14
On the third day I owned the EEEPC, I installed Gutsy. I installed the patches recommended at the EEE forum. I use my wireless at home (right now, as a matter of fact), at work and as a guest at University of Virginia Med Center while waiting for a doctor's appointment. I also use wired at work and a Sprint USB broadband card. Though there are people who can't get it to work, I have not had ANY negative issues at all.

I am IT director and have been doing this for years and years. To me, the EEEPC is the best. I gave up my Thinkpad X40 running XP Pro, and I have been able to do everything I need to do. The last hurdle was being able to fill out and digitally sign PDF forms. With Acrobat 8.x Pro's ability now to extend rights to Reader (allowing the saving of PDF forms and digital signatures) and Reader 8 for Linux, I'm set.
