### Post by Jorenko on 2007-05-05
Yesterday, my iPod (5.0G video) ran out of batteries. When I got home, I connected it to my computer (running feisty) and went to sleep. Upon waking this morning, it would still not boot and was not recognized by my computer. Apple's support says that this can happen for up to ten minutes after plugging in a fully discharged iPod, but of course it had been hours.

I decided to boot into windows since, of course, I'd have the official drivers there. After 2 minutes in windows, I unplugged the iPod and it booted immediately. It was still at about 1% battery. I'm forced to conclude that there is something in the official driver that wakes a fully discharged iPod up and allows it to charge, which is missing from the linux driver.

Has anyone else seen this, or know where to report it to get it fixed?
