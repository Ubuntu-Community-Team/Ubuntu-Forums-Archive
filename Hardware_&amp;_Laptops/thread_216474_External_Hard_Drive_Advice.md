### Post by futz on 2006-07-15
> **PhilB said:**
> One that has cought my eye is the Lacie 500 GB Brick as it seems to offer a lot of hard disk for the money.
You might wanna check Google for reviews/articles on those. I heard one horror story of a Lacie product (might have been that one) that failed WAY too soon. They pack two big hard drives in a tiny case with no cooling or even air space around/between them and the drives cook till they die. 

Big, fast drives make a fair bit of heat. They need some airflow past them to keep em from getting VERY hot. I had two 200GB drives mounted together in my #1 case. Even with the steel drive cage sinking heat from them, they got scary hot. The whole front of the case was hot to the touch. I finally separated them by one drive space and installed a front intake fan in front of them to move some air past them. Cured! Nice and cool now.

Also, you can buy a cheap USB2.0 enclosure and a single big drive and a cable and assemble it yourself (it's easy). They work great! Look for an enclosure with some form of active cooling if possible. If your computer is old and doesn't have USB2.0 built in, you can buy a PCI USB2.0 card for around $7.00 or thereabouts.
