### Post by Dngrsone on 2008-01-03
Surge suppressors will only work so much... a lightning strike or huge surge can blast through a powerstrip and kill a computer easily.

Likely the PSUs are shot, but as a technician, if I think a computer has been lightning-damaged, I call the entire unit dead-- it may be only a PSU is damage, but more likely the entire machine is at least degraded, if not downright dead.

### Post by SoloSalsa on 2008-01-03
Dells?  I don't really like that company, but they have done one great thing: they put lights on their desktops for debugging.  Every Dell box I have ever used or seen has four LEDs (labelled 'a' through 'd') on the case.  For example, I just went to the Dell site, picked a model at random, and this is the page about it: [System Codes and Messages]("http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/dmum/codemess.htm").  They do this for quickly identifying problems with their millions of built-to-order machines.  It really is a great idea, and it surely has helped them as the worlds biggest computer brand.
So, anyway, try looking those up.  Find you model number in [this list]("http://support.dell.com/support/topics/global.aspx/support/my_systems_info/manuals?c=us&l=en&s=gen"), go to the manuals and documentation section for your model, then find the article about system codes or whatnot.  It should tell you where you can find those diagnostic lights.
