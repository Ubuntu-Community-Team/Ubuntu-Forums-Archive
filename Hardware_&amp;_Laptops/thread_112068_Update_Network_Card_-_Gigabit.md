[/quote]

Yes. Completely automatic. If the card is supported, you should have to do very little to get it working again, just re-check your settings in the network setup screen to see if they are all still correct.

> 
2. Is there wide support for Gigabit Ethernet adaptors in Breezy?


There *is* support for gigabit in breezy, and all modern linux as far as i'm aware. The best thing to do is before you buy a particular card, google for the exact card model and the word 'linux' to see if it is supported. If a module exists for it, and it's a fairly popular card, it should be supported.

> 
3. What card would be easiest to get working under Breezy?  I have looked at a D-Link, Netgear, Linksys, TrendNet, etc.  How do I know which one will (may) work?


D-Link, Linksys, Cisco, and sometimes Netgear are usually the best to go with. I have always had best luck with Cisco kit when coming to networking. It's more expensive, but it's always worked first time, including all wireless stuff.
