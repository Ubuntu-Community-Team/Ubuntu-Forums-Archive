### Post by Packard on 2006-06-28
How exactly are you trying to install the printer? I'm using KDE and all you have to do is click on "System Settings" in the K-menu, then printers and then add printer. There you can choose your printer and driver, click OK and it's all done.

If you want to manage CUPS through your browser, you first have to adapt the file /etc/cups/cupsd.conf and allow this.
