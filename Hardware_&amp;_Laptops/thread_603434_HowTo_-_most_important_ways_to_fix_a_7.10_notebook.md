### Post by carac on 2007-11-05
Please only post in this thread important fixes, workarounds or hints - if you just describe a problem/bug please do so in one of the threads dedicated to that subject (like for instance [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=579781](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=579781) ).

In my own personal experience with Ubuntu 7.10 gutsy on a number of laptops the most important source of problems are:

**[COLOR="Red"]1.  bcm43xx driver[/COLOR]** (for Broadcom wireless cards) - it might work on some cards but it seems that from around bcm4318 and up it is broken (certainly on bcm4324) - very high chances to have problems on a/b/g Broadcom adapters; there are [COLOR="Navy"]**2 symptoms**[/COLOR] generated by that - **[COLOR="Navy"]1a) very-very slow wireless network connection/speed[/COLOR]** and more important **[COLOR="Navy"]1b) problems on suspend/hibernate/resume !!![/COLOR]**
**[COLOR="Lime"]The workaround[/COLOR]** is to blacklist bcm43xx (add 'blacklist bcm43xx' in /etc/modprobe.d/blacklist) and use ndiswrapper instead (a real fix would be to add better support for 4318/4324 to the bcm43xx driver).

[COLOR="Red"]**2. the new 'intel experimental modesetting ...' video driver**[/COLOR] - having experimental in the name should have been a hint to avoid making it the default video driver for all intel video configurations :)
[COLOR="Navy"]**Main symptom - problems on suspend/hibernate/resume**[/COLOR] - might not be as consistent as the problems with bcm43xx but still in certain configurations it will generate problems!
**[COLOR="Lime"]The workaround[/COLOR]** is to replace 'intel experimental modesetting ...' video driver with something like i810 or similar (it can be done from GUI - System -> Administration -> Screens and Graphics); a good idea for certain laptop configurations is to also replace the default plug&play screen with the precise resolution of your LCD screen (in order to minimize the amount of autodetection that can go wrong - the hint for that is when on resume you find yourself in a strange resolution).

There are certainly many other potential problems (like 3D or using newer ATI/NVidia cards) - but people that have actually fixed such problems (ideally on more than one notebook model) should post the workarounds/fixes/hints for that!!!
