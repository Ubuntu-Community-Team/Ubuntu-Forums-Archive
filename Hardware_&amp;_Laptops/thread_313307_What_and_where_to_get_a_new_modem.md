### Post by Bartender on 2006-12-05
The trick is to find a full hardware modem, one that does all the computing work instead of pawning part of it off on the CPU and the Windows operating system.  

One of the hands-down simplest ways to do this is go to eBay and buy either a serial US Robotics 56K external modem (5686 series) or the US Robotics internal PCI (5610 series) modem.  Watch out for old 14.4 or 28.8 versions.  You want a fairly new one.  The black USR externals are newer than the beige ones.

There are other brands, but the USR's are well-known for just working.

If you get an external USR set the 3, 5, and 8 dipswitches down, the rest up.  That's the way my 5686-03 is configured.  I checked with a couple of other people and theirs was the same.
