### Post by jeffbarish on 2006-12-16
I struggled with my Epson Perfection 3170.  When I first installed 6.10RC, I got it to work using the Epkowa drivers and Vuescan.  After a week, it mysteriously stopped working.  I believe that some routine apt upgrade caused the problem by overwriting something in the nonstandard Epkowa installation.  I was never able to get it to work again.  After I installed 6.10 (final), it did not work.  I read the postings here from people who have struggled with the same scanner, including a few who reported success.  I got as far as converting the Epkowa rpm's to deb, but then I stopped.  I was uncomfortable installing a package by way of alien, especially when the conversion required the use of --scripts (read the warning on the man page).  Would I be able to back out if the installation failed?  Would the installation overwrite something else?  Also, if my theory is correct that a routine apt upgrade broke the previous functional installation, I figured that I would be fighting an ongoing battle to keep the scanner functioning.  A solution that uses standard software installed in the standard way should be more robust.  So, I purchased a Canon LiDE25 scanner ($50 at Newegg).  It just worked as soon as I connected it (after installing sane).  I figure that my time is worth way more than the $50 I spent, and this scanner works with standard frontends like Kooka and xscanimage.  The scanner is probably not as good as the Epson -- it's certainly slower -- but I figure it's good enough.  If I ever upgrade it, I will be sure to choose one that is supported by sane.  I must have been insane wasting so much time trying to get the Epson to work.
