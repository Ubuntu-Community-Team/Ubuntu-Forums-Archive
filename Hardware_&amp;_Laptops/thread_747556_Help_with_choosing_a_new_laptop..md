First just a bit of background on where I'm coming from, so as to put into perspective my thoughts here. With all due modesty, I'm somewhat of a Laptop Expert. I have owned over 60 laptops in the last decade....am I sick or what? ......:)

I have used a laptop as my main computer for that same period of time. I travel extensively and am the founder / CEO of a technology company, therefore a laptop is ideal for me. The brands that I have first hand experience with are many. In every case I have owned at least 3 of each brand before forming an opinion. A partial list is: Toshiba, Compact, IBM, Lenovo, Apple, Acer, Gateway, Dell, HP, Fujitsu, Asus, Sony, Panasonic & Sharp. 
Bottom line, after it's all said and done, I have found that the IBM (now Lenovo) ThinkPad lineup to be the very finest laptops by a wide margin. I've owned and used them as my main laptop of choice since the advent of the 600 model. Due to the nature of my work, and my enthusiasm for laptops , I have upgraded to the latest ThinkPad (T-Series) every 10 to 12 months since I put my first 600 into service. The T-series has been, and continues to be my mission critical laptop of choice. Since I have been using them since the IBM days, and now the Lenovo days, I can honestly say that to date, there has been absolutely no compromise in quality, or performance since the Lenovo transition. I have extensive experience with their T & X series machines having owned many of each. My current inventory includes: T60, T60p, T61, X60, X60s and finally my R51e which I just installed Ubuntu 7.10 on only 3 days ago. I chose the R model as an inexpensive way to try out Ubuntu on a new ThinkPad.  

ThinkPads have world class keyboards, second to none. Terrific displays, first class components and build quality throughout. The track stick is very precise, and now we have both to choose from, the best of all worlds. While some have been critical, calling the touch pad "small", I will personally assure you that it's so responsive and accurate that it simply does not need to be any larger. The case material is very durable with hinges that are stellar. The "ultra-bay slim" is a great creation, as one can insert a wide variety of devices. Because of their light weight, I carry an optical drive, an ultra bay battery, and the bay in mine currently houses a second hard drive. While as stated earlier I have owned many laptops, most of the others have been just for fun, to see what's new, and to carry as an "extra" if needed. However that said, I have never been without my trusty ThinkPad which I use everywhere, everyday for several hours. 

I'm positive that you will be thrilled with your purchase and find that you will enjoy a great return on your investment. Finally regarding the "Lenovo delivery issues / delays" this has been an ongoing situation dating way back to the IBM ThinkPad T series intro. The rumors, or statements of a disorganized distribution system are wrong. The popularity of this series remains very strong, and the ability of Lenovo to keep up with the demand does indeed fluctuate based on component availability and other factors. Your patience (and mine) when ordering and waiting to take delivery of a new ThinkPad will be nothing more than a quickly forgotten memory once you have it in your hands. The wait is well worth it. 

Cheers!
