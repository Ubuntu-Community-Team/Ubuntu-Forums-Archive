### Post by bmt on 2007-04-03
I recently bought a Lenovo -- not a very high spec. but quite a good price as the dealers were clearing old XP stock, I suppose.

Broadcom 4311 wireless was not a problem (ndiswrapper) and the Intel graphics are fine for me.

However, the BIG problem is the Intel audio.  It just doesn't work at all, without breaking many other features (almost all of acpi has to be turned off).  Even then, it doesn't work well -- for example, the internal speakers cannot be turned off, even with headphones.  Unfortunately, this makes the machine useless for many of the things I bought it for, so I will probably keep XP loaded.

The Intel audio problems affect many brands and models (just Google hda-intel ...).  Some can be fixed with simple option settings, but many continue to have problems.
