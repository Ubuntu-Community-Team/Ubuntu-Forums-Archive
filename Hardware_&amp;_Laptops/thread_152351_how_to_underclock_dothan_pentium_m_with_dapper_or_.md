### Post by YuHoo on 2006-03-29
I'm not positive about what the packard bell run for a BIOS, but with my Dell there is an option to allow CPU scaling. It automatically decreases my CPU clock to increase battery life and decrease the heat output. I would also make sure that your laptop supports this function before trying to do a software sided under/over clock. This can sometimes damage your CPU, but even more important is 99% of the time it will automatically void your warranty just for even trying. So you're really only limited to what Packard Bell gives you through the BIOS or software they give you, but whatever you do, please do not force it. Laptops need their warranties to be happy, without it they kill themselves out of spite:rolleyes:.
