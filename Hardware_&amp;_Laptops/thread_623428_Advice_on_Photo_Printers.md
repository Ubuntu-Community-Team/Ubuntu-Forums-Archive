Evening friends,
As my title suggests I'm looking to buy a photo printer. While I do have a laptop running windows I want a photo printer (Not necessarily a MFP) that prints very good photos and is compatible with Ubuntu. 

My criteria are these;
1. Compatible with Ubuntu
2. Available for sale now, not 2 year old stock that is hard to find.
3. Prints great photos
4. No more than $230 before taxes.
5. Uses more than 2 print cartridges, preferably 4 or more. 
6. I don't care that much about how well it prints black text I have a laser for that.

I know that is a lot to consider. Honestly if your game to help start with criteria 1-3 and I can worry about the last 3. Compatibility is the hardest to find out.

### Post by SonicSteve on 2007-12-13
OK so in case anyone cares.....The winner was
HP photosmart D7260. With the HP imaging and printing for Linux software installed it worked perfectly. I connected it with ethernet meaning it's fully networked. The printer is great. Photo's are very good. Not lab quality but good enough that I don't feel cheated.
I sent a note to HP thanking them for the HP linux project.

EDIT
One more thing, You'll need to download and install the opensource HP drivers from HPLIP
[http://hplip.sourceforge.net/](http://hplip.sourceforge.net/)
The install was fairly easy and they have documentation to help as well. I tried installing on a 2nd different PC and had unfortunately different results.
