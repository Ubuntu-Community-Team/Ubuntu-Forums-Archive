### Post by saspo on 2008-04-14
I have been thinking how much the style of the EEE pc had to do with it's success.  I do believe that it was substantial.  If we were to ride the same wave and introduce the world to VESA mounted (on the back of your LCD monitor) mini pc's I do believe we can succeed as well.  The unit I have in mind is the e-Box4300 ($231)   [http://www.embeddedpc.net/eBox4300/tabid/190/Default.aspx](http://www.embeddedpc.net/eBox4300/tabid/190/Default.aspx)
This will make for a good conversational piece by virtue of its size and low carbon footprint, 15 Watts.  Maybe Canonical can team up with the vendor to do a promotion similar to the one with KOOLU [http://koolu.com/Koolu-WE-Appliance/Works-Everywhere-Appliance.html](http://koolu.com/Koolu-WE-Appliance/Works-Everywhere-Appliance.html) 
which comes with Ubuntu preloaded.  I like the KOOLU but I have a soft spot for the e-Box 4300 because it disappears behind the LCD.  The KOOLU is definitely better in terms of simplicity and in power consumption, at <10 Watts.  Finally both boxes need to upgrade to DVI, vs. the present VGA, and embrace 1.8" SSD's, or soldered on flash, a la EEE, min. 4gb. I just like the idea of complete silence, no whining hard drive noises. If only a manufacturer can combine the VESA capability of the Ebox with the simplicity of the KOOLU, then we have a serious box for Ubuntu.  I do blieve a price point  of $250 or less will be ideal.
