### Post by anil_robo on 2005-12-25
The gold standard of an operating system is, according to me, one that even an idiot can use, because it'd be so simple.To simplify installation, Ubuntu developers are already doing an excellent job! All praise to Mark Shuttleworth and his team! :)

However, to go even further towards a level where even an idiot can install and use Ubuntu, the hardware manufacturers have to do something on their behalf too. Just like some modems are manufactured so that they work only on windows (Winmodems) and SD memory cards don't work under Linux. If the hardware manufacturers keep Linux in their mind when they make hardware, everything really would be so simple! You plug it, and it plays for you! :)

Another solution would be what Apple is doing. They are manufacturing complete units with their own components, so there are no problems of compatibility. They ship the entire working system to you, and it works! :)

Ubuntu would have problems following the same strategy, as it would tie it down to money, and the entire pleasure of open source could be spoiled this way.

Initially I hated reading how-to's for Linux because I hated (and still hate) terminal, but I'm kinda liking it sometimes (when I have free time) because I "learn" something that way!

With Linux by your side, you couldn't help learning! :D
