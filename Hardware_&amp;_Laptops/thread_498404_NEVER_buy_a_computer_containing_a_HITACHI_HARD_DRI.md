### Post by arsenic23 on 2007-07-12
I think perhaps you should be complaining about Acer and not Hitachi.  I really don't think Hitachi has had a bad model since their legendary IBM 'deathstar' back in... was that '99.  Well, anyway any drive manufacturer can put out a bad drive, it's pretty easy.

The point is that pretty much any company is going to do the same thing to you.  When you buy a retail PC you get the retail warranty you paid for, you should never expect a company to honor the OEM warranty on a particular part ( though sometimes you can trick them into doing it ).  About a year back our shop used to sell Acer laptops, and we never had any problems with the higher end ones, but mid and lower end laptops just seemed to drop like flies, and we had so much trouble with them that we dropped Acer all together.

We sell a lot of OEM drives so I thought perhaps you might be interested in my experiences, particularly because we handle all the warranty issues for our customers down to trying to retrieve their data off of botched drives:
******Western Digital and Hitachi drives have been our long lasters, they almost never end up back in the shop.
******Cheap Maxtor drives are just that, cheap.  I'm talking about the ones that are abnormally thin, I forget the model/type as we only sold a few of these before I stopped us buying them.  Other then that type I haven't had problems with Maxtor since 40gig drives were top of the line.
******Seagate drives seem just as good as the next guy, but most of them seem to run a little hotter then the competition.
