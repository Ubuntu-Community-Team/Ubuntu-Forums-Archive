### Post by ssam on 2005-05-24
ok, i'll answer this myself.

i ended up thinking i should get one of these
[http://www.griffintechnology.com/products/imic/](http://www.griffintechnology.com/products/imic/)
[http://www.turtlebeach.com/site/products/audioadvantage/producthome.asp](http://www.turtlebeach.com/site/products/audioadvantage/producthome.asp)

the imic had thumbs up from mac users, and they tend to care about quality, so i went for that.

now i have very low interference :-)

maybe sound card just should not be inside a computer.

sam

ps: i had to turn off the onboard sound to get it working. actually i probably didnt have to, but it seemed easier to flick an option in the bios, then mess around with outputs and sound servers, and kernel modules.
