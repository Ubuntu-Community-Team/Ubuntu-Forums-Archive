### Post by Lil on 2007-11-06
I have never had any problems with my T40 with a Radeon 7500; and I don't have any plans to upgrade for a long time yet. Video has always run like a champ (except H.264 in HD resolutions but that's a processor speed issue.) I have a T40 Pentium M 1.5Ghz and 1GB RAM and it works like a dream on Ubuntu 7.10.

The 3D effects do work and surprisingly well, they're not jerky in my experience, perhaps not as smooth as someone with a GeForce 8000 series or ATI Radeon HD series card but all the same impressive. The 3D cube has a few jitters but it's OK. I personally don't use 3D effects because I just don't need them but the basic level of compiz works fine; I've chosen to have it disabled as there are some glitches with Compiz enabled e.g. FSpot Slideshows which glitch a lot when Compiz/Desktop Effects are enabled.

I think you'll find the Radeon 7500 fine for what you want; the windows wobble fine as does the 'spogey windows' and mimising window effects.

Why not take a Gutsy live CD to it, boot it up, turn them on and see what you think. Otherwise I whole heartedly recommend the T4x series, fantastic laptops.

For reference:

T40:

- Some with Radeon 7500 - 32MB
- Others with Radeon 9000 (SXGA+ models mostly) 32MB

T40p

- FireGL 9000 (Based on Radeon 9000) - 64MB

T41:

As T40

T41p:

- FireGL T2 (Radeon 9600 based) - 128MB

T42:

- Radeon 7500 (Some) - 32MB
- Radeon 9000 (Some SXGA+) - 64MB
- Few with Radeon 9600 - 64MB

T42p:

As T41p

T43: Radeon X300 or Intel Integrated 915 (64MB)
T43p: Radeon FireGL V3200 (Based on Radeon X600) (128MB)

Vicky

### Post by Lil on 2007-11-06
Depends on the resolution, 640x480 (or roughly NTSC resolution) should be fine, but at 720p or 1080i/1080p it'll stutter. 720p you might get somewhere but it might still stutter.

To run H.264 in 720/1080 resolution I would recommend something in the region of a 1.86GHz Pentium M which might be a bit out of your budget. I have tried 720p on a 1.7GHz T42 and it was OK on some clips and not too good on others. 1080p was still out of its reach.

For really decent H.264 processing you are probably going to need something like a 2GHz Pentium M; that should handle 1080p fine as well.

I'm sure others will be able to tell you that they've got on fine with 1.7GHz Pentium M's; which maybe they have but I notice skipped frames *like that* so my standard is quite high. :)

As such you're going to be looking at T42s for built in fast Pentium M processors; although any T40/41/42 can be upgraded to 2.1GHz if you're handy with replacing the processor and cooling fan.

Vicky

### Post by Lil on 2007-11-06
Thanks, I am working on a revised guide for the T4x series in general for the latest version of Ubuntu Gutsy Gibbon.

As for the CPU upgrade, I've not done it myself as I have no need to upgrade the Pentium M Banias 1.5GHz; however on the Thinkpad forums ([http://forum.thinkpads.com](http://forum.thinkpads.com)) you should find plenty of guides there.

It's not difficult, you will need to replace the T40s short fan if you go above 1.6GHz probably with a 'long fan' and the upgrade is a drop in replacement. You can take a T40 up to 2.1GHz with a 400MHz bus Dothan but those are rare and too extortionate to really upgrade to. However you should find a 1.8GHz Dothan at a reasonable price.

Just make sure you don't look at the 533MHz FSB Dothan processors as those will only work on a T43.

Vicky
