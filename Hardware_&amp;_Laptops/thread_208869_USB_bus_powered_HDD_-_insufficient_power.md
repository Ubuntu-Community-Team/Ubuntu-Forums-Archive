### Post by woland on 2006-07-04
This post has very little to do with Linux, at least as I think. There is no problem for Ubuntu to recognize and access the drive.

I have a 2.5" Hitachi 40GB drive, 5200 rpm with power requirements of 1W at 5V.
It rests in bus powered USB enclosure with 2 connectors, one for data and power (mini USB) and the other for power only. There are two cables for these connectors, both plug into the USB ports of the computer.

When connected to my computer and some laptops, the drive is making a clicking sounds. It happens at power up's and/or at high load. 
It doesn't happen on new computers, or even when just the power cable connected to a new computer (data cable still connected to my computer). Furthermore, on many new computers, the drive operates without a glitch on data cable alone, on others it requires both of them.

I suspect that the problem is that, either the power supply or the main board fail to supply the HDD with enough power. And the clicking noise is made when the head performs an emergency parking when the voltage drops.
For now refrain from using the drive at home, in order to prevent possible damage.

I have a no-name, allegedly 400W power supply (at least that's what the label say) and the main board model is ASUS A7V880.

I can think of several possible solutions:
1) buy a new power supply
2) get a powered USB hub
3) use an external 5V adapter

Could you please advice, what would be the best choice?
Buying a new computer, or a new main board isn't a valid option ;)

### Post by thingy on 2006-07-04
Getting a powered hub is the best choice here. You get more USB ports(can't have enough of those) and a stable power supply for your USB enclosure.
