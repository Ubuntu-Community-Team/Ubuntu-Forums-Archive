Now I must ask, why these two small discs? Why a parallel ATA disc at all? I would say that you're best of buying at the lowest price point (price per gigabyte that is). A 40 GB PATA disc seems very bad value for money at the moment. I did not check your supplier. but last time I checked mine the best prices were for 320-400GB SATA discs. NOw of course such a disc will still cost more than a disc with lower capacity, but I do think that one 320 GB SATA disc is cheaper than a 160 GB SATA disc and a 40 GB PATA disc together. Anyhow, if you do want two discs, then why not buy two SATA discs of at least 160 GB each (the value for money for smaller discs is really very bad, the price difference between a 80 and a 160 GB disc being about 10$ and possibly less). The only exception to the above would be if you want a high speed disc like the Raptor.

As opposed to what that magazine lists, the BENQ only has 18 bit colour, the listed 16.2 million on the BENQ website means dithering. The Viewsonic also uses 18 bit with dithering, the Samsung should have 24 bit colour. What do you want to use the computer for? For games and watching videos the dithering shouldn't matter too much, and the higher speed more, for graphics work, you'll want to have the `true colour' (24-bit) display. I don't know how much the dithering will hinder you when using the computer for other tasks, from programming to writing to using the internet and what not more, I can say however that I did not yet find any problems with the lower speed of my true colour monitor in games nor videos. You can check the Viewsonic pro series as well if you want true colour. I assume you'll want to use the monitor for games (why else would you install windows XP), as well as more serious things. You should go and check some monitors in a shop, preferably both of the dithering but faster type and the true colour but slower type to see what works for you.
