### Post by Chris Tucker on 2006-01-12
i had the same problem once when overclocking a desktop, but undoing that particular change fixed the prob.

have you tried "kicking it in the ass"? that is, plug it in, turn it on, pull the power cord out, and yank the battery out. then reconnect and check.

do you have any devices that dont interface to the data pins but use the power? (usb light/fan?) i found these essential to figureing out why my keyboard wouldnt work. this makes sure that the problem is power not getting delivered, not just the device not being acknowledged.
