### Post by nkhansen on 2006-01-20
It will probably not run ubuntu.

I have tried with a Thinkpad 380D

Pentium MMX 150 MHz
48 mb ram
1,3 gb harddrive

It boots slowly, and it runs slowly in console, if you use a very lightweight windowsmanager, such as fluxbox. Haven't tried with GNOME, but I advise you not to either. GNOME runs slowly, even on 196 mb RAM.

I have found debian sarge to run a bit better, if you use the 2.4 kernel.

I installed DSL 2.1, and it runs a lot better than both Debian and Ubuntu. I have troubles getting my keyboard layout to work, though.

### Post by jnorguet on 2007-10-11
I remember great installation of Linux on the IBM Thinkpad 765L. Still, it was a Debain 2.0 at that time and Ubuntu has included many adidtions since then. However, I have received [successful reports of installing RedHat and SuSE Linux distributions on the Thinkpad 765L]("http://www.alternatifs.eu/jip/cs/linux/TP765L.html"). I do not foresee any reason why Ubuntu would not install at his turn, unless the thinkpad has no CD-ROM plugged inside. I also have received [several reports of BIOS issues]("http://www.alternatifs.eu/jip/cs/linux/TP765L.html") preventing from booting on the CD-ROM. Hope this helps!
