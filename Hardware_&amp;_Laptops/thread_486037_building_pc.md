### Post by dabl on 2007-06-27
YES, on everything but that Lite-On DVD burner -- you'd better search this forum for LITE-ON problems, and make sure about that one.  That may be a case where the hardware is ahead of the Linux drivers.

I have the same Nvidia graphics card -- it works great.

One more thing -- spend the $40 or whatever and get an Arctic Freezer Pro CPU heatsink, and the Arctic Silver 5 thermal paste.  Newegg has them.  Throw the included Intel heat sink away. Be super careful mounting the CPU, and then the heatsink on that -- it's very easy to mess up the little "feet" on these LGA heatsinks while trying to pop them through the holes on the motherboard.  Just give yourself plenty of time and elbow room.

:D

P.S. I'm just a little nervous about your PSU -- it might be a tad on the lightweight side. ### Post by xelink on 2007-07-23
to be blunt, i wouldn't trust that PSU. I own one myself. I only use it when I have nothing else. my old 125W units from PCs back in 1995 are more reliable and of better build quality.

my personal suggestion
Enhance enp-gh
[http://www.ewiz.com/detail.php?name=PS-E5150GH](http://www.ewiz.com/detail.php?name=PS-E5150GH)

same as the silverstone ST...plus 500W but without the price markup(enhance is one of sivlerstones OEMs)

FYI don't get case/PSU combos 95% of the time you WILL be disapointed. Masscool is on the list of mediocre/subpar PSU retailers FYI.

when buying PSUs, don't just look at rated wattage. What's rated and what's delivered can be two entirely diferent things. Check for ripple and voltage regulation etc. Often the only real way to find that out is from review sites. jonnyguru.com is a ratehr good one, he's well established throughout the overclocking communities.

then again I'm a bit picky with PSUs, I hang around overclocking forums and have the high quality PSU mantra forced onto me...
