### Post by mlapaglia on 2008-03-26
what card do you you have now? by smooth dual screen do you mean the setup, or after installation. I know that nvidia-settings can set up dual screens a lot better than the default installed application, although that might change for hardy (the new gnome has a better screens and resolutions app i think.)

For the virtualbox, if you try vmware the extra program you can install inside of windows to interface with the vmware program allows for much better performance. I run SPSS on a virtualized xp with a geforce go 7600 (laptop) and a core duo 2.0 ghz processor and when vmware is in fullscreen i don't even realize it is virtualized.

I don't play WoW, but i do play CS:S and garry's mod, each of which i can get ~80 fps on high settings..

so, depending on what card you currently have, the geforce 9's might be a good choice, but something cheaper might get the performance you want.
