### Post by summersab on 2007-11-01
I have a Gateway M275 tablet laptop, and I have gotten the stylus and rotation to work under Gusty (it was SOOO easy). However, when I rotate the screen clockwise, the calibration of the stylus is off. Under Xournal, the writing dot can be seen just above where it should be, but when I write, it writes several pixels above even that. Even stranger, when I go to the left edge of the screen (left under rotation, therefore it is the top edge of the screen technically), the cursor jumps around everywhere and almost acts as if it is being drawn back toward the center of the screen. Everything works fine when not rotated. Is there some calibration I can do using wacom-tools or xorg.conf that would fix this? Thanks!

---

### Post by summersab on 2007-11-01
That was easy. The solution is simply to rotate that screen FIRST and THEN open the program. I assume that this applies to users of GIMP and other tablet-type programs. Cheers, all!
