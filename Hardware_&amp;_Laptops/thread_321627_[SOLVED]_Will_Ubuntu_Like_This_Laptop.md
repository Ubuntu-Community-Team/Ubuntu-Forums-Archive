### Post by Dale61 on 2006-12-21
Because my laptop had Windoze pre-installed, everything worked when I tried the LiveCD.  Sound, display, modem, etc all worked as it did as per Windoze.

With that in mind, I decided that Windoze had to go, and did a clean install of Ubuntu.  By doing so, I wiped out all the Toshiba software drivers, and had no sound, no modem, no networking and no touchpad.  Fortunately, I had been using a USB mouse for some time and that worked with Ubuntu, so the loss of the touchpad was no big deal.

I found the wiki very helpful in getting the sound to work, but it was months of frustration before I was able to get online due to no modem or network card recognition.  I decided to take both my laptop and desktop into a pc repair shop and after three days (and A$80), I was able to connect my laptop to my desktop and update all that needed to be done.  In fact, this is being written on, and posted from, the laptop.

The only problem I'm having now is related to overheating - the laptop shuts down unexpectedly soon after the fans start running at full revs - and I often have the cpu indicate it is working at 2.79 GHz (with max at 2.80).

The only problem I've had with the DVD-ROM/CD-R/W drive is that I often have to insert the disc 2 or 3 times before it's read correctly.  When it doesn't read correctly, it shows that it's either a blank disc, or that there is no disc inserted.

Finally, I've read elsewhere that if your Toshiba laptop has a Phoenix motherboard, this could also cause problems.  The reason being is that a Toshiba with a Phoenix m/b is not really a Toshiba laptop at all.  Toshiba just put their name on it so as to get rid of obsolete (and sometimes faulty) parts.

### Post by Dale61 on 2006-12-21
Haven't bothered to even look at getting the touchpad to work.  Along with the mouse, I also have a USB keyboard, so I rarely use the bottom half of the laptop anyway.

Sound was easy enough, once I knew which wiki to follow.  The answers can be found on this forum, but with all the Edgy problems being posted, they could take some time to find.

Display drivers working fine.  I have an ATI Mobility Radeon 9100 graphics card, and have 3d rendering working.

I did have an overheating problem when with Windoze, but the problem is worse now that I have Ubuntu installed.  However, the main concern is the cpu constantly maxing out when doing seemingly simple tasks, like loading a webpage.  Even playing MP3's from the hard drive causes the cpu to max out, the program freezes (XMMS and Songbird), initialising the fans to run at full revs, then the laptop just goes black - shutdown.

The cd/dvd drive problem still has me scratching my head, as it has baulked at correctly reading discs since the install.  As it was annoying me, I actually used an external drive for OS installation, and have just learnt to live with it.  I've still got the external drive, but only have two USB ports, and I haven't yet tested to see if my external 4 port USB hub actually works with this configuration.  If it does, my aim is to have the mouse and keybourd plugged into the hub, plugged into one of the laptop ports, with the external drive plugged into the other.  This was the set-up I had for Windoze, but i just haven't been game to confuse the OS any more than necessary.

I've been using Ubuntu since late July, and now that I've got the gist of command lines, etc, I've vowed to never return to Windoze.
