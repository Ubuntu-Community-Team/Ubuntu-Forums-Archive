### Post by matthew on 2006-10-22
> **Aleksandersen said:**
> :-k I am used to iPods where Apple's software takes care of sorting music and such... How will this work on the iAUDIO 6? And what is the music playback interface like? Folder based?

:neutral: How did the firmware update go for the M5, matthew? Was the update easily applied to the player?
The firmware upgrade was easy and worked well. Basically you download the upgrade, unzip it, and move it into the "firmware" folder on the M5. There's a clearly written procedure on the iAudio firmware download page that workd perfectly.

The iAudio doesn't really sort the music. What I did (which is the same as on my hard drive) is put all of my music in folders and sub-folders based on artist/album name. So, for example, Eric Clapton has a folder with 5 or 6 different album sub-folders within it. The playback interface has a nice big information display that shows (using the tag data) the song name, artist, time and encoding and a couple of other settings-related things. You can play your songs according to the folder they're in, just one folder at a time or a folder and all its sub-folders, or from all folders, in order or randomly. You can adjust the EQ settings and a ton of other stuff. It's a clean and easy to use interface. You don't have any sort of search ability that I know of other than being organized when you load the player and then remembering where you put stuff, but I've used computers so long that I remember when that was the only way to find stuff on my hard drive and I still tend to keep stuff well organized, so this isn't an issue for me...it might be for some.
