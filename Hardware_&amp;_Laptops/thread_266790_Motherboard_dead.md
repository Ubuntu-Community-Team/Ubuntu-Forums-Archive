### Post by em3raldxiii on 2006-09-27
Mmm, hard to say my friend.  One of the first things I have learned to check nowadays is the capacitors on motherboards that misbehave.  I have found SO many boards with pooched capacitors it would make your head spin.  Here's what ya gotta do:

Have a good look at the capacitors.  They are the little mini soda-pop cans poking out of your board all over the place.  Some are big, some are small.  The ones we are MOSTLY concerned about are the medium-big ones, but check 'em all.  You are looking for the following symptoms:

1.  Obvious leakage out of the top (looks kinda like rust coming out of the top surface)
2.  Obvious leakage out of the bottom (looks like a dark stain usually, under the capacitor)
3.  Capacitors sitting at wierd-*** angles.  They should be just about stick-straight and perfectly flush on the bottom.
4.  Capacitors with bulged tops.  These ones can be hard to notice, but they shouldn't technically have ANY roundedness to their tops.  They should be PERFECTLY flat on top (often with a little shape stamped into it's surface, like an X or a little Y or a little K)
5.  Capacitors that have a little bit of rubber showing at their bottom.  This is probably the hardest type to notice because they look perfect in almost every other way.  If you can see a little black or dark brown rubber at the bottom of the capacitor, it could be toast.

Your computer can usually run passably well with a blown capacitor or two.  But if you have several, or if it's TOTALLY hooped (leaking and drooling all over the place), then your computer will hang, spontaneously reboot, not boot, beep crazily, or do other strange and wonderful circus tricks.

Now, we need a wee bit of clarification about your boot process.  You say you get to the BIOS screen, but I think you really mean the POST (power-on-self-test) screen, the one that asks you if you want to hit DEL or F12 to enter setup.  "Setup" here actually refers to the BIOS where you can edit all your settings.  And when you say "boots up fine"  I bet you really mean that it turns on and you get to see your boot screen.  Now if you touch nothing, does it boot all the way to your Operating System.

Here's a robust website dedicated to the world of blown capacitors:  [http://badcaps.com/]("http://badcaps.com/")
