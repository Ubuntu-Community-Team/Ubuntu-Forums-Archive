Sometimes motherboards are smaller or bigger, and that will force the case studs to be relocated accordingly. I am not sure I understood what the problem is. Is the stud touching the motherboard in a place that is not supposed to be grounded (like a grounding/installation hole for a screw)? If that is the case be aware that it may scratch the laquer protection layer and if left like that, it may shorten or ground the board where it is not designed for. Possible malfunction or even shut down may occur.
I would suggest you either remove that stud post, use the motherboard without it, or possibly relocate it into another threaded hole inside the case, opposing an appropriate grounding hole in the motherboard. Generally, some cases allow these grounding posts/ssssupprots to be relocated, to match the motherboards's installation holes that may vary slightly.
