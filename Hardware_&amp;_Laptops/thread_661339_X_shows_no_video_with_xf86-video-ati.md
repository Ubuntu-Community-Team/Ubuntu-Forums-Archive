### Post by noppengummi on 2008-01-13
I'm still trying to get my S-video to work too but I think I might tell you what i've found out so far:

- If you get a load bar at all then the problem lies in the xserver and therefore in a) your driver or b) your xorg.conf

Since you don't want to use any output but the tv-out, unplug your crt and rely on ssl and vnc to remotely configure through your network. I'm saying this because (at least in my case, radeon 9100 igp) some ati chipsets have a hard time finding out about the status of their tv-out if a vga is connected - some shared ressource thingie.

Then follow the steps given by the xrandr homepage: [http://xorg.freedesktop.org/wiki/radeonTV](http://xorg.freedesktop.org/wiki/radeonTV)

lastly, in order to force detection of s-video make sure you have the following line in the device-section of your xorg.conf:


```
        Option "TVDACLoadDetect" "TRUE"
```

This got me to a point where my tv gets some sort of signal, x loads without error. Unfortunately the screen just shows an unstable black picture but i figure thats a matter of configuring frequencies / modelines...

please keep us postet how it worked for you.
