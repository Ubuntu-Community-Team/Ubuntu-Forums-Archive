### Post by clsgis on 2008-01-18
This isn't really an Ubuntu issue.  You'll get better answers in a hardware forum.

30 C is pretty good for the CPU die temperature.  ICs are typically designed to work up to 70 C. and then the timing falls apart.  CMOS circuitry slows down as it heats up.  If you can keep it below 50, don't worry about it.

One mistake I've seen first time PC builders make is too much grease between the CPU and the heat sink.  Compared with aluminum or copper, grease is a heat insulator.  Too much grease and you have wrapped your CPU in a nice blanket to keep it toasty warm.  Use only enough grease to fill up the air gaps that come from the roughness of the metal.  I find spreading a thin layer with my finger and then wiping most of it off with a single edged razor blade works pretty well.
