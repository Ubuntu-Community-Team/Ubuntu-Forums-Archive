I recently set up a new computer for myself. I only use Linux and so I had as primary concerns:

Buy Nvidia - the chipset in motherboard and the graphical card.
Buy a supported sound card (with hardware mixer) - SoundBlaster Live! 5:1 (very cheap, sound is great, Linux supports it).

Apart from looking for good material (memories, disks and such), I also bought an AMD processor, but I think this is a matter of personal taste.

I had an IDE disk that fulfils my needs and an old CD recorder, so I skipped that. But take care about SATA2. I think there are some compatibility issues with it on Linux, but someone more experienced can clarify you that.

Lastly, I chose the most silent hardware I could get. Again, just a matter of personal taste and needs.



I'm happy with the result I got. My computer is running fine and stable. I pleased to notice that my concern when choosing the hardware have paid off.

Hope this helps you.
