### Post by ugm6hr on 2007-10-30
If you are donating to an institution, the important issues are:
1. Is the OS licence legal?
2. Who will support the computer after donation?

Because of 2. - I would suggest XP is a better option, since it is likely that an existing volunteer or employee will be able to ensure the computer is maintained correctly.  Unless, you know someone who is an (X)ubuntu user there already.

Of course, if you are not giving the institution your XP license, then I'd stick to Xubuntu.

I have installed Xubuntu 7.04 on a 750MHz P3 128MB system, and it's about as fast as XP once loaded, but perhaps a little quicker to boot.  I suspect 7.10 is similar.

If you do use Xubuntu, I would suggest VLC and Flash be added, since they will cope with most media between them.

The other option is a faster Desktop / Window Manager (e.g. IceWM, OpenBox etc), which would speed things up.  Fluxbuntu (7.10-based) may be an option, but Fluxbox is not particularly intuitive for Windows users .... [http://fluxbuntu.org/js.html#](http://fluxbuntu.org/js.html#)
