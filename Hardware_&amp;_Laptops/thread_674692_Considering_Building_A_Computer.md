### Post by dsauxier on 2008-01-22
> 
Celeron 420 1.60 GHz FSB 800 MHz L2 512 KB = $43.99

BIOSTAR P4M80-M4 478 VIA P4M800 Micro ATX Intel Motherboard – Retail = $79.99
WINTEC AMPO 512MB 184-Pin DDR SDRAM DDR 400 (PC 3200) Desktop Memory – Retail - $23.99


I'm thinking you could get a lot more bang for your buck by going with an AMD dual core.
I just put together a new machine using a [Gigabyte AM2]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16813128043") motherboard that was $59.99
You could put an [Athlon 64 FX2 4000+ (2.1Ghz)]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103774") in it for $59.99 - the CPU costs more than the celeron, but you're saving $20 on the motherboard and you end up with a dual-core system.

To make absolute best use of the dual core, you would go with 2 memory sticks, but according to Tom's Hardware, for the economy PC it makes sense to go with just 1 stick.

> 
EDIT: Forgot to mention, I don't know what power supply or fan to use.

You might keep looking for a different case that comes with a little bigger power supply.  They go on sale all the time... I bought one with a 500 watt power supply (which is overkill) for $40 (after $40 rebate).  I'm not sure exactly how big a pwr supply you need, but 300 seems kind of small.

Other things to consider for saving cash : 
Make sure they offer free shipping on the case.
Make sure the CPU you choose comes with the heat sink/CPU fan.

### Post by Whiffle on 2008-01-22
I would suggest getting a case that doesn't have a power supply, or one that has a brand name power supply with it.  $50 cases that   come with a power supply tend to have very junky power supplies, i've seen many fail, and they can take other parts with them too.  Spend the extra money and get a decent power supply.  

If you've    got time to wait, keep an eye on slickdeals.net for a while.  I could have built several fairly nice computers for not much money off of there.
