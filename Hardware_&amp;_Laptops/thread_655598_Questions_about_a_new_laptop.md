### Post by The Fire Snake on 2008-01-01
I am researching like crazy to find the least expensive/most powerful Linux compatible laptop myself.  I don't want to go for the Dell preinstalled one.  The biggest problem I am having is trying to buy a laptop online, especially from sites like Tiger Direct, newegg etc, like you.  They don't provide enough info about the hardware, they just say wireless 802.2 abg :confused:.  I would suggest you visit some local shops and look at the various pieces of hardware through device manager and such to make sure it has Linux compatible parts.  Or you could shop from Dell, Lenovo etc where they give more info about the hardware and you can build your own machine.  

If you buy any old laptop you might just wind up regretting it.  Most of the wireless nic cards are not linux compatible and you will be stuck running Ndiswrapper and many video cards are not that well supported as well, even the nvidia ones.

Also for that gateway one you might be able to find more detailed specs on the Gateway site for it.  Good luck.
