### Post by ericesque on 2006-07-05
If you have an older hdd, you may find that the most effective upgrade is a new hdd.  Ram can give you a boost, but you're buying outdated ram--which would be more expensive than it's worth.  The hdd is typically a system's bottleneck meaning that upgrading from a 5400 rpm drive with a 2mb cache to a 7200 rpm drive with 8 or 16mb cache could give you a nice boost that will be usable in a future computer as well.  

If it were my money, I'd put it into a nice hdd.
