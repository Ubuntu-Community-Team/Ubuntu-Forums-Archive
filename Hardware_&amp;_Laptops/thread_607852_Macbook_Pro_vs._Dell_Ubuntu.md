A macbook pro runs everything extremly well because its a very well engineered piece of hardware from a manufacturer with 20+ years experience making computers. it costs $2500 because they use great hardware and "spare no expense" . I am pretty sure that the Macbook Pro and the Inspiron 1420 are both Intel Santa Rosa chipset's so with equal processor speeds and equal memory sizes the users experience should be very similar.

### Post by Erunno on 2007-11-09
Although I've read a couple of reviews that state the overall good sturdiness of a MacBook Pro I would never buy a MacBook (or any other Apple product) without using Mac OS X on it. Part of the deal which make Apple's offerings appealing and the price bearable is the circumstance that you get software and hardware from the same vendor where both have been optimized to work well with each other. For instance, Mac OS X 10.5 runs incredibly fast on my MacBook (Core Duo 2 GHz, 1.25 GB RAM) and I didn't experience any compatibility and stability problems yet.

If you plan to use anything other than Mac OS X I'd say save your money and get a Dell Latitude which is also said to be very robust and reasonably fast for a much lower price. Or if you need a more powerful graphic card the Vostro series looks also also appealing  from a cost/benefit point of view although as far as I know it's a spin-off of the Inspirons.
