### Post by tommie-lie on 2006-01-15
[QUOTE=Muffe]I have an Acer Travelmate 3000 laptop that I have Ubuntu 5.10 on. It has 512 MB DDR2-RAM, and an 80 GB 2,5-inch 4200 RPM HDD.

What is the biggest bottleneck on this system? Shall I upgrade the HDD or the RAM first? I want both 2048 MB RAM and an 100 GB 7200 HDD, but can't afford both. What shall I buy first?[/quote]That depends completely on what you want to do.
What do you need 2GB of RAM for? If you often have to run many applications or applications that use huge amount of memory (i.e. if you plan to develop, do CAD, 3D-Animation, video processong), you should update the RAM. Usually, you only start the apps once when you start working, you surely can stand a bit of waiting for the hard disk. But *while* you are working, you sure do not want to wait after each mouse click due to the slow paging on harddisk, just because your RAM is too small.
If you want to store huge amounts of data in the harddisk and really need the 100GB, then you are better off when you update the harddisk first. But this is a kind of hen-egg-problem. Applications that produce such amounts of data often also need enough RAM (video processing, 3D animation).
You see, the "perfect" hardware configuration depends on your needs and your priorities, nobody of us can tell you what you should do if you don't tell us what you want to do with the hardware.
