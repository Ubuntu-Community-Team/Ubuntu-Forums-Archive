### Post by aaahoopy on 2007-04-24
All,

I blew away my perfectly working edgy install to do a fresh install of feisty on my Dell Inspiron 600M.  After install, the first thing I noticed was I no longer had scrolling on my touchpad.  After seeing all of the carnage related to touchpads and feisty I was cynical but decided to play anyway. 

I install qsynaptics and it made the situation worse.  After installing it, holding my finger still on the touchpad resulted in the cursor slowly drifting in a northwest direction.  Then I tried running the edge setup wizard (Is this what it is called? - on the bottom of the first qsynaptics page).  After running the wizard to reconfigure the edges of the touchpad, everything worked.  It doesn't work as well as it did on edgy (which required no configuration..), but it works.

I found that if I changed anything in the qsynaptics settings anywhere, my touchpad stopped working properly again, but rerunning the wizard put it back.

Everything else seemed to work pretty well.  Sound, graphics, etc.  

YMMV, Good Luck
John
