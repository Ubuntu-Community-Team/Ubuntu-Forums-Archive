### Post by teaker1s on 2007-04-04
While I do not know the answer, I can tell you how to go about finding out.

As a member of the hardware and unanswered question team we get many different types of hardware that we haven't seen, we can help yourself and others by finding out a **vendor and chipset id**.

My suggestion is, email or call the manufacturers technical department of the company, explain that you are planning to purchase this adapter.
You require to know whether it is a straight adapter(example usb to ps2 adapter) with no software driver required on any operating system.
Or if it needs a driver, could they provide you with **vendor and chipset id**. so you can determine if there is linux support for the chipset
