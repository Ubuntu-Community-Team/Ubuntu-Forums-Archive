### Post by dougr on 2005-10-07
I currently have an original ipod 5GB.  I am able to sync it up with gtkpod, and all that.  But like many others, I still have problems after unmounting the ipod, and then trying to plug it in again.  Most of the time after 2 times of plugging and unmounting, it will not be recognized again until I reboot the computer.  This is no big deal for my music collection, since I don't copy over a lot.  But I often like to listen to podcasts daily, so I don't like to be hassled rebooting all the time.  This computer is up 24/7 and I like it that way.  What I am looking for is a flash or small hard drive based player(prefer flash) that is 1GB or more.  I want it to be mass storage compliant in the sense that I can just drag and drop songs from my computer onto the mp3 player.  Then I will be able to browse these folders on the mp3 player, and play the song.  I don't want something that i need to create a database and all that like the ipod.  The player I was most interested in was the 1GB iriver IFP799T, but I read that it is no longer UMS compliant.

If you can recommmend a player that I can drag and drop mp3's to and then just play them, it would be appreciated.
