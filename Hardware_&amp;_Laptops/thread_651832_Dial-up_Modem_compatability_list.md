### Post by Crinos512 on 2007-12-28
I am attempting to compile a "Sticky-worthy" list of Dial up modems that work with Ubuntu with little to no problems... Therefore, if you have a success story with a dial up modem please reply and tell me what model it was, and if you needed to download any drivers to use it.

I would also prefer to stay away from the ["linuxant"]("http://www.linuxant.com/") modems which require you to buy drivers for a fully functional modem. (A simple link here should suffice.)

>  
[COLOR=Black]**  External Modems: (MOST Serial or Parallel Modems will work)** 
* Actiontec  56K EX560LKU (USB or Serial)
* Best Data 56USBSL (USB)
* D-Link WebCruiser DRM-560EL 56k (Serial)
* Hayes Accura # 03328-C (Serial)
* Hayes 5670 56k (Serial)
* US Robotics 56k (Serial)
* Zoom 2985 (USB)
  _________________________________________

**  Internal Modems:**
 * 3Com 3CP3298-DEL "Voice Interface Card" (PCI)
 * Actiontec V.92 call waiting internal modem (PCI)
 * Agere Systems WinModem built on the DSP 164x (Mars) series with [Martian]("http://www.barrelsoutofbond.org/downloads/martian/") Drivers (PCI)
[/COLOR]
(Also please do not waste your time nor mine telling me to just get a broadband connection.)
