Prioritize and take one problem at a time.  Use search engines, "A110" not complete model, and "Linux" as well as "Ubuntu."

Toshibas are popular with Linux users and are no more problemmatic than other brands.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesLaptopsToshiba]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesLaptopsToshiba")
[http://tuxmobil.org/toshiba.html]("http://tuxmobil.org/toshiba.html")

It is vital that you specify which WIFI adapter you have, if you expect any help.  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported")

Finally, you will have to learn to use search engines and do some of your own detective work to get everything on your laptop to work under Ubuntu or any other Linux.  If that doesn't suit, you would really be better off with Windows.
