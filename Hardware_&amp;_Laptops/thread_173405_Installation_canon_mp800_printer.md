### Post by OzzyFrank on 2008-03-15
Well, my MP800 worked out of the box, but it only goes to 600dpi max. The scanner works fine using **[COLOR="Indigo"]Xsane[/COLOR]** (install it; it will be in your Graphics menu). As for the printing side of things, with higher resolutions that 600dpi, and getting the **CD Tray** happening, I really do suggest **Turboprint** (it doesn't suck that you have to pay a little for it; it does suck that Canon makes this the only option!).

As for the [COLOR="DarkRed"]**CD Tray Printing**[/COLOR], see the guide I made:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=657865](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=657865)

The solutions are at the bottom, along with some screenshots.

Now, my question is how to get **Vuescan** working, as it sees the MP800, but tells me Ubuntu doesn't support it, hehe... morons. At least **Xsane** has absolutely no problems. For those interested in following that thread:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4520000#post4520000](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4520000#post4520000)

And for those wanting to abandon Canon in the future (like, I suppose, they've abandoned Linux users - read more: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4520036](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4520036)), I would really do my homework first, as from what I've seen in threads, HP and the like are not much better! I would suspect that the drivers out there that work and support all the features have been written by those in the Linux community.

And for those tempted to blame it on Ubuntu... well, blame the manufacturers for lack of support for Linux in general. At least Ubuntu has such a large community that there are those who work on these things.

Though you can still curse Canon... preferably in email... and stating you and all your friends have moved to Ubuntu and no longer use Windows or Mac (you can exaggerate if it helps change their minds, hehe!). Cheers
