### Post by brundlelinux on 2007-10-14
So, here's the scoop....

My girlfriend buys a new Dell 64 bit system and a Dell AIO 926 printer.  Of course, she's still Win-doze dependant, so she gets it shipped with Vista.  Two weeks in, and she's had it up to her eyeballs with Vista.  Finally, after dealing with me using Linux in the same room as her for a year, she agrees to give Ubuntu a shot.  She LOVES it.  All I can say is that we'd have a new, total convert if it wasn't for one thing..... the printer.

As has been well established in the forums, the "Dell" printers that Dell is now selling under their name are actually rebranded Lexmark printers, dressed up for Halloween, in Dell costumes.  And also, as we all know, Lexmark, as a matter of company policy, has made the choice to not embrace Linux / open-source.  They keep their proprietary device drivers guarded very closely, under strict lock and key.  This, of course, prevents Dell from offering support for "Dell" printers to those of us using Linux.

I'm not going to ask the same old question.... "Hey, how do I get this non-supported device to work?"  I'm sure that some tech-head out there has found a way to provide some functionality for this model printer by taking some other driver and tweaking it and filtering it through 50,000 other programs and adding custom-written script and then altering that mass of code so that it's a "driver" that will provide grainy black-and-white printing capability.  I'm not interested in that.  It's too much work for too little pay-off.  The juice isn't worth the squeeze.  It's a color printer/copier/scanner.... and if it can't function at 100%, what's the point?

However, this is what I will ask.  I was tooling through the forums a few weeks ago and read something that now I am kicking myself for not bookmarking for later reference.

I saw something posted about an officially released program or script that basically interfaces Windows drivers in a shell for use in Linux applications.  Kinda like Wine, but applied to drivers.  Has anybody else heard of this at all?  

I know the potential is high that it's bogus.  I mean, something like that would be HUGE in the Linux community.  I can't imagine that if it's legit, I would have returned no results from 3 hours of forum and Google searching.  But.... posting a question is free and buying a new printer is not.  So... there it is.
