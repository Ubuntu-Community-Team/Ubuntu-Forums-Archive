### Post by wolrahnaes on 2005-02-14
My Compaq R3000T is quite well supported out of the box.  Ubuntu is so far the only distro that properly configured the 1680x1050 widescreen.

Sound doesn't work, but the problems I had earlier with the keyboard not working with SMP kernels have been fixed.  The ATI 9600 Mobility is well supported by the fglrx driver, and the Broadcom wireless LAN works with ndiswrapper.

The touchpad works, but appears to need drivers for the scroll area to be functional.  Right now it just acts like more of the touchpad.

The built-in card reader recognizes SD cards (they show up as /dev/hde), but I get I/O errors whenever I actually try to do anything with it.

As soon as I figure out how to make sound work (it worked in FC2, and ALSA claims to support the ATI IXP) then I will give this notebook a 95% perfect score for Linux compatibility.  The 5% off comes from the card reader not working and the need to use Windows drivers for the wireless.
