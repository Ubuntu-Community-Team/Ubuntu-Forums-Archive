### Post by Mike T on 2007-11-05
Linux will effectively use the resources that are available to it.  More RAM means less disk swapping and a faster system.

Two things that you can never have enough of are hard disk space and RAM.  That being said, I have have Gutsy running on three different machines ranging from a PIII 800 with 512MB of RAM and an Nvidia 5200 card to a more recent AMD Opteron 185 with 1GB and an ATI Radeon 9550.  The other computer is a HP Pavilion dv9205us laptop.  The desktops are dual boot with XP/Ubuntu and the laptop is dual boot with Vista/Ubuntu.

The two most annoying things about Linux are support for bleeding edge hardware (especially graphics cards and printers) and getting wi-fi to work.
