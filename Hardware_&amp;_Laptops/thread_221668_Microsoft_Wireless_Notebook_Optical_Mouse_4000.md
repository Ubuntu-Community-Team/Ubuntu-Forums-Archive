### Post by Shay Stephens on 2006-07-31
Ok, update time.  I got the mouse today and first thing was to plug it into dapper.  The mouse responded right away, and the core functions (left, middle, right, scroll) all work.  The thumb button acts like the left mouse button, and the side scrolling portion of the wheel does not work.

The mouse is nice, a tad small, but for travelling laptop use that is fine by me.  The speed at which the pointer moves across the screen is faster than normal (for me) but without changing it, I have gotten used to it already.  The scroll wheel spins smoothly, very smoothly, there are no detents.  It feels very good.

The USB dongle fits in the bottom of the mouse and turns the whole thing off if installed there.  The battery (a single AA) slides into the unit at a slight diagonal.  It is very easy to replace.

The light is dim, so it won't be putting anyones eyes out if you look at it like some other optical mice I have used ;-) and the build quality seem really nice.

I think I will buy another one for desktop use.

p.s. I booted into windows to see what the other features would be like.  Only after booting to the desktop, did the mouse get detected and installed.  The same core functions are supported, but it looks like without a driver, at least in XP, you won't be getting the thumb button or the side scrolling.

### Post by Shay Stephens on 2006-07-31
One last thing.  I just tested the line of sight distance I could get.  I have the dongle plugged into the front of desktop and the maximu range was 3-4 feet.  As you got to 4 feet you start to get skipping, and by 5 feet the mouse pointer will not move.

If you are gaming, you will probably want to stay within 3+ feet.  If you are just browsing the web and such, you could push it to 4 feet.  Also, make sure the surface you are mousing on does not absorb radio waves or your distance will be cut drastically.  If you have a metal topped desk, you might have trouble using the mouse.  If the mouse gets about 3" or more away from the metal, it will start working normally again.

I have a wood top desk.
