### Post by JumpmanXXIII on 2007-07-03
It's usable but three factors keep it from being desktop replacement

1. Ram - 200mb is very limiting when working with anymore than a few apps at a time

2. Cell - not fully optimized and most of the spe are disabled

3. RSX - can't access the 256mb ram on the video card and the it's processing power which means and beryl and any other 3d accelation apps are out of the question as for right now

### Post by lognok on 2007-07-13
I have installed 7.04 on my ps3 and it's only ok for surfing and watching moives when using VLC or Mplayer and only SD-stuff, HD-stuff will surely kill it. Totem movie player takes more processing power and thus makes playback difficult for SD-stuff.

Bear in mind that the cell processor is derived from the power processor and doesn't include the full instruction set.

This and the fact that it also has to render the graphics (due to no access to the RSX core) makes it naturally sluggish.

For now I only use it for Pidgin and surf the web because Firefox rules over the included Playstation3 browser.

On the good side: The resolution on my tv is very nice due to 1:1 pixel mapping and Blue-ray movies look amazing, although not Ubuntu related.
