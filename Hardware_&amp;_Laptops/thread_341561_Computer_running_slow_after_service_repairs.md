### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-01-18
Are the fans running?!?  Can you see what the system temps are doing?

I had Dell replace the cooling fans in my laptop once (tech came to my office) and the ______ ______ "forgot" to plug the fans in before rebuilding the thing!  (Gets even better in this case - I dismantled the laptop and found he also installed the _original_ bad fans!  He had them out - I saw them laying on the table beside my gutted laptop! ](*,) )

If the fans aren't running, the CPU is probably scaling waaay back to save itself which would also give the results you describe (100% CPU, etc).

I suppose it could also be something wrong with the heatsink/heatpipe attachment to the CPU, but why they would take that off for a screen replacement...
