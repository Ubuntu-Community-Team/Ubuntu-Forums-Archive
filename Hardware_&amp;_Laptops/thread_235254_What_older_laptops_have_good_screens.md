### Post by s0lar on 2006-08-12
I don't think a lot of OLD laptops have screens above 15". But to find a laptop with a good screen, search for business models instead of home user models. They are also smaller most of the times and more practical to take along with you. I just bought a Compaq evo n600C. That's 14,1" and although I wanted a 1400*1050 model I only found a 1024*768. Then I will have to get used to extra workspaces to divide my work. Some laptopscreens go dark on the edges and while this one is at least 5 years old, it is in very good condition. Just search for an older laptop and check it out before you buy and if possible take one with a high resolution.

### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-08-12
If you can get your hands on an 8000-series Inspiron, most (that I have seen) have 15" screens running 1600x1200. Later models (the 8100s and 8200s) have nice quality and are very bright. I use a modified 8000 at home and I love it. 

Most of those machines are about 4-6 years old and for that reason you can pick one up for less than $300. Parts are very cheap; the most expensive thing I ever got for mine was the video card, and that was less than $120 used.

You can also put in a second hard drive, a second optical drive, and there are dual layer DVD+/-RWs available.

The downside is most of them never got much faster than 1.2Ghz ... which might be acceptable for your purposes. (EDIT: I take that back; I see there are 8200s with 1.8Ghz processors in them. Cool!)

Cheers!
