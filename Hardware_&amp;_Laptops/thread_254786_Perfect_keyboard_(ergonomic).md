### Post by malacoda on 2006-10-09
The extra shortcut buttons (web/home,search, mail, volume, calculator, etc) don't work out of the box and I'm not fluent enough w/ linux to know if they can be made to work but - as someone feeling the effects of RSI - I love the MS Natural Ergonomic Keyboard 4000.  

All the standard keyboard keys including number pad, function keys, etc. work just fine in linux.  Takes up a little more space than a typical keyboard but for me exchanging a bit more desk top real estate was well worth the improved positioning of my wrists.

### Post by benow on 2006-11-23
Well, if you plan on typing for years and years to come, and have a bit of a budget, you could try a [datahand]("http://datahand.com/products/personal.htm") or [comfort]("http://www.comfortkeyboard.com/keyboards_ergomagic.html") split keyboards.  There are [chair mounts]("http://www.datahand.com/products/datachair.htm") which place each side of the keyboard at the end of the chair arm.  The combo of open shoulders, straight wrists and lack of reaching (and integrated mouse on the datahand) eliminates rsi and the possibility of it occuring in the future.  That said, they're not cheap and they take a bit of practice (the datahand especially), and lead to sadness when forced to work on a 'normal' keyboard :)
