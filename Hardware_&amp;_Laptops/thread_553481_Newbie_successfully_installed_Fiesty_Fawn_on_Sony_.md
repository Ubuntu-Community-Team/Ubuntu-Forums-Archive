### Post by fascistpig on 2007-09-17
I got sick and tired of Windows XP and Microsoft in general. Too poor to buy a Mac, so I decided to give Ubuntu a second chance. My last attempt was sometime last year at 5.xx and it was okay, but I wasn't ready to make the full move then. I got a new laptop this year and it came with Vista. Used it for a month and then dumped it. Reinstalled with XP Pro, but Sony doesn't support this model for XP, so I had to spend weeks downloading and testing various drivers to get it working as it should. 

Now XP runs great. I'm not super wealthy, so bitorrent became my new best friend, but the constant irritation of having to maintain virus and spyware software that didn't protect you fully had a part in my decision to change. Then Microsoft shutdown AutoPatcher and I began to worry about having a properly updated OS (I was using an unofficial version of XP Pro).  But the last straw came when I had to work on computers for a group of my friends in a  3 week span. I had to reinstall XP on about 7 machines and I realized it was taking me a good 6-8 hours per system to install everything they needed. This was work I was doing for friends so there weren't too many financial rewards, but it was physically exhausting. 

I downloaded the desktop Ubuntu and Kubuntu ISO's and tried both. What a difference a year makes. After testing out both with the LiveCD, I decided to stick with Ubuntu simply because I thought it was more intuitive to me than KDE. The decision was solely based on the UI. I simply like it better. 

EVERYTHING WORKED! Okay, sorry, not everything. The FN keys kinda work, but that's okay cause I don't use them except for the volume controls. The video, wireless, modem, audio all work perfectly. The best thing? I had a fully working machine in 20 minutes after power up with all the basic apps that I need 

I remember getting codecs was a hassle last time I tried Ubuntu, but this time it was a breeze. Again, what a difference a year makes. It was so easy, I smiled. Over the weekend I tested a few more things and now it is sitting pretty on my laptop.

One thing I did notice that was a bit surprising was how much cooler my laptop was running. Could it be that XP was overworking the processors unnecessarily?  I don't know, but the system does seem cooler. 

I am hooked. I built a desktop machine over the weekend so I can turn that into a file/print server. I will install Ubuntu on that machine as well and attempt to do some simple networking with it. I am very impressed by 7.04. The community has done a great job in such a short amount of time. Thank you. 
Here's the support page for my particular laptop: [http://esupport.sony.com/US/perl/model-documents.pl?mdl=VGNC240E](http://esupport.sony.com/US/perl/model-documents.pl?mdl=VGNC240E)

Finally, I realized how refreshingl it was to not have to worry about software licenses or remembering to update each and every little thing. I have converted to FOSS and Linux.
