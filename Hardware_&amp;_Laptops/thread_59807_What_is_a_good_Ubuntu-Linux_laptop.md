### Post by npaladin2000 on 2005-09-05
I've heard good things about Dell Latitudes working well too. Most people seem to have reasonably good luck with Dells, and HP/Compaq.  But if you can afford it, go for the best compatibility and grab and IBM/Lenovo ThinkPad. 

Whatever you get, make sure it uses the Intel ProWireless. I think Dell's is Broadcom...I KNOW HP's non-Centrino Intels use Broadcom, and it would follow that they use them on the AMD setups too. ....IBM's non-Centrino use an Aethros chipset that works somewhat well with madwifi, but the Intel drivers are a bit better. Broadcom drivers under Linux are fairly nonexistent and require using NDISWRAPPER...basically, using the Windows drivers and an adapter layer.  Not impossible, but not the most stable solution. 

I wonder if anyone sells AMD laptops with the Intel ProWireless...no reason they couldn't, since it's just a MiniPCI card.  Anyone try an Intel miniPCI with an AMD laptop? :)
