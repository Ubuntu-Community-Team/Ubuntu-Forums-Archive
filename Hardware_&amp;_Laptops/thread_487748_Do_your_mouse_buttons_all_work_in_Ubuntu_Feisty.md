### Post by kidders on 2007-06-30
Don't forget guys ... Linux isn't Windows! If you want something that works like Windows, then ... well ... you know the rest hehe. It's also important to remember *why* certain hardware preconfigures itself on non-free operating systems.

One of Linux's greatest strengths is its configurability. You have to be prepared to spend a little time tweaking it & getting to know it properly, in order to get the best out of it. Former Windows users who expect to be able to hit the ground running when they install Linux are misguided imo. Being able to make rudimentary tweaks to things like X is a basic skill.

My best advice would be to try to be patient. Different OSs each have their own quirks & ways of doing things.
