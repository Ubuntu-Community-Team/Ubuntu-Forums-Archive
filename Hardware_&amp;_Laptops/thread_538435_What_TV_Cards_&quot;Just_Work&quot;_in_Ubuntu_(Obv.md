### Post by OzzyFrank on 2007-08-30
Now, like a lot of people using Ubuntu (or any Linux), I've never been able to get my Leadtek Winfast TV Expert 2000XP to work (the system recognises it though). Using the bttv and modprobe tips etc, does nothing but waste time and increase frustration (so I just record my weekly FM radio show in Windoze for now). Anyway, knowing full well Leadtek don't have Linux drivers, I contacted them asking if they had any for that card, or for ANY of their cards, and the response was:

"We are sorry for this item no  provide  linux driver yet now. We suggest you contact linux forums to ask linux power user."

So basically the answer for "If there are no Linux drivers for this card, which current cards of yours do you recommend?", the answer is "None!".

So I am here asking you guys what brands of cards, and perhaps models in particular, do you recommend. The less fiddling, the better... ideally, no fiddling at all, and tvtime and mythtv etc just find it straight away and start using it (same for FM radio programs). Or perhaps cards with reliable and easy-to-install drivers? Or just some easy tweaking of a config file ot two that actually works for EVERYONE? You get my drift... anything but the nightmare of Leadtek in Linux, hehe. If there are some clear winners, I'll then be able to recommend cards to other people.

Oh, and I suppose the cards should be fairly recent, as older ones would be hard or impossible to find. Pity I can't just get Leadtek again, as I set up one of their new DVI cards on a Vista system for a client and it picked up all the digital TV channels for this part of Australia instantly (radio has yet to spit out audio though, hehe). Cheers
