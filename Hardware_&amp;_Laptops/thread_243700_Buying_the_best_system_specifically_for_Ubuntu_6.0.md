### Post by John.Michael.Kane on 2006-08-26
> **Magnes said:**
> It's not quite simple. For Athlon you should (must?) stick with DDR, for Intel DDR2 I suppose. SDRAM is not used any more.

AMD socket AM2 supports 240pin DDR2 
AMD socket 939 supports DDR 400 (PC 3200)

INTEl socketT (LGA 775) supports 240pin DDR2 **Note: not all their chisets on these boards are ddr2 capable.**

**Note: some intel LGA775 boards still support standard DDR**

### Post by Cynical on 2006-08-26
Sockets are the slots on the motherboard that you put the cpu's into. They are designed for a certain type of CPU. AMD's latest cpus use their AM2 socket and Intel's latest cpu's use the LGA775 socket. So basically you shouldnt be asking which sockets are better, but which processors are better. And if you are choosing either of those sockets (the latest) you will need DDR2 memory.
