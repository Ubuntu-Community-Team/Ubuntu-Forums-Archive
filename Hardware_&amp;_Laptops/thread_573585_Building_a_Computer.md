### Post by rsambuca on 2007-10-11
The video editing and encoding is cpu intensive, and doesn't really use the gpu that much.  Compiz will run quite fine on way less (I am using a 6600GT with beryl, one of Compiz's predecessors).  I guess it all depends on what you are going to do.  

If you wanted to cut back, why not go with a core 2 Duo, save money on the cpu, cut back to a 7series card, and go for a dual monitor setup.  Once you have used two monitors, you will never go back, especially if you are doing video editing.  Also, a huge speed gain can be had by using a small 10,000rpm Hard Drive for the main OS.
