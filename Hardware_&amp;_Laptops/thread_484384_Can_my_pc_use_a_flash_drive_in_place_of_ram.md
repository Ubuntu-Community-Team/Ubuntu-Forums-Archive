### Post by bigfox on 2007-07-20
I think you could user gnome partition editor to create a linux swap partition on the flash drive, however, I would not recommend this.

A)  I have not actually tried it myself.  Proceed at your own risk.

B)  Flash memory is often very slow.

C)  Flash memory has a limited number of read write cycles.  As sections of the flash drive get rewritten more then they can handle, the flash drive will pull them out of service and your flash drive would keep getting smaller until it died completely.  Virtual memory flogs the drive with read and write cycles and makes this happen much quicker then if you were just using the drive normally.

Ready boost isn't actually using the flash drive as RAM, I think it is just using it to store the most needed parts of the system for quick access without having to wait for the seek time of the hard drive.  I am not too sure about that as I have never used Vista.
