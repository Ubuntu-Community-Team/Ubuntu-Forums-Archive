### Post by chele on 2006-08-15
> **GeorgeAD said:**
> The USB cases I've seen have two leads coming out, apparently to take power from two USB ports to drive the disk.Nope. You only use 1 port. The 2nd port is for attaching other devices. 

USB devices can be daisy-chained, one plugged into the other. For example, you can plug the USB HD into your computer, a USB printer into the HD, a scanner into the printer, and your mouse into the scanner.

Just don't get carried away, I doubt the USB bus can handle thousands of devices at a time.

### Post by maubp on 2006-08-15
Actually some USB caddies really do use two USB inputs, one for data one for power.  If yours does, and your computer only has one USB port left, then a powered USB hub might be worthwhile.

You'll find 3.5 inch drive USB enclosures usually have their own power block instead.

### Post by GeorgeAD on 2006-09-14
My laptop has a ps/2 socket for plugging in a mouse or keyboard, as well as the single USB port. Using the USB to ps/2 adaptor that came with a USB mouse, I plugged the power connector of the external drive into the ps/2 port, which seems to solve the problem. So the USB port carries data, ps/2 supplies power and there's no need for the external power supply, which is very handy.
