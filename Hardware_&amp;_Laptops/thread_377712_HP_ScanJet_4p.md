### Post by ofb on 2007-06-10
This is a late answer, but may help someone else doing a search.

My ScanJet 4p works on 6.10 using the included XSane Image Scanner. 

A test scan of a colour image comes out far too dark at default settings, but everything is adjustable.

Same default scan under Windows comes out a little on the light side, but near enough to be usable. Neither result is particularly colour correct, and detail is sub-par compared to generic cheap scanners available today.

Of note, a scan under Ubuntu takes much longer than under Windows and sounds like a shy fire alarm. Not the photocopier-style buzz-swish the HP makes under Windows. You probably don't want to run a test while people are sleeping.

Only other thing I can think of is this Ubuntu was installed with the HP SCSI already in the computer. Adding it afterwards may or may not require a few more steps.
