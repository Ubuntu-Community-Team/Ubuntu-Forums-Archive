### Post by onemanwaking on 2007-08-22
I've bought a number of Toshiba Satellites for my business and almost all of them have had Ubuntu run perfectly out of the box. The only issues I've had on some were adjusting screen resolution and configuring sound to work with Intel High Def Audio.

The best advice I can give is to bring a Feisty Live CD with you to BestBuy etc. and start trying it out on the laptops they have on display. They shouldn't mind if it means a sale. If possible, try to get one with wireless working right out of the box. In my experience, all other issues are fairly painless to resolve, but an unsupported wireless chipset (some Atheros units, for example) will not be easy, or even possible to get working. Stick with big name brands because you will find the most user support online for those brands.

And whatever you do, make sure you buy the laptop from a retailer with a good return policy. FutureShop around here (Ontario) is great for returns - no questions asked.

Good luck!:)
