### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-20
I would guess that there are two laser diodes on the read head.  One optimized for reading and writing CD's and one for reading and writing DVD's.  DVD's have much tighter writing tolerances, so a weak diode would immediately cause problems with the DVD and not the CD.  If it was a driver/interface problem then neither would work.  Interface failures are more common with hard drives since they pump out data at higher rates.

If the drive is under warranty, Sony is pretty good about replacing it.  If it is out of warranty then Sony engineers did a good job designing it to fail on time.

I assume you are pissed because the drive has seen light use.  If you had been using it 24 hours a day every day burning disks to sell at a weekend swap meet then you probably wouldn't post here.

Sony (from my 1983 Trinitron that still works) is not the same as yesteryear.  My 1996 Trinitron required two repairs in 10 years--one was a surface mount $70 jungle chip that I repaired myself.  That was a pain.

Read the reviews on the web and find out what folks are having good luck with.  I had a defective HP Lightscribe replaced recently, don't think I can recommend that.

The good news--you can still boot Live CD's with it!

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-20
Perhaps they were built at the same time as mine.  It's an epidemic problem and I'm not sure why people aren't raising a protest about this.

About all we can do is share our experience.  After some research, the Sony Drive appears to be a rebranded Lite-On drive--which is generally good--but others have experienced problems with the DVD crapping out with light use.

It could be a bad batch of laser diodes affecting several brands.  I can understand if I was using it daily to burn disks, but failures with light use is unacceptable.

Donate the drives to your local linux club and let them hack on them--at least they can do some testing and try some firmware upgrades to try to revive them.  If nothing else, the elastic drive bands can be used to revive other drives and the metal casing can be reused to make new cars.

Do your research and then post what you have decided on.  Afterall you are not the only one with a bum drive.
