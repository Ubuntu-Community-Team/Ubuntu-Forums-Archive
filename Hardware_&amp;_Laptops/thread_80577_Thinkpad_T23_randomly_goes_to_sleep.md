I installed Ubuntu 5.10 recently on my Thinkpad T23 laptop and have (so far) only been having one problem.  Randomly when I am working the laptop will go into a hard drive sleep mode (I'm not sure how to describe it, but the only way to awake the computer is to then press the power button.  Further more the screen dims, so I am guessing it is OS initiated).  On recommendation of another website, I added the text 'acpi=off apm=on' to the kernel boot entry of the menu.lst file.  Now the computer still randomly goes to sleep, though instead of the power down sleep, it just turns off the monitor and locks the screen.  On neither of these modes does the sleep light light up.

The really annoying part of this is that the sound does not work after awaking from one of these random sleeps.  The sound does work, however, after awakening from an inititated sleep (typically me closing the screen).  Any ideas?
