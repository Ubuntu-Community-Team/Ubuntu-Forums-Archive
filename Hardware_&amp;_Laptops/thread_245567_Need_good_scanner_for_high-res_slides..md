### Post by robc02 on 2007-07-11
I have been looking into this as well. The following sites are useful:

[http://www.scantips.com/](http://www.scantips.com/)

[http://www.hamrick.com/](http://www.hamrick.com/)

The scantips site gives a lot of info about scanning in general. In particular it advises buying a dedicated film scanner if you want decent results.

The hamrick site is all about some software called "vuescan". It runs on Linux, MAC and Windows and a wide range of scanners are supported. Unfortunately you have to pay for it, but this might be preferable to having to maintain a Windows set up just to use your scanner. There are quite a few reviews recommending this software over that supplied by the scanner manufacturers (regardless of operating system) - especially for photographic purposes.

I see your original post is quite old, so you might well have bought a scanner by now. If so, what did you get and how well does it work?
