### Post by Afishionado on 2007-09-12
If you just want to have the desktop mirrored on both displays, you can just plug the monitor in and go (you might have to reboot).

If you want to have Xinerama (desktop stretched across two monitors) I don't know of a way to do it without messing with the xorg config file. There's a tutorial here:

[http://www.paralipsis.org/2006/01/enabling-xinerama-in-ubuntu/](http://www.paralipsis.org/2006/01/enabling-xinerama-in-ubuntu/)

(If anyone knows an easier way, please share!)
