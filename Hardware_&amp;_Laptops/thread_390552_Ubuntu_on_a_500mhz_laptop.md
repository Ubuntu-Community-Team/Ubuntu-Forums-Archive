### Post by JoxBG on 2007-03-22
Do a 'light' version of UBuntu, you'll be surprised at what it can do. I have it on a Thinkpad a22m, PIII, 256M with a server install, followed by fluxbox and IceWM as window managers (switches back and forth), wdm as login manager (though even GDM performs well), Rox-filer as file manager and sometimes desktop (the single click folder navigation will blow you away). I used mainly light (fast) versions of applications but even TBird and FF runs very well. Most recently installed sopcast and now the old laptop is a movie server and recorder. Not to mention kernel rebuilder. And netbeans 5.5 runs even better on this machine than my dual-cpu workhorse workstation (OK don't get carried away, my workstation has a gazillion things running at the same time ;) ).

So get that old lappy going.

Jox

### Post by esaym on 2007-03-22
Use xubuntu and try and get as much ram as possible.  I don't know if you are buying this laptop or what but I just bought a dell latitude c610 1ghz 768mb for $315 total shipped.  Best deal I have had in awhile.  I am really impressed at with the shape that it was in.  The only show thing about it is the slow hard drive.
