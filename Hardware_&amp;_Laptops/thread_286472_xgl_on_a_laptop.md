### Post by thrillhouse on 2006-10-27
I just upgraded to Edgy this morning on my Thinkpad T41 and everything seemed to be working well from the get go, so I decided I would take a shot at putting Beryl/XGl on here because it looks cool as hell.  So after getting it installed with surprising ease and feeling pretty good about myself, I notice that my lap is getting extraordinarily hot.  I look up to notice that my CPU frequency (from CPU Frequency Scaling Monitor) is at 1.5 GHz (full bore).  Under ordinary Gnome, it never had a problem shifting up to 1.5 when I needed it and simmering back down to 600 MHz when nothing was going on.  I assume Beryl is responsible for this.  Is there a way to get the CPU to not stay at top end like this while running Beryl?

---

### Post by thrillhouse on 2006-11-08
Figured it out.  Its because I had BOINC running.  It wouldnt run at max when I wasnt running xgl, but when I am using it, it kicks in to high gear.  :)
