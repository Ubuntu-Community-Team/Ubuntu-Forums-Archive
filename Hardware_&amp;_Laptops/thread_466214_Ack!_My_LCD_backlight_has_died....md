### Post by Bluecircle on 2007-06-10
This is caused by 1 of two problems (or sometimes both!):

- A bad backlight (the actual fluorescent tube in the bottom of the LCD)
- A bad inverter (the small circuit board inside of the laptop screen area which converts the DC power for powering the backlight)

If you have a bad backlight, there will never be any light on your screen. If it's a bad inverter, the screen may light up for a few seconds or flicker.

You can buy replacement inverters and backlights on ebay, or search Google. All you need is the actual fluorescent tube, or the inverter circuit board. You do not need an entire new LCD assembly.

We repair these at my work all the time. I'll check and see where we get ours from. It's a lot cheaper to just buy the replacement part for $30-70 and install it yourself rather than buying a whole new laptop or paying Dell a few hundred to fix it. We charge $300-500 so it's definitely cheap to DIY.
