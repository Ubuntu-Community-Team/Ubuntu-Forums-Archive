Well I'm writing this on an XO laptop and other than the tiny little keyboard it's actually a cool little machine.  You can get to the development console and a shell prompt and underneath it's very much a standard minimal linux setup (based off of Fedora).  It's an interesting little machine.

Now my question is what will the Ubuntu community do for it?  I can't imagine someone not coming up with an Ubuntu image to install on it.  It seem to be very much inline with Ubuntu's philosophy.

The security scheme, Bitfrost, would make it a bit more difficult to change the images of those deployed to children around the world but the donor machines are suppose to be unlocked.

Thoughts?
