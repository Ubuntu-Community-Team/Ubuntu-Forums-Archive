### Post by keith11 on 2007-10-23
TorchlightJay,

I have an X61t, which is the tablet version of x61. I have installed Kubuntu 7.10 and I am dual booting my system. If you need the tablet functionality in Ubuntu, you will be disappointed, unless you are satisfied only with a program like Xournal or Cellwriter. You will not have the versatile text input functionality in Ubuntu, which it works very well in Vista.

On a side note, I think you should wait for a little longer if you intend to buy an x61t because currently there is a [manufacturing defect yet to be fixed]("http://forum.tabletpcreview.com/showthread.php?t=8282"). The bezel of the tablet comes off sooner or later for almost everyone and finally, after a few months, Lenovo recognized it was a problem and found a solution to it. They will still take some time as they said they are waiting for the new bezels. You can read all about it [here]("http://www.lenovoblogs.com/insidethebox/?p=93"). Keep an eye on that thread and you will also read an announcement when that problem has been solved. 

Other than the bezel issue, there is a whole new story about terrible customer service. It's extremely frustrating how for every customer service call it takes almost 2 hours of wait, and no, this is not an exaggeration! If you have a little more money to spend, I think you should go for Fujitsu t4220. Hope this helps.

Keith.
