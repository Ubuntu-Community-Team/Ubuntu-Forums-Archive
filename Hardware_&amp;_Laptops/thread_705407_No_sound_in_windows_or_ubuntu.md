### Post by a-real-ninja on 2008-02-24
No its no the hardware i have this same problem with my laptop both my VAIO and my toshiba. if you can acces the internet then you need to log into windows and reinstall all your default driver from the toshiba website. do not restart when prompted to, wait until you install the last driver. then once you do that you need to boot into Ubuntu and then turn your sound all the way down. double click on the sound icon and then file, change device, and it should show 2 deviced. something like HDA Intel or something to that effect and then the realtek driver. select the realtek driver, set all your sound settings the way you want em and then reboot back into Ubuntu and then it should be fine. If the problem persists then yes it is your hardware but for me it was a software issue and that is what it took for me to fix it.
