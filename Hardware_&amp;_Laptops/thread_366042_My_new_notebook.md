### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-20
My firm bought me a $3500 laptop (T20) in 2000 and it is worth about $200 today.  A used Thinkpad is better constructed than a new HP or Dell.

Because of tax laws, businesses are encouraged to toss old equipment and buy new equipment.  There are a lot of used Thinkpads floating around, just waiting for Ubuntu to be installed.  It's difficult to buy a used machine without seeing it--you don't want one with loose hinges for instance.  Find someone "down under" to search one out for you.

HP, Sony, Dell, not sure about Acer, have pushed for cheaper and cheaper components to the point that their laptops are disposable.

Check out system76.com.  They are doing it right.

### Post by anaconda on 2007-02-21
Remember that the display card is really really important factor in computers speed nowdays. Bad programmers, who make bloated software can and do make even very small programs, which just wont run without hardware acceleration.

here is some info of display-cards.
[http://forum.notebookreview.com/showthread.php?t=39568](http://forum.notebookreview.com/showthread.php?t=39568)
the nvidia 6150 and Intel GMA950  are in the same class, but the nvidia one is a lot better. I tried farcry-game (3d-shooting with amazing graphics) with both and it worked with 6150 and not with 950.. 

I would favour Acer laptops.. because they are as cheap than hp:s, but better with ubuntu. I had a bit problems installing ubuntu to my HP and getting everything to work (mic still wont work), but with the 2 Acers I didn't have any problems (which is a big plus).. even their ATI-display cards worked out-of-the-box with the correct resolution!

And I wouldnt buy a laptop with 1024X768 display. it is too small. and 512MB memory is the minimum, 1GB is better.

And I absolutely dont agree with "everything else than IBM breaks in 1 year".. nonsense. I havent broken any of my laptops (the siemens is from 1998 ).. (have broken CD and floppy drives and batteries, but that is to be expected..) And there is the 1-2 year warranty..

the lenovo T60 has only 512MB RAM, bad display card and only 1024x768 display.. not good. and slover processor than the HP AND is more expensive... Do the math. And every laptop will brake or get damaged if you drop it from 1,5m

and there are some cheap notebooks with a little better display card like Nvidia GeForce Go7300.. it does make a difference
