I have an M-Audio Audiophile USB that I use in both Windows and Linux. The alsa drivers in Linux are a bit hit-and-miss (e.g. at the moment, the digital output doesn't work) but it seems to work for most other things (including digital input). Changing the mode (e.g. enabling/disabling digital input, changing bit resolution, etc) requires turning on and off the device though.

I didn't have any real problems with the M-Audio Transit with Linux, however I suspect you will want more inputs/outputs than it provides. I haven't tried any other M-Audio devices in Linux though.

Terratec is another brand you might want to check out, but once again check the compatibility with alsa drivers.
