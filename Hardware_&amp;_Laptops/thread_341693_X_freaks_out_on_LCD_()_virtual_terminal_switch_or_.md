### Post by Tonren on 2007-01-19
Hi guys.  Sometimes, on shutdown, or when I'm switching X virtual terminals with Ctrl + Alt, or switching users/sessions, my display screws up.  After my desktop manager releases control to X, but before X constitutes the next session, the screen starts sliding a series of white and black gradiated stripes up and down, and the bottom 1/5th of the screen becomes a bar of black-to-white gradient blob.  Switching to another virtual terminal with Ctrl + Alt and switching back fixes this problem.

From research done in the #ubuntu chat, I think it only happens on LCD monitors.  It might be an ATI problem, but I don't think that it's specifically a DRIVER problem, because I use "ati" and someone who uses "radeon" gets the same problem, though less often.  I'm pretty sure it happens with "fglrx" anyway.
