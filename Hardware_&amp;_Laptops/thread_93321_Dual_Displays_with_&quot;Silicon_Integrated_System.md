### Post by poofyhairguy on 2005-11-21
[QUOTE=kennethb]
My result is that both monitors work, but they show the same thing. What I want to do is to be able to drag an open window (ie, Evolution) over to my laptop's screen/display, and then open new windows/apps on my external monitor/display.[/QUOTE]

I hate to be the one to tell you this, but you really can't have what you want.

The xserver will only allow you to span (the feature is called Xinerama) a single desktop if both monitors have the EXACT same resolution.
 If thats what you want and I miss read you, follow the instructions for the non-Nvidia cards here:

[http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Dual_Monitors](http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Dual_Monitors)

 Unlike an Apple Powerbook where you can plug it (with a 1078x864 resolution) into a 34 inch monitor (using God knows what resolution) and it makes them one screen, the Linux xserver is not developed to the point where it can really do that well.

The xserver is the one place where the Linux desktop really lags behind. Even the optomistic don't think that what you want will be possible for at least a year for most cards. Be happy you have cloning (thats what the copying of the monitor is called) as many cards can't even do that.

Its important to know the limitations of the software you are using. Here is one of those limitations. I hope this is not a "killer feature" for you because I have looked at what it will take to get such a situation to work and its a BIG problem.

### Post by poofyhairguy on 2005-11-21
[QUOTE=kennethb]poofyhairguy - Thanks for the clarification and explanation. Since I am able to clone my desktop across multiple monitors, is there a way that I can use the "desktop switcher" in Linux to put Desktop1 on one display/monitor and Desktop2 on the other?[/QUOTE]

Not really. The desktop switcher is a visual trick of Gnome's window manager. Its not real desktops in the sense that the xserver would be able to tell them apart.

What you CAN do (or I did once with my Nvidia card but I bet there is ways to get other cards to work) is make it so each display runs its own xserver. When you do this each can be their own resolution, but you can not drag applications or anything between the two screens.  Its almost if you have two totally different computers.
