### Post by blackened on 2007-04-30
The beep was an error warning generated by the BIOS when it performed its POST (power-on self-test). The significance of the beep pattern will vary depending on the type of BIOS you have (generally Phoenix, Award, or AMI). Check the site of your BIOS manufacturer to decipher the meaning of the beep patterns.

I have a hunch something on your motherboard is failing. The error beeps are usually generated when the BIOS encounters a problem prior to initializing video, which is why it doesn't just print the error message to the screen.

To be thorough you should open your machine and make sure the video card and RAM are fully seated in their slots. Also go through and check all connections to the motherboard and peripheral hardware.
