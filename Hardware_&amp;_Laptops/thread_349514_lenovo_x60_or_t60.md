### Post by mustang on 2007-01-30
I was actually in the same position as you this past December. The X60 has phenomenal battery life (upwards of 9 hours on 12 cell!) but was simply too expensive for me (upwards of $1500?) Regarding the lack of optical drive, I do not think it would be too difficult to install from another source.

The t60 appears to be overall best laptop by Lenovo. The whole thinkpad line has excellent linux compatibility (maybe perhaps wireless for which ndiswrapper is always available). I didn't opt for it because it was out of my price range ($1100+).

I actually opted for the macbook (first generation) which I got ~$740. I'm not sure what you mean by "recurrent construction problems". Apple has always been king of build quality and form factor. The only complaint I have is that linux compatibility isn't quite there yet (but Feisty looks promising). This has forced me over to using OSX for most of the time which, believe it or not, is not too shabby. I still prefer Ubuntu over it but OSX's ease of use is quite refreshing after some bad days of dependency hell and xorg crashes in Ubuntu world :)
