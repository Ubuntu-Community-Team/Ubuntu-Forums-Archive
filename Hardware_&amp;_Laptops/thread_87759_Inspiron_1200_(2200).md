### Post by jml on 2005-11-08
I've not used either of these laptops with Linux, but I've read on more than one forum that there seems to be a lot of trouble getting the 1200 and 2200 series properly configured.  Multiple problems have been mentioned from video config issues, to sound problems and even difficulty getting the on board network card working.  It seems that to keep the price down, a lot of non-standard components are used.

If you do not need  PC Card support, you might want to check out the Balance Laptop sold on the Wal-Mart web site.  Rather modest specs, but more than adequate for office productivity apps., e-mail, web surfing and modest gaming needs.  There is no accelerated graphics here, so no Tux Pacer, or Tux Cart.  For under $500  US they ship it with Linspire and 60 days of click and run support.  I'm not a fan of Linspire, but this is a laptop that does support Linux.  If you are not in a hurry, I've heard rumors that on the day after Thanksgiving, Wal-mart is going to sell a laptop for $399  US.  At that price, it most likely will have Linux on it and may be a good "starter" unit.  Hope this helps.

Joe
