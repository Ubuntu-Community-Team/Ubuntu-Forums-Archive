### Post by Eric064 on 2007-02-11
I installed Ubuntu last night and left it idling on the desktop for a few hours and came back to discover my LCD is damaged.

I had been showing off Ubuntu from the live CD to my dad and went ahead and showed him how easy it was to install.  I left with nothing running, just displaying an empty desktop.  Afterwards, I back about 5 hours later and took it out of standby to find a lot of horizontal lines across my display and four vertical lines.

The pictures I took show my screens pretty well.  19" Benq LCD and a 17" Samsung CRT.
[http://www.cse.ohio-state.edu/~goodmaer/benq.html](http://www.cse.ohio-state.edu/~goodmaer/benq.html)
The desktop in both pictures is the same - I used the same dark gray to fill in paint - and the black block is to show that the distortions aren't perceptible on an all black screen.  Other solid colors also hide the lines fairly well and grays and dark colors emphasize them.

My LCD is a  
[Benq FP39GX]("http://www.benq.us/Products/LCD/index.cfm?product=594")

I have used Ubuntu before on this LCD but with different hardware.

I was using
MSI 256MB Geforce 6600gt AGP
AMD Athlon 64 3200+ Venice
Gigabyte GA-K8U-939
2 Geil, 512MB DDR400 2.5-6-3-3

I had to reinstall XP after buying new hardware and went ahead and formatted both my hard drive a few weeks ago.  I just reinstalled Ubuntu last night.

I am using
EVGA 512MB Geforce 7950gt PCI-e16
AMD Athlon 64 X2 4200+ Windsor cpu
EPoX EP-MF570SLI Socket AM2 motherboard
2 OCZ, 1GB DDR2 667 4-4-4-12

All settings in Ubuntu were the default after a fresh install.
The only thing different was that I had to use the noapic option with this motherboard and I didn't need to do that before.
