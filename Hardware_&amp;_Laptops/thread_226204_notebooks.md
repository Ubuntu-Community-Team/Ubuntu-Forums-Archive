### Post by eXcentra on 2006-07-31
I have a Samsung Sens P28. 
The only con that I can think of would be that its body is plastic but it's not that bad. 
The screen is also the normal soft kind. It really doesn't matter to me but I guess the glossy one would have better protection. 

Pros would be the keyboard. It's very firm and solid, and the keys (function keys, special keys, etc) are well placed. The keyboard is also more compact in size. 

I'm quite satifised with it.

### Post by didobuntu on 2006-08-01
I have an Asus W2JC, with Core Duo processor T2500, and 17" WSXGA screen (with 1680X1050). The video card is a Mobility Radeon X1600 256MB dedicated memory.

What runs well? almost all : 3D rendering with the fglrx driver adapted (very well by the Ubuntu Team from the repos.), the two processors run like a charm with the 2.6.15-26-686 kernel, the wifi ethernet connexion is now perfect and I work out of the box nicely , wired ethernet runs also very well since the realtek driver r1000 is now in the Ubuntu repos. And the built-in bluetooth mouse device is perfectly recognized (although it is an Asus signed, it is in fact a Logitech bluetooth V270 wireless mouse).

What does not run at all ](*,)  (for the moment) : is the sound ... it is an hda-intel chipset with an ALC882 Realtek chipset that I did not have success to make it run ... Anyway, following the few topic I read, the Alsa Project team are on it and I don't worry about their excellent job .. 

Another thing, there a second sound card detected by alsa: an saa7134 device which is probably related to the built-in TV Tuner that I did not test yet. Maybe there is a conflict between hda-intel and saa7134.
