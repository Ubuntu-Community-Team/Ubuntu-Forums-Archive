### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-02-26
I am using the HP bluetooth receiver (BT308) on my iPaq (runs winDOZE 2002se) It COMES with the map software when you buy the package.  Maps are fairly accurate, but you get NO FREE updates .. they want to charge you.  Voice on the system sux.  

Have not looked into the Linux stuff yet as have been unable to flash the iPaq and load Familiar Linux (the current available PDA program.) 

BUT, there are several out there that use the USGS and other governmental maps of US for navigation and they keep their data base up to date so you can keep YOURS up to date.  Would be fine if you are using a laptop.

Found it very nice to put my selected areas on an SD card and use that.  Then it is only USING the map when you want it to!

### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-02-26
gpsdrive is probably the best out there right now.  Windows has a much larger selection.  With the price of car navigation units dropping, find one that has web-based map updates.  The maps really become the important item and you can't beat the form factor of these small units.

If you just want to fool around in the back yard, buy a USB gps for $60 to $80--get one with a SirFStar III chipset, load gpsdrive and have fun.

I've used these and they work well:
