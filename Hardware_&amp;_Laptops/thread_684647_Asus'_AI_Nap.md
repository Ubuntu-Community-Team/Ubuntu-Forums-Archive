### Post by shimojimatto on 2008-06-17
This reply is a little late... sorry.

I'm no expert. But from what I can tell in Windows, AI Nap is pretty much entirely pointless. Theres nothing that really needs to be supported in Ubuntu. It seems to me that you can use Window's or Ubuntu's normal power saving features to accomplish the same thing I would expect. 

AI Nap never did anything for me in Windows except turn off my monitor... I uninstalled it because of its uselessness. If you have your CPU set to throttle when not under full load, and you have your disks set to turn themselves off after a certain period of time I think you're doing as much as you can. Use  BIOS settings to run the fans quietly so they don't keep you awake at night and thats really all you can do. 

Does anyone have a different experience? Do you know what AI Nap is actually powering down or setting to a power saving mode?
