Honestly, for that price my first reaction is to say "get a minimac" just because they are slick.

But in reality you probably want an x86 machine. For that, you just need to make sure your computer has compatible parts- it doesn't matter who you git it from.

For example:

1. Make sure you get a Nvidia card. I'm so mad about ATI's Linux support! 

2. Get one of the cheaper AMD64's if you want to try out Ubuntu's release for that platform. It would be as fast as can be, but that means you can't get a Dell. They only drink the intel kool-aid. I've found AMD64 computers in your price range on HP's site!

3. I personally can say that my Sony PC works as well as I could hope with Ubuntu. Its has served me well- but be warned!- it has many custom parts so its about as easy to upgrade as a laptop. (I noticed that the other day when I tried to replace the motherboard- it wouldn't work!). If you can live without AMD64 (I couldn't if I got a new machine), Dell has HUGE deals fo your price range. They will throw in a monitor, a printer, a scanner, a continental breakfast, and a pack of smokes.  Finally- for the "best one from best buy" pc- the winner is Emachines. They are fast, have AMD64 chips, have non-custom hardware, and work great with Linux (well mine do).

4. Do more research than asking the board. Most importantly, findout EXACTLY what hardware is comming with you computer and get online and find out if it is all compatible first! Which company puts it in the box doesn't matter, what matters is what parts are in the box!

5. It needs to be said again. DO NOT GET AN ATI CARD. Some people are fooled into thinking that ATI is A-Ok because their crappiest cards have open drivers for them. Don't be fooled- any new x86 computer you get (besides a minimac :) needs to have an Nvidia card in it! Only Nvidia gives a damn about Linux!

6. Have fun. Getting a new computer should be fun!


Its more about the parts than the whole....
