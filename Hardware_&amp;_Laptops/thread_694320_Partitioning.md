Well, I'd like to point out that a fresh Ubuntu install is about 2.4GB.  You still need room to install additional software, etc.

Nobody here can tell you when your hard drive is going to die.  I've had the same internal hard drive for about 3.5 years now and it's still working fine.  I've also had an external hard drive for about 3 years and it's still chugging fine.

So, moral of the story is I don't think Ubuntu is what killed your hard drive.  It may have just been a bad hard drive, old, or you may have been unlucky.

I'd give it another shot.  Ubuntu is a great linux distro if you are diving into the linux world.  If you need help installing search the forum or make a new post.  There's a lot of information out there.
