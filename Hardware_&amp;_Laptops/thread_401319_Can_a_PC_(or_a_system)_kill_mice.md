### Post by lepascal on 2007-04-04
Hi!

This is my first post on this forum but I have been a frequent reader since I installed Ubuntu some weeks ago. I encountered a strange problem, I'm not sure if it is even possible that Ubuntu is the cause but I'll describe it anyway.

I had a Logitech V200 cordless mouse and used it for several months under Windows, never encountered any problems. Then I installed Ubuntu and it worked fine. After waking up the PC from hibernation or rebooting, the mouse didn't work anymore. I tried switching USB-Ports, pressing the reset button and always got it to work again. Yesterday those strategies didn't help anymore, so I supposed that the Hardware was faulty, went to the store where I bought it and changed it for a Logitech Mini Notebook cordless mouse.

I booted into ubuntu, plugged the receiver in at the login screen and it worked fine. While working in ubuntu I decided to plug in my iPod in the port where the mouse was, so I changed the receiver's port. From that moment on, the mouse just doesn't work. It is brand new, but the receiver isn't even recognized and shown in lsusb. I booted into Windows on this machine and on my sister's one and the mouse just doesn't move.

A wired mouse from Genius works perfectly, on all USB-ports, like my iPod does. So now I am asking for your opinion: Why is it my laptop's favourite occupation to kill Logitech's mouse receivers? 

P.S.: I have got a Dell Inspiron 6400, by the way. Did anyone experience similar things with a Dell laptop?

### Post by Rhubarb on 2007-04-04
The only thing I can think of is to grab the latest firmware for your Dell laptop, and upgrade the BIOS.
There's a chance this could be a hardware issue - and this might fix it up.

Software can not damage hardware (ok it is possible, but the software would almost have to be designed to do this, and it'd only work with some hardware - like CD burners for example).
