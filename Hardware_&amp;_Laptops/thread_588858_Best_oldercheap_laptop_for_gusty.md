### Post by fargle on 2007-10-24
You didn't specify what "cheap" means, but I'm typing this on a Dell Latitude X1, which is actually a Samsung something-or-other, and I can tell you that Gutsy runs insanely well on it.

It has a 1.1 GHz Mobile Pentium M processor, 1.25 GB of RAM, an 80 GB 1.8 inch hard drive, Bluetooth, 802.11B/G, a CF slot and an SDHC slot, VGA out, 2 USB ports, and Firewire, along with a 1280x768 screen and a very typable keyboard.  It's /small/ for the power.  Runs very snappy.

Everything in it works perfectly straight after install, including suspend-to-RAM.  I haven't tried suspend-to-disk on it yet.  Compiz runs great, including effects, transparency, the dreaded cube, you name it.

I get an estimated 1.5 hours of battery with the standard battery, but extended batteries for it aren't much bigger and I think it will easily get into the 3-4 hour range plus with one of those - plus they're small and it's easy to carry an extra.

I'm seeing them on Ebay in the 400-800 dollar range.

I love mine, I can tell you!  Definitely recommended for nice small system you can easily carry with you wherever you go.
