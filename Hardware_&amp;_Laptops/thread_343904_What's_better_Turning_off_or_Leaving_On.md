### Post by tribaal on 2007-01-22
Well from a purely ecological point of view, you should turn your hardware off whenever you aren't using it.
And your filesystem accesses your harddrive every once in a while, even when idle, to write journaling information to it. So even if it's absolutely not a lot of information it can possibly wear your hard drive (if you leave your computer running for years :) )

I suggest your turn your hardware off when you're not using it. And you'll save money on your power bill too.

### Post by mand0 on 2007-01-22
Some will say that turning your computer on and off often causes a lot of wear-and-tear to your hardware.  Computer parts like to stay in a constant temperature as opposed to a cycling between hot and cold.  I am not too sure now how big of a difference this really makes though.

I have some friends who keep theirs on all the time but I turn my off every night unless I have a specific reason to keep it on (downloading, uploading, etc).  I really haven't noticed a difference on performance.
