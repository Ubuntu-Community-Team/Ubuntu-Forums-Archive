### Post by NaOH on 2006-06-06
Recently I purchased one of those neat little japanese subnotebooks, the vaio pcg-u3.  Of course it was equipped with a japanese home version of windows xp having never been released in the US.  I had been debating on what OS to put on it, and for the sake of simplicity I decided to go with an english version of windows xp.  I spent hours trying to gain use of the multitude of different function keys for the LCD, volume, scroll wheel, hibernation, etc. squeezed onto the tiny keyboard, and couldn't get it operating as it did with the original japanese drivers.  My next OS choice was ubuntu, having such a good experience with it on my desktop.  Amazing, nearly everything worked as it did with the factory drivers with ubuntu (had to do the audio drivers myself, but most importantly, all the function keys were fully operable).  Hats off to a truly versatile OS and to those responsible for its development!  :D
