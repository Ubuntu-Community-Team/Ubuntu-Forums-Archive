### Post by mrbaz on 2005-07-10
I've got this 400MHz PII
It only has 64MB PC100 SDRAM in it.

---

### Post by az on 2005-07-10
It will make a *huge* performance improvement.

Your swap should be at zero, for maximum performance.

Even going from 64 to 128 will be tremendous.  256 would be excellent.

### Post by musicman2059 on 2005-07-10
I dunno about 128.  I have 192 in mine (minus 8 for onboard video... YES!!!) and usage after everything boots is about 80%, quite a bit of it being cache.  I say go for 256.

### Post by mrbaz on 2005-07-14
Installed 256MB today.  It was definitely a big difference.  Ubuntu access teh HDD a lot less now.  Even on a 400MHz machine, it's still a pleasurable experience.
The only thing that I found that shows its age is when scrolling a webpage.  It is not a smooth scroll, it takes it a while to refresh the screen, making scrolling a little cumbersome.
