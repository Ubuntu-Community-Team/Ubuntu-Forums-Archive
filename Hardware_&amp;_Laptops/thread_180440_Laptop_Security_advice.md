### Post by costoa on 2006-05-22
I have yet to see one of these "stolen laptop recovery" programs survive a hard drive formatting. They're basicly snake oil. 

Someone needs to build a stolen laptop recovery app that could either:
- Sit in the BIOS.
- Sit on the flash on some eth cards.
- Sit on a mini-pci card and also act as a modem

At least the app would survive a disk formating but mosy likely someone would create a hack for it within about two days of initial release. =)

If your laptop gets grabbed your chances of getting it back are slim and none (and Slim just caught the last bus out of town). Almost all laptops have a cable lock slot. While it can be bypassed it will stop most "chance" thefts.

IMO get the best rated cable lock and a stick-on motion sensor/alarm. If someone really wants your laptop nothing you do will stop them from using decent cable cutters and a well padded backpack to hide the alarm to get your machine. Luckly very few people are so equiped. Assuming you're not getting a 17" MacBook Pro you should be safe. 

Off topic but a very common place for laptops to get grabbed is US airport scanners. Basicly a two man team gets in front of you, the 1st guy goes though security without delay, the 2nd guy has change in every pocket and ties up the line while the 1st guy grabs your machine (which has already been sent out the back end of the scanner) and no one is the wiser since your average TSA worker is normally "not the sharpest tool in the shed". Be careful of it.

### Post by paritybit on 2006-05-23
To be honest, the best laptop security is making sure you don't put your laptop in a position where it can be stolen. Most things have workarounds and they have your laptop so they can spend all the time they want cracking it.
The best bet is to just make sure you always keep an eye on it, never leave it unattended.
There are also ways to encrypt the contents of partitions and such but still the best bet is just to make sure you laptop doesn't get stolen.
As for wifi, always good not to go around associating to unknown access points besides that I don't think there is too much of a securty risk here.
