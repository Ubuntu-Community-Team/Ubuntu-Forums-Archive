### Post by reh4c on 2007-10-03
Hello.  With a Core2Duo processor you can run the 64 bit version of Ubuntu, which should give you greater processing speed.  It's optimized for the 64 bit architecture.  I just built a T7300 Core2Duo based laptop using an MSI 1221 barebones and a T7300 processor from bestbuy.com.  With Ubuntu Gutsy Beta 64bit, most features are working, except for the webcam, fingerprint scanner, and brightness keys.

### Post by cubeist on 2007-10-08
If your budget is less than a thousand you may want to consider a laptop with AMD proc.  I know intel markets the heck out of there processors, and they do make a great product, but for the price I am a big fan of AMD...  For example, my latest laptop was an hp with 160gb hd, 1 gb ram (ram is really cheap to upgrade after the fact), 17inch screen, and a 1.7ghz dual core AMD 64bit.

For all applications that I run in linux there is more than enough power in this chip... in fact, AMD chips automatically change the frequency they run at on the fly and most of the time my computer just noodles along at 800 Mhz.... Anyway, I only paid 800 cdn for my machine (which is also 800 US!). Similar intel models were over a thousand...
However, I don't do much gaming...so that was not a factor when I was buying, but 3d apps like blender are nice and smooth.
