### Post by vtel57 on 2007-01-18
The hard drive could have experienced electronic-based failure. In which case it's loading down the IDE buss on your system. There's probably not much you're going to be able to do yourself to save whatever data you have on the drive. However, there are companies that will disassemble the drive and install the platter on their jigs to access the data and burn it to CD/DVDs for you. The cost for that service varies, but is around $300. If you have data that you feel is worth at least that amount on your drive, you can give it a shot. Otherwise... let it go. Learn the lesson, though... BACKUP! BACKUP! BACKUP!

Best of luck to you!
