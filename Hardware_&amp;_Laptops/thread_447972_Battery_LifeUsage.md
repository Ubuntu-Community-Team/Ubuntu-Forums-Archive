basically, what makes Lithium-Ion batteries lose their capacity is heat, frequent deep discharges, and mostly time.If you want it to last the longest it possibly can, discharge it to about 40% and put in in a cool, dry place.  However, the advantage you gain by doing that isn't significant enough to warrant taking the battery out if you're going to be using it on a regular or semi-regular basis.  Here's a do-don't chart from the same site: [http://www.batteryuniversity.com/partone-21.htm](http://www.batteryuniversity.com/partone-21.htm)

### Post by lavinog on 2007-05-18
I would think that having the battery in would help prevent issues caused by power failures. Unless you buy a UPS for your laptop. Being that a UPS costs about the same as the battery I would just stick to the battery.

also leaving the battery out for long periods could have some negative effects.
I have noticed my old cell phone doesn't hold a charge anymore even though it did when I replaced it a year ago.

I would be more concerned about your power adapter. The charger for my compaq laptop when it is running under a full load gets really hot. That has to degrade the components in it. While leaving your battery in can offset large changes in load.
