### Post by pablored on 2005-07-30
No problem, glad to help.  

If buying a card reader I would suggest going for a USB version, with USB in the kernel and mass storage support it 'should' just pop up as another drive.   It would be best to find one that is reported to work in linux, so no risk involved, but then again mine only cost 3 pounds.  WIth regards to the flash memory I would suggest looking at [www.ebuyer.com](www.ebuyer.com), where people post reviews of the products there.  There should be no linux issues, just to make sure it is good quality memory.  I only have experience with compact flash.  

The card reader isn't really required though, I just find it easier working with lots of different computers.  You could just see how it goes hooking up the camera for a while (with this approach you never have to take the memory out).  

Cameras and memory card readers seem very well supported to me.  I just wish the photo printing situation would improve, for now I create artwork in Linux and keep a windows machine for printing.  

Regards
Paul

### Post by SickTwist on 2005-07-31
André,
I have always had good luck with Hewlett-Packard printers. HP sponsors a free (as in freedom) printer driver project for Linux. [http://hpinkjet.sourceforge.net/](http://hpinkjet.sourceforge.net/) Also, you may want to check out [www.linuxprinting.org](www.linuxprinting.org) for more information about printers and Linux.

As for digital cameras, anything that operates as a standard USB filesystem device should not give you any problems.
