### Post by finite9 on 2007-10-24
I have an Acer 5021WLMi with WinXP and a HP 9297ea with Vista and I just got an IBM Thinkpad T61s with WinXP.

I am completely blown away by the power management software on the Thinkpad...It is light years ahead of anything other laptop manufacturers provide (non-existant on Acer and HP...they use Windoes in-built power schemes) and puts Linux to shame.  I wish we could get the same ability in Gnome and KDE!

There are a few different schemes that let you maximise battery time, performance or power efficiency, and they update the battery time left in real-time when you choose a new scheme.  It is a very elegant way of changing usage patterns and seems very accurate.  Simply having a battery icon with time left displayed looks really basic after having seen IBMs utility.

There is also a status display showing power output/usage of the battery and a battery stretcher utility that reduces all possible settings to minimum to give longer battery life.

It seems that these tools basically alter system settings to lower everything to minimum, but the utility does this in a very elegant and user-friendly way.
