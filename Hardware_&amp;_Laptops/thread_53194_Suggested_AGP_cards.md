### Post by pablored on 2005-07-30
I believe that a good card would be a matrox one, from the G series.  

For example the G450.  It has great support out of the box, and can be got cheaply off ebay for approx. 15 pounds.  

If you are looking for a cheaper alternative, I also set myself up with a G200 for a few pounds.  Although this is a pci card.  The matrox cards have great 2d performance, but I wouldn't expect too much from 3d gaming... although tuxracer runs fine.  

If you start looking at new cards, the G550 could well be more expensive than an nvidia.  If you are looking to get a new card, I would take the plunge with a cheap nvidia and install the binary drivers (or go with the nv option as has already been stated).  Even if you don't need the gaming performance, the opengl support could be good in the future (for example Enlightenment 0.17).
