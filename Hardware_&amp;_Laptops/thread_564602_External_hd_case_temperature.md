### Post by Soybean on 2007-10-01
It's not all that likely to be a problem -- a lot of overheating hard drives get that way because they're stuck in close quarters with other hot drives, and in the same box as some other, hotter heat sources (CPU, GPU). A single hard drive in a container by itself will likely be fine. I wouldn't worry about it at all unless your room temperature is unusually high, or you've got the drive working hard constantly, or it's a drive that's known to run hot.

And I wouldn't recommend just drilling holes... "more air" doesn't always equate to "better airflow." It might work, but without hddtemp, you can't test it to find out.

### Post by dinub1 on 2007-10-01
Modern external HDD need to have typically ventilation holes/slots. By example my WDC "Book edition" has slots on the sides. If I put the HDD enclosure horizontally, it gets quite hot to the touch. However if I place it like a book (vertically with the slots on bottom and at the top) air circulates efficiently and drive is much cooler. Solution: you need holes to disspate the heat. Alternatively you can use a metallic external HDD case which has good thermal conductiviy and this way it can disspate heat (even if it has no holes). Plastics are insulators therefore need holes for extra ventilation. Hope this helps.
