### Post by AwesomeSauce on 2007-05-17
Not too long ago when I was still using Windows, I can't remember having anything to do with a noisy PC. Then, when I switched to Ubuntu, the first day of trying it out, my PC seemed pretty loud. Ok, I opened up the case and dusted it the best I could. That seemed to help.

Then, a day or two later - all of the sudden I heard a fan start hitting something. Then, it switched from a slow whap-whap to a scary grinding noise. From what I could tell, this noise was coming from the fan in the power supply.

So I took multiple looks at it, and I saw a wire behind the fan that was most probably the obstruction. So, I used a small object to poke in there and move the wire aside. Well, I think it worked. I no longer get any sudden grinding noises.

But... now, my computer is just.. too loud. Really, I don't remember it being this loud back when I used Windows. Obviously there's no more grinding noise, but, the fans are just.. LOUD.

I'm not much of a techie, but would there be any reason as to why the fans would be noisier after switching from Windows to Linux? Or could it just be that my hardware is starting to go to crap after the past 4 years that I've had this machine?

Any replies would be appreciated.

**Edit:** I just removed a narly amount of dust from the heatsink and fans. I hope this will help. I'll let you know if the problem persists.
