### Post by comfurtn on 2007-02-05
these HP notebooks tend to run Ubuntu fine, although some models have trouble with acpi (power management)... most times there is an easy fix.

the hp pavilion notebooks come in two flavors:  AMD and INTEL hardware derivatives.  I myself have an intel-based laptop, so I don't have many of the issues that other users have with amd and nvidia hardware.

Do you know what the wireless chipset is?  Some brands require the use of ndiswrapper, which can be a trick to setup, but works great afterwards.

Other than that, I'd say its a good deal.  There's always help around the forums if you run into problems.

EDIT:  And about the 64-bit capibility, I can't speak from personal experience... but you can install the 64-bit version of Ubuntu to take advantage of it.  Search around the forums to see how other users have faired with 64-bit processing.
