### Post by Mike'sHardLinux on 2007-01-05
Nice CPU!!!  :biggrin: 

It's hard for someone else to decide for you. But, here's my .02:

Search the forums and look at the things that still don't work in 64bit. Are any of these softwares something you even care about or need to run? (I honestly don't know what isn't working at this time.)

Also, search for all the 64bit how to's and notice how often they require more work to get working. Notice how some things work only partially. Are any of these softwares something you need or care about? Is the effort all that big of a deal to you? Maybe, maybe not.

All my machines are 64bit hardware (well, my main desktop is EM64T...good enough to run 64bit OS). I run 64bit FC4 on my server, and that has worked like a champ for quite some time, and I had no trouble installing (often compiling from source) and running ANY software I wanted. On my desktop, I run 32bit Edgy, because I don't want to deal with the hassle of some more complex installs, or when some things install but only have limited functionality. Also, for me, 64bit performance isn't much different from 32bit. At least, for all the apps I use, I can't tell a difference. I am not suggesting the same course for you. Merely sharing my experience.

Do me a favor, though. If you do install 64bit and like it, **please** don't turn into one of those "64bit blows 32bit out of the water" snobs just based on your *perception* of how well it runs. If it's soooo much faster, show some real data.  (Convert some movies or mp3s, or fold some proteins.)
