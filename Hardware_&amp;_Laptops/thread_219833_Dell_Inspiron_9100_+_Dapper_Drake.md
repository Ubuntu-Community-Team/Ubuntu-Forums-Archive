### Post by Kamiyay on 2006-07-20

The 3d Acceleration didnt work on my ATI card, the wireless did work but wouldn't tell me signal strength (I used the ndiswrapper to get it working) and every time the power cable bumped a little bit it would suspend (huge turn off from the sexiness that is Ubuntu), but the biggest one came from the battery life (the all of 20 minutes I got. Now that Dapper has come out I was wondering if anyone else with a Dell Inspiron 9100 had instlled it and had any problems. I have been using FC5 at work on a vmware since then. And now I do believe I have a bit better grasp of the world known as linux. (yum kicks *** btw)

So to break it down, I plan on dual booting XP with Ubuntu because I am a gamer and dont want to hassle with Cedega or WINE. Also if I game on the Windows side I dont have to worry about the Direct3D on the Ubuntu side. So to anyone with a 9100 (or close)

Hows the Wireless in Dapper?
Does it overheat? (I was hearing about this before)
What about the power management? Battery Life?
Is it sexy?

Don't be scared by the last question. I may sound crazy, but that's because I am.

### Post by morgengenuss on 2006-07-20
i have a dell inspiron 9400, which should be quite close to your notebook. (see specifications in my signature)

i haven't experienced any serious problems since i upgraded to dapper.
actually the latest driver for my ati x1400 even enabled auto-throttling for the gpu.
i had no problems with acpi, although i can't control the fans manually. but everything seems to work out fine and temperatures are quite the same as in xp (especially after i installed the latest fglrx-driver).

wireless worked out of the box. i can see signal strength with wifi-radar (a very nice little prog i can only recommend).

so, for me (x)ubuntu is quite sexy.

### Post by Komissar on 2006-08-04

Does it overheat?
-- I measured it and it falls in its operating temp, fans go on and off regularly. I don't seem to have any issues.
