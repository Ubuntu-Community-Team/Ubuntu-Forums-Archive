### Post by lexhan on 2008-03-05
The advice I have is now out of date since 7.10 Gutsy Gibbon.

Have you tried an install? I was installing with Feisty Fawn and things have improved since then.
It might well detect your graphics card and wireless card okay.
You should try it and come back to the forum if you get stuck. It might work perfectly well.

If not, the worst that will happen is that it will be workable, except you will have to use an ethernet cable for internet and your resolution will be lower than it should be. 
This is temporary. It can be fixed and everything work ok. I was new to Linux and not knowledgeable but I just followed instructions from this forum step by step.
You should search for up to date information regarding the* Intel integrated graphics card* and *AutoResolution915*.
I found the following helpful, but, again, it might be out of date:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/i915Driver#head-6fcd801a3621b9769f7bf9f48fa4e3ad6e256654]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/i915Driver#head-6fcd801a3621b9769f7bf9f48fa4e3ad6e256654")

[http://roland-lopez.blogspot.com/2007/03/auto915resolution-ubuntu-resolution-fix.html]("http://roland-lopez.blogspot.com/2007/03/auto915resolution-ubuntu-resolution-fix.html")

If the wireless card doesn't work, this is a common problem. Search the forum for up to date information regarding *Broadcom wireless card*. The method I used involved *Ndiswrapper*. 
Now there are tools that will make this sort of thing easier to deal with. For example:
[http://sourceforge.net/projects/wifix/]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/wifix/")
