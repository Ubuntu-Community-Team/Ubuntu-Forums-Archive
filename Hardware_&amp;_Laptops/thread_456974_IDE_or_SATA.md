### Post by Lucifiel on 2007-05-28
SATA = new standard of hard disks. They are better than IDE. However, there is Sata-1(also called Sata) and Sata-2(which is the faster standard of Sata). 

IDE= older standard of hard disks. It's been in use for a very long time and is going to be phased out soon. 

What is your motherboard model, though? And do you have a laptop or desktop? We need to find out if your motherboard supports Ide or Sata. 

Also, if your motherboard is an old version(about 2 to 3 years old), then it might not support Sata-2 mode and you might have problems booting up your pc if you try to use Sata-2. However, you can set a jumper mode to enable Sata-1 mode.

I don't recommend 64 bit Feisty at the moment because you might have trouble running plenty of programs as they do not support 64-bit yet. Unless, you only intend to run a certain number of programs, just stick to 32-bit.
