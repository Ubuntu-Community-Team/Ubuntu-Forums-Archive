### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-01-30
This is not an Ubuntu-specific question, but I wanted to ask here because I know I'll get better help than I'd find in an arbitrary hardware forum somewhere on the Internet.

A friend's LCD screen on a Dell laptop has gone very dim, almost invisible unless you look really closely.  If the computer gets rebooted, the screen will be fine for about ten seconds, and then it goes dim again.  I am sure that it's the screen that's at fault because the laptop is fine when connected to an external display.  I also booted the machine to Linux (it was running XP) and witnessed the same problems, so it's not the software's fault.

From the research I've done, it sounds like this is a classic backlight/inverter failure.  My major question is: how hard and expensive is this to fix yourself?  I know we could buy an inverter on ebay for under $100, but I've read conflicting information about whether or not I need to find one specific to the laptop model; is this true?  And in terms of reinstalling the inverter, is it that hard?  I have plenty of experience taking desktop systems apart but I've never opened up a laptop or an LCD display, and I don't have any special equipment on hand, just some screwdrivers.

Thanks in advance for any feedback.

### Post by vociferous666 on 2008-01-30
f you have verified all connections on the screen by taking the top cover off the screen and made sure all wires are free from being pinched,  then you can go ahead and buy an inverter.  

an new component is not always the fix though, if the light inside the LCD is going out, then you my friend, are ******.  

now as for buying an inverter, you need to find out if you need one for your specific model by finding some dell hardware forum.  im not a dell guy myself.

most times the screens are very similar and share many components making production cheaper. you should be able to buy one from the same model series and have it work in your favor.  

you may have to use a flashlight to see the screen.

that sounds like a capacitor not holding charge.  which dismisses my rant about wires and such.  it is most likely yor inverter and not your physical connections.  so yea go buy a used component on ebay.  it will probably have you up and running.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-01-30
If all connections are good, the backlight is going out.  Happens to the best of them.  IF it is still under warranty, have him call Dell and they will replace it at no charge.  IF NOT under warranty and your friend is handy with a screwdriver and soldering iron, the backlight CAN be replaced for a lot less than the cost of a new monitor (that is what Dell will do and then re-sell it as refurbished on eBay.)
