### Post by jml on 2007-04-18
fettuohi, check out this link.

[http://www.system76.com/](http://www.system76.com/)

System 76 is a company that sells laptops and desktops with Ubuntu preinstalled.  The value that they add in my opinion is that they make sure everything works out of the box, (3d accelerated graphics, sound, wireless networking, etc.)  They also sponser a subforum on this site, under the 3rd party forum section.  They sell great products and offer very good customer support.  And, no, I don't work for them, but I did buy a fully loaded Darter from them and am very happy with it.

If you want to install Linux yourself, than another suggestion is to look at the HP small business web site:

[http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/us/en/sm/WF02a/321957-321957-64295.html?jumpid=re_R295_prodexp/busproducts/computing-notebook/business-notebook-pcs&psn=notebooks_tablet_pcs/notebook_pcs](http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/us/en/sm/WF02a/321957-321957-64295.html?jumpid=re_R295_prodexp/busproducts/computing-notebook/business-notebook-pcs&psn=notebooks_tablet_pcs/notebook_pcs)

These laptops can be ordered without Windows if you like.  If you do order it with Windows, they usually do not load a lot of extra usless software/trialware on them so there is less to delete.  Be sure to configure it with an Intel wireless card rather than a Broadcom one.  It can be tricky getting a Broadcom card to work in Linux.   Just my two cents worth.

Joe
