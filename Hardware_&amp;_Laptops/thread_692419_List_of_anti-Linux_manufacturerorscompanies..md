this is my list so far, I would be happy to see to add other ( I am sure there are more).
So people if you intend to buy hardware to be used with Linux do not buy from the companies above.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-02-18
add a comment for D-Link .. found that technical support is excellent, and they back up their product UNTIL the warranty expires, then they WILL stick you (or try to) for replacement parts.  The weakest issue with D-Link is their wall warts (power supply/transformers) .. all you have to do is Google to find that out.  REPLACE IT and some of YOUR issues may go away!

The Athersos based chipset wireless cards install out of the box .. others are problematic and the issue there is the CHIP MAKER and not D-Link.   

Before you start accusing hardware makers of being ANTI LINUX, you should check the chipsets they are using and therein you will find the true issues.

You think it is trouble installing on a desktop? (not if you shop your hardware FIRST) ..

Then there is the laptop issue, an area that .. if you read this forum alone .. will show you is a total crap shoot and mine field with various makers.

but the WORST area for install is on a PDA!  Lots of lip service but NO SUPPORT or really active forums at ALL. (If you have ever tried to utilize WinDOZE on a PDA, you will know why Linux is a MUCH better idea!)(8 pages and 15 screens to install Wi-Fi .. and then there is no assurance it will WORK!)

The easiest way  to get manufacturers to support any form of Linux is to ONLY use those that make items that WORK or can made to work with THEIR co-operation.  The marketplace has to start dictating.  When they see sales increases in the stuff that works in Linux, they will make more of it. (great example is NVidia!)

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-02-20
Just because an item does not work for YOU, does not mean it does not work and that the company is anti linux .. but when you see the same brand come up on this discussion board over and over again and on different platforms and with the same issues, you can begin to assume that there most likely is an issue with the product/manufacturer.  I have observed that there are far more issues with the semi-proprietary "on board chip"  builds of laptop makers than those of desktop makers.
AND the decided advantage of a desktop is being able to choose your cards and hardware pre install (what I have learned to do) and to level the playing field.
The Ubuntu wiki pages are loaded with info on stuff that works out of the box.  It behooves you to look there before you totally leap into the program or start jamming a particular manufacturer. 

an add on:
On the laptops, just because it has a makers name on it, does not mean they made it. (If you Google "who makes laptops?" you may be surprised!) 
IE:  Alienware laptops are made by Sager and just put in their case with some added programs. (Sager is about 1/2 the price!) (and now Dell owns Alienware)

Sort of like the H3 Hummer is a Chevy Suburban with a different body and beefed up suspension!
