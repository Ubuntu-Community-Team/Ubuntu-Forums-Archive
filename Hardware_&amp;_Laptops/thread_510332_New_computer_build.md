### Post by brent113 on 2007-07-26
I'm not an expert on the latest processors, and video cards just get the best you can afford.

Ram wise, 2 gigs is perfect.  That should last you as long as you need.

For the hard drive, I recommend the Seagate 500Gb 7200.9 series.  They are cheap on newegg ($110) and really fast.  What I don't understand is why they are still a little bit faster than the newer 7200.10 series, so I'm still running the "outdated" 9 series.  10K drivers are a lot faster, but they cost a lot more too, so for your budget I don't recommend it.

500W PS will be more than enough, I have an Antec 450W with a similar config and a power hungry video card, and it runs fine.

### Post by dabl on 2007-07-26
I've got 4GB of RAM and a Core 2 Extreme CPU, and I've never seen it use over 500MB of RAM, and I've never seen it use 1 byte of swap.  And I've loaded 2 sessions of Gnome Wave Cleaner up with WAV files and maxed out both CPU cores for 5 minutes at a time (warmed up the room nicely!).

So I agree that 2GB of RAM is the max you need, and a Core 2 Duo would be a good combo.  Newegg has the E4400 on sale for $126USD.

On the motherboard, you might want to consider the cost of a better board like mine, which includes the Intel HDA sound system, versus a cheaper mobo but you gotta buy a separate sound card.  I'm really happy with my audio performance -- I'm doing recording of old records, and it works great and sounds great on my Altec speakers.

Power Supply -- do NOT go cheap on this!  I bought a Antec TP3-650, to replace the first one I bought which was a 500-watt Antec Smartpower that died on the first powerup.

That's my two cents' worth!  :)

### Post by brent113 on 2007-07-26
I have used Asus (excellent board, a7n8x deluxe, but expensive when I bought it), and right now I have an Intel board.

I'd recommend the Intel board for what you're going to use it for.  There is no overclocking or risky features, it's just a solid board, with excellent stable drivers, and guaranteed compatibility.

You would probably be able to get a little better performance out of a different manufacturer, but I'd take stability over tiny performance gains any day (never have been a big fan of overclocking).
