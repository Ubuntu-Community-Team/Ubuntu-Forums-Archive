I am a seasoned Linux user with a brand-new Dell E1405 laptop. I have just installed Ubuntu Edgy on it, and everything works pretty much the way I want it, including WPA encrypted wireless, Beryl, suspend/hibernate, and all the other normally complicated stuff. But one thing bugs me.

The laptop, like most, has keys for increasing and decreasing the monitor brightness. These will work only if I use them while the computer is booting. If I press either one while the computer is either at the GRUB screen or the bootup splash screen, they will work. If I don't touch them during bootup, then they do nothing from then on. Either effect lasts until the next time the computer is powered down (either shutdown or hibernate, but not suspend to RAM). 

Edit: Ok, it's not actually that simple. I just ran into two instances where I used the keys only a few times during boot and they still stopped working. Presumably there is some critical time during boot in which I have to activate the keys by using them.

Does anyone have any idea what's wrong or how to fix this? I would accept as a workaround a way to tweak the monitor brightness via a bootup script, just to "activate" the keys.
