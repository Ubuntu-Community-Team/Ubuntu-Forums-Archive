### Post by fizikz on 2007-08-17
1. You just need a hard drive enclosure. They're generally not too expensive. Just make sure to note whether the internal is a 3.5" (desktop) or 2.5" (notebook) hard drive and get the appropriate enclosure.

2. You generally don't want to boot and run programs, especially games, off an external hard drive, as their transfer speeds are bottlenecked by the interface (usually USB or Firewire). If you have an eSATA port on your laptop, that will give you quite good performance for an external drive, but in my opinion you should keep your OS and applications on the notebook's internal drive. Use the external for data and backups.
