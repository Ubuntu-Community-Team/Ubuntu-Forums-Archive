### Post by trevorparsons on 2007-10-14
My experience with EfficientPC is mainly excellent. I recently advised a client to buy an Isis desktop, which arrived fine and is a good piece of kit well set up.

The only problem with the system was a bug where enabling 'desktop effects' (ie compiz) resulted in a really bad screw-up of the Gnome multiple workspaces, to my client's bemusement. There are various bug reports along these lines but I couldn't find a fix and so have had to disable desktop effects for the moment. Maybe the Gutsy upgrade will sort it out.

I reported the bug to EfficientPC 12 days ago, but haven't had an acknowledgement yet.

A friend, who is keen to buy the Wraith Series laptop trailed as 'coming soon', has also been disappointed in having no response to an enquiry asking for an ETA of the product. Put together with various other non-response reports in this thread and elsewhere, it does seem that responding to email is currently a problem for EfficientPC.

I can appreciate that Dale is probably experiencing capacity problems in trying to deal with the unexpected popularity of his offering, and understandably fulfilling orders comes higher up the priority list than answering ancillary email. However, given that the only communication method made available to customers is email, it's important that it should be answered promptly, even if it's not possible to provide a definitive answer.

I really want EfficientPC to succeed, and I am still keen to recommend it to clients and friends who want PCs preloaded with free software. But unanswered email is a real downer and, for a small company that absolutely depends on personal recommendation, is likely to result in a loss of confidence and sales.

Dale, reassurance please!  :)
