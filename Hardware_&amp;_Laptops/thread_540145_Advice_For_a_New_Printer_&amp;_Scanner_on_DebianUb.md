### Post by eggdeng on 2007-09-01
Totally recommend HP's all-in-ones (my model is a PSC 1510). Installing is a piece of cake (plug in and choose a driver from the list) and printing and scanning work perfectly straight off. I print mostly black-and-white & find cartridges are cheap and last a lot longer than with my last printer (an Epson Stylus). I think it's important that we support companies who not only support Linux but also do a first-class job with the drivers.

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-09-01
Thoroughly agree with eggdeng.

I have a HP psc series all in one and it just works.

Also you can then use the HPLIP toolbox to monitor ink levels, perform head cleaning and all of the other housekeeping stuff.

I have also found that it is sometimes better to install HP printers using the HPLIP toolbox, instead if just using Linux's Add Printer function.
