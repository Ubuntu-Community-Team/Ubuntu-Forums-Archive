### Post by porcorosso on 2007-07-16
This is really just a whine for commiseration more than a call for help.

What the heck? I installed Ubuntu 7.04 on an old Inspiron 7500 last week. This system had been loaned to another person for a couple of years. Unfortunately it experienced a couple of failures under Windows XP Pro at the hands of that user who has always been a little "trouble-prone." (Why, no, I didn't install anything new! Oh, you mean those 10 spyware-laden pieces of crap I got from my buddy who set up P2P for me?) She gave the thing back to me because she said it was no good. I was inclined to think that it probably had seen better days, but I decided to see if I could get it working again.

The WinXP installation was truly farkled, so I decided to play around with Ubuntu. I am new to Linux, so I just went for a plain-Jane setup, using the guided partitioning / installation process. There were no hitches in the process. 

After the installation I was able to figure out how to use Synaptic to find and install a driver for the Synaptics touchpad so that I could turn of the infernal tap-to-click behavior. I got the native 1400x1050 resolution working. I tested the file system thoroughly and ran memtest overnight to see if the system was okay.

Everything was happy, and I used the heck out of the thing all week long. On Friday, bolstered with a (as it turns out false) sense of security, I ordered a replacement for the AC adapter that the previously mentioned user had tossed out. (I've been borrowing an adapter in the meantime.)

So I crank the Inspiron up this morning, and Ubuntu is behaving strangely. I'm hearing click-click-click from the drive. I'm having trouble getting the system to do anything for me. Apps are quitting with no warning. I have trouble with a terminal session. I try to look at logs and then I get a spontaneous reboot. I see ext3 "not found" errors on the text screen. I try to reboot, and upon logging on I get a window telling me that my session lasted less than 10 seconds, etc. I try rebooting in recovery mode and wind up having the partition manager quit on me while I'm trying to use it. After this it just sits there and blinks the CapsLock and NumLock lights at me when I try to boot it.

I boot off the CD and try another install. This time it freezes at 15% on the Detecting File Systems portion of installation. Nice.

Hey, Ubuntu, I am impressed. You are a nice operating system. You are easy to install and easy to configure -- at least for someone who is willing to do a little research. But you shouldn't have let me order that danged AC adapter! You should have told me that I was trying to install you on a crappy piece of hardware.

How come the hardware failures I've seen (under Windows and now under Linux) are so damned coy? I'm going on record right now stating that I want my hardware to fail in a frank manner! I want the danged thing to blow up in my face like an exploding cigar! No more of this passing tests for several days and then lying down and dying crap! You hear me, hardware manufacturers? I want C-4!

Okay, I feel better now.

If someone can tell me how to avoid similar experiences in the future, please advise.
