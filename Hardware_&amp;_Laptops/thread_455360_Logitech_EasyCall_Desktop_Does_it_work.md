### Post by blueadept on 2007-05-29
Yes,

I have one of these desktops running under Slackware Linux, it is picked up as a standard wireless USB keyboard/mouse & a standard USB audio device...  The "application" specific buttons don't return any scancode that I can see, but to be fair I have not tried it with skype under Linux... however I am able to use the headset and speakerphone with Ekiga and Teamspeak on Linux without any problems... 

One minor issue that I have with every USB headset etc under linux is that the system doesn't distinguish between the different audio devices for the volume buttons... so they turn the main volume up and down.

In summary, the telephone device works, but you can't use ANY of the buttons on it... (unless they work in the Linux version of skype)... but this isn't really a problem.

I wonder what possesses people at Logitech to make this device tho, becuase there is no way to switch between speaker and headset without unplugging the headset... that's nothing to do with Linux either... just a missing facility... which seems obvious to me, it was the first thing I wanted to do after I got it working...
