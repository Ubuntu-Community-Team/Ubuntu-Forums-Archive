### Post by volksolwagen57 on 2007-04-15
sometimes it's a matter of taste. just keep in mind the price vs. performance ratio. do you get that much more power from a chip that's $69 more dollars? keep in mind that both are very efficient and there are hardly any programs that fully utilize dual core, that is multi-threading in the form of parallel tasks. also keep in mind futur-proofing. the LGA775 will support the new ***real*** quad core which will actually be four cores on one die, instead of their current quad core which is actually two dual cores slapped together causing bottlenecking. but then again what software is there out there that can really tax a processor like this? look how long it might be until LGA775 or AM2 will phase out.
personally, i'm going to wait for this summer for AMD's true quad-core to come out, and anyway AMD makes good chips for cheap and they have the underdog quality that i've always loved.
