Why not just buy a SATA hdd and then get a 3.5" SATA/USB enclosure for it?

It will be cheaper, and you will get large warranties (5 years??) on the hard drive.

Plus there will no firmware "surprises" in store for you. btw, this is the first that I have heard of ANYBODY restricting ANY content on external drives. I prefer to dismiss this as a rumour, as I can't really see how this can be done.

That said, I would recommend that you store torrent downloads to your fixed drive rather than your external drive. Once completed, you can archive (with the move torrent feature) and share the torrent from your external drive. This is because if your torrent download is interrupted, it will be rechecked by your client, and on a USB drive will not be as fast as on an internal drive.
