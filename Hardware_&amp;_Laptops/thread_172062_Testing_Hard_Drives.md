### Post by yager on 2006-05-07
Your best bet would be to first sort the disks by manufacturer and then download the specific diagnostic tools from that manufacturer.  As an example, here is the link to the Western Digital Support Page where you can download old versions of their diagnostic tools.  Of course the downside is that you will have to take the time to install each drive to make this happen.

[http://support.wdc.com/download/downloadxml.asp](http://support.wdc.com/download/downloadxml.asp)

Seagate:
[http://www.seagate.com/support/seatools/](http://www.seagate.com/support/seatools/)
