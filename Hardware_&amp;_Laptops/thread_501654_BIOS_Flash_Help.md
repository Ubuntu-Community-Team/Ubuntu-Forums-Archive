### Post by kidders on 2007-07-16
Hi there,

I've been in this boat. Annoying as hell, isn't it?!

To be perfectly honest though, unless of course you have a dual BIOS, I wouldn't be too enthusiastic about messing around with ways of flashing it that aren't explicitly supported by your manufacturer. Turning your laptop into a paperweight would be ... ehh ... unfortunate.

Although other users may have different advice, mine would be to play it safe, throw the oldest, crappiest version (ie the quickest to install) of Windows your flasher supports onto a spare disk partition for 10 minutes, and be done with it. Sorry ... I know this is exactly the advice you *don't* want.

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-07-17
I agree 100% with the above advice.

Flashing a BIOS is a risky business. Sure, many people do it without problem, but if it does go wrong, you have a very expensive paperweight.

The ONLY reason you should even consider flashing your BIOS is because you are experiencing problems that can only be fixed by installing an upgraded BIOS.

Otherwise leave well alone.
