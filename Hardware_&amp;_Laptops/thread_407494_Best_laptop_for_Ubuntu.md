### Post by garrido on 2007-04-12
This will probably get you millions of answers as it's very difficult to say what is "best" in any circumstances...  Find out as much as you can about the hardware in the models you are considering, then check if all that is supported.  I have a Vaio FJ170 where everything "kind of" works, with a little bit of trouble.  The webcam started working on Feisty, the Fn keys are a pain but work, and I haven't tested VGA or TV out...

The key here is to select a few models in the price range you want and find out all you can about their hardware, then check Google for success stories :)

### Post by jml on 2007-04-12
I agree that you will probably get a lot of responses, so I will add my opinion.  For a new relatively inexpensive laptop, I would recommend HP.  If you look at their small business web page as opposed to their consumer web page, you will find several very inexpensive laptops.

[http://www.hp.com/sbso/busproducts_notebooks.html](http://www.hp.com/sbso/busproducts_notebooks.html)

  I bought a pre-configured one for $499.00 US.  The other reason I like these laptops is the fact that HP does not load them up with a lot of junk you have to delete if you plan to keep a Windows partition.  You can also order one without Windows!  They substitute FreeDOS which is a small foot print MS-DOS clone.

For used laptops I would check out this site:

[http://www.usanotebook.com/index.php](http://www.usanotebook.com/index.php)

They sell a lot of laptops and the ones I would recommend are their IBM ThinkPads. I purchased one from them about a year ago and it was really a good deal and it was in good condition.  ThinkPads have a reputation for being very Linux friendly.  Just my two cents worth.
