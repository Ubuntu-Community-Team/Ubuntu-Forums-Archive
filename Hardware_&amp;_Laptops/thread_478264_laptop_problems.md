### Post by Atomic Dog on 2007-06-19
I came accross this type of behavior on a Compaq laptop once.  It would start, no BIOS screen, then shut down.  The only difference is occasionally it would boot.  I also suspected a battery/charging issue.

After lots of frustration I swapped out the hard drive and it started worning normally.  Now you would not expect a bad HD to cause a laptop to not even hit BIOS, but it did.

For kicks and giggles remove the HD and try to boot it. with a live CD.  Or just see if BIOS appears after removal.

### Post by RU-In? on 2007-06-19
I have a few suggestions that come to mind right away...

Your information posted is a little limited but what you did say about the lights sounds like maybe it is powering down after a few seconds

-  Try to make good confirmation that it *really does not *stay powered up... Put your ear to the area where the hard drive is and listen for activity and so on (esp. after the lights go out). I've seen situations where one thinks that the laptop is not powering up but in fact, it was actually starting with the external video and not the built in lcd display (if you think it did stay powered up, try to switch the video output with a hot-key, usually fn-[something]). which brings me to this, if you think it might be powered up...look at the lcd with a flashlight and see if you can see video "inside there", maybe view it at an angle. Maybe you just blew the backlight unit.

- Make sure that the all of the buttons and keys are working freely and not stuck in any position.

- This is just a shot in the dark, but did you consider the CMOS Battery? A lot of systems won't even power up if the CMOS battery is no good. This problem has caused many of systems to hit the trash can or be given away! And, by the way, I have never had to purchase the $12-$40 battery from the manufactuer. I have always been able to grab one of the same specs. from Radio Shack or Even sometimes a Dept. store. You then re-use the connector from the original battery.

Post back with some more information...
