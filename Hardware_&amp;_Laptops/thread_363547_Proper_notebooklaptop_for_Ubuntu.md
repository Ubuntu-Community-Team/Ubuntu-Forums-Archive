### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-02-18
I have an Acer TravelMate 4101 that works perfectly. This is now nearly 2 years old and has been out of production for some time.

However, it is always a minefield when buying something new, as laptops are often cutting-edge and there may be problems with the screen resolution and aspect ratio. I believe the ATI video chipset can cause problems. It would be a good idea to have a good look on the forums, before deciding what to buy. The intel graphics chipsets seem to work OK, when the 915resolution graphics driver is installed. Also, the nVidia chipsets seem to be quite compatible.

I know that quite a few Acer models actually come with Linpus Linux BE factory installed, so hopefully, there shuould be little problem in installing Ubuntu over it.

Regards.
