### Post by nukedathlonman on 2007-10-25
I've got almost the same lappy (5002WLMI - only difference is a slower CPU).  As you say, no OpenGL support due to SiS's ignorance...  But in another sense, it's kind of okay as the video is a frame buffer device that utilizes system RAM - it would be terribly slow even if OpenGL support was officially added.  However, it does support 2D acceleration.  While frame buffer devices with the frame buffer drivers enabed may or may not introduce instability, you can unblock the kernel frame buffer driver to enable it, and also load the other SiS video driver.  I have no issues with using these driver's to enable 2D acceleration, and it helped quite a bit.  Other people's mileage may vary on that.  I'm not sure about your particular notebook, but mine came equipped with two 512MB DDR-333 memory sticks, and I swapped them out for two 1gb DDR-400 memory sticks a few montsh ago.  Switching the memory speed from 333 to 400 made a huge speed increase to the system (esp in regards to video performance).
