### Post by jdos2 on 2008-01-02
I can't endorse Toshiba as a Laptop company for their terrible Linux support, but I found a Satellite A215 for $499.
It's got a 150GB fixed disk, came with 2GB ram, and a dual core AMD processor. 

Yes, it's a troublesome Intel sound. Yes, the WIFI needs to be compiled (RTL8187b-"modified"), yes the USB sometimes needs manual rescan before it recognizes the sound card, yes, the "fn" key isn't recognized in Ubuntu 7.10 (though was in previous versions)  and yes, of course, it doesn't suspend with Ubuntu 7.10 (though it did in previous versions), but...

It's fast. Battery life is almost three hours. It plays DVD's just fine (I actually when out to purchase a DVD player, believe it or not, when I saw the cheap laptop!) and the sound, after it's ironed out, is really nice. In spite of Toshiba's best efforts, it's a decent laptop, and it'll get better as support for it gets better. 
250 pounds, I think. Not bad.
