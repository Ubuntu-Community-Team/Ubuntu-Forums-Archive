### Post by jaboo on 2004-10-30
I have an Epson Stylus Photo 900 printer that I have connected to a USB port on my notebook. At first, I did not even see the Stylus Photo 900 listed among the printer drivers. So, I added the cupsys-driver-gimpprint from synaptic, and this added several more printers and options to my drivers list, including the Stylus Photo 900. So, I set everything and pressed the print a test page button. I got a print out. However, it was poor quality, and it's margins are off. It looks like it is shifted down about one inch. Does anyone else have any experience with this line of Epson printers? Any advice to get a better quality print out? Thanks for any insight. BTW, I was able to get this same printer working just fine on Gentoo Linux, but linuxprinting.org does not seem to have their cupsomatic ppd generator around anymore. Thanks.
