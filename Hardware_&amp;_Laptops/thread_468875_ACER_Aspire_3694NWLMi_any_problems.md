### Post by tommcd on 2007-06-09
I just bought an Acer Aspire 3680-2633. It is one of the cheaper ones. You might consider buying it.  Ubuntu installed without problems. Ubuntu even picked up the atheros integrated wifi. You will need the 915 resolution package to get 1280x800 resolution. No problems so far though, even with the 2.6.20.16 kernel. 
For what it's worth, Zenwalk runs well on it also, on kernel 2.6.21.3.

### Post by CyberBob on 2007-06-12
I've purchased two Acer Aspire 3680-2633 laptops from the same store recently (one for my son, one for me).  They had different wireless chipsets, the one one for my son had the AR5005G chipset and I eventually got it to work "mysteriously" after a third fresh install of 64-bit Feisty.

My laptop still does not work with the built-in wireless - I'm using a wired connection at the moment.  I've posted details of this hardware [in this other recent post]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=472317").

There are no guarantees it seems.  Sometimes we get lucky, sometimes not.  This community is wonderful in helping out when you do have issues.  Whatever you decide on purchasing - enjoy the new computer and good luck!
