### Post by Perpetual on 2007-11-05
Can't answer your question regarding the wireless, not on my Ubuntu machine either but *I beleive* that a lot of the problems with Gutsy are upgrade related when people have 3rd party software installed.  There are some people having problems with clean installs as well but I would bet the ratio is less where clean installs are a problem.

With that said, if you have Gutsy installed and it is working seemingly well, I wouldn't downgrade to Feisty.  Gutsy works great for me, faster, better battery life and Gnome 2.2 is nice.  If you can, give Gutsy a week and see if anything comes up.  You may find it's fine and quite enjoyable.

### Post by mummra1 on 2007-11-05
As another option, you can always install ndiswrapper.  This worked wonderfully for me with my Belkin wireless which would not come up in Gutsy.  You can install ndiswrapper off of the cdrom, but you do need the .inf and .sys files for your wireleless driver.
