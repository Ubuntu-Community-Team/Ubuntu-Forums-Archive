### Post by mdmarmer on 2006-05-09
Most laptops can be made to work fairly well in Linux

Buy at a store and take a live CD with you -- this will give you more confidence in your purchase

Try out the live CD on several laptops

If you see problems, many can be fixed -- of course some problems are difficult

HP probably has the best linux compatibility if you don't mind a little work getting Broadcom wireless to work

However, HP does not have the build quality or reliability of a Toshiba, Sony or IBM

Also I don't think HP are available with NVidia card

Toshiba have some unique features which you may have problems with, but you will be able to get most things working in Linux

I have Toshiba M45 - almost everything works in Linux

Also, some HP models show overheating in Ubuntu, particularly 64-bit  -- there may be a fix for this soon

What else?

Mike

### Post by mdmarmer on 2006-05-09
If I had to guess, I would say the Toshiba P100 is very new, has a lot of proprietary features, and you will have major problems in any Linux distro

For now -- don't get dual core, new wireless card, SATA drive and more new things in the same laptop -- just my 2 cents -- but you'll be able to see this when you take the live CD with you and see the problems first-hand

Mike

P.S. -- If you do get a Toshiba and want dual boot, you may want to use the Windows boot loader -- GRUB will not recognize Toshiba special buttons
