### Post by KirbySaysHi on 2007-03-06
This was posted in the general help, but I'm thinking this really belongs here.

My problem: My keyboard and mouse geek out and freeze at random intervals.

I have a logitech 510mx usb mouse and an old IBM Model M keyboard (the clicky one) with a ps/2 to usb adapter on it. I don't have any issues in Windows XP SP2.

In Edgy, when I do anything more than browse the internet, like install windows in vmware, or open gimp, my mouse and/or keyboard become unresponsive. They don't always go at once, and it's not always the same first (ie, sometimes the keyboard dies, then a few minutes later the mouse, or reversed). I can fix this by unplugging each and replugging them back in, but this is EXTREMELY annoying, since it happens almost every 30 seconds when doing intensive things!

After searching the forums, I think it could be two things: something to do with my logitech mouse (although why would that affect the keyboard?), something to do with my wacom tablet (which is hooked up but doesn't have any drivers installed), or my chipset (nforce4).

Any ideas, anything?

My system specs:

Intel Core 2 Duo 2.44 gHz
nvidia 7600gt
2 gigs ram
1 PATA WD hard drive (root and swap)
2 SATA hard drives (Windows and other files)
Creative X-Fi ExtremeGamer (no drivers for it yet)
Asus P5NSLI mobo (onboard AC97, Marvell Yukon, SLI support)
a firewire pci care (possibly adaptec)

Peripherals:
Logitech 510mx usb mouse
IBM Model M w/ ps/2 adapter
Wacom Intuos 3 8x12 tablet
Various others (ipod, palm, etc)

Please help me! Tell me if anyone needs more info!

Drew*

### Post by jjborcha on 2007-06-30
Check your BIOS options and turn off "legacy USB support" or something similar.  I had an Asus P5NSLI with the same problem of mouse freezing randomly and this took care of the problem.
