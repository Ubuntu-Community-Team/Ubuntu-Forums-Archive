### Post by stream303 on 2006-11-21
I had a GX150 with the same problem as I was trying to drive a 19" monitor at 1280x1024.  Don't know if that's your situation, but to get you off the ground you can try lowering your default color depth to 16 instead of 24.  Here's the snippet from your xorg.conf file:

```
Section "Screen"
Identifier "Default Screen"
Device "ATI Technologies, Inc. Rage 128 Pro Ultra TF"
Monitor "DELL E771p"
**DefaultDepth 24**
SubSection "Display"
```

Depending on the size of your monitor, 16 might be your only bet.  For some reason, 24 just would not work - about 95% working for me, but would frustrate me so much I gave in and used a 16 bit depth.

### Post by strider_mt2k on 2007-08-24
I really dodged a bullet with the GX240 I picked up from eBay.

I accidentally won a half-height nvidia MX400 64MB AGP card on an earlier auction (thinking it was full height) and swapped it out for the 32MB ATI card that was installed prior to installing Feisty Fawn.

I know it's trite to simply say "get another card" but in this case it appears to have saved me a TON of hassles, and was a very inexpensive upgrade to boot. (pardon the pun)

The install went flawless, and it plays videos and even desktop effects nicely.
