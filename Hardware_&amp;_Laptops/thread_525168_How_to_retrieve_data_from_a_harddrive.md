### Post by K.Mandla on 2007-08-14
Your first idea is probably the best. Find a working machine that has a spare connection for a hard drive and set the jumpers on the drive to slave position (you'll have to check the drive itself or the manufacturer's Web pages for a diagram of how to set the jumpers). Connect, reboot and you should be able to access the drive normally, provided there wasn't any damage to it. Copy the information to a new drive, or to a CD or a USB key or whatever.

It's probably easier to just attach the drive as a secondary, rather than replacing the master drive on a different computer. It might take a little time for the system to adjust itself to new hardware, and you could be otherwise reclaiming your data.
