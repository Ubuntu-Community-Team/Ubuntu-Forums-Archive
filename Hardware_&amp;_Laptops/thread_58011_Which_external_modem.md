### Post by blastus on 2005-08-19
Gernally any external serial (RS-232 interface) modem should be OK. Stay away from external USB modems and any modem with a Conexant chipset. 

A lot (probably most) USB modems are fully "soft" modems and will only work with Windows. And my experience with Conexant modems is that they are flaky and unreliable (difficult to establish a connection and randomly drops connections) even with updated drivers for both Windows 2000 and XP. Furthermore, to get a Conexant modem to work on Linux you may have to buy drivers for them (see [http://www.linuxant.com/drivers/](http://www.linuxant.com/drivers/))

I have an internal PCI Lucent WinModem from Zoom but it has a DSP (Digital Signal Processor) so it is not an entirely "soft" modem. I was able to get it to work on Ubuntu...with a little bit of hard work. On Mepis my modem is recognized right out-of-the-box.
