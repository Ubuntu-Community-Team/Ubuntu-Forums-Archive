### Post by az on 2006-04-06
If it is an ATX power supply, they are common and easy to replace.  You should be able to get one from a used computer store.  

You need to unplug the wires which power the hard drives, cdroms and floppy, then remove the big plug that connects to the motherboard.  There is a clip thinggy that you need to press so that you can pull it out - that also clues you in on which way to plug it in when you put in the new one.

Then unscrew the power supply box from the case and replace it.

Good luck.

BTW:  Power supplies are designed to protect your motherboard and peripherals from this sort of thing.  Everything *should* still work once you replace the power supply.
