Hi,

I am running Breezy (5.10) on a Dell Latitude D600, with kernel image 2.6.12-9-686.

I put Warty on this box when it first came out, and one of the nice things was that the ACPI support Just Worked, including suspend to RAM when I hit the Sleep button (Fn+ESC) or closed the lid. It continued to work fine through upgrades to Hoary and, I'm pretty sure, to Breezy a month or so ago.

Now, in the course of routinely saying OK when offered upgrades, sometime in the past two weeks, my notebook's ACPI support has deteriorated. THe sleep button no longer works. When I close the lid and reopen it, the box is locked up. If the machine sits idle for a little while, the mouse response gets very jerky, like the machine is not polling it often enough.

One of the reasons I was happy to switch to Ubuntu from Debian was that the notebook features worked well with the stock kernel image. Before I get into messing with compiling a custom kernel, does anyone have a good Howto for Ubuntu with the Dell Latitude, or any pointers at all?
