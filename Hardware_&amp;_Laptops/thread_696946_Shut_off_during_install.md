### Post by kidders on 2008-02-16
Hi there,

It's possible that the power supply is being overstretched ... did your roommate build his computer himself, or maybe replace or add something to it since he bought it?

Optical drives tend to draw quite a variable amount of power. By contrast, things like hard disks & sound cards place a much more consistent load on a PSU, so your suggestion that high-speed CD access might be causing the problem seems to fit.

I'd be curious to know what's plugged into your roommate's PSU, and what it's rating is.

**Edit:** Incidentally, I just thought I'd mention that under-loading modern computer components can be quite damaging, so I wouldn't be too keen on experimenting with trying to provoke these sudden shut-downs.

### Post by kidders on 2008-02-17
If an alternative PSU is a problem, you could always get him to temporarily disconnect as much stuff as possible, and do a quick test installation. If he can limit himself to on-board graphics, use only one hard disk, plug out lights/fans/extra optical drives/etc. and get through the install process, then it might be worth picking up a new PSU.
