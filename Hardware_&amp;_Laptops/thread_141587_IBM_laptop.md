### Post by glug101 on 2006-03-08
In my experience, the only laptops worth their salt are the ibms (err, lenovo now), the panasonic toughbooks (built incredibly tough) and the apple laptops.  Others may disagree, but that's to be expected ;)

The ibm laptops are great, and it sounds like you will be looking for the x series laptops for size.  However, I think you will not be able to find a small, light laptop that has a full size enter key like you described.  The small laptops try hard to save space, and so have no use for the upper part of the reversed 'L' enter keys.

Since you are allowing up to $2200 (US i assume?) you might want to check out the Alienware laptops.  They are usually not small (or cheap) but they pack a LOT of performance, and they are supposed to be well built (though I've never used one.)

Hope this helps:)

### Post by jml on 2006-03-23
I also think that Thinkpads are very good laptops.  Very Linux friendly.  About the only thing that you may have trouble configuring would be wireless networking.  Its the only reason I still dual boot my Thinkpad with Windows.  I have personally used the R31 and the R4o laptops with good results.  One thing I suggest is to decide what is most important to you, long battery life or light weight.  The X series Thinkpad is very light weight, but there are trad offs.  The battery life tends to be a little shorter, and if memory serves me, the optical drice is not built in, but snaps on the bottom of the unit adding bulk and weight.

The T series laptop seems to be the best balance of size and battery life.  Another point is the lower speed processor options in any given line, all other factors being equal have longer battery life.

As far as the Alienware laptops, they are very solid machines, but are  really designed for gamers.  They tend to be very heavy and have a very short battery life.
