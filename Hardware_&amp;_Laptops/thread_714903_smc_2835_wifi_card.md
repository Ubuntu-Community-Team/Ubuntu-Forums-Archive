### Post by andradx on 2008-03-04
This thread is not exclusively related to getting smc 2835 running, it has also some catches.

I got to a lot of trouble trying to get a **smc w2835 wifi pcmcia** card working on a Toshiba Sattelite (don't know the model but it's pretty old, it runs on a 433 MHz celeron).

The first problem I had to solve was what Linux OS I could install. As the BIOS dates before 99 Suse, Ubuntu 7.10, Fedora and a couple other didn't even booted. As Debian (4.0 r1 I think) was the only one who installed I tried sticking to that and get the card to work. However as that proved to be impossible and the ultimate decision of returning to win xp os pressed harder, in an extraordinary wise decision I burned a copy of Ubuntu 7.04.

I remembered that at a time while booting the live cd it complained about the BIOS but it booted allright and after installing Ubuntu 7.04 smc w2835 wifi card was up and running.
Since I've upgraded to 7.10 and it never stopped working.

Sorry about the more literary than technical english. Hope that it may prove worthy to have posted this.
