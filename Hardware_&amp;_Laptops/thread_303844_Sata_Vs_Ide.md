I am planning on putting together a new computer.  To keep the cost down I plan on purchasing a SATA drive and using an 80gb IDE drive from my old system I purchased about 2 years ago.  I am wondering if this is possible since all the power supplies I have looked at only have SATA power cables.  Also the motherboard I plan on purchasing has only SATA data connectors.  Is there some kind of converters that I can use for both the power and data connectors or do I have to somehow move my info from my IDE drive over to another SATA drive?  On the 80gb drive is my home partition which has information that I do not want to loose.  Any help on this would be great thanks in advance.

### Post by daou on 2006-11-21
The power connectors IDE uses are standard, the same used for DVD and CD drives. Every power supply has these. And if you need to convert a std. power connection to a SATA power connection, you only need a cheap adapter available in most computer hardware stores.

But if the motherboard does not have an IDE data connection, then you are out of luck on getting data from the IDE to the SATA drive. The easiest way, I suppose, would be to find a friend who has both and use their computer to transfer the data.
