### Post by Craig Caldwell on 2006-12-07
After trying to work with my 4th gen ipod for a year and a half. I've given up and bought an iriver T10 512MB.
 After going to this site [http://www.iriver.com/html/support/faq/sufq_view.asp?idx=387](http://www.iriver.com/html/support/faq/sufq_view.asp?idx=387) 
and upgrading from "Plays For Sure" to UMS I now have a usable player under ubuntu. Love the drag and drop action and the crazy battery life. 
 I tried Rockbox several times for my ipod but the battery life was very poor. I was getting about four hours. Crashing was an issue unless you compile with a maxed out processor clock. This made the battery life suffer even more.
 I also used banshee to sync but it would stop working after about a month every time. Once the sync quit I wasn't able to get it working with out a fresh install of ubuntu and a restore of the ipod.
 The iriver brand is getting very hard to find in Canada. I picked up mine at Staples for 65 bucks. I think you can order iriver from NCIX but the big retailers are only stocking ipods and a bunch of Windows only players.
 This is a shame because the iriver products buck the trend of using cheep parts to meet a price point. Sure it seems as though the price of iriver gear is high but most of their stuff can be run DRM free and the audio quality is so good.
 This might be one of the best mp3/ogg players for use on a linux box.
