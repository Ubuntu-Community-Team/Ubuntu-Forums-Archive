### Post by goatflyer on 2006-12-26
According to:

[http://www.linuxprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Canon-i470d](http://www.linuxprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Canon-i470d)

many people have difficulty getting that model to work. (I saw a few links showing howtos, they seemed pretty involved for a newbie).

When I checked Canon for an official Linux driver it comes up empty.
[http://software.canon-europe.com/](http://software.canon-europe.com/)

What all this means:
 Canon isn't supporting that printer on Linux.

There is something you could try:
[http://www.turboprint.de/english.html](http://www.turboprint.de/english.html)

According to their website, they support your printer.  You could try to download their evaluation software and try it.  If you still can't get your printer printing, then I'd say its hopeless (for now).

If it does work, then your alternatives are:
  1) buy their full version.  (ugh, but sometimes you do what you have to do)
  2) continue the struggle (and eventually succeed!) once you know more about working with linux.  In the meantime suffer without printing and dual boot back into windows when you need to print --- knowing this is temporary.


Getting Canon printers to work under linux is tough work -- its why I dual booted with windows for a long time.
