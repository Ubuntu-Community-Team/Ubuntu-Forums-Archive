### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-20
Dear Colyn,

My first choice would be a used 15" powerbook G4.  It's got built-in firewire 800 so you can use fast external drives (for photo backups).  It's got a decent screen for photo review.  2 USB ports are handy for card readers or other external devices.  Dapper 6.06.1 runs OK, few minor problems but it is improving.  The powerbook is a wide screen, not sure why that is a problem.

For a  normal 4:3 screen, I would go for a used T-series Thinkpad.  Integration is not as good as the powerbook, but the hardware is well-supported by Ubuntu.  There are lots floating around since businesses dump them every 3 years.  A used powerbook (loaded) will run about $1000 (US) a used thinkpad will run about $700.  Using Ubuntu . . . priceless.

A Mac Book Pro with Tiger 10.4.8 is hard to beat in terms of speed, functionality, and reliability.  Everything else is hacking.

Good luck,

Terry
