### Post by Miguel on 2006-04-14
Hi yozef,

You could have a go and ask at the notebookreview.com forums. These people look helpful... though not many seem to use linux (well, like the rest of the planet). Anyway... you should keep in mind a couple of things. [list]
[*] First, that while you can find Turion processors on 15.4 notebooks easily, most thin-and-light (and smaller) use centrinos.
[*] Second, if you are decided for AMD, their best notebook processors are currently the Turion MT. The ML's perform similarly... but draw 8 watts more.
[*] Third, if your laptop has a dedicated video card, look if it is an ATi X1000 series (X1400 or X1600). These still **_do not_** work with linux. Not even with propietary drivers
[*] Fourth, I don't know how well does the ATi X200 chipset work on linux, if your laptop will have this, inform yourself (I am an ATi user, if you haven't noticed)
[/list]

If you don't mind, I will make a few remarks about the Fujitsu. I've seen a couple of fujitsus on a local store, and these seem to be well built. Maybe not PowerBook class, but it isn't far either. Certainly better than acer and much better than my Dell Inspiron 8600.

The particular model you linked looks pretty good, too. It might be a tad too small for continuous desktop usage, though (13.3 inches, non widescreen = slightly cramped keyboard). I would ask about the ATI X200 chipset on linux, I personally don't know. Oh!!! and, on the options, go for the 5400rpm hard drive. do not doubt it. These are 50% faster (in real life) than 4200 and do not heat noticeably more. 

And, finally, the linux-on-laptops link:

[www.linux-laptop.net](www.linux-laptop.net)

PS: The Turion X2 should be out in summer, but, as they say... *"If you need the computer, buy it now. If you don't need it, wait"*

PS2: I hope I haen't puzzled you even more
