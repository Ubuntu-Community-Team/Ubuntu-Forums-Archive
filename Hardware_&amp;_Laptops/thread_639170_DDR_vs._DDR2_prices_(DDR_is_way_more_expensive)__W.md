### Post by xyphur on 2007-12-12
Supply & demand. DDR is older, and therefore less in-demand. It's always been like this. Go check prices on PC133 SDRAM and you'll see it's even more expensive per megabyte.

You've reached the point of diminishing returns on your investment... from a financial perspective, you'd be best served dropping your cash on a good cuttin-edge motherboard, and opting for the lower-end of it's supported CPUs (which will still technically be an upgrade from what you have). As for RAM, go for the max speed the new board supports. RAM clock speed is typically more important than the amount of it you have installed - of course, if you need more, you can always add it when it's come down in price - the key here is to keep track of what today's prices are as well as what's on it's way tomorrow and how soon it's expected to become mainstream. If you're on top of these trends, you'll keep your cost of ownership to a minimum during the course of one particular machine and it's various upgrades during it's service life.

Hope that wasn't too much to read. :P

I imagine it is due to manufacturers shifting to primarily focusing on producing DDR2 and even DDR3 memory now.  Used DDR ram is probably still pretty cheap, but I would thing DDR is going to get more expensive as time goes on much like PC133 ram is now over $100 per 512mb.

On whether or not to upgrade, I guess it depends on what you plan to do with the computer.  I have given up on upgrading my 939 system for gaming.  At least if you upgrade the whole system, the new AMD AM2+ and AM3 processors are supposed to backward compatible back to AM2 so you would be able to use DDR2 with an AM2 motherboard for at least another couple years.
