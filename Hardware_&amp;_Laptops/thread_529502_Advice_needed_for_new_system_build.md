### Post by Steve1961 on 2007-08-19
It's difficult to give specific advice here because the vast majority of components will work with Linux.  My advice would be to choose kit that's not too bleeding edge - perhaps 6-12 months old.  This should ensure that the Linux kernel has support built in for the hardware.  Personally I've always used AMD CPUs and MSI, Asrock or LanParty mobos without any issues.  That said, my next upgrade will probably be an Intel Core 2 Duo CPU.  As for RAM, any brand should be fine, but as it's reasonably cheap at the moment go for at least 1GB.  Also, get an Nvidia graphics card.  On your budget you might be better off sticking with integrated graphics initially and then upgrading once you can afford it.

Happy tinkering :)

ATI (duh)
