### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-02-03
Yet another nVidia lover here! :D

One further point I would suggest you check on when selecting a card - does it use a cooling fan?  I have bought all kinds of nVidia cards, most on the cheap end of the spectrum (not being a gamer either) and the crappy little fans so many manufacturers use are an absolute nightmare.  I've had quite a few die - usually noisily, so while annoying at least I knew what happened.  But I've also had one or two die _silently_ and the first you know of it is things locking up or dying.

When I built my current system one of my goals was to get a fanless card, and there are a fair number out there although they seem to be a little more expensive.  But the knowledge that I don't have yet another cheesy fan to die unexpectedly is worth it!

The card I just bought (actually two, I have a triple-head setup) is dual-DVI output, ships with a DVI-to-VGA converter.  [This MSI card]("http://www.newegg.com/product/product.asp?item=N82E16814127211") at Newegg, which is currently $110.  As a bonus, it still plays UT2k3 & 2k4 nicely... ;)  Obviously not a perfect fit for you as it's PCI Express, but perhaps will help your searching...
