### Post by teh.element on 2007-05-02
You should be able to get just about any card that was released within the last couple of years and have no trouble. NVIDIA has much better linux drivers than ATI, so I personally would go that route. My 7600 (in my laptop) has worked great when i tried dual monitors. I plugged in a CRT into its VGA port for that and it worked well in both WinXP and Ubuntu. Obviously the newer the card, the better graphics wise.

NOTE: If you go the NVIDIA route, check to see if the card you are looking at is supported by the newest NVIDIA driver. You won't want to have to use the older legacy driver as there wont be as much support for the card and dual monitors. However that shouldn't be too much of a problem.

My 7600 is great! It can play all the new games at Medium-High settings, but since it is in my laptop it gets up to 70~ degrees Celcius which is ridiculous. Hopefully my laptop cooler will ship soon ;)
