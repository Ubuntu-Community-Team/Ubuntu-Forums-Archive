### Post by dimeotane on 2007-10-20
The sansa e2xx series is pretty good, and supports USB mass storage with ubuntu, while the creative zen players require MTP transfer only.  

I was dissapointed at the sansa player and linux not accepting playlists.  The sansa required one format only ... plp, and wouldn't accept m3u or pla format.  I couldn't find anything on ubuntu  that would easily generate the playlist for the sansa e240.

Why are playlists so important?  Even if I copied files onto the unit organzied  in folders, the unit listed files by the album name, artist name, song name, and genre in the ID3 tag.
Playlists seemed to be the best way to organize music on the player. 

I'm still looking for the right unit to buy for ubuntu.   At least they keep getting cheaper and better the longer I wait.

### Post by dimeotane on 2007-10-21
I saw the Creative Zen V + on sale a futureshop here in Canada for $50 off.   I thought I'd give it a try and return it if is wasn't going to work.  I was reluctant at first because I read that it uses MTP mode instead of being a mass storage device.   (I read the the Creative stone uses mass storage mode which may be worth checking out). 

Altogether I'm pleased and I think will keep this player. With my new Gutsy install and rhythmbox it works pretty darn good where I can manage my MP3 collection ID3 tags and send tracks to the player and remove them. 

I found gnomad2 is needed creating playlists and for relabelling the ID3 tags of tracks that are on the player (can be done in bulk!) for example to set the genre of an album.
Also gnomad2 is needed for sending photos or video to the player.
