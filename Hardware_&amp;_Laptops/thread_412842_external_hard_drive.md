### Post by d.j.schroeder on 2007-04-18
Both rpm and cache can affect speed of file transfers.  Usually the slower rpm drives are slower in file transfers but use less power than the faster ones.  You'll have to decide how important those issues are to you to decide.  Cache memory can affect transfer speed but doesn't always, depends on what you're accessing on the drive and how.  

Short answer, if you aren't going to be doing huge file transfers and speed isn't really important to you, go with the cheaper one.  If you are going to be booting off of the drive, from your post I wasn't sure.  Then I would definitely go with the 7200 rpm and cache of at least 8MB. 

For brand, I usually buy Western Digital drives and I like them, but I think Seagate is good as well.

### Post by Zuph on 2007-04-19
Are you planning on buying a USB or Firewire hard drive?  If you buy USB, make sure your laptop and your enclosure are USB 2.0, or it'll boot extremely slowly.

In the end, though, the rest of the speed differences come out in the wash.  Obviously 7200 with 16mb of cache is going to be the fastest, but you probably won't notice much of a difference between that and a 5400 8mb drive.  

There aren't really any bad companies out there for hard drives.  You'll have fans and flamers for all of them.  Personally, I like Maxtor, Western Digital and Seagate.
