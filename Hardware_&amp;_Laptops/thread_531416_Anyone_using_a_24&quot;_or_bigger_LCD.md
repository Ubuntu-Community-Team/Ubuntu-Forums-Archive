### Post by serj_alligator on 2007-08-22
Well, If you go anywhere more then 1280x1024 resolution, I would highly recommend that you go with DVI, because on VGA picture gets doubled and blurry. So make sure your lappy has DVI port and compare your laptop's maximum resolution with the screen's you are going to buy. I think that's all pretty much...
I have a 20" Samsung 204B...good pixel pitch and it's 4:3 which I like because it's easier to configure 4:3 aspect ratio screens in linux...

### Post by johnny4north on 2007-08-22
I just bought a new videocard with a DVI connector, for my Linux box.  its outputting @ 1360x768 and the 26"Sharp HDTV native resolution is 1360x768.  the picture is super sharp and clean.  i would also recommend a LCD screen w/ DVI and the regular vga plug-in as well.  you would be able to plug in two computers to one monitor.   I would recommend a fast 8ms or better for high-performance gaming and multimedia applications. i cheated with the install of my nvidia   videocard, i used Envy to install my drivers. the native drivers(and Envy installs native drivers) from nvidia will put in the tools make it easer to configure the Lcd monitor, esp if you use DVI connectors.  when if first boot up the computer it was at 480x640.  i installed the latest native drivers w/ Envy then rebooted it and i went into the Nvidia settings.  i choose the correct settings for my 26" sharp, then clicked quit. easy :)  if you have have the native drivers or the restricted drivers, you may already be set. :)  LG is a solid company.  congratulations on upgrading to LCD monitor :).
