### Post by matthew on 2006-12-22
I've changed the hard drive on my laptop twice*...it's not terribly difficult once you get over the fear of opening the case. Look for a place where the hard drive fits in, a hatch of some kind attached with screws. Once you remove the hatch the hard drive will pull out somehow. Take some time to look at it and study it as every laptop is a little different.

As far as I know pretty much any laptop hard drive can be used, just get the right size, both physically and in storage capacity for what you need, and it should just slide in there. Then you can boot with the live cd/installer and you will be able to reinstall Ubuntu right away if you want or restore from your backup.

EDIT *I tend to be kind of hard on stuff sometimes. My drives get as much use in 6 months as some people's do in 2 years.
