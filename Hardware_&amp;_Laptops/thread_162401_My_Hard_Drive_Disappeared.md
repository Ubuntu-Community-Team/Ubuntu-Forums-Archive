### Post by yager on 2006-04-18
Im not an expert but it sounds like either your BIOS lost its information or you have a faulty drive controller.  If all of the BIOS information is correct including the clock but all of your IDE devices are missing, you may have a failed IDE controller.
Since your motherboard has onboard IDE control according the info I found on the net, you may be SOL.

If the BIOS information is not correct, you need to replace the battery.

I ran across this link while researching your board.  Some of the symptoms are similar and pointed to a bad power supply.  May or may not help.
