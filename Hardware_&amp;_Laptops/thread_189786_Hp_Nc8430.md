### Post by jml on 2006-06-05
The hp laptops that hp markets to business are rather Linux friendly.  I don't have experience with the laptop you mention, but I have used the hp nx6110, a very basic laptop (for a very basic price of $499 for their promotional unit)  You won't be playing Planet Penguin Racer with it, but its a good basic unit.  The only problem I have is it, and many of hp's laptops come with a wireless card that uses the Broadcom chip set.  Getting it to work in Linux requires NDISWRAPPER and the Windows drivers for the card.  I ordered mine without a wireless card and am using a NetGear WG511T.  Its recognized by Ubuntu out of the box.  The only problem is that Ubuntu does not support WPA encryption out of the box.  So if you need that, you will need to do a bit more work.  

Some of hp's laptops can even be ordered without Windows, they come loaded with FreeDOS, an MS-DOS clone that can be easily deleted.  So, no Windows tax.  You can configure some of their laptops to use other wireless cards, (Intel for example)  

As a general rule, its a good idea to not buy a laptop with the latest and greatest hardware.  It usually takes Linux developers a few months to catch up.  Although I have read on this forum that Ubuntu has been able to work with the new dual core processors, so your milage may vary.  Here is a link to a siter discussing Linux installations on various laptops.  Hope it helps.

[http://www.linux-laptop.net/](http://www.linux-laptop.net/)

Joe
