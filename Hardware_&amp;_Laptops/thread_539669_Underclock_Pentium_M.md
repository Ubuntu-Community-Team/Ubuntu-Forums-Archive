### Post by Milk &amp; Toast &amp; Honey on 2007-08-31
Hi, I've faced this problem with Pentium M, the temperature goes to 90C, one thing I done is, open the notebook's case, pull out the CPU's heat sink (fan), and clean out the dusts it has, and everything back to normal :) .

Just google on how you can open your specific notebook case.

Or, if you have something like vacuum cleaner, you can try to vacuum the dust from fan through the air duct.
