### Post by Palmyra on 2007-11-01
If Ubuntu does not work for you, it doesn't kill you to either try another distribution, or even Windows.  There is nothing wrong with whatever operating system selection you make, and you shouldn't feel obliged to use one or the other.  

If you have the time, and you feel you want to take on a challenge, feel free to stick around and contribute by providing information on what failed, how it failed, and if possible, solutions.  If this is impossible, I am afraid Ubuntu is not what you are looking for.  Finding solutions requires input from everyone, and your help is certainly appreciated, but if you do not have the will, nothing can be done.

Make sure you visit launchpad.net to report bugs you find. 

I don't use Ubuntu because I feel it is necessarily a better operating system, but because I believe in the philosophy.  It just so happens that I am more comfortable in a Ubuntu environment than I am in a Windows environment, but this does not mean that one is better than the other.
