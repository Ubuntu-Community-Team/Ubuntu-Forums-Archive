### Post by fritz_monroe on 2006-06-18
I'm using Ubuntu on my Sony Vaio VGN-FS742/W.  I use the laptop for school and the "normal" home user type of stuff.  Pretty much everything was set up automatically for me when I installed.  I didn't have any hardware that was not detected.

That said, since I've been playing around on this laptop, I've found that the various models, even within the FS series, the hardware may or not be the same.  For instance, some of the FS models have hardware support for the acpi drivers, while others do not.

I've had this laptop for less than a year, and when I was shopping for it, I made sure that every model I looked at had the Intel wireless.  I didn't want to have to mess around with NDIS wrappers.  Hope my input helps a little.

### Post by Rumo on 2006-06-19
I am very pleased with my Samsung X20 XVM V (Intel Pentium M 1.6 GHz (new models: 1.73 GHZ), 80 GB harddisk, 1400x1050 15" TFT, Intel i915 graphics). Wifi, 3D acceleration and even the SD card reader work out-of-the-box. You need to install 915resolution for using the native resolution, tough. 

The notebook is very quiet and only weighs 2.5 kg. The price is very reasonable (around 1200 Euro).

The only real drawback is that Samsung isn't distributed in the US.
