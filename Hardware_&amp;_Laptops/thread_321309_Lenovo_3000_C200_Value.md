### Post by Monstroxus on 2006-12-19
I have ubuntu only on a new Lenovo 3000 C200 laptop. Everything works (installation no problems, screen no problem, but...

I had to fiddle around to make wireless broadband work. Install a special driver and stuff - go to this link for instructions: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=185174](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=185174)

I tried everything with other possible solutions but this one worked!

Secondly, the sound doesn't work (the sound buttons on the laptop do work though, but there is no sound) However, I "fixed" this problem by simply buying a nice set of USB powered (SOUND through USB and POWER through ONE USB plug - so TWO IN ONE - POWER AND SOUND USB SPEAKERS)

Like these: [http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/pr...ONTENTID=10908](http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/pr...ONTENTID=10908)

(you can also find cheaper brands though, mine was 15 EURO) These speakers have an internal mini soundcard which will be recognized by Ubuntu and automatically configured - simple stated it works! Basically you just don't make use of the laptop's internal soundcard, but the build in soundcard in the speakers!) So laptop speaker sound = NO, external USB speaker sound = a simple YES. If you consider that the sound through the laptop's build in speaker is shitty, you can consider buying a nice set of USB speaker and go for this option. That's what I did.

The Lenovo 3000 C200 is build like a brick, something that I like, its very durable and inexpensive (it looks like a Soviet laptop or something)

Another sound "solution" which I applied is to buy a USB sound plug, that is a little USB plug which you will have to plug in one of the USB ports (of course), now you can just plug in any male conventional audio plug for a regular set of external laptop speakers. This USB plug will also solve any microphone in problems, because it has this option too. Just plug in your microphone plug in this USB plug. (that's a lot of plugging, lol)

I like this laptop! Strong, durable and no nonsense.

### Post by jml on 2006-12-20
The ThinkPad series of Laptops were the ones originally made by IBM and have a reputation for being very rugged and Linux friendly.  I'm still using a three year old R40 without any problems.  The other line has a more spotty record with Linux.  I would worry about tje laptop that does not specify what type of wireless chipset is being used.  It may be a Broadcome chipset which is a real pain to set up in Linux.  You might want to consider checking out System 76, they sell computers with Ubuntu installed and they make sure everything works.  I plan to get one from them soon, (no I don't work for them.)  They also maintain a subforum here.  Just a thought.
