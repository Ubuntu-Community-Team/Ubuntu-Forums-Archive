### Post by StarsAndBars14 on 2006-10-15
If you haven't installed the drivers right, Mesa will still show up in your OpenGL section under glxinfo.  I don't use a newer ATI card so I can't help you out all that much but you can try to uninstall linux-restricted-modules, uninstall the ATI drivers and reinstall them.  

If that fails, try commenting out GLX or DRI separately.  Having them both loaded at once has been an issue for some people.

Just at a glance, and this isn't really going to fix your problem but it'll be good to have in case you decide to enable transparency or composite effects - put ```
AccelMethod "XAA"
``` under your Device section and below "driver"
