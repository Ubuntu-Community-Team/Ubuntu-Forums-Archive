### Post by russell.h on 2007-04-10
If it has no CD drive and no floppy drive (I remember those things) how the hell are you supposed to install anything at all on it? They sure as hell couldn't expect people to download stuff in 1998 on their 56k modems. And what happens when your windows gets messed up?

But yeah, I would say a USB CD drive would be your best bet, followed by swapping the hard drive. Or if you have a big enough USB flash drive you might search for one of the various tutorials for making a live "CD" (except not) on a USB drive. First make sure you will be able to boot your laptop off of it though.

### Post by jjordan on 2007-04-12
I had several IBM laptops in the late 90's and ran Linux (RH I believe) on them.  You should be able to install from a USB CD drive without difficulty.  The install will be slow becuase you have a USB 1.0 or possibly 1.1 connection.  After you get it in installed you may be surprised at how well it performs.  A lightweight Window Manager would be a good idea.  You might try Xubuntu to start with.

Are you sure there is not an ethernet port?  That would open up another possibility.
