### Post by oldsmobile_mike on 2008-03-22
Hi!

I run Ubuntu 7.10 on a Sager 5600P, which is just a re-badged Clevo laptop.  Clevo also manufactures laptops for other companies including, off the top of my head, ProStar, Kapok, and Alienware.  Many people have never heard of Clevo, but have certainly heard of Alienware, and sometimes you'll find more help by looking for the equiv. model from these other companies.

As far as experience, I'd say it's been a mixed bag, but only because of g'damn compiz not working right with my built-in ATi 7500M video, and causing the laptop to freeze up at random times, usually right after I've written a long post, but right before I hit the "submit" button, haha.  :confused:

Other than that, it works great.  Ran the liveCD installation and it automatically detected all of my hardware including the video, sound, USB ports, firewire, etc.  I added a miniPCI wifi card, and it detected that (as soon as I bought a card that was properly supported by Ubuntu, anyway), I also upgraded the processor and ram since installing Ubuntu, and it detected those upgrades without any trouble.  It's been very fast and performed well in general, and I'd definitely say "go for it", but watch out for whatever type of video card comes with that model, and if it's affected by any compiz video issues (just google "compiz freezes" and see the 46,000 entries for it), if you plan on using the fancy compiz effects.

Good luck, I hope you enjoy the laptop - it's most certainly going to be better than anything you'd get from Dell, HP, etc.  :)  Alternatively, you could look at System76 - they have great laptops, too, and come pre-installed with Ubuntu and offer a lot of support for it.
