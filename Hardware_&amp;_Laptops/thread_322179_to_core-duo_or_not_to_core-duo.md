### Post by mcduck on 2006-12-20
Let's just say that performance-wise you shouldn't even mention Celeron and Core-CPU's on the same day ;)

Core is currently the fastes CPU architecture available, and even cheapest single-core 32-bit Core CPU's outperform many high-performance CPUs with price tags over 1000$ last year :)

Celeron on the other hand is very low-performance CPU, I wouldn't recommend them to anyone, even less when the price difference for older Core Solos and Core Duos is so small.

Thank you! Intel made an extrememly poor choice in naming when they called it the "Celeron M".  

Unlike the Desktop Celeron chip, the Celeron M is merely a Pentium M with less cache and no speedstep, just as girichv said.  And guess what, the Core-series of chips are based on the Pentium M architecture.  So the Celeron M and the Core Duo are in the same family.

Anyways, it depends what you're using your laptop for.  A Core Duo will far out perform the Celeron M, and you will likely get much battery life.  The Celeron M is as good as retired, as it and the Pentium M have been replaced by the Core chips.    

Of course, if all you're using your laptop for is chatting online and checking email, a Celeron M will do you wonders... mine has.
