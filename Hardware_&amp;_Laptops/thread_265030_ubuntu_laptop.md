### Post by kondor on 2006-09-27
Finding specs on the HP site is fairly simple. Also, the small & medium business area is a good place to look for a notebook as some of them are certified for certain Linux "flavors". Note also that the warranty for those items is generally higher. Also, they have many refurb laptops in their refurb store at great price reduction that offer a 1 year warranty, just as the new home retail machines.

I found the dv8000t specs with two clicks and typing in that model. So, it is just you.

My personal opinion but buying a new MacBook or MacBook Pro simply to run Linux is money thrown away. They are typically priced higher than a PC and many times offer outdated components along with their proprietary MBs. That Mac OS10??? is a favorite of Linux folk is due to its Unix system. Wouldn't make much sense to buy a notebook that was more expensive than most, then rip out the Unix operating system to install a first cousin. Darwin has allowed Mac folk to NOT replace the system with Linux but simply to port anything they wanted from Linux or use the Linux program directly.

Good luck on your search.

### Post by phetre on 2006-10-16
Hi Rhubarb and Kondor,

In defense of the Macbook crowd, Apple has some unbelievable educational promotions.  I got a brand-new 13" Macbook with a 2GB iPod Nano for $1050, after a rebate.  Since I already have a great mp3 player, I sold the Nano to a friend, but had I kept it, I would have loaded Rockbox in order to use .ogg and other free formats.

If you find a good deal on a Macbook, I'd recommend grabbing one.  Three months ago, I would have agreed with the previous posters that they're a waste of money, but the build quality and battery life are noticeably better than anything I saw in a comparably-priced PC.

I use Linux for all my regular work, and the hardware is supported about as well as anything else people have mentioned in this thread.  However, the power-management features of OSX make it my environment of choice when running on battery (about 6 hours with normal use).  I prefer OSX to Windows as a secondary operating system, but as you know, these Intel Macs run Windows too, so you can triple-boot if you wish.

In summary, I bought it about two months ago and haven't regretted it for a second.  Absolutely recommended if you can get a good price.
