### Post by haakon on 2006-07-12
I've had my Lenovo 3000 N100 laptop (core duo, 1GB ram, 100GB disk edition) for some weeks now, and there is one very annoying problem it seems I may be alone with, which makes me worry there is a hardware problem. I would like some feedback from owners of similar laptops.

There is a known problem that when you plug earphones into the earphone plug on the side, the internal laptop speakers do not mute. Other forum users have solved this problem by just switching off those speakers in their mixer programs. I try this by opening alsamixer, going to "External", and pressing m for mute. This does mute the laptop speakers, but it also heavily distorts the sound going to the earphones. It becomes softer and seems bursted and thin -- somewhat like a really low-bitrate MP3 stream. If I enable the internal speakers the earphone sound is great -- but then I have to endure the laptop speakers too.

I have only tried this on my (K)Ubuntu Dapper system, since I have removed the Windows installation that came on it. I've tried using KMix instead of alsamixer with the same result, and I've tried most tweaks and combinations of the other mixer settings with no fix.

Are owners of similar Lenovo laptops seeing the same, or should I send it in for service or whatever?

Thanks!
