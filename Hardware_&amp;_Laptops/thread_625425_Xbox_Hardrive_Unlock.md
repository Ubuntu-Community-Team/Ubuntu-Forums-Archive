### Post by x64Jimbo on 2007-12-01
> **thorwood said:**
> 
and to regards to the first reply:
 1. Its a free hard drive
 2. Learning experience
 3. Why waste a drive that I could Linux?
You're certainly free to do what you want with your equipment, but here are my reasons...
1. The fact that it's free doesn't make it good. IMO, it's too small to be worth any money. 
2. The only reason you'd ever need to unlock an Xbox hard disk would be to use it in your computer, and if all Xbox hard disks are created equal, refer to point # 1.
3. Every piece of electronics has its useful days, and then it must be retired. I used to try and horde all of my old electronics because I thought, "Maybe I will find a use for it." The bottom line is that you have to make a choice and determine how valuable your time is. If you're OK with blowing several hours on finding the solution to this problem so that you may have a used 5-10 GB IDE HDD in your system, then that's what you should do. My course of action would be to go out and get a new SATA hard disk and trash the old one. As file sizes have gotten bigger, hard disks have grown bigger and faster to keep up. Why put your files/OS on a slow hard disk with extremely limited space? 
Lastly, the older a hard disk is, the closer it is to having a crash. Like a human being's lifespan, every hard disk has a lifespan, at the end of which lies disk failure, which can result in data loss. Your Xbox hard disk is at very minimum 2 years down the curve, which means that, statistically speaking, you have 2 years less time to enjoy the use of that drive before you have to start worrying about S.M.A.R.T. errors and "imminent failure."
I'm just trying to pass on my experience to you. I hope whatever solution you decide upon works well for you.
