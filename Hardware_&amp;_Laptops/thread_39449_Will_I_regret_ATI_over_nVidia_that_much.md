### Post by oritpro on 2005-06-05
Nvidia has a larger development team commited to Linux driver development according to an article I read, and historically, they have always had better driver support for Linux than ATI. This from experience.

I own five systems, two of which have ATI 9800 Pro's, and I've had no sucess getting OpenGL support to work right on either of them.  Since my kids use these boxes, it would be a great incentive for them if they could play games under Linux.  Without that, they run Windows more often than I'd like.

The rest of my systems have Nvidia and they work fine. So I'll be Ebaying the 9800 Pro's here shortly to replace them with Nvidia 6600GT's.

The thing to keep in mind if you are planning to stick with Linux for the long haul is that at the moment, Nvidia has better support for Linux and there are other applications out there other than games that use OpenGL.
