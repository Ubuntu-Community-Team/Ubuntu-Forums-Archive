### Post by weird_c00kie on 2007-07-23
I'm in the market for a new laptop, since mom has somehow managed to ravage my old one, and I thought to myself, "why not get one that runs Ubuntu?"
So I went off and had a look at the System76 offerings ([www.system76.com](www.system76.com)) and was happily filling out the order for for a Pangolin when I realised that they only ship to the US and Canada. Completely disheartened, I wrote an email to them, whining and asking if there was any way I could get my hands on one of their laptops. That was earlier today, so I'm still waiting for their response.
Having failed at acquiring a system76 machine, I went over to Dell's website, since I heard that they too now offer systems that come with Ubuntu pre-installed. I searched in vain on the Australian site but found nothing related to Ubuntu, so I put "ubuntu" in the site searchbox and hit enter. There it was! The Dell Inspiron 1420, which looked pretty good for a reasonable price. Once again, I did all my customisations and proceeded to the checkout, only to discover that they too only ship to the US and Canada!

Throroughly annoyed at having wasted more time, I called the Australian branch of Dell and asked them about it. After being put on hold 3 times and talking to 3 different people, explaining myself from the start each time, I eventually found out that they're not available in Australia in any way, shape or form.

Has anyone else from a country other than Canada and the US had any luck with getting an ubuntu laptop? Who did you get yours from? I guess my only other option is to try and build an identical one from the Australian Dell site and waste money on a pre-installed version of Vista, hoping that it will be 100% compatible with Ubuntu once I install it... :(
