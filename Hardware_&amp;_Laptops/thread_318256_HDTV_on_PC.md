### Post by Fully216 on 2006-12-13
This is possible.  You have to buy a card that has video output.  Most capture cards do not provide this feature, hence the name capture card.  This will tend to up the price of the card as well as (in most cases) having to set the channel you watch with the computer instead of cable box.

I went the cheap route and split the video input, one going to the TV and one to the computer.  This works pretty well without amplification, and also means I do not have to record what is being watched on TV because of the separation.

On a side note, I would recommend a card that does the video encoding in hardware (usually mpeg2, cutting edge in mpeg4).  This will lessen the computer burden dramatically so you may watch shows and record (possible multiple) channels at the same time.  This is the one feature I wish I had in my card (winfast Tv expert).
