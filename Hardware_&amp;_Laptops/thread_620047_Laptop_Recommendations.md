### Post by gobbles414 on 2007-11-22
Hi mntlasasin,

Well of course, Dell is now selling an Ubuntu laptop (Inspiron 1420N). I personally don't like the specs on the machine -- its Windows cousin comes with more hardware features, still loaded with Ubuntu 7.04, etc. -- but at least one of the major manufactures it trying to support consumer versions of Linux. I tried for more than an hour the other day to convince Dell to sell me a different laptop without Windows. I also offered them the alternative of giving me a refund for the Windows license after purchase. Despite the fact that Dell Germany is on record as having refunded licenses for Microsoft software in the past, Dell USA doesn't seem open to that at all. On the plus side, at least Dell will send you a REAL Windows operating system disk if you request one (versus system restore disks with pre-installed crapware). So in theory, you could install a dual-boot or run Windows from a virtual machine. I believe that only certain versions of Vista are licensed for virtualization.

EDIT: I just read about a new Dell laptop, the [ Inspiron 1525/1526]("http://laptoping.com/dell-inspiron-1525-1526.html"). Seems VERY promising...

I personally own an Asus G1 Laptop (pre Santa Rosa). Unfortunately, Asus has also proven unwilling to refund Windows. I purchased mine a Best Buy, but built-to-order (BTO) and barebones websites will often sell Asus computers without an OS. I doubt that Asus would send me a real OS disk if I requested one. And Asus support sucks... I reported an overheating power adapter and Asus blamed my Linux installation! Oh well, if my power adapter catches fire, at least I will know who to blame! With the exception of the power adapter, the laptop itself seems to be VERY well constructed. The built-in webcam worked for awhile in Ubuntu, but the driver I installed for it must have gotten corrupted by another program at some point, because now the webcam doesn't work. 

I believe that Ubuntu runs well on the new Mac laptops. If you're willing to pay the premium for Apple hardware, and excuse the fact that Apple is the new Microsoft, a MacBook or MacBook Pro might be the way to go. MSI, Compal, and Asus are all major laptop manufacturers in the BTO/barebones market EDIT: that you should consider.

Make sure any Intel-based laptop you purchase is a Santa Rosa. Otherwise, you will be purchasing an obsolete system. And finally, try to get a laptop that is compatible with DirectX 10 -- just in case you decide to switch back to Vista at some point.

Best Regards,
gobbles414
