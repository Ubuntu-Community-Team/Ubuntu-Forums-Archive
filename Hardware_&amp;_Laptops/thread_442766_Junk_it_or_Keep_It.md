### Post by kyphi on 2007-05-13
A computer you got in 2000 means that it is 7-year-old technology.  If you were to upgrade, you would have to put in a new motherboard and that also means a new processor and new RAM and a graphics card and so on.

I would suggest to you not to upgrade but to save your money so that you can buy a new machine when you can afford it.  Technology changes very rapidly and a 7-year-old computer is well past its use-by-date.

There is also no need to go for the very latest and greatest because that will cost far too much.

You are lucky to have had good service from your old machine so far.

Some manufacturers sell 'bare bones' computer systems where you decide on what components you want.

Alternatively, you could try to build your own but if it does not work you cannot get it repaired.

Good Luck.
