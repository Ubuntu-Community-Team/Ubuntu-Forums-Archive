### Post by luca_linux on 2005-07-18
When you are in front of the Grub menu at boot, press "e".
Then add that parameter next to the others in the line related to the kernel you're running.
Then press "b" to boot.
This won't be saved. If it does work, you'll have to do the following to save it:
```
sudo gedit /boot/grub/menu.lst
```
Then add the parameter into the right line.
