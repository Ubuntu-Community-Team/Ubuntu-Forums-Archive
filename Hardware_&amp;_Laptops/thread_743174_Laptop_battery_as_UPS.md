### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-04-02
Yes, you are correct, constant charging can degrade a battery, but most lithium-ion batteries have "smart" charging so that only a trickle charge is used to keep the battery fully, but not over-charged.  

Lithium-ion batteries have a time-limited life (3-4 years) and a charge-cycle life (200-400 full recharge cycles).  Normally after 3-4 years you will hit that cycle count and the battery will expire quickly at that point.

On a G4 powerbook, I rotate between 2 batteries.  Every 3 months, I change the batteries.  This seems to extend the life somewhat.  My current battery is at 76% and 248 cycles after 3 years.  It's one of those exploding batteries, so I will have to turn it in soon for a new one.

I don't understand your question.  You should leave the battery plugged in all the time.  The wear and tear on the contacts would be a greater risk than battery aging.

Gnome 2.22 has some better battery history in the power management settings panel.  You can use this to keep track of the run time.  When your battery drops below 70% of original capacity, it will drop off quickly.  That is time to shop for a replacement.

Also, if one of the cells goes bad, the "smart" circuitry will notify you that the battery is bad.  Sort of like an ink cartridge telling you it's out of ink.

It's good practice to use the battery (draw it down near-completely) once a month.  This will also recalibrate the battery runtime algorithm.
