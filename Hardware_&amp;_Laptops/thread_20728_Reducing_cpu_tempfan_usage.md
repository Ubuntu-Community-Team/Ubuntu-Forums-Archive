### Post by carpman on 2005-03-18
Hello, ok thought i would share my experiences with cpu temp and fan noise on notebook.

I have a Parkard Bell EasyNote C3 with XP-M 2500+, the fan was always coming on and is not the quietest fan by long shot.

The intake fan is underneath and i notice that it had removable plastic grill with tiny holes sitting under main intake hole and grill. Now in the past with desktop boxs i have always cut away the inbuilt fan grill on boxs and replaced with screw on crome grill, this because the standard case grills cause a lot noise as air travel through them.

So with this in mind i begain to take notebook apart, i soon got to the point where i could remove the plastic grill with small holes intending to glue fine filter on outside to prevent dust intake.

To get to this point i had to dismatle most of computer and so exposed the cpu and heatsink, umm on desktops i always lap in heatsinks and coat with artic silver compound i thought i would have a look this heatsink.

I removed it and found that lapping was not going to be possible but did see small dark patch on both heatsink and cpu top, not a burn more a blemish.

I cleaned it up as best a possible and gave it artic silver treatment and rebuilt system.

Now the fan is little quieter not much but all helps, but the artic silver treatment seems to have improved heat trasfer as fan does not come on as much, though still comes on a bit to much for my liking even with cpu running at 457mhz.


So if comfortable dismantling notebook it may worth checking and treating cpu/heatsink.


Off to google a quieter fan :)
