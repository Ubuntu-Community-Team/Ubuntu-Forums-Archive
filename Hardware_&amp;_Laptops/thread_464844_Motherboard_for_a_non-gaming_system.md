### Post by steevc on 2007-06-05
I'm looking at building a new PC. The time has come to move to dual-core. I don't need screaming games performance, but I would like improved performance for things like video/audio transcoding. The PC will be used for general internet, audio recording, video editing and some simple games (e.g. Tux Racer).

I've been looking at the available motherboards, but am just getting confused as to what will work best. I don't really mind if the CPU is Intel or AMD. I care more about power efficiency and quietness.

My criteria are:

[LIST]
[*]Firewire for my video camera. I have a PCI card, but would prefer not to have to use a slot
[*]Plenty of USB ports. I'll assume they will be USB2.0 these days.
[*]A spare PCI slot after using two for my TV card and a decent sound card (multiple inputs). This would seem to exclude Micro ATX, but I'm probably going for a larger case anyway
[*]PATA. I want to use my existing drives, but I will probably buy SATA in future
[*]Reasonable on-board graphics. Preferably something that can use open drivers with good enough 3d for simple games and desktop effects. Dual-head would be nice, but not essential. I would consider using a basic graphics card if on-board is not available, supported or underpowered
[*]PCI-e for future expansion. I probably don't need SLI
[*]Passive motherboard cooling to minimise the fans
[/LIST]

Any thoughts? Is there a site that has recent sample systems designed with Linux in mind?
