### Post by Natume on 2007-02-02
My wacom tablet's been working fairly well since modifying xorg and whatnot to work with it.  Most often, I use the mouse that comes with it, which is set to 'relative' mode, instead of 'absolute'.

The one issue that I have is that the sensitivity is quite high, even when the mouse is put on the absolute lowest sensitivity setting.  About 80% of the time, after moving the cursor, it will 'wobble' in its new position, so to speak, even though my hand is quite clearly off the mouse, the mouse thus not moving.  It should be noted that I didn't have this issue in Windows XP (er, don't have the issue ; running a dual boot), so it's not a problem with the tracking mechanism, I'm pretty sure.

At most, it's a little annoying, especially for applications when I need a little precision (or simply when there's small font involved, as is selecting).  The cursor will jiggle several pixels in place on the screen (as much as 10 or so), and will occasionally move to the side (in a straight line) for as many as 20 or so pixels, over time.  The movement is jerky, and doesn't seem to stop for several minutes, until it's 'settled' itself, so to speak.

If this is a known issue, or if anybody has suggestions about modifying the sensitivity to an even finer degree than through the mouse preferences, I'd be much obliged.
