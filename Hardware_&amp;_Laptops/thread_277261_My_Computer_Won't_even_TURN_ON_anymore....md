### Post by xmastree on 2006-10-14
There's an easy way to test an ATX power supply. First, unplug it from everything in the computer (the motherboard and drives). Then look at the large plug you just took from the motherboard, in the middle is a green wire amongst the black ones.

It's called PS_ON here:

[img]http://www.xbitlabs.com/images/other/atx-psu/p1.gif[/img]

Using a short piece of wire, connect that to any of the black ones around it (they're all 0V) and the power supply fan should start spinning.

If it does, then the PSU is probably ok and the fault lies elsewhere.

### Post by tommcd on 2006-10-15
Does the computer make any beeps at all? If the fans won't even spin it does sound like the power supply. Also, check all connections to the motherboard (with computer unplugged of course). In particular, check that the power cable from the CPU heatsink-fan is plugged in. Also check the wires from the front of the case are not loose where they connect to the lower-right section of the motherboard.
