### Post by krusbjorn on 2006-04-05
7 years, thats quite some time. If we stick to Moore's law, the high end computers would have CPUs of about 16-20ghz by that time. I guess that really doesnt say much about what computer power you would need to run the operating systems though. 

If you want to minimize the amount of money to spend, you are probably better off buying some low end second hand stuff now, and repeating that procedure in 3-4 years. Otherwise, I guess you will need to spend a considerable amount of money right away.

I guess there will always be linux distros that dont require too much power from your computer, but who knows if people will still use DVDs in 7 years? You will probably need to get better equipment in a few years anyways, because the size of video files, download speeds and CPU power required to play the videos will fill up your hard drives and make your CPU barf, even if it is one that is pretty powerful with todays standards.
