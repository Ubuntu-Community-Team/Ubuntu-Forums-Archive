i can tell you for sure that lexar makes what they call a jump drive which is like a usb flash stick but a flap opens and you can insert both sd cards, mmc cards (multi-media) as well as Sony MS sticks but not compact flash sorry. SInce they are so different in width I am guessing you're gonna have to find a rather large one. i love this jump drive cause it gets both power and usb data connection thru usb port. like I said it's just like a usb flash drive but a little wider. check it out: [http://www.lexar.com/readers/trio.html](http://www.lexar.com/readers/trio.html)

i have a 2gb sd card by kingston that I use it with. just so you know also, both win and linux can always read a usb stick, it all depends on what you format the filesystem. so even if you bought a sd card that said it was win only, it's most likely fat32 and linux will read it and even if it couldn't, you could always reformat it to ext3 and install a driver in windows (driver is free) to make it so your windows computer could read and write to ext3 I believe. good luck.
