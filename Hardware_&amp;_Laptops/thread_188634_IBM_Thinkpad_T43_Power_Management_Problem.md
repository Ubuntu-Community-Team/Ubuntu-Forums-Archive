### Post by Nathaniel on 2006-06-04
I have a standard IBM Thinkpad T43 with Intel graphics and chipset, Pentium M 1,6Ghz and 256+1024 MB RAM which is running a freshly installed Kubuntu Dapper.

Now, the standard klaptop power management daemon works just fine. It reads my battery, it supports suspending to RAM and it shows my battery status somewhat accurately. The FN+F4 (suspend2ram) shortcut however did not work.

I tried the new PowerSave daemon yesterday, and it seemed to be a great improvement to the old klaptop, but some things were a little odd.

None of the suspend modes would work, but the FN+F4 shortcut was suddenly active again. It seemed to show battery status more accurately and it certainly was snappier att applying different schemes and profiles (klaptop wouldn't change from powersaving to performance profile, no matter how much I begged).

Something which is a little odd in both programs, is that sometimes the battery monitor "stalls" at 9x% and won't move. It correctly displays if I'm running on battery or off AC, but it never reaches 100%. When I unplug my laptop, the percentage slowly decreases and when I plug it back in, it increases. But it almost always stalls at somewhere above 95%.

It seems both programs complement each other perfectly. Too bad I can't have the best of both. In the meantime, is there anything else I should try tweaking on my Thinkpad to make it work better?
