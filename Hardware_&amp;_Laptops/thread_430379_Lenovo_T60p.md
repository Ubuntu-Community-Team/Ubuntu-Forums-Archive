### Post by gradedcheese on 2007-05-02
MP3 and DVD, etc:

1) add the PLF repos to /etc/apt/sources.list

```

deb http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/repo/ edgy free
deb http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/repo/ edgy non-free
deb-src http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/repo/ edgy free
deb-src http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/repo/ edgy non-free

```

2) sudo apt-get update

3) install things:
gstreamer0.10-fluendo-mp3
gstreamer0.10-fluendo-mpegdemux
gstreamer0.10-plugins-bad
gstreamer0.10-plugins-bad-multiverse
gstreamer0.10-plugins-ugly
gstreamer0.10-plugins-ugly-multiverse
libdvdcss2
libdvdnav4
libdvdread3
libdvdplay0

4) grab the w32codecs if you want them too

5) you should now be able to play and encode MP3s, watch DVDs (I only used totem for that), etc

as for beryl, I think you have to switch to XGL.  Read ubuntuguide.org for instructions specific to ATI cards, I think what they describe will work.  I tried it and didn't really like it, but it's worth a try I guess.

### Post by pmon_killer on 2007-05-29
You were on the right track when you suspected that your Device identifier was set incorrectly. I just recently switched from a T43 to a T60p with the V5200 ATI card. It took me a week of fighting with both the open source and restricted drivers. I even tried the proprietary drivers without much luck. I finally figured out that I needed to change my identifier to be "ATI MOBILITY FireGL V5200 (M56 71C4)" for the direct rendering to work. There is a great page [http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Feisty_Installation_Guide]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Feisty_Installation_Guide") that shows you all the steps you need to do to enable the proprietary drivers. I had to switch back to the VESA driver to successfully remove all traces of the restricted and open source drivers before I could get the proprietary drivers installed and working successfully.
