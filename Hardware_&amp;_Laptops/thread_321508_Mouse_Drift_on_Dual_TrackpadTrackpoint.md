### Post by duffrecords on 2007-08-21
I removed the keyboard and had a look inside.  On the Inspiron 8200 the trackpoint is connected by a blue 4-conductor ribbon cable, which joins together with a larger ribbon cable in a connector.  I opened the connector, removed the blue cable, and put it back together.  No more mouse drift.  And no irreversible cutting of wires involved.  The trackpoint is now disabled but its associated left/right buttons still work.  That's ok with me because I prefer the touchpad anyway.

### Post by Oderik on 2007-11-05
I have a Dell Inspiron 4100 running as a Latitude C610 and I am having this issue for a long time now.
This thread solved the problem for me. Thank you all! I would like to summarize the information most important imho:

The weird mouse behaviour is generated by the trackpoint. It occurs due to "bending" the case of the notebook.
The trackpoint can be configured separately to the other pointing devices. I.e. can it be separately disabled by software, preventing the problem.
The solution described by Thundar_2001 worked fine for me on Gutsy Gibbon.



By the way: I can confirm that there is also a windows driver that is capable of disabling the trackpoint separately to the touchpad like mentioned above.
