Greetings unto Ubuntians all,

 After much frustration (days), modprobing, lspci -nn, and other strange textual incantations upon a dual boot system running, (gibbon, win2k) I stumbled upon a simple solution. It did not involve muddling with wake-on-lan settings on the windows side or installing windows drivers on the linux side. It was far simpler. I simpler moved the card on pci slot over and left the funny dangling cable that is supposed to be socketed into the small four prong widget on the motherboard UNPLUGGED! And astonishingly the internet was suddenly present under both systems. Even after repeated bootings to and fro it now works. So in this case was it a bug or hardware incorrectly setup, what does that odd slight little cable actually do other than foul up the internet under Gibbons?
