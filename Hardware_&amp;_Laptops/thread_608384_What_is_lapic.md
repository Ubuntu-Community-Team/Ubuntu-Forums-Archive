### Post by BoneKracker on 2007-11-10
An APIC is an Advanced PIC (Programmable Interrupt Controller).  It's a part of some motherboard chipsets that assists with management of resources (assigning IRQs, handling interrupts).  Useful (in my somewhat uninformed opinion) possibly on complex machines (like multi-processor boxes) and machines making heavy use of ACPI instead of handling resources via the operating system.

Bottom line: I wouldn't bother.

If you want to enabled it, first make sure you've actually got one.  Find and read the documentation for your motherboard and chipset.

You can get an overview from the links provided here:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Programmable_Interrupt_Controller](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Programmable_Interrupt_Controller)
