### Post by slider on 2006-06-27
[QUOTE=greggh] I don't use it for games, but for $60-$70 do you think its worth popping in another Gig?[/QUOTE]

More RAM ==better

Best thing to do is watch your memory usage under a typical load. If you have plenty of overhead with the 1 GB and aren't hitting the swap very often, then more ram won't do much if anything. I'm sure there are more sophisticated ways to do it, but the GUI performance monitor in gnome, will do the job.

I bought another GB when it was cheap. Even if I don't need it very often, it's comforting to know it is there when needed.

### Post by woedend on 2006-06-27
from what I've seen, programs tend to stay in memory until its needed again.  That is, the first time you launch firefox it takes a few seconds.  Each subsequent time it takes maybe .75 seconds or so.  Now, if you were to do intense encoding/compiling/video editing, then come back and launch firefox, it takes a few seconds again.  
It couldn't hurt, but it won't make a world of difference either.
