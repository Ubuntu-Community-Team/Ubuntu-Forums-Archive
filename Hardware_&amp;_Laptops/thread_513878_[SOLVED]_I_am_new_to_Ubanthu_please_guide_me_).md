### Post by ndefontenay on 2007-07-31
Yes.

If you put your ubuntu CD in your computer, you should be able to boot from the CD.

You will have to tell you computer to start from CD at startup (should be F2 or F10 or another shortcut depending of the PC). You should be able to read what button to press at startup. It's fast!

When you boot from CD, Linux is loaded and you can use it as if it's installed...

From that point you will see that your graphic card is supported (you got graphic interface), sound (play a CD) and your modem (go on the internet).

If all this work, you can install it happily.
