### Post by Pollo on 2005-06-05
I just wanted as a first poster to introduce myself as a new Ubuntu user.

I recently installed Ubuntu Hoary Hizzlemeister on a Toshiba Tecra 9100 with a Cisco 350 series wireless card.  I just wanted to give all of those involved with Ubuntu a well deserved thanks for making such a great performing distribution which worked extremely well for this notebook.

It all started when I  was handed this laptop recently at work with Windows knowing that the whole systems administration team including myself  never get to see nor touch a windows server at all so course it was my job to bring that tecra home and not come back to work on monday until a linux distro was up and running.

Anyways as a diehard Red Hat guy  I loaded Fedora Core 3 no issues but this was way too easy plus I was looking for a distribution with  more solid wireless support applications, so I went on a tried Suse, Jesus that was a pain and a half to just to get X to run properly,  so I decided to give Ubuntu a try considering that Debian has a pretty good support of wireless applications for laptops.   I was a bit leary at first because I obviously was used to YUM, YAST, and RPMs, and never had to deal directly with apt-get, synaptic, dpkg.

As a recomendation to everyone, if you are planning to use pcmcia wireless devices I recommend you leave those plugged in while installing it will save you headaches. Luckily I did not suffer any since I've read a few articles on this site and saved me a lot of time and fustration.


Well installation went flawless, all devices were recognized without a hitch, all keys work flawlessly.  I have not yet tested the wireless card but the hardest part  is hardware recognition which is there ( I'll update the wireless situation once I can test). Now the rest I have to get used to well are all OS specific like starting , restarting services,  changing system services upon start up , ect.  I was used to the ntsysv, chkconfig, and other utilities that were available in redhat.

I know this post does not really provide a lot of technical information well because there was nothing too technical about the installation.  If I could hug each and single individual who contributed to Ubuntu and for making such a great distro.  Now the magic question is... When will Ubuntu make it to my home desktop and I say bye bye Fedora.  \\:D/ 

Cheers

Pollo
