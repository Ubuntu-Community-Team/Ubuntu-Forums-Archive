### Post by elcasey on 2006-12-29
I wish I'd seen this post earlier. I'm not going to "Monday morning quarterback" you and say you made a bad purchase, but I would've steered clear of any of those "big name" pre-built PCs.

Best reason to stay away from Dell? Proprietary PSUs and mainboards. I'm an AMD guy from way back but Intel's Core 2 Duo is the game to beat right now. I'll be putting together a Newegg system for myself in the next month or two, so I've been hitting HardForums, Anandtech, etc. pretty hard these days.

Since you did buy an HP and you are going to run Windows (with a 300GBer why on Earth wouldn't you dual-boot?!) the very first thing you ought to do is blow out that hard drive and install a fresh copy of XP without all the bloatware BS that comes with systems like Dells, HPs, Compaqs, eMachines, etc. You'll be slowed to a crawl within a month or two otherwise.

Anyhow, I've never had too bad an experience with a pre-built machine as long as I blew everything out of it immediately and started from scratch. Don't leave us for too long! ;)
