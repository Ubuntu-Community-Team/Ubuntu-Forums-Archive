### Post by Mat10 on 2005-04-23
Hello out there all you folks.  This is my first message out on Ubuntu online.  I installed without Internet options the first time cause I knew my Linksys wireless nic was a dud !  After reading the docs and looking at the update features available online I decided to rob the Cisco 350 card from my Mandrake Box, just to see if Ubuntu was up to the task.  I let Ubuntu reinstall and ticked the Cisco Card it saw, and whala here I am, no choosing this choosing that, it just works without a problem of any kind.  Still got to set up mail accounts and all but looking forward to it.

I checked a few sites for used and new Cisco 350 series cards and saw a lot out there.  Some higher than others but still available.  Good news is the PCI 802.11B works with any Linux distro.  One point to be made about this Cisco Card is the fact that is it first class quality.  It looks like gear that you would find on the space shuttle.
I have the Linksys pci WMP11 and it is nothing in quality as this Cisco Card.  You just get what you pay for I guess.  I am just fortunate enough to have it left over from an older dish system I had to use in Nevada.

Good luck to all, you just have to have the right gear to enjoy Linux.

Tom
