### Post by tripinva on 2007-07-21
I'm a Toshiba fanatic myself, and I love the hardware quality and their support, but check out what I had to go through on my brand new one to put Linux on it:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam/ToshibaSatelliteP200-ST2061](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam/ToshibaSatelliteP200-ST2061)

I'd recommend Toshiba anyway just due to the fact that I've had wonderful experiences with their hardware, but...

Actually, the one you're looking at has older parts and stuff in it, so it may not be as bad as mine was.  You'll still have to do some work with the wireless card, but hopefully the video controller won't be so much of a pain.  Not sure how the sound will be.  But don't let the wireless controller put you off; it's Intel's newest one that supports wireless "N" and they have drivers on their website.

Good News for you!

I have a fujitsu siemens Amilo Pa 1538 - the exact model you specified.
I don't have any troubles running Ubuntu, in fact, I'm running the x64 version now!
Almost everything works out-of-the-box, except:
 - The NVIDIA card driver (you install it easily);
 - The screen brightness buttons;
