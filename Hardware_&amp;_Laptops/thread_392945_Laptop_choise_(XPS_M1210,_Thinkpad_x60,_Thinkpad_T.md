### Post by Alfdog on 2007-03-28
I've owned:

A Sager 4780 - a 17" desktop replacement
A Dell 700m - which is really not a Dell but an Aopen re-badge

I loved the 700m so much more, the small size and weight made it much more "usable".

The 700m was starting to show it's age, so I was contemplating between the black macbook and the m1210 for a while, I ended up going with the m1210.

Here are my thoughts.

1.  Value/Cost - For a black macbook with 2g of ram, I believe I was looking at a minimum of 1600.  I got my m1210 off of the dell refurb site, and I have the thing decked out for less.  Nvidia 7400, 7200 hard drive, SD card reader, 9cell battery, ect.

2.  Aesthetics - Right after I got my m1210, my girlfriend ordered the white basic macbook.  
Honestly the macbook is... Sexy.  With the lid closed the thing is so smooth, it feels great in your hands, the slot load drive, the magnetic lid and power cable, the tiny webcam and hidden speakers are all great.  What I don't like about the macbook are the sharp edge by your palms/wrists, the one button on the touchpad, the way apple uses their ctrl, alt, apple buttons.  Now for what I like about the m1210 after using for a while.  The dedicated page up/ page down buttons, this is small, but it's very convenient.  The multimedia buttons on the front base are handy.  The m1210 also has an external on/off switch for wifi/bluetooth/cellular wifi, and a reversible webcam.  The m1210 looks good also with it's black metalish matte black top, opened with the keyboard showing, I think it looks... plain.  Also the macbook and m1210 both use 1280x800 screens, so even though one screen is bigger, they both have the same amount of "real estate".  As for keyboard feel, I like the macbook aside from the key placements.

3.  OSes - If you are familiar with OSX and that is a priority, get a macbook.  Also after I received my m1210, I found out that OSx86 can be installed.  So currently I'm triple booting Vista, OSX, and Ubuntu 7.04 beta.  After being a window user for years, what can I say about OSX... I really really like it.  But I'm also a hardware junkie, and one that values performance over looks, that was probably my biggest reason for the m1210, performance and value.  As for Ubuntu, 7.04 beta is... wonderful.  Everything except webcam and sleep works without tinkering.

My final reason why... I don't think I could use OSX "full time", I have too many apps I use, and a lot of other windows machines.  I can't handle the apple 1 button touchpad, and their key placements.  I like the macbook's looks, but size, power and battery life mean more to me.

I'm not sure about the IBM's, they make great machines though,  I think that cost was a reason they weren't included in my initial buying choices.  Sony also makes a model that gets thrown into these comparisons, and there are people that really like them too.  Personally, after selling Sony's in retail, I stay away from them.

Good luck with your decision, they're all great machines.  Whatever you choose, you won't be making a bad choice.

I hoped this helped.

alf
