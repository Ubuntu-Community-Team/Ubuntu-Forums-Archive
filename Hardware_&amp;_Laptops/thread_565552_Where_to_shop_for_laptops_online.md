### Post by hardyn on 2007-10-02
if you are going to buy online... determine what the resellers commitment to you is, who pays shipping if it breaks, do you ship to the reseller or the vendor, etc, etc, etc.  (expect that you will have some trouble with it at some point)  

see what is available locally too... even if you don't buy one locally; notebooks are a pretty personal thing with keyboard feel, matte or glossy screen, weight, display size, etc. its very much a tactile shopping experience.  

as for extended warranties, they WILL try to sell you one.  before you buy, read about experiences people have had, think about how long you will actually own the notebook before an upgrade.  in my experience anything that will go wrong with a notebook will go wrong inside the first year, with the exception of a hard disk, which is sort of par-for-the-course with a notebook, they are a little prone to hard disk failure.

levono was IBM before selling off the laptop department, lots of people have been very happy with them on this forum, but i believe its with the higher numbered models... I have seen a few and have not been impressed IMHO.

there is a "which notebook" thread just about every two days on this forum, have a search and see what people have been having luck with, and what problems may arrise (most laptops have something that is not 100% in linux) 

I have a older Asus which works very will with linux, about 95%, but i would probably not buy another based on my past warranty dealings with the vendor.  but please make your own decisions about a laptop.  defiantly listen to peoples experiences, but peoples bias do come out in their reviews, and may not impact your experience (i projected two of my own biases in the above paragraphs)

good luck!
