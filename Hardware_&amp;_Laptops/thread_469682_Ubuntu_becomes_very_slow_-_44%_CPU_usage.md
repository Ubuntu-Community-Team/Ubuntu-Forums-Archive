### Post by jotagab on 2007-06-10
Hi!
I don't know how to solve your problem, but I'm not sure that you're looking at the right place, i.e. the ATI drivers. I think that if something was wrong with that the Xorg process would have a high CPU usage, but from your snapshot the problem doesn't seem software-related, because it's the (h)ardware (i)nterrupts that consume 45% of your CPU.
Have you added some new hardware recently (even an USB key...)?

### Post by ofir_k on 2007-06-10
Like jotagab said, the problem was an hardware problem...

My conexant driver (for my modem) did all this mess, so I simply disabled it from the "Restricted Drivers Manager"!
