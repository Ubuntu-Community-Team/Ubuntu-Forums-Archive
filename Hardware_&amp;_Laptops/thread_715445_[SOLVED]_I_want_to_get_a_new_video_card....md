### Post by jrusso2 on 2008-03-04
The card memory doesn't matter.  What you need to look at is what type of slot you have.  AGP or PCI IE

If its agp you should also know if its an agp 4x or 8x slot.

I would go with Nvidia as this seems to have better support in Linux then ATI

Also what use do you use the computer for?  If you running games you will want a card that works well with the games you run
or if you do intensive graphics or video.

For regular desktop use its not that important, but make sure the card supports the resolutions of the monitor you using.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-03-05
to answer one question .. setting the amount of video ram is for on board chips only.  As mentioned, plug in card carries it's own ram that is run by it's OWN processor.  Machines switching over from on board to card video will run much faster as it frees up the ram and a shared portion of the on board processor for other things.
That is the reason that you NEVER will get the performance out of laptop that you can get out of a desktop.  Laptops have a lot of demands on both memory and processing that are not on a desktop with separate  audio and video and networking cards.
Laptops are NOTEBOOKS .. you save stuff on them and then download to a real computer!  LOL  They ARE getting better .. but you should Google "who makes laptops" before you plunk down a big chunk of money on something that can not be updated nor repaired by YOU.
