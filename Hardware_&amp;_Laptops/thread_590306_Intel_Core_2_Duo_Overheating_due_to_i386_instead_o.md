### Post by maxwells_demon on 2007-10-24
Yes, I am aware that there are already posts on "i386 Vs amd64" and this ever-persistent problem of laptop overheating. However, I can't find any that connect the two topics, so I feel this warrants its own discussion. 

Firstly, my laptop (Vaio VGN-C2Z) regularly overheated using Feisty on a Core 2 Duo T5500. I scoured internet forums to find all sorts of voodoo solutions (loading various modules, compulsive cleaning etc. ) all for little or no avail...
 (My core temperatures were 50-60+ with no activity, hitting far higher on processor-intensive tasks, ultimately leading to unpredictable freezes and system crashes.). Again, as was the case with others, this did not happen on Windows . 

Installing Gutsy i386 did not solve the problem. Aware that my processor was capable of 64-bit computing, I’ve now converted to Gutsy amd64, which seems to have solved the problem... (With no activity, I read a stable 42 degrees, 55-60 during intensive activity, and no crashes so far!). 

I know my problem is fixed, but won't make conclusions about the reason until I find more evidence... Have others with Core 2 Duos tried this? 

(Please note: amd64 is not limited to AMD architectures, read this for more explanation:  [[COLOR="Blue"]http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X86-64[/COLOR]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X86-64")  )
