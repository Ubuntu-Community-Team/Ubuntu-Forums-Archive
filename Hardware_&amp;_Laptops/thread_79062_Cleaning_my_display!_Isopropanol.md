### Post by Chuckaluphagus on 2005-10-20
I use 91% isopropanol to clean my notebook screen, it certainly doesn't seem to do it any harm.  This notebook is ten months old and I clean the screen about once a month, so if it were going to have an adverse effect I would expect it would have happened by now.

I don't like using water because it's not good to have water around electronics (I'm clumsy) and because isopropanol evaporates very, very quickly, meaning everything is dry again within seconds.  

If your screen has some sort of glossy finish or coating to it, you might not want to use alcohol for cleaning since it could damage the finish (this is why I don't use isopropanol on CRTs).  However, there are cleaning solutions you can purchase at any office supply shop that are safe for screens with glossy finish, anti-glare coating, etc., and they're not expensive.
