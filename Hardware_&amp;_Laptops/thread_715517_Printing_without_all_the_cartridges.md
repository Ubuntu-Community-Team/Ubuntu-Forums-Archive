### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-03-04
The more cartridges or toner carts in a unit, the more it will cost.  8 color units are available in jet style with Epson leading the way with some super photo printers (I have one and love it)  Pigment ink on quality paper is archival quality .. will last 200 years.  I have seen a print live in a glass of water for 3 days and no damage done to the print.
Laser multi color printers are VERY expensive still .. and really justifiable only for a business that can write it off.

But it is not going to be a 55 buck at Rite Aid Drugs jet printer that will fill your bill.

Augmenting is what is called a CISS or Constant Ink Supply System that can be added later (and a waste ink recovery system is almost mandatory) .. bottom line will be in the 500 buck range for the whole package if shopped judiciously but your ink costs will drop to about 20%.  So you have to balance the AMOUNT of printing to justify the initial cost.

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-03-10
none with the Epson . the Epsion I have is a wide bed (18") and is an 8 color and AIN'T cheap.  Not something suitable for all round use or for small office use.  (Epson R1800)

PCL is computer keyboard control
[http://www.nefec.org/UPM/ccPCLfrm.htm](http://www.nefec.org/UPM/ccPCLfrm.htm)

NOT SURE AT ALL if this will work within Linux at present. (but HP printers do work on MAC as that is how they got their start in printing!)

Post Script is a universal TYPE language.  Every platform going has provisos for working with that as it was the FIRST printing language developed and they continue with it.  (it determines type face and spacing and size through the printer options and gives you a bunch of universal fonts.

So you see, the two are not related

You can get PCL+Post Script or PCL alone or Post Script alone or none of the above with some makes and models of printers.
