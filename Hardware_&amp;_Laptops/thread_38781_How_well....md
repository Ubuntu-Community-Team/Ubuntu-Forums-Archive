### Post by thrift on 2005-06-02
Well it won't be extremely fast that's for certain.  Your main problem will be the RAM.  I haven't tried using anything but GNOME in Ubuntu, but a fresh install booting right into GNOME without using anything else is >100M of RAM on my machine.  cutting out GNOME will save you some space, but no matter what you will be tight.  If you get your memory upgraded to 128MB or more, you should be able to run pretty decently with a light window manager, and even ok in GNOME.  It'll probably hurt a lot to go out of the RAM though, because with your swap on a laptop drive your not going to be able to read and write to swap very quickly.

To say the least it's not going to be faster than BeOS.  Now if you installed say...Slackware or something extremely minamilistic like that on there, that will probably faster than BeOS, but will a modern Linux distro be faster than an operating system as old as BeOS? no.  

Will it be worth the pain(see: lack of speed) due to usefullness? probably.  

Might you want to look into upgrading the RAM or looking at a more minimalistic distro if you can't do that? Possibly, you can probably find a more acceptable performance/feature ratio by going with one of the more minimalistc distros and configuring it to run just what you need.  Of course if you have the money to drop on the RAM you can probably use Ubuntu quite happily as well.

I assume the disk is a 2.5" IDE, are you aware you can't just bump that into your average PC and install?
