### Post by hardyn on 2007-06-16
if HP is glueing the heat device to the CPU then yeah, the arctic silver will not work... at all; good luck getting the old one off!

if the device is held down using screws, clamps, springs or whatever, the arctic silver will work just fine (just pay attention to how it is applied)  I have used it on my laptops, and it works great... i know they claim amazing and wonderful things, and it does appear to be better than the paraffin wax based stuff, but don't expect to drop the CPU temp a whole lot.  the thing that the online stores might have made mention of is if the CPU comes with an integral heat spreader.  if the silicon die is exposed, a little 1/2" x 1/2" square in the middle of the substrate, go for it with the AS; however if the CPU  is mostly metal on the top 1"x1", the AS guys do not recommend its use, then you may be better off with the standard white silicone stuff.
