### Post by markitect on 2007-02-01
Unfortanetly with Li-ion that procedure needs to be done before they go bad to do any good at all.

With laptop batteries there is usually no bringing them back; they have a tendency to slowly loose life, then suddenly(as little as a week) drop dramatically to almost nothing.  It will probably stay at about 15 min for a good 6months, but its new battery time.  If you don't wanna spend the cash on a new one check ebay, or there are a few how-to's on replacing the real batteries inside your laptops pack(google around).  One time it worked for me, and another time the battery pack broke completely(probably due to my soldering).  This process is what most ebay batteries have gone through, but with experienced people doing it.  Also make sure to get li-ion cells with the tabs pre-soldered on and exact model number matching is always a plus, but you can get away with identical voltage cells.

DISCLAIMER: I can't be held responsible for anybody catching their laptop on fire from a bad li-ion cell transplant.
