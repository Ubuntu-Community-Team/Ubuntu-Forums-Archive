### Post by hidarikani on 2008-02-20
I have a socket 754 motherboard and a Sempron 2600+, which is 32 bit.
I want to install Athlon 64 3200+, which is 64 bit.
Will Ubuntu still boot, and how do i switch to 64 bit Ubuntu,
and will I even notice improvement in performance?

Thanks in advance

---

### Post by Sukarn on 2008-02-20
Your OS should still keep working without any need for you to change anything, but switching to 64 bit would require a re-install of the entire OS (in this case, Ubuntu).

Whether or not you notice improvement in performance depends on what kind of applications you use your computer for.

---

### Post by hidarikani on 2008-02-20
Are you sure I can't just download some packages via Synaptic to make my os 64 bit?

---

### Post by Sukarn on 2008-02-20
Nope. That cannot be done, as each and every package (including the kernel) will have to be replaced with its 64-bit counterpart, and because of this, there is no documented way of converting the 32-bit OS to 64-bit.
You will have to re-install Ubuntu from a 64-bit cd.
