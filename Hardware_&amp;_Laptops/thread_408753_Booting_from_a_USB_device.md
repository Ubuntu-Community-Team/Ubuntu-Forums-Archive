### Post by kidders on 2007-04-13
Hi there,

The answer to this question is entirely up to your BIOS. You should check your motherboard's manual, to see what your system supports. If booting from USB devices isn't supported, you could take a look at the manufacturer's website, to see if any BIOS updates are available that introduce the feature.

Ubuntu (along with most Linuxes) will happily boot from anywhere ... as long as the computer itself supports booting from that location.
