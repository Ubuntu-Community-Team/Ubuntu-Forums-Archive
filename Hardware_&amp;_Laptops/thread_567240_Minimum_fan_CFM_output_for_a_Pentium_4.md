### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-10-04
Install lm-sensors or add the temperature applet to the top toolbar.  Tracking your CPU temperatures is simple.  The Gnome temp applet can log temps to a file, send an email, or sound an alarm when temps exceed a certain value.

When playing a DVD, you don't want your CPU temps to exceed 60C.  My 2.8 GHz P4 normally runs between 38C and 43C during normal web browsing with a stock Intel fan and heatsink.  I have my alarm set at 55C.  It rarely goes off unless I'm pushing it hard.
