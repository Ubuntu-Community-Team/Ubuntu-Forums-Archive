### Post by tturrisi on 2008-01-09
I have had best results using the Microsoft wireless keyboards and mice.
Stay away from Logitech!  I've had to t-shoot and repair countless Windows systems due to buggy Logitech drivers/software.  MS even has Knowledge Base article re Logitech wifi devices.  The MS wifi hardware works well w. Linux.

2 GB RAM is plenty, but may as well get 4 GB now.  Linux can use the full 4 GB wheras XP and Vista 32 bit will only utilize 3 GB.  Not that you'll ever need it unless get into heavy image edits, video-music edits.

Get nVida graphics, preferably PCI-express, minimum 256 MB memory, 512 would be better.

Get SATA 2 drives.  If have the bucks then get a solid state drive!

### Post by logos34 on 2008-01-09
+1 in favor of nvidia graphics.  

Don't even think about skimping on the power supply.

ditto on sata drives.  Just read the reviews--you can't generalize across the board based on make...I have two maxtors (IDE) and they are not loud,  And they definitely don't run hot.  (Nor does my Seagate sata, but it's actually slower).  I know the Seagate perpendicular drives run hotter and louder.  WD caviars are probably the quietest.  They're very popular.  If you can, get two smaller drives vs. one big drive so you have a disk for backups.
