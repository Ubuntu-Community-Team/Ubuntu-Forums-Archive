### Post by hardyn on 2008-01-31
this exact question gets asked alot.

the two big things that hold up compatibility are sound and wireless.  aside from that, notebooks are pretty much all the same.

if notebook does not advertise why type of wireless chip it is using, it is probably using a broadcom; and this is something to pay attention to.  not all of the broadcom chips work well in linux.

sound has been a problem for many of toshibas in particular.  again, find out precisely what audio hardware the notebook is using as search for its compatiblity with kernel version you are planning to use.

the dells seem to be well supported, but do require workarounds on some models.
Asus seem to have reasonable compatibility.

hp, tosh, compaq seem to be a little touch and go, from model to model.  If your sales person is serious about making a sale, im sure they would have no issue with you test driving a live disk.

cheers.
