### Post by tpg on 2007-06-10
I'm considering buying a hard drive upgrade for my laptop. However, I've read that volumes larger than 140GB need something called "48 bit LBA" on the motherboard/in the bios in order to access the drive. 

Now, from some research on wikipedia, I've found that this particular feature was introduced in the UltraDMA 5 specification. My BIOS page seems to give contradictory information, saying that the hard drive I have at the moment (40GB) supports UltraDMA 5, however in the ATA mode selection box, only lets me select up to UltraDMA 4 (at the moment, the option is set to auto). 

Does anybody know how I can tell if my laptop supports 48 bit LBA (all the utilities I can find are windows only, and don't seem to work under wine)?

Thanks in advance! :)
