### Post by jrial on 2007-05-28
My two cents: if gapless playback is important to you, try and get one of the players supported by [RockBox]("www.rockbox.org"), or one that does gapless playback natively. For most music this doesn't matter, but when you have albums with songs that are supposed to blend into each other with no pause in between tracks, you'll get annoyed pretty soon when this feature is missing from your player.

Ogg/Vorbis should in theory have no problems with gapless playback, since it doesn't pad the end of tracks with silence to fill a "block", like mp3 does. However, most players will not start loading the next track into memory until the current one is finished, and this causes a slight delay and again an audible gap in playback. So ogg/vorbis support is no guarantee for gapless playback when encoding your albums in this format. Your best bet is either a player that does gapless playback out of the box, or a player that's fully supported by RockBox (a firmware replacement for a couple of mp3 players that adds many features, including gapless playback).

Be sure to check rockbox's documentation pages on your specific model though; not all "supported" players are fully supported; for example the Sandisk Sansa E200 series currently don't have support for the MicroSD driver, the USB driver nor the FM Radio, so using rockbox on this model would leave you with a somewhat crippled device, IMHO. Source: [http://www.rockbox.org/twiki/bin/view/Main/SansaE200Port](http://www.rockbox.org/twiki/bin/view/Main/SansaE200Port)
