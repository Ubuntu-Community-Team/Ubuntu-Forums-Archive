### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-03-03
not yet and not for a while!  
The actual requirements for Blu-Ray are daunting to say the least.  processing speeds above 3.6.  2gb of memory to match. full 3D rendering capabilities on the video display to name a few (1080i or above wides screen  (wxuga just barely makes it)
It is POWER hungry and most laptops will not be able to keep up with it without becoming hot plates.Then there is the cost of the burner and the EXORBITANT cost of the media.  If you are made of money, you won't be bothered by 15 bucks or more per disk.
Software is another steep issue, since the goal of Blu-Ray is to totally eliminate any means of copying a disk, it will be a long time before software will be available to do so and there will be legal battles that will have to be done.
There is the compatibility issue .. be it equipment or region.

This is a good read: [http://bluraysucks.com/](http://bluraysucks.com/)
