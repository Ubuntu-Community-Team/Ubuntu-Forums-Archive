### Post by moephan on 2006-04-11
Don't fret. Wireless is often something that takes people a little bit of tweaking to get working, even on Ubuntu.

To start, can you tell us if showing up in the network manager? Start at the System menu, choose System->Administration->Networking. Enter your user passwork when prompted. In the dialog titled "Network Settings". If there is an entry for "Wireless connection" try clicking on it and clicking the "Properties" button. In that dialog, you may need to click on "Enable this connection" checkbox. Then you can choose the wireless network from the drop down.

If the "Wireless connection" is not available in the Network Settings dialog, then I would suggest Applications->System Tools->Sustem Information(hardware info). When that loads, you should be able to find your wirelss card listed. If you post the information for your card, then perhaps someone else with that card can tell you how they got it working. You should also search the forums to your card to see if the solutoin to your problems has already been posted.

Cheers, Rick
