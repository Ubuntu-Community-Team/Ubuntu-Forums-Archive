### Post by PilotJLR on 2007-02-10
No, use a normal VGA cable.

A couple things: DVI-D is digital only, and can never ever connect to a VGA interface (unless an intermediate converter box is present).
DVI-I is "integrated" digital and audio signals. Pins unused in DVI-D can carry analog under DVI-I. So for a vid card with DVI-I output, you can slap on a cable converter to move the analog signal out to a VGA only monitor.

In your case, this converter could not work (even if it did, there is no benefit in quality). The converter cables will only take DVI-I from the vid card over to a VGA-only monitor.
