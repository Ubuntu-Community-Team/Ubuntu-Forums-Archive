### Post by MrCheese on 2007-03-06
Just had a look at the web page for this item - 

> Asmax AR 801U ADSL Ethernet USB Combo Bridge/Router with Conexant Rushmore chipset and is full compatible with ANSI T1.413 Issue 2, ITU-T g.992.1 (G.dmt) and G.992.2 (G.lite). AR 801 U operates on full ADSL transmission up to 8 Mbps for downstream and 960 Kbps for upstream. The main interface LAN RJ45 10/100 Mb and USB port make this router the most universal equipment on market - perfect to co-operation with all ADSL services.
User can use different interfaces dependently of it's needs. To share the connection among many users (up to 253) you can simply link up the switch or hub.

AR 801U supports dynamic and static IP routing, DHCP and NAT function. It has built-in SPI firewall and work in ADSL annex A or B. 

As it says there is an ethernet port, go for that instead of USB as per the previous answer. You should find that with the modem connected to the ethernet port on your pc (fit a cheap card if it really hasn't got one, I'm not sure how cheap you can get one in Poland but over in the UK you'd spend less than 5 Euros to get a reasonable one). The manual which came with hte modem should explain how to set it up over Ethernet.
