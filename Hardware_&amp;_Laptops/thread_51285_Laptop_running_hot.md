### Post by aggiechemist on 2005-07-23
First and foremost, laptops run hot. It is just sort of in there nature. I know you said it was cooler in Windows, and that is good, but I wouldn't be too paranoid about being warmer in Linux. Since laptops do tend to be warm, manufactureers keep taht in mind when they put them together. The only time to be really worried is if you suffer burns from them. It actually reminds me of a study done a few years ago. one showed that laptops tend to be so warm that if you keep one in your lap constantly, it can be bad for various male reproductive bits.

Anyway, the other issue is why is it warmer. I would look at the various stuff in your laptop. For instance, is the harddrive constantly in use? If the harddrive is always spinning (you should be able to hear a whining sound when it goes) then that will get warm. If for some reason linux is accesing your hard drive more, then that would be warmer. I would also go look at the system monitor (under applications->system tools). Is the CPU always busy? That will make it warm. Are you using a lot of virtual memory (swap usage)? That would make the harddrive warm. If you can figure out what is being used more, than maybe you can find a way to back that off and run cooler. But given that most laptops are OK running pretty hot, you may not need to worry.

Good luck.
