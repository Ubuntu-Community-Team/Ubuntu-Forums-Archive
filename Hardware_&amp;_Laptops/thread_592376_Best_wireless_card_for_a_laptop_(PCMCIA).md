### Post by buddhadba on 2007-10-26
My wife's got a Netgear PCMCIA (I think it's the "Extended Range" one) in her Dell laptop running Feisty, and I recall it working rather slickly upon installation. I haven't upgraded her to Gutsy, yet, but my Dell broadcom wireless internal in my laptop sucks with the restricted driver from Ubuntu in Gutsy, so I'm going to try using her Netgear, and if it works better, I'll be buying another for her laptop. I tried going the ndiswrapper way in my Dell, which has worked okay in the past, but no joy this time. It absolutely wouldn't work.

So, bottom line, nothing definitive, yet, but I'll try to post back once I test it on my laptop. Signs are good, though, that the Netgear PCMCIA should work with Gutsy, because it installs and works fine with Feisty. Of course, there were a number of other things that worked with Feisty that don't now with Gutsy, but I'm keeping my fingers crossed. Hope that helps. -- Rick

### Post by buddhadba on 2007-10-26
Yes, the Netgear RangeMax WPN511 PCMCIA card of my wife's works soooo much better than the Broadcom internal modem. I plugged it in, booted up, and Gutsy installed the restricted driver automatically. Up and running and access speed is much faster than the internal. I'm gonna buy one or two more for the other laptops Hope that helps.
