### Post by spd106 on 2006-11-30
The direct comparator would be a geforce 5600 or 5700 series card. But they are very old and quite obsolete now.

If you're stuck with AGP then have look at the 6600 or 7600 series cards. You should still be able to find some second hand cards around, though they are getting rarer as people have moved on to PCIe. For that you would need a new motherboard.

If you want new, then the 7600gs agp can be found quite easily for about £75-£90. If you can't stretch that far then a 7300 may be good enough. Be quick though as AGP is dying.

For background research check out the ars technica and anandtech websites for some good reviews, stats and buying guides.

### Post by spd106 on 2006-12-01
The fastest card with open source drivers will probably be the X850 from ATi. Since only the open driver support tfp you'll have to use them for compiz/beryl. It's roughly equivalent to a 6800 nVidia, but for that card only the binary drivers have 3D support. 

Phoronix have a recent article about this, conveniently using Edgy as a testbed.
