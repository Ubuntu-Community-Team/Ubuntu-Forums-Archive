### Post by oddhack on 2008-04-02
I'm running 7.10 on an Athlon XP / Abit NF7-S 1.2 motherboard, with a Dell 2005FP flat panel. When devices (flash drives, printers, scanners) are plugged into the flat panel's USB ports (which is itself connected to the motherboard), they are not recognized (e.g. nothing even shows up in /var/log/messages to indicate that the kernel recognized a new device, much less say, flash drives being automounted). Devices plugged directly into the motherboard ports don't have this problem.

Through some experimentation, I found that 'wc /dev/bus/usb/*/*' would kick off a process that causes the kernel to recognize devices connected to the flat panel.

Any idea what's up here, and how I can tweak it to automatically recognize devices when connected to the flat panel? There's an old bug against 6.10 but sounding similar [here](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=54160), but no solution was identified in that thread.
