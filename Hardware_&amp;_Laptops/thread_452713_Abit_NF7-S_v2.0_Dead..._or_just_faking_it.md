Here's some :popcorn:  It'll be a long winded thing.  

**System Specs:**

Antec ATX case / with SmartPower 350W (new)
ABIT NF7-S v2.0  (new)
Athlon XP 2500 Barton (new retail box)
1GB (2x512) OCZ PC3200 with copper heat spreaders (new)
ATI 8500 DV
40GB WD 8MB cache (new)
Liteon CD 32x (old spare laying around at the time)
Windows XP Pro SP1 (I know... but he is NOW having to use my old Dell v350 with Drapper) ;)

He later added a HP DVDRW

The box was built 3 years ago... give or take.

**The drama:**

Roughly 3 weeks ago, his computer starts flaking out, so who gets the call... you got it! He's been having trouble burning cd's and dvd's. In fact, made a ton of coasters it turns out. So I stop in to see if I can diagnose the problem. Pulling the side cover off the box horrified me. Still having nightmares about it!

The box was full of crude including, but not limited to, hair - carpet fibers - dust - and smoke residue. Of course the smoke made all the stuff coagulate... so to speak. Now is my chance to take revenge for all the times he got on my butt (though I deserved it at the time), because he deserved it.

Not having the proper amount of time it would take to clean her out properly, I turned her on. The machine went through POST and booted right up. Logged into winders and 40 kinds of hell broke loose. A end now dialog box popped up saying that SuperAntiSpyware was not responding, so I clicked on "end now." It did just that, too! The machine just decides to quit, in a manner of speaking, because she did nothing more from that point be TRY to keep restarting... over and over. Managed to get into the BIOS and load defaults... when she did it again. Tried getting into safe mode... but couldn't! Unplugged the machine and put her in the closet telling pops, "I'll come by on the weekend and see if we can track the problem down."

**The weekend: Drama - scene II**

The first course of action was to clean the system... piece by piece. Break out the air compressor, q tips, and rubbing alcohol. So we proceed to "Get 'er Done."

With everything pulled, cleaned and reinstalled.... I give her a chance to act right. But no! 

From there...

Disconnected cd and dvd completely - No change!
Swapped HDD - no change!
Reseated RAM - no change!
Reseated video card - No change!
Swapped PSU with an old Dell 250W and whadda ya know... She fired right up!

I go now to get the PSU from my old Antec case, it's the exact same thing as his. All is well for about a week and then poof! Gone again with the same problem. :(

**My conclusion so far:**

One of three things is wrong...

1- The RAM is/has gone bad
2- The CPU is going bad
3- The MOBO is going bad

At this point, not having a way to test these components.. I'm stuck.

Can anyone provide some assistance with a direction to take?

Thank you in advance... your wisdom will be greatly appreciated!
