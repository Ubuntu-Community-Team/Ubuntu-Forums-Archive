### Post by kidders on 2006-10-19
Hi there,

As I understand things, hibernation consists of storing the contents of your RAM on disk and shutting down. The only difference between that and a normal shutdown is that what's been left on your hard disk can be thrown back into memory to restore the computer to whatever state you'd left it in.

I imagine laptops use a teenie weenie bit of battery while they're powered off, but it really should be very, very small ... enough to get thousands of hours out of.

Does that seem right?

### Post by kidders on 2006-10-19
Well, hibernating a computer can be a little risky in some circumstances. Shutting it down properly can be smarter if you plan on removing your battery, or plugging lots of hardware in/out (particularly PCMCIA cards).

But in terms of battery life per se, there's nothing to be gained from shutting down all the time.
