### Post by coffeecat on 2007-07-06
I may not be able to help you much as I have no experience of Acer laptops, but after 4 hours I thought you deserved a response.

Firstly, and I mean this kindly, you are going about it in the wrong way. You're doing what you would do for Windows. Chances are that most of the drivers you need are already in the system. If there are things that are not working, then you need help with configuring them. If you do need extra drivers we'll need more information to be able to point you to where you can get them.

Please give us more details of what is and what is not working. Then I am sure someone will be able to help you.

Sound should be no problem at all. Bluetooth I don't know about. Wireless can be either very easy or quite fiddly depending on what wireless chipset your machine uses. For example, my laptop has an Intel wireless chipset and it works out of the box with Ubuntu. In fact I find it simpler to configure than in Windows.

What do you mean by 'VGA driver'? Is the display working OK?
