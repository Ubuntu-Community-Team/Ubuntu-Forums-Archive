I've been reading up on this and I've learn a few things about them that I did know. Covering spin up speeds and other things like command queuing, but every story I read doesn't give clear answers and keep pointing out about the new tech coming soon, plus some stories where even a couple of years old.

So whats better...
SATA is cheap with high speeds, command queuing and each device has its own channel.
SCSI is expensive with very high speeds and high reliablity, command queuing but possbile 'channel overloads', and own dedicated SCSI bus processor.

But what 'wins' over all as they both has good a bad points.
Will SCSI always be the best solution for servers? And SATA just replace IDE in the home market... Or could SATA start to cut into the server market? Maybe bring SCSI prices down and make them more viable for the home user....
