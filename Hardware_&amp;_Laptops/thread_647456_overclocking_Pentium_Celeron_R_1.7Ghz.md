### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-12-22
Look at your computer BIOS.  (Del, Tab, Esc, F2, F12 whatever brings up the BIOS when you boot).  Look for a tab that says "Advanced settings or Frequency".

See if you can increase either the bus clock speed (say from 100 MHz to 112 MHz) or increase the clock multiplier (say from 17X to 19X), or a combination of both.

Make sure your computer is clean (vacuum out the dust) and the fans are working.  Install some sort of temperature display so you can monitor your chip temperatures while you are running your system.

Intel locks out certain chips from overclocking, I'm not sure about a Celeron R.  

You can normally get ~30% boost in speed before things start dropping out.  Memory, hard disk access, or temperature limits.  There is really more danger to burning out the motherboard than the processor because the support chips have to work harder and they don't have the same cooling that a processor has.

A google search for overclocker will yield a few sights that are dedicated to this practice with all sorts of tweaks and results posted for common processor/motherboard combinations.
