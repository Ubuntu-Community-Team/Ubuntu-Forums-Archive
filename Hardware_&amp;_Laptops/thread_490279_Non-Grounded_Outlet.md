### Post by Shazaam on 2007-07-02
Laptop or desktop? If a desktop, as an experiment, with your pc turned off and plugged in lightly brush your hand over the case. Do you feel a slight vibration/tickle? If you do this may eventually damage your pc/electronic equipment AND could electrocute you. If you aren't a certified electrician I wouldn't try to fool around with any house wiring. Hire a licenced electrician and have the house wiring fixed. The cost will depend on the age and condition of said wiring.

### Post by CrispyFried on 2007-07-02
if all your equipment is ok it shouldnt be a problem but surge suppressors only work on grounded outlets as they route the surge to ground. but over the years Ive uses several computers on non grounded outelts with no problem.

sometimes the center screw that holds the outlet cover is grounded and you can use a 3 to 2 prong adapter that connects to that screw. you can buy outlet checkers for about 5 bucks that will tell you if the outlet is grounded, they have several colored leds that indicate the outlets condition.

you really should have an electrician look at it.
