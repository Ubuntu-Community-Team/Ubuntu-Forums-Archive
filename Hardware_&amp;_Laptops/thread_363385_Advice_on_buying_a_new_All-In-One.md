### Post by bodycoach2 on 2007-02-17
I've used:
Epson Stylux Photo RX500
HP psc 1210xi

Both these machines are all-in-one. Both work perfectly in Ubuntu, and have settings in CUPS. I prefer the simplicity, size, and price of HP. The Epson hasn't delivered on promised 'photo quality' with Windows, Mac, or Linux for me. In fact, the HP makes better photos. Cheaper ink too.

Scanning is easy on both machines. I use the HP for copying, or scanning to .pdf. I like that function, as I scan important documents, receipts, and other stuff, and upload them to an offsite file. Just in case. Learned my lesson on that one in Hurricane Charlie!

If you work in an office or home-office environment, where printing is important, keep all the devices seperate. Don't buy an 'all-in-one' If one part goes bad....you get the idea.

In the future, I'll be sticking with HP. They seem to support printing in Linux best.

I may be wrong about this so please, take caution.  If I'm wrong someone correct me.

Mac OSX uses CUPS. The same printing system used in linux and all other *nix enviornments. So if the box has a Mac face on it then it is more likely to work in linux than one without the face.
