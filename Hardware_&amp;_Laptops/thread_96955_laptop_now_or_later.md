### Post by fezzik on 2005-11-30
I would definitely go with the 64 bit processor.  I had one and AMD has done a great job with these.  I ended up selling it to my dad when he needed a better computer and I needed money, but it worked great.  I used Linux on it once with a Mandrake install dual booting to Windows and several times with live cds.  I didn't use it long since wireless was my only networking and I had no idea how to get wireless working and wasn't brave enough to jump in with both feet at that time.  Since then I have made my intel laptop a Linux machine.  The reason I say go 64 bit is the support is getting better all the time.  People are starting to write and compile in 64 bit for Linux and it will only be a matter of time before they get 64 bit all worked out and when they do it will be really nice to have.  In the meantime 32 bit programs and operating systems work beautifully on 64 bit machines.  If you use Ndiswrapper drivers from what I understand you might have trouble using 32 bit drivers with it on a 64 bit kernal.  So until they get 64 bit drivers if you use that for your wireless needs you might stick with a 686 kernal image and wait to go with a 64 bit kernal when the drivers and everything are working with them.  They may have gotten that ironed out by now too I am not sure as I say I am using an Intel Processor on my laptop right now.  I am a big AMD fan too the only reason I even got an Intel is because I had heard great things about the Hyperthreading and I have not been dissapointed.  It really has helped Intel with people that tend to Multitask a little.   

Summary Go AMD 64 it will work great with what is out now and offer greater growth potential for future developments.  AMD my not have the raw speed but ends up working better because of the greater power like a pickup vs a sports car.  You want the sports car if you go fast and don't cary a load but for everyday driving and use you get more out of the pickup cause you can drive on the interstate with a load and still reach highway speeds.

### Post by jml on 2006-01-31
wwbdd, if you are really interested in the Intel powered macs, I'd definitely wait a bit, at least until it has been reviewed a couple of times.  I also use a Mac Powerbook and according to what I've read, no one knows for sure if the new "PowerMac" (Apple's name for the new line,) will actually run Windows.  It all depends on how Apple configures the hardware.  There are also rumors that Apple may install some type of DRM firmware on the units, so there is no gaurantee that they will load Linux either.  

64 bit is definitely the wave of the future, but it really depends on what you plan to run.  Some applications are not yet supported by 64 bit versions.  Plus  in many ways you are still paying a bit of a premium for the 64 bit architecture.

Another alternative would be to buy a refurbished laptop from one of the on line resellers.  Many of these are corporate laptops that have been very well cared for, but were simply replaced on a schedule.  You can get a really well configured IBM Thinkpad with good, not cutting edge, specs for a very reasonable amount of money.  Think of it as a "disposable" short term solution.  I've done that several times and have been very happy with the result.  Just my two cents worth.
