I have used both cards recently, just making the switch to AMD/ATI. It's depends on what you want for capabilities.  Yes, it's true that ATI/AMD is making promises to open up their platform.  However, you must decide based upon the limitations of both cards and their respective drivers, proprietary and xorg.  

Although the opening of ATI was the impetus for switching to ATI, there are some limitations with ATI, using the xorg drivers.  If you want 3D, you will have to the use proprietary driver.  

With Nvidia, I used the restricted drivers that came with Ubuntu.  Everything worked fine until the Nvidia card malfunctioned.  Xorg did not have any open source drivers that worked effectively.

### Post by jmate24 on 2008-02-24
one warning about nvidia. do not unplug display while your computer is on using the restricted drivers. i have had a terrible time configuring my monitor and had to set it as a generic even though it is supported in order for the drivers and video card to work and to not have to constantly go into the bulletproof linux display config. 

Despite this warning go with nvidia when you install the restricted drivers you can play a lot of openGL games. And get one that has 128MB or more of ram. I have a GeForce FX 5200 and I LOVE IT.
