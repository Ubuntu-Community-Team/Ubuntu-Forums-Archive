### Post by Therion on 2008-03-18
> **RadiationHazard said:**

By "mac for intel" do you mean OS-X?  If that's what you're mean hardware is not really a consideration.  Hardware suitable for one is suitable for the other.  

As for liquid cooling, I advise against it unless you really, and I mean REALLY, need it.  And guess what... You don't.  Setting up a rig with water cooling, even a basic setup, is going to be relatively complicated.  The system will require regular maintenance, be expensive and they're totally unnecessary 99 percent of the time.  Take the advice you're being given: don't do it.  Leave the water-cooling set ups to hard-core overclockers who need it to keep their 8Ghz Itanium rigs from bursting into nuclear fireballs.  In my opinion, if you're rig requires more than two 120mm fans (three tops) you're doing something wrong somewhere.

### Post by L473ncy on 2008-03-18
IMHO

You don't need 4 GB of RAM drop it to 2. It's probably good enough anyways, anyways from what I've been told there's a problem with 32 bit OS'es (if you're using ubuntu 32bit rather than the 64 bit version)  addressing 4 GB of ram, it just can't do it. The most the 32 bit version of windows can do is something like 3.6 GB or something close to that.

Anyways continuing:

The GPU (Video card, the 8600GT) can probably be dropped to a DX9 (instead of the DX 10 you currently are planning to put into it). Either drop it to a Radeon HD3850 if you're going to go budget DX10 or go for a x1950 pro or 7950 GT (as you could probably find it dirt cheap anyways).

For the DVD+/-RW don't get lightscribe unless it's the same price as a non light scribe burner. The reason is that you need a special disc to take advantage of lightscribe which is expensive (as it's proprietary to HP).

Here's the basic break down of what you have.

1 Mobo: OK it's an ASUS so you have some guarantee it's going to be quality.

2 CPU: Q660, not much to say it's Quad core and pretty good.

3 RAM: Patriot (from what I hear) is pretty good so no worries there. Any RAM will work basically the only difference between them is the warranty. Two modules will be almost the same and different ones won't have too much an effect (ie. a stick of 512 G.Skill Ram vs 512 of OCZ vs 512 of Corsair), they might have different RAM timings but that's not a big deal unless you're an overclocker.

4 Tower: Whatever floats your boat I guess, also the PSU (Power Supply) included probably isn't any good so you could probably chuck it if you're going to do any overclocking (the ones that come free with cases you should generally be wary about, as sometimes they don't deliver enough amperage/voltage/wattage and are generally made of crap parts so the transistors will blow if you put too much load on it). Unless the PSU is from a good brand name company you can probably chuck it or sell it.

5 DVD: Whatever is cheapest I guess. (There is generally little difference (not enough to warrant a different price) between a $40 burner and a $35 burner s o just get the cheaper one.)

6 GPU: 8600 is a DX10 card, do you really need DX10? Are you a hardcore gamer that NEEDS DX10 with full settings? If not then go down to a DX9 card like 7600 GS/GT or x1600 (which are dirt cheap by now)

7 HDD: Whatever is cheapest, brand doesn't really matter too much so get the cheapest one (but first gauge what you need. ie a 80 GB vs a 750 GB) then find the cheapest one (a 320 GB Segate vs a 320 GB WD generally has little difference.
