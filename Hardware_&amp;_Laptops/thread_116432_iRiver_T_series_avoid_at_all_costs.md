### Post by beatgroover on 2006-01-12
Hi all,

I have heaps of ogg files, so I'm ditching the iPod shuffle (which work 'OK' under Ubuntu 5.10+) in favour of a player than I can load my MP3 and Ogg files.

I notice the iRiver T30 plays Ogg files, but that the file storage is ONLY accessible via Media Transfer Protocol (think: Windows media player 10), and reports are that you can only upload your MP3 and Ogg files onto this device with Ubuntu via a tool called gphoto2.

Seriously, what an annoying and useless hassle. Why not just make the player a standard USB storage device with a /music folder? Why even support Ogg files if it is so Linux un-friendly. And what about our Mac brothers and sisters? Sheesh! :???: 

Can anyone suggest a good 512Mb-1Gb player for Linux/Ubuntu? Preferably with FM record and line-in?

### Post by matthew on 2006-01-12
I realize this isn't exactly what you are looking for but I have had excellent results with my Cowon iAudio M5. It's a 20Gb hdd player that mounts as a standard usb device and will play mp3, ogg, wmv, flac, and possibly other formats. I bring it up because it is so easy to use with linux that you may want to investigate the company and see if they have something with specs similar to what you are looking for.

### Post by mattbatt on 2006-02-05
Actually in windows my T30 loads as a Mass Storage Device or at lest is accessible via Windwos Explorer.  As a matter of fact there are two main folders Music and Data (ie thumb drive).  I have not yet sync'd it to media player for fear that it will change the association or something.  BTW anybody who is interested the line in on the T30's recording sounds great.  It has 3 record settings low medium or high the high is 320 kb and the medium is 270.  I recorded 4 45 minute band sets and they sound great but it does chew through the AAA recording. 
In my humble opinion I love the T30 and would recomend it somebody will figure out how to mount it in Linux I have to keep XP around for my wife anyways.
