OK, it doesn't matter which way you plug power and reset switches. 

The speaker, if it's a piezo type, needs to be plugged in by polarity or it won't make any sound (I think - could be wrong about that one). Anyway, backwards won't hurt anything on that either. And if your case is very old, with a real speaker, polarity doesn't matter at all.

All LED's must be plugged correctly for them to light up, but backwards won't hurt a thing. They just won't light up. I've found them to be semi-random for which way they plug in. Just try one way and if you get no light flip em over. I seem to remember labels down works most times.

The plug that **will** burn a mainboard (and the USB device you plug into it) if plugged backward is front panel USBs. Be VERY careful to plug those in the right way or suffer the consequences.

### Post by deep sunshine on 2007-03-25
Found the issue with my pc not starting.
The Antec case I bought has a faulty power switch.

Turns out the power supply was good after all. The front switch
had a bad connector in the square piece, either smashed or gobbled 
up with something.

The local computer guy found that if he would touch 
the 2 prongs on the motherboard with a screwdriver, 
for the power switch plug, it would allow the computer to start.

So I replaced the wire from the old case and did some sanding on the square 
insert, for the front of case, and now it works. This is only tried for the new Asus 
P4V800D-X mobo I bought for a family member and not the Asus A7A266 that I have and 
use. Though it should solve both motherboard/power supply issues I was having.
