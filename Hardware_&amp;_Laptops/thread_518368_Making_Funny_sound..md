### Post by BoneKracker on 2007-08-06
Just kidding.

It's probably just case/component rattling that occurs when the PSU fan does into overdrive because your motherboard is drawing more power than usual.

You could try verifying that all the fasteners (screws, clips, etc.) and cable connectors are tight.  Especially check the screws that mount your psu.  Sometimes placing rubber washers under these screws helps.

Also check the regular case fans -- these are notoriously loose, usually being mounted by simple pop-in clips.  Put padding around them if they're not tight.  

You could check for small wires (like fan monitor wires, led wires, etc.) that aren't anchored well.

You could try adding some foam packing where you cover abuts the psu (in gaps where it's not normally in contact but close enough to touch if something is vibrating).  

You could make sure the case is on a level and hard surface.  Optionally, you could try rubber pads/feet under the case.

[Edit]: and if none of this works, it could just be that your PSU has shitty bearings.  Some PSU's are just noisy, usually because of poor-quality bearings in the fan assembly.  If it really bothers you, you might want to consider replacing the PSU with a quieter one.

Caution:  If you use any kind of padding or other material inside the case, make sure it is securely affixed and will not come loose and get sucked into the fan of your psu, cpu or gpu (or short-circuiting something on the mainboard).
