### Post by hellfried on 2006-08-20
sorry if this is the wrong forum but it is driving me nuts. i currently have 4 partitions on my 160G hd; winxp, suse 10.1, ubuntu dapper and swap. i spend most of my time in ubuntu. lately i have noticed that when i reboot ubuntu (after updating and such) my desktop seems to get stuck when it should be rebooting back into grub. 

i see all the scrolling notices of ubuntu shutting down various processes and finally the line that says 'will reboot now'. i will hear a click at the end and then nothing. the light on my reset button on the pc is lighted up but it just cannot reboot. what i do is i have to switch off the power supply to my pc and after a few seconds switch it back on. all of a sudden the whole pc will kick back into life and proceed to reboot back into grub as if nothing happened.

i also get this problem when i decide to boot into the other os from ubuntu. i feel that it may be a hardware problem but am not sure. this desktop is about 9 months old and is running on the pentium D chip with 1G of ram. this problem started about a month ago when i did a clean install of dapper(i was originally using breezy). any help would be greatly appreciated.:D




updated : i finally found out what the problem was. my power supply unit was going down. my pc could not start up this morning and when i took it back to the shop they swapped out the psu and voila, everything was back to normal!
