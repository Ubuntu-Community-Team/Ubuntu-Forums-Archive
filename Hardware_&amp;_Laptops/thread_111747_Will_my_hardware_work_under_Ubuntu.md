The processor, RAM, graphics card and HDD should be fine. That's all you need to install Ubuntu. :) The ethernet adapter, integrated sound and DVD burner *might* be tricky but they will usually work. The Wacom Graphire 3 Tablet and the video capture device are probably the most likely to give you problems out of everything that you mentioned. That's not to say that they won't work, but they will probably require more time and tweaking than any of the other hardware.

Since you have not purchased a USB device yet, this is the perfect opportunity for you to check for Linux support *before* you buy one. Try looking through the [Hardware Support]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport") section of the [Ubuntu wiki]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/"). Usually flash drives will work OK but it never hurts to do research beforehand--it will save you trouble later. (For the record, I have a 1 GB PNY Attaché flash drive that works flawlessly with Breezy.)
