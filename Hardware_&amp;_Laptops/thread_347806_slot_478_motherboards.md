> Also, how is a techie able to tell the difference between a dead power supply and mobo?

The local guy took his money and said both were bad, nothing said of the processor, and never offered to replace or do anything with the pc. If this continues, a brand new computer could have been bought for all the messing aboot that's been done.
Test mainboard with a known-good power supply (PSU). You also have to verify that the problem isn't being caused by a messed up or misflashed BIOS, dead battery, bad CPU, bad RAM, detached (broken?)front panel power switch wire or bad front panel switch. There are probably other things that could be wrong that I forgot to mention.

Test PSU by plugging it into a known-good system. There are PSU testers you can buy too.
