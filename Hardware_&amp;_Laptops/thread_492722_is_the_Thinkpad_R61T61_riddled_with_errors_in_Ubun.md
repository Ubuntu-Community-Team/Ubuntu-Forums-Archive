### Post by ciphermonk on 2007-07-06
There's nothing wrong with the T61s and Linux. I've been running Fedora x86_64 on mine for over a week now with no ill effects. I have all of the devices working and the Compiz Fusion window manager running without a problem. You will have to do some work to get all of the devices working properly as things like the video card and sound card don't work properly in the default installation. This is due to their being very new hardware. The process of what I did to get everything working can be found here...  [http://forums.fedoraforum.org/showthread.php?t=159516](http://forums.fedoraforum.org/showthread.php?t=159516)

### Post by meanimal on 2007-07-07
In my experience so far, the hardware is excellent.  I've managed to fix most if not all of the problems encountered when running Feisty but it was no easy feat.  This is perfectly understandable considering the Santa Rosa platform is merely months old, and Linux drivers are generally speaking developed after products hit the market.  However Intel is the exception, they developed preliminary support for the gm965 graphics, and new wireless, however of course there were some bugs that were only discovered as people started trying the hardware out.  Considering Feisty and Fedora have 6 month release cycles, of course we're going to have to wait that amount of time before the hardware is supported smoothly.
