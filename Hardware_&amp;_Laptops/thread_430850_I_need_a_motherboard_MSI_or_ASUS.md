### Post by ohgod on 2007-05-02
The compatibility from Linux isn't going to be specific to the company that makes the board, but rather to the components that the board uses (like the nForce 430 MCP chipset, sound card, video card, etc.)  

Since the chipset types on the boards are the same the level of support in Ubuntu should be about the same.  I have Realtek sound on one of my boards and it's supported fine.  And the NVIDIA drivers for Linux are pretty decent.  But just to be sure, you could check out the Linux hardware compatibility list and make sure the different components of the board are listed there.

Check out this HCL:  [http://www.linuxquestions.org/hcl/](http://www.linuxquestions.org/hcl/)
