### Post by BlueStreak on 2006-11-04
I'm troubleshooting a friend's pc that won't power on.  My first thought of course was power supply.  I noticed something odd about the existing psu.  When I plug it in to a wall outlet with NOTHING connected to it the fan spins up.  This is odd, it should require a trigger from the mobo to turn on.  Voltage is present but it's a little high, I read 15v and 8v instead of 12v and 5v.  I tried connecting it to the motherboard and this time the psu fan did not come on but the cpu fan did.  The voltage then measured 2v and 4v. Sure would seem like a bad psu.

I then connected a known good psu.  This time the psu and cpu fans came on and voltages were good at 5 and 12 but still no response from the machine.  The power switch (which is good) had no effect and it was funny that the psu kicked on without pressing the power button.

So it would seem that the logic in the original psu is screwy seeing as it turns on with nothing connected and the voltages are way off.

Then it seems like a bad mobo as well since nothing works with a known good psu.  Does this assessment sound right?  Anything else I should try?
