### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-01-20
32 bit apps won't always work in 64-bit.  Anything OSS will, as it's been compiled for 64-bit.  But then I guess that's not 32-bit anymore...  Binary packages compiled for 32-bit won't work natively, there are extra steps/packages required to get them going.  If you are willing to do that, then great - I haven't heard that anything flat _won't_ run, just that some things are more work than others.

If you want everything to Just Work without any extra effort, then run 32-bit Ubuntu.  This is what I do - my Core 2 Duo system is my primary workstation, and I wanted the Just Works experience.  If I had a second C2D system, I would be running 64-bit to play with it, but no luck there yet... ;)

While you supposedly get a small speed bump by running 64-bit, even with 32-bit on here this system absolutely blows everything else I have out of the water, including the 3GHz P4 HT system.  No comparison! :cool:
