### Post by SeanTater on 2007-05-11
***How it started**:*
About 2 months ago, my computer just stopped dead in it tracks. I had to force-restart, because even the networking has ceased and I could not SSH into it.
When it booted up again, I was surprised to find that the BIOS had a message for me. It explained that the hang was because the CPU had the wrong frequency, so it asked me if I was sure I had the right frequency. I had read /proc/cpuinfo a few times before, so I verified that it was indeed correct, at 2200MHz.
Being the uptime maniac I am, this had peaked my interest. So, I looked through dmesg to no avail. Xorg also had nothing to say about it so I let it go.

A month later, it did it again. The logs still had their lips sealed about it. So, I proceeded with my backups. Before they were finished, an hour later, it died again. Following another reboot, the terminal had barely started before the computer turned into a corpse.

[I]**Now:**
[/I]I realized I had an expensive brick at my feet when the POST beep never happened. The fan, whose speed is controlled by the motherboard, got no quieter, even though it always had. The DVD drive acts like it's searching for something for a second, but that's it. I rebooted several times and left it alone for about 20 minutes to see if it would snap out of it, but it continued.

I *think* I know the cause, as we had vacuumed near (but never in) it some time before, and the vacuum had serious electrical problems, as we learned later. But I'd like to know what I need to replace.
Since I have another (very old) computer, I set the DVD and HD drives in it to see if they are affected. The HD is fine and the data is intact, but the DVD drive is having some serious issues.

I had memory problems in the old computer that brought the same message, so I have a hunch the memory has something to do with even thought the message concerned the CPU.

So what I really want to know is what to replace, and possibly how..
