### Post by kayvortex on 2007-08-13
It all depends on your budget and how much hassle you are prepared to take. I have an ati X1400 card on my Dell Inspiron 9400. To get it working reasonably I had to do a fair bit of googling. For example, beryl would not work straight away: from the beryl forums I found that I had to run it with a specific flag. Even now, resizing windows can't be done without the graphics crashing.

If you choose to go with an ati card you will have to accept that you will encounter problems, most of which will be solved after some looking, but not all of them. I have a newly installed nvidia card on my pc and I have experienced no problems so far. I don't want to completely put you off an ati card if you feel that it would best suit your other needs, but I still recommend nvidia.

I agree with what both fredj and jexxie have said: a 6200, 7100, or 7300 series nvidia card should give you what you want. But, you can get a 7600 card for a bit more, and it'll be MORE than capable for what you want. I usually get my stuff from overclockers.co.uk, and they have an [asus 7600 GS]("http://www.overclockers.co.uk/productlist.php?groupid=701&catid=56&sortby=nameAsc&subid=356") for £50 (~$100); but I'm not sure if they ship outside of the uk. For entry/mid table graphics cards, I'd say that an nvidia card would be equal to a similarly performing ati card that is about $20-30 cheaper.

I'd conclude that generally you would want to go for an nvidia card, but if you do decide to get an ati card, you should find out as much information about installing drivers and getting it to work with linux as possible before buying it; and if you feel that you can accept a bit of annoyance and a few chronic problems for a performance/price benefit, then go for it.
