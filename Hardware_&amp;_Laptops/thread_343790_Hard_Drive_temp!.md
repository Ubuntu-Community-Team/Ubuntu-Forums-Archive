### Post by wiresquire on 2007-01-22
Some laptops have poor ventilation for HDDs. I'm not familiar with yours, but can share some experience. The area above and below the HDD on mine gets very warm. You might say it's always been a ball burner.

I had used a passive heat sink, mainly to save my crown jewels. You could probably get the same effect by just putting making sure the laptop is raised off the desk or your lap so there is airflow under it. 

When I upgraded my HDD to 7200rpm, I was worried about extra heat.

First, I have a fairly aggressive spindown of 2 minutes.

The easiest solution seemed to be to go to an active heat sink. It runs off a USB port. The one I got decreased HDD temperatures by 10C. In winter this brings the HDD temp to about 40C. In summer, I can often see it creep towards 50C with ambient temperature at 30C+. Then I know it's time to turn the air conditioner on....

There are some other things that are more 'hacking'. Here's a couple of links that I have bookmarked if I start to get more worried:
- [Heatsink guide]("http://www.heatsink-guide.com/hdd.shtml")
- [Homemade heatsink]("http://www.barefeats.com/heatsink.html")

Oh, and I do back up regularly, even if the drive has a 5 year warranty...
