### Post by chavo on 2006-10-24
Afaik this is not possible with the Nvidia drivers. They will always make the Vga connection the primary monitor. 
 I have a setup where one monitor has VGA and the other has DVI and had tried for quite some time to make the DVI the primary monitor. 

Apparently this bug has been addressed in the new beta drivers. I couldn't get my second monitor to work at all with these drivers, and maybe this is why. I didn't spend much time working on the beta drivers though, it was a busy 2 weeks for me.

Anyway here's a thread about the problem, the news about it being fixed is on the last page. [http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=54638](http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=54638)
