### Post by deathmonkey on 2007-03-13
Hi all.  I've had Dapper Drake installed for about two weeks now.  I have had a few monitor issues in that time, but when I went to bed last night, everything had been working swimmingly for several days after a second fresh installation.

When I woke up this morning, my monitor was dead.  It will flash on for an instant, but then goes 95% dark, making it impossible to see or do anything.

So, I see two options:

1) my monitor died.  It was two years old, this was its time, and this is a coincidence.
2) I had a bad refresh rate set without realizing it, and although it held out for a while, after two weeks my monitor couldn't take anymore and finally bit the dust.

So my question is: how likely is option 2?  Xorg had correctly identified both my video card and my LCD, and I hadn't touched a single setting in xorg.conf.  So it seems to me everything should have been fine, but this just seems like one heck of a coincidence.

Any thoughts on this would be greatly appreciated.

(And before anyone asks, I checked the monitor on a Windows XP machine, and a second Dapper Drake machine, and it behaves identically in every case.)

Thanks.

--jim

### Post by peabody on 2007-03-13
I think #2 is highly unlikely.  Any LCD monitor I've dealt with won't even display anything if the refresh rate isn't within an acceptable range.  The OSD on the device will post something up to that effect, informing the user of the problem.

Spontaneous hardware failure sucks, and it almost always seems coicidental.  Murphy's law.
