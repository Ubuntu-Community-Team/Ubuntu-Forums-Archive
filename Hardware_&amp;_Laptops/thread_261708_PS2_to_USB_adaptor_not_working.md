### Post by A. J. Rimmer on 2006-09-23
Sorry to say, I think you are out of luck with this.

:-{

A few weeks ago I tried to buy a similar adapter.  I have an old PS2 mouse, and wanted to use it on my laptop, which only has USB.  A local shop did not have any in stock, so he ordered one for me.  When he got it in, we opened the package and gave it a try, but it did not work.  The shop owner then tried it on a desktop computer in his shop and it worked.  Then he tried it on a new Acer laptop he had for sale, and it didn't work.

He went to his computer and googled for a while, then came back and said that apparently many laptops don't provide sufficient current on the USB  port to make the adapter work, although many desktop computers do.

I offered to pay for the adapter anyway (he had ordered it specially for me), but he refused, saying he would not sell me something that would not meet my needs.

I ended up buying a new, cheap USB mouse for $10.

I also learned that there is a version of this adapter that will work with laptops, but it is several times the cost of the cheap version.
