### Post by Yoooder on 2007-03-08
As Almight stated, almost any PC will run Ubuntu--it just may have some unsupported devices.  Generally these are odds-and-ends video cards, sound card, winmodems, and other things such as that.

The best way to get a 100% compatible machine is to custom build it to you liking and needs, and to verify the components are compatible do a google search for "Linux Hardware Database" or "Linux Compatible hardware" etc...

Most major/commercial distros will maintain a database of tested hardware and it's level of compatibility, Mandriva, RedHat and SuSE all have there own.  There are a lot of others.

Of course if you have something in mind, doing a google for the device +Linux should find answers fairly quickly.

### Post by Yoooder on 2007-03-14
The biggest problem in my eyes with OEM hardware is that they very rarely give you any control over the motherboard.

You can have monster wheels, a 2' lift, and a 454 on a vehicle, but if it's build on top of a Geo Storm you are just wasting your money.  IMO it's always advantageous to buy a very good motherboard and put decent hardware on it, and upgrade when the prices of the top-end drop.
