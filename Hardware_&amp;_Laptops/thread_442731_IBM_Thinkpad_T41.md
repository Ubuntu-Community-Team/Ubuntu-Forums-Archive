### Post by doepain on 2007-05-13
I have been working with Linux (servers) for 4-5 years, and I really love Linux as an operating system.
However whenever I would try and incorporate it as my primary desktop (for my laptop) I would run into issue with wireless not working, and other little things that would annoy me.  I wish I could live online in forums and try to find a fix for my issues, but I don't have the time with work and family.  

I know that most people  that are members of the Linux community are a knowledgeable bunch, and they should just come up with a universal fix or solution for things (ANY "things", not just mine).  Rather I found that a lot of users seemed very arrogant, unhelpful, and extremely condescending towards other less knowledgeable users.  An could not believe that I was unable to get wireless working, or bluetooth... whatever.

I been hearing a lot of good things about Ubuntu from other users that it was a great Windows replacement, great community ...and works well with laptops with little to no configurations.

Thus far I agree 100% ...I have installed it on my Thinkpad T41 and right from the start EVERYTHING was working with no configuration.  I have colleagues and friends in similar boat as me, and I have been turning them to Ubuntu and they have been very impressed with it.

I am placing this post in hopes that someone may find it while doing a Google Search, and give Ubuntu a try.

Not all of us are college students who have the free time and resources to delve deep into Linux to be gurus who just keep RTFM -ing to everyone.
