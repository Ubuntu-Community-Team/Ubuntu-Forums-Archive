I generally pull off the side cover and look at the mainboard with a flashlight. Almost all manufacturers silk screen their name and the model number on the board. Might not be so true with oem boards, but mostly it is.

I had one board I was trying to identify that had no markings at all (either side). Had to pause it just after POST (Power On Self Test) during bootup and write down the numbers from the screen. After some googling I had it. Found out why they weren't proud enough of their work to put their name on it. What a crappy board, from a crappy company - think it might have been SuperMicro, tho that doesn't ring a bell... I remember this company sells into Brazil a lot, or they used to anyway. Absolutely zero driver support for older boards. Nothing. Nada, and I'm VERY good at googling for that kind of stuff. The machine got tossed.
