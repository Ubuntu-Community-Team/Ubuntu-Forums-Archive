### Post by jml on 2007-07-17
IBM sold thier computer manufacturing line to Lenovo.  Lenovo still sells a ThinkPad line of computers, along with another, more consumer oriented line of laptops.  Here is their website.

[http://shop.lenovo.com/SEUILibrary/controller/e/web/LenovoPortal/en_US/catalog.workflow:CompareByNeed?current-category-id=653343E0DE54435882FABC3CE1BC569A](http://shop.lenovo.com/SEUILibrary/controller/e/web/LenovoPortal/en_US/catalog.workflow:CompareByNeed?current-category-id=653343E0DE54435882FABC3CE1BC569A)

I only have experience with ThinkPads manufactured by IBM, (R30, and R40e,) but from the reviews I have read, the Lenovo ThinkPad line is still pretty Linux friendly.  I have read mixed reviews about Lenovo's 3000 line.  The general impression is that the 3000 line tends to use less Linux friendly hardware.

Back to the ThinkPad, I would recommend thr R series.  Its not the most compact nor the most modern line, but it gives relatively good value for the money and it does not use any esoteric hardware.  I have heard about problems with their X series and their convertable (laptop/tablet) line.  I have also heard that their T series is also pretty Linux friendly.  If you have a choice, I would buy a laptop with the nvidea graphics chipset,as the ATI cards tend to have more problems with Linux.  Although, my old ThinkPad R40e has a 32 Meg ATI Mobility Radeon 7500 graphics card and works well with many flavers of Linus, including Ubuntu.

If you would like to get a ThinkPad on the cheap, you might want to check out this site:

[http://www.usanotebook.com/ibm_thinkpad_computer_laptops_notebooks_sale.php](http://www.usanotebook.com/ibm_thinkpad_computer_laptops_notebooks_sale.php)

They sell used, and refurbished laptops with a limited warrantee.  They offer several ThinkPads at reasonable prices.  I have purchased one from them and was very satisfied both with the quality of the laptop, and their service.  Hope this helps.
