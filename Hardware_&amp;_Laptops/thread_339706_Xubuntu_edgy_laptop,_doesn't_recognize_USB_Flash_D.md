### Post by survient on 2007-03-12
A follow up to this. it has to do with my laptop's firmware. I have an Averatec C3500 convertible notebook. Any of these laptops loaded with 1.0 or higher firmware, their USB ports and a few other hardware devices will not work with xubuntu or ubuntu for that matter. The only solution I've found is to downgrade the firmware, which I have done, but the install froze on me, and I had to update the firmware, now I'm stuck at firmware 1.1, and any bios flashing I try to do fails. So, be careful when doing this.
