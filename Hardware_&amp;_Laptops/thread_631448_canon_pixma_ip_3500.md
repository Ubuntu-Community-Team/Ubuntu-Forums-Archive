### Post by Cubby on 2007-12-08
Hi,

I have a Canon Pixma iP3000 and after three days of spending much time following forum instructions to get my printer working, which gave me no good results (either test printout was faded or printer would spit out a blank page) I decided to try TurboPrint.  It is now working.  I just did a test print from Firefox at the website - Los Angeles Times Crosswords, which requires Flash for the puzzle to show up.  Anyway, it worked great and looks just like it does on my iMac or W2000KPro OS's.  

[http://www.turboprint.info/](http://www.turboprint.info/)

The free version of TP prints its logo on any page you have printed, and to get it without, you have to shell out $30. bucks or so.  To me, this is worth it. 

P.S. I even tried to install the drivers mentioned at Mepis.org 
[http://www.mepis.org/node/6250](http://www.mepis.org/node/6250)
for Canon Pixma printers.  A Japanese gentleman has created his own, but I couldn't get them on my ubuntu system.  I did try them out a year ago on KDE desktop in Mepis distro, and they worked pretty well for being free drivers.  

I wanted to add that I installed the tgz file and then went to system/preferences/turbo-printconfig and changed paper/page size/ from A4 to US Letter

Regards.

### Post by dorite on 2008-01-17
Nixie21 -- Yes, I think I've gotten my iP3500 to work.  At least the colors all seem to be in reasonable spots.  The Ghostscript drivers I tried had them all over the place.  So what I did was read the HowTo Canon PIXMA IP4000 drivers doc (from the Ubuntu Forums ... Tutorials & Tips tree), then googled for Canon drivers.  That took me to Canon Europe, which had drivers that seemed as if the procedure in the tutorial would work.  So I did that.  And it worked.  Almost (trying to print on anything but 8-1/2 x 11 paper crashed f-stop).

But then I found Canon of Australia!  Go to 

[http://www.canon.com.au/products/printers/colour_bj_printers/ip3500_support.aspx](http://www.canon.com.au/products/printers/colour_bj_printers/ip3500_support.aspx)

get the .deb packages and the manual and FAQ .tar.gz packages.  Read the manual, then follow the instructions.  Everything I've tried so far works.  Note that I haven't tried many things yet -- just a couple of paper sizes and a couple of resolutions.  Tomorrow (time permitting) I'll try 3x5 cards, 4x6 photos, and half-sheets.

Luck to you.

### Post by dorite on 2008-01-18
OK -- I've tried everything I thought of, and everything works -- although not necessarily the way you'd expect.  I strongly recommend that you download the driver and manual from Canon Australia, then (if you know as little as I did) learn to print from the terminal.  That's Canon's way of making more of the 3500's features available -- printing from an application (e.g. firefox, gimp) limits the number of options available to you.

Again, luck to you.
