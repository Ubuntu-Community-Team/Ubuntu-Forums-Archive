### Post by hellion0 on 2008-02-05
If Gnome won't load due to a full HDD, you can always use ctrl+alt+F1 to back out to a terminal, and rm files or dirs you no longer need... and NOT the important ones, be careful with that...

Also, I think you could hook the old drive up as an external to a different Linux install and copy the files over. As long as the system in question can recognize the filesystem format of the old disk, it can read it at the least.
