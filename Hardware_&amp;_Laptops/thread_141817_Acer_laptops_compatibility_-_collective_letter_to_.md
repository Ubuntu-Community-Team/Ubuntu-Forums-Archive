### Post by iNerdSure on 2006-03-09
This thread arises from the discussions, research and work done in resolving lack of drivers and other compatibility issues with Acer laptops.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=76019&page=20]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=76019&page=20") 

In particular, Acer TM4060/4061. This model was sold with Linpus basic installed as an "OS". But several users experienced poblems with no sound, wireless, battery meter and bluetooth.

The view is that Acer should not sell a system with only a basic Linpus Linux OS. Such a marketing approach suggests or implies to unknowing customers that they will be able to install a full Linux OS from their choice of various Linux distros.

Acer should disclose that their laptop model has not been fully tested with any  distro, and that customer may not be able to get various features to function fully. That is a more responsible business practice, :-k  rather than omitting such information or product warning to customers.
