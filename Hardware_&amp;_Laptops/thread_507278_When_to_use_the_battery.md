A few weeks ago, I got my first laptop: a Dell E1505N with an 85 watt-hour, 9-cell lithium ion battery. Can I get away with using AC power all the time except when I'm on the go, or should I always alternate between completely charging and discharging the battery? Which is better for the battery?

### Post by 5-HT on 2007-07-22
> **jordon said:**
>  Can I get away with using AC power all the time except when I'm on the go,
Yup. LiOn batteries are 'smart' so there's no need to always do full discharging/charging: partial discharges will not hurt the battery and you do not have to worry about overcharging.
Batteries loose their charge capacity much quicker in heat so it would be best to avoid having the battery in the computer if you're using on AC a lot. On the other hand, let it go through a full discharge at least once a month (both overuse and underuse reduce the expected life of the battery). As there are a limited number of (full) charge cycles before the charge capacity dramatically decreases, it's best to just use the battery when needed and AC otherwise. If you'll be using it on AC fo a while, I'd consider taking the battery completely out in those cases.
There are quite a few great sources around for help on prolonging battery life.
Overall: You bought a laptop for portability...enjoy the freedom! :)
