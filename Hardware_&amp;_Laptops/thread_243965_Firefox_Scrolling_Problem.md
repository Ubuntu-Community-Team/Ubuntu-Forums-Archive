### Post by garrye on 2006-08-26
check and see if the images you are running into are driven by Macromedia's flash/shockwave player.  You can test this by right clicking on the image. If the context menu produced contains the phrase "about macromedia..." among other options then you have a case of what I call "context/focus hijacking" that occurs not only in firefox but also in IE 6 under windows as well. Have NOT discovered a workaround for this.  Simply the nature of the beast.  Unfortunately we are seeing an increase in the amount of web page content that is dished out using flash player.  The content provider industry likes macromedia for a variety of reasons, none of which IMHO benefit the end user.  Ho Hummmmmmm.
