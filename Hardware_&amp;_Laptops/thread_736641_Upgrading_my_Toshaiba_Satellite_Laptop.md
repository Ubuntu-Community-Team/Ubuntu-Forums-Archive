### Post by Existentialist on 2008-03-27
For the ram, you will want to check what your computer has in it already.  You have 2 slots which each support up to 2GB of pc2-5300 or pc2-4200 (both DDR2).  You may have 2x512mb in there now, in which case you will need to buy 2 1gb dimms.  Keep in mind that if you do want to go with 2x2gb for a total of 4gb, you will need to be running the 64bit version of ubuntu to utilize it all.  Memory upgrades are pretty straight forward, and should not result in any issues.

Unfortunately, on almost every laptop out there, there is no way to add a graphics card.  The integrated chipsets are a part of the motherboard, so short of replacing the whole motherboard, you are stuck with what's in there.
