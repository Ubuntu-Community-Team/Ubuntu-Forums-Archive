### Post by Aninhumer on 2007-07-08
I have been having the same problem as you, and I have successfully reverted to the edgy drivers.
Just add the edgy repo into your sources.list alongside the feisty ones, and then force the edgy version of the driver. Then you can tell it to hold at that version so it doesn't screw up when you update again. Alternatively, you could download the edgy i810.deb package and install it manually, which avoids unecessary fluff in the repos :P
I hope the gutsy versions work properly.

PS: Thanks for telling me that the edgy version worked, I wouldn't have got it working without that info. :)
