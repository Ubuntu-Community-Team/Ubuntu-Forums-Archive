Pentium D is Netburst
Core 2 Duo is Conroe

In efficiency, Conroe > Netburst

Thus, a Core (2) Duo processor will calculate more in one clock cycle than a Pentium.

The E6750 at 2.66 MHz uses about 90 watts of power in a built pc.  A 2.8 GHz Pentium D uses about 120 watts in a similar configuration. ### Post by jespdj on 2008-01-01
"Is Hertz all that matters?" - No, absolutely not.

An Intel Core 2 Duo processor is a lot faster, even at lower clock rates, than a Pentium 4 or Pentium D and uses much less power, because of improvements in the architecture of the processor.

I have a Core 2 Duo E6600 (2.4 GHz) at home and a Pentium 4 (3 GHz) at work. Even programs that run on only 1 core of my dual-core E6600 run faster than on my Pentium 4 at work. Forget about the Pentium processor when you're buying a new PC; it's obsolete.

And to stress what Temüjin says: Even if your current motherboard has a Socket 775 socket for the processor, that doesn't mean you can put a Core 2 Duo into it. The Core 2 Duo has specific requirements with regards to the power that the motherboard provides to the processor, and older Pentium D motherboards don't have the right components to support the Core 2 Duo. If you want to replace your processor with a Core 2 Duo, check the manual / website of your motherboard to make sure that your motherboard supports it.
