I had made my first post at [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1478531#post1478531](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1478531#post1478531) speaking of the erratic behavior of the touchpad on my HP DV8000, AMD64 laptop running on Drapper.

Since then I searched on U forums and found advice to implement the touchpad, cut and pasted the lines and did have the touchpad working very well.  I next turned my attention to getting the wireless to working.  More searching and cutting & pasting and I now am wireless with either a broadcom or prisim based card!  Yippie!

Aurguh!  The misbehavior of the touchpad has returned since getting the wireless to work.  I'm definitely a low end user and haven't the foggiest idea of what is conflicting.  However, I did take some time to observe, experiment and make notations.

The first thing I noticed is that the cursor becomes 'not normal' if moved while a program is doing something.  Examples:  in Mozilla (v1.7.12) or Pan, during the 'active-wait' icon, if the cursor is moved the cursor becomes erratic.

Sometimes the focus of the cursor becomes attached to certain areas of the screen.  Examples:  the panel (GNOME Panel 2.14.3) at the lower edge of the screen or the right half of the upper bar if in Mozilla.

Closing open programs (when the correct one is closed) seems to release the aberhent behavior of the cursor and return it to 'normal' behavior.

Restarting, rebooting or booting into 'recovery mode' sometimes returns it to 'normal, but not always.'
