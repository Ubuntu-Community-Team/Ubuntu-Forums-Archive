### Post by teeleef on 2007-10-28
Well I finally saved up my pennies and had a leap of faith, 

I bought a HP Pavillion DV9000 series machine.  It cost £900 here in the UK which cost a lot of cash.  The local high st store threw in a laptop bag for free,  thanks very much!   PCWorld wouldn't offer anythingf not even knock 20 quid off the cost of a bag. It was quite comical, there I stood wearing my Ubuntu sweatshirt and the sales guy said "we can give you a reduced price on Norton or 2yrs free remote support to keep your machine up to scratch?"......yeah right!!!!
"Will you be wanting Office?"..........err no!   I felt like really venting my spleen at this guy, but realised he was just doing his job, in fact I felt a tad sorry for him....just trying to earn a crust I guess.

I was chuckling away to myself as  none of the staff clocked the Ubuntu logo.

Anyway the intrepidation started once I got home.  Switch the laptop on, hold delete key, eject cdrom tray popped in Gutsy and pray.  No keeping Vista just blind faith in Ubuntu,Linux and you guys.

The LiveCD just loaded.  I deleted all partitions (this laptop has two 160gb drives) and the bugger installed.  It took me two hours to realise the wifi had a toggle switch....doh!!!!   20 second mod to get the sound working.  Wireless and music both working 100% :)

So really this is a big thank you to EXCid and the rest of the hp testing team and to the rest of you how take the time, patience and perseverence to get Linux to the point it is at today were newbies like me don't get too many grey hairs and sleepness nights.  

THANK YOU 


Teeleef
