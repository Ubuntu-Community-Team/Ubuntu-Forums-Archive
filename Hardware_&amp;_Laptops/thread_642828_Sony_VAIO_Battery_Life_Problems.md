### Post by d0nny2600 on 2007-12-17
Ok first..Is the battery actually running out after 30 mins?
Try discharging the battery completely using your BIOS and then recharge fully before booting back into Ubuntu. Then check how much battery life you have left.

Ubuntu is usually pretty efficient with battery life and I have noticed a marked improvement in my laptop after I wiped Vista from it

### Post by Daelyn on 2007-12-18
Hum. 
Just a tip, but try download and run "powertop" on your machine. You will find it in synaptics power manager. It will shut down services/devices not needed/used.

I have seen a noticeable drop in power usage on my Sony Vaio when I use that. Although, it is kind of manual and you have to actively choose to agree on things it wants to shut down. Also, If I reduce my screen brigthness a few notches, I see yet another increase in battery life.

I can get up to 4.5 hours on mine, if I use it for divx playback only. Active use 3-4hrs.
