### Post by Atomic Dog on 2007-06-06
Neither.  The pentium dual core is a cheap competitor for the cheaper AMD chips -basically a cut down core-duo with 1/2 the L2 cache and 533 bus (yuck).  Look for a core2duo.  The T5300 doesn't have VT built in and a 533mhz bus, but will smoke your choices.  I am no fan of AMD mobile chips and that dual core pentium seems is a dog too.  Shop around and really try to get the core2.  Might cost you $100 more but it will be worth is.  Also the centrino laptops will almost assure you fairly easy feisty install and usage (sans a few tweaks).  I bought an HP dv6xxx laptop and it works great.  I opted for the even pricier T5600, but the T5500 (1.66ghz/667bus) or T5300 (1.73/533bus) should do you fine.

I had a core-duo HP dv6xxx and then bought the core2duo.  Same processor speed.  The core2 was snappier, encoded video way faster, has better game performance.  It also is 64bit which can be good for future OS'.
 
BTW for those who think I'm an AMD hater...I have AMD in my home-built workstations at home, and mostly AMD at work.  I just am not impressed with their mobile products and performance.
