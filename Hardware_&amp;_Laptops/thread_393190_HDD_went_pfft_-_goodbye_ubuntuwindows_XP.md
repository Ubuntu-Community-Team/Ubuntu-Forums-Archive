### Post by hardyn on 2007-03-25
If your bios is not detecting the drive, and anther machine is not detecting the drive, you have a hardware failure, and has nothing to do with the ubuntu install... it would have failed under windows as well.

hard disks fail just as any other part can fail... while PCB failures are less common than mechanical failure, they are not unheard of.

### Post by dfreer on 2007-03-25
Sounds like your hard drive just went kaput. From what I understand of things, partitioning errors shouldn't be able to physically damage a hard drive. If it was a problem with partitioning you could fix it by simply wiping the drive and starting over.

I always have wondered whether a hard drive could be physically damaged by an incorrect device driver (say a virus modified it), that causes the hard drive to spin up too fast or the heads to move in such a way as to damage them. But that's just a side thought, you probably just had a bad hard drive.
