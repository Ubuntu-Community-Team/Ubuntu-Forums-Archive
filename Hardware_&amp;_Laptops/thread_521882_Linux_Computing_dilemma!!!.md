### Post by AlbinoButt on 2007-08-09
Tamarku, let me preclude this by saying that [B]this is just my personal opinion.
[/B]

I think that you should use the tools that get the job done best. If Windows Vista is suiting your needs for school/productivity/software/etc, then that's fine. Putting Linux on the computer might be a ton of trouble to get everything working the way you want it.  Even then, you still might have to run Windows for specific apps.

I think that the most ideal solution is to purchase a laptop with Linux preinstalled, mainly because you'll have official support and be confident that the hardware will work properly. (This isn't an option for you since you got a Toshiba with Vista). I purchased a Sony and it has so much proprietary stuff that's meant to work with Windows that I'm having to do a lot of work just to get all my hardware working. It's sad that we have to choose between the hardware that we want and the software that we want. Until more vendors sell Linux as an option, we'll keep having these problems.

My personal experiences with Windows and Microsoft have made me do anything necessary to break out of proprietary software. But hey, this isn't a viable option for everyone.  Especially people with jobs/school that **require** you to be locked into Windows.

Tamarku, if you'd like, you could install Ubuntu in a virtual machine under Windows. That way you probably won't have as many hardware issues and you don't risk causing damage to your Windows installation.
