### Post by kahlil88 on 2008-01-27
I installed Ubuntu 7.10 "Gutsy" on my friend's laptop, which is a Compaq Presario V5000, and it's having a few problems.

1. When booting up, the screen goes totally black for about 2 minutes, then the desktop loads. Before the screen goes black, I get the following errors:
> 
PCI: Cannot allocate resource region 7 of bridge 0000:00:05.0
PCI: Cannot allocate resource region 8 of bridge 0000:00:05.0


2. Ubuntu finds the modem, and I installed the restricted driver, but I can't connect. I don't even get so much as a dialtone to at least know if it's sort of working.

3. The computer lags when watching videos in fullscreen. The videos I've tested worked just fine with Windows, so I'm guessing the video driver isn't good enough.

4. Music skips when I switch between workspaces. I think this might have something to do with integrated audio and video.

5. Fn+F5 puts the computer to sleep as it should, but I can't get the computer to actually wake up, so I have to turn it off and then on again.

There are probably other problems that I haven't noticed yet, so I'd really appreciate feedback from anyone who has run Ubuntu on this laptop.
