### Post by i_TheDevil_i on 2007-11-10
=) it's basically the problem of the model. what i mean is the Amilo L1300 is the problem. i found a lot of people having this problem. and there is a solution, not best, but it's quite a good one. u have to remove the cooler and put a good thermial grease on it & on the processor, then put the cooler back... i used the Zalman Super Thermal Grease ZM-STG1... the result was: 1. the fan became less noisy 2. it started turning off, what means that it cooled the CPU so much that it could turn off... 

the bad side of this solution is if u work/play/etc. on the laptop too long, the fan becomes as noisy as it was before the "upgrade" =)

### Post by KAARE99 on 2007-12-26
To fix this problem replace the Celeron with a Pentium M. I used a Pentium M 725 Dothan since it has stepping it runs much cooler at around 600 MHz instead of the 1500 Mhz the Celeron M370 ran at. It gives you one more meg of L2 cache as well handy. Make sure to blow clean the laptop cooling and remove all the old thermal paste and apply new with the new processor. This fix is brilliant the L1300 is a sweet lappie after my wife is rather brilliantly pleased.
