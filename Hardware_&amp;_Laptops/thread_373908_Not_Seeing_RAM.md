### Post by CattyKid on 2007-03-01
My friend recently purchased a new Compaq Presario SR2150.  
It came with 1 512MB PC2-4200 DDR2 SDRAM (240-pin, DDR2), so she bought another with the same specs, also 512 mb.
He computer now only sees 840 mb of RAM with both sticks in.  With the new RAM in the first slot and the second slot empty, the computer only sees 328mb of RAM.  It does not matter what slot the RAM is in, it is still recognized as only 328 mb.  She runs Vista and Ubuntu, but I don't think either are the problem.  Even when booting, the computer shows as 840 mb of RAM.
What do you think it could be?  Shoddy RAM?  Maybe some of the modules on the stick are not functioning properly?  Originally I had thought it was a BIOS problem, but being a new computer it can surely accept upwards of 1 gb of RAM.  So, should she return it and buy another stick in its place (she had wanted to avoid this as she bought it online)?

Thanks a lot

### Post by xur17 on 2007-12-16
I saw this post while searching on google, and even though it is pretty old, I figured I could answer.  This ram is showing up as 384mb instead of 512mb because the integrated video card is using part of the system ram, and therefore the system is only displaying that it has a portion of the ram.
