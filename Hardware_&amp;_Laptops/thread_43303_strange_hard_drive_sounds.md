### Post by intangible on 2005-06-21
Sounds like it to me, I wouldn't trust that drive with anything anymore, better make backups.

It could last another 6 months, or maybe just another week, you just don't know.  The S.M.A.R.T. monitoring on the drives aren't perfect, they don't catch everything that could possibly go wrong.  One of these days you'll start up the system and the hard drive just won't be recognized, or worse, it could corrupt data without you knowing it's being corrupted.

Try this, download this ISO, burn it to disc and boot off it, maybe it'll tell you some more:
[http://support.wdc.com/download/index.asp?cxml=n&pid=999&swid=30](http://support.wdc.com/download/index.asp?cxml=n&pid=999&swid=30)  (Just right click the file and select "Burn to Disc..")

### Post by PhilippWollermann on 2005-06-21
Mmh.. my Hitachi hard-disk makes some nice sounds too.. sounds like a "meow" from a cat. Very cute. ^^ And it's perfectly normal too.. it's some sort of thermal calibration. But I think Hitachi drives are the only ones, where this noise is normal.. my older IBM hard-disk broke a few weeks ago.. well.. it worked again, then it wouldn't respond to IDE commands anymore.. very strange. I deleted all data from it and I'll see what happens.

In short: Clicking noises are *usually* not normal and you should start backuping... There are some exceptions, but you should explicitly verify, whether your disk is allowed to make strange sounds or not.

Philipp
