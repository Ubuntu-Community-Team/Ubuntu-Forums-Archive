### Post by lojic on 2007-02-12
I hope I'm not confusing folks with a post about how well something works, but I was just so thrilled I wanted to throw out a "well done" to the Ubuntu folks.

While working on my Ubuntu 6.10 machine and adjusting the volume a lot using the mouse and a volume control applet, I commented to my wife, who was working on the Mac mini next to me, how I miss the volume controls on the Apple keyboard.

Then a thought occurred to me, "hey, this is Linux, it can do anything, I should Google for how to map a key to the volume control". So I googled for a bit, and it wasn't until then that I realized I actually had volume control buttons on my MS keyboard - d'oh! So I pressed one, and to my utter amazement, it just worked! (as did other keys such as 'mail', 'calculator', etc.)

I guess being an old time Linux user, I just assumed I had some work cut out for me to get this to work. I don't even recall telling the installation utility much detail about my keyboard - it just works! Awesome.

Of course, I kind of hate this particular keyboard because of the key action, so I guess I will need to figure out how to map a "regular" key, or combination, to the volume control eventually. If anyone has an easy way to do that, comments are welcome :)

I'm going to have some popcorn :popcorn:  to celebrate.
