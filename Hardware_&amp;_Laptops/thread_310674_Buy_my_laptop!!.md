### Post by BarryTice on 2006-12-05
I've got a ThinkPad 390E I bought used off eBay about four or five years ago. It came with W98 on it, and has since had Red Hat 8, RH 9, Fedora Core 1, Hoary and now Edgy on it.

In all the other Linux distros, there were problems with the USB port. Edgy seems to have addressed that nicely.

This generation of ThinkPads definitely had hinge issues. There's a lot of plastic anchoring the hinges, and I've had to rebuild one of my corners with JB Weld. But it is an old box (Pentium II, for Pete's sake!), and they've gotten a lot better.

I have a T41 for work that I sometimes have to travel with (though most of the time it lives on a docking station), and though I don't have any Linux experience with it, it's been rock solid. Coworkers with T41s or T43s haven't had any hardware problems that I've heard of, though there have been plenty of Windoze issues.

If I could afford a new laptop for myself and money weren't an issue, I'd absolutely consider another ThinkPad.

Meanwhile, I also have an HP laptop that I acquired cheap off a friend because the power inverter is going out and the screen sometimes goes dark for no reason. A little research on the 'net shows a lot of people selling those inverters, which suggests there are a lot of customers. That's a scary thought.

As for the hard drive, what you would want depends on how you're going to use it, and you haven't told us that.

I've got nearly 100 GB of music on my home box. If this will be your only computer and you like music, hard drive size becomes very significant. (On the other hand, you can get a 300 GB external drive for about $160 these days, and rotate music onto your laptop to match your mood. You don't _really_ need to have more than 3-4 GB of music on at a time, do you?)

My Edgy ThinkPad only has a 6.2 GB hard drive, and that's more than enough for what I do with it. But this is not a "power user" laptop by any stretch. I'd be inclined to spend less on the internal hard drive and more on as much RAM as you can put into the beast, personally. Oh, and pop for a large external drive.
