### Post by mercurus on 2004-11-08
I'm sure someone has made a multimedia keyboard specifically for Linux, but for those of us with standard ones, GNOME has native support for most layouts.

Have a look through the Computer Configuration menus specifically Keyboard and Keyboard Shortcuts. You can select a keyboard layout, write your own, and map multimedia buttons to specific tasks.

I have my (cheap as chips, re-badged generic, Dodgy Brothers Inc, less than $AU 80 wireless optical desktop) multimedia keyboard working under Ubuntu. I have the keys mapped to evolution, firefox, multimedia controls for Rhythmbox, my home directory, and opening a terminal.

Most multimedia keyboards can be made to work with X, take a look at the lineakd project (google for "lineakd")

Cheers
mercurus
