### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-03-26
If the power circuitry on the motherboard lets go, then yes, it could burn out the CPU.  Most Intel processors are well-protected from voltage and thermal cycles.  If the heat sink comes loose then the processor will normally start to boot then shut down due to overtemp.

I assume that you tried removing the battery and running off of the AC cord alone?  Sometimes a shorted battery will cause boot problems.

A quick google search shows that the processor alone is $244 to $360.  The motherboard would probably be ~$200.  So if you can let the Dell goons repair it for $500 that would be worth it.  To replace a display on a Mac G4 powerbook:  $800.

If it's a thermal/power issue, then I would expect it to crap out again after a year or two.  Dell has had some parts sourcing issues from overseas (i.e. non-Texan) suppliers.
