### Post by DarkMachine on 2007-12-23
I have heard that this is a known problem with AGP cards that actually have PCI-E chipsets, and then have some sort of bridge from PCI-E to AGP.  This causes the card to be recognized as PCI-E even if you dont' have any slots.  I have a Radeon x1950 AGP and it shows agp = 0x with newer drivers.  Glxgears even runs, opengl screen savers run...most of the time..but forget compiz, or any 3d games.  I found that if i went back to the 8.37-8.40, instead of the newer drivers 3d works.

I hope they get this bug fixed, once a month i download the new drivers...try them...fail..and then go back to the 8.40 drivers :(
