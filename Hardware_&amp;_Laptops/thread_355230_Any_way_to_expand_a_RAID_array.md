### Post by fcigoi on 2007-03-02
I don't think it's got anything to do with the operating system. This is normally a function carried out by your RAID controller and looking in its manuals or browsing through its users forum, if it exists, may help a lot more than looking here.
Also, a lot depends on the type of RAID config you have set up. If it's a 5, you will have to rebuild the whole system, or leave it all as it is and use the new disk as a stand alone disk outside the array
