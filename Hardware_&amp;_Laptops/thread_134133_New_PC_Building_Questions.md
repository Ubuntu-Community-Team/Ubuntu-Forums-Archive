### Post by ekarni on 2006-02-21
What kind of "media serving" do you have in mind?  If you're talking video, I'd think about getting a solid video card, maybe with TV out.  If you're just talking audio, you might be able to get by with a lower end processor (I know, you can never have enough horsepower, but here's where the $$$ tradeoff comes in).  You *might* be able to run ok on 512MB of RAM, but that's not where I'd cut corners on quantity.  Optical drive shouldn't be a showstopper, I paid <$50 for a LiteOn DVD-RW.

Regarding my own experiences building last summer:
-Yes, my AMD64 3000 with OEM heatsink does run VERY cool under normal load (LM sensors claims 20C which is cooler than ambient - yeah right! -  but regardless clearly heat is not a problem!)
-Bought an Antec SLK1650 case based on good brand recommendations; would NOT advise getting one.  The 350W PSU it came with wasn't strong enough to even boot my system - had to replace it with a 430W Enermax.  Also, it came with a 2 wire case fan - for what I paid, it should have come with a sensor fan!  (and those slick blue LED's!)  Last thing, the (black) finish scratches rather easily.  Next time I'm going to buy a cheap off-brand case locally and stick a quality PSU and fans of my choosing in it.
-A floppy drive is nice to have when you're swearing at a machine that won't boot.  If you don't buy one new, maybe you can borrow one from a friend or cannabilize one from an old machine.
