### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-02-28
i just happen to read this artivle yesterday and it shows favorable results in buying the E4300 over the E6300 due to the overclocking capabilities. If you wait till Q2 of this year, you can get the CPU even cheaper, by probably $40 or more dollars. I just bought mine from mwave.com and I paid $175.00 for the E4300. I am going to run the stock hsf and probably just overclock to what they did in the article. One statement in the article stands out: **When overclocked however, the E4300 can hang with the best of the best and thus you'll see comparisons all the way up to the X6800 and Athlon 64 X2 5000** The article overclocked it to  3.375GHz (375MHz x 9.0) at 1.468V using a stock Intel cooler which is faster than the Core 2 Extreme X6800 for only $162 bucks right now. You can't beat that!!! 

Note: The article does state "feature no support for Intel Virtualization Technology" so I don't how that impacts your requirement for a Virtual Machine with windows. I have never ran a Virtual setup. Good luck
