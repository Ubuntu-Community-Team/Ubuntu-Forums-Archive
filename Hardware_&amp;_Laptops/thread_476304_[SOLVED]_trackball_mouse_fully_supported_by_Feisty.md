### Post by saphil on 2007-06-17
It is kinda funny but I have a Microsoft-branded mouse working on my fileserver running Feisty.  It works great.  It  is PS2 USB compatable and I have it attached to a USB port.

It has no extra buttons, just 2 each Left and Right and a center scroll wheel/push-third-button.

Welcome to the world of hardware testing!  To find out whether the extra buttons on a new trackball mouse work or can be  programmable in Feisty, you will probably just have to get one and see.  Then you will be the hero as you post your findings to the hardware forum here.  

Wolf

PS  I have some fancy sound features on the motherboard SB-Live built in, and it turned out that ALSA could work with most of them.  Only one volume knob that didn't work, but I never used it with win98 either.  You may find you are happy enough with whatever trackball you choose, but if it needs special drivers, then choose hardware that is a year old or so if you are conservative and cautious.
