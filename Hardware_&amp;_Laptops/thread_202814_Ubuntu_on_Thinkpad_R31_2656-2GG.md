### Post by NotonyourNelly on 2007-08-24
I have installed xubuntu 7.04 and more recently ubuntu 7.04 on an R31. This had 1100 mhz processor and just 128MB or less RAM. Few of the IBM keys worked intially and I think I have a very fussy CDROM drive as it was a pain to load

xubuntu 7.04 - I struggled to get loaded at all until I used the alt cd to install beside XP. It was a slow install but it ran well but as there was little or no space left on the 10Gb drive I couldn't do much. I installed 512MB ram and it was much happier - dare I say that the IBM XP install was faster.... 

ubuntu 7.04 - I took the plunge and installed tis and it was a real struggle as the CRDROM drive seemed to reject or stall on may disks, even a slow burned alt install CD. In the end it did load from a standard LIVE CD - there were very long pauses between CD activity and I gave up a number of times out of a lack of patience. Then I took out the lowly 128MB ram and left the 512MB in and it seemed to load without a hitch...perhaps mismatched RAM played a part or just poor CD burns

Amazingly the IBM keys now work with lovely graphics showing levels volume, brightness etc showing on the screen. The battery level indicator appeared straight away in ubuntu with estimate of 3.5 hours though it fades v quickly so this needs to be replaced I think. Sound works out of the box. All the issues I had with little everyday extras I had to add with xunbuntu are there and I'm happy now.

I tried the preview of Beryl graphics and the machine didn't crash as it does on my desktop..... I didn't like the sliding style at all, it made me feel queasy - perhaps if I upgrade a graphics card on the desktop I might try it again as it could need more memory.

For people considering loading this try the LIVE CD and play with this without installing - it is a very good experience - Note that if you install you will need a 20Gb hard drive (esp if you want dual boot XP on the same drive) and at least 512Mb RAM - probably more of the latter to speed the install at least.
