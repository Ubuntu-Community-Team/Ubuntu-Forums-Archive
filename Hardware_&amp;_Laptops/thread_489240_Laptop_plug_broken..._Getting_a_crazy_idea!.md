### Post by wie6Ein0 on 2007-07-01
I'm having a very tough time. The port on my laptop where my power adapter plugs into is broken. At first, I thought I had a short in the adapter cord, so I did a little cutting and patching -- all for nothing.

I'm getting very annoyed because my battery is no longer being charged and to get the laptop to turn on at all requires plugging in the adapter, bending it very hard to one side, wrapping it around the display screen a few times, and then taping it down really tight. The slightest move sometimes jerks the cord the wrong way and causes the whole laptop to turn off.

**[COLOR=Red]REMEMBER: IT'S THE PLUG THAT IS BROKEN, NOT THE POWER ADAPTER/CORD![/COLOR]**

All this frustration has made me try to find some alternative to making the laptop work. Here's what I'm thinking (not knowing if it's possible or not): There's two points from the plug that are soldered to the motherboard. If I strip the cord on the adapter plug AGAIN and solder the correct wires in the right places, it should power the laptop just as if it was being plugged into the port, right? I mean the port is there mainly just to allow the power adapter to be easily connected and removed. If what I'm thinking is possible, it should just be like making a permanent connection.

Before I go and do this, I'd like to here your thoughts... If it's not going to do anything but blow up the laptop (or catch it on fire), then please let me know now.

### Post by raynman68 on 2007-07-01
A friend of mine had a similar problem with his HP laptop a few months back. The hot lead from the connector to the system board had broken off after months of doing what you are doing to get yours to work. What I did to fix it was to solder an insulated jumper wire from the plug to the system board. He has been using the laptop without a problem ever since.

Is it the plug on the end of your cable or the socket inside the laptop thats at fault?  If its the socket in the laptop the above post is the go.  If it is the plug on the cable try a bit of very carefull disection and try and re solder the wires on the pins or if available get a new plug.
If none of that works try a new plug and socket of a different type maybe installed on a short pigtail.

### Post by CrispyFried on 2007-07-01
I did what raynman68 did to an old laptop I had, it worked well till the day I retired it - about a year later. you just need to be good with an iron. I used solid insulated jumper wire to connect it to the adapter leads because the adapter used stranded and I didnt want to mess with it as it was too thick.

you could take the idea a step further as antmenj said and buy a socket and mating plug and bring the new socket out of the case on a short pigtail, that way you can still unplug it.

just use a small 25 watt iron and make sure everything is grounded - the usual precautions. 

your laptop socket may just have a bent lug (whatever its called) inside thats not "springy" enough to make proper contact. depending on how its made you may not be able to do anything about it though.
