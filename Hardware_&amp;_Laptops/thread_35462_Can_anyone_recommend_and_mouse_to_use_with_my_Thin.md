### Post by tmowerman on 2005-05-19
I just bought a logitech usb mouse, plugged it in whilst still running and it worked.  The extra buttons took a bit of work to get working though.

I have also successfully plugged in a PS/2 mouse, and it just worked.  However, I have noticed that some new laptops don't come with PS/2 ports, so USB may be better for future proofing.

### Post by Arthemys on 2005-05-19
I use a Logitech® Cordless Optical Mouse for Notebooks - Silver

[http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/products/details/US/EN,CRID=2146,CONTENTID=7109](http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/products/details/US/EN,CRID=2146,CONTENTID=7109)

It's size is slightly larger than expected, but that makes for a good desktop mouse too. It's comfortable enough long periods of usage, but is still small enough to be portable. The range on the little USB adapter is amazingly good, and there are no drivers required, plug it in and go.

I often leave the USB adapter plugged into my docking station if I know I'm not going to need the mouse during the day.
