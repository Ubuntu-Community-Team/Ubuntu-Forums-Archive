### Post by jml on 2007-04-27
In my opinion, the hp nx series of laptops are a better choice for several reasons.  1.  The nx series is marketed to business users.  You can see the complete line by going to HP's small business web site.  I bought two nx6110's last year and I am very happy with the quality of the construction, and the fact that HP's business laptops like the nx series do not come loaded down with a bunch af useless software.  In fact, they can be ordered without Windows if you did not plan to use Windows!  My 6110's only came with a trial version of Norton Antivirus, so there was less junk to delete.  The one thing to watch out for is the fact that nx series laptops are offered with either Broadcom or Intel Wireless cards.  If you have a choice get a laptop with an Intel card rather than a Broadcom card.  They tend to be easier to set up.

2.  Many nx series laptops still come with a PCMCIA card slot, so you have the option of adding cards, like wireless cards, (that's what I did until I got my built in Broadcom card working.)  The other laptop you listed, does not, it comes with a newer PCI express card slot which is not compatible with PCMCIA hardware, and has very limited Linux support.

3.  Finally, I think that the nx series hardware is generally more Linux friendly than HP's consumer models.  That's just my opinion and others may have differing opinions.  Hope this helps.

Joe
