Hello everybody.

A cousin is planning to purchase a laptop in few weeks and asked me for advice. He does not want to spend a lot of money on it because his computing needs aren't really great: he only needs to do basic internet and office stuff. So a budget PC will do.

In a local store I found an [HP 500 RQ258AA laptop]("http://www.digitalife.com.mx/detalle.php?id=3453&promocion=0") with the following specs:
Processor Intel Celeron M 350M 1.3GHz
Memory DDR2: 256MB.
Intel Graphics Media Accelerator 900 128 MB
40GB 4200RPM HD.
DVD/CD-RW
Audio: Conexant CX20468
Intel® PRO/Wireless 2200BG 802.11 b/g.

The best part of this is that it is OS free (no Windows!) and the price is around USD$550 (its the cheapest we've found). I know this won't be by far as fast as a Core Duo system but considering his current machine is a Pentium 100MHz with Windows 95 he will find it really fast. Besides I would be able to convert him to Linux and here is where my question arises:

**Is this machine enough to run Feisty? Has anybody put Ubuntu in a machine like this? Known issues perhaps? Is it really difficult to install Ubuntu in an HP laptop?**

### Post by teddmeister2 on 2007-06-24
normal ubuntu would probably work fine too.  The only problem you might have is a little slowness in opening programs, considering your 256 megs of RAM.  But my wife has a desktop with 256 MB of ram and an onboard graphics chip that runs ubuntu beautifully, and even runs Beryl (the fancy 3d Desktop Cube program) without any issues, except the one I already mentioned.
Also, as a laptop, it's pretty ideal for ubuntu in terms of driver availability, since both your wireless card and your graphics accelerator are intel.

Hope your cousin enjoys the beauty of software freedom.

P.S. I just saw a laptop with similar specs go for less than $400 on ebay.  So be careful.
