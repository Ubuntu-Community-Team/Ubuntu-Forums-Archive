### Post by Keith_Beef on 2007-12-26
> **roger_1960 said:**
> .does anyone know how to make the Caps Lock, when enabled, make the top row numbers work (rather than the upper case of the &, é etc)  Normally this is how french keyboards work.  I have tried various optional settings but with no joy.

In my experience, the shift key is what gets you the numbers. I'm not familiar with the layout of French laptop keyboards, only with full-size keyboards. Does your keyboard have separate keys marked "maj." and "verr. maj."?

These abbreviations are respectively, "majuscules" for "capitals" (in place of shift) and "verrouillage majuscules" for "caps lock".

Aside, a symbol such as "&" has no case, and in French typography, accents are usually omitted from upper-case letters, so that "é" capitalises to "E" and not to "É" in most cases.
