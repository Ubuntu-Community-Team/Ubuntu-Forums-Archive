### Post by w4ett on 2007-09-29
There are some suggestions for benchmarking your card in this thread:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-11925.html](http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-11925.html)

68 degrees is not such a bad temp  level (saying that, don't know which card you are using)  One thing you might consider doing is to apply secondary heatsinks to your memory chips on the card as well, and possibly an upgraded GPU fan (this can never hurt)

Make sure all your power and ide/sata cables are neatly bundled and positioned so as to promote good airflow in the case.  If you have the space available, an additional case fan is a cheap way to ensure the best possible cooling.

Good Luck
