### Post by onlineapps on 2007-09-29
I'm running Kubuntu Feisty on a Dell Dimension 4700 with a ATI Radeon X300 card and the fgrlx driver from the repos at 1280x1024.  The monitor is (I think) a Dell 1704FPV.  Recently, I messed around with fglrx from ATI.com, and then uninstalled that and reinstalled fglrx from the repos.  Before, I couldn't get into 1280x1024 (max was 1024x768), but now I can.  So that's what I'm in now.  Unfortunately, anytime an app goes into full screen (like a game in WINE or VDrift), the screen goes black and displays:

Analog input cannot display this video mode

Dell claims "This means that the monitor cannot synchronize with the signal that it is receiving from the computer. Either the signal is too high or too low for the monitor to use.  See Specifications for the Horizontal and Vertical frequency ranges addressable by this monitor. Recommended mode is 1280 X 1024 @ 60Hz."

But dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg had it at 60Hz (as did KControl).  So, what's going on?
