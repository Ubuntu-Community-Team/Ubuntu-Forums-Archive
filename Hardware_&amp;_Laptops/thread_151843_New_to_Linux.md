### Post by jml on 2006-03-28
I would have to agree with ezworldwide.  I have been playing with Linux for about 5 years.  I have never been able to a Linksys card to work with Linux.  I also gave up on configuring ndiswrapper.  Never could get it to work for me.  The card I would recommend is the Netgear WG511T.  Its an 802.11G cand and is recognized by Linux without needing to install any other drivers.  You just have to go into network manager  to configure and activate the card.  Ubuntu supports unencrypted wireless and WEP encrypted networks.  WPA is not natively supported.  For WPA you need to download an application called wpa_supplicant.  Install and configure it.  Hope that this helps.
