### Post by Decade on 2006-11-17
Intel graphics should be the best driver and the worst accelerated speed. Nvidia proprietary drivers should be better than ATI, but ATI is better at letting third parties make slow open-source drivers, maybe a few years after you get the laptop (No AIGLX for you!).

Intel should have the best restricted-distribution-binary-blob wireless driver. Broadcom wireless is bad but a free driver might be able to make it work slowly without authentication or encryption. Atheros also seems flaky, and Intersil is unpredictable if you find it; Conexant is evil like Broadcom.

I haven't much heard about Acer and HP (Quanta, Compal, Wistron, Inventec, Mitac, Asus design customers anyway? Not just manufacturing like Apple), but "Toshiba" and "overheat" come together too much for comfort. IBM is gorgeous and never on sale.

Core 2 seems to be like 10% faster than Core, but it also has x86-64, which slows it down a little in some benchmarks and speeds it up in others (Disable macro-op fusion vs more ISA registers, I guess), and is ooh so 64-bit.
