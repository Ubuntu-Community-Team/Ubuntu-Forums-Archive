### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-09-02
I've had a lot of success with Dell laptops; I think their hardware is sufficiently mainstream to make them easy to work with. Ubuntu installed perfectly on an XPS M170, and older machines -- like 8000-series and Latitude CP machines -- are equally easy.

My worst Ubuntu laptop experience was with an HP zv8000, but I blame that on the fact that it came with an ATI XPress 200m card, and I just couldn't get it properly configured. It worked, just not the way I wanted.

My advice would be to stay off the bleeding edge of technology. For what I've seen, it seems the just-released-yesterday gadgets are what disappoint people the most, although in most cases it's just a matter of time until they work with Ubuntu.

And if I was buying a new laptop ... I'd definitely check out a [system76]("http://www.system76.com").
