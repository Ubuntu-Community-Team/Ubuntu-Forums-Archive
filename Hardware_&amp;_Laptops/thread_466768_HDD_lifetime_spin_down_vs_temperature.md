### Post by kilou on 2007-06-07
2 things seem to kill down a HDD: high temperature and spinning down/up too often

I'm not using spinning down for now on my laptop because to me spinning down/up hurts the drive. However my constant spinning drive can run as hot as 48-50°C sometimes after (it's maximum rated temp is 55°C, it's a 7200rpm Hitachi 60Gb drive). If the disk was spinning down this would allow its temperature to get cooler.

So basically I wonder what's best for the drive: 

- preventing it from spinning down/up but allow it to reach high temperature

or

- avoiding high temperature (by allowing spinning down) at the expense of higher mechanical stress during spinning down/up??

Any idea? I must say I'm slightly worried by the temperature of 48°C...but spinning down/up doesn't look too good either

### Post by Syke on 2007-06-07
I doubt you'll find a hard and fast rule for which is better. You are still running in spec, so you're probably better off leaving it on full time.

Another thing to consider is the annoying delay when the drive has to spin up when you go to use it.

Try for better ventilation. Clean the dust out of the fans. Don't obstruct the vents. You probably have a laptop, but if it's a desktop there are ways to improve the cooling of your system.
