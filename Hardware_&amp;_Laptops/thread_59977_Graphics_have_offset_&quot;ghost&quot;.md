### Post by damonkohler on 2005-08-31
Ah, found the solution:

Ghost like image: Inexpensive video extension cables cause this effect. (Usually white in colour) If you need to extend your video cable, you will need to purchase a low-capacitance video cable. These are usually black in colour and are more expensive. These cables have low signal loss and provide better signal from the video card.
If you do not have an extender cable hooked up, try turning the contrast down in small increments until desired image quality is obtained. In some cases a video card may try to overdrive a monitor and by reducing contrast you are able to obtain a better image quality.

[http://www.kdscanada.ca/Misc/crtmon.htm#ghosting](http://www.kdscanada.ca/Misc/crtmon.htm#ghosting)
