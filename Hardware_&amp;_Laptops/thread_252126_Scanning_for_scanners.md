### Post by jinxed on 2006-09-06
One piece of hardware I own has been keeping me from getting rid of WinXP once and for all --- my scanner. I own a Canon Canoscan 5000F, which, if you check the Sane web site, is an unsupported scanner. Searching the Canon website yields a lame response to an FAQ stating that Canon has no intention of supporting Linux at this time.

This experience has led me to investigate other options. I started by perusing the supported scanner list on the Sane web site. But, this list has one major problem (IMHO), many (if not most) of the scanners on that list are obsolete, and it takes forever to go through the list to find a scanner that's not obsolete. 

This leads me here, seeking advice from the wise Ubuntu community. My question is this: which scanner(s) have you used with Linux, and are there any you would recommend? Why?

### Post by zxee on 2006-09-06
I've used several usb HP scanners in linux and all have worked well. I would recommend that you give ebay a look some of those "obsolete" scanners can be obtained at reasonable cost and are, I think, very servicable.
I would take a look at the sane hp list [http://www.sane-project.org/cgi-bin/driver.pl?manu=HP&model=&bus=any&v=&p=](http://www.sane-project.org/cgi-bin/driver.pl?manu=HP&model=&bus=any&v=&p=) and only look for a scanner in which the support is listed as complete e.g 4200 & 5200 good luck.
