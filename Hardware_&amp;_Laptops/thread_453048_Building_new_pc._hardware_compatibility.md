### Post by nss0000 on 2007-05-25
JB:

First point is a "hunch". Yes I know-da-numbers giving INTELs Core-Duo a significant advantage over AMD. Nothing to argue there ... but I see an analogy.

The AMD_3200+ was the finest ( stable, flexible, quick...) chip of its' generation, and will comfortably power 'legacy' x32 Linux systems for the next couple years. 
I believe the AMD6000+ holds the same position w.r.t. x2x62 kit.  With a strong (SLI) mobo and 4-G ram that AMD6000 will provide superb function five years from now. The price doesn't suckerpunch you now, and that extra SLI_slot will allow an aftermarket "GCPU"!   IMVHO

Overclocking is fun-&-games no doubt, but I wouldn't consider it for 'serious' kit.

By "2x4" I mean #-of-cpus <times> #-of-cores/cpu. So I'm talking OPTERON or ZENON kit and a supporting mobo ( say  Tyan or ASUS ) with two cpu slots. **BTW** I'm not convinced that sufficient multi_threaded apps will be available to make 4-core more useful than 2-core + (sli)GCPU.
