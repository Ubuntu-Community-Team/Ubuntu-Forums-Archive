### Post by ryantmer on 2007-06-23
Over the pas few days I have tried to install (I kid you not) about 13 Linux distros onto an old Pentium 133MHz, 32MB RAM, 3GB HDD, 2MB video IBM laptop. Obviously, this has not gone so well. The majority of the distros, as they are on LiveCDs, won't even boot due to lack of memory. I tried adding more memory, but the smallest size of SODIMM that I have is 64MB. Unfortunately, when you add 64 to the 32 that is built in, that makes 96MB of RAM... which is  6MB above the BIOS limit. >_>

So now I have a conundrum. I can't boot anything, really, as many non-LiveCD installers are also graphical, and too much for this laptop. I would love to get Ubuntu running on it (my favourite distro, no doubt), but I don't even think it would run 7.04 or 6.10. Is there any way I could get my hands on an old release, maybe from at least 5 years ago or so, of Ubuntu? I've looked around the Ubuntu site and Google, but can't seem to find anywhere to get them from. Or, if anybody has a 32MB SODIMM chip that they'd let me "borrow", that would be nice too... >_>

Thanks.

### Post by raja on 2007-06-23
Dont even waste your time trying live cds with that much RAM. Use a text-based installer. It comes as the alternate cd in Ubuntu. I would suggest xubuntu - it has xfce as the window manager. Also almost all distros have the option of doing a text-based installation.
