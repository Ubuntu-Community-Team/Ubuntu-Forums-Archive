### Post by brundlelinux on 2007-10-14
I'm also having issues/frustration with Dell and their printers with use on Ubuntu.  After several hours of research, here's what I've come up with...

"Dell" printers aren't really Dell printers at all.  They are Lexmarks.  Dell has a deal with Lexmark to "rebrand" certain models of their printers as "Dell" printers and sells them as such.  So, technically, a Dell AIO 966 is really a Lexmark model whatever, but dressed up for Halloween in a Dell costume.  The issue here is that Lexmark has chosen, as company policy, to be VERY non-supportive of the open-source concept and guards their proprietary drivers very closely, refusing to adapt drivers for use on ANY Linux flavor / distro.  Obviously, this means that Dell cannot provide any drivers for their "Dell" printers for use on Linux either.

So, as cool as a "Dell" printer may be, and no matter how awesome it works on Windows.... it's nothing but a paperweight when applied to Linux.  Sadly, this shows NO sign of ever changing, unless Lexmark decided to crawl into contemporary times and embrace Linux / open-source.

My only suggestion is to contact Dell and see if you can't return / exchange your printer for a brand that supports linux.  Hewlett-Packard is VERY Linux friendly and almost every HP model works perfectly with Linux.
