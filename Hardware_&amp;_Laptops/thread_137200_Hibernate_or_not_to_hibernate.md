### Post by jimbren on 2006-02-27
I use Kubuntu exclusively on my laptop at home, and I don't use hibernate.  It works well enough, for the most part, though there are some problems that a) I either haven't devoted enough time to fixing, or b) just don't work right.

Here's a few of them:
1) startup time: there isn't any real speed advantage that I've found from hibernating rather than just shutting down. There's maybe a 30 second difference in speed between the two.

2) My USB ports always die.  I'm sure this is something I could fix if I poked, I just don't care enough to do so.

3) Display is flaky.  Sometimes I come back up with no display at all, and can't get it back until I reboot.  Again, something I could fix, probably, but don't care to spend the time.

4) Apps aren't saved in session where I left off.  If I leave an application open, like say, Gedit or OpenOffice or whatever, the application will open when I come out of hibernation, but the document doesn't.  That really doesn't help me much.

All in all, I just don't see much of an advantage.
