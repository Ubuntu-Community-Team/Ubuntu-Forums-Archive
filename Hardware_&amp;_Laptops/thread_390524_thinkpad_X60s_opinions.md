### Post by auroraborealis on 2007-04-15

### Post by Kokopelli on 2007-04-15
I have had an X60s since release.  Quite literally I have one of the first production runs of this laptop and it has performed flawlessly for me.  I stumbled upon this thread trying to figure out if (with updated BIOS) I could upgrade to 4GB of RAM and thought I would chip in.

With wireless off and brightness down I get about 4:30 on battery.  3:00 and change with wireless on.  But this is not a new battery at this stage (holding 55260mWh from the design capacity of 74880)

I am currently running Feisty on it and  I have everything working with the possible exception of the SD slot, but I have not tried that in over a year now.
- Finger Print reader works (using thinkfinger 3.0)
- USB/Firewire works
-  sound works for me
-  I do not experience the high pitched noise on battery w/ Bluetooth on, but also do not have the most sensitive of hearing.
-  Suspend always works for me when I trigger it from XFCE, but stopped working when I just close the lid.  Not sure exactly when, but during the Feisty dev cycle.

Limitations:
- Fn + home/end also has stopped working for me, though it was working prior to the last week.
- can run Dual Monitor or Beryl/Compiz, but not both.
- can undock and redock from the slice fine, but on redock the DVD drive is not recognized.
- The gigabit ethernet does not reinitialize when waking from suspend, currently I "sudo rmmod e1000;sudo modprobe e1000" and it works fine (though I do find it inconvenient.)
- VMWare server interfaces are also dropped on suspend and resume, for this I restart vmware. Looking for a better solution to this one.

I do not find the laptop gets too hot, though it can get quite warm, especially on the bottom.  On the other hand it is fairly cool as far as modern laptops go.  I will sometimes force the fan to 7, which keeps it cool enough to rest on my bare skin while in bed without discomfort.

Finally I strongly advise you go for the 7200RPM 100GB drive, it makes a significant difference in responsiveness.
