### Post by Zars on 2007-11-03
I bought a Dell 6400n for educational purposes, and it's great. The reason I went with Dell is 1) I like to choose the spec of my laptops 2) the warranty will not go void just because it has linux on it, and 3) buying from a multi-nation (sept Microsoft...) is like being hugged by a warm cuddly bear. 

If something goes wrong, I phone the foreign people at the tech help desk, send it to them and get it fixed for free! As I had bought through Dell before, I got 4 years 'wear and tear' warranty for the price of one year (£40).

I know that it is more expensive, but you are buying the badge a bit too. I found the customer service great, the laptop came 2 weeks before they said it would, and I managed to get the carbon-fiber Snap On cover free!

I see your point, but so far, I'm very happy with my purchase.

Zars.
