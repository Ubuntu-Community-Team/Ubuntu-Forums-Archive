### Post by Super TWiT on 2007-07-26
I am switching from a Celeron to a Pentium 4 because in the process of applying a new coat of thermal grease a pin broke off of it. When the new processor is put in should I reinstall Ubuntu?  And if I don't reinstall will I get the most performance out of my new CPU?

---

### Post by az on 2007-07-26
If that's the only thing that has changed, no, you don't need to do anything.  The kernel uses whatever features are present in the cpu and detects that at boot time.

If you were to change video cards or hard drive location, you would need to reconfigure some stuff.

### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-07-26
NO, that's the grand thing about linux, you can change anything and boot it up.  As long as the hardware is compatible it'll work, there's no more having to call MicroSloth to reactivate or what have you.

I actually changed my motherboard, my CPU, and my RAM about 6 months ago and she fired right up!!! I went from a Foxconn cheapy, Celeron D 2.4ghz, 1.5gb DDR 400 ram to a ASRock motherboard, Core2Duo 1  1.8ghz, 2gb DDRII 533 ram. What an improvement!!!
