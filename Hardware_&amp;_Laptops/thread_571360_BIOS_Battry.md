### Post by ddrichardson on 2007-10-09
You don't state the model, but checking with ACER will probably give a solution. The CMOS battery is usually a small circular type, but often the mainboard will have a jumper setting to reset the CMOS - listed in the manual.

With laptops this isn't always the case and you may need to contact the manufacturer. There are ways around BIOS passwords - particularly for Pheonix BIOS's which have backdoors. I'm not going to discuss them on a public forum - you can google them though.
