### Post by drpaul on 2007-03-09
Here is what I would try:

Look at the Device Manager

In the list of things there will be a hard disk [under either SCSI for SATA or IDE for ATA]

That should give you the info you need to look up its characteristics so you can buy a compatible model.

OTOH, you could also remove the plate [usually on the bottom], and look at the drive. Then buy the same or similar model.

HTH

Paul

### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-03-09
Sorry to hear about your hard drive troubles.  To answer your question about what hard drive to get, pretty much any notebook hard drive with the ATA-6 / ATA100 specification should work in your notebook pc.  One thing you may want to keep in mind though is that larger capacity hard drives tend to be more prone to disk errors and general failures becuase they are far more dense than smaller capacity drives.  

I would recommend replacing your old hard drive with an 80 GB drive.  Less power consumption, less heat = greater reliability.  I have been using Western Digital drives for the past 3 years in my pc's and they work great.  I have 2 SATA'S in my desktop pc and 1 PATA in my home server and they work great.  Quiet and fast.  

I have purchased all of my pc components from [www.newegg.com](www.newegg.com).  I'm not sure if they ship to the UK (I live in the U.S.).  I can't say enough about their service, fast shipping, great prices. 

Something like this should do the trick for ya.
