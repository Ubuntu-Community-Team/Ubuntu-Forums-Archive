### Post by saintjohnny on 2007-03-10
Hello, A fellow thinkpad user?  The exact reason for your problem (ironic that you mentioned it) IS the ATI driver.  The default driver will not effect your usb mouse, but the fglrx driver will.  I have dealt with this D*MN problem for some time now.  I chose to have video hardware drivers over mouse support.  Its basically choose one or the other right now.  Some have said disabling legacy USB in the BIOS will fix it, this has not worked for me.  I'm gonna try the next release coming up (might be already here?)  and see if they changed anything.  I dont have a PS/2 port otherwise i'd use my USB to PS/2 adapter.  (I'm assuming that would bypass any USB issues.)  

Good luck!

### Post by saintjohnny on 2007-03-10
Oh yeah I forgout about adding this to your startup line when you reboot your PC and select which ubuntu to boot, you can type E to edit the startup stuff.  

Adding "noapic" AND "irqpoll" to the end most likely right after "splash" (for the splash logo)  Also the irqpoll bit is to prevent the broken network reaction to noapic  Just remember to hit "B" to boot, because if you escape out too far, the edit wont be remembered.

"digitalp1mp" on this board found the above solution.  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1738666](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1738666)

As i'm typing this i've added those and it seems to be working for record time 23 minutes...Weird thing is I tried that a while back and it didn't work.  Let me know if this works or if you find any other solutions bro.
