> [SIZE=-1]A part of the motherboard that maintains system variables in static RAM. **It also supplies a real-time clock that keeps track of the date, day and time.** CMOS Setup is typically accessible by entering a specific sequence of keystrokes during the POST at system start-up.
[[COLOR=#008000]www.ontrack.com/glossary[/COLOR]]("http://www.ontrack.com/glossary")[COLOR=#008000]  [/COLOR][/SIZE]

I usually call it the BIOS, though I don't know if there's a difference between the two terms.  Basically, when you start a computer, it comes up before launching the operating system.  My laptop is a Dell, so I hit F12 repeatedly (usually) to get into the CMOS settings (or BIOS settings).  

I hope that helps!  :)
