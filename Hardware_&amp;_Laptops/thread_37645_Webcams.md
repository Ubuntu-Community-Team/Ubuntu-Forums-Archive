### Post by kancept on 2005-05-30
I have an older IBM webcam- nothing spectacular.  It woreks without doing a thing.  Well, I have to plug it into the USB port, but after that, GnomeMeeting sees it without issues.  Of course I don't webcam to anyone in particular, but those folks are running GnomeMeeting, too, which is flaky overall.  Not really spectacular.  

Now the way we "webcam'd" on another OS I run is to use yer vidcam (or webcam if it was an RCA input) into your vidcap board, and stream your audio.  Each person would fire up their music player, log into each other's audio stream.  The vidcams would actually be capturing and loading images to a webpage.   ;-)   Not real time, but real effective.
