### Post by qriopal on 2008-01-07
Hi, 

I am using Ubuntu 7.10 on a IBM Thinkpad T20 with 512MB RAM (single boot, no Windows on HD). For the last two days, I am having a strange problem that I havent quite figured out whether its a software related problem or a hardware related one. All of a sudden, without any particular warning, my mouse freezes up (I move the mouse but the mouse cursor on the screen does not move) and simultaneously my keyboard stops responding and the system seems to crash (although the screen displays whatever it had been displaying before the system crashed.) I tried using Ctr-Alt-Backspace and nothing happens. So I power it down by pressing the power button. When I restart, I am unable to boot. It gives me the the following message: 
kernel panic - not syncing: Attempted to kill the idle task! 

I googled the kernel panic and somewhere found that people have resolved the problem by taking the RAM sticks out and switching their places in the two slots (putting the RAM stick in slot 1 into slot 2 and vice versa). I did that and restarted and was able to book again in Ubuntu. But after about an hour of use, again my mouse froze and simultaneously my keyboard froze just as last time. So I again power down and switch the places of my RAM sticks and boot it. Works fine again for 5 minutes before crashing. It has been happening for several times now and I havent figured out where the problem is. Some people in some other forums suggested that it might be the powersupply. Indeed, I recently moved to Europe although my AC adapter for my thinkpad is for US sockets but it says that it works with input  of 110 and 220 Volts. I had been using 220V and so after this problem came up, I decided to see if plugging it behind a 400Watt 220-to-110V transformer would make any difference. No difference at all. I need to keep opening the RAM door under my laptop and swap places for the two RAM sticks before I can boot after my computer hangs up. I tried to boot it with a Damn Small Linux live CD to see if the problem is with Ubuntu as installed in my hard disk but my computer hangs up the same way even when I boot it with a live CD. 

I wonder if my laptop is totally useless now or I can still get it back to normal. Can anyone help me resolve this issue or atleast suggest a possible cause of the problem? Thank you.
