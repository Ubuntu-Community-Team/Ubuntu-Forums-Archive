### Post by jml on 2006-05-23
You can determine the ESSID of your wireless access point by logging into the setup screen of the router.  You will probably need to have the access point's administrative password and need to be hardwired to the point through an ethernet cable.  If you search through the set up screens, you should be able to determine the ESSID along with the other settings.  If you still are having problems, check out this link.  Its the Ubuntu Wireless Troubleshooting Wiki and may be of help to you.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WirelessTroubleshootingGuide](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WirelessTroubleshootingGuide)
