### Post by n8bounds on 2007-04-25
Feisty installed great on my HP/Compaq nc4200!  I had to do one little thing though.  The card reader was not working, so I put:

```
 setpci -s 02:06.3 4c.b=02 
```

in my /etc/rc.local file (BEFORE the line that says "exit 0")

after a reboot, the card mounted fine (I have a Texas Instruments PCI6411-ish Secure Digital Controller)

I don't claim to understand all the details, but this command tells the kernel to use its driver instead of the kernel module one that was failing to address it properly...I think.  If you know any better, please enlighten me.


Other joys:  the wireless card joined WPA2 networks out of the box and the graphics card works so well that beryl installed without a problem.  Oh, and it resumes from suspend REALLY fast.  (Usually about 7 seconds.)
