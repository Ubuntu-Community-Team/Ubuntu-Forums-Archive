### Post by n0dl on 2006-09-27
My dell inspiron 6400 just arrived last week and I have noticed that my hard drive makes a soft, quiet, yet noticable clicking sound whenever the cylindrical green light flashes (and sometimes not). Does anyone else experience this? People tell me this is usually a sign of impending hdd failure. Other sources tell me its due to the hard drive "parking heads". I tried setting hdparm -B 255 but that didnt stop the clicking sound. When I ran the Dell diagnosis cd it passed all the tests. Furthermore, my hdd was automatically fskced (fsck after 3o mounts) and everything went fine (data in tact and everything). So, I am not sure if the sound is characteristic of the hard drive or its a sign of impending failure. Anyone who has experience with Samsung laptop harddrives or has a dell inspiron 6400 (or e1505 i think), can tell me what the hard drive noise is (or if they experience the noise as well) please respond :)

### Post by listerthrawn on 2006-09-28
I wouldn't worry about this too much.  I've just got one of these laptops and haven't noticed the noise but will listen out for it.

My previous laptop when I first got it made sickening noises from the hard drive that got me running for some backup software.  I've now had it for 3 years, still going, still makes the noise, never lost one bit of data.  Seems to be something that laptop harddrives do.  I bet it is something like head parking and spin down to save battery.

Don't let it affect your sleep ;)
