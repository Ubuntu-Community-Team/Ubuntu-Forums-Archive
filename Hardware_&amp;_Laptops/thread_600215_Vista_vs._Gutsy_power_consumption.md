### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2008-01-14
You could probably get better battery life on Vista if you experiment with a program called CrystalCPUID. Manufacturers' voltage specs tend to be vey conservative. Example: my mom's Athlon 3500 is spec'd to run at 1GHz @ 1.1V and 2.2GHz @ 1.35V
I was able to undervolt it to 1GHz @ 0.9V and 2.2GHz @ 1.125V (Prime95 stable for >8 hours)

The difference might not be so pronounced on a laptop chip, but remember that power drawn is proportional to the square of the core voltage. Small drops = big savings.
