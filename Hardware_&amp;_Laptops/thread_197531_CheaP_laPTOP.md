### Post by rokky on 2006-06-15
I don't think this forum is meant for hardware issues like that (I may be wrong as a newcomer, but it seems likely) since it is not really relevant to Ubuntu, or even Linux issues.   Check out the newsgroup, comp.sys.laptops.

Anyway, here's a "freebie":  my experience with a number of laptop "projects" is that usually there are screws holding it together that must be accessed from the underside - often Torx bits are needed (esp for Compaqs), but just as often Phillips heads.  Flip it over, and look carefully with a bright overhead light for recessed holes.  There might even be arrows embossed in the casing pointing at the screws if you are lucky.

BTW, if you want more relevant advice over on comp.sys.laptops, specifying the brand and model number is more likely to get specific info - laptops are not anything at all alike.

Good luck.
Rokky.
