### Post by zyth on 2007-09-07
Laptops in general have more closed and restrictive hardware - system manufacturers don't always release specifications for the BIOS on these systems, and the individual pieces are highly specific.  With desktops you have commodity parts, and many people have them.  Less people have a specific laptop model.  There are many reasons, but these are some of them.   Laptop hardware is also often customized by the OEM in some way or another ('Mobile' graphics chipsets, etc)
