### Post by thrift on 2005-05-30
X(your graphics server) probably isn't aware of what your graphics card/monitor combination is capable of.  One way to attempt to fix this is to right click on your desktop and then click open terminal.  now type:

sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/Xorg.conf.backup

enter your password and hit enter.

sudo gedit

enter your password and hit enter.

Now gedit is running as the root user.  The first line created a backup copy of a file we are about to edit.

Open /etc/X11/xorg.conf (click open, in the top right there are probably three buttons such as 
[] [home] [yourusername]

you want to click [] then click etc in the window right below that, then locate X11 in that window and click that, then find xorg.conf in the same window and click open)

Look for 

Section "Screen" 

Once you have found this go down more and you will find several lines like:

Modes "640x480"

You want to change all those line to represent all the modes your monitor can handle, here is mine for an example:

Modes	"1600x1200" "1280x1024" "1280x960" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"

This will allow X to be aware of what modes it can attempt to run at.

I'm not sure how exactly to specify the refresh rates to run at, but something that may help you is telling X to use DPMS to ask the monitor what Horizontal Sync and Vertical Refresh to run at.  To do that you can locate

Section "Monitor"
blah
blah
blah
EndSection

Right before the EndSection if it's not in there already, try adding

Option "DPMS"

On it's own line.

Save the file.

Now you can log out and try to log back in and see if that helped.



This could make X refuse to start, if that happens simply login on the terminal(which it will take you to if X can't start).

sudo cp /etc/X11/Xorg.conf.backup /etc/X11/xorg.conf

enter password

sudo /etc/init.d/gdm restart

enter password

This should restore our backup and restart X.

Good luck.  Hope this helps you out.

### Post by thrift on 2005-05-31
Try going to system->preferences->screen resolution and see if you can change the resolution there..  

If you can't change your resolution there, X may not know the correct video driver or  the monitor settings.  The easiest thing to check can be if 

Option "DPMS" 

is in /etc/X11/xorg.conf file in  

Section "Monitor"
blah
blah
blah
EndSection

If it isn't you could try putting that in there.  Another 2 options to try in that same section are

VertRefresh x
HorizSync y

With x being your monitors vertical refresh and y being your monitors horizonal sync.

You may also want to check in

Section "Device"
blah
Driver "drivername"
blah
EndSection

and make sure the correct driver is set for your card.  You can just switch the

Driver "drivername" 

for your driver.

For a list of X drivers, check /usr/X11R6/lib/modules/drivers

these files without the _drv.o part at then end of their name are the names of all the different drivers X can use.

Hope you find some of that helpfull

### Post by thrift on 2005-05-31
.will
your vertical refresh and horizontal sync are two numbers or possibly two numbers ranges with a dash between them, such as

VertRefresh 50
HorizSync 30-70

You can possibly find them by looking on the back of your monitor.

You may also be able to find them by looking at the windows driver(inf file) for your monitor and looking inside the file for the vertical refresh and horizontal sync.

You could also search for your monitor model on google and see if you can find someone else who has already found these numbers.  It's quite possible you'll find an xorg.conf with a comment that mentions your monitor model with the VertRefresh and HorizSync lines right below it.

These numbers almost always make X behave correctly in regards to resolution.

bpilgrim1979
I've never had to do this before, but i think I found what you are looking for. In your xorg.conf look for 

Section "Serverlayout"
   blah
   Screen          "Default Screen" 
   blah
   blah
EndSection

You can add arguments to the Screen keyword like the following.

Screen "Default Screen" Absolute x y

with x and y representing the coordinates of the upperleft hand corner.

I found this with a man xorg.conf.  I'd recommend you do the same to be sure of the effect of this option.  Unfortunately I've never tried this myself.  Anyone else have any ideas?  Tell us how/if this worked.

Good luck to the both of you.
