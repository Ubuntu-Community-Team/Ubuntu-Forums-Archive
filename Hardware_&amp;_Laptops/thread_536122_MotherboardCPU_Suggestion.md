I have a [Antec P150]("http://www.antec.com/us/productDetails.php?ProdID=81500") case at home, and it currently has an old mobo and AMD processor.

[IMG]http://www.antec.com/images/160/P150_q.jpg[/IMG]

I want to replace the motherboard and processor with something more powerful and stable. Let me tell you about the rest of the existing hardware in the box.

I have 3 IDE hard drives I'd like to keep in this machine. The motherboard is going to need IDE controllers for these 3 drives. My case has hard drive bays, so expansion into the 4th would be great.

I have 2 IDE disk drives already in this server. I'd like room to add a third, but I imagine 4 IDE controllers on one mobo might be hard to come by. I have a total of 3 spots for 5 1/2 drives.

Note: I realize SATA is better and faster, but I just don't have the $$ to upgrade all the drives.

I'd like the motherboard to have onboard graphics (preferably intel for linux support), onboard ethernet, audio, etc etc.

I have a few PCI nic cards I'd like to take advantage of. I also have an ATI RAGE XL PCI card that might be used for dual monitor support. I am also exploring the possibility of making this a MythTV server, so I'll need the card slots for a couple of TV tuners in the future.

If you notice the specs on the case I linked, it has two audio jacks (headphone and mic), 2 USB jacks, and one firewire jack on the front. The mobo will need the appropriate pins to connect to these.

I have a total of 3 case fans as well, so extra control over these via the mobo would be nice, but not required. 

In the end, I'm looking to justify all the noise and power this machine uses (with a 450 watt PSU) by turning it into a beast of a server for my media needs at home.
