### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2008-04-09
Your system should be just fine unless the PSU is really junk or you have an OC'd Pentium D. Under normal operation, desktops disks generally use about 8W at idle and no more than 15W during seek.

If the power draw of the drives was going to be a problem, you would probably figure it out when your system wouldn't turn on because of the power surge from the drives. If you're using a SATA power connector (a native one with a 3.3V line, not an adapter) and the BIOS & drives support it, then that's even better because a lot of drives will do a staggered spin-up ([http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Staggered_spinup](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Staggered_spinup))
