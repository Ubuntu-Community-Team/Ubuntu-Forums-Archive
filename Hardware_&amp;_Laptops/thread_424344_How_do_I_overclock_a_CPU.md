### Post by Zuph on 2007-04-26
Your motherboard's BIOS has to support it, and your memory has to be robust enough.

You usually go in and change either the FSB speed or the clock multiplier.  On some chips and motherboards, one or both of these are locked.  The CPU speed is the FSB speed multiplied by the clock multiplier.  Depending on your CPU, you might get anywhere between a 0% and 100% overclock.  It's based a lot on your particular CPU, particular RAM, particular motherboard, etc.
