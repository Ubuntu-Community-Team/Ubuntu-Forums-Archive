### Post by mapman on 2005-08-31
Try this on the command line: sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg


Make sure you have your horizontal and vertical scanning frequencies for your monitor handy so you can enter it in when prompted for the advanced monitor settings towards the end of the configuration.  It's not entirely necessary but it helped me for problems i encountered.

Your might want to check out this link ( [http://www.ubuntuguide.org/#installnvidiadriver](http://www.ubuntuguide.org/#installnvidiadriver) ) afterwards to install and enable the nvidia drivers.

Hope that helps!

 When the 7667 drivers didn't work for me I was able to revert back to the nvidia-glx driver by doing the uninstall from the 7667 driver.  Look it up on the nVidia site.
