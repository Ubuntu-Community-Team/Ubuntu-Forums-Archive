### Post by BrianElliott0218 on 2008-01-02
You're kind of jumping in with both feet aren't you?  :-)

I think if you have two drives and decide you want to do a dual boot system, you will find that no OS conflicts will occur.  If you get a drive drawer, or something similar, you can just swap out the HDD and never have an issue with a dual boot system.  It won't be dual boot, just two dedicated boot drives.

As for hardware issues with Ubuntu, I think many of them can be avoided altogether by doing a straight up install on a drive (which works well with a dual dedicated drive solution as above),

You used to be able to find a hardware switch for two (and sometimes as many as 4) HDD's to be able to boot.  Just push the button and you boot to a different drive configuration.  I haven't seen these in a while.  If you are technically proficient, you may be able to put in a simple swith solution too.  Just get a drive cable convertions and stick them together.  Sort of like the old parallel printer switch boxes...

Good luck!  And Happy New Year!:guitar:
