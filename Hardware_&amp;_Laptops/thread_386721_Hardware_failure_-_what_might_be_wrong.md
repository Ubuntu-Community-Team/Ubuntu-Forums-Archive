### Post by hardyn on 2007-03-17
desktop?

discrete grahics? (not integrated)?

how comfortable are you taking it apart?

- Cables Cables Cables!
- is it not possibly the monitor?
- reseat the video card if possible... push the video card back into the slot, sometimes they wiggle out, esp. if you move the computer alot.
- try flushing the BIOS settings, usually done with a jumper, refer to motherboard manul. (CMOS batter may have died and scrabled the settings)
- remove ALL unnessisary expansion cards... remove everything but the video card, disconnect all but the hard disk.
- reseat ram
- swap ram slots
- borrow ram from a friend, borrow a video card from a friend, borrow a monitor from a friend
-make sure the CPU fan/heat sink has not fallen off
-make sure the CPU has not siezed (alot of motherboards will not start if the CPU fan has 0 RPM)
-check your hard disk connectors

-**did you try to overclock it** -- (you don't have to admit it to us, but sometimes too agressive settings will prevent a boot, see flush bios) 

and finally... 1h of labour at your computer store will likely be cheaper than jumping to a motherboard right away... it might be worth somebody having a look at it.

now above is an example and a SEQUENCE (that got somewhat out of order, i was writing off the top of my head)... do those things one at a time... until the computer starts working again or doesnt.
