### Post by nanotube on 2007-08-27
well, a smaller laptop is easier to carry around... for someone who does computer work in a lot of different places, a smaller package makes up for the smaller screen.

for your actual workstation space, though, throw in another couple hundred bucks, and get another 19-21 inch lcd screen. that way, most of the time, you'll have two screens to work with - and only have to settle for a smaller screen when you are actually on the go.

that said, i myself have a dell 5150, with a 15inch 1600x1200 screen. it's a heavy one, and sometimes i do wish to have a smaller one so it's not such a pain to carry it around... but i also don't have an external lcd screen, though i'm planning on getting one at some point. two screens ftw! :)

### Post by technikalKP on 2007-08-28
I have the different point of view - I can't see me ever wanting a laptop with a screen bigger than 14.1".  

I own 12.1" and a 14.1" laptops.  Both have the same screen resolution so I'm not really giving up any screen real-estate.  I don't notice any difference in heat.  The 12.1" keyboard is comparable, though some of the control/cursor keys aren't as logically located.

I guess if you rarely move the machine, having a large portable as a desktop replacement would be ok.  However, I couldn't imagine lugging a 17"+ laptop around all day.  It wouldn't fit in my briefcase or backpack, wouldn't fit easily on the tray on an airplane or train, etc.  They don't look like they'd be comfortable to use on your lap, either.

So, maybe it's all about portability.  I'll trade screen size for smaller weight, form factor and battery life since I tend to move the computer around a lot.  If I were less mobile, I might be more interested in a larger notebook (or a desktop).
