### Post by JRy1 on 2008-03-15
I just purchased a Dell Inspirion 530s for my grandfather, it came with Windows Vista loaded on it. I was told by the service rep that Ubuntu was not an option they provided for that model, but he made it clear that the machine was entirely compatible with Ubuntu.

I managed to get Ubuntu installed, and it's mostly working perfectly fine. The only issue is that the onboard ethernet port is not being picked up. The motherboard is a Foxconn G33M02, I can't seem to find any drivers or much info at all on it.

Any help would be greatly appreciated.

Thank you

### Post by JRy1 on 2008-03-15
I guess I can sort of consider this issue closed. This desktop is a slim line model, so naturally a regular size PCI Network Card isn't going to fit. I used a pair of snips and cut a few inches of metal brace off of an old NIC card and viola! It works!

Sure it's an unbelievably horrible work-around, but at least my gramps will be on Ubuntu instead of Vista. :)
