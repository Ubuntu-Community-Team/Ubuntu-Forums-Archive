### Post by elizabeth on 2007-07-25
Hey folks,

In IRC we've been discussing having a Philly & SE PA BBQ on August 11th. Several ideas have been floated around as to location, but based on a number of things (cost, location, transportation) we've narrowed it down and are now looking at [Norristown Farm Park]("http://parks.montcopa.org/parks/cwp/view,A,1441,Q,37325,parksNav,|.asp") in East Norriton for a location.

The only downside to this location appears to be the public transportation situation, it's over a mile from the closest bus stop, and even further from the closest train station. We can probably work out some carpooling and/or picking people up from the train station if we plan appropriately though.

I've also dropped an email to the park for some details about their group and grilling policies, but from what I've seen poking around online we should be fine. Hopefully I'll be hearing back today, I'll make a note when I have confirmation that things are good to go.

Next step will be planning food, I figure we can decide on a basic menu and have people sign up to bring stuff.

### Post by elizabeth on 2007-07-25
Hurrah, I got a reply from the park superintendent.
 
> Hi Elizabeth,

The Norristown Farm Park has 690 acres available to those who wish to
come out, enjoy nature, hike, etc.

The two large picnic pavilions are currently reserved throughout the
summer months.

However, there are several areas in the park where smaller picnic
shelters, picnic tables and grills exist.

These areas are not reserved and are first come, first served.

I would recommend that when you and your group come into the park, park
at the Main Visitor's Parking lot, across from the park office and walk
behind the park office.  There are 2 picnic tables and a grill that no
one uses.

There is another small picnic shelter by the other larger pavilions that
also does not get used.

Since you do not live very far from the park, I would recommend coming
out and looking at the accommodations and see if they are suitable for
your club.

Also, as an FYI, there is no alcohol allowed in the park.

If you have any questions, please let me know.

Yours truly,

Jeff Larsen
Superintendent

Looks like this will work out fine. If I can I'll swing by the park sometime before the event.

### Post by elizabeth on 2007-08-05

The place we'll be using is near the main office set of buildings for the park (old farm buildings). There is a parking lot (very rural, not some giant unattractive parking lot) and about 3 picnic tables and a couple grills spread around the area. It'd be nice if they were closer together, but my plan is to have it there because:

[LIST=1]
[*]It's easy to find
[*]It's not the most desirable place in the park, so we won't have a problem getting that spot
[*]Easy parking
[/LIST]

I'd suggest bringing along some folding chairs so we're not all huddled around the tables.

Driving home I remembered we needed stuff other than food! So I added these things to my list on the wiki of what I'm bringing: *ketchup, mustard, relish, plates, napkins, utensils, charcoal, BBQ utensils*.
