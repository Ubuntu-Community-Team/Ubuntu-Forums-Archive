### Post by snowpine on 2008-11-28
Hi everyone, I've been using Crunchbang since 8.04 and recently installed 8.10 on my eee. 

First off, I agree that #! is not "light" in the same sense as SliTaz, DSL, Puppy, etc. Those micro-distros will always have a niche, especially for very old hardware. To say that Crunchbang is "bloated" is to say that Ubuntu itself is bloated. :) If you're curious why Crunchbang is based on Ubuntu, here are its creator's thoughts: [http://crunchbanglinux.org/forums/topic/62/why-not-debian-lenny-as-base-for/](http://crunchbanglinux.org/forums/topic/62/why-not-debian-lenny-as-base-for/)

I am blessed with a brand new computer, so I'm no longer concerned with "which distro works best for 64mb of ram." What I want is a distro that feels "snappy" on modern hardware, has an excellent suite of applications, and helps me to be productive. My favorite thing about #! is that it includes almost every application and codec I need, so I've spent the least time "tweaking" it compared to every other distro I've tried. (Compared with, for example, SliTaz, which gives me the same happy sense of minimalism, but whose base installation doesn't include the ultimedia and office applications I need for work.)

Eragon, I would not worry too much about the "CrunchBang disclaimer." It is true that #! has some bugs, however, most of the bugs and inconveniences I've encountered have been common to Ubuntu and all of its derivatives, not to #! itself. Also, there is a fairly well-tested Hardy version if you feel the Intrepid version is not yet stable enough.

I'll conclude by saying, anyone who follows my posts knows I love Openbox (and Fluxbox), not just for old hardware, but for an efficient and productive experience on modern computers. I really enjoy a productive and distraction-free environment, and CrunchBang delivers.
