### Post by toupeiro on 2007-09-13
The "worlds most advanced OS" title comes primarily in its far superior utilization of core system hardware over any other OS out there.  Nothing can touch solaris for its CPU and RAM efficiency and benchmarks.  It surpasses the 2 largest mainstream linux distro's in server and high end workstation classes (SuSE and RedHat), and if you add the SPARC hardware archtype in the mix, then you're at a plane most x86 or x64 based systems can't compete with.  I've yet to find any single socket (or dual socket), dual or quad core solution that can do databases better than the single socket Sparc T2000 processor for the price and rackspace.

Now, if we're talking about the ability to play World of Warcraft, I can't say solaris is your first choice, but being able to play games doth not the most advanced OS in the world make. :-D


It's all marketing, but in Suns case, they can back it up with real numbers. As with all situations, it depends on how you apply the O.S.
