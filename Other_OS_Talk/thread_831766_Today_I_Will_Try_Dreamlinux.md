### Post by Vince4Amy on 2008-06-17
Until the Release of OpenSUSE 11.0 I've backed up everything and I'm trying different distributions to see if any others are as good or better than OpenSUSE.

So far I've tried:
**Kubuntu** - Worked fine, but I could only change monitor settings through Catalyst Control Centre. It didn't feel as polished as the gnome counterpart.
**Debian Stable** - I got to the installer and it couldn't find 2 of my 3 HDD's, I searched for a while and then gave up, I was only trying after all.
**Mandriva One 2008** - This Distribution was Excellent, it was fast, supported most of my hardware out of the box and was easy to configure, however there was no 64-bit edition and I wanted to take advantage of the full 4GB of RAM I have.

Today I'm going to try Dreamlinux, I've visited the website and I'm so far impressed, I've seen the screenshots and visited the forum and I am pleased with the way that the forum is run also. 

Overall the Distro seems very polished and complete and the forums seem to be calm and helpful. I will download and try this when I get home from school.

Until then does anyone have any opinions or experiences with Dreamlinux, I will post back later once I've tried it.

### Post by dlmoak on 2008-06-20

I didn't particularly care for the expanding icons, though I assume that could be changed.
