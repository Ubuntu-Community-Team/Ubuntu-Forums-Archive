### Post by 67GTA on 2007-06-29
I switched from Ubuntu to Mint(I feel like I'm having an affair). The artwork is nicer, and there are a few extra scripts in Mint such as:
mintinstall- installs apps from the mint software portal
mintmenu- suse/sled menu
mintdesktop- choose what icons are shown
mintconfig- glorified control panel
mintdisk- auto mounting
xserver xorg gui frontend

All multimedia codecs and browser plugins are installed so you can have a normal desktop system at installation. You have to install your proprietary graphics drivers. It is built on top of Ubuntu, so almost anything that applies to Ubuntu is the same for Mint such as repos, howto's, etc. I love it.

### Post by VCSkier on 2007-06-30
The primary difference between Ubuntu and Mint is that Mint includes and widely supports the use of proprietary softwares.

I think Mint, as well as many other distributions sporting attractive proprietary software support OOTB are fine and good, and even can be very beneficial; they attract new users to linux, and can even help those current users how find distributions like Ubuntu (who more strictly adhere  to the FOSS philosophy) to be inconvenient or bothersome.

I'm becoming increasingly worried though at the growing general ignorance concerning Free/Open Source Software.  Linux users should be aware of what FOSS really is, and why it is important.  IMHO, it is fundamentally what makes us different from Windows/Macintosh.  It is where we came from, and ultimately, our only means to a future.  If linux users stop supporting and encouraging the use and development of FOSS, its strength will diminish, we will lose our distinctiveness.  Without FOSS, linux based OS's will become similar to Windows, but with a smaller, more divided user base, and less funding.

Don't misunderstand me, I use some proprietary softwares too, like Flash and mp3, but I plan on exploring gnash as soon as I can, and my next DAP will certainly support ogg or flac.  Just don't let the convenience of proprietary software cause us to abandon FOSS projects.

For what it's worth... [http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html](http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html)
