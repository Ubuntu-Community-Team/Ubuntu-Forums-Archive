### Post by DrMega on 2007-12-14
I read an article about this once before I switched to Linux, when I was trying to decide what distro to use.

The article pointed out (not that it needed to) that there are some seriously questionable licensing implications with it (same is true of Linspire and Freespire), not least that one of the themes you can choose (according to the article) is an MS WinXP theme, and MP3 and other propriety codecs are installed by default.

I see nothing wrong with someone trying to make a Linux distro that looks a bit like Windows (Linspire/Freespire), as long as it doesn't try or pretend to be Windows.
