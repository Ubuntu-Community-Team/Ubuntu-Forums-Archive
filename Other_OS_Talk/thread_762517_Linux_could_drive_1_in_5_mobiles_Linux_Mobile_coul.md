Linux could drive one in five mobiles
22/04/2008 09:31:00 - by Andrew Beutmueller


[http://web20.telecomtv.com/pages/?newsid=43026&id=e9381817-0593-417a-8639-c4c53e2a2a10&view=news](http://web20.telecomtv.com/pages/?newsid=43026&id=e9381817-0593-417a-8639-c4c53e2a2a10&view=news)

Perennial burr under the Microsoft’s PC OS saddle, Linux now has its sights set on the mobile market where it has ambitions to, if not unseat both Windows Mobile and OS leader Symbian, at least give them a run for their money.

New data from ABI Research says that Linux is capable of grabbing 20 per cent of high-end mobile market share by 2013, mostly through the efforts of two mobile OS developments: the open software  LiMo (Linux Mobile) and Google's Android, which is Linux based.
