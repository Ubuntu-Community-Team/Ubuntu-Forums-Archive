### Post by ugm6hr on 2008-09-22
Testing a system *appropriately* before recommendation is difficult, since many of these requests for suggestions involve old technology.

I have seen many PuppyLinux (v2+) fans recommend it for systems with <64MB RAM; they have clearly tried (and liked) it, but there are "low end" and **low end** systems.  For those unfamiliar with it - Puppy v2/3/4 is unusable with <64MB RAM.

I have mentioned GreyCat (a floppy based distro I have never used) for some of the really old systems, since sometimes an option that might make their system productive is better than no option at all.  I will normally make it clear by my language that this is something I have no experience with.

Obviously, if there are plenty of forum users making appropriate suggestions, then it is sensible to restrict yourself to personal experience.  If, however, there are lots of inappropriate suggestions (or no suggestions at all) - possible (albeit untried) options are acceptable.  This can even prompt other users who have tried your suggestion to pipe up with a +1.

In summary: discussions about distro options are acceptable, bearing in mind there is a difference between talking about and recommending a distro.  Hope that makes sense.
