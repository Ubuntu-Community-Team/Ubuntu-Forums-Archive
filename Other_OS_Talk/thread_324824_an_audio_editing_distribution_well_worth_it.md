[*][13 Recommendations for the download]("http://www.musix.org.ar/en/download.html#Recommendations_for_the_download")[/LIST]** Release announcement, Musix 0.79 **

 Musix project is pleased to announce a brand new multimedia version of the 100% free operating system for artists and users in general. 
Now Music GNU/Linux is completely focused on the creation of multimedia content, this is: musical production, audio editing, video editing, 3D animation, graphic design, image editing, web design... without setting aside programs that traditionally are on a desktop PC. 
Hundred of software packages have been updated like for instance Ardour 0.99.3 and Rosegarden 1.4.0, and incorporated other new ones: Cinelerra (video), Bluefish (web design), Xara (vector design), Blender3D (animation) are most outstanding. 
In order to include these important programs have been removed other ones having duplicated functionality, like Amsn (-->gaim), Amarok (-->xmms, xine), Firefox (-->konqueror), Vim (-->kate), Gtk-Gnutella (-->amule) and practically every package referring compilation utilities, which are easily re-installable by means of the restore-dev.sh command. 



** System and desktop startup **

 Since Musix 0.59 the system boot is completelly graphical. It was added a boot "splash screen" that tries not to confuse new users with little understandable messages. Desktop also starts without user interaction. 
In case of failling the desktop startup due to a wrong video hardware recognition, it rises a menu with some options: it's advised to type the command videoconfig.sh, choose the first video detection method ("Musix"), and then re-start desktop typing kdm, inicio o starten. 

** Video **

 The new video detection management should simplify user's configuration of graphic cards and monitor. Now there are 3 types of video self-detection: the "Musix" method (which uses new Xorg features), the "Knoppix" method (default), and the "Gentoo" method. 
We suggest to try the Musix method at least once to see if it produces a better performance (more resolutions to choose, 3D acceleration, etc.). 

** Sound **

 Since Musix 0.59, sound card detection also changed, since the hotplug system was replaced by udev, following general trends of all GNU/Linux distributions. Now the self-detection it's made by the old alsaconf script, but reformed to act automaticaly. 

** Update without re-instalation **

 Musix 0.79 should be able to update better a previous version without reinstalling the hole system.  
This task *must* be made from the Kanotix installer (knxinstaller). It *must not* be made from the Knoppix installer, since the latter has a bug on the matter. 

** In summary **

 Musix 0.79 tries to offer the greatest amount of tools for creative users who need to express their works probably in several multimedia formats. For example, nowadays for a rock band, recording one song is not enough. Perhaps they need to design a Web site, film and edit a video and graphically design the cover of their CD or DVD: Musix is a complete artistic factory.
