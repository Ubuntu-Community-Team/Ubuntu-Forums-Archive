### Post by icett on 2008-01-08
I am not posting this just for starting a flame war or for fun but I just could not resist to share my view with the Linux community here and I request everyone to take this seriously and understand what I wish to get out of my chest with cool heads. The three Linux distros I mentioned in the subject of this thread are really the type of Linux Oses, the Linux community should be proud of. While retaining their open source bases as well, they have opted for commercial versions of their Oses as well thus giving both the fanbases as well as customers the best of the both Open source and commercial softwares. Their MS relationships should not be confused with anything other than their commercial dealings with any other companies. They made deals with MS with their interests in view and the same is with MS. What Linux badly needs today is the commercialization of its Oses and a single platform/standard for all Linux  distros. This can be achieved if all distros merge and become a single company as strong as MS or there should be formed a regulatory authority which assures that all Linux distros are based on a single platform/standards/set of rules. With commericalization would follow heavy advertisements of Linux which would increase users, which would in turn make hardware/software/games manufacturers take Linux
seriously and release their products for this OS. Also commercialization would create incentives for the distros to make their OSes very user friendly as well as resemble MS Windows in their looks and use in order to get more users, and follow commercial practices with which Linux would very soon become a mainstream operating system worldwide. That would be a boon for Linux as it would give the best to users both from open source and commercial sources. Having both the free versions as well as commercial versions of their Oses, Linux distros would get an edge of MS. However bad is their OS, MS has been successful to monopolize the OS market worldwide with their commercial practices. Just think what Linux with its solid Unix based could do if it were to copy MS's success. As Deng once said "It doesn't matter whether the cat is black or white as long as it catches mice"!:)

Schools today do not teach freedom, they teach us to be slaves, to give up our freedom and money for software that takes years to get a security patch and useless customer support.

Ok... enough, Im getting political :p
