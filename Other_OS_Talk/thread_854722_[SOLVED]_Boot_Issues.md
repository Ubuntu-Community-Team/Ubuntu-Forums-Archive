### Post by azurepancake on 2008-07-09
Today I was called to look at a friends computer that is not booting up correctly. The system lost power during a thunder storm and that is when the problem began. The system powers on and displays the blue HP splash screen during the POST process. It on the bottom of the splash screen it says 'F1' for CMOS and 'F4' for "System Recovery" or something along those lines. 

This system runs Windows XP.

The system is completely unresponsive during these moments. Pressing 'F1' does nothing at all. Eventually the splash screen disappears and you are greeted with a black screen and a blinking cursor. 

And that is about it! The keyboard is PS/2 and functions and the system only lets out a single beep like normal.

I've never encounter a problem like this before and I am not too sure on what to do. If anyone is familiar with this particular problem or has any ideas of what could be going wrong, I'd greatly appreciate it.

If you need anymore information just ask. I am kind of short on details of the system currently, but I'll be sure to post them soon if needed.

### Post by falcon61102 on 2008-07-09
If it's a desktop, you can normally reset the CMOS with a Jumper setting on the mainboard.  It may be printed right on the mainboard, or  you might have to look it up.  That should reset your CMOS to its default.

---

### Post by ironcitypete on 2008-07-09
If you pull out the battery on the motherboard it should also reset the bios.  It should be easier to find then a jumper. I would unplug the computer for about thirty seconds and then plug it back in sometimes letting the capacitors discharge on the motherboard fixes those types of issues.
