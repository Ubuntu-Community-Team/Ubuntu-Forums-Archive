### Post by kellemes on 2008-01-08
> **marsmissionaries said:**
> It would be as fast as kde4 is on any distro, it's all the same kernel.

Kernel has little to do with it..
If you find KDE isn't fast enough on Kubuntu or whatever distro, you can "undress" your system to leave more resources for KDE.  DSL will offer a much more basic system without a gazillion daemons running (hardly any point in undressing DSL) and this *could* mean a better performing KDE.

It's mostly about resources.. an efficient compilation of the kernel *can* give you a performance-boost but it's just one of many things you have to do to get a noticable performance-boost.

If you need a high speed KDE look at ArchLinux, they have [KDEmod]("http://kdemod.ath.cx/") made for eye-candy and performance!
