### Post by arvevans on 2007-06-13
"Smoker" correctly noted that "sudo" is prior art that was done for the UC-Berkley derivative of UNIX, and it appeared about the same time on DEC PDP-11/70's as a UNIX command at Bell Labs in Naperville, Illinois (I was there).

Posts that include intentionally controversial statements, especially if they are erroneous, only serve to waste valuable time, storage space, network bandwidth, and efforts of those who contribute here as a way to help others.

Those who have worked with the patent office and patent attorneys are probably aware that you can* apply* for a patent on just about anything.  Many patent applications are superfluous, some are attempts to intimidate others, some are attempts at piracy of intellectual property, and a very few are legitimately new invention.  The sad fact is that the patent office sometimes makes a mistake and allows inappropriate patents to be registered, which ultimately requires litigation to resolve conflicts with prior art or previous patents.
