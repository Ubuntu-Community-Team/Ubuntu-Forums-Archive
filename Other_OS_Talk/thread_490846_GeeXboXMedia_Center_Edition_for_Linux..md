### Post by Dark Star on 2007-07-02
GeeXboX is a free embedded Linux distribution which aims at turning your computer into a so called HTPC (Home Theater PC) or Media Center. Being a standalone LiveCD-based distribution, it's a ready to boot operating system than works on any Pentium-class x86 computer or PowerPC Macintosh, implying no software requirement. You can even use it on a diskless computer, the whole system being loaded in RAM.
[CENTER]
[[IMG]http://www.imgx.org/pthumbs/large/728/gx-boot-full-omc.jpeg[/IMG]]("http://www.imgx.org/public/view/full/728")  [[IMG]http://www.imgx.org/pthumbs/large/727/gx-boot-full-halloween.jpeg[/IMG]]("http://www.imgx.org/public/view/full/727")[/CENTER]

Despite his tiny ISO image size, the distribution comes with a complete and automatic hardware detection, not requiring any driver to be added. It supports playback of nearly any kind of audio/video and image files and all known codecs and containers are shipped in, allowing playing them through various physical supports, either being CD, DVD, HDD, LAN or Internet.
[CENTER]
[[IMG]http://www.imgx.org/pthumbs/large/730/gx-menu-omc-full.jpeg[/IMG]]("http://www.imgx.org/public/view/full/730")  [[IMG]http://www.imgx.org/pthumbs/large/729/gx-menu-aqua-full.jpeg[/IMG]]("http://www.imgx.org/public/view/full/729")[/CENTER]

GeeXboX also comes with a complete toolchain that allows developers adding easily extra packages and features but that might also be used to give birth to many dedicated embedded Linux systems.

I used geexbox even before i started using "desktop" linux. The fun thing is that i used geexbox as a media player because windows couldn't play the same content as my geexbox could. Especially not on my amd k6-2 media box (64 megs of memory).
So you could say i could play about _any_ media/music clip on linux, but not on windows. Aah the irony.

In the end, technologies like geexbox got my attention, and i started looking at linux. Nowadays i run my mediabox using freevo, on arch linux though. (easier to install to hd, and has more packages available...)
