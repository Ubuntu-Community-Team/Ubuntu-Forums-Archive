### Post by montgoej on 2006-12-26
I would suggest dapper definately.  Only thing is, if she has a small hard drive, trying to dual boot you may run into problems, as she won't be able to share a home/my documents dir with Windows, and if she's like I was when I first started using Dapper, as a dual boot, half of the files had to be in both the windows my documents and the ubuntu home, which took up a TON of space. I suggest also finding some kind of "cheat sheet", making about 5 copies(it never hurts to have extras), laminate it, and stick it to the side of the monitor or something.  Believe it or not, it'll drastically cut down the number of calls.
