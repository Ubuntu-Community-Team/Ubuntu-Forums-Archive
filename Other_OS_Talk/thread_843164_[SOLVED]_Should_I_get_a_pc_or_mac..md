### Post by volkswagner on 2008-06-28
Looking at your requirements, it seems a new mac is out.  Building via a 3rd party is the next best thing to doing it yourself.  Perhaps an adult friend/family member will put you mom at ease.

When in a tight budget it is difficult to beat the prebuilt systems from big box stores (usually involves rebates).  When you add all the parts and cost of OS it gets expensive.  

There a pros and cons for build myself/preconfigured pc's.  It is usually cheaper for prebuilt for similar hardware.  I prefer the retail windows license vs OEM.  The bloatware with OEM systems usually need to be flushed.  If you can find a system that meets your specs and is well supported by linux, can be tough.

Often times the specials on sites such as buy.com for new or refurbished systems are hard to beat. 

Fist make your component wish list and see if any OEM systems come close. 

My two pennies worth.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-06-28
I'd say build a computer yourself. If you treat the components with due care, and research compatibility with Linux, then you'll have a trouble-free time.

It's seriously pretty easy to build a computer if you have a HOWTO in front of you and have chosen parts that fit together :-)

Double-check your work before turning on the power, and when you do, have the case open so you can check that the fans are running. I've built two computers so far and I haven't fried or damaged anything.

And of course, a custom-built computer is the best way to get a Vista/Linux-compatible computer. Plus you learn a bit about computers :-)
