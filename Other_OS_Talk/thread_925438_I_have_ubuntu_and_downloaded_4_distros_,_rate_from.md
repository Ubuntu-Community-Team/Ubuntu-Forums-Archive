### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2008-10-05
My opinion:

1) Ubuntu - Just the best balance overall. It's an extremely "middle of the road" distro to me in that it doesn't do anything exemplary, but there's also nothing problematic.

2) OpenSUSE - For all its bloat, probably the most professional feeling distro I've used. There's just a polish on it that makes it feel like it's meant for business use.

3) Fedora - Great stuff, but lots of hiccups made me scrap it. I couldn't get my touchpad working, for example.

4) Gentoo - Never used this one, but it's so out of place I'm not sure why you went for it. The idea of compiling absolutely everything so doesn't appeal to me.

5) Knoppix - Hardly a distro in my eyes, made just for LiveCD use. I respect the idea, but at this point a LiveCD is so common there's no reason to have it around.
