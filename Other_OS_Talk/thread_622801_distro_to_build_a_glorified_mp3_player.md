### Post by Scheater5 on 2007-12-07
I concur with the suggestions of GeexBox.  I am doing much the same thing right now with an old Compaq.  GeexBox is tiny, fast and intuitive.  Should sit comfortably in your 192MB of ram, or you can install it to a harddrive.  (By default, if booted off a cd, Geexbox will load into ram and eject itself, freeing your cd drive).  
I've recently been toying with building a media center out of an old pc possibly with dvr capabilities, but it's beyond my budget at this point - but what you may consider is this: get a flat-screen tv with vga inputs, and run a geexbox computer into it.  You won't get to pause live tv, but at the touch of a button you can have all your music and videos on your tv.
