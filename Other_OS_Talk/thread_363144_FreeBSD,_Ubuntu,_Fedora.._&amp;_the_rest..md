### Post by Arisna on 2007-02-16
Gentoo is supposed to be a performance distro.  You don't have to compile everything--for example, the normal method of installation doesn't come with much or any compiling--but to install software from official repositories, you'll be compiling things.  Personally, I didn't notice any slowdown when I switched to Ubuntu from Gentoo.  Although I've never used it, I understand that Arch is a distro based on Gentoo that lets you use binary or source packages conveniently.

As far as FreeBSD, I liked a lot of things about it, but certain graphical applications, namely Firefox, Kopete, Kexi, and some XScreensavers, segmentation faulted and crashed frequently during normal operation.  This was in version 5.4.  I worked with 6.0 as well, albeit less extensively, and experienced similar problems.  This was on a variety of x86 class hardware.
