### Post by Tux.Ice on 2008-11-02
Ok,
Hello, I know some of us have been wondering what happened to Enterprise Linux, and here is my explanation:

We moved servers and our STILL working on getting everything set up, We are also redesigning our site. We will not have a seperate domain for enterprise linux yet. Until some .iso's get compiled we will not have a domain. To get iso's compiled in a virtual EL environment, we need to come up with a complete software list. We also need to talk about every aspect of EL. EL will have its own repository but default will be with GNOME, X11 and Metacity. These can easily be changed from the installer off of the Live CD (which will be created at a later date.) as long as your internet connection is up. You will have the choice of XFCE, KDE, or GNOME. All versions will be newest that is GNOME 3, KDE 4 and whatever is the newest version of XFCE.

For those of you who do not know what Enterprise Linux is here's an outline in a nutshell:

Enterprise Linux is a Linux designed especially for use in office environments. It will have backwards Windows networking capability through SAMBA and will have office applications installed by default. We will have our own Repos and we might even have some applications made. Ive already created some wallpapers and other icons/logos that you will see, if you have any more questions ask here.

Thanks,
Adam
