### Post by darrelljon on 2008-02-17
> "a "distro" simply refers to a particular assortment of applications married with a particularly compiled kernel, such that its "out-of-the-box" capabilities meets most of the needs of its particular end-user base." - [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_distribution")So a distro will support different hardware and have different applications out of the box. Distros have different libraries (some have mp3, flash, DVD), different ways of installing additional software (package management) and different ways of being tweaked.

So yeah I have the same problem. I know what Desktop Environment/Window Manager I like (KDE, IceWM, JWM, e17) but not necessarily what distros.
