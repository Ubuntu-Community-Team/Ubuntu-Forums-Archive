### Post by L14UX_1NS1D3 on 2008-11-14
Well, if you live in the US. Your most likely to have never seen a business or any other entity using Linux. But Linux is become widely used as a inexpensive alternative to windows or mac for that matter. In Paris, the whole police department switched their systems to linux. Many companies are moving their entire infrastructure to Linux, as Linux is an extremely scalable and inexpensive alternative to other modern operating systems. Not only is Linux finding a following in corporate settings but also in the embedded market. More and more consumer device are running embedded versions of Linux. some linksys routers run their own versions of and a whole slew of other consumer devices are using the kernal. Linux can even be found built into some motherboards. Asus has recently come out with mobo that has a special ROM bios dubbed "spashtop" that boots into a minimal desktop running linux in 30 seconds. Linux has been also been ported to many devices to enhance/extend their capabilities. Some of the device that have been port to include:
Wii 
PSP
DS
Xbox
Ipod 
to name a few. The Linux community is always finding new ways to hack/improve software and hardware beyond the give capabilities. At Home I have a Mp3 (sansa e280, PDA (n800), router (Linksys WRTg54) and of course all the computers running linux. and After using for linux for about two years I don't think I could ever use anything else.

### Post by markbuntu on 2008-11-15
Most large corporations run all their mission critical applications and back office stuff on Unix or Linux. Windows is used for a desktop front end only. No system adminstrator in his/her right mind would let microsoft into the back end.

Also, most embedded and custom control systems use either unix or linux these days and that market is as big or bigger than the desktop market. Those sceens in the airport terminals do not run on windows. 

The only thing windows has is the desktop and that is a not so big a piece of the computing world. In some IT circles, windows is already considered as just another desktop manager competing with gnome and kde.

Forget the Paris Police Department, the entire adminstrative structure in France is moving to Open Source. It is a policy decision made a few years ago. As the Microsoft licenses expire, they are not being renewed.
