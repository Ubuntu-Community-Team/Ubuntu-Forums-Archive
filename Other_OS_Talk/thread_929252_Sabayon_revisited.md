### Post by wolfen69 on 2008-09-24
after giving sabayon 3.3 and 3.4 a go, i thought i would give some quick thoughts on 3.5 which i have installed on a test drive.

first off, some people would say that it's bloated. if you mean bloated as far as # of apps, well that it up to the individual. i did not find it bloated in terms of speed though. response time was surprisingly quick. 3D effects were absolutely spot on. very fast.

it saw all partitions and had no problems accessing them.

most things seemed to work very well, except for the new version of vlc that they include. (hate it)

it comes with every meaningful app under the sun. k3b, skype, google earth, k9copy, nexuiz, battle for wesnoth.... the list goes on forever. and every codec and plugin. multimedia was seamless.

i even like the new bright blue theme better. with emerald, it looked fantastic.

*however*, i found package management to be less than stellar. perhaps i am just spoiled by the speed and efficiency of debian package manager. it was slow, painful and non-intuitive. and i'm not crazy about the menu layout, as i found it a little confusing.

but in the end i give it a thumbs up overall. for those that want everything and the kitchen sink from the start, it's definitely worth a look. makes for an incredibly good live cd also.

just thought i'd throw this out there.
