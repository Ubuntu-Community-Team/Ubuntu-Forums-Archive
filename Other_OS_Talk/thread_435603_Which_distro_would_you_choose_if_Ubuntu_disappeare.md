### Post by rufousfelix on 2007-05-08
Or to put it another way, with what distro would you replace ubuntu?

I have to say that after a few days with puppy linux 2.14 I've done exactly that.  Puppy puts fun back into computing in a way I haven't personally seen since I first encountered speedy BeOS more than six years ago.  I used BeOS for five years before buying a new ThinkPad on which the BeOS didn't run.

For me, puppy is an elegant, formidable, complete and blindingly fast distro, running completely out of RAM (your HDD sees little if any action).  It meets my computing needs (I am a writer & text editing is, of course, no computationally-intensive task) and with SeaMonkey I am surfing the Web faster than I thought possible on dial-up.

In my opinion, ubuntu's vulnerability is it tries to be too much for too many people, so one has a rather bloated distro.  For example, I am happy with four or so fonts.  I don't need a fonts from a sampling of most languages on the planet ;-)  I also find ubuntu trying to keep up in the feature race with the Apple & Microsoft--another reason to bloat.

To its credit, puppy linux stays true to some old unix virtues: small bullet-proof apps, elegance, speed, plus the documentation is far better than I expected.

I will continue to keep ubuntu installed on one laptop on a legacy basis, but for getting work done and saving time, it's the puppy from now on!
