### Post by mips on 2008-02-26
As someone that has briefly used Gentoo or a Gentoo based distro & lately moved to arch I would have to say 'go with arch'. I just find it to be a nicer experience and I have nothing against Gentoo, just like arch more.

I don't see why compiling from source should give your more bloat as you can choose what to compile. Compiling from source can allow you to fine tune stuff for your particular cpu & system. Some people complain about compile times in gentoo but this is only really an issue with big packages like Gnome, KDE, Open Office etc but some of them have precompiled binaries available. I actually loved portage, but then I also love pacman on arch, both are good at what they do. Arch is i686 optimised so there is no support for older cpus and it is very fast. With arch you can still compile stuff if you really want to.

### Post by scottro on 2008-03-09
Compiling from source means that you download nothing but a Makefile.  The Makefile will pull in a tarball and then compile the code on your machine--sometimes, the compiling requires a great deal of space--for example, on FreeBSD, to compile OpenOffice, it is recommended that you have at least 2 gigs of RAM and 9 Gigs--yes, gigs, that's not a mistype--of free diskspace.  

Gentoo is great, and although there are sometimes binary packages, most things are compiled from source--this can be time consuming especially when there are bugs in the code--few things are more aggravating than an hour of compiling to have it die at the end. 

Arch is quite similar in many ways, save that its package manager uses binaries.  If you want to compile from source, you can, using their abs system, but you don't have to do so.  

Most importantly, imho, they have excellent docs--their wiki is quite good and usually current.  (I think one of the main reasons Gentoo got so popular so quickly is because Daniel Robbins is one of the best document writers around--he made a fairly complex task simple in his initial install documentation.)

So, I'll go with everyone else here and say Arch.  :)

That's not a dig at Gentoo--however, Gentoo can be quite time consuming, while Arch sort of gets out of your way and lets you learn Linux, the command line and everything else.
