### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2006-12-05
Hi, Guys!

After reading a couple of reviews, I decided to download the Mandriva One Live CD and give it a test run.

I must admit that it had no problems with detecting the hardware on my Acer TravelMate 4101.
After loading-up, changing the screen resoltion to 1280x800 was simplicity itself. I just went into config scrren and it asked if I wanted to load 915resolution. After doing this my screen was set-up perfectly, which is a major difference from the experience that I had with Dapper, which took me 3 days to resolve, with many posts to the forums. In retrospect, the fix was dead easy, but like a lot of Linux newbies, trolling the forum posts was often just too confusing, as were a lot of answers to my questions.

All of the fancy 3D desktop stuff, like the spinning cube and wobbly windows were also a breeze to set-up. After reading many posts on the Ubuntu forums and the involved command line stuff to get it all working in Dapper, I have decided to pass trying on my Dapper install.

Having said that, I will stick with Ubuntu for two compelling reasons.

1. Synaptic. In my opinion, this leaves RPM for dead.

2. The Ubuntu Forums and technical assistance.

With the release of Edgy, I feel that if the gurus can it it to install as painlessly as Mandriva One and especially if they can get it to be as friendly on setting-up screen resolution for Intel and other brands of graphic cards, they will be in an unassailable position.

Comments? Criticisms?
