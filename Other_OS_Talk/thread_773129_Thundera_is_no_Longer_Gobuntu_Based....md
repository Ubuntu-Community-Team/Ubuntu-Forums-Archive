### Post by notwen on 2008-04-30
I also would recommend Debian(or Ubuntu) over Arch.    

If you go w/ Arch you would have the possibility of breaking your whole system due to it's rolling release ways, although there are very few complaints of this actually happening.  Arch's User Repo also provides lots of packages for you to implement as well.  Depending on how user-friendly you're wanting your distro to be, using Arch as a base may deter some users from the start.  That said Arch isn't difficult to install if you take your time and read up on the Beginner's Installation.

Ubuntu on the other hand you'd need to strip down to meet your standards.  You have a huge repo to choose your 'light-weight' applications from.  Depending on how much you change it, users could still rely on ubuntuforums for support.  

Personally I believe Debian would be your best bet, as you could do a netinstall and then just build up from there.  It's repos are somewhat dated, but using these dated packages provides a certain level of security/stability only Debian can offer.  If you've never built a Linux install from the ground up, I highly recommend doing a minimal Debian install and adding to that base to get an idea of what you're in for.  You'll learn a lot from doing so and this will only help ensure the quality of your future Thundera release.  

Best of luck w/ your distro.  =]
