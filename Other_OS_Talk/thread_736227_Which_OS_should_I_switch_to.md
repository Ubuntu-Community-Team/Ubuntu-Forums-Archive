### Post by spamking2000 on 2008-03-26
Depends on how much tinkering you want to do versus how much money you want to spend.

I would suspect since this is Ubuntu Forums, you should find that people are here because they are generally satisfied with what they are using, or some may be Linux and Open Source techie elitist types. I'm not one of them. From a technical perspective, I like the Ubuntu based varieties mainly because I think the user interface is simple enough for me to handle and the repos are arguably the best. I would love to see a world where Linux on the desktop is closer to 50% market share.

That said, its not for everyone and generally it still depends on programs and the experience that the user is looking for. I'm not a Windows fan, but that doesn't mean that it isn't the right option for everyone. For example, my brother uses a pretty complex software that does professional music mixing. Very expensive, definitely a niche product, very involved. The software itself is written for Windows, not Mac or Linux so it would be silly for him to use anything else.

For you, I like that you actually listed what you want to use. Sounds like you have a non-Mac laptop that you want to install on, so certainly Ubuntu or an Ubuntu based Linux distro would make good sense from that perspective. I use Quicken 2000 through Wine, but don't have any experience with MS Money. I also have KMyMoney, which seems to be a good enough equivalent to do what I want and its native to Linux and on the repos.

Where you're going to run into trouble is iTunes. There is no iTunes client native to Linux, so its off to alternatives. If you like to buy music through iTunes's software, I'm not sure there is a one-size-fits-all software to do that. You could try installing iTunes for Windows under Wine, but I would imagine it probably has some links to Windows Media Player that may make that difficult to impossible. I'm not a technical Linux expert by any means, so I usually don't spend a lot of time trying to play with things to make them work. If it works, it works, if not, I move on and find something that does. I have a SANSA and personally I just manage the music through RhythmBox. Its easy enough.

Really its your decision though. Sounds to me like you may have some sticking points, and I'll be iTunes is going to really be one of the main factors. Everything else is relatively easy... even Wireless if you add NDISwrapper from the repos isn't that bad. If you absolutely must have iTunes specifically rather than an alternative like Banshee or RythmBox, your best bet is probably a Mac... on the other hand, if you already have hardware, why spend another $2000 on a PowerBook unless you absolutely have to.
