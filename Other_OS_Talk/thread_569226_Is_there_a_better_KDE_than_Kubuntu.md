### Post by DarkOx on 2007-10-06
PCLinuxOS is better in many respects. First off, it has an excellent control centre for managing different parts of the system. Secondly, it's much more unified visually -- splash screens, logos, backgrounds all sport the PCLinuxOS logo/theme. It uses apt-get for installing software and comes with codecs already enabled. However, while PCLinuxOS has most things, there's far less software available in their repositories than in Kubuntus'. Also far fewer third party repositories (think things like Avant Window Navigator or screenlets) are made for it. 

I've been trying out openSUSE 10.3 in a VM, and it also looks like it'd be an excellent choice for KDE.
