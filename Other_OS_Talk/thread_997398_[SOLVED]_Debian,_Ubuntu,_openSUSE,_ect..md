### Post by Xiong Chiamiov on 2008-11-29
Although there are many tools that will pick a distro based on your needs, they usually neglect some of the most important aspects, which are documentation and community support, both of which are excellent for Ubuntu.  Really, a distro is just a collection of software written by other people, so you can have the same eye-candy on Slackware as Ubuntu, but Ubuntu comes with more of it preinstalled.

Although there are many things about Ubuntu I dislike, it is a great place to start with Linux.  These forums are what set it apart from the other popular "user-friendly" distros.

The linux distro chooser isnt that accurate though, in my experience experimentation is the best way to learn.
However here is a small list of my top distro's for beginners:

Mepis Linux: My first major linux experience and so far overall best, it is easy and great for beginners.
In eye candy Mepis is not the greatest, but in terms of a good beginners distro I would reccomend it.

Linux Mint: Based on Ubuntu, it is media ready and eye candy ready.
It comes with things like flash preinstalled and stuff like that, it has a decent version of gnome and has a simular feel to windows thanks to its layout.

Mandriva Linux: Mandriva is the pioneer of Linux destop distros. It has a good reputation for being easy and its version of KDE4 isnt half bad (better then Kubuntu)
Good on eye candy, good on effects.

Ubuntu: Ubuntu is of course what this forum is about, Ubuntu is sort of between a super easy distro and a more advanced distro.
Its great on the desktop, good eye candy support once it is tweaked.
Ubuntu is perhaps not the best for beginners, but it is among the best out there.

Debian: What Ubuntu/ Mepis/ Mint is based on, I know you said its out of your list but it still deserves mention.
Debian based systems are the cornerstone of linux
