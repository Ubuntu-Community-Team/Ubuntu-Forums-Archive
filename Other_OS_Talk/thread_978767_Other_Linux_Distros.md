### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2008-11-11
If Ubuntu works and you have no complaints, rather you're just feeling some "wanderlust", right off the bat there's no reason to use Mint or Debian (in my mind).

Outside of a different package manager and what comes installed, there's little that separates Ubuntu from MEPIS, Mandriva, Suse, and Fedora. That's all cosmetic though I admit they do "feel" somewhat different from one another.

If you want to try something really DIFFERENT, then peep Gentoo, Arch, Slack, Zenwalk, DSL, Puppy, etc. Look at distros with actually different goals in mind.
