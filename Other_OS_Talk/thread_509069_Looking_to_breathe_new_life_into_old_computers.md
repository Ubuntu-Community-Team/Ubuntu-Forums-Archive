### Post by Kowalski_GT-R on 2007-07-25
reviving oldies is a great sport :)

I'm afraid with those quantities of RAM you are stuck between Puppy Linux (a great little distro) and Damn Small Linux (don't know much about it)


Personally I'd suggest to take all the RAM you can and throw it all at one PC, wich will help a lot.

16+8=24 ......then I suggest you buy some more :) :)
I found out 64Mb is the bare minimum to have something with a Graphical Interface running decently.


With 64 Megs and a little spare time you could try a "minimal install" from Xubuntu Alternate CD, and build it up from a command line.
I'm also new to Ubuntu, but I found it very easy, almost magically straightforward :cool:

That's what is am currently after on a 233MMX Pentium with 64Mbs RAM, and I have to say I'm well chuffed with it,

quite snappy too! You'll have to choose the applications carefully, bu you'll find all the answers in the forums, wich are very supportive.

try it! have fun!
