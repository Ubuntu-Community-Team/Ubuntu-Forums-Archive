### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-06-25
Wireless performance is much better in openSuSE, if that's important to you. From my experience, here are the top five distros for wireless performance:
[LIST=1]
[*]Pardus
[*]openSuSE
[*]Ubuntu
[*]Sabayon
[*]PCLinuxOS
[/LIST]
The difference between #3 and 4 is large, and I stuck PCLOS at the end because it was all I could think of amongst decent wireless distros. I've left Mint, etc. off this list, as they use Ubuntu's wireless settings.

PCLinuxOS is faster in most respects, and its 3D effects are smoother than any distro, except maybe SAM (which is PCLOS based but uses a lighter base). OpenSuSE is a little more full-featured, and its installer is significantly better than PCLinuxOS's. The same can be said for package management in PCLOS, which, although slower than Debian-based apt-get, is still passable, and offers decent selection. It's missing a variety of applications I'd expect from a major distro though, and the same for openSuSE.

At the end of the day, both will get the job done, and well. openSuSE boots slower and runs a tiny but slower, but YaST edges the Control Center in PCLOS because of the additional features it offers which you won't likely find in any other distro. Package management is easier in PCLOS, but I expect that, because of the prevelance of compatible RPMs and the ubiquity of SUSE, that openSuSE is more extensible if you're willing to track down addditional repositories, etc. PCLOS includes more codecs and drivers than does openSuSE, which will make configuration easier in PCLOS.

I'd say use PCLOS unless you need reliable, good wireless access, especially to open networks. PCLOS still gets this done though.
