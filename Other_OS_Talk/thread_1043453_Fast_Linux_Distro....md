### Post by fwojciec on 2009-01-18
It is true that DE/WM has a big effect on perceived speed, but this doesn't mean that distributions are of no consequence.  Custom built Arch with Openbox is going to be faster than Ubuntu with Openbox, although it is true that Arch will take more setting up initially.  It could be Debian, Gentoo or Slackware in place of Arch, by the way, and the result would be still the same.  Manually configured distros streamlined for a particular set of hardware and configured to do what is required of them well are always going to be faster than one-size-fits-all distos, though this comes, of course, at the expense of the effort that goes into initial configuration.
