### Post by odelay on 2007-04-14
I haven't tried out too many flavors...but if I had the time to try out a new distro, I know exactly which one it would be.

SimplyMepis 6.5 (brand spankin' new)

I just downloaded the LiveCD (that's CD, not DVD) and could not believe my eyes.  I'm currently a Kubuntu user, and I have an allegiance towards KDE.  Mepis could have been the easiest linux distro I've ever seen.  From the LiveCD I was able to switch between the default wireless manager and Network Manager just by clicking a bullet...there was nothing to download.  Same goes for ATI drivers.  3 Bullets: fglrx (ATI official), xorg (default), Radeon (for Beryl).  Samba worked out of the box...I just clicked on network places and found my old PowerBook on the wireless network.  I copied a H.264 mini video I had laying on my Mac's desktop and proceeded to play it instantly.  All the codecs are already installed.  I then started Amarok and mounted a shared media drive on my laptop and began playing my mp3 collection.  I was also able to use samba from my Mac to connect to Mepis--something I haven't been able to do in Kubuntu.  

Even more, Firefox was set up as the default browser, and actually configured to look like it belonged in the OS (I'm looking at you Kubuntu Firefox!).  For someone who relies on Google apps...this was huge.  Now I'm thinking, well since they used Firefox as the default browser, they might make Thunderbird default mainl---no!  It's like they're reading my mind...Kontact (my fav) is good to go.  They've pretty much picked my favorite app as default for every category.  Everything just worked.  Oh, and I'm not a Beryl/Compiz fan...but as icing on the cake it was built right in and only a click or two away.  And not to mention, all this seemed pretty snappy for running off a CD and some RAM.

As a laptop user...it finally feels like someone made a linux distro with us as something more than an afterthought.

I'm definitely waiting for Feisty Kubuntu before making any switches...but WOWY WOW WOW!!  It made me feel like I was back in OS X (my OS of choice).  I sure hope Feisty is this good, b/c I don't think I'm ready to let go of the wonderful Ubuntu community yet.
