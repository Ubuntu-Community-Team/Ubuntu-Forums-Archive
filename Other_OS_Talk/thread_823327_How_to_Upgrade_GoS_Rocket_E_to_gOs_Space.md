It may be possible, but it's impractible. For one thing, Entrance - gOS' login program - doesn't allow you to choose your default session (GNOME, Xfce, KDE, Enlightenment, etc.), so every time you would want to use gOS Space, you would have to choose GNOME as your session every time.

But it's possible. You just have to install GNOME (easiest route is to sudo apt-get ubuntu-desktop) and install the gOS Space icon theme, the gOS Space Metacity theme, the gOS Space GTk theme, 

But it's not really worth it. I suggest getting a blank DVD and downloading the gOS Space ISO and burning it to DVD.
