### Post by cprofitt on 2008-07-11
Reliability:  HP (business class) or Dell (vostro) or Lenovo Thinkpads are the way to go. My preference would be a Thinkpad.

Performance:  No need to over-pay for an Apple. Any of the three brands listed above can meet your performance goals.

Game support:  Well... if you really want to play games. on a PC you want to run Windows. Sure there are games for OS X and Linux, but the bulk of PC games go to Windows. To be honest even on the Windows side of things it appears that PC gaming is close to dead.

Media editing capabilties:  For simple editing OS X on an Apple computer is fantastic. For more advanced editing you can use a Windows PC just as easily as an Apple computer. I am not sure what abilities Ubuntu Stuido version has, but it might be worth looking at.

OS support:  Well... obviously due to the draconian policies of Apple the only way to legally run OS X is on their over-priced hardware. That is not an issue of hardware; it is an issue of Apple controlling what you can load their OS on by using a TPM chip to verify their hardware. If you have to run OS X you have to do it on an Apple. I can not support a company that does such things... this is far worse than Microsoft. In fact it is a good sign that Microsoft can make their OS run on any x86 or AMD/EMT 64 hardware.

Geek, Programming, Freedom, etc: A PC with Linux on it is your best bet here. Apple controls the vertical and the horizontal when you use their stuff. Microsoft controls the vertical.

-----
Summary:

A dual-boot system with Windows (gaming only) and Linux (everything) else sounds like it most meets your needs w/o violating your stated 'philosophy' while the Apple hardware and OS while being better for media would violate your stated philosophy.
