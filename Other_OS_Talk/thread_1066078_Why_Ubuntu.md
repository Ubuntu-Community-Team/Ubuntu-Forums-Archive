I will say that Yum has the best console output of a package manager I've seen, it's really too bad that apt & zypper don't adopt a similar reading-friendly approach (zypper is great with listing updates or repos but otherwise has the same "package1_package2_package3" output for most other commands).

I like openSUSE because of how 'professional' it feels.  All the little things they give art to that tie the system together (GRUB, boot, shutdown, and hibernate/resume all have splash screens that share a style), OpenOffice & GIMP get oS splash screens, even the DVD install looks professional. OpenOffice in oS 11.1 has almost all the add-ons that Sun ships with Star Office + the Go-OO enhancements, and the Build Service is second to none.

With every release of openSUSE the complaints about it get chipped away.  You can often tell instantly how old someone's experience with oS is by what they complain about.  Still despite all of this I would recommend Ubuntu to some users because of the little elements of automation that Ubuntu includes like Jockey ("restricted drivers").  In oS the Nvidia & ATI repos are really easy to add, but you need to know that you have an ATI or Nvidia card in the first place.  Something like Jockey makes things very easy for the new user.
