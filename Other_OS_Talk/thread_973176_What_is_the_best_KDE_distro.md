### Post by mips on 2008-11-18
> **chucky chuckaluck said:**
> i prefer kdebase on arch to kdemod. i can add what apps i want instead of being stuck with what kdemod thinks is a good idea. i think it's actually more the 'arch way' than kdemod.

Huh? kdemod has it's own 'base' which is modular

kdemod-kdebase
kdemod-kdebase-doc
kdemod-kdebase-konsole
kdemod-kdebase-kwrite
kdemod-kdebase-runtime
kdemod-kdebase-runtime-doc
kdemod-kdebase-runtime-icons
kdemod-kdebase-workspace
kdemod-kdebase-workspace-doc
kdemod-kdebase-workspace-wallpapers

No one is forced to do a **pacman -S kdemod** or **pacman -S kdemod-complete** (This will make me puke), this is just what the creators think is a good starting point for most people.

Normal kde splits base up into 
kdebase
kdebase-runtime
kdebase-workspace. 

To me it looks like kdemod gives you more 'choice'. 10 options (kdemod) vs 3 options (kde). And this applies to the rest of kdemod as well.

I suspect people are probably not that familiar with kdemod and what it offers the end user.

Have a look at the repos and compare how many packages kde is comprised of vs kdemod. I think you will be amazed.

kdemod is more the arch way than kde is you ask me :) The devs are Arch users first and then kde users, that is the reason they created kdemod. If it was the other way around kdemod would have been available on other distros.
