### Post by igknighted on 2007-09-12
Sabayon, Mandriva, PCLOS, Mint, Mepis, Ubuntu and Fedora (amongst many others) all come with either Beryl or Compiz installed by default.  Now, if you have an Nvidia card you cannot use it without installing your proprietary drivers (included in Mandriva One, PCLOS, Mepis and Sabayon).  Intel and some AMD/ATI users can use it on all of those.  Other ATI users will be left trying to get XGL to work with the fglrx driver.  Sabayon, Mandriva and PCLOS can all do this by default.

There are two other 3d Desktop packages that I am aware of.  The first is called Metisse, and it is available with certain Mandriva One disks.  It is more funtional than the purely flashy Compiz, but looks pretty damn good none the less.

The last is looking glass.  I cannot imagine trying to use this on a production desktop (its fairly stable, but not very useful).  Look on distro watch for the looking glass live CD, which is built on SLAX.
