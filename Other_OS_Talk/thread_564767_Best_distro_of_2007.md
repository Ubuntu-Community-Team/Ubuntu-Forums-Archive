### Post by igknighted on 2007-10-18
> **wolfen69 said:**
> what can i do on sidux that i cant do on ubuntu? i tried sidux and found it to be ubuntu, only harder to set up. my vote goes to gutsy for its ease of installation and setup.

Sidux lets you run the very latest kernel, the very latest version of applications, and run a much more "slimmed down" system by cutting out stuff you don't need (for example, use dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg and look at all the choices it gives you for graphics drivers... if you have an intel graphics chip, why do you need all the rest?).  It is not a distro for new users, or for the faint of heart.  But it is innovative, good looking and more customizable than Ubuntu, and deserves credit for that.  Besides, compared to the state of Ubuntu at the end of 2006, we really haven't done that much.  Especially compared to what Sidux has done.  Thats why it got my vote.  Similar reasoning for Mandriva for putting out a tremendous product and coming back from some very rough times not too long ago.
