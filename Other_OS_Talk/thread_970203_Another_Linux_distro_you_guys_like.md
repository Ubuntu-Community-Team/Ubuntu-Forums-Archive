### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2008-11-04
> **SunnyRabbiera said:**
> my only problem with arch is its definitely NOT for beginners, you can tell its geared at the hardcore linux user and not the average joe who just came from windows looking for something new.
Arch has a lot of potential, but i think it needs a more user friendly approach in the future

In my opinion, Arch isn't intended to be a "mass appeal" distro any more than Gentoo or Slack are (though Arch is certainly easier). There is an Arch philosophy and either you like it or you don't. I really don't think their goal is to make Arch as appealing to the masses through design alterations, rather they want to convince people that their idea of "simplicity" is best.

Far as I'm concerned, a Windows user should go to Ubuntu. Period. Ubuntu and its kindred spirits (Suse, Fedora, Mint, Mepis, Mandriva) are going to appeal to Windows users looking for a change of pace. Arch is meant for people comfortable with Linux who want to get their hands a little dirtier. To try and "simplify" it to make newbies more comfortable betrays the whole idea.

Remember, I'm saying this as a Linux-tard who slowly taught himself these things. And it took me a while. Some guys on here apparently whacked through Arch in a weekend and had it all rolling. It took me probably five tries. :lolflag:

### Post by estyles on 2008-11-04
> **dirtylobster said:**
> 2nd the Linux Mint. It's so much better than the "real" Ubuntu.

I use Mint, but I wouldn't go quite that far.  It does some things better (I love Mint Menu), and does some things worse (for one, updates lag well behind Ubuntu).  The Mint community is friendly, but just no where near as big and helpful as the Ubuntu community.  Luckily, because there isn't much drift yet, you can get Mint support on Ubuntu forums.  That likely won't always be the case.

All-in-all, after using Mint since Elyssa came out (shortly after Hardy), I'm planning on switching back to Ubuntu when I get a few hours to install Intrepid.  I'm hopeful on being able to find Mint Menu as a .deb and seeing if I can install it on Ubuntu, but even if not, there are various minor reasons that I feel I prefer Ubuntu.

As for Arch, I've tried to install it about 5 times, with varying degrees of success.  I think I've finally decided that I'm going to give up on it until I get a new computer and then do everything from scratch.  My problems with Arch may have been hardware-related, or they may have been due to using the 64-bit version, or they may have been due to specific buggy packages - I'm not sure.  I think it's an awesome idea and intend to go back to it again in the future.  I do wish they had more GUI tools available...  I don't think that GUI tools necessarily go against the "Arch Way", something can still be powerful and infinitely configurable and still offer you easy-to-use tools - their developers don't necessarily agree.  Most things are still doable without GUI tools, but tend to take more time and research to do correctly.  Not that it's a deal-breaker.

Mandriva is very good for hardware support.  But otherwise I'm not a huge fan of it or OpenSUSE.  Both look very slick, but I'm just more used to the Ubuntu way of doing things.

Elive is very nice from the few minutes that I've played around with it.

OpenGEU (formerly Geubuntu) was a distro that I wanted to like - but I guess I just wished it was more Gnome and less Enlightenment.
