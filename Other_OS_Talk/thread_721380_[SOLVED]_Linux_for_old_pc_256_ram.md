### Post by MONODA on 2008-03-11
> How difficult is arch to learn and setup - say in comparison to Ubuntu?
say ubuntu is a 1 out of ten in difficulty, depending on how much you know the difficulty of setting up arch can vary. If you dont like working in the terminal or in text mode, then arch is  going to be a 10 since it does not come with a GUI installed or any other gui frontends for configuring your system, it is all using vi or nano. But if you are kind of familiar with this stuff and dont mind using nano or vi to configure your system, then it would be a 5 since there may be troubles with wireless cards and such (there were for me). But once you get your system setup, there's most likely no more configuring to do, ever, since arch is a rolling release meaning there isnt a real Official Release, it is always being updated. you just update to the latest version with one command, sudo pacman -Syu. I say use xfce for arch if you choose to use it. BTW the forums are pretty good, I got some useful info there. The wiki is awesome, check out the begginers guide.
