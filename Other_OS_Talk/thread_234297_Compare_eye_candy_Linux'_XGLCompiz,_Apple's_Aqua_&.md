### Post by hizaguchi on 2006-08-11
The important thing here is that it doesn't matter which is better right now.

I say this because whatever Vista ships with will be all there is for years to come.  Microsoft will keep the eye candy sources closed and will use .dlls that recognize each other and refuse to work with 3rd party effects.  Anyone who does come up with some anyway that are nice enough to become popular will get a cease and desist order. (see also: Windows XP visual styles)

Likewise, OSX will probably improve the effects with each incremental release, but will not offer a reasonable upgrade allowance to current users, ensuring that no matter how cool it seems now, you'll feel like you're being left out in the cold a couple years down the road when the new effects and features aren't available for your version of OS version 10.  (see also: Dashboard and Panther users)

Xgl/Xegl, on the other hand, will continue to improve and continue to be freely available to anyone.  It is (or will be) capable of anything the others can do, but will also remain customizable and extendable.

### Post by Rhapsody on 2006-08-18
Well, once Firefox froze after playing both the Mac and Vista demos (though strangely not after the XGL/Compiz demo), I had the following thoughts.

Whoever made the Linux video was clearly showing off. It was far longer than either of the others, and demonstrated a load of features that you'd only use to show off to inexperienced users. I had trouble figuring out at what point any of that was more useful than just hitting Ctrl+F1/F2/F3/F4 to switch between my desktops like I do now. I also thought of my PC screaming at me to stop if I ever attempted the 'video across two desktops' trick. Could a GeForce4 MX *ever* do that with any sort of speed? But why would I even want to watch a video like that?

The Mac video was fairly well done, and the ability to see all four desktops at the same time and drag a window from one into the other caught me eye (though I do get the impression that the whole point of multiple desktops is to avoid doing just that). It was the worst looking of any, but demonstrated some sensibility. Funny that Apple is gaining common sense while Microsoft seem to be haemorraging it at the moment.

The Vista demo was about what I expected. Terrible. Most of the features were minor advancements over what I got in Windows XP, nothing to jump up and down about. The 'flip-through' windows looked fairly impressive, but I also got the impression that it would be more fiddly and long-winded than just using the taskbar or Alt+Tab to switch windows. Additionally, I could've sworn I saw the bottom of the video say 'Reduced Productivity' at one point, which is pretty appropriate. Most of the features I've seen here will reduce productivity in the short-run while people play with them, but make little difference in the long-run.

Edit: Now that I think about it more, I'm also finding myself imagining people to associate with these videos.

The Mac guy is calmly explaining to a group of people how these new GUI features will enhance their computing experience and productivity. The Vista guy is looking rather nervous and trying to explain to a large group of critics why they seem to have so few new features to show. Meanwhile, the Linux guy is jumping up and down, waving his arms about wildly, and shouting about how great XGL/Compiz is to everyone in a five mile radius of him.

I'm probably still going to be using XGL/Compiz at some point in the future, but whoever made that video was really just showing off. The Mac and Vista videos were techincal demos, the Linux video was some guy showing off his 'awesome new video card'. Or at least that's how it looked to me.
