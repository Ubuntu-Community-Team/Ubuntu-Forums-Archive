### Post by PurplePenguin on 2007-03-24
Stable releases come out every six months (regardless of what you consider to be "stable").  Enterprise releases (with long term support and enhanced operating stability and polish) are expected to come out every 12 - 24 months.

There will always be a period after any release where bugs will pop up and need to be fixed, LTS or not.

Not sure where you get your info, but most people have had no issues with Edgy.  The ones that have are just very vocal.  If you are refering to the recent kernel upgrade that broke packages, there was an error with people using 3rd party repo's that didn't update packages quickly enough, or people with 3rd party drivers that didn't know they needed to update them with a kernel upgrade.  This could just as easily happen with Dapper (and if/when they upgrade dapper's kernel it probably will).  Its not Edgy being unstable.  As for other stuff, just run the liveCD and see if it supports your hardware.  Lots of things are better supported in Edgy and lots are better in Dapper.  If you remember, the Dapper release was a disaster.  There were way more issues at the time of release than with edgy.  After a few weeks the issues get fixed and life moves on.  They even released an updated Dapper install disk due to all the errors (6.06.1).  The same is true of Edgy, except the kernel deal, but thats not edgy's fault.

If you are using Dapper, going to edgy is like having Win98 or win2000 and running that when XP is available.  Upgrading in rare cases causes issues with a few things that aren't supported as well, but overall you get better performance along with newer stuff.  These days people don't build stuff for dapper because its old.  Even beryl doesn't care about Dapper really.  You should stick with the newest version unless you have a specific issue with your system.

The next LTS likely wont be out for another year ( April '08 ).  You will want to upgrade before then or you will be left behind.
