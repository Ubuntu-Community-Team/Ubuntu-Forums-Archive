### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-03-10
When I first started tinkering with Linux, I tried Ubuntu first, but the old IBM Thinkpad a30 (yes kids, A-30) I was using did not really get along with Ubuntu. Too much OS, not enough system, I think (XP just laughed at me by the way, at least Ubuntu TRIED). My next step was Puppy...

It was beautiful! Don't get me wrong here, I love Ubuntu and I use it on my new laptop now, but Puppy was the one to really get things moving for me last year. It's use of RAM really amused me, and when I was reading about it, I figured it would look like hell, since it was so small. Boy was I wrong...

I have since moved puppy off my laptop and onto my keychain. The USB booting is flawless, though the brand new Dell does not like Xorg in Puppy. (even the old Thinkpad did not mind Xorg) Other than a wireless issue that I am currently working on when I am not here lurking nd yaking with y'all, it simply works just as well as Ubuntu. I have not yet made it pretty, but that is comming.

It is too bad that the motherboard on my old thinkpad died, I would have liked to do a side by side with the Dell Insperion bought to replace it. (After a near nervous breakdown at the cost.)

The only grip I can really think of for Puppy is that is activates everything with one click, and no matter how much faster it is, every time I boot Puppy, I forget and it annoys me for about two seconds.

Leo
