### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2009-01-22
From the article:

> [SIZE="3"]Netbooks have become a problem for Microsoft because they ship with a version of Windows XP Home that's about half the price of the desktop version. According to solution providers, Microsoft charges local OEMs $32 for XP Home on netbooks, compared to around $65 for XP Home on desktops. Large OEMs are believed to pay much less. [/SIZE]


The article is interesting - but a new pricing structure is pretty much expected, especially if W7 is as good as people say it is.

I have never been able to work out why MS discounting XP to an entire market segment (to as low as $10 per licence, I believe)has not been regarded as uncompetitive behaviour (at least by European authorities).  It seems no different in principle from "dumping" low priced product onto a geographical market in order to capture market share and kill off competitors.  I understand that behaviour is illegal in many countries (mine included) yet no regulatory authority has even blinked at MS for its current netbook strategy.

It was particular funny to me that in a separate article about members of the French Parliament seeking to expand Linux adoption, several "commentators" were screaming "protectionism!" - and yet there was no hint of artificial disadvantage in what they were suggesting - just enforcing genuine price/performance/features competition.

MS will still have to be particularly clever with their W7 pricing in this market (netbooks) if they are to keep the pre-loaded windows machines at the pricing sweet-spot achieved by ASUS, Acer et al.
