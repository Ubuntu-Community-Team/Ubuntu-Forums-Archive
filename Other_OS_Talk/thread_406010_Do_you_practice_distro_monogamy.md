### Post by wrycatcher on 2007-04-10
I've noticed a tendency of many users to boast over the number of distros they've been with over the years.  There are some regrettable "one-nighters"...("why did I ever install **that**?").   Other distro relationships seem to end painfully after many years with acerbic statements like "*you just aren't what I thought you were*" or "*you've changed*", "*I need more than just a pretty interface*", or the popular "*we've just grown apart*".  Some users seem to treat their distro relationship as exclusive while other users see multiple distros at the same time, keeping things intentionally non-commital.

Most Linux users will admit their somewhat painful searches for the distro (or operating system) of their dreams.  They have rejected the "arranged marriage" (Windows).  Some of us are still searching, not ready to commit to any one distro, while others of us think we have finally found what we have been seeking in an distro, and are ready to "settle down".  I'm somewhere between the two camps.   I don't have the patience or time to evaluate the dearth of distros out there, and yet I keep restlessly thinking "*am I missing something...is there something better*"?  To temper this discontent, I'm always quick to remember that no distro is perfect.  Each has its specific strengths and weaknesses.  So, while my presently-preferred distro (Ubuntu) will fall short in some areas, I also realize that I could do much worse considering the half-baked distros that clutter the Linux landscape.  Also, I realize the each distro is maturing and could get better with age.  Distro monogamy is rare in Linux land, but it does exist.   In reality, most of us are forever looking over the fence at the new neighbor distro who just moved in...

Personally, I've tried Ubuntu, Fluxbuntu, DSL, Debian and Fedora.  I'm currently content with Ubuntu (Edgy) though I eagerly anticipate Feisty's release.  I guess I tend to keep one primary Linux distro installed at any one time, running LiveCD versions of other distros for ongoing evaluation purposes.
