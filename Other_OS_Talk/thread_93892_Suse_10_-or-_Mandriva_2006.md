### Post by asimon on 2005-11-23
[QUOTE=bored2k]Compared to Debian's, how good are the repos? I'd love to try it... but it's just ridiculously big.[/QUOTE] I would say good, but of course there is still big room for improvements. There are plenty of apt repositores at [gwdg.de](ftp://ftp.gwdg.de/pub/linux/suse/apt/SuSE/10.0-i386). The main problem is that there are many different repos and sometimes packages from one can clash with packages from an other repo (very annoying for newbies). I had one or two such cases but the maintainers where friendly and quick to fix the issues. Most packagers seem to do a good job maintaing the packages in their repos and keep them up to date. The different packagers are currently working under the opensuse umbrella on general packaging guidelines and other improvements and for next year SUSE wants to offer a world-class infrastructure for the packagers like build servers, bug tracking system, etc. I also remember that I read on the mailing list that for SUSE 10.1 they want to improve the package management module of YaST, and want especially make it easier for people to add third-party repos. Overall my expieriences of using SUSE 10.0 (and it's betas) for three month very very good. I didn't had more problems with SUSE packages then with Ubuntu packages, I encounter problems with both, just different problems. I really can't say that there is a clear winner in quality or otherwise. Both are great distros. SUSE may have a little advantage with YaST and it's various gui modules to configure hardware, network connections, monitor resolution, etc. Neither gnome's system tools nor the ones for KDE offer currently as much as YaST.

EDIT: Yes, it's rediculous big. Having to download 5 CD images is really a little bit overkill. There is already somewhere a 1-CD install project. It would do SUSE good to get that official.
