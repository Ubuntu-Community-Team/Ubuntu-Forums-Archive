### Post by shafin on 2007-12-07
I finally got KDE 4 up and running on virtualbox,I've been trying since the alpha days,tried out the alpha1 liveCD,the beta liveCD,and finally,the RC1 LiveCD today.
I have to admit that i'm impressed, plasma has been a heck of an improvement with the panel and widgets,they feel smooth even in my 384 MB Ram virtual machine. The oxygen theme is great,it just beats almost everything out there.And dolphin looks sexy too. 

BTW,i'm still a Gnome boy, so KDE will remain in the Live CD images and virtualbox,but I really hope screenlets make some progress like plasma.

As I was in a virtual system,I cant really review the hardware improvements like the sound system,when I burn the image,i'll let you know.

### Post by marsmissionaries on 2007-12-08
I've been compiling from SVN a little over a month. Everything works beautifully. (most large bugs are gone) The biggest issues i see right now is just the saving of configuration which is being worked on. Also, some ARGB issues that are of relation to other projects, and an intel issue because they don't identify screens correctly.

All of this will probably be fixed soon, if you guys are curious watch [http://planetkde.org](http://planetkde.org) and the mailing lists. I also frequent the IRC channels as it's a great way to see what kind of bugs are floating around in there.
