### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-07-21
I've been playing with Sabayon Business Edition 1.0 for the past day, and while at first I was unimpressed, it's now growing on me. I'm reviewing the distro as a guest for an upstart blog (this isn't the review) and in doing so, I'm going to use this edition for at least the weekend.

If you don't know about Sabayon Linux, you could check out [its Wikipedia article]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabayon_Linux"), or read [some reviews that have been written for it on Distrowatch]("http://distrowatch.com/table.php?distribution=sabayon"). What makes this version different are a few things:
[LIST=1]
[*]The ISO size is 1.8GB, or about half of the size of the full Sabayon 3.3 DVD
[*]No 3D is included, nor are any games (except the standard KDE Games)
[*]Users can turn on or off about six package groups during the install
[*]The distribution uses the Stable branch, instead of Sabayon's usual use of the Test branch
[/LIST]

When you install Sabayon, your system (assuming it's not extremely new) will run slowly, and I think that this is because Sabayon, not being binary, does a shoddy job of compiling the source for permanent use for the end user. However, as you replace these components, your system performance will get closer and closer to a pure Gentoo system. Right now, I'm using the really neat GenKernel tool to compile a new kernel for my system, and it's far easier than I expected. You just grab the sources, read over the GenKernel article in the Gentoo wiki (and look at the Sabayon Wiki's custom kernel article), run GenKernel, and you enter a menu that lets you easily enable or disable all sorts of kernel options. This allowed me to disable NVidia support, as well as Firewire, Macintosh devices, and other devices I have no use for. I'll update on how this affects my system performance later.

Anyway, this is a nice "sober" version of Sabayon for those who would like to jump into Gentoo (at their own pace, which is the beauty of Sabayon) and have no desire for big-time 3D and gaming support. It still includes a great suite of productivity tools, and as always looks great, with the best font rendering Linux has to offer.
