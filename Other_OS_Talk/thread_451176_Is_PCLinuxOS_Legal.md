The codecs used to play Windows or MPEG media files are open source implementations and completely independent of any Microsoft software. There is NO issue of *copyright infringement* in including such codecs with a distribution; nor is there any issue of *GPL violation* as the codecs are open source code. There MAY be an issue of* patent infringement* but (as Aysiu described) that is really up to the courts to decide (and governments are becoming decreasingly sympathetic towards software patents).

In a similar vein, DVD playback is neither an issue of patents or of copyrights. It MIGHT be considered a violation of the US's DMCA legislation to use 'libdvdcss' but this has never been tested in court and 'libdvdcss' proponents argue that it is not a violation because the mechanism that it circumvents is not a copyright protection scheme (i.e., you can copy DVDs without ever unencoding them).
