### Post by AdamWill on 2007-07-27
Let's see, a few points :)

On the 'stable' point, Freddy is kinda right and kinda wrong. It's true that after we merged with Conectiva we shifted to a one-year release cycle; 2007 came out one year after 2006. The main reason for this was to try to improve stability. In a sense it did work: 2007 was a better release than 2006. It turned out to have drawbacks too, though. We've now gone back to a six month release cycle (2007 Spring came six months after 2007 and 2008 will come six months after 2007 Spring), and in practical terms, our general approach to stability versus freshness is pretty much the same as it always was in the past. Merging with Conectiva was more or less coincidental - it wasn't the *cause* of this or the *result* of it.

rocknrolf - current Cooker (the development distro) has kcheckgmail-0.5.6-1mdv2008.0 and miro-0.9.8-4mdv2008.0 .
