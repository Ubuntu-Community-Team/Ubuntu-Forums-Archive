Why Microsoft is trying to extort us....
Sep 12 '07

The Bottom Line Why the Interop Vendor License is boloney!

And why it is not working!

By now you've probably become aware of Microsoft's latest brain fart, patent "protection" for Linux users, all the creator of your distribution has to do is stab Linux, Free Software, the GPL, and their user's Freedom in the back, and in exchange, we can all use the Tahoma font and Microsoft WMA!

This is a bunch of horse hockey, for many reasons, but the law, even in the United States, sides with Linux and the GPL, in saying we are obligated to license absolutely nothing from Microsoft, in order to use compatible functionality.

And for the most part, the Microsoft "intellectual property" issue in Linux is moot, for the average user, there could very well be no Tahoma font, or WMA codec, and we would neither have any good reason to miss it, or for that matter, to care, not just because nobody on Linux has any reason to want to use Microsoft media formats, but nobody in their right mind would use those on Windows either, since it locks them into Microsoft's arbitrary "upgrade" cycle, else their media just may suddenly "stop" working, if they fail to use the newest version of Windows.

So what is Microsoft's Interop Vendor Alliance?

Essentially Microsoft has walked up to Linspire, Xandros, and Suse, and bribed them with a truckload of money, in exchange the Linux vendor agrees to the absolute farce that they owe Microsoft the time of day, and then Microsoft gives all users of that distro a "license" that they didn't need, to use Microsoft technology.

Essentially Microsoft has no credible case, and so they pay off these Linux vendors to cooperate with Microsoft's goal of making an end run around the GNU GPL license, and the Freedom therein.

So what is the GNU/Linux community doing to sound the alarm, and raise the shields?

Well thats sort of what the GPL version 3 is all about, stopping illegitimate "deals" Microsoft makes in violation of the spirit of Free Software.

GPL 3 was designed to guarantee that our Freedoms are preserved, by making it ILLEGAL for any software licensed under the GPL to concurrently have one of these arrangements with Microsoft.

So how does this affect me?

Well, for the present, it really doesn't, but as more and more software is licensed under GPL3, it means that it will be illegal for these renegade Linux vendors to bundle GPL 3 software in their distribution, unless they back out of this asinine Microsoft deal first.

What that means is that, it might well end up being in the future that users of these systems cannot legally update their kernel, their window managers (such as KDE and GNOME, and others), and their other basic software.

So essentially you can start viewing Suse, Xandros, and Linspire as dead platforms, that are living on borrowed time, unless they can find a way to back out of their Microsoft backstab deal.

In the end, it's sad that GPL 3 has had to make users decide between their favorite Linux distribution, or their Freedom, but in this case, the ends justify the means, because it guarantees the future of Linux belongs to us, and not the whim of the Redmond Bully.

So if I want off Windows, because I'm sick of it, what does this mean for me?

It means that you should find a distribution other than Suse, Linspire, or Xandros, which isn't a big loss since those distributions are not as user friendly as they're commonly trumped up to be, and there are still hundreds of other choices open to you, so hopefully the GPL 3 can put out the spike strip in front of Microsoft's pursuit to destroy Linux, time will tell, but we've dealt with this kind of crap from Microsoft time and time again, and always managed to hold the line, if not this particular "brand" of attack, but I have faith that Linux will hold the line.

The Bottom Line:

You know, what convinced me to switch to Linux, is Microsoft's daylight theft of basic user Freedom in their own operating systems, finally reaching a point in Vista that is the equivalent of an "intolerable act".

Microsoft is using their time tested strategy of "cut off the head, and the body dies", but in Linux's case, two more just grow back in it's place.

Whenever I think about Vista, the Product Activator, and the DRM technology it is based on, in addition to being absolutely horrified, I can't help but to think of the Master Control Program and Tron from the old Disney classic:

Master Control Program: "You're in trouble, program. Why don't you make it easy on yourself. Who's your user?"

CLU: "Forget it, mister high-and-mighty Master Control! You're not making me talk!"

Master Control Program: "Suit yourself."

(Master Control Program proceeds to derezz the user's program for obeying the user's commands)

Later in the movie:

Tron: "My User has information that could... that could make this a free system again! No, really! You'd have programs lined up just to use this place, and no MCP looking over your shoulder."

Who knew Ubuntu was compatible with the ENCOM mainframe?

Anyway, if you need me, I'll be painting my face blue and putting on my kilt, so that I may moon Microsoft and shout "You can take my Tahoma font! But you'll never have my Freedom!"
