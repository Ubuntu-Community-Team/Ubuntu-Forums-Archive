### Post by Darganot on 2008-03-16
The answer to this question really depends on your knowledge of and skill with Linux and CLI.  You could probably build a proper Gentoo kernel for your hardware with enough time and patience.  Arch is a simpler version but still requires some config.  

Sabayon Linux is a pre-built version of Gentoo that might work well for your advanced hardware.  They also have a dedicated community with many ex-ubuntuers.

Have you looked into Fedora or any other RPM based distro?   Fedora and some of it's derivatives tend to offer a wide variety of hardware support.  I prefer BLAG but that's for reasons of principal.  I think Yoper is RPM based but it's a very small distro, I wasn't even sure it was still going.  Try something mainstream like Fedora 8.
