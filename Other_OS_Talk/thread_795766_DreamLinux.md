### Post by Ravenwolf37 on 2008-11-20
Being rather new to Linux, I wanted to see as many distros as I could. So I booted up an old machine. Found a windows boot disk, partitioned and formatted my drive, downloaded a bunch of iso's and started installing. Of course I am a HUGE fan of Ubuntu. I've tried all of the flavors too: Xubuntu was different looking, but it made me not like xfce (from that moment on I perceived it as "cheap" if that makes any sense even though it's all free :P). Kubuntu was cool, but too slow on my 4 or 5 year old test mule computer. Ubuntu Studio got my hopes up as I have been into computer music studios on windows based machines for a long time now (25 years give or take a few :P), and although I was very impressed with just how much was offered for free, it really isn't there yet (but getting closer, and very very cool for free I might add). I tried fedora, jacklab, puppy linux, damn small linux, Linux Mint 5, Linpus (although it was just a live cd and I never did find out how to install it :P), PCLinuxOS2007, and tinyme. After all of that I installed Ubuntu as my favorite and used it as my desktop on my upstairs computer for spreadsheets, word processing, email, and web browsing. I loved the interface and was always amazed that this was free! :). But then after about a month in Ubuntu began to lock up on me. I'd be working/playing and the mouse would just freeze and game over! I was highly bummed. So I went online searching for a fix and stumbled upon a new name (to me anyway) dreamlinux. I've always wanted a MAC but just can't get my cheap self to buy one. I can easily afford one, but after going to the store and playing with one for 2 hours, I mean really taking it through the paces, I determined that, yeah it's pretty cool, but not worth the money if I can get a good linux system up and running. So anyway, I see what dreamlinux looks like, but then I see it is xfce (xcfe?? whichever it is :P). I am intrigued though. I download the iso, and try to install it. I have to admit, it was not fun to install, very confusing indeed. Partitioning was a bear so I said screw it and formatted the entire drive and did a complete install. The live cd screen shows "easy install" on the desktop and I was like cool! This should be easy LOL. Here this is to easy install apps after you are done with the operating system install! There is a DL something or other icon that handles the install. That's all I can say from memory is that the install screen should be followed carefully to not mess anything up. I made it through after a few attempts (maybe I'm just slow though, very possible :P). Anyway, after all is said and done I LOVE dreamlinux. I am using it right now. Now I'm still an ubuntu lover, all the distros give it their best and should be proud. But for my particular setup it works great (an old emachines computer that someone was throwing out due to viruses on windows :P celeron 2.66, 512mb ram, 80 gb hd). The interface took a little bit to setup the cool awn bar at the bottom of the screen, but once I figured that out, it is now quick and easy to change the icons. The 3d graphic cube thingie works great (even with an integrated graphics card), and it is fairly quick, the graphics are really clean and nice looking. And the main thing I love about it on my particular computer (I am going to try another install on another junker I got for free to make sure it's stable on that one as well), but the thing I love is that it hasn't frozen up on me once yet (knock on wood). Anyway, I just thought I'd share my own experience with dreamlinux 3.5 something 4? I think is what I burned onto cd?? shoot, I don't remember now :(. Anyway, I give it a 10 for my needs: stability, cool look and feel, rather quick, email, web, word processing, spreadsheet, music player, photos, disk burning... peace :)
