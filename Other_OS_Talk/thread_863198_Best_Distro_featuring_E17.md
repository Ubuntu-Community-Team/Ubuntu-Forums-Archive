### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2008-07-18
I agree with Molom - elive was great when it started but lags terribly now.

The three leaders (IMO) are [Maryan]("http://en.maryanlinux.com/content/view/7/1/"), [OpenGeu]("http://opengeu.intilinux.com/Home.html") and [OzOS]("http://cafelinux.org/OzOs/content/ozos-iso-downloads-torrents").

Avoid the "e" build of gOS rocket.  It is ancient. 

Maryan, OpenGeu and OzOS each takes a different approach to e17 on Ubuntu and it is "enlightening" to compare them.

They all use fairly up-to-date builds of e17 (OzOS actually compiles e17 directly from the cvs and is update-able daily if you want to go that far.)  On the other hand Maryan uses more e17 gadgets and apps by default and OpenGeu tends to be hybrid between e17 and Gnome.

On why e isn't more popular, I think its history of instability, total rewrites and drawn out development has been a disappointment to many who saw, or even still see, its potential as a unique and powerful desktop.  (I personally forgive the devs everything). 

I'm not an expert, but I've followed it for the past year and I'd have to say that it is "very close" to being finished.  If you have used elive and survived, I think you will find any of the suggested distros a breath of fresh air. 

I currently use OzOS on three machines (PC, laptop and netbook) and have both Maryan and OpenGeu in VMs.

(I am also part of the OzOS team, so take my personal recommendation as biased)
