### Post by Ateo on 2007-04-24
i don't understand. what's the point of 'installing' a distribution? That won't teach you much, if anything. Perhaps you should understand what a distribution is before embarking on this journey.

A distribution takes the Linux kernel and wraps  bunch of software around it. Distributions have a 'base system' unique to that distribution which includes a bunch of startup scripts to get your system up and running loading mods and doing what it needs to do to actually GET your system up. They are all the same in concept, just different in methods.

After the base system comes your custom system which is mostly the same across Linux/UNIX platforms. In other words, it's the stuff you want to install be it a desktop or LAMP. They all compile nearly the same at this point.

Probably the most most important thing to learn is how any given distribution works... How THEY operate... If you're comfortable with it, stick with it.

The bottom line is, unless you're a developer of the kernel, you'll never learn Linux in and of itself. You will only learn how to operate any given distribution. However, learning one makes learning others a hell of a lot easier.

In my opinion, it's best to learn and understand the most popular distributions at the time. Just because you've 'installed' one million distributions doesn't mean you know anything. It just means you've installed one million distributions. Congrats.
