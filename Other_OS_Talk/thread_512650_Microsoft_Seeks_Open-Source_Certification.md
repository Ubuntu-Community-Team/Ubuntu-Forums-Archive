### Post by shafin on 2007-07-29
> After months of antagonizing the open-source community, Microsoft Corp. now appears to be trying to engage it by seeking an official stamp of approval for the licenses that the company uses to share its own software and source code.
During a keynote speech at the O'Reilly Open Source Convention in Portland, Ore., on Thursday, Bill Hilf, Microsoft's general manager of platform strategy, said that the software vendor is submitting its so-called [shared source licenses]("http://www.microsoft.com/resources/sharedsource/licensingbasics/sharedsourcelicenses.mspx") to the [Open Source Initiative]("http://www.opensource.org/") for certification as true open-source licenses.
The plans were also detailed on [Port 25]("http://port25.technet.com/archive/2007/07/26/intelligent-design-the-osi-and-microsoft.aspx"), a blog written by workers at Microsoft's Open Source Software Lab.
