### Post by Grey Box on 2007-11-26
It depends on what level you're taking things. Actually, on the surface there is the most difference. The look and feel can be quite different with, say, Ubuntu vs. OpenSuSE or something like that. Some are using different window managers (take KDE vs. Gnome vs. Fluxbox vs. Xfce, etc), and some are using different default applications and security policies. Some offer very nice automated distro-upgrades (like Ubuntu), and others do not.

But at the bottom level, it's the same basic utilities, commands and so on to get the basic job done. Most store stuff in the same places and so forth, but as ppl say, you find differences in scripts and booting sequences and so on, but I imagine that if you're going to go playing around there you'd be reading howto's and manuals fairly closely. 

But, anticipating the age-old complaint "too much variety" - it might seem so at first, but if you think about how you'd go about installing an OS for your mother, your kids, your server, your bank, yourself, your router, your old laptop, your new laptop etc., then the differences are a godsend.
