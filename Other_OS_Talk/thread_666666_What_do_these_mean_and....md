Hello Fiel. This is a major oversimplification, but it might help you understand.

I am going to make an analogy from a house. The Linux kernel would be the foundation for the house. Everything else sits on it. The next layer of this house is your X-server, which comprises the floor joists and sub-floor. Your next layer is the desktop environment, which would be the floor. 

The desktop environment or window manager is the program that controls how your windows look and feel. Three popular desktop environments are Gnome, KDE and Xfce:
Gnome: [http://www.gnome.org/](http://www.gnome.org/)
KDE: [http://kde.org/](http://kde.org/)
XFCE: [http://www.xfce.org/](http://www.xfce.org/)

In Windows, you only have one window manager and you can't really customize it all that much. Linux, however, is about choice, so you can choose how your desktop looks, feels and acts. The different desktop managers are choices of different types of floors. You might like carpet, or you might prefer hardwood, or you might want something plain and basic like concrete. 

Once you've chosen your flooring, you can add the rooms (programs) and make your computer truly yours.

To your other question, I may be wrong, but I think the biggest difference between Ubuntu and Fedora is package management. Ubuntu is based on Debian and uses apt-get to manage the .deb packages. Fedora is Red Hat based and uses yum to manage the .rpm package.

### Post by dptxp on 2008-01-16
To understand install one first, say Ubuntu (gnome). Next install kde-core (smaller than kubuntu-desktop). You can choose any at time of log-in. Similarly you can add XFCE and others. The Desktop managers change the look and feel of your desktop. Some are hard on resources, some are light. The choice depends upon your taste (choose lighter ones for old machines).
MEPIS uses KDE. So does PCLOS. And Knoppix. And Sidux. KDE gives user more power, there are more controls.
Gnome is easy to navigate, I find KDE too cluttered. But then there are hardcore KDE fans, they will never settle for Gnme. Some use Fluxbox for speed. Puppy Linux perhaps uses JWM, another light DM.
