### Post by fistfullofroses on 2008-12-17
So, I recently acquired an old IBM Thinkpad 365XD (16mb RAM, 1gb HDD, floppy, cdrom, 2 pcmcia slots), and in trying to figure out what OS I should install on it, I figured I would relive the glorious days of IBM's OS/2 as the drivers for this laptop were available for OS/2. 

Installation was easier than I remember, as was the driver installation process. I had forgotten IBM Works, what a delight. I had forgotten the built-in DOS and Win3x emulators as well. Further, I had totally forgotten about the voice recognition software that shipped in the bonus pack. 

I was wondering if any of you had used OS/2 recently or in the past, and whether or not you have any thoughts on this long forgotten operating system (that was poorly stabbed in the back by Microsoft's NT, ending its life prematurely).

### Post by ezsit on 2008-12-18
I used OS/2 from version 2.1 though 4.0 and loved it. I ran it on a 486, a Pentium I 120mhz, and a Pentium II 400mhz.
