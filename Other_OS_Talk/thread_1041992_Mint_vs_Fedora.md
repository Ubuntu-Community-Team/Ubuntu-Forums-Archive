### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-01-17
Mint is Ubuntu with multimedia codecs installed and Mint-specific tools. Fedora doesn't baby you with these things; if you want something like Flash, and don't want a free implementation, you will have to add a repository like RPMfusion and install from there (or manually). Outside of their philosophical differences, the packages managers are the biggest difference, IMO.

Outside of that, you are comparing, basically, Ubuntu to Fedora, which has been done many times. Look [here]("http://polishlinux.org/choose/comparison/?distro1=Ubuntu&distro2=Fedora") first. Then, for discussion, look at these threads:

### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-01-17
Do I prefer one over the other? Absolutely.
While Mint is high on my list for those new to Linux, Fedora is on my personal favorites list.

Which would I suggest? It depends.
If you are looking for a quick setup and having a fully functional, media ready, system "out of the box", Mint wins. If you are looking for a system that requires a little more input on part of the user, and won't hold your hand as much without being completely "do it yourself", Fedora wins. 

These are very general breakdowns, and I *actually* suggest trying both and choosing one yourself. Remember you are on Ubuntu forums, and while many of us use and appreciate other distributions, many of us are also biased, especially toward Debian-based/Ubuntu-based systems.

I personally use Ubuntu to provide the most accurate help when I'm on the forums, but for my personal work I use FreeBSD or (GASP) Fedora.

Side note: Fedora is no longer referred to as Fedora Core, and since Fedora 7, has simply been "Fedora".
