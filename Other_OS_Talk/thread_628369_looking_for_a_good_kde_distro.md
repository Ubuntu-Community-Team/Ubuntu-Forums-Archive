### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2007-12-01
PClinux is a good bet, granted it does not meet your "bleeding edge" requirements but it does its job well.
Pclinux has one of the best teams of packagers out there, tex and the ripper gang have made a wonderful combination of functionality, stability and a semi decent repository.
I have found also for a KDE distro PClinux is actually very ace, its actually a featherweight compared to kubuntu in many respects.
Its release cycle is a bit slow around the edges, but this is because the team would rather have stable and working system as opposed to a bleeding edge one.
really bleeding edge software should be last on your list of priorities, remember that latest doesnt always mean greatest...
look at Vista, it is the latest version of windows yet it sucks compared to XP in stability.
stability should be your top priority, sure having the "latest and greatest" stuff sounds nice at first but once you realize that sometimes it can be like a glass house on the San Andreas fault you will be thankful that some software is made of reinforced steel surrounded by earthquake proof concrete
