### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-01-26
It's funny, but I was just thinking, "It's been a while since we've had a Solaris/OpenSolaris thread". :D

I like Solaris/OpenSolaris, and definitely think it has a place. I enjoy testing their releases, but I'm on an x86 machine, and despite the fact that it runs, I can't help but think it would run happier on a SPARC. 

That said, I found OpenSolaris 2008.11 to be an improvement over 2008.05, despite the face that the installation took nearly twice as long on my testing machine. That was a major disappointment. The package management increased its friendliness between the two releases, and there was better hardware support. The 10/08 release of Solaris somehow saw better results on my testing machine. 

ZFS *is* hungry, and when compared to the testing of BTRFS and the HAMMER filesystems, comes up short from my experience.

Overall, I expect to continue seeing great things.

On the surface they look *very* similar. However, Ubuntu is a Linux Operating System. If I'm not mistaken, Solaris is one of few operating systems that are still Unix, as it passes the Single Unix Specification. Ubuntu/Fedora/etc are only "unix-like."

I would argue that most casual computer users (those who word-process, play games, use their computer for general office use/media work) could not tell a difference between Solaris and Ubuntu other than appearance.

However at it's core, Solaris is significantly different than Ubuntu.
