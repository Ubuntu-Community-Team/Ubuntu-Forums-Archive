### Post by b9anders on 2007-12-11
e17, much as I love it, is a terrible choice for stability, tbh.

But yes, debian is definitely the way to go for stability. Go etch/stable if you want it rock solid - most desktop users will find lenny/testing to be more than solid enough for their needs and it has more up to date packages than ubuntu. So you can compize, add/remove and synapticate to your heart's delight. Except for synaptic, they don't come pre-installed though. But for compiz, I'd suggest having a look at the repository of shame (google it). A simply eminent repository for compiz fusion packages for any debian user.

The beauty of debian is that you choose your own stability and upto-dateness. You can start from a base of etch, pull in packages from lenny if you want something newer, upgrade to lenny altogether if you want a newer system and from there pull in packages you like from sid/unstable if you require the very latest of the latest, or upgrade to sid altogether if you want a system living perpetually on the bleeding edge. Each step of the way you sacrifice some potential stability for it of course, but the choice is yours. For most users, lenny, with the occasional dipping into sid gives a very nice blend of stability, new packages with the option for going bleeding edge on choice applications when you just can't wait for it to trickle into testing.

a) Navigate to Daryna > Administration > Language Support
b) After confirming the installation of additional packages, scroll down to Korean, select it, then click Apply.

a) Navigate to Daryna > Preferences > Keyboard
