### Post by timzak on 2008-06-19
A little bit of tweaking to Xubuntu will get it using a lot less memory, and allow you to use one of the most user friendly distros out.  Just disable as many unneeded services and auto-started apps as you can and you should get it down to about 80-90 MB of used ram on bootup.  Then just use Synaptic to uninstall all unneeded apps and make sure you use low memory footprint apps only.

Another good one that is pretty user friendly is AntiX.  It uses about 50 MB out of the box, with Fluxbox and Ubuntu repositories.
