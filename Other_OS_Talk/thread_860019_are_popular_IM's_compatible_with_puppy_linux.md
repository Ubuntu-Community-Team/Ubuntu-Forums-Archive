### Post by issih on 2008-07-15
When it comes to puppy, I have only ever tinkered with it for my own amusement, so I don't know that much. I can tell you that there are linux IM programs capable of communicating using lots of different IM protocols.

Pidgin (nee gaim) is one such program but there are many others.

I have no idea whether these things are readily available from puppy's package manager system (i.e. as a dotpup or dotpet) but its probably easiest by far for you to just play and find out.

Puppy has a very neat trick whereby it can be run from a livecd, but any changes are written back to that cd/dvd platter as a multisession track.

[http://www.puppylinux.com/multi-puppy.htm](http://www.puppylinux.com/multi-puppy.htm)

This means you can follow those instructions, then boot from your cd drive and play to your hearts content installing different IM clients until you decide if you are happy. In the mean time no changes are made to your computer, but every time you throw that dvd into your drive and boot from it you will be back to where you left off last time.

In essence you get to mess around in your very own sandbox, without fear of trashing anything. even better, if you do totally bork the setup, just choose not to save the file when you reboot and the changes in that session will be thrown away.

Once you are confident that puppy is going to do what you want you could then install it to the hard drive, if you wanted.

Hope that helps.
