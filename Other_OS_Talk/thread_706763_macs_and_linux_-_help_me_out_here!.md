### Post by igknighted on 2008-02-25
Security is about even between the two.  Both are secure enough that you are really only at risk of user error except in very rare occasions.

As for ease of use... that totally depends.  For someone that wants to let the computer do everything and not deal with anything themselves, Macs are great.  They usually can set everything up with little to no fuss.  Even software install is just drag/drop into a folder.  However, if you want a Mac to do something in a way other than it wants to default to... get ready for a huge fight.  In this regard, if you are the type of person that (a) needs to use some non-standard configuration, or (b) likes to be able to do something yourself, then you will find GNU/Linux much more to your liking.

Some might say that 95% of users fit into the OSX category (making it "better"), but I don't think that's really true.  I would argue that about 15-25% of users want some form of control over their system (ie, possible linux candidates), another 25-35% want to be able to do lots of stuff while letting the computer deal with the mess (ie, OSX candidates), and the remaining 50% would be happy with either OSX or Linux, as all they do is email, chat, music and photos, which either OS is equally well suited for.  They wont do any major setup of anything anyway, so Apple's drag/drop install is just as easy as Suse's one click install.
