### Post by Rhapsody on 2006-09-16
Actually, it is techincally possible to have a Pentium running at 7Mhz. From my somewhat disturbingly good knowledge of retro computing, I happen to remember old PCs having a 'turbo button'. On a Pentium 75, this would cause it to run at 7.5Mhz, which Windows may report as 7Mhz. All you'd need to do is find a Pentium 75 with a functional turbo button or another PC with a functional turbo button that could be upgraded to a Pentium 75.

Though I am fairly convinced that the picture attached the original post is fake. Zooming in on it, you can see the '7 Mhz' figure is blurred and looks perfectly straight despite the fact that the rest of the text is on a slight angle. Both signs of an image that has been though Photoshop (or at least some sort of image editor). Simply taking a PNG screenshot of an XP desktop on a regular machine, editing it to say '7 Mhz', and displaying it in fullscreen would've made for a more convincing shot. They clearly didn't think of that though.

This looks like the new [Project E.U.N.U.C.H.](http://totl.net/Eunuch/), except that it's not as entertaining.

Edit: I also noticed some blurring around the 'um' in 'Pentium-Processor'. This reminded me that 'Pentium' is a registered trademark of Intel, so there should be ® after it. It was obviously lost while they were editing the image.
