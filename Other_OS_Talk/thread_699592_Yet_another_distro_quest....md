### Post by mivo on 2008-02-18
> **cardinals_fan said:**
> So far, the only distro that has stood out from the rest is Arch.  Does anyone have other suggestions, or should I install Arch when school gets out this summer?

Arch is remarkable. It's very different from most other distros, and the packaging system as well as AUR are simply wonderful. I have always liked Debian-based distros and the packaging system, but yes, Arch sets a new standard in that regard. Of course, it's not all roses ... Arch does require the user to at least read the wiki, learn a few new tricks, and think about what they want. I don't feel Arch is only for experts, though, it does have an awesome [Beginner's Guide]("http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Beginners_Guide"), but it is definitely for people who want to get familiar with the OS, get into a real relationship with it, and not just use it. People with little time or users who just want a working, fully featured system out of the box are better off with a more traditional Linux distro. Ubuntu is among the best choices here. 

But if you already like Arch and agree with how things are done the [Arch Way]("http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/The_Arch_Way"), then I think you won't find anything more suitable for you. :) Arch is very charming and once you fall in love with it, it's hard not to want to start an unforgettable romance with it.

(If I keep this up, I'll take a day off work and put Arch on my work desktop! :p I have it running on two other systems.)
