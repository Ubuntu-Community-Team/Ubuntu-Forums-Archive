### Post by Iandefor on 2006-07-05
They have to make the code of their kernel and the GNU core available to you, or, at the least, offer for up to three years to send you a copy (but you usually get to pay the bill for delivery) if you buy the program. But that only applies to the 
GPL'ed code, and they can distribute executable formats in pretty much any way they like. Also, the above really only applies to the GPL bits. Anything else, they can license any way they like.
