### Post by smoker on 2007-06-27
one of the best things about any linux distro, is it is free to download and try and see! i have ubuntu as my main working operating system, but i also have a few 'spare partitions' that i use to occasionally try out any other distro that takes my fancy. what i have found is there is no 'right' and 'wrong' distro, each seem to have their own appeal, and the best way to find out if that appeal, is perhaps more suited to your taste, is to try it for yourself. other peoples idea of what is a 'good' and 'bad' distro, may not be the same way you would judge what is right for you.

enjoy yourself, download one of two distros, and experiment,

best of luck

### Post by 67GTA on 2007-06-28
Check out this link. [http://www.zegeniestudios.net/ldc/](http://www.zegeniestudios.net/ldc/) It will help you narrow down the possibilities. Most distros are basically the same except for a few custom applications, desktop environments(gnome/kde), and package management(deb/rpm). After you try several you will find that there really aren't that many different distros. Most of them are just variants of older distros like Slackware, Gentoo, Debian, Red Hat to name a few.
