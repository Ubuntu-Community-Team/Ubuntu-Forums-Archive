### Post by BigSilly on 2008-07-13
It seemed to be growing and moving on to big things. Now, it's all but disappeared. I know they keep it up to date, but from looking around the net it seems that maintaining a rolling distro in this fashion isn't entirely trouble free for the user, and performing fresh install with a 2007 disc leaves the user downloading huge amounts of updates that don't always work as they should.

It's a pity as I felt it was a fine distro, with a good user friendly "face" to attract newcomers. A quick look on their site shows that they do seem to be planning a new release, but it's been promised for quite some time now without showing up. There are still crotchety responses to anyone even mentioning a new version on their forum. 

The problem is, what with Mandriva suddenly turning out really great operating systems, is there still a place anyway for PCLinuxOS, considering how close it was to that distro? Has it's speedy uptake popularity outstripped the capabilities of the chap who puts it together? Do rolling distros even work in the long run? I tend to think not really myself.

Anyone here still using it?

### Post by MisfitI38 on 2008-07-14
I think that PCLOS is rolling right along just fine. 
Along with PClinuxOS, Gentoo also comes to mind as a distribution that is always surrounded by hype, and that naysayers are always labeling as "in danger of imploding"..but Gentoo is still here, too.

As for rolling releases;
It is preferable for users who embrace this design model to simply install once, and upgrade to newer versions of software and kernels. It is a more consistently interactive approach to system upgrade. 
Gentoo and Arch both take this approach, as does Debian, in a modified way. 
Arch, Debian and Gentoo, though quite different, all offer a high quality distribution within the rolling release system.
One could easily make a scientific argument for the superiority of such a system.
