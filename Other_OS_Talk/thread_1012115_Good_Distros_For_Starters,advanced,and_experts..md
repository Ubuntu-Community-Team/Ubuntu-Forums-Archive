Well, I think you have this all wrong. I would say that darn near every linux distro would fall somewhere between beginner and intermediate. ### Post by chachawpi on 2008-12-16
> **Hallvor said:**
> Yes. Even Linus Thorvalds prefers "easy" distros. He *could* run Slackware, Gentoo or whatever he wants, but I think he prefers Fedora with KDE.

I could use a much more "advanced" distro myself, but I prefer them easy since the more work I do on the distro, the less time I have to get work done.

You could make the argument that distributions like Gentoo and Arch are actually less work in the end because they are rolling releases.  Once you get them setup, which is where 95% of the administration time comes in, you only have to type a simple command to keep the system up to date.  You don't have to worry about doing distribution upgrades or reinstalling from scratch.  Likewise, you don't have to remove useless services that get pre-installed in other distributions.

Also, if problems do arise with new software, you will incur them incrementally rather than all at once.  I find it a lot easier to type "pacman -Syu" than to fiddle around in some specialized GUI to figure out how to update my system.  It is also a lot easier to edit /etc/rc.conf to add/remove services than to try and figure out "the Mandriva way" or "the PCLinuxOS way."

### Post by fistfullofroses on 2008-12-17
I think that something has to be said for to what you are accustomed. For example, I grew up using BSD, because my father was an electrical engineer and wanted me to know "what the real men used" as he put it. So, I learned BSD, Solaris, and AIX which are the systems used at his work. Coming to GNU/Linux, I found Slackware to be easiest for me. My ex-wife's mother worked at AT&T when she was younger, and as she was coming to GNU/Linux she asked for something similar to the UNIX she had used. I showed her Slackware, Arch, and Gentoo. She thought all three were amazing and chose to go with Gentoo. She thought the source based 'emerge' was the coolest thing ever...

I never would have switched to GNU/Linux had BSD had as good hardware support, and as large a community. That said, I think that GNU proper is even worse off. Of all the Unices currently available, GNU/Linux is the only one that seems modern enough and popular enough to stand against OSX and Windows as a real competitor in the business world, desktop market, and in the end on my personal machines. 

The different flavors are no more or less hard than one another in all truth. If you want easy to use Slackware, you always have Slax. If you want easy to use Debian you have Ubuntu. If you want easy to use Gentoo you have Sabayon. Any distribution can be made to be nearly exactly the same as another distribution, you simply must be willing to put in the time and effort required. Personally, I love Slackware and GoboLinux. That's about it. Most other distributions just frustrate me to a point that is totally out-rageous.
