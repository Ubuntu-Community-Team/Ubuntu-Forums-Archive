### Post by cymen on 2007-06-26
I'll also throw in my support for Arch. I installed it for the first time the day before yesterday, and I'm really impressed. Half an hour for a base install, and then another couple of hours to install and configure X, Openbox and all the programs, plugins and codecs I like to be happy. I'd say the hardware detection was a tad better than Ubuntu, at least on my laptop anyway - all I had to do was add a synaptics driver to make my touchpad be recognised as a touchpad rather than a mouse (just for extra functionality, it did work before), and then get wireless working by installing and activiting madwifi. In Ubuntu I had to mess around with ATI drivers, wireless never truly worked properly and reliably, there was some sort of obsession with my touchpad being a wacom tablet, keymapping could be a bit iffy, my wired network sometimes complained...

Trust me. The manual config is worth it. Oh, and power management seems to finally be working properly. :D

### Post by Enverex on 2007-06-26
The speed increase from an "optimised" distro doesn't come from what it's compiled for, it comes from the fact it's not pre-loaded with a million and one apps which use lots of RAM and cause the machine to take longer to start. I'm speaking from 3 years experience using Gentoo on my desktop and laptop and 5 years with it on my server. Ubuntu would be just as fast if you "trimmed the fat" so to speak. Compiling and using --omg-sofast does not make a humanly noticeable difference.
