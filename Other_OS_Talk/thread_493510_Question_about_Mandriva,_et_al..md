### Post by pelle.k on 2007-07-05
note that mandriva comes in more than one flavor. I quote from their wiki;
>     *  the "One" edition is an installable live CD integrating the latest proprietary drivers, available for download free of charge. Purely free software editions of One are also available in GNOME and KDE flavours.
    * the "Free" edition is a pure free software (free as in speech) version, without any of the non-free packages bundled with other editions; it is also available as a free download.
    * the "Discovery", "Powerpack" and "Powerpack+" products, for beginner users, power users and SOHO users respectively, including support, services and many third-party applications like LinDVD and Cedega. [http://wiki.mandriva.com/en/Releases/Mandriva/2007](http://wiki.mandriva.com/en/Releases/Mandriva/2007)

The "One" edition is a pretty good choice if you're used to ubuntu i guess. That is the one i tried out. The only "shady" stuff i had to install from a different repo was libdvdcss if i remember correctly. The rest was pretty much there in "contrib" repos. The rest you would get by using [http://easyurpmi.zarb.org/](http://easyurpmi.zarb.org/)
I agree that mandriva website is confusing, and their support forums are often in a foreign language. Mandriva as a distro is quite diffrent from debian/ubuntu so just be prepared to adjust yourself to something "else".
