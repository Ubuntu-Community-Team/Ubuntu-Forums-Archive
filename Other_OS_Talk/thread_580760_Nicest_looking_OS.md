### Post by Room 34 on 2007-10-19
When I first installed Ubuntu a month ago I went all crazy over it and downloaded KDE (Kubuntu) and Xfce (Xubuntu) on top of my base Ubuntu install and switched between the three GUIs a lot. In the end I decided there was a reason the Ubuntu developers settled on GNOME as the default GUI. KDE is slick but it's a little gaudy for me, and I really don't like the vertical tabs up the left side of the Konqueror window (and the general everything-but-the-kitchen-sink approach to the interface). Xfce is not bad, but almost too minimal, and not really that different from GNOME. GNOME strikes a nice balance, and there are some really clean looking themes that I like.

So, that leads me into another question that's perhaps best asked in another thread, but what really is the fundamental difference between the various Linux distros? It seems that the kernel is basically the same across the board, right? And then there are these various GUIs out there that are not really distro-specific, either. So it seems to me that it's mainly about how it's all packaged up and, well, *distributed.* Is that really the main/only difference?

If so... from what I've seen and dealt with previously (which I'll admit is quite limited), I'm sold on Ubuntu, no need to look any further.

### Post by VCSkier on 2007-11-06
First of all, the question is relative to what you find attractive...

I'm surprised no one has mentioned Fedora.  It has a truly beautiful and exceptionally well thought out artwork that integrates with rest of the feel of the system very well.  It achieves this beauty without being flashy.  It's just a kind of simple, clean, and very polished attractiveness.  I would most likely recommend this for you.

openSUSE also comes across as polished and attractive because of its consistency of theming, but IMHO, to a lesser degree than Fedora.

With regards to 3D effects, Ubuntu 7.10 really is the best there is, so if that is how you define beauty, you will be disappointed with other distros.

As many have mentioned, if you set the standard of beauty on OSX, you would probably like Dreamlinux, but that sort of clone work is not for me; I value uniqueness.

Lastly, Enlightenment based distributions also have a nice degree of "flash" type eye candy, without using 3D effects.  For some systems and indivuduals, this is more practical, and so if that is the case for you check out Elive or something like it.
