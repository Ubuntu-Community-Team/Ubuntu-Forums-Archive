### Post by apoclypse on 2007-10-20
So I've been hearing a lot about Fedora for while and I decided to try the latest test version (8) and the live cd installer since I really didn't feel like downloading a dvd full of stuff I could just get off the network.. I must say I'm extremely disappointed in both the community and the live installer. I was initially impressed by the artwork and what they have done with the distro since the last time I used it (Fedora 4) and there were some things I liked about the livecd. However the installer would not work at all, and there was no information on the forum at all that was helpful, even doing a Google search for the error came up with nothing at all. I've been using the live installer in Ubuntu since edgy with no issues whatsoever and it usually works flawlessly. The Fedora installer wouldn't even let me get past the language option. Apparently it couldn't recognize my promise controller, something which I never had an issue with in Ubuntu. I don't even have my drives raided.

Ah, well i guess thats what i get for straying. I really was hoping to give fedora a chance. It was my distro of choice back in the day and I was an early adapter after Redhat dropped 9. I'm not actually looking to switch to it this time around, I was going to use it to get familiar with redhat again. Maybe, I'll wait until the official release. I have yet to have an issue in Ubuntu that i can't find a solution in these forums or at least someone here has had the same issue before.Their forums are laid out terribly and are not as intuitive as the ubuntuforums are. I know the fedora project has been trying to do some things the same as Ubuntu (hence the live cd and installer), I think they should get someone to make their forums usable.      

.This is not an Ubuntu vs Fedora thread, I'm just peeved that the installer wouldn't work, the lived wouldn't let me mount my drives (unless I logged in as root) and that I couldn't find any answers at all on how to fix the issue. It has promise though and I can see it taking off in the future if they keep on this path.

Therefor, in the forums you wont hear much chatter about the betas.  Try the mailinglists or the bugzilla for these questions.
