### Post by steevk on 2007-04-26
What you need to ask yourself is this: what do you want to learn?

Do you just want to learn KDE? Install kubuntu-desktop.

Do you want to learn how to administer a server? Try Etch.

Do you want something that's not Debain based? Try Gentoo.

Do you want something that doesn't have a package manager at all? Try Slackware.

Do you want to use a different package manager? Try something like Fedora, with Yum rather than apt.

Do you want to figure out how it all works? Give Linux From Scratch a try.


It all boils down to what you want to get out of the experience.

### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-05-03
So here's how my mini install marathon went:

1) Mandrive 2007 Spring One: Wow, very professional! Metisse is very, very cool, and has a ton of potential. But overall, the distro was very slow. In addition, when fonts are anti-aliased, it's so blurry it's difficult to read anything.

2) PCLinuxOS: Faster than Mandriva, couldn't find an easy way to get to wireless. Always had to go deep into the folder hierarchy and through a control panel. Very nice, but a carbon copy of windows. I'll recommend this to someone who can't work in an environment other than Windows. Should be called WindowsLinux.

3) Sabayon Mini: Tried to boot to the live disc once, and to the graphical installer the other. Froze both times completely. Coaster!

4) SAM: Basically, PCLOS on Xfce. Overall it's pretty nice, but not as nice as Mandriva or PCLOS.

5) Sidux: I didn't see the hype about this one. I had access to the latest software, but speed was comparable to Ubuntu.

So, in went my trusty Etch DVD. I installed Etch, ran an upgrade, changed my sources to Lenny, and I'm flying now! I forgot how fast and stable Debian was.
