### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-05-12
I have to say that, of Linux distros, I've found PCLOS to be remarkably good. While OSX is the best if youy have the money, I would whole-heartedly recommend PCLOS to anyone looking to get into Linux, and anyone who wants a simple, FAST operating system.

Initially, I was put off by many things about it, and in semi-protest never gave it a try. For one, the default theme is essentially a rebranded XP. The logo (not the PC in the ring) is just a rehash of the Windoes logo. In every way, it screamed that it was just a Windows knockoff, and being a Mac guy, I was none too encouraged.

However, after a little tweaking, it resembles any other KDE distro I've used in terms of look and feel. Except, it's very fast, easily as fast as Ubuntu and far faster than its ancestor Mandriva. Everything so far has worked very well (except with a small install problem, but this has more to do with my HD) and it may live up to the name it gave itself as the "distro hopper-stopper" for me. My two favorite features are the wealth of frontends (good things happen when you combine well-thought utilities with the plethora of KDE utils) and the superb speed. Not quite as fast as Zenwalk or Debian, but certainly faster than Sabayon, Ubuntu, and others such as openSUSE (not saying much) and Mandriva.
