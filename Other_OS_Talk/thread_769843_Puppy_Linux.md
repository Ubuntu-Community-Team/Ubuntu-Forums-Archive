### Post by Chilli Bob on 2008-04-27
I'm typing this on exactly what you want to achieve.  I replaced xubuntu with puppy.  Just use the Puppy universal installer (running live of the cd) and follow the wizards.  It will give you a choice between a full install and an install that co-exists with the existing OS.  Choose the former and it will completely remove Ubuntu and replace it with Puppy.  Follow the wizard for the rest of the setup (basically choose defaults except where they suggest you select MBR - you'll know it when you see it.)

I can't remember the exact terms Puppy uses, I'll fire up a live CD and check.

### Post by Chilli Bob on 2008-04-27
OK, I'm on the live cd now.  When you get to the point where you have to choose between OPTION 1 (aka "frugal" install) and OPTION 2 (aka "full" install) choose option 2 and you'll be on the right track.
