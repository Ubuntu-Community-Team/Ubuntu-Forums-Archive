### Post by VCSkier on 2008-07-09
I'm not the best person to make this recommendation.  My inner linux user has grown up on Gnome, but I'm trying to acclimate to KDE so I can appreciate it as well.

In my experience though, I would agree with most of the recommendations thus far, particularly Sidux, Vector Linux, Arch, and Mepis.  I must say though, that I have tried both Mandriva's and OpenSUSE's implementation of KDE and both left me with a negative impression.  They feel too bloated to me and they strike me as too commercially oriented; they remind me of Windows in these ways.  That is only my impression, obviously many people disagree.  If you want something with an overall feel of Windows, I would recommend one of those two, otherwise, try one of the others.
