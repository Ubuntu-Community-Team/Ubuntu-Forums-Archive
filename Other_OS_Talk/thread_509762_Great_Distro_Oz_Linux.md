### Post by RAV TUX on 2007-07-28
> **wolfen69 said:**
> ok rav tux, so what would oz do for me that would enrich my life? what makes it better than ubuntu? all im saying is that no matter what OS i use, i wind up doing the same things on it.

I did not say that Oz is better then Ubuntu or any distro out there. All though it is better then most.

I do believe that it is more stable and runs faster but that is very subjective and dependent on hardware compatibility.

I developed Oz for a specific purpose, that purpose is my long term goal.

I am working on optimizing Oz specifically for specific Hardware. While Oz will always be free I would like to design, build(have manufactured) and inevitably sale Systems that are Optimized for Oz.

There are currently two main models(main-meaning there are more models) of distribution(beyond the one that us as Linux users are familiar with):

1. The Windows Model-A distribution via computer manufactures. This is the model that Mark wants to stamp out Windows as being the major player...This is being realized with Dell selling Ubuntu machines. A worthy project that is working.

2. The Apple Model-A distribution via pre-built computer hardware. I would like to develop a Linux model that sells custom built computers utilizing a custom built Linux OS: that custom OS is Oz.

After, writing this I realize that I am more environmentally aware and also want to continue to have a Linux OS that works on anything, much like any Linux OS including Ubuntu. I strongly encourage all to recycle before you buy new.

I have some things in the works, that will be developed with future versions.

I have also developed Oz Rx, a system that I hope to become a health care solution that can be used by doctors offices and hospitals. This is a grand task, I manage a doctors office, I am well aware of the dependence many offices have on windows due to medical Hardware vendors making a system dependent on Windows.

The base system of Oz, is simply a fast and reliable solution much like many Linux OS's.....my plans while shared by some already, are a bit different.

The beauty of Linux + GNU Open Source, is that we are constantly developing and evolving, this system works for the greater good. This evolutionary process will inevitable have a resulting out come of major players. We should not be limited to major players. I find new projects hold more promise: ie Wolvix, elive, dyne-bolic, rPath....

Oz is based on rPath, which is it's unique strength....

To see an example of the potential of rPath see the post I have made on Foresight(a rPath employee developed distro, for all intensive purposes rPath and Foresight are the same since Foresight is based on the yet to be released rPath2)...this is a Conary based management system that is web browser based, for software installs, updates even rollbacks on your computer

see here:
Foresight System Manager screen shot tour

(I used my wife's account: Purpleleahdragon to publish this)

They have since renamed the System Management to rPath System manager, but for all intensive purposes rPath and Foresight are the same, simply that Foresight utilizes more cutting edge stuff while rPath is more stable.

I have yet to implement this in Oz, but will must likely do it after rPath 2 is released.

Many people have asked why develop a new distro then, why not just help with rPath inc.'s projects. While this question is a bit naive, I understand the logic, rPath has developed a unique system called rBuilder, when I develop or build or anybody builds or develops utilizing rBuilder this development, (troves) are available to any body "cooking" a rPath based OS. You may say well it is all Open Source, what makes this unique: the uniqueness is in rBuilder and the ease of use developers can share their development solutions.

This creates a unique community of developers that can utilize the simplicity of rBuilder and this is why in the rPath model if you have a 100 or 1000 development projects whether you are developing a specific appliance or a distro the community as a whole benefits.

Under this system the more projects the better, you can work individually on a system but since your base is always rPath utilizing rBuilder all benefit from all development.

I as a immediate goal have to develop a "build environment" within this system and start creating "troves" to give to the community. This development goal will be actualized inevitably.

As stated before I suggest you stick with what your comfortable with, if that is Ubuntu then I encourage you to stick with Ubuntu. I have specific reasons and goals to develop Oz based on rPath.

If anybody wants to use Oz as their OS, I am overjoyed and hope you like it, if you don't I understand. Oz as is, is meant to be more stable then cutting edge, so if you are looking for the latest and greatest(cutting edge), then Oz will not meet your expectancies....I suggest you try Foresight if you want to try a more advanced rPath based OS.
If you want more cutting edge in general then I suggest you try UBeryl if you want to try a cutting edge Ubuntu based OS. I suggest elive if you want to realize the full potential of e17. I suggest Wolvix if want the most advance Slackware based XFCE system.

I can go on and on, on suggestions....

I hope I have answered your questions: What makes Oz unique

to recap:

1. Oz is unique because of it's base chosen, rPath
2. Oz is unique because of it's specific development focus

If you try Oz now what will you realize now, a nice stable OS. Is it much different from the 800 plus different distros out there? Well yes, after trying and using over 500+ distros, many distros for my hardware don't work, many do. Oz works on my hardware I would hope it also works on your hardware also.

(a side note, I have started Linux Nirvana to be more cutting edge in the future, for now it is not much different then Oz, but it's development focus is completely different)
