### Post by Iam138 on 2008-03-05
The only drivers you should really need to update on a regular basis are graphics drivers and the others are quite correct when they say you should get those from Nvidia,ATI/AMD what have you.

However in the spirit of actually answering your question, drivers should the need arise can usually be updated via Device Manager which can be accessed several ways.
Click Start, click Run, and then type devmgmt.msc.
or
Right-click My Computer, click Manage, and then click Device Manager.
or
Right-click My Computer, click Properties, click the Hardware tab, and then click Device
Manager.
or
Type the following command at a command prompt:start devmgmt.msc

*Yes you can still run commands in Windows. Shocking I know! Of course the Linux 1337 would have you believe otherwise.

Then to update you simply.
1.	Double-click the type of device you want to update..
2.	Right-click the  device driver you want to update.
3.	Click Update Driver to open the Hardware Update Wizard. Follow the  instructions.

This assumes your using XP or 2000/NT. The procedure for Vista varies slightly.
