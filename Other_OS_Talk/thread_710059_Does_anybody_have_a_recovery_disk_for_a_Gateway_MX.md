### Post by mips on 2008-02-28
I would actaully approach this from a different angle. Companies like Gateway, HP etc do not always have the latest drivers. The latest drivers come from the chipset manufacturers and they usually update their drivers long after the like of Gateway or HP have stopped doing so.

My advice to you would be to download a free utility like PC Wizard 2008 or HWiNFO32 to identify all the chipset and components on your Gateway.
[http://www.cpuid.com/pcwizard.php](http://www.cpuid.com/pcwizard.php)
[http://www.hwinfo.com/](http://www.hwinfo.com/)

Run the software and let it detect who the manufacturer is, the chipset version etc.

Next get on the web and go and download all the required drivers from the respective manufacturers websites as they were listed in the above utilities.

If I setup a new or old pc with windows for someone I follow the above route all the time as it is the only sure way to get the most up to date drivers.
