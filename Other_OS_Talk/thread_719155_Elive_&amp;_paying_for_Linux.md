### Post by scottro on 2008-03-08
I haven't checked the different links to see if they insist upon a donation or not. 

However, the whole idea is that Free Software is Free as in speech, and not necessarily Free as in Free Diet Mountain Dew.  You also have the freedom to not download it.  It's not going to stop your computer from functioning. 
It means that you choose one of their competitors unless you feel it's worth it. 

I remember finding it rather troubling that Daniel Robbins, who created and popularized Gentoo, had to go work at Microsoft.  He left the Gentoo project because he had to support his family and had $30,000 in debt.  Thirty thousand dollars.  That would be nothing to Gates or Jobs.  

If every Gentoo user at the time had donated a dollar, I suspect he wouldn't have had the problem.  (At present, there's about 119,000 registered users I'm sure it was less then, but still.)

So, while I understand your aggravation, I don't blame them.  I'm not a big enlightenment fan, so I probably wouldn't pay it, but if one of my favorite distros started asking for donations, I'm sure I'd cough up something.  

People have to eat.  Usually, (and I say this without criticism) I've found the people who get angriest at distros that ask for money are those who are still being supported by their families, and haven't yet experienced the joys of wondering how they're going to manage to pay the bills this month. 

Please understand, I'm not saying you are one of those people, I'm just saying that in general, (and I"m thinking of things that happened several years ago) that seems or seemed, at least, to be the case. 

So, I repeat--I understand why you find it aggravating.  However, consider how much an MS or Apple developer gets paid, and how much work it takes for the elive people.  We don't know their circumstances, maybe, like Mr. Robbins, they really need money and are trying to avoid giving up the project.
