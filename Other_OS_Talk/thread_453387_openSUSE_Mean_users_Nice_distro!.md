### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-05-24
Yesterday, I was offended by the conduct of some people in the openSUSE IRC freenode#opensuse forum, which I recounted [in this post (Community Cafe)]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=452677"). I still went ahead and used the DVD I'd burned of 10.2, and it is marvelous.

Boot time is average. Slightly slower than Ubuntu, faster than Sabayon. In fact, performance in general is average: some things are snappy, others aren't. As a whole, I'm impressed by 10.2 The graphic design is very good in every way, and while this is no knock to KDEmod, the implementation of the SUSE menu is, predictably, much smoother and better than with Arch's version via KDEmod, or Sabayon's.

YaST has worked fine. I think the key is to be plugged in during the install so that you get some important updates done early, as well as having at least a couple your repositories setup by default. This was a major roadblock for me the last time I tried openSUSE.

As a whole, it's just a very smooth distribution. Updates have gone very well, and I have yet to encounter YaST's alleged ugliness. Every part of KDE has been finely tweaked, from the Desktop control center, to the buttons, to the "My Computer" window. As a whole, it's very refined, and I think it rivals PCLOS as the class of the "tweaked desktop environment" distros. Ubuntu is more of a middle-ground in this respect, following Debian's model of offering plain packages; whether they intend this is debatable, as they could just inherit it from Debian's packages.

In any case, the smooth update of patches and apps bodes well for October when 10.3 is released, specifically for the transition to KDE 4.

All this being said, I will only involve myself limitedly with openSUSE's forums and IRC rooms after that very negative experience. This is not a big deal, as I'm the type who searches forums, wikis, and Google before posting a problem.

If you have any other questions for this Distro-Junky, let me know!
