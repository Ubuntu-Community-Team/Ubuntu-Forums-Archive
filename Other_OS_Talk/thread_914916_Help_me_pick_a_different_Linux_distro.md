Hi Jiran, it's tough for me to make a recommendation without knowing 1) your system specs and 2) what you are looking for (the latest and greatest with all the bells and whistles, fast and minimalist, are there any specific apps you need to have, etc.) I predict this thread is going to turn into lots of people telling you what their favorite distro is. :)

Personally I am on an Openbox kick right now. I find it has just enough functionality without a lot of distractions. There are several good Openbox distros, ranging from Crunchbang (based on Ubuntu 8.04) to SliTaz (an incredible 24mb Live CD). 

I've been using Linux for less than a year, and I made a promise to myself that I would stick with Ubuntu for the first year rather than try a million distros (although sometimes I cheat, using VirtualBox). Personally, if I had to start all over again, I would either use Debian stable (plus Openbox of course) and never worry about anything breaking, or learn how to use Arch and set up a totally custom, super-speedy minimalistic system.

Good luck! :)
