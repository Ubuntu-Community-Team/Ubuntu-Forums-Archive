### Post by clockwork on 2008-04-22

You would be wrong. First off Solaris has *long* since supported far larger systems than Linux without modification, it scales better as well. Speaking from experience I have hot added and removed entire cpu boards, tens of gigs of memory and so on from heavy iron Sun boxes.

Secondarily although its nice to have service segregation on servers its not a necessity on any modern OS, unix has been multi-user and multi-task since day 1 circa 1969-ish back in Bell Labs. Before Linus was even born for those keeping track.

For a home user Solaris doesnt offer much (ZFS is a good one if you have the storage needs), but for mid to upper tier server workloads (ie a place where you have a dedicated sysadmin with a pager, and SLA's) it has a ton more features and 'tweaks' that make it highly desirable and flexible.

(As a side note, when was the last time that the Linux community developed something as useful AND cool as ZFS ?)

...and NFS, and JAVA, and ...

For those not too familiar with Solaris, keep in mind that Sun and Solaris have only been in the x86 space for a short time so Yes, their desktops leave a lot to be desired, and hardware support for every piece of equipment under the sun takes time.  But put Solaris on SPARC for real back-end server work and you'll be hard pressed to match it with any Linux box in terms of performance, scalability, stability, and supportability.

This is a paradigm triangle with three points. Value, Performance, and Reliability, and with any two, you sacrifice one. With Linux, you get Value, and Performance. With Solaris (on SPARC), you get Performance, and Reliability. SPARC hardware is expensive. There's a big difference between Linux in a SOHO or Mom-and-Pop shop, and Solaris (or HP-UX, or AIX) in any mid to large enterprise.
