### Post by caucuz on 2007-10-01
Not so fast 

> **Mandriva Admin@Mandriva Forums said:**
> This is rumour control, here are the facts. 
What is happening is basically an acknowledgement of the existing situation. You can't make people pay to join a user community. So the 'community' element of the Club and the 'paying for specific services' element are being divorced. From now on we're just going to have a model where you pay for a specific service.

In practical terms, the change is really pretty small. Instead of advertising paid Club membership with the somewhat nebulous benefits that are listed, we are simply going to advertise a download subscription service: pay X Euros / dollars per year in order to have access to download the Powerpack ISOs and restricted packages. Since this is the only really valuable feature of 'Club membership' at the moment anyway, this is obviously not a big change in practical terms. It's just a clarification of direction.
So there won't really be any practical change for existing paid Club members - you'll still have access to all the same stuff as you do now, including the commercial downloads. We may change prices and rebrand stuff, there will be more official announcements and so on about that forthcoming.

### Post by AdamWill on 2007-10-08
jrusso2: in case it wasn't obvious from the post of mine that was quoted above - that all changed a long time ago.

it was never quite as extensive as you say...for I think two or three releases, we released ISOs to Club members around a month before the general public. The *tree* went up for the public around the same time as Club members got ISOs, so you could do a network install (those with long memories may remember SUSE used a similar arrangement before they were bought by Novell).

Official updates - security and bugfix updates - were never released to the Club first, exclusively, or separately. They have only *ever* been released through our security team to our official public mirrors.

What we did do for the Club for a while was stuff like new versions of KDE and GNOME for the most recent stable release. We didn't do that for very long, though.

We stopped doing the 'release early to Club' thing with the release of Mandriva Linux 2007. Since then, all releases have been made to the public and the Club at the same time.

We stopped doing the 'KDE / GNOME' backports thing after backporting KDE 3.5 to Mandriva 2006, IIRC. (Don't shoot me if I'm wrong, please, my memory goes fuzzy sometimes :>).

So, as I wrote, in practical terms, for quite a while, the only thing Club members have really been paying for is Powerpack access. It just wasn't obvious from the way we promoted and sold stuff.
