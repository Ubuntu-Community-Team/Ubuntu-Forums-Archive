### Post by Hex_Mandos on 2007-04-14
No, this is not a joke. My grandomther (70 something) has never used a computer before.  Today she said something to the effect of "I want to buy a computer and learn to use the Internet. That way, if I ever want to, say, see photos of Norway, I'll be able to find them in a few seconds." Since in my book finding photos of Norway is a good enough motive to buy a computer, I applaud her decision to learn new stuff.

As I'm the family techie, I got the job of buying her a computer, configuring it, and teaching her how to use it. As soon as I heard that, I figured I'd be installing Linux on it, for several reasons:

1) Security. This should be evident
2) No migration troubles. She's never used Windows, she doesn't have to unlearn anything. She also has no special needs.
3) No installation/adminning troubles, since I'm going to be her sysadmin. And I'd rather do that with Linux
4) Ease of use. Gnome seems more user-friendly than XP to me.
5) Cost. Hardware, in particular, will be a lot cheaper (ie I won't need to buy more than 512 mb of ram)

Now, I'm not sure what distro to install. Ubuntu was my first distro, and is still the one that I use regularly. However, most of the rest are as easy to use as Ubuntu, once they're configured. And since I'll be installing everything for her, this shouldn't be an issue.

Right now, I'm considering Ubuntu and Debian Etch w/Gnome. I've used Ubuntu far more than Debian, but I think Debian's stability might be better than her. She doesn't need the latest and greatest, she needs something that just works. On the other hand, Ubuntu seems to be standardizing as the "gateway drug" for Linux, so there are more hacks and workarounds to provide a better experience.

What do you think? Does anyone have another good suggestion? (Remember, I'm doing all the maintenance stuff, and while I'm not a *nix guru, I've installed several different distros). I'm mostly interested in accessiblity, the Gnome desktop, and stability. Cutting edge features aren't needed.
