### Post by dca on 2008-02-27
When you purchase a computer from a big-box retailer you get one or two things.  A CD/DVD w/ the actual HDD image or a recovery partition on your HDD.  The recovery partition on larger HDDs you'll see your pri C drive partition w/ say 100GB and maybe a D drive w/ 7 or 8 GB listed as 'Recovery'.  Using those two methods of reinstallation are the easiest because all your drivers and bloatware and everything else is there.
 
You can also use the Windows XP Home Ed OEM CD installer with your license key.  The downside is you will need a list of all hardware installed on your system and make sure you d/l the drivers that allow them to work in Windows.  Then once there's no more exclamation points in your device manager proceed to d/l & install the six years worth of updates pending for XP.  Downside #2 after you enter your license key, XP will want an activation code.  So long as your current license is valid you should be able to call MS and explain HDD crashed or whatever and they will issue you new activation code.
