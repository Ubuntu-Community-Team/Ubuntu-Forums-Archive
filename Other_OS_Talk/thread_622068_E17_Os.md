### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-11-25
> e17 people despises compiz , beryl or any X composition

I can't speak for the devs, but I don't think this is quite true.  I have heard from a reputable (free and open) source that QuinnStorm, a key Beryl dev, has joined the e team.  Anyway, the subtle e17 eye candy is wonderful!

Enlightenment is moving quickly (I never thought I'd ever write _those_ words :-) ) and the e17 build is nearing a "final".  It has evolved in a powerful suite of tools well beyond the elegant desktop and is now the WM/DE of choice for a number of distros and, increasingly, handheld devices.

Rui Pais (of these forums) has been working to package the more stable of the latest cvs packages into a .deb that can be used as a desktop metapackage with debian-based distros.  It is early days, but I have used it on Xubuntu and it is sweet.

You can try it, or just follow the development process, at Rui's site[ here]("http://ruialeixopais.planetaclix.pt/linux/e17/"), or look into the OzOs project.  OzOs uses the cvs and related things to package up some alternative Ubuntu desktops.  OzOs is at  the CafeLinux forums [here]("http://cafelinux.org/forum/index.php/board,91.0.html").

### Post by Rui Pais on 2007-11-27
Hi,
i've been so busy that i hardly read the forum... just now i found this one.

Any issues, please ask/report in one of the referred thread, here or at CaféLinux. 

Sorry about confusion with the download site of the deb. It was just a temporarily place i made till we put our repos on-line.

I had put links to debs here and there, that people copied too (e17 it's spreading fast this days) and soon it was a nightmare of out-dated links... :S

Now it's very simple. Just add the repo to your apt-sources, update with apt and install with apt-get install e17-cvs. :)

A note not mentioned before. 
The e17-cvs deb package it's different from others debs for enlightenment in one fundamental point. 
It's a wrapper (+some confs) for a script that what it does is download and compile the latest CVS code. 
It won't contains pre-compiled binaries. It's always up-to-date (and updated every time user wants) and it's always pure Enlightenment, no tweaks/forks in binaries, just (selected) e17 code, compiled on your machine.

Thats why i say, again, **dont' mix other e17 repos with this deb**, or soon libs starts to duplicate on different paths and compilation will occur against incorrect ones with unpredictables effects.


@Masterizaak
Sorry to hear that your 1st try failed. It must be the boring ewl bug.
On fresh installs,it fails to compile at 1st time, and one needs to repeat the install command (the apt-get install) 2 or 3 times, till it overpass what ever makes it abort. 

Untill you see the message:
> We hope you will enjoy your trip into e17... Have fun! it will not be correctly installed and Session on gdm/kdm would not be enabled.

Good luck, 
and please all, gives us your reports 
(failures, success, problems)
