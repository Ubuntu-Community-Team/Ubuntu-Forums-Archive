### Post by richg on 2009-01-31
I just installed Mint  6 and it is awesome. I am not a techie type, I just want something that works without a lot of configuring.  I never, ever format,partition or dual boot. I have gone with the defaults for five years. This is excellent for those who want to be free of Windows and do not like messing with the Operating System.

 Everything works that I require with out being configured by me. Somewhat like Ubuntu which I had heard. Since I use removable hard drives, it was no big deal.

I have been using Ubuntu 8.04 since it came out but had to do quite a bit of work (searching the forums, downloading stuff) for certain things to work.

With Mint 6, I could play a commercial DVD movie with no configuring. Sound and video great. 
My Canon Lide 20 scanner with no configuring.
My HP 2210 all in one scanner portion with no configuring.

This is the first OS I have installed that requires no extra work. I have used Mepis, Linspire 4.0, 5.0, 6.0, Freespire 1.0 and 2.0, plus Ubuntu 8.04.

DVD movies would never work in Ubuntu movie player. VLC worked three times out of fifty or so tries. Never could quite figure out a click sequence. I did a few hours of searching for suggestions and downloading stuff.

I hate to abandon Ubuntu but I hope the powers to be can get things together. Don't even suggest 8.10. Been there done that.

Give it a try. Your mileage may vary.
