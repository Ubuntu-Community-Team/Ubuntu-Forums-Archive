### Post by igknighted on 2007-07-19
I run 2 servers that I recently moved from Debian (well, one was Ubuntu Server) to CentOS.  I run a Scalix email server on one and RHEL/SLES are their supported platforms, but the other is just a web server.  Honestly I just like Red Hat based systems better.  I also run Fedora on my desktop (well, until my sound decided not to work on 2.6.20 kernels or later...) and just find YUM/RPM to be better than APT/DPKG.  I think that while Red Hat and Fedora may be losing ground on the home desktop, they (along with SLES/SLED) are the dominant distribution in the corporate world.  Plus, Red Hat is planning a linux "for the masses" distro to be an easier alternative to the more intermediate level fedora.  After all, my uni still distributes Fedora to all CS/CIS students instead of Debian or any of its derivatives.

Red Hat & Fedora still have a strong following.  Don't worry.
