### Post by 1cewolf on 2008-06-15
I use Linux Mint instead of Ubuntu for a couple of reasons. 

It "just works" right out of the box. Everything you need to get up and running is included and there are graphical tools to handle just about everything. I'm a user who hates using the terminal and in Linux Mint, I have the luxury of rarely having to use it. 

I also like its style and appearance. The Mint Menu is really amazing and the artwork is pretty spiffy, too.

I think the best part, though, is that Clem really listens to his users and adjusts things accordingly. This is one area where Mint really runs circles around Ubuntu. I agree with jrusso's assessment that Ubuntu seems to ignore the users and that was a significant factor in my initial move to Mint. Mint is already excellent and seems to be going in a very positive direction...I can't say that in regards to Ubuntu.
