### Post by jdong on 2008-12-06
> **DDurcsak said:**
> To all:

Saw this msg and couldn't help but chime in.

The original msgs discusses how 8.10 causes issues.  I am experiencing the right now and would very much like to go back to 8.04 or Hardy as was mentioned...problem is how can I do that??

Will the install allow me to revert back to an older version?

Many thanks for your assistance


It is **EXTREMELY** discouraged to use APT pinning to downgrade Ubuntu or Debian. Colin Watson, a Debian Developer and Ubuntu Core Developer, has said this several times extremely explicitly in IRC. Few if any packages on Debian or Ubuntu are tested for the downgrade route, and there's nothing in Debian or Ubuntu policy that mandates they support this path...

The best recommendation I can give is to restore your 8.04 system from backups. But if  you're like 99% of people out there who should've but didn't make backups before attempting a major OS upgrade, I'd recommend using the install-preserve-home mechanism of the desktop installer -- choose to mount your old root partition as / in setup but **don't check Format**, and the installer will then tell you it's going to remove everything except your /home directory (where your documents and settings reside) and do a fresh install.
