### Post by alarclin on 2008-07-24
Interesting that the poll seems mainly to divide between people who like or dislike using a terminal-emulator. 
I'm kind of a freak: a complete non-geek who prefers using a terminal-emulator, or even better a console without starting X at all, whenever practicable. It find "pasting code into a terminal" etc actually to be far less confusing and frustrating that dealing with often poorly designed, time consuming interfaces that require endless clickclickclicking. 

I am utterly charmed by apps like w3m or elinks or moc that let me do so much that most users depend on graphical interfaces for, but much faster, with less distraction.  And since I've started learning to use apps like vim, for example, and become less mouse-dependent, I find my fingers and wrists are much much happier! (I think it's a real shame that newbies are so often warned away from Vim, which can make life easier for anyone who writes a lot, not just coders) 

If only gnome/metacity and xfce4 were less mouse-dependent, I'd be very happy.
