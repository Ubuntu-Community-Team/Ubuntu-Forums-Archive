### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-04-04
I'm not trying to be hard on you, but I see tremendous potential for Linux if efforts are consolidated. In Linux layman's terms, what makes your distro different and/or better from the rest?

I look at everything in the Linux world in a tier/tree diagram. Debian, Slackware, Gentoo, and a couple others are on the first level. These are the oldest, have the original ideas and some superb developers. On the second level are Ubuntu, Zenwalk, Vector, Sabayon, and a bunch of others. On the third level are the ones based on Ubuntu, etc.

Developers should try to get into the first tier first. These have the widest reputation and longest history. Then you work your way down the tree until there's one that will accept a new developer with your skill(s). If none will, then a new distro is suitable. If none are similar to your idea, same thing.

Am I off-base in thinking this?
