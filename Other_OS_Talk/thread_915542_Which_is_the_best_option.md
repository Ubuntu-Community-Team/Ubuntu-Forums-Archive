### Post by crimesaucer on 2008-09-11
I used xfce4 for my first couple of years on Linux. I used it with both Beryl/Compiz-Fusion and with xfwm4 composting. I used a custom gtkrc-2.0 for my panel, and I liked xfce4 a lot.


Now that I use Gnome on a computer with 4GBs and a dual core, I really like some of the gnome things like the panel/menu, the easy-to-use multimedia keybindings "Keyboard Shortcuts", and the Gnome sounds for clicking buttons and logging in/out.


I think I liked Thunar's custom actions better than Nautilus scripts, and even though I like the look of nautilus better than thunar, it seems that thunar "doesn't get in the way" as much as nautilus when doing certain tasks. In fact, the whole desktop environment of xfce4 is designed to be simple and fast. Plus, xfce4 fonts were really nice. xfce4 Terminal felt better than gnome-term, but I prefer gedit to mousepad.


I would still be using the xfce4 desktop if it changed it's panel and menu, if it included the option to have system sounds, and if it made thunar with tabbed browsing with and an updated look. Also if it had out-of-box multimedia button recognition for my hp like gnome does. 


I also prefer xfwm4 composting to compiz or gnome w/compositing.... it's faster, and I don't really use any of the cheesy compiz plugins, or the cube....
