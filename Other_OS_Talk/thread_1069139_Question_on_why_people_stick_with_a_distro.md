### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-02-13
Older packages within the older distors are more *stable*, since people have had time to identify bugs, and the community has time to fix them.  Of course, they don't necessarily have the latest features, or support the latest hardware, but this is not an issue if you were happy with an older distro.

I've stayed with Hardy, since it works well for me, and I'm now tired of the 6-monthly cycle.  I figure the 2-yearly LTS cycle will be fine for me.  And I waited until 8.04.1 before installing Hardy too (most significant package bugs had been fixed by that stage).

I have an Intrepid media centre, which I don't use much, so am happy to tinker with (and break).

I tried lots & stick with Arch, though still have a testing machine for looking at other distro's & their newer versions or whatever.
