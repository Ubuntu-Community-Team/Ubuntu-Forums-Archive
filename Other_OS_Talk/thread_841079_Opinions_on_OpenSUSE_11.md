### Post by NightCrawler03X on 2008-08-30
My favourite distro is still ubuntu/arch, though

ubuntu -- like with mac osx, everything "just works". It's also an *awesome* development platform, and has plenty of support (be it commercially or through forums, etc). It's just... *completely* user friendly. Comes with ALSA and PulseAudio by default though. Not exactly my ideal choice, but that doesn't matter; I just replace ALSA and PulseAudio with OSS4 and ESD... everything sound-related then works flawlessly on my system, problem solved.

arch -- flexible, to death. Honestly, this system is so customizable that I wouldn't even consider it a "distro", but more of a "grounds" for creating your own system. I mean, it installs with only the basics (kernel, shell, package manager, text editor, some libraries, etc), and you go from there, hacking and tweaking your system to whatever you want it to be. Hell, I would even consider it "normal" (compared to most other distros)-- much of it's design resembles that of BSD, somewhat.
