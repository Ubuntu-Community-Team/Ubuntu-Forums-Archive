### Post by Erunno on 2008-05-04
openSUSE 11.0 Beta 2 is pretty much without competition at the moment when it comes to a solid KDE 4.0+ implementation. The developers backported a lot of stuff from the 4.1 development branch (aka trunk). Among others I spotted the KDE 4 versions of KMail (with reduced functionality), Kickoff and K3B. Some quirks like the black borders around desktop icons have been patched out (appear on hover only) and due to the Oxygen fork Ozone one can finally easily distinguish active windows from inactive even without compositing support turned on. Oh, and plasmoids use the Aya(?) theme by default thus they look like the rest of KDE (i.e. the applications).

As with the other distributions KDE 3 applications have been used where it was not viable to backport their KDE 4 counterparts.

Hardware detection with the Live-CD was a bit wonky. I rummaged through bugzilla and most of the issues I encountered where closed so it may be a live-cd issue only (which is bad enough, first impression and all).

I gave the new zypper a try and it is ridiculously fast compared to its predecessor in 10.3 and, hooray, it now supports priorities which  makes dealing with all those build service and third party repositories a lot less painful.

Well, I'm really looking forward to the final release of 11.0. Seems like I'll be able to use it on my private desktop after all.
