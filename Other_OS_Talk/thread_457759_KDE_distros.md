### Post by igknighted on 2007-05-31
> **sethmahoney said:**
> Its been a while since I've tried KDE, and I'm finding it kind of slow.  OpenSUSE definitely loads slow, but the desktop itself seems kinda sluggish, and I seem to remember that being the case with Sabayon as well.  Is this something that holds across distros, or are there any where KDE is at least as fast as Gnome on Ubuntu?

KDE in and of itself can be faster than Xfce or slower than gnome... it depends what apps you use and what options you turn on.  If you keep GUI options like fading windows and such things to a minimum, and then use KDE apps only, it is (IMHO... there are some numbers to back this up, but the studies were done by KDE people so take that as you will) the fastest DE there is.  KDE shares libraries amongst its apps, so if you use Kopete and Konqueror and Konversation and Ktorrent (add your KDE apps to the list) on KDE, it will be faster than Pidgin, Epiphany, Xchat, etc. on a gnome (or even Xfce in some cases)  desktop.  I wish I still had the link for the data... I found it from this forum but it was like 6-8 months back.

Point being, take some time to learn the options.  KDE is nice the way it comes OOTB, but if you spend time customizing it (not just look and feel stuff like gnome) then you can have a DE that really truly is formed to your needs.

### Post by igknighted on 2007-06-04
> **babyhuey said:**
> How good are the PClinuxOS repositories? That's the one thing that holds me back from leaving Ubuntu. PCLinuxOS uses APT right?

PCLOS uses apt4rpm, which means that the apt program has been modified to install rpm packages.  It is not debian based, nor does it use .deb packages.

The PCLOS repos are quite good.  While they aren't as big, I find them more useful, since they have programs like cinellera included and also get updated a little more (pidgin was added after release).  I would think about donating the $20 to get the faster repo's as well, ibiblio and most of the main public mirrors are rather slow (70-80 kbps)

EDIT: I agree with Adamant, Fedora has a nice KDE setup if you change the icon theme, and Mandriva has one of the best KDE setups out there.  Both are worth a shot.
