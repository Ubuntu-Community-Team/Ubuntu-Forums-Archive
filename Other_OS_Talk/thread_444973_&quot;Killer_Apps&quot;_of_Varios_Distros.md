### Post by Pobega on 2007-05-15
The only distribution I can speak for is Debian, since I've used it exclusively for about 70% of my time on Linux (Albeit, I haven't been using Linux long, but that's even more of a reason for me not to know much about other distributions)

[list][*]Strong foundation. The only distribution that will **never** disappear.
[*]The Debian Free Software Guidelines; It's like the bible that I live by.
[*]Stability: The stable branch generally has extremely stable packages, which is amazing for mission critical servers. Of course, you could use apt-build if you need to compile in your own features, but I think vanilla packages should serve well enough for most servers.
[*]Rolling Stability: This is the one thing that is unique about Debian testing against other rolling release distributions. Debian testing packages are thoroughly tested for 10 days before they even reach the testing repositories, and there are by far more Sid users than there are testing. Other rolling release distributions (Like Gentoo) generally don't do much testing of their packages before throwing them in the portage tree/repositories/whatever.
[*]Package Selection: Debian hands-down has the largest repositories compared to other distributions/operating systems, the only ones that come close to it are Gentoo (I think 13,000 packages) and FreeBSD (16,300 in Ports last time I installed).
[*]Debian Packaging Guidelines: The one thing that makes me love Debian is the packaging guidelines. All executable files **must** have a man page, whether it's supplied by the upstream developer or hand-written by the packager. This avoids confusion a lot of the time, and in my opinion is extremely useful, and I HAVE been left on other distributions without a man page. It's not nice.[/list]
