### Post by Erik Trybom on 2007-04-01
I generally know enough of a distro before I try it not to be swayed by the design of the web page.

However, there are some things that do put me off:

1) A page that gives an impression of inactivity. You don't want to use old software.
2) REALLY incompetent design (tables, frames, black background etc).
3) Huge banners saying FREE TO TRY or PREMIUM SERVICE, combined with an all-to-shiny design.

Well, that's it. I understand that programmers aren't web designers so it's OK having an ugly page like Debian or Slackware, but the above things really do say something about the quality or focus of the distro.

I'm a complete noob when it comes to compiling and building apps from source, so I wanted a distribution that knows how to handle installation of applications for me.

I gave KNOPPIX a try on the live CD and the same for UBUNTU. Both worked rather well, but help was faster available in Ubuntu and Gnome just felt better, so I stuck with ubuntu. ....Happy ever after.
