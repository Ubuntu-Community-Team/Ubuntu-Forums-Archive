### Post by Sorivenul on 2008-10-01
I've used Absolute.  For those with some Slackware experience it takes some of the hassle out of the initial setup, but leaves you with a fairly basic system and is true to the Slackware philosophy.  For those with no Slackware experience Absolute may still be a bit advanced, at least at first.

The new ISO tracks Slackware-current, which is fine, as in all my time with Slackware I have never had major breakage tracking the current repo as opposed to the stable/release repo.  Just manage updates wisely.  

Absolute is underrated as far as Slack-based distros go, out-recommended by Slax and Zenwalk, both of which are also fine distros.
