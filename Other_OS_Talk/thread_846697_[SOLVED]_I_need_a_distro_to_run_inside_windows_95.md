### Post by LaRoza on 2008-07-01
Do you have 1 GB or more of RAM?

If not, a Virtual Machine isn't really a solution.

If your work place supplies inadequate equipment you have three real choices:

[list]
[*] Request suitable equipment using whatever system is in place for this
[*] Use what they supply and go home with the knowledge you got paid for it, and it doesn't matter you are using something older than some members of this forum
[*] Bring or supply your own equipment (laptop, etc)
[/list]

Naturally, the first option is the optimal one, but probably unlikely to happen. The second one is the most likely to work without problems, except your own frusteration, and the third is a last resort and probably a sign of a problem with the work place.

If you are looking for features like multiple workspaces, you could try to find something like that for DOS based systems.
