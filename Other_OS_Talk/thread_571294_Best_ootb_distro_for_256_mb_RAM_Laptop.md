### Post by MalachiConstant on 2007-10-09
Hi everyone! I have a Toshiba Portege 2000 and it seems I finally found a way to install a new OS on it without any bootable devices except HD. But which distro should I chose? As I can't just boot the LiveCDs, i'd like to hear your opinion!

Here are the system specs: Pentium M 750 mhz, 256 mb RAM, 20 gb HD, onboard graphics. More details here: [http://linux.toshiba-dme.co.jp/linux/eng/pc/ptg2000_report.htm](http://linux.toshiba-dme.co.jp/linux/eng/pc/ptg2000_report.htm)

So what am I looking for in a distro?

1. ootb hardware support. I'm still new to Linux and I need a working machine asap for my university work, especially wireless internet is important. My current XP install is not really usable anymore...

2. Performance. As you can see my Laptop is not among the fastest, especially the RAM is limiting I guess. XP ran surprisingly well on this machine - for a few months...

3. Repositories. When I first installed Ubuntu on my desktop PC (RIP) and realized that any app that I need is just a few clicks away, I never wanted to go back to windows. Well, here I am again.

4. Clean coherent look. Yes, optics are important to me. I like distros that look like they were made out of one piece, not just a compilation of some cool stuff.

These are the distros I was thinking about:

1. Ubuntu. I know this distro and I love it. It doesn't have the best looks, but that can be fixed. What worries me though is performance. Will it run at decent speed on my poor old machine?

2. Xubuntu. Maybe Xubuntu then? It has the same excellent HW support as Ubuntu and is supposedly much faster. But I read a lot of times that it doesn't use Xfce very smart and is basically just a dumped down Ubuntu with better performance. What do you think?

3. Zenwalk & Vector Linux. These distros are supposed to have great perfomance. I also liked the screens I have seen and Zenwalk's "one task one programm" philosophy. But what about HW support? I just don't have the time to spend hours on setting up the wlan card or other integral HW. And the repos are probably nowhere near the size of Ubuntu's?

So what's your recommendation? Do you have other suggestions? Any feedback greatly apreciated!
