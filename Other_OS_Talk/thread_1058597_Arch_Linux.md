### Post by Rumor on 2009-02-03
There are pages for most every de / wm in the [Arch Wiki]("http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Main_Page").

I'd recommend you read through those pages to see what style might appeal to you before you install. You might like a tiling window manager like dwm, xmonad, awesome or one of the many, many others. 

You might prefer one of the *boxes like fluxbox or openbox. The choice is going to be based on what you like and don't like. Arch supports a plethora of desktops. The configuration and customization of those desktops is up to you.

Read the wiki pages, and browse the screenshot threads in the Arch forums to get an idea of the possibilities with each window manager or desktop environment, and then jump in with both feet.

### Post by cardinals_fan on 2009-02-03
Xfce: reasonably light, but still very complete.  Avoids the "bloat" associated with GNOME/KDE through modularity; all components can be used individually.

LXDE: a collection of separate projects.  Uses Openbox as the window manager.  Very modular.  A bit lighter than Xfce.

Openbox/Fluxbox/Pekwm: similar *box style window managers.  Not total desktop environments, which is either good or bad depending on your needs.

Tiling WMs: a whole different deal.  These "tile" your windows so the screen is always filled and nothing is left "buried".  Xmonad, dwm, awesome, and wmii are all popular choices.

### Post by chucky chuckaluck on 2009-02-05
go with openbox. it's wicked fast, it's simple and it gives you access to your apps without all the carnival extras. obconf and obmenu are great tools to use with it. you can use any variety of apps you want. it doesn't come with a panel, but you can use any panel you want, including gnome panel and xfce panel (there's also pypanel, tint, perlpanel, etc). if you just want a clock, you can use conky for that and skip the panel entirely (just use the middle click menu to access your minimized apps). there's lots more to it, but that gives you a taste. oh, did i mention it's wicked fast, yet?
