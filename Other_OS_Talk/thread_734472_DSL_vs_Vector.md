### Post by saulgoode on 2008-03-24
I would suggest Vector as a much better solution than DSL (unless you are interested in running the GNOME desktop). Vector is a complete system that is targeted at machines precisely such as the one you describe.  

DSL is designed to fit on a 50Mb ISO and, in its initial configuration, is based upon the GTK 1.2 versions of applications which are generally not maintained by the upstream projects. DSL is also uses the 2.4-series kernel -- though this is not a great concern as long as "it works". I would imagine that, like most distros, the potential is there to morph the system into whatever you like but why start out with less than what you think you will need? Unless the 50Mb ISO holds some benefit for you.
