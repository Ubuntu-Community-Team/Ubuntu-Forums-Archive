### Post by JawsThemeSwimming428 on 2008-04-22
Mepis! ([www.mepis.org](www.mepis.org)) I used Ubuntu for awhile and as you said after Feisty I didn't find much reason to continue using it as my main OS. I tried a few distros out but was very impressed when I came across Mepis. Excellent hardware detection, my wireless worked right away, and Debian based. Also, I think one of the best things about Mepis, is the community. The Mepis forums, [www.mepislovers.org](www.mepislovers.org), is one of the most helpful and friendly user forums there is. You can DL Mepis and run it as a liveCD with the option to install at any time. It's worth a shot, I haven't even thought about replacing it since I found it!

Regarding Arch Linux, myself and a few "Mepislovers" recently tested Arch out (we actually still are) and wrote a little something about it. Here is the link: [http://www.sblinux.org/pages/arch01.html](http://www.sblinux.org/pages/arch01.html)  This will bring you to the main page and if you go to the drop down menu in the top left you will see three entries under the Arch section all the way at the bottom. Might want to read through and see what you can take from them that might help you decide about Arch. I like it, but I feel more at home with Debian based (however, Pacman is one of the best package managers I have ever seen). If you can get past the install Arch is almost everything they say it is! Good luck.
