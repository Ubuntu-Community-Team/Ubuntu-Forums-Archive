### Post by mthei on 2007-10-08
Sidux is one of my favourites right now. Debian Sid running KDE. All the really cool features though are available post-installation, like the smxi script, which really helps for upgrades and configuration. Still, it's the fastest I've ever seen KDE run on a liveCD.
Elive is another good one, also based on Debian, but with the Enlightenment window manager. As a liveCD, it's great to try out E17, which is much better than E16, which is the one in Ubuntu's repositories.

### Post by greenstar on 2007-10-08
My favorite live CD's are the mini-distros:

BeaFanatix: (It uses Gnome, stripped down for speed.)
[http://bea.cabarel.com](http://bea.cabarel.com)

Puppy Linux: (Extremely fast on old hardware, excellent dial-up modem support, great hardware support & some great community remasters.)
[http://www.puppylinux.org](http://www.puppylinux.org)

SLAX: 
[http://www.slax.org/](http://www.slax.org/)

NimbleX: (You can create a custom live cd right from the website.)
[http://www.nimblex.net/](http://www.nimblex.net/)

DSL: (A little too light for my taste, but has an unmatched level of functionality.)
[http://www.damnsmalllinux.org/](http://www.damnsmalllinux.org/)


My favorite full-size live CD's are:

PCLinuxOS: (Great distro, I consider this one on par with Ubuntu, excellent community remasters.)
[http://www.pclinuxos.com](http://www.pclinuxos.com)

SAM Linux: (PCLinuxOS XFCE variant.)
[http://www.sam-linux.org/](http://www.sam-linux.org/)

TeenPup: (Larger selection of packages, etc, Puppy remaster.)
[http://www.murga-linux.com/puppy/viewtopic.php?t=15956](http://www.murga-linux.com/puppy/viewtopic.php?t=15956)


Utility Live CD's

Ultimate Boot CD: (Can't say enough about this disc.)
[http://www.ultimatebootcd.com/](http://www.ultimatebootcd.com/)

GParted
[http://gparted.sourceforge.net/](http://gparted.sourceforge.net/)

Parted Magic
[http://www.partedmagic.com/](http://www.partedmagic.com/)

Ghost4Linux
[http://sourceforge.net/projects/g4l](http://sourceforge.net/projects/g4l)

Greenstar

Knoppix with it's awesome hardware detection is great when nothing else will do, but I just don't like to use KDE if I don't have to. 

The exceptions to that are SLAX and PCLinuxOS. The folks at SLAX have managed to make KDE a pleasure to use, and you gotta love the all the extra modules you can add to SLAX. 

As for PCLinuxOS, you just can't beat what they've done with Mandrake/Mandriva, Synaptic, the PCLOS Control Panel, KDE and an excellent community. It's an excellent combination and beats the pants off of most distros. 

I still enjoy my Ubuntu way too much to go to another distro full-time. I do enjoy trying other distros, and from time to time, I recommend a non-Ubuntu distro, usually PCLinuxOS as it's one of the easiest distros for a noob to pick up and learn.
