### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-10-06
For my money, Arch is still the speed king. I know Gentoo is faster, but it's faster once it's set up. The time it takes me to get it going more or less negates the speed I win back with Gentoo. I'm willing to blame that on my inexperience, but I've heard other, more knowledgeable Gentoo users say the same thing.

Arch gives you a working text-based system in 15 minutes from a low-level-formatted drive, and the speed never stops. There's a learning curve attached. It's tricky to set up sometimes -- I fought with a wireless card for weeks and never did get it working ... Ubuntu configured it automatically. But if you know your hardware, there's nothing to compare, in my opinion.

After that, I'd go with a Slackware distribution ... Slax is my favorite these days. Although to be honest, I've got an Openbox setup in Ubuntu on a 1Ghz machine that goes from cold boot to the desktop in just under 41 seconds, and only needs 36Mb to get going ... :biggrin:

### Post by ahaslam on 2007-06-06
> **pelle.k said:**

I've never even tried a 64 bit distro out on my core2, so naturally i'm wondering...

When I started this thread I had an old laptop with a Sempron precessor & there was little to nothing between Zenwalk & Arch 32. 

Now with my Core2 desktop, Arch 64 boots about 30% faster than Zenwalk & general system responsiveness feels the same. Where it really excels is when encoding music or videos, this is largely due to the 64bit support but it's truly impressive. I have also experienced better performance in 3D games under Arch 64. The only downside for some people may be the extra memory usage (at least 10% higher than Zenwalk), but if you've a gig of ram this shouldn't be an issue (it's not swapped yet).

So it you don't do any encoding or gaming or have less than half a gig of ram, you'll probably notice little/no difference.

_*-EDIT-*_
It does swap. After playing Nexuiz I noticed that 160MB of swap was used. I think this is where the performance comes from, it actually uses your resources when required (that's a gig of dual channel DDR2 @ 900MHz 4-4-4-12). So maybe a Gig is the minimum level where extra performance in noticeable under Arch 64? I'd hate to think how much swapping would be going on with less.
