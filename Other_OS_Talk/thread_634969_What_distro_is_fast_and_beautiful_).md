### Post by fwojciec on 2007-12-08
> **doubledouce said:**
> It looks good. :) Is it fast?

I've been reading about Arch linux + KDEmod. Have anyone here tried it? It seems to meet my expectations with no bloatware, fast and (potentially) good looks.

Arch is, kind of, like a lego set of Linux.  It can be very light, it can be very beautiful but you need to "build it" yourself from the pieces that are provided.  It is developed as a universal base for whatever you want to achieve rather than as a particular end product.  

IMO it makes no sense to use Arch if you want things to be automatically configured and "just work" - if this is what you expect then Arch will just be frustrating in the long run since it is designed to empower users rather than to make decisions for them.  Most things will just work, but not everything and to get the best results you'll need to configure things by hand and troubleshoot minor problems every once in a while.  If, on the other hand, you don't mind reading and learning about Linux and if you like to tweak and customize then Arch is very close to perfection.  

I'd recommend you have a look at Arch website, the forums and the wiki to get a sense for what it's like before you decide to install it.

### Post by b9anders on 2007-12-11
I am using arch with xfce and compiz fusion and loving it.

screenshots:
[[IMG]http://img29.picoodle.com/img/img29/5/12/11/t_Screenshot1m_1d47c9d.png[/IMG]](http://www.picoodle.com/view.php?img=/5/12/11/f_Screenshot1m_1d47c9d.png&srv=img29)

[[IMG]http://img01.picoodle.com/img/img01/5/12/11/t_Screenshotm_09347d0.png[/IMG]](http://www.picoodle.com/view.php?img=/5/12/11/f_Screenshotm_09347d0.png&srv=img01)

A few tips for choice apps to allow you to avoid having to pull in gnome and kde stuff:
Use wicd to manage wireless instead of network-manager
slim instead of gdm
mirage for images
mplayer or vlc for video.
audacious for music
galculator for maths
squeeze for archives
epdfviewer for pdfs
htop/conky for system monitoring

will give you a good selection of very lightweight and very functional apps for basic tasks. Arch+xfce4+compiz-fusion is less than 160 MB on startup for me.

There is an xfce branch in development for awn, to avoid gnome dependencies. I am compiling it right now, but have run into a few glitches awaiting some support. I'll let you know how it turns out.

my laptop is 1.4gzh/512mb ram and runs with no lag at all with such a setup, except when first opening the really heavy apps like opera, gimp, exaile and openoffice.

good luck with it. as long as you rtfm (the beginner's guide is excellent) installing should be a breeze. :)
