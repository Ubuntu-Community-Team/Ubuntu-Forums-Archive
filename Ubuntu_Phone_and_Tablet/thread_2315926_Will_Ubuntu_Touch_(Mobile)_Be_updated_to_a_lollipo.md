### Post by DuckHook on 2016-03-03
Welcome to the forums, raamizh.

I think you are confusing hardware and operating systems here. Neither kitkat nor lollipop have anything to do with hardware. They are different versions of the Android OS which is all in software. The point of Ubuntu Touch is to replace Android altogether. So the phrase kitkat base or lollipop base have no application to Ubuntu Touch.

The reason that Touch has not been ported to the Nexus 6 has nothing to do with it being a "Lollipop device". There really is no such thing as a "lollipop device" because once Google updates it to Marshmallow, then it's running Marshmallow and is no longer Lollipop. Rather, the reason that Touch does not run on Nexus 6 is simply because no one has ported it to the Nexus 6 yet (to my knowledge). Admittedly, my knowledge is very lacking when it comes to Touch. There may be a community port already. BTW, a "community port" is one created by hobbyists and not sanctioned by either Ubuntu or Google. If such a port exists, other forum members will have to help you with it.

I just wanted to clarify what seemed like a confusion you have between hardware and software.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-03-03
As I understand the documentation, install Ubuntu for Devices to an Android device will remove the Android OS whatever version it is at.

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/)

To put android back on the device an Android image has to be downloaded and flashed to the device.

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/reinstalling-android/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/reinstalling-android/)

It also seems that no one is working on a port for the Nexus 6. It could be that the Nexus 6 is under-specified for Ubuntu for Devices. It was the Nexus 7 that was chosen as the reference device that the Canonical developers would use to test the code.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)
