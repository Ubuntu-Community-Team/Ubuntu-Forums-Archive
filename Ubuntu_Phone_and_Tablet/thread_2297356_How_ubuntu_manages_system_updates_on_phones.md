### Post by howefield on 2015-10-03
Updates are released approximately every 6 weeks.

The updates are phased out over a 24 hour period.. in order that any critical issues can be highlighted before everyone gets them. Ordinarily that would mean everyone would have the update within 24 hours of release. Happy days :)

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-10-03
There are big differences between the way things are done with Android and the way things are done by Cannonical.

To start with, every OEM modifies Android in some way. This means that the upgrading of Android is the responsibility of the OEM. With Ubuntu Phone Canonical has a way for an OEM to brand the phone without modifying the underlying OS. Canonical takes responsibility for upgrading the OS. So, it does not matter whether we have a model from BQ or Meizu or some other (future OEM) we will all get the upgrade to the Ubuntu Phone OS. At present, the intention is to provide upgrades to the OS (called OTAs - Over the Air) every 6 weeks.

Upgrades to applications are the responsibility of the application developer. But it is easy and quick to get an application accepted into the app store and it is possible to have more than one version of an app in the store at the same time. So, upgrades to the community apps happen as fast as the developer can make the changes and push them into the store and the user can can select updates from the systems settings.

Regards.
