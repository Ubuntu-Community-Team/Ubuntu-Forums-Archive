### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-08-03
There are two commercial companies selling 3 Ubuntu phone models. Development of the apps is open to all comers. There is an SDK (Software Development Kit) that is useful in creating apps and very helpful in getting apps packaged and published in the app store. The number of apps and app developers is doubling every 6 weeks.

The existing phones models get an Over the Air (OTA) update every six weeks. OTA 5 was recently pushed out. So, anyone who has purchased one of these phones has an OS that is up to date. 

There are a couple of ways a person can keep up to date with development. Every Tuesday at 15.00 UTC on Ubuntu On air there is a Community Q & A that often covers the Ubuntu phone. This was last weeks Q & A

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=47GczsHH9aI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=47GczsHH9aI)

And then there is Ubuntu Insights with a section on the phone and tablet

[https://insights.ubuntu.com/phone-and-tablet/](https://insights.ubuntu.com/phone-and-tablet/)

And then there is planet Ubuntu which aggregates certain blogs and some bloggers are involved in the Ubuntu phone development. Here is an example:

[http://popey.com/blog/2015/07/28/easily-port-mobile-html5-games-to-ubuntu-phone/](http://popey.com/blog/2015/07/28/easily-port-mobile-html5-games-to-ubuntu-phone/)

Do not expect people on this forum to know anything confidential about Ubuntu phones and tablets. We are not in the know.

Regards.
