### Post by denaxin on 2015-07-29
You can't *currently* install, nor run, GTK-written (Desktop software) code on **Ubuntu _Touch_** devices. You're tied to whats avaible on the Store, or what 3th party developers post around the internet for sideloading. If you're still interested in having **Ubuntu touch** on your tablet, I would recommend the* Samsung Nexus 10*, as its the only *officially* supported device with 10-inch screen ratio.

An other alternative is buying a **Windows-powered TabletPC**, with 10-inch screen ratio and running, either **Unity 8** (Ubuntu-next) or regular **Ubuntu 15.04** on it. I wouldn't recommend either of these, as **Unity 8** is unstable, and **Ubuntu 15.04** _*is not optimized for touch*_. If you're still interested i would recommend a **Microsoft Surface _*Pro*_ 1** (the first generation, because it's cheaper and is more supported) and follow [this]("http://bit.ly/1OP7RZt") tutorial on how to dual-boot **Windows** with **Regular Ubuntu**.

Hope this helps! Cheers!
