### Post by robcar on 2015-08-03
Tonight I got some time to further check the bluetooth/A2DP issue; this is my experience.

I removed the BT pairing between my car (Toyota Verso 2012) and the MX4; then I paired again the device.
I noticed that at some point at the beginning on the stereo display a text like "checking your device's available services" appeared  and then it said "telephone service added".
At this point only receiving/placing phone calls was possible; I noticed that I could see no text messages nor calls history.
On the stereo I went to Setup/BT settings and saw three icons for the paired MX4: phone, music and Internet. Only phone icon was lit; I manually enabled music and TADA! music was streaming from my car's speakers!
Then I changed the song on the phone and somehow the output was now from the MX4 speakers... uhmmm I struggled a bit, then opened and closed CuteSpotify: ok, the car's speakers again.
I turned the car off while the music was playing; then after a while music played from the phone; then turn the car key again to start it and, after a while, music was playing from the car's speakers!

So, in conclusion; I'm happy things are working *somehow*. I guess the device should advice better its services while doing the BT pairing (not only telephone ones, but also music and internet).

I still cannot use the device to listen to music though, because as soon as the device shuts the screen off, music stops.
Plus for my android phone I got a super useful app named Bluetooth connect & Play, which let Spotify automatically start whenever it paired to my car's stereo.
