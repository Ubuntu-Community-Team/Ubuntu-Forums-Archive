### Post by steve-wilson on 2017-02-21
I've been a keen and enthusiastic openSUSE user for over 10 years but have had very little experience with Ubuntu. Nevertheless, with very few options on offer when it comes to a linux os tablet I took the plunge and went for the aquaris m10 ubuntu edition last year.

I wish my Phablet experience to date could be similar to that of my Desktop openSUSE, but other than making use of the Phablet's (slightly unstable) web browser (no flash option) I don't really have much use for it at the moment. There's no convenient CUPS printer option, no networking config, in fact very little in the way of applications or system options. And with very little, if any, improvements being offered in system updates in the past year, it's getting frustrating.

My question is this...... Is the eventual aim of the Canonical team to create a fully functional Linux environment on a tablet, or is the Phablet project simply going to aim at the 'entry level' market as an alternative to Android etc with basic apps like web browser, email and camera? If it's the first, I'll hang on and patiently wait, but if it's the latter I'll probably load Android!

Comments from those that have a better understanding and experience of the Phabet than I have had to date would be very much appreciated.

If I were buying a new netbook today, it would NOT be a chromebook, but I'm not in the market, so don't have any specific models.
