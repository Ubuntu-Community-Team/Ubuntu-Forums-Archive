### Post by dtarrant on 2016-05-26
I wanted to try out desktop mode. I decided to try my 2.4G Mini Wireless Touchpad Keyboard that I originally bought to use with my Raspberry Pi. These are available on ebay for about £10.

The mini keyboard is supplied with a special usb dongle. I connected this to the M10 with an OTG cable. It worked fine! Touchpad has left and right buttons, so dragging is supported. Downside is that keyboard is ultra mini (really tiny) so is of no practical use for writing your memoirs.

I then decided to purchase a KKMoon 81 Key Bluetooth Touchpad Keyboard from ebay (about £13). This is the same size as the M10 and worked fine except for one important limitation: there are no mouse buttons, so dragging isn't possible. You can run LibreOffice Writer in full screen mode to write your memoirs, but without dragging, you couldn't use the drawing package.

I am writing this on my shiny new M10 in touch mode and I had planned to add a photo of the ultra mini keyboard. Unfortunately the manage attachments feature of this forum doesn't work for me.

To help you identify the ultra mini keyboard on ebay, it has a letterbox footprint with a distinctly rounded bottom right hand corner.

According to the KKMoon user guide, dragging is supported by Windows, but not by Android.

I have just discovered that the [B]kkmoon keyboard does have mouse buttons.

See my subsequent post.

[/B]

### Post by dtarrant on 2016-05-26
I have discovered more about using the M10 in desktop mode:

1.  The touchscreen remains active when the kkmoon bluetooth keyboard is connected.

2.  You can drag with your finger to move and resize windows.

3.  Most importantly, the kkmoon does have mouse buttons! You have to press gently on the left or right region of the touchpad which then pivots slightly and clicks!!!

So I'm pleased to report that the kkmoon 81 key bluetooth touchpad keyboard does a pretty good job and makes a fine companion for the m10. Good to write your memoirs and create diagrams!!!
