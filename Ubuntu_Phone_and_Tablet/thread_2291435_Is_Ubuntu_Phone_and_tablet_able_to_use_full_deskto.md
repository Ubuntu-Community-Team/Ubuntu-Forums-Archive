### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-08-20
Do not expect too much from Ubuntu mobile devices. We do not yet have a Ubuntu tablet for retail. And we are told to expect the first "converged" Ubuntu mobile device later on in the year. How "converged" that will be will depend not only on the hardware but also on what we understand convergence to mean as well has the amount of progress made in that direction.

As to actual experience of using Ubuntu for Devices on a Nexus 4 phone then read this blog

[http://mhall119.com/2015/05/working-from-my-ubuntu-phone/](http://mhall119.com/2015/05/working-from-my-ubuntu-phone/)

Applications on the Ubuntu phone are Click packages which is a development of Debian packaging. Click packaging has evolved into Snap packaging and the Ubuntu phone will in time be based on Snappy Ubuntu which does not use apt-get. See, what I mean about not expecting too much of a Ubuntu mobile device?

The OS will be a snap package and read only. Applications will be snap packages and will run under confinement and access to information on other parts of the file system will be tightly controlled. This is a completely different form of Ubuntu. It will change what we expect from a version of Ubuntu. This blog post from a Canonical engineer may clear things up but it will mostly produce more questions than answers.

[http://www.olli-ries.com/t-242d/](http://www.olli-ries.com/t-242d/)

Regards
