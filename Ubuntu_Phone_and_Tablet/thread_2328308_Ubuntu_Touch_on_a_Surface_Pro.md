### Post by huwwevans on 2016-07-20
Neither is perfect but gnome is slightly better for touchscreens because the on-screen keyboard is bigger (possibly not relevant to you as I believe the surface has a physical keyboard).  The menus are all easier to navigate in gnome too and you are less likely to open a new window while closing a new one as the pacing of the menu is different.  

The differences are minor but they add up to a slightly better user experience on gnome.
