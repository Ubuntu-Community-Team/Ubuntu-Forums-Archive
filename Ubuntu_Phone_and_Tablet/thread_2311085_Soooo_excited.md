So, first reports frankly like a duck to water.

The set up process takes probably 1 minute max and your good to go.

There is a demo on the screen to show you how to navigate the screen. But to be honest after a day I will be used to it.

You can sync you Google account to get your contacts and other bits of information, but do a refresh on your contacts screen after syncing otherwise your contacts will not show.

For the low spec phone, it isn't all that slow, the screen rotates pretty quick and it loads different scopes and apps from the tool bar quick enough.

Obviously I need to let it charge now, but trust me boys and girls this is going to be good.

I will post more tomorrow when I have had chance to play a bit more.

### Post by JOHN_ELLIS on 2016-01-28
Day 1 with the phone

First off, I love music, I listen to it at work when i'm on my own, the phone is my car stereo, so you get the picture. The speakers are great, plenty loud enough and good bass.

The phone is light in weight, so it doesn't swing round in your pocket.

The battery life is great, despite plenty of use I have almost a full battery left.

Scopes, it didn't take me long to get used to them and I like them. I love the idea that all the music I need is on one scope, same with pictures and video's and the Today scope is so useful.

I also like the fact that you can reply to a text message from the notification area, no need to open apps or navigate to a certain page, just type and send.

I have shown the phone to around six different people at lunch time and they were impressed by it.

My honest opinion of my experience today is that this phone could easily survive in the world today, yes it still needs some development but it has everything you need bundled with the OS to get on with things. It wouldn't hurt to have a bit more spec with the phone but even with the 3G and slow processor and limited memory it didn't bother me. I am happy to have this as my everyday phone.

I will miss my mobile banking apps and maybe a couple of others but am I going to miss them enough to want to go back to my Sony, absolutely not, pretty much everything I did with my Sony I do with my Ubuntu phone.

Canonical have started something here which in the near future is really going to turn heads, i'm looking forward to the day I can go into my local mobile phone store and see the Ubuntu logo sitting proudly with a range of phones for people to buy.

John

### Post by JOHN_ELLIS on 2016-01-30
Well after a few days of testing I conclude, Ubuntu Touch rocks. I now have a fitness app that tracks my runs and cycling and uploads them to Strava.

I find the "near by" scope really useful for finding things that are near wherever you are from cycling shops to automotive shops, cinema's whatever you need you can find it easily. I can't really pick out any faults at all with the Ubuntu Touch OS.

I took some pictures whilst out today and the camera is really good, even with the picture quality on its lowest setting the pictures are good.

Back to the serious note, there are bugs and they are being fixed with every update, as long as people keep reporting them then they will be fixed and the OS will just keep getting stronger and better.

I would love in the future to see a handset come out with much better hardware specs and i'm sure in time that there will be a sat nav app come out like "here drive" since we already have "here maps" i'm not sure why they haven't released it already I don't know maybe they are working on it, but like I say it will come.

Onwards and upwards :)

Cheers
