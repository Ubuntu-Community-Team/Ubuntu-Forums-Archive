### Post by bernard-godard on 2016-04-27
I am interested in using the keyboard  [http://www.logitech.com/en-roeu/product/living-room-keyboard-k830](http://www.logitech.com/en-roeu/product/living-room-keyboard-k830) with the M10.
However it requires Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE marketed as bluetooth smart) support.

I have contacted BQ about this and their reply is that the M10 hardware supports BLE but the current firmware does not support it.
I don't know if by firmware they mean the Ubuntu system image (linux bluetooth stack upgrade) or the bluetooth chip firmware/microcode.

BQ has opened a Launchpad bug: [https://bugs.launchpad.net/canonical-devices-system-image/+bug/1574540](https://bugs.launchpad.net/canonical-devices-system-image/+bug/1574540)

On a different note, according to the M10 ubuntu edition user manual, the tablet can only handle one bluetooth device at a time; that is unless you have a combo bluetooth mouse+keyboard you need to plug one of them via USB. However from what is reported in [http://askubuntu.com/questions/759899/bq-m10-bluetooth-keyboard-and-mouse-at-the-same-time](http://askubuntu.com/questions/759899/bq-m10-bluetooth-keyboard-and-mouse-at-the-same-time) , this does not appear to be the case.
