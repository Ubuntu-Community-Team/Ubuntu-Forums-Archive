### Post by dtarrant on 2016-11-30
The soft keyboard normally appears and disappears automatically. In most cases this is fine. In most cases it can be closed manually by swiping down from the top edge of the keyboard. In some cases it can be re-opened by swiping up from the bottom edge. In other cases however, it cannot be opened in this manner. I have recently experienced the situation where the keyboard was automatically hidden to enable me to select an option from a drop down box. However, to continue, I needed to confirm my selection by clicking enter. The trouble was I couldn't re-open the soft keyboard. I believe it is essential that a foolproof way of opening the keyboard should be provided. On my Blackberry Playbook, the keyboard can always be invoked by swiping diagonally upwards from the bottom left of the display. This always over-rides whatever the intelligent control has set. If I could always open the ubuntu touch keyboard by swiping up from the centre of the bottom edge of the display, I would be quite satisfied. I guess that at present, the variability may be application dependent. Maybe it would be better if this control were made independent of the shortcomings of individual apps. What do my fellow ubuntu touch afficionados think? BTW, on this page, I can't re-open the keyboard by swiping up from the bottom edge, However I can re-open it by clicking witin the text to be edited. So it's possible in most circumstances, but inconsistent and in a few circumstances, it seems to be impossible. This may seem trivial, but it has caused me to waste much time. This morning, I had to resort to my ubuntu laptop to accomplish what should have been a simple task.
