It's important to remember that - as has been pointed-out many times - the *current* mobile devices with Ubuntu for Mobile Devices are *_not_* aimed at *everyday users*... They are *specifically* aimed at developers and "early adopters" like myself. Therefore, it is possible that the warranty terms may change once products are released targeting everyday users...

In saying that, there *are* many manufacturers offering products globally that have similar (or the same!) warranty policies - for example, the immensely-popular "Pebble" products require users to send their "smart" watches back at the *owner's* expense (don't quote me, but as I understand it, Apple only just changed their policy to something along these lines the other day!)... So even if bq stick to their *existing* policy, they wouldn't be the *first*.
