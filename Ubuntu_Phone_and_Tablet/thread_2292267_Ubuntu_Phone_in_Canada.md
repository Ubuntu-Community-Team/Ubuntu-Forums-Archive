### Post by adnan-ahmed1 on 2015-09-01
I believe the phones only have non-Canadian frequencies for the 3G or higher capabilities.  For example, the Aquaris E4.5 ([http://www.bq.com/gb/aquaris-e4-5-ubuntu-edition](http://www.bq.com/gb/aquaris-e4-5-ubuntu-edition)) has the Canadian frequencies for GSM (2G) that would match Rogers, Fido, and subsidiaries, but in 3G/HSPA the frequencies are not what is used in Canada.  Including LTE now, Canadian frequencies are 700, 850, 1900, 1700, 2100.  So you will need to be sure that the carrier of your choice can support 2100 in 3G/HSPA before getting the Aquaris.  Even if it supports it in LTE, it may not do so for voice (LTE in Canada is data only, voice is carried on 3G or 2G networks).
