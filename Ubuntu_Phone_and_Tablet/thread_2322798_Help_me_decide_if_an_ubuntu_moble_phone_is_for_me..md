### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-04-30
We have a section on this forum for Ubuntu Phone & Tablet. Have you looked in that section?

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=460](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=460)

There are two OEMs producing Ubuntu mobile devices - BQ (Spain) and Meizu (China). I think that each company has at present 2 phone models & one of them has a tablet model.

What do you think a Ubuntu phone would be like? The Ubuntu distribution running on a Smartphone that will let you do everything that you can do on Ubuntu installed on a desktop or laptop machine? If so, then a Ubuntu phone is not for you.

Do you expect to be able to run Android apps on it? After all, Ubuntu is Linux & Android is, well, a kind of Linux. No Android apps. In fact do not expect a Ubuntu phone to have anything like the same number of apps in the app store as an Android device. Not for many months. And some of people's "must have" Android apps will not be ported to the Ubuntu phone until the owners of those apps see a possibility of making some profit from the conversion work.

Are you willing to be an early adopter? If not, the present generation of Ubuntu mobile devices is not for you. Ubuntu for the desktop is free to download but we should not think that Ubuntu mobile devices are cheap, low specification devices. 

Do you want a device that has the latest version of the OS even if your device is not the latest model? That's a Ubuntu phone. The Ubuntu phones & tablets get an OS upgrade (called Over the Air - OTA updates) every six weeks. All models get the OTA.

Do you want a device where you get the latest versions of apps as soon as the developer has upgraded his app & put it in the app store. That's a Ubuntu phone.

Do you want a device that becomes a PC when connected to a digital monitor and keyboard and mouse? Then make sure that the model does HMDI video out. But do not expect the full Ubuntu desktop experience for another 6 to 12 months. The foundation of convergence is now present. The full implementation is yet future.

Regards.
