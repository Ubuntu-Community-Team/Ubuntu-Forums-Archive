You may be thinking of Ubuntu Touch which is downloadable and installable to certain devices.

See [https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/)

There is a list of officially supported devices and also a list of community driven ports. If your phone isn't on either list then you are out of luck. Installing Ubuntu onto a phone is a much more involved process than installing onto a PC. Most of us will be waiting for Ubuntu to be preloaded as per the phones that are now becoming available through BQ and Meizu.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-06-28
You are in the situation of porting Ubuntu Touch to your device. And because Ubuntu Touch is open source code you are allowed to download the code and experiment with it. And you are invited to do that. But keep in mind it is an experiment and you will be the experimenter. You might be the first person to port Ubuntu Touch to that device and not all the hardware may be recognised.

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/porting-new-device/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/porting-new-device/)

Just as your work in porting Ubuntu Touch to your device will not be officially supported by Canonical, so the work of others porting Ubuntu Touch to their devices is not officially supported by Canonical.

As already pointed out, officially Ubuntu Touch is for manufacturers to pre-install on devices for retail. All the work done on Ubuntu Touch by Canonical is to that end. We just want you to understand the situation. We have been following the development of Ubuntu phones for the past two years and many times people have come to this forum expecting to able to install Ubuntu to their phone and they also expect it to be an easy things to do. And that is not the situation.
