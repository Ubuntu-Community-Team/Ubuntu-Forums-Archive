### Post by aleksander7 on 2016-03-10
Well first of all you need to check if your phone can run it, not all devices are supported yet.
Ubuntu Touch is still in an early stage so you can't do as much as with Android or IOS. There isn't a lot of apps yet but you can get apps like Facebook, different emails apps, Here maps, Spotify client, Instagram, some of them unofficial. 
If you need/use Spotify, Snapchat, instagram, Netflix and so on then I would wait a while. 
When it comes to reliability and performance I would say its pretty good, my Meizu MX4 is a little buggy but it works without any major issues. The camera app doesn't have a lot of features and the auto capitalization doesn't work so good, sometimes you get two big letters. The last one could be different with another device. 

You can check out the Ubuntu Store here: [https://uappexplorer.com/](https://uappexplorer.com/)

What device are you planning on installing on?

### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-03-10
Here are the links to Ubuntu for Devices.

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/)

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/devices/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/devices/)

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/)

Unless we are using one of the supported devices then we will not be installing Ubuntu on the phone but porting Ubuntu to the phone. This is another matter entirely.

[https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/porting-new-device/](https://developer.ubuntu.com/en/start/ubuntu-for-devices/porting-new-device/)

There is something in the desktop software centre called unity8-desktop-session-mir. It is for evaluation & demonstration purposes only. Do not think of it as Ubuntu Touch on the desktop it is not that at all.

You will get a login session that will switch from using Xserver & Unity 7 to using MIr & Unity 8 + a few core apps. And nothing much else. It proves the concept works. And I think that is all it is meant to do. We cannot use with a proprietary video driver. And click apps that are built for an ARM CPU phone will not run on an X86 CPU in a desktop machine.

I have installed unity8-desktop-session-mir on 14.04 upwards to 16.04. And it has been broken, loading to a black screen on 15.04, 15.10 and now 16.04. So, I am not impressed at all with unity8-desktop-session-mir.

There are people on here that can explain more on this but-

I would consider backing up your phone (not just contacts, etc, but the entire OS) on your computer (I have some experience with "Odin," there may be better ones out there).
