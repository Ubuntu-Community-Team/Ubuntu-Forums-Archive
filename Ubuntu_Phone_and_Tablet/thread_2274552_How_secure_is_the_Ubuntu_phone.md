### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-04-21
In what areas of security are you inquiring about?

As you know all mobile communications go through the computers of a service provider just as all communications by email also do. In fact all our use of the internet is going through someone's computers. All that is a potential point of privacy vulnerability that is outside the scope of the computer OS.

Also, unless we are using an encrypted WiFi connection the transmissions between the device and the WiFi access point can be detected by anyone with the right equipment who is in range of the radio signals. 

The Ubuntu phone is designed to be a normal kind of smart phone for the mass market. I do not think that Canonical, the sponsor of Ubuntu, would get involved in making the kind of computer OS that you seem to desire.

On the other hand a lot of work has been done on the Ubuntu phone OS to prevent malicious code being installed and doing unauthorised stuff on a Ubuntu phone. And when the benefits of all that work on the Ubuntu phone come to the Ubuntu desktop, then our desktop and laptop machines will be even more secure.

Do, you wish to ask about that side of security?

Regards.
