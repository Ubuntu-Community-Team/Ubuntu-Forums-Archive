### Post by aeyoun on 2016-03-28
It&#8217;s not just an issue with Ubuntu for phones. The Unity desktop for computers also lacks CalDAV and CardDAV support. In Settings: Internet Accounts, you can only choose from a handful of proprietary branded options. The same situation holds true for GNOME Online Accounts (GOA) and KDE Online Accounts. The irony is of course that when you choose one of the branded options in these &#8220;account managers&#8217;, you&#8217;re **actually setting up CalDAV and CardDAV sources**!

I wrote an article about how well [GNOME supports CalDAV and CardDAV]("https://www.slightfuture.com/technote/gnome-caldav?") once you actually figure out how to set it up. I&#8217;ve also tried pushing GNOME developers in multiple channels to improve the situation. I even submitted a &#8212; mostly ignored &#8212; [patch to GOA]("https://bugzilla.gnome.org/show_bug.cgi?id=720519#c8") that would expose CalDAV and CardDAV options. It&#8217;s just not a priority.

As it stands now, all open source desktops seem to only promote proprietary branded services&#8217; implementation of open standards rather than promote the open standards and an open web and service provider independent ecosystem. Now that I&#8217;ve got to think a little more about it, I think I&#8217;ll write another article on the topic.
