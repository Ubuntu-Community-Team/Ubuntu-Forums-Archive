The camera also auto-focusses, so no touching is needed unless you want a specific focus point. That said, the auto-focus is poor under low lighting and the touch focus point is not much better under those conditions. That is bug-reported.

Two things about image size: firstly, there is a bug in that the full resolution of the MX4 sensor is not used. This is seen by looking at the pixel size of the image. Front camera likewise is  wrongly dimensioned. Even so - and the second point - I believe what you describe is variation in image byte size and that is an artefact of jpg compression. Images with high potential redundancy, such as large areas of relatively uniformly coloured sky, can be compressed more than those with lots of variation, as jpg tries to honour the original according to the quality factor chosen for the compression. Typically, that is 85/90%.
