### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-06-29
Desktop and laptop hardware is reasonably standardized. So, it is possible for Linux developers to produce driver modules for most computer hardware. It is a different situation with phone and tablet hardware. There is a great variation in hardware and the Linux developers have not been targeting mobile devices in the same way that they develop for desktop and laptop machines.

Canonical, the sponsor of Ubuntu, partners with two hardware manufacturers to get Ubuntu phones and tablets to market. Canonical is certainly not trying to produce a version of Ubuntu that can be installed on all kinds of phones and tablets. That has never been the intention.

Work on porting the Ubuntu phone OS to other devices is done by anyone who likes a challenge. Here is the work being done by one individual mainly at his own expense.

[https://devices.ubports.com/#/](https://devices.ubports.com/#/)

Your device is not in his list. But if anyone is willing to buy him an Xperia Z1 then he will have a go at porting Ubuntu Touch to the Xperia Z1. And that illustrates the problem. Without the cooperation of the manufacturer the task of porting is not so easy.
