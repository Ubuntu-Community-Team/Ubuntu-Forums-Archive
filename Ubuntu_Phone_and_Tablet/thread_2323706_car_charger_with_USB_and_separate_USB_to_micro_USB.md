### Post by Tao_Joannes on 2016-05-07
With chargers I've found the amps output to be the speed determinant. Chargers meant for apple devices/tablets generally put out 2.1 amps in the states, others are a crapshoot, about the only thing you're guaranteed is 500 MA.

Expense/quality has more relevance to how long it will last, how much abuse it'll put up with, and whether it will burst into flames unexpectedly.

### Post by Tao_Joannes on 2016-05-07
My own experience with cables is that sometimes they don't carry enough power to drive the device the way they should. With USB drives/DVD they might not spin up enough to properly initiate. If you're just charging, crappy cable = less amps = longer charge time.

Not that you should overspend on some monster cable BS, but that significantly thinner and flimsier cables likely have thinner conductors and can carry less power.
