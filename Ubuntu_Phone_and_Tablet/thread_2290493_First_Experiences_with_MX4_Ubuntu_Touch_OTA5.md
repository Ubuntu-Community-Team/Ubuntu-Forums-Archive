### Post by andrew-q2 on 2015-08-12
Here are some observations and questions&#8230;
 
Overall I&#8217;m pleased with the phone and OS.  The only smartphone I&#8217;ve previously used is a Windows 8 one.  I had a bit of a look for some kind of tutorial but didn&#8217;t find anything so have just played with to get familiar with it.  Inevitably I&#8217;ll go on about the problems more than the things that work well&#8230;
 
It has a habit of not being quite up to date!  The home scope &#8220;Today&#8221; might say no texts today when I know I had one.  The GPS seems to play up in the Nearby Scope - often it&#8217;s seems correct (or fairly close anyway - not sure how accurate it should be) but other times it is stubbornly showing a location from say the previous day.  Swiping down and then releasing to refresh makes no difference right now as I write this.  Sometimes turning Location off in the Nearby Scope, then back on, seems to kick it into life.  I saw another post about GPS and it seems the problems have been going on since last year.
 
When you swipe down from the top edge to get the system indicators, the typeface is very small and I find the greyed out items difficult to read.  Perhaps these wordings could be made bigger? - there&#8217;s nothing else on the screen competing for space - well not that I&#8217;ve seen so far.  For me the typeface is a bit small generally.  I like the Readability app which takes any webpage and presents the text (and pictures) very clearly, simply laid out, and at a good size.
 
When you enter a phone number in contacts, it auto-adjusts it to a 5-6 format.  I&#8217;d rather I could put in spaces how I want and it would remember it.
 
The phone has crashed several times.  I had trouble adding a photo to a contact.  I took the photo during the edit contact journey but it didn&#8217;t then appear for linking to the contact as I recall.
 
In Settings, GPS is not an item in its own right (like e.g. wifi) but part of Security and Privacy.  Not a big deal though!
 
The battery seems to last ok, though I&#8217;m probably a light user.
 
**** A Question please: If the phone is &#8220;sleeping&#8221; i.e. display black, can you be confident that neither the OS nor any apps are executing, that is, the battery is not being hammered and data usage is not being consumed.  I suppose an exception must be any audio apps running.
 
**** Question 2 please:  Is there any difference between a Restart, and a switch on from &#8220;cold&#8221; for an &#8220;ordinary user&#8221; like me.  By that I mean someone who is just getting the main OTA updates and using the standard apps, and therefore not doing &#8220;risky&#8221; stuff like flashing the phone.
 
I sent an email using Dekko linked to my Outlook.com account last night.  It got to 50% then said it couldn&#8217;t complete, and I tried two more times, getting the same.  However I later found all 3 in my sent mail in Outlook.
 
The &#8220;Battery&#8221; option in settings sometimes shows bizarre info but today has looked very sensible, showing a shallow decline.
 
**** Question 3:  Are there useful apps which are safe to install (safe from a privacy viewpoint, safe from corrupting the OS&#8230;) which are outside of the &#8220;store&#8221;?
 
**** Question 4: Other OSs and their apps collect data for marketing and other nefarious purposes.  I get the impression Ubuntu and Canonical are different, and that perhaps this doesn&#8217;t go on?  Am I right?  What&#8217;s the deal with Touch re. 3[SUP]rd[/SUP] parties collecting data?
 
Ok well that&#8217;s it for now.  The above are the minus points, but overall I like it.  Next I must connect it up to Win7 via USB and see about files transfer and music and photos.  I wouldn&#8217;t normally bother to write all this about a product, no way, but I like the whole Ubuntu concept, and this is my contribution. Thank you to all the developers and designers.
 
Andrew

### Post by coffeecat on 2015-08-13
I can't answer your questions, but some feedback on some of your comments from this new-ish BQ Aquaris 5 user.

> **andrew-q2 said:**
> I had a bit of a look for some kind of tutorial. 

Since I couldn't find a Meizu manual online, [here's a link to the BQ Aquaris one]("http://it-bqcom15-media.s3.amazonaws.com/prod/resources/manual/Aquaris_E5_ubuntu_user_manual-1433775885.pdf"). It's hardly a tutorial, but the instructions for using the OS might be of help.

> **andrew-q2 said:**
> The GPS seems to play up in the Nearby Scope - often it&#8217;s seems correct (or fairly close anyway - not sure how accurate it should be) but other times it is stubbornly showing a location from say the previous day.  Swiping down and then releasing to refresh makes no difference right now as I write this.  Sometimes turning Location off in the Nearby Scope, then back on, seems to kick it into life. I saw another post about GPS and it seems the problems have been going on since last year. 

I do see some oddities with this, but I think they're all to do with the strength of the mobile signal, which can be decidedly iffy in some of the places I've tested the phone. Sometimes I can make calls but get no internet access through the mobile signal. The data connection icon showing E, H, 2g or 3g disappears, leaving only the signal strength icon. On such occasions the location can appear to be stuck on the last time it was able to refresh properly. Can you do some testing in a strong signal area and then a weak? I'd be interested to hear what you experience.

When the signal is good enough, I find I get location accurate to about 200 yards, so if I repeatedly refresh, the details vary but do give me an approximate location. The here app consistenly gives me an accurate location on the displayed map, so I don't think the GPS is at fault. 

One real oddity I've seen is that when I first boot the phone up, the Nearby scope tells me my location is a particular place in London (which I'm not), so I guess it might be showing me the location of my network provider's main server.

> **andrew-q2 said:**
> When you swipe down from the top edge to get the system indicators, the typeface is very small and I find the greyed out items difficult to read.  Perhaps these wordings could be made bigger?

I disagree - and my eyes are old. On the Aquaris, which has a smaller screen than the Meizu, the typeface of the indicator is small but readable. If it was bigger, the indicators would spill sideways and there would be room for fewer on the screen, making the sideways swiping a bit confusing.

> **andrew-q2 said:**
> When you enter a phone number in contacts, it auto-adjusts it to a 5-6 format. I&#8217;d rather I could put in spaces how I want and it would remember it.

I guess you must be in the UK, and that you've only tried a limited set of numbers, the ones with the 01xxx xxxxxx or 07yxx xxxxxx pattern, where y is not 0. Try numbers beginning with 020, 070 and 0801 (for example) and you'll find it formats it differently from 5-6 but according to convention. The contacts app in Android phones does the same. I imagine the requirement for custom insertion of spaces is considered to be not of high priority, if it has been considered at all.
