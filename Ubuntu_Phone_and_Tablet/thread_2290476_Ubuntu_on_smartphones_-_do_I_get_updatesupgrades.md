### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-08-12
I do not have a Ubuntu phone but I have followed its development. Canonical has taken the responsibility for keeping the Ubuntu phone OS updated. So, the Ubuntu phone OS does not have the fragmentation that exists in Android and users do not have to wait for the OEM to update the OS. Or buy a newer model.

The Ubuntu phone has what are called transactional updates. And Canonical is pushing out what it calls Over the Air (OTA) updates every six weeks. Ubuntu phones whether by BQ (Spain) or Meizu (China) recently received OTA 5. This is what users can expect from OTA 6

[http://news.softpedia.com/news/ubuntu-touch-ota-6-update-to-arrive-in-approximately-6-weeks-487389.shtml](http://news.softpedia.com/news/ubuntu-touch-ota-6-update-to-arrive-in-approximately-6-weeks-487389.shtml)

So, unlike desktop Ubuntu where Long Term Support versions get 5 years support and Interim release versions get 9 months with Ubuntu phones there is no defined support period. The updates will just keep coming. And each Ubuntu mobile device will be as up to date as the newest devices being retailed.

Updates to applications are again being done differently to the method used on Ubuntu desktop. All applications in the App store are Click packages and not Debian (deb) packages. It is very easy and quick for a developer to get his app into the app store. So, when a developer updates his app he pushes it into the app store and the user is able to update the app. It is possible to have more than one version of an app in the app store.

Does its work? Of course it does. It would not be offered for sale it Ubuntu phones did not work. But keep in mind that it is at this moment an early adopters device and do not have too an high expectation. Do not expect Ubuntu phones to run Android apps. Do not expect Ubuntu phones to have as many apps as Android. Do not expect it to have the same apps as other mobile devices. And do not expect a Ubuntu phone to be Ubuntu on a phone. It most definitely is not Ubuntu desktop squeezed on to a phone. Do expect your Ubuntu phone to get OS improvements as time goes on.

An important thing to remember about Ubuntu phones for sale by any OEM is that Canonical has worked with the OEM to get the best out of the hardware.
