### Post by AboSamoor on 2009-10-19
Hi, Saif. Welcome to the world of freedom.

Maybe the best to help open source is to start by helping yourself. I mean, try to use open source alternatives to the proprietary ones. For example, if you use MS c++ compiler in the university, try to compile your code against GNU C compiler so you be sure of how to switch to the open source later. The same applies to anything you use in university.

Try to make your friends aware of the open source world, and try to show them how much it is superior to what they used to. If you build a good community around open source in your university, it will be a good idea to organize events and activities with [http://www.jolug.org/](http://www.jolug.org/) or [http://www.just.edu.jo/opensource/](http://www.just.edu.jo/opensource/).
