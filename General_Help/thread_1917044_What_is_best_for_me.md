### Post by TheFu on 2012-01-29
You can load lots of different desktops without changing your entire distribution. Do that.  I've used Gnome2, gnome3, unity, KDE, XDE, LXDE and lots of pure window managers over the years.

Stay with your ubuntu, just load different DEs through the package manager and select then at the login screen as you like.

### Post by sgage on 2012-01-29
Gnome Shell can be made to look and feel a lot like old-school panel-y Gnome with a few easily installed extensions.

Gnome Classic (i.e. the gnome-fallback-session package) needs very little tweaking indeed to be very similar to panel-y Gnome. There are guides (including one pinned to this forum) with hints and tweaks. 

No need for KDE, Lubuntu, Mint, etc.
