### Post by t0p on 2009-06-03
Okay, I don't know what you mean when you say you "played with" the Sources. If you have changed settings in Software *Sources* (not "resourses"), you might end up with some updates that you don't particularly want/need - but I can't see how that could damage your system.  So don't fret!

As to what to do: you have a couple of options:

1. Accept the updates.  I don't think they'll break your machine!
2. Reject the updates.  This could possibly be harmful, if you reject a security update that your system actually needs and you end up with possible vulnerabilities that some nasty person might exploit.
3. Look through the updates, accept the security updates, dismiss the others.

There's nothing about your screenshot that looks worrying.  My system is set up to get all Community and Third Party updates except for source code.  But make sure your system is looking for security updates.  They're the only ones that I would consider crucial.
