### Post by gingermark on 2006-02-20
I would agree. amaroK works fine, just don't enable the collection options, and disable the context switch, and you'll have a basic player, but one which still offers the tagging options.

You can use the in-built file browser to import music files and playlists, and you can save absolute or relative playlists as well.

As well as amaroK I personally use XMMS most of the time, and think it's fine, but if you do want to rummage through the other KDE players then Kaffeine is reasonable for playing music (in addition to video, obviously) and juK also supports playlists. Noatun and Kaboodle are the other two KDE players that come to mind, but I don't think the support playlists.

Other non-kde players you could have a look at are Quod Libet, Listen, and Zinf, but I'm kinda scraping the barrel now.

There was also a linux port of the rebranded Winamp 3 player (when they released that code under an open source license) but I can't remember the name of it. I do remember it being pretty shoddy.

If you are a Winamp fan though I still think your best bet is XMMS. Granted, it's a little antiquated now, but it's still a damn good player.

### Post by Jucato on 2006-02-20
amaroK has an option to make it display like XMMS (player, equalizer, and playlist can be separated). It also has a built-in system tray functionality (you need to install a plugin for XMMS to get this). It can also play streams well. The only thing I have against it is that it's a bit of a resource hog, compared to XMMS or VLC.

XMMS is light, can be skinned (and with SuperKaramba, who needs skins?). Unfortunately, I haven't been able to play some AAC and OGG streams successfully on it (Amarok can).

VLC is also a good player, because it can play both Audio and Video. It's a bit also lighter than Amarok (resource wise), but the choice of skins are very limited.

Kaffeine is a good audio/video player. It's a bit better than VLC for me except that it doesn't play some formats that VLC can (bin/cue, for example). Also, I haven't exactly been able to play streaming audio/video on it. Though I'm not sure if I'm doing something wrong.

I'm also looking for a lightweight alternative to amaroK, so maybe I'll try JuK and Beep/BMP. Noatun is a no-no for me. Somehow it's having trouble w/ Xine, and I don't think it can play streams.
