### Post by vasa1 on 2013-04-27
> **rmcellig said:**
> Is it possible to add more styles to Xubuntu 13.04?
Short answer: yes! People have been adding more "styles" or themes to distros for a while. Search the software center for themes. For even more themes, search the internet.
Themes you get from the internet are better installed in ~/.themes rather than /usr/share/themes, IMO.
The software center should offer you Ambiance and Radiance when you install "light-themes". Then there's High Contrast that you get by installing gnome-accessibility-themes.

### Post by Dennis N on 2013-04-27
For Xubuntu, look for themes that support GTK2, GTK3 and xfwm4 (window manager) so that things match - examples: Zukitwo, Greybird. The authors usually say what is supported on the theme pages. Otherwise you can download and check the folders inside the archive.

Theme shopping:

[http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/)

[http://xfce-look.org/](http://xfce-look.org/)
