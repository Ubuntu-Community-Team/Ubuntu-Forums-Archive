### Post by Foxmike on 2009-05-14
Actually, you just have to make a copy of a picture that you might want to modify and then edit it afterwards.  Digikam is very flexible in terms of how you (the user) want to manage your pictures, and it allows you to do pretty much everything with your pictures.  If you want to override them, then you can.  If you don't want to override them, you can as well.  Make the tour, this is the only kde application I run under my gnome desktop!
