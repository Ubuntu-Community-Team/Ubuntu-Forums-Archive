### Post by taurus on 2006-11-17
I assume you meant 128MB of RAM instead of 128K!  Even with 128MB, it is not a good idea to run Gnome or KDE.  I believe the smallest memory you need to run those two window managers is 256MB.  That's why your swap space is swapping like mad, causing your harddrive to spin like crazy.  So, I recommend you use xubuntu (running XFce4 window manager which is not bad) for your machine instead.
