### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-04-16
He made a virtual instance of Windows and had it automatically open on a certain desktop. There are a few different programs that virtualize OS's, including the one sold through Ubuntu's webshop, Parallels I think is it's name, or something similar. Then he most likely set it to open on a certain workspace, which I believe is done by right clicking on it in the notification bar and selecting which workspace it is to appear on. I may be wrong there, sticking programs to automatically open on a specific workspace is not something I have tinkered with, since I have not had a need to do so.

Word of warning, virtualized instances of windows have limits, chiefly they do not do 3d (games) very well, if at all.

Fluxbox is the name of a Windows manager, not a verb. It's sort of like a different version of XFCE. (I know that is not technically what it is, it is simply a good analogy.) Theoretically, if you can set it up that way in Fluxbox' compiz, it can be done in Gnome or KDE with compiz, since it's the same program.
