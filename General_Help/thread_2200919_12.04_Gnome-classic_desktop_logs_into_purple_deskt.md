### Post by neu5eeCh on 2014-01-21
So, via long-distance, I was helping another user install Ubuuntu 12.04.

He didn't/doesn't like the Unity desktop, so I had him log out and switch to Gnome Classic. When he logged back in, all he got was the purple-splotch wallpaper, but no panels. There was a notification that popped up saying that he was connected, then back to the purple splotch. Unfortunately, he couldn't log out and had set Ubuntu to auto-login. I had him go into TTY and we edited /etc/lightdm/lightdm.conf, but to no avail. 

This evening I received an email stating he solved the problem by removing Gnome:

sudo apt-get remove gnome-shell
sudo apt-get remove gnome-session-fallback

Autologin booted back into a functional Unity desktop. So, my question is, what happened? Why didn't editing Lightdm work and what would have caused gnome-classic to log into a blank desktop?
