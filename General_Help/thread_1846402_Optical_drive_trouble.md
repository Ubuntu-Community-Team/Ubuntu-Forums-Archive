### Post by pme 72 on 2011-10-03
Sorry, I did not realize you had a laptop. It is easy to replace a DVD drive on a desktop. When I was experiencing issues with my DVD burner I could boot into my XP partition and the burner still worked, at least for awhile.  

How old is the drive and can you still use it to create CD's? I read somewhere that the optics for burning DVD's are a lot more sensitive than required for burning a CD. Something about heat and that the rails for the burner were metal but were mounted in plastic. Over time they just get slightly out of kilter, just enough to create a problem with DVD's. Do not know how true that is but it seemed to make sense to me. My drive was almost 5 years old. Not saying you have the same issue though.

I mostly used mine for backups of data and when I started to have issues I switched to flash drives for backups. They are a lot more user friendly.
