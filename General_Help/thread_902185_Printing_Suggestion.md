### Post by Infinite Indefinite on 2008-10-13
Have you tried Xerox?  They have a number of multifunctional printers, (although they happen to be laser, rather than inkjet) and they have fairly good Linux support.

(Xerox printers which are Duplex tend to have the suffix D, and Network printers have the suffix N.  So, you might wish to consider everything from the Xerox Phaser 3428DN to the Phaser 7760DN.)  I use the former for personal printing, but it's not a multifunctional printer.
