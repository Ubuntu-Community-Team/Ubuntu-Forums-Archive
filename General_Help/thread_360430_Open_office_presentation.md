Well, the difference is really what comes with your install. "Normal" Ubuntu comes with Gnome as desktop environment and some accompanying programs, Kubuntu comes with KDE and it's programs.
You can easilly switch by just installing for example the kubuntu-desktop package if you are in the "normal" ubuntu and then have no difference in functionality except you'll have your old Gnome programs left to uninstall if you don't want them anymore.
