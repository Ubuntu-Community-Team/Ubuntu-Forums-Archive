### Post by FluffyElmo on 2008-08-22
Right click on the speaker icon in the top right of your screen and select "*Open Volume Control*". Make sure that there are sliders for Microphone and Lin-in are turned up and that they aren't muted. (If they are muted the little speaker below the slider will have a red line through it. Click the speaker to unmute)

On the "Option" tab you may have to select a different microphone using the drop down selector. Your system may default to the line in instead of the internal microphone. 

On the switches tab I had to select Microphone Boost to get any volume in Skype. I also selected Microphone Capture but you may not need this with an internal mic.

In each case, when you change a setting test using the Sound Recorder application located under the top left menu at *Applications->Sound & Video->Sound Recorder*. Select an input, choose record, stop then play back what you've said. If the microphone works in Sound Recorder it should work in Skype and it's easier to test using a local application.

Hope some of this helps...Good Luck!
