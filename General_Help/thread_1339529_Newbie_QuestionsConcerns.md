### Post by snowpine on 2009-11-27
1) Apple has chosen not to support Linux. Support for the iPod is hit or miss. You cannot easily use iTunes in Linux, purchase songs from their store, or play protected songs you've purchased from the store in the past. Linux has many great "native" music apps, like Rhythmbox and Amarok. 
2) You'll be able to use your full resolution once you enable the correct video driver. Try going to System->Admin->Hardware Drivers to see if the correct driver can be detected; if not, search for your video card here on the forums to get advice from other users.

1.  I have a 1st gen and 4th gen nano and both work great with Rythbox.  Just plug it in and transfer files (much like iTunes).  However, for some reason the album artwork does not transfer.  There are some fixes for this, but none have worked for me I don't care enough for that to put a bunch of work into it.  You can also try iTunes via PlayOnLinux (ie WINE) if you like.

2.  As for your resolution the best you could do is either isntall a driver from nVidia's website (not very easy) or try enabling the propeitary drivers from System/Administration/Hardware Drivers.
