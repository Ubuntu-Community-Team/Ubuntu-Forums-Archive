### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-01-26
Avidemux should be able to do it.  If you have problems with Avidemux, try Handbrake.  Both of them have several presets for various devices.  Usually I try Avidemux first, because it lets you keep audio and/or video streams that are compatible with the device without transcoding them and losing quality.  Handbrake reliably produces compatible files, but always transcodes both audio and video.
