### Post by howefield on 2010-01-10
Yes, you can use Ubuntu without installing to the hard disk.

Boot from the CD and the operating system will load into RAM and not touch your hard disk.

It will be slower than a real install, but most likely it will run OK.

You may need to go into your BIOS settings to enable the computer to boot from CD.

### Post by ibuclaw on 2010-01-10
Yes on both of your questions.

The Ubuntu installation disk is a LiveCD - meaning that you can boot into a volatile copy of the OS for testing. Volatile meaning that anything you may do - anything that you save, will be lost when you reboot.
