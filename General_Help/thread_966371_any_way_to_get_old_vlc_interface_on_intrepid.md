### Post by loz618 on 2008-11-11
I totally agree, the old interface was summed up the kind of elegant, functional simplicity I like about vlc and went perfectly with my xubuntu desktop.

Just to clarify, vlc was never gtk, they used to used a toolkit called wxwidgets before the switch to qt.

I had a look on the vlc boards, and found [this]("http://forum.videolan.org/viewtopic.php?f=15&t=50184&st=0&sk=t&sd=a"). No solutions really, but its interesting to hear from the admins there exactly why theres no going back.

One useful thing someone mentioned though was you can change that atrocity of a volume slider to something more ordinary if you go:

Preferences=>All=>Interface=>Main Interfaces=>Qt

and uncheck "Use non native buttons and volume slider"
