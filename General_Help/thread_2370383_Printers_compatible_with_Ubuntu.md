Brother supports Linux very well IME. Having said that, I just replaced a pretty new Brother MFC-J485DW with a Brother HL-3170CDW color laser printer. The inkjet's print head appears to have failed and I'm tired of messing with it. I do have a Brother MFC-6490CW that has been a trooper but it has one shortcoming. It cannot sit for more than about a week-10 days without printing something or the ink appears to dry out. I need to print a large color patch in order for the color to be correct. A difference between the MFC-J485DW and the MFC-6490CW is that the MFC-6490CW is labeled "Professional". Perhaps the machines intended for office/business use have higher quality more durable components. They also cost more. 

Lasers don't use liquid ink so don't dry out. And if you do use an inkjet frequently ink costs are pretty high. The Brother color laser is a refurb I got from Walmart for $158.50. A good inkjet costs nearly that much. About the only advantage for the inkjet over laser IMO is initial cost and photo printing.

Samsung, Epson & Canon also offer manufacturer support for linux. If you can't find linux support in your country's manufacturer web site, try another. For instance, Canon USA may not offer linux support for a machine but Canon Asia does. The model numbers may not be identical but they may be close and the driver will work. Somettimes one linux driver will support many or all models from a manufacturer. Samsung and HP are like that and I think Canon might be as well but I'm not certain about Canon.

Edit: One other consideration, you mentioned non-wireless. The cheaper inkjets often don't have ethernet, just USB & wifi. You can usually download documentation for a model of interest to check on connections available. The Brother color laser I just installed supports USB, ethernet and wifi. I can have USB and one of the networking connections active at the same time but not ethernet **and** wifi.

Does the one connection go through a wired or wireless router?

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2017-09-10
> **Jack_Shankle said:**
> I think wiffies computer has only 1 Ethernet connection and that is used for the internet.
Brothers's HL-3170CDW has an Ethernet port.

Using a printer with a network connection is very different from having it connected directly to the computer via USB.  The printer operates as a standalone device with its own IP address, and jobs are sent to it over the network.  A network printer is available to everyone on the network without any additional printer sharing.

My 3170 is connected directly to my network router.  I gave it a static IP address.  CUPS will detect this printer automatically.  I usually use the [http://localhost:631/](http://localhost:631/) interface for CUPS.  I installed the Brother printer app on my Android phone and can print from that as well as the computers on my network.
