sofasurfer, BoxNet is nice, but the free version only allows a single file size of 25MB, you must pay them for larger files.  I often need to send large PDFs to people (usually files for printing). If the receiver can't accept such large files through email I use a site called [MailBigFile]("http://www.mailbigfile.com/free/").  

It's a quick & dirty solution, but it works well in a pinch.  I think it allows files up to 512MB to be uploaded. It keeps the files for a few days then deletes them. When you upload a file, a download link is sent to the recipient by email and all they need to do is click the link and download the file. That's it. 

Gmail's attachment limit is 25MB now, so I rarely need to use MBF, but it's been a lifesaver on occasion. (and no, I have absolutely no connection to them at all, it's just a service that comes in handy sometimes.)
