### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-11-08
Reading your post, it is not clear whether or not you already have Ubuntu and whether or not you already have your Virgin MIFI. However, as you say, in theory it should work as just another Wifi spot.

The easiest way to find out is to try. If you don't already have Ubuntu, [download it]("http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download") from the Ubuntu website, burn it to a USB stick (instructions on the same page), try it (instructions also on the same page), and attempt to connect.

If you can connect, your answer is "yes". If you cannot, your answer is "maybe" (because an installed Ubuntu may have extra drivers that the USB does not.
