### Post by Impavidus on 2015-02-03
I hope you mean 14.04.1? And it won't be long before 14.04.2 is there, which will support later hardware than 14.04.1.

It's really about what's inside the laptop. Graphics and wifi mostly, other parts usually just work. Integrated Intel graphics are reliable, but not very powerful, nvidia is quite reliable but will need some tweaks, ati has a slightly worse reputation. For wifi, atheros seems quite reliable, broadcom less so. Most laptops will work with Ubuntu, but some need some tweaks. I have an HP laptop, a few years old, and everything worked out of the box, as usual with HPs. However, I read they can nowadays be problematic as theis EUFI doesn't like booting Linux (only Windows), but workarounds seem to exist. I heard that Lenovo is relatively reliable too, but never tried myself.
