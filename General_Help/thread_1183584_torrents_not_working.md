### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-06-10
The simpler explanation is that the torrent could be dead. This could be caused by lack of peers, tracker being down or no longer existent or torrent not being tracked anymore.

Try downloading a torrent of an Ubuntu distribution from [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/downloadmirrors#bt](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/downloadmirrors#bt) and see if it works. These torrent would be probably alive and kicking, so if you can't download them, then we can  proceed troubleshooting your issue.

### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-06-10
I forgot to mention that another possibility is that your proxy server is being blocked by most of the peers, so you can't connect. This would be a simple explanation too, because most experienced torrenters use proxy blocklists.

The proxy server could also block torrent connections, since it could be considered abuse of the network, specially if the proxy service is offered for free.
