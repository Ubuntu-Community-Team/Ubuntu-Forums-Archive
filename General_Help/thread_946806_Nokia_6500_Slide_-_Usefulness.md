### Post by black_whispers on 2008-10-13
Upon buying a Nokia 6500 Slide phone (on network three ¬¬) I found that I could not transfer files (such as images or mp3s) to or from my Ubuntu desktop without bluetooth (and I'm not paying for a bluetooth adapter)

The PC suite that comes bundled with the phone works on XP or Vista *groan* and multiple attempts with Wine failed to bring a result. (ie, the CD would not install to my Ubuntu despite installing on an XP computer).

I then attempted to find a PC suite on the net, and that failed too. A search on google brings back people looking for a simple no command-line way to transfer files.

So here is the simple fix for that.

------
First, make sure that you have a memory card inserted in the phone. If you didn't get one with your Phone, go and buy a cheap micro SD card (128 mb will do fine).
Put it in your phone. (Check your phone's documentation on where to put it).
Turn on your phone. You should have recieved a cable with your phone which has a USB-plug on one end and a small thing on the other which goes into the top of the phone.
Plug that into your computer and your phone.

Now, on your phone, there should be three options;
PC Suite
Printing & Media
Data storage.

Pick data storage.

It comes up with a loading bar sometimes. If it does not, then take the cable out of your phone and put it in again, and choose data storage again.
The loading bar says 'Transferring Data'. It then comes up with 'USB mode: Data storage'.
NOTE: Sometimes it says 'USB Mode: Data storage' before the transferring data'. Leave it about 10 seconds.

Look up at your screen and you should have a new window that has appeared. This is your phone's memory card.
Pull your files across to it.

On the left will be the name of your memory card (also at the top of the window). On this left bit, right click the name and click 'unmount' or else it will not save the files to the SD card. Give it a moment to save your things to the phone. 

On the phone Click done. Then click cancel on the phone because it comes up with the three options again. Unplug your cable and away you go.
