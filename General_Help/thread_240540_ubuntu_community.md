### Post by Jucato on 2006-08-21
Just a few points:

1. There is just the possibility that no one knows the answer to your problem. The forums and the IRC are more of "volunteer" support sources, that is, people giving help there are users themselves. They can only give help based on their own experience and knowledge. Not all possible situations can be experienced or covered. I have been on the receiving end of it, so I know how frustrating it feels. It doesn't, however, mean that the community is terrible. It just means that we don't have people who know the answer to your particular problem.

2. The Ubuntu "community" usually refers to the forums. The reason why most people find it "great" is because it doesn't have that all-high-and-mighty attitude. Otherwise, you would have already been flamed down for this thread.

3. You are very much welcome to try the dozens of available Linux distros. Search around for those that might solve your problem. Ubuntu is not the only Linux distro around. You can try others like Fedora Core, SUSE, Debian, MEPIS, Knoppix, etc. Most of them are free or have free versions. Just because you had a hard time with one distribution doesn't mean all the rest suck.

Just remember that people here in the forums help the best way they can, to the best of their abilities, to the best of their knowledge, based on their own experiences, and based on what they do know. We are not paid for this, but volunteer to try and help others. No one doesn't answer your questions because they intentionally don't want to. Also, there are other venues of support. The forums and IRC are just one of them. I'm not sure how active the mailing list is, but it's another option. You could also try Google and might find answers in the most unlikely places. This page shows you the different forms of support that you could get: [http://www.ubuntu.com/support](http://www.ubuntu.com/support)
