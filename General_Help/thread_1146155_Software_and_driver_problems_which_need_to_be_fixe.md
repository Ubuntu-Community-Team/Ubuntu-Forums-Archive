### Post by DeMus on 2009-05-02
Isn't it time for the people of Canonical to have serious talks to companies like Google, Intel, AMD and nVidia? I mean lots of problems Ubuntu users have come from faulty VGA drivers and crappy 3rd party software.
How often do you see a thread here about not being able to install the right video driver, or to use 3D, or to get Google Earth working? Every day more than once.

Okay, I understand it, there are less Linux users than Windows users but this will always stay like that without good software and good working drivers which are easy to install. Less users means less interesting for the big companies.

A company like Google who, to say it mildly, is not so keen of Microsoft (and vice versa) should be more interested in Linux. Why is it then so hard to get Google-earth working? Why isn't it just as easy as in Windows?

Why do so many people complain about their VGA drivers, stick to 800*600 resolution, can't get 3D working? Why not make an installation program which is easy to start and which performs all the necessary things to have the new driver installed, in a way that everything is working? Is this so hard to do? Of course not. People just don't want to do it. Why not? Because it is Linux. Because it is open and free software.

Google-earth is free, drivers are free. Those companies don't loose any income when more and more people start using Linux.
What other reason can there be for not helping the Linux community? Is it because Microsoft is behind all of this? Are they afraid they will be excluded from the Windows software? This can't be true, can it?

All I ask is let Linux people, and for us it is Canonical, talk to the big companies and let them improve the software so many people around the world love so much.
