### Post by TheFu on 2023-05-13
If you don't pay for a service, then **YOU are the product being sold.**  Almost all free VPNs are selling your data ... or capturing it for background information about YOUR habits (many govts do this).  Using a free VPN has fair reaching impacts on your life.

Whether you need to use an outbound VPN or not is something only you can determine.  This is very different from whether you should run your own VPN server to allow remote access to your home systems.  Many people should be using a home VPN to protect their own data stored inside their home LANs.  

There are a few threads here discussing VPNs.  Please go read those to gain a deeper understanding.  There are many pros and many cons to using VPN for outbound connections. There are many subtle issues even if you decide to use one.

I'd never use a free VPN and of the paid VPNs, I'd look for one that is proven to not retain logs, ensure my connection does what is needed for short-term logs to be removed, at least daily. I'd try to ensure that I was paying enough that my data didn't become part of the products being sold.  I'd also research the ties to govts I don't want to gain access to that data so those are avoided.  Mainly, it just means avoiding govts that don't respect the rule of law.  To determine that, I'd look for where companies refuse to do what a govt requests, has legal protections from govt overreach and that a number of companies have actually won in court to refuse those govt requests.  Of course, no govt is perfect.

BTW, when I pay for VPN service, I do it anonymously.  Anything less breaks the chain I'm trying to avoid.  For example, I never use this computer with the VPN.  I never visit these forums and post with the VPN active. I never login to any accounts I've ever used without a VPN while the VPN is active.  Preventing the breadcrumbs from each different use being connected is hard.  In my mind, I have a "VPN mental mode" when using an external VPN.

OTOH, when I'm away from home, connecting to my LAN over the VPN server that I'm running, I'll use all my normal accounts.
