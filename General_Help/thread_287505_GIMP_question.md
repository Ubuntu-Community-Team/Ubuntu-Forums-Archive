### Post by SendDerek on 2006-10-28
I think I understand what it is you're asking and I think I have the answer...

There is software out there that I have experimented with before to enlarge photos with minimal quality loss, but it's not what you would think.

It's called interpolation software and it can be found pretty easily.  The most advanced method of this enlarging is called S-Spline Interpolation.

Here is one company who has some good software (probably not free though):
[http://www.benvista.com/main/content/content.php](http://www.benvista.com/main/content/content.php)

Here is a website that has good content, but a very crappy layout:
[http://www.americaswonderlands.com/digital...terpolation.htm](http://www.americaswonderlands.com/digital_photo_interpolation.htm)

I hope this will help you in your quest.  Unfortunatly, you're not going to be able to get the absolute best quality from enlarging something.  It's never going to be as good as the original.
