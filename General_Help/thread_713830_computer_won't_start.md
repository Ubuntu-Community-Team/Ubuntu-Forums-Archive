### Post by sillyxone on 2008-03-03
well, back to troubleshooting basic: strip it down to barebone (mainboard, CPU, video, RAM), see if it can boot up. If it's OK, keep plugging in other components, one at a time. If it's couldn't stay on with just those four basic components, you can try each component on a good computer to isolate the problem.

oh, and by barebone, I mean just those four, don't even plugin mouse or keyboard, just try to see if it will stay on. Also, the power supply is one of the suspect as well.
