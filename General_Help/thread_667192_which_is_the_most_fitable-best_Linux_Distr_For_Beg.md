### Post by Circus-Killer on 2008-01-14
well, surprise surprise, i'm also recommending ubuntu.

but let me take it a step further.
when i first got into linux (around 1999), there was no ubuntu. the first distro i tried was red hat. that was just a terrible place to start.

next i tried mandrake (now known as mandriva). aimed at new users, and at the beginning very pleasing. but as soon as you run into a problem, it can cause massive headaches.

after that i went to gentoo. definately not for new users, and for anyone who cant stand going through hundreds of pages of howtos and manuals. but its biggest advantage is that it teaches you a lot about linux.

after that came ubuntu. when i first tried ubuntu i was very unempressed. it was so easy to install with about 2% of the install options that you would normally get. at first i didnt like this. it gave me the impression that it was neither robust nor very configurable. however after a while of fiddling, i realise how wrong i was.

ubuntu is great because it is easy to setup for a new user. you learn at your own pace. and whilst the learning curve is far more gentler, it still leaves room for customization and development. so i recommend ubuntu not because this is an ubuntu forum, but because i truly believe ubuntu is the best distro to start on.

### Post by balaknair on 2008-01-14
If you've never used Linux before, I'd recommend PClinuxOS or Mint to try out and decide if Linux is for you. If you want to continue using Linux then you can always switch to Ubuntu. The best thing about Ubuntu isn't the packages that come with it, it's these forums. I suggest PClinuxOS and Mint because they offer multimedia support out of the box unlike Ubuntu where you have to enable restricted drivers and codecs- it's a very simple step, but I've found it can still confuse some first time users(speaking from my experience with people like my brother and some friends to whom I've recommended Linux).
I've tried different 'easy' distros(Sabayon, Mandriva 2008, Fedora 8, OpenSuSe 10.x, Mint Cassandra, and PClinuxOS before coming back to Ubuntu) and I think the one best suited for someone with absolutely no exposure to Linux is PCLinuxOS. It has it's limitations but it's still a great easy to use distro.
That said I think once you've gotten used to the Linux way of doing things, you might want to switch to Ubuntu. It's the best Linux distro(in my opinion) with the best ethic out there right now. And yeah, the killer feature that no other distro can claim- ubuntuforums. To be honest that's what made me stick with Ubuntu in the end. The Ubuntu community is something special. 
As I said I've already tried other distros, and one that worked for me was Ubuntu(Kubuntu 7.04 actually). I suggest you do the same- spend a little time trying out various distros before deciding on what works for you. OS choice is a matter of taste and convenience, not a religion. All distros have failings and strengths. In the end the most important is what works for you. Regardless of what distro you choose, I am sure if you show up here at ubuntuforums, we'll always be glad to help in any way possible
Wishing you a happy Linux experience
