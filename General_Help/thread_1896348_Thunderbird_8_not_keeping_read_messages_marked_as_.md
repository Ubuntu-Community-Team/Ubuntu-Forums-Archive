Hello all,

I've been using the gmane.org mail2news gateway to read various mailing lists via Thunderbird for a few years now.  Generally speaking, it works slick.  I can read/post to lists like they were newsgroups, without the hassle of filtering emails to keep them from cluttering my mail box or worrying about exceeding my mail storage.

Basically... I have Thunderbird (v.8.0, on Ubuntu 11.10 if it matters) configured to connect to the server, on new groups ask me before downloading more than 500 message headers, and by default mark all the rest as read.  When I browse thru the tree of subscribed groups, I have them set to only display threads with unread messages, and I mark each group as read before moving on to the next one.  The account settings are configured to check for new messages on startup, and at a regular interval thereafter as long as I have the program open.  Same thing I've been doing for years.

Lately I've been experiencing some odd behavior.  Essentially when I go to check for new messages it suddenly shows groups (not all, just some) that were previously all marked read, as suddenly having any where from dozens to hundreds of new messages.  Invariably, those turnout to be the same ones I marked read last time... and the time before... and the time before that... all the way back to whatever limit I have on the account (i.e. keep the last x hundred messages or delete anything over 30 days old).  I mark everything read *again*, and move on.  Next group may, or may not exhibit the same behavior.  The next day... everything behaves normally and only truly 'new' messages show up.  A couple days later... hundreds of 'new' old messages again.

Any ideas on what may be causing this?  How do I track down whether its a gmane thing or a thunderbird thing or something else?
