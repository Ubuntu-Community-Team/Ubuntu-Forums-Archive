### Post by Carbonbasedmistake on 2006-05-09
I have two CDs - one from Nara Jones and one by the Canadian band "The Tea Party", "Seven Circles". Both are from EMI Records. 
Both Cd's are protected by "Copy Control" technology which is basically where they take a few of the tracks and mark them as data tracks with size in megabytes rather than as audio files with time in seconds. 
To a stupid CD player such as one of the many bookshelf  or small carry about players the player will just crusie on through and play each track as an audio track even if it is miss-labeled as a data track. The PC player shipped on the CD is only good for WIndows. 
My home deck is a Toshiba and my PC runs Linux, neither of which will play the CDs. I have sent two e-mails to EMI ( using their drop down e-mail address for "Copy Control Technology") and have received two auto replys and no human replys. Simply put EMI doesn't care. I only wanted to hear the music that I paid for and to rip for my own personal pleasure on my IAudio player. But no... basically I bought 16 dollar coasters. 
From now on I'll search Google for 'Copy controlled" CD listings and won't ever even think of buying one that has this poor excuse for technolgy on the tracks.'
