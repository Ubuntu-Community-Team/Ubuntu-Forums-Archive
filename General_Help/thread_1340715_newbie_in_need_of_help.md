### Post by ed-koala on 2009-11-28
Ok, some advice for new users.

Try the Live CD first before installing.  Saves countless headaches if some piece of hardware isn't gonna work right from the start.

Start with the most recent Long Tern Service Release, **not the most recent release!**.  This can't be stressed enough, and isn't really obvious to new users.  Version 8.04 is the most recent LTS (long term service) release, and is very stable and will probably give you the least troubles.  You can upgrade later once you get your feet wet.

Research your PC system first.  Know what type of hardware you have, and check the forums for known issues.  This alone will prevent 100 suicides a year (just kidding, it will save you a ton of potential trouble though).

Just doing these 3 things will make new user experiences better, and help Ubuntu take over the world!
