### Post by idi0tf0wl on 2010-12-05
I've had this problem before... actually, what I found worked really well was using the Startup Disk Creator in System->Administration. If your system sees the disk, you should be able to (mis)use that little utility to wipe the drive clean, the successful completion of which will result in a new Nautilus window popping up to allow you access to your new drive.

Not exactly the 1337est way to do it, but it worked for me...
