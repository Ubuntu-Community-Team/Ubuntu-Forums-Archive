### Post by Gemnoc on 2011-03-11
The problem is, it is against the EULA to resell most CAD software. There was a lawsuit between Autodesk and a guy who sold used AutoCAD copies on eBay. The guy won first, then the ruling was reversed on appeal.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vernor_v._Autodesk,_Inc](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vernor_v._Autodesk,_Inc).

The heart of the matter is: you don't buy neither own software. You buy a licence allowing you to use it. You have very limited rights over it.

And BTW, the DWG file format only changes every 3 releases. Draftsight for Linux just came out this week. Functionality-wise, it compares to AutoCAD LT (no ACIS modeling), supports DWG 2010 (which means it will be compatible up to AutoCAD 2012), and it is free for personal and commercial use.
