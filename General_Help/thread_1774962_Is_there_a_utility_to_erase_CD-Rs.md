### Post by mcduck on 2011-06-06
Pretty much every individual component in your computer has a firmware. Actually pretty much every electronics device has one. They can usually be flashed to the devices, but finding one that would do want you want is unlikely.

It's possible, for example, to replace the firmware of some DVD drives with one that removes region codes from movies. And some graphics cards can be flashed with a firmware that allows them to use professional drivers. The problem is that backwards engineering a devices firmware is complicated, and changing it or making a new one is even harder. I doubt anybody would bother making one for a task that's better handled with a pair of kitchen scissors..
