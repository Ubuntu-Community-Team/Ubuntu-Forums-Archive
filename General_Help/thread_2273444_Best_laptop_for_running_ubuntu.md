### Post by pmi2 on 2015-04-13
There is a website that may have all the answers you can wish for laptop compatibility with Linux... "Notebook Review", with a section called "Linux Compatibility".  The link below should take you to it (but seems to be offline at times, or not responding every time)

[http://forum.notebookreview.com/forums/linux-compatibility-and-software.1029/](http://forum.notebookreview.com/forums/linux-compatibility-and-software.1029/)

I found a lot of good information on laptops running different versions of Linux there.

At least in the US, one can usually buy professionally refurbished versions of laptops coming off business leases after about 4 years.  

So for example, a tested HP Elitebook with an i5 processor and 4Gb of RAM may cost about $300 +/- $50, depending on disk space and cosmetic condition.  +$50 for an i7 quad core processor. I mention the Elitebook b/c I believe it was fairly popular in the EU also, at round $1000 when it was new.

One advantage of getting a laptop sold at the end of a business lease, is that at the same time, spare parts like LCDs and keyboards, latches, hinges, top covers, DVD drives etc, in other words all normal wear parts, are also available and sold off at a discount.  So even if you happen to get a machine where something is amiss, at least for the near future, spare parts will be plentiful.
