### Post by Garyu on 2007-06-27
Well, there is always the popularity contest in the repo's. But then not all users are reporting to that. And even so, I don't know the numbers.

Downloads of .iso's from mirrors would be misleading as at least I download one copy and then use it on 4+ machines. I suspect many people do this. The same problem is true for shipit CD's.

And then you have the issue of "what is an ubuntu user"? For instance, I have two stationary computers and one laptop. I run dapper drake on one computer, feisty fawn i386 AND x64 on the other one, and on the laptop I got feisty and windows XP. So do you count me as one user or three? In my parents house there are two computers with feisty only on one and fiesty + XP on the other. These two computers are used interchangeably by my mother and father, but my two brothers and my sister also use them on occasion. So is that two or five users?

What I am saying is: trying to figure out how many users there are is not an easy task. I doubt you will ever get a correct figure.
