### Post by dbott67 on 2006-10-23
> **Tobster said:**
> ...I started to wonder if they either use GNOME or KDE how can they really differ.


Gnome & KDE are both desktop environments.  You are free to use either desktop environment on any distribution, as well as XFCE or other Windows Managers such as fluxbox/blackbox, enlightenment or any one of a dozen or more choices.

The DE/WM gives a familiar user interface for the user, however, the underlying distribution is where the differences are.  A good analogy would be to compare automobiles.  On the surface, they all have 4 wheels and get you from point A to point B, but upon closer inspection some models may be better at performing certain tasks (V8 vs. 4cyl; van vs. compact; truck vs. car; etc.)

Hope this helps.

### Post by qamelian on 2006-10-23
> **Tobster said:**
> GNOME is created by Novell so why does SUSE use KDE?
SuSE wan't always owned by Novell. Before their acquisition by Novell, SuSE was predominantly a KDE-based distro. Although you could install either GNOME or KDE (or WindowMaker or AfterStep or Enlightenment, etc.), KDE was the environment that they spent the most effort on. KDE tended to be kept more current and more polished. 

Since being aquired by Novell, SuSE seems to expend almost equal effort on both of the "big 2" desktop environments.
