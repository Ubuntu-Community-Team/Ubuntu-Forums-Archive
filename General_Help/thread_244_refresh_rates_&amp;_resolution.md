### Post by FLeiXiuS on 2004-10-12
If your running the live CD, this won't be possible.  I would suggest moving to installing Ubuntu onto your Hard Drive.  Its fun and much more satisifying. :-)

Edit your XFree86 configuration file.
```
sudo $EDITOR /etc/X11/XF86Config-4
```

Add the HorizSync / VertRefresh to your Monitor Section
```
Section &quot;Monitor&quot;
        HorizSync       39-75 
        VertRefresh     50-70 
EndSection
```

To add a custom resolution, browse again for the Section Screen with a SubSection Display and manipulate the Modes. Your depth may vary depending on your graphics card.  Mine was 24bit, but you can change it to whatever you need.

```
SubSection &quot;Display&quot;
                Depth           24
                Modes           &quot;1400x1050&quot; &quot;1024x768&quot; &quot;800x600&quot; &quot;640x480&quot;
EndSubSection
```

That should do it for you, any questions let me know.

	FontPath	"/usr/share/X11/fonts/CID"
