### Post by Zorael on 2008-06-05
Sadly, no software can stop social engineering, so the more secure your OS the less (potential) damage can be wrought by malware. That said, malware with root permissions could botch stuff up something fierce. But at least most/all of that would be salvageable, provided you backed up your personal files, whereas in Windows you'd get a nice, black screen telling you to bust out that CD and reinstall.

(chroot, new installation, restore home directory, fetch old apps from repositories, learn lesson not to trust sites that tell you to "Install this codec to play everything!", or warn you about "You're the millionth visitor, install this to claim your prize!", ..., profit)
