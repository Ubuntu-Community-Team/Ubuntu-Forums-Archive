### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-10-02
When the AT footprint came out it had "on board" video and sound but they suked SOOOOO badly, plug in cards became the norm.  As the ATX footprint developed, so did the chip technology and on board sound and video along with 10/100 networking and eventually now wireless networking are all BACK on the boards.  And the quality for all but a hard core gamer or audio/video fanatic (such as I) is quite sufficient. (older ATX motherboards still did not have on board items.  It was a slow return process as they added the on board items (chips) with each new board update/release.

BUT on board has two major drawbacks.  They use both on board ram and processor space.  Cards, on the other hand, come equipped with their own processors and memory.  Thus freeing up the on board stuff for other progams!

### Post by enchesss on 2008-10-02
Some mobos annoyingly DO NOT have on board VGA. Your best bet is to in the meanwhile search for a cheap PCI VGA card. You should be able to get one for free because they are so old. I have found several in old computers being thrown out and there have even been some old AGP cards thrown out too. These cards have been handy in testing other computers without on board vga. Depending on the type of slot you have (e.g. PCI or PCI-e) you could even pick up a second hand card or a new one from the shop pretty cheaply. Just explain to them what is happening and then use it in the future as a spare when you get you real card back.
