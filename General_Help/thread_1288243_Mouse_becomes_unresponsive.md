### Post by adiosgang on 2010-07-31
I realize this is an old thread, but I was having a very similar problem and I thought I'd post my solution for the next person that google directs here.

My MX Revolution mouse would be come unresponsive or sluggish randomly over the past two years.  Every time I upgraded Ubuntu, I figured the problem would fix itself, but it kept persisting.  I did some sleuthing, but could never come up with anything.  And it kept happening across 8.04, 8.10, 9.04, and 10.04, so I was pretty much stumped.  Ignoring the mouse for a bit or just walking away from the computer usually fixed it and next time I sat down it was working.  Well today it was happening again and it was just about as bad as its ever been, so after running through my usual list of things to try (power off computer, power off mouse, move the usb dongle, etc) I turned the mouse over in my hand and noticed something in front of the laser.  I have 3 cats and there hair is everywhere.  A cat hair had found its way into the little cavity in front of the laser and was bisecting the laser.  I think it must have been in there for the past two years and whenever the mouse became unresponsive it was because of the hair.  It would finally start working again when the hair shifted from me being mad and slamming down the mouse or just leaving in frustration.  I pulled the hair out with a pair of tweezers and its been working great since!
