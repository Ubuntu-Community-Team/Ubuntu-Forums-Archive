### Post by meng on 2007-01-14
I'm not familiar with those applications and their performance under wine (or Crossover Office for that matter). However, I would say that if he's not open to considering alternatives, then Linux really isn't for him. Wine does not replicate WIndows perfectly, and even minor glitches with certain applications may frustrate him.

One might even ask, if his mind is set on using these applications, then why does he wish to change to Linux? I ask this because if his motivation is just "security" or "stability" and nothing else then Windows can be made secure and stable too (with good habits). If he's not into experimenting and enjoying open-source software, then the Linux experience may not appeal to him.
