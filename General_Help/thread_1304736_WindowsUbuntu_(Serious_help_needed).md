### Post by tuahaa on 2009-10-29
TO get rid of Ubuntu you need to format your whole drive that contains it. If you still have the live cd of Ubuntu, you just need to boot into the disc and run ubuntu off the live CD. There is an app called Gparted (or shown as Partition editor in menu sometimes). You can right click and format. Try formatting to NTFS. That should erase all your stuff on the drive (or the separate partition if you selected that). Thats how you get rid of Ubuntu but I'm not sure about your problem with Windows; try cleaning the disk wit a dry cloth- that helped me :D

### Post by carml on 2009-10-29
If you downloaded a Desktop version of the .iso,just insert the cd into your drive and boot,choose Try Ubuntu and when you get to the Desktop,go to System->Administration->Partition editor,there you'll be able to format again your hard disk.
You'll get a window similar to the one attached,right click to unmount the partitions you see (the ones with a little key at left),then choose format as fat32 and once you've done turn-off your pc then use your disk of Windows and it should install now.
Just ask if you need more help or explanations and someone will answer :)
