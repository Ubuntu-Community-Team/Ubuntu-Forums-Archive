### Post by 2hot6ft2 on 2010-02-17
If the real issue is drive space why not get a bigger drive and clone the one that's in it now and replace it.

You get an external 2.5" drive plug it in and clone the internal drive to it.
Remove the external from it's case and swap it out with the internal one and boot it up, then just resize the partitions or create an additional one for file storage.

Put the internal drive in the external case and you can either use it as a backup or reformat it and you have an external for storage.

It takes a while to clone a drive and the amount of time would depend on the original internal drives size, the speed of the laptop, RAM and such. It's easy to clone a drive with linux using dd. Start it in the Evening and look for it to finish the next morning. If you're interested I can post the info I saved when I did mine.

### Post by OliW on 2010-02-17
Before you buy anything in the way of hardware, concentrate on your business. Not having access to your CRM will hurt your workflow.

If you're serious about dumping Windows, you're going to need to find a replacement for ACT. Rather than looking for an open source replacement (one might exist), I would personally look to the internet to help you here.

There are dozens, if not hundreds of online CRM packages. I'll leave you in Google's hands but with a little looking around, I'm fairly confident you should be able to find a replacement.

After that, it seems like you're free to do what you like. You can either keep the current computer and use something like gparted to remove/shrink the XP parition and expand your Ubuntu partition, or look to a company that supplies no-OS or Linux-bearing machines.
