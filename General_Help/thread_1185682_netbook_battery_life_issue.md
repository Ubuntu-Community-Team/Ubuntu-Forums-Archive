### Post by Chronon on 2009-06-20
I use Eee-Control to turn off WiFi, card reader, camera, etc. when I am not using them.  This also has Performance, Normal or Powersave settings for setting the FSB and CPU voltages.  [http://greg.geekmind.org/eee-control/#features](http://greg.geekmind.org/eee-control/#features)

Maybe there's something similar for your hardware.

I haven't done any real battery benchmarks so I don't know how much battery life has improved, nor how it stacks up against battery life while running XP.  Honestly, I haven't used it enough to drain the battery in XP yet.

*Powertop* does help a little.

Remember you're comparing Microsoft's 2001 operating system to Canonical's latest. In terms of system resource usage there's not really a surprise.

I sidestepped the issue by buying a big 9 cell battery off eBay. They're pretty cheap and they do the job marvellously. Alternatively, you could put Acer's Linpus on there, which has the same battery life as Windows XP (that's what my netbook shipped with).
