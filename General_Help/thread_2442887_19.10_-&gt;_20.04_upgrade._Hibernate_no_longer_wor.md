### Post by GhX6GZMB on 2020-05-08
I used to have a wonderfully working Hibernate function in my 19.10 installation: menu, lid etc. could invoke hibernation.

After upgrading to 20.04, this no longer works, and it must be a change in the LXQt environment, as systemctl hibernate works.

Setting up Hibernate is a bit complicated and involves modifying:
/etc/fstab
/etc/default/grub
/etc/initramfs-tools/conf.d/resume
/etc/polkit-1/localauthority/50-local.d/com.ubuntu.enable-hibernate.pkla

This has all been done.
I suspect that a new blocking mechanism has been included in 20.04 (I'm aware that the Lubuntu team discourages Hibernate, but I need it in certain situations).

Does anyone have an idea where to look?

Thank You.


PS: please do not respond telling me to use Suspend instead. I'm totally aware of what I need.

---

### Post by GhX6GZMB on 2020-05-08
SOLVED! Enabling the lid switch suddenly made both Menu and Lid work. I'm completely happy, sorry for wasting your time.
