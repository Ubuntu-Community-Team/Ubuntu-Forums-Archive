It is mainly a matter of taste, especially what concerns Ubuntu (Gnome) and Kubuntu (KDE). Xubuntu's Xfce desktop has the advantage of being less resource-hungry, i.e. good for low end machines (say less than 256 of ram). ### Post by notwen on 2009-02-26
I'd recommend letting them do their daily tasks using a LiveCD of each for a week or so and ask them which they prefer.  Keep in mind that while in a LiveCD environment, the system will be running completely off memory and will likely be noticeably slower than if you were running the OS off of the HDD.  By doign this you can actually let them decide which DE they prefer between Gnome(Ubuntu), KDE(Kubuntu) or XFCE(Xubuntu).  

That said Xubuntu will use the least amount of memory, while Kubuntu would likely use the most.  Ubuntu would likely be right behind Xubuntu, their speed differences might not even be noticeable.
