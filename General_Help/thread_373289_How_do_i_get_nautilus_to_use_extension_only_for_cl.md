### Post by GeirG on 2007-03-01
You are of course entitled to you opinion regarding what is wasteful and complicating.
I do however think that you will find there is a lot of people who find it outright ridiculous to deduct a files content based on whatever name somebody has given it. 
Some people are likely to agree with you though , especially those coming from an MS Windows environment.
I assume you are quite new to the world of GNU/Linux as you are just discovering this little piece of automagic file type recognition, but don't you think it is a bit rash of you to condemn functionality that obviously has been used for years by tons of people as generally wasteful and complicating without finding out why/how it actually works?
Did you consider for even a second that somebody might have decided that this for some reason is a good way to do it?

You are of course free to disagree, and to feel it is complicating things, and to believe it is wasting lot of resources, but I strongly suggest a slightly more open-mindedness when exploring your new OS. Not everything is done as in it is in Windows, and although you might very well not approve of it, it is likely to be done so for a reason.

BTW, I don't really know if there is a good way to make Nautilus to use the extension as you describe.
