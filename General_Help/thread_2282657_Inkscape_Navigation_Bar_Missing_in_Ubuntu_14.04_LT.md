### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2015-06-16
It is probably the global menu that confuses you. The menu is on the top 'panel' of the screen. You can restore it to the applications' window by clicking the gear icon on the top right, go to System Settings > Appearance > Behavior , go down to Show the menu for a window  and check "in the window's title bar" The global menu has some advantages for a small screen but IMO it doesn't make a lot of sense for a large one.

### Post by mcduck on 2015-06-16
I'm not sure which panel you refer to as "navigation bar", but just go to View->Show/Hide and enable which ever panels you want. Might be the Commands Bar since that's the one that has icons for open/save, zoom etc...

edit. Actually, you do have the navigation bar there (it's just older version of Inkscape than what I have so it's still on top instead of right side, and has different icon theme. So I think monkeybrain20122 is right in assuming you are missing the menu bar instead? You'll find it in your top panel when you hover the mouse over the Inkscape name.
