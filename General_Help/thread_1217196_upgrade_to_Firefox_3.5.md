### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-07-19
> **geovino said:**
> a couple weeks ago I installed Firefox 3.5 as shiretoko. today the upgrades have the real Firefox 3.5 and when it upgraded it left Firefox as 3.0.11. How can I replace the Shiretoko version with the regular version?

Today's update just updates *Shiretoko 3.5* to *Shiretoko 3.5.1*. There is no such a thing as "real" *Firefox 3.5* in the repositories because *Shiretoko 3.5* **is** *Firefox 3.5* **without the official branding** (logo and name). The "real" version (with orange logo and Firefox name) won't be available until Ubuntu Karmic Koala is released.

If the logo and name bothers you so much, there are plenty of threads and posts giving you alternatives, but why would you want to change it anyway? The software IS THE SAME.

If you want alternative methods that install Firefox with official branding, then...

> The 4 most popular methods of installation of Firefox 3.5 (Psychocats, Ubuntuzilla, Repository, Shiretoko/Swiftfox) are available in the "Install Other Versions" section of the [**[COLOR="DarkRed"]Firefox optimization and troubleshooting thread[/COLOR]**](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1193567). 

There is also a detailed explanation of each installation method provided, comparing the most important differences. I personally tested all of them and they work like a charm, but I recommend methods 1 (Psychocats) and 2 (Ubuntuzilla).
