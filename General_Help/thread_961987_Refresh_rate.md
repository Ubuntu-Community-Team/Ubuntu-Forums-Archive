### Post by nitrousoxide82 on 2008-10-28
Since you state you're having problems with low refresh rate, I am assuming you have a CRT monitor. LCDs generally don't have this problem due to the way they work: its light source is constantly on, and the panel modulates the amount of that light that passes through it toward the eyes. 
I'm also guessing that for some reason the horizontal/vertical frequency ranges for your monitor couldn't be read automatically, so you may have to set them manually in [font="Courier New"]/etc/X11/xorg.conf[/font]. Please post an excerpt of your [font="Courier New"]/var/log/Xorg.0.log[/font] file (it is created upon initialization of the graphical interface) mentioning anything "EDID". [font="Courier New"](II) fglrx(0): Ranges: V min: 56 V max: 75 Hz, H min: 30 H max: 71 kHz, PixClock max 110 MHz [/font]
This is the information we're interested in. If this doesn't match your monitor's specs, or for some reason the EDID data can't be read, you'll have to enter the information manually on [font="Courier New"]/etc/X11/xorg.conf[/font].

To do so, open the file as root with a text editor, look for the "Monitor" section, and add lines for HorizSync and VertRefresh. It'll look like this:
```

Section "Monitor"
    Identifier "Configured Monitor"
    HorizSync 30-71        # add this line
    VertRefresh 50-160    # add this line
    # rest of "Monitor" section goes here
EndSection

```

HorizSync range is in kHz and VertRefresh range is in Hz. The values stated here are for an old 17" CRT I had - capable of 1280x1024 at 68 Hz. Find the correct values for your monitor, usually the manual has that information. If only the maximum ratings are given in the manual, 30 kHz for HorizSync and 50 Hz for VertRefresh are safe minimum values.

### Post by nitrousoxide82 on 2008-10-29
I looked up some info on your monitor, but couldn't find this specific detail. As far as I can tell from the Xorg log, the NVIDIA driver is able to read your monitor info, and is trying to set 1600x1200 at 60 Hz. It may be then that the timings for that mode are not correctly set. There is a small utility called "gtf" which will generate timings based on something called a "General Timing Formula". These usually work with most recent (manufactured circa 1998, or later) monitors. Here's the result of this utility for a 1600x1200 mode at 60 Hz:

```

  # 1600x1200 @ 60.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 74.52 kHz; pclk: 160.96 MHz
  Modeline "1600x1200_60.00"  160.96  1600 1704 1880 2160  1200 1201 1204 1242  -HSync +Vsync

```

You can see by the comment it auto-generates that the horizontal frequency is 74.5 kHz (which I think is definitely in range for a monitor that supports 1600x1200). Try pasting this within the "Monitor" section of your [font="Courier New"]/etc/X11/xorg.conf[/font], starting X again and see what happens.

### Post by nitrousoxide82 on 2008-10-31
Try generating a modeline for 75 Hz yourself. If you are sure your monitor will support it, then open a terminal and type
[font="Courier New"]gtf 1600 1200 75[/font]
and paste the result into xorg.conf at the "Monitor" section. Then restart X and see what happens. 

In case X starts and the monitor goes blank (or shows a "Frequency out of range" message like my old Philips 17" did), press Ctrl-Alt-F1 (to take you into the text-mode console), log in, edit xorg.conf, comment the code you just pasted out, press Ctrl-Alt-F7 to go back to X and then press Ctrl-Alt-Backspace to restart it.
