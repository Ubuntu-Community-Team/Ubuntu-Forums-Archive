Yeah, you're probably out of luck if there is special software involved in connecting to your ISP.  I wouldn't say that Ubuntu is pointless without the internet (OpenOffice can still be used, many multimedia applications are still functional, you'd just have to dual boot to check stuff online) but many things suddenly become more complicated.  Updating and adding new programs, fixing simple stuff, all made difficult by lack of a connection.  

Don't let that stop you from using your computer the way you want to though - If you really like Ubuntu, you might consider a change of ISPs.  However, getting what you want, simply and "sanely" is important too.
