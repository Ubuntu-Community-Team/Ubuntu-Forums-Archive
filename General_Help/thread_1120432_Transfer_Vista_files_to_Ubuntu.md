***Getting your personal files over to Ubuntu from Vista:***
If you have it dual-booting all you need to do it boot into Ubuntu and once at the desktop click on "Places" and select the other partition from the list. It will prompt you for your password, enter it, and then you have your Vista partition mounted. Now just simply browse to your personal files and copy them to your home folder on Ubuntu.


[B][I]
Allocating more space to your Ubuntu installation:[/I][/B]
Not sure if this is what you're asking but...If your Vista partition is working fine and you want to keep it but just resize it to allocate more space to Ubuntu then simply boot up your Ubuntu Live CD and open up GParted (System > Administration > Partition Editor) then just resize your Vista Partition and "Grow" the Ubuntu one.
