### Post by TheFu on 2016-04-17
You need to have excellent, versioned, backups whenever encryption is used.
The location of the failure on the physical media matters, but often enough, those failures happen in very critical, much used, locations.

Yep.  You want excellent **versioned **backups.  There are many other reasons that backups are useful, not just failed hardware.  Encrypted file system corruption, viruses, worms, stupid-user mistakes ... and about 100 others.

I should mention that whole disk encryption is better than just HOME directory encryption for both system security and performance.  Oddly, whole drive encryption also makes backups easier than HOME directory encryption, at least IMHO.
