### Post by efflandt on 2010-11-09
I do not believe that a smart grid like that is implemented yet, unless you have a demand meter that charges you a variable rate for electricity based upon system demand at different times.  But I do not have one of those, so not sure if it connects to a phone line (my water meter is read by phone).

There is powerline networking locally.  Netgear and Linksys have such devices to extend ethernet by simply plugging modules into wall outlets where you need them.  And of course X10 switching devices that can be controlled through the powerline have been around for many years, but that is probably a simple low frequency.

I doubt if your refrigerator can phone home though the power grid (yet).  Our president wants a smart grid, so when you plug in your electric car, it can charge your car batteries while subtracting a charge from your wallet.
