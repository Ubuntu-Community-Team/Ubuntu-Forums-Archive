### Post by lorap on 2005-04-28
hi buddy!
there're 2 i use myself: VLC and XMMS.
vlc can play any kind of files, i mean video and audio.
xmms renders audio files and looks cool.
to install them you've to do the next:
system-->configuration-->synaptic package manager
once you get a synaptic window open go to the:
settings-->repositories
then once you get the repositories window open proceed with this:
press settins(or advanced i'm not currently in the linux, but it's at bottom left corner) button then mark all checkboxes possible then press ok. after the process completes close repositories window if it wouldn't close itself and press reload button. after the reload process completes press search button and write in VLC then press ok.
at the moment synaptic picks it up for you mark it for install and press apply button.
after the VLC installation press the search button again and write in XMMS then ok.
mark it for install and press apply button. once you're done close the synaptic and press ctrl+alt+backspase to reload gui. now you're ready enjoy listening to the music you like and watch the movies you do to :-)
have a nice day.

### Post by bobmitch on 2005-04-28
For multi-track audio, Ardour is the best package on linux for sure.
Hydrogen is a decent Drum machine / sequencer.  (You can even coax it into behaving like a rudimentary sampler and synch it to ardour via jack)
Rosegarden is a decent cubase clone, but still not exactly mature.
