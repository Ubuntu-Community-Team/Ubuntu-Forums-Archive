### Post by darolu on 2010-03-21
Gedit is a very good text editor, it is underestimated imo, give it a try, it should preserve your indentation and it comes with Ubuntu by default.

The most popular text editors are Vi (or Vim) and Emacs, they are excellent text editors you can do it all with them but it takes time to master them as they use unique key-combinations. There are CLI and GUI versions of them although I don't know if they qualify as "light-weight".

Other options are Kate, Mousepad and some IDEs like Anjuta. All of them should preserve the indentation in wrapped lines.
