### Post by ctdahle on 2011-10-15
Frankly, I was shocked when I did the upgrade this morning and it was all Unity. I wish I had known. Although not knowing was my fault, I should have done my research before I blithely hit the upgrade button.

However, I don't think my naiveté was completely unreasonable. Every other upgrade since I started with Jaunty was mostly painless and accompanied an obvious performance increase. A few minor glitches I expected. The 6 hours of tweaking and the data loss disaster I got was decidedly NOT anticipated.

I was horrified that the upgrade dumped Evolution. This was seriously NOT in the Spirit of Ubuntu and it has seriously damaged a number of my ongoing projects.

It's not that I really care that much about Evolution, but in dumping Evolution, it dumped all my stored mail, my inbox message rules and all of my contact information. This is especially annoying because I THOUGHT I had everything set up to be archived on Ubuntu One.

Yes, I know, everyone is moving all their e-mail stuff to the web and yes, I have a gmail account too. But I live in an area where internet service is often erratic or non existant, so I need an email client that operates off line so that I can respond at my leisure and send when I have a connection. So I have lost a number of ongoing conversations which contained records of lengthy correspondence and this is pretty damned annoying.

Thunderbird seems like a perfectly good replacement, but SHAME on Canonical for not explicitly warning Evolution users that they would lose all of their stored email when they made the upgrade to Oneiric. This was a move clearly NOT in the spirit of Ubuntu.

However, it is water under the bridge. I will hear back from all of my correspondents and over time, they will provide me with the quoted text of our correspondence and any documents that were not otherwise saved somewhere.

So then the question is, Is Unity really a bad move and I think the answer is no. Actually it works quite well. It has quirks and it has lost me some work today, as well as much of the morning troubleshooting a really STUPID issue, but it is a pretty good interface and appears to have the potential to become great.

Now that I have worked out the bugs, I am pretty happy with Oneirc and Unity. I wasn't happy about making the change, but it was a hell of a lot less painful than the XP to Windows 7 debacle I had to put up with at work and which pushed me over to Linux for all my personal machines to begin with.

Anyway, it's done, and I have learned a valuable lesson...never be the first kid on the block to upgrade. Let some other sucker do it first and then upgrade after they've worked out the bugs.
