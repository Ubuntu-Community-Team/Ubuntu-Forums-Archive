### Post by |{urse on 2008-06-08
Try this first. 
1.) power off the system and unplug it from the wall.
2.) touch something metal to discharge any static on your person.
3.) get a screwdriver and remove your left-most sidepanel.
4.) locate the largest plug (atx power plug) coming from the power supply and connecting to the motherboard (the great big circuit board)
5.) unplug that plug and plug it back in.
6.) (hopefully) power up your machine
If that doesnt work
1.) repeat steps one and two above
3.) locate your ram modules. These are long thin, and rectangular
they look like this ===> [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image:Memory_module_DDRAM_20-03-2006.jpg](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image:Memory_module_DDRAM_20-03-2006.jpg)
4.) there should be locking tabs on either side of each ram module. If you have two sticks remove one of them and plce it immediately to the left of your machine (don't touch it again or walk around with it in your hand. Static destroys ram.
5.) with one stick safely removed try to power on your machine again.
Theres a few other things it could be, If you are willing to try these two things first I'd be willing to walk you through the rest of them.
