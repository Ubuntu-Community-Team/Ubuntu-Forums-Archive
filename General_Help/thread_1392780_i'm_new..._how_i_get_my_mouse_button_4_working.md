### Post by no0ne on 2010-05-07
Since you're using Razer hardware I suppose you understand what they mean by onboard memory and configuration. The thing is, the razer default device config relies pretty much on little bits and pieces in their proprietary driver. However, once you load a new configuration into the mouse it will remain in effect indefinetely. In order to do this you need, afaic, a single one time access to a windows platform with either the driver installed or administrator priviledges for installing that driver.

[Here's a post I made earlier]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=8462984&postcount=4") when I realized this and found to my surprise that it was not well documented on ubuntuforums. Skip the part with the VM if you just want the buttons working. Most of the configuration options razer gives you in the windows program will work quite identically on linux, though more hardware releated options tend to rely on the proprietary windows driver.
