### Post by PhilGil on 2015-01-03
In this case you may have to reinstall Windows. Unfortunately, it sounds to me like your neighbor's website requires [ActiveX]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ActiveX") to function properly. To the best of my knowledge ActiveX only works with Windows and Internet Explorer. Because of this limitation (and ActiveX's poor security record), it's rare these days to find websites which require it. As far as I know there are no reliable work-arounds in Linux, and dual-booting (or running Windows in a Virtual machine) is often too complicated for average users to wrap their heads areound.
