### Post by Dkkline on 2010-05-19
Quick and easy way to setup Empathy (universal chat program, I believe (don't use it myself, so bare over with me))

1.) click the envelope (mail icon) to the right of your clock (top panel where the shutdown button is) press "setup chat"!
(if you have already tried this, the welcome to empathy wont pop up, fix this by "F4" in empathy or go Edit -> Accounts) 

2.) Click "Yes , I'll enter my account details now" then click "Forward"

3.) Choose the Accounts you want to Add to Empathy, let's say Messenger, find the "MSN" under the "What kind of chat account do you have"-menu, it will ask for your Windows Live ID, and Password, 

4.) if you have more accounts you want to add check "Yes" if you don't check "No, that's all for now"

5.) it will ask for your personal information, type them in, or check "I don't want to enable this feature for now", press "Apply"

this should do?, I don't have a account myself so, hope this helps

Cheers

p.s. (you can always go under the "Edit -> Accounts"/"F4" to change or Add your accounts

### Post by tango_ninja on 2010-05-19
Empathy is perfect and, as previous posts have stated, you can access & configure the gui from Applications > Internet > Empathy IM Client.

Empathy will allow you to consolidate your MSN account along with any other chat program (google talk, ICQ, yahoo etc.) in one window.

However, I believe MSN-specific features such as games will be lost.
