### Post by trivialpackets on 2006-02-23
Well, Synaptic is the easiest way to do all of what you say, but clearly not the only.  If you're connected to the internet, then open it up, then search for xine.  If it's green or whatever color, then it's installed.  If it is not, then just check it and click apply you'll have it shortly.  Alternatively, you can go under Multimedia and see if it's there, and if it's not or listed under something else, as I thought that xine came by default, although I could be mistaken, just open up a terminal and type xine.  Should start it up.  Whether you can play what you want or not...we shall see eh?
