### Post by ram2500 on 2009-11-06
I don't think that either Ubuntu or your computer is the problem, although your hard drive might be old and about ready to go (40 GB drives are now a thing of the past, so I'm sure it's several years old). Try reinstalling it again. If you have an extra HDD around, try that. It may not even be the hard drive, it just may be a corrupted file or error (a lot can happen here). Your hardware is very common - and Dells are common as well, so you should have zero compatibility problems with Ubuntu.
