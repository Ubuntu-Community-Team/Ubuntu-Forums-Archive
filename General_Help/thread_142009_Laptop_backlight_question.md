### Post by jbennett on 2006-03-09
I have a dell laptop that was previously running Windows and I had it configured to power the screen off after x minutes of no use.  You can do the same in Ubuntu (and it should turn the backlight off, it did for my dell).  Go to System -- Preferences -- Screen Saver and click the second tab.  On the right hand side of the window there is a group box with power management settings.  You have to click the check box to enable the settings, then the first option lets you select an amount of time to wait before turning off the monitor.  Because these are power management settings, designed to prolong battery life, there are also options for suspending and hibernation.  If you don't want your laptop to go to sleep or hibernate (which I don't) then just set these to 0 minutes.

Hope that helps.:)
