### Post by squaregoldfish on 2009-04-13
Unfortunately, webcams and flash drives are dumb devices - they only do what they're told to do, and for that you need some kind of computer. For example, when you plug a webcam into the computer, it's the computer that sends instructions to the camera telling it to activate and take pictures.

It would be possible to create a small dedicated computer that could act as the controller - you can buy a small, complete PC board with a processor, a bit of RAM, and some USB control on it, with an embedded Linux OS (e.g. [here]("http://www.armadeus.com/english/products-processor_boards-apf9328.html")). Whether or not you could add a webcam driver I don't know - I would think it's possible but difficult.

The helmet cameras you can buy (e.g. [this]("http://www2.oregonscientific.com/shop/product.asp?cid=6&scid=14&pid=709")) basically contain the above. Probably a lot easier, and not that expensive really.

Alternatively, buy a NetBook and sling it in your backpack ;)
