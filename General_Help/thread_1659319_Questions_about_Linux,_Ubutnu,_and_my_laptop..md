### Post by cipherboy_loc on 2011-01-03
For your first question of what is the difference between Linux and Ubuntu, the answer is somewhat complex:

Linux proper is just a kernel; everything that you see eventually interfaces to the kernel. Without the kernel, nothing will work.

GNU/Linux is a mesh of the Linux kernel with added software (because with only a kernel, your computer does not do much). The GNU and FSF foundations (both headed by Richard M. Stallman) develops the "free software" which makes up the GNU part. Most users don't see this, because most of the applications are GUI applications.

Ubuntu is a distribution of GNU/Linux which adds extra software (like a window manager, firefox, etc) which is where most people use GNU/Linux, more commonly known as Linux. That is, most people get GNU/Linux from a company which pulls together and develops software that makes GNU/Linux useful to most people.

I think I covered the first question.

My computer (which you can see the stats on in my signature) boots from the Grub2 menu to full desktop (not including the time it takes to log in), in under 50 seconds. To launch Firefox 4.0 takes only another 5-10 seconds max. 

As for C#, there is mono, but I don't know much about it.


Hope that answers some of your questions,
Cipherboy
