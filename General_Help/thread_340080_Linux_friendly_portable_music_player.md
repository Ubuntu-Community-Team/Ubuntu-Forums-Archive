### Post by towsonu2003 on 2007-01-16
I like cowon's products -mp3 players- very much. see [http://www.cowonglobal.com/](http://www.cowonglobal.com/) or [http://www.cowonamerica.com/products/](http://www.cowonamerica.com/products/) for a list. 

It's plug-and-play with Linux (it's displayed as a storage device), even updating firmware is possible using Linux. 

They have choices for hdd-based [[link]("http://www.cowonglobal.com/product/product_iaudio_hdd.php")] and flash-based [[link]("http://www.cowonglobal.com/product/product_iaudio_flash.php")] file storage. I think they all play ogg (check the feature pages for each) but I'm not sure about flac. 

I particularly like (and own) iaudio X5 (plays ogg & flac). available at amazon.com for about $250. For more battery-power, try to find one with an L extension (ex: X5**L**). 

notice the attached (below) logo on their music players' feature list page, which is something you'll encounter extremely rarely!

edit: I just noticed you want something small (you mention ipod nano), so maybe you'd be interested in "iAudio T2" (plays ogg & flac). Here's the [link to that]("http://www.cowonglobal.com/product/product_T2_feature.php").
