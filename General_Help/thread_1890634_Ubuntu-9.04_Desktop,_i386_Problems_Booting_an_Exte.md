### Post by Newbie1111 on 2011-12-03
My Hewlett-Packard ZE1110, Pavilion notebook computer, purchased in the year 2002, contains an Insyde Software, JA.M1.31, Basic Input Output System (BIOS) which does not support the booting of my computer from a Universal Serial Bus (USB) drive.  However, it does support the booting of my computer from its internal 3.5-inch diskette, Compact Disc/Digital Video Disc (CD/DVD), and hard-disk drives.  So l have been trying to implement the instructions in the section entitled "Using a CD" under "The minimal Linux method" at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromUSB](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromUSB) on the Internet for booting a USB drive by first booting my computer from data written on a Compact Disc (CD).  I used data from an Ubuntu-9.04 "Live" or "Desktop" CD for my try at this in an attempt to nearly match the software with those instructions.--Gratefully ubuntu-9.04-desktop-i386.iso was still available on December 2, 2011 for downloading from [http://old-releases.ubuntu.com/releases/9.04/](http://old-releases.ubuntu.com/releases/9.04/) on the Internet!  And in using that download I didn't find any differences in file names or paths from the instructions which I think were written appropriately for Ubuntu 9.04 beta.  I "burned" that International Standards Organization (.iso) image to an optical disc using a "Burn Image to Disc" option of an optical-disc-writing program.

The main result so far was that I could not boot an external CD writer when it was USB-cabled to a USB-1.1 port of my computer while that CD writer contained a bootable either Partimage Is Not Ghost (PING) or Ubuntu-9.04 "Live" CD.  Other results in detail were that I could eventually "piece together" from several boots of my computer what I read on a screen which could only be briefly seen in each such boot: "Boot CD-ROM.  Non-emulation booting. loading stage2", followed by a message about bash and the GRand Unified Bootloader (GRUB) prompt "grub>".  I woud have expected the title I input into the file menu.lst to have appeared on my computer screen.  But I didn't notice that during such booting.--And that may be one of my problems.

I got the impression that the statements in the file menu.lst were basically GRUB commands.  So I tried inputting some of them after GRUB prompts.  But the results of that were unfortunately disappointing.  For example, after I input one of the menu.lst commands, namely "root (cd)" after a GRUB prompt, apparently GRUB's "response" was "Error 23: Error while parsing number."

Given my initial failure in getting the mentioned instructions to work for me, another problem is my own.  That is I don't yet understand and/or know all of the details of the workings of GRUB, the commands "root (cd)", "kernel /boot/vmlinuz file=/cdrom/preseed/ubuntu.seed boot=casper noprompt cdrom-detect/try-usb=true persistent", and "initrd /boot/initrd.gz", what is supposed occur first in the booting procedure, why I didn't see the title I input in menu.lst, etc.  In particular, after the International Standards Organization (.iso) file was "burned" as an image onto an optical disc, where is /cdrom/preseed/ubuntu.seed located, inside vmlinuz or initrd.gz?  Otherwise I did not see the directory /cdrom on the "burned" optical disc I discuss here.  Another puzzle is that in preparing the directory called "iso" the files menu.lst and stage2_eltorito were copied to iso/boot/grub; but in my corresponding "burned" optical disc, produced by "burning" the .iso image to disc, I found the slightly changed, but the related file names MENU.LST and STAGE2_E.  In some respects I have been somewhat like an instruction follower without understanding enough of the instructions to be able to answer with certainty why some things did not work for me.

The instructions I found, but have not implemented at [http://www.manycodes.com/2009/07/22/linux/make-a-usb-boot-cd-for-ubuntu-9-04/](http://www.manycodes.com/2009/07/22/linux/make-a-usb-boot-cd-for-ubuntu-9-04/) were quite similar to those that I tried to follow at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromUSB](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromUSB).  Besides the amount of waiting time in the command I am about to mention, one difference I noticed was in the former case to place the "WAIT=" line at the end of the file /etc/initramfs-tools/initramfs.conf instead at the top of it, as in the latter case.

Please help me diagnose the problems I encountered and enable me to boot an external USB drive with my computer.  Any teaching of me someone would be willing to do could be useful, too.---That is by analogy, unless a man is dying of hunger, teaching him how to catch fish might in the long run be more valuable to him than giving him a fish.
