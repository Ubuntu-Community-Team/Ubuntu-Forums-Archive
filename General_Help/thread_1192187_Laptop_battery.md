### Post by Scotty Bones on 2009-06-19
This might sound strange, but it worked for a Sony notebok once.
Shutdown the system, unplug it from AC, remove the battery, hold the power button down for 10 seconds, reinsert the battery (do not reconnect AC power) then see if the system will power on.

or

> **LynneC said:**
>  It appeared to charge to 100% but since then the laptop will not recognize the new battery. It sends me the message that "No battery detected". 

These batteries have a sort of "smart technology" built into them. Among other things, that technology is designed to prevent batteries from over-charging, but sometimes it still happens. Check the batteries documentation to see if it has a quick discharge feature, then try it again.
