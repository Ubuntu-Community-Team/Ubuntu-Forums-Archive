### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-14
As to the question about an encyclopedia...

If the computer has an internet connection, there's always Wikipedia.  It's arguably as [reliable and informative]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reliability_of_Wikipedia") as any of its electronic or print competitors (particularly in the areas of science and math), and is always more up-to-date.  There are various other online encyclopedias, such as [Encyclopedia Britannica Online]("http://www.britannica.com/"), [World Book Online]("http://www.worldbookonline.com"), and [Grolier Online]("http://go.grolier.com/").  Most of these aren't free, however.  [Bartleby]("http://www.bartleby.com/") also has several encyclopedias available.

If the computer doesn't have an internet connection, Encyclopedia Britannica has [limited support]("http://support.britannica.com/linux/linux.htm") for Linux for those people who have the CD version.
