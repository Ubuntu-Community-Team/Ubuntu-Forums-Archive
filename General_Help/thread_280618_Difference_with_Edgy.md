### Post by BitTorrentBuddha on 2006-10-19
Much has changed, most notably are the upgrades to the kernel and init system. It does default to a lot of beta software, but essentially, it's a completely new verison (as Dapper was to Breezy) except that it has retained the old artwork of dapper, and dapper is still supported for quite a while. Stay with Dapper if you have it working and aren't too fascinated with beta software, the upgrading process can be more of a hassle than it's worth.

### Post by crumja on 2006-10-19
Most of the changes are to software versions, artwork, and underlying tools. Stuff like gcc, Xorg, upstart, teardown, init, etc are supposed to lay the foundation for a much more adventurous 7.04 release. Speed improvements are quite impressive too with latest gnome optimizations, kernel speedup patches, and gcc (of course).

Generic kernel has replaced k7, 686, and smp.
