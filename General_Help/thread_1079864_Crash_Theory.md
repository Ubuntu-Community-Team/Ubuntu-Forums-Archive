### Post by Unanimated on 2009-02-24
I suppose this is actually a tip, seeing as it involves doing something about your hardware. Many apologies if this is in the wrong place.

I think I know why Ubuntu (at least, mine) will randomly crash. I've been doing simple things - Banshee, Firefox, OpenOffice - when Ubuntu would just randomly crash. Tonight, I believe that I found the reason for it. You see, I was watching a show on Hulu (great site, by the way), and I was stupidly poking and prodding my motherboard with a screwdriver. I have one of the side panels on my computer taken off - I think it looks cooler. Anyway, I was using a screwdriver to get some dust off. I accidentally nudged a transformer (I hope that's what they're called) on the motherboard, and Ubuntu crashed. The sound was repeating, no visual movement, nothing. When I hard reset my computer, I immediately went into the BIOS. I checked the CPU temp, it's usual 150°F range. I kept it on this screen, and began to poke and prod the board again. I looked back at the screen, and it was frozen. The only way I could tell is because the CPU fan RPM rate wasn't moving, whereas it was moving around a lot before. It's crashed like this before. So, I suppose my tip is to keep your motherboard free of dust, keep it on a stable surface, and... don't prod at it with screwdrivers. :p

As a rule of thumb I'd say that poking powered electronic devices with any conductive things is not a smart thing to do.. :D

If you want to clean electronics, power them off first and then use a can of compressed air to blow the dust away.
