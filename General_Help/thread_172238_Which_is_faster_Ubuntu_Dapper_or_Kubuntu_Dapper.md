(If you know of any published benchmarks comparing 32-bit binary performance versus 64-bit, please let me know).[/SIZE][/FONT]

 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]So while the tests I ran were on only a few applications and in limited ways, the results seem to show that indeed 64-bits do generally run slower.  However, there are there are a few issues to consider.   [/SIZE][/FONT]

Also, the largest disparity I was able to see was around 20%, and not the many-times-slower disparity that I've seen some claim.   [/SIZE][/FONT]
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]Since this was a limited number of applications in limited scenarios, the best way to know for yourself is to give the applications you're concerned about a try in both modes.   [/SIZE][/FONT]
 [FONT=Arial][SIZE=2]In the end it's the library and compiling issues that are the most compelling reasons to stay with 32-bit binaries, and not so much performance or size.  I think it's safe to say that you're not missing out by going with the simpler-to-manage 32-bit binaries, unless your application can specifically benefit from 64-bit.[/SIZE][/FONT]
