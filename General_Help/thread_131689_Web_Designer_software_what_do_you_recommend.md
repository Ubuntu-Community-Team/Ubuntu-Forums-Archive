Perhaps it is closer to the truth when discussing "corporate" design - I wouldn't know as that's not my field.  I mostly deal with small clients, who want sites with just a few pages. Conceptualizing site designs is a creative process, and people can (and do) use whatever tools they find best for that part of the process - for many people, WYSIWYG is ideal for this.

### Post by GoodPanos on 2007-04-13
Well, I have to agree with most members here that WYSIWYG is not the way to go.  As you become better at web design you'll realize that.  Even when I use Dreamweaver I don't use the visual part of it much.

What does though become important is intelisense.  As you type a tag you want the application to auto fill for you.  This is very helpful when you are doing CSS.  I get rusty sometimes so it's helpful to see the available properties/values.  Makes it faster to develop too.

As far as good web apps in Linux; Quanta, Screem, Kompozer (carried on from NVU).  Aptana looks like is going to become the next hot Web Design IDE .
