### Post by zoomzoomzoom on 2008-03-28
There is no shortcut, you have to download that scanModem detection software [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DialupModemHowto/ScanModem](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DialupModemHowto/ScanModem) and find out what chip your modem has and then [http://www.linmodems.org/](http://www.linmodems.org/) to find if its supported.  

One workaround is a tiny usb modem the size of a pkg of gum called Zoom 3095.  Its unfortunately not super easy to get working though it claims on box to support linux.  Do a search, I own one and made post how to get it working.  It is  a good modem once you get it working.  Being very compact and not needing separate power supply its great for laptops.  Dont pay full retail, they can go very cheap on ebay.  Depends if time or money is more important to you I guess.  There is another simular modem sold by Dell that also supposed to work with linux, but I dont have experience with such so cant personally recommend it.
