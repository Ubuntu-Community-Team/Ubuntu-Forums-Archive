### Post by Autodave on 2019-05-13
Xubuntu 18.04 is still available in 32 bit.  It is the last release for Xubuntu that will be 32 bit.  18.04 is a LTS (long term service) release and will be kept updated for the next 5 years.  I would urge you to install that and keep it updated.  That will give you at least 4 more years of use out of that machine.

### Post by Impavidus on 2019-05-13
To be a bit pedantic, Xubuntu and Lubuntu 18.10 still support 32 bit, but only have 2 months of support left. The best option if you need 32 bit is Xubuntu or Lubuntu 18.04 LTS. Ubuntu 18.04 doesn't come with a 32 bit installer and if your computer is old enough to need 32 bit, it won't run Ubuntu very well anyway (unless it's a server). Ubuntu LTS releases have 5 years of support, the lighter flavours 3 years. I don't know whether the 32 bit kernels for 18.04 get 3 or 5 years of support.
