### Post by crlang13 on 2010-05-11
+1 to those who mentioned that, depending on the type of computer, some Ubuntu releases will work out of the box, some will not.  I recently updated to 10.04 on my MSI netbook and it's been generally problem free.  There have been a couple of hiccups, but nothing to worry about.

My advice to the OP is this:

Stick with 9.10 for now, since it appears to be working fine.  It's a shame that 10.04 isn't working for you, and I'm not going to try and defend it, bugs happen.  BUT, it is a brand spanking new version, and alot of the bugs may be ironed out in the coming months.  Alot of people wait a few months before updating for this very reason.  

With that in mind, as you convert more of your clients to Ubuntu, I would giving them 9.10 for now, and then when 10.04 gets a little more ironed out, and you're confident with it, start throwing that at people.
