### Post by josephellengar on 2008-10-14
To do any of this stuff you have to have the effects enabled in Advanced Desktop Effect settings.  You have to get that package through your favorite package manager.  I think that it's called ccsm, but it would be best to do a search in synaptic for "compiz" and then check.  There is simple and an advanced settings manager.  The one you want is the advanced one.  Then you can go into the settings manager and choose and from far more effects than you would ever want.  My favorites are the Desktop Wall and the Animation effects.  Once you have the Desktop cube effect eneabled you can activate it with ctrl-alt and then drag the Desktop either way with the mouse.
