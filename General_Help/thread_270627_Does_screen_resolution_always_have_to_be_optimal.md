### Post by aysiu on 2006-10-03
I'm have kind of a weird problem at both work (with Windows XP) and at home (with Ubuntu Dapper).

In both places, I have a flat panel LCD monitor whose optimal screen resolution is 1280x1024. My home monitor is 19" and my work monitor is 17".

On both computers (so this is for both Windows *and* Ubuntu), if I use the optimal screen resolution, everything looks crisp and clear.

And on both computers, if I use 1024x768, all the fonts and even the borders of windows get fuzzy.

Is that normal?

### Post by Rhapsody on 2006-10-03
It looks blurry because the 'optimal resolution' would be more accurately described as the 'only resolution'. The screen is always at 1280x1024, the monitor just internally scales the 1024x768 display to fit. It incorporates bilinear filtering, which helps (it'd look even worse otherwise), but still can't make it look as good as a proper 1024x768 screen.

CRT screens don't have the same restrictions, and hence can look crisp and clear at a multitude of resolutions.

The Wikipedia articles on the [liquid crystal display](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_crystal_display) and [native resolution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_resolution) have more on this subject.

### Post by Fred Doolie on 2006-10-03
One note. A 1280x1024 LCD will also look sharp at 640x512 or even 320x256 (though very pixilated) if your system could do those resolutions. Any other resolutions and the system has to kinda force the image to fit onto the existing screen pixels.
