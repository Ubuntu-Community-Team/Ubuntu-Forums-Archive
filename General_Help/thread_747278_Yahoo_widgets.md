### Post by sekinto on 2008-04-06
I think Yahoo! Widgets are only for OS X and Windows.
This is what someone on the Yahoo! Widgets management team said:
> Hey people. Don’t be sending us a bunch of naive sounding things to support (you know who you are) saying that "oh, i saw you’re multi-platform, but there’s no linux!". You see the two icons of the platforms we are on. Cut it out :-P It’s not going to make us move any faster. We know linux has a bigger market share than Apple at this point, but it’s still not what I’d call large. We’ll get to it when we get to it (I think in many ways it’s inevitable). But first we have some other releases to get out. As it is, the Mac version is kind of lagging behind right now, so the thought of adding yet another platform is a bit much at this point in time.

So, my bottom line: we already know people want a linux version. We would like to do one ourselves. Let me ask this because I don’t know linux very well (I only know *real* unix from Mac OS X/BSD [/me runs]): what is the most popular desktop environment, and do we know if it supports the crazy graphic things that we do with transparent windows? Part of the issue with linux is all the different desktop environments. If we do this, we’ll pick exactly one and focus on that. We won’t dilute our effort.

EDIT: the real fun part won’t necessarily be bringing the engine over and the windowing, it’ll likely be the more esoteric things like measuring battery life/wireless in a standard way. But linux and the word standard don’t generally seem to go together.

You may be interested in Gdesklets, Screenlets, and Adesklets though, which are gadget programs for Linux.
