### Post by medley on 2007-02-20
I've had Beryl working for more than a month. It took me several weeks to get to that point. I thought I had learned my lesson (ie, don't touch anything once you have a working system). Stupid me: I checked to see what could be upgraded on my system via adept. Having already set a rule for myself of not upgrading ANYTHING Beryl-related, I did upgrade Emerald lib type stuff, and GTK. 

Big mistake.

Of course ONCE AGAIN my windows decorations have disappeared. The fix I knew about (ie make sure beryl-settings referred to python2.5) no longer works.

Why is it that if you want a 'stable' system in the Linux desktop world the secret is "DON'T TOUCH ANYTHING!!!"?

This is getting old. I keep bragging about my cool Linux box, and how it's the new desktop for the masses, and then something breaks and a guy like me with 30 years of computer experience can't fix it. Nope; Linux on the desktop isn't ready for prime time yet. 

Sad. It's sooooo close.

I'll walk away again for another couple of years and try again some time in the future.

Bye for now.
