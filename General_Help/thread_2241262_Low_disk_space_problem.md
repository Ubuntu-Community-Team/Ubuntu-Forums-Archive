### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-08-25
When you installed 14.04 you created a /boot partition and it is too small to hold all the upgraded Linux kernels than come from time to time. And it is possible that the updates includes a new Linux kernel. At the Grub menu open the Advanced Options for Ubuntu sub-menu and select Recovery mode. At the Recovery Menu select Clean - Try to make free space. See if that makes a difference.
