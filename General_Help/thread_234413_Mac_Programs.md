### Post by MarinaraOfDeath on 2006-08-11
Well, many programs that work in X can be used on either, and anything you can compile from source has good odds of being transportable from one system to the other if there's a compiler for it and it doesn't rely on binaries compiled for that OS's architecture. For those, it's often a matter of tracking down all the differences between the two OSes. OS X, contrary to general opinion, is not unix -- it's a heavily tweaked version of unix fused with many many other things. And really, linux isn't fully unix either, it's a decade and a half of development and addition to a reverse-engineered version of unix.

The best way I can think of is to check and see if the source is available and if the program installs binaries or compiles from source during installation. If it's commercial, email the company and ask if they support linux. Get creative in searching for information about the process, you know, like asking here ;).
