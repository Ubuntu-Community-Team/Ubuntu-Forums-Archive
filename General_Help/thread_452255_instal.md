### Post by tommcd on 2007-05-26
Just remember that windows needs space for swap (virtual memory in windows) just like linux. Also, you need at least 15% of the drive free for windows defragmenter to work properly. Plus, you will want some room for programs, and documents perhaps.  I would make it 10GB if you got the space, otherwise 5-6GB should be barely enough.
