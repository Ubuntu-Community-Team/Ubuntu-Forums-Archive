### Post by Neobuntu on 2008-02-06
I was pleased to find a "linux" version of fatsort in the Kubuntu repos(Universe). This is not needed on your computer because you can view files (sorted there) anyway you wish. Yet not on some flash drives and then in their phones, PDAs, NDS and other micro devices. Some do not sort the files (like MP3s or games); that you may want, in order. 

The "fatsort" man file says, it may corrupt your drive if it is inconstant. Well a brand new formated and file installed flash drive, was not "inconsistent" and most FAT/FAT32 drives are inconsistent! I find the program trashed the file names with gibberish and made it where reformatting was the faster solution (better do your backups).

Fortunately, my backup of my trashed backup was at the ready to restore after re-formating.

Please govern yourself acordingly.

Backup! **Twice!**

Perhaps Fatsort should not be in the repositories. Do **not** try it.

Edit: Well, now I can't remember (in fairness) if this was before or after my bad (MicroSDHC to USB2.0) HC "reader". Either way, "Foldersort" instead (via WINE 100%), instantly sorts VFAT16 Flash files (for sorted use inside mobile devices); while mounted,

Never assume you have a bad Flash drive until to you try a good quality (SDHC) reader(more than One).
