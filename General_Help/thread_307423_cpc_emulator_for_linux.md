### Post by Average Joe on 2008-07-17
I have also been trying numerous Amstrad CPC emulators. Both for Windows and for Linux. For Windows I liked [WinCPC]("http://www.wincpc.ch/index.php?topic=projects-wincpc") the best. It is very easy to configure. I especially like the "Reduce CPU usage" option. That saves my laptop fan a lot of work. Every other emulator immediately boots my CPU to 80% or more, just for showing the basic screen. 

It actually surprised me that finding a good emulator for linux is much harder. By far most for them you need to compile yourself and require lots of libraries that I didn't have installed. I managed maybe half of the times to finally get it compiled. After that it didn't work at all or very buggy.

I went back to running WinCPC under Wine. That works fine, the sound as well. The developer says he is planning to port to it to Linux. So maybe one day...
