### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-06-10
You can't actually slow down the rotation of your drive. Hard drives are designed to "spin up" and maintain a constant rotational velocity.

All you can do is power it down.

Every drive makes SOME noise, which is most noticeable when the heads are doing seek operations.

But if the noises are loud, that's an indicator of a possible failing drive.  In that case, the best thing to do is back of the contents to another drive.  You can try disk imaging (using CloneZilla) but if there are failing tracks, that might not work.
