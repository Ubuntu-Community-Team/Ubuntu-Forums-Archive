### Post by mastablasta on 2019-04-01
that are easy solutions (reduce cycles, add load...) but they will slow down the video and might not solve the issue.

if this is a desktop there are a couple of solution that are better:
1. check if correct GPU drivers are installed. With hardware GPU acceleration the CPU will hardly get any load when watching videos. there are various GPU chips available that have no
2. remove the CPU cooler, clean the CPU and re-apply the thermal paste.
3. install a better cooler with a larger fan (they spin slower, quieter, and cool off just the same).
3. install a watter/liquid based cooler.

to avoid noise in the future get fanless CPU's or at least GPU's.

yes, you can do on win10.
