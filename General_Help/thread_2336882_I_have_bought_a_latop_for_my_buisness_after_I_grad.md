### Post by ian-weisser on 2016-09-12
> **vanquishedangel said:**
> 3. I plan on using gnucash to keep track of expenses, income, and to help with taxes.

Pick a tax accountant first. They will help you set up your bookkeeping. Your accountant and your bookkeeping needs will determine the software you use.
Generally, GnuCash is useful for individuals and some businesses. It is not, however, suitable for many other businesses.

Many good tax accountant charge by the hour. As an example, let's say $200 a year if you bring a QuickBooks company file on USB vs. $600 a year if you bring them a pile of printouts from GnuCash that they must wade through and puzzle out. Is paying $400 a year worth keeping GnuCash?

Maybe, maybe not. It's your decision...but don't lock yourself in stone until you find an accountant you like to work with.
