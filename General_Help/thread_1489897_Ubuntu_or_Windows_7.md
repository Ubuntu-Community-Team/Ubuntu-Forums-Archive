### Post by jerome1232 on 2010-05-21
I am unsure how easily it will be to configure the hdmi dual display but for everything else Ubuntu will be able to fit the bill.

Picasa works for Linux. Ubuntu comes with a torrent client by default, there are quite a few of them availbe as well. Deluge is the most similar to utorrent. It also comes with Open Office.org, an office suite similar to Microsoft's Office.

I would advise you to just dual boot at first and try Ubuntu out, 99,9% of drivers will be part of Ubuntu right of the install, other's (like nvidia) can be installed from Ubuntu's package manager known as apt, it will download the files from an online repository of software and install it for you. 

You also may find it that you can play all of your Windows games using Wine, however you may also find that none of them work. Check WineHQ's database to find out if your games work. [http://www.winehq.org/](http://www.winehq.org/)

There is also a pretty good selection of linux specific games. Check out the gaming section of these forums for some ideas.

You get the vast majority of your software from Ubuntu's repositories. There are thousands upon thousands of programs in there.
