### Post by p_quarles on 2008-08-05
Well, there are two basic approaches to this: Virtualization (tried and trusted), and Wubi. 

Virtualization is a very stable and mature technology, and you have lots of virtualization apps to choose from. The problems with it are resource usage (you're running two operating systems) and some more complicated hardware features (you won't be able to run Compiz or any other 3D accelerated software). 

Wubi avoids both of those problems, but is relatively young, and I think there is a lot less experience with it on this forum. Most people here, I think, prefer a traditional dual-boot setup.
