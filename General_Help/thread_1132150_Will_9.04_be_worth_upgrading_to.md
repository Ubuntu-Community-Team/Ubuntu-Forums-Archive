I would suggest worth is in the eye of the beholder. You're running an LTS, and depending on your hardware, upgrading could be difficult.

I am sweating the release of the next LTS and upgrading what was a robust Thinkpad T61p that has tons of memory and a fast processor.

### Post by cimh on 2009-04-22
You should get quicker boot times. Many netbook users use their machines differently from desktops, so boot time can be a factor in deciding.

Below are the boot times I get counting from switch on to desktop (8.04 time in brackets)

Ubuntu 9.04b ------  32s  (44)
Ubuntu remix 9.04b - 35s  (50) 
Xubuntu 9.04rc ----- 39s    
Crunchbang 9.04b --- 24s  (42) ubuntu based but uses openbox
9.04rc LXDE -------- 27s 

So these are very significant improvements over 8.04 ext4 contributes about 2s to the reductions. I also upgraded the bios (first time in 6 months) this cuts 3s off the time.

Anyone else surprised by the relatively sluggish xubuntu time? xfce should be faster than gnome. by way of comparison moblin which also uses xfce boots in 18s!

So in reply to one commenter gnome seems to be working more efficiently than xfce at least on boot up. 
In terms of boot speed it seems to be: openbox < lxde < gnome < xfce
