### Post by dragonfly41 on 2017-09-28
First, can you specify the &#8220;new pc&#8221; you have.  Does it come with a pre-installed operating system? Which one?

Do you have a USB flash drive to create a LiveUSB for Ubuntu installation?

In your old laptop you have the option of manually removing the hard drive (containing Ubuntu) and placing this is a USB container which you can purchase at a local PC shop. 
This gives you one option of connecting your old OS (in the old drive) to your new PC through a USB connection.

But much depends on what has actually &#8220;failed&#8221;. e.g. I'm limping along now with a failing laptop with a failed primary terminal and am using a secondary terminal to work in the dying phases of my laptop. I have a new desktop on order.
If you only have Windows you can use Windows to create a LiveUSB.

So you need to provide more information about (a) failed pc (b) new pc so that a path can be mapped out to suit your circumstances.

Otherwise we will waste time guessing options.

Incidentally you can purchase laptops with Ubuntu pre-installed.  e.g. Dell.

### Post by speartip on 2017-09-28
Rather than ask someone to do it for you, it would be a good learning exercise to do a bit of googling & do it yourself. Ubuntu in particular is one of the easiest systems to install.
some details are required though if you're to be talked through it here. For example, do you wish to dual boot with what is already on the laptop, or are you willing to wipe it clean and just use Ubuntu?
It would also help you if you downloaded the latest iso from here:
[https://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop](https://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop)
Burned it to a disk or usb, & played with the system a while, to make sure it ran smoothly & was compatible with the hardware on your laptop.
A good guide to installing can be found here:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation)
