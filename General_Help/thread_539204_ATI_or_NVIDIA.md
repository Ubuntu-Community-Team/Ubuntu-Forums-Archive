### Post by sloggerkhan on 2007-08-30
It's an interesting question. There are rumores that ATI/AMD will open source or improve linux support, but I'll believe it when I see it. Not sure what to tell you. Best bet is 6xxx or 7xxx series nvidia cards, IMO, (I hear 8xxx doesn't have support for some hardware video decode acceleration). If you can get a used 6600 or something for cheap, it might be better to hang on to the other card and see what happens, (at least if you like taking risks)  but if you're going to end up shelling out for a new card, I don't see the point in hanging on to the old one. You can probably sell it on craigslist or to some Windows Gamer for closer to original value than you'd expect. Of course I have vowed never to pay more than $135 for a graphics card.... I can only imagine what it would be like to have an expensive card not work.

I have a laptop with an x700. It's interesting because the open source driver works well, just locks up or has texture problems on a couple of games, while the closed driver works well on games but precludes compiz-fusion :(
