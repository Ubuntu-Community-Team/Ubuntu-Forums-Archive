### Post by sgosnell on 2009-05-12
Both HP and Brother support Linux very well.  Installing their network printers is easy, but it is slightly easier to get the scanner working with HP.  Brother requires manually downloading a couple of drivers, and activating them via the command line.  It's not hard, but it's a little harder compared to the more automatic HP installation.  Once installed, they both work about the same.  Both are much more difficult to install in Windows, and I never got my HP to scan under XP.  Using Linux it worked immediately.

### Post by sgosnell on 2009-05-12
If you get an HP or Brother, they supply downloadable Linux software.  HP probably offers a more complete setup, comparable to the Windows version, and it works better than Windows, IME.  There is no problem at all using any brand of peripherals with a Dell computer.  Dell just pushes their peripherals in order to make more money.

When you install the HP or Brother, the Ubuntu installer will find the printer and install the software for you, you just have to confirm the make and model.  If there is no choice for your particular model, just pick the closest model and it should work.
