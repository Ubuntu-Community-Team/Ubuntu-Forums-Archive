### Post by mgmiller on 2007-04-04
3 years sounds like it's about time to do some house cleaning.
Assuming it's a hardware issue, try shutting down, opening the case and cleaning out any dustbunnies from the CPU cooler and power supply fans, etc.   Especially, make sure the CPU cooler fins are clear of dust.   Use one of the cans of air to blow stuff out while holding a vacuum cleaner nearby to collect all the dust.   Check for any air filters in the front or back of your case and make sure they are clean.  I have seen filters that are on the front bottom edge and can only be removed if you hold the front of the case up in the air.  Make sure all vent holes in the case are free of dust or pet hair.  ( I saw a machine literally stuffed with cat hair once). 

Also, if you have  case fans or fans on your video card and motherboard, make sure they are working.  They should spin easily with almost no effort if you touch them.  (Just observing them with the computer running and the side of the case off can also give you visual evidence that they are running.)  Sometimes peeling back the label on the fan (could be on the top or bottom of the fan) will reveal the bearing beneath and 1 drop (1 drop only!) of electric motor oil and then replacing the sticker will get these fans to work again.  If not, they are usually easily replaceable.  

One other more advanced thing to try if all this does not work, is to remove the CPU cooler and clean its mating surface and the top of the cpu with a q-tip and a little rubbing alcohol till they are both very clean and apply a fresh coat of Arctic Silver thermal paste before reassembling.   If you have no experience removing and reinstalling CPU coolers, then read up a lot on your specific hardware before attempting this.

Good luck.
