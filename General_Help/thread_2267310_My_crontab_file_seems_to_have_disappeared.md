### Post by nerdtron on 2015-02-28
There is a difference between:

crontab -e             ---> crontab of normal user
sudo crontab -e     ----> crontab of root

to see current crontab: (small letter L)
crontab -l
or 
sudo crontab -l


Also, you may have reset your crontab. Instead of writing crontab -e, you have written crontab -r which will erase all crontab entries.
Look into your command history and see you may had a typo.
