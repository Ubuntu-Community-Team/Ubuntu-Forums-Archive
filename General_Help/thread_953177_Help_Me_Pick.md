### Post by Rhubarb on 2008-10-19
On some motherboards overclocking it too far results in a computer that won't boot.
This sounds like the situation that you're in now.

The good news is, is that this can be fixed quite easily.
Open up your case, and find the battery in it (usually a CR2032).
Depending on the manufacturer of your motherboard, you can take the battery out for a minute or two, then put it back in again.
And / Or find the memory reset pins (often located near the battery), and short them out with a jumper / something metal for 10 seconds.

Doing this clears the CMOS memory, and resets everything in your CMOS (or BIOS) back to defaults, which is usually always in a usable state (like when you got your computer new).
