### Post by crshbndct on 2010-05-01
i can tell you... its bloody amazing!!

at the moment i only have a 56k internet connection, so i got a friend to download the alternate iso for me, and installed it on my pb easynote c3:

amd xp-m 2400+ (1800 mhz)
256 mb ram (32 mb shared for video)
30gb 4200 rpm hdd
sis 740 northbridge/video.

after the first install, i thought i had killed my machine. swap file was 384 mb full, ram was at 92% capacity, and lynx browser was ridiculously slow. i am talking about 1-2 minutes to respond to me hitting <tab> about ten minutes to get from post to desktop. mouse was laggy as hell

so i thought, oh well, that 256mb limit must be a very minimum requirement.

i only just got the laptop, and i have another 512mb memory on the way, so i figured i would just keep using xp (which was also quite slow) until my memory arrives.

so this morning (nz time) i switch on the laptop, forgetting that i had installed ubuntu (i had been out all night, so forgive me) and went to make a coffee. come nack and ubuntu login is on the screen. expecting a twenty minute wait i move the mouse to select restart, but then i notice that the mouse seems to be pretty responsive. so i decide to take a chance and login. about 3 seconds later i am looking at the desktop. everything is as quick as can possibly be expected for an os which is running off a 4200rpm drive with too little ram. in fact it bloody snappy. so i restart the computer, and about 15 seconds later i am looking at desktop again.

well done to the development team. you have pulled off an absolute ripper here. cant wait till my ram arrives, then i can actually try a bit of multitasking. 9.10 was never ever this fast on this machine.

just a bit of a rambling post, but i really like it. and it easily outpaces xp on this machine.
