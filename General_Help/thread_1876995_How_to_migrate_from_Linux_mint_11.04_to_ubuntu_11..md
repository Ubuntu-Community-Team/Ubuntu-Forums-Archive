### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-11-07
Linux Mint 11 (based on ubuntu 11.04) is one version below Ubuntu 11.10, so would need to both distro upgrade and also add the ubuntu-desktop afterwards, maybe one step too many.

I have played around with Mint in the past, but never added ubuntu-desktop to it, so I have no idea how problematical that operation might be.   However, I think you will need either to wait until Mint 12 appears and upgrade to that from your Mint 11, then add ubuntu-desktop, or add ubuntu-desktop to your current Mint 11, and then see if it is possible to distro upgrade.

My feeling is that you are destined for disappointment in some way or other, particularly with the change from gnome2 to gnome3, but as long as you have good backups of everything, why not give it a go?  If everything goes to worms, you can reinstall Ubuntu, or Mint, in about 15 minutes.
