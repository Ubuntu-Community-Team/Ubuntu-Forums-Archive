### Post by holiday on 2010-11-10
You might find it useful to set up persistent sessions in Ubuntu, then when you boot back in Ubuntu, everything you were working on will come back up. 

This will slow your startup time, but it may save you some work.

I suspect that you want to game a bit, have some Ubuntu fun, game a bit more? That's not going to happen unless you get another machine. They don't cost much - you can get a decent Dell for 300 or so and most will run Ubuntu - and you can save on peripherals by getting a desktop switcher so that you can switch your keyboard and monitor between the two machines. 

I think that's going to be your best bet. 

There may be a magical solution, but you've been waiting a while, so...

Good luck.
