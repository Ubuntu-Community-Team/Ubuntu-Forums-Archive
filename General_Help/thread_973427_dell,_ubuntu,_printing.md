### Post by Thomas Lord on 2008-11-06
I recently needed to replace a workstation and I needed the replacement to (a) be available quickly, (b) come with a good warranty, (c) run a good GNU/Linux system and consist of compatible hardware.

It *seemed* as though Dell would be a safe choice.  I selected a system from their list of systems with Ubuntu pre-installed.  I chose a modest "Inspiron 530".   A variety of optional components were offered by their web site including a "Dell 948" printer, which I chose.

I paid extra for 2-day delivery and a delivery date for the printer was set so I arranged my schedule to wait around for several hours to receive it: the courier did not appear and the printer was delivered only the next day after multiple phone calls to the courier.   I paid extra for fast delivery but got no such thing.

Worse, as it turns out, the Dell 948 printer has no linux-compatible driver and is certainly not supported by Ubuntu.   Thus, I bought a paper-weight!

Worse, Dell has offered to accept return of this printer but only at still further expense to me!  My losses would likely be less if I simply sell it on Craigslist!   Even if I do return it, it appears that Dell will profit from falsely advertising the bundled printer and selling it to me under false pretenses!

Worse, the false promise of this printer was one of the reasons I chose the workstation itself, from Dell.

Worse, It took quite a few hours on the phone with Dell to even get them to acknowledge their mistake!   Not only did they propose an inadequate remedy but they forced me to waste more than a day of my time getting them that far!

It's evident that Dell sold this "bundled printer" without ever having once so much as done even a minimal amount of testing, and yet they advertised it as a special offer at a slight discount to sweeten the deal of the Ubuntu system I purchased.

Am I alone in such experiences?

I would like to be "made whole" in the sense of quickly receiving a working and compatible printer of the same or greater capabilities, and having the bogus Dell 948 carted off both at no further expense to me.  If Dell can not quickly provide such a printer then I want monetary compensation for the time spent dealing with their rude and mostly ineffectual customer service department, for the money wasted on the slow delivery, and so forth.  I don't see how anything less is reasonable.

Meanwhile, if this is Dell's approach to selling Ubuntu systems, then surely they are not doing their part to help create satisfied customers either for themselves or for their partner Ubuntu.

I realize that in the grand scheme of the world my damages here -- this entire issue -- is quite small.  I'm not so egotistical: I realize people have larger problems in life (even me).  But this experience with Dell has really left a very bad taste in my mouth and if I just meekly spend more money to return the printer and eventually get a refund not only will I have wasted more money than I can afford but I'll have helped Dell to profit from ripping me off this way.

How can the community pressure Dell to clean up its act?   And does the community hear have any experience with suing Dell in small claims court?

-t
