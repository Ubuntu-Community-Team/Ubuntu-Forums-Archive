### Post by maheshasolkar on 2009-04-07
You can lock your screen every time you go away from the computer. You can also set things up so that screen locks automatically if the computer is idle for a certain duration of time.

  [https://help.ubuntu.com/8.10/keeping-safe/C/lock-screen.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/8.10/keeping-safe/C/lock-screen.html)

Another solution could be TrueCrypt, if you haven't already tried it. They have an Ubuntu .deb in their downloads section:

   [http://www.truecrypt.org/downloads](http://www.truecrypt.org/downloads)

### Post by markbuntu on 2009-04-07
Autologin can be very bad for you. Especially when some update or mistake on your part messes up your system and gives you a autologin black screen. Believe me, it is worth the 5 second wait for timed login to have recovery mode available. It can save you hours and hours of command line hell.
