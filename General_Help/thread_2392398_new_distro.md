### Post by r.vdlee on 2018-05-24
Ubuntu is great for general office use. As already stated here, Ubuntu comes shipped with a huge list of [supported printers]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters"). Take a peek if you want to make sure that other people have tested already with the printer that you own.

Most of the time the wizard or printer takes care of the drivers. It's as easy as "just to add the printer" via Settings. Scanners idem dito. I actually prefer the [Linux scanner software]("https://launchpad.net/simple-scan") over the Windows variant and I've found in our own work environment that the Linux variant is much more responsive.

You're getting pretty vague when saying office tools. Could you elaborate on what you expect under that name?
