### Post by cb34 on 2008-12-31
i would remove the card, after turning the power off and flipping the switch on the back off the power supply, and look around the slot for crust and dirt, blow in it like mad, but dont start sticking qtips and cleaning things in there, the microscopic fibers etc you could leave behind is not good. a good blow job on the board should do it.:popcorn:

if all these cards you put in die, check that first, and check to make sure nothin in and around the vid card is touching the vid card circuit board anywhere.. and make sure the card is seated very well in the slot without breaking it..lol

umm.. do you have some other fans, like the power supply inner fan/s blowing heat on the vid card or something.. check for any broken or old crusty dangerous looking connections/wires...

maybe your motherboard itself, the slot to the vid card is faulty. and was always faulty. one time, ifthis is such a long time problem and keeps happening, i would pay someone an hr or 2 to check the voltage and the resistors on the actual motherboard and vid card and slots and everything..  i never go to a computer store for anything, but if it kept happening to diffent vid cards, i would take it in to check with a technician.

another idea... could it be maybe you have some out of whack setting in your bios maybe frying anything attached to that vid card slot(very unlikely). i couldn't help ya there in detail, but somethin maybe you should check into.
