### Post by Marsolin on 2006-11-16
There are quite a few. The key is looking for support for the specific network you will be using. [Gaim]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/gaim") and [Kopete]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/kopete") are a couple of the more popular messengers that support multiple protocols. [aMSN]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/amsn") is a good one for MSN.

### Post by strabes on 2006-11-16
p2p messenger? there's tons of options. I can't think of a protocol that you CAN'T use on any OS. Here's a few: AIM, MSN, Yahoo!, jabber, google talk (yes i know it's jabber), IRC, gadu-gadu, etc etc. Look through the protocols supported by gaim if you want more. You don't have to be using the same client to connect to the same protocol i.e. your friends could be using the (nazi) AOL AIM client and you could be using gaim and you could still talk. These would require wifi though...Not sure I understand your question...
