### Post by flippo on 2010-02-18
What you should do is:

Comment the NTFS line from your fstab
reboot
sudo mkdir /media/NTFS
Uncomment the NTFS line in your fstab, and change it to```
/dev/sda1 /media/NTFS ntfs-3g defaults,locale=en_US.UTF-8 0 0
```
reboot
If the problem does not fix, and your sick of working on it, then just remove the line from your fstab and the file will go away, but your NTFS will not mount automatically, you'll have to mount it with the "mount" command.

DO NOT delete the /media/NTFS file, that would be bad.

Here is an explanation of whats going on (if you want it):
/etc/fstab specifies what you want automounted, how to mount it, and where to mount it.  It is autogenerated at install, based off of the volume labels on your disk.  The volume label uses the escape code \040 as a space.  The filesystem does not, \0 means null (I think), so it is doing very strange things when the drive tries to mount.  In order to mount it properly you need to remove those nulls (hence shortening it to NTFS) AND the directory has to exist (hence mkdir /media/NTFS).  If both of these are true, and your fstab is good it should mount.  If it does not then I don't know whats wrong, but its most likely a bug somewhere.

Hope this helps clarify whats going on, why things are not working, and what you need to do to make them work.
