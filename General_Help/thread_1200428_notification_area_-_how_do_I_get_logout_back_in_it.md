### Post by linuxrockz on 2009-06-30
Right click on the notification area, clock and uncheck lock to panel (if it is not already unchecked). Then right click on the logout button and click move. Now move your mouse to move the logout button. When you reach the place you like to place to logout button just left click to place the button there. You may now lock their positions to prevent any further change in location.

Thats it:p
Have a good day..

No, it wasn't in the notification area. It simply was next to it, and you can definitely move the applets anywhere you want.

Notification Area, User Switcher, Clock and all others are separate applets as well.

Unlock all your applets, and you can then move them freely around by dragging with middle mouse button. (To unlock/move the Notification Area-applet click in the barely visible handle on the far left end of the applet)

The handle for Notification Area is not at the end of the panel, it's at the left end of the Notification Area. Just click directly to the left of the leftmost icon inside the Notification Area-applet. (how visible the handle is depends on your theme)

And the notification area really, really is just the Notification Area-applet itself, a place where running programs may put their icons to notify you about changes etc. No applets run inside the notification area. Everything else you see in your panels are separate applets.
