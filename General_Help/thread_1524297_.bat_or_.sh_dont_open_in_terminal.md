### Post by intrader on 2010-11-24
Sorry, but maybe not one of those replies is correct. The reason is that it may be a vfat volume (referred to as FAT or FAT32). In these volumes, since about Nov 10th update to Maverick, it is not possible to change the execution permission of a .sh file.

### Post by WorMzy on 2010-11-24
It's never been possible to change the permissions on files stored on a FAT or NTFS partition, neither of these filesystems support Linux file permissions.

Either copy the files to a filesystem that *does* (e.g. your root partition, preferably in your home folder) or create an entry in fstab to mount the partition with specific ownership and permissions.

I wrote this tutorial for NTFS partitions, but it should work the same for FAT, simply use vfat as the filesystem instead of ntfs or ntfs-3g:
[[COLOR="DarkGreen"]HOWTO: Mount NTFS partitions with specific ownership/permissions[/COLOR]]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1604251").
