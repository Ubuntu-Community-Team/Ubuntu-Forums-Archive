### Post by HereInOz on 2011-05-06
I, too am curious about that.  I am assuming that you have a dial-up connection using a standard modem and a standard phone line?  If that is the case, it is highly unlikely that you will be achieving 65kbps - it is beyond the capabilities of a standard modem to do this.

Whereas 28kbps is quite achievable (unpleasant, and damnably slow, but achievable).

I tend to think that in this instance, given that my assumptions above are correct, that Ubuntu is not reporting the download speed correctly.  It is perhaps that Ubuntu is reporting the port speed, rather than the actual data speed down the line.

I wish it was something more exciting, but that is the only explanation I can come up with.
