### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-03-31
CDs and DVDs actually contain more than their rated capacity, as a side effect of the media creation process. The amount varies.

Many CD/DVD burning programs have the option to "overburn"; that is, burn more than the rated capacity.

Having said that, sometimes the ISO is actually less than 700 megabytes, but whatever operating system you're using is reporting an incorrect/inaccurate size. Possibly due to the difference between Mebibytes and Megabytes (the latter is 1000 kilobytes, the former is the "correct" amount of 1024 kilobytes).

It is also possible that the PowerPC disc was generated automatically and nobody else actually noticed that the ISO is too big.
