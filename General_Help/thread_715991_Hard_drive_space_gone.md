### Post by Helltime on 2008-03-05
I installed Ubuntu a while ago and liked it. But there was a problem with my computer and it ended up getting the hard drive reformatted. I have a 250GB hard drive and i did the windows, ubuntu split and gave the majority of the hard drive to ubuntu. Even after the computer getting wiped i still only have 71GB of space on my hard drive for windows and i no longer have the option of useing ubuntu (its just plain not there, that i know of anyways). What can i do to get my lost hard drive space back? I need it! And i tryed seeing if maybe i could put more than the 71GB on there, it won't let me. Please help!

### Post by Fire_Chief on 2008-03-05
You can either create a second drive in Windows using Disk Management or boot from a live CD like GParted and expand the existing NTFS partition to fill the rest of the drive space.

If you are unable to see the extra space (drive is only recognized as a 71GB size), then you may have a misconfiguration of your BIOS. It has a few ways of addressing hard drives and changing it would cause problems reading the drive correctly. The other possibility is the drive went bad though I doubt that's the case here.
