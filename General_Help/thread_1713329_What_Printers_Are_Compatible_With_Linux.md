### Post by walt.smith1960 on 2011-03-24
HP is well regarded for their compatibility with Linux but I know about being soured on a brand, I've felt the same way.  I'm an advocate for Brother.  You can go to Brother's Linux driver web site and know what's supported.  Their installation directions seem more complex than necessary but they do work.  Look at machines that interest you and note the model number.  Then go to this page and make sure there are printer and scanner drivers available for that model:  [http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html).  

Brands that seem to be problematic are Lexmark & Dell-Dell printers are relabeled Lexmark.  Lexmark "business class" machines may have Linux support available, their $49 big  box specials probably do not.  Canon may have software support for a given model on their European or Asian web sites but not on their N. American web site, I'm not sure why. I think Epson has pretty decent Linux support but I have no experience with them. The recurring theme is to go to the manufacturer's web site support pages.  If you don't see mention of Linux,  that brand/model is not a good candidate.  Speaking of web sites, here is another though it's not real active or current:  [http://www.linuxfoundation.org/collaborate/workgroups/openprinting](http://www.linuxfoundation.org/collaborate/workgroups/openprinting).  I hope this is of some help in making a choice.

### Post by walt.smith1960 on 2011-03-27
> **wildscribe said:**
> I have had excellent results with networked Brother laser jet printers. Right now I am using HL-2170W. It's a easy set up. Just plug the ethernet cable into your network and Ubuntu will find it for you.  

I have yet to try Ubuntu with a wireless printer. I don't have time to troubleshoot these days. Maybe I'll try this weekend.

I had a problem  using wireless with the printer not being found when I printed to it. it was found and installed fine but would not be found when a print job was sent. This occurred days or weeks after the initial printer install.  This problem occurred months ago before some CUPS updates so the problem may longer exist.  What I did that seems reliable is like this.  Navigate to  the printer properties device URI and change dnssd://whatever  to  socket://xxx.xxx.xxx:9100 where xxx is the I.P. address.  I also had a problem with my MFC-7820N taking FOREVER to print multiple pages--like 1 page/min.  I went into synaptic and found Brother CUPS drivers and checked the box.  Then went back to printer -> properties -> make and model and found "MFC-7820N for CUPS" entry and enabled that.  The print speed went up what I'd come to expect in Windows.  Just something to keep in mind if your printer speed is not what you expect.
