### Post by mixmaster on 2007-05-08
The broken and solid vertical bars have a long and sordid history in the development of the PC.  

The basic rule is that you want the one which corresponds to ASCII 0x7C (124) not the extended ascii bar (0xB3 I believe).  As it happens, on my UK keyboard under Ubuntu both shift-\ and AltGr-` (key to the left of 1) correspond the the ascii value of the pipe symbol but display as a solid bar, which is useful but possibly technically incorret.

I am told, although I've never been able to confirm the story, that the original broken bar was the result of a defective slug when printing the original Ascii table and that the mistake was copied and distributed too widely to be corrected (a bit like the upside down Hebrew character Aleph used for infinity in maths).  Later a corrected solid bar was introduced into the extended Ascii character set on the PC, but of course all existing software expected the broken bar ascii value to be the pipe symbol.  The extended ascii solid bar was introduced onto some European keyboards much to the confusion of all concerned.

Here endeth the history lesson
