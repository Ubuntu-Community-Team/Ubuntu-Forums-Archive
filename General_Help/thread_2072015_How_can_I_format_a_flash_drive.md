### Post by cottfcfan on 2012-10-17
I always use gparted.
Install from synaptic, after opening, pick the device you want to format from the dropdown box in the top right hand corner.
Then right click on the partition/drive you want to format.
You may have to unmount it first, then format to whatever you want.

### Post by kurt18947 on 2012-10-17
One thing I would recommend checking if you use the resulting flash drive on a Windows machine.  This assumes a format to FAT32.  After formatting with Gparted, make sure the drive is recognized on a Windows machine.  I've had some problems with that.  It may be fine but something worth checking.
