### Post by rsay on 2007-04-28
I use the hp C7180 multifunction printer and it handles photo printing nicely. I use gwenview to print borderless photos and it works great. **HP **provides step by step instructions for full bleed borderless photo printing using kview, gwenview and gimp here: [http://hplip.sourceforge.net/howtos/printing.html]("http://hplip.sourceforge.net/howtos/printing.html")

The printer also supports direct printing from cameras (or other usb devices) and memory cards.  

I couldn't be more pleased with the way this printer works. I have it set up as a wireless network printer and all of the features of the printer/scanner/fax work in linux. I initially purchased another printer from a company that doesn't provide good linux support and ended up returning it after hours of frustration hunting around the net for linux drivers. 

Whatever you are considering I would recommend checking at [http://openprinting.org]("http://openprinting.org") to see if the unit is linux compatible. Look for a printer that is listed as working "perfectly" and you shouldn't have any problems. (The first printer I bought was listed as "Paperweight" btw.
