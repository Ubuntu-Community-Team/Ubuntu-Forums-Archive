### Post by StooJ on 2007-10-26
I am looking for the ultimate fileserver solution. Can it be done? Linux is great for servers after all...

Here's the situation:

I write database software for Windows machines (and they have to be Windows machines, no question). I'm also moving into the possibility of making it run on OS X as well, since some customers like fancy pretty looking computers.

Basically, I seem to collect Operating Systems and have to use them all fairly frequently. Naturally Ubuntu is far and away my favourite platform for most things, but I may be using XP, Vista, OS X or Ubuntu across 2 desktops and a laptop. My girlfriend also has a dual-boot PC (XP & Ubuntu), as does a lodger.

Currently I have a (Xubuntu) fileserver with pretty much all my personal files and work files. Lots of MP3s, lots of downloaded programmes, pictures, the usual gubbins. This serves ('scuse the pun) me very well at present.

On one of my PCs I have my Thunderbird profile saved in an NTFS directory (made possible by NTFS-config and now Gutsy) so I can get my email from any one of the three OSes on that PC and read all my mail on it too. This is utterly *wonderful*.

However, could I make it better?

I've been thinking about a much more substantial data sharing fileserver. Could I share everything? All the information would need to be accessible to Ubuntu, XP, Vista & OS X. Things like:

Email
Contacts
Calendar
Sticky Notes (I love these)
Music database (at present I have a seperate media library on each OS on each computer - that's a lot of updating when I add an album to my collection)
Instant Messenger Contacts (I use Pidgin and get frustrated when I have to rename contacts to something sensible a dozen times)

I have a Treo 650 as well. This doesn't seem to sync with linux sadly, but I'll be looking to replace it sometime soon. It'd be great if I could sync it with any of my PCs and have the change reflected across all of them. For backups this would be a case of syncing to a folder on the Fileserver, but contacts & memos, to dos and calendar data, that'd be trickier.

How about syncing to my laptop when I'm not connected to the network. Would there be some way of updating the fileserver when I rejoin the network?

I could go on and on, and I'm getting a little carried away.

When Thunderbird was first released I tried hosting my email on the server and connect to it from multiple locations. Disaster - wiped all my emails completely. Luckily I had backups.

Anyway - I'm looking for ideas, possible programmes and solutions. Pointers in the right direction would be lovely too. I know that I'm asking for a lot on this one, but I really think this would change the way I work, and it'd be a fantastic showcase for Linux in general and Ubuntu in particular.

Many thanks all.
