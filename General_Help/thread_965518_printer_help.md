### Post by jesuisbenjamin on 2008-10-31

I would begin with:

[LIST]
[*]Checking the hardware: ie. Is there colour ink in your printer? Does it work if you use it with another computer/OS?
[*]Does it print black and no colour on a document with both black and coulour?
[*]Is there anything happening at all when printing a colour document or is the document enqueued and the printer simply not reacting?
[/LIST]

Then i would:
[LIST]
[*]Go to System > Printing
[*]Check if the local printer is the same model as you expected
[*]This may help others to help you better.
[*]Check the printer option tab and see if the color mode is the one you expect.
[/LIST]

If the printer listed in your local printers is not the one you have, then i would reinstall the printer manually. As i cannot see your printer model in the list suggested by Ubuntu, this may mean that the appropriate driver does not exist.

I remember using a driver from another model when i had an old HP Deskjet printer and it worked. **However this alternative solution may not work or even damage the printer, and as i do not know the consequences i would suggest not to do so until you get advice from a more qualified user than me.**

At least this may hopefully help others to help you.
