### Post by Sef on 2008-07-20
> Also, for every distro I install, do I need to partition an additional swap space or can I use the same one for different distros? I'm also confused on what a swap space really is and how it affects performance, can someone enlighten me?

Only one swap is needed.  How much swap is needed depends on how much ram you have.  If you have 1 GB or more, than you only need 1 GB swap.  If you have 2 GB or more, then swap is not really needed.  512 mb ram or less you need 1 1/2 - times ram for swap.

> I installed this after Vista, so I don't know what the problem is. Should I reinstall Vista and then follow with various other OSes?

Install Windows first, then GNU/Linux.

> How many gigs is enough for an OS like Ubuntu or Fedora? I plan on trying out other distros to see what is available and to see what suits me best for my needs.

Ubuntu should have about 8 - 10 GB.  Fedora is about the same.
