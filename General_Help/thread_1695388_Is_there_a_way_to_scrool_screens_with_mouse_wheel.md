### Post by doas777 on 2011-02-26
if the window is the active window, and you mouse is within the borders of a control with a scrollbar, the mouse wheel should do what you want by default in almost all applications. you shouldn't hold alt to do it though. 

open a window to a folder with many files, and size it so that scrollbars appear. then click in the whitespace between files, and move your scrollwheel. does it move? 

if you want to switch between desktops as Nytram mentioned, click the desktop, and scroll, while your mouse cursor is over the desktop. this will only work however if you have visual effects enabled. without compiz effects, scrolling on the desktop does nothing by default.
