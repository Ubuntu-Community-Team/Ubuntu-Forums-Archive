### Post by Remthewanderer on 2006-03-29
I am completely new to Ubuntu (i installed Breezy about a month ago) but i have found it to be a fantastic alternative to M$ or apple.  However, the one thing that i find to be the most frustrating is getting an ipod to play nice with programs like gtkpod or amaroK.  My solution was painstaking, i manually moved all the music i had on my ipod onto my linux box.  Then i wrote a little shell script that stripped the files out of their subfolders (F00, F01 etc) then i used easytag to rename the file names to something that made sense. Then i took my ipod and docked it into my windows machine and wiped it clean.  Once i had it clean i used amaroK to put all my music back onto the ipod.

this was probably not the best way to do things but from everything i have read there is no pure plug and play option for an ipod on linux.  gtkpod was extremely annoying but amaroK is probably once of the nicest linux progs i have used.

this is just my two pence from a ubuntu/linux newcomer but hopefully it helped a little.
