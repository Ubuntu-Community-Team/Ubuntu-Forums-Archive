### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2009-05-30
When using the internet, it is always up to the end user to ensure that they are being protective of their data, passwords, etc.  While Ubuntu is very secure out of the box, there is always more that can be done (see the Ubuntu Security link in my signature).  For online transactions, make sure that the website you are at is the actual banking site you are looking for and not a spoof - this includes checking the URL's spelling, and seeing that the SSL certificate is signed correctly.  Some banking sites have additional security measures, like images shown to you the you pre-select - this helps them prove to you that they are the real site, and not somebody trying to spoof them.

Also, you shouldn't use public computers to do online banking if at all possible, since people can install keyloggers on these, or otherwise make the boxes insecure or vulnerable to attack.

Being paranoid with your information is healthy, but you shouldn't let it prevent you from being productive online, as long as you are careful.
