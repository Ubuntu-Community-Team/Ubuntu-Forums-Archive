### Post by roberto16900 on 2012-12-05
I started with Ubuntu on my local network two years ago and I was delighted of natty narwhal. On my PC I had a double boot option (Ubuntu and Windows 7 ultimate SP1)
Everything was fine until three days ago! Then my windows drive crashed and I was unable to boot my system as the grub boot loader was situated on this drive. Big problem! So I decided to do the installation from the Ubuntu CD again.(Upgrade natty narwhal to natty narwhal) The comment for that was that nothing of my personal data would be lost. That was not true. All the work was wiped out   Only the essential software was working! Firefox, Thunderbird, wine, gimp, bombono dvd creator and lot more of installed and configured software was gone in the nirvana! Now I am not anymore delighted with Ubuntu because with this event I found out that Ubuntu is not easy and it takes me much more time to get it configured for my needs as for example Windows 7.
I use my PC and network as a working tool but if you must spent more time to repair your tool than the tool is inefficient!  This event showed me too that I will never upgrade to Oneric-Ozelot because I had to configure all my samba stuff for my LAN again and I had to configure my in-house Apache server which runs on a Ubuntu server machine and which is mounted in the whole network. Fortunately my personal data was saved in backups (Thunderbird, Pin data source for Firefox and a lot more)

Generally I found out that there are to much people working on the Ubuntu system and I think the coordination is not good. Much "Developers" are doing their own stuff which will sometimes not work with Ubuntu and the installed software. I find it very annoying that I cannot change things in the OS without using su gksudo etc. It is my PC and no one should hinder me ore make it inecessary difficult  to configure it to my needs. What is the idea behind this policy? I am a grown up person who is responsible for my own doing! I do not need people which are thinking they are better than common people and which are trying to control my actions! Most of this people I think are members of the development crew of Ubuntu.  You failed with your target to make an easy to use OS and your slogan for Ubuntu is simply wrong. I am very disappointed about that and I'll never tell people again to install Ubuntu on their machines.

Ciao
Roberto[-(
