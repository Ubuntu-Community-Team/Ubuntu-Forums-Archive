### Post by Sutekh on 2005-12-12
There are alot of threads dealing with this issue.  If you can log into KDE fine, or GNOME with root or another user, than I think you can join the list.  

Alot of posts have advised deleting all folders pertaining to the user's GNOME configs; 

.gnome, .gnome2, and more.  

A search with words like .gnome, login, freeze, user, should bring up some options to look at.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=102359&highlight=login+gnome+user]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=102359&highlight=login+gnome+user")
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=96621&highlight=.gnome]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=96621&highlight=.gnome")
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=88278&highlight=.gnome]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=88278&highlight=.gnome")

I wasn't able to find any threads where the OP had solved their problem, when I had this happen to me, I created a completely new user, and deleted the old one.  That may be an option for you too.
