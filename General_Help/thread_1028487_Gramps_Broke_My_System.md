### Post by Celticowboy on 2009-01-02
I've been running Ubuntu 8.0.4 for several months on a computer built for my mother (replaced MS ME),

Everything was more or less ok, what I expected. The machine was faster and more responsive under Ubuntu, and would do what we wanted.

One of those things was genealogy. I installed Gramps and built up an extensive data-base - huge actually.

I did a manual install of a new version of Gramps because it wasn't listed in the repository. It is supposed to be a version for 8.0.4, and was several generations newer.

It crashes often, won't open the database reliably, and won't save changes. An uninstall and reinstall of the earlier version didn't work.

FireFox updated itself around this same time, and is a mess - the bookmarks were lost, and it won't create new bookmarks. Evoloution e-mail stopped working as well. I wanted to email myself a few key documents, in case of a general melt-down, but nothing - the app opens, but won't connect and draw down new email, won't send out.

I could never get the burner to work, believe it is the unit itself. I'd just wipe and start over, but that data-base was soooo much work, I hate to loose it.

And the general system seems screwed - won't shutdown from the system, just hangs and requires a hard shutdown from the power button.

Any help would be appreciated.

### Post by mdebusk on 2009-01-02
> **Celticowboy said:**
> One of those things was genealogy. I installed Gramps and built up an extensive data-base - huge actually.

I'm sorry I don't have a solution for you, but I will recommend replacing Gramps with a Java-based solution, [GenealogyJ]("http://genj.sourceforge.net/wiki/doku.php"):
[INDENT]*GenealogyJ is a viewer and editor for genealogic data, suitable for hobbyist, family historian and genealogy researcher. GenJ supports the Gedcom standard, is written in Java and offers family tree, table, timeline views and more. GenJ is free software so it can be redistributed and/or be modified under the terms of the GNU General Public Licence as published by the Free Software Foundation.*[/INDENT] 

It's very stable and easy to use, and it won't mess with your system the way Gramps (apparently) has.

You'll probably have to get your database opened in Gramps long enough to export it to GEDCOM, though.
