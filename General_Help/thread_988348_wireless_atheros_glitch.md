Hi,  just a bit of info that I came upon by accident: -


In my ongoing quest to inform friends and family about Ubuntu and GNU/Linux, I try when possible to get people to try it on a hard drive which I loan to them for a month or two.

I generally install Ubuntu on their laptops or PC's using a couple of spare hard drives I purchased for this task.  Recently I was having problems trying to get a laptop to  recognise my wifi at home.  I would set up the wireless name, password etc but even though the laptop was only feet away it wouldn't connect.  I checked 'MAC' settings and scratched my head why there was no wifi?:(

After a while I thought 'I'll disable my hidden essid and try again'. The laptop saw the wifi and bingo connected first time!!!!:)
I then enable the router so the essid was hidden and the laptop still reconnected.

This has happened on 3 occasions with different laptops the most recent being a Samsung R40.  
I don't know if this glitch is well known but on all occasions making the essid visible ensures a connection straightaway.

Hope if helps someone who been in a similar pickle.:lolflag:
