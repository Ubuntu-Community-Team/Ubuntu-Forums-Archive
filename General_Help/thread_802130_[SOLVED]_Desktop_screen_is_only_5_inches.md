I've been wanting to make the switch to Linux for years now and finally got my hands on a laptop so I don't have to mess with the family computer.  It's an old Dell Latitude C810 and to get Ubuntu to install I had to install using the "Safe Graphics" mode.  Everything is working great except instead of having a full 15" monitor, I'm stuck with about 5" in the center of the screen.  I've done all the updates, I've searched to forums here and installed nVIDIA drivers to try and fix it.  I've also tried changing the screen resolution and the largest available is 800x600.

Knowing that I am 100% new to Linux, I will tell you that I am not afraid of the command prompt but I would need some detailed instructions. ### Post by kwdrysdale on 2008-05-21
I have just learned my first lesson about Linux.  I had used EnvyNG to install the nVIDIA drivers and assumed it was enabled.  I went and checked out the Hardware Driver settings and noticed it was NOT enabled.  So, I enabled it and everything is the proper size now.  MUCH nicer to look at and use. :)  Sorry for the newbie posting.
