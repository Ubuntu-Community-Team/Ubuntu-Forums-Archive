### Post by lightnb on 2008-09-06
Since switching to gnome, there's a couple of things 'missing' that made life easier. The first is the "Edit as Root" context menu option. In KDE, when you right click a file, you have the option to open it for editing with root permissions. It asks you for your password, and basically does what "sudo gedit /some/file/name" does. Except that you don't have to stop what you're doing, open a terminal, cd to the directory and then type out the command.

Second, and equally helpful, is the "Open Terminal Here" context menu option that appears when you right-click inside the window manager. It opens a terminal, and like the name implies, automatically cds to the directory you're viewing in the file manager.

Is there any way to get these back, in gnome?
