### Post by groggyboy on 2006-11-13
k3b works - which got me thinking, if k3b can do it, why couldn't a gnome-based cd-burning app like gnomebaker do it too?  and it turns out it can!  yay!  i get to create an iso AND stick with a gtk based app!

thanks for putting me on the right trail, chriscando!

**gnomebaker howto** (i presume k3b or other burning software would be similar):

>Start a new project as if you are going to burn a cd.  Put on all the files you want in the iso.  When you are ready, click the "burn" button.

>In the dialog that pops up, under options choose to "only create image".  Specify where you want to save it, and click "start".  A progress bar appears.  On my computer, it only took a minute and a half.  Gnomebaker plays a sound to alert you when it's done.  Voila! An ISO!
