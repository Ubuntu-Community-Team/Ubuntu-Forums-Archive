### Post by agillator on 2012-05-12
It sounds to me as if you do have an overheating problem. I just went through one myself with my laptop - of course it occured right in the middle of taxes! Oh well.

If I am correct, the problem is that the thermal grease between the cpu and the heat sink is old and is no longer doing its job. In my case the remains of the old thermal grease were removed, cleaned and replaced with new. No more problem. If you know what you are doing it is probably something you can do yourself since any computer parts store should have the grease. If you DON'T know what you are doing you can/will turn your computer into an expensive door stop, so if you have ANY doubts (or think that is not the problem) take it to a professional to repair. It shouldn't be an expensive repair - it will probably just be a minimum time charge.
