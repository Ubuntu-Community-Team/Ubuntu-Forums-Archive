### Post by nikhilk on 2009-07-17
> **lse123 said:**
> you mean 8.10 small updates last 18mo BUT IF I upgrade to 9.04 I will have another 18mo of free small updates ?

Ubuntu 8.10 (Intrepid Ibex) will be supported till April 2010 (i.e. it will get free security and critical updates).
Ubuntu 9.04, Jaunty Jackalope will be supported till October 2010.

So yes, if you want your OS to continue receiving security updates you should upgrade to 9.04. But bear in mind that before 8.10 reaches EOL, 9.10 will be released which will be supported till April 2011.

### Post by CatKiller on 2009-07-17
Just a quick clarification in case you're confused. It's not the free part that changes - Ubuntu is always free - it's the updates part. Normal versions are supported (meaning that the devs bother packaging and hosting security updates) for 18 months after that version's release. LTS (Long-Term Support) versions are supported for three years. There's quite an overlap between a new version being released and support being dropped for the older versions.
