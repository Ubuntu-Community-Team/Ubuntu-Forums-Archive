### Post by davarino on 2008-03-08
If you live in an area that has a community college (junior college), check with them.

Academic advisors love to talk with people such as you who are excited about the possibilities of life and learning. They often can give you valuable advice. Sometimes, though, they may simply regurgitate things that they have heard from others.

You would also benefit by becoming an active attendee at a local LUG (Linux Users Group) or computer club.

The most important thing to remember in all of this is to take nothing as gospel from anyone until you've had at least 2 years of looking at things. See what you want to try to do, and then be openminded until you are sure of the special direction you'd like to follow.

Good luck! Be persistent.
