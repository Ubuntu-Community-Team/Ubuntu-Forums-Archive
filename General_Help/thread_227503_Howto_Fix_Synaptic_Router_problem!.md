Just wanted to post this because I searched the internet for two days and tried many different sugestions and could not get synaptic to connect to the web.  Firefox and ping were working fine!  Finally I came upon a Mepis forum and read this 

"I usually have to set my DNS manually, to 4.2.2.1 and 4.2.2.2, before Synaptic will work. I don't understand it, I just do it. I have a Linksys ADSL2MUE with a WAP11."  I tried to sign up andf thank him but they are not accepting anymore members. yea I guess Mepis is bigger than Suse now?  

It worked! [http://ubuntuforums.org/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif](http://ubuntuforums.org/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif)  Synaptic works now!  If you are having this problem just put 4.2.2.1 and 4.2.2.2 in System - Networking - DNS area.  Now I just did it and do not know if you will have to do it everytime Ubuntu starts up.

What is the deal with this problem anyway?  I Suse 9.3 and 10 never had this problem.  I just switched over to Ubuntu because Suse is getting too big and bloated.

New information!  I wrote this several days ago, since I have found that every time I booted my computer the DNS had been set back to 10.1.1.1 and I could no longer use Synaptic or apt-get until I manually changed the DNS back to 4.2.2.1 & 4.2.2.2.  The solution was to configure the router to 4.2.2.1 nad 4.2.2.2 and to save the configuration.  Now it works great and I can upgrade my Ubuntu Box at any time I desire.  I am using a D-Link 500 and it comes with a pdf file which you use to configure the router.
