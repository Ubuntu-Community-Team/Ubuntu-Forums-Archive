### Post by Ramses de Norre on 2006-05-17
There are two different versions, each in .tar.gz and .tar.bz2 format and each of those formats with their md5 sum (so 8 files as you see).
You'll probably like the highest version, and the .tar.bz2 is the smallest to download (higher compression).
If you want you can use the md5 sum to check if you're download didn't corrupt the file but this isn't really necessary.

Use tar -xvjf /path/to/file.tar.bz2 to extract it, then follow instructions (readme file).
