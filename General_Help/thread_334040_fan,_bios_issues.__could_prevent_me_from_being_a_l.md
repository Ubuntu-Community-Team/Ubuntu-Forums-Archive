### Post by reclusivemonkey on 2007-01-08
I take it this a fan inside the case, and not your CPU fan? Have a look in your BIOS settings to see if there is a temperature at which the fan is activated. Alternatively, you can change the fan for a low noise fan (they can be bought fairly cheaply). If its just a case fan, and you are happy with the temperature inside your PC under heavy load, then you can simply unplug the fan. HOWEVER, overheating can damage your PC. Its a judgement call only you can make really.

### Post by halw on 2007-01-08
When is the last time your opened your case and cleaned/dusted it? Dust build up and such restricts air flow and thus things heat up quicker.

As a general rule you should to this once every six months or year. More often if your case sits on the floor.

Depending on your case you may have a removable, washable filter in the front which should be cleaned also.
