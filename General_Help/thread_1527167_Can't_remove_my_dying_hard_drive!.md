Okay. I take it this is your motherboard:

[IMG]http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf-JAVA/Doc/images/c00258619.jpg[/IMG]

Here is the documentation - [http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/document?lc=en&dlc=en&cc=us&docname=c00257657](http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/document?lc=en&dlc=en&cc=us&docname=c00257657)

Some parts of reconnecting are very straight forward. The big one with a lot of prongs goes in the big slot with a lot of slots. (ATXPWR1) (This gives power to the motherboard and components like PCI cards.) The smaller one with just 4 (2x2) prongs is the power for the CPU. It goes next to the big one.

The CD/DVD drive(s) plug into the primary IDE port on the bottom end of the drive near the PCI slots (blue in the picture). Unless your hard drive uses an IDE port, that is, but likely, your new hard drive will plug into one of the SATA ports, located above the BIOS chip (bottom right, yellow sticker).

Your power supply should have power plugs that are 4x1 and sort of rectangular at the end. These plug into the drives to provide them with power.

### Post by chessnerd on 2010-07-09
> **TheNerdAL said:**
> I don't know what connects to the long blue slot.

The long blue slot is the primary IDE port. It will either be for the hard drive or the CD/DVD drive(s).

If the hard drive has a connector that would fit there, it is for the hard drive. If the hard drive has a small connector that would fit into one of the two ports above the BIOS chip, then use that blue one for the CD/DVD drive(s).

It probably won't make much of a difference which one goes where if both the CD/DVD and hard drive have IDE (or PATA) connectors, but I'd put the hard drive in the blue slot as it is the primary one.
