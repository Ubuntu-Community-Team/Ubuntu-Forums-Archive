### Post by Zorael on 2008-12-18
I generally have lots and lots of tabs (Vertigo/Tree-style Tab), and I use the Session Manager extension rigorously.

If I in XP suddenly load 40 tabs, Firefox will naturally stutter and limp for a bit until they're all loaded. During that time, I could technically go into Preferences and make some changes there, or prepare to open another bookmark, or whatnot - all while it's still fetching and parsing the tabs I told it to. If I open the same amount of tabs in GNOME/KDE, Firefox's interface will freeze completely, working in the background with no responsiveness to user interaction until it feels it's more or less finished. Then in a big rush it'll process the key-presses and mouse-clicks it received when it was in its page-loading frenzy, like an afterthought. "Aah, there we go. Hmm? You were saying? Oh, right."

Is there any way to make Firefox's page rendering more [*nice*](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nice_(Unix)), in the sense that its GUI doesn't grind to a halt when it's under heavy load? I don't mind much if that makes it load pages a smidgeon slower.
