In Windows, I have a program called [sharpkeys]("http://www.randyrants.com/2008/12/sharpkeys_30.html"), and I use it to remap the scroll lock to the mute button on my laptop (which is function + F9) and the numpad + and - to increase and decrease the volume, respectively. ### Post by lidex on 2010-03-16
Yes. Go to Applications menu: "System>Preferences>Keyboard Shortcuts". Should be self-explanatory ;)
