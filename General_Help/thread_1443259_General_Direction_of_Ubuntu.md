### Post by KayakJim on 2010-04-01
> **kokkus said:**
> How can you have a separate /home partition?
Is that something you can do from the installation program or something?
And when you have a separate /home partition, will the installation program make a new home folder then or will it read the separate /home partition automatically?

There are several forum threads and blog posts that outline how to create a separate /home partition.

I used a different method because I was doing a fresh install. I moved all of my files to an external hard drive. Then during the installation, chose manual partitioning and created a 10GB /root partition, a swap partition equal to my RAM, and the rest of the drive for /home. Of course you can add other partitions but that is just what I did.

Then, any time you do an install you will need to select the manual partitioning method and when selecting your /home partition select to not format.

Doing that allows to you try out every version of Linux available without losing any of your settings (e.g. .bashrc, etc) or files.

Just be sure to keep a backup of your /home files until you get the hang of it.
