### Post by spiritraveller on 2006-06-02
I tried using midnight commander for a while, until I learned about bash completion... just hit tab and your head will spin at how fast you can get around.

If you get hooked on the command line, there are a lot of options for applications if you just look for them.


"Remind" is a great program for scheduling.  And "wyrd" is a really nice visual interface for it (ncurses).

As I said already, I like mutt for email.  But there is also pine for a more user-friendly experience.

You can browse newsfeeds superfast with snownews.

Newsgroups with slrn or tin.

You can IM with naim or any of a bunch of other apps.

It takes a little bit of learning with CLI apps, but once learned, they tend to be a lot faster to use than GUI apps.

[Linux.com](http://www.linux.com) has a really good series of articles on CLI apps called "[CLI Magic](http://www.linux.com/search.pl?tid=89)".
