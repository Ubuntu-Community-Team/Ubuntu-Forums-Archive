### Post by WorMzy on 2011-04-10
Ubuntu is distributed under the GNU GPL, so yes, you can sell it, there is no clause within the liscence to prohibit such an action, afaik. However, like you mentioned, the OS is available for free anyway, so charging people for it is either taking advantage of their neivity, or simply misleading them. Either way, you'll have to reason with your own conscience to decide whether you can distribute Ubuntu for a fee or not. Also, there is nothing stopping one of your clients from *also* selling Ubuntu, since the exact same license which allow you to distribute Ubuntu, permits them to distribute Ubuntu too.

### Post by guilleme on 2011-04-10
Yes, you can sell it. 
Linux is under the GNU GPL licence, and, what that means for this case, is that you indeed can sell live CDs, and re-distribute the software in many forms, changed or un-changed. You must, however, redistribute under similar or the same licence, and make your clients note that you are charging them for something they can get for free by themselves.
It's been said many times, but apparently not enough, that "free" software should be taken as "free speech", not "free beer". (The ideas are the cost-less thing). 
The only foreseeable problems you might have are FSF and FOSS guys jumping on you and telling you... Way Many things, but I believe it is ok to sell live Cds myself, and I personally do it. And I am not afraid to tell it.
Many people want to try Linux, but they might not have the time or enthusiasm (to gain the knowledge  to) make a live cd. Why not to sell one you made to them? They already know they can get it for free if they do it themselves, but they (might) not have the knowledge to do. You are, in that case, selling not the OS nor the Live CD, but the knowledge you have about making those.
It's just a point of view. 
And, no, it is not illegal to sell LVCD.
