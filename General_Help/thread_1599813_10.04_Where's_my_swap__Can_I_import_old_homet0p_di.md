### Post by Topsiho on 2010-10-18
Hello,

I think Hippytaff gave good advice, only he forgot to mention the size of the swap partition.
Usually, as a rule of thumb, the swap must be twice the size of the RAM, so in your case app. 2000 MiB. Swap is used when more workspace is needed than there is in RAM, so the contents of RAM need to be stored temporarily elsewhere, the elsewhere being the swap.
Whether that amount of swap is still needed, with the larger RAMs of today, might be a matter of discussion (and dependent on what you use your computer for :) ).

Topsiho
