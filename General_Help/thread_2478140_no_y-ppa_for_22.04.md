### Post by guiverc on 2022-08-17
y-ppa?

PPAs are [***Personal Package Archives***]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Repositories/Ubuntu"), and the owner of the PPA can decide what releases they support & thus build for (*of the currently supported releases*). If you want to know why a release isn't supported; you should be asking the owner of the personal package archive, as they are 3rd party software sources.

I have no idea what y-ppa is?  nor why it's useful. But have you checked to see if the owner has a reason for no build for *jammy* (22.04), as there maybe a reason, or its no longer a maintained program thus contains added *risk* & should be avoided. Either way a *build request* to the owner maybe the easiest approach for you.
