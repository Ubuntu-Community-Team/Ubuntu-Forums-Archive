The monitor of a sound device, let's say in your case (and usually), the one of your default audio device, is everything being played out by your device at any given moment. Thus, if you hook up a recording app to it and start recording, then you'll record any sound that is currently being heard on your desktop; useful in some occasions, e.g. video tutorials, etc.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-05-13
The dock microphone could refer to a laptop dock that has sound connections.  It could be a USB camera with a microphone, it could be a separate microphone plugged into the laptop.  It could be a "line-in" input jack that needs some boosting with an amplifier to work as a microphone jack.

Monitor is used for feedback so that you can hear yourself speaking.  That way you can "monitor" your speaking volume and quality.  You can turn it on and off and adjust the volume level.  Turn it up too high and you will get squealing--also called microphone feedback.
