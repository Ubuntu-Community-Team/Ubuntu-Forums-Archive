### Post by jlochhead on 2009-03-10
The major distros are all pretty similar tbh, main differences in looks usually being the Window Manager for the basic user. Ubuntu is as good as any.

The heavy desktops are GNOME2, installed in Ubuntu by default and KDE4, installed in Kubuntu by default. I would stear clear of KDE4 as, at least in my experience, it zaps even more system resources than GNOME (not to mention the branding is really annoying).

Two of the most popular lightweight WMs are XFCE, which you have already said is probably unsuitable and LXDE.

I would recommend LXDE as it is looks a lot like GNOME but has a much faster UI.

### Post by cap'n leaky on 2009-03-10
Well,

Since you won't list the specs of the computer, I'll relate the experiences on my antique pc (built in 2002!).

My system is a 1.4Ghz Celeron (socket 370) with 512Mb ram and 40Gb hdd, Nvidia FX5200 video.  I dual boot XP and xubuntu 8.10 (upgraded from 8.04).

I ran xfce for quite a few months, and it runs better than XP.  In fact, I recently installed the ubuntu-desktop package, and even Gnome works better than XP on this machine.  Some people may think it slow, but I have no problems with the speed of the machine.  Just don't use Compiz or any desktop compositing.

I have also run LXDE which is very fast, and looks very "windowish".  I think any of the other light WMs (flux/openbox, windowmaker, etc) may be too different if they are used to XP, but they will work even better.

I have also run the Puppy live disk as well, and it really flies on this old machine, even off the live disk.  I think the default setup is a little cluttered (and ugly to my taste), but it might be my first choice for a really low resource machine.

So, not knowing the age of your parents machine, I don't know what to tell you except if it is anything over 1 Ghz and you have at least 512Mb ram, I would go for straight Ubuntu with Gnome.  I still think Gnome has the cleanest, easiest desktop.

just my $.02
