### Post by szaka on 2007-06-05
Remove the ntfsfix line completely from your fstab. Your only problem was that you had a space character in your directory name. You don't need either chkdsk or ntfsfix (it's dangerous to use, so ntfs-3g takes care about the needed issues itself).

The ntfs-3g driver is being very seriously quality tested non-stop and we had quite a lot of positive feedbacks about its reliability and stability: [http://ntfs-3g.org/quality.html](http://ntfs-3g.org/quality.html) In fact we are not aware any stability or data reliability problem.
