### Post by grsing on 2006-06-02

### Post by ubnoobie on 2006-06-02
playing drm'd files is not going to happen without some potentially illegal hacking.

your easiest course of action would be to use windows media player to burn those protected files to regular audio cd's and then re-rip them into mp3's (or some other non-protected format)

this method is actually suggested and supported by the online music store i purchase music from (in drm'd wma format) as a way to get their music to play on an ipod.

note that windows media player defaults to ripping cd's in a 'protected' wma format (which is the first thing i change on any new windows install). so if you've ripped your own cd's using those default settings, you can also just rerip your original cd's on your linux desktop.
