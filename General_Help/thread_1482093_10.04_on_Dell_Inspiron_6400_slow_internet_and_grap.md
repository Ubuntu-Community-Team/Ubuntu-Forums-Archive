### Post by efflandt on 2010-05-17
It could be that your video does not like the new kernel mode setting (KMS).

[http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/releasenotes/1004#Working%20around%20bugs%20in%20the%20new%20kernel%20video%20architecture]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/releasenotes/1004#Working%20around%20bugs%20in%20the%20new%20kernel%20video%20architecture")

The ATI X1300 on my older HP desktop booted fine, but seemed sluggish at some things in 64-bit 10.04 LTS, and would refuse to go into suspend or hibernate.  Either **nomodeset** or more specific **radeon.modeset=0** kernel parameter made it as responsive as 9.10 and fixed suspend and hibernate.

My Inspiron 6400 work laptop has ATI M52 [Mobility Radeon X1300] and on that I boot 64-bit Ubuntu from a USB hard drive (WD Passport).  Sometimes it would boot fine, and sometimes the video ended up scrambled during boot and I would have to manually power down.  Since Intel video works fine either way on my personal laptop, I added **radeon.modeset=0** parameter in /etc/default/grub of the USB drive to just disable KMS for radeon, and that boots fine on the Inspiron 6400.

I don't know if Broadcom STA works in 10.04, because that has always been weak on the BCM4311 that laptop and I could not get a signal, so I deactivated that and use a Linksys USB54GSC v1.0 instead, which is stronger and supported out of the box with Ubuntu.  When I tried Broadcom STA in 9.10 with BCM4312 on a newer Dell 1545 I had temporarily, it was much stronger than the BCM4311 in the older Dell.

PS: I hadn't noticed that you had different wireless, and different ATI video X1400 vs. my X1300).  I have seen other posts about X1400 video, but the nomodeset is worth a shot if you have not tried that.

### Post by _Slike_ on 2010-05-30
Efflandt's suggestion to turn off the KMS setting did the trick for me.

All I did was changing a line in */etc/default/grub*:
```
# ORIGINAL: GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX=""
GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX="nomodeset"
```
Of course followed by a Grub-update (sudo update-grub) and a reboot.

Ever since I've done that, I haven't had any single problem any more (incl. the wireless issues).

So thanks a lot!
