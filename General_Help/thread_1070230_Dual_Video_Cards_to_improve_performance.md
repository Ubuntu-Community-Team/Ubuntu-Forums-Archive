### Post by djsroknrol on 2009-02-14
From my experiences with multiple cards, SonnHalter is right and I wouldn't suggest it either. They can cause alot of problems and grief...some cards just don't play well together no matter what you do. Alot of the time, the only way to run multiple cards is to run the same exact cards which, need I say is a costly way to go.

I think that you'd be much happier if you went with a dual headed card. There are some great deals out there right now. For example, I just purchased a pure ATI Radeon X1600 Pro, brand new, on the old "E" for $30.00 US.
