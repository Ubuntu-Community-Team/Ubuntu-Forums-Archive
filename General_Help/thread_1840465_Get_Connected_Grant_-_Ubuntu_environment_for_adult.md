### Post by Zanon on 2011-09-07
Hi all,

I am posting this thread to get general advice from forum members over the next few months for a project to provide accessible ICT to adults with learning disabilities based around Ubuntu.

I am currently employed with an organisation that operates 9 care homes and supported living projects for adults with learning disabilities in the London (UK) area. Money and funding is a big issue in the care sector, particularly so at this time because of local and health authority (our funders) budgets cuts resulting from the 2008 financial crisis.

A little over a year ago I put in an application for Get Connected Grant funding (provided by the Social Care Institute for Excellence [SCIE]; a UK government body). The grant application was based around providing accessible ICT to over 70 adults with learning disabilities, some of who will have severe sensory and communication needs. Even the most able of our service users will have difficulty with reading and writing and poor fine motor skills, as well as very limited experience of using computers. The solution I confidently proposed was based around Ubuntu.

I probably didn’t think that my organisation would actually achieve funding but we did! We were very fortunate, and very grateful, to be awarded Get Connected Grant funding. My organisation needed to put in some matching funding and will have to meet the shortfall in funding that has resulted from a general increase in costs (and also I made some mistakes in the costings [less said about this, the better]; not too much but it will have to be met by my organisation).

It is quite strange that at the same time that we are experiencing severe cutbacks in general funding, that we should get a grant award for a specific purpose (in this case providing ICT to vulnerable adults). But I guess that's the way it goes!

I am now seeking to source the equipment and purchase decisions are quite stressful. I am very conscious that I need to purchase the right equipment because there is no way I will ever be able to obtain replacement funding.

I am not a Linux, or even a computer, person and I am not employed for that purpose. However, I will be installing and configuring the equipment (there just isn’t enough funding available for a computer professional). I will also be having to train staff and help staff support our service users with the computer equipment. My employment should see me through the period of installing the equipment and getting the project running properly.

Also, I am conscious that if the project has difficulties the small number of naysayers in my organisation (they are everywhere) will helpfully advise me that I should have gone with Windows. This is quite aggravating because when things go wrong with Windows it seems that people tend to accept that there wasn’t anything else you could have done.

By the way I didn’t go with Windows for purely economic reasons. The time taken to maintain windows systems is ridiculous. There is no way our organisation could resource maintaining an additional 11 Windows computers. Too frequently whole weekends are wasted just getting a single Windows computer back to an acceptable level of performance; I never, ever want to do a Windows reinstall again as long as I live! 

Also, the fact is that Windows systems become obsolete quicker and my organisation would never have enough money to purchase licences for the next version of Windows. 

So in my view we will get many, many more years of use out of the equipment with Ubuntu. Whereas, with Windows, we would be left with only poorly functioning equipment within five years and no funding available to upgrade.

I have had to be quite assertive with a small number of my colleagues who wanted to redefine the project as Windows (but I was able to trump all arguments with a reference to the original grant application).

What I thought I would do is make regular posts (i.e. weekly or so) to let you know how the project is going, but also (and more importantly) to ask for any advice that forum members might be kind enough to provide.

I do need some immediate advice on equipment and I will make that the subject of my next post. I will also post a summary of the project aims and objectives that were agreed as part of the Get Connected Grant award.

I am conscious that this is thread might be more suitable as a blog that a thread in a support forum. However, I will not be able to set up a blog and the project is starting now! I hope that forum members won’t see this as abusing the forum?

Thanks for your time. I will post again shortly asking for some advice on compatible equipment for Ubuntu.

Kind regards,

Adrian

### Post by nothingspecial on 2011-09-08
Hi

Before you buy, I would consider looking at a single supplier. I have no idea how the computer hardware trade operates. But in the business I'm in, if you need many specific products at one time it is often cheaper dealing with a wholesaler.

To each individual supplier your order is small. To one supplier your order is significant which gives you bargaining power. If you can find one company that sells everything you require, then even if their listed price is higher for each item, you may be able to negotiate a price for everything that beats going direct to each individual manufacturer.

(Very simplified) example (I'm a wholesaler)

Manufacturers list price for one box is £10

It costs them £6 to produce.

They sell it to me by the hundred for £7.50 (one delivery, $150, easy).

I do all the work to sell it on which they need, I'm like a salesman for them. They are making this stuff anyway.

My list price for one box is £12

But if you want 10 boxes and more importantly want 10 boxes of 10 other items then I might charge you £9.

I'm happy, tyhey are happy, all the other manufacturers are happy, because I'm going to order more at the same price I was paying anyway..... And you're happy because you got it cheaper.

It may not pan out that way for what you want but it is worth considering.

Sorry if you realise this already and it seems like I'm talking down to you. I'm not. It just seems to me that your interest lies in helping the adults with learning difficulties rather than procurement. 

Good luck :)
