### Post by Steveway on 2007-09-12
This is complete marketing bullsh*t.
Those Thumbdrives are slower than HD's and they have limited read and write capabilities.
Meaning that those will be not only more expensive but also slower and it will die pretty fast.
If you really want then you can create a linux-swap partition on your thumbdrive but keep in mind what I said before.
If you have low RAM then partition some diskspace as linux-swap.
It's faster, cheaper and it will live longer.
