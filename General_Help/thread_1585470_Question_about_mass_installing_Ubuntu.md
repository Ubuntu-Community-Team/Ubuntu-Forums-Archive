### Post by snowpine on 2010-09-30
Welcome to the forums! Before you do anything, you should use Ubuntu for a while yourself. If you are not an Ubuntu user yourself, it is not fair (in my opinion) to encourage other people to use it. :)

The next place you should look is the [Ubuntu System Requirements]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements"). Here is the recommendation for an inexperienced Ubuntu user:

>     * 1 GHz x86 processor
    * 1GB of system memory (RAM)
    * 15GB of hard-drive space (although this can be split onto 2 drives, a 5Gb / and a 10Gb /home fairly easily)
    * Graphics card and monitor capable of 1024 by 768
    * Either a CD/DVD Drive or a USB port (or both)
    * Internet access is helpful 

To answer your question, yes, Ubuntu is very easy to install and to use compared with other types of Linux. I recommend you burn a "Live CD" and try it "with no change to your computer" today, then you will have a better idea of what Ubuntu is before you recommend it to others. Good luck! :)
