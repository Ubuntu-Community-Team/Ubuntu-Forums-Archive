### Post by Redsandro on 2008-07-01
Apparantly no one knows this, so let me post what fixed it for me.

I have a TV on the same video card as my monitor. My TV is a partial twinview clone of my main screen, so I can fit a movieplayer in the corner that plays fullscreen on television.

I did not know that changed the way the screen goes grey.

So I made the TV pan the whole screen, and now it's fixed. The whole screen goes grey.

old:
**Option "metamodes" "CRT: 1280x1024 +0+0 , TV: 720x576 +0+0"**
new:
**Option "metamodes" "CRT: 1280x1024 +0+0 , TV: 720x576 [COLOR="DarkRed"]@1280x1024[/COLOR] +0+0"**

### Post by Pater Phil on 2008-07-12
Turns out that the solution is pretty simple.

From Travis Watkins at [https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/compiz/+bug/159584]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/compiz/+bug/159584"):

> You need to add the following lines to all your Device sections in xorg.conf:

    Option "AddARGBVisuals" "True"
    Option "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"

This is why your decorations are not appearing.

Worked like a charm in about five seconds for me.

Except now I can see the icons when they load. I just cannot visibly manipulate them after that.
