### Post by flar on 2006-06-03
Ardour is a multitrack recording studio
Rosegarden does midi
Hydrogen is a good drum sequencer
Jack is a daemon that is able to control the routing of sounds among apps

qjackctl controls the jack daemon and you can achieve low latency full duplex operation with a decent sound card

These are just the main things I use for recording.. there are tons of music apps in linux of varying quality.

I actually prefer using these linux apps to my old cakewalk sonar setup.

The only thing is that the linux apps don't look as nice, but even in their current state they provide me with a fully functional studio

### Post by cleentrax on 2006-06-21
The Linux program most like "Reason" is called Wired. It's promising, but not yet in the Ubuntu repositories.

Audacity is a capable editor, but is ugly and unintuitive.

Other than that, I haven't been impressed with Linux audio apps. The OS foundation is in place (plugins, JACK, etc.) but the apps are at least 7-10 years behind their Windows counterparts.
