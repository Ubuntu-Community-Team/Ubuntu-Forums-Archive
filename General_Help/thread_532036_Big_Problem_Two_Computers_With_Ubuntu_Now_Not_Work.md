### Post by expatCM on 2007-08-22
You say that your data is backed up.   I think what you mean is that it still exists .....  recovery may be more fun.

In the case of the desktop, when your new drive arrives (guess this is the plan since you say that the existing drive is not doing so well)  it may be as simple as setting up the new drive and then installing the old drive and copying over (not moving) the data.

Why did it happen... ?  Very hard to know but perhaps this is a function of the drive failing which is what you have been noticing.

The laptop is a whole bundle of fun.  I had a look on google at the error messages.  There are lots and lots of results.  They are all over the place and make my head hurt big time.

I would suggest that the easiest way forward would be to first secure your data (so wait for the desktop machine to be completed first) and then simply set up the laptop from scratch rather than attempting to fix it.  The reason is that the google results give many straws to clutch at which will take time to work through.  Installing Ubuntu from scratch takes only half an hour so it may be the fastest (and most certain) way to proceed. 

That is all my opinion ....   I hope others will comment since there must be other views...  :)
