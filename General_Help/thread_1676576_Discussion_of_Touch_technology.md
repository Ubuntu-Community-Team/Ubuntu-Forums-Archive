### Post by Robing Goodfellow on 2011-01-27
I would like to start a discussion that will hopefully interest more experienced folk than myself. I'm not even sure if this is the right place for this kind of post, but figured it was a start and someone would tell me if the thread needed to be moved.

I'm a retired Navy CPO now in my second career as a High School math teacher in New Bedford, Mass.  In these tough economic times almost all school districts are hard pressed to add tech to their classrooms/curricula, but being an inner city school in an already depressed area, we are particularly hard pressed to "rub two nickels together."  As all teachers do, most of us make up the shortfalls from our own wallets.

Most of my classes are "revisited."  Students who for one reason or another have already failed their math classes at least once, usually more.  As such, I'm always looking for innovative ways to reach these kids, and I don't do so bad, but could do better with some tech.  To this end I use robotics, computer programing, internet resources (such as Kahn Academy), whatever I can find or think of (and afford) to get through.

One of the things that instantly engages kids is computer tech and one way to engage a whole classroom of them is a smartboard.  Unfortunately these are very expensive.  I have been researching alternative methods of doing this sort of thing (alternative meaning I can afford it by myself) and have come across some wicked cool ideas, technologies and just plain spit and bailing wire hacks.  To list just a few:

WiiRemote interactive whiteboard
Kinect technology
UTouch / MultiTouch

I'm new to Ubuntu (mid December 2010) but relatively well versed in computer hardware and software for an amateur.  I also have somewhat limited time constraints as I work full time and need to be on my toes at all times with my students, plus have a family.  I'm dedicated enough that I'll put in whatever time is required, I just can't go on 3 day coding binges or anything like that.  Ergo, **I thought why not get a discussion going here and perhaps I can develop enough interest that maybe a few of us could work on some ideas as a team or some such and move the thoughts forward?**

Ubuntu is marvelous stuff, we're going through some exciting times with new tech coming out, etc etc, could we not take many of the disperate packages out there and put together a "package" or "bundle" (not sure of the correct term here) that lets a cash strapped school or district or even individual teacher (like me!) anywhere in the world use things like the kinect and wii sensors along with bluetooth and a reasonably inexpensive projector to put a smartboard in every classroom?

Could we not also expand that idea a bit so that it could be used for things like robotics in the classroom to teach math and physics (if you are not aware, robotics is the up and coming educational idea, but its meeting a lot of resistance from "the old school") instead of old fashioned chalk and talk?  You want to see kids sit up and demand learning, put a machine in from of them that interacts with them, can be controlled by hand movements, can do facial recognition, among other things.  They'll immediately want to know how to do it and then will come up with the most amazing things that no one has ever thought of.  One of my students, who is learning programming, math and electronics in my room with a Parallax BoeBot asked yesterday, "Mistuh, if I can do anything, why couldn't I build a robot that could make me a sammich?"

He could.  I have his interest.  His learning curve just exponentiated.  I won that battle, he'll figure out how to do it, he'll learn the math he needs along the way, he'll pass the course and the MCAS (after 4 tries for both) with that math, and he might even get a scholarship with a robot that can make a sandwich.

Kids today are smarter than we ever were (I'm 51).  They have more opportunities etc.  BUT you have to entertain them to reach them. If you have a WOW!!! factor, they WANT to learn.  Most of my kids are really poor, but most of them have access to a computer.  They don't do much on it other than facebook because they don't know what else they can do and they think all software costs money.  If I can show them all the stuff that can be done WITHOUT spending a bunch of money (Ubuntu, GIMP, the list is endless as you know) they can do anything.

Its cool that Ubuntu has all the neat calculators and great education apps, but that's useless unless I can get it up where 30 kids at once can see it.  If I can't make it more than a movie/tv show (read "if its not interactive") they'll lose interest fast and its just a fancy text book.  If they can't use it, play with it, make something with it that does something cool, its just a movie.

I perceive that all the tools are available, but have not yet been put together, are not a comprehensive tool.

If anybody can make this kind of magic happen, methinks tis the Ubuntu community.  I just hope I've explained it well enough and conveyed enough enthusiasm to get some of you out there to understand and want do something about it (Is Canonical listening???).

Anybody interested enough to at least shoot some discussion back and forth?

regards, Richard
