### Post by Therion on 2009-03-12
You want the 'Switcher on your gnome panel, right?

Right-click on a blank spot on your gnome panel, click on "Add to Panel".

Select "Workplace Switcher" aaaaaall the way down at the bottom of the list then move it where you want it on your panel.

Right-click on the Switcher and check the box to "Lock" it into position.


Are you having trouble getting the Desktop Cube going?  Easy-peasy... Just let me know if you need help with it.

### Post by Therion on 2009-03-12
Well, not sure what to tell you then.  You *could* reset your gnome-desktop but that would wipe out a lot of configuration files at the same time.  If you use Evolution, for example, resetting your desktop wipes out your email configuration, meaning you'll lose any email in your folders, your email account setting's etc.  Don't believe it when it says it leaves all your setting's intact.  If that happens consider yourself lucky, but don't expect it.  

If you're okay with that, here's a link that walks you through the process.

[http://linuxfud.wordpress.com/2007/02/14/how-to-reset-ubuntugnome-settings-to-defaults-without-re-installing/](http://linuxfud.wordpress.com/2007/02/14/how-to-reset-ubuntugnome-settings-to-defaults-without-re-installing/)

You might wanna wait, though, for someone else to come along with a less drastic solution.
