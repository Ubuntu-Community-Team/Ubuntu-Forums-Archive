### Post by Dangertux on 2011-07-22
Usually, I am all about security and user privacy, and would not normally offer suggestions on this type of thing. However, I am also a parent, I have 2 young children (young enough to not use computers yet) , however I have thought about some things I might do when they are old enough.

While I would not condone any of this if they weren't your children, I was raised under the philosophy, that you live in my house on my dime you will respect my rules. 

As far as monitoring solutions go I really like the [smoothwall router project]("http://www.smoothwall.org/") (DD-WRT may have something similar to this as well.

Smoothwall has a LOT of built in logging and monitoring , in fact it can even monitor emails and IM's (keep in mind encryption will break this functionality)

Note : Smoothwall runs on it's own computer (you can buy a junker off ebay for less than 100 bucks and stick an extra network interface or 2 in it, which are 10 bucks at Frys or whatever the equivalent where you live is)

For restricting traffic it's important to keep two factors in mind. You want to keep all the traffic on your network. This means you want to restrict the use of proxy servers as much as possible. You can do this a number of ways, I recommend at least as a decent first step blocking common proxy ports, (8080, 3176-3200, 9050 , 8118, etc) you can google a full list. There may be other apps that help you block proxy services. (Note many of these may not bypass web based proxy services , so you will have to watch the URL's your child is visiting and blacklist them accordingly.) Alternatively and much more restrictively, you can whitelist only sites that you approve. This will become, well very obvious very quickly you are doing this.

Make sure that there aren't any other wireless networks in the area that are unencrypted , if there are , you may have to disable wireless access on the machine in question. (Like I said for control of traffic you want all the traffic to stay on your network). If neighboring networks are encrypted just look for activity related to cracking the wireless networks passphrase. IE : downloading aircrack , kismet , etc. If they are encrypted with WPA and don't have an extremely weak passphrase you will have enough response time before the child can crack the passphrase to do something about it. WEP on the other hand you really need to be careful with as those can be cracked in minutes, and believe me kids are creative.

Consider also using public DNS like google's DNS. A good amount of "malicious" sites are blacklisted by default. (This is more of a security thing, but I know they also blacklist sites that traffic in stolen credit card info and the like.)

For hostbased monitoring solutions you should consider using something like Gnome Nanny ([tutorial here]("http://www.linuxbsdos.com/2010/07/13/how-to-install-nanny-on-mint-9-and-ubuntu-10-04/")). As far as keylogging goes, there are plenty of keyloggers out there, you are going to have to experiment with that on your own, since condoning the use of them violates the forum code of conduct.

Hopefully this information will be helpful. While I don't necessarily agree with a "police state" home, I can certainly understand your intentions. Good luck ;-)
