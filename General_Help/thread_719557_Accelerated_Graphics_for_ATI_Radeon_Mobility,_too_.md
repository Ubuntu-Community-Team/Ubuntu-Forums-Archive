### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-03-09
Your card is too old to use the fglrx restricted drivers, so you should get rid of those before trying to troubleshoot anything else.  Check out this link, it has a section on removing fglrx and will tell you how to get the "ati" or "radeon" open source driver setup again - [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver)
Also, can you please post the output of:
```
sudo lshw -C video
```
Even then, your card may not have full 3d support.  My Mobility Radeon 9000 does since it falls into the rv250 category, but your card seems a bit older.
