### Post by Achtung on 2008-04-22
Haha, the one question to rule them all. 

A quick google of "KDE vs gnome" returns just under 4 million results. None is "better" than the other. In the end, it's all a matter of personnal preference. I find KDE to look too Windows-like, so I prefer to use Gnome or Xfce. Then again, you get more visual tweaks with KDE. 

Anyways, if I were you, I'd simply get both the gnome and kde version and test-run them as live CDs before installing. Even after you've picked one and installed it, you can always install the other DE along side the one you originaly picked without affecting your config. So I guess I'm saying ... get both, and see the one you like best!
