I'm typing this on a Samsung netbook (Atom, 10 inch screen, webcam), so probably about the same specs as your netbook.

The netbook is running Ubuntu 10.10, and that works perfect.

I would advice you to live-boot Ubuntu 11.04. Ubuntu 11.04 both offers the new Unity look-and-feel, and the old-skool (Windows-like) interface. You can choose at login.

If you can't boot from your flash, you will need a 2GB USB stick. Or better: 4GB. A 4GB USB stick will cost you 5 Euro / 7 USD.

Most easy way:
[LIST=1]
[*]On a computer with a CD-writer, download Ubuntu ISO. Write to ISO to the CD
[*]Still in the same computer, boot the Ubuntu CD. Choose "test run" (do not install).
[*]When booted, choose to create a Startup Disk to your USB stick.
[*]Then take your USB stick to your netbook, and boot from it. Again, choose "test run"
[/LIST]

You can also download the ISO image on your netbook and write it to your USB-stick using unetbootin ([http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/](http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/)), but I have less good experience with that.

### Post by Ernesto RD on 2011-06-10
My 2 Cents...
Apart from 2 desktops, Theres 2 netbooks in my house, (i need them for my work), both are Dell mini 10v (atom cpu, 1gb ram, 150 GB HD, 10.1" display), Both came with windows xp home, it ran "ok" (once i removed all the garbage dell puts on it), but i find xp home to be too feature-less and old.
The OS i wanted needed to be:
1.- **stable** (this is priority 1)
2.- with good modern features (at least the features/software i need for my job)
3.- as eficient as possible with the limited resources a netbook offers. (fast, long battery life, etc) 
4.- Reasonably easy to configure and use

Since i have 2 netbooks, ibe had a chance to test a TON of oss on them, i would install an os on one of them, and (try to) use it for work for a few days, while the other one was the "backup" in case something went wrong. 
I have tried...
**Ubuntu:** every version starting from 8.04 including all "Netbook remix" editions, and some xubuntu kubuntu, and lubuntu.
**Other linux distros** debian 6, fedora (14 and 15), openSuse (11.3 and 11.4), linux mint (dont recall the version).
**MS Based** Windows 7 starter , Windows 7 Professional, windows xp home.

So wich one met my needs (mentioned above) best?, well, from better to worse, my "Top 3" would be...
1.- Ubuntu 10.04 lucid (standard, not netbook remix)
2.- Fedora 15
3.- Windows 7 professional

To me, Lucid has the **stability** i need (only had one a couple of minor glitches with it) and provides the best balance between features, efficiency, and ease of use. It boots is about 40 seconds, it runs VERY fast, i get about 2:20hrs of battery, and has all the features i need.

Fedora was also pretty nice, but a bit harder to setup and configure (samba was a pain), but i noticed less battery life, more heat, it ran a little slower, and its desktop (gnome 3), as nice as it is, its still a little "green".

Windows 7 pro was rock stable (never had even a single glitch), has all the features i need, and is **very** easy to use. But the computer was very slow.

I also gave ubuntu 11.04 natty a try, and to be honest, my _**personal opinion**_ is that from all the OSs i tried, it was probably the worse. Not only did i not like the desktop, but my computer had quite a few crashes, its battery lasted about 45 minutes (it gives me more than 2 hours with lucid or most others), it ran very hot, and it was slower that it was with windows 7. to _me_ natty was like the exact opposite of what i was looking for, unstable, very slow and ineficcient, and very hard to use.

I suggest you Give lucid a shot, if your criteria of "better" is something like mine, im shure that youll like it.
