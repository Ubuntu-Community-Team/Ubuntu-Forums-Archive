### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-06-21
BEFORE you attempt this, I would suggest you talk to your Work IT department about two things:
1) Is it OK to access your personal computer from Work
2) Is it OK to install personal software on your Work computer

Mentioning this because it's not uncommon for either of these to be a violation of security and/or IT policies -- and companies I've worked for don't hesitate to FIRE folks for violating these policies.

You just want to make sure that it's not going to cost you your job to start down this road.

Aside from your employer's IT policies, you will also have to talk to the IT department about HOW you can safely negotiate the security perimeter without compromising their overall security -- provided they allow for that.  What bandwidth restrictions would apply?

A suggested method for connectivity with your machine in a different location is somewhat dependent on things like that.
