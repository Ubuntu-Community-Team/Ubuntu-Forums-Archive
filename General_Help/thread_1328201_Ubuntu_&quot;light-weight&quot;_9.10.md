### Post by P4man on 2009-11-16
You can uninstall most things that you dont need through the software centre. Thats fairly safe. Uninstalling random packages through synaptic will most likely render your system unusable because there is a ton of packages you do need that you may not know what they do.

You can also do a server install and then add those packages you need (including gnome), but I guess the question is: what do you want to achieve? Are you short on diskspace or RAM or is it just a matter of cleaning up the menu's? Thing is, you wont gain a whole lot of anything by removing those games and tomboy..

I never installed ubuntu server, so im not entirely sure, but I would think if you install gnome on top of it, by default it may still install the default gnome games and tomboy. etc But like I said, I could be wrong. 

Honestly I think in your case the best solution is just removing the apps you know you dont need through the software centre. just click on "installed software" and remove anything you dont want.

If your objective is to reduce disk usage and RAM, you might go with a more lightweight distribution. If you want it ubuntu based, there is xubuntu and crunchbang linux. If you dont mind trying any other distribution your options are endless, ranging from [slitaz linux]("http://www.slitaz.org/en/") (30mb and surprisingly functional) and DSL to puppylinux and at least 100 others :)
