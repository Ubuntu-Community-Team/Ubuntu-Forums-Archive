If you want to use WinKey ([http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_key](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_key)) in XFCE simiraly to MS product(opening WhiskerMenu) or different (e.g. AppMenu), you just need...

A) just open Settings by clicking button in Whisker Menu...

B) find Keyboard in Settings window (Hardware section)
[[IMG]http://imagizer.imageshack.us/v2/150x100q90/843/0nqt.png[/IMG]](http://imageshack.us/photo/my-images/843/0nqt.png/)

C) Next go to tab: App Shortcuts

You will see sth like this:
[[IMG]http://imagizer.imageshack.us/v2/150x100q90/840/dxu.png[/IMG]](http://imageshack.us/photo/my-images/840/dxu.png/)


Now  you see what type of combination means what. You could try what happen  if you press the combination of keys. Ctrl+Esc for me will open Whisker  menu. (default shortcut settings).

D) Look for xfce4... (selected in image)

You  just need double click on the right cell near proper activity. (on red  place:  [[IMG]http://imagizer.imageshack.us/v2/150x100q90/850/tq1x.png[/IMG]](http://imageshack.us/photo/my-images/850/tq1x.png/)  )

Then...  [[IMG]http://imagizer.imageshack.us/v2/150x100q90/840/9u2h.png[/IMG]](http://imageshack.us/photo/my-images/840/9u2h.png/)
...type your combination.
