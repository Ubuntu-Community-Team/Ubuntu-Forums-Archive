### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-03-01
The big difference is the DE that you use; gnome/unity or kde.

Install the OS with the DE you prefer, then install the packages you want or need; they will all work in either DE, though a kde app may bring with it a huge amount of the kde library files, just to allow it to work.

Use what **you** want, not what others tell you is best.
