### Post by paulfromniagara on 2010-02-08
I have a very strange problem. When I open the gedit text editor and press the control key, the text 'hfk' (without the quotes) is inserted at the beginning of the document and ';o]' is inserted at the end of the document.  The exact same problem occurs in jEdit.  If anyone can help me solve this, I would be most grateful.

---

### Post by paulfromniagara on 2010-02-09
Problem solved. My keyboard is a Microsoft Internet Keyboard.  I went to System->Preferences->Keyboard->Layouts and changed the keyboard type to to a Generic 101-key PC and re-booted the system.  All works perfectly.   JOY!

---

### Post by paulfromniagara on 2010-02-09
Another bizarre twist in the story.  The problem is back and I have no idea why?

---

### Post by paulfromniagara on 2010-02-09
Well, I connected a Logitech keyboard and re-booted.  It worked perfectly.  I disconnected the Logitech and re-connected the microsoft keyboard. It now works?

---

### Post by paulfromniagara on 2010-02-09
... and now the microsoft keyboard doesn't work.  Back to a Logitech keyboard and all is well again.  The moral of story for me: If you're running Ubuntu, keep away from microsoft keyboards.  This should probably have been obvious to me as I can't imagine they would put any effort into the cross-platform compatibility of their keyboards.
