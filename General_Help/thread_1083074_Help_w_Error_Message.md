### Post by substanceD on 2009-02-28
Can you see the disk in BIOS? If you turn your computer on and press a bunch of the F-keys (like F2, F4, etc.) in the range of 1 through 6 at the POST screen you should be able to pull up your compy's BIOS. Depending on who made your motherboard things will look different, but you should be able to see which hard drives are connected somewhere, which would tell you if your primary drive really is connected properly.

If it doesn't show up, you should probably just take your uncle's advice and play around with the SATA cable's connecting ends until things work again.
