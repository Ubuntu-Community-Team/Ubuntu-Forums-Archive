### Post by loomsen on 2008-10-16
Hey guys.

I've got two screens, my laptop-lcd and my lcd-tv. 

TwinView basically should be the easiest option, as long as all screens have the same resolution. 

TVs and laptops usually don't. So in this case you have to configure different X-Screens, which isn't to much of a problem actually. I'd always prefer this method to a "workaround" like virtualizing of a large desktop output. Even with Xinerama disabled you'll be able to move your pointer across screens edged to the next one, plus you are able to set up completely independet profiles for each connected screen.

Redirecting output to another screen is pretty simple:
DISPLAY=:0.1 foo-bar
will launch foo-bar on display 1. Given you define an
alias TV='DISPLAY=:0.1'
the cmd even shrinks to TV foo-bar

Here's my xorg which works just fine.

```

EndSection

    Identifier     "Screen0"
    Device         "Videocard0"

    Option         "TwinView" "0"

        Depth       24
    EndSubSection

    Identifier     "Screen1"
    Device         "Videocard1"
    Monitor        "Monitor1"
    DefaultDepth    24

    SubSection     "Display"
        Depth       24
    EndSubSection
```

Simply define Sections Monitor, Device and Screen in each case, and melt it all together in the Server Layout section.

This way you'd be as well able to assign a touchpad to only one screen for instance, or your keyboard to the outer to but not to the middle one, or whatever.

I'd always prefer independent X-Screens to TwinView, as well as manually configuring your X-Server rather than having it reconfigured after already being started or such. 
Twin View is ok, but a workaround in my eyes. Applications having their alignement configured relative to screen edges definetely run rings around TwinViews capabilities. At least I don't want to have to reconfigure my kiba-dock, just because I plugged off my TV.

The line at the left basically is the connection of your peripheral to your X-Server. The X-Server assembles all together then.

As you maybe noticed it's pretty similar to the modular filesystem. You may basically hang in Input and Output hardware to your X, the server provides a gateway melting all together in the end.

Before, you have a BIOS(Basic Input Output System) with your generiv VGA Monitor connected. Only when the graphics adapter are hung in it's starting to get a superior Input Output System.

Alright, what I wanted to say, you most likely will be able to point two monitors to Screen 1 for instance, and the whole Output of the last Monitor to one Screen.

As an option you may specify turn left or turn right (90°), so that you'd basically have one large screen, well, with some plastic in between :)

Got it?

The fact that Xinerama doesn't, and will never work with Composite, except maybe by some ugly workarounds, is just logical. Where should compiz turn the cube to if you had Xinerama enabled? 
Just impossible.

But the way above, with turning the screens per 90° to make it one large virtual Screen should work.
