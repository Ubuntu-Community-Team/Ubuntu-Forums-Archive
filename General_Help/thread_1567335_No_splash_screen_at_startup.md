### Post by Silent Warrior on 2010-09-03
Do you mean having gone from usplash+xsplash to Plymouth, or the splash-screen on logging into Gnome? Since you mention animation, I'll have to assume it's the former case. And, in which case, I essentially don't know how to help you. It's THEORETICALLY possible, I'm sure, but I remember fighting a FIERCE battle with ArchLinux over this. It won't be easy, I can tell you that much... First thing, though, would be to check whether xsplash and usplash are available in the repositories at all. If they aren't, well, Plymouth stays. (And forcefully removing Plymouth is going to break your system in so many ways you'll go cross-eyed thrice, so don't even think about it.)
There are, of course, some other themes for Plymouth you might be interested in. Some are in the repositories, a few can be found on Gnome-look.org. Changing themes will not be easy either, though, and there are very few instructions on Gnome-look. I mean, they EXIST, but I've only seen one or two postings with instructions out of... dunno, nine?
