### Post by jefsview on 2012-09-11
It's a Xubuntu re-spin, a bit large in size to download, but well worth it. It installs like a dream. Uses the same installer as Xubuntu.

It's what comes with it that is the "extra."

It is a French re-spin, but aside from a very few right click on desktop menu entries remaining in French (after choosing English as your language of choice), and a link to French TV application, it's very usable.

I've had it installed since it came out.

I use a HP Media Center Pavilion, at least 5 years old, dual core with 8 GB RAM. Runs like a dream. 

I did ditch Avant Window Navigator, since the Xfce panels do a wonderful job in and of themselves. I did upgrade to Xfce 4.10 through a PPA, and changed the color theme, since I loathe orange. Xfce 4.10 has a new Application Finder, which is a lighter-weight alternative to Synapse, so I ditched Synapse.

Basically, I chose Voyager because it was Xfce (Xubuntu) and because of the wonderful CONKY Control -- over 20 different Conkys to choose from, and by setting them in your start-up panel, you run as many as you like everytime you boot up. Nice and easy.

It's the Best Xubuntu respin in the market, and includes Thunar custom actions, back up applications, a grub customizer and firewall applications. It's all ready to go after installing the restricted codecs, etc. 

Basically, it has more than Xubuntu and is a great starting place for your own customization, since it includes applications you might not of heard of or used previously. 

I hope this helps. 

I haven't looked at another distro since I installed Voyager 12.04.

-- Jeff
