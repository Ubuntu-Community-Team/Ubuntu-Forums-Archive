### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-09-10
You probably want to give OpenVPN a try.  You haven't given us much in the way of specifics about the operating systems on these computers or where they're located.  OpenVPN supports Windows, Linux, and OS X.  For a simple point-to-point tunnel, take a look at [this document]("http://openvpn.net/index.php/open-source/documentation/miscellaneous/78-static-key-mini-howto.html") for a start.  If you're new to all this, you're in for a lot of work.  Maybe the company should just purchase an off-the-shelf solution and have it installed by a consultant, or buy a pair of VPN routers like [this one]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833124160") and try setting it up yourselves.

Unless you and your employer value your time at some ridiculously low figure, buying a pair of $200 routers with commercial support makes a lot more sense to me than your spending a person-week or two trying to cobble together a solution on your own.
