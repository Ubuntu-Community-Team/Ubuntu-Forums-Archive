### Post by &#1058;&#1086;&#1084;&#1080;&#1094;&#1072; on 2012-08-08
I can share my experience with you. I've been using windows for 20 years before I tried my first Ubuntu. And it took me about 15 minutes since I first saw its login screen to fall in love with it, without knowing a single thing about it before that.

I first tried the USB persistent install, because I was a bit scared. Of course, it turned out that I had no reason for being scared, but I can recommend a USB install to anyone who's coming to Ubuntu from Windows for the first time. This is how you do it:

1. Download an Ubuntu .iso of your choice. I recommend using torrent to download Ubuntu Precise Pangolin 12.04 64bit. Here's the link:
[http://releases.ubuntu.com/12.04/ubuntu-12.04-desktop-amd64.iso.torrent](http://releases.ubuntu.com/12.04/ubuntu-12.04-desktop-amd64.iso.torrent)
2. Download "Universal USB Installer" from [http://www.pendrivelinux.com/](http://www.pendrivelinux.com/) . While you're at it, take a look around the "Pendrive Linux" website, it's full of useful hints.
3. Run "Universal USB Installer" and follow the instructions. It's very easy and shouldn't give you any trouble.
4. Plug in your USB stick to any computer and reboot from that USB.
