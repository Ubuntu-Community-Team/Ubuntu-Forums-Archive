### Post by audiomick on 2010-03-08
Without knowing the exact context I have to generalise.
The phrase means "as it is delivered". To say something works "out of the box" means that the device can be turned on and operated without having to take any extra action. To say that a wireless card works "out of the box" with a particular OS indicates that the OS includes appropriate drivers for the card, finds it correctly, and is able to use the card without any extra installation or adjustment required.

### Post by The Cog on 2010-03-08
Yes. it means that the necessary drivers are included in Ubuntu so the wireless adapter will work straight away - no messing around installing adapters needed. My 3 wireless boxes are Intel, Athreos and Realtek and all of these worked that way - just install Ubuntu and choose a network to connect to (maybe I've just been lucky). On the other hand, you see lots of posts here from people having difficulty getting Broadcom adapters to work, even after downloading their copyrighted driver software.
