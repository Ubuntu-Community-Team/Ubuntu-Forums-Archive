### Post by poodoopealeoap on 2011-11-24
The others above me are right. Lubuntu and Xubuntu are really light distros that work really well on slower computers. The thing about it though is that you want to make sure that you get the right balance of speed with functionality. I wanted my computer to "rape" when it came to speed, so I tried Xubuntu and Lubuntu. I really liked how everything was extremely fast but a lot of their programs weren't so great. Using these distros will have you sacrifice your Libreoffice/Openoffice. Also, you will have some really low graphic versions of all of your other programs to help lower HD space usage and ram usage. Also, it is quite a bit harder to customize these distros. So, again, these distros are extremely fast and efficient... but you also have to be willing to lower your standards when it comes to certain programs, graphics, and customization.

p.s. you can always install xubuntu or lubuntu, uninstall the default programs you don't like, and install some of the regular programs that work/look a lot nicer.
