### Post by audiomick on 2010-02-15
As far as the sound quality goes, firstly, you will never get top quality sound using mp3 files. It is a lossy format, i.e. the file compression process loses data, and, in my opinion at least, they just don't sound good.

Having said that, you will probably get better sound from a cheap laptop if you use an external sound card rather than the one that is in it. I can't make a recommendation, but I believe Edirol makes affordable USB audio interfaces that work ok. I have a firewire interface from Presonus for my acoustic analyser program that I am happy with, but I haven't tried it on the Ubuntu machine yet. The program runs on the windows laptop.

You should have a look here
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsSoundCards](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsSoundCards)
regarding support for any card you might consider.
