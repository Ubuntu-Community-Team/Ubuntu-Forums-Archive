### Post by jonobr on 2009-06-23
I feel your pain brother....
My laptop was stolen 3 weeks ago,.

I spoke to my brother about it (he is a cop) and I mentioned I was concerned about passwords and cracking etc and they could do this or that or the other. My mind was running away with a lot of thoughts.

He said , (I think he is right), Im giving them too much credit,
He said it was probably some opportunistic crack-head who handed it over to someone else in exchange for a quick fix.

The guy who receives it will probably wipe it and stick it on ebay or craiglist.

So, if your running ubuntu, the criminal has to know how to use the cd to change the password (not figure out what it is) to be able to get at your stuff. However, for piece of mind, I changed passwords on everything. 
I watched my accounts in the bank closely in case I had my passwords stored, and so on...

### Post by Cheesemill on 2009-06-23
Change all of your passwords.

All someone needs to do is boot up in recovery mode and they can change your password and gain full access to your system.
If you use webmail and that password is saved in your browser then they have all they need to change the password for any of your online accounts. This data could be worth alot more than the re-sale value of the laptop.

Chances are the thief / new owner will wipe the drive straight away but better safe than sorry.
