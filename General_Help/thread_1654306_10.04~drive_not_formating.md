### Post by mcduck on 2010-12-28
Also note that if the drive is formatted in FAT or NTFS, it will not support permissions and ownerships as Linux uses them. Windows filesystem aren't able to handle such things.

So you can't use chown/chmod to change the ownership or permissions on FAT/NTFS. Instead they are set for the whole partition at the time when it's mounted. And what permisisons are used for that filesystem are defined in /etc/fstab file. If your partition doesn't have entry there, you should add one.

(also, since you didn't tell anything about what you are actually trying to do, I'll also add that optical medias are always read-only filesystems...)
