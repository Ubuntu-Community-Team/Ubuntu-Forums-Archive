### Post by AusIV4 on 2008-09-19
I just tried downloading the free mp3 codec from the Fluendo store. I believe they use the same delivery mechanism, and just have it listed as 0&#8364;. After making the "purchase" I was taken to a page where I could download the codec. It came in the form of a .tar.gz, so you could easily archive it to be used later. It also said I had 100 downloads remaining. I'm not sure if they offer 100 downloads for the paid codecs, but I suspect they'll let you download more than once.

Regardless, if you decide to make the purchase, I'd just tuck the installer safely away somewhere so you'll still have it next time you upgrade your system. Their terms of use says that the license is on a per computer basis, so there should be no problem with re-installing after upgrading the same computer.
