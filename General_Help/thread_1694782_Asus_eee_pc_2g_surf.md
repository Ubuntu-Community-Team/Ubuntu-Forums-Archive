### Post by guilleme on 2011-02-24
Hi, I would recommend anything lightweight, maybe using GNOME is a bit too much for the computer, but you could try with Xfce or something like that. 
If your'e looking for a specificall distribution, why no to take a look into qimo linux ([http://www.qimo4kids.com/)?](http://www.qimo4kids.com/)?) It's meant for kids, but it is a lighterweight ubuntu, offering everything ubu can do.
Another choice would be puppylinux ([http://puppylinux.org/main/Overview%20and%20Getting%20Started.htm](http://puppylinux.org/main/Overview%20and%20Getting%20Started.htm)), which is also very lightweight ([http://puppylinux.org/wikka/MinReq](http://puppylinux.org/wikka/MinReq)). 
good luck!
