### Post by StuartN on 2010-03-14
Is there any simple method of previewing a sample piece of text in a particular font? Without any font substitution or character-mapping rules? On my Ubuntu desktop?

The problem is to preview how an electronic book will appear on the electronic book reader device, using either its own (very limited) built-in fonts or specific fonts embedded in the ebook.

I tried some font applications that allow working with font files (ttf, otf, etc) that are not installed - FontyPython shows one line of modifiable text, the gnome Font Viewer does not (seem to) have modifiable sample text, FontForge has a very elegant and lengthy multilingual sample (but not obviously modifiable) and the remainder display the installed system fonts. None can display a page from an ebook.

Firefox, Calibre, Open Office etc can all display a page from an ebook, but have some very clever substitution rules that I see no method for disabling. They always manage to locate foreign character glyphs and substitute them - which the ebook reader device can not do.

Quite simply, I have some foreign-language texts and want to preview the readability in different fonts, one at a time or side by side. I can then either embed the font in the book or transfer it to the reader device and adjust the stylesheet of all my books to share one installed font.
