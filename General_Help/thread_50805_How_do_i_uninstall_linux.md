### Post by aggiechemist on 2005-07-21
I'm not going to give a full explanation here, since this is something that a lot of people are interested in doing. Since so many people do it, there are already a lot of forums and web pages giving much more detailed information.

What you want to do is usually referred to as "dual boot." Use that as a search term here in these forums, or go try some other places (for example google or linuxquestions.org).

Generally you will use that restore disk to wipe the hard drive and install windows. You will want to be sure to divide up your hard drive so that you have a space for windows and a space for linux. There are many forums that discuss this in good detail, and can give you hints on the best way to achieve the desired result.

Like I said, a great search term is "dual boot." A few minutes research with that should get you some info. Another good term is "partition," which is the name for the process of dividing up your hard drive. I did a little searching myself and found [this](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=13042)  as a starter site.
