### Post by bossaline on 2008-11-08
I do not know what the problem is with the Ubuntu developers, but we have a serious problem at hand that keeps getting ignored time after time again. Yes, the finger can easily be pointed to the Gnome developers, but it is the choice of the Ubuntu developers to include the gnome-screensaver, rather than xscreensaver.

Now you may ask yourself, what is such the big deal? Well I can tell you that if an operating system is not capable of providing settings for something as simple as screensavers, that does not tell me much about what else the OS may provide. Granted, screensavers are not what make a distro what it is, but come on now and get with the program. I shouldn't have to hack the distro immediately after the install just to get these settings. I mean, at one time I remember Ubuntu did use xscreesaver instead of the gnome-screensaver. What was the big deal that this had to be changed?

I don't care what Gnome thinks that the user should only have the default settings for each screensaver. This is wrong. Ubuntu isn't created for what Gnome thinks people should do. Ubuntu is created for anybody and everybody in the whole world. The authors of the screensavers do not create options just to be hidden and not used. As I said above, I shouldn't have to hack the distro everytime a new version is released. The screensaver settings should be working flawlessly out of the box.

I also search for a fix for this issue every single time a new version of Ubuntu is released. Only one time I was actually able to find something that pinpoints this problem head on. Unfortunately, the thread is two years old now, and I am sure the fix is not identical to what it should be now. Here is the link though: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.p...saver+settings](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.p...saver+settings) Now that link doesn't work anymore, but here is my initial post in regards of the same issue, but for 8.04: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=791922](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=791922) I am going to continue to post this message each time a new version is released, but only until this solution is fixed.

I am still curious how many more versions will be released before this problem is fixed. If you do not think this is a problem, then you are in major denial. It's sad that I have to use Kubuntu to get screensaver settings to work out of the box, unless that has changed recently too, on top of I prefer Gnome more than KDE. This also prevents me from introducing new people to Ubuntu and Xubuntu that are not as computer savvy as I am. Don't let the Gnome developers limit the screensavers settings, and start to make everyone happy. If and when Gnome does provide settings within gnome-screensavers, that is when you make a permanent change to it.
