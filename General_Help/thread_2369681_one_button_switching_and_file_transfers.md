### Post by QIII on 2017-08-25
While virtualization sounds like a great idea for those of us with the money to run over to Fry's and purchase componts to build machines with loads of resources, others may not be so fortunate.

OP:  the way you are proposing the solution will not work.  Short of virtualizing one of the machines, two OSes cannot run simultaneously on the same machine.  That means that you would have to shut down and reboot.  You can do so relatively easily by ensuring you boot to your Linux drive and then update GRUB.  This will allow you to choose either OS at the next reboot.  That is not "last century" at all.  Virtualization takes a certain amount of resources and some machines simply don't have enough.

Would you please provide the specs of both machines.  This will give us some information to guide our advice.

Cheers!
