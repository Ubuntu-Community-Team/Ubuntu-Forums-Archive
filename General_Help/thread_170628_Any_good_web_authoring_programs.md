### Post by mattotoole on 2006-05-05
Simple text editors do not have the site building and management tools that programs like Frontpage, Fusion, and Dreamweaver do.  These programs will create new pages and add links to all the others with a couple of mouse clicks.  There is nothing like this for Linux.

Nvu, Amaya, and I think Quanta will do WYSIWYG editing though.

We all have our favorite ways of working, and many people work like this.  Making comments to the effect of, "all you need is a simple text editor, stupid," is not only unhelpful, but a bit obnoxious.

FWIW, I use Bluefish, after prototyping site structure and pages with Citydesk (a Windows program).
