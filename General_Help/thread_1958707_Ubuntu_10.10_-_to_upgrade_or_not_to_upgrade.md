Thanks!
1. Do nothing means no security updates. So if you are connected to the internet, it is a very bad option. (Otherwise it would be OK, as long as you don't want or need some new software.)

2. Since upgrading is stepwise, except for LTS versions, which also jump between LTS versions, it would mean 3 steps to the version that will soon be released, 12.04 LTS, Precise Pangolin.

10.10 - 11.04 - 11.10 - 12.04 LTS

I see little meaning in upgrading to 11.04 now, and it is too slow and risky to upgrade in three steps.

3. I suggest that you do a fresh install of 12.04. Either wait until it is released (before the end of April), or now, starting with a good beta version, depending of how you use your computer.

- If it is important that it works all the time, wait until May to install, and allow a week or two to get rid of the worst bugs.

- If you are curious and can accept a few bugs in the beginning, install 12.04 beta now.

* There are a couple of extra alternatives (this is what I'm doing now)

a. Try some or all of the flavours of Ubuntu live (no install, just boot from CD or USB) with different desktop environments
Lubuntu, ultra light-weight with LXDE
Xubuntu, light-weight with XFCE
Ubuntu, heavíer than 10.10 with Unity or gnome shell, new look
Kubuntu, powerful and heavy with KDE 

b. Create and/or use some free space of a drive and install 12.04 as dual boot and test it before switching.

you will be able to upgrade directly to the new LTS, however, you may get new bugs and might find that you need to do a fresh install anyway. you will likely want to wait an extra 2-3 weeks just to wait out the initial flood of bugs that come with a new version
