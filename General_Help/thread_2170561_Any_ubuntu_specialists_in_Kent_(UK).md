### Post by eternal243 on 2013-08-26
Well, if you are going to pay someone to sort it out for you, then it really doesn't matter much where they live, all they need is SSH access to your laptop and some good Linux experience. I live in Sweden and have gotten help several times with different Linux problems from a friend living in the US. It all comes down to trust, but that would be the same even if you asked someone close to you to come by and fix it for you. =)

**EDIT:** SSH stand for "Secure Shell" and is basically a way to remote control other computers over the internet. You can read more about it on this link.
[http://www.ubuntulinuxhelp.com/how-to-ssh-on-ubuntu-a-simple-guide/](http://www.ubuntulinuxhelp.com/how-to-ssh-on-ubuntu-a-simple-guide/)
