### Post by Holger_Gehrke on 2015-01-19
The word you were missing is 'duplex' or 'duplex unit'. That's what the gadget that turns the printed page over and feeds it back into the printer is called.

I just tried "hp laserjet p1102w duplex" in google. So now I know -- from checking 2 different (old) reviews of the device -- that it doesn't have a duplex unit builtin and a short check on hp.com showed me that they don't offer one as an accessory. You'll have to do it manually by printing the odd pages, turning them the right way, putting them back into the printer's paper tray and printing the backsides. From one of the two old threads that came up when I searched here on the forum I know that the windows printer driver offers some support for manual duplexing. Because of the architecture of the linux printing system, this kind of thing is very hard to do. Manual duplexing is the application's job not the printer driver's; Libre office for example can do it.

### Post by dragonfly41 on 2015-01-19
To answer your question on search strategy ..

On any google search page you will find "advanced search" option.
There you can refine your search pattern.

For example[INDENT]all these words ...
this exact word or phrase ...
any of these words ...
[/INDENT]

You can wrap phrases in quotations

and eliminate unnecessary phrases (such as "HP LaserJet") which might cause too many hits.

So we can narrow down the pattern to 

"double side print" NEAR "P1102w"

Or as written in post above use "duplex print".

If you get no relevant hits on google you can also try alternative search engines such as [www.altavista.com]("http://www.altavista.com") (now Yahoo).
