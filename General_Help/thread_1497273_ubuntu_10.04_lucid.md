### Post by colorlessprism on 2010-05-30
on any computer system it is a very good idea to have some sort of backup strategy. wether its burning to disc, daily incremental, or full blown full backup, no system in the world is above hard drive failure.
that being said you should be able to copy everything you want to keep to disc/USB. once you have done that try the upgrade option. by upgrading you shouldn't have to worry about losing your picture, music, etc but it can happen. since everything is backed up even if you have to do a complete reinstall you havent really lost anything. 
i prefer to use remastersys, its pretty easy to install if you follow the instructions, and when its used you can create a fully installable backup. that being said, remastersys is pretty limited to the size of what it is backing up so i use rsync to augment remastersys. 
by having a good backup stragety in place it can reduce a lot of concern when upgrading (i usually do a complete re-install). i was able to put a new hard drive in my laptop and with use of my remastersys backup in conjunction with rsync i was running in no time (you would never even know i had reinstalled)

[Remastersys Homepage]("http://www.geekconnection.org/remastersys/ubuntu.html")
[Rsync Homepage]("http://samba.anu.edu.au/rsync/")
