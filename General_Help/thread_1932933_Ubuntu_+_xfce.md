### Post by snowpine on 2012-02-28
If you're currently using Ubuntu, then you don't need to reinstall at all (that would be a waste of time & bandwidth), rather just install **xubuntu-desktop** from the Software Center and you will have the option for Xfce on your login screen.

If you want to ditch Gnome entirely then: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexfce](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexfce)

### Post by MARP1961 on 2012-02-28
Xubuntu is XFCE with extra 'trimmings'. Why don't you just keep Ubuntu and install Xubuntu Desktop? At login you should then have a choice of Xubuntu or plain XFCE. You could try them both.

I'm not particularly keen on the Xubuntu mouse/rat styling so prefer XFCE as a start point. However, under the skin they are both essentially the same and can be customized almost as well as the old Gnome 2.
