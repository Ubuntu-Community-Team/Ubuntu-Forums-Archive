### Post by sf-it-services on 2010-06-22
Computers will normally come with an OS installed, or pre-broken as I call it when Windows is involved.  A computer without an OS isn't really a good computational device, its just a BIOS box.  Some manufacturers will sell machines without the OS but most retail stores will not.  Windows 7 is useless, I had a copy over a year ago and have gone back to XP Pro on my dual boot machine now.  

{K}Ubuntu will fit nice on most laptops now, you could get specs and then check for compatibility and drivers but its all almost sorted now on that front.  If you buy a new machine and do not want windows then before you start it up for the first time put a live CD in the Optical drive and boot from that.  Don't risk making your hardware dirty.

Yes there are ... but you have to look for them.  

Look in any of the Linux magazines and you will find advertisements for vendors selling machines preloaded with Linux distros.

As with MS Windows, they preload their machines with their own selection of distro.  The good is that you get a machine guaranteed to work; the bad is that THEY choose the distro.  Not a whole lot different from buying a machine preloaded with MS Windows -- just a lot cheaper.
