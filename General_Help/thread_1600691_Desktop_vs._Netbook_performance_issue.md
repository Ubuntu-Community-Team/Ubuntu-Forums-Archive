### Post by 3Miro on 2010-10-19
That is not backwards at all, actually it is exactly what you were supposed to do. Netbook edition is for the small netbooks (as in Intel Atom CPU), you have a laptop, so you need the regular "Desktop" edition. In the Linux world, a desktop PC is just a laptop PC that doesn't have a battery and is constantly plugged into the power. As for the netbook edition, as far as I know, the main difference is the interface, which is supposedly optimized for small screens, however, many people are having trouble with the new netbook edition, Intel seems to have messed up the drivers, among other things, so many people use the desktop version even for their netbooks.
