### Post by Macchi on 2010-01-19
My two sons (now 7 and 10 years old) have been using Linux, and more specifically Ubuntu, for almost four years now. Here are some thoughts and experiences:

SOFTWARE
They appreciate Gcompris (completed with Gnucap and Gnuchess), along with Tuxpaint, Tuxmath, Tuxracer, Supertux and Memaker if I recall correctly. For school and other tasks they use OpenOffice, Evolution GoogleEarth,  Firefox, and Skype.  

FILTERS
For some time I had their systems filtered though Willow but it was not very practical to maintain. Lately I have been customizing OpenDNS as a filter to avoid exposing young kids to the sometimes very raw contents of the internet.

NEW PLATFORMS
Unfortunately, after upgrade to Karmic 9.10 their systems became unstable and I had to rewind their computers to 9.04. For some time I had to fight against instabilities and now I am sad to inform that since Christmas 2009 we all moved to two iMacs and two MacBooks. Mac OS X became the main computing platform in the family. My kids still use Windows in their school and have Ubuntu on their netbooks, but for the moment the Mac is the winner and I hope that Ubuntu and Linux will be able to make a comeback for them as a cool platform.
