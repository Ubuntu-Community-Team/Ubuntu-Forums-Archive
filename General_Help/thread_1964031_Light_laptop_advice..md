### Post by cortman on 2012-04-23
I borrowed a friend's netbook for a couple months. Hated to give it back. For all that tech writers et al proclaim that netbooks are dead, I found it to be immensely useful, and there are still many good models on the market.
I got an Acer Aspire One 722. I upgraded the RAM to 4GB and put a 120 GB SSD in it. It came wtih a dual core 1.33 GHZ processor, so I am very nearly ultrabook spec, for nearly a third of the price.
Things I like about the Aspire one:

11.6" screen with 1366x768 resolution = incredible detail- this resolution is actually slightly higher than the original iPad or iPad2.

Small, fairly thin and light. Very portable.

I've had the best experience with Fedora and Ubuntu 11.10 on it.
