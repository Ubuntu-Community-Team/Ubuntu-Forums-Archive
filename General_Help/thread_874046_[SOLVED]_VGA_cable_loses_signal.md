### Post by ModelM on 2008-07-29
I bought a 2 meter vga extender cable once & the monitor I was using didn't like it at all, the image was horrible. Another monitor I had, though, worked fine with it. So, at least some of it seems to be monitor dependent.

I'm sure, though, that 50 feet would be out of the question. Some folks even have trouble running video signals through a kvm switch.

Those store displays you see where they have piped a signal to many monitors at once, sometimes far away from the computer, are accomplished by running the signal into a special amplifier/splitter box & then out to the monitors.

### Post by eival on 2008-07-29
yes thats true. analog video will lose quality over distance no matter how shielded the cord is, anything longer than 20 feet should use an video amplifier. i work at an AV company an we use those anytime someone askes for 50'-100' cables
