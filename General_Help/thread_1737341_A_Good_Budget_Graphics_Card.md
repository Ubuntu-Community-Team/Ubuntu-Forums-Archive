### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-23
Are you asking for an advice for a new card?

Nvidia has historically the best Linux support. You will be able to get the maximum amount of effects and performance with Nvidia + the Nvidia proprietary driver. However, the drivers takes some time to load, so you may not be able to get the fancy bootup effects.

ATI has historically the worst Linux support, however, this completely changed once AMD took over. As far as effects go, you can use an ATI 4xxx or newer with the default driver and get all the fancy effects.

However, if you want to play wine games, Nvidia is still your best option.

### Post by coffeecat on 2011-04-23
I have an ATI Radeon HD4350 PCIe card. It works completely reliably with the default open source driver in Lucid, Maverick and Natty. I get 3d and compiz, and Unity runs just fine with it. I don't play games so I haven't tried the proprietary fglrx driver. No need - the open source ati driver supports compiz just fine.

A couple of bonuses. Mine is passively cooled = SILENT. And in the UK you can get one for between £25-30. You don't say where you are but I would guess in the US you would pay the same number of dollars.

Oh yes, translucency is fine. Plymouth boot screen is fine, that is not corrupted the way you get with the proprietary driver.

And I can play HD videos with no lagging or stuttering. Could you ask for more? :)
