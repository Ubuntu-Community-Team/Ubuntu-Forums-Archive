### Post by ninjaaron on 2011-05-02
I don't know how many people know this, so I just thought I'd tell everyone. The Panel works perfectly well in Unity, and you can make special hidden panel with whatever you're missing from classic interface (menu, window list, etc.) without missing out on the new features. It works just like it does in classic mode.

If you wand to try it, hit Alt+F2 and type [FONT="Courier New"]gnome-panel[/FONT]. works nice.

If you want to keep it, put the command in "Startup Applications."
