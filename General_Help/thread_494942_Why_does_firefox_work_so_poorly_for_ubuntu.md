Well... maybe not the truth, but my theory.

Firefox is clunky crap on Linux (be it on GNOME or KDE) because Mozilla developers concentrate development mostly on Windows.

Keep in mind that when a project starts platform-centric, it is much harder to port to another platform, much less maintaining it stable.

One of the reasons for the IceWeasel fork was because the Debian developers kept fixing bugs on Firefox for Linux but Mozilla didn't find it a priority to place developers analyzing these patches.  Mozilla had other priorities.

What other priorities?  Windows.

Why Windows?  Money.  Mozilla receives money from every Google search made from the Firefox main page and, I think, from the search bar.  Guess where are most Firefox users?  Windows, even though Fx is an open-source application.  And you gotta please your users.

OK, don't shot me.  It's a theory, although I think that it's very likely the cause.

### Post by Ubuntiac on 2007-09-30
Trouble is... I'm getting it (slow scrolling, tab changes etc) with my otherwise beautiful Nvidia card (w Nvidia driver). I've been hunting this bug for a while, and it seems to me that:

**1.** It's more than just firefox (although Firefox seems the hardest hit). Other browsers seem affected (Swiftweasle, Gran Paradiso, Opera, Konqueror).

**2.** It's more than just the video driver. The person above is using ATI. I'm using Nvidia. People (inc me) have drivers working fine *and* have this problem.

**3.** It's more than just running Beryl / compiz. It happens even when using default window managers (Gnome/KDE)

**4.** It mainly seems to affect page redraws such as tab switching and scrolling. (Suggesting not IPV6 etc)

**5.** So far, reports of this bug seem limited to Feisty / Gutsy (Gutsy Beta being current at this time)

**6.** Xorg usage, often seems unusually high (70%+ for some people, 100% for others) EG see [bug 145696]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/145696")

**7.** It's not an underpowered PC thing. There are at least two cases of FF _in Windows XP under VMware_ running much faster than _natively in Ubuntu_.


My guess is that it's some combination of the above such as a bug in Xorg that is affected to a greater or lesser degree by how apps output their page rendering with Firefox hit hardest. Some people have also suggested that a bug in the recent kernals may be affecting those running XGL (which as I understand was switched on by default in a recent Gutsy update).

Whatever it is, **it would be useful for people to post their computer specs on this thread for comparison**. I will as soon as I'm back on my usual computer.

Cheers

Ubuntiac
