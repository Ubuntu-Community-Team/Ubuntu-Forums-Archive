### Post by SeijiSensei on 2013-01-30
Go search YouTube, and you will find tutorials on how to hijack someone's Yahoo mail account.  I manage some email listservers and have seen both Yahoo and AOL accounts compromised.  The mail was sent from your webmail account; your computer had nothing to do with this.

GMail seems the most secure of all of these, especially since they introduced two-factor authentication.  (E.g, if you submit a request to change your password, a code is sent to your mobile phone or another email address that you must enter to confirm the request.)  Still even GMail isn't bulletproof, as you can see by reading [this blog post]("http://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2011/11/hacked/308673/4/") by well-known journalist Jim Fallows at *The Atlantic*.
