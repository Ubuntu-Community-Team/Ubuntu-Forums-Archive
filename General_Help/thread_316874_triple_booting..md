### Post by igknighted on 2006-12-11
Well, to be honest, suse has a very pretty graphical GRUB, which has better OS recognition thatn Ubuntu.  Also, you get the benefit of easy management of GRUB via YAST.  Therefor, I would just let SUSE overwrite GRUB with its own and use that, It recognizes Ubuntu just fine and Win XP shouldn't be an issue either.

EDIT:  I absolutely love SUSE 10.2.  No major bugs, hardware detection is second to none, and it's got all the usual suse perks.
