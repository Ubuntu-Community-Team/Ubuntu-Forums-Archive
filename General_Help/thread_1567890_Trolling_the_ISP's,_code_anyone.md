Tests of bandwidth at the near end don't give you information on where the bottleneck is in the path, may be due to your ISP, but may be due to something entirely unrelated to your ISP,  upstream from your ISP. Your ISP is selling you a "pipe" with the ability to run at a certain bandwidth, not guaranteeing that your connection will always be saturated. Matters also what the other end is capable of putting out and how it is routed through the Internet and anything that affects transmission along the way.
