### Post by t0p on 2009-07-06
According to the [OpenPrinting database]("http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Lexmark-X4650"), your printer is a *paperweight*.  That basically means that it's useless for anything other than sitting on your desk looking pretty.

I echo what ktp said: Get Hewlett Packard!  I've had endless problems getting other printers to work.  Even printers that are listed in the OpenPrinting database as "mostly working" with Linux have refused to print.  But HP are pretty damn good about getting their printers to work with us.  So get yourself an HP - even a cheapo one will be okay I reckon.

**EDIT:**  When you want to find out if a certain printer works with Linux, [www.openprinting.org](www.openprinting.org) is the place to go.  They have an extensive database of different manufacturers' printers and their compatibility (or otherwise) with Linux.
