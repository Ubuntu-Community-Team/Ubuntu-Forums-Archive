### Post by paradigm2 on 2009-05-19
You might consider Linux Mint Fluxbox community edition:

[http://www.linuxmint.com/rel_felicia_fluxbox.php](http://www.linuxmint.com/rel_felicia_fluxbox.php)

> 
System Requirements

    * x86 processor
    * 128 MB of system memory (RAM)
    * 2 GB of disk space for installation
    * VGA graphics card capable of 800x600 resolution
    * CD-ROM drive


Mint is based off of Ubuntu.  Fluxbox is a very lightwight but no frills.

Another alternative is to do a minimal ubuntu install and then add things only selectively (such as X and Fluxbox).

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD)

However this will be difficult for anyone who is not at least an intermediate user.

Another option is either Arch Linux (Intermediate to Advanced)  or Gentoo (Advanced to Expert).

IMO, Linux Mint Fluxbox edition will probably give you the best results on your machine with the least work.

### Post by Bartender on 2009-05-25
A 12-year old PC that's crashing and acting weirdly.  It's highly probable that a new OS is not going to solve things.

You should open this thing up and blow out the dust bunnies with a can of that expensive air, or an air compressor set to a very low setting (20 psi or so).

If it were me, I'd also pop the CPU heatsink off, clean the old thermal gunk off, reapply thermal paste, and put it back together.  Unless this is one of those dopey cartridge style CPU's.

Also replace the watch battery on the motherboard.  If it's never been replaced it's got to be almost dead.  BIOS will reset to default when you do this.

I bet the power supply is original too.  Good chance that it's failing.
