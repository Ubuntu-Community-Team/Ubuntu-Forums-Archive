### Post by HereInOz on 2013-01-16
The first question I would ask anyone wanting to buy a printer is:

How many pages a month are you going to be printing?

The answer to this will determine whether you buy a cheap printer with a high cost per page, or a more expensive printer with a lower cost per page.
Remember when printer manufacturers quote page costs, or number of pages per cartridge, they use a standard coverage of 5% on the page.

Example.  A cheap Brother black laser printer may cost you, say, $80 but will cost you 10 cents per page (at 5%).  A more expensive Brother black laser printer will cost you, say, $300 but will cost you 3 cents per page.

Do your sums and work out how long it will take you to pay back the more expensive printer due to the lower page cost.  That gets 90% of the decision made for you.

Colour lasers, on the other hand, can be horribly expensive, but the rules are the same.  What is your cost per page, how many pages are you printing, and how much does the printer cost.  

Just be careful with some colour laser printers which quote drum life in "impressions" rather than pages.  An "impression" often means one colour on the page.  Hence, a full colour print will be four "impressions".  So a drum life of 30,000 impressions can easily become 7,000 - 10,000 pages, if you are not careful.
