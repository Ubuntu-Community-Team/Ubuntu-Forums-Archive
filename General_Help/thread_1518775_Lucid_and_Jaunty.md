### Post by stderr on 2010-06-27
Whilst I haven't yet updated to 10.04, one wouldn't normally expect much performance difference between versions. 

IMO, your CPUs are likely to be affecting performance far, far more than running different versions of Ubuntu. Celerons are considerably slower than P4s in many ways anyway (they're the cheaper range: far less cache, that kind of thing), and you've got a considerable frequency difference too. Also, it's possible that your desktop has more/faster RAM, a hard drive that spins faster/has more cache, etc.

Have to see what some 10.04 users have to say too...
