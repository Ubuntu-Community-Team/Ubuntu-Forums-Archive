### Post by mrsteveman1 on 2008-02-25
If you really want to you can get an integrated wlan router with a dsl port on it, but they are rare. Linksys makes a cable version of one of its routers but I'm not sure about DSL. Cisco for sure makes one, they are somewhere around ~$600, which might be a bit much for this sort of situation.

A better idea would be to just make sure that your DSL modem is simply providing a clean ethernet connection instead of PPPoE (which i hate). If you can do that, by putting the dsl into bridge mode, you can simply ignore it and pretend you have an ethernet line coming into the house (which is mostly true).

Linksys made rock solid routers with the WRT54G boxes until they started using vxworks and cheaper hardware. I have a few V2 and V4 boxes, and they outperform the newer V8 boxes in every way, the reception sucks on those things in part because they have bad antennas.
