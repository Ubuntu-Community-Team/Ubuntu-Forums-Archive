### Post by wirelessman on 2006-11-06
I am going to build a new computer and install Ubuntu 6.10 on it.  My question is, can you copy my complete "Home Folder",from an old Ubuntu 6.06 computer, to a USB hard drive, then copy it to the new computer? Will it move my emails? Are there files from my old computer that don't need moving?

In other words, what the best way to move to a "new" computer?

---

### Post by 23meg on 2006-11-06
Moving your home folder will do it. If you're using Evolution, your mail is kept in the hidden directory ~/.evolution/mail . The ~/ bit represents the path of your home folder.

### Post by turbojugend_gr on 2006-11-06
Yup, moveing your ~ will do it, but I suggest if you only need ur emails, you should only move the ~/.evolution or ~/.thunderbird fodler, as moving all ur home means you take all the configs along... SO the best choice is to move all ur desired app folders, usual ~/.APPLICATION_NAME is the appropriate fodler but that may vary.
