### Post by drs305 on 2008-10-23
I usually go to [http://support.asus.com/download/download.aspx?SLanguage=en-us]("http://support.asus.com/download/download.aspx?SLanguage=en-us") to check for BIOS updates. I enter the information (motherboard, socket and model), linux, and then go to the BIOS page. 

The numbers you will usually see will include the most recent and previous BIOS versions. You see your number but you may see a higher number, which would be a more recent BIOS version. For mine, I can see 1502, 1604, 1701. If you had 1502, you could download either 1604 or 1701 to update to a more recent version. If you downloaded 1502 you wouldn't be changing anything.

The standard caution is that updating a BIOS is not without some risk and should normally be done only if you are having problems or have a specific reason to upgrade it.
