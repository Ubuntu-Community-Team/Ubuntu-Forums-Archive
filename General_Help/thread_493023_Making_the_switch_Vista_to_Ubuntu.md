### Post by amoore on 2007-07-05
Gaming and graphics just are not up to speed in Linux. Thats not to say that some games and design software can't be emulated or suitable Linux software replacements can't be found. I would propose three suggestions. 

1. Create a duel boot system with both Windows and Ubuntu. Slowly make the change from proprietary hell to OSS.

2. USE THE RIGHT TOOL FOR THE JOB! Sometimes Linux does "X" better, Sometimes Apple does "X" better, Sadly sometimes Windows does "X" Better. 

3. Ask lots of questions and be ready for change. Linux is not Windows or Mac. Don't expect Linux to operate or work like Windows. You may be a Windows Super User but if your new to Linux then your a Noob. And thats OK!  Use the forums, wiki, or Google. Most importantly be ready to invest time to learn how Linux works. Just know that answers don't always come instantly, you may need invest some time to find what your looking for.

Ubuntu is by far my favorite OS. Ubuntu is very powerful, highly customizable, flexible, dependable, "free",  and best of all has a great user community. 

Linux is growing fast!!! Good look jumping in to Ubuntu
