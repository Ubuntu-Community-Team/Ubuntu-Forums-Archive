### Post by Kabamaru on 2007-02-17
If your current plan doesn't work out, here are two thoughts:

1) I have not used these Gmail features myself, but they sound promising...
- Gmail allows you to forward your mail from Gmail to another account
- Gmail allows you to forward your mail to Gmail from another account
- This would mean that you could (at least in theory) forward one of your Gmail accounts to the other, receive notification of new mail as you have been, and all replies would be sent from the correct account.  The question will be whether or not Gmail will allow you to do this with another Gmail account (as opposed to other email providers).

2) My current method of managing multiple accounts and receiving notification of new mail...
- Running an email client.  Thunderbird works well for me, although you may like other clients.

### Post by villindesign on 2007-02-17
I second the Mail Notification suggestion. I use it for only one Gmail account, but I know it can manage more than one. I use ```
mail-notification --sm-disable
``` to start Mail Notification when I login.

> mail-notification features include:
                 * multiple mailbox support
                 *  mbox,  MH, Maildir, Sylpheed, POP3, IMAP, Gmail and Evolution support
                 * SASL authentication support
                 * APOP authentication support
                 * SSL/TLS support (disabled, see README.Debian)
                 * automatic detection of mailbox format
                 * immediate notification (depending on your settings)
                 * HIG 2.0 compliance
