>   So I am also wondering if anyone has a good recommendation for a raid controller that is easily supported in Ubuntu.

You should take a moment to read about RAID in these forums.  My **STRONG** recommendation is to use the softRAID build into Ubuntu.  I use it on my server and it works great!.  Most MBs come with a SIL labs fakeRAID chipset that gives your SATA and RAID 1+0.  STAY CLEAR OF PROMISE TECH.  Their hardware rocks and is over powered for most average users, and their driver department sux @ss.  No Linux support at all!

The point is, read up on the dozens of posts from other people wasting weeks trying to get RAID card X working when the softRAID works extreamly well!
