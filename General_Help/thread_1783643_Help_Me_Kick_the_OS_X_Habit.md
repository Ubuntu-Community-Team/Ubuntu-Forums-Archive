I'm coming back to the distro from an absence of five plus years spent on OS X and a peppy little MacBook Pro. The lappy was becoming very thoroughly unusable due to overheating issues, and when looking for a replacement on a budget, let's just say that the House of Jobs was no longer in the picture.

Anyway, I've not found myself missing too much about OS X. Very nice...and I haven't been able to find a blessed thing to replace it!

This is a bit of a deal for me, as I have multiple GMail accounts for sorting different types of correspondence, and I've become spoiled by having the logout/login process automated for me. ### Post by Thewhistlingwind on 2011-06-16
No offense, but I think what you really need at this point is a full featured mail client like Evolution or thunderbird. (Evolution is preinstalled, thunderbird will need to be installed.) 

Since these exist, theres probably no replacement for what you describe.

However, a mail client has extra features that make it IMO better then a wrapper to the native interface. 

Including:

Spam filters on your terms.
Abillity to backup to Mbox format
Offline reading of emails, for when you don't have wifi
Mailing list managers.
Plugins
