### Post by Mahngiel on 2009-12-31
Let's get a bit more indepth, shall we?

While yes, the only safe computer is one powered off, Linux distributions are primarily much safer due to the root powers you mentioned.  Root powers is the way of the system to make changes  to the important files, and these files are the ones that viruses target.  As you've noticed if you spent even five minutes on Ubuntu - or any other distro for that matter - you need to enter a password for any changes you make effecting the system; this is a safety measure.  In windows, for example, you can go into your C:\ folder and delete anything you want to, without any security measure, this is what viruses target. Once on your system, they can do anything they please, which is what makes them dangerous.  So, due to the fact that you have to enter a password to modify essential parts of the system, it would make it damn near impossible for a virus to function.

Another key note:  a lot of people despise microsoft, and for various reasons.  Therefore, their malice is turned to any users of the system, and they create distructive programs.  This is part of the reason why Mac can boast it's security- not so much that it's invulnerable, it's just that people don't devise viruses for them. Don't get me wrong though, if somebody DID want to get a virus on your system, they 'could' do a bit of damage, but mostly only your personal files, i.e. music and photos... but that's why you back-up often, ya? :)

Anyways, you can do just about anything on Ubuntu that you can do on Windows.  Gaming seems to be popular, and there are avenues to game, but it's nowhere near the capabilities of windows/mac yet, which is why most linux users still dual boot.

Hope this provides some answers.  Enjoy your Ubuntu. :)
