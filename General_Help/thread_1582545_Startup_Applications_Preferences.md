### Post by 2CV67 on 2010-09-26
Without going to extremes, I like the ideas of Ubuntu reacting quickly & of not carrying around unnecessary junk.

Frequently, I see invitations to eliminate unrequired applications starting up automatically.

So it is good to find a simple-looking tool to tidy things up.
System > Preferences > Startup Applications.

But from there to being able to decide which of the 23 items to remove...
[IMG]http://i838.photobucket.com/albums/zz309/2CV67/apps1.png[/IMG][IMG]http://i838.photobucket.com/albums/zz309/2CV67/apps2.png[/IMG]
Explanations like
- GNOME Keyring: PKCS#11 Component
- PolicyKit Authentication Agent
- Load module-x11-xsmp into PulseAudio
- GNOME Keyring: Secret Service
don't really help decision-making, at least for me.

Not having Bluetooth, I guessed I could do without that one.
Not using Evolution, that was another.
With a desktop, I assumed I didn't need Power Manager?
I took a chance on Gnome Login Sound & Remote Desktop.

But, even after a lot of Searching, I could not find decent information on the other items.
Best sources I found were:
[http://ubuntu-virginia.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=8329072](http://ubuntu-virginia.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=8329072)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1464833&page=1](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1464833&page=1)
but even there the explanations were minimal at best and the opinions sometimes contradictory.
Of course opinions can be contradictory, especially for different circumstances.

It would be really useful (not just for me) if there was a reference document somewhere (maybe this thread?) which could list, for each potential application:
- What it does, in comprehensible terms
- What happens, or doesn't happen, if you uncheck it
- Which conditions (Desktop / No Bluetooth...) make it totally unnecessary
- Which conditions make it absolutely necessary, or strongly advisable

Any starters?

Thanks!
