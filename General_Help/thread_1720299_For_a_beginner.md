### Post by Topsiho on 2011-04-03
The main difference between Ubuntu, Kubuntu and Xubuntu is the window managers, the way the graphics are put onto the display, and for Ubuntu and Kubuntu the ***default*** applications that go with it.

Xubuntu is the lightest of the three, for low powered computers with less ram. Ubuntu and Kubuntu need 512 MB of ram or more to run well, Xubuntu a little less, you should google this for yourself (as in fact anything you don't know :) ).

All applications that are in the repositories work in all three flavours of Ubuntu, just install them, and their necessary dependencies (other programs and libraries they need) are installed automatically too.

The best you can do depends on how adveturous you feel. Ubuntu 11.04 is a stable release, which lasts for 3 years (until April 2014), 10.10 is the present newest release, which will be supported 18 months (from October 2011), and 28 April the 11.04 will be released.

To ***my*** taste the desktop of Kubuntu needs some getting used to, and the Gnome desktop of Ubuntu is much simpler and therefore the better one.

I am now experimenting with some other distro's such as Fedora, Suse and some others, and am astonished how far they have fallen behind Ubuntu. No one runs without some tweaking, which is not for beginners. Ubuntu runs most of the times flawlessly immediately after installing, but not always.

Topsiho

### Post by linuxnewb2 on 2011-04-03
Might want to give linux mint a look. As a fellow beginner having heard so many great things about ubuntu for linux newbs from guru's. I of course installed it, though found it's close cousin mint along the way. Switched to linux mint after checking ubuntu 10.10 out for awhile. Just overwrote mint onto ubuntu's partition.

Mint seems a lot more user friendly to me. The people who made ubuntu 10.10 went out of their way to distinguish it from winblows. Think they went a lil too far. Panel/toolbar on the top of the screen, many things reversed. Gonna leave any windows folks who try it, scratching their heads and aggravated by the layout.

Mint is more in keeping with the desktop layout for a migrating windows users or linux newb ( like me ) than ubuntu 10.10. I'm really liking mint though and it's based off of ubuntu. Like somebody else already said and I agree. Would stay away from latest release for awhile too. Go with one of the LTS versions if you're going to pick ubuntu. Let the people who know what they're doing find and work out the bugs of the latest distro.
