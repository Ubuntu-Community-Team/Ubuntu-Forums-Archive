### Post by VCoolio on 2010-08-14
It's the same list as on the left pane of nautilus (the file manager). Right click it there and delete. The panel will pick up on that, at least after the next login or maybe "killall gnome-panel" (the panel will automatically respawn after that).

### Post by scorp123 on 2010-08-14
You can correct the location if you want to. Open your Nautilus file manager, e.g. ***Places > Home Folder*** (or whatever that would be in Dutch)

.. then when Nautilus is open there should be a "Bookmarks" menu in its menu bar. So you go:

***Bookmarks > Edit Bookmarks ... (Ctrl+B)***

And voila, a new small pop-up window should have opened allowing you to correct and adjust the properties of every location.


I hope this helped ... ?
