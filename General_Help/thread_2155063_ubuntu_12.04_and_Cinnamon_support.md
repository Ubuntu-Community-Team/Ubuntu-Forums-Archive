### Post by craig10x on 2013-06-17
Unity was designed by Canonical for ubuntu, so it is and will be the officially supported UI of the main edition...Originally they tried to work with gnome when it was designing the gnome shell for gnome 3, but from what i read, although they were willing to work with Canonical they weren't open to adopting any ideas they would come up with....thus, ubuntu went it's separate way and developed Unity which i think is much nicer then what gnome came up with....All unity is really a dock on the left side of the screen, so apparently you are not into docks...i'd imagine you probably don't like the mac's UI either...
And aside from the unity dock, there is also a global menu (like the mac) and the "dash" search which is also kind of mac like...

Other desktops environments like Cinnamon session and Cairo Dock and Gnome Fallback session will work in ubuntu and are set up as separate sessions, but don't expect ubuntu to go with Cinnamon or any other desktop...Unity is not only for desktops, but for mobile devices, tvs etc...

As far as others being turned off by unity...some are....when it first came about in 11.10, there were probably many that thought the way you did, but i'd say the majority came around and very much enjoy it now...
including myself...
