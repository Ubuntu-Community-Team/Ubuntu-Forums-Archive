### Post by crazyness003 on 2008-11-12
False. LCD is much better for your eyes. The way CRT (Cathode Ray Tube) works is that is shoot electrons out from the back of the tube to the screen at 60 or 70 Hz. These electron particles are like radiation. The same effect from sitting in front of an old tube TV. 

LCD on the other hand basically has a Florescent tube that shines thought the liquid crystals (hence Liquid Crystal Display). Much safer for your eyes, since they don't actually emit a lot of harmful dosage of radiation.

Check out [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube)
and
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_crystal_displays](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_crystal_displays)

### Post by cb951303 on 2008-11-12
there are a lot of things to consider

Personally, for my eyes, most important 3 properties of a monitor is

1. Refresh rate: This is probably the most important among 3. Higher refresh rates are easier on the eye.
2. Luminosity: Too much luminosity hurts my eyes. Especially at night.
3. Contrast rate: I don't think it's directly related to eye health but if the monitors contrast is low I often find myself closing my eyes a little bit to see more clearly. Even though it's a reflex and has nothing to do with better vision, I still do it and it hurts my neck and causes headaches.

I cannot comment on 2 and 3 but I know CRT monitors have higher refresh rates...
