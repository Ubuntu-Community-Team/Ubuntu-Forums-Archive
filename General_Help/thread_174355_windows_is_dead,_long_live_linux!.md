### Post by pellgarlic on 2006-05-11
i just have to say, thank lord tux for linux. 

i've just emerged into fresh air from a long, tiring tunnel, involving a traumatic motherboard replacement scenario which has served to complete my transformation to the "dark side" (read: liberation from the cruel clutches of an inflexible and stubborn operating system into the welcoming bosom of freedom).

i already had a dual-boot set up with windows xp and ubuntu 5.10, and wanted to keep both operating systems, as i had use for both. as is my ritual, i prepared backups of both os's partitions, using acronis true image, and i also made an additional backup of my ubuntu partition using this method: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=35087&highlight=tar+backup](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=35087&highlight=tar+backup) , just in case.

having been through numerous motherboard, processor, hard-drive, graphics and sound card upgrades before, i expected to run into problems - that is par for the course with such a substantial change as replacing a motherboard, and i also expected windows to be the worst culprit, but i wasn't ready for the reality of it. 

i thought i might need to do a bit of d.i.y. repair on the ubuntu installation, hence the two backup options. in actual fact, ubuntu didn't even skip a beat - it just booted as usual, as if i hadn't done a thing! i thought this was a possibility, due to some information i had learned about linux treating the hardware it runs on as generic 386 equipment, but i was (pleasantly) surprised by how well it handled the change.

windows, on the other hand, went apoplectic! as i said earlier, i expected to have some problems, but not this: the Black Screen Of Death....

now, i consider myself a bit of an "expert" when it comes to whipping windows into shape - when members of my family have computer problems, it's me they come to for a fix, and i can usually come up with the goods. i also spend the vast majority of my time at work dealing with various hardware or software related problems, but this time windows threw me a curve-ball.

i've never even heard of this "Black Screen Of Death" before, but when i tried to "repair" my xp installation after installing the new motherboard, up popped this ominous message: "TRAP 0000000D ====General Protection Fault====", accompanied by a slew of other "information", and further attempts to perform the repair produced yet more daunting errors. 

i duly resorted to my favourite reference manual - "google" - to find a cure. finding dire messages about corrupted software and registry hives, and tedious, laborious methods of fixing it, i threw up my hands and declared "that's it! i'm through with windows! i've had enough!"

this experience really cemented my already low opinion of windows, and showed me how linux shines in comparison. i have been a slow convert, but taking the time to learn the art of linux has really paid off. now, instead of spending the next few days/weeks/months trying to resuscitate the terminally damaged windows installation, i simply forget about it, and don't feel like i've lost a thing. (well, except maybe macromedia studio, which i must admit, i prefer to gimp :) maybe i just need to get to know gimp a bit better though...).

so ends my saga, and they all lived happily ever after in linux-ville, where things just make sense.
