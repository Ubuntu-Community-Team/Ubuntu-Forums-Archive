### Post by rscott5 on 2007-09-04
Download speeds can vary based on the torrent being downloaded (how many people are seeding/downloading at the same time as you). Also, your ISP may be throttling your torrent download speed. This link has a pretty good list of ISPs and how they treat torrent downloading: [http://www.azureuswiki.com/index.php/Bad_ISPs]("http://www.azureuswiki.com/index.php/Bad_ISPs")

That being said, if you're ISP isn't slowing your torrents, maybe something else is. Are you using a wired/wireless router? If so, what kind? I have had issues with Linksys routers which say they support UPnP (Automatic Port Mapping), but don't do it very well. You may need to log into your router and forward your torrent port (in your case, 50000) to the local ip address of your computer. I've found that to be very helpful.
