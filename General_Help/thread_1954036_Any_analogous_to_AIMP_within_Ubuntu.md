### Post by Trollercoaster on 2012-11-01
What I (personally) like most in aimp is that it instantly plays mp3. Double click and go.
Missed that "feature" waaaay too much in modern media players.
Too much messing up with libraries. Mostly unneeded.
I've been giving rhythmbox and vlc (re)tries for a decade now...
(The latter is pretty nice for videos, although it seems to me that every change is for the worse.)
Madplay uses fixed point arithmetic, like aimp does, but (besides the command line part) I don't know if there are similar results in the aspect of quality.
There must be a frontend of it, of which I am unaware.
Used to love amarok. Became too heavy for my old athlon.
Xmms resembles good ol' winamp, but seems totally out of environment. Not bad to give it a try, though.
I would recommend mp3blaster, but that's a totally different philosophy, and requires that you tolerate a pure console environment.
If the quality of audio decoding is what you like about aimp, since it is totally custom, I believe that it is difficult to find something similar.
