### Post by Gizenshya on 2009-04-16
so.. you reformatted...

did you do a quick format, or a full format?  If you have a large hard drive, and you did a quick format, you *might* be able to get some of the files back.  but it would take a lot of scan time on a file recovery program.  I recommend taking out the drive and slaving it in another computer.  It is always best to do file recovery in that manor.  You can run testdisk from the master drive, on the slave drive, and transfer files to the master or some third drive.  I've used testdisk before with great luck, but the drive wasn't formatted-- xp just got screwed horribly somehow, and no other program could access the files.

But, to be honest, I'd put the chances of you getting your files back at 10%.  and that is probably too high...  and if you did a full format, you have no chance.  unless there are multiple partitions, and you only partitioned one of them... and the data happened to be on another...
