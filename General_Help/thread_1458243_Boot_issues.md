### Post by colorlessprism on 2010-04-19
the majority of people who have this issue are able to fix it with a BIOS setting. it seems the people who have this issue have "memory hole remapping" enable (note: there are variations to the name of this setting i.e. memory mapping, hole remapping etc). try working through each of your bios options and maybe you will find something to this effect and disable it. i would look under "chipset features first" with any luck this will get you going. otherwise we may need to look deeper
