### Post by fallingleaf on 2008-12-03
I have found that the thing that slows FF the most is Flash content.  Flashblocker add-on might be worth trying.  You can enable Flash content when you want it, but not have to deal with a lot of Flash-based ads, which are the source of all evil in the universe.  I've also had a problem with Google Reader.  The browser gets very slow after I've gone through about 100 articles.  My workaround is to just refresh the page every once in a while.  This is probably due to Flash content in the feeds.

### Post by fallingleaf on 2008-12-04
I just remembered a couple of other things that helped speed up Firefox for me.  I uninstalled the Firebug extension, which is known to cause big problems.  Also you should search in Synaptic for "firefox" and see if any of the extensions you use are in there.  Installing the Web Developer extension from an Ubuntu package instead of from Firefox's addon manager made a big difference for me.

Edit: Make sure you review what's being installed in Synaptic, though.  Some of the packages are for Firefox 2 and will automatically install that as well.
