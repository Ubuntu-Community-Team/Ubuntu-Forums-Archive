### Post by DirtyPC on 2011-01-07
put this into terminal:

sudo mv /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.oldconf

this saves your exising xorg.conf

now open a new one:

gksu gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf

when it opens, enter this

Section "Monitor"
	Identifier "Monitor0"
	VendorName "Sony"
	ModelName "SDM-E96D"
	HorizSync 28 - 80
	VertRefresh 56 - 75
EndSection

Section "Device"
	Identifier "Device0"
	Driver "fglrx"
	VendorName "ATI hd4200"
EndSection

Section "Screen"
	Identifier "Screen0"
	Device "Device0"
	Monitor "Monitor0"
	DefaultDepth 24
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth 24
		Modes "1280x1024"
	EndSubSection
EndSection

That should do the trick, just replace the resoultion with the one you want (native), and change the make and model of your screen if you want to (where mine says sony sdm..)
