### Post by celem on 2008-12-02
I have found my winner! I am migrating to 100% Linux, thus a Palm solution was truly needed. I have a Sony Cleo SJ-22 plus two maintenance spares (given to me by friends), so this IS the PDA platform that I use - besides it does everything that I need, and more. One application that was a MUST for me was a password manager that I could use on the Palm AND the desktop. For years I have used Clicklite's TopSecret program but there was no conduit for Linux, only Windows.

My solution is Keyring for Palm OS:
[http://gnukeyring.sourceforge.net/](http://gnukeyring.sourceforge.net/)

I use jpilot on Ubuntu and the Keyring conduit permits me to view/modify my passwords on the desktop as well as on the Palm. A perfect solution. By the way, I use jpilot instead of gnome-pilot because gnome-pilot syncs to evolution and evolution's calendar is unusable for me since its printed monthly format relegates the weekend to two unsable small boxes. Evolution is totally work oriented and didn't suit my purposes. So I removed evolution, loaded Thunderbird and jpilot - perfect for me.
