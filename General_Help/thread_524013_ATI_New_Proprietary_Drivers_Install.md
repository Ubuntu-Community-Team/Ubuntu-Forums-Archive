### Post by pyrokenx on 2007-08-21
unfortunately the quality of ATI's proprietary drivers for Linux are not so great, hell, they arent typically so great for windows half the time..  Because of this cedega does not officially support ATI cards, although much does work on them, there is alot which does not..  This is generally due to ATI's Linux drivers being vastly inferior to NVidia's Linux drivers.  They dont support all acceleration features as NVidia.  Sorry I cant be of much more help on this.


oh and in regards to the restricted drivers.. the Open Source drivers available for Linux DO NOT support anything abve teh 9xxx series, and they only really support fully the  Radeon 9200 and below.  the proprietary drivers have better support on 9700 and above cards however support is very incomplete.  This is why restricted drivers are necessary.

I am sorry to say that these circumstances are more or less out of control, they are improving with every day of development but very slowly.  ATI is not being very helpful in documenting their chips or assisting open source developers, and they are not producing very high quality proprietary drivers of their own.. This has made alot of the progress made on the open drivers a result of painstakingly complex reverse engineering of the cards.  A very tedious process.  Until we see more help from ATI its not likely to improve, but theres hope on the horizon as AMD has said before that there are plans on improving the drivers and perhaps opening them up.
