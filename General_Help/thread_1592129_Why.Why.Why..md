### Post by mrinehart93 on 2010-10-10
I ran 64-bit on my desktop awhile back, but I accidentally messed it up and had to reinstall. Unknowingly I reinstalled a 32-bit version. I have to say though, the 32-bit version seems to be quite a bit more stable than 64-bit, and I have had everything working perfectly. What you can do though, to make up for not being able to get more than 4GB of RAM,is to install a PAE-enabled kernel. I installed a PAE-enabled kernel on my 32-bit install, and I can see all 4GB of my RAM.
