### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-07-31
> **houshdaran said:**
> I have recently moved to an area where seemingly only a wireless internet connection can be used;but I need to buy a wireless network card and it is somehom expensive.

I think you're getting confused between two things:

1. Wireless networking. Basically, you have a wired broadband connection (such as Cable or ADSL) and a router in your house, that allows you to access your own internet connection and network from anywhere in your house.

2. Mobile broadband. Wireless internet that runs off mobile phone towers.

If you're talking about moving to an area where "only a wireless internet connection can be used" then it seems like you're talking about an area that doesn't have cable or ADSL; an area where you can only use mobile broadband.

USB dongles for mobile broadband are only about $99 from your carrier (well, here they are). Some mobile broadband plans may have a free dongle included, if you're going down the 'plan' route.

I'd highly recommend getting an internet connection to use with Ubuntu. Otherwise you can't really download packages - you can conceivably do it on another computer, but it's a LOT of messing around.
