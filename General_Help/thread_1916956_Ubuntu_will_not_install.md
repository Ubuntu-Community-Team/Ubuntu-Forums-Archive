### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-01-29
The "lots of errors no matter what you do" scenario you described is often an indication of a failing, or failed, hard drive.

If you can remove the hard drive from this PC, you should do that and connect it, via adapter, to another PC and see what is on the drive.

IF the drive won't mount, or if there is no filesystem it can find, then likely, the drive itself is trashed.

Also, if you can connect the drive to a Windows PC, the major drive manufacturers generally provide a downloadable utility you can run to check out the status of the drive.
