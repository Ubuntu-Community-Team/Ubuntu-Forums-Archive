Yes, a possibility but extremely unlikely.
Car stereos with CD player typically support CD-Audio and some will also support a data CD with MP3 files on it but that not a given unlike the CD-Audio support. 

Either the OP's device doesn't support MP3s on a CD or they must be doing something very wrong now or, at least, using usupported formats, too high bitrates or not actually MP3 files.

You may want to try removing any art work embedded in the mp3 file, if it has any. I found that some mp3 players (probably the older ones and in car players) do not play mp3 files containing art work. You would need to use a mp3 file editor to do this. Just a thought.
