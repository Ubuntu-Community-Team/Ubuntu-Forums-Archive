### Post by TokyoGhost on 2015-10-02
Browsers are RAM eaters, so its logical they will consume more and more RAM per tab opened. Make sure you are not running anything heavy on several tabs, which can cause slowdowns.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-10-02
Browsers are now full operating systems.  They are RAM hogs, they have applications, add-ins, widgets, social-networking, notifications, adware, and they grab your data for future use.  I have a 2GB laptop and after an hour, the RAM is full and hitting swap.  Try clearing cache, closing firefox and then reopening.
