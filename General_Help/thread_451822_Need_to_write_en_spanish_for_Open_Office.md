### Post by hartz on 2007-05-25
Don't know about the spell checking, but to get the keyboard, you should go to the Menu item for keyboard/layouts.  I don't have ubuntu in front of me and it looks differently in xubuntu, but the keyboard layout tool allows you to select/set up additional keyboard layouts.

Find and Add the "US Intl WITH DEAD KEYS" keyboard from the menu down along the left-hand side of this applet.

Also right-click on the menubar and add the panel/desklet/dock-app/whateveryoucallit for the keyboard indicator.  Clicking it will switch between your active/selected keyboard layouts.

When you have the "with dead keys" keyboard active (It shows a US*), you can type
~n and it will produce the "n-with-~"
Many other sequences work similarly.  Examples are 'a or `a or ^a or ^o, etc.
To produce a normal ~, ', ^ etc just tap space after entering the symbol, or switch back to the normal keyboard layout.
