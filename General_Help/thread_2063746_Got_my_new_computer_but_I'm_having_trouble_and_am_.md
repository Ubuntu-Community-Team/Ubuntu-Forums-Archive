### Post by MutantJohn on 2012-09-27
I'm assuming that a Netgear adapter is something like what I have, a USB plug-in that gives your computer wireless. So it sounds like you just don't have the proper driver installed for you adapter. It's an interesting problem because to get the internet working on your computer you need the internet.

You need to find an internet source and go the website of your adapter's manufacturer and find the correct driver software for your adapter. Then you need to copy it over to your computer and install it.

I only claim all this because I bought a wireless adapter for my desktop and when I first started using it, the kernel I was using automatically supported it. But I upgraded kernels the one day and found that it no longer supported my adapter. Luckily, I had the driver software downloaded from when I was too dumb to know I didn't need it at the time.

So I lucked out, installed the driver and now it all works. Sounds like you might have to do the same.
