### Post by devosion on 2010-05-09
Ok this is a dumb question im sure with an easy fix, but I need to be able to login to terminal and I can't seem to find an easy way to do it from the login screen, or elsewhere that I would normally find in an older version of ubuntu. I need to run fsck with drives unmounted so some assistance would be greatly appreciated.

---

### Post by lisati on 2010-05-09
Restart your computer and hold down "shift" while it's booting. You should then be able to access the grub2 menu and recovery options.

---

### Post by x C0MMAND0 x on 2010-05-09
Another way is to hold down "Ctrl - Alt - F#" (Where # is 1-6, F7 is graphical interface).

---

### Post by Lucky75 on 2010-05-09
You can also just swtich to one of your tty sessions by hitting <Ctrl><alt><F1-F6> once you get to the login screen. Then kill the x-session if you want. Or you could just use a live CD.
