### Post by falconindy on 2009-10-31
ext3 and ext4 filesystems **do** become fragmented over time, but at nowhere close to the rate of fat or ntfs. The best way to keep an EXT filesystem healthy is to keep it below 85-90% capacity so that the journal has room to grow.

Just for the record, though, there does exist such a thing as an online defrag utility for ext4.
