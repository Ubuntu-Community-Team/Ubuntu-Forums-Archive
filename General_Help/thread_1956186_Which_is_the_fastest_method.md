The two options I've considered:
1, install a faster HDD (not sure that this will make much difference given the 3Gb/s spec of the motherboard)
2, install a second device to store the OS and use my current HDD as the home folder.

I have considered (software) RAID 0, and have experienced good results, but the boot speed seams to suffer.

### Post by forrestcupp on 2012-04-10
One of the best ways I've seen to get the most bang for your buck is to have a smaller SSD (solid-state drive) to run your OS from, and buy a larger HDD for storing all of your data.

### Post by PhantomTurtle on 2012-04-10
You should get an SSD, one with a small capacity will do. Then you can store your home folder in your HDD. SSD's will give you a great performance boost. Even though they are expensive, they are worth it.
