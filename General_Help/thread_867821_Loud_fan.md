### Post by squaregoldfish on 2008-07-23
Sounds like a lot of vibration, which is either from its fixings coming loose, or more likely the bearings in the fan motor. If you open up the case and the fan isn't obviously loose, it'll be the bearings. In that case, you'll have to replace the fan. May I suggest a quiet one (eg [www.quietpc.com](www.quietpc.com))? They're a tiny bit more pricey, but they're well worth the money.

Steve.

PS No affiliations with QuietPC.com - it's just a good place to look, and I love having a quiet PC.

### Post by tagra123 on 2008-07-24
DIY for the do it yourselfer.


I've quieted a several fans using graphite powder (for locks) and a drop of sewing machine oil.

Pretty simple. Pull the sticker off the fan inside there is usually a rubber seal (not all fans have this. Then put a puff of graphite on the bearing and a drop of oil. Spin it a couple of times and put it back in. Clean off any stray oil or graphite where the sticker goes with a paper towel. If the sticker doesn't want to stick -- tape works. I've found the oil on a q-tip works very well for cleaning the dust from the blades too. 

There are many posts about this here and there on the internet
