### Post by p_quarles on 2007-12-23
Pidgin is the same thing as Gaim, just a new name. AOL threatened legal action against the dev team violating the AIM trademark, so they changed the name. When you try to install Gaim, you're actually installing a "transitional dummy package" that points to Pidgin. So, basically, there's nothing to be fixed there. 

The other part of your question, though, I don't have an answer for.
