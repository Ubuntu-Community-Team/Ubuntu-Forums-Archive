### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-11-20
It's a new system and there are no released tools for it yet.

9.10 is not an LTS release. 9.10 gets features out the door so people like me can develop the supporting tools for the LTS release (next April).

There are many posts on this, and other sites, providing different themes and instructions for using them. Google is your friend there.

I installed kde-desktop and configured kdm as the login manager. It has lots of themes available and it still knows about XDMCP capabilities, unlike the default 9.10 display manager (gdm).

KDE is kinda big, so make sure you have disk space before jumping on that wagon.

### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-11-20
Adding kde-desktop, and configuring kdm, will change your login experience.

At the login screen, instead of just have "Gnome" as a session option, you can select "KDE" and see what that's like. (Different, pretty, cool, terrible menu structure)

Select GNOME and it'll be what you're used to.

From KDE, you can change the login themes and they even let you grab new ones (from kde-look.org, I think) from the interface. (Menu -> System Settings)

Then you can log off of KDE and log ionto your GNOME session in style.
