### Post by gdonwallace on 2010-01-08
You can actually download it from Mozilla's website.  There is nothing wrong with that.  The only real issue is that there is a some work on your part to get it installed and running.  As opposed to using synaptic to install and maintain.

That would be the only reason I can think of that someone would advise against installing a download from Mozilla's site.

Other than that, there should be no problems.  I do it all the time and have absolutely no issues.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-01-08
If I remember correctly, one of those files is simply called firefox and is a executable binary, or link to the binary, that runs when you double click it.  You can either copy that file to one of the directories in your path, such as /bin or /usr/bin and then make a launcher for it, or you can leave it in your home and make a launcher for it there.
