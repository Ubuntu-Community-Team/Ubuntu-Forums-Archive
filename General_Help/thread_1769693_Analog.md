P.S. CRT monitors are analogue devices. VGA is used to connect to them and as there are still many CRT monitors around VGA connectors are standard on both graphic cards and monitors even those monitors that are digital devices. It is a simple working system that can be used as a fall back. With VGA the digital signal in the computer is converted to analogue for use on an analogue (CRT) monitor. When VGA is used with a digital monitor the analogue signal has to be converted back to digital. So, using VGA to connect a digital computer (which they are) to a digital monitor requires two unnecessary layers of signal conversion.

P.P.S. this could also be caused by a failing graphic card. Is it overheating? Or by the wrong setting in the display driver configuration utility. What graphic card does the machine have? Does it use a propriety driver? Does it come with a driver configuration utility?
