### Post by etienne@Rofti on 2011-10-29
[SOLVED!]

Dear all. This is a kind-of workaround, but it works well makes life easy.

I make use of the FreeSerif font, which has full, and well-implented, Ancient Greek support. Then I installed the Ezra SIL font, (which can either be downloaded from the internet, or installed via the ttf-sil-ezra packaged in Software Center).

Then, in LibreOffice Writer, I go to Tools>Options, and under Language Settings I enable CTL. Then, I go to Styles and Formatting, and make the CTL font Ezra SIL at 10.5pt size, which corrosponds to the 12pt size that FreeSerif is set to.

(Alternatively, I can use the Ezra 10 pt size in conjunction with the Linux Libertine 12 pt size.)

It works, but it is a work-around nevertheless, and I hope someday that Libertine or FreeSerif will support Hebrew as well as they support Greek.
