### Post by WorMzy on 2010-06-17
Are you specifically trying to learn how Ubuntu works, or are you interested in Linux in general?

If you want to get down and dirty with Linux in general, then I recommend installing a barebones distro like Arch, and install everything from the ground up. You'll learn much more that way, but it's a lot harder to get support when something goes wrong, especially if you don't know what you're doing.

Ubuntu is a great distro for people who *don't* need to know/care about how things work, as it will (almost always) work right out of the box, giving you a pre-configured desktop environment that you merely have to tweak to get things the way you want them; plus there's a veritable army of support volunteers here to help you when something goes wrong.
