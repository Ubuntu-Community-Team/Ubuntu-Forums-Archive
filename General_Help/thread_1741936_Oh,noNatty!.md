### Post by hwttdz on 2011-04-28
It's probably safe as in little risk of data loss.  There was a major interface change, and a lot of people are having difficulty adjusting to unity (personally if/when I do upgrade I'm going to continue to use gnome, if you do this the changes aren't nearly so monumental. You will also be able to update later, by doing 
sudo do-release-upgrade
at the command line.  Graphical interfaces sometimes confuse me so I have no idea how often that might or might not prompt you, but the command line will always be there for you.

Generally speaking, there will always be problems when a new update is released.  The developers try their hardest to ensure the OS works as flawlessly as possible, but there are a lot of different hardware configurations out there.  It is thus inevitable that some people will experience some problems as it is  neigh on impossible for the Ubuntu developers to ensure the release works perfectly with everyone's computer.  But they certainly try their best.  

And as wilee-nilee says, the forums are the first place people head to when they are met with trouble, and the last when they are met with success.  

On a side note, what you can always do of course, is download and burn an .iso image of Natty and try it in a Live session.  If everything works well there then you may as well install it.  Whether you do that through an upgrade or fresh install is your choice, but: 1) as wilee-nilee, *always* perform a thorough backup before you do anything and 2) ensure your system is fully up to date before doing the distribution upgrade, to minimise the possibility of something breaking.
