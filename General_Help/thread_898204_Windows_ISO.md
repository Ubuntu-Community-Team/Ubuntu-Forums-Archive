### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-08-23
What do you mean by "use"?  An iso isn't specific to Windows.  It's just a disc image.  You can mount it in Linux with the commands "sudo mkdir /media/whatever; sudo mount -o loop /path/to/myisofile.iso /media/whatever".  Or you can burn it to a disc using something like Brasero or K3B.
