### Post by gpilkay on 2007-12-07
This is not a 'computer' based solution, but what I have found myself is that many DVD/CD drives have a certain 'point' where they balance, like a tire on a car, and anything that throws that off, such as changing the speed, can have the noise effect.  One thing that I do myself that you could try if this is a desktop is take a level (NOT A MAGNETIC ONE!) and set it on top of the computer, using small shims (I use old credit card halves for this) till the noise goes away, usually very close to perfect level.  Of course, this depends on how level the internal CD drive is mounted, if it is off-level in the case, the case itself will be off-level when you balance it.

Other then that, I have not really had much experience with down-clocking the CD burner...sorry.
