How small? The standard these days is 14 inch. The smallest and yet powerful might be 11.6 inch. Then there are variation like 13.3 inch and 12.5 inch.
For example [ASUS ZENBOOK Touch UX31A]("http://www.asus.com/my/Notebooks_Ultrabooks/ASUS_ZENBOOK_Touch_UX31A/specifications/"). From [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AsusZenbookPrime](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AsusZenbookPrime) it says '12.04.3 (Kernel version 3.8.0) works without major issues', there's no note on 14.04 though.
Dell  XPS 13 Ultrabook&#8482; Touch, 13.3 inch LED  Touch (1920 x 1080). Dell XPS 12 Ultrabook&#8482; Touch, 12.5 inch LED  Touch (1920 x 1080). There's also Dell  XPS 13 Developer Edition that comes preloaded with Ubuntu 12.04.
HP Spectre 13-3010ea Ultrabook. There's no quick info on Ubuntu compatibility.
So you have three example of high end small laptop. With high performance, comes high price though. Asus Zenbook, you might encounter small hiccups here and there. Dell XPS, you won;t have problem. Since there is no mention of budget I had listed some of the normal brand.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-09-17
Used Thinkpad [X-Series]("http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Category:X_Series") with an extra battery might serve your purposes.  You can find used docking stations and Ultrabay accessories cheap and they make a decent coding workstation with large external screen, keyboard and mouse.  On the road, they have crappy battery life, but you can get an Ultrabay battery or extra-capacity battery, or just a spare battery and swap (quickly) during sleep.  That gives you 4 solid hours of compiling on the beach.
