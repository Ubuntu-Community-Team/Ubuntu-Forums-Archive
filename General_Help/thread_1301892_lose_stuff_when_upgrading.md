No, it's not a stupid question.  (Some people say there are *no* stupid questions, but I dunno about that...)  Anyway, if you don't know, you don't know.  And the only way that'll change is if you get to know that which you don't know.  You know?  :p

Anyway: no, if you upgrade you do *not* lose your files (though it's always best to backup your stuff first, just in case).  This is why people choose to upgrade rather than do a clean install: when you upgrade, you keep your files; including configuration files, so all apps should work just as they did before the upgrade.

Unfortunately, upgrading doesn't always work.  It can fail especially if you change a lot of stuff from the defaults, locally installed apps and the like.  I've never had a successful upgrade, and now I don't even bother trying.  But I think it's a cool process if it works for you.  Give it a go!
