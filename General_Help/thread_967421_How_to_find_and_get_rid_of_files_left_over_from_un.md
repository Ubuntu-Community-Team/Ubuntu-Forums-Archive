### Post by Pro-reason on 2008-11-02
I presume you're talking about user preference files, with names beginning with a dot, stored in your home directory.

They are less scattered around than they are on Windows.

Open Synaptic.  There is a section for programs which have been removed, but which have user configuration files left on disk.  Do “completely remove” to get rid of those.
