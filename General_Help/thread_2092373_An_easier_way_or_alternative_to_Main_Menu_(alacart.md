### Post by raen on 2012-12-07
After installing Ubuntu 12.04, I decided I wanted a lot of variety and so, in no particular order, I also installed GNOME Classic (which is my preferred DE), KDE/kubuntu, LXDE/lubuntu, XFCE/xubuntu, MATE, Cinnamon, and I'm still looking around for that partridge in a pear tree. (Insert Hyperbole and a Half reference here.)

*ahem* Anyhow, my multiple installations brought with them a lot of near-redundancies. I have more variations on Terminal than I can shake a stick at, not to mention a whole host of text editors and various and sundry other apps that I just had to have.

While in GNOME Classic, I fired up alacarte and began to organize my applications menus. I made subfolders/submenus to keep like things together. I liked the result, but not the process. Each individual item had to be moved one at a time, and it copied instead of moved, so then I had to go back and delete it from where it had been. Very tedious and time-consuming, and I'm not done.

I installed MATE yesterday and I was noticing how similar it is to GNOME Classic. Then I looked at the menus. It doesn't seem to support user-created submenus. Everything I had in submenus in GNOME Classic, MATE shunted into the Other menu. That really undoes any kind of organization I was hoping for.

Is there an easier way to edit my menus? Something more akin to how it can be done in Windows? That is, selecting multiple items at once and dragging and dropping them to where I want them to be? And properly moving rather than just copying? I'd like to be able to organize my applications menus (without submenus, I guess, to keep MATE happy) without it taking ten years.

Thanks.

(**EDIT:** Apparently, mozo does support user-created submenus. It just doesn't port them well from alacarte. My main question stands, though: Is there an easier way than alacarte/mozo?)

### Post by Rebelli0us on 2012-12-07
You can edit/create menus in MATE, right-click on start menu and select "edit menu". The classic gnome menus are also available in MATE Alt+F1 .
