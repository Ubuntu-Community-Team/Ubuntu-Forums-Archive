### Post by DrMega on 2008-03-19
I can't speak for Nokias as I always end up with Samsung phones. On my Samsungs that I've had, there is an option in the settings somewhere where I decide whether the USB connection is going to work like a modem or as a mass storage device. I set it to Mass Storage Device and then my phone just acts like a USB memory stick as far as the computer is concerned.

Alternatively, get a USB Bluetooth dongle and transfer wirelessly.

### Post by mehulved on 2008-03-19
Install the following packages - bluetooth, gnome-bluetooth, libbluetooth2, gnome-vfs-obexftp, obexftp, obexfs, obexpushd and obextool. See if you're able to get anywhere with these apps and bluetooth.
My sister just got 3500 classic and we did easily connect it to the PC and access the memory card, in addition to phone memory.
As a bonus, I could use phone manager to send SMS. I tested it from her phone to mine and was able to receive it easily.
Now, I am playing around with wammu, it's reported to work with wammu but no luck so far.
