### Post by YuHoo on 2006-03-29
That's a power saving feature found on almost every new laptop since the biggest drain is from the LCD backlight. To change it there are usually keys on your laptop to do an on-the-fly adjustment as well as when you first turn on your computer you have the option of entering the bios. It usually has the maker's logo flashed on the screen and a small bar below it. Check for the button to press to enter it, it is usually in the upper right hand corner but it can be anywhere; the button is usually F2 or delete. After getting into the bios you can browse around but try not to change anything you don't know about. Go to power options and there should be an option to adjust the brightness of your screen in different situations.
