### Post by sedawk on 2009-07-31
As was said before there are some vendors that deliver system
without M$ software but replace it with cheap or free operating
systems (FreeDos, Linpus Linux, etc). Very often this is limited
to special hardware setups (notebooks, netbooks).

But what you normally can do is to go to a local PC shop
where they also build PCs after you selected a list of components
from their list (motherboard+cpu+memory+graphics+...).
Normally they have cheap offers that are so cheap because they
do not include an M$ OS and if they build a special configuration
for you this doesn't have to include a M$ OS.
But ask them if you can bring a LiveCD (Ubuntu, Knoppix, ...) so
you can test the system works before you pay for it.

Testing is exactly the problem - in some shops it is mandatory
to buy an M$ OS because installing it on the PC is their only
way to test the PC before delivery ...
