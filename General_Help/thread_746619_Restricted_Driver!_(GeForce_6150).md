### Post by warp99 on 2008-04-05
No the driver is not 'restricted' per se, but it's not an open source driver and because of licensing issues with the GPL (General Public License) the driver can't be included with the distribution, therefore they're called restricted.

There is very simple way to handle this. Open the 'Restricted Drivers Manager' under System > Administration, then tick the box for your card (Nvidia), enable the drivers then reboot.
