### Post by jimmydee on 2008-12-05
If you can afford it, buy a new hard drive, and put it in one of the bays in your computer case. Install linux on this drive, and you won't need to shrink your existing drive. The original drive can stay inside your computer. To boot on to a particular drive, you need to go into CMOS and configure the boot sequence. You get into CMOS on most computers by holding down the delete button as the computer starts to boot. If you don't know how to do this, you can ask a computer savvy friend to help you.
