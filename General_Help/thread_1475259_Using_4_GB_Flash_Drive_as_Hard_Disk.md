### Post by quadproc on 2010-05-06
> **UCSBGauchosRock said:**
> Hi everyone. I have an important concern that I need serious help with... My hard disk on a Dell Latitude D600 caved and it is now unusable so I installed ubuntu 10.04 on a 4 GB SanDisk Cruzer and have been using that as a hard drive for booting and everything...
Flash drives are not rated for very many write cycles; using one as a replacement for a disk will lead to a short life for it.  This is a consequence of the fact that the memory function is achieved by storing charge on a floating gate in the flash drive's IC; getting charge into or out of that gate reuires electrical forces just short of destroying the IC.  With all that wear and tear, they don't last long when they are written to.

A flash drive can be read without causing significant degradation so they are good for applications where you write occasionally and read often.

Unfortunately, there isn't much that you can do about the slow speeds of the flash drives, other than to choose the fastest manufacturer for the size of drive that you need.
