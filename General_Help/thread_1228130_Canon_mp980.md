### Post by blur xc on 2009-10-14
I just realized people have been replying here, but I've only been replying to their pm's...  

So- here's the rundown.

I have my printer on our home wirelss network.  That's the biggest problem.  You need to connect the printer to a windows computer via usb cable, and run all the canon supplied software to get it connected to your home wireless network.  After all of that is done, you need to download and install Turboprint (costs $40).  Then, go to the printer manager, and it *should* find it on your network and install itself.  After that I was printing fine.  The only caveat is that you can't scan w/ it over your network.  I don't know if you can w/ it connected though.

The work-a-round is to plug a usb flash drive into the front of the printer, scan, and save the image to the flash drive.  Then you can transfer the image to your pc.  

My #1 solution- go buy an HP printer, since HP provides Linux drivers for most of their printers.  It's only my to-do list, but it'll probably have to wait until after Christmas...
