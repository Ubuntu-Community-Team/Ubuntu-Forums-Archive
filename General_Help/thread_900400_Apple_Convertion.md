### Post by conundrumx on 2008-08-25
This has to do with a default behavior in GNOME.  When it detects a compositor (Metacity with compositing enabled, Compiz, etc) it makes the Volume display transparent and fancy.

Unfortunately, there is no setting for this in GNOME 2.22, you'd have to modify the source.  I don't know of any workarounds.
