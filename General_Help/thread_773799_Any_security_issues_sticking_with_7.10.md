### Post by ryanhaigh on 2008-04-29
You should be fine sticking with 7.10 for some time although you will probably end up changing before sept 09. I always wait a month or two after a release before upgrading (actually I do a fresh install to get the most out of the system without having to worry about my 3rd party repos and self-compiled apps getting in the way) so that things can stablize a bit more and major bugs are sorted out. You only have to hang out in the forums after each release to see many people with issues.

As long as you have security repos enabled you should be fine.


There are always more ways to secure your system google can help you with that eg. [http://www.linuxforums.org/security/locking_down_ubuntu.html](http://www.linuxforums.org/security/locking_down_ubuntu.html)

But if your just doing normal stuff and are sensible about what you click you should be fine.

### Post by ezsit on 2008-04-29
Are you running a personal desktop system? If so, sticking with 7.10 is just fine and secure, even past the Sept. 09 end of life period. Run your computer behind a hardware firewall/router and you should be fine for quite awhile.

I used Ubuntu 5.10 until 7.04 came out, never a problem running my home desktop. I stuck with 7.04 until this past February, months after 7.10 was released, never a problem. Old software doesn't become useless until it stops providing the functionality you need. The only reason I upgraded was to get newer versions of OpenOffice and various other bits.

Heck, with Linux's security, I'd bet an Ubuntu 7.10 box would be more secure in 2010 than a Vista box.
