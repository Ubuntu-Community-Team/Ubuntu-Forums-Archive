### Post by TheFu on 2018-09-10
1.
Feasible? Yes.
Easy?  Depends on your skills.

2.
Most likely yes. 
* The RAM needs to be compatible and added in compatible ways. Some systems are picky and won't work with RAM from different manufacturing runs.
* SSD needs to fit and the connectors need to be compatible.  There are many different sorts of SSDs now, so you can't just go out and buy one without doing a little research first. And some SSDs are made with cheap storage chips that wear out in 2-3 yrs, so if you are getting this and hope to get 5+ yrs, it is best to choose a reputable, long-lasting, model.

3. 
Find one that works with Linux, get USB3.1 if you can.  Even if your system doesn't support USB3, having the faster components will help.  If it were me, I'd install the SSD first, BEFORE doing the data transfer.   And I'd expect to need the Win7 rescue disk to correct partition stuff at the first boot after the data has all been cloned (bit-for-bit) to the SSD from the external USB storage.
