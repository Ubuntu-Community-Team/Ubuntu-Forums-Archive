### Post by Paqman on 2012-05-23
> **Quarkrad said:**
> However, as Linux/Ubuntu is essentially safe re virus/malware attacks what is the 'real life' consequencies of her staying with 10.10?

Sorry to say, but it doesn't work like that.

Linux's better security reputation is in large part due to the fact that it has a good update system. Unlike Windows, use of centralised repositories means that an exploit in _any_ package can be patched out of contention in a couple of days. Windows can only do that with core OS components, and even then it takes them weeks or months.

Linux isn't inherently any more immune to malware than any other system, it's just that the attack windows are extraordinarily short, and users generally get their software from reputable sources. So it's a very difficult ecosystem for malware to spread in.

Bottom line: you cannot use an out-of-support Linux box and expect it to have good security, because that support is a big part of what made it more secure than Windows in the first place. Your wife's machine would have absolutely no defence against zero-day exploits.
