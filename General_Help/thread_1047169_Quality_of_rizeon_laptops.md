### Post by cmnorton on 2009-01-22
I have installed Linux on three laptops, Ubuntu on two of them. They ranged from a Compal, followed by an Acer, and then to a Thinkpad. 

I noticed you can purchase a rizeon without an OS, which is a nice option. From experience, having installed Ubuntu on what were previously Windows laptops and having just purchased a System76, I would find a vendor who has Linux experience and purchase it from them.

If you want installation experience, grab an old desktop that meets or exceedes minimum requirements, and install away. At least if you purchase the rizeon, please think about publishing your installation experience. You can do so in community documentation or as a tutorial.
