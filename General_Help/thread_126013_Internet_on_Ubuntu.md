There are two ways to share an internet connection. One is wireless, the other is wired. Wireless is slightly slower and slightly less reliable, but it's better (and much easier) than having cables running all over the place. A router generally goes for about $40, and you just hook you DSL modem up to it. Then you need to buy a wireless card to hook into your Ubuntu machine. Ubuntu should automatically detect the wireless card, and you'll be able to connect to the internet right away on either machine at the same time.

Oh, and the default Ubuntu CD includes lots of packages, including the terminal stuff. So, no need to worry about that.
