### Post by CatKiller on 2009-04-23
> **howlingmadhowie said:**
> then he didn't know what to do with all his money and founded canonical.

To be fair, he did go into space first :)

Also, the question of "getting paid" is a pretty nebulous one. Obviously there are people that make money directly from support and services, like Red Hat and Canonical. Then there are people for whom having Linux/FOSS be better directly benefits their sales of other things; think IBM servers or Intel graphics, or web services that use Apache.

But a significant part of "being paid" for many developers isn't about money at all, it's about code. Because of the collaborative nature of development, if 10 people each put in an hour's worth of coding, then they each get 9 hours of coding for free. The software that they use gets better, and everybody wins.

### Post by CatKiller on 2009-04-23
> **roshanjose said:**
> Closed source has better services than Open Source...because it does come out with perfect OS with very less bugs as compared to open source

That's a pretty ridiculous assertion. All software has bugs, whether open-source or proprietary. There is no such thing as a "perfect" OS.

The advantage that Free software has is that if you find a bug you can either fix it or get someone else to. With proprietary software you're at the mercy of the vendor for whether they can be bothered to fix bugs. There's also the fact that proprietary software developers are free to put user-hostile features in their software (think DRM, or nag-ware, or user-tracking). Free software developers have no such incentive, and the community is perfectly capable of removing such things.
