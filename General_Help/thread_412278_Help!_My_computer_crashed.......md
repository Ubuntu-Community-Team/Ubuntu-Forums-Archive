### Post by rmemm on 2007-04-18
I think it depends on what you mean by does not recognize.  

Does the drive spiin up and does your BIOS see the drive?  If so you have a chance it's still working.

If the drive seems to be working can you see the partitians?  If you can than it means that it's probably a file system problem.  If not -- means your partitians table may have been corrupted.  Both situations can potentially be recovered though it will take effort.

Other option -- if you don't care whats on it -- just repartitian and reformat and test it.  At the other extreme you can also send the drive to a disk recovery company and pay big money to get what's on it.
