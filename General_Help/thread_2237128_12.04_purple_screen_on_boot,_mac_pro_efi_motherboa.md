### Post by andrew-krohn on 2014-08-04
So it seems no one with any answers is reading this, but in case someone else has this experience, I did manage to get a efi-bootable usb.  To do this, I had to find the old HDD that came with the mac and still had OSX on it and swap out my RAID array for this single drive. Then I went through the steps at the ubuntu site on making a bootable usb from a mac, and the thing now works.  Pretty easy in the end, but it still shouldn't be that hard.  Since my array was RAID10, I simply installed 14.04 onto the first drive and then ran mdadm to assemble the remaining RAID parts (had to pass --stop before --assemble would work).  I am currently archiving all the data I still need to my external RAID.  When finished, I will turn the remaining 3 drives from the original RAID10 into a RAID0 and set up regular backup jobs to the external RAID.  This should provide the drive size I require (some projects have exceeded 5TB already), a little extra speed in processing, and yet the security of regular backups.  To ease administration of the new RAID array, I installed webmin.  Looks super simple.
