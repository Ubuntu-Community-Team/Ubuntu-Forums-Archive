### Post by ridgeland on 2010-10-06
I built a PC for myself with a TV tuner card - HD from over-the-air.  Here in the boonies that means PBS and FOX (only on for the Packers games)  I bought all the pieces at NewEgg. I used a 3 core AMD that reviews said could unlock the 4th core.  But I saw 3 cores is overkill.  I built another PC with a TV tuner card for my spouse with a single core AMD Sempron.  It works fine too.  It's lower power use with just one core (Kill-A-Watt reads 62 watts on 1 core PC vs 70 watts on 3 core PC).  Also costs less.  We both have cable TV connections and use S-Video from the cable's modem (ISP not the old coax).
We use MythTV with Ubuntu 10.04, not MythBuntu.  My PC is connect to a HD-TV as the monitor, the TV card is connected to an antenna and to S-Video from the Cable-Modem.  Her PC is connected to a Cable-Modem with a DVR and an antenna she never uses.  Output is to the stereo system and HDMI sound to the TV speakers.  We didn't replace the stereo just added to it.
If you build a PC don't settle for a power supply that's less than 80 PLUS.  Replacing the cheap PSU that came with the case in my wife's PC dropped the watts from 79 to 62.  Since it will be on a lot that matters.  Here 1 watt 24x7x365 costs about $1.
