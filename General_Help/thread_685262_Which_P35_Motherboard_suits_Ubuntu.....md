### Post by musther on 2008-02-02
I've never had problems with motherboards and linux.  You could have issues with built in graphics cards (although it's most likely to work), or with built in wireless or something like that, but if you're buying a general motherboard (with nothing unusual) you should be fine.  The things you need to be careful when picking are Graphics, Sound (if you want anything more advanced than just normal output), wireless and any peripherals like printers etc.

Best of luck!

### Post by IanW on 2008-02-02
I'm looking to move to a Gigabyte GA-P35C-DS3R.

My reasons are:-
1:- Both 4xDDR2 and 2xDDR3 RAM slots (You can't use both at once)
2:- Most other makes have 6xSATA & 2x eSATA ports on the back that you can't use for internal drives, this has all 8 SATA ports on the inside (and an adaptor plate if you _want_ eSATA).
3:- It's fairly cheap (73GBP / 160USD)
