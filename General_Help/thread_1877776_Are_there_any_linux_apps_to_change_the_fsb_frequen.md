### Post by Nixarter on 2011-11-08
I would recommend upgrading your CPU heatsink and fan.  I think you would be much better off doing that.

Secondly, it is generally a much better idea to use your BIOS options to tweak clock, FSB, and related settings.  It seems to me that you're looking for OS-level programs because your mobo doesn't support such functions (or, perhaps, because you are new to overclocking).  The problem is that if your mobo doesn't support it... then you can't do it.

That being said, I don't know of any fsb tweaking programs under linux.  But then again, I've never looked.  Have you tried searching for overclocking software in linux?  If your mobo supports it and you insist on using OS-level programs, then that would be a good bet.  I've never heard of any software just for slowing down FSB clocks or multipliers, but programs that adjust with the intention of speeding up computer can just as easily set them to lower levels.  So that would be a good place to start.
