### Post by seawolf167 on 2011-06-29
You almost always have to go one release at a time, so 10.10 -> 11.04 -> 11.10.

That said, I recommend creating a separate /home partition, then just reinstalling when you upgrade so everything is nice and clean and there are no [potential] package conflicts. The reinstall only takes a couple minutes anyways so it's pretty painless.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-06-29
I do not think so. The update centre should be offering you the upgrade to 11.04 right now.  It has not been my experience that it will by-pass a distribution upgrade. Only after you have upgrade to 11.04 will the Update Centre offer a distribution upgrade to 11.10.

This is my understanding. Think of it this way. A lot of us will be going from 11.04 to 11.10. You want the process to be able to handle going from 10.10 to 11.10. Why not also 10.04 to 11.10 or 9.10 to 11.10, and so on. The process then becomes an extremely complicated set off instructions to take into account all the files that might or might not be present and require replacing or may be not.
