### Post by bretto_40 on 2008-10-18
RE: online banking. I do all of my online baking through my install of Ubuntu and never had an issue.

From what I understand it goes like this.... where possible run your internet connection through a router with a built in firewall (i.e. don't connect directly to the internet if that can be avoided).

Reason being is that hackers then have a physical barrier between you and your info. That's the setup I have.

Apart form that, just be sensible. Make sure that whenever you're doing any online banking or online purchasing that you check that your browser has the pad lock icon in the bottom right corner indicating that it is a secure page.

From what I understand, a secure page means that the browser has been able to verify the page's security certificate. Basically all this does is tell you that you are definitely dealing with the people you THINK you're dealing with.

Aside from that it doesn't actually provide any "security" in that sense. But generally if that icon is there you should be right.

From what I know one of the common ways hackers get your credit card details is through key logging malware... which I believe is harder to contract on Linux than Windows... and then add that router and you shouldn't have any probs.

Security is pretty damn good out of the box with Ubuntu and most Linux distros. Until the hackers out there start creating viruses en masse like they do for Windows then we don't have to worry too much.

Hopefully Linux will become king... but unfortunately then it will be the target. But until then you should be fine.

If I'm wrong, please correct me anyone :)

I hope this helps.
