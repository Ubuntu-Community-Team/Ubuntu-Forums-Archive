### Post by polybius on 2010-02-15
I am trying to support a 92-year old grandmother on Ubuntu Linux long distance and having quite a bit of trouble.  She lives on the East Coast, I live on the West, but I visit her every so often.  About a year ago I set her up with Ubuntu 8.04 on a Dell Dimension (with Gnome, it's a standard Ubuntu install).  I made it auto login to her account.  She is on broadband internet.

I did the setup here on the West Coast and let my wife and family use it daily for over a month to make sure everything was ok.  Then I shipped it to her.  It was working fine when I left.  I have been back twice since then, for a week each time, and it has been working fine for me each time.

When she is by herself, however, she has troubles.  Initially I set her up with a simple desktop with 3 icons:  Thunderbird, Firefox and Openoffice.  

Her first problem was that her icons kept disappearing.  I found out when I visited that somehow she was moving them to trash without knowing it.  So on my first visit after installation, I created 5 copies of each icon, so if she accidentally moved some of them to trash, she would have others.  I know it's easy to open Applications->Internet->Thunderbird, right click and get an icon on the desktop.  But she can't do that, and she can barely follow my instructions when I try to walk her through it over the phone.  
Anyway, the multiple icons solved the icon-in-the-trash problem for a while.

Most of the time she uses Thunderbird ok, but every so often she manages to delete some range of emails in her inbox, spanning several months.  I wouldn't know how to do that with Thunderbird if I wanted to (not in a few clicks anyway), so I don't know how she's doing it.  But it's easy to go to Trash (again, things are in the trash) and move them back to her inbox.  

Another problem that has cropped up lately is that she manages to insert twenty or thirty blank pages in the middle of her Openoffice documents.  She's trying to write the history of the family, and is using Openoffice.  I don't know how she is doing this.

I suspected that she was randomly right clicking on things and getting delete and other functions that she doesn't want.  She doesn't actually need to right click anything, so I had her cram some kleenex under the right mouse button to disable it.  I'd disable it in software if I knew how.  

Unfortunately, that didn't help.  Even with only a left mouse button she managed to delete all 5 of her Thunderbird icons on her desktop (this  time they are not in the trash and I don't know how she did it or where they are).  Also, she deleted half her inbox (which contains emails going back a year or so).

I left her system with one account open to ssh, so I can access her machine from here and fix things up sometimes, but it is a lot of trouble, and she is getting discouraged.  She is getting convinced that there is something wrong with the computer and wants to call some service person where she lives so she won't have to bother me any more.  I doubt if she can find a service person who can help her where she lives.

My questions for this group are:

1.  Has anybody had any similar experience with older people who have difficulty coordinating mouse and/or keyboard actions?

2.  Does anyone know of a way (under Gnome) to disable the right clicks?

3.  Does anyone have any idea how she could manage to delete half her inbox in Thunderbird (it's an imap account) accidentally?  Could it be one of the keyboard shortcuts?  I looked at them and don't see how they could be responsible.

4.  Does anyone know how I can disable all keyboard shortcuts (in Gnome, Thunderbird, Openoffice....), at least to eliminate them as a possibility for the errors?

Many thanks.  

I would buy her a Mac but I don't know that that would be any better and she would have something else new to learn.  Right now she mainly just wants to read and write emails and write her family history (on Openoffice).

By the way, she is mentally competent but does not have great strength, and her hand coordination is poor.

### Post by crlang13 on 2010-02-15
Another OS won't be any better.  I've helped my grandparents with windows and they're always doing similar things.

I took the time to type out (and print) instructions for all basic uses.  She may be mentally competent, but I've found that with both sets of grandparents that they are daunted by trying to learn a new thing.  It's less about forgetfulness and more about lack of confidence.  Written instructions are good to keep them confident.

As for deleting random files and the like.  Have you tried changing user permission settings?

### Post by crlang13 on 2010-02-16
> **Swagman said:**
> Slow down the double click speed...considerably.

Good call, mate.  I did that for my grandparents because sometimes that wouldn't double click fast enough and the program wouldn't open, so they'd just click alot, opening the program many times.

I also found that my grandparents had trouble keeping track of where their cursor was, so I changed the default cursor to a BIG flashing red one.  I found they would get confused when the cursor changed (from and arrow to a finger for example) and just made the big red cursor for pretty much everything.
