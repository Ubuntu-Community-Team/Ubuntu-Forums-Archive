### Post by oldtimer7777 on 2011-12-05
> **hillbillyhotrod said:**
> What I need to know is how long before I can go back to my comfort area, what I know and feel safe with.

AMDs are great if you are going to build your own PC..  you get more bang for your buck with AMD CPUs, as long as you can get a nice compatable Nvidia PCI Express card for graphics..  But when it comes to Laptops/Notebooks?  Meh.  AMD is kinda cheap in comparison to Intel Dual Core because of the video that AMD usually ships with in combination..  Nice CPU, inferior Video adapter combo when you buy the unit.  Now, with my entirely Intel based system the really cool thing is that I don't have additional drivers I have to install to get my Toshiba rocking in Ubuntu. None.  If you know what that is like, you may want to avoid dealing with 3rd party driver issues in Linux and AMD based Laptops/Notebooks.  I'm not sure if that is what you are asking about, but that was the most important thing I learned to shop for when it comes to new Laptops/Notebooks and using strictly Ubuntu as my primary OS.

Notebook/Laptops = I always stick with Intel CPU, Intel Video, Intel everything.  No ATI. No issues anymore with installations.
