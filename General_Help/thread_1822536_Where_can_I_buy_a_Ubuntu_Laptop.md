### Post by Zill on 2011-08-10
kyral210:  It is logical to hope you could avoid paying the "Windows tax" by buying a laptop with Ubuntu, rather than Windows, preinstalled.

However, you may find you can get a good, or even better, deal by buying a laptop with an *old* version of Windows preinstalled.

For example, I recently bought a brand new [ACER ASPIRE D260A]("http://www.ebay.co.uk/itm/ACER-ASPIRE-D260A-160GB-10-1IN-NETBOOK-SILVER-/350473100366?pt=UK_Computing_Laptops_EH&hash=item5199d31c4e") netbook for just £160.  This came with Windows XP, which I immediately replaced with Crunchbang Linux and now it runs like a dream. :-)
