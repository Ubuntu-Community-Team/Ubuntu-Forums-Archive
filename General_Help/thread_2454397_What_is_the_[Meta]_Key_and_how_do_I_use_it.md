### Post by DuckHook on 2020-11-28
Most of my meta shortcuts are available through the Alt key. Of course, some applications and their shortcuts that are tied to that key are legacy. They predate the subsequent evolution of keyboards and have earned something of a right to hang on to their traditions. I've always mentally subbed Alt for Meta and it has never been any big deal.

You are always at liberty to nuke whatever shortcuts you want, or to reassign them, or to ignore them. This is Linux after all. Customization is king.

I suppose you could also go into keymapping and specifically remap some key to the actual "Meta". I've never researched it because the issue has never been of any importance to me. If you want to wander through that dark jungle, please see the link in my sig: **Remapping Keys**

If do you embark on that journey, be sure to follow the cautionary advice in my tutorial. Fiddling around with keymapping is a fraught exercise and could leave your system unusable. Best to experiment in a VM first.
