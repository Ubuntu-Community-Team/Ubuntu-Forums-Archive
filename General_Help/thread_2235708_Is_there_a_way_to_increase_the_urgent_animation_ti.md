### Post by Roasted on 2014-07-22
Hello friends. I am working on Ubuntu 14.04, fully up to date. Something that I have always been disappointed in with Unity is the way you receive urgent animations. What I am talking about when I say an urgent animation is, for example, Empathy's behavior when you get an instant message. This applies for all windows, really. The icon gets a very, very slim sky blue highlight and it wiggles for all but one second. Considering that a lot of users are working on higher resolution monitors these days, or multiple monitors at that, it is wildly easy to miss a notification given that the icon never wiggles after that.

I'm wondering if there's a way to adjust that. Logic (in my world, at least) suggests that an icon that wiggles, say, every 30 seconds until you click on it, makes sense. It allows a user to be consistently re-notified that an application needs attention without being irritating. Other operating systems incorporate a similar behavior and it works out well for them.

A while ago I [filed a bug]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/unity/+bug/1319542") in regard to this, but it has seen little/no activity. I can't help but wonder about there being SOME way, somehow possible to make this happen on my own accord. It gets old, really, really fast when you begin missing notifications because you looked away from your monitor or stepped away from your desk for all but a few seconds. Aside from this, I really like Unity, but things like this certainly need to be addressed.

Does anybody have an idea of how this can be done?
