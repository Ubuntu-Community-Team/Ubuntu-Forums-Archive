### Post by jbrown96 on 2010-05-18
Apple's are more about fit and finish than raw power. You get superior build quality and great battery life. The screens are also very good. 

I looked at the basic MBP 15" and Alien MX15. Bumping up the RAM, HD, and CPU to match the MBP yielded a price about $230 less. However, the MBP still had a slightly better graphics card (based purely on model number). The only upgrades on the MX were ATI graphics cards, and while they are fine in Windows, they are terrible in Linux. 

Honestly, the laptops are really not that different. I tend to keep my tech for several years, and ammortizing out $230 over 3-4 years really isn't that much. I'd really say they are practically even on price. I don't really push my hardware too hard, so I'd go with the Apple. For gaming, it might be better to go Alienware; the graphics cards are more impressive. However, I wouldn't expect that computer to be very mobile. 

I prefer the Apple because I care about ergonomics and battery life. If those are important, then I think that the MBP is the best option. If you really need speed, especially graphics, then the Alienware will be able to deliver better performance with a mobility downside.
I've had solid experiences with both companies. As long as you don't abuse your equipment, I don't think either laptop will have reliability problems.
