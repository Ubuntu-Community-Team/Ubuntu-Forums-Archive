### Post by disturbedite on 2007-04-29
thats one way that dvd manufacturers have gotten around the end users being able to easily copy dvds.  they use dvd9 (which is somewhere between 7 and 9GBs) and the only ones we consumers can buy are dvd5 (which is 4.7GB).

you must use a "shrink" program to lower the video quality and/or eliminate some extra features from the dvd then program will produce a dvd image that can be burned on a dvd5.

there are many, the one i use is k9copy.

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-04-30
I've had good success using k9Copy to shrink a DVD9 down to a DVD5, with no noticable loss of picture or sound quality. It doesn't cut out anything and is a true 1:1 copy of all menus and features.

I then use k3b to burn the iso to a DVD5 disk.

Although k9Copy has the facility to automatically burn the iso, I have had problems with it doing so and it just crashed and deleted the iso, as well. Hence I just tell k9copy to save the iso.
