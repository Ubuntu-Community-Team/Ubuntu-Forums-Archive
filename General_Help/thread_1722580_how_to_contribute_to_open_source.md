I use sourceforge, but you can also use launchpad. Sourceforge and Launchpad are excellent. Both will allow you to allow others to jump in on your projects and lend a hand, they both come with detailed tracking to know who, what, and when your sourcecode is being downloaded and used.

### Post by 1clue on 2011-04-05
Lots of ways.

The first thing to do is figure out what is important to you that seems to be lacking or, alternately, find a community (like apache) that does a bunch of good things you like and keeps doing more.

In other words, you need to find the place you most want to offer support.  Keep in mind that while you could contribute to Ubuntu or whatever distribution, you may more effectively contribute by finding the specific package you want to improve and give there.

Next thing to do is find out what they need, that you have.  It can be:
[LIST=1]
[*]Money.
[*]Hardware they need.
[*]Programming skills, either things you do directly or that you have your employees do.
[*]Documentation.
[*]Forum help.
[*]Services (web hosting, phone support, whatever)
[*]Any number of other things that may not occur to you but that the staff at whatever organization would jump on.
[/LIST]

Keep in mind that what you have and they need might have nothing to do with what would normally be called contributing.  It may not be anything the regular contributors even thought of, but something you can see that bugs you might be that way because nobody thought to change it, or they don't have somebody who can or will.

### Post by 1clue on 2011-04-06
Find the project you want to work with, and then look on their "get involved" link to see how to contact them.  It varies from project to project.

I would strongly recommend that you become a member of whatever forum is attached to the project, become familiar with it and know your way around before volunteering, or you will take a huge amount of resources away from the project while they try to train you.
