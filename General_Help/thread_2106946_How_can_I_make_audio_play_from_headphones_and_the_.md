### Post by xVehemencityx on 2013-01-20
Hi there.  

I have an ASUS G60VX Laptop.  My problem is, like many owners of this laptop, the headphone-detecting switch inside the headphone port broke, so now Ubuntu thinks there are always headphones plugged in, which renders my speakers useless.


In Windows, it's a simple key-change in the registry, but I can't figure out how to do it in Ubuntu.  Hopefully it's something that doesn't require a reboot every time I want to switch it, but if it is, Ubuntu reboots way faster than windows.


Any ideas?  
Thanks



EDIT:  I figured it out.  If I open Alsamixer, scroll over to the right, and disable "Auto-mute," the speakers start working.  That's a super simple fix. Thanks!


EDIT2: If that doesn't work for you, check out [this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1687823") guide.
