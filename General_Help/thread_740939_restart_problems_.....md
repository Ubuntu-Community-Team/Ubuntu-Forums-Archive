### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-04-04
OK, so at least you're using the correct driver - these older ATI cards do not accept the proprietary "fglrx" drivers, so we are stuck with the open source "ati" ones.  There is extra stuff in your xorg.conf file that is not needed, like the modelines under the Monitor section as well as some other misc stuff.  Have you ever reconfigured X manually?
I normally would've suggested this right from the start, except that you said it sometimes works and sometimes doesn't.  
Assuming you're using Gutsy or older (i.e. NOT using Hardy Heron, still in development), you can follow this guide to reconfigure X - [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=690760](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=690760)

Let me know if you still have problems after that, we may need to submit a bug report or try and determine if your hardware is acting up.

### Post by prshah on 2008-04-04
> **anemul said:**
> 
The problem I am having is sometimes when I start up my box , it gets past the splash screen and the it appears I lose video connection because the monitor light starts blinking , 
sometimes it goes on into the login screen and allot of the time it doesn't , it just sets there with the monitor light blinking , I have to restart the box and try again . It does this when rebooting too . Thanks for your help !

This is because your refresh rates are wrong for the monitor. To confirm, the next time your monitor starts blinking, use Ctrl+Alt+Numpad- and/or Ctrl+Alt+Numpad+ to change resolutions.

If you get your screen back, you need to reconfigure the monitor and can do so by ```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```
