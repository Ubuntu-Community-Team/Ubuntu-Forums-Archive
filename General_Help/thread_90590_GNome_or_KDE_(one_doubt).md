### Post by BIGtrouble77 on 2005-11-15
On the surface gnome is very simple because Ubuntu configures it that way.  Virtually every gnome/nautilus characteristic can be edited in the 'Configuration Editor'.  I've changed tons of things because I think too much is oversimplified to the point of hurting my workflow in gnome (how could they disable the address bar???).

Visually, gnome is amazing to work with.  Themes are all based on CSS so changing color and behaviours is as easy as it can get.  Icons are sooo easy to replace.  It's just too bad no one's built a gui to edit theme stylesheets yet, that I know of.   

KDE is just more in your face and hits you with a lot of features.  Not my style, but I'm glad we have KDE because of all the awesome apps(k3b, amarok, koffice, etc.).  I've never noticed performance issues with either, but I run on pretty high end hardware.
