### Post by gdonwallace on 2011-06-12
The easiest way to do it is to download the XFCE desktop from synaptic.  (xfce on ubuntu = xubuntu)  That way it will download the necessay xfce desktop files and you don't need to install xubuntu.

When searching for it, it will pull up a lot of things that can be installed that belong with xfce.  Chose "xfce4" and it will install what you need.

Restart your machine after the install, click on your name to login, at the bottom of screen click on the up arrow and choose xfce and then type your password and login...xfce will be installed.

### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-06-12
You can keep your files and settings easily if you make a separate home partition (google knows how to do that), you can't port your programs over, you will need to install those again.

EDIT: what Gdonwallace said also works, but you will end with a little heavier computer than pure Xubuntu. Apart from that, there are no differences.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-06-12
Just install **xubuntu-desktop** package and then if you want to remove the gnome ubuntu packages, and have a pure xfce setup, have a look at [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexfce](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexfce)

I have never done this and it may remove some packages you would rather keep, so scout through the list of removals and take out any from it that you wish to keep.  The easiest way to look at this list is to copy the full command in the appropriate black box on that page, and paste it in gedit or other text editor.
