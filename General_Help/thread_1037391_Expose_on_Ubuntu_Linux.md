### Post by beetlejuice321 on 2009-01-11
I am just writing this post for people like me and the rest of use who always seem to have a hard time turning on the Expose' feature in Compiz Fuzion (Desktop Effects) in Ubuntu.  Mainly because the category is so hard to find, and it doesn't help they currently use such a strange icon to represent the settings.

The Expose' effect similar to that found in Mac OS X is known as "Scale" in Compiz Fusion. 

To enable Scale do the following:

Access "Advanced Desktop Effects Settings" (Found in "System, Preferences")

Navigate to "Scale" or search for it, you will find it under the "Window Management" category.

Select the "Bindings" tab.

Now there are a lot of ways to initiate Scale, I prefer to use the mouse and corners of my display like in Expose'.  

To do this simply choose the button directly to the right of "Initiate Window Picker".  the button default is set to "None" click on this button, and from the desktop pop up you can choose the area of your screen you wish to use to initiate Scale.

I have also included a screenshot of the scale settings for those that may find it helpful.
