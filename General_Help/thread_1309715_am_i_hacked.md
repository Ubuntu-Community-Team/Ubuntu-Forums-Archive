### Post by Sarai the Geek on 2009-11-01
Short answer: no. 
Although we tend to think think of IP addresses as identifying information, the way many networks are set up, potentially hundreds of people at any given time may be sharing an IP address. You should be able to get a new one by restarting your cable/satellite modem or killing and restarting your dialup connection. To lessen the chances of this happening again, you may want to speak with your ISP about getting a static IP address- it usually costs a few dollars but it doesn't change all the time and won't be shared by as many people.
