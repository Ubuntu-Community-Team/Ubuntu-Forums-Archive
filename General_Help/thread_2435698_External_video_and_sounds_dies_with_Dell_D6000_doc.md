### Post by ieblj01 on 2020-01-24
Hello all!

I have recently purchased 2 Dell XPS 13 (9380) with Ubuntu installed from Dell.  We are using the Dell 452-BCYT D6000 Universal Dock.  We are experiencing a problem where the video (and sometimes the audio) through the dock will cut out.  The dock itself still shows it is receiving a signal from the laptop (status light stays white).  We have to disconnect the dock, reconnect it, open the laptop lid, let it show the desktop on the laptop screen, then either hit F8 again, and/or close the lid to get the video/audio signal to correctly be passed to the dock again (and shown on the external monitor/speakers/headphones).  

The laptops are running 18.04 LTS with all the latest updates and the latest D6000 linux Displaylink driver.  I have replaced the dock as well as cables and the issue remains.  It usually occurs during intensive video activities such as video calls and game play.  Dell says this is an Ubuntu issue and they can't help.

I have seen where others have posted video lagging issues with the D6000 because of the use of DisplayLink and how it uses the CPU and GPU to compress data intended for the displays.  Has anyone else experienced this?

I also see recommendations to use the WD15 or TB16 docks instead because they do not use the DisplayLink and therefore will not suffer from its drawbacks.

Any insight into a better dock recommendation is greatly appreciated!

---

### Post by ieblj01 on 2020-01-25
I ended up buying a TOTU 12 in 1 Type C Hub and it works perfectly.  No issues what so ever!
