### Post by nathanscottdaniels on 2008-10-03
Every time I use my flash drive on Ubuntu and then Vista, I see a ".Trash-100" folder.  It's annoying.  Can I make Ubuntu NOT put a trash folder on the USB drive?

---

### Post by sayakb on 2008-10-03
Unless you delete anything on the flash drive, no .Trash folder is created. Shift-delete the files to make them delete permanently on a USB flash drive.

---

### Post by rocknrolf77 on 2008-10-03
You can also in the preferences for nautilus. Choose "include delete" option. That way you can delete files permanently without holding shift.
