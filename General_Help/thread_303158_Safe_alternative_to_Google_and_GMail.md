### Post by tminuszero on 2006-11-19
Your ideal web service isn't free (as in beer).

"Is used by Richard Stallman?" He doesn't use a GUI often, if I remember correctly. I think he checks his mail via the command line.

As for "don't log or keep e-mails after they are deleted by the end user" - good luck with that. Most emails are logged in some fashion, but they aren't necessarily stored. Most companies do not care about the contents of your emails, and they don't store them.

As for "Doesn't change their policies for other countries" you'll be hard-pressed to find someone who doesn't change their policies to abide by the laws of the country they are operating in. I agree with you about Google and Yahoo and Microsoft's policies in China, but frankly there's little you can do about it. Unless you find someone who explicitly states that they do not allow users in other countries. In that case, look for a small local group.

I would just look into a pay service like GoDaddy or someone like that, or start hosting your own. You could buy your own domain and host email at Godaddy for a few bucks a year. The best solution to fit your needs is to host your own. You might have to look into your ISP's restrictions on hosting, or find a different ISP. Or build/buy/lease a dedicated server.

I'm from just outside Atlanta, by the way. Spent all day downtown :)

### Post by altaaa on 2006-11-23
You could take a look at [Track Me Not]("http://mrl.nyu.edu/~dhowe/trackmenot/").

This program 'floods' the common search engines with fake searches, therefore masquerading your real searches.

There are some reports on the net which say the program doesn't work as great as the maker says it will. For instance, because the program uses a predefined set of words, it is possible to filter out searches that contain those words. Also, it's said that using this program will send about 60 MB on fake queries *per day*...

Your choice.


As for the email thingy, look into running your own mailserver with some sort of encryption. There are some fine open source mailservers and webmail interfaces out there... just search for them. (yes, with google :))
