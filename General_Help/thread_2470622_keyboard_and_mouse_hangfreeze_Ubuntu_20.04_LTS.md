### Post by Autodave on 2022-01-05
I look at that sort of problem as more of a hardware issue and not an OS issue.  Overheating is the first thing that comes to mind.  Is this a laptop or desktop?  If laptop, have you tried blowing the dust out of the cooling duct?  If desktop, remove side panel and check for dirt build up especially on the CPU cooler.  Gently hold the fan from spinning and blast the dirt out with compressed air.  Short of that, hold fan gently and try running a hose from your vacuum cleaner over it.  The fan is brittle...BE CAREFUL!  Are the other fans working?

If that doesn't help, run PC with side cover removed.  A small desk fan could provide the extra cooling that it needs for now.  Try that.

When computer freezes, turn mouse over and see if the laser light is on.  If not, probable power supply issue.  If light on but frozen, probable RAM issue.
