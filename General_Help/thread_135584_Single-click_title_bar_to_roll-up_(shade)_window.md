### Post by Jucato on 2006-02-24
Is there a way to do this in KDE (using KWin window manager)?? It goes something like this:

I click on a window title and it rolls up (like in shade). I click it again and it rolls down and stays that way until I click the title bar again.

The default action of shading (rolls-up when your mouse leaves the window) can be sometimes annoying.

Any ideas would be appreciated. Thanks!!

### Post by sylvester_0 on 2006-02-24
Yup, just go into the Window Setting Dialog by right clicking a title bar and clicking Configure Window Behavior. Then go to the actions section and you'll see the settings you want. Actually, it looks like you can't assign the left mouse button to shade when active, but the middle button can do that role.

As what you said about it being annoying with the windows shading when you move your mouse off of them, yes that would be very annoying! I remember this happening to me once, I think it had something to do with X-mouse (activation follows mouse). Not exactly sure where to enable/disable that ATM.

### Post by ace2005 on 2006-02-24
If you go into the Window Setting Dialog and then go into Advanced and uncheck the "Enable Hover" check box, it will stop the window from rolling back up.

I actually find this useful since i set always on top to the tool dialogues in the gimp and they roll down when the mouse goes over them and then when i move off them to work on the image it roles up again.
