### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-02-14
Can you please provide more information to your problem. When does the problem occur? Does it occur only when running one particular problem? What program is at version 14 or 15?

It's probably not worth worrying about the warning message you got, unless you keep getting it (at which point, it's probably best to reinstall Ubuntu). It's rare to see, and most of the time it's a false alarm (you've clicked on another window in the split-second between starting Gksudo, and Gksudo grabbing control of the keyboard and mouse, resulting in Gksudo not having exclusive control of the keyboard and mouse).
