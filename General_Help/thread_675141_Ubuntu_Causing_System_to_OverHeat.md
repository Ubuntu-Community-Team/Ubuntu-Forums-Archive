### Post by silviur on 2008-01-22
It's is true that some apps strain the system, and the system overheats. I don't know how to manipulate fan speeds in Ubuntu, but I wouldn't recommend it, because the software can fail.
   However, check your BIOS for the shutdown CPU temperature. For example, the default value was 60 Celsius in my cause, which is a little low in the summer or in case of high demanding apps. The CPU becomes damaged at around 90 Celsius, so in my case I changed the value at 70. It never reached that temperature.
    My advice is to check this possibility (temperature), but the main problem is the software that causes the high load, that needs to be updated or the situation corrected in some way. I hope this helps.

### Post by rosegarden78 on 2008-01-22
If you're on a laptop this can happen with old batteries.  Because they don't hold a charge your computer keeps sending them current.  If you recharge 15-minute NiMH AA batteries you'll notice how hot they get.  When I removed my 3-year old laptop battery my system ran cooler.  I think it's because more air ventilated through the battery compartment.  My computer never overheated or shut down though.
