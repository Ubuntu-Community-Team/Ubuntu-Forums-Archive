### Post by Esotericstyle on 2009-07-03
As far as I noticed, there was no way to just transfer or save files during the installation. Ubuntu formats your drive just like any other O/S will. You will need to back up everything you want to save first on another computer or external drive, or even an online hosting service. As far as game compatibility, any Flash or SWF games should transfer over just fine, as long as you download a compatible Linux Flash viewer. In my experience, Wine is more trouble than it's really worth, as most Windows programs just don't run all that well on it. However, I am new to Ubuntu myself, so maybe more experienced users can help you more there. As far as the issues about internet connectivity issues, I had absolutely no problem switching my system over. I literally stuck in the ethernet cord, and was good to go. I don't even have problems with the connection recognizing my router.

There are a few downsides to Ubuntu, mainly just software compatibility, but if you are looking for something more efficient than Windows, it's a good place to go.
