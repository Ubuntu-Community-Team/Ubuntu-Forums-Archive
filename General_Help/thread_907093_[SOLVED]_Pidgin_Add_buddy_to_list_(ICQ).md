At present I have 2 accounts, setup in Pidgin, one for ICQ and another for IRC.

The dialogue box has a drop down list which shows my two personal accounts with entry fields for the "buddy's username", "optional alias" and a drop down for adding the buddy to a group.

### Post by rouben on 2008-09-01
Unfortunately since Pidgin supports a whole slew of different chat protocols (systems) its terminology can be a bit too generic (i.e. confusing). Here's what you need to do:
1. Select your ICQ account from the drop down list (you're adding an ICQ contact, right?)
2. Type in your friend's ICQ number into the username field
3. You can give this contact any nickname you wish in the alias field (I usually leave that one blank in order to see my friends' custom names that they themselves set)
4. Finally the group entry lets your file your contact under a category in Pidgin called a buddy group. Just like the alias, this is completely arbitrary and up to you. You can reassign people into groups later on anyway.

Hope this helps clarify things a bit. Happy chatting! :)
