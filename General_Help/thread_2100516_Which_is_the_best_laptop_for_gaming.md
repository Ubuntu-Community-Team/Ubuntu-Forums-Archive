### Post by Rsjc741 on 2013-01-05
Trying to choose a laptop that you might consider great is like finding straw in a haystack.

Except that we're blind, and that we only know the straw is longer than the others.

Look at places like Microcenter.com or Bestbuy.com

Tigerdirect.com and newegg.com .

Really, a "under $1000" price range is so broad. So assuming you want one within a $750 to $1000 range, you can be expecting this:

15 or 17 inch screen
6Gb-12Gb RAM
1+ TB HDD or <128Gb SSD
Handoff graphics, probably a Intel HD 3000 + Nvidia GTX 640M / Radeon equivalent.

Honestly, if you want performance disk-wise.. Look for a SSD.

Go with a laptop with a decent CPU and motherboard.

Don't even look at the memory or HDD.



Then go down to your local computer parts store, and pick up 16Gb of laptop memory and a Good 'ole 128-256GB SSD.

A 128Gb SSD will run you about $150.
