### Post by Neko_no_Nya on 2015-05-23
I hope this is an appropriate place to ask this question. I was going to post it in the hardware subforum but this isn't a question about getting hardware to work with Ubuntu, but getting my hardware to work at all! I currently cannot get my computer to turn on. When I press the power button the fans spin for a split second and then shut off.

I started with these specs:
AMD FX-6400 6-core 3.5GHz CPU
Biostar TA970 AM3+ Motherboard
Raidmax RX-530SS 530W PSU
G.SKill Sniper 8GB DDR3 1600
MSI G210 GPU
3TB Toshiba 7200 HD PH3300U-1I72
Blu-ray Burner (can't remember model number, although it doesn't really matter)
Xigmatek ASGARD 381 CCC-AD38BV-U03 Case


I recently decided to upgrade my GPU so I could start doing some PC gaming again. I bought an EVGA GTX 960 4G FTW. When I went to put it in, I couldn't find my anti-strap anywhere. My case has no bare metal so I just scratched off a little paint from the PSU to touch a screw driver to. Looking back I realize this was incredibly stupid. I was anxious to get the card in and forgot you are actually supposed to hold the frame and touch a screw driver to it to ground yourself. Yes, I know, I was stupid.

So when I put everything back together and tried to turn my PC on, all that would happen is the fans would spin for a split second and then turn off. I don't have a speaker on my motherboard so there were no beeps or anything. After doing some research I decided this sounded like a PSU issue. I did the paper clip test and it worked but that is only good for determining if your PSU is bad, not if it is good. I figured once it was under load it was failing. So I bought a new PSU, an EVGA 600B 80 Plus Bronze Certified 600 Watt PSU.

I installed the PSU and the same thing happened. Fans spun and then shut off. Note that to eliminate other variables I only hooked up the bare minimum. Power to motherboard, Ram, CPU fan, and a case fan.

So, I figured it must be the motherboard. I decided to make a little bit of an upgrade and bought a MSI 2133 970 Gaming mobo. I also bought myself a new anti-static wrist strap to make 100% sure I wasn't introducing any shockage.

I just put it in and am having the same problem. I only hooked up the bare minimums again. When I press the power button the PSU, Case, and CPU fan all spin for a split second and stop. Again, I have no speaker on my mobo but there are some lights. These light up for a split second and then turn off.

There are two things I think may be wrong, either the CPU or a faulty power switch. My power switch can be a little sticky. Sometimes it gets stuck and I have to jiggle it to get it back out. But even if it gets stuck the system keeps running. Plus, it's just a momentary switch. You push it and it pops back up.

The other possibility would be the CPU but I have had bad CPUs in the past and the system would always still turn on. Obviously, I couldn't boot to anything but the fans would spin and stay spinning. Also, the speaker would make a constant beep.


I would greatly appreciate any help anyone can give me. I'm at my wits end with this.

Thanks in advance :KS
