### Post by TheFu on 2013-09-28
TL;DR.

IF your ISP blocks port 25, you 'll need an outside service to forward your email to/from. You cannot solve this without an external machine.

However, you can probably use the ISP as an outbound gateway.  Check out the relay-host setting.  It is also possible to have an external email address and use fetchmail to pull things local.  This could be a way to "feel" like you have an email server without actually having an external server/service.  A few people here handle their gmail accounts in this way.  To send, using an authenticated connection will probably be necessary - I think that is handled in the **transport** file.  Didn't look it up.

1 problem at a time and you seem to ahve a bunch here.

DNS settings must go into /etc/network/interfaces now, not directly into /etc/resolv.conf - that was changed (for the better/worse) in 12.04 and later. The settings are:
dns-nameservers
dns-search

**man interfaces** will explain.

The DNS record look wrong.  No FQDN should have 2 dots back-to-back. There are millions of correct examples to copy. Just pick any other simple domain ... NOT gmail. ;)

I hope this helps in some small way. You are learning a bunch - sadly, it is harder to do what you want and doing it while you are still learning makes it tougher.  We've all been there.
