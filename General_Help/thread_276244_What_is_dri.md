### Post by kuja on 2006-10-12
DRI is X's Direct Rendering Infrastructure. Most of X's graphics drivers (which are in fact quite seperate from DRI itself) need to have DRI enabled for the driver to work, it should be enabled by default. 

Your problems are more than likely with the graphics driver yourself. What to do with that? I don't know. (I gave up on using an ATI card in my linux box a long time ago, seeing as the drivers nvidia provides are far better)
