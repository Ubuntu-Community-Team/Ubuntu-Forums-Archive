### Post by herrma29 on 2007-07-30
I have been on a search lately for a good music player for gnome. I can't help but feel like kde has gnome beat on music players. The best one I have been able to find is Listen, but I have had a number of problems with duplication of library entries. I have around 2000 songs on my computer and the library listing shows 6000.

So, here is a list of attributes I think make a well rounded music player:

Lyrics fetching (preferably like Amarok or Listen rather than having to open a new window everytime or search through infinite menus...basically lyrics on their own tab)
Context menu
Last.fm support
Wikipedia info fetching (I like Listen's where it gives you the option for artist, album, or song info)
Musicbrainz support
M4a, MP4 tagging support
Library organizing (Amarok is the only one that I have found that allows for a decent level of organizing)
Related artist info from Last.fm
Ability to remove dead and duplicate songs
Music stores, free and nonfree (I really like Jamendo in rhythmbox)
Some degree of freedom in the placement of items in the window (ability to move context menu to left/right, etc)
Web Radio (I have yet to find a player that supports pandora, so if anyone knows of one, I would really appreciate it)
Automatic cover art fetching
This might sound ridiculous but, a STOP button


I really appreciate what Amarok has to offer. The way it handles tagging and organization of my library is really a positive. And the huge number of available plugins is a definite plus too. The only beef I have with it is it isn't gnome.

Hmm, maybe it would just be easier to have a way to theme kde programs with gnome themes...


Anywho, feel free to add suggestions and ideas so maybe we can get some people with the abilities working on either plugins or development of a music player with the attributes we want all rolled into one.
