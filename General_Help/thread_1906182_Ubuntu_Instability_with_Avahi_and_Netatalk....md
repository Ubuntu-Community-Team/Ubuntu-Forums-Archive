### Post by SchneiderIS on 2012-01-08
This may be more of a rant as I don't expect I will find any solution.  It seems contrary to the nature of ubuntu to establish and maintain a stable environment as I have lost weeks of my life to the environment.  So much so that if it were not for a couple of software that I need to run in Linux I think I would move back to Windows.

Avahi and Netatalk have been around for years and continue to supposedly progress yet I cannot get a stable build from them.  This is not entirely true as I had a stable build under 9.04 but everything else in this world pushes you to have to install the latest and greatest in order to do the things that you want.  Along the way though stability and thus usability went out the window.  I find myself constantly having to figure out why something as simple as Avahi and Netatalk will suddenly stop working.  They were configured and working but then they start to fail, restart, or don't run at all.

I have Automatic updates turned off on my machine yet when I come back to it a week later there are a bunch of updates ready for install.  When I say I have it turned off I mean I told it to "Never" check.  The reason I do so is that invariably an update will break my box, almost always Avahi and Netatalk.

I did one patch to go to .14 of the kernel and ever since then I have to force a reset (hard button on the box) to get into the grub menu and pick the last kernel for booting into.  The joke here is that that kernel didn't work for booting until I installed this latest one. 

The new user interface is a complete disaster too.  I tried working with it for a few weeks and after giving myself a concussion I implemented the old gnome workaround which is only a halfway fix.

I suppose the answer will be to go through the logs but here too is a disaster.  What log file to look at and what do the line entries actually mean!  There are plenty of log files but for the most part they are completely useless!!!  How about someone taking the time and interest in an actual problem solving tool.  They could start with a Log viewer that is more than just a glorified editor (it isn't really even that much) and put in tree structures, colour coding of entries, and cross links to other log files based on the activity at the same time as an event.  Most importantly would be links to actual solutions.

At one point I had thought, back when I was new to this all, that I must have screwed up an install.  By now though I have installed so many copies of Linux (Ubuntu and Fedora) that I can't buy into that.  They all start to do their own thing and fail to work so easily.

If I had a viable replacement for MythTV and its ability to schedule and distribute content I would probably be gone from Linux.
