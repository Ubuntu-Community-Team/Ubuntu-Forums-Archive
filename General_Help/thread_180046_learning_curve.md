### Post by rich.bradshaw on 2006-05-21
I don't use Ubuntu, but use Kanotix - it's better at detecting hardware and doesn't use the ugly Gnome! :) It's based on Debian Sid as well though, so it's similar in a lot of ways.

I think that the Debian distros are better for people who want to use the computer for everyday tasks. I don't care that things aren't 100% optimized, they are fast enough for me. I don't have to compile everything from scratch or mess around with dependancies. I can learn how to administer my own pc using prebuilt tools.

Gentoo particularly, but also distros like Slackware go back to basics a bit more. If you can use those distros then you can say you really understand your pc, but for people like me I just don't care!

On the other hand, having GBs of packages I have never used is rather silly!
