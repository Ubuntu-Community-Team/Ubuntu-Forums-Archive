### Post by robmypro on 2009-02-14
For all those people that are just starting this journey into Ubuntu Linux, all I can say is...stick with it. I tried 3 or 4 different distros a few years back, and basically gave up. So I stayed with Windows for a few more years. Then about a week ago (on a whim) I decided to give Linux another try. Well, it was a whim if you factor in I had another week of Vista problems. So I did a search and read a few articles on Ubuntu. Well, every day that passes makes me more pissed I stayed with Windows this long.

With Ubuntu, I am experiencing none of the frustration I had with Fedora, Red Hat, etc. This is the first Linux distro that helped me break the Windows habit. I still use Windows. I need it for a few apps that have no Linux version. But it is relegated to the B team now. 

In less than a week I went from having no idea what I was doing to running Ubuntu desktop and server, sharing our files (samba), and having VirtualBox hosting a Windows XP internet game. It is all working smoothly, and now I am focused on backups, security and other stuff.  

My next project will be converting another Windows 2003 Server to Ubuntu Server. This machine runs VMware and is the workhorse of our company. Win2k3 will once again become a VB guest, and thus be relegated to the B team. After this I have no plans of ever buying another OS from Gates and Company again. Total crap.

There is a sort of cloud you have to get through when you start, but once you get through that cloud the beauty and elegance of Ubuntu Linux become obvious. This is great stuff. My hats off to the developers, and the community. 

And to those just starting off? Stick with it! Ubuntu is worth the effort! 

I also want to thank the countless souls on this forum that helped me out! I still have a lot to learn, but your help has been appreciated!

Happy Valentines Day!
