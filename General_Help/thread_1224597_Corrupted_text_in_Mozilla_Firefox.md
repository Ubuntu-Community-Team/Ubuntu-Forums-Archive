### Post by earthpigg on 2009-08-16
my general advice to you, madam, whenever you get _*any*_ used computer would be to do a fresh install of the operating system of your choice to deal with any/all customizations the previous owner has done. Ubuntu (linux in general) is *extremely* customizable and modifiable - and there is no guarantee that the previous owner was not doing all sorts of silly things on a regular basis that you will end up having to deal with for months and months to come. it certainly looks like the last thing this guy did before selling the laptop was mess up the install in some obscure way that may or may not be easy to figure out and fix.

If your operating system of choice for a fresh install is Ubuntu, (your computer, your choice. I am obviously a fan and prefer it, but you may or may not.) Ubuntu is *incredibly* easy to install.

the directions found below are very bullcrap free and easy to follow.
