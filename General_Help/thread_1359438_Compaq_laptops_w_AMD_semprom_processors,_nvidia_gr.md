### Post by ram2500 on 2009-12-19
> **fragos said:**
> IMHO seems you may be dealing with users new to Ubuntu. Play it safe and install the 32 bit version.

This is a not too bad idea. Aside from beginners, there are many "intermediate" users, including myself, that have 64 bit machines but use 32 bit software (although I have 64bit Windows). It's less of a headache in terms of compatibility with program choice and worse, wireless drivers. Besides, a lot of entry and mid-level notebooks come with less than 4 GB of memory, which anything above 3 GB requires 64-bit software to access the extra memory. 

We are still living in a 32-bit world, even though there are programs and OSes being released in 64-bit. In all actuality, 64-bit may (and is) an advantage in Windows, where I can use more memory and have a wider communication bus utilized by the software, thus making everything faster, but Ubuntu and Linux in general fares well in 32-bit alone.
