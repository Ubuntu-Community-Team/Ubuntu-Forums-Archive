### Post by canadianwriterman on 2005-12-06
EDIT: THE FOLLOWING IS SIMPLY A COMMENTARY AND NOT INTENDED AS A REAL LETTER

Dear Mr. Shuttleworth,

Last September I was introduced to the wonders of Linux with a distro called Xandros. I tried Linspire and SUSE before stumbling across a review of something called Ubuntu. I came to your Web site and was inspired. I downloaded and installed your distro and was pleased. I went to your forums for help and was thrilled.

Then, I recently started to read the posts in a couple of threads that made me sick. They were full of vendictive and venom. Where had the Ubuntu philosophy I had come to admire disappeared to?

In fairness, the open warfare seemed limited to a small group. The majority of posts continued to be as friendly and helpful as they had always been. But, as the season of peace and brotherhood approaches, I can't help but have a burning feeling in the pit of my stomach that something has changed. Maybe it's the natural result of having more people involved in your community. Maybe it's the change of seasons. I don't know.

All I know is that I pray for the return of the philosophy of Ubuntu to these forums. Thank you for listening and for continuing to provide us with the finest distro available.

Best of the season.

Sincerely,

Stephen Wilson (canadianwriterman)
