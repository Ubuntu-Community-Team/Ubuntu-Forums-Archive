### Post by QIII on 2012-04-17
First:  Be prepared for what may be a shocking change in the desktop environment.

Ubuntu now uses Unity, which is a radical departure from what you may be  used to.  Doesn't mean it's good or bad.  It's very different, which  will take some getting used to.

You might as well try 11.10 since it is the most current release.  12.04  is just around the corner, but it is still a Beta.  Darn good one, but a  Beta.

To replace what you have (assuming you don't have any data on a three  year old computer that you want to dust off -- if you haven't bothered  with it since 2009, how much is it worth to you?), just install the new  version.  That will overwrite what is there.

"Best" is a matter of opinion.  I don't care much for the Unity interface, so I use KDE and Xfce.  That's me.  Your choice will be a matter of your tastes.
