### Post by StephanG on 2011-07-27
I'm going to assume that the overheating problem didn't exist before you installed Kubuntu.

Does it also overheat in Windows?

Because in that case, the problem might have nothing to do with Kubuntu.  My PC had a similar problem when I tried to 'optimize' things in my BIOS.

Otherwise, it sounds as though Kubuntu is working very hard trying to do... something.  If you press Ctrl + Esc, which processes are using the most CPU power?

If there's nothing there, it could be that Kubuntu is simply using a lot of power over all, I suggest you take a look inside 'Power Management' in System Settings.

And lastly, it could also be that the problem has nothing to with the laptop itself.  If you put it down on a soft surface, such as a bed, often it just doesn't get enough air into the slits at the bottom to keep it cool.

And, well... That's all I can think of.  Hope you find out what's wrong.
