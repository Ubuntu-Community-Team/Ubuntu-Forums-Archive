### Post by prodezigner on 2007-07-26
My home network uses a Brother HL-2070N. It's cost effective, and I've printed over 500 pages with it (not a lot), but the toner level is still at 97%. And that's with no toner save mode on.

If you decide on this printer, it uses the 2060 drivers (built in with Ubuntu *The Fawn*) and it works seamlessly.

The printer supports CAT5, USB and LPT ports. I've got mine hooked up with CAT5 since we have eight computers in our network.

AND it's cheap... I got mine for 150.

### Post by npman on 2007-07-26
Thanks for all of the recommendations, it appears that there is something to this whole HP thing.  :)

I have looked over linuxprinting.org, but I approach their reviews with some caution.  I work with several sites that use Ubuntu thin client networks, and use many different printers, and my experiences are often quite different than the things I see on linuxprinting,org.

I suspect that there is a difference between what some people mean by "works perfectly" and what I mean by "works perfectly". 

For some, if the printer can manage to print a page of ASCII text without exploding, that is the criteria for "works perfectly", But I have had such printers simply crumble in the face of heavy daily use with office documents, etc. My folks want to use several high-capacity trays and have the output come out of Tray 1 when you select Tray 1 in the driver- this sort of straightforwardness seems to be a distant dream with the Lexmark.

I think we may end up with something along the lines of an HP 4xxx...

I just got a used HP LaserJet 1022n on eBay and it does in fact work in Linux.....I'm using Feisty.

If you do get one and you want to use it via ethernet (router). I think the main thing is to make sure that:

A) The device type = Socket/HP DirectJet

B) The device URI =   socket://yourprintersIP:9100

C) Driver = foo2zjs (recommended)


A cool thing I just found out about while installing this printer is that you can manage your printers/CUPS system through your web browser....it's how I installed my printer......type      [http://localhost:631/](http://localhost:631/)   into your browser! I prefer it.
