### Post by WinRiddance on 2012-01-20
**UPDATE ...**

For Christmas we purchased two of these for our home, and one for our parents ... MagickJack recently came out with a "PLUS" version of their device. It costs $70 bucks initially, then **$30 per year** for the entire service which includes 24 x 7 phone calling nationwide as well as to Canada, with options for Overseas calling for as little as .02 cents per minute to dozens of countries.

Although still not fully Linux compatible, the MJ PLUS can also be plugged directly into a wall outlet and the Internet router. So we bought two new DECT6.0 phones, each with two additional handsets. After registering the two MJ devices via Windows (it's the only thing that requires Windows, the registration process), we plugged the main units into our router. Then we placed the two handsets where they were convenient for us in the home. Now we have two phone lines in the house, both powered by MagicJack, and it's costing us about $30 per year. Since we also purchased the 5 year extension for a discount price, **the total cost of our phone service** for each line during the next 5 years is going to be $170 dollars, which includes the price of the MJ. Wow, $170 bucks for 5 years of phone service.

**The voice clarity is nothing short of phenomenal**, nothing at all like the "old" MJ devices. Initially we did notice a little static so we attached a DSL line filter directly to each MJ device followed by connecting the phone line to the DSL filter as opposed to connecting it directly to the MJ. We ported our original ATT number over too, since we didn't want a (free) new number. Heck, for $10 bucks per year you can even have a personal vanity number if that's your thing ...

**The bottom line** ... If you want to save yourself a small fortune on phone costs, especially over the long term, then even Linux users like us can now benefit with the new MagicJack PLUS since it can be connected directly to one of your router LAN ports. We have 4 Linux machines in our home and two MJ devices. Since our router only had 4 LAN ports, we installed a second (Netgear) Router that's connected to the primary router, thereby extending our LAN ports from 4 to 7. Nothing to configure, just plugged it in and it worked. **It all works smooth as silk and it was a breeze to set up!**
