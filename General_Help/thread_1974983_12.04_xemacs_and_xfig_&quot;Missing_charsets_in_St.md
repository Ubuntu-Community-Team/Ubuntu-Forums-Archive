### Post by ajdoakwood on 2012-05-06
Since upgrading to ubuntu 12.04 I have problems with xemacs, gnu emacs and xfig.

xemacs and xfig both display the warning "Missing charsets in String to FontSet conversion"
after starting. 

xemacs does not display text correctly all the time. From time to time text disappears from
the display, although it is not deleted from the file. 

xfig often hangs when exporting to eps. The only way I've found to get round this is to kill it. It crashes completely approximately 1 in 10 times an export is made.

Any help in diagnosing the cause of these problems would be welcome.:

If I switch to gnome classic (no effects)  these problems go away: that is xemacs displays characters perfectly,
(and in particular ctrl-K does not make lines of text disappear even though they remain in the 
buffer). 

Xfig no longer crashes on every third file opened. Also, everthing runs more quickly.

There are no longer strange lines appearing in odd places e.g. in menus.

Unity and gnome+effects are as bad as each other in causing these problems.
