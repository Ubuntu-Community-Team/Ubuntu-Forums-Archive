### Post by MelissaCutler on 2011-01-09
Hi,

I am relatively new to Ubuntu.  I recently installed Ubuntu NBR (netbook book remix) on my netbook, which originally came with Windows 7 and now dual boots.

The machine is fairly new, and usually has a fantastic battery life (7 hours(ish) in Windows).  Also, in Windows I use sleep mode alot (Windows is so slow starting up and shutting down....) so for a while I was practically always putting it to sleep rather than shutting it down.  That all worked fine when I was doing everything in Windows....

Now things have changed; I've learnt to use Ubuntu and I like it (a lot)!  Unfortunately I have noticed a problem with sleep mode.  It seems that in Ubuntu, when I put the laptop to sleep, the battery discharges exceptionally quickly; even more quickly than it does when the netbook is in use.  Yesterday for example I used my netbook on the train for about an hour or so, as I approached the station I was changing at I looked at the battery monitor (the icon indicated about 50-60% percent of the battery life remained).  I then put the computer to sleep.

Within about 10 minutes I was back on a train, and I woke the computer up again.  The power indicator icon now showed an empty battery coloured red, and when I clicked on it I saw an estimate of 15 minutes battery time remaining.

I thought: maybe that battery is just cold, and the power monitor is thus confused and misjudging the remaining charge.  But when I touched the battery it felt quite warm.  I waited a minute or so to see if the battery monitor would revise it's estimate of battery life, and it did (it started to say 10 minutes remaining!).  At that point I restarted, to see if that would fix the battery monitor applet.

After restart the situation was the same.  So I shut down.

What is going on?  Is the battery shorting out or something when Ubuntu goes to sleep?  Is this damaging the laptop or battery?  Could this develop into a safety issue?

I have changed my power management settings to hibernate when the lid is closed, and from now on I will not use sleep in Ubuntu.  But should I report this?  (where? how?)  I am worried that other users may be affected by this problem.

I've never noticed this happening when Windows is put to sleep on the same machine, but I have noticed it repeatedly when Ubuntu is put to sleep.  My netbook is a Samsung NP-N150 (or just N150).  It has a special feature regarding the battery; to reduce battery wear, it can be set up to never charge the battery above 80% (thus preventing overcharge from wearing out the battery).  Could this non-standard feature be confusing the Ubuntu battery monitor, and be related to the problem?

Thank you,

Melissa
