### Post by ASchweitzer on 2009-03-04
To determine whether or not your graphics drivers are installed and working can be fairly lengthy, but older machines with non-onboard graphics are generally well-supported. There are plenty of general threads here, as well as hardware specific guides.

For WoW, you'll want to check out Wine, which will let you run the windows version, without any problems as far as I know. Check out [http://www.winehq.org]("http://www.winehq.org") for that. To install it use synaptic or Add/Remove Programs.

For student stuff, check out Open Office (you'll also want to install the added features of OO through synaptic, for doing formulas and stuff). Also, look into Tomboy Notes (installed by default), or Basket (I besieve is the name) for note-taking.
