### Post by andrew.46 on 2009-04-17
Hi tuckeroil,

In my windows days I also used Dreamweaver, actually the version before Adobe took over so I guess it was a while ago. My experience with Dreamweaver leads me to suggest that there is not, and possibly never will be, a Linux equivalent for this program. I suspect your best bet is to run Dreamweaver in a VM or run it on a windows partition as previous posts have suggested. Drives are big these days...

But the reason I do not use Dreamweaver now is a little more basic. If you stand back a little from Dreamweaver, and perhaps even from your own life at times, and think 'do I really need all of this?' the answer in almost all cases is 'no'.

Handcoding html, with a little assistance from programs like bluefish, brings you closer to the message you are trying to put across to the world and is often the difference between a glossy site devoid of meaning and a less ostentatious site that delivers. And if you say 'well, I can't run the 1,000 pages for monstrous.corporation.org by handcoding' I think *this* is the time for some examination of your own goals.

And yes, I am in the middle of a series of lectures on Buddhism :-).

All the best,

Andrew
