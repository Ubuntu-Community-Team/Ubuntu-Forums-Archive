### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-06-25
Your music isn't "in iTunes". It's on your Windows hard disk.

If you go into iTunes and into the Preferences, you can see the location that iTunes stores your music. Go there using Windows Explorer and copy the files to a flash drive or external hard disk, and then transfer them to the Linux partition. Tell Rhythmbox to get its music from that folder.

If it's music that you've bought from the iTunes Music Store, then it's probably encrypted with Apple's "Digital Restrictions Management" that is designed to stop you from playing your music outside iTunes. Fortunately, Apple still allows you to burn an audio CD of your music from within iTunes - so do that, and then re-rip the music in Ubuntu.

There might still be a program floating about that can intercept the music as iTunes decrypts it, and then save it to a file, but the last time I told anyone the name of the program I got given an infraction here. It's been a long time since I used it, so I don't know if it's still around and if it still works. You could always try looking for it.
