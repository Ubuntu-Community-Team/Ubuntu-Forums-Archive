### Post by doppel.ganger on 2011-11-17
Idea: This may or may not work. I suggest you back up your stuff before trying this. Anyway, download the 11.10 iso ([http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download]("http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download")) and burn it do a disk. Start up from the disk as if you're doing a fresh install. Let the disk start up like normal. When you get to the page asking you what you want to do (Try Ubuntu or Install), choose install. Keep going until it gives you options on what to do with the Ubuntu installation (side-by-side, upgrade, just Ubuntu, or something else), choose upgrade (if it's there) and follow the instructions.

If you don't want to do this, I suggest you back up your stuff and do a fresh install. Doing a fresh install gives you the programs that will work best with your Ubuntu release, and therefore runs faster.

Good luck!

-DG
