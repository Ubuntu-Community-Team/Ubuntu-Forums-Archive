### Post by zugu on 2006-11-01
Hello.

I just installed Edgy. While browsing through the menus, to see what's changed - apart from the fact that System>Administration>Disks is missing :( - I noticed something called "Keyring Manager". "All right!", I said to myself, let's see what this is all about. I clicked on it and a very plain window appeared. Only it was hard to tell by the looks what could it be used for. "Allright!", I said to myself again, let me look in the Help section of it, I'll surely figure something out. I clicked on Help>Contents.

A nice Help window appeared, with only two sections: Keyring manager - a blank section, and Introduction - a section containing a single word: "Welcome!".

I know that this might seem hilarious to some of you, but think about it a little. Isn't it frustrating? I almost felt like this was a prank, but in the next second I thought about the developers/QA testers. How could they include such a pointless application in Edgy? Don't get me wrong, maybe this tool is valuable for those more knowledgeable of you, but for me is just pointless, as long as I can't find out what does it do.


This is something that should not happen in a distro such as Ubuntu.
