### Post by phillw on 2010-08-17
Well, if it is a grub issue, you can always try re-installing Grub.

**_When a good Grub2 becomes a poorly Grub2_**

Also known as ** HELP !! My computer will not boot **

Very scary, I know. But - Don't panic - your data is safe & so is the windows area if you dual boot !!

First stop would be over to Section 11 of --> [The Grub2 Guide](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1195275)
Followed by Section 15 of the same thread.

If you've still no joy, then a re-installation of Grub2 looks to be what is needed, for that you'll want to be heading over to Section 13 of the above thread.

It looks scary, but it isn't - Do as it says & you will be up & running in no time.

There are some other resources for checking things out at [Grub](http://forum.phillw.net/viewtopic.php?f=4&t=5) which is where I keep my list of links for Grub problems.
