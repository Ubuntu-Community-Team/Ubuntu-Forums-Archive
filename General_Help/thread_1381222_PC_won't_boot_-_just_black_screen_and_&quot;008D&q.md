### Post by t0p on 2010-01-14
I haven't used my Hardy-running desktop computer for a while.  So, I turned it on earlier and it booted up fine.  I logged in, then started VirtualBox and clicked to start my XP virtual machine.  I left the room to make some coffee while it all got going.

When I came back in, the monitor was black except for the characters "008D" in the bottom right corner.  No keys or mouse clicking did anything, so I finally turned it off by holding in the power button.

I switched it on again, I got an "Intel" screen telling me about the mouse and keyboard, and that the USB mass storage (external HDD) was loading, and in the bottom right were the characters "0078".  Then the screen went all black again except for those characters in the bottom right corner that now said "008D" again.

And that's all that's happening: I restart the computer by turning it off and on at the power switch; and the screen is either black with "008D" in the bottom right corner; or I get the "Intel" screen with characters "0078", which then turns to blsck screen and "008D".

If I hold down F2 while I hit the power button, I get the BIOS screen, and I can set boot device order and the rest of it: and once I've finished with that and press to continue boot, I get that black screen and "008D".  I can open the CD drive by pressing its button: I've put a live CD in the tray and rebooted, but I didn't boot from the CD - just that black screen and "008D".

Anyone got an idea what's going on???

### Post by t0p on 2010-01-14
Okay, I appear to have fixed it.

I sat for a while thinking what could have caused this weird fault: and I had a dim recollection of something similar happening when I upgraded the RAM in a desktop.  I put new RAM in, switched on, and I got a similar thing -black screen and numbers.  So, I opened up the case, removed the RAM and replaced it, then rebooted: and it worked!  My word!

Remember I said I hadn't used this computer for a while?  Well, it was dusty, so I gave it a wipe before I first switched it on.  So I'm thinking maybe I knocked some dust into the case, onto the RAM contacts?  So the dust interfered with the circuit, until I pulled out the RAM and reinsterted it?

Well, that's all I can think of.  I've marked this thread [SOLVED].  Let's hope it stays solved, eh?
