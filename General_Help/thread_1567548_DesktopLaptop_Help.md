### Post by harrismh777 on 2010-09-08
Oh, ok. The netbook edition is a special purpose edition for (well) netbooks, and those are different that notebooks. 

Your desktop version should work fine on your laptop (notebook) depending on the age of the notebook, its chipset, its wifi manufacturer, and of course its video. Some notebooks like the Lenovo (ThnkPads) machines run ubuntu great. Some of them (like the acers, and other such) may or may not depending on a truckload of issues. 

Usually the main issues will be the audio, the wifi, and often (even today) the video, although less frequently than earlier years. So, try it. I have resurrected several old notebooks (less than five years) either because they won't run the new windoze stuff, or because viri clobbered them. I am still running my R30 ThnkPad (2001 model) today... with 9.04... works great... before that it was openSuSE, and before that it was RedHat Fedora. I have even replaced the ccfl once... that thing is like a timex... just keeps on ticking.   sorry , I digressed.

Anyway, try it... no blunder... it will either work or not... you won't loose anything sounds like.
