### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-12-14
How about a USEFUL suggestion -- instead of yet another "Windows Sucks" derogatory remark ...

There are a couple of AV websites that offer free, bootable, versions of their AV apps.  These are Kasperksy and Avira.  I think Avast might as well, but I don't know.

If you go to these sites, you can download an ISO of a bootable CD, burn that to CD, boot from that, and run the AV against your Win8 setup.

That's a LOT less work than trashing it and starting over with a completely different OS.
