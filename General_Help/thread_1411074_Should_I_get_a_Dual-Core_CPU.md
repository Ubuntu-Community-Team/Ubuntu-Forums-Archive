### Post by blur xc on 2010-02-19
I think the os decides who uses what core of your processor- but maybe I'm wrong.

As far as individual apps go- it depends on if they are coded to take advantage of multiple cores.

Also, beside core count and clock cycles, the cpu cache has a lot to do w/ perceived processor speed.  If you are going from an old single core cpu (pentium 4 3ghz is what I had last) to a newer, slower clock cycle cpu (core 2 duo 2.8ghz, just as an example) your computer might actually end up faster even on single threaded apps.
