### Post by mlomker on 2005-10-16
A lot of people have mentioned their displays being set at 60 hz even though they are capable of more.  The first thing to try re-running **sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg** and entering the Horizontal and Vertical sync rates from your monitor manual by selecting the 'advanced' option.  I'd also recommend selecting only **one** resolution.

When you select one resolution the script will provide you with a modeline (32-bit systems) that you can then tweak the refresh rate on.

For example:
```

Section "Generic Monitor"
   Identifier "Generic Monitor"
   Modeline	"1280x800@60" 83.91 1280 1312 1624 1656 800 816 824 841
EndSection

```


Modelines force the video driver to use specific settings at a particular resolution.  If you use an older monitor/video card (or don't like the rate that your monitor is recommending to the driver) then you can try an [online mode generator]("http://www.sh.nu/nvidia/gtf.php") to create a new line that uses a different refresh rate.

The /var/log/Xorg.0.log contains X startup information, including the modes that the driver detects.

### Post by SickTwist on 2005-10-28
FunkyPoopMonkey,
I know it seems fustrating but don't let it discourage you. I have posted how to fix this issue [here]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=443513#post443513") but you must do a little research about your monitor in order to do it.

You need to find the **horizontal sync** (usually in kHz) and **vertical refresh** (usually in Hz) rates for *your* specific monitor. [COLOR="Red"]If you use numbers outside the range your monitor can support, it could damage the monitor.[/COLOR] If you have any printed documentation that came with your monitor then these numbers might be listed there. Otherwise, you might have to look online on the manufacturer's website or try searching google with the manufacturer and model of your monitor. We can try to help you with this if you give us some more information about your monitor. The numbers will look something like this:

Scanning frequency range:
[INDENT]Horizontal  50.2 kHz  (+/- 500Hz)[/INDENT]
[INDENT]Vertical    47 to 80 Hz[/INDENT]

In some cases, Ubuntu detects the monitor and its supported resolutions and refresh rates just fine, but ends up picking a resolution that does not support anything but 60hz, by default.  I have an older 15 inch monitor on one of my computers that fully supports 75hz (And maybe even 85hz) at 640x480, 800x600, and 1024x768, but Ubuntu picked 1280x1024, by default, on a fresh install that only supported 60hz and had the irritating, visible flickering.  When I removed "1280x1024" from the "Modes" lines in the X config, it picked 75hz @ 1024x768 on that monitor, without me having to specify any vertical or horizontal refresh rates manually in the config (as in having them commented out or not having the vertical or horizontal refresh settings lines in the config at all).
