### Post by James78 on 2010-10-12
I think that is fine. I'm not 100% sure, but I think Ubuntu is GPL v3, most software on Linux/Ubuntu is. Anyways, if you edit software that is GPL v3, you HAVE to keep it free and open source. Since it sounds like you are including software with it, it should be fine, as long as it doesn't violate THEIR license, and you might have to keep everything free.

Bottom line, minus the mumbo jumbo up there, you should be perfectly fine.

Disclaimer: I may be completely wrong, as I'm not a lawyer or "license professional", and these are my initial opinions on careful consideration of the subject. However, I'm pretty certain that most of what I said is right, and at very least holds mostly true. You should wait for someone that has more experience to answer however.
