### Post by Joscha on 2019-05-19
Hi, 
 I'm only posting this thread because it's a question I used to have and that I have come across in loads of places: "How can I add Google Drive to Ubuntu?" Here, I'm talking about 19.04 Disco Dingo, using the Out-of-the-Box Ubuntu desktop. 

 **Short answer**: to the best of my knowledge, you cant.

 **Better answer**: you so don't need it -Nautilus can do the trick.


 Let me explain the **Better Answer**:


[LIST=1]
[*]Press [Superkey], then type something like "Set". The "Settings" should come up. Open it.
[*]Look for "Online Accounts" in the menu to the left. Click.
[*]There's the option to "Add an Account". Choose it and find "Google".
[*]A popup errm... pops up, asking you to fill in your Gmail address, then password. Do so.
[*]A new popup will ask you to confirm that you allow GNOME to access your Google account. If you're sure, accept.
[*]Another popup pops up, asking about permissions. Give or take all the permissions you want, then confirm by just closing it.
[*]You may have to reboot to apply all the changes.
[*]Nautilus should now be able to mount your Google Drive as if it were a (rather slow) hard disk -you an access it via "Files" (i.e. Nautilus). Also, unless you told it not to, your GNOME calendar app should now sync with your Google Calendar(-s).
[/LIST]

  So, if Nautilus can do it, do you really need an additional app? 

  See you,
