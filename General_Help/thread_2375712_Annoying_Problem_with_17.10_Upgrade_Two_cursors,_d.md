### Post by SuperVectorGirl on 2017-10-26
Ever since I upgraded from 17.04 to 17.10, I have had two cursors on my screen, one apparently for my Wacom, the other for a bluetooth mouse that work independently from one another and I have tried everything I could think of, installing Wacom drivers and restricted extras and uninstalling and reinstalling stuff, even unplugging the bluetooth sensor and removing the batteries from the mouse, but still, two annoying cursors! Any help would be greatly appreciated, thanks in advance.

---

### Post by SuperVectorGirl on 2017-10-29
I found an answer. I reverted back to Xorg session when I logged in (the drop down at login), and I no longer have the problem. There is also a bug report about this with the Wayland display, which is default in 17.10. I posted on Ask Ubuntu, and received replies here:

[https://askubuntu.com/questions/970440/ive-got-two-cursors-on-my-screen-on-recent-upgrade-from-17-04-to-17-10](https://askubuntu.com/questions/970440/ive-got-two-cursors-on-my-screen-on-recent-upgrade-from-17-04-to-17-10)
