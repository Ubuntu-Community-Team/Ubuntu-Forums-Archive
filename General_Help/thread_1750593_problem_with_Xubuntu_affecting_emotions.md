### Post by geokker on 2011-05-05
I upgraded to Xubuntu 11.04 the day it came out. In the short weeks since, I've realised I must uninstall it and go back to Windows or buy another Mac. 

It was blindingly sunny afternoon in Soho. I was to attend a meeting at a swish post production house to discuss bandwidth and storage solutions for their growing needs. I was early, it was nearing lunch so I made towards an underrated eaterie I had recently discovered. Avoiding the throngs of tourists and office workers released for vittles, the empty table to the side of the stucco doorway beckoned, as the splinter of a memory from Tuscany. 

It was then that I broke down. The speed of boot. Thunar supporting SFTP. It had all become too much. My mind had become trained to believe joy was but moments. The configurable notification area and panel fly-out menu pushed me over the edge. A maelstrom of confused thoughts, contradictions and doubt consumed me. Who was I? The compositor was completely stable consumed all most no additional resources. 24 pixels for the panel and no other encroachments. So stable. So stable. The weeping. The terrible weeping.

Full screen, HD Flash plays flawlessly on a Dual Core Pentium.

I'm afraid.
