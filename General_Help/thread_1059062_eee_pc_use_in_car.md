### Post by AlexBellisBrown on 2009-02-03
In answer to the OP, the power cable itself transforms 110v(America) or 230v (Europe) Down to 12.7v, so hence, a car adapter would up the 12v to a 110v, only for the power leads transformer to take it back down to 12.7v. Its a long way around, but its a correct way. Hooking up a battery "direct" is slighty unstable, bearing in mind voltage drop (say for example turning the cars lights on) could cause undervoltage (or voltage drop), and mess up your laptops internals. Id stick with the transformer and the origional cable, its much safer, and the transformer takes any small drops and evens it out. :)
