### Post by warped0ne on 2008-10-31
I have a 19" monitor with a 1440x900 resolution.  This is great for watching movies, looking at websites, and playing games, but it's kind of hard on the eyes when trying to pick out icons and text on the screen, especially since I'm near-sighted.  So, I started looking for a theme that would give me bigger icons and screen text that also looked nice.  I found the Ubuntu Mobile Desktop.  This makes the on-screen text a little larger and the icons larger.  

This package depends on a couple of other programs, the most notable is Cell Writer.  If you are using a tablet PC or a touchscreen monitor, cell writer will help with on screen typing or writing.  

Anyway, if you'd like to give this a try, but you're not quite sure how, I've made a little video (no sound) to show you.

Just click [here]("http://home.mchsi.com/~tk1/mobile/mobile.html") and you will be directed the Flash video.  

If you decide to do this, but end up not liking the changes, there is also a uninstall instruction in the video.  

Hope this helps.
