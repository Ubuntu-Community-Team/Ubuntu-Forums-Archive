### Post by gotonpo on 2007-08-02
I'm stuck on Xubuntu.  I felt Ubuntu's Gnome could use a little more performance (although it's by no means slow), and XFCE provides exactly what I want.  It's a great halfway point between something as full featured as Gnome and something as stripped down as Fluxbox.  Plus, it has it's own compositing feature built in, so I can have transparency w/o turning off all the bloat Beryl has.  All the Gnome apps I was used to still work great.

And it's not only for older/slow machines.  I'm running on a 2.13 Core 2 Duo w/ 2 gigs of RAM & dedicated nVidia card.  You can feel a performance increase even on newer machines.

Try it!
