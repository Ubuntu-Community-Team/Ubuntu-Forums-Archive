### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-09-04
It's completely up to Acer, but my guess would be NO -- they will not give you a rebate for the OS YOU decided not to use.

Furthermore, as I already mentioned in your OTHER thread about this very same topic, since this came preinstalled, it is an OEM license, and that is tied to the physical machine on which it is first installed.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-02-16
I'm not so familiar with other parts of the world, and IANAL, but:

1. It's usually not illegal to bundle two things together and sell them as a single product.
2. It's not illegal to NOT provide a refund for part of a single product that the consumer doesn't wish to have/use.
3. If you make a big enough fuss to your computer manufacturer, they might send you some money to get you to be quiet and keep you happy, but this does not mean that the OEM copy of Windows is a separate part that should be refunded if unused.

If you want to make enough noise about it, you might get a "Windows refund" - but as far as I'm aware, in law you are not actually *entitled* to one. Just make sure that you're willing to put in the time and effort to pursue this.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-02-17
Come on folks, quit whining and get real!  You're NOT being forced to pay for something you don't want or don't need.  In their advertising, the OEMs clearly indicate what comes WITH the PC you purchase. 

Sellers have the legal right to bundle products as they see fit.  Buyers have the legal right to NOT purchase them.

If I buy a new car, and it comes with Goodyear tires, and I decide NOT to use those but, instead, replace them after purchase with Michelin tires -- do you really thing the car dealer is going to refund me the cost of the four tires?  NO.  I made the decision to purchase the car WITH the tires.  What I do after that is my business.  It is not up to the dealer to refund me for things I later decide I do not want anymore.

If you're really hung up on NOT paying for a MS Windows OS on a new PC, then consider the following options:
1) Contact the seller in advance and see if they have a no-OS option.  They just might.
2) Buy a PC from one of the sellers that includes Linux distros, not Windows OSs.
