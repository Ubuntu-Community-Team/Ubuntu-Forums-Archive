### Post by hotani on 2006-12-12
Best I've found so far (I liked Omni Outliner too), as much as I hate to say it... is Google docs. Sorry, there just isn't an acceptible outlining app for linux. 

I do lots of bulleted lists - it is how I organize and brainstorm for most projects. Open Office wouldn't know a good bulleted list if it came and bit it on the arse. Nor would abiword. They are both decent word processors but blow chunks in the complex, nested listing department.

Google docs works very well however, is logical and intuitive - just don't expect to copy/paste into OOo or Abi - it will end up a disaster, I tried it. Google has it figured out, hitting return once in a nested list gives you a new list item, hitting return twice takes you to the next item in the parent list (Open Office just assumes you are done with your list at that point, then you have to mouse up to start a new one). 

If I am on item '2' in google docs, then hit 'tab' - it begins a child list of '1' - Open Office just tabs in the '2'. 

For now it looks like I'm stuck with Google docs. And I *really* wanted to find a good linux alternative...
