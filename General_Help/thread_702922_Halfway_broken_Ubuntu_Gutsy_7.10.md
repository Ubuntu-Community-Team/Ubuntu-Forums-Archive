### Post by socceroos on 2008-02-20
Do you have the original Ubuntu CD you used to install Ubuntu on your machine?

If you do, pop it in your CD drive and reboot your computer. All going well, you should get a pretty little 'ubuntu' menu that asks you if you would like to install ubuntu. Choose the top option which should be 'Start or Install Ubuntu'.

Once Ubuntu has started up (it will take quite a while - so be patient), it should detect your Ubuntu installation and 'mount' it as another 'hard drive'. You should see an Icon on the desktop (when you've booted the CD) which looks like a white hard drive.

Open this 'mount' up by double clicking on it. If the folders you are seeing are like 'Documents and Settings' and 'Program Files' and 'WINDOWS' then you have opened up the wrong 'mount' ......so open up the other one on your desktop (white-ish icon).

Now, if all goes well then you should see a whole heap of different folders. The one you are interested in is the one named 'home'. If you double click on this folder then you should notice a sub folder in it that is named after your normal login username. Double click on it.

Ok, if that worked then you should be looking at all the normal folders you see when you booted Ubuntu before it was broken.

What you need to do now is: plug in your Flash Drive (it will need plenty of free space on it) and copy all your important files from your compter to your flash drive! (Your flash drive should automatically pop up when you plug it into your computer)

Let me know how you go.
