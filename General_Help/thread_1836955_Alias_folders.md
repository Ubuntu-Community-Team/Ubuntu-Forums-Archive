### Post by pederbruusgaard on 2011-08-31
Hi,
I'm new to Ubuntu 11.04 (been using mac), and there's one feature I can't seem to find in Ubuntu that I used a lot in OS 10.x, namely alias folders.

I used it specifically with Dropbox, where I created aliases of the sub-folders of my Dropbox folder and put them in my regular Documents folder, so that whenever I saved or edited files in certain sub-folders of my Documents folder it would automatically also pop up in my Dropbox, both for convenience and as a sort of back-up.

Does anyone know of a similar function in Ubuntu 11.04?

Thanks!

---

### Post by papibe on 2011-08-31
Right click on a folder, then select 'Make Link'. The linked directory will appear on the same directory. After that you can move it wherever you want.

Hope it helps,
Regards.

### Post by mcduck on 2011-09-01
