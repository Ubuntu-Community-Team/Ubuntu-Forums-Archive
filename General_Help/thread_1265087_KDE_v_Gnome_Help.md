### Post by harrismh777 on 2009-09-13
Yes, in general, you can have both desktops installed, run one of them (say KDE) and then run gnome apps like the gnome calculator. 

Your desktop in linux is comprised of several layers.

The first is the X server... xorg.

Then, you have a window manager... and there are many.

Then, you have a "desktop," like gnome, kde, whatever...

Then, you have applications.  Some of those apps have desktop related "hooks" that are designed specifically for kde or gnome.  Others, will run on *any* desktop regardless.
