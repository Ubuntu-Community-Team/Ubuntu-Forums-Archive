### Post by cmost on 2006-10-14
In theory, yes.  Ubuntu should automatically detect the new hardware and adjust.  I'd be sure to reset your video driver to a generic vesa before you attempt this, in the event that your new computer has a different video card (i.e., ATI versus nVidia, etc.) than the old one.

In practice, though, you should be aware that things don't always work according to the way they should.  I recently upgraded my motherboard and video card. While Ubuntu did load up following the upgrade, there were numerous small glitches which prompted me to wipe everything out and start again.  Now my machine is darn near perfect!  :-)  Good luck!
