### Post by NilsE on 2008-06-07
As to the brightness, right click on the panel, select add to panel, and add the brightness applet.  You should then be able to use it to change it.  OR, go into the configuration editor and under gnome-power-manager there are backlight brightness options.  OR, if your laptop had a display brightness set of buttons that should change it also.

AS for the battery, sounds like you may need a new one if it is acting the same in both Vista and Ubuntu.  Just had the same problem with one of my laptops with a 3 year old battery and changed the battery this week and all is well again.

### Post by cybrsaylr on 2008-07-20
How old is your battery?
Those batteries usually have a life of 2-4 years depending on how they are used.
Also they have a type of memory that can mess up your laptop's battery meter readings. It's recommended to fully discharge them every month or two to recalibrate them so you get more accurate battery meter readings from your laptop.
