### Post by issih on 2008-05-16
Emerald only themes the window borders (i.e title bar etc)

Full Themes are made up of:

icons
pointer styles
window borders (emerald/metacity styles) 
control styles (gtk styles)

You can set these all separately by going to System->Preferences->Appearance

Then click customise on the theme tab.

The Controls tab is what will change what you are referring to. Unfortunately the ubuntu wiki page you posted states that there are no gtk themes available yet, so you aren't going to get the full look of those images (they are mock ups).

The best you are likely to get right now is suggested by the comments in the compiz-look page you posted.

You want to go for the clearlooks controls theme, and then adjust the colours on the colour tab to those in this image:

[http://compiz-themes.org/content/preview.php?preview=2&id=74770&file1=74770-1.jpg&file2=74770-2.png&file3=&name=Union+Limited](http://compiz-themes.org/content/preview.php?preview=2&id=74770&file1=74770-1.jpg&file2=74770-2.png&file3=&name=Union+Limited)

I'd just use the little dropper to pick the colour directly from that image.

Having done that the controls are close at least, but won't be exactly what you are after...sorry but that's as close as you can get for now, unless there is actually a union gtk theme lurking out there somewhere
