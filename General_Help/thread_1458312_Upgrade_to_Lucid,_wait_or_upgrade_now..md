### Post by JedMeister on 2010-04-19
It really depends what your angle is and whether you wish to remain an end user, or whether you'd like to get more involved.

If you would just like a stable operating system with as few bugs as possible, I would wait at least 2 weeks after release, probably 3 or 4 weeks if you want to be on the safe side (although there are still no guarantees - after all 9.10 still has some bugs). Also from my experience you will often (but not always) encounter less issues if you do a clean install as opposed to an upgrade. Obviously this will involve more work setting your system up how you like it, but may well be worth the effort.

OTOH If you don't mind dealing with a few bugs (and helping to make the OS better) then do it now (10.04 beta 2 is now available). [This page]("http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/lucid/beta2") may be of interest. Obviously if you need this machine for serious work (or study) this may not be a good idea, but if everyone waited for 4 weeks before they installed then only developers would be finding (and fixing) bugs. Then everyone else would need to wait for 6 or 7 weeks to ensure the new version is stable and the majority of bugs have been addressed! If you go this direction and find any bugs, then have a read [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ReportingBugs") on how to report the bugs you find. If the idea of bug testing (and making Ubuntu even better) appeals to you then consider joining the [BugSquad]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BugSquad").

There's also the middle path, whereby you wait until the final release (about another 9-10 days) and jump straight in then. If you're very lucky, then it's possible you may encounter very few bugs.

Good luck whichever direction you choose to take.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-04-20
Just be aware that if you do an in-place upgrade, there is no simple way to roll-back to what you had before.  It's not like you can run a Linux version of System Restore and get your original system config and settings back.
