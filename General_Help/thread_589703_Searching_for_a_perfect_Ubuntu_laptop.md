### Post by buntunub on 2007-10-24
Well, my HP dv2415nr (2000 series) laptop works quite nicely out of the box on Feisty. Gutsy has some issues with xconfigs due to their new unbreakable X and Screens and Graphics utility which is completely useless and non functional on any of my 5 Ubuntu/Xubuntu machines. So, if you dont get sidetracked by that as I did, then the Lappy works equally as well or better in Gutsy if you just stick to using nvidia-settings utility instead. Btw, that Lappy is truly the workhorse of my home setup so its the most thoroughly tested of the lot. Right now, dual screens (seperate X servers) each have Compiz-Fusion with full effects working quite well. I had an issue where it was having frequent random lockups with Compiz running, but that seems to have sorted itself out after yesturdays patches (and turning off Xinerama seemed to do the trick). I use that Lappy for work and play and also have all my machines tied into it with vnc/firefox vnc plugins, and works remotely as well for when I have to travel. All works beautifully and with minimal fuss out of the box on a fresh Gutsy install, and all hardware seems to work without a hitch including the built in usb webcam. Heck, even the speaker/mic bug seems to be fixed in Gutsy because now when I plugin a headset, the external speakers turn off. Also, hibernate/suspend works every time, and I use suspend quite alot. So, if your looking for a new lappy that works very well with Ubuntu, the HP 2000 series is a good bet.
