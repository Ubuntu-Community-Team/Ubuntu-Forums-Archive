### Post by kwilliam on 2010-05-19
I've been away from Linux for awhile (college classes and personal life taking up more time) so forgive me if this is a dumb question. Does Kubuntu ever "force" the computer to reboot like Windows can? I recall it just using a taskbar notification that politely informed the user that for the new updates/kernel would not take effect until you reboot, instead of threatening to do it for you if you don't click Cancel in the next 10 minutes like Windows does. ---

### Post by DawieS on 2010-05-20
No version of Ubuntu will ever force a reboot after an update. You may continue using the old kernel you last booted from, for as long as you wish.

The notice displayed merely notifies you that there will be a new kernel available at the top of the Grub menu on your next reboot.
