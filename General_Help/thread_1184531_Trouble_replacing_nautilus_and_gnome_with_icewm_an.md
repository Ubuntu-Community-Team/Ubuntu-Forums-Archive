### Post by JKyleOKC on 2009-06-15
I use icewm and rox on one system here, which runs Mandriva 2005 LE distribution. As I recall, it took me more than a week of trial and error to get it to start reliably -- and now, four years later, I don't remember how I did it!

However I have four other boxes all running xubuntu, and it didn't require any significant tweaking at all to get things going. Three of the four are old, slow systems with limited RAM (256 MB on two, 512 MB on one) and all were running Win98 when booted to Windows. Performance is good. I'd suggest that you try the xubuntu option rather than the full-sized ubuntu version and see whether that suits your needs.

One note: I've not found a way to put icons directly on the desktop, but rather put launchers on the top panel for the applications I need most often. This may, or may not, be a problem for you...
