### Post by Corvillus on 2006-01-12
The superuser is a user account that has unrestricted access to the operating system. In the case of Linux, this account is called root. In Ubuntu, if you need to execute a command with superuser/root privileges, you prefix your command with sudo. Then enter your password (the one you use to log on to your computer at boot).
