### Post by -kg- on 2010-03-01
I'm not quite sure what you're trying to do, but if you want to delete certain emails from Thunderbird without them going to Trash, you can delete them immediately and permanently (not to trash) by highlighting them and press <shift-del>.  This will delete the selected message(s) immediately and permanently.

Oops!  You mean with filters!  One moment...

OK, there is no control to actually permanently delete a message.  What you could do is create a special folder for such mail, have the filter move it to that folder, then occasionally navigate to that folder and <shift-del> the contents.

As far as automating the process, I'm not sure that can be done.  Perhaps there is some sort of add-on that might do this...I don't know.

Good luck!

Another thought:  Do you have your junk filters set up?  I know you might search that folder, as well, but it's a thought.
