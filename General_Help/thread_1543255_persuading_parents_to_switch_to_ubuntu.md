### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-08-01
You're not saving them money if they already have a working machine; instead, you're causing them grief by forcing them to do what YOU want.

How would YOU like it if someone swapped out your Ubuntu machine for a Vista machine? What. NO?

Linux in general, and Ubuntu specifically, is about having the ability to CHOOSE your OS, not having the choice forced on you by MS.  YOU forcing Ubuntu on someone else is no different, in principle, from MS forcing their choice on you.

While I agree that folks should be given a chance to experience something different, I am a strong opponent of changes being forced on folks.  So, the best approach would be to setup dual-boot so they can experience Ubuntu when THEY want to -- not when YOU want them to.

BTW, if they are unwilling to do something so simple as select an OS from a menu of choices, what makes you think they will be comfortable with having to change anything else?
