### Post by WorMzy on 2011-10-14
GNOME3 came out and GNOME2 (what you're used to) has effectively been shelved. Look forward to a long future of nerfed customisability, a stupid overlay, and a one-app-per-workspace mentality that (imo) just gets in the way.

Or, switch to a different Desktop Environment. XFCE is an ideal replacement for people used to GNOME2.

There is a GNOME2 fork, called Mate, but I can't personally vouch for it's usability.

Or you could use the opportunity to try something completely different. I'm currently on Openbox (a standalone stacking window manager), but am experimenting with i3 (a tiling window manager).

### Post by Alan.Brown on 2011-10-14
I have been using Linux in one flavour or another for over 11 years now so I am not really a noob!  I have tried Unity for over a month now and given it a fair go.    I personally think it is nowhere as good as Gnome 2.   I have tried Gnome 3 (with Fedora 15) and find it very similar to Unity - in my opinion both are a step backwards from Gnome 2 as far as choice of settings, easy of use and productivity is concerned.

Another alternative is KDE of course - stable, easy to use and good for a production environment.

These are only my opinions but seem to be reflected in other peoples' comments.   Maybe there is a strong enough reason for someone to listen to us and not to force unpopular changes onto the market.   After all there are now many very good distros around that are viable alternatives to Ubuntu with Unity.
