### Post by moephan on 2007-04-01
Gabrielle, Welcome to Ubuntu and Linux.

Answer to both of your questions and many more are covered in a great resource: [URL="http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy"]http://ubuntuguide.org/URL]. 

If I understand your first question, you'd like to be able to sit in front of a Windows machine, and use your Ubuntu computer. That's covered in the [Remote_Access]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy#Remote_Access") section. 

I think for your second question, you'd like to log onto your Ubuntu computer, and have it play nice with the Windows computers on the same local network. That's covered in the [networking]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy#How_to_change_computer_Domain.2FWorkgroup") section. To join the domain, you first install a package called Samba, which lets your computer talk the same language as Windows.

Some of these instruction might require you to use the "command line" or "terminal." Terminal is under Applications -> Accessories. It's actually quite easy to copy and paste instructions into the terminal, even if you don't understand what the commands mean.

Good luck!

Cheers, Rick
