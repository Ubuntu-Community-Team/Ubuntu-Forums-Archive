### Post by Jay Car on 2010-06-15
Truly, the best way to see if a program meets your needs is to install it and try it out. I don't have experience with AutoCad, but I did have to find ways to replace all of my Adobe apps. Illustrator and InDesign being the main ones as I do a lot of layouts for publications destined for print. 

Even after asking around for alternatives, in the end all I could really do is install and try various ones. Inkscape and Scribus worked for me, though it did take some time and effort to learn them. I'm still, after nearly 3 years, not comfortable with Gimp, but it's improving and so am I, so it'll happen.

Just try some of the apps on that list, it's really the only way to know, because what works for someone else may not be what works for you. [edit] See if these might be what you are looking for: [Archimedes, QCad Community Edition 2.0.5.0, BRL-CAD 7.10]("http://www.osalt.com/autocad")
