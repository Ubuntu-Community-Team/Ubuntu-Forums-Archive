[QUOTE=Donnut]Hi all.

The general rule is that you should make a copy of anything that you don't want to have to do over again.

Frequency of backup depends on how you feel about redoing everything since your last backup.

This applies to paper systems as well as computer systems.

### Post by zekolas on 2005-10-27
[QUOTE=Donnut]Hi all.
Got some questions, does Ubuntu ever go bad like Windows?  Does it have viruses?  Should I make entire system backups frequently?  Thanks.[/QUOTE]

I am not sure what you mean by "go bad". I know after installing and un-installing lots of software windows seems to slow down as time goes on, and sometimes gets unstable if it hasn't been rebooted for many days. With linux there is less of a slow down as you install and uninstall software. Linux is more stable and you don't have to reboot for days on end.

Yes linux does have a few viruses, however they are very rare and mostly target webservers. Also with ubuntu most of the time you are logged in as a user, with limited privlages so if you do get a virus it cannot spread. However in my 8+ years of working with linux I have never actually encountered or even know someone that got a virus or even spy/ad ware.

System back ups are always a good idea, hard drives do fail everyonce in a while(regardless of what OS is installed). However I wouldn't bother with full backups, just save your personal data, programs can always be re-installed,
