### Post by 2CV67 on 2013-05-13
I recently upgraded my LTS system from 10.04 to 12.04.
At the same time, I started using Xfce desktop.

First time I switched on my Canon Pixma iP4300 printer, I was very impressed that it immediately started a detection & installation procedure for drivers which seemed to go cleanly, including printing the ubuntu test page.
Minor problems were that the colours were muddy (needed to change from RGB to CMYK as usual) and format was Letter instead of A4.

So far - so good.

Next, I tried printing my own tiny test page, which is a LibreOffice Draw A4 sheet with little red, blue & yellow dots with thin black borders at the top of the page.
The printing of the actual dots was OK (confirming CMYK) but the page stuck at about 25%, even though there is nothing else to print there.
The print queue said "Processing" then "Held" (I think) but there was no further action.
Just steady blinking of the top lamp on the printer, usually meaning "job in progress".
Deleting the item from the print queue made no difference & I could not complete paper travel or turn off the printer.
In the end, I had to power-down, then power-up & turn on the printer again, when the paper emerged as normal.

Thinking it might be a LibreOffice problem, I made another tiny test page using Gedit, with just one "x" at the top.
This produced exactly the same result - printed OK but paper stopped at about 25% with top light blinking slowly.

I tried several times, always with the same result.

I tried the troubleshooter but didn't see anything useful.
