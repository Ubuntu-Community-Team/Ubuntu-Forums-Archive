### Post by Impavidus on 2017-02-08
That screen shot is a bit hard to reach. I had to view the page source, locate the link and copy and paste it into the address bar of my browser. No link is visible in the post. Usually it's best to post images as attachments to your post. (Edit: fixed by the mod now.)

Anyway, as I understand it okular is one of the best pdf readers around for Linux. It's standard on Kubuntu, available from the repositories on the other flavours. Expect a lot of kde dependencies. There are some other pdf readers too, search and you may find one that works.

The problem is that pdf is actually designed as a simplified printer control format, not as a file format to describe interactive GUIs. So it's not really surprising many pdf readers can't handle all the later features.
