### Post by Docaltmed on 2009-05-19
I've been on kubuntu exclusively for several months, but recently switched back to gnome, at least for a while. There's a lot to like about both. KDE is really pretty, and once you get used to the way things are arranged, very functional. Some great apps based in kde. Downside is yes, a little buggy, but not big bugs. Just annoying little things, like window resizes not sticking, and defaults not defaulting, and sometimes the network manager has hiccups.

On the other hand, when I'm on ubuntu, I know I'm no longer in MS land. It's straight up, doesn't get in the way of my work, but the aesthetic is -- to this guy who grew up on C/PM, MS-DOS and all of the Windows iterations -- satisfyingly different. Everything on the Ubuntu desktop feels more solid to me, a little less fragile and not quite as high maintenance.

But when the next long term release comes out and I upgrade from Intrepid (assuming all of the Tablet PC issues have gotten straightened out), I'll probably switch off to KDE again. At least for a while...

### Post by Mirge on 2009-05-19
I've had freezing issues that I can't help but think is due to something specific to GNOME...Kubuntu doesn't give me ANY grief at all, and IMO looks a bit better. I installed Ubuntu 9.04 first when it came out and ended up switching to Kubuntu 9.04. I've now got Kubuntu 9.04 on 3 of my 4 computers (the 4th is a Vista comp that never gets booted up).

Kubuntu on ext4 runs circles around my Vista comp.. so does Ubuntu. IMO, gnome was a little faster in "feel" than Kubuntu, but it's very negligible at most. Neither had video issues with the nVidia card... and I did have video issues with ATI until I stuck with the open-source driver provided by Ubuntu 9.04--then no issues period.

It's largely a matter of preference. Hard drive space is cheaper than dirt these days, try 'em both out!

EDIT: I also heard about people having issues with Wifi in Kubuntu 9.04... someone said to just enter a blank pass for KDE Wallet, which I did. It gave a fatal error crash (for that application--not the system). Tried to connect to network again (wireless)... worked flawlessly. Set to auto-reconnect. Rebooted, logged out, switched users, etc several times... reconnects immediately every time. Zero issues.

### Post by Mirge on 2009-05-22
Right-click an empty area on the panel. Panel options -> panel settings. Or you can click the far-right side circle-ish icon. Screen Edge lets you put the panel on bottom, top, left, right, etc. Height adjusts the height.

Use the "Network Manager" widget (add to panel) to do your wireless networking. Everything works for me in Kubuntu. I agree though, it DID take me a few minutes to figure it all out and get comfortable with it, coming from gnome... but once I did, I fell in love!
