### Post by Erik765 on 2008-07-18
Well, after being a KDE3.5x user for two + years on various distros, (primarily Fedora and Kubuntu), I've decided to write down what I love and hate about it while trying to keep it as KDE-specific as possible.

I'm not writing this in contrast to any other window managers, just my opinions on how I think the KDE 3.5 project should not have been pushed to the back burner after a severely premature KDE4 release.

What I love about it:

** Well, where do I begin?! It's amazing. In my opinion, anyone (esp. ex windows users) should at least try a live CD of it and see how customizable it is... this is harder than I thought trying not to compare... ok... just one little comparison- about 10 TIMES more customizable than Gnome.
Don't get me wrong, gnome is an excelent window manager that I've also used for about a year.

If you're the type of person who loves having a unique desktop that doesn't have a blue task bar and a green start button just like 90% of everyone else in the world, then KDE3.5x might be for you. Oh, wait... I guess you can change the task bar to green or silver! Yes, I can compare those since XP is not a window manager!

** Personally, I like the KDE app base very much. Not that you can't run most KDE apps in other environments, I just like them better.

Some apps I really like:

Kcontrol is fabulous and allows you to make changes and tweak your system very easily and in real time.

KBFX is also great. Even better than the new KDE4 style in my opinion.

Amarok is also an excellent app, best Linux-based music player I've seen by far.

Some apps I really don't like:

Konqueror (as a web browser). It's mediocre at best and has fallen way behing in keeping up with new web-based languages and plugins. Yes, it's fast but mostly incompatible.

SuperKaramba. Nice looking, and I probably mostly don't like the people who write really bad or resource-hog themes for it. Liquid Weather is a nice theme, but it crashes Superkaramba entirely often for me. Some CPU monitors take up tons of cycles.


What I hate about it:

Though there are few things, they're definitely annoying!

** Main issue: The task bar icons.
What in the world is up with the icons of your running apps, such as skype, Knetworkmanager, etc. coming off of the task bar with a window border around them and just hangning out on the desktop with no apparent way of getting them back on the task bar... short of restarting your session? Wow, I might have been rambling a little there!
This primarily happens when I install a new app and KBFX restarts to reflect it, but happens at other times too.

** Dolphin does not provide a multiple tab option like konqueror does.

** There is no 'snap to' option for the mouse pointer. That would be an awesome improvement.

** The 3rd mouse button doesn't really work like a 3rd mouse button but rather seems to just emulate pressing the right and left buttons at the same time. This can be really annoying in games such as WoW.


KDE4 thoughts:

It saddens me that the potential here of KDE3.5x is pushed to the background in place of 4.x and that many new distros are just lapping it up like a Brawny on spilled Koolaid!

I've given KDE4 multiple chances and the first thing I always try is changing the style of my taskbar to see how much progress they've made. But, still a no go since last time I tried it!

It's at least a year early, and even though it's been out over 6 months, there are still major issues.

That's just the beginning and I'm not saying anything more about KD4 as that was not my intention of this post.



Anyway, just a few little things that bug me and some positive highlights of my experience with on the better OSs and window managers I've ever used.
