### Post by az on 2006-04-15
1.  99 percent of ethernet cards "just work" in linux.  They have fully-functional drivers build into the linux kernel.  If your card is not working, it's because it's not working, if you know what I mean...  I bought a box of eight brand-new ethernet cards for $1.15 on ebay.  Local pickup.  

You just open your case, unscrew a screw that is already there, pop the card in and screw the screw back.  If it is an older computer, chesk to see that you have the right slow (pci is newer, isa is really old.  Isa is bigger and fatter)

2.  Linux should recognise a lot of usb network hardware, but probably not all of it...
