### Post by chewearn on 2010-02-12
The marketing got hold of it and said 16GB is better because it's bigger.  In actual fact, the drive has 16,000,000,000 Bytes.

This is recalculated to 16,000,000,000 / (1024*1024*1024) = 14.9GiB.

Notice the extra "i" in GiB.  This is the IEEE notation, solving the confusion between "giga" in 1000's vs 1024's.

Similarly, MiB vs MB, KiB vs KB, etc.
