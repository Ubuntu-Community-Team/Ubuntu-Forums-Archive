### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-04-02
Nautilus actually provides the desktop for Gnome.  It's kind of nice, really, since it gives you a sliding scale for your graphical environment.  

You could have nothing but a simple window manager like Openbox.  You could add a lightweight panel like PyPanel to that, as well as a few applets, now that you have a panel.  One step up from that is to use Nautilus as your file manager, and let it provide your desktop.  Or you could just move up to a lightweight desktop environment like Xfce.  Of course, if you go the Nautilus route, you could swap PyPanel out for gnome-panel, and from there it's a very short leap to running Gnome.

As to your specific issue, however, you can have multiple file managers installed at once.  You only lost your desktop because you removed Nautilus.  You could keep Nautilus to provide your desktop, but use Thunar as your default file manager, if you want.  Or, as you said, you could just remove Gnome entirely and switch to Xfce.  Or you could even keep both, so you have the choice at login which desktop environment to use.  To make the trifecta, you could install KDE as well, so you can change your desktop environment with your mood.

Linux is flexible like that.
