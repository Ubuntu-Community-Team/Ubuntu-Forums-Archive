### Post by radi0j0hn on 2009-05-29
Unless you have a lot of memory and space, running Ubuntu in a virtual environment on a Netbook may not be fun.

ON my Asus Eee PC 900A, I can put an OS on an SD card and (while powering up) press "ESC" to pick whatever storage I wish to boot from (Internal storage, SD card or USB thumb drive.

Some Asus netbooks do not repond to the ESC key option.  Often removing the battery from the back and the putting it back on resets the unit and then it works.

I'd look into that before trying to piggyback on on the other.

BTW, while I prefer Ubuntu, booting Puppy Linux on a Netbook is really nice.
