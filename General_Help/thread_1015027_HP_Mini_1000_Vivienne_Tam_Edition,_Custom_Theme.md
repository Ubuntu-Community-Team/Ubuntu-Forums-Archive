### Post by jsully1 on 2008-12-18
Hi all,

I just got a [[COLOR="Blue"]Mini 1000 Vivienne Tam Edition[/COLOR]](http://blog.laptopmag.com/hp-shows-off-10-inch-netbook-the-next-mini-note) and installed Ubuntu 8.10 on it.  Happy to report that (as expected) everything big works out of the box, including suspend and resume.  I haven't tried the webcam/mic yet.  

Anyway, as it's a Christmas present for my girlfriend, I also took some time to build a custom theme to not only match the laptop, but to help make the miserable 1024x600 resolution a little less noticable.  

Aside from the theme and colors that are built to match the Vivienne Tam Edition, adjustments include:
- buttons, checkboxes etc and are as small as still usable
- title bar shrunk and frame shrunk by several pixels in each direction
- gotten rid of borders and bufferes where possible
- shrunk menus and lists
- shrunk icons and spacing on the main menu
- set the font size to 8 all around
- adjusted firefox with Compact Menu plus, small icons
