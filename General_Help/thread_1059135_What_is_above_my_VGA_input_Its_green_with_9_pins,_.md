### Post by mcduck on 2009-02-03
Sounds like a COM port.. (also known as serial port or RS-232) It was commonly used for modems and such devices in the past, these days it's rarely needed on normal use because of it's low transfer speeds. It's still used for some special purposes because of it's flexibility and that it's easy to program for.

You won't be able to connect any displays to it. If you need another display you probably need to buy a graphics card, these days pretty much all of them have connectors for two displays so even cheapest ones would work if you don't need lots of 3D performance.

edit: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serial_port]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serial_port")
