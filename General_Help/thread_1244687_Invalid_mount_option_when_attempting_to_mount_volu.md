### Post by matthewbpt on 2009-08-19
ok i see your problem, for some reason in your fstab /dev/sdb1 is reserved for cdrom filesystem =S. this can be changed easily, type the following:
```
gksudo gedit /etc/fstab
```

A text editor should open after prompting for your password. put a # symbol in front of the line '/dev/sdb1 /media/cdrom0 udf,iso9660 user,noauto,exec,utf8 0 0' (thats the third from last line) just like the first 5 lines have, and save and exit the text editor, now unplug your hard drive and plug it back in. See if that works.
