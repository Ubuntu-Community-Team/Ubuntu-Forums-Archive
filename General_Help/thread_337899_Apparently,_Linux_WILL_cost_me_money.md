the free version prints for free without any banner if you print in draft resolution, which it claims is at 300dpi.

my printouts with such settings, however, look like 150dpi in windoze.


if you print with medium-600dpi, it looks like 30dpi in windoze, however it addsa banner "Printed with turboprint" or something like that...


solution: print with the draft-300dpi option (aka150dpi quality), but make the driver print with medium-600dpi(aka 300dpi quality):

here's how:

sudo gedit /usr/share/turboprint/printers/Canon_PIXMA_MP150.tpp

look for the lines:

[Mode0_0_0]
Name=Draft
Quality=1
Description=300dpi
ApplicationDPI=300,300
PrinterDPI=300,300
Curves=300dpi_quick
Borderless=0
Param=1,128,128,1

and change them to:

[Mode0_0_0]
Name=Draft
Quality=1
Description=300dpi
ApplicationDPI=600,600
PrinterDPI=600,600
Curves=600dpi_quick
Borderless=0
Param=1,128,128,1


as far as i know, this is perfectly legal, as their website claims it prints 300dpi for free...but it may not be legal though :)
use this hack at your own risk.

also, they will probably prevent this hack in the future by generating a hash for each driver file and store it in the binaries of the driver, making any such alteration ineffective...by then, hopefully my printer will have died and i got a new one, or i could always use the same version of the driver i've got - it works fine on 1.94-3
