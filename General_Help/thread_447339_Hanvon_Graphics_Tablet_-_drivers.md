### Post by fragos on 2007-05-18
I suggest you try using the Wacom drivers by editing /etc/X11/xorg.conf.  At least some of the Hanvon features may be available that way.  The Wacom drivers even support the touch screens on notebooks that have them.  For the Wacom drivers to work your Hanvon should be plugged into a USB port before booting.

Sorry this didn't work out well for you.  I've had similar problems and found the answer is research before purchase to see if it works for someone else.  With regards to tablets Wacom is always supported.  For printers and scanners I stick with HP.  Some devices seem to be universally supported like mice, keyboards and CD/DVD writers.  For advanced video performance, Nvidia continues to be good to me and ATI is still to be avoided although some ATI chip sets work.
