### Post by sayakb on 2008-07-06
Metacity is a Window manager and emerald is a window decorator. By default, you have compiz + GTK Window decorator (ie. if you have desktop effects working). You can replace GTK Window decorator with emerald. You may though use Compiz + Emerald + GTK Themes (just that the window border will be an emerald one)
Get emerald themes from [http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/)

Metacity is a replacement for Compiz. Metacity is a window manager that doesnot use compositing like compiz and hence, does not need heavy graphics. Metacity has simple animations with transparency support under hardy. You cannot use emerald with metacity but compiz.

### Post by wdaniels on 2008-07-06
> **nkri said:**
> I've been reading lots of posts about how Emerald is so great, but none of them really say why; some say "it's more customizable," or even, "it's better."  These don't help AT ALL.

The customisation here refers mostly just to the graphical appearance and compositing effects of the window border, buttons, title etc. Emerald will not make any real difference to the behaviour and management of windows on your desktop. The advantages of Emerald apply more to theme designers really - it allows for the creation of fancier themes with more unusual layouts and graphical effects.

> **nkri said:**
> What do you think is better?  Emerald or Metacity?  Why (please be specific)?

As explained by LinuxIsInnovation, Emerald and Metacity are different layers of the desktop. You cannot use them together, but only because Emerald is tied to the Compiz window manager, not because they are alternatives to each other.

Emerald always used to be quite buggy. I've only recently started using it again, and it seems more stable now, but unless there is some particular theme you want to use that needs emerald, I can't think of any good reason to switch to it.
