### Post by ajgreeny on 2016-04-02
You could probably set up a script to run **pop3browser** at regular intervals, though I don't think I have the ability to write such a script, nor the time at present to even try.

I used to use it years ago to check a largely defunct email address I had previously used.  It allowed me to see how many emails were in the inbox, from whom, to read the first few lines of each message, if needed, and most importantly, (it was for me at any rate), to delete any message, spam or rubbish from the mail server without having to download them first.
