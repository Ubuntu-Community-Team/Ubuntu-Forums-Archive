### Post by nickleboyblue on 2010-11-17
First, are you using KDE or GNOME?  The following works for gnome, but I am not as familiar with KDE:

Go to System->Preferences->Screensaver in the menu, and uncheck the box labeled "Lock screen when screensaver is active."  Once unchecked, that should stop the the system from asking you to give your password every time the screen blanks.

As for the wifi connectivity, in generic Ubuntu, the connection is generally automatic unless you specify otherwise.
