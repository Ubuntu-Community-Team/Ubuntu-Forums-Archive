### Post by Iendicis on 2009-03-07
As I said, dpkg-reconfigure didn't work.

BUT I solved it anyway, as I usually do when I post on a help forum. -_-

The issue was the cable connecting to the monitor. It was old and some of the prongs were missing. Xrandr wasn't able to get all of the information it needed on the monitor when this cable was used.

Once I replaced it with a newer cable, the monitor was able to detect my resolution. Voila, I have a very nice, snazzy looking desktop!

### Post by fabricator4 on 2009-03-07
Glad you got it fixed, however I was forced to find a fix that worked because I'm running through a KVM that doesn't pass all the EDID data correctly.  I tried all the stuff you tried, and it just didn't work for me either.

The fix is simple, just give the frequency ranges for the monitor in the monitor section of xorg.conf, and the system does the rest by allowing the VESA screen modes pertinent to that configuration.  eg:

Section "Monitor"
        Identifier      "HP"
        ModelName       "1740"
        Option          "DPMS"
        HorizSync       30.0-83.0
        VertRefresh     56-76
EndSection

This allows 1280x1024 @ 60Hz on my HP1740.  So simple, but so hard to find the right answer that works.
