You may need a more specific kernel for your processor, such as a 686 for Intel or a K6 for AMD, rather than the 386 that is usually installed. 

 What you want to do may be very hard or impossible. It really depends on the video chip you have. First see if this is possible with your laptop in windows. I know on my HP that you can only have the same screen on  one of  LCD, CRT or TV. There is also the BIOS controlling the video at boot to deal with. On mine, when the CRT is plugged in at boot, Ubuntu turns off the LCD. This of course is what I want. If you don't want it to happen there is probably a setting in ACPI that can change this.

Finding the driver to make everything work may also be hard. My ATI video chip is unsupported by ATI even for windows. The laptop builders must create there own driver. I hope you can do what you would like and if you do be sure to write a howto. The Hacker code is that no problem needs to be solved twice.
