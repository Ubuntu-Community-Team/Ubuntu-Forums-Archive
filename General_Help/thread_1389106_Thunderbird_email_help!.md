### Post by HereInOz on 2010-01-24
First up, you will need to know who your ISP is.  Assuming that the ISP will handle both your incoming and outgoing mail (not always the case) then you need to use the settings which that ISP gives you.

Contact your ISP for this, but do not tell them that you are running Linux.  Most ISPs glaze their eyes and close their mind when you say that.  Only tell them that you need the POP and SMTP servers for your internet connection.

Also, if you are using a 3rd party mail service, like gmail, or yahoo, (rather than [email]user@your.isp.com[/email] (you get my drift, I hope) then you are going to have to use the POP server setting which is appropriate for that email service, leaving the SMTP server set as your ISP gives you.
