### Post by simosx on 2008-11-21
> **bradosav said:**
> here ;)

Indeed, the characters are Greek, however the language is not Greek.
What has happened is that instead of latin characters, their Greek equivalent is shown. So, instead of a, you get &#945;. For example, in the place of File, it shows &#934;&#953;&#955;&#949;.

This is some weird configuration issue. Some other people reported something similar, however they did not investigate further and I cannot tell you what is the root of the problem.

What you can do

1. Check that you have the default fonts in the Preferences (Sans, Serif, etc).
2. Verify that the openoffice.org-gnome package is installed.
3. Erase your openoffice.org profile and start OpenOffice.org again (profile is ~/.openoffice.org2/)
