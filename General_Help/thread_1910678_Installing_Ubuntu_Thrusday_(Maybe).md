### Post by Topsiho on 2012-01-17
I support stefangr1.

In addition to that, telling us that your computer is from 2003 doesn't say much. Far more important to know are the main specs, the amount of RAM should be 512MB or more, is the main one, a >1GHz processor another one, and your hard disk, or the free space on it, should be 10 or more GB, dependent on what you want to use the computer for: 6 or more GB for the root partition (/), twice your RAM for swap, and if you want a separate partition for your personal files and settings (/home) than you use the rest of the available space for it. The wanted size of /home depends on the size of the files that you put in it.

If you don't want a separate /home partition, than the partition for / should be larger, as it will also have to contain the /home folder.

Lubuntu and Xubuntu are lighter versions of Ubuntu as they use a lighter desktop.
