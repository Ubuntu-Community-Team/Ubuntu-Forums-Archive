### Post by freerick on 2007-03-08
After playing around with it for a long time I found out that the problem occurs because Xinerama kills RandR, and Beryl checks for Randr at startup. Randr is not essential for Beryl to function properly, and a thread has been opened at the beryl developer forum. I lost the link, but I'll post it here when I come across it. The developers said that they're working on a fix that that it might come out in the new version. They also said to try using the trunk, multi-monitor support should be supported fairly well at this point.

Indeed the solution is to switch to TwinView, of course in my case I had to compromise because I have three monitors and TwinView only works with two. I'm going to try and see what I can do about enabling my third display, I'll post here when I find a solution.

Good luck!
