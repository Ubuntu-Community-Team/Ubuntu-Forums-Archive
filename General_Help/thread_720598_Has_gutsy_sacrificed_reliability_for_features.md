### Post by themusicwave on 2008-03-10
I also have a Dell inspiron E1505.  I have noticed a few problems, it seems to me that Gutsy is less stable then Fiesty.

Sometimes I have an issue where everything completely freezes.  The screen stays constant, the mouse won't respond, keyboard won't respond and all I can do is turn off the machine.  This doesn't happen often, but it never happened in Fiesty.  In fiesty I could always at least do a CRTL-Alt-Backspace to restart the session.

I have also noticed that gnome gets confused and loses my settings.  I will boot up Ubuntu and gnome will have a message saying it can't find my settings.  All my icons and my background will be different.  I then doa CRT-Alt-Backspace and login again and magically everything is back to normal.  This may be related to your desktop setting issue.

Even still, I must say that I am rather hard on my computer.  I like to tinker and break things.  I fiddle when no fiddling is needed.  As such, Linux is much more forgiving to me than Windows XP.  I have broken XP in horrible ways.  I have also had a few nasty virii that really corrupted my Windows boot.  Even with these little quirks, Gutsy is still better than XP.

I am also holding out hope for hardy, hardy is a LTS or Long Term Support release.  These tend to be much more stable.  The interim releases usually are a little more rough around the edges.  Hardy comes out next month, perhaps it will provide the stability you need!
