### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-02-07
My rule-of-thumb is to never trash a working system.  Spend the money on a bigger disk drive or second disk drive and put the newer Ubuntu on a second partition.  This way you can evaluate the performance of the new distro.  If it doesn't work well, then you can always boot back into your stable distro.

When you upgrade over an existing install--there is no going back.  You trash a working system, and the new disto may have some showstopper bugs.

So you are forced to do a clean install of your older, working distro.  Frustrating and time-consuming.
