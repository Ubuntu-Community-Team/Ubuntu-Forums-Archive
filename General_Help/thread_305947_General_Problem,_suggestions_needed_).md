### Post by eXisor on 2006-11-24
As someone who's been supporting a distributed network of non-pc-literate buddies for years... This is what I think:

- To get your grandfather to do any of this himself is going to require major handholding. This'll probably cost you a small fortune in phone bills, and may even strain the realationship in the short-term. IMO not worth the effort.

- Although the Xubuntu-alternate will load and run on his machine (I have it running .... slowly .... on almost exactly that setup - same cpu and memory - it'll be very frustrating to use. Also, setup is kinda tricky and will need real tweaking to get it usable.

What I suggest is:

- That he waits for the xubuntu cd
- That he takes the system to a local computer shop and tells them to wait for a call from you
- Then you phone them and tell them to format the hdd, install Xubuntu, and to setup his modem with his isp
- Help them install a vnc server like x11vnc.  
- Be prepared to walk the computer shop techie through all this every step of the way.
- Be prepared to spend much time helping your grandfather telephonically thereafter

Longer term, the x11vnc server on your grandfathers machine will mean you can takeover his machine when you need to... for updates, tweaking and the like.

Good luck, and be prepared to discover new depths to the word 'patience'.


The good news is you won't have to de-window-fy him first.  :)
