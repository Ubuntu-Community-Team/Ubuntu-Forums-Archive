### Post by viperdvman on 2011-11-30
Usually, there are no problems with running 2, 3, or even 4 different browsers on the same operating system. Do note, when you install said additional browser and start it up for the first time, it will say *"Opera/Chrome/etc. is not your default browser. Would you like to make it your default browser?"* or something to that effect. Whether you click on yes or no is a matter of your choice, as it doesn't affect your browsing experience any if you click **no**.

Go ahead and install a second browser. It's not going to hurt anything. Choices are wonderful :)

### Post by JadeMoonlight on 2011-11-30
Chromium is my personal choice for a second browser. Chromium is the open source equivalent of Chrome and has a blue logo rather than the typical Google colors.

Choosing a browser is somewhat up to personal preference. Depending what websites you'll need your browser for, not all of them will be compatible. 

Firefox and Chrome/Chromium are pretty widely compatible with many websites. The neat thing about Chrome (and I think Chromium as well) is that it it updates and enables the Adobe Flash plug-in automatically. This makes it less of a hassle to use sites like YouTube.

I've never personally used Opera, but I've heard good things. Opera is also one of the oldest browsers, which can probably be bad or good. I also see that they tout themselves as being the most secure browser. If security is a big concern for you, then this could be the way to go.

Again, try out a few and see what you like best. Happy browsing! :D
