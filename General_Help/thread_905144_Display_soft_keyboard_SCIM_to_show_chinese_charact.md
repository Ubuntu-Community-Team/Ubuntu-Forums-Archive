### Post by 3dAnim8d on 2008-09-15
Hey, I don't about whether or not you can display the onscreen keyboard with Zhuyin, but you can type traditional characters with pinyin.  They show how to in this thread:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=194513&page=3]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=194513&page=3")

Basically what they say to do is use the format in the simplified characters menu called &#26234;&#33021;&#25340;&#38899; and then use ctrl + / to cycle through the options. You'll see it go from &#20013; (for all the Chinese characters) to &#33521; (for English) to &#31616; (for simplified characters) and then to &#32321; (for traditional characters).  I hope this all helps.  It sure helped me.
