### Post by howefield on 2010-09-10
> **Pacopag said:**
> But when you talk about breakage, what do you mean.  What are the causes of crashes and breakage with the unstable release?

You talk about "unstable", it is more accurate to say "development".

In other words, it is being developed, and with something undergoing change, there is a risk of breakage. There may not be, I don't suppose developers set out to break things, but neither can they get it right forst time, every time. 

> What are the benefits of using the unstable release?

You get a taste of what's coming, and are able to test and report bugs, thereby helping to make the release stronger and more robust.

> I mostly only use firefox, gedit, and some commercial software, so I don't see how I would notice a difference.

Sure, but you might notice the difference if a package is updated to the extent that your machine won't boot, or crashes.

### Post by howefield on 2010-09-10
> **Pacopag said:**
> how do "unstable" releases perform compared to "stable" releases?

Just to clarify, every version is considered stable when it is released. 10.10 will be considered stable when released on the 10th of next month.

That's not to say it won't be improved and made more robust as time goes by.

Releases designated as LTS (Long Term Support) tend to start from a more conservative base, hence they may appear more "stable".
