### Post by az on 2006-04-10
[QUOTE=Ubuntuud]What is the difference between Fedora, Ubuntu and Gentoo (and others).[/QUOTE]

Fedora is what RedHat uses to build itself.  RedHat used to be free and the most popular distribution, but when they became a pay-for distribution, they created fedora to make a free community-supported distribution to be a testbed for technologies to put into redhat.  They gathered all the following that redhat used to have.

Gentoo is a source-based distribution.  You are supposed to get some benefit from compiling everything yourself, but most people find the benefits negligeable.

Ubuntu is based on Debian.

Each distribution is just a collection of free-libre software that is configured to work well together.  Each distro has different focuses and strengths.  You get to chose which one you want to use.
