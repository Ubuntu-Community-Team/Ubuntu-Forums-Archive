### Post by Tom Collier on 2010-12-07
Why do you need that high of a resolution?  For most on-screen viewing, 72 dpi to 90 dpi is adequate. Screen dpi is actually pixels per inch.

Unless you're printing something like a detailed art print giclee, 150 to 300 dpi matches the resolution of most printers. Print dpi is actually dots per inch and not the same as screen resolution "dpi"...a misnomer that leads to a lot of graphic confusion.....
