### Post by lisati on 2008-11-12
On the subject of defragmentation, look here: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defrag](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defrag)
The wiki mentions some tools that can be used with the ext2/ext3 formats that are commonly used with Linux.

And there is always the option of what you have running when your computer starts up. But be careful: you want to make sure that removing something from the list won't hurt your system. Not having something like skype start up when you login is probably more of a nuisance than a big deal, while there are other things that will be needed for your system to work properly, and thus shouldn't be removed.
