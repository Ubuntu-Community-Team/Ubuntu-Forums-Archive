Most of the Automatically Started Applications in Lubuntu 12.10 are unchecked by default. Something like 'Power Manager' and others are OFF by default. Although there are many common startup items between Lubuntu and Ubuntu, in Ubuntu they are ON by default. Can anyone explain this situation? And what are the consequences of enabling & disabling them in Lubuntu 12.10?

---

### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2012-12-10
Lubuntu is based on Ubuntu but has the lightweight X11 desktop environment and is designed to use fewer resources.  If you start an application on boot by default it will use more resources.  No big deal unless you have a real old computer with low RAM.

### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2012-12-11
> **amr-maro said:**
> So, I understand that it is OK to leave them unchecked/disabled, even the power manager? And no functionality would be lost?
That is correct.  You can of course start the application from the menu or lxterminal.  What you are talking about is starting them when you start your computer.  Many people un-tick them because they have a shortage of RAM or just don't want them running automatically.
