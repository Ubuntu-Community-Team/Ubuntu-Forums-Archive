### Post by Bashing-om on 2016-11-08
thecoffeeguy2; Hello;

If I may, You can not know what "you" like until you try and see what you like . The myriad of choices exist for a reason.
Now when you have tried all the rest .. and still lacking ...what I have found to my preference ( as I have good ideas of what I like/want) is the minimal core install.
Be aware in the minimal all you have is a booting kernel and a wired networking ability . That is ALL. It is up to you what you build. And you will have some hurdles to overcome taking this approach - there is a finding out learning curve that can be rather steep .

If your priority is a fast system I can attest that the minimal is some kind of fast . I multi boot and have my minimal on a 7200 RPM hard drive that is almost as fast as a install of xubuntu on a SSD on this box .
