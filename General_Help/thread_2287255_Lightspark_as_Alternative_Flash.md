### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2015-07-18
Forget it. Lightspark and gnash were 100% garbage and the projects have been dead for a few years now. I bet people who  recommended them as replacement for flash in Linux blogs had never actually used them and they used flash. l tried them before (I didn't have flash installed in Fedora until 21 because I wanted to test freshplayerplugin, also tried them on some Ubuntu releases) 

Lightspark never worked, just froze my browser. gnash worked maybe for a grand total of 6 Youtube demos and the performance was much worse than flash and almost all options were missing. They were reversed engineering flash versions that were obsolete even back then. Even Totem had a flash plugin (totem-vagas) which was marginally more useful (played a few Youtube clips through Totem, which was better than flash resource wise but also 99% didn't work and missing options) but I think that is discontinued as well.

For Youtube you basically don't need flash, there are tons of way to watch it without flash: html5, vlc, mpv-addon, gecko-mediaplayer+ viewtube, standalone Youtube viewers: smtube and minitube, all work a lot better than flash. For everything else I have recommended mpv-addon (doesn't work everywhere, but enough for many people) You can of course use an agent switcher to fake an iPhone like Kerry S recommended but that is too much hassle for me.
