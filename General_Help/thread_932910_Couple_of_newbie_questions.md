### Post by Dean Powell on 2008-09-28
Hi, and welcome to Ubuntu!

1) I assume you're using the default Gnome desktop.  <ctrl>+<alt>+<left arrow> and <ctrl>+<alt>+<right arrow> will switch your desktops without using a mouse.  You can also move your cursor to somewhere over your desktop (i.e. not over an open application window) and use the scroll bar to switch desktop spaces.
2) I have heard rumblings that the Firefox "Save as Wallpaper" doesn't work under Gnome.  The best thing to do is right-click on the image, choose "Save Target As..." and save it to your hard drive.  You can then right-click on your desktop (again, not over an application window) and choose "Change Wallpaper" (or "Set Desktop Wallpaper" or something like that).
3) I personally don't use anti-virus on my Linux box.  I have been using Linux in one form or another since 1994 and have never been hit by a virus.  However...your mileage may vary.  Linux is inherently more virus resistant than Windows, but that doesn't mean it's completely safe, either.  Generally, you are logged in as a regular user, and don't have access to change any of the underlying operating system files.  To me, it's much more important to practice "safe web surfing" habits.
4) Gedit supports both syntax highlighting and line numbering.  [http://www.gnome.org/projects/gedit/images/gedit2.png](http://www.gnome.org/projects/gedit/images/gedit2.png)
I believe line numbering is turned on/off in "Edit/Preferences...".
5) Between work and family, I've no time for chat!  Sorry, no help to you there!
6) No answer for that either, I'm afraid.  Anyone else?
