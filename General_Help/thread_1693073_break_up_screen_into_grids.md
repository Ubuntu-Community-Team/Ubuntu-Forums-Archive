### Post by fabyouless on 2011-03-14
It seems you may want to switch to a different window manager.

I follow linux_training on twitter and remember reading about a window manager that allows for dynamic reconfiguration of all the windows open on the desktop. Basically, none of the windows are covered by another, and the full desktop is covered. Very geeky.

It's a Tiling Window Manager called Awesome. [http://awesome.naquadah.org/](http://awesome.naquadah.org/)

Just an example of how there are lots of solutions out there. Personally, i didn't feel the need for a Tiling Window Manager, yet, but you may want to consider it.
