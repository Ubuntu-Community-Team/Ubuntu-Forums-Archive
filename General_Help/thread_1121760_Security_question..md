### Post by 3Miro on 2009-04-10
In terms of the original repos, yes. Everything that is provided by Canonical is open source and it is practically impossible to add spyware and malware to an open source program. ### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-04-10
It's really hard to sneak malware into open-source software, since everyone can view exactly what the software does.  As long as the software you're installing is open-source, chances are extraordinarily high you're safe.  All of the official Ubuntu repositories, as well as Medibuntu, are well tested and safe.  Launchpad PPAs are also safe, since setting up a Launchpad account involves providing information that can trace any problems back to you.  And any independent repositories maintained by major software projects like Deluge and Wine are also generally safe, again because any included malware is easily traced back to the person responsible.

Installing closed-source, proprietary software is a different matter.  However, since most such software is developed by a major corporation, you're again pretty safe unless their servers are cracked.

One of the several reasons Linux is more secure than other OSes is because software isn't anonymous.  Often the only compensation Linux developers receive for their efforts is recognition and respect from the community.  Malware developers generally don't want you to know who they are.  As long as you're installing open-source software and/or software from a well-known developer, you don't really have to worry about this sort of thing.
