### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-08-23
Here's another really nice [site]("http://www.getdeb.net/welcome/") for debian apps. You can select the apps based on your Ubuntu release. But before you download and install them, make sure they aren't either in the Synaptic Package Manager or the Ubuntu Software Center. Install it from there, if it's available, if not just install it from the the debian app site. I suggest you make that a rule, only install (from net sites) that which isn't available from the Ubuntu repos.

The best approach to learning in my opinion is to invest some time tinkering. You may even crash the system once or twice, but in the end you'll have a better understanding of the OS. Don't follow every Google or Linux link site, instead ask yourself what your needs are and focus your efforts on finding apps that best fit your requirements.

In the beginning the learning curve seems intense, but just be patient and as I said focus on your own needs. Later you can expand your horizons with the comfort of experience. Most important is to always remember that the forums are here to help and no question is dumb or foolish one, except the one you don't ask.

Have fun and enjoy the experience, make it a challenge and don't hesitate to ask for help.

Good Luck

Most of the files located in the user/bin are executables or links to executables. If your searching for an app executable that would be the first place to look.

One thing I might suggest, is to stop thinking Windows. Ubuntu/Linux is not a Windows clone, it a unique OS that doesn't duplicate Windows. I know the learning curve seems steep, but it's no worse than learning Windows.

Remember, it took a while to learn Windows and it will take some time to learn Ubuntu. One of the big mistakes people make is assuming since they know Windows they will have no problem using Ubuntu.
