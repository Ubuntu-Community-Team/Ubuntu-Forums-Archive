### Post by whatsnext on 2017-09-30
Hi Satimis,

I am not big expert (or for the case small expert) on Linux, but I have tried this software a couple of days ago and installation was straightforward, and performance is being problem-free so far:

[www.rambox.pro](www.rambox.pro)

This is a software (i think is java) that allows you to kept together all your social accounts, including whatsapp, in a single location.

Download the appropriate version (32 or 64 bits), unpack, launch exec, and the software will be up and running. Then select service (whatsapp), scan the QR with your cellphone, and you will have whatsapp in your desktop. It's this easy.

That has been my experience. I hope it will work for you as well.

### Post by whatsnext on 2017-09-30
Hi Satimis,

Just install the rambox software. When you select whatsapp service, rambox will generate the QR and will give you the instructions on screen. If I remember, is as easy as go to your cellphone whatsapp menu, select whatsappweb option, and scan the QR, and in 3 seconds you will have the same contents on your desktop screen.

One thing you need to take into account is that for the system to work, both desktop and cellphone need to be connected to the same network (this is how the wtahsappweb service work).

Please do not be afraid, it is really easy.
