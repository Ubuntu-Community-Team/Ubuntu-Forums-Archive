### Post by nolag on 2010-12-08
You should be able to the kernel is the same.  The only thing is that some of Mint's apps are customized slightly.  This can cause config problems if you copy your settings.  I would recommend not copying those.  Also if you have the bandwidth I would recommend insalling apps as opposed to using ubuntu's.  If you know they are the same (like from a repo you added or other things that you know mint did not change), then you can copy the .deb's from your computer to the mint one and install them to save the bandwidth of redownloading them.  There is not too much difference between the distros, I switched to mint when 9.04 came out and the installer had problems on my comp (I don't know why the mint one did not it was the same...).  I just switched back for 10.10.
