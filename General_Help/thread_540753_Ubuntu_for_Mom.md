### Post by Delkster on 2007-09-02
My parents are also running Ubuntu. Both of them have used some computers in the past but haven't had much knowledge of any system. What I've tried to do has been to set up everything I imagine they may need. The problem is that you can't anticipate everything and only time will tell what they will actually need, so it's difficult to do really do that with such short notice.

If it's likely that they will want to watch videos sent or brought by friends or relatives, install the codecs that they will probably need. If they're going to use a digital camera, make sure that it's easy to import photos and instruct them how to do it. Install Java, Flash and whatever you think they will need, and show them how to use Add/Remove Applications just in case (my parents never really touch that by themselves but, on the other hand, I live just a couple of kilometers away from them). Set up the printer for them if they use one. Try to figure out what kinds of things they're likely to use and imagine how you can make it as easy as possible -- and test it yourself. All if this takes some time and effort, of course.

Good luck!
