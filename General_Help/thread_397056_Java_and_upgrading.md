### Post by Ryba on 2007-03-30
> **Thulemanden said:**
> The main point of my post is expressing wonder over I after 10 years with Linux distroes still have to install a Java (Blackdown) by hand.
In that ugly desktop, click on the applications menu and goto add/remove software. You will see sun's java 5 (java 5 and java 6 if you use fiesty version of ubuntu). Just double click it and install. It might tell you that it has to enable a repository if it is the first time you are installing non-open source software on the system through that add/remove software thing.

> **Thulemanden said:**
> Also the Totem WMV plugin doesn't play the videos on [www.jp.dk]("http://www.jp.dk") (optimised for Windows Media Player)

Actually the WMV plugin will play up to media player 11 videos just fine but *ONLY* if they are not encoded with DRM restrictions. Due to legal restrictions, I'm not sure if it is possible to legally support playing DRM restricted media videos but they are working on that.

Yes it sucks but it doesn't make linux "unusable" for the mainstream public. The mainstream public has chosen to support and authorize this and until people tell vendors that they don't want DRM video encodings and they are leaving (surprisingly, I've gotten 2 vendors to stop encoding with DRM by writing them. they didn't even realize they were placing DRM restrictions when doing their encoding for that matter), people will continue to be locked into windows only (not even OS X is allowed to win this one) even if that is through wine or vmware or xen or whatever else.
