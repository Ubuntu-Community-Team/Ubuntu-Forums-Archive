### Post by bcbc on 2011-03-11
> 146.2GB: boot/grub/grub.cfg

I saw one of these the other day that Drs305 figured out. Some older bioses can't read beyond 137GB. So it would fail to pick up the grub.cfg and drop you at a grub prompt (shouldn't be the grub rescue prompt - that's different).

So there's a potential that some time in the future when you rebuild grub.cfg you could have the same issue.

Best to split your partitions so that either root is <=137GB or you have a separate /boot partition.

Here's that thread I am referring to: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1701273](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1701273)

Of course this may not apply to you, but it is strange that it stopped booting after an update like that, and it should be fairly easy for you to check this.
