### Post by ghostmunch on 2009-05-21
I'm late to reply and I can't really call myself an expert but here it goes.

****(1/2) **
The above will work as a square root in the equation.

Your right about y= being default. Or f(x)= if you prefer.

I don't know what you know about 3d plotting so I'll just write some 'stuff'.

3d can be coordinates(x,y,z) and one of those has to be the dependent variable which in kalgebra's case it happens to be z which is common any way.(Sorry if you already know most of this.)

It will be in the form:
**z=f(x,y)**
or:
**z=x+y**
or:
**f(x,y)=x+y**

Just put in **x+y** in the 3d graph and you'll see.
Add some square roots, powers, divisions and just go crazy with it.:)
