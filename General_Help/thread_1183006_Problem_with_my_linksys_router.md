### Post by Seq on 2009-06-10
Typically you would log into your router's administration page and enter your information. It will probably be one of these: [http://192.168.1.1](http://192.168.1.1) or [http://192.168.0.1](http://192.168.0.1) .

You will then need to change your connection type. It might say something like "DSL", or "PPPoE". Or you could just look around the first page or so for a username/password field. AT&T will have provided you with a username and password.

Linksys does know how to enter this as it is extremely common. I'm rather surprised if they indicated otherwise.

### Post by gunrunner45 on 2009-06-10
Dark_Blond

Try this.

I used to have AT&T DSL.  Anytime your DSL modem is power cycled you must reconnect the DSL modem to the Internet before you router will work again.

Disconnect the cable between your router & DSL modem & plug the cable from the DSL modem directly into your PC's Ethernet port.

Power Cycle your DSL modem then wait for the light to turn green again.

Follow the setup instructions provided by AT&T for setting up your account login & passwords.  Usually done by running the Install CD.  NOTE: You do NOT have to install all the extra software they are pimping on their CD.

Once you have completed the initial setup:

Reset your router back to factory defaults by pressing the reset button on the back of the router for 5 seconds.

Power cycle the router.

Plug the cable back in between the router & the DSL modem.

Plug your PC into on of the routers Ethernet ports.

NOTE: Resetting the router to defaults also reset any wireless security settings you might have saved to router previously.  Login to your routers Web interface as Seq suggested  using [http://192.168.0.1](http://192.168.0.1) or [http://192.168.1.1](http://192.168.1.1).  If you change nothing else on your router at least configure your wireless security to keep other people out of your network.  There are plenty of instructions on the net on this subject.

A good place for Linksys Info:  [http://www.linksysinfo.org/index.php](http://www.linksysinfo.org/index.php)


If all went well you should now be connected to the Net through your router.

NOTE:  Anytime your DSL modem is power cycled you will have to plug your PC directly into the DSL modem and reestablish a connection then reconnect your router.


Hope this helps.
