### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-05-22
> **FinalShot said:**
> Ok great, I guess its not possible to switch to Ctrl? Otherwise that'll take some getting used to ;)

Not as far as I know, but it wouldn't surprise me if you can change the shortcut. Bear in mind that you're changing operating system, and it's not like Ubuntu is a close relative to Windows... far from it. There will be plenty for you to get used to.

You could try Chromium as your browser; it has those keyboard shortcuts.

And I don't believe a virtual machine will suffice for games. I just tried it yesterday with two games on a Windows Vista guest, and mouse control was unusable (probably due to the load of the virtualised 3D graphics).

Some of your games may work in a Linux program called Wine. I currently run Portal, UT 2004 and Race Driver: GRID in Wine. However, don't have unrealistic expectations about its compatibility; statistically less than 20% of Windows programs are usable in Wine. You'll still be dual-booting, or buying a games console.

### Post by WorMzy on 2010-05-22
Ctrl+Tab cycles through tabs, like Alt+Tab cycles through programs. (Likewise, Ctrl+Shift+Tab, like Alt+Shift+Tab, reverses the cycle.)

There's only one shortcut that I liked on Windows that doesn't work on Linux (by default), and that it pressing backspace goes back in a tabs history (& Shift+Backspace goes forward). It can be enabled on Linux by going to about:config and changing browser.backspace_action to 0. It wouldn't surprise me if there's a way of enabling Ctrl+X, although I've never heard of that one before this topic.
