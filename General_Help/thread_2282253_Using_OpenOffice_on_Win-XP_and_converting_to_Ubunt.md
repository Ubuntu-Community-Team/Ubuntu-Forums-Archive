### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2015-06-12
Libreoffice is a fork of OpenOffice because Oracle basically left the project to die after acquiring Sun (the people who made OpenOffice.org) The developers walked out to form the Document Foundation which picked up the development. Oracle refused to donate the name "OpenOffice" to the DF so they had to come up with a new name and that was LibreOffice. Oracle claimed at the time that it was still the legitimate custodian of the OpenOffice project and would continue with its development despite the split, but nothing happened after a few years, Oracle has basically given up on OpenOffice and it is now given to the Apache foundation. I have no idea what happens since then. 

LO is 100% compatible with OO as far as I know.
