Thanks.The checkerboard isn't part of the image, it's used by image viewrs/Editors to show where the image is transparent (I assume you saved as PNG or GIF). If you don't want transparency you just have to tell Gimp (remove alpha channel, or add a background layer).

The problem with image editors is that correct image editing is not as simple as it looks for good results. Many operations you request cannot be implemented without layers, for instance.

### Post by stn21 on 2012-02-28
> **A Traveller said:**
> As mentioned, I have already tried GIMP and they make the easiest of tasks a pain ...

With GIMP you have to understand a few basic concepts. Once you have those GIMP becomes very intuitive and straightforward. 

The main concepts IMO are layers and masks. 

Once you start to see your workspace as a number of mostly transparent layers, one on top of the other, everything becomes obvious. Kind of like transparent plastic sheets with small pictures or text on each one. Disney made his movies that way. And now we can too :biggrin:

So don't worry about having to go through a few seemingly painful tutorials. You only have to do that until you get the system. Took me a while and now I would not do it any other way unless I really have to.

For really trivial tasks like viewing, cropping, flipping and cutting/pasting parts of pictures I still use any program that happens to be available, like irfanview.
