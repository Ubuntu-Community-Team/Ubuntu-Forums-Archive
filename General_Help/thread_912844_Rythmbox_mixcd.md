### Post by vanadium on 2008-09-07
It probably can be done to redirect the output of any audio player to a file. Such a procedure would preserve the digital quality. Unfortunately, I have no idea how (if it can be done in the first place).

Do not consider a procedure involving playing and recording for your CD project: the quality will significantly decrease because you have an analog conversion in between.

An alternative solution for you, that actually isn't that bad at all, is to use the audio editor Audacity to compile your CD. It involves importing the tracks. Then you can move the tracks in sequence, having them overlap a bit and using the volume envelope, realize the cross fade. With this approach, you have ultimate control on how the transitions are going to sound.

When done (or during editing), place markers between the different tracks. Audacity can automatically split the audio according to the markers to different files that are ready to be burned to an audio CD.
