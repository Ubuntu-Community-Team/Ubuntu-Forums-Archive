### Post by deadflowr on 2015-01-01
Lubuntu is a full-on linux distribution, it installs across all the directories.

Do you mean lubuntu-desktop?
Then yes, it'll mix it's own packages/apps with whatever is already installed on the system.

Lubuntu is nothing more than a customized version of the lxde desktop environment built on top of the Ubuntu base.
(customized meaning it adds a few extra packages versus a basic lxde setup)

Most desktop environments have some kind of development version always in the works, so yes you can *possibly* change it from the defaults.
Though, not all development versions are easy to install, or are even much different than what you would use anyway.
And by their nature development versions are unstable.

I wouldn't think about trying an out of date older version of a desktop environment, since support for those are most likely to have been dropped.
And typically older versions of anything need older versions of packages, which tend to through off the entire landscape of the system when installed.

I hope some of this makes sense, perhaps you should give us an idea of what you are trying to achieve.
