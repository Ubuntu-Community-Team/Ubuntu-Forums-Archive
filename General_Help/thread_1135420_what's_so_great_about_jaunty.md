### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-04-24
The biggest improvements for me personally are that boot time is much faster and my wireless card is supported natively.  Also, the Volume, Network Manager, Power Manager applets have some improvements, both visual and functional.  Jaunty has many other aesthetic improvements, such as a new login screen and some improvements to some of the default Compiz effects (such as the desktop background being fixed when switching workspaces).  And of course the applications in the Jaunty repos are more recent than those in Intrepid.

The only downsides I've seen so far are that the version of Amarok in the Jaunty repos is Amarok 2, and the version of VLC in the repos has a couple of bugs (i.e. video can't be embedded in the player, and controls won't display in fullscreen).  Amarok 2 lacks many of the features of Amarok 1.4 (including support for the Gnome Multimedia Keys script) and is IMO an inferior application.  And those bugs in VLC are almost deal-breakers even though it's my favorite media player.  Fortunately, both of these problems were solved through the addition of a couple of PPAs to my software sources, but they were fairly annoying for the hour or so it took to notice them and track down the PPAs.

User-friendly is relative.

I, for instance, am frustrated by graphical interfaces whose ratio of clicking-to-doing is greater than 1:1. The command-line is faster, simpler, and far more flexible.
