### Post by linux4me on 2010-02-13
> **twj said:**
> Just to update everyone. I installed Ubuntu 9.10 and it's running smoothly at the moment (hopefully nothing goes wrong). So now, I want to check with you guys what are the necessary applications to install for Ubuntu 9.10 for a start? Haven't install anything other than Adobe flash player.

Most everything is already there. You have Open Office (Applications -> Office) to take the place of M$ Office, Firefox as a web browser, Evolution (Applications -> Internet -> Evolution Mail) for email, GIMP (Applications -> Graphics) as a replacement for Photoshop, Evince for viewing PDF's, Rhythmbox (Applications -> Sound and Video) for playing your music files. There is a lot more already there I haven't listed.

If you're missing codecs for proprietary media, say Quicktime movies, you'll be prompted to allow download and installation of them when they're required. 

If you do specific things for which there isn't a default application, just search the forums or ask and you'll get plenty of suggestions. Another good approach is to go Applications -> Add/Remove and take a look at the applications available in the repositories. Most have ratings that will help give you an idea what's the most popular if there is more than one option in a category.
