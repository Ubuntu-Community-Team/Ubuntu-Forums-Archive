### Post by matthew on 2009-11-13
I'm sorry. I am aware of PDF versions for sale. I should have been more clear. I'm not aware of any location where the PDF has been posted for free download.

The thing is, we have a contract with a publishing company. The major expense of any book is not the physical (or digital) format, it is the writing, editing, digital layout and typesetting, and other production costs. While they allowed the book (copyrighted by Canonical) to be published by them with Canonical's requirement that it use a Creative Commons License, that doesn't mean they are going to distribute it freely, at least not until they genuinely feel they have recouped their investment and made enough money on top of it to help them take the risk of publishing other books--less than 20% of books published ever earn enough money to pay the advance costs, and realistically, about 2% of books published finance the remaining 80% of books that publishers will risk publishing "just in case" it makes money.
