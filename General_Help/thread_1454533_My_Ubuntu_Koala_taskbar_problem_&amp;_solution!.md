I thought I'd post this since it might help out someone else.

After doing a **clean install** of **Karmic Koala** on my Dell Dimension, installing the Ubuntu **updates**, and installing the latest **NVidia driver**, everything *seemed* to be working fine.

However, after **restarting** my system I found that I had lost my Gnome **"taskbar" panel** and the desktop right-click **context-menu**, and the only keyboard **shortcuts** that worked were **Ctrl-Alt-L** for login and **Ctrl-Alt-Del** for shutdown options.

I managed to get **online** via the **Help** button at the bottom of the shutdown options window, and while I found numerous people on the Ubuntu **forums** with **similar problems**, and numerous different **suggestions** for solving the problem - with varied results - I couldn't solve mine.

The problem kept returning after a number of clean **re-installs**, but on the last one my desktop context-menu returned! (I don't know *what* I did here, if anything. Sorry.) So, this allowed me to create desktop **launchers** for the programs I couldn't get to via the missing taskbar panel.

To make a long story shorter, I created a launcher for the **taskbar panel** on the desktop. (The command is **/usr/bin/gnome-panel**.) *However,* while the launcher worked, the panel would *always* be gone when I **restarted** my system. *That's* when I realized I could add the taskbar panel to my ***startup applications**!*

*Problem solved!* And I hope my little story can help anyone else having a similar problem.
