### Post by Evelyn on 2008-02-09
For those who are wondering, accatagon gave this answer in another thread. I have copied and pasted it below.

In probably one of the more bizarre twists on the design of openoffice, you can rotate images, but they have to be loaded as an openoffice draw object first.

If you wish to rotate the image, insert it into openoffice.org draw, then simply copy and paste it into writer. After that, you can simply right click on it and go to Position and Size, and it should let you rotate it.

Yeah, I know. It's weird. I would recommend checking and seeing if the openoffice team has any plans to implement easy image rotation in later versions&#65288;assume all pictures are draw objects?). You might consider checking their bug database, and if it's not included as a bug or a new feature, politely telling them about it (or perhaps asking in their forums why the poor design).

As another note, if you plan on rotating the image 90 degrees, it might be simpler just to rotate the image itself.

On yet another note, if you need office, you might be able to get it running using wine.

On one more note: if you want a nice lightweight word processor (openoffice is NOT lightweight) then I am a fan of abiword. It's probably not powerful enough for you (It REALLY doesn't like to rotate images), but it starts in 3 seconds on my seven-year-old computer, and it does all the basic formatting for a simple paper or document.
