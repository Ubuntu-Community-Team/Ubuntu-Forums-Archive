### Post by robenroute on 2007-01-13
I'm a Gnome user. I have nothing KDE on my computer. Let me correct that, I *had* nothing KDE on my computer, until very recently. I got so tired of the Gnome offerings for Bit Torrent clients, I finally threw in the towel.

Ktorrent just has a lot more to offer: one can easily import partial downloads, easily find torrents using the Search Tab, but most of all, it just doesn't stress my computer. I've got a laptop (P4-Mobile 1.8MHz), running Dapper and Edgy. As soon as I start a download with Bittornado (this is on Dapper, as I haven't installed Ktorrent on Edgy), CPU utilization climbs to 25%, roughly (without anything else running). When I start a second bit torrent download, another 25% is added. A few days ago, my laptop just switched off, and the brief moment before going black, I saw a notification on the screen mentioning something about having reached critical temperature (91 degrees Celcius) and having to shutdown. Don't get me wrong, I was awfully glad it actually did shutdown instead of frying my CPU, but I was somewhat gobsmacked as to why. I had to wait a few seconds in order to reboot and decided to install gkrellm and start the downloads again. As I was starting up the 5th download, I noticed the CPU hurtling at 100% and the temperature shooting for the stars. After stopping the downloads (still Bittornado), everything went back to normality. Went on to the Net and read something about Ktorrent behaving modestly. Gave it a shot, and guess what: I can have 20 torrents going at the same time and gkrellm shows me the CPU hasn't even warmed-up properly, let alone broken into a sweat.

I've tried the standard Bittorrent client (bad performance), Freeloader (too simple) and Azureus (what a monstrosity!), but they're all not as neat as Bittornado, or as fast. So, yes, I'm happy with Ktorrent not stressing my system and offering faster downloads than the standard Gnome utilities. However, I'm not happy with having KDE stuff on my system, it just looks and feels completely off.

Is this normal behaviour (no, not mine, Bittornado's)? Are there other, Gnome-based, alternatives? I'm open to suggestions.

Thanks.
