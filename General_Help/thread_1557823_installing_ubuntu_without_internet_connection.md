### Post by howefield on 2010-08-21
First off, you probably want 10.04.1 which is 10.04 plus all the updates/patches since April.

I don't believe the DVD offers you much more functionality than the CD. The size is mainly taken up with language packs ect. 

> DVD downloads
Don't be confused, even though DVDs can hold far more data than the typical Ubuntu CD, the main benefit of the DVD downloads is to get access to all of the available language packs. Most people will be fine with the standard CD installer. There are fewer download locations for the DVD images and this list is updated less frequently than for the CD images. 

So even with the DVD, for all it's size, you will still need to install codecs/packages that you may need just as you would with the CD edition to play DVDs. 

OpenOffice would be on the DVD, your graphics driver would depend on the make/model of the card.
