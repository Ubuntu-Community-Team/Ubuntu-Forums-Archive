### Post by dougalkerr on 2010-11-27
Any version of Ubuntu will load faster than Windows 7. I use Windows 7 at work and Ultimate Edition and Ubuntu 10.10 at home.

I can tell you that Windows takes 1 minute 40 seconds.

Ultimate Edition takes just under 1 minute.

Ubuntu 10.10 takes 20 seconds.

Both Ultimate Edition and Ubuntu 10.10 take 5 seconds for a complete shutdown as opposed to Windows 7 merely hibernating in quick time. For a full shutdown of Windows 7 takes at least 20 seconds depending upon which processes have been running...

KDE will not run any faster than Windows. I have a reasonably young computer at work and I can say I don't notice any difference in speed of operation between say, Open Office for Windows and Open Office for Linux in any environment. The differences come in the boot times and shutdown times.

KDE looks pretty but, I have found it has the odd hang-up every time I have tried to use it.

Hope this helps.
