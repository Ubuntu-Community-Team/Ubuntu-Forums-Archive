### Post by etonry on 2019-06-04
My screen, in any program with a window longer than one page, is moving around by itself, without my help.  I can scroll the screen up or down, but it does that by itself, too.  I notice now that typing in this box brings the screen back to where I can see what I'm typing, but not necessarily a consistent place.  Holding the mouse button down sometimes keeps the screen from moving, but not always.  At times the problem stops, but not permanently.  The only way to read a long online page is to move the cursor to another window.  That window will bounce around, but not the one I'm reading. I don't even have to click on that screen for this to happen.  This window was bouncing around too much, even with the typing, so I moved the cursor to another window.  I did not click in that window, just placed the cursor over that window. I have now continued typing here while the other window bounces.

I've been using computers for decades, and have never seen anything like this.  I have been using Ubuntu for several years, and I used to be a computer tech, and I've never seen automatic, unattended scrolling.  This just started this week, though I can't give an exact date.  Today I installed linux-generic kernel 4.15.0.51.53 / 55.  But this was happening yesterday, and maybe earlier.  

And sometimes the scroll wheel won't work.  I'll have to grab the side scroll bar and move it up and down.  And once in a while the window goes full screen for no good reason.  I've had the same computer, same hardware for a few years without problem.  I haven't downloaded any software recently either.

Anyone have any idea what might be causing this?

Ubuntu18.04.2 LTS  Dell Optiplex 990

### Post by Autodave on 2019-06-04
A long shot but.....try another mouse.  My guess is that the scrolling button on the mouse is failing.  Putting the cursor to another screen puts the problem scrolling wheel over there.

### Post by etonry on 2019-06-04
Problem solved. As Autodave and CatKiller suggested, it was the mouse.  I swapped it for another which worked fine.  And the errant mouse had the same problems on another, Windows, computer.  The mouse has been sent to mouse hell, and everything is fine now.  Thanks for your quick replies.  I had made the switch before I saw your comments, but they are still appreciated.
