### Post by thisnamestoolong on 2009-05-06
Just thought I would throw in my $0.02 here... if you only have 20 songs or so, it is not a big deal to rip to CD and then reburn as an MP3, but you will experience a very serious drop in sound quality. This also becomes unfeasible very quickly for those with large libraries, for obvious reason (time, CDs, losing track info, etc...). As of now, I am not aware of any way to circumvent iTunes 8 DRM (it was definitely possible with earlier versions), but I would not recommend using the burn to CD method either. I figured out a fairly simple work around for playing Fairplay files in Linux (install quicktime and windows version of songbird in Wine), which is kind of messy, but it is a better way to hold off until a better solution comes around the bend... it is only a matter of time before the iTunes 8.x encryption is beaten (not that I would do that, as that is against iTunes ToS and DMCA :)).
