### Post by detroit/zero on 2008-07-27
I've noticed that behavior more times than I can count on my Toshiba laptop. I've seen that it's possible to move the mouse cursor and register clicks without touching the mousepad, but just coming close to it.

Without going into all sorts of tehno-babble, mouse touchpads on laptops are capacitive in nature, meaning they detect charges and respond accordingly - similar technology to, but obviously more complex than, those kinds of lamps than can be turned on just by touching the metal surface of the lamp itself.

The severity to which it occurs could be related to a million things - humidity and the general environment your typing in, the cleanliness (or lack thereof) of your hands, are you wearing shoes, are you in a carpeted room or on a cement or tile floor, did you drag your feet as you walked and build a static charge, etc.

There's two ways to demonstrate this. Take your shoes off and drag you feet around the house on a dry day and build up some static charge. Go to your laptop and hover your finger about one or two mm above the pad (depending on acquired charge) and you should be able to move your mouse pointer without touching the pad. It'll be erratic and inconsistent, but it will move just the same.

Another way is to use two fingers on your pad. Put one finger on the pad and move the pointer. Then place a second finger on the pad and begine moving them both around. You'll see the pointer move in random patterns, jump to places on the screen and disappear to somewhere else, things like that. You should even be able to affect the way your pointer moves by not even touching the pad with your second finger, but just hovering it closely to it like in the step above.

I don't know how you'd go about curing this, but that's my guess as to what's going on.

Go to a Radio Shack and buy a ground strap or something, hook it into the third-prong on your electrical outlets, and wear it while you type?
