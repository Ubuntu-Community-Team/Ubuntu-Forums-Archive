### Post by Impavidus on 2020-09-06
The desktop basically shows the contents of the Desktop directory and the simplest way to do this is by ordering them in the same way as in any other directory – alphabetically on a rectangular grid. Automatically arranging them is not the feature, the possibility to arrange them manually is. It's not surprising that automatic is the default.

Annoying? Maybe. I guess the devs don't put anything on the desktop. Neither do I. I use the desktop as a weather monitor, so I want to keep it clean.

### Post by TheFu on 2020-09-07
My desktop doesn't allow any non-running programs.  Any icons on it are for processes which have been minimized (iconified really).  Different programs get iconified to different parts of the current workspace, which doesn't really matter, since a list of all of them is just a right-click away - or alt-tab - but with 30 processes in a workspace, alt-tab isn't a good solution.  There are settings to control whether processes are shown in any other workspace or just the current one where the window/icon are located.

Just because gnome does something a specific way, doesn't mean that is the only way it can be done. OTOH, if you need point-n-click customization, then stay with the most popular DEs/WMs and live with what they provide or what you can find a plugin to provide ... until 22.04 comes out and they redesign the entire DE experience to make it "better" again.
