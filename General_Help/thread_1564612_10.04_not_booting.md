### Post by varunendra on 2010-09-03
Given the symptoms, it looks like the boot process doesn't even reach the grub loading stage.

While booting, press and hold 'Shift' key to see if you get the grub advance boot menu.

Otherwise, go to BIOS, see if it has somewhere an option to set delay time for detection of hard disk, or time to wait for hard disk to boot. If there is, just increase it to say 5 or more seconds.

You may also try reinstalling grub through the Live USB as buntunub already said, the process is given [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Grub2#Reinstalling%20GRUB%202") (any of the three given methods should work if it is a grub error. However, try all three if needed.)

One more thing, it is evident from your boot info that you are triple-booting with Vista (:confused: you have real patience & courage) and Win7. If you have bootable DVD of any of these, you may try restoring their MBR through their recovery console ([see how]("http://neosmart.net/wiki/display/EBCD/Recovering+the+Vista+Bootloader+from+the+DVD"), you may have to repeat through step 2 three times) and see if you become able to boot into them. If you get any of them running, run a disk-scan on all visible drives selecting repair option.
