### Post by Simian Man on 2009-02-25
The main difference in terms of setup is that to get restricted drivers for Fedora, you must [enable RPMFusion]("http://rpmfusion.org/Configuration") and install the needed packages.  Post back (or at the [Fedora Forums]("http://forums.fedoraforum.org/")) if you have any trouble with that.  It's not harder than Ubuntu's method, just different.

Apart from that, the systems are pretty similar except that you get rpm/yum instead of dpkg/apt and newer packages.  Both are advantages in my opinion.  Hope you enjoy Fedora!
