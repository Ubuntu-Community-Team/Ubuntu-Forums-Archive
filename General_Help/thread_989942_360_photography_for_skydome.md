### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2008-11-22
When using the Compiz effect "rotate cube" and you select "skydome>animate skydome" it will take an image that you set to it and wrap it around a hemispherical environment that the desktop cube resides in.  It's kind of like the view you would have from inside  a snow globe, with the desktop cube as the object inside.  I've done a bit of messing around with it and I've used a photo of Zakk Wylde's "Wall-of-Doom" that I modified.  four Mashall Full-Stack guitar amps, and I copy/pasted several more of them together so that it was like a ending wall of guitar amps.

I've discovered that for an image to not look skewed in the skydome, it must be skewed just like the top half of a Mercator projection, and the image width must be 4 times the desktop width and twice the height, so getting an image to look right is difficult and requires a bit of number crunching.  

That being said, I've been thinking that it would be awesome to use 360* panoramic photos of city-scapes for the skydome.  has anyone tried this, and if so, where could I find some good images to use?  I've tried the usual places--www.gnome-look.org, deviantART, Google image search, WinCustomize.com (I know, I know... it seems like blasphemy, but they have a lot of good stuff that can be adapted from Stardock applications for use in Linux) and I've not been able to find anything really useful.

My ultimate desire would be to have a 360* panoramic photo of one of the on/off ramps at the "stack", which is a portion of the freeway system in Phoenix, AZ where several of the freeways come together and overlap each other (hence the name "stack") and it's located near Skyharbor Intl. Airport.  I'd like to get a photo of this in late evening/dusk in order to capture the unique brilliance of a sunset in the Sonoran desert, while contrasting the natural beauty of the sky and surrounding landscape with the modern architecture of the Phoenix freeways and the always cool "river of light" effect of using time lapse photography with cars driving past.  And would it be too much to ask for the photo to be taken during Meteor season?

...okay, so my particular choice of image is very off the wall and undoubtedly does not exist (yet... and none of you better steal my idea!).  And the equipment that would be required to capture such a unique shot would probably cost a small fortune, not to mention the fact that I wouldn't have the first clue as to how I would go about it.  But anyway, does anyone know where to find good 360* photographs?
