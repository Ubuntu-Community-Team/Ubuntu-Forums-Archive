### Post by ferulebezel on 2008-09-01
I just installed Ubuntu on a machine that previously had Fedora 3.  In the past I always just saved my bookmarks.html somewhere and copied over the same file in the new installation.  This doesn't seem to be working.  The new installation just seems to be ignoring them.  I don't want to copy my whole old .mozilla directory because I know that the extensions won't work.  I would like to also migrate my history, and passwords if possible.  How do I do it?

---

### Post by aysiu on 2008-09-01
You can copy the whole .mozilla directory over and then start Firefox with ```
firefox -safe-mode &
``` and then disable all the add-ons. Once Firefox has started up, you can actually uninstall the add-ons and then you'll have history, bookmarks, and passwords.

But I don't see why you think your extensions definitely won't work.
