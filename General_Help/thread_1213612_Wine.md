Why did you install ubuntu?

### Post by CatKiller on 2009-07-15
> **curtis_murray said:**
> Whoa, it's like a second Add/Remove. Whats the difference between Add/Remove and Synaptic Package Manager?

They are both front-ends for [APT]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Packaging_Tool"). Synaptic shows you all the packages that you could install, including libraries and suchlike. Add/Remove has a simplified interface that only shows you some things. That kind of thing happens a lot with Linux; because a lot of tools are front-ends for other things there are a number of alternatives that do the same thing in a slightly different way. It means that you can use a tool that you like rather than only having one way of doing things. Linux has also inherited the UNIX philosophy of avoiding large complicated applications in favour of small simple applications that do one thing very well but that are designed to be easily combined with standard inputs and standard outputs.

Good luck with your exploration of Ubuntu.

BTW, I'm one of those people that really likes Amarok. I used iTunes briefly, and it's not even close to being as good as Amarok. But then, people prefer what they're used to.
