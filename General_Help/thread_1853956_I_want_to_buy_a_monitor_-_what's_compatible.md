### Post by mcduck on 2011-10-03
The monitor doesn't care what you plug into it, as long as it gets standard signal using whatever connectors the monitor has and using a resolution & refresh rate it's capable of displaying.

And on the other side the computer and graphics card doesn't care what monitor you connect to it, it's just outputting whatever it's told to output.

...which means that the only thing you need to worry about is your graphics card, if that works correctly then all displays with suitable connectors are compatible. It makes absolutely no difference what operating system you use. The monitor doesn't care. :)
