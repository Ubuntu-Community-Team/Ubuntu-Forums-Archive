### Post by HiImTye on 2012-04-18
if you are comfortable with the command line then I would give ffmpeg a try. there is nothing more powerful for capturing, encoding, transcoding, combining input streams (i.e. audio and video from separate sources), streaming, etc..

if you prefer something graphical then Avidemux is a great option

### Post by HiImTye on 2012-04-19
if youre interested in stitching together video files, then Pitivi is a great choice also. I always use a combination of FFMPEG, Avidemux and Pitivi, depending on what I'm doing
I often use FFMPEG for straight conversion as its the fastest of all choices, since there is no wasted overhead on a GUI, but depending on your needs you may find you need Pitivi or Avidemux as well (for editing, stitching clips together, etc)
