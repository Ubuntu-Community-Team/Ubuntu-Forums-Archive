### Post by Worker 11811 on 2009-05-17
I had occasion to fix up my Mom's computer shortly before the New Year.  (2008 > 2009)

She has a very old IBM Aptiva 2270 she got through People PC.
(Against my stringent objections.)

It has a 533MHz Celeron and it came with 64MB of memory.

She got a nasty virus and I was unable to repair the system.  I asked her for the rescue CD that came with the computer but she couldn't find it.  Without that disk the computer was trashed.

The computer was not capable or running XP or Vista.  I was not willing to go hunting around on eBay for a scavenged copy of Windows 98.  Mom was not very hip to the idea of having to pay money for a new comptuer.  I went home and thought about it for a while.

I looked through my junk box and found a couple of sticks of old PC-100 RAM and an old PATA hard drive.  This would max it out at 128 MB of RAM and 120 GB of hard drive.

I have an old Dell comptuer that I bought from work as a "beater" machine.  I put the HD into the Dell, installed Ubuntu (8.10) and tested it.  When I was satisfied with it, I pulled the hard drive out and packed it plus the RAM and a tool kit in my knapsack.

The next day I appeared on Mom's doorstep, hard drive and Ram sticks in hand.  It took me less than 15 minutes to have that old computer running again.

The thing works perfectly.  It's old and slow but it works well.
In fact, I would say it works better than it did when it was new!

Mom kicked and screamed a bit because it wasn't what she was used to but when she was faced with the choice of having Ubuntu for FREE or paying for a new computer, she resolved herself to use it.  It took her, probably, two weeks to get used to it but now she actually PREFERS Ubuntu to Windows!

For the first time in more than 10 years of owning a computer, she actually learned to upload pictures from her digital camera to Flickr.com!  (Using "F-Spot".)  She now e-mails all her friends the links to those pictures!  :)

This is the best part!...  
About a week ago, she stopped in to the public library to use the computer.  She was out shopping and she wanted to send a quick e-mail to a friend about something she saw in the store.
She logged in to the computer and, when faced with the prospect of having to use Internet Exploder to read her e-mail from Yahoo Mail, she logged out and went home!

After using Ubuntu for a little more than 4 months, she doesn't want to go back to Windows!  :lolflag:

For more than 10 years she has struggned with her computer, just to send and receive e-mail.  Now that she has Ubuntu, she now uses Open Office to read and write word processing documents.  She uses Firefox and Thunderbird to get e-mail and read the internet.  She's quickly becoming an "ace" photographer, using her "CoolPix" digital camera and F-Spot.

Mom is over 65 years old and she has always been a "fumble-fingers" when it comes to using computers.  Now she uses it almost every day!  It's almost like she has a brand new computer!  She actually LIKES using it now!  :)

So, given that experience, I would not hesitate to recommend upgrading your Mom's computer to Ubuntu!  8-)
