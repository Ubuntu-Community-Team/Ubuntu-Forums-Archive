### Post by kamicosmos on 2008-01-10
I picked up Practical Guide to Ubuntu Linux last Friday.  The ink was practically still wet from the presses!

I haven't got to use it much yet, hoping to tear into it this weekend.  I picked it up since it was 7.10 specific.  The other Ubuntu books at that particular Border's were all older versions.  One thing I have noticed about it, is it definitely has a wide range of knowledge levels.  I saw basic 'What Does This Button Do?' up to scripting exercises, setting up Apache, security stuff, etc.  I am pretty excited that hopefully I have a book that will actually help me learn my particular dist of linux.  My past experiences with linux books have always been 'Oh, this looks handy, now to hop on the web and spend hours figuring out how to do in on MY linux box....'
