### Post by aaronp on 2009-04-14
You should find you can use it for anything that you can use Win for. Some things will be much easier to do and others will be a bit harder to configure. I use Ubuntu for everything at home.
I got a new PC with Vista on it and immediately partitioned my hard drive and re-installed Ubuntu 8.04, wiping Vista within an hour of taking delivery of the PC.

Maybe have a look at some of the apps you use on your other PCs for actually achieving your work tasks and see if you can find some Ubuntu/Linux apps that will do the same thing for you. You could test it out and maybe start to decrease your reliance on Win.

If you have it all networked to the other PCs, it may be a good way to set up an intranet (using apache and maybe php/mysql) which you can serve to the other PCs from the Ubuntu server. Also maybe a file server or backups etc.??

Good luck and have fun
