### Post by Impavidus on 2017-08-28
You can. Use manual partitioning.

But why do you want those 4 partitions? Linux is not Windows, the reasons why you want those partitions in Windows may not apply to Linux. In Windows, all those partitions have their own directory tree, in Linux all of them are combined in one directory tree, making the partitions almost invisible to the user. But having a separate /home partition is often useful.

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2017-08-28
Don't underestimate it takes some basic homework to install Ubuntu.    Saying Windows has four partitions could mean you have two physical drives with two partitions each, or some other combo.    Two of those partitions are likely for system recovery and system boot manager/loaders, etc.

Best to send us a screenshot of your drive topography (using Windows system tools or the Gparted program in Linux).

If that sounds difficult or like too much work . . . probably best just to stick with what you know.
