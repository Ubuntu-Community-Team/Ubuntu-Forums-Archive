### Post by IWantFroyo on 2010-12-26
We all hate the "Google it." There are too many things on Google to get you pretty much anywhere (although Google is pretty darn awesome).
On to the driver problem.
Go to Systerm-Administration-Additional drivers
Wait a moment...
Find something with a name that relates to wireless connectivity (like for me it says Broadcom B43 Wireless Driver)
Click remove, then hook your computer up with an Ethernet cable and re-"activate" it.
Voila.
Hope this helped!
