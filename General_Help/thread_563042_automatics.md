### Post by rsambuca on 2007-09-29
It is recommended by the Ubuntu developers that you do NOT use automatix.  While it does get things installed on your system, in some cases it does so with methods that will cause system unstability later on.  It can definitely cause problems with future upgrades as well.  Here is a l[ink to the specifics of why Automatix is not recommended]("http://mjg59.livejournal.com/77440.html").

If you let us know what you are having trouble installing, I am sure that someone will be able to assist you quite easily without automatix.  Most of the stuff it installs nowadays is easily installed without it.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2007-09-29
I agree with rsambuca, don't use it.  Just about everything it can do is easily done with simple additions to your sources.list file, allowing you to install the packages that are not in the dedicated ubuntu repos.

In particular have a look at the pages here [http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/](http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/) for **medibuntu,** and here [http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/packages.php](http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/packages.php) for the packages available; do what it tells you, and then you can get everything you need for multimedia, etc etc.  It will also, incidentally, help you to learn so much more about linux and its workings, rather than just doing everything that Automatix does without thinking about what's going on and why.
