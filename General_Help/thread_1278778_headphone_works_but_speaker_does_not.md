### Post by Megrimn on 2009-09-30
It could be volume settings, then.  Try going into the sounds settings and selecting each of the 'tracks' individually and check the volume on each one by turning up the volume by the normal method (ie keyboard buttons or clicking on the volume icon on the panel).  The last 'track' you select before you close the sound settings will be the one that will go up and down when you turn the volume up and down normally.

### Post by ZaHACKieL on 2009-10-09
I had a similar problem once with a Dell Studio 1735. I wrote about that here: 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1009182](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1009182)

What happened at last was that the card where the headphones jacks are was damaged and at the same time I had a bug where you get sound only from the headphones. 

To see if it's a hardware problem you should run the Dell Diagnostic Tool. You can choose to test all the hardware or just the audio parts. Be ready to have a headphone and a mic to plug in the jacks.

Try that and let me know what happens.
