So has iPod. Unfortunately not OGG, but MPEG-4 and two lossless codecs (WAV and a compressed Apple format). The latter is great for using the iPod as a studio tool and playing on hifi systems.

> playlists

So has iPod.

> sound settings configurable

So has iPod.

> optical-in and line-in recording

Granted, the iPod only has microphone input. However, for real audio recording I never use less than 24-bit / 96 kHz anyway in order to edit properly afterwards.

> regular firmware updates

So has iPod.

> you can even install linux on it...

As with iPod.

In addition, you can watch videos, store digital photos from your camera and show on a TV, store RSS newsfeeds and read them on the bus, check your calendar and address book on the road, and so on. Granted, you need iTunes to maximise it's potential (simple updating of music, lyrics, videos and podcasts), but even without it has a lot of features that the competition misses out on.
