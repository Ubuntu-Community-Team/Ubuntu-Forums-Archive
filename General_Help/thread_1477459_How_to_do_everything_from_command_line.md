### Post by Ordes on 2010-05-08
If you dont want those sys-tray icons, you could also just remove the notification area. However, i think most applications do not create a sys-tray icon. Does who do, are mostly apps that are supposed to run more in the background (e.g messenger, media-player), and its mostly to gain more control over those apps (changing status, etc)..For some apps you can choose whether or not to show a sys-tray icon.

If you just want more space, or dont want to have a top-panel, than u could setup your custom bottom panel by (right click) and adding the parts u like.
