### Post by elchebud on 2010-08-25
Basically it's the same thing. LPI an CompTIA have come to an agrement in 2009 that Linux+ certification and LPIC-1 certfication is one same thing.

[http://lpi.org/eng/about_lpi/what_s_new/comptia_and_lpi_join_forces_to_advance_global_linux_workforce](http://lpi.org/eng/about_lpi/what_s_new/comptia_and_lpi_join_forces_to_advance_global_linux_workforce)

So if you pass 1 , you are certified for both.

### Post by srs5694 on 2010-08-25
I'm the author of those books. They're *almost* identical, but the Linux+ title has a few minor corrections and updates, so I'd recommend getting it whether you're taking the LPIC-branded or Linux+-branded exam.

Backstory: Originally the CompTIA Linux+ and LPI's LPIC-1 certifications used different exams, but when CompTIA updated the Linux+ exam recently, they struck a deal with LPI to use the LPIC-1 exams as the updated Linux+. In response, Sybex had me "revise" my existing LPIC-1 book for the Linux+ exam, which involved going through and changing references to the exam and making updates to reference changes in the Linux world. Most of these changes are very small, though -- an extra sentence here and there, mostly. I caught a few typos, too.
