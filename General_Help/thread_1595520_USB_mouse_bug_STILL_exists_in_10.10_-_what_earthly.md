### Post by qamelian on 2010-10-13
Have you actually filed a bug report or contributed to an existing one that relates to your problem? No bug report means no fix. Even if a bug report exists, it needs to be confirmed by enough people to demonstrate that it is important enough to give some priority. Obviously, bug reports that appear to affect a large number of users gets attention faster than a bug that affects a relatively small number or that can't be confirmed.
