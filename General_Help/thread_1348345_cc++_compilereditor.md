### Post by Menthu_Rae on 2009-12-07
I haven't taken a huge in depth look at it myself, but as far as I know geany can do #3 (definitely) and #1 (fairly sure in the way you want). As for #2, not really sure...

Anyway it's very small (2MB) - take a look at it:

```
sudo apt-get install geany
```

(there are plugins and addon packages for it, too)

Be sure to adjust the options in preferences. Also note it has a compile/build button/hotkeys so you just hit those and you're away :D (has a built in terminal window, too).
