### Post by blazemore on 2010-05-13
Technically yes. But don't do it. A better solution in almost every situation is to back up your files, make a note of any important applications you've installed, then just download the 64-bit CD and do a clean install.

Besides, unless there's a truly compelling reason to switch to 64-bit, like you have >4Gib of RAM, don't consider it an "Upgrade". You're more likely to have problems (Java, Flash, etc) on 64-bit, at least in my experience.
