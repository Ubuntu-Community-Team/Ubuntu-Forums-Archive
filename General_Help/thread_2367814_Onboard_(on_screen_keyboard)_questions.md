### Post by sudodus on 2017-08-03
I think it is convenient enough to select "Show Onboard" from the Onboard widget/icon on the top bar (the four squares forming a big square or a window). I think this is the system tray or at least works like a system tray.

Certainly you can put [a copy of or symbolic link to] its desktop file in Startup Applications. Or you can put a symbolic link to its desktop file on the Ubuntu Desktop.

But it is also rather easy to use dash -- Click on the Ubuntu symbol in the top left corner and type the two letters 'on' to get the two Onboard icons to click alongside a few other icons or three letters 'onb' to get only the Onboard icons.
