### Post by DrMelon on 2010-08-07
If it only lets you boot to safe mode that sounds like a driver issue to me. Is your PC well-ventilated? I've killed a graphics card from overheating before, and that's what gave me this issue.

Reading over your post, I think the only thing you can do is to replace the graphics card - or contact NVidia and explain the issue to them. They may have some diagnostic software that could shed some more light on the issue.

Formatting your harddisk will not solve the issue if it's a problem with your graphics card.

### Post by neoargon on 2010-08-07
There is a simple way to know whether the problem is hardware related . Download a free cd of [Kubuntu]("http://www.kubuntu.org") or [Ubuntu]("http://www.ubuntu.com") and boot into it as live cd (you can run these operating systems from cd. Without installing) . Install Nvidia driver . Install and run some 3d games like "extreme tux racer" or "alien arena" . If there is no problem in running these games, your problem is virus infection . Otherwise it is a hardware problem  

Factory restoring is not equivalent to re installing windows . Virus (if any) would still persist
