### Post by phredbull on 2010-02-08
I noticed this on my laptop as well when I plugged into a decent set of speakers. I think it's just the poor quality of the built-in sound card. (Interference caused by audio, power, and data being crammed close together with poor insulation.) I guess dropping it could have made it worse. Replacing the internal sound card would probably be more trouble than it's worth, but you can get a USB audio interface.
