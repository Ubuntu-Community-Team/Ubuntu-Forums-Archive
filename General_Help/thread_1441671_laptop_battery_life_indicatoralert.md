### Post by chessnerd on 2010-03-29
Sounds like something got bumped in the power management settings. Go to System>Preferences>Power Management and then go to the "General" tab and, if it isn't already, check the "Always Display Icon" option.

As for having the laptop hibernate on low power, you can go to the "On Battery Power" tab and choose Hibernate under "When battery power is critically low." 

I don't know enough about the system to tell you how to set up alerts or choose precise percentages for the various actions, but I hope this helps.
