### Post by aesis05401 on 2009-05-02
The most important thing to remember is that a properly administered Linux install 'matures' instead of 'ages.'  

I am relatively new to desktop linux and I can tell you that my Ubuntu boxes have morphed significantly over the eight months or so that I have been running them. 

At first I tested the bounds of their multimedia capabilities and played with various theme/effect packages... Then I cut most of that out and tried a couple different networking/virtch configs... Then I used tasksel (what a wonderful little tool!) to play with services on my lan.

After the initial experimentation period (about six months of hobby time) I had a small core of linux boxes that had evolved to fit my specific needs.  

For comparison (I support MS solutions during most of my working hours), I usually reinstall my Windows boxes before re-tasking them due to a lengthy history of instability developing over time on that platform.

That is the difference between maturing and aging in OS terms.  Platforms that mature grow to fit your needs, platforms that age become brittle over time.

There is definitely a learning curve to getting the most out of your Linux installs - but if you dedicate a bit of hobby time each week you will be amazed at how fast it all comes together.  It is hard to imagine going back to what now seems like a 'generic' OS like Windows when I can have each Linux install optimized for exactly what I want.
