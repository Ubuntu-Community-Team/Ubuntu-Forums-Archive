### Post by Paddy Landau on 2010-03-25
I don't fully understand your question.

Did you mean that, if you delete the RAR file, then there would be sufficient space, but you can't delete it because you haven't extracted it yet?

In your situation, I believe that you have no choice but to save the RAR file elsewhere (a 4Gb flash drive is cheap these days). Then extract the RAR file into the free space.

Or even better, get a 16Gb flash drive, extract the RAR onto it, then delete the RAR. Now you have your extracted files *and* lots more free space!
