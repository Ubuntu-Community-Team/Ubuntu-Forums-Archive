### Post by earlra on 2008-03-21
I regret to inform you that it sounds like you are in the same boat I'm in.  I am at the tail end of migrating to Linux (and loving every minute of it).  The last hurdle is that my printer is not Linux compatible - yep, it's a Lexmark (rebranded under Dell).  

Check yours out on [http://www.linuxprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Lexmark-x5470](http://www.linuxprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Lexmark-x5470)
The short answer is, to make these all-in-one printers cheap, the processing power is offloaded to the PC, but Lexmark does not produce drivers for Linux.

I am so happy with Linux that I am already shopping for a printer.  I'm looking at HP, since Ubuntu has pre-installed an HP printer driver utility (HP LIP Toolbox).  Be careful, though, as not all HP printers are compatible.
