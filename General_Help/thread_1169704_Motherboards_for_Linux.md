### Post by lindsay7 on 2009-05-27
I did not look at your specs for the PS's but overall I would say that the modular designs are a lot easier and cleaner installs since the cabling allows for that and you only use what you need. You will get better air flow too and it just looks better.  My rule of thumb for a PS is to just look at the picture of it first, if the fan is vented to the rear it is not normally a good PS, if the fan is vented down, it normally will be a good PS.  The reason, cheap under powered (there is a difference between peak power and normal loading) PS's will not have a big fan and the configuration of a big fan can not be offered if it vents to the back of the PS. The bigger fans can only vent down.

### Post by tommcd on 2009-05-30
The AMD 790X / SB750 chipset is still pretty new. Phoronix tested the Phenom X3 CPUs on Ubuntu 9.04 with the AMD 780G Chipset with the AMD SB710 Southbridge. Ubuntu 9.04 would not work. They had to use Ubuntu 9.10 alpha 1:
[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=amd_phenom2_x3&num=1](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=amd_phenom2_x3&num=1)
It seems that the SB710 southbridge was the problem there.
Phoronix.com is an excellent site for linux hardware reviews.
Just to be safe, I would research the compatibility of the 790X chipset with linux before purchasing this motherboard.
In general, slightly older hardware usually has better compatibility with linux than brand new hardware.

Is anyone using the AMD 790X / SB750 chipset in Ubuntu?
