### Post by satanselbow on 2011-12-07
> **tartalo said:**
> The advantage of a book for a novice is that the information is supposed to be ordered and correct, while a forum is known to be a chaos were it's difficult for a newcomer to distinguish correct information from misinformation or pure trolling:

The disadvantage with books is they very rapidly become outdated - especially in the current climate of rapidly evolving desktops, updated kernels and migrating bootloaders... you don't specifically state which version of Ubuntu you are currently on but there are very few quality 11.04 / 11.10 publications - most authors will be hanging out for 12.04 before publishing as it is an LTS. 

If you are Ubuntu centric in your Linux Educational requirements (not a bad place to start!) this forum would indeed be a good place to start as would:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/](https://help.ubuntu.com/)
[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/)

and

[http://www.webupd8.org/](http://www.webupd8.org/)



As suggested before - work out what you can do already and what you would like to achieve...

Just as an example - there are apps in the repos that just do not work out the box or are "crippled" due to licensing issues but do work when compiled from source. PCSXR being a good and fun example ;) or adding addition codec support to ffmpeg :D

There are plenty of options and paths to choose :popcorn:
