### Post by 3Miro on 2012-07-17
Avast has a .deb package on their web-page, you should be able to just download it, double-click on it and it should install.

Why do you need an AV software on Ubuntu. The only time you need AV software is when you are moving tons of files between Ubuntu and a Windows machine. Avast and the other AV programs don't look for "Linux viruses" at all, they only look for Windows viruses. Avast will not make your Ubuntu any more secure, it can only help Windows machines that may be sharing files with you.

The only way AV software on Windows can help with security is if you constantly keep the virus definitions up-to-date. However, if you are going to update, you might as well update your system and patch the vulnerability that the virus is exploiting. Effectively this kills the virus.

It is not that Ubuntu is immune to viruses, it is just that Ubuntu kills the viruses before they can do any damage. Just keep your system updated and you are safer than you would ever be in Windows.
