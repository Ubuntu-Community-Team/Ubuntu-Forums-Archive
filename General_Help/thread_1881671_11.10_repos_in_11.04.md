### Post by beew on 2011-11-16
In general it won't work and you may break your system. What packages do you want to upgrade? Usually you can do it easily with ppas. I recommend a targeted approach.

I have done that before (using 10.10 repositories in 10.04) but only for "small" packages that didn't have any external dependency,--I needed the updated versions because the versions in 10.04  repositories were either broken or lacking some features I needed and I couldn't find debs or ppas, and didn't know how to compile things then. After I got my updated packages I immediately disabled those repositories. You wouldn't want to accidentally "upgrade" a whole bunch of system libraries and end up breaking compatibility with a lot of stuffs.
