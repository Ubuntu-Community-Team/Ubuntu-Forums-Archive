### Post by amitkher on 2008-08-26
Depends on your needs and inclination. I have tried many for my EEEPC 701. 

1. All Ubuntu based distributions have the problem that Ubuntu loves to open huge unresizable windows (synaptic and add/remove software). If you are comfortable with command line apt-get or aptitude, this problem will not apply to you. Again, your 9 inch screen might fare better than my 7 inch one.

2. You will not want to install gcc, devtools etc. on your little storage space. Even if space is enough, compilation will be slow with EEEPC, so you will want readymade software rather than compiling. Hence, choose among popular distros like ubuntu,debian,fedora,suse etc. so that you can get most software from their repositories.

3. Hardware support is best for eeebuntu among the distros I tried.  Except a glitch with wired LAN which keeps resetting every minute, eeebuntu supports all hardware out of the box. I tried damn small linux, puppy, eeedora, fedora 8, fedora 9, eeebuntu NBR 1.0, eeebuntu classic 1.0, ubuntu hardy.

4. damn small linux is great if you do not need additional software. It has 2.4 kernel, so I couldn't get my cisco vpn to work with it. It takes around 15 MB RAM for general operation. Doesn't support camera as far as I know. Compilation from source is always possible.
