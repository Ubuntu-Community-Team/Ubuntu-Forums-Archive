### Post by Roasted on 2010-01-20
I have Docky installed on two computers. My work laptop and my personal desktop. Both running Ubuntu 9.10 64 bit with Docky installed from the Gnome-Do in the repos.

On my desktop, the only icons that show up in Docky are the currently active windows. If I close Firefox, the Firefox icon disappears from the dock.

On the laptop, if I close Firefox, Firefox remains in the dock but the white dot below it (which signifies whether or not it's a running application) goes away.

How can I set the laptop to mimic the desktop? I like to treat my dock as a way to manage ONLY open applications, instead of it being a quick launcher for everything. How can I adjust this?

### Post by Ahriman on 2010-01-20
The icon that doesn't have the white dot underneath it is a launcher. Drag it off the Docky bar and let it go on your desktop (you will see the 'poof' animation) and it should only appear whenever it's an active application, not as a launcher.
