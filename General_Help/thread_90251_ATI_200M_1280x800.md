### Post by Remmelas on 2005-11-15
I had a similar problem with mine.  I had to manually work modelines, add them to /etc/X11/xorg.conf, and set the resolutions in the same file.  Many people end up with simpler solutions however, just be setting values in the monitor section, mainly HorizSync and VertRefresh.  An example of my monitor section is below, but it needs to be taylored to your specific monitor, don't use my data, could trash your screen.

```
Section "Monitor"
        Identifier      "KDS XF-7s"
        Option          "DPMS"
        Modeline "1152x864" 109.72 1152 1184 1600 1632 864 880 891 908
        Modeline        "1024x768" 78.75 1024 1040 1136 1312 768 769 772 800
        Modeline        "800x600"  56.25 800 832 896 1048 600 601 604 631
        Modeline        "640x480"  36.00 640 696 752 832 480 481 484 509
        HorizSync       30.0 - 70.0
        VertRefresh     50-160
        DisplaySize     304     228     # 1152x864 96dpi
EndSection

```

you can use a modeline calculator(google is your friend), then xvidtune to fine tune to your monitor, and show you a useable modeline.
