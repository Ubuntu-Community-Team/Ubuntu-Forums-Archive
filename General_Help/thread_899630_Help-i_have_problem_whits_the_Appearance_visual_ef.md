### Post by windoze87 on 2008-08-24
this probably belongs in the desktop effects category... oh well. First, you need to find out what card you have and make sure the drivers are installed. If you're using the Gnome desktop, you can find out by going to System>Administration>Hardware Drivers. It'll prompt you for your password. once you do that, itll pop up a box listing any propriatary drivers on your system... you need to look for Nvidia or ATI (I think intel's pretty much supported but i might be wrong) and see if the "Enabled" box is checked, and if "Status" says "In Use". If the enabled box is NOT checked, check it. Ubuntu will download the drivers for your video card from your distro's repository, install them, and then ask you to restart. Once that occurs, on restart you should have neat effects. 

On the other hand...if you just have an onboard graphics card you're probably not going to get the special effects compiz provides.
