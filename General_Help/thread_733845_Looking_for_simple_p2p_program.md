### Post by Nameless_one on 2008-03-24
First, I think that you shouldn't install Hadry on their computer, as it's still a beta and they might encounter problems they won't be able to solve. Gutsy works just fine. 

As for your p2p needs, Frostwire might be what you're looking for, although it's really cluttered with viruses and spam. If your parents only download music and videos, I don't think they'll have a problem. The best choice in my opinion would be DC++ (linuxdcpp in the repos I think), if you set it up for them so that it autoconnects to a rich hub when it starts, and then they can simply search and download. 

I don't know about Dutch translations in either of these.
