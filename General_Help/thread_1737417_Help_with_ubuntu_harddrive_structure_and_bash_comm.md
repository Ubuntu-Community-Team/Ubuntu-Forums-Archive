### Post by mikewhatever on 2011-04-23
The equivalent of C:\ in Linux is simply /, and to browse to /etc/.xxx just 'cd /etc/.xxx'.
There are more bash tutorials then one can mention, you can just google, but here are some well known:
[http://linuxcommand.org/](http://linuxcommand.org/)
[http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Bash-Prog-Intro-HOWTO.html](http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Bash-Prog-Intro-HOWTO.html)

dir - ls
mk  - mkdir
cd  - cd
cd..- cd ..
cd\ - cd/
