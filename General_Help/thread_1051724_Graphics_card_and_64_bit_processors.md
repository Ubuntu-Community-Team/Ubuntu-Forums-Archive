### Post by VeeDubb on 2009-01-27
I don't know about opera, but wine most certainly works just perfectly on a 64bit processors.

Also, most 32 bit applications can be run on 64 bit processors with a very minimal amount of effort.

As for using a non-nvidia graphics chipset, ATI is fine, but I suspect that you will be disappointed if you go intel.

Nvidia and ATI both have very good linux drivers, but the last time I tried, intel chipsets had VERY poor 3D acceleration under linux, and that would certainly cause a problem for compiz.


As for which specific processor you go with, the truth of the matter is that even the cheapest processors have so much power these days, that you really won't see a big difference unless you are doing some very resource intensive things like video editing, multimedia transcoding, or heavy gaming, and even then, the difference between intel and AMD is meaningless.  I used to be a really big AMD thing, especially when the first gen of P4 came out and sucked.  These days, they are interchangable. 

(For that record, that's a statement that is likely to tick off AMD fans, AND intel fans. lol)
