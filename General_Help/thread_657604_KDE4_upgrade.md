### Post by cmost on 2008-01-03
Considering that KDE 3.5.x and KDE 4.0.0 are essentially two different OS's, I don't see what difference it makes what version of the KDE 3.5.x series you attempt to upgrade from.  I think your best bet would be to update from the release candidate since that's the closest relative to the final version.  Speaking of which, you are aware that the upcoming 4 release isn't considered "final" per se, aren't you?  This is more like a jumping off point for developers.  The developers have made it clear that this is an incomplete OS and that there is still much work to do!  I would hesitate to use this as my every day system.  You might want to keep your KDE 3.5.x handy.  I'm sure you'll need it.

### Post by der_joachim on 2008-01-04
When RC2 came out, users were instructed to remove a few RC1 packages first. These were very clear instructions and upgrading to RC2 was a breeze. KDE3 and KDE4 coexist happily and KDE4 even uses a number of KDE3 settings. 

However, the general sentiment of RC2 is that it is neither very customizable or fast, and that you'd better wait for 4.0.0 or even 4.1. I agree with these sentiments, but that is just MHO. If you cannot wait for seven days, just follow [this guide]("http://kubuntu.org/announcements/kde4-rc2.php"). Good luck. :)
