### Post by agillator on 2012-11-29
Welcome to Linux and Ubuntu. It can seem overwhelming at first, but it will all fall into place. 

Your question, as stated, is really impossible to answer with any confidence. What do you use your computer for? What do you really need? Word processing, graphics, video editing, rocket guidance? What do you do on your computer? You see the problem.

You might go to [http://www.ubuntugeek.com/](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/) and see if anything there strikes your fancy. That would be a start. Then perhaps you can come back and ask your question again with some specificity.

A trip to your local book store to browse the books available to see what catches your eye will probably also be profitable.

One thing I will point out to try to save some frustration. They way of thinking in Linux is totally different from Windows. A windows user tends to expect a program to do a job from beginning to end. That means the user is tied to what the author of the programs thinks. On the other hand, Linux inherits its attitude from Unix. It expects user to know what he wants to do and to think. A linux/unix program tends to do only one thing, but do it very, very well. The user is expected to tie programs together (pipe) to accomplish what he wants accomplished which may be something no programmer ever considered. This means, to me, far more power available to the user but the potential for more frustration until you get used to it. 

Good luck, happy hunting, enjoy the trip. Don't hesitate to ask questions here, and don't be surprised at how quickly little things fall into place and you begin understanding the big things.

### Post by mungatsuma on 2012-11-30
This is something that most windows users will never believe, ubuntu is simpler to understand and use than windows. You want a text editor: on the Dash just type > text, if its a spreadsheet, just type that > spreadsheet or even > office and the application for that will be among the choices. Really that simple try it. Hover the mouse over the entry and you get a brief defination about the entry. The commonly used applications are on the launcher, if you are using unity, same thing hover the mouse over an Icon and you get info.

Only caution is use the right terms, like presentation and not powerpoint , spreadsheet not excel and mail, not outlook.
