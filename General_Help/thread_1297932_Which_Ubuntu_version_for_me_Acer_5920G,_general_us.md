### Post by phillw on 2009-10-22
A very good guide for dual booting is available from here ....

[http://apcmag.com/howto_search.htm?q=Keyword&catid=198](http://apcmag.com/howto_search.htm?q=Keyword&catid=198)

I know it works - coz it's the one I used !!!

As always, you really should backup your data. If you have access to a usb hard-drive, or decent sized usb flash drive - there is stuff on the net to allow you to backup.

You can do it, using the Ubuntu LiveCD ... HowToGeek is a nice site, full of usefull information - and, very importantly, like apc, above, plenty of screen-shots !!

[http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/windows-vista/use-ubuntu-live-cd-to-backup-files-from-your-dead-windows-computer/](http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/windows-vista/use-ubuntu-live-cd-to-backup-files-from-your-dead-windows-computer/)

The setting up for dual booting is suprisingly pain-free - a couple of things to note ...

1) Burn the CD at 4X speed MAXIMUM, people have reported grief when burning at faster speeds

2) When you do the Ubuntu install - Don't have the ethernet lead plugged in. It took me 4 installs and much messing around to get my wireless to work - When I did the instal with no ethernet lead in - it worked quite happily !!!!

Welcome to the forums.

Phill.
