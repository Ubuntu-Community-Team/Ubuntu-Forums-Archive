### Post by kinetic-jw on 2009-10-15
9.10=Karmic Koala. It has not come out yet (end of the month it should be out)

8.04LTS (long-term-service) is meant for people who want to not upgrade for 3 years. 10.04 will also be LTS.

You may want to upgrade to 9.04 before 9.10 comes out, as sometimes upgrades over more than one version in one go can cause problems.

After support ends, you can still use your computer. You will just not get security updates.

The versions of ubuntu are number after the year, and then the month. Hence [0]9.04(April). Then they are given a name, like "Hardy Heron" or "Karmic Koala". It just serves as an easy way to remember/refer to that version.

### Post by KhanTG on 2009-10-15
You don't have to upgrade your installation if you are comfortable with it.. but, as inetic-jw stated, eventually the support will go away for them and you will have to fend for yourself when it comes to updates and whatnot. I like to get the latest and greatest iteration because of kernel updates and more hardware support (uh.. keep in mind that newer versions might not include features and drivers that the previous versions, I learned that the hard way-- upgraded from 7.10 to 8.04 to 8.10 and 8.10 didn't like my hardware on one of my machines, but I had much better luck with 9.04)
  Regardless, it's all about your freedom of choice.  You choose what, when and where to make changes to your OS (IF you make changes). Have fun with it!
