### Post by anjilslaire on 2009-02-17
A single layer DVD can actually only hold ~4.377Gigs of data, not 4.7. IT's a matter ogf how they define a mb, 1000kb or 1024kb, extrapolated up into gigs.

You can read morew about single layer media here:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DVD%2BR](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DVD%2BR)

Slightl differences between -R &+R, but same principal. So K3b sees you have more data added than a single layer can hold, and defaults to teh only thing it can burn to hold that volume, a dual layer disc.

### Post by jms1989 on 2009-02-17
Yep, I agree with anjilslaire. A single DVD will only hold a maximum of 4.4GB. That has always been the way I see it. Even if you have one extra MB, you'll need to place it on a double layer DVD. That so-called 4.7GB wis what the manufacture measures it as but the computer measures it in powers of 2 while the manufacture measures it in powers of 10. So they see 1GB as 1000MB and computers see 1GB as 1024MB.

A little math. 4.4GB * 1024MB = 4505.6 and 4.7GB * 1000MB = 4700

Personally, I believe manufactures do that to rip their customers off and confuse people. That's my personal opinion.
