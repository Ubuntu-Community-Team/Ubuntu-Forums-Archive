### Post by BathroomNinja on 2005-12-16
It's been 1 month now since I quit using XP and switched over to Ubuntu.  Since 1998 and RedHat 5.2, I have been using different distributions of Linux/GNU to help satisfy my 'geek need'.  I still remember the thrill of installing each one.  It's somewhat akin to the feeling I got when I was 8 and torn wrapping paper revealed my first 'All in One' electronics kit that allowed me to listen to the radio and police scanners out of something I created.  That feeling of creation filled that geek need for the first time.  

With all of my efforts to make those distributions last, I was always unable to get past my frustrations.  I would always go back to what I knew best; Windows.  I always had a spot in my heart for Windows.  My dad was a programmer, so we always had a PC at home.   When I was younger, my friend Nate had a Mac with it's fancy GUI and I had DOS.  Sure, I had Police Quest and various other games, but he had a GUI!  When Windows 3.11 came out, my dad decided it was 'ready' for his desktop.  I was left to argue with Nate about how Windows was the way of the future and Mac OS was on the way out.  Although I had no real facts to base my Windows enthusiasm on, I was a young fanboy.  It worked, I knew it and I loved it.  I think this was why I hung on to Windows for so long.  

Having worked with a multitude of OS's with my time at Dell and a few dot coms, I came to understand that some OS's work better for some people than others.  Almost any OS can find a way to 'work' for you and your needs, but some work better than others.  For example, you can host a website on Windows XP, but Windows Server makes it easier, and various versions of Linux do it even better.  The best thing about Linux/GNU is choice.  There have always been choices, Linux gives you even more choices.  The problem is that none of these choices worked for me as well as Windows.  

Over the years, my needs have changed.  I now play most of my games on my Xbox (when my wife lets me) and I like to be able to tweak my desktop to my liking.  Windows is very limited on the tweaks.  I was becoming frustrated by simple things like not having multiple desktops and not being able to adjust mouse focus settings.  
    I had tried out Ubuntu 4.10 when it came out, and really enjoyed it.  SuSe had also made it's way on my hard drive but neither was ready for me yet.  I tend to get agitated when people claim that a certain OS is or isn't ready for the desktop.  The real question is not "Is this OS ready for the desktop" but "Is this OS right for me?"  Just like shoes, certain OS's fit certain people better than others.  A size 4 Kangaroo shoe was ready for my foot when I was 6, and even had the pouch feature that I loved.  But that shoe would be way too restricting for me today.  A golfer tends to wear a cleat made for use on a golf course and it works great for him, but a bowler would find that shoe to have the wrong features and instead be a horrible fit.  
    OS's are the same way.  Windows is the right choice for a lot of people.  Mac OS is the right choice for a lot of people.  Right now, Ubuntu is the right choice for me.  I can go into all of it's features, but I won't because you can easily find these features better explained on their site.  

What I will say is that I'm beginning to get frustrated with every file manager I have used.  I have hundreds of folders and sub-folders (or directories if you want to call them that).  I have a system that I feel works well for me.  All of the pictures I took while in Iraq go under MEDIA>IMAGES>PICTURES>MARINES>IRAQ.  All of the videos I took in Iraq go under MEDIA>VIDEO>HOME VIDEO>MARINES>IRAQ.  My diary is filed under DOCUMENTS>PERSONAL>TOM>MARINES>IRAQ.  The problem comes when I'm trying to view all of my stuff from Iraq.  I have to open 3 or more folders to see all of my stuff.  The same thing happens in countless other situations.  Right now, I just deal with it.  I think a better way to handle it would be much in the way Flickr handles pictures with the TAG feature.  Default tags could be assigned based on directory names.  So any images under MEDIA>IMAGES>PICTURES>LIFE>CONCERTS>CHEVELLE, would easily be linked to all of my music under MEDIA>AUDIO>MUSIC>ROCK>CHEVELLE.  A system like this would save countless time and frustration to millions, I'm sure.  

Did I mention that I REALLY like Ubuntu 5.10?

Enough of me.  What fits YOU best?
