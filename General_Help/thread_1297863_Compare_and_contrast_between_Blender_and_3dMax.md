### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-22
Blender isn't very big, but if you need to ask how big a package is, then 3D graphics work is not likely something you'll do much of (you'll run out of space on the first project).

Blender does a lot of the things 3DSMAX does and a few things that it doesn't do.

I haven't seen a feature-by-feature comparison chart, but 3DSMAX is maintained/added-to by about 100 people, while Blender is maintained/added-to by a few thousand people: it doesn't matter what the features are, one to the other, since Blender has, or will, exceed 3DSMAX inevitably.

My son is in the business. He started with Blender but has to use 3DSMAX at work. He likes them both, equally.

I guess it boils down to that $4000 v $0 thing that someone else posted.
