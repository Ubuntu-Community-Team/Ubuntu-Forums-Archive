### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-12-20
I do not understand what you mean by "vertically maximise." I have found that an application will always open the same size it was when it was closed. So, if the application window was the full length of the screen when it was closed, then it would open the full length of the screen but just a few pixels from the top panel. 

I guess that placing the top of the window a few pixels from the top panel is a precaution to prevent the top of the window opening under the top panel. That did happen in some earlier versions of Unity. Once the top of an application window goes under the top panel it becomes difficult to grab the window to move it down again and we lose access to the maximise, minimise and close buttons.
