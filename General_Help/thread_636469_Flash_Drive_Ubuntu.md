### Post by CalonDdraig on 2007-12-10
Hello!

A good pendrive on USB2 (most modern computers) will be a lot faster than running from a live CD as you acctually have swap space on the pendrive. Also its seek time is much better than a CD which is an important consideration.

I've found boot time to be slower than a hard disk but much faster than live CD (again, seek time) 

You wont get much more than 10Mbps read/5Mbps write from a pendrive, but the seek time is the advantage like I said. 

Make sure you use a recent pendrive over USB 2 though... an older pendrive would be worse than CD.

RE: flash drive life, on DSL I've run it almost constantly for almost a year... no sign of deterioration there, but it is more frugal with disk *writes* than most OS out there... Any device including your HDD will have a lifetime measured in writes/reads... fortunately its an astronomically high number, so you should be ok... I dont have experience with how many writes Ubuntu makes... more than DSL for sure, but probably you'll get a good year or so from a pendrive. 

Hope this helps... all my experience on this comes from Damn Small Linux not Ubuntu, but the principles are the same... if you have any more questions, just reply and I'll respond as soon as I can!

CalonDdraig
