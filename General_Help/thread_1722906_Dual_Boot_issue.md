### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-04-07
The "10.10" is the version of Ubuntu, not the kernel.

I'm not at my Ubuntu box, but if I recall correctly, you HAVE the most current kernel version -- it's at the top of the list.

You can remove the old kernel entries, but it's generally a good idea to keep one old version -- in case there are problems with the newest kernel, this gives you the ability to reboot into the older kernel and continue to use your PC.
