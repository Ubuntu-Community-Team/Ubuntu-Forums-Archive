### Post by majagray on 2009-07-17
Hey there, Dub!

I've also since asked on the OOo [community forum]("http://user.services.openoffice.org/en/forum/viewtopic.php?f=7&t=19188&start=0"), and they've been pretty much stuck for what to do too. It does seem to be a problem specifically with a document that was originally saved as a .doc - for whatever reason, these simply will not show their indentations. And once they've been saved once as a doc, they're like that forever - changing them over to, say, an odt, doesn't work. I've tried reinstalling OOo a few times, both in Ubuntu distribution and the official Sun one, and neither changes the issue. 

Also, not every person has the problem - my wife has OOo 3.1 on Xubuntu Jaunty, just like me (although a different type of laptop), and hers works fine. It seems we lucky ones :rolleyes: have to cut our losses with it. 

My advice - from now on, if you're going to need to open or work a file in Open Office, START it in OOo as an odt. M$ Word can handle it.
