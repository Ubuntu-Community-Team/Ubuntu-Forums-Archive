### Post by vanadium on 2009-03-26
Audio CD extractor is a separate application. It will have placed the ripped audio on some place of your hard disk, and you will have to find out where that is. 

When I load Audio CD extractor (Ubuntu 8.04), then Edit - Preferences, I see that for me music will be placed in the "Music" folder. Thus, open a nautilus window, and find your Music folder. You should find the ripped files inside.

You can, as Sidewinder1 suggests, try to "find" the files. By default, your audio will not be in mp3, but in ogg format. You can find all ogg files on your system using nautilus again: Go - Search for files, type "ogg" en press the enter key.
