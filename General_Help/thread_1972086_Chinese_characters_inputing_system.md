### Post by davidvandoren on 2012-05-03
Open Ibus.
An Icon on the top left will appear.
Click on it and select preferences. 
Select the input method tab and add the input method of your choice.
You might need to open Language support first and install Chinese simplified first.

### Post by Brian1215 on 2012-05-03
You might find that ibus doesn't work for you and need to install scimm. I have a new laptop on which I hadn't installed Chinese input. It took me a little while to get it working. Following the guide I linked to it now does after about 2 hours. You don't enter the Chinese characters directly. Once you are set up enter the Pinyin (in Libre Office Ctrl+Space) and you will be offered a selection of characters. One of the things I found in the latest version of Libre Office is the option to change from traditional to simplified Chinese and vice versa. Zhong Guo comes out as &#20013;&#22283; or &#20013;&#22269; without making a total mess of things. There are a few problems when trying to mix both languages in one file but they are manageable. Setup is a bit fiddly but then you didn't learn Chinese in 5 minutes did you? Don't forget to right click on that teeny keyboard icon and don't let it beat you. The setup and use is as simple as using a left-handed corkscrew wearing boxing gloves once you figure it out. (Actually I fell asleep). The chinese characters come from Libre Office and the install.
