### Post by johnc4510 on 2006-05-25
I've found the easiest way to partition is before you install. Go to this web site and download and burn the gparted live cd:[http://gparted.sourceforge.net/livecd.php](http://gparted.sourceforge.net/livecd.php)
Then put it in your cd drive and reboot. You can then partition your drive as needed.       
Then exit and put in the Ubuntu cd and when you get to the partitioning part you should see the partitions you just made. Note: Ubuntu and Linux require a swap partion of at least 512 Mb in addition to the main / partition. I would also recommend a /home partition, but it is not necessary.
