### Post by Herman on 2009-02-18
Where I live power surges and out of spec power occurs fairly often and especially when there are thunderstorms around. It's definitely not safe to run any kind of sophisticated electrical appliance in the Australian bush without at least a surge protector or if possible, a UPS. Of course, if you get a direct hit, even the best UPS won't save your equipment, so sometimes I even unplug and shut down when a storm is very close.

The telephone wire is possibly more attractive to lightning than other wires. I was almost struck by lightning myself once when it hit a telephone not very far from where I was sitting enjoying a conversation with some friends. I unplug my telephone line from my ADSL modem when an electrical storm is close, and I unplug the telephone too.

My brother recently had his phone and all his computer equipment destroyed by a lightning strike, (fried).

Someone told me the a simple overhand knot in the telephone wire can help by turning a lightning strike around in a loop and it will go back out the way it came, but I'm not sure if there's any sound scientific evidence to support that idea or not. It doesn't cost anything or take much time to try it, so I guess it's worth doing. (LOL).


Regards, Herman :D

### Post by handy on 2009-02-18
We have our computer equipment plugged into power-boards that have an insurance policy attached to them, where the company who makes the boards puts an < $25,000- guarantee on anything connected to the power-board not being damaged by a power spike, be it mains or storm centric.

Apparently when something is damaged they pay, so that is good enough for us.

We live in a stormy, rural area, where the power fluctuates due to dairy farmers milking; we have been using these boards for a few years now & have not had a problem, so we are pretty happy about that.

By the way, our systems are completely isolated by these boards, including our internet & LAN hardware.
