### Post by manimal347 on 2008-01-10
Well, you're going to learn something. Playing DVD's in Linux has long been a sordid wink-wink affair, involving buggy programs, programs that don't even support menu's at all (Mplayer), programs that look like death (Ogle), and in every case but a few commercial DVD players probably not one end-user was ever able to buy, generally involve violating both US patent laws and the DMCA (cracking 40 bit CSS encryption!). If you don't live in the US, playing commercially released DVD's is often still impossible to do legally. Now you know why Multiverse and Universe need to be on to get most media players in uncrippled form, and libdvdcss2 is only hosted on the unofficial Medibuntu repository- lawyers and fear of criminal charges. (There was a mass hysteria and lots of legal action when CSS was first cracked.)

I personally like Totem, but VLC is also a very good suggestion. Don't use Kaffeine unless you use Kubuntu, also run the KDE WM, or have already installed other KDE apps. Running both Gnome and KDE backends eats a good chunk of extra memory and represents just one more thing to go wrong.

If you live in the US, your congressional officials want (demand?) you buy a new Ubuntu Dell or an old (ancient?) copy of TurboLinux if you would like to legally play commercial DVD's. Be annoyed at Intervideo and Cyberlink, as they've been making Linux DVD players for about eight years, but will only sell to OEMS like Dell, not you and me.

I suggest you read this:
[http://www.techliberation.com/archives/038757.php](http://www.techliberation.com/archives/038757.php)

And add this after checking the desired packages don't violate your local ordinances....
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu) (link to the guide on adding the  mediubuntu repository)
