### Post by TheFu on 2013-08-16
If you want to send email, you'll need the settings from your ISP and you can use any email client.  Most of the time, output email has to go through a specific ISP server ..... mail.isp.com - this is SMTP.  To read email, you'll use either POP3 or IMAP and those come from an ISP specific server ... pop3.isp.com/net or whatever name server they specify in their how-to documents.

We really cannot help much more. Check your ISP for more information.  I hope you aren't using any of the big free email accounts. [http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/08/13/gmail-privacy_n_3751971.html](http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/08/13/gmail-privacy_n_3751971.html) When you send or receive email with those services, chances are that you've agreed they can do whatever they like with the content.
