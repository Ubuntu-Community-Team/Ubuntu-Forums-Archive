### Post by Futant on 2017-03-21
Do lots of reading (this forum is a good place to start), research each of the problems you're having, attempt to fix them. Be prepared to be more specific in your questions - this community is very helpful but nobody owes you an answer, and the more specific you are the easier it is to help. 

For future projects use the 'try before installing' feature of the live disk/usb to test the operating system on your hardware. Dual booting is an excellent and simple way of having both Windows and a Linux system on one computer.

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2017-03-23
To create a boot-able usb thumb drive or flash-stick, the easiest program is called "Etcher".   It's designed for the newbie in mind.   See the Etcher website for details.   This will solve the problems of other apps that don't work as reliably (startup disk creator and unetboot) or are a bit complex for new users (mkusb).

[https://etcher.io/](https://etcher.io/)
