### Post by cb474 on 2007-02-20
I'm just in the process of switching from Windows to Ubuntu and I have to say I noticed right away, with evince, that it does not render files as well as acrobat reader in Windows.

Doing some comparison, I find that acroread in Ubuntu is also not as good as in Windows, although it's better than evince. In one file I have, evince renders just the simple courier font in a really funky bad looking way.

Also both evince and acroread tend to, at certain zoom levels, render some individual characters in a word larger than others. Acrobat reader in Windows never does this. Moreover, in Windows, even with just text files, the fonts are just a bit more distinct and smooth and significantly darker (than acroread and evince in Ubuntu).

On the other hand, acroread and its plugins in Ubuntu takes up 100Mb (how is that possible for a document viewer?) and is much slower to start.

That's my 2 cents.
