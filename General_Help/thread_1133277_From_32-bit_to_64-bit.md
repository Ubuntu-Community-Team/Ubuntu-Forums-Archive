### Post by ju2wheels on 2009-04-22
Most if not all of your settings will be in your home folder. However, under certain circumstances, it is advisable not to save some of these settings especially if upgrading from one Ubuntu version to another as certain settings may be added/removed and cause issues when you switch. I would only save the settings for those programs which you dont want to reconfigure as opposed to saving absolutely everything in your home folder in terms of settings.

If you dont use your machine for an ssh/web server or other services then all the settings are in your home folder.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-04-23
To be more precise, save EVERYTHING and replace things gradually to check that there's no problems. There usually aren't any problems.

Make sure you back up all the hidden files and folders inside your home directory as these are the ones that contain your settings.
