Hello.

What do you mean by this exactly?  That it shows up as an option in the menu..... OR

that if you log in as her, go to the terminal and do:

```

id

```

that the group 'audio' appears somewhere?

If not, you need to add her to the audio group.

See:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SoundTroubleshooting](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SoundTroubleshooting)

> 
Useff had a very annoying problem where he could get sound through alsa from one user, but not through is main account. [http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1221754](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1221754). He and I managed to fix the problem by making sure the main account was in the audio group in /etc/group (which he was) and deleting the .asoundrc file in the main account's /home directory.

You say you log in first.  When and how does she log in?  You log out and then she logs in locally from the computer itself, right?

Another thing to check might be the levels set under her account using 'alsamixer' or the gnome GUI version which I believe is gnome-alsamixer (not 100% sure, you will likely need to install it using Synaptic).  Especially if she gets no sound or if it is very low.  Sound settings are per user.

Next thing to try that I can think of is to delete '~her_username_here/.pulse' and then reboot. 

To troubleshoot you also might try logging in as her after a fresh reboot just to see if it works, then tell us here.  Every little clue might help someone to fix it. :)
