### Post by P4man on 2009-09-28
Hi,

Im tired of having a widescreen monitor and always being short on height and having "unneeded" width. Just think how often you scroll vertically, and how rarely horizontally. So I figured it would be nice to make use of the available width/

What  I'm writing here is more an idea than a question, but I was thinking it would be nice to have a mix between a dock and a viewport on the right side of the screen where you could "dock" windows. Have 3 or so such "dockports", and you just drag and drop an application window, and it would be scaled down to, say 50% (as with compiz scale plugin) and keep running maximized in a small viewport.

This could be used to monitor all kinds of stuff, from movies, to IM, to bittorrent client, having a console at hand, email, you name it. Drag a window from the dock back to the desktop and it would be restored.

If you maximize another window, it would not cover the dock, it would maximize to the rest of the window (in ~4:3 on a widescreen monitor). Of course you'd add a button to hide the dock.

Ideally you can drag the dockborders to resize them, remove them or split them in to more (like in a spreadsheet).

Attached 2 rough mockup images that illustrate the idea. 

What do you think? Doable? Good idea? Is there something close to this already?
