### Post by Skye on 2006-06-01
I don't have a thinkpad, so I can't answer all of these questions.  But, overall, thinkpads tend to be very well supported in linux (see: [http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/ThinkWiki](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/ThinkWiki) )

I've answered what I can:

1- Skype is slow, in my opinion.  I'm using it from the official skype repositories, and it really isn't quick to respond, and doesn't work very well in general.  I may try the non-deb package sometime soon, though.
2- I don't have a thinkpad, so I don't know, but my Compaq v2402us goes into and out of hibernation well enough (at least it did last time I checked, which was awhile ago.)
3- I think so, but i'm not sure of the name of it.  Considering that playing any DVDs on linux without first paying a liscence fee is basically illegal, I'm sure there's a way around regions- **[legal talk]**but that may be illegal, and hence I do not encourage it**[/legal talk]**
4- I think so.  You will have to read up on the mplayer encoder (mencoder, I think).
5- Gnomebaker works well for me in Dapper, but then again it always had.  Another good burning application is k3b
6- The volume hotkeys, as long as your system recognizes them, should pop up a volume meter on the screen.  It works on my laptop, at least.
7- I think it depends in large part on your video card, and how well it is supported.
8- OO is kind of clumsy in linux, I agree.  I'm pretty partial to abiword myself for all my text-editing needs.
9- You should be able to change the "browness" by either switching to another theme already installed, or downloading and using one from [www.gnome-look.org](www.gnome-look.org)
10- No need to appologize, questions are a good thing! Good luck!
