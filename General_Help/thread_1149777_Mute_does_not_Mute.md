### Post by Balcerzak on 2009-05-05
So, I discovered, to my embarrassment, that while the Mute setting will properly mute things while I have my headphones plugged in, if the headphones are *not* plugged in, the default mute does nothing.

A bit of fiddling showed that when the headphones are not plugged in, in order to gain proper mute functionality, I need to open Volume Properties, and select and mute the PCM column.  Oddly enough, physically adjusting the volume slider of the Master column will actually make the volume increase or decrease.  Only the mute button is impotent.

I did a brief search of the forums, and while I found many "mute" issues of various incarnations, I didn't seem to see any that matched the behaviour I'm experiencing, and was wondering if there was a fix.  While muting the PCM is a work-around, the laptop short-cut key for mute affects Master, apparently, and it's more work to have to open the full menu, than even just right-clicking the icon and selecting "mute".

I'm running Ubuntu 8.10 - the Intrepid Ibex, on a Asus X83VM-X1 laptop.  If any other sort of information is needed, I can try to get it.
