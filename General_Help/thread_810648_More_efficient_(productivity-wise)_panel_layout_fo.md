### Post by Mazza558 on 2008-05-28
In yet another customize-like-crazy afternoon (I was using just the AWN panel an hour ago!), I came up with (in my opinion) a much more productive gnome panel setup. It uses 2 panels like normal, but in different ways. 

- I realised that program windows work better as "desktop tabs", where they're at the top and feel more like tabs than buttons. If you have several maximised programs, it feels a lot more natural. In addition, nearly all the buttons on windows that you may want to use are at the top. You hardly need to move your mouse.

- Having the most-used applets in screen corners makes things a LOT easier. You don't even have to look at the cursor to make sure it reached its destination - you know it has. 

- The bottom panel works very well with the clock in the centre. You keep track of time better.

### Post by Ub1476 on 2008-05-28
Two options:

Two panels

Zero panels

I'm currently using zero panels, in Openbox. I do have Pypanel, but it's very small and trancluent and you don't notice it as much as gnome-panel.

Why?

With two panels your eyes gets focused on the middle of the screen, where they should be. Both panels drag your eyes toward it, and you end up in the middle.

With just a WM, no panels, your eyes is just focused on the window whatsoever.

When I ran at 1024 x 768 I liked having both panels...because I like being able to easily access certain things with 0 (time, date, weather, etc) or 1 click (e.g. places folder).  I left it like the standard ubuntu setup with the addition of some applets (weather, system-monitor, etc) and shortcuts.  My top panel was almost full of stuff. So I guess it's kind of backwards.  The lower your screen resolution, the more panels you need (and thus the less desktop space you have left).  That's my take on it anyway.
