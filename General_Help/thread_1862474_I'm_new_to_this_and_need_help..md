Experience talking here... you really, really should have any data you want to keep in at least 2 locations.  That's pictures, music, documents, bookmarks, all that stuff.  It's not just when you want to make a major system change (like re-jigging the partitions to squeeze in another OS), it's ALL THE TIME.  If there is anything on your computer that you don't want to lose, back it up.  Drives fail, installs fail, people make mistakes.  

People trust computers way, way too much.   That said, I've had very good luck with getting an Ubuntu install to dual-boot with Windows.  Never failed me.  But, I wouldn't even consider it without doing an extra backup just before I started the process.  I've learned my lesson, over and over and over, until it somehow sunk into my thick head.  Backup? Yes.  If you have to ask, it's past time to do it.

### Post by pbpersson on 2011-10-17
You mentioned that you had DLL file problems and you did not know if you should transfer them.

Please note that DLL files are part of what make your applications work in Windows.  You would not transfer them over to Ubuntu since you cannot run Windows applications in Ubuntu......unless you use something like WINE but that is beyond the scope of this text, that is a whole different topic.
