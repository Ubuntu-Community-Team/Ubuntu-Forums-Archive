### Post by HermanAB on 2007-07-24
It most probably won't work at first.  You would most likely need to use a Windows driver with an emulation layer called 'ndiswrapper'.  However, that method works very well and I have been running a Broadcom WiFi card with ndiswrapper and Windoze driver for years.

So, go ahead, but keep a copy of your Windows driver disk ready!
