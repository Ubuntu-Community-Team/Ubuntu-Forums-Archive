### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-01-06
What version of iTunes did you get running with Crossover? Last I checked, they didn't offer anything above 4.9, which the music store no longer accepts connections from, plus, as soon as iTunes 6.0 was released, any account that had connected using 6.0 could no longer use 4.x.

If iTunes 6.0 or 7.0 is somehow running on Linux, (I did get 6.0 running after a fair amount of difficulty), I still wasn't able to connect to the Music store.

Finally, if you do ever manage to connect to the Music store, you still won't be able to update your iPod. Crossover Office has a very lengthy explanation of how you can get this to work, but everyone I've ever talked to failed to get it working.

My advice: give up on the music store. There is software out there that will let you convert your iTunes music to stuff that can be played on Linux (a well formed Google search will make it easy to find). Then use Amarok or gtkPod to manage your iPod. Amarok also has podcast support, though I've not used it myself.

If the iTunes Music Store is an absolute must, Linux isn't an option.
