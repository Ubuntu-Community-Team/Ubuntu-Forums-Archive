### Post by tgalati4 on 2011-01-01
It could be both.  The hard drive pulls a lot of current at start up.  An older drive will have sticky bearings requiring more current to spin up.  It's possible that your power supply is cutting out with the additional current pull at start up.  When the machine is running, any process that causes the CPU to load up can cause a current pull that could cause the power supply to shut down suddenly.

Put a meter on the 12V leads of the HD connector (yellow and black).  If less than 11.5 VDC then it's time to find another power supply.  Back up your data while you can.

Yes, old fans, an overheating CPU or other MB component can cause this sort of thing, along with a bad PSU.

If you hardware is old (> 3 years) you could end up spending a lot of time and money replacing the PSU, CPU, fans, hard disk and then the MB trying to chase down the root cause of the problem, or you could be very (**very!**) luck and fluke it quickly.

It may be time to consider a new system.
