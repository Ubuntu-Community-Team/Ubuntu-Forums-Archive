### Post by gobbles414 on 2008-08-09
What you need is a UPS (Uninterruptible power supply); according to my research, AVR is not the same technology as UPS. A UPS has an internal battery which continuously charges, and is always running. Thus, power interruptions never reach your computer -- or any other devices which are plugged into your UPS. At least in the USA, UPS devices are available almost anywhere where office supplies and/or home electronics are sold.

PS: It probably won't cost much more than a UPS to just get your room's light switch repaired. :)

PPS: I recommend that you backup your data and then erase you current Ubuntu install with a utility like [GWSCAN]("http://support.gateway.com/support/drivers/search.asp?param=gwscan&st=kw") -- which will also make sure that your hard drive has not been permanently damaged by these repeated power failures. Then reinstall Ubuntu.

### Post by zxscooby on 2008-08-09
sounds like you got a poor connection in your switch and it is causing arcing across the blades as the switch makes contact or shorting to ground for an instant and drawing power away from the circuit.
It is also possible there is a problem in the light fixture itself or the wires going to it. I would have that looked at because it could be a real fire/explosion hazard.
