### Post by oakz on 2006-01-13
I have been using OO for powerpoint and word document editing at my employer where most people use Windows for a couple years now. It *mostly* works, I still run across an occasional document that won't open or the formatting is messed up, but 95% of the time it looks fine. Be sure to save in the Microsoft native format, not the OO fomat if you want Office apps to be able to read the docs...

### Post by timczer on 2006-01-13
I agree, I have been using openoffice for the past year or so to handle documents while everyone else in my company uses windows.  For the most part it is seemless moving back and forth between the two, just use microsoft formats.  I do have some issue that when I save an excel document with openoffice and send to someone, they get an unknown extension and can't open the file (probably happend 5 or 6 times out of a couple of hundred documents).  Also formatting and how macro's are handled sometimes are a little different (you can hit space in a cell to remove a number in openoffice, and if it is in a line of summed cells, the total will still be correct.  Open this file in excel and the formula gives an error because in excel you have to delete the number from the cell.  I frustrated one of our office assistants for a couple of weeks with this until I realized that what I saw in the document wasn't what she was seeing)

I justed started doing some work with impress and powerpoint and opening back and forth, and it seems to be ok so far.
