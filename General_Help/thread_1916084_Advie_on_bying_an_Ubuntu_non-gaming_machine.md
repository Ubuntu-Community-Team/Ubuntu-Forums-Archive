### Post by fordman1090 on 2012-01-27
Iv had good luck with corsair power supplies. Iv had a GS600 that worked great for an all around machine and my current rig has a TX850 that seems to keep up with what im running without trouble.

Case wise, you really will get what you pay for. The cheap ones often rattle, the little plastic clips will break, the included fans are cheap and noisy, they are small and a tight squeeze if you have a full atx board. I got an antec P280 case that i love so far. Its not a gaming case with the clear side and all the extra lights and fans. Its a simple case, lots of good features, plenty of space and mounting options, very quiet!!!!

SSD's are the wave of the future. Im gonna be getting at least one here before long. But they are a little pricey and lack capacity, but a cheaper HHD can fill in for storage. If you wanna stick completely to HHD, i would look at a Western Digital Caviar Black. WD has been great to me, I have several of there drives that run in server applications and have never had an issue. Well, i had one issue, but that was kinda my fault, however they RMA'd it immediately.

Unless you plan on burning and ripping blurays all day you should be fine with a rather inexpensive dvd drive.

Good luck with the build. My current rig is a monster and i built it for about the same price that you would pay for a mid-range consumer box. Custom built is the way to go if you have the ability, you can set it up for exactly what you want and you can make it easily upgradable for the future technology.
