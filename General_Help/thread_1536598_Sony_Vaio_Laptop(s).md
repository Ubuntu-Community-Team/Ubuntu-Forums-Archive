### Post by Quackers on 2010-07-30
Vaios are different to other laptops. They use SNC (Sony Notebook Control, aka Sony5001, aka Sony6001, aka Sonypi) which has a big say in how hardware is handled. Vaios don't communicate with their screens like other laptops, due to this program, and it can cause problems. SNC does many other things too. To be honest, it is a miracle that any graphics drivers work at all on a Vaio in Ubuntu. The Mac OS X hackers haven't solved it yet (except on the odd Vaio that has onboard graphics as well as a separate graphics card). In fact I would like to know how  my driver works because it doesn't in Mac.
