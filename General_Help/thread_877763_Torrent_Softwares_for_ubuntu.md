### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-08-03
I recommend Deluge.  It's pretty similar to uTorrent (for Windows), if you're familiar with that.  Transmission is a bit too basic for me, and some of the others I've tried (particularly Azureus) were a bit slow or buggy.  Deluge has plenty of features (such as supporting encryption, DHT, and UPnP, as well as having a plugin system to allow you to add other features like scheduling or creating torrents) and has a fairly straightforward interface.

### Post by crhylove on 2008-08-03
I want to second the vote for transmission.  It isn't as fast as uTorrent (which I run in an XP VM (along with Civ 2 and Cool Edit Pro 2.1)), but it "just works" and is dead simple.  Also, it comes pre-installed, and that's kinda nice.  I DO NOT RECOMMEND Azureus.  I can't really see a benefit of ktorrent or halite or deluge over transmission.  They all seem adequate but similar, and are all still mildly inferior to uTorrent, which is kind of the "gold standard" torrent app if you ask me.

Besides torrents, I also run Shareaza in my VM.  It's nice for quick searches for one particular item that often won't show up as a torrent.  Be careful however.  You can hose your VM with viruses easily on any gnutella/edonkey client, which Shareaza is both!

Best of Luck!
