### Post by superlan on 2007-12-03
NComputing does NOT support LTSP and it for a really simple reason which is as follows;

LTSP was designed for old (and new) PC's to be used to connect to each other on a network. The PC's could be dumb terminals or have a hard drive.

NComputings Philosophy is to drive down the unnecessary purchase of new PC's for every user as well as the unnecessary use of old PC's due to the high level of maintenance they require (we have seen plenty of old PC's been dumped into Africa with countless problems). The NComputing terminals have several advantages over PC's as terminals such as power consumption, heat, noise, space and cost. The biggest bonus is that the terminals dont require any maintenance and can be configured and managed remotely. The Protocol that is used in these terminals is also more efficient than LTSP thus reducing bandwidth and increasing speed.

LTSP has its place but so does NComputing and both should be used in the correct instance. If you have old PC's then use LTSP but if you are wanting to deploy a new network of users you cannot beat NComputings value.
