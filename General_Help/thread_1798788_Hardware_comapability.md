### Post by seawolf167 on 2011-07-07
> **busigast said:**
> 
4 DDR DIMM slots for dual-channel DDR400 SDRAM

Match your RAM speed. Check your open slots to determine if you are going to replace your current RAM or simply add more DIMMs. An example RAM search for you is [here]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=100007611%20600006085&IsNodeId=1&name=DDR%20400%20%28PC%203200%29"). If you can, find the timings of your current RAM (either through BIOS if available or your system specs sheet) and try to match that (not an issue if you get all new DIMMs).
