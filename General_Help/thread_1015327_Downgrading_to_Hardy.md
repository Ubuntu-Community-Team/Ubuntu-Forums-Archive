### Post by clegends on 2008-12-21
Yeah, that's how I'd do it. Actually, I find Intrepid runs really fast on my machine, much faster than Hardy. This might be I've put less on it, but seems built better. I've got 3Mhz processor with hyperthreading, 512 Mb, Pentium 4 processor, custom built machine. Anyway, I didn't update from Hardy, I backed up and reinstalled. It may be your update didn't go smoothly. Unless there's a problem (like cups for instance) you can't fix otherwise, try reinstalling and going with Intrepid. And please ignore anyone who posts saying you don't reinstall things in Linux, that's utter rot. It's a much better system than MS Windows, and requires a different headset, but sometimes a good 'ol backup and blowup is by far the best option. good luck.
