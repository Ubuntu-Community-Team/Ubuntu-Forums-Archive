### Post by NoNo_231 on 2006-12-11
If you use texmaker or kile there are some wizards give some standards like the one you want. However most of the unis have their own templates so I don't believe that could help. There is a little script, which converts OO to .tex files. This is used at the export button of openoffice with good enough results. This is also a good way to start. Finally what ever you want to do (e.g. tables, trees, etc) give at google latex + something and you get really good results. 

If you use some front-end like kile or texmaker it will help you much. There is also a gedit plugin you could try ([http://live.gnome.org/Gedit/LaTeXPlugin](http://live.gnome.org/Gedit/LaTeXPlugin)).

Finally I would suggest to install texlive as backend because it has more packages and you can do more and not having to install by yourself many of them.

I hope I helped.
