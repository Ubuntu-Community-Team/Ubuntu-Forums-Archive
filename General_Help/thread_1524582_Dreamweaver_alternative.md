### Post by Achillobator on 2010-07-05
Hm... I don't use WYSIWYG authoring programs often myself, though I have played around with Kompozer once or twice. Unless I'm mistaken, yes, it probably is about as close to Dreamweaver as you're going to get free and open-source; the interface is pretty dissimilar to Dreamweaver's if I'm remembering them both correctly, and Kompozer is much, much lighter-weight (though there might be some plugins that could extend it to something approaching Dreamweaver, I wouldn't know), but if it's WYSIWYG you're looking for then it will get the job done. [Here's the website's feature list;]("http://www.kompozer.net/features.php") check it out and see if it has what you need.

There's also [Amaya]("http://www.w3.org/Amaya/"), which is actually made by the W3C. I imagine it's also very light-weight and probably not even of the same caliber as Kompozer, but since it's the W3C's own creation then at least you can be certain that what you create with it is valid, if potentially simple.

Those are just the first two hopefully-reliable ones that came to mind; I'm sure a Google search or a quick jaunt through the repositories would turn up at least a few more (possibly including some that a free but not open source, if you're willing to go that way), but I'm not aware of any that are as widely recommended as the above so they're probably your best bets.

Perhaps if you specified exactly which of Dreamweaver's features you need, I might be able to think of something a little more suitable for you. (Alternatively, do you already have Dreamweaver? Which version? The older ones, for example the Macromedia versions, might be able to run on [WINE]("http://winehq.org").) In the meantime, give Kompozer and Amaya a try. Hope this helps.

EDIT: Haha, took me a while to find the links and Silver_fox_ got there before me. So I guess I'm just seconding his/her recommendations, then. :) Good luck finding what you need.
