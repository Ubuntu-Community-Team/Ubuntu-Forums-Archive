### Post by fooman on 2009-02-27
if you left click (and hold) on the top panel,  you can drag it to the bottom.   once it is there,  you can right click on the bottom panel and choose "delete this panel" to get rid of the bottom,  leaving you only the top panel,  now in the bottom position.  then you can right click on that panel and choose "add to panel",  then add the missing items that were on the old bottom panel, like trash, etc...

hope that helps.

### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-27
In my experience, Gnome is quite a bit more configurable than KDE, or at least more easily configurable.  With a little bit of research and tweaking, I don't think you'll find much disappointment.

You can edit a lot of settings about Gnome and its applications (including Nautilus) with gconf-editor.  You may have to install it with Synaptic.  And while you're installing things, you might want to have a look at nautilus-actions, nautilus-gksu, nautilus-open-terminal, and nautilus-wallpaper, to add some functionality to Nautilus.  Nautilus Actions, in particular, is pretty cool, since you can add any command you want to the context menu.

And you *do* know you can mix and match Gnome and KDE applications, right?  If you want to use Konqueror or Dolphin as your file manager in Gnome, you can.  I personally use KDE and Amarok instead of Brasero and Rhythmbox.

Oh... and I just noticed you said you're using Hardy.  You might want to upgrade to Intrepid, since it has quite a few features missing in Hardy -- such as the ability you mentioned to right-click on tabs in Nautilus, as well as the ability to restore items from the Trash.
