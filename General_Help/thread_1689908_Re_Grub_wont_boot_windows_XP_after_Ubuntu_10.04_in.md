### Post by coffeecat on 2011-02-17
If you can get hold of a Windows installation DVD, boot with that and go to the command prompt. I think you need the 'bootrec /FixBoot' option, but have a look here:

[http://support.microsoft.com/kb/927392](http://support.microsoft.com/kb/927392)

Don't use the 'bootrec /FixMbr option because that will overwrite grub stage 1 in the mbr and you will have to repair grub before you can boot into Ubuntu again. That is also why I suggest you don't use the startup repair option in the recovery environment because that might  do both a fixboot and fixmbr and a fixmbr is what you don't want.

If that repairs Windows' booting I suggest the first thing you do once you get into the Windows 7 desktop is to make a repair disc. It's a useful thing to have. :wink:
