### Post by freerick on 2007-03-06
Ok, this is really strange, but no matter what I do, my X server refuses to set its resolution above 1280x1024. I have an NVIDIA 6200 running on Edgy 6.10 and I'm using KDE. I installed the NVIDIA drives from the repo as described in the HOWTO and I got them to work fine. The NVIDIA control panel comes up in my display settings and it correctly changes my settings, etc.

**The only thing X doesn't do is set my resolution above 1280x1024. All other resolutions work fine.**

My monitor's native resolution is 1600x1200, so, needless to say, I've been running it at that resolution with that video card for a while with different operating systems. For some reason, I can't get to to switch to the right resolution in Ubuntu.

I gave up on the control panel and started messing with the xorg.conf file by hand. I commented out all mode lines except the one for the resolution that I need, and in my display subsection, that is the only resolution listed.

Whenever I start the X server, it chooses the next-lower specified resolution. Why!! It's driving me insane!! :confused:

### Post by hikaricore on 2007-03-06
Post the contents of /etc/X11/xorg.conf


personally I had to add the lines:

> Option         "UseEDID" "False"
Option         "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"

to my _Section "Screen"_ to get my resolutions working  
*(Depending on your driver version, you may need to put those lines in Section "Device" where you video driver is located instead.  I had issues with this when I upgraded my kernel which is why they now reside in the Screen section.).*

I believe the UseEDID option is the important one, the other has to do with rendering options.

Let me know if this help.

--Aaron

### Post by hikaricore on 2007-03-06
I don't see anything wrong with your xorg.conf file, I would check to see if the ranges for your Samsung SyncMaster are correct, but I suspect they are.  One thing I did notice is that you're using the stock nvidia drivers right now.  These are the open source drivers which come default in some distributions of linux.  You may want to install the proprietary drivers and see if this resolves the issue.  Your video card is supported by these drivers and I expect you'll have better performance from them.  They aren't supported by the ubuntu community as they are not open source, but if you want the best, they're availible.  AKA if you plan on any gaming or highend graphical use, they're probably your savior.

I suggest Alberto Milone's Envy script:
[http://albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html](http://albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html)
