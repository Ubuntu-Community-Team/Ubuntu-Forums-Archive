### Post by mssever on 2008-01-27
Firefox 2.0 has some session management, but the session management features included in the Tab Mix Plus extension do exactly what you mentioned. Tab Mix Plus even preserves the back and forward buttons. It's available from the Firefox extentions website.

### Post by ryanVickers on 2008-01-27
There is an option in Firefox to open to your previous tabs and pages, instead ob a home page.  Also, in Ubuntu under Sessions (I believe), there is an option to restore current sessions by re-opening your apps.  This does not work very well however, so I would recommend just changing Firefox and then opening it when need be.

### Post by aysiu on 2008-01-27
Well, if Tab Mix Plus doesn't work out for you, you could try the Bookmarks > Bookmark All Tabs option, which will put all your currently open pages into a new folder in your bookmarks. The only downside to this is that you may want to later delete that set of bookmarks.
