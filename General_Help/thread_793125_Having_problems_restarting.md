### Post by Patb on 2008-05-20
> **brett11808 said:**
> I don't know how to check the cmos battery???
The guides etc for laptops are often somewhere on the web:
[http://h10032.www1.hp.com/ctg/Manual/c00312379.pdf](http://h10032.www1.hp.com/ctg/Manual/c00312379.pdf)

See page 5-45.  But if the laptop is fairly new, the cmos battery is not likely to be dead.  And if it were, the beeping would probably be immediate and consistent.  

Also, particularly for a laptop, it sounds more like it could possibly be CPU overheating or something related.  That would explain it working after leaving overnight or waiting a couple of minutes.  From your description, I'm not sure whether it locks up before it has time to start loading the operating system.  It could be something to do with the system's power management but that's way out of my field, sorry.  You might like to install something like lm-sensors and see what you find for CPU temperature.  

Cheers, Pat.
