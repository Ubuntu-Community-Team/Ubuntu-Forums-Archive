### Post by hikaricore on 2007-02-01
I had a similar issue with a generic mouse for the longest time, on both dapper and edgy.  I never found the cause of the issue, but simply replacing the mouse with an old HP mouse I had lying around solved it.  My guess would be that one of the "eye beams" near the gears that roll with the mouse ball is flaking out.  I found mine gathering alot of dust and even small hairs, so it could be as simple as taking your mouse apart and cleaning it very carefully.  Oddly in windows this mouse worked fine on 4 different computers that I tried over several days.  Otherwise it's just a random glitch with the hardware or software mouse driver used by X.  Like I said I replaced my mouse and it solved the issue, so I didn't do any further investigation.
