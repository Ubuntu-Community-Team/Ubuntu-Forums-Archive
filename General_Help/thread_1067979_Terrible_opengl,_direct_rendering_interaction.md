### Post by Thelasko on 2009-02-13
> **MattPhillips said:**
> I'm using the i810 driver (according to xorg.conf)

That's your problem right there.  Your video driver should not be specified in your xorg.conf file.  Specifying the driver hasn't been done since 7.04 if I recall correctly.

Make sure you have the xserver-xorg-video-intel package installed, then you may want to try changing the driver from "i810" to "intel".

If that doesn't work, or breaks your setup, roll back to an older version of xorg.conf or just let the recovery mode create a new one for you.  As I understand it, if you have both monitors plugged in while in recovery mode, it should configure them both.

i810 is a very old driver, and does not utilize all of the capabilities of your video card.

### Post by Thelasko on 2009-02-20
Don't add that.  Your virtual screen size cannot exceed 2048 in any direction, or you will lose direct rendering. Therefore adding the lines in bold ```
Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Monitor		"Configured Monitor"
	Device		"Configured Video Device"
       [b]SubSection "Display"
           Virtual              2048 2048 
       EndSubSection[/b]
EndSection
```
If you think you can use a smaller virtual screen, do so, as it will improve your video card's performance.

P.S. If you are not already aware, lines of xorg.conf that begin with "#" are ignored.
