### Post by kleo skywalker on 2008-03-04
Griffith seems pretty alright, but it lacks one important feature that I like in Alexandria.
In Alexandria, you can mark each entered book as one or more of the following: **Own it**, **Read it**, **Want it**. So it's possible to enter and correctly mark a book that you don't own, but have read. There's also an option to see books for each "tag".
Griffith assumes you own the film, and you can only choose to mark it as **Seen**.

### Post by LaneKT on 2008-03-20
I've used a couple different apps. in the past as well as created my own in excel.  

Right now I am using [http://www.moviecollectorplus.com](http://www.moviecollectorplus.com)

It is easy to add your movies and all the details are stored with it, including box shots.
You can sort your collection, as well as print or export.

What made this the best choice for me was the ease of adding new releases (they're always updated and on the home page) b/c it saves me a lot of time.  But, the real cincher was that you can create a network of friends and they can log in to see your collection, too.  You can compare collections with your friends, request to borrow movies through collections and there is a loan tracker as well.

It's pretty cool... and FREE :)  Check it out... hope it helps.
