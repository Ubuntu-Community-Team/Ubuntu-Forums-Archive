### Post by filburt1 on 2010-08-06
I have Ubuntu Desktop x64 10.04 on a Dell Optiplex 980. It has two Radeon HD 4550 cards: one of them has a single DVI monitor connected, the other has two connected (one by a DisplayPort adapter). All three monitors are identical (Dell 2007FPs).

In Windows 7 x64, I can have all three monitors working properly, including with Aero. In Ubuntu, I'm having major problems. With a default installation, I can get two monitors (i.e., the card with two monitors connected) with the third in power save. Compiz works using this stock driver and the system behaves perfectly normally. However, the Monitors preference window only sees two monitors.

With the Catalyst driver from ATI's site, one of several things happens:
[list]
[*]If I try aticonfig --initial -f, all three monitors initially work at the login screen, and I can log in. If I move the mouse to the edge of the monitor, the system completely locks up (i.e., even Num Lock won't work) and I need to reset it. The only fix is to delete /etc/X11/xorg.conf. It also hard locks up after a few seconds regardless of what I do, including a failsafe GNOME session.
[*]With other configurations, and I can't track down specifically when, the *other* card--the one with just one monitor connected--works, and the remaining two monitors just show what was last on them, which is usually the Ubuntu bootup animation frozen. The working display is incredibly slow and obviously not running Compiz.
[*]If I start the Catalyst control center, it only sees one of the cards. It shows another display as "Unknown adapter" and only thinks it can run at 640x480, and enabling it does nothing. However, the command-line tools recognize that there are two cards, as does lspci.
[/list]
So, any ideas? I'm willing to sacrifice Compiz and maybe even allow two X servers if necessary, but I just can't get all three monitors to work.
