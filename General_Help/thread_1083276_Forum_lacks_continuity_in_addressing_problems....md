Hello,

As a newcomer I have been using or rather attempting to use the Ubuntu forums as an aid in resolving startup issues with Linux. There seems to be many people with suggestions and comments as to what may be causing a problem. But what I have encountered is no continuity in solving a particular problem. I cannot be the first person who has grown frustrated waiting on a reply to a post. Sometimes there are none.

To me the effectiveness of this forum would be substantially improved if some sort of monitoring and assignment was used to ensure people were getting resolution to their problems.

Perhaps a Live Chat feature would be more beneficial. I have used this concept on other websites with success.

If I don't continually repost my problem to keep it at the top of the forum "food chain" it seems to get lost and forgotten. 

If someone is going to help someone else out then be prepared to stay in contact with them until they reach a resolution or determination.

I appreciate what Ubuntu Forums is doing but I believe many newcomers such as myself become disheartened when they have to repeat their problem over and over and not make any headway toward a resolution.

Frustrated,

### Post by Gramps on 2009-02-28
Sorry you are not getting the responses that you want. Please remember that all the people here are volunteers.

You might try using the Ubuntu Chat Channel to talk to live people that maybe able to help you faster. [http://www.ubuntu.com/support/community/chatirc](http://www.ubuntu.com/support/community/chatirc) 

I don't know what problem you are having but try to give as much information as possible. Describe the problem the best you can. When leaving information about the problem and take a minute and ask yourself what information would I need to try and solve this problem. As a rule to much information is better than not enoough. 

A brief description in the title is better than "I'm new need help" this will help attract the person that can help with your problem.

Alot of times you can help yourself by searching the forums for your problem maybe it has been solved already. Also I have pretty good luck using Google when searching for a solution and adding Ubuntu to the search criteria.

Good luck...
