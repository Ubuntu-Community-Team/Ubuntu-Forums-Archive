### Post by cascade9 on 2011-02-27
I wouldnt buy a media player from a lot of the companies making them because they have poor linux support. Some of them *cough* apple *cough* seem to do the best they can to make the media player incompatible IMO.  I'd much rather give my money to people who do have linux support. 

That said, any player with MSC mode should be usable with linux. 

Cowon and Sansa are probably the best for linux support with smaller media players. The also tend to have support for codecs that the others dont, like flac and ogg. 

The problem is that they tend to only come in smaller sizes because they use flash memory, not a HDD. Some models have microSD slots, and microSD is cheap these days. It would be easy to carry a couple of hundred GB in size smaller than a CD case.  

But if you really want big storage space, video capability, and more GUI functions, maybe a 'pure' media player isnt what you want? I'd seriously think about  a android/ARM tablet. Maybe an Archos?

### Post by patjennings on 2011-03-07
well i got the archos, first impressions are good. I had to register and do a firmware update when i first got it, registration always annoys me but it only took 5 - 10 minutes all said. After that I plugged it in to banshee to see if it would recognize it. Success the archos was recognized by banshee, when trying to sync my music library it started off working but would crash 4% of the way through and close banshee on me. I realized I probably had some drms files mixed in with my music and that was what was making it crash, that or just the shear amount of files in different formats that needed conversion. My computer probably isn't powerful enough to do it all at once. So I have been managing my music manually 100 songs at a time and that seems to be working. Once everything is converted and the drms files have been segregated I will try the sync option again to see how well it works. Some of the other options like the web browsing (some flash working some not) and web radio are good. Some of the cons are you have to pay extra for some things like the ability to play other formats and gps etc. videos have to be converted, more info at [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=822407&highlight=archos+video](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=822407&highlight=archos+video)

After some time using the archos, it is usable but not recommended, there are some major design flaws in my opinion, first of all   it is sync-able with banshee witch is good, but there is a limit of 5000 files on the hard drive which is very bad, (I have way more than 5000 song in banshee). So when you add more than that amount, the songs are in the archos music folder but not viewable or playable on the actual UI of the pmp. To get around this you can manualy create folders within the archos hard drive, I did this alphabetically, at first I was fine with this but when trying to shuffle the songs you are only able to shuffle within the individual folders themselves, shuffling songs with bands that all start with the letter A gets real old after a while. Say what you will about apples business practices( and I will probably agree) but at least they make a decent UI. Not like this windows media player designed b.s. All that said it is actually a really nice device I would have just designed the user interface a little differently if it was up to me.
