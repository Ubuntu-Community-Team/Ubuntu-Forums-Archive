### Post by bixin on 2006-02-17
I am no expert but I can maybe point you to the right search. On my wifi connection(broadcom), I had to use ndiswrapper. There is plenty of info on it and there is even a wiki page for the program. In addition to this you will need to find the driver for your card. If you use ndiswrapper then you will need your original driver off the disk. But still look around the net for the a linux equivalent  it might have support already. Try here 

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WiFiDocs](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WiFiDocs)  

They have diffrent walkthroughs for wifi cards yours might be there and fyi some are listed by driver name.  Hope it helps.
