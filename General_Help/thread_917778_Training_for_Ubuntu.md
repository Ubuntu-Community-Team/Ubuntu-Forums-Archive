### Post by dlublink on 2008-09-12
Hello,

I was talking with one of the guys at my work and we determined that the best way to get a converted user to stay on Ubuntu is to give a mini training. The idea is to install Ubuntu for the user and spend a few short lessons with them on how it works.

Of course some users can figure it out on their own, but might get frustrated too quickly and switch back to their former operating system.

The short training would give the users the tools they need in order to not reach the frustration needed to switch.

I know there are forums and lots of online documentation, but I am discovering a lot of users won't read this. 

Is there any condensed documentation that can easily be printed and used as a guide for the above mentioned training?

Ideas : 
Lesson 1 : a tour of the menus, different softwares, how to change background + screen saver
Lesson 2 : How to get help and documentation and explanation of the word "free" when used to speak of free software.
Lesson 3 : a brief overview of OpenOffice and some of the differences from MS office ( such as page layout )
Lession 4 : A brief overview of gimp, explaining concepts such as layering and the magic selector and pidgin
Lesson 5 : Overview of using external devices such as digital cameras, mp3 players and others

What do you think?

Thanks,

David
