### Post by Hollyecho_Montgomery on 2011-03-23
Most do not consider Incredimail malware.  I have been using Incredimail since the late 1990's.  I do understand that Linux fundamentalists do like anything that even closely resembles html in email formatting.  But, I like it.  Its easy, pretty, makes on the fly stationeries, to throw more pretties out.  Backgrounds with sound and color (yes I like digital cameras over 35mm, and love all my pictures to be in color).  and yet I have been a computer tech (professionally) since 1986. (when BBS had color wildcat ansii I WAS THERE!!!)   

Uneducated people consider the free version of Incredimail to be "SpyWare" for it adds random ads on the end of the sent messages, the pro version does not.  The ads are randomly generated.  

But, my mission is to find all the things my CUSTOMERS want in a computer, without the viruses of Windows, I have several I said I would get them on Linux.  I have settled on Kubuntu, and trying to learn as much as possible, to make the things my customers want on their computer to work as they are familiar, without having to come out every week to learn more code to add or fix something to their computer, I simply want it to work for them, so I can have more to do.

Comments like "Just Settle" for something doesn't work.  I got here to this forum today for there were several (false as I see now) leads that said Incredimail for Linux.   

I have a lot more to learn (tons and tons) for I have only repaired/fixed/cleaned/upgraded/built PC's, so you might see a lot of what most consider "dumb" questions.

What I find most frustrating is how alot of helpful comments forget that most the people looking/browsing for answers don't know where the xconfig. file is or how to edit it, even when you put what to edit in it, doesn't help when you don't know exactly how to get there, or that most of us would rather not do it in terminal, for that is why we are going to a GUI interface to get away from that.

Sorry for the rant.  But, Incredimail is NOT malware, it is just fun and easy. (to use, to backup and restore is a slight trick, but like in Kubuntu not when you know how)
