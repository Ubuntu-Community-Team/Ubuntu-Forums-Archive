### Post by kaydeejay on 2018-05-23

But in firefox on windows, if I entered a password into a website that firefox didn't recognize, a little window would pop up asking me if I wanted to save the username and password for that site. And, conveniently, it would stay up even after the login was successful (or not) so I would know whether or not to save it. If I'm very confident in the password and quick with my mouse, I can save the password before I've successfully logged in and then the password will be saved. ### Post by Quarkrad on 2018-05-24
You could check to see if firefox has saved the password.  Go **Edit - Preferences** and then click on **Privacy and Security**.  Make sure there is a tick in the box **Remember logins and passwords for websites**. Then, to the right on that box click on **Saved Logins**. When the Saved Logins window pops up type in the part of the website address and see if your username has been saved for that site - you can see if your password is saved by clicking on the** Show Passwords** box.  If you do not have File, Edit, View, etc at the top of your firefox screen you need to go into Customization (accessed by clicking on the 3 horizontal lines on the right hand side at the top of the screen).  At the bottom of the customization screen click on **Toolbars** and put a tick in the **menu bar** box.
