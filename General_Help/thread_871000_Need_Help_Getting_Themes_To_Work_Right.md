Getting a desktop PC themed is not one thing or "program" that you install to get a look that you desire, it has **many** elements.

You asked for advice and I gave you the link to the website that 99.9% of the customized desktops you see on the web downloaded their themes from.

(gnome-look.org)

You have to search the seemingly endless files available at gnome_look.org until you finally come up with a look that you and **only you** are happy with.

Since the Beryl days I have changed my desktop theme numerous times only to have it get better and better each time.

My desktop theme is now unified, meaning that the desktop theme, Firefox theme, icon theme's and mouse pointer theme all go together.

Even the default font in Ubuntu AND Firefox match so that if anyone looks at my machine it looks professional and put together, not various themes slapped on just because.

I look at my laptop daily as I use it for work and play. The color scheme works for me and I find it pleasing to my eyes since I look at it for close to 14 hours a day sometimes, but 8-10 hours daily normally.

Go look at the resources that I gave you links to. Install some stuff and play around with the settings. It was blatantly obvious that you weren't even pushing buttons on your own machine. **It's YOUR machine**. Look around, push some buttons, change something. **[COLOR="Blue"]Experiment![/COLOR]**

The PC police aren't going to bust into your home and arrest you if the default colors aren't on your Ubuntu desktop.

This is the beauty of Linux. **Endless customization**.

Many of us in the quest of a custom desktop failed and the only way home was to reinstall and start over. That's OK. Do it, you may learn something in the process.

Right click the panel on the top and mess with the settings, make it transparent or change the color.

Now do the bottom one!

Now mess with the system colors!

Go to gnome-look.org and find something wild and install it!

Look at art.gnome.com and find something cool there that you can use.

When you change one custom thing in your themes, you can save each theme with a different name. Even if it looks the same but has a different mouse cursor, it is different.

I have dark themes for working in the dark or in an airplane and light themes when I'm at home or the office.

I have a theme for when my grandson comes to sit in my lap.

So, you see, what you should have discovered on your own, now you know.
