### Post by blazemore on 2009-11-20
Is there a bit of software that will take my (many) Google Reader feeds, and sit in my taskbar, checking every minute or so, and pop up when it gets a feed?

I know this seems like a stupid question, but bear with me.
Most desktop RSS readers don't have Reader integration. I know Google let you export your feeds into one RSS feed, but this doesn't inform the desktop reader of the origin of the feed: as far as the desktop reader's concerned, all the updates are coming from the same place!

Also, when I read a feed locally I'd like it to be marked as read on Google Reader as well.

I'd even accept Firefox extensions, although a nice native notification app would be better :-)

What do you use for feeds? How would you improve it?

### Post by KenJean on 2009-11-24
For the record, there WAS the perfect app for this very thing. It was included in a programme called gtk-desktop-info written by kaivalagi.

gtk-desktop-info is a python-based conky variant that can display information directly on the desktop. The Google Reader module was very sweet: You could set the update interval, fonts, size of display, etc.

It worked like a dream ... until Google changed their log-in.

So I've been searching this forum regularly to see whether someone has updated the log-in script for this.

Sorry that this is not helpful, but at least it's good to know there are kindred spirits out there.
