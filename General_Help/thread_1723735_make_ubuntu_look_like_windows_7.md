### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-04-07
> **branko.savic said:**
> I have seen a script that can change the apparence of ubuntu so it can look like a mac OsX, but is there something similar to make it look like windows 7?

A while back, I played around with themes and emerald to see what could be done to make Ubuntu take on the appearance of Vista.  Did it more out of curiosity than anything else.

So, based on my experience, there are really two very different answers to your question ...

First, by going to Gnome-look, downloading themes, installing emerald, and messing around a lot with the general appearance, you can force Ubuntu to take on some of the appearance elements of Windows 7 (or Vista, or Mac OSX).  However, there are elements of the integrated Aero-based graphical interface built into Win7 that either can't be replicated in Ubuntu, or if they can, take an enormous amount of work.  So, at the end of the day, you can't replicate every visual element and feature of Windows 7 in Ubuntu.

But second, and maybe more important, you can't make it WORK like Windows 7 (and some would say this is a good thing). And, experience and testing in such situations has shown that, in terms of human-computer interfaces, the more you make two different interfaces look the same, the more confusing the activities become.

So, basically, if someone wants a PC desktop experience that looks exactly like Windows 7 (all the elements, all the features), the only way to really do that is to switch over to Windows 7.
