Hi, Mint or Ubuntu. If Ubuntu, use 10.04, the Long Term Support version. Thats what it is for and is guaranteed to be the most tested and stable. ### Post by Ghost|BTFH on 2011-05-10
As a computer tech and support specialist, might I suggest that the first thing you do is introduce him to the OS and allow him to try it out for a day or two and see if he can utilize the other programs.

Let him onto your or your mother's computer, see if you can find a reasonable alternative for the software he uses.  Also, you should check with the person in his office who does the real work for him, and find out if THEY can handle it.  If your dad's just fine with it, that doesn't mean the person stuck in the office who has no clue what Libre Office is, will be able to just fire it up and run it.

ALWAYS ALWAYS ALWAYS put a casual user into the kiddy pool of Linux first (and yes, I agree with a previous post - if you're providing tech support it BETTER be the same product as your own, otherwise, YOU are going to be as screwed as THEY are...because they're not going to be very happy with you when they ask a question and you go, "I have no idea...") and see if they can swim without drowning.

Always test it out.  I've had clients who swore all they did was email and web...and then suddenly they're asking me where their Power Point is...and where their Quicken is...and (it just goes downhill from there...)

Good luck...you'll need it.

Cheers,
Ghost|BTFH
