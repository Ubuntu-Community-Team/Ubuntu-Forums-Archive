### Post by skychris on 2008-02-11
Hello everyone,

a little more aggressive title for this thread to express my frustration today. I've switched to linux a few month ago and I'm completely convinced by it. 

BUT

discovering this new environment "on the go" I decided to stick to some basic rules like :

- clean format the drive,
- installing the latest version from scratch (Gutsy)
- not installing any software that are not in synaptic
- only update when they become available through the automatic update manager  

and see how it goes.

 Well, thinking it would go smoothly, I unfortunately have to say that it's far from it... I realize that with so many different hardware put together in so many different ways the task is not easy but almost every time, the update manager creates more troubles than it solves and comparing with an other OS that I shall not name here that manage to update their system flawlessly, it's pretty frustrating.

The principle of getting help through a community is awesome, but I always feel like Dr Livingston,   chopping through the deepest jungle, trying to find my way to a solution, and although it helps me to understand better the problems that other people have, it's quite time consuming and confusing, especially when "the best How to" thread out there which is 3 month month old by the time I found it, solves a problem that has already been messed up by a new update and the two combine creates even more mess, forcing me to chop for a few more weeks and so on and so on...

Now, I won't give up, so here's some ideas that would really help me, and other as well, to have a better overview of some problems.

-would it be possible to separate the threads or even the whole forum, by version (Gutsy, Feisty, etc) because usually everybody kicks in with their own bit of solutions regardless on the version they're on and it sort of "pollute" the path of finding the answer.

- is there a way to have a time line or historic of the official updates coming from the "update manager" so we can compare with the great "how to" in this forum and therefore not upgrading part of the solution and downgrading another part, resulting in even more troubles.

thanks for reading me and keep up the good work
