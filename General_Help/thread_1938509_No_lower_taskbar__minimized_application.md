### Post by squilookle on 2012-03-09
Hi Robert

One: the launcher on the left should be showing your running applications as well as your 'favourites'. If you have three Firefox windows open and you click the Firefox button, my understanding is that you should get an expose type view of all the open windows for that application. If you really wanted a bottom panel, you would need to look at modifying the set up (there are some tutorials around) or changing your desktop to something like (but the list is not limited to) KDE or XFCE, etc. 
*I'm using Gnome shell so hopefully someone can conmfirm this

Two: I'm unsure: if it is not in the repos, then I would suggest heading over to the Adobe website to see if they have any downloads. Perhaps someone else will come by with a better answer. 

Three; Yes, click on the one you want to move and hold for a second, you can then drag it to the position you want.  

Last: I wouldn't have thought there are many differences between the two at this stage, but I believe you would need to uninstall LibreOffice and then get the OpenOffice deb for your chosen language and architecture from the [website]("http://www.openoffice.org"). I haven't installed OpenOffice from anywhere other than the repositories of my chosen distro for a long time, but I do remember the process being fairly painless. 

Good luck.

### Post by forrestcupp on 2012-03-09
You can't expect Linux to be just like Windows. Unity is different. It is meant for all of the things that are familiar to you to take place in the left side bar and the top bar. But if you have to have the lower taskbar, I agree that you need to look at KDE or Xfce instead of the default Unity desktop environment.  If you switch to one of those, you won't have the left side bar anymore. Each desktop environment has its own way of doing things.

[Here is how you install Adobe Reader](http://www.jonathanmoeller.com/screed/?p=3351) in Ubuntu.

About OpenOffice, I tried to uninstall LibreOffice and install OpenOffice once. I ended up having a migraine and not having a working version of OpenOffice. In the end, I just undid everything and reinstalled LibreOffice. It's not really worth it.
