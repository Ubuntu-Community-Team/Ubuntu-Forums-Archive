### Post by ad_267 on 2009-06-13
What you download is an iso image, not a whole lot of files. You have to use a disc burning application that will let you burn an image to disc, most should be able to do this. After you burn the CD you should be able to see a large list of files and folders inside. If you just see the .iso file, you've burnt it wrong.

Most modern computers will boot from the CD automatically. You just put the CD in the drive and restart your computer. If that doesn't work you might have to change the boot order in your computer's BIOS. The procedure for this is different depending on your computer, but usually you hit F12 or Delete just after turning the computer on to get to the BIOS menu.

See these pages for more information:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BootFromCD)

Good luck, hope you enjoy Ubuntu!

Edit: Oh, here's the download link: [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download).
You can use bit torrent to download if you want. You'll want the 9.04 desktop edition, and either the 32 bit or 64 bit version depending on your computer.

Also, these are useful:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installingsoftware](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installingsoftware)
