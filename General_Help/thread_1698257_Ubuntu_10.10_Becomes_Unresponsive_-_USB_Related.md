### Post by synz11 on 2011-03-02
I've been having some issues with Ubuntu 10.10 becoming semi unresponsive after a random amount of time.  Sometimes it won't happen at all but most often after about 30min to and hour it will happen.  I'm new to Ubuntu so unfortuantely I'm not sure the best way to tell what is going on when it becomes unresponsive however I have narrowed it down to the USB devices.  I'm not sure if it my specific devices I'm using or the OS.

The only two devices I'm using is a 750GB myPassport and a Logitech wireless mouse.  What happens is I'll be working on my computer and suddenly my mouse clicks become unresponsive.  The only part of the OS that is responsive is the bottom bar which displays my open programs.  If I click an open program from the bar, that window will maximize like it is supposed to.  Everything else is unresponsive (top bar of OS, clicking anything in the window including the menu).  My keyboard still works at this time, using tab or alt+F to open the windows file menu.  I can CTRL+ALT+DELETE and select restart.  Sometimes restarting the computer will fix the problem for awhile and other times it won't.

Now the interesting part:
If my OS becomes unresponsive, all I have to do is unplug my USB devices and BAM!  Everything works perfectly fine.  I'll plug them back in and sometimes they work for hours, others after about 5 minutes it all becomes unresponsive again.

Is there anything I can do to see what is happening when the OS becomes unresponsive?  I know I can look at the log but what am I looking for and under what tab?  I'm currently in Afghanistan with the military so my options are limited, I can't just reinstall a new OS.  I'd love to get this figured out.

Thank you in advance!
