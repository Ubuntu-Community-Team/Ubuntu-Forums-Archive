### Post by 3Miro on 2009-04-29
When you load a youtube video it is stored in some temp folder (/tmp, /usr/tmp, /home/user_name/tmp, something along those sides). Then you can just copy it to another location.

There are also some FireFox extensions that do that.

Yes, when you start to play a video from youtube, check your /tmp directory.  There will be a new file in there with a name similar to 'FlashqpEAD2'.  Copy that file to wherever you want and you'll now have the video on your HD.
