### Post by sefs on 2012-02-01
A way that occurs to me (but I am sure is not very safe/secure) is to somehow edit your sudoers for your main user such that that the admin password is never required.  But note that would open you to a host of other problems such as applications being able to do anything at their behest because they now have by default "root" access.
