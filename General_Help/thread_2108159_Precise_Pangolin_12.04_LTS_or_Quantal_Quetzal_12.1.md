### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-01-23
12.04 might be a little more stable because it has been made more conservatively, but unless you are having trouble with 12.10 there is no need to downgrade.

12.04 is supported for 5 years, for businesses using Ubuntu. Individuals usually prefer to stick with the latest release, which is not "beta" but just a little more experimental as it will only be supported for 18 months. The longer support time for LTS is for businesses. You, as a regular user, would not want to stay on 12.04 for five years as everything would be old and crusty by that point.

### Post by iowabeakster on 2013-01-24
It depends on your *style*

I'm pretty lazy, and hate updating OSs.  So I stick with LTS.  I just made the jump from 10.04 to 12.04 a couple weeks ago.

I also take the approach that if it ain't broken don't fix it.  I also don't believe in reinventing wheels.  The changes are often not significant.

Updating invariably wastes a number of hours that I'd rather not spend wasting.  Then there is a week or so of finding things that need to be changed or replaced, and me often just reverting to my old OS so I can just get things done, without solving any new problems.  A transitional period...

That said, the transition from 10.04 to 12.04 went pretty smoothly.

I also use Xubuntu on my Desktop, which has a shorter LTS period than the current LTS 12.04 standard Ubuntu.
