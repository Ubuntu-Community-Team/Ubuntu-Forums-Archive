### Post by allwin on 2007-01-20
Are you using GNOME or KDE or what?  GNOME has the ability to have a taskbar at both the bottom and top of the screen.  Maybe you switched them off somehow.  I think you can hit alt-f2 and type in "gnome-panel" on an empty desktop and you will see one which you can configure, but I'm too much of a noob to tell you how to get it to come up every time you log in again.  However you should be perfectly able to minimize and open another window and so on and so forth just like the competing OS!  I think System/Admin/Sessions allows you to specify a start up program and you could always stick gnome-panel in there, I suppose, then you'd get one every login.  Panels also autohide.  I don't game, so my answer may be overlooking the fact that the design of some games won't let you do that, in which case, never mind!
