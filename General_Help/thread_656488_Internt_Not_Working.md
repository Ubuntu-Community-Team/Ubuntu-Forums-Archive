### Post by pmg on 2008-01-03
That (192.168.1.1) is most likely your router.  To verify run **netstat -nr** and look at the "gateway" on the line that starts with 0.0.0.0.  Is your router a DSN server?  My guess is it's not, and that should be the DNS server of your ISP.  To confirm this is the problem, try using the IP address of a web site in the various commands, rather than the hosname.  For example try **ping 64.233.167.99** (one of the [www.google.com](www.google.com) addresses) or in a web browser try **[http://64.233.167.99](http://64.233.167.99)** and see if you have better luck.  If so it confirms your problem is just DNS lookups.
