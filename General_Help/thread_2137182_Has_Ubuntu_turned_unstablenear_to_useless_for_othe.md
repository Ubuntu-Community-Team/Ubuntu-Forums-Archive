If you're more conformable with Windows and less inclined to work with tweaking the environment for Linux, Windows might be your best best.

As far as service, I'm certain you can find someone in your area that will service your Linux environment as long as you pay. The fee shouldn't be much different from what you'd have to pay for servicing a Windows machine.

Keep in mind that you'll find just as many people having problems with Windows and seeking support as you find for people having problems with their Linux machines.  Actually I generally find the free online support for Linux substantially better.

Personally, I'd prefer to investigate on purchasing different hardware that is already Linux tested before considering Windows.

The latter would be substantially less expensive.
