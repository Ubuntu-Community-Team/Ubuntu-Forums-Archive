### Post by bowens44 on 2010-04-18
Open a terminal and enter the command:

uname -a , if its 64-bit it will say  x86_64 GNU/Linux in the string.

For example:
Linux net23-admin 2.6.31-20-generic #58-Ubuntu SMP Fri Mar 12 04:38:19 UTC 2010 x86_64 GNU/Linux
