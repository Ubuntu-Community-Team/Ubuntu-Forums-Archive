What troubles is the onboard card giving you?  And what is the card?

If all you're looking to do is do some web development, don't mind using the restricted drivers, and you feel you absolutely cannot do it with what you have, I suggest finding a cheap Nvidia-powered card you can find in the stores.  I did this for a home theater PC displaying HD video and it runs without a hitch, even accepts the latest VDPAU restricted drivers which offload some of the work to the video board.  And I think it was $40 USD or so.

If you don't want to use restricted drivers, try a year-or-two-old ATI model with the standard radeon driver.

EDIT: I agree with earthpigg as well - checking the compatibility list is a great idea also...

### Post by Zoot7 on 2009-09-29
Anything fairly up to date is a-okay I've found.

Some people will tell you only get nVidia, but in my experience the newer ATi drivers are fine. I've Catalyst 9.8 on my home built PC (see sig) and it's fine, I've even got a few games running in Wine and the framerates are comparable to what I get in Windows. The only issue with ATi is with older cards, such as a friend of mine's card doesn't work with Jaunty because it's not compatible with the new version of X, so he's still on Intrepid.
If you follow any of the steps in the guides here you'll be a-okay on an ATi card:
**[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu")**

I've sort of found the nvidia drivers more awquard to install, and more iffy. I've often had to try out a few drivers before I got one that worked okay.

So i'd say either of the 2, but fairly up to date. nVidia has a better history with Linux support, but ATi has got a lot better in the last few years. :)
