### Post by Iowan on 2008-04-15
Unplug the power cord, pop the cover, and check the fans.  If you're brave enough, plug the cord back in and power it up (I have several machines that haven't had covers for years - I'm NOT recommending you run yours that way...)  It's possible that a fan may have just plain quit.  Another option is to get a can of component cooler (or use a can of aero-duster turned upside down).  Shoot the CPU heat sink occasionally (not continuously) to provide a li'l extra cooling.

### Post by lespaul_rentals on 2008-04-16
> **Stephen4785 said:**
> It will start to boot from the cd but it after about 1 1/2 minutes everything shuts down so it never gets a chance to fully load. Iv been told that I should check the power supply or that my mother board could be over heating. Well, that pretty much rules out a hard drive issue.  As for a motherboard problem, it's possible, but I would first go to the CPU.  Pop open the case, as Iowan said, and check for dust buildup and the like.  Any bulging capacitors?  Start up the computer with the LiveCD and leave the case open.  When the computer starts to die, see what happens inside.  Does a fan stop?  Does a beep go off?

I would personally suspect CPU overheating.  It'd be somewhat rare to have a motherboard die spontaneously.  Definitely possible, but unlikely unless your computer experienced a power surge or something.

However, it could very well be a power supply problem.  Unfortunately, the only way to really test it is to replace it with a known good unit.  Do you have any friends you could borrow one from?
