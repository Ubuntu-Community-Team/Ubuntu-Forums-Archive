### Post by Elfy on 2008-07-12
If they are downloaded from the net then synaptic won't know they are there. If they are .deb files, once you have installed them then they will be recognised in SYnaptic so you can use it to uninstall them.

Edit - to elaborate a bit - packages that you see in synaptic originate from the repositories (software sources)

If you download a deb form the internet - that is what it is a standalone download, once it it is installed the installed package becomes recognised as such

If you download a rpm - sometimes you can convert to to a deb with a package called alien

If you download a compressed package - it usually measn that you have to compile it from the source - there is a way to make it a deb  which will again be seen by synaptic AFAIK
