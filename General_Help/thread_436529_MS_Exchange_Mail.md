### Post by WNDS1701 on 2007-05-07
Hello jesangt,

I'm new to Linux, too, but I found something on the internet. You can simply use Evolution Mail. With adding a new account, you can choose "Microsoft Exchange" and add the Outlook Webaccess parameters that you use. For 1&1 in Germany it is:

username: EXCHANGE\e********** (the stars are a unique number)
OWA URL: [http://www.profimailer.de/exchange/](http://www.profimailer.de/exchange/)

Perhaps you will have to try to find the right parameters but it's quite comfortable. Many shortcuts are like in MS Outlook.

Hello,


I didn't get it: You're at home and not wired? So how do you get a connection to the internet to use a browser?

If you like, look me up at ICQ, MSN or Skype to fix this problem together. I had it, too and I managed to do it, so we can do it live.

Like m.musashi has written: You have a webadress on which you log on, in my case [http://www.profimailer.de](http://www.profimailer.de). Expand this address to [http://www.profimailer.de/exchange](http://www.profimailer.de/exchange) and try to figure out, what your user name is. Usually it is your unique identifier for the access to the mailbox with an EXCHANGE/ in front of it. Let's say your unique identifier was xyz123456, then you would use ECHANGE/xyz123456 as your user name.
