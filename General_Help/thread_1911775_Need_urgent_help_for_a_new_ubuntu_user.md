### Post by snowpine on 2012-01-19
Hi Greg, I recommend you burn a "Live CD" of Ubuntu and try it in Live mode (without installing). This will have 2 benefits:

1) You will know if Ubuntu works on your computer (and if you like Ubuntu)
2) You can use the file manager to navigate to your hard drive and copy your files/documents to an external drive for backup

### Post by Reg71 on 2012-01-19
+1.  I have done this very thing several times to salvage a botched Windoze system then wiped the HDD with a low level and stuck a fresh install of preferred OS on for user.  As a matter of fact, nowadays a good option is to stick a bootable version of Ubuntu on a USB stick and keep it on your keychain.  Then you can go to BIOS, switch to boot from USB and take a look at the computer that way.
