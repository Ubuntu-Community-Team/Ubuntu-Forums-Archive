gosh, sorry to hear about that, but it sounds like something went wrong with your filesystem, on the next reboot it got auto repaired, and you were back in business. 

I recall one of the reasons I switch to linux was the fact that Windows seemed to be prompting to run chkdsk all the time on an old desktop I needed some further life from. Having the o/s auto repair for you seems much better to me than running all the steps at:
[http://www.updatexp.com/windows-xp-chkdsk.html](http://www.updatexp.com/windows-xp-chkdsk.html)
but that may be just me. Good luck. Hardware like harddrives will eventually fail.

you can find out more about fsck by typing 'man fsck' at a command prompt, or visit a page like:
[http://linux.die.net/man/8/fsck](http://linux.die.net/man/8/fsck)
