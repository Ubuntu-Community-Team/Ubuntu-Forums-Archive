### Post by L a r r y on 2010-05-19
One observation I have noticed, whether I am using a Mozilla product, OpenOffice.org or now even gedit, there are some common faults in the spell checkers.

Contractions are one issue that causes a spell checker heartburn. Words containing apostrophes are not properly treated.

Case in point:  As long as it is a word before the apostrophe-T, the spell checker does not complain, but if it isn't, it doesn't recognize the contraction as being spelled right.

So far while writing this today, none of my examples in Firefox 3.0 have backfired.  Perhaps I have added these words to the dictionary over time.

But I have seen the same issue in Thunderbird, Evolution, OpenOffice.org, and now even gedit.

Besides contractions, possessives cause an issue with the spell checker.  The word in singular form spells correctly, but add an apostrophe-S in lowercase and it often comes up as misspelled.

One other regular area mishandled by spell checkers is common protocols and Internet provider names, terms that commonly would be discussed in an email or on a web site, or are part of an address:
http://, www., .org, names like Hotmail, MSN, AOL, Earthlink, NetZero and Juno.  Terms and abbreviations like ISP, IP address, website, Gmail, and even email (as opposed to e-mail) usually trigger spell checkers.

Is there a place I can go to find out how to examine the contents of a dictionary and edit it?

This being open source, if someone like myself can go in and edit and then maybe make the revised dictionary  available, the product would be improved.

Thanks
Larry
