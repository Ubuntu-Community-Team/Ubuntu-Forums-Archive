### Post by rsambuca on 2006-12-23
First off, let me just say:  Welcome to the ubuntu community!

For partitioning your drive, I assume Partition Magic will work fine, although there is a partition tool on the ubuntu live CD called gParted which you can use as well.  Personally, I prefer to use the latest live-bootable version of the gParted CD, which you can read about and download [[COLOR="DarkOrchid"]here[/COLOR]]("http://gparted.sourceforge.net/").  It is strongly recommended that you defragment your XP drive a few times before partitioning, and backup your sensitive data first.  How big is your hard-drive?  It will help us to know to offer advice as to the sizes of your partitions, etc.

As far as the 32-bit vs 64-bit thing goes, you will get a different answer from just about every person here as to which route to go!  If you are patient and are willing to learn as you go, then I say fine, go ahead with 64 bit.  Just be prepared that some things may require a bit of tweaking to get working properly, things like flash and Firefox, etc.  If you just want to start off a little more slowly, I would recommend the 32-bit version, and see how it all works for you.  I actually have installed both the 32 and 64 bit versions of ubuntu on my PC, to go along with XP, Vista, and another linux distro called Sabayon linux.  I am also quite new to linux but figured I would just jump right in!:rolleyes:   Frankly, unless you are doing some specific video editing, or intense image manipulation, I don't think you will notice much difference between the 32 and 64 bit systems - maybe a few seconds here or there!

Beryl works great with ubuntu.  I have mine running without any problems.  If you want to try out beryl without affecting your current system, you can try the Sabayon live CD or DVD as beryl is installed out of the box.  On ubuntu, there is a how to, as well as the beryl project site.  I just followed the directions by copying and pasting the commands found on those pages and things worked for me first try.  Only took about 10 minutes to get it going.

7900GT?  Nice card!  I can't help you there as I am using a 6600GT. 

Sorry for the long post.  Keep us updated, and I am sure you will find the ubuntu community is second to none!

Good luck
