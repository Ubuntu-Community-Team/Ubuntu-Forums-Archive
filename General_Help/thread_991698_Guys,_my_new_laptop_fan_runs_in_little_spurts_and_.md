### Post by blazemore on 2008-11-24
I think that's a bios setting, rather than the operating system.
Does Ubuntu even control the fans?

Look in the BIOS for fan startup temperature, and consider raising it a little.

On my desktop system, there's something called PWM. You can control by how much the fan speeds up when it does, and lowering PWM step makes it less of a dramatic transition.

I don't know what it's like on your system obviously.

Also, have you tried getting a can of compressed air, and blowing it in the vents to dislodge any dust, and then hoovering it all out? This will mean the system will run cooler so the fans might not have to spin up at all.
If you do this, make sure the fans don't spin in the air, as a motor is just a generator in reverse, and generating an EMF through the circuit can damage your motherboard, so hold it still with a paperclip or something :)
