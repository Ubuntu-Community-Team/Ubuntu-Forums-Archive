### Post by rons6281 on 2010-03-25
I currently live at a ministry house with approx. 50 residence.  We are based in Liberty, MO.  We have a food/clothing pantry where we support the residence here and help throughout the community, delivering food to the elderly, doing mass food distributions etc.. While Mythubuntu is multimedia oriented we will never use MythTV.  We do however need multimedia capability.  Some residence here need their GED's.  Other take online college courses.  Still others are youngsters who are home schooled.  All the residence need to be able to view spiritual materials, be it articles, Bibles, uTube videos, downloaded movies, etc..  Streaming video has been a major sticking point.
  Given the limitations of processors, RAM, and video cards that I'm faced with, it all boils down to this.
  Which flavor of Ubuntu will best fill these needs without overtaxing the systems I am forced to use?
  Any help and/or advice will be greatly appreciated.

### Post by snowpine on 2010-03-25
The system requirements for Ubuntu are [well documented]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements").

My personal experience is that the "Recommended minimum requirements" are more realistic than the "Bare minimum requirements." 

> Recommended minimum requirements

Ubuntu should run reasonably well on a computer with the following minimum hardware specification. However, features such as visual effects may not run smoothly.

    * 700 MHz x86 processor
    * 384 MB of system memory (RAM)
    * 8 GB of disk space
    * Graphics card capable of 1024x768 resolution

If your hardware does not meet the recommended minimum, Puppy Linux and SliTaz are good options. Both will run well with as little as 128mb of ram (though streaming video may not be smooth on older hardware).

[Here]("http://support.microsoft.com/kb/314865") are the system requirements for Windows XP; as you can see, a computer that is too old for XP (state-of-the-art in 2001) is probably also too old for Ubuntu (state-of-the-art in 2010):

> The minimum hardware requirements for Windows XP Home Edition are:

    * Pentium 233-megahertz (MHz) processor or faster (300 MHz is recommended)
    * At least 64 megabytes (MB) of RAM (128 MB is recommended)
    * At least 1.5 gigabytes (GB) of available space on the hard disk
    * CD-ROM or DVD-ROM drive
    * Keyboard and a Microsoft Mouse or some other compatible pointing device
    * Video adapter and monitor with Super VGA (800 x 600)or higher resolution
    * Sound card
    * Speakers or headphones

Good luck with your project, it sounds exciting!

(edit) I also found the [system requirements for Mythbuntu]("http://www.mythbuntu.org/requirements"):

> Recommended System Requirements:

    * 2.0 Ghz x86 or x86_64 Processor
    * 2GB MB of system memory (RAM)
    * 10 GB disk space (Frontend Role)
    * 160 GB+ disk space (Backend Role)
    * nVidia 8xxx+ 256MB+ Graphics Card
    * Supported TV Tuner Card (Backend Role)
