### Post by aeiah on 2011-04-13
ogg and mp3 are already compressed. you arent going to be able to find an easy way around this. perhaps some of the cleverer algorithms like bz2 or 7zip may shave a few percent off, but not 10%

you'll have an easier time just filling up your mp3 player and then making 1 dvd for the stuff that doesnt fit.

external hard drives are pretty cheap nowadays, i suggest you get one for all your backups, as im sure you have other important stuff other than music.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-04-13
Anything spoken-voice can be downconverted to 64kbps or 80kbps mono. I have some stand-up comedy CDs and have saved space in this way. If you have audiobooks, then greater saving. Otherwise, just make a really good decision about exactly what music you want to listen to - with 22 gigs of music I'm sure you don't want to listen to absolutely all of it.
