### Post by Het Irv on 2008-06-20
It may, but that depends on the hardware in the two machines.  If the hardware is different, you will have to install drivers for everything, including the motherboard.  Drivers for Windows are not to hard to find though, most manufactures keep them on their sites.

### Post by Emarian on 2008-06-20

### Post by Het Irv on 2008-06-20
Yes and No.  Most of the time, like if you were to get a HP or Dell, the copy of Windows that they send with it already has the drivers for that hardware on the disk.  Windows also has a ton of Generic Drivers, so everything should function at least partially.  For the best performance, you may want to download drivers after you move the system files.
