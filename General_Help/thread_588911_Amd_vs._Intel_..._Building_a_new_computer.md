### Post by hardyn on 2007-10-23
there was some trouble a while ago with maxtor hard disks and nvidia chipsets.

the only problem that i could forsee with the motherboard is the interface of your two existing harddisks... new motherboards are coming with few if any ATA connections now, if your harddisks are ATA you may not be able to cable them up to a new motherboard.

i personally like a quiet computer... which means cooling smartly... not though lots of fans.  I would NOT do liquid cooling... its silly.  look at a good heat pipe cooling system and use a motherboard that has smart fan speed control, so the fans are not spinning at full speed when not required.  An adequate cooling system is required,  but you do not get any performance benefit from having more; unless you are overclocking, which is not recommended.

It is nice if you extend the smart fan to the power supply as well, spend a little more and get a power supply with a thermostatic fan controller.
