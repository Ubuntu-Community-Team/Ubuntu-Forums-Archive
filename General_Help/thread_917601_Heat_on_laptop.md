### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-12
The only thing I can think of that might be a problem with leaving a laptop on is that it will decrease the battery life if you leave the laptop on and plugged in with the battery in it.  I burned up two batteries by leaving my old laptop (a 17" desktop replacement that rarely left the house) on and plugged in all of the time before I figured out why the batteries were dying so quickly.

You'll greatly extend the life of your battery if you take it out when the laptop is plugged in (especially if you keep the battery in your refrigerator when not in use -- weird, but true).   Of course, the downside to this is that if the power goes out or your laptop is unplugged, you don't have the battery to fall back on (though that's really not any different than a desktop if you think about it...).
