### Post by caleb12 on 2008-10-08
You could look into AWN - Avant Window Navigator, it's a MAC OS X dock kinda thingy... Other than that, you've pretty much covered the big ones. 

Compiz is the sh!zzle... no doubt about it... Do a good amount of reading before you implement it, and use the fusion-icon... it makes starting it and stopping it very easy. 

Mostly the only points you have to look out for is if your video card can handle 3D, I have compiz running on an ATI Radeon X600 using the standard open source drivers for it... Not even the proprietary ones. So, it's pretty resilient once its setup correctly... 

Besides the look on a Mac lover's face when the cube spins... really, is just priceless.

### Post by airtonix on 2008-10-09
cairo-dock is another style of dock/launcher/taskbar gots lots of unique aspects to it..but i find awn is much more stable.

if your into running minimal mode i suggest you look at using openbox...really gives those low end machines more breathing space. It also has a expose type switcher and many awn like docks available, although setting these up will require you to exercise your google powers and conf editing skills.

the latest enlightenment version coming out also seems quite awesome...kind of like compiz but for low end machines, really looks awesome.
