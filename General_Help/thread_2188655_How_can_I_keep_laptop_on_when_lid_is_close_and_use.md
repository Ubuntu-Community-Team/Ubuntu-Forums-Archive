### Post by rscarlett on 2013-11-18
Just got a external monitor to use with my laptop. I am trying to have it so that the computer stays on when I close the lid. I have been searching the forums for a solution to this problem with no luck of a successful solution for me. Any suggestions?

Nevermind I figured it out!!! :razz:

A lot of the forums answers I look at told me the right thing to do without telling me the last step.

1. Go to Powers setting. 
2. Change the option "when lid is close" to do nothing.
3. Close lid of laptop. (**NOTE:**  the external monitor screen will black out. I didnt know the screen  would black out so I assumed it wasn't working at this step. However...)
4. Once you move the mouse the external screen becomes the primary screen and will come back on.

Hope this helps someone else!!

### Post by germanix on 2013-11-18
Are you using Ubuntu? If yes you need to do the following. First go to system settings, then to "power". Under the power setting you must set under the tab "when the lid is closed" to "do nothing". Then you go to "System settings" and then "Displays". Here you set 1. the position of the external monitor by clicking on the monitor in the window (you should see two monitors, the external and the laptop monitor) hold the left click and move the monitor in the position you want. (drag and drop) (is your laptop to the left or to the right of the laptop) Once that is done you check on the same tab if the resolution is set correctly and decide if the launcher should be displayed on only the external or also on the laptop. Do not tick the box to the right wich asks if you want to mirror the displays. This gives you bad resolution. After all these settings are done hit the "Apply button" bottom right.
Normally this should be enough but this does not work in Ubuntu 12.04 or Ubuntu 13.10. as expected. Some bug I suppose. However, you will now find that when you close thew laptop lid your external monitor goes black (no picture) but no worry, you now click the left mouse button and the external screen is now working. So this is a work around but I have the same problem and this is how I work it.
In Kubuntu using the KDE desktop this problem does not occur. Just do the normal settings as described above and you can close the lid and all is fine. When you use Ubuntu you have to "restart" the external screen everytime you close the laptop lid with a left click of the mouse. Hopefully this gets fixed sometime.
Hope this helps you too.
