HI Folks
I have recently tested Ubuntu and was delighted with it's performance as a basic operating system which could conceivably be seen as a suitable Windows substitute.

However the distinct lack of support for even some of the more common devices most people have attached to their systems makes it difficult to consider it for much more than "something to play with".

I have searched high and low for a suitable Canon D320 laser printer driver and found a myriad of questions from people with similar equipment and/or problems finding drivers for their situation.
Which leads me to wonder just how many people cannot find a suitable device driver for their existing equipment ?? 

Should a function as basic as printing be presenting such an challenge for a new product seeking user acceptance ? I radily admit that it must be a daunting task trying to provide support for everyone's unique equipment configuration but the product was presented so well and installed (with a couple of exceptions) so easily that a stumbling block such as a printer is a bitter disappointment.

That's it just my two cents worth - keep up the good work though all those involved - it looks great
