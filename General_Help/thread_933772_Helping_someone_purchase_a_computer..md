### Post by JC Cheloven on 2008-09-29
In the following context:
-> From a purely practical point of view (stability, performance and applications)
-> If you don't mind to pay more for the hardware & Operating System
-> If you don't mind to spend more money later in software 
-> I you're not concerned by your "freedom" and "rigths" related to computing

Then, IMHO, a Mac is by far the best available option. I have several friends which own a Mac, and, from that practical point of view, they're very satisfied.

BTW, 1Gb is a bit scarce for a mac.
