### Post by whoop on 2009-06-07
If you want the latest an greatest go for ubuntu 9.04 (you will have to upgrade every 6 months, at least till an LTS comes along), If want more stability and don't want to upgrade every 6 months go with ubuntu 8.04 (this is an LTS version and is supported longer).

Just let the ubuntu installer decide what filesystem your need (it will choose ext3). If you need any spare partitions for both windows and linux I would suggest fat32 or ntfs (if you store large files) for a shared partition.

Also I think the Intel I7-940 supports 64 bit (so you can try a 64 bit version of ubuntu for that processor). Actually you need the 64 bit version for that or you won't have access to all of your 6 gigs of RAM.
