### Post by CocoAUS on 2007-02-04
ALL YOU HAVE TO DO is make sure that your HorizSync and VertRefresh values are correct for your monitor.  If they're not correct, even if the resolution is included in your depth line, it won't allow you to use it.

[http://othello.alma.edu/~07tmhopk/ubuntuhowto.html#resolution](http://othello.alma.edu/~07tmhopk/ubuntuhowto.html#resolution)

### Post by CocoAUS on 2007-02-04
You're thinking dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg?

The problem with that command is it needs you to put things in manually.  It'll ask for a lot of stuff that most people don't know, so while it might get your resolution up, it'll also lose a lot of other information that Ubuntu configures for you on install.
