### Post by yeats on 2009-01-24
Have you fully investigated Linux options for multimedia?  I've found that there are many MORE options for Linux than for Windows in this regard, though they will not work like their proprietary counterparts.  Have you tried Cinelerra, for example?  It allows multi-stream editing of audio and video.  There's a version in the repos, but you could go here:

[http://cinelerra.org/]("http://cinelerra.org/")

Note:  there are "two" Cinelerras at the moment since it's a forked project - the Cinelerra at the cinelerra.org site is being redeveloped as "Lumiera"

Give Cinelerra a shot - you might find you never have to use XP again :-).
