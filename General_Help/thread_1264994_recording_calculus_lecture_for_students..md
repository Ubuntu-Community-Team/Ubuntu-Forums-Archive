### Post by StuartN on 2009-09-14
> **executorvs said:**
> 2.) What's the minimal video recording equipment needed
3.) How about for audio capture? what kind of mic? how might one cleanup the audio?

I did this kind of thing when I was teaching, and obviously the software has advanced incredibly in the last few years beyond what we had. But in answer to these 2 questions:

2) A decent video camera comes before any software. Either manually focused in advance or autofocus and free of obstructions (using closest-object mode, if it has multipoint autofocus, to keep the moving lecturer in focus) and definitely on a heavy tripod. You can not expect to capture text on a board unless the text is very big or you have a human operator. Capturing video with a camcorder will prove far more reliable and far less painful than using a PC. It is easy to upload later. If you could capture a video of the lecturer and a separate video of the visuals, then cutting them together would be neat.

3) On-camera mics can be extremely effective, but try it in a full lecture theatre to check the ambient noise. A good off-camera mic close to the speaker, or a clip-on electret type lapel mic can also help. Try to capture the most pristine source in the first place rather than trying to clean up the audio afterwards. Audio is the most difficult aspect of a video recording to get right.

You are spoiled for choice with the editing software, and the two you mention seem to be the best.

### Post by zman58 on 2009-09-14
I am not a video expert, but wouldn't you want to record straight to MPEG file format? That way you would not have to capture video at all. You just download the MPEG file from camera over USB to your system and play it back using the desktop media player. That is how I would do it. No video capture required because the camera does it in real time.

So I would recommend a video camera that records directly to digital file format. A digital video camera which records in standard MPEG format. Not a DVC recording camera. 3 of your 4 selections will do this. For a bit more money you can go beyond 640x480 to higher def, but for presentation you probably would not need that.

If you need to you can cut up and use parts of the digital video file in a video presentation using some of the video tools available on Linux. Again, I am not an expert in this area but have read some very good reports about cinerella video editor.
[http://heroinewarrior.com/cinelerra.php](http://heroinewarrior.com/cinelerra.php)
