### Post by AndThenWhat on 2010-09-27
Yeah I would recommend you using PulseAudio, as that is the sound server that I have been happily using for a while.  I have never installed Ubuntu Studio, but I do occasionally record guitar and singing on my Dell Studio laptop on a regular Ubuntu 10.04 install.  I use Audacity to record the guitar and singing and I can also add in drums from Hydrogen and then export to MP3 or FLAC etc.

If you need to install things such as PulseAudio or JACK and you don't have an internet connection, you can put your Ubuntu CD into your CD drive and go under System -> Administration -> Software Sources...and from there check the box next to "Ubuntu CD-ROM" or something like that and then you can go into Synaptic Package Manager and install some packages off of your CD.  If the packages you need aren't on there you will have to download the .DEB files off of the internet onto your flash drive.
