### Post by mark2741 on 2006-12-12
I've tried just about every open-source/free program for personal finance on linux, after switching to Ubuntu from Windows about a year ago. I used Quicken and then eventually MS Money (which I found to be better than Quicken). I liked MS Money a lot, though Quicken was better than anything on linux. 

I recently broke down and purchased a license for MoneyDance. 

It's far from perfect but it is far and away the best for my needs that runs on linux. One of the things I require my personal finance app to do is the ability to download bank transactions directly (none of that download the file and then import crap - I'm talking direct connection). MoneyDance is the only one that does that out of the box on linux. GnuCash claims that they have that feature but I and many others (check the forums via search on the topic) have tried to get it running with the directConnect feature working and it just doesn't work on Ubuntu.

If I had to make a choice among the free apps I tried (and again - I think I tried them ALL), I'd pick Jgnash (I think that's what it's called). It had the best UI of the free ones and worked pretty well, but again - no direct connect/download of bank transactions.

### Post by rioghal on 2006-12-13
I have two checking accounts and three savings accounts and the only thing I use for keeping track of these accounts are spreadsheets in OpenOffice.org calc. This way I can enter transactions, descriptions, keep a balance, and at the end of the month, I archive it away for record keeping and start a new spreadsheet. I have created some templates (see the attached tarball) and this works very well for me. I can even open them in OpenOffice.org calc and save them as Microsoft Excel files for use on my Palm PDA.

Save the attached tarball, unpack it, open the files in OpenOffice.org calc, or double-click them, and OO.o calc will ask you to save them as a different file so you don't end up over-writing the templates themselves after you have entered some transactions in them.

This works great for me and I take them with me on my PDA and enter stuff into them after each purchase.
