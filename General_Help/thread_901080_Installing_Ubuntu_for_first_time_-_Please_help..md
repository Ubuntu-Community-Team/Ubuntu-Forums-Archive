1) Firefox is both fast AND compatible.. Once the plugins are installed (Flash, Java, etc.), there is never any problems. Installing plugins is as simple as clicking "install missing plugin" and typing your root password (I think you need to type the password), and restarting Firefox. Pair it with Thunderbird, and you got a winner.

2) F-Spot works wonders. It also has basic photo editing tools. Ubuntu also ships with The GIMP for picture editing. I'd also look up GNU Paint in Synaptic Package Manager for minor touch ups (adding a line of text, etc.) when you don't need a full suite.

The GIMP is like Photoshop
GNU Paint is like MS Paint.

3) VLC is a good one, but Totem (which is also pre-installed) works well too. Unfortunately, I don't watch many movies, but Totem takes care of me.

4) My favorite is Rhythmbox (Pre-installed). It's quick, does what I want it, and doesn't fool around. If you're into Internet Radio stations, look up XMMS. XMMS is almost identical to Winamp (You can even use v2 skins). Exaile is quite nice too, but I found it a little complicating. AmaroK is Exaile for KDE basically, they're very similar. 

I have been using Ubuntu for 2+ years with NO antivirus or spyware programs. The reason is that (rough figures, I'm probably way off, but this is just to explain a point.) 80% of users are Windows, 15% are Mac, and 5% are linux. Why would a hacker waste time inventing a virus to infect 5% of the population, when he can make the virus for Windows and infect a larger piece of the pie? 

Openoffice DOES ship with Ubuntu, and works extremely well.

Aside from this, once you are more comfortable in Linux, you may be able to use a variety of programs (Wine, Virtualization programs, Crossover, VMWare (if VM makes a version for Linux, never checked)) and run some programs for Windows on Linux. Check in the forum for HOWTO's on how to install your favorite programs, they may just work. (I play  World of Warcraft like a religion, and it runs perfectly in Ubuntu)

Welcome to Ubuntu, don't get discouraged if things mess up, after all, this is how you learn.:) Ask questions in the forums, no question is dumb.
