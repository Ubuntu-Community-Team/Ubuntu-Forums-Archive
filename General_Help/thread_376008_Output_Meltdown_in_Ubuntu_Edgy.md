### Post by lotu on 2007-03-04
What you have sounds like a hardware problem.  The most likely problem is that your monitor is dying especially if it is an old CRT.  To track down the problem you should swap out components (if you can) with components that you know work. Also if the monitor really fails very randomly you could try and track down the cause, maybe it fails after being on for five or ten minutes, or when you play a movie.  Once you know how to cause the problem it is easier for you to test the new components to see if they fixed.  Good luck.
