### Post by got_milk? on 2008-08-04
A custom-built machine is always, imo, better than just buying a branded one. You can save a lot of money by building your own machine, and when you build your own you can make it the way _you_ want, instead of just using what they give you.

As for AMD/Intel, it's a good idea to pick out your speeds/number of cores and start comparing processors in that range. Each one has their benefits and downfalls, so it's not easy to just say "Well, [insert brand here] is **better**."

Motherboard suggestions might not be a good idea at this time as you're unsure whether to go with an AMD chipset or an Intel chipset. Motherboards are generally designed to work with either Intel chips or AMD chips, not both.

### Post by kernelhaxor on 2008-08-04
Opteron and xeon are mainly server processors .. Since your primary use is HD video editing and 3d animation, I would suggest Phenom or Core 2 Quad processors .. they are quad core processors and video editing does take advantage of the multiple cores ..
Regarding motherboard, depending on your budget, I would suggest a decent motherboard with a PCI x16 slot which supports the above processors or if you wanna spend more, you can get a motherboard which supports SLI or Cross fire so you can hook up two graphics cards ..

When it comes to quad core processors, one of these would be my choice:
Intel Core 2 Quad Q6600 ($179.99 at Newegg)
Intel Core 2 Quad Q9450 ($329.99 at Newegg)
