I like emacs very much, and especially like using C-M-v and C-M-S-V to scroll other window up and down. It is cool, especially when I am coding and referring documents.

using C-M-v to scroll down is OK in both terminal and X,

### Post by jpkotta on 2010-10-26
Many keys don't work in terminals, and AFAIK this is a limitation of terminals.  You just have to find keys that you're comfortable with and that work across X and terminals.  

It seems like by default those other-window scroll commands are bound to M-<next> and M-<prior> (i.e Alt-PageUp and Alt-PageDown), and those do work in the terminal I tried.
