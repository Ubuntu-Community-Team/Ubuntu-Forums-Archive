### Post by Autodave on 2017-05-10
Possibly a bad VGA cable. Try reconnecting it: worth a shot. There is a single wire in that cable that tells the video card about the monitor. The wire may be bad. I have even seen cheaper cables made w/o that specific wire.  Maybe borrow a known good cable and see if that fixes it.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-05-11
As above. Also, is vga your only option for connecting to the monitor? If not, try another. HDMI would be a good shot. If you can, try another monitor and see if that makes any difference. If it does, there's a clue.

What is the make/model of the monitor you are trying with this? The good news, if you could call it that, is that it is probably the monitor or cable playing tricks rather than the software.
