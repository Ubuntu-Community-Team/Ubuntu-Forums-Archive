### Post by DFreeze on 2007-05-07
I've had a similar issue with NVidia. My monitor allows for well over 80 Hz at 1600x1200, but I couldn't get it to go over 58 Hz. Even adding the correct refreshrates in xorg.conf didn't do the trick. 

sudo nvidia-settings

And entering the correct resolution there did the trick but only for the remainder of the session. Rebooting gave me the old settings back.

FIX:  add "Option         "DynamicTwinView"    "FALSE"" in your xorg.conf like so:

Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Default Screen"
    Device         "nVidia Corporation NV17 [GeForce4 MX 440]"
    Monitor        "HP vs17e"
    DefaultDepth    24
    Option         "UseEdidFreqs" "FALSE"
**    Option         "DynamicTwinView" "FALSE"**
    Option         "AddARGBVisuals" "True"
    Option         "NoLogo" "True"
    Option         "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"

It seems this is a bug in the NVidia driver. The driver allows for better twinview, but messes with your config when your one of those poor ol' laymen with only one screen. Good luck!
