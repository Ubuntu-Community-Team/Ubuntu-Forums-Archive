### Post by robsoles on 2010-09-22
Welcome to UF.

If you 'close the last window' of a program, in the case of most programs, it is meant to stop functioning.

Programs like rythmbox are written to pin themselves to that part of your panels and persist through their window being closed but programs like firefox just run as long as their window runs and do not persist through the window being shut.

Some programs are set to close with their window with the option to persist as an icon in the notification area, finding their 'Preferences' or 'Settings' options and taking a look is an easy enough way (in most programs that can) to find it if the one you want to persist actually can.

### Post by DoubleClicker on 2010-09-22
From your questions, it's obvious that until recently you only used Mac.  Whereas on a mac, a window is a conatiner for the document and the menubar is the container for the application.,       In linux, the concept of a window  is that it is a container for the program, and the menubar is just part of the window (even if it is moved to the panel with an applet).  So when you talk about a window closing here, most people equate that with quitting the program, not with closing the file.
