### Post by gerbman on 2006-03-17
From the puppy site: "Given that the distros are about 50M - 60M, something has to be left out...hardware detection is a work-in-progress...puppy is built from scratch by myself." Anyone who has dealt with hardware support troubles in the past will agree that the above statement is probably a good enough reason to _not_ choose Puppy when good alternatives like Ubuntu are available. Also from the website:  &#8220;every time you boot Puppy, it's as though you have done a fresh install.&#8221; This is fine in certain usage scenarios but seems quite impractical for a regular-use machine like a home computer. My main question is why you would want to unnecessarily constrain yourself by choosing a distro like Puppy. For example, I don't think you will have choices as to which window managers you will get to use, and there are many very nice ones out there.
