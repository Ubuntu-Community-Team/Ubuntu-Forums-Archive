### Post by jesuisbenjamin on 2010-06-26
Gimp is great, but can you really compare it to PS? I haven't used the latter in a while.

You can run non-linux programs thanks to Wine. You should check out proper linux alternative though, it may be worthwhile. You can try some which can be installed on windows, i think it's the case for GIMP.

Some things you do not have gnu-licensed equivalents (not yet).

You do not want a dual boot, and yet it may be a good way to start and try Ubuntu for a time and get familiar with it. Then you may chose to do a clean install later on.

As for the look of your desktop you need not worry, if there is something you can do with Ubuntu/Kubuntu is customise the looks of your desktop. cf. [http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/) and [http://kde-look.org/](http://kde-look.org/) for examples.

### Post by Spr0k3t on 2010-06-27
1. Photoshop, Which version are you really needing?  The Gimp runs fairly well.  I personally like The Gimp over Photoshop myself.
2. iTunes, people have been able to get it working in WINE... but there are TONS of music apps that far outperform that bloated piece of appleware.  If you have an iphone that you need to sync to itunes with... I'm sorry to hear that.  Apple does not like Linux.  I've heard of people using the iphone and syncing without itunes... but it's not pretty.
3. Teamveiwer, VNC, VINO, RDPViewer?  No clue what the features are of Teamviewer as I've never heard of it until now.
4. object dock, skinning software with dock-app?  Customizing Linux in general is one of the draws it has.  Not to mention, you can take your pick on the several different dock bars.  Be it Cairo-Dock, Docky, Conky (not a dock, but cool stuff), Kiba-Dock, AWN, and tons more.  Each offer an extensive list of features. 
5+ a lot of video games, check out [http://appdb.winehq.org](http://appdb.winehq.org), if it's not gold or platinum... it's best to wait or just dual boot.

Overall, don't try and switch to any operating system without considering native applications.  Native applications generally make things run smooth.  If you really absolutely can't live without those apps, try it with WINE.
