### Post by M3ta7h3ad on 2005-12-13
Its because in the short term converting the entire government infrastructure to linux based operating systems will mean a hell of a lot more outlay than just continuing with what they have.

For every variant of linux out there that they consider using, you will need training for the staff, technicians to train them, desktop support to be provided, the software rollout contracts, and you'll also have to factor in the "loss of time" which will effect business spent in switching over, training, the teething problems and so on.

Not discounting also the fact that people are always reluctant to change. You will find that in any company you will have staff complain about the current computer system, when you replace this computer system... more staff will complain about how different it is to the old system. This inherent reluctance will mean they are more adverse to training on the new systems and so the entire process can take longer than anticipated.

You are also forgetting that in most cases the following is true.

- Bulk discounts on large purchases of microsoft licences.

- Most contract workers (people who work on rollouts) are extremely familiar with the workings of MS Windows, less familiar with linux.

- Most workers at home use MS Windows, thus lowering training times.

- MS has a central and vast support network. With linux if you go with redhat for example... you are dealing with a smaller company in retrospect, you will not have the capacity for support there such as a large company as microsoft does with regional offices, and support staff and registered partners all over the world.

So yes... in short, while the money "could" remain in country (I know several people who regularly take contracts for rollouts and support in delhi and brussels fly over... and do the job, then fly back home for example), and the long term costs "could" be cheaper, due to worker familiarity training costs will increase, due to needing to alter the way things are done, possibly seeking out alternative programs or hiring programmers to code new platform independent tools this again will cost more money... the actual change over costs will far outweigh any benefit from a linux based system switch.
