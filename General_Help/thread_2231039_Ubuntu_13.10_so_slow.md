### Post by QIII on 2014-06-22
Hello!

I would say that the most likely culprit is the small amount of RAM.  Unity is more resource hungry than many other Desktop Environments.

It is likely that Xubuntu or Lubuntu, with their lighter desktops, would work better.  Still that isn't much RAM.  You might also try Bodhi Linux, which is a different distribution based on Ubuntu.  It is extremely light, using the Enlightenment Window Manager rather than a full-blown DE.

Also please note that support for 13.10 will expire in July, making any attempt to get it working "too little, too late."  I would suggest installing either 12.04, which will be supported until 2017, or 14.04, which will be supported until 2019.

Be advised that if you stick with 13.10, forum policy will have us closing any threads you might start asking for help on that release after July.

### Post by deadflowr on 2014-06-22
You're running Ubuntu on 256MB RAM?
(Not Lubuntu or Xubuntu, but plain ole Ubuntu?)
There's the problem.
You'll get a decent performance if you increase the RAM.
Preferably to at least 1GB.
At 256MB, I'm surprised it's even functional.

It's unclear how much it would help, but maybe increasing the swapiness could help, a little.
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SwapFaq#What_is_swappiness_and_how_do_I_change_it.3F](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SwapFaq#What_is_swappiness_and_how_do_I_change_it.3F)
Note: Where as most who change the swapiness setting do so because of abundant RAM, you would go in the opposite direction.
Someone with lots of ram would change it from the default 60 to 10, where as you would want to change it from 60 to 90, or something like that.
I don't think it'll be a major game changer, but might help a little.
