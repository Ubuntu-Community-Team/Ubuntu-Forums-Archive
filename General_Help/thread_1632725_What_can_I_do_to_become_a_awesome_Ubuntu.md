### Post by Locke_99GS on 2010-11-28
Tinker and experiment, and when your tinkering breaks your system, try fixing it yourself. (Failing that, ask for help)

Set things up for the hell of it. Have a spare webcam? Try setting it up with "motion" as a security device. Spare, unsued computer somewhere? Set it up as a Linux server, then remove the monitor and keyboard. Interface with it via SSH. Install an Apache and FTP server to play with. Play Minecraft? Set up a Mincraft server. Use ventrilo? Build a bot to record things when you're away with libventrilo. Leave your server on all the time? Learn all about ipchains and iptables. Try setting up MPD on a spare computer with it's audio piped to your home stereo, and control it with clients such as Sonata or Remuco from other PC's, or even your cell phone.

### Post by Dimarchi on 2010-11-28
I agree with the Cog's points there...especially the chore and interest part. The other comments provide good points too, though. Start small, with some thing that interests you, experiment...you'll gain experience and confidence. At some point something just clicks and you'll find yourself thinking "Yes, that is how it can be done, and perhaps even this way...", for example. Build upon it. This may take time (or not...it is a personal thing...I'm in the slow turtle camp, myself). In the future, you might find yourself in a situation where you are humbled by the amount of things you know but that was not immediately evident in the past.

Do not burn yourself out by trying to learn everything and do everything as fast as possible - unless that is just the way you do things.
