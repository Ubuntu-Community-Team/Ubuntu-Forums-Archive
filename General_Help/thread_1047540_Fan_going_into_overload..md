Sorry, but what do you like to hear from me?
It's a fact that when the cpu gets hot, the computer increases the amount of rpm the fan is spinning. And yes, with some computers it is pretty loud.
My wife has a Dell computer and at boot for a few seconds it's just a vacuum cleaner. Then it becomes quiet, and I really mean quiet. Sometimes with a high load it turns back into the vacuum cleaner again.
You can look for a fan with less noise, a more powerful processor which is not taken to the limit with a Flash movie or a game so the fan will not spin at maximum speed.
I don't think there are a lot of things you can do. Sorry if this answer doesn't help much, and my apologies for the first answer. It was just so obvious that I could not resist.
