### Post by aseriesoftubes on 2008-03-27
Yes and no. The drive will be detected, and you'll be able to use its non-BD/HD capabilities without any trouble, but playing BD/HD-DVD movies is more complicated. Basically, you have to rip the movie to your hard drive and strip out the DRM before you can play it. It is not at simple as putting the disk in your drive and pressing Play, and you'll need a lot of hard drive space. 

In my opinion, it is not worth the effort for casual watching (ie, renting a Blu-ray Disc from the video store), but it could worth it if you're running a media server with plenty of storage space.

See this article for more information: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats/BluRayAndHDDVD]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats/BluRayAndHDDVD")
