### Post by Stealth on 2006-05-23
Well, unless your burning software does it for you, you're going to **have** to convert it into an mp3 or something, because real media files don't "just work" on other things. :P

Here's a how-to on that: [http://linuxathome.com/index.php?p=91](http://linuxathome.com/index.php?p=91)

Then just use your burning program (like K3B) and make a music CD. Drag those new mp3s in there and you should be good to go.

Now unless your car somehow SUPPORTS real media files straight off the CD (which I've never heard of such thing) then that's a simple drag and drop and burn. ;)
