### Post by P4man on 2009-09-01
im guessing you're using a winmodem (softmodem) that needs some kernel modules and hackery to work. My hat is off to you for getting it to work in the first place!

It wont make you love linux any more, but just realize these things are such a pain because they were designed exclusively to work with windows, and most of them without any documentation that allows linux developers to write proper drivers for them. (Never mind the manufacturers would actually make their own drivers. After all, we are talking about a concept that saves them $0.2 per modem by not including a hardware synthesizer and relying on the cpu and software to do that).

You can save yourself a lot of headaches by buying a hardware modem from ebay for $1 + shipping...
