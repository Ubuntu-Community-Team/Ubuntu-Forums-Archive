### Post by woedend on 2005-07-20
I am/was on a quest to see if we could get the shockwave player for linux.  This was the lengthy reply I received for any interested.
---------------------------------------------------------


Alex,
I figured I would take a minute and respond to your message as hopefully
it will help expose our point of view with respect to any new platform
for our players or authoring tools. Moving to a new platform is a very
expensive proposition, a lot of development time and engineering effort
are required both to initially implement as well as continue to support
such a new player or application version. So in considering such a port
we need to look at the total cost versus the benefit and decide what
work is "best" to do each release, port to Linux or expand the current
feature set or ???.

It's true, many thousands of folks have signed various petitions and/or
indicated their desire for a Linux Shockwave Player, but please
understand the numbers we deal with when looking at these decisions.
There are an estimated 300 million users of the Shockwave Player out
there today*, do we incur an additional 30% engineering and QA cost
going forward for a Linux player? If you imagine that a full one million
people had made the request that would still only be 0.333% of the total
estimated Shockwave user-base population, does that population yet
justify the cost required to deliver to them? Maybe yes, maybe no...
(obviously the Linux population is growing and is likely to continue
growing, thus changing that 0.333% to a larger number  ;)  )

I'm just trying to convey the magnitude of numbers we deal with when
considering player changes so that it helps set our context when looking
at thousands of users on up to hundreds of millions of users. Yes, I too
think that Linux is a rapidly growing and increasingly important
platform for both our players and our tools that we *must* keep a close
eye on as there are likely many more potential users out there beyond
those that we've heard from directly. It's just that thus far the
company and our product team have seen other areas of development as
offering more return on the investment and so a Linux player hasn't
happened yet**.

And for what it's worth, nobody from Macromedia has ever said that a few
thousand requests is all that we'd need to hear in order to begin doing
this work. The truth is that we must always do business case analysis
for any major work like this and that involves a lot more than simply
hearing a few thousand requests, we must look at all the issues outlined
above in combination. Yes, hearing requests does indicate some
importance, but it's certainly not the only thing we need to see/hear.


Please understand that I am not saying a Linux player will never happen,
it is something we continue to look at closely. Part of that continued
consideration on our part is due to the efforts of folks like yourself
who alert us to their desire for a Linux player. So ultimately thanks
for the feedback and hopefully we'll be able to offer you our player on
your platform of choice (Linux) in the future. Feel free to reply to me
directly if you have any further questions or comments about this issue
as I'd be happy to discuss it further if you'd like.
