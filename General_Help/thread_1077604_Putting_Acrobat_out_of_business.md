### Post by Bill Day on 2009-02-22
The Lawyers' Social Group is trying to come up with a suite of Linux tools that would obviate the need to use Acrobat to produce PDF files. See [http://ubuntuforums.org/group.php?do=discuss&group=&discussionid=369](http://ubuntuforums.org/group.php?do=discuss&group=&discussionid=369) 

Among the things we need to do on a regular basis are scan documents, OCR them, compress them, edit them, make forms, index and search them, and file them in court.

As lawyers, we are mostly avid but not necessarily highly technically accomplished users.  Some ideas are obvious, such as using OpenOffice.org or LyX to convert word processing documents or Xsane for scanning.  However, we would definitely be interested in suggestions that would provide a more complete environment for dealing with PDFs.

Thanks!

### Post by prinny42 on 2009-02-22
I've never had to modify a PDF directly before, as I generally simply make things in other formats and then convert them, but is pdfedit perhaps the thing you're looking for (or one of them)?

From the apt-cache description:
> Complete editing of pdf documents is made possible with PDFedit. You
 can change either raw pdf objects (for advanced users) or use
 predefined gui functions. Functions can be easily added as everything
 is based on a scripts.
 .
 Scripting is used to a great extent in editor and almost anything can
 be scripted, it is possible to create own scripts or plugins.
