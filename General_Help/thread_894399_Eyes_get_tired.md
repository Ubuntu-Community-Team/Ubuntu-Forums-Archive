### Post by forger on 2008-08-19
If I were you I would compare what I do in front of the Windows computer with what I do while using Ubuntu Linux.
If you're playing action games in Windows (counter strike?), your adrenaline keeps you focused and practically keeps the pain away - that's how gamers can stretch up 10+ hours of gaming (they probably get a huge headache afterwards :P)
Also, getting used to a new operating environment involves "learning", your brain is used to in the Windows environment. It's just like going on vacation at a friend's place and trying to figure out where the dishes and knives/forks are :)
You just have to give some time for your brain to get used to doing stuff the linux way.

On the other hand, it could be the refresh rate, make sure it's as high as it can be, 60Hz on non-LCD monitors can be really painful for the eyes in a very short amount of time.

I would recommend pausing what you do every hour or so, do something else for 5 minutes and then continue again!

### Post by zami on 2008-08-19
I had this same problem when I first started using Ubuntu, and I'm also using an LCD screen.

For me it was 100% the refresh rate.  Which, I *thought* should be fine - there were settings for the refresh rate under System>Preferences>Resolution - bumping that to the top should have been enough, yeah?  
No.  Turned out I didn't have the correct video driver installed, and once I got that fixed my options about doubled, and I was able to bump the refresh rate high enough my eyes no longer caught the refresh.  Such relief.

If you havn't already, I'd suggest making double sure you've got the correct video driver.
