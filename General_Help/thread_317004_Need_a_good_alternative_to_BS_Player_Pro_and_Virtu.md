### Post by PurplePenguin on 2006-12-11
Agreed, although I think you can also use VLC to watch movies with external subtitle files.

I also use avidemux to put hard subtitles (that is, subtitles that are burned right into the movie itself - no need for external .srt files) onto discs so I can watch subtitled movies on my set-top DVD/divx player.  Avidemux might look really complicated at first (it's filled with filters that can do pretty much anything), but it's actually quite simple once you get used to it. :)
