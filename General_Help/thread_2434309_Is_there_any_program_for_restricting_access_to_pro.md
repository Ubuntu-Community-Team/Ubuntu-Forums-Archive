### Post by TheFu on 2020-01-03
You could setup times when the programs don't work just by manipulating your aliases during those times. Or change your PATH during those times. Basically, you'll make it so the programs cannot be run.  It might just take removing them from the menu system for some people.  I don't have any menus, so that won't work for me.

If you have a smart phone, put it into airplane mode all weekend. See how that goes.
Disable notifications on your smartphone and computers.  This is pretty easy.  On Linux, I simply switched to a different window manager, one that doesn't support notifications is fvwm.  Don't launch the email program or other social network programs until after work.

Self control is always a good thing.  Everyone lacks it in some area of their life.
If you can enable these things, then you'll know how to disable them too.
