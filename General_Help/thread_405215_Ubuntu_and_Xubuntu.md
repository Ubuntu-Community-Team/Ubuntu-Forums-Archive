I would just like to know what are the differences in the Ubuntu distribution and the Xubuntu distribution?

I know that Xubuntu is supposed to just be a lighter version of Ubuntu(for older or slower PCs), but what are actually differences?  For instance, what are things the Ubuntu distro can do that the Xubuntu distro can't?

### Post by newlinux on 2007-04-09
The main difference is that xubuntu uses xfce for it's desktop environment, and ubuntu uses gnome. There is nothing that one can do that the other can't do. The difference is in what desktop environment they start with. You can add xfce to ubuntu, or gnome to xubuntu. Xfce is a lighter desktop environment than gnome.
