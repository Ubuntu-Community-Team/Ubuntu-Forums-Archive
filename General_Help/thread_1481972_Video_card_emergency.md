### Post by prshah on 2010-05-13
> **J V said:**
> doesn't give access to the ball bearings, but rather to a chip 


What I normally do with stuck fans is use a syringe to inject oil in the gap between the top and bottom casing in the fan (Sorry for vague explanation, please see enclosed pic for the 1000-word version.)

However, if you find the fan is moving freely (blow into the vanes) then I don't think you need to do this.

Otherwise, inject a couple of drops of ink, rotate the vanes, repeat... until the vanes move smoothly when you blow on them.

As to the thermal grease, though manufacturers and enthusiasts alike ask not to mix, I feel it is too risky to try to remove the old thermal paste. So I never remove the old paste unless it's dry and flaky and comes away easily. I just squirt in a bit more, squash the fan down upon it, and go my way. 

btw thermal paste is _not_ a glue to hold stuff down. It's just designed to pull heat away quickly from the chipset and pass it to the heatsink. The fan is held by the screws.

Standard disclaimer: Everything at your own risk, please.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-13
I've been doing thermal paste for years now, and this is what I do -

Just drop a tiny amount of thermal paste the centre of the heatsink. About the size of half a grain to a grain of (uncooked) rice. Then, slowly put the heatsink down, clamp it with whatever clamping method you are using, and you are good to go. 

Lots of people actually spread the thermal grease like butter on bread- I've found that in most cases you dont need to do this. With a light heatsink on a video card its not that bad an idea, but shouldnt be needed really (the clamping pressure and weight of the heatsink on CPUs will always flatten out the thermal paste). 

If you do spread the thermal paste, use a credit card or something hard and plastic like that. Dont use tons, its just a waste...at best.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-15
8800s are pretty old now. The 9800s and GTS250 are based on the same chip though....

How high up the ladder should you be aiming? Depends on what you use the card for. 

If its just for display- whatever card is cheap. 

If its for dsplay and video- get a GT220. Has all the VDPAU features (nvidia hardware video decoder). 

If its for display, video and gaming, get a GT240. 

If its just for display and gaming- GTX260, or better. 

You can get passive cooled (fanless) video cards. They arent so great if you have a clogged case, or minimal airflow. But if you have good airflow, or a case with some room, they are pretty good.
