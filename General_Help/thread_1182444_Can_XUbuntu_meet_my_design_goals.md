### Post by SweetBearCub on 2009-06-09
Hi.

I will shortly be building a new desktop PC from Newegg parts. It will be to the following specifications:

Motherboard: ASUS M4A78T-E AM3 AMD 790GX HDMI ATX
Processor: AMD Phenom II X3 720 2.8GHz 3 x 512KB L2 Cache 6MB L3 Cache Socket AM3 95W Triple-Core Black Edition
Memory: Crucial Ballistix 2GB (2 x 1GB) 240-Pin DDR3 SDRAM DDR3 1333 (PC3 10600) Dual Channel Kit

It will also have 2 SATA HDs, a SATA DVD Burner, a USB ATSC (Digital OTA TV) TV tuner, 2 channel speakers, a PS2 keyboard with multimedia keys, a USB mouse, and a 1200VA UPS. It will not be overclocked, at least initially. I will be using the ATI Radeon HD 3300 IGP that is built into the 790GX chipset. The system will be 64-bit and VT capable.

For several reasons, I am considering the use of XUbuntu as my OS of choice. I have very little Linux experience, and will likely need a large amount of help to make this work. However, to start, here are the design goals of the system I am building. Can XUbuntu support these things?

- Heavy web surfing (30+ tabs open at once in Firefox)
- Productivity/Connectivity (OpenOffice, Calendar, ToDo List, Gmail, Multi-IM Client w/ Webcam, Audio, and File Transfer Support)
- Media Hub (Gobs of BitTorrented shows, Hulu streaming, DVDs, OTA DTV Viewing/Recording/Commercial Stripping, MP3s, Streaming Radio)
- Virtual OSes (Mainly for nostalgia purposes, MSDOS622/WFWG311, MacOS 9.0.4/OSX, C64, AppleIIe/IIgs, Amiga, etc.)
- Gaming (World Of Warcraft, NES/SNES/PS1/PS2 Emulation, MAME, various older DOS/WIN games, etc.)

The system will not be used as a media center system, but rather as a conventional desktop. The TV tuner does have a remote and some software, but I doubt I will use either of those if native Linux software is up the job.

On gaming, which as far as I know, is the area where Linux has the most problems, I read an article lately that some VM software now has accelerated 3D graphics capability, but I don't know the specifics. I am not willing to dual-boot this system.

Thanks!
