### Post by kelvin.illa on 2009-08-22
I think you can emulate the effect with compiz (using the compiz-settings-manager) with the 'Opacity, Brightness, and Saturation' and 'Invert' plug-ins.

In Invert:
 - enable it and press the assign key/s to activate it (or assign a new one)
 - you could remove the Desktop exclusion in the settings to include the Desktop in the effect.

In Opacity, Brightness, and Saturation (for grayscale):
 - i'm not sure about this because i think the effect is on a 'per window' basis.
 - check it out anyway

conclusion: i think the invert effect should be 'soothing' enough assuming your screen is predominantly in bright colors (i.e. with whites)
