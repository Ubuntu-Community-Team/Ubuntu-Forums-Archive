### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-06-20
and have you verified that all the references to anything that has a UUID is correct to what is shown within /dev/disk/by-uuid. If so and you still have problems, than you'll either have to wait for the bug to be fixed or you can switch to using the /dev/sda1 device id instead of using the UUID. that's up to you, somtimes when they update the kernel, the ide/sata driver gets changed from libata and something else so you drives may be seen by ubuntu as /dev/sda one kernel, then the next kernel they get referred to as /dev/hda which is why you should stick with the UUID's BUT if you know that this could be a problem after a kernel update, then you would be prepared for Ubuntu failing to boot and you could fix your menu.lst, your fstab files from a livecd and you'd be ok then.

As I said, this is up to you if you want to change all the UUID references to the normal device id's being either /dev/hda or /dev/sda and I have explained the risk of doing so. Other than that, I can't help anymore.

### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-06-20
yes, you just need to make sure that the UUID within /dev/ matches all the references that call out the UUID's, meaning the fstab and the menu.lst files. if they are wrong, then change them within these 2 files to match what they are and then you'll be good to go. What this is doing is basically doing what the bug fix WILL DO once it's released so you're safe in doing this. Just make sure you write everything down before and after modifying and that you don't transpose any numbers when you change them to match the /dev/ id's.
