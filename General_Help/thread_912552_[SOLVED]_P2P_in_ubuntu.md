### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-06
Well, I think the best way of doing what you're talking about is to use a bittorrent client.  Bittorrent is by far the most popular form of P2P right now, which means you're more likely to find what you're looking for, and the download is likely to be faster.  Most bittorrent clients allow you to select specific files in a torrent to download, rather than forcing you to download the entire torrent.  Deluge is my personal favorite, but there are many clients available for Linux to choose from.

However, if for some reason you really don't want to use bittorrent, there are a few other P2P clients available.  You might try gtk-gnutella, which is a gnutella client.  I've used it before, and while it's not pretty, it gets the job done.
