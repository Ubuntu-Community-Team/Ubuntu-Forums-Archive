### Post by Pnuts on 2009-05-29
most hotels I've gone to that require you to pay for internet work this way:

Plug the wire in, open a web browser. Any web page you attempt to view auto redirects you to an internal hotel site. You either enter CC info into the system or it auto detects your room and you have to accept the charge, it will then show up at checkout.

This works with any browser. I have never seen a hotel require ou to install something in order to get internet, so it shouldnt matter if its windows\linux\mac\etc.

-Pnuts

### Post by Dakiraun on 2009-05-29
Agreed - be it wired or wireless, all the hotels/motels I've been at either direct connect you or redirect you to a page where you have to register and/or pay a fee (if it's not included already in your bill).  Then they let you surf.  

A word of caution is just to watch what you're doing since hotel networks are usually poorly designed and make it easy for others to try and sniff out what's going on in them (particularly wireless).  So keep to sercure connections for entering any passwords to check e-mail and such, and avoid doing things like on-line banking unless you know you're well encrypted.
