### Post by Quarkrad on 2012-04-23
I'm a newbie but this what I do to clean my system. I do use Bleachbit.  Click on Edit and then Preferences - in the Genral Tab make sure there is a tick against Hide irrelevant cleaners. Under the Drive Tab I just have home/user name  because most of my personal data is stored on a different partition to Ubuntu.  Close the Preferences window and look down the list of tickable boxes in the main Bleachbit window. You could put a tick in every box - there is an explanation of what will be cleaned in the right hand pane.  However, there are warning messages against some of them (mostly system related) as they are experimental and/or really slow - I do not put ticks in these boxes.   That;s it - once you have ticked what you want hit the Delete key.   The other app I find useful is for cleaning is Ubuntu Tweak - on my 10.10 system there is an option called Package Cleaner - in 11.xx/12.04 it is called Janitor.
