> The best method to securing your wireless LAN

Is to TURN IT COMPLETELY OFF

Ah, yes. The age old security saying! ;) True, yet unpractical. 

I vote for a layered approach. Don't let your wireless encryption mechanism be the only point of blocking access to your network and you should be fine from most common attacks. If WEP is a necessity, by all means, use it. It is better than having an open WLAN. Simply set into place additional security measures.

If you want to be sure of your security, by all means take an offensive approach to testing it. WEP cracking is trivial, but it can be fun. I say take it further and see how far past your WLAN you can get. All of that is a great learning tool and can be very useful in determining mitigation steps.
