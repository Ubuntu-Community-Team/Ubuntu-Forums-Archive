### Post by kperkins on 2005-11-17
I've come to this kinda late, but...
First off, if your parents have 5 years worth of data in their Money file, it's time to back up 4 years worth of that to a CD, and clean it out, you should never have that much (finacial) data on a harddrive..
Then pick either Moneydance (non-free--costs money, and not OSS), or Grisbi, or GnuCash (though I think the interface sucks, other people like it), or even KMoney (or whatever they kall it), and save all your Money accounts as .qifs, and import them. I've found that Moneydance has a user interface that people familiar with Money might like (and it imports .qif files better than the other programs). Grisbi does, too, but is slightly harder to get used to, and GnuCash is a pain to pick up as far as I'm concerned.
