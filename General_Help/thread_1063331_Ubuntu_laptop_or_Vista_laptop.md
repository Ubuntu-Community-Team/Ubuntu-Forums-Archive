### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-07
I have had two Dell laptops, and currently run Ubuntu on both.  My suggestion is to pick out the laptop for the hardware, and deal with the operating system later.  Even if I purchased an Ubuntu system from Dell, I'd want to reinstall to change the partitioning scheme.  And whether or not you buy a Dell with Ubuntu preinstalled, you still don't have to pay for Windows.

There's a section of the Windows EULA that states:> By using the software, you accept these terms. If you do not accept them, do not use the software. Instead, return it to the retailer for a refund or credit. If you cannot obtain a refund there, contact Microsoft or the Microsoft affiliate serving your country for information about Microsoft's refund policies. See [www.microsoft.com/worldwide](www.microsoft.com/worldwide). In the United States and Canada, call (800) MICROSOFT or see [www.microsoft.com/info/nareturns.htm](www.microsoft.com/info/nareturns.htm).

Dell only offers certain models with Ubuntu preinstalled.  Go ahead and buy a Dell with Vista preinstalled if you like the hardware better.  Then, before you power up the computer for the first time, call Dell and tell them you want decline the EULA and return your copy of Windows. They're legally bound by the EULA to give you a refund.  That's what I did with my second laptop.  It took about 15 or 20 minutes on the phone (mostly waiting for them to process my request), but I got a refund of about $150 on the cost of my laptop.

The only thing you might want to be mindful of when picking out your Dell is the wireless card.  Anything with a Broadcom chipset will not be natively supported by Linux and require you to use the Windows wireless driver to use it.  For simplicity's sake, you should try to pick a [wireless card supported by Linux]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported").
