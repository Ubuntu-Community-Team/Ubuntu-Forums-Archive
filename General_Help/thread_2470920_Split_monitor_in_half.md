### Post by DuckHook on 2022-01-16
Welcome to the forums, alfredohuerta

Not quite sure what you mean by "split the monitor in half". If you mean having one monitor display simultaneous output from two separate display servers, then no. One monitor per display server. That's the way the the display server/monitor relationship works. But if you mean having two windows displayed side by side taking up the whole desktop, then the <super>+<left/right arrow> trick should work.

[LIST]
[*]Are you running Focal or later?
[*]Do you have vanilla Ubuntu or another flavour? To my knowledge, the above trick works only with vanilla Ubuntu.
[*]You must have the window that you want to manipulate in focus before pressing <Super>+<Left/Right>
[/LIST]

EDIT:

I should read more carefully. You are running Lubuntu. The above manipulation does not work in Lubuntu.
