### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-07-14
Four years is getting a bit "long in the tooth" for a hard drive -- but its useful life really depends on how much time it spent in use in those years, not just the age.

I've had drives last twice that long; I've had drives fail inside of six months.

I'm guessing that this is either an old IDE drive (probably 100, not 133) or an old SATA drive (probably 150), right?

Either way, I would regularly back off stuff you don't want to lose.
