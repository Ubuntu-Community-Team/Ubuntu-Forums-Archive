### Post by HereInOz on 2006-10-01
Just a thought that you may have faulty RAM. Are you able to change the RAM for another module?

It is probably a hardware issue, either with incompatibility or just something is faulty.  However, I do agree that this could be a difficult one to diagnose, and you may end up spending more time than it is worth.

If you have another RAM module, replace the RAM, and also replace the hard disk, and the HDD cable, (if it uses a cable connection - many don't) if you are able to.  

My only other suggestion is to try an earlier version of Ubuntu, and see it that has the same problem.  If so, then it is probably hardware faults, if not, then I would suspect a compatibility issue.
