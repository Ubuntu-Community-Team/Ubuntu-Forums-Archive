### Post by JKyleOKC on 2012-09-22
Have you used Nikon's software to convert the RAW image file into a more conventional format such as, say, TIFF?

A friend uses Nikon to shoot photos for a small magazine that I edit; about a year ago he shot a class reunion but for some reason his camera had moved to the RAW setting rather than its usual JPG, and the image files were extremely small and could be viewed only as thumbnails. We thought they were lost!

However, he had purchased the Nikon software at the same time as the camera, but had never installed it. He brought the CDs to me and I installed the software in a WinXP virtual machine. I then used it to open the RAW files, and it gave me the option to save in any conventional format I desired. I chose TIFF, and it created the expected huge files at maximum resolution!

Apparently their RAW format is very highly compressed but with some non-lossy algorithm, for the resulting TIFF files had more detail than do his usual JPG images. I've tried to convince him to shoot everything in RAW, but he prefers to stay with the formats he's used for years (he's a retired chief photographer for a network TV station and a member of our state's journalism hall of fame, so he's no amateur at this -- but he's not comfortable with computers)...
