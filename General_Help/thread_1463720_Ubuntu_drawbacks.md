### Post by Calash on 2010-04-27
That is a really broad question.  I am going to assume you mean in contrast to Windows.  In this case you are really looking at a few items that could be seen as "Drawbacks"


- Different installing system:  In the Windows world you would buy a CD, or go to a website and download an installer for your application.  Ubuntu does this via the Software Center.  Check there first before going any place else for your software

This is only a drawback until you get use to the system, then it is a benefit.

- Fast update cycle: Unless you stick with a LTS version, such as 10.04 that is going into release fairly soon, you are looking at a 6 month update pattern.  Think of it as getting a service pack every 6 months.

It could be seen as a drawback, but most see this as a benefit.  The rapid update cycle helps software development and getting updates to you much faster.  You also have the option of sticking with the LTS versions.  You will miss out on the in-between updates but the system will be supported and subject to fewer updates over the years.

- Different software: Open Office instead of MS Office, Firefox instead of Internet Explorer, etc etc

Not a drawback in my mind but it is probably the largest hurdle Windows users have to deal with when coming to any Linux distribution.

- Security Model:  Not as bad if you come from Vista or Windows 7 but it is still different and will take some getting use to.  Overall it provides a much more secure environment.


If you have more specific concerns let us know.  Many of us have made the transition between different operating systems and will be able to give you a good idea of what to expect.

### Post by bumanie on 2010-04-27
OK, some wireless devices are problems, but most aren't an issue. Some 3g modems can have issues as can some printers. However there are lots of documentation regarding which things work and which don't, it just takes a bit of research before purchasing. As far as wireless cards go - I have never had a problem. Recently had an issue with a 3g modem, but that is now fixed. HP provide excellent open source/linux  drivers for their line of printers. I have used digital still and digital movie cameras in linux - made the movies into home playable dvd's etc...etc...There are very few drawbacks and many advantages. Once one learns how linux works, for the most part, it is easier to use than windows, but that's provided one is willing to go on a steep learning curve initially.
