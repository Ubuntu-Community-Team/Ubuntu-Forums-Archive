### Post by kyphi on 2009-05-27
You are very lucky that you have not sustained greater damage.

On your motherboard there is a small Lithium battery that lets the BIOS function with passing on identifiers of your hardware.  The disruption to your power supply probably wiped the saved instructions and when you re-saved them the machine was able to start normally.

Do please consider getting adequate power filters and surge protectors to protect your computer equipment:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uninterruptible_power_supply](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uninterruptible_power_supply)

...and forget about the ones you can buy in the supermarket ... they will not protect your computer.
