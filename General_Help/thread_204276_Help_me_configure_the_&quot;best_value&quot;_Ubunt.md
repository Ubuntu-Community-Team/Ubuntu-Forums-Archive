### Post by PandorsBox on 2006-06-29
Pentium D 920 is the best bang for your buck if you want to overclock.  It can do everything the 805 can do, except better... and the price difference ~$55 is negligable.  The 805 is dirt cheap, but it does overlock nicely -- but it's a hot chip... hotter than the 920.  So where you can get to with watercooling the 805, you can get there with a good aircooling rig with a 920 (or damn close).

Keep in mind you're going to have to shell out the bucks for any decent socket 775 board. 

Get the 920, and go aircooled (something copper from zalman), and save your cash for when you upgrade in two years when AMD outperforms intel again and conroes are cheap.  

The real important question is to go PCI Express or stick with AGP for a while longer... guess it depends on whether you want a kickass video card or not.

If you're looking for a cheap computer that doesn't suck... AMD Athlon 64 3000+ w/abit AV8 socket 939 mobo = ~$160 total.
