### Post by LakeWind on 2008-03-18
This is more of an annoyance than a real problem. Since upgrading to Gutsy, I've noticed that the printer icon appears in the system tray constantly after printing until I log off and log back in or until I manually click on the icon and click "Hide". 

With Feisty and other previous Ubuntu versions (the ones using the gnome-cups-manager package), the printer icon would appear when sending a job to my printer, then after successfully printing the job, the icon would disappear unless a problem was encountered.

Now with the new system-config-printer program, it seems that the printer icon just stays in my system tray all the time, eliminating the easy and obvious way of knowing if the print job actually printed properly or encountered a problem.

Is there a way to force the printer icon in the system tray to disappear after the job successfully prints?
