### Post by snek on 2010-08-10
You can drag&drop whole folders into Brasero (or whatever burning app you use.. for Ubuntu it's Brasero, look under Applications->Sound & Video).

Burning files will not degrade the quality, no idea where you got that from.
The ONLY thing which could happen is that there are slight digital differences (somewhere a 1 becomes a 0 or vice-versa) while burning but you shouldn't even hear that, and should only occur if you have bad/cheap DVD's.

I wouldn't worry about it, but do realize that DVD's also go bad with age. So if you want to keep them for  a long time be sure to store them out of sunlight, away from heat sources and dust-free.

Brasero can check your burnt copy btw, so if you are paranoid enable that option.
Personally I would burn to DVD and copy all files to a 16GB memory stick, just in case.
Or borrow/buy an external HDD and copy to that as well... It will be faster copying the music back ;)
