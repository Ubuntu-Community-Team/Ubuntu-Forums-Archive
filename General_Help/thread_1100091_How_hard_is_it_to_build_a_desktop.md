### Post by Therion on 2009-03-18
> **cptrohn said:**
> I have heard it wasn't really all that hard, but I've never tried it.. I've been looking at stuff online about it tonight.  Is downloading the bios the worst part of it?
The motherboard will have a BIOS installed to boot from initially.  From there you can update it.  Different motherboard manufacturers have differing ways of updating BIOS.  Typically, if it's a "big name" like Asus or MSI, you'll have to go to their website and either download a BIOS updating utility or the BIOS update file itself and follow their install instructions.

Bear in mind though, the BIOS that comes on the board will get you through the night.  It may not be the latest and greatest version, but it WILL be there and it WILL work.

IMO, the shaky part has always been getting the Heatsink/Fan (HSF) assembly attached to/on the CPU.  HSF sockets are getting easier all the time though, but I still get a little nervous if I find myself having to apply what starts to feel like "too much" pressure to get the HSF to "seat".  That's usually just about the time it does though...

### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-03-18
Before you spend a lot of money on parts to build your new computer, see if any of your family or friends have some old computers collecting dust, and see if they'll let you take them apart.  This is particularly helpful if you have two or three old computers available to play with.  Familiarize yourself with the different parts of the computer and how they're connected.  Mix and match parts of different computers to make the biggest and best you can from the available parts, as well as one that has the bare minimum necessary parts to actually boot.  

Once you feel fairly comfortable with a pile of computer parts laying on a table in front of you, then you're ready to buy some parts and build your (first) dream machine.
