### Post by maclenin on 2010-04-10
I have created a very simple spreadsheet - using gnumeric - with small numbers and simple formulas - the numbers fit easily inside their cells - the formatting all seems in order BEFORE I try to print.

When I set up the spreadsheet for printing - and "print preview" the document, I see hash marks in place of numbers in a majority of the cells.

When I print the document, the hash marks are printed, in place of the numbers....

When I view the document under normal circumstances, for editing - the hash marks are not present. All seems well.

I cannot print the spreadsheet without the hash marks (#####) appearing!

Thanks for any guidance!

---

### Post by maclenin on 2010-04-12
I think I've just answered my own question...

It seems one needs to have the original document shown at a "zoom" level of at least 100% in order for the print preview to render / size correctly and for the document, therefore, to print the numbers properly - in place of hash marks.

Perhaps there are other things going on that I am missing, but "zoom" 100% and wee bit of table re-sizing seemed to solve the problem.

I hope this helps someone....
