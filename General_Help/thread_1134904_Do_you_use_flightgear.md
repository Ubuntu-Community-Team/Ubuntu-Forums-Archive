### Post by ushills on 2009-04-24
More of a real flight simulator than a game.  Used it extensively last year, you can set it up to start from a specific airport near you even airfields and select the plane you wish to learn to fly.

Works best with a proper flight sim joystick and is very accurate simulation,  not much fun unless you are interested in flying or learning to fly.  Landing is incredibly hard to do properly although you generally do not burst into flames.  I understand it is similar to x-simulator from Microsoft.

There are install guides in google at at flightgear site.  Download and extract a tar and the landscape for your area (US is included in native install), command line launch options set defaults (location, time of day, resolution etc).  Read the pdf manual for more information.
