### Post by ScarySquirrel on 2010-04-23
I have had this problem many times. 
The way I fixed it was by trial and error. Here's what worked: 
When you see GRUB, pick the "Repair" or "Restore" or "recovery" or whatever that option is under your regular linux option. 
It will ask you what your language is, keyboard, and a bunch of other bullshit. Eventually, you will come to a blue screen with several options outlined in a reddish rectangle in the center of your monitor. One of these options will be "restore X". Pick this, and disregard the warnings, if you dare. 

This fixed my XFCE on at least three occasions, where before my mouse had been a little x, and my desktop a blank screen. 

Sorry if my help is a bit vague, if you let me know what you see when you do the recovery mode I might be better able to remember what I did.
