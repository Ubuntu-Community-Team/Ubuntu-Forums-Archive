### Post by tacantara on 2010-02-19
Download the iso and burn it to CD...essentially, create a Live CD of Karmic and see how it responds to your system.  Karmic has come along nicely since the initial release back in October.  Just remember, even the Live CD won't be as up-to-date as an install with updates, so if the Live CD works on a general level (i.e. wireless/ethernet connection, graphics), then try installing it and looking for updates.  The kernel has been updated a few times, enhancing certain operation factors (from my experience, at least).

As always, make a copy of your home folder on an external hard drive, so you won't have to worry about losing important documents, pictures, etc.
