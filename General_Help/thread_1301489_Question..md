Next time try to be more descriptive in your posts title, that will help you to get responses. Now, when you are choosing an accounting software, check to make sure that it will accept Quicken Interchange Format files (thats what the .qif stands for I imagine). So, check with the software company or web site first and even ask the software maker questions, such as, will I be able to accurately import my .qif files from x version of Quickbooks (or whatever Intuit program you exported it from). Yes, I know it is simply exported in ASCII format, but that doesn't mean that any program that will import ASCII will import .qif files.

By The Way: The other poster was pointing out that your post title needed to be more descriptive, hence he just wrote answer, since your title is just "question". Your title should have been something like "having trouble importing .qif files into software" , then people with accounting software knowledge may see it and read it to see if they can offer some assistance. When your title is "question", most people won't even bother reading your post.

Qif is not actually a format. It is a pile of data structures which changed from year to year, and from program to program. So, depending on what application you used to generate this file, you may have to make some manual adjustment.

If you contact the KMyMoney mailing list, perhaps we can help you. The about dialog has the contact info.
