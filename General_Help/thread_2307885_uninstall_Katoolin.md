### Post by DuckHook on 2015-12-29
The only legitimate use of Kali I am familiar with is that of security experts involved in penetration testing. Otherwise, I am under the impression (perhaps mistaken) that it is the province of shady crackers using it to do bad stuff. I am happy to be corrected.

It would not surprise me if a package like Katoolin breaks all sorts of stuff in Ubuntu. It is, after all, designed to break stuff.

I would suggest that you back up all important data and reinstall. Then stay far far away from anything not in the software center. You are starting to see what constitutes good security practices in Linux, the culture of which is to refrain from installing anything and everything from the crazy unknown world out there. You may wish to study these basic security primers to get into good Linux habits from the start:
