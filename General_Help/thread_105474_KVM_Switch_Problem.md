### Post by BathroomNinja on 2005-12-19
Sounds like the problem is with the KVM.  However, try this.  After you do the scroll lock-scroll lock -M, press spacebar on the keyboard a few times, *then* try the mouse.  Does that do anything?  Also, try the numlock key.  Does the light on the keyboard change?

### Post by seasteve on 2005-12-23
I think you will find this is a "Hardware" issue. I us a mechanical KVM switch (I have to push one of 4 buttons) to switch beteen my XP box, my Ubuntu box, and other PC's.  I use a machanical KVM because it works independent of the OS.

Any time one of the PCs is booting up I have to ensure THAT PC is selected on the KVM so it will load the correct keyboard/mouse drivers.  Also, if I switch from a machine without a INcorrectlly loaded keyboard/mouse driver to any another PC, the keyboard/mouse dosn't work on that second PC.  This problem happens to any PC with any OS.

I've learned to be carefull.
