### Post by justin_guerin on 2007-11-11
Rsync is cross platform, actually.  Google for rsync windows, and you should see plenty of pages with the general idea.  Here's a couple to get your started:

[http://optics.ph.unimelb.edu.au/help/rsync/rsync_pc1.html](http://optics.ph.unimelb.edu.au/help/rsync/rsync_pc1.html)
[http://brentnorris.net/rsyncntdoc.html](http://brentnorris.net/rsyncntdoc.html)

However, if you'd like something else that's cross platform, there's always Unison.  Their home page is [http://www.cis.upenn.edu/~bcpierce/unison/](http://www.cis.upenn.edu/~bcpierce/unison/).  The Wikipedia page has some links to advice on using Unison at the bottom of the page: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unison_%28file_synchronizer%29](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unison_%28file_synchronizer%29).  Don't let the fact that Unison isn't under active development scare you off.  I've used it, and it works quite well.

Good luck,
Justin
