### Post by col48 on 2013-09-25
If by "everything" you just mean your data, copy to an external medium (USB stick, Dropbox, DVD, whatever), switch HDD then copy back.
If you are talking software, better to reinstall cleanly from repositories in the 12.04 context.  That way, the Update Manager will be able to do its job, which is important for your security.

### Post by spacesamurai2 on 2013-09-25
I have been a fan of using the Ubuntu Backup Tool, also know as Deja Dup, to save required files to a remote disk.

Then you can just restore your files from any other cilent with the same tool :)
