### Post by Autodave on 2010-06-05
> **KarinBee said:**
> Yes! He has that.  Now I'm not sure what I'm looking for but what should the settings read?

 is it worth getting him yo update drivers?

You may have to update drivers.  And that will not be easy for you to do while trying to walk him through it by phone!

If he can get to MONITORS, see if he can pick a resolution of at least 1,024 wide.  That should enable him to see everything on the web page.  If that is not possible, he may have to lower the number of COLORS he can see in order to free up some video memory to use a higher resolution.

Again, this is going to be rather difficult for you to do over the phone.......

You might also tell him that when he chooses a higher resolution (if he can actually do that), a screen will pop-up as soon as he selects it.  This screen will give him the option to either save the new setting or go back to the previous one in case the screen doesn't look right.

If the above post doesn't help or he cannot chose a higher resolution, you will have to find out what video card he has in the machine.  He will need to download (SYSTEM > ADMINISTRATION > SYNAPTIC PACKAGE MANAGER) search for this file: gnome-device-manager.  Have him install that and then you use that program to identify the video card in the machine.
