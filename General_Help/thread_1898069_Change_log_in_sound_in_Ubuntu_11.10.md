### Post by Canaryguy on 2011-12-20
Hello,

Is there a way of changing the login sound in Ubuntu 11.10?
I've found out that you can change the sound if you go to **/usr/share/sounds/ubuntu/stereo** and then you just have to substitute desktop-login.ogg by the sound that you prefer that should have also the name desktop-login.ogg.

But is there also a way to just deactivate the sound not just changing it?

---

### Post by Canaryguy on 2011-12-20
I have just found out that you can deactivate the log in sound if you go to Startup Applications and removing the tick on GNOME Login Sound. 

This option was not here some time ago (at least on my computer) Maybe it was included after the Ubuntu updates.

### Post by lechien73 on 2011-12-20
**Edit:** Hmmm...I see we type at the same speed :D

On my *Oneiric* install, go to the Shutdown & Settings Menu (the little cog in the top right), choose **Startup Applications** and uncheck **Gnome Login Sound**. That prevents it from running on startup.
