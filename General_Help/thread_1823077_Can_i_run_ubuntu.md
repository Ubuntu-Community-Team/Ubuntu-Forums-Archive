### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-08-11
The main problem is the amount of RAM. For Ubuntu to work you need at least 500 MB.

I would suggest installing a lighter derivate of Ubuntu like Lubuntu or Mint LMDE Edition.

You could also try puppy (not ubuntu based), that's really fast.

You could indeed also solve it by upgrading the RAM.

@drpjkurian: I believe Lubuntu is still lighter than Xubuntu in RAM usage. But you have to sacrifice some customisability for it.

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-08-11
On this hardware I would try Ubuntu 10.04 LTS (I think it is a bit lighter) or Xubuntu (10.04 or 11.04). The XFCE environment is considerably lighter than Gnome, although you will probably lose some functions (especially on the 10.04 version).

You have two big problems, RAM is one of them and increasing the RAM will probably work well even with your current Ubuntu 11.04 (no Unity of course). The second big problem is the GPU, but I am assuming this one would be harder to upgrade.
