Lemme explain, I use notebooks most of the time, but even otherwise, I love the shallow key presses of laptops and their flat smooth heights. I tend to scoot my fingertips around the keyboard and that is only possible with a laptop. Furthermore, they make less noise. 

With normal dekstop keyboard, they make far too much noise, are too clickety (I hate the Das keyboard: [http://techreport.com/articles.x/16138](http://techreport.com/articles.x/16138) which some people consider the epitome of keyboard design). So clickety, clunky and noisy but my main gripe is a functionality issue, you can't scoot your fingers around because the keys are not flat and the rows all have different heights. So if I try to scoot from the bottom ZXCVBNM row to the top QWERTY row, my fingers hit the middle ASDFG row keys on the surface facing me (as opposed to the top face which is the one we press).
Maybe I have my typing technique all wrong but I love that you dont have to press much on laptops and hate that you have to press a lot more on normal keyboards.

I might add that I do realise that some notebook keyboards are atrocious but Lenovo, Toshiba and HP make some very decent ones. Lenovo especially. What about Sony Vaios? What are their keys like?

What do you prefer? Low profile or normal profile?
