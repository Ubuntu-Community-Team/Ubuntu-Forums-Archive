### Post by agillator on 2011-12-05
The above answers are correct. However, I suspect we are not understanding what you really want to do. Be a little more descriptive and specific, please. You can, for example, create a group and control the membership on a computer. Then you set the permissions on the necessary directories/files so that only members of that group (and root) have access. This is on one machine, of course. As to the network question, there are several ways to do that depending upon what you really want to do: secure shell, simply give access to files, etc.
