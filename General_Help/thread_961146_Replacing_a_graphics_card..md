### Post by beno1990 on 2008-10-28
No; many graphics cards which come with a CD are compatible with Ubuntu if you use drivers other than the Windows ones which typically come on the CD, but some won't be.

You need to see if an individual model will be compatible with Linux or not.

### Post by jespdj on 2008-10-28
There are three main graphics card manufacturers: Intel, nVidia and ATI.

Historically, ATI has the worst reputation with regards to Linux drivers. Two years ago or so they promised to improve their Linux drivers, but I don't know if their current drivers are that good.

Intel has good driver support, but Intel graphics cards are not as powerful as nVidia or ATI graphics cards.

So, if you want a reasonably powerful graphics card that works well on Ubuntu, nVidia is probably your best bet. They have drivers that work well.

You don't need to get the drivers on a CD that comes with the graphics card; you can just download the drivers from Internet. And Ubuntu itself has drivers for many graphics cards; you'd have to select System / Administration / Hardware Drivers to activate the driver and that's all.
