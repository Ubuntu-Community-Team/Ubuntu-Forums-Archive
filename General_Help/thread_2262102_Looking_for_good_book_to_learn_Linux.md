### Post by Topsiho on 2015-01-23
Hello,

On internet you may find many tutorials on bash, the most common shell (the program that interprets command line commands, and executes them).
Googling for them with "bash command line tutorial" (without the quotes), shows you many sites immediately, the first one (for me) is

[http://ryanstutorials.net/linuxtutorial/](http://ryanstutorials.net/linuxtutorial/)

which looks promising (at a glance) to me.

The basics are not difficult at all, though this depends on how deep you want to get into it.

I find that I can make do quite well with a rather limited subset of the most common commands, forgetting the other ones that I almost never need.
But in such a case, if you need it, but forgot, you can find it in the man pages, in an online tutorial, or whatever.

Knowledge you have of the command line does not depend on the distribution, and almost never gets obsolete, though sometimes a particular command may become deprecated.

Being a middle aged man means that you are probably some 30 years younger than I am, so it's quite worthwile for you to learn something new :)

Learning about Ubuntu and Debian: just google (or duckduckgo) for it, and you'll find tons of information.

Succes,

Topsiho
