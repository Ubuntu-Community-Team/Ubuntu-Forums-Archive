### Post by palteater on 2019-05-25
I am not a programmer, I have no clue how the bug tracking database works, nor do I have an inclination to haul myself over arcane database thresholds just to be able to contribute with suggestions once in a while, and I bet I am not unique in this regard.

On the other side, I fully understand that developers do not wish to be flooded by a biblical tide of complaints and bellyaching, ranging from moronic to P=NP hard impossibilities.

I also understand that the exchange balance here is seriously askew. I contribute by telling that something is lacking, I wish to spend as little effort as possible on getting it solved, and the solution is valuable to me. Developers spend a lot of effort fixing problems, and generally get nothing in return save for the vague satisfaction of making their thing work better. As such, I realize that my error reporting is pennies to their golden krugerrands.

The information only goes in one direction.

My question can be condensed from these ramblings into: what paths are there for the information to go the other direction? Are bug-tracking databases the *only *way to reach developers? Am I missing something that already exists? Is the world missing something that does not exist? 

In that case, can we make something? Like offer incentives? A bounty system, like Kickstarter for software developers? Or will that only encourage them to add more bugs, to earn more prizes?

### Post by PaulW2U on 2019-05-25
palteater, these are user forums and although Ubuntu developers do visit from time to time they generally don't make their presence known or answer questions from Ubuntu users. Most Ubuntu developers are paid by Canonical. Ubuntu [flavour]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuFlavors") developers are in the main community members and not paid employees, although there are or have been a couple of exceptions over the years. I doubt if a "bounty" system is of interest to any of them.

[https://lists.ubuntu.com/mailman/listinfo/Ubuntu-devel-discuss](https://lists.ubuntu.com/mailman/listinfo/Ubuntu-devel-discuss) and [https://discourse.ubuntu.com/](https://discourse.ubuntu.com/) are mediums where users can talk to developers but don't be surprised if you don't get a reply from your intended audience.

Bugs, and indeed "wishes" for changes to existing configurations can be reported to Launchpad, see [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ReportingBugs](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ReportingBugs) for further information on the process. However, a developer's time is limited and unfortunately there are far more bug reports than can be handled by those tasked to deal with them.

Also, don't forget that Ubuntu is not an operating system but a distribution of software obtained from many other sources. Many of the bugs, changes or enhancements need to be reported directly upstream to the developers of the software such as the [Linux kernel]("https://www.kernel.org/"), [GNOME]("https://www.gnome.org/"), [Mozilla]("https://www.mozilla.org/"). [LibreOffice]("https://www.libreoffice.org/"), etc.
