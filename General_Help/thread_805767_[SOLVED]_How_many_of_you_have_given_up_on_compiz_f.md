bumping up your on board memory will help. Usually the Nvidia will share the onboard memory regardless. Say for example you have a 128 video card, the onboard memory will give up 128 to support the video card. So you might very will likely be trying to run your computer on just a little over 300mb even on window XP this is very low and is a contributing factor for your slow OS. Vista needs a minimum of 1G to function and THATs pushing it.:(  One of the cheapest upgrade you can do is upgrade that memory. Try "Newegg" you can pick up two 1G sticks for about 49 to 59 bucks. This will help you quite a bit. :)  

Personal note:
Both my Nvidia cards are 256MB, the minimum requirement for most video games that are hitting the shelf. 512 is getting to be the new minimum in a big way. 

Example: running Crysis(@ medium video level) on Vista64 takes almost 3G sometime it peaks a little above 3G!!
