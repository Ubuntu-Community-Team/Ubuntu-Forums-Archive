### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2015-01-16
I think I would agree with marseille 2 on this one. If you definitely want one of the 'buntus, go for Lubuntu, with LXDE. 

But my personal recommendation would be to go for one of the Puppies, without a shadow of doubt. The entire O/S runs in RAM (it's decompressed into RAM from the .sfs file format that Puppy uses), and uses about 200 MB on the current release, 'TahrPup' 6.0. I've been using this for about 2 1/2 months now, and (I almost feel guilty for saying this), it's been getting MUCH more use than my 'buntu installs!

The 'Quickpet' feature makes installing a selected range of apps a doddle; these have been carefully optimized to work with Puppy. You'll be surprised just HOW fast the Puppies really are; they were originally developed to run from a wide range of removable media.....including floppy disks, and the old-fashioned 'ZIP' drives. Many people are unaware of how fast RAM is, as a working medium; since it's all in RAM already, there's no running back-and-forth to the hard drive...and Puppy doesn't do any writing to the hard drive until the very end of your session, when changes are written to a personal 'save-file'. Boot time on my Compaq desktop is in the order of 20 seconds or so.

If you're interested, download the .iso from here:-

[http://distro.ibiblio.org/puppylinux/puppy-tahr/iso/tahrpup%20-6.0-CE/](http://distro.ibiblio.org/puppylinux/puppy-tahr/iso/tahrpup%20-6.0-CE/)

You will want the 5th entry in the list; 'tahr-6.0-CE_PAE.iso. Dowload and burn in the usual way, and you can use it exactly like a LiveCD.

My desktop is around 10 yrs old, and runs 'Tahrpup' from a frugal install on the HDD like greased lightning.....it REALLY is that quick! I also have it installed in an elderly Dell Inspiron laptop, running a P4 @ 2.6 GHz. Works nicely. 

Try it, and ENJOY.


Regards,

Mike. ;)

BTW; I'm currently writing this in 'Tahrpup', installed to a flash drive, on a mate's machine.....
