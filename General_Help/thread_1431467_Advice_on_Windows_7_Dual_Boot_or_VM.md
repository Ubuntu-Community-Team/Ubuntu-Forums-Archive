### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-03-17
I have a slightly older model Tablet PC (t4020) and Win7 works great on it.  Full set of tablet functions, auto-rotation, bezel keys, AND hibernation!

However, I'm using it in traditional dual-boot mode, not with a virtual machine.  So, sorry, can't help you there.

If you create an NTFS partition on the drive for the Win7 install, you won't have to deal with shrinking it later.

Also, as soon as you get Win7 working to your satisfaction, first thing to then do is create and burn the Win7 Repair CD.  That could come in handy later.
