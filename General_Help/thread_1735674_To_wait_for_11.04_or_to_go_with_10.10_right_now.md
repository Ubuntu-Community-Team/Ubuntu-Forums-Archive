### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-04-22
In general, if you're knew to Linux or Ubuntu, you should stay away from pre-release versions.  They are incomplete, they are buggy, and they will give you a BAD first impression of Ubuntu.

Also, from experience, I would not rush into upgrading to 11.01 the day it gets released.  Read the forums, especially looking for 11.04-related issues.  It generally takes a couple of months for the early bugs to get identified and worked out.

Realize that an upgrade is a one-way street.  There is no easy way to roll-back to the previous version.  So, don't be in a hurry to jump into a new release.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-04-22
I would advise you to jump straight to 11.04.

The people who say "Don't, it's still beta" are forgetting two things: (a) It's only a couple of days out from release, and (b) It's already stable.

I'd also advise using the Unity shell for Gnome (which is the default on Ubuntu 11.04). When Ubuntu 11.10 comes out, you won't be able to use the old "classic" Gnome desktop, so why bother learning it now? Unity is the way Ubuntu is going to be: And it's not "dumbed-down" at all.

If you install 10.10 or 10.04 you'll have old software and you'll sooner or later want to update, so save yourself some time and hassle and just get the later one to begin with. Learning Ubuntu 10.10 is really not the best use of your time when there are big changes in 11.04 that will stretch into the future.
