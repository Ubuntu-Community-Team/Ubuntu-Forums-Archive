### Post by kag on 2008-12-22
I've ditched Windows completely almost two years ago, and I've been using Ubuntu exclusively since. Generally, the experience has been extremely positive. However after 24+ months of use, I have come with a few points that really annoy me.

[LIST=1]
[*]On each reboot, I have to go in System / Appearance / Assistive Technologies, click on Keyboard Accessibility, go in the Mouse Keys tab and *uncheck* Pointer can be controlled using keypad.


[*]There's a very slight delay before a click is registered. For example when I want to move a window, I will position the cursor on the titlebar and rapidly click + drag the window. 50% of the time, the window will not follow the cursor. Same thing happens when I attempt to move an icon on the desktop. Same thing happens when I attempt to resize a window.

I will agree that I have an old machine (Athlon XP 2500+, 1.5GB RAM, Geforce4 4600 w/128MB), but it doesn't seem like an intensive task to me. I never had that problem before in Windows on the same machine.


[*]I'm still having problems with PulseAudio. Everything but a USB DAC is set as PulseAudio in Sound Preferences. The USB DAC is set to "Burr-Brown from TI (OSS)" (ALSA doesn't work and there's no PulseAudio) in the "Music and Movies" section. So I cannot simultaneously play music from two sources to my headphones in the USB DAC.


[*]The default cursors are too "thick". For example if you try to highlight a small portion of the word "illicit", the "text cursor" is so thick that you're never sure on which side of a narrow letter you are. I think it's 3 pixels wide (black-white-black), where the equivalent Windows cursor is 1 pixel wide.


[*]I don't know if it's just me, but the programs that I keep open most of the time (Rhythmbox, Nautilus, Terminal and gedit) all have icons with similar hue. I find it hard to quickly distinguish Nautilus from Rhythmbox from gedit.


[*]In addition to this, the icons all look more pale when they are minimized, which makes it even more difficult to instinctively locate the wanted item. I have to "analyze" the icon.


[*]Last but not least, I feel like there's not enough contrast in the taskbar between the active window item and the inactive ones. I find it difficult to quickly locate the active one.
[/LIST]

Does anyone have a solution for #1, #2 and #3?
Does anyone else share the same feeling on the other points, or am I just being too picky here?

### Post by greyfox1 on 2008-12-22
> **kag said:**
>  That way I'm not loosing an hour to get things back like they were after each time I reinstall.

JUST an hour?  Man, you're quick... :P

Honestly though I hear you.  I think you'd be better off mounting a separate position as your home folder (one tutorial can be found [here]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome")).  That way you can re-install Ubuntu without losing any of your settings or themes.  Heck, you could even try out another distro and mount your home folder there for an easy migration!  It is a bit of an undertaking the first time around, but well worth it.

As far as your last issue, I do agree with you.  I know you aren't particularly interested in customizing things, but I think the best solution to this issue (other than convincing theme artists to fix it for you) is using [Gnome Color Chooser]("http://gnomecc.sourceforge.net/").  You can use it to change, among other things, the colors of the window buttons in the panel.  Hope this helps!
