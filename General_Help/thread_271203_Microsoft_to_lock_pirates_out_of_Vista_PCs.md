### Post by handy on 2006-10-05
M$ have a growing black list of ID numbers of frequently pirated XP installation disks.  

These disks are usually corporate install disks that don't require M$ activation over the internet or via phone. They come with licenses for sometimes 100's of installations.

Over approx' the last year the windoze updates have been installing software that checks your XP installation against their list, & if it is on the list, then you are notified that it may not be legal & that you should contact your sys' admin' to verify that it is, or call M$ & talk to them about it.

After a few months of being told this everytime you boot up, it then went to the next level, via windoze update, that says that you must contact M$ & pay them for a legitimate licence.

You can often get around this by using System Restore to take the XP installation back to a time before the windoze security update was installed, & then turn off windoze update in System Properties.

So, it looks like M$ have been implementing steps re: **[Trusted Computing]("http://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~rja14/tcpa-faq.html")** (*Treacherous Computing*) insidiously, over the internet to XP users for quite some time now... :(

& all this has been pre vista!

The next step is to stop your machine from being able to use the unlicenced software, which will work I expect the same way as it would if you don't activate your installation within 30 days...
