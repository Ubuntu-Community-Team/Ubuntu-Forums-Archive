### Post by kanikilu on 2009-05-01
My G1 works just fine in Ubuntu. Although, most everything is done from the phone. Updates come OTA, apps are installed and downloaded in the Market app on the phone, contacts come from Gmail, etc. The only times I connect it to my computer are to charge it and to transfer music and pictures to/from the SD card.

// Edit - Actually, I did update the firmware to RC33 manually since I was impatient waiting for it to come OTA, so it can definitely be done ;)

### Post by kanikilu on 2009-05-01
> **crispeto said:**
> Cool that's exactly what I wanted to know and hear. Thanks for your reply. How do you like your G1? I came from a Blackberry, so it's different. I had to buy an application for it let me connect to my Exchange email at work. But all-in-all, I think it's a cool little device (despite my iPhone-toting brother's teasing about it's aesthetics :P )

Physically, I haven't had any of the squeaking/creaking problems that some have had. It's a bit bulkier than I expected, but the large screen makes up for it.

In terms of the software/OS, I haven't resorted to rooting it, but I think I'll like it better when the latest firmware (cupcake) arrives ;) There are a few annoyances that are supposedly fixed in it.

As a device, my only complaint so far is battery life. Depending on what I'm doing and what services I'm using (GPS, WiFi, G3, etc.) it can drain pretty fast - I once had to do a full charge twice in a day.
