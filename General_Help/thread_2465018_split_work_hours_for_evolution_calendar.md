### Post by TheFu on 2021-07-19
Two different accounts?  I've never met 1 person who actually worked 24-hrs a day.
Maybe two different calendars?

I don't use Evolution, but I do use Thunderbird and it supports having multiple calendars. I'm using 6 now. Whether this will actually work or not depends on whether the defaults are stored with the account or with the calendar.  

Long ago, when I was forced to use a specific, non-Linux, mail/calendar program, The way I prevented others from scheduling meetings at certain times of the day was to mark those as busy-private and setup an auto-responder to reject any meetings that didn't go into free times.  Because we could all see free/busy times on each other's calendar (across 65K employees), the person scheduling the meeting would need to decide whether I was THAT important for their meeting or not.  For the first 1-3 months of a project, I was critical, but after that, not so much. Good communications was the key.
So, you could setup work from midnight until 6pm, but mark the 2am-noon time as busy-private time. I suspect the Midnight - 2am isn't for daily work, just special situations?  I was for me - it was usually 10pm - 5am on Fri and Sat night for change management stuff. I would be there for a very specific reason - along with all the other people working that effort. Fortunately, since 2000, I only had to do that about 5 times, but in the 1990s, I was on-call 24/7/365 for a few years and unable to leave my part of the metro area due to the job requirements.
