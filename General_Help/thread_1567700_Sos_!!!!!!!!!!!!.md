### Post by coffeecat on 2010-09-04
It looks as though you have set your application font to Dingbats. Why did you do that?

I'm not sure this will work, because you won't be able to read the font in the  Appearance window and it will be difficult to navigate around, but let's give this a go. Follow my directions carefully!

Right-click on the desktop and select "change desktop background". If you can't read the right-click menu, that's the bottom line. Click on the fonts tab - you probably won't be able to read this - it's the third from the left. The top bar in that tab is the Application font bar - again you probably won't be able to read this. Click on it and a 'pick a font' window will open (which you still won't be able to read :p). Since you won't be able to read the names of the fonts, highlight one at random in the left pane, and click on OK. (That's the button you can't read at bottom right of this window. ;))

If the font changes but you still get something unreadable, simply try others at random until you get a normal font. Now change it to the one you prefer. I would suggest Sans would be a good choice.
