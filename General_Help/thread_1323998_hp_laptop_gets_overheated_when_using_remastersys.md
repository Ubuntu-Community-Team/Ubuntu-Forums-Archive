### Post by JHan816 on 2009-11-12
Make sure the cooling vents on the bottom and side are clean. Dust can build up and block these vents and cause overheating. Try cleaning on the outside first using some compressed air or brushes. 
 
I own a different laptop (acer). I recently had overheating shutdown. I opened it up cleaned a lot of dust around the cooling fan and heat sink and it solved the problem.
 
Opening the laptop case can be difficult to do.
