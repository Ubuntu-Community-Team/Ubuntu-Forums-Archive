### Post by Habitual on 2013-01-04
[QUOTE=]....there is some kind of security issue they are trying to resolve. Along with "Hynix memory parameters".[/QUOTE]

unless the "Hynix memory parameters" is a firmware update to allow more RAM, (say your BIOS only sees 3G RAM, this update gives you the capacity for 32G), then I'd install it. Fixing a BSOD you haven't experienced isn't a fix. ;)

I tend to distrust "security issue" fixes from a Windows-centric mentality.

You could alternatively, save your current BIOS settings in CMOS to a saved profile and run the update. 

All I am saying is some discretion may need to be used.

### Post by Pjotr123 on 2013-01-04
A BIOS update isn't likely to break your dual boot, as it doesn't touch the hard drive. It only touches a memory chip on your motherboard.

Indeed there are risks attached to flashing your BIOS, but they aren't big, provided that you proceed with care and follow the directions of your manufacturer. I've flashed BIOS's hundreds of times on many different computers, in the course of 10 years. Only once a motherboard was bricked because of it.

I like flashing a BIOS; it gives me the feeling that I have brand new hardware after the flash. :)
