### Post by Brandon Williams on 2009-11-26
Use Ctrl+Alt+F1 to switch to a console, login there, and check in ~/.xsession-errors. Is there anything in that file indicating a serious problem? If you're not sure, you might want to just post the full content of the file here.

I had a similar problem with Xubuntu that was solved by deleting everything under ~/.config/ and then reconfiguring after login. For standard Ubuntu, the equivalent would be deleting everything under ~/.gconf/. This is a pretty severe step and not even guaranteed to work, so be sure to check in ~/.xsession-errors first.
