### Post by steveneddy on 2008-03-07
Uh...hmmm...Let's start here.

Linux has four desktops by default.

A cube is four sides.

Therefor, the cube will show all four desktops on four different sides.

You can change this from two to 64 (I think) but somewhere between 4-6 desktops, or sides to the cube, look and act the best.

In Compiz, all four sides are actually one desktop split four ways, kinda.

Hope this helps.

### Post by jespdj on 2008-03-07
Maybe this does what you want:

On a window, right-click on the small icon in the top-left of the window. You'll see a menu with Minimize, Maxmize etc. Select "Always on Visible Workspace" there.

If you now use the cube, you'll see that window on all sides of the cube.
