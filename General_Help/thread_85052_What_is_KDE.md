### Post by canadianwriterman on 2005-11-01
[QUOTE=iAlta]:confused: What is KDE,  and how is it diffrent from GNOME?:confused:  And what is GNOME?:confused:[/QUOTE]

Hi, and welcome to the sometimes confusing world of Linux. Linux, you'll find, is all about choices. One of the choices you have is what kind of desktop you can use.

The two major desktops available are KDE and GNOME. Essentially, there is a difference in the "look and feel." Which one people prefer is a matter of personal choice. You'll also find that there are applications specifically designed to integrate with each of these desktops (however, you can usually download and install any KDE or GNOME application and they'll work just as well with eachother.

You can find out more and have a preview of the look and feel at:

[www.kde.org](www.kde.org)
[www.gnome.org](www.gnome.org)

You probably know that Ubuntu comes with the GNOME desktop standard, but you can also install Kubuntu, which uses KDE. Hope that's helpful
