### Post by kidders on 2008-01-31
Yikes ... It must've taken you ages to put that last post together!

Basically, I was looking for (well ... almost *hoping* for) a glaring, obvious problem, but there doesn't seem to be one.

**RAM**
A gig of RAM is a little low, but your system doesn't seem to be swapping very much, so I don't see that as a big problem.

**Unnecessary extended partition**
Again, that's not a major issue. In theory, it means that your system has to do a redundant seek, every time it wants to find something on your disk, but in practice, it doesn't make a huge difference.

**Xorg**
Your X server seems to be using Nvidia's kernel module. While you *may *be able to optimise its configuration a bit, depending on what card you've got, you certainly haven't done anything ridiculous that would account for your performance issues.


One of the things that often creates a performance bottleneck is I/O. I'm curious about whether the 2%wa figure in your "top" screenshot is a typical value.


Anyhow, to answer your questions...

> could the extended and ntfs partitions be slowing me down?Possibly. It's generally best to stick with filesystems that perform well. If your use of NTFS was causing a problem, you would notice your %wa time increasing, and the filesystem I/O helper processes using more of your CPU, while your system waits for read/write operations to complete. Using logical partitions instead of "real" ones carries a theoretical performance penalty ... but I'd be surprised if it was perceptible, to be honest.

> i dont really know how to stop the x server and then be able to check cpu loadDropping to a text console and typing** sudo /etc/init.d/gdm stop **ought to do the trick. You could then run **top** and see what happens.

> i dont think ive modified my kernel.If you'd done that, you'd definitely know it hehe.


From what you've posted, the impression I get is that there are a few things you *could* have done better, when setting up your box, to get the most out of it. The burning question, I suppose, is whether a long list of very minor performance tweaks would combine to make a noticeable improvement. I'm skeptical. The best advice I can think of at this stage is to try to identify any bottlenecks. For instance...
[LIST]
[*]Perhaps something you're running is buggy, and using up far more CPU time than it should.
[*]The problem could be I/O related. Operations like paging or reading files off your hard disks might be taking longer than would be nice.
[*]Are you making unrealistic demands of your PC? Perhaps you might be running lots and lots of desktop widgety gizmos that're simply leaving no CPU time for anything else.[/LIST]
