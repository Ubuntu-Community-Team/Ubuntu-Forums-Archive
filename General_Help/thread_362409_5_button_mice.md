### Post by kdub432 on 2007-02-15
I have an 11 button mouse, and getting it to work was tricky.

You might want to open up "System>Preferences> keyboard shortcuts" and select an option(like play/pause) and click a button on your mouse. If it changes to something (usually a hex number like 0xa6) you're in luck, cause that means that your mouse already has support in the kernel. If it doesnt do anything(which mine didnt) you can try searching google for a driver, and then installing it. However, you'll have to recompile your kernel, and its a tricky process at best. I was lucky enough to have found a driver for my logitech mouse, but if you're mouse doesnt have any specific drivers, then you're out of luck (unless you know how to write linux drivers that is.....)
