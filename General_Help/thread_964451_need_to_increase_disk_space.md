### Post by Zack McCool on 2008-10-30
What do you mean by "installed inside Windows"?  Did you use a Virtual Machine (VMWare or VirtualBox) or did you use Wubi?

If it was Wubi, I don't think there will be any way to increase it, though I am not an expert with it, as I've never used it, just did a little light reading.

If it is a VM, you could add another virtual disk (with the file for it stored on your 2nd drive) and mount that in your install.

The best bet, really, is to do a straight dual-boot, where you can install Ubuntu in whatever space(s) you can come up with...
