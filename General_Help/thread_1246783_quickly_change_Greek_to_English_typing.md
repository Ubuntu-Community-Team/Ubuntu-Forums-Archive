### Post by lazylew on 2009-08-22
When I used winXP before, there was a very easy fast way to switch between languages - is there a way in Ubuntu as well?

Say I want to type a message in Greek on a greek Ubuntu forum, and immediately after that switch to typing English again. 

Please keep your answer in English, because I'm just a beginner with Greek (2yrs evening classes :))

---

### Post by Icebear on 2009-08-22
go System>preferences>Keyboard

then click layout options

click on the +add button
and choose Greek and the variant you want

then click the other layout options button

then hit key(s) to change layout

and choose what best for you

---

### Post by P4man on 2009-08-22
you can also add "Keyboard Indicator" to your gnome panel (right click, add, and select it). Its not only an indicator, if you click it it switches kb layouts.
