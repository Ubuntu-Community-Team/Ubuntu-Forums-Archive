### Post by RavenOfOdin on 2006-05-08
At worst, your ISP will have absolutely no support for Ubuntu, unofficial or otherwise.  I draw a distinction between the two because I'm running Comcast HSI and their software wasn't mentioned as able to be used by Linux.  

At best, you'll have a brand new connection.

I'd go with BellSouth if I were you because they have a good track record. I've used their service multiple times on other platforms and IMO they're worth the money to plunk down.  I don't know much about Time Warner, though.

### Post by rmaple on 2006-05-08
I been using Time Warner Roadrunner for about 3 years now, with few problems.  I have the cable modem plugged into a router, with both the wife's  XP box and my Ubuntu (formerly fedora core 1) box connected.  I've never installed the "roadrunner" software on any of my machines, and I've only contacted tech support once or twice for problems outside the OS, so I can't comment on their linux support.  Overall I have been very happy.  (WAY better than dial up!)
