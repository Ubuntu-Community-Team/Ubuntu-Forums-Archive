### Post by The Cog on 2012-10-06
I don't think it's possible to downgrade - you would have to reinstall.

Lack of updates means that newly discovered security flaws will not get fixed. Personally I don't think this is that much of an issue for personal PCs where you don't get remote users, but it certainly is if you are running services such as web or ssh where remote users could connect and try to hack you. You are better off sticking with one of the Long Term Support (LTS) versions. 12.04 is an LTS version.

If recent Ubuntu is too much for your PC, it would be worth trying a less demanding GUI such as XFCE (Xubuntu) or LXDE (Lubuntu).

### Post by Lars Noodén on 2012-10-06
In addition to Lubuntu or Xubuntu there is also the option of dropping the desktop environment completely and just running with a window manager.  That would require even less RAM and CPU.  You could use Openbox, Fluxbox, Metacity or FVWM to name a few.  Plain FVWM might be a bit plain, so you could look at FVWM-crystal instead.
