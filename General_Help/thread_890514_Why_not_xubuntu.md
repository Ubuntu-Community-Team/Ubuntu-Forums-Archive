You don't expect people to be using an antique type of PC nowadays especially that prices of "high-speed" PC's became affordable. So, to keep track with this high-speed PC's, they tend to lean on GNOME/KDE as their standard desktop environment. XFCE would just be an option for those old-aged PC's/laptops where hardware upgrade could be impossible due to lack of support or had already been faced-out.

### Post by fiddler616 on 2008-08-15
Most people are fine with running GNOME. IMHO, GNOME also has the nicest combo of features and intuitiveness (I tried KDE, and was ready to bash my face against a wall). However, Linux is all about choices, and some people prefer KDE, and have it available. Other people prefer the extra edge in speed, or have older computers, and are willing to make small sacrifices because of it (I tried Xfce too, and loved it, but lamented it's lack of bundled apps), and therefore can take the extra step to use Xubuntu.
But for people who only have enough knowledge for the default choice, GNOME is probably the easiest to get started with.
...Just my opinion
