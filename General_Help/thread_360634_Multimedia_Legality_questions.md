### Post by igknighted on 2007-02-13
The way I understand it:

Internet stream ripping: legal if the music ripped is not copyrighted, illegal if it is.

DVD copying: Legal if not distributing.  The day I cannot backup a DVD I payed for is the day I lose faith in humanity.  How is this different than taking your own CDs, ripping them to your computer as security, and possibly burning them so you can carry the burned copy and store the original safely, JIC.  Distribution of these, however, would be illegal.

RealPlayer: Perfectly legal, they distribute their own codecs only IIRC.

Win32: Legal for you as an end user (regardless of whether or not you own windows I believe) to download and use these, but illegal to redistribute.  Ergo, you cannot distribute them with linux, but if the users download them, thats OK.

mp3: Illegal to distribute, but legal to download and use.

I could be wrong, if I am please correct me because I really want to know.

EDIT: libdvdcss, required for DVD playback, IS illegal for use in the US, as it was hacked from some copyrighted program or something silly.  If you download linDVD (a non-opensource program from Mandriva), however, you can get legal DVD playback (I think, you might need to actually need to buy Mandriva's box set for this to be true).
