### Post by steveneddy on 2011-02-06
Formatting a hard drive means to erase all of the info and make it ready to install data or an operating system.

Unmounting means to the computer "Do not use or look at this hard drive" - if you mount the drive you can use it, but since you formatted the drive you are now ready to reinstall Ubuntu becuase look like windows is gone.

There are recover programs out there and someone will chime in to help you recover your files most likely.

### Post by JC Cheloven on 2011-02-06
Let's see: If you actually formatted your hard disk, you will not be able to boot from there, of course.

You should be much more specific to get some useful advice:
- What exactly you did to "format" the disc?
- What is that "recovery disk" you have, and what did you exactly do with it?
- Is some post-disaster recovery tool what you are interested in?

If it's the last thing what you want, there are some tools that can scan drives and try to reconstruct the erased partitions, provided that you have not actually written any data on them of course (that means: don't do anything else with your HD if you plan to recover something). Please [see here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DataRecovery#Lost%20Partition").
