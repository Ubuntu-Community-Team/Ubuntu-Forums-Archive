### Post by uc50_ic4more on 2013-02-24
Would you like to back these emails up in a way that is easily readable (ie. perhaps a bunch of individual text or rich text files that could be opened separately with any application capable of displaying text and images?) or as a more obfuscated single file that may be password-protected?

For what it's worth, using Chrome allows you to have your mail offline, and using Thunderbird and IMAP, of course, stores a copy of each email both on the remote server and locally on your drive. Digging into Thunderbird's storage folder ought to avail access to your chosen folder, which you can back up.

### Post by uc50_ic4more on 2013-02-24
Yikes. If you want to have readable copies of emails under a specific label in Gmail, you may have to do it the hard way: Print 'em all individually to a .pdf or some other format.

I know that via your Google account you have the option to download copies of your data. I've never done this and therefore do not know how a .zip'ed archive of the entirety of your email(s) would be delineated in terms of the labels you've assigned to your emails; not what eventual format they'd be in.

Honestly, though, despite the fact that you've said you'd prefer not to set up Thunderbird, it's really a snap with Ubuntu. Once you have everything set up you could pretty easily "Save As..." each email you want as a .eml file, which is pretty "readable".
