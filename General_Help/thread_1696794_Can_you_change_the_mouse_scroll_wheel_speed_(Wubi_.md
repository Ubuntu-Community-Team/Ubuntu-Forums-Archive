### Post by rolandrock on 2011-02-27
Some MS mice have a scrollwheel bug.  Try unplugging (wait a few seconds) then replugging usb cable.  If that fixes the scroll speed then your mouse has this bug.  There is no permanent fix that I could find except to buy a none-MS mouse.

Good Luck.

Here's something else that worked for me. It doesn't involve unplugging anything.

I have Ubuntu 12.04 & Windows 7 PCs shared via a KVM. 

Yesterday I replaced the KBD & mouse with a Microsoft Desktop 800 set. After switching from the W7 box, I got the warp-speed scrolling problem on Ubuntu.

After reading this:
[http://techietalkz.com/2011/04/10/how-to-workaround-for-microsoft-wireless-mouse-5000-scroll-wheel-issue/?like=1](http://techietalkz.com/2011/04/10/how-to-workaround-for-microsoft-wireless-mouse-5000-scroll-wheel-issue/?like=1)
I installed Intellipoint 7.1 on the W7 box, and told it my mouse was a 'Basic Optical Mouse'. 

That seems to be enough to stop W7 from tweaking the clicks-per-rotation in the mouse hardware, and I get comfortable scrolling on both PCs.
