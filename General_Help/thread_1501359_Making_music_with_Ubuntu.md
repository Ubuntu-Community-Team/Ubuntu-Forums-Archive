### Post by Universal344 on 2010-06-04
Ardour probably doesn't have what you want since (as far as I know) it is strictly for recording.  LMMS has a piano roll and loop editor and I think it can interact w/ a MIDI keyboard, not sure about a keyboard.  Rosegarden and MuSE are more traditional MIDI sequencing.  Whatever you use you want some soundfonts or the like.  You can find *some* in the repos.

### Post by StuartN on 2010-06-04
> **t0p said:**
> generate a different note for each keypress

I misunderstood this (I think) and I guess that you actually mean getting music from your QWERTY keyboard?

Try the virtual keyboard vkeybd. You will also need a synth (standalone, or one of the previously named applications) and the ALSA Connection manager aconnectgui to plug the keyboard output into the synth input.

Starting with a real application like Rosegarden or Ardour (rather than a simple synth) is worth the effort. If you find it even remotely interesting, then you will be out to get a midi keyboard in the morning.
