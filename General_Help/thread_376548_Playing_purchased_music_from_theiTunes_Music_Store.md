### Post by Concorde on 2007-03-05
I'm in the same situation as you jrattner. The only advice I can give you (I just started using Ubuntu on Saturday) is to burn your music colection to cds and then extract them into Ubuntu

It's a pain, but once you get the hang of it, it does the trick. (I will have to redo 3 of my tracks as they have a noisy background)..

I burned them using Itunes (Will look to see if I changed anything in the burning applet)
Second, I extracted them using the Sound Juicer CD Extractor (located in the Applications menu under Sound & Video menu) and placed them in a folder named My Music in the Home/User/ directory.
Third, I used EasyTag (you can find this in the Repositories, under Sound & Video) to edit the track contents (artist name, song name, etc..).
Then finally I used Rhythmbox to play the music files. I have my library for Rhythmbox pointing to the same directory where I extracted them to (Home/User/My Music).

I will hop back into Windows to look at Itunes to see if I modified  anything in the Burning applet.

Keep XP, alongside Ubuntu, I use it to buy my songs then I just import them into Ubuntu. This works for me until I can find a suitable alternative (such as Whine + Itunes).

Didn't change anything in Itunes, you should be able to just burn your tracks to a CD and then import them to Ubuntu with the instructions above.

Concorde
