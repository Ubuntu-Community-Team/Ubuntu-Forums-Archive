### Post by 6foot3 on 2011-10-21
Hey guys, I'm having the same issue here. This happened the first time I rebooted after my most recent update but, I cannot believe that something like this could be the result of an update. Regardless of what I can or can not believe, based on the experience of others, this may well be the case. If so, this is a major issue for the hundreds (thousands?) of netbook remix users since, the computer is basically a brick in this state. 

Fortunately for me, I stumbled into the keyboard shortcuts Alt+F1 and Alt+F2 and was able to get to the Applications Menu. So, that's one workaround. After a bit more messing around I found that using the Applications Menu to go to System>Administration>Login Screen, you can change you default session from "Ubuntu Netbook Edition" to "GNOME". That launches you into a standard GNOME desktop on your next login which is different from the netbook remix but, at least it's use-able.
