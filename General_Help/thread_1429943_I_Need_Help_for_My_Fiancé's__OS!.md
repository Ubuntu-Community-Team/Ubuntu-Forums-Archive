### Post by howefield on 2010-03-14

Well if you are looking at Red Hat, you are probably wanting to install Fedora, which is a very fine system, but takes you into a different packaging system and is probably more "cutting edge" than Ubuntu meaning that it might be a little harder for your fiance to handle/more problematic as well as something different for you to get your head around.

Mint used to be Ubuntu with codecs and a green theme, but as it has developed it has become a little more than that, and out of the box appears to be very good for beginners - no hassle hunting down codecs or flash ect. (There is a lag between new versions of Ubuntu and Mint as you'd expect). My own view is that it isn't different enough to warrant moving from Ubuntu. YMMV.
