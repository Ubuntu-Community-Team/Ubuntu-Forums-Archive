### Post by lukjad on 2009-07-26
Hardy Heron is the LTS (Long Term Stable) release. That means it will get updates and support for [three years]("http://www.ubuntu.com/news/ubuntu-8.04-lts-desktop"), and so if you run a business or just don't like doing upgrades, that is what people take. Jaunty Jackalope has new programs but is only supported for 18 months and a newer version comes along every 6 months, though you don't have to upgrade to it.
