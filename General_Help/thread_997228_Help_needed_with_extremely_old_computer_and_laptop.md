### Post by lswb on 2008-11-29
A system that old may not be capable of booting from CD. Also , some older cd drives cannot read cdrw disks, only factory-pressed CDs. Can you access the BIOS and see if it has any kind of boot choice options? You may be limited to booting from hard drive or floppy disk. Probably [http://www.damnsmalllinux.org](http://www.damnsmalllinux.org) is your best bet for running linux on this system. freedos would certainly be installable, and if you could find a copy of windows 98SE and some appropriate vintage software, might make for a usable system
