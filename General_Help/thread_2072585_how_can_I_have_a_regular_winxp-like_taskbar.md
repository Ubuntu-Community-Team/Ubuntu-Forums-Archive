### Post by Baldrick_NZ on 2012-10-20
The thing is, Linux is NOT Windows. They are both completely different OS's in their approach to computing. So, you can't expect them to do things exactly the same way as each other.

That said, I have found that Linux seems to do things in a logical (human-thinking) order, whereas Windows chooses a less logical way. You have just stumbled across one such scenario. It makes complete sense to lock down administrator rights for root commands - for your protection, whereas Windows doesn't - potentially opening your computer to whatever nasties are out there.

It's this swing back to logical thinking that for a lot of Windows users have problems with Linux, hence a real learning curve. 

My advice would be to revert back to logical (as YOU think it should be) computing, and you'll find Linux a breeze to learn and understand in a relatively short time span.


All the best.
