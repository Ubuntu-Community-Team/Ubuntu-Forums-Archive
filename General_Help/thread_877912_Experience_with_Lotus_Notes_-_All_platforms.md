### Post by jcbkr on 2008-08-18
You'll need a support contract for issues and someone willing to learn the administration of the server and client. This is not a simple run it once and use it forever type of program. It needs someone to care for it and watch it grow. It does do much more than just email and it takes some handling and care not to screw it up. I use to work in the support side and trust me, someone who doesn't know what they are doing can be dangerous with this stuff.

### Post by skybro on 2008-09-05
I have used Lotus Notes for 10 years, and am certified in it's administration and development.  IMO, you will not find a better corporate email system.  I'm not saying it is perfect, it does have it's drawbacks. Configuration is not as difficult as you would think, but, of course, use of advanced features requires advanced support.  I was very exited to hear that IBM is backing Ubuntu and will have a native Notes install for it.  just my 0.02$
