### Post by Linux_junkie on 2012-11-06
Without having full details of your system, it sounds like the upgrade did not perform correctly and may have caused conflicts within the Gnome environment.  Did you upgrade from Update Manager or from the terminal?

It doesn't really matter which way, the best way to avoid conflicts with old and new versions of packages when upgrading is to perform a clean installation from a CD or USB disk.

My advice would be to perform a clean installation but backup (copy) any files you want to keep first.  Or you can try and identify the cause of the conflicts and remove / replace those packages.

Hope this helps.
