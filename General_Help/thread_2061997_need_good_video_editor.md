### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2012-09-23
The videos might have been encoded improperly or they might use a codec that was/is not fully supported. You don't need a video editor you probably just need something to transcode them to another codec (like Handbrake, FFmpeg, or VLC).

### Post by nmaster on 2012-09-24
> **lukeiamyourfather said:**
> The videos might have been encoded improperly or they might use a codec that was/is not fully supported. You don't need a video editor you probably just need something to transcode them to another codec (like Handbrake, FFmpeg, or VLC).

This is probably the case.  I would recommend VLC both for the transcoding as well as for general viewing.  To transcode, go to the "Media" dropdown menu and select "Convert/Save".  You can choose the file and after you click "Convert/Save" there are some preset options that should be useful.
