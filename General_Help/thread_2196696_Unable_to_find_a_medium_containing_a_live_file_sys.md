Hello everyone. I've been around linux a little bit as a hobby user. I keep an old machine under my desk for rainy days. I left ubuntu for debian, (terrible idea) and now I came back. I ran into the fairly common problem of getting stuck in initramfs/busybox on installing 12.04. I originally was trying to do this with a USB drive. I tried two different flash drives, two different programs (pendrivelinux and unetbootin) and got the same error with both. My machine doesn't have anything about ACHI to IDE in BIOS. Found a DVD laying around, burnt the ISO to it, and voila. Installed, albeit slowly. I'm not sure if I'm in the right place or if I should post what I did to fix things here, but I haven't seen that suggested in any of the 300 threads I read about fixing it. 

Short version: Burn ISO to dvd instead of flash drive if you get stuck in inintramfs.
