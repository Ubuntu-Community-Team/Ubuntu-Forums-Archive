### Post by TheFu on 2019-04-28
NAT is provided by the router or upstream network via DHCP.  The way networking works is that everyone has to play together on the same subnet or bad things happen.  In order to help, we'd need more information about the LAN and WAN connections. Can you provide that?

If you have physical access, then you can almost certainly gain elevated privileges.  How to do that is taught very early in most Linux administration courses.  I used it 6 times in the last 24 hrs to fix a system that I broke doing some less-brilliant things. Sometimes we all make bad choices.
