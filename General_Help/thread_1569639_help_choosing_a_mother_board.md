Errr...that didnt make sense. 790GX = an ATI chipset, onboard ATI GPU. 

7XX series ATI chipsets are superseded by the newer 8XX chipsets. 

Best bet for AMD/ATI is to get a 870/980X/890FX chipset (no onboard ATI video) and an nVidia card (probably a GT210/220, or a 8400GS if you are really scratching for $$$). 870 is the 'base' model, the 890X and 890FX chipsets have more PCIe lanes, etc. Which is only really handy if your a gamer and going to run crossfire, or a nasty hardware RAID card. For most uses, the 870 is the way to go (and is a fair chunk cheaper to boot). From tigerdirect, this is the best choice IMO- 

[http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/SearchTools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=6089174&CatId=5528](http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/SearchTools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=6089174&CatId=5528)

### Post by GeoPirate on 2010-09-19
I understand that everyone has their own opinion, but if you are going to steer him away from an i-5 please don't compare apples to oranges.  The clarkdale i-5s that came out last year only had 2 physical cores, but the lynnfield i-5s that have come out this year have 4 physical cores.  The AMD quad or triple core chips mop the floor with the dual core i-5s but the newer quad core i-5s currently offer better performance than the chips AMD has at the same price point.
