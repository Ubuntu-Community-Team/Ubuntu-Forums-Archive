### Post by mltucker on 2009-04-13
I'm booting to XFCE (xubuntu 8.10 intrepid) on an eee pc 901 (read: lots slower than your PC).  My load times are much faster than what you are getting.

When I switched from Gnome to Xfce, my boot times got faster by 10 seconds or so (I also added conky when I switched).  I'm not sure if this is normal.  You could always just install xubuntu-desktop and try booting to it.

If you really want fast boot times, try out something very light-weight like Fluxbox.  It's a small install and you can always just choose what kind of session you want at the login screen.  After booting to the login screen, getting to a usable desktop is almost instantaneous.

### Post by monkeyKata on 2009-04-13
yeah, if you like Gnome then maybe just wait for the Jaunty update which has improved boot times and overall "snappiness" for a lot of people.

When you think about it, once you've booted into your system (whether it be xfce, fluxbox, or gnome) your wait is over, and the difference you're talking about is what... a minute, thirty seconds?

It's funny we can spend hours using our computer, fiddling around on the 'net, perhaps wasting time, yet with all this talk about boot times it seems we want to start up instantaneously rather than in one or two minutes, when really the difference is just an instant.

I think overall responsiveness and quickness using the desktop environment/OS is probably more important, or at least what has more bearing on our actual computer use.  Not to mention simply how well we like the DE compared to another.
