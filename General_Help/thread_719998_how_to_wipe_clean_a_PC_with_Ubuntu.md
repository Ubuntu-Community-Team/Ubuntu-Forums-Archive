### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-03-09
You can boot from the LiveCD or the GParted LiveCD and delete the partitions using the System->Administration->Partition Editor.
GParted LiveCD is good if you don't have a LiveCD yet b/c the download is really small: [http://gparted-livecd.tuxfamily.org/](http://gparted-livecd.tuxfamily.org/)

---

### Post by M4rotku on 2008-03-09
Boot from the ubuntu disk and use the gparted partition manager to delete all of the partitions and then make a new blank partition.  gparted can be accessed through the administrative options.
