### Post by str8line on 2008-12-28
I have a EEEpc that I installed eeebuntu on.  It works beautifully and have had no issues what so ever with the install or operation.  Works just like regular ubuntu but scaled for smaller screen size.  

The EEEpc also does not have any optical drive, the solution is installing from a USB stick.  To install from USB use [unetbootin]("http://downloads.sourceforge.net/unetbootin/unetbootin-eeeubuntu-linux-276?modtime=1220745873&big_mirror=0").

To use this you will need a USB stick with at least 1GB capacity, download the ISO from [www.eeebuntu.com]("http://www.eeebuntu.com") and then insert your USB stick, run the program and then point it to your downloaded ISO...it will format and configure your USB to boot.

Once the information is moved to the USB reboot your pc, from what I could gather from a quick search on google...hit 0 to access boot options and point it to removalable devices.  Will run through full setup and leave you with a sleek, kick *** desktop scaled to the size of a Netbook.

Hope that helped.
