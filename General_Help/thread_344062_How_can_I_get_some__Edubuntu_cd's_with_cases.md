### Post by thenetduck on 2007-01-23
Every High School has a "Head Tech" that makes all of the major disitions about what the best OS for their servers/desktop pc should be. They want to be easy to maintain and not cost a lot. 

With this in mind, I didn't need to talk to anyone "High Up" in the school admin to get them to switch to Ubuntu servers (most of the school administrators hear linux and don't even know what it is). The way I would go about doing it is presenting ubuntu to him on a cd, if you want him to consider edubuntu for the desktop pcs I would install a version on a labtop and present it to the network admin, showing him all of the cool features that are included on it for "FREE" with good support and then hand him a cd. Here is a guy that even has a nice presentation all ready written up (just ask him before you use it) 
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=174803&highlight=schooltool](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=174803&highlight=schooltool)

The way I got my admin to use Ubuntu for his servers was I was a student at the highschool and for extra credit I would help him network and maintain all of the computers. I asked him if on two of the computers I could install Ubuntu because that was my favorite OS and other kids might like it too. He say it was all right and when I graduated I just left some server and desktop cds in his office (hehe). A year out of college I am now back talking to him about it and because he liked the os and remembered how much I liked it, he switched to Ubuntu. I think I will present to him that same presentation (if the guy who created it will let me use it) and show him the cool features like school tool [www.schooltool.org](www.schooltool.org) and other such things. I hope this helped. I will update on any sucess.
