### Post by sikander3786 on 2011-07-24
Did you use your PC in the duration after the files were deleted? If yes, probably the sectors containing your files have been over-written with some other stuff. When you delete something, it isn't actually deleted from the HDD. Instead, those sectors are marked as empty by the OS for later use. If the PC wasn't used during the in-between duration, stop using your PC right now.

Instead boot an Ubuntu Live CD/USB and try using Photorec to recover your files.

If you used your PC previously, there are still some chances that your files (most or few of them) can be recovered. Give Photorec a try at least. It won't recover the file names, but it would recover the stuff at least. More here:

[http://ubuntu4beginners.blogspot.com/2011/01/recover-deleted-data-using-photorec-in.html](http://ubuntu4beginners.blogspot.com/2011/01/recover-deleted-data-using-photorec-in.html)
