### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-02-12
Have you tried going to System Settings>Software and Updates>Additional Drivers tab and seeing if it offers you Nvidia 331.38. While you are in Software and Updates you can go to the Ubuntu Software tab and see if you have enabled Proprietary drivers for devices (restricted). If that is enabled you may get updates to the Nvidia driver with the usual update method. But be aware. You may end up rebooting and getting a broken desktop. It happens. My advice is, if your have a proprietary driver that works, then do not update it unless you want to experiment.

Additional Drivers will tell you what driver you are using. So will the Details page (About this computer). If you see Gallium mentioned then you are on Nouveau open source driver.
