### Post by yoink23 on 2007-03-10
A program called f-spot has this feature.  It also happens to be a pretty nice photo manager.  You just tag your photos you want in the screensaver as "favorites" and then set your screensaver (system-->preferences-->screensaver) to "F-spot photos."  Is this what you are looking for?

Also if you make a "Pictures" folder in your home folder, and populate it with photos, then set the screensaver to "Pictures Folder" a similar effect will be achieved.
