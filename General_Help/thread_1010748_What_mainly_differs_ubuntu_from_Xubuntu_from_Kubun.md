### Post by 7raTEYdCql on 2008-12-14
Edubuntu is basically a cut down ubuntu which is specifically meant for educational institutions, where interconectivity of all computers is needed. Eg. ssh in edubuntu is open by default, which is not in the case of the others.

The other three just differ in desktop environments, and their preinstalled packages. Just to give you an example. In ubuntu you will get the preinstalled suite of gnome, while in kubuntu-kde and xubuntu-xfce.

If you are looking for simplicity and speed then go for xubuntu. Otherwise i will always recommed ubuntu. Anyway all the extra stuff that you may find in kubuntu/xubuntu you can later on install in ubuntu, therefore no point of comparing. It is ultimately personal choice as far as which desktop environment you are comfortable with. I would recommend gnome.

And yes the cd's are bootable ones.
