*****

And some more things to consider...

3.5" hard drives are far more durable than 2.5" hdds.  Its always good to have whatever data is on the drive backed up somewhere.

I say this because you mentioned durability, and it seems pretty important to you.  The fact is, though, that the drive's durability is probably more important.  Those 500GB drives are probably pretty good as far as durability goes, when compared to most 2.5" hard drives. (Especially the WD, as its a 'blue' series.  don't know about the others, though).

There are 2.5" drives that are built for durability.  They are a lot more expensive (though I don't know by how much, as I've never actually purchased one myself), slower, and hold less data, but are much more durable/reliable than standard consumer versions.  They are probably available for the general public as well.  If you want some information about them, just ask and I'll try to round up some data and sources.

### Post by Gizenshya on 2009-02-03
As Therion said, you need a SATA II case.  SATA I cases work with SATA II technology... and would be fine for an external case (as you are only going to get SATA I speeds, anyway.  SATA II drives are back-compatible with SATA I cases, and vice versa), BUT I don't know if SATA I cases support 500GB or not.  Its an older technology.

You do need a case that says it supports at least the size of your drive.  The circuitry on the drives that don't (basically) can't count that high, and you would have lots of problems, if it even worked at all.

Most of the cases in the links I provided were aluminum... all HDD (hard disk drives) are aluminum (that I've ever seen).

The durability of the case doesn't matter too much.  The drive is far more important.  In fact, a plastic case could 'sacrifice' itself (break), which could increase the time variable in the acceleration formula enough to save the drive, whereas an aluminum case would transfer all the energy of an impact directly to the drive in a very short span of time (this is why cars crumple on impact).

But... realistically speaking, you don't need to worry about it.

One comment on newegg said that models with the "j" in the name have "free fall protection," which would presumably lower the chances of damage if you drop the drive while its on.  You would need to check up on that, though.

OR... just get a full-sized (3.5") drive.  2.5" drives aren't durable.  period.  same goes for their cases.  Do you want a backup drive, or a portable drive?  There are different requirements of each.  I wouldn't (and don't) trust ANY 2.5" drive to store only copies of stuff.  I just use them for transfers and whatnot.  They just aren't anywhere near as reliable as 3/5" drives (in any relevant category).

I have some aluminum 3.5" cases and metal ones.  I've dropped them, kicked them, stacked books on top of them, and done pretty much everything else you're not supposed to do to them..and all of them are fine.  Just don't play kickball with the thing and you should be ok ;)

I wouldn't keep it plugged in all the time, either.  Its just unnecessary wear on the drive.  If you want it as a backup, then plug it up once a week or whatever and backup stuff.  If you want it as a portable drive, then plug it in when you need to transfer.  Other than that, keep it in a drawer out of the way of dangers.  you'll add years to the life of the drive like that.  Another way to think about it, your drive will die years faster if you have poor usage habits ;)

Heat will not be an issue with 2.5" drive in any external case.  They just don't get hot.  You shouldn't even take that into consideration.

Now, this is what I would do (and have done).

get a 500gb or 1tb drive, and put it in [this case](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817173042).

I suggest a [WD Caviar Black (all the latest data protection mechanisms)](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136284).  its very popular, though.  you'll have to wait for it to come back in stock.. and then it won't likely be there too long again lol

I have both of the above, and LOVE them both!  That case is amazing.  eSATAand USB.  fan (withon/off switch), and a metal mesh screen for the fan.  And that mesh screen is no joke.  The case and drive have survived being dropped and stepped on, sandstorms, and extreme heat (Afghanistan during summer for 7 months).  That fan managed to keep it cool for video editing and lots of other stuff in regular 120+ degree weather.  The only sign of damage (other than some rubbed off paint on edges) is the mesh screen is a little bent in (someone stepped on that part, or something big was dropped on it).  But, the mesh took it, and everything still works great ;)

If you don't really need the durability, just get the 2.5"

If you need durability (which you probably don't), get what I mentioned.  and if you need more than that...I suggest you step away from your computer and go play rugby or something :p
