### Post by ubukool on 2010-12-27
I've been tearing my hair out with this one and have looked everywhere for a solution. I'd suggest using Pana, which is a fork of Amarok 1.X. 

[http://pana.bunnies.net/](http://pana.bunnies.net/)


Clementine, which is also a fork, might also work.  

I had problems getting Pana to recognise my ipod and it takes a bit of fiddling but you can do it.  You just need to enter the mount point of your ipod which is something like /mount/(name of ipod)but now it writes and transfers playlists like a dream!  I'd almost given up on finding anything that could.  Try it and see if it works for you.
