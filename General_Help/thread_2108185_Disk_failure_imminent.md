### Post by elgordodude on 2013-01-24
Best thing to do is shut the machine down and unplug the drive cables until a replacement drive can be put in. "Imminent" is a warning of sorts, ubuntu is telling you that your data is likely okay, but won't be if you continue to use it. The sooner you shut it down, the better off your data will be.

### Post by elgordodude on 2013-01-24
> **imsudheer98 said:**
> Is there any way to attend this problem other than replacing the drive

No, ubuntu is reporting a physical problem, not a software problem.

> make it work temporarily because the system is dead slow and hangs for  every ten minutes and have some things to take care of before changing  the drive. 	

This also points to a hardware problem, but yes you should be able to save your current work and exit. Keep in mind that imminent is a relative term, most of your data should be okay, but the longer a dying drive goes the worse it gets. SMART can give you more details, but having been there it's best to replace the drive and copy everything over, rather than kill it and try to rescue.
