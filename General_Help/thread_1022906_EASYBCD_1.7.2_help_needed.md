### Post by WiFi Ed on 2008-12-27
Your post is a little unclear, but if you are trying to use EasyBCD in Ubuntu, you can't. It's a Windows application.

If Ubuntu somehow buggered up your Vista boot files, you might be able to recover Vista by booting up your Vista DVD and choosing the Repair option when you get to the setup screen. I forget all the details, but if you google "restore Vista MBR" you'll find lots of info.

This assumes you didn't let Ubuntu overwrite your whole Vista installation during the installation process. If so, you'll have to re-install Vista.

Best of luck to you!
