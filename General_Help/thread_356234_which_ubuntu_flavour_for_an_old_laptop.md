### Post by Bartender on 2007-02-08
sdide's intentions are probably good, but his info is inaccurate.

First off, a basic Ubuntu install doesn't come anywhere near 10 GB.  

Second, it'll never work with 64MB of RAM.  There are hundreds of posts on this subject.

If it were me I'd scrounge around for some more RAM.  2 problems with this - you may not want to spend any money on the old thing, and old legacy RAM is darned expensive.

I don't know about Fluxbox, etc. but have tried Xubuntu.  Make sure to download the Alternate install CD, not the LiveCD.  The LiveCD's have too many balls in the air during installation and low RAM machines can't handle it.
