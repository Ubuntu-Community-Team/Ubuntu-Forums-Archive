Isn't the launch bar supposed to come up whenever you mouse over the left edge of the screen?

I am reading reading information from a website, typing up notes in a word processing document and then adding lines of code in the Arduino IDE, so switching quickly from one application to another.

### Post by ctdahle on 2011-10-15
I think I figured it out. It's a human factor, not software.

I am using a laptop computer (HP G61) with a built in touchpad. Usually I have the computer on a raised shelf in my shop and I use a separate wireless mouse and keyboard. I generally switch off the onboard touchpad using the convenient little push button, but I started my work this morning in the nice warm kitchen and didn't take the computer out to the shop until it was warmed up in there.

Consequently the touchpad was turned on and I forgot to shut it off when I connected up the external mouse and keyboard. I think the two devices don't get along and when they fight, Unity decides to ignore them both. With either the touchpad turned off or the keyboard and mouse disconnected, the launch bar works just fine.
