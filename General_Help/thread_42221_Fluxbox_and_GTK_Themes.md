### Post by DAC1138 on 2008-06-06
This is really really old, but has not been solved. I hope this doesn't upset anybody.

Just install the program "gtk-theme-switch2". Once installed, run "gtk-theme-switch2" from a terminal and select your theme. Click apply and you're done.

This saves the theme settings in a .gtkrc theme file. Whenever you go into another desktop environment, like XFCE or Gnome, you may have conflicts with your theme. I had this issue. To fix it, all you have to do is delete the .gtkrc file in your home directory.
