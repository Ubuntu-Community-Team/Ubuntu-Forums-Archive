### Post by drs305 on 2008-11-30
When you delete files, they are normally moved to the Trash Bin. They occupy disk space until you empty your trash. Even then, trash may remain in bins you aren't aware of or trash which you cannot delete as a normal user.

I think this guide I wrote on finding and deleting trash will probably answer your questions:
[Disk Full? - Check Your Trash Bin(s)]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=898573")

It covers why a normal user can't delete all trash, how to find all the trash on your system, and how to delete it.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2008-11-30
There's also an option in nautilus under Edit -> Preferences (Behavior tab) to add a permanent delete entry to the context menu. Personally, I never use the Trash. If I want something deleted, I send those files directly to the great bit bucket in the sky!
