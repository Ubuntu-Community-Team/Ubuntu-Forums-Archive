### Post by Whiffle on 2008-03-28
I hug my thinkpad on a regular basis, mostly because I've had it for a year now, brought it with me to class most days, to a summer camp, and a couple of road trips, and it has yet to give me any issues, except for when I got a rock stuck under the ctrl key...but thats another story.   I'm not certain how the newer ones are, but my T43 is pretty much flawless under linux.  Check out [www.thinkwiki.org](www.thinkwiki.org), theres plenty of information to be had there.  

Macs...I think the only time I would buy one is if linux were'nt available or I got tired of linux.  Sure they're shiny, but when it comes down to it, they lack features that I do use on a daily basis, such as the nipple mouse (trackpoint) and more than one mouse button on the touchpad (I have 3 and use them all...).  Furthermore, you won't find such things as hot-swappable drive bays that can hold an extra hard drive, battery, or cdrom.  Apparently there isn't much of a docking station option for them either if thats of any interest.  On the other hand, OS X is a pretty sweet OS.  

To me, it really depends on what you're looking for out of a laptop.

### Post by Whiffle on 2008-03-28
Well, truth be told, as far as I'm concerned, battery life on any laptop in linux these days still leaves something to be desired.  They can be tweaked to get pretty good battery life, but it takes some fanagaling.  I don't think linux is quite "there" yet.  That said, I've managed to get mine to work pretty comfortably at about the same power consumption as windows.  One of the really nice things though is the tp_smapi module, it will tell you everything you ever wanted to know about your battery, and allow you to set the charging threshholds (good to help extend the operational life of the battery, by not charging it 100% all the time).

Mic shouldn't be a problem, thats part of the sound card and not too finicky.

Webcam, I did a little googling, seems they're usb and they work, but I didn't find a whole lot on them.  No idea about skype video though.
