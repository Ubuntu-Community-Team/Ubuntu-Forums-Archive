### Post by ryanVickers on 2007-05-25
in ubuntu, it's in system > preferences > fonts, but I don't know about xubuntu.  I would guess it's a similar setup though.  Look for font control in where ever the settings controls would be.  I can assure you it has nothing to do with the video card.  Enabling the video drivers activates use of your video card.  This is needed if you want to do anything in 3D.  Back to fonts, there are different anti aliasing settings and powers. try changing them before totally turning it off.

It could also be a resolution problem.  If you do not have it at the highest your monitor can do (and it might not be listed) everything will be blurred/fuzzy.
