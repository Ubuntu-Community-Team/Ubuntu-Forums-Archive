### Post by TheFu on 2019-03-22
Without the correct browser plugin installed and enabled, no video playback will work in any browser.
Every plugin is a security risk.  Google via Chrome and Chromium browsers include support for the most popular video formats and because of their world-wide reach, they have the pull to get many, but not all, video websites to choose those video formats.

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_audio_coding_formats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_audio_coding_formats)
[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_video_codecs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_video_codecs)
[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_video_container_formats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_video_container_formats)


There are probably thousands of different video file types when we mix the containers, audio codecs and video codecs. Each of these have to align - the container, video and audio for any video file to be playable by any specific program or addon or plugin.  I think Firefox supports mkv/vp9/ogg files since they are all patent-free formats.
