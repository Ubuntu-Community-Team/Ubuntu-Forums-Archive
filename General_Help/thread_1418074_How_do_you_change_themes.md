### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-02-28
Yes, of course.

Go to the Preferences part of your Mint Menu and go to Appearance. The first tab is Theme, and you can choose between several preinstalled themes.

You can mix and match parts of themes too, by clicking on the Customise button and then changing the Controls, Window, Icon and Colour themes.

If you see a theme online that you like, just drag the archive (.tar.gz) into the Themes tab and it will install. If it's not a complete theme (i.e. it's just a 'GTK' controls theme) you will need to click the Customise button to enable it.

Themes only control the look of the screen controls, window borders, icons and/or colours. They don't contain docks, on-screen information displays or panel layouts. These are usually extra programs or extra things that you have to do yourself.
