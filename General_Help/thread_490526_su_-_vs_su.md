### Post by Slammer64 on 2007-07-02
su is simply changing to "superuser" i.e root, su - is changing to superuser with all the paths that being a superuser includes.

### Post by reckless2k2 on 2007-07-02
this will make it easy.....

if you do an "su" only, you'll stay in whatever directory you are currently in with root permissions. you can nav around still with root permissions too until you exit. 

if you do "su -", you'll change to the root directory "/root/" with root permissions. you can nav anywhere with root permissions until you exit.
