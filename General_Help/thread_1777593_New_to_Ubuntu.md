[http://www.futureshop.ca:80/en-CA/product/zotac-zotac-desktop-computer-featuring-atom-330-processor-maghd-nd01-english-maghd-nd01/10169718.aspx?path=527e8ad38bf13f6f8f0489215f86b014en02](http://www.futureshop.ca:80/en-CA/product/zotac-zotac-desktop-computer-featuring-atom-330-processor-maghd-nd01-english-maghd-nd01/10169718.aspx?path=527e8ad38bf13f6f8f0489215f86b014en02)

It's tough. I assumed I could pick up a desktop pretty cheap as it all laptops these days, but they all seem pretty pricey at the future shop.

Here is what I would do, I would purchase a tiny USB flash drive, and install Ubuntu 11.04 on that drive, and then I would go down to the computer store, and physically boot whatever computer I was considering purchasing, so that way you know it works, and the video driver exists for that system.. That's the best way. That's what I do when I buy a new computer. I bring a live stick of Ubuntu and plug it in the laptop/netbook I want to test it out on, and then if it works, and I can see the video driver works with it, then I make the purchase..  Otherwise I keep shopping around until I find one that works with my Flash Drive of Ubuntu 11.04.

It is very simple to build your own Live USB Flash Drive to boot from:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download)

It's the second option on that page. Select create USB. It's honestly the best way to be sure you don't buy the wrong computer, in my experience. It may seem like a hassle but it saves a very considerable amount of energy and time that way in the end.
