### Post by tocleora on 2006-12-30
Those of you that are familiar with me know that I love to brag on Ubuntu any chance I get.   Well, I have to admit I haven't had a lot of luck in the games department.  I tried to install the (newer) Wolfenstein demo, didn't work, got America's Army working but it's either choppy or I just don't know what the heck I'm doing with that game (but yeah it's a little choppy).  I got another FPS installed and was able to play it but it too was choppy.  But I don't shy away from installing new games if for any other reason just to *see* if I can get them to work. I love when I'm successful at something in Ubuntu (if you couldn't tell! Hah!).  

Well on digg.com yesterday they were talking about a game called OpenArena.  This game is based on Quake 3 Arena if I'm correct except its free. Although they weren't promoting it as a linux game, it sounds like something the linux community would have a part in so I checked out the web site.  Sure 'nuff, on the web site is a deb package, < 80MB in size, I decide to download it and try it out.

Guess what?!... Not only did it install, it started, single player worked smoothly, perfectly!  So I decided to take my chances with multiplayer... Now I should mention that my first attempt was choppy.  But I tried again today and it was as smooth as when I play Halo in Windows!  FINALLY!!! (Note: yes this makes me curious to try the other games now, possible lag issue with other games?)

Now I'm obviously not speaking for the whole community, different hardware setups, different versions of Ubuntu, whatever the case may be may not yield the same results for you.  But it's at least checking out if you have had problems in the past or just like these types of games!

Oh, I should also mention, I've read a thread on here about OpenArena that frowns on the GPL license. I have to admit I'm not just extremely familiar with what the differences are in the licenses, but thought I'd mention that in case it was important to you. :)

I would love to know if others are playing the game or also have good experience with the game, and if you've had similar problems with the games I mention above if you have other recommendations as well.  For now, here's the link:

[http://openarena.ws/](http://openarena.ws/)
