### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-04-15
Maybe I just don't understand what you're looking for, but there is an implementation of the Classic Mac OS' application switcher menu preinstalled with Ubuntu.

Right-click your top panel and choose "Add To Panel". Scroll down to Window Selector, click it and click Add.

I once ran without a bottom panel (well, a tiny one with only a workspace switcher) and the Window Selector applet was one way to switch between Windows. The other way was the Scale plugin of Compiz, triggered to activate if I threw the mouse into the top-right corner of the screen.

XFCE also has a Window Selector applet for its panel.
