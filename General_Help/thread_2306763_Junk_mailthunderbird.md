### Post by lisati on 2015-12-18
You might want to take a look at Thunderbird's junk settings, which, amongst other things include options to either delete the email or move the flagged email to the junk folder. You can access them for each account you have set up in Thunderbird by clicking on Edit->Account Settings.

### Post by deadflowr on 2015-12-18
> [COLOR=#000000]How can I view which emails I've marked as junk[/COLOR]
If looking at regular inbox entries, you can add the junk status option to the top line (the line that usually says subject, from, date, et cetra)
just right click on that line and a list of available selections will show, simply press on the junk status option and it'll show up on the line.

Normal non-junk entries will have a blank dot, and junk entries will usually have a flame or something.

You can then click on that junk status icon in that top line to toggle it descending or ascending, which ever you prefer.
(You can also move that junk status selection across the top line by pressing and holding left click and dragging to where you want it.)

Hope it make sense.
