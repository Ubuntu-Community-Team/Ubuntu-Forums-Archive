### Post by underquark on 2010-02-01
To easily speed up your computer you just install a version of Linux or re-install your Windows XP.  Both will wipe your settings and data, though.

A better idea might be to delete any rubbish from your HDD and defragment it and see if that helps.  Also, adding RAM is a good way to speed up most systems.

Then, if you like, download ubuntu and burn a Live CD.  Boot from this and choose to shrink your Windows partition down and install ubuntu in the remaining space.  Dual-boot with the option of ubuntu or XP at start-up.  If you like ubuntu, there's your answer.  If not, then go back to Windows.
