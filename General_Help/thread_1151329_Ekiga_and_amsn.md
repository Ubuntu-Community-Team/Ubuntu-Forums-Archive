### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-05-06
No to the first question.  Ekiga is VoiP, while MSN Messenger is IM.  They're two completely different technologies.

As to the second, there used to be a few free PC-to-phone VoiP clients...  I seem to remember using Dialpad for free when I was in college.  But I've looked around and while there are quite a few PC-to-PC clients, I can't find any that offer free PC-to-phone.

### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-05-06
Skype is also VoiP.  Skype should be able to connect to Ekiga (though I'm not entirely sure), but only MSN messenger clients with voice messaging support can connect to other MSN messenger clients with voice messaging support, and MSN messenger clients can't connect to VoiP.

Think about it like this... you can't play a cassette tape in a CD player, even though cassettes and CDs both store music.  VoiP and IM voice clients both let you talk to someone PC-to-PC, but they do it in very different ways.
