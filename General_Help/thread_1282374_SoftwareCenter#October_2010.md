### Post by infamousrev on 2009-10-04
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SoftwareCenter#October](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SoftwareCenter#October) 2010
> October 2010
Integrate the ratings and review mechanism from Launchpad into Ubuntu Software Center. This will likely involve:
An interface within the Center for rating and reviewing software that is installed now (or that has been installed recently).
A mechanism for reporting, and staff for moderating, inappropriate reviews (e.g. those that use offensive language).
Establish a mechanism for establishing and conveying a trust level for software in PPAs, and for easily adding PPAs within the Center.
[color=red]Provide the ability to purchase software from within the Center.[/color]

The future plans of software center include making it possible to sell software.

I personally oppose this idea, and a have a real life example to explain why.


Take the android market, before it was possible to sell applications on the market there were all sorts of great programs for free download.

But, when it became possible to sell the software easily many developers decided to make their freeware software paid software. Brutally forcing the existing user base to either pay or leave.

Other developers decided to cut important pieces of functionality from their applications and make a "pro" version with the functionality included.


I foresee this happening with ubuntu too, many open source enthusiasts like to contribute, but thats also because they like using a open source operating system, and they simply cannot earn from their efforts working on the environment they love.

What are your thoughts on this?
