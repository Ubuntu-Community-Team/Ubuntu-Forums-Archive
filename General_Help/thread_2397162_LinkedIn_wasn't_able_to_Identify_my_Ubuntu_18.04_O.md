### Post by Topsiho on 2018-07-27
As far as I am aware (no specialist, but read books and articles) information is sent from one's PC in packets, that are each guided through the internet individually. They all contain headers, which are lines of information such as the destination, and from where (the IP-addres of your computer) they are sent. So any information that you send contains your IP-address.
From your IP-address it is possible to see where you live. Not exactly, but any address is in a range of addresses that is given to your provider.
Using a VPN, which allows you to use an IP-address which is not yours, you can hide this info. If you want, or need, to.

Anyone who can correct this information, is free to do so :)
