### Post by darko.baruh on 2010-01-18
From my point of view (noob who want to use something diferent and get rid of windows) it was very difficult until I find what suites me best for my netbook Compaq mini 700EI (same specs like HP mini out of the box, except mine is upgradet to 2 gb ram). First I tried:
1 - Ubuntu Netbok Remix:
- Worked everything out of the box but, I didnt liked the big icons that made me feel that my screen is even smaller,I didnt managed to change some icons that were looking crispy, I had some problems with the fan that when I plug the power it was spinning like crazy and the processor was hot, but the choice of installed programs was perfect for me. I liked the integrated Evolution mail and etc. Simply I felt satisfied but, there was something...
2 - SUSE:
- The wonderful collors were beautifful from the KDE interface, but there everything stopped. I had problems installing the driver for the wifi, difficult to go try the meny and hated the YaST, the battery power program was very simple, nothing special.
3 - Kubuntu desktop edition:
- I didnt liked the UI and choice of installed programs I had it only 20 minutes, so you can imagine how much impressed me.
4 - Kubuntu Netbook ( beta or something, far away from stable):
- The design, concept and the graphic are amazing, still not finished but left me to think and to wait for the stable version to try it. I had problems with the menu interface ( some icons that I removed they were returning back and some of them were duplicated, but we know its still not stable edition ). I hated the mail program it was so  far away from evolution, the Konqueror web browser felt faster from me then the Firefox. I hated the notifications that were poping out one on top of the other covering the entire screen. In the meantime I was downloading Ubuntu desktop 9.10 and asking questions on the forum about the mentioned edition. I went to DistroWatch.com (suggested from [snowpine]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=479885")) to read about other distros and what I read was ok but nothing compared to Ubuntu. So now I am writing from my netbook on **Ubuntu desktop 9.10** and I love it.Basically the same like Ubuntu Netbook Remix except desktop instead of the UI designed for netbooks mentioned above from my point of view.
So just to point that I dont know much about linux because I am on linux from a month ago but this is just approach from an ordinary user who wants to migrate from windows to linux and his expectations from the linux user friendliness and visual designed UI.
I hope that this "review" will help someone.
