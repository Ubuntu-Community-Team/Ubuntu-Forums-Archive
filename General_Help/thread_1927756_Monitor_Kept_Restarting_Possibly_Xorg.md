### Post by robsoles on 2012-02-18
It was really hard to make it through your post because it is just such a blob of text.

What you describe (once I managed to make my way all the way through it) can be caused by a couple of different things; You may have a failing HDD, you may need more Virtual RAM or your may need more physical RAM - I'm betting on the HDD for now.

My Boss rang me yesterday (day off, Saturday) and told me the the 'from' bar in Thunderbird had collapsed and wouldn't allow him to switch which of his identities he was going to send as. I was about to start some complicated crap and go in to the office to help him when it occurred to me to ask a couple of simple enough questions:

Are there any updates being indicated in 'System'->'Administration'->'Update Manager'? **Apply them**

Is there a message about a restart required? Why not restart anyway? When is the last time you had a restart? (lol - he hadn't restart for three months, I told him it was about due.)

My boss applied the updates and then he restarted his computer and lo and behold his Thunderbird had it's "from" dropdown back in its email composer and we decided I didn't need to go in to the office after all.

BTW: This is the community cafe where we chat and don't necessarily really try to provide support so if you get blocked at any of these points I suggest you take what you are blocked at and make a proper support thread of it in Absolute Beginners or something.

Now, coz this can be caused by errors in your file system and 'broken' binaries I think you should find out how to check your file-system for errors properly and sit through that at least once soon; Then you should (perhaps) find out how to use the rescue option on Grub boot menu to get to the package installer to have it attempt to repair broken packages.

Once you've done those things I think you should try to use your computer 'normally' again and then try to find more common terminology for whatever feature(s) of failure it brings up and have a hunt through the most related sub-forums of here before choosing one to elaborate your problem in for a much more helpful and directed reply than this one is.
