### Post by XBNCPRk on 2014-04-11
The stats on there show that there are 80 bad sectors that have been reassigned, and others (30) that are waiting to be. As a matter of course though, drives contain a certain number of unused sectors that are used to replace ones that are bad or no longer useable. If you see the number of bad sectors start to rise quickly, thats an indication to replace it ASAP. The drive will remap as many as it can to the usable 'spare' sectors but as soon as that random number runs out, youll start corrupting data and losing information. 

The drive however will fail eventually; when is anyones guess, though with the high-temp warning noted on there, I would bet on sooner rather than later. Given that the stats show you use the computer about 2 hours a day, I wouldnt forsee it failing in the next few days or weeks but its always easier to replace a disk before it fails, not after.

### Post by gordintoronto on 2014-04-12
At minimum, you should make two copies of all the data files you care about to an external drive, the cloud, DVDs or another computer on your network. You may find that some files can not be copied because some of the data is on bad sectors; the drive has used up all the sectors reserved as replacements.

Don't forget to save email and browser bookmarks, which are not in standard data files. Then buy a new hard drive!
