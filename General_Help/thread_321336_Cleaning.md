### Post by tari on 2006-12-18
The filesystem gets checked for errors at boot time (fsck), ext3 is good at not requiring defragging, spyware is virtually nonexistent, and the package management system is very good at deleting things when you tell it to.
Basically, there's no reason to, although you could do
```

sudo apt-get autoclean

```
if you really feel the need to do something.
