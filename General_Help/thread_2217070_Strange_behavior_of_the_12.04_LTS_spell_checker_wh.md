### Post by kpfuser on 2014-04-15
Following a just concluded exercise of adding a second language (Greek) for typing an occasional document or email in it, a number of malfunctions appeared that self-corrected after the addition of the second language was completed successfully except for the peculiar behaviour of the spell checker which I detail in what follows:

1. When using LibreOffice Writer or Calc, the spell checker (for typing in English) works as expected.

2. If I go to Yahoo Mail and begin to type (in English) the spell checker does not work as it fails to highlight intentionally misspelled words.

3. Leaving Yahoo Mail open in Compose mode and starting a thread in this forum runs into the same problem, i.e., the spell checker does not flag intentionally misspelled words.

4. Leaving the thread open, opening gmail, going to Compose mode, and beginning to type shows that the spell checker works as expected.

5. Closing gmail and returning to Yahoo Mail Compose mode or to the abandoned thread in this forum shows that the spell checker is working now as all initially intentionally misspelled words appear properly flagged.

The question is of course how I can restore the proper function of the spell checker in all applications that make use of it.

Since it may be of relevance, I shall outline briefly what I did in relation to adding a second language to my 12.04 installation.

1.  I downloaded and installed a Greek language pack from Dash/Language Support. Then in the order of languages used I moved Greek to the top of the respective list. This had the undesirable effect of changing all menus to Greek while giving me no way to toggle the keyboard layout between English and Greek.

2. After obtaining help from this forum, I  went to Dash/Keyboard Layout/All Settings and by clicking on the "+" sign in the lower LH corner, I added Greek as a second language. Then continuing with Options/Key(s) to change layout, I chose a keystroke combination (Shift + Caps Lock in my case) to toggle between the English and Greek keyboard layouts as needed. This satisfied my need of a second language for typing an occasional document or email in Greek but left all my menus appearing in Greek.

3. To bring back English as my menu language, I dragged Greek to third position in the language list below English U.S. and English. After restarting, all menus appear in English, as was my intention, but the spell checker lost its bearings and turned to gmail for counselling and guidance. Is there some way, short of reinstalling, to wean the spell checker from this unexpected dependence on gmail?
