### Post by Enigma12 on 2017-04-19
Since you are even newer to Lubuntu than I am, I will offer what I hope will help you. 

First, I have no idea what that error message means, and I won't even try to find out what it means. 

Second, I am using a different Dell computer than yours, but it uses the same CPU as yours and runs Lubuntu 16.04.2 perfectly. Mine, however, has 2GB of RAM. It does everything that I want to do with a computer. You should bump your RAM up beyond that 512MB to do more than simple stuff. Should be cheap. 

If your computer has a DVD burner, try burning (NOT copying) the ISO file to a DVD. If it doesn't have a DVD drive, you can download Unetbootin and load the ISO onto a USB thumb drive with that. Start your computer. Press (probably F12) to go to Boot Menu, and highlight and press Enter on the USB drive containing your thumb drive. Try without installing to see if all is well. If so, do the installation and enjoy.
