### Post by Diabolis on 2008-08-21
There is nothing special about installing themes. Go ahead and customize your Ubuntu.

Compiz only deals with "how" the windows are drawn. Themes are about the appearance and the colors of your windows, the "what".

### Post by paulmerchant on 2008-08-21
I was sort of new to themes a couple of weeks ago. Here's what I learned:

If you're on Gnome, GTK2 themes are for your buttons, text, tabs, backgrounds, etc. Basically everything inside of your window.

Emerald themes can handle everything around your window: boarders and titlebar (and they add a bunch of shadow, transparency, etc.).

It's fun to find theme suites that come with GTK2 themes, Emerald themes, and icons, but you can come up with your own combinations.
