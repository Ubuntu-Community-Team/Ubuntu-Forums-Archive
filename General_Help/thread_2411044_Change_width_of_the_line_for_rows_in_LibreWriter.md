### Post by Holger_Gehrke on 2019-01-24
If you mean the lines between cells, you can find what you're looking for in the menu Format->Cells->Tab "Borders".

And this forum accepts Unicode, so you could just hit ctrl-u followed by a four hex digit codepoint or use the character map accessory and copy and paste :

### Post by Holger_Gehrke on 2019-01-24
Sorry, I must have had some kind of brain-freeze ... My answer related to Calc, not Writer. But the principle is applicable for tables in Writer, too. The difference is where to find the option. Mark the cell(s) for which you want to change the line(s), open the context-menu and select "Table Properties". There's a tab named "Borders" in the dialog you will get. You can select which lines around and in between the cells you want to change and can change line style, width and colour.

Holger
