### Post by dehuszar on 2007-01-03
> **cbahimsa said:**
> Openoffice fine so long as I use the "send as word (or pdf)" within the application if I want to share it with my colleagues.  Formatting a problem if you convert to or from wordperfect unless you have a few extra minutes to so. I have not actually created pleadings in openoffice so dont know if even doable. But drafting is fine in it.  

If you haven't learned how to use styles in OpenOffice.org, that'll solve a lot of problems going back and forth between other apps.  Especially as a lawyer, setting up page styles would be very useful for those awful formatting rules required at the top documents that have to run through the courts.  OpenOffice.org should also recognize any templates you might have made from Word(Perfect).

You may know all this, but for the benefit of others, using styles in OpenOffice.org to their maximum potential helps because one program may measure a [tab] by so many points/pixels, or a line break may have so many pts/pxls above or below, but a competing app measures differently.  So when the document from one app meets another app, those differences in measurements cause the formatting to get all hinky.  Okay, everyone knows that.  

But if your paragraphs all have a style attached to them, and that style says, margins are x wide on this and that side, there's no space above lines, but .25" of space below, first line indents 7 characters (or whatever) and you are using a page break function instead of just hitting enter until you're on the next page, you have a 3-fold better chance of having it look as it should in a competing app.

It also speeds up document creation tremendously because you can just type out what you need to say in unformatted text, then grab different sets of text (even non-contiguous chunks) apply a style, edit the style and they all update as one.  Once you get a handle on that, you can create master styles which appear in all new documents, so your personal preferences are available for use.  

So for a pleading, or a motion, you can create a page style which lays out the courts required "so and so vs someone else" without having to hit tab 8 times and type in the '}' at the end, knowing when your colleague opens it, it will look like hell.

Anyway, there's a great site for this kind of stuff over at : [http://openoffice.blogs.com/openoffice/](http://openoffice.blogs.com/openoffice/)

All tolled, while it may not have every feature that Word or WordPerfect has, but I think it does the features it has in kind much more elegantly.  

Hope that helps,
