### Post by TBABill on 2010-11-12
Depends on the version of Mint, the quality of the download and CD burn, hardware, etc. Probably best to ask the folks on the Mint forums, but here's my best guess. You didn't provide much info to go on.
 
If you are installing Mint 10 it does not support some older video cards out of the box (nor does Ubuntu 10.10). Search the forums for those, but the ones I remember are nVidia running the legacy driver and some ATi using fglrx. I'm not certain of those, just stating I have seen posts regarding those and incompatibility.
 
Regardless of the version, did you verify the download using md5sum to make sure your result is the same as the one posted on the Mint site? If it does not match then you have a corrupted download. Did you burn the CD or DVD at the slowest speed possible? Does another OS currently work on that machine?
 
With a P3 you may need to do some searching online to find the best distro for that machine. I'm not sure a *buntu or a derivative of *buntu would do well (currently supported versions). Perhaps DSL, Puppy, TinyCore, etc. would be better?
 
You also have memory constraints to deal with. P3 machines rarely had much RAM. Look up the system requirements for the version of Mint you are installing to see if it can even run on that machine.
 
Sorry, that's the best I can do with what you provided.
