### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-08-01
This is a very personal choice, but in spite of using gnome in its various versions, I use kmymoney2 for my own financial software, and have done for some years.

I have also looked at homebank and gnucash, but the former didn't quite attract me enough to keep using it, and the latter, with its double entry book-keeping was far too complicated for a simple bank account reconciliation application.

### Post by PhilGil on 2012-08-01
I use gnucash, too. It's a bit clunky and there is a learning curve, but once you get the concept of double-entry accounting it's no harder to use than any other home finance program. It's also feature complete; there's not much that gnucash can't handle, no matter how complex your finances are.
