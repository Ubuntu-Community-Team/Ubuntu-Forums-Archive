### Post by joe343 on 2006-07-28
In Paint Shop Pro, there was some functions to automaticaly enhance a photo.  
For example:
1-Automatic color balance
2-Automatic color saturation
3-Automatic white balance
4-Sharpen
5-Edge preserving smooth
6-Noise reduction

All those functions were available individually and also through a button called "One click photo fix".

The beauty of those functions is that they were all preset, you didn't have to adjust anything (unless you wanted to).

So when I took my photos from my camera, I used the "one click photo fix" button and the photo was "instantly" (without any manual setting) looking better.
I liked those functions because it was a real time saver, and most of the time, the results were very good.

Is there a way to do this in linux ?

I'd like to have the same 6 functions that would be "predefined" (although I know all the same settings can't be applied on all photos) so I don't have to play with sliders to add a "little more Red" and a "little less Green" to make the colors of my photo more realistic, or play with the radius to remove the noise, etc...
