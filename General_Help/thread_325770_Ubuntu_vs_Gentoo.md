I have been a Gentoo user and fan for a couple of years.  Gentoo can be faster then Ubuntu depending on if one knows what they are doing when setting it up.  The problem with Gentoo is that it takes a lot of time to prepare and do a Gentoo install and then to maintain it.  I remember upgrading from GCC 3.4 to 4.1, it took about 3 days to rebuild all the packages. The box was still useable while I was doing this but it was still a real pain.  On the plus side Gentoo is a distro that gives you tons of control from the start, what logger, kernal, cron, to what services and apps.  Everything on it is because you put it there, it gives you complete power on deciding what you want.  Plus Gentoo has more packages IMO.  Now that being said the whole time thing really is a big pain, and was one of the number one reasons I use Ubuntu.  I just don't have the time anymore to really mess around, plus it is really nice to have things just work out of the box.  Ubuntu is really nice that way, when I installed Ubuntu on my laptop, all the mulitmedia and special keys worked flawlessly, I did not have to do anything, with Gentoo it would have been hours maybe days figuring out changes I would need to do in xorg.conf to make it all work right.  Just my 2 cents.  Use Gentoo if you have time and love to have complete control, but understand you will spend a lot of time troubleshooting, use Ubuntu if you want things to work out of the box.  Ubuntu is just a really well put together distro.
