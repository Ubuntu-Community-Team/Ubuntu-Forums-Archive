### Post by DuckHook on 2020-05-21
Please don't take this the wrong way, but your question is bit like asking: is it safe to walk the street at night wearing a particular brand of shoes?

The risky activity is not the brand of shoes; it's walking the street at night. If you practice good risk management, don't do dumb things, stay on safe avenues and are always on the lookout for trouble, then it's relatively safe to walk at night. If you do the opposite of those things, then no brand of shoes will save you.

It's not an exact analogy, but hopefully, it conveys the key concept.

You will have to make your own assessment about Facebook. Ubuntu has almost nothing to add or subtract from that assessment.

### Post by TheFu on 2020-05-22
Facebook has never been safe to use, regardless of OS.  Don't put anything on facebook you wouldn't want on the front page of the NYT, London Times and The Standard, etc.

If you want to contact your family, use email, preferably from a non-huge provider who actually values your privacy. If you value privacy, then both you and the recipient should use **gpg** encryption for the messages.  Stay away from centralized messaging/voice run by the big internet tracking companies like google, facebook, apple, msft, oracle, IBM, amazon and their partners.  Assume everything is recorded, saved, analyzed.

It amazes me how many people are willing to give such private details of their lives to complete strangers (the companies) over the internet. Marking posts as "friends only" is just a toggle on a DB query.  Programmers make mistakes all the time and those queries aren't perfect.  Random people see private data all-the-time.  This applies to any data in a DBMS. It is really surprising it doesn't get reported more often.

Don't even think about using SMS/text messages on the phone network. Those have zero privacy.

For the non-technical people, using something like telegraph is probably the best you can do.  

My family uses RetroShare and a private Nextcloud server to share stuff.  RetroShare was a little difficult for some to setup and get working. I run the main node that is always on the internet, under my physical control, so all the others can communicate.  
Same for our Nextcloud server.  I physically control it. It has chat, voice, and video conferencing capabilities. 5 of us can hop on a video chat on my limited bandwidth. Works pretty well.  It wasn't a 1-click in stall like most nextcloud addons, but it wasn't THAT hard either.

How much is your security and privacy worth? We each have to ask that question, consider our knowledge and options. I feel bad for people who have no technical skills, since they don't think there are any options to withhold their data and retain privacy. It isn't always easy. There are trade-offs, since half my extended family uses facebook and doesn't care/understand what they've given up.  Kids under 30 have given up trying to retain privacy. There isn't any easy answer and just a few mistakes break the privacy veil.  Then there's all your friends posting photos of you, then tagging your name/profile thinking they are helpful. They are not.

I've been blocking facebook, twitter, and as much google tracking as possible for over a decade.  Been blocking ads in a similar way about 20 yrs.  But even I have compromised to allow gmail SMTP connections. I've seriously considered blocking all gmail, yahoo, outlook, hotmail and other centralized email messages from my server, but I'd be like a water droplet in a raging river trying to go upstream.
