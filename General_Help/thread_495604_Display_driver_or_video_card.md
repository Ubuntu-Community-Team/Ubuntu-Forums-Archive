### Post by labtek on 2007-07-08
Hello to the group. I recently installed Kubuntu Feisty Fawn and I really like how it looks and works. In particular, of the various Linux distros that I've tried, this is the only one that I can use my HP 1315 3-in-1 printer/scanner/copier in the scan mode. I was very pleased about that feature.

The display resolution is maxxed out at 1024x768 at 60 Hz on my KDS flat panel 19 inch LCD monitor and as a result, the system fonts and icons are quite large and I get a lot of scroll bars. I tried another video card with the same result. The monitor is detected as a generic monitor in Kubuntu Device Database and there are no listings for KDS monitors.

I'd like to get a higher resolution for the system. I'm using a high end Pentium 3 or low end Pentium 4 running around 1 GB CPU speed and 256 MB RAM with a fresh install of Kubuntu after zero filling the 40 GB Seagate hard disk.

Is this a video card or display driver issue (or both)?

Thanks in advance for any thoughts or advice.

### Post by labtek on 2007-07-09
Hello to the group. Thanks to all who read my post.

I seem to have solved this problem. During my initial install of Kubuntu, I used the computer connected to a Samsung CRT monitor. I then substituted the Samsung with my KDS flat panel LCD monitor and then started having the resolution problem. After much fiddling and twiddling, I reloaded Kubuntu with the computer connected to the LCD monitor and suddenly, I had a screen resolution of 1280x1024 which seems perfect for me. There were no other hardware changes.

It seems to me that the DVD loaded the correct driver for the monitor during the install.

Perhaps this might assist others who are having a similar problem.

Thanks again.

Kubuntu is a great OS.
