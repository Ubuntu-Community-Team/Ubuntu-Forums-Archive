### Post by xENObRAIN on 2005-11-27
The way you phrase your post you look like you're looking to stir up a kde vs gnome debate. Don't. There's a reason there are so many windows managers available for gnu/linux- choice. GNOME and KDE are great, but I've met people who swear by the fluxbox way too.

Every WM has a distinct way of doing things, and obviously certain people are going to be more comfortable with a certain way of doing things, most likely dependant on thier previous computing experience.

As for my love of KDE, the interface is great, very customizeable and intuitive for me. But really, it's the KDE projects apps that keep me coming back. Of course any WM will handle them fine but they simply work best under KDE.

My 2 cents...

edit::: btw, if you think KDE is bloated, check out kde-lite.
