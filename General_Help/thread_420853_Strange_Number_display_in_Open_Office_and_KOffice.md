### Post by BFris on 2007-06-05
Well, I'll answer my own question. I upgraded to Feisty Fawn hoping that would fix my troubles. That wasn't very helpful

Then I deleted all of my OpenOffice configuration files located in the .openoffice.org2 directory in /home/<username>. I wiped out the whole directory and reinstalled OpenOffice. That did the trick. If I had done some more work, I might have been able to find the offending setting. But I didn't have any custom dictionaries or other important settings I cared to save.

Now OpenOffice is actually usable! I don't have to switch to Windoze to do word processing or spreadsheets, etc. Hallelujah!
