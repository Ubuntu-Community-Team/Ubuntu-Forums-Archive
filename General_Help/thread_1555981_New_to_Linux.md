### Post by pizza-is-good on 2010-08-18
Hi there, welcome to Ubuntu/Linux!

The best protection against malware is yourself. Don't surf iffy sites and so forth; the usual precautions.

That being said, there is very little malware and viruses for linux and ubuntu. While they are not non-existant, the chances of getting a computer infected are almost zero. I have never had a computer infection in ubuntu in the year and a half that I have using it, but then again, I have never gotten a virus in windows in the 15 years I've been using it.:lolflag:

Here is the catch. The viruses that exist in linux need to have the user's permission to do anything. Just be careful when you type your root/admin password and make sure it is for something you want the computer to do for you.

Probably the most dangerous thing in linux are malicious commands. Read this thread about them [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/announcement.php?f=326"). Linux assumes you know what you are doing, so if someone abuses the fact that you might not know what you are doing, then what might happen would probably not be pretty.

If you are unsure of a command you found online, you can always type ```
man
``` before it, to check what it does.

I am not trying to discourage you from using linux. It is great. It is the safest 'line' of operating systems that exist. Also, the tech support is great. The people that help you in these forums for free are way better than Microsoft tech support :p.

Let me/us know of anything you need help while you explore and learn ubuntu. We are here to help.

Once again, welcome!

~pizza
