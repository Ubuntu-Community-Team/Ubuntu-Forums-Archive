### Post by SFN on 2005-09-08
It appears that the problem is that MSNBC checks for specific versions of IE, Windows Media Player and Flash. If you don't have that combo, you can't even get to the location containing the videos. Chances are if you could get to that location, you could see them just fine. CNN has the same "requirements" and even tells you that you don't meet those requirements but has a "try anyway" type button and when you press that button the videos play.

So this is just another lockout attempt by Microsoft.

If you must have content from MSNBC, look into using Wine. That should allow you to run the MS apps that MSNBC claims you must have.

Personally, I'd just use another site.
