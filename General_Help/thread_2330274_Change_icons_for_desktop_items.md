### Post by deadflowr on 2016-07-10
Right click on the icon.
Go to properties.
Then simply click on the icon picture in the top left corner of the properties window and navigate the file manager pop-up to where the new image is.

Of course, afaik, this only changes the icon for that one item.
(But also, afaik, the change persists forever, regardless of whether or not you also change the system's icon scheme.)

### Post by Frogs Hair on 2016-07-10
Right click the icon, select properties, click the icon image, and navigate to the folder and icon you would like to use. When you have selected the icon select open on the bottom right of the select custom icon window.

### Post by QDR06VV9 on 2016-07-11
All that has been said holds true..but a walk through might help.
For this I just created a plain document on the Desktop.
Now I right click that document and go down to properties and click that.
In the Basic Tab you should see a blank or plain icon in the left hand side.
Click that and you now change the Icon to a stock icon located in /usr/share/icons..there is a lot to chose from in that location.
Or you can add one from your own files but be aware that photo must be in a .svg or .png format
