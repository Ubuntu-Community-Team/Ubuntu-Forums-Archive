### Post by Temposs on 2008-10-09
Two possibilities I can think of here.

The first is that your hard drive has failed and is not booting properly. Do you think this is possible? I have a Toshiba Satellite M35(similar to yours, I think), and my hard drive died a year and a half ago. However, since you say you reinstalled gutsy, I'm not sure about this idea, since maybe an install wouldn't work on a borked hard drive. But, it could be the hard drive error is not occurring where gutsy got installed, but affects the boot process.

The second is that something is wrong with X, although I'm not sure why it doesn't boot you to command line. Maybe you should try booting in recovery mode, to see if that works.
