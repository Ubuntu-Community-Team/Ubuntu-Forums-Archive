Way back in Heron release, when Open office upgraded, it was devoid of any icons. Instead, there were only text and no icons on the menu bars, task bars, or toolbars.

It turned out that the icons failed to install.

However, I thought that the missing icons were better because it's easier for me to locate buttons just by reading the text. One of those "it's not a bug, it's a feature" thing. I work with windows and other desktop environments and unless the icons are the same or similar, I end up hovering over the icons and reading them anyway.


Does anyone know how to disable icons in open office? I would like to have them disabled instead of being deleted. Does deleting icons break Open office?

### Post by todak on 2009-02-13
Under **Tools> Customize** click the **Toolbars** tab. On the right side is a drop-down box entitled **Toolbar**. Click on it and a menu will drop down and enable you to select icons only, text and icons or text only. After selecting, click OK and there you are! :)
