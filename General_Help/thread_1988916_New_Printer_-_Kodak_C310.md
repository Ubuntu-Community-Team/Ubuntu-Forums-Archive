> Yeah and how much do the cartridges cost on a HP? I could switch to win7 every time i need to print smthg off.

 Consumables is why I like Brother. A set of refillable cartridges and bulk ink.  [http://www.ebay.com/itm/4-Refillable-ink-cartridge-Brother-LC71-LC75-LC77-73-LC79-printer-pre-filled-ink-/220951441695?pt=BI_Toner&hash=item3371bb711f](http://www.ebay.com/itm/4-Refillable-ink-cartridge-Brother-LC71-LC75-LC77-73-LC79-printer-pre-filled-ink-/220951441695?pt=BI_Toner&hash=item3371bb711f)  [http://www.inkowl.com/index.php?C=10&S=25](http://www.inkowl.com/index.php?C=10&S=25)  are a couple sources. The useful thing is that Brother cartridges have no 'kill me' chips or other electronics in the cartridge.  I buy bulk ink locally.  I had some nozzle plugging with pre-filled refillable cartridges so buy empty cartridges and use locally acquired bulk ink that I've had good luck with.  I know the older HP cartridges were spendy.  We retired an HP-940C (It still works great) when a color refill was $39 and we got the Brother MFC-J430W with ink for $69.  The HP 61 series cartridges seem more reasonable.
