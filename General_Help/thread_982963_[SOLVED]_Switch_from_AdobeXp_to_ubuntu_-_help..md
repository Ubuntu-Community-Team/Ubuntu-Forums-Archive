### Post by TeXtonyx on 2008-11-15
Wine works pretty good on CS2 not on CS3 or CS4; ok for office 2003
but not 2007 yet. So yes, it is my expert opinion that you need to
dual boot. Look before you leap. Install Ubuntu and test Wine first
before you get rid of XP. It might take you a few weeks to make a
transition. VirtualBox also is a good idea. But install it and have
it working for any key application before you delete XP. You should
practice and get this working first, it isn't going to fall in place
for you in a day or a week, so do you want to do without while you
progress the learning curve? That means dual booting until you are
on safe ground. I doubt that you could install Word 2007 in Wine
if you spent 8 hours trying. You'd have to be asking questions on 
a forum and waiting for responses or doing Google searches and
wading through lots of results. Dual booting is quite obvious. 
To start with you will have more problems than solutions. For 
instance, do you have a high-end video card, Ati or nVidia?
That can take a few hours and that is before you arrive at a 
functional status (the driver). Do you know about permissions? 

[http://tldp.org/guides.html](http://tldp.org/guides.html)
Introduction to Linux - A Hands on Guide

If you are quite smart, then it will take you a few weeks rather
than a few months. It takes awhile to get up to speed/productive.

### Post by Ginsly on 2008-11-15
Ubuntu is a live disc... After you've burned the iso image file to disk, when you pop it in your dvd drive and boot up, the whole OS will load up and run in RAM, without touching anything on your hard drive... So you can play around with it, and see if all your hardware works first before you decide to install...

Just make sure the settings in your BIOS are set so that your computer boots from the DVD drive first, and the hard drive second... If u dnt know how to do that, have a look for a message that flashes up telling you to push a particular key (esc, or F1 something like that) just after you push the power button..
