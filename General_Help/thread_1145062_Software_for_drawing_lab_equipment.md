### Post by fiendishlyclever on 2009-05-01
I teach science in a UK secondary school.  In my windows days I used a piece of software to draw science diagrams from a company called [Focus]("http://www.focuseducational.com/html/product_overview.php/pid/2").

I was wondering if anyone had come across something similar for Linux with a bank of clipart for commonly used pieces of lab equipment (e.g. beaker, test tube).  I've tried Wine but the software didn't work.

This is one of the few pieces of software I haven't found a linux equivalent for.

Thanks

### Post by ohzopants on 2009-05-01
In my days as a student I used to do this with OpenOffice Draw.  It doesn't actually have premade clip art (that I know of), but over the years I managed to create my own library of "tools" that I needed to insert into diagrams.

Dia is another diagram program I toyed with briefly.  It has some shape libraries included, although I'm not sure they have one for chemistry.

[http://projects.gnome.org/dia/](http://projects.gnome.org/dia/)
