### Post by danwood76 on 2009-10-17
VLC doesnt require codecs, the VLC team wrote X.264 (H.264 codec).

If an x264 wont play on your system it could be caused by slow hardware.
x264 is much more CPU intensive than xvid.

I havent ever had problems with playing x264 in Ubuntu and VLC should play it with no issues, there could also be a problem with the file.

I would guess that either the file is not x264 or corrupt.

### Post by DeMus on 2009-11-12
Solved the problem:

I had Cairo-dock installed and that caused the crashing of VLC. Now I un-installed it and I can watch the movie without any problem.
I looked on Google for similar problems and people mentioned Compiz (which is also a pain in the butt when talking stability). I don't use Compiz but I had Cairo-dock installed. So I thought, I'll try without that, rebooted and my problems (well this problem) are gone.
