### Post by northrup on 2010-01-01
[This]("http://www.ima.umn.edu/~arnold/omnibook/specs.pdf") is the laptop you are working with, right?

Do you have a floppy drive module?  One might have come with your laptop (for some reason, the Omnibook 6000 doesn't seem to have any drives permanently built-in; the drives themselves are all removable...).  You could create a GRUB boot disk to boot into Ubuntu.  Check the [GRUB homepage]("http://www.gnu.org/software/grub/grub.html") for further information on that topic.  You should be able to perform all the steps required with a blank floppy disk and your Ubuntu LiveCD.

Personally, I think Ubuntu as it is would be a bit too resource-intensive for 512 MB RAM and a 700 MHz at most CPU.  You could try an alternate Ubuntu distribution, such as [Xubuntu]("http://www.xubuntu.org/") or [Fluxbuntu]("http://fluxbuntu.org/"), both of which are intended for lower-end computers.

Best of luck with your installation :)
