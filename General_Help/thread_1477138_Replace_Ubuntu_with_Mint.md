### Post by WorMzy on 2010-05-08
I'm afraid that I don't think it's possible. You should backup all your personal files (located in /home/<username>) first, then I suppose you could try installing Mint over the top of Ubuntu without reformatting the partition first; however, this is potentially dangerous, and may cause a lot of problems, so I cannot recommend doing it. The best way forward is to just backup your files, reformat the partition, install a fresh copy of Mint, and redownload any packages you need.
