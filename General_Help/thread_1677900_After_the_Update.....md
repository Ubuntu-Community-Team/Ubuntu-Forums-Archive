### Post by Darkness Des on 2011-01-29
RPMs and DEBs are very similar, just two different ways of packaging the same thing. However, they are processed by different package managers. On an RPM system, you will use RPM files. On a DEB system, you will use DEB files. Ubuntu is a DEB system.

As *Darkness Des* explained above. Another explanation would be .exe for Windows, .rpm for Fedora/Red Hat, .deb for Debian/Ubuntu/Linux Mint. You'll discover everything in the recent times ;-)

The best and easiest way to install any software in Ubuntu is to go to Applications > Software Center and search for your software and click Install. And guess what, you are done.
