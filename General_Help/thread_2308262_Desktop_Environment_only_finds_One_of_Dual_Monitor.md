### Post by reneviht on 2016-01-04
I don't have the problem anymore for some reason.

I tried switching from my NVIDIA card to my motherboard's built-in video card, to see if I could get multi-monitor support that way. Like a fool, I forgot to install the drivers for the motherboard's card, so of course nothing showed on either monitor after the switch. However, when I switched back, both monitors work in a dual-screen setup. I had to switch their positioning in [FONT=courier new]nvidia-settings[/FONT], but that was trivial.

I'm not changing the thread title to **[SOLVED]**, however, because I have no idea what caused the problem in the first place. Furthermore, I have no reason to think the problem won't happen again any time I reboot my computer.
