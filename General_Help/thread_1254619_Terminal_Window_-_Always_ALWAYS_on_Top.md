### Post by DaithiF on 2009-08-31
Hi, are you familiar with guake -- nothing to do with always on top :) but it gets to the same end-goal, ie. a terminal within easy reach (ie. one keystroke and it slides into view).

or, install wmctrl which allows you to script window actions very easily, by assigning shortcut keys then not just your terminal, but any other of your often-used apps can be brought to the front & focused with a keystroke.
