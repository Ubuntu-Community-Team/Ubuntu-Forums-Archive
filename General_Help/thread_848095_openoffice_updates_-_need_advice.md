### Post by fsando on 2008-07-04
Hi
In my view the issue is that applications are up to date only right after a new Ubuntu release. Some apps are kept in sync during the whole release cycle orhters are not. Openoffice wasn't kept up to date during 7.04 and 7.10. The differences between the two weren't great or serious but a few known and annoying bugs were not addressed in Ubuntu's version. Therefore a lot of people (me included) chose the one from Openoffice's site.

After that the old Ubuntu ones sat in the package manager as 'updates'. Another issue has been that there are several packaged versions of Ooo floating around and one of them that I tried caused corrupted packages etc.

I don't know this but my feeling is that the reason for the slow update of Ooo is that it's a very complicated app to support and having a working version is far more important than having 'the newest', so if it works it's simply kept steady until next Ubuntu release even if it means living with some annoyances.

I suppose that how well-synced a package is also depends on how important the developers think Ubuntu is. I've seen some encouraging changes in apps that I use where they now have updated ubuntu packages on their sites. This also takes the burden away from the Ubuntu-devs.
