### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-04-26
Linux supports most hardware, so pretty much anything you buy should work.  The only two pieces of hardware that might be problematic are wireless Internet cards (for which support recently got quite a bit better with the release of several new drivers), and video cards.  nVidia cards are generally well-supported.  ATI cards give some people problems.

Wal-mart sells Dell and Toshiba computers (at least according to their website), both of which I've had good experiences using with Linux.  I know you said you don't want a laptop, but in terms of energy consumption, they really are the way to go.  Otherwise, look for a computer with an [80-Plus]("http://www.80plus.org/") certified PSU.
