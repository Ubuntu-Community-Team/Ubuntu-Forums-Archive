### Post by Stochastic on 2008-06-19
The best way to get timecoded vinyl to work in Linux is with either MIXXX (Hardy's version has timecoded vinyl support) or with XWAX.  Both programs are open source and use the same library for decoding the vinyls, which supports a variety of formats (serato, finalscratch, traktor, etc...).  This is by far the cheapest way of doing things too.

My attempts at getting FinalScratch to work were very sad and I soon realized the poor quality of the included soundcard was just not worth the effort.
