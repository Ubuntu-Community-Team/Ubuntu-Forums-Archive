### Post by pseudonym on 2008-12-13
It depends what you use your system for. If you have a 5200 i guess you're not into heavy gaming, so maybe a basic model nvidia card of a more recent vintage is what you're looking for.

2nd hand or new? The equivalent models from later series are 6200, 7300, 8400, and 9500 IIRC. The latest 200 series is brand new and it doesn't have a budget model yet, I don't think. In any case, they're damned expensive.

Also consider your motherboard - AGP or PCI-E? I think nvidia stopped making AGP cards after the Geforce 8 series.

Support for ATI cards has improved this year. The latest models are still a bit iffy (I'm finding that out by bitter experience), but a card like the X1300 should work well. Unlike with nvidia cards, though, you'll want to be using the open-source driver.

HTH
