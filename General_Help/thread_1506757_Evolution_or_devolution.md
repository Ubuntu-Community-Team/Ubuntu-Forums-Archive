### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-06-10
I'll assume your referencing your Evolution inbox e-mail and not what's on the server.

Just a thought, but when setting up filters one needs to be careful with terms such as "any", "all","starts with", etc.

Have you tried removing all the filters to see if any of the filters are deleting all your e-mail. Was the deletion of e-mail from your inbox an issue prior to creating any filters?

Do the e-mails show up in the trash folder or are they being expunged (deleted, bypassing the trash folder)? Generally, when an e-mail is deleted (even by a filter) it's moved to the trash folder. If any folder is expunged all the e-mails in that folder are permanently deleted and not moved to the trash folder.

When is the deletion taking place - prior to closing Evolution or after Evolution is closed and then re-opened. Or, are your e-mail being deleted immediately upon download and not being sent to the trash folder?

*** After downloading your e-mail check your trash folder and see the e-mail your wanting to keep are in the trash. If so, I suspect a filter is sending them there and your deleting your trash upon exiting Evolution. Then when reopening Evolution all your e-mail have been expunged from the trash folder.**

**** Also check your Junk folder. If your junk  filter is turned on, any e-mail's identified as junk will be moved to that folder on download. If Evolution is setup to delete junk on exit (edit/preferences), the same thing will happen as if it were sent to trash by a filter (you setup). If the e-mails your wanting to keep are located in the junk folder after download, right click on the ones you want to continue receiving in the future and mark them as "not junk".**

Good Luck & I hope this helps.
