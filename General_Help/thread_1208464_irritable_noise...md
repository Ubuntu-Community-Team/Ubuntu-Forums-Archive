### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-07-09
> **kuldeepsidhu said:**
> so this cannot be cured..but the sound is very irritable and very high...

If you don't mind messing around inside your computer, you could install a quieter CPU cooler. When you buy a CPU, the manufacturer also bundles in a very basic, noisy, ineffective "stock" cooler. If you open your computer and see a fan in there with the Intel or AMD logo, then you've got a stock cooler.

You will first need to unplug the computer and remove the current cooler; your computer's manual might tell you how to do this, otherwise there should be instructions available on your CPU manufacturer's website. The CPU will have a thin layer of "thermal paste" still stuck to it - clean this off with a scourer as much as you can.

Now follow the instructions that come with your new cooler. There's often a little bit of preparation that you need to do to the cooler, and you will need to apply some fresh thermal paste to the CPU (put a pea-sized drop on the middle of the CPU, when you push the cooler onto it and turn it left and right like a steering wheel, you will spread the paste properly).

When the cooler is mounted, plug in the fan into the set of pins marked "CPU_FAN" on the motherboard.

Some coolers will come with a fan and thermal paste, some will come with one, some will come with none. The cooler I'm using is the Noctua NH-C12P; it's very quiet and disperses the heat really well. It also has the advantage of fitting into cases that the tower-style coolers won't. Plus, it looks pretty awesome, comes with a fan and thermal paste, and if you scan in a copy of your reciept and send it to Noctua they'll send you a sheet of stickers and a case badge!

The instructions were pretty good with the Noctua too.

Installing your cooler may require taking the motherboard out and installing a back-plate on the CPU socket. It might not. Also, do some research and find out what type of socket your CPU uses, and check that the cooler you buy is compatible and includes the equipment you need to connect it to your socket.

If you want to make your computer quiet, replace the CPU cooler and optionally replace the case fan, for something quiet. As I've mentioned, I love Noctua's fans and coolers, but some people swear by other brands :-)
