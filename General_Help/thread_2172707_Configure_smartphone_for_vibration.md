### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-09-06
As already noted it is not easy to confirm features of software that is still under development. Also, the vibration feature will only work if the hardware for it is present. I do not have a mobile phone but I think that a silent (vibrating) alarm must be standard going back to the days when mobile phones were not smart.

Clock and Calandar apps have been a requirement of Ubuntu Touch from the beginning of the idea of a Ubuntu phone/tablet platform.
> 
Clock was one of the original set of apps we decided should be included in the  Core Apps project. It rapidly became mostly functionally complete with  only final design work, location detection and alarm capability  outstanding.

[http://popey.com/blog/2013/09/02/ubuntu-touch-clock-and-calculator-hack-day/](http://popey.com/blog/2013/09/02/ubuntu-touch-clock-and-calculator-hack-day/)

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/CoreApps/Clock](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/CoreApps/Clock)

My advice would be not to buy a Ubuntu phone without having a hands-on session. This applies to any phone/tablet on the market.
