I have a triple-booted aspire one with xp, 7, and fedora. 

It works with regular ubuntu but you have to add drivers for wifi after the install. Fedora however works almost perfectly out-of-the-box.

Mac Osx works on it *without wifi*

Many things like Ubuntu Netbook Remix and eeebuntu and Ubuntu eee also work but I haven't tried them yet.

I would suggest the aspire one or lenovo. I think the lenovo might be better if you are looking to improve the parts (ram, HDD). I've added ram to the aspire one and it requires you take out the motherboard.

Edit: I would highly suggest getting a [sata/ide-to-USB adapter]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16812232002"). They are around 20 dollars and work great. No more messing around with flash drives.
