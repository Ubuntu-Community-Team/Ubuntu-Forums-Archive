### Post by WorMzy on 2010-12-06
OS is the label of one of your other partitions, the other one (213 GB Filesystem) doesn't have a label, so it gives you a description it instead. "Mount" it the term used for making the contents of the partition accessible while inside Ubuntu. If you click one of these menu items, it will open a folder with the partition "mounted" onto it, allowing you to access files from these partitions.

It may help you to think of these partitions as D:\ and E:\, which is what Windows would probably list them as.

Oh, and for your other question: you don't need to do anything about it, it's perfectly normal. :)
