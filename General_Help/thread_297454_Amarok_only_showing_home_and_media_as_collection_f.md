### Post by Jucato on 2006-11-11
You still have full control on your system. If you didn't have such control, you wouldn't be able to alter this behavior at all.

It is not Microsoft only that assumes anything about its users. Apple, GNOME, and even KDE work on certain assumptions, otherwise everything will be chaotic. Even the so-called "defaults" work on a certain assumptions. So to be fair, lets not point to any implementation that hides things as "Microsoft-like", because they are not the only ones to do so. The /home directory itself is testament to that.
