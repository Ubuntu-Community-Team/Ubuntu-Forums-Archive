### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-10-05
The pop is *completely* normal and exactly what you would expect.

Solution: turn your speakers down or switch them off before shutting down your computer. Simple. I'm an audio person and musician, among other things, and this is 'rule of thumb'. Before switching on and turning off amps, always turn the amp/speakers down. Some amps even have a speaker mute button for this very reason (try pulling the cord out of an electric guitar when you have the amp volume up. Ouch!).

Your computer has a little amp, you may have your computer plugged into and amp and your speakers plugged in to that, or maybe your speakers are powered. In every case it is exactly the same in principle. In other words, you effectively have speakers connected to an amp that is not turned down when you switch your computer off. 

Yes, continued popping, particularly at high volumes, can damage speaker cones. Switch them off or turn them down, *then* turn off your computer. There is no special trick or fix to this. There may be other ways around it using a script or manually turning down volumes before log out, no idea, but wouldn't bother personally. Good luck. ;)
