### Post by jyba on 2007-08-06
Mr Mandela speaks English with a strong accent, and I'm not sure I understood every word he said, but this is my transcript (if anyone can improve on it, please do):

**INTERVIEWER TIM MODISE**: Many people regard you as a personification of Ubuntu. What do you understand Ubuntu to be?

**NELSON MANDELA**: In the old days, when we were young, a traveller through a country would stop at a village and he didn't have to ask for food or for water. Once he stops, the people give him food; entertain him. That is one aspect of Ubuntu, particular of various aspects ...
	
... Ubuntu does not mean that people should not address themselves. The question therefore is "Are you going to do so in order to enable the community around you to be able to improve?" These are the important things in life and if one can do that you have done something very important which will be apreciated.
