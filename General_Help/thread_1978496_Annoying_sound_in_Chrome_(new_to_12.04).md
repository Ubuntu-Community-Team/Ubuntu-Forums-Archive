### Post by coldjeanz on 2012-05-11
Yeah it's from the sound effects. If you go to System Settings > Sound > Sound Effects and mute it then the sounds should go away. The only thing is that it will mute all sound effects so if you like certain sound effects it's kind of an all or nothing situation here. I just ended up lowering the volume of the sound instead of muting it all together.

### Post by doriad on 2012-05-12
I guess I forgot to mention I was in KDE. I found the solution here: [http://askubuntu.com/questions/130474/how-do-i-turn-off-click-button-sounds-in-kde-in-ubuntu-12-04-lts](http://askubuntu.com/questions/130474/how-do-i-turn-off-click-button-sounds-in-kde-in-ubuntu-12-04-lts)

"Run gnome-control-center inside KDE then go to Sounds and in the next screen, click on the "Sound effects" tab and click on the on/off button on the right."

Works like a charm.
