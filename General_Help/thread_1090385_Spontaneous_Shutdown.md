### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-03-08
I have experienced this with Windows. The problem was a melted down motherboard component. The computer worked several days even with this problem, until I realize the real problem. Sometimes it simply shut down and the only way to make it work again was to unplug it from the power outlet.

I also experienced this once with my new machine running Ubuntu. The problem was a drop down in power supply, fast enough that I couldn't perceive it in the surroundings but enough to mess with the machine. I also experienced a couple of similar situations recently, but instead of shutting down, the machine froze displaying very weird bars on the screen. Again, the only way to reboot was to unplug it from the outlet. The problem was a faulty power supply, but I was aware of it because my house lamps were blinking very fast. 

I don't know why this happens or if it is your situation, but maybe this a clue towards power supply issues.

### Post by wpshooter on 2009-03-08
If you happen to have another computer that is also running Hardy, put it on this power outlet and see if it does the same thing.  If it does then you know you have a power supply problem.  If not, then there is something whacked in your O/S or computer hardware problem.
