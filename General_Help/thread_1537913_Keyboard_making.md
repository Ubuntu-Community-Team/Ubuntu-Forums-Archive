### Post by kaldor on 2010-07-24
If you don't mind doing a bit of a hillbilly job, you can just use sticky keys on a regular keyboard.

[http://wiki.laptop.org/images/e/eb/Urdu-MP.png](http://wiki.laptop.org/images/e/eb/Urdu-MP.png)

Look at that picture, and use sticky keys (or make your own labels) to place on the keyboard, then set the keyboard layout to Urdu in GNOME (or whatever you are using).
