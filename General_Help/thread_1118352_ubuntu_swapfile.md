### Post by andyswarbs on 2009-04-06
It all depends on what you do with your computer, that is what programs you run, and with what data.  

If all you're doing is editing text files with a simple editor, then no.  If you are running mulitple applications concurrently and some of them are either heavy in their nature, or the datasets you are working with are big, then yes.  For example, working with a very large document, say a book, with lots of high-quality images embedded in it is bound to stress your computer.

On the other hand what harm is there in creating a swap file, even if it never gets used!  That is assuming you have sufficient disk space, which should go with 3gig ram.
