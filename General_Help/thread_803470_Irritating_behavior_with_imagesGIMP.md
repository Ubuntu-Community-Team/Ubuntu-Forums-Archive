### Post by stream303 on 2008-05-23
Unless I'm reading you wrong, this is the way the Gimp UI is designed.  Each successive opening of an image brings up another image window, so you have to manually close the other one.

I think this is what you are seeing, rather than two instances of Gimp loading - just two images loading.

Since Gimp is an editor, this design makes it easy to say open up two instances of the same image, and you can easily see the effects of your edits as compared to the master image.

In your case, I can see how it might be annoying.
