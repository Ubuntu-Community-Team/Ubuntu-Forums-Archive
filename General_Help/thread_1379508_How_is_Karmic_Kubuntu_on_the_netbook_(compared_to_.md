### Post by garryknight on 2010-01-12
I've been running Kubuntu on my EeePC 701 4G and my Advent 4211-B (an MSI Wind-clone). Kubuntu 9.10 was installed from scratch on the EeePC. I installed 9.04 on the Advent then upgraded to 9.10, so it still has some 9.04 features (Grub 1, for example).

The EeePC runs well, even with the KWin effects all turned on. It only has 1 GB of RAM and has no swap (< 4GB storage) so there are times when it's not so snappy, but it's certainly usable. I've just installed Mandriva 2010 One on it for a comparison and it doesn't seem quite so smooth.

9.10 on the Advent also runs well, just as well as 9.04 did, though the webcam doesn't work despite (what looks like) the correct modules being installed. It's a Microdia Sonix camera so unless you have a 701 4G with one of those, I'd say go for it.
