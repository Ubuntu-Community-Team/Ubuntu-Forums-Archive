### Post by RAOF on 2005-12-16
Rhythmbox is installed by default and is pretty good as a media-library type music player.  You'll need to install extra codecs in order for (anything) to play mp3s, though.  Just follow [these instructions]("http://help.ubuntu.com/starterguide/C/faqguide-all.html#sect-music-and-movies").

### Post by aysiu on 2005-12-16
In my experience... JuK is pretty good.

On my machine, Rhythmbox and AmaroK are a bit sluggish (take a long time to refresh the library and don't have instantaneous response when I want to move to the next song).

JuK also (like AmaroK) has global keyboard shortcuts you can define and have work in Gnome, KDE, and XFCE. That way you can quickly change tracks no matter what application you're using.

Unfortunately, though:

1. JuK doesn't have some of the advanced features of AmaroK--such as keeping track of how many times you've played a song or being able to fetch the lyrics to a song.

2. It has a weird graphical bug when put in the system tray *and* used with the Kwin-Baghira KDE theme. 
**Edit**: Apparently no longer a bug... I just installed Baghira, and it's good now.

3. It's not a native Gnome application (i.e., it may load more slowly than Rhythmbox in Gnome, and drag-and-drop won't work unless you're dragging and dropping to a Konqueror window).
