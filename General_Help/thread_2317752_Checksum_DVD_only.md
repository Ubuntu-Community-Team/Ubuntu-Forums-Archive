### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-03-19
People come on this forum explaining that after having downloaded and burnt the ISO image the live session isn't loading.

So, in answer we give all possible ways for identifying the problem. Years ago I would only burn a DVD at the slowest speed. And this was because of standard advice. Now, I do not even think about the burn speed. And I have not heard anyone mention burn speed in connection with this subject for a long time.

Poor quality internet connection could be a reason for a corrupted download. Poor quality DVD could be a reason for a poor quality ISO burn. Smudges of oil from finger prints, dust and surface scratches can all contribute as well.

If something works, then away we go. If it fails, then what do we do to avoid wasting time repeating failure? When "third time lucky" fails, what next?

Oh, by the way, I have done lots of installs of Ubuntu over the years and I have never checked a MD5SUM. Not after download and not after burning. Never needed to.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM)
