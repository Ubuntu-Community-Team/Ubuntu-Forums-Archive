### Post by sudodus on 2012-05-16
Standard Ubuntu has the desktop environment (DE) Unity, which is new and quite different, trying to unify the user experience with hand-held devices etc. Furthermore, Gnome 2 is no longer developed, and there will be no security updates (after end of life of Ubuntu 10.04 LTS in April 2013).

But there are other versions of Ubuntu with DEs, that you might recognize better, and you can download the iso files and test them live from a USB drive.

Kubuntu with KDE (powerful DE)
Lubuntu with LXDE (ultra-light DE)
Xubuntu with XFCE (light DE, maybe most similar to Ubuntu with gnome)

You can also modify Ubuntu (from Unity and use Gnome 3 in different ways demanding more or less computing power).
