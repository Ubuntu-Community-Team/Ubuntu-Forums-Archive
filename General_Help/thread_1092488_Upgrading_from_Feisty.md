### Post by measekite on 2009-03-10
I am currently using Feisty Fawn 7.04 and intend to upgrade to something during June or early July.

I have not had problems with Feisty except during installation when I had to track down nvidia drivers to get the 1680x1050 resolution and had to search far and wide to get a driver for my Canon IP4000.  I had to finally install it twice, once for each paper source since the driver did not allow switching and had to give up duplex and borderless since the driver did not support it.  

Currently I tried livecds of OpenSuse, Intrepid, and Fedora 10.  I did not like OpenSuse but I liked Fedora 10 because of the look and feel but more so because it recognized my nvida and gave me the 1680x1050 resolution and it installed my Canon IP4000 and after testing provided all of the features of the printer.

I did not test the printer in Intrepid as yet but Intrepid locked up when attempting to install the default nvida driver so I would imagine I would have to get drivers from nvidia again.  Hopefully they will work like they do in Feisty.

So I have narrowed down my choice between Ubuntu 9.04 when it comes out and Fedora 11.  Both are due around the same time.  I am having difficulty in choosing objectively between Fedora and Ubuntu even after being an Ubuntu user for 2 years and playing with the Fedora livecd for a couple of days.

Fedora seemed a bit faster.

I hope there are enough respondents out there who have used both distros and can shed some insight on their experience that will help me choose.

There is a lot of support for both but I think Ubuntu support is better and the tone of the postings seems more down to earth and less propellar head type postings.  Some say there is more software in the Ubuntu repositories but from what I seem there is enough in both and they are very similar.

So even though no cash is involved it is a difficult choice since I do not especially like changing system frequently.

I would also like to use the system for programming in a language as yet to be determined other than C, C++ or C sharp and using an IDE that is yet to be determined.

I welcome your advice. :popcorn:
