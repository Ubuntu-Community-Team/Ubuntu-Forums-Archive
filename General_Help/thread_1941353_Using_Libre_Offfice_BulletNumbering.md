### Post by Zill on 2012-03-15
TurtleKing:  Not sure how relevant this is but if you are using styles in your document, rather than "direct" formatting, it is possible that the "auto update" checkbox is selected for your particular style.  This means that every change to a single paragraph style (such as bullets) will be reflected throughout the document.

Just open the "Styles and Formatting" dialog box, then right click on the style in use and take a look at the "Organiser" tab.  Deselect "AutoUpdate" and see if this helps.

### Post by forrestcupp on 2012-03-15
If that doesn't do it, make sure to press Enter on the line above where you want the bullet so you make sure that it is actually a new line and not part of the same paragraph. Also, another way you can make a bullet is by typing an Asterisk (*) followed by a space, followed by your sentence. Then when you press Enter, it will convert it to a bullet point and make a new point for the next line, which you can delete if you don't need it.
