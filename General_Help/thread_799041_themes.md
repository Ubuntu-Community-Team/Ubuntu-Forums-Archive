### Post by sistarbliss on 2008-05-18
I downloaded the gk2.x thing, but haven't used it.  My partner somehow crashed his system with it, so be careful! 

The easy way to do it is to drag your theme tarball into the themes window.

system>preferences>appearance>theme tab

This will install the theme. This is where you install icon sets too. You can further tweak out the themes by going to the customize button.  There are tabs for controls, color, window border, icons, etc.   Once you have your theme set the way you want it, you go back to the theme tab and where it says *custom* ...right click on that and save them as whatever you want to call it.  You can keep a special background to match your theme there too.  It will also save your icon sets to go with that theme. 

If you change your theme, the blank one named *custom* will appear again and you can save another.  If your colors get mixed up, you can reset them to default by going to the *colors* tab and resetting it to default. 

Hope this helps!  I twiddled with trying to install my themes the hard way, then finally figured it out with a tutorial on art.gnome.org I think.  I am just now delving into the themes on gnome-look.org

I haven't installed any of the beryl or compiz stuff yet, because I'm afraid that I'll break my system!  You can do quite a lot by using these options though! 

I really like the Enigma theme on gnome-look.org.  If you play with it, you can get your panels to have a very nice glossy look, and if you have two apps or windows open, the one in the background has a sweet tranparency.  

Good Luck & Have Fun!
