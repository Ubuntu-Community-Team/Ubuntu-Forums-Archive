### Post by QIII on 2022-01-22
Either re-installing fresh (which I recommend because it gives you the chance to clear out an attic full of junk) or doing a release upgrade (which I don't recommend because you end up with the same useless junk in the attic.)

The version number represents yy.mm.  Even-numbered years with the release in the 4th month are Long Term Support versions.  I use only those for my daily drivers.  I spin virtual machines for the others.  When 21.10 support ends (better yet, when 22.04 is released), I recommend installing 22.04 to get back into the LTS cycle. That is supported for 3 years.  I typically re-install on the two year LTS cycle rather than waiting the full support period and being off cycle again.

Vanilla Ubuntu, by the way, is supported for 5 years instead of 3 like the flavors.
