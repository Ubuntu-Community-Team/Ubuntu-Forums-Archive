### Post by Robster2 on 2009-12-28
The natural replacement for Microsoft Word is Openoffice.org Word Processor, it has spell check and a thesaurus built in and can save to .doc files.  
At the panel on the bottom right of your screen you will find four desktops right clicking it and selecting preferences you can have as many desktops as you like. Memory is your only limiting factor and memory is cheap.

As this is part of the standard Ubuntu installation I take it you have tried it and found it unsuitable for some reason.  Some of your reasons would help us help you better.

As for light weight I find Tomboy notes very useful I keep it on my panel and when every I find some thing that I want to recall I just click on it and it is there.    

Hope this helps.
