### Post by Miademora on 2008-01-05
Just ask yourself if you have more than 1 application at the same time and if its beneficial for you to have them on the screen at the same time and maybe maximised. Or can your application take advantage of more than 1 screen. If yes then pick the 2 screens. If no then theres no advantage in my experience. For me as a developer having the code and the final result at the same time on the screen is great and the overall increased screenspace makes my workflow  less distracting. Switching between applications gets annoying. For the record im using 3 screens.
