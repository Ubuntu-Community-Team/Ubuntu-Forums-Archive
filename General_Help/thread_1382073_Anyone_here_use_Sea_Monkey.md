### Post by gradinaruvasile on 2010-01-15
Yes, it is better than Firefox speed and stability-wise. The engine is the same - it has Firefox + Thunderbird incorporated in one package.

It isnt that ergonomic though OOTB. But a few extensions (sea tab x would be the first) and you have all Firefox's functionalities - and searching is just like in Chrome/Chromium/Opera, in the address bar. 
Also it has a built-in mail client that is essentially Thunderbird - and this feature comes with little memory penalties. Its damn easy to browse and dont need another program for mail. One click and you can read/write mails. For those interested it has support for PGP (enigmail extension).
I use the generic builds found on their page - no need to wait till someone builds them in PPA, and can be updated from the browser (like in Windows).
