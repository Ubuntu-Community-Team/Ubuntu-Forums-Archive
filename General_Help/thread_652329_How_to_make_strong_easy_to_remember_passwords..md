### Post by MalfunctioningMartian on 2007-12-28
How to make an easy to remember strong password?

First get a witty, memorable phrase e.g.
>  Is "I hope you all die a painful death" too strong?

> Linus to the hardware manufacturers that refuse to release the specifications of their hardware so they could operate with the Linux kernel.

Shorten it
> I"IHYADAPD"TS?

Put the important/emphasised parts in uppercase and the rest in lower.

> I"IhyADaPD"tS?

Twist it. Use your imagination e.g. 1337.

> I"1hyAD4PD"t5?

Add something extra that you think of when you think of the phrase e.g. > <- Linus

Now we get 
> I"1hyAD4PD"t5? <- Linus Something no sane man can call weak. I'd suggest typing it a couple of times to get it into motor memory.

Remember this isn't a fixed guide, don't follow the orders to the letter.

Use your imagination e.g. instead of emphasis put the first 13 letters of the alphabet in uppercase.
