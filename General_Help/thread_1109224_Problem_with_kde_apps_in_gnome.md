### Post by 3Miro on 2009-03-28
In general my philosophy is to avoid mixing Gnome and KDE apps as much as possible. That is particularly true for the post KDE 4.0 apps, Firefox and Geany are unusable under my KDE 4.2 (Konqueror suits my needs just as well as FF would and while KDevelop is heavy, it gets the job done).

Having said that, you can play with the settings: Compiz and/or whatever you are using. There is a QT tab in the Gnome desktop settings (somewhere, I am not sure where). You can try to remove the .gwenview and .marble directories (make sure you have not saved any important files in those). Ultimately you can try to reinstall the apps.

IMHO currently there is no way to make Gnome and KDE apps work well with each other, I have Gnome Laptop and KDE Desktop and using different media players, browsers, CD Burning Software, text/code editors and so on....
