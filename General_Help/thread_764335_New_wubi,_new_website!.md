### Post by ago on 2008-04-25
Absolutely Joshier, and I will have to add you back to the credits for all your great artwork that accompained us since 7.04!!!

In fact I'll provide a bit of insight.

7.04 was wubi first release. For the launch of 7.04 I wanted a style different from the Ubuntu one, since at the time we were a third party project and did not want to create any confusion. At the same time I wanted to convey the idea that we were Windows-friendly. So we decided to have a background reminiscent of the XP famous desktop, but with a Ubuntu sun (which became the Wubi logo). This was my original sketch ([http://wubi-installer.org/art/old/wubiworld.png](http://wubi-installer.org/art/old/wubiworld.png)) and Joshier took it to the next level and did that great design that has been the Wubi hallmark for over a year! [http://wubi-installer.org/art/old/wubi_source_files.svg](http://wubi-installer.org/art/old/wubi_source_files.svg)

For 8.04, I wanted to convey the idea that we are "officially supported", but after having abused of Joshier kindness for days on the previous work, I felt guilty to ask again. I also had some artwork handy to take inspiration (and graphics) from: the brilliant DVD covers of Ubuntu [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork/Incoming/Hardy/Alternate/DVD_Cover_(Fela_Kuti_Chocolate)](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork/Incoming/Hardy/Alternate/DVD_Cover_(Fela_Kuti_Chocolate)) . And here we are, open source at work also on the websites :)!
