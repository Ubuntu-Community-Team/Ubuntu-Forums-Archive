### Post by starcraft.man on 2009-06-27
For a better answer, I'd need to know the specs of this aged machine. It's not really a question of distros, as it is one of desktop environments.

My rough guideline would be and some may disagree:

[Ubuntu]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download") - If the machine is P4 (maybe even P3) or later, 384+ MB RAM, decent dedicated graphics card Nvidia 6000/ati 0600 series or later. Graphics card not required, I guess that's my personal preference.

[Xubuntu]("http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu/xubuntu") - P2 or P3, less than 384 RAM, integrated graphics card.

Xubuntu is a very light distro, it uses the xfce environment and lots of people like it on older hardware.

### Post by snowpine on 2009-06-27
Ubuntu Netbook Remix (despite the name) is not just for netbooks. It's really just Ubuntu with a different user interface (lots of big icons instead of a traditional Gnome desktop). Assuming her system specs are high enough (I would recommend at least pentium 4 with 384mb of ram) it could be a good, user friendly option.

If her specs are very low, an intriguing and minimalist choice is Browserpuppy: [http://browserpuppy.com/](http://browserpuppy.com/)
