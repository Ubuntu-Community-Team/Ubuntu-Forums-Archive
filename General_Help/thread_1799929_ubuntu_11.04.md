### Post by coffeecat on 2011-07-08
> **liquidmonkey said:**
> i'm using 10.10 and am thinking of upgrading to 11.04 but have heard many people having problems and that 11.04 is in fact a BETA release.

11.04 is **not** a beta release. 11.04 went through its beta phase in late March to early/mid April. It was released fully at the end of April. I've seen these comments about 11.04 being "beta". This is typical of the FUD being spread around by a vocal but misguided minority. 

**FACT** - given the right hardware, Natty works very well. I have installed it on 5 different machines and I have no significant issues on any of them.  I prefer 11.04 to 10.10. That's not because I disliked 10.10. I liked it; I simply prefer 11.04

**FACT** - some people are experiencing problems related to hardware, and some older video chipsets that worked well enough in earlier versions of Ubuntu, give problems in 11.04. As always, your mileage may vary (YYMV).

**FACT** - there are a small number of Unity/Natty-haters who make a lot of noise which is out of all proportion to their understanding or importance - in my opinion. :wink:

What would certainly be a sensible precaution would be to download the 11.04 ISO and run it from a live CD or live USB before you upgrade your 10.10. Just to make sure you won't run into any hardware problems. There is one caveat - if you have a nvidia card, the default open source driver will not support the Unity desktop. You would need to install the proprietary driver (or an experimental open source extension to the nouveau driver) before you can get the Unity desktop. Which, of course, is not possible in the live session. What video card do you have?

**EDIT:**
> **liquidmonkey said:**
> is there a huge difference between the two versions?

Yes, the Unity desktop. For more information click on the first link in my sig. If you don't like the Unity desktop - and that's fine - you can log into the classic desktop which is almost the same as what you are used to in 10.10.

### Post by Alwimo on 2011-07-08
11.04 uses something called Unity that runs over Gnome. This means three major changes to the user interface: there's a launcher (dock) along the left hand side of the screen, the Applications/Places/System menu is replaced by a button and you type what you want into a search field, and the application menus (File, Edit...) are now in the top panel instead of being attached to the application's window.

It's buggier than a basic Gnome 2.x-based release, yes. But at this point it's pretty good with that, at least for me. Whether it's worth using is a matter of opinion that you can see for yourself, using a live CD.

You can log into Classic Ubuntu in 11.04 at the log-in screen, if you don't want Unity. Or you could install another desktop environment that you prefer, through Synaptic.
