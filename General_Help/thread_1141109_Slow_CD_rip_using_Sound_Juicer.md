### Post by ASellors on 2009-05-22
Quick update on my own situation - after much trawling through other pages of the Ubuntu forum I stumbled upon a thread which recommended various CD ripping software packages. One of those mentioned was Exact Audio Copy, a Windows program which runs nicely under Wine. The interface is quite complicated to get to grips with, but with a bit of persistence (and pig-headed determination!) I managed to get it working in the way I wanted. After a bit of experimentation with EAC, I am now happily ripping at a brisk 20x speed again, but only after altering the settings in the program so that the CD operates in Burst Mode (quite what that means I have no idea - being non-technically minded, but it works and that's all that matters to me :))

This doesn't solve the problem of why Sound Juicer is now sooo slow, but it has provided a speedy working solution which I can use until such time as I find a way to make Sound Juicer operate in Burst Mode (or whatever the equivalent term used in the Linux world is !)
