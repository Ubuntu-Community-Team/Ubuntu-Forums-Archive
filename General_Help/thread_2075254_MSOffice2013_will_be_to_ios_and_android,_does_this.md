### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-10-23
Don't know.  MS is making a LOT of noise about running Office from the "cloud" -- meaning, you login to their cloud server, send your documents there, and work on them "remotely" -- which is OK if you don't mind MS have access to all your documents.  Personally, I thing the "cloud" is one of the best con-jobs of all time and am NOT going to use it.

As to using Office 2013 on Linux, Wine is most likely NOT going to be able to support MS Office 2013 -- for a LONG time.  It took the Wine folks three years to get Office 2007 working under Wine and by then Office 2010 was out -- and they had to start all over again.

If you do a search, you might be able to find threads where folks, after LOTS of hand-tweaking, got SOME of Office 2010 working under Wine.

That said, if you really need to rely on Office components daily, and want to use a current version, you're most likely going to have to dual-boot with a working MS Windows install, or install VirtualBox, Windows (all over again), and MS Office (all over again).
