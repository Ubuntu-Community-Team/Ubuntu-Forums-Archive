### Post by hyper_ch on 2009-01-19
generally it is advice to have "offsite" backups also. E.g. a lighning could hit the house and then all harddisks could be damages (or fire/flood as you say it).

However besides that, another risk is that if someone can enters that box (physically or remote) then they could possible also damage all your backups there.

An off-site backup is IMHO very recommended.

For fast backups however, you can do it on a different harddisk in there :)

### Post by drs305 on 2009-01-19
It is safe*R*. If you *have* to back up on the same computer, it is certainly better to at least do it on a separate drive. This will protect against data loss due to a primary hard drive failure. It might also be beneficial if the partition table of the primary drive became corrupted for some reason or you did an accidental reformat of the entire drive. 

Of course, as you know, it doesn't protect against damage to the computer the way a removable drive would. You can't just disconnect it and run out of a burning residence. 

So the answer is that it is safe*R*: better than backing up on the same drive, but not optimum. I backup data on a separate drive and an external drive, which is stored in a safe place when not in use. Others back up to remote locations. It depends on the importance of your data, the amount of effort you wish to make, and your financial resources.

### Post by stefangr1 on 2009-01-19
When you look into the causes of hard disk failure you will see that disk crashes, regular failure (electronics), and software issues account for a far majority.

The chance that you drop your computer on the ground, that a lightening strikes your house, or that a failing PSU blows up the system is much smaller. Not to mention fires, hurricanes or floodings: the chance that you'll ever experience one of these is very small relative to the occasions where the second harddrive in the same system would save you.
