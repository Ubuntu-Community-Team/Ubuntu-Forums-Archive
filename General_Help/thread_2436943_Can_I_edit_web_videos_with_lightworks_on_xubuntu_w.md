### Post by TheFu on 2020-02-16
Maybe. [https://www.lwks.com/index.php?option=com_kunena&func=view&catid=21&id=179802&Itemid=81](https://www.lwks.com/index.php?option=com_kunena&func=view&catid=21&id=179802&Itemid=81)
It depends on what you mean by "edit", what the video length is, what the resolution is, how many effects, transitions, etc.

There are many other video editors.  Some don't have a GUI, so it really depends on what you mean by "edit".

Regardless, 2G RAM isn't going to be fun.  Heck, if you are trying to edit 4K video, 29G of free storage won't even allow a local copy of the uncompressed video for an hour.

With snap packages, you might find that you cannot access external storage at all and an additional 1 GB of RAM might be necessary just to load the programs.  Without snaps, I'd not hesitate to say, yes, no problem for accessing NTFS storage connected anywhere.
