### Post by steveneddy on 2010-07-01
Actually you should be calling it Ubuntu Linux, not Linux Ubuntu.

But there are lots of packages available in the official repos and many, many more available from the net in .deb format or .tar.gz so you can have all the fun of compiling yourself.

Linux itself is actually the kernel of the OS, the part that actually talks to the hardware:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_kernel](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_kernel)

the every distro add the packages is wants to make certain types of Linux.

There are various distros avail that do specific tasks and distros made to work with only certain hardware.

Look at the link in my sig about the world's smallest linux server.

Also look at [distrowatch.com]("http://distrowatch.com/") - here you will find LOTS of distros and many that are specialized.

### Post by WorMzy on 2010-07-01
Just to clarify, very few programs are created specifically for Ubuntu. They're created for Linux/Unix-like OS' in general. Canonical do a considerable amount of work optimising the most popular programs for Ubuntu, but they didn't create the vast majority of them.
