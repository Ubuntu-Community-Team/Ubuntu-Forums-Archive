### Post by Subito Piano on 2012-01-05
There is a kernel update available that may help (check around on the forums for how to do this).  Personally i've downloaded the lubuntu package on top of my regular Ubuntu (10.04) to conserve resources (i like it better than the regular LXDE package) - or try XFCE.  If you aren't familiar with various window managers, a simple internet search will guide you.  Outside of that, i long ago figured out that having many, many Linux distros is a real blessing since every machine is different, so don't be afraid to dabble with other distros...just remember that they ALL are sluggish before installing, i.e., when running live off the CD.
