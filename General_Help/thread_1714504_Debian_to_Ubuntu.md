### Post by snowpine on 2011-03-25
Welcome to the forums! Debian to Ubuntu is actually a very easy transition (I went the opposite way, from Ubuntu to Debian). They are 99% the same in terms of daily use (the biggest differences are in installation and the development cycle).

The first step you can take is to try a Live CD. This will give you a "first impression" of what Ubuntu will be like on your hardware. (Keep in mind that performance from a Live CD can be kind of slow, so don't be discouraged by loading time.)

I agree with the suggestion above to create a "dual boot" of the two distros while you are learning and coping with the transition. There have been a few times I've overwritten a perfectly fine Linux partition, for curiosity's sake, and wished I hadn't. :)
