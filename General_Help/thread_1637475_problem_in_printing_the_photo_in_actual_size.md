### Post by AlphaLexman on 2010-12-04
Jpeg's and .png files keep 'dpi' information or 'ppi' dots per inch or points per inch (not exactly the same but similar.) The image may only be saved at 72 dpi.

Your screen has a 'dpi' setting too. Maybe around 120 dpi. Your printer, if it is fairly recent, should output at least 300 dpi and even maybe 600 dpi.

So if you put a ruler on your screen on an image that is 640 x 480 and it measures 6 3/4 by 5 1/4 inches, that is not the actual size of the image.

Print size and screen size are enormously different. Professional printing companies will not use anything under 600 dpi.

That means your 640 x 480 image is only slightly larger than an inch wide printed at 600 dpi.

This is also why newer digital cameras have so many megapixels.

Hope this helps!
