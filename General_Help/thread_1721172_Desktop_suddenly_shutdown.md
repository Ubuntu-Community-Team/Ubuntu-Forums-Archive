### Post by mendhak on 2011-04-04
Try taking any extra cards out - just have the mobo and keyboard + mouse + monitor connected.  Have the monitor connected to the onboard graphics port, then try running it.  If it still isn't working, it's very likely a busted motherboard.  You can also try removing RAM sticks and only having one stick in there at a time to eliminate bad sticks.

When you say the fan spins a little and then nothing - is the monitor on with no message?  Does the machine still hum or does it shut down immediately?

### Post by antaios256 on 2011-04-05
i would consider a power supply issue first. certain parts of a computer such as the cpu and ram require a specific voltage. if it varies by as little as .01 it can cause the hardware to shutdown. i would recommend checking another power supply first. (preferably one that doesn't require paying for it.)
