### Post by coffeecat on 2011-11-10
The random untitled folders is a "feature" (read usability issue) of the gnome desktop. If you double-right click on the desktop and move the mouse cursor fractionally into the context menu between clicks (which don't have to be fast or close together), you get an unwanted untitled folder on the desktop. But how this is happening with the lid closed is beyond me. Unless you've changed the power settings, closing the lid should suspend the laptop and you shouldn't be able to create new desktop folders. But if you have changed power settings...

I'm not being facetious, but I must ask this. :) Is there a mouse plugged into a USB port when you leave the laptop unattended and do you have a house-cat prowling around?
