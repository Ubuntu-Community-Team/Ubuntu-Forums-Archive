### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-04-08
Google tightened up authentications a while back.

Have a look at [http://googleonlinesecurity.blogspot.de/2014/04/new-security-measures-will-affect-older.html](http://googleonlinesecurity.blogspot.de/2014/04/new-security-measures-will-affect-older.html) and [https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6010255?hl=en](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6010255?hl=en).

There is a workaround to "Allow less secure apps" linked in the second URL. You need to be logged on to a Google account to see it.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2015-04-09
From [https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/thunderbird-and-gmail](https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/thunderbird-and-gmail)

> Gmail accounts created in or about June 2014 (exact date unknown, Google only mentions "second half of 2014" in their new authentication blog post) won't work with Thunderbird until the Thunderbird team implements Google's non-email-standard authentication. This is currently scheduled for Thunderbird 38 which will be released on May 12, 2015. See bug [849540]("https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=849540") for the full technical details.
