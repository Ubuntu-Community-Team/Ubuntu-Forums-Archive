### Post by t0p on 2009-09-02
If you want a particular kind of app to be written for Linux, the simplest way is to write it yourself.  But if you're not a developer/coder, that's easier said than done.

If you want to get a dev to write your app, you need to find someone who is interested in that kind of problem, and interest him in your specific project.  That will not be easy if you don't actually know someone who does that kind of coding.  You could approach the people who created the Windows version of the app and see if you can interest them in porting it to Linux.  But as Linux apps tend to be free (cost-wise *and* open-source-wise), those devs might not have much incentive to do the port.  At the end of the day, you might have to incentivize them yourself - by paying them to do it.

If there really isn't a Linux version of the kind of app you need, you are probably better off dual-booting Windows, or running Windows in a virtual machine, so you can use the Windows program.  But bear this in mind: just because you don't know of a Linux app, doesn't mean there isn't one.  There are lots of software projects out there.  Searching Synaptic or Sourceforge might turn up just what you need.
