### Post by wyomingpat on 2010-03-08
Moving to Ubuntu a about 6 months ago I cannot find any application that has the full functionality of MS Outlook let alone Act! for Windows. We need decent fully relational contact manager or information manager.
Many years ago in 1995 there was an application called Netmanage Ecco. It was fully relational in that any piece of information could be linked to any other. Contacts were linked to todo's, correspondence, memos, appointments etc, and open any one of those items and you could display all the others. You could log calls, link files and search on anything. It was the perfect PIM. And it ran on a 256k machine! It never crashed and never got corrupted. Packrat 4.1 was almost as good.

I ran it under Windows 98 and it was fast. You could also synchronise by email, direct connection or via a workgroup. Simple to set up it was a dream. It does not run under Vista or W7.

An application like that would be a godsend under Linux - why do we have nothing that compares 15 years on? In the right direction we have Chandler I guess. I am happy to buy it - it does not have to be free. The few contact managers that are available are so complex to set up - why does evrything have to be so complicated?
