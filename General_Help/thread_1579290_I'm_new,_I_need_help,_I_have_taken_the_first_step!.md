### Post by macem29 on 2010-09-21
I just switched my system from the netbook remix to gnome desktop without a reinstall,
may be a better option if you have some stuff, apps or whatever and don't want to do it over
again, pretty simple process....

synaptic package manager, search for ubuntu-netbook, right click and mark for removal,
don't apply changes yet...search for ubuntu-desktop, right click and mark for installation,
now apply, the netbook desktop goes, and the standard ubuntu desktop is installed, it's a pretty
hefty D/L, so make sure you're plugged in, not on battery...the desktop will remain as is until
you log out or reboot, on next login you'll have the standard gnome desktop 

this next part is very important, before restarting the system, go to system/admin/login screen and
select GNOME as the default session or else you will restart to the netbook session which you just
killed, ie: you will have no GUI...I know this because I did it and it was a head scratcher getting it fixed
