### Post by YQ002lc2 on 2010-11-04
I was just looking back through some of my subscribed feeds and editing. I now use a lot of different Chinese IMEs in Linux. But my favorites are:

ibus-pinyin
ibus-bopomofo  (a zhuyin IME or &#19968;&#20491;&#27880;&#38899;&#36664;&#20837;&#27861;)  -- one great thing about this IME is that you can type a string of zhuyin sequences and have it select the best candidate.

ibus-chewing&#12288;&#65288;Another zhuyin method. I believe it's name is &#37239;&#38899;&#36664;&#20837;&#27861; in Chinese&#65289;
ibus wubi86   (wubi is &#20116;&#31558;&#23383;&#22411; in Chinese)
ibus wubi98
ibus cangjie (m17n)
ibus easy-big    &#65288;a dayi method &#22823;&#26131;&#36664;&#20837;&#27861;&#65289;
array   (&#34892;&#21015; in chinese)
array30

I like and mostly use only traditional Chinese characters, but some of the methods above can type simplified characters as well if you are into that.

Notably ibus-pinyin, bopomofo, wubi methods, and array methods that I am aware of.

there are lots of other methods available that only use simplified characters, but I only chose the ones that could at least input traditional characters.

I hope that helps anyone who is looking at this later on.
