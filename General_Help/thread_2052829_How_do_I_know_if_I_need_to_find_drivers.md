Hi JustinChina

I don't know if this helps, but I'll try.

I have never actually had serious driver issues with any of the GNU/Linux distros I have used seriously since about 1996. When I did they were usually video card issues, in the earliest days of GNU/Linux there were quite diverse video chipsets and card architectures, many more so than nowadays, and the generic linux drivers were sometimes a bit limited (I am talking Redhat 5 here). Since my Redhat 7 days I have never really encountered major issues. Ubuntu has always been good with video drivers, the third party drivers install easily and are updated reliably, by 'Administration -> Additional Drivers'

I have never, ever, needed to load specific Mobo Chipset drivers for any mobo I have ever run any GNU/Linux on. The generics have always been functionally fine,  and I have never even noticed any sub-optimal functionality. But I have never been looking very hard nor loading GNU/linux on very non-standard kit.

There have been issues reported with some printer drivers, from time to time, but I personally have never encountered them with Ubuntu - I use HP and Brother printers. (Although when I used Fedora, up to about 5 years ago, configuring printers could be a PITA).

The only whinge I have on the present driver-state for Ubuntu is that I skype with a Cyberphone W. No 3rd Party driver available so I cannot key in numbers on its touchpad. Big Deal! Functionally the handset works find when it comes to speaking and listening to the remote caller; so it's not a technical issue I'll ever need to chase to the end of the world.

The take away message? Don't waste your life worrying about driver issues if your hardware seems to be working ok. Fiddling around with alternative drivers is so often a pathway leading straight to a complete re-installation job. If it ain't broke don't fix it - unless that's what floats your boat.

Hope this helps
rjbl
