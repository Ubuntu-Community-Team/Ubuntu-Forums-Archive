### Post by QIII on 2020-04-04
If it is 14.04, that fact is significant.  14.04 passed its standard support period in April of 2019.  Unless you are paying for the extended server support that would take you to April 2022, your release is out of date, likely unsafe to use and probably will not be updated.  Any sort of problems you are encountering could be from any number of sources, but attempting to fix them would not be worthwhile.  I suggest a clean install of a supported release -- after backing up all of your important data.

What you have described seems to be a problem that is getting worse and worse.  File corruption, hardware starting to fail, malware ... take your pick.
