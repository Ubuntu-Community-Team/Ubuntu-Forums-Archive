### Post by ivanovnegro on 2010-11-17
> **COKEDUDE said:**
> Could anyone give me a good definition on what a ppa is? I see the word ppa used a lot when talking about repositories.

A PPA is an additional package what is not in the official repositories of Ubuntu.
You have to install it manually by adding the PPA in your Sourceslist of software.
For example you can add the PPA of Banshee to your system and you will have a more up to date package of this program but it could be unstable too or simply doing problems as it is not a part of the official tested packages in the Ubuntu repository.
Here a [Link]("https://help.launchpad.net/Packaging/PPA") for more detailed infos.
