**_INSTALLING THE CANON PIXMA MP180 USB Printer in Ubuntu 7.10 (Gutsy Gibbon)_**

*First thing - The MP180 is completely compatible with Ubuntu 8.04 (Hardy Heron).*

Because the instructions above were quick and short I would like to give detailed instructions as to how I installed the Canon PIXMA MP180 USB Printer. I got on OK because I'm somewhat experienced with Linux, computers and installing Printers but first timers may find the quick instructions too vague. My instructions might be drawn out and thats because they cover any of the fears first-time users might have about how and when to do this or that, or should I do this etc.

1. Turn on Printer and your PC. 

2. When logged in to Ubuntu plug Printer USB lead into a free PC USB port. Note: Nothing will happen, nothing will appear on screen or make a noise.

3. On your Ubuntu desktop click on the "System" menu at the top of the screen and go to "Administration" and then "Printing".

4. When "Printer Configuration" opens click on "New Printer", it will search for printers attached to the machine.

5. A list appears with about 5 or 6 options in it. Even though "Canon MP180 USB#1" appears in the list don't choose it, select "Gutenberg USB Printer #1" instead and click "Forward".

6. The next 2 screens that appears lets you choose what printer manufacturer and model you're using. Scroll down the list to "Canon", select it and click on "Forward".

7. The next screen is Printer Model, scroll down the list and choose PIXMA MP150 (you're right it's not the correct one but it works anyway and it's the only one we can use), click "Forward".

8. The next page is the description page where you can give the printer a name (i.e. Canon MP180 Printer), a description of what it is (i.e. Colour Inkjet Printer and Scanner) and Location (i.e. In the office, on the floor). Except for Printer Name none of these fields need to be filled out if you don't want and you can just hit the apply button with whatever it provides anyway, otherwise when finished entering the information you want click on "Apply" and you've just installed the Canon PIXMA MP180 USB Printer!

9. When you are back at the "Printer Configuration" page select the printer on in the left hand pane and under the "Settings" tab you'll see a "Print Test Page" button, click it and a test page should come out of your printer if everything went correctly.
