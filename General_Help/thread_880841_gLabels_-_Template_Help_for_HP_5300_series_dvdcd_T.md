### Post by OzzyFrank on 2008-11-21
Unless you've had success in the meantime, it probably is glabels that is at fault, not the dimensions you've put in the template. I simply can't use gLabels to print anymore! I can create a disc label and "print" it to a PDF which then prints fine as soon as you open it, but **gLabels has died**.

I've actually posted all the info I could at the project/bugs page, and the developer couldn't give me any good advice other than uninstall it and try the new version. That sort of made things worse, though not really, as before it would print on a small portion of the disc (the rest would be printed on the CD tray itself) and now doesn't print at all to the discs (OK, maybe that's actually better, as no wasted discs and messy cleanup). It actually goes to do it, like spends the time lining up the disc... and if the print is just some text at the bottom of the disc, you can see it lining it up for that... then it just spits it out without even trying to print on it.

The solution isn't perfect but not that much of a workaround: just print it to PDF (it will end up in ~/PDF) then open that and hit the print button. In case anyone has hassles doing this with the default PDF viewer, I use KPDF which is perfect. So as long as your template dimensions are fine, and I assume you've experimented with figures trying to get it right, printing the PDF should be the answer (and a good way to see if your dimensions are right).

As for gLabels, looks like it is becoming a lost cause for me... can barely use it without it starting to grey out like it is going to crash (which it can do fairly easily - even by typing too fast in the text field! hehehe... what a joke!). I just wish there was another program I could use so I could just uninstall gLabels for good.

### Post by OzzyFrank on 2008-11-21
Oh, as for the dimensions, I just played with the figures till it was perfect. Yeah, means printing on a few discs, so unless you've kept some dud discs for this task, you'll have to print on a few new ones. You can do something like have a full yellow background, with perhaps the word "Backup" at the very top and very bottom, and see what happens. You can then measure and see which way the figures need to be altered... and eventually will have a perfect template! And those discs can then be used for backing up some files, so nothing is wasted!
