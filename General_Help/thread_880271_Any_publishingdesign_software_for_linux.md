300 dpi is actually kind of low for high quality printing. I think "buy some crappy $1000 software" is more to the point for them.

I have to say, too, this is sounding like a vanity press for CDs rather than books. I know nothing about "publishing" music CDs, but if it's anything like books, you don't need a company to "publish" them, you can do it all on your own, and much cheaper.

> What other formats should I ask them to provide the templates in? 

Actually, I think OpenOffice comes with a CD template--but it may just be for inserts. However, OpenOffice's native format (*.odg, I think) would be internationally acceptable.

> We should try and figure out the best ever desktop publishing format, one that is far superior to any proprietary one, and get companies to use that. They basically aren't getting my business because of this.

Or I think the Scribus team should try and reverse engineer those formats. Any thoughts on the legality of this?

Again, I'm talking books and magazines here, but the way it's **always** worked is how the publisher gets the publication to the printer: which has always been in its final format. In the old days, we provided huge sheets of paper with everything laid out on it (stuck to the paper with special glue) and the printer phtographed that and turned it into printing plates. Nowadays, they work with pdfs. What you use on your desktop is irrelevant to them.
