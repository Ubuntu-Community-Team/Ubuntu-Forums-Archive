Hello all.

I decided after a lot of deliberation to go with Ubuntu 12.04 from Mint 12.

I am actually starting to enjoy the Unity interface.  It's a very clean look and gives the OS a very polished look.  With Ubuntu Tweak and MyUnity I can change a lot of parameters.

Now the problem.  When I use Firefox, the bookmarks menu is on the main menubar.  When I drop down the bookmarks I want to be able to right mouse and choose open in new tab, open in new window, etc.  Also a middle mouse click is supposed to be an open in new tab function.  How can I get that back?

### Post by colin.p on 2012-05-30
I found the only way (as of yet) is to disable "Global Menu Integration" in extensions. A link to a similar issue is at:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11931931&postcount=19](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11931931&postcount=19)

### Post by stchman on 2012-05-31
I simply disabled the Global Menu Bar Integration in the Tools-->Add-ons in Firefox.  Now there is no more menu in the menu bar and all works as it should.
