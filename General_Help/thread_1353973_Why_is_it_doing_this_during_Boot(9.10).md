### Post by JC Cheloven on 2009-12-14
Emachines are notorious for being low-end computers ;-) I own one myself :-D

If I understand you, both acoustical noise and screen misplacements occur. It could be a problem of electrical powering.  Have you something plugged (usb...) that is power-consuming at boot? How stable is the AC power in your house? Does it give the nominal voltage? Are some big electrical machines in the house, or nearby?

I think the problem may be on this side. The solution can be to purchase a cheap electrical stabiliser (don't know the name in english, they look as a regular set of outlets, but have some internal circuitry), or a more expensive  UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) device. 

Anyway, if it affects only the booting, it's not a real problem, although not a good thing either. I wouldn't be surprised if sometimes ubuntu starts in low graphics mode, or similar things.
