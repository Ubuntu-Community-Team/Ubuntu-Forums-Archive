### Post by avtolle on 2008-07-12
There are more than two things to consider here. First, when trying to play music, which application were you trying to use? Second, under System>>Preferences>>Sound, have you tried to change the default options and then press the test (?) button to see what happens? 

I've a basic feeling that the onboard sound card is not being found by the system (I've that problem), but I've been able to get any number of media players to produce sound by ascertaining that the pulse audio plugin was installed for the app (installing or selecting, as the case may be, the plugin) and that the app was configured to use the default sound device. I'm no sound expert, as may be obvious, but while no system sounds are available, I've been able to get sound from multiple players.
