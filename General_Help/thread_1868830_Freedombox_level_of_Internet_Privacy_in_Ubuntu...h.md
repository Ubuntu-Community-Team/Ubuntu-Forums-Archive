### Post by Conzeit on 2011-10-24
Alright, so I've recently become aware of freedombox and Eben Moglen's talks have inspired me to try and make my online experience as cloud-free and privacy aware as possible, even before the Freedombox is released.

as I understand it, to browse privately I should use TOR and disable all extra content like Flash and Javascript (which it does by default) There's something else which I' like to do and it is to be able to make anonymous search in google without losing functionality (like search in TOR instead of Scroogle)

Freedombox Foundation has made a couple of lists to collect all sources of cloud-free alternatives to cloud dependant services

[http://wiki.debian.org/FreedomBox/LeavingTheCloud](http://wiki.debian.org/FreedomBox/LeavingTheCloud)
wiki.debian.org/FreedomBox/ExampleProjects
This is one of their sources.
[http://wiki.autonomo.us/Wish_list](http://wiki.autonomo.us/Wish_list)

I am aware of Tiny Tiny RSS as a cloud-free and privacy aware RSS service to replace Google Reader. I use the online version but I notice there's a pattern in all these services to encourage you to host the service yourself, in order to have true privacy.

My information inside my hard drive. How do you protect it? is the fact that I'm in a linux enviroment enough, or should I just...encrypt my whole drive with truecrypt or something?

Email. all of the options on the list seem to require for privacy that you host your email client yourself. is it feasible for a user that has never coded in his life but is willing to read some guides and learn to set up a computer as a host?....what hardware would I need?

I know this post has a lot of questions...but hopefully it will solve the doubts of more people than I.

### Post by oldtimer7777 on 2011-10-24
I'm pretty old school.  Tor was private a few years ago. Now the government is sitting at the end of each of those Tor servers, logging, saving everything.. Tor isn't private anymore.  Whoever told you that is not up-to-date with current intelligence gathering techniques today. If you want Tor privacy you will need to use a proxy and you will need to be the admin on the other end of the proxy as well. Otherwise, who knows?  Honestly, simply using your own paid POP3 mail account on something like Godaddy instead of those other free email services that mine your data is proabably the most you are going to do to improve your privacy on the internet these day. Unless you want to learn about VPN tunneling, which would require some college coursework. Oh and hard cash. Otherwise it is just a simple waste of time and super paranoia.
