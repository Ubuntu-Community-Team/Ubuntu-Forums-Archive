Hello to all....

I am an entrepreneur.  I am building computer systems with UBUNTU/GNOME installed on them for resell in my local community.  I find the UBUNTU operating system to be very user friendly and almost perfecto in all areas except one.  Printer compatibility.  

 Every other type of external peripheral I have tested successfully  (scanners, faxes, cam/webcam devices, ipods, music player, usb devices, etc,etc) but not my new Canon  Pixma MP280 printer.

Sooooo, where the heck am I going to find a printer on the listing to purchase,,,,   Hopefully my customers will have an older printer that is compatible.

### Post by walt.smith1960 on 2011-08-06
HP printers and all-in-ones generally work when plugged in, or after loading HP-LIP-gui.  I've had good luck getting Brother printers to work -- they have drivers and installation instructions available for  a great many models.  The poorest linux support  AFAIK is Lexmark though they're supposed to be getting better.  Lexmark has resonable linux support for "business class" machines,  good luck with the Lexmark $39 specials at the big box stores.  Canon drivers are often found on Canon's European or Asian web sites.
