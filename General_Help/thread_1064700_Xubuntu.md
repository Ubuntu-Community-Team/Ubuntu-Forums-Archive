---

### Post by howefield on 2009-02-09
Key difference is that it is lighter on resources than gnome, it uses the xfce desktop environment.

You can install it alongside Gnome and choose it from your Options menu at the login screen. You won't lose Gnome, but will be able to choose between the two.

To install, search for xubuntu-desktop in Synaptic Package Manager.

Or if you just want xfce, search for xfce4.

### Post by 4Orbs on 2009-02-09
I've used both during my first year with Linux, and have tried a bunch of other brands as well. I love them all, but Xubuntu is the one at the top of my favorite's list. It has been absolutely bulletproof, quick to install, configure and modify. You might miss Gnome at first, but once you become accustomed to the Xubuntu desktop you'll appreciate things in xfce that you never previously thought much about.
