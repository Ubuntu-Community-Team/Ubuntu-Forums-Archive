### Post by motsteve on 2010-04-10
Make sure that you do an update-grub2 on your booting partition.  The grub menu now doesn't work with normal drive designators, it uses UUID's.  That had me going in circles for days after I installed Karmic in my multi-boot system.

### Post by oldfred on 2010-04-10
While grub2 is very good at finding other systems, with old grub I was a fan of chainbooting as then each system would update its own menu and I would chainload into the updated menu. I think the grub2 menu gets too large to handle.

With 4 drives you have 4 MBRs and can have 4 different systems in the MBR to chainboot to. Old grub works from a partition so you can install it to the partition of any installs still using grub legacy. Then you can turn off the os prober and create your own chainboot entires to the other MBRs or PBRs to boot. The only disadvandage is you chain thru two menus to get to the other systems.
