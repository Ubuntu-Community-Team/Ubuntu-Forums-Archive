### Post by tired-triclops on 2014-09-08
Depends on the specs of the laptop. If the computer is really low end/old, I'd go with Lubuntu, though it can be used with any hardware that Ubuntu can be. If it's a modern lower-end laptop, I would use Xubuntu, personally. Both have access to the Software Center. 

If wireless issues persist, try connecting to wired internet and going to restricted drivers under "software and updates." (L/X/*)Ubuntu will scan the hardware to determine if there are any components that require closed source drivers (e.g. Broadcom wireless cards, which Dell and other companies like to use). If a closed source driver for your wireless card is available, install it and your issues should be resolved.

### Post by amanchesterman on 2014-09-09
My advice is to create usb live disks for both Lubuntu and Xubuntu and **try them out before you install**. Personally I have had better results with Xubuntu in getting wireless to work on old laptops but it may be that Lubuntu will work just as well on yours, and you may prefer the desktop. If they work in trial mode then they will work when you install them.

Also -- obviously -- check that your wireless problems aren't down to the router or something else. Try it with another computer, a Windows machine, a smartphone etc.
