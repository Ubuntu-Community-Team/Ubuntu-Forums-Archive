### Post by bcdeck on 2008-02-04
When backing up, make sure you really have what you think you have.

I recently decided to switch flavors of Ubuntu and decided to do a clean re-install. I dragged the home folder icon from my desktop to a CD and burnt what I thought were the contents of my home folder. What I actually copied was a link to that folder, which was gone after the re-install (I opted to reformat the drive as part of the reinstall). Yes, I know I may not have needed to reformat -- it was a habit of an ex-Windows user.

Moral of the story -- verify contents of a copy before ridding yourself of the original.

Second moral: Consider an actual backup utility rather than manually copying files.
