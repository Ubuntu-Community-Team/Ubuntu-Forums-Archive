### Post by drs305 on 2011-01-06
Grub2 will normally provide an error message. My guess is that it is autobooting to Ubuntu and you have a video problem.

If you hold down SHIFT during boot will the menu appear? If so, press 'e' to edit the first entry, add "nomodeset" following "quiet splash" in the 'linux' line, then press CTRL-x to boot. If you don't have "quiet splash" just add it to the end of the line.

If it boots, go to System, Administration, Additional Drivers and add your video card.
