### Post by GTKpower on 2005-05-06
I've been following AbiWord closely for about a year, and I've been impressed with what they've been able to do (without trying it), especially with the loyal following they've established, despite the ubiquity of OpenOffice.

So, since AbiWord looked a bit "cleaner" to me than OOwriter - in terms of interface and menu structure, I decided to give it a go, and installed it from the Hoary repos.

Things that left a bad taste in my mouth:

1.)  AbiWord doesn't seem to recognize a particular font's quotation marks.  Times New Roman has curly-quotes, Palatino has striaght/slashed quotes, etc.  It simply prints generic ones, both on-screen and on paper.  Selecting the proper quotation marks from the special characters list is far too inconvenient for it to be an option.

2.)  It isn't WYSIWYG.  I typed up a few paragraphs, and made them justified, which is a fairly standard look.  The printout (HP Deskjet 5150 - fully configured) had characters that in some cases were unevenly spaced.  Yet, on-screen they looked fine.

OpenOffice (2.0) has none of these problems, although certain very minor font characters must be selected from the special characters list.  Entirely manageable.

Might be just Linux still having font issues afrer all these years . . . 

Don't get me wrong, I like where Abi is going, and the interface is very clean and well laid out, even though you can't customize toolbars, it seems.

Any help?

Thanks

### Post by markw on 2005-05-06
To answer your first problem; normal quotation marks are actually better, IMO. It's not really a problem.
And second, it still is WYSIWYG, what you see is what you get, just maybe not when printed out. As an experiment you might want to try saving to html and then printing with Firefox, to see if it is Abiword's fault.

Lastly, If you don't like Abiword, don't use it ;). No one is forcing you.
