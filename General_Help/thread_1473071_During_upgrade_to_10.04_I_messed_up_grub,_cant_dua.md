### Post by prions on 2010-05-04
Load up your Windows recovery cd and get to the command prompt and type in: 

bootrec.exe/fixmbr

this will fix the master boot record, allowing you to boot into Windows. 

But, when doing this, you'll inadvertently remove Grub. Follow this guide to restore it:[http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/ubuntu/reinstall-ubuntu-grub-bootloader-after-windows-wipes-it-out/](http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/ubuntu/reinstall-ubuntu-grub-bootloader-after-windows-wipes-it-out/)

and your done. :p
