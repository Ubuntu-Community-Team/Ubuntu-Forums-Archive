### Post by angelsguitar on 2008-06-21
It seems your problem is more a configuration issue.  I would suggest you play with mplayer plugin configuration and test different values on its parameters.  There's a very nice multimedia how-to that talks about this and some other important things that might interest you.  You can find it at this post:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=766683]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=766683")

Take a look at the "Streaming with Firefox" section.  There you have the option of configuring mplayer or installing gecko mplayer pluggin, which is an excellent alternative too. Everything is well explained, including how to uninstall previous plugins so that they do not conflict with each other. I might even suggest you follow the entire how-to; that way you'll make sure you have your system equipped with the necessary plugins to handle most media types out there.

Hope this helps!
