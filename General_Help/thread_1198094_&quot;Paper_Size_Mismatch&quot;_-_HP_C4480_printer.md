### Post by memories on 2009-06-27
I have an HP C4480 All-In-One printer. Scanning works, but when
I try to print, it gives me a "Paper Size Mismatch" error. I have
chosen different paper sizes (A4, etc), tried different sized paper. Sometimes the printer works, but rarely.

This was not always the case. The printer is only about 6 months old, 
and began having this problem 2 months ago. The ink is brand new, and
there's paper. The problem might be that the paper size sensor is 
dirty or broken, and I read somewhere that I can physically disable/remove
this.. or maybe in the software. 

Please help!

### Post by Cajhne on 2011-06-20
System > Administration > Printing
Select your printer
Then Printer > Properties > Printer Options
Under General, choose your paper size, click "Apply" or "Ok" then close that dialogue manually once the Apply and Ok grey out. (it's not stuck, it's just a very unintuitive/sloppy way for the interface to tell you it's done).

That fixed it for me.

PS- The dialogue may close its self if you click Ok. I clicked Apply, and fear to touch any of it again, now that it's working. :P
