### Post by vanadium on 2019-06-06
Easiest approach will be to make the other monitor your primary monitor. Gnome Shell only provides controls and full workspace switching capabilities on the primary monitor. The other monitor is kind of static, and would allow to place some content that you want to refer to in a glimpse now and then.

Alternatively, explore the multi-monitors add-on extension. It allows you to provide full control on all of your monitors and has several configurable options.
