### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-02-27
> **waloshin said:**
> Does Linux have any software that works well with quick books?

I use QB 2008 quite a bit.
Linux applications can communicate using the QB add-on OBDC interface, or using the slower-but-built-in QBXML or QBFC.

QB does not run on Linux at all. Talk to Intuit about that choice.

There are many alternatives to QB that do run in Linux...they are NOT QB clones - there are many differences.
QB uses vendor lock-in. QB won't export your data in any kind of easily-importable format, so there's a (sometimes significant) set of costs to changing. 

Also, look at your costs: Will your accounting bill be different? Your accountant may need extra billable time to use a system they are not familiar with. You pay for their learning curve.
