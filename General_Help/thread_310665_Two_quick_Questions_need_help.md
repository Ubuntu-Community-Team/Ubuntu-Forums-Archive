### Post by wpshooter on 2006-12-01
I don't understand what your question #1 is, unless you are looking for the fact that Ubuntu is a distribution of LINUX.

As far as #2, I think you are going to find that Ubuntu is not yet as mature an O/S when I comes to properly recognizing as wide a variety of hardware components as M/S windows. But one of the big reasons for that is not Ubuntu's fault but the fact that many hardware component manufacturers have not yet choosen to make Linux compatible drivers for their products.

But having said that, I would not necessarily let that stop you from trying and using Ubuntu.  I think in about two to three Ubuntu MAY surpass M/S windows as the predominant home desktop O/S.  So get in there and get a head start on a lot of the computer users.

Good luck.  Ask questions, if you need any help.
