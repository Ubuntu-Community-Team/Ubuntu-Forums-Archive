### Post by papibe on 2013-03-09
> **carmen2012 said:**
> ... which I can stream movies.
Online content or local content, maybe both?

For online content, a PC can't compete with the new streaming little devices. I've read good reviews on the Roku (locked, but based on Linux).

For local content, there are several budget media player that although are a little limited on what codecs can play, they work pretty well for the common formats. For instance: NeoTV, WD TV, Micca Speck, etc.

Recently I have my eyes on this device: [Pivos XIOS]("http://www.pivosgroup.com/xios.html"). It runs android, so you would have access to a lot of streaming apps, and services. The selling point for me: you can install XBMC on it! (see [here]("http://xbmc.org/natethomas/2013/01/29/xbmc-12-0-frodo/")).

Just a few thoughts.

Ohh, I see.

Alternatives to the previous devices:
[LIST]
[*]Refurbish PC + low end Nvidia card (e.g. 610 or 620). Around $200-300.
[*]Raspberry PI Model B + Raspbmc. About $50.
[/LIST]
The PI will play H.264 1080p video, with regular audio. You can also play complex audio like AAC, or DTS, if you have either a receiver, or a TV that decodes the audio by itself.
