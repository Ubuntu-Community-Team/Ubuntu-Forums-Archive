### Post by TimelessRogue on 2006-01-06
Well, folks, I took the test ... several times for both laptop and desktop scenarios ... and guess who came out on top as the recommended distro?  You got it ... US!

Oh, what test?  Well, to back up a step, I subscribe to ZDNet News and their latest newsletter included a story titled "Linux newbies get a helping hand" ( check it out at [http://news.zdnet.com/2100-9590_22-6019925.html?tag=nl.e539](http://news.zdnet.com/2100-9590_22-6019925.html?tag=nl.e539).)  Basicly the story is about a test (beta version) to help newbies choose which Linux distribution would be best suited for them to install based on their knowledge of computers, OS and software installation, type of computer (laptop or desktop), etc.  As I said, it's the beta version with a final coming out shortly and another test being offered up soon for more experienced users.

I was naturally curious so I went ahead and took the test ... it's pretty simple really and only took a couple of minutes.  If you do take it, just be honest when it asks you about your experience and capabilities.  No point in fudging ... 

Anyway, the long and short of it is after taking the test several times and imputing different criteria as to my knowledge, experience and equiment (but keeping all of it no higher than beginner or intermediate level) ... Ubuntu/Kubuntu and Debian came out on top as the recommended distribution for beginner and intermediate users.  Debian edged out Ubuntu when I input that I was an advanced user.  Oh, and I indicated that it had to be easy text or graphical installation on a middleaged (1gb or less) computer and I needed both wireless and bluetooth but I didn't care which packaging system but it had to be free and have Live CD availability. 

Curious?  Go on over and take the test ... what the heck, it doesn't take much time.  And it was kinda neat to have reinforcement for my own choice.
