### Post by Impavidus on 2023-02-26
Any particular reason to believe that a new CPU will fix your problems? If it's just slow, then a newer CPU may sometimes help (but not when it's caused by poor graphics hardware or lack of memory), but your problems seem more consistent with messed up software, a bad keyboard, loose RAM or a failing hard drive. First try a live disk or a fresh install before investing any money.

If you're going for new hardware anyway, a 5 year old second hand laptop or desktop may give you very good value for money. Businesses often dump them on the market when they update their equipment, but they still have 5 to 10 years of life left.

> But now after some erratic behavior like for instance: being unable to  type in search, the last letter keeps on displaying itself and even  being unable to boot up the OS.

That says to me - keyboard! This machine is a desktop so it should be using a detached keyboard? Can you borrow another keyboard from a friend to test if it is the keyboard? All sorts of stuff gets between the keys and can prevent them from working correctly. Turn the keyboard upside down and give it a good shaking. See the amount of stuff that falls out. Take the back off and try cleaning it.

Ubuntu has an on screen keyboard that works with the click of a mouse. We activate it in Settings>Universal Access. WE can read about it in Ubuntu Help. It can also be activated at the login screen. Click the icon of a human that is in the topp panel.
