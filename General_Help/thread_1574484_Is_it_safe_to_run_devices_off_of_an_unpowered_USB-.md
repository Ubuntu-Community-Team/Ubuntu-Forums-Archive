### Post by annoyingrob on 2010-09-14
USB is smart enough to detect the power draw through a USB port, and shut it down if it exceeds what the port can provide. Plug in the devices, and if they work, you're safe. If they try to draw too much, the computer will either disable the device telling you that it can't provide the power required to the device, or it will power down the port entirely if the device refuses to disable, and notify you of a power surge on that port.

Yes, those sorts of devices would be fine.

Anything that transfers data (like storage devices, audio interfaces and network adapters) should be self-powered or on a powered hub, so bear that in mind. You can't damage a USB port or controller through hooking up too many devices anyhow, as long as they are real USB devices.
