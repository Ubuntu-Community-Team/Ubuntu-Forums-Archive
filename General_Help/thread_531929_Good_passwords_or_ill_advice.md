### Post by Dylnuge on 2007-08-26
4 elements of good passwords:

1. Not a frequent password. Words (even wOrDs wRiTtEn LiK3 t41s) in the dictionary are bad.

2. Non-alphanumeric characters used. You don't need those crazy characters, ASCII chars like *, &, ^, !, ., ,, and more work fine.

3. Length. No, it does not need to be 40 characters, but with a brute force attack, a 2 character password has only 65,025 possible combos, eaisly cracked (eventually) through repeted tries.

4. Alternative security. Change the password every few months. Make the system block access to people who guess incorrectly three or five times. Don't use the same password twice-if someone breaks your FTP account, can they get into your control panel? Your email? Your bank account?. Remember that no password is safe from human error-don't post it, don't say it, and don't choose anything someone close to you could guess-it starts with your friend defacing your site as a joke, and ends with a hacker who knows your friend destroying everything.
