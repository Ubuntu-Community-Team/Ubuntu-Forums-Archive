### Post by ernied on 2012-01-11
I upgraded to Ubuntu 11.10 from v10.something when it came out, and one of the more recent system updates has introduced some *very* strange behaviour. I'd say that this started happening in the past two weeks.

Every once in a while, my USB keyboard just stops working, and menus just don't come up anymore when I click them. I can use the mouse, change applications, browse the internet, and generally do anything *but* use the keyboard. After a few minutes (at least 5, maybe 10) everything goes back to normal and I can go about my day until it happens again. 

There's one even weirder exception to this: I can switch out of X by pressing Ctrl-Alt-F1 to get the login console, and do things there like there's nothing wrong. Top(1) shows that nothing is really out of the ordinary - Chrome is at the top of the list using about 5-10% of the CPU like normal, nothing is eating RAM and the load average is low at about 0.05 or 0.10.

### Post by ernied on 2012-01-12
If anyone else is experiencing these problems, it turns out the problem doesn't lie with Ubuntu at all, but with Chrome. Restarting Chrome will fix the problem in the short term, and I imagine they'll come out with a new update that will fix it soon.
