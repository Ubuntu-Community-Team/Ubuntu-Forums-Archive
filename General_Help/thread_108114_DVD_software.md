### Post by zoiks on 2005-12-25
I've tried qdvdauthor on another distro.  The interface looked decent, but I couldn't get it to work.

In another thread someone raved about tovid.  The website

[http://tovid.berlios.de/en/index.html](http://tovid.berlios.de/en/index.html)

seems to have some impressive screenshots.

Also, dvd::rip seems to be well-liked:
[http://www.exit1.org/dvdrip/](http://www.exit1.org/dvdrip/)

I myself have only successfully made dvds from command line tools.  Generally, I use transcode to convert from a video format to an mpeg2 format compatible with dvds (and audio, i.e. to ac3 format), tcmplex to multiplex the audio and video streams properly, dvdauthor to make the directory structure that's compatible with dvd players, and then growisofs to actually burn it to dvd.
