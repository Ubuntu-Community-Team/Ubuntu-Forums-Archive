### Post by Hendrixski on 2007-04-15
> **zenwalker said:**
> Ubuntu is a live Cd, So just boot the system with it and dont install. Instead use it as live cd and then test all your hardware if they work under it or not.

What Zenwalker means is that if you just put the CD into your computer it will boot the operating system off of the CD and not touch the hard-drive at all.  You can play around with it: do things like launch Internet explorer, or browse the Examples folder (shows you what kind of office documents you can make with it, or will have a video of Nelson Mandela talking about the word "ubuntu").  It's pretty cool.  If everything works then you know it will work when you install it.

At that point, just click "Install" on the desktop, and it will walk you through a very easy install process.  :-)  

Welcome to the Ubuntu Community,  we hope you stay.  Be sure to check out the loco teams (linked to at the bottom of the forums) and it will connect you to other people in your area who use Ubuntu, and are often very willing to help because they love it so much.  I wish I had done that when I was starting out, because there is a small learning curve.

### Post by teddybairs1 on 2007-04-18
kystorms - you have to go into the "repositories" window in Synaptic. I don't remember the exact menu item it's under but just pull up synaptic and have a look at the items on the menu bar. One of them should say either "repositories" or "manage repositories". Click on that. You should pull up a window with some tabs at the top. Browse through them. One of them should have an option to enable more repositories (universe, multiverse, restricted, etc.). Enable all of them, and close out of the window. It should tell you to reload the repository information after that. Do it and then give it a minute or so. Then try reinstalling beryl. It should mark all of beryl 's dependencies for installation automatically.

Remember also, the distribution upgrade for Feisty will be out tomorrow. I don't recommend upgrading to it for about a week, give it time for the servers to calm down. Compiz is installed by default, and is easy to enable on Feisty.
