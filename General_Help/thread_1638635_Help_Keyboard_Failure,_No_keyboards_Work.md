### Post by Matt West on 2010-12-06
It is possible this could be caused by the BIOS. Try loading into the BIOS (usually F2 or DEL when the computer first comes on), there will usually be a section called 'Integrated Peripherals' or something along those lines.
In this section there may be two options for USB Keyboard and Mouse try changing these settings. Save the BIOS and reboot the computer.
See if this helps. There have been some reports of similar problems being caused by the BIOS.

Let me know how you get on :o)

### Post by onaridge on 2010-12-06
The problem is fixed. In my reading travels I found some advice that I tried today. I unplugged everything from the PC including the power cord, pressed the start button and held it in for 20 seconds. Then I plugged everything back in and voila!, the USB ports are working again. Whatever caused the problem must have been held in memory and when I discharged the remaining electricity, the memory reset. That's my theory anyway and I am a happy camper. I was shopping for motherboards too. :-)
