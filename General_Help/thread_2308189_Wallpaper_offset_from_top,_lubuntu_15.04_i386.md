### Post by nginmu on 2016-01-01
I have a dual monitor setup using a compaq presario v6000 laptop with an integrated intel 945 video chip

external monitor is an nec lcd4000 1280x768 native resolution

I have a wallpaper exactly 1280x768, but try as i might I cannot get it to fit exactly on the external monitor. There seems to be some kind of toolbar maybe 15 or so pixels high, which never contains anything, that sits at the top of the desktop and 'pushes' the wallpaper down so the bottom 16 pixels fall off the bottom of the display.

I tried disabling the 'global toolbar' which is what I thought it was, but that didn't seem to help.

Tried all the menu settings on the external lcd itself, can shift the picture around with complete freedom but the 'bar' at the top seems to be coming from within lubuntu itself (i.e. monitor is displaying what it's being sent perfectly well, it's what's being sent that's off by 15 pixels so to speak)

This 'bar' at the top seems to be present on the laptop's builtin display as well.

What might it be and can I remove it?

If that's not advisable and the bar has some purpose, what is it and how do I use it properly (and do I need to slice the bottom 15 pixels off my wallpaper or resize it to compensate?)



[edit] solved - right click desktop, desktop preferences, wallpaper mode > 'stretch to fill the entire monitor area'
