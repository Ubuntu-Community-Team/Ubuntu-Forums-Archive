### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-01-25
As others have said, you don't really need an anti-virus or firewall with Linux, though if you dual-boot with Windows, you may still want to get a virus scanner to help prevent accidentally infecting Windows.  ClamAV is ok, but I prefer [AVG for Linux]("http://free.avg.com/download?prd=afl").  Keep in mind, though, that it (like ClamAV) is only a manual scanner, and will not perform scheduled scans or automatically remove any infected files.  OTOH, in Linux, all you have to do is delete an infected file and it's gone with no risk of infecting the rest of your system.

For more info about security on Ubuntu check the link in my signature.
