### Post by cbecker78 on 2010-05-06
Can you boot into a recovery console?  Often this will fix startup issues for me- just booting into it, then back out.  Try booting into recovery, logging in with your username and password at the prompt, then type "sudo reboot".  and log into your usual desktop.

If you have grub set to no timeout and you don't have options at boot (i.e., it goest straight to ubuntu boot, hold down escape during boot (for grub legacy) or shift (for grub 2) to get to the grub menu.  

If you are really pressed and can't get something to work, remember you can head to your nearest bookstore and pick up a linux magazine for around 15 dollars (USD)... almost all of them come with a live cd of some variant.  I know Barnes and Noble stocks several of them if you live in the United States.
