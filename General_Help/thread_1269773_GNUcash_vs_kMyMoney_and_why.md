### Post by Hei Ku on 2009-09-25
> **morganpatrick said:**
> I've been seeing better reviews for kMyMoney so I'm trying that now.

I feel like it's either no good for what I want to do, or I'm not understanding how to use it. For instance, I borrowed 80 bucks from someone and want to pay them back when I get paid. So I tried to enter a loan and it insists that it be connected to an institution, insists on calculating an interest rate and how often it compounds.

My job is basically this: I have to pay rent and hosting and go out once in a while. I pick up one-off web design projects, name a price, get half down, finish the job and then bug people for money. How do you even enter that into a money management program?

Yes, loans insist on having interest. For what you want to do, you better use a plain liability account, which you pay back with no interest.

There are several ways you could do this, but:
rent and hosting can be expense categories
advance payments may go to a liability account (since you will have to give it back if you don't fulfill the job)
the payment goes into cash, savings, whatever
You have an income category for web design projects. Perhaps even a sub-category per project

If that's all you want to do, I think KMyMoney suits just fine, but it might not be obvious if you don't take a look at the manual. [http://kmymoney2.sourceforge.net/online-manual/makingmostof.html](http://kmymoney2.sourceforge.net/online-manual/makingmostof.html)

### Post by meske on 2010-04-07
In my personal experience, I am loving KMyMoney.  

GNUCash is good, but the AQBanking interface / wizard for setting up online bank accounts was extremely manual and when I was trying it out, there was not a way to update "all accounts" at one time.

KMyMoney on the other hand has been flawless in online banking setup, and with a single click, I can update the balances of all my checking / savings / credit card / investment / loan / etc. accounts.  It's the closest to Quicken that I've found in the open source world.

If the GNU folks worked on the online banking wizard a bit, I might be inclined to go back to GNUCash, but for now, KMyMoney I think is the best option out there.
