### Post by Therion on 2009-03-22
Which version is "better" is too subjective a question to answer.  That's one of those "It all depends" questions.  Also, bear in mind that releases of Ubuntu come every six months - like clockwork - so they're more incremental, really.  It's not going to be same as going from, say, Windows 95 to Windows XP would be.

My personal experience with Ubuntu, from 8.04 on anyway, has been as follows: 8.04 (Hardy Heron (an LTS)) was rock solid.  An awesome choice, in my opinion, if you want to install-and-forget for a couple years.  8.10 was a disaster for me.  I couldn't get it to run smoothly on my machine for love AND money.  I went back to 8.04 and bided my time.  9.04 has been on my machine for about two months now and even though it's still in Alpha, it runs better and faster than even 8.04 did on the same hardware.  Hang around though, and most likely someone will post with experiences directly opposite my own.

With Jaunty being just around the corner, my suggestion would be to try it now and see how it runs, even though it's still in Alpha.  A LiveCD will tell you a lot without messing with your current Windows install.  Heck, download LiveCD's of Jaunty, Intrepid (8.10) AND Hardy (8.04) and determine for yourself which one seems to run the best on your system.
