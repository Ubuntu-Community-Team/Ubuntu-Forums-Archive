### Post by strabes on 2007-10-01
You can add the "Keyboard Indicator" applet to the panel. Right click on a blank spot on the panel, click "Add to Panel," find "Keyboard Indicator," select it, and click "Add." To change keyboard layouts simply click once on the newly-added applet.

### Post by ramvi on 2007-12-26
Got it!

> **Early Uiniverse said:**
> Easy solution to this... You can still have your Windows shortcuts i.e. left Alt+Shift to change your keyboard layout. The way to do this

System > Preferences > Keyboard > Layout Options tab > Group Shift/Lock behaviour 

and there you mark whatever you want to change group in your case 

Alt+Shift changes group

This works when you have say 2 languages and each is in a separate group which it normally is...

Hope that helps... :)
