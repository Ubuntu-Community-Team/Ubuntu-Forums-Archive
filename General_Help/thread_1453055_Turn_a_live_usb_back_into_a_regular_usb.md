### Post by adam.ec on 2010-04-12
If you're not a command line junkie download Gparted from the repos and it will give you full control over the boot flags and let you format the drive back to FAT32 (or whatever you want).
 
Make sure to get a backup of your installation 'disk' though as you'll have to download it all again if you need it.
