### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-03-31
> **kia4ever said:**
> First off, I'm in computer sales and I have a customer that wants to replace all his macs and windows computers with ubuntu. 

Don't want to discourage you -- but you should find out how much your customer relies on Apple and MS apps to do their work. There is going to be some relearning involved, regardless of what they use.

You will hear here about Wine (to run Windows apps) -- but my own experience with Wine has been abysmal.  Plus, the more kludges you have to force into Ubuntu to get it to do what you want, the more "hands-on support" demands you force on yourself.

Just want you to be able to avoid taking on a full-time system administrator role when your customer starts beating you up -- insisting on doing everything in Ubuntu either "the Mac way", or "the Windows way".
