### Post by coffeecat on 2010-08-31
If you've already configured the printer to connect to your network (to your router), you can also set it up by going to System > Administration > Printing, clicking on 'Add', and then highlight 'network printer'. It should find it and autoconfigure it for you if your printer is supported in Linux.

Brother printers are usually well supported in Linux, but just in case, post the exact model number and/or check it out here:

[http://www.openprinting.org/printers](http://www.openprinting.org/printers)
