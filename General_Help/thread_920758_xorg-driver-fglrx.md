### Post by FJGamer on 2008-09-15
Compiz and other effects will mess with your 3D apps. When I had them on, I wasn't able to run half the programs I had installed. Just a warning.

Also, fglrx is the proprietary driver from ATi. I'm not sure you need it. There's an alternate driver built into Ubuntu. You say you can't use debs or autoinstallers... what else is there?

I'll assume you can already access the GUI. I'm not an expert, so i can't honestly help with anything not already documented, but here's a page I found useful: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver)

For the record, I used Ubuntu Hardy (8.04), but I upgraded to Intrepid today, and I'm having some trouble with my ATi driver again, but I'm told it's an on-going battle to get anything working with ATi cards. My card is the Radeon X1300.
