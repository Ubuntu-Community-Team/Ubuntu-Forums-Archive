### Post by sudodus on 2012-01-20
If you have a laptop or netbook, maybe your hand is touching the touchpad causing this thing to happen. In that case there are several solutions,

1. put a cardboard on top of the touchpad (simple but efficient, sometimes I fold a big cardboard piece so that one part is under the computer and one part is covering the touchpad; it is easy to apply/remove)

2. set the touchpad not to listen while you are typing. I once used software designed for that, but I don't remember the name right now

3. use a separate keyboard (and a separate mouse)
