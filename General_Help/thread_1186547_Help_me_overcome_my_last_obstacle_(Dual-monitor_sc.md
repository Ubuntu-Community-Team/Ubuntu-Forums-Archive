
That restored my xorg.conf back to the original without the reference to the drivers I added. after a restart I was back to my original post.

on aa weird side note, I restored my backed up xorg.conf and rebooted. Once back up, I used System -> pref -> display and unchecked mirror, disabled the laptop screen and rearranged them so monitors were side by side. I stuck the disabled laptop screen above Monitor 1.

Apon applying this, it came out exactly as I wanted. I think that I have an updated driver and placing the disabled laptop screen above the main monitor made the difference. Placing the disabled laptop screen on either side doesn't work (although once settings are applied, thats where it ends up, go figure).

Restarting out of the dock, the laptop worked as normal. restarting it in the dock however did not work, but changing them the same way in display returned the results I wanted.

While this is not exactly what I wanted, it is workable as I rarely use the laptop outside of either the docking stations so the inconvenience of changing the displays as above is extremely small. I'm sure 9.10 will have better driver support for this card as Ubuntu's Devs have been doing a great job and my issue will be non-existent then.
