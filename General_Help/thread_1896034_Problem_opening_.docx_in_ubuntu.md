### Post by Hagar Delest on 2011-12-16
Better avoid the OOXML format anyway, have a look at: [MS Office 2007 OOXML file format (docx, xslx, pptx, ppsx)](http://user.services.openoffice.org/en/forum/viewtopic.php?f=5&t=4542).

Standards like ODF (native format for OOo) have been designed for interoperability. OOXML has not bee designed for that. MS will continue the vendor lock-in policy to keep their market shares...
