### Post by gektor650 on 2013-03-14
I have the same problem
Press CTR+ALT+F1 , login in console and read /var/log/ Xorg logs
My problem vas solved by topic:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2024161](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2024161)
> 
[COLOR=#000000][FONT=Ubuntu Mono]# in the upstart-config-file /etc/init/lightdm.conf[/FONT][/COLOR]
...# add the following line just above the exec-Call to "lightdm" :
sleep 5
 [COLOR=#000000][FONT=Ubuntu Mono]...[/FONT][/COLOR]
> [COLOR=#000000]The idea behind:[/COLOR]
[COLOR=#000000]It's a race-condition which happens if your system boots extremly fast (fast CPU and fast boot-SSD). The kernel switches at some point to a high-res-text-mode (i think with DRM). And through the lightdm-Script the X-Server will be startet, which also wants to switch to some Graphics-Mode (also with DRM). And perhaps the second one comes too fast, so that DRM is still 'busy' (or however you want to describe this).[/COLOR]

[COLOR=#000000]A shorter sleep than 5 second smay also be sufficient. I didn't try this yet, i wanted to share my idea as soon as possible with you.[/COLOR]
