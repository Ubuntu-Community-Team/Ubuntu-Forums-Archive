### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-03-20
If the fans and LEDs come on when you power-up the computer but you don't get any video no matter what monitor you plug in, it's entirely possible you have a bad video card.  If you have another computer sitting around or have a friend or family member with a computer they don't mind you opening up, you could try swapping out the video card to see if that helps.  They generally just plug into a slot on the motherboard, so they're pretty easy to work with.

If that doesn't work, then it could be your motherboard.  If you played with Legos a lot as a kid, it's not incredibly difficult to replace a motherboard, and certainly costs less than buying a brand new computer.

### Post by 123456789123456789123456 on 2009-03-20
does the lights stay on, the lights on the keyboard?
I am assuming that you don't hear a beep from speakers for from built in speaker.
it is possible a POST freeze.
but usually if POST detected vedio card problem, you would hear a beep code.

Power On Self Test(POST) will stop the boot up process, if it detects a problem with any part of the computer.  if the problem is with video, and before it tests vedio, a beep code will sound, after video checks out, POST will display a text error message.

If the problem is with the motherboard, it would almost have to be centered to the video controller.  If you did get a beep code, then you probably need to replace the video card.  If you open up the computer, does the video card appear to be damaged, burnt components, etc?  That could be another check method.
