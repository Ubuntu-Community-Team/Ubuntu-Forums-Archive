### Post by NightwishFan on 2010-02-21
Hi and welcome to Ubuntu forums! :D If you are a beginner the links in my signature may be of great help to you.

Unlike Windows the drivers are included in the kernel. There are some exceptions such as graphics card drivers and some wireless cards. Nvidia cards are generally supported, and many wireless cards work out of the box.

My advice to you is to try the Ubuntu live CD. It will run and not delete Windows or your data. (Note the installed ubuntu is many times faster). If you have no problems, back up your data and have Ubuntu install to the entire disk. That way it will be the sole OS. I think you will be pleased at how easy Ubuntu is to install and use. Good luck and feel free to ask any questions.

NOTE: Remember to check the CD for errors before you install! There is a menu option when you boot the live CD.
