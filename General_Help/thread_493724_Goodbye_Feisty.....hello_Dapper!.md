### Post by MrCheese on 2007-07-06
I rebuilt my machine with Feisty a couple of nights ago following my wife's assassination of my Dapper system.
The Feisty instalation seemed to take quite a long time.

My system is nothing esoteric - HP dc7100, 3GHz P4, 4Gb RAM, NVidia 5200 PCI-X graphics, Atheros wifi card, onboard AC97 sound, 2 x 160Gb sata drives and a dvd-ram drive. 

First problem - sound - through pc speaker only. I eventually solved this by going into the Advanced properties of the mixer, enabling Headphone Insert Detect which then muted the pc speaker & re-enabled my external speakers. Why? No other distro has done anything like this.

Second problem - Slow startup. The whole desktop feels very sluggish.

Third problem - Midnight Commander - taking a couple of minutes to start up. Not acceptable - I use mc a lot. Also looks awful in a text vc.

Solution - stick the Dapper cd back in, blow it all away & start again. I'm now back with a highly responsive desktop, mc opens in a flash and sound comes out of the right holes without any messing about. Thanks to Automatix I can bung on a lot of the bits & bobs I use without too much hassle. I reckon from putting the cd in to having an updated system & hacking grub's menu.lst to the right disk numbers took less than an hour.  

I went around similar loops with Mandrake before I ditched it (and SuSE before it) as the distro started to become more autonomous & did things I didn't want it to do. Please, Ubuntu developers - take note. I don't want to go distro hunting again.
