### Post by mister_p_1998 on 2006-08-03
Just enjoying a warm fuzzy glow after getting a helpful reply to a problem in the Forums, and it took me back to when I worked in a Microsoft Sponsored School in the UK. We had a huge problem with kids remotely shutting down each other in classes, so we applied security measures in active directory to stop this, instead they just booted in safe mode which turned off all restrictions, and carried on remote shutdowns. I contacted MS asking how I could turn off the boot options (F8) menu. They told me its impossible - its an integral part of XP. Hmm thinks I, Im not convinced. After a bit of Googling, I found a bit of code from a virus which when installing itself, the first thing it does is disable safe mode. I pasted this into the registry, re-booted and guess what? No safe mode!
Thanks for your informed information MS!
Our network was then secured from annoying kids. (Bit of a pain if XP messes up though, even I couldnt get into safe  mode!)
