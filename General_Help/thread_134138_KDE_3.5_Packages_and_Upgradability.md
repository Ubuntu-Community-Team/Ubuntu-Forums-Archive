### Post by jvoegele on 2006-02-21
I am using Kubuntu 5.10 (Breezy Badger) with KDE 3.4.3.  I am tempted to upgrade to KDE 3.5.1 using the process described at [http://kubuntu.org/announcements/kde-351.php](http://kubuntu.org/announcements/kde-351.php) .  Given the number of people on this and other forums that have successfully installed the KDE 3.5.1 packages, I am confident that the upgrade would go smoothly.

However, I am concerned that installing these packages might limit my future ability to upgrade.  Specifically, when Dapper is released, I would like to upgrade from Breezy to Dapper without reinstalling from scratch.  If I were to install the KDE 3.5.1 packages, would I lose the ability to cleanly upgrade from Breezy to Dapper?

I have heard reports that using the Breezy Backports repository makes a dist-upgrade difficult or impossible, so I am wondering if the same is true of these KDE 3.5.1 packages.

Thanks for any help you might be able to provide.

[I posted this in the forums at kubuntuforums.net as well.]

---

### Post by jvoegele on 2006-02-22
I received a response to this inquiry on [http://kubuntuforums.net](http://kubuntuforums.net) indicating that upgrading to the KDE 3.5.1 packages would not limit the ability to upgrade, and I have therefore went ahead and installed them.
