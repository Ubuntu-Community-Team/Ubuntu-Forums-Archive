### Post by jetpeach on 2006-04-25
As the last post points out, I think all this talk of visual style stuff for KDE4 is a moot point - KDE is extremely customizable, should stay that way, and the how it is customized should be up to the distro (Kubuntu people should be the ones to talk to about design/style etc).

But as for KDE features, I do have some things I really hope to see.  In no particularly order, they are:

- better hard drive/partition management in the control panel, including the ability to format & partition a newly installed drive; also fsck disk scanning and debuggin tools in there
- ability to handle startup crashes more gracefully, for example if the monitor frequency/resolution is not supported, there should be a nice basic 640x480 gui that allows the user to select new settings.  i'm tired of changing a video card and running "x11-org.conf or whatever the command is blah" and hitting enter 300 times...
- better ability to prelink or preload or do something to often used applications, such that they will load faster.  I've tried some third party apps and configured some things to do this but have not had the best luck so far.

there's more but that's what came to mind quickly...

one last note on "trying to copy windows too much", I disagree with that comment strongly.  The startup sound for KDE should be customized by the distro, so that example has no meaning.  And offering a default interface that is similar in style to windows is probably a very good idea, or else you can end up with hundreds of complaints, but this again should be customized by the distro anyway.  KDE is not windows, never will be, and I'm not worried is they choose, for simplicity and to prevent unneeded backlash, default style settings similar to windows.  Let those KDE developers concentrate on the bugs (which is a statement I do agree with).  Squashing bugs and improving the speed of KDE should be the priorities.

### Post by ossi on 2006-04-27
I switched during Breezy Badger from Gnome to KDE - one point was just curiousity, but the heavier argument for me is, that I think KDE simply offers better software combined with a better system approach. I like that all out of one hand thought and therefore think, that KDE in general has got a better concept than Gnome, where a lot of stuff for me just seems bundled together (isn't Ubuntu actually just the result of a good Bundle?). As long as you don't care about a lot of options in the preferences etc. (I don't - I think a GUI should not hide anything from you) it will be easy (probably easier than Gnome, where certain things you simply can't do from the GUI) to deal with KDE. By the way, in a lot of menues there are already "advanced modes" like somebody already mentioned before should be used.

Another thing I already mentioned ist the better software - I simply missed apps like Amarok, K3b (both of them I already used in Gnome), or other system apps or system setup tools which are very good in KDE or something as complete as the control-center. When it comes to functionality, I don't think Gnome can compare with KDE. It's just more complete, with the single apps better integrated in one another with a more complete package (not necessarily absolutely complete - I don't use a Gnome native app anymore, but this may be just like that in my case). By the way, the two most important programs - Konqueror and KMail, are from its concept outstanding and really good and absolutely complete. I think KMail is better than Evolution and Konqueror can be put on the same level as the Firefox (except Compatibility of course - anyway its still good enough to use it as a default browser - I use it).

When people talk about bugginess - I don't think that is true. The enviroment as a whole has crashed a lot less than Gnome in my case. Of course, single apps crash sometimes - like the browser or anything else, but the enviroment as a whole is absolutely stable and usually finds a way to get back on track - I think KDE here is better than Gnome.

So, the last point for me is looks. Unfortuneatly I have to say, KDE still doesn't look the way I want it to (and I care about that - this also is part of the workflow and important). Though everything is customizable, Gnome is much better in that point (with nice finished packages you simply install and switch between that really just work). There is simply nothing in the KDE world, I would like my KDE desktop to look like (except my wallpaper probably *gg*). Ok, so people might say - Why don't you install Baghira and let it look like OSX with a dock probably or install any other thing from kde-look. To be honest, I think KDE either looks like OSX or Windows - I don't like that and think it is not ok for KDE to be that poor here. So either people didn't care about that much or didn't do good work yet. 

Finally: KDE is really good - at the moment I wouldn't switch back to Gnome, just because the features and the quality of the software keep me here - and there is a lot of work happening at the moment (2 days ago I installed Krita 1.5 which seems to make a big step forward). However, there still is a lot of work to be done concerning the workflow and especially the looks of KDE. With that I mean, using KDE has to feel simply better. Some may say, that's superficial (in the most simple meaning of that ;-) ), but there is nothing better than an enviroment which does not only work good, but look and feel good too. There are some good approaches already made, but it just needs a nice window decoration (then one from Dapper for Gnome is really well done - in comparison KDE's is poor) and a more nice looking menu. Icons are not that bad, as people say here. So KDE needs a lot of new Artwork and concepts here. Apart from that, I think it's better than Gnome.
