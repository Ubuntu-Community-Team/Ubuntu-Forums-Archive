### Post by dragonfly41 on 2017-06-11
I often get freezes on my old laptop .. it is a regular routine.

With the blank screen you can try pressing Ctrl+Alt+F1 to see if you can get to terminal.
If you can, then immediately type Ctrl+Alt+F7 to get back to your desktop. If you are lucky the screen will refresh.

Another suggestion is to blindly type your password on a blank screen .. don't forget return ..  since your user login box may not be showing.

For example my laptop internal monitor has now failed (not working) and to login I have to remember the keystroke sequence to navigate my grub menu.
Then I can view on a second spare monitor.

If that doesn't work then do not force shut down using not power button but instead hold down Alt then pressing in turn 
Print Scrn. R  E  I  S  U  B 

These are tips just to overcome a blank screen.
