It's also frustrating when 'how-to' instructions are not kept rigorously up to date AND written in an unambiguous way that does not confuse the novice, and that has been checked thoroughly for clarity, accuracy and ease of use.

Note to geeks:

I really do appreciate that you usually know your stuff, but can't always transfer that knowledge in a way that novices understand - you have become 'used' to dealing with the subject, and are familiar with the 'culture' therefore you tend to assume some things are 'obvious' (such as the insertion of 'sudo' where appropriate). This is far from obvious, and novices need clear guides that assume nothing and takes them, step-by-step through the process. A good idea would be to give your 'how-to' to a complete technophobe friend and leave them to it on their own for a while and see how they get on - if they are successful, you can be sure that you've transferred your knowledge well - if not, work with them until it is clear to them and written as such - what's immediately clear to you is unlikely to be so to me, or other novices or near novices.
