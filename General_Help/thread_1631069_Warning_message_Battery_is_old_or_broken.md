### Post by lobralleo on 2010-11-26
I had a similar problem on a HP Pavilion, and I solved it (in part) by recalibrating the battery; you should be able to find information about this on the manufacturer website or the manual that came with your laptop, if any (t usually involves doing a few full discharge-recharge cycles).
The battery was actually old and indeed running at reduced capacity, but the procedure gave me more accurate readings and got rid of the message.

Even if you keep your laptop plugged in, it's usually a good thing to unplug it and let it run on battery every now and then (say, once or twice a week). This helps maintaining the battery in a healthy state and extend its life. I didn't do it with the aforementioned laptop and found myself with a burned out battery in just one year.

Hope this helps!
