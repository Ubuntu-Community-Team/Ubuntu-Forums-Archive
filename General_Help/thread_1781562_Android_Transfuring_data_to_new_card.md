### Post by searchfgold6789 on 2011-06-13
You will need two SD card readers, they are available cheaply off of eBay, and they can also be bought at RadioShack. Your computer may have one already built in, but you will need two in the end. What the reader does is it gives you the ability to plug it in to a USB port, and plug your card into the other end, and then you will be able to read data off the card just like a USB thumb drive. I have even been able to install and boot from Ubuntu in a pinch using one of these readers and a SD card.
Once you have both of your USB SD card readers and cards, plug them both in to different USB ports on your computer.  Open GParted or the Disk Manager and delete the partition off of the new one you got and copy the partition from the old one there. Then extend the size of the copied partition so that it fills up the entire disk. Once it is finished you are then ready to go. I would not expect it to work, however, if your new phone does not support the size of SD card you have - Most probably if the size is not supported it will fill up to the supported size limit and then have an error.
These are general instructions; If you need more help I'll be happy to oblige.
 - search
P.S. Don't forget to 'unmount' both disks first!
