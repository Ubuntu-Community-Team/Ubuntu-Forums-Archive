### Post by TeoBigusGeekus on 2011-01-01
Themes in linux do not work the same way as in windows.
There are themes for your window decorations (metacity/compiz), themes for your actual windows, ie. their colors (gtk), icon themes, etc.
You've probably downloaded and applied a gtk theme, which only changed your window colours.
By the way, nice sites for wallpapers:
[http://4walled.org/]("http://4walled.org/")
[http://wallbase.net/]("http://wallbase.net/")

### Post by Frogs Hair on 2011-02-06
Some themes include wallpapers , but you have to set them as desktop background manually. Gnome and other Linux themes don't come pre-packaged like Windows where you download click and all changes are made. Users have a choice about What elements they want to use  

Icons , controls , pointers , window borders , and colors can be changed by  using the Customize Tab in Appearance Preferences.
