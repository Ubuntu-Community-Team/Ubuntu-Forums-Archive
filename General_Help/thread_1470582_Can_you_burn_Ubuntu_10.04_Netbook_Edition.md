### Post by JimTaverna on 2010-05-03
Yes, yes, yes, yes, yes, yes, etc...](*,)

Most netbook owners don't have an external CD/DVD drive. Flash is the primary option, hence it is explained in detail for those who might not have installed an OS on their netbooks before, using a stick.

This does not preclude you from installing it from disc...if you are one of the lucky few who have/use a CD/DVD drive.

### Post by GSF1200S on 2010-05-03
> **WarrenSH said:**
> I do not want to use my flash drive. So is can I use a CD-R instead? Has anyone done this before (meaning) burn the netbook edition to a CD-R

I currently have Ubuntu 10.04 UNE installed on a Asus eeepc 1005HA-P, and I did it from a CD-R and an external USB CD/DVD ROM drive. Works perfect..

The reason the instructions are that way is because netbooks do not have CD/DVD drives, and usually the person who owns the netbook doesnt have a USB external CD/DVD drive. A netbook is a computer just like any other computer, it just doesnt have an optical drive at all. You just need to tell the netbook in bios to boot off the CD/DVD USB drive and it will work fine.
