### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-07-18
That is within acceptable heat ranges for laptop drives.

I had an older HDD in my laptop, which regulary set off temp alarms when it got over 48C (120F).  But, I replaced that with a newer drive, and it mostly idles around 35C, and rarely goes over 40C.

However, that said, if it's going over 55C and staying there for long periods of time, the drive is running hot.
