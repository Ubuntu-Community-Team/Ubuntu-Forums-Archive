### Post by cyauch on 2008-09-23
Been messing with Linux for a few years, but never really spent the time to get into it.  A few weeks ago, I decided to remove Windows from all my PC's.  Because of the complete switch to Linux, I had to dump my Lexmark X5150 - old, but still worked, just not with Linux. 

So, to make a story short, I bought an HP PhotoSmart C4580.  With latest HPLIP downloaded almost two weeks ago, the support hadn't been added yet.  HOWEVER, once I used a Windows machine to setup wireless to grab an IP on my network, I used hp-setup to install the printer using the PPD C4400 series files, and printing is great (near as i can tell, the only difference between the C4400 and C4500 series is the 4500 series have networking.  same printer otherwise).

For scanning, I can't remember the hp- command line i ran, but it asks to probe USB, LPT, and network resources for scanners.  When prompted for the IP, entered it, and XSane worked great.  I can scan and print wirelessly from any of the Ubuntu machines in the house.  

Long post I know, but my travels and google searches have turned up that others haven't been able to print / scan.  My point is that I've learned HP supports Linux very well, and in most cases, as long as the device your buying isn't a major model change (like from an '02 Ram to the '03 Dodge Ram), generally you can use a driver from a previous model in the series until its picked up by HPLIP.  It just takes a little patience and logic in most cases, and everything works out fine.  Probably from being spoiled by Windows, but I love not having the bloat-ware that comes with it.  


 My $.02 pre tax :)
