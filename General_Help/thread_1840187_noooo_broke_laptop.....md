### Post by TBABill on 2011-09-07
A static free cloth and isopropyl alcohol can be safely used on electronics because they leave no residue. Just make sure it's not plugged in, the battery is removed and keep yourself at the same potential as the device if possible (use ESD safety precautions...electrostatic discharge is ESD) by using a grounding strap to a good ground and your wrist or the platform you disassemble the machine upon. Just don't want to chance blowing a chip with built up static from your body.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-09-27
> **Grenage said:**
> Glad to hear it. :)

Personally, I think additional hardware insurance is a rip off.  Home contents cover, or even Credit card benefits can cover you, and cost nothing much extra.

Basically, ALL insurance is a rip-off, when you get right down to it...I know because, in my earlier days, I was an Insurance Underwriter.

But, these "additional coverage" packages come in handy in situations just like this. Why? Because, from experience, I know that the equipment warranty is going to exclude any damage caused by personal negligence -- which is clearly the case here.

You forget that the the willingness to honor any warranty rests solely with the seller, not the buyer.  So, even if the OP bought this at a computer store that does service equipment, when they open it up and discover beverage residue inside it, they are very likley to DENY and warranty coverage claim.

Case in point, this year alone, I've seen the FREE replacement of as high-end laptop and two smart phones due to accidents by the owners -- and reading the original warranties, in NONE of these cases would the equipment have been covered.

Next time you buy a piece of electronic equipment, look closely at the warranty.  All to often they limit coverage to "manufacturer's defects" -- which does NOT cover breakage.
