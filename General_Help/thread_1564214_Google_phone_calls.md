### Post by pbrane on 2010-09-01
I'm pretty sure Google phone will only use the computer. It's a "soft client". And yes in order to use this as your primary "phone" service you need to have a Google voice account and leave your computer on when you want to place/receive calls.

Any VoIP service will have to have landline trunks into a landline telco somewhere. That's how all calls are placed to any landline service. This costs money.

VoIP is only as good as your broadband service. You should also check your upstream (upload) speed. As most consumer broadband is asymmetrical, this will be the lowest speed and you can get an idea of which codec may be selected for VoIP. This will impact the voice quality.

Also remember E911 services may not be available using Google phone and/or E911 may not be able to locate you in an emergency. You should check that out.
