### Post by bscbrit on 2007-06-12
You could start reading at the end - it might give you a clue as to whether the problem is solved (usually, yes) or whether people are still looking for an answer (it happens....).  

The entire thread is left for a purpose.  It allows others to search for problems and find solutions.  The answer to someone else's problem, which might sound identical to your own, might not have the same answer.  If you have the entire thread, you can read it, try each of the suggestions in turn and, most importantly, learn from it.  In addition to the solution to a problem, you can also learn how others go about fault finding, narrowing down the possible causes and then implementing a solution.  Subsequently, you are better placed to do much of the fault finding yourself and then return to the forums with a specific question that one of the experts here can help with.

Ultimately, of course, you are also in a better position to help other new users with the basic problems and then, when you reach the limit of your own knowledge, pass the problem back to the forum for someone with more expertise to try to solve.  This helps the community and frees up the experts to do what only they can do - solve the difficult problems!

I have been using linux for over 5 years, and Ubuntu for about 2 and a half.  I still help where I can on the Absolute Beginners or General Help threads.  The clever bit is knowing when you have reached your own limit and to pass the thread on to someone with more knowledge than oneself.

I would NOT support deleting closed threads.  The aggregated knowledge base contained in these forums is worth a tremendous amount.

Sometimes people do leave an endnote explaining that the problem was solved.
