### Post by tristan on 2006-06-14
I know it doesn't really answer your question, but you could always try installing xfce. It takes about 10 minutes work to get it looking almost exactly like ubuntu's gnome desktop, human theme and all. As a bonus it's much snappier, and if you have composite enabled in xorg.conf you can easily switch on composite effects like shadows, transparent window decorations etc without messing around with XGL. I've done this, and find myself using gnome only rarely now.
