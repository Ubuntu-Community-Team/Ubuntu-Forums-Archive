### Post by cascade9 on 2010-07-08
I *think* its possible to get the nForce 750a chipsets going to linux...but I'm also fairly certain that its a real pain (no, its not just you). 

I'd go for an ATI chipset, one with no onbaord video. You will need AM2/2+, as your CPU wont run in a AM3 motherboard, and you would need new RAM for AM3 in most cases as well.

That leaves 3 chipsets- 770, 790X, 790FX. 

770- teh cheapest option, but in most cases they only have 1 x PCIe x16 slot. If that isnt an issue (its not for 95%+ of uses) get a 770 chipset board. I'd go for this one- 

Gigabyte GA-MA770 UD3. 

[http://www.gigabyte-usa.com/Products/Motherboard/Products_Spec.aspx?ClassValue=Motherboard&ProductID=2978&ProductName=GA-MA770-UD3](http://www.gigabyte-usa.com/Products/Motherboard/Products_Spec.aspx?ClassValue=Motherboard&ProductID=2978&ProductName=GA-MA770-UD3)

A 790X has more PCIe x16 slots, so if you wan to run corssfire (doubtful) or use a PCI-e x4/x8/x16 RAID card, this is the one get. 

790FX is the 'top end' chipset. Even more feautes (up to 4 way crossfire), better for overclocking, but a bit overpriced. Its getting hard to find a 790FX chipset around now as well (mostly they have been discontinued in favour of the newer, AM3 890FX chipsets). At about x2 the cost of a 770, or x1.5 the cost of a 790X, they arent really worth it.
