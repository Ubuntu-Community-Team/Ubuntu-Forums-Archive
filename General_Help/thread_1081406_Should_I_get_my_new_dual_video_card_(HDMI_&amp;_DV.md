### Post by FluffyElmo on 2009-02-26
Check for driver support before buying any TV tuner card. Many of the all in one cards don't have Linux drivers for TV capture. Pricing tends to be comparable anyways so  if you have enough free expansion slots you're probably better off going with separates.

I had very good luck with the PVR-150 and recommend it if it suits your needs. MythTV used to have a list of supported hardware, but they've changed their site design so I don't see it. Probably worth looking around there and in their forums before buying. [http://mythtv.org](http://mythtv.org)

### Post by kevinatkins on 2009-02-26
Hi Psychedelicwonders,

Yes, I'd agree with FluffyElmo and suggest you go for separate video and TV cards. Hauppauge's cards tend to work well (you don't say whether you're going for a digital or analogue card; I've used a Hauppauge PVR-250 analogue card without problems; for digital TV, I've got a very cheap unbranded unit which, surprisingly, works well!)

As for bluetooth and wireless, I'd suggest you go with a PCI wireless card (Edimax make natively supported units)  and a USB bluetooth dongle.. I've got one made by 'Peak' and it works fine.
