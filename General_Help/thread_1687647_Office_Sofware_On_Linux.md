Welcome to the forums. Please remember that all the software you need can be mostly found in the ubuntu repositories (web based servers) from within ubuntu OS. Forget the windows way of downloading apps from third party sites. Also in most cases a reboot is unnecessary.

I would suggest if you've installed ubuntu explore the menus.
Top left corner of screen under applications. Also at the bottom of the apps menu is the Ubuntu Software Centre. Have a poke around.

### Post by PaulW2U on 2011-02-14
Ubuntu comes with [OpenOffice]("http://openoffice.org/") by default, a minimal version of the suite is automatically installed, further applications can be added from the repositories.  The next version of Ubuntu (11.04) due in April will have [LibreOffice]("http://www.libreoffice.org/") instead of OpenOffice.

In addition Gnome Office is available for Ubuntu and KOffice for Kubuntu but I don't think either are anywhere near as comprehensive as the first two I mentioned.

### Post by coffeecat on 2011-02-14
> **PenguinJoker said:**
> is it easy to find printer devices.

Depends on the printer. If it's a Linux-friendly make such as HP and USB, just plug it in, switch it on and before you can say "Wow!" the system will almost certainly have autoconfigured it for you. If it's a supported network printer, then it's only a few mouse clicks away.

But if it's from one of the manufacturers that produces Linux drivers for only a few of their devices  (*cough* Canon *cough*) then you might have some - ah - interesting times ahead. 

What is your printer? If you have problems setting it up, simply start a new thread and someone will help.

**EDIT**: I read your "is it easy to find printer devices" as finding and configuring devices from Ubuntu. If you mean finding one to buy, then that's a different matter. Note that even HP fails to produce Linux drivers for a few of their devices. Which did you mean? :)
