### Post by Muskegman on 2010-02-18
I use a sierra wireless usb aircard to connect to the internet as it is the only way i can get online living in rural canada. Ubuntu detects it with no problems at all. The only thing you have to remember is if you buy a new aircard you will have to activate it on a windows machine first , such as XP or Vista or win 7 , or a Mac OS and then you can take it and plug it into your ubuntu OS and it will work fine. Same thing goes for software upgrades plug it back into a windows machine to get your upgrades.

Sadly our ISP here always tells us that "yes your aircard will work fine in linux , but we dont give any support for linux only Windows and Mac"

Like i said earlier just plug it into another windows PC to activate it for the first time and it will work great.

ubuntu always detects it and even on a fresh new ubuntu install or upgrade i am usually online within 20 minutes with no need to install any software:D
