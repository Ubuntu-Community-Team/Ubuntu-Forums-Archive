### Post by Cheesemill on 2012-03-23
Maybe something like this:
[http://www.onlinespyshop.co.uk/product.php/822/bt-telephone-recording-lead/86f314dacf2bf35a7eefa2426565ef14](http://www.onlinespyshop.co.uk/product.php/822/bt-telephone-recording-lead/86f314dacf2bf35a7eefa2426565ef14)
Plugged into your phone line and then connected to your mic/line-in socket on the computer. You could then use any one of a number of audio applications to record your calls.

As for doing it directly with the router I don't think that's possible. With the way broadband works the telephone signal never even reaches the router, it's blocked by the microfilter.

As for the legal issues as far as I am aware all you have to worry about is informing the other party that you are recording the call, attaching the equipment itself to a BT socket isn't a problem.

Disclaimer - I am NOT a lawyer, please consult one if you wish for proper legal advice.
