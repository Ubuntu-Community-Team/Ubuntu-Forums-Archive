### Post by inspired on 2009-01-04
Hi folks,
I've been playing around with the appearance of my new installation of Ubuntu 8.10 intrepid.

I installed the "Global menu panel applet" whilst following some instructions on converting my Ubuntu to look like OSX Leopard.

I can not, for the life of me figure out how to reposition or delete this from the panel. By mistake I placed it a little right of centre. I want it to the far left. Most panel items I can right-click and they give me options like Delete and so on. This one, however, just shows various options related to the current application window, but nothing related to the actual panel item/applet.

I'd be very grateful for some advice.

With thanks,

Jonathan

---

### Post by inspired on 2009-01-04
SOLVED
I discovered that the leopard theme I installed had such a dark tool bar background that it was hiding the little double dotted lines next to applets.
When I changed the theme to a lighter coloured one I could then see those little lines and grab them and move the item around.
Maybe this helps someone else in the same situation.
Thanks,
Jonathan
