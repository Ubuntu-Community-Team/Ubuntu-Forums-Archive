### Post by skymt on 2006-04-18
Calm down, xyz. He's asking for help. Don't alienate newcomers, we need everyone we can get when the revolution comes. ;) 

He didn't say Linux sucks. He said sound handling is one of the biggest issues with Linux right now, and he's right. ALSA, OSS, JACK, GStreamer? Who needs it? It should Just Work, right? But too often it doesn't, and new users are just stuck. It would be much better to fix the root problem then waste effort yelling at people who are just confused at a confusing situation.

Okay, lecture over. Cowlikk, here's what you probably need to do. Go to the System menu > Preferences > Sound. Choose the General tab and un-check "Enable sound server startup". (I know, it sounds counter-intuitive, but it usually works, and for a very good, complicated, reason.) Then log out and back in, and you should have sound.

If that doesn't work, post back for more help. Good luck with Linux!
