### Post by Daisuke_Aramaki on 2009-04-16
> **anjohnso said:**
> After having all I could take of Vista, and not entirely fond of windows in general I was pointed towards Ubuntu. It totally depends on the type of Windows programs you intend to use. Some will work with Wine, but not all. Since you are a beginner, it would be better for you to first consider dual boot, and once you are pretty comfortable with linux, you can make the complete switch. And preparing and resizing partitions to make way for linux is pretty easy. I think Ubuntu can do that automatically during an installation process, completely recognizing your other os. I don't know about vista. But you can probably check that out. Also gparted is a great tool to partition disks, since it comes with a nifty gui and all. look up gparted as well. fdisk and cfdisk are great tools, but if you know what you are doing, just go ahead. Good luck.
