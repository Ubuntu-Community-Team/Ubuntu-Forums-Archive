### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2008-07-12
Yes, k9copy is a KDE application, but it runs fine under GNOME.

When you install it, a few extra files will be installed, allowing you to run virtually all KDE applications under GNOME.

I have found that k9copy does a great job of compressing a DVD9 onto a DVD5 disk with no discernible loss of quality.

However, I have found it best not to use k9copy to actually burn the disk. I just save the iso and burn it with Brassero, or k3b.
