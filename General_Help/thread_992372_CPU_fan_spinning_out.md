### Post by ozguitarplayer on 2008-11-24
I don't think it's supposed to work this way how ever since I've added a second hard drive and installed Ubuntu the CPU fan speeds up as the harder the hard drives work. 

On startup the fan will be around 2200rpm, both hard drive temps in the low 30 degrees

after working a while fan can increase to 4400rpm and hard drive temps... one in the low 40 degrees and the other hanging around 50 degrees.


I have look in the bios and cannot find a way to control the fan.......


any suggestions as to why this would be happening, and how to fix it?

---

### Post by cariboo on 2008-11-24
It looks like  the heat from the hard drive is causing the fan sensor to think that the CPU is heating up causing the fan to spin faster. If you have room in your case, move the hard drives further away from the cpu. If that isn't possible get some hard drive coolers.

Jim
