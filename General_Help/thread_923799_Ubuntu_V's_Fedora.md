### Post by exploder on 2008-09-18
Both are really good distributions. Fedora takes more work as far as getting all multimedia to play. I used Easy Life to get multimedia in Fedora 9. Easy Life is a script that allows you to configure everything.

There are big differences in the forums. In the Fedora forum you are expected to search for things before you ask a question. In the Ubuntu forums you can ask whatever you like and someone will more than likely help you out or point you in the right direction.

The package kit in Fedora 9 requires some updates to work properly, once updated it works just fine. Ubuntu is very quick and easy to update and install software.

Do a little reading and compare the Live CDs. Take some time and check out the forums to see where you will feel comfortable and have good support.

### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-18
There are some technical differences between Fedora and Ubuntu, but none that make much of a difference from a newbie's perspective.

For someone new to Linux, I really don't think there's much of a question of which distribution to pick.  Ubuntu has the largest user base of any Linux distribution, which means a larger support structure when you have problems.  The Ubuntu forums are so active and helpful that people using other distributions will come here for help.  Also, Ubuntu focuses on usability and ease of installation.  In other distributions, you'll probably have to do a bit more work initially to get everything working.

So for your first foray into the world of Linux, I suggest going with Ubuntu.  You may later on decide that you like another distribution better, but I think Ubuntu's the best choice for your first Linux experience.
