### Post by ender4 on 2010-10-12
I apologize if this is the wrong place to post this.  I'm not really sure where the best place would be. 

I would like to relate an experience I recently had in the hopes that it will help someone else.  

The keyboard on a desktop my family owns appeared not to work.  At least it didn't work whenever the GDM login screen was visible.  As the technical expert in the family I spent several hours trying to fix it.  I scoured forums and google searches for something related to my problem, I read through several configuration files, and even more log files, and couldn't find a single thing that could cause the problem.  

I ran across a forum entry where someone had enabled "Slow keys" and had caused the keyboard to become unresponsive, however, after a check that that was not the problem *as a user*.  I forgot about it.  

And then, I left the computer for a while, and when I came back I was confronted with the login screen, and not really thinking anything would come of it I checked the accessibility options for GDM, and voila someone had accedentally enabled "slow keys."

There are two lessons from this story.  1.) If you start having problems with your keyboard you should check the accessibility options, and 2.) Sometimes (but not always) the answer to a problem is really simple.
