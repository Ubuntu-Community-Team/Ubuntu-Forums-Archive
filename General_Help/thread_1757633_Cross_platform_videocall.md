### Post by Maythe on 2011-05-13
Heya Penguinboy,

Let me first say that if I were in your position, I would torment my friend with incessant reminders that he or she should be less narrow-minded until he or she actually gives in and downloads something more linux friendly.
 
Of course, I understand and respect that you're probably a lot nicer than I am (and probably have more friends) so I'll help you out.
 
This thread actually covers pretty much the same dilemma you're having:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=673207](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=673207)

(I know it's often hard to find the information you're looking for on this website, so I'll let you slide.  Other people may very well yell at you though, so be prepared.)
 
Anyway, the thread suggests that you try kopete (for KDE) or empathy (for gnome); both programs can connect to msn and claim to have videochat support.  If one doesn't work for you, try the other, as a gnome system can still run KDE and vice versa.  If neither program works for you, the thread lists several others, including ekiga and OpenWengo (both old software, but maybe useful if you're desperate)

If none of that works, you can probably tell your friend that you tried half a dozen programs to no avail and humbly request that if you can try that many, surely he or she can try just one other than msn.

Hope this helps!
-Maythe
