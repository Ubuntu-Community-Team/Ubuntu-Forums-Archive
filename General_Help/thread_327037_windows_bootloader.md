### Post by bernied on 2006-12-28
Yes it can. But don't expect this to be as easy as the normal ubuntu method.

[Here's](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=208951) a howto. I started reading this and my eyes glazed over, as it goes into much detail without saying what is done.

[Here's](http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Dual_Boot_from_Windows_Bootloader_(NTLDR)_and_why) a gentoo howto

There is another way of dual booting without disturbing your MBR, if that is your aim - you can use a boot floppy, CD or USB stick with grub on it, then to boot your alternate install, you just plug or slot in the stick/floppy/CD before startup
