### Post by sdennie on 2008-07-15
As you guessed, it's not very likely that the kernel will be upgraded to 2.6.25 (I'm basing this on the fact that Dapper is still on 2.6.15) but, I would say compiling a new kernel should be your last resort.  

For very isolated drivers like that, it's sometimes the case that you can find them standalone and compile them against whatever kernel you happen to be running (sound/webcam drivers are a good example of this).  The Ubuntu kernel also backports a lot of updates from new kernels (maybe only security related updates though) so, it's possible that the driver will eventually show up in the stock Ubuntu kernel.

Otherwise, you can try to build your own kernel.  There are various ways to do this.  You may want to have a look at these links:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=618563](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=618563)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=311158](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=311158)
[http://www.howtoforge.com/kernel_compilation_ubuntu](http://www.howtoforge.com/kernel_compilation_ubuntu)

Wimp. ;)  Ok, just kidding, I wouldn't do it on an important machine either, but that's just me. But try virtualbox (install by apt-get or whatever) or your other favorite vm to test stuff like this.

Another possible option is that this driver you want may be compilable as a module that you can load into your current kernel without too much fuss. It just varies on how much new stuff the driver depends on. So try downloading your current kernel's sources, the new driver's sources to the right spot and just learn how to compile a module. There's not really much chance of causing anything worse than a panic and a reboot. I've done it before and gotten it working iirc.
