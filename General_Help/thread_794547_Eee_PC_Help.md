### Post by zamboni on 2008-05-15
If you buy your eeepc from traditional store you might/should save a few  dollars, enough to offset some of the cost difference between the 2g and 4g models. Id  go with the 4g model myself for the difference. That's what I own, and I picked up the 4g701/webcam model for 399,00 back in December 2007. (They're still available at that price today from  the same retailer). Tigerdirect is often  10-20% more for  items, and  since your budget is really tight, shop  around retail. Unless you're in a remote place....


Installing ubuntu in 2g of SSD might be possible, but you'll probably want to trim a lot of stuff that you don't need or use. If you have experience on other platforms with installing ubuntu, you'll know  what to do. 

Currently I have Gutsy Gibbon and Hardy Heron installed on an external  WD Passport 160g, and am about to install Slackware on a third partition. Booting from the WD Passport has shown no problems for me. The drive assignments tripped me up during the install but after asking a question here I got it sorted.

Another point to consider: The battery is a little more powerful on the 4G (5200mAh) according to the wiki -- the 2g battery rated at 4400mAh. 
It's your money! Me? Im a cheapskate and always shop for value! ;)

Oh yeah Im also located in canada so the price I quoted above is in canadian dollars.

### Post by aysiu on 2008-05-15
2 GB is a pittance. If that were all storage space, that would be fine, but it's total for the operating system *and* basic storage space (configuration files, temporarily downloaded documents, etc.). I'm not even sure a regular Ubuntu installation (without tweaking) can fit on 2 GB.

But, yes, you can use a USB stick to install Ubuntu on the Eee. Just follow these directions:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick#head-f8d0d804f646d0b18a951747c214a1be07f89e1b](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick#head-f8d0d804f646d0b18a951747c214a1be07f89e1b)

To boot from USB, press Esc during the bootup screen and select the USB device instead of the SSD.

I would not recommend stock Ubuntu, though. eeeXubuntu is tailored for the Eee specifically. And even that has a much slower boot time than the Xandros that comes with the Eee. I'd stick with Xandros.
