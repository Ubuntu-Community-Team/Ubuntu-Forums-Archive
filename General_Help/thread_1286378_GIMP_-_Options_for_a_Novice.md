### Post by Tybion on 2009-10-08
GIMP is a great photo editor, but it takes a while to get used to it. For me, changing some of the preferences has made it more user-friendly. These are my suggestions ..

From the main menu, choose **Edit/Preferences**[INDENT]**'Tool Options' - 'Save Tool Options on Exit'**
[ATTACH]131277[/ATTACH]
The tool options you set in this session will be remembered - you will not need to re-do your work.

**'Image Windows' - 'Use dot for dot by default'**
This is good if your pictures will be e-mailed around or put on a web-site - they are shown in GIMP at their real size.

**Untick both these -**
** 'Image Windows' - 'Resize Window on Zoom'**
** 'Image Windows' - 'Resize Window on Image Size Change'**
It is distracting for windows to be resized while you are working.

**'Image Windows' - 'Initial Zoom Ratio' = 1:1**
Reasoning as above.

**'Image Windows/Appearance' -**
Change both **Canvas Padding Modes** to **'Custom Color'** and set the colour to light grey or your preference.
This replaces the default black and grey checkbox pattern with something more neutral.
[/INDENT][INDENT]**'Window Management' - 'Save Window Positions on Exit'**
This is important. You have probably spent time positioning and
resizing the 3 GIMP windows so they are just right. You want this
remembered in your next GIMP session.

Click **'OK'** to save these preferences.

[/INDENT]Now click on the **'ToolBox'** Window, and then the **'Move Tool'** button.
Click on **'Move the Active Layer'**
I think this is the most intuitive option, but will still not allow you to move a part of the image that you have selected using one of the many nice select tools.  To do this you need to make a new **layer** from the selected area.  After you have selected an area, press Ctrl-C (Copy) or Ctrl-X (Cut) then Ctrl-V (Paste) - the area will then be an active 'floating' layer that you can now move.
[ATTACH]131632[/ATTACH]


Now click the **'Layers, Channels, etc.'** window and then click
on the **'Auto'** button. This ensures that the layers shown always apply to the active image.
[ATTACH]131633[/ATTACH]


Once you have GIMP set up the way you like it (using my suggestions or not), you can apply these settings to any other computer by copying the directory structure under **/home/*username*/.gimp-2.6** in Linux or **"c:\Documents and Settings\*username*\.gimp-2.6"** in Windows.
