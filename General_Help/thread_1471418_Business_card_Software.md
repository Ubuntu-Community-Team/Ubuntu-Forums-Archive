### Post by gvernold on 2010-05-03
Hi Alan,

Not sure if you're looking for something for the actual graphical design of the card or more of something that will lay the cards out on the pre-cut card sheets that are available. If it's the latter I wouldn't bother trying to use business card software. I have tried both Xerox and Fellows software on Windows for getting these cards right and they never print in the right places - the perforations always end up in the wrong place. You can measure it up yourself and copy the layout to Desktop Publishing Software. Test the print out with some card of the same thickness as your precut cards to make sure the printouts line up correctly...... your printer will drag the card in at different speeds to other models and lining it up is a bit of an art.

If it is for the cards graphical layout I would set up Scribus according the the size of the card you want. Particularly if you are going to get your cards professionally printed always make sure you leave an extra 2-3 mm of graphics on each side for bleed. Scribus is a great DTP package and I have produce the last six sets of my marketing materials with it (business cards, leaflets, headed stationary etc). It can also save to PDF which is quite acceptable at most printers these days.

Hope this helps.
