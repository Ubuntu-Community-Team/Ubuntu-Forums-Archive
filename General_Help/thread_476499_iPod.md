### Post by bronze on 2007-06-21
To eliminate basically all doubt here, I have a handy solution. It's called [Rockbox]("http://www.rockbox.org/").

**Edit:**Here's a link to wikipedia's article about rockbox. As you can see, it supports quite a wide range of both lossy and lossless audio formats. [CLICK ME.]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockbox")

It's an alternate firmware, but it will not delete your old firmware. With this installed, you can switch between the official firmware and rockbox firmware. Rockbox don't use any proprietary software. You can just copy/paste all you files and folders onto it, and you can just browse through them. Not just music, but photos, text documents and movies aswell (for movie, use plugin).
There's a catch, though... The battery runs out faster, but for me, this is not a problem.

It's skinnable.
