### Post by taladan on 2008-11-07
The easiest way of testing is to do the following:

[LIST=1]
[*]Download an iso of the distribution you want (Kubuntu, Ubuntu, Xubuntu, etc)
[*]Burn it to a disk
[*]Leave disk in tray, reboot.
[*]Make sure the bios is set to boot from the CD rom drive.
[*]Run the distro as 'try it out without installing'.
[/LIST]

If it runs rather well (it should be somewhat slow as it's running at CDRom speed) and it picks up all your hardware including your mouse, you know that it will run on your system.

That's the quick and dirty way to test system compatibility without having to nuke your windows install.

Hope this helps,

Tal

### Post by taladan on 2008-11-07
Once you burn the iso to a cd, just reboot the computer and make sure it's booting from the CD.  Run it as 'try ubuntu without installing' and if it picks everything up and runs, you'll know your equipment will work with it.  There are other commands you can use once you have it up and running to see what all your equipment is, but this is the quickest way to see if everything is going to work correctly.  The internet shouldn't be an issue.  I'm assuming, of course, that you have a standard broadband modem and that it connects to your computer using a Ethernet cable.  You can also open up a web browser during your LiveCD test and browse the web (it's a fully functional OS running from CD) and make sure that it sees your net connection correctly.


Tal
