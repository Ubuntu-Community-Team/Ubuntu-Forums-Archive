### Post by 3rdalbum on 2007-02-27
There is much controversy over the actual benefits of using a 64-bit operating system. There are generally two types of people:

1. People who say that 64-bit software on 64-bit OSes on 64-bit chips operates faster than a 32-bit setup, that graphics performance is much much better.

2. People who say that 64-bit on a 64-bit processor just makes the system use a bit more memory, and gives it the ability to use more than 4 gigabytes of RAM.

I've tried 64-bit on my processor, and I didn't notice any performance benefits. I do audio and video transcoding, so I really should have noticed a benefit if there was one. For simplicity, I really think it's better to run 32-bit Ubuntu, as Debian and Ubuntu don't really have multi-arch support and you can get some strange problems.
