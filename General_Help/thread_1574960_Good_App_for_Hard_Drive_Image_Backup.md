### Post by sikander3786 on 2010-09-15
> **polardude1983 said:**
> I thought of clonezilla but its linux only not windows :/
From the Clonezilla home page,

> 
Filesystem supported: (1) ext2, ext3, ext4, reiserfs, reiser4, xfs, jfs of GNU/Linux, (2) **[COLOR="Red"]FAT, NTFS of MS Windows,[/COLOR]** (3) HFS+ of Mac OS, (4) UFS  of FreeBSD, NetBSD, and OpenBSD, and (5) VMFS of VMWare ESX. Therefore you can clone GNU/Linux, [COLOR="Red"]**MS windows,**[/COLOR] Intel-based Mac OS, and FreeBSD, NetBSD, and OpenBSD, no matter it's 32-bit (x86) or 64-bit (x86-64) OS. For these file systems, only used blocks in partition are saved and restored. For unsupported file system, sector-to-sector copy is done by dd in Clonezilla.
