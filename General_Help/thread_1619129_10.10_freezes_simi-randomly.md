### Post by allan.refsgaard on 2011-03-31
Easy fix!!! and it's so simple, really. I too, have spent weeks downloading, installing, re-installing, downloading again, and so forth, but Ubuntu 10.10 has just continued periodically freezing totally, leaving me at my wit's end. I've tried everything suggested on the net, and there are many related threads and blogs out there, believe me, but nothing has worked in the end. 
Turns out, whereas I've always downloaded these small (about 700 MB) quick-fix ISO installation cds, which never worked, what I needed was simply to go back to the origins, - where the software actually stems from, and download the real deal. I found my answer at cdimage.ubuntu.com/releases, where I picked an ISO image that fitted me, at 4.1 GB. This larger, and more comprehensive (I wold assume) ISO image , did take a lot longer to download, and the eventual installation took about an hour, but Ubuntu works now, and never freezes. So, the moral of the story is, forget the easy, fast fix, if you want something done right, you have to work for it!!!    \\:D/
