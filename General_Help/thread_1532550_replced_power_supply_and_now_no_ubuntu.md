### Post by QIII on 2010-07-16
As above, you really need to get some more info on the board (what model of Asus?  The beep codes may change by model, even) and the BIOS.  The beep codes may vary widely.  That will be the only way we can help you move forward.

If the old power supply went out on you, as mentioned, it may have taken important mobo components along with it.

When you put it in, did you ground yourself to the chassis?  Did you perhaps touch anything if you were not grounded?  I don't do anything inside the chassis without a wrist strap.  The amount of current required to destroy some of the sensitive and critical components of your machine are measured in milliamps.  Maybe even microamps these days.  The slightest static discharge can be devastating.
