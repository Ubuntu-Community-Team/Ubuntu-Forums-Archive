### Post by eriktheblu on 2010-03-19
I have a USB hard drive that I use with my work computer.

This allows me to reboot with Ubuntu without having to modify any of the information on the internal disk. The admins would pitch a fit if I installed Ubuntu on their machine.

I find the performance to be quite acceptable. The limitations are more to do with the CPU, GPU, and RAM than the HDD speed.

One potential disadvantage is hardware. If you move the disk to another machine, it may take some work to reconfigure the OS to the new system.

Another I have encountered is that Ubuntu seems to like to overwrite the MBR of the internal HDD when installing to USB. What happens then is the computer will not boot without the external device. There is an install option on the live CD (since 8.10 I believe) for installation to USB devices, but from what I gather, that is not the same as an actual install.

The most certain way to avoid this is to remove the HDD before installing. This may invalidate some warranties.
