### Post by scooper on 2006-12-11
For the kernel and other general Linux stuff I'd check out O'Reilly books ([http://oreilly.com/)](http://oreilly.com/)).  The better-written non-free books can give a more organized/gentler introduction than the typical web article.  Of course that's a generalization that has many exceptions. :)

[http://www.ibm.com/developerworks/](http://www.ibm.com/developerworks/) has some decent articles introducing Python.  Python.org has links to other resources.  I have not found great printed books for getting started with Python.  So this is a case where the web is better.

Unfortunately over the years I've found that most of my learning has come from taking other people's work apart and trying to hack/tweak it.  It's uncomfortable at first, but a few small successes lead to a lot of knowledge sneaking up on you before your realize it. :)
