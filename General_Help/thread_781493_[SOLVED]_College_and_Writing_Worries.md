### Post by Zorael on 2008-05-04
I think you'd do great with an *ubuntu. You have the OpenOffice.org suite for your word processor/spreadsheet/presentation tasks, so you needn't worry about that. That said, if you haven't tried any of the other *ubuntu flavors, I'd suggest you download and burn a live cd of Kubuntu, and possibly Xubuntu if you mind alot about memory overhead and minimalism. KDE, Gnome, Xfce, and all the other smaller desktop environments all have their pros and cons, and it's up to personal preference to figure out which one you like best.

I'm a KDEling for life; Gnome prides itself on being minimalistic and simple, but I'd rather go with Xfce for minimalism. But that's me. It's the Discussion To End Them All. [*jab*](http://tinyurl.com/7kwms)

All applications will still run "as well" regardless of desktop environment, though most are created with a set window framework in mind. Gnome has GTK and KDE has Qt. KDE can, with the proper libraries, run all GTK programs - and vice versa.

You needn't worry at all about your laptop hardware falling short. There are peripherals and devices that need some tinkering to get to work, and (sadly) some that plain won't work at all (yet). However, to get this in perspective, try installing Vista on a laptop a few years old and see how far that gets you.

You might run into trouble if your university needs you to run a Windows-only application, but at least in Sweden most university computers run Linux to begin with, so at least over here that's not much of an issue. Of course, seeing as you've some experience with linux already, you know the deal with WINE and virtual machines.
