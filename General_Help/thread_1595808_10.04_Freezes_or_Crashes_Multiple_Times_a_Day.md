### Post by drs305 on 2010-10-12
@ tk189,

I don't want to stray too far from the thread topic, but there are options you can place on the *GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT="quiet splash"* line of /etc/default/grub.

These are special instructions passed on to the kernel. Examples include: noapic, nomodeset, acpi, apic, etc. 

Here is a list of [kernel parameters]("http://www.kernel.org/doc/Documentation/kernel-parameters.txt") and brief explanations of what they do. You'd have to know which ones might be beneficial for your situation and you wouldn't want to just blindly add them to the instructions. 

If you were searching the Internet and found something similar to your situation and someone mentioned adding kernel options as a solution the */etc/default/grub* file is where you would add them.
