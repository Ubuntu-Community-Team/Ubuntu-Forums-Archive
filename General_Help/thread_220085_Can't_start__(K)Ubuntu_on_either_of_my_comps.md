### Post by zhoux on 2006-07-21
there are two types of cds to use for installing ubuntu on a desktop - alternative and desktop.

IMHO the alternative cds are much easier to use and install, and the desktop cds must meet certain requirements to run, such as 256+ RAM.

so if you are sure of installing, then just use the alternative cds.

p.s. i've also read somewhere that there seems to be a lot of problems with the live cds...not constant problems that happen to everyone, but capricious problems...
