### Post by prem1er on 2009-05-12
You most likely have a dead drive, making recovery nearly impossible.  If however the drive is spinning (and you can't hear it) there is a solution that I would suggest.  Placing the drive in a freezer bag and placing it in your freezer for the night.  The next morning take the drive out of the freezer and let it warm up for about and hour.  After it has come to room temperature reconnect the drive and see if you are able to get it to mount.  This solution works because of the expanding/contracting that takes place with the drive parts (usually the read/write arm).  It usually only works for a limited time, so copy the data quickly.  If the drive is not spinning at all, than this solution will not work.  Good luck.
