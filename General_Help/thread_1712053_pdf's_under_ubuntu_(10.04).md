### Post by ZaphodFJ on 2011-03-22
PDF Xchange is a great piece of work.  If you are happy with it under WINE, stick with it.  I haven't found any other piece of PDF software, on any OS, that is as good.

Yes, Ubuntu supports sending out PDFs as a virtual printer and therefore lets you chop out pages via that route.

However, PDF Xchange has everything under one roof.  Plus, it's really efficiently written.  I've seen Acrobat and Nitro (for example) choke on a 2,000 page document with really complicated layers (Win XP, Dual-core CPU). PDF Xchange processed the same work inside 10-20 seconds. 

I think open source is a great philosophy.  Practically though, open source has not provided an excellent PDF solution, yet.
