### Post by noah++ on 2011-05-17
You might try burning with K3b. It's a KDE app (which can run under GNOME). If that doesn't work either, then the problem is almost certainly with a low-level library like cdrecord, or your burner, or the interaction between the two.

### Post by tredegar on 2011-05-17
If the source files are readable, then the burnt ones should be too.

Maybe your CD media is faulty? If it's CD-R, try CD+R, or just try another manufacturer (and remember the name of the faulty one, so you do not buy from them again).

Cheap CDs tend to be expensive in the long run, as they do not burn reliably at the speeds they say they can work at.
