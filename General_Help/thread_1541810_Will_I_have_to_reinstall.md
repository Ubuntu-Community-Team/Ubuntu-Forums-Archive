### Post by sydbat on 2010-07-30
It will likely cost less to get a brand new laptop. The labour alone for taking apart and rebuilding a lappy will cost the most.

Regardless, if you get a new motherboard or a new laptop, just plug the hard drive in and boot. It'll tell you right away if it will be successful or not. If not, pop in a LiveCD and reinstall your / partition (you do have your / and /home in separate partitions, right??). Make a backup with the LiveCD running first.

### Post by WorMzy on 2010-07-30
A few weeks back my water-based CPU cooler exploded and got coolant all over my motherboard, so I had to replace it with an older one. Both my Ubuntu installations (9.10 and 10.04) from the old motherboard worked fine afterwards, but Arch Linux that I installed refused to boot, as did the installation of Windows 7 that I had.

So it's hit and miss. A motherboard replacement *may* bork your OS's, and you may need to install them, but at the same time, you may not need to.
