### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2009-01-19
Be aware that you need to distinguish between MLC and SLC variants. MLC variants will only yield the performance advertised under perfect circumstances and will for the very most time barely come close to the performance of current drives.

The SLC variants provide awesome performance at awesome cost...

### Post by Slim Odds on 2009-01-19
> **Titan8990 said:**
> I believe what you are actually looking for is known as a SSD (solid state disk). They are much faster than tradition hard drives.

They are much faster for READS, not for WRITES. The also have a limited number of write cycles (this is getting better, but the still wear out).

So they are not a direct replacement for all disk activity (i.e., swap space).
