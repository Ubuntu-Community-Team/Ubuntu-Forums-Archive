> **akakingess said:**

Sorry, English is not my main language, so maybe I misunderstand your first question.
If not, what is usenet: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Usenet](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Usenet)

How you can assist:
Download the ubuntu ISO-file (preferably also the md5/sha/etc. hash files), check your download-checksum, and put those files together in a rar-file. You might want to split the rar-files into +/- 50 MiB-files, not needed, but usual they are splitted.
Then produce some parity-files (par2) for those rars. That way, if some blocks are missing, they can be repaired without redownloading the whole file.
To finish you'll put them on usenet, through a usenet-provider.

Although, don't put too much effort in it, as I found several linux-distributions are uploaded on usenet around the same moment they are "final". The last version (9.10) was uploaded by [email]ub@un.tu[/email] 169 days ago.
If one puts it on usenet, that's enough, because everyone with a usenetserver can access that one.
