### Post by quixote on 2010-06-16
The good news is: it sounds like you have a simple problem.  I found this site: [http://ubuntuguide.net/howto-fix-ureadahead-problem-after-upgrading-to-ubuntu-10-04](http://ubuntuguide.net/howto-fix-ureadahead-problem-after-upgrading-to-ubuntu-10-04).  The whole post is worth reading, but the short version is:

--boot with a live CD
--open terminal (Applications > Accessories > Terminal) and disable ureadahead.conf by typing this to do it:

```
sudo mv *[COLOR="Blue"]path-to-your-harddrive[/COLOR]*/etc/init/ureadahead.conf *[COLOR="Blue"]path-to-your-harddrive[/COLOR]*/etc/init/ureadahead.conf.disable
```

Then try booting again and keep your fingers crossed!
