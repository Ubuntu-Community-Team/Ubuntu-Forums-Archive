### Post by bobince on 2009-08-22
Do be aware level1.gz and other blacklists aimed at copyright enforcement orgs are pretty much useless today. The real anti-piracy orgs use home DSL connections you cannot distinguish from normal peers.

All PeerGuardian and level1.gz will do is block (a) actual users that are at work from a company that has something to do with the media, and (b) random other networks that have been included for no good reason.

The other lists may still have some merit, but are of little use for filesharing purposes.

### Post by aphirst on 2009-08-22
You can never be *absolutely safe*.

However, I've gone for several years on:

> using updated blocklists
> randomising ports
> forcing inbound and outbound encryption
> ensuring that the torrent has a good reputation amongst other members of the tracker
> avoiding material which seems "more likely" to get me stung

...and I've been absolutely fine.
