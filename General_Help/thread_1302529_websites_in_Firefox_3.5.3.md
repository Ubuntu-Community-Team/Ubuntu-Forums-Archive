I'm running Shiretoko - with these plugins .. NoScript, WOT, Web Developer, User Agent Switcher, Show IP, IMTranslator, ChatZilla, Download Helper..

Stable as a rock, has been since Ubuntu suddenly popped it on my system.

There is a live & active thread on FF here ....     [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1193567](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1193567)


The sites that seem to have problems, are those that fail W3C Validation disgracefully (Seriously, over 200 errors on the main page ?!!!) They are falsely claiming, say xhtml, compliance - So, FF, takes them at their word and applies those rules.

If you're wondering why FF is getting picky - I 'think' some of it has to do with the developers getting fed-up of writing loads of extra code (thus making FF slower, bigger ...) to handle these horrendously slung together web-sites.

Pop Web Developer into FF - Watch for the ticks on the right hand side of your screen ... Then watch for the red crosses and exclamation marks ;)  It's become a hobby for me .... 272 Errors on the home page is my high score for a commercial site's front page :o

You can catch a peek of how WebDeveloper looks at [http://phillw.net](http://phillw.net)  (Although it's not posted there for that reason - It's the having Karmic Koala on the front page of the BBC News Web-Site ... KEWL !!!!!

You'll notice that you can confirm my complaince to both xhtml & css by clicking the button at the bottom of the page. With Webdeveloper, you find the option under 'Tools' ... It's great fun ;)
