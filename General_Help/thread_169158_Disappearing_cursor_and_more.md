### Post by orbit on 2006-05-02
I used Eye of Gnome to view either a jpg or ppm image fullscreen.  After pushing ESC to get out of fullscreen there were a few problems with Gnome:

1) mouse cursor was gone.  Clicking, scrolling and back clicking did nothing. I managed to get into a terminal.  'cat /dev/psaux' displayed garbage when I moved my mouse (like it is supposed to).  
After getting into  gnome-mouse-properties with the keyboard I enabled "Highlight the pointer when you press Ctrl".  When I pressed Ctrl it highlighted a spot on the screen.  After moving the mouse and trying again the spot that was being highlighted moved.  It was like the mouse was behind all the windows and desktop.

2) I could not use the keyboard to get into the Applications/Places/System menu.  If I pushed alt-F1 the Application button highlighted like it was being pushed, but the menu did not show up.  

3) Alt-Tab did not switch windows.  Minimized windows did not become unminimized even when there were no other windows on the workspace.  Background windows did not come up infront of a focused window.  I could switch between worspaces fine.

Ctrl-Alt-Backspace is the most gentle way I know how to get the mouse back.  When I did this, just after all my windows and desktop were gone but before all gnome was totally gone the mouse showed up again!

This has happened twice while using eog in the last two days.  Any help would be much appreciated.  Either a fix or a better way to bring the mouse back would be great!  

I use Ubuntu Breezy Badger and eog 2.13.2-0ubuntu1~breezy1.  If you need anything else, just ask!


Joel Faber
