### Post by Khaled Khalil on 2010-02-18
Hello everybody
Have you tried to synchronize your contacts and messages from your series 60 3rd phone to your computer, but got tired of hours of file configurations, using X/Gnokii, Wammu/Gammu or Multisync/Opensync ? and yet you got no success, because gnapplet won't install on your device, or syncML isn't supported by one application, while other's AT mode's "limited functionality" means literally "no functionality" ?

If this is the case, don't despair, i found lately this promising project, [[COLOR="Blue"]Series 60 Remote[/COLOR]](http://series60-remote.sourceforge.net/), finally my 5700 phone had some luck, it should works with virtually [[COLOR="Navy"]all[/COLOR]](http://series60-remote.sourceforge.net/index.php?module=Content&func=view&pid=8) series 60 3rd edition phones.

Features i should mention:
1- NO need for any pre-configuration, a very minimal wizard will find your phone by bluetooth and guide you through two straight-forward steps.
2- Contacts list db, so you can search contacts easily.
3- Messages history, where you can browse your incoming/outgoing messages by date and filter by sender/receiver.
4- Message statistics.
5- Chat through messages.
6- Filemanager that lets you get files from phone.
- Device information.
- Very comprehensive GUI.

The program is really straight forward, so i don't think that any tutorial is needed.

Now after the 0.4 beta 2 just released, you can install it easily by the .deb package from [[COLOR="#000080"]the download page[/COLOR]](http://series60-remote.sourceforge.net/index.php?module=Content&func=view&pid=7).


Bear in mind that currently only bluetooth is supported, but if you can only connect your phone by USB stay tuned, a later version may [[COLOR="#000080"]support USB[/COLOR]](http://series60-remote.sourceforge.net/index.php?module=Dizkus&func=viewtopic&topic=4&start=0#pid11).
Calendar events is also planed in an even earlier update.

[[COLOR="DarkOrange"]LuHe[/COLOR]](http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=642747) is one of the fastest developers, he is very smart and cooperative, so if you found any problem post it to [the project's forum](http://series60-remote.sourceforge.net/index.php?module=Dizkus) and he may help you.

Best Regards
