### Post by amanchesterman on 2014-05-31
I don't have a Sony reader but I do have a Kobo and a Kindle, so I have some experience in this general area.
Calibre is great when you have downloaded the e-text file to your computer, but I think your question is mainly about how to get the file in the first place?
The simplest way I have found is wireless connection. Does the Sony reader have wifi? If so, and if the books have been purchased on your wife's account on the Kobo website, then you may be able to download them direct from the Kobo server.
I did get Adobe Digital Editions to run on Ubuntu using Wine, but I couldn't get it to detect the reader when it was plugged in to USB.
Maybe someone else on the forum with more experience can add to this.
