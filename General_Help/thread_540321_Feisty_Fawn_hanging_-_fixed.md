### Post by berniedd on 2007-09-01
Lately my computer (1.1 GHz Sempron) has been freezing that only a hardware reboot could wake up from. I thought it might be the motherboard again because the symptoms were the same when it still ran Windows XP. These symptoms in its earlier life were fixed by replacing all electrolytic capacitors larger than 500 uFd on it with good quality low ESR (effective series resistance) Samxons 2 months ago.   I thought I heard some clicking sounds from the 2 year old DVD RW drive, so on a hunch, I powered down everything and removed the power and IDE cables from that drive. Now everything's back to normal.  

On hindsight, could it have been the power supply unit going marginal, and removing the dvd drive's voltage and current load improved the situation? Probably not, as the PSU is only 2 months old and unlikely to fail so soon. Of course,the best test would be to replace the dvd drive with a new unit, and maybe I'll get around to that in a few weeks. Right now, I don't need it yet.

PS   For others experiencing the same symptoms, bad RAM or an overheating CPU with a dead fan could also be the culprit.
