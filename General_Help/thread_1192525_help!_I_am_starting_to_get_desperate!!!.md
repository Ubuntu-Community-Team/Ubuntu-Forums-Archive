### Post by jdeppel on 2009-06-20
If you installed Ubuntu OVER your previous Windoze installation, the partition would have been reformatted, since Ubuntu uses a different file system than Windoze. 

You're pretty much hosed. I'm sure some of the data from the Windoze installation could be extracted off the hard drive, but it would be very hit and miss, and probably not enough to resurrect a bootable system.

### Post by el.otro on 2009-06-20
As long as you didn't have any important, valuable, personal files, there's no pain: you could always download a copy of Windows and boot from it, as long as you use the same *version* and *use your original key* (you paid for it after all), you'll have no problems.  Windows creates a "recovery partition" from which you can *reinstall* windows (I had that problem once), but if you chose to install ubuntu on the entire disk you reformatted that partition...

    I recommend you install windows in a partition half the size of your drive, and then reinstall ubuntu on the the second half, or chose whatever % you like, ubuntu only takes around 5GBs full install.  Don't give up on linux, there are always ways to fix your problems...

Oh, and by the way, _always_ do manual partitioning, with a little planning you will know how your drive is organized, and you'd have chosen the order yourself...;)

good luck

The problem with a copy of Windows downloaded from the internet is that it is going to be a pirated copy and will almost certainly come complete with a load of viruses and trojans.

However... **computer n11**, el.otro's suggestion is a good one and may work **if**

1 - you can borrow a valid Vista install disc from someone. You'll need the proper retail MS disc, not an OEM one.

**and**

2 - The product key is printed on the bottom of your laptop or in the documentation somewhere. If you've got the product key, you've paid for Vista so I would hope this would be OK from a legal point of view, although usual caveat: IANAL.

The problem will be is that you will also need a number of special hardware drivers. If Acer is any good, you'll be able to find them on their website. If not, well....

Alternative suggestions:

My Sony laptop comes with a restore partition, but also prompted me to burn a set of restore DVDs. I can reinstate the restore partition and/or reinstall Vista with these. Does Acer do something similar? Did you make the DVDs?

Failing all that - contact Acer. They may be able to sell you install-type restore DVDs for your particular model of laptop. Hopefully, they'll be cheaper than a retail Vista DVD, but it'll still cost I should guess. So long as you've got your product key that came with the laptop the licensing issue should be taken care of.
