### Post by raevin on 2007-03-02
My question's simple...

While doing ./configure, is there a way to tell it to name the executable a certain filename, or do you have to manually rename it yourself after you run through the make/make install stuff as well?

And, just in case I'm not making sense, this is what I mean:

Have a program, that after you do the configure/make/make install, is named "job" (w/o quotes obviously).

Instead of having the program be installed as "job", I want it to be installed as "jobwelove"...is this possible without having to do, say...cp job jobwelove/rm job?

---

### Post by david_2001 on 2007-03-02
Running "./configure --help" will get a listing of the possible options.  I don't know of any easier way of achieving what you want than "sudo mv job jobwelove".

### Post by po0f on 2007-03-02
raevin,

Unless you know how to edit the Makefile, the answer is no.  You'll have to manually change the binary name after you compile it.
