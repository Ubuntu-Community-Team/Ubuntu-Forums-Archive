### Post by pizza-is-good on 2010-11-15
Google says no...

A little insight for you here. Back when I took my SAT, I came across a report that showed that students who used an SAT prep software did not really increase their score.
Contrary to what many think, the SAT measures ability to learn, not knowledge. There really is no software that can increase ability to learn.

Other than that, good luck. The SAT is important, but not as important as they tell you (or at least what they told me...) unless you get a perfect score. The valedictorian in my high school class got a perfect score, and he got a full scholarship so some fancy business school. Many were not far behind him and didn't get anything...

By the way, seeing as you are a ham, you might also want to take the ACT. It seems to me that techy people do better on the ACT than in the SAT. At least that's how it was for me.
