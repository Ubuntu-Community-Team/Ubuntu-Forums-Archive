### Post by ram2500 on 2011-01-02
You have nearly 1 GB of memory, so it shouldn't slow down; however, the processor is probably in the ranks of an Intel Atom. What are your main tasks with this computer (video editing, graphics rendering, etc.)? Try shutting it down, restart it and *immediately* type into a terminal **top**. and post it here, even though you've done that before (but while you had programs running). It would be beneficial to know if there is anything loading during startup that causes your computer to be slow, as you mentioned that a shutdown or restart would not do much. I have noticed that Ubuntu doesn't freely place everything in startup, like Windows tends to do with many programs, but you might have a program that has a startup daemon, and a BIG one.

It's kind of difficult, at least for me, looking at the output as it isn't "in line" and formatted, so if you could perhaps do a print-screen and include an image of top running, that would be easier. :p

At last resort, it would probably be beneficial to not only upgrade, but perhaps reinstall. I find that even Ubuntu benefits from a reinstall at about once a year.
