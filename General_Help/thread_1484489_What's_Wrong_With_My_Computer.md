### Post by BoneKracker on 2010-05-15
> **HalfEmptyHero said:**
> Ok, I'll try that next time it happens. Its been fine for 2 or 3 hours, so hopefully it doesn't happen again.

It's the gravitational effects of the 2012 alignment or our solar system with the galactic core.  Now that the Moon has swung out of alignment, you're okay until that time again tomorrow. :P

Try booting from a CD yet?  I would have tried that first to rule out software problems.

The ribbon cable from the LCD is usually firmly mounted to the actual LCD but then snaps into the mainboard.  It can become unseated.  You could check to make sure it's seated properly and inspect the ribbon cable for damage.

BIOS batteries are cheap.  You can bring it into Radio Shack and they can test it (they should test it "under load".  If the person you're talking to doesn't know what that means, either walk away or just buy one.  Usually like $15 or something.  If the machine is less than 3 years old and hasn't been in adverse conditions (beer spill, left in car in Winter, etc.), then that's probably not it.

You might have onboard graphics, but if you have a GPU it could possibly have come unseated.  RAM can also come unseated, although you'd probably see different symptoms.  Make sure everything is firmly seated.
