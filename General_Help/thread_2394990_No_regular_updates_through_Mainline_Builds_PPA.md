### Post by Doug S on 2018-06-25
Hi and welcome to Ubuntu forums.

The [Ubuntu mainline PPA]("http://kernel.ubuntu.com/~kernel-ppa/mainline/?C=N;O=D") contains the leading edge kernels and not to be considered (Ubuntu) stable and to do be considered, very much, use at your own risk. It simply would not make sense to in any way automate the use of it. It must be a conscious decision to use it.

Now, you might argue that some kernels, such as [4.17.2]("http://kernel.ubuntu.com/~kernel-ppa/mainline/v4.17.2/") are stable. Yes, they are considered stable at [kernel.org]("https://www.kernel.org/"), but the mainline PPA version has not had the Ubuntu specific patches added, nor had any Ubuntu regression testing done so one would still want to manually decide to install it.

My answer is opinion based, and other opinions may differ.

Hope this helps
