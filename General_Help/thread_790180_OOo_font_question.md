### Post by Vivaldi Gloria on 2008-05-11
There is an option in M$ Office which embeds the fonts used into the document so that where ever and whenever you open the document you see it with the right fonts. 

Is there a way to embed fonts into documents in OOo? I couldn't find it anywhere in the options.

### Post by metiosarius on 2008-05-11
> **Vivaldi Gloria said:**
> There is an option in M$ Office which embeds the fonts used into the document so that where ever and whenever you open the document you see it with the right fonts. 

Is there a way to embed fonts into documents in OOo? I couldn't find it anywhere in the options.

You couldn't find it because it doesn't exist. It appears that there are certain legal issues with embedding fonts that OOo doesn't have a license for. If you want more detailed information, view: [http://www.openoffice.org/issues/show_bug.cgi?id=20370](http://www.openoffice.org/issues/show_bug.cgi?id=20370)

Although I am unsure of the legality, (and it is most likely entirely illegal for proprietary fonts - but that shouldn't be a problem, there are thousands of open source fonts) as a work-around, you could just send the TTF with the document for installation on the target system. Most fonts in Ubuntu are open source and are available at /usr/share/fonts/truetype and are generally under 100k which is fairly small in terms of email attachments. Just make sure you are only transferring open source fonts, otherwise there could be legal implications.
