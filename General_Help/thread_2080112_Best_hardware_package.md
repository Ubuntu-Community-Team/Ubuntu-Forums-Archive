### Post by hal8000 on 2012-11-03
I jumped ship along time ago.

Check out hardware on the Linux HCL:

[http://linuxhcl.com/](http://linuxhcl.com/)

If you want to see what Ubuntu have certified:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/desktop/](http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/desktop/)


Most hardware should work with linux providing the manufacturer has provided sufficient details about their product so that someone can write a driver.

If you were to buy something brand new like a wireless dongle, then the
chipset may not be recognised, so its always better to google what you buy
and search for problems.

When i built my new computer I took several months reasearching the forums
before deciding what to buy. I'm quite happy with my Gigagbyte GA-P55A-UD3, Intel i5 750 and ATI 5770 card. Support is slightly better for Nvidia than ATI, but ATI works fine on most linux distributions, but no ATI accelerated driver for FreeBSD yet.
Hope that helps.

### Post by gordintoronto on 2012-11-04
Very often, brand new hardware is not supported well, whether it's video, sound or networking. If you buy components which appeared a year ago, everything usually works perfectly.

I'm pretty sure Dell offers both AMD and Intel CPUs. Their offerings vary by country.

My two-year-old (cheap) HP G62 laptop works great with all flavors of Linux. It's not available any more, of course. Initially, there were issues with sound, not switching properly between on-board speakers and earphones. Also, I needed to connect by ethernet cable to install the wireless drivers. Both those things are not an issue in 2012.
