### Post by srs5694 on 2010-09-03
I'm the author of the Sybex Study Guide books others have mentioned. The latest Linux+ exam is a re-badging of the LPIC exam, and my *Linux+ Complete Study Guide* (ISBN 978-0-470-88845-2) is a minor update/refresh of my [i]LPIC-1 Study Guide, 2nd Edition[i] (ISBN 978-0-470-40483-6). Note that there were earlier versions of both books, so be sure to get the latest one. Since the Linux+ book includes some minor typo corrections and a small number of technology updates, it's a bit better, even if you're studying for the LPIC-1 exam.

As to being able to tell which book is best by the tile, I'm sure the publishers would include "this is *the best book* on the subject!" in the titles, but with every publisher doing it, this wouldn't really help. ;) If you have the cash, it wouldn't hurt to buy at least two books. If nothing else, that'll give you extra study questions and fallback text in case something in your primary title seems unclear or less complete than you'd like. If you don't have the cash for two books, check your local public library. Even if they don't have a book for the exam you plan to take, chances are they've got other Linux books, which can help by providing another perspective on the topics. Read your primary textbook, then skim the second book to pick up additional details and that second perspective.
