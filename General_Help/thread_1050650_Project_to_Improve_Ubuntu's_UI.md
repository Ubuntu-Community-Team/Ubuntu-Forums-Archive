### Post by dyous87 on 2009-01-25
As I write this post I am in the process of setting up a MediaWiki installation for a new project I'd like to start. The Wiki is located here: [http://netforgeweb.com/renaissance](http://netforgeweb.com/renaissance)

Right now I am naming it Project Renaissance and its purpose will be to add to the greatness of Ubuntu by creating for it a unified, modern, and attractive look and feel for that focuses on simplicity and usability. 

 That will be this projects main goal. Getting the Ubuntu UI up to par with modern standards. This is not to say to say that the current Ubuntu Desktop is terrible. Simply that it can be much much better. It can truly be something unique and beautiful. This is one place where in my opinion Apple shines where other operating system designers don't quite come as close. 

 The Ubuntu Developers have promised us a fresh new theme for Ubuntu for the past several distribution releases now, however for whatever reason we have not gotten one. I believe we have gotten to the point where the default Ubuntu desktop and the default look of Gnome have become quite dated by modern standards. 

Ubuntu is of course an incredible operating system. It is easy to use and setup and it gives users the power of Linux in an easy to use and stable environment with hundreds upon hundreds of great high quality software to choose from. Some may argue that the default look and feel of Ubuntu is irrelevant because any user can customize it however he or she likes. I however believe that a unique and attractive default look is vital to the long term success, acceptance and image of Ubuntu. 

I propose that we organize a team to achieve these goals: 
  
 I am looking forward to input from as many people as possible and would love to have mockup designers, graphic designers, themers and programmers joining me in accomplishing this. We can brainstorm together what we think the best interface design will be for usability and simplicity. 

First let me list what I think are some of the major areas of the Ubuntu Desktop that can be improved. 

[LIST=1]
[*]I don't believe a two panel system is the best way to organize the desktop. What are everyone's thoughts on this?
[*]The Gnome Menu bar I think is very dated and ugly looking. It has cluttered menus that does not leave much space for usability and attractiveness. Other alternatives have arisen such as the Mint Menu, or the SUSE Slab menu. Another way of doing things is the Mac OSX Finder which opens up a new window with access to all utilities and applications. We should look at all these examples and decide what is the best way is in terms of usability and astetics.
[*]The Gnome panel notification menu is also very dated and ugly looking. Look here for more details: [http://www.bomahy.nl/hylke/blog/ugly-notification-area-in-gnome/](http://www.bomahy.nl/hylke/blog/ugly-notification-area-in-gnome/)
[*]I also feel the same about the Gnome Window List Applet. What is a better way of listing open applications? We have Avant Window Navigator however is this too Mac like? I also believe there are usability issues with this system however and I'd love to hear what everyone thinks about this?
[/LIST]
     This is just a starter list and I'm sure more will come up with all of your help.  
  
 Overall I believe we need a theme and a look that is attractive and simple while remaining seamless and sophisticated. It needs to reflect Ubuntu's "human" roots and should definitely not try to be something it is not. We love Ubuntu because it is Ubuntu. We love it for the very reason that it is different then OSX and Windows and for that very reason we need a unique feel to it that can be both recognizable and loved by those that use it and those that will come to use it!  
 
Just as a side note, this will be a community project that will distribute a version of Ubuntu solely with a modified look and feel for the purpose of simplicity and usability. We are not trying to get the official Ubuntu releases to change to the look and feel that we come up with. If this does happen however then by all means our work will have succeeded beyond what I'd ever have imagined.
