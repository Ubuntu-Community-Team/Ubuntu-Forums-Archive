### Post by akiro.yamamoto on 2006-04-04
You may have to enter the frequency ranges for your monitor.... it's a pain I know but I had a similar problem with my monitor when I switched from the default 'nv' 
to the 'nvidia'  driver.
It should be something similar to
```
 
Section "Monitor"
    DisplaySize    300 230
    Identifier    "Generic Monitor"
    Option        "DPMS"
    HorizSync    30-86
    VertRefresh    50-130
EndSection

```

You should be able to get the info from either your manual or online....
Hope this tip helps ;)
