### Post by RTrev on 2010-05-09
Keeping your Ubuntu system safe is mostly common sense, and includes things like:

- Never run as root unless you have to.

- Keep your physical computer secure.  If someone has physical access to the machine, then they own it.

- Social engineering, such as tricking you into entering your password on a site masquerading as the site you intended to go to, works equally well no matter what OS you're using.

IMHO, you only need to worry about anti-virus solutions IF you are going to be sharing files with Windows machines.  This may change in the future, but at the moment the malware is going after the low hanging fruit and attacking Windows machines.  There are so many of them out there, and they're so easily compromised, that the malware authors don't bother with us. :)
