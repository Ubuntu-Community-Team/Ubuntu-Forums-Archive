### Post by QIII on 2012-06-12
I update daily.

The number of available updates you see is normal shortly after a new install if the release is a month or two old.  Things are constantly being updated.

You can choose to accept only security updates if you choose, but you miss all the incremental improvements to things.

Coming from the Windows environment this often seems strange to people.  Changes to the kernel, the distro, the installed packages, etc, come out as they are made and available without having to wait.  Continuous improvements are made to various things on almost a daily basis.

If you don't want to do it daily, wait until you are notified periodically.

### Post by lisati on 2012-06-12
Having a lot of updates is normal after a fresh install, and is usually nothing to worry about. They can sometimes take care of issues that would otherwise have you search the forums or rushing off to Google.

Once your system is up and running, the choice is yours how often you run updates. My preference is to not wait too long before applying updates, otherwise there could be an enormous time consuming download. What's more, we have the option of choosing what and when!
