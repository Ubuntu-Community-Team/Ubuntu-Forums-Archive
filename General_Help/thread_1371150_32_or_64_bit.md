### Post by Shippou on 2010-01-03
If it were me, I would use x64, just for the heck of it.

Seriously, why would I use x64? Correct me if I an wrong, but since 64-bit is backwards-compatible with 32-bit, it enables me to use cutting-edge(?) technology while not worrying that my programs would break.

And consider this: what if you decide that you need more than 4GB of RAM? If you are in a 32-bit OS, you would have to reformat in order for the OS to recognize the >4GB. If you are in 64-bit, no reformat is needed at all.

I have read the post that says that he/she has 32-bit Xubuntu detecting more than 4GB of RAM (if I understood correctly). I am really interested on how he could have done this, but as an ordinary user this really would not work.

Caution: It does not mean that having a 64-bit OS means that your system would run twice as fast as a 32-bit system. It still depends on how powerful your CPU is (how many cores, hyperthreaded, etc.), how large your RAM is (in GB, PB, or EB), how fast is your I/O, and other things. It also depends on how optimized your OS is, what applications you run, etc.

Well, just my two Philippine pesos. (I am from the Philippines, you know.)
