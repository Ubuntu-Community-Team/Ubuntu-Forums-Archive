Hi, <incoming text wall be warned!> 

I am having the same issue after I switched to fglrx-update from xorg drivers from the Additional drivers menu. Please bear with me for I am a total newbie here and this is the first time ever in my life I am using a Linux/Ubuntu.
 I use AMD Radeon Caicos (6450 HD) as my GPU. So what I did was to purge all the fglrx driver packages and then re-install Xorg ati wrapper and that got this issue resolved. 

Now the problem is when I am trying to play a 1080p or certain 720p videos on VLC when using the open source drivers the colours seem to be dull and the video playback is choppy. Seeking is a nightmare. I thought may be open source drivers don't support hardware acceleration for video playback so I decided to get back to fglrx-update/fglrx-core from the additional drivers menu and Voila! the colours and the video playback was hella fine and was so much better than my codec pumped movie watching days on windows. I just double checked under the VLC>preferences>codec menu to see hardware acceleration was enabled on both cases. Now this makes me using fglrx-update drivers irreplaceable for me.

Now before I did all this I followed this thread and tried all the workarounds like checking if there is an issue with Xauthority and other replacing "quiet splash" stuff and nothing solved the login issue for me unless I purged the fglrx drivers and re-installed xorg drivers.

So what I have wildly concluded is I am able to login when using open source drivers but whenever I am using my fglrx-update drivers it pushes me back to "drumsound>login screen>enter credentials>blackscreen that lasts for about a second>drumsound>login screen again" loop. Unity desktop must have some issues with hardware acceleration on fglrx.

As a temporary workaround and as a force of habit, every time as soon as I login I switch to fglrx-update drivers and when I am about to shut down I switch back to Xorg ati wrapper from the additional drivers menu so that I don't mess up the login. It was pretty annoying in the earlier days (just a week ago)  where I would just forget to switch back to Xorg before shutting down and then have to do a purge and reinstall open source drivers the next time I log in but now I made myself to never forget and put a huge sticky on my desktop to switch back to xorg drivers before shutting down. 

I will be so glad to help to get this issue resolved and stay on fglrx-update drivers without having to worry about switching back to Xorg drivers every time I shut down my machine for my next successful login. :D

PS: I forgot to mention that not only VLC video playback for 1080p is choppy when using open source drivers but also youtube 1080p playback. :popcorn:
