### Post by Tipped OuT on 2009-05-30
You can lower the opacity of the entire windows with compiz. You must enable the plug-in called opacity/brightness/saturation in the compiz config. Then put your mouse over the desired window, hold ALT and scroll down with your mouse or touch pad to lower the opacity. 

If you don't have compiz config then enter this command into terminal:[B] sudo apt-get install compizconfig-settings-manager
[/B]
And you can find it in **System< Preferences< CompizConfigSettingsManage**r

If you mean just the window borders then you can use a window manager called Emerald.

Go to **System< Administration< Synaptic Package Manager**

and search for "**emerald**". Install, then hold **ALT+F2** and type "**emerald --replace**"... and voila! You can download different emerald themes from gnomelook.org and install them.
