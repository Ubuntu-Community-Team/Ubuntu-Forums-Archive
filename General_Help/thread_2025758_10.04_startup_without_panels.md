### Post by L a r r y on 2012-07-14
**Retitled:: UBUNTU 10.04 - Restart without Icons | Restart without Panels**

Hello.
I run two computers, one running 12.04 and Unity, the other running 10.04 with Gnome 2 Desktop.

I have an issue with with my 10.04 installation in which I have been doing an automatic log-in.  I arrive at the Desktop with Panels in place, no icons on the Desktop.

To resolve this, I have a launcher for "No Icons on Desktop" on my top panel.  It runs:
```
gksudo /etc/init.d/gdm restart
```
in which I have to enter my password and it restarts the Desktop, and displays the icons, but no panels.  There is space reserved top and bottom for these panels, but nothing visible there.

So I also have a launch icon on the desktop, called "Restore Panels" which runs:
```
killall gnome-panel
```

Killall kills the failed panel operation, and successfully restarts it.  The Desktop icons for a moment occupy the reserved space at the top of the screen, then are returned to their position below the now-functional top panel.

I figured, I have to log in anyway, to restart the Desktop, let's remove the automatic log-in.

Now, I hit the Log-in screen, enter my password and up comes the Desktop, minus visible panels.  I run the "Restore Panels" icon (killall gnome-panel) and panels are restored.

As it was, I had to enter my password once whether I did automatic log-in and restored icons, or logged in manually, with no need to restore icons.

Could someone with some scripting knowledge take a look at my installation, and let me know what I need to do to fix this issue?

You ask me for the logs, the output of a command, lines of a certain script, and I paste the request into a reply to this thread for your examination?

My thanks in advance.


Some additional info:


The other day, I double-clicked the  icon to restore panels, and it didn't take -- one of these deals where one click brings the focus to the Desktop and I need two more clicks to double-click the icon, in essence a triple-click.  

And I have wondered why it is that it is necessary to click three times.  I think what I did when nothing happened with my double-click, consisting of once to get its attention and once to highlight the icon, I said some blue language as I quadruple-clicked or more, starting the killall gnome-panel operation twice.

I killed it all right.  I was forced to open a Terminal and run gnome-panel because there was no process to kill.  And was forced to minimize the Terminal window for the duration of the session, for if I closed the Terminal, the panels went with it!

Today's restart of the computer resolved the issue with the missing process.
