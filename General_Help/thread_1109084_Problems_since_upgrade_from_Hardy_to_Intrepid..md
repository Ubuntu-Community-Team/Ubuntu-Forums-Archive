### Post by jfinner1 on 2009-03-28
I'm not sure if I should make each issue a different thread or not, but since all of these problems started happening withing a few days of upgrading, I have a feeling it's somehow all connected. Here are my problems:

Firefox: Every time I open FF, click on an external link, or open a new window, it does something strange. It *almost* looks like I've hit the F11 hotkey, only instead of full-screening the full page, it just covers my menu panel at the top, and cuts off the title bar. I can fix this by hitting F11 twice, once to continue full-screening, and again to put it back to normal. At first I thought that this was just Firefox, but then I noticed it do the same thing a few times in a few other programs. Basically, anything that opens maximized has a chance of opening in this odd full-screen mode, but only FF seems to do it every time.

Wireless: Every time I Hibernate my computer, it won't connect to the wireless when I wake it up. In fact, as far as the computer's concerned, there are no networks in range. When I reboot, it's fine. It was giving me even more problems, but I fixed some of them following the advice in [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1084296").

Backgrounds: I have my desktops set up with a different image on each of my four desktops, using Compiz. Now, they just randomly disappear. Sometimes, it will just be one of the images, and the others will be fine. Sometimes it's 2, 3, or all 4. The really odd part is that on the few occasions prior to the upgrade that Compiz froze (I think it happened twice) all my backgrounds went away at the same time and it had the default background instead. Now, there is no background, it just turns the whole background black. Confused...

Sound: This one is a little hard to explain, and it happens kinda randomly, but I think I've figured out what sets it off. Sometimes when I have more then one application with sound running, such as listening to music and playing a game, it will "freeze up". It sounds like a cd that's stuck in a skip, and I'm really not sure how else to explain it. It's tends to only happen if both application have been running for a while, and I can usually avoid it by close one or the other for a few minutes every half hour. But, once it happens, closing the applications will not get the sound to stop. I have to reboot my computer. I previously had problems with sound before the upgrade, but they were fixed in [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1000648"). I figured I'd mention that in case the problem I'm having now is related. 

Time: Sometimes when I turn on my computer, the time is wrong. I don't mess with it, and it fixes itself on reboot, but it is a bit annoying.

I think that's it. Most of this is stupid little stuff, but I swear that I've had to reboot my computer more times in the past month since I upgraded, then I've had to since I've installed Ubuntu. Oh, and another random side-note. When I open the "About Ubuntu" that's supposed to tell me my version, all it says is this: 
Thank you for your interest in Ubuntu - the - released in .
It doesn't tell me my version anymore... What's with that? Thanks in advance for any help.

Long story short, I too had no end of weirdness with Intrepid, and that after ages of blissful stability with Hardy.  In my (limited) experience with Ubuntu this represents a crossroads for me.  And by "me" I mean "you"...

I can take the more difficult path and (attempt to) work out all the weird little issues individually (you wind up learning a lot this way), or you can stick with what works and hope the next release is your Salvation.  Like Jaunty is for me.  

I just plain skipped Intrepid after beating my head against a wall trying to make it work.  I learned a good deal about Ubuntu, generally, by sticking it out with Intrepid as long as I did, but then at some point I just wanted the pain to stop as well.
