### Post by bclintbe on 2010-01-28
Open the document and get stuff as well placed on the screen as possible (everything visible). Press the "Print Screen" button on your keyboard (usually found to the right of F12). This will allow you to save a screen capture. Save the file to someplace easy like your desktop. 
I usually like to cut away the edges (stuff not part of what the iron on will be) so open the image with GIMP, select the part you want, use Copy, then Paste as New. Save this image as a png file (like ironon.png) to your desktop. 
Double click it to open it with the default image viewer. Choose Image> Flip Horizontal. You may also want to rotate clockwise. When you click Print, you can see what it will look like on the page by clicking Image Settings tab. Adjust as necessary.
I know it's a lot of steps, but it works.
