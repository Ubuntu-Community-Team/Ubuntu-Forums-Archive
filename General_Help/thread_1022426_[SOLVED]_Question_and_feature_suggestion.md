### Post by the real omni on 2008-12-26
Now and then a window will hang - I can click the "Close" or "Cancel" buttons or the close-window button in the menu bar, and the window will stay until I hunt down the PID and kill -9 it.

It would be totally AWESOME to be able to right-click on the task in the taskbar and instead of choosing "close" having an option to "Send kill -9"

So, that's my suggestion for future releases of the gnome-taskbar.

In the meantime, how can I go about manually adding that option to the context menu?

---

### Post by MarblePanther on 2008-12-26
A really quick solution is just alt+f2, type xkill,hit enter, click on the application's window

I find it fast enough

You can also add ForceQuit to the panel

---

### Post by Shazaam on 2008-12-26
Until you find a context menu app, right-click a panel and choose "Add to panel" then add "Force Quit".
