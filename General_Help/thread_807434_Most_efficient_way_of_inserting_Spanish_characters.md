### Post by knowledgeispwr on 2008-11-21
> **DavidCain said:**
> Hi. Okay, so I type in Spanish quite frequently. I switched to Linux a while ago, and I quickly found that the Alt + xxxx combinations don't work. I know you can make a custom character chart on a panel for quickly inserting characters, but I find that horribly inefficient. What's the easiest way of inserting characters? I only care about Spanish characters.

You can right click on the panel at the top, and add the "keyboard indicator" applet to the panel.  Then right click the new applet, select "Preferences" and add a layout, US International with dead keys.  Make sure you select a keyboard shortcut to switch back and forth between layouts (I use *left Shift+ left Alt*).  Then, when you have the US International keyboard layout selected, you only have to type ' (single quotation mark) then 'a' to get an acute accented-a: á, for example.  No more messy Alt+ xxxx combinations.
