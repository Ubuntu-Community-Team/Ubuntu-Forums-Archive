### Post by adam.smith on 2011-05-13
Zotero standalone is currently in alpha, though approaching beta.
Mendeley is probably closest to what you want - its pdf management is quite good (not quite Papers, which benefits from the great pdf tools available for mac, but much better than Endnote). If your biggest concern is future proof, you may want to reconsider Zotero, though, which is FOSS whereas Mendeley is mostly closed and still relies on venture capital funding - you do have pretty good data export from either, so we're not talking total lock-in in any case, but moving your data is never loss-less and Zotero has a more secure future outlook:
[http://quintessenceofham.org/2011/05/06/zotero-versus/](http://quintessenceofham.org/2011/05/06/zotero-versus/)
I personally also have data privacy and ownership concerns with Mendeley, but that may be a matter of taste/priorities.
The idea that a browser plugin isn't robust is ill founded - Zotero is more stable than Mendeley, certainly under linux.
