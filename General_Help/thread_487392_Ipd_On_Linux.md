### Post by PaulFr on 2007-06-29
I do not understand: Do you want to access your IPOD files from Ubuntu, **or**
Do you want to share your IPOD files (which are already mounted on Ubuntu) with other systems on the network ?

1. If you want to access your IPOD files from Ubuntu, please see **[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/?action=fullsearch&context=180&value=Ipod&titlesearch=Titles](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/?action=fullsearch&context=180&value=Ipod&titlesearch=Titles)**. Then see _[http://www.linuxjournal.com/article/9266](http://www.linuxjournal.com/article/9266)_ for some interesting applications for your IPOD.

2. If, on the other hand, your IPOD is already mounted and accessible on your Ubuntu system, and you wish to provide files for other systems on the network, then you have two choices:

2.a. If you wish to provide files to Windows machines or a mix of Windows and Linux machines, **[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SettingUpSamba](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SettingUpSamba)** for how you can set up Samba.
2.b. If you wish to provide files to other Unix / Linux machines only, please see **[http://doc.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/serverguide/C/network-file-system.html](http://doc.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/serverguide/C/network-file-system.html)** for how you can set up NFS.
Hope this helps.

Not sure of the following, but from Apple's documents, there seems to be four things to check: 1. Your USB/Firewire port is high-power or powered, 2. Your computer does not hibernate or sleep, 3. The IPOD is directly connected to the computer USB/Firewire port and not a hub/external drive/reader. 4. Enable Disk Use check box is selected in Settings pane for this IPOD in iTunes.
