### Post by TheFu on 2018-07-08
Gnome3 is heavy and not a good choice for low-end computers.  That is what you've installed. In 18.04 the default "Ubuntu Desktop" comes with a newer version of Gnome3. It is also heavy.

The N3450 CPU is definitely capable of providing an acceptable desktop experience if you choose a desktop environment, DE, that isn't as demanding as KDE, Gnome3 or Unity.  That leaves LXDE, XFCE, or Mate as the major desktop choices.  There are 20+ other choices which are less popular, but they all have lighter graphics requirements.

So, that single requirement you have is likely something that shouldn't be met for the happiest result.

Of course, other people will have different opinions about this. My chromebook CPU is roughly twice as fast as yours and I think LXDE is too heavy.  It is all a matter of perspective.
