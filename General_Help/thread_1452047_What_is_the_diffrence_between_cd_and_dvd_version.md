### Post by stilling on 2010-04-11
The basic difference is the amount of storage on each disk. CDs have 700MB. DVD single layer have 4.7GB. And dual layer are 8 gb. So DVDs can hold easy over 6 times the data. You can use both for pretty much the same things. DVD is the disk of choice for movies games, etc etc that are over 700MB.
On the technical side the increase in the density is accomplished primarily by using a difference type of laser to burn the pits dvd lenght are shorter but for more info try

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_media_preservation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_media_preservation)

hope this helps you
