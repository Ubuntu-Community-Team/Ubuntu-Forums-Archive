### Post by howefield on 2016-01-10
I would use kdenlive (in a stock ubuntu installation this would bring in a load of kde dependencies - not a problem for me but may be for others). Will let you separate out the audio, edit video or sound as required then render out when finished. There may be simpler applications, I just like kdenlive (one of 3 "kde" applications I use.
