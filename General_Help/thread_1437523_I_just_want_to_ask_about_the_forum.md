### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-03-25
Don't know HOW you asked your questions before ...

But, to get responses (at least from folks like me), you really need to do the following:
1) Make the title informative -- the more specific the better.  Saying "high temps" for example, tells us almost nothing.  Saying "CPU keeps overheating, need help" is a lot more useful.
2) Show some patience.  Many of us are only able to provide support in our spare time, so depending on where you're posting from, it could be HOURS after you post before we come here to start looking for folks that need help.
3) Don't start bumping frequently.  Similar to above.  I come here, see a post that has been bumped four times in five hours, I give it a pass.
4) Use ONE THREAD for EACH PROBLEM.  Like others, I respond based on thread title.  The all-in-one threads become too difficult to read after a while.
5) Follow advice and provide feedback.  IF we say run something (as in a command), then do it.  Don't just come back asking the same thing again.  We need detailed info to debug problems.  Simply whining over and over about a problem will not get our support.

Finally) and probably most important --  do some Searches first.  Searching is easy here.  You can search ALL the forums or only one.  In many cases, the problems have already been answered.  You can benefit from what other folks have tried and from their solutions that have worked.
