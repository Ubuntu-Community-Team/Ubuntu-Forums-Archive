### Post by kherring7383 on 2011-07-26
If you wish to use it for both Windows and Ubuntu, make sure the drive is partitioned for NTFS which is accessible by both operating systems. If you already have files on the drive, back them up then use gparted to make as many partitions as you need and format the drive to NTFS and then after you put the files back on the drive you should be good to go .

### Post by Brushstroke on 2011-07-26
First of all, let me say that I love your username. 

As for your question, screw the software. It's not needed. Make sure it's formatted as NTFS (it should be by default), and just drag and drop your files onto the hard drive. It should be detected on both Ubuntu and Windows by default.
