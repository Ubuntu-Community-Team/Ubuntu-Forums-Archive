### Post by jdackle on 2010-04-07
You can usually setup most modems/routers (whatever type) for whatever ISP almost automatically.

Here's the *usual* scenario:
1. Ubuntu recognizes your modem correctly when connected.
2a. You are asked to answer some questions in order to set it up, "it" being your connection (to your ISP) through that modem. For this, you will usually just need to know a few infos you can definitelly get from your ISP (if not directly from the device's box and manuals, definitely by asking them from your ISP phone number, e-mail, shop, ...). They will usually say they don't support Linux, so just ignore that or simply don't even mention it - just ask all the infos you need (this will usually be your username and password (these you definitelly have with you already), the phone number to which the modem is to make the initial call, kind of connection (e.g. ADSL over Ethernet, ADSL over ATM, ...), simple stuff they'll be glad to tell you.
2b. Sometimes the previous point is even easier. In some cases, you can just choose in which country you are, what is the name of your ISP and what is your rate plan and that's it!
2c. Previously in 2a and 2b, I assumed Ubuntu automatically asked you to setup the device. This may not be automatic. If this is your case, just look at the Network Manager icon in your tray and click it (try both right-clicking and left-clicking and see what options you get).
So... just try connecting the device to your computer (ignore the CD or whatever they gave you) and see if Ubuntu can configure it for you (either by just providing the infos you already have (your username, password, country, ISP, etc or by answering a few more technical questions for which you only need to call your ISP).

If your device (modem/router/3G card/...) isn't automatically detected, you'll need to give more details on your next post so the people in this forum can help you more.

BTW, it is not only sensible to wait a little more than a few minutes for a reply in a forum, it's also nice to actually tell the people you've solved your problem when you do solve it. ;)
