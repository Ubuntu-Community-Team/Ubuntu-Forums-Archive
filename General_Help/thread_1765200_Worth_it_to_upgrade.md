Well, you'll get a lot of different opinions about whether it is worth it, that's for sure . . .

If you liked the idea of Unity, but the deal breaker was how slow it was, then I'd say it's worth upgrading.  It's been completely rewritten, and runs well on my netbook which has 512MB ram.  There are two main problems people have with it: 1) compiz can crash 2) People are lost when trying to find applications.  In my experience, compiz crashes are rare unless you tinker with ccsm a lot and, if you used Netbook Edition Unity, the second problem shouldn't be much of a one for you.

However, as they say, YMMV.
