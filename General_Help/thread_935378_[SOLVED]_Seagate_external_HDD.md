### Post by nickstephens on 2008-10-17
Unfortunately on a personal level I do have quite a bit of expereince with this particular drive. 

To paint a clear picture however. I am a bit of a computer geek but not nessarily ubuntu and I am in a unfortunate position where a large percentage of the people I work with are fully attached to windows. 

The Seagate drive has worked for me particularly well for just under 1 year but has been reformatted for compatibility with windows (NTFS). Intially the problems highlighted elsewhere in the forums with having to unmount the volume, and in my experience even unmounting the volume before shutting down ubuntu or windows. A fix for this has been suggested elsewhere but I have not tried this yet. Generally I have to use a windows machine to write the information to the drive during unmounting so that the ubuntu system can recognise the drive problem free. Damn unfortunate if you dont have multiple computers/OS lying around  like me! The recent problem involves an apparent corrupted drive. This appears to have been due to an unsuccessful backup of lots of information at once. Not a big problem apart from when entering the corrupted folders on the drive ubuntu freezes completely, needing a return to windows. There appears to be something missing with my ubuntu system (hardy Heron 8.04)that can recognise a drive fault. To be honest this may not be any problem with drive and more to do with trying to do to much with the drive.    

Used in a basic way and without downloading large amounts of data at once, the drive appears to work fine. I can not compare with other drives, only servers. However the suggestion from me would be to keep the drive windows or ubuntu and reduce complications. 

On this note, it would be useful if there is somebody who understands the filesystems better than me, what is the most realiable format to run this drive in to avoid loosing my backups like I have today.
