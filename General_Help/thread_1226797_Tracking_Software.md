### Post by t4thfavor on 2009-07-29
If its on his laptop he will circumvent it. What you do is put it on a router, and then make him think its on his laptop. he will spend all his time tring to figure out how to circumvent it, that he won't have time to surf porn.


I have a 13 y/o (non techie) nephew who found an exploit to get root privs on his macbook, so that he could disable the OSX parental controls. 

And he was successful.

Even if you do put it on a router, he will still sooner than later get around it. It's just impossible to stop.

Some router apps that do what you ask are 

Untangle (requires a spare PC but does alot more than just content filtering)
dansguardian (runs on any Linux PC sits between your WAN, and the router, or on the router if it's a PC).

If you need anymore help, let us know. I have set up quite a few content filters for churches, and schools, so I am fairly versed.
