### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-05-13
Don't mean to be discouraging, but EACH of the areas you mentioned easily has enough of both breadth and depth to be its own specialty.

My advice (having just retired from over 40 years in Computer Systems Engineering) is to do the following;
1) read up on each of the specialties to develop an idea of what working in each would be like
2) pick one to be your primary specialty
3) pick a second one that is very different from the first
4) keep an eye on employment/advancement trends in the two specialties and be prepared to switch if one is not working out.

One thing you will learn (as I can attest) is that ANY specialty related to computer engineering is going to require nearly constant relearning to keep up with the trends and the changes. 

So, if you don't like learning new stuff all the time, computer engineering is not a good career field.
