### Post by Usufruct on 2008-12-26
For the awesome Conky-ness, check out [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=6365702").

A few of those screenshots had a dock that looked a lot like AWN.  You can install AWN (avant window navigator) through Synaptic.  Make it look awesome by switching the themes, I suggest simply googling AWN themes, there are tons of them.  

A lot of the rest of those customization screenshots are using a combination of screenlets (#1 is using NowPlaying and Clear Calendar), themes, and transparency.  Sexy firefox themes help too.  

One quick thing that's easy to do and looks great (to me) is to right click on the gnome panel, in your case at the top of the screen, select properties, and adjust the opacity (and color) under the background tab.  Mess around with it :)

Installing different themes (gnome-look.org, art.gnome.org), configuring Conky, using AWN (or Cairo dock), and even poking around in screenlets is going to give you some serious freedom to make your desktop look however you like.

Cheers!

### Post by thraxy on 2008-12-26
The problem is that you've taken the bottom menu bar and moved it to the top. With two menu bars at the top, of course it's gonna look bulky. What you want to do is get rid of one menu bar.

If you right-click on the top menu bar, you can chose "Add to panel". This will allow you to add all sorts of things to that panel, including things that currently are on the bottom panel. After you're satisfied with the look of of the top menu bar, simply right-click on the bottom menu bar and delete it.

Then you can go from the normal two panel look: [http://thraxy.com/images/desktop/haha2.png](http://thraxy.com/images/desktop/haha2.png)

To the more slick one panel look: [http://thraxy.com/images/desktop/singlemenubar.png](http://thraxy.com/images/desktop/singlemenubar.png)
