### Post by seatea on 2006-10-11
Everytime I restart or bootup dapper, the  volume is set to mute. I can uncheck it through the  applet in the gnome-panel or open the volume control and turn mute off, but the  next time I start up, the volume  is muted again.I don't see an option  to control this behavior in the  sound preference. Is there some way can I reset this so it doesn't keep happening?

---

### Post by seatea on 2006-10-11
After messing with this for a couple of days, I have resolved my problem. In the volume control window under Edit > Preferences there  is a series of boxes with options to display in the volume control panel. Auot-mute is  one of the options. It was not checked on my install. I checked it, then went to switches in the volume control window and can now see that Auto-mute has a check next to it. I unchecked Auto-mute, which was checked by default. I restarted and the volume was not muted.This is what is most aggravating about Linux. It takes hours of hunting to find a simple solution. At least there was a solution though.
