### Post by DuckHook on 2018-01-10
Welcome to the forums, etudeness.

This question crops up often on the forums, and those of us with old equipment usually advise the installation of a lightweight flavour instead of Ubuntu. In your case, with your specs, I would definitely avoid any of Ubuntu, Ubuntu-Gnome or Kubuntu. All three are resource hogs, at least for your specs.

Better flavours would be Lubuntu, Xubuntu, Ubuntu-Mate or Ubuntu-Budgie. These are all designed to be light OSes built for lower spec'ed machines.

It bears noting that the most resource demanding processes these days are not, in fact, the OS, but modern browsers. They are almost nested operating systems in themselves, with audio visual codecs, code-running routines and major security risks. It doesn't look like you will be running memory hogs like GIMP or CAD, but the browser may be enough to slow you down. You may wish to consider a less resource-hungry browser like Midori as an optional app to Firefox (which is the default browser in all of the flavours). Your mother would then have both and can choose which one she prefers.
