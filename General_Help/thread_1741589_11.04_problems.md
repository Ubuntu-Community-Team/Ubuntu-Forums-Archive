I hate to quote something so long, but this was an amazingly useful tool in configuring successful DUAL monitor setup. However, I did want to add a couple of extra things. 

-**It is not necessary to modify the 'xorg.conf' file.** At least not directly. The key part of this post is that it is necessary (until further notice) to swap the monitor inputs. As Jan said, it's just a little backwards.

-**Do not enable Xinema. **I tried just about everything I could, but Xinema would only result in black screens on boot. 

-**If you have monitors of different vertical proportions, you will have a black, unusable portion on one of your screens. **I'm using 1680x1050 and 1280x1050, so it's a small part of my screen, and does not bother me. This can be resolved by reducing the resolution of your "secondary" (what you will use as primary) monitor.

-**The vertical part of your workspace switcher will be upside down. **The two screens on top represent the two on bottom and vise-versa.

That being said, have your "secondary" (the one you will be using as primary) positioned as the 'Absolute +0+0'. Your "primary" (one used as secondary) selected as primary display for X screen. Set the position to 'Absolute +*WIDTH OF OTHER MONITOR*+0'. 

Apply to make sure it pleases you, tweak as necessary, and save to your X config file. Just remember: [COLOR=DarkOrchid]do not use Xinema, mark the monitors as TwinView, there isn't a need to open up the xorg.conf if you aren't comfortable doing so. Should you, just be sure to keep a backup. [/COLOR]


Best of luck to you guys. I hope this helps you as much as Jan's post helped me. :P
