from linotype:

> [Windows TrueType supports the standard western European character set. It is
an old font format, but still works with current Windows operating systems,
although its quality of visualization is not very good.

OpenType is the standard font format for Windows 2000/XP/Vista and MacOS 10.
There are three variations:

OpenType Std (Standard) always contains the standard western European character set. These fonts are PostScript-flavoured (ending in .otf).

OpenType Pro (Professional) always contains central European characters in addition to the western European characters, it might also contain design-related characters like small caps, old style figures, etc. These fonts are PostScript-flavoured (ending in .otf).

OpenType Com (Commercial) always contains western and central European characters, including Turkish, as well as along list of extra characters. This, for example, is Neue Helvetica 55 Roman
