### Post by williejones on 2011-05-03
If you decide to stay with Long Term Support only, go to:
System--Administration--Software Sources
Click the Updates tab, look at the bottom at Release upgrade and select
Long term support only.

Both Update Manager and Synaptic Manager use this for updating.

### Post by MARP1961 on 2011-08-15
With Linux (and Ubuntu) there are always choices! You do not have to use the default settings. In the next version of Ubuntu (11.10) you will be able to install Gnome Shell through Software Centre or Synaptic Package Manager. This will give you a choice at login of using Unity or Gnome. The new Gnome desktop will also be a huge change from what people are used to:[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ALCUPp-5UwE]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ALCUPp-5UwE")

If neither of these appeals to you then keep Gnome 2 on Lucid Lynx until 2013. Eventually you will lose Gnome 2 but there are always choices. Gnome 3 has a 'Fallback Mode' that can be made very similar to Gnome 2 for the traditionalists. Other desktop environments exist: You can try KDE, XFCE etc. etc. Just Google them all, use You Tube, watch the reviews and experiment. That's the fun of Linux.
