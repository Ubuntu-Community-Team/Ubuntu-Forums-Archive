### Post by Morientes on 2007-05-05
When I am playing a movie from an NTFS drive that runs via ntfs-3g, the disk light is blinking during the entire movie. Is this because it accesses the disk the entire time? Isn't there a way to set some sort of buffer size, so that it loads it into RAM? The movie runs fine, no stuttering, but the light is a little annoying to look at! :-)

---

### Post by Theimon on 2007-05-05
When watching a movie from the harddisk, there always will be some disk activity. Data-read, caching, nothing unusual. Setting a bigger buffer may help but not much. Just put something in front of the blinking LED and you're home free.

### Post by OniAnubis on 2007-05-05
You could cover it with tape? 
No really the disk light coming on is normal. The system should be buffering the video, that's why there's no skipping, but the buffer has to constantly refill with new data, that's why the light stays on.
