### Post by detroit/zero on 2009-03-08
I'll tell you from experience: All the VOIP clients I've seen for Linux are crap - Skype is far and away the best one available. But you're never going to find anything that matches the look, feel, functionality, or options available in Skype for Windows.

Ekiga is, from my experience, a nightmare to use. Even when it's configured properly on both ends, the sound and video quality is spotty at best - when you can even stay connected.

I use Ubuntu exclusively for day-to-day computing - from internet, to email, to networking at work, for side projects.. I even do my best to convert people as I go. I have two fellow engineers at work I've coverted to Linux in the last couple months. Ubuntu fits the bill for me and keeps me very happy with almost every aspect of my computing needs, except this one.

As an American living abroad and trying to keep in touch with my friends and family, I firmly recommend the Windows version of Skype. (Related thread here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1037172](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1037172))
