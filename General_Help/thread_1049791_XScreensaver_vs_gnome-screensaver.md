### Post by Sam Lars on 2009-01-25
Here's how I understand it:
XScreensaver used to be the default used, but then Gnome made its own screensaver manager.
The "modular" part refers to the fact that it can be used in place of Gnome's screensaver manager.  Either one can use all screensavers installed.
So, there's no difference in screensavers offered, since you can get to them all either way.
I think that Xscreensaver offered more options, whereas Gnome's offers approximately none.
Go ahead and try both.  Gnome's is in the menu, and if Xscreensaver doesn't show up, just use the command.
