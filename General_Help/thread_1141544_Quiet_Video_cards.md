### Post by CatKiller on 2009-04-28
If you're feeling brave, and don't mind voiding the warranty, you can generally remove the heatsink fan that's on there already and replace it with any number of after-market cooling solutions. There is quite a community around that sort of thing, so I'm sure you'll find recommendations and instructions if you hunt around. I understand that a large heatsink and a large-but-slow-moving fan is the generally recommended setup.
