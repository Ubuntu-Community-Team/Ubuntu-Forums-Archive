### Post by 2F4U on 2012-05-20
You may need to go into the BIOS once to let it know about the new RAM size (I have one laptop that requires that, others don't), but if your laptop requires that, it will let you know by saying something about a RAM size mismatch.
Otherwise it is always a good idea to verify the new RAM by running memtest, for example, from a liveCD. I always do that right after installing the new RAM, since it is better to know right away that it is not working than recognizing this during important work when the machine crashes.
