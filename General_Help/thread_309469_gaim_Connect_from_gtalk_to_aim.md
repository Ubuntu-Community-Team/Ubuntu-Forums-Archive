### Post by nekr0z on 2006-11-30
Well, you *would* be able to connect to other networks using the single account, if you weren't using Gaim.

The thing is, there are programs called *services* running on different jabber servers all around the world. Some of these are actually gates to other networks, such as ICQ or MSN, so you basically just have to tell your jabber server that you want to use service X on server Y in your account. If service X is a gate (it can be chatroom or weather forecast or anything at all), you'll have some questions from the service and then your roster (jabber name for contact list) just gets additional contacts from that network you are now gated to. This is how it works and this is what I love about jabber.

Now here comes the problem: Gaim has no option (or at least I couldn't find it, and anyone I know couldn't find it as well) to discover services and connect to them! You can connect to a jabber server and use your jabber account, but you are unable to add any service to it. This is why personally I have dumped Gaim for some other properly working jabber client. To my opinion, the one called psi is the best, you may find some other client more comfortable for you... But Gaim surely isn't worth running.
