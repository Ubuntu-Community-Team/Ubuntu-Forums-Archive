### Post by PmDematagoda on 2008-04-12
Your laptop can easily support Ubuntu/Kubuntu especially if it could run Vista;).

Now about your programs, therein lies the problem now here are the applications:-

Learn to speak Spanish:- No Linux version, so you may have to use Wine or look for a Linux alternative.

MediaMonkey:- No Linux version but there are good alternatives but unfortunately there may not be one which has all of MediaMonkey's features under one roof, some applications you can look at are:-

Audio Players(In order of quality in my opinion):-
Amarok
Rhythmbox
Exaile

Digital DJ software:-
Mixxx

Burning software:-
k3b
Brasero
Gnome baker

CD/DVD ripper:-
dvd::rip

Audio editing:-
Audacity

All of the programs mentioned above are free and open-source, so they are in the repositories as well for easy installation.

Filesharing does work but that involves using Samba, which is something I am not so good at so you would be better off asking that in the Networking and Wireless section. And Kubuntu is stable enough for many people, so you shouldn't have any problems with it.
