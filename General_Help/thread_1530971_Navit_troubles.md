### Post by Herman on 2010-07-14
I haven't tried navit yet.
I hope you can get navit working, but there shouldn't be any problems  with using Tango GPS.
I'm in Australia and it's generally hard to get maps for free here.
For me, Tango GPS is working fine and it does collect maps, but it takes time to collect all of the tiles for an area, for each of the various zoom levels. Patience and persistence are the key strategies.
Tango GPS creates a folder for each zoom level, and collects tiles when  it's connected to the internet if you pan around in the area you need at  various zoom levels. Maybe in some localities it's faster, but where I live it takes a little time and effort to collect the tiles. Once the maps are collected though, they remain and can be always readily used while off-line.
Take a look through your settings again, maybe you missed the checkbox for enabling the map downloading.

I use qgis, (Quantum GIS) for working with my larger and more detailed maps, and in my opinion at least, those two programs compliment each other very nicely. 

If you get navit working, let me know what it's like, (if it's any better). :)
