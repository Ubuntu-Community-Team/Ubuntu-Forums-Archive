### Post by anlag on 2011-06-16
I'm running Lubuntu 11.04 on a netbook, having done a clean reinstall after previously using eeebuntu. By default the desktop appearance appears to be that of the netbook remix, which I don't recall installing - though it was a couple of months ago so I can't swear I didn't pick some option to that effect in the installation process. It looks like this:

[http://www.thevarguy.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/2010-05-17-031058_1024x600_scrot.png](http://www.thevarguy.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/2010-05-17-031058_1024x600_scrot.png)

Now, as I gather this isn't the normal default appearance of Lubuntu/LXDE, so I'm not sure why I get it but my /home partition is intact since I was using eeebuntu, and indeed with a netbook remix interface, so I would say most likely this is the explanation. It is (somewhat strongly) supported by the fact that my NBR favourites menu contains a couple of applications I used under eeebuntu but have not installed on Lubuntu.

Either way, what I would like to be able to do is either one or ideally both of two things: 

* configure the NBR interface; for instance removing, adding or changing icons. I think it was enough to right click on things in eeebuntu. That does nothing here.
* remove the NBR stuff altogether, and simply use the normal LXDE desktop.

I've spent quite a while googling for solutions to this but at this point I'm stumped. Very grateful for any help!


Edit: Was installing things and all of a sudden when I minimized my windows I was looking at a normal, NBR free, desktop background. Upon a reboot, I found that the desktop manager selector was on "Lubuntu Netbook", which had previously been on "Default" causing me not to have a look at it. Changing that to simply "Lubuntu" worked fine. That's alright then.
