### Post by peabody on 2007-03-17
The software used on Linux distributions for scanners is called SANE.  I believe the hardware compatibility list is here:

[http://www.sane-project.org/sane-supported-devices.html](http://www.sane-project.org/sane-supported-devices.html)

Could be wrong though.

Doing a search for your scanner seems to indicate that it has "basic" support (translation: pretty much works, no vouching for quality).  Don't worry about the comments on Linux 2.4 only, I believe they mean at least 2.4, the kernel in Ubuntu is 2.6.+

### Post by yopnono on 2007-03-17
I have a canon LIDE 60 and it works just fine with linux, but canon don't provide any drivers for linux based system and they don't have any plans to help the linux community with this.

Bottom lines is, that the LIDE 60 works just fine without the help from canon.

Most say that the LIDE60 is a flate (thin) scanner and it's fast, good quality.
