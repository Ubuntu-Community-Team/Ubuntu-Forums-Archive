### Post by S. Cornelissen on 2010-06-01
Dear Ubuntu enthusiasts,

I have bought a Xerox Phaser 6140 V/DN laser printer. I have successfully installed it and it works fine in general. I have come across a problem though that I was not able to solve myself. Any ideas?

[IMPORTANT EDIT: I had a problem printing on envelopes from Open Office, see below. I discovered a workaround by exporting to Pdf from Open Office and then printing from Acrobat Reader. The key is that when I print from Acro Reader and click Properties, I **do** get an entry Media size, where I can select DL envelope. Then it works fine. Thus my new question is: can I use this option from Open Office in some way as well? This would save me the workaround.]

**Printing on DL envelopes (or any non-standard size for that matter)**
I want to print on DL envelopes (11x22 cm) by introducing them to the manual feedslot of the printer. The printer takes them in fine and prints them. However the printed stuff is in the wrong position. I have the feeling that this has to do with the fact that the printer is not aware that it is printing on DL envelopes instead of the standard paper size. I have checked the Xerox User Guide and it says I should:

"Select the Phaser 6140 PostScript printer from the list of printers and then click the Properties (or Preferences) button. In the Paper/Output tab, do the following:
a. From the Paper Type list, select Envelope.
b. From the Paper Size list, select the envelope size.
c. From the Paper Tray list, select the tray you are using."

Unfortunately, when I print from Open Office, the Properties tab does not have these options. When I click Properties and then Paper, I can only choose the paper sizes A4, Legal, Letter and US Folio. 

I know the printer should be able to handle DL envelope size from a hardware perspective. Any ideas?
