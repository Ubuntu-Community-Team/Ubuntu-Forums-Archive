### Post by dmfarley on 2011-01-03
i don't think that's very accurate.

first of all, the thermal properties can vary widely from machine to machine. my 1202N, for instance, most certainly does NOT leech most heat through the keyboard.

i *always* use "blank screen" for my closed lid option. simply because if you want to move your 'top the most mechanically sound config to do that in is with a closed lid. 

if my machine is on and closed and will be for a while, i just leave it upside down.

and to clear up the vague definitions of "hibernate" and "suspend": hibernate uses no power, and saves a snapshot of your machine's state. suspend continues on ultra low power. sleep... well that seems to vary from service to service and i still can't figure out what ubuntu means by it since my 1202N turns itself off after 10 minutes on battery when in the settings i've told it to go to sleep...  :-\
