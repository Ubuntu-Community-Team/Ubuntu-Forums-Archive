### Post by Frogs Hair on 2013-07-17
There are no supported repositories for 9.10 . A clean installation of 12.04 would be the best plan and if you have old hardware consider Lubuntu or Xubuntu. There is no grantee that a current version of chromium would even work on 9.10 . You could add the old releases source list as described at the link, but they offer nothing new and are intended for EOL upgrades. 
 [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EOLUpgrades](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EOLUpgrades)

### Post by gleedadswell on 2013-07-17
Yup, Frogs Hair is correct.  It is a bad idea to run an old unsupported OS (whether it is linux, windoze, or anything else).  As long as you do you will constantly find it difficult to install anything new or reinstall anything old.

I second Frogs Hair's suggestion that a new version of Lubuntu or Xubuntu is your best bet.
