### Post by VeeDubb on 2009-01-27
I see that the thread really turned out to be more about finding and installing the right theme, I thought I'd take a crack at answering the question that was posed in the subject.  i.e. "Why all gtk themes are gray or black"

To state the obvious, the people designing the graphics are graphic designers.  

Graphic designers (or at least the smart ones) put a lot of time and energy into doing things like color calibrating their work spaces, profiling their monitors, etc.

The reason, is that if your monitor has a green tint to it, and you make the graphics you are designing look just right on your monitor, they are going to look red on everybody else's monitor.

Since they go through so much trouble to make sure their monitors don't have any color shift, they are also going to set up their desktops so that there is lots of white, grey and black, because if you make a bright pink theme, and try to design graphics in it, all that bright pink is gong to mess up your color perception.
