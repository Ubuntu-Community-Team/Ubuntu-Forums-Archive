### Post by TheFu on 2023-07-01
The easy way is to print the PDF file, find a pen and fill out the form with ink.

A number of different PDF handling programs understand PDF text entry fields.  Try them until one you like works.  Tabbing between the fields should be possible.  When the PDF form was created by the form author, they may not have set the form tabbing in the expected order, so don't be surprised if tabbing jumps to different places than expected. I think the order that each field was created is the default for tab order. That isn't really useful if you aren't the author of the form.

### Post by Paulgirardin on 2023-07-08
I use XournalPP . As well as embedding signature images it allows many other ways to edit a pdf by manipulating layers and then exporting a flattened pdf.
For a true electronic signature , look at Annature /[https://www.annature.com.au/](https://www.annature.com.au/) They offer a pay-as-you-go option of AU$1.75 per envelope (containing multiple documents).
You upload your docs and they email them back to you electronically signed including the certificate. You can get the docs to include multiple signatories.
