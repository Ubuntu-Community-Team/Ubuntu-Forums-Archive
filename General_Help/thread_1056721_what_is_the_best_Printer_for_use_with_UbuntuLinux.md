### Post by blackened on 2009-02-01
Historically, I've always had the best luck with HP printers. All that I've owned have worked perfectly with little to no configuration. Granted YMMV with the all-in-one models, scanning and fax will require more effort to get working properly.

### Post by lensman3 on 2009-02-01
Any printer that says on the box "postscript".  You will pay a little more but it is worth it.  I now have an HP 3390 printer and use the postscript engine in it.  Postscript drivers are available for M$, Apples, and Linux.

I have recently gone to printers that hook up via Ethernet.   That way no one PC has to be running to print. The interface is to the printer is usually through a browser.  The HP3390 printer is also "sane" compatible and any M$, apple, or Linux can access the scanner features.
