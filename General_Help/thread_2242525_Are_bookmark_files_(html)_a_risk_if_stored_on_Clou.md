### Post by TheFu on 2014-09-02
No more than any HTML link that is stored on a remote system.  Bookmarks may give away more personal information than you like to other people. I wouldn't assume that any cloud storage is secure - ever.

Anytime we connect to any link, there is a risk of getting more than just a webpage.

Firefox has protections against loading local files due to the normal browser security model which wants to compartmentalize websites into their own internet domains. Crackers want to trick the browser into jumping outside their domain (so do advertisers), hence why 3rd party cookies, flash objects, html5 local storage and other methods to set a unique set of identifiers for our machines, programs, browsers, scripting engines, etc. are used.

Almost every page on the web is paid for by advertising and tracking.  The main exceptions are retailers who get paid directly, middle-man apps for retailers, and corporate "good-will" information websites.  Of course, lots of people run blogs paying the costs from their own pockets too.

The old adage still holds - **if you aren't paying for a service, then YOU are the product.**
