### Post by zemega on 2013-05-23
I agree than 13.04 is definitely faster. I'm using 1225B, and 13.04 is definitely faster. 8Gig HD? You mean SSD? You might want to upgrade that capacity, I believe a 128 GB SSD prive is on par with 1TB external HDD in my place. getting a bigger SSD means you can use bigger swap. And put in some more tools. Maybe check up your SSD model number and search the Internet and see if there's 'bad reputation' about it, although I believe Asus would not use bad component (unlike Acer SSD model before). Or upgrade the firmware on the SSD if its available.

Now with 13.04, there is Unity-Tweak-Tool. I use no blur, 0% transperecy/opaque where applicable. Those settings really speed up overall perfomance.

Using zram might help you, after all its a program/tool designed to improve perfomance computer with low RAM. 

I looked up the spec, you can upgrade your RAM, just make sure you get the spec correct, write email to Asus for clarification on the type of RAM you can use. and then max resolution of 600x480 pixel, the launcher is usually 48 pixel, thats already 8% of your screen length. You can use Unity-Tweak-Tool to decrease the size of launcher in 13.04. Now, your BIOS, some says (Internet forum of course), upgrading your BIOS can increase your perfomance, again ask ASUS. You can use SD card for increased storage.

If you can't change your SSD, you can install 13.04 into an SD card instead. Not cheap one though, at least class 6, even better class 10. But only some SD card are suitable for running OS, you need to find one with good 512k/4k read write speed. You can even install Ubuntu on external harddrive or USB thumbdrive. Not much difference in perfomance because of your spec, but at least more space.

Updating BIOS tips. use low size usb drive, 2gb or less. use fat16 partition. [http://vip.asus.com/forum/view.aspx?board_id=20&model=Eee+PC+701SD%2FLinux&id=20090107202109268&page=1&SLanguage=en-us](http://vip.asus.com/forum/view.aspx?board_id=20&model=Eee+PC+701SD%2FLinux&id=20090107202109268&page=1&SLanguage=en-us) .

Edit: that SSD might have been choosen by ASUS to works wonder with SHE (Windows). So overall sugestion is, update your BIOS, upgrade your SSD and RAM. After that, you can try 13.04 instead of 12.04.
