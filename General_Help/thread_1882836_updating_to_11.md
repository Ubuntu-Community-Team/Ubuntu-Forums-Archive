### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-11-18
Interesting way of asking ... and I'm tempted to respond with -- NO, beyond a shadow of a doubt, there is no way ...

But the truth is, something can always happen.

Personally, I would advise against doing version updates to Wubi installs.  While it may go OK, there is a history of such updates failing in the past, and when they did, they left Ubuntu in an unusable state.  Wubi is intended for short-term, trial use.  If you're going to use Ubuntu long enough to need to do version upgrades, you need to seriously consider a traditional dual-boot install.

Yeah as Mark Phelps said - there's no way, but noone would say that. I've run a wubi upgrade to 11.10 and it went smoothly.

But before you do it, backup your \ubuntu\disks\ folder. For some people the upgrade does fail - whether for hardware reasons or due to their specific setup, or just running out of space - and if you don't back it up you could end up with an unbootable install. Then if it fails you can just restore to the old version.
