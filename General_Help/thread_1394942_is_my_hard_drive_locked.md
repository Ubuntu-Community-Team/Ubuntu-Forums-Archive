### Post by blueshiftoverwatch on 2010-01-31
Did you make sure that the jumper on the back of the HD is set correctly and that your BIOS is configured to accept it? On modern BIOS's you can set the HD's to be master or slave without setting jumpers. But from what I've heard your better off setting the jumper manually to master/slave and just selecting "automatic" under the HD's BIOS settings. Supposedly a manually set jumper will work 100% of the time wheras setting it to master/slave in the BIOS only works 80% of the time.
