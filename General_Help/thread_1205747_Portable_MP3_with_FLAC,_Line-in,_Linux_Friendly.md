### Post by Bucky Ball on 2009-07-20
Reporting that I bought the Cowon iAudio 7, plugged it in and up it popped as an icon on the desktop 'iAudio 7' (*not* '8Gb Media'!) and it is great. We are on holiday at the moment so it has been getting the real test with a lot of driving (plugged into the car stereo) and I highly recommend. Very happy with it. Sounds fantastic on my Sennheiser HD455 headphones and even better on the HD555's (I think they are, haven't got them with me).

My advice? Decide exactly what you want the little bugger to do then find one that does it and buy that. Audio quality, FLAC, Line-in and Ubuntu-ness was what I was after and I got 'em all. Cost a little more than what I was going to pay, but couldn't be happier with it ... :)

ps: They say 50 hours; I would say we got less than this, probably about thirty. I may not have charged it fully first time, but either way, it is certainly a power pack (lasted over a week including around 12 hours of driving).

pps: If you purchase one of these make sure you are running at least the 1.18 firmware. I think it came with 1.15 and when I plugged it in after it was pretty random. Once I loaded the firmware, sweet.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2009-07-20
haha, I have just spent some time getting one to work under Ubuntu for my in-laws while I am visiting them. It is the Sansa E260 and yes, they are readily available here. The sound is not in the same ballpark but a cool little machine for the price, definitely. :)

* One point about the Sansa: if you are having trouble dumping files onto it, *it doesn't take long filenames!* Oddly, I thought of it straight away but many might not. Error was 'Filetype error' or something like that. Shortened the filename and no problem. You could use 'Bulk Rename' for this job (in some instances it is excellent, others not, all depending how the files are named in the first place.
