* Ok, so it's not fine, I have videocard hardware problems or something weird, but that shouldn't be an issue, right?

Boot with the working kernel, go into Synaptic and select "reinstall" for all the new kernel files - and make sure that you also upgrade any special hardware drivers (like video drivers) that may be dependant on the kernel version.

BTW, "386" kernels are for old (ancient) hardware, you should probably use the 686 version at least (or a "lowlatency" version is even better).
