### Post by CrispyFried on 2007-05-27
modern IDE drives manage their bad sectors internally and should never present them to the OS. if they do, it means the drives internal spare sectors have been used (and it should have more than enough) and your drive is about to drop dead. 

that being said, i dont know of any way for ubuntu to find any.. aside from ubunto dropping dead along with the HD. Im an ubuntu noob though.
