### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-03-31
The first icon, the one with the four concentric arcs radiating outward, is the standard icon for the network manager. You will see that icon until the wired connection is established. Then it will change to the two arrows going in opposite directions.

If someone does not have a wired connection, then they will only see the icon of the four concentric arcs. When you are not connected then a red exclamation mark will appear over this icon. It does not matter if the failure to connect is wired or wireless, the same icon is used.

Do you have a wireless adapter? Or only wired ethernet? I have both and I get a connection with both. The wired connection usually connects first and then the wireless connection connects. The icon I see is the two arrows.

I would not worry about the icons. Concentrate on the failure to make a wired connection that you say sometimes happens. I know it sounds silly but are you actually disconnected at these times?

Regards.
