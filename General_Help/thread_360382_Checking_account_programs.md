### Post by mcwtlg on 2007-02-15
We (my wife and I) had to figure out what to use as a checkbook program and the search for a solution was long.

First, we both come from Windows backgrounds and had been using a Windows program for years that we liked.  I was getting more and more frustrated with Windows so I moved to Ubuntu (dual booted WinXp and Ubuntu) while my wife stayed on Windows.  When I needed to do our checkbook, I logged on to the Windows partition.  I felt that was a waste so I looked at a lot of different programs that ran on both Windows and Linux.  None were to our liking so I did some research and found that our favorite app, AceMoney Lite, ran in WINE.  Since our bank data resided on a spare HD that was formatted with FAT32, e could access it no matter what OS was booted.  I installed WINE and installed AceMoney, pointed it to the drive with the bank data and presto!  We both have access to our bank data from either OS.

The only minor glitch is that AceMoney will not sort by date in WINE (it is a known bug).  Other than that, it works very well.

AceMoney was one of my favorite freewares from my Windows days.  It is small but does a good job.

JMHO / YMMV.
