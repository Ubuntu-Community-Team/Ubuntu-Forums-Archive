### Post by efflandt on 2011-01-15
Bad sectors are a bad sign that the drive is failing.  A drive normally reserves space to automatically transparently replace bad sectors with good sectors.  So if you actually start seeing bad sectors, that means that the error sites are all used up and the drive can only get worse.

If it gets very bad, you might not even be able to partition and format that area of the drive with gparted.  For example I could only partition and format less than 40 GB of a 60 GB hard drive, because if I tried to format any more, after gparted finished it would say unknown filesystem for a partition it just formatted itself.  So I just formated the begining of it and used that to test different distros on an old PC.

Later I somehow was able to successfully partition and format the rest of it.  But when I ran **sudo e2fsck -cc** on that partition, I gave up after 11 hours.  It is trash.
