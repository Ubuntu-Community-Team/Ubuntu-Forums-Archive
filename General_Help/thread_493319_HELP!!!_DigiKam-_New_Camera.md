### Post by p_quarles on 2007-07-06
I haven't used either of them specifically, but any USB camera should work fine. Personally, I like Kodak. Canon has a nasty habit of using proprietary batteries. 

In any case, even if DigiKam doesn't automatically detect the camera, it will still work fine as a USB flash drive. Digital camera compatability is not really something to worry about on any system.

Any modern system, I should say. It will not work with a Commodore PET. :-)

### Post by timcredible on 2007-07-06
actually, that's not true - not all cameras will mount as usb drives in linux.  my fuji s6000fd won't connect in any mode, and i can't pick pictbridge mode manually (it does that automatically if it sees it's connected to a printer).  however, my fuji Z1 connects just fine, go figure.  anyway, relatives have 4 different models of kodak cameras, and they all connect up to linux fine.

so, what do you do if your camera doesn't connect?  no problem, buy a $5 card reader and connect it to your pc, and take your memory card out of the camera and plug it into the card reader, that will work.  so there's no reason to base your purchase on whether or not it will connect to a linux machine.

also, not sure what you're looking for in a camera, but i got the s6000fd for $250 after rebate - it's 6.3MP, 10.7x lens (a real lens, one that you can add filters to), extremely good for the price.  i was going to bite the bullet and buy a sony alpha for $900 (including the 300mm lens to be equivalent to 10x), but it didn't have a movie mode, so i went with the fuji.
