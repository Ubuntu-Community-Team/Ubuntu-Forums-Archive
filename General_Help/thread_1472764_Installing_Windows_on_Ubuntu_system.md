### Post by jerome1232 on 2010-05-04
1 of two things probably, the disc your using is toast or you just need to set the optical drive as the first boot option in your bios.

The way to set it as the first boot option will vary from computer to computer, when you first turn on the computer there should be a message on the screen, something like del for setup, or f11 to enter setup. usually it's delete, or f12, or f11, or f10.

Anyways you need to mash whatever button it is you need to mash while the computer starts up and you should get into your bios, look around (probably advanced bios options but could be called anything) search for something about "boot order" and configure your optical drive to be the first boot option.

Hopefully that helps.
