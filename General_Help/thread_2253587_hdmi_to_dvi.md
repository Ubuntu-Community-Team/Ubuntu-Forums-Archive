### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-11-21
My Monitor is actually a digital TV and capable of 1920 x 1080 @ 60 Hz but I could not get that using a VGA connection. The TV did not have a DVI connector. So, I used VGA until I was able to purchase a HDMI cable. Then I could get the full range of the screen. Thankfully, my graphic adapter had all three connectors.

VGA is a very old system that was never designed to cope with capabilities of modern digital screens. It worked fine when the monitor was an analogue Cathode Ray Tube (CRT) screen. It converts the digital output of the computer to analogue for the CRT. But to use it with a digital monitor is to require the signal to be converted from analogue back to digital for the screen. It is better to go digital (computer) to digital (screen) with DVI or HDMI if those connectors are available.

### Post by mcduck on 2014-11-22
> **kccv42 said:**
> What do you mean that vga will only display 1920x1080 at very short distances?  Please clarify.

VGA is analog signal and suffers more than digital signals as the cable length increases. Without any additional amplifiers between, the recommended max length for a VGA cable is 30 meters for low resolutions, and only about 7 meters for anything above 1600x1200. However in real life it also depends on if there's any devices nearby that could cause interference, and the quality of your cables.

Anyway, if you have the option of using a digital connection, then that's what you'll want to do. And DVI is definitely good enough, in fact it's better than what HDMI is (for computer use, at east, HDMI is more geared towards home theater use so it's not designed to transmit high resolutions or refresh rates you might have on computer use. DVI and DisplayPort are better suited for that).

...and of course if you don't actually have VGA connector on your computer, then it's even easier, since you can't easily convert HDMI into VGA (using a HDMI-DVI and then DVI-VGA adapters won't do it, since DVI-VGA only works for analog part of DVI standard, and converting the digital signal from HDMI into analog requires additional, and possibly quite pricey,  hardware)
