### Post by vinnn on 2007-03-18
**Amarok **does all you're asking for with regards to music on your iPod and is excellent for organizing large music collections, it even automatically downloads covers, displays lyrics (usually), band info, displays similar bands to the one you're playing (pulled from last.fm), plays streaming radio & last.fm radio, downloads/plays your podcasts via RSS, scobbles your music to last.fm. Keeping my 160+ GB music collection organized is a breeze in Amarok.
For transferring videos to & from your iPod, use **Gtkpod**.
**GPixPod** is a program intended for managing photos on your iPod

**Warcraft 3** has Gold support status in Wine, therefore it runs fine, you'll likely notice that loading times may actually be quicker under Linux/Wine. In fact most of the Warcraft games including WoW runs fine under Wine. [LINK]("http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=897")

You can easily install all the codecs you need in Ubuntu, just open up the 'Add/Remove Programs' and look for the '**GStreamer Extra Plugins**' & the '**GStreamer ffmpeg plugin**'. **VLC** also plays many formats including wmv natively.
To play encrypted DVD's (i.e. most commercial movie DVD's) open up the 'Synaptic' package manager and search for and install **libdvdcss**.
On top of that **MPlayer** & **Xine** are good players for DVD. Although having libdvdcss & the appropriate codecs installed as above should get DVD's working in all players.

**MSN Messenger** is a hideous program, the sooner you stop using that 'Security vulnerability on a stick' the better. **aMSN** will do you fine.

**Songwrite** is a guitar tab program you might want to check out, plays tabs through MIDI, natively supports .sw, .xml & gtab files and is able to import ASCII, Guitar Pro 3 & Guitar Pro 4 files.

**Myspace IM** kinda runs under Wine with some workarounds - [LINK]("http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iVersionId=5007")

There is a Linux version of **Peer Guardian** which uses the Linux kernel's built in firewall/iptables called **moblock**. Download the deb package at [moblock-deb.sourceforge.net]("http://moblock-deb.sourceforge.net/").
On Ubuntu it's just a case of right clicking the deb file and installing.

HTH  :guitar:
