### Post by Yougo on 2014-11-28
Hi,

What graphics card and driver do you use?  depending on the driver you could disable displays while keeping them plugged in. in this case your integrated laptop display.

keyboard backlight, i don't know. is it just always on, or is there a hardware button (or Fn+[key]) that's supposed to toggle it? i'm assuming it once came with Windows. could you toggle the keyboard backlight in Windows? if so, then in theory the button can be mapped to a script that does the toggle. otherwise you could set any key combo, as long as it isn't used for something already.

since you're not using your laptop display and keyboard, you may just set the close lid action to 'do nothing', attach an external keyboard and just close the laptop?
