### Post by papibe on 2011-06-05
I have 2 external USB hard drives:

Western Digital My Book 1 TB: [IMG]http://ak.buy.com/PI/0/45/205761335.jpg[/IMG], and a Iomega 1 TB: [IMG]http://images.deals.woot.com/cc593a35-9e81-44e9-b2b8-a31435091285.jpg[/IMG]

Both of them has power saving settings. After a few minutes without use, the Iomega reduces its spinning speed to save energy. The WD goes even further and actually stops spinning after a while.

Although saving energy is great, it has some cons. You will face almost like a OS freeze sensation when you access a disk that has  entered the saving mode.

For a external data disk is a good trade off, but I think it could generate problems if you boot and run an OS on it. I imagine that an OS can't wait 20-40 seconds to access the swap area in a giving time, for example.

I have to acknowledge that this is just my opinion, I wouldn't do it ... but I don't have hard evidence to prove my point.
