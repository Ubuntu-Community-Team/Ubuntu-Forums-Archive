try downloading and burning the ubuntu cd to disc, then boot from it and 'try without installing' aka Live CD. See if that works.

If not, you likely have an incompatible graphics card or something similar. There are some workarounds listed here: [http://ubuntu-tutorials.com/2010/05/06/ubuntu-10-04-lucid-blank-screen-at-startup-workaround/](http://ubuntu-tutorials.com/2010/05/06/ubuntu-10-04-lucid-blank-screen-at-startup-workaround/)

Note: if you trying to use a workaround when booting from the CD, then look at the bottom of the screen as it boots and when you see a keyboard icon, press any key to get the extended menu.
