### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-26
DSL is 'broadband'- 

> Broadband is often called "**high-speed**" access to the Internet, because it usually has a high rate of data transmission. In general, any connection to the customer of 256 kbit/s (0.256 Mbit/s) or greater is more concisely considered **broadband Internet access**.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadband_Internet_access](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadband_Internet_access)

DSL can have issues (its more variable depending on the other users on your node). It can 'go down'....but so can cable. 

I've been on ADSL for the last few months, and apart from once or twice losing the conenction its been very good. The lost connection was easy fixed by restarting my modem. Its also a lot cheaper, and faster than the cable I was on before- though thats because I am fairly close to the exchange.

### Post by Gemnoc on 2010-05-27
From what I could understand, the problem with DSL is, the actual speed degrades the further away you are to the phone station/central/whatever it's called. It's not a problem for high density urban areas, but in suburbs or rural areas, where stations are farther apart, it can mean that your advertised 5MBits connection will only give you 2MBits or as low as your ISP considers "sufficent". I've seen it happen with family members who are on DSL.

For that reason, I'm sticking to cable (even if it's more expensive around here) until they all convert to fiber optics, thank you very much. I've been on broadband cable for 12 years in two different cities, always had a great service. Of course, some ISP's are worse than others.

A good forum to get info and customer feedback about such things: [http://www.dslreports.com/forums/all](http://www.dslreports.com/forums/all)

Cable internet is running on frequency division multiplexing where many users in your neighborhood share the same connection. More users on the same connection, the further impact on the bit rate. So, cable and DSL have pros and cons.
