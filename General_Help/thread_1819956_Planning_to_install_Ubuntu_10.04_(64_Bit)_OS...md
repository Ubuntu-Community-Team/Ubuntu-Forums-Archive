### Post by mikewhatever on 2011-08-07
As always, run it from the CD/USB to test, but I doubt there will be problems with Ubuntu itself. Some third party programs and plugins may be glitchy (flash, skype), and also some printer/scanner drivers are available as 32bit only, but that's rare.

### Post by flipper T on 2011-08-07
you are very unlikely to notice any difference at all

perhaps a small performance improvement for multi thread enabled apps involved in very intense cpu usage eg video encoding

my advice : stick with what you have until the next time you choose to do a distro upgrade

that said, i dont think 64 bit ubuntu has the incompatibility problems of the past


ps however, i have found that a number of apps that work via WINE in 32 bit, dont under 64 bit
