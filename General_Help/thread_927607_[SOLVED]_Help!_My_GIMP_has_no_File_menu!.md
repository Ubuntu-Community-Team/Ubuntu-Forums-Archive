### Post by Wild Orchid on 2008-09-23
Hi, I tried to delete this post but short of erasing everything (and maybe making someone mad that I erased an entire post! LOL) I couldn't, so I will just leave it intact and also say what I found the problem to be.  Maybe it might help someone else who stupidly does something like I did.  HA  Moral of this short story, when tweaking, know exactly what your newly tweaked conditions will be!!!  ON TO THE STORY.  haha


*"I have used a few versions of Linux in the past and decided I would give Ubuntu a try, I'm using the Hardy Heron.  So far I really like it but I have a problem. *

*GIMP for some reason doesn't have its menu.  I'm attaching a screenshot to show exactly what it looks like after it first starts up.  There is no menu (File, Xtns, etc), no way for me to begin a new picture, etc.  I have tried updating, and then removing, and then reinstalling from apt-get, but nothing seems to work. I love GIMP and use it quite a lot... this is driving me nuts!!  I have googled and also searched through the forums but can't find anyone else having this particular problem.  Also I have tried changing my themes, etc., to make sure that wasn't creating a problem and no matter which one I tried, the effect is always the same, no menus."*

SO, OKAY... this is what happened.  

I had installed the **Global Menu Applet for Gnome**, this is the first time I ever used that... and that is where my menus went.  I just noticed some other missing menus in another program too and I was like.... hmmmmm.... and I remembered my GIMP menu problem started about the same time as that change I made, but I was so involved in my tweaking I didn't really notice until later on that something was majorly different.  I couldn't see the menus all that well on my panel, so I had decided to just remove the GMAG... however, not realizing that #-o ... my menus would go with it.  Eternally. Somehow I didn't make the connection that it had actually MOVED all my menus from one spot to another.  Of course now I feel really dumb.  

So putting the applet back of course restores the menus. \\:D/

I am wondering though, if I uninstall this applet, if the menus will go back to the way they were before, that's the next thing I'll work on, is removing that.  I thought it would be fun to try but I really don't like it as much as I thought I would when I installed it.  :/

Sorry guys, hope I didn't waste anyone's time over this. #-o But on the bright side I learned something and if anyone else ever does the same thing, maybe this will help them! 

KNOW YOUR TWEAKS, especially one that has the word MENU in it! hahaha :lolflag:


**P.S.  YES Uninstalling took care of the problem, it asked if I wanted to delete from the configuration at next boot up, to which I resoundingly said YES PLEASE.  Me and Gimpy are back like we used to be. ;)**  

THE END.  :P
