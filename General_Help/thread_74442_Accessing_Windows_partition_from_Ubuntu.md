### Post by Zelut on 2005-10-11
absolutely.  If you've got a dual-boot system you can 'mount' the windows partition and copy your personal files just like that.  I don't recall the auto-mount command offhand (rarely have to deal with it) but I am sure its on the [www.ubuntuguide.org](www.ubuntuguide.org).  Check that out (just search for the mount) and that should tell you how to do it.

When the drive is mounted you'll be able to browse thru all of your files/folders just as if you were in windows, copy them into your /home folder and then you're set.  

Let me know if you've got any more questions after checking out that site
