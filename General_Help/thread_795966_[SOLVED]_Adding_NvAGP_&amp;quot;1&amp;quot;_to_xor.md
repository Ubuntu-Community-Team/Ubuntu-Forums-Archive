### Post by EXCiD3 on 2008-05-16
I would take a look at [ftp://download.nvidia.com/XFree86/Linux-x86/1.0-8174/README/32bit_html/appendix-f.html](ftp://download.nvidia.com/XFree86/Linux-x86/1.0-8174/README/32bit_html/appendix-f.html)

Looks like the default linux kernel AGPGART is preventing suspend to work correctly. :(

I noticed > On Athlon motherboards with the VIA KX133 or 694X chip set, such as the ASUS K7V motherboard, NVIDIA drivers default to AGP 2x mode to work around insufficient drive strength on one of the signals. You can force AGP 4x by setting NVreg_EnableVia4x to 1. Note that this may cause the system to become unstable.that might be worth a shot.

Some really dated topic on disabling AGPGART that might be helpful [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=20351](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=20351)

### Post by FuturePilot on 2008-05-16
Thank you again! I actually stumbled across this thread
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=410745]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=410745")
And found out that I needed to blacklist the agpgart and intel_agp modules. Now everything is back to normal and I'm using NvAGP 1 in my xorg.conf
```
Status: 	 Enabled
Driver: 	 NVIDIA
AGP Rate: 	 4x
Fast Writes: 	 Disabled
SBA: 		 Disabled
```
