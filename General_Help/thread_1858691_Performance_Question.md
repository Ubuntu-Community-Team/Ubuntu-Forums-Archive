### Post by cryptotheslow on 2011-10-12
Possibly. Really depends on your laptop, the USB connections available and your usage of the system.

e.g. if you are maxing out your 2GB RAM and the system is using swap, then shifting the swap partition / file from internal drive to USB2 thumb would be a bad performance hit. Whereas moving the static OS files to USB2 thumb would slow down initial startup but maybe free internal HD space for swap and bring overall performance up.

Basically we need more info about your use case.
