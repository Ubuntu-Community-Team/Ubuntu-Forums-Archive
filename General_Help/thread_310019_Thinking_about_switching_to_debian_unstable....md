### Post by koenn on 2006-11-30
debian unstable is where debian first introduces new packages. The packages themselves still require testing, as does their relation with the rest of the system. That's why the branche is called "unstable". 
Think twice, and be prepared to have a lot of your questions about how to fix things answered with something along the lines of "this is 'unstable', what did you expect. Use 'stable' if you can't deal with this".

### Post by Marsolin on 2006-11-30
I use Etch on one of my machines, and I get better access to new packages in many cases, but there are also a lot that Ubuntu has that Debian doesn't. If you do decide to use Etch, you'll want to add the Marillat repo ([http://debian-multimedia.org/](http://debian-multimedia.org/)) for multimedia support.

Personally, I find that I prefer Kubuntu these days. Having the latest packages is nice, but there are so many updates with Debian that something will ultimately break and you'll have to spend time correcting it.
