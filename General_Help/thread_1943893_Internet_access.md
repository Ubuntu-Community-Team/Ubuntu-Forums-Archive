### Post by agillator on 2012-03-25
I know of nothing that will do what you want. You might investigate the parental control programs for Linux, although it looks like you may have already. I am well beyond the years of using them so I know nothing in that area. There is the obvious 'if there isn't one, why don't you write one', but for most of us that goes far beyond our skill (or interest) level. The only thing I can think of is to set your firewall to block everything and then give that person a script to open the firewall when the internet is to be used and another to close it when done. However, that opens it for everyone during that period of time and would be trivial to defeat so would really be worthless. You can block every ip on a network except for one, but not every user except for . . . .
