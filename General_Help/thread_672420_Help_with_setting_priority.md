### Post by DennisSullivan on 2008-01-19
I have a USB turntable and I am using Audacity to record off of vinyl.  I am experiencing choppy audio.  The Audacity forum suggest increasing the priority of Audacity (setting the nice value to -10). For me that makes the problem worse.

I get the best performance if I minimize the priority of all active processes especially Audacity.  I used the System Monitor to view the Active Processes then set the priority of each to +19.  (In my case this list is composed of Audacity, compiz.real, gnome-system-monitor, and Xorg).  Now I get almost perfect audio input (still an occasional dropout).

I am using an AMD 64 dual core machine with 2 GBytes of RAM and a 300 GByte SATA hard drive (with DMA turned on) running Gutsy Gibbon.  The USB audio connection shows up as 12 MBps.

Why is my experience with priority the opposite of the recommendation?  How can I fix the last of my audio dropouts?
