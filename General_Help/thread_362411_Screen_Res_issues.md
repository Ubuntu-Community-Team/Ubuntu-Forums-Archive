### Post by punx45 on 2007-02-15
have you tried changing the res. setting in the control panel?  it might be a simple matter of xubuntu assuming low power hardware and defaulting to 800x600.

when i first installed ubuntu it defaulted to 1200xsomething or something like that, and that hurt to look at on a CRT so i scaled down to 1024x768
moral of the story being that just because it boots into a res, doesn't mean its the only option!

bonus points for going straight to the *.conf file to troubleshoot tho!

### Post by veloce on 2007-02-15
Do you have enough video ram to do 1024x768 at 24 bits?  You may have to reduce it to 16 bit.

Someone else with similar hardware had to change their xorg.conf to have the options 1024 by 768 at 16 bit and 800 by 600 at 24 bit.

Search this forum for your graphics driver name.
