### Post by upbeat.linux on 2008-10-27
When you say 3G internet you are referring to an ISP such as AT&T offering internet over their 3G network via a USB device such as AT&T USBConnect Mercury ([http://www.wireless.att.com/businesscenter/USBConnect-Quicksilver/index.jsp](http://www.wireless.att.com/businesscenter/USBConnect-Quicksilver/index.jsp)?

I would first check to see if the device you're looking at has supported drivers in *nix. I would guess that telco in brasil is a bit more realistic in supporting platforms other than windows or mac so you might have good luck getting.

Also, check your coverage area and contact customer support in regards to the number of 3G towers that are available.  I have a couple of clients that use the AT&T 3G cards here in the states who have intermittent coverage problems.  The card will connect to the 2G network for the first 15 minutes and then will connect to the 3G network.  When we first purchased the cards we didn't have too many problems but AT&T's release of the 3G iphone coincided with limited connectivity and bandwidth.  I think AT&T's 3G network has packet prioritization (QoS0 for iphone apps and the overall infrastructure isn't scaling up very well to handle the load.

That aside, I'd be somewhat hesitant of using 3G as your primary means of internet connectivity. It would be interested to see what the availability of 3G network(s) looks like in Brasil.
