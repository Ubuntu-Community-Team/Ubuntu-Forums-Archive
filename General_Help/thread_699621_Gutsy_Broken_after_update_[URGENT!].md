### Post by em3raldxiii on 2008-02-17
Hmm, you have extraneous entries for "monitor", "Screen", and "device", so we are going to have to work through that.  And it sounds like your nVidia driver is not loading correctly, so we may have to reinstall it.  ... now I will take some time to read thru your xorg.conf and let you know what I think.  I may not be able to respond again, though, because I am at work and don't get much time at a computer.

EDIT:  Note at the top of your xorg.conf it recommends you do something like this:

```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg
```This is a good idea because it will re-generate your xorg.conf file "somewhat" more correctly.  However, you should be able to get better results just by editing the file yourself ...

EDIT:  Okay, interesting file.  I think you can probably get alright results by:

1.  BACK UP YOUR xorg.conf file:  sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf.backup01 ... it's a good idea to put the date where the 01 is there ...

2.  Try deleting this section:

```
Section "Device"
    Identifier    "Failsafe Device"
    Boardname    "NVIDIA GeForce FX (generic)"
    Busid        "PCI:1:0:0"
    Driver        "nvidia"
    Screen    0
    Vendorname    "NVIDIA"
    Option        "AddARGBVisuals"    "True"
    Option        "AddARGBGLXVisuals"    "True"
    Option        "NoLogo"    "True"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
    Identifier    "Failsafe Monitor"
    Vendorname    "Generic LCD Display"
    Modelname    "LCD Panel 1280x800"
    Horizsync    31.5-50.0
    Vertrefresh    56.0    -    65.0
  modeline  "800x600@56" 36.0 800 824 896 1024 600 601 603 625 +hsync +vsync
  modeline  "800x600@60" 40.0 800 840 968 1056 600 601 605 628 +hsync +vsync
  modeline  "1280x768@60" 80.14 1280 1344 1480 1680 768 769 772 795 -hsync +vsync
  modeline  "1280x720@60" 74.48 1280 1336 1472 1664 720 721 724 746 -hsync +vsync
  modeline  "1280x800@60" 83.46 1280 1344 1480 1680 800 801 804 828 -hsync +vsync
    Gamma    1.0
EndSection

Section "Screen"
    Identifier    "Default Screen"
    Device        "Failsafe Device"
    Monitor        "Failsafe Monitor"
    Defaultdepth    24
    SubSection "Display"
        Depth    24
        Modes        "1280x800@60"    "1280x720@60"    "1280x768@60"    "800x600@60"    "800x600@56"
    EndSubSection
EndSection
```

3.  In the Section "Device" that remains, change the driver from "nv" to "nvidia"

4.  In the "device", "monitor", and "screen", you should remove the # sign you see there.

Now doing all of this will remove the extra crud that's in there and get you back to something closer to what you want ... however, there will be nothing in the xorg.conf to indicate specifically what your resolution should be.  The modeline supplied by failsafe there is probably not correct for your monitor.  But you might be able to use nvidia-settings after you've rebooted.

Now, if all this makes a big mess after you reboot, get to a terminal and do this:

```
sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf.backup01 /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

Remember to change the ....conf.backup01 part to whatever you used (like with the date)

I tried messing around with some stuff before I had a chance to come back here and read your reply, so can't say whether or not it would've worked. Thanks just the same though :)

For anyone else that this happens to, my solution was this (must have envy installed):

[LIST=1]
[*]inputted "nvidia-xconfig" into my terminal
[*]Ran envy. Allowed it to fix up the xorg while it updated the drivers.
[*]then went through my xorg and literally removed every size entry other than 1280x800
[*]re-enabled restricted drivers. this time they worked, and I was able to switch desktop effects back on.
[/LIST]
