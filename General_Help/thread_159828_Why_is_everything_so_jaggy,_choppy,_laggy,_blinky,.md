### Post by evilghost on 2006-04-13
Looking at your Xorg.conf you are not using the open-source ATI driver nor are you using the closed-source (from ATI) ATI driver.  You're using the vesa driver which explains exactly why you're system is slow; graphic rendering is slowing things up.  While the DMA changes and prelinking will certainly help speed up you're system they're not going to do much for your initial complains; system sluggishness when scrolling/rendering graphics.

Either way, I hope I've pointed you in the right direction.  After installing the ATI drivers be sure to look at enabling DMA as well as prelinking and disabling services that are not necessary.

### Post by evilghost on 2006-04-14
I guess your definition of clarity differs from mine, no where in your original post did you mention the installation of Ubuntu between removal of the ATI card and use of the onboard VGA, only uninstallation of the actual card. ;P

The VESA driver isn't the only driver you can use for Onboard VGA.  We need to determine what chipset/manfacturer you are using for onboard VGA, and the output of lspci will certainly help.

For example, my laptop has onboard VGA, a NVIDIA Go6700.  My wife's has a S3 Trio64, and by using the Mach64 driver I was able to enable direct rendering.  Again, the fact that the card is onboard has little or no bearing whereas the manfacturer and type of the card are what matters.

Increasing the "shared memory" with this onboard VGA card using the Vesa driver is going to do little for you, the amount of available memory is really only going to be able to determine the maximum resolution you are able to run at a given color depth.  With Direct Rendering enabled, that would be another story.

According to Google, it appears the "ASRock K8upgrade-VM800" board has a " Intergrated UniChrome Pro 3D Graphics in VIA K8M800 chipset 
- Shared Memory Max. 64MB (with the pre-condition of at least 256MB system memory)" while the "ASUS P5S800-VM" has a "SIS Real 256E" VGA controller.

It appears S3 makes the "UniChrome Pro 3D Graphics", and further research shows there is a driver compiled for Hoary that also works for Breezy, located at this thread:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=76037](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=76037) which should get you started in the right direction.
