### Post by upbeat.linux on 2009-06-02
What was the original action you bound to F10?  Is this application dependent; meaning does it happen in multiple applications or just one?

First take a look at your default gnome keyboard bindings and find  the entry for F10 if it exists.  Remove it.  Next, launch compizconfig-settings-manager make sure that F10 is bound appropriately.
