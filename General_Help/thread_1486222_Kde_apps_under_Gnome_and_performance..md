### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-05-17
The only real performance hit you will probably see is that the KDE apps take a few seconds longer to open from cold as a lot of kde libs have to be loaded first.

I agree with you about the interface of gnome vs kde, but that's a personal choice we both make.  The kde apps will certainly look strange on a gnome desktop, but does it really matter if they do the job you want?  In the past I have always added k3b to my gnome system as I found brasero too fault ridden, but I think at last that has been sorted and it seems OK now.

Go with it if you want a few kde applications.  You will find they open slowly but you will not see any difference in speed terms to your system once any of them are open, they will just look peculiarly blue and square, if you see what I mean.
