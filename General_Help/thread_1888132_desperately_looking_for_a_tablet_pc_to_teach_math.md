### Post by gman16000 on 2011-11-28
your first and second requirement will make this hard to do. there are lots of "VNC" ubuntu tablet "tricks" out there but very few (if any) successfully run ubuntu natively.

so if you relaxed those two requirements (if you are willing), then you could try xoom with hdmi output and tabnotes ([http://android-apps.com/applications/productivity/tabnotes/](http://android-apps.com/applications/productivity/tabnotes/))

i think that would be pretty slick and xoom tablets are on the cheap these days. also when you plug in hdmi to xoom, it automatically mirrors exactly what you see on the tablet to the 2nd display. 

of course you can stick to your requirements and have to root, flash roms (if available) and worry about compatibility issues. one day i'm convinced that there will be lots of real ubuntu choices for tablets but that day is not here yet.

happy hunting.

### Post by bmullan on 2011-11-29
Take a look at the ExoPC (Canada) and the WeTab (Germany) are identical and are both made by a subsidiary of ASUS.

ExoPC/WeTab come in a couple models with the biggest difference being storage and/or 3G.

Both support WiFi a/b/n

Since these are both powered by an Atom N450 cpu Ubuntu runs fine on them.

There is even a guy in Europe that has customized Ubuntu to include a lot of tablet specific capabilities.   His distro is called YAWOS and although the install is initially in German its relatively easy to change the language ... as it is ubuntu.

[ExoPC website]("http://www.exopc.com/")

There is also a good writeup on the Intel AppUP website that shows you how to setup either of these tablets in a triple OS Boot scenario.

[How to Create a Multi-Boot ExoPC-tablet]("http://appdeveloper.intel.com/en-us/blog/2011/07/07/creating-multi-boot-exopc-tablet") 

Ubuntu 11.10 works fine, as does YAWOS.   I have not tried Windows but since Microsoft also sells this same tablet at their online store I have to assume it works well also.

[YAWOS website - use Google Translate to help read through it]("http://www.yawos.de/")

Note that the developer behind YAWOS is working on an English version also.

I bought mine on Ebay and have been very happy with it so far.
