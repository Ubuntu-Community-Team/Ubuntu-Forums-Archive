### Post by Randy Brinson on 2011-10-30
I, too, could not open a Terminal window with Ctrl+Alt+t or Ctrl+Alt+T despite the "Launch Terminal" keyboard shortcut being set in System settings. After an hour of fruitless Web searching it occurred to me that maybe my Dvorak keyboard layout was a factor. So I tried Ctrl+Alt+y and to my delight, a Terminal window opened. The Dvorak 'Y' key is in the same location as the QWERTY 'T' key. So to fellow Ubuntu users who prefer the Dvorak layout for reasons of efficiency and to lower the risk of RSI, keep in mind that not all parts of Ubuntu are aware of your keyboard layout.
