### Post by viciouslime on 2006-08-18
I've tried quite a lot of programs to do this both free and ridiculously expenive and none of them have been sucessful, even a simple mp3/wav with only one instrument and regular timings requires editing after it has been converted as there are always a few notes that are out. But anything even bordering on complex and it'd be far quicker to make the entire midi from scratch even if you've never made one before and know very little about music theory...seriously. 

I know that's not what you wanted to hear and it's not very helpful, but I really would save yourself the bother. Your efforts would be far better diverted towards learning how to make midis using some sort of composing software.
