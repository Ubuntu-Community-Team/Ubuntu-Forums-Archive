### Post by urlwolf on 2009-08-18
[This thread]("http://www.donationcoder.com/Forums/bb/index.php?topic=18837.0") at donantioncoder (DC) started it all.

I, like many, have been looking for a notetaker for linux (or better yet, multiplatform) and couldn't find what I need.

Here are my requirements:

    [LIST]
[*]Keeps indentation, Main text area must be an outliner in itself (discards: scrivener)
[*]  Table support, basic formatting (bold, headings)
[*]     History (back to previous edit location, like a browser, or ctrl+o in vim)
[*]     Does not need much mousing to start notes (discards: basket, treenotes)
[*]     Search must be useful (all notes, highlight all matches). Discards most apps (!)
[*]     Paste html keeps formatting, url location. Discards most apps (!), none keep the url location but onenote and rightnotes. This should be easy to implement
[*]     Import/export keeping most formatting. Prevents lock-in. (discards: onenote)
[*]     Copy/paste to a word processor keeping most formatting (discards: lyX, although no big deal)
[*]     Deals with images by just pasting them
[*]     Good overview and moving items around
[/LIST]

Your requirements may vary, but you may be in a similar situation.
At DC, we often get together and make a nice donation to a project. Either as a group or individually. The idea here would be to donate to one of the many linux notetakers to get it to a point where it can compete with say onenote but saving to an open format, for example odf. Actually, it doesn't even have to be that great (e.g., no need for OCR or anything fancy).

This joint donation for features is something you can see in several FOSS projects, example: [Lyx]("http://www.lyx.org/Donate")

Would anyone here be interested in such a thing?
Also, do you know if linux keeps the url when one copies something from a browser? One nice thing that onenote does is to keep the url of anything you paste from the web, and attaches it at the bottom. Iphi (see original thread) seems to believe linux doesn't do this by default.

Thanks!
