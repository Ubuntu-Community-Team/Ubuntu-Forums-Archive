I currently use [Opera]("http://opera.com") for mail.  The mail client uses filters that are like (and pre-date) Gmail's labels -- the mail is in the mail store once, and you can see it in multiple "views."  

My problem is that Opera's support for S/MIME and PGP/GPG is non-existent, and its support for client certificates is terrible.  Does anyone use a mail client that works like Opera's M2 mail client, or like Gmail?  I'm on Ubuntu 9.10 with a delightful laptop from [System76]("http://system76.com").

### Post by andrewdied on 2009-12-06
With a lot of tweaking I've found Thunderbird to be close.  "Saved searches" can act like most filters, though not any of the address book ones.  It has an "unread" folder view, where it just shows you folders where there is unread mail.  It shows ALL the mail, though, so I made saved searches for my RSS feads and inbox. 

Enigmail is a great add-on for Thunderbird.  It gives GPG support, and with some good menus and dialog boxes.  So, now I have S/MIME and GPG support, just not with my poor beloved M2.  

What I still really miss is the "g" key (mark read, go to next unread), a "mark read" button in the button bar, and automatically making filters / saved searches / labels for my contacts.  3.0 for Thunderbird is coming out soon, maybe that will help.

(Evolution was a waste of time.  Clunky looking and refreshed slowly.  This was disturbing, since my laptop is not a dog.)
