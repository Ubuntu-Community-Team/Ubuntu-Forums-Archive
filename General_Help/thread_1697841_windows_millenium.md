### Post by seawolf167 on 2011-03-01
Welcome to the forums!

This shouldn't be a problem, though another option to consider is [XUbuntu]("http://www.xubuntu.org/"). Download the LiveCD, insert it into the computer and boot off it, then instead of selecting to "install side-by-side" simply select to "overwrite and use the entire partition".

I suggest making 3 partitions (though you can always lump / & /home together into one big partition and have swap as the only other partition, its up to you)


[LIST=1]
[*]/ - 7GB
[*]/home - 12 GB
[*]swap - 1GB
[/LIST]
Here is the [how-to ]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation")for installing Ubuntu

### Post by Jerry N on 2011-03-01
Any version of Ubuntu will run poorly, if at all, on this machine. I have tried a lot of so called "lite" versions of Linux on machines of this nature and the only ones I have found to be even marginally satisfactory are Puppy Linux and Damn Small Linux.  DSL hasn't been updated or supported for several years and although I think it can still be downloaded.  DSL is also really weird.  

Ignoring the cries of outrage from Linux True Believers, I have found that Windows 2000 with SP4 works best on these old machines and I believe Avast antivirus still supports Win 2000.  I had to support a Win ME machine in a Missions office for a while and came to the conclusion that ME was a pathetic piece of Cr*p.
