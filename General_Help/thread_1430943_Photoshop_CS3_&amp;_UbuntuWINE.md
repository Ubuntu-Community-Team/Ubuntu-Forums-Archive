### Post by prodigy_ on 2010-03-16
CS3 support is rather poor in Wine. Your best options are:
- downgrade to CS2, because it's well-supported;
- upgrade to CS4, because it's more likely to work than CS3.

[u][AppDB - CS2](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=2631)

[AppDB - CS3](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=6584)

[AppDB - CS4](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=14318)

[Script to install CS4 automatically with PlayOnLinux](http://www.playonlinux.com/en/topic-3104-Adobe_Photoshop_CS4.html)[/u]

If you need to rely daily on using CS 3 and Adobe Illustrator, you made a bad decision...

Below is the Wine AppDB link for Illustrator:

[http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=20]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=20")

Sorry, but you have the misfortune of being dependent on two of the few MS Apps that don't work in Wine.  So, if you really need to use these two apps on a regular basis, you're locked into MS.

And, before all the Virtual Box fans flame me for what I just said, installing VB and then installing an MS Windows OS, is STILL being "locked into MS".
