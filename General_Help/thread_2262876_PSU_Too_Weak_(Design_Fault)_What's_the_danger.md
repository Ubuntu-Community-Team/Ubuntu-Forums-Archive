### Post by mastablasta on 2015-01-28
if the PSU is too weak it can manifest in various ways. most common ones would be crashes, random reboots or shut downs of the computer. if you do not experience any of those it means the PSU is adequate.

as for the GPU you need to know how much the GPU card itself uses/needs. I doubt it's really 400 W unless you have some powerful card in a weak PC. so you need to check how much it needs as well as various other components and then you add it all together and the your power usage (approximately). if total power usage is around 250 W or 300W you will be fine with a 350 W PSU.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-01-29
Older Dells had (perhaps still have) a problem with poor-quality power supplies because of poor-quality capacitors.  Over time, the capacitors leak and short causing the power supply to either crap out completely or damage the motherboard with unregulated voltage.  I remember reading an article that this cost Dell $2B in warranty claims.  If you have the original power supply in that machine, then it's time to get a new one.  Try to get one greater than the sum of the power requirements of the components in the machine.  In your case, a 500-watt, 750-watt, or even 1,000-watt PSU may useful.

Of course, having bad capacitors on the motherboard can cause similar issues.  So look at the motherboard carefully for any bulging or leaking capacitors.
