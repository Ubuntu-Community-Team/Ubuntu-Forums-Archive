Just a little tip for those who haven't found out yet:

When searching for the solution of a problem, It sometimes occurs that you find someone else that had the same problem and posted it in experts exchange. It sais "This problem has a solution", but you have to register (and pay) to see it. That can be very frustrating if you really need the solution.

But then I told myself: If google can see the answers, so can I. So I clicked on the "cached" link of the google result. It still sais "All comments and solutions are available to premium service members only"...
but if you scroll down almost to the bottom, you can see the comments and awnsers.

### Post by artanis00 on 2008-10-12
Actually, it's easier than all that.

Experts Exchange got in trouble with Google a while back, because they'd serve up pages containing the solution to Googlebot, but mask that information when a user actually visited the page. Bad. Very bad. IIRC, Google delisted them or did something suitable horrid to them.

Now, the site has two behaviours I can see:
[list=1]
[*] When you browse the site itself, it hides the comments and solutions and prompts you to subscribe.
[*] When you search for a solution and click to the experts-exchange page, it serves you the part you'd see as above, then a **huge** cluster of text, just lists of categories at least 1 printed page's worth long, and then all the comments that were hidden above are now in the clear. Just enter a page from google (or another search engine, I'd assume) and scroll down to the bottom.
[/list]

Just a guess, but I'd say that they now serve the same page they give to the searchbots to visitors that have referrals from search engines. Makes it unlikely for someone to notice the solution is on the page, and satisfies the letter of Google's rules.

You can test this by visiting [http://www.experts-exchange.com](http://www.experts-exchange.com) and looking at a problem. Referral: experts-exchange.com and hidden solution.
Then, search for the topic from google and click the link. Referral: google.com and the solution is in clear text at the very bottom of the page, after a bunch of crud to discourage you.
