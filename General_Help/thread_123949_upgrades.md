### Post by MartinG on 2006-01-31
AFAIK, "official" upgrades are confined to security matters and serious bug-fixing. Feature upgrades are saved till the next release (currently, 6.10 or Dapper Drake).  It's felt that stability during a release's 6-month cycle is the most desirable feature, and those who want to be more bleeding-edge can use the development version (Dapper); if you can't work out how to use the development version you shouldn't be trying and should stick to the current "proper" release :)

This explains why OOo2 and Firefox 1.5 are not in the Breezy repositories, for example.  Some stuff appears in backports, of course, but this seems a bit dependent on the packagers' whims and workloads?
