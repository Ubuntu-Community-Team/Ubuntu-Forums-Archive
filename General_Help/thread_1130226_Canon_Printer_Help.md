Here's what not to do. Don't bother googling your butt off trying to find a solution; many have tried, and all have failed.

Here's what to do, though. Carefully box up your printer and send it  back to whoever sold you it, asking for your money back, or a replacement printer that not only says it works with Linux, but does (HP is a good start). 

Canon claim in their sales blurb that their LBP range operates with GNU/Linux, blithely providing drivers which don't work, and which they neither maintain nor support (don't waste your time there either).  That contravenes EU sale of goods legislation, and the vendor owes you your money back. Don't be shy - demand it, it's your right.
