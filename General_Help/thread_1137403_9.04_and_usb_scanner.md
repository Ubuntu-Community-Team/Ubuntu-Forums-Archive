### Post by Michael Schmidt on 2009-04-25
I just upgraded from 8.10 to 9.04 (64 bit).  All went well and congrats on the nice refinements.  However, now my USB scanner has permission problems.  I went to Menu>Administration>Users and Groups and added scanners to my personal privileges.  No help.  

With 8.10 there were some files in /etc/udev/rules.d but not with 9.04.  Based on my success with 8.10 and some advice from the web, I created a file etc/udev/rules.d/45-libsane-permissions.rules with the following two lines:

SUBSYSTEM=="usb", ENV{DEVTYPE}=="usb_device", MODE="0666"
SUBSYSTEM=="usb_device",MODE="0666"

Reboot and still can't use the scanner.  I am able to "see" the scanner with 

sane-find-scanner 

and it works with 

sudo scanimage -T

so this appears to be a permissions problem.  Is this a bug in 9.04 or a BKAC (between keyboard and chair) error?

Thanks

### Post by Ruhani04 on 2009-04-26
From what I understand the permission rule files have moved to lib\udev\rules.d in jaunty.
I changed rule 50-udev-default.rules and got my usb scanner working. Before that I only could use it with
"sudo xsane". Hope that works for you.
