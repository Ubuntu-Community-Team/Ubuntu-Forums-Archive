### Post by 3rdalbum on 2005-12-25
[QUOTE=Rowin]Please remember the iMac 333 CD-drive only reads 650 MB CD's. It won't play 700 MB ones.[/QUOTE]

Correction: The iMac's CD drive reads 700 MB CDs, but it dislikes the newer CDRs (mostly because they're all cheap bollocks compared with what they used to be).

I've had success with the Verbatim Vinyl discs and Imation CD-RWs in my iMac's drive. Another good one to look out for is TDK - the blue ones, not the gold ones.

Finally, if you've got some old unburnt CDRs lying around, try burning onto those.

### Post by ninotob on 2005-12-25
Does this imac have firewire?  If so, you could probably boot from that.

I've installed ubuntu on a g3 300 mhz clamshell ibook (no firewire) and g3 466 ibook (w/ firewire).  I know for a fact you can't use the usb port for booting either.  Of course, on both of them, the cds were bad so I did a netboot of the installer.

I described it here (it's rough description):
[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=311246#post311246](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=311246#post311246)

Before you go to too much trouble, how much memory do you have?  These old g3s are pretty sluggish -- even the 466 with 576 mb of ram really crawls under X.  You could make dinner and desert from scratch in the time it takes for openoffice to load.  Abiword isn't too bad though.

Anyway, I'm not sure what the minimum ram is, but if you aren't in the neighborhood of 256mb, I wouldn't even try it.
