### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-06-28
I find that web page difficult to read but then, I do not read Urdu. 

How is it difficult to read? Are the characters (letters) in a style that makes it difficult to read? The font is set by the web page designers. They may have chosen a font that makes it difficult to read. May be you do not have the font installed on your computer and the browser is substituting another font that does not show the characters clearly.

You may need to change the character encoding of the browser. In Firefox that is View>Character encoding. If the website is using Unicode ( UTF-8 ) encoding then Linux browsers will show the characters in a good way. But if the web site is using a Windows font then you may need to set the encoding to Western (Windows 1252). You could try Auto-detect. 

Regards.

P.S. I have looked again at the page and I see that some characters have been replaced by rectangles. That tells me the the font being used on the page is not a Unicode font. (I may be wrong about this). Or it is a special font. I have tried other character encoding settings and they do not solve the matter.

Perhaps you need to contact the publishers of the web site and ask them what font they are using and if they will provide a copy of it for you to install on your system.
