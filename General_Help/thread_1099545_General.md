### Post by kryptikos on 2009-03-18
This is a good little book to have as well. It is free for download in PDF format (use evince or xpdf to open) or you can purchase if you prefer something tangible. :) 

[http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/download2.html]("http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/download2.html")

BTW, Welcome to Linux country! Feel free to ask lots of questions, you'll find most people are friendly and helpful. A few words of advice when starting out: 

* Remember to have fun. Don't let some of the newness or perceived complexities damper your spirits. Linux is very powerful and fun to do things with....so when things seem frustrating don't give up hope just hit the boards.

* When posting issues try to give as much information as possible. If you know where to look in log files (as you learn) post it. Tell people what you are trying to accomplish. In Linux you'll quickly find there is usually 5 different way to accomplish the same thing...which makes it great. Posting more information will better your chances that the thread will get picked up plus gives the opportunity for other people to learn as well. 

* Don't be afraid of the command line. It may take a little while to get comfortable with but it is actually your best tool to learn and use. Think of it as driving a standard transmission. Anyone can drive an automatic (Windows), but those who can drive a standard can operate any system...you are not limited. 

* Linux is very forgiving so don't be afraid to dig around under the hood and see what it is made of and what you can make it do. 

Again, welcome! :)
