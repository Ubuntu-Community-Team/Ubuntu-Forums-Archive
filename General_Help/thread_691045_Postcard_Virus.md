### Post by joeashcraft on 2008-02-23
I just got an email about the postcard virus titled "A TRUE VIRUS WARNING".


My reply was simple.

> 
The same safe computing rules apply, and always will. Don't open attachments from unknown addresses. To really be safe don't open attachments from known addresses unless you're expecting it. You can always pick up the phone to confirm that they sent you an attachment on purpose.
I prevent this by [signing]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_Privacy_Guard") all my attachments as well as my emails. In order for an email to go out with [my signature]("http://keyserver.veridis.com:11371/export?id=-827260914712377789&created=1159515997000"), I must put in a password. This prevents any virus from sending email on my behalf.

Also, since I [don't run Windows]("http://www.whylinuxisbetter.net/items/viruses/index.php?lang=") I couldn't be affected by a Windows virus.

Joe
