### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-10-02
> **CLubout said:**
> 
1.Make the DVD itself copy protected

You'll find that Linux users abhor - or even loath - copy prevention. Not only is there no way to copy-prevent a DVD on Linux, but even if there was I doubt many people would actually tell you how.

You can be comforted, though - DVD copy prevention is so easy to break, and the tools to break it are so widespread and so userfriendly, you might as well not even try to encrypt your DVD.

> 2.Hide the video files in some way so you cant just browse the disk and copy them off.

If you were talking about Linux users here, you could make a VCD. Will play in most/all DVD players, is easily playable on Linux and Windows, and also easily copyable on Windows simply by dragging and dropping the files to your hard disk. However on Linux, there doesn't currently seem to be a way of extracting VCD files. There used to, but it was difficult for newbies and involved a kernel driver that I bet doesn't work anymore. You can still do a straight disc-to-disc copy though.
