Is there any way that you can give yourself permissions in Ubuntu even though you may not be the root?

---

### Post by holiday on 2010-08-20
You can give permissions for only those files that you own. 

But if you are the administrator of your machine, you can have root privilege using sudo.

I prefer using 
$ sudo su

and then giving my user's password. You will then have root privilege and can do whatever you please.

However, unless you know what you're doing, be careful with root privilege. The dangers are not dire as many will claim, but the dangers can be significant. Be careful re-assigning privilege to system files. 

Actually - that you're asking this question suggests that you should seek more specific advice. Which files do you want to re-assign, exactly.
