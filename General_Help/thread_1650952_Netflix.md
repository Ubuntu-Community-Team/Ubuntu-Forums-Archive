### Post by endotherm on 2010-12-22
there has been much discussion on this topic, but it comes down to the Silverlight DRM. the MS drm modules are not open so they are not part of the spec upon which Moonlight (the open Silverlight implementation) can code. Its all Licenses, not technological limitations. Most parties involved in Moonlight do not expect to ever be able to implement the DRM. Thus the best bet is to shame Netflix into moving to a more open platform. 

the roku box ([http://www.roku.com/](http://www.roku.com/)) has been said to have a hardware chip to perform the decoding, but so far as I am aware this chip is not commercially available. 

the most common workaround I've seen is folks will virtualize an XP install (with virtualbox) and run their netflix through it, but this is understandably a far from perfect solution.
