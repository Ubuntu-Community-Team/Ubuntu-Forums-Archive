### Post by issih on 2008-05-29
If you are dragging an application window it will switch workspaces as you get to an edge, just don't let go...

Provided the plugin "Viewport Switcher" is enabled, you can also switch workspaces by focusing on the desktop then flicking the mousewheel.

You could use the little workspace switcher in the panel, just add one if you don't have it already.

finally you have the option of the expo command (enable the expo plugin) and the default command is (windows key) e...that ones kind of pretty.

That about covers it, it doesn't switch workspaces when you move the mouse to an edge all by itself no, but wouldn't that be massively annoying unless you have very precise mouse control and much more self control than me.I am continually clicking all over the place when I'm reading things, I'm a fidget :)

Concerning your question about moving the cursor to the side of the screen to change workspaces, it can (sort of) be done without much trouble. Go into compiz settings manager > rotate cube > bindings, click on the "rotate cube" drop down menu to see the bindings for rotate cube.

The 3rd and 5th option "rotate left" and "rotate right" with little mouse icons next to them give you the option of designating corners/edges of the screen to initiate the option. This'll allow you to, for example, move your cursor to the right of the screen and left click to move one workspace to the right
