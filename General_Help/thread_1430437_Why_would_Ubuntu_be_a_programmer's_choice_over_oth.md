### Post by srs5694 on 2010-03-15
IMHO, the biggest advantage of Ubuntu for programming is that it's very popular. Therefore, if you develop and test software under Ubuntu, you can be sure it'll work on the very popular Ubuntu distribution. If, OTOH, you use something less popular (say, Arch Linux), then it's possible that it'll have problems when compiled and installed in the more popular Ubuntu because of library differences, different configuration file locations or settings, etc.

For your specific situation (and old and slow computer), I'd definitely go with a distribution designed to be less resource-hungry than the main Ubuntu distribution. I believe there's a limited-resource Ubuntu variant, or you could go with Debian, Slackware, or something else that doesn't install everything and the kitchen sink by default.
