### Post by mgmiller on 2010-01-08
I have an external USB/eSATA docking station that I use all the time.  My mobo has eSATA ports on it, so I just needed 2 cables and the plugs to go into an unused slot on the back panel.  

There are no drivers or other setup needed beyond plugging in all the hardware.  The behavior is a little different depending on the version of Ubuntu.  

In 9.04, when you power up the drive it automounts and appears on the desktop as an icon.  

In 9.10, it does not automount.  It appears in Places as a drive name or volume name.  You click on that to mount it.  Otherwise, it works great.  In 9.10 when you click the drive to unmount it, it even spins it down.
