### Post by mrkazoodle on 2010-02-02
About the reinstallation of Ubuntu:

If you can't transfer your files, I'd advise you to make a new partition (search for: Gparted).
Depending on the size of your partitions, you should choose on which one you would like to reinstall the OS of your choice and on which one you'd like to keep your files.
After you reinstalled your OS, you can move your files from one partition to another and use Gparted to remove the old OS's partition and enlarge your new one's partition.

If you are desperately in need of (free) external storage, consider uploading your files to a Gmail account (already 7 Gigabytes of storage!) to get some free space on your hard drive.

Edit: Network Attached Storage devices are the best, everybody should have those (however they often don't work very well with linux) and use them for backups!
