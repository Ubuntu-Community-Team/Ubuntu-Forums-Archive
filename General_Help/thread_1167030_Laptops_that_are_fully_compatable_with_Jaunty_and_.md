### Post by yaroto98 on 2009-05-22
> I have often done this however the live cd has its limitations and for some reason i could never get a usb drive to boot right 

Even when using the tool on the live cd for making a bootable USB?

I have a HP Pavilion (dv2000) everything works just fine.
Intel centrino duo, nvidia graphics card (GeForce 8400M GS), 2gb ram, and i can game fairly well on it. If you're looking for a new laptop that's linux friendly, the biggest thing you should focus on is getting a compatible wireless card. It's the most frustrating thing, having a laptop, but no wireless. Intel cards work pretty well out of the box, broadcom has been notoriously poor in the past, and Atheros is made for linux users in mind. if possible get an atheros card.


depending on what you want to do, you can be picky about the video card too, but you have fewer options in laptops. intel integrated graphics controllers are sub-par. if you get a fairly popular nvidia card you'll be fine.
