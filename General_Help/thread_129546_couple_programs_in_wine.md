### Post by drdnl on 2006-06-13
I recently switched to ubuntu as well and found a decent music player home in Amarok but.... I really missed the organizational functions of mediamonkey and at long last i have found a solution:

I installed VMware, installed XP (any windows will prob. do), shared my music collection (cannot be ntfs) on a read/write partition and mounted that shared folder into windows. Now I can edit and move around music as I please without ever leaving linux.

In case anyone wants to try, this is the howto I followed:

[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183209&highlight=vmware](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183209&highlight=vmware)

and here you can find plenty of tips and tricks.

[http://www.ubuntuguide.org](http://www.ubuntuguide.org)

I tried wine, huge hassle, VMware is much easier and runs very well on my pc (3000+ amd64, 1 gig ram)
