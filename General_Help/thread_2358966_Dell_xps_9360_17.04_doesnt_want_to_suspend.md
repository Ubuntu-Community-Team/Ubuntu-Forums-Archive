### Post by zeratul2 on 2017-11-05
What worked for me was to remove the intel microcode proprietary drivers. API was broken with kernel update and Intel doesn't have an updated package yet.
In Software & Updates, Additional Drivers, choose "Do not use the device" near "Unknown: unknown"
But this was on an XPS 9550 (Ubuntu 17.04 and 17.10)

---

### Post by pshchelo2 on 2017-12-01
I can confirm that the solution from the previous comment works for XPS 13 9360

**HW**: Dell XPS 13 9360, CPU i7-7500U Kabby Lake, BIOS 2.3.1 (latest as of this writing), Ubuntu 17.10, kernel 4.13.0-17-generic
**Initial symptoms**: the sleep was more like a 'freeze' - display down, processes seem to be on pause, but the power button is still on, and if you press your ear against the notebook you still hear some churn inside. Plus similar to above messages in syslog. Quite long return from this state to normal on 'wake' too.
**Solution**:  disable proprietary intel-microcode in 'Software & Updates" -> "Additional Drivers" tab. Now notebook goes to (seemingly) proper sleep on lid close etc.
