Yea - that's a list (I remember one like this a few days ago, too...)

Honestly you'd be better Googling to start, because there are many websites that list just what you are asking - and many opinions.  "Windows Programs in Linux"

Everyone has their opinions...

GIMP is cross platform image editor but a PITA to figure out - but then again so is Photochop...

Open Office does PDF - unfortunately the repos don't have 3.0 yet but it can be done - I did it pretty easily.

WINE is the real only way to do Window programs - you MUST look at the specific application and fine any "tricks" go to the "WINE HQ" and see about that game.

Isn't Limewire just a Napster P2P?  Come on "Everyone" is using Torrents now, for all their legal downloads...  Dunno on that - I burn all my own - but learn to use FLAC - it's MP3 without the artifacts - (Go find an iAudio X5! - they are AWESOME sounding with FLAC!)

Any media player these days does MP3 - and surely you didn't get suckered into any of that MP3 DRM crap.... :(  

Brasero does DVD's nicely, and it's native to Ubuntu.

Programs?  Depends on what you want to do and need - Just look at Synaptic - there's BAZILLIONS out there!
