### Post by ubersolid on 2009-01-20
Yes linux supports music and video files.
By installing these you get almost all multimedia playability:

ubuntu-restricted-extras
gecko-mediaplayer
adobe-flashplugin

These are all available in the ubuntu repositories and installable through synaptic.
The adobe-flashplugin requires that you enable the "partner" repository through "software sources" found in System -> Administration.
I hope this answers your question.
