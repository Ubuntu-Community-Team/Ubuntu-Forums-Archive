### Post by crazybill on 2005-05-15
Thanks. I actually figured it out. It was simple. I rebooted and upon reboot, there was a quick message at the bottom to hit ESC which I did. Then there was a menu with one option being recovery mode. I clicked on recovery mode and it booted me in as ROOT. I then made the changes that I needed and all is well.

By the way, as a backup I made a live cd. I did not need it, so I decided to use it on my  laptop that runs Windows. I booted up the computer with CD as first boot and the live CD booted me up in Ubuntu linux. I am submitting this post with the live CD! Cool!
