### Post by QIII on 2012-04-04
Yes, they can heat up that fast, but it would be unusual.  In the old days we used to check to see if a cpu was dead by pulling the HSF off and flipping the machine on just long enough to feel the cpu getting warm.  Doesn't take long.

OK.  Troubleshooting.  Pulling the CMOS battery, putting it back in and getting a nice V8 rumble out of your machine when you start it up makes you want to do a pretty thorough troubleshooting session.

I often start by getting out the manual, opening it to the POST beep codes, then stripping everything not soldered down off the mobo (except the CPU and HSF, but sometimes I even do that.).  Turn it on and you should get the beep code listed as "Mommy!".

Following the order of the list you mobo does its POST in, start plugging stuff back in and turning it on to see what it complains about next.  Some boards don't say much other than "Mommy!" unfortunately.

If everything goes back in, then use the heat sensors as described.

If that goes well, get out your pen flashlight and do a very thorough inspection of your mobo.  Capacitors are a great culprit, although when they go they usually go once and for good.  But they may hold enough charge from time to time to get things to start, depending where they are.  Look for swollen caps and anything that looks burned.

And sometimes its the slap yourself in the face things like a sata cable that won't stay connected well.
