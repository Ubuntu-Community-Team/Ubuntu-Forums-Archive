### Post by polybius on 2010-05-21
Several months ago I asked for help in this forum with troubles with Ubuntu 8.04 and a 92-year old grandmother.  That thread is kind of old so I'm starting a new one, but here's the link:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1407896&highlight=polybius](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1407896&highlight=polybius)

First off, thanks to everyone for their ideas and suggestions.  I finally solved the problem, so I'm posting again to document what I found out.

Basically she complained of the screen jumping all over the place by itself, and, indeed, although I did not know what she was doing, I could see that she had accidentally deleted half her emails on Thunderbird, and inserted hundreds of blank pages in her Openoffice documents, etc etc.   I suspected she was clicking on things randomly, or hitting keyboard shortcuts by accident, or something else.  I tried every hypothesis I could think of.

Before giving her that computer with that installation of Ubuntu I allowed my wife and family to use it (same computer, same hardware, same installation of same OS) for a couple of months, with no trouble.  I also used that computer myself for a week at the grandmother's house last summer, again with no trouble.  But starting last November, she had many complaints of a computer going berserk.  She wanted to pay some local computer guy to fix it, but certainly the only ones she was going to be able to find where she lives are Windows "experts."  

I live in another state, but I use Ubuntu 9.10 on similar hardware (a somewhat older Dell).  

By March I had reached a state of despair, thinking that I would have to visit her and sit over her shoulder for a day or two to see what she was doing to cause half her emails to get deleted etc.  I wouldn't know how to delete half my emails on Thunderbird if I wanted to, in fact I tried on my own system to see if I could reproduce her problems.

I tried everything.  Finally I thought maybe I should switch her to a new mouse, since the one she was using, a Logitec  model, has a light touch.  I was using the same mouse myself.  So I ordered a vanilla Microsoft mouse and had it shipped to her.  She unplugged the old one and plugged in the new one, and her problems disappeared.

Now the odd thing is that starting just about that same time, my own mouse started acting strangely.  It behaved as if it were at random times generating a whole sequence of random electrical impulses sent to the computer.  The cursor would jump around, random things would get  "clicked" and the corresponding actions take by the software, etc. 

It took me a while to put 2 and 2 together, but I finally realized that my mouse was doing exactly what the grandmother had been complaining about for months!  I could hardly believe it!  So, I unplugged my Logitec mouse and swapped it for another Microsoft mouse, and all is well now with my computer.

I have no idea if this problem would occur under Windows or another OS, as I haven't tried.  But I would like to warn people about this particular brand of mouse and relate my experience. 

The bad mouse is a Logitec RX250 Optical Mouse, Mfr part  #: 910-000184, that I purchased from PC Connection.
