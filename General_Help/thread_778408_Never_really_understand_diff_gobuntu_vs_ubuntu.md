### Post by Zorael on 2008-05-02
Naturally, Ubuntu (and FSF and the whole movement) strives toward getting everything to be free-as-in-freedom, but that'd entail no Flash plugin, no Microsoft fonts, no .avi support, and other "core" apps/plugins that users more or less need. You wouldn't be able to convert a whole lot of Windowlings to linux if they couldn't get the same functionality (as good or better) as they've come to demand from their computer. There's still a bunch of "core" stuff that doesn't even *exist* for linux yet. iTunes, Photoshop, etc etc. So there's still the hurdle of getting corporate support and having them understand there's a serious linux userbase that's here to stay, porting their apps to the platform, before getting them to open source their work.
