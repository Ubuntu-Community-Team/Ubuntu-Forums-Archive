### Post by markr on 2006-05-31
I have used both Gnome and KDE under Dapper (and Breezy) and have to say that KDE is alot more stable than it was in Breezy.  Under breezy I had real problems getting suspend and wireless networking stable enough to actually use.  Now they are both working fine (using KPowersave and KNetwork manager respectively).

I still feel that Gnome is more 'polished' than KDE in Dapper, but that just reflects the comparative work carried out (Most of the developers work with Gnome I believe).  KDE will go from strength to strenth though now that Mr Shuttleworth has publicly commited to upping the ante on KDE - only good things to come I think.

I did have to do some manual tweaking to get KDE to do what Gnome did out of the box (Fn-VolUp/VolDown keys on my laptop for example worked in Gnome, but I had to write a small script to get them working in KDE - no big deal though).

Just wait until KDE 4 arrives......

Mark.
