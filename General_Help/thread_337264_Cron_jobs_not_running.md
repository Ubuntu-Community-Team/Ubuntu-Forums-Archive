### Post by sardion on 2007-01-12
If you do "/bin/sh -c ..." you will get 2 processes... one for the /bin/sh and one for the ... command

The /bin/sh command is *not* an instance of x10 in your example.  It is akin to running x10 in a terminal without putting and & after.

That said, you do have lots of them running.  Post your crontab.
