### Post by thetragedyofhumor on 2006-03-25
the bigest problem with this, and looks like any linux system, is the lack of comunication. im not saying that there is no comunication, but its not by any means what it could be. no one should have to wait on somone elses reply to a thread to get help with somthing. and no one should have to weed through thirty-three pages of one thread before someone says 'hey, this particular method only works on older machines' (wich is somthing that i have had happen before)

linux claims to be so comunity based, and Ubuntu in particular, but i say that we need a central knowledge base. (micro$oft sort of has one, but its not very helpful). We, as in all linux users, need someplace that keeps an open library of user-tested 'for this distro, with this flavor, having this problem, follow these steps' style articles. somthing that would sit down and say, step one is 'this', wich acomplishes 'that'. (one of the bigest problems i have had is that, when talking to a total noob, no one explains what is actualy acomplished by exicuting a specific action)

a perfect example being this thread: [HOW TO: Configure wireless cards with Broadcom chipsets]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=25683")
now, dont take this as a personal attack, but more an example that i have encountered personaly. i mean, dont get me wrong, its a beautifuly writen how-to, and im sure it works just fine, minus the fact that half of that _goes over my head_, and chasing after elaboration is not a productive use of time.

what i segjust is a fully involved how-to database, writen by people like jonny there, who know what their doing, formated kind of like this:

> 
Step One: Open Terminal
-The terminal is the linux command prompt
--Click _Here_ for terminal help
Step Two: ```
sudo rmmod ndiswrapper
```
-Sudo stands for 'superuser do' and allows you to do 'this'
--Click _Here_ for Sudo help
-rmmod stands for 'remove modification' does 'this'
--Click _here_ for rmmod help
-ndiswrapper stands for 'this' and is 'this'
--Click _Here_ for ndiswrapper help

With each 'help' link being a list of links to pages like that, going into how to install or configure each element. This style of 'step, explination, help, step' is very easy to understand, and if moderated like [wikipedia]("http://wikipedia.org"), could be very very powerful. In wikipedia, everyone contributes what they can, and if someone adds information that isnt completely right/is flat out wrong, there is a comunal outcry, and the misinformation is corrected.

Now, i may just be totaly inept at using a Linux machine (i wouldnt be suprised), but i have had more trouble with linux than i ever have with window$ (though, o god it pains me to say that). The reason i stick with it is because of the GREAT potential that a comunity based os has. I called Dell recently for suport for my laptop, and was told that i couldnt talk to a technition because my 'service contract' had run out, and i needed to buy a new one. I ask you, _since when is there a price to knowlege?_ And i answer you, when it is realised that knowlege is power, and when greedy men think that they can feed off of peoples thrist for knowlege. Well, i agree that knowlege is power - the power to make our machines our own - but, my friends, brothers, sisters, it is ours, and can be free, if only we can learn to centralise.

Though i call none of you by name, as there is no blame in this situation, and even action is to your descretion, i do call every one of you to ask yourselfs 'Can what i know help the less experianced?' and 'What is the best way to share my knowlege with the public?'
