### Post by b0b138 on 2011-03-27
As far as staring on it, heres what I did

1. Started playing with just booting to the cd. From there installed virtualbox and ran it as a vm.  Do this for awhile and don't be afraid of messing with things. There will come a point that you "break" it, and while it could be fixed, its uaually easier for a newbie to just reinstall.  This will also give you lots practice going through the install, and most importantly learning how to use the partitioner. You could even install windows and ubuntu in the vm so it resembles you're actual computer. Once you've gotten comfortable with partitioning, (and stopped reinstalling every few day lol) move on to step 2...

2. Dual booting. Now that partitioning has gotten more comfortable, you should be able to make room for ubuntu and install and you hopefully WON'T ever have to post "ive installed ubuntu and it deleted my windows" ;)

Now you can use it at full speed, maybe not break it as much, but know how to have it reinstalled in 10 minutes.

I did this for a month or 2, then one day decided to see how long I could go without booting windows... 6 months later I deleted the windows partition :D
