### Post by jerrrys on 2011-11-28
If you remove NM like you say it will remove the desktop-enviroment which in turn removes gnome.

I have built my install from a terminal install and do not have the desktop-enviroment installed.  And your right, its pretty bare bones.  And I do have NM installed, but can remove it without taking out what little I have installed.

If you want to try less than the full Gnome-Desktop-Enviroment maybe it can be replaced with gnome-core.  Can't say for sure since I have not tried this.
