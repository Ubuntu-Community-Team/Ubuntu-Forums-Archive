### Post by deadflowr on 2016-03-13
You mean the name that usually shows up in the terminal, like John@something~$ ?
In that case it does not matter as that is the hostname you gave the machine at install.
(I assume you missed that during the install, so the installer name it for you.)

You can change it if you want by editing the name in two files.
/etc/hosts and /etc/hostname
Both need root-level rights to edit.

If you mean something else, then ignore the above.
