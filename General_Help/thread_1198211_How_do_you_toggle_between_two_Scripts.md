### Post by qwerty57 on 2009-06-27
I do about 99% of my typing in English, but occasionally I need to type a few words in Thai, which uses a different script (alphabet).  

I tried to enable Thai script by going to system - Administration - Language Support & checked both English and Thai (with English being the default) and clicked apply.  My computer went through the update process, but I still can't figure out how to toggle between English & Thai.  I've tried right & left clicking on the upper & lower bars & on the desktop, I've tried shift+space, Alt+space, etc... and nothing seems to work.

Any ideas folks?  TIA

---

### Post by geirha on 2009-06-27
System -> Preferences -> Keyboard. On the second tab (layouts), do you have both an English and Thai layout listed? If not, add the appropriate layout. Then select one of the layouts and click the "Layout Options" button, and under "Layout Switching", choose the desired key combination for switching keyboard layouts.
