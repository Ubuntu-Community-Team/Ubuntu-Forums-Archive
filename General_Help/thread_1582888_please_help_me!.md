### Post by WorMzy on 2010-09-27
Security is a very broad subject. You can look at **chmod** (file permissions) and **chown**/**chgrp** (file ownership) for starters. After that you could look at **iptables** and **netfilter** (firewall), and possibly **clamav** (antivirus). Then you could look at more advanced things like **apparmor** and **SELinux**. Also worth a look is **LUKS** (Linux Unified Key Setup - disk encryption), **PGP** (Pretty Good Privacy) and **GPG** (GNU Privacy Guard - text/file encryption and signing).

I'm far too lazy to chase down links for all of these for you, but google is your friend there. Wikipaedia should have articles about most (if not all) of them, which would be decent enough for introductory reading.
