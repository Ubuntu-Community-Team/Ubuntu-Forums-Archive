### Post by jimmy the saint on 2008-09-13
I have a small dilemma.  I am currently torn between Xubuntu and standard Ubuntu for my main system (core2duo, 4 Gigs RAM, Nvidia 7900gs).  The complication is that I have two monitors of unequal size.

I first installed Ubuntu, but the horrible dual monitor support was a real drag.  because the smaller monitor is on the left, there was a lot of "dead space" in which icons and windows would be drawn, though unseen.  This was a real pain.  I switched to Xubuntu because, though it is much lighter, it has the ability to understand that it shouldn't place icons where they cannot be seen. (Wake up Gnome devs!! this is important).  Also, I noticed that Firefox performance is far superior under Xfce than Gnome for some reason.  Far less pages do the 'scroll-lag-scroll-lag......

Unfortunately, Xubuntu introduced a number of other issues that have yet to be resolved, and cause me concern.  Most notably, I cannot properly eject my Seagate Freeagent drive.  It always kicks out an error.  This is a known issue that seems to affect many removable drives.  There are other issues, but suffice to say, I am growing tired of dealing with them and move on.

At this point I feel that I am trying to choose which issues I can live with.  I use the second monitor a lot, so I don't want to deal with that problem, but I never had so many issues with Ubuntu, even with the buggy releases.  I am hoping a fresh perspective can help me decide.  Any advice is welcomed!!

Thanks
JTS

PS, I am not asking which will perform better.  I am fully aware that Xfce is lighter and that my machine is more than capable of handling Gnome.  Thanks.
