### Post by frytek on 2011-12-08
> **sandyd said:**
> Does anyone know of any TTS programs/voices that sound better than mbrola?

I don't. But most probably this is not the problem with mbrola but with a program you use for feeding mbrola. Mbrola doesn't interpret the text but takes input from outside program. I guess this is espeak (or graphical gespeaker). And this is the reason. I don't know any alternative, though. :(

I did some experiments in the past and I came to a conclusion that pure espeak makes a better job than with mbrola. I mean: this voice is artificial and awful, of course, but when you give it a try you will soon find it's more understandable and actually you can play the text faster and still get it with no problem. But surely it's not something you would impress your friends with. ;) 

We have to wait for SVOX. It sounds quite goog (I tried it) but somebody would have to write a nice wrapper, because at the moment it accepts only a few words in a row. 

Finally, I decided to install vmware player (vmware player may not fit to your system version -- I use it on 10.4), then I installed Windows XP in it (most probably your laptop still has a Windows license label so it's legal) and downloaded Balabolka. It is a free TTS system which is really good. There's a demo on youtube. 

If you have problems with vmware player, use virtual box. I guess it's in the repos. 

I know this is not what we really want, but why not use virtual machines if Linux gives us a chance to do so. :)
