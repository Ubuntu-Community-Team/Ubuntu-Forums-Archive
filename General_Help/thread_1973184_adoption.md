### Post by |{urse on 2012-05-04
Before you adopt Ubuntu I'd suggest inventorying all systems and make sure that everything is supported. It would be best if all systems were on the Ubuntu Certified hardware list. But that does not mean it won't work if it isn't on this list.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/desktop/](http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/desktop/)

What functionality do you require from your systems currently? Are there any proprietary softwares that are necessary for your institute? It would be a good idea to research what the linux-based alternatives are for these softwares.

After installing, simply learn as much as you can here on the forums or hire a tech with at least one linux certification. You can also get support/answers from canonical directly if necessary for a fee.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/business/services/overview](http://www.ubuntu.com/business/services/overview)
