### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-03-05
To answer your original question, there are two general advantages to using Ubuntu in its own partition(2):
1) Speed -- it's a little bit faster.  After all, it's using a Linux filesystem in native mode.
2) Survivability -- it's free from complications brought about by corruption of the Windows partition filesystem.  You're not inside a Windows filesystem partition, so you don't have to worry about any filesystem problems.

Apart from that, the experiences and capabilities are essentially the same.
