### Post by rbmorse on 2009-01-18
Hi AJ. 

You're not going to win this on your own.  Your IT team is not going to be interested in anything except maintaining their institutional program.  In a way I sympathize with them...what they're doing may not be the best of all possible approaches, but it's what they've been trained to do and more importantly, they are (should be) being held accountable for working that system and not what you or anyone else would rather do in it's place. 

That said, at my last command I had pretty good luck with setting my machines up to dual-boot with XP, or running a Windows virtual machine under VirtualBox or VMware. That was until  the Information Security Nazis figured out what I was doing and turned me into my Wing Commander.  He politely asked me to stop.  Since I came up with a positive result when I subtracted the number of stars on my collar from the number of stars on his collar I decided the future lay in (mostly) complying with the regs. 

I've sort of lost the bubble on the current Information Security program requirements, but I suspect not much has changed. This is the military after all, and like a lot of things they are pretty much their way or the highway.  Nature of the beast. 

Good Luck.  Pick your battles carefully. 

Ron Morse
Captain, U.S. Navy (ret)
