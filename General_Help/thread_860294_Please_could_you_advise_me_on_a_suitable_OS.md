### Post by snowpine on 2008-07-15
Hi Cymarr, just to echo what others have said, the general consensus around here is that it's better to go with a *newer* OS that is designed to be lightweight, rather than an older version of Ubuntu. Newer versions will have regular updates and patches, plus they are likely to have better hardware support.

Xubuntu will be the easiest transition for you (most of the concepts are identical to Ubuntu) and I bet you can get it running at an acceptable speed. One tip: you can go to the System menu and there's an option, it's called either Services or Sessions (can't remember right now) where you can select which services are enabled at startup. If you turn off services you don't need, it can speed things up. I personally got good results from turning off bluetooth, printing (don't need it on this computer) and the tracker search tool. Here's a thread with some other good tips: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=840264](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=840264)

And here is a thread on lighter alternatives to Xubuntu: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=732499](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=732499)
