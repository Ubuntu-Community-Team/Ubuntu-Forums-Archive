### Post by Wtower on 2010-06-09
One of my first concerns when I decided for my whole office to switch to linux was how to deal with groupware. Already I had been planning to use Exchange, but luckily caught me up.

I spent the last couple of days evaluating several open source solutions around and I would like to share my experience.

* OpenGroupware and SOGO. Looks like a honest try. Average UI. Good capabilities (or so it claims). I quite disliked the difficult enough installation and postgre over mysql.

* eGroupware and phpgroupware. My best choice so far. Adequate UI. Good capabilities (or so it claims). After a long night's install I still haven't manage to sort out a severe session problem that I found common. I am afraid many more like this are to come around.

* Zimbra. Attractive solution. Very good UI. Good capabilities, but limited for the community edition. I disliked the fact it is very heavy and no easy installation.

* Open x-change server. Attractive solution. Very good UI. Adequate capabilities. Problematic install.

* Citadel. Lots of years around. Meduim UI. Medium capabilities.

* Tine. Very nice one. Very good UI. Good capabilities. Unfortunately lacks e-mail handling.

Several others are available that kind of fall to this category.
Some of them are project management tools (kproject, phproject) with some groupware characteristics. Unfortunately they lack the connectivity strengths of other groupware.

Also, some CRM software could be used as a collaboration tool (heavier), such as sugarCRM and vTiger. Sugar is supposed to be the leading open source solution in the market. Unfortunately the community edition stays behind in reporting. I also disliked the mix of different technologies under the sugar platform. For instance, extJS is used heavily but only from a number of modules under sugarcrm, but the whole UI is far from that impression. I am currently investigating vTiger.

Conclusion: For anything light I would even go for a simple PIM with google sync. But for my office I chose to go for egroupware and pray it works one day. Until then, I will keep looking around and let you know.

Wtower
