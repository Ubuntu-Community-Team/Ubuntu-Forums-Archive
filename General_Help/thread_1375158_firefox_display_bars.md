### Post by mcduck on 2010-01-07
eight toolbars? Insane, I say. :D

Unless all your toolbars are completely filled I recommend just combining some of them. I always move address & search fields next to the menus, and combine the toolbar & bookmarks into same toolbar.

I also put most of the bookmarks in the bookmarks toolbar into subdirectories to have them as dropdown menus in the toolbar, to have more bookmarks easily accessible without cluttering the toolbars with to many bookmarks.

(And of course the tab bar is set to hide when there's only one page open, and I only enable the web developer toolbar when I need it.)

So most of the time I only have the menu bar, one toolbar and the tabs.

### Post by Vaphell on 2010-01-07
menus - File, Edit, View... you got sh.tload of space to the right

'combine bars' as in 'put stuff from two on one'
```
[ bookmarks                                     ]
[ some other stuff                              ]
=
[     bookmarks      ][  some other stuff       ]
```
just enter the customize mode and drag and drop anything anywhere to see what happens. In default layout anything except File, Edit... menus can be moved, coupled with something else on the same bar to occupy less space, i don't know how additional installed toolbars behave though.

if you know your bookmarks by their icons, delete names in bookmark properties
