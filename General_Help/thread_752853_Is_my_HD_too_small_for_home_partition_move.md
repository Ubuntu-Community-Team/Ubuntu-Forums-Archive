### Post by duncan.hawthorne on 2008-04-12
maybe an easier solution would be to leave home where it is, and move the system to another partition, by reinstalling onto another partition, (ubuntu install can fit comfortably in 5GB if /home is somewhere else)
then mounting your old drive as /home

this is if all you want is /home to be seperate from your system

as your home directory contains all your settings for your programs, you can be back to the system state you are in now, in no time

be sure to back up a list of program in synaptic "save markings as" (+save full state not just changes)

then you will have all your programs, and your settings, and your files. easy

i hope that is useful

if all else fails, check out the gparted live cd, for parition resizing and stuff, but dont do this with critical files. and it will also take a long time
