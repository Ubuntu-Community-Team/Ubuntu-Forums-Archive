### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-09-15
We'd need to know the exact model numbers of the Dell. The machines are roughly of the same vintage from what I can tell. I suspect the HP may be slightly better as it's got an AMD CPU (around that time they were better than Intels) but it all depends on the amount of RAM and hard disk size.

The RAM might be interchangable. CPUs definitely not. Graphics cards and hard drives, it depends on what connectors they use (they were transitioning from AGP to PCI-E, and from IDE to SATA around that time).

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-09-16
> **hatry said:**
> Yeah the Dell has a graphics card it looks like. could I take that card out and put it in the HP?

If it fits, yes. One computer might use AGP and the other PCI-E; but it's physically impossible to put an AGP card into a PCI-E slot and vice-versa so if it fits it is compatible.

> I tried moving the sticks of ram but the dell uses ddr2 and the hp uses ddr? Is there much of a difference between those two types of ram?

Yes, they're not interchangable.
