### Post by nitrousoxide82 on 2008-10-29
First off, welcome to the Linux world!

I'm not entirely sure of what you're talking about, but if you mean a dial-up internet connection (possibly offered by Microsoft an affiliate) probably what you're talking about is a Windows-only "dialer"-type program. Most popular dial-up providers (free and paid) here in Brazil have programs like those.

I think all you would have to do would be to configure this dial-up connection (modem device, phone number, username and password) with a dial-up connection utility (at least for Ubuntu 8.04 you can find this on the Network Manager applet on the top bar). What the "dialer" program does on Windows is take care of those settings for you.

And if you mean MSN Messenger (with Microsoft now calling their Windows client "Windows Live Messenger") you can't install it, since it's Windows-only, but you don't need to, you already have it. Ubuntu comes with Pidgin, a multi-protocol instant messaging client that supports MSN.

I had configured a dial-up on my laptop on Xubuntu 8.04 a while ago, but can't remember the details. If anybody more experienced with that kind of setup may help, I offer thanks both on behalf of our newbie friend here and on my own.
