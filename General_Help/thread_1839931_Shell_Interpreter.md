### Post by sisco311 on 2011-09-06
Your file is in DOS format.

Carriage Return (ASCII 13). Often written as ^M or \r. CRs are found just before newlines in text files generated by DOS/Windows apps. You can see them with "cat -e". See [http://mywiki.wooledge.org/BashFAQ/052](http://mywiki.wooledge.org/BashFAQ/052) to get rid of them.
