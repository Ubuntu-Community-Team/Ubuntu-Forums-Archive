### Post by hal8000b on 2015-01-02
Without knowing which audio player you use, you need  on synaptic for normalize plugin.
For example audacious media player has a normalaize volume plugin:

[http://redmine.audacious-media-player.org/boards/1/topics/846](http://redmine.audacious-media-player.org/boards/1/topics/846)

I searched synaptic and found mp3gain and normalize-audio packages. Any volume
correction/compression function will distort the dynamics of your music. If you have
some mp3 that have been recorded at too high or too low volume you can always
amplify or reduce by running the track through audacity.
