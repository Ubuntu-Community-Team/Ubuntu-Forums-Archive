### Post by appsmanster on 2010-08-12
Actually the page is sort of there. The problem seems to be a lack to generate the image the on the first page. Example, if you set the homepage as ***about:blank*** and start Firefox you get a blank page. Use the above work around in reverse order, ***about:blank|about:home***, will produces a blank page. Now here is how you know the page is actually there but not rendered. If you click on the home tab or the second tab and the click the first tab the top sites is now visible. Thus the reason about:home|about:blank seems to work. The ***about:blank*** tab is not highlighted until you click the tab which regenerates or does some sort of a partial reload of the page. And just reloading the tab does not work. The focus has to be on another tab. So what takes place when you select another tab? Its a place to start.
