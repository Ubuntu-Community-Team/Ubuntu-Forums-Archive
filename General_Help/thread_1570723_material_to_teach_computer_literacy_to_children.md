### Post by silverglade00 on 2010-09-08
> I'd also prefer cross platform information. I'd like them to learn how to use computers, not one OS or another...

This is probably the important part. Once they can get past the stage where they have to click on the blue E to get to facebook, then it opens up the whole world of computers for them. I would set at least two computers side by side and show how to do the same things in Windows and Linux, or whatever you are using. Show them how to make indented bullets in Word and Writer. Then they can understand that they don't need a specific program to do something and that it is not scary after all to look through the menus. 

Also show them that it is ok to make mistakes on the computer. Go ahead and push that button in Gimp and see what it does. If you don't like it, click Undo. Simple. No need to be afraid of the computer or blowing it up. Then teach them when they should have respect for it (sudo), but not fear. 

Another thing you can do if you have a spare desktop computer around is disassemble it and give them the parts with an Ubuntu CD. See if they can fix it and give suitable rewards (I'm thinking let the 1 year old have that pony if they can do this). 

Finally, be patient. Let them learn and explore. Let them figure things out on their own and just give them a nudge when they need it. Have fun with them and make the computers a group activity with them instead of the solitary thing that it is for so many of us. 

It sounds like a fun thing that you are doing and I hope to do that with my own kids someday when I have them.

*Edit: Teach them how to use proper forum codes so they don't screw up their quotes *blush* Not that I know anything about that...*
