### Post by Joris Donders on 2011-07-26
A Wubi installation is something that you better leave aside ! 

As a solution: your USB -mouse has certain drivers ? I'd suggest that you just reinstall this driver in W7. 

But also to say; a USB-mouse should work as is !! Regardless of a Wubi installation or not, I think either your mouse is dead or as said the USB-port has issues (what is easily tested with another mouse or memory-stick). 

It's for my feeling a coincidence of things. A WUBI -installation DOESN'T overscribe essential Windows-files such as drivers. 

Consider a dual-boot installation if you really like Ubuntu; just test further the installation and when liked I suggest for a dual-boot installation. (E.g. when Windows goes down for any reason your Ubuntu-installation is then also gone)

To remove the Wubi-installation just go to software adding/removal and select the Wubi (ubuntu) installation and remove the complete 'program'. 

Succes !
