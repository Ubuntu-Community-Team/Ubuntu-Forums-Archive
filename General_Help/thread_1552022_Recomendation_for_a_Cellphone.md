### Post by Elaztic on 2010-08-13
I have a Nokia N900 which I am quite happpy with for the day to day use.
I would not recommend it to others though since the new generations of phones have much better hardware features.
Nokia has also shot the phone in the knees by not developing a 'Nokia Ovi Suite' for Linux but only for Windows.
I am yet to discover an online service that offers sync support for the N900 - have searched a long time and the N900 is more or less the only phone not supported by any of them.

I have not tried any other smart phones but I think the HTC Desire and Sony Ericsson X10 look very nice. I read somewhere that the new Samsung Galaxy does not support over the air upgrades but only from Windows PC - hope that is not true because the Galaxy looks promising as well.

In conclusion: I would definitely recommend and Android based smart phone. Which brand of phone you like is a personal preference. One thing to consider is screen size. I think less than 3.5" is too small but going large is just not practical to carry around - especially if you have it in your trouser pocket while bicycling or (roller)skating.
