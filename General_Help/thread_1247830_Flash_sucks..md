### Post by verysimple on 2009-09-14
This is simply another example of what happens when a company gets too much power over a segment of a market.
- Microsoft has done it with its windows, IE and Office (and will try to do it any chance it gets with any technology).
- Apple has done it with its ipod (was itunes necessary to transfer file to the iPod?)
- Adobe is doing it now with flash. I know, it's hard to believe that it's the same company that brought us the pdf.
- Even facebook that claims to support the OpenID standard couldn't resist rolling out its own Facebook-Connect.

How do we let these things get so bad? Well, we drink the kool-aid.

I can see a couple of things at this point that could help this situation:
- if someone at google had some spark of clairvoyance and decided to migrate youtube to a more open and standardized format, sometime in the foreseeable future.
- if some benefactor decided to financially back a project like Gnash and give it the extra push needed.
- if everyone who complained about flash on linux actually made some sort of a [contribution]("http://www.linuxfund.org/projects/gnash/") to the gnash project. Oh wait... that, we can actually **do**.
