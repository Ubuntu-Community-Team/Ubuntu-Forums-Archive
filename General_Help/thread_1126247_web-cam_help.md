### Post by blueturtl on 2009-04-21
You could try a program called 'camE'.

camE is a small app that will take snap shots through your web cam and transmit them via ftp or scp to a place you desire. Then a person you want to view the cam could simply go to a web page say, and have it periodically refresh.

I know this is not as elegant as video on MSN->MSN but I've found that you really can't expect a web cam to work without getting a real certified MSN client from Microsoft (and they won't ever release one for Linux).

How have you tried sending the video through aMSN? Does it give an error?

### Post by blueturtl on 2009-04-21
It could have to do with the firewall.

MSN on Windows sends everything through the Microsoft server (as far as I know, and this avoids firewall issues).

I'm not sure if aMSN works different, but if it does attempt to establish a direct connection with your friend, then either one of your routers may be at fault. What happens is that aMSN would send the other MSN user your LAN IP address instead of your modem's IP, and then there would be no route to your cam.

This is all speculation though. I don't use aMSN, but you may want to have a look at it's options / preferences and see what kind of connection settings are available.
