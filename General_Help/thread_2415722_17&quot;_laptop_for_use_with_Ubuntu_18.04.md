### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2019-03-30
Without knowing anything else but just going by general rule of thumb I would go with the Dell since they usually have good Linux compatibility (my friend just got an Alien, the latest model and installed Ubuntu 18.04 on it with no issue, I am impressed how everything almost just worked, the touchpad needed a bit of work but turned out there are a lot of info online so that worked too. )

I have had good experience with older models of HP, but from the forum many HP laptops use a non standard implementation of uefi and may require extensive workarounds (if work!) to install a different OS than Windows. See the numerous posts by Oldfred and the links he provides on the update and installation subforum. 

Of course you can never go wrong with system76. I wouldn't worry about the bulkyness though, it is not like you carry a 17'' laptop around like a netbook anyway, it is kind of a desktop replacement. The consideration for system76 usually boils down to price. They tend to be a bit expensive.

Never had ASUS, no idea.
