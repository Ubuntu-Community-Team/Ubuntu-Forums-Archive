### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-12-28
IF you're planning on doing this from home, you need to FIRST, check with your ISP to be sure they are OK with this. Most home usage accounts have LONG terms-of-service agreements (which nearly nobody reads) and somewhere in there is a clause that you won't use your service for commercial purposes.

You set up a server, and your ISP is going to notice the huge growth in your bandwidth usage.  They might contact you to force you to convert to a commercial account (which is what my ISP did to me), or they might just cancel your account -- for violation of the terms-of-service.

### Post by jim_deadlock on 2011-12-28
tejli007, your main issue is that your internet connection will have fast download speeds but your upload speed is probably only 10% of the download speed, which is good for a home user who mostly downloads stuff, but a server needs to have good UPLOAD speeds because it delivers website pages and mail to users - it would only take a couple of moderately busy websites to slow your server to a crawl and this is the reason why you are not going to be able to run a commercial hosting business from your home, even if your ISP allows you to.
