### Post by mb_webguy on 2010-05-05
So basically you don't want to see Gnome-specific apps in KDE, or KDE-specific apps in Gnome?  Well, since the apps are going to be installed on your system regardless, I'd suggest just editing the Gnome/KDE menus to hide or remove the unwanted apps.  Since the settings for Gnome and KDE are separate, changes you make to one menu won't affect the other.

You could create a new account that had KDE as the default session, but it wouldn't really help you any.  You'd still have to edit that user's KDE menu to remove the non-KDE apps, and the KDE apps would still show up in your current user's Gnome menu.
