### Post by QDR06VV9 on 2016-05-06
The only time I hear of this kind of  problem is when the cord is yanked or pulled out with out turning the monitor _**Off**_ first.
Just to be sure,  using the Display app located in system settings > Select that monitor and turn it off before yanking the cord out.
Or simply powering off the laptop before pulling the cord out.
Hope this is useful.
