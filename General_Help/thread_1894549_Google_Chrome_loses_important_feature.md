### Post by oregonbob on 2011-12-12
Any Google Chrome users should know it appears Google wants to program your internet experience like the AOL of 18 years ago. There used to be an excellent dynamic 'New Tab' page that displayed links to recent websites visited. They looked/worked like large buttons with a preview of each website as button labels. Users had the ability to anchor favorite pages by clicking a thumbtack in upper left corner of each button.

It is my understanding that [Google upstairs wanted the anchor feature stripped]("http://www.google.com/support/forum/p/Chrome/thread?tid=0e78e32dd2c7ba4f&hl=en&fid=0e78e32dd2c7ba4f0004b3ee9d942742") and made dynamic with no user control. I wonder if  Google wants to control it to steer users to Google-preferred/ranked sites?

Needless to say I loved the anchor feature on the New Tab page. I hope Google changes its mind or I'll have to look for a new browser.
