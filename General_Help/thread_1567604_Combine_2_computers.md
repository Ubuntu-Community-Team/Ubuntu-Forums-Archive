### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2010-09-04
What you're talking about is a [cluster](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_cluster).  Theoretically you could build one, but very likely it would be:
 - time consuming
 - difficult
 - Not useful when (if) completed.

My understanding is that software has to be specially written to run on a compute cluster; unless you're looking to do some massive mathematical computations, it's not going to be worth the trouble.  And even then, 3 old dells won't get you far.

Unless, of course, you just want to do it for the sake of doing it.  In that case, there's a somewhat old but possibly useful howto on the linux documentation project website: [http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Cluster-HOWTO.html](http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Cluster-HOWTO.html)

Probably you can get better use out them by re-purposing them as servers, internet-only terminals, or just selling (or giving) them to someone who doesn't need/can't afford a fast new computer.
