### Post by viperdvman on 2011-10-09
11.04 or 11.10, ja? Well, that depends on what desktop environment you'll be using. If you want to use Unity, then I'd say go with 11.10 as it has a more mature Unity than 11.04 does. If you still want to use your GNOME 2.x that you're probably using in 10.10, then I'd upgrade to 11.04 and on the login screen, set it to log into the Ubuntu Classic environment.

As for clean install vs. upgrade: If you're upgrading to 11.10, just back everything up and do a clean install. If you're upgrading to 11.04, just upgrade it.

### Post by coffeecat on 2011-10-09
> **soulcat said:**
> over the last, nearly 6 months i have been put off installing 11.04 due to all the problems it was having

The "problems" of 11.04 have been grossly overhyped, imo. Natty has run flawlessly and stably on 5 different systems for me. Undoubtedly some people experience instability or problems related to specific hardware, but a lot of the negative publicity for 11.04 has been compounded by some people's dislike of the Unity desktop.

My own personal view is that Unity in 11.10 is much more polished than in 11.04, so if you're doing a fresh install you might want to consider jumping Natty and going straight for Oneiric. On the other hand, there are bound to be a few teething troubles, which may or may not affect you, in the first couple of weeks after release.. Try the live CD/USB of 11.10 after it is released and see how well it suits your hardware.

By the way, I'm posting from 11.10 now. I've been testing it since alpha, and it's been stable enough for me to use routinely since somewhere between beta 1 and beta2.
