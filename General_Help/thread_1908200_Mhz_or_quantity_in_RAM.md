### Post by bluexrider on 2012-01-12
There's no difference at all. Well, that's not  *technically* true,  but there's a billion other things that come into play before RAM  speed. 8GB of 1333Mhz  will perform so close to the other speeds as to  be unnoticeable. ### Post by varunendra on 2012-01-14
Reputation of the brand/manufacturer counts too - sometimes too much! And usually better brands/models are costlier.

Other things to consider about RAMs:

[LIST]
[*]Latencies
[*]Whether your motherboard supports that much higher frequencies without problems (again- you may have to rely on brand reputation or professional reviews)
[*]As *0011235813* pointed out, it is much better to have 2x4 GB RAM in dual channel mode than a 1x16 GB RAM in single channel mode in practical usage (which assumes you are never going to exceed 8GB usage in the near future). And that holds true even if there is a lot of difference in frequencies between the modules (well.. the modules used in a dual-channel mode have to be same).
[/LIST]
As such, assuming the brand/manufacturer of the 1333 module has a good reputation, I think you should get better performance with 2x4 GB 1333 MHz modules than a 1x8 GB 1600 MHz module. But I am open for more suggestions or corrections to my assumption.
