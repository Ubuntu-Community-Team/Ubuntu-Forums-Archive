### Post by amanchesterman on 2016-02-15
I think the answer is yes. Certainly I have successfully used my (X)ubuntu laptop in this way. I connected the lead from the projector to the special socket on the back of my laptop and pressed a key combination someone told me about (I think it was Ctrl f5 or something like that), and then whatever was on my laptop screen went out via the projector as well. If you have the right kind of output socket and if (unlike me) you can remember the key combination, then it should work. There are others, much more experienced, on this forum who can help you with the technicalities.
However -- and this is just a well-meant comment from an ex-teacher! -- don't assume that you can take your existing powerpoint presentations and show them via LibreOffice. If the presentations are very simple (basically just text and minimal graphics) they may well work OK, but if they include complex graphics, animated transitions, external sound files etc. then you may run into problems. I would advise that you try out your powerpoint stuff in LO before you make the leap to Ubuntu as well.
