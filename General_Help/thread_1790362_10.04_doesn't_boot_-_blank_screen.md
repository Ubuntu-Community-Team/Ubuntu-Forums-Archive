All of them are pure indications of hardware problem, nothing to do with OS on the hard disk.

Troubleshooting steps would be:

[LIST=1]
[*]Disconnect from AC power.
[*]Remove RAM, clean its contact pins with a rubber eraser.
[*]Re-seat RAM in its slot making sure it seats down properly.
[*]Reconnect AC power and power on the system to see if it works. If not, then follow the next steps-
[*]Power off -> Remove all the connections and additional cards from motherboard, leaving only power connectors, Monitor's VGA cable, keyboard and on/off switch connected.
[*]Power on the board.
[*]If no display appears on monitor - it's time to visit a repair shop. Although you may try a few more things like re-seating CPU, cleaning & drying motherboard, etc.
[*]If display appears and you can go into BIOS and navigate through BIOS menus without hangs/jerks, then follow the next steps-
[*]power off the system and start replugging cables/cards to the mother board - one at a time - power on to check if it is okay - then power off - plug next.
[*]Repeat above step until the defective item is found (plugging which makes system unstable), or until all the devices are reconnected.
[*]If a defective item is found, take appropriate steps or report here for possible help.
[/LIST]
Let's hope it's just a matter of a loose RAM or some loose cable.
