What release of Ubuntu are you using?  Where did you get the installation image?

Please provide a list of your hardware.

Are you running a bare metal installation or are you running in a virtual machine?  A dock may or may not be problematic, but we can get to that.  Dell products generally support Linux and are generally well supported by Linux.

Unfortunately, by running all over the internet chasing answers from sources we don't know, you may have made it difficult for us to untangle the issues.  Would you have anything to lose if you were asked to completely trash your current installation, install again clean and then come back with your questions so that we can know the state of your machine?  It may not come to that -- hopefully! -- but that might be where we end up.

There is a steep learning curve with Linux when Windows is your only experience with computing.  That is for sure.   Blindly cherry-picking answers to problems all over the place can make the curve doubly steep and add to a sense of hopeless confusion.

Just to let you know: this is not a Stack Exchange venue, so we can take the time to give you individual support and work through your issue with a lot of back-and-forth diagnostics.  Everyone here gets personal attention to their particular and sometimes unique issues.  We are all volunteers with our own lives, so you may be passed on to different hands from time to time, but you will get continuity.
