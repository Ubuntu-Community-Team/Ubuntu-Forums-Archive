### Post by Impavidus on 2012-12-14
Evolution is available in 12.04 too, maybe not by default, but it's definitely in the repos. You don't have to switch email program (but you may if you wish).

For back-up and restore, see: [http://library.gnome.org/users/evolution/stable/backup-restore.html.en](http://library.gnome.org/users/evolution/stable/backup-restore.html.en)

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-12-14
If you're connecting to an IMAP server, your mail resides there.  Thunderbird should be able to see your entire mail store the same way Evolution does.

If you're connecting to a POP3 server, then all your mail is on your machine.

### Post by Impavidus on 2012-12-17
Here is the relevant section in the on-line evolution manual: [http://library.gnome.org/users/evolution/stable/index.html.en#data-migration-and-sync](http://library.gnome.org/users/evolution/stable/index.html.en#data-migration-and-sync)

Just click some of the links. It should explain everything, unless you're using a very old version of evolution. You should be able to export all data or make a complete backup (seems easier), upgrade to 12.04 and reload everything in your new version of either evolution or thunderbird (I don't know how to import data in thunderbird though).
