### Post by orb9220 on 2006-10-30
Gnomebaker is in synaptic. And does NOT do what you are asking to do. InCD and DirectCD are based on the Mt. Rainer format and makes your cdrw like a giant floppy. And there is no support in linux for that feature. Could be wrong but I don't Think so. 

Apps are just now showing up to do multi-session discs which is just adding files to a disk that is not full but you still have to run burning app to add.
