### Post by lithopsian on 2011-03-16
The two relevant preferences should be:
layers.acceleration.disabled
layers.acceleration.force-enabled

The meaning should be obvious.

Your card may also be blacklisted.  Look in about:support.  At the end it will tell you if your hardware acceleration is on or not.  If not, you may be able to spoof your Firefox into accepting your card although that may cause it to crash or render badly.

And you can look in the error console (or web console) at startup or when your force-enable acceleration and see if it gives you an error.

### Post by lithopsian on 2011-03-16
Whatever you might think, accelerated browser content still requires a chunk of CPU.  Ever tried running a 3D game on an old CPU and a top-notch graphics card?  Doesn't work.

That is a pretty good framerate, but you can get that with software rendering on a good CPU and only 20 fish.  Software rendering with hundreds of fish will slow down, hardware rendering should be able to keep up pretty much right to a thousand fish although maybe not on your graphics card.  Non-accelerated Chrome does seem to get about half the rate of non-accelerated Firefox in FishIE.  I'm guessing they just never tweaked their Javascript engine to try and win that benchmark.

Did you look at about:support in Firefox?  Do you want to find out what is happening and maybe fix it, or do you just want to rant about how much CPU you're using?  Then get glxinfo and see what it says about direct rendering and your glx versions and renderers.
