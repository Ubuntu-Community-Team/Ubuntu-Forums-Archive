### Post by angryhomer17 on 2007-05-15
I'm not sure about on-the-fly converting of ogg to mp3, but you can try a couple of things. 

1. iTunes can play ogg or flac files with a quicktime plugin
[http://www.tipstrs.com/tip/93/Play-flac-or-ogg-files-in-iTunes](http://www.tipstrs.com/tip/93/Play-flac-or-ogg-files-in-iTunes)

2. You can install rockbox on your ipod and then play ogg files directly
[http://www.rockbox.org/](http://www.rockbox.org/)

I would not suggest converting ogg to mp3 either. That's converting from a lossy format to another lossy format which will not sound good at all, though, it may not matter depending on your headphones. Storing your files as flac is a better idea. It's a lossless compression technique, though the files are still large (about half the size of a cd for an album) You could convert flac to mp3 without extra degredation.
