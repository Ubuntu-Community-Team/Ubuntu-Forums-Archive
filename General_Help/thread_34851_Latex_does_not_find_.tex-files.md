### Post by thane on 2005-05-17
Hi,

I have been trying to come up with a solution now for the last several minutes but can't. Since Lyx can interpret the file and generate output, I assume that you have both a correct and working installation of tetex, and that you have created a proper LaTeX document. You are also correct in having the .tex extension at the end.

I have also been trying to figure what output you are getting at the prompt. That may be of some help to the more experienced LaTeX users.

As for getting your feet wet in LaTeX, while the purists may scream and shout, I personally think, from my own experience, that Lyx would give you a good introduction to LaTeX. You can get quick feedback about what your document will look like, and be able to get started writing documents immediately. You can also "less" your documents at the prompt or open them in a text  editor to get an idea of what the LaTeX tags look like, and how and when to use them.

If you decide you like LaTeX, become more familiar with its coding, and want to use some of its features that are not quickly and immediately available in Lyx, you can then move on to install and use auctex along with emacs.

/Thane
