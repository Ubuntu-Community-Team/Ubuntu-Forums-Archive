### Post by Casper Hansen on 2008-09-17
> **russlar said:**
> yup. right click on the toolbar, and select "delete this panel"

You can't delete both.

- You have to go into System > Preferences > Sessions and then delete GNOME panel.

### Post by Fixman on 2008-09-19
> **Casper Hansen said:**
> You can't delete both.

- You have to go into System > Preferences > Sessions and then delete GNOME panel.

GNOME panel isn't executed from the startup programs, but you must delete it from the current sessions manually. However, doing that also disables all services provided by gnome-panel (like the alt+f2 menu, or by having the menu at alt+f1).
