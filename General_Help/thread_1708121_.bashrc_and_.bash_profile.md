### Post by Guenhwyvar on 2011-03-16
I am trying to edit my ./bashrc and ./bash_profile, but the changes do not affect the terminal unless I use . .bashrc or . .bash_profile. How do I make them permanent (as soon as I open the terminal)?.

### Post by wt8008 on 2011-03-18
You should be editing the ~.bashrc and ~.bash_profile files, where ~ will map out to your home directory (/home/username/.bashrc).

I believe the new settings apply when you open a new terminal window.

### Post by andrew.46 on 2011-03-18
I am not completely sure what you are asking but the conventional way to reload these configuration files is to use *source*. For example:

```
source ~/.bashrc
```

### Post by AlphaLexman on 2011-03-18
At login, bash reads **/etc/profile** and then the first file found from:
[LIST]
[*]~/.bash_profile
[*]~/.bash_login
[*]~/.profile
[/LIST]
After that, ~/.bashrc is invoked.

So, your best option is to put your $USER settings in your ~/.bashrc
