### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-01-31
What all of your slowed-down activities have in common is one thing -- they all need to access the hard drive.

If a drive starts failing, you get into the situation where sector reads (and writes) are done over .. and over ... and over.  This can cause the PC to grind nearly to a halt.

Since the problem affects both OSs, and the hard drive is common to both, I would strongly suspect that the drive is failing.

And, before you say a fairly new drive wouldn't fail, I recently had a Seagate 2TB drive only a few months old start to fail.

If you can connect the drive to a Windows PC, you should check the make and model and see if the drive manufacturer's website offers a downloadable drive checking utility.  Most do -- but those tend to require Windows to run.
