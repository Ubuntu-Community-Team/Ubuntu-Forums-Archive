### Post by bobcollard on 2011-02-19
Speed is a matter of the choice of desktops you  use when it comes to scrolling and moving files etc.  KDE is slow, but pretty.  Gnome is a little faster, but bloated.  When you get to Xfce you have most of the abilities in setup of gnome without the excess bloat and speed.  LXDE is bare minimal when it comes to taking respourses but does not give you the options Xfce does.  We are talking about Kubuntu, Ubuntu, Xubuntu and Lubuntu in that order.  The next thing that causes a speed increase is to upgrade your Kernel.  2.6.32 is much slower than 2.6.37 which is the current Kernel in some distros or with Liquorix upgrade.  Hope this helps.

### Post by Grey on 2011-02-19
Sounds to me like your immediate problem is your video drivers.  Your card is quite old, and AMD has dropped Linux support for it.  There are no proprietary drivers for your card.  Which means you are stuck with the open source drivers.  They aren't terrible, but they aren't terribly fast either.  You might want to be sure that you are using the Radeon driver, and you could try a few of the tweaks mentioned.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RadeonDriver)

As for wifi, I'm not sure what the problem is there.  I find USB wifi sticks to be unreliable in any OS (bad antennae).  So it actually surprises me that Windows 7 gives you that much performance.  Perhaps you were particularly lucky with the amount of radio interference present when you tried it in Windows?

ppa:xorg-edgers/ppa

Add Source
