> KDE has changed out of sight in the last couple of years. I wouldn't bother with that 8.04 cd. Download the latest 9.10 kubuntu preview and use that as a live disc.

+1, Kubuntu 9.10 is a huge improvement over previous versions. Not sure about your actual question though, you should be able to explore the menus and sort out what most stuff does. 

Instead of Add/Remove you use kPackageKit in Kubuntu. Note that kPackageKit is broken in the beta release at the moment. You can install Add/Remove or Synaptic if you want though.

KDE has developed quite quickly recently, and unfortunately the documentation hasn't really caught up.
