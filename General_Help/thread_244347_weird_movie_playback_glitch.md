### Post by durand on 2006-08-26
I was watching a trailer for a movie and I just decided to take a screenshot of a cool stunt. I presses the Prt Scr button on my keyboard, the gnome-screenshot dialog came up and I was about to save the image when I noticed that it was blank. So i moved the window to see if that did anything and I noticed that the movie playing in the background could be seen in the screenshot thumbnail! I then saved the screenshot and reopened it in Eye of Gnome while the movie was playing in the background and I got the same glitch! I could see the movie through the screenshot while the screenshot actually maintained its features like the window border, the panel, the controls in totem...everything was in the screenshot as is supposed to be but the part of the screen where the movie is supposed to be playing was basically a cut out where u could see the movie that was playing underneath it. This glitch seems to work on most video formats, i tried it on mpeg4 and avi, both worked in exactly the same manner. I played the movie in mplayer and totem, same glitch! I also tried it with different movies, the glitch was still there. To replicate the glitch, all you have to do is:

1. Play the video file you are going to use in either mplayer or totem. It might work in other players, I haven't tried... You have to leave the movie on for the whole time or else the glitch doesn't work. You might have to be using gstreamer instead of xine, I haven't got a clue if it makes a difference:-s 

2. Use gnome-screenshot to take a screenshot while playing the movie. For me, Prt Scr button on my keyboard did the job, if not, you can use Menu > Accessories > Take Screenshot. That works too. It might even work with other screenshot programs...

3. The screenshot dialog asking you to save the screenshot will pop up. You should be able to see the movie thats still playing in the background though the screenshot thumbnail. You might have to look closely or move the window over the movie playback window. Move the window about a bit, the movie should continue to show.

4. Save the Screenshot. Then open the screenshot in pretty much any native image viewer or editor. Kolourpaint, Eye of Gnome and The Gimp all work. You should be able to see the movie if its still playing in the background! You can even edit the screenshot and your edits are overlayed onto the movie frame! ;) 

By the way, I told my friend about this glitch and he reported that it even works in winblows!

Anyone have any ideas why this happens? I'm Confused!:confused:

Sorry, I have no screenshots because it just shows a blank space where the movie is playing!
