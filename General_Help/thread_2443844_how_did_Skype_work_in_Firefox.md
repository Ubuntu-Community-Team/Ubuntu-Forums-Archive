### Post by DuckHook on 2020-05-21
FF constantly rolls out a ton of security, privacy and anonymization features that can break webapps that rely on promiscuous browser behaviour. Their paranoid philosophy is the reason TOR chose FF as the basis for TOR Browser. In contrast, Chrome is designed by Google to be more friendly to advertisers. After all, that's Google's business model. Therefore, there are published Chrome hooks that developers can easily leverage to implement the communal A/V sharing that is what social media is all about.

Moreover, when FF introduces new security features, they don't give a lot of thought to what social media breakage that their new restrictions will cause, because that's not their focus. This has bitten me a few times too.

Chrome is a lot more "considerate" about not breaking things, but I suspect that this comes at the expense of less privacy and anonymity&#8212;though I freely confess that this is just a suspicion.

That suspicion is the reason I use FF as my main browser and only fall back on Chromium when FF doesn't work for some sites.

It looks like Jitsi is working hard to make their servers work with FF: [https://community.jitsi.org/t/software-unusable-on-firefox-why/22143](https://community.jitsi.org/t/software-unusable-on-firefox-why/22143)

Your guess is as good as mine as to if and when they make it work.
