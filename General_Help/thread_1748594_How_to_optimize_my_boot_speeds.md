### Post by ingothomas on 2011-05-03
This is no answer to the problem, but on notebooks it's a sledge hammer: Don't shut down, go to standby. Unlike Windows (XP) where half my system fell into pieces after waking up, Ubuntu returns exactly to where it was. Another advantage is the fact that Linux systems don't depend on periodical reboots after having handled applications.

But to the question, the only way to speed up the  boot process (likely/sadly insignificantly) is to control the start-up applications. I shut down my Bluetooth management and some other stuff I know, I won't use.

And back to no-answer: Try [Peppermint OS]("http://peppermintos.com/"). It's a blast. Rather slim framework and configuration is strongly config-file driven, but the breeze is unbelievable. If applications and web browsing are more important than Window and local file-management, it's a jet engine.
