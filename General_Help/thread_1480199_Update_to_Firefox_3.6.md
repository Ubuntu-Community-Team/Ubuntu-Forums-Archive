### Post by lovinglinux on 2010-05-11
At this point, most Firefox extensions actively maintained are compatible with FF 3.6. Nevertheless, you can still override compatibility check and most extensions compatible with 3.5 will work on 3.6.

You won't mess with FF settings by installing version 3.6. I switch versions all the time for testing and don't have any problems. Nevertheless is always a good idea to backup your profile regularly.

If you are using 32bit, then the painless method would be to install Ubuntuzilla or simply download Firefox 3.6 from Mozilla and extract it to your home directory.

See the [Profiles](http://firefox-tutorials.blogspot.com/2010/05/profiles.html), [Backups](http://firefox-tutorials.blogspot.com/2010/05/backups.html) and [Installing Other Versions](http://firefox-tutorials.blogspot.com/2010/05/installing-other-versions.html) section of the [Firefox optimization and troubleshooting thread](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1193567)
