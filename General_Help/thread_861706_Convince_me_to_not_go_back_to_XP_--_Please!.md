### Post by Bigtime_Scrub on 2008-07-16
iPods do indeed work with Linux I use mine everyday with Ubuntu and amaorK. When you first start it up it can be a frustrating experience though. It is Apple's fault not Linux. Apple in its infinite wisdom has tons of firmware and DRM restrictions that actively try to block you out of your purchase to force you to use only iTunes with OSX or Windows. 

If you want help trouble shooting your iPod I will happily give it since I had the same problem and someone else helped me when I first started out. You need to be willing to learn, google, and trouble shoot a lot of things yourself. You will need to learn and if you aren't prepared to learn then you should probably just move on. 


iPod with Linux:

Get rid of its firmware that locks out 3rd party apps. If you have ever used iTunes with it you will need this step.

Back up all your media.

Connect your ipod with the USB cable and right click the icon that comes up a few seconds later. There should be an icon on your desktop.

Under the right click menu look at its contents and left click an icon once. Enter Ctrl-A and then delete. This restores your iPod to factory settings and deletes anything added after the fact. 

Right click the iPod icon again and unmount volume and then disconnect the ipod. Turn the ipod on and go through the initialization process of a new iPod. 

From terminal:

```
sudo apt-get install amarok
```

Connect your iPod again and fire up amarok. It should be under Applications->Sound & Video

On the menu bar in amaroK there should be an option to connect. Try that. It probably won't work if it does than it saves you some work. If it doesnt,  go to settings-> configure amaroK. Under Configure on the left select media devices. Scroll down to Apple Media Device. Click Apply, Ok, and close. 

if you need to enter its mount, run a terminal with the iPod connected.

```
mount
```

There will be a bunch of stuff come up, what you want is near the bottom. It will say something like myIpod mounted at /dev/sda1 or something like that. That gives you what the name of your iPod is, the name your computer gives and recognizes and also the mount point you need if it asks. 

Connect again. 

Load media by drag and drop method to the left side of the amaroK menu and then click transfer. 

Thats it!
