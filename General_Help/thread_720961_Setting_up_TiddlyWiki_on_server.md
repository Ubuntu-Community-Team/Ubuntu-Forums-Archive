### Post by MCrittenden on 2008-03-10
I'm by no means an expert on this, but I do know that the default TiddlyWiki does not have a server backend.  Here's some information from the site. (If you know all of this already, just ignore it and I apologize!)

> 
Out of the box, TiddlyWiki doesn't have a ServerSide back end. In many applications that's a great strength because it means that you can work with TiddlyWiki without having to be connected to the Internet or, because it's SelfContained, installing any software.

In other applications, a ServerSide can be very useful, particularly if you want to edit a TiddlyWiki while it's online, or you need lots of people to be able to edit a TiddlyWiki at the same time. The development Community has come up with several ServerSide implementations that are suitable for a range of applications. The most popular and up-to-date ones that you can download and install on your own servers are:

* [http://www.ziddlywiki.org/](http://www.ziddlywiki.org/) from BobMcElrath and TimMorgan
* [http://cctiddly.sourceforge.net/](http://cctiddly.sourceforge.net/) from CoolCold
* [http://www.minitiddlyserver.com/](http://www.minitiddlyserver.com/) from SeanHess and SaqImtiaz
* [http://ptw.sourceforge.net/](http://ptw.sourceforge.net/) from BramChen
* [http://www.patrickcurry.com/tiddly/](http://www.patrickcurry.com/tiddly/) from PatrickCurry

[http://tiddlyspot.com](http://tiddlyspot.com) offers a fantastic, free hosted version of TiddlyWiki with some very cool features (although at present it does not support simultaneous editting by multiple users).
