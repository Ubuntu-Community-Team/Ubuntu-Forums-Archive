### Post by howefield on 2016-10-13
> **sasafrass452 said:**
> I believe it is NOT an LTS version, but since new releases come every 6 months, I wouldn't worry about it! ;)

That's correct, generally only .04 releases on an even year are LTS, so the last one being 16.04, you can rely on 18.04 being the next and so on.

Interim release have a 9 month support life cycle so you have up to 3 months after a new release to make the upgrade to the next supported version.

---

### Post by QIII on 2016-10-13
LTS releases come in April of even numbered years, so 12.04, 14.04 & 16.04 are LTS releases.

One thing that installing or upgrading to 16.10 will force you to do is upgrade at least every 9 months until the next LTS.  Non-LTS releases come out every 6 months with 9 months of support.
