### Post by ijgrant on 2008-08-29
Hey everyone, this is a continuation of the thread "How to make your ubuntu (gnome) desktop look amazing" but this is more focused on the cairo dock instead of awn. Both are good-looking osx dock imitators but cairo doesn't slow login and is a bit more flashy...unfortunately cairo is more complicated. attached it a screen shot of my cairo dock. Also before you ask any questions please look at my other thread (the one I mentioned earlier) just leave out the parts about avant-window-navigator(awn).If you want to know which one is better then I have the answer: neither, you see it depends on what you want...If yo want a simple yet snazzy dock then you want awn, if you want a complicated more customizable, better looking dock then you want cairo-dock. Also take not of system requirements, I would recommend at least 2 gigabytes for a desktop like my own (screenlets, osx dock imitator, compiz-fusion, and run heavy apps). The video card is minimum of 70 megabytes and recommended 128(I have 512). You don't really need a good sound card. Also I would recommend 1.5GHz (I have a 2.1GHz intel pentium dual core). To get cairo-dock go into the synaptic package manager by going to system-administration-synaptic package manager. Once it opens and loads all the programs search for cairo-dock and cairo-plug-ins, once you've found them check them and click mark for installation then apply and when installation is over exit the window. Once it's installed go to applications-system tools-cairo dock and choose the theme you want, when you click ok cairo dock will open...to to configure right click on it then go to cairo dock then configure.

### Post by Upochapo on 2008-08-30
Socca:

If you are getting black borders, that probably means that you don't have compositing enabled.

First, make sure you have the proprietary drivers installed for your video card.  Then to enable the compositing go to System>Preferences>Appearance. Got to the last tab, visual effects and select either Normal or Extra.  Either one will work.

Alternatively, you can simply right click on ur desktop to Change Desktop Background and select the Visual Effects tab that way.

Takebacker:

The Firefox icon is probably part of a theme.  There are lots of themes to look at on Gnome Look.org
[http://www.gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=120x121]("http://www.gnome-look.org/index.php?xcontentmode=120x121")

Select the highest rated tab and you will see a firefox icon there.
