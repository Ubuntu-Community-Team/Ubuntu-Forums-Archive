### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-04-24
Ubuntu has several logs for different reasons, but it does not log *everything* you do. It does log, for example, any errors, logins, and program installations.

With regard to Internet access, firstly your ISP may log everything you do, depending on how intrusive the law is in your country.

Secondly, your browser will record a history of where you have been, data you have typed into forms, and pages you have downloaded into a cache. (Different browsers have different options relating to this; you may be able to turn off some of these.)

If you use Firefox, you can temporarily turn on Private Browsing to prevent recording of history, but you'd probably want to clear the cache afterwards anyway if you're paranoid (what are you doing that you are so worried :shock:).

### Post by Frogs Hair on 2011-04-24
Opera and other browsers offer the option to save website passwords but I never save passwords . There are monitoring options available for network administrators to monitor activity of computers on a network. Temporary internet files and browser history keep a record if you choose to save them , again with the browser. If you would like to keep track of your own activity , Activity Journal software will do that. I don't use wireless , so I don't know where the password is stored . I think it is in Password and Encryption Keys on the preferences menu.
