I'm running it from its KDE LiveCD right now, and it sets up a *"Plain Desktop - Desktop without toolbox"* by default; you can, however, switch to the *"Desktop - Default desktop containment"* easily enough *(though it beats me why you would even want to).*

I wish I understood how to develop a *"Desktop Containment"* myself, so I could create a *"Plain Desktop"* for use under Ubuntu, but I have no idea whatsoever how KDE works *"under the hood."* :confused:

Anyway, the Ubuntu KDE implementation has more problems than just this cosmetic *"cashew"* issue--annoying as it is. Setting up wireless networking, e.g., is a pain, compared to openSUSE. I would love to seriously try out KDE, but Ubuntu KDE just doesn't cut it. Under openSUSE, KDE works a whole lot more pleasantly, but I don't generally like openSUSE... :cry:
