### Post by Scott Baker on 2017-10-22
Howdy, hope I can help out here. First, when installing the version of Ubuntu that you did, you are asked during installation if you want to either "A" install Ubuntu next to a preexisting OS, or "B" do you want to install Ubuntu OVER the existing OS. If you chose the latter, Windows (and your preexisting pics, games, music, etc) are likely gone. Windows installs generally allow for a "safety partition" on the hard drive that will allow for recovery and repair of a borked Windows install, but Ubuntu is REALLY good at COMPLETELY wiping a hard drive. Now that the bad news has been delivered, lets address the good news. Ubuntu is very polished and reasonably easy to transition to. There is now VERY little that you can't do on Ubuntu, including the once difficult gaming. Office tasks are handled by "Libreoffice", internet can be addressed by Firefox, Chromium (Chromes "kissing cousin") or Opera. Geary or Thunderbird gets E-Mail tasks in order. The list goes on. If Windows is required (some colleges have proprietary software that is Windows or Mac only) then you will have to repurchase and have a tech reinstall it. If on the otherhand you're willing to give Ubuntu a chance, you my be pleasantly surprised. One request though, share what variety of Ubuntu you installed. There are MANY varieties out there, you may have one that doesn't feel "familiar", yet there are varieties that feel (and behave) almost like Win 7. Someone may have a suggestion as to what variety to try out. Hope this helps
