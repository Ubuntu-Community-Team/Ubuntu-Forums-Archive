### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-01
This is often due to poor shielding on the cables that connect the southbridge chipset to the audio out jack. There's a LOT of RF interference inside a laptop and lots of things to make sound go bzzzZZZZZzzzzt.

Right-click your speaker icon and open the mixer or click on the Volume Control... button. Make sure the CD Input is muted and that the Mic is muted.

Still having problems? Are you using external speakers? How confident are you that the cable that goes from the laptop to the speakers is shielded adequately?

Does it have a digital (SPDIF) audio output? Do you have an amp that can take SPDIF input?

Worst case, you can crack the case and try to trace the noise with an oscilliscope.
