### Post by rewrittenfromscratch on 2009-05-17
I understand that my title is perhaps a bit confusing, but I'm having a difficult time expressing exactly what it is that I'm searching for.

I'm a stand-up comic, and I have somewhere around 75-100 jokes in my repertoire. I have them organized rather simplistically in an OpenOffice file by category, but when it comes time to arrange a set-list (my selection and order of jokes for a show), it's rather difficult to string them together into a natural flow simply by title.

I went a step farther by creating 3X5 index cards for each joke, with topic tags relating subject matter covered, in order to simplify the set-list-making process. Now, I can simply lay out my index cards on the kitchen table and try to lay them out in such a way that the topics will flow naturally. 

Ultimately, though, what I would like to find is a software-based system for organizing my jokes. I'd like to be able to tag my jokes with subjects/topics, possibly even color-coding them by category, and drag-and-drop them into position. Further, I'd like to be able to change it at any given time by the same process, and then print the final output.

So far, I've tried FreeMind, and it didn't seem to be able to do quite what I was expecting. I also tried a few Windows-based virtual notecard programs (none of them even remotely what I was hoping for).

Does such a program exist? Or perhaps something similar that I can adapt to my purposes?

### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-05-17
Hrm... you could do something vaguely similar to what you want by using a desktop wiki like [Zim]("apt://zim").  With a wiki, you could create a page for each joke, organize them into categories, and include links to related jokes.  Once you have your collection organized, you could create a routine simply by clicking through links from joke to joke, sort of like those old Choose Your Own Adventure books.

I'm sure there's a notecard-type application more similar to what you described, but I don't use them and can't offer any suggestions.

### Post by erixoltan on 2009-05-17
To tell you the truth, I think you're on the right track with the notecards. Write the joke on the front of the notecard.  On the back write multiple keywords like "religion," "lawyer," etc.  Also draw from one to five stars depending on how funny you think the joke is.  Shuffle the cards and deal them face down.  Then arrange them into a stack according to the information on back.  

Finally turn the stack over and see if it's a good routine.  If something is out of place, it means there is something missing from the back of the card.  The secret is to get just the right things, not too much but not too little, on the back of the cards.  

If someone wrote software to help with this, it should be centered around a database and it would be a lot of work to write it.
