### Post by ene_dene on 2010-05-04
> **CaseSensative said:**
> Is there another program like WINRAR that is more Linux based that I can use. I am hopeing to get at least 30% compression rate. thank you for your help.

I am using Ubuntu 10.04, 64 bit. 8 GB of DDR3, AMD athlon x4 620. That is about 2.6GHZ per core. I do a lot of  video conversions and compressions.
rar works on Ubuntu as well. Install package ubuntu-restricted-extras for codecs and rar and xarchiver if you want graphical user interface.
You can't get 30% compression rate of video and audio, if not in raw formats with rar. Not on windows, not on Linux.
But you can encode the video files, and I bet that's what you meant. For that, good software is Avidemux and Handbrake. Make sure you have ubuntu-restricted-extras for codecs installed.

Everything works on 64bit version just fine.

Btw, handbrake is not available in repositories, but you can fetch a deb file on their site.
