### Post by Dale61 on 2011-04-22
The reason why I switched from OO to LO was that I read about Oracle taking over the code for OO, and that they were giving some serious thought about making it commercial software - as in we would have to purchase it.

Some (most) of the OO staff didn't like that proposed direction, so jumped from Oracle and started up The Document Foundation, and with that, LO was born (or moreso LO continued from where OO left off).

The important thing was The Document Foundation was going to continue with LO as FOSS, and Oracle have now realised that they couldn't continue OO in the direction they planned as competition to LO, and have now released all OO code to The Document Foundation staff.

That's how I read it, but if I'm wrong I'm happy to be corrected.
