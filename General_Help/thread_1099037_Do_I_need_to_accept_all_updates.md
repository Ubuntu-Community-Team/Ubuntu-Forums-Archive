### Post by sdennie on 2009-03-17
No, there is no absolute rule that you must accept all updates.  It's encouraged that you do but, if you don't, it's very, very unlikely that anything horrible will happen to your machine.  Updates are sometimes bug fixes and often security fixes.  If your machine isn't at risk for the security fixes to begin with then, installing the fixes isn't strictly needed.  

Still, install them.  On the rare occasions it says you need to reboot, do it.  The updates aren't done to make your life miserable, they are done to make your machine operate better.  If you don't like the frequency of updates, it's easy to change.  Go to System->Administration->Software Sources and change the update frequency.
