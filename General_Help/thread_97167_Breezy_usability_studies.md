### Post by Mizzou_Engineer on 2005-11-30
Well, why not just look over their shoulders when they work on the computer? See what apps they use the most and what they do with them. Note their screen resolution and how many windows they have open, etc. Then I'd ask them what they would change if they could. Bigger icons? Have some things more accessible or easier to get at? Are there things that they would like to do but can't?

After that, set up Ubuntu and migrate their files on over. Make sure to put shortcuts for what they use the most so it's easy to get at. And try to tackle some of the problems they said they had with Windows 98 and wish they could fix (if there were any.) 

I'd also realize that they will have a lot of questions about how to use things as things are differently set up between Windows 98 and Ubuntu (or even XP for that matter) and try to be very available. I had to do that once my mother got an XP machine and got rid of her old Windows 98 unit as the UI was a bit different. Too bad I had to migrate her to XP instead of Linux, but her computer is more or less used as a controller for a computerized sewing machine and the apps are a mix of 16-bit legacy DOS code and heavily DRM'ed NT/2K stuff, and there is no way that the latter would even run under WINE.

### Post by aysiu on 2005-11-30
If people are used to Windows 98, a "usability study" on Ubuntu won't give accurate results. It's better to call it a "migration study."

Usability needs a minimum of prejudice--small school children or adults who've never used computers before.

Hell, even Mac OS X lacked a lot of "usability" when I started using it after Windows XP.

"Easy" means "familiar" to most people.
