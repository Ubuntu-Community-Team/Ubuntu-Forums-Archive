### Post by Mze on 2008-05-05
Download and burn [Puppy Linux]("http://www.puppylinux.com/download/index.html") to CD. Reboot the computer from CD/USB and use the Puppy Linux environment to transfer data.

You could use the Ubuntu Live CD too but I prefer Puppy Linux for such tasks.

Check this [thread]("http://www.techsupportforum.com/hardware-support/motherboards-bios-cpu/33284-problems-sata-ide-setup-winxp-asus-p4p800.html") on the Techsupport forum if there's any info there you could use.

### Post by Mze on 2008-05-05
The harddrive should show being detected by BIOS at bootup, if not, then you'd have to check your motherboard manual with respect to SATA drives or see if there's a BIOS update to your motherboard.
