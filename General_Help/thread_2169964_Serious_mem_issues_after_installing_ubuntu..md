### Post by d-cosner on 2013-08-24
It sounds like one of your sticks of RAM has gone bad. Ubuntu did not cause it, it just let you know with memtest. Luckily RAM is really cheap right now. I would run memtest with one stick of RAM at a time until you find the bad stick and replace it. This has happened to me a couple of times but at least you have a tool like memtest to help sort it out. I had a brand new strip of RAM fail within a month after buying it, it happens sometimes.
