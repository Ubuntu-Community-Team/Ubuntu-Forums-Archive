### Post by diablo75 on 2009-01-24
1.  Open your volume control panel by double-clicking on that speaker in the upper panel by the clock.  Click on the Preferences button and check everything off (or at least everything that could produce sound, e.g. "Front PCM" "Front" "Surround PCM", etc.  After you close that, your volume control panel should expand with those new levels.  Try playing with those while some music or something is playing to see if adjusting any of them produces sound.  Also, and this is a dumb moment of mine in the past:  I once installed Ubuntu on a laptop and thought there was a missing driver.  Turned out the little volume control dial on the side (which I thought was for nothing but headphone volume) also control speaker volume.

2.  The SD media card reader... probably won't work.  Mine has never worked.  It would a lesser headache to purchase a USB adapter or perhaps you have a camera with a mini-USB port and can read the card for you.
