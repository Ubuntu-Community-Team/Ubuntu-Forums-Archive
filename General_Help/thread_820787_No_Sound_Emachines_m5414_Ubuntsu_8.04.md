### Post by studlychris on 2009-01-24
I have solved this problem on my E-machines M5414.  It's so easy I was kicking myself.  


1.  Start an MP3 or other audio file and set to loop so you will know when the audio kicks in.  
2. Click on speaker in tool bar in upper right corner, which will open up the volume control. 
3. File -> Options -> at the bottom of this window under Useful Options you will see "External Amplifier" option.  Make sure it is checked.  Hit "Close"
4. Back in volume control window hit "Show Options" Button on bottom.  
5. Un-Check "External Amplifier" Check box.  Enjoy your sound!! :D

### Post by jimloomis on 2009-01-27
Studlychris, you are the man!

I'm just going to refine your instructions:

1. Double click on the sound icon in the upper right hand corner of the desktop.
2. Go to Edit>Preferences and click on the checkbox for "External Amplifier".
3. Click on "Close".
4. Choose the "Switches" tab, and deselect the "External Amplifier" option.

The sound should work.
