### Post by Scott Baker on 2012-02-16
Howdy. Two things I'd like to address here. First the answer to your question, can you use Ubuntu to recover files from your Windows drive. Yes, it can be done, and is actually quite easy. ( secret here, PAID techs often use some variety of Linux to do exactly what it is that you want to do ). First, a question though. Have you tried to start your comp in safe mode? By starting in safe mode, you may be able to correct the issue that caused your machine to barf in the first place. In safe mode, you can (possibly) do a system restore, which will allow you to roll back your machine to an earlier date, when it was working correctly. If this is out of the question, you access the hard drive through the heading "computer" (different versions of Ubuntu have this located in slightly different locations) The Unity desktop, which is the default for the last 2 versions of Ubuntu has it located at; desktop > go > computer. Opening  "computer" should show you at least 2 drives. One will be "file system". That will be the Ubuntu files. You should see also see a drive marked " *** GB hard drive, file system". that will be your Windows drive. Double-click that drive, and you'll have access to your files. When you're done, make sure to UNMOUNT your drive. This one is very important, you don't want any problems. Good luck, and let us know how it all works out.
*** the above is based on the assumption that you are using a thumb/flash drive or CD/DVD of Ubuntu to access your comp to complete these repairs ***  [-o<
