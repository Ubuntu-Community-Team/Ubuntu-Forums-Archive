> Do I have to buy Verbatim DVD's or would the Sony DVD+RW's and DVD+R's from WalMart work just as well? 
For general use the sony's should be fine. Sony, like most 'name brands' doesn't make their own blank media, it's manu. by one of several co. and re branded. Stay far away from obscure names and _store branded media_ (mostly garbage)

### Post by danwood76 on 2008-07-25
Most of the name brand disks are of poor quality.
They all rebrand disks as there are only a hand full of manufacturers in the world (most situated in the far east)

Good quality manufacturers are Taiyo Yuden or Ritek, Ritek being cheaper but still good.
I have bought many name brand disks when I was testing different media out a few years ago and most of them were the same cheap nasty manufacturers which tended not to read well and contain lots of write errors.
Some companies like Verbatim re brand Riteks and Taiyos.

What I do now is buy from special online media stores where you can buy disks by manufacturer as opposed to brand.
I buy really cheap CMC (Off shoot from ritek) disks which have actually been really good and burn really fast.

I have never burned RWs never seen the point, I dont do RW cds either.
A bit wasteful but I always use DVD-Rs or CD-Es due to how cheap you can get them, my current DVD-Rs cost 8p per disk and my CD-Rs cost 6p per disk which is far cheaper than you will get in any high street store.

[http://www.cdfreaks.com/](http://www.cdfreaks.com/) is a good site to see how different media performs in tests btw.
