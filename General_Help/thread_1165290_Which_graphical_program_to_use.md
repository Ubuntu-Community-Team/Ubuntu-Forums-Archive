### Post by mcduck on 2009-05-20
Yes, Inkscape is perfect tool for such tasks. Gimp is a bitmap editor and isn't really made for this kind of purposes, while Inkscape works with vector graphics which is what you'll want for all logos and that kind of graphics.

Additional benefit is that vector graphics can be scaled to infinite resolutions without loosing quality, which makes them perfect for all printing purposes.

Just create the circle & your text, select both and then choose Text/Put on Path from the menu.

So, do you want one piece wrapped around the circle (so that at the bottom of the circle the text is upside down)?

Or two pieces of text so that the both texts are correctly oriented?

In other words, should the result be like the picture bacardiandwatermelon posted above, or like the one attached here?

For this kind of result you'll want to make two circles, one bigger than the other. Wrap the first part of text around the smaller circle and second part around the bigger one (use the "flip"-buttons in the toolbar to get text inside the circle instead of outside, or convert the circle to path and select Path/Reverse). You can use the align tools to get both circles correctly centered and rotate & scale the circles until the texts fit nice. Then you can just hide the outer circle.

### Post by hugmenot on 2009-05-20
I once did a logo like this.

The trick is that you can shift the text on the circle sideways with some keyboard shortcuts.
Maybe < and > or that plus Alt, or something. 

I also used two circles. When you »switch« the direction of the path (circle goes clockwise or the other way) then the text will be on the outsifde or on the inside. So one circle is clockwise and the other one is anticlockwise.
