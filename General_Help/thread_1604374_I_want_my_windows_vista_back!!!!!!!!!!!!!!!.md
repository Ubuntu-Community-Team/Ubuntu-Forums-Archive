### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-10-24
In my own experience, restoring from a set of recovery disks, especially if they are really CDs, not DVDs, can take hours and hours.

You should not have to do ANYTHING other than insert the first CD and start it working.  Full-restore programs will wipe the entire drive, reformat it to the OEM default settings, and then rewrite the hard drive -- one sector at a time.

And, it won't really give you your Vista back (as in, the state it was in when you installed Ubuntu); instead, it will restore Vista to the state it was in when you first turned on the machine.

As to virtualbox, everyone has their own opinions, but if you really want to have BOTH Vista and Ubuntu on your machine, I would recommend traditional dual-boot instead.
