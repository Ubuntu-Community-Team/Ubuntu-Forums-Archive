### Post by fidelis2 on 2015-09-21
You could try out "gnome-disks". (It's in the Software-Center from Xubuntu, as well as in the command line: "sudo apt-get gnome-disk-utility".)

It is a Redhat program to create images from a drive (or its partitions) and to write images to a drive (or its partitions). It's a very reliable program and I use it to mirror my harddrives and SD-cards with an USB SD-card-reader/writer.

It could work for you, too, and is worth a try I think.
