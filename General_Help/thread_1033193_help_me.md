### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-01-07
Can I just ask: Why do you need to know?

If you are starting out with Linux (as your "join date" and "Posts: 1" suggests), then you don't need to worry about it. Just stick with Ubuntu's default, which is ext3. If you are using an SSD, then you might find ext2 a better choice as it uses fewer writes.

With filesystems, it's pretty much the case that "if you have to ask, then you don't need to know". The people who really need a different filesystem will already know what they need or be able to find out on Google.
