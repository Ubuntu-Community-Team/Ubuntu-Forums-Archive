[quote=munkymonkjr]cyberpower - same as ibuypower pretty much, slightly better tech support from what i hear.[/quote] I had a horrible experience with cyberpower in 2004...since tech support is being mentioned in relation to them I will share my story. Your mileage may vary.

I bought a system from them. Within 3 months the wireless card died...just a defective component, no big deal, right? I call tech support...no one answers so I leave a voice mail. After a week I repeated this, after another week I began to repeat this at various times of day/days of the week. I also emailed as well. After several weeks I finally got a response via email telling me to try pulling the card and reseating it. (I had done this and said so in my first email weeks earlier.) We bounced emails for three weeks or so, them telling me my card wasn't defective and implying I am an idiot. Me showing the exact steps I had taken in my attempts to get it working and the methods I was using to determine what to try, etc.

Bottom line, I had to call the Better Business Bureau for their area and file a complaint to get them to replace a $30 (or less) part after I had spent over $2000 on the computer.

They have good prices (at the time I got what I wanted from them cheaper than building it myself!), but I found the service to be lacking a bit and would not choose to buy from them again.

Oh! I second the person who suggested you consider building the system yourself. It's not that hard on a desktop and it would be a good learning experience. I do understand if it is still a bit intimidating, though.
