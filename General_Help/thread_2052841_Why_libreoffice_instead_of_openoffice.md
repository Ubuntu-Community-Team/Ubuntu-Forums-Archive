### Post by jwbrase on 2012-09-04
Because after Oracle bought out Sun, it was uncertain what they'd do with OpenOffice. This lead to the creation of LibreOffice and it's adoption by Ubuntu (as well as several other distributions).  Fortunately, Oracle turned OpenOffice over to the Apache Foundation, which assuages the worries that lead to the creation of LibreOffice, but by this point LibreOffice has enough momentum going that it would probably be more trouble than it's worth for Ubuntu to switch back.
