### Post by taurus on 2006-10-29
The fastest way is to back up everything in your $HOME, including the . files...  Then, just dump everything to your new laptop when you have installed Ubuntu on it.

### Post by taurus on 2006-10-29
If you are not sure, copy one directory over at a time and log out and back in again.  See what has changed.  This way, you know exactly which directory causes the trouble.  Yes, it's slow going this way but at least you would get all your settings that you want back.  ;)
