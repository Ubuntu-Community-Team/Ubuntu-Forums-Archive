### Post by pksings on 2007-02-22
Aside from the legality aspects. Even with the codecs you probably will not be able to play the songs from Walmart.com as they are DRM protected. And the method they use requires windows to play them. Discussion about how to get around that in linux is a different post.

This is what I do.

I burn the songs directly to CDROM and then rip them to mp3 or ogg-vorbis format files for use in my portable player. This method removes DRM and allows you to legally use the music you purchased yourself. (obviously it is illegal to distribute them).

It is a little more labor intensive as I do have to type in the title and artist names when I rip them. But I'm a fast typist and don't mind as it's worth it to me to be able to play them freely in my portable (Neuros) or my desktop machine.  I intend to build a MythTV box and put all of them on it for home stereo use also.

Enjoy

### Post by mcduck on 2007-02-22
> **pay said:**
> It would be the same if I said would downloading a copy of Windows of a torrent site be legal if I owned a copy of Windows?

Actually it is legal, how you get the install media doesn't matter, as long as you have a legal registration key, and you are not using that key on any other machine.. The license is about the registration key, not the physical CD :)

My friend had to call Microsoft when his Windows CD exploded inside his CD-drive. They told him that for a small fee they could send him a new disk, but he could just as well use any disk of the same windows version as long as he uses his own registration key.

(USA has some rather funny laws so it's of course possible that it's not legal there ;))

### Post by mcduck on 2007-02-23
> **pay said:**
> I heard that it is legal to use mpeg 4 codecs if you compile them from source code. Is that true?

The licensing of MPEG 4 file format is not clear, but as far as I know it's free (as in beer) for individual people to use. (this doesn't mean that all codecs that use MPEG 4 are free, but just the format itself.)

If you are interested, read more about MPEG 4 here: [http://www.ncsu.edu/it/multimedia/mpeg4.html]("http://www.ncsu.edu/it/multimedia/mpeg4.html") and here: [http://apple.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=02/02/13/041234&mode=nested&tid=107&threshold=3]("http://apple.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=02/02/13/041234&mode=nested&tid=107&threshold=3")
