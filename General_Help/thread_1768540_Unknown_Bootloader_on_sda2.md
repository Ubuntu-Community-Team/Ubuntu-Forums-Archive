### Post by garvinrick4 on 2011-05-26
So sda1 is your / or file system and sda2 is extended and sda5 is your swap. You can run
sudo fdisk -l (lower case L)
and will tell you.
I would not worry about that if is the extended partition. As long as you have grub2 in the mbr and looking for grub files in sda1 which it must because you are booting. That would
not slow anything down nothing is looking there for boot files. Enjoy your Ubuntu.
