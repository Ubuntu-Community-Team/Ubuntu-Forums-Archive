### Post by ajgreeny on 2009-04-03
Unfortunately Creative are not very good at providing any sort of linx drivers for their sound cards.  If your motherboard has onboard sound enable that and remove the Creative and try again.  It may not be as good as your Creative card in Windows, but at least you should get sound of some sort.  Stand-alone sound cards are slowly disappearing, it seems to me, as on board sound gets better and better.

### Post by aikiwolfie on 2009-04-03
Yeah the X-fi range doesn't work too well with Linux. There is a sort of beta driver available from Creative. But by all accounts it's not particuarly good. Creative just don't have the resources to support Linux and Windows and they aren't really willing to play the open source game.
