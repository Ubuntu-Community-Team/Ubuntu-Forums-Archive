### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-12-07
The process to setup a "Live" mode usb-stick is fairly simple.   Just be advised that a "Live" Linux on optical or usb-flashstick is a "read only" system by default.   

Perhaps a more friendly way of learning how to do it is by watching some Youtube videos.    For example:   [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CFeri7UiYNs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CFeri7UiYNs)

That's a pretty decent, easy to follow video . . . however, it's a bit lacking in the area of how to set up your PC  to boot from something other than the internal hard drive (or internal SSD).   All I can say is every setup is different depending on the make of the PC and the maker of the setup firmware (aka uefi/bios).   Essentially, you want to go into the area on the firmware screens that control the boot order or boot sequence, and change it so usb-hdd at the top of the listing.  Then save your changes and reboot.

Everything else on the video is pretty well explained.   Good Luck.
