### Post by Rich78 on 2007-02-04
You have to think very hard about their requirements before doing this, hardware being one of the main priorities, then if they're into games as a secondary consideration.

You also have to know enough to get them out of trouble, or they won't thank you for breaking their computers.

Don't get me wrong, it's great to see more people wanting to encourage others to adopt, but there's a lot to consider and many things you may not have thought about.

If you haven't already I would give yourself a good bit of time with it before you pass it on to others if they're not used to Linux or technically minded.

### Post by benson444 on 2007-02-04
Firstly I'd recommend taking it a bit slow. Install it on one of your friends systems and see how things go. If you can get all the really important things for the average person coming from Windows working, like graphics drivers, wireless/wired internet, video, sound, printers, mp3 players and so on, then there would be little reason for them to learn too much about the command line. If they need to use it then you can talk them through it, or do it yourself. Then if they are willing to learn then they can ease themselves into it without any vital need to do so.

Write a text file in their home directory with as much basic user info as you can. Tell them about their home directory, file system, simple shell commands, permissions etc. Put a few links to useful information, like this forum for one.

No matter what you do though, your going to get a fair number of questions. I suppose it depends on how much work you are prepared to put in. My brother came round and saw beryl running. I showed him some of the bling, and the next day he phones me saying he's dual-booting edgy on his laptop. Oh, and "how do I get the usb wireless working?". After lot's of "what is the terminal? What does tar -zxf ... mean? It says no such file." type questions he brought it round. By total luck I came across a guide that worked.

So, as I say, it depends on your knowledge of Linux and how much responsibility for their systems you're prepared have. I certainly wouldn't install it on *all* my friends systems though...
