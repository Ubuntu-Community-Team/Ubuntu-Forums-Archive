### Post by mikehattingh on 2010-10-22
Can anyone point me in the right direction ?
I'm looking for electrical cad software similar to [AutoCAD Electrical]("http://usa.autodesk.com/adsk/servlet/pc/index?siteID=123112&id=13846963") or [PCSchematic Automation]("http://www.pcschematic.com/english/index.htm"). I need something to do WIRING diagrams as apposed to circuit schematics like Egal. Electrical not Electronic. I'm talking about power distribution, fuses, relays, PLCs, motors, solenoids, switches, buttons, and cables. I am _not_ talking about transistors, capacitors, integrated circuits, and printed circuit boards. Sorry to put such a fine point on it, but there are a lot of PC board design tools out there that use the word "electrical" in their description, but are not useful for diagramming the wiring connections in a machine.
 
I think an electrical cad program should have the following features in order of importance:

Parent Child relationships between symbols
Automatic Parent <-> Child references across multiple pages
Automatic wire numbering and wire number updates
Automatic generation of Bill Of Materials
Symbols can be pulled from a database and placed on the drawing
Proper dynamic title blocks
Terminal/wire number lists
Cable lists


Here is an example of the sort of thing, but wiring diagrams can represent an entire factory, documents with hundreds of pages.
[IMG]http://www.plctalk.net/qanda/uploads/A091208A.JPG[/IMG]


As I said if anyone can point me in the direction of such a a piece of software, or in the direction of a project developing one, I would greatly appreciate it.

Thanks
