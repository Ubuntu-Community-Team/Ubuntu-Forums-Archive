There is separate .Trash for root and every user. .Trash directory is maintained by system. If there is nothing in .Trash even the directory may be absent but is comes there immediately when you delete something. You may browse the hidden .Trash-directories in nautilus when you press CTRL-h .

### Post by pjkoczan on 2007-11-01
> **bone2006 said:**
> 
~$ ls -al
-rw-r--r-- 1 bone2006 bone2006   19 2007-11-01 13:03 .Trash


I hate to break it to you, but your .Trash isn't a directory, it's just a regular file. A directory's mode would show up as drwxr-xr-x (d for directory, x bits set so you can cd to it).

So, try rm'ing the file and then making a .Trash directory. It shouldn't be a regular file.
