### Post by LinuxFan999 on 2011-12-30
There is no way to revert to a previous version of Ubuntu without formatting the hard drive and doing a clean install of the version of Ubuntu that you want to go back to. It will be necessary to back up your data.

### Post by saneearth on 2011-12-31
You can do as mentioned above and only format the root partition and reinstall 11.04 again. I also go into the home folder prior with a live cd and delete all config files that are associated with anything but known programs for which I want to keep settings. I have found in the past that if I do not, then I have had oddities with my desktop afterwards. I would imaging you would for sure want to rid yourself of .pulse as this could be your issue. I just normally get rid of all hidden folders with the exception of my browser settings, email, pan, etc. Good luck. I also use the same username and password and it seems to always work. I have literally done this dozens of times experimenting, but my New Years resolution is to try and leave well enough alone. :)
