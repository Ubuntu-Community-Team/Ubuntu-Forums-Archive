### Post by raptir on 2014-08-28
Dropbox has never been a particularly good deal as far as storage goes. Google Drive and Microsoft OneDrive provide much more storage with their free plans and much better pricing for larger amounts of storage.

[https://www.copy.com/price](https://www.copy.com/price)

It looks like Copy is 15GB free, same as Google Drive or OneDrive. When you start paying for storage Copy loses out though. Copy is $9.99 for 250GB of storage while Google gives you 1TB for the same price. 

The one draw of Copy would be that it has a native Linux client, but Google Drive is pretty easy to get working.
