### Post by marcw on 2006-06-04
Yes, I'm just as giddy as everyone else about the new release.  It really is faster, better, etc.  And there really are many improvements.  But there's a couple of issues leftover (from previous versions) that drive me bonkers.  I don't think you could call them bugs.  Just, well...  issues.

Proxies and Passwords.

**Proxies**
Some of us are forced to run our workstations through proxies because of firewalls.  In order to do that successfully with Ubuntu I have to specify proxy settings in no fewer than 5 locations.
- System Prefs->Network Proxy
- Synaptic
- Every browser
- /etc/apt/apt.conf
- /etc/wgetrc

Not all of these locations are apparent or intuitive.  It took awhile to figure out why some things weren't working for me.  Hopefully someone will eventually be able to figure out how to lessen this number.


**Passwords**
Even though there might be some very good reasons for this, I need to remember several passwords just to use Ubuntu.
- login
- keyring
- master (Firefox certs)

To their credit, Ubuntu has eliminated the need for a specific root password.  Now if they could just work on getting rid of a couple others...



There's also still some things with wireless and bluetooth that leave me less than impressed.  Even so, I still have to agree with the consensus opinion that this is a terrific distribution.  I'll be upgrading a few relative's machines with it.
