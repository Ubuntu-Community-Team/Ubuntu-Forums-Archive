### Post by iam2 on 2011-08-07
[SIZE=4]It has been going on two months since the problem began.  I have Ubuntu 9.10 dual booting with Win98 on a 30GB HDD, and tried to make a Remaster of the OS's.  One problem after another has taken me two months to figure out, with a lot of help from the Forum, especially, the ever patient and helpful "kanishkdudeja".  In the final analysis it turned out 9.10's root was too small, as was the swap.  I would suggest that is one of the first things anyone with a Remaster problem check, because if they are not big enough it will be like chasing chickens around the barnyard.  That is the sum of it.  However, it may also be worth noting that during this ordeal I tried making a clone or copy of the OS using, first, Clonezilla, which for some reason would not work for me.  Then I successfully used a "free" program called PC Disk Copy or PC Disk Clone.  The word "free" is in quotes because it comes with strings.  It works, no doubt about that, but it is very slow, and worse, it messes with the operating systems.  It jumbled up all of my settings in both Linux and Windows and kind of makes them act like a virus.  In fact, it set the Windows Bios clock back to the year 1980!  On Linux, one of the things it did, was leave a cookie or some kind of trace in the system so that on the next boot into Mozilla browser instead of getting my home page, I got PC Disk Copies Home page.  All they want to do is sell you stuff, and when I contacted the company for some help along the way all I met was a bunch of flack and constant sales pitches.  As far as I am concerned it can be a real risk, but like I said, it got the job done.  

Nothing beats the Remaster once there is elbow room in root and swap for it to do its thing.  

Oh!  I should mention, however, that Remaster's home page needs updating because the directory I was sent to for the Remaster repository for 9.10 is out of date, which was also part of the problem.  It was only in my long hours of searches of the Forums that I just happened to bump into someone else with the same problem and it was mentioned that a newer version of the repository was required in order to make the Live CD boot.  So, once root and swap were enlarged, and with the proper version of Remaster for 9.10 it works great.

Thanks to all the folks on the Forums who gave their input too.  It all helps – believe me!  It all really does help.  Thanks again.[/SIZE]
