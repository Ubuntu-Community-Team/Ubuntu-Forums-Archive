### Post by Impavidus on 2022-01-17
I've never heard of ctrl+p to paste. Normally that's print, but that may be different on non-qwerty keyboards. Paste is usually ctrl+v. In the terminal however, those simple ctrl+<letter> combos are already used to send signals to processes. Instead, ctrl+shift+v is used to paste. You can also use right click &#8594; paste, the menu or select, than paste by clicking the middle mouse button (no need to copy).

### Post by solamarpreet on 2022-01-17
CTRL + SHIFT + C to copy
CTRL + SHIFT + V to paste
The reason for this extra shift is that the usual CTRL C and several other inputs serve different purposes in the terminal.
