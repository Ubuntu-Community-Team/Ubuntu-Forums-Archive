### Post by jbeiter on 2007-12-08
I am a long time Unix and Linux fan and I have been very excited about how well Ubuntu is working. I started with Feisty on my work laptop and I am truly a fan. My intent is NOT to post a bitchy-whiny critique of Ubuntu, but to give heartfelt feedback as a fan. My experience is a bit frustrating but I'm anticipating great things from this development team and anxiously await new releases.

I made the jump at home by migrating a Redhat system that I have been using as my home system over to Ubuntu 7.10. It has been rock solid for years and is well endowed with RAM, disk, and CPU.

The performance has been frustrating and it appears I am not alone by the many searches I've conducted for solutions.

Printing - I have an HP 722 that worked fine with Redhat. There may be a bug with CUPS with it but nothing I've been able to try will make this thing print on Ubuntu.

Sound - Has worked.. today, it decided to not work anymore. After searching and and trying dozens of suggestions, tried un-installing all of the sound stuff, then reinstalling it again (along with gdm and ubuntu-desktop since that gets removed too :/) and it works again... A bit microsoft-kludgy for Linux.

USB - my most recent frustration. I can't mount a Linux friendly mp3 player (Neuros) that I've been using happily on Redhat. Well, sometimes it mounts, sometimes it doesn't, sometimes it mounts/unmounts/mounts/unmounts/rinse-repeat.

Gnome Desktop - It looks great but it sure is much slower now.

Wireless - Wireless on the laptop has always been really bad with Ubuntu. At work we have Cisco wireless with peap and I still can't figure out how to configure wireless such that it works at home and at work. Windows can do this with ease. With Ubuntu it's like squeezing blood from a rock.

I'm sure I will muddle through these issues and find the work arounds like I have done on other *nix systems for the past 20 years.

Being that you're 10 years more experienced than I am I feel confident you'll figure your issues out. I recently had an issue with sound after installing pulseaudio to get mpg123 to work again. Installing pulseaudio completely messed me up.
