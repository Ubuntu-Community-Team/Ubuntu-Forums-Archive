### Post by atomicspin on 2012-08-28
This happens a lot on older laptops.  Every year or so I take them apart and use a bit of canned air to get the dust out. (My PS3 is the worst culprit)

There are tutorials all over for taking apart your laptop.  Take it apart as far as you feel comfortable and make sure you blow out all the little vents and the back and even the keyboard area.  You'll notice quite a difference.  

If you do that and it STILL keeps happening, then it may be that your chip has become unseated.  Get some thermal paste and check out YouTube to see how to re-seat your processor.  The thermal paste wears after a few years and sometimes this becomes necessary.
