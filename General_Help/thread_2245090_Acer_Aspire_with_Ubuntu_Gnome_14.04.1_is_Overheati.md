### Post by Pelvur on 2014-09-22
I don't think it is Ubuntu problem as such. Drivers may be, but not the system. From my experience, Ubuntu heats up laptops less than Windows. I used to have windows laptop that would overheat greatly especially when closed. Although I did see some heat onto it with Ubuntu as well it was not that bad. Even though the finally problem was kind of with hardware - I had too much thermopaste on the processor. Once I fixed that, it stopped heating in both systems. 
I don't have windows installed on that laptop anymore, but recently I got heating problems with Ubuntu. I was about to try to change thermopaste again, but then I just removed back cover and cleaned the fans and cooling channels properly. Rarely hear the fans since then, no heating even when closed. 
So try to define what exactly overheats (not sure how to do that, probably it would be an element with highest temperature) and try to see if newer/different drivers can resolve the problem. Look at your processor performance as well, it might be that it runs at highest clock all the time in Ubuntu.
