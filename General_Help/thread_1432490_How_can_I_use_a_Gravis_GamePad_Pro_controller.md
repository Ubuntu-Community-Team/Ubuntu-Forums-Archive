### Post by t0p on 2010-03-17
Recently I was given a bag containing some bits of peripheral hardware - leads, old fashioned switch boxes, an internal modem... the item that really caught my eye is a Gravis GamePad Pro by Advanced Gravis Computer Technology Ltd.

I'd like to use the Gamepad Pro to play Alien Arena and a whole bunch of other games.  Thing is, the plug at the end of the cable has a top row of 8 pins and a bottom row of 7 pins.  This plug does not resemble my computer input sockets at all.  But according to the leaflets that come with the controller, it should plug into "a standard PC games port", that should be a socket with 8 pins across the top and 7 across the bottom row.  I can't find alternative sockets that should do the job.

Any help or advised are very welcome.  I'm looking forward to dealing with you again.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-03-18
Long time ago ... PC's came with lots of different kinds of ports, among them parallel printer ports and game ports.  Nowadays, you see different ports.

To connect this gamepad, you obviously need something with a game port.

Since your machine doesn't have such a port, you will have to buy an add-on controller card that has such a port, and one that will fit into one of the slots in your machine.

If it's a fairly new machine, you probably want a PCI card.

There may even be such a thing as a game-port-to-USB adapter cable which would then allow you to plug this into a USB port.
