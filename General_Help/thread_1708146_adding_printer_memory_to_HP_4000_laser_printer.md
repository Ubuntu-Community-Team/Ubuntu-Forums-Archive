Hello,

I have worked on HP laser printer systems for years and can tell you that more memory will help in the long run. But it also depends on what actual model you have. If its a straight 4000 or a 4050 as the engine on a 4050 is faster. But in general adding more memory will enable the printer to print faster to the extent of the speed of its print engine.

In short, It most definitely would not hurt at all to add more.

### Post by srs5694 on 2011-03-16
There's a way to print your current configuration (called "Print Configuration" on one of the menus accessible via the physical buttons on the printer's top panel). This will display various information about the printer, including its currently-installed memory. FWIW, mine's got 20 MiB, which is fine for my needs. Given the prices on the page to which you link, though, you'd be insane to add 16 MiB rather than 64 MiB, at least if you buy from that vendor!
