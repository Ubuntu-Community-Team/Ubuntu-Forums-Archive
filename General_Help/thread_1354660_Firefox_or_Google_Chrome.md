### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-12-14
Firefox is much more mature than Chrome, has lots of extensions that increase productivity and improve the interface. It does what I want and looks the way I like.

My Firefox runs smoothly, even with more then 40 extensions installed. I don't care about the extra speed of Chrome, since Firefox compensates on other areas and the upcoming versions of Firefox are really fast. Mozilla is improving it on every new major release.

Opera is the worst in my opinion. It's a myth that Opera is faster than Firefox. Perhaps it wins in terms of page loading if you enable the turbo mode, but then say bye bye to your privacy.

Anyway, depending on how much stuff you add to Firefox and how much stuff you do with it, it can become slow. But this can be easily solved with some maintenance. See [Firefox optimization and troubleshooting thread](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1193567) to learn how to speed it up.

> **kellemes said:**
> [Opera]("http://www.opera.com/") is just as fast as Chrome.

No way. Just do some benchmarks with [Peacekeeper](http://service.futuremark.com/peacekeeper/index.action) and you will see.
