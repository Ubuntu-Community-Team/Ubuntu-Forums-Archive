### Post by BCtom on 2011-05-01
Hi, -Ziggy-

Unity does not have the weather app. That was from the Gnome Desktop. I have no idea about getting rid of the other items on the panel? But I can help you with the weather! :)

There is a really good way of getting the weather indicator back on the Unity Desktop top, upper right panel.

I got the instructions from this blog here:
[http://kedaver.blogspot.com/2011/04/weather-indicator-applet-for-ubuntu.html](http://kedaver.blogspot.com/2011/04/weather-indicator-applet-for-ubuntu.html)

First: You basically go to the Ubuntu Software Centre, 7th ICON down that looks like a Ubuntu shopping bag, on the left of the 11.04 Desktop. Open it up. 

Second: In software centre, Download and install "indicator-weather" app from the repository.

Next: Click on the Applications icon, and find "Accessories" and you should see the orange weather app icon (near the bottom). Click on it, to run it, and it should appear as a orange dot where the weather icon used to sit in the Gnome Desktop. 

Last: The settings should be obvious. Search your town or city, follow the three steps. 

Hope that works!

Cheers.
