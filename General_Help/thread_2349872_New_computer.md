### Post by 1clue on 2017-01-20
Frankly I don't know why people keep thinking Linux only belongs on  low-end machines. I don't run anything except Linux on the hardware, and  if I'm not going to get something fancy to put Linux on then that means  I don't get something fancy.

That said, the part about bleeding  edge peripheral hardware is dead on the money. I'd avoid getting latest greatest  hardware of any sort for a Linux box, except for maybe CPUs and  motherboards from Linux-friendly vendors. This includes sound cards and video cards, and pretty much anything requiring a separate driver to work.

You can drop that much  into a really good server-only system no problem. Or a desktop with last year's audio/video options.  Mainstream peripherals generally are cheaper  than the new stuff anyway.
