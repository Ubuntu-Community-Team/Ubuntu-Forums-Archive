### Post by kerry_s on 2008-07-11
a desktop is the whole deal, does everything.
a window manager, is just a part, you use other programs to add only the features you want.

there are many to choose from in the repo, it's just a matter of finding what you like.

for instance a lot of users like fluxbox, openbox and icewm. most of us who use a wm usually build up from a base install to get a very light and fast system with only what we want to use.
example: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal)

if your just starting out though, it's best you just add to your current system to get use to things.

if you want to give it a try, open up synaptic and click on the left to highlight a line, now start typing> icewm < it will do a search as you type kind of thing, it's faster to find programs.

once you have it installed, log out and select icewm in the menu and log back in.
