### Post by ebasa on 2011-04-20
SPAM filters work great and are good at helping you not seeing them, however they do nothing to stop SPAM. Is there a way to BOUNCE SPAM so the spammers think your e-mail address is non-functional? If not then why not?

### Post by blueridgedog on 2011-04-20
Almost all spam has a forged "from" address, so bouncing spam is useless.  Some mail servers can be configured to reject spam.  I have done this in my professional life, but many consumers do not want to trust individual mail hosting solutions to decide what is and what is not spam.  Your email host provider already rejects a certain amount of mail they know is spam based on the IP address of the mail server sending the email.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-04-20
On top of that, a large proportion of spam comes from compromised Windows machines that are part of a "botnet."  Even if you bounced mail back to the SMTP envelope sender, chances are good that machine has no way of receiving your mail.

The only solution to spam is filtering.  Anything else is a waste of time.
