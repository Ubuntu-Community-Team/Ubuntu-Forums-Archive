### Post by papibe on 2011-03-28
> **BNZBKK said:**
> ...1. Can I re-install without reformatting ?
Only if you partitioned your hard drive so that your personal files are located in its own partition (usually /home), you would be able to chose not to reformat that partition in the install process. You have to format / (root) any way.

If you did not, you have to backup your files before reinstall, and then restore them to their final location.

I hope it helps.

### Post by tordeu on 2011-03-28
As mentioned, you can choose not to format your single partitions when reinstalling and afterwards you would be able to access your files.

My advice, however, would be to back your data up first and then do a reinstall. This is just a security measure so that you don't accidentally delete your data during the reinstall.
If you have a second hard drive or a flash drive which is big enough (to at least hold the most important data), you can back your data up by booting from the Live CD (using Ubuntu CD, but instead of "install Ubuntu" clicking "try Ubuntu").

Of course, we can help you with that, if you need help to do that.  

Now, some questions:

[LIST]
[*]Do you have a second drive or a flash drive to backup  your data first?
[*]Do you know if your /home folder is on a separate partition?
[*]Do you any kind of encryption for the hard drive or your /home folder?
[/LIST]
