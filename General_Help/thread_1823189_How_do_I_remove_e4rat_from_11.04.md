Well if you used e4rat, then the damage is already done. e4rat is an online defragger. If you did not notice any speed improvement then you somehow done something wrong or updated you computer, which requires another defrag. e4rat works by moving your boot chucks as close to the edge as possible of the hdd. Unless you have an ssd then there should be an improvement, if not something went wrong

I guess you can remove e4rat from the grub menu and that is about it
