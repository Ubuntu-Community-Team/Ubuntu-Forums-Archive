### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-01-28
I used to reinstall Windows at least once a year -- once before the beginning of each semester when I was in college -- because it was easier and faster to do that than clean off all of the accumulated adware and spyware that had sneaked past my security software, orphaned files and folders from software I'd uninstalled, and unwanted entries that had crept into the registry.

Since I started running Ubuntu a few years ago, I've reinstalled a few times, but only once was because of problems with my installation (which I myself caused from playing around with the system).  The others have been to upgrade to a new version of Ubuntu (because I feel that a clean installation is generally better than an upgrade, no matter what software you're talking about), or to alter my partitioning scheme.

With better software management, no registry, and no malware or viruses, I have far fewer reasons to reformat in Linux than I did in Windows.
