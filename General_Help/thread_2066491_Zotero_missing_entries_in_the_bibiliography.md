### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-11-13
As an endnote to this, no, there is no way of connecting them back together. There is a glitch, which I found with some digging and posting on the Zotero forum.

For other users out there with this problem, the trick is you need to cut and paste. That keeps things connected. When you copy a footnote number from one place in the body of the text to another (say for identical references) the copy is not connected to Zotero any longer, only the original. This is why, over the period of about eight months, things had been moved around quite a bit and any references I'd copy/pasted were no longer connected to Zotero and therefore didn't appear in my bibliography when I hit the plugin icon and created one (my original problem).

For instance: Say if you have two footnotes you have *_inserted_* with the Zotero plugin. That's fine. The first one will always be the full reference, for instance:

   Peretz, Isabelle, and Robert J. Zatorre, &#8216;Brain Organization for Music Processing&#8217;, *Annual Review of Psychology*, 56 (2005), 89&#8211;114  (p. 110) <doi:10.1146/annurev.psych.56.091103.070225>

... the second instance of the footnote, depending on the referencing style, would be something like:

   Peretz and Zatorre, 2005 (p. 112)

But if you copy the original and create a third footnote it will be the full reference, not the abridged version, and that won't change with a Zotero refresh (there is a 'Refresh' icon in the toolbar of Libreoffice).

So, rule of thumb: if you cut and paste, all's well. Otherwise insert all references via the plugin and this will mean all consequent entries of that footnote will be the abridged version, as it should be, and references for all footnotes used will appear in the bibliography. The moment you copy a footnote you will have trouble and possibly create, as we say here, a dog's breakfast, as I did. 

I spent some time reinserting every footnote that required it and everything is now joined back together. I hope my little saga will save someone some time down the track. Cheers.
