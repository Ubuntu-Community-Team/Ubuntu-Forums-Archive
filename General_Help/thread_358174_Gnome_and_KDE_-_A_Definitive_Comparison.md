### Post by ardchoille42 on 2007-02-10
Keep in mind that your mileage may vary. But, I have used both KDE and gnome and here is my opinion.

1) Crashes: I have had many crashes of different apps in KDE. I installed KDE with 'sudo aptitude install kbuntu-desktop'. Now, these crashes may be the fault of the Ubuntu packagers, KDE packagers, the individual KDE apps or KDE itself, I don't know. However, if a user finds multiple crashes in KDE, as I have, they are usually going to say that KDE is unstable. I don't know how stable KDE is.. I just know that I installed KDE from the Ubuntu repos and I had lots of crashes on my systems. I have had no crashes at all in gnome on Ubuntu.

2) Tweaking: I use gnome because, for me, it requires very little tweaking and the default configs are good. That is not to say they are good for everyone. I disliked the fact that KDE has a bajillion configurations for every little thing and KDE required more time to get it the way I wanted it.

The above is simply my opinion. It's hard to be unbiased when you like a certain desktop environment or window manager.

The bottom line is.. try each of the desktop environments and stick with the one that works best for **you**, one man's trash is another man's treasure. That is the only way to really see which is best.
