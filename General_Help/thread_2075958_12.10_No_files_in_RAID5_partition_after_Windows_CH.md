This was great and I spent a day playing with Ubuntu and listening to music off one of the RAID partitions and deleting a couple of rubbish songs along the way.

The following day I booted in Windows which did an automatic CHKDSK as part of startup. This produced many lines of text that including terms "Deleting indexing" and "Restoring files".

Once this had completed Windows started normally with no apparent issues but after rebooting in Ubuntu the partition with the music had no files in it after automatic mounting.

That is: there are files in that partition in Windows but none in Ubuntu; the other three partitions on the RAID work fine in both Ubuntu and windows, although I have not tried writing to them in Ubuntu (for fear of killing them too).

I have spent the day reading but advice seems to be fairly thin on the ground.


Running ```
sudo dmraid -ay
``` again produces many errors including: ```
ERROR: pdc: reading /dev/dm-2[Input/output error]
```many times with other errors.

And ```
sudo mdadm --examine /dev/*
``` produced ```
mdadm: No md superblock detected on /dev/dm-2.
``` among other things.

Any advice would be appreciated.
