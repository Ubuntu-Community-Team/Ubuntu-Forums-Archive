I wouldn't be surprised if it can made to work on Linux. I assumed it was just an adapter that did everything in software. That was proven wrong when I went to help someone with a 9 year original Athlon with 256 MB of RAM, that had insane performance problems. (Billed them an hour to tell them a new computer was the only fix, but that is another story)

The Magic Jack hooked to it works perfectly fine, no sound degradation, dropped calls etc. Proves that all the voice stuff is built into the adapter, which I think would make Linux support possible from 1 or 2 interested programmers, but I think many Linux users are rarely the demographic for Magic Jack. The only people I've ever met that have one are in their 70's and keep using the same computer way past its time, even though it is obvious they have had the means to replace it. So the interest to make it work may not be high.
