### Post by AbleTassie on 2019-06-27
Thanks for everybody's replies. I have concluded it is a hardware problem. The mouse is a corded usb mouse. I opened the mouse and cleaned the dust out of the wheel and made sure the buttons are clean. I tried the mouse on a windows PC and it did the same thing. I tried to solve the problem with stuck keys in windows and it still did not work. 

I bought an old (but unused) usb 2.0 mouse and that does not seem to have the problem, so I have concluded that it was a hardware problem.
