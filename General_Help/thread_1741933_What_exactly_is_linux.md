### Post by seeker5528 on 2011-04-28
From a pure technical standpoint, Linux is just a kernel.

In order to be a complete OS, you need additional tools and utilities, which is where distributions come in (Ubuntu, RedHat, Debian, etc...)

Since Linux distributions use Linux as their kernel, people commonly just refer to them as Linux. In the bigger picture, since people *do* commonly use the term Linux to refer to distributions, I would say it is correct to say Linux is an OS. 

It's really a matter of context. If you are referring to the kernel when you say Linux, it's not an OS. If your are referring to one or more distributions when you say Linux, it is an OS.

Of course if you are RMS or one of the people drinking the Gnoolaid, a Linux distribution is not Linux, it's GNU/Linux. :P
