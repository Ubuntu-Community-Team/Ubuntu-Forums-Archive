### Post by PacShady on 2006-12-01
I'd recommend Dapper over Edgy, simply because Edgy seems to have quite a few bugs in it, at least at the moment, whereas Dapper tends to be much more stable. As for 32 vs 64 bit, well it depends on whether you feel the extra time configuring certain programs and compatibility issues are worth the extra speed boost or not. 32 bit is MUCH more compatible with other software than 64 bit, although there are sometimes ways of getting things to work on the 64 bit version, perhaps by 'tricking' programs into thinking you're running on 32 bits, etc.

'Shady

### Post by Lord_Butler on 2006-12-01
I agree with the above posters that you should start out with Dapper. I recently converted my roommate to Linux with Dapper. We spent a week making sure that he had everything working right as far as networking goes. Then we upgraded to Edgy with no problems.

Unless you're planning on using this computer as a server, I think your best bet would be to go with 32-bit. There is simply not adequate software support for the 64-bit to be for my liking at this point.

Hope that helps and good luck!
