### Post by Sealbhach on 2009-01-09
There's a "Take Screenshot" app in your Application/Accessories menu. You can set a delay of a few seconds then swing the cube into action.

### Post by steveneddy on 2009-01-10
Let's not forget

**ksnapshot**

You can set it to take the shot after a 3 second wait. Just push the "Take Screenshot" button, set up the cube and wait until it takes the pic.

### Post by jesterthejedi on 2009-01-10
Wheel Mouse!
Use the middle scroll wheel to drag the cube into rotation, then ust use the print screen button to capture. You have more manual control of how you make your cube and can copy anytime your like. The one drawback is that your mouse may make it onto the screen shot. This was not a problem in Hardy, so maybe my Compiz is set up differently.
