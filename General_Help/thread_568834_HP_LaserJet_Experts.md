### Post by lintoon on 2007-10-06
Your printer is a laser printer and so does not use ink.  It uses a toner cartridge.

If you are getting marks on the printout at equal intervals down the page the chances are the problem is either with your toner cartridge or the fuser assembly.

Take your toner cartridge out and have a look at the roller for marks.  It should be spotless.  If your toner roller is ok the next most likely part is the fuser assembly.  The fuser assembly is the part at the back of the printer which gets very hot.  It's job is to "fuse" the toner onto the paper.  If memory serves me correctly you have to take the covers off this particular printer in order to get to it (I may be wrong).  Other lasers you just have to lower the back cover to get to it.  

The fuser assembly has "claws" which guide the paper around the fuser roller.  Over time these claws pick up paper particles and dirt which harden onto the claws.  This excess scores the upper fuser roller and causes a mark around it.  The result is a vertical line down the page.  It is also possible that the upper fuser roller has dirt/toner on it which is causing marks at equal intervals down the page.
