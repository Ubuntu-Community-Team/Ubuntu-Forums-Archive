### Post by ricnow on 2010-10-04
This post is more about Unbuntu design decisions so is a bit long winded as not about a technical problem I am experiencing, as luckily (so it seems), I did not upgrade or update with kernel 2.6.32-25.   

I installed Lucid on a multiboot (all linux) 86 system a month or so ago and have been extolling its virtues (amazing speed and stability) ever since, in particular in that it would be very suited to using by the vaste majority of computer users not interested at all in the technology i.e suited to take over from Microsoft. But I had noticed the "Update Manager" may be, would not be obvious to use by a complete non-techy newby.  More importantly, my installation default shows "Recommended updates" all ticked and I up till now have been taking a quick look at these and leaving ticked and completing the update installation.

The appearance of linux kernel 2.6.32-25 recently rang caution bells, especially because of words "(New Install)" so I did not install.   I looked on forums etc instead and found there were problems with 2.6.32-25 so it seems.  See for example <http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1583569>  while <http://fridge.ubuntu.com/node/2119> was written on 2 Sept.

I have not got "Proposed updates (lucid-proposed)" selected in the Software Sources/Updates box  but have got "Recommended updates (lucid-updates)" selected.  I had not altered these from default at installation.  It seems 2.6.32-25 should have been under proposed rather than recommended and is not a stable version yet, perhaps.

But what a disappointment to my mind to find Lucid fails on this point for the type of user I describe above.  Such a fantastically good distribution for me though.
