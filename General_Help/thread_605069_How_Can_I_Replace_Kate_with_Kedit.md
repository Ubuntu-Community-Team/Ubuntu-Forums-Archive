### Post by ardchoille42 on 2007-11-06
Open KDE Control Center (alt+f2, type in kcontrol). In kcontrol, go to the KDE Components section, then click on "File Associations". under "Known Types" click on "text" to expand that section and click on the "plain" file type. Over on the right you'll see a list of apps that are associated with that file type. Move kedit to the top of that list and click the "Apply" button at the bottom right. Congrats! Kedit will now be associated with that file type.

Additionally, you can go through the other file types and move Kedit to the top of those too.
