### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-18
If it's a standard keyboard (and I mean in construction, not in layout), then you should be able to *carefully* pop off the keys (or at least the "[" key and its neighbors).  Once you do, you can take a cotton swab and a very small amount of suitable cleaning fluid -- and I'm talking about something like plain water or rubbing alcohol, and certainly *not* anything like bleach, ammonia, or WD-40 -- and clean off the keys and springs.

A friend spilled a soda on his keyboard, and I cleaned it for him like this using a convenient bottled water and a small pile of Q-tips.  A week later he had another spill, and tried cleaning it himself.  He decided to use some penetrating oil (possibly WD-40, but I'm not sure) to fix a sticky key.  And he decided to do this with his computer on and his keyboard plugged in.  Needless to say, he ended up buying a new keyboard.  The eyebrow did eventually grow back, though.

### Post by Peter09 on 2009-02-18
You need to get a spray that leaves no residues, water, oil etc leave residues which can do that same thing as milk. There are sprays to clean keyboards, it may be worth giving one a try. Basically spray as much on as possible in the hopes that it will dissolve and then run-off with the milk residue. Any remaining cleaning fluid then evaporates.
