### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2008-10-02
A harddrive can't be damaged in any way that prevents a specific OS of being installed onto it.

An existing OS can possibly prevent another one of being installed (you certainly will not find Linux doing that) by means of software technology. But even in that case, as soon as you format the drive, you remove those restrictions.

It is possible that an OS that issues wrong commands to a hardware component will damage it (mostly applies to bad hardware design). In terms of harddrives, this would mean the harddrive would be broken and you won't be able to install anything on it. The OS will certainly not break it for any other specific OS (short of very weird coincidence).
