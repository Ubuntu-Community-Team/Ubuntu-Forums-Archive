### Post by Grey on 2005-12-08
[QUOTE=ace2005]* Volume Control on the task bar somewhere[/quote]

Should be there by default.  Right click on the panel, and got to "add applet".  Then add the volume control applet.

[QUOTE=ace2005]* Transparency, inactive windows and moving windows? does it have a context menu when right clicking on the window border to change th settings?[/quote]

It's available, but only through an extension, and it's buggy as hell.  There's a thread around here [somewhere](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=75527&highlight=composite)

[QUOTE=ace2005]* Transparent taskbar?[/quote]

Right click on the panel, and go to properties.  Go to background, and select solid colour.  Then you can adjust opacity.

[QUOTE=ace2005]* Any cool points about gnome?[/quote]

I like it because it tends to not get in your way as KDE does, while still looking good.  I feel at home in it, and can get work done.  KDE tries to be my friend just a little too much for my liking.  But it's a personal preference, and I say that if you really like KDE, then stick with it.  But for my purposes, Gnome does the job just fine.  It's simple, not cluttered, and perfectly useable.  I would like to have some of the transparency effects that KDE has, but I don't really miss them that much.  My bit of eye candy on my desktop is the 3ddesktop program, and transparent taskbars and terminals. 

Anyways, sorry I couldn't help you more, but that's what I know.
