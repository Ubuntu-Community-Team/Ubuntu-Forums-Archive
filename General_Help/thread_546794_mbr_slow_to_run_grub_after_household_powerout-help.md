Power outages (and spikes) could have corrupted your BIOS settings and/or damaged your hardware - from just data corruption in various places on the disk to sectors that now take multiple re-reads just to get past the damage.

The first thing to do is reset the BIOS to defaults, then backup any valuable data off your hard disk and do a thorough surface scan of the whole thing.

The problem could well be something totally different, and if it is I would recommend not complaining that people in a software forum don't have a solution for your hardware problem.
