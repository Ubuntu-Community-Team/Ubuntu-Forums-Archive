### Post by walt.smith1960 on 2011-05-25
I would avoid Dell.  AFAIK Dell is Lexmark with a different badge and Lexmark has probably the poorest Linux support of any major manufacturer. HP seems to have the best rep,  Brother & Epson seem quite good.  Canon users seem to go to Canon's Asian or European sites to find software.  Models seem to change with the phases of the moon but if you could find a model that's been out for a while I'd think the likelihood of it working would be better.  Check with [http://www.linuxfoundation.org/collaborate/workgroups/openprinting](http://www.linuxfoundation.org/collaborate/workgroups/openprinting) for any information on models you may be considering though that site can be behind on recently introduced models.  I saw at least one model listed as a paperweight which is now working.
