### Post by drewsus on 2011-02-11
The first one, and the default one if you don't touch anything and let it boot on its own, is your current kernel. Ones after that are older kernels just in case the new one messed things up for you. 
I recommend you google what a kernel is as this is probably what is causing confusion for you.
