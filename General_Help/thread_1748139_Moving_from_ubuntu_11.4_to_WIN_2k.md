### Post by jerome1232 on 2011-05-03
No, you would just burn the image as an iso (right click and write to disk)
Then you would insert the disc into your cd-drive and boot from it, run the installer ensuring you format the entire disk.

You will lose all files currently on your disk, so you may need to backup first.

btw you can choose Ubuntu Classic at the login screen to get gnome-panel back. I also doubt you have even given the Unity interface a chance, It's actually quite nice once you customize it, I can't wait until it gets some real polish on it. I had issues with a network upgrade personally, I opted to try and fresh install and everything worked beautifully so beware that could be the issue.

There are also other user interfaces you can install, unlike Windows Ubuntu doesn't force any UI on you, you can use any that is available for Linux. You might like LXDE.
