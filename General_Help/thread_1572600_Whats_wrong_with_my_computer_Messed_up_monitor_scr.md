### Post by akakingess on 2010-09-11
I would say 99% sure that it is the video card, the only other thing that it could be (since you've eliminated everything else) is the actual slot that the card is in. So, I guess the last part of troubleshooting it would be to move the graphics card to another slot (if you have one available) and see if the problem persists. Otherwise, it's going to be the card or the driver for it, but more than likely the hardware itself. Let us know what else you find out.
