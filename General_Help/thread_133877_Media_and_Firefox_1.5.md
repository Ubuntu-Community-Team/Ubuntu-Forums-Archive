### Post by astronerd on 2006-02-20
Hi all, I just got Firefox 1.5.1 from automatix and am re-customizing all the things I had on my 1.07 setup.  One of my major gripes is I dont like the mplayer media package that gets installed.  I used to have it when I would click on a video link(wmv or mpg) from a site it would open totem or VLC and I could watch the video there.  Now it opens the video in the browser in a small, non-adjustable, mplayer window.  Is there anyway that I can set it up so firefox will open files with the player of my choice.  I know that you can do it going to pref and download options, but I dont know what dir or folder to assign so it finds VLC and totem.  Thanks, 
Charles

---

### Post by nanotube on 2006-02-20
in firefox, go to edit>preferences>downloads
there click on the "view and edit actions" button, and edit away.

---

### Post by nanotube on 2006-02-20
and by the way, vlc and totem are both located in /usr/bin. easiest way to see where an app is located is to open up in terminal and use command "which", such as "which vlc", e.g.
