### Post by derrick81787 on 2008-07-30
I wouldn't be surprised if this is related to something in your xorg.conf.  I think that with the inclusion of xrandr as default for Hardy, they changed the default xorg.conf from being completely filled out to being very minimal, since Ubuntu and the xorg people are wanting X to auto-detect the optimum settings.

I would try to find the settings that should be included in the xorg.conf for the touchpad.  I'm not 100% about all this, but I think this is a good guess.

Also, in Buzzdee's xorg.conf that is posted, The input device is listed as "Mouse0" and the driver is listed as "mouse".  I'm at work right now, but I believe that my settings for my touchpad (which works) have the driver set to "synaptic" or something similar.

That may be worth Googling around for.
