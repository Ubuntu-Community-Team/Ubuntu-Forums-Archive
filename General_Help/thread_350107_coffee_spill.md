### Post by Rhubarb on 2007-01-31
I wouldn't power it on at all until you do a quick checkup on it.

Coffee isn't just water, and doesn't just evaporate - it leaves a residue.

Most keyboards are designed very well and naturally doesn't allow much liquid to interfere with any circuitry beneath.

Assuming you've spilt most of the coffee on the keyboard, you'll need to remove the keyboard panel and check to see if any coffee has managed to make it's way into any of the circuitry / motherboard.

If you don't know how to do this / don't feel confident, get a technician to help you do this.

I'm not sure what coffee's corrosive properties are (I know Coke, Noodles, and anything salty are very bad), so it might be ok.

You might need to clean the keyboard too - the keys can be removed, use a slightly damp cloth, never use any cleaning agents like alcohol (weak soap solution should be ok).

### Post by Rhubarb on 2007-01-31
> **slipperhead said:**
> edited to say i manage to get the keyboard board off but some coffee had got into a hole under it and when i tipped the laptop, was dripping out from the front.

Ok, that's quite bad.

If coffee is corrosive, then it has already eaten into some tracks on the circuit board.  Which means your motherboard could well be dead.

Don't leave your laptop to dry out - it only gives the coffee a chance to corrode (if it is corrosive that is).

If you do turn on your laptop, it won't explode or anything (take the batteries out 1st), it's more likely to just not turn on / certain parts may not work.

I recommend you take it to a technician to check - even someone who fixes TVs will do.

If you're feeling adventurous or if the laptop isn't worth much to you, then open the laptop up, pull everything that has come into contact with the coffee apart, and clean with water and a toothbrush (in some cases shellite can be used to clean).
Before you turn the laptop on, I recommend you do so with: No RAM, no hard disk, no hard drive, no CD-ROM drive, no battery.
When you turn it on it should complain about having no RAM.
Turn it off, insert the RAM, then turn it on again.
Keep on doing this until you have a complete laptop.

If you have any important data on the hard drive, get it backed up!
You can put laptop hard drives into regular desktop PCs with an adaptor to aid the backing up process.
Get a technician to do this if you don't know how.

Post back here if you need more help / guidance.
I hope your laptop will make a full recovery!

### Post by scbonney on 2007-02-01
I spilled diet coke on a older thinkpad and lost some key function.

I finally took it apart carefully down to the circuit boards under the keys.  This meant cutting through the plastic welds underneath the keyboard.  After that I carefully cleaned the places between the printed circuit lines where I could see a bleed from the water damage.  Use water, not solvent.

Then used an inexpensive soldering iron to remelt the weld sites and re assemble. [ I first tried glue... don't suggest this. ] 

Presto it worked.  Don't suggest if you have a warranty, New keyboards are available if your machine is worth it and not too hard to install [my experience].

Just keep the circuitboards clean and make a tight weld. keep as dust and fingerprint free as you can.

Mine works fine now, the tab key is a bit insensitive because the weld is not holding the circuitboards firmly.
