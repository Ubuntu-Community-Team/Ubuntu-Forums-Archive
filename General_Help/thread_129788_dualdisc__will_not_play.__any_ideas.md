some interesting stuff from the wikipedia page about these discs [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DualDisc](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DualDisc)
> Because the 0.9 mm thickness of the CD layer does not conform to Red Book CD Specifications, which call for a layer no less than 1.1 mm thick, some CD players may not be able to play the CD side of a DualDisc due to a phenomenon called spherical aberration. As a result, the laser reading the CD side might get a "blurry" picture of the data on the disc; the equivalent of a human reading a book with glasses of the wrong strength. Engineers have tried to get around this by making the pits in the CD layer larger than on a conventional CD. This makes the CD side easier for the laser to read; equivalent to the book using bigger print to make it easier to see, even if the person's glasses are of the wrong strength. The inevitable downside to this, however, is that the playing time for the DualDisc CD layer decreases from the standard 74 minutes of a conventional CD to around 60 minutes.

Looking into the problem, I've read that dual-disc's deviation from the red book standard can cause some drives to fail at reading it (hence the warning that should be on the package).  I have a Plextor 708-A and it seems to be completely incapable of reading the cd side of my dual-disc.  I've updated firmware and tried the disc under windows xp and ubuntu dapper, both failed to even mount the disc.  If you continue to have problems with dual-discs, try using a friends computer.  Maybe you'll get lucky and their drive will be able to read it.  As an aside, this experience has convinced me that my money is better spent elsewhere.  The RIAA can shove it.
