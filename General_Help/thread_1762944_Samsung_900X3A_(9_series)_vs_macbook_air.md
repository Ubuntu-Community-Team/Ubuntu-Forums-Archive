### Post by idjaw on 2011-08-03
I've been researching the exact same thing. I haven't purchased either (I already have a laptop I'm forcing myself to be happy with :)). But if I may provide input on my research maybe it could help or start some kind of discussion about it. It would help both of us. :) 

The Samsung series 9 has a bigger price tag. However, from what I can see if you are deciding between the two I would dish out on the Samsung 9 only because it has features that would make it a more long term product than the Air which I can see being a more short term product you would want to change in a few years. One huge feature that sold me was the fact that you can bump the Samsung to 8GB of RAM if you like...the Air has a max of 4. 

Granted, you really don't need more than 4 for lightweight work. However, if this is something you want to keep REALLY long term (it's how I purchase my computers) then knowing you can expand to 8GB is pretty damn important. 

A few other awesome features that make it more towards a desktop replacement than a companion to an existing computer at your home: (these are also features that are not in the mac book air) 

1) USB 3.0 
2) hdmi 
3) bluetooth 3.0

Advantages that the air has over the samsung 9
1) highest resolution you can get with the 13" option is 1440 x 900
2) According to tests the air slightly beats the samsung in the battery life department


If I may just make a comment about the OS, is that if you have any interest in using Mac OS then it would be something to remember that you are supported running a dual windows / mac os on the apple hardware but not the other way around on the samsung. Just wanted to mention that in case you didn't know. 

Me personally, I'm very happy with dual booting linux/windows. 


If money weren't an issue I would take the Samsung hands down because of the awesome ports and ability to upgrade to 8GB RAM.

### Post by coleman_ian on 2011-08-08
I own the Samsung 9 series and have been running Ubuntu on it since when I bought it in April. I have not owned or had experience with the macbook air.

I recommend looking at this thread regarding the litany of problems with Samsung and Ubuntu.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1737086](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1737086)

To give you an idea of where the Samsung sits with Ubuntu compatibility, the guy who started the thread has sold his Samsung and bought a macbook air which he runs Ubuntu on. I have given my Samsung to my girlfriend with Windows put back on it after using Ubuntu on it for about 3 months. Unfortunately the Samsung just doesn't cut it, there are too many bugs for it to be properly usable.

Maybe your experience will be better and you may be more tolerant of the faults, but I would personally (based on my experience) recommend not getting the Samsung if you intend to run linux on it.

On the other side of the coin, the Samsung is an amazingly beautiful machine and it was painful for me to give it away. I loved the machine but its inability to run linux was a gamebreaker for me.
