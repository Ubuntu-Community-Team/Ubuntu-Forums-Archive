### Post by silkstone on 2008-07-30
If you've removed an existing heatsink/fan from the CPU, you need to clean both surfaces before applying a small amount of thermal paste (or thermal grease, depending on where you are) and reassembling. The best thing to use is isopropyl alcohol (IPA) which you can get from many electronics suppliers - it's a general purpose cleaning fluid that doesn't harm most plastics. Alternatively, you could try methylated spirit or lighter fluid.

### Post by Krupski on 2008-07-30
> **mikedemario17 said:**
> whats the name of that white gel you put on the CPU and whats the reason? and can i use something other then it?...im fixing up an old pc...

Buy yourself some stuff called "Silver Thermal Compound". It comes is a small syringe-like tube. You can get it at any computer store. It looks like gray vaseline.

To clean up your existing hardware, toilet paper works well. Just wipe from the edges to the center until most of the old compound is gone from the CPU and the heat sink. Then, use a clean piece to remove the rest.

Then, when you're ready to put it back together place a small ball of the silver heatsink compound in the center of the CPU.

In this case, more IS NOT better. Place a ball of compound that's about 3/16 inch diameter (imagine larger than a BB but smaller than a pencil eraser).

Don't spread it around or anything... just place the heatsink and fan assembly on top of the CPU and the pressure of the mounting hardware will spread the "stuff" where it needs to go.

Lastly... make sure your CPU fan is OK. Make sure it spins and make sure the bearings are good. When you spin the fan with your finger, it should "bounce" to a stop... a little "wiggle" just before it stops spinning.

If it just stops dead (or if it's stiff to turn) you need a new fan.

Good luck!
