There are a couple of other options if you want downloads that are up to date so that you have little updating to do the first is daily live cd.  [http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/daily-live/current/](http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/daily-live/current/)  the next are the mini install which leaves you at the CLI at restart.  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD)  I find these the best if you just want a upt o date install, but if your looking for just the main release or taking a load off the servers then I can see the use of the torrent option.

### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-10-18
Keep in mind that since your ISP blocks searches with the word torrent, it probably will also throttle your BitTorrent downloads. Take a look at [BitTorrent optimization and troubleshooting guide](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1259923) for some tips.

I also advice you to read your ISP contract, because you might be breaking the contract by downloading torrents. If there isn't any about torrents in your contract, then you can sue your ISP. Anyway, I would switch ISP if possible, because this censorship is not cool.
