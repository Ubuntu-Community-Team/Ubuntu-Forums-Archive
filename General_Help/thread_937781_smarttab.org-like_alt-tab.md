### Post by Frenziefrenz on 2008-10-04
On Windows I use [smarttab.org]("http://smarttab.org/") as an alt-tab replacement. It shows all running programs in a convenient list and as an added bonus you've got fast and easy access by all of them through numbers and letters on your keyboard [1] (see screenshot from official website). In Ubuntu/Gnome this funcionality is lacking and it's holding me back from using it more often instead of Windows. Does anybody know a way to get a **usable** task switcher in Ubuntu instead of one that just looks cool?

[IMG]http://smarttab.org/images/rcms_extendedtext_1667_1090143586.jpg[/IMG]

In Ubuntu, the default Compiz alt-tab is completely unusable (no offense to whoever made it, but it's nothing but useless eye candy to me [2]). Using the mouse and clicking an item on the taskbar is far more convenient because that way it's not a complete surprise what window is going to come up next and that's simply not good. None of the alternative Compiz alt-tab like alternatives are any good either (thumbnails in a circle tell me virtually nothing for example). I know that I can get a sort of default Windows XP-like alt-tab by disabling Compiz altogether, but but then things like dragging windows around seem to be a lot slower and I can actually see redrawing then. Sadly, even the Windows XP-like task switcher seems like a big improvement over the various options that Compiz offers.

I've managed to configure most things more or less to my tastes, but this particular aspect is really holding me back from Ubuntu/Gnome. I hope that something better merely slipped by me because of some wrong search terms.

[1] First you press alt-tab, then if you want to go to #6 immediately, or in my case I usually have more than just the 10 available numbers, after which it does the 26 letters of the alphabet, so you keep alt pressed and then type the number and you go there immediately.
[2] It's actually more usable than the default stuff in Vista, but that doesn't say much.
