### Post by Zill on 2011-12-16
> **techiewannabe said:**
> ...the problem has to do with my system freezing with any version beyond 9.04. (I thought 10.04 was working, but it ended up freezing also.)...
We all appreciate your concern to "get this problem fixed" but, unless you want to pay someone to visit your premises and fix the system, I suggest you have to be prepared to answer some questions and then try some suggestions made here to help you fix the system yourself.

Your problem may be related to hardware and/or software.  Freezing *can* be caused by bad RAM chips, or even a problem with the PC motherboard.

If the problem is software, rather than hardware such as RAM, then it may be related to upgrading from 9.04 to a later version, rather than a fresh install.  Would you please advise exactly *which* version is currently installed and exactly *how* this was achieved.  i.e. upgrade or fresh install.  Has this version *ever* run smoothly on your PC?

It will be useful if you test your RAM as explained in this [memtest86]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FaultyHardware") documentation.  Run the test for quite a while (eg. overnight) then please advise us of the results.

It may be frustrating to have to do this but it should be possible to get your system working reliably again.

ps.  You may also like to do a quick websearch to find a local Linux User Group (LUG) in your area that may be willing to help.
