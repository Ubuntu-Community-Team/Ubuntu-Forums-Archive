### Post by silkstone on 2008-08-08
It may not function at all, or it could overload the PSU. Unfortunately the power supplies that come with cases tend to be optimistically rated. Good brands like Seasonic, Corsair, etc are more dependable, but really I wouldn't trust a no-name PSU that came with the case (assuming it is a no-name PSU!).

The 450W PSU in a new PC I built recently lasted half an hour - with an Nvidia 8400GS card! It was obviously faulty, but even so I wouldn't trust a direct replacement.

### Post by silkstone on 2008-08-08
You probably don't need anything as powerful as 800W for a single video card. A good brand of 600 or 650W PSU should be plenty.

PSU failures are quite common with cheaper models, and can take out other components including the motherboard, so I'd always recommend buying a reputable make.

EDIT/ Just thinking about this again - I'm not sure how much power the cathodes consume - probably not very much, but it depends on how many you have! The LEDs won't take much. Anyway, if in doubt go for something bigger than you think you need! :)

### Post by kihjin on 2008-08-08
You might want to invest in a Kill-a-watt electricity usage monitor. It'll be the best $15-20 you've ever spent. It'll tell you exactly how much power your machine draws, or any electricity drawing device for that matter. How about your tv when it's turned off but still plugged in?

I would test the machine both in idle and heavy load. If it's idling 80% of the time, you can multiply your idle power usage by .8 and your heavy usage by 0.2, add them together and you get an estimated representive usage.
