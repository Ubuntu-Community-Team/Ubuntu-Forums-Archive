### Post by Krytarik on 2011-03-13
I had the same issue once with a video editor in Windows7. I had audio, but the video was simply white.

Apparently Flash does under specific circumstances try to draw its video onto the other screen, if you have a dual monitor setup, or even more.

In Windows7 I could fix it by switching to "clone" instead of individual outputs.
