### Post by deadflowr on 2016-06-26
You browse installed apps by going down to the bottom of the dash (Sidenote:the search function is called the dash, the launcher is the side bar on the desktop, fwiw)
and in the bottom section of the dash should be several icons, the first is the home icon and the second is the apps icon.
Click on the apps icons and there should be 2 or 3 fields to choose from.
The first would be recently used, and the second will be installed apps.(the third field is usually suggestions of apps you might like, you can ignore it if you want)
The installed apps feild can be expanded to show all apps.
Apps are listed alphabetically.
You can also use the fileters option to filter down what apps you want to view.
Note that the filters option only last the length of time the dash is open.

Hope that makes sense.

Alternatively to the icon, you can open the dash directly in the apps section by either right clicking on the dash launcher and choosing applications, or by using the keybinding "super + A".

good luck.
