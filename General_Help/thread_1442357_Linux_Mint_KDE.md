Yes, it is 100% compatible. The only differences are the multimedia system that works out of the box, more just installed applications, custom application for system administration, no-kde fanatics, little and helpful community, firefox and thunderbird by default...

About no-kde fanatics: for this I intend that they use gnome apps when works better than kde ones, like network-manager-gnome that is more complete and stable then knetworkmanager.

For me Linux Mint KDE 8 Helena is the same thing like kubuntu karmic. And I use a lot of ppa from the kubuntu community with mint.
I recommend mint-kde until lucid. ;)
