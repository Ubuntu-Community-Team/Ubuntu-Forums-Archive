### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-03-25
OK ... well basically, unless you know what you're doing, you should NOT be downloading individual packages and installing them.

The better way is to use the Software Center and/or Synaptic.  Either of those will AUTOMATICALLY identify the needed dependencies and download those packages as well.

If you're going to bypass the preferred way of doing things, you have no basis to complain that your chosen approach is harder to do or undocumented.  You chose to avoid doing it the easy way.
