### Post by dlmoak on 2008-10-31
I would be surprised if *any* Windows-based programs port to Linux.  It doesn't make sense in their business model.  I have to use a couple of programs in my business that are written exclusively for Windows (Quickbooks and an intranet package).  I installed XP in VirtualBox on my Ubuntu system for the purpose of running those programs only.  For me, the benefits of running Linux outweigh that minor bit of trouble.  And, when I save the machine state when leaving VirtualBox, I can get back into those programs quicker than I could starting them from scratch on my Windows machine.  When someone needs a document in Word or Excel format, I've had no problems reported with documents exported from OpenOffice. I can read and edit pdf documents without reported problems from the recipients.

It may not work for you, and that's fine. However, I wouldn't hold my breath waiting for companies who sell Windows-based software to decide they want to be open source.

### Post by dlmoak on 2008-11-01
My work does not require that I stay in Quickbooks all day.  Most days I use it for no more than 1.5 - 2 hours.  Some days are heavier depending upon what is going on, but I would say never more than 4 hours.

The advantages are as follows:
1. The rest of the time I am in Ubuntu, which operates much faster for me than XP.  I use OpenOffice and other apps for the rest of my work.  I only access the internet from Ubuntu except for the required connection for Quickbooks (I never open Internet Explorer and check email only from Ubuntu).

2. Windows, running within VirtualBox, within Ubuntu, runs much faster for me because it is as minimal an installation as I can install.  The only Windows-based program installed is Quicken (except for the intranet app I mentioned, which has its own browser.  I am in THAT program no more than 1/2 hour a day).  I am not using Windows antivirus protection.  I have been doing this for 6 months and have had no viruses, malware, etc., etc.

3. When I need to get in Quickbooks, I access the program from its saved state and am right back where I was when I last left.  It takes about 20 seconds.  When I ran only Windows on my machine, Quickbooks would take about 2 minutes to open, which meant I left it on all day to avoid irritation.  Windows is completely unaware that it is running under VirtualBox - I can print, backup, etc. and if I need to flip back to a Linux app or the Internet I can do so without shutting down Windows.

4.  So while I do have a legal version of Windows XP that I've bought, I do not have to buy any other software (except Quickbooks).

5.  I'm running both systems on a 5 year-old, 1.8 gig Pentium with 1 Gig of ram and an 80 Gig hard disk.

I'm trying to figure out a way to run Quickbooks under Linux without VirtualBox and XP, but don't expect to crack that nut with current versions of Ubuntu, Quickbooks and VirtualBox.

As for your second point, you are absolutely correct.  Quickbooks could make a linux version and sell it.  I wish they would.
