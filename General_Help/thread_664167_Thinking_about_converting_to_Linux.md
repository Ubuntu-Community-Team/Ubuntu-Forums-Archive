### Post by bodycoach2 on 2008-01-11
At Free Geek Central Florida, most of the machines we get in fall between 500 MHz and 1Ghz. We get a few in better than that, but not many right now. 

We use Xubuntu on 90% of our installations. We make sure they have at least 256mb ram, and at least a 15 gb hard drive. When possible, and if necessary, we make sure a wireless solution comes with it. Ubuntu lags a bit on that type of hardware, but Xubuntu works just fine.

These computers go out to people who have rarely, if ever, used a computer. With 30 minutes of training, they are off and running. I've found that for the average user, there is little difference between Xubuntu and Ubuntu as far as usability.

I would start with Xubuntu, and upgrade to Ubuntu if you want to bother trying it.
