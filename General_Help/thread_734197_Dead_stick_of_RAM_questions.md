### Post by Therion on 2008-03-24
> **drbob07 said:**
> If I buy another 1GB stick of DDR2 800, from a different manufacturer, will it work in dual-channel operation, or will the 2 sticks just act independently from each other. (In other words do I **have** to buy a paired kit to get dual-channel functionality, or do I have to buy from the same manufacturer? (Which I probably won't be doing any time soon)
You don't need to use one of those "matched pair" kits for dual-channel RAM configs.  Convenient, yes; required, no.  As long as the memory timings aren't wildly out of synch between the two modules, you'll be fine.  Your best bet would be to try and get the make/model number off the existing RAM module and match it up though, if you can, with the new one.  I mean, lets not tempt fate if we don't have to.
