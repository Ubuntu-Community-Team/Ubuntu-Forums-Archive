### Post by BathroomNinja on 2005-12-21
A+ is a good start to get you a tech support job.  Back in the late 90's, I made it quite far with only my experience.  But after the dot com bust, people started looking for more.   I HIGHLY recomend that you work a tech support job, even part time, while you get a degree.  Work on getting your MCSE and CCNA.  The MCSE is pretty easy to get, but a lot of companies get discounts from Microsoft if they have MCSE's working for them.

If you are able to afford college on your own, that's probably your best bet.
I used the Marine Corps to pay for my education, but I'm a crazy idiot that likes getting shot at.  

Bottom line; If you have the Trifecta of a degree, experience and certifications, you become a very sought after person.  If you have a good personality, you're almost a sure bet.

EDIT- haha, I almost forgot.  Keep working with linux as much as you can!  Learn a few langs like Java, C++, etc...  Last but not least, figure out what you want to do and focus on that.  As a side note, I got my A+ when I was 15 as well, and started working at Dell at 17, going down the same path you are taking.  My biggest mistake was note getting my degree right out of highschool.  A Degree is very important.  I can't stress that enough.
