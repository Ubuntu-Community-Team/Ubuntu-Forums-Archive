### Post by Spyderkid on 2011-02-23
Ever wanted to make your desktop look like windows 7?
maybe not...
but here's how to anyway...

1) [download the pack click here]("http://gnome-look.org/content/download.php?content=113264&id=1&tan=86769970&PHPSESSID=b2eb1416de4b69fd2c0d77f458bc32cb")

2)move it to your home-folder.

3)extract it (by right clicking and choosing extract)

4)open the folder just created and click on install.sh file and choose run in terminal

5)folow installation instructions. install everything to make sure you get the perfect windows 7 look.

6) logg out and logg back in again.

7)right click on the panel at the bottem towards the right middle and choose properties, then go to background and click the background image circle and choose the style you want. the panel images should be in
_Filesystem > usr > share > themes > Win2-7(Pixmap) > gtk2.0 > Panel_

8 )Then right click on the panel and click "add to panel" and select Gnomenu

9)Then right click on the new icon that appeared on the bar and right click on it and choose properties. Then choose...

for menu, win2-7Basic

for button, the win2-7 orb.

for icon, Win7_icons_1.1




10)read the CompizReadMe file on the desktop, do what it says.

11)press Alt and F2 at the same time, into the boox write ccsm and press enter

12)then in the window that pops up scroll down to Window Decoration under effects click on the icon, then click enable window decoration on the side, then click on the box taht says command next to it. type in /usr/bin/emerald

13) go to system > preferences > emerald theme manager and select a theme for the borders.

**finished!**



NOTE:
If the window borders ever seem to dissapear, then press Alt F2 and type *emerald --replace* and your sorted.







this will work for most major types of linux. For more details click the link below
