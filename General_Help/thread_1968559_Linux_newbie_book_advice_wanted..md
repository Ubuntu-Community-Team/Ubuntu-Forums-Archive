### Post by hal8000 on 2012-04-29
This is possibly the best advice anyone can ever give you:

Your library.
The computer section always contains a large selection of books on linux and this way, you can read them at home, because what is right for one person may not be right for another; in other words, they may be too easy or too dificult.

The problem with linux is that it is advancing at such a fast pace. The books att your library may refer to kernel 2.4 or even 2.6 kernels. Currently Ubuntu 12.04 is running 3.2 and there are other changes.

The X window system now uses kms (kernel mode settings) so its no longer necessary to write or use an /etc/X11/xorg.conf file.

Also have a look in WHSmith. Linux Format, Linux Magazine and Ubuntu User are available and often books.magazines on Ebay.

I learnt by reading "Using Linux" by Bill Ball, but this was back in 1999 in the days of kernel 2.2. Even in 1999 Linux was still ahead of development, stability and usuability than anything microsoft could produce an still is.

Hope that helps.
