### Post by snowpine on 2010-08-12
Ubuntu's policy on Firefox just changed; it is too early to tell what will happen with FF4.

In olden times, Firefox was "frozen" so 6.06 used FF1.5 for its entire lifecycle; 8.04 was scheduled to stick with FF3.0 too.

However, Mozilla changed their support policies, FF3.0 support is ending, so Ubuntu was forced to "backport" FF3.6 to Hardy for security purposes.

If FF3.6 support ends while 10.04 is still active, they will be forced to backport FF4 I would imagine. But it is too soon to tell whether that will happen.

Of course, you are free to install any version of FF that you want whenever you want; the above only refers to security updates. :)
