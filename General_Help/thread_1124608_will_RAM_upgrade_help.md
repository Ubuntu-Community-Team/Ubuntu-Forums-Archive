### Post by Dakiraun on 2009-04-13
1 gig will suffice for running Linux with Gnome, though if the hard drive is only 4200rpm, that might make for long load times.  True, new machines are cheap, but a quality laptop will still run you over $1000 US if you stock it up right.

My suggestion though would be to put the upgrade cash towards a new(er) machine.  Even if you get your existing one to 1g of RAM, and slap in a newer, faster hard drive (and you will very likely be limited as to the size you can install), it still won't be the performer a newer laptop would be, and you have no reached your configuration limits with the box.

A happy and good medium here might be to look for a slightly-used laptop that was, say 2 years ago, a workstation-class business machine.  You can pick those up now up for around $500 to $800, and they're very nice machines with Core2 Duo CPUs or Turion X2s, take 2 to 4g RAM (as their max) and have SATA drives.  Much more expandable, still very affordable.
