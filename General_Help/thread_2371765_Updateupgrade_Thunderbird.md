### Post by ajgreeny on 2017-09-18
12.04 has now become End-Of-Life, EOL, so will get no more updates of any kind, not just for thunderbird, and thus is a huge security risk.

You should update to a supported version; I recommend 16.04.3, which is the latest long term support version, supported for 5 years, ie, until April 2021.

### Post by &amp;KyT$0P# on 2017-09-18
> **PDA1 said:**
> See what I'm talking about, 
I don't.  I used gtkpod in Ubuntu 16.04 just recently, and it worked well.

> Do you have any suggestions or ideas around this problem?
You already use VirtualBox, so try 16.04 in a virtual machine and set it up like you would your real machine.  That'll show you most of the compatibility difficulties you'll encounter.  Plus, if you take snapshots as you go, the VM is a nice disposable environment where you can solve those difficulties with no fear of screwing up.

Since you have older hardware, you might consider Xubuntu or Lubuntu 16.04 instead of Ubuntu.

Once your 16.04 VM is good to go, try a live CD/DVD/USB of 16.04 and see if you encounter any additional problems.  You may want to **back up your entire system** before trying this step, especially if you feel ready to install.
