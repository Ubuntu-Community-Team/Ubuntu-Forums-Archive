### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-01-10
Every email application will let you leave mail on the servers, but you will need to set that option in the mail application's "Accounts setup" system, and how you do that depends on the application.  If by "bounce spam" you mean send it back to where it came from, I strongly suggest you don't do that, but by a system of filters and rules you send it to a spam folder, and then check it every so often and delete the real spam.

There is no point sending anything back to its source.  The sender will not know it is back and will not act on it even if they did, and it just is more un-necessary mail in the system.
