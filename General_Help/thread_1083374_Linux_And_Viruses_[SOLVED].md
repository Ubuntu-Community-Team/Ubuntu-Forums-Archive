### Post by -kg- on 2009-02-28
To my understanding, to date there are very, very few virus written for Linux, and most of those are written to infect server computers, and the majority of those are very out-of-date and have been patched against long ago.

As far as personally (i.e., _your_ computer), as long as you don't override the inability to log in as superuser and always use sudo or gksu for your administrative tasks, you have an exceedingly small chance (to none) of being infected by a virus.  Yes, new virus can be written, but you have to "invite them in" for them to be installed, and the only way they can install themselves is if you give them permission.

The only other reason to install antivirus is so you don't pass them on to other Windows users, like your friends.  Just because you can't get infected doesn't mean that you can't pass those virus to others.

This is for now.  Of course, there are a lot of very smart people out there with nefarious agenda, and one day someone might come up with a solution to infect Linux with a virus in spite of all the security a Linux system offers.
