### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-09-06
My understanding is that you get that message (kernel panic) on startup, and if you reboot you can successfully get into Ubuntu without the kernel panic.

1. Are you overclocking? If so, put your CPU back to its normal settings, or decrease the overclock a bit. Or try increasing the voltage to the CPU a little.

2. Check your RAM. Check it the old-fashioned way, by taking some out and seeing if the problem still occurs.

3. Check your power supply, it might be getting old and not providing enough power for startup until it's been running for a while.
