### Post by x1a4 on 2007-11-06
Use an AMD processor on a main board that supports LinuxBIOS--Tyan makes good ones at a decent price as does Gigabyte, and for decent quality at an even lower price MSI.  
Nvidia video, or for less money an MSI board with a Nvidia GPU. 

Once you have the main board, processor and video the rest should be a breeze.  

For hard drive(s) I recommend a Maxtor.  Yes I know Maxtor tends to have a bad rep but I've been using Maxtor drives since the early 1990's and never had any problems.  As a matter of fact I still have my first 350MB Maxtor drive and it works.  Maxtor's prices aren't bad either.

Just make sure the motherboard is not too new (potential problems with kernel support) and that you get something with good Linux video support (like Nvidia).

If you go with DDR2 RAM, make sure that you get TWO matched modules (instead of one bigger one) because the hardware should be able to use that configuration to basically double memory I/O speed.
