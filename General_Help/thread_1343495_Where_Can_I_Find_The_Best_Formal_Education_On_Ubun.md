### Post by winh8r on 2009-12-02
I am not entirely sure if you mean "Free" or "Paid For" education.
If you are looking for paid for professional training and support then Canonical are the people to speak to
```
http://www.canonical.com/services
```

As far as "free" formal education goes, I can only speak from personal experience and say that I have learnt more from the members on the Ubuntu Forums than any one person or book could have taught me.
The very act of searching for a solution to your "headaches" often provides you with further information and prevents future headaches.

Linux is free,Ubuntu is free, Learning is free, the only thing it will cost you is a little time and effort.

I assume that you are now fairly comfortable with Ubuntu and are managing to get around the issues you have experienced. Might I be so bold as to suggest that you join a local Linux Users Group or if there isn't one near you, contact the next nearest one and start a group in your own area.
That way you will not only get knowledge from other users but you will also be providing a community resource to help others who are struggling to get to grips with Linux in general or Ubuntu in particular.

If you take a good look around the Ubuntu Forums , you will see a fair few posts from people who really just want someone to "tell them how to do it" or "tell them how to make it behave like W*ndows"

People who want to learn, will learn regardless of how formalised their education process is.

I know for a fact that there are sysadmins,programmers,developers and teachers using these forums and all that I have encountered have been excellent in offering concise and easy to interpret advice and instruction to those in need of it. What the receiver of this advice does with it is key to their further advancement in understanding how and why their system works.
I understand why you are asking this question, but in response I would say that you have already found the best method of becoming educated in the ways of Ubuntu.

Sorry to ramble on, I just think that in todays world the act of going to a physical location and being formally educated is largely pointless as you are only receiving one persons take on how best to understand the subject.In a cyber place like the Ubuntu Forums there are often discussions about how best to do something and what can go wrong in the process depending on your hardware configuration or software conflicts.

May I congratulate you on posting an extremely thought provoking question, which has led me to another question.
What would happen to M*crosofts domination of the world if all schools,colleges and universities started adopting an "equal opportunities " policy when it came to teaching students about computers?

Thanks for your question and again I apologise for such a long winded answer.
