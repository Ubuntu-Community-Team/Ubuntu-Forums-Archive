### Post by aysiu on 2006-01-07
Gtkpod, as far as I know, isn't a substitute for iTunes--it just helps you load songs on your iPod and organize playlists.

For full iTunes functionality, you usually need four separate programs in Linux:
1. a CD ripper (like Goobox or KAudiocreator)
2. a music organizer/player (like Banshee or AmaroK)
3. a music store (Sharpmusique)
4. an iPod organizer (Gtkpod)

Have you considered running iTunes with Crossover Office?

Generally, Gtkpod works pretty well with a version behind the most current iPod. So if you have a G3 iPod, you're cool. If you have a Nano or Video iPod... you may be hurting.
