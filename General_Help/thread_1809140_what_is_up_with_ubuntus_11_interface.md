### Post by Idefix82 on 2011-07-21
When you log in, you can choose what desktop to log into. After clicking on your name, choose in the drop down menu at the very bottom of the screen "Ubuntu classic" instead of "Ubuntu".

But I recommend taking 10 minutes to explore the new desktop. Once you get used to it, it becomes pretty cool.

### Post by jim_deadlock on 2011-07-21
When you get to the login screen there is a dropdown menu at the bottom where you can select 'Ubuntu Classic' which will give you back your old Gnome desktop.

If you have auto-login you'll have to temporarily change it via your 'Login Screen' app.

### Post by Vaphell on 2011-07-21
> I guess my question is why did they change it?

I actually prefer the fedora/RH /CentOS interface but I am using this on my laptop so I like Ubuntu for a few things it has for the Laptop environment.

they changed it for 2 reasons:
- gnome2 used up to 10.10 is obsoleted, gnome devs work on gnome3
- ubuntu people have their vision of ubuntu desktop

also new fedora uses new gnome 3 interface.

unity is not without problems but it should be quite ok for laptops. 1st of all - you are starved for vertical space (most laptops come with 768px vertical, downgrade from 800, downgrade from old 4:3 displays) but not so much for horizontal space (1366px when web pages are usually tailored for 1024) so bar on the side makes sense. Also unity allows to easily navigate with keyboard so you don't have to play with touchpads (i hate them so much). Win+number runs apps from launcher bar, win alone opens dash where few chars typed find what you need.
I suggest you sacrifice half an hour to read about unity workflow and shortcuts, you may like it.
