### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-06-28
Sorry, but basically -- NO.  

I have a tablet PC and, for a long time, used Windows Journal, together with OneNote.

When I dual-booted it with Ubuntu, I found Tomboy, BasKets, and CellWriter.  The second replaces much of the notes features in OneNote; the third provides a way of entering characters -- one at a time.

One of the main reasons I stopped using Ubuntu on the Tablet is that there was, and as far as I know, still IS, no replacement for Windows Journal, at least, from the handwriting standpoint.

There may be apps that allow hand drawing, but I didn't look into those.
