### Post by kenan6346 on 2007-12-18
I used Kubuntu for a while, but then went back to Ubuntu because I didn't really like it that much (plus I've got a relatively low screen resolution and KDE sorta needs a resolution larger than 1024x768). But the one thing I did like about it was one click instead of a double-click to open, for example, a folder or program. Does anyone know how to do this in regular Gnome Ubuntu and also reverse it if I decide to go back to double click for whatever reason?
Cheers.

---

### Post by wharp on 2007-12-18
A quick search of google turns up the following:

Open Nautilus.  Click on Edit>> Preferences>> Behavior

That should be what you're looking for.

---

### Post by Chainz on 2007-12-18
There are two places to set it up.
One is in preferences of your file manager, for instance Nautilus.
Second is in mouse preferences.
