### Post by beboylips on 2012-05-20
Only Prescott, Cedar Mill and Celeron D with socket ** LGA775 **. 

Motherboards work by socket types, not necessary by processor names. Intel decided to upgrade their sockets from P4 Northwood to Prescrott, that's why they are not compatible.

### Post by Revolutionary101 on 2012-05-21
> **Shadius said:**
> Thank you so much for helping me with this. :) I was getting really confused with all these processors. Now I know where to begin my search for a processor to go with this motherboard. 

Question: I always thought Pentium was better than Celeron, or it was newer than Celeron, am I wrong?

Celeron D is newer than Pentium 4. That being said not all Celerons (non D series) are newer than Pentium 4s. The Celeron line started wayyy back in 1998 but has undergone several revisions. When looking for a new processor you have to use take it on a case by case basis. You can't overgeneralize and believe that all processors in one line are better than another, technology just moves forward too quickly for the naming schemes to keep up haha.

Note: If you are interested in seeing the progression with these processor lines, you can see the release dates on the right hand side of the CPU row. It is kinda interesting to see how these have improved over the years.
