### Post by sgosnell on 2010-12-26
Almost all printer manufacturers use the Gillette model - give the razor away very cheaply, or even free, and make the money on the blades.  In this case, on the cartridges.  You can get refill ink fairly cheaply, though.  I've found that Lexmark is rather extreme in this area, and at least in the past, you could buy a new printer with cartridges installed cheaper than you could buy just the cartridges.  The printer is truly disposable.  

HP and Brother provide excellent Linux support, and almost all of their printers will work with Linux, and the few that don't work immediately will work very quickly as drivers become available.  Other brands can be hit or miss.
