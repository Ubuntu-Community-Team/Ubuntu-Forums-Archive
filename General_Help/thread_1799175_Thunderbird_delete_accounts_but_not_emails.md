### Post by nomko on 2011-07-07
What i did was moving my profile to an external drive. So this gives me the advantage of having everything stored (emails) on 1 location. Very handy when i upgrade from an older Ubuntu version to a newer version without loosing my mails. 
 
When i add my mail account the only thing to do is modify the path from local to the external location. After restarting Thunderbird i have all my mails.
 
What you need to do is copying (or moving) your folders under your profile to another location. Then modify the path in the account settings and restart Thunderbird. When you have to re-install Ubuntu or upgrade Ubuntu you won't loose your emails.
 
When you modify your ISP info, there's no need of copying/moving stuff. The change will be done in your own profile, no other profile will be created.
