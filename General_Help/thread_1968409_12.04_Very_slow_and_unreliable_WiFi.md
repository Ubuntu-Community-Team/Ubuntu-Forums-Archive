### Post by jespdj on 2012-04-29
Yesterday I've updated my laptop and desktop computers to Precise Pangolin. I did a clean install on both On the laptop, it works wonderfully, no problems at all.

On the desktop it was much harder (probably some problem with the graphics) but finally I got it installed.

However, the Internet connection on the desktop via WiFi is very slow and unreliable. After every few seconds or tens of seconds it just stops working; it doesn't disconnect, but just stops downloading and uploading data. I have to manually disconnect and reconnect and then it works again for a few tens of seconds.

The speed is also much too low. I used [www.speedtest.net](www.speedtest.net) to check download and upload speeds.

Laptop (Ubuntu 12.04): almost 50 Mbit/s download, 4.8 Mbit/s upload
Desktop (Ubuntu 12.04): **only 1 Mbit/s download, 1 MBit/s upload**
Desktop (Windows 7): about 20 Mbit/s download, 4.6 MBit/s upload

My Internet connection is 50 Mbit/s down, 5 Mbit/s up. So on the desktop, it's also much slower than it should be in Windows (20 Mbit/s) but in Ubuntu it's almost nothing, just 1 Mbit/s.

What can I do to find out what the cause of this problem is?

I'll have to go back to Oneiric if I can't fix this...
