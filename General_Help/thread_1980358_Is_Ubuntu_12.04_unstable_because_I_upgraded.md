### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-05-15
Most folks strongly recommend a fresh installation instead of an upgrade! It's cool that it worked once, but not surprising that it didn't work the next time.

I suggest a thorough backup of all your data, and a nice, clean, fresh install without the leftover configuration files and stuff that can cause bugs and little bothersome stuff.

I have read that upgrades are only fully successful and bug-free about half the time. Fresh install is always the way to go! And another reason to stick with a version for it's entire support life rather than feel compelled to upgrade every 6 months.

If you are a software enthusiast then try the releases as soon as they bring forth. But instead if you use Ubuntu as your primary OS and does some very serious work, then I would advice you to one of the releases as long as their support lasts, preferably LTS(12.04 LTS).
