### Post by oldfred on 2019-07-09
I have not purchased USB2 flash drives for years. Even with my old BIOS only system with USB2 ports I found a newer USB3 flash drive to be 10% quicker. And then in 2014 when I built newer system with USB3 ports  I already had several USB3 flash drives.

My problem is Microcenter is only a couple of miles away. And flash drives are behind checkout counter. Each time I would go in (try not too often) flash drives were lower in cost, or same cost larger capacity or then USB3 was only $1 more, so only purchased USB3.

But flash drives are slow. And some brands are better than others.
And use of Windows formats is slower than Linux formats, but you need settings (noatime) to reduce writes on reads to improve reading performance.

I can install Ubuntu to SSD from RAM in under 10 minutes, but it is over 40 minutes to my USB3 flash drive. I used to think part of issue was USB port. But I installed to an external SSD on USB3 port in just over 10 Minutes.
