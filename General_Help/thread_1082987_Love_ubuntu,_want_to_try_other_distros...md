### Post by Rinzwind on 2009-02-28
Install Virtualbox in your Ubuntu install.

Install Suse or Cent OS (=Redhat) inside VirtualBox and you can have a look at how these 2 work. Mind you: when you look at Gnome/KDE it will look the 'same' as Ubuntu. Biggest difference is the package manager. 

- No need for partitioning; so no riscs in deleting any of your other OS.

### Post by Simian Man on 2009-02-28
Definitely use VirtualBox.  As far as I know no other distro provides something like Wubi (it isn't really the best for day to day use).  Trying to create a triple-boot and up can get fairly complicated.

With Virtualbox you can screw around to your heart's content.  As for distros to try:
[LIST]
[*]Fedora for new technologies that will come to other distros.
[*]Mandriva or OpenSuse for nice KDE integration
[*]Gentoo or Linux from Scratch for trying trickier installations
[*]Zenwalk, Foresight, Crux or Arch for minimalism
[*]A BSD for something totally different
[/LIST]
