### Post by kaldor on 2010-05-28
In my opinion, monitoring kids at younger ages could be a bad thing; would you like it if your kids were listening in on your phone calls without your knowledge?

If you *really* feel the need to do this, try VNC; it's not the same as what you mentioned, but you can use it to view what's on their screen and even take over without them knowing. There's a panel applet called "remote desktop viewer" which allows this. Not sure of the name, but it's along those lines. Just get the IP of the desktop they use and use that panel applet to connect. Of course, set up the desktop Ubuntu to allow incoming connections from you :)

This is what schools in the area use to monitor activity.

Edit:

To elaborate on what I mentioned earlier...

Kids want a way they can escape from their parents constant watching when they are at home. Computers can be abused, but if your kids are smart they'll learn more about life and how to better figure out how to deal with things. Personally, when I was younger using the computer when I was 8-12, I absolutely HATED having parents try to monitor me. It made me feel like I wasn't being trusted and like I was unable to do certain things out of fear my parents would disapprove of it (no, not porn). 

My girlfriend's mother is a helicopter parent who tried (and still tries) to monitor every aspect of her life. She's 19 now, and her mother goes into her bedroom every time my girlfriend leaves and goes through all of her things. When her brother gave her a laptop for christmas, she asked me why things were becoming so slow. I looked at it, and her brother was secretly monitoring and remotely connection to her laptop and sending reports to her mother. My girlfriend was unable to use the computer until she was around 17, and even then she was only allowed 1 hour a day. It causes immense amount of stress between the two, she isn't even allowed to ride a bike down the road (last time she tried to ride a bike, her mother hopped in the car and chased after her shouting for her to come home). On top of it, her mother goes around telling friends/family everything that she finds in y girlfriend's room, including personal stuff that shouldn't even be mentioned. She is trying her best to move out by getting work and a job, but even that is proving difficult because her mother doesn't want her going to a higher education because it's "too much" for a girl. 

That's the extreme end of things, but even small measures of monitoring can cause problems. That's just my opinion though, and I don't know you or your children. For all I know, they may truly NEED monitoring :)
