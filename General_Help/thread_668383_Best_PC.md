### Post by BruisedQuasar07 on 2008-01-15
I find older PCs are best with Linux distros in general; state of art today PCs 
provide worst headache. You mentioned you are a newbie of sorts. 

My advice is stick with a PC that is very popular such as a fast single processor
Dell or IBM. If you got quad core, expect headaches. Linux distro auto detect
hardware utilities function better on PCs that are about two or more years past hardware PCs.

If you have your eye on a particular system, I would use search in these forums
for that PC and see what you come up with. There are a few manufacturers
who support Linux. Dell is one of them. You can call Dell tech and find out which
systems are their highly compatible Linux systems. Dell favors Ubuntu by the way.
