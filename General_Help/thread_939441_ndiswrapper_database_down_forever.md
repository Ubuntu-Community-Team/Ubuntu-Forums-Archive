### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-10-05
ndiswrapper used to have an immensely useful database on its website containing a list of wireless card names, PCI IDs and the Windows drivers (often with links to download locations for them) that people used to get their cards running under ndiswrapper.  This database has been broken I think for at least a couple of weeks--it just returns a blank page when you try to access it, e.g. [this]("http://ndiswrapper.sourceforge.net/joomla/index.php?/component/option,com_openwiki/Itemid,33/id,list_a/").

I copied Google's cache of the database pages onto my personal site so that they'll still be accessible even if the ndiswrapper people never fix their site.  The database mirror can be accessed [here]("http://burnthesorbonne.com/?page_id=32").

I'm still interested, however, in finding out if anyone knows any more about this.  Is the ndiswrapper project going to fix this?  Their site doesn't seem to contain much more than unanswered forum posts regarding the problem.  I know that ndiswrapper is becoming less and less necessary as native Linux wireless drivers improve, but some people still need ndiswrapper, and that database was invaluable for helping new users get their cards working...
