### Post by Mike_Andrews on 2016-12-01
Quite a few Linux users are experiencing difficulties when their ubuntu-software starts loading a blank, with the error dialog, 'no packages found,' or similar.

After reading of all the suggested fixes and cures that have been posted and even writing my own suggestions in a post, I now come full circle, back to square A with a different approach.

IMO this is something that points to a problem within the software distribution community, and as I have learned, is NOT necessarily related to a glitch within the Ubuntu-Linux OS.

Interestingly, I've discovered that the fix for this perplexing little foible is much simpler than previously noted.

Here's all you need do if ubuntu-software stops loading on your machine:

First off, it's a SERVER issue that has little to do with personal computers. So when/if ubuntu-software loads a blank, click System Settings> Software & Updates> Download from> Other and MANUALLY select a new server located within your country of residence.

DON'T opt for the 'Select Best Server' option, as you may very well end up right back where you started from.

After the selection is made and you click 'Close,' a popup appears to advise that your cache is outdated. Allow the reload to run, which usually takes 30-40 seconds, and when the window disappears reboot the machine.

Back at Desktop again, click on the orange shopping bag icon for Ubuntu Software and see if you don't have your access restored.

You can go back later to the main server and successfully reconnect with the pipe that services Ubuntu Software, even though you were previously 'dropped.'

It appears in lieu of the foregoing that a time criteria exists within the narrow range of that server array dedicated to ubuntu-software, so that after X-number of days the connection to your machine may be dropped.

If the above is correct. . . and I know that it is, then it's a waste of time playing around in Terminal trying to code your way back to access.

Just humor me, okay? Try it as laid out for you above and see what you come up with.

. . . What have you got to lose?
