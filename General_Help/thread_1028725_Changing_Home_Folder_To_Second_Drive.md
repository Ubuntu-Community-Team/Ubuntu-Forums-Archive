Oh no...don't reinstall; that would be a pain for something that is easily fixed.  What it sounds like is that either the "/Data" drive isn't mounted on startup, or alternatively it isn't mounted directly off of the "/" partition.  Both are possible; if it is being mounted at boot, it's probably listed as /media/Data or something along those lines.

I may be able to help you though;  could you upload the contents of your /etc/fstab file, as well as the results of this command:

ls /dev/?d*

(The fstab file instructs your system on which drives are to be mounted on startup and where they are located.  The ls command displays which drives your Ubuntu installation has detected).  Knowing these will help in determining the best way to mount that drive as your /home partition.)

--Alterax
