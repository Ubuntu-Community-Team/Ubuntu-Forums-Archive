### Post by Flyers2391 on 2010-05-07
> **rmcellig said:**
> On my Mac, when I press F2, I am able to take a screen shot of a selected area. Is it possible to assign a hot key to do the same thing on my Ubuntu 10.04 PC?

I assigned "super" (windows key) this role via CompizConfig Settings Manager.  If you don't have it already installed you can add it from the software center and then launch it from System > Preferences.

In there, Extras > Screenshot.  Mine is set at <Super>Button1 so I can left click and drag the area I want.

I was assuming you weren't using a mac ... if you do you can map that button to something else if you want.  "Super" is between ctrl and alt on "PC" keyboards.  

Either way, choose the key you want (F2 perhaps) by clicking the button in the screen shot you took and choosing from there.  Then, hold that button down until you start to drag, once you drag the area you want, let go and the screen shot area should be right on your desktop.
