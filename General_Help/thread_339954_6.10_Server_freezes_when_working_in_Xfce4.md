### Post by Matt Yun on 2007-02-14
I had a similar problem with my dual-PIII system with Dapper, with symptoms exactly the same as you describe.  The system would hang in under 5 min. if I tried to do *anything* with it (incl. ssh'ing into it).   However, my system ran perfectly with non-SMP kernels.

So I recompiled my kernel from vanilla kernel.org sources, with SMP enabled and the evms patch applied.  I posted my efforts here:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=262735](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=262735)

Compiling from Ubuntu kernel sources did not fix the problem, so I suspect that the SMP bug is due to one of the Ubuntu performance patches.

Your original video driver is probably not the source of your problem.  My system still hung with the default Ubuntu SMP kernels even when I tried booting into runlevel 3 (console only).  Using the closed-source fglrx driver may inadvertently introduce further bugginess to the system (as evidenced by the countless fglrx threads in various linux support forums).  

I suggest you go back to your original video setup and try my approach.  If you don't have a backup you can revert to, you will have to do the 'dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg' method of re-detecting and re-configuring your xorg.conf.

Since compiling my own SMP kernel, I have not had any problems and have been enjoying dual CPU goodness.  My current uptime is 92 days.
