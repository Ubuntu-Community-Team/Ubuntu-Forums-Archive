### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-09-20
Slightly off-track, but in the interest of saving you money and all of us the environment ... Black and white printer? If so, if you're buying fresh ink cartridges everytime you run out, you are using the wrong printer or are getting ripped off ... or both.

I use a Brother black and white duplex printer and get all my ink refills from a place called Tonerstop here in Australia. You don't replace the cartridge, you fill it up! Five refills cost me about AU$40 (about $35ish or less US$). Have a look around Tonerstop and you'll find some that can be refilled. Have a look for your printers. You might get lucky. 

Refilling the cartridge is an easy job once you've done it once.

That covers you if you are in Australia and are running a black and white. I would be willing to bet the house that there are similar stores worldwide. Ignore what many manufacturers say about 'you must buy a new cartridge'; many cartridges can be filled. Many smaller capacity 'starter' cartridges that come with lots of new printers can also be turned into larger capacity ones by the addition of a small piece of plastic and a spring that comes with the refill. Or you can fork out even more cash for the larger capacity one (which will be the same as what you've got probably, with the bit of plastic and spring added!). 

That they all use different ink is a good ploy to get you to buy an OEM ink made by the printer manufacturer. Many HPs and Brothers use the same ink, slightly different cartridges. This marketing ploy is environmentally unsound and shows the manufacturers have little conscience in that regard. They are willing to sell you another big chunk of plastic, potential landfill rather than tell you you can fill up the one you already have. Unethical! ;)

PS: From what I've seen around here, Epson seem to work, but I don't know anything about whether their ink cartridges can be replenished.

### Post by efflandt on 2017-09-23
Other than possibly an HP printer, look for printers that do postscript since that was the Unix way to print long ago. I don't think they are still current models, but when our HP network printer at work died, I got a Lexmark X205n b&w laser all-in-one ($150 US) to temporarily substitute for it, all I had to do was set it to the IP address that the HP printer was using, and it simply worked for local Windows PC's and printing from our main HP3000 at our factory 1700 miles away (via VPN) without having to change any drivers on anything. The Lexmark X205n does postscript and HP PCL @ 1200 dpi, so it simply worked. It is at home now and Ubuntu uses postscript for it. Its scanner driver works in 64-bit 14.04 and 32-bit version works in 32-bit 16.04, but the 64-bit driver does not seem to work in 16.04 or newer (maybe due to change in init scripts). So I currently use Ubuntu 14.04 in virtualbox to scan.

When I got a Lexmark C543dn color laser duplex printer, it worked fine in Ubuntu using a C534 driver before Ubuntu had a C543 driver. It likewise does postscript and HP PCL, so it simply worked.

Many years ago before I used cups, I needed to print a 26 page PDF file at a Kinkos Copy Center (now FedEx Office) while out of town. I could not find any printcap info for their Xerox printer (which I think was actually a parallel port rather than network), so I simply set one up on my laptop for "generic level 2 postscript" and it worked great. They spiral bound the manual and I had it to use for a startup and to give to the customer.
