### Post by mgmiller on 2006-10-05
I can echo the sentiment for my canon i960 printer which I finally got working with Turboprint, but that is not the question here.  The question is about digital cameras.  I have a Canon SD550 (7.1 megapixel) that I love.  Carry it everywhere.  It is plug and play in Ubuntu Dapper.  No drivers, no nothing, just plug it in and it works.  Gthumb recognizes it and allows you to import and then properly delete the pics from your camera.  No problems, no issues.  It is not mounted as a mass storage device, so there is no icon on the desktop.  It is purely handled by gthumb.  Also, Picassa linux sees it and can also import/delete without trouble.  Hope that helps.
