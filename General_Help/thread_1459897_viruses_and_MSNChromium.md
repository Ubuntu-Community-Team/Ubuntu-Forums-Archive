### Post by Ryan Dwyer on 2010-04-22
Anyone who tries to send her malware over MSN will send malware designed for Windows, which will not work on Ubuntu. So you don't need to worry about viruses or malware.

You don't need to worry about the firewall settings - the defaults are OK.

The phising and malware protection options will talk to Google (I think?) each time you visit a site to find out if it's been flagged as bad. If so, it displays a warning. For example, if someone sets up a fake banking login screen and manages to make you browse to it, having this option enabled would make a warning appear so you don't accidentally give them your banking details.
