### Post by chilinski on 2008-04-14
Acrobat 8 Pro now allows people making forms such as this to extend rights to Acrobat Reader. It's a simple two-click process on Pro to make a form so it can be saved with Reader. It also allows you to use digital signatures on an enabled form. 

I run Acrobat Reader 8 on an EEEPC that has a lot less power than what you have, and it runs perfectly (under Gutsy). 

I know this doesn't help you because the enabling has to be done on the form-generating end. Perhaps you could let them know they might find advantages to making their forms rights enabled under Reader.
