### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-05-03
Overclocking will ALWAYS cause the processor to run hotter -- which is generally OK in a desktop PC where you can add a powerful aftermarket cooler, or water cooling, to dissipate the extra heat.

Overclocking on a laptop is ASKING to burn out your CPU!

Is it really worth destroying your laptop just to get a slightly faster clock rate?
