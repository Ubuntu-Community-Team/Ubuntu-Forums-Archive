### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2010-03-26
Please be patient and give the community at least 24 hours to respond before bumping your post.  Also please keep in mind that we are all volunteers here, and responses tend to already be significantly faster than any other free software support.  Being in a hurry often results in oversights and other problems, I suggest you take your time with your transition in order to get the most out of it.

To answer your question, I will assume that you are currently using Windows.  If you install Ubuntu, the default is to setup a dual boot, which keeps your existing files on your Windows partition.  That said, you should *always* keep backups of your files, especially if you are making system changes like installing a new OS or otherwise repartitioning your hard drive.  Your files don't get "transferred", they exist exactly where you left them before, but you should be able to access them from Ubuntu.
