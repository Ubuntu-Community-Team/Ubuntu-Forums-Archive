### Post by gibdogg on 2007-09-24
My monitor setup has always been a problem with Linux, so when I heard Gutsy had better support for multi-monitor setups I installed it to investigate. Unfortunately the situation is not improved in the slightest.

I'm hoping someone will read this and suggest a better workaround or that the situation will be improved in a future release!


**MY SETUP:**

My motherboard has on-board NVIDIA graphics card, DVI output is plugged into a 4:3 monitor, it also has a RGB output which is plugged into the RGB input of my widescreen LCD TV.


**HOW I WANT IT TO WORK:**

I want to do most things on my 4:3 monitor, the only reason I have my TV plugged in is for watching video. When I open a program like Firefox etc. I ONLY want it on my main monitor, however when I use Movie Player I want to either have it open on my TV or be able to drag it across and fullscreen it. I want my 4:3 monitor at 1280x1024 and my TV at 1600x1024.


**HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKS:**

Ugh this is such a mess!

So I download and install Gutsy Tribe 5. At this point both monitor and TV display the same thing (1280x1024 desktop, stretched to 16:9 aspect ratio on the TV).

I install the restricted NVIDIA drivers, do a dist-upgrade and restart. Now nothing is displayed on the monitor and I still have my 1280x1024 stretched desktop on the widescreen TV.

Ah! This is where Gutsy's super-duper new multi-monitor configuration thing comes into its own I thought. So I open the 'Screens and Graphics' dialog only to find my 4:3 monitor isn't detected and there's no way of adding it through this GUI.

So I have to edit xorg.conf by hand, which I thought was going to be a thing of the past with Gutsy... (see below for my modified xorg.conf)

So finally that leaves me with the same workable but hardly ideal situation I had under Feisty:

I'm left with a dual-monitor desktop, both the 4:3 monitor and the 16:9 TV are at 1280x1024. I can open videos, drag it across and fullscreen it on the TV and do other stuff on the 4:3 monitor at the same time (which is good!). For some reason Totem detects my widescreen TV and squeezes itself giving me a squashed picture on the 4:3 monitor but displaying at the correct aspect ratio when on the TV (this is also good, but if Totem knows my TV's widescreen why doesn't the rest of the desktop?)

There are a few other annoying things about this setup:

If I open an app like Firefox etc. it almost always opens on the TV part of the screen, this is especially annoying because I have the TV pointed in the opposite direction to my monitor so can't just drag it across. Little dialog boxes etc. also always open on the TV which is annoying. As far as I can tell there's no way to restrict windows to opening on my main monitor.

Also, if I enable desktop effects with my modified xorg.conf it seems to crash the computer.
