### Post by txinga on 2006-04-10
XP will see that you have new hardware and it will want to confirm that change with your registered hw with microsoft. It may come back and tell you that you are trying to install the os on too many different systems. Just give m$ a call (toll free, if I remember) and talk to the nice folks in India who will give you a new authorization code to enter.

### Post by tmahmood on 2006-04-10
use the recovery option from windows XP setup. you will have your XP installation back. no harm to boot loader.

I upgraded my proccessor to AMD64 from Intel a few days back and did a recovery for XP. its working just fine. and one thing is I didn't had to reinstall Ubuntu. just changed my kernel to k7 from i686 and it was good to go :D
