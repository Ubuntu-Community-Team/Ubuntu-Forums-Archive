### Post by nimrod_abing on 2007-06-11
Bishop,

You do have a legitimate complaint. I too wish there was a better way to handle crashes and most of all, recover from them. Since you are running Beryl, which is very unstable, experimental, and beta software as pointed out by more than one user in this thread, I would have to assume you are running into a bug which causes a kernel panic. No use emailing the Gnome or Kernel devs because you are using proprietary/closed source drivers on your system.

Your shortcut key to the Gnome System Monitor is useless because your system has gone into a hard crash. It doesn't matter how "powerful" you make the Gnome System Monitor. If you hit a kernel panic or hard crash your system is next to dead anyway and you can say goodbye to that essay you were writing. You would think that your system is still up because you can move the mouse around. But that's only because the crash did not affect part of the system that handles mouse movement.

In case of a kernel panic or a very hard crash (where keyboard does not respond) you can avoid hitting the Big Red Switch **most of the time** by using the "Magic Sysrq" key sequences.

Read more about them here:

[http://www.linuxhowtos.org/Tips%20and%20Tricks/sysrq.htm]("http://www.linuxhowtos.org/Tips%20and%20Tricks/sysrq.htm")

The pertinent part:

Alt+SysRQ+e (sends TERM-signal, processes can shutdown properly (e.g. save data))
Alt+SysRQ+u (a sync will be done when unmounting anyway)
Alt+SysRQ+i (for the processes that didn't listen for the TERM signal, this is a kill -9 process)
Alt+SysRQ+b (reboot).

Better and oft recommended:

Alt+SysRQ+r (puts the keyboard in RAW mode)
Alt+SysRQ+s (sync disk cache to device, ensures sync happens and not to rely on sync-before-umount)
Alt+SysRQ+e
Alt+SysRQ+i
Alt+SysRQ+u (notice umount comes after kill -9 is sent above, ensuring processes have access to all filesystems prior to termination)
Alt+SysRQ+b

This is the safest way to reboot your system instead of just BRS'ing it. If you continue to experience crashes and you suspect that Beryl/Desktop Effects is the culprit, why not turn it off if you value your work? Or if you are using OpenOffice, have it autosave your work more frequently.

[COLOR=Red]Added emphasis to "most of the time" because there are times when you will be left with a completely dead system and even Magic Sysrq will not work. In my experience, 9 out of 10 crashes I ran into I was able to safely restart using Magic Sysrq.[/COLOR]
