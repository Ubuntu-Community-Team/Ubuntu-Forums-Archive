### Post by blueridgedog on 2011-03-20
They appear to be identical in that an external party (your ISP or Google) will host your email.  If you have a prior relationship with Google and want to add your domain email to it, then it is a logical fit.  If not, then you should see what you get from your ISP that is included with the price of the purchase.

One way to get the best of each world is to have your ISP host the email, then use a google email account to access the ISP's mailbox via pop and then have your emails on gmail.
