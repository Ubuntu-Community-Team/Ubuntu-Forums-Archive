### Post by sharp11 on 2008-09-22
I had been despairing of Firefox, even though I have become dependent on many of its great features. It just seemed to get slower and slower and spend so much time using >90% CPU. I tried Epiphany for a while, and started using Opera, too. 

But they weren't that much better, and though they also have great features, I missed the FF that I had extensively customized. I went back to it, .... and ... was reading the NYT (with about a dozen tabs open) when I saw a particularly obnoxious flash ad that was playing on every single page. FF went to 98+% CPU. I couldn't even switch tabs to use Flashkiller. 

So then I found [Flashblock]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/433"). It blocks *all* flash -- unless you click on it to run it. Perfect. Perfect.

All is right with the world. Thank you flashblock. Thank you Firefox. Thank you ubuntu!
