### Post by coffeecat on 2009-05-22
Most HP and Epson printers (but not all) work with Linux. As you've discovered, many Lexmarks don't. Canon is another manufacturer to avoid if you're running Linux.

Have a look at this link:

[http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting](http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting)

Your best plan is to identify a particular model from a high-street or internet store that's in your budget and then check its compatibilty from that site. Simply click the 'Printers' link on the left, fill in the details and you should get all the information you need. Be aware, though, that not every model for every manufacturer is listed.

You want a multifunction. Unfortunately, if the printer works, this doesn't necessarily guarantee that the scanner is supported, although with a HP it probably will. To check the scanner part, have a look at:

[http://www.sane-project.org/](http://www.sane-project.org/)

The sane site is not as well organised or maintained as the Openprinting one, which is a pity, but it may give you the information you need.

N.B. I have the OfficeJet 6310 as well. Excellent machine. With Ubuntu, it 'just works'.

**Edit:** just noticed this:

> **lilmizkit said:**
> I have looked at HP's as I read on here that they are good but to buy the ink's your looking at alot of money

Lexmarks have notoriously high running costs. I doubt that HP ink is more expensive. Look at how long a cartridge lasts, not the unit cost.

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2009-05-23
I've been running a low-cost HP psc1350 all-in-one for nearly 4 years now.

Every 3 months I take the top off and do a thorough clean.

I am only on my second set of cartridges, after printing over 21,000 pages, as I re-fill the cartridges BEFORE they drop below 25%.

Another trick is to add about 1 mil of Windex, or other ammonia-based window cleaner to the cartridge when re-filling. This goes a long way to prevent head clogs.

Also if you do get problems with clogging, just hold the cartridge slightly submerged in hot water for a few seconds. It should dissolve any blockages.

If you follow these guidelines you will find the cost of ownership dramatically reduced.

Also, if you do a search on the internet, you should be able to find how to re-set the cartridges so that the HPLIP package accurately displays ink levels.
With my cartridges, it just requires placing some tape over a couple of the contact pins and plugging them in and out three times.
