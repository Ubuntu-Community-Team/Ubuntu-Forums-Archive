### Post by mcduck on 2011-10-20
Are you usign Unity or Gnome Shell?

Unity should have Shut Down option in the power/system menu in the top right screen corner by default.

Gnome Shell only has a log out option by default, so you are supposed to log out and then shut down fro the login screen. It's possible to add the shutdown option, of course, there's a Shell extension for the purpose (the Alternative Status Menu extension): [http://www.webupd8.org/2011/10/official-gnome-shell-extensions.html]("http://www.webupd8.org/2011/10/official-gnome-shell-extensions.html")

Anyway, even without such options, plugging the power cord would be the absolute last thing you'd ever want to do. There are plenty of better ways for shutting down your computer that have much smaller risk of destroying your data and leaving you with a broken, unbootable operating system.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-10-20
> **mcduck said:**
> Are you usign Unity or Gnome Shell?

Unity should have Shut Down option in the power/system menu in the top right screen corner by default.

Sometimes it kinda gets hidden just off the top-right corner. Click on your username and then move the mouse to the extreme right and the menu should pop up, giving you the option to shut down. (if you want to reboot, select Shut Down from the menu, then click the Reboot button).

Alternatively, "sudo halt" and "sudo reboot" work in the terminal.

> Gnome Shell only has a log out option by default

The "Suspend" option in Gnome Shell turns into "Power Off" if you hold down the Alt key.
