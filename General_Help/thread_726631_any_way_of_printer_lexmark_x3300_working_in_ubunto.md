### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2008-03-17
The Open Printing Database says that both the X3330 (x3300 is not listed) and the straight 3300 are paperweights.

If you have an old PC lying around, you could always use that with a minimal Windows installation and use it as a print server.

If not, a new printer may be required.

I suggest that you have a look at HP, as they are pretty well supported in Linux.

However, always check here before purchase:

[http://openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi](http://openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi)

### Post by Sef on 2008-03-17
> Give it ago, they should work with most inkjet printers.

The OP's has a color laser printer, so that idea won't work.  Lexmark is not GNU/Linux friendly,  so often their printers are paperweights.  The only exception is the Optra line, which is business line.   

Either use ramjet_1953's idea of a print server or get a new printer.  HP will be best, followed by Epson.  Check out [Open Printing's suggested printers]("http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting/Database/SuggestedPrinters").
