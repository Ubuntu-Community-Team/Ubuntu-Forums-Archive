### Post by amingv on 2008-06-06
Don't write your posts in all-caps, it's not considered good network etiquette.
Most HP printers work out of the box (many other brands do too, but avoid Lexmark if you can.)
For the wifi card check here [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported)
Dlink, Belkin and Netgear have always been nice to me, but it depends a lot on the model.

### Post by Sef on 2008-06-06
HP is the best printer for GNU/Linux.  Almost all their printers will work OOTB (out of the box.)  When I installed Hardy, my printer was automatically detected, and since I have a PCS, the scanner and card reader was automatically detected too.

For more information, read [Suggested Printers]("http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting/Database/SuggestedPrinters").
