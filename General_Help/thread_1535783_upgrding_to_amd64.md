### Post by 3Miro on 2010-07-21
1. Most benchmarks give 64-bit 10-30% percent improvement of speed for regular apps. For heavy compiling/video editing/numerical computations the advantage is much higher. I am doing a lot of scientific computing and I only use 64-bit.

2. The only way to go from 32-bit to 64-bit is to make a clean reinstall.

3. The wireless drivers depend on the specific manufacturer/driver. Try with a 64-bit live CD and if it finds a wireless driver for you, then you are good to go. I would say that 64-bit wireless generally wouldn't be harder to find then 32-bit, but there is always an exception.
