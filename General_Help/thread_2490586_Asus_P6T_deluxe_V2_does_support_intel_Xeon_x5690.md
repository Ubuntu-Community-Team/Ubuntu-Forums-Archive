The motherboard is pretty old. For $150-$200, you can probably find a huge upgrade MB+CPU+RAM that will have 4x more performance.  Just sayin.

A few days ago, I saw a deal for $400 on a Intel Core i9-12900K + MSI Z690-A PRO WiFi + 32GB G.Skill Ripjaws S5 DDR5.  That's 40K passmarks. 
Whereas that Xeon is only 6900 passmarks.   [https://www.cpubenchmark.net/cpu.php?cpu=Intel+Xeon+X5690+%40+3.47GHz](https://www.cpubenchmark.net/cpu.php?cpu=Intel+Xeon+X5690+%40+3.47GHz)

Heck, I have a 2017 Core i5 laptop that was $305 which was just a tiny bit slower.  Right now, the Ryzen 5 5500 is the best "value" for the price @ $85 for 20K passmarks - that's 3x faster.  Without searching too hard, found a MB+CPU combo for $193 with a Ryzen 5600X and a B450 motherboard. Add $30 of DDR4 RAM ... and reuse everything else you already have.

Just saw this [https://slickdeals.net/f/16912153-dell-refurbished-optiplex-micro-desktop-i5-8500t-8-gb-ram-500-gb-hdd-110](https://slickdeals.net/f/16912153-dell-refurbished-optiplex-micro-desktop-i5-8500t-8-gb-ram-500-gb-hdd-110) - it is one of those off-lease SFF business systems, but has a Core i5-8500 CPU for $130. That's 9600 passmarks. [https://www.cpubenchmark.net/cpu.php?cpu=Intel+Core+i5-8500+%40+3.00GHz](https://www.cpubenchmark.net/cpu.php?cpu=Intel+Core+i5-8500+%40+3.00GHz) It is kind of amazing what can be had with just a little flexibility. Years ago, I wanted "server" hardware and got some.  What I found was that it was loud, used too much power, and getting typical desktop addons was a problem.  Then I had a PSU fail and replacing it was more than I'd paid for the used "server".  Since then, I've gone with having 2 nearly identical desktops to get my redundancy needs handled, got quiet PSUs, a few UPS battery backups, and some extra network ports. Quality PSUs probably made the most difference - and the UPSes to go along helped.
