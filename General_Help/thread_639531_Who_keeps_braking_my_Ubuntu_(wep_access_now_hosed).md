### Post by kzutter on 2007-12-13
This is why I have switched to Debian. Ubuntu was, in part, a response to Debian slow release cycle. While Debian was too conservative, Ubuntu has demonstrated that being too aggresive has its faults, too. Ubuntu is essentially a new distro at every release. I thought the LTS releases would provide some stability, but Dapper was largely ignored by the developers. Since new releases were all new instead of improvements on a base, backporting become impossible in many cases. 

Now you have my $0.02

BTW - I am grateful to Ubuntu for familiarizing me with the Debian way. Prevoiusly I was a Redhat/Fedora guy and Debian seemed liked an oddball. Now I am hooked on Debian.
