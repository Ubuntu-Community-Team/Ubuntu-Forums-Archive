### Post by Known unknownZ on 2012-03-16
As the others said, the BTC client itself takes up very few resources.

That said, if you're going to be doing any sort of mining with BTC (using DiabloMiner or some similar prog), it's recommended that you do so on a seperate mining box.

If you're *that* dedicated to the cause, there are myriad vendors out there that sell pre-made Bitcoin rigs (don't feel the urge to pimp out one particular site or another, but a quick Google search or so should bring up some results).

But if you'd rather stick on the 'safe side' and avoid having any trace of BTC apps on your PC, you could always just use an online wallet like the ones listed [here](https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Browser-based_wallet). Hope that helps some
