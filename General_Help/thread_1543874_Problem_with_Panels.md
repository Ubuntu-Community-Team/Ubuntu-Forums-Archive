### Post by malspa on 2010-08-01
I've run into this issue a lot -- in earlier Ubuntu versions and even recently in Mint Isadora.  For some reason, the issue didn't show up for me in Lucid.  It also hasn't shown up for me in Debian Squeeze.

When not expanded, the GNOME panels have been buggy for a long time -- and not just for me.  If you do a Google search you'll find lots of bug reports and complaints.

This might sound crazy, but a few times I've gotten things to stick by simply putting everything back in place, locking panel items back down, and logging into the session again.  After the second or third time, things just stayed in place.  Worth a shot -- I really hope it helps.
