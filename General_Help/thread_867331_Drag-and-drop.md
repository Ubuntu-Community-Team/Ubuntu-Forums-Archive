### Post by wizardfait on 2008-07-22
I have a general question, about Ubuntu, but more specifically about the GNOME desktop aspect...

In windows, when you drag and drop, you can use a left click, which does the default action (Depending on what drive it's coming from it'll either copy or  move) however, you can also right-click drag and drop, to then get a menu of either copying, moving, or creating a shortcut, with the default option being bolded.

Is there such a related effect in GNOME? I'm using Ubuntu 8.04 (Don't know if it's changed all that much through the versions, but it doesn't hurt to know. :D) When I attempt a right-click drag and drop, it simply acts as if I right clicked, then let go, not moving the mouse, and brings of the right-click menu.

I understand that there may be copyright issues with doing it exactly the same, but do they have this in there? Like maybe a certain button combination before drag-and-dropping said file?

### Post by loboc on 2008-07-22
Hold Alt Down before you release the mouse button . 
OR Use the middle mouse button instead of the left button

With Left Click
the deafult is to move 
holding CTL down will force a copy 
holding down ALT gives you the menu
