### Post by Native Dialect on 2010-04-16
MP3 may be common, but it is a copy-written format. OGG is an open source codec that also happens to be free. If people trying to switch are that concerned, they can find an mp3 to ogg converter. There a quite a few options online or via the synaptic pack manager. So long as a person's collection is not DRM locked, they can make the switch and not have to bat an eye.
