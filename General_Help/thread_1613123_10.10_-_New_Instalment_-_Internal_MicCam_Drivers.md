### Post by Cheesehead on 2010-11-04
Most common webcams, microphones, and speakers shold be automatically detected by your system. No special drivers are usually necessary.

Skype: 
  Open Skype, then open the Options Menu (CTRL+O).
  Look for Sound Devices.
  What are the current settings for Microphone and Speakers?
      If not 'PulseAudio server (local)', try that setting if available.
  Next, look for Video Devices.
  In the 'select webcam' field, are there any entries? What are they? What is currently selected? What happens if you select something else? 



Further troubleshooting if fiddling with Skype preferences doesn't work:

Webcam:  
  Do you have webcam software like cheese or camorama installed? 
  If so, do they work?
  If they fail, do they give an error message?
  Do you know how to tell if you have a webcam app installed? (Have you looked in your application menus?
  Do you know how to install a webcam app?


Mic:
  Right-click on your little speaker icon and look for something like 'sound preferences' or 'audio preferences'
  Is there a tab for 'input'? Or a little mixer bar for a microphone?
  Is there any field labelled 'device'? Is it empty? If something is in the field, what does it say?
