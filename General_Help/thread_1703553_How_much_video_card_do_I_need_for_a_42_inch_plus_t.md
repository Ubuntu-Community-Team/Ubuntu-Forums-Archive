### Post by lithopsian on 2011-03-09
Yup, TV size doesn't matter.  It matters if it is HD or not, and also 720 or 1080.

It takes very little memory to handle even an HD TV image.  Bear in mind that "full HD" is about 1920x1080, not really much different from a standard PC monitor at 1600x1200.  The massive amounts of memory now included on graphics cards are almost entirely for 3D rendering.  In practice the card will need special features to convert the output stream to the right format in real time, but that is fairly common now.

So all that matters is that your card can process HD output smoothly to an HDMI output, and most importantly that the rest of your hardware can smoothly process the video.  That includes your CPU and hard drive or optical drive.  Any new PC will be able to do this, some Netbooks perhaps not so well, and most PCs from the last couple of years should have no trouble.
