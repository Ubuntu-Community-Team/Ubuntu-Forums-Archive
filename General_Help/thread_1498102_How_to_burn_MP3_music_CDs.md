### Post by howefield on 2010-05-31
The first one you mentioned, Brasero, like most of the others will burn CDs capable of being played on a stereo system.

Begin by selecting to create an audio cd, add the mp3 tracks that you want, and burn. The program will convert your mp3s as required.

If you just want a CD full of mp3 files, burn a Data disc.

### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2010-05-31
you can't burn an audio CD with mp3 files. you can, however, burn a data CD containing mp3 files. if your CD player is capable of reading data CDs, it will recognize the mp3 files.

### Post by WinRiddance on 2010-05-31
You can't burn MP3 files with Ubuntu by default, but it can be done without too much hassle. Just follow the link in my signature and locate Advanced tips, then look for the line about MP3 files. For the burning process you'll also need some additional packages which can actually be found right in the software center, **Ubuntu restricted extras** amongst others. If you type MP3 in the upper right search of the software center you'll find everything that you need.

In Ubuntu 9.04 and 9.10 my preferred burning app. was RubyRipper. However, RubyRipper seems to have some compatibility issues with Lucid i.e. making it take virtually "forever" to burn a music cd. Tried several other apps from the software center and now my preferred choice is **Ripper X** which is also available there.

### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2010-06-01
you can leave a data CD "open" as well. the difference is that you can write to an "open" CD at a later time. until the CD is closed, the CD has to be "indexed" every time it is accessed (and the index has not been cached yet), however.

as for your stereo, it's quite possible that your stereo has a slow processing unit that has a hard time decoding the mp3 format.
