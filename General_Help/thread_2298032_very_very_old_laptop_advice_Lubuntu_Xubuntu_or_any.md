### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-10-08
Tiny Core Linux or proteus.  Try to find more RAM for it, otherwise browsing the web will be a painful chore.  You want at least 256 MB or 512 MB.  Be sure to make a swap partition of double your RAM.

### Post by Sweet_Baby_Jamie on 2015-10-08
Adding RAM is your best choice, but I know it's hard to find RAM for a computer that old.  My old computer has some kinda RAM that is obsolete, not something I could find at Office Depot or Best Buy.  But an old used used computer repair shop had what I needed and just let me have it for free since no one uses the old stuff anymore.  

A favorite default browser in some of the "ultra-light" Linux distros is Seamonkey.  It works like Firefox with Thunderbird built-in, but it's alot leaner and meaner.  It's not in the Lubuntu repositories but you can get it by following the steps on [this page]("http://linuxg.net/how-to-install-seamonkey-2-29-on-ubuntu-14-10-ubuntu-14-04-ubuntu-12-04-debian-8-debian-7-and-derivatives/").
