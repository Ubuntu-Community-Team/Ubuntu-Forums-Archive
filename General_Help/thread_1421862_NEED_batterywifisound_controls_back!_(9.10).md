### Post by jimbren on 2010-03-04
I believe to resolve this problem you have to go back to a default desktop and redo any customizations you may have.  You won't lose actual data, though, so don't worry.  

I'm not in front of my ubuntu machine right now so I'm going from memory here, but there are two directories inside /home that contain your desktop settings, the first is called .Gnome2 and the second .Gnome2private...or something like that.  You need to move or remove those directories and log out of and then back into your session.   Everything will be back to defaults, including your battery/wifi/and sound.
