### Post by Quarkrad on 2010-12-13
In my experience it depends on the complexity of the formatting.  Many documents I have converted has maintained the formatting - only one (very complex formatting/footers) was there an error.  I have found that working with big MS docs my system kept stopping - very frustrating. So I opened the .doc document and then saved it as a .odt document before any changes.  Worked on the doc and then when finished sent it back as a .doc and saved both a .doc and .odt on my machine.  It was certainly quicker working in .odt compared to .doc.  It was as if the app was trying to convert on the fly and with a large complex doc it didn't like.  This may be incorrect but on my system it was much better to convert and work in .odt format and then convert at the end.

### Post by Cheesehead on 2010-12-13
OO generally treats all non-.odt files as 'foreign' and translates them into .odt anyway.

For example, if you try to import a .doc and then export it as a .pdf, under the hood the .doc is converted to .odt as part of the opening process, and the .odt is converted to .pdf as part of the export.

So every time you open a .doc the whole file gets converted, and every time you save as a .doc the whole file gets converted back. I have found the process to be reliable, but the point is that converting every time is much slower than simply working in .odt and converting back to .doc only at completion.
