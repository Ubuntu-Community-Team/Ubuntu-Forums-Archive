### Post by nzjethro on 2011-06-25
Hi tehrealbinglebob, and welcome to the forums. :D

What you should do is log into Windows 7, then "shrink" your Windows 7 partition ([this website]("http://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/2672-partition-volume-shrink.html") will help). You want to shrink your Windows 7 partition to creat an unallocated partition (of at least 20GB or so) into which to install Ubuntu.

Once you've done that, run the Ubuntu installer again, and choose your unallocated space to install Ubuntu into. In the "Allocate drive space" menu, you select your unallocated space (that you freed up by shrinking your volume), then choose "Add", select "ext4" as your format type, and "/" as your mount point.

If you have any further questions, post back here and someone can help you out.
