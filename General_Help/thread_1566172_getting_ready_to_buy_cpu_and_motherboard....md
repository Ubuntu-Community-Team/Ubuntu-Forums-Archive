### Post by nigsy on 2010-09-02
You don't say what budget you are looking at, but as a guide, you need an i7 chip, or AMD equivalent, a MOB that supports DDR3 RAM and sata (3 gb/s) if possible. A decent mid range video card, but if you're not gaming no need for anything to extreme.
I've always built with Gigabyte boards and intel cpus so I'm going to be biased towards these!

You want a least 4gb RAM and a resonable sized HD (500gb min)

This might all sound like overkill for what you us it for at the moment, but as you get more into audio and video processing - especially if you start to render high def, you will be thankfull for the i7 and increased ram!!

My current set up

Intel i7 running W7
Gigabyte UD-5 MOB
Gigabyte Graphics card
6gb corsiar dominator DDR3 (3 x2) matched
2 x 1tb WD Black Hds in Raid 0
Panasonic DVD/RW 
Akassa Nero cooler
Corsair 750 watt PSU

I do a lot of HD video editing and photos editing in .raw format and the i7 chip has really benefited me.

### Post by wkulecz on 2010-09-09
Windows 7 really benefits form an i7, but all our Linux boxes are AMD CPU and Nvidia video.  About as problem free as can be.

I just buy what is on sale at Fry's or Newegg.  No reason to get less than a quad core and DDR3 these days, if you are not gammer or dual booting into Windows, go AMD and whatever Nvidia video card strikes your fancy, but the GeForce 210 is cheap and effective.

I don't think its worth buying higher end now to support future upgrades, the cost-effective time window to max it out is too short and ultimately you pretty much have to replace the Motherboard, CPU, and RAM as a unit anyways. So don't go too much beyond your immediate needs.  OTOH, I've got cases, power supplies, DVD burners, and hard drives that have seen three or four motherboards, my keyboard, LCD monitor (top of the line in its day) and trackball go back to when Windows 2000 was current. So top of the line here can pay off in the long run.  I refuse to buy hard drives with less than a three year warranty, with five prefered (unfortunately getting hard to find :( ).
