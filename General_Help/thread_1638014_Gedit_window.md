### Post by AlphaLexman on 2010-12-05
Good to know! I wanted to do the same thing a while back...

The only solution I could find was to 'gksudo gedit filename' alongside a normal gedit window. Otherwise a new tab is always given.

### Post by owiknowi on 2010-12-05
Another option I found (credits go to Paolo Maggi on gnome.org):
1. Open gedit (normal way or through the terminal command gedit).
2. Open terminal and give command:
 gedit --new-window
