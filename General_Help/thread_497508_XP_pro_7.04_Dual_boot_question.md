### Post by dabl on 2007-07-10
Here's a decent little tutorial that would be worth your time to review before you pull the trigger:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partitioning](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partitioning)

In XP, do your disk cleanup and defrag before you change partition size, if you're going to do that.  I like the "Alternate Install" CD better than the live CD for installation -- you get more control and visibility of what's going on, versus Live CD is kinda "point and shoot and live with the result".  Also, you can plan on fiddling around with your video display to get it working correctly -- it's all been done before and there are many posts on the topic.

With your data properly backed up, the only thing you can really lose is the time required to reinstall something. :lolflag:

My sentiments exactly!

But, keep your expectations about Ubuntu realistic, with regard to how long the learning curve is.  Here's a synopsis of my experience, so you can see how it goes (and I did have a bit of unix experience back in the early 1990s to build upon):

- Installed Kubuntu Dapper on a spare IDE drive in September, spent days figuring out how to make an ATI card work
- Built a very nice desktop system (with an Nvidia card), and installed Edgy when it came out in October
- Spent 3 days proving that Grub won't go on a SATA drive when XP is already on an IDE drive in the same system
- Re-installed Win XP and Kubuntu about 6 times getting it set up the way I wanted
- Got brave and started playing with the Feisty Herd pre-releases in February
- Got braver and installed e-live on the IDE drive, and learned a bunch from that perspective
- When Feisty came out in April, installed not only Kubuntu but a 64-bit Ubuntu low-latency system (same platform, different drive)
- Learned how to capture and digitize old vinyl and 78 rpm record albums with Audacity and Gnome Wave Cleaner
- 10 months in, I'm contemplating a video capture system

Gee, has it been almost a year already?  And I'm now set to DUMP WINDOWS ENTIRELY AND FOREVER!
