### Post by JoelOl75 on 2010-07-07
Intel graphics are pretty much worthless.  My recommendation is any late Nvidia card.  I prefer the Quadro cards as they are built by Nvidia and are of very high quality, but there are also "low-end" Quadro cards that suck too. These are more geared toward CAD/CAM and Video production work, not games, although they work fine for games as well. You will need to find a half-height card though. I like the Quadro FX3400 or better but these are PCI-E amd full height.

You would be better off getting a whole new system in the long run.  Now is a great time to upgrade price wise!
[http://www.newegg.com/Store/ComboDeals.aspx?ComboStoreID=1&name=CPUs-Motherboards](http://www.newegg.com/Store/ComboDeals.aspx?ComboStoreID=1&name=CPUs-Motherboards)

I just hooked myself up with an AMD 6 core beauty with a Asus MB and some quick DDR3!  Worth every penny when it comes to running 5 Linux Virtual Machines in Ubuntu (I compile gtk-gnutella for many linux distros at my website)  [http://stinker.serveftp.net:8000/gtkgnutella/gtkgnutella.html](http://stinker.serveftp.net:8000/gtkgnutella/gtkgnutella.html)

Ebay would be the best place to get a half-height Nvidia AGP card IMO if you plan on keeping it.  Something Geforce 3 Ti 200/500 or better (NOT 4, the 3 is better than the 4).  Stay away from mx designated cards (ie. GeForce 4MX440)

an FX 5800 or 5900 should be a great upgrade at a decent price point, although with an AGP card you arn't going to be getting DirectX11 so don't plan on Windows gaming.  Ubuntu runs the older hardware just fine, but Don't go too old as there are 3 different drivers.  The latest driver supports only the newest hardware that's on this page: [http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux-display-ia32-256.35-driver.html](http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux-display-ia32-256.35-driver.html)

Hope this helps.  I have had nothing but problems with ATI cards in Linux (Except my old trusty ATI All-in-Wonder 9800 card) and Intel can be problematic at worst and just plain slow and useless at best.
