### Post by Vivaldi Gloria on 2008-08-28
For cleaning junk files see

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=140920](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=140920)

To be honest, these are unneccessary.


An important thing is to backup config files incase something goes wrong. Nicedude's script is good:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=792639&highlight=script](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=792639&highlight=script)


Keep your ext3 disks less than 80-85% and you don't need defrag. If you insist then look here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=865162](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=865162)


If your disks are ext3 then they are already checked once in a while during boot. Occasionally, check their health status using smartctl.


I physically clean my computer every month using air compressor.
