### Post by JerryK on 2007-06-29
The UPSs that I have worked with recently have a maximum power level off the battery.  If you try to draw more power that than level, the UPS will shut off completely.  Without knowing detailed specs on your computers, I cannot tell you the exact load you would be drawing.  But, based on my recent experience, I suspect that you are drawing more than the UPSs can handle.

I currently have a 350VA UPS on ONE computer and it only provides enough power for about 5 seconds.  I plan on buying a 750VA UPS for that one computer this weekend.  The 350VA UPS will be reassigned to my answering machine!

You have two computers on each 500VA UPS, which is roughly equivalent to having one 250VA UPS for each computer.  If your computers are only drawing 250VA each, you MIGHT get a few seconds of runtime.

With that in mind, look at what you said about the 520VA UPS.  It has only one computer on it and it runs fine.

My recommendation is to put only one computer on each UPS.  You will need to get more UPSs if you want all your computers protected.

Hope this helps.
Jerry
