### Post by Chronon on 2009-09-20

So, queue indicates tracks that will be played in a certain order but not committed to the playlist.  This behavior already assumes the existence of playlists, though.  Rockbox supports queues in this way, but it's heavily playlist oriented.  If you take the playlist out of the picture there's nothing to resume after the queue and the queue is just, itself, a playlist whose contents you forget after you have played it.  

Also, if the user never intends to save the playlist, then there's virtually no difference between inserting tracks into the playlist and queuing them.

I would say that a queue is another layer of playlist structure/behavior, not something that replaces it.
