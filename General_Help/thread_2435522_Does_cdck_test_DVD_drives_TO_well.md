### Post by orthoducks on 2020-01-22
I've been trying to use cdck to check the condition of several used DVD drives that I keep on hand. I'm beginning to question its value. Here's why:

Initially I tested about eight drives, and was distressed to find that they all were out of alignment. The message I got was: "Even though there are no unreadable sectors, the disk is unstable!"

It occurred to me that the DVD I was testing with might actually be out of alignment, not the drives. It was a DVD ROM sold by a reputable software publisher, but still... At another location I found a similar disk that tested good on a drive in one of my computers there. I brought it back to my test system and found that with it, cdck still reported the drives were "unstable."

One of these drives has been in the test system since I built it about three years ago. I have used it regularly through most of that time, and it hasn't given me a speck of trouble. Only cdck thinks it has a problem.

Is cdck telling me something I didn't know about this drive? Or is my experience with the drive telling me something about cdck?

If cdck is applying unrealistically rigid standards, as I suspect, is there an alternative that will give more accurate results: that will say a drive is OK if it's working well enough to perform reliably, and not just if it gets a perfect score in lab tests?
