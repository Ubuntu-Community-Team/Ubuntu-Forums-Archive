### Post by lazerdave on 2009-12-01
I'm going to have to print several hundred photographs from a holiday party my office is holding for underprivileged children.  The pictures are taken by employees using their own cameras using various memory card types, and printed on a series of their own printers.

This bottlenecks badly, and most of the families can't wait (sometimes hours) for the printout or come back so that this process could be done slowly but surely.

My proposed solution is to plug all of the various printers into a USB hub and a multi-format memory card reader into another USB port.  The pictures would be pulled off of the cards into a working folder on the drive and sent sequentially to any of the printers when it was available.  (Ideally once each picture was printed, it should be moved into a separate folder called "Done" or something.)

Surely people do things like this all the time around this time of year.

It seems like tea4cups is the right solution, but I'd prefer not to go 3rd party if I don't have to.  (I think it costs money as well, which I don't have.)

Any help would not only be appreciated by me, but also by a staffload of community healthcare workers, and most of all - the families and children who will cherish these pictures for the rest of their lives.

There's a bit of a time-crunch here also.  The party is on 12/17/09, and I'll want to test solutions before the big day.

Thanks!

### Post by robertmbowes on 2011-05-10
I know the post is very old, but there is a way to do this. In Ubuntu 10.04, you can add all of the printers and then add them all to a "class". It will then print to the first available printer and take the load off of just one printer.
