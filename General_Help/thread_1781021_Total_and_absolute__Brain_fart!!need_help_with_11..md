### Post by ventrical on 2011-06-14
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Driver/Ndiswrapper](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Driver/Ndiswrapper)


The above link is one of many 'must reads' that I found very helpful. I finally got my Linux Mint 11 (which is Ubuntu based) to work with the wireless Atheros card.

What I had to do was:

1.Eject Linksys Wireless -G Notebook Adapter (Broadcom chipset is reported with 'lspci')

2.Right click Network connections on menubar.

3. Edit Connections.

4. Delete most recent (or all) wireless connections.

Voila! Upon clicking the Network Connection Icon the available networks were ready. All I had to do was put in the WEP key.

  I think now the same will go for Natty install.

I'll keep any interested posted on this topic.
