### Post by OrangeCrate on 2009-02-03
Right click to unlock on any item that you want to move on the bottom panel. Then click move, and drag them to the top panel. When you have what you want on the top panel, then right click on the bottom panel, and click on remove this panel.

### Post by timcredible on 2009-02-03
right-click on the bottom panel and remove it.  right-click on the top panel and add window list.  

the burn effect is part of compiz, but that only works if you have a 3d capable video card.  if you do, install compizconfig-settings-manager via synaptic to get the menu option to manage effects (system->preferences->compiz settings manager).

There is the solution here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=887288]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=887288")

Edited: if you don't see the burn option then click on Animations Add-On. This provides additional animation schemes that, when enabled, will appear in the primary animations settings. These include burning windows, beaming in/out, paper airplane effects, and others. However, the detailed settings for the add-ons (speed, # of burn particles, etc.)  is under the Add-On dialog. If you don&#8217;t see these, you may have to add additional compiz fusion plugins using System -> Administration -> Synaptic Package Manager. I hope this helps.
