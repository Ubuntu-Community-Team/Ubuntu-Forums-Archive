### Post by goober99 on 2006-03-22
I use KTorrent. You can install it through Synaptic Package Manager. I used [utorrent]("http://www.utorrent.com") on Windows, and I have not found anything that compares to it for Linux. I chose KTorrent because it can download multiple torrents, and you can selectively download files within a torrent. I miss features from utorrent like DHT, Peer Exchange, and Per Torrent Bandwidth Managing from utorrent, but KTorrent is the closest I've found for Linux. I know Azureus would do all those things (and more), and it is the "more" part that bothers me. Azureus is just too big and complicated for my tastes.

I don't like the torrent client that comes with Gnome because you can't do multiple torrents--same with BitTornado plus it is just ugly. The Official BitTorrent client does both multiple torrents and DHT (which KTorrent is missing to my knowledge), but not being able to selectively download files in a torrent makes KTorrent win out.

I can't wait until utorrent comes to Linux.

That's where you're wrong.  Force download has nothing to do with going around private trackers, and torrents from private trackers disable "Use decentralized tracking" and "Supplied from another peer" from torrent sources.  Also, most private trackers only allow a connection from the IP address that the member logged in from, or from a key embedded in the torrent file (banning the member who would be sharing files with their key will block all other sources).

See this screenshot for what really happens from a private tracker:
[[IMG]http://img122.imageshack.us/img122/3136/azureus4kh.th.png[/IMG]](http://img122.imageshack.us/my.php?image=azureus4kh.png)

[quote=goober99]I know Azureus would do all those things (and more), and it is the "more" part that bothers me. Azureus is just too big and complicated for my tastes.
It's really not complicated.  The options section is kind of a mess, but they now hide features based on whether you pick "Beginning" "Intermediate" or "Advanced".  And really, the only thing most users need to adjust is their max upload and download speed (so your connection isn't so bogged down while downloading) and their port...and that's easy to do.
