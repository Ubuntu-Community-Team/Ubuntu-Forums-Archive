### Post by P4man on 2009-08-26
Im not sure I understand the question(s).

You already have 2 ubuntu installs, and I assume you can boot both.. isn't that dual boot ? :). Or do you mean you want to change grub so you can chose the OS from the grub menu instead of using the BIOS boot selection ? If so, that shouldn't be too hard..

As for the other question.. you mean you want to share that drive between both ubuntu's ? Sure.. wouldn't know how to prevent it :). You can simply attach the drive, format it as whatever filesystem your prefer (say Ext3) and that partition will be accessible from either ubuntu's without any effort on your part. just go Places > Nameofharddisk.

If you use the same username on both installs, I don't see what would prevent this (if you dont, there might be some permission stuff to sort out).
