### Post by Agent.Logic_ on 2009-04-04
Gnome is installed by default with Ubuntu. You *can* switch to KDE on Ubuntu and uninstall Gnome completely if you wish to. Or, you could have them both installed, and choose during login which display manager you want for that session. To explain everything here is exhausting to say the least. Instead, I'm going to point you to a very good comparison (albiet not comprehensive) on KDE and Gnome right here: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome). This link tells you how to go about installing KDE on Ubuntu: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kde](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kde).

Regarding themes, the best place I know to get Ubuntu themes is [http://gnome-look.org](http://gnome-look.org). The installation is straightforward... right click on the desktop, choose "change desktop background." Click the "Themes" tab, and click the install button. Lead it to the tar.gz theme you downloaded, and select it. It will automatically extract the theme, and will be displayed in the Themes tab.

Good luck and have fun using Ubuntu!
