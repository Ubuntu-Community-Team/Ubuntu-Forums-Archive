### Post by swarup on 2016-08-19
In Google Drive there is a new and very annoying notification which pops up every time one transfers a file from one folder to another, when the folders are shared with others. Even if the folders are shared with the same people, the notification "some people may lose access" pops up and will not go away until one clicks 'ok'.

There is a [solution]("https://productforums.google.com/forum/#!topic/drive/icjJMeKN-UU") for pc and mac users: 

> To remove the notification, all I had to do was:Click on the Google Drive Sync icon.Click on the Menu 'dots'.Choose 'Preferences', then under 'Advanced', remove the checkmark from 'Show confirmation when removing items from shared folders'. 

But in the Linux version of Google Drive, when I click on the 'Google Drive sync icon' and select 'preferences', in the window that opens there is no "advanced" option. So it is a dead end. There should be a solution for Linux users as well. Is it possible the advanced option may be in another location in the Linux version of Google Drive?
