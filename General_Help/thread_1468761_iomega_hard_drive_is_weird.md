### Post by coffeecat on 2010-05-01
I don't know much about this, but I don't think this is a problem. It's a feature.

On my WD NAS drive, in the "Public" share, I have the same three folders - except that they're called "Shared Pictures", "Shared Videos" and "Shared Music". I found that my Sony PS3 autodiscovered the contents of the three folders and I can stream videos, music and photos from the NAS to the PS3 over the local network. Really useful. When I first bought the PS3, I just started it up and it found the 'Public' share without me having to do anything. My guess is that any good media player will do the same and that's why you have the three folders.

Question: are the three folders in your Iomega in "Public" and can you create extra (password-protected) shares? My PS3 doesn't see my personal password-protected share, so I simply keep any multimedia files and pictures I want to view with the PS3 in the public folder.

One problem I have found is that some mp3 filenames get corrupted when I copy them to the WD NAS. Whether that's an issue with samba or Western Digital (or something else), I have yet to discover.
