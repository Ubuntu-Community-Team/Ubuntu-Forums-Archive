### Post by antikristian on 2009-04-13
In the default layout of ubuntu you have a small icon that displays signal strength while connected to wireless networks in the upper right corner of the screen. It's called network manager (applet)

If you want to disconnect you can right click and select disable wireless, just remember to enable when you want to go back online.

If you want the list of available networks, left-click on the icon and a list appears.

If you do not have this icon, three things might have happend, you may have specified the network settings manually in system -> prefrences -> network connections

Or the applet might have crashed, restart it with alt+f2 nm-applet, or the taskbar might have been removed, add the taskbar by rightclicking on the panel and selecting "add to panel"
