### Post by tweedledee on 2007-01-09
> **darweth said:**
> I just noticed that alt-codes do not work.  Like Alt-0241 for an accented n... I noticed this was also the case in gaim under Windows.  Does it have something to do with alt-codes not being unicode?  Is there anyway to enable to them?

CTRL+SHIFT+U (you should see a little underlined "u" appear), then keep holding CTRL+SHIFT & type the unicode string.  Works in almost everything, is currently broken in openoffice.  The character map can be used to identify the unicode strings for each character.  Or, at least with gnome, you can just add a character palette to your panel & add the characters you frequently use (though I personally find the palette really ugly on my semi-transparent panel).
