### Post by wjaydavis on 2010-06-22
I have Ubuntu 9.10 on my desktop with VMware installed to use some windows software.  If I upgrade to 10.04 LTS, will I have to re-install VMware and all the windows software?

---

### Post by Revolutionary101 on 2010-06-22
It depends on how you upgrade to Ubuntu 10.04. If you do a clean install from a CD, yes all of your programs will be wiped. Although, if you update to 10.04 from the update manager, you will not have to reinstall your programs.

---

### Post by dabl on 2010-06-22
Your Windows VM will not be touched by an upgrade.

However, you can find many posts about problems with upgrades.  If your upgrade hangs or blows up or something, you might not be able to reboot your Linux system.  In that case, you might have to reinstall.

So, better save/copy your VM on another disk or partition.  That way, in the worst case, you might reinstall *buntu, then install VMware, then copy your VM from wherever you saved it.
