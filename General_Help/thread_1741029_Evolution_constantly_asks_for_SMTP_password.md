### Post by umiwangu on 2011-04-27
I'm working at a children's hospital here in Malawi, Blantyre (cure.org), and we're switching some of our workstations over to Ubuntu 10.04. For the most part, the change has been good. But.... Evolution just doesn't seem to work very well at all. We're using Google apps with our domain, and our connection is quite slow (512/128 VSAT for ~45 users). Evolution has a lot of problems sending and receiving email. I don't know if it's because of the slow connection, but before, we didn't seem to have problems with Outlook. I'm using the Mail.app on my Mac, and it sending/receiving is slow, but things eventually get through.

On the Ubuntu computers, Evolution really seems to get hung up on sending. It just sits there. I've had one user where their messages just sat for a day and never got sent. When I went in to look at it, I canceled, started the process again, and it asked for the SMTP password (which it seems to do a lot). Once it had the password, it was able to send. Receiving, however, was still very very slow and usually nothing comes in (although there are clearly new messages).

Maybe this title would be better put as, What other email client do you recommend? What about Thunderbird?

Any ideas as to why this might be coming up?

Thanks!

Seth (@cure.org)

### Post by apochry on 2011-04-27
Hi Seth,
although I am an Evolution fan and I'm using it all the time, I will recommend to switch to Thunderbird. Since you will use it in your work, I guess you need high reliability. Well, Thunderbird is more reliable, it's also simpler. Considering your slow connection, I think that TB will be better choice for your hospital. You could also try Claws Mail, it's pretty robust.
