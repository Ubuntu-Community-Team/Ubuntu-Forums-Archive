### Post by RobRobinson on 2014-07-01
Here is the (temporary) fix in the terminal mode:

First use cvt command:


robby@robby-Lenovo:~$ cvt 1920 1080

It returns:

# 1920x1080 59.96 Hz (CVT 2.07M9) hsync: 67.16 kHz; pclk: 173.00 MHz
Modeline "1920x1080_60.00"  173.00  1920 2048 2248 2576  1080 1083 1088 1120 -hsync +vsync

The I copy the part after Modeline into a new mode such:

robby@robby-Lenovo:~$ xrandr --newmode "1920x1080_60" 173.00 1920 2048 2248 2576  1080 1083 1088 1120 -hsync +vsync

and finally add the mode:

robby@robby-Lenovo:~$ xrandr --addmode VGA1 "1920x1080_60"

Ten, when I exit out of terminal mode and go into displays under system settings, behold!  The new dimensions are there in the options.

This only works untill you shut down or re-start the computer, then you have to do the same thing again.  Next, I have to figure out how to make it permanent.
