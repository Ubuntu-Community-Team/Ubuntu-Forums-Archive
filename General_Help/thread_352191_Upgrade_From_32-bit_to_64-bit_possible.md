### Post by phossal on 2007-02-03
I ask why because many of us with 64-bit architectures end up coming back to 32-bit Ubuntu for various reasons. There wasn't a specific advantage I noticed in using it, but there were significant *disadvantages*. Firefox plugins are a nightmare. I'm java/eclipse dependent, and I found the 32 Bit packages to be the most responsive (best performing) after trying every combination. I ran them on 64 bit Ubuntu, but when I realized my wireless card wasn't supported, I finally gave up and made the transition back. 

I don't regret it. Everyone gets their pleasure from something different, but I've yet to read an argument for 64-bit Ubuntu that was based on productivity or business needs.
