### Post by quixote on 2009-05-30
8.04, called Hardy Heron, is a long term support version.  That means you can get updates, incl security updates, for 3 years on the desktop version.  

8.10, Intrepid Ibex, is an interim version with a shorter support cycle because the assumption is you'll be following the 6-month upgrades.  For instance, as of April we're already on 9.04, Jaunty Jackalope, now.

Improvements come in all the time, so, really, I'd say the most recent is the best.  Some of us have had trouble with filesystem problems in 9.04, but if it runs on your hardware (eg Dells seem to have no problems), then it's the best so far.
