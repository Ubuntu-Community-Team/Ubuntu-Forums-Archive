### Post by Ubuntiac on 2009-07-02
Kdenlive shows up (on my KDE system) under Multimedia. If you're on Gnome, there's a good chance it's not showing up because it's a KDE app and Gnome and KDE have different menu systems (although many people work to try and get them as interoperable as possible).

Regarding Kino,

Kino is designed to ONLY edit .dv. Kino's developer now works with the MLT / Kdenlive projects, creating an editor capable of editing different formats.
