### Post by sdennie on 2008-10-27
Ubuntu can be "booted" VERY quickly if you don't shutdown your machine but instead use suspend/resume (if it's supported by your machine).  I don't want to sound argumentative but, I've never understood peoples obsession with boot times.  If you spend 1 hour improving your boot time and it goes from 60 seconds to 30 seconds (an extreme example), it will take you 120 reboots before you recuperate the hour you spent optimizing your boot time.
