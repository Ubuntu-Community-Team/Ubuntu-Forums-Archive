### Post by Wandering on 2008-05-13
It won't work!  

All the hardware drivers on the old install are for the stuff in your old machine, and they won't work with the much newer hardware of the new machine.

It is far more work to try to fix a kludge like that than it is to just do a clean install on the new machine, and then transfer over whatever data you want.

If you try it, most of the time, XP will just lock into an endless reboot loop and never start, because the drivers all fail to load, XP thinks they should, and it defaults to rebooting to make it happen. It won't happen. It won't happen in Ubuntu either, but I'm not sure how it would handle it.

Good luck.

### Post by strabes on 2008-05-13
Just back up your home directory from the old computer, install ubuntu on the new one, then copy your home folder contents over to the new installation. This will save all your user preferences. If you just install all the same packages as you have on your old computer you should be fine.

### Post by angryhomer17 on 2008-05-13
XP will have a problem with the new hardware. The last time I tried transferring my hard drive to new hardware, xp tried to install the new drivers. This may or may not work, esp since your rig is quite old.

Ubuntu may be better at recognizing that you have new hardware, but you also may run into problems. Unfortunately, this sucks.

I used to work IT, and we sometimes had to switch out hard drives. This worked, but only because, for the most part, we had very similar hardware, and the drives were imaged the same. But even then, we sometimes ran into problems.

I'd suggest backing up your data, trying the transfer out, and if it works, good. If not, well then you can just do a clean install and transfer your backed up data over.

Sorry this isn't easier.
