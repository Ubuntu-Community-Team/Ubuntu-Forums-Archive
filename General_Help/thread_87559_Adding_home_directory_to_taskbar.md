### Post by Who on 2005-11-08
Yep. 
I'm sure it is available by right clicking on the panel, choosing 'Add to Panel' and then selecting the right kind of applet. However, I am not at an Ubuntu box right now, so I will tell you a cumbersome way that will certainly work
Open a filebroswer, navigate to /home middle click on your home folder and drag it to the panel you want it on.  (If middle click doesn't work, then use a left click and drag)

You may be able to do the same thing by going to Places-->Computer and clicking and dragging the 'Home' Directory there.

HTH
Who

### Post by Arktis on 2005-11-08
Another way is to go to the applications menu > Accessories > right-click File Browser, 'add to pannel'.  Since nautilus defaults to the user's home directory when launched, you'll effectiveley have an icon on your taskbar that opens your home dir.
