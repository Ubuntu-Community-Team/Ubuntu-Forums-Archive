### Post by cariboo on 2010-06-16
If you didn't finalize the disk, you can add more data, but you can't overwrite the data on a DVD-R. If you have a DVD/RW then you can use it as many times as you want until it isn't usable any more.

### Post by lisati on 2010-06-16
> **zjmichen said:**
> There isn't ANY way?

DVD-R and DVD+R fall into the category "write once, read many". The only way I know of adding data is if you never finalized the disk, or if you used a "write many, read many" disk such as +RW or -RW.

Have a look here: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DVD-R](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DVD-R)

This wiki quote gives a brief explanation

[QUOTE=Wikipedia]The recording layer in DVD+RW and DVD-RW discs is a phase change metal alloy (often GeSbTe) whose crystalline  phase and amorphous phase have different reflectivity. The states can be switched depending on the power of the writing laser, so data can be written, read, erased and re-written. DVD-R and DVD+R discs use an organic dye.[/QUOTE]
