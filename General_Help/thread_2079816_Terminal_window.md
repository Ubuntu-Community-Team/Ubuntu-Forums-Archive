### Post by snowpine on 2012-11-02
You weren't born knowing how to launch Terminal in Gnome 2 by clicking the little tri-colored circle icon, going to the Applications, then the Accessories submenu, then finally clicking the Terminal icon. So you have to assume there will be a (very small) learning curve with the new Unity interface as well. :)

Personally I think the "tap Super key and type what you want to do" system is incredible easy, simple, intuitive--I can't say enough good things about its usability vs. trying to navigate through nested sub-menus of dozens of applications like in the old Gnome 2! But if that's not your thing then Ctrl+Alt+T is pretty easy, too. :)
