### Post by fogelfink on 2010-03-22
I am looking for a method to integrate Gmail into linux.
What I want to synchronize is: Mail, Contacts & Calendar

Can anyone tell me of a solution to have them all together in one application?

Here's what I tried and where it seems to fail me:

**Evolution:** has a terrible IMAP bug, i.e. when one tries to click onto the Gmail account folders instead of retrieving folder content, it collapses the folder view.  Calendar seemed to work, contacts as well.  Unusable as mail client.

**Kontact:** I am currently using KDE, so I would love to make use of Kontact, looks promising.  Excellent IMAP integration and once I got Akonadi configured Calendar sync with Google works fine.  But the address book is messed up: the google contact resource for Akonadi doesn't show address and phone numbers, only email-addresses.  Editing adresses in Kontact does not show up in Gmail, and vice versa.   Unusable as address book.

**Thunderbird:** In 9.10 the default repository version of Thunderbird is still version 2, for which there is no google contact addon.  Unusable as address book!  Installed Thunderbird 3: great IMAP support, Contacts work, but guess what: No 64-bit version of Lightning is available.  Unusable as Calendar.

In conclusion, I have to say I have not found an integrated Mail/Calendar/Contact client that truly integrates a Linux-desktop with my gmail-account - and I'm not looking for a working windows alternative, but would love a solution that works in Linux (64-bit).
