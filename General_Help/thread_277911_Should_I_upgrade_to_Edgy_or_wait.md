### Post by the_0ne on 2006-10-15
This is more a philosophical question, than a question of should I as a user.  I'm wondering if it's worth it for people like me to switch to Edgy or just wait for the next release.

I'm not a bleeding edge user.  I have a 3.0 ghz laptop, which I pretty much use full-time at home.  Writing code, email, browsing the net, those kind of things.  I have a 1.6 ghz machine server in my basement which hosts a usb external drive for backups and does things like samba, some things like that.  Both are running dapper.

I'm seeing things like this from news articles...

*Shuttleworth urged programmers to push the limits by adding new features to that version. "I would encourage members of the community who have been thinking of a cool new feature or plan to seize the opportunity to get it into Edgy. The tradeoff, of course, will be that some of these new ideas will not land perfectly first time. So there may be shakiness, or outright bumpiness, in Edgy," Shuttleworth said on his blog introducing the version. "Risk is good, when you give it a place and a time."*

I do like to stay current (in regards to ubuntu).  I don't like to upgrade 2 steps ahead or 2 steps behind.  However, I don't want the bumps.  I just want to be able to my work (web development, script development, messing with linux).

One thing I'm afraid of with not staying current to Edgy is the next version, will ubuntu support going from dapper straight to edge+1?  I know there were problems with people going from hoary straight to dapper instead of upgrading to breezy first.

So, what will you ubuntu users out there do that don't want bleeding edge, you just love ubuntu and want to use it for your work.  BUT, you also like to stay current?  Will you upgrade when Edgy is released and live with the bumps.  OR, will you wait for Edgy+1 and then possibly still go through bumps due to waiting 2 releases.

Thanks for any insight.
