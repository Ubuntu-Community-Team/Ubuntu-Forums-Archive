### Post by MG&amp;TL on 2012-04-25
There are a number of things you can do, but it's a question of what is worth the effort-for instance, compiling your own kernel will give you a small performance boost, but it's hours of effort for a potentially unstable result. the things I can think of right now are:

-play with desktop environments-if lubuntu's not your thing, try xfce, and unity2d.

-if you like unity3d, install compizconfig-settings-manager from the software centre, navigate to "Ubuntu Unity Plugin", click on it, head to "experimental" tab, then set "Dash Blur" to static or no blur-should make opening the dash a lot faster.
