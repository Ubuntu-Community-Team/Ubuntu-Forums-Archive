### Post by dwasifar on 2006-11-02
When I travel I usually take a few DVDs with me by ripping them to the HD before I leave.  Saves space and battery life.  

I recently switched my laptop from WinXP to Ubuntu.  I can rip the DVDs same as before, using DVD Decrypter, and I get the usual assortment of VOBs and BUPs and such.  Using PowerDVD in XP, I would just choose "Open DVD Files on Hard Drive."  But I can't find any such option in Totem or Kaffeine.  You can open a directory in Kaffeine, but the DVD menus don't work.  VLC works for this, but I'd rather use Kaffeine or Totem if I can.  Is it possible?

---

### Post by marc_c on 2006-11-22
Browse to the folder containing your DVD rips and right click on the folder of the movie you want to play and select "Open With -- > Open with "Movie Player".  Movie Player should open and start the movie.  If "Movie Player" is not an option, select "Open with other Application" and browse to "Movie Player" or "Kaffeine".  The next time you play a movie, it should be an available choice.
