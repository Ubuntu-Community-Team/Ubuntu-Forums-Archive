Bitcoin is a online currency that is maintain through a network of bitcoin miners

People donate processing power to earn bitcoins. However, bitcoin are computationally expensive.  It is inversely related to how  many compute power exist. With more people, it gets more expensive. The best way to mine is through a block. To give you an idea how expensive it is to make bit coins, a person with a top of the line ATI card cannot mine even .1 coin in a entire day of full operation. Mining in a block might migrate the effect a little and get a much better change of getting bitcoins

Right now bit coin algorithm is faster on ATI cards than Nvidia.

Here is card spread sheet to see the differences
[https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Mining_hardware_comparison#Card_Comparison_Spreadsheet](https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Mining_hardware_comparison#Card_Comparison_Spreadsheet)

Btw, the catch to earning bitcoin is that it will almost never be worth the cost to your system. There are too many people with high end machines.

Bitcoins are traded on a currency exchange. It kidda like changing euros to dollars or vice versa. However, bitcoin have an inheritance problem with deflation because there can only be a limited amount of bitcoins. Bit coin price do fluctuate like stocks so be wary

It is perfectly legal. Trades are non reversible

Edit: I forgot to mention. Do not get GCN ati cards. they are not as good as mining as VLIW cards

### Post by pakopako on 2012-11-05
> **Zirts said:**
> But, how does that block mining work ?       Do you have maybe some link to refer to about that?
Think of bitCoin mining like [Grid Computing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grid_computing). Someone has a project that needs a supercomputer. They can't afford one. They ask a bunch of people to volunteer timeshares of their home computers to help crunch numbers.

Some notable projects include SETI@home, Mersenne@home... bitCoin is [on the list](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_distributed_computing_projects) as well.

Someone, somewhere online, is trying to hack something. Be it a mathematical quandary or password, they are getting some reward or bounty for doing that. But they lack the hardware and cannot buy it quick enough so they ask the community to donate. And like how Kickstarters can have "reward-tiers", BitCoin miners get rewards for each "coin" (answer) they produce.
