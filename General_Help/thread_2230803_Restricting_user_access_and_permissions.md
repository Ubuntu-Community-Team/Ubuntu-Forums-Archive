### Post by bapoumba on 2014-06-21
I will only stress out that there is no better parental control than parents themselves.

I have 3 boys, quite grown up now, and never ever allowed them to use the computer on their own when they were young. Period. They had regular accounts with no admin privileges, nothing else. It took some time before they ran into some problems, heard about their friends experiences and each time we talked about it, why this is dangerous, why they should not be doing that, why this browser extension, how to use social networks, games only after homework is done etc.
They got their own computer when they were 13. Youngest one is 14 now. If they mess up, they either know how to clean up or ask. No screens whatsoever after dinner, except now they have grown up and (apparently :)) need it for school.

Any fancy setup, they'll find out how to get around. A parental control will never be a nanny or a parent. I have a full time job, the rule was no computer until I get home and no screen after dinner. If that would just leave them with half hour per day when preparing dinner, then that was it. They knew I could kick them out of the network after I did it once by accident testing out stuff.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-06-21
Make sure you son does not get to know your user password. It is an administrator password. Same applies to your credit card details.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/14.04/ubuntu-help/user-admin-explain.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/14.04/ubuntu-help/user-admin-explain.html)

Web browsers should have add ons for parental controls.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ParentalControls](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ParentalControls)

Oh, test to see if the controls actually work. Ask your son how to bypass the controls. You may be surprised by how much he actually knows. :)
