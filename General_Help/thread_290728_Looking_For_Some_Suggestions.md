### Post by Joe_CoT on 2006-11-01
I use a good ol' fashioned iRiver H320. Supports ogg, mp3,wma, and even video (with the korean firmware).

Newer iRivers have never been as good in my eyes. However, if you're willing to make the investment:

[Rockbox for iRiver](http://www.misticriver.net/forumdisplay.php?f=126)

[RockBox](http://www.rockbox.org/) is an open-source music player operating system which supports numerous mp3 players, including Archos, iPod and iRiver. Thus you can buy a newer iRiver, modify the firmware as instructed, and use RockBox to listen to music. I've used it on my H320 for over a year, and it works great, and obviously supports numerous codecs (though not wma), and has lots of other features (skins, plugins, etc). If you're willing to buy an mp3 player and then void the warranty :-D it's certainly worth a shot.
