### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-01-20
Did they actually _give_ it to you, as in you keep it from now on forever and ever?  Or is it still property of the school?

If it's actually yours, there may still be some stipulations that you not alter the system while it's in use at the school - but you may well be able to set up a dual-boot system for use outside school.

If it isn't yours, I wouldn't mess with it unless you get permission *in writing* from the responsible party in the school.  Who knows, they may just say yes, but odds are they won't.

It isn't worth loss of privileges / suspension / getting-charged-with-hacking (no, seriously!  It's happened before!) just to run Linux.  Wait until you have your own system, or get permission ahead of time.  (Again, *in writing*!  It's amazing how bad some peoples' memories can get at times...)
