### Post by Quackers on 2010-11-06
This is from the old sticky regarding Lucid Lynx but it may be worth you giving it a try

Ubuntu shuts down after unplugging Laptop power cord
A problem known with MSI wind and some Vostro users.

Current workaround is to open gconf-editor and browse to:
Code:

/apps/gnome-power-manager/general

And de-select the option use_time_for_policy

There is no need to restart, just close the configuration editor.

I also think I saw the same problem somewhere else on here too. Try the search perhaps.

### Post by bouncycastle on 2011-02-01
I had similar problem with three ubuntu 10.04  laptops shutting down when charger removed despite them  having fully charged batteries.    

 To solve it i clicked the battery icon in the top bar  and then <preferences>  which brought up the tick box options in power management ,in both "on battery" and "on  mains" options FOR  "when lid is closed" I chose   <blank screen> 

Afterwards when the charger/s were removed the screen went blank but could be restored with a simple mouse pad left click.

Some ubuntu users also have problem with laptops randomly switching off, it could be related to a loose charger plug producing the problem of intermitent  switch off  outlined in the post ,solution as above.
