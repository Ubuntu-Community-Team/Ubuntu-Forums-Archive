### Post by damu on 2007-02-24
For now, there is no way you can do audio production with Ubuntu, without a lot of hacking.
As for Ubuntustudio, the forum on this subject has been closed ages ago. Nowhere does it give a serious direction about the choice made for the coming  audio production platfrom. Will it support multi-core systems? will it come as a live-cd? etc? etc? etc? 

If you want to produce audio and music with linux , I would suggest to test [studio-to-go]("http://www.ferventsoftware.com/"). The version 2 which is in second beta release now is by far the best audio platform on linux ever produced : support of VSTs, jackdmp for multi core computers, qjadeo for video post production, compatibility with debian etch, are just some of the few top functionalities that you won't find anywhere else. One of the producer of Studio-to-go is the core developper of Rosegarden and many other open source audio tools on linux. So , not only you will have a state of the art support, but you will contribute to audio for linux.

Like[ 64studio]("http://64studio.com/"), it comes with all the main audio tools available on linux : Ardour, Rosegarden, Hydrogen, synths, plug-ins, etc. Studio-to-go comes as a live-cd, (which is so practical when you work with other musicians, sound studios) and 64studio needs an install on hard drive.

Good luck

### Post by lyceum on 2007-02-24
From the pages of Linux Format:

[Ardour 2.0](http://www.ardour.org/) Studio-quality audio recording. "Sounds like a revolution" (from the website)

[SoundTracker](http://www.soundtracker.org/) line up patterns to make a song.

[CheeseTracker](http://www.reduz.com.ar/cheesetronic/index.php?section=2) made #2 on their list for sound trackers.

Others I found:
[amSynth](http://amsynthe.sourceforge.net/amSynth/), [aRts Builder](http://www.arts-project.org/), [BEAST](http://beast.gtk.org/), Freebirth for bass (website was not working), 

you may also want to check out [JACK](http://jackaudio.org/). There jackbeat drum machine as well.
