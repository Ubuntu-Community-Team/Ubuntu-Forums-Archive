### Post by DeMus on 2010-10-17
Hi,
I have used Evolution in my previous setup. I did a complete re-install of the OS, for completely other reasons than the ones related to this subject, and started using Thunderbird as e-mail client again.
I setup my Gmail account and it received 2 messages in a still completely empty mailbox in Thunderbird. The mail was fetched too soon.
I then imported all my mail from Evolution, including calendar and address-book. Somehow the original 2 e-mails are lost, probably because the inbox contents have been overwritten by the import action.
The 2 messages are still available at Gmail. When I open my Gmail webpage I still can see them, but Thunderbird refuses to fetch them simply because it has done so already. Somehow the program knows this and doesn't fetch them again.
How can I get those two messages into my Thunderbird inbox. I use POP btw.

---

### Post by DeMus on 2010-10-17
Sorry to have bothered you but I found the solution. On my Gmail webpage I had to change the POP settings so my e-mail client (Thunderbird) was allowed to fetch messages which were fetched before. After that I returned to my normal settings.
I now have the 2 important messages and therefore mark this thread as solved.
