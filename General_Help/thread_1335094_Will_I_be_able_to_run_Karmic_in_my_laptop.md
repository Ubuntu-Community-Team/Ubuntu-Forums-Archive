### Post by prshah on 2009-11-23
> **dpc011 said:**
> I have a 1.4 GHz processor, 256 MB RAM, 150 GB hard drive Dell laptop. Can I run Ubuntu Karmic Koala
 
Will I face any problems with 2 OS in my laptop?


Even though you have 256MB RAM, some part of it will be used for graphics memory (Maybe 16MB)? While the live CD may run anyway, it may cause a problem during installation or be very slow. You can try it though, no harm will be done.

There will be no problem with running 2 OSs on your laptop.

You may not find Ubuntu very enjoyable with 256MB RAM. In the long term, I suggest you upgrade your RAM to 512/1024 MB.

Please note that aside from your Windows partition, you may have a "hidden" Dell recovery partition; please be careful not to overwrite this partition.

You will be able to partition the drive during ubuntu installation; I suggest you devote at least 20GB of space to Ubuntu (including /home), and atleast 1GB of swap.

Notwithstanding the above scary-sounding warnings, installation is usually easy and painless. Please post back with details if you want more help on any specific issues or problems during installation.

Good luck.
