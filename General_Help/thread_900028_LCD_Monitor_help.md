### Post by wannadumpwindows on 2008-08-24
You don't need anything special to drive an LCD over a CRT. I can use my computer run either one with a VGA cable. The only thing you really need to worry about is maximum resolution.

The graphics chip in the computer has a maximum supported resolution. If the monitor has a higher res than the card, you will only be able to run it at the chips max.

Other than that, it's all pretty much the same. You shouldn't really have anything to worry about. I don't imagine you're going out to spend $1000 on a 30" high def. wide screen, so it shouldn't be a problem.

### Post by mssever on 2008-08-24
One thing to note: LCD monitors have a native resolution. If you set your resolution to anything other than the native resolution, it'll look bad.

Other than that, there's no difference between an LCD and a CRT, as far as your computer is concerned.

(And yes, odds are that your laptop uses an LCD screen.)
