### Post by dbos on 2008-08-19
Hi,
Here is my issue:
I'm a university math student and want to get my math typing to be very fluid so I can digitize my notes as I take them because I'm hopeless at organizing hard copies of notes and I'm terribly bad at writing etc... I'm sure most people would recommend I suck it up, but instead I looked into "OpenOffice Formula" which allows converting latex text typed in writer into formulas which get embedded in the document. 

Thats great and all, but there are so many symbols that have to be typed out for simple things like greek letters and logical operators (e.g. having to type "%epsilon"). I even have a keyboard with tons of extra keys (logitech G15), but openoffice has a very limited number of available keys to set macros too. I would have to unbind things and conform to a very unpleasant layout that I could never memorize, since they don't allow the alt or super keys. I was able to make pleasant macros for making fractions, integrals and matrices that I can tolerate, but typing out all the greek letters, and symbols like "drarrow" for an "implies" sign will just be too inefficient considering how often I use them. I did investigate how this issue stands with the openoffice crowd and it seems they are unsure whether they want those keys to be available for use in macros so I guess I have to look for a different way.

Is there some software that offer a better solution or will let me bind the ~50 things I want to bind to keys that are somewhat natural choices, like the 18 totally unbound ones to the left on the G15 (ideally with modifiers too) outside of openoffice for use in openoffice?

I'm using regular ubuntu hardy

Thanks for giving my question your attention!
