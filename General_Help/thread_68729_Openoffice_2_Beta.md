### Post by heimo on 2005-09-24
Yes, actually you can upgrade only parts of your Hoary to Breezy, but its quite likely that it involves upgrading lo...ots of libraries. (I'm not sure, might be painless too.) You would have to learn a bit about apt pinning, but as the release of Breezy is so near, I wouldn't bother. 

If you have some extra time, bandwidth and disk space... you could download Breezy Live-CD and test how well it behaves on your system. Then you could make some space for another root partition and install Breezy on that (don't share your home dirs directly). Or you could clone your Hoary to another partition and try upgrading. :) Maybe you will find out that Breezy is already ready for you.

I always try to keep my systems as clean as possible - no packages outside package management. It makes life easier in the long run - you get updates from repositories (no manual upgrades), better integration with other applications and so on.
