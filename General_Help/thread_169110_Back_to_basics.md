If you really, REALLY want to learn about Linux... try Linux from Scratch: [http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/](http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/) That will give you a real grounding in how a Linux system works, under the hood. 

I don't know how well it will work on a 486 with no CD-ROM. I'd just throw a cheap, second hand CD-ROM in there. Your biggest issue will likely be ram, not processor speed, I'm thinking. 

Or better yet, if you have the room for it, take the old HDD out of the 486 and put it into the modern machine. Then you can dual boot a second distro. 

For learning Linux, Gentoo, Slackware and Crux are good Distro's to try, and less daunting to set up than LFS. 

Or you could do a 'server' install of Ubuntu. That gives you a working insall with the basic command-line utilities. 

Hope some of those ideas help,
