### Post by 23meg on 2006-01-13
I use amarok, even though I don't use KDE. Reason: it's beyond a player; it does just about everything needed to manage audio files. I usually don't like all-in-one solutions but amarok is an exception; its ID3 tagging is very intuitive, it transfers to ipod without the problems I have with gtkpod, the OSD feature is neat, collection management, contextual info and last.fm integration are excellent features.

Before amarok I used BMP, which is my second favorite. For video I use totem-xine and gxine.

### Post by Cliff76 on 2006-01-13
I used to futz with Xmms but it was always glitchy to me and never did quite what I wanted it to do... manage and organize my media like WinAmp. It got a little close when I combined it with Madman, which has awesome management features but lacks the ability to properly tag MP3s or manage WMA files. I then fell in love with Amarok which was a love/hate buggy relationship from the start. After a few upgrades it started to behave even better and now I can't live without it. I've come to rely on MPlayer for video because it looks way better than what I've ever seen in xine, gxine, totem-xine or whatever. (I suppose these apps would look much better in a Gnome environment.) I've used VLC under Windows and I know I can fall back on it if I ever find something that my other players don't reckognize but, to date, I've not used/touched it under Linux (just nice to know it's there). As far as Beep, I don't understand why everyone loves it so much. I just loaded it up for the first time ever right after reading this thread and it looks just like an Xmms clone. Maybe it's just a better Xmms, I dunno. I haven't used it but I'm curious. I have the same curiosity for the raving over totem I've seen in the past. To me all of the Xine based apps have confusing configurations and kludgy interfaces (though, like I've said that maybe because they need to run under Gnome). I will say this much though, MPlayer doesn't work as well for me since I switched to Kubuntu from Mepis and that's regrettable. Multimedia has always been an issue in Linux and I have to credit Mepis for getting it right out of the box. There has to be some optimizations (other than the one I just learned about in another thread) to get MPlayer working appropriately that I'm missing.

My reccomendation, used Amarok with MPlayer and install the MPlayer plugin for Mozilla/Firefox. That'll cover upwards of 90% of your multimedia needs. now for TV tuning there's a TVtime app or something that I was told about a while back...
