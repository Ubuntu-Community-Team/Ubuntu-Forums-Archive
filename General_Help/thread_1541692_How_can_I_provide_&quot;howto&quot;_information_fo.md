### Post by Herman on 2010-07-29
:D Read the fine print in your internet deal with your ISP and see if your ISP offers 'free' web space bundled with your existing internet account deal.  Many ISPs do, but only a few customers are aware of it. If your ISP does offer an amount of webspace as part of your internet deal, then you can set up your own web site there. If not, shop around and consider switching to a different ISP.
I use kompozer in Ubuntu for making .html pages and I upload them to my website by FTP. It's like anything, it's really quite simple after you try it for the first time.  Kompozer is as easy to use as a word processor. The advantage is, you can concentrate on the subject you're trying to explain without the need to keep thinking about translating it into hypertext markup language or html code at the same time. It allows you to get a lot more work done or have a lot more fun.

If you have a GMail account with google, (free), you can also have a free google website and you can use the google web page creator for publishing the content with. As with the ISP bundled web space, most GMail users are unaware of the existence of the free google websites. It requires some looking around in one's google account settings to find 'page creator', but once you try it out it will be simple enough to use and you can have a lot of fun with it, and help others. :D

I do have web space from my ISP and I also have a domain name of my own with some quite useful options at minimal costs. I have given serious thought to setting up something in my own space but it's a case of the optimum solution rather than the perfect solution. The drawback of html is a limited number of editors i.e. me. I could set up my own wiki but it feels like reinventing the wheel for the 100th time and there's the problem of managing contributions from other people. Then there's the question of how many people would actually see it. 

It is my observation that the internet tends to follow the "feast or famine" metaphor; you either get ignored or overwhelmed. I have enough interests in my life to keep me fully occupied for at least 48 hours in every day. I am willing to give some of my time to contributing details of my own experiences but a wiki of my own could be too much of a commitment. 

The internet and linux have the characteristic that it's easy to start something and then there's a very steep learning curve and a lot of unforeseen effort. One of my long term objectives is to make the initial learning curve much less steep by making basic knowledge more accessible.
