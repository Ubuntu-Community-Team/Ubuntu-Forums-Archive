### Post by darkhelmetchris on 2010-10-28
I have a ViewSonic PJ551D (1024x768 native) and previously had an Optoma EP719 (800x600 native).

I have had excellent results with both units.  Drivers are not required.  You just plug it in and start up the PC.  If you're on a laptop and you want to use it, plug it in (preferrably at power up) or go into System, Preferences, Monitors and pick the appropriate options for mirror screens or dual screens.  Easy pie.

Ubuntu treats your projector as if it were any other monitor.  Most recent projectors (like monitors) will support plug-and-play handshaking and will show up easily under Ubuntu when detected properly by your video adapter.

As a side note, I did enable the accelerated video driver for my video card, so if you don't see your monitor/projector properly detected, you might first try System, Admin, Hardware Drivers / Additional Drivers and activate yours, if applicable.
