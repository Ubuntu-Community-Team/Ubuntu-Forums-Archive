### Post by Ubun2to on 2012-05-16
I have found 1 paid option (I haven't dug very deep to find free options yet). PGP disk encryption is compatible with 10.04 64 bit, so I would contact Symantec to see if they have any plans to make it work with 12.04.
[http://www.symantec.com/products/sysreq.jsp?pcid=pcat_info_risk_comp&pvid=wd_encryption_1](http://www.symantec.com/products/sysreq.jsp?pcid=pcat_info_risk_comp&pvid=wd_encryption_1)
You could set a root password in your BIOS, which is free, and works pretty well. Only pay for encryption software if you have sensitive data (like top secret plans, financial info, or hospital records), and always keep a copy of the key on a sticky note or something that you can easily access.
If you just need to keep that lazy roommate from stealing your 20 page that is essay due to the professor tomorrow (who always manages to guess your account password), set a root password (and make sure it's one you will remember, and someone close to you has a copy of it, such as your parents).
