### Post by roderick on 2008-05-27
AFAIK, Lexmark printers (at least any made within the last few years) do NOT work (well if at all) with Linux, as there are no (decent) drivers for this printer.

HP works at a near 100% as they support Linux and contribute to drivers and an installation utility for the printer.

Lexmark has refused to contribute anything in recent years.

Anyway, some people have had some success (moderate/partial). You can find some answers here if the previous posters suggestion doesn't work:

[http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Lexmark-2500](http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Lexmark-2500)

If you have the ability to return the printer and get an HP, it would be worth it to get better support. Remember to verify at openprinting.org before making a printer purchase.

Cheers.
