### Post by aysiu on 2010-06-29
I don't think going the Firefox route is going to do it. Firefox (and most other web browsers) now have something called "private browsing" (what some people call "porn mode"), which does not keep history, cookies, or cache from sites visited during that session. So unless you're copying over the cache *while* your son is looking, your plan just isn't going to work.

What you can do, which your son may also be able to bypass if he's resourceful enough, is use [OpenDNS](http://www.opendns.com/) at the router (not computer) level to monitor what sites he's been going to... or just to filter out "inappropriate" sites altogether.

### Post by nothingspecial on 2010-06-29
I wouldn`t let my sons near an admin account.

Create his own account anyway so he doesn`t hose your system one day.

I use a content filter on their accounts with a really secure password. If they try to access anything with certain words on the page, a message pops up saying DAD SAYS NO!

Something else unrelated is [COLOR=Magenta][timekpr]("https://launchpad.net/timekpr")[/COLOR] which you can use to limit the amount of time he spends on the computer.

For real snooping, I access the computer remotely from another room to see what they are doing.

For simplicity you could use the remote desktop viewer that comes with Ubuntu. If set up his account so he can`t change the settings, then set it up so that no icon or warning appears when you access it, then you can snoop away.

Just don`t touch the mouse :-\"
