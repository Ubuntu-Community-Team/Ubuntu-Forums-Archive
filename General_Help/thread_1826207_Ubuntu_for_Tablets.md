### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-08-16
Actually, I have a Tablet PC (one of the original Fujitsus) and USED to have Ubuntu running on it.  That was YEARS ago when Tablets were new and all the rage (long before Google hit the scene).

It was a major effort to get everything working on the Tablet -- but with the help of folks from these forums, I was able to do that.  I got screen rotation to work, the stylus to work, even the bezel keys (absolutely necessary in Tablet mode where the keyboard was hidden), even was able to detect the second battery.

AND, it ran SO MUCH BETTER than the Vista install on the Tablet.

And then, Ubuntu 8.10 came out -- and almost nothing worked anymore.  

Have been trying Ubuntu LiveCDs since then (including 11.04) and there has been no improvement to things not working.

Folks with ThinkPads have fared better, since there is a Wiki devoted to that, but when Win7 came out, and everything worked again, I had to abandon the Ubuntu install on my Fujitsu and go with Win7.  I needed to have it working in Tablet mode and Win7 did that.

There are folks on this forum that continue to provide GREAT support for Tablets -- but the overall Linux community support apart from them is very sparse.

Until a worthy competitor to the Ipad2 comes out in quantity, I don't expect Tablet support to pick up much.
