### Post by ajgreeny on 2015-04-26
Personally, I think thunderbird is hard to beat, and I have not used any other client for so long that I am not able to suggest anything else.

However, I suspect that your problems may be a result of a corrupt profile so I suggest you rename the ~/.thunderbird folder in your home as a backup then add back all your mail accounts to see if that solves the problem.

Any mail that you may have downloaded in the past will still be sitting in the mail folder in the old profile, which is why I suggested a rename rather than deletion.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-04-26
Email client choices are pretty limited. If you search for something like "Linux mail clients" you keep seeing the same few apps all the time.

 Evolution has been used in Gnome for umpteen years.  Recent versions work well for me on Ubuntu.  It's a serious tool and it's in the repos.

Geary is a newish mail app that seems geared toward less-than-heavy mail use.

KMail is there for KDE.

But, yes, before you jump into the hassle of switching mail clients, try to get Thunderbird working right.
