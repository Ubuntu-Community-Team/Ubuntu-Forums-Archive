### Post by ted_rmt on 2011-05-06
Hooray! Solved for one person anyway.

I only found the session bar by accident when playing with Ubuntu netbook remix 10.10. A very fortunate accident.

It caught me by surprise and I was wondering why I never saw it before, then realised it is because[COLOR=Blue]** the session drop-down list does not appear unless you click your user name first.**[/COLOR]

That opens the password input box and at the same time, three drop-down lists appear at the bottom of the screen. You can then click the sessions tab and choose Classic Ubuntu.

As previously stated, if you are set to log in without a password, you will not see any of these options and Unity is your daddy.

Perhaps in a later distribution, they will ask if you would like session options to appear by default. Only reason I can think of that they made them hide was that they wanted the login screen to look simpler . .  . until you click something you have to click anyway.
