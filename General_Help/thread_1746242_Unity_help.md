### Post by r-senior on 2011-05-01
The applications list in the Unity Dash uses Zeitgeist, which tracks your patterns of application usage and makes your most used apps most prominent. Until it learns what you use, the list of applications will seem random, so it's worth persevering.

Quickest way to run an app is:

a. Clicking on the launcher.

b. Hit the Ubuntu button or Super key and type the first few characters of the application you want to run. It's reasonably intelligent, so if you type "spread" for example, it offers LibreOffice Calc (spreadsheet).

When the Dash is visible, you can drag your most commonly used items to the launcher, or when an app is running, right-click on its launcher icon and click "Keep in Launcher".

c. If you want to use the Applications lens, note that you can also right-click to narrow down the list of applications it offers. As I say, it will improve its suggestions the more you use any of the methods above. Remember also that in the "Installed Applications" section in the Dash, you can also expand the initial list it gives you.

Do also have a look at the guide and videos:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1742326](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1742326)
