### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2012-12-28
sounds like a graphics driver problem.  After the screen goes black, try hitting "ctrl+alt+f1" and see if it drops to a prompt.  If it does, then there is simply a problem with X or you drivers.  If nothing happens then you may have a bad CD and creating a disk at the slowest burn speed possible may be the next logical course of action.

[EDIT]

On second thought, please clarify something.  Do you mean that the computer will not boot into the live CD, or you have already tried to install Ubuntu unsuccessfully and now the computer fails to boot from the hard drive?  Either way, this sounds like a bad burn.  Download a new copy, burn it to a CD at the slowest speed possible, or if you have 1GB or larger flash drive handy, put your installer on the USB and boot from that instead.
