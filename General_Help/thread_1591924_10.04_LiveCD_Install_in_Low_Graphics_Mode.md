### Post by terran4000 on 2010-10-12
Glad my site helped you! Also thanks dcstar for linking to it!

Just to note, after I posted the article ... is that those options may or may not work when trying to get into a 'safe graphics mode' AFTER installation. Meaning, after you got everything installed and going into the real boot.

If you need a 'safe graphics mode' on your actual installation, I've found one of the only ways for it to work is to use a boot cd. 
* boot into the live cd
* mount the root partition
* edit /etc/X11/xorg.conf and change the driver to 'VESA'
* save and reboot

Best of luck!
