### Post by coffeecat on 2006-10-30
A common cause of inexplicable freezes is faulty RAM. I had a similar problem down to not one, but **two** faulty sticks. :(

First thing - by all means try memtest86, but it's only of use if it shows a failure. Faulty RAM can still pass memtest - mine did. Some people say run memtest for at least 24 hours or for a few days. Well - hmm.

Suggest taking out the RAM and reseating it. Or better - beg, buy, or borrow a compatible stick and try running the machine with that.

A few questions: Do you remember changing anything just before the trouble started? How old is the machine? How old is the RAM?
