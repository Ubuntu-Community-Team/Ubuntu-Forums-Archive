### Post by Epidural on 2010-06-15
I wasn't entirely sure where to post this, but recently my main computer took to shutting down and rebooting itself over and over again every once and a while (maybe once a week, it would reboot 6-10 times before successfully rebooting). Now, it won't turn on at all. When I take the cover off, I can see that after being completely powered down (unlpugged), then plugged back in and having the power button pressed, the CPU fan budges slightly; nothing else happens. No hums, no PSU fan, nothing. If I remove the 12V CPU power 4-pin connector, the fans spin up and it sounds like it's booting (but of course, the CPU has no power). Does this for sure mean my CPU is fried?

### Post by bigsmitty64 on 2010-06-15
I had this exact issue a couple years ago, ended up being the motherboard. Just for the sake of saying you tried, I would try a different cpu fan, if that fans toast the cpu will heat over heat in a second, possibly causing the reboot, and eventual no start. Also, try re-seating the ram AND graphics card.Try everything no matter how dumb an idea you think it is. Good luck man.

### Post by Epidural on 2010-09-16
This is a very old thread, so sorry, but it's been solved: The power connector to the CPU (the 4-pin) must have something wrong; blown this or short-circuited that. Either way that is the problem. In other words, the mobo is fried.

Thanks to those that helped.
