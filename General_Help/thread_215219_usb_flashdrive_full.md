### Post by Nonno Bassotto on 2006-07-14
I had problems with the recognition of the dimension of pen-drives too. I don't know how to fix it, and I'm interested too. Anyway, the following not very satisfying trick worked for me.

Go on a computer that recognizes your pen-drive correctly (for example a windows one) and fill it as much as you can. Then plug it into your Ubuntu box: the system will recognize that your drive has at least x space, where x is the dimension of the files on it. Now empty it, this won't change the dimension of the drive, and you will be able to use your space.

### Post by Canguçu on 2006-08-27
Maybe earlier the file manager "deleted" some files, moving it to a hidden .Trash folder in your thumbdrive. It happens often.

To verify it, plug in your thumbdrive, open it on the file manager, and click on "view" menu and in the option "hidden files".

If there is a .Trash file in your thumbdrive, delete it.

To delete files on a thumbdrive without really moving it to a .Trash folder, when you want to delete a file, hold the shift key and then delete it (not recommended in your hard drive! do it only in devices with low capacities,
