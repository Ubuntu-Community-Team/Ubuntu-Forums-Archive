### Post by GregBrannon on 2010-11-29
Based on the history you've shared so far, this may be more than you're capable of doing (I mean that in the nicest way possible):

1. Build a Rescue CD (usually accomplished by downloading an .iso file, writing it to a CD)

2. Boot your computer from the Rescue CD to determine the current state of your hard drive.  If there is a recoverable Windows partition, the Rescue CD should tell you and offer to do it for you.  If there is NOT a recoverable Windows partition, the Rescue CD should tell you.

Note: I say CD out of habit, but you can also build a bootable USB, HD, Etc., whatever works best for you.  Both of the sites below have the tools you need with instructions.

Possible tools for the job:

[SystemRescueCD]("http://www.sysresccd.org/Main_Page")

[GParted -- Live CD/Etc.]("http://gparted.sourceforge.net/livecd.php")

You'll know better what your options are once you know what you currently have.

If you decide to continue to be your computer's doctor, please be patient, don't select options to write, format, partition, wipe, etc. without understanding what you're doing and accepting that it's your best option considering the circumstances, and come back if you need help.

The tools above are meant to help one navigate out of undesirable situations, but they can be used to drive further down the road to ruin if not used 'responsibly.'

Good luck!
