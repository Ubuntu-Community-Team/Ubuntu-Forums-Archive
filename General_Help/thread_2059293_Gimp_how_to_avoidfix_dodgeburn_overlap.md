### Post by t0p on 2012-09-17
I'm working on a photo: the sky's a tad pale and needs burning.  But there are fiddly hills and stuff in the background.  So I can burn in most of the sky okay; but when I switch to another brush size to burn in the sky round the tops of the hills, I get that "double burn" effect where the 2 instances of burning overlap.

How can I avoid this problem?  (I really do want to burn in the sky, so please don't suggest I avoid the problem by not doing any burning in the first place.  Thank you.)

---

### Post by robtygart on 2012-09-17
Work in layers, have a layer for each edit.

Also your brush has a brush pressure setting, and each layer should have an opasity setting, if you over cook anything you can drag it back and tone down that layer.
