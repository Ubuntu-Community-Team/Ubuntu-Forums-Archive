### Post by pirlo89 on 2010-03-08
I had that problem, and it always turns out to be the hard drive.

1-If you have an external hard drive (a big one with a power supply) connected to an outlet, and that hard drive is not turned on, then you will hear a high pitched sound.

2-If you have an internal hard drive (other than the one your OS is booting from), then it might go to standby mode (will be turning off and on) and you will hear a buzzing sound every now and then.

The solution: disconnect the hard drive your not using. Or if its an internal one, set it up so that it becomes powered on all the time.

### Post by The Cog on 2010-03-08
The scariest thought is that it could be the hard drive on the way out. I have known hard drives that buzz intermittently, depending on which area of the disk it left the heads hovering over. Buzzing when running windows but not when running Ubuntu could fit that description. Heads rubbing on areas of the disk is not good - they should float on a cushion of air without touching. If that's it, then it is only a matter of time before it dies completely, so you'd better make sure your backups are all updated regularly.
