### Post by CocoAUS on 2007-02-04
On a said note, as much as I love Gaim, you might be better off using Kopete.

Gaim 2 has been in beta for over a year, now, and the development seems incredibly slow.  One of the lead developers was taken by Google, though he still contributes some to Gaim.  They promised voice and video support in version 2, but have now said that they *hope* to include it in version 3.  Again, Gaim 2 has been in beta for over a year now, so just imagine how long it will be until Gaim 3 comes out.  The website is horribly, horribly out of date, and Gaim removes more and more options each time they put out a new release.

Kopete, as flawed as it is, has active development, and includes (for MSN, anyway) voice and video support.  It is also a "proper" Jabber client, and whether you use Gnome or KDE, it'll work just fine with your desktop.
