### Post by hikaricore on 2008-12-14

Moving right along, your best bet is to just get a new drive if that's a possibility.
There's nothing you can do to make bad sectors good again, this is a sign of a drive going bad and should not be taken lightly.  If this really is the case you're probably setting yourself up for some data loss which you do not want.  I recently lost a 1Tb drive by not paying enough attention to the signs at hand >.< not fun at all.

If on the other hand your drive isn't going bad, copying everything to another drive or cds/dvds that you need and wiping the drive completely might get you your drive space back.  Be aware that you will always get less drive space than advertised, also depending on the filesystem in use you will lose a lot of space just for formating and to reserve space for file tables and such.
