### Post by PaulW2U on 2018-06-20
> **jgw said:**
> First, I thought I would do the ask ubuntu thing.  I am logged in here so I assumed I was completely logged into ubuntu.  I guess not and my login doesn't work.  I don't understand.
Ask Ubuntu is not an official Canonical site. It's part of the [StackExchange]("https://stackexchange.com/") network. Your Ubuntu One sign-on won't work there although you can connect your Launchpad profile if you have one as part of the sign-up process.
> **jgw said:**
> I also went to my profile to check my emails.  I noticed that I have exactly 0 views even though I have some with replies.  Don't understand how this is possible.
I'm assuming that you went to your forum profile page to view your recent posts. The number of views to the forum's threads and posts doesn't work at present and it seems there is no hurry by Canonical IS to fix it. Even when the view count did work it would include hits by various bots and web crawlers so it simply gave an exaggerated count of the number of users actually reading a post or thread.
> **jgw said:**
> I posted an email about a network connect enp1s0, never had any views or replies.
Presumably you mean you created a forum thread to ask for help. It may well have had views that were not registered but if no one has replied then you can add a post to the existing thread with the word "Bump". This will move the thread to the top of any search by other users when they search for new posts to reply to. You can do this once every 12 hours or so.
