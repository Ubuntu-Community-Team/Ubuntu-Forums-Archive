### Post by sudodus on 2013-02-18
Lubuntu would be my favourite, but ***Xubuntu 12.04 LTS*** with support until April 2015 is also a very good alternative.

Download several iso files and install them to create your boot USB drive and try them live before deciding which flavour is best for you.

It depends a lot on what you intend to do with your netbook, what programs you will use.

- Do you need maximum speed?
- Do you want some special effects?
- Do you plan to keep Windows and have dual boot?
- Have you considered booting from USB or from an ISO file?

### Post by briggers on 2013-02-18
I have used Xubuntu in various versions for a few years on an Acer Aspire One A110 and have found it to be adequately fast and fine for a netbook. The AOA110 has a N270 processor which is much the same as a N450.

Alternatives:
Peppermint Linux if what you mainly want is to be web centric - its small and fast but has full access to the Ubuntu repos
Puppy Linux  if you want to run mainly from a USB stick - I don't think it works so well if installed on the hard disk. Very small and fast.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-02-18
> **Mateusz91 said:**
> **eM350 with Atom N450 is very slow netbook. I think about good distro for this machine.**

My ideas:

Lubuntu 12.10
Mint 14 with Mate
Ubuntu with Fluxbox
There are really two major concerns with choosing a distro -- performance and drivers.

In terms of performance, Lubuntu is a much better choice for such a lower powered PC than the other two.  Neither Mint nor Ubuntu is going to perform well.

In terms of drivers, you really don't know until you try -- which is why the suggestion by sudodus is so important -- in terms of booting from a CD or USB and trying out the distro BEFORE you install it.
