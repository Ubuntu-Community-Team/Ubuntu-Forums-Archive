### Post by silverwolf636 on 2009-03-03
I know there's been some controversy as to what motherboards (like Foxconn) will work with Linux (or Ubuntu in particular). Well, I recently installed a new BIOSTAR Ver 6.x P4M900-M4 Motherboard in my system and upgraded to 2G Ram.  I used my hardrives from my previous 1.7G with 1G ram system.  The chipset is VIA P4M900 and VIA VT8237A.  Graphics is Chrome9 HS 3d/3d with a max of 256M ram. Sound is ALC861VD 5.1 channels audio out, high-def audio support.  The documents states that it is Windoze 2000/XP/Vista compatible with reserve to add or remove any OS. 
I just wanted to write that the motherboard was a charm to setup. I set it up with my two harddrives without reformatting them from my previous system.  The only hold up was the vid.  I did find this thread with info for that vid **VIA Chrome9 Beta Driver Released! WOOT!**([http://linux.via.com.tw/support/downloadFiles.action](http://linux.via.com.tw/support/downloadFiles.action)) and installed it.  With my previous systems I never did have much luck with KDE 4+ but for Gnome and KDE 3.5, it works.  The highest resolution I could get though was only 1024x768.  If anyone would like more specs on this motherboard, just let me know.  

Oh yea, my CPU is an Intel 2Ghz and the MB is upgradeable to 3.4Ghz. And I have not tried the hibernate yet.
