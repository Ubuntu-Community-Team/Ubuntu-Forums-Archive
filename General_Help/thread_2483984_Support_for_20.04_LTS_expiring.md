### Post by BBQdave on 2023-02-14
[https://ubuntu.com/blog/what-is-an-ubuntu-lts-release](https://ubuntu.com/blog/what-is-an-ubuntu-lts-release)

This article breaks down the support structure for LTS. My understanding, some applications may no longer receive patches because new versions exist that the application developers now support.
So your core system will be patched, but some (maybe most) of your applications are older versions no longer supported by their developers. Doesn't mean your applications will stop functioning, just no more development on that version.

On a side note: that is what *snap* is trying to address. Current versions of an application on a stable long term support system.
