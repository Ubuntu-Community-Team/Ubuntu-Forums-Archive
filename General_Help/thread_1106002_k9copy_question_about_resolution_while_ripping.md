### Post by punx45 on 2009-03-26
i dont know what options you have with k9copy, but the best thing you could do for a DVD (if space is no object) is to image the disc, or pull the whole TS structure off the disc.  (may still need ripping software to eliminate CSS) players like VLC can play these, and there is no recompression, so you have the full quality of the disc.  the downside is that you are looking at as much as 9GB per disc if its a dual layer.   since you are viewing on a big screen, this would be the best option.   if compression is a must, h.264 mpeg4 is my favorite
