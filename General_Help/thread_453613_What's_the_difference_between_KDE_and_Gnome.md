### Post by pbw on 2007-05-24
Here's a little comparison article that might help explain some things as well (with a couple screenshots) - [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome)

Quick Summary:
> 
Generally, KDE focuses on offering as many features as possible with as many graphical ways as possible for configuring those features. Fans of KDE highlight the functionality it has. Critics of KDE say the menus are too confusing. 

 Gnome, on the other hand, opts for simplicity and often hides certain configurations in order to achieve that simplicity. Fans of Gnome think the simplicity of Gnome offers a cleanliness that allows the user to get stuff done. Critics of Gnome think it just lacks certain functionality. 

 Once again, this comparison touches on only the surface of the differences and similarities between Gnome and KDE. As you can see, both offer the same functionality&#8212;theming, file management, device management, etc. It's up to you to pick the desktop environment that works for you.

### Post by thayerw on 2007-05-24
You can read all you want, but you won't know until you try them both.  It comes down to the type of user you are.  They both have pro's and con's.

KDE allows you to customize virtually EVERY aspect of your environment, from the desktop to the core applications.  For some, this can be overwhelming.  GNOME is considered by many to be a simplified desktop, with minimal options so as to not confuse the user.  Some may argue that point and this could easily turn into a heated discussion, so your best bet is try them for yourself.

I ran Ubuntu with GNOME for nearly 6 months, but now my wife and I are both running Kubuntu with KDE and we are never looking back.
