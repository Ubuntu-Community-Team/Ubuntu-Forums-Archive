### Post by The Cog on 2011-07-22
Sorry, but my guess is that the drive has died. If it was a desktop, I'd say to try unplugging and replugging the drive cables, but that's tricky in a laptop. 

You might be tempted to think that Ubuntu broke the drive, but I think that's unlikely - I think it's probably just coincidence. I've been using Ubuntu on laptops for years without issue.

You might be able to confirm that the drive appears to have disappeared by booting from a live CD or USB, and using the command > sudo lshw | less and reading through the list of discovered hardware.
