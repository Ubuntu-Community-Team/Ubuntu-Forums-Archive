### Post by fluffman86 on 2009-09-28
Linux has something like 1 or 2 percent market share on the Desktop.  Adobe would spend more money than they would make to port all of their products to Linux.  At least they give us flash player and reader! :) :P

Anyway, here are some Adobe alternatives you might like.  If not, then you can use virtualbox to run them, and some will even run in wine. 
[http://www.winehq.org](http://www.winehq.org)

Synfig is an animation studio, like Flash. 
[http://www.synfig.org/Download](http://www.synfig.org/Download)

I like Kompozer for WYSIWYG HTML editing.  Like Dreamweaver-lite...if that existed.
[http://kompozer.net/](http://kompozer.net/)

I know that Photoshop CS2 runs in wine, but I find GIMP to be an adequate replacement.
[http://www.gimp.org/](http://www.gimp.org/)

You can download the latest versions of the programs from the websites listed, and all of them are in the repositories as well.  Just search Applications > Add/Remove... for them.
