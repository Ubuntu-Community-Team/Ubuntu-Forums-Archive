### Post by Trafferth on 2010-06-23
Hey frenchn00b,

I am a researcher in physics, spectroscopy and physical chemistry, and I use Origin pretty much every day. Of the programs you list, the closest is LabPlot, although QtiPlot (which is not in your list) is even closer.

I would say, however, that both QtiPlot and LabPlot are a bit buggy.  QtiPlot, for example, is quite flaky when importing existing .opj files.  Both are in the Ubuntu repositories.

I have had limited success in running Origin under WINE; Origin Version 7.5 Pro works more-or-less as under Windows.  I have not tried running Version 8.0 under WINE yet; I simply re-boot to Windows in order to use it.

Of the other programs you list and that I have tried, GNUPlot is a useful command-line plotting tool, but there are no analysis features.  The output is produces is quite good, however.  Octave is pretty much line-for-line compatible with MATLAB, so if you have used MATLAB before you should have zero trouble converting.  You can even use your existing scripts with minimal (or sometimes no) modification.  SciLab is also quite similar to MATLAB, and it has a nice GUI too.

I hope this helps a bit.  Feel free to ask further questions if you like.

Regards,

Trafferth
