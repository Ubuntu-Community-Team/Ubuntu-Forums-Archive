### Post by hansdown on 2009-07-17
Hi Splibiff.

A long term support version is very stable. You'll notce that the kernel tends to be somewhat older than the newer releases. This gives it the stability.

You may see what a kernel upgrade can sometimes do to your system. The 
developers are very quick to fix any problems that may come up.

Kudos to the devs!

### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2009-07-17
Imagine you have 200 machines running Ubuntu for your company.  All they run is a web browser, open office, and some kind of home-brew application to manage the production of cheese graters.

You want to make sure all your systems are patched for major bugs and security flaws, but how often do you want to upgrade 200 machines to a new OS version?  Every three years, or every six months?
