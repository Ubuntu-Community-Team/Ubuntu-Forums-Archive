### Post by GreyFox503 on 2006-02-24
I think you can just remove them with Synaptic.  Open it up and do a search for "linux".  You will get quite a few results, but a few of them will be the kernels installed and available for your system.  There you can uncheck the 386 stuff if you're sure you're not using it.  Be careful uninstalling stuff here!  :)
