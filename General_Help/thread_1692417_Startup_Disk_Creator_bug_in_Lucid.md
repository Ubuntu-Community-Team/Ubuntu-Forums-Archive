### Post by sites on 2011-02-21
This is just a heads up for anyone else having this problem.  I didn't find anything about it with a search.

I just downloaded the 10.04.2 release of Lucid & attempted to create a usb startup disk using this new iso, but the installer wouldn't allow it.  The options for customizing free space & such remained grayed out.  The workaround is simple enough.  Instead of choosing the new .2 iso I selected the old 10.04.1 iso which then gave me access to the previously grayed out options.  When I switched back to the .2 iso those options remained available & startup disk creation completed successfully.
