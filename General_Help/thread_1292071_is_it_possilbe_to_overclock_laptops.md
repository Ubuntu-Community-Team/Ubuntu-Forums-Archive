### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-15
Yes, but you'll likely have to provide your own BIOS and you will definitely void the warranty.

The best way to boost a laptop's performance is to swap the CPU for a faster model in the same form-factor (eg, Socket P). Bigger cache is better than more Ghz.

[Here's a blog]("http://miket-blog.blogspot.com/2008/03/dell-vostro-1500-cpu-upgrade-to-penryn.html") about some guy modding out his Dell Vostro 1500.

I modded the heck out of my Gateway 7811FX: New wifi (4965AGN), new CPU (P9500), dual 500G hard drives....
