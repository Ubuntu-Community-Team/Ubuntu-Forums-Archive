> **Smarty_T2 said:**
> Thanks a lot for the links....:)

Ok, well, someone has probably already told you, but I'll take a go also, SWFlash, the Flash pluggins, and gstreamer (and others) are available but only through software repository (synaptic package manager, and|or Applications Add/Remove)

The flash stuff is closed and proprietary, so for legal as well as philosophical reasoning they are not shipped with Ubuntu. But, they are available for download, easy to install and use, and work great. 

At one point in my linux career I was more of a zealot bigot purist about open code and would use "nothing" except Gnu Linux (or equivelant like Debian or Ubuntu) strictly. I still feel this way (pedigree Richard Stallman, FSF) generally speaking; however, I do realise that open standards (coming soon enough) are going to take time overall. 

I am interested in promoting open standards, and pushing for that goal across the board is the correct viewpoint--- Debian, Ubuntu (etc), and others with Gnu Linux mindset are clearly working hard in this direction. 

I do not use wine at all any longer... in fact, I have no need for windows at all either. Like anything else if you force yourself to live without it sooner or later you will realise you never needed it.
