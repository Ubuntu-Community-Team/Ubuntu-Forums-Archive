### Post by akiro.yamamoto on 2006-03-11
The w32codecs can't be added to the default system due to distributions
restrictions. It is legal to redistribute the codecs only in some countries 
(Non US), if they added it to the distro they would be inviting legal action
from M$ Gates et-al. I think some distros (Xandros) provide them but they
pay a fee for each user that is part of the cost of the distro. Ubuntu ships
free of charge as well as free ship ;) Someone would have to pay the fees
either the user or the distributer. Of course you could move/copy the
codecs from your M$ install (if legal in your contry :rolleyes: ).
