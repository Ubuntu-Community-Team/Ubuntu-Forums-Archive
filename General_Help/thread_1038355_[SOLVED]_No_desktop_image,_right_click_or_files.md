### Post by hikaricore on 2009-01-12
It is possible you've disabled XFCE's control over the "desktop" somehow.

Let me go look at my Xubuntu system real quick and I'll tell you where to look for the options to try and solve this.

---

### Post by hikaricore on 2009-01-12
Applications / Settings / Settings Manager / Desktop

There should be a tickbox that says "Allow xfce to manage the desktop".

Is this checked?
