**Hey everyone,**

I am a tech admin for a small K-12 school.
I'm fairly new to Linux, I know just enough to install and tweak the standard Ubuntu the way I like it.

What I have the most questions about is choosing the best distro (or version of a distro) for each computer.

For example,

I have some Gateway laptops that came with XP but were around the time when they were "Vista Capable" 

Those with 1-2 GB of RAM run 12.04 with a few tweaks wonderfully.

What I am really interested in is bringing some of our older computers back to life with Linux.

The specific computers I am thinking of have Intel Celeron single core processors and 512MB of RAM.

They were sold with XP on them, but even XP doesn't run as well as I and the users would like.

Which distribution and version of distribution (Kernel version?) would you recommend and any advice on how I would choose this would be great.

### Post by sidzen on 2012-10-08
LiveCD (no install necessary) [DouDou]("http://download.doudoulinux.org/") Linux deserves a look -- minimal requirements are 256MB RAM and a 800MHz processor.

IMHO, even lubuntu and peppermint would be stretching it a bit, performance-wise, with the single-core Celeron and 512MB and FSB speeds you are talking about.  LiveCDs have their advantages as well as their cons.  USB key may be a better way.  They (the two 'buntu distros) would work, but it's your call.   

No worries about kernels this way, either.

Best wishes in your endeavors!
