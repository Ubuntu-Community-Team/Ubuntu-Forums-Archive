### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-23
It depends on the make and model of your DVD drive.  If you purchased your computer from an OEM like Dell, HP, or Gateway, you can usually get drivers from their website.

However, why don't you do your "video editing and such" in Ubuntu?  There are quite a few very good multimedia editing applications available for Linux for varying needs and levels of expertise, including Cinelerra, KdenLive, Kino, LiVES, Open Move Editor, and Avidemux.  Quite a few professionals use Cinelerra and Blender (for 3D animation) in Linux, so I don't think Linux is terribly lacking in the multimedia department...
