### Post by Rhubarb on 2006-10-19
I don't really know, personally I'll be going for my LPI (Linux Professional Institute) certification.  A book on it costs 68 euro, the exam costs about 100 euro.  The online course costs 500 euro.
Contrast this to the Comptia Linux+ course = 1,900 euro, and about 200 euro for the exam.

Why am I choosing LPI?  Because it's not Distro-specific.  And there is also different levels of certification.  The community keeps the LPI up-to-date.
So once I've got my LPI, I feel I can tackle my way 'round any Linux OS.
Consider my LPI proposition anyway, as I am currently in love with Ubuntu myself, I can't say that I'd want to commit to Ubuntu for the long term.
Why have a certification for a specific distro when you can have one certification to rule them all?

### Post by Seveas on 2006-10-20
LPI Certification in a nutshel is a good read, but misleading. You will need knowledge of both dpkg and rpm and some topics are again exchanged between 101 and 102.

For Ubuntu certification, the official Ubuntu book is not a bad read. The topics you need to know about forcertification are listed on [http://www.ubuntu.com/partners/certification/pro](http://www.ubuntu.com/partners/certification/pro)

If you're new to linux, the best way te get certified is to get your hands dirty. Mess around a lot and do a lot of things that are specified in the LPI book. Once you can make the test exam and review questions with few or no errors, you're ready.
