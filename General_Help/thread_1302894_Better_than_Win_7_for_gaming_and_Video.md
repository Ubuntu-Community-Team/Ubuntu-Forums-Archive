### Post by ppirilla on 2009-10-27
If you want to play games that are released for Windows, your best bet is to still use Windows.  WINE and other services are constantly improving their ability to handle windows software, but it will never be as good as playing the game on the system that it was meant to be played on.

Video playback works perfectly fine in Linux.  In my experience, things look smoother than in Windows, but not as good as on a Mac.

As for your rotating wallpaper, I'm pretty sure that's a built-in feature in Ubuntu, but I've never used it myself, so I could be mistaken.

### Post by Earl_Maroon on 2009-10-27
Frankly, Ubuntu is unlikely to be any good for you if you are a gamer.

However for anime it's great. SMPlayer is as good as CCCP for subtitle rendering, and HD video plays flawlessly.

For torrents, you can use the native deluge bit torrent client, which is for me as good as uTorrent on Windows.

As for the wallpaper changer, I'm sure I've read before that it's possible. I can't tell you how to set it up though.


My advice would be to go with Ubuntu and get a games console.
