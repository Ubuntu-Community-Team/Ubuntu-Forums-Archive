### Post by P4man on 2009-08-24
..claimer..?  or ahm, whats the opposite of disclaimer lol: I have given computer lessons to seniors. (I can tell you its not the most fun job in the world).

First of all, -1 for XP. For someone who never used windows, ubuntu is the obvious better choice if someone else can install it and make sure it works well. 

BUT..  it will take a long before he even cares about the OS. The biggest challenge will indeed be the keyboard. There are 102 keys on it, and most of us never think about it, but to someone who never used a PC, difference between backspace and delete, enter, shift, control.. how to get numbers, numlock, scroll lock.. tab!.. that a LOT of keys.

Also figuring out what the difference is between a mouse pointer and a cursor. Using a mouse, not as easy as you think if you grew up with one. You wouldnt believe how many seniors i saw trying to click, and ending up catapulting the mouse 5m furher on the ground. A wired mouse is a PLUS lol.

Windows.. I mean that as application windows. Not a simple concept for a new user. Minimize or close, whats the difference? Why would he care?

Anyway. Indeed I hope for your grnadfather that he has someone close by for the first days / weeks / months. He's gonna need it. But if it can give him any courage, my 75 year old mother is now a blogging tweetering skyping ubuntu addict, and she started only a few years ago :)

### Post by gldearman on 2009-08-24
I disagree with Mark20 and doas777.

Ubuntu is no harder to learn than WinXP.  It seems more difficult sometimes, because most people learn on Windows machines.  But this guy is *tabula rasa*.  So, he'll have an easier time learning Ubuntu than most, because he has nothing to unlearn.

Secondly, he's feeling left out because he can't use the web or the functions of a computer, not because he wants a computer that is the same as everyone else has.  If a teenager is felling left out because he doesn't have a car, would it be bad to give him a red car when his friends all have blue ones?

Besides which, he wants a computer to *use*.  He doesn't want all the hassle of constantly keeping his antivirus/anitmalware/firewall programs up to date.  That will sour him on computers quicker than anything.

Give the guy some credit -- you can't get to 70 years old with grandchildren by being an idiot.  He's as smart as the rest of us, but his experiences are in a different area.

Good luck!  He's lucky to have a grandchild like you to show him the way.  If he really wants to become a computer-user, he'll get excited about it and learn on his own.  Once he's mastered the basics (mouse, getting on the web), show him this forum and how to post questions, in case he needs help when you aren't around.

### Post by The Cog on 2009-08-24
That's quite a task. He has to learn lots of things that you just stop even thinking about after a while - how to point and click, to double-click, what "buttons" look like, how to switch between windows and so on.

For surfing, I think he's better off with Ubuntu than windows - he has no chance of coping with keeping AV up to date or coping with the malware that he would pick up. Set up an automatic login and put launchers for his common programs on the taskbar. Games like solitaire and mahjongg might help practice the hand-eye coordination without conscious effort.

Setting up VNC over SSH tunneling will allow you to help out remotely from time to time.
