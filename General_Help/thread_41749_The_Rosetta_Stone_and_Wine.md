### Post by argux on 2005-06-27
I tried it a while ago. It was a bit of a funny experience. I think I used CrossOver Office, because I don't remember it being a hassle at all, just mount the cd (the installation cd, mind you) and fire up the setup program. If you have a problem with CrossOver, you could try installing winetools, to help you set up your wine installation.

The reason why I say it was a funny experience is because everything worked fine except for the voices who were sslllooooowwwww. The sound wasn't clippy or chippy, it was just in ssslooowww mmmoootiiiooooonnn. Of course, maybe your computer is newer than mine (Piii-500) and has a better sound card than the one I had back then.

And hey, the slowness might even help you catch the pronunciation! Ooootoookooo no kooo tooo onnnaaa no kooo. 

Cheers!
