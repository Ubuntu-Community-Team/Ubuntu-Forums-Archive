### Post by sofasurfer on 2010-10-23
I performed the steps at the beginning of the following page...the part about editing the "config" file.

[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-fix-firefox-slow-problem-in-ubuntu-10-04lucid.html](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-fix-firefox-slow-problem-in-ubuntu-10-04lucid.html)

This did not seem to help, so I read farther down the page. Under the article, where the readers post comments, was this comment,

```
David says:
August 9, 2010 at 6:49 pm

I made these changes with little effect. What finally made Firefox perform as it should was editing the way Firefox connects to the Internet, and checking the “no Proxy” box. That simple change made all the difference.

```

I found the proxy setting at edit>preferences>advanced>network>settings. 
I then checked the "No proxy" box.

I immediately had very fast connection.
