### Post by o_fortuna on 2006-01-25
[QUOTE=Elvish Legion]What gives? Sometimes my machine will just slow to the point of becoming unuseable....I think it has to do with mplayer (I've noticed it usally happens after a site has music playing (some blog sites))

Is there a way to stop this?[/QUOTE]
If the computer seems to be slowing down, go into the System Monitor (Applications-->System Tools-->System Monitor) and look at the processes. Does any one take up tons of memory? If so, that's your problem. It may be that Mplayer, or something, has a memory leak (I had the problem with XMMS a while ago).

If it is Mplayer (it may or may not be), I suggest using a different audio player, for example, AmaroK and Rhythmboxes are decent alternatives. If it is Firefox (a possibility, I suppose) you could try reinstalling it (in Synaptic), or you could download [Automatix]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=80295") and install Firefox 1.5 (the newest version) or Opera (an excellent alternative). If something else is causing the problem, please post. If nothing is apparently causing the problem, I'm lost.
