### Post by lisati on 2008-08-18
[LIST=1]
[*]Forget any hype you might have read in these forums or elsewhere about not "needing" antivirus - it's up to you to decide for yourself, and is most relevant when you are in regular contact with Windows machines.
[*]Visit  [http://free.grisoft.com](http://free.grisoft.com) and download the "debian" Linux version (you might have to search for it) AND the relevant documentation
[*]Open the ".deb" file, which will guide you through the installation
[*]add your user name to the avg user group (so you can download the updates)
[*]log off
[*]log on again
[*]go for it!
[/LIST]

EDIT: Although "antivirus" is commonly considered not to be "needed" for Linux, some email providers have a "don't pass on malware" clauses in their terms and condititions of use. There is also a tutorial on Ubuntu security at [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=765421](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=765421)
