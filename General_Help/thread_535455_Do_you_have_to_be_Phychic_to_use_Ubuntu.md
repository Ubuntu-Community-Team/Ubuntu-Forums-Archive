### Post by _simon_ on 2007-08-26
Just to be clear.

You download an ISO, you have to burn the ISO as an IMAGE to a CD.
You put the CD in your drive and reboot.
Assuming your BIOS is set to boot from CD first, you will get a menu, choose the first option and wait a while for it to load.
Once loaded you can try Ubuntu as a live session, if you like it then you can choose to install it.

^ That's pretty much the same with any Operating System, although non-Linux don't have a Live Session.

### Post by Happy_Man on 2007-08-26
He did help, that link is the single best source of info on ISO burning there is. 

Now, I see from one of your previous posts that you have gotten it on a CD. Great! Now, stick it in your primary CD drive (usually the one at the top) and reboot. If your computer is fairly recent (2-3 years old), it should automatically boot from the CD and from there it's easy. If not, check on the screen that comes up when you reboot (the screen with the PC manufacturer logo) for something that tells you what key to press to change the boot order. Press it, and select your CD drive. From there, you are good to go.

### Post by steveneddy on 2007-08-26
Look, dude. Here are some things that you will need to know about before you start this journey.

1. Your computer. You have to be smarter than the machine.
2. Burning a CD - burning an ISO image, which is different than a data CD, which is what you did.
3. Partitioning a hard drive. If you install, your hard drive will be partitioned. Find what that means and study how to do it yourself if necessary.
4. How big is your hard drive? in gigabytes
5. How much free space does your hard drive have? in Gigabytes
6. You will need an open mind.
7. You must be willing to learn.
8. You must read a lot.
9. Be able to follow instructions.

BTW - do you know how much memory your machine has?

Welcome to Ubuntu.

After you use kerry's link to burn an ISO imaged CD of Ubuntu from the file that you downloaded, put it in the CD drive of your machine and the operating system will run off of the CD without installing anything. 

Honest. 

Like a trial run.

BUT - you MUST have at least 512 mb of RAM for a live CD to work well.

Good luck.
