### Post by fheinsen on 2007-10-19
I had the same problem and was able to solve it by reinstalling all packages listed as "broken dep" in the update-manager log, and then I just followed the regular update instructions.  To do this, open a terminal (Applications -> Accessories -> Terminal) and copy & paste the following line on it and press <Enter>:[INDENT]```
 for package in ` cat /var/log/dist-upgrade/apt.log | grep "has broken dep on" | awk '{print $NF}' `; do sudo aptitude reinstall $package; done 
```[/INDENT]Note that reinstalling all the packages could take some time -- depending on how many packages are listed as broken dependencies in your case.  Once all the packages have been reinstalled, just follow the regular 7.10 upgrade instructions -- though I'd recommend waiting until the download servers are a bit less overwhelmed.  Hope this is helpful!
