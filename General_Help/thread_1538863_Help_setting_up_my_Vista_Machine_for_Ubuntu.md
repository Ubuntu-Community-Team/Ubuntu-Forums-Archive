Honestly, start here:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download)

and when you get to the actual boot the Ubuntu installer will walk you through shrinking your Windows partition to create a new partition for Ubuntu.  This is the *guided partiioning or guided resizing.  *Through this, you will have a new partition and one with Ubuntu one with Windows, you'll be given a quick option at boot-up on which OS you'd like to jump into!

It's easy, I did it, and I had 0 problems.  However, I did *not *back-up and defrag my original Windows installation.  Which many people say you *should *to prevent any mishaps.  I mean I didn't have any but lady luck might have just been at my side. ;)
