### Post by mikeym on 2009-08-31
OK, the [ultra-basics]("http://www.learnphp-tutorial.com/"). 

I would recommend learning *php* and using it to make some example pages together with *html* and *mysql*. Basically in this setup your browser calls a *php* page or an *html* page with embeded *php* and the php in executed on the server performing your functions such as looking up information from your *mysql* database. The executed script produces an *html* page which is sent to your browser.

**[COLOR="Red"](edit)[/COLOR]** Also on the same page is an [example]("http://www.learnphp-tutorial.com/ManagingData.cfm") of working with *mysql*.

This is the [example page]("http://www.evolt.org/PHP-Login-System-with-Admin-Features") which I found and used as the backbone for my *php* site. It's well laid out and I found it extremely useful.
