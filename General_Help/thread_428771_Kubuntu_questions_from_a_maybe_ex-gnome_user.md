### Post by Tanker Bob on 2007-05-06
I use Kubuntu with few problems--none that Ubuntu doesn't share.  KDE is more graphics intensive than Gnome, so some apps may run "slower" depending on your PC's hardware.  I found on both my old and new PCs that Kubuntu runs very briskly.  My problem with Gnome is the lack of user access to system settings that KDE provides as a matter of course.  Programs like Krusader and K3b are native to KDE and work better there.

Like many other things, desktop software is largely a matter of personal taste.  The brown that permeates Ubuntu doesn't do anything for me.  I much prefer the blues of KDE as well as the overall appearance and additional functionality.
