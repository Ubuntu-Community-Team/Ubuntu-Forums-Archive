### Post by PJs Ronin on 2013-08-12
I use Shotwell to transfer images from my camera (Nikon DSLR) to a data hard drive.   There are some issues with this as Shotwell must have the Camera in an unmounted state which is not exactly intuitive.   Anyway, I have an issue with Shotwell grinding to a halt b4 I can transfer files and I thought this had something to do with the mount/unmount status of the camera until I figured out that b4 turning its attention to the camera, Shotwell was re-creating all the 'embedded' and 'shotwell' files that Shotwell extracts from the RAW files.

I make a point of deleting all these embedded/shotwell files on a regular basis as 1) I don't want them, and 2) I use Nikon software to do any further work.   However, every time I crank up Shotwell it drags off re-creating those superfluous files.

Anyone know how to turn this feature (creation of 'shotwell' and 'embedded' files) off?

Edit: Ubuntu 13.04 and Shotwell 0.14.1
