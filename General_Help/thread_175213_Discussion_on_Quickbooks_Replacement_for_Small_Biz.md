### Post by cthornhill on 2006-05-14
While not a free package, I suggest you also check out mybooks from Appgen [http://www.appgen.com/aptus/index.htm](http://www.appgen.com/aptus/index.htm) . This is multi-platform accounting for one to 200 stations. It has a lot of conversion freatures from Quickbooks that might be of use, and is designed for system integrators and resellers. The pricing model is very flexible.

OK I know it is not free. There are some small free packages, but the fact is, when you go for more than personal accounting you wind up spending at least some cash for support. If you have looked at products like Compiere (as you say you did), you will know that they are not actually free if you install them at a customer site - In Compiere's case you must buy Oracle and support/documentation to run the ERP. It is still very cheap, but not actaully free.

For personal accounting there are totaly free and open packages, but as far as I know to get some support and a well documented package that has confomance to international or US accounting standards you will have to pay at least a small amount.

If you think about it, this is not so strange. Accounitng is pretty complex, and needs to conform to lots of oversight and other rules. The maintenece is not easy, and it needs to be highly customized. Hybrids of open source can do well here, but support costs make pure open projects a bit rare.

I am about to try a more detailed review of Compiere for some clients, so after that I can say more about it.

### Post by cssutto on 2006-05-15
Moneydance and similar packages are fine for personal use.  I use Moneydance.

However, for any bussiness that has employees or accounts payable/receivable and inventory, in my opinion one needs three things.

A tax package that keeps track of all payroll taxes, with provisions for overtime, saleried and hourly workers, etc.  Also state and local sales taxes.  And if one is doing business in more than one state in a manner in which more than one state taxes must be paid, multiple state payroll and sales tax accounting.

The tax calculations must allow one to adjust them each year without paying the exhorbitant "tax update" that some charge, which is really a rip off as anyone with any ability at all can change the tax schedule in 15 minutes.  The exception to this would be where one is doing business in multiple states, especially those states that have complex state, county and local tax structures.  Also if one is in something like the construction business where employees are in another state for so long a period that they are subject to that state's income tax on a portion of their wages while subject to taxes in their home state for the main portion.

The third requirement must be that the tax package print out all federal and state forms:  941, 941E, W-2, 1099, etc.

Most of the light weights are designed for personal finance and will do on line banking, investment tracking, etc., and are fine for that.  But payroll is a pain in the butt if you don't have the right package.

CSSJR

### Post by Dcoyaz on 2006-06-23
I've used Quasar for my computer consulting/service company and has worked pretty well. Quasar's release schedule is a little odd. They typically only release their public versions once a year. They run their new versions through their commercial clients first before releasing their public version. I picked Quasar due to it being a true gui and not web based, that's my preferance when using an app with lots of input. It's language includes many candadian terms such as Cheque for Check and a couple of others. It can be modified but requires a deeper knowledge of dealing with linux, or you can buy support and have them do it.

Another package is sqlledger sql-ledger.com. It's a pretty powerful package if you like/don't mind working in a web interface. It has the advantage of publishing over ssl to the public side of you server, allowing web access by your accountant.

These are both accounting packages and not payroll or ERP/CRM systems. Although they can do some level of ERP/CRM. They do handle inventory, invoicing, sales tax, etc... I can't imagine why one would want to handle all the details of payroll themselves anyways :), use a service.. to many liabilities....

The only big problem i've ran into is accountants not knowing or dealing with these systems so you may have to handle much more of your accounting process unless you go with a strictly paper based accountant.

With either of these i would not do a dual boot or run them on your primary desktop. Use a server, especially since both use postgresql as their backend.


Hope that helps,
