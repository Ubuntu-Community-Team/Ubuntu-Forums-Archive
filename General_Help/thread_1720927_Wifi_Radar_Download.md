### Post by 311005901 on 2011-04-03
You should be able to install it through the Ubuntu Software Center, but since you have no internet connection, you're going to have to manually download the packages from [here]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/maverick/wifi-radar"). Just download all the packages listed under "depends". After that, double click them and install them.

But you should be able to connect to your WiFi network through the Network Manager at the top right of your screen. Click on the panel icon that looks like a network and you should be able to connect through there. Installing WiFi-Radar won't give you any added connectivity, just a different graphical interface to connect to your network.

If you are unable to connect to your network through the default Network Manager or with WiFi-Radar, it's probably because you don't have the drivers installed for your WiFi card/connection.
