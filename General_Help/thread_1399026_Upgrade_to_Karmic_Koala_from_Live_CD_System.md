Hello all. I have the System of 9.04, but have a Live CD of Ubuntu 9.10 too. But, I do not want to download all the massive Upgrades. Still, Ubuntu 9.10 is the best! So, can I upgrade 9.04 to 9.10 using the Live CD? (Offline).

### Post by fluffman86 on 2010-02-05
The best thing would be to do a fresh install.  Just back up your /home directory to an external drive and then install with the 9.10 live CD.  Or, if your /home directory is on a separate partition, then you can specify that by choosing the "manual" option on the partition page during the installation.

Otherwise, I believe you'll need to download the alternate CD in order to do an in-place upgrade without network access.  And even then, you'd need to connect later to get important security updates.

### Post by warfacegod on 2010-02-05
Agreed. Baring unmentioned circumstances, your best option is to do a fresh install. Upgrades are never as "clean" and can cause small, possibly unnoticed issues, to get much worse. If you are concerned about compatibility issues, perhaps installing 9.10 to its own partition is a safe alternative. After using it for awhile, you could resize your 9.04 partition and leave it in place as a backup or just do away with it entirely.
