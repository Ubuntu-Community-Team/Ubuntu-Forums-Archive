### Post by tanha on 2010-02-25
Under the "on ac power" tab, there is an option "spin down hard disks when possible," what *specifically* does this mean?

---

### Post by johngreth on 2010-02-25
Your hard drive stores its data on a disk that spins like a cd. The system normally keeps the disk spinning so that data can be retrieved and stored faster. Spinning down the disks just means that they will stop spinning. The up side to spinning the disks down when not in use is that it will save power. The down side is that it may take longer to get the computer going again after it goes into power saving mode, but the extra time it takes is usually so small it's unnoticeable.

### Post by johngreth on 2010-02-25
After the "Put computer to sleep when inactive for:" limit is reached, the disks will spin down until the mouse moves and the computer wakes up again. Source: [Power Management - Ubuntu Wiki - Power (Saving) States]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerManagement#Power%20%28saving%29%20states")
