So - A while back I bought an HP Officejet 6500 Wireless, set it up, intalled the latest hplip from hp's website and have been happily printing, scanning, and faxing from my computer, on the wireless until just the other day.  The printer showed up as unavailable, even though it was on, and connected to my network.  Power-cycling didn't work- so I did the next thing I could think of, which was to remove the printer and fax from the pritners dialog and re-add them.  No big deal...  Adding then through the hplip interface annoyingly was telling me to connect the printer w/ a usb cable, but that would be a pain, as the computer is in the kitchen inside a cabinet all tucked away, and the printer is in the living room, in another cabinet all tucked away.  But going to system - preferences (or administration, don't recall exactly) - printers, and adding it  back through there picked it up off the network automatically.  So, I was printing again but could not figure out how to add the fax device back to my system.

So, now I thought I'd check around hp's website, and decided to email tech support.  I fill out the form, and I get a response very quickly, withing minutes, to tell me they don't support my operating system!  So, I replied and told them that they did, but I'd look for help elsewhere (since then I found that hplip has it's own support site), and he replied again, saying that he already told me they don't support my OS.
