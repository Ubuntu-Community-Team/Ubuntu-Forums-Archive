### Post by fizikz on 2009-01-17
After much searching, I'm surprised that there doesn't seem to be much information about infrared or radio frequency remotes being used in linux. 

However, I did find a solution: Griffin Airclick [http://www.griffintechnology.com/products/airclickusb]("http://www.griffintechnology.com/products/airclickusb"), and although for some odd reason it seems to be discontinued by the manufacturer, I was able to find it easily and for a reasonable price (~$15 USD) on eBay. 

Perhaps the more interesting part is that linux software exists for this device: [http://sourceforge.net/projects/airclick/]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/airclick/"). Since this program maps button presses to any keyboard combination, it can be used for pretty much any kind of control in addition to audio programs. I'm quite happy with this flexibility.

On the down side, this remotes buttons are slightly finicky at times, in that on some occasions it won't register if the button hasn't been pressed squarely. Also, double clicks are a bit tricky, but that may be due to the need for some tweaking of some parameters in the configuration file. 

Overall, I'm very happy that I've found a workable and satisfactory solution, and I see good potential with this device and program. It has the kind of functionality I wished for, and at a good price. A big thanks to Brian Taylor for writing the airclick program!
