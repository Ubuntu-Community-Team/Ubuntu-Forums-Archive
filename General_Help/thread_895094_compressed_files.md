### Post by pauper on 2008-08-20
You should ask the person who created that archive. Have you downloaded it from
a website? I honestly don't get these people, who upload something as shareware
yet put a lock on it. Try looking for an answer in FAQ or commentaries of that
source. There is a good chance that the URL of the website, from where you've
downloaded it, is the password (but I mean not rapidshare.com or megaupload.com
as the password).

There are some free password-cracking programs, floating around the web though.
Can't recommend any particular.

If contents of the archive are not something unique and rare I would try to
find a password-less alternative.
