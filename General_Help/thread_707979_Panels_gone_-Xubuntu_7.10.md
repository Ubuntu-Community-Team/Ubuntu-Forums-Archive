### Post by caterpillar322 on 2008-02-26
I occasionally have the same problem and the way that I fix it is to hit 
ALT+F2 and type xfce-panel. It usually sticks for a few days and then the panels disappear again. It's only a minor annoyance to me so I really haven't researched it any further. I hope this helps some.

That's right!
Just to add a little something: If once you reboot your computer the panels aren't loaded, you could add **xfce4-panel** to the autostarted applications list. Just go to **Applications/Settings/Autostarted Applications** and add it.
Good luck!
