To have Evolution notify you of appointment, they must be in your calendar, and the appointments must show on the calendar when the calendar application is in Evolution's foreground. In the upper left is a menu of calendars available, and the checkbox must be checked in order to show your appointments both in the calendar application and when you click on the clock applet.

Clicking the clock applet shows the mini-calendar, and clicking on a date in bold activates a drop-down menu that shows all appointments for that day. The clock applet is in the upper right of the screen in the default configuration of Gnome Ubuntu Desktop. The Notification Area applet is close by and shows other things like your network connection, power status, and in the most recent version, also the sound volume.

If you set an alarm when you entered the appointment, a flashing clock will appear in the applet panel in the notification applet at the time specified when you set the alarm. It can also make a sound when the clock icon is flashing in the notification area applet.

A "lighted" envelope icon will appear in the notification area when you have new email if you have it set up to automatically access your mail server and download email when the program starts and at designated intervals. This option must be set for each email account that is set up in Evolution for the special icon to appear in the notification area when new email arrives. Go to Edit, Preferences, then Mail Accounts. Highlight an account, select Edit then select Receiving Options. Under Checking for new mail there's a checkbox to automatically get email at an interval you can set.
