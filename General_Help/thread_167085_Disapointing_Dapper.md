Dapper is not supposed to make big changes. It is supposed to be Breezy, but polished up and with as many bug-fixes as possible. There are a few new hooks behind the scenes... Beagle integration, Xgl and Compiz packages  and Network-Manager available for installation to name a couple. I'm personally glad NM was not enabled by default (at least in Beta), as it could not connect to my wireless network. Simply refused to actually set the WEP key after asking for it. 

A large amount of effort also seems to have gone into internationalization and accessibility. 

If you're expecting amazing new things out of Dapper you're kind of missing the point of the release. Take a look at the announcement for [Ubuntu 6.10 (Edgy Eft)]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=162550"), and see if that whets your appetite a little more...
