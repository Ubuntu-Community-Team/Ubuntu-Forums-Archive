### Post by plucky on 2013-03-02
> **johnsd said:**
> .....but are there any other ideas before I do? Why are the kernel files there but not recognized by apt-get to remove them?

I always use **synaptic package manager** to remove old linux-images and linux-headers from the /boot,as it takes care of removing other packages and also updating grub.

Open synaptic package manager and search for linux-image and select the ones you need to remove,leaving at least one old kernel just in case the newest kernel breaks, and search for linux-headers and select the ones you need removing and then select apply is all you need to do.

Good Luck
