### Post by commonlyUNIQU3 on 2007-10-24
Hello.

I too am an Evolution Mail user (and love it) in a MS Exchange environment at my work (not crazy about it).
I go through about 200-300 emails a day, many with attachments, and we have size limitations to our accounts on the Exchange server so I am often faced with the need to archive my emails offline.

The new Evolution in Gnome 2.20 (installed by default in Ubuntu 7.10) has an archive feature built in, but I have not yet tried it.  The best solution I've found so far, which works without a hitch is as follows:

1. Make a subfolder to the "Inbox" under the "On This Computer" tree ( I call mine "Archive")

2. Drag and drop the emails you want to archive into this folder.*  
NOTES: This will move the selected emails off of your Exchange server/account and into this folder (and into local storage) - unless you do a copy & paste instead of drag & drop.  
**You may need to have the setting for downloading emails for offline access enabled for this to work as desired.*

If I recall, the new integrated backup feature creates a compressed (.zip) archive from which you can later restore the email (haven't tried that just yet).

Hope this helps & good luck!
