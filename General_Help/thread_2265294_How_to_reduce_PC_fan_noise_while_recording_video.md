### Post by nerdtron on 2015-02-14
recording screen takes a lot power so your cpu fan probably works to cool down the system. You probably can't  do anything about it unless you use another cooling system.
for heatsinks and fans, try getting bigger fans with slower speeds. They transfer the same (or larger) amount of air for lower rotation. Meaning they are quite.

### Post by nerdtron on 2015-02-14
It's a laptop, then that limits your options. Basically, the fans will rev up when it detects a high temps when you do something that loads the CPU, unless your laptop cooler pad (i assume) is powerful, i don't think it will be enough to maintain the laptop on a temperature that won't trigger higher fan speeds.

I you are concern on the fan noise, (I assume you use the internal mic of the laptop), then it's better to use a headset or a separate microphone when recording audio.
