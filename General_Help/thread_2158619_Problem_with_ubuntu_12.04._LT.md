### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-06-30
I had symptoms like that. The crashes got more frequent over time. Crashes seemingly at random, but more frequently when I was ripping DVDs, and I could cause a crash straight away by running the memory benchmarks from Phoronix Test Suite.

I look out the two sticks of cheaper RAM and I still got the crashes. So I put them back in, and then took out the two sticks of brand-name (Kingston) RAM. My crashes disappeared straight away. That was probably five years ago, and my computer has been rock solid ever since.

Check your RAM. Windows reserves up to 2 gigabytes of RAM for itself and then doesn't use most of it; the bad spot in your RAM sticks may be within that part allocated but not used. Linux, however, makes all your RAM available and tries to use as much as possible, which tends to expose RAM problems more than Windows.
