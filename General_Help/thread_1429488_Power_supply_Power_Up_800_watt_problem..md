### Post by efflandt on 2010-03-14
Do you really have or need an 800 watt PSU for your computer?  That sounds way too big unless you are discussing a UPS (uninterruptable power supply) instead of a PSU (power supply in the computer).

I have had UPS units fail in a little more than a year (I don't buy that brand any more).

I have never had a PSU, or fans on it, fail in the 25+ years that I have been using PCs at work or home.  A Celeron 300 computer in my basement (no monitor), which has files on it from 2000, has been running non-stop since 2006 with only infrequent shutdowns for updates or power failures for years before that.  I did have to upgrade the overrated 250 watt PSU in my 2004 desktop when it would not even boot with a hotter video card.  After upgraded to conservatively rated 330 watt PSU, I found that it was not using more than 130 watts max from the wall outlet (Kill A Watt meter).  That PSU has variable speed fan(s?) that it controls itself.

If you are sure you know what voltage the fans are, you could possibly get power from a drive cable.  But it sounds like you need a better quality PSU.
