### Post by Kuolio on 2005-11-23
I'm at school atm, so I cant make a powernowd and graph-frequency manual for you but rather to point you to some powesaving-options that you can look up from google or these forums.

Basic stuff:

Adjust your screen to lowest possible brightness setting that you can use. Your display consumes lots of power, so dimming it may help you to save some juice.

Set powernowd so that it doesn't raise CPU frequency to max unless something like 95% stress on the CPU for more than 5 secs. (makes your computer act very slow btw). And make it snappy to drop down from the higher frequensies. Look for man powernowd and google for insturctions.

Set your graphics card to work with smaller frequency to save power. 

How to do all those.. consult google and your laptops manual.

Buy and "extended" battery with longer operational time.  :)
