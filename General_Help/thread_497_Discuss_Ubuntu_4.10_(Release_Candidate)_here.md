### Post by woodstock on 2004-10-20
My first impressions were.

**Installation** - This was pretty damn painless, as I'm coming from a Slackware/Arch/Gentoo installation background, and I've done so many installations of all of those. So this area doesn't really matter for me.

From a newcomers perspective though, I would have to say that it was the same story with exceptions around setting up a dual boot and properly partitioning your hd. This will always be painful for newcomers, but thats what we're here for ;). 

**Initial Boot** - Now problems here, autodetects everything I have on my rig without and problems. Had to add a couple of things to hotplugs blacklist, because of a strange bug with the 2.6 kernels and thinking that I have some hardware that doesn't even exist in my tower... But I know about this bug, and it's not an Ubuntu probelm. 

**Desktop use** -  I simply love Gnome and I love the fact that Ubuntu uses nothing but free software and stays away from things like mplayer and whatnot, Though I have to admit that I miss some things, but I'll live :). 
Desktop is fast and responsive, and ontop of a (modern) debain base system it's quite stable. 

**Package Management** -  I love apt-get and I love how I don't ever (so far) have to download a new cd for every new release, can anyone say dist-upgrade? Everything that I have installed from the main repositories and from universe have worked with no problems.

I'd like to thank the dev's for this wonderful product they have put together and I would like to also say that I will try and do my part by contributing to this forum, the wiki, the irc channel and by reporting any bugs I run into along the way, of course with possible solutions if I come by any.
