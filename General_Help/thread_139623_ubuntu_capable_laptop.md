### Post by oscar on 2006-03-04
njzillest, ubuntu does support propper cpu scaling.
Right click your panel and go Add to panel...
Select CPU Frequency Scaling Monitor.
now open up a terminal and go
```
sudo chmod +s /usr/bin/cpufreq-selector
```
You should now be able to left click on the applet and select a frequency.
To change the mode right click on the applet and go Properties.
At the bottom under where it says frequency selector change the menu type to Frequencies and Govenors.
Now when you click the applet you should get both a frequencies and a govenors menu. To save power and increase battery life change the govenor to powersave.
