### Post by Raphae1 on 2008-09-02
Watching those small flash videos on a big display can be stressful to your eyes. Yes I know, that with the [newest Adobe Flash plugin]("http://www.adobe.com/shockwave/download/download.cgi?P1_Prod_Version=ShockwaveFlash&P2_Platform=Linux&P5_Language=German") the fullscreen button actually works, but it really slows down the framerate. When you have a working **[compiz]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/CompositeManager/CompizFusion")** setup, you can press the left "windows" button on your keyboard and use the scroll wheel on your mouse to zoom in. I've observed much better video frame-rates doing so. There's only one more annoying thing: The mouse pointer usually stays on top of the video screen. This is where an ubuntu package called "**[unclutter]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/hardy/unclutter")**" really helps. After you run unclutter, the mouse pointer will disappear after 5 seconds without any mouse movement. Enjoy!
