### Post by coffeecat on 2016-07-01
I was getting this problem in 15.10 but, so far, it hasn't afflicted me (yet!) in 16.04. Even though the UK layout was set as the default, often I would find myself typing @ for " and vice versa, just like you.

Really irritating.

Most probably a bug affecting only some hardware since others I discussed it with weren't affected, but I must admit that I didn't expend too much energy seeing if one had been reported or doing so myself.

I can't offer a cure but I can offer a suggested workaround which is a little quicker than going into System Settings each time this happens. See this screenshot of the top-right hand corner of the desktop after clicking on the En1 icon:

[ATTACH=CONFIG]269875[/ATTACH]

You'll probably find that the English (UK) option is checked even though you are getting a US layout. Simply click on the English (UK) line. I got into the habit of doing that every time I booted up before I started typing the wrong characters. Hopefully, that will keep you going until someone offers a proper fix.
