### Post by varunendra on 2012-01-27
Can you share the model no and your connection plan in use? Does the signal strength seem good enough in windows?

I would recommend to just delete the existing connection in network manager, replug the modem and proceed to automatic 'Mobile Broadband' installation/configuration steps. Usually, you don't have to manually provide the ID-password while configuring a supported mobile broadband modem, and as far as I know, all models of reliance are well supported in Ubuntu. If the modem and plan are natively supported, you shouldn't need a single step of manual configuration.
