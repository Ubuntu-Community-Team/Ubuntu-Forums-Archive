### Post by BBQdave on 2013-06-10
My Toshiba Satellite c655-s5503 was advertised for upto 5 hours of battery life. I tested hardware with a live Linux CD, purchased hardware and wiped hard drive - then fresh install of Linux.

I can not compare Windows 7 battery performance with Ubuntu Unity 12.04LTS on this hardware, because I never even booted Windows 7. I believe I do have good battery performance with Linux: using Ubuntu with multiple applications open, music playing - I get close to 3 hours before I need to charge. If I have multiple applications open, music playing, and I am working on other things - using my notebook off and on, so screen dims and rests, I get close to 5 hours before I need to charge.

The battery performance with Linux is the same as what I experienced on a couple of iBooks and a MacBook - as far as actual battery life under use, compared to max battery hours advertised.
