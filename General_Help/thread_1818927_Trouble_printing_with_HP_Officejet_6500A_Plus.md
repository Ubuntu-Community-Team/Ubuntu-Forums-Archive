### Post by colin.p on 2011-08-05
"The third computer is a Windoze 7 box that my son connects to wirelessly but he can no longer print as well."

Could be the printer acting up, or it may be the router?
I have a 6500 as well. I had it originally hooked up wireless, but it would disappear on the network too (had a cheap $20 Trendnet router). I got sick of it and hooked it up wired to the router. However, I also had two other computers, hooked wireless, that would also disappear, from time to time, requiring reseting the router.
I got sick of that, and bought a better router ($29 d-link, well not much better, but I did put DD-WRT on it). Still have the printer connected wired, and it never drops the connection, BUT, the wireless computers also don't disappear, much better wireless connection.
So the moral of the story, connect the printer as wired, and/or take a look at your wireless router. You could also have the router have DHCP reserve an IP for the printer, so it would also have the same IP. That makes it alot more stable, at least what I found.
