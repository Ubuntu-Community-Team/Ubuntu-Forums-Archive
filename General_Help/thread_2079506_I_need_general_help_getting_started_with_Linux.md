### Post by oldfred on 2012-11-02
Generally if you have a wired connection, then it will offer the wireless drivers. 

from the system settings window.
Next, click on Software source, go in the additional drivers tab.

Some have specific issue. Post all the details of your wireless card in a specific thread with that in the title. Good titles help attract those who may know how to help.

You should not need any Windows drivers. 

I prefer synaptic but "they" consider that too cryptic. Software Center works pretty good now.

Just about everything can be done without terminal, but we often post instructions with terminal as that is common. With Ubuntu you can be running many different desktops and the exact methods to get to the correct menu may be different. Anything posted here can just be copy & pasted into a terminal. 

A master thread on learning/books/terminal/bash/Linux etc
Linux Command Line Learning Resources
