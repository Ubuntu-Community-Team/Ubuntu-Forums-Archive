### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-08-12
Replacing individual laptop keys is not advisable because it's very easy to break the key press/return mechanisms.

Should you decide to replace the whole keyboard, you will need to do the following:
1) Use Google to locate a maintenance manual for your laptop.  You will need this because you will have to remove the old keyboard -- and each laptop tends to have it's own sequence of steps for doing this.
2) Remove the current keyboard.  Be VERY CAREFUL when disconnecting the ribbon cable connecting the keyboard to the laptop. If you break or damage the connector or cable in any way, you will end up with a laptop that will be all but useless.
3) Insert the new keyboard.  Again, be very careful about connecting the ribbon cable.
4) Reassemble the laptop.

If you're not at all experienced in doing this, then take the laptop to a repair facility and pay THEM to do it.  The cost is well worth the risk of damaging the laptop if you're not experienced in doing this kind of work.
