### Post by snowpine on 2011-11-06
What is the graphics card? This detail is often overlooked in "Ubuntu is too slow" discussions, but can be just as important as CPU or RAM in the perceived speed of the system.

At any rate you should read the [Ubuntu hardware requirements]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements") and try a lightweight desktop such as Xfce (xubuntu-desktop) or LXDE (lubuntu-desktop) if your specs fall short:

> Ubuntu Desktop Edition

    * 1 GHz CPU (x86 processor (Pentium 4 or better))
    * 1 GiB RAM (system memory)
    * 15 GB of hard-drive space (or USB stick, memory card or external drive but see LiveCD for an alternative approach)
    * 800 by 600 screen resolution
    * Either a CD/DVD drive or a USB port for the installer media
    * Internet access is helpful 


You might also consider rolling back to the 10.04 "long term support" release, as it will be more stable and less buggy overall, with support through April 2013, plus it uses the Gnome 2 desktop, which many users prefer to the new Unity (and Unity also runs poorly with certain graphics cards, bringing us full circle).
