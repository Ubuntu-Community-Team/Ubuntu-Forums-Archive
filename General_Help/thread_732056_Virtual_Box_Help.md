### Post by ibuclaw on 2008-03-22
Also to note, even if you could, it wouldn't be very efficient to do so either.

Just think.
A "Power Hungry" Game on a "Power Hungry" Guest OS under a "Power Hungry" Desktop (If Gnome/KDE is your Desktop of choice for Ubuntu) with the option of "Power Hungry" effects (Compiz).

Once you see the bigger picture, it isn't really worth trying on a Laptop.

Perhaps trying the game in Wine could grant access for you to play it.

Else, just constantly bug the developers for a fix (or the Game Company for a Native Version in Linux).

Unfortunately, there's not really much else you can do.
