### Post by zero2xiii on 2012-01-06
I am guessing you should unplug en replug your harddrive by the discription you posted. However this will be a handful operation on a laptop as the drive is seated in the laptop body and the cables are rarely long enough to unseat the drive and still keep the laptop in its normal position.

I will not recommend "Hot plugging" of any hardrive, since you never know wether the data has been written or not. I have had numerous data corruptions on an eSATA drive due to the enclousure I use automaticaly "suspends" the drive after a certain period of inactivity, and I boot windows from the eSATA drive.

Good luck though
