### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-02-13
Do not distribute a kernel with proprietary modules already built-in. It is a legal grey area, not specific to Ubuntu but specific to all GPL-licensed software.

So, this means no ATI or Nvidia proprietary drivers by default, but with Ubuntu the end-user can just use the Hardware Drivers program.

Redistributing Flash Player is against the Flash Player license unless you distribute it as a complete installer; this means you cannot preinstall it into a browser's Plugins folder before selling the computer. I'm not sure of the exact conditions so you should consult the Flash Player license before doing anything!

For non-commercial selling (for instance, selling a computer to a friend) it's almost impossible to be found out and prosecuted with this :-)  I distributed Flash Player and FGLRX preinstalled on a computer that I sold to a friend. But if you're planning on selling to strangers, better to obey the letter of the license!

### Post by blazemore on 2009-02-13
You are free to copy, modify, sell, distribute and share GPL software... as long as you do so under the terms of the GPL (You can't sell Ubuntu and not provide access to the source code, for example, and you can't prevent others from sharing etc).

As far as restricted software is concerned, the best bet is don't include it, but provide a simple way for people to install it if they want.
