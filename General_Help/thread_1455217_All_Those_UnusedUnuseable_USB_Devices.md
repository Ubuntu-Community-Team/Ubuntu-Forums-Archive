### Post by oldefoxx on 2010-04-15
There is a whole family of smartcards out there, which appear as USB devices under Places/Computer.  At least I guess that is what they are.

Anyway, they can be found all manner of places, from the front side of
PCs to monitors to printers.  And you can have, like four to 13 or more
such devices in your system.

Okay, so some people like this and take advantage of a few, meaning they can move photos and music files around, or even install an OS on one and
have another way into that PC, or any other PC that they attach it to.
But so many?  There has got to be a few duplicates in there you know.

Windows does something I like, which is not something I frequently say.  You can go under Settings/Control Panel/System/Hardware/Device Manager and see a whole map of your PC hardware, with indications of what is working and not working, and if you see devices you don't want to see, you can disable them and they seem to go away.  Now I don't imagine many people know about this feature, and it is some going to get to and use it, but it does exist.

So why am I plagued with all these USB icons under Computer with Ubuntu and no apparent way of just disabling those that I don't want to see and have them disappear into the woodwork? Or is there a way and I just missed it?
