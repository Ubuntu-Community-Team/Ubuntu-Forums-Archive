### Post by Holger_Gehrke on 2018-09-30
Of course there are Basic interpreters and compilers available for Linux. You can find the packages 'bwbasic' (ByWaterBasic, minimal ANSI-Basic with parts of full ANSI-Basic, aimed at using Basic as an alternative to shell-scripting) and 'yabasic' in the repositories. There's quite a few other Basics out there, like Gambas (aimed more at the VisualBasic crowd) or freebasic (compiler, compatible with QuickBasic). And if these are not close enough to GWBasic or QBasic for your needs, you can run those through dosemu or dosbox or in a VM running FreeDOS. 

Holger
