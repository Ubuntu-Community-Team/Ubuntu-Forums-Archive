### Post by zero244 on 2007-08-05
Hello: There is a learning curve with Linux, try and keep the faith. What I did was install Ubuntu then did all the experimenting I wanted until I messed it up to the point I had to do a fresh install.
One of my problems I installed a defective video card after about 30 days of using Ubuntu and it caused a huge amount of problems, because I didn't know it was defective for about 2 months.
Once I replaced the video card 99 percent of my problems went away. And I was able to continue to learn Linux.
One of the best investments you can make experimenting with software is buy a extra hard drive and back everything up, it will save you a ton of grief in case something big happens to your data.
And most likely something big will happen unless your real conservative with how far and how fast you move with Ubuntu.
I dont plan on ever going back to Windows except for gaming and a few things that wont run on Linux yet.
If you stay with XP eventually MS is going to stop supporting it.......meaning no more activation support.
Which will force you to buy the next big abomination from Redmond.
If nothing else keep Ubuntu in a dual boot situation and learn it at your leisure. In the end its worth the trouble.
Using Linux........your virus free and Steve Balmer free too.
So hang in there.......and good luck.
