### Post by zarqoon on 2009-07-30
I am using a [Logitech Trackman Wheel]("http://www.logitech.com/index.cfm/mice_pointers/trackballs/devices/166&cl=US,EN") for some years now and I do not want to go back to regular mice.
There are several Advantages.
1. The Cable really does not matter since you do not move it around. No hassle with batteries and no disadvantages from the cable
2. You do not need much space. You can even use it on the armrest of a chair with no problems at all.
3. Less pain. Since you do not have to move you hand around there is no pain in your wrist or arm. You only have to move your thumb.

The first time you use a trackball you will most likely be kind of slow but its not hard to learn.

### Post by zarqoon on 2009-07-30
> **Andy06 said:**
> Do you guys use it for some specialised application like image editing? Vector/Raster graphics editing?

Or just general use? Don't you have to like.......roll like a hamster? Is the experience similar to that of a touchpad?

Thanks

I use the Trackball for everything. I even play fps games with the thing.
There are similarities to touchpad use, but it feels very different. 
You move the pointer by moving your finger over a surface.
But:
1. You move a ball that is conveniently located directly under your thumb. Spherical movement feels more natural than planar.
2. Also you do not get the level of precision from a touchpad.
3. Your finger sliding over a surface feels very different from moving the surface with your finger. You do not have to overcome the static friction of your finger on the pad which, next to precision, is the biggest problem of touchpads because it makes it hard to do fine movements with them.

I prefer a mouse over a touchpad any day but give me a trackball and I am happy.

And as a little bonus its funny to watch other people move the thing around on the table like a mouse when they try to use your computer.
