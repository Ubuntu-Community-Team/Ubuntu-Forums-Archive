### Post by grashdur on 2016-10-08
Rhythmbox podcasts were jamming (all fifteen of them), so that on the left side of each podcast name I saw a circular arrow -- updating was frozen and nothing was being found or downloaded, even after restarting the program and the computer. I looked around for answers, then I tried fiddling around a little more on my own. All relevant discussions on this are now closed, so I'm offering this here. Here's what I figured out:

1. Finish listening to and delete any podcast episodes that you have (drag any to a folder if you want to save a copy). So, make sure your list of episodes is totally empty. In my experience, it might start working at this point, starting to find and download new ones.
2. On the list of podcast feeds, select each podcast in turn, in the upper pane, and click the Update button three times really fast. Then deselect it by selecting the next podcast down the list. The circular arrow will disappear and downloading will start. This worked for me today. 
3. If these things don't work, try deleting what someone on another thread called "the config thing." I have done this in the past and it has helped with Rhythmbox problems. Sorry, I don't remember now what file this was and didn't write a note to myself about it. But I recommend saving a backup of the file you delete, just in case.

I keep using Rhythmbox, for I guess ten years now, even though it has so many bugs and sometimes mangles my song files (specifically, by swapping the album art randomly among songs, and deleting song information). But somehow I keep using it because no other program is organized in such a simple and practical way.
