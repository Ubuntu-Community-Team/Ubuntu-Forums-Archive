Something to consider regarding screensavers ...

> Screen savers are a left-over solution from a previous technology. In  spite of their name, screen savers no longer “save” anything – all they  do is waste electricity. Screen savers are not necessary on modern,  flat-panel LCD displays.

Source : [http://www.howtogeek.com/128644/htg-explains-why-screen-savers-are-no-longer-necessary/]("http://www.howtogeek.com/128644/htg-explains-why-screen-savers-are-no-longer-necessary/?PageSpeed=noscript")
Question: are you still using a CRT monitor with your gaming ? 

If NOT, you don't really need the screensaver, disable it in settings for uninterrupted gaming, as the link notes they "save" nothing in normal use on flatscreens etc. They really only ever worked as designed on CRT monitors.

If SO, I don't know how to get Xorg to recognize inputs from gamepads etc under these cirumstances, sorry. Just posting mainly to check what sort of monitor you (and the OP) are on and to point out the link info.  Cheers.
