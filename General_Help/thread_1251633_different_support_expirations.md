### Post by tgeer43 on 2009-08-27
8.04 is an LTS (Long Term Support) release.
This is done periodically and aimed mainly at business users who need the longer term support so they are not constantly upgrading all of their machines.

Personally, in your situation I'd either opt for 9.04 or just wait until October for the release of 9.10 Karmic.

Each new release gets better in terms of hardware support and other aspects.

### Post by tgeer43 on 2009-08-28
curtk69@fast,

To answer your last two questions:
> is 9.10 karmic a lts version?No, 9.04 is a regular release with 18 months of full official support. The next Long Term Support release (3 yrs. for desktop, 5 yrs. for server) will be 10.04 LTS in April, 2010.
But as HiImTye said in post #5, you can just do rolling updates from version to version pretty seamlessly. If you really had a pressing need for an LTS release (you don't appear to), you can stop upgrading versions at any time, like for instance when 10.04 LTS comes out.
> is laptop compatibility a normal known issue?I can only speak for myself and what I've heard but with very few exceptions laptop/notebook compatibility, indeed hardware compatibility in general just keeps getting better with every release. I've run 32-bit 8.04, 8.10, 9.04, 9.10 and now 64-bit 9.10 on the notebook in my signature below with no problems that couldn't be resolved. I honestly couldn't imagine ever running Windows on this machine again, like the disaster Vista that came installed on it and brought my $3000 notebook to its knees.

By comparison, the 4 machines you mentioned in your initial post will likely run Ubuntu just fine. Even Mom's 4-6 yr. old box will probably get along though you might not be able to do all the cutting edge desktop effects but I doubt if Mom will care (or even notice).

And since you seem to be the one who takes care of these machines, just think - no more of *any* of the headaches that you mentioned. No crapware, virus or other malware issues, licenses running out, bogging down over time, ***buying software***, etc.

I say go for it. Maybe start with the newest machine and work back so you'll gain more experience in case you *do* run into issues with the older one(s).

tgeer
