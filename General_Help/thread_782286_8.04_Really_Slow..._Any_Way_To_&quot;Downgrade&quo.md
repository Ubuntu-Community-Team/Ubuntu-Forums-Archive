### Post by fmartinez on 2008-05-04
> **GameBox 47 said:**
> Also, when using "Add/Remove Programs" or something that requires admin password, it says "Starting Admistration" on the taskbar but never starts it, which freezes the package manager and renders upgrades useless... :(

You might want to check that all your source repsitories are ok. In a terminal you do this command: 
#sudo apt-get update
If there are any errors you should get something back. Sometimes when you upgrade from from distribution to another... the sources list may not fully update. 

For the slowing issue a reinstall is always a good thing. 
Usually for future references: I would put my /home and usually some other directories on another partition just in case I come across this situation. That way I don't loose my data or custom programs. 

Another thing to maybe look at is if you have "desktop effects" enabled. This sometimes causes high CPU usage. Especially if your repositories are not updating correctly.
