### Post by freebeer on 2007-08-26
I've looked at gnucash. but haven't really put it through its paces, however I've read that others have had great success with it... and they came from Quickbooks backgrounds.

Personally, I'd be reticent to change my accounting application - there's so much history to either duplicate or abandon that it's a difficult transition.  (Your mileage may vary.)  If you're using the payroll facilities of QB, this may be a stopper.  Payroll rules change all the time and I don't think any open-source application can keep up with the sheer volume of localized changes that may happen to payroll calculations and reporting. 


There's nothing wrong with dual-booting for those particular instances.  It may even be possible to run Quickbooks in a virtual server under Ubuntu, but I haven't tried it myself.
