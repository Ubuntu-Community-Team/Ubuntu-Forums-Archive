### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2018-05-08
If you haven't saved your data already, boot into a liveusb and use it to move your data to an external storage if the file system is accessible. Then wipe everything and do a clean install. There is little point trouble shooting a blotched upgrade, I wouldn't even bother trying (and even if you manage to get it to work somehow after spending hrs, you never know if some deep settings are messed up and may bite you later in some unexpected ways, I would never trust a system recovered from a failed upgrade)

 I would go further to say there is little point in "upgrade", a clean install is a lot faster (~ 10 mins here) and a lot safer.
