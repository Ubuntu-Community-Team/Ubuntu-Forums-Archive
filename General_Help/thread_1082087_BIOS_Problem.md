### Post by smani on 2009-02-27
You can't really, otherwise what would the whole point about passwords be:P Some laptops offer quick-boot-menus, usually by pressing F12. If your current boot device is set to cdrom, you can try out PLOT ([http://www.plop.at/](http://www.plop.at/)) which will offer a boot menu on his own (I used it to boot from USB stick on older PC's that didn't support booting from USB). Otherwise, I guess removing the CMOS battery is the only thing you can do.
