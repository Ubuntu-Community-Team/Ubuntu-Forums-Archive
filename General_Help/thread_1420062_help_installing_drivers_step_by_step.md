### Post by darolu on 2010-03-02
In most cases, means your video card drivers are not proprietary but free/open source; this drivers are installed by default due copyright/non-fair use/etc. Usually you just have to click on that icon (looks like a little video card) and activate the proprietary driver; if the little icon is not there anymore, press ALT+F2 and type "jockey-gtk" and press enter.

### Post by mechro on 2010-03-02
You have drivers installed, it's just they are not proprietary. If there is nothing advised in Hardware Drivers then Ubuntu is saying your open source drivers are fine and you don't need to install anything.

If you are not happy with the current open source drivers e.g. you're not getting the screen resolution you think you should be getting then you may want to investigate installing proprietary drivers.
