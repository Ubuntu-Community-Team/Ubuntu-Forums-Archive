### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-08
If you're talking about computer aided design, then Blender might not be the best choice.  Blender is generally used more for 3D animation.

The CAD program I see suggested most often for Linux is [BricsCAD]("http://www.bricscad.com/en_INTL/").  It's apparently very good, but it's a commercial applications, and not cheap (though at $334 USD, it's about 1/10 the price of AutoCAD, the most popular professional CAD application for Windows).  [VariCAD]("http://www.varicad.com/en/home/") is another Linux CAD app for mechanical design that looks pretty good, though it's slightly more expensive than BricsCAD at $640 USD.  A few free CAD apps I've seen suggested for Linux are [BRL-CAD]("http://brlcad.org/") (which was initially developed for the U.S. Army), [FreeCAD]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/free-cad"), PythonCAD, and Qcad.  The latter two are in the Ubuntu repositories.

### Post by cb951303 on 2009-02-09
Blender is no good for CAD.
Also, correct me if I'm wrong but bricscad is for architectural use.

The best value mechanical cad application for linux is VariCAD. Get the demo and see for your self. And it is 100% dwg/dxf compliant (AutoCAD format). It doesn't support simulations since it's only a CAD.

The most expensive full mechanical CAD/CAM/CAE solution with simulations and all etc. is Unigraphics NX6 (by Siemens) but I doubt you can afford it.
