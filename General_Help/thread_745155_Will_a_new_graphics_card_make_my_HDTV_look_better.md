### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-04-04
The drivers for ATI cards under Linux are not great.  Without the model number, it's hard to comment on experience with HD content on that card.

Since your TV does not have a VGA port in the rear, any desktop display will look like crap through the analog ports.  Samsung professional displays (42, 50, and 63") that I have hung look OK through the VGA port.  There is some slight shadowing of characters but the screen is usable when using the native resolution for each display.

You generally need a professional graphics card (studio quality) to get decent screen images on large displays.  A half-height card isn't large enough for the scalers required to compose such an image.

It's hard to get a display (due to different design specifications) to properly view both HD content and computer screen display.  Samsung has some decent LCD's that can handle both digital and analog content up to 46".

If you get the card at Fry's you can always return it if it doesn't work to your satisfaction.  Let us know the model number and the image quality.

One test is worth a thousand expert opinions.
