### Post by brunogirin on 2009-04-20
How did you install the ISO file to USB?

Otherwise, your computer should allow you to get into the BIOS at startup by pressing a function key, such as F2. Look for a line that says something like "Press F2 for setup" just after the initial splash screen.

### Post by Volt9000 on 2009-04-20
In my experience very few BIOSes support USB booting, but you can still see if yours is one of the few.

But typically you use Delete or F2 or something similar to enter BIOS and change your boot settings there.

Although IMO you're better off just burning to a CD.
