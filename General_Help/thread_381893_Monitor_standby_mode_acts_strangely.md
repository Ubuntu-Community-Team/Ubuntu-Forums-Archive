### Post by XeroZohar on 2007-03-11
I use a Samsung SyncMaster 900NF monitor.  It's a good monitor, works with Linux well once I get the refresh timings correct manually.  But anyway, I have a weird problem I can't seem to diagnose on my own, nor can I google any answers (though I admit, I'm not the best googler).

I use Xscreensaver (4.24), like most do, and I have the power management set to activate my monitor's standby mode after a certain time period.  Well, this issue isn't about whether that works.  I have DPMS enabled in the x.org config, and so it does go into standby mode after a certain time.  The problem I'm having is that the monitor will inexplicably come -out- of standby mode at some point in the night, and will refuse to standby again without me rebooting.  As such, I wake up to my monitor being fully powered (though at least the screen saver is on).

I suppose it's not a huge problem or anything, but it does suck extra power, and in the long run will reduce the life of this monitor (which is going on five years now anyway).  This didn't happen on my slackware box, though.

Anyone ever have a similar experience?  Is there some program at work here that's deciding it's time to wake the monitor up without reason?

Also, be aware that the mouse and keyboard are on a steady surface, and in a closed room, so no animals are responsible. :) (Even if they were, it should theoretically go back into standby mode after the alloted time)

Thanks!
