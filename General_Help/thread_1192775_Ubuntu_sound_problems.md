### Post by fooman on 2009-06-20
if the monitor has speakers,  then it should also have a volume control.  try scrolling through the controls that are built into the monitor itself (brightness, contrast, etc...should be something for volume in there as well).

also in the top panel of your desktop,  on the right side,  there is a speaker/volume icon...right-click on it and choose "open volume control"

...make sure all the sliders are turned up and unmuted.

hope that helps.

### Post by fooman on 2009-06-20
there is no volume control in the top panel?

wait,  are you using xubuntu?

if yes,  then after adding the mixer to the panel...left-click on the icon and when the mixer box pops up click on the "select controls" button.  in the list that comes up,  put a check next to master and pcm.  close the select controls box and then in the mixer you should see sliders for master and pcm...make sure they are unmuted and turned up to the max.

does that help?
