### Post by Herman on 2010-08-27
:D I agree with mike555 and I also want to add, F-Spot Photo Manager is a program that will be worth a try, I think it's having some improvements added to it and it's integrated well in Ubuntu. 
I saw an option in 'System', 'Preferences', 'Screensaver' to show fspot photos, that would be cool!  

In my opinion, it's entirely unnecessary to use any of those horrible vendor-specific camera programs and I think most of them are nothing more than despicable attempts at 'vendor lock-in'. I have people coming to me for help to try to recover their photos from programs like that.

Maybe I'm a little old fashioned, but when I plug any camera into a computer I prefer to open the camera's storage device as if it is just another drive. It doesn't matter whether I'm using Windows or Ubuntu. I like to just navigate to find the image or movie files and just cut and paste them to a new directory which I make for them inside my operating system.  I have not found any brand or model of digital camera yet that I can't simply cut and paste the files from. It's nice and simple and I retain complete control of my own photos. 

As with most things, it's easier and better in Ubuntu. I have a new Sony DSC HX5V and it takes movies as well as photos. I can get the movie files out of it easily using Ubuntu, but as far as I can tell there's no way to do that in Windows even with the camera's own programs, or at least I can't find the instructions for how to do it. Windows can't even 'see' the movie files, but in Ubuntu it's easy. :D
