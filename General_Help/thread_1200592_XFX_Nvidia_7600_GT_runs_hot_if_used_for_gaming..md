### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-06-30
One solution that came to mind is a PCI fan to cool the heat sink. I don't know if it's possible to alter the NVIDIA driver or how to - even if it's possible.
I use this fan
[http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/SearchTools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=120565&CatId=804](http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/SearchTools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=120565&CatId=804)
for my 6200 and it works great. Dropped the temperature by almost 50 degrees depending on ambient room temperature. It increased stability of my video card substantially.

Or I guess you could purchase an off the shelf case fan and find a way to mount it in the case to pull air away from the heat sink. Keep in mind the need to have air exchange capabilities in the case. I removed a couple of PCI slot covers to increase the air exchange capability of my case.

Case Fan example: [http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/searchtools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=3478779&csid=_25](http://www.tigerdirect.com/applications/searchtools/item-details.asp?EdpNo=3478779&csid=_25)

Good Luck and hope this helps.
