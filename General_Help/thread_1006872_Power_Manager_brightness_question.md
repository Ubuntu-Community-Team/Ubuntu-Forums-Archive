### Post by bedake on 2008-12-09
Apparently the power adapter socket on my DV1000 motherboard is broken causing the laptop to constantly switch between a charging and decharging state while connected to a power supply.  This causes my laptops display to constantly dim and brighten as well as causes the gnome power manager to display a brightness dialog box every time it happens.  To remedy this problem I have been stopping the gnome-power-manager process to prevent the box from popping up.


Is there a way for me to prevent the power manager from displaying the brightness window when my laptop switches between charging and uncharging?

And if not, what affect does stopping the power manager process have on my system?

---

### Post by Shwefty on 2008-12-09
Well, in System -> Preferences -> Power Management you can turn off the Reduce Backlight Brightness checkbox under the On Battery Power tab.  It won't pop up the window, but it won't dim your backlight either...
