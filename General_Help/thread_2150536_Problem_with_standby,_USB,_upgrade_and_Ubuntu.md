- Please explain exactly what you mean by standby! Rememeber many of us do not use English as the first language, and the translation of standby, sleep, hibernate etc is often difficult to get right.

- It would also help if you let us know the size of RAM and swap, and it won't hurt if you give also the other main specs of the computer: cpu, graphics.

- Please explain exactly at which point in the standby process, that you get the error "Unable to enumerate USB device on port...".

- Are the error messages in Windows independent of Ubuntu, or do you get them only when you log in to Windows after an Ubuntu session?

- Is it possible that you have a hardware problem with the USB system or with one of the USB devices? This can be checked systematically.

- Could it be lack of power supply? In that case an external USB hub with its own power supply might help.

***Edit***: The LTS versions are the most stable. 12.04 is an LTS version. Now the newest, most debugged and polished subversion is 12.04.2. Xubuntu has support until April 2015, Ubuntu and Kubuntu have support until April 2017. Few if any other linux desktop distros have longer support. The Ubuntu flavours have a good combination of hardware and software support and stability. Debian stable is more stable, but is harder to configure to do what many people want to do with multimedia and internet.

If you have problems with drivers for old hardware, you might try another distro, you mentioned Puppy, there is also Knoppix.
