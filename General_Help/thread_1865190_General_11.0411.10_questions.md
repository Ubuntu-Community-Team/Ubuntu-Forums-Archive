My solution could sound strange and a bit too much, but it works really great for me for a couple of years now..

I really want to try every single version of Ubuntu that comes out. But half of them I won't probably like, and would prefer to have kept my old one (set up the way I like). So what should I do?

Live CD? Doesn't really give you the feel of the system.. It's limited, and you can't really try it for like a week this way!

Another computer? Old and cheap? Won't probably give me an accurate idea about the performance and the full features of the new system.. New and pricy? Only for test purposes??!! I don't really have that much money..

Install it on another partition? Sometimes problems could affect even your first system.. I don't wanna take the risk..

The solution? A second hard disk. For less than $40 you could get a very good hard disk and you can use it to test what you want. MBR of the new system should always go on the test hard disk, and you can choose which disk to boot through the motherboard settings.. Pretty much risk-free. You can run BOTH systems long enough to be able to choose.. If you really like the new system you can just keep using the one you already installed and use the other hard disk as test for the next release. No back-ups needed, it's pretty much affordable, and it solves your dilemma for all the future releases..
