### Post by adie273 on 2005-12-29
If the drivers support dual output and i see no reason why they shouldn't. Then using 'xinerama' you should be able to set it up.

In order to do it, you need to edit your Xorg.conf file. which should be found in...

/etc/X11/xorg.conf

Or something very similar.

You need to be very precise about editing this. I did it before and if you make a mistake in the wrong place, then you'll get no output from your monitors what so ever.

So take note, to work, you need to list both devices in the xorg.conf file, and give both monitors their own identity. Since you seem to have the apptitude to be able to do this, i'll give you my Xorg.conf from another system i have ubuntu on. What you need to do is take a good look at it, (specifically the device, monitor, screen, serverflags and serverlayout sections) and see where it lists 2 monitors, and how they are identified, and at the bottom, are both joined together using the 'xinerama' command.

That probably makes no sense at all i know. But i'll leave my Xorg.conf file below. if you still don't know what i mean exactly. copy and paste your xorg.conf file in a private message to me (will take more notice if you do, rather than pasting it here), and i'll TRY and help you out.

Good luck...
