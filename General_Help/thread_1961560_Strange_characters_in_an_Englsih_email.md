### Post by hwttdz on 2012-04-19
Probably their fault.  Even if you get multiple it's probably their fault.  An unlikely but possible explanation is that the email is utf8 or iso-"ladeda" but your client is interpreting it as the other.  I wouldn't worry too much about the strange characters, and I'd worry even less about malware causing it (if it is an issue it has to do with choosing the default character encodings for emails).
