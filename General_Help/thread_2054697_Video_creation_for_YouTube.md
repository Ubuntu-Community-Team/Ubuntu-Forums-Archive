### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-09-07
Most likely your sound track used copyrighted songs.  YouTube uses a "signature" matching technology that creates hashes from the audio content and compares them to a database of known songs.  If the sound track includes copyrighted material, you are infringing.

They could have simply forbidden you to upload and "[perform]("http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/101")" (in the legal sense) the copyrighted works.  Instead it appears these days that Google surrounds infringing material with advertising.  Presumably they send all or a portion of the advertising revenues to the copyright holders in compensation for your infringment.
