### Post by Revolutionary101 on 2010-01-08
Don't worry about it. Linux is one of the most secure operating systems out there. One good thing is that Windows viruses, rootkits, and trojans will not work on Linux just like how Windows programs will not run on Linux natively.

That doesn't mean you should not take precautions. If you want to make Ubuntu even more secure go visit this thread.

### Post by Revolutionary101 on 2010-01-08
Only way I know that someone could access your computer is via ssh but you would have to have the openssh-server package installed (doesn't come installed with Ubuntu). Also even if you did have this installed they would have to know your password. Just a little fact about computer hackers they usually just scan for random computers to attack. If there is even is the slightest security they will pass over it and continue looking for an unsecure one. They usually don't want to waist their time on computers with security.
