### Post by bigblop on 2006-12-06
In gnome you can add a drawer to you taskbar that can contain programs without filling up the taskbar. Is there something like this for KDE? I have tried to add the Quick Launcher rocket but it shows all the icons instead of hiding them.

### Post by shin on 2006-12-06
Afaik there is no such feature in kde.
But this is what you can do instead (found on google groups):

You need to right click the
KDE menu, choose Menu Editor, and make your own menu called Drawer.
You can use Copy and Paste to paste items in Menu Editor into that
Drawer. You can even make multiple Drawer menus. Be sure to change
that icon to something that looks like a drawer or whatever you want
-- the package box icon looks fairly ugly for this purpose, right?.
Now, to put each drawer next to your KDE "start" menu, right click the
taskbar and choose Add, Button, then browse for your menu. When you
find it, choose Add this Menu. You now sort of have a Drawer on your
taskbar (kicker).
