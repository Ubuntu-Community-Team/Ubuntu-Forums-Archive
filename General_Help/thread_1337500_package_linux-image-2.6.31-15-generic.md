### Post by drs305 on 2009-11-25
New kernels are added to the menu, whether you use Grub or Grub 2. You can hide them or remove them. Hiding removes them from your menu but keeps them in the /boot folder. Removing them via Synaptic or APT removes them from the computer *and *the menu.

In Grub legacy a GUI app did a good job of displaying/hiding as many kernels as you wanted. There is a link in my signature line (SUM) that explains the menus. Near the end is a section on how to remove kernels from your system.

It's best to keep one older working kernel. Some users have had problems with -15, so I'd keep -14 around for a while.
