### Post by cagliostro on 2009-04-27
I am not sure where to post this. I have been working on a U.S. English spell checker dictionary for about 15 years. This can be named as en_US.dic and used in Open Office. Or named as en-US.dic and used with Firefox. There is a 63,000 word difference between this and the current Open Office spell checker, mostly because of the garbage words in that dictionary. The size is the same as the original. You can read my discussion at:

[http://lingucomponent.openoffice.org/issues/show_bug.cgi?id=92383](http://lingucomponent.openoffice.org/issues/show_bug.cgi?id=92383)

Note that there are two attachments. The most recent version is dd_2009_04_en_US.dic, and this can be downloaded. Note the license restrictions for use are those demanded by Mozilla.

I had hoped Open Office might be interested in this, due to the errors in the original. I thought the dictionary maintainer had simply downloaded some lists and MUNCHed them, without any of the exhaustive checks that I have used. However, I recently got an e-mail from him saying that he would block any effort to replace HIS dictionary. Since no one else at Open Office responded I assume there is zero interest. Nemeth is concerned with the Hungarian dictionary. So I am posting this so Ubuntu users will know this dictionary is available.

EDIT: I have now filed issue #101500 with Open Office. (There is a link from issue #92383.) Here one can download my autocorrect file, acor_en-US.dat. This will allow the correction of hyphenated words (the Hunspell spelling checker won't handle hyphens). Install it, and type "broadminded," and see "broad-minded." Enter "self control" and see "self-control," or "razzle dazzle" and see "razzle-dazzle."

Since Open Office won't consider my dictionary, I added a few grammar corrections. If you enter "aircrafts" you get "aircraft" [the plural of aircraft is aircraft]. Same with "macaronis," there is no such word, though it is in the original dictionary. So a handful of errors from the original dictionary are now corrected.
