### Post by velo on 2006-10-16
There are quite a few programs that may fit your needs, to list a few, you could check out:
f-spot, digikam, gnome photo collector, google picasa*, gthumb or gwenview.

These are, of course, more Irfan than Photoshop, for more serious image manipulation needs there is always the gimp.

*) You'll not find Picasa in the Repositories, but it's available [here]("http://picasa.google.com/linux/index.html") for free (the beer type).

### Post by DarkN00b on 2006-10-17
I kinda like Xnview.  I used it for so many years in Windows that it is nice to have the familiar to work with sometimes. I even converted the install file into a *.deb for easy future installation. I have yet to hear back from the webmaster at Xnview.com as to whether they want a copy or not.

Anyway, Xnview is great for viewing almost any type of picture and writing to some I haven't even heard of. There are a few basic filters you can apply as well as manipulations like very good quality resizing of images and the like. In use it is the closest thing to Irfanview I have seen. The only drawback seems to be that the author focuses more on the Windows port than he does on the Linux one.

Congratulations! 
Also, if you are not afraid of the command line, you can easily resize/convert pictures -or whole directories of pictures at once, which has saved me hours in the past- with the tools provided by the program 'ImageMagick'. 
Here is an overview over those commands: [http://www.lsw.uni-heidelberg.de/manuals/ImageMagick/www/tools.html](http://www.lsw.uni-heidelberg.de/manuals/ImageMagick/www/tools.html)

But remember to backup your files before you try something new, 'mogrify' for example overwrites the source files ;)
