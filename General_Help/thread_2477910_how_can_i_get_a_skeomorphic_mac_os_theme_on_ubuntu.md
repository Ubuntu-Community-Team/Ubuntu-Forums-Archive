### Post by QIII on 2022-08-10
Although there may still be some applications with skeuomorphic interfaces, such things have mostly gone the way of skeuomorphic wooden signs carved in the shapes of what might have been a business name when people where less literate.  A carved boar's head over the door of the Boar's Head Inn, and the like.

For Linux I think you will be hard pressed to find a modern theme that will apply such an effect to the entire UI desktop and everything else.  And finding applications that would follow such a selected theme would be harder still to come by.  Even Microsoft raised the ire of a huge swath of their users by getting rid of that sort of thing and going to a pretty brutally flat theme.

There are still people developing Unity for Ubuntu, which may favor what you are looking for.  Bodhi Linux has some themes for their Moksha desktop that provide some elements of skeuomorphic design, but not all the icons and such are designed that way.  And you'd still probably have the issue whether specific apps would comply.  But you could take a look there and see what you find.

If you can't find it for Ubuntu, Bodhi is a fine distro (based on Ubuntu) and that might work for you.
