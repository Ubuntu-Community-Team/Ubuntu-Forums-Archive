### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-18
This depends on your laptop's BIOS. Some BIOS would let you press F12 and then you can choose "boot-device" (check the first screen to see if ti has anything about "select boot device".

If there is nothing of this type, then you have to enter the BIOS settings (usually by pressing "del" key during boot). From the BIOS settings, you go into "Advanced Bios features" and change the "boot order" so that  the first boot device is CD-ROM (HDD should be second).

### Post by Frogs Hair on 2011-04-18
Bios screens are often different from one computer to another . When booting there is sometimes a keyboard option displayed to enter the bios and some laptops will use an Fkey or super key. Do a search to find out how your computer enters the bios and when you find it and enter the bios and look for something like boot options. Migrate to the boot priorities using the arrow keys , make the change , save and exit.
