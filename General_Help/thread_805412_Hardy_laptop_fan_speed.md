### Post by sdennie on 2008-08-15
I don't think most laptops have this problem.  I would recommend:

1) Cleaning all the vents of the laptop with compressed air to help the airflow.

2) When the fan switches to high speed, use "top" in a terminal to see if any application is hogging the CPU.

3) Use the power savings features of the laptop to reduce the heat and thus reduce the fan noise.

The only other thing that's really possible is that your BIOS is simply not playing nicely with linux.  That's not common but, it does happen (I've run into this on Toshiba laptops) and it can be painful to fix because it can involve modifying the "BIOS" yourself.
