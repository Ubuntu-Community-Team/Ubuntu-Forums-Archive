Hello

What do you mean by KDE being 'bouncy?' 

I was the opposite to you, I started with GNOME which I loved but I found that I liked the look of Kubuntu and that I used a lot of KDE programs in GNOME. All this in like 9 months of switching to Linux based distro!

I get what you mean when you say that you feel that KDE isn't as user friendly, but I think that that might stem from the minimalistic appearance of GNOME, which does feel simpler to manage when you have just started. What I do like about the cluttered-ness of KDE menus is that there are more options at your disposal, rather than having to dig deep like in GNOME. 

When I first used Kubuntu Gutsy Gibbon, I also felt as if the crash event pop-up was appearing far to regularly, but after changing the way I set things up, the problem doesn't seem to happen as much these days.

Anyway, to get to the point, in my opinion if you're thinking of upgrading, I'd move on to Hardy Heron but just install KDE 3.5.9. As you can expect, there are some bugfixes to problems under Gutsy but as with every upgrade there is the occasional problem. Unless you are willing to put up with various things that could well be very buggy, I would avoid KDE 4 for now. I think the fact that it is going to take until the next release for KDE 4 to be the standard shows that it is no where near ready yet for the masses. Stick with 3.5.8 or move to the next one up. 

You'll have a better experience with KDE 4 in October than you will right now.
