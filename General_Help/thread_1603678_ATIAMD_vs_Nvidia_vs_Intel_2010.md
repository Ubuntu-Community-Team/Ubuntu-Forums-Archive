I've been running KDE4.X on various nVidia cards (mainly a 8600GT, but also a 8400GS, and a 6600GT), and I'm yet to have any tearing/slow downs. Sounds like a driver issue to me...... 

ATI does have XvBA-

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-Video_Bitstream_Acceleration](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-Video_Bitstream_Acceleration)

From what I've seen of it, XvBA is immature and buggy (and thats being nice!). Intel does have soem support for VAAPI, but I've never given it a serious go so I cant say how good or bad it really is- 

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_Acceleration_API](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_Acceleration_API)

If your doing much gaming at all, avoid intel graphics. Intel has never had a video chip that can get close to the ATI and nVidia offerings. 

Persoanlly, as much as I'd love to say 'get an ATI' at the moment ATI still has more issues than nVidia. Intel......meh, dont bother.
