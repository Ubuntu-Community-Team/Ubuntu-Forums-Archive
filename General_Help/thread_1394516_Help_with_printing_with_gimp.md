### Post by M1GEO on 2010-02-01
What are the dimensions of the picture?  Just thinking if it is a really large image then the scaling may cause that.  Check that when you print the image in Gimp, you have suitable values for dpi, etc.

From **File > Print** check the tabs at the top.  Key options would be that you have the correct paper selected (page setup), you have *reasonable* values for X/Y resolution (image settings), resolution (under image quality), printing quality and the ICM colour profile (all Advanced).

These options will vary with your printer.  I have a office colour laser, so i dont get inkjet-esque options.

Could you try printing the image from eog (Eye of Gnome) or KDE equiv?  Rather than Gimp.  Or convert to PDF and print via Document Viewer?

HTH,
George
