### Post by JamesNorris on 2006-03-19
Enabling administrator mode in kcontrol has been an issue for me as far back as I remember. It either crashed kcontrol, or simply doesnt work. 
My way around it is to try sudo kcontrol from konsole which runs it as administrator anyway. 
What you said about your network card being disabled, I had that problem too, and enabling it form kcontrol crashed kcontrol, so I had to use gnome's networking app. If you haven't got it working yet try these things.
