### Post by timgood on 2010-09-24
If you do not use the default Evolution PIM and mail client, you will have noticed that there are not a lot of options to integrate other clients like Thunderbird etc. with Gnome Panel and receive bubble pop ups to notify you of incoming mail.

I have recently downloaded and installed Popper - a generic email notification system that integrates seamlessly into Gnome Panel.

Popper can be configured with a multiplicity of options, will use POP and IMAP without a problem, is not resource hungry and I heartily recommend it. I think it should be included in future Ubuntu releases.

You can download the latest Popper .deb [here]("https://launchpad.net/popper").

Hope this helps.

It uses the standard mail (envelope) icon in the notification area. You configure it by going to Applications/Internet/Popper Configurator. Once you have configured it, the envelope will turn white when you have mail and you will get a pop-up notification. 

Hope this helps.
