### Post by ram2500 on 2010-03-31
You are somewhat right, but not quite: Free software means "free to modify and share" -- although you can still charge money for it. Novell, Red Hat and IBM sell Linux (and support as well), although you can still modify and share (possibly, I'm not sure about commercial versions...) Free software is usually synonymous with open-source, though free literally means zero cost. 

It's inevitable to have closed-source ideas, code and methods in open source.
