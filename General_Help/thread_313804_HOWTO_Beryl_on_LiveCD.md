### Post by Scheater5 on 2006-12-06
As a relative newbie, the whole idea of this new "Beryl thing" freaked me out.  I'd heard mention a few months ago about it causing major problems and being a pain to fix if it did.  I'd heard about it requiring high end graphics cards, and I definitely don't have one of those.  I'd heard it required 3d acceleration and that my graphics card couldn't do that.  

Well, I'd after hearing so much I had psyched myself out of *doing* anything.  

Out comes the LiveCD.  If I can figure some way to get it up and running on a liveCD I might brave a full install.  

Alright, so, I still don't understand completely how Beryl works, but I have dispelled alot of things a newbie might infer from the information floating around - as I did.  And, more importantly, got it working on a LiveCD so anyone else with a weak stomach like me can give it a go.

For the most part, I took the instructions straight out of [the Beryl Wiki]("http://wiki.beryl-project.org/index.php/Install/Ubuntu/Edgy/XGL").  But the instructions on the Wiki assume a full install, and include all the scripts for getting it on the GDM, KDM, etc, and require a restart - in other words, not suitable for a LiveCD. The solution is deceptively simple - follow steps 2,4, and 5.  Then simply skip the configuration and run (step 7), and viola - if it's possible to run Beryl with your graphics card, you'll see the emerald icon in your tray.   

I'm writing this message from Konqueror in Kubuntu 6.10 on the Live CD with Beryl running.
