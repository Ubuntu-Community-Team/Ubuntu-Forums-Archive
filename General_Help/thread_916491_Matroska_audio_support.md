### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-11
This is a bit of a question and a bit of just thinking out loud, so please bear with me...

I've been working with multimedia a lot recently, and have fallen in love with the Matroska container.  It supports any number of video, audio, and subtitle tracks, the newest and best video and audio compression formats, and even attachments...  Definitely cool.  I can rip a DVD movie using h.264/AAC and retain all of the alternate audio and subtitle tracks, all in one file.  I could even throw in the cover art as an attachment if I wanted.

The popularity of and support for mkv's has been getting progressively better over the last couple of years, due in no small part to the increase in availability and popularity of high-definition media.  As I said, h.264 video and AAC audio with multiple audio and subtitle streams (or even alternate video streams, for those special feature tracks with multiple camera angles) is niiiice.  Quite a number of video players (e.g. VLC, MPlayer, Totem, Winamp, WMP, and basically anything based on GStreamer, xine, or DirectShow) support the Matroska container, and I've been seeing more and more mkv files (particularly with HD content) available on bittorrent sites.

So I've been wondering... With the increasing popularity of Matroska video, where's the support for Matroska audio?  While the media players that support mkv files (and let you select from those alternative audio, video, and subtitle tracks) will play mka files, I've yet to see a music player like Amarok, Rhythmbox, or Winamp offer support for mka files other than *possibly* playing them.  As far as I can tell, Amarok won't even *play* Matroska audio even though it can use xine or GStreamer as its engine.

It seems to me that a container that is free (both gratis and open), supports all available audio compression formats, allows lyrics and album art to be included with the music, provides all the metadata capability you could ever want, and even allows entire CDs (again, with album art and lyrics for each song) to be stored in a single file would be a Very Good Thing.

So I guess my question (partially because I'm genuinely curious and partly to justify this post's existence on the General Help board) is this:  Are there any music players (preferably ones with nice media library support like Amarok or Rhythmbox) that currently provide more than basic support for Matroska audio files, or that plan to do so in the near future?  For example, can any current music player store/read album art or lyrics in/from an mka, exploit the greater possibilities for metadata of the mka container, or allow selection of alternate audio streams/tracks such as in the case of an mka containing an entire album?
