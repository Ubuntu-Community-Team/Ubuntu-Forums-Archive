### Post by mc4man on 2012-08-18
"pre-released updates" are proposed package updates to fix a targeted bug(s). They go to proposed so they can be checked as to whether the bug is fixed & whether they cause any regressions. 
If ok then they generally will leave proposed & be available thru the normal repos

Most users should not enable proposed unless to check for bug fixes that could affect them & test the new packages. The proposed repo  should not be blindly updated from and not left enabled.

"unsupported updates" - backports are usually newer versions of packages that someone has 'backported' for your Ubuntu release.
Since ppa's became popular backports isn't used that much. You can have it enabled as the packages in will should not cause you any issue.
(though you'll probably not use or rarely at best
