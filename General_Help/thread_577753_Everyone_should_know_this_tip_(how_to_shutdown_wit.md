### Post by floke on 2007-10-16
Ok so we all know there are several ways to save ourselves when our system locks:

CTRL-ALT-Backspace (restarts X)
CTRL-ALT-Del (restarts PC)
CTRL-ALT-F1 etc. - drop to tty and use 'sudo reboot' or 'sudo /etc/init.d/gdm restart' etc.

But what can you do when none of the above work? Hard reset?

Well - this has saved me from this several times (the last being after a complete freeze on Gutsy 10 mins ago - just switching users!).

Hold down ALT and the Sys Req key - then press the following letters in this order...(waiting a few seconds inbetween each one)...

R S E I U B

This will safely reboot your system.

I remember it with the phrase

**_R_**eboot! _**S**_o **_E_**verything _**I**_n **_U_**buntu _**B**_reaks? 

Happy reboots!
