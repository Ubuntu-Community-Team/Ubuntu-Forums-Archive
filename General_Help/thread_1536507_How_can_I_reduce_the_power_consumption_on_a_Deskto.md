### Post by cascade9 on 2010-07-22
A few other things you can do- 

Underclock and undervoltage the CPU, and/or change the CPU to a less power hungry model. 
Underclock the GPU, and/or change the GPU to a less power hungry model. 
Change 7200RPM HDDs to 5400/5000RPM 'green' drives and/or SSDs. 
Get a more efficient and lower powered power supply. 
Disable or slow all the fans you can in the system.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-07-22
> **aeiah said:**
> more efficient, yes, but lower powered doesn't really do much. the PSU only draws as much power as it needs

Well, yes, the PSU only draws as much power as is needed. The way that you 'save' power from having a lower powered power supply is because as the power supply drops the load, the efficiency drops as well. See the graph here- 

[http://www.xbitlabs.com/articles/cases/display/seasonic-psu-roundup_2.html](http://www.xbitlabs.com/articles/cases/display/seasonic-psu-roundup_2.html)

That drop is typical of power suppplies. They dont all do it, but the majority of them do. 

As you can see, with the Seasonic S12-II 430, when the power draw drops under 75watts the efficiency stats dropping as well. Once you get to about 50watts its under 80%. 50watts is more than possible as an idle wattage draw. 

So compared to if it was running at 100watts+, its about 10% less efficient. 

Sure, it only adds up to a few watts extra draw, but its there. 

> **Joe of loath said:**
> I would say that keeping the fans on high, and thus keeping the components cool, would save more energy than turning them off, as resistance in the components increases with temperature.

Not from anything I've seen. I dont have 'hard' data, but when I was playing with a power consumpiton metre and a few systems, increasing the fan speed did seem to increase the power draw. Not by very much though....

IIRC, the difference in resitance between copper @ 25C and copper @ 40C is about 5%. 

BTW, with an underclocked and undervoltaged CPU, the temps would be a lot lower, so dropping the fan speeds should still be farily cool.

*edit- I forgot, I've had someone here send me a message about how they dropped power consumption when they moved to a minimal install.
