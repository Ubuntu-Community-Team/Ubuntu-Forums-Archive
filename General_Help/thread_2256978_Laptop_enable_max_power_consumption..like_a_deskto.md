### Post by QIII on 2014-12-16
Let me make an analogy.

Assume you have a car with a powerful engine and and automatic transmission.  At a stop light you could conceivably stand on the brake pedal and mash the accelerator to the floor.  The engine would race and make an impressive amount of noise.  Depending on the robustness of the rear brakes, the power transmitted by the engine might overcome the friction between the rear brake pads and the rotors and your rear wheels might spin.  You could smoke up the rear tires and make quite an impressive show.

You'd wear out your brakes, your tires, your engine, your transmission.  You'd burn a lot of fuel.  You'd heat up the engine oil and coolant.

And all the while you'd be sitting at the stop light going nowhere.

Would this make sense?
