### Post by chuckmoney on 2009-06-02
Well...here's my problem.

I have a T-Mobile G1.  I love it (made even better since Android is built on Linux) and I wouldn't trade it for all the Treos or iPhones in the world.  One of the main reasons I use it is because it works great with Google Talk (aka Jabber with a third party voice system).  I also like using GTalk/Jabber on my laptop, which stays plugged in and running pretty much 24/7 - and this is where the problem starts.  Because the vast majority of Jabber clients don't make people pick which Jabber "Resource" they send messages to, most of those clients choose the system that actually gets the message based upon Jabber Priority numbers.  This is all fine and good, except that I cannot set either my Laptop or my G1 to have a higher priority.  I want messages to go to my G1 when I'm not sitting at my laptop, but when I'm here, I want them to bypass my G1 and just come here.

So, what I think I need is a Jabber client that will drop its priority when I am away or idle so that any new messages will then come to my G1, then when I return it will raise my priority so they will resume heading to my laptop.  However, I have not found any client that does this.  I was considering that maybe just being away or idle would give my G1 preference, but I have tried Digsby on Windows 7, Pidgin in both Windows 7 and Ubuntu, and the GMail Chat built into GMail, and in every case, even when my laptop is Away and my G1 is Available, the message goes to my laptop because of the higher priority.  So again, I need a Jabber client that will dynamically change its Jabber Priority based upon my idle/away status.

I am running Ubuntu 9.04 now and I love it but I do have a piece of hardware (a NanoVision UM-710) that it does not support, so there is always a chance I may switch back to Windows 7, or more likely, dual boot Win7 and Ubuntu, so if you have a suggestion that's cross-platform, that's a real plus.
