### Post by skymt on 2006-08-03
Technically, gcompmgr is just a GUI for xcompmgr. So no, there isn't anything specifically for GNOME.

You can transplant xfwm or kwm into your GNOME environment, or wait for XGL to become stable, or wait for the composite manager built into metacity, which requires X.Org 7.1.
