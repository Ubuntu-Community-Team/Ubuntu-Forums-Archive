### Post by JoxBG on 2007-06-11
If you want just the terminal window, there's no need for extra software. In gnome open a terminal. Go to the terminal's menu and select Edit->Current Profile. Select Effects tab, choose transparent background, then set the slider to the level of transparency.  While in there, look around at the other tabs for options, like colors, fonts, removing scrollbars, and removing menu bars. Have fun.

Jox

### Post by Nythain on 2007-06-11
just thought i would point out that (and i could be wrong here) gnome terminal's transparency is a pseudo transparency, meaning it just uses the section of your desktop background that it covers as its background...

i believe gnome terminal MIGHT have true transparency capabilities but it quite possibly requires a patch and a composite manager (ie beryl/compiz) to accomplish

the moral of this story is if you make gnome terminal transparent and put it over another apps window, you are going to see your desktop wallpaper and not the window underneath the terminal.

a popular method of achieving transparent terminals is to make them borderless and titlebarless and transparent and have them sit on your desktop somewhere, making it look like they are part of the desktop
