### Post by ^Pho[T]on on 2007-06-09
.exe definitely is really meant for windows, but with windows emulators like wine u can make them work pretty nicely in linux. as for linux, most executables do not have a file extension (eg for firerfox, vlc, nautilus, gdm, xchat, apt-get etc). however files which have . dll in windows (ie. libraries) have a .so extension in linux. as for installing drivers, i doubt u can use windows drivers on linux because of the way linux handles them. the linux kernel does understand .exe's. u would need to find drivers made specifically for linux (usually from the hardware manufacturer's website), or research for a linux driver that can work for your hardware. as a last resort u may have to recompile your kernel, but thats an EXTREMELY last resort. trust me, its gruesome and must be avoided at all costs. (i had to do it for my sata hard drive to work)
