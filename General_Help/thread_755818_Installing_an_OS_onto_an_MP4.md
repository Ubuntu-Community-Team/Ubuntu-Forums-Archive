### Post by jespdj on 2008-04-15
This is not going to be as easy as you think, most likely it isn't practically possible.

Do you know what processor is in that MP4 player? It's probably not an Intel x86-compatible processor, so you will not be able to run any standard Linux distribution for a PC on it. Maybe the OS of that thing is baked into a ROM and not replaceable at all. Creating a completely new OS for it is going to be really difficult, and you would have to know the exact specifications of the hardware in the device. The manufacturer most likely has not published that documentation.

It's going to be hard to help you with this, since you told us almost nothing about the player. What brand and model is it?

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-04-15
You got an 8 gigabyte MP3 player from eBay? Sorry to go off-topic, but format it and then check how much space it really has. Some MP3 players on eBay have been modified to report more space than they really have.

If you have to ask how to begin to make such a thing, then you don't have enough knowledge to do it anyway. It would involve you knowing a massive amount of information about the player; you'd probably need to disassemble the original firmware (or a firmware upgrade file) and the player itself to know enough about it to begin.
