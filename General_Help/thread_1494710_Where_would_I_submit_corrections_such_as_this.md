### Post by fredbird67 on 2010-05-27
I'm a Christian and was just having my daily devotionals this morning using Xiphos with the King James Bible and Matthew Henry's Commentaries, all of which were downloaded from the repositories.  Well, I noticed an error in Matthew Henry's commentaries.  The error I found in his commentary of Romans 2:1-8 reads thusly:

> ...Even Gentiles, who had not the written law, had that within, which directed them what to do by the light of nature. Conscience is a witness, and first or last will bear witness. As they nature. Conscience is a witness, and first or last will bear witness. As they kept or broke these natural laws and dictates, their consciences either acquitted or condemned them.

The "As they nature" line jumped right out at me, of course, and therefore, I'm pretty sure that it should instead read:

> ...Even Gentiles, who had not the written law, had that within, which directed them what to do by the light of nature. Conscience is a witness, and first or last will bear witness. As they kept or broke these natural laws and dictates, their consciences either acquitted or condemned them.

Since I downloaded this from the repositories, where would I go to fix this and send the error I noticed to the package maintainers?

Thanx in advance,
Fred in St. Louis

### Post by philinux on 2010-05-27
Open a terminal and use this if this is the offending package.

```
ubuntu-bug sword-comm-mhcc
```

This will take you to launchpad to raise a bug report. You'll need a launchpad account.
