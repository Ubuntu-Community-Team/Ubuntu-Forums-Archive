### Post by hojjat on 2015-02-27
Hi. I have a few simple questions:

1) What package does Ubuntu use for spell checking?

2) What package does LibreOffice use for spell checking?

3) Is there any way one could suggest addition of words to either of those spell check dictionaries?

Note that my question is not how to add words to my local custom dictionaries. I want to help contribute to the upstream dictionaries.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-02-27
Open the software Centre and search for "spellchecker." A list of spell checkers available for download will appear. Not all of them will be installed. It will all depend on the default language of the system. Three spell checker stand out - myspell, hunspell, and aspell.

You can do an internet search for the websites of the developers of those applications. Libreoffice will make use of installed spell checkers although Libreoffice does have its own extensions for different language dictionaries

[http://extensions.libreoffice.org/extension-center?getCategories=Dictionary&getCompatibility=any](http://extensions.libreoffice.org/extension-center?getCategories=Dictionary&getCompatibility=any)

Open Libreoffice, go to Tools>Options>Language settings>Writing aids and you will see that Libreoffice is using Hunspell. At least it does on my installation.

Regards.
