### Post by Adam_GUI on 2007-07-12
Is this a tower or a laptop?
If it's a tower, I'd recommend attaching the old HDD to the middle of the ribbon cable.
(Not the end, because the BIOS will read the end drive as HDA1.)
When you boot, it should mount the old drive and it should show up on your desktop.  That is, provided you're using GNOME or KDE and have desktop icons enabled.

Then, you should be able to copy/paste your files over that you want to keep.

Mind you should probably back them up to a thumbdrive or CD-R for safe-keeping anyway.

Oh!  And be sure to ground yourself with an ESD wrist-strap.  You don't want to kill either your HDDs or any other component you touch in the tower with Electro-static discharge while you have the case open!

If it's a laptop, and/or you have more than one system.  I'd suggest a cross-over cable for direct transfer of files.  You can do this with an FTP client.

Or, you can hook both up to your home network and share the files through SAMBA.
