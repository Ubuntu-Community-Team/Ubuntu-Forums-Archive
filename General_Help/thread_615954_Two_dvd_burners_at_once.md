### Post by x64Jimbo on 2007-11-17
It's not really the speed of the computer that matters most, it's whether or not the two drives are on independent buses. If they are both on the same IDE ribbon, then you're probably not going to be able to do it. You could probably burn one while the other is playing, but not two burning at once. On the other hand, if you have them plugged into two separate buses, then you should be able to do it. I would just use the built-in ISO burner in Ubuntu by right-clicking on the ISO file. This should allow you to do two at once. I think most other burning programs will only allow you one instance at a time. 
Just out of curiosity, why are you burning two discs at once?
