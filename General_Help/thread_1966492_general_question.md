### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-04-27
> **StatusFresh said:**
> I'm saving the download file and it the title amd64 within it.  Does  this have anything to do with amd processor cus my laptops cpu is  intel.

It's only called "amd64" because the first 64-bit PC CPUs were made by AMD and thus the 64-bit instructions were named after AMD. Intel's CPUs are fully compatible with amd64 instructions; so the short answer is that the "amd64" version of Ubuntu that you are downloading is the best one for your computer.

> And i had been advised as to decline when ubuntu prompts me  about something relevant to proprietary hardware because it will  apparently suck alot of power, how legit is that advice?

Sounds like bad advice. Usually when you are prompted to install a proprietary driver, it's because said driver is made by the hardware manufacturer and works better than the one the Linux developers can make; the hardware manufacturer knows a lot more about their own hardware so can make a better driver. Power management is usually better with a proprietary driver.

Usually blanket statements such as "If it asks you to install something, tell it no" or "If you have sound problems, remove this part of your system" constitute bad advice and you should always get a second opinion specific to your situation.
