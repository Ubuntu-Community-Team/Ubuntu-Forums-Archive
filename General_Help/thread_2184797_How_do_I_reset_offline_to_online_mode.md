### Post by Impavidus on 2013-10-30
Your wireless networking has been disabled by the hardware switch. There must be a (physical) switch somewhere on your computer to enable it. On some laptops you have to press Fn+some function key. Maybe you accidentally hit that button.

11.10 has been unsupported for half a year now. I recommend that you upgrade to 12.04, which will be supported until 2017. Right now, security issues in especially firefox and flash won't get fixed.

### Post by Impavidus on 2013-11-01
The upgrade to 11.10 was a nasty one, as so many things changed. Maybe upgrading to 12.04 will be easier, but many people advise to do a clean install. As upgrade problems always seem to hit the same users, that would be a good idea for you too. You can backup most of your settings to restore them quickly. Most settings working in 11.10 still work in 12.04. If you stay with 11.10 your system will be less secure and it will be more complicated to install software from the repositories.

If you don't want to upgrade or reinstall every six months, stay with the LTS releases. You can upgrade or reinstall every 2 years.
