### Post by ayampanggang on 2009-11-14
As others implied,
unfortunately no you can't use iTunes in linux, but there are other alternatives:

Amarok: KDE-based player, what sets it apart from other player at a glance is the nonexistence of the tabular song view exist in most of players. So it might be more of hit and miss. In my case, it's been a miss. 
Rhythmbox: Gnome-based, very mature with little problems. Last time it tried it supported my iPod nano (2nd edition, the slimmer one with bigger screens)
 
Songbird: Overall is a very nice player, the highlight will be its various extensions (since it's built using the firefox "engine"). Every feature that it has has been stable and mostly bug-free.
The main drawback is that it can't burn CD. And what makes it worse is that I tried to drag and drop the songs in the playlist into my CD burning program Brasero and the drag and drop DOES NOT WORK. 

Those three are by far what I use the most. There are others like Banshee, Exaile etc but I rated those three the highest. And no this review doesn't reflect all three objectively. You'll have to use them one by one or atleast look for other reviews to get better pictures.
