### Post by kaffekopp on 2007-06-23
I see it's a while since you wrote on this thread, but I just thought I'd comment.

Uim has worked fine, but it's not totally satisfying to use, for a couple of reasons:
1. As has been pointed out, it sometimes chooses strange kanji. E.g., if you write "tanoshiku" (pleasantly) and press "convert", you get &#20182;&#12398;&#24066;&#21306; (something like "another part of the town"). This is just totally random. 
2. This wouldn't be so irritating if it would "learn" as you went along, ie. that it would remember how you partitioned the inputted conversion candidate and do it that way the next time. Unfortunately, it doesn't. So I keep having to correct it again and again.
3. Overall, the conversion engine feels a fair bit below the level of that in Windows. Its conversions are too often random, something that forces me to write short sections for each conversion. I know, I know, MS has a multi-million R&D section or whatchamahavit, whereas the linux people do this out of the goodness of their hearts (and I truly appreciate the fact that there IS Japanese input that works for Ubuntu), but there's still some way to go for UiM.

Any native Japanese Ubuntu users out there who'd like to comment this? Do you know any tricks or hints that the rest of us don't?
