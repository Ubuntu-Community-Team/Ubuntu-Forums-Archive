### Post by T700 on 2006-07-07
It is completely unneccesary.  Your Linux system is totally unaffected by any Windows viruses.  Because of the nature of Linux and the way permissions are handled, viruses in the sense of what you're used to simply do not and can not exist.  The only reason to run an antivirus program is if you are operating a mail server or if you are very careless and worried about passing on infected emails to Windows users.

Trust me, I know how hard it is to get out of the "defend the castle" mode.  I was a Windows admin and IT guy for years and it was almost painful the first time I ran a box with no antivirus.  Keep up with your updates for the OS and programs (done automatically in Ubuntu), avoid running as root when you don't need to, keep physical control of your PC, and you'll be fine!
