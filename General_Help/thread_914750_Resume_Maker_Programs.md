### Post by kd0afk on 2009-07-01
I went to the link and I noticed something right off the bat.
Here is the text copied and pasted from the description of the first entry on the resume templates. I will color the humorous text in red.
**         [COLOR=Blue][Basic Resume]("http://templates.services.openoffice.org/en/node/1312")[/COLOR]    **

     [COLOR=Blue]This is a close copy of my engineering resume. Resumes are made to be very simple, in fact, they should only be one page for professional positions. A CV is a longer document typically 2+ pages that shows more detail. In general, a resume is used to get a 'bite' from a company. The company you apply at will use the resume as a disqualifer so that they don't need to look through so many applications for a position. Have a CV prepared for when they call you back, so you have additional information available for them. [COLOR=Red]Often resumes are rejeected for grammar, spelling, and other careless errors. [/COLOR]Show that you can be effective by cleaning everything up and reviewing your resume each time you send it. You vastly increase your chances of getting a job if you vary your objective statement to fit the position you are applying for. Include company name, and make sure that the company name isn't just interchangeable. Be specific. your objective statement is really a set of goals you have in working with that company. Be creative. In 5 years you want to be a manager, right?[/COLOR]
