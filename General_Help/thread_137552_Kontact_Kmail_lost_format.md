### Post by MartinG on 2006-02-28
The KMail authors are a bit strict about HTML; it's less secure than plain text, and it bloats a message hugely, so its use is generally disapproved of.  Microsoft, of course, think differently, and their encouragement of the use of HTML in e-mail is one of the reasons that viruses etc. spread so far and fast.

So, although you can do some formatting etc. in KMail it's not really written that way (and personally I'd encourage you to avoid it).  If you feel you *must*, perhaps Thunderbird, Evolution or Opera give more choice.
