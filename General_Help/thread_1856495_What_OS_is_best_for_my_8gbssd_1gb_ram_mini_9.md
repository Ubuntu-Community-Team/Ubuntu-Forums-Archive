### Post by WasMeHere on 2011-10-08
I think any version of Ubuntu or Linux Mint (based on Ubuntu) would work. It is a good idea to test a few of them live: Xubuntu is good, test Lubuntu too, and test the corresponding Mint versions as well as the standard Mint version with gnome desktop environment. One advantage with Mint is that you get full multimedia functionality in the live system. If a light version can do what you need, it leaves more of the memory (RAM and SSD) for your applications :-)

You had better test the games you want to play, because that is one of the few weaknesses of linux. Most of the industry making games make them for mainstream operating system.

Good luck

### Post by snowpine on 2011-10-08
I have the exact identical hardware (right down to the 8gb SSD). I have tried literally dozens of Linux distros and have yet to find one that does not run well on the Mini 9.

Since you are on the Ubuntu forums I will highly recommend Ubuntu or one of its variants such as Xubuntu or Lubuntu. (Currently my Mini 9 is running Salix Ratpoison edition but I am sure that's not what you're looking for!!!)

ps It is false that "netbook edition is required/optimized for SSD." Any recent distro will have good SSD support. Personally I recommend NOT using a "swap" partition on a small SSD; 1gb is enough RAM that you shouldn't need swap (unless you intend to use the Hibernate feature, in which case swap must be equal to or greater than your RAM).
