### Post by isecore on 2009-02-07
> **garethsimpsonuk said:**
> I have an athlon xp 2400+ which is socket a. The listing says its socket a but then lists various athlons, and mine isnt on it. I always assumed that all sockets a's were the same.

All socket A's are the same. However, there are some coolers (older, usually) which are designed for the early Athlon XP's and thus can't cool any of the newer higher speed XP's.

These are however exceedingly rare these days. I haven't seen a cooler that would fit a socket A that couldn't cool even the fastest of CPUs that you can slot in one of those.

So, provided the clearance is enough for the cooler (9 cm sounds quite a little in my ears) most any cooler should be up to the task of cooling your 2400+. Back when I ran a 2200+ I used a dirt-cheap Arctic Cooling-thingy that was virtually quiet and could cool anything. If you can find one of those I recommend it, but coolers for socket A are getting difficult to find these days.
