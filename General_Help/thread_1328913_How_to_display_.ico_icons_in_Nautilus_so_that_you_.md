### Post by kentechy on 2009-11-16
I have searched high and low for help on how to display .ico file icons, so that you can see them and use them.  I figured out, as well as saw a hint on line, that if you open them in Gimp they will then be visible.  After you open them in Gimp they will be visible from then on.  You can also rename the extension to .png and they will magically appear.  Well, if you have a few hundred of them this could be a real pain.  What I did, and forgive me if this is old news, was:


[LIST]
[*]Open the folder that contains your .ico files with Nautilus.
[*]Click on one of the files to highlight it, press CTRL-A, to highlight all of the other files in the folder you are in, assuming they are .ico files as well.
[*]Right click on one of the highlighted files and select "Open with other application" and select to open with "Gimp Image Editor".
[*]Sit back and wait for all of the files to open.  It will make a mess on your desktop...
[*]When it is finished close the Gimp program and any other windows that are open.
[*]Finally, go to Nautilus and open your folder with the .ico files.
[*]You should now see the images on all of your .ico files...
[/LIST]
