### Post by alex.wifiguy on 2010-10-21
I can no longer put up with Empathy, I'm heading back to Pidgin.

I voluntarily switched to empathy at some point.
The switch to Empathy was originally for better VoIP support or IRC, I don't remember which. Both clients were far to simple to handle my IRC needs, I quickly abandoned both of them for X-chat, then Konversation.

Empathy has it's flaws and is defiantly not as advanced as Pidgin, but I absolutely loved it's integration, for the most part, in Lucid.
**Empathy's strength and it's weakness rest in it's simplicity**. It is undeniable that a non-geek or anyone for that matter would be able to configure, for example, a Facebook chat account(XMPP) faster and easier.

I loved Empathy's integration into the system, even as a power user I was able to overlook its lack of features due to this. The second I reinstalled Pidgin I was greeted by an account set up that would make a non technically inclined user explode. For anyone wondering why the switch was made from Pidgin in the newer releases look no further.
Wouldn't it be grand if the projects merged and all was right with the world, a pidgin with empathic powers.

I could ramble on all day about what I do and don't like about Empathy, but I have other business to attend to.

Until the day Empathy's lack of any possible way, built in or otherwise to pop up a window, or at least **provide some significant way of telling me another human being is trying to contact me** I shall rely on this carrier-pidgin of a client.
It takes a little work initially to get it to fly right, which could easily be remedied, but is a far more capable client that empathy.
