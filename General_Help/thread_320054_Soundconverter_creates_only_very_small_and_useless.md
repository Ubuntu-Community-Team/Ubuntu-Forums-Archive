### Post by ubu-for on 2006-12-17
One day later... :evil:

Even Windows Media Player 10 and 11 for Win XP don't play the bought files AND the test file until the player gets the license over the internet.

So don't buy WMA files with DRM if you don't have a legal copy of Windows because you can only burn an audio CD with the Windows Media Player (WMP) which checks your Windows before the installation. Maybe Nero for Windows could be an alternative.

Even the WMP licensed files can't be converted with Linux programs because the license stays at the WMP and doesn't change the files. So they are still protected and you can't listen to YOUR songs for example with XMMS.
