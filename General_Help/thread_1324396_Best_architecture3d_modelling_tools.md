### Post by Chas of Nevada on 2009-11-13
I've tried most of them and they are lacking, even amateurish, at least the free versions. 

In 2001 Visio went out of business and put it's source code for Intellicad 2000 on the internet as a free and licensed download.  It was a very nice AutoCad clone.  I was using AutoCad R14 at the time.  A few bugs but very usable. In the mean time a consortium has been setup to manage the source code and license it to resellers at a modest fee.  All the MS Windows AutoCad clones you see that are a ceap clone of AutoCad are based on this release.  IMHO.

I wonder what the legal issues for a port to Ubuntu would be?  The source code that was released was developed in MS Visual Studio v6.0 and ran on MS Windows 2000.  I'm not a C programmer but it seems to me that it would be beyond a single programmer to do the port.  It's a very complex program.

hmmmmmmmmmmm.  Would fill a pretty large hole in Ubuntu's market share.  :smile:
