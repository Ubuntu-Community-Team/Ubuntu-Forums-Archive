### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-12-21
Well the facts are that Edubuntu, ubuntu, kubuntu and xubuntu are all much too fully featured and modern for a PC with less than 256mb ram and integrated graphics card. You're kinda lucky it runs at all.

For a low spec system a person can live with you'd be obliged to explore alternatives. The ease and sophistication you enjoy with ubuntu is pretty much out unless you can get a min of 256mb ram in there (384 is what you might want to shoot for as a stable, reasonably performing number). Some choices:

- Puppy Linux.

- Damn Small Linux.

- Fluxbuntu.

- A very careful "hand-rolled" build of ubuntu starting from a server install, probably centered around IceWM as window manager (most Windows-like of the lightweights and with the IceBuntu theme you can find around here it looks ok). Pick light apps, etc. OpenOffice is out. AbiWord is in if you're lucky. You should be able to bring in educational / game apps from the repositories without much trouble as most are light.

- Others I know little about.

Two fellow forum members have some help online in various aspects of light systems:
[http://www.psychocats.net](http://www.psychocats.net)
and especially
[http://kmandla.wordpress.com/](http://kmandla.wordpress.com/)

It's great to keep "legacy" units in service, good for folks, good for the environment, etc. And you can. But generally the "turn-key" modern distros don't target those specs so you do have to roll your own to some extent. Good luck to ya.
