### Post by yetiman64 on 2012-01-22
Not sure about putting them inside the case as they are physically different sizes (the laptop HDD would be sitting loose inside the normal HDD bay, and as such if the case is moved could possibly do damage to it). Though the power and data connections are the same.

It is possible to purchase a single external dock that will accept both sizes of HDD, 2.5 and 3.5 inch, as both have the same connector layout. The one I use is a "Welland" (brand name) dock and takes both sizes, it connects to a machine either by usb2 or e-sata connectors, (note: it needs its own power supply to be mains supply connected).

Maybe if you found a way to secure the laptop drive it may be usable as you suggested (directly in the desktop case).

### Post by leth on 2012-01-22
You can purchase brackets that are made for mounting 2.5" drives in a 3.5" bay.  You can find them at any real computer store (one that sells parts, not a big box store like BestBuy) or on eBay ([http://goo.gl/sL41R](http://goo.gl/sL41R))

The SATA cables in a desktop computer are the same as a laptop, so connecting it won't be a problem.
