### Post by oscarivera9 on 2012-04-30
So you wish to upgrade.  Very good, I strongly recommend it.  Ubuntu 12.04 is a Long Term Release and 11.10 is not, so eventually you'll have to upgrade anyway, might as well do it soon.
Here's my advice on how to go about doing it, based on exactly how I did it.  First thing you should do is download the 12.04 version and burn an iso image on a disc to see if your computer will work well with the 12.04 Live CD version.  If your PC gets along well with Ubuntu 12.04, then you can proceed.  Unless you have made some custom changes to your current setup, and you don't want to loose your customized installation, it's much easier to do a fresh install.  Either way make a backup before you upgrade.  

1. If you will do the fresh install, then backup at least your /home folder so that you can keep whatever files, documents, music, pics, videos, etc. you might have accumulated in your /home folder.  Then do the fresh install, as it will be much, much faster than the online upgrade through update manager.

2. If you are like me, and you have made lots of changes to your installation that would be too time consuming to have to redo, then go on with the direct upgrade through the update manager.  This was the preferred route for me because of the level of customization that had become my 11.10 install(which in turn was also upgraded direct from 11.04).  I still went ahead and backed up EVERYTHING, just in case.  You can never tell how things will go and it's better safe than sorry right?  Here's a link for a good way to perform a good backup:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BackupYourSystem/TAR](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BackupYourSystem/TAR)
After you have finished your backup, then go on and bring up the update manager and proceed with the upgrade.  It is a very long process, especially when compared to the fresh install.  Also, there are a couple of instances during the upgrade process that you have to make decisions such as yes/no in order for the upgrade to continue; so it's not like you can just begin the upgrade and forget about it until it's done.  I'm not saying you have to be glued to your computer, but you also don't want to completely forget about it.  For me, it took me about all of Friday.  I began the upgrade process, left home, came back and had to answer questions at the terminal, left again, came back to more questions, went to work, came back, and left again until it was finally finished by Friday night.

So there you have it, but remember, it is only my opinion.  Wait a bit to see what other people have to say about it and then make the decision.  Honestly, it's way easier and faster to do the fresh install, but like I said, if you have to then go ahead with the online upgrade (which is time consuming but I wouldn't necessarily say that it's hard, because it really isn't, it just takes a loooooooong time).

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-04-30
The length of time for upgrades will diminish as the frenzy dies down and more servers come online internationally. Upgrading generally takes 2-3 hours, depending on your download speed, but as 12.04 has just been released it's unpredictable ... Things may be a little quieter now (a little!). If you're in no rush wait a week or two and upgrade. If you don't want to wait and you're going for a fresh install the torrent is the safest and the fastest method of downloading the ISO.

12.04 LTS is supported for five years, incidentally ...
