### Post by goatflyer on 2007-01-04
Interesting question.

According to the .NET terms of service which they claim to have updated in July 2006,

[http://tou.live.com/en-ca/default.aspx?HTTP_HOST=tou.live.com&url=/en-ca](http://tou.live.com/en-ca/default.aspx?HTTP_HOST=tou.live.com&url=/en-ca)

> 
   ...
4. How You May Not Use the Service.

In using the service, you may not:
    ...
    * use any unauthorized third party software or service to access the Microsoft instant messaging network (currently known as the .NET Messenger service);
    * use any automated process or service to access and/or use the service (such as a BOT, a spider, periodic caching of information stored by Microsoft, or “meta-searching”);
   ...
You can find a list of authorized third-party software at [http://messenger.msn.com/Help/Authorized.aspx](http://messenger.msn.com/Help/Authorized.aspx).
  ...


The list of authorized "third-party" software includes products made by Microsoft subsideries, these are not exactly arms-length 3rd parties.  Probably only companies that have paid some kind of licensing fees to Microsoft to be included on the approved list.  You can bet your backups that anything Linux or BSD won't appear on that list.

I'm no lawyer, but you have to wonder where that places Novell SUSE Linux users who use gaim to access MSN.  Microsoft recently signed a mutual non-litigating agreement with Novell.  What will they do?  Pull the plug on SUSE users?  Or will MS release MSN Messenger for Linux?  (with source rpms!!) How can/will their MSN service distinguish IP packets from gaim running on SUSE with gaim running on other Linuxen?

Maybe by the time we figure it out, MSN will update their TOS yet again making this whole legal tangent moot.  In the mean time, you should decide for yourself what it means for you and act accordingly.  If MS tomorrow changes their TOS to say all users of MSN must run Vista, what will you do?
