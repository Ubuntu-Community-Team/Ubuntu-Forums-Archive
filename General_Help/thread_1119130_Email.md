### Post by Squalo_ch on 2009-04-07
Hello Cowboy,

I don't know for Thunderbird but with Evolution you can configure that option: **Edit> Preferences> Mail Accounts** then select the mail account you like, click **Edit> Receiving Options** and check the "Leave message on server" option under **Message Storage** section.

Hope that helps.

### Post by davec64 on 2009-04-07
Sorry but I can't answer your question directly but your University might also have the IMAP protocol available as well if you ask.
The benefit of IMAP is in simple terms, you set any email programme up pointing to the IMAP server and it reads your emails and copies them down to your local machine. When you delete an email the next time you sync with the server it is then deleted. The benfit is you can configure across several PC's.

I know it's not what you asked but it was just a thought :)

### Post by cowboy_mcd74 on 2009-04-08
So, I figured it out, and just in case anyone stumbles upon this thread looking for a solution to the same problem.... Thunderbird's shown me, once again, that it's amazing. =D

If I go back into the settings after I set up the account, and before I let it get my mail, I can change the server settings to "leave messages on server" and remove them from the server "after I delete them". Tested it, and it works perfectly. =D
