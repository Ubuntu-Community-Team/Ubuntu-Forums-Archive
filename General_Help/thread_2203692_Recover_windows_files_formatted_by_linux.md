### Post by Impavidus on 2014-02-04
Edit: Zerothly, welcome to the forums.

Firstly, I hope you have proper backups. Win XP is nearly end-of-life, but of course you want to keep your documents. That's why we recommend to make backups, especially before changing partitions.

Secondly, you may try some data recovery. It's unlikely you will be able to recover it all, but you may get something back: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DataRecovery](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DataRecovery). I can't say what's the best strategy. I never had to do this myself. In the meantime, don't do anything that may cause processes to write to your disk.

Thirdly, Ubuntu 9.something is end-of-life. If you want to install Ubuntu, take either 12.04 or 13.10.
