### Post by samigina on 2009-12-01
Theres many post with this question.. the answer is always the same:

- Lightweight linux like Crunchbang (based on Ubuntu, a little hard interface), or Puppy Linux (faster and lighter distro ever seen, but harder for a common user).

- Xubuntu, the light version of our beloved Ubuntu, but this requires a little more resources than the previous both.

### Post by Leppie on 2009-12-01
There's also Deli linux, a distro specific for dated hardware.
This kind of implies that a lot of cool new things may not be available (I believe the kernel is 2.4.xx).
DSL is an ultra-light (the whole system fits on a 64mb usb stick) debian distro, but uses Fluxbox and JWM as window managers (like Crunchbang's Openbox window manager harder to use for windoze users).
