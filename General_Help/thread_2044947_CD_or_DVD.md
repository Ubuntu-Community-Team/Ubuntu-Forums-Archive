### Post by cbanakis on 2012-08-20
A regular 700MB CD is perfect for Ubuntu.

There is a larger DVD image available, but as long as you have internet access, the DVD is not necessary.

The CD installer pulls what is needed for "your" system from the internet, and the DVD includes what is needed for "every" system.  (I believe)

### Post by cbanakis on 2012-08-20
> **Manhi40 said:**
> I recommend using a flash drive, it's much faster and not as noisy, I used a cd, it was something like 680 mb and it worked fine...

I have to respectfully disagree.

I was all about the flash drive, because of the speedier install, and since you dont waste a CD on it.

But I and others have experienced issues installing from a flash drive.

For example...
Sometimes the Ubuntu installer puts the boot-loader on the flash drive, instead of the hard drive.  (This results in the flashing curser on reboot that many have experience)

There are a few other issues that spring up from time to time that are caused by a flash drive install, but I can't think of any off the top of my head.
