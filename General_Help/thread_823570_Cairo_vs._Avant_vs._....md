### Post by ringer on 2008-06-09
i, like you, used awn for about a month, and then decided to try the other ones.
i am now using cairo dock now and love it.  

awn is the best for new linux users.  not too many options to tinker with, and pretty straight forward, and acts just like the taskbar in XP did.  
cairo-dock on the other hand (IMO) offers more 'eye-candy', it has the fish-eye animation that is more like the Mac dock, has pre-loaded skins/themes, and sub-docks that are very useful.  

now i have cairo set up the way i want i will never go back.  and it acts just like awn did, perfectly, showing open windows, seperating applets from applications and launchers.

oh, and i had also played around with kiba-dock for a while and it was cool, (once you got in installed, which was a pain), and it crashed on me once in a while.  

also, cpu usage, im not sure about, i think cairo is the highest out of the three.  right now its taking about 7mb.  i forget what avant and kiba are like.  

anyways, take a try with cairo and see if you like it.

### Post by pmaconi on 2008-06-09
I have used both AWN and Cairo-Dock. I prefer AWN because it is very simple to use and set up. Cairo-Dock was plagued with poor localization and a configuration dialogue that was overly complicated. 

I also prefer the right-click behavior of AWN over Cairo-Dock. 

If you don't mind messing with settings for hours, definately give Cairo-Dock a try. It runs very smoothly and it is very nice to look at (assuming you take the time to make it so). Otherwise, stick with AWN.
