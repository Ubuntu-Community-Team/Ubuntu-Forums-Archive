### Post by Sweet Spot on 2008-03-20
I just tried Songbird, and you don't have to install it at all really. Just download the tar.gz file, extract to your folder of choice, and click on the executable file to run it. I think this is the best way to do it for anybody just wanting to check it out, really. 

And I'm glad I did, because Songbird is nowhere ready for prime time. It's all very nice and flashy, but BLOATED to hell right now. 182 Mb of RAM just to play music ? No thanks. And a constant 30% draw on the cpu... 

I haven't found ONE single music player that was easy enough to use for Ubuntu/Linux that accomplishes what a music player should by nature.. Which IMO, is to be able to at least play tracks the way there were meant to be heard, ie; With NO gaps. And I'm not talking about cross fade tricks either. Just plain ol' gapless playback. 

It's funny to me about music players in Linux in general, that the most fundamental feature in relation to the application's purpose, should be left out totally, only to be replaced by snazzy little distractions which only serve to change the nature of the players intended purpose all together. 

I'm all for the integration of feature sets, but getting the basics right the first time, is probably a good thing not to forget, IMHO.  

At any rate, Songbird looks like something that should certainly be on anyone's watch list. It is a very nice app, with some great ideas, and I'm looking forward to seeing how it progresses. If it manages to consume the same amount of resources as Rhythmbox does while just playing music, I'd consider giving it more of a go. And it really looks like it kicks the crap out of iTunes, feature wise.
