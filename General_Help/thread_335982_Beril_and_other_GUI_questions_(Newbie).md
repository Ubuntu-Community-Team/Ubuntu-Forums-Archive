### Post by Sef on 2007-01-12
From [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beryl_%28window_manager%29"):

> Origin

Beryl is the new project name for the Quinnstorm branch of Compiz, announced on 19 September 2006 after Quinnstorm and the development team decided that the fork had come too far from the original Compiz started by Novell (compiz-vanilla). After the Novell XGL/Compiz team (mostly David Reveman) refused the proposition to merge the Quinnstorm changes with compiz-vanilla, the decision was made to make a real differentiation.[1][2][3] As of 20 October 2006, version 0.1.1 has been released into the original compiz-quinnstorm repositories and mirrors. On 25 December 2006, version 0.1.4 was released for testing purposes.

### Post by dabl on 2007-01-12
I have been playing with Beryl this week on my Kubuntu Edgy desktop system, with Nvidia 7900GS video card, and latest Nvidia driver.  Beryl is not entirely stable on my system -- some "window decorations", like Aquamarine, break my taskbar or other parts of my desktop.  If you search this forum or Google for "windows border missing" or "window decorations problem", you can learn a lot.  Also, if your video card has a small amount of memory, you can get blank/black windows.  But Beryl is very cool if you can make it work on your system.
