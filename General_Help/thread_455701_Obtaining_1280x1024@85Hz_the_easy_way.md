### Post by mozetti on 2007-05-27
> **xenomorph99 said:**
> That's the one that asks the questions you're unlikely to know, isn't it - so you hope for the best and guess them then are not surprised when it still doesn't work. 

Nope. It's the one that assumes you're willing to spend 15 minutes researching the right information about your hardware, so you can enter the right settings. If you're not willing to do anything, then no one will be able to help you.

But, if you either find the manual that came with your monitor or search for it online, you can find out your horizontal and vertical sync ranges. Then you just run: ```
dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```

Choose the "Advanced" option and put in all the information. Remember to mark each of the resolutions you want to have available to you. I did this for my wide-screen LCD -- it took all of 15 minutes to do everything (including going up to the attic to get the manual out of the box).

So, your options are:

1) Be lazy and pout because your monitor doesn't display the way you want it
2) Do something for yourself, learn a thing or two, and get your monitor and video card working for you.
