### Post by Kralizec on 2007-09-17
Once files are on a CD-R, nothing can be added or removed. For all intents and purposes, after data is put on a CD-R, the cd is essentially locked and nothing can be moved to or from (with the exception being data/media ripping, but even then the data stays on the disk). Sorry to say, but you'll have to burn another disk. You might want to look into CD-RWs, which work more the way you want them to (allows for removal and addition of data; the R in CD-R stands for Recordable, the RW in CD-RW stands for ReWriteable).
