### Post by Dylan1473 on 2012-08-04
You might be surprised at the hardware some smart phones come with. I saw one in store a while ago that had I believe a dual core 1.5ghz processor, which isn't that huge but would I think be able to handle Ubuntu. The desktop environment/window manager I'm currently using (Enlightenment) also offers features for mobile phones and I believe works on Android.

Ubuntu isn't really a big resource hog compared to Windows, and can be further trimmed down. Linux in general can of course be made to run on very weak hardware. As I'm guessing you already know, Android itself runs on a heavily modified version of the Linux kernel.

EDIT: _[The system requirements]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements/")_. If you look through you'll see it's something that higher end smart phones should be able to handle without much trouble.

EDIT AGAIN: It looks like he's actually running Ubuntu with the LXDE desktop environment, which has even lower system requirements.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-08-06
Rooting an Android phone is no more illegal than wiping Win7 from your store-bought laptop and replacing it with Ubuntu (or any other OS).

But, in both cases, you've voided any warranty support you got when you bought the devices.

With a laptop, depending on how you replaced the OS, it may be a simple and cheap process to restore it to it's original state -- and if you're careful with the smartphone, you can do the same.

One difference with smartphones is that if you obtain it through an ISP (like Verizon, Sprint, T-Mobile, AT&T -- and others) they will REFUSE to service your phone, once they discover you rooted it, and might also terminate your phone service.  Is this last part legal? Probably NOT -- and if you have around $30,000 USD lying around (the ballpark cost of a "simple" civil lawsuit), you can try to find out.  IN contrast, should HP (just to pick one vendor at random) discover that you NOW have Ubuntu on your HP laptop, there's nothing they can do about it.
