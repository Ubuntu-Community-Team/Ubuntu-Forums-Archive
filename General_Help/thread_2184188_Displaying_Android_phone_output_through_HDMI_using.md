### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-10-29
Yes and no.  The HDMI port on a laptop is designed as output only--it allows you to hook up a laptop to a TV using an HDMI cable.  I don't know of any laptop that has an HDMI input.  The cable end is the same, so you can't tell by looking at the cable.  Some phones and tablets allow you to hook up a mini-HDMI cable to a TV and mirror the phone or tablet on a large TV.  I have a blackberry playbook that allows that.  You can play movies on the tablet and display them on a 60" screen, which is rather interesting.

I don't know of a way to do that wirelessly through Android.

Some TV's support DNLA and if the TV is connected to your network (wired or wireless), then you could serve DNLA content from your phone to your TV.

So perhaps explain in detail your Use Case.  What exactly are you trying to accomplish?  There may be other ways to do it.
