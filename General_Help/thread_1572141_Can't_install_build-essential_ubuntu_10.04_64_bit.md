### Post by afloatingfish on 2010-09-11
I know this is a blasphemy, but for beginning programming, and starting with a low level language like C, C++, I would recommend going with the Winblows side of your computer. Great GUI with Winblows VB (Visual Basic etc.) and free tools. Bottom line: use what your instructor and lab uses. 
  Note too: If I were teaching beginning programing, I would start with Ruby, a higher level language, but conceptually complete for the neophyte, and a quicker ramp-up to: "look what I made!"
   Lastly; I've never had a Winblows/Ubuntu partitioned disk that didn't eventually fail catastrophically. You can't go back and save one side without blowing the other... but your mileage may vary.
