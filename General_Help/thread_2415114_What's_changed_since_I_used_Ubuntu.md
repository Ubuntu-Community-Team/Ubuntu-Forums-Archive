### Post by mastablasta on 2019-03-20
i use Kubuntu and i hardly ever touch the console. i guess it depends on how the applications are done. if they have an UI, they do not need console. if the UI is lacking features then you need UI. in general KDE philosophy is to put everything in GUI (all settings etc.) while gnome will have less settings and options (in general) available in GUI as to not overwhelm the user. things are changing, but KDE still has most settings available in GUI, so if you like GUI then why not use KDE?
