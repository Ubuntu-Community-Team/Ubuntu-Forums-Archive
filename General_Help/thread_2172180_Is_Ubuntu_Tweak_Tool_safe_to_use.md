### Post by JKyleOKC on 2013-09-03
I use it regularly and have not seen any problems resulting. However, like all tools that do automated removal of files, one must be careful about what you let its "Janitor" clean off of your system. Fortunately it's quite easy to see a list of what it proposes, before it does anything drastic, and equally easy to take anything critical off of the list. My rule is simple: "If in doubt, leave things as they were!"

### Post by JKyleOKC on 2013-09-04
The "Janitor" feature is only one of many tweaks offered. I find several of the others to be quite useful, especially the ability to list many PPAs that can be added to one's repository list simply by checking a box. However all tools that modify the system itself must be used with caution. Blindly poking around to see what will happen is a good way to get lots of experience in repairing a broken system and salvaging precious files.

FWIW, I've seen many more cautions about misuse of Bleachbit and CCleaner than about Ubuntu Tweak, over the years. All such tools carry a bit of risk, but users of Tweak seem more cautious than others...
