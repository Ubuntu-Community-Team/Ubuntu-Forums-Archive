### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-07-15
This post should really be in the Community Cafe, not in General Help.

Windows' GUI is not very user-friendly compared to Gnome.

Linux competes very well with Windows, full-stop. Multi-touch has been implemented on Linux, but it's more of a token gesture (pun intended) because only a handful of people actually want to use it.

Think about it: Big monitors (22 inch and above) are becoming more popular, but you have to sit back from a big monitor otherwise you get eye strain. With multi-touch, you have to be within arm's distance of the monitor. But let's say you use a small monitor: Now you have to have big widgets on a small monitor, so you can accurately prod them with your big fingers. Losing screen real estate on a small monitor will be about as popular as gut rot.

In short, multi-touch is a good idea for embedded computing (handhelds) but is ill-considered on desktop computers. Linux has multi-touch for the embedded computing side, but few expect it to actually take off on general-purpose computers.
