### Post by Ubun2to on 2012-07-11
I have a question about suspend.
On Windows, being able to put my laptop into Hibernation was great-it put all my processes into swap, so I could safely change the battery when I was about to die without a charger in site.
So, my question is, can I use Suspend to switch batteries if I don't have my charger with me? I don't want to mess up my laptop when it arrives. I also don't feel like using Hibernation on Ubuntu until it isn't buggy (I've heard it is extremely unreliable).

---

### Post by GreatDanton on 2012-07-11
No. Suspend put's all your processes into Ram, so you need power. Hibernate put's all your processes on Hard disk drive so you don't need power (it's simmilar to shutdown).

Hope this helps.

Regards.
