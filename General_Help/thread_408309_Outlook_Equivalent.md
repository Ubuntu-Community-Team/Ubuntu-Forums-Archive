### Post by 50words on 2007-04-16
I am in the same boat as you. I feel like every time I ask about this, however, people focus too much on the e-mail aspect. Yes, Thunderbird is great for e-mail, but Outlook is pretty darn perfect as a PIM. One of the features I love most is the ability to link e-mails, contacts, tasks, and appointments to contacts. That, plus the "Activities" tab/button are indispensable tools for me that I have not found anywhere else. (I am an attorney, and I need a way to associate contacts, tasks, e-mails, and appointments with a "case" contact, and this is the best way to do it.)

(I am dual-booting Ubuntu and WinXP, but I am no Linux guru like you are.)

That said, indexed search like Beagle and Windows Desktop Search, along with search folders, are close to eliminating my need for linking. True, you turn up more junk with search than with linking, but it saves a lot of time on the front end, since linking isn't exactly the easiest thing, especially when it comes to e-mail.

I like Evolution, though, and it does have instant search. It does not have linking, however. It works great as a PIM, but it lacks the ability to associate tasks with contacts, etc.

Kontact/Kmail is a bit better, since you can customize some fields. I think I am going to try Kubuntu when the next release comes out and try using the custom fields as a place to associate forms with a "case" label. That may do the trick.

But in the end, Outlook is just plain better than anything currently available for Linux. Zimbra may end up being the best solution, but if there is an easy way to use it yourself, I have missed how to do so.
