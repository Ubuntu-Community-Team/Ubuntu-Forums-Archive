### Post by 23meg on 2006-12-29
About privacy breaches and spying: it's always possible, but technically, the possibility is inversely proportional to the amount of peer review. If it's a distro used by   a reasonable amount of people, a good percentage of whom are willing and able to look into the source code, there's little or no possibility.

That said, in terms of tradition, will and intentions, people who deal with Free software in general, and those who make Ubuntu derivatives in special, do what they do in good intentions of helping others. I've never heard this nice status quo being exploited.

About security: if the distro is introducing new features, they have to be able to provide security fixes for them. You should look at how competent they are at this and decide accordingly; every distro may not do equally well.
