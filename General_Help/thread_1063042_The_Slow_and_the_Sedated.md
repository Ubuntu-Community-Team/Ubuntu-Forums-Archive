### Post by cmnorton on 2009-02-07
Unfortunately, the default requirements for each Ubuntu release require more horsepower. Given your current hardware, maxing your memory, if not already done, helps, especially on boot. It might even help with Firefox and other applications. 

I have a new laptop, with a decent dual-core chip -- not the fastest -- Intel graphics, and a 7200 RPM hard drive. The RAM is maxed at 4GB. If I can get six years out of the laptop across two or more Ubuntu releases, I will be very happy. Unfortunately, some of our day-to-day uses/needs do not increase as quickly as each release demands from its hardware.
