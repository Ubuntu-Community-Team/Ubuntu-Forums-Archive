### Post by Nepherte on 2008-12-26
Compatibility is not an issue, whatever brand you choose. External hard disks are (almost) always formatted in fat and linux has built-in support for it. Other file systems are no problem either, but you won't find them easily on external disks.

### Post by geodeath on 2008-12-26
Although what i am going to say is mostly about internal hard drives, i think the same will apply for external ones.

Firstly (my advice only) avoid western digital hard drives. I am a computer geek for more than 10 years and i have seen more than 5-6 western digital hard drives failing for no reason (randomly, after a couple of years time) and denying access to certain portions of the disk. Eventually i was able to get data back with one way or another and a couple of these disks worked fine after formatting, etc, but what matters most for me is my data and from what i ve seen i do not trust any western digital. It is quite hillarious that i have no western digital drive left from "the old days" while i have a lot of maxtors and seagates. What i would suggest is to go for seagate disks, preferably with 32mb of cache (if they even put these in the external range).

Other than the above, stick to esata external hard drives cause they will give you all their bus speed (80-100 mbs) where usb ones will max out at 22mbs or close. You really do not want to fill a tb of data at such a rate. There are some disks that will have both usb & esata connections which is what i suggest. 

Also, do not start a flame war if western digital is your life/trusted partner/lover. This is all from my own experience and no speculation. I am a happy camper with seagate drives and unless i have one break up on me i wont change my preference.
