### Post by cascade9 on 2011-01-18
You could put 'swap' on a flash drive, but that would proably be slower than swap on a HDD (USB 2.0 flash drives at top speed go about as fast as a modern HDD running as slow as they get).

Even if you could add a flash drive to your system memory, it wouldnt help much, due to the slowness of USB (and the amount of CPU use that USB has).

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-01-18
> **bobblues said:**
> I am using an old IBM laptop the supports only 512 of memory. Is is possible to use a stick drive to expand my internal memory? Think pad A21m. Ubuntu 10.10.

You may be thinking of ReadyBoost -- a claim made in MS Windows to boot system performance using USB stick-based memory.

All that does is improve fetching and caching; it does not substitute for real system memory.
