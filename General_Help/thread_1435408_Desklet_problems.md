### Post by skibum3027 on 2010-05-10
After trying all the different  desklets for weather, I do like Cairo-dock's best. 

The good:
1. It easily swaps between fahrenheit and celsius
2. It has decent themes
3. Accepts zip codes for locations, very easy to configure
4. Nice five day forcast with customizable quick data 

The bad:
1. An annoying question mark frequently pops up for the days high and  low. I'm not certain what causes this yet, but it tends to happen near  peak heat and cool points in the day. Does this happen when the current  temp is outside the predicted range? I don't know.
2. When your icons aren't locked the days on the 5-day forecast can be  re-arranged. This is very annoying the first time you accidentally do  it, and I see no reason this would ever benefit anyone.
3. System Requirements. IMO, Cairo-dock isn't the same without OpenGL.  It's also not terribly practical if all you want is weather and will be using AWN anyway.

Back to your original question though, your choices for desklets.
Some of these have different focal points, but your main choices are  Google Gadgets (personal favorite because of gmail), Screenlets,  gDesklets, Conky (mainly system monitoring) and SuperKaramba. There's also  all sorts of widgets built into KDE that are either included with  Kubuntu or easily added.
