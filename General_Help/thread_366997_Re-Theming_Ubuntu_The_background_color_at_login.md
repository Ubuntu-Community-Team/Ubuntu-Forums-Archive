I am new to ubuntu (but not to linux) and I absolutely love it. the one thing that bugs me is its unfortunate color scheme. I never liked brown or beige, so I am seeking to skin it to something else.

I have done most of the skinning, but I'm running into a problem: 
Right when I log in, the screen switches from the GDM screen to a beige screen with my new gnome splash screen. After it loads, it switches to my desktop's background color (black), and finally it displays the background image.
Q. How do I change the color behind the splash screen when it's loading?
I am using Ubuntu 6.10 (Edgy Eft)

### Post by haricharan on 2007-02-21
You can change it in the login window properties in the local tab the below the available login screens...It is in System -> Administration -> Login Window. Hope thats what u wanted... :)
