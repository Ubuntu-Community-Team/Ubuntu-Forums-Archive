### Post by Exsecrabilus on 2008-06-19
Well I was kinda joking, but if you do want to do a fresh install, do the following: (I'm assuming it's been only a few days since your Ubuntu install.)

Turn on your computer, and right before it shuts down (due to the problem,) open the CD slot.
Put Live CD in it, and it should just boot into the CD.
On the menu, press *Enter* on **Try Ubuntu without any changes to your computer**.

When the desktop finishes loading, open up Nautilus (*Places -> Home Folder*.)
On the sidebar, click on *xx.x GB Hard Drive* (which contains your files.)

In there, open up the folder called *home* and in there, open up the folder with your username.
Click it, and copy and paste (to a USB stick) any personal documents, images, music, or anything else you need.

There is nothing else you need to do, since it's only been a few days since your first installation.
Now, after copying all your important files, click **Install** on the desktop and install.
(Sure, you might lose some packages you installed, or some preferences you changed, but it's your choice.)
