### Post by oldfred on 2013-01-26
You actually should see it. Do you have a label on it as then it will automount under the label. Otherwise it may mount under 120GB or whatever size it is. But if hibernated it may not mount.

Generally best to create another shared NTFS data partition and have anything you may want from either system in the shared data partition. But even then do not leave anything open in Windows 8 when booting Ubuntu.

### Post by oldfred on 2013-01-31
Even with hibernation off, the Linux NTFS partition exposes all the normally hidden files & folders in Windows. That can lead to accidents. Generally better to create a shared NTFS data partition and use that for any data you may want in both systems. And then set the Windows system partition as read-only in fstab.

I have Firefox & Thunderbird profiles plus all photos still in my shared NTFS partition even though now I am not booting XP. That gave me the same email, bookmarks & photos in all my systems. I did not always have exactly the same version of Firefox in all my systems and only on startup a minor issue of some add-ins not working was the only difference.
