### Post by TBABill on 2011-04-18
> **PCaddicted said:**
> I have noticed that,in Ubuntu 10.10 Maverick Meerkat,the windows have their 3 buttons[maximize(a square),close(x) and minimize(-)] at the left(like in MacOS),while in earlier Ubuntu versions(for example,8.04 Hardy Heron),these buttons are at the right(like in the Windows operating system).Why did the developers of Ubuntu do this change?
In Unity (11.04) you will find that when you maximize a window, your buttons are actually integrated into the top panel. If they were on the right side they would fall on top of the time, speaker icon, network manager, etc. Having them on the left allows for functionality of all those items without conflicting with each other. It's a bit odd at first, and looks very out of place in standard Gnome, but within Unity it begins to make a little more visual sense when a window is maximized.
