### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-07-30
That's good to hear!!

But ... best to keep an eye on it, anyway.  Typical problem with dropped laptops is that you get a hairline fracture in the motherboard -- which often does not present any problems, that is, when the laptop is cold.  But when it gets hot, and the motherboard expands slightly due to the heat, the fracture opens, one or more of the traces disconnects -- and the laptop suddenly crashes!

So, if you do start to see this behaviour, it most likely a damaged motherboard -- which, for laptops, are really hard to find, and harder to replace.
