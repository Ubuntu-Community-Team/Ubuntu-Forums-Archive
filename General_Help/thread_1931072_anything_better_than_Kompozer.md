### Post by Gemnoc on 2012-02-25
Kompozer's development stopped 2 years ago. I found it very frustrating to work with, its only redeeming quality being its FTP site manager.

I suggest you have a look at BlueGriffon. It's a WYSIWYG editor, HTML5/CSS3 compliant, and the project is very active. Unfortunately they don't offer a .deb package, but rather an installer script and a .tar.gz archive. The last one has my preference, as you only need to extract it and launch the executable inside.

[http://bluegriffon.org/](http://bluegriffon.org/)

@ Adam Jensen
Drupal is NOT a web design program, but a content management system (CMS) that you install on your webserver. It's completely different. As a current Drupal 6 user migrating his blog to WordPress, I will say that Drupal is relatively easy to use, *once it's been set up*. That setup, and the ongoing maintenance is just a **major** PITA. Hence the move to WordPress.
