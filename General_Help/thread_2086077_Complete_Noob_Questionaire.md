There are some decent games available that run natively on Linux, others may run through wine but this may be buggy or may not work at all, so no guarantees for your favorites. ### Post by drmrgd on 2012-11-20
I too will throw my hat into the dual booting option.  My build is not too dissimilar from yours (more RAM and the X79 chipset on mine), and I've found it to work quite well with Kubuntu.   Performance processing extremely large datasets is quite impressive in my opinion.

I messed with trying to game on the same system for a little while and always ended up being frustrated in the end.  Before Steam started their port to Linux, I installed it using Wine and got mediocre results; some games worked OK, some just didn't work at all, and I found Steam itself to be buggy as anything.  So, for $100 I bought a copy of Windows 7 and dual booted basically just for gaming purposes.  With an SSD, the boot time is very fast and I don't have a single compatibility issue and can enjoy super high performance from modern games.

In the coming months, Steam for Linux Beta will be over and they'll have a stable port.  At that time maybe some of the games I have will run better under native Kubuntu.  However, it'll be up to the rest of the game publishers to port their games over.  So, I think we're going to be stuck dual booting for some time to come.  Who knows, though.  Things could turn around and we'll be able to do everything natively under Linux.  For now, though, I think dual boot is the least frustrating and best solution to the problem.
