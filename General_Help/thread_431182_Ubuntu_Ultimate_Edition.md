### Post by m.musashi on 2007-05-02
I'm not sure I can fully answer all your questions but I'll try to give a bit of insight. The ultimate edition as well as a number of other derivatives are just that - distros built on Ubuntu in some way but also with additions (and subtractions too I suppose) to meet their target. Only Ubuntu from Canonical is the official release but other like Mepis have switched to Ubuntu repos.

If you install a different distro (whether based on Ubuntu or not) and you put it in the same partition you will lose whatever was there. You can certainly have multiple versions on different partitions if you want. Many people dual, triple, quad boot.

I'm not sure others would agree, but if you are newer to Linux you might want to stick with one well supported distro until you feel comfortable playing around. If you can, play on a computer that is not your main one or at least do religious back ups of your stuff.

As for the specifics of UUE I don't really know but I think it's based on Edgy. A Feisty version may be in the works.
