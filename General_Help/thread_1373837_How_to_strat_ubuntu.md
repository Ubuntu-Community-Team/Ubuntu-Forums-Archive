### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-01-06
Whenever you start your computer, a screen will appear with your computer manufacturer's logo, or motherboard manufacturer's logo, or just a text screen that says some technical information about your computer.

On this screen, it will say something like "Press DEL to enter Setup" or "<F2> Boot Sequence" or something like that. Press whatever key it says to push (you need to push it quickly before the computer's startup proceeds).

This will bring you to a "Boot Sequence" screen where you can select the CD-ROM drive to be the first device to check. Or, it will bring you to a screen where you can change all sorts of settings to do with your computer, and one of those settings will be for the boot sequence. Change the sequence so that the CD-ROM drive is higher up in the sequence than the hard disk.

Insert the Ubuntu disc and "Save changes" to this settings screen (if applicable). The computer should start reading the Ubuntu CD and give you a menu where you can choose what language you want to use, and whether you want to try Ubuntu or install it.

The rest is fairly self-explanatory. Just make sure that if you want to install Ubuntu, you back up all your Windows data first, just in case. And if you want to stop running Ubuntu from the CD, just shut down from Ubuntu and it will ask you to remove the disc from the drive. When you next reboot you will be in your regular operating system.
