### Post by jimmy the saint on 2008-05-17
I installed Liferea because it is the recommended "offline reader" and news aggregator for ubuntu according to many forum posts and articles.  

I cannot find a way to get it to download and display entire articles.  For most feeds, it simply downloads a few lines and gives you a link to "read more."  Fine, if that is what the feed provides that's great.  Just don't go calling a product an OFFLINE reader if you can't read the articles offline.  When I am offline, that means I don't have a connection to the internet.  If I don't have a connection to the internet, I cannot download the article.  If I cannot download the article, I certainly cannot read it.  Hence Liferea should be called an "Offline see the headlines to articles you can't read till you go online viewer."

Unless I am missing an option somewhere in which case Im a complete idiot.

JTS

### Post by bornagainpenguin on 2009-09-08
No you're not missing anything--the application's documentation lies as do its fans and promoters.

The only Linux feed reader I am aware of that really does offline feeds is Straw and that application has ceased active development and will be removed from the repositories because it no longer functions after Intrepid Ibex.  It still works well in Hardy Heron and Intrepid though, it's when you upgrade to Jaunty and beyond that it stops working.

Supposedly there is a way to set a filter script in Liferea to force it to become an offline reader, but I've had no luck with it.  See here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1251672](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1251672)

If you manage to make it to work or discover a feed reader that actually works offline in Linux, please feel free to post about it!

--bornagainpenguin
