### Post by Donkinator on 2008-08-05
Do not bother installing Ubuntu on the HP Touchsmart unless you want absolutely limited functionality. And I mean no touchscreen ability, no wireless LAN, no lightscribe CD burning, no TV tuner etc. Basically all the cool things the machine is capable of. I have one, and installed Ubuntu on it. So I know what I am talking about. The mish mash of cheap components hp used has little to no possible driver support outside of a windows environment, and wine won't help you there. I actually contacted the company in New Zealand that makes the touchscreen, and asked for help fixing it to work with Linux. They were friendly enough, and sympathetic to the cause and they had their tech guy mail me. He asked for a readout of my (xorg.conf) which I quickly emailed him. Alas he was in Asia working and too busy to help me out with a simple script to send to the touchscreen to recalibrate it.

In short don't waste your time. Install Ubuntu on a more worthy machine :D
