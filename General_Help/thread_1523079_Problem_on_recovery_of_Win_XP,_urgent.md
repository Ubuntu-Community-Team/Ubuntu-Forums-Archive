Most IBM / Lenovo computers I've worked on have a recovery partition, which you can access by hitting something like 'F10' during boot. This will allow you to reinstall XP without CDs (assuming you didn't overwrite that partition when you installed ubuntu). If you have, then your best bet may be to borrow / use a normal Windows XP CD to reinstall the OS and then manually install whatever drivers you need from IBM's website. Your license key will work as long as it is the same version of XP (i.e. professional).
