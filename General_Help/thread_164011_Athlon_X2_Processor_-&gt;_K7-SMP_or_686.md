### Post by RAOF on 2006-04-22
Either.  Both those kernels will support the two cores of your processor, and the performance difference between the K7 & 686 will not be large.  But why not try both (and see if you can tell the difference ;)) - it won't hurt to install both, and you can choose at boot time which to load.

Everything else will work the same, regardless of which kernel you choose.
