### Post by cpmman on 2011-09-25
I keep several distros on my laptop but always have a current version for working on and the previous version which is a copy of my working version before upgrading. My routine is to upgrade the previous version through the current version and an update to the new version in the knowledge and experience of what the first upgrade required to make it "mine".

I last did this yesterday from my old 10.10 to 11.10 via 11.04 and with a few minor changes to such as UK English format date (which upgrading does not retain) and my preferred keyboard assignments I now have a working version which just needs checking for my different printers to become the "live" working version. The time taken to perform these upgrades and updates is negligible in relation to the stability experienced when completed.

This method retains most of your set preferences and your data (the data can be symlinked).

Upgrading to 11.04 took a little extra time as the Unity and panel problems following updates had to be resolved but simply by dropping a CCSM launcher onto my desktop allowed me to recover from all the initial glitches even when I had no panels.

Good luck with your upgrade however you choose to do it.
