### Post by Cpierce on 2010-07-24
I don't know if this post is in the correct area, but I hope so. I have an ATT aircard, which is the PCI type, not a usb. I have had the same issues with both 9.10 and now with 10.04. I plug the card in, and the laptop sees it. But when I click to connect, it never does. The lights on the aircard are lit to show it sees the 3g network, but they stay orange which means it is not connected. Once connected, the lights turn blue. I have left the aircard in and rebooted, but it still doesn't work. I thought it might be a firewall issue, so I have enabled/disabled but it doesn't seem to make a difference. It is just something little that I am overlooking. 

Thank you in advance because I really need to get this working.

### Post by Cpierce on 2010-07-24
I got it working. When Ubuntu makes the connection profile, it put in a username and password. I called AT&T and they said to leave those blank. I deleted the username and password and now it  works like a charm.
