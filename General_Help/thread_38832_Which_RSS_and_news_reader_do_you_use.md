### Post by wulf on 2005-06-02
I used LifeRea for a couple of months, and sill have it installed at work to monitor some feeds generated on our Intranet. However, for Internet-based feeds, I've taken to using [Bloglines](http://www.bloglines.com/). It's browser based and quite comprehensive. That means no firing up the browser to follow links (as it's already open).

I'm also benefitting from being able to go to one central aggregating server rather than having to send requests to all 40+ blogs I regularly follow. Not only does that consume less bandwidth but it also means I can check my blogroll at the start of the morning, mark off the ones I've read and then catch up with unread and new entries later on when I take a break at work - relying on a separate, local application would mean I'd keep on seeing stuff I'd read flagged up as new when I went to a new location.

Wulf
