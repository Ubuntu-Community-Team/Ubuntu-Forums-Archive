### Post by earthpigg on 2011-04-11
sounds like he is closing the lid, and the power management is set to 'suspend' when lid is closed.

the power management tool lets you pick what happens when you close the laptop lid.

you want it to 'hibernate' or 'shut down' when lid is shut on battery power.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-04-11
There might be a bug in that particular version of Ubuntu (or rather, one particular driver in it) that causes one of the components of your computer to stay on and use power, even when the rest of the computer is off.

Ubuntu 11.04 comes out later this month; if you're not using it already, then you should try upgrading when the Release Candidate or the full release happens.

Until then (or if the problem occurs in 11.04) you might need to remove the battery after shutting down. You can put it straight back in again.
