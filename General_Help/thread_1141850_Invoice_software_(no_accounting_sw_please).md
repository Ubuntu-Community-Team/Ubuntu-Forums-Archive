### Post by Oceanwatcher on 2009-04-28
I have been searching all over for a straightforward invoicing software for Linux/Kubuntu. But it seems like a dead end.

Maybe it is because I am not a native English speaker and do not know all the nice things to search for...

Before I tell you what I am looking for, let me tell you what I am NOT looking for: Any kind of accounting programs. GnuCash or whatever they are called, sales support systems etc. are not going to help in this case. Neither will online apps. I do not trust my livelihood to the internet.

Here is what is should be (I already have a couple of Windows programs that do this job very well, but I need it for Linux...):

- A small database of products that I can add to whenever I need. No need for inventory control. It is just nice to be able to re-use items in future invoices.

- A customer database - I am more than happy if it can tie in with the addressbook in KDE. That would be plain awsome!

- Invoices in a database with the possibility to add info when an invoice was paid so I can send out reminders if necessary.

- Possibility to create templates for invoices so they can be tailored to the standard of the recipients country.

I guess most of this should be possible to do using OpenOffice Base, but I have not been able to find something that was ready made. And as I am not a programmer myself, I hesitate a bit on starting a project like that. It is easier to boot a Windows PC to do it. But a lot of people around me ask about this when I suggest Linux. The biggest problem I have is to explain that there is a lack of some business applications there.

Do you know any good applications?
