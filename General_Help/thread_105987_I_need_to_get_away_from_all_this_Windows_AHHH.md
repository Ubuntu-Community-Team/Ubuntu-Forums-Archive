### Post by shakin on 2005-12-20
Ethnocentrism is the practive of judging the values of another culture based on one's own beliefs. All those posters who are asking vanillalisterine to move out are wrong. Opposing her culture is something that vanillalisterine will need to do if she ever feels it is necessary.

So...

The only way I can see to fix the network problem is by reconfiguring the network so your sister's computer doesn't affect your own. Right now I assume it works like this:

```
router/modem - > sister's computer - > vanillalisterine's computer
```

But it needs to work like this:

```
router/modem - > vanillalisterine's computer - > sister's computer
```

Or like this:

```
router/modem - > sister's computer
router/modem - > vanillalisterine's computer
```

It's a matter of physical connections. If your computer's internet connection passes through someone else's computer, then that other computer will need to be on for your internet to work.

Your sister must have internet connection sharing activated. If you want to put your computer in front of hers (effectively switching places on the network with her) then you'll need to setup internet connection sharing on your computer. Please make another post if you want to learn how to do this.

Another option is to keep the computers separate on the network so no one computer relies on any other computer to be powered on. To do this you need a switch, which you probably already have if you have a router, but you probably don't have if you just have a modem. The router physically sites between your computers and the modem and works much like a splitter for a phone or cable TV does. A router sells for about $50 - $100 and would not require much, if any, configuration changes on any of the connected computers, except possibly that your sister will need to have her networking setup for DHCP rather than PPTP or whatever she must use now to connect to your ISP.

Now that I've said all that, I can think of another option. You can buy a wireless network card and install that in your computer. Post a new thread asking for which models are best with Ubuntu and I'm sure you'll get great advice. The card may cost up to $100, but probably more like $50. There is no guarantee, but if one of your neighbors has an unsecured wireless router you'll be able to hop onto their network and use their internet. You can use Ubuntu's network profile switcher to switch between your regular internet when it's working and your neighbor's wireless internet when your father shuts down your sister's computer.
