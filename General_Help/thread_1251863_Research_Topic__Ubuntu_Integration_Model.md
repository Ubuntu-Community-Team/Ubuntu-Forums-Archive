Hello Friends,

My name is Pratish, currently I am working at Univesity of Lüneburg in Germany on a Research Project.

My research focus is to define a Open Source Software Development Platform based on Service-Oriented Architecture. The fundamental idea is to form an IT Backbone based on SOA for any organization willing to adopt and develop Open Source Softwares. 

The processes include defining components and integrating them over Enterprise Service Bus. The fundamental components after thorough analysis of OSSD processes are,

Lower Half of ESB, usual legacy , distributed and also Internet-based systems

- Source-Code Management System
- Package Repository 
- Bug Tracking Systems
- Forums  & Mailing Lists

Upper Half of ESB, systems which interact directly with users or other OSS organizations.

- User Portals , generically four 
  * Customer - Entitled to paid support and services. also, some 
    non-open source software components
  * Basic User - About 95% users who use the OSS, take part in forums, 
    occasional Bug Tracking 
  * Advance User - Help in Bug Tracking , Debugging, Package 
    Management and Forum Management
  * Core Team - about 5-20 people depending on size & scope of project.
    Individuals contribute to all about mentioned tasks, but are most 
    often are paid and draw economic advantage from their contributions
    as well.

- Up-Streams : Like Ubuntu->Debian upstream or Ubuntu->Linux upstreams
  
- User Package Management : Automatic in Ubuntu Case. 

- Collaborations : norms for using & distributing software (code & 
  packages, obviously) developed by other OSS organizations like 
  Gnome, KDE, Debian, GNU, TUG and so on.

I am interested in knowing opinions of Ubuntu Advance Users & Core Team also, who regularly contribute to its upkeep.

- Bug Tracking & Debugging
- Package Management
- Programmers
- Also, Forum Managers

If , your opinions are to the point and relevant, I would be obliged and happy to mention your help along with my research work.

Thank you , all.

Pratish.
