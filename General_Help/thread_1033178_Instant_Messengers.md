### Post by jgoguen on 2009-01-07
You may have luck with Mercury Messenger.  It isn't in the repositories, but you can get it from [http://mercury.im/](http://mercury.im/).  I used it for a while, and it's a great program, but I found I preferred something that could do multiple protocols in the same program (Pidgin).  It has experimental support for Yahoo contacts in the most recent version (you may need to sign up for the forum and download a beta version, but I've always found that the betas and even alphas for Mercury were quite stable).  You'll probably need to ask for support on their forums, which I found were pretty good.

As for getting the Yahoo client itself, I don't think it'll happen.  I fought with it myself trying to help someone over IRC a while back, and neither of us could get far enough to log in.  Most of our fighting was with the Windows version under Wine, but IIRC there's also a Linux version that we just couldn't get installed because of dependencies that couldn't be properly resolved without fully rebuilding the package and removing the requirement for the dependency...which would probably make it blow up anyway :).
