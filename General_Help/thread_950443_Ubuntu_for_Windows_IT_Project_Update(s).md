### Post by Black Razor on 2008-10-17
As likely very few of you are aware, I have recently started a project to help ease the conversion from being a Windows expert user to using Ubuntu Linux.  I myself recently made this transition, but have found after being a Windows power user for so long, its much more difficult to re-learn how you think about your computer.

So, thus came to me my idea for the Ubuntu for the Windows IT project.  It's aim and purpose is to document the things a Windows IT would need to successfully rival his/her knowledge in Ubuntu Linux.  Eventually, it will likely cover even study guides for the Ubuntu LPI test.

So far I have accomplished the following:

#1. Created project wiki on Wikidot.  As of now its a private wiki available only to team members until we have a working beta version.

#2. I am having my attorney review the GNU FDL and Creative Commons licenses to which license is in the best interest of this project.  I am more likely going to use the Creative Commons Attribution-Noncommercial 3.0 United States license, I just dont like the terms of the FDL license, honestly. 

#3. I am starting to learn the DocBook format so that I can put things together in a platform neutral format.  Hoping eventually to build man pages which would be included into official documentation for Ubuntu.

#4. Registered project and team on Launchpad.  Not sure what to do with it quite yet, but I know its standard procedure. Awaiting approval from Launchpad since they seem to dislike anything thats not completely free.

#5. Created Wordpress blog for the team to communicate with each other.

#6.  Created G-Mail account for the team and project to streamline communications process.  E-mail may be sent to [email]ubuntu4win@gmail.com[/email]

#7.  Recruiting for team members...so far its just me, lol.

#7. Starting to outline the actual scope of the project.  


In regards to the outlining process, I have the following ideas so far:

(a) The official declared purpose of the project is to make it easy and fun for Windows experts to transition to using Ubuntu easily.  Please note that this is different from trying to convert every day users into Ubuntu users, and while that is a needed endeavor, I believe IT people are actually MORE hesitant to switch because they feel powerful in Windows and crippled in Linux...at least at first.  Also, the way I am looking to license the project, someone is more than welcome to take the work produced by the team and reshape it into a guide for everyday users, as long as its compliant with the projects license.

(b) The big picture dream of the project will include a great Wiki with flash video examples on nearly everything you can imagine.   This Wiki is also forum enabled to allow users of the project to talk to each other.

(c) Topics To Be Covered (so far):
-OS Installation
-Partitions and Mountpoints
-Desktop Environments
-Package Managers, Software Installation and Compiling
-Hardwired Networking
-Wireless Networking 
-Printing

...thats about it for now.  Not sure what else to  include in the projects scope.  I'm still strictly in the planning stages, and have a LOT of planning left to do.  I'm hoping to be in beta production by Spring, we'll see how it goes.
