### Post by Polygon on 2007-02-03
Well, with wine you have to be open to the fact that there WILL be bugs... just like this one.

And for sound, that depends on what program you are trying to run through wine. Most of these problems can be found by searching through the winehq.com -> appDB -> search for whatever program you are using, and then searching through the maintainers comments and the fourm posts. A lot of the time, its as simple as changing the sound driver in winecfg

but what version of nero are you trying to run? i can tell in your screenshot

and also, why are you trying to run nero through wine? is there something that nero can do that linux programs like k3b or gnomebaker can't do?
