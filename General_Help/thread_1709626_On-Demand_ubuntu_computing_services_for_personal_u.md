### Post by wormyblackburny on 2011-03-18
Well, you have to look at it from an admin's point of view. First, there is the issue that most users will be doing completely different things on the cloud, therefore you would have to have the right programs installed on the virtual machine. The other issue is how to charge: should you pay the same to use a tiny bit of processor/ram on your virtual machine vs someone whose work uses a TON of resources? This is why they mainly focus on business users. They are using their datacenter to host, asking what you need, and building it for you. Then I imagine they are selling you a specific chunk of resources on their cluster. I bet it will never be something that will ever be cheap enough to offer to basic end users, but it is an interesting idea. i'm just not sure there is enough of a market for it to be profitable vs the cost of the infrastructure and the maintenance of the vms.
