### Post by TheFu on 2016-10-08
Don't know of any curated stuff, but normal RSS feeds can do this. You just need to setup which ones you like.

I've been using a firefox addon - **Sage** - for this, but recently switched to using a **NextCloud** addon "News" to centralize my news reading across multiple platforms - then use **Wallabag** as an off-line reader on a tablet I travel with  or when no connectivity is available.  For me, what I'm reading is a matter of privacy, so using a centralized service is something I want to avoid.  It is bad enough that the BBC knows I'll using their RSS feeds.

Wallabag has changed altered by ingestion of tech news completely. Every article gets an approximate time for reading added, so when I'm waiting for someone or something to finish, I can quickly find an article to read of the proper length to fill the expected time.

Of course, NextCloud and Wallabag aren't perfect. Both give me major security concerns since they are using php. Plus setting up a web-app might not be what you seek.
