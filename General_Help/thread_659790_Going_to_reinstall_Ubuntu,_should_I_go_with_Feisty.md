### Post by rubicon on 2008-01-06
Well, Gutsy have improved support for WiFi and obviously overall newer.

Frankly say, the best way to discover a better version is to try LiveCD. If your hardware works fine from LiveCD, then it should work fine after installation. Other differences include compiz (which is enabled in Gutsy by default), ntfs-3g (which also works in Gutsy out-of-the-box), newer kernel (which -- again -- may support your hardware better) etc.

Feisty have lower system requirements (256MiB vs 384MiB for Gutsy), thus may go smoother.

If you want maximum stability, you may look to Dapper Drake, but if you want to have up-to-date Ubuntu, then install Gutsy. 7.10 still have some bugs, but they are not critical for everyday use.
