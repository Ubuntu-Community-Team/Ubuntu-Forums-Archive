### Post by davidvandoren on 2010-11-05
I am not sure what your requirements are or better the standard you need to achieve.

However from the description I was not clear if the device does the digital recording and you upload it via usb or if the pc has to do the recording and is fed with a live high quality audio signal.

If the device records it and you only need to upload it will usually be better.

Here is what you should look for 

  
**[SIZE=2]Alesis USB Recording Kit[/SIZE]**

24-bit/48kHz analog-to-digital conversion




**[SIZE=2]Edirol UA-25 EX[/SIZE]**

24bit/96kHz performance with pro-grade mic preamps

24 bit is better than 16 and 96khz is better than 48khz but not necessarily mean much.


If you can upload the file from the device 48khz is most likely better than recording it on your laptop from 96khz audio

**[SIZE=2]Olympus LS-11, portable Stereo Recorder[/SIZE]**

for example has 24 Bit/96 kHz, 2 build-in Mic's 
doesn't say anything about external mics though


For recording on your Pc you can use Audacity it's in the repos.
