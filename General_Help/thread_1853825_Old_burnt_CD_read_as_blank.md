### Post by HereInOz on 2011-10-03
All I can offer is to try it in another machine, but you have probably done that already, provided you have one available.  If two machines don't read it, then it may be end of life.

Check for scratches, particularly circumferential scratches (ones which track around the CD, rather than ones that track radially across it.  If the CD has a bad scratch, you can sometimes polish it out with a fine plastic polish.  In Australia we have a silver polish called Silvo - it is a fine abrasive - and this is very good for polishing out CD scratches.  You may have something similar.

Apart from that, I am out of ideas.
