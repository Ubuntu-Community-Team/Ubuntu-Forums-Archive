### Post by arjayes on 2008-03-03
Well i don't see the difference .  Rythymbox can do the things you described pretty well too 
It can download the album covers automatically and also gets the lyrics ( Right click on the song . In the pop-up menu Go to properties-> lyrics tab ) .True it doesn't have artist information feature ( but if you're trying to get artist information i wouldn't mind opening my browser an going to the wiki page)  My only complaint is that the sorting/ searching in Rythtmbox is not as fast or smooth as say amarok . The tag management feature is not that good either ( again compared to amarok ).It has no problem playing streaming media , last.fm or any internet radio .
I don't see the point of installing exaile . Apart from the fact that it looks a little better I don't see any reason to switch from Ubuntu's default music manager/player . 
My favourite music player is still amarok . unfortunately i couldn't get it to connect to the internet . Rythymbox is a good second choice . 
Another huge drawback for Exaile is that it can't be easily configured for proxies yet . Configuration for proxies currently involves a lot of tweaking ([https://bugs.launchpad.net/exaile/+bug/135946]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/exaile/+bug/135946"))
Rythymbox worked out of the box . I think Exaile is a more advanced media player and probably not suited for the default player on ubuntu . Rythymbox is more appropriate
