### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-09
I'm afraid there's not a simple answer to your question.

It depends on your video tuner card, and what it can pick up.  If you have a digital video tuner, you can play any clear QAM channels your cable provider carries, or if you are connected to an antenna, the digital broadcast channels.  By law, U.S. cable companies are required to provide the broadcast networks (e.g. ABC, CBS, NBC, Fox, etc.) and public television.

Alternatively, if you have a cable box or DVR, you can use MythTV to view and record any channel you can receive.  The only problem with this is having MythTV control the cable box.  Some cable boxes have a Firewire port that will allow an external device (such as a computer with a Firewire port running MythTV) to control them and transfer the media.  However, some cable companies do not have these Firewire ports active on their cable boxes.  Some of these cable companies will provide cable boxes with active Firewire ports on request, while others won't.  And while you can purchase a third party DVR, some cable companies will refuse to activate a third party box.  On the bright side, if you're lucky enough to have a cable box or DVR with an active Firewire port, you probably don't even need a video tuner card, since the Firewire can transmit the media to your MythTV box.

If the cable box or DVR doesn't have an active Firewire port, you can still have MythTV control it with an IR transceiver in the same way you'd use a remote control.  This requires you to add an IR transceiver to your MythTV box, though you can get Linux-compatible USB IR transceivers.

### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-09
I'm afraid I'm only familiar with the issues of getting channels on MythTV in the U.S., from research I did a while back when I was thinking of setting up a MythTV box for myself.  I do seem to remember reading that the laws/technology/etc. used in some other countries made setting up a MythTV box easier -- for example, I believe I read that SkyOne boxes in the UK were fairly easy to connect to a MythTV box -- but I don't know any specifics about Australia or Asia.

Still you're only going to be able to pick up channels that are provided by your cable/satellite provider, or broadcast channels off of an antenna.  MythTV isn't going to let you pick up an Asian channel in Australia if you can't get it otherwise.  MythTV is only DVR software.  It will let you watch and record whatever you feed into it, but it doesn't provide access to media you don't normally have.
