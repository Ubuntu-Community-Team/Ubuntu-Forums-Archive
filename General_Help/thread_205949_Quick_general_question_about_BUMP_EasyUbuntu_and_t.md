### Post by 23meg on 2006-06-29
The libs required for mp3 playback and the Flash player are in the official repositories, so you don't really have to resort to external repositories or software, which also means they're known to be legal, since Ubuntu has no problems distributing them in the repos.

For what I know, libdvdcss and w32codecs are the two packages that aren't fit for redistribution in the US, and maybe elsewhere.

### Post by dolphinsonar on 2006-10-08
The whole practice of reserving one technology for exclusive use, then charging people a fee to have the privelege of using it is why many people end up switching to Linux.  The Internet is a place where people can share knowledge, and restricting knowledge to make a profit is frowned upon in the Free Software and Open Source communities.  That is why people who make alternatives to proprietary software are viewed as robin hood like heros.  Except they don't steal from the rich, they just make a better widget than them.
