### Post by tgalati4 on 2011-07-03
[http://sugarcrm.com](http://sugarcrm.com) has elements of time tracking.  You can create extensive logs of employee activity, such as when they logged in and out, what records were accessed and when, etc.  There are also modules that you can plug into sugarcrm such as sugartime:

[http://www.sugarforge.org/projects/sugartime/](http://www.sugarforge.org/projects/sugartime/)

Time-tracking is tricky because it depends on business processes and they are so varied that you often need a custom solution anyway.  Sugarcrm has that flexibility, but you will have to do some programming to get it set up so that everyone uses it as a matter of course and not constantly grumbling about how crappy this time-tracking system is.

There's a huge difference between line workers punching in at the beginning of a shift and attorneys billing 15-minute increments spread across several clients.  Sugarcrm can handle both, but it requires tweakage.
