### Post by davidmohammed on 2010-06-30
do a 

ps -ef and look for your process thats running

dad       2486     1  0 21:06 ?        00:00:00 /bin/sh /usr/lib/firefox-3.6.6/f
dad       2491  2486  0 21:06 ?        00:00:00 /bin/sh /usr/lib/firefox-3.6.6/r

look at the third column - your process should be equal to 1 - 

e.g. above - firefox launches two processes.  If I force kill 2486 this will kill the child process 2491.

If your third column is not 1 then look up that process ID in your second column.  Repeat until you find the associated "1" process in the third column....
