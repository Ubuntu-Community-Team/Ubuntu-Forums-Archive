### Post by ireneshusband on 2009-04-30
I have just moved to KDE in Jaunty after being a Gnome user (which means that my system probably is different from a proper Kubuntu one). One of the problems I am having is that neither the sleep button nor closing the lid of my machine cause it to sleep. However sleep is still working; I can initiate it from the K menu. Needless to say, both the sleep button and closing the lid worked in Gnome. Does anyone know how to fix this?

Thanks in advance :)

### Post by SuperSonic4 on 2009-04-30
what are the settings under 
KMenu -> System Settings -> (Advanced Tab) -> Power Management

for the appropriate profile you're using

---

### Post by ireneshusband on 2009-04-30
I went to the power management settings and changed in all profiles the value for closing the lid to "suspend to ram", and that now works fine, i.e. closing the lid induces sleep. However it still doesn't work for the sleep key (Fn-F4 on my thinkpad R61i), even though the setting for all profiles is likewise "suspend to ram".
