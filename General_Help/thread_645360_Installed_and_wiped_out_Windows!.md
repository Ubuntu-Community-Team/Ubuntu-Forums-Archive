### Post by joeyteetops on 2007-12-21
Photos are typically highly recoverable as they are small and typically non-fragmented.  As long as you don't do anything stupid.  Whatever you do I'd highly recommend not booting that machine up as a boot drive.  Just starting up an operating system will be writing log files, temporary operating system files, etc.  While writing files the operating system believes it's writing these files to free space.  Now a lot of that free space is probably a bunch of your old photos so that would be a bad thing.  

If you are capable, I'd recommend making an image copy of the drive to another disk to ensure you don't butcher the original.   Run data recovery software of your choosing from another drive or CD, and for goodness sakes don't install it on the machine you are trying to recover and don't restore any recovered data to the drive you are recovering.  If you aren't up to the challenge or are too paranoid give the guys at gillware a call and they'll help you out but they'll charge you probably 350 bucks for their data recovery expertise. 

Best of luck.
