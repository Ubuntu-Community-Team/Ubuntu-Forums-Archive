### Post by wwwolf on 2010-05-01
Bash scripts are easier to write and you get an immediate benefit i.e. not having to wait for a build.

However the initial investment of time for compiling/building a program will pay off in subsequent runs.

I cannot say that ALL programs are faster than their bash equivalent because programs are written in a multitude of languages. Some languages were designed for convenience being close to the human language while others were designed for speed being closer to machine language. Writing a program in binary code quickly and efficiently would be an ultimate goal!

I know this might not be the answer you were looking for but the question is relative to the specific benefactor of the executed program.

Hope that helped.
