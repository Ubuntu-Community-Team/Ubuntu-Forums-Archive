### Post by dwasifar on 2009-11-17
I installed Lotus Symphony to try it out.  I use Avant Window Navigator, and when I tried to drag the Symphony launcher from the Applications>Office menu to the AWN bar, it didn't work.  I wound up with an "Invalid Launcher" entry in the AWN launchers list.  (Actually I wound up with several.)

I tried adding the launcher to the desktop and adding it from there, and also to the panel and adding that, but neither worked consistently.

Eventually, through trial and error, I discovered that the only way to get a Symphony launcher to successfully work in AWN is to have TWO copies of the launcher in the location you want to add it from.  I put two copies of it on the desktop (by right clicking on the launcher in the Applications menu and choosing Add to Desktop, twice), and that made it work.  Deleting one copy from the desktop made the AWN launcher fail again.  However, re-adding a second copy fixed it again.

Finally, to work around the problem, I deleted the launcher from AWN, moved both desktop Symphony launchers into my home folder, and dragged one to AWN from there.

I have no idea why this should be.  I don't even know how to start to figure it out.
