### Post by Onesimus on 2007-06-01
Having been someone that has reading the forums regularly since installing Feisty.  I wondered whether the forums could be improved by having a web link on everyone's signature that would provide the specification of their computer.  Often I see posts that say 'this doesn't work for me...' but then provide no information as to what their system is.  This would allow people to assess whether a particular fix may or may not work for them, in addition allowing for developers to see just how wide spread the bugs [or unexplained functionality ;)] is.

The specification could be captured by a program and uploaded to each person's area.  I am aware that system administrators may have a number of computers to oversee and hence this facility may have to allow for multiple specifications.

What do people think ?

### Post by viciouslime on 2007-06-01
Although this does sound like a good idea at first, I have seen other forums try to implement similar things and people forget to keep their system profile up-to-date, others simply don't know how to fill it in in the first place, i.e. don't know what make/model their motherbaord is etc.

In the first case people end up wasting there time posting solutions for a system without a problem and in the latter case you seem to get people who refuse to help anyone who hasn't kept their system profile filled in and up-to-date.

It might perhaps be possible to make it a feature of ubuntu, like the hardware database thing, so ubuntu itself could collect all the info and post it to your profile. However, then ther is the issue of people posting on other people's behalf, or from other machines to the one that has the problem, or indeed bugs in the program doing the detecting and posting causing issues.

In short, I don't think this would really be as useful as it might first sound.
