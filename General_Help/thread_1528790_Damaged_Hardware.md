### Post by WorMzy on 2010-07-11
Damaged hardware will cause problems on any OS. If it's a damaged graphics chip/card, you may see artefacts appear on the screen, the screen may randomly freeze, change colour or go blank; if you've got damaged RAM, then AFAIK, the damaged sectors will be detected by the bios and won't be available to the OS, this may cause problems if you have a small amount of RAM to begin with, and may lead to large amounts of 'swapping' to the hard disk, slowing down applications and your PC in general.

Etc.

I don't know if there's a way to detect these faults beforehand, but if you're experiencing any problems, list them here and people can tell you if they're a product of software or hardware problems.

### Post by efflandt on 2010-07-11
Windows can become more bloated (especially Vista or Win7), and if you do not have enough memory will begin swapping memory to disk (virtual memory).  Your manufacturer or various programs may install things to keep themselves updated.  And anti-virus programs can slow you up checking all files (or without that a virus infection can slow you).

A 4 GB Win7 laptop I got someone was using 4 GB just booting up.  And a new 8 GB Win7 desktop I got is using 3.6 GB just booting before running anything.  In comparison Ubuntu on the new PC uses less than 500 GB after booting and can get by on less.  I had to rob some RAM out of an old laptop for another laptop, and it runs fine with 512 MB RAM and some of that shared for video.

So it could just be that Linux generally runs more efficiently without all the bloatware.  64-bit Ubuntu 10.04 certainly boots much quicker than WinXP on my old 2 GB desktop PC (which takes many minutes before finally settling down).

PS: If you dropped it while running, that may have damaged the part of the hard drive used by Windows and not the part when Linux is installed.  I replaced a drive in one laptop used by a 12 year old twice (original drive failed, then replacement drive replaced under warranty).  But when WinXP refused to boot a 3rd time, the drive tested fine, but was infected with a virus that I had to clean off.
