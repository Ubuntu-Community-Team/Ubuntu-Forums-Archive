### Post by mivo on 2007-10-20
Well, some people did say prior to the release that Gutsy may need a few extra weeks before it is really ready. Those problems do not affect everyone, so there are plenty of people to whom Gutsy is a real improvement with many new, attractive features.

I had tried the beta and the RC, and it gave me some trouble with my box (freeze-ups, and I also had the OpenOffice crashes that you mentioned), so I went back to Feisty and decided to stick with it for some time (it runs rock-stable and there are currently no new features that I must have). I have a second machine on the way to me, so when that one arrives I will put Gutsy on it and fiddle some with it.

I see Ubuntu as a work in progress that changes constantly, and the releases are just rough landmarks when one might consider upgrading if they so wish. But waiting a few weeks, even a couple of months, has absolutely no downsides if your current hardware is supported by the previous release. I do believe that Gutsy was released with too many unresolved issues, especially since it was so "hyped" everywhere, and that is a little unfortunate and harmful to its good reputation. But upgrading an OS, any OS, on the release day of a new version is also kind of risky. 

If you have a separate /home partition, downgrading should be fairly painless. Sorry this happened to you, though, it's definitely not a pleasant experience. :(
