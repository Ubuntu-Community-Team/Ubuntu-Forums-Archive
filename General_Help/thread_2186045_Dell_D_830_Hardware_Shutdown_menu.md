### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2013-11-05
I think that is pretty much normal behaviour. I believe the idea is to have a way to cause a graceful (meaning a warning is sent to all running aps that shutdown is imminent, so you are less likely to have filesystem corruption) shutdown even if, for example, your graphics card failed so you would be typing in the dark on blind faith or pointing and clicking on even blinder faith if you tried to do it the "normal" way. The action that the power button causes CAN be changed, at least to either of several pre-set options, and I suspect if you dig deeper you could find ways to change it to anything you wanted, but I suggest you give the matter serious thought before you do it. After all, if you are in a position to be able to respond to some sort of gui shutdown/reboot/logout/lock sort of dialogue with buttons on it (I gather that's what you were expecting the shutdown button to do) you can make it pop up with mouse or keyboard so you don't need the shutdown hardware button. But if your machine is hung and won't respond normally or your monitor just died after the cat peed on it you might be glad to have a hardware shutdown button that works normally.

Having said that, if you still want to change what it does, try looking in some preferences dialogues and you'll probably find it pretty easily. I've never used Cinnamon so I don't know where it keeps that but I doubt it will be hard to find. I think it similar to gnome 2, and it's been a while since I used that but, since this is only a "for instance" example anyway I'll say I think in that DE it was something like Administration - System - Preferences and after that I don't recall but you shouldn't have any trouble finding it.
