I have been wondering this for quite a while now but it just never bothered me enough to ask about it.

Ever since ubuntu decided to split Log out shutdown out of the regular gnome-panel/gnome-menu is is not easily accessible by keyboard to the best of my knowledge.

Using Ctrl+Alt+Del is a half alternative since it does not show all the options, it lacks "Logout", "Switch user" and "Guest session" (on systems that allow guest sessions).

Alacarte also does not have the option of re-adding "Log out" and "Shutdown" to the System menu like they exist on Debian (at least not fast and simple).

Why do I care?
Even though I have enough knowledge to exit my session in multiple different ways using keyboard only, I still like to navigate menus with keyboard only (and sometimes have to for lack of a mouse).
And though as I said I know other ways to do what I need to do I do feel that they lack the niceness of doing it "the way it's supposed to be done".

Also some of my ways are way beyond what I can expect from my users. (Although I doubt they even know Alt+F1, but still if they do they should be able to shutdown the computer from there...)
