### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2014-08-22
@gordon99:

One solution you COULD employ, although it's not really necessary, is to install a Linux antivirus program. Linux doesn't need antivirus, in large part due to the way it's structured (vis-a-vis the root permissions, and the fact that even the administrator in Linux runs as a restricted user by default).

However, most of the Linux AV programs out there (although they WILL work on home PCs) are primarily intended for server installations. To this end, they are fitted with email and spam filtering features, and from reports I have had, they work well in this respect.

Nevertheless, what you really need is something that will fulfill just this particular aspect of a Linux AV; trust me, the rest of it is REALLY not necessary. Off the top of my head, I don't know of anything that does this in Linux, but I will do some research, and perhaps can come up with some suggestions.

Regards,

Mike.

### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2014-08-22
After quick research, you would probably find that SpamAssassin, which is to be found in the Software Centre, will perform the function that you require. It is configured to work with Evolution in particular.

Just type SpamAssassin in the search box at the top right corner of the Software Centre.

Mike.
