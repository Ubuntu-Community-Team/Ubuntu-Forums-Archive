### Post by matt_symes on 2010-11-23
Hi

Welcome to the forums.

In the first instance you want to download the iso from the ubuntu download page. There are two new versions 10.04 LTS and 10.10. The choice is yours, but 10.04 is the long term support edition. Burn it to CD in windows.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download)

Next you want to ensure the CDRom is set as the first boot device in the BIOS. Pop the CDRom in the drive and restart the computer. It will load the CD. There are a number of options. One is to try Ubuntu. I would suggest you use this option first. It will not make any changes to your hard drive and you can have a play and get a feel for Ubuntu. It is also a good way to test for hardware issues.

The you want to look into dual booting Ubuntu with windows.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WindowsDualBoot](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WindowsDualBoot)

I suggest you have a play first with the LiveCD. There are loads of people here who can help with in the install.

Kind regards
