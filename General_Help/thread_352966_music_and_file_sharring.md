No one is discussing *illegal* music downloading here, Taurus. I think he was simply wondering if there was an Ubuntu-friendly way for him to access *legal* music and files, as you might at Limewire or Frostwire. Sure, there are a lot of people who abuse the system, but LimeWire *specifically* asks if you intend to use their software for copyright violation. If you say yes, they don't let you have the software. Everyone follows the rules.

I happen to have a few mp3's laying around - properly licensed (as they're my *own* recordings) - and I allow them to be shared using LimeWire and FrostWire.

**[COLOR="Green"][SIZE="2"]LimeWire and FrostWire[/SIZE][/COLOR]** 
are a great source for .mp3's. 

tip: Try to avoid downloading all of those *illegal* .mp3 files that are around there. Downloading all of those available *illegal* .mp3s wouldn't be the right thing to do.
