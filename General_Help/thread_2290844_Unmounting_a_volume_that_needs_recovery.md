### Post by outofnicks on 2015-08-15
I wanted to prepare a USB stick for a live boot/install, using Startup Disk Creator. The stick was mounted, it had another distro installed as a boot disk and my intention was to reformat it with SDC. Big mistake! I also had a 64 gig micro SD card mounted in a USB reader, with a lot of very valued images and videos which were not backed up. I selected the USB stick I wanted to use for a boot disk and clicked the "Erase Disk" button. Somehow, the SD card was erased instead, I've no clue how. About 75% of the files do show up in the file manager, but most of them return errors when I try to open them. The card still shows the same amount of used and remaining space as before which gives me much hope. 

Testdisk does not recognize the SD card. So next, I was going to try safecopy. I don't have enough space on the current PC the card is mounted on. I could transfer about 40 gig of content to another computer to make room. But it would be much easier to move the SD card to the other computer and use safecopy there. Or what ever app will be best to make a recovery attempt. Both boxes are running Kubuntu 14.04.

Will moving the SD card endanger recovery of the files at all? Anyone know why Testdisk doesn't recognize the card? Any help would be very much appreciated.
