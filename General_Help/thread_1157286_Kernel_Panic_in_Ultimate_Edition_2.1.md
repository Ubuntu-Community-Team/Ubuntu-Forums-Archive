### Post by WIGGMPk on 2009-05-13
Looks like init died during the boot process.

Did you recently update to a newer kernel?
If so, did you try and load the previous kernel?

Im not familiar with Ultimate (if its changed) but you should be able to hit escape and see a list of boot options available in the Grub menu.

Kernel panics can happen because of lot of different things. If you recently updated the kernel, it could just be hardware incompatibility. It could be your BIOS firmware is out of date and needs updating. It could be a bad stick of RAM.
