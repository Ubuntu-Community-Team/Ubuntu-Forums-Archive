### Post by caesarb on 2005-05-28
You're in luck. This is one of the few things I can help with ;-) 

First you have to have Windows Media Player installed on your machine in addition to iTunes. 

1)Open the WMP and click "Library" at the top of the screen.

2)Open iTunes. You have to highlight all the tracks within your playlist. 

3)Drag them into the "Now Playing" section of the WMP window.

4)You can now save the playlist by clicking "Now Playing List" right above your songs. 

There will be an option to "save playlist as" You can change it so it's saved as an "m3u" playlist file.

Now a few more changes have to be made before it's quite ready for Ubuntu. Once you booted back into Ubuntu, you can edit the playlist files using gedit. You will have to change the paths so they point wherever you have them stored. The easiest way to do this is to hit "Edit/Find & Replace" Here you should be able to change everything around in a few minutes. After you're done, just import is using   your cheosen media player and that's it. I have roughly 18GB of music, and it didn't take me long at all. Hope that helps.
