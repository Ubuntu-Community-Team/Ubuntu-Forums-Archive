### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-04-27
Please stop spreading FUD.

Xubuntu, like Kubuntu, is independently developed but officially recognised and supported by Ubuntu. It is up to the developers of Xubuntu to take responsibility for supporting Xubuntu with security fixes. They are not able to do this for 5 years. If more people were involved in the development of Xubuntu then may be things would be different.

For a while now Canonical has been paying one of its employees to develop Kubuntu. That is stopping from the release of Ubuntu 12.04. This is not the same as Ubuntu not supporting Kubuntu.

In fact, according to one of the Kubuntu developers, Canonical has spent "millions" on Kubuntu.

Ubuntu is open source. That means that anyone can take Ubuntu and remove bits and add bits and turn it into a different looking OS. And this is what has happened with Xubuntu, Kubuntu, Edubuntu, Mythubuntu, Ubuntu Studio and now Lubuntu.

Not to mention those other distributions that have deviated so far from standard Ubuntu that it would be wrong to use the term Ubuntu or buntu in the name. And so they are not allowed to do so.

Derivatives of Ubuntu do not lose their independence when they become part of the Ubuntu family.

Sponsorship of a distribution is not the same as buying the distribution. I do not think anyone could buy Kubuntu. Now, it remains to be seen what these new sponsors of Kubuntu want from Kubuntu for their money. In fact it is not easy to find out who they are and where they get their money from. But the responsibility for Kubuntu and it future rests as it always has done on the Kubuntu council.

Regards.
