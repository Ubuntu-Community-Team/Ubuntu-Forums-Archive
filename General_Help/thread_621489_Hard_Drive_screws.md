### Post by ronmarley1 on 2007-11-23
I'm guessing it's a torx head screw.  See: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_10664](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_10664)
You should be able to buy a cheap torx screwdriver with interchangeable size bits at Home Depot or any hardware store for about $10 US.

### Post by Chuck_W on 2007-11-23
If they are Torx then Home Depot is a good place to go. Some time back I picked up a couple of drivers made by Husky. The HD-74501 S comes with 4 combo bits-Phillips on one end and flat on the other. The HD-74502 V also comes with 4 double ended bits. the sizes are T4,T6, T7, T8, T9, T10 and T15. Each was about $6-7 US. If you need a larger size a holder with a bigger shank will be needed.

That is a Torx bit.  any autopart or hardware store will carry them.  You can find them in a fold-out kit much like the hex/Alan keys.  I believe they are metric, but I may be wrong.  

The alternative, unless you plan on using your old system case, is to try grabbing the screw head with a needle-nose pliers and just getting them loose then you should be able to remove the screws by hand, but this usually ends up completely destroying the screw.
