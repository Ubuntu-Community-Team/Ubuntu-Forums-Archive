### Post by jpkotta on 2007-01-01
You don't literally type '0' 'x' 'b' '2'.  That is a hexadecimal number that identifies the key.  Usually, the more common keys are replaced by a more descriptive name, e.g. 0x62 is the 'b' key.  When it is not replaced, it's a pretty safe bet that it is one of those "media keys" that are strewn across the top of non-barebones keyboards.  

You should go into the keyboard shortcut utility and click on the shortcut you want to change.  Then just type the key (possibly with modifiers like ALT).

For more info look up "xmodmap" and "xev".
