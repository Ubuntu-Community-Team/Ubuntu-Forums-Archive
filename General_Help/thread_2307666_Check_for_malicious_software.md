### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-12-27
Are you concerned about something that might be on your system now?  Or just looking for information in general?  Are you using Ubuntu?

Windows viruses target Windows. They can't execute on Linux. Linux users can pass them to Windows users in email or in files.  Checking for that is the purpose of ClamAV.

Much more malware exists today, beyond viruses.  Much of it depends on a user acting in a foolish way. There's little Linux or any other operating system can do to stop us from being foolish.  If we open a bogus email and click on a link that says it's our bank account and then happily give our bank username and password to the crooks on the other end, that's our fault.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-12-27
There is a section of this forum called: Ubuntu Community Discussions. That section has a link to Ubuntu Weekly Newsletter. Near the end of every issue there is a section called; Updates & Security for 12.04, 14.04, 15.04, 15.10.

If we are interested in the subject of protecting ourselves from malware we might be interested to read about the vulnerabilities of Linux that are being discovered and what is being done to fix the vulnerabilities.

For example, on 7th December a Ubuntu Security Notice was issued regarding OpenSSL. We can see that all affected versions of Ubuntu were patched to remove this vulnerability. So, by keeping Ubuntu up to date we do a lot to protect ourselves & others.
