### Post by AdrianStrays on 2008-05-06
I found a quite effective solution to this problem, and I thought I'd share.

Rather than go through the tedious taste of burning 237 cds worth of music I found an amazing Windows program called Noteburner.  Its a virtual CD-RW drive created for just the purpose of getting past the DRM.  It artificially simulates the burning of the music to a CD and then converts the burned tracks to MP3.  The process is faster/cheaper than actually burning/ripping music.  

This REALLY should be sticked or included some guide.  I spent a week trying to solve this problem, and this is the best solution out there.
