### Post by 64bit or bust on 2011-04-22
You have lots of choices in the free department. Wings3d, Meshlab, Blender, POV-Ray, Art of Illusion, K-3D, etc. And if you install Wine, you can run many of the windows versions as well.

But i am not exactly sure of all the things you would want in a program. 
I do lots of character based animation stuff, which includes designing scenes.
I mostly use Carrara Pro, Poser 2010, Hexagon, Wings3d, and a few others not suited to architectural stuff.

I also use smaller tools such as Morph Manager, Uv Mapper Pro, etc.

It would also help to know the output formats and level of detail you are looking for.
I have designed characters that are close to a million polygons, and lesser programs don't do so well with that kind of detail.

If you are doing detailed arch stuff, free might not be the best way to go. Most of the free stuff does not include specific libraries to speed up the design.
