### Post by vanadium on 2008-10-20
Audio CD is uncompressed audio. If an audio application supports one audio format, it will be uncompressed Microsoft WAV. However, most audio burning apps will be able to use compressed audio formats such as ogg, mp3 or flac. If a format does not work, simply convert to wav first.

Quality wise, you would rip from an audio CD to wav or flac or another lossless compression format. mp3 and ogg are compressed formats, and therefore are lower in quality than the original CD audio. Converting these lower quality files to audio CD will not increase quality: you will just retain the lower quality of the compressed file. Use either WAV or FLAC files (or other lossless formats like wavpack, monkey audio, ...) that have been created straight from CD.
