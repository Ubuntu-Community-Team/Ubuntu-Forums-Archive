### Post by coffeecat on 2008-07-24
[I'm afraid I have bad news for you]("http://www.sane-project.org/unsupported/canon-lide-90.html"). Is there any way you can get the supplier to exchange that scanner? But before you try, check out the scanner you are interested in at [http://www.sane-project.org/](http://www.sane-project.org/) .

> **shelbyvision said:**
> I had no idea Ubuntu was so picky about scanners.

That's not really fair on Ubuntu or Linux generally. The problem is manufacturers who do not co-operate with the Linux world by writing drivers. Hewlett-Packard and Epson are generally thought to be the good guys here. And from what I've read, Canon and Lexmark are two bad guys.

And before you buy a printer, I suggest you check [http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting](http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting) .

Actually, Mac users are in a similar situation at least as far as printing is concerned. Mac OS uses the same CUPS printing software and Mac owners know to check beforehand that printers they buy will work with their machines.

No consolation to you I know, but my Hewlett-Packard printer-scanner network multifunction machine worked out of the box with Ubuntu. It worked fine with my Mac too once I'd installed all the software. :wink: But if you do decide to go for HP, do check those two links first. Even though HP is a good guy, not all their devices work with Linux.
