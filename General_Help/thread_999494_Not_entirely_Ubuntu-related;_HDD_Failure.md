I know this isn't exactly the right place to post this, but these forums are very active and I need an answer.

There is no click of death. I opened it up and tried to see if one of the drive heads was scraping the platter; it was, very slightly. I slid a piece of paper under it and kind of coaxed it away from the platter and now neither of the heads scrapes the platter at all, but it still won't boot.

I know, very long post. The essentials:

### Post by PocketDog on 2008-12-01
I used to work in a clean room in the early 90s making HDs for a huge computer company. Even then, a human hair on a platter is akin to a Boeing 747's undercarriage hitting a car while travelling at 250mph. A bit messy. The tolerances these days are infinitely closer than then. I'd say the read head at the very least is completely beyond repair, and sending the drive off to a professional rescue company is your only hope of success in this case. I hope I'm wrong...!
