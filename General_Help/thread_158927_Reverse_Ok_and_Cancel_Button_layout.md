### Post by wylfing on 2006-04-12
I don't think you can do what you're thinking of. The button layouts are designed into the programs themselves.

FYI, the "design" in Windows (if such a thing can be said to exist), where you tend to see OK and Cancel from left to right, is generally quite wrong from a human interface standpoint*. I am too tired to go dig up all my HIGs, but trust me, OK is supposed to be on the right.

* There is something else very wrong about it too, which is that the terms OK and Cancel are used in all kinds of places where their meaning is ambiguous. This awfulness of design often appears in Gnome and KDE as well, however, so it's hard to bash Windows for that.
