### Post by Katalog on 2009-08-30
> **SuperBaby said:**
> Thanks for the many reply. I still have some doubts.

1) How do you change the theme of the UNR interface (not the desktop interface).


When you change the icon theme and gtk theme, all it does is change the icons and the window decorations, just like in GNOME. In order to change the color of the UNR interface, you just change the desktop wallpaper to a different color, since the UNR interface is pretty transparent. You can also add, delete or modify categories and applications, or just hide or show applications and categories on the left hand  side by checking or unchecking the box next to it, just like you would do with a regular GNOME menu by going to "Preferences>Main Menu". That's also where you can assign a custom icon for an application or category if you like by right clicking on it and selecting "Properties".
The only thing you can really customize on the right hand side is the folder list from your home folder. Whatever folders you put in the shortcut pane on the lefthand side of the Nautilus file manager will also show up as shortcuts to those folders in the right hand column of the UNR interface. 
The panel along the top is just a regular old GNOME panel that can be customized to your heart's content just like any other GNOME panel by changing it's background, color, and transparency as well as adding and removing applets and application launchers.

If you really want to dig in and do anything with main part of the UNR interface (called the netbook launcher - it's located in /usr/share/netbook-launcher), you have to tweak the svg and png files that make it up by using GIMP and Inkscape. _[COLOR=Red]I **highly** recommend you back up any files you modify or replace in case you mess something up or change your mind later on, and in fact if you're going to play with *any*[/COLOR][COLOR=Red] of the netbook launcher files, I would back up the entire folder.[/COLOR]_ I have made mine about 80% transparent so that the graphics on desktop backgrounds show through better, plus it makes it easier to get it to match the color of your GNOME panel if you have it's transparency set to the same percentage. You can also replace the graphic along the top of the netbook launcher that says "Ubuntu" by replacing it with any 90x23 pixel png image and renaming it to "ubuntu.png" and placing it in the /usr/share/netbook-launcher folder.  You have to have root access to edit any of these files, and you can do that by pressing the "Alt+F2" keys and typing "gksu nautilus" in the dialog box that appears and then clicking the "Run" button.

One of the only things I haven't figured out how to do yet is change the orange highlight color for the text in the netbook launcher, but I'm still experimenting. :P

As far as Avant Window Navigator, airo dock and gnome-do go, I haven't used any of them so I can't really make any recommendations.
