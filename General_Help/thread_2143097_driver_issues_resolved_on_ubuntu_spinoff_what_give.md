### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-05-08
Standard Ubuntu has an Additional Drivers utility that will install proprietary but free video and wireless drivers. That is the usual way that these issues are fixed in Ubuntu. We can also tick to install third party software during the installation process. That often sets the system up just fine but their have been issues of late with proprietary video drivers and I have see posts on this forum regarding Broadcom wireless adapters. It all depends on the adapters on your motherboard.
