### Post by az on 2009-07-28
> **user 123 said:**
> I have 2 PCs - A Dell Optiplex GX270 and an old old Packard Bell iMedia. When I plug a 256MB stick of RAM into the PB it only detects 182MB whereas the Dell detects 244MB. Anybody know why the PB would detect so much less? It really makes a difference in performance capabilities.

Different sticks of ram can have different densities and timings.  Some old motherboards can only deal with one timing or density.  Some can only deal with the first one they encounter.  If you are using more than one stick, try changing the order in which they are positioned.  

It may be that your stick of ram won't work properly in your motherboard.
