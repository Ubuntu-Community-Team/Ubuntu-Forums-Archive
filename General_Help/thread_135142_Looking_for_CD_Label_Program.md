### Post by bobpaul on 2006-06-27
I would completely disagree. If he's looking for something to print labels, then it's presumably the round kind that are made for CDs, which will not have adhesives that will damage your discs.

Labels prevent scratches. Scratches on the bottom of the disk can be buffed out with something like a DiscDoctor, but scratches on the label side will kill the disc. The foil label actually contains the ink the data is written on. The rest of the cd is just clear plastic. Take a key or a knife and scratch the label side of a bad disk and you'll see what I mean. The only CD-Rs and DVD-Rs I've ever damaged have been either bad burns or damage to the label. I'll never burn something I intend to be semi-perminent without putting on a sticky label.

Thanks for the GLabels tip! Works great!!
