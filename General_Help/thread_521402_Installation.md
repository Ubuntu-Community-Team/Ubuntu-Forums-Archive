### Post by apetresc on 2007-08-09
Are you talking about Ubuntu itself? It's not a regularly installable program -- it's an entire operating system. It doesn't run under Windows, it runs *in place* of Windows.

To see what it's like, and to install it, you must burn the .iso file you downloaded to a CD-ROM, put it into your drive, and reboot your computer. Instead of going to Windows, your computer will boot to Ubuntu. Once you're done, you can reboot and take the CD out, and you'll be back in Windows.
