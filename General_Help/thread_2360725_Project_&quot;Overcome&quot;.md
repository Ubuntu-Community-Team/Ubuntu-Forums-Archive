### Post by TheFu on 2017-05-07
Probably want to use a VPS for something like that, not your own hardware. Might be best to buy a website building host.  They cost more per month, but remove all the stuff you don't currently know from the solution.

Running a server on the internet isn't trivial, at least not if you want to do it and not be hacked.

If you want to do it yourself, then a VPS will have 1000x more bandwidth and keep all the hacks off your local network.  Better to get hacked at a VPS than inside your network. Depending on where you are in the world, something like digital ocean or Vultr might be the low-cost, yet fast, solution you seek.  Of course, you can run the stuff locally, at least as you learn more.  You'll need a place to backup what you put at the VPS anyway.

An old laptop might not be a good choice for the methods to be recommended (at least by me).  For example, I'd use virtual machines for each service to limit the issues should one service be hacked.  A good defense is important when running services on the internet. It is effectively the wild west out there.  

There are many skills in the 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 (Profit!) steps to be learned. If you say what you know how to do, someone might be able to provide a list of skills that are missing to get to the place you want in the end.  More than basic skills will be required, but you don't need to be an expert in anything either.
