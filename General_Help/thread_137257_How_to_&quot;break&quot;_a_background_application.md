### Post by o_fortuna on 2006-02-27
[QUOTE=Fred Doolie]Right now I'm playing midis in Gnome with Timidity (because it's the only player that works) but they play invisibly in the background. How do I "break" a background task so I can get the music to stop?

Edit: nevermind that "only" part. hehe Kmid was set up to use the Miniport; not the sound output. Duh! I'd still like to know how to break an invisible background operation.[/QUOTE]
Go into Applications-->System Tools-->System Monitor and look for the process. You may have to right click the process and select something from the context menu to see if it's the right one. Once you found the process you want to stop, just select "kill" from the right-click menu.

By the way, make sure you know what you're doing, killing an important process could make you lose all your work and have to restart the computer.
