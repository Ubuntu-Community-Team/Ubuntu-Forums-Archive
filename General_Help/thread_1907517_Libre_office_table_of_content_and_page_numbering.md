### Post by Mariane on 2012-01-11
I am creating a table of contents for a book using Libre Office. 

I start with selecting the chapter titles one by one and doing 
Insert - Indexes and tables - entry

Then I go to the last page and I do
Insert - Indexes and tables - Indexes and tables
I select "Table of content" and it is created. 

The problem is that the pages are numbered from the start of the actual text, not from the first page. There are 6 pages with stuff (or nothing) before the text starts. 

So the table of contents says the first chapter is on page 7 and if you look at the page numbers the first page of chapter 1 is page 1. 

The table of content cannot be edited but it can be copy-pasted on a new page, where it becomes editable. So a workaround is to simply copy-paste it, delete the original, then substract 6 from every page number. 

I was just wondering if someone knew a better way of doing this?
