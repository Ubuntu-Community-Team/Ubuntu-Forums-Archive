### Post by yetiman64 on 2012-02-10
> **illstate said:**
> ...does ubuntu ever become easy to use...like just weeks of no technical errors and such? ...

Yes most definitely from my experience. I've had installs in use last 12 to 18 months without a hiccup, only reason they are still not running is that I've switched them for a newer release.

Usually once I've gotten over an initial setup and ironed out any annoying little quirks (usually by scouring this site and google for solutions) which can sometimes take a week or two, everything tends to run pretty smoothly. I can have a very stable install for many months on end, unfortunately I love to tweak and as is said "you reap what you tweak" including for the worse. Too much tweaking can easily break a system (goes for any OS really).

Get your system to a comfortable level of usability and then don't tweak too much and you should end up with a very stable system. This is all dependent on you conquering "the learning curve", learn how to do things "the Linux way" and you will find the stability and security many users talk about to be very real. Expect Ubuntu to be a clone of Windows or to do things "as I did it in Windows" and it will be as comfortable as bashing your head against a brick wall (the wall will always win that contest :)).

It takes time to come to grips with when moving from Windows (took me at least 18 months of dual booting before I was completely comfortable), but It does fall into place eventually.
