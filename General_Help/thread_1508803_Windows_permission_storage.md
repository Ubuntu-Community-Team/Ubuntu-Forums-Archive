Your intentions for doing so do not change the fact that it is hacking. If this is a business system, trying this could get your friend fired. If the guy is head of the IT department, then there's not really anything you can do. You friend just shouldn't put any personal sensitive data on that machine, and if for any reason he has to for his job, he should quit, and give IT's refusal to secure a system to his boss when he leaves. If he's not head of the IT department, talk to whoever is. If it's a home system that the jerk owns, he could press charges if you hack it. Your friend should just refuse to use it, and possibly buy his own system. If it's a home system that someone else owns, then whoever owns it has the right to do whatever they want with the system, and could simply demand that the jerk give him access admin access (at which point he can then de-admin the jerk if he wishes), and could press charges if the jerk refuses to give him admin access.

It's simply a matter of who has legal authority over the machine. If it's the jerk, you can't do anything. If it's not, then you can request whoever does do something about the jerk, but if they refuse to deal with him, trying to hack the system will end you up in hot water really fast.
