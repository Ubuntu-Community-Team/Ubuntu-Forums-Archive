### Post by DuckHook on 2018-08-02
This really is a matter of personal taste/practice/risk tolerance, and everyone's advice will vary.

I did not even wait for the first point release to upgrade. But then again, I:

[LIST]
[*]have a separate 16.04 partition that I can fall back on at any time, and
[*]feel confident enough in my troubleshooting skills that I can take more chances
[/LIST]
The first point release is where Canonical judges enough bugs to have been squashed that they feel comfortable upgrading everyone. That may be enough of a vote of confidence for you to also take the leap. I'm not sure that waiting for the second point release gains you anything—by then, point releases tend to be more about keeping up with the latest bells and whistles than squashing bugs.

There is nothing wrong with just sticking with 16.04 if it does everything you need it to do. There is still almost 3 years of life left in Xenial.

If you are really worried about a broken system, then you may wish to do what I do and always keep an old LTS version on a separate partition permanently.

### Post by Impavidus on 2018-08-03
You should wait at least until the upgrade is offered, and, assuming you set the system to notify you of upgrades to LTS releases only, the upgrade should be automatically offered just after the first point release is released. That's when Canonical says the upgrade is good enough for ordinary LTS users. I you want even more reliability, you could wait longer. If you want the latest software, you could upgrade as soon as the upgrade is offered.

But keep in mind that things can go wrong, so don't upgrade at some critical time when you can't afford to lose your computer for a few days to fix a broken upgrade.
