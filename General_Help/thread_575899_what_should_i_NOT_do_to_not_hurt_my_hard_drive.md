### Post by Shazaam on 2007-10-14
First and foremost, hard drives are very sensitive to jarring/banging while the pc is running. If you bump it (or drop it) the read heads CAN come in contact with the disks causing either data LOSS or disk damage.
Second is old age. Most hard drive companies post the MTBF (Mean Time Between/Before Failure) specifications for each drive they make. This list is compiled under "clean room" testing so under real world use the interval will be less (oh, and they fudge the results sometimes too).
Third is static discharge. Walking across a carpet then touching components inside pc's WILL lead to failure of said components.
The rest is usually user caused ie. spilled liquids; disconnecting power while the pc is turned on, etc.
As far as trashing the drives by partitioning; installing os's, etc. please read and understand before you commit (or RTFM).

### Post by jespdj on 2007-10-20
One more thing to watch out for is that your hard drive does not get too hot. I had problems with a hard drive that gave some random read and write errors, and installed a program ([HDD Health](http://www.panterasoft.com/), for Windows, scroll down the page) which showed that the disk was getting hotter than 50 degrees Celsius (122 F).

I bought a hard drive cooler (a thing with two small fans that you mount on the hard drive) and the errors went away.
