What can you ABSOLUTELY not afford to lose?



I keep 3 seperate HDD based backups of my /home partition.  Yes 3.  _***He who laughs LAST-probably has several good backups***_

OS is EASY to start over with.  No matter WHAT you do--starting over in a worst case scenario will take a few hours to get back to what you had before--either in re-setup or in time it takes to copy over backed up files. at least an hour.


What I do?  

I have an APTonCD backup of my ATP-get packages--makes it EASY to get wireless working again, I have a install CD.  Now, presuming a dead OS does NOT take out my seperate /home partition (it is unlikely-unless the drive itself is borked).  I reinstall *Buntu on a seperate root partition--and tell it to use my existing /home partition.  I After that, I reinstall all critical APTonCD packages.  I then reactivategraphics drivers and reboot. Then redo settings.  Then reinstall apps via wireless.

I'm well practiced, and can do the above procedure in about 2-3 hours.  Recopying over my 50GB /home partition takes more time (have NOt had to ever do that yet).

No matter WHAT you do, you need a system and a in writing procedure for use when needed.
