### Post by Autodave on 2017-04-14
Several things to try that have worked for me on various laptops:

1. Make sure AC power is connected when you power up the machine. After machine is fully booted, then you can remove AC power.  Powering up the machine with AC power disconnected is OK, but charging indicator / charging rate may be affected.

2. This is my favorite: don't laugh because it has fixed *many* laptops over the years. Power down, discvonnect AC power, remove battery. Wait 10 minutes and insert battery, connect AC power, boot up machine. See if it works now.
