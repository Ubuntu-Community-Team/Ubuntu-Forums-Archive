### Post by tgalati4 on 2009-09-01
I have a reader, but I use Windows to run the Sony Connect software and log into the Sony website and download books.  Do a google search for firmware and you will find a few tweaked firmwares that improve the reader.

In linux, I believe it's drag and drop.  You may have to format books that you get elsewhere (such as gutenberg.org).  I use the Sony software because I'm lazy and you get 100 books for free, and they are preformatted.  That will give you at least a few years of reading before the batteries or screen craps out.

Treat it gently, the E-ink screen is delicate.  Don't let it get squished in a handbag.  One sharp depression on the screen and it's toast.  Don't drop it either.  Even though the case is aluminum, the E-ink screen is glass and it will crap out with a simple fall to the floor.  Don't get it wet by the poolside either.
