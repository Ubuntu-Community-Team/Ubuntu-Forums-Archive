### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-10
Why get an Ati card just because your getting an AMD CPU? 

I've been running an AMD CPU, ATI chipset (790X) and an nVidia card (8600GT) for a while now, and havent had any problems. The Ati cards dont work any better (or worse) than nVidia cards on the Ati chipsets. I know of a few other people running AT chipsets/nVidia cards as well. Its not a problem at all. 

I'd get an nVidia card, not only because of better driver support, but also for VDPAU (hardware video decoding).

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-01-10
I personally think you should buy an Intel CPU and an Nvidia graphics card, but I echo cascade9's post. There's no advantage AT ALL to using an ATI card with an AMD CPU, over using Nvidia card with AMD CPU. In fact, it's the opposite: Nvidia's Linux drivers are literally years ahead of ATI's, so you always get better performance with Nvidia.

It's not like Asus monitors work better with Asus-branded graphics cards, and Gigabyte's PSUs don't work better with Gigabyte's motherboards.
