### Post by an.echte.trilingue on 2006-11-13
Root is not dissabled on Ubuntu, if it were the system would not run.  What happens is that the installer simply sets a long and random password that you don't know.  What most people here call "enabling" root is nothing more than changing the password from something you don't know to something you do know.  If you prefer the way it was before, simply change the root password to something secure (somthing long, with at least two upper case, two lowercase, two numbers, and two special characters) and then forget the password.
