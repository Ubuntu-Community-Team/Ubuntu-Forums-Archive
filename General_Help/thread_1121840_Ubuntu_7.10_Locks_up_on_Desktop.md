### Post by CatKiller on 2009-04-10
> **Teasdale said:**
> I noticed today that the PC is making a loud sound as if the fan, or maybe the drive, is having to work hard and is almost grinding during the restart process. Could that be related?

Potentially. If you happen to have a way to check the temperatures (in the BIOS or what-have-you), that would probably be useful. My guess is that it's the fan on the video card that's failing (since you're seeing X problems), leading your video card to overheat. If you open the case, you should be able to localise the sound to either your video card fan, your heat sink fan or your power supply fan. It's pretty straightforward to either put some heavy oil in whichever fan it is, or to replace it.

If your hard drive is making grinding noises then you'd have bigger problems, but computers don't generally almost work if the hard drive is tearing itself apart.
