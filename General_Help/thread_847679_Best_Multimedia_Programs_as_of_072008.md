### Post by all2ez on 2008-07-03
I love Amarok as an iTunes replacement.  I used it to organize my huge mess of music files into folders and the tag editor is easy to use.  I believe the latest version in Hardy Heron can download cover art (I'm on gutsy right now and it doesn't work due to changes Amazon made to their cover art service).  The best part about it is that I can sync music to my iPod touch.  I think Exaile is based off of Amarok so if you really want something you can use right away this should work great for you.  [http://amarok.kde.org/](http://amarok.kde.org/)

If you want something that looks and feels more like iTunes you'll probably want Songbird.  I haven't used it much but it looks good and has the advantage of being cross-platform so if you use OSX and Windows sometimes than this is a great solution.  There are some cool looking add-ons, too.  It supports lots of different iPod models listed on their website but not my iPod touch so I can't vouch for how well that works.  [http://getsongbird.com/](http://getsongbird.com/)

I don't know about iMovie or iPhoto, I have to say good luck finding something as sleek and full featured as those.  I use Mirage (from the repositories) for photo viewing and slideshows but I'm not very hardcore about organizing my photos into albums and such.

For Office I would use my Innotek VirtualBox where I have XP installed as virtual machine.  Of course you need an XP disc/key but VirtualBox is free with a choice between open source and proprietary editions.  I use VirtualBox to run a couple of Windows only programs that I only need to use once in a while.  I just installed Office 2007 as a test for you and installed fine and appears to be working at seemingly native speed (i have 2GB with 512MB allocated to the VM).  The setup is well documented in the community docs.  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/VirtualBox](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/VirtualBox)
