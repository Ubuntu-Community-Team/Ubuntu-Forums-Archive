Welcome to the forums! [The system requirements for Ubuntu are well-documented]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/SystemRequirements"), and as you can see, 512mb of ram and a 1ghz processor are the minimum recommendation.

I would recommend you take a look at Puppy Linux. It is designed to be fast and lightweight on older hardware, and it is also beginner-friendly and has a welcoming community.

Of course, if you upgrade your hardware, then Ubuntu may be a good choice.

ps All Linux distros have the capability to connect wirelessly to the internet. If your wireless card needs "restricted" (i.e. non-free) firmware, you can usually install it easily if you can temporarily connect with a wired connection.

### Post by lightstream on 2010-05-11
I have to second the previous poster who points out that XP is not really able to run with 64MB of memory. If it says it will on the box, that should be viewed as just a marketing ploy to sell more units, not a statement of truth.

However if you are happy with how XP runs on a 64MB machine, then I'm sure you'll be fine with how Linux runs.

I would suggest a more lightweight distro/flavour such as xubuntu, lubuntu, or Puppy Linux. 

Puppy is very basic, and more closely resembles Win 95 in appearance and features. The latest release (v4 is it?) has prettied up the icons and stuff though, and looks a lot nicer although I haven't personally tried it.

Of course Puppy is still Linux, so you can add software onto it, in fact you could keep adding bits on to it until it ~was~ Ubuntu.
