### Post by lswb on 2009-10-05
I'll let others with direct experience advise on the best way to shrink a Vista partition, but I would question if you really have used up 33GB on a recent install. Unless you have a load of videos or music or some other kind of data, there may be some other reason why you cant install updates. Besides, updates don't usually take much space anyway. What error messages are you getting? 

You can free some space on the ubuntu partition by emptying the trash (for all users if there is more than one) and might get a few dozen to a few hundred MB more by "sudo apt-get clean" and "sudo apt-get autoremove"
