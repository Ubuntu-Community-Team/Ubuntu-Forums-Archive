### Post by Brightrock on 2007-03-24
Ok, first the situation is solved and I found the pictures.

Thank you both for your quick responses.  I did try them, and in the process of trying them I realized I should retrace my steps exactly.  The program that comes up when you accept the Ubuntu offer to import photos is "gThumb Viewer." So I opened that up and right clicked to "Move..." and then you get an "Choose the Destination Folder" dialogue, which includes bookmarks of the last few places you've moved photos.  I remembered that before I moved pictures last night there were 3, and to today there were 4.  So I checked more carefully in the folder that was at the top of the list and the photos were there sandwiched between some older photos of mine which is why I missed them when I had looked before. 

So this one was an operator error, at least sort of.  Here's what I figure I did.

I went to the parent folder and created a new folder and then clicked open without selecting the new folder, hence I had actually chosen the parent folder instead of the new folder.  I feel like there is something a little off about this procedure, but it could be just operator error.  Anyhow, I'm glad to have my pictures, and I'm glad it wasn't Ubuntu's fault.
