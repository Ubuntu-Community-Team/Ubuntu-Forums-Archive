Hey everyone,

I've been having some rather strange events with my laptop over the last day or so. I run 10.04 and I had it setup to go into suspend after two hours so that if I fall asleep while watching a movie or something, the laptop won't be on all night wasting energy and heating up.  

I woke up last night, though, and it was still on, and when I would hit the power button to try to put it into suspend, it would show signs that it was trying (e.g., it would disconnect from the wireless), but then it wouldn't go all the way to a black screen, it would just start turning everything back on as if I had awoken it.

So, I figured I'd just plain shut it down, but when I tried that, the system completely froze and I eventually shut it down by holding down the power button.  Upon restart, the shutdown button was 'locked' meaning it had a somewhat grainy appearance and was not clickable. Moreover, when I tried ctrl+alt+delete, I was only given two options: restart and shutdown.  I thought this may have been associated with the earlier manual shut down, so I chose restart.  On top of that, I got a warning message saying the power management program was not responding and asking whether I wanted to continue, which I did.

Upon restart it took what felt like an eternity for the panels to load, the shutdown button was still locked, and when I tried to get online firefox also took forever to load.  Then, by chance I guess, the update manager popped up and I thought maybe that was the problem, so I allowed it to update, but upon restart everything was even WORSE: the load times were slower, I couldn't even get ctrl+alt+del to bring up a window, and my firefox had been replaced by a different program.  Even though I've heard viruses are relatively rare on linux machines, the whole thing spooked me, so I backed up my files on an external HD and re-installed Lucid.

For the most part, my problem seems to be fixed--the shutdown button is working, I'm getting the option to go into suspend or hibernate, my firefox is back, etc--but the programs are still taking a long time to launch, so now I'm wondering if this may be a hardware issue.  I'm wondering if my RAM might be going bad on me considering the suspend function was the first problem, but I'm also worried it may be an early sign of hard drive failure.  Then again, maybe there was genuinely something wrong with my old version of Lucid, and I just need to tweak something in my fresh version so that firefox and open office launch quicker.  Obviously. I'm no computer professional, and am just hoping one of you might have a vague idea of what might be the culprit given my description.

All helpful advice appreciated (i.e., please no 'just buy a whole new system' posts or 'switch to apple!' posts; although my primary goal *is* to fix the problem, my secondary goal is to learn a little something more about computers, so such posts just aren't helpful to me).
