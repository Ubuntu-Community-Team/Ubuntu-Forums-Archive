If they pay for their software and OS, they can afford it and if they don't pay, what do they care about open source anything.  you get one school onboard, 'bang' there's 300 kids.  you want them to be able to do their homework at home, you send a live cd for them to use in their home computer.  their parents show an interest... and on it goes.  but the current state of affairs for edubuntu is that our particular brand of problems and questions rarely find answers on the other forums.  and there needs to be a location where developers and users can communicate so that the programs being developed match the needs of the students that will be using them.
