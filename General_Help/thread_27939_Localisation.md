### Post by Lakefall on 2005-04-18
Hi!

I'm looking for a Linux distribution I could recommend for anyone. I am using Debian as my desktop, but I think it's mostly suitable for geeks and computer professionals, not for "human beings" as you say it. ;-) Nevertheless there are many good things in Debian, so I would like to suggest a Debian based distribution. Obviously, this is where we get to Ubuntu.

I burned the live CD and booted from it and chose the Finnish language, which I admit isn't very widely spoken. As the desktop appears the first thing I see is "Applications Places System". Yes, that's in English. Most, but not all, of the menu items are translated, but the menu names are not. This is pretty much the worst first impression you could give from the localisation point of view and the live CD is the first impression CD. The other applications didn't seem to be translated either, but I suppose you have limited space on the CD. Still, having the main menus half translated like this isn't going to impress the Windows users. I haven't tested how well the other locales work.

The shocking part is the Gnome menus are translated better in Debian testing. I do not want to diminish Debian, but I thought Ubuntu was putting more weight on things like localisation and you are using Debian as your basis. I think the practical reason is you have a newer version of Gnome and they redid the menus and didn't provide the translations. Still, if this problem also exists in the installed version, I suppose Ubuntu is not suitable for those Finns, who do not like to use their computer in English. Maybe the next release will be better.

### Post by erkki70 on 2005-04-18
Terve!

Most of the Gnome 2.10 desktop is translated to Finnish as you can see here:
[http://l10n-status.gnome.org/gnome-2.10/index.html](http://l10n-status.gnome.org/gnome-2.10/index.html)
Now, for other applications including some of the essentials, look here and you'll see that there's still a lot of work to be done...
[http://l10n-status.gnome.org/HEAD/index.html](http://l10n-status.gnome.org/HEAD/index.html)

I also used Debian sid before and made the switch without a glitch of hesitation, because of the numerous advantages, like Xorg (had troubles with sid), Gnome 2.10 (even not entirely translated yet) and a real ease of use plus stability with not much tweaking as IMHO required in Debian.

Now, I'm running my boxes in English, and sometimes in Finnish or French.
So I'm not that annoyed by the lack of translation when it happens. ;-)

So you're right, definitely not a good asset to impress Windows users if all the menus aren't yet in a different language than English.
Things are improving though, and quite quickly as Ubuntu has already demonstrated.
It's a big work in process, but things are getting done and I really think that when putting aside little annoyances such as translation, Ubuntu is really worth having a closer look. The transition is even easier if you are already a Debian user, you'll feel at home with Ubuntu.

Cheers,
Erkki
