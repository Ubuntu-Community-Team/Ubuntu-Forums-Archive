### Post by lavinog on 2011-01-26
SataII is likely SATA version 2 which has the 3gbit/s limitation.
The 750mb/s can exceed 3gbit/s:

[Wikipedia:](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serial_ATA#SATA_Revision_3.0_.28SATA_6_Gbit.2Fs.29)

### Post by cascade9 on 2011-01-26
SATAII = SATA3Gbit/sec. 

SSDs are nowhere near 750MB/sec. About 350MB/sec is as fast as the fastest, newest models go. Still, thats faster than even the theoretical top speeds for SATAIII (6Gbit/sec) so there is a benefit.

 Actually, even drives that have transfer speeds far lower than SATAII can benefit to a limited degree from SATAIII, but who really cares about how fast a drive can write to cache? 64MB (max) at 6Gbit/sec isnt going to make much different over 64MB at 3Gbit/sec.
