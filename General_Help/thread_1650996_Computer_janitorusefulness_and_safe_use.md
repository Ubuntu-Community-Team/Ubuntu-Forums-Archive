### Post by iosuna86 on 2010-12-22
That's a good question. 

I have used it and I have seen that it is pretty safe whenever you install things from Synaptic or Software Center, but whenever you install something from the terminal or thru .deb packages, it may not be so safe, because it may remove some apps that you may want to keep.

But there is a difference that may prevent you from cleaning necessary stuff. 

Some packages have the following description...

> "Package is no longer supported: it is no longer in the package archive". 

...those, I think, are safe to remove.

However, when you find that after that the following message appears...

> "(It may also have been installed from an unofficial archive that is no longer available. In that cause you may want to keep it.)" 

...I wouldn't remove them.

That's what I do and I haven't encounter any problems.

Hope that helps.
