### Post by squilookle on 2012-03-16
The best thing for you to do would be to burn off a few live CDs and give a few distros a try, then install your favourite of those and try and get on with it for a while.

Versaility/Customization means different things to different people, making it hard to recommend something to you. To one person, it might mean the ability to alter the way the core of the OS works, to another, it might mean the ability to move the position of the launcher or change the wallpaper. Most Linux distros are versatile/customizable in one way or another.  

Also, you might want to ask yourself why, if you have run Windows and Ubuntu side by side and booted mostly into Windows, you want to come back to Linux? Is it the love of free software, are you tired of Windows, etc? Is there something specific you want from Linux that you cannot get from windows? If you can identify that, it might help you pick a distro.
