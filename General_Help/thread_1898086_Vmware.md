### Post by t0p on 2011-12-20
If you want VMWare in particular, then e79's suggestion is best.  But if you are interested in virtualisation software in general, rather than VMWare in particular, I would suggest you check out VirtualBox at [www.virtualbox.org]("https://www.virtualbox.org/").  VirtualBox can do most of the stuff you'd get with VMWare and there are Free (as in speech) versions available.

Of course, if you don't care about Freedom (liberation) I believe you can get free (gratis) versions of VMWare Player.  I think.
