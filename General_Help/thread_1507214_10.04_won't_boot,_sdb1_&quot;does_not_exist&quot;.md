### Post by bcbc on 2010-06-14
When you see your grub menu press 'e' to edit the first menu item.
Then change where it says /dev/sdb1 to /dev/sdc1 and CTRL+X to boot.

What it looks like - is for some reason your 2nd drive is being labeled sdc instead of sdb
I've no idea why this happened - have you plugged anything in lately or made any hardware changes?

Once in ubuntu, run ```
sudo update-grub
``` to regenerate your menu. If your computer starts seeing the device as sdb again, do the reverse.
