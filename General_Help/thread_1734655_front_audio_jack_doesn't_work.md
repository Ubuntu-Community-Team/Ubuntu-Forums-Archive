### Post by prankstar008 on 2011-04-20
Do you know if the front jack used to override the rear one (ie. plugging headphones in the front, made sound stop coming out the speakers that were plugged into the rear)? If so, maybe try pushing the broken plug in as far as it will go (given the situation, this can't hurt). Although it is unlikely, the broken plug could be in an unusual position and causing a short. Like I said, unlikely, but something you might not have thought of. Obviously, if the front never overrode the rear, this won't work at all.
