### Post by codeseer on 2009-04-23
Some of the Intel drivers are pre-installed with the kernel; yours may be also.

Try:

```
sudo apt-get update && sudo apt-get install xserver-xorg-video-intel
```

Edit:

You might also want to take a look at this: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/JauntyJackalope/ReleaseNotes#Other%20known%20issues]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/JauntyJackalope/ReleaseNotes#Other%20known%20issues")

### Post by duruttye on 2009-04-24
Hey there, I had the same issue, and I followed one of the few instructions on this bug:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/xserver-xorg-video-intel/+bug/359392](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/xserver-xorg-video-intel/+bug/359392)

namely, I edited the xorg.conf file, added 
[HTML]Option "MigrationHeuristic" "greedy"[/HTML] this to the device section, and now it's a little more faster.

You can test the speed with glxgears command then wait a while, so it will measure fps. Measure it before and after the change.
Mine was around 400 before, and now it is around 700. Still, it is very slow, compared to my other laptop, a dell inspiron 1501 (1.5 GB, 1.8 dual core), I'm using a dell vostro (3 GB, 2 Mhz dual core).

Hope this helps!

Remember to log in and out after you add that line in your xorg.conf file!

good luck
