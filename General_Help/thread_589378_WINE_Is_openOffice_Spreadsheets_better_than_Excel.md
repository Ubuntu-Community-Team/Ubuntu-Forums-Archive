### Post by GingerAlex on 2007-11-10
In my experience, open office matches excel pretty closely. Almost too closely!

VBA routines/macros are a bit of a problem.

Also I believe I once saw the two applications give different answers in the least squares regression tools (automatically fitting best fit straight line to scatter chart data) - due to using different parameters, not because either routine 'got it wrong'. Obviously this kind of thing could introduce problems in ported spreadsheet.

If you are considering transporting a big spread sheet with lots of 'hardcore maths' in I would propose the following test procedure:
1) in excel, fill the spread sheet up with lots of data covering the full range of sensible inputs
2) export the spreadsheet to text, CSV or tab delimited whichever you prefer. e.g. excel_answers.txt
3) Repeat steps 1) and 2) in the open office version, e.g. to oo_answes.txt
4) Use a file comparison utility, ideally a good visual one, to compare the two outputs.
Obviously, there may be some differences due to formatting issues between the two apps, but you will probably be able to filter these out. Any differences in Numbers are obviously more serious and need some attention.

OO spreadsheet has one brilliant feature that excel [at least up to MS Office 2003, 'cos I have not used newer ones] does not have. Handling complex formulas.
