### Post by wylfing on 2005-12-21
What **handy** said is true. Because almost 100% of what gets installed on your Ubuntu system is open source software, it is virtually impossible for spyware to be installed as a "rider" with some other piece of software, which is a prime vector for getting spyware on Windows. In addition, there are other aspects of Linux that make it hard for unwanted software to be installed without your knowledge. That's not to say it couldn't happen, but there's just not the same level of vulnerability with Linux.

As for viruses, there's little reason to fear infection at the moment, because there's few Linux viruses in existence (something like 120 of them, which is quite inconsequential) and I think none are in the wild. Viruses and worms will become more of a problem in the future, but for now it's nothing to worry about, because there simply aren't any viruses to infect you.
