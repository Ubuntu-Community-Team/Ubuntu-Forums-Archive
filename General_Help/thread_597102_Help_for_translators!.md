### Post by asbjornu on 2007-10-31
I'd just like to point out [this blog post](http://charismacode.blogspot.com/2007/01/powers-and-repositories-ubuntu-and.html) about Rosetta, Launchpad and particularly how translating Ubuntu feels like for an "outsider". I especially want to highlight the following, which very neatly describes exactly how I feel about this:
[quote=Lars Risan]Obviously, it would not take long before a Norwegian newbie &#8211; someone who did like me &#8211; to run into the Norwegian &#8220;Ubuntu translation page&#8221;, probably before getting to Rosetta, and read about the translation effort that also takes place with Skolelinux. Thus, a set of social relations and web pages outside of Rosetta would most certainly prevent an unwanted fork of the kind that I provoked in my little experiment. The point, however, is this: The web, in the small community of Norwegian translators, that prevents this forking exists outside Rosetta and Launchpad. Rosetta itself does nothing to prevent a fork. There is no systematic feedback from Rosetta to the upstream translation. No system or systematic attempt of information that prevents double work. No system for merging work done at different places, and for resolving conflicts if there is double work that cannot be merged automatically. Rosetta acts and exists as if it is independent of upstream.[/quote]
