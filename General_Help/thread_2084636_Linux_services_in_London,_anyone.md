To all readers based in London,

Ubuntu is very much to my liking. And the Ubuntu forums are great - no doubt about it. But since I'm far from a PC expert, I often could do with a bit of hands-on assistance: in some situations, it is better to speak to another person or walk into a shop 

With Linux, however, this is usually in short supply. If you don't have a Master's in IT under your belt - like me - finding someone who might be of some *practical* help is like looking for a needle in a haystack. 
Canonical's customer service is not great either, in this regard - in my personal experience, anyway. And after registering for my regional LUG, some time ago now, I received a welcoming message - and the rest has been silence until this day.

In London, where I'm based, I know of only two computer shops providing Linux services. One of these (DinoPC) recently informed me that they don't provide Linux services any more; the other one is located in Wood Green (on Lordship Lane) and trading as before. 

For such a vast metropole, that's extraordinarily limited for choice, I should think! Hence my question to all London readers: have you heard of any other places in the capital to go to (in person) for information/service?

Thanks very much.

tnob
