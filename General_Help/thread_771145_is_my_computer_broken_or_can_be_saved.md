### Post by ibuclaw on 2008-04-27
First, open up your desktop case.

A) Check all the batteries on the motherboard. Make sure that there are no batteries leaking fluid (ie: there is something other than dust that is on top of them).

B) Turn on the PC, make sure all fans appear to be functioning properly.

C) Take a good look at all the components and decide whether or not they look in good nick (compare them to another machine if you have one).

Only attempt the next three steps if you are comfortable with building Computers from scratch.

D) Take out and re-insert all components connected to the motherboard. (ie: the RAM, the Hard-drive, etc.)

E) Take off the CPU fan and check that the CPU chip looks OK.

F) Dismantle each piece part by part and (ideally) test them with another machine.
ie:
Test the power supply.
Test the RAM sticks, first each one, then all together.
Test the CPU.
etc.


G) If all hardware appears to work in isolation, it's probably the motherboard. But in one last vain attempt.
Turn on the PC and go into BIOS and set all settings to their Factory Defaults.


Usually the event of a PC turning on but not doing anything usually is to do with RAM not being inserted properly, or you have two clashing RAM sticks inserted (the idea is to stick to DDR400 if your first one was DDR400, don't mix it with an SDRAM or whatnot).

But things that start as a one off and gradually build up tend to be a serious motherboard problem (usually batteries) that escalates.

But you'll find out once you start testing.
