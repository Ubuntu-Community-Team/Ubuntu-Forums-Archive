1. The terminal is a part of your Ubuntu system, if that is what you are running. Mostly in Windows things are done though a user interface, the Terminal is a way of doing many of the same things, (running/installing programs, changing system settings etc...) but though text commands, not a graphical approach. The Terminal is part of the operating system, you will have it.

2. Jaunty and Hardy are different releases of the Ubuntu Operating System. It would be analogous to Windows XP and Windows Vista. Jaunty was released in April of 2009, Hardy was released in 2008.

3. To close an application, simply right click on its bar on the panel and click "close."

4. To begin running basically any distribution of Linux, simply burn the iso onto a blank CD and put in the CD. Then turn the computer off and on, as it starts up hit either F11 or F12 to go to the boot screen, (it should specify which "F" key needs to be pressed.) From the boot screen, select "boot from CD drive." The CD should start up and guide you through the rest of the process.

I hope that this helps and I wish you good luck.:D

### Post by staf0048 on 2009-09-12
Point and click method for forcing an app to close.

First get the icon on your panel:

1.  Right click on panel
2.  Click on "Add to panel..."
3.  Scroll down to "Force Quit" and add it to your panel

Now that this is on your panel, whenever anything freezes or just runs too slow and it's bugging the living hell out of you, simply:

1.  Click on the "Force Quit" icon you just placed on your panel
2.  Click on your annoying app.
3.  Click on "Force Quit"

Done and done!
