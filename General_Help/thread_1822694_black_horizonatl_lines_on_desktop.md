### Post by CatKiller on 2011-08-11
The manual appears to say that the native resolution for that screen is 1920x1200, although it isn't very clear. Perhaps there were many models with the same name but different hardware? Similarly, the manual appears to say that the default amount of RAM is 256 MB. That's really not going to work well. How much have you got in there? Performance at the low end is very dependent on the amount of RAM you have available; not enough and you'll be spending all your time swapping data back-and-forth to the hard drive, which is sloooow. More RAM will almost always help.

If your computer is under-powered, which seems to be the case, using a lighter-weight desktop like LXDE (Lubuntu) or Xfce (Xubuntu) will probably help. They can either be downloaded and installed fresh, or installed like any other package and selected from the login screen.
