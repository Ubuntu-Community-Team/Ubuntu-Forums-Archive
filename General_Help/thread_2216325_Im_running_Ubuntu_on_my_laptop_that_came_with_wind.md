### Post by Lars Noodén on 2014-04-11
Malware can't cross over from XP to Linux so leaving the old OS on the system won't affect Ubuntu.

If you disconnect physically from the net before booting to the old system, it should be fine indefinitely.  Not too much malware is spread by sneakernet these days.

What applications are left causing dual-boot?  You might see if they run under Wine.  Some do very well.

In order to give a good recommendation for a new computer, we need the specs of this one, at least

- Brand name and model
- CPU
- RAM (size)

### Post by Impavidus on 2014-04-11
> **drew14 said:**
> IF I want to have a computer that can utilize Ubuntu os without windows being stuck on it, what computer would be recommended to buy for say under 500.?
Ubuntu runs on most computers out of the box, Windows or no Windows doesn't matter. Finding someone who sells (second hand) computers without (broken) Windows is a different matter. Actually, people are known for selling fine computers because Windows is broken and they don't know how to fix it. Excellent Ubuntu machines.

Ubuntu should do well on most 5 year old second hand computers, but also on new computers, as long as they don't have too outlandish hardware. You may be able to find one for less than 500 ... reais? Francs? Krónur? Oh, yes, this is an international forum, so it's a bit confusing when people give numbers without units. I always use euros, but rupee, pound and dollar users are quite common too. But if you find something with decent specs and a decent price and you don't have to decide at once, use google or ask here to find out whether anyone has had problems with any component of that computer on Ubuntu. Pay special attention to wifi and graphics hardware, those cause most problems.
