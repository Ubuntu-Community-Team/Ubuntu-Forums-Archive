### Post by Xiong Chiamiov on 2009-02-28
Gimp is a raster-graphics editor, like most photo editing programs.  Inkscape uses vectors, which means that you can't just drag a brush around, because everything is stored as a series of mathematical shapes.  This gives very clean lines, however, and you can resize an image as much as you want without loss of quality.

### Post by fragos on 2009-02-28
GIMP is the more powerful of the two and has lots of photo editing functions. You can do anything with GIMP except vector graphics files (.SVG). For that you can use Inkscape but I'm not sure that's available for Windows as GIMP is. If you don't want to edit photos and draw things then look a Pencil [http://www.les-stooges.org/pascal/pencil/](http://www.les-stooges.org/pascal/pencil/). It runs on Linux, Mac and Windows. They are free so you can try them to see what you like.

As a Windows-only (but still free) solution you might try Paint.NET.
