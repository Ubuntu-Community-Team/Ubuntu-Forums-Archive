### Post by yukinagato on 2012-01-06
Basically I am getting audio through the Logitech USB headset in everything except in web browsers.  Initially the headset had no sound at all which required setting kmix to use the headset as input/output rather than the internal audio however which allowed audio to play in everything but the web browser.  There were a few threads on this issue back in 2009 with older versions of Ubuntu but they've out of date as the advice they give for fixing this no longer works in 11.10.  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1614871](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1614871)  [http://www.linuxforums.org/forum/ubuntu-linux/124531-firefox-flash-has-no-sound-my-headset.html](http://www.linuxforums.org/forum/ubuntu-linux/124531-firefox-flash-has-no-sound-my-headset.html)  [http://getsatisfaction.com/twones/topics/no_sound_in_firefox_ubuntu](http://getsatisfaction.com/twones/topics/no_sound_in_firefox_ubuntu)

### Post by yukinagato on 2012-01-06
EDIT: sigh...   sudo apt-get install pavucontrol   trial and error of sound outputs/inputs eventually gets the sound to work in all browsers...  for future reference pavucontrol is the name of the Pulseaudio sound control settings GUI.
