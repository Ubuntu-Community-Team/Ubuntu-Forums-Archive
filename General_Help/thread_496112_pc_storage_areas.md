### Post by southernman on 2007-07-08
Well, the lesser of the two evils (you are correct) is the crawlspace. When you call it a crawlspace, is it not a full on basement? I thought all yanks had basements... ;)

Where are the machines occupying space now? Floor, Desk, corner of a room...

The best option would be to build a rack that would take as little footprint of a room as possible. Even better would be in a closet, but you would want to put a poot sucker type fan in the ceiling to help draw out the heat from them.. as well as putting a vent louver in the door. A friend did the closet thing. It should go without say, he had to give up "his" closet space to do it... Women and their schtuff! :D

If you have no other option (read the wife is ready to make a decision on you or the machines staying), could you put a rudimentry type shantee in your crawlspace? Something you could put a small a/c unit in... or at least a good dehumidifier. Ofcourse with the latter of the two, you'll be stuck having to empty the reservoir unless you engineer a drain system. Without something to "help" draw out excessive moisture/dampness (the heat from the machines alone will help, but not be enough), I'd venture a guess that after the first winter you'd start to see a corrosion problem.
