Hey guys,

I've been doing Ubuntu for about a month now - I'm really happy with it and have managed to solve most of the major problems I had from the beginning, so no more Windows for me. There's just one little thing that bugs me...

I'm quite multilingual and therefore write in three or four different languages on a quite regular basis. Since I have an English interface (which I want to keep) Ubuntu also uses an English spell-checking function, meaning that whenever I write in any of my other languages, it highlights exactly everything. This is easily solved in programmes like OpenOffice, where I just shut down autocorrect, but in programmes like Pidgin, Firefox et cetera, I haven't found a way to override the default spell-checking. It's really bugging me that everything I write in Pidgin is marked as faulty... :)

So to my question - is there a way to totally exterminate all spell-checking so I don't have to worry about this? Like uninstalling some packages or something? Help much appreciated!

### Post by forestpixie on 2008-02-22
you can turn off mispelled words in pidgin - preferences - conversation and in firefox preferences- advanced - general

you could look into the installed languages through synaptic - maybe uninstalling from there would help, but I'm not sure
