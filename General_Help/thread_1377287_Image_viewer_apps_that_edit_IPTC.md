### Post by Digital Magi on 2010-01-10
I found four so far other than the ones you already tried. Hopefully one of them will be the right one for you.

(1) [Raw Therapee]("http://www.rawtherapee.com")? It's feature list includes IPTC & EXIF support.

(2) There is also the command-line [exiftool]("http://www.sno.phy.queensu.ca/~phil/exiftool/") and an optional [GUI for exiftool]("http://freeweb.siol.net/hrastni3/foto/exif/exiftoolgui.htm") that also lists editing exif/ITPC/maker notes/etc in it's feature set.

(3) A third one is [mapivi a "cross-platform picture manager & organizer"]("http://mapivi.sourceforge.net/mapivi.shtml") it also lists IPTC capability in it's feature set.

(4) [jBrout]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/jBrout")

Don't give up yet. I haven't found anything I wanted to do on Ubuntu that couldn't be done. Just takes a bit of searching and experimenting sometimes.

I know zip about photo management, just found these via google for you.

### Post by clevertomato on 2010-01-11
Just in case this helps anyone that stumbles onto this thread...

I tried jBrout... it sounded good, but it is quite elementary in function and appearance. Mostly, I could never find IPTC editing even though it clearly claims to support it. If it does, it only supports IPTC keywords and not the standard IPTC fields (caption, city, state, creator, etc)... at least not that I could find.

I tried Mapivi... also sounded good. Again, quite simplistic with very elementary appearance. It did offer standard IPTC fields to edit, but was quirky overall.. i.e. the IPTC dialog window was too tall to be seen on my screen but still offered no way to scroll down and see or use the fields that didn't fit on the screen.

As I said in previous post, I already had RawTherapee installed and have used it for a while. I guess I mentally pigeon-holed it, thinking of it as ONLY a tool to manipulate RAW photos, but it is quite well developed. Although not exactly "advertised" as a photo organizer, it is pretty robust, offers great photo manipulation, full IPTC and EXIF support, and a wide array of file format support... along with batch processing. So, thanks to someone's urging, I took a second look at a tool I was already using and have decided to use it more to its fullest capabilities.
