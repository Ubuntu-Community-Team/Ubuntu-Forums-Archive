### Post by Les_Caesars on 2007-10-08
I'm pretty sure they mean AMD processors and Intel processors that are 64-bit, where "64-bit AMD and Intel" is written. After looking your processor up (and seeing that it was 64-bit), I would have to say that I disagree with rfruth and would suggest that you just download the 64-bit iso.

32-bit is a safe choice no matter what, but your 64-bit hardware is probably going to perform better if it was running 64-bit Ubuntu instead of 32-bit. However, even then, you may want to run 32-bit anyway, just because it probably has been debugged more rigorously than the 64-bit version of Ubuntu.

But if I were you, I'd download 64-bit. You can just replace it with 32-bit if 64-bit ever craps out on you.
