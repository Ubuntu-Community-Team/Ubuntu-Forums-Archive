### Post by TheFu on 2023-12-16
Some opinions follow ... 

Usenet used to have free mirrors around the world.  Sometimes an ISP would sponsor it for their users.  That all ended around 2002-ish to my knowledge. Now only paid Usenet services exist.

Without a paid usenet feed provider, you are stuck.  Do you have one?

Then you'll want to use a real newsfeed reader. I hate to say this, but Thunderbird's Usenet support is pretty weak. It isn't fit to be bothered with at all, but you can try it.  Just setup a "News" server and subscribe to the different feeds, like you would IMAP folders.

Again - without a commercial usenet service, you are unlikely to get very far - at least for all the groups that made usenet the most useful.
In the 1990s, some companies setup news servers for their specific product(s) and provided feeds for free.  I'd be very surprised if any of those still exists today.

I fear the best you can hope for is to find a per-Group FAQ document that is 20 yrs old.

I miss Usenet.  When my ISP stopped providing them for free, I stopped using them and never jumped to google groups, which are/were an abomination of the ideas behind usenet, I'm sad to say.  Google just wants ways to learn more about us, build profiles about us and make claims about us to companies desperate to advertise at us.

### Post by TheFu on 2023-12-17
> **eliyahu22 said:**

[https://www.techradar.com/best/best-usenet-providers](https://www.techradar.com/best/best-usenet-providers) has some ratings.  I've never used a paid service, so I have little opinion, except that I'd expect the paid providers to retain all feeds for at least 3 months, but it looks from the reviews they appear to far surpass that time - 5000+ days.  The top one in that link is $10/month with 3/months free.  The free trials usually have some GB limitation to prevent people just out to violate their local laws.

The alt.binary.* newsgroups effectively ruined UseNet for everyone else.  The groups that were their purely to exchange knowledge and expertise, not cracked software or violate media copyrights were fantastic.  I spent months learning on UseNet. That doesn't really cover how much time I spent there, learning, for my $day_job.  For the first few months I was working in a Unix lab, it was like a fire hydrant of knowledge.  My peers were probably very unhappy with me, since they could easily see my news reader running when I should have been coding.

BTW, lots of people only use Usenet with a full, paid, VPN too.  Gotta hide from their national govts and others worried about IP/media theft, I suppose.

Most kids these days don't know that Usenet was social media for 30+ yrs, but without the advertising and tracking.  NNTP rocked.
