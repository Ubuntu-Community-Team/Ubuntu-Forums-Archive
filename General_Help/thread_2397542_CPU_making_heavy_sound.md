### Post by QIII on 2018-07-31
Your description indicates a cooling problem.

Laptops often suffer from heat sinks impacted with dust and debris.  My first suggestion would be to use compressed air (from a can is best -- an air compressor may provide pressure and flow that might damage your hardware) to blow through the cooler intake to attempt to clear any obstruction.

If that does not work, we can help you look at other possible issues.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2018-07-31
If the applications you run are CPU-intensive, then you would expect the fan to speed up.

> Blowing from the outside would push the dust further in.

There's usually an intake vent on the bottom. I would start by using electronics duster there. It is best to open up the chassis, but that's not for everyone, especially those who don't like working with small fasteners or don't know how to take ESD precautions. Personally, I struggle most with plastic snaps. I'm an impatient, ham-fisted, brute force kind of guy and tend to break cheap plastic that's not designed for repeated assembly/disassembly.
