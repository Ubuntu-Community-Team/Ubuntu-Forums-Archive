### Post by jbrown96 on 2010-01-02
I try to stay away from printers as much as possible, but I have heard that some applications cause problems with printing. Have you tried some other apps to print from (evince, writer, firefox, etc.)? Does it happen with all of those?

You might have tried this, but you make try deleting the printer and reinstalling it in Ubuntu.

### Post by sgosnell on 2010-01-02
Almost any HP or Brother all-in-one should work.  You may have to wait a little while for the newest HP models to have Linux drivers available, but it shouldn't be long, as IME HP provides excellent Linux support.

You have to put the printed alignment page through the scanner?  That's amazing.  The problem might be that the scanner has gone a little bonkers, or the alignment might be off, but I'd bet on the scanner being wrong first.  Do you get good scans?  If it's skipping lines, it could be the software or the print cartridges, hard to say without seeing the printouts, and probably still hard.  Printers are pretty cheap these days, and you should be able to get a comparable new one for ~$100.
