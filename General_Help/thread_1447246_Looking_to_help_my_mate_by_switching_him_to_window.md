### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-04-05
Since I'm one of those unfortunates who knows enough "about computers" that I end up providing free support to my extended family and friends ... I have been through this more than once.

My advice is to set up your mate's machine to dual-boot Windows and Ubuntu.  Then, sit with them and show them HOW to do their stuff using Ubuntu.

If they're really impressed (and they often are!), they will try this on their own

What's the downside?  You have inherited lifelong support for this person (if you don't already have it).  Why? Because you are where they will turn now for EVERYTHING they want to do with their PC.

Things that are a (relatively) simple in MS Windows (i.e., watching Youtube videos, setting up network access, adding network printers, getting WiFi to work by default, getting hardware 3D video acceleration for Windows Games, getting Windows Apps (MS Office, typically) to work) -- ALL of these now become YOUR problem to get working. They can no longer stick in a CD, click SETUP, and five minutes later, be done.

IF you're up to providing the support, then by all means, try to get your mate to make the switch.  But realize what you're getting into before you do.
