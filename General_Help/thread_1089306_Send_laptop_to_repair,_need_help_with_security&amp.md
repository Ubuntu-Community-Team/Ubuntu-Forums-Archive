### Post by sdennie on 2009-03-07
If they have physical access to your machine and your data isn't encrypted, there is literally nothing you can do to prevent them from easily doing whatever they want with your files.  Just booting from an Ubuntu LiveCD is sufficient for them to read/write and file on the disk if it isn't encrypted.  There is also a very good chance that just "to be thorough" they will re-image your machine and wipe all the data off it anyway.  

The only way to absolutely insure your data is intact when you get your machine back is to not send them the disk in the first place (maybe convince them it contains sensitive information and you can't send it to them) or image the disk and completely wipe it before sending it to them.
