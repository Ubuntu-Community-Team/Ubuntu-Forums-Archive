### Post by paulparker2 on 2018-05-14
Suspect your settings may be using **Suspend & Power Off**  with the setting showing **Automatic Suspend**.  


When Suspended things are frozen work ceases, until the computer is woken up. 

Try pressing a key (example Shift or CapsLock or click with the **mouse **to awaken a Suspended  computer. 

Suspend is when your computer goes to sleep, your applications held in their before Suspend state for when you Resume, applications  and documents remain open, other parts of your computer switched off to save power.




Check your settings: 
Search using top of screen search for then open  **Settings** 
,   
Search for then open **Power ** 

view settings for  **Power Savings** 
then view **Suspend & Power Off ** 
Then if displays **Automatic suspend**  click on that display to see the settings. 
IF you see **OFF** it should NOT suspend.
IF you see **ON**  check delay time for system being idle before it Shuts Down or Suspends.

You can select settings to suit your needs,  with **Automatic suspend ON** you can chose between 15 minutes and 2 hours. 

With **Automatic suspend** set to **OFF**  it will NOT Suspend.  




IF screen just auto-locking, this can be with no screen display. . 
 
Usually find moving mouse, or pressing keys [examples Shift, CapsLock]  may show image of the screen locked, to login in again.  




To check your settings: 
Search using top of screen search for then open  **Settings** 
Search for then open **Power ** 
view settings for  **Power Savings** - **Blank Screen**  
IF it says between 5 minutes and 15 minutes, try change to Never.   
BTW only use Never when in a safe environment, always remember to Lock or LogOut before you leave.
