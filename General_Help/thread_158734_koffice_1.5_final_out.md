### Post by jdong on 2006-04-11
KOffice is really integrated to KDE. So is OOo2 now, but at times KOffice simply feels more KDE.

The real advantage, KOffice is very light on resources compared to Openoffice. If you're running a lower end system or just lack RAM, you may benefit from KOffice.

Downsides? Fewer total features and still some compatibility/formatting problems with complex document layouts.
