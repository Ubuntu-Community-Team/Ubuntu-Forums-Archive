### Post by donkyhotay on 2007-01-06
I have never personally used it myself however I have heard puppy linux is designed for people that are learning computers so it may be your best choice. A friend of mine once told me that DSL (damn small linux) works great on machines with limited capabilities but is designed for more advanced users. I have given ubuntu to a couple of people that literally know nothing about computers (no OS biases) and after spending about 10 minutes explaining the login procedures and the basics of working with the gnome desktop were happily browsing the web, writing letters, and working on genealogy which is all they really wanted. If this is for educational purposes I would focus on ubuntu (or it's variations like xubuntu or edubuntu if you prefer) or puppylinux. As previously posted of course download live CD's of every distro capable of running on your machines and mess around with them a bit before making a final decision.
