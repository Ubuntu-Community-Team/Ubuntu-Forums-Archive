### Post by Soarer on 2007-06-09
I don't think it's your speakers. The inside of a PC is very noisy electrically, with all those processors & power supplies and disk head solenoids and what have you. It is very surprising to me that we can get any reasonable sound out of them at all. 

I have found this worst of all with laptops - everything is so crammed in that the inverse square law doesn't have much of a chance to get going, and shielding is minimised to save weight.

When you are downloading or surfing the processor, disk & wireless card are all working hard, so it could be any (or all) of those which are causing it. 

One (maybe expensive) solution would be to use an external sound card.

### Post by hikaricore on 2007-06-09
I've noticed this on systems with integrated audio for years under a variety of operating systems.

You can probably overcome this issue by decreasing the PCM/Wave volume level with the master at max until the problem is gone or nearly gone.  Then just adjust your main volume to a reasonable level.
