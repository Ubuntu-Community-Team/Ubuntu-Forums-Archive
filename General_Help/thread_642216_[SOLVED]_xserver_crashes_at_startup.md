### Post by onetb on 2007-12-16
As nearly as I can tell, xserver is crashing at restart, but I'll supply everyone with a bit more information.

I tend to watch things from bed, so I had to fix this.  Rather hastily at 2am, I found a thread and attempted the changes it detailed.  I applied the following changes to my xorg.conf file:

THIS DID SOMETHING!  Now, after reconfiguring (leaving most options default) I was able to get to the login-splash!  But after entering my login credentials, the OS begins to startup.  Just after my desktop is loaded, however, it crashes and starts back at the login splash.

ANY IDEAS?

### Post by santaslittlehelper on 2007-12-16
You could try to boot into recovery mode from GRUB and have a look at you're xorg.conf 

```
cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf_backup
nano -w /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

"ctrl + x" to save, "y" or "n" then enter to confirm.
