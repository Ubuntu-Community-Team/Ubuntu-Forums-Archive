### Post by ilrudie on 2008-08-21
Ubuntu will see it as 1 drive with 2 partitions on it.  Not separate c and d drives.  When you are installing if you don't care about the data on those partitions just click the use entire disk button or box.  Ubuntu will then blow away the partitions and the data and you start with 1 drive again.  Ubuntu will partition it up for its own use (a / [called root] partition and a swap partition) and you are ready to go.  I usually recommend making a separate home partition where your files will live separate from the system files.  That way if you have to reinstall the system you don't have to move all your music/movies/docs/etc... off the computer.  Making a backup is of course always recommended even when using a separate /home.
