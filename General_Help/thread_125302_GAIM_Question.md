### Post by TechSonic on 2006-02-03
Ask your buddy to request Direct Connection.  If that doesn't work, you are behind a NAT (router) or ISP Firewall thats preventing it.  Your out of luck if your ISP is running a Firewall that prevents it.  If you are on a NAT (router), you can access the device control panel and setup "virtual server" it's a port forwarding trick.  Just inter the TCP and UDP ports for AIM's Direct Connection settings and you should be able to link up. 

If you are running Firestarter (linux firewall), disable it when direct connecting, or ad the policy to handle it.
