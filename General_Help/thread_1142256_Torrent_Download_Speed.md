### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-04-29
What is your connection speed?  (You can test this at [SpeedTest]("http://www.speedtest.net/").)  What client are you using?  What settings are you using in your client?  

Keep in mind that K**B**ps is not the same as K**b**ps.  SpeedTest will give you your connection speed in Kbps or Mbps, while many torrent clients use KBps.  Since one **B**yte is equal to 8 **b**its, you'll need to divide by 8 to convert Kbps to KBps.

Remember, a large number of seeds alone won't equate to fast download speeds.  The ratio of seeds to leeches is much more important.  A torrent with 100 seeds and 50 leeches will likely be faster than an otherwise identical torrent with 1000 seeds but 2000 leeches.  Even though the latter has 10 times the number of seeds, more people are trying to download the torrent than are uploading it.

### Post by kpkeerthi on 2009-04-29
> **Desolo_Amour said:**
> Ok I have been downloading a few different torrents and it is taking to long for my liking. I was curious if anyone knew some ways to make the torrents download quicker. If so I'd love to hear them.

Possible reasons:
1. You bandwidth is limited.
2. Your ISP is shaping your bittorrent traffic.
3. You are not seeding or uploading enough so the tracker is limiting your download speed.
4. You have too many torrents actively downloading more than your bandwidth can handle.
5. You have too many open connections that your router/modem is struggling to cope up with.
