### Post by Pontiac on 2007-03-12
If your USB key works within windows, you've already installed the drivers for it.  If your key has never been attached to your Win98 box, you have to go to the appropriate manufacturers web site to download drivers for it.  99% of the keys I've seen do NOT come with a driver disk, but they do come with instructions on where to get the drivers.

For example, if you have a Kingston USB key, go to [http://www.kingston.com](http://www.kingston.com) and see if you can find the drivers for that particular model of key.  The packaging your USB key came in should have a web site stated somewhere.

### Post by Pontiac on 2007-03-12
Any FILE can be copied to the USB key with not a lot of difficulty under Win98.  Open up My Computer, and you should see all your drives.  If the USB key was installed properly, you'll see an extra drive listed as a removable device, or removable drive, or something like that.  (Been a while since I've played with 98).

Go into that new drive (I'll call it U: for now).

Open up My Computer again and then go into My Documents or C drive and start copying the files over to U: by dragging and dropping.

Are you trying to copy just documents and pictures and stuff like that, or are you trying to copy over applications as well?  Applications you can copy, but whether or not they work when you copy them back to a 98 machine depends ENTIRELY on how the software was written.

In other words, its best to copy just the documents, pictures, and database files (MS Money, Quicken, My Family Tree, etc) over to the key, and not the full applications.
