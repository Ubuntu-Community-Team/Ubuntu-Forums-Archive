### Post by deadgoon on 2006-06-01
Well, I stand by my recommendation.  If you haven't created CD or DVD backups of your data, you should.

I thought I was being smart.  I backed all my data onto my secondary hard drive, verified it, and formatted my primary drive in preparation for installing the Beta.  I thought about unplugging the secondary drive as well, but thought that was overkill (I was wrong).  Install went smoothly and everything seemed to work perfectly after reboot, until I tried to mount my secondary drive.  It didn't even recognize it.  I tried several recovery tools, with no success.  I finally used an $80 Windows utility to recover the data.  And the data I got wasn't even from when I did my backup, that data was completely corrupted.  Amazingly I was able to pull the data from an old NTFS partition on that drive from back when I had Windows installed.  Of course I lost a lot of stuff I had done recently, but nothing I couldn't rewrite or redo (not looking forward to reripping my music collection).

So word to even the wisest among you, make backups and backups of backups, and if you don't want it lost, unplug the drive.  :-P
