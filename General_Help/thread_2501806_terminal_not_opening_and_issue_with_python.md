### Post by grahammechanical on 2024-10-21
I suggest, but not as a solution, that you uninstall the Gnome Terminal and then reboot and then re-install Gnome Terminal. I am assuming that you are using Ubuntu with the Gnome Desktop Environment.

Open the app store. Search for Gnome Terminal. Click - See all results for "Gnome Terminal." Change Filter By from Snap packages to Debian packages. Scroll down ignoring those alternative terminals. WAit until you see the panel with an icon of a terminal and the comment: "Use the command line." Click on that panel and the terminal home page will open. You will know that it is the right terminal because you are given the option to Uninstall.
