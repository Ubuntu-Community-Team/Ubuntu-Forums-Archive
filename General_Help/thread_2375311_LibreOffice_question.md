### Post by #&amp;thj^% on 2017-10-23
> **starmom41 said:**

This has always worked for myself. More here: [https://help.libreoffice.org/Common/Relative_and_Absolute_Links](https://help.libreoffice.org/Common/Relative_and_Absolute_Links)
You can insert hyperlinks in two ways: as text or as a button. In both cases, the visible text can be different from the URL.

Place the text cursor in the document at the point where you want to insert the hyperlink or select the text that you want to put the hyperlink on. Select Hyperlink command from the Insert menu. Alternatively click on the
Icon

Hyperlink icon on the Standard toolbar. The Hyperlink dialog appears.

    To jump to a specific line in a text document, first enter a bookmark at that position (Insert - Bookmark).
    To jump to a cell in a spreadsheet, first enter a name for the cell (Sheet - Named Ranges and Expressions - Define).

Hyperlinks can also be inserted by drag-and-drop from the Navigator. Hyperlinks can refer to references, headings, graphics, tables, objects, directories or bookmarks.
If you wish to insert in a text a hyperlink that refers to Table 1, drag the entry Table 1 from the Navigator and drop it in the text. To do this, the Insert as Hyperlink drag mode must be selected in the Navigator.

### Post by Buntu Bunny on 2017-10-24
First you need to bookmark a landing place in your document for your hyperlink.
Choose your hyperlink destination and click on it.
From the insert menu select "bookmark." 
In the dialogue box name your book mark and hit "OK." 
Now highlight the word you want to use as your link.
From the insert menu select "hyperlink."
In the dialogue menu that pops up, click on "document" in the left column. 
Find "Target in Document" and click on the little disc-looking icon on the right. 
A "Target in Document" box will pop up, select the drop-down arrow by "bookmark"  
Your list of bookmarks will appear.
Select the destination bookmark for your hyperlink.
Hit "apply" then "close."
Back in the hyperlink dialogue box, the book mark name should appear in the "target" field.
Click "apply" then "close."
Your hyperlinked word should now be underlined and blue.
To select your link, hold down Ctrl and left click the link.
You should find yourself at your target destination within the document.

You can create as many links within the document as you want. Just be sure to give each bookmark a unique name.
