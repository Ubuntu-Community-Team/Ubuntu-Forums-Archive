### Post by pmlxuser on 2009-08-07
To change the boot priority has nothing to do with OS, you set boot priority during bios boot. try pressing f12, del, f2 or what ever you system says before the os loads.

where it says press "somthing" to change setting etc. if your Motherbood allows booting from USB it will show in the bios.

if you don't know the key sequency try looking at the site of the manufacturers or instruction manual.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-08-07
If you know what the model number of your motherboard is, surely you have the manual that will tell you how to get into the BIOS?

When you turn the machine on and it shows you some sort of splash screen right away, it should say something like:

<F2 - Boot Priority>

or

Press DEL to enter SETUP

or something similar.
