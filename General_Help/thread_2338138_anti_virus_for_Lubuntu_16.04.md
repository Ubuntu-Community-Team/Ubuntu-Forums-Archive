### Post by ajgreeny on 2016-09-25
Hello bluesfloyd, and welcome to the forum.

I suspect you are under the impression that an antivirus will protect you from viruses infecting your Linux OS, however there are no viruses at present in the wild that can infect Linux; what an antivirus program will do is find any files that could infect a Windows OS.

I have not used an antivirus program in my 11 years of running *ubuntu OSs and never had a problem or infection of any sort, so my answer would be not to bother with anything.  Just be careful not to click on unknown links in emails or do anything that you don't understand.  In Linux I have heard it said that the weakest point in the OS is the person sitting in front of the screen and using the keyboard.

If you really want to go further with this have a look at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Antivirus](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Antivirus) and go also to some or all of the links at the bottom of that page.
