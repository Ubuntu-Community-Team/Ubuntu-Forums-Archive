### Post by lisati on 2009-04-12
The only way I know of avoiding spam completely is to have no connection to the internet whatsoever.
Spammers use a number of tricks to find out email addresses and IM usernames. Some times the guess and get lucky, other times they "spider" web sites and harvest names. I've even received spam at an email address that I've only used on Ubuntu-related websites. The last one I received (of the "I'd like to do business with you" variety that is a poorly disguised effort to rip you off) I sent back a polite reply pointing out that typing in capitals is unprofessional, and asking that they refrain from contacting me with that email address unless it has something to do with Ubuntu. So far, I haven't had a reply back yet.

### Post by nandemonai on 2009-04-12
Pidgin -> Tools -> Privacy

Then for whatever accounts you have in pidgin change the settings to reflect what you'd like to see happen.

IE: Allow only the users on my buddy list
