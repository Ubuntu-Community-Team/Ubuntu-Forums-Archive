### Post by tomski on 2006-01-16
i think the only way you could come close to this would be to run a firewall on a seperate pc and restrict the 'bandwidth' that that connection uses

BANDWIDTH:
A measure of the amount of data that can travel through a network. 
Usually measured in kilobits per second (Kbps). For example, a modem line often has a bandwidth of 56.6 Kbps, and an Ethernet line has a bandwidth of 10 Mbps (10 million bits per second).
 
also 
Bit Rate - The number of bits transmitted per second. In theory, a 56 Kbps modem, for example, can transmit up to 56,000 bits per second.
