### Post by howefield on 2015-08-13
Google for trackball images and ask your Aunt for the 3 that she likes the look of, then research for compatibility. I'd expect that most (if not all) would work but some may have functions which would need special care to get working.

In this scenario form seems to be a higher priority than function, that is, ease of use and comfort in the hand is more important than the latest bells and whistles.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-08-13
Ideally, take your aunt to a store that sells trackballs so she can actually use them first. 

Whether a traditional mouse or a trackball, try to get a device that fits her hand size.

You can check the Logitech and Kensington sites for the trackballs they sell. They seem to be the most common.  

Wireless versus wired issues may not matter so much since you don't move a trackball.

Note that only a few trackballs have a scroll wheel.
