### Post by kidders on 2007-03-04
Hi there,

It's unusual for mail servers to bounce spam, since doing so would double the amount of unnecessary traffic on the world's email infrastructure. That doesn't mean that's not what happened though! The mail's headers will give you clues.

In any case, it's impossible to prevent someone using your email address to send messages. You, for instance, should have no trouble constructing a message from [email]president@whitehouse.com[/email] to [email]calderon@presidencia.gob.mx[/email], declaring war on Mexico.

If you post the mail headers, they might provide some insight.
