### Post by Jeff_Morrow on 2015-07-10
I run 32-bit Ubuntu 14.10 on an Asus eepc(a netbook).

I'm completely new and this is my first post. I searched the forums with someone with the problems I've been experiencing and I haven't come across one quite like mine.  So, here it goes: I was using 14.04 originally and after more than a year without a single problem, all of a sudden I lost the ability to click within applications. Sometimes it wasn't isolated to applications, and I was unable to even click Desktop icons or the menu bars.  After a bit of research, I found out that resetting Unity sometimes fixed the problem, but when it did fix it, it was only temporary. I had recently gotten a kernel update(as in, immediately before I started experiencing the problem) and so I figured that was the culprit. For some reason, I saw jumping ship from 14.04 as a viable option, so I upgraded to 14.10.  

After upgrading, the problem persisted. After more research, I downloaded the unity-tweak-tool ran unity-tweak tool --reset-unity from the Terminal and then I rebooted. At last, I no longer had the problem with clicking inside of applications(or anywhere else). However, now at seemingly random intervals, I get random right clicks wherever my cursor is(even if I'm not touching the keyboard at all). Has anyone ever encountered anything like this or does anybody have any insight as to how to solve this problem?
