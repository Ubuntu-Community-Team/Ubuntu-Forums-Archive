### Post by ammofreak on 2012-11-29
Hi: ):P
Had the same problem. Am running Mint 13 with a Microsoft Comfort 4500 mouse. I use to have to unplug the USB mouse and then plug it back in to get the wheel scroll speed down to a 'normal' pace. However I did some more research on this scroll wheel quirk & came across a package called *[SIZE="3"]resetmsmice[/SIZE]* which works for certain Microsoft mice being used on different flavors of Linux. I downloaded it in the .deb package, ran it, rebooted my system, & voila, the problem was gone! The scroll wheel works perfectly & I do not have to unplug & plug back in the USB connector each time I start my system. [Sorry but I did not come across anything for Logitech, etc, types of mice.] The package is found here: [http://sourceforge.net/projects/resetmsmice/](http://sourceforge.net/projects/resetmsmice/)

Edit: click on the Browse All Files underneath the green Download box to get the .deb file if you prefer it instead of the .tar.gz file...
