### Post by sanubie on 2009-12-15
Did you just install? Are you not getting a signal at all? On the top screen you should see an indicator for wired and wireless connections. You'll need to configure your access point. If it's protected, and you just installed Ubuntu, then it doesn't know the key yet. If you don't know or remember the key, you'll have to reset your router and put in the new key. After that, it will connect automatically.

### Post by alancop on 2009-12-19
Do NOT reset your access point. What you have to do is connect your laptop to the router with a wire. Then go to System--> Administration--> Hardware Drivers and install the Broadcom B43 Wireless Driver.  This will allow you to use your WIFI card with no problems.
