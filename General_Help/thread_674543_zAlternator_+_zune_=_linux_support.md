### Post by erpo on 2008-01-22
I was going to blast you for not reading the documentation of the tool you wanted to use, but it doesn't look like there's much documentation to be read. However, my gut feeling is that the program modifies the computer rather than the zune.

The reason is that you have to "unlock" the zune every time you plug it in before you can use other tools. If zalternator really modified the zune so that it truly appeared as an ipod or MTP device to the host computer, this wouldn't be necessary.

The bottom line is that as long as you're using proprietary hardware and software like the zune or an ipod, you're going to be at the mercy of the manufacturer. Many manufacturers seem to think they still own the hardware even after they sell it to you, as evidence by the fact that they're trying to restrict what software you can use with your hardware. (Yes, even Apple is now trying to stop people from using anything other than iTunes to load music on the ipod.)

If you want trouble-free listening, sell your zune on ebay and buy something from Cowon or iRiver. Linux geeks who have them tend to rate them highly, and they're based on true open standards. Many even play ogg vorbis!

Happy listening.
