### Post by hoborocket on 2007-10-15
I just upgraded to Gutsy on one of my systems, and I noticed it replaced gaim with pidgin. I used it on this system and I hate it. Is there a way to keep it from replacing it when I upgrade other systems?

I thought of saving the .deb for gaim and just reinstalling it afterwards, but I suspect there's an easier "don't mess with this" button somewhere, that will let me keep my configs and such.

---

### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-10-15
Go to System -> Administration -> Synaptic

Find the package you want to put on hold ... select it ...

Now go under Package -> (from the synaptic menu) and select "Lock Version"
