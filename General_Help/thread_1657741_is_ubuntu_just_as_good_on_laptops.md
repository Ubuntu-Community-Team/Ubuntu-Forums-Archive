### Post by perspectoff on 2011-01-01
Desktop version. Do everything the same as a desktop.

I don't know what you have in the UK. I have always been pleased with my Toshibas, which are sold worldwide and seem the least expensive of the lot. But Acers have always been quite Linux friendly and also very inexpensive -- Acer made its initial reputation on being Linux compatible.

My newest laptop (one year old) is a HP, and I really have liked it except for two things -- battery only lasted a year and the CD/DVD drive is somewhat cheap (compared to the Toshibas.) Nevertheless, it's performance is great and the wireless/sound/graphics have all been compatible out of the box with (K)Ubuntu.

I have no desire to use netbooks which are much, much lower powered and no fun to watch movies. Also, Netbooks generally don't come with built-in DVD drives. Plus, I type a lot and don't like small keyboards. But YMMV.
