### Post by Damanther on 2007-11-13
I did a very similiar thing. Mostly because I like some of the KDE apps that are available, and partly because I like the KDE desktop a little better.

You should have all the functionality of a fresh Kubuntu install. At least I haven't run into anything that isn't there. You will also still have some of the gnome apps available to you as well, which is handy.

One warning though, under the System and Settings tabs, you probably still have some apps listed there that are really apps to configure gnome specific stuff and will not have an effect on your KDE environment. I'll edit this post later when I get home and list the specific ones that I found.

Anyway, good luck and welcome to Kubuntu.
