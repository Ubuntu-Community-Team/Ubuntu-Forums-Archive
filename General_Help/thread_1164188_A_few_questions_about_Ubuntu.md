### Post by Legace on 2009-05-19
First of all, Ubuntu (and Linux in general) does support wireless devices.
Check for a list of supported devices here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported)

Second, if you dual-boot, you will use more space of your harddisk. This is logical, because, in addition to the Windows Vista system files, you will now also have the system Ubuntu files. A normal Ubuntu installation, however, will only take a few gigs of space, so you shouldn't be worried about your drive filling up :)

Third, Ubuntu does not wear out drives faster than Windows. I would even say, that Windows will wear out drives faster, as it uses more RAM (thus it uses the pagefile more), and has more apps running (e.g. anti-virus apps, which Ubuntu doesn't need) which constantly read/write from/to the drives :)

If you try Ubuntu 9.04 you will have your best bet at support for Wi-Fi IMHO.  However, if you have any doubts, try either using the CD to run without install to get a feel for it. OR you could go with a dual boot install, find out how much space your currently using in Vista and allow a little extra for future needs (which you may not actually use once you fall in love with Ubuntu).  Then partition your drive with enough space for Vista+growth, and the rest for Ubuntu.  I'd be willing to bet that if you have support for your Wi-Fi, barring a forced need for Vista (like school or work) you will probably find yourself going more and more to Ubuntu on boot-up :)  It just happens that way it seems.

Good luck, and remember to check in here for ?'s.  Look around and READ all that you can and you will find alot of answers already posted.  If your's is not, post here, I've seen this great group of folks helping new users for several years and they really can be a great help.  And lastly, if you do decide to make the switch, come back and let us know how it goes and what you think.  

Wiley
