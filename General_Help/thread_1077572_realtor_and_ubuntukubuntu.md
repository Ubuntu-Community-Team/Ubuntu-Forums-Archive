### Post by cecilx22 on 2009-03-14
My mom is a realtor and we just went through a whole big hullabaloo trying to get her to Linux.  Two MAJOR, seemingly unsolvable problems at the moment:

1) MLS (Multiple Listing Service) sites are almost ALL IE6 or IE7 only.  Most report limited success with IEs4linux, and in most cases, only limited functionality

2) Many offices use USB based electronic keys to open lock boxes on houses being shown.  There is -absolutely- no software for these devices for Linux (and not likely to be any time in the future, as the demand is quite low).

The only real solution to these issues is to run a virtual Windows machine from within your Ubuntu OS.  Look into VirtualBox if you are interested in this.  Unfortunately, you'll need to install the non-OSE version available directly from SUN, NOT the one in the repositories, as it does not have USB support (at least, not the last time I checked).  The version from SUN is still free, as in beer, though.

Hope this helps.  Sorry to be the bearer of bad news.
