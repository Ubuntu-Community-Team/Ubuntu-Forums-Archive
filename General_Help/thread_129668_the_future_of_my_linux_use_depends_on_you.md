### Post by ncappel1 on 2006-02-14
I would suggest to keep ubuntu on your system with a windows dual boot. If you have to do a quick simple task or have some time and are willing to learn something new, use ubuntu. There are still programs which I have not been able to get working perfectly on ubuntu, so if I ever need to use them (which is rarely) I log into windows. 

The most important thing would be to not use ubuntu, expecting it to be your windows machine. Just the same way that a mac computer has a different dynamic than windows, you'll only get frustrated if you try to do something the "windows way," on another OS. It may take some time, but do a little forum/internet research on what you want to get done, and after a bit of tinkering, i'm sure it will be working in no time. There is nothing bad about gradual change!
