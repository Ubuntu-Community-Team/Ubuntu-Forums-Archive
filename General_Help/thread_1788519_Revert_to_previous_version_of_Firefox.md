### Post by chepprey on 2011-06-22
Ok here's how to go back to Firefox 4 (thanks to Khun Yee Fung, [http://comments.gmane.org/gmane.org.google.gwt/66791](http://comments.gmane.org/gmane.org.google.gwt/66791) )

1. Start Synaptic Package Manager
2. Search for firefox. And then click on the line.
3. Click on the "Package" menu item at the top (between "Edit" and
"Settings").
4. Choose "Force Version".
5. Select 4.0.1.

Click "Apply" to get FF 4.0.1 back.

Every time you update Ubuntu from now on, make sure FF is not updated
by un-selecting the checkbox beside FF.

### Post by winh8r on 2012-02-07
Version 3.6 is the only "old" version that will be maintained for a "very short time" according to Mozilla.

Please bear this in mind if you are going back to version 4 as it will 

effectively mean that your browser is unpatched and will most likely have 

some sort of weakness/instability/security issue that brought about the

introduction of version 5.

Browsers are best kept up to date as they are the first point of contact between your system and the Internet. 

This is the reason that M*cros*ft have made browser upgrades "non optional" as they are a massive security risk.

Just be sure that you have weighed up the risks between having some add-ons and plug-ins and the security of your web browsing before you proceed.
