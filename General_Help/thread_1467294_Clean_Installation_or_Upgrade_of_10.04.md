### Post by briandu on 2010-05-01
Golden Rule - backup before dist-upgrade!!!!!

You can continuously upgrade on the fly BUT you will not derive all the goodness of the latest version - e.g ext4. The upgrade from 8.10 thru 9 to 10 means you have ext3 partitions.  So you lose out.

You need to judge when to refresh start or upgrade - this depends on what is the major change.

Basically, it is to get a complete listing of your current installed programs (in a text file) and with backup of your data, you are good to go either way; new install will give you ext4 and fresh partitioning less any junk which was accumulated over the time in previous install and you can easily restore your necessary programs less not needed programs. Originally Posted by bowens44  
from terminal:

This will list all installed

dpkg --get-selections

This will direct it to file

**dpkg --get-selections > filename**

Originally Posted by CatKiller:
To re-mark the packages from the list on the new system,

Code:

 **dpkg --set-selections < filename**
