### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-02-11
In Software & Updates we can tick Proprietary drivers for devices (restricted) and we will get updates to proprietary video drivers. But by an large, the drivers offered by Additional Drivers are stable drivers and not bleeding edge. Each new release of Ubuntu will give us newer Linux kernels and newer proprietary video drivers. Provided those newer video drivers still support our video adapter.

My video adapter is consider obsolete by Nvidia. So Additional Drivers does not offer me anything later than 340.96 this was the case when I was using 15.10 and it is still the case now I am using using 16.04. But I do think that there are drivers later than 352.63. I think it will depend on what version of Ubuntu you are using.
