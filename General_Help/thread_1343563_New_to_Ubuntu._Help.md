### Post by running_rabbit07 on 2009-12-02
Welcome to Ubuntu Forums and the Ubuntu OS!

Go to Ubuntu Software Center in your menu and get acquainted with some of the programs Ubuntu has to offer. 

To make browsing the net and watching vids and listening to music easier, you may want to install ubuntu-restricted-extras via System>Administration>Synaptic Package Manager or entering this command in a terminal ```
sudo apt-get install ubuntu-restricted-extras
``` This will install codecs, fonts, and flash to make your system look and run better.

Download and look over the manual that was sent to you when you joined. Lots of good info in it.

Visit [psychocats]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php")  where you will find loads of information to make your Ubuntu system work for you.

### Post by Vaphell on 2009-12-02
big part of that 'ubuntu requires computer skills' comes from the fact that people are used to windows because it's everywhere and different = hard. Also people buy their windows boxes with OS already installed and configured to match hardware. Linux distros are general purpose systems and they run on the wide range of the hardware out of the box, but there is a price - sometimes you need to get your hands dirty. When you install vanilla windows you get similar pains, there are always dozens of drivers to be installed manually.
It's entirely possible to configure linux to meet all needs of unexperienced users doing very basic things with their boxes. Internet? check. mail? check. music? check. movie players? check. office software? check. Once you configure the box, there is no need to touch it anymore and things just work, even for grandma.
On the other hand advantage of repositories is not to be underestimated - you can install apps with single click. How cool is that? :-) That's definitely more user friendly than scouring the net in search of dozens of setup.exe's one has to do in windows world.

I like your attitude. It's not common to see that. Often times people just run into issues with their illegal copy of Windows and think Ubuntu is the free/legal fix, and don't understand why it won't operate the same way.

I think in time your idea between Windows and Ubuntu may change, though. I got started on Ubuntu completely on accident. I built a computer, forgot to buy Windows, and just wanted to get online on my new PC - so I tried Ubuntu and I got hooked.

Now, this is my personal opinion, but I do not think Ubuntu is really that hard to use in comparison to Windows. It's really not about which one is harder, I just think Windows has such a foot print on the technological society today that it's overcome the "hard" factor and now it just looks normal to us.

The more time goes on, the more sense Ubuntu makes in its menu system and how it's laid out. As a result, Windows is the one that begins to look alienated to me - and that's pretty bad since I use Windows all of the time at work!

Anyways, you're going into Ubuntu with the right mind set. Give it time, ask questions, and you may find yourself doing what I did -  giving Ubuntu primary reign of your desktop and kicking Windows off to a small little partition at the back of the hard drive. :D
