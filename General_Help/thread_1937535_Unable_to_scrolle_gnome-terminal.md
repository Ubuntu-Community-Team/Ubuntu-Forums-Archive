This might not sound like a helpful answer but to me this idea of "scrolling" a terminal is just completely alien. A terminal provides everything needed for its output to be managed, you have paging, redirection, tailing, mutiplexing .. the ways that output can be managed are simply too many to mention. Why someone thought that a terminal needed scrollbars is, well, so un-unix. I simply never use it because there is nothing that has scrolled out view that I could possibly need.

The habit of using a scroll bar comes for a world that the terminal doesn't belong to, it a GUI afterthought trying to make a CLI somewhat less alien to its notion of interface, it has a negitive effect because the CLI isn't designed with a 'window' as its metaphor, so it breeds bad habits in that you don't use the CLI in the way that it was designed, its like operating a car with a joystick.

So, I can't help you with your problem (sorry) but I might be able to encourage you to take a different approach to the problem and use the CLI in a more productive way.

The only thing I could think you might try is to remove Gnome Terminals preferences ... otherwise I'll leave this in others hands.

best ... khay
