### Post by mrcbrown on 2007-06-02
Ubuntu is sometimes a day behind simply because they have to re-package/re-build the new source provided by Mozilla to get a package for Ubuntu users - if you want to avoid that, you could simply install the linux version of Firefox, and not the Ubuntu packaged one. would get you updates sooner.

As for Plugins - always a risk at some level, if your really want to be safe, avoid them :) but honestly most active add-on makers for Firefox tend to keep up to date if issues are found, just watch for updates.

### Post by blackened on 2007-06-02
The ability to spoof and redirect the Firefox location bar would be a problem on any OS. Say you use online banking and were unknowingly redirected to an exact replica of your bank's website where you enter your userID and password. This would give someone the ability to log into your account and do whatever damage possible through said medium (this depends on what features your online banking has: automatic billpay, money transfers, etc.). So no, this wouldn't just be a problem for Windows users.
