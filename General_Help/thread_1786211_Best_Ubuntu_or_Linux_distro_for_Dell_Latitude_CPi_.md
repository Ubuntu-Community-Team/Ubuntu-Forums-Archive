### Post by mendhak on 2011-06-26
Hi flatlined, it sounds like it's a problem with the way you're burning the DVD, rather than the OSes themselves.  You'll need to burn the ISO that you download (Ubuntu, Puppy, DSL...) to the DVD, rather than 'add' it to the disk as a data file.  

The difference in how you write to the DVD is what makes it bootable.  Where are you burning the DVDs right now, from within XP?  If so, download something called CDBurnerXP, it's free.  Use it to burn the image to disk (not write a data disk).  Then reboot with the DVD in the drive and it should get picked up. 

As for which OS... I'd suggest Lubuntu (it's a lightweight Ubuntu) or DSL or Puppy.

### Post by snowpine on 2011-06-26
Hi Flatlined, my very first Linux computer was a Dell Latitude Cpx, big brother to your Cpi (500mhz pentium 3). Unfortunately the power supply finally gave out a few months ago.

Anyways your specs are very limited and you'll have to choose distros carefully. Some of the distros I had success with on my Cpx include: CrunchBang, AntiX, SliTaz, Puppy.

Do not expect a miracle; it will still be an old and slow computer whichever distro you choose. But any of the above should allow you to do basic web surfing, as long as you avoid Flash and other multimedia-heavy pages. (There are Firefox plugins that will block Flash.)

Mendhak is on the right track suggesting you make sure you're burning your Live CDs correctly (you need to burn a disk image rather than a data disk, and obviously use a CD not a DVD). Another thing to check is that the CD-ROM drive actually works on your old laptop; they do tend to wear out over time and the CD drive on my Cpx was getting quite fried toward the end.
