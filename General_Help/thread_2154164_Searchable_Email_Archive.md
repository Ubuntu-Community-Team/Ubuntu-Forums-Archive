### Post by zerubbabel on 2013-06-13
I am using Kmail/Kontact (KDE 4.10.3) and like it very much, except for its slowness, especially on the first startup after a reboot. That's when akonadi/nepomuk seem to go crazy, as if it's re-indexing and caching every single email. When the intense disk activity finally ends, then Kmail becomes reasonably responsive, though not quick by any means. This is on a Core i5 with 4 cores running at 2.5 GHz, and 16 GB RAM.  

Anyway, I thought that if could archive a lot of my older emails it might speed it up, but I hesitate because I haven't yet found a tool (gui) that makes it easy to search within the bz2 archives that are generated with the "Archive folder..." action. Is there such a tool? Better yet would be something actually designed for maintaining a searchable email archive.

---

### Post by zerubbabel on 2013-06-17
I discovered the answer to my own question. I saved the archives as zip files (perhaps it would also work for bz2), and discovered that the Recoll search engine that I've been using for years indexes these archives just fine, and makes access to the individual emails trivially easy.
