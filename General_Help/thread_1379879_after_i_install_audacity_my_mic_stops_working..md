The biggest weakness of this OS is sound issues.  They obviously didn't do much testing on sound at ALL.  It's general across all platforms.....laptops, desktops, many brands.  It's seems that no one sat down and worked on such things as microphones and audio.  It's quite ridiculous.  Other than sound problems I haven't had much trouble at all.  But it's irritating enough for it not to be usable to me in many ways.  I was going to install Audicity myself so that I can record my music.  But I'm glad I read your issue before I installed it.  I'll wait to see if there are solutions.

The whole deal with native Alsa and Pulseaudio is ridiculous.  It causes all kinds of problems.  A fix for one problem causes yet another on some programs.

I sure hope that Ubuntu 10.4 doesn't present us with the issues that seem to have plagued others.  9.10 is my first Linux OS, but I have read the horror stories from people trying to upgrade from distro to distro.

I think the 6 month time frame between releases is unrealistic.  I think they should devote at least two years per OS and work on making each one stable and easily workable for end users like us.

If Ubuntu were more compatible without sound and other areas, it would easily replace Windows.  I will take a cue from the past and wait to upgrade to 10.4 until it's totally stable and fixes for problems are created beforehand.

I like Ubuntu.  It's more stable than Windows and I am not plagued with viruses or malware and for that I'm grateful.  There is some room for improvement, but your issue with Audicity, which I could use myself, is proof that the 6 month time frame per release is too short and unrealistic.
