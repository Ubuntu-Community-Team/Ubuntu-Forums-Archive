### Post by 3Miro on 2011-10-03
The Software Center has most of the apps available. Other developers sometimes make apps that are not necessarily included and you can download those in the form of .deb files (somewhat like .msi) or source-code (usually .tar.gz) that you have to compile yourself (compiling can be tricky). You should note that those apps have not been tested and may contain malicious scripts (for example a random .deb file may wipe out your entire HDD).

There are also commercial professional apps for Linux in general. Those come in various formats and would usually run on Ubuntu. MATLAB is one such example.
