### Post by ohnonotagain69 on 2009-02-19
Hi,

After yesterdays update on "su", when my laptop comes out of standby the screen stays blank. I'm 99% it started happening after the update.

I have tried cltr-alt-F1-F5 to bring up a console, ctrl-alt-backspace to restart X (which works, but the screen still stays blank), I can even log into a console blind. Eventually I have to restart (RSUB). This happens every time.

As it was su, I thought maybe there was a permission problem, and /sys/class/backlight/sony/brightness couldn't be set even by sudo, so I changed permissions to a+rwx, and now I could change brightness manually, but still no joy when it comes to fixing the standby problem.

The machine was working perfectly until this point. I've noticed this a lot lately; 4 out of 10 times an update is run, something little and annoying goes wrong and I have to spend an hour or so fixing it. Usually something to do with the ACPI.

I don't mean to be rude, but does anyone check for after effects of updates? I have no idea what the process is in Linux. Are we the beta testers? Or is it because ACPI isn't offically supported? 

Now, ubuntu is still better than that other OS, but it's getting to a point I'm going to start ignoring updates. Is this wise? Is the reason because I'm using an oldish version? I'm using 8.04 LTS, 64bit version and I don't really want to upgrade yet. It's a sony laptop, so standby mode is kinda important.

Any advice would be gratefully received.

### Post by ohnonotagain69 on 2009-02-22
Hmmm.

Well after two days of buggering around with /etc/acpi sleep.sh and other crap I worked out that is was the new hal-info that had been upgraded 4 hours before the sudo upgrade.

So I dug out the old .deb and force-installed and older version.
"sudo dpkg --force-overwrite -i hal-info_20081124-0ubuntu1~hardy_all.deb"

Standby/resume now works as it did before this upgrade.

So, thanks for your help. No really. Thanks.:roll:
