### Post by asmoore82 on 2010-03-20
Yes. It's *possible*.

It is also *possible* to jump off of the Empire State building.

If you have to enter your password often enough that it's bugging you,
you probably have your system misconfigured in some way.

For example, if your user account needs to edit documents on a web server everyday,
**do not** edit them as root, assign ownership of them to your account _instead_.

### Post by lisati on 2010-03-20
For everyday use of your computer - emails, browsing, using Open Office etc - there shouldn't be the need for root privileges.

As has been pointed out, there are ways of permanently elevating your user privileges; before we go there and risk having the thread closed, it might be a good idea to read the [link]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RootSudo") provided by Wojox
