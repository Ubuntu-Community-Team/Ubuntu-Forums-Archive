### Post by efflandt on 2010-03-09
Another alternative for any application that can print, is the cups-pdf package.  It sets up a PDF printer which creates a pdf file for anything printed to it as a file in ~/PDF (a PDF dir in your home dir).  That can be handy for saving things from the web, like order confirmations or receipts, maps and directions, etc.

_**A small correction:**_

You don't select "Print To File", rather, you do select to print your file through your PDF Printer.
Assuming you have a HP 1000 Printer in your home, then simply instead of printing the document through your HP 1000 Printer you choose to print it through your PDF Printer.

You said the user needs to select "Print To File" however this isn't accurate because you don't always have an option to "Print To File", you do however have an option to print through your PDF Printer.
