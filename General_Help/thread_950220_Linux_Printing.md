### Post by Topsiho on 2008-10-18
If you consider buying a new printer that just works in Linux you should consider HP. HP does a lot to make their printers work in Linux, you should google with HP printing Linux to just get some idea.

I have an All-in-one from HP, with a scanner and copying capabilities, which al just work fine, and installs quite easily: it is detected and it works.

When installing Ubuntu the HP-software (hplip) is installed automatically. If not you can always install it later.

Other manufacturers are less Linux-friendly.

And no: I do not own HP stock :)
