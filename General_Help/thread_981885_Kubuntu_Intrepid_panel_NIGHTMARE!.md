### Post by davetv on 2008-11-14
I have an Acer 3623WXCI Laptop (and several other boxes but the laptop is my work machine). I use it mainly for web browsing, mail, openoffice, programming in c++ under codeblocks using wxWidgets 2.8 and various other stuff like rosegarden (midi editor). I tried Hardy with kde4 on one of the spare boxes and was also totally disappointed - in fact - kde4 was so buggy as to be unusable. Various hangs and other problems occurred during tasks etc.

I still run Feisty on my laptop with kde3.5, install new apps once in a while  when I need/want them. It is stable, I have compiz running transparent windows ... and everything just works ... no problems. I installed the OS over 18 months ago.

The point I am trying to make is that if you want a stable production environment then stick with what works. Dont upgrade for the sake of it. I will no doubt upgrade my laptop OS and desktop in the future but until I see a need to adopt kde4 and hear glowing reports as to its stability - no chance. However - I enjoy seeing the progress of (k)ubuntu and associated apps/infrastructure and once in a while check out the advancements on a spare box.

No complaints here! But if your computer is needed to ensure your livelyhood etc ... dont fix what aint broken!
