### Post by pme 72 on 2010-10-11
With Ubuntu you just right click a open space on a panel, top or bottom, and select Add to Panel..., the Add to Panel window opens. Scroll down to Workspace Switcher, left click on Workspace Switcher to highlight and then left click on Add. The icon for Workspace Switcher installs on the panel. 

You can move the location of the icon by right clicking the icon and choosing Move. If Lock to Panel is checked you will need to un-check Lock to Panel to make the Move menu item active. Drag the icon to a preferred location and click on the desktop to make it stay put. You can re-enable Lock to Panel if desired. 

Workspace Switcher is enabled by default in Ubuntu and is located in the lower right hand corner. I seem to remember that it is also included with Debian. I would be surprised if Mint did not have something similar.
