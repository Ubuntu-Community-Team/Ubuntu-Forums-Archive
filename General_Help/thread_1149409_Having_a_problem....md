### Post by hansdown on 2009-05-05
Hi itsStephen.

I can't help with the cause of the problem (probably update), but a safer 

way to reset your machine is to hold ALT+PRINTSCREEN and type R-E-I-S-U-B.

This will safely reboot your box in cases where CTRL+ALT+DELETE doesn't

work.

You can download a printable copy of the ubuntu reference 

cheat sheet.
