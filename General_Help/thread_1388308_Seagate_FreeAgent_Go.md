I'm not using seagate's software to put anything on that drive, but I haven't formatted it or anything. If I wanted to connect it to my Ubuntu system to get to some of the files, would I need to do anything specific to make it work, or will it automount as soon as I connect it? ### Post by warfacegod on 2010-01-23
I've never heard of anybody losing their files except through their own stupidity or a hardware failure. You shouldn't have any issues with yours as long as you safely remove it from the machine it's connected to and do the same when you disconnect it from Ubuntu. In Ubuntu it's called unmount. I assume your drive is NTFS or FAT32. Probably NTFS. Ubuntu can read an NTFS drive just fine, however, it can not write to NTFS with out installing the support package for it. So, if you wish to beable to save anything to it, you'll have to do that first. Your drive should automunt just fine and an icon will appear on your desktop.
