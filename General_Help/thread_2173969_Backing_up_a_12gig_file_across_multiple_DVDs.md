### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-09-12
And depending on the file in question you could always make a .7z archive of it, which may end up much smaller, and split that if needed whilst it is being produced.  You will need to install **p7zip** to do that, which is not part of the default ubuntu installation, but is available in the repos.

### Post by Lars Noodén on 2013-09-12
If you loan them out they will get scratched up after 6 uses as most people are careless and abusive with optical discs.  But the theoretical reuse capacity of RW is very, very high.  I haven't heard of or encountered a limit at 30 or so.  I have some that are over that but they are getting scuffed up a bit.  My problem is that eventually they get scratched enough that they won't write properly.  I can avoid big scratches by being careful but the small scratches seem to happen no matter what.  Either way, I get to reuse them far beyond what it would take to pay for the difference between R and RW.
