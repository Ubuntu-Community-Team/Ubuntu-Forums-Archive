### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-09-05
320 gigabytes is too small. For only a little more, you can get a 500 gigabyte HDD. For a little more still, you can get a 1TB hard disk. Go the terrabyte. You might be able to save a couple of dollars by going with Western Digital; they are usually a little cheaper and just as good, if not better.

I'd also say, go for a 9600 rather than a 9500 - the 9600 used to be put in some gaming machines, so it's obviously worth it. Both cards will support Nvidia's VDPAU video acceleration so you've landed on your feet.

850 watts is massively over-engineered for your computer. 500 watts should be plenty for you, but as always buy a brand name (Antec, Thermaltake, Corsair or something like that).

CPU is fine, as long as you prefer multi-threaded performance to single-threaded performance. Intel tends to price their good dual-cores at the same sort of price as their entry-level quads, so if you want to be able to do one or two things fast, then a dual-core might be more appropriate for you.

Motherboard I can't comment on; it appears to be alright, but I have barely heard of the brand. There are lots of brands in the US that I haven't heard of though. I almost always buy Asus motherboards.

RAM is fine. The case is very aggressive and it looks like it has heaps of drive bays. It also has two extraneous fans that you really don't need to cool your gear. If the gamer-style design and lighting is not exactly what you want, you could have a look at something like an Antec Sonata; they are smaller, a bit quieter (in noise and visuals!), and some models come with a good 500w power supply.

Also, there's nothing wrong with using the stock Intel CPU cooler, but it won't give you much overclocking headroom, and it will be definitely audible (though you might not hear it over all the other fans in that case!). I can't comment on the best cooler for overclocking, but a Noctua CPU cooler will be among the quietest coolers you can buy. I have one that pushes my Core 2 Duo to 3.6GHz AND barely makes a noise. Excellent.

I don't see any problems in putting all those things together anyway, so if that's what you have your heart set on, then go for it.

### Post by indytim on 2009-09-05
Soon to start my 5th build.

I'd recommend the following:

1.  Opt for 32 M cache on the h/d.  Little price premium.
2.  Memory, I'd recommend staying with one of the leaders like Crucial or Corsair.
3.  Same with the m/b stay with Asus, Intel or GigaByte.

You can always find "off-brands" that will give you cheaper prices.  How does the adage go.... you get what you paid for?  It's your system, stay with quality parts you'll be much happier with the results (opinion).
