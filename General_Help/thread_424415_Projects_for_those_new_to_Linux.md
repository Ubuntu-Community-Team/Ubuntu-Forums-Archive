### Post by zolodon on 2007-04-26
As a relatively new Linux user with many years of Microsoft experience clouding how I look at things (nothing against Microsoft, but they view most things differently), I'm having trouble learning how Linux works as a whole.  I can usually follow directions to get tasks done (if properly spelled out), but I'm not learning why I should be performing those steps...versus the many other potential steps listed on the next Ubuntu forum or Google search.  I appreciate how the customization of Linux and the infinite ways of doing different tasks can be a huge win, but for those learning the ropes (like me) this just brings more confusion...which yields a lack of real troubleshooting skills (not understanding my problems enough to articulate them to others).  To help with this, I'd like to identify some projects to help me learn...

If I had a list of "to do" projects that helped me accomplish different useful tasks while also exposing me to the underpinnings of the operating system, the learning curve would be much more natural.  So, I have a question for those ahead of me on that curve...

Does you have any suggestions for beginner-intermediate projects that a newcomer to Linux could take on to get a better feeling for why and how Linux does what it does?  If it helps, think of this as a request for geek homework from a foreign exchange student hoping to move into the community.  :) 

Thanks in advance...

-Zolodon

P.S.  If you know of any great online learning sites for Linux/Ubuntu, please feel free to direct me there (as long as it's more than just reading man pages).
