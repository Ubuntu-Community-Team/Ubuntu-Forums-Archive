### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-11-21
What Ubiquity bug?

> Ubiquity is a simple graphical live CD installer designed to integrate  well with Debian- and Ubuntu-based systems, written largely in Python,  using d-i as a backend for many of its functions for ease of  maintenance.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Ubiquity](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Ubiquity)

Ubiquity is not one of those applications that is default installed on Ubuntu.  Therefore, there can be no update to the OS that will cause a bug in Ubiquity to do damage to the OS because there will be no updates to Ubiquity.

ISO images of different versions of Ubuntu will most likely have different or later versions of Ubiquity as the installer.
