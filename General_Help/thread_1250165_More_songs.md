### Post by dmonkey on 2009-08-26
It depends a bit on whether you want to just backup the mp3s or to actually play them from the CD, like with a stereo.

If you just want to archive the songs, then you can burn them as data, so they will take up the same space on a CD as on your hard drive.

Some music players out there will play mp3 files from a CD, which means that you don't need to decompress the mp3 files before burning them to CD. If you think that the average mp3 is around 4mb, then a normal CD of 700mb should hold roughly 175 songs.

This is a list of such mp3 players found on CNET:

[http://reviews.cnet.com/mp3-player-reviews/?filter=500891_11669_](http://reviews.cnet.com/mp3-player-reviews/?filter=500891_11669_)

You can also get hifi-type cd players that play mp3s.
