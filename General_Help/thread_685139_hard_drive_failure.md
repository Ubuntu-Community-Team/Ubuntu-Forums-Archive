### Post by az on 2008-02-01
No, that's not the click of death.

A drive that is damaged like that will not show in up the BIOS.

That doesn't mean that things are good, just not hopeless.

See here:

[http://help.ubuntu.com/community/DataRecovery](http://help.ubuntu.com/community/DataRecovery)

See also [http://ubuntu-rescue-remix.org](http://ubuntu-rescue-remix.org)

Also, you can try putting the drive in the freezer for a few hours before trying to image it.

### Post by macogw on 2008-02-01
Putting it in the freezer will make it contract and that *might* be enough to get data off of it, but if it's going to take a while, you might want to keep it on dry ice.  Trying to turn it on a lot is probably going to make it worse from it spinning up.  A data recovery place (pretty expensive usually....) is probably your best bet.
