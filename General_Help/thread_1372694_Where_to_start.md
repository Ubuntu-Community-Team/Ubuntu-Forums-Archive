### Post by jamesisin on 2010-01-04
Install Ubuntu on one of your machines.  Visit these forums and read any post that looks interesting.  You can subscribe to any post without having to comment, and by this you can follow a thread of support to resolution (hopefully resolution).

Keep a terminal open all the time and try out some of the basic commands.  Great ones are cd, ls, and man [somecommand].  These are exciting because they have output that will make sense to you and you can use them indefinitely without any risk of harm.

Though you can run a Linux system these days without the command line, you just shouldn't be afraid of it.  It can be very useful at times, and in troubleshooting you will likely be asked to provide terminal output of various kinds.
