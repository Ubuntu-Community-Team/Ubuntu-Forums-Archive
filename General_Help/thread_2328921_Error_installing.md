### Post by Impavidus on 2016-06-26
That may give us an error report that nobody understands except the developers of the installer, as the installer supposedly contains a bug. But we know that the installer has worked fine for many, so we may first consider other possibilities.

Boot the live disk and use the option to check the disk for defects. That's now the most likely cause of the error. If everything turn out fine, you can try the installation in little steps, with manual partitioning beforehand and skipping installation of restricted software or drivers.

With no more than 2 GB of ram, you may prefer one of the lighter flavours of Ubuntu. My favourite is Xubuntu, but you can also pick Ubuntu Mate or Lubuntu.
