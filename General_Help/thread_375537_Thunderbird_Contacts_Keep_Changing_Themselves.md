The problem is if she gets an email from "Joe", but she knows that his full name is "Joe Blow" so she puts him in as first name: Joe, last name: Blow, then the next time she opens an email from him his name will go back to being just "Joe". ### Post by kolinab on 2007-03-04
I understand what you mean. I have the same problem, and as far as I know there is not another way around it. For instance, I'll add someone to my address book with his regular name "Biff Barker," but Biff might be one of those people that his email client set up to show his messages are from "Biff the Brain Masher" or some thing. When I look in the 'sender' column in my T-bird I see all kinds of annoying variations . . . :) 

So what I do is turn off automatically adding outgoing addresses to my address book:

In Thunderbird, go to Edit . . . Preferences . . . Composition . . . Addressing. Then uncheck 'automatically add outgoing email addresses.'

Then when I reply to "Biff the Brain Masher" or something, that ridiculous name doesn't get added to my book. I add him manually as the Biff Barker he really is.
