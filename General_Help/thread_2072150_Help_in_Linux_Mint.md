### Post by cottfcfan on 2012-10-17
Hi aabed91,

The answer is that there is very little difference between Mint & Ubuntu.
Basically Linux mint is Ubuntu, with all the codecs installed by default & Cinnamon used as the desktop environment instead of Unity, plus a few other aesthetically pleasing touches.
There is nothing you can do in Linux Mint that you can't do in Ubuntu.
You can install the 2 of them side by side by partitioning your hard drive.
