### Post by Patb on 2008-04-26
Agaudio.  This is just a general response because your question has lots of different answers depending on just what tweaks and settings you have.  

It is often a good idea to have your home directory on a separate partition.  If you do, you can choose not to format that partition during a fresh install and all your settings pertaining to specific programs will be retained (and updated if necessary) when the program is next run.  
> **agaudio said:**
> ... if I do a fresh install, won't I have to install all the additional programs I have installed again?
Yes, you will.  That is why many people do an upgrade instead of a fresh install.  Every few releases, they go to the trouble of doing a complete new install to "clean out the dead wood" so to speak, ie clear out old programs they don't use and perhaps have forgotten they installed.  I have no real idea whether this is important but it seems to make sense.  

I hope this helps.  I'm sure there are lots of other useful responses/comments to add.  Please respond with more specific questions if you can.  

Cheers, Pat.
