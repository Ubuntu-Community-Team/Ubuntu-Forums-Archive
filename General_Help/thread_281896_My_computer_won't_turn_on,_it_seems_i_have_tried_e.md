### Post by catlett on 2006-10-21
Have you tried a different power connector to the motherboard? Are your mouse and keyboard plugged in all the way. (I use to have a computer with a bios option that if enabled, the computer would only start with a mouse and keyboard attached.)
Is anything new on your motherboard? That is the number one thing but it appears you already checked that. The only thing to do is take stuff off and try booting with different things connected. I would use just one stick of memory and use the on-board sound and video. Disconnect any video card, sound card or any pic boards such as a wireless adapter. Strip it down as much as possible. If you get it to boot, add things on at a time until you find the thing that fails. If you had it running and didn't add anything new, it must be a failed component. 
Hopefully you can find it by stripping it down and adding things one at a time. Sorry to not be of much help, maybe someone with more hardware experience will see the post now that it gets bumped to the top after this reply and has a better suggestion..
