### Post by whitefort on 2008-04-13
As stated above, the best thing to do in cases like this is to email them and politely point out that more and more people are starting to use Linux (which is true), and request that they support your operating system.

This kind of protest/request recently produced some significant results at the British Broadcasting Corporation. Their new 'iPlayer' used Windows Media Format and digital rights/restrictions management.

This led to a petition of over 16,000 signatures, lots and lots of emailed protests from Linux users, and even a demonstration outside the BBC's Television centre in London.

When I emailed them at an early stage in the whole thing, I was told that Linux would be supported within a few years.  Then all the protests and petitions really kicked off.  The result?  They did it in 2 months instead of 'several years.'

It's still not perfect.  Linux users can watch streaming content, but not download it, like Win/Mac users.  But it shows what can happen if we actually protest about being excluded, instead of messing with our browsers and pretending to be Windows users.
