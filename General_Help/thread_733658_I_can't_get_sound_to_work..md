### Post by balaknair on 2008-03-24
The X-Fi isn't supported on ALSA yet.
[http://www.alsa-project.org/main/index.php/Matrix:Vendor-Creative_Labs](http://www.alsa-project.org/main/index.php/Matrix:Vendor-Creative_Labs)

```

	 [Unsupported] [PCI] Card delivered to developers. Completely new architecture. Creative actively preventing support due to no datasheets being released to ALSA developers. Reverse engineering work not started due to lack of time.
 
```

### Post by Nexusx6 on 2008-03-24
Like Bala said, there are essentially no drivers for Creative Cards for Linux right now, and with Creative's disgusting track record I'm going to have to tell you to not hold your breath. Drivers, if they ever appear for 32bit, probably won't show up for at least 2 years.

For now, the best you can do is go for sound from your mobo, and truth be told for some boards its really not that bad of a fate. Though its not going to square off with dedicated sound cards, on board sound is a lot better now that it has ever been. On the 680i I gotta say its pretty decent.
