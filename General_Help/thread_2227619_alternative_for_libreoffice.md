### Post by river2 on 2014-06-05

I have used FreeOffice for a while, too. IMHO it is well coded, fast and reliable, and it has the best compatibility with Microsoft Office formats. Now I use SoftMaker Office for a few months, which includes more features and more templates, a better and extended ui, free technical support, and lets you save docx, xlsx, and pptx formats faithfully (not only open as FreeOffice) etc. What I found out and this might be interesting for all FreeOffice users: as a registered user of FreeOffice, you are entitled to get SoftMaker Office for the cheaper upgrade price (€29/$34 instead of €69/$79).
