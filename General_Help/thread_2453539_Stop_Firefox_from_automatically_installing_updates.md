### Post by Impavidus on 2020-11-12
Firefox isn't the one automatically installing updates; it's Ubuntu's package manager that does it. Go to your settings for software and updates and make sure it's configured not to install updates automatically, not even the security updates. You can set it to automatically check for updates. You'll get a pop-up when updates are available and choose to install them now or remind you later.

BTW, I think that things should be fixed if fragile, even if not yet broken. And whether you like it or not (I don't), websites change, so browsers have to change too to work with those new websites.

> **archaeometrist said:**
>  ... It's happened several times when I was working - forcing me to loose everything and re-find the pages I needed again.

No need for that. Firefox stores your session. You can just go to 'Restore Previous Session' in the Hamburger Menu or 'History'->'Restore Previous Session' in the Menu Bar and everything comes back just the way it was when you closed Firefox. The only thing you would lose would be entered data in forms you haven't yet send off to the server.

### Post by DuckHook on 2020-11-12
I'm somewhat perplexed by why this issue is even being considered a problem. As Impavidus and ajgreeny have already pointed out, it is simplicity itself to turn off auto&#8209;updating while leaving the notification on. This gives us the best of both worlds: we are alerted to the availability of updates, but can choose when to apply them.

When I am notified of Firefox updates (or T&#8209;bird or LibreOffice or any running app for that matter), I always close off the app before allowing the update to proceed. This is just prudent practice with any desktop GUI app.

So there's no need to "leave Firefox" at all. Just make a simple switch to the way your system processes updates and Bob's yer uncle.
