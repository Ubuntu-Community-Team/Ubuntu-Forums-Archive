### Post by ebash on 2008-03-09
I'm not a lawyer neither do I live in the United States, but from my understanding the clerk at the apple shop might me right. Let me explain why:

In the United States you have to respect the [Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DMCA") which criminalizes production and dissemination of technology, devices, or services that are used to circumvent measures that control access to copyrighted work. In other words it punishes the breaking of some encryption algorithms. 

I think that the communication between an iPod and a computer is somehow protected through encryption. The linux software that enables to communicate with an iPod is able to do it because the encryption keys used by iTunes and the iPod have been cracked.

This is similar to what's happening with the DVDs, technically under Linux you can't watch a DVD unless if you purchase a valid DVD software player which will  include the proper keys for decrypting the DVD. That's why many Linux distributions don't include support for playing DVDs out of the box. Under some countries this is illegal.

Personally, I'm starting to be feed up with this kind of laws, which at the end are forbidden us to use legally the goods that we have bought.
