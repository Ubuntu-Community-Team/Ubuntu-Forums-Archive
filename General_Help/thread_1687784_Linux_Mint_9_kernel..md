Hello all.

I recently put Mint 9 "Isadora" (Lucid based) on my laptop.  All went fine and Mint is pretty much Ubuntu with all the CODECs installed and different packages.  This just made for a quicker install.

My thing is that Mint 9 comes with kernel 2.6.32-21 installed.  Ubuntu Lucid comes with 2.6.32-24 and with the latest kernel being 2.6.32-28.

You can upgrade to 2.6.32-28 on Mint, but they consider it to be level 5 upgrade with that being very unstable.

Any thoughts.

---

### Post by TBABill on 2011-02-14
It's rated 5 on Mint because it is a kernel update, not because the update is dangerous. It's identified that way so you are aware that the upgrade has the highest level of potential to cause system instability should anything go wrong. That kernel is so well tested that there should be no worries upgrading to it. Mint just lets you know more info about your updates than Ubuntu...not good or bad, just different and a feature of Mint that differentiates it from Ubuntu.
