### Post by aysiu on 2010-02-01
If you are unrooted, you should be just getting over-the-air updates from your mobile provider, whoever that is (T-Mobile, Rogers, etc.). There is no need to download ROMs.

I have found, particular for the HTC Magic (I have the MyTouch 3G, otherwise known as the Magic 32B), rooted ROMs are much better. Rooted is faster than unrooted. And you can do a few other things, too (Wifi Tether, USB Tether, Underclock/Overclock).

For sheer speed and simplicity (if you do root), I would suggest the BoogieDonut .5 ROM. If you want a lot of frequent updates and full unambiguous legality, you should go with Cyanogen (whatever the latest version is). Not quite as speedy as Super D, DWang, or BoogieDonut, but still faster than stock unrooted.

Avoid Eclair ROMs like the plague. They are all buggy for Magic right now.
