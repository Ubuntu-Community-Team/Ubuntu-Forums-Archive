### Post by editrix on 2009-02-09
You need to set up a "compose" key in your System settings/Regional and accessibility/Keyboard layout.

There, you access the Xkb options tab and Enable the compose key. Most people use the right Windows key as their compose key, because otherwise it's useless in Linux.

After that, it's really easy to make characters with diacritics. Typing the Compose key, a double quote and your letter will give you ä, ë. ï etc.

Compose + apostrophe + letter gives you á, é, í etc.

You can also create your own shortcuts for characters, providing your font has them. And it depends on what language you're mainly using, and what the character encoding is.

K/Ubuntu has changed what Compose files get installed on your computer since I learned all this stuff. But you can learn more here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ComposeKey](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/ComposeKey)
and here: [http://www.ubuntugeek.com/special-characters-made-easier-in-ubuntu.html](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/special-characters-made-easier-in-ubuntu.html)
