### Post by mjpatey on 2010-05-04
I've been reading how fast Ubuntu 10.04 is on boot-up, and was thrilled after doing a fresh install just now, to note that my boot time is only 18 seconds!

That's 18 seconds to *login*.

After login, I wait for another 27 seconds before my Desktop appears.

Now, don't get me wrong, I like the ability to login quickly, but this seems a little shady. Ubuntu claims it's  reduced boot times, when all it's really done is moved the bulk of the startup time to after the login.  This is something that happens to Windows installations over time... the computer "boots", but you're stuck waiting for another minute or so for your desktop to finally appear before you can actually do anything.

So... my actual boot time is the same as it ever was, 45 seconds.  Not awful, but for the first time, I feel like Ubuntu's marketing is out-performing its OS.
