VLC has more options than any other media player I can think of except possibly the terminal version of mplayer.  I can't think of any other media player that can play nearly any file you're likely to encounter, can use skins to change its appearance, has a graphic equalizer, has controls for hue, contrast, brightness, saturation, and gamma for video, has numerous video filters, has the ability to easily crop video and change resolution, can act as a streaming media server, can convert media from any type it can play to any other type it can play, etc., etc., etc....

The main thing it's lacking is any kind of media library functionality, but that's something more common to music players like Rhythmbox or Amarok rather than to general media players.
