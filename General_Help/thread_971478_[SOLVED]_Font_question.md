### Post by Andreas1 on 2008-11-05
-open synaptic, install "msttcorefonts" (also included in "ubuntu-restricted-extras". this already includes a few fonts like comic sans and times new roman.
-go to "/home/yourname/" and create a folder called ".fonts" (due to the "." it will be hidden) and put additional fonts there. maybe you can copy them from a windows system or something.

i am not entirely sure if that's what you asked for, but i don't think there should be a difference between OpenOffice 2 and OpenOffice 2.4. they just show the fonts that are installed on your system. not sure there though, i am not a big office user at the moment. But my OpenOffice 2.4 has them, i just tried it out.
