### Post by snowpine on 2010-06-15
Hi there, to get a better answer from the community, you should give more information about your situation. For example, your hardware specs, your reasons for needing something lighter (is your boot time slow? your hard drive getting full? graphics are choppy? etc), and what applications/features you need in the final install.

"CLI" is short for "Command Line Interface." It gives you the lightest possible install, just the terminal (no GUI). You can then install whatever desktop environment and applications you like.

Here are a couple of good links on the topic:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD)
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal)

You might also be interested in Xfce or LXDE, two popular lightweight alternatives to the default Gnome.
[http://www.xubuntu.org/](http://www.xubuntu.org/)
[http://lubuntu.net/](http://lubuntu.net/)
