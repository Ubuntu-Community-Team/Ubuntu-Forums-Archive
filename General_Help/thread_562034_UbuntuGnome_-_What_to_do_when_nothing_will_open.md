### Post by Wolki on 2007-09-28
I've had such a thing happen on an unstable hard drive that for some reason got remounted readonly. I'm not saying it's a hardware defect in your case, but some checking (memtest/badblocks/etc.) certainly wouldn't hurt. Also, take a look at your disk usage to make sure you've got some free space.

[edit]Forgot to add, if you leave a terminal open somewhere and, if it happens again, try to start a program from there and see whether any error messages occur.
