### Post by nereid on 2007-02-15
Hi and welcome to the Ubuntu forums.

Yes, you should make a backup of your harddrive (which you should do even if you don't want to install Ubuntu). As it deletes your harddrive all your music will be lost if you didn't made a backup and you can sync your iPod with Ubuntu.

About the games, some will run with the help of Wine and some won't. Sure you can do almost everything that you can do on your Windows PC (if you are using Firefox or Opera already you won't notice any difference).

1) Well, thats your option.  You can choose to dual boot (so you have both windows and linux installed, on seperate HD partitions, or you can wipe your drive and only have linux.

2) Yes, if you wipe the drive completely.  No if you just shrink windows.  You can sync your ipod in linux, but be aware that you cannot play copy protected music from the iTunes store on linux.

3) Very few games work at all.  It is possible to play some on Cedega or Wine (Cedega costs money but is better, wine is free but not very current).  Look into this as it might be something you are interested in.

4) Well, any windows .exe file wont run on linux (unless wine is present, in which case it might).  There are many many linux programs you can download.  If you like to explore software, browse the Ubuntu repositories, not the internet.  It's safer, and there are 20,000 packages in the repositories, that'll take a while to get through.

5) Good luck to you, hope this helps
