### Post by audiomick on 2010-07-11
What notbitmonk described is correct for establishing a wireless network connection between your computer and an access point, but I think that is not what you were asking for.

In answer to your question: yes, you should be able to use your wireless network printer in conjunction without your wireless capable router.

To establish the connection between the printer and the router, you will need to refer to the manual of the printer. This should be fairly easy, I think, if not completely automatic.

To connect you computer to the router via wireless, see notbitmonk's post.

Once you have a connection established between printer and router, and between your computer and the router (this can be via cable or wireless), you then need to install the printer on the computer.

Go to system> administration> printer and select "new". It will look for printers. If it doesn't find any, a window will open offering ways of installing a printer. One of the options is "search for network printers". Try that. With a bit of luck, I think the computer should then just automatically find the printer and install it.

I can't give you any more detail than that, because my printer connection is a bit different (printer is connected to router via USB and appears as a LPD printer), but in your case the printer would be directly in the network, and the process should work as I described.
