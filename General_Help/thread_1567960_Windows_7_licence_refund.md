### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-09-04
> **borth92 said:**
> not to be intrusive but do you think you might give the key away if you do not receive the refund and decide to wipe it?

He's not ALLOWED to give or sell the key.  Since the machine came with Win7 preinstalled, it's undoubtedly an OEM license -- and those are tied to the physical box on which they are initially installed.

Selling or otherwise distributing that key to others is a violation of the license agreement and is illegal.

### Post by guisar on 2010-11-15
Just wanted to relay my very pleasant and quick experience with Amazon. I purchased a netbook for my daughter and called Amazon to ask about what documentation they might need from me to prove that I didn't accept the EULA, didn't intend to use the OEM system and would like a refund. I was very polite and did this over the phone. 

The system came with WIndows 7 Starter so they initially thought I wanted to upgrade but I made it clear I was just responding to the EULA and wished to get the refund. 

At first the CSR said they didn't have a procedure and they had NEVER ENCOUNTERED THIS BEFORE! I said it's a popular topic among Linux users; that I liked Amazon for their excellent customer service and the refund was unavoidable since Amazon didn't allow me to purchase a computer without it. 

She indicated there was no set price and therefore they couldn't give a refund. I was polite but insistent and indicated I understood this was slightly symbolic but I felt strongly about this. Bingo- 45sec later I got the $10 refund I asked for. I had heard (rumor mill) this was the per-machine amount Asus and netbook manufacturers had to pay...

So, if you purchase a netbook from Amazon PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE call and politely but firmly indicate you haven't accepted the EULA and would like a refund. Vendors will never know there's a market unless you let them know...
