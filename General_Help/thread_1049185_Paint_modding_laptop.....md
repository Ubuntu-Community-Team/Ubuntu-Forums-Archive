### Post by easybake on 2009-01-24
I used to make stencils a lot see[ here]("http://the-ovin.deviantart.com/gallery/#Stencils"). 

The easiest way to go about doing it is to start out with a design. (it is important to choose a design that will work as a stencil. 

Print it out on regular paper. Tape the paper to a manilla folder.

Use a razor blade to cut out the design. (Take your time here and go slow. Don't press extremely hard while cutting. Go over the lines a couple times if you need to. Messy cuts or a dull blade will make the stencil lose a lot of detail.)

When you finish cutting. Lay the stencil as flat as you can on the laptop. Any part that isn't completely flat will get overspray and ruin the sharpness of the lines.

You might want to tape the stencil down so that it doesn't move when you are spraying.

Be sure to mask off or completely cover all the areas that you don't want paint on.

Get yourself some flat spray paint. Glossy paint usually won't look right in the end. 

When you are spraying don't start the spray over the area you want paint. Start it off of the object, spray the area, and stop when you are completely off the area. (For example, if you wanted to spray paint a road you would start the spray on the left sidewalk and end the spray on the right sidewalk.)

Spray in many light lines. If you spay to slow the paint will drip.

It's better to do a light coat and then come back in 20 minutes to do another light coat. Don't try to completely satuate the area in one spray. Its better to be safe rather than get drips. 

When you are all done you can throw some clear coat on it if you want it to have a glossy  finish.
