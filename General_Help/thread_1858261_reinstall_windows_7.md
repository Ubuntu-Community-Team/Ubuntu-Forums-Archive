### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-10-12
You don't use Repair on a Windows disk to do an installation; you use that to repair a broken existing installation.

If your laptop came with Win7 preinstalled, the disk could be a Restore disk, in which case, you boot using it and it will then attempt to reinstall Win7 from scratch -- but if it finds Linux partitions on the drive, that may cause it to fail.

There's also likely to be a function key sequence you can press that will also restore the original Win7 install.  You will need to check your laptop documentation to see what is available.
