### Post by randallecook on 2011-04-14
Both of these cards are very old. If you really care about performance, I would get something newer, like less than a year old. But don't necessarily get something released a few days to weeks ago: ultra-new cards might not be well supported in Linux yet.

If other factors require you to use such old hardware, then it's not completely obvious to me which card will provide better performance on your system. It also depends on what you are planning to do graphics-wise on your system (web surfing, video watching, software development, game playing, game development, etc.).

You could look at the specifications for each, but unless Linux can take advantage of those specs, you really need to compare what Linux can do with these cards, rather than what these cards can potentially do for Linux. If you have access to both, and a system that you can play with freely, I would try them both in turn and go with whichever one feels better.

I think they were both on the market at the same time, so perhaps you could dig up some old product reviews and comparisons from a decade ago. Look at PC Magazine. They often did good comparisons, and although they were very Windows-centric at that time, you might find something useful.

It is not clear to me whether either of those cards can give you full compiz desktop effects, if you are looking for such eye candy.
