### Post by MrsUser on 2012-10-19
Ubuntu:
[http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Ubuntu](http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Ubuntu)
[http://ubuntu-manual.org/](http://ubuntu-manual.org/)

You may also want to google for "Ubuntu Unleashed".

Bash guide for beginners:
[http://tldp.org/LDP/Bash-Beginners-Guide/html/index.html](http://tldp.org/LDP/Bash-Beginners-Guide/html/index.html)

Linux - Rute User's Tutorial and Exposition:
[http://rute.2038bug.com/index.html.gz](http://rute.2038bug.com/index.html.gz)

To make a career in the Linux world, you should first decide what you want to do. Do you want to develop applications for Linux? Or do you want to develop applications using Linux? Or do you want to become a system adminstrator? Etc etc.

I guess you first have to learn more about Linux to make such decision. Then you can focus on it, decide if you want to take up a study in computer science, for instance. Or if you want to be an autodidact. After all, the major steps are always the same:

1. Get information
2. Make a decision what you want
3. Acquire knowledge & tools for what you want
4. Apply for a job you want, based on your skills
