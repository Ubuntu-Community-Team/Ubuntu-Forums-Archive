### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-12-21
Could be your internet connection. Or your ISP. Or maybe the server that holds the Fedora 20 image is very busy.

Download it through Bittorrent. It will download in tiny chunks, and if your internet connection cuts out while downloading one of the chunks, it will automatically redownload that little chunk without you even realising anything's been wrong. If a chunk gets corrupted in transit, Bittorrent will know immediately and try redownloading that chunk again.

Basically, as long as there's somebody else on Bittorrent who has the whole file, you are guaranteed to eventually end up with the whole file correctly. And for really popular torrents, it's quicker than downloading from a central server.
