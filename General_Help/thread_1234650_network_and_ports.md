### Post by tommcd on 2009-08-08
Here are 2 good articles on using the secure shell protocol (ssh) in linux:
[http://www.linux.com/archive/feature/119744](http://www.linux.com/archive/feature/119744)
[http://www.cyberciti.biz/tips/linux-unix-bsd-openssh-server-best-practices.html](http://www.cyberciti.biz/tips/linux-unix-bsd-openssh-server-best-practices.html)
To share a network between windows and linux computers you will need to understand **samba**. There are lots of tutorials on the internet about samba:
[http://samba.netfirms.com/](http://samba.netfirms.com/)
[http://www.reallylinux.com/docs/sambaserver.shtml](http://www.reallylinux.com/docs/sambaserver.shtml)
And for Ubuntu:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/6.06/ubuntu/serverguide/C/configuring-samba.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/6.06/ubuntu/serverguide/C/configuring-samba.html)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SettingUpSamba](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SettingUpSamba)
I have never actually setup a home network, so I can't help with specific questions about this. I have used ssh though. Those 2 articles on ssh are very good; and they are really all you need to know about ssh.
