### Post by elamericano on 2007-11-06
I'm using Evolution at work and we have an awful exchange server. They never supported their previous solution, so it underperformed, and there were outlook interop problems. Now they bought new equipment, much more disc space, memory, and processing power. They've instituted a disc space quota, and they've hired and exchange expert to pretty much do only e-mail support. Well, if they'd done that for our open source server, don't you think it would have performed better? :mad: What a waste of money for a calendar and e-mail!

Anyway, Evolution works pretty good, although the IT guys know nothing about it, so I have to configure it myself. Don't even ask, is my new policy. Maybe I'll pretend to have outlook when I want to know the ip address and port for SSL. It's better than Thunderbird at this point, because it will open the webdav calendar appointments and add them to the built-in calendar. Sunbird/Lightning is not there yet.

If that's not a requirement, then go with Thunderbird - that way you can pretend that you're running the windows version, and your IT administrator won't throw a fit 'cause he only knows windows.

Good luck.
