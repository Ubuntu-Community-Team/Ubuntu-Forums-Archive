### Post by efflandt on 2009-11-28
After finding that Ubuntu 9.10 on USB hard drive booted fine on my laptop after latest updates, but same USB drive would not on my desktop, except in (recovery) modes, it got me thinking.

Apparently gfxgrub or spash or something does not like the DVI video modes of the 1280x720 HDTV I connected recently in place of my DVI 1280x1024 monitor.  So as soon as grub tries to do vbe graphics, it throws up its hands and gives up trying.

Gnome has no problem with the HDTV.  In fact I swapped monitors while my PC was in standby, and it worked as 1280x1024 stretched when I woke it up, and as soon as I logged out of gnome and logged back in, gnome instantly recognized it as its native 1280x720 widescreen with no display configuration necessary.

So I switched from DVI to VGA, and suddenly grub2 booted fine.  Now gnome does not see its actual resolution, but offers an alternate 1360x768 mode (close to most 720p HDTV's) that works fine.

I'll play with GRUB_GFXMODE and splash settings in /etc/default/grub to see if I can get it working in DVI.
