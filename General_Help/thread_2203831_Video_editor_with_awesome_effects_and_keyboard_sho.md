### Post by RedRat on 2014-09-17
I would suggest that you have a dual boot computer, with Windows and Linux (whichever flavor you might prefer). Free software, even in the Windows environment that can do top quality editing, is going to be hard to find. Forget it on in Linux environment for now. Here is the ugly truth about software, both development and maintenance, is that there are many initially promising software programs but then as newer and newer developments and needs come along, it takes coding time to include them in the current versions. What worked wonderfully several years ago, may well be inadequate today because the coders/developers just plain got tired or just don't have the time to keep the progs up to date. So what you end up with are some progs that were great at one time but today are just mediocre if not very functional. Heck, I found Digikam, which was not too bad back in the Ubuntu 10 and 12 versions today under Ubuntu 14 riddled with bugs that don't seem to be addressed. 

When you have complex programs doing some sophisticated stuff, you need motivation to keep it up to date and reaching its potential. Sorry, but that motivation is money. Basically you need to pay for the program. I kind of think this open source/free software has pretty much run its course.
