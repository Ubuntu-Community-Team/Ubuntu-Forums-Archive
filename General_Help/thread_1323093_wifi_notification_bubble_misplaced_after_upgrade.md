### Post by metamatt on 2009-11-11
I recently upgraded to 9.10 (love it) but I've encountered a small glitch that I'm trying to rectify.  The network notification bubble (i.e. the one at the top-right in gnome that lets you know when you've connected to a wifi network successfully) used to pop up right beneath the panel bar, resulting in a nice clean tight aesthetic.

Post-upgrade (I was using 9.04 before) the network notification bubble now appears about an inch below the notification bar.  I wonder how those settings were changed during the upgrade...

anyways, its a small thing, but I still want to correct it and get the network notification bubble back to it's more aesthetically pleasing placement.  Any ideas?

many thanks :)

### Post by mcduck on 2009-11-11
Sorry, but the location is like it is by design, and it's not configurable (at least for now). I've understood that the first place right under the panel is reserved for important messages, while normal notifications will appear under them.

So, you can stop worrying about something being broken, it's working exactly as it's designed. And hopefully we'll get some configuration options for notifications in future Ubuntu releases...
