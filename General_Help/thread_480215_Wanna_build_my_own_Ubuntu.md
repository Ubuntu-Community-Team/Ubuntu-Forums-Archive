### Post by kuja on 2007-06-21
For the case you can get whatever you like. For a computer that cheap it probably won't be too pretty, but you'll be able to put things in it. Umm, lets see, processor wise I'd probably go with a AMD processor, they're really cheap anymore. Probalby an Athlon 64 X2 or an older Athlon 64. . If you have something against AMD then you could probably get a decent deal on a Pentium D or Pentium 4 (or equivalent celeron, though that would really hurt performance). Motherboards are all up in the air, but definitely be careful when you're picking one out, some of them can be sketchy. Look for something with integrated graphics and integrated sound - that'll save you from having to get those. For that price the biggest sacrifice in that system will probably be the quality of t he power supply and the memory. Memory usually isn't too cheap, but you can probably get a gig for about $80-$100, perhaps less if you use 512MB sticks. As per power supply I'd shoot for something in the 450watt range for a low end system like this (just to be safe), which you can probably get for <$50 depending on brand. 

Oh, and do avoid retail if you value your wallet. Online is the cheapest way to get things, though you will get hit with a shipping charge (which should be offset by not paying retail prices, and then some). TigerDirect and Newegg are pretty good places to go. Be sure to read reviews before actually buying things, it's worth the extra time spent.

### Post by longlegs on 2007-06-24
Hi.
Choose your motherboard THEN make sure your case/powersupply will handle it.
Don't go with celeron cpu on mobo.
A 2g cpu with fast FB on mobo is reasonable speed and price.
Ubuntu has all kinds of problems with SB sound cards, get on-board AC97 sound.
Make sure mobo can boot from USB, (this is in bios) Make sure the mobo supports USB 2.0. If it has built in network card that's fine. 
I have ATI All in Wonder 128 Video with TV in and it works fine with ubuntu and knoppix (Tyan 2266 mobo). I have an older Dell machine with ATI rage video card. It works with ubuntu 5.1-7.04 and Knoppix 3.7-5.1
Use your hi-fi stereo system for listening to music, so you can get a cheapy set of speakers for your computer. (personal opinion)
Get the biggest flat screen (LCD) monitor you can afford after building your computer. I have a 4 year old Syncmaster 912N that is great. BTW for a 19 inch screen, 1024x768 is a good resolution, so don't pay much for anything over 1280x unless you have really good eyes or a larger monitor.
I use SONY DVD burner but several other brands are as good and probably less expensive.

Good luck
longlegs
