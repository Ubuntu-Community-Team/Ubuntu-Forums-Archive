Have not seen any Packard Bell computers for a while, since they stopped selling them in the US.

If the fan is attached to the top of the heatsink with 4 screws and has a 2, 3, or 4 wire connector that plugs into the mother board, then most likely is is a standard type of a fan. Power down the system, unscrew the 4 screws, unplug it from the mother board, take it with you to your local computer shop, and get the closest match that you can, if it is a different thickness you will probably need new screws as well. 

If it is a non-standard fan, you should be able to find out pretty quickly when you try to match the replacement by size/connector type. The 2 and 3 wire connectors are the same, the third wire that is missing on the 2 wire fan is for monitoring the fan speed. The 4 wire connector is very similar to the 2/3 wire connector, but a little bit wider to allow for the 4th wire that allows the fan speed to be controlled, if the existing fan is 4 wire, the place were you plug it in is usually designed to allow a 3 wire fan to be plugged in, you just lose the speed control.

If the fan attaches to the case and a plastic housing is used to funnel the air through the heat sink, then you may have more difficulty getting a replacement fan as these are more likely to either be a different size than your normal case fan or have an odd connector and possibly custom heat sensing mechanism that makes it difficult or impossible to use a fan that was not designed specifically for your system. 

If it's a smaller form factor case than normal, then you never know what you are going to run into, the smaller form factor designs often use non-standard fans.
