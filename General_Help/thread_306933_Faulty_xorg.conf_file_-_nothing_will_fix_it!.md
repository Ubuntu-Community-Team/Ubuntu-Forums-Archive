### Post by CatKiller on 2006-11-25
You could try using the **vesa** driver in your xorg.conf (sudo nano -B /etc/X11/xorg.conf if you're at the command line) to get some sanity back - it won't be pretty, but it will be graphical.

> The Ubuntu live CD will not even mount my /home folders.

Yes it will. It just doesn't by default, since the whole point of the live cd is to try the environment **without affecting any previous installs**. There are already many posts that will tell you how to mount a partition from the live cd.

### Post by CatKiller on 2006-11-25
> **sarc said:**
> How do I enable VESA on xorg.conf (and how do I disable NVIDIA)

In the **Driver** line of your **Device** section, change it to ```
    Driver    "vesa"
``` instead of whatever you've ended up with. Then you can come back here and post your specs and your xorg.conf and whatever error messages you're getting in the hope that someone can help you fix it.

I hadn't realised you were on Edgy. I think there was some kind of change with Edgy that made the drivers take an extra step. Something to do with the kernel headers or the restricted modules or something. Possibly that's why it wasn't working for  you.

> Re. LiveCD, if I backyp my /home, reinstall Edgy, then copy my /home back, will I get back my settings, installed software, preferecnes etc. etc.?

You can preserve your settings and preferences that way, but you'll need to install your software again. There are methods to keep the packages so that you only need to install them again without downloading if you're on dial-up, but I don't know exactly what they are.

It's for this kind of purpose that some people have a [separate /home partition]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome"). You might consider that if you're planning on breaking it again :)
