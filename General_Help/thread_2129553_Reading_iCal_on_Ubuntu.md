Most of the Ubuntu users have Thunderbird/Sunbird installed and can typically read the calendar system after a bit of setup. ### Post by gifford on 2013-03-26
You could try using Google Calendar to see if it is better at the invites. Thunderbird can sync quite well with Google. You would sync the iCal with google, then sync thunderbird/lightning with the google calendar.
Here is a link that might help. [http://superuser.com/questions/489189/accept-and-decline-button-missing-in-thunderbird-when-receiving-an-invitation](http://superuser.com/questions/489189/accept-and-decline-button-missing-in-thunderbird-when-receiving-an-invitation)

### Post by tartalo on 2013-03-26
Google Calendar doesn't use ical or caldav anymore.

KDE has been working on the problem for a long time, with Akonadi as the center of the system and Kontact / Korganizer as the front-end. I don't know if this will solve your problem, but I would give it a try.

It's my understanding, and maybe I was not clear enough in my first post, that iCal will sync with Google Calendar. So after the syncing with iCal and Google calendar is done, sync Thunderbird/lightning with the Google calendar.
