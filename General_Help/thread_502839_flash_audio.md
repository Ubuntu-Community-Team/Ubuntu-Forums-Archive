### Post by brent113 on 2007-07-17
It's possible using a flash decompiler.  [http://www.eltima.com/products/flashdecompiler/](http://www.eltima.com/products/flashdecompiler/) is a good one.  This will give you an "approximation" or the original source, including the source audio files.  You will need flash for this.

A different option is to use a soundcard output capture to record audio. [http://www.sound-recorder.com/](http://www.sound-recorder.com/)
