You can open the two folders from the desktop. There are three ways go go about this:
1. You can go to your home folder then go to desktop and open the two folders.
2. You can open folder middle click the up button and open the other folder.
3. You need the side pan enabled for this one(click view and check the box for side pan). When you open one folder you will be able to see all the folders in the directory you are currently in(all the folders on the desktop in this case) you can middle click any one of those folders and another tab will pop up. You can also navigate through any file tree from the side pan and do the same thing.

Hello,

You simply press ALT-key + any of the digit keys. Please note, they are ordered from 1 - 0, i.e. 1 takes you to the 1st tab while 0 takes you to the 10th tab (would you use that many tabs...;)).

Also bear in mind that this is related to each split pane (toggle this with F3-key). In other words you need to toggle focus (by F6-key) between the two split panes and then choose the tab. Heck, this way you can cheat your way to shortcut key control of 20 tabs after all! :)

Cheers!
