### Post by 4Wheeler on 2009-10-25
I installed Ubuntu 9.04 last week and have a dual boot with Windows XP.  All of my personal documents (music, pictures and regular documents) are stored on an external hard drive.  If I want to access an MP3 on the external hard drive, I have to click through several folders just to reach the song.  Can I create a link between my Home folder and the documents on the external hard drive for faster access?  Any help would  be appreciated.  Thank you.

### Post by fluffman86 on 2009-10-25
Right click on the folder in your external drive you want a copy of, then click "make link"

You can drag that link anywhere you want (like to your desktop) and it will stay connected to the folder you want.

Also, you can use Rhythmbox or Banshee (my favorite) music players to search for music and play it from there.

---

### Post by mgmiller on 2009-10-25
You can also create a bookmark in Nautilus (your file browser).

Once you have clicked through all the folders to display the folder you want, at the top of the window, click on Bookmarks > Add Bookmark.

This will put an entry in your "Places" drop down in the top panel.

I prefer this method to the symnlink (shortcuts) on the desktop solution.  I like to keep my desktop as uncluttered as possible.
