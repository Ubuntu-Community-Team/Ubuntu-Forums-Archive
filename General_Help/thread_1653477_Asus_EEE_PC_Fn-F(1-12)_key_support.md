### Post by tredegar on 2010-12-27
EEEbuntu probably has the **eee-acpi acpi-eee** or **eee-control** module installed, because it has been specially customised for the EEE line of computers. You can check with **lsmod** on EEEbuntu to see if it (or something similar) is listed.
"Regular ubuntu" does not, I'd guess, but you can check again on that, and work out what the difference between the distros is.
