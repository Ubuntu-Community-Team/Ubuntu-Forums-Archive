### Post by geraldm on 2008-06-15
There is another distro, see [http://www.gNewSense.org](http://www.gNewSense.org) that has a similar purpose as Gobuntu.  They have testing of liveCD; but I do not know the version details.

If you are looking for wireless drivers, you should realize your choices are much more limited in terms of which wireless device to own.  The ones that need 
proprietary drivers are much more likely to stay needing drivers unless a very dedicated driver developer is hacking on it.  

Likewise, you can get get your chosen distro to use proprietary games which runs under wine.  But when you do choose Gobuntu or similar distro, you should 
expect to be able to do much of the work yourself, as it goes against the stated purpose of the distro.  It is less likely that they would implement a non-free repository with the programs you would like to use.

I think Gobuntu and gNewSense distros use volunteers for their development, and the usual answer is "it is ready when it is ready".  (I could be wrong about their staffing)

Gerald
