### Post by cj.surrusco on 2010-07-12
I think that a hard drive uses about 15 watts on average. Though the USB drive would use much less, the slower read/write times would take a possibly larger toll on the power consumption of the rest of your computer, like the CPU. But if the PC is left idling a lot, you might save a *little* power. If you're looking to save power, maybe you should look into a more eco-friendly CPU or PSU.

### Post by libssd on 2010-07-12
I do all my test installations on an 8gb USB flash drive or SDHC memory card. They run acceptably, but are sluggish. If you replaced the HDD with a 32gb SSD, you should save a little bit of energy, but it's going to cost you around $100 (and up).

### Post by bobcollard on 2010-07-12
You are trying to accomplish a SSD (Solid State Drive) which in itself is efficient, but what dragos2 said about the power supply still applies.  It uses a finite amount of power whether the computer is at rest or not, unless the screen is asleep and the HD is not turning.  Buy a netbook and save money.
