### Post by mcduck on 2013-05-18

You could of course relocate it inside the case, or use a case where the power button is behind a lockable door or something.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-05-18
Do more than unplug the power on/off switch - unplug it and short the link out so the connection to the motherboard is still made. Is there a power off switch on the back of the of the PSU? Use that to power off while you do this. The case power off  switch is more than a simple make/break switch. There are sensors on the motherboard to detect when it is held in for more than 4 seconds. That link to the motherboard needs to be maintained.

You could also detach the power on/off switch from the case and let it dangle inside. Put tape over the hole. Or a rubber grommit to plug the  hole.
