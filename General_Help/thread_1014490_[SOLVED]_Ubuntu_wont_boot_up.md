### Post by cta16 on 2008-12-17
For some strange reason, my laptop is not booting up at all. I haven't upgraded an packages or installed anything recently. This just seems out of the blue. When I turn it on, it doesn't even go as far as showing Grub. It doesn't even show the HP's logo (my laptop is made by HP). It just displays a black screen. I've tried to pop in a Ubuntu live CD into the optical drive but it doesn't even boot from that. Any ideas how I can approach this?

My specs are:
OS: Ubuntu 8.04 dual boot Vista (both 32 bit)
Laptop: HP Tx1120us

Any help would be appreciated as I have a presentation in 2 days and everything is on my laptop.

EDIT: After looking around, I've found other users of the same laptop that have the same problem. Many of them strikingly similar (mother board and wifi failing just after 1 year of usage). [This page]("http://forums13.itrc.hp.com/service/forums/questionanswer.do?admit=109447627+1229585649957+28353475&threadId=1268501") shows some other people who have the same problem. 

The problem is, even if HP replaced the whole laptop and gave you a completely new one, it would likely fail within another 2 years. Therefore, even if you get a new one, it won't solve the problem. This goes for all Tx1000 series and probably Tx2000 series as well.
