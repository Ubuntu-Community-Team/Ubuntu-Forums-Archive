### Post by snowpine on 2008-10-30
Ubuntu releases a new version every 6 months. Most are supported for 18 months. This means you can download security updates, bug fixes, and additional packages for 18 months. Every 2 years comes a LTS (long term support) release. These are supported for 3 years.

Some people choose to upgrade every 6 months to get the latest and greatest features. Others choose to use only the LTS versions and upgrade every 2 years so that they have a stable and familiar system. It is really a matter of personal preference and what your needs are. 

Hope that helps explain things. :)
