### Post by Zorael on 2008-06-25
I **suggest** you also try Kubuntu. It's another desktop environment with the same linux underneath. While Ubuntu uses [GNOME](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME), Kubuntu uses [KDE](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE). There are also several lighter environments, such as Xubuntu's [Xfce](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xfce), if you want to keep things really slimmed down.

The point is; burn live cds of those you feel might be candidates, boot each up and try it out, ***then*** decide. There is no clear-cut answer as to which "should" be used. I defaulted to Ubuntu, then found Kubuntu a few months afterwards and retroactively went back to change all my systems over to it. I put Xubuntu on a slower laptop and its environment (Xfce) also has its merits.

As for 32-bit vs 64-bit, there's a good sticky about that over on the 64-bit forums. As a rule, you don't *need* 64-bit unless you plan to use more than 4gb of internal RAM memory, or do some heavy multimedia editing. If you want to make the most of your hardware though, 64-bit is the way to go.
