### Post by Vaphell on 2012-12-22
serif, sans-serif, monospace etc are merely abstract families of fonts grouping real fonts according to their common characteristics.
Sans-serif is resolved by the system: windows picks Arial, Ubuntu something like Dejavu Sans (Arial being proprietary is not even installed). In browser prefs you can override the defaults and set some particular font.

Then there is difference between rendering engines of windows and linux (patents involved too), even the same font will look slightly different.
