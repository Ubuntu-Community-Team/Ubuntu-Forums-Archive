### Post by crazy bird on 2011-11-22
> Before I go through that, are you the Processor wont limit upgrade of Ram to lets say 4GB? Also I have DDR1 memory?
50% true, the other 50% is determined by the limitation of your motherboard.
If your motherboard doesn't support more then (let's say) 2 gig, then there's no reason to put 4 gig in your system.
 
The other 50% is indeed determined by your cpu. A 32-bit cpu supports up to 4 gig, only 64-bit cpu's supports more then 4 gig RAM.
 
So,in order to upgrade your RAM, check the manual of your motherboard first. The maximum RAM given in the manual is the maximum RAM you can effectively use. There's no reason to go beyond that limitation since the BIOS won't see more RAM then it is programmed for.
