### Post by earthpigg on 2009-08-16
linux is a *multi-user-system*.

it assumes that if jefferson is logged in, all the applications should start with jefferson's defaults. in this case, it seems your brother replaced your pidgin info with his own. 

create an account for your brother, then log out and let him log in. then, when he starts pidgin or firefox or anything, it will start with his preferences and his bookmarks, etc.

system -> admin -> users and groups.

from then on, ensure jefferson is logged in if jefferson is using computer and jefferson-brother is logged in if he is using the computer. it takes about 5 seconds, and your bro can have his own firefox bookmarks, pidgin accounts, desktop background, etc etc, and be unable to mess with your stuff by mistake.

### Post by lessgov2007 on 2009-08-16
You could run multiple accounts on Pidgin, but it's a pain. What you'd have to do each time is disable your brothers accounts for each account, then enable each of your own. Would be annoying. Best thing to do is to just create a second user account for your brother, which would keep things clean on your account, and also give you the option of preventing brother from doing things you don't want him to do on the computer, such as deleting your stuff by accident, etc. If you create an account for bro, you don't even need to log out, you can simply switch users. I'd recommend logging out though, keeps from using excess system resources to run both accounts at the same time I suppose.
