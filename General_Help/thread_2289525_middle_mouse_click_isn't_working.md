In browsers, like chrome and firefox, if you middle click on a link, it'll open in a new tab.

I think middle click is also the clipboard function you can highlight some text and copy it into a text editor or a terminal using the middle click.

Also, in Ubuntu with the unity desktop you can use the middle click to open a new session for an already running app.
(Example if you have gedit open already but want to open a new gedit window, you can middle click on the gedit icon in the launcher to open a brand new gedit window.)

### Post by deadflowr on 2015-08-05
If you mean what I think you mean.
Does doing this help

In firefox go to Edit (you can press Alt + E to open Edit).
Then go down to Preferences.
In Preferences go to Advanced.
In Advanced go to General.
Enable Autoscrolling. (set a check mark to it)

Close preferences tab and you should now be able to scroll with mouse click.

If that's what you want.
