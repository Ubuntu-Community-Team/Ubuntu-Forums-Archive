### Post by AlanRogers on 2007-01-24
> **Shatrat said:**
> DVD-Rs are cheap per gig but not convenient for stuff you access often.
If you have the space in your case and the dough in your pocket another internal hard drive would probably be the most convenient solution.  I'm not real fond of externals since they all seem to have shortened lifespans.I'd endorse this approach too and the advice I give to those that ask me is simple:[LIST]
[*]For everyday stuff, differential backup to another hard drive (internal or external)
[*]For stuff you really want to keep, like photographs, move to DVD (more than one copy)
[/LIST]This means that you've got a backup of the files that you're working on regularly and stuff that's really important but not accessed all that often, such as photos, you've got more than one backup on a medium which has no moving parts = less chance of failure.

NB: Any backup is only ever as good as the last time you verified it. Periodically, restore an unimportant file, to confirm that the backup is good. If you leave it until you need to use it in anger ...
