### Post by Phurious on 2007-12-16
When I build custom systems we use vinyl dye to color the face plates of CD/DVD-ROMS to match color.  You can buy it at most automotive stores or on line.  The benefit of the dye versus paint is if the faceplate gets scratched once painted, it looks like just that - a piece of plastic that was painted.  Vinyl dye actually penetrates the surface of the item being dyed.

I'm sorry, but that's a terrible idea.  Those plastic front covers are not normally air tight, so you could easily end up injecting small amounts of paint and what not into the drive itselft.  Taking the fronts off of those things takes all of 7 seconds.

> **Phurious said:**
> The benefit of the dye versus paint is if the faceplate gets scratched once painted, it looks like just that - a piece of plastic that was painted.  Vinyl dye actually penetrates the surface of the item being dyed.

This man has a point.  Normally when I paint these things I'm also painting the case.  I use fairly high quality automotive paint, putting down primer first and then a clear.  But if your just painting the drive covers and not getting into anything else this may be the most efficent way to get a permanent look without a ton of trouble.
