### Post by The Flying Penguin on 2010-04-30
The upgrade should preserve your home folder but regardless I highly recommend you backup your home folder before performing an O/S upgrade. I know some people have had problems when performing an upgrade and there is always a potential risk of data loss.

In the future you could put your home directory on a separate partition which should leave it untouched during an upgrade and even a clean install. Perhaps that is a good excuse for you to just backup all your data and perform a clean install. I personally never do the upgrade. I always perform a clean install, perhaps a habit that has carried over from being a Windows user for so long. A clean install may not take that long anyway. It took me about 20 minutes to install Lucid from start to finish on my ul30vt with  a 1.3ghz Core 2 Duo SU7300. Your mileage may vary but IMO its faster to do a clean install then to have fix problems that may occur during an upgrade.

No benchmark of any kind but startup/shutdown is FAST. I haven't had enough time to really play around but everything seems quite responsive. I'm not sure how much more responsive Lucid is than Karmic but it seems to run pretty smooth so far.
