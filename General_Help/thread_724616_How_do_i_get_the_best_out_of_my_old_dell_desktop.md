### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-03-14
For the old system, that would be really snappy with Puppy... but if you want to stick to Ubuntu, try Xubuntu. It's lighter, and doesn't use Nautilus, which seems to be a little resource needy.

what is different about enlightenment, as opposed to Gnome or KDE, is the stock version doesn't have all of the features. You have to add them all one by one. Everything from the panel to the menus, to the file manager.

It's labor intensive to start, but once it is up and running, it works the way you want it to, since you put it together.

You can pick up one of the variants of Ubuntu, fluxbuntu, or OpenGUE (formerly Geubuntu) Just google them and I am sure you can find their web pages. They are complete OS installs though, so bare that in mind.

Leo
