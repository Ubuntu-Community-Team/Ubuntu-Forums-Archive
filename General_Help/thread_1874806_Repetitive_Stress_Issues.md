Don't be so quick to knock keyboard shortcuts. They have been the single biggest factor to help me get my wrist issues under control. ### Post by fragos on 2011-11-06
I've carpel tunnel syndrome which at one point was so bad I couldn't even button my shirt. My recommendations are based on personal experience with my particular affliction but I believe they can also be helpful in other situations. What worked well for me was a separate USB touchpad which I could hold in my hands and easily switch right or left side of the keyboard operation as well. Still there are times when a mouse is just better for the job. In Nautilus change from double to single click -- that will help as well. When using a mouse make sure your arm is well supported. For this I moved the mouse pad farther back on my desk and rest my arm on an Ergo beads bag positioned just forward of the elbow. To vary things more I also use a Wacom tablet with a stylus. This allows me to choose the method that is most comfortable at any particular time. All three devices control the cursor with Ubuntu responding to which ever one it senses motion from. This works well for me and I now have very little numbness in the tip of my thumb making buttons once again usable.

My advice, borne from experience:

1. See your GP to check whether or not it is RSI.

2. See your physiotherapist to check if your posture can be improved.

3. Perhaps consult a ergonomist on how to position desk, chair, keyboard, mouse, display etc.

4. In any case, take regular breaks, do some exercise, change your position at your desk regularly, stop in time (I'm going offline in a minute...), live healthy etc.
