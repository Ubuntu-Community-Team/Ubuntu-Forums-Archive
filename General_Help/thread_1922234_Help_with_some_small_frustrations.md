### Post by kyphi on 2012-02-10
Since the switch from Gnome 2 to Gnome 3, many Linux users are experiencing frustration at not being able to do what used to be very simple customisations.

There is no "effects" program entry.

The option of using Gnome without effects is an available option for those whose graphics cards are incapable of producing the effects built into the full desktop environment that Gnome 3 can provide.
Gnome 3 has raised the bar just like Microsoft used to do regularly and to get effects you will have to upgrade your equipment.

Gnome 3 can be customised, however, by adding "extensions".

[https://extensions.gnome.org/](https://extensions.gnome.org/)

There is a "System Settings" entry available when you right click on your name in the top right corner of the top panel.
