I have repeatedly found that HP supports Linux well (you will need to install [hplip-gui]("apt:hplip-gui") from the repositories). Although HP is sometimes (but not always) a tad more expensive than other makes, generally you don't need to fuss and faff to get it to work.

### Post by tjoff on 2012-02-13
Not too costly in terms of purchasing price, or operating costs (cost per page printed)? Most often there is a strong inverse relationship here.
Your total cost of ownership will depend heavily on how much you are going to use the printer.
I use a HP OfficeJet 6500A+ on Ubuntu 10.04, and I works great for both printing and scanning over the wireless network. It is not the cheapest to buy, but the cost per page is one of the lowest I could find.
