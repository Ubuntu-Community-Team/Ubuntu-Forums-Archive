### Post by gradedcheese on 2007-02-12
I would like to mention that the reason that ATI's (and others') drivers stink is that they are **binary blobs** (ie: proprietary modules without source).  These types of drivers are not considered to be acceptable in the Kernel, and they pose a [security]("http://www.linuxsecurity.com/content/view/126516") and stability risk.  They are also the reason that users have trouble with [kernel upgrades]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=357767") (as developers cannot recompile them for new kernels) and architecture issues.  The biggest culprits are graphics card and WiFi drivers.

Please ask the manufacturers to either:
[LIST]
[*]release open source drivers
[*]or work with the community on open source drivers
[*]or just release enough specifications and documentation for the community to write a driver (free of charge!  such a deal)
[/LIST]

...and you will have a much more pleasant Linux experience.  If you settle for [binary blobs]("http://www.openbsd.org/lyrics.html#39"), you're setting yourself up for a world of pain and instability in the future.

A (somewhat) good example is Intel, who recently provided some specs to the community and worked with them to develop [high-quality drivers]("http://ipw2200.sourceforge.net/") for their WiFi chipset.  A bad example are ATI and Nvidia.  If/when I buy a new laptop, they can be assured that I will not install their drivers, cute 3D graphics be damned.  I will also choose a laptop whose onboard WiFi is supported by a high quality open source driver.
