### Post by Scott Baker on 2012-03-13
Howdy Silas. You actually need to set up your mail through one of the mail programs. The 2 most popular are "Empathy", which was the default through Ubuntu for a long time, or Mozillas "Thunderbird" package. Both have their pros and cons (read the reviews when you look for them in the software center). A lot of us have tried both, and have seen the good and the bad. I've used Empathy until recently, but switched recently to Thunderbird, because Empathy had a lot of extra functions that I really didn't need. Set up on these is normally pretty fast. Don't forget that you'll want to tweak your settings afterwards (especially with regard to junk/spam settings). When you check your installed software, check under "internet" and you'll find one (or both) installed. All you need to do is pick one, and start setting it up. It's very easy, and will walk you through the whole process. (And so you know, for me, the Thunderbird set-up seemed to be a bit easier)  \\:D/
