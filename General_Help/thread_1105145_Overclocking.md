### Post by James_Lochhead on 2009-03-24
Basically you need a good knowledge of hardware, especially cooling. I suggest you have a poke around on google. There are lots of techies in Ubuntu but I doubt many of them bother with overclocking a Linux machine - it would be better to look for specific websites/forums on overclocking.

By the way, I don't actually think overclocking is worth it unless you're going to be gaming.

### Post by 3Miro on 2009-03-24
My take on overclocking is that you shouldn't do it unless you understand very well what you are doing, and if you understand everything very well, you probably wouldn't be overclocking anyway.

Linux takes so little resources that it runs just fine even on a very old CPU. Unless you have a specific app that you wish to run faster (i.e. movie conversion, some game ...) then it makes no sense to OC.

(I wouldn't OC my system no matter what, I would sooner spend a huge amount of money upgrade than OC)
