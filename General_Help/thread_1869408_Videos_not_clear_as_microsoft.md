### Post by jnorthr on 2011-10-25
The video we see on screen is produced from a piece of software called a device driver. Drivers are written by the vendor of the hardware and are typically proprietary - meaning they won't tell us how/why the video card works or produces the signals to your monitor, so we have to guess at these things. Most we can come close to what the vendor produces but vendors want to lock you and your computer manufacturer into their products so they refuse to give out details especially to the open source community. It's a tough job and many time we come close to producing a compatible driver, but some times it is not as close a result. Blame the video vendor, they make life hard on the open source community and so you (the buyer) ends up with less than pleasing video. ](*,)
