Since you have a budget, I would suggest either a gigabyte or asus motherboard, AMD FX6300 or 6350 and 16gigs of mem if you can afford it. ### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2015-03-18
CPU: AMD or Intel will both work fine, but they have their pros and cons. I like the Pentium G3258: It's cheap, power-efficient, and has great single-threaded performance. If you're doing a lot of multi-threaded tasks (video encoding, VM's and/or a ton of web browser tabs), you may find it a bit lacking. FX-6300 is probably better in that case.

Mobo: I think Phoronix has some nice reviews. [http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=category&item=Motherboards](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=category&item=Motherboards) . Most mobos should work just as well on Linux as they do on Windows, though don't expect tech support from mobo companies...

GPU: GTX 750Ti 2GB. It's an ideal card if you're gaming at 1080p. You may have to boot with nomodeset parameter so you can install Linux and setup the proprietary driver, but once that's done, you're good to go.

Power supply: Don't overspend on high-wattage units (380W-500W will be plenty for a system like this). Don't underspend on bargain basement brands. Something like a Corsair CX430, Antec Earthwatts 380 or Seasonic 380W seems ideal.

Case: It's not like you can get one incompatible with Linux. Personal preference dominates here.

> I would suggest ... 16gigs of mem if you can afford it.
Disagree (adamantly). Based on the budget, the money would be better spent elsewhere, and based on the intended usage of the system, 16GB would a total waste.
I have 4GB of RAM and I never find myself wanting more. I do lots of video encoding and even run the occasional VM, and I've never seen the free command report more than 3GB used.
