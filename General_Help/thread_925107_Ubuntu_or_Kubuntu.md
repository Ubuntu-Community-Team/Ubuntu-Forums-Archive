### Post by howefield on 2008-09-20
Both Ubuntu and Kubuntu are essentially the same apart from the desktop manager.

Gnome which comes with Ubuntu is a little lighter on resources than KDE which comes with Kubuntu, but both should run well enough on your computer.

There is no reason why you can't have both, whichever desktop you start with, (my preference is Gnome) you can add the other by installing the relevant desktop packages and switch between them and you'll quickly get an idea of which you want to stick with.

### Post by sayakb on 2008-09-20
Definitely you can. Plus, even if you have ubuntu, you can get the whole KDE package altogether. You have to install the **kubuntu-desktop** package. After you do that, you can click on the **Sessions** option at your login screen to choose between Gnome or KDE. Also after doing that, you shall be able to run KDE apps under Gnome.

Btw, installing both KDE and Gnome might make the menus a messy. So you might have to uncheck a few apps off the menu!
