### Post by larryfroot on 2007-10-16
Well I haven't got any psychedelic armour but i do have a really cool pair of swimming trunks with "Swimming Trunk Hero" written across them witha special hat that lights up and says "Geek" whenever I'm near an elephant using linux...

OK then...get the default table up and running in your document...then right click in the first cell, I suppose that would be top left. Then a table menu comes up when you right click. One of those options is 'column' - go into that and choose 'insert...' and then enter the number of columns you wish to have. Then hey presto...

Another way of creating columns out of rows would be to press the shift key and keep it pressed whilst you click in adjacent cells going downwards for as long as you wish the column to be. The highlighted cells will be blacked out. Then release the shift key. Then right click **on any part of the black area** and choose cell...then choose merge. These will turn all those separate cells into on single row or column depending on which direction you wish to go in. Merging cells can give you some pretty complex and flexible tables..play with it. One thing you can do to get an idea of its power is to create a table 5 rows and 3 columns. In the first column press shift and hold it down whilst you highlight the first three rows of that column. Then release shift key and right click on any part of the blackened area...choose 'cell' then 'merge' Now check out the table you just made. It can make for some excellent layout options, can 'cell merge.'

Hope this helps...and I do like your stick by the way. Bit of a stick man myself, actually...

### Post by larryfroot on 2007-10-17
Hallo again,

If you use the insert table from the table menu on the tool bar you are given the option of declaring how many rows or columns you want. Selecting 1 row and two columns looks like you only have one row, split into two. The reason why it looks like a row of two cells rather than two columns is that the columns at this point are very short. They will get longer as you input more text, pictures etc into them. 

I am doing just that with my version of openoffice to make sure. And they do drop down as you put more text into them. And you end up with two columns. 

And merge cell is always an option...if you are going to be using tables it is well worth getting to know it!

I am currently bashing my head against it. I sort of thought it would be straightforward as I am more used to dealing with tables in HTML than in openoffice. 

But I will give it a real try if this workaround doesn't give you the effect of having two separate columns. 

So, a workaround suggestion? Have three columns, but set the border outline to none, then use the first and third columns...the second providing the space in the middle. If however you want borders around the text / images in the first and second columns...hold on I am just going to try this out...yep...I have set the border to none...set the background to blue just for the hell of it...then filled first and third columns with text. Now we have two columns separated. However you might want a box out effect, that is a separate outline around each column without giving the game away as to the third columns existence. 

On the menu bar, underneath the top menu bar is one full of icons. near the right hand end there is an icon of a brush painting a blue curve.

Select that. At the bottom of the document you will see a new set of icons.

Choose the square. You will be presented with a cross hair cursor as you mouse over the document rather than the usual cursor arrow. You can get the cursor back at any time by pressing the arrow icon at the far left of the new drawing icons bar. Go to the top left corner of the first column. Now press and (keeping the mouse button pressed) drag out a square - or indeed an oblong that will cover the text you have inserted into the column. When you have finished, release the mouse button and you will have an oblong with whatever border you have selected for it covering your text! 

This is not a problem to fix. Right click on the oblong you have drawn and pick 'arrange' from the menu...then 'to background'. Your border will be in place and the text visible. 

There are little green boxes around the square or oblong. Just place the cross hair cursor over them and you are presented with a double headed arrow, so you can drag their dimensions around to get them right. Mouse over the box itself and you can press and drag it to a new position. 

It isn't too time-consuming and it does at least visually give you the layout that you are looking for.

### Post by Zill on 2007-10-17
Not quite sure if this is what you are after but it is worth a try :-)

Writer v2.02

Format, Columns, select number of columns required (eg. 3).
This will split the page vertically into three columns but you will find that you can only type anything in column 1.

To be able to type into the other two columns the "direct cursor" function needs to be temporarily enabled:
Tools, Options, check "Direct cursor" box.
It should then be possible to type something into the two new columns.

Once these columns have some data in them the direct cursor function should be restored to its normal setting (unchecked).  It should remain possible to move around all the data in the usual way with the mouse.

Hope this helps!
