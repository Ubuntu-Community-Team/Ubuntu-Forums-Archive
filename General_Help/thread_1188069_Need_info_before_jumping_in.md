### Post by Polaris96 on 2009-06-15
Well, I suppose the first thing you need to hear is that there absolutely WILL be issues with coming over to Linux.  Few open source apps "just work."  Every project on this and most other Linux distros is worked as a "labour of love" by people who habve other "real" jobs.  All of our software testing and tweaking is done by us.  We don't have massive r/d dept's like apple.

That said, as you mentioned, microsoft stuff doesn't "just work" either.  As a matter of fact, I finally took the plunge into Linux after four hours' work in the XP registry STILL couldn't get the $100 .pdf professional app I had just installed to run without trying to install Roxio media manager (I didn't even own Roxio media manager).

If your hardware is older, there's a pretty good chance Linux will support it.  I'm not in the pro-audio field, but there are people on ubuntu who do use it for pro audio.  I'll let them weigh in on hardware specific issues.

My advice is to try it.  The download is free.  The installer and Live CD WILL "just work".  TRY IT OUT!  what've you got to lose but time?  Even if you like it, KEEP your windows system available on a dual boot in case you need it "in the clench" for business (I learned that the hard way).

Also, buy a book.  For beginners, I like the titles from "No Starch Press"  they are reasonably priced and give a lot of useful info.  And they have several ubuntu specific titles.

Finally, if you stay on, you will eventually become a terminal geek.  Programming will become geek not greek to you and your friends will be saying, "Man, what the (*^&&%&^ happened to you?"  Welcome aboard and good luck.
