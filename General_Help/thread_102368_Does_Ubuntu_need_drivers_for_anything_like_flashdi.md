### Post by matthewv on 2005-12-11
ubuntu does need drivers. Many of the drivers, e.g flash disk, are preloaded, but some, like wifi, are not. To make things more complicated, many manufacturers dont supply linux drivers, so things can get complicated.

With a flash disk you'll be fine, with the wireless adapter, probably not... If your going to get a wireless adapter, take a look at [http://wiki.ubuntu.com/Ndiswrapper/](http://wiki.ubuntu.com/Ndiswrapper/)

### Post by NeverDream on 2005-12-11
It depends. Many Linux distributions come with most drivers you'll need. Ubuntu especially comes with a lot of drivers. The answer is that in most cases, no you will not need to install drivers. That is, if you keep your system up to date. However some hardware is proprietary enough to limit Ubuntu from supplying the drivers (e.g., ATI drivers).
