### Post by 3Miro on 2010-09-20
I have had good experience with Xubuntu. It comes with XFCE as opposed to Gnome and while it lacks some features, XFCE had much lower system requirements. Lubuntu with LXDE is another alternative, however, LXDE is still under heavy development and may not be the best choice for someone with no experience.

I have not used Mint, though I have heard good things about it. Gnome should be OK so long as you keep visual effects to "None".

When you pick a system, you can get a live CD and make sure to test it first. Wifi should either work or you should get a message from the Hardware center for drivers that you can install.

I cannot fix laptops, just desktops.

PS Your CPU only supports 32-bit system.
