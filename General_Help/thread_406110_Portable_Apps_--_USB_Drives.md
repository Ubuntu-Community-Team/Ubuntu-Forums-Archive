### Post by tikey on 2007-07-09
There are different things that you might be interested in. First, there are portable apps which don't only run under Windows but also under other OS. I think OpenOffice portable (or XOpenOffice) is one of them. There's also a password manager (KeePassX). You can find more of them at wikipedia: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_apps](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_apps) 
You just have to look which of those applications also run under Linux. A good bet are the X-applications (with a X at the end or the beginning).

Another thing that you might have a look at is something like Damn Small Linux. You can put that on your usb-stick and then start it at any PC. It's not like a LiveCD where you have to boot from the CD. Here you can start it as a application under Windows so that you have your Linux running in a Windows-windows. There are some more distributions like this. Again, you can check it at wikipedia: [http://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damn_Small_Linux](http://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damn_Small_Linux)

I hope this'll help. I just started to look at stuff like this today so I haven't tried anything of this but I certainly will b/c it sounds promising!

cheers
