### Post by ACupOfCoffee on 2010-07-06
That sounds like a simple case of overheating. If your computer is a desktop, just take the side off and blast out the dust with a little bit of compressed air or those computer dusters they sell at office supply stores. You could also vacuum it out. Just make sure you use an attachment with a fairly small opening.

If it's a laptop you will have to do roughly the same thing, but getting it open will be a lot more difficult.
