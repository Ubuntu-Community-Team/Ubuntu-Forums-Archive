### Post by tgalati4 on 2009-08-15
Yes and no.

I have an ibm thinkpad t43p with an ati firegl v3200 chip.  I'm using the open drivers under Jaunty.  I have compiz with many effects enabled.  I can run googleearth smoothly, but I have to turn off compiz with a script because it causes some compositing issues with phototags in googleearth.  Haven't played any 3d games yet.  I haven't tested googleearth performance yet in windows xp as this machine is dual boot.

So depending on your video card the open drivers may be OK.  Real gaming still needs to be done under windows, so either use a virtual machine, or dual boot.

Post your experience with your card and the open drivers.  Over time, I think the open drivers will get better.  AMD seems more open to provide specs on older cards, so even though they aren't supporting the proprietary drivers for older cards, the open drivers seems to be getting better.

So I'm satisfied on this 3-year old thinkpad, but others with high-powered ATI HD cards seem to think the open drivers suck.

So downgrade if you have to or use the open drivers and let us know what works and what doesn't.
