### Post by dd76 on 2011-10-29
I searched and only found threads for playing purchased songs directly, so not sure if this has been posted before.  For anyone having problems playing purchased music from Itunes on Ubuntu this process works and is not illegal to the best of my knowledge.  In Itunes (assuming you have a windows partition)select the song(s) that you want right click them and select make mp3 version this converted file transfers and plays just fine in rhythmbox.    This is a whole lot faster than burning them then re-ripping them plus its using Apples software to do it.  From what I have read on Apples website this feature was introduced to allow people to place music purchased through Itunes onto other mp3 players and/or WMP.

### Post by RaganN on 2011-10-29
This is the process I use on all my iTunes music, because I can use MP3 in almost anything, on any system.  This only work on music that was purchased in the last two years or so, however (I can't remember when iTunes decided to remove DRM but it was a few years ago).  If you want to play older purchased songs, this won't work.  

Also, the "convert to MP3" might say a different format if your import settings are different.  Before doing this, go to preferences, check your import settings, and set them to MP3 at the desired bitrate.
