### Post by tamoneya on 2008-08-20
when the final release comes you will get a prompt asking you if you want to upgrade.  If you choose yes it will process the version upgrade just like it does with normal updates but it will just be a little larger than normal.  A restart will be required afterwards.  You should know that you dont have to upgrade if you dont want to.  Some people choose to stay with the LTS (long term support) releases like 8.04.  They have support for 3 years on desktop and 5 on server.  Personally I will be upgrading my desktop and laptop to 8.10.  As for my servers I will leave them at 8.04 until the next LTS release.

### Post by silkstone on 2008-08-20
If you are currently using 8.04 and have Update Manager set to notify only of future LTS releases, you will not be invited to upgrade to 8.10

If you uncheck that option, you should get the distro upgrade invitation when 8.10 is released. It's then up to you whether you do the upgrade - it usually works OK provided you haven't manually changed anything critical from the standard installation.
