### Post by Old *ix Geek on 2010-08-23
Because I'm a hardcore *nix person. Started with Tandy Xenix in 1985. I couldn't BELIEVE how pathetic DOS was when I had a brief, miserable exposure to it circa 1988. I was like, wait!, how do I get to another console screen? how do I run 10 programs at once? how do I run something in the background? how do other users log in from their offices? ](*,)

Anyway...I've been using Linux since its first release in 1991. Somewhere in my garage, in one of many boxes I still haven't unpacked since moving 4 years ago, there's a floppy disk with the very first release on it. Yeah, it fit on a floppy!

I've had the unpleasant experience of using windoze machines over the years (we had some windoze workstations at my last job and as the programmer and sysadmin there, I had to administer them. *ugh*)

I've lost track of how many different distributions I've tried, but a few I can clearly remember are: Red Hat, Caldera, SUSE, Knoppix, and Slackware. I didn't dislike any of them, but once I tried Ubuntu + KDE I was hooked.  Now that's all I use.

There is no comparison between Linux and windoze. In the *nix world we've had features like multiple desktops for DECADES that 'doze users still have no clue about. Plus, *nix was built from the ground up to be multiuser, multitasking, networked, etc. Patching together DOS and then windoze to force those functions just isn't the same. The security and stability of *nix were also built in from the beginning.

Let's just go back to what I said at the beginning: I'm a *HARDCORE*, old, *nix user who wouldn't have windoze on her computers if you paid me to do it! :lolflag:
