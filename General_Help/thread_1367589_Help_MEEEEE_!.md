### Post by arvevans on 2009-12-29
He said he was a newbie.  Please give him a break.

Kingof:
Normal etiquette on this group would be to summarize your question as the header line, and then expand problem information a bit in the body of your posting.  Things like what type computer configuration you might be running (RAM Size, CPU Type & Speed, etc.).  Is the problem reproducible, and what do you do to reproduce it are always helpful.

This layout for questions lets the system sort problems based on what keywords are in the header, and makes it much easier for those browsing the posts to know if they can be helpful.

Ideally you would "ask a question" and "someone answers that question" so that not a lot of time and resources are wasted in discussing the fine points of your problem.

Don't let the nay-sayers get you down.  Some on this group are genuinely helpful, even for new Ubuntu users.
