### Post by castrojo on 2008-04-24
> **limefest said:**
> My question, why is **BETA** software included in a production release?! I still a have firefox-2 binary, but all the extensions are disabled because it still sees Firefox 3. I guess I can uninstall the Firefox 3 package... but that is beside the point here.

The reason for including FF3 is explained in the release notes. I spoke with Chris Blizzard (from Mozilla) about this @ Lugradio Live, and shipping Beta 5 is what mozilla wants, otherwise they wouldn't have allowed it. 

I realize that it seems weird, but FF3 updates will come for Hardy. If you absolutely need extensions that haven't been updated, then FF2 is available in the repositories. Hardy will be supported for three years on the desktop, and the only sane choice was to ship a FF3 beta.

### Post by GeigerRulesBig on 2008-04-24
Ignoring the extension compatibility issues, FF3 includes substantial advantages for the desktop user. FF2 was slow and had a tendency to freeze. FF3 is much more stable and snappy and is a much needed improvement for the majority of desktop users. I have been using FF3 since beta 3, and the beta 5 version is stable enough to include in an Ubuntu final release. The final version of FF3 should be released in a couple of months, and it makes much more sense to simply include the beta version and upgrade from that rather than force users through what could be a messy upgrade from 2 to 3 just a few short months after they installed their system (though I did make the upgrade from 2 to 3 on my XP, and it was quite painless.)


-Geiger
