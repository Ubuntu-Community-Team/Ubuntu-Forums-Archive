### Post by QIII on 2012-07-25
Remember that there is always someone who has less know-how than you and look for posts where you sure you have a good answer.  But avoid throwing out "Maybe this and maybe that or maybe the other" type posts.  Those are not helpful (sometimes harmful!) and they are often confusing.

If you can, it would be worth having another install, say a dual boot or an old beater machine in the corner, to deliberately try to break and recover.

Use that install or machine and read things like:

[http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html](http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html)  (a little dated, but good)

[http://ubuntu-manual.org/](http://ubuntu-manual.org/)

[http://debian-handbook.info/download/stable/debian-handbook.pdf](http://debian-handbook.info/download/stable/debian-handbook.pdf)

Pick up a copy of "Linux in a Nutshell" at a local used book store.  Not Ubuntu specific, but full of general stuff.

Goof around.  Break your install.  Fix your install.   Break it again.  Fix it again.   If you have that extra installation, breaking it will harm you no more  than shrugging your shoulders and saying "Oh, well!  That screwed things  up!"

Fun and entertainment for the whole family!

### Post by cortman on 2012-07-25
Find a problem in a forum thread about which you know almost nothing, then research it to death until you are able to offer a sensible solution. I've learned a lot by doing this on occasion; it takes dedication though. Even if you don't understand it well enough to help you've at least learned something.
+1 to what QIII says. There's a whole wiki page of helpful CLI resources in my signature if you're interested in that.

### Post by Zill on 2012-07-25
Jimstehrman:  Everyone here is a volunteer, just trying to help out where we can.  None of us know *everything* about Linux and Ubuntu but all of us know *something!*

When you see a question here that you *really* think you know the answer to then go ahead, post your response.  Although other forum users may also know the answer, we all have other commitments and so it may take a while for someone else to respond.  Obviously, the sooner the OP gets a good answer the better so it is in everyone's interest to post as soon as possible.

I suggest it really isn't a good idea to break your primary system so, as QIII recommended, it is good advice to have another installation that you can play around with and try things out without being too concerned if you break it!
