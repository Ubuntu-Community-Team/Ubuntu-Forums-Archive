### Post by mcduck on 2008-08-09
I suppose what you are trying to do is an MP3 CD (as there's no way you could fit more than one album of music into Audio CD).

If your car CD player is able to play MP3 CD's, any CD burning app will do the trick. Just remember to burn the disk as **Data CD**, not as Audio CD. Then just add all the MP3 files you want to the disk.

MP3 CD *is* just a normal Data CD with MP3 files in it, even though some CD burning apps choose to call it as "MP3 CD" or some kind of Audio CD.

On the other hand, Audio CD contains uncompressed digital audio in 16-bit 44,1kHz PCM files. This is the format used by music CD's you buy from record shops, and one disk can only contain maximum of 80 minuts of sound (originally 74 mins, to be able to fit Beethoven's 9th symphony ;))..
