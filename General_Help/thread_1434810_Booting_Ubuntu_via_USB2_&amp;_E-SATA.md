### Post by abohsin on 2010-03-20
Well. If the OS is installed on the drive, it wouldn't matter how you point at it, you can boot and choose your boot drive, if it contains an MBR, it will boot, done.

I once installed Ubuntu on a SATA drive, which i used to carry around. If plugged in SATA cable, boot PC, it boots normally. Then when I move around, I plug it through SATA/USB cable, choose boot from USB, and bingo, my laptop boots from it.

It works, no worries.
