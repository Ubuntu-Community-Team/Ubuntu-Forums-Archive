### Post by SeijiSensei on 2010-10-21
You're stuck with 1 GB of memory on the Mini 10 because it's [soldered on to the motherboard]("http://gdgt.com/discuss/is-it-possible-to-upgrade-the-memory-383/").  The linked article has some suggested alternatives to the Mini 10.  I looked at the current Dell offerings and didn't see any that you could upgrade to 2 or 3 GB.  I tried to find the "10v" model with a memory slot that was mentioned in the link, but it's no longer available.

Linux has an efficient memory manager that takes advantage of pretty much all the available physical memory either for running programs or for caching the disk(s).  These techniques makes the system highly responsive.

You're probably going to be using this machine for a few years.  Give it some future breathing room with more memory.  

I sent my daughter off to college with an [Asus 1201N]("http://gdgt.com/discuss/is-it-possible-to-upgrade-the-memory-383/"). It was a bit pricey for a netbook at just under $500, but I'm hoping it will be all the computer she needs for the next four years.  (Her Dell [Inspiron 640m]("http://arstechnica.com/civis/viewtopic.php?f=9&t=295735&p=6583611") served her well throughout high school. It cost $1,000.)  I chose the 1201N for its larger screen and keyboard, better video (NVIDIA), and for its attractive design.  It has 2 GB of memory, and everything (Win7 and Lucid) runs smoothly so far.  Newegg has the next generation model, the 1201PN, for sale at $400.  This doesn't have the dual-core Atom processor in the 1201N but has NVIDIA's new ION2 platform.  I preferred performance and chose the 1201N. It's discontinued now, but it's in demand and [still sells for $450-500]("http://www.google.com/products/catalog?client=ubuntu&channel=fs&q=1201n&oe=utf-8&um=1&ie=UTF-8&cid=7390645050537507025&ei=24PATKuUPML48AaZ0tHXBg&sa=X&oi=product_catalog_result&ct=result&resnum=1&ved=0CCAQ8wIwAA#").

[Newegg]("http://www.newegg.com/Store/SubCategory.aspx?SubCategory=772&name=Netbooks") has a variety of netbooks under $300. Read the reviews to see how well these computers work with Ubuntu.  Newegg has a reasonably decent number of Linux users among their reviewers.  Most all the reviewers suggest upgrading to at least 2GB of memory if you can.
