### Post by dodle on 2008-09-01
This is for anyone who has been looking for an alternative to Microsoft Office 2007

During my first semester of college, I was told that I would need Microsoft Office 2007 for my web based computer class, as the entire school had switched to it.  Since I didn't want to pay for a new office program (even though I could get it at discount through the school), I figured that I would just keep using OpenOffice.  I didn't know very much about Office 2007, or Office in general for that matter, but I soon realized that OpenOffice wasn't going to work when my instructor began emailing assignments in the .docx format.  I studied up on OpenOffice a little bit and found that it wouldn't have .docx support until later versions (3.0 I think).  I found the solution at Microsoft's website, although I haven't tested it with Wine, but It will work on Windows.  Microsoft provides a free compatibility pack to convert Office '07 files.

It is located here: [http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/details.aspx?FamilyId=941B3470-3AE9-4AEE-8F43-C6BB74CD1466&displaylang=en]("http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/details.aspx?FamilyId=941B3470-3AE9-4AEE-8F43-C6BB74CD1466&displaylang=en")

Install and run your .docx file through it and it will be converted to a .doc.  I believe it also works for other new extensions in Office '07.  It will automatically change your file extension associations during install, so you will set those back to what you had them before.  But now you can open your newly converted .doc file in OpenOffice.

I will try testing this i Wine.  If you already have would you post the results?

I hope this can help somebody.
