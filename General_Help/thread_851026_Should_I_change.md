### Post by venturosa on 2008-07-06
I started my linux experience with 'Feisty' struggled with some things and continued to use 'XP' for much of my work. After the upgrade to 'Gutsy' I found I was using 'XP' less and less and finally made the switch to have it as my main OS.  After the upgrade to 'Hardy' I'm having to use 'XP' more often again. Many of my problems are usb related ex.:-  scanner (which I eventually got to work),  digital camera ( I can import the photos but can't access the files on the camera to delete them). Both these worked without a hitch in Gutsy.
On 'XP' I used CorelDraw quite a lot, so persevered with Scribus and Inkscape as substitutes only to find that I can only print from them using the alternative lpr driver where the results are rubbish.
I spend hours trawling the net for fixes and workrounds to the numerous problems, usually finding that it results in "there's a bug in . . . ."
The OS gets bigger with each update and can do all sorts of clever stuff but shouldn't the developers concentrate on getting the basics right first. I'm beginning to dred the arrival of the next edition and having to start all over again trying to get everything to work
I consider myself as an average home computer user in that I want to be able to surf the net and do emails (no problems there) print from various programs, scan the occasional image, copy the odd cd now and again and load photos from my camera.
I really don't want to go back to Windows of any flavour. Is it time to look at a different linux distro? If so, which one?

### Post by ibutho on 2008-07-06
If your issues are with specific applications then switching distros may not help because they more or less have the same applications in their software repositories. Trying out a different distro is not necessarily bad though. I recommend you try openSUSE, Mandriva, PCLinuxOS and Fedora.
