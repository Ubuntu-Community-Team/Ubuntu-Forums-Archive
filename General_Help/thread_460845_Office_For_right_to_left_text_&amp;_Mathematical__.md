### Post by kevinlyfellow on 2007-06-02
> **Dougie187 said:**
> No... I dont know about the curly brackets. I hate it because every time i write something i get question marks all over my equations. Even using their formatting buttons to input stuff into it. Its really irritating. Its like its missing some of the font or something.

Curly brackets are used to group info together but will not appear in your final equation.    As for the question marks, those mean that there's a formatting error, for instance.

```
integral from a to b x^2 dx
``` will give question marks because it recognizes the "from" and "to" but it unfortunatly doesn't recognize the word "integral".  The correct equation would be ```
int from a to b x^2 dx
```

Here's an example with curly brackets:
```

{vec f cdot vec q int from a to b x^2 dx} over sum from n=0 to infinity {1 over n^5}
newline
"Compared to the equation without curly brackets"
newline
vec f cdot vec q int from a to b x^2 dx over sum from n=0 to infinity 1 over n^5

```
