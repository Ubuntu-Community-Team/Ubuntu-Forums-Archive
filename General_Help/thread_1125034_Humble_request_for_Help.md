### Post by lisati on 2009-04-14
First of all, you need to give a bit of thought to what partitions you want. Do you want to have Ubuntu only on your system, or do you want to keep Windows (or whatever else might be on your system)? The Ubuntu installation process will give you a choice, which one you select will depend on how you want to organize things.

### Post by Agent.Logic_ on 2009-04-14
Hello ami.hirpara,

Welcome to Ubuntu and the Ubuntu community! You can read about different partitioning strategies [here](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partitioning). It isn't very complicated, the entire article tries to be down-to-earth all the way. [Here](http://www.easy-ubuntu-linux.com/dual-boot-ubuntu-defined.html) is a simple diagram which explains partitioning. And finally, [here](http://beginlinux.com/desktop_training/ubuntu/1073-ubuntu-intrepid-ibex-install) is a guide that actually tells you *how* to create partitions. Instead of following the strategy in the guide, follow your own, which you would have formed after reading the first link.

Compiz installation is detailed [here](http://geekdomain.wordpress.com/2008/12/27/how-to-install-compiz-on-ubuntu-810-intrepid-ibex/). Please note, Compiz requires 3D acceleration. There are quite a few graphics chipsets/video cards which don't have much driver support for 3D acceleration. If you have any doubts, please feel free to post your doubts here. The community is always here to help! Good luck with your install!
