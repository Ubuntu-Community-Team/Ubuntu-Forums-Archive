### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-10-01
The Zune is worth exactly nothing if you can't put media files on it, and you can't put media files on it with Ubuntu.

You need to use Windows, either running on real hardware or virtualised.

Why not just get any other sort of MP3 player that mounts as "USB mass storage"? (they usually advertise "plug and play no driver required" on the box).

The best full-featured media player with Linux compatibility is the Sony Walkman X series. It's more expensive than the iPods but it has some real premium features - brilliant OLED screen, noise-cancelling headphones, ClearBass, long battery life, "flick to browse" for album covers and for searching through videos, wifi (for podcasts, Youtube and web browsing) and physical Play, Skip and volume controls for the times when you don't want to put your fingers all over the screen.
