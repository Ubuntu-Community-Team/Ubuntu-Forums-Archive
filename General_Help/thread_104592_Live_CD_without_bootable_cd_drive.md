### Post by Qrk on 2005-12-16
You can boot from a USB stick. Ubuntu is probably too heavy for it (unless you have a 1 GB thumbdrive) so a better choice would be Slax, which comes with a harddisk installer and can be easily configured to boot from a USB disk.

Unfortunatly, most (older) computers cannot boot from a USB stick. 

If a thumbdrive doesn't work, you could try a floppy. Debian still provides floppy images. They contain the installer and the minimum amount of stuff; you download the rest of the packages over the internet. The packages won't be as up to date as in Ubuntu, but they will be perfectly fine.
