### Post by iMisspell on 2010-03-20
You can "dual boot", install Ubuntu next to windows.

Search google (or these forums) for "ubuntu and windows dual boot" and read up alittle.

By dual booting, you will still have your windows instaltion as it is now with all your current files. When you start your computer you will have a choice to start windows or start ubuntu. While in ubuntu, you will have access to all your windows files and do what ever you want with them.
