### Post by bung33 on 2007-05-24
Ubuntu, or shall I say, Linux in general comes with a firewall. By default, Linux will be more secure than Windows. You will not need to change any of the default settings unless you are experienced with Linux. When it comes to anti-virus protection, you really don't need it because most malware programs were created for the Windows platform. However, if you really decided you need it, then I would just recommend AVG for Linux, because it's free. I have never used any anti-virus or anti-spyware programs on Ubuntu, and I have never been infected with anything. You will find that Linux is pretty good at protecting you right out of the box...

### Post by fragos on 2007-05-25
At this stage of the game the purpose of the virus checker would be to prevent you from passing on Windows viruses.  Common sense is the best protection against most virus attacks.  In short anti-virus isn't really required to protect you.  My answer to the problem is using Gmail which has excellent virus, spam and scam filters.  I even set up gmail to make available to a POP email client any email I don't delete or it marks as spam.  Evolution, Thunderbird or email package of you choice can be used to retrieve them.  The Linux firewall we all have is iptables.  I recommend you experiment with firestarter which you can use to make the firewall stronger and to examine blocked traffic.
