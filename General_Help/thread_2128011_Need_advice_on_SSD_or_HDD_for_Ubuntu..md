### Post by oldfred on 2013-03-21
About a year & half ago I added a 62GB SSD to my system. It does boot faster and loads larger applications quicker. But once apps are loaded there is not any real difference. I also have 4GB of RAM and almost never use swap. Linux caches recent activity in RAM so unless you load many apps you have everything in RAM which is quicker than the SSD.

I have two 30GB / (root) partitions on my SSD. My wife was complaining about too many changes as I always updated every 6 months, so I have 12.04 as my main working install, have 12.10 in the other / and 13.04 on hard drive to experiment with. All my data is on hard drives and mounted into all my installs. Without data my /home is small, so it also is on SSD in /.
