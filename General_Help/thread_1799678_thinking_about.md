### Post by searchfgold6789 on 2011-07-07
Once you get the drivers all set up, all you need to do is install Wine from the Software Center and you should be good to go. You can double check with the Wine AppDB with your games to see if they will work, and to help with the driver setup you can always post back here if you encounter anything. 
Edit: To answer your second question; I don't know as it varies from person to person, but most people are fond of the sheer speed and usability of the OS. I like the desktop effects and window management. Other popular reasons for switching are the ease of dual-booting Windows, the huge amount of unique software, and others. Good luck,
