Hello...MS Office offers much more than legacy abilities.  It is (At least to me) much better than OO.  Although, I believe it always goes back to something simple: Software is to assist you in completing your tasks or fulfilling your needs.  If OO does perfectly fine for you, then go with it.  Same with MS Office, if it fulfills your needs, go with it.
.
...Some will pose the general "home" use argument which varies among users.  For instance some may consider home use as never using a single macro.  Others, as myself, make VBA functions for my home use.  Also, the ribbon...Which some hate it, I actually enjoy it & am very glad that the placed this powerful advancement in MS Office applications.
.
I have seriously tested OO & attempted to implement it in place of MS Office & it just wasn't providing what I needed.  It certainly doesn't cleanly work with Word documents.  Generally speaking, I find many, many times OO changes the formatting of a Word document.  Requiring me to rebuild things or simply use Word.
.
Back to the original question about PowerPoint...I believe I have successfully used it in Linux.  I have used the PowerPoint viewer successfully, but I use the application so little that I am not a good tester for how well it works.  Just to clarify, the viewer is made by MS, not some 3rd party.
.
In my view of Linux, it is about choice, right?  If so, my choice of using the OS, but with a MS (As well as other) windows based software never defeats the purpose, but actually fulfills the freedom that we enjoy & expect with Linux.  So I consider this a great benefit of Linux being able to do this.
