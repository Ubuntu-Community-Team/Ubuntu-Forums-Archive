### Post by CaptainMark on 2014-02-18
Well mac-d is best overcome by having text and pictures as large as possible, there are a couple of good zoom managers plugins available, whilst I've never used them myself I understand Zoom Page and No Squint are the better ones, there's not much you can do to enhance contrast online because it largely is up to the web pages design, you could turn up the screen contrast slightly, this will help her. Also it's not quite clear from your post which desktop is in use, you've said Ubuntu but tagged the post Lubuntu, there are some screen magnifiers available that will zoom right in on the mouse you can look into

### Post by whitesmith on 2014-02-18
If her UA is Firefox you might consider installing NoSquint. It enlarges/reduces text on web pages over a wide range. NoSquint also remembers the settings, so the appearance of a page is the same on subsequent visits as it was after the initial change. Screen magnifiers are another possibility. Google is your friend. Cheers.
