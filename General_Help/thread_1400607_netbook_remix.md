### Post by macabrem on 2010-02-07
If you want to stick with Ubuntu-esqe reliability, you should try Xubuntu for a potential speed enhancement.  You can install Xfce in Synaptic and then choose to login using XFCE environment without reformatting the whole computer.

My understanding of the Ubuntu 9.10 Netbook remix is that it is basically the same as regular Ubuntu but with a different GUI.  That being said, I've had mixed results with Ubuntu 9.10 and the netbook remix on my netbook.  Under 9.04, the regular Ubuntu seemed to run faster.  Under 9.10, the remix works great.

Hi Comzee, 

1. Netbook Remix isn't any faster than "regular" Ubuntu, maybe a little slower in my experience.
2. Flash is always slow in Linux; it's Adobe's fault. ;)
3. As mentioned above, switching to a lighter desktop (Xfce/Xubuntu or LXDE are very popular) can give you a nice performance boost. This does not require a reinstall.
4. Asus eee 1000 has a slow Atom processor and very slow SSD drives; it's not going to be a speed demon with any operating system! :) 
5. Make sure you've installed Ubuntu correctly (to the 8gb drive, the faster of your two SSDs, and just use the 32gb drive for extra storage).
