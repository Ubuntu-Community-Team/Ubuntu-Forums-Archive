### Post by blueturtl on 2007-05-11
57 degrees is a bit on the warm side but nothing to really worry about. Once the temps go 60+ I would consider raising the fan speed or installing a heatsink that deals with the heat more efficiently. CPU life does go down the higher the temperature, but I have never heard of someone's CPU giving out before the person got a new one (unless they fried it, but that's a different scenario).

If you don't have a case fan, I would consider installing one. After I added one my CPU temp dropped by a whole 10 degrees. Also my hard-disks run cooler.
