### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-05-17
Yeah, running out of storage can wreak havoc on your configuration files.  Normally, deleting files and creating some more space on the drive will fix the problems, but sometimes your configuration files will become corrupted.  My suggestion would be to backup all of the configuration files and directories (e.g. the hidden files and directories in your home directory, which start with a "."), and then delete them.  This will let you start with a clean slate, so you can selectively restore your configuration files one application at a time, or just redo them completely.
