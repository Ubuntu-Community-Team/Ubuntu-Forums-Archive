### Post by ram2500 on 2009-12-16
I have an HP laptop that's a little less than two years old with a Pentium dual-core processor @ 2.3 GHz (around that) and 3-4 GB of memory AND a 7200 rpm hard drive and the battery never runs out below 2.5 hours. At work I have a Dell Latitude laptop--the same age as the HP--which has a single core Celeron at 2 GHz and 1 gigabyte of memory and it barely lasts 1 1/2 hours. The HP has a bigger screen, a 7200 rpm hard drive and all the extras (its called an HP Entertainment PC) yet the cheap Dell has horrible battery life among other horrible problems, like a cheap-feeling lid that flexes every time it's opened and closed. The power connector is feeling loose...I'm waiting for the day the sysadmin will cart these off to the e-waste facility.:P Take my suggestion...get HP or Lenovo Thinkpad (I don't know about System76 as I have never owned one.)

The Core i7 laptops from HP might have slightly less 2.5 hrs of battery life as it's quad-core...but, I must say that a dual-core machine which had Vista (now Win7) performed well on my laptop, and if the "Vista test passes", then it can do anything. Core i7 might not be necessary.
