### Post by vanadium on 2014-09-16
Actually, it is the dash that is designed to find files and folders, next to applications and other items. Opening the dash and typing the name of your folder should display it there. Use the shortcut "Super+f" to open directly the file lens (and exclude searches from other sources).

The aim of the HUD is to select menu items from an application. In old nautilus versions, bookmarks would directly appear under the "Bookmarks" menu. thus, they also would appear in the HUD if a nautilus window or the Desktop has the focus. Now, the bookmark menu just contains an entry that opens a dialog. This may be the reason that bookmarks do not anymore show up in the HUD.
