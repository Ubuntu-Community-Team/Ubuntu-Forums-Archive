Recently, I've been putting my computer to sleep instead of shutting down. I put it to sleep whenever I'm on the road or if I'm moving from one classroom to another. I also noticed that it consumes less power than I expected.

### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-12-04
I moved your post to the General Help section since it is a technical question.

The amount of power that laptops consume while in standby varies from model to model.  Newer batteries last much longer than older ones, but in my laptop that is 4 1/2 years old, it consumes a few percent of battery per hour in standby.  Because going into standby still uses power, you are getting some loss, but the computer comes back to a ready state much quicker.  An alternative is to hibernate the machine, but this takes longer to bring back up (but not quite as long as a clean boot) and saves the state of your computer, similar to standby.
I definitely think sleeping/standby is worth the tradeoff, esp. in short term situations (like less than an hour or two, or perhaps overnight when the computer isn't being used but you don't want to shutdown or hibernate it).  I wouldn't expect your battery shouldn't depreciate any quicker than with normal battery usage.
