### Post by InvIsiBlekID on 2008-03-21
By a quick research, BackTrack appears to be a slackware based distribution. The packages will not be compatible with ubuntu.  You could try to use synaptic to search for the packages that you want to install.  This would actually be much easier than copying the programs from that distro, even if they were debian packages (which is what ubuntu uses), since synaptic (APT) will take care of dependencies for you.  If you can't find packages for the programs you want, you could always compile them from source.  Here's a guide for how to do that: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/CompilingSoftware](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/CompilingSoftware)

But I would suggest using syaptic, as I've said it will take care of your dependencies.
