### Post by Wuxin on 2012-05-15
I have just installed the current version of Xubuntu on my IBM ThinkPad T43p.  For the past two years I was using Ubuntu 10.10 and it was wonderful.  Everything seems fine with Xubuntu except that I can't get it to generate automatic sleep mode...what can I do?  I can manually hit the suspend button when I click on the power switch [It gives you the options to turn off the machine, restart, or "suspend."]  "Suspend" functions much like sleep mode--it powers down the unit and holds everything in place until the next time you open the lid on the laptop.  Is there a way in Xubuntu to make this function automatic so that the machine goes into sleep mode when you close the laptop?  That would be a useful convenience.  Thank you for your help in advance!

### Post by Toz on 2012-05-16
Have a look at the Power Manager applet in the Settings Manager. In both the "On AC" and "On Battery" sections, there is an option where you can set what the laptop does when you close the lid. Set it to "Suspend"
