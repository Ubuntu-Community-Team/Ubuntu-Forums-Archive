### Post by blazemore on 2009-11-10
In a word, yes.

I may regret saying this, but...

All USB external hard drives work on Ubuntu.
Ubuntu can read all major filesystems including Fat32 and NTFS, which are the most common for external drives.

if you're looking for the best performance, format it with ext4 when you get it, although bear in mind Windows can't read this type of partition.

A good idea is to go to Ebuyer and get whatever hard drive is on special offer. There's some decent stuff on there; I got a 1tb (Internal) hard drive for £35 (About $60)

### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-11-10
USB anything will work.

That said, maybe you want something a bit ... better.

Paying a few extra $$ for eSata will have a huge payoff in improved performance, compared with USB.

USB 2.0 = 400Mb/s
eSata = 3,000Mb/s

You don't really even have to do math on that one...

USB interface will provide I/O that is comparable to a normal 5400RPM laptop drive (pretty OK).

eSata interface will provide I/O that matches any desktop system's drive (more than OK, and quite a bit faster than the internal drive[s] on your Acer).

### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-11-10
External hard drives are just high-speed raw storage. Most external drives are just a sealed case with a drive inside it. You can't actually touch the disk drive; just the case.

DVDs are low speed semi-fixed storage. The front of these drives is exposed and they have a button which, when pushed, causes a tray to slide out. That's not a cup holder. You can then put a CD or DVD in, press the button again, and it will close the tray. If you have configured your system correctly, music or a movie will begin to play.

They're different physical devices and their use is different as well.



If you like what you have, don't upgrade. Your version will be supported by Ubuntu, and us, for years and years.
