### Post by akakingess on 2009-10-03
I would recommend formatting the partition, and if you want to get a little more technical and not stray too far away from Ubuntu you could go with Debian, upon which Ubuntu is based. Keep in mind, there are I believe 3 ways to go with that distro, stable (doesn't support new hardware as fast, but is extremely stable), middle ground (try to get some of the newer hardware in there, but don't go to far and try to keep it stable), and then experimental (which will attempt to immediately adopt hardware and therefore MAY have more problems/bugs). I am sure I'm not explaining it as well as others, so I would check out there web site, just a suggestion.
