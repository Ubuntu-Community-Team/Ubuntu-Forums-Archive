### Post by raptir on 2015-02-02
I don't have first-hand experience, but I can tell you a little bit about it. The Celeron CPUs that are currently being developed can be either Bay Trail (Atom) based or Haswell/Broadwell (Core) based. The CPU in the E1-510 is Bay Trail based. This puts it more in the realm of high-end tablets than other laptops. Though benchmarks only give you limited info, the 2820 gets a 1003 on Passmark, which puts it about on par with the entry level Core2Duos from 6-7 years ago. The integrated graphics are similar. Having used Ubuntu on systems with older chips that have similar performance, you'll likely only be able to run a lightweight desktop environment like lxde or Xfce and the laptop will feel slow overall. For video watching it will handle 720p but will likely struggle with 1080p videos. For online streaming that typically does not take as much advantage of your GPU you will likely only be able to get SD resolution to play fluently. 

All newer laptops use UEFI in place of BIOS, which will add a hurdle to getting a dual-boot setup but it shouldn't be a major issue.
