### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-05-03
Actually, P2P means "peer to peer", not "pair to pair".  This is to distinguish it from client-server systems (such as the World Wide Web) where one central computer shares files with numerous others.  In a P2P network, all computers are equals, or "peers".

P2P networks are commonly used by file-sharing clients.  The most pupular P2P file-sharing protocol is currently BitTorrent.  The use of file-sharing clients is perfectly legal.  It is the sharing of copyrighted material that is not.  It's the difference between driving a car (which is legal) and driving a car in reverse while drunk the wrong way down a one-way street (which is obviously not).

The default BitTorrent client for Ubuntu is Transmission, but many people prefer to use other, more full-featured clients like [Deluge]("http://deluge-torrent.org/").

To use a BitTorrent client, you first need to find a torrent containing the file(s) you want on a tracker website like [IsoHunt]("http://isohunt.com/") or [The Pirate Bay]("http://thepiratebay.org/").  When you download the torrent, it will open in your BitTorrent client, which will begin downloading the files.

Just about everything you could ever want is available for download using BitTorrent, but as I said, it may be illegal to download (or especially to share) certain content.

Various websites offer music for download at a price.  Some use file formats such as mp4 or wma which may be protected by a form of DRM (Digital Rights Management), which would restrict how you can use that music.  If you're interesting in purchasing music for download, I suggest [Amazon]("http://www.amazon.com/MP3-Music-Download/b?ie=UTF8&node=163856011") and [Walmart]("http://mp3.walmart.com/store/home"), since they use the DRM-free mp3 file format.
