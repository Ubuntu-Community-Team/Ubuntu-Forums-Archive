### Post by nanotube on 2009-03-29
> **pjmlmas said:**
> Do most long time users of Ubuntu prefer to let Synaptic/Ubuntu handle firefox, openoffice, pigeon, etc or do you handle it with Ubuntuzilla?

couple of things:

ubuntuzilla /only/ does mozilla stuff - firefox, thunderbird, seamonkey. 

also, /most/ ubuntu users use the stuff from the repos. there are at most about 10k users of ubuntuzilla, and millions users of ubuntu. 

that said, there are a few valid reasons to use the mozilla builds (which is what ubuntuzilla lets you do with some convenience):

1. you are experiencing some problems with the repos versions of firefox/thunderbird/seamonkey (e.g., I myself run the ubuntuzilla version of firefox because the repos version used to crash on me at random times with no warning, while the mozilla version never does.)
2. you would like to get the updates faster (there's usually a delay of a few days to at most a couple of weeks for firefox updates to come through to the ubuntu repos, a few weeks for thunderbird, and unfortunately, a few months to never, for seamonkey. for instance, seamonkey is still at v 1.1.12 in the intrepid repos, but the current release is 1.1.15 already.) [for this reason, i also run the mozilla builds of thunderbird and seamonkey - too long of a wait for critical security updates. firefox is not too bad in this regard, but for tb and especially sm, the length of delay is unacceptable to me.]
3. you use seamonkey (as per above, if you want to use an up to date seamonkey, you basically can't rely on the repos)
4. you just want to. :)

In summary, ubuntuzilla is essentially a niche project, but it has its uses (though i'm biased, being its developer and all. :) ).

hope that answers your question.
