### Post by fallofshadows on 2012-01-15
I figured I'd give you guys an update :D

I used the Clonezilla Live CD to make a system image of my entire disk and back it up to my external drive. It took 1.5 hours for each partition (two of them), but I figure it's worth it.

Contrary to the advice given, I decided to use the update manager to update to 11.04, and then to 11.10. I figured that if anything went wrong, I could easily wipe the partition and do a clean install. Luckily, nothing went wrong, so that saved me from the mess of restoring data/changing all my settings around.  That took another 5 hours or so, because it took at least 2.5 hours for each update. Downloading all the files was horrible for me, because my net speed never gets over 200kb/s. 

That being said, I am now running Ubuntu 11.10. I've come to discover that I love the Gnome Shell feature of it, so I'm rockin' that instead of Unity. :D

Thanks for all your helps!
