### Post by lwsiv on 2007-02-18
I have a Dell Latitude D820 and the associated Dell docking station. I have used latitudes/dock stations in the past (not this specific model/station) with Fedora and have never had this issue.

I had ubuntu installed on my laptop and when I docked it, the video (to an external) monitor did not work (the screen would show "Input not supported"). At first I thought it was the monitor and I switched out monitors. Still didn't work.

I needed to clean up this laptop anyway, so I wiped the disk and went to reinstall Edgy Eft on the machine. I installed it docked (to make sure everything would as automatically as possible get picked up). The Ubuntu disk boots correctly and shows up on the monitor, up to the point of your choice to "install", shortly after that, I get back to the same condition as the original install.

Not sure what to do to get past this. Any suggestions? I am really liking Ubuntu and would have to have to go back to Fedora.

### Post by veloce on 2007-02-19
Quick fix that worked for me was to use the Fn+F8 key to switch over to the laptop monitor.  Then by lowering the screen resolution I could switch back to the external monitor.

The laptop's resolution of 1400x1050 wasn't supported by my external monitor.
