### Post by ardchoille42 on 2007-01-13
I always build my own computers.. I haven't bought one of the store shelves in years. One of the things I do when shopping for components is refer to this page:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport)

I have memorized that URL because most of the retailers I shop with will allow me to use one of their computers to surf to that page while I am in the store.

The way I started is to buy what is called a "bare bones" computer, typically it only has a power supply, motherboard, CPU, and possibly a stick of ram already connected inside a case. The good thing about this is that I don't have to mess with motherboard settings or cabling. The bare bones computers I buy are anywhere from $100.00 to $180.00 US. Then I can build from there. I typically add 1Gb - 2Gb ram, DVD+/- R/RW, one or two hard drives, video card, CF/SD/MMX/etc card reader and any other items I want. I recently built a nice multimedia box (I believe the parts cost me $500.00 US), installed Ubuntu, and sold it for $1,000 US.

One of my favorite sites for components is: [http://www.newegg.com/](http://www.newegg.com/)

### Post by Roger Mudd on 2007-01-13
I also built my own computer. It's a great experience and I've been very happy with the performance to date.
One thing to keep in mind, however, is heat and noise. These factors are actually related. The hotter your machine runs, the more fans you will need and the louder your PC will be. I didn't take this into account and built my machine around a P4 2.8 Prescott. This is one of the hottest running processors around. As a result, I had to install a bunch of fans. Now my machine sounds like a hovercraft under my desk.
This rant is obviously not directly related to your original inquiry, but hopefully it will help you avoid the same mistakes I made. Building a computer is very rewarding experience, but be sure to do all the necessary research first.
