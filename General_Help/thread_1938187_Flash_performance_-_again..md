### Post by dimpie_h on 2012-03-09
The main problem is that flash is proprietary software. As far I understand, adobe is the maintainer of the flash software for linux. They do not have the goal to make flash work optimally in linux. The windows version of flash gets a much better treatment off course, because it has a much larger user base. Although there are steps you can take to make your web experience more smoother.

Installing a flash blocker plugin  will help a lot.
In chromium I use FlashControl for blocking flash.

Using a flashblocker will give you the option to only allow the flash elements you want to use on the webpage you are visiting. This means the other still blocked flash elements won't slow your computer down. This method enables my netbook to watch sd youtube vids.

I hope this helps you.
