### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-05-15
I find Xubuntu's interface super-simple, and if you set it for them, you could have a sweet little panel for their favorite apps, the weather instantly (click the weather applet and get a whole Accuweather forecast), analog clock, etc. And the "right-click anywhere on the desktop" to bring up a customizable menu with icons and everything is a super-simple, kid-and-senior-friendly system. Lucid ships with Abiword and Gnumeric instead of OpenOffice, so it's lightweight and in my opinion a lot simpler than OpenOffice.

My only issue was with sound, cured by removing PulseAudio (your mileage may vary). The rest is delightful for seniors and even the little kids at the dance studio take to it instantly (I just have a few little favorite apps in the panel - one click and they're in).

Honestly, I wouldn't look any farther than Xubuntu for super simplicity - even in the choice of applications - and all the stuff most seniors might use a computer for.

Delighted,
