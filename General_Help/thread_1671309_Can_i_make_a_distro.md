### Post by piquat on 2011-01-22
Yes.  Remastersys allows you several options, though you are constrained to the size of a DVD, so keep that in mind.  In other words, if you load up 10 gigs of games, they're not going to fit on the DVD, of course.  But even in that situation you can do a the 'distribution' option instead of the 'backup' option and it will take the entire OS, including all the customizations you might make, leaving your program installs behind.  Or you can do a complete 'backup' and take everything if you stay within the constrains of the size of a DVD.

For what you want to do, I'd customize it just like you want, don't install any large games (why install that along with the OS, do that later) then use remastersys's 'backup' option to take everything.  Then you'll have your own customized distribution.  You could make one just for you and a version all "windowsed up" for a friend your trying to ween off Windows, or a parent who is only comfortable with windows.

It's really easy to use too.  I can have it doing a backup in 4 clicks.

Good luck!

---

### Post by piquat on 2011-01-22
I thought I'd add one more thing.  If you do a complete backup, it will backup EVERYTHING.  If you have, say, the passwords to your banking website in a hidden file in your home directory and you do a complete backup, that file will be on the DVD.  So be careful what your backing up and who your handing them out too...;)
