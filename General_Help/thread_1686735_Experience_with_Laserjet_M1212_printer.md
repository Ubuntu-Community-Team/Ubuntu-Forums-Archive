### Post by yitzstokes on 2011-02-12
I am a newbie to the Ubuntu world coming from Windows/Vista

I wish to share a story about my new printer that others may learn from my experiences.

My old printer , a HP Lazerjet 1000 is unrealiable/unsupported via Ubuntu . So  bought a new printer.

Upon bringing this printer home, I plugged it in and Ubuntu proceeded with an auto-discovery download of a "close" driver. I proceeded with checkout. Discovering that I needed more via a log file .
I proceeded to use a link that I obtained from pre-sales support
[SIZE=2][FONT=Tahoma, Verdana][SIZE=2][FONT=Tahoma, Verdana][SIZE=2][http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/softwareCategory?os=2020&lc=en&cc=us&dlc=en&sw_lang=&product=3965849](http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/softwareCategory?os=2020&lc=en&cc=us&dlc=en&sw_lang=&product=3965849)[/SIZE][/FONT][/SIZE][/FONT][/SIZE]

And proceeded to re-install

The driver that was eventually downloaded is hplip-3.11.1.run.

Now the emoticon at the top of my screen shows a HPLIP Status service icon giving me the ability to complete the checkout process of this machine

I can only hope that someone else may benefit from this abbreviated experience
My new printer is very nice , Thank you
:razz:

NB I am enjoying the Ubuntu experience.  I am a contractor DBA and am enjoying this"lean" experience
