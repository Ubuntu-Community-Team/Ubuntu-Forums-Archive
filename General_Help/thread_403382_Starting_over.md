### Post by newblacknoise on 2007-04-08
you've probably already taken this into account, but if you really want a clean slate, make sure you get the live cd setup to format all the partitions ubuntu will be using.

i only mention this seemingly trivial detail as i spent an entire day recently reinstalling ubuntu multiple times because i was having screen resolution issues. i took me a while to realise that by not formatting my /home partition, i may keeping my nice wallpapers etc, but i was also keeping a bunch of evil config files which gave me the same problems as before, even on my fresh install.
