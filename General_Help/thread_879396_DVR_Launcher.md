### Post by VeeDubb on 2009-03-06
At the moment, I'm not aware of a single front-end program that would do everything for a media center PC.

I actually have a simpler suggestion for how to handle that, but it's based on a couple of assumptions.

1. You will set up the device to behave like a piece of AV equipment.  i.e. it will autologin, and instead of being shut down, it will be suspended or hibernated.

2. You will do most of your controlling with either a remote control or a wireless keyboard/pointer combo.

What I would suggest would be to do three things.

1. Rather than worrying about mpd, etc, narrow all of your applications down to 2.  

Your favorite MythTV interface, and Boxee.  Boxee can play pretty much any type of media you throw at it including local and network resources, has built in functionality for viewing and browsing a variety of web-based video.  It gathers media information from the web, and basically does everything you could possibly want a media center PC to do, except record TV, which is where your favorite front end for mythTV comes in.

2. Set your system up with 3 desktops, and set Boxee to launch at boot on the first, MythTV on the second, and leave the third desktop free for doing whatever.

3. Set an extra button on your remote for switching desktops, or simply use a standard keyboard shortcut if you go with a keyboard/pointer combo.
