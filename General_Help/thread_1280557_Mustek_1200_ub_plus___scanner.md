### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-02
I assume you're using xsane or something that calls xsane.

I've seen this w/ an Epson and it seems to happen when the scan resolution is set to something other than one of the manufacturer's "blessed" resolutions.

I think it's some kind of mechoptical harmonic. (Like that term? Ya heard it here first.)

Try something obvious like 150dpi, 300dpi, etc. and see if it does the same thing.

These images are obviously scanned at different resolutions.
