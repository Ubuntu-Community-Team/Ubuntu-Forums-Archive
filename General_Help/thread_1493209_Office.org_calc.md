### Post by cloyd on 2010-05-25
If I understand you correctly, it is a simple matter. Key in your first formula in D2. Then control c with cursor on d2.  Paint the other cells down which you would like the new formulas in and then press control v.  The spreadsheet should keep track of all the relative positions of the cells in your formula.  
 

Look at your resulting formulas to see if you have  what you want.

### Post by cloyd on 2010-05-26
The function for today's date is =today()    The parenthesis stays empty.  Bear in mind, if you use this on the spreadsheet today, and open the sheet tomorrow, it will then reflect tomorrow's date.  If you want to enter the formula, and then convert it to a value so that it wont change, put the cursor on the formula and press f2, then f9 and the formula will be converted to a value.
