### Post by squaregoldfish on 2009-02-13

Then buy a new hard drive!

### Post by squaregoldfish on 2009-02-13
I can't think of anything else at the moment, unless the logical structures on the disk have gone mad - fsck will detect this.

Just because a drive is not very old, it doesn't mean it can't break! Go to the drive manufacturer's website - they might provide a custom utility for checking the drive. In any case, the drive will probably still be in warranty, so at least a replacement will be free.
