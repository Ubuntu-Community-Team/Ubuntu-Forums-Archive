### Post by bobjenkins on 2008-05-20
Quite simple :) 

Enable Desktop cube and **Rotate Cube** (From what I can tell you don't have rotate check enabled that is your problem)

Now just hold Alt+control+left mouse then drag your mouse
Also when your mouse is on your desktop (not on a window) Simultaneously press left and right mouse then drag
And finally you can also hold Alt+Control then hit down arrow, you get the screens then use left and right to change screens

post back if you have problems

### Post by issih on 2008-05-20
You'll need to select, desktop cube and rotate cube to get that working. Its worth going into the settings of each plugin and having a look at the bindings tab to see what the default shortcuts are for triggering different effects, you can then set them to whatever you like obviously.

The one thing that might bite you is that you need to set your "virtual desktop size"

Find the "General Options" plugin and ensure under the Desktop Size tab you have a virtual horizontal size of 4 and a vertical one of 1, the number of desktops should also be 1.

Those settings will give you a cube, changing the horizontal size can give you a piece of paper, a triangle a pentagram etc, just leave the other values at 1 for now :)

Hope thats useful

### Post by Kevbert on 2008-05-20
Try this....
Firstly, how many desktop workspaces do you have ? You need 4. Right click on the boxes in the bottom RH corner, select preferences and set to 4 columns.
Next, go to System - Preferences - Advanced Desktop Effects - Double click on Rotate Cube, check its enabled and set Flip Time to 200, Speed to 1.7, Zoom to 0.8. Now go to Desktop Cube, Behaviour, check its enabled, set Acceleration to 4, Speed to 1.7 and Timestep to 1.2.
You should now have a cube when you select a new Workspace when clicking the workspace in bottom RH corner or using Ctrl-Alt-Left or Right arrow.
