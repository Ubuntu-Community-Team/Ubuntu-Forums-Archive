### Post by Mighty_Joe on 2009-07-22
I gave you links.  Did you follow them and read up?
I don't know about Sri Lanka, but in the US, a copyright is granted when someone creates something (a song, a book, a piece of software).  Copyright gives the creator exclusive rights to that work.  The creator can defend his copyright in court should someone violate his copyright by copying/broadcasting/selling his work without his permission.  
A software license is an attempt to further control the use of a piece of software by limiting the number of times one can copy it, prevent reverse-engineering, require the source code be made available (GPL).  They do not have force of law and I do not recall any US court ever hearing a case that involved a software license.

### Post by benj1 on 2009-07-22
to answer to OP
i don't know about the law in sri lanka, but i believe its fairly standard in the US and europe

copyright is what you get when you create a work (book,music,computer program, invention), you get it automatically and is basically to stop people copying it (although you can put it in the public domain and any one can do what ever they want with it)
a licence is basically a contract between you (the creator) and the user which they have to agree to before they can use you work.
in the case of the gpl its basically to keep the source open, along with what ever changes a user makes to it.
some licences like the bsd licence permit virtually every thing whereas others just allow you to use the software, no redistribution, no installing on multiple machines etc etc.

### Post by Mighty_Joe on 2009-07-22
> **benj1 said:**
> if no licences are valid, than than you may as well tear up all contract law, as that is what it basically is. 
[IANAL]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IANAL"), but I believe written contracts are different from software licenses.  
In a typical contract there are clauses which specify what the structure of the contract is and how one agrees to it (usually by initialing and signing in specified places). There is a long history of case law behind written contracts providing guidance on how a contract is agreed to and how it binds the parties involved.  For example, many people who are having their homes foreclosed on have frozen the process by demanding the bank produce the original paperwork ([see here]("http://commonlaw.findlaw.com/2009/02/produce-the-note-foreclosure-delay-tactic-can-encourage-lenders-to-negotiate.html")).  Try to produce the original "paperwork" for a software license. Did someone sign it?
Wiki has some [legal judgments]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_license_agreement"), but the legal binding of a user by a software license depends on many factors and is far from settled.
