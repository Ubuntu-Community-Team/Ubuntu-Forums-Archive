### Post by PilotJLR on 2009-02-04
I haven't validated these particular parts for compatibility, but I'll bet you have. Everything looks good to me.

I would go for a 500GB hard disk... they are only marginally more expensive, so why not do it now?

I'm not a big fan on onboard video, but it really depends on what apps you plan to run. If you want to play games or do anything graphics intensive, then add on an nvidia card.

The X2 processor is a good one, and is affordable. The new intel Nehalems are faster, but also more expensive. X2 is a good choice for most uses.

Also - probably want to add on an optical drive and a nice keyboard and mouse, unless you already have these ready.

### Post by lindsay7 on 2009-04-14
Two things.

1. Ram is cheap, for a few more dollars I would at least get 4 gigs of matched dual channel ddr2. Then you do not have or worry about matching it later.

2. Power supplies that come with cases are notoriously cheap and under capacity compared to what the say there rating is. I.E. peak vs. running. Look at the picture of it if there is one on the info for the case. If the fan vents to the rear and looks like a small fan, this is not good.  The better power supplies vent down and have a large fan.  Also the better p.s.'s have modular cables which makes it nice and easy to install. You only use what you need. What I have found in building computers is bigger is better when it comes to the p.s. They can be the biggest weak spot in you system and you will not know it until you have a big problem.  The system you are putting together is going to be really good and the extra money you spend on the p.s. now will be worth it.
