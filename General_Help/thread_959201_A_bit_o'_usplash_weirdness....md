### Post by fredbird67 on 2008-10-26
OK, I just installed Kiwi Linux the other day, which is basically a Ubuntu clone.  The reason I did so is because I disagree with Ubuntu's decision to include Evolution for an E-mail client.  It's not just because I like Thunderbird, but it's also because of a bug in Evolution where you can't access your inbox anymore if it reaches 2GB or more.  What's more, this is a bug that's never been fixed, from what I understand, and it's a nasty one, too!  Furthermore, it's a pain to remove Evolution from Ubuntu, plus Ubuntu will keep bringing it back whenever you update your system, and I didn't wanna deal with that headache.

Kiwi, OTOH, comes with Thunderbird installed.  Nevertheless, I'd prefer to have the default orange Ubuntu usplash appear.  I found something else on here that installs it, but the weird part is, my bootup usplash is Kiwi's usplash while my shutdown usplash is Ubuntu's.  Obviously I've got the orange Ubuntu usplash installed, but how do I get it to use Ubuntu's usplash for both bootup and shutdown?

Thanx in advance,
Fred in St. Louis, MO USA

### Post by fredbird67 on 2008-10-26
The solution was a rather simple one -- don't know why I didn't think of this earlier.

Anyways, what I did was go into Synaptic, found "usplash-theme-ubuntu", clicked it, and selected "Mark for Reinstallation", rebooted, and VOILA!  I was greeted by the standard orange Ubuntu usplash (I like orange, what can I say?).
