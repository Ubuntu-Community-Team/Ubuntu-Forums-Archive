The Unity notification bar shows only what is available from the application.  It's the menu bar for that application.  Every time you click on a different application the menu bar becomes different.  It looks one way for the gnome-terminal application and a different way when you have the Nautilus application selected.

The developers of Nautilus dropped Icons from their application.  So what you see is what you get.  Our screen looks the same way as yours.

There are lots of complaints from the community that the Nautilus developers dropped the icons.  But unfortunately the complaints fall on deaf ears.  What you see is what the developers have provided.  Nautilus is stripped of icon and menu choices.

Select Firefox, then look at the notification bar.  First fox gives lots of choices just as gnome-terminal does.

By the way, there are two buttons on the navigation bar of Nautilus that has some other options.  The far right is a gear and to the left of that is a drop down tab.  Check out those options.  Also, can you specify exactly what you think you're missing.

The developers used the logic that they didn't want redundancy for removing many default icons and options that are so common for just about any other browser or application.  If you specify what you think you're missing or can't do, I'm sure I can direct you how to get there from this stripped down interface.

If you're having trouble seeing the two items to the far right of your navigation bar (just above the Places item in your image), others has suggested tweaking your view.

In conjunction with their suggestions you can also use, "gnome-tweak-tool" (apt-get install gnome-tweak-tool).  With this you can change the colors of the theme.  It won't add any thing that isn't already there.  But you can make the colors and other features look different.
