### Post by jimbo99 on 2008-10-27
I have looked at it and I have read the reviews. Of concern is that the folks at Canonical are loosing focus on the human being and are putting more focus on the technical.  Even their terminology is getting more and more obscure.

Several issues that have bothered me are 1) not just the arbitrary decision to not include Open Office 3.0 but their summarily closing discussions by people on the brainstorm site that points to no good reason not to include it.  In fact, of all the comments laid out by Canonical all of their reasoning could easily be rebutted by better logic.  For instance, they stated the delays of the official release of Open Office 3.0 created the issue.  In reality they've had the beta for months and months.  They've had plenty of time to test the beta install; and since the official release would have been out over 2 weeks prior to the ubuntu 8.10, they could easily have prototyped those months ago, and then substituted the officials files relatively easily.  2)  Another issue is that they are not fixing the issues with the solid gforce 4 cards to use the accelerated drivers. They are blaming this on nvidia and xorg when in reality since this is open source they should have been taking the time to resolve the issues themselves and give the fixes back to at least xorg.

I have drawn the conclusion that Canonical is getting more and more baked in middle management with some maverick types that are trying to direct the show (if even by just pressuring people in meetings to acquiesce to their choices).  At one point Mark Shuttleworth stated that not even he was able to override the decisions of the team, yet now it seems someone is.

I've been watching the brainstorm site and have found that the devs are essentially blocking valid suggestions, closing threads, and summarily dismissing solid ideas at the whim and excuse of single individuals.  Once example was someone suggested a change and the dev laid out a "single" person's countering argument (which was flakey) and then closed the thread.

Does this mean I won't be going to 8.10?  No, I will, but it also means that I am looking at other distros for alternatives even though I have used Ubuntu as my primary OS for years and have been a proponent of it to many others.

If the situation gets any worse and the devs begin to belittle more people and they start to summarily dismiss more threads by closing them arbitrarily then I will move on.

Be advised that though they have resolved some wireless issues it is pretty much a guarantee that they will break others--it always happens.  But also keep in mind that with the broadcom wireless it still won't work in many cases as this driver relies on the fwcutter firmware which itself is very buggy and incomplete thus not covering the full range of wireless systems based on broadcom, especially those in the 2-3 year old arena--and that means a lot as many of those older notebooks were from HP and they don't seem to work well with fwcutter.

I downloaded the release candidate and booted with the Live CD.  When I got to the desktop I looked to see if it had detected my wireless card in the laptop.  It had not.  I then went into the hardware option off the system > Administration menu and check to see if it saw that there was hardware there.  It did not.  It does not support the wireless on my less than 2 year old laptop with a dual core processor.

As well, I checked to see if it handled compiz.  By default it did not.  It should have configured it at the boot but it didn't.  Fedora had detected it without problem.  After I told it to use the hardware driver provided it failed to work properly.  When I clicked on a different window then moved my cursor over the titlebar of the inactive window the whole titlebar became corrupt.  The min/max/close buttons were distorted, etc.

Mark Shuttleworth implied that he has no intention of trying to make money off the desktop version of Ubuntu, when really he could.  He said it was never his intent.  That means there's a real lack of commitment on his part to further remove bug number one.  We may have to wait significantly longer before we find a distro with a real commitment to the desktop.

### Post by maestrobwh1 on 2008-11-22
Unless you are using one of the older Atheros cards... then Intrepid for the moment is annoying, at least it had been for me.

Two years ago I pulled out my Broadcom card in favor of an Atheros card: I wanted out of the box support.  There is too much confusion and effort to get the Atheros card working reliably through the updates/upgrades.

I am not saying that there wasn't or won't be an attempt to make everything better... I agree that almost everything is better. Running iwconfig, it even seems to run at 54 Mbps, and when I not surfing, it drops the power so it seems and my battery life seems a tad better.  

My laptop also resumes from hibernate properly 100% of the time.

Can't log out and back in though and have kdm come back up... there is a bug with my video card.

All in all, once I got wireless working again, and with my old card, I am happy with Intrepid again.
