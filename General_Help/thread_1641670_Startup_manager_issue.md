### Post by Krytarik on 2010-12-10
Ubuntu uses GRUB2 instead of GRUB (version 1) since v9.10.
Oviously the developers of "StartUp-Manager" didn't adapt it to the new structure/commands of GRUB2 for the advanced features too up until now, instead they disabled them.:-s

Nevertheless you could change the options manually, look there: [GRUB2 Splashimages]("http://members.iinet.net/%7Eherman546/p20/GRUB2%20Splashimages.html")

---

### Post by uRock on 2010-12-10
Ubuntu's use of Grub2 and Plymouth has changed the way Startup-Manager works and, therefore, caused a loss of some of the functionality.
