### Post by kurt18947 on 2011-11-26
I think the Software Center is intended to provide sort of a 'shopping experience' for those new to  Ubuntu/Linux who don't really know what they want.  How many ads do you see on primetime TV or ads in glossy magazines extolling the virtues of Inkscape or GIMP?  Great software that precious few people know about.  Software Center can help in that situation.  Synaptic is more for 'I'm knowledgeable enough to know that I want ___ package.'  Anyone knowledgeable enough to know what apps/packages they want are knowledgeable enough to install Synaptic.   I agree with Synaptic & Gdebi as part of the post-install updates.
