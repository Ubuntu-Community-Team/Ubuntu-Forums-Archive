### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2011-01-09
The best option would be to buy/use an extra hard drive for testing, you don't need a good one, 20 GB will be fine for Ubuntu.
When you want to install, disconnect your XP drive and plug in the spare. This way you will not worry about messing up Windows or the MBR. Once installed you can leave them both plugged in and choose between them with BIOS.

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2011-01-09
> **isee said:**

I'd really like to learn how to plug them both in and choose from BIOS!  Right now I have to switch manually, but I rarely use my Windows drive anyways.  I was hoping there might be some type of device to choose which drive, as I was told you can't have two drives with OS's plugged in together.

Having 2 drives with diferent systems on each is fine. When you boot up, you should use f11/f12 to get a one time boot menu and select what drive you want to use (just like booting from a live cd).
If you go into BIOS you can select which drive to have as default.
