### Post by Bartender on 2007-11-10
> **Arwen said:**
> How can I check if PSU is ok or not?(I can't use it in my pc cause the m/b supports ATX and the PSU is microATX)

I'm probly not getting the picture here - if you open up both PC's and remove PSU's, the physical shape of the PSU is immaterial.  The only consideration will be power (m-ATX PSU's are usually lower-powered) and cable connections or lack thereof.  If the functional PSU is only 20-pin and the PC to be tested is 24-pin, well, that will hold you up too.

Generally speaking, PSU's are pretty interchangeable.  Can you go either way?  Either get the suspected dead PSU into your PC, or take yours over to the non-functioning PC?  Or find someone else who's got a spare PSU and/or is willing to pull theirs out?

Also inspect the dead PC for sign of physical damage on the motherboard.  It's possible that something shorted out and killed the PSU.  Plugging in another one could then kill that one too.
