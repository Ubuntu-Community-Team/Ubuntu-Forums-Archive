### Post by mrdek11 on 2010-08-06
Apparently there is a unicode character specifically for a minus sign. It is #2212.  A quick Google search says you can hold ctrl+shift and type the number, 2212, to type the character, but I'm not at home, so I can't try it out for you. Good luck!

### Post by simosx on 2010-08-06
> **mrdek11 said:**
> Apparently there is a unicode character specifically for a minus sign. It is #2212.  A quick Google search says you can hold ctrl+shift and type the number, 2212, to type the character, but I'm not at home, so I can't try it out for you. Good luck!

It's Ctrl+Shift+u then type 2212 and finally press Space: &#8722;

You can check at [http://www.unicode.org/charts](http://www.unicode.org/charts) whether characters are considered equivalent in Unicode. Probably hyphen and minus are equivalent.
