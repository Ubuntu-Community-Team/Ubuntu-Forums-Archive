### Post by jimimc on 2009-06-24
> **Legace said:**
> What do you mean by docking? That it always stays there, on top of all other windows? If so, right-click on the Pidgin entry in the window list and select "Always on top".

Always on top is not the same as docking. When you dock it takes up a portion of the screen and the remaining portion can be used by any other window. When you maximize any window in that remaining portion it only takes up that remaining space instead of going "behind" the "always on top" pidgin buddy list. The advantage to this is you can see the entire window, just a smaller version of it, instead of having text hidden behind pidgin.
