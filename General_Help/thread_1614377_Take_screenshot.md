### Post by oldefoxx on 2010-11-05
I can't speak for the Ubuntu community, but there are other occasions where leaving a timer set at zero disables it.  Besides, if you did specify 0, and click on Take Snapshot, wouldn't you expect some risk of not returning to the correct screen in time?  I often allow 5 to 10 seconds, especially if I have to use the mouse to screen the portion of the screen of greatest interest to me.

As to software that bumped up, things do change, both in controls and in offered features.  Nature of the beast.  Probably the most common reason that some people have in not getting on board with the latest and greatest.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-11-05
I think the **Alt+Print Screen** failure to get an active window screenshot is due to the keys already being part of a newer standard keyboard shortcut for killing the xserver.

I have simply changed the option in Keyboard shortcuts to Winkey+Print Screen.
