### Post by ph1 on 2008-04-28
There are two ways to get around this, as detailed by the good folks over at lifehacker:

1.  Download & install the firefox extension "Nightly Tester Tools."  Then, when you open the addons window, you'll have a button that says "Override Compatibility."  It'll warn you that this can make your system unstable, but click it anyway and you'll get your extensions back.

2.  You can also work some magic in about:config, which is even more comprehensive then the above fix, but a little less easy.

To see the lifehacker guide on how to do #2, pay them a visit at [this site]("http://www.lifehacker.com.au/tips/2008/02/14/make_your_extensions_work_with_the_firefox_3_beta-2.html").

I know how awful it is to be deprived of your favorite extensions.  I was pining away without Tab Mix Plus.
